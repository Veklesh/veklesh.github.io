Episode 35 Unnamed Old Tree

The Yarkuk territory is a transit point between Celta and Refun. Belonging to the former kingdom of Ferius, this land is treated as an adjunct to the Refun area. The only role it played was as an ore transportation route. Among them, the ancient road defended by Fort Eskish is the least valuable due to its inconvenience. It was an open secret that half of the value of the position lay in the fact that it was a quiet position where troublesome people could be removed.

The man who had held the position of general for many years in the fort was Ratu, the chief of the hundred. He was an honest but ridiculously serious man with little sense of organizational politics. He was a smoky, troublesome man to have under your command. He was a hot-tempered superior officer who did not know how to get off his shoulders from soldiers he had not known for a long time.

He would often order his soldiers to run along the trails in their full equipment and to repair the fort, which was hard work. To make matters worse, Ratu always took the lead. The soldiers say that he is watched at all hours by a small group of sympathizers who are inspired by his words and actions, and he is unable to cut corners. The Highserk empire and even the Great Outbreak had forgotten about the existence of Fort Eskish, although they wanted their lives to be fully scattered on the battlefield. He was an old man, ignorant of warfare, ignorant of reality, and ridiculously large in stature. That was the evaluation of Ratu.

Ratu was in his room, staring at an old map. Rare communication tools were placed in the most important strongholds, and Fort Eskish had no choice but to rely on fast horses and wolfsbane to send messages. No further news had been brought since the departure of the Highserk knight from Lehun.

"Where is it, where does it go?

The notes and writing on the map were made by Ratu. He knows the area around the fort better than anyone back home, having done a lot of running and surveying.

“Either we've already passed through, or the rest of the troops are ...... leaving me behind again.”

Ratu is not a drafted militia member. He was a volunteer soldier of his own volition. He was inspired by the tales of heroism told to him by the elders of his village. Although it was not popular with those of his age, Ratu was so eager to learn it word for word that he memorized it as a child.

“You're getting wrinkles again.”

Looking down at his hand, he sees the evidence of aging that he cannot hide. It's not just the wrinkles. The muscles in his arms and legs were also beginning to deteriorate. He has been wearing armor for a few years now, and has been circling the animal trail to cultivate his physical strength, but that too has been a waste of time and energy. His body is still in a state of decline in the face of his approaching old age.

As a professional soldier, his role is to protect the assets and lives of his people. The fact that the fort is free from warfare is proof that he is fulfilling his duty. Even though I tell myself this, the fire that smolders in the back of my mind only grows stronger with age. A fortress and an old general left behind by the times. He says he volunteered to become a soldier because he wanted to be on the battlefield, but at this rate he will die of old age on his bed. What an irony. I would add that his heart is also so weak that he laments the environment in which he is placed.

“Old man, you can't take away my longing!”

He slapped his face to remind himself and went to the command center. The soldiers inside greeted him with a salute. Ratu responds with a salute and asks them to be seated.

“General Ratu, do you think the Demon Fire will pass through here?”

The one who asked anxiously was an old soldier who had been with him for a long time. The generals in the command center are the most unusual ones in the fort who adore Ratu. The generals in the command post are the odd ones who adore Ratu among the fort's soldiers.

“I am convinced that they will come because it is a forgotten fortress.”

The commander's assurance brought back the color to the soldiers' faces. The commander should not speak of his fears. This time, this time, this time - his determination was evident in the armor he wore. Aside from a few short water breaks, the men in command kept their gear on even while they slept. On the one hand, they want to be rewarded, but on the other hand, considering the villages adjacent to the fort and the militia who are like babies, they would prefer peace.

He was aware of this. It is foolish, selfish, and sinful to wish for a battlefield. But the longing for that heroic tale will never fade. The only thing Ratu can do is to prepare for the coming threat.

"However, I am not surprised that the militia that has gathered here is being dispersed. What is the head of the thousand people in the main domain thinking?"

“That's how early he wants to detect the threat.”

The chief of the thousand, who is high up in the chain of command, gave the order to deploy the militia even to the beast paths. It is no mistake that more manpower would increase the chances of contact. However, Ratu was also concerned that the soldiers, who were like babies, would be killed before they had time to cry out. To tell the truth, he would have preferred a concentrated operation at the fort. But as a soldier, his orders were absolute.

“Isn't he a big man like an ogre?”

More than Ratu-sama?"

“If Ratu-sama is an old tree, we are new shoots.”

A modest laugh echoed through the command hall. He had a strong yearning for the battlefield, but this atmosphere was probably not so bad either. Although I am a fool, perhaps I will be able to tolerate some peace before I die. Despite this calm feeling, Ratu was roused by a faint noise. The soldiers followed suit belatedly.

"The sound of battle!

“No way, this is...”
