"Of the five leaders who once fought against the Black Calamity, one had a particularly mysterious background. To this day, I have been unable to uncover any information about her..."

Silence fell upon the woods as Wuyue spoke calmly, her eyes shrouded in mist as she gazed at the Southern Venerable before her.

"Are you referring to the Western Sage?"

"But asking me is a mistake. I don’t know much about the Western Sage either. All I can tell you is that she is very mysterious. Do you suspect she comes from the Palace of Defiance?"

Southern Venerable smiled gently.

She withdrew all her thoughts. Ever since she had been imprisoned here, she had lost all contact with the outside world.

High Priest Wu Yue rarely came to see her, let alone ask her for any news.

Only on certain occasions, when Nan Qing met with her, would High Priest Wu Yue appear to open a passage for the two of them to meet.

It had been this way for all these years, without a single unexpected occurrence.

But now, judging by the fact that High Priest Wuyue had taken the initiative to ask her about the Palace of Defiance, it seemed the situation outside was already fully under the Heavenly Host’s control.

That is why the Heavenly Host now has the leisure to turn their attention to the hidden realms and forces lying within the Vast Expanse.

But the Palace of Defiance has always been shrouded in mystery; even among cultivators, very few know the name of this faction. What could she possibly know?

“Even if the Western Saint isn’t from the Palace of Defiance, she’s still inextricably linked to it. Otherwise, after all these years, it would be impossible not to have uncovered any clues or leads regarding her,” Wuyue said calmly.

Southern Venerable smiled and said, “If the Western Saint is truly connected to the Palace of Reversed Fate, how could you possibly have found out?”

Wuyue replied, “It seems you do indeed have some understanding of the Palace of Defying Fate.”

The Southern Venerable did not deny this, continuing, “In consideration of how you, the Grand High Priest, have treated me with courtesy all these years, I can tell you this much: the Palace of Defiance does indeed have intricate ties to the Nine Heavens.”

“You might have the Heavenly Host storm the Nine Heavens Realm and track down the so-called Patriarch of the Overheaven Sect—then you’ll learn of the Palace of Defiance’s existence.”

“However, at present, it is unclear who exactly is currently leading the Futiang Sect.”

Wuyue’s eyes flickered. She had indeed heard of the Futiang Sect, but the Nine Heavens was a place of great peculiarity to the Vast Expanse. Investigating it was exceedingly difficult; even the Dark Clans found it hard to infiltrate and locate a path there.

However, with Nan Qing currently in the Nine Heavens, this presented the Heavenly Host with a golden opportunity.

She said, “It seems you, Southern Venerable, know quite a bit about the Nine Heavens. I happen to have some news I’d like to share with you.”

Southern Venerable looked at her with slight surprise and asked, “What news?”

“Nan Qing has already entered the Nine Heavens. Barring any unforeseen circumstances, she should retrieve the Mirror of Reincarnation and clear the path for our Heavenly Host’s army…” Wuyue said.

Southern Venerable’s expression changed; the calm and composure he had shown moments before vanished.

She fixed her gaze on Wuyue and asked, “Did you send Qing’er to the Nine Heavens?”

“No, she encountered some people by chance and was able to enter it through a stroke of fate. If you don’t want anything to happen to Nan Qing, you’d best tell me everything you know.”

“I care deeply for Nan Qing, and I certainly don’t want her to face any danger,” Wu Yue said calmly.

The Southern Venerable’s chest heaved with emotion. After a long moment, he finally regained his composure and said, “The Nine Heavens Realm is extremely dangerous. You’d best not act impulsively. It is one of the most perilous and treacherous places in the Vast Expanse. Without the Lord of Heaven leading the Heavenly Host, dispatching as many dark spirits as you like would be a waste of effort.”

“Is that because the Nine Heavens are backed by the Palace of Defiance?” Wuyue asked.

Southern Venerable nodded.

“Then tell me everything you know,” Wuyue said. “I will make my own judgment.”

Time passed. Decades had elapsed since the collapse of the Righteous Unity Alliance, followed by the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s consolidation of its territories and the construction of a barrier to resist the Dark Clans and counter the Great Dark Ritual.

During this time, the Dark Tribes had not ceased their Great Sacrifice; they continued, as always, to harvest the great realms of the cosmos.

"The Righteous Unity Alliance has fallen, and even the whereabouts of the Ancestor Xu are unknown..."

"Could it be that I’ve truly been outmaneuvered by him? Is my Black Lotus Sacred Realm about to come to naught?"

"Could the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance really be..."

Deep within the gloomy underground palace, the Formless’s face was grim, a mixture of fear and dread.

He stared intently at the scene before him. The final black lotus hovering above the Ethereal Sacred Realm was beginning to crumble and shatter under the combined assault of numerous powerful beings.

As the threads of faith energy snapped, he even sensed a gaze following the energy, attempting to pinpoint his location.

The Formless acted decisively, immediately severing the source of all faith energy and completely concealing his presence.

On this day, the Piao Miao Sacred Realm—which had been quiet for a long time—finally showed signs of disturbance. The terrifying black cocoon burst open, and the black mist filling the sky seemed to be illuminated by warm sunlight, continuously dissipating and fading away.

Through the combined efforts of all the guests present, the Black Lotus Sect’s conspiracy was finally thwarted.

Those terrifying black lotuses, which had once obscured the sky, eventually vanished without a trace. Only the living beings and cultivators corrupted by the Black Lotus Sect’s doctrines remained in a coma; it would take at least several hundred years to truly awaken them.

The major clans of the Piao Miao Sacred Realm suffered heavy losses in resolving this calamity, and even the Piao Miao Sacred Sect was no exception.

Even the Piao Miao Holy Ancestor, by the end, was utterly exhausted. He sat cross-legged atop Piao Miao Peak to rest, and the other distinguished guests did the same.

As for the entire Piao Miao Sacred Realm, it lay in ruins. The cosmos was shattered everywhere; it could be said to be completely devastated. For the next few hundred or even thousands of years, it would be unsuitable for cultivators to live and practice there, and they would need to relocate.

On Piao Miao Peak, watching the black mist disperse, the numerous distinguished guests and dignitaries finally let out long-awaited smiles, the weight on their hearts finally lifted.

Decades of time, to them, were but the blink of an eye, hardly worth dwelling on. The only uncertainty now was the situation outside. With the Righteous Unity Alliance and the Dark Clans joining forces, and Gu Changge, the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, trapped here, the situation outside could hardly be good…

Thus, many remained deeply concerned.

The high-ranking members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance fared slightly better, as Gu Changge’s intervention in the end helped alleviate much of the pressure. Facing that terrifying Black Lotus and the nearly indestructible Ancestral Dao Realm guardians, they did not struggle too much.

Lord Luo and the Eternal Primordial God also contributed their fair share. Their strength was in no way inferior to that of the Ethereal Holy Ancestor. Though they did not know Gu Changge’s true intentions, seeing that he showed no signs of attacking the people here—instead, acting as if directing a play to eliminate that massive Black Lotus—they tactfully joined in the effort.

As the vast black mist outside the Piao Miao Sacred Realm began to dissipate, civilizations from all corners of the True Realms took notice. Many ancient figures immediately appeared and rushed over, attempting to investigate.

Everyone quickly grasped the immense shifts in the situation within the Vast Expanse during this period, and they all froze in place, shaken to the core.

No one had expected that, while they had assumed the Heaven Slaying Alliance would face a crisis, it was actually the Righteous Unity Alliance that collapsed first. As for the grand ritual initiated by the Dark Clans, that was, of course, within everyone’s expectations.

After all, with such events unfolding, how could the Dark Clans possibly sit idly by?

It was Lord Luo and the Eternal Primordial God who were the most shaken and trembling, especially Lord Luo. He had a vague inkling of what was happening on the side of the Heavenly Host. After all, he had once been Gu Changge, one of the Eight Department Lords, stationed in the Piao Miao Sacred Realm. He had clearly done nothing, yet the situation outside had not affected the chessboard he had laid out in the slightest.

He must have foreseen the upheaval that might occur among the Heavenly Host long ago.

The collapse of the Righteous Unity Alliance meant that the mastermind behind it had either perished or fled—in short, he had been defeated in his power struggle with Gu Changge.

As for other matters, he dared not speculate.

"The Righteous Unity Alliance has now been wiped out, and the Immortal Dao Alliance exists in name only. From now on, we can only rely more and more on the Alliance Master..."

"Our original intention was to establish the Immortal Dao Alliance to keep the Dark Clans in check, but now that the Dark Clans have already held their grand ritual, it is too late to close the barn door after the horse has bolted."

The Ethereal Sage Ancestor himself did not harbor great ambition; his support for the Immortal Dao Alliance was merely in fulfillment of the Western Sage’s entrustment.

Now that the Righteous Unity Alliance has fallen and the Immortal Dao Alliance has waned, it is simply not on the same level as the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Rather than establishing such a nominal alliance that exists in name only—and thereby inviting speculation from the civilizations of the True Realm—it would be better to align with the Heaven Slaying Alliance outright.

During the years they were trapped within the Piao Miao Sacred Realm, the various distinguished guests and visitors witnessed the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s might firsthand and dared not harbor the slightest thought of resistance.

Gu Changge did not intervene himself; the two mysterious figures by his side subdued the Black Lotus Sect’s Ancestral Dao Realm guardian with a single palm strike—a display of power rivaling that of the Piao Miao Holy Ancestor.

Now, to resist the Dark Tribes, they could only rely on the Heaven Slaying Alliance, regarding it as their backbone; otherwise, the strength of a single True Realm Civilization alone would be far from sufficient.

With the Piao Miao Holy Ancestor’s voluntary submission, the numerous distinguished guests present—each representing their respective True Realms of Civilization—all echoed his sentiment, expressing their willingness to submit to the Heaven Slaying Alliance and join forces to confront this catastrophic dark calamity.

Zhao Tianqi, the former Deputy Leader of the Righteous Unity Alliance, along with other high-ranking officials, also spoke up, declaring without reservation that they would submit to and align themselves with the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

During his time in the Piao Miao Sacred Realm, Zhao Xiuyan had fully revealed that Gu Changge was his master. Zhao Tianqi was overjoyed and deeply moved; naturally, he had no objections whatsoever to aligning with the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

As for the task of eliminating the remnants of the Black Lotus Sect, Gu Changge did not intervene, leaving it to the Piao Miao Holy Ancestor and the others. Although he was in the Piao Miao Holy Realm, the various events unfolding in the Vast Expanse were all within his sight and as he had anticipated.

In short, the course of events was entirely within his plan. Since the Black Lotus Sect still served a purpose to him, he did not follow the chain of causality to seek out trouble for the Formless.

Of the former Eight Clans, only a handful remained. The Formless was one of the Eight Clan Leaders of the Pujing Clan. Compared to Yan Zu, who had perished in the Nightmare Demon Realm, the Formless was far more fortunate.

The Ancestor Xu, fully aware of his greed, used Formless to deal with the Lord of Destiny, persuading him to agree to the deal, and then joined forces to head to the Nightmare Demon Realm to confront the Heavenly Host.

Had Gu Changge not allowed Jing Tianyuan to send a wisp of his primordial essence, Wuyue would have suffered a major setback this time. The Primordial Ancestor Xu was extremely cautious and cunning; even when manifesting in the Nightmare Demon Realm, he had only deployed one of his illusory forms, while his true body remained hidden in some corner of spacetime.

As for the Master of Destiny, his decisiveness actually earned a slight measure of admiration from Gu Changge; he severed that physical form without the slightest hesitation.

However, in his eyes, the Master of Destiny was nothing more than a wriggling little insect; no matter how many tricks he used to escape, he wouldn’t be able to stir up much of a storm.

"Alliance Master, my father...?" Jiang Weiyang had always been deeply concerned about the whereabouts and situation of the Emperor of Weiyang.

Especially after learning from Zhao Tianqi that, during the days of the Righteous Unity Alliance, Emperor Weiyang had been the Master of Destiny’s most favored disciple. Now that the Righteous Unity Alliance had been destroyed and dissolved, Emperor Weiyang and many other high-ranking members had vanished without a trace, just like the Master of Destiny.

Gu Changge looked at her and said, “When he handed you that silk pouch, he already knew his fate.”

“No matter how many times you ask, his fate will not change, and you know full well what that outcome will be.”

Over the years, Jiang Weiyang had often asked about her father’s fate and the consequences of his actions.

After all, on that day in the Piao Miao Sacred Realm, when she opened that silk pouch in front of numerous distinguished guests,

it was tantamount to Emperor Wei Yang betraying the Master of Destiny.

The Master of Destiny must have already learned of this. How would he deal with a traitor afterward? After all, he had placed great expectations on Emperor Wei Yang and had always groomed him as his pawn.

"Father... he’s going to die, isn’t he..." Jiang Weiyang looked sorrowful.

Gu Changge remained silent; this was Emperor Wei Yang’s own choice.

Although he could intervene, locate the Lord of Destiny, and rescue Emperor Wei Yang, doing so would disrupt his future plans.

To him, Jiang Weiyang was nothing more than an insignificant nobody. Had it not been for the schemes involving the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty at the time, he wouldn’t even have met with her.

He would not change anything just because of Jiang Weiyang’s pleas.

“You may go to the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and seek out your ancestor; she can help you,” Gu Changge said.

Ji Hanjing was a descendant of the Central Lord, and Jiang Weiyang’s veins actually carried his bloodline as well. The curse that had previously blinded her eyes was one that Ji Hanjing had cast long ago.

“My ancestor…” Jiang Weiyang stared blankly for a moment, then quickly understood. She bowed and hurried away.

Yao Yao followed close behind her, and they departed together.

After the matter in the Piao Miao Sacred Realm was settled, Gu Changge led the senior officials back to the Heaven Slaying Alliance. Ling Yuling briefed him on the changes and activities of the Heaven Slaying Alliance over the years, but Gu Changge paid it little mind, simply instructing her to act as she saw fit.

As for the Lord of Luo and the Eternal Ancestor, they both remained at the Heaven Slaying Alliance without question, ready to follow orders. As for their origins, no one asked.

Zhao Xiuyan also stayed at the Heaven Slaying Alliance for a time; she had always regarded meeting Gu Changge again as her goal in cultivation.

Now that she knew his true identity, she felt even more acutely the vast gulf between their respective statuses and powers, which only spurred her to train with even greater diligence and determination, never daring to slack off.

During her time at the Heaven Slaying Alliance, she actually became friends with Mo Tong, whom Gu Changge had taken from the arena in the Xianling Civilization.

Although Mo Tong practiced the Way of Slaughter and was as cold as an ice sculpture, rarely smiling or speaking, after spending time with Zhao Xiuyan, their relationship improved, and they often sparred together.

Originally, Zhao Xiuyan had regarded Mo Tong as a hunting target, believing that the dense fortune and profound blessings within Mo Tong would surely bring numerous Destiny Points to her Hongmeng Golden Book.

However, once she realized that Mo Tong had also been instructed by Gu Changge and bestowed with Daoist teachings, she abandoned that notion and came to regard him as something akin to a fellow disciple.

When it came to Yao Yao, she strictly adhered to the rules and addressed her as “Senior Sister.”Although Yao Yao felt a bit unhappy inside—thinking that Gu Changge had taken on too many disciples, not just her alone, and that this had diluted the affection meant for her—she had matured considerably with age and no longer threw the tantrums of her youth. She simply nodded in response to Zhao Xiuyan’s greeting.

After Ling Yuling’s reorganization, the Heaven Slaying Alliance had grown considerably stronger than before.

The territories and civilizations that had disintegrated following the collapse of the Righteous Unity Alliance were gradually absorbed and annexed by the Heaven Slaying Alliance, and the Immortal Dao Alliance had voluntarily submitted and aligned itself with them.

In just a few decades, the Heaven Slaying Alliance had consolidated its power across the Vast Expanse, establishing a dominant position. The remaining civilizations of the True Realm, both large and small, were also forced to submit under the threat of the Dark Tribes.

Previously, with the Righteous Unity Alliance still in existence to counterbalance the Heaven Slaying Alliance, there was still room for survival in the cracks between the two powers.

Furthermore, since the Dark Clans had not previously held such brazen and arrogant Dark Grand Rituals, many clans and Civilizations had maintained an attitude of waiting to see which side would prevail. But now, the situation is different from the past; there is no room for choice.

For ordinary factions, without a Dao Realm expert to hold the fort, even crossing the Vast Expanse is difficult; attempting to migrate and join the Heaven Slaying Alliance is even more of a daunting task.

Moreover, the Heaven Slaying Alliance does not simply accept every tribe or civilization that comes to submit. While the threshold was once low, the conditions are now extremely harsh.

Under the current circumstances, without protection, one is destined to be swallowed up and devoured by the Dark Clans. Though the Vast Expanse is vast and boundless, with endless time and space and countless universes, there may always be a place to hide—but given enough time, the Dark Clans will inevitably track down one’s location…

Some powerful beings have sought to emulate the Heaven Slaying Alliance by establishing so-called “safe zones,” but without the absolute power to deter the Dark Tribes, these safe zones are nothing more than sheep pens.

The Divine Path Civilization serves as the best example: after being attacked and overrun by the Dark Clans, it collapsed swiftly. Only a few Ancestral Gods remained, abandoning their divine realm to flee deep into the chaotic void, where they vanished without a trace.
