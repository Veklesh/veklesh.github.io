Civilizations across the Realm of Truth were all watching and speculating; at this moment, no one dared to intervene rashly.

From every realm where the dark beings now dwell, terrifying gazes pierced through time and space, turning to watch this great battle.

Many reclusive clans that had long since withdrawn from the world were now equally shaken and astonished.

Times have changed; this is no longer the era of a glittering constellation of civilizations, where races vied for supremacy and the True Realms of various civilizations maintained a delicate balance.

Whether it was the Heaven Slaying Alliance or the Righteous Unity Alliance, both had grown so powerful that they far surpassed ordinary supreme realms. At this juncture, individual civilizations no longer had the standing to compete with such terrifying forces.

Once this great war concludes, the entire Vast Expanse will likely usher in a new era.

After all, looking back through the countless eras since the birth of the Vast Expanse, how many powers have ever reached such heights?

In ancient times, the mere emergence of a single power comparable to the Zhengyi Alliance was enough to strike terror into the entire Cangmang, instilling apprehension and fear in all races and True Realm civilizations.

Let alone a situation like the present, where the Heaven Slaying Alliance, the Zhengyi Alliance, and the Dark Beings stand in a three-way stalemate.

One could say that all of this is somewhat too fantastical and unbelievable—and perhaps a bit too coincidental.

Had the Dark Spirits not reawakened and returned, the Zhengyi Alliance would never have come into being. Everything feels as though it were predestined from the very beginning.

It feels as though an unseen hand is orchestrating and guiding all of this.

No race or civilization can remain aloof from these events as they once did.

The entire vast and uncertain landscape has begun to tremble and grow unpredictable due to the great war between the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Zhengyi Alliance.

No one knows if the flames of war will reach them the very next day.

At a time like this, the destruction and extinction of entire races seemed almost insignificant; even the very universe and spacetime they inhabited might be silently obliterated.

At the headquarters of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, a colossal tower stood, soaring to an unknown height of tens of thousands of zhang. It was shrouded in boundless, resplendent light, with ring upon ring of chaotic mist, resembling nebulae, swirling around the tower.

Within that nebula-like substance, one could see small worlds rising and falling, surrounded by living stars. As they collided, they emitted a rumbling sound akin to the rotation of the world.

Surrounding this tower, one could faintly make out one terrifying, shadowy figure after another.

Each figure was as towering and imposing as an ancient god, clad in golden armor and wielding battle spears, standing guard nearby. Vaguely discernible yet concealed within the void, they exuded a terrifying aura that sent shivers down the spine.

On the endless jade staircase leading to the tower, clouds swirled, rosy light spread, and figures multiplied—everywhere were people wearing expressions of excitement and elation.

However, due to the array patterns, the true faces of those coming and going could not be discerned.

This is the Tower of Military Merit, restored at great cost by the Heaven Slaying Alliance, standing eighty-one stories tall.

Each level is a self-contained realm, vast and boundless, engraved with over 583 intricate array patterns.

Any member of the Heaven Slaying Alliance may enter the Tower of Military Merit with a Merit Token to exchange it for corresponding opportunities.

From the divine sources of the Daoist Canon, ancient celestial arts, and forbidden techniques, down to various hidden texts, divine medicines and elixirs, Daoist artifacts and divine weapons, as well as celestial materials and divine ores—it encompasses everything under heaven.

Within the Heaven Slaying Alliance, it is widely said that there is nothing within the Tower of Battle Merit that a cultivator cannot exchange for—only things they cannot conceive of.

Even those in the Ancestral Dao Realm yearn intensely for the various rare treasures and Daoist canons within the Tower of Military Merit.

This alone speaks to the immense and boundless depth of the War Merit Tower’s resources.

Since the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s inception, it has swept across and conquered vast territories. The accumulated essence of countless civilizations, realms, and ancestral lineages—the result of ages of development—has all been absorbed by the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Now, the Leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance has openly placed these treasures within the Tower of Military Merit, allowing cultivators to exchange them for battle merits.

When the Tower of Military Merit was first established, it caused a tremendous sensation.

Every cultivator of the Heaven Slaying Alliance was stunned and appalled, for this move was tantamount to opening the granaries to the public and laying everything bare.

The accumulated foundations of various races and sects are treated as family heirlooms and the very pillars of their clans—treasured above all else. How could they ever be revealed so lightly? Let alone being made public in this manner, giving everyone the opportunity to glimpse the genuine artifacts and qualify to study and gain insights from them.

The Heaven Slaying Alliance Leader’s magnanimity is beyond what ordinary people can imagine or fathom.

Upon learning of this, many civilizations and realms were deeply shaken and awed. Some clans even expressed a willingness to join the Heaven Slaying Alliance in hopes of securing eligibility to enter the Tower of Military Merit.

After the War Merit Tower was constructed, it indeed played a significant role. Every member of the Heaven Slaying Alliance yearned for the divine artifacts within, and during this period, they fought relentlessly against enemies, waging war across all fronts to accumulate war merits.

Even those who had long followed Alliance Leader Gu Changge—such as the Ancestor of the Zhuo Clan, Zhuo Fengwei; the Distant Ancestor of the Hun Clan, Hun Yuanjun; and the Spirit Emperor of the Lingxu True Realm—were all engaged in battle, undertaking various missions, and accumulating battle merits.

Only those who have actually entered the Tower of Military Merit can truly comprehend the vastness of the treasures within; even descriptions like “as brilliant as the Milky Way” or “as numerous as the sands of the Ganges” fall far short of capturing its grandeur.

In recent years, the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, in addition to constructing the Tower of Military Merit, has also instructed the Alliance’s high-ranking officials to gather erudite scholars from all races and collect the treasured texts and records held by each clan, with the intention of compiling a grand compendium.

No one knows the Heaven Slaying Alliance Leader’s true intentions. While ordering campaigns to conquer all directions and expand the realm, he simultaneously compiles this grand compendium and gathers hidden texts—could he truly be planning to usher in a new era?

"Even I nearly lost myself in it, forgetting the heavy responsibility I bear. I have no idea how many more rare treasures and divine artifacts lie waiting to be discovered within this Tower of Military Merit." At that moment, a coldly beautiful woman dressed in black, holding an ancient scroll, finished reading it with rapt attention before coming to her senses as if waking from a dream.

She looked at the teeming crowd around her, shook her head, and let a wry, resigned smile play at the corners of her mouth.

Even she had nearly become lost in the vast treasures of the Tower of Military Merit, almost neglecting her duties—let alone the others.

Wherever the Ji Chan clan passed, the ages shifted, eras cycled, and all things were renewed—they were hailed as the chroniclers of heaven and earth.

They chronicle the history of heaven and earth and record the annals of the ages; the clan’s treasured collection of classics is naturally vast as the clouds, and the items that catch their eye are few and far between.

Yet, within the War Merit Tower of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, she had recently come across numerous records that piqued her interest. For this reason, she had specifically taken on numerous missions, accumulated sufficient war merits, and exchanged them for those few volumes.

This had nearly made her forget the very purpose of her journey to the Heaven Slaying Alliance; she had almost become intoxicated by it all, devoting herself entirely to becoming a battle merit fanatic.

"The fate of the Ji Chan clan still awaits my intervention. My eldest sister’s whereabouts are unknown, and her life hangs in the balance. Little Nine has gone to the High Priestess to appease her wrath for the sake of the Ji Chan clan—she may well face humiliation..."

"I’ve actually wasted the battle merits I worked so hard to accumulate on these scriptures."

The woman in black sighed, carefully putting the scriptures away, her expression returning to its usual cold and solemn demeanor.

Before joining the Heaven Slaying Alliance, she had believed that, relying on her own strength, it would be easy to meet the Alliance Leader, Gu Changge.

But once she had actually joined the Heaven Slaying Alliance, she discovered that even for the Alliance’s current high-ranking members, securing an audience with Gu Changge was incredibly difficult.

For an outsider like her, the only chance was to accumulate enough military merits to earn the qualification to request an audience with Alliance Leader Gu Changge.

As for whether the Heaven Slaying Alliance Leader would actually see her, that was entirely up to fate.

Even so, she had to find a way to accumulate enough military merits, for only then might she be able to slightly alter the plight of the Ji Chan clan within the Ancient Zang Civilization.

Due to the True Land, the Ancient Zang Civilization held a unique status; even as major wars erupted and turmoil spread across the vast lands of Cangmang, it remained largely unaffected.

Neither the Dark Beings nor the Zhengyi Alliance had ever encroached upon the Ancient Zang Civilization.

But the Heaven Slaying Alliance was different—ruthless and reckless. Even the Ancient Zang Civilization feared them immensely, unwilling to provoke them and doing their utmost to avoid any involvement.

And it is precisely for this reason that her only chance to alter the fate of the Ji Chan clan lies here.

The woman in black gathered her thoughts, her figure flickering as she left the area. When she reappeared, she was already standing in a vast, boundless plaza.

Towering pillars stood as high as the heavens, majestic and imposing like mountains, radiating the aura of Daoist principles. Jade slips floated around the pillars, waiting for cultivators to come and take them.

Upon arriving, the woman in black scanned the mission details on each jade slip, selected a few that best suited her current needs, and then vanished in a streak of light.

For decades, the flames of war have raged unceasingly. Great battles have swept across the cosmos, the conflict burning relentlessly and spreading far and wide.

The borderlands of the Zhengyi Alliance were torn apart by the vast, surging armies of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, which advanced deep into their territory. The terrifying roars of battle echoed throughout the cosmos.

On battlefields of every level and tier, fierce and brutal combat raged. Even beings from the Dao Realm were no exception; they continuously entered the fray, sustained injuries, and their blood stained the heavens.

This was only the beginning of the great war; no one knew how long such carnage would ultimately last.

Across endless time and space, across countless universes, countless civilizations and races have been swept up in the chaos and upheaval.

At the border battlefield between the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance, no civilizations remained; over the years, they had all chosen to evacuate and relocate, fleeing the chaos of war.

The territories occupied by the Dark Beings remained shrouded in black mist, a bleak landscape devoid of all life.

The Dark Clans have not intervened in the fierce battles between the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance; instead, they seem to be using this time to truly re-establish themselves in the vast wilderness, aiming to restore their former Dark Realm.

And because of the peace treaty signed between the Zhengyi Alliance and the dark beings, the dark beings seemed to have truly settled down, no longer choosing to invade other civilizations.

Instead, within the original universe, they nurtured various races, even going so far as to create separate worlds specifically suited for their survival.

This scene had a rather dreamlike quality to it.

Amid the current turbulent and unstable situation, the Dark Beings were, in fact, able to provide a peaceful haven for the races within their domain.

Of course, the various races were well aware that this was not out of kindness or benevolence on the part of the Dark Beings, but rather that they were raising those races as livestock, waiting for the right moment to harvest them.

"I knew this great war would break out sooner or later. The leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance has grand ambitions; it’s certainly not just about Cangmang..."

"There’s a mastermind behind the Zhengyi Alliance, though they’ve yet to reveal themselves—quite intriguing. I wonder if it might be an old acquaintance."

Meanwhile, in a vast, lush world brimming with primal energy...

A strikingly handsome man stood with his hands behind his back on a sheer cliff face. Before him, mist swirled, enveloped in a chaotic aura, resembling a primordial landscape from before the creation of the world.

Before his eyes, as the mist swirled, one bubble-like substance after another emerged.

These entities were as clear as mirrors, imbued with the essence of the world, and simultaneously reflected scenes from every corner of the vast cosmos—truly a sight of extraordinary mystery.

"Now that you have reshaped your body, when will you fulfill the promise you made to me regarding the 'Outline of Transcendence'?" A stunningly beautiful woman clad in a golden gown, her gaze cold and distant, stepped through the air from the other side of the cliff.

“Since I promised to give you the ‘Comprehensive Guide to Transcendence,’ I will naturally do so. However, back then, I was besieged by powerful enemies. To prevent the guide from falling into their hands, I deliberately left a handwritten note in my cave dwelling to mislead future generations. The true ‘Comprehensive Guide to Transcendence’ is actually kept elsewhere…”

The heroic man smiled faintly.

His eyes were profound, gleaming with a faint golden light. As they opened and closed in an instant, it seemed as though scenes of chaos arising and vanishing, the collapse of the universe, and countless other visions emerged from nothingness, only to dissolve back into nothingness.

The woman in the golden gown furrowed her dark brows and said coldly, “If that is the case, why won’t you let me leave the territory of the Eternal Divine Clan?”

“I fear that if you leave, you might reveal my whereabouts. After all, I faked my death back then and deliberately left behind a hidden treasure. Who knows if those great enemies are still watching?”

“I’m merely asking you to remain within the Eternal Divine Clan’s territory; I’m not restricting your freedom. Come to think of it, didn’t you once impersonate the Eternal Divine Clan’s Holy Maiden? What’s wrong with staying here?” the heroic-looking man said with a smile.

Hearing this, the woman in the golden gown furrowed her dark brows even tighter, her eyes betraying no attempt to hide her coldness and disgust.

She had never expected the Eternal Primordial God to honor the agreement—that after she found the Eternal Divine Clan and had her body rebuilt, he would, as promised, hand over the Transcendence Compendium she had been searching for all along.

But she never imagined that, even after restoring her body, the Eternal Primordial God would refuse to let her leave, confining her to the current territory of the Eternal Divine Clan.

Luo Xiangjun has been trapped here all these years.

She has witnessed with her own eyes how the Eternal Primordial God, starting from the frail state immediately after reshaping her physical form, has gradually recovered to her current unfathomable state.

To reach such a level in just a few short years, she did not believe the Eternal Primordial God had specifically sent her to seek out the Eternal Divine Clan merely to facilitate the reshaping of his physical form.

He must have left some sort of contingency plan within the bloodline of the Eternal Divine Clan—something that would help him swiftly regain his peak strength in the future.

Such a cunning old fox—his schemes and methods could only be described as terrifyingly inscrutable.

Luo Xiangjun even suspected that the news of the Transcendent Master’s departure from the Eternal Primordial God’s body might have been deliberately leaked by him, allowing him to feign death and set up this scheme, thereby evading detection for countless eons.

What exactly is the motive of a figure who has existed since the Primordial Era, willing to set up such a vast scheme?

Who exactly are his so-called “great enemies”? Or is this merely his own one-sided account?

All races of the Vast Expanse must undergo the baptism of the Great Calamity. Every universe, every Great Thousand World, and even the True Realm will face the reckoning of the Great Calamity. Like cultivators rowing against the current, they must endure countless trials and tribulations to refine the essence from the dross and shed their impurities.Every world and universe, in order to transform and ascend, must face the Great Calamity."

Can your former arch-enemy truly endure so many cataclysmic purges and still survive in this world?” Luo Xiangjun stared coldly at the Eternal Primordial God and asked.

She knew the Eternal Primordial God would never allow her to leave the Eternal Divine Clan.

Rather than sitting idly by and waiting for her doom, she might as well try to see if she could pry something out of him.

How did the Transcendence Canon end up lost in the Vast Wasteland? And how did the Eternal Primordial God manage to seize it?

After all, following the end of the Primordial Era, virtually all primordial beings and clans had perished and been wiped out.

Even those primordial beings who had survived that terrifying catastrophe would have lived cautiously, treading on thin ice, and kept their whereabouts hidden.

How could they possibly be so brazen as to seize the Transcendence Compendium?

"You underestimate me. Do you really think you can coax anything out of me with mere words? Do you not realize that I am well aware of everything you’ve said? My greatest enemies are, by nature, far from simple—and certainly not something a Post-Heavenly being like you could possibly fathom."

Hearing this, the Eternal Primordial God smiled faintly, utterly unconcerned.

Luo Xiangjun continued to stare at him coldly and said, “If innate beings are so powerful, then why did you end up resorting to feigning death to escape?”

Hearing this, the Eternal Primordial God showed no anger. With a touch of nostalgia and emotion, he said, “At the end of the Primordial Era, heaven and earth had not yet been separated, and the distinction between the pure and the impure still existed. Even the Vast Expanse had not yet been born, let alone the countless universes that would be born in later ages.
