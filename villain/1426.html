When the golden wind meets the jade dew, their union surpasses all earthly joys.

To Yue Mingkong, this embrace felt as though it had spanned countless eons and ages. She gently rested her head against him, her nostrils filled with that familiar, faint, and pleasant scent. Her flawless, jade-like features were tinged with a hint of mist and longing.

Gu Changge held her quietly in his arms, gently stroking her long, flowing black hair that cascaded like a waterfall or a cloud. His eyes, deep as an abyss, closed softly, and his mind found a peace it had never known before.

The soft warmth before him felt more reliable and reassuring than ever before.

The two remained silent.

No words were needed; their long-standing rapport and spiritual connection meant that a single glance or gesture was enough to understand what the other was thinking.

After a long while, Yue Mingkong lifted her delicate head from his embrace and gazed at Gu Changge’s handsome, refined features, mere inches away.

Her exquisite, otherworldly beauty, like a painting, was filled with tenderness and love.

At this moment, she was no longer the stern and cold Empress who looked down upon the world; she seemed merely an ordinary wife at home, warming the food and wine, mending robes and cotton shoes, and waiting for her husband’s return.

Gu Changge opened his eyes, gazed at the divine face before him, and smiled faintly.

A faint blush spread across Yue Mingkong’s flawless, porcelain face. Her long eyelashes trembled slightly as she suddenly leaned in, wrapping her fair, slender arms around his neck.

Gu Changge lowered his head and responded to her.

“Let’s return to the bedchamber…” Yue Mingkong’s gaze was dreamy, her voice faint.

A ripple of spatial energy flashed, and the two vanished from where they stood.

Several days later, the clouds had cleared and the rain had ceased, and the spring breeze had gradually died down.

Within the palace perched atop the World Tree.

Mystical mist swirled, and curtains fluttered, creating a scene reminiscent of a fairyland.

On the celestial bed, Yue Mingkong wore a sheer, mist-like veil casually draped over her shoulders as she leaned lightly against the headboard. Gu Changge, his eyes half-closed, rested gently against her legs.

Yue Mingkong gently massaged his brow as he listened to Gu Changge recount the many events that had unfolded in the Vast Wasteland during this time.

Gu Changge’s voice was warm and calm. Events that could have shaken the entire Cangmang and sent countless orthodox factions into a state of terror and dread were, in his narration, described in a matter-of-fact manner, as if they were of little consequence.

Yue Mingkong knew his capabilities, so she wasn’t worried about his many actions. Their conversation seemed as casual as small talk over tea or a meal.

Of course, for Gu Changge, these matters were indeed nothing worth dwelling on, but to put Yue Mingkong’s mind at ease, he recounted them to her one by one.

However, there were still many far-reaching and complex matters he did not discuss; he merely mentioned how he had established the Heaven Slaying Alliance in the Vast Wastes and continued to expand it.

Yue Mingkong knew that Gu Changge had once served as the Demon Lord, but she was unaware of his experiences prior to that.

Therefore, she remained somewhat puzzled and perplexed by Gu Changge’s establishment of the Heaven Slaying Alliance—an endeavor that seemed to genuinely aim to overthrow the heavens.

Nevertheless, she would support any decision Gu Changge made and would always stand by his side.

As for the events that had transpired during his seclusion—including the doubts that had been weighing on her mind—Yue Mingkong had shared them all with Gu Changge over the past few days.

Although the "Great Dream Return to Immortality" technique was her own creation, as her cultivation deepened, she gradually realized that this technique was not as simple as she had initially thought; rather, it had always existed within her memories.

She didn’t know why she possessed this technique, or why, during her cultivation, she would return to the past, journeying through the river of time.

In her dreams, she seemed to transform into a river—naive and ignorant, guided solely by instinct. Born from a void of chaos, she followed her instincts, flowing through countless realms: lands of chaos and slaughter, peaceful utopias, ancient battlefields, and the murky, unknown ages of the past.

Later, she settled in a place whose name she did not know, ceasing to flow and gathering into a vast expanse of water.

As her consciousness gradually sharpened, she heard people call her the Dream Marsh.

Her branches spread throughout the vast cosmos, gathering in every corner of time and space, flowing silently through the dreams of every living being.

She, however, remained an observer, comprehending the joys, sorrows, fears, and loves of all beings, as well as their grudges and passions.

She did not know how long this process had lasted, until one day, she seemed to faintly hear a voice. That voice was obscure and profound, ancient and timeless, as if reciting some ancient scripture whose name she could not fathom, yet also as if narrating a story.

When she came to, she saw only an ancient, unadorned scroll lying in the center of the Dream Marsh.

Thus, guided by the teachings within that scroll, she began a naive, subconscious spiritual practice.

"My memories of what followed are rather hazy, but the one thing I do recall is that during that time, heaven and earth seemed undivided, the world was a vast chaos, and the myriad races of later ages had not yet come into being..." Yue Mingkong said softly.

When she regained consciousness and came to her senses, she had already returned to the present day.

And her cultivation had, without a sound, broken through to the Dao Realm—a breakthrough so subtle that even she herself had not noticed it in the slightest.

This process left her bewildered and confused, and at the same time, it filled her with worry—an inexplicable sense of unease and fear.

"This is likely just your destiny; there’s no need to worry too much," Gu Changge said as he sat up, gently taking her delicate hand in his, his voice gentle.

That said, he could still sense the faint tremor in Yue Mingkong’s voice as she had just recounted all this.

She appeared strong, but deep down she was terrified, anxious about this unknown situation.

As her husband, the only thing he needed to do now was to put her at ease and help her overcome this fear of the unknown.

"I’m so worried that when I wake up, I’ll become a stranger to myself—no longer who I am..." Yue Mingkong gently rested her head against his chest.

"That won’t happen. No one will take you away from me. No matter what you become, I will find you and bring you back," Gu Changge whispered.

Judging from what Yue Mingkong had said, and the anomalies he’d detected while investigating the Dream Ashes Era,

In an era before the vast expanse had come into being and the heavens and earth had been cleaved apart, there were already those laying plans and scheming. It was also possible that from a past spacetime, they had glimpsed some uncertain causality and connections, which is why they intervened in that era, attempting to alter something.

In the Realm of Truth, each of the Three Ancestors possessed such power. Not only could they interfere with past eras, but rewriting the entire ancient history, refining the Chaos, reopening the heavens and earth, and making everything start anew would be a trivial matter.

The only thing Gu Changge could be certain of now was that the other party had likely come from a future timeline, where they had detected his schemes and ambitions, and thus turned the tables on him, devising corresponding plans within the timeline Gu Changge had chosen.

Whether it was Yue Mingkong, Gu Xian’er, or the many beautiful wives within the Divine Realm, they all represented his vulnerabilities and weaknesses on this particular timeline.

One could say this would be his only vulnerability in the future.

Of course, the opponent’s scheme was also quite subtle, as it was based solely on a single observed timeline and a single possibility. What if Gu Changge didn’t choose this timeline? Or, upon sensing something amiss, what if he decided to overturn the table, directly erasing this timeline to restore everything to its original state and start over?

No one can be certain of future changes or outcomes, especially at their level of power.

Consequently, these subtle arrangements were actually very difficult to detect.

The reason Gu Changge speculated this way was that he sensed a faint danger lurking in the shadows; perhaps the future would indeed bring unforeseen variables, and he might fall.

The Three Primordial Ancestors cannot foresee or fathom one another, but they can perceive the timelines pertaining to themselves.

Even if countless prodigies and extraordinary beings were to emerge from the vast expanse—beings of unparalleled power, qualified to touch the Realm of Truth—in the eyes of the Three Primordial Ancestors, they would still be nothing more than insignificant ants.

When they chose to observe and project, no fate of any person or living being in this world could escape their gaze.

Gu Changge was certain that his current arrangements and plans were flawless, revealing no weaknesses whatsoever.

Even if the other two Primordial Ancestors were to awaken and cast their gaze down from the Realm of Truth, they would find nothing amiss in his current identity. For one thing, his present identity is a variable he has personally crafted, shrouded in mist; even if the Ancestors were to dispel that mist, the future they would see is one Gu Changge has already fabricated.

Unless, of course, they had the time to keep a constant watch on this anomaly, observing every step he took and comparing that portion of the future.

Of course, Gu Changge would never allow them to do such a thing.

“Do you remember that voice reciting the sutras?” Gu Changge snapped back to the present and asked Yue Mingkong.

Yue Mingkong furrowed his brow, trying to recall, then shook his head. “I don’t remember that voice very clearly, but my only impression is that it seemed to be a shadow shrouded in a hazy, clear light—it looked somewhat like a woman…”

"A woman?"

Gu Changge’s gaze flickered slightly. She asked no further questions, but gently stroked her back and said, “I’ve left a mark on you, so you needn’t worry. Should anything happen to you, or if there’s any abnormality in your soul light, I’ll sense it immediately. No matter which realm of time and space you’re in, or which era, I’ll rush to your side at once…”

Although, in Yue Mingkong’s account, that memory did not seem to belong to her—it might have belonged to someone else—Gu Changge knew full well that her soul light had remained unchanged from the very beginning.

She had always been herself; she had never changed.

On the contrary, during this process, her power was indeed growing stronger—and it was growing in a way that felt like a return. It was as if those energies had once been scattered and drifting throughout the heavens and earth, but as she secluded herself in meditation, they found their source once more and began flowing back into her body along the river of time.

"Returning to Immortality Through the Great Dream" is, in truth, more like retracing one’s past footsteps within a dream, returning to the Primordial Era to reclaim one’s own power.

Setting aside that scripture and that voice, Yue Mingkong’s origins should be traced back to the Primordial Era.

"As long as you’re here, I won’t be afraid," Yue Mingkong whispered.

She knew that after waking this time, she would soon retreat into seclusion once more, and she had no idea how long she would slumber during that period.

Gu Changge knew this as well, which was why he had made a special trip back from the Vast Expanse.

"The Prosperous Dao True Realm should be bordering the Vast Expanse by now..."

"In my spare time, I’ve carved out a realm within the courtyard where I usually rest. When the time comes, I’ll take the entire Divine Realm with me. That way, I’ll always be by your side while you’re in seclusion," Gu Changge said.

This was a plan he had long since devised. Although the Prosperous Dao True Realm was a newly formed true realm, with foundations and power far inferior to those ancient true realms—or even the most powerful true realms—it possessed a natural advantage: it once harbored a spark of immortal Civilization.

However, this no longer mattered to Gu Changge.

He simply wanted Yue Mingkong to stay by his side, so she could feel at ease.

"I’ll do whatever you say," Yue Mingkong said quietly as she leaned against him, a reassuring and charming smile on her face.

Suddenly, as if a thought occurred to her, she turned her head and looked up at Gu Changge, a hint of mischief playing at the corners of her mouth.

"Changge, now that I’ve reached the Dao Realm, do you think we could have a child…?"

Gu Changge was slightly taken aback, a bit surprised—he hadn’t expected Yue Mingkong to say that out of the blue.

Children…

He hadn’t actually given it any thought at all; the idea hadn’t even crossed his mind.

Seeing that Gu Changge fell silent and didn’t respond, Yue Mingkong wasn’t surprised. She smiled and said, “I was just saying that offhand. My body is in a special state now; even if I were to conceive, I might not have the time to carry the child to term.”

Gu Changge understood what she meant and sighed softly to himself.

Yue Mingkong continued in a soft voice, “But what about Yin Mei and Chu Chu…”

Gu Changge shook his head, cutting her off, and said softly, “Let’s discuss this matter later. The situation is turbulent and uncertain right now; I don’t want my children to grow up in such a chaotic and unstable era.”

Yue Mingkong nodded and said nothing more.

Then, as if sensing something, she smiled faintly and said, “You’ve been with me these past few days; Chu Chu and the others must be waiting anxiously.”

Outside the Divine Realm, news of Gu Changge’s return to the Prosperous Dao True Realm had already spread far and wide. However, finding it a bother this time, he did not summon anyone to pay their respects, so as not to disturb the tranquility he shared with his beloved companions.

Jiang Chuchu, Wang Zijing, Yin Mei, and the others had all arrived. The hall was filled with the sweet chatter of women, a lively scene.

Beyond the rear courtyard lay a vast, crystal-clear immortal pond, flanked by towering immortal mountains and ancient peaks. For the past few days, Gu Changge had been spending his time just like an ordinary mortal, accompanying them as they rowed boats across the lake.

Yue Mingkong even took to the kitchen herself, her hair pulled high into a bun, sleeves rolled up, preparing a feast of delicacies. At night, the campfire flickered, casting a glow over the water and the sky—a sight of unparalleled beauty. Cup after cup of Immortal Emperor’s Wine went down their throats, and before long, the women were utterly drunk, stumbling and swaying.

Gu Changge had no choice but to escort them one by one to rest.

But when she reached Wang Zijing, the girl suddenly sobered up. She sat there with a radiant smile, her eyes sparkling like stars. She hadn’t drunk much at all—she had merely been pretending to be drunk.

It was another sleepless night.
