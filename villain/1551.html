At that time, the Righteous Unity Alliance had not yet collapsed, and the Dark Clans had emerged, causing widespread panic and chaos across the Vast Expanse.

Relying on Gu Changge’s protection, Wang Zijing was completely unafraid and traveled to many places.

Of course, her own cultivation was not particularly strong; when she was traveling through the Vast Expanse, she hadn’t even reached the Dao Realm yet—she only broke through to that level after the Righteous Unity Alliance had disintegrated.

Consequently, she encountered many dangers during that time, but fortunately, thanks to the protective barrier left behind by Gu Changge, she emerged unscathed.

Later, as the Righteous Unity Alliance disintegrated and the Dark Clan wreaked havoc across the land, even the Nine Heavens emerged into the world. The Heavenly Avatar broke its seal and descended, sparking widespread chaos.

She paused to stay in a city, and to break through to the Dao Realm, she had been in seclusion for some time.

When she emerged from seclusion, the entire Vast Expanse had descended into chaos; even the Heaven Slaying Alliance had erupted into a major war with the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

The original Nine Heavens no longer existed, and the indigenous peoples had set out on a journey of migration, seeking new places to settle.

It was precisely after that period that the sense of that call grew ever stronger. During her seclusion, Wang Zijing would have a strange dream.

In that dream, a vague, shadowy figure stood with its back to her, seemingly walking into the light.

But she had no idea who that vague, shadowy figure was; she didn’t even know what the other person looked like.

She simply had a feeling that there was some kind of connection between them.

Later, as the dream grew clearer and the sense of calling from the depths of her being became more distinct, she arrived at the site she had sought in the Nine Heavens.

It was on that very day that a faint portal of light appeared before her.

Without a moment’s hesitation, Wang Zijing stepped through it and found herself in this lakeside world, a veritable paradise.

"Who are you, exactly? And why have you left me here?"

As her thoughts returned, Wang Zijing’s gaze fell upon the scene of the great battle on the front lines of the Ancient Hidden Civilization, reflected before her.

Everything that happened there was reflected here from start to finish.

The mysterious woman before her, who looked exactly like her, possessed unfathomable abilities. With a mere wave of her hand, a realm separated by billions of light-years and layers of spacetime appeared before her as if it were a projection.

Such a feat could likely only be achieved by a so-called True Path-level being.

During her travels through the Vast Expanse, Wang Zijing had gained a clear understanding of the realms beyond the Dao Realm, as well as the specific abilities associated with each level.

"I told you, I wouldn’t harm you, but it wasn’t me who kept you here."

“It’s because you came looking for me.”

“If you’re worried about the Heaven Slaying Alliance, there’s really no need.”

The woman reclining on the soft couch gave a lazy smile. Though she looked exactly like Wang Zijing—their features indistinguishable—every expression and gesture carried a distinct charm.

This made it easy to tell them apart.

Except when in the presence of Gu Changge, Jiang Chuchu, and others, Wang Zijing remained reserved and taciturn, rarely adopting such a languid demeanor.

Of course, for the woman before him, this languid demeanor was not a deliberate act, but something that emanated naturally from her.

It was as if nothing in this world could stir much of an emotional ripple within her, so no matter the situation, she maintained a carefree, indifferent demeanor.

"Gu Changge is sharp as a tack; I’m not worried at all."

Wang Zijing pursed her lips slightly. Having stayed here for some time, she hadn’t sensed any ill will from the mysterious woman before her.

Apart from her initial surprise, she gradually relaxed, though strange memories would occasionally surface in her mind—as if, through certain perspectives or experiences, she had personally lived through some of the events the woman before her had faced.

However, she hadn’t mentioned any of this to the mysterious woman before her.

"Yes, she’s quite the sharp one..."

The mysterious woman smiled faintly, her eyes crinkling with amusement. She then set down her palm-leaf fan, leaned forward slightly, and poured a cup of tea from the red clay stove for Wang Zijing.

Wang Zijing glanced at her and snorted lightly, “You make it sound as if you know him very well.”

“Just as you can sense me, I can naturally sense you. How do you know I am not you? What you have experienced, have I not also experienced? You know him—how could I not know him?” The mysterious woman curved her lips into an inscrutable smile.

A sudden chill ran down Wang Zijing’s spine. Wasn’t she implying that she had always known her secrets—including her experiences and everything else?

“You could say that we actually share everything. I can sense your memories, emotions, feelings, and thoughts.”

“What if I told you that your birth in that Shanhai True Realm—no, it should be called the Prosperous Dao True Realm now—was actually my doing?”

“That you met Gu Changge and became his woman—was that my doing?” The mysterious woman’s smile grew even more amused.

"……" Wang Zijing suddenly fell silent. This feeling of being controlled made her extremely uncomfortable and left her with a sense of powerlessness.

"Of course, there’s no need to be upset. In truth, you are me, and I am you—you just don’t understand that yet. When the day comes that you remember, you’ll accept it." The mysterious woman sat up slightly before leaning back again. She gently swirled her teacup, brought her lips close to it, and began sipping it in small sips.

However, her gaze remained fixed on the great battle unfolding at the front lines of the Ancient Hidden Civilization, still intently focused on the events there.

"Come to think of it, the contingency plan he left behind should be taking effect by now. It’s time for this farce to end…"

the mysterious woman murmured softly.

……

At the same time, in the Vast Expanse where no living being could ever set foot.

Here, there was no law, no restraint, no rules, no principles, no traces, and no cause and effect. It was like a vast, profound realm beyond the primordial, existing neither in the past nor the present, and unnoticed by any being.

To be precise, the concept of scale no longer applied here; it was impossible to know where its boundaries lay.

Even Chaos itself could not fill this void.

In the Vast Expanse, it is simply impossible for any living being to reach this place.

Even a being like the Heavenly Lord of Absolute Darkness, who possesses the Heavenly Seal, would feel oppressed and terrified here, as if constantly watched by some indescribable, unfathomable gaze.

"Interesting..."

"So the subtle sensation I sensed back then originated from Wang Zijing. After waiting this long, a faint clue has finally emerged."

"It was well worth the wait."

Gu Changge, clad in a luxurious, heavy black robe, stood with his hands behind his back, observing the shifting tides of the Vast Expanse.

His eyebrows were slightly raised, yet his eyes remained deep and serene, as if he had foreseen this all along.

Whether it was the stroke of Heaven’s Will that appeared the moment he first arrived on this side of the Vast Expanse—a stroke that severed the very concept of Heaven Slaying—or the outflow that transcended the Master Plan, both pointed to a single truth: a force from beyond the Vast Expanse was interfering with and influencing this side.

There was no other explanation besides the machinations of the Land Of Truth.

Ever since that time, Gu Changge had been waiting for the other party to slip up. After all, such methods would require the ability to influence the Heavenly Will of the Heavenly Dao itself—yet it was highly unlikely that such power existed within the Vast Realm.

If he were to employ True Ancestor-level methods to penetrate and deduce the truth, it would only alert that unfathomable force, and the inevitable result would be the collapse of his entire plan, bringing it to a premature end.

The collision of the two forces would overturn the table, plunging the Vast Expanse into chaos, and everything would start anew.

This was clearly not the outcome Gu Changge desired.

After all, he was merely playing the role of an unexpected “variable.” The power of this variable must not be strong enough to arouse the attention of that force; thus, he continued to follow the trajectory of destiny as he could see it.

Of course, in the eyes of that force, everything Gu Changge did did indeed align with the future and destiny it perceived—everything proceeded by the book, devoid of any hidden intent.

Including the current state of affairs across the Vast Expanse.

Gu Changge waited for the other party to reveal the slightest clue, and now, just as he had anticipated, everything was slowly coming to light.

The blueprints for the First Civilization’s Treasure had indeed passed through the other party’s hands, circulating outward to forge the Civilization’s Treasure—a weapon designed to suppress his “Lord of Heaven” persona.

Fully aware of this, Gu Changge naturally abandoned the identity of the “Lord of Heaven” and appeared in the newly born Mountains And Seas True Realm as the Demon Lord.

It could be said that all of this was unfolding exactly as the other party had envisioned—as a “pawn” or, perhaps, a “tool.”

This included the fact that the other party had long known he would establish the Heaven Slaying Alliance, so they had long ago eradicated the taboo concept of “Heaven Slaying” from the minds of all beings, ensuring that when the Heaven Slaying Alliance was first established, there was far less resistance.

As for the circulation of the "Transcendence Compendium," in Gu Changge’s view, it was merely a bait designed to lure out the big fish among the Vast Expanse of worlds.

Of course, the "Transcendence Compendium" itself was a forgery.

For a seasoned angler like Gu Changge, such tactics were nothing short of child’s play. The initial release of the “Transcendence Compendium” merely sparked a scramble among a few Ancestral Dao Realm beings. Even the Eternal Primordial God, despite seizing it, failed to break through to the End of the Path tier. It was only through feigning death and gradual accumulation that he eventually penetrated that realm.

A truly wise person might not even bother competing for that so-called "General Framework of Transcendence."

This, in fact, aroused Gu Changge’s suspicion.

"Hmph..."

"Well then, I’ll see just how long you, this old fisherman, can hold your ground." Gu Changge withdrew his gaze from surveying the Vast Expanse and pressed his palm forward.

Instantly, waves of energy—transcending law, the Dao, and all conceptual boundaries—surged through the air, as if infinite supreme Daos were colliding.

The so-called authority of the Heavenly Dao here felt like a colossal, raging tempest.

In the deepest recesses of the boundless expanse of time and space, a terrifying will stood in opposition to him.

From afar, it resembled a glowing golden cocoon, radiant and dazzling. Its brilliance was as resplendent as the sun, radiating to every corner. At its edges, one could discern thread-like tendrils—like tentacles—piercing through the void and extending into the deeper realms of spacetime.

Each of these tendrils seemed capable of connecting to a separate super-multiverse. Countless tendrils spread out from this region, stretching endlessly into the void—so numerous they were impossible to count.

Fate, causality, time, and space—any concept or substance—seemed as fleeting as bubbles in a stream before these tendrils, evaporating at the slightest touch.

Within that golden cocoon, a succession of shifting faces contort and manifest, while the resonant echoes of celestial spirits and countless races—endless reflections of the cosmos—hum and reverberate.

This is, without a doubt, the true Heavenly Will governing the Vast Expanse of the present age.

Compared to the mere tip of the iceberg manifested when Gu Changge first arrived, the Heavenly Will at this moment could be said to have fully revived.

Even the slightest fluctuation of energy in this place is enough to annihilate the very essence and form of a True Path-level being.

Even a Heavenly Lord like the Supreme Yin Heavenly Lord, possessing the power of four Chaos Heaven and Earth Epochs, would be crushed to dust.
