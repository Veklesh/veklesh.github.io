Above the heavens, beyond all worlds, that mysterious and unknown realm of darkness is slowly drawing near to the vast expanse.

The barriers and boundaries between the realms now seem to have become transparent, like two distinct rivers flowing at a steady pace, awaiting the moment they converge.

There, a dense, mist-like darkness surges forth ceaselessly, spreading in all directions to permeate the myriad realms of the heavens.

This scene is utterly terrifying; even ordinary beings can see it clearly. It seems like an omen of a great cataclysm, or perhaps a countdown to death.

No one knows when that mysterious, unknown realm will collide with the vast, boundless worlds, nor how the surging dark matter will permeate and spread.

It was as if a sharp sword hung above the heads of all living beings across the multiverse. Everyone could feel that terrifying sense of urgency, a chill running down their spines as they awaited judgment.

Ancient warships crisscrossed the cosmos, speeding away into the distance. Ling Yuling, standing proudly atop one, looked up, her gaze unfocused as she watched the events unfolding in the sky. She remained silent, letting her long hair blow freely, brushing against her cheeks.

The high-ranking member of the Heaven Slaying Alliance who had been questioning her saw this and tactfully fell silent.

The others, who had been discussing and deliberating, also fell silent, and a profound stillness descended upon the ancient warship.

Everyone knew that Ling Yuling was a venerable figure who had once fought against the Black Calamity. Since she had played a part in sealing the source of that calamity, her efforts and contributions were undeniable.

Now, seeing that mysterious realm reappear in the world once more, her heart could hardly remain at peace.

However, compared to the panic and urgency felt by the various races across the realms, the members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance remained remarkably calm and composed, showing little fear or concern.

For in the eyes of many, with Alliance Master Gu Changge presiding over the Heaven Slaying Alliance, even if the Land of the Dark Source were to plunge into chaos and darkness were to engulf all realms, the Alliance would still possess the strength to defend itself.

“I will personally go and confront that mysterious white-robed powerhouse. You need not worry unduly,” Ling Yuling said, her voice clear and calm, breaking the silence aboard the ancient warship.

She withdrew her gaze from the expanse beyond the heavens and paid no further heed to that realm of the Dark Source.

"Since Senior Yu Ling has stepped in, we naturally have no need to worry any further," said the Heaven Slaying Alliance expert who had asked the question earlier. Hearing this, he exhaled in relief and bowed respectfully.

Ling Yuling’s strength was on par with those forbidden beings; having her personally step forward to test the true capabilities of that mysterious white-robed powerhouse was, of course, the best possible outcome.

The White Bone Ancestral King, Hun Yuanjun, Chu Xiao, and the others said no more. Although the Vast Realms were now plunged into panic, filled with boundless dread toward that mysterious and unknown source of darkness,

However, the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s primary target remained the Zhengyi Alliance.

The emergence of the Dark Source Realm merely instilled a sense of urgency in them; they must take down the Zhengyi Alliance before it’s too late, lest delays lead to unforeseen complications.

"Why did that place appear so early..."

“Has the power of the seal weakened? Or was it summoned by dark beings? Or is there some other reason?” Ling Yuling’s figure flickered and vanished from the ancient warship. Her dark brows furrowed tightly as a sense of foreboding welled up within her.

She had participated in that sealing battle herself and knew that the seal on the Source of Darkness would not weaken so easily, much less emerge so prematurely.

Could some unforeseen variable have arisen? Or had a dark entity discovered its spatiotemporal coordinates and destroyed the seal prematurely?

But regardless of the possibility, the premature emergence of the Source of Darkness was now an inevitable outcome.

The only thing she could do now was to find a way to prevent it from colliding with the vast realms of the world, and above all, to ensure that the Ruler of Heaven sealed within it did not emerge.

Once the Ruler of Heaven awakens and reigns over the world once more, who knows what terrifying calamities it will bring.

All realms and worlds will be engulfed by boundless darkness.

"Alliance Leader..."

Ling Yuling furrowed her delicate brows, pondering for a moment. A flash of light gleamed from her slender, fair hands, and a crystal-clear jade communication talisman appeared.

She intended to contact Gu Changge to inform him of the urgency of the matter and its dire consequences.

At this critical juncture, continuing to confront the Zhengyi Alliance was not a wise choice; it would likely allow the dark beings to reap the benefits and profit from the situation.

Both the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Zhengyi Alliance should put aside their past grievances, set aside their differences, and join forces to stop this impending disaster.

Soon, the runes on the jade talisman flickered, and a faint image appeared. Gu Changge stood with his back to Ling Yuling, seemingly brewing tea, as relaxed as ever.

Ling Yuling cut straight to the chase, voicing her concerns and thoughts without any preamble.

“You need not worry about this. The Zhengyi Alliance has colluded with the Dark Beings, acting in concert with them. To prevent this impending catastrophe, we must first deal with the Zhengyi Alliance. Everything is under my control.”

Gu Changge’s voice remained steady and unhurried, as if he didn’t care in the least about the impending catastrophe facing the vast, boundless realms.

Ling Yuling gazed at his silhouette within the jade talisman. She parted her red lips, as if to speak, but the words died on her tongue.

Since Gu Changge had spoken so decisively, what more was there for her to say?

Although she was nominally the Deputy Leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, every order and instruction required Gu Changge’s approval.

If he refused to reconcile with the Zhengyi Alliance and join forces to prevent the Dark Source from drawing near, then no amount of pleading on her part would make a difference.

No one could sway Gu Changge’s decision.

"As for that mysterious white-robed powerhouse who’s appeared in the Zhengyi Alliance recently, it would be best to capture him alive. You might not be able to take him down on your own, so convey my instructions to Mu Fuguang, Tong Xian, and the others—have them assist you." Gu Changge’s calm voice came through the jade talisman once more.

Ling Yuling was taken aback. Did Gu Changge already know about this? It seemed that although he never left

, nothing in the vast expanse of the multiverse could still be hidden from him.

But why was he so certain that she alone could not defeat that mysterious white-robed powerhouse?

And what was the background of that mysterious white-robed powerhouse?

Ling Yuling was still filled with doubts, but the glow on the jade talisman before her had already faded, and Gu Changge’s figure had vanished.

Neither the Heaven Slaying Alliance nor the Zhengyi Alliance had ceased their fierce battles and slaughter simply because the Source of Darkness had reappeared in the world; the war raged on with undiminished ferocity.

The vast and uncertain situation grew increasingly urgent, and all the various races, sects, and civilizations were desperately seeking a way to respond.

Amidst this turmoil, many reclusive clans that had previously remained silent and hidden from the world could no longer sit idly by.

To halt this calamity, numerous ancient figures emerged into the world, visiting the various races to try to dissuade the Great War between the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance, shifting their focus to preventing the Dark Source from drawing near.

Even those traditional civilizations that had previously adopted a stance of watching from the sidelines—keeping their hands off and looking out only for themselves—in the face of the reappearance of dark beings in the world, have been forced to grit their teeth and step into the fray.

The dark beings of today were already troublesome enough; if they were allowed to lead others back to the Source of Darkness and awaken the Lord of Heaven sealed within, the consequences were simply too terrifying to imagine.

It must be said that the sudden reappearance of the Source of Darkness has instilled a terrifying sense of urgency in all the vast civilizations of the various races.

Previously, the various races and lineages had each looked out only for their own interests, ignoring the greater picture; but now they were forced to “unite.”

Many civilizations have begun searching through the manuscripts left behind by the sages who once fought against the Black Calamity, hoping to find a way to counter the Land of the Dark Source and prevent its approach.

If the Lord of Heaven truly breaks the seal, revives once more, and reigns over all worlds, how will they resist? Will they simply replicate the methods of those ancient sages?

Where are the six treasures of the first Civilization now?

Meanwhile, during this time, numerous orthodox factions have begun to direct their attention toward the dark entities, believing that it is their reappearance that will draw the source of darkness back to this realm.

They must be utterly annihilated before the Dark Beings allow the Ruler of Heaven to awaken.

While the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Alliance of Righteous Unity engaged in a fierce battle, a series of punitive campaigns erupted across the various cosmic realms now occupied by the Dark Beings.

Many territories held by the Dark Spirits were under attack, as powerful figures from various races led massive armies to drive out the Dark Spirits and purge the darkness.

"Human nature is truly laughable. Yet at this critical juncture, the Source of Darkness has suddenly emerged, throwing the entire cosmos into utter chaos. Battles rage across the heavens, and karmic retribution knows no bounds—could this be yet another of your doing…?"

"I’m growing increasingly suspicious of your true identity."

In the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, Ji Hanjing’s lips curled into a faint, contemptuous smile, yet his gaze remained profoundly deep.

When true disaster looms, all factions and clans begin to act without regard for the consequences—they’ve gone somewhat mad.

Previously, when tasked with confronting the dark beings, they would deliberate and weigh the pros and cons time and again. But now, with the safety of the entire Vast Expanse at stake—and their own survival on the line—the blade hangs directly over their heads, and they can no longer sit idly by.

This is the true nature of humanity.

However, none of this had anything to do with her. No matter how chaotic Cangmang became, or what turmoil lay ahead, it was none of her concern.

All she wanted in this lifetime was to slay her enemies with her own hands, to avenge all the pain and torment she had endured over countless ages.

Snapping back to reality, Ji Hanjing’s gaze swept downward. Her tone was cold and unruffled, radiating boundless authority.

“So you’re saying that Emperor Wei Yang, Jiang Yun, once ventured alone into the depths of time and space, disappeared for a long period, and seemed somewhat off ever since his return?”

An absolute silence hung over the grand hall, broken only by a tall, imposing man standing there with his head bowed in reverence, cold sweat beading on his forehead.

He was none other than Zhu Cangning, the Divine Martial General of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty—the Emperor’s right-hand man, who had fought alongside him in every direction to establish the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, earning countless military merits.

"Y-yes... yes... Ancestor..."

Zhu Cangning didn’t dare lift his head, his back drenched in cold sweat. He chose his words carefully, afraid to conceal anything.

He had no idea how this Ancestor had come to know of this matter; before the message was delivered, he had never imagined he would be questioned about such a thing.

After all, the fact that the Emperor of Weiyang had once ventured deep into the depths of time and space and suffered severe injuries was known to only a handful of people throughout the entire Weiyang Immortal Dynasty—not even Princess Weiyang was aware of it.

But apart from him, who else could have leaked this information?

The Emperor of Weiyang had explicitly ordered that none of them were to disclose this to outsiders. Who would dare defy the Emperor of Weiyang’s command?

Could it have been Grand Master Chi of Xingming who leaked it?

“So it really happened back then?”

While Zhu Cangning was deeply perplexed, his thoughts in disarray, Ji Hanjing spoke as if to himself, his eyes growing ever more deep and icy.

These words first startled Zhu Cangning, then shocked him, and a look of disbelief spread across his face.

He had already guessed the meaning behind the ancient ancestor’s words; after all, he had harbored suspicions and doubts before.

But he dared not dwell on that possibility, for it was too horrifying and terrifying. If even the slightest hint of this were to leak out, it would undoubtedly trigger a massive upheaval in the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, stirring up boundless turmoil and chaos.

He considered himself to know Emperor Jiang Yun of Weiyang intimately, having accompanied him through campaigns across the Three Thousand Immortal Mountains, the Eight Hundred Demon Lands, and the Thirty-Three Realms of Purgatory, subduing every major sect and clan along the way.

Therefore, he believed that a wise and martial figure like Emperor Jiang Yun of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty would never agree to marry off his daughter, Jiang Weiyang—whom he regarded as his most cherished treasure—to Ye Wudao, the Ninth Prince of the ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao.

Nor would Emperor Jiang Yun of Weiyang ever make the decision to select a husband for Princess Weiyang.

All of this either concealed some hidden truth or indicated that Emperor Jiang Yun had a separate plan; however, judging by his subsequent series of actions, it did not appear as though he had planned this in advance.

This series of actions left Zhu Cangning completely baffled. Even the Chancellor of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and others could not fathom or decipher the Emperor’s intentions.

"Ancestor, are you suggesting that at that time, His Majesty the Emperor had already been tainted by dark matter?"

Although he suspected as much, Zhu Cangning still couldn’t be certain, and couldn’t help but ask cautiously.

Ji Hanjing seemed to have snapped out of his reverie at that moment. He gave him a calm glance and said, “It seems you’re not entirely foolish after all; you’ve managed to figure it out.”

Receiving Ji Hanjing’s definitive answer, Zhu Cangning felt both a heavy weight in his heart and a sense of relief.

He had not wanted to see the once wise and virtuous Emperor of Weiyang undergo such a drastic change in temperament; knowing that he had merely been infected by the dark substance finally brought him some relief.

But what followed was boundless worry.

After all, throughout history, every being infected by the Dark Substance has inevitably fallen into darkness, becoming a creature of the shadows. No one has ever managed to purge the Dark Substance and return to their former self.

Not even an existence as vast and mighty as the Emperor of Weiyang, who had towered over all ages past and present, was an exception. Once tainted by the dark substance, his nature began to change drastically.

"Ancestor, is there any way to resolve this?" Zhu Cangning asked cautiously.
