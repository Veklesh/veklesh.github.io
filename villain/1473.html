"The Third Princess has returned..."

"That aura just now was definitely the Third Princess."

"Wonderful..."

This is the ancestral land of the Ji Chan clan, though now, only a single ancient tree—so vast it obscures the sky—stands here. Yet this ancient tree has been severed by some immense and terrifying force, leaving only half of it remaining.

That half of the tree still appeared vast and boundless, its edges completely invisible. Every branch and leaf was incredibly lush and soared to the heavens, drawing in endless chaotic energy and starlight. Its sprawling roots alone stretched across countless realms of time and space.

In the palaces scattered throughout the tree’s canopy, members of the Ji Chan clan, sensing the approaching presence, all wore expressions of surprise and delight.

In the void, ripples spread out, and within the ancient palaces hidden within them, figures one by one opened their eyes.

"A-Zhen..."

"I thought you would never return to the Ji Chan clan in this lifetime."

At that moment, within the grand hall, upon hearing these words, the woman in colorful robes sitting cross-legged there finally opened her eyes slowly.

Her dreamlike, glass-like eyes seemed to reflect the myriad scenes of the heavens.

Her voice was enchanting, like the celestial music of the heavens, enough to make any man lose himself in its spell, unwilling to awaken.

"This is the place where I was born and raised; how could I possibly not return here?"

“Our clan has dwindled considerably over the years.”

The complex expression on the woman in black’s face softened slightly.

The journey back to the Ancient Hidden Civilization from the Heaven Slaying Alliance had been smooth. Aside from crossing the Vast Expanse—where they had to avoid a few dangerous areas—it hadn’t taken much time.

Moreover, with the Heaven Slaying Alliance now on the verge of unifying the realm, aside from the Dark Clans invading and seizing the Nine Heavens, the various territories across the Vast Expanse were actually quite stable, with no major wars or bloodshed breaking out.

Given the turbulent and urgent situation across the entire Vast Expanse, this could be considered a rare period of peace.

During her time in the Ancient Hidden Civilization, she witnessed many ancient warships, shrouded in rosy light and the aura of chaos, being led by Dao Realm beings as they traversed the Vast Expanse, attempting to migrate toward the territories of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

But the Vast Expanse held countless indescribable dangers. She witnessed with her own eyes an ancient True Realm being swallowed whole by an unknown, colossal beast from the void during its migration, causing billions of lives to perish in an instant.

For the Ji Chan clan to migrate to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, it sounds simple, but in reality, there are still many unknown dangers.

She wasn’t even certain she could persuade her second elder sister to take the entire Ji Chan clan with her.

Not to mention the elders—stubborn and obstinate, they refused to face the truth despite knowing the Ji Chan clan’s fate, insisting on remaining in the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

The woman in the colorful robe smiled gently and looked at her, saying, “The last time I saw you leave with such determination, I thought you would never set foot in the Ancient Hidden Civilization again in this lifetime.”

“Seeing that you are safe and sound, I am relieved.”

In this generation of the Ji Chan clan, there were nine sisters, each named after one of the nine colors. The third-born, the woman in black, was named Ji Qi Yi.

The woman in the colorful robe, the second-born, was known as Ji Xianmiao.

“I’m sorry for worrying you, Second Sister.”

Ji Qiyi looked guilty, then, as if remembering something, asked, “On my way back to our ancestral lands, I noticed our numbers have dwindled significantly. What’s going on?”

As a clan of the Ancient Hidden Civilization that drew its strength from the Mother Tree of the Era, the Ji Chan clan held a uniquely special status, far surpassing that of ordinary clans or factions.

Even the major royal clans treated the Ji Chan clan with great courtesy.

However, upon returning to the clan’s territory, she could clearly sense a gloomy and desolate atmosphere, and the number of clan members had dwindled significantly—even though she had only been away from the Ji Chan clan for a few thousand years.

For those of profound cultivation, this was merely the blink of an eye.

Why had such drastic changes occurred in such a short time?

Could it be that the High Priest of the Temple of Sacrifice was still suppressing the Ji Chan clan because of that incident from long ago?

Upon hearing this, Ji Xianmiao seemed somewhat helpless as well. She shook her head and said, “The Imperial House of Guzang is mobilizing forces for some unknown purpose. All tribes of the Ancient Hidden Civilization have been ordered to send their elite warriors, and even our Ji Chan clan is included in the mobilization list…”

“Recruiting troops?”

Ji Ziyi froze, a chill spreading across her cold, striking features. “Could it be that the Gucang Imperial House intends to go to war with the Heaven Slaying Alliance? Is that why they’re gathering forces?”

Ji Xianmiao shook her head. “I don’t know the details either. Perhaps they intend to go to war, or perhaps they’re carrying out some kind of repair work… Little Five went to confront them to ask about the Ancient Zang Royal Family’s intentions, but he was injured by their men. He’s still recovering, so I can’t leave the clan territory, and the elders have advised us to be patient.”

"The Imperial House and the Five Great Royal Clans are all plotting their own agendas. The Ancient Hidden Civilization is no longer as peaceful as it once was."

"Especially since I have a premonition that the Great Calamity of this era is about to descend..."

"If we cannot survive the Eon’s Winter, my Ji Chan clan might well face annihilation."

Hearing this, Ji Qiyi’s expression grew even colder. After all, the Ji Chan clan had guarded the Epoch Mother Tree for generations and still held an irreplaceable status and role in the current Ancient Hidden Civilization. Yet the Gu Cang Imperial Family had actually gone so far as to injure Xiao Wu.

Did they really think the Ji Chan clan was easy to bully?

"What do Mother and the others at the ancestral court say?" Ji Ziyi asked.

“Mother has also urged us to be patient,” Ji Ximiao replied, still shaking her head, though a faint trace of helplessness flickered in her eyes.

Ji Ziyi clenched her delicate hands tightly. “Why must it be this way? Just because the Eon Mother Tree was severed, does that mean our Ji Chan clan must be branded as a criminal clan and forced to yield at every turn?”

"Why can’t the various tribes of the Ancient Hidden Civilization see the merits of our generations of guarding the Mother Tree of the Era? Are they truly going to negate all the merits of our clan simply because of a momentary mistake?"

Ji Xianmiao sighed and said nothing more, though a faint sadness flickered in her eyes.

"Qi Yi, you’ve always been a stubborn soul, but you must understand that often, the more stubborn one is, the more one ends up battered and bruised."

Ji Ziyi felt a wave of melancholy wash over her, followed by a sense of helplessness, and she said nothing more.

"Let’s not dwell on that. Ziyi, how have you been all these years out in the world?"

"I’ve heard plenty of rumors about the Heaven Slaying Alliance. Sometimes I wonder if you were too impulsive when you left back then, but I also wonder if the decision you made might actually have been the right one..."

“I’ve also had our clan keep an eye on the Heaven Slaying Alliance, to see if we can find any news of you,” Ji Xianmiao said with a smile.

Ji Ziyi hadn’t expected her second elder sister, who was usually so detached and serene, to care so deeply about her. Because of their long lifespans, the bonds between the sisters of the Ji Chan clan were generally much weaker than those in other clans.

However, though their bonds were thin, they were still family—blood is thicker than water—and they could not simply abandon their own kin.

“I’m doing quite well in the Heaven Slaying Alliance. It’s just that the Alliance is now teeming with experts, and I’m hardly anyone of note among them. Plus, since I’m a latecomer, I’ve had virtually no access to the higher-ups,” Ji Ziyi said, shaking her head.

“Even with cultivation at the Ancestral Dao Realm, you still can’t get close to the higher-ups?”

Ji Xianmiao was quite surprised; the Heaven Slaying Alliance was even more powerful than she had imagined.

Ji Ziyi, however, spoke frankly on the subject, her words tinged with a hint of admiration. “From what I know,” she said, “the Heaven Slaying Alliance boasts dozens of Ancestral Dao Realm cultivators. Among them, there are quite a few who have survived eight Celestial Waning Tribulations. Additionally, there are some supremely ancient figures, such as the Piao Miao Holy Ancestor from the Piao Miao Holy Realm, who is already very close to the Realm of the End of the Path…”…"

"Furthermore, the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance is accompanied by two mysterious figures who appear to have entered the Realm of the End of the Path. His own strength is unfathomable; he may well be a Supreme Shattered Being."

"The Realm of the End of the Path..."

Upon hearing this, Ji Xianmiao’s eyes flickered with a hint of longing, and she sighed softly, “If Big Sister hadn’t taken Little Nine’s place and been sacrificed to the other side back then, with her talent, she would likely have reached that realm by now…”

The eldest sister was the oldest among them, and also the most powerful in cultivation.

Ji Ziyi’s gaze dimmed slightly.

When their eldest sister was still alive, she treated them all with great kindness. Even when the Ji Chan clan encountered the Five Great Royal Clans, they had the confidence to face them calmly.

After Eldest Sister was sacrificed and departed, their mother’s vitality waned, and her strength was far from its peak; the Ji Chan clan’s status had also fallen far below what it once was.

"Second Sister, you must be nearing your ninth Celestial Waning Tribulation as well..."

Ji Qiyi observed Ji Xianmiao’s current cultivation level, though she could not see it clearly.

That mist-like, chaotic substance, resembling the wisdom of the Prajnaparamita, swirled around her second sister, blocking all prying eyes. She only felt her second sister was even more unfathomable.

"It should be soon," Ji Xianmiao said with a faint smile.

Ji Ziyi nodded. Although the two sisters appeared to be catching up and chatting, they both tacitly avoided mentioning the crucial point.

A long time passed.

Ji Ziyi sighed inwardly and decided to reveal the purpose of her return.

"Second Sister, the reason I’ve returned this time is actually to have the entire Ji Chan clan relocate away from the Ancient Hidden Civilization..."

Upon hearing this, Ji Xianmiao wasn’t the least bit surprised. She understood her third sister’s character well; returning at this time could not possibly be without reason.

She did not object immediately, but asked calmly, “Where would we relocate? To the Heaven Slaying Alliance?”

Ji Ziyi looked into her eyes, which shimmered like lapis lazuli, seemingly filled with a myriad of colors, and bowed her head. “Aside from the Heaven Slaying Alliance, there is nowhere else to relocate to now.”

Ji Xianmiao sighed and said, “But you should know this: my Ji Chan clan can go anywhere, yet we absolutely cannot go to the Heaven Slaying Alliance. You must know the reason as well.”

The Ji Chan clan exists because of the Eon Mother Tree, which is regarded as a gift from Heaven to the Vast Expanse of living beings.

If they were to migrate to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, it would, in essence, be an act of disrespect toward Heaven and a renunciation of the faith they have upheld for generations.

Ji Qiyi understood this truth, but her mind was made up, and her gaze was resolute and unwavering.

"Now, our Ji Chan clan has no other choice. I do not wish to see generation after generation repeat this fate. My eldest sister was sacrificed to the other side, and now even my ninth sister is by the shores of the Sea of Reincarnation, suffering day and night from the corrosive chill and poison..."

“I want to change the fate of the Ji Chan clan,” she said in a solemn voice.

Ji Xianmiao stared at her in a daze, saying nothing.

“Second Sister, I know you’re not at peace with this either. Now, an opportunity has presented itself before our Ji Chan clan.”

"The leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance has already promised me he would intervene to guide us along the way..."

"You may not know this, but Xiao Jiu and the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance have a history. For Xiao Jiu’s sake, he will help us," Ji Ziyi added.

“Ziyi, how can you guarantee that once my Ji Chan clan arrives at the Heaven Slaying Alliance, we’ll be able to escape our fate?” Ji Xianmiao sighed softly.

"By overthrowing the Realm of Truth and the so-called Heaven, we can break free..." Ji Ziyi’s voice was more resolute than ever.

Ji Xianmiao remained silent for a long moment before saying, “In that case, I won’t stop you. Go to the ancestral court and discuss this with Mother and the elders. If they agree, then I naturally have no objections.”

“Now that our eldest sister is gone and Mother’s vitality is waning, I must consider the entire clan. If I were alone, I would naturally stand by your side.”

Ji Ziyi looked at her and said with utmost seriousness, “Second Sister, you know Mother and the elders are stubborn to the core. They’d rather have Xiao Jiu go to the High Priest to confess and accept punishment than show any sign of dissent. Do you really think they’ll give my words any serious consideration?”

“No. They’ll only think I’ve been brainwashed by the Heaven Slaying Alliance. They might even join forces to subdue me, lock me in a dungeon, and make me face the wall until I come to my senses.”

“Now, of the entire Ji Chan clan, you are the only one who can help me. I must act first and report later.”

Her tone was resolute, imbued with an indescribable determination and will.

Ji Xianmiao was stunned.

She hadn’t expected her third younger sister to have already considered every possible consequence and outcome. This was completely different from the impulsive younger sister she once knew; it seemed that during her years in the Heaven Slaying Alliance, she had truly changed.

Seeing Ji Xianmiao’s silence, Ji Ziyi knew she was hesitating.

She spoke with even greater resolve, “Second Sister, just hand over the jade talisman that grants access to the Ji Yuan Abyss to me. Whatever the consequences, I will bear them alone.”

“If the clan elders hold you accountable, just tell them I deceived you and stole the jade talisman.”

“What do you intend to do in the Ji Yuan Yuan Xu?” Ji Xianmiao froze once more. That place was the root of the Ji Yuan Mother Tree, as well as a forbidden land, guarded by the Ji Chan clan for generations. After the collapse of the multiverse, the remnants of many universes would eventually flow there.

Even the Ji Chan clan required a clan jade talisman to enter.

"I intend to take the Primordial Mother Tree. Only by starting from there can I succeed," Ji Ziyi said.

“It seems… you’ve truly lost your mind…”

Ji Xianmiao sighed. A haze of rosy light swirled around her slender, fair hand, and a simple yet crystalline jade talisman emerged—shaped like an ancient cicada and radiating mysterious, mystical vibrations.

Seeing this, Ji Ziyi understood her second sister’s intentions. A look of deep emotion flashed across her face, but she said nothing. She took the jade talisman and, in a flash, vanished from the grand hall.

"Third Sister is right."

“In that case, I will help you this once. If anything goes wrong this time, I will share your eternal damnation.” Watching her third sister vanish, Ji Xianmiao stood in silence for a moment before her jade-like feet moved lightly, transforming into a streak of colored light as she headed toward the ancestral courtyard of the Ji Chan clan.

The elders of the Ji Chan clan were all engaged in secluded cultivation at the ancestral court, and now the Empress of the Ji Chan clan—their mother—was also convalescing there.

As Ji Xianmiao made her way toward the ancestral courtyard,a mist-like, iridescent vapor rose from her body. Vaguely visible within it was the silhouette of a massive, beautiful, multicolored cicada, spreading its wings in the void. From each wing flowed boundless starry rivers and the essence of chaos, with world particles forming within, enveloping the Ji Chan clan’s ancestral lands and concealing a certain presence and activity.

Within the Ancient Hidden Civilization, many figures of formidable power seemed to sense something at that very moment, and one after another, they opened their eyes.

Wave after wave of terrifying energy swept across the cosmos, crashing like colossal waves against the heavens, causing entire universes to tremble in fear.

Boom!

Meanwhile, in the Vast Expanse beyond, ripples spread out as a passageway opened up. One ancient warship after another, flying the banners of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, descended from that direction.

The vast, endless army, standing upon those ancient warships, was as overwhelming as a raging torrent, striking fear into the hearts of all.

On one of the ancient warships, within a pavilion, Jiang Weiyang stood quietly by the railing. Her demeanor was serene and graceful; she wore an indigo-blue gauze skirt, her hair styled in a cloud-like bun with misty tendrils, a phoenix hairpin slanted in her hair. Her slender neck was as long and graceful as that of a swan.

Her graceful, slender figure bloomed like a white lotus in full bloom, her skin as pure and flawless as snow-white lotus roots, radiating a crystalline luster.

Behind her, General Zhu Cangning of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and his entourage stood in respectful formation.

In addition, there was the Star-Illuminating National Preceptor, who had once been loyal to the Yinxu Cave but had gone into hiding after the collapse of the Righteous Unity Alliance and had not been taken away by the Master of Destiny.

After the Heaven Slaying Alliance had quelled the chaos across the land and established a unified realm, he reappeared, arriving at the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty to resolve Jiang Weiyang’s doubts and reveal the imperial edicts left behind by the former Weiyang Emperor.

It was precisely through National Master Xingming’s revelation—that he had sensed the Master of Destiny’s karmic aura, which was currently concealed within the Ancient Hidden Civilization—

It was only then that Jiang Weiyang and Ji Hanjing abandoned their plans to explore the Nine Heavens and shifted their focus to the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

Consequently, the entire Weiyang Immortal Dynasty mobilized its forces. Cavalry charged like dragons, with billowing clouds and mist sweeping through the cosmos, tearing open a passageway as they raced through space and time, descending upon the scene with a roar that shook the heavens and the universe.
