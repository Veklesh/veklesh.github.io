The woman stood there quietly, her figure graceful and slender. Her eyebrows were as delicate as slender willow leaves, and her almond-shaped eyes shone like black gems. Her nose was straight and delicate, and her skin was as white and tender as fine porcelain. Her full, delicate breasts curved gracefully.

"Elder Chen Yi, how about you accompany the Zhengyi Alliance’s envoy delegation this time to confront the Sect Master of Xieyue Sect in the Heaven Slaying Alliance?" The Sect Master gazed at the woman for a moment before speaking, his tone tinged with resignation and guilt.

The elders and disciples gathered in the square were all startled upon hearing these words.

Many elders, who had been holding their breath in fear of being chosen, now breathed a deep sigh of relief before turning their gaze collectively toward the woman named Chen Yi.

A woman dressed in a green gown, her figure graceful and elegant, could not hide the mockery and schadenfreude in her eyes.

Elder Zhou, who carried a long sword on his back and wore an aloof expression, had also not anticipated this outcome; his face betrayed shock and disbelief.

“Elder Chen Yi?”

Many disciples couldn’t help but whisper among themselves, clearly shocked by this outcome.

Some male disciples, in particular, made no attempt to hide the disappointment and regret on their faces.

Elder Chen Yi was well-known within the Slanting Moon Sect, though not for her cultivation, but for her beauty.

Many elders had pursued her, hoping to become her Dao companions, but she had rejected them all, her stance as firm and unshakable as a rock.

She was also privately referred to by many elders and disciples as the "Stone Beauty."

That she was the one chosen to accompany the Zhengyi Alliance’s envoy delegation to the Heaven Slaying Alliance came as a complete surprise.

This came as a surprise to many, including numerous elders who hadn’t anticipated it. Yet, it also put their minds completely at ease, though they couldn’t help but feel a touch of regret and disappointment.

"Master"

"How could this be?"

Lan Xin, among the disciples, could hardly believe her ears.

Her beautiful eyes widened, her body trembled, and she nearly lost her balance; her face quickly turned pale.

This was essentially sending her Master to his death.

It suddenly dawned on her why Xu Huigang had worn that mocking expression earlier—she had known all along.

A wave of helplessness and bitter anger welled up within Lan Xin.

"Senior Sister Lan Xin, are you all right?" Gu Xian'er was also taken aback and surprised by this unexpected turn of events; she hadn’t anticipated this outcome and reached out to steady Lan Xin.

That stunningly beautiful Elder Chen Yi of the Xieyue Sect was none other than Lan Xin’s master.

“The sect is sending my master to her death in the Heaven Slaying Alliance…” Lan Xin’s voice trembled, her face pale. Her eyes, normally bright and beautiful, were now dull and lifeless.

Gu Xian’er looked at her expression, unsure how to comfort or persuade her.

However, as the only senior sister in the Slanting Moon Sect who cared for and treated her kindly, Gu Xian’er couldn’t bear to see Lan Xin in such a state. She whispered,

“Senior Sister Lan Xin, aren’t you worrying a bit too much? The Heaven Slaying Alliance probably isn’t as dangerous as you imagine—it’s not some den of lions and tigers…”

“Elder Chen Yi shouldn’t be in any danger…”

However, before she could finish her sentence, Lan Xin quickly shook her head, cutting her off.

Her eyes still seemed to hold a trace of fear and dread; even mentioning it seemed to send shivers down her spine. Her face remained pale as she said, “Junior Sister Xian’er, you don’t understand. You’re still too naive. You haven’t truly experienced those fierce, brutal battles. You have no idea just how terrifying the Heaven Slaying Alliance is—the very group that has annihilated so many civilized nations in recent times.”

“Do you know its name? In this vast expanse, to dare to take such a name so brazenly—that alone is an unthinkable taboo.”

“Even the dark spirits dare not attack the Heaven Slaying Alliance. You should know just how terrifying it is.”

Gu Xian’er hadn’t expected that Lan Xin, who had always presented herself as dignified and elegant in her presence, would react so strongly when the Heaven Slaying Alliance was mentioned.

Though she wasn’t entirely sure now whether the Heaven Slaying Alliance that struck such fear into the entire Vastness was actually the same one she knew.

But she still couldn’t help but whisper in her defense, “But I’ve also heard that the Heaven Slaying Alliance has indeed provided a sanctuary for many beings and cultivators, protecting their homes and loved ones.”

“Those are just rumors. Besides, does a bad person become a good person just because they did one good deed?” Lan Xin shook her head, her face still pale.

Meanwhile, Chen Yi herself, standing at the front of the square, was equally shocked and surprised—she hadn’t expected to be the one chosen in the end.

She furrowed her delicate brows, noticing the woman in the green dress not far away, her face brimming with schadenfreude, and suddenly understood.

The Sect Master’s announcement of this matter in front of all the elders and disciples was undoubtedly a premeditated decision, not a spur-of-the-moment one.

Moreover, certain elders must have known the outcome well in advance.

“Chen Yi, I bet you didn’t see this coming. This expedition against the Heaven Slaying Alliance will be the day you die.”

The woman in the green dress let out a cold laugh and whispered to her, making no attempt to hide her schadenfreude.

Chen Yi shot her a cold glance but paid her no mind.

The woman in the green skirt was named Luo Bi; she was also an elder of the Xieyue Sect. Due to certain events from years past, the two harbored deep-seated animosity toward one another. They couldn’t stand the sight of each other and longed to eliminate the other at the earliest opportunity.

She didn’t know if Luo Bi would interfere now that the Sect Master had chosen her to lead the expedition against the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

“Sect Master, may I ask the reason?”

Then, Chen Yi looked at the Siamese Moon Sect Sect Master ahead of her and asked.

She knew full well what the Heaven Slaying Alliance was like—a place the elders all feared and avoided, a place no one wanted to go. She was no exception; she had no desire to risk her life by going there.

"This is the rule of the Zhengyi Alliance. As a member of the Zhengyi Alliance, my Xieyue Sect naturally must abide by it."

"After much deliberation and consultation with the Supreme Elders and the Ancestors, we have determined that Elder Chen Yi, with your formidable cultivation, are the best candidate to accompany the Zhengyi Alliance’s envoy delegation to the Heaven Slaying Alliance."

"I understand that this mission may involve unforeseen dangers, but in order to resolve the Black Calamity once and for all, we have no choice but to ask Elder Chen Yi to take this risk."

The Patriarch of Xieyue Sect spoke in a calm tone, showing no surprise at the reactions of Chen Yi and the other elders and disciples.

Upon hearing this, Chen Yi fell silent.

“Sect Master, you and the other Grand Elders must surely be aware that Elder Chen Yi is injured. Under these circumstances, isn’t it unreasonable to ask him to take such a risk?”

At that moment, an untimely voice suddenly rang out. Elder Zhou stepped forward, his brows furrowed, his tone clearly displeased.

Some time ago, Elder Chen Yi had been severely wounded while venturing out to harvest a rare medicinal herb; he had been attacked by the ancient ferocious beast guarding it. Since then, he had been recuperating at Xieyue Sect.

It was for this very reason that she had been unable to lead her disciples on an external training expedition; subsequently, Elder Zhou had voluntarily stepped forward to take her place.

“Could it be that Elder Zhou intends to go in Elder Chen Yi’s place?” A cold chuckle rang out as Elder Luo Bi, standing not far away, spoke up, unable to bear seeing anyone speak up for Chen Yi.

She had paid a heavy price to ensure the Sect Master and the others would approve of the candidate.

The fact that Chen Yi was injured was no secret within Xieyue Sect, and her injury was no simple matter—it was a poison so potent that even figures like the Grand Elders and the Ancestors found it troublesome and feared it.

The toxin had already penetrated her divine soul and true spirit, making it nearly impossible to expel. It would gradually spread with the passage of time, eroding the very source of Chen Yi’s life and corroding her Dao Fruit.

Over the years, no matter how Chen Yi had nurtured and treated her wounds, there had been no signs of healing. On the contrary, her cultivation had stagnated, and she had even begun to show signs of regression.

Compared to the other Elders, who were in the prime of their power, Chen Yi—who had no future left—was naturally a more suitable sacrificial pawn.

Chen Yi had come to realize all this by now, so when Elder Zhou spoke, she politely declined his offer.

"I understand, Sect Master. I will make the necessary preparations and accompany the envoys of the Zhengyi Alliance to the Heaven Slaying Alliance. However, I do not wish to take any disciples with me."

“Furthermore, I have a request I hope the Sect will grant.” She raised her clear eyes and looked calmly at the Sect Master of Xieyue Sect.

The assembly of elders fell into a moment of silence. Many who were close to Chen Yi in daily life let out sighs, their hearts filled with complex emotions and a heavy sense of frustration. The way she stood there clearly suggested she was already making her final farewells.

“Speak. Whatever the sect is capable of, we will grant you.” The Sect Master of Xieyue Sect nodded, his sense of guilt growing deeper.

They truly had let Chen Yi down this time, but there was nothing they could do about it.

Yet her calm and composed acceptance only made them feel even more uneasy.

Yet they could not disobey the Zhengyi Alliance’s orders.

"I hope the sect will treat my disciples well. Furthermore, since I am about to depart for the Heaven Slaying Alliance, I wish to take Gu Xian’er as my disciple before I leave."

Chen Yi spoke calmly, then turned her gaze toward Lan Xin, Gu Xian’er, and the others in the distance.

Previously, she had refused Lan Xin’s request to take Gu Xian’er as a disciple precisely because she feared it would invite misfortune and ill omen.

But now that she was about to set out for the Heaven Slaying Alliance, she naturally no longer cared about any ill omens or misfortune.

Taking Gu Xian’er as a nominal disciple before leaving was also a way to grant Lan Xin’s

, and in the future, the sect would likely not mistreat Gu Xian’er out of respect for her.

The Sect Master of Xieyue Sect and the many elders all showed a slight change in expression.

They weren’t surprised by Chen Yi’s previous request.

But that she would actually want to take Gu Xian’er—whom everyone else was eager to avoid—as a disciple? Had she truly given up, no longer caring about karma or ill omens?

After a moment of silence, the Shattered Moon Sect’s Sect Master finally said, “I agree.” Chen Yi smiled slightly, then transformed into a streak of light and vanished into the distance, preparing to head to the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Among the disciples, Gu Xian’er had not anticipated that the matter would ultimately involve taking her as a disciple.

She stared blankly at the complex gazes cast her way by the other disciples, her own feelings even more complicated.

She understood Elder Chen Yi’s goodwill, but in truth, she did not wish to take her as her master.

Her masters were in the Daochang True Realm, not at Xieyue Sect.

"Master has finally agreed to take Junior Sister Xian'er as a disciple..."

“But for some reason, I just can’t bring myself to feel happy.” Lan Xin’s face still bore a look of sorrow, and her spirits were low.

This should have been a joyous occasion, yet it also meant that her master had cast aside all regard for life and death.

After leaving the square, Gu Xian’er couldn’t find Elder Chen Yi. She planned to go directly to her and politely decline her offer.

With no other choice, she could only return to her cave residence for the time being and recount today’s events to the white-robed woman and the scarlet bird there.

Even now, Gu Xian’er didn’t know the white-clad woman’s name, and the woman herself couldn’t recall it either.

When the conversation turned to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, Gu Xian’er noticed the woman in white seemed to zone out for a moment, as if those three words had triggered some memory, leaving her lost in thought for a while.

However, in the end, she still couldn’t recall anything; she only felt her headache growing worse.

The scarlet bird, sensing the situation, tactfully refrained from discussing its origins or Gu Xian’er’s background in front of the woman in white.

Both the woman and the bird were well aware of the matters concerning the Heaven Slaying Alliance; they had even discussed it many times before, speculating on the exact nature of its relationship with the Heaven Slaying Alliance of the Daochang True Realm.

After all, the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s reputation in the vast expanse of the present era was indeed immense—as evidenced by Lan Xin’s reaction today alone.

As for the Daochang True Realm, from Gu Xian’er’s current perspective, it was merely a newly formed true realm.

Back then, the strongest cultivator in the Daochang True Realm had only just reached the Dao Realm tier and had survived one or two Celestial Waning Tribulations.

In the context of the Xieyue Sect, they wouldn’t even qualify as pillars of the organization.

One must understand that even some of the young prodigies of the Xieyue Sect—those with exceptional talent and immense blessings—have already crossed that threshold, and suppressing ordinary Dao Realm beings is a trivial matter for them.

Only those who have survived three or four Celestial Waning Tribulations are barely qualified to be called Elders.

Although the Heaven Slaying Alliance in the Daochang True Realm was founded by Gu Changge, its underlying strength and resources pale in comparison to the Heaven Slaying Alliance in the Vast Expanse—the two are worlds apart, operating on entirely different levels.

The Heaven Slaying Alliance in the Vast Expanse did not exist from the beginning; it suddenly emerged thousands of years ago, a period that coincides with the time Gu Changge left the Daochang True Realm.

Gu Xian’er didn’t know if the Heaven Slaying Alliance had anything to do with him, nor did she care.

All she knew was that she hadn’t heard from Gu Changge in a very long time. She had no idea where he had gone or what he had been doing after he left the Daochang True Realm without a trace.

After leaving the Daochang True Realm, she had tried to track down Gu Changge’s whereabouts and follow in his footsteps, but all her attempts failed; she found not a single trace or clue.

In fact, it was precisely because of this that she encountered numerous dangers; in the end, it was only through her wit and luck that she managed to narrowly escape them all.

After leaving the square, Gu Xian’er sought out Elder Chen Yi partly because she wanted her to take her along.

She wanted to go to the Heaven Slaying Alliance to see it with her own eyes.

She had a premonition that she would see the person she most wanted to see.

Time passed quickly. Upon returning to her cave residence, Gu Xian’er didn’t rest for long before noticing that it had already grown dark outside.

“I wonder if that old woman will appear at the ruins of that ancient site tonight.” “I must find her and ask why she brought me to the Slanting Moon Sect back then.” She rose and left the cave dwelling, taking care not to disturb the white-clad woman resting inside, nor did she bring the crimson bird along.

The mountains were exceptionally quiet at night; not even the chirping of insects or birds could be heard, let alone any signs of wild beasts or mountain spirits.

A faint white mist rose from the forest. Gu Xian’er transformed into a streak of light and soon arrived at the long-abandoned, crumbling ruins.

After last night’s events, she had grown much bolder, knowing that no malevolent spirits or strange phenomena lurked here—only that old woman weeping beneath the moon.

During the day, the old woman had all but vanished; Gu Xian’er hadn’t seen her in quite some time, making it impossible to find her to ask any questions.

Otherwise, she wouldn’t have chosen to come here at this time to try her luck.

She didn’t know what this place had once been.

Why did that old woman wander here so persistently, filled with such profound grief, weeping at the moon in the dead of night?

The ruins were vast, containing almost nothing but collapsed and shattered structures.

If there were any treasures, they would likely have been looted and taken away long ago by the disciples and elders of the Xieyue Sect.

Gu Xian’er made her way to the small hill where she had seen the old woman the night before. After a moment’s thought, she decided to hide in a collapsed alchemy chamber first, then used an ancient talisman to mask her aura.

After all, the old woman from last night had vanished only after noticing her.

The night grew ever darker, and a chilling breeze began to blow across the vast, open expanse. By the dead of night, even the moonlight had dimmed.

Gu Xian’er waited there for a long time but saw no sign of the old woman. Feeling a bit disappointed, she was just about to leave.

Just then, the sound of voices suddenly rang out nearby.

Gu Xian’er’s departing footsteps halted involuntarily. She was quite surprised—how could anyone be wandering through these ruins in the dead of night?

“Young Master, did your journey to the Yao Guang Ancient Realm go smoothly? Did you meet Princess Wei Yang?” In the passageway of the shattered ruins, a middle-aged man in gray robes, wearing a tall crown and exuding an air of refinement, walked toward her while conversing with the young man at his side.

Gu Xian’er was startled; the middle-aged man in gray looked vaguely familiar to her. He seemed to be an elder of the Xieyue Sect—the kind with profound cultivation and unfathomable power.

He actually addressed the young man beside him as “Young Master”?

Could it be that she had once again inadvertently stumbled upon someone’s schemes or secrets?

But when she got a clear look at the young man’s face, Gu Xian’er was even more astonished.

"The disciple named Song Yu—the foremost young talent of the current generation in the Xieyue Sect?"
