During the time Gu Changge was away from the Heaven Slaying Alliance, both Ling Yuling and Yao Yao had come looking for him several times—one to discuss Jiang Weiyang, the other to inquire about the Immortal Dao Alliance—but without exception, neither had managed to find him.

With the founding of the Immortal Dao Alliance imminent, the Righteous Unity Alliance had dispatched numerous high-ranking officials to attend.

Now, all the major sects and clans across the Vast Expanse have turned their gaze toward the Heaven Slaying Alliance, closely monitoring its every move.

Yet, at this very critical juncture, Gu Changge had vanished without a trace. Even Ling Yuling, who was generally considered his closest confidante, had no idea where he had gone.

This left Ling Yuling feeling slightly helpless, as if she were the eunuch fretting while the emperor remained unconcerned.

The high-ranking members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance—such as Chu Heng, the White Bone Ancestral King, and Hun Yuanjun—were all suggesting that Ling Yuling take the lead in deciding how to handle the ceremony marking the birth of the Immortal Dao Alliance.

After all, Gu Changge had previously stated that he would not make things difficult for the Immortal Dao Alliance, and he did not wish to make too many enemies, becoming the common enemy of all the major sects and realms across the Vast Expanse.

"Let’s wait a little longer," Ling Yuling said, shaking her head gently in the grand hall.

Currently, aside from the high-ranking members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, many civilizations outside believed she was still trapped in the Void Realm.

Even many of the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s powerful figures were unaware that, in fact, she had been rescued from the Void Realm by Gu Changge the very next day after the great battle between the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance had ended.

As for Gu Changge’s strength, Ling Yuling had never harbored the slightest doubt.

“But I’m worried that the Righteous Unity Alliance might interfere and stir up hatred between our Heaven Slaying Alliance and the various civilizations of the True Realm,” Hun Yuanjun frowned deeply.

Chu Heng, standing nearby, sneered, “In the eyes of the various races of the Vast Expanse today, aren’t we of the Heaven Slaying Alliance simply heretics? The animosity has long existed. If it weren’t for the Alliance Master’s presence—a figure feared by all—we would likely have been attacked by a united front long ago.”

“In my view, the formation of the Immortal Dao Alliance plays right into the hands of the Righteous Unity Alliance. They will likely find ways to join forces and oppose our Heaven Slaying Alliance in the future.”

"The best course of action is to resolve this once and for all by preventing the Immortal Dao Alliance from forming. Since a major war is bound to break out sooner or later anyway, it’s better to settle this now to avoid it becoming a major threat in the future."

In reality, the upper echelons of the Heaven Slaying Alliance are currently divided into several factions.

Chu Heng happens to belong to the hawkish faction. He enjoys leading vast armies to wage war in all directions, expanding their territory, and accumulating sufficient military merits—all to satisfy Gu Changge’s promise to help him resurrect his wife.

Hun Yuanjun, Zhuo Fengwei, and others, however, belong to the moderate faction. They are not fond of overly aggressive tactics and prefer to proceed cautiously in all matters. Their strategy involves first using tactics to undermine the enemy’s internal cohesion, then encircling and absorbing them.

Alternatively, they would first intimidate the enemy to force their submission; only as a last resort would they resort to military force.

Over the years, Gu Changge had never interfered in these matters, leaving everything to Ling Yuling to handle, adopting the attitude of a hands-off owner.

On a daily basis, he only makes decisions when major events arise; for instance, the last bloody battle against the Righteous Unity Alliance was ordered by him.

When it came to matters of this magnitude, no one could sway his decision.

Listening to the senior officials arguing fiercely within the hall, Ling Yuling furrowed her delicate brows slightly and rubbed the bridge of her nose with a hint of exasperation.

However, at that moment, she seemed to sense something. She glanced outside the hall, pondered for a moment, told everyone to wait there, and then transformed into a streak of light, vanishing into the distance.

“Could it be that the Alliance Leader has returned?” speculated the White Bone Ancestral King.

At his side stood a gray-haired girl with snow-white skin and delicate features, as if carved from ice and snow—none other than Mo Tong, whom Gu Changge had once rescued from the arena of the Xianling Civilization.

She had inherited the White Bone Ancestral King’s legacy, attaining enlightenment through slaughter. Among the countless young prodigies of extraordinary talent within the Heaven Slaying Alliance today, she stood head and shoulders above the rest.

Mo Tong had surged ahead, and her apparent cultivation level was already closing in on Mu Yan’s.

Mu Yan, however, due to her laziness and complacency, has made very slow progress in her cultivation. If Gu Changge hadn’t expelled her from the courtyard some time ago, she likely wouldn’t have taken the initiative to go out and train to improve her cultivation and strength.

"Master, I’d like to attend the founding ceremony of the Immortal Dao Alliance this time," Mo Tong said in a low voice to the White Bone Ancestral King after Ling Yuling had disappeared.

"There’s no problem with you participating, but we still can’t be certain of the Alliance Master’s true intentions," the White Bone Ancestral King replied with a nod.

“The Alliance Leader won’t take action against the Immortal Dao Alliance, because the Alliance will keep the Dark Clans in check. For the sake of the greater good, he won’t easily turn against the Immortal Dao Alliance.”

“Right now, the Dark Clans are the greatest threat,” Mo Tong said softly.

The Skeleton Ancestral King glanced at her, smiled, and neither confirmed nor denied it.

Soon after, Ling Yuling, who had left the grand hall, returned, though her expression was slightly peculiar.

"Regarding the Immortal Dao Alliance, the Alliance Leader has stated that he will personally go there when the time comes."

“At that time, the Bone Ancestor King, along with Tong Xian and the others, will accompany him.”

She scanned the assembled high-ranking officials in the hall, then turned to Chu Heng. “Commander Chu Heng, the Righteous Unity Alliance requires you and the Demon Emperor Di Kun to lead the army there. The Alliance Master intends to give the Righteous Unity Alliance a massive surprise during the founding of the Immortal Dao Alliance.”

“On that day, the Alliance Master may personally intervene to break through the Righteous Unity Alliance’s defenses.”

The moment her words fell, the hall instantly fell into a deathly silence.

All the high-ranking members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, regardless of their cultivation level, shuddered, their faces filled with astonishment and horror.

Even the few Forbidden Beings who had remained silent nearby—Mu Fuguang, the Compassionate Zen Buddha, Tong Xian, and others—could not help but turn their gaze toward the scene, their faces betraying profound shock.

What did it mean that Gu Changge might personally intervene?

The Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance were likely to face a true decisive battle.

The Vast Expanse was about to undergo a major reshuffling.

They had witnessed Gu Changge’s overwhelming power firsthand—a force no one could possibly match. They understood that only if the true mastermind behind the Righteous Unity Alliance stepped forward could they stand a chance; otherwise, with the Righteous Unity Alliance’s current strength and resources, they stood no chance against Gu Changge.

Or perhaps Gu Changge is trying to force that person to reveal themselves?

He had never intervened in the major clashes between the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance before—why had he suddenly made such a decision?

Even Ling Yuling could not fathom Gu Changge’s intentions. As soon as she sensed his presence returning to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, she hurried to the courtyard where he was in seclusion.

But what she saw was a crowd of beautiful women surrounding Gu Changge—all of them stunningly beautiful, with faces as lovely as flowers and as radiant as the moon, their beauty in no way inferior to her own.

She almost wondered if she had come to the wrong place at the wrong time.

Yet Gu Changge remained as calm and composed as ever. Seeing her arrive, he showed no surprise; he merely introduced them casually, explaining that these women were all wives he had married through proper channels.

Ling Yuling knew nothing of Gu Changge’s past, but witnessing this scene, it took her quite some time to recover.

So, during the time he had been gone, he had been visiting his mistresses—though she had never once imagined that he actually had a family.

Ling Yuling had always believed that Gu Changge was a lone figure, standing alone in the world.

It was likely that no one in the entire Heaven Slaying Alliance knew about these matters.

No, wait—Gu Changge’s disciple, that young girl Yao Yao who always wore a red dress, she must have known.

She really should have guessed all this long ago; after all, since he has a disciple, it only makes sense that he would have a family as well.

As Ling Yuling’s thoughts were in disarray,

Among them, a sly, mischievous young woman stepped forward, addressing her as “sister.” She casually slung her arm around Ling Yuling’s, then began asking about her name, background, and the details of how Gu Changge had won her over.

This was more than Ling Yuling could bear; her face flushed, and she felt that Mu Yan’s temperament was every bit as intense as this woman’s.

She had once witnessed Gu Changge opening up a world and refining the chaos within this villa, and had felt a mix of curiosity and confusion—wondering if he simply had too much free time. Now, it seemed he had been making preparations long in advance to bring all these women here.

This stirred an inexplicable pang of jealousy and envy in Ling Yuling’s heart.

So Gu Changge was capable of considering others after all.

Later, she remembered the matter at hand and asked about the Immortal Dao Alliance. Gu Changge then informed her of his various plans and instructions for the future.

Ling Yuling did not wish to disturb him further and left the villa.

Meanwhile, in the grand hall, the high-ranking members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance were all in a state of complex and conflicting emotions.

The establishment of the Immortal Dao Alliance had already thrown the situation in the Vast Expanse into further chaos. If Gu Changge were to truly launch an attack on the Righteous Unity Alliance next, the Vast Expanse would truly descend into an age of turmoil.

"It seems he has no intention of giving the Righteous Unity Alliance even a moment’s respite. Perhaps the Righteous Unity Alliance still believes that, with the great war just concluded, the Heaven Slaying Alliance needs to withdraw its forces and recuperate."

"This sudden turn of events is something no one could have foreseen," Mu Fuguang, Tong Xian, and the others couldn’t help but sigh deeply.

They had not participated in the previous great battle because they sensed that the Righteous Unity Alliance also harbored beings of comparable power, lurking in the shadows and biding their time.

But now that the Righteous Unity Alliance and the Heaven Slaying Alliance had erupted into a decisive battle—and with even Gu Changge stepping in—they could no longer stand idly by and remain uninvolved.

Given Gu Changge’s temperament, he would never allow them to simply sit back and watch the spectacle.

It was quite possible that several more Ancestral Dao Realm beings of the same level would perish in the coming conflict, and even they might find themselves caught in the crossfire.

"He wasn’t in a hurry before, so why has he suddenly decided to intervene personally? I can’t help but feel there’s more to it than meets the eye." Mu Fuguang was arguably the longest-lived and most unfathomable of all the Forbidden Beings.

Yet at this moment, she sensed a terrifying malevolence descending from the void, poised to engulf all worlds and realms.

Meanwhile, within the courtyard, Gu Changge had finally settled his Divine Realm. Though traveling to the Prosperous Dao True Realm was merely a thought—a matter of a few breaths—to Yue Mingkong, Jiang Chuchu, and the others, it felt as though they were separated from him by billions of light-years and endless spacetime.

Now that he had established the Divine Realm within this courtyard, it had become a separate, self-contained universe—essentially his own private garden.

For Jiang Chuchu, Wang Zijing, and the others, seeing him was now easy; they could emerge from it at any time. Although the cultivation process was tedious and dreary, it brought them an added sense of peace and enjoyment.

Splash…

Ripples and splashes spread across the surface of the pond as Gu Changge released the fish—the reincarnation of Xiyao—into the water.

The rich essence of the sun and moon, along with the spiritual energy of heaven and earth, flowed into her with each breath. Her originally crystal-clear and beautiful body began to be overlaid with a substance resembling chaotic qi.

After all, the Vast Expanse was unlike the Prosperous Dao True Realm; the world’s dimensions and the laws of heaven and earth were entirely different. The foundation Xiyao was forging through her cultivation and transformation here far surpassed anything she had achieved in the Demon Realm.

Once her spiritual awareness awakens and her innate wisdom resurfaces, her cultivation will likely catch up to that of her previous life in no time.

Moreover, within this pool of water lay fragments of divine essence that had been released by the deity Gu Changge had accidentally caught while fishing for the Taoxuan Ancestral God.

For Xiyao, this was a rare elixir.

Of course, Gu Changge did not interfere with her growth; he simply let nature take its course.

However, speaking of that Great Ancestral God, Gu Changge recalled the contingency plan he had left behind within her—it should be about to take effect soon.

After all, bait that had passed through his hands was not something that could be easily reclaimed—even if it was something that originally belonged to her.
