The great battle between the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance has captured the attention of the entire Cangmang.

Many undecided civilizations now began to waver.

Previously, they had been considering whether to join the Righteous Unity Alliance, but judging by the current situation, if even the Righteous Unity Alliance could barely save itself, how could it possibly protect them?

It was doubtful whether the Righteous Unity Alliance would even survive this war.

Furthermore, the Dark Beings were now watching intently, attacking the Righteous Unity Alliance’s defensive lines from all directions, rendering the Alliance’s once-unparalleled natural advantages completely obsolete.

In contrast, the Heaven Slaying Alliance is a force even the Dark Beings fear and are reluctant to provoke.

Although joining the Heaven Slaying Alliance would inevitably mean submitting to its authority and governance, it was still better than being annihilated and losing one’s life.

In the blink of an eye, numerous civilizations and realms chose to switch sides, meekly migrating to join the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Even civilizations like the Posa Sacred Realm and the Ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao—which had long been secretly negotiating to join the Righteous Unity Alliance—began to waver and hesitate.

However, it was precisely at this critical moment that the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty took an action that caught the civilizations off guard.

The Emperor of Weiyang personally issued orders; edicts flew out from the capital of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, piercing through the fabric of the cosmos and descending upon all corners of the realm.

Subsequently, the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty dispatched vast numbers of powerful warriors and armies to traverse the vast expanse. Ancient warships and chariots opened stable spacetime corridors, heading out to reinforce the Righteous Unity Alliance.

The mighty army of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty soared into the heavens; their iron cavalry surged like dragons, clouds of smoke and mist swirled, and the starry realms trembled as they raced through the cosmos, their roar shaking the eight corners of the universe.

This spectacle sent shockwaves through countless cultivators and sentient beings, leaving them in awe.

Just when everyone had written off the Righteous Unity Alliance, the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty took this action—one that caught everyone off guard and defied all expectations.

What does this mean?

With the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty taking the lead, several sects and factions allied with it also entered the fray. As if assured of some guarantee, they followed closely behind, dispatching vast numbers of powerful cultivators to support the Righteous Unity Alliance.

"The Heaven Slaying Alliance is full of ambition and seizes every opportunity to plunder. If we allow the Righteous Unity Alliance to collapse and disintegrate, there will be no force left to check the Heaven Slaying Alliance in the future."

"Therefore, regardless of the reason, we cannot allow the Heaven Slaying Alliance to continue expanding and dominating unchecked. Moreover, why haven’t the Dark Beings taken action against the Heaven Slaying Alliance? Is it truly because they fear its leader? That may not be the case."

"The Heaven Slaying Alliance’s origins are unclear, and its leader’s background is even more dubious. I fear there are intricate connections between 02 and the Dark Beings."

"The Heaven Slaying Alliance is brimming with ambition and acts against the natural order; they are the common enemy of all realms. At this critical juncture, we must unite our forces to resist them."

Many cultivators and beings are also speculating that if we do not act now to check the Heaven Slaying Alliance,

once the Righteous Unity Alliance collapses, there may be no force left in the entire Vast Expanse capable of restraining the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

At that point, all living beings would become mere prey, at their mercy.

Moreover, unlike the Righteous Unity Alliance, whose origins and nature were well-known, the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s existence is truly too mysterious. In just a few thousand years, it has grown into a force that the entire Cangmang dares not underestimate.

This is virtually unheard of in the history of the Vast Expanse, for no traditional civilization has ever established a firm foothold without being tempered and refined by the passage of time.

This is a variable even stranger than an anomaly—one that cannot be predicted or fathomed.

The leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance is even more shrouded in mystery; it would not be an exaggeration to say he appeared out of thin air.

Looking back across the vast river of time—from the dawn of the ancient era, when the Human Emperor first ascended and the myriad races began to flourish, all the way to the past and the future—it is as if he were shrouded in a thick fog, as if he had never existed at all.

Above all, his power is overwhelmingly terrifying and sky-shaking; there is simply no one who can stand against him.

This has led the Heaven Slaying Alliance to grow increasingly rampant and domineering; even when facing civilizations with deep-rooted foundations and a long history, they do not hold them in the slightest regard.

If the Heaven Slaying Alliance can find any excuse to attack the Righteous Unity Alliance, will they need a reason to strike against other orthodox civilizations in the future?

Today they seize five cities, tomorrow ten—and then they expect to sleep soundly? Looking out across the four borders, the Heaven Slaying Alliance is already upon us.

As the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty raised its armies to aid the Righteous Unity Alliance, many reclusive clans from the vast, boundless expanse began to step into the fray, choosing to enter the game at this critical juncture.

Unlike those renowned civilizations of the True Realm, the origins and longevity of some reclusive groups are in no way inferior to those of the most powerful, time-honored civilizations; they simply remain incredibly low-key, unwilling to engage with the world or become entangled in the karma and mundane affairs of mortals.

The vast expanse was in turmoil, and the situation was incredibly chaotic, much like the surface of a water jar. Originally calm and undisturbed, but as ripples and waves began to form, these clans and factions could no longer remain hidden. Otherwise, when the water jar shattered, no clan or faction would be spared.

The existence of the Heaven Slaying Alliance is the anomaly that shatters this tranquil surface.

In the depths of Purple Xiao Mountain within the Yuan Hope Civilization, Zi Wanhe, seated cross-legged in the center of the palace, suddenly opened his eyes.

He looked ahead with a mixture of surprise and doubt, his expression gradually growing grave.

"What is this..."

Strands of strange, magnificent light spilled into the void before him, like mist.

His skull, too, was now glowing, becoming crystal clear. A mark resembling a purple droplet burned with heat, like a candle in the darkness, guiding him toward something.

"Damn it, those bastards actually left a mark on me. I never should have made a deal with them in the first place. Those cunning bastards never let anyone get the better of them."

Zi Wanhe’s face darkened as he immediately sensed something amiss within himself.

Back when Gu Changge first arrived at Zixiao Mountain, he had asked Zi Wanhe to prepare a grand gift for Chu Gucheng.

After much deliberation, Zi Wanhe had struck a deal with this group of mysterious figures, leaving him indebted to them.

In return, they had procured a birthday gift for Chu Gucheng of Xianchu Haotu: a replica of the "Coffin of the Underworld," which contained a substance resembling dark matter.

After the fall of Xian Chuhoutu, these individuals approached him again, asking him to investigate Gu Changge’s background.

Although Zi Wanhe had agreed on the spot, he knew full well that in today’s vast and desolate world, who would dare—and who would have the ability—to do such a thing?

Even if he had submitted to the Heaven Slaying Alliance and served under Gu Changge, he would never have dared to entertain such a thought.

For this reason, Zi Wanhe had directly severed that memory from his mind and erased all traces of the incident.

Although he didn’t think Gu Changge would be so idle as to probe his thoughts and intentions, what if one day, on a whim, Gu Changge decided to scan his mental energy? Wouldn’t he be in trouble then?

"I never expected that even after I’d severed those memories and karmic ties, the marks left by those creatures still lingered. They’re truly tenacious—I must say, they truly live up to their reputation as the legendary Dream Demons."

Zi Wanhe’s expression grew even more grim. He had no idea why the marks were burning this time—or why those Dream Demons had come looking for him on their own.

The Dream Demons are a legendary race, existing in dimensions invisible to ordinary people.

As long as one possesses their token, one can make a deal with them.

Dream Demons are omnipotent; as long as the terms of the deal are reasonable, they can fulfill the trader’s wishes.

It was while Zi Wanhe was searching for a way to break the "Bridal Robe Immortal Art" that, through a series of twists of fate, he came to possess the Dream Ghost’s token.

Rumor has it that the Dream Demons are actually the transformed forms of the Dream Clan’s deceased members. Thanks to a certain power of the Dream Clan, they remain in the mortal world, able to traverse the realms of reality and illusion.

Zi Wanhe had lived for countless ages, yet he had never seen the Dream Clan and did not know if this tribe still existed in the world.

He had once come across records on Purple Xiao Mountain mentioning the existence of the Dream Marshes, divided into the Cloud and White regions, with the White Dream Marshes being the dwelling place of the Dream Ghosts.

A magnificent and strange purple mist spread out before Zi Wanhe. The void before him rippled in waves, as if a pebble had been tossed into calm waters. Blurred, hazy figures emerged from the other side—some serene, some indifferent, some sneering, some wild and defiant…

Exotic flowers and rare herbs, slender bamboo and towering pines, a thousand ancient cypresses, and ten thousand stalks of tall bamboo—even the wild grasses growing in the crevices of the rocks were imbued with an ethereal, otherworldly aura, making the place truly extraordinary.

On the highest of the purple peaks here, there is a cave entrance several people tall, with two youths—a boy and a girl, both appearing to be in their teens—standing on either side.

Not far from the cave entrance stands a square, green stone stele.

Standing three zhang tall and over six chi wide, the three ancient characters “Yinxu Cave” were carved with silver hooks and iron strokes. The brushwork danced like dragons and serpents, exuding a natural Daoist aura, as if interpreting the myriad Daos of the heavens, shrouded in a hazy glow of rosy light.
