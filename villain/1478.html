The fierce battle between the Dark Clans and the Nine Heavens has long captured the attention of the Vast Expanse, and with the retreat of the Dark Clans, the Nine Heavens have been fully revealed to the entire cosmos.

This has also completely lifted the veil of mystery that had shrouded the Nine Heavens throughout the ages.

After the Dark Clans retreated, the Nine Heavens did not, as in past eras, choose to withdraw into the depths of time and space, but instead revealed themselves fully to the Vast Expanse.

The Nine Heavens resembled nine stacked continents, each vast and boundless, surrounded by swirling nebulae.

The layers of the Nine Heavens that had been destroyed and collapsed during the great war were rapidly being restored through the combined efforts of numerous supreme beings.

All the races and sentient beings within the Nine Heavens received word that the realm might now abandon its former stance and confront the impending Great Calamity head-on.

This news instantly swept through the entire Nine Heavens like a hurricane, filling all living beings and cultivators with dread and unease.

Even the Nine Heavens, which had always remained aloof from worldly affairs, was now forced to confront the Great Calamity?

In truth, the many supreme beings within the Nine Heavens also felt a deep sense of helplessness regarding this matter. Unless absolutely necessary, who would willingly face the Epoch Reckoning? It often resulted in being shattered to pieces, with both body and spirit annihilated, leaving little chance of survival.

In the Epoch Reckoning, whether one exists in the Immortal Realm or the Dao Realm, the outcome is the same: the will of Heaven is like a blade, rampaging and reaping without mercy.

In the past, figures at the End-of-the-Path level still had means to conceal or evade it. But now, after their demise, the traces of karma have been left behind; even if they hide, they will be found during the Epoch Reckoning.

There is simply no way out.

Deep within the Nine Heavens, numerous overwhelmingly powerful auras returned from various realms of time and space, seemingly engaged in earnest deliberations on how to respond to the situation.

"That ancient mirror must be the Mirror of Reincarnation—one of the six treasures of the First Civilization that we have been searching for all along."

“I never expected the Holy Maiden of Xi Yuan to appear in the Nine Heavens. How did she gain entry? Now that the Mirror of Reincarnation has been reclaimed by the Dark Clans, what are we to do if we wish to seal the Lord of Heaven in the future?”

The Lord of Heaven had only just awakened, yet already possessed such terrifying and unimaginable power.

In the grand central hall of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, the space was vast and majestic, interwoven with the essence of the Dao and imbued with profound depth, yet now filled with the clamor of commotion.

Numerous high-ranking officials and leaders had gathered here, using their means to observe the fierce battle raging between the Nine Heavens and the Dark Clan—a conflict that had captured the attention of countless civilizations, and the Heaven Slaying Alliance was no exception.

In particular, the Dark Clans and the Heaven Slaying Alliance maintained a sort of ambiguous, “honeymoon-like” relationship—neither interfering with the other. Yet this did not diminish the deep wariness and caution with which the Alliance’s high-ranking leaders viewed them.

The Eternal Primordial God and Lord Luo were both present in the grand hall. As the two strongest figures in the Heaven Slaying Alliance aside from Gu Changge, they always remained close behind him whenever he appeared.

This has led many of the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s high-ranking members and powerful figures to speculate that these two supreme beings must be held in the Alliance Leader’s power over life and death, which is why they show such deference.

Otherwise, how could figures of such stature grovel and submit to another’s commands? To put it bluntly, those who stand proudly at this level are, in the truest sense, the absolute pinnacle of power in the Vast Expanse.

Apart from the presence of the Supreme Shattered Realm and the True Path Realm, who could possibly stand against them?

However, witnessing the profound and terrifying depths of the Nine Heavens Realm with their own eyes this time left many high-ranking members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance trembling with fear.

The world knows only that the Nine Heavens Realm is incomparably transcendent, yet its true might and terror have always revealed but the tip of the iceberg.

"Still, the Nine Heavens Realm revealing its true potential is enough to intimidate the Dark Clans. The situation in the Vast Expanse will improve significantly from now on."

"Judging from what just happened, the two beings that appeared outside the Nine Heavens Realm were both at the End of the Path tier—something that would have been virtually impossible to witness so easily in the past." Many still felt a sense of awe, yet their hearts were now filled with greater confidence.

The undisputed strongest figure in the Heaven Slaying Alliance today is undoubtedly the Alliance Leader, Gu Changge. The fact that he could command two figures at the pinnacle of their realms to obey him so obediently finally gave the Alliance’s high-ranking members a somewhat clearer understanding of his true strength.

Before this, no matter what kind of enemy he faced, he remained calm and composed, easily subduing them with a single palm strike. This had left everyone’s perception of his true strength rather vague—as if viewed through a haze, they could never see it clearly.

Now, they finally understood why the Dark Clans dared not invade the Heaven Slaying Alliance as long as Gu Changge stood at its helm.

After all, this was undoubtedly a being at the True Path Realm or even the Supreme Shattered Realm. Unless the Lord of Heaven were to awaken, how could the Dark Clans dare to invade?

With these thoughts in mind, the numerous high-ranking officials and leaders gathered in the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s grand hall turned their gaze toward Gu Changge, who sat majestically at the very center, their eyes brimming with reverence.

The Vast Expanse, which had once seemed veiled by a light mist, was now lifting to reveal the truth to all.

"Ju, Si, and the distant ancestors of the Gu family..."

Gu Changge’s gaze grew profound as he withdrew his gaze. Judging by the aura he had just sensed, he now had a clear understanding of the current state of the Nine Heavens’ foundations.

In the Vast Expanse, those who wielded authority were traditionally referred to as the True Path.

But in Gu Changge’s eyes, the True Path and the End of the Path were one and the same—there was no path to begin with, and whichever one was taken led to a dead end.

Rather than being the "True Path," it was, in reality, merely a further step beyond the Ancestral Path—a path of mastery.

The aura of the authority of Heaven and Earth manifests in only nine strands, each representing a portion of Heaven’s authority—a number that signifies the ultimate limit.

Throughout the Vast Expanse, boundless, and ancient ages, only nine strands will ever appear.

In the Vast Expanse itself, the End of the Path already represents a limit beyond which no further advancement, leap, or transformation is possible; the power that Heaven and Earth can permit can only reach this level.

The subsequent emergence of the Supreme Shattered Realm or the True Path Realm are indeed two paths that the sages and predecessors within the Vast Expanse have groped their way toward through various means over the course of endless, ancient ages.

As for transcendence?

In truth, this is merely a matter of interpretation; there is no such thing as a realm of transcendence. In the understanding of the Ancestral Dao Realm, perhaps reaching the end of the path is what touches upon that realm.

But in the eyes of those who have attained the Supreme Shattering, transcendence may only be achieved by forging a new path and journeying even further; thus, to them, transcendence is merely a deception, a dead end.

And in the eyes of those beings of the Immortal Path, perhaps leaving the world that nurtured them to journey to vaster, more boundless realms and attain a more enduring, endless life—that is transcendence.

But what, exactly, is transcendence?

Even the Three True Ancestors do not believe they have already transcended; transcendence is merely an illusion, a concept…

At least in Gu Changge’s view, transcendence is defined by one’s original state of being; it is a state of mind and a sense of perception.

The transcendence that the Vast Expanse has always sought is, in essence, nothing more than a deception. Even if one truly crosses to the other shore and reaches the realm of reality, does that truly mean one has transcended?

Gu Changge’s gaze grew profound.

After the Great Calamity of Creation, the pure qi rose upward while the impure qi sank downward, evolving into Heaven and Earth. The pure qi accumulated and transformed; through the supreme and inconceivable methods of the Three True Ancestors, it created the Realm of Truth. Meanwhile, the impure qi settled and accumulated, becoming the Vast Expanse—the fertile soil that nurtures all things and gives rise to the universe of sentient beings… All of this was merely to sustain the Realm of Truth.

Of course, this is merely the "God’s" concept.

Each True Ancestor holds their own philosophy. Were there no external interference or obstruction, perhaps after countless unknown, chaotic, and unpredictable ages had passed, the Vast Expanse and the Real World would reorganize and merge, transforming into the most primordial and primal realm of primordial chaos—a single initial point—to begin the process anew, continuing until they found what they deemed the most correct answer.

“Alliance Leader, ever since a moment ago, the Deputy Alliance Leader’s expression has seemed somewhat off…”

From the side, a respectful reminder came from a high-ranking member of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Gu Changge glanced at Ling Yuling. She was clad in pure white robes that fell smoothly to her waist; her complexion was fair and flawless, as beautiful as a painting. Yet at this moment, she appeared shrouded in mist, her eyes somewhat vacant, as if countless scenes were flickering within them.

Ever since he had taken the Mirror of Reincarnation in his capacity as the Ruler in the Place of Heaven, Ling Yuling had been in this state.

He cast only a single glance before looking away; as for why Ling Yuling had become this way, Gu Changge knew full well.

To explain this, one must go back many years.

Back when they were still in the Yuan Hope Civilization, on Mount Zhongmiao, in order to sign a peace treaty, the Yuan Hope Holy Maiden had secretly sought out Ling Yuling. She had told Ling Yuling that she had glimpsed some of the future within the Mirror of Reincarnation, and thus had formed certain speculations regarding Gu Changge’s identity.

As one of the sages who had fought against the Black Calamity, Ling Yuling also knew the location where the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor—belonging to the Lord of Heaven’s Place—was sealed after the calamity ended. She knew she was no match for Gu Changge, and if he truly was the Lord of Heaven’s Place, she certainly could not let him discover the sealing site of the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor.

To preserve those memories and karmic ties, she deliberately severed them and entrusted them to the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan.

The Holy Maiden of Xiyuan, still uneasy, sealed her own corresponding memories within the Mirror of Reincarnation as well.

Later, after the Peace Pact concluded, Gu Changge foresaw that part of the future. He deliberately left a crack in the barrier at that time, allowing the Holy Maiden of Xiyuan to escape using the Mirror of Reincarnation. From then on, her every move and whereabouts remained under Gu Changge’s watchful eye.

He personally witnessed her losing part of her memory, meeting Gu Xian’er, recuperating and cultivating in seclusion at the Crescent Moon Sect, and later departing for the Nine Heavens.

The Holy Maiden of Xiyuan had always believed that her severe injuries, which had damaged the very source of her divine soul, were the cause of her memory loss. In reality, this was not the case. After she broke through the barrier and escaped to the heavens, the Mirror of Reincarnation, following the method she had previously set in motion, automatically reactivated and sealed away those corresponding memories.

Now, as the Mirror of Reincarnation was taken by him in his capacity as the Ruler of Heaven, those corresponding memories, while the mirror resisted its revival, gradually returned to the minds of the Holy Maiden of Xiyuan and Ling Yuling.

It was just that the Mirror of Reincarnation’s resistance at the time seemed far too feeble, so no living being took notice…

The Eternal Primordial God and Lord Luo exchanged a glance at Ling Yuling, vaguely sensing the truth. Yet, given the current state of affairs, even the being from the True Path Realm who had intervened within the Nine Heavens Realm moments ago remained silent.

Even if the members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance knew the truth, it would serve no purpose.

They had all been deeply shaken moments ago, yet remained unclear as to exactly what means the Vicar of Heaven had employed to intimidate the Nine Heavens.

After all, Gu Changge had remained in the grand hall the entire time, making not a single move. Was the current Ruler of Heaven merely a strand of his consciousness, a projection, or was there some other reason?

"It’s nothing. She’s just recalled some past events; she’ll be fine in a moment," Gu Changge said.

“Sister…”

Inside the grand hall, Ling Yuxian, Ling Yuling’s younger sister, also felt a dull ache in her head, as if something were about to emerge. She and her sister shared a special bond, often able to sense each other’s thoughts and feelings.

Now that her sister had suddenly frozen, with memories churning in her mind, Ling Yuxian, too, found many vague memories surfacing.

Gu Changge gently tapped her with a finger. A series of dazzling silver ripples spread from the center of her forehead, subsequently calming her spirit.

"I... I seem to have gained some memories..." Ling Yuling’s eyes, which had been slightly dazed, suddenly cleared.

She looked at Gu Changge and spoke softly, though her gaze held a complex depth.

Among the memories that had returned were those she had once deliberately severed and handed over to the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan, as well as her experiences as a former sage. Only now was she truly whole.

Had she not been worried that Gu Changge might discover the karmic implications of those memories, she would never have resorted to such a desperate measure.

But now it seemed her concerns and worries had been entirely unnecessary. During the period when she had lost those memories, without them to guide her, she had subconsciously forgotten her former doubts about Gu Changge’s identity.

Now that the Ruler of Heaven has reappeared in the world—regardless of whether there is any mystery behind it—she has already become Gu Changge’s person, standing firmly on his side. This is now a fact.

Given the current Vast Expanse, even if she were to regain that portion of her memory, it would change nothing; even at the height of her power, she was still far from reaching the end of the road.

“It seems you must have recalled something important,” Gu Changge said.

Ling Yuling hesitated for a moment, then nodded slightly. “It concerns the sealing of the former Ruler of Heaven.”

Gu Changge wore an expression of no surprise; he merely nodded, without asking any further questions.

However, the numerous high-ranking members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance present in the hall were all startled, clearly taken aback.

Ling Yuling looked at Gu Changge, thought for a moment, and decided to speak frankly. She said softly, “It concerns the causal threads related to the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor that the Lord of Heaven’s Place once forged…”

“Once the Lord of Heaven’s Successor revives, he will likely reclaim the Eight Desolate Demon Halberd—a weapon once feared across the heavens—as well as the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor.”

“The whereabouts of the Eight Desolations Demon Spear are unknown, but as for the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor, there is a way…”

Upon hearing this, both the Eternal Primordial God and Lord Luo were slightly startled. The gaze they cast upon Ling Yuling carried a hint of inexplicable strangeness, and then they glanced at Gu Changge.

Yet his expression remained calm from start to finish, as if nothing could stir him, and he offered no response.

Ling Yuling seemed to want to say something more, but her red lips moved slightly before she fell silent.

The senior members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance did not linger long afterward, intending to take their leave. Before departing, however, someone inquired about the conflict between the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

After all, the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty had now submitted to the Heaven Slaying Alliance and was considered one of its vassal powers. With this sudden outbreak of war against the Ancient Hidden Civilization, they needed to know if assistance should be sent.

Many of the high-ranking officials gathered here had originally come specifically for this matter. However, the sudden revival of the Lord of Heaven had caused everyone’s attention to shift toward the Nine Heavens, causing them to overlook the fact that a major battle was actually raging in the Ancient Hidden Civilization as well.

But compared to the commotion outside the Nine Heavens, that conflict could only be considered a minor skirmish.

“Let’s wait and see.” Gu Changge merely waved his hand dismissively.

Soon, only Ling Yuling remained in the grand hall. She hadn’t left; she simply stood with her back to Gu Changge, head bowed, seemingly lost in a thousand thoughts. Her usually calm and serene face now betrayed a mix of melancholy and inner turmoil.

Gu Changge, his hands clasped behind his back, turned to look at her and said, “If you have something to say, just say it. You’ve been with me for quite some time now, and over the years, you’ve devoted a great deal of effort and energy to the expansion and development of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.”

“You don’t need to be so evasive or hesitant in front of me. That’s not like you.”

Ling Yuling looked at him in a daze; her mind seemed to calm down at his words. She tucked a few strands of black hair that had fallen near her ears behind her ear and said softly, “Then… what should I call you?”
