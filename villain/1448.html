"The Age of the False Heavens..."

The Eternal Primordial God fell silent for a moment. A terrifying aura surged within his eyes, but it quickly subsided. With a hint of helplessness, he said, "Tell me, what exactly are those three beings? Why is even the true Way of Heaven powerless against them..."

An anomaly beyond the Way of Heaven?

Or beings born by latching onto and occupying the Way of Heaven?

In any case, they cannot be like those primordial beings of old—creatures born from the chaos, the very first beings in this universe.

The existence of the Three Ancestors is beyond investigation, beyond description, and beyond any attempt at deduction or reconstruction.

Lord Luo’s expression changed instantly. He looked up at this world and, seeing no signs of disturbance, whispered, “Be careful with your words. Heaven has its eyes and ears; across the Vast Expanse of realms and myriad worlds, nothing escapes its scrutiny. You’d best seal your five senses and keep these thoughts to yourself.”

“You remain as cautious as ever.”

The Eternal Primordial God laughed, “In the Vast Expanse of the outer realms today, even the Heaven Slaying Alliance has emerged. Why should you worry that these words might be detected by them? This age is already vastly different from the past. I suspect they have long ceased to observe the Vast Expanse as it is now; only when they occasionally awaken do they cast their gaze upon it.The more cautious you are, the more timid you become, and the less you dare to act. The Luo I once knew was not like this at all."

Lord Luo shook his head. “You don’t understand. You have no idea how terrifying They are…”

“If an ant bites you, you might not even feel the pain. But in their eyes, the Vast Expanse is nothing but ants. No matter how powerful an ant may be, it is still an ant—one that can be crushed with a single foot…”

“You’re still so pessimistic. If that were truly the case, why did so many escape the Cataclysm of Creation and survive?” The Eternal Primordial God felt that Lord Luo had lain dormant and hidden for too long, eroding all his sharpness and will, which was why he had become so pessimistic.

"Such is the way of fortune. If you crush a swarm of insects with a single palm, and a few happen to slip through your fingers, would you even care?" Lord Luo said indifferently.

"So what if they’re mere ants? Many ants can bring down an elephant," the Eternal Primordial God retorted with disdain.

Upon hearing this, Lord Luo saw no point in arguing further; their philosophies were too different, which was why they had parted ways in the first place.

The Eternal Primordial God fell silent for a moment, but then, as if a thought occurred to him, he asked, “You once followed the Lord of Heaven’s Mandate. Who exactly is that Lord of Heaven’s Mandate? Is his true form in the Realm of Reality, entangled with those three?”

This was, in fact, the question he most wanted to ask Lord Luo during this visit.

In the current Vast Expanse and uncertain situation, it seemed only the Heaven Slaying Alliance, the Righteous Unity Alliance, and the Dark Clans were involved, but who knew how many eyes were watching from the shadows.

Vaguely, he even sensed a vast net being woven and cast down; the master behind it might close in at any moment once they met their end.

This sense of foreboding unsettled him, which was why he had decided to seek out his old friend.

Lord Luo glanced at him and shook his head once more. “The Ruler of Heaven is indeed far more powerful than anyone could imagine, but even his might cannot touch that Realm of Truth. The Realm of Truth cannot be affected by the forces of the Vast Expanse—it is simply impossible.”

“I do not know his origins either. Perhaps he is an anomaly, or perhaps, like us, a being who once lived amidst the Chaos.”

“Or perhaps he is the creation of some figure from the Primordial Era—or even an alias…”

"But he is indeed very strong—so powerful that since the dawn of the Post-Heaven Era, it’s nearly impossible to find anyone who can match him. The reason I chose to follow him back then was actually to see if he could accomplish what none of us could."

Before the creation of the heavens, there was nothing but chaos. Even they themselves did not know how many races of beings were born and nurtured within it.

It seems that figures who left a profound mark in past eras were, to a greater or lesser extent, connected to the Age of Creation. They might have been incarnations of a certain being, or perhaps merely fleeting aliases.

It wasn’t until after the creation of the heavens—that is, after the Vast Expanse gave birth to all things, the so-called “Post-Heaven” era—and the emergence of the “Pseudo-Heaven Era” that things changed.

Those powerful figures who once appeared—those who ushered in a new era or ventured to the very limits of existence—were, to a greater or lesser extent, the handiwork of those beings from the Age of Creation.

For instance, the Dream-Ashes Era, which plunged the myriad realms of the Vast Expanse into endless nightmares;the Dream Lord, who gave birth to illusory forms; the Tathagata Buddha, who established the Buddhist and Daoist systems, abolished all other teachings, and exalted Buddhist arts alone; the Three Purities Daoist Lord, who rode a blue ox, spread purple qi across the celestial realms, established the Ten Great Daoist True Abodes, and left behind the legacy of Daoist teachings; the Great Ro Daoist Lord, who created the Wuji Immortal Realm, practiced the Great Ro Arts, and had disciples throughout all worlds; as well as the pioneers of the Divine Dao Civilization—there are simply too many……

In fact, when we examine the eras that followed the birth of the Vast Expanse, we always find that during certain periods, a few towering figures emerge, amplifying the momentum of that age to infinite proportions.

Some of these figures truly emerged in response to the times, born in accordance with the cosmic cycles spanning hundreds, thousands, or even tens of thousands of eras. From the moment they were born, they stirred boundless turmoil and rapidly ascended to the pinnacle.

Yet due to various unforeseen circumstances, they eventually perish or meet an untimely demise; very few, indeed, meet a truly peaceful end. Of course, there are also those who vanish without a trace…

Some of these entities are indeed the handiwork and strategic arrangements of those deeply hidden beings from the Primordial Era.

Even the Lord of Luo once, for a time in the past, attempted to chart a path for future generations, leaving behind legacies and opportunities. Yet he later realized that such efforts were merely a drop in the bucket, and so he gradually abandoned them.

Since its birth, the Vast Expanse has existed for an immeasurably long time—so long that no one can truly count the years.

Countless universes, boundless spacetime—how many small worlds, void spaces, unknown dimensions, and mysterious voids do they encompass? The number of beings hidden within them is actually quite large.

Compared to other eras, the Age of Black Calamity was simply darker and more despairing. Its influence remains profound to this day, unlike certain eras of the past that have truly vanished and been buried in the torrent of time, forgotten by all.

"Exercising the authority of Heaven is not something the beings of later ages would do..."

The Eternal Primordial God sighed, “Then do you believe the demise of the Age of Black Calamity, and the sealing of the Lord of Heaven’s Mandate, was the will of Heaven, the work of human hands, or merely a calculated scheme?”

Lord Luo fell into deep thought; in truth, even he remained unsure to this day whether that great war was the result of human agency or divine will.

The emergence of the First Civilization’s Sacred Treasure had indeed taken everyone by surprise.

If it were truly divine intervention, then the Lord of Heaven’s Successor surely wouldn’t have been sealed away so easily.

Therefore, he preferred to believe that it was, in fact, a human scheme…

Just as the Heavenly Host had always believed, the Ruler of Heaven might one day break through the seal once more and awaken.

"You came here because you wanted to ask me whether the Ruler of Heaven and the current leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance are the same person, isn’t that right?" Lord Luo looked at the Eternal Primordial God.

“The Vast Expanse of the present has been stirred into utter turbidity by him; this is forcing many to take the plunge,” the Eternal Primordial God nodded.

There are certainly no shortage of figures like Lord Luo and the Eternal Primordial God who have chosen to feign death to evade detection and conceal all traces of their karmic aura.

Others are even plotting the grand scheme, biding their time and lying low, waiting for the day to “rise up in rebellion.”

Now, with the emergence of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, it seems they intend to overturn the Vast Expanse table, forcing everyone to take the stage.

The Eternal Primordial God, of course, did not care. His goals differed from those of certain others; he acted solely for himself, seeking nothing else. The murkier the waters of the Vast Expanse became, the more advantageous it was for him.

"I wasn’t sure just now, but after seeing her, I think I understand."

Lord Luo suddenly turned to look at Luo Xiangjun, who stood behind the Eternal Primordial God. A distinct shadow of gloom flashed across his silver-bright eyes.

Although Luo Xiangjun hadn’t meant to eavesdrop on their conversation, the way they spoke so openly and without any regard for her presence meant she couldn’t help but overhear some of it.

Yet at this moment, she remained utterly composed, her face a mask of shock and bewilderment. Facing the gaze of her distant ancestor, she felt as though her blood were freezing, a metallic taste rising in her throat, and her face turning pale.

“Luo, what is the meaning of this?” The Eternal Primordial God was equally bewildered and puzzled, clearly taken by surprise.

After all, Luo Xiangjun was a descendant of the Lord of Luo, and it was because of her that he had been able to find his way here.

"She carries the coordinates..."

Lord Luo’s expression darkened. It wasn’t until this moment, when his gaze fell upon Luo Xiangjun, that he sensed something amiss.

Earlier, he had simply regarded her as a descendant of his bloodline and hadn’t given it a second thought. Reuniting with an old friend, he had inevitably let his guard down—but he never expected to be outmaneuvered like this…

"What do you mean?"

"Coordinates? How come I’ve never known about this?"

The Eternal Primordial God’s expression also shifted slightly, then grew grim.

He looked at Luo Xiangjun. His eyes, once shimmering with golden light, suddenly grew cold and indifferent, brimming with murderous intent.

"Pfft..."

Facing the oppressive pressure from these two terrifying beings, even Luo Xiangjun—despite having survived six Celestial Waning Tribulations—felt as though her body was about to explode. A sweet taste filled her mouth, and she spat out a mouthful of fresh blood.

And just as she spat out the blood, her expression frozen in shock and bewilderment, a jade pendant deep within her sleeve suddenly trembled slightly, then split open. A beam of clear light burst forth, enveloping her.

"What is this..."

Luo Xiangjun herself was deeply shocked. Though this clear light appeared feeble, it managed to neutralize the terrifying pressure bearing down on her, even shielding her for a brief moment.

She hurriedly retrieved the jade pendant, then froze in place. The scene in her mind froze at the moment she had agreed to Gu Changge’s request to retrieve the Eternal Treasure while in the Immortal Spirit Civilization.

Later, unable to break the seals within, she had returned to seek out Gu Changge. At the time, Gu Changge hadn’t suspected her; instead, he had casually handed her some artifacts, telling her that if she couldn’t open the treasure, she could use those items.

At the time, Luo Xiangjun had merely sensed that his expression held a deeper meaning, but she hadn’t given it much thought.

Who could have imagined that, at this very moment, they would be used here…

"When exactly did this power of causality become attached to her? Who left it there?"

The Eternal Primordial God’s expression also shifted dramatically, filled with shock and disbelief. He had never even considered the possibility that Luo Xiangjun might bear a causal coordinate—when had it been planted upon her?

Since he had taken up residence within Luo Xiangjun and guided her to the lineage of the Eternal Gods, it was simply impossible for anyone to have tampered with her during that time—he would have detected it immediately.

Could it have been before Luo Xiangjun went to the secret cache he had left behind…

A chill suddenly swept over the Eternal Primordial God’s entire body. He had already figured out who had left it—and he had even been conversing with the Lord of Luo just moments ago.

"We shouldn’t linger here. We must leave immediately."

Lord Luo’s expression darkened. Without hesitation, he turned to leave. The entire world grew dim, as if it were being peeled away—this realm was his true form, and naturally, he intended to take it all with him.

"I’ve finally got a fish on the hook; how could I possibly let you escape?"

In the Ethereal Sanctuary, aboard the ancient warship that had just swept through, Gu Changge sat cross-legged in the courtyard.

His gaze was calm and detached. The moment the coordinates of cause and effect materialized, he extended a massive hand, instantly piercing the boundless spacetime before him, breaking free from all the constraints of the laws of the universe, and reached out to seize the Luo Clan’s territory.

"Who is that?"

In the Luo Clan’s territory, the stars trembled and the earth cracked. A terrifying force, as if the heavens were crashing down, left all the clan members in a state of utter terror and horror.

All they could see was a massive hand suddenly reaching out from the endless void, powerful and overwhelming, obliterating everything in its path. The mountains and land before them exploded. Some, enraged, charged forward, but before they could even make contact, they disintegrated into dust mid-air.

Among them were even several ancient clan members who had survived six Celestial Waning Tribulations. They charged forward, yet their efforts proved futile; they were crushed by that massive hand, turning into a mist of blood that filled the sky, leaving behind only a few strands of primordial essence resembling their souls.

"What is this..."

The Grand Elder of the Luo Clan wore an expression of shock and disbelief. As a being who had survived eight Celestial Waning Tribulations, he now felt as though a mortal stood before a deity. A terrifying chill swept over his entire body, paralyzing him in place.

The entire secluded realm crumbled and disintegrated as the giant hand descended, and the mountain behind the Luo Clan’s tomb began to tremble.

All members of the Luo Clan trembled in utter terror, watching as the giant hand reached down toward them. Every protective array and seal left behind was being eroded and shattered. The mountain behind the tomb split open, and a half-opened coffin was torn from the earth. Terrifying energy poured out, a chaotic mist,shrouding everything in a haze. It seemed as though some entity was struggling to break free, yet it was clamped fast by that massive hand, unable to move.

"That is the aura of our distant ancestor..."

All the members of the Luo Clan were stunned, unable to believe their eyes.

The sky grew hazy, and the entire secluded realm seemed on the verge of shattering under the pressure. Only as the massive hand withdrew did that terrifying aura gradually dissipate.

All the members of the Luo clan stared blankly at the collapsed mausoleum on the back mountain. The Ancestor’s tombstone had long since been shattered, and inside, there was nothing—not even the coffin remained.

"Could the Ancestor still be alive?"

“But… but how could the invincible Ancestor have been snatched away like that?”

“What on earth was that just now?”

Chaos reigned throughout the Luo clan’s territory. Every clan member was utterly horrified, and for a long time afterward, they could not calm their nerves.
