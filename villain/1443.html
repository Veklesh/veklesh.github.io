"Someone with the surname Gu?"

Upon hearing this, the elders of the Crescent Moon Sect all froze, then exchanged glances, seeing only confusion and bewilderment in each other’s eyes.

The Gu surname?

That surname was quite rare, and among the disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect, they had never heard of anyone bearing the surname Gu.

Had they come to the wrong person, or the wrong place?

The many cultivators and beings on the main street, upon hearing this question, were equally surprised and perplexed, unable to fathom the meaning.

"Could it be that clan?"

However, some very elderly cultivators had a vague inkling of what was happening. As they looked at this group of young men and women of extraordinary lineage, their eyes filled with shock and disbelief.

Ji Yu and his Uncle Lan stood beside the Great Sage Ziwei, watching the scene unfold. One wore a grim expression, while the other looked incredibly grave.

One of them was the disciple of the Eternal Dao Lord, the current Patriarch of the Futiang Sect in the Ninth Heaven, and a true descendant of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court—a figure of exceptional lineage.

The other, however, was a genuine Ancestral Dao Realm cultivator—a figure whom even the Eternal Life Dao Lord would treat with great deference.

Consequently, their understanding of the hidden affairs of the Nine Heavens far surpassed that of ordinary residents, which was why they sensed that something was amiss...

The Gu clan rarely ventured into the Nine Heavens; they maintained an aloof stance, almost as if they took no interest in the affairs of the outside world.

Why would they suddenly send out a clan member, specifically to the First Heaven, to search for someone of the Gu clan?

And just as they were bringing the members of the Crescent Moon Sect to the Nine Heavens?

This was certainly no coincidence. Even Uncle Lan, with his Ancestral Dao Realm cultivation, felt a slight pang of unease when facing that smiling old man.

His strength was undoubtedly superior to Uncle Lan’s.

For such an ancient figure—one who had likely survived eight Celestial Waning Tribulations—to personally appear in search of someone was a level of treatment and importance that was simply unbelievable.

"An ordinary member of the Gu clan would never receive such treatment," Uncle Lan said, his gaze heavy, a vague suspicion forming in his mind.

Ji Yu clenched his fist and whispered, “Perhaps it has something to do with that woman in white that Gu Xian’er brought back. Isn’t her strength so profound that even you, Uncle Lan, cannot fathom it?”

Uncle Lan remained silent upon hearing this, merely shaking his head.

The Purple薇 Great Sage, clad in a purple robe, was equally deep in thought. “Yesterday, for a fleeting moment, it seemed as though some incredibly powerful being had reawakened. Its aura swept across the Nine Heavens, jolting me awake from my seclusion and leaving me drenched in cold sweat. Could this be related?”

"The outside world is currently in a time of turmoil; even the Nine Heavens are no longer at peace. The fact that this clan has begun to stir signifies that a major upheaval is about to occur."

Though it appears that each of the Nine Heavens has its own ruler, many beings of immense longevity know that this is merely a facade.

The waters of each heaven run deep; no one can be certain that a secluded figure—a true dragon or qilin—might not be hiding within some ancient cave or ruin, or amidst the mountains, forests, and fields.

Some deeply hidden lineages and even entire clans never make a public appearance in the mortal world.

Take, for example, the Patriarch of the Futiang Sect, the Eternal Life Dao Lord. The Purple Star Great Sage was well aware that the Eternal Life Dao Lord had emerged from some extremely secret and ancient sect within the Nine Heavens...

He knew nothing of that sect’s name, its foundations, or its origins; the only thing he knew was that it seemed to be connected to “Heaven.”

As for the clan before him, its origins and heritage far surpassed even that sect; in fact, in certain parts of the Nine Heavens, one could faintly hear mythical rumors about them.

As the ruler of the First Heavenly Realm, the Great Sage Ziwei didn’t even have the standing to be looked at directly.

In front of the inn, several elders of the Crescent Moon Sect held their breath and focused their minds, daring not to let their guard down. They then honestly reported their current situation: there was no one with the surname Gu among either the disciples or the elders.

"That’s impossible. For the first time in all these years, outsiders have truly arrived in the Nine Heavens, and they’re all here. My senses couldn’t possibly be wrong."

Hearing this, the group of young men and women—each radiating an extraordinary aura and divine light—could not help but shake their heads.

One of them, in particular, had a piercing gaze with brilliant divine light flickering within. He scrutinized the Crescent Moon Sect elders before him, intent on determining whether they were lying.

Cold sweat beaded on the foreheads of the Crescent Moon Sect elders. Although they sensed that the young men and women before them had not yet reached the Dao Realm, they still felt considerable pressure under their scrutiny.

“Do not be rude.”

The elderly man in the gray-white robe waved his hand, casting a casual glance at the young men and women beside him. He then turned to the group of Crescent Moon Sect elders and said with a friendly smile, “We mean no harm. We merely sensed the bloodline of the Gu Clan here, so we have come to inquire.”

At that moment, the disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect who had been inside the inn also emerged.

Gu Xian’er walked slowly, trailing behind Lan Xin and the others. Her expression betrayed a hint of confusion and puzzlement.

In fact, while she had been inside the inn earlier, she had heard the commotion outside and realized these people were searching for someone with the surname Gu.

While at the Crescent Moon Sect, she had always gone by the alias Gu Xian’er and had never revealed her real name.

Including Lan Xin, Ji Yu, and the others, they had always believed her name was Gu Xian’er.

So she truly couldn’t understand why, having just arrived in the Nine Heavens, she was already being sought out.

Could there be another Gu family here?

Due to her experiences as a child, Gu Xian’er had actually had very little contact with the Gu family of the Prosperous Dao True Realm; she rarely visited them and spent most of her time in Taocun.

Naturally, she had never learned from those elders that the Gu family of the Prosperous Dao True Realm was, in fact, merely a branch of the Vast Expanse of the Gu clan.

At this moment, Gu Xian’er was also hesitating, wondering whether to reveal her true identity and background.

In fact, if there were a Gu family in the Nine Heavens as well, her current situation would be much better.

But why had they gone to such great lengths to find her? That was what puzzled her.

“May I ask your name, young lady?”

Just as Gu Xian’er stepped out of the inn, the elderly man in the gray-white robe—who had been speaking politely with several elders of the Crescent Moon Sect—immediately turned his gaze toward her.

As if sensing something, his slightly stooped frame trembled, and his clear eyes betrayed his unhidden excitement.

However, to avoid drawing unnecessary attention and trouble, he forced himself to suppress his inner excitement and asked as gently as possible.

Hearing this, the crowd of cultivators and sentient beings lining the street all turned to look.

Many were instantly captivated, struck by a sense of awe.

This young girl in white had features as delicate as a painting and a demeanor as ethereal as a fairy. Her clear, flawless eyes and porcelain-like skin made her strikingly beautiful, radiating an otherworldly aura as luminous and pure as jade.

"My name is Gu Xian'er. I believe I am the person of the Gu surname you have been seeking, Senior."

A whirlwind of thoughts raced through Gu Xian’er’s mind, but she did not hesitate; she simply stated her true name.

In a place like the Nine Heavens, she suspected that even if she tried her hardest to conceal her true name, it would eventually be discovered.

Moreover, the elderly man before her exuded a warm and approachable aura, causing her to instinctively trust him and believe he harbored no ill will.

The moment they heard Gu Xian’er’s words, the elders and disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect all froze in stunned silence.

Lan Xin, too, looked at Gu Xian’er in astonishment.

"Gu Xian'er? Gu Xian'er?"

“I should have realized it sooner. This person has been hiding in the Crescent Moon Sect all along, plotting to seize the Jutian Sect’s legacy—of course they wouldn’t reveal their true name.”

Ji Yu’s eyes darkened, his fists clenched tightly, filled with bitter resentment.

In fact, just moments ago, he had harbored a sliver of hope, speculating that the person of the Gu clan the Gu family was searching for might be that amnesiac woman in white.

"Young Master, regarding that matter of the legacy, we need to consider it carefully."

“We must avoid provoking the Gu Clan at all costs. If we can avoid entangling ourselves in karmic debts, we must do so.” At his side, Uncle Lan wore a grave expression. Sighing inwardly, he transmitted his voice.

As a being at the Ancestral Dao Realm, he was well aware of the clan’s terrifying power.

The fact that they had gone to such great lengths to find Gu Xian’er spoke volumes about how much they valued her. Regardless of what made Gu Xian’er special, she was not someone they could take on at this stage.

Ji Yu took a deep breath, forcing himself to calm down, and replied, “Uncle Lan, I understand. I won’t overreach myself or throw myself against a wall.”

“I knew I was right. This aura is indeed the purest bloodline of the Gu family.”

The elderly man in the gray-white robe looked at Gu Xian’er, smiled faintly, and said in an incredibly gentle tone, “You must be from a collateral branch of our Gu family in the outside world. You need not worry; I am the Fifth Patriarch of the Gu family today. If you have met your ancestors from the outside world, they should know of me.”

Gu Xian’er had no mind to pay attention to the astonished and shocked expressions of the Crescent Moon Sect members. Upon hearing this, she merely shook her head and said, “I have never met my ancestor. My arrival in the Nine Heavens was merely a coincidence.”

The Fifth Patriarch of the Gu Clan nodded, still smiling. “It matters not. Whether it be the Gu Clan of the outside world or the Gu Clan of the Nine Heavens, we are all branches of the same Gu lineage. The same blood flows through our veins.”

The reappearance of the Gu Clan’s distant ancestor’s true spirit was a matter of great significance. Although the entire Gu Clan had been shaken by the news, only a few high-ranking members were truly aware of it at present.

Naturally, it would be best to avoid any unnecessary complications before bringing Gu Xian’er back to the Gu family.

That was why the Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan had not revealed much.

"Since ancient times, our Gu family has strictly adhered to our clan rules and placed immense importance on every clan member. Therefore, you need not worry unduly. Once we return to the Gu family, I will answer all your questions." The Fifth Ancestor of the Gu family spoke gently, continuing as if he knew exactly what was on Gu Xian’er’s mind at that moment.

Gu Xian’er nodded thoughtfully.

In the Nine Heavens, if she had a power like the Gu Clan backing her, her situation would be much better.

At the very least, Ji Yu would likely no longer have his sights set on the inheritance within her.

The Gu family had mobilized such a large force to search for her; there must be a reason. They would certainly find a way to bring her back to the Gu family.

So for now, the best course of action was to agree to the Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan standing before her and follow him back to the Gu Clan.

The Fifth Patriarch of the Gu Clan did not rush Gu Xian’er, giving her ample time to consider her options.

Many elders and disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect had not yet recovered from their shock and disbelief.

Although they didn’t fully understand what the Gu family meant within the Nine Heavens, they could vaguely sense that its background was absolutely terrifying.

Gu Xian’er—no, Gu Xian’er actually had such a connection.

As for Gu Xian’er concealing her real name, few actually paid it much mind.

After all, Gu Xian’er had originally been brought to the Crescent Moon Sect by that mad old woman, and only later became a disciple of the sect—a process that had largely been a series of fortuitous coincidences.

Lan Xin, however, was genuinely happy for Gu Xian’er. With such a background, Ji Yu surely wouldn’t dare to do anything to her…

In the end, Gu Xian’er didn’t hesitate for long and agreed to return to the Gu family with the Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan, on the condition that she could bring Lan Xin, Qing Nan, and the others with her.

As for the elders and disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect, her relationship with them was neither particularly good nor bad; they were merely fellow disciples in name.

She wasn’t so self-righteous as to worry about their future circumstances; she had seen right through their true colors along the way.

The Fifth Patriarch of the Gu family naturally wouldn’t refuse Gu Xian’er’s request; he agreed with a smile and assured her that Lan Xin and the others would be treated as honored guests at the Gu residence.

Qing Nan had initially intended to decline Gu Xian’er’s offer, as her understanding of the Nine Heavens Realm was actually quite limited.

The Gu family’s waters were clearly deep, and she didn’t want to take any risks, fearing she might slip up and end up in trouble.

Although she had left various traces and auras along the way that went unnoticed by Gu Xian’er and the others, the Grave Keeper had still caught on to something.

Who knew if there might be even more powerful and mysterious beings within the Gu family than the tomb guardian?

However, considering that the legacy of the Jutian Sect was still in Gu Xian’er’s hands, staying in the Nine Heavens alone would only be a waste of time.

With that time, she could actually accomplish much more in the outside world.

If the Gu family were to detect any unusual activity on her part, she could always make a forced escape. Although the Nine Heavens Realm and the Nightmare Demon Realm are separated by an endless expanse of time and space, as long as she taps into the power within the Coffin of the Underworld, she could easily open a spatial passage and depart safely—there should be no problem with that.

The Gu clan arrived quickly and departed just as swiftly.

The elders and disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect were left feeling a bit dazed and regretful. If only they had befriended Gu Xian’er sooner, perhaps the Gu Clan would have taken them along today as well…

Now, with Gu Xian’er and the others gone, Ji Yu, left behind, wore an exceptionally grim expression. Though he felt a thousand kinds of resentment, in the end, he could only force himself to suppress it.

Gu Xian’er had deliberately taken Lan Xin with her, precisely to deny him the chance to lay a hand on her.

As a result, all his scheming and planning over the years would come to nothing.

"First, she’s from the Heaven Slaying Alliance, and now she’s connected to the Gu clan..."

"What other identity does she possess? What gives her the right for that mad old woman to choose her and pass on that legacy to her?" A flicker of madness and malice flashed in Ji Yu’s eyes.

The news of a member of the Gu Clan appearing in the First Heaven had undoubtedly spread rapidly throughout the Nine Heavens, causing a massive stir and upheaval.

The fact that an ancient being claiming to be the Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan had personally descended to search for her was proof enough of the clan’s gravity regarding this matter.

This woman named Gu Xian’er soon drew the attention of various clans and factions across the Nine Heavens, sparking a flurry of speculation and discussion.

However, perhaps due to the Gu Clan’s excessive mystery and aloofness, such speculation and attention did not last long.

It was as if an invisible force had suppressed it; even the memories of many cultivators and sentient beings who had witnessed Gu Xian’er’s true appearance outside the inn that day began to fade.

Even the ruler of the First Heaven, the Great Sage Ziwei, was no exception. Upon realizing there was a problem with his memory, his face turned pale. He then acted decisively, cutting away all memories related to that day with his own hand, leaving no karmic ties behind.

The fact that merely witnessing her true form entailed such immense karmic consequences meant that if he were carelessly drawn into it, all his years of arduous cultivation would likely go down the drain.

In the Nine Heavens, within the grand hall of the Futiang Sect.

Ji Yu stood with a dejected expression behind the Eternal Dao Lord, who was clad in a Da Luo Dao robe, recounting to him the many experiences and events that had occurred during his time at the Crescent Moon Sect.

"I know you feel resentful, but do not meddle in the affairs of the Gu Clan. Though you are of the legitimate bloodline of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court, I have recently deduced that a figure resembling a former princess of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court has seemingly emerged in the Vast Expanse."

“With your current strength, the task of reviving the Yao Guang Ancestral Court is truly a long and arduous journey. For now, you must devote yourself to cultivation; breaking through to the Dao Realm as soon as possible is the priority.”

"Your strength is simply too weak. I don’t know why that elder asked me to teach you so diligently, but never mind. Once your cultivation breaks through to the Dao Realm, I will send you over there. Do your best to catch up as soon as possible."

The Eternal Dao Lord shook his head slightly. In his deep, piercing eyes, a multitude of scenes unfolded, as if he were beholding countless future vistas.
