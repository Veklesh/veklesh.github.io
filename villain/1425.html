Of course, when Gu Changge sent Jing Tianyuan into the Nightmare Realm, he didn’t intend for him to enter openly; it was enough for him to sneak in covertly.

With the Heavenly Hosts about to face an unprecedented crisis, Gu Changge, after much deliberation, decided to give Wuyue a heads-up.

Given his current status, he couldn’t meet Wu Yue face-to-face.

That is why he turned to Jing Tianyuan, instructing him to take this item to the Nightmare Demon Realm.

Knowing Wu Yue as he did, Gu Changge was certain she would grasp his intentions.

"But perhaps I’m overthinking this. So many years have passed; Wu Yue must have grown and improved. They say ‘out of sight, out of mind,’ but she’s different…” Gu Changge’s gaze held a touch of emotion.

His thoughts swirled. He still remembered the day he found Wuyue—she had been nothing more than a frail, sickly little beast, abandoned by her own demon clan. She lay crouched beneath the barren, lifeless ice plains, calmly awaiting her fate as prey for other beasts.

That day, snow and wind swirled through the sky, and the world was blanketed in pristine white. All around the ice plain, many beasts had been drawn by the scent, circling like wolves, their eyes fixed on the Nightmare Beast, waiting for it to draw its last breath.

The Nightmare Clan is a race that feeds on nightmares; they possess naturally powerful demonic energy and rarely stray from their clan’s realm to fall into the Demon Realm.

To ordinary beasts, however, even an unremarkable young Nightmare Beast possessed terrifying power.

As its breath grew fainter, the demonic power emanating from its body increased, resembling a drifting purple mist—exquisite and dreamlike—enveloping the icy sky.

Just as its life had been—born of the heavens and earth, and returning to them upon death.

The wind and snow grew fiercer, and the gathering of demon beasts grew ever larger.

All were waiting for this feast to begin.

Gu Changge didn’t know what emotion had driven him at that moment. Perhaps it was seeing the strangely calm gaze of the little beast awaiting death as he looked down, or perhaps it was a hint of pity and curiosity.

Perhaps he didn’t understand why life was so fragile, or perhaps he simply found it intriguing.

So he walked over, picked it up, and said, “From now on, you’ll come with me…”

His thoughts returned to the present. Gu Changge watched as Jing Tianyuan vanished, then sensed something in the air, which came as a bit of a surprise to him.

“Ming Kong has emerged from seclusion?”

“And he’s successfully broken through to the Dao Realm.”

A faint smile touched his lips.

He wasn’t the least bit surprised by Yue Mingkong’s rapid progress.

For long before this, Gu Changge had already sensed the unique qualities within Yue Mingkong’s body. Moreover, some of the profound subtleties within the Great Dream Return Immortal Art she had created were so intricate that even beings at the Dao Realm found them difficult to fathom.

After all, that was merely a Daoist technique she had created within the realm of Immortality.

However, Gu Changge did not delve deeper into it nor press for answers, for in his view, since the two had already married, they must first learn to respect and trust one another.

In the past, Gu Changge had never chosen to trust anyone of his own accord, but this time, he wanted to try—starting with the person closest to him.

Of course, Gu Changge also knew that Yue Mingkong would certainly tell him everything.

Soon, ripples rippled through the space before him. Gu Changge took a step forward and vanished in an instant.

Meanwhile, at the Heaven Slaying Alliance, on Zixiao Mountain.

“It’s been so long, yet it seems the Lord of Zixiao Mountain still hasn’t given us a satisfactory answer. Do you really think that by dragging this out, everything will be fine?”

In the center of the grand hall, a swirling mass of purple mist gathered and dispersed, drifting erratically.

A terrifying and eerie aura permeated the air, as if countless demonic shadows stood there.

The voice that emerged from within was cold and stern, radiating a chill that seemed to freeze the entire expanse of time and space.

Cold sweat beaded on Zi Wanhe’s face as he clasped his hands and said, “Ladies and gentlemen, it is not that I am deliberately stalling, but it is truly difficult to track down Dao Chang’s whereabouts. In this vast and boundless expanse, across countless realms of time and space, there is not a single domain or faction named Dao Chang. It is as if a terrifying force has erased those two characters from the entire cosmos.”

“The Dream Ghost Clan possesses vast supernatural powers and is capable of anything. If even you cannot find Dao Chang’s whereabouts, how could I possibly find him?”

He was inwardly lamenting his plight.

He shouldn’t have signed that so-called deal with the Dream Ghost Clan in the first place. Not only was he bound by their constraints, but he also lived in constant fear.

If even the Dream Ghost Clan couldn’t find Dao Chang, what made him think he could?

"The prophecy clearly states that Daochang is connected to the Heaven Slaying Alliance. You’re a high-ranking member of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, yet you can’t even uncover such information."

A cold, almost angry voice emerged from within the purple mist.

Zi Wanhe was startled. The Dream Ghost Clan hadn’t told him a thing earlier—how was he supposed to know Dao Chang was connected to the Heaven Slaying Alliance?

Could it be that they wanted him to seek guidance from the Alliance Master?

At that moment, the gaze emanating from the purple mist seemed to sense Zi Wanhe’s thoughts. It spoke coldly, “Lord of Zixiao Mountain, this is the last chance we’re giving you. If you still fail to deliver, don’t blame us for breaking the contract and fulfilling the transaction by our own means.”

As the words fell, a faint purple symbol on Zi Wanhe’s forehead seemed to come to life, glowing brilliantly and crystal-clear. It then began to wriggle incessantly, as if intent on burrowing into his mind.

A look of terror and agony instantly flashed across Zi Wanhe’s face.

Even for someone like him, who had survived eight Celestial Waning Tribulations, this terrifying agony was nearly unbearable. Cold sweat poured down his face, quickly soaking his robes.

If he allowed this symbol to burrow into his sea of consciousness, who knew what would happen.

The Dream Ghost clan appeared as swiftly as they vanished, leaving Zi Wanhe no chance to resist, and soon they had disappeared from the scene.

Zi Wanhe had no idea how the Dream Ghost Clan had managed to arrive at the Heaven Slaying Alliance so silently.

Could it be that they truly dwell within the dreams of all living beings?

"Such tactics are truly tyrannical and utterly despicable. If only I’d been more cautious during the transaction and refused to sign the contract, I wouldn’t have to suffer these constraints now." Zi Wanhe’s face darkened with frustration and humiliation.

He, a being who had survived eight Celestial Waning Tribulations and once looked down upon the vast realms, had never before been forced to grovel and suffer such torment.

And all of this was simply because he had signed a contractual agreement with the other party, leaving his very life in their hands.

“The ancient texts record that there once was an era called the Dream Ashes Era, and the Dream Ghost Clan is believed to be connected to that era…”

"The one who ushered in the Dream Ashes Era caused all sentient beings in the vast cosmos to live within a dream. Even those at the Ancestral Dao Realm could not distinguish between illusion and reality. If the Dream Ghost Clan is truly connected to that figure, possessing such abilities would come as no surprise. I should never have underestimated the Dream Ghost Clan back then..."

His expression shifted between light and shadow, tinged with regret. If he had known this sooner, perhaps he would have had a way to deal with it.

Alas, by the time he came to understand it, it was already too late.

Subsequently, Zi Wanhe summoned several elders of Zixiao Mountain, gave them some instructions, and then transformed into a beam of divine light, departing from Zixiao Mountain.

He had no idea whether Gu Changge was currently at the Heaven Slaying Alliance. With the date set for the Immortal Dao Alliance’s invitation to attend the ceremony fast approaching, he was completely unaware of which figures within the Heaven Slaying Alliance would be required to attend, nor what their stance on the matter was.

In recent days, numerous high-ranking members of the Righteous Unity Alliance had already set out, bearing congratulatory gifts.

Or could it be that the Heaven Slaying Alliance actually had other plans?

"Never mind, what do these matters have to do with me? My immediate priority now is to preserve my cultivation first and foremost." Zi Wanhe shook his head and sighed.

Because of the Dream Ghost Clan’s affairs, he was currently swamped with work and had no energy left to think about the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

He didn’t even have time to accumulate battle merits to exchange for those divine artifacts in the Hall of Battle Merits that he so coveted.

If he couldn’t resolve this deal with the Dream Ghost Clan, even if he didn’t die, he’d be left battered and bruised—and might even lose his cultivation.

“Come to think of it, why exactly is the Dream Ghost Clan searching for Daochang? Is there something in Daochang they’re looking for?” Zi Wanhe pondered.

Meanwhile, Gu Changge, having returned to the Daochang True Realm, did not immediately go to see Yue Mingkong and the others.

For the moment he set foot in the Daochang True Realm, the Gu family’s ancestors had sensed his presence and sent a telepathic message instructing him to visit the Gu family first.

Gu Changge made no effort to conceal his presence; aside from the Gu family’s ancestors, nearly all other Dao Realm beings in the Daochang True Realm could sense his arrival to some degree.

After a moment’s thought, he did not refuse. With a few strides, he returned to the Gu family estate where he had once resided.

The Gu family remained as prosperous and powerful as ever, unquestionably the strongest clan in the Daochang True Realm today.

In addition to Gu Changge, the Gu family had produced many individuals over the years who possessed profound destiny and abundant blessings.

Furthermore, with the Gu family’s ancestor—a figure who appeared to possess only the cultivation level of the Void Dao Realm, yet whose true strength was unfathomable—holding the fort, the Gu family’s foundations and lineage were on an entirely different level compared to those of other clans.

"Lord of Demons, are you aware of the changes in Yue Mingkong?"

The Gu family’s ancestor remained reclined in the wicker chair within the courtyard, looking quite at ease. Only after Gu Changge arrived did he rise slowly and pour him a cup of clear tea.

Gu Changge, however, paid no mind to his attitude and replied casually, “I know a little.”

He was indeed aware of the changes in Yue Mingkong, but his knowledge was limited.

Of course, he could have intervened to investigate directly, but while Yue Mingkong wouldn’t have minded, Gu Changge could no longer afford to be indifferent.

After all, Yue Mingkong was now his wife, not the mere tool he once was who didn’t have to care about her feelings.

“Even you only know a little?”

“You’ve changed quite a bit yourself…”

The Gu family ancestor smiled. Although he held deep respect for Gu Changge’s status as the Demon Lord, in a certain sense, Gu Changge was still a member of the Gu clan; the blood of the Gu family flowed through his current body.

However, what surprised him was that Gu Changge would say he only knew a little.

With his power, was there anything in this world he didn’t know?

It seemed he simply didn’t want to intervene and probe the secrets within Yue Mingkong’s body.

Gu Changge asked, “Did you summon me here just to ask these questions?”

The Gu family ancestor looked at him and said, “Since you’ve returned, I naturally have no cause for worry. I’m only concerned that Yue Mingkong might be affected—after all, her breakthrough into the Dao Realm involves secrets from a bygone era.”

“What era?” Gu Changge raised an eyebrow slightly, seeming somewhat surprised.

The Gu Clan Ancestor said solemnly, “The Dream-Ashes Era.”

The Dream-Ashes Era was an age even more ancient and distant than the Black Calamity Era. Throughout the vast expanse of history, eras like the Dream-Ashes Era and the Black Calamity Era were not uncommon, but no era was as terrifying as the Black Calamity Era, nor as eerie as the Dream-Ashes Era.

The Gu family ancestor had come across records of this era in the family’s ancient texts and thus knew a few things about the Dream-Ashes Era.

So, after Yue Mingkong emerged from seclusion, he sensed fluctuations in her aura that bore a striking resemblance to certain descriptions of the Dream-Ashes Era found in those ancient texts.

“The Dream-Ashes Era?” Gu Changge nodded slightly.

He had never delved deeply into the vastness of history, for if he wished to know, he could at any moment peer into the river of time to uncover its secrets.

The moment the Gu family’s ancestors uttered those four words, Gu Changge’s gaze instantly pierced through the vast expanse of time, spanning countless eras, and settled upon a hazy, eerie age that seemed buried and obscured by an unknown force.

A hazy mist pervaded the air, as if spanning the ages; the entire spacetime universe seemed enveloped in a vast, gray fog, drifting utterly isolated from the flow of time.

Gu Changge furrowed his brow.

It was, once again, a power akin to that which transcends the fundamental principles.

"I fear that during her seclusion in the Primordial Dream, Yue Mingkong may have been tainted by some strange energy, but I also hope that I am merely overthinking this," said the Gu family ancestor.

“As long as I’m here, Mingkong will be fine.” Gu Changge nodded, withdrew his gaze, and continued his investigation without alerting the source.

Ever since the Transcendence Master Plan appeared, he had harbored suspicions, and now even Gu Xian’er had begun to come into contact with the power of the “Taboo.”

And now, Yue Mingkong has actually been drawn into the era of the Dream Ashes?

It seemed his suspicions were correct—besides him, someone else was plotting and scheming.

He was waiting for them to slip up and reveal their weakness, but they had already laid a trap, waiting for Gu Changge to take the bait.

"I just wonder which one of you it is? Or is it all of you?"

Gu Changge’s gaze was deep and unreadable, his emotions showing little fluctuation.

In truth, he had long harbored such suspicions. Even though he could no longer draw upon the power of the True Realm, it was no easy task to block his investigations.

In this vast expanse, could such a being truly still exist?

After leaving the Gu family, Gu Changge’s figure appeared at the center of the Divine Realm within the Daochang True Realm. He gazed at the vast expanse of magnificent palaces before him and set off toward them.

Inside the temple, a tall, slender figure sat quietly in the seat of honor, her head slightly bowed. Holding a memorial in her pale hands, she was in the midst of reading something when she, too, seemed to sense something.

She turned her head, and a breathtaking smile suddenly blossomed on her picture-perfect, ethereal face. “Changge…”

That single smile caused the entire sky to pale in comparison; even Gu Changge felt a momentary daze, and a wave of gentle warmth surged through his heart, which had remained undisturbed for so long.

He walked over and gently pulled her into his arms.
