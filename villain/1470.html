Deep within the Heaven Slaying Alliance, in a secluded courtyard.

Gu Changge watched silently as changes took place on Yue Mingkong’s body. She sat cross-legged on a soft couch, her black hair cascading down, and on her exquisitely fair face, one mysterious and ancient mark after another seemed to shift and transform.

Her entire aura was constantly rising; the essence of the Dao filled every inch of her skin, as if even her cheekbones had become translucent.

"A dream is merely a medium..."

"The true essence it touches upon is actually the passage of time..."

"Besides the past, have you also touched upon the future this time?"

He watched the changes in Yue Mingkong and fell into deep thought.

The Dream Ghost clan is actually somewhat similar to the Dark Clans; they do not survive through bloodlines, but rather by falling into dreams, remaining trapped there until death, giving birth to new wills and lives within their original physical bodies.

This allows the Dream Ghost Clan to use dreams as a medium, wandering and appearing within the dreams of all living beings.

As for the Dark Clan, they are either corrupted by dark matter or their original physical bodies become prisons, trapping their former souls, while a new will is born within those empty shells.

Long ago, amidst the Vast Expanse, there emerged an era known as the Dream Embers. The being who created this era was known as the Dream Lord.

Gu Changge had never actually investigated Yue Mingkong’s secrets, and now there was no need for him to do so; the many changes in her were already sufficient to reveal her identity and origins.

The last time she awoke, she had already informed him of this.

"Dream Lord..."

Gu Changge shook his head gently, choosing not to force Yue Mingkong to wake. Whether she was the Dream Lord or not—that identity held little importance to him.

What he was pondering now was that Yue Mingkong had once told him she had dreamt of returning to the primordial beginning, where she had transformed into a lake known as the Dream Marsh. One day, a scroll of scripture fell into the water, and through instinct, she had naively embarked on her path of cultivation.

Who had left that scroll?

In the dawn of the Primordial Era, the Vast Expanse had not yet come into being. It was a chaotic, ancient age where Primordial deities and sentient beings coexisted, long before the myriad races that would later evolve.

Gu Changge’s gaze swept across the boundless domains of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, traversing countless realms of time and space, and beyond the endlessly pulsating void, she beheld a pool of water as clear and pure as the primordial source. Within it, a lotus was about to bloom; its petals unfurled slowly, each one carrying a breath of destruction.

"The World-Destroying Lotus has already sensed the aura of the cosmic calamity in this realm and is about to bloom in full splendor."

He observed it for but a moment before withdrawing his gaze.

Now, in many mysterious regions of the Vast Expanse, signs foretelling the cataclysm have begun to emerge. This is a force beyond human reach—the grand momentum of heaven and earth, which has always been so since time immemorial. All beings living within the Vast Expanse will eventually face such a day.

At that moment, as if sensing something, Gu Changge’s figure vanished from the area. When he reappeared, he was already in the central hall of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

"I wish to know the whereabouts of the Master of Destiny."

In the vast and majestic hall, where immortal mist drifted and golden lamps shone brilliantly, Ji Hanjing appeared, accompanied by Jiang Weiyang.

Upon seeing Gu Changge appear, she asked bluntly without mincing words.

With her help over the years, Jiang Weiyang had regained her sight. Her clear, bright eyes gazed at Gu Changge, and there was a hint of anticipation in her gaze.

"Even you haven’t found him?" Gu Changge asked casually.

"I’ve already looked for him, but there’s simply no way."

“Wei Yang’s father and mother are still in the Master of Fate’s hands.”

Ji Hanjing looked at Jiang Weiyang beside him and explained, “Now that the Righteous Unity Alliance has collapsed, given the Yunzhu’s ruthlessness, who knows what he might do to Weiyang’s parents…”

Gu Changge glanced at Jiang Weiyang. It was clear that Ji Hanjing intended to groom her as the successor to the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and treated Jiang Weiyang with great kindness.

He had originally assumed that, given her past experiences, Ji Hanjing would harbor deep resentment toward her sister’s descendants, but it now seemed she was far more magnanimous than he had imagined.

Not only had she helped Jiang Weiyang resolve the curse, but she was also so determined to help her locate the Master of Destiny’s hiding place.

"I’m not entirely sure either. The waters of the Vast Expanse are murky these days; if someone is determined to hide, it’ll be very difficult to find them," Gu Changge said, shaking his head slightly.

A shadow of sadness flashed across Jiang Weiyang’s eyes. She didn’t know why her ancestor seemed so familiar with Gu Changge—unlike ordinary people who would be so respectful, they were instead quite casual.

Over the years, Ji Hanjing had been deducing and searching for the Master of Destiny’s whereabouts, combing through nearly every corner of the Vast Expanse.

After the Righteous Unity Alliance collapsed and disintegrated, the Master of Destiny vanished without a trace, taking all his followers with him. No one had any idea where he was hiding.

With countless realms and endless dimensions of the void, tracking down a being like the Master of Destiny—if he truly wished to hide—would be like searching for a needle in a haystack.

"I know you must have a way; at this point, you’re my only hope."

Ji Hanjing stared intently at Gu Changge. As the princess of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court and the former Empress of Yao Guang, despite her pride, her tone carried a pleading quality for the first time.

Gu Changge waved his hand, his eyes devoid of any emotional fluctuation, and simply said, “The situation within the Heaven Slaying Alliance has only just stabilized; I really don’t want to disrupt it.”

"However, if you’re determined to search, you can go to the Nine Heavens or the Ancient Treasure Civilization. You might just find something there."

Ji Hanqian nodded, exhaled softly, and said, “Thank you.”

She knew Gu Changge must have his own reservations, but now that he’d spoken so plainly, it wouldn’t be hard to guess where the Lord of Destiny might be.

“Thank you, Alliance Master,” Jiang Weiyang added.

The two did not linger long and soon left the central hall.

Gu Changge watched until Ji Hanjing had departed before turning his gaze away. Naturally, he did not care about the life or death of Emperor Jiang Yun of Weiyang; after all, it was he who had sent Jiang Weiyang to seek out Ji Hanjing and ask for her help in the first place.

Ji Han Jing’s intervention now was entirely within his expectations.

However, given Ji Hanjing’s current strength, even if she found the Master of Destiny, it would be of little use—the Master of Destiny’s power far surpassed her own.

The reason Gu Changge responded was actually to gauge the reactions of the Master of Destiny and the Ancestor Xu. It was about time for the Ancient Treasure Civilization to make its move.

At this moment, before the Hall of Military Merit of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, on a vast, boundless plaza.

Pillars reaching to the heavens stood tall, majestic and imposing like mountains, exuding the aura of Daoist principles. Jade tablets floated around the pillars, waiting for cultivators to step forward and take them.

A masked woman in a black gown silently studied the jade slip in her hand, as if intent on counting every single battle merit recorded within.

"Finally enough..."

"As an outsider, just to catch a glimpse of the Alliance Leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, I’ve had to endure such hardship. Even with my strength, it took me thousands of years to accumulate enough battle merits."

"As for other cultivators, they likely won’t see him even once in their lifetime. Only those who followed the Alliance Leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance from the very beginning have the opportunity now to pay him a visit at will."

She gathered her thoughts, clenched the jade slip in her hand, and then made her way up the tiered celestial steps toward the Hall of Merits to exchange it for the privilege of an audience with Gu Changge.

At the same moment, Gu Changge sensed her presence. He had been about to leave the central hall, but with just a single glance at the woman in the black dress inside the Hall of Military Merit, he knew her purpose.

“A member of the Qingyi clan?”

“Have you been accumulating battle merits in the Heaven Slaying Alliance all these years?”

Gu Changge’s gaze paused briefly. After leaving the Prosperous Dao True Realm, he had actually paid no further attention to Qingyi. His objective had already been achieved, and the traces and karmic ties he had left behind already existed in the world.

Thus, his current identity would be beyond reproach.

From the very beginning, Qingyi had been nothing more than a pawn he used to justify his own existence.

During his time as the Demon Lord, whether it was Qingyi or Hongyi, they were all the same in his eyes…

With a flick of his sleeve, the masked woman in black robes still inside the Hall of Military Merit saw everything before her blur. She then felt a force that shook the heavens and earth, a power that warped time and space.

She found herself in a vast, expansive hall, where a young man with his hands behind his back was looking at her.

"Alliance Leader..."

The woman in the black skirt was shaken to the core, realizing this was Gu Changge’s doing. She quickly composed herself and bowed her hands in greeting.

“Are you Qingyi’s third elder sister?” Gu Changge asked, seemingly offhand.

The woman in the black dress froze in surprise, instinctively standing still. She hadn’t expected Gu Changge to actually know the Ninth Sister.

“As the third princess of the Ji Chan clan, you’ve traveled all the way from the Hidden Civilization just to see me. How interesting.” Gu Changge paid no heed to her inner turmoil and continued speaking as if nothing had happened.

"It seems... nothing can be hidden from the Alliance Leader."

Apart from shock, the woman in the black dress felt no other emotion at that moment. Her background and identity were now completely exposed before Gu Changge.

With just a single glance, he had seen through everything.

She forced herself to remain calm, yet she could not bring herself to ask how Gu Changge knew Xiao Jiu.

"Alliance Leader, I’ve actually come to ask for a favor. All these years of accumulating military merits were for this very reason..." The woman in the black dress took a deep breath, preparing to voice her request.

However, Gu Changge merely waved his hand, cutting her off. “I already know what you’re asking.”

“You want the Ji Chan clan to break free from the current of their original destiny and alter their predetermined fate, so you want me to help you relocate the entire Ji Chan clan from the Ancient Zang Civilization?”

“But the Ji Chan clan depends on the Eon Mother Tree for survival. If you ask me to do this, aren’t you essentially uprooting the Ancient Hidden Civilization?”

“Furthermore, you should be well aware of what the Mother Tree of the Eon represents…”

The woman in the black dress looked resolute and determined as she said earnestly, “The Mother Tree of the Eon is a gift from Heaven, but we of the Ji Chan clan have long since ceased to revere Heaven…”

In the Vast Expanse of the cosmos, the Realm of Truth is Heaven itself.

The Ji Chan clan is an exceptionally unique tribe within the Vast Expanse, named the Cicadas that Bear the Era.

A single tremor of a cicada’s wing signifies the passing of an era and the demise of all living beings.

From ancient times to the present, wherever the Cicadas of the Era pass, the years change, eras cycle, and all things are renewed.

The relationship between the Era Cicada and the Era Tree is akin to that of the paulownia tree and the phoenix.

The Era Cicadas seek out the Era Trees to dwell upon; the two coexist, sharing a common destiny.

The Epoch Tree nurtures all living beings and shapes the cosmos; it is also known as the World Tree, the Ancestral Tree, and the Tree of Origins—and this is the very destiny that the Epoch Cicada clan has revered since time immemorial.

Since ancient times, their clan has diligently followed their destiny, serving and revering Heaven.

Yet the Mother Tree of the Eon, with which they had coexisted for so long, was ultimately severed by the supreme being of the Realm of Truth. The fortune and destiny of the entire clan were utterly shattered, and they even became the scapegoats of the ancient Civilization.

To say that their clan does not hate Heaven, does not resent Heaven, or does not wish to break free from their long-standing destiny would be impossible.

However, the rest of the Ji Chan clan simply lacked her courage and resolve.

Upon hearing this, Gu Changge merely shook her head slightly. She did not agree outright, but instead asked, “Where is Qingyi now?”

The woman in the black skirt paused, her eyes darkening slightly as she replied instinctively,“Xiao Jiu went to the High Priestess to atone for her sins and accept punishment. The High Priestess represents the supreme sacrificial temple of the Ancient Zang Civilization, standing above imperial authority. Xiao Jiu has now had her wings severed; she stands before the Path of Reincarnation, guiding the souls of the departed, while day and night she is eroded by the怨力 of reincarnation…”

Gu Changge remained silent upon hearing this.

The last time he had met Qingyi was atop the canopy of the Eon Tree. She had been slender as a painting, gentle as flowing water.

Had he not acted on that impulse back then to sever the Mother Tree of the Eon, the Ji Chan clan would never have been branded as sinners of the Ancient Zang Civilization, and the young Qingyi would not have fled on her own, bringing great calamity upon the Ji Chan clan.

Every peck and every sip is a matter of cause and effect.

"If you are determined to defy heaven and rewrite the fate of the Ji Chan clan, then I might be able to help you..."

"Whether it’s for Qingyi’s sake, or because of your persistence over the years."

Gu Changge seemed to ponder for a moment. He raised his hand, and the void before him rippled, as if a fissure had opened. A misty, luminous substance then poured forth from within. With a wave of his hand, the light coalesced into a jade-white treasure bottle, shrouded in a mist of rosy glow, an utterly wondrous sight.

"Pour the contents of the bottle entirely onto the roots of the Eon Mother Tree, and you may take it away."

“When your Ji Chan clan migrates, if you encounter any obstacles along the way, I will intervene. As for the imperial family and the Five Great Royal Clans of the Ancient Treasure Civilization, if they know their place, they won’t interfere lightly,” Gu Changge said casually.

The woman in the black dress stared blankly at the white jade vase that had landed before her, as if sensing the terrifying gravitational force within—enough to crush the void and sink the universe.

She accepted it with great care. Her red lips parted as if to speak, but Gu Changge’s figure had already vanished into the Central Sanctuary.

“Is that the person Xiao Jiu encountered back then…?” she murmured to herself, then made no further delay and swiftly departed from the scene.

The Ancient Burial Civilization lay within a multi-dimensional spacetime continuum. The void there was utterly warped; any matter that came into contact with its barrier would be annihilated and shattered, just as its name, “Ancient Burial,” implied.

Now that the Dark Clans had already made their move against the Nine Heavens, if her guess was correct, the Heaven Slaying Alliance would next turn their attention to the Ancient Burial Civilization.

Gu Changge returned to the annex.

The uniqueness of the Ancient Burial Civilization lies in its border with the Realm of Reincarnation, which is the most crucial location within the Vast Expanse. After every great cataclysmic reckoning, countless sentient beings are sent there.

Each of the five great royal clans within it is backed by an Ancient Emperor of Reincarnation, and every one of these Ancient Emperors can be said to be a vanguard commander during the reckoning of the Great Calamity.

Disaster, Retribution, Longevity, Exhaustion, Emptiness.

It can be said that the Five Great Royal Clans, as well as the Ancient Zang Clan behind the Imperial House, all share intricate and profound connections with the Realm of Truth.

And the Lord of Reincarnation, who presides over the deepest reaches of the Ancient Path of Reincarnation, is an entity that wields the authority of reincarnation. Of course, to the various races of the Vast Expanse, he is nothing more than a “lackey” of the Realm of Truth.
