This scene sent a shiver down the spines of all the beings who witnessed it, filling them with dread and making their scalps prickle.

The Heavenly Lord of Absolute Darkness’s brow furrowed deeply—the first time he had ever shown such an expression.

Upon closer inspection, that terrifying, boundless black vortex was, in fact, a mouth so vast it seemed to have no limits.

It swallowed the entire Source of Darkness whole.

"Crunch..."

The sound of chewing echoed through the Vast Expanse of ages, the infinite cosmos, and countless dimensions, sending a chill through every bone.

The ethereal forms of the six treasures of the First Civilization shattered and disintegrated in an instant, their energy scattering like a chaotic torrent.

The Land of the Dark Source collapsed and exploded, vanishing completely.

It seemed that only in this way... could the resentment and hatred sealed away for countless ages be vented.

"It... it swallowed... the phantoms of the six treasures of the First Civilization..."

"Is it because these six treasures of the Primordial Civilization had sealed it away?"

Within the Nightmare Demon Realm, Xi Yuan the Holy Maiden, Nan Qing, Gu Xian’er, and the others could not help but tremble slightly, deeply shocked by this scene, a chill running down their spines.

That was the very source of darkness, a place no one across all worlds could ever set foot in—and now it had simply disintegrated and vanished.

The sound of chewing still echoed slowly, reverberating throughout the cosmos and sending chills down everyone’s spines.

Countless Dao runes within the Dark Source Realm were simply obliterated; the chains that had once bound the Lord of Heaven were effortlessly gnawed to pieces, turning into a torrent of ash.

"Not enough..."

A cold, savage, deep growl—as if an ancient demon had awoken—struck terror into the ages and brought chaos to all worlds.

In the depths of that boundless, vast darkness, a pair of terrifying, eerie eyes opened—indescribable, cold, and merciless—suspended high above, gazing down upon the heavens and surveying the countless living beings.

Only then did countless beings realize that the endless darkness was, in fact, a blurred face.

These 693 faces filled countless universes and realms of time and space; the sun, moon, and stars seemed no more than specks of dust in their presence.

As those eyes swept across the cosmos, every corner of the universe—even the shattered remnants of broken worlds—seemed to be swept away by a world-destroying gale, stirring up the tumult of eons.

Everyone trembled with terror; at that moment, their souls seemed on the verge of annihilation, dissolving into nothingness.

Regardless of which True Realm civilization or which lineage they belonged to, at this moment, they could feel nothing but boundless terror and trembling.

No living being could describe this spectacle; even those at the End of the Path level felt their scalps prickle and their blood run cold the moment they laid eyes on it, freezing in place.

What kind of being is this?

Even as the Chaos Heaven and Earth Era drew to a close, the grand, ancient Judgment Altar suspended at the very edge of the unknown beyond had never radiated such terrifying malice and icy chill.

On the front lines of the Ancient Hidden Civilization, the Ancient Hidden Emperor, the World Lord, the Lord of Reincarnation, and others felt their very bodies freeze the moment they were met by that gaze.

Especially the World Lord and the Lord of Reincarnation, who held the authority of creation and reincarnation, felt as though the authority of the Heavenly Dao itself was shattering, about to be obliterated by that gaze.

This caused their expressions to shift abruptly, filled with disbelief and trembling dread.

"Ancestor Xu..."

Suddenly, their pupils trembled violently, contracting repeatedly as they struggled to hide their terror.

Before the dark, blurred visage that had once shrouded the infinite cosmos—the very source of darkness that had crumbled and vanished—an indescribable black hand appeared. Two of its fingers clamped together, as if pinching a struggling ant or grasshopper, and slowly brought it before the gaping maw that was chewing.

The two recognized the figure of the ant—it was none other than the Ancestor Xu, who had long since vanished from the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

The Primordial Void of this moment bore no resemblance to his former self—his hair was disheveled, and blood streamed from his entire body. Holes pierced through his flesh, and the flesh and blood within had long since rotted and decayed, exposing stark white bone fragments—a sight of utter horror.

Most notably, his primordial essence had already dissipated, leaving him on the brink of annihilation.

"Hahahaha…"

"Lord of Heaven, you are nothing special after all..."

"All that remains is a divine remnant—a conglomeration of darkness and malevolence. You cannot overcome the Heavenly Will..."

"You wanted to act as Heaven’s proxy, you wanted to engage in Heaven Slaying—but you could never do it..."

"You, too, are a failure."

Despite this, facing certain death, the Primordial Void showed no trace of fear on his face. Instead, he laughed wildly, as if in liberation, or as if witnessing the Lord of Heaven’s defeat with his own eyes, which brought him a sense of satisfaction.

However, that blurry, dark visage paid him no heed whatsoever—seeming both disdainful and indifferent—simply tossing him into the massive maw that continued to chew slowly, as if feeding it a meal.

The sound of chewing never ceased, nor did it change in the slightest. To this being who had reached the End of the Path of Ascension, he was nothing more than a snack or a pastry.

"Yuan'er, Xiaoqing, I have come to see you..."

All light before the Ancestor’s eyes was suddenly engulfed by darkness, yet he remained unrepentant and fearless even as he was swallowed by that infinite darkness.

A memory from the distant past flashed through his mind.

It was a wild and primitive era, when even the methods of cultivation had not yet spread. He was merely a young apprentice in a village, following a shaman as he traveled from place to place, gathering medicinal herbs for him.

The village was small, with a population of just over a hundred, and they had to fend off raids by savage beasts year-round.

His father had died young while defending the village against the beasts, and his mother had fallen gravely ill—so much so that even the shaman could not cure her—and passed away soon after, leaving behind his infant sister.

Without their parents to rely on, they became a burden to the village, and no one was willing to take them in.

So, at such a young age, he took on the responsibility of raising his sister. Worried that she might go hungry in the village, he would even carry her on his back when he went up the mountain to gather herbs.

When his sister was hungry, he would use the small knife he carried to cut his index finger and let her suck the warm blood.

When he couldn’t find anything to eat, he would gnaw on soft mud mixed with sweet-tasting leaves, saving the precious fruits to trade for animal milk for his sister.

Those days were bitter and difficult, but every time he saw his little sister’s face lit up with a giggle, all his weariness would vanish, and it all felt worth it.

Later, as his younger sister grew older and was able to walk on her own, she became very obedient, well-behaved, and understanding, often helping him out, which made his life much easier.

She had inherited her mother’s features; though still very young, she was beautiful, with large eyes like flawless black gems. Many people in the village adored her.

But he never could have imagined it.

The village chief’s youngest son also coveted his young sister. While he was out gathering herbs, the boy sneaked into his home and defiled her—she was barely seven years old.

When he returned to the village, he found his dilapidated home covered in blood and chaos, and his little sister, already lifeless.

Her wide eyes were open, her tiny arms stretched out toward the door, and her mouth silently cried out, as if calling for her brother… But at that moment, he could hear nothing, could do nothing…

She was only seven years old… She was only seven years old…

He stood frozen in place, numb with shock. Everything before his eyes seemed to go dark; it felt as though the world had lost all meaning for him to go on living.

Soon, villagers gathered outside the house, chattering away—some expressing sympathy, others regret, and still others indifference… No one came to his aid.

The door was wide open, yet why did no one hear her? Why did no one hear his little sister’s cries? Why did no one hear her screams?

Did no one hear her despair?

Later, that village vanished, and everyone disappeared… He spent three years killing everyone, grinding their bones to dust, and using a knife to peel their skin off piece by piece.

Later still, he was taken under the wing of a cultivator from the Demon Sect in the Great Wasteland. He became a demon cultivator—unscrupulous and ruthless.

He also met another woman who would become the most important person in his life. Her name was Yuan’er; she came from a distant city, the daughter of a wealthy family, and had arrived in the Great Wasteland with a merchant caravan.

She was gentle, with flowing hair and a graceful smile always playing at the corners of her lips. She loved wearing green dresses and holding a lacquered paper umbrella in the misty rain, often bringing food to her younger sister and to him...

Yes, Su.

He didn’t let his younger sister leave him; he transformed her into a demonic puppet, so the two would never be parted.

Yuan’er, his younger sister, and him.

Those were the happiest days of his life.

But Yuan’er was, after all, merely a mortal. She could not cultivate, her life was short, and she did not wish to become a demonic puppet.

He watched her grow old, watched her youth fade away, watched her hair turn white and her face become lined with wrinkles, and finally, he personally buried her when she died.

Later, he broke his vow, dug her up, and kept her by his side.

He vowed to find a way to revive her and reunite them. To this end, he mastered myriad arts, sought out countless paths, and transformed the false into the true… Later, the world revered him as the Void Ancestor, viewing him as a monster detached from the world.

Yet in the beginning, he had been nothing more than an ordinary boy who simply wanted to live out his days with his younger sister.

"Yuan'er, Xiaoqing, wait for me..."

The Ancestor Xu murmured. The darkness before his eyes faded, giving way to a misty rain. Before an ancient bridge, a woman stood holding an umbrella. Her hair flowed freely, and she wore a long green dress. Holding the hand of a little girl with pigtails, she smiled gently at him.

He suddenly spread his arms wide, smiled, and walked toward the two of them.

……

"Crunch..."

The sound of chewing echoed across the Vast Expanse of the ages. Not a single drop of blood flowed from the Ancestor Xu’s withered body; it quickly dissolved into a torrent of dissipating energy.

The top-tier figures from all factions felt their scalps prickle with dread and trembled uncontrollably. An existence of the End of the Path Ascension tier, who had left a fearsome reputation even in ancient times, met such a pitiful end.

Within the Nightmare Demon Realm, the Moonless High Priest, Gu Xian’er, the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan, and the others could not help but fall silent.

This was the divine will of the Lord of Heaven. Once awakened, it manifested with a terrifying might capable of shattering the heavens and engulfing all worlds.

Wave after wave of divine consciousness collided across the various universes and realms of time and space. Many supreme beings, their hearts pounding with dread, sent out their own messages.

Though many still did not know what this was, they had already vaguely guessed the truth from the events that had just unfolded.

"It is not enough..."

A low, icy, and savage roar echoed once more.

At the farthest reaches of the heavens, those terrifying, dark eyes suddenly fixed upon the multitude of beings at the End of the Path and True Road-level beings currently locked in fierce combat on the front lines.

Without a single word, a hand as dark and colossal as a pillar of the heavens reached out with a swish. The layers of spacetime before it were torn apart as easily as paper.

The rippling aura swept in all directions, and the scattered energy was instantly reduced to dust.

With a thud, one of the Five Sovereigns of the Ancient Hidden Civilization—a figure at the End of the Path—was unable to dodge in time and was seized by the black hand.

Far inferior even to the Ancestor Xu, his body began to corrode the moment it was touched, disintegrating layer by layer. His expression was one of sheer terror and despair; there was simply no escape.
