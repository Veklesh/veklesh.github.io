On the cliff’s edge, mist swirled as a middle-aged man in white robes stared intently at the chessboard before him, furrowing his brow in deep thought. It seemed as though he were trying to determine exactly where to place his next move, and for that moment, it felt as if he had been pondering it for countless ages.

Gu Xian’er and the elders and disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect stood silently by his side, none daring to disturb him.

Although Song Yu had not introduced him, they could all tell that the middle-aged man in white before them possessed immense and unfathomable power.

Even the Dao Realm elders of the Crescent Moon Sect, if they closed their eyes, could not sense this man’s presence; before them, it was as if there were nothingness—a void that did not exist within the spacetime of that moment.

This alone was enough to demonstrate the white-robed middle-aged man’s immense power.

They had only just set foot in the Nine Heavens; who knew what levels of power lay ahead?

Song Yu stood respectfully to the side, waiting without a word to disturb them. It was clear he held this middle-aged man in high regard and seemed to know his identity and background.

Gu Xian’er furrowed her dark brows, observing coldly from the sidelines.

On the journey to the Nine Heavens, she had already realized that Song Yu’s close ties to the Nine Heavens stemmed from the fact that he actually hailed from there.

He had been lying low at the Crescent Moon Sect all this time solely for the sake of the Nine Celestial Restraints’ legacy. Now that the Crescent Moon Sect had been destroyed, Song Yu would undoubtedly set his sights on her.

After all, that Mad Old Woman was the last heir of the Jutian Sect.

Back then, after the Mad Old Woman had temporarily regained her senses, she had taken her away in front of everyone, only to vanish without a trace afterward. And the only person with any connection to the Mad Old Woman was her.

Naturally, Song Yu would certainly want to extract clues from her.

During their subsequent journey, Song Yu frequently dropped hints, attempting to inquire about matters related to the Mad Old Woman. In particular, he spoke openly about the Nine Taboos of the Ju Tian Sect.

Although Gu Xian’er brushed him off repeatedly and paid him no mind, as they drew closer to the Nine Heavens, he noticed that Song Yu was gradually ceasing to hide his true intentions.

The elders and disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect, though they could guess at Song Yu’s intentions, pretended not to notice, believing this to be a private matter between Gu Xian’er and him—one in which they had no business interfering.

Furthermore, once they reached the Nine Heavens, they might need to rely heavily on Song Yu, so it would be unwise to offend him along the way.

Nevertheless, quite a few elders still urged Song Yu, appealing to their shared past as fellow disciples, not to go too far.

Song Yu responded calmly, stating that he was merely curious about the mad old woman’s background and had no ulterior motives.

Gu Xian’er merely watched coldly from the sidelines, neither confirming nor denying his words.

Of course, Song Yu knew she was connected to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, so he dared not harbor any ulterior motives.

Not to mention the taciturn, white-clad woman by Gu Xian’er’s side, whose mysterious background even made his guardian, Uncle Lan, feel a sense of apprehension.

Throughout the journey to the Nine Heavens, Song Yu had kept a low profile.

Of course, Gu Xian’er was well aware that once they set foot in the Nine Heavens—Song Yu’s territory—anything could happen.

Who knew what tricks Song Yu might pull?

As for the Nine Celestial Restraints taught to her by the Mad Old Woman, she had been studying them diligently during this time. Unfortunately, she hadn’t even begun to grasp the first restraint—since she hadn’t yet reached the Dao Realm, her comprehension of it remained vague and incomplete.

That concept was profoundly mysterious, like a fog—vague yet profound. It seemed that only after she reached the Dao Realm could she truly comprehend and master it.

Gu Xian’er finally understood why the Mad Old Woman had said that only after reaching the Dao Realm and surviving the first Celestial Waning Tribulation could one unleash the First Taboo.

Each subsequent seal required surviving at least one Celestial Waning Tribulation before it could be cast.

In other words, to successfully unleash the Ninth Seal of the Nine Seals of Heaven, one must survive nine Celestial Waning Tribulations and reach the Realm of the End of the Path.

This technique is incredibly fierce; once unleashed, there is no turning back. One must remember not to force its use before reaching the appropriate realm, for failure to do so will not only result in the technique’s failure but also invite boundless backlash, with consequences that are utterly terrifying.

"Although the path ahead is uncertain now that I have reached the Nine Heavens, this place is untouched by the turmoil and warfare of the outside world, and it is indeed the best place for me to break through to the Dao Realm," Gu Xian'er murmured to herself, her clear eyes filled with unwavering resolve.

Ever since she had firmly established her Dao Heart last time, she had known exactly which path to take from here on out.

Therefore, she was fully confident in her ability to break through to the Dao Realm; she was merely waiting for the right opportunity.

"After so many years, someone has once again set foot in the Nine Heavens. But unlike the last time, when that fellow found his way here on his own, the one who has come here this time was actually brought by you."

Just as everyone’s thoughts were in disarray, the middle-aged man in white, seated cross-legged on the cliff, seemed to finally snap out of his reverie.

He glanced at Song Yu, seemingly recognizing him, then swept his gaze across the members of the Crescent Moon Sect one by one. His eyes lingered on Gu Xian’er, the woman in white, and the woman in green robes named Qing Nan, as if slightly surprised.

“When I left the Nine Heavens Realm last time, I was greatly indebted to your guidance, Senior.” Song Yu bowed respectfully.

The middle-aged man in white shook his head. “Since you’re that fellow’s disciple, I’ll naturally look out for you a little.”

Song Yu’s demeanor grew even more deferential.

To the Nine Heavens and Earth, the middle-aged man in white before them was akin to a guardian deity—his strength unfathomable, his existence ancient and timeless, and his origins unknown to all.

In the Nine Heavens Realm’s Nine Layers of Heaven, regardless of one’s cultivation level, everyone had to refer to themselves as juniors in the presence of this middle-aged man in white.

"This is the first time in my life I’ve encountered such a situation—three beings I cannot fathom appearing at the same time."

"Who exactly are you?"

The middle-aged man in white fixed his gaze on Gu Xian’er’s exquisitely beautiful face and asked, his tone tinged with surprise.

He looked at the three women before him with a calm, profound gaze. Yet at that moment, it seemed as though countless mysterious visions flashed through his eyes—the myriad realms of the universe and the ebb and flow of fate and time all transformed into a series of elusive, ever-shifting rivers.

Yet as his insight probed deeper, a vast mist began to rise from those rivers, shrouding everything in an impenetrable haze. It was as if a terrifying giant hand reached out, shattering every prying gaze in an instant.

Especially when his gaze fell upon Gu Xian’er and the woman named Qing Nan, he furrowed his brow, appearing even more astonished.

As for the white-clad woman who had kept her face veiled in sheer gauze, remaining cold and silent, he paused to ponder and deduce. It seemed he had come to some realization, yet he said nothing more.

This was the first time Gu Xian’er had encountered such a situation; she was equally surprised and perplexed.

Who exactly was she? Wasn’t she Gu Xian’er? The cherished daughter of the Gu family, the eternal lineage of the Prosperous Dao True Realm?

Or could it be that the middle-aged man in white before her had sensed something?

Was it because of Gu Changge standing behind her?

This was also the first time Song Yu had seen the middle-aged man in white before him display such a look of astonishment and bewilderment, and he was deeply shaken.

Gu Xian’er came from the Heaven Slaying Alliance; he could understand why she was held in such high regard by that mad old woman, given her unique background and origins.

But what about the woman beside her, dressed in a blue robe, who had been taciturn the entire journey and gave off the air of a simple, ascetic monk? Why would this elder tomb guardian speak of her in such a way?

Everyone present, including Gu Xian’er—who had always been addressed as “Senior” by the white-robed woman—turned their gaze toward Qing Nan.

“What do you mean by that, Senior?”

Watching this scene, Qing Nan couldn’t help but ask aloud.

A look of astonishment and confusion also appeared on her face; she seemed just as baffled, completely in the dark.

At that moment, as her thoughts stirred, an inexplicable and vast power surged within her, causing the mist she had deliberately shrouded herself in to dissipate significantly.

The middle-aged man in white gazed deeply at her, his eyes piercing, as if he were trying to see right through her.

“Have you ever been tainted by darkness?” he asked, seemingly offhand.

Qing Nan paused, then thought for a moment before nodding. “I’ve clashed with the Dark Clan before. They hunted me down. Had it not been for Miss Xian’er asking that elder to intervene and save me, I would have perished at the hands of those dark beings.”

Encountering such an odd fellow at the gates of the Nine Heavens was somewhat unexpected for her.

However, it seemed this fellow couldn’t see through her true identity.

Upon hearing this, the middle-aged man seemed to understand. His expression returned to calm as he said, “That Dark Clan is quite clever—using you as bait to cast a long line and catch a bigger fish…”

As he spoke, he reached out his hand. From the void behind Qing Nan, a sharp, furious screech seemed to erupt.

He pulled out a strand of eerie mist resembling a black thread, then crushed it in his hand, reducing it to ashes.

“What is this…”

This scene sent a shiver down the spines of everyone from the Crescent Moon Sect, including Song Yu, leaving them trembling with shock and disbelief.

Had they truly sensed nothing at all along the way?

How despicable of the Dark Clan—deliberately leaving a trace of their presence on Qing Nan, fully expecting to be rescued by them?

A flicker of fear and trembling crossed Qing Nan’s face. She then bowed respectfully to the middle-aged man in white before her and said, “Thank you, Senior, for your intervention.”

The middle-aged man in white let his gaze sweep past her and shook his head. “It seems the situation outside has become so turbulent that the Dark Clan has grown completely brazen. However, their target is likely not you, but you…”

As he spoke, he turned his gaze to Gu Xian’er, as if he, too, were trying to see through the veil of mystery shrouding her.

Gu Xian’er was completely baffled; with her current knowledge and understanding, she simply couldn’t make sense of what he was saying.

Why would she become the Dark Clan’s target?

After all, she had never had the slightest contact with those Dark Clans.

"However, this is merely my conjecture. I haven’t left the Nine Heavens in a very, very long time, and I have no desire to meddle in the affairs of the outside world."

The middle-aged man in white said, his words exuding a profound sense of detachment and transcendence.

Gu Xian’er remained noncommittal. Although she sensed that this middle-aged man in white possessed truly unfathomable power, his words were always so vague and cryptic—why couldn’t he just speak plainly?

"The last person who came to the Nine Heavens must have come from the same place as you," the middle-aged man in white continued, speaking as if to himself.

Gu Xian’er knew exactly who he was referring to.

Previously, when the Prosperous Dao True Realm faced an invasion by the Xianling Civilization, a man named Qingfeng had set out on a journey to the Nine Heavens. He sought out elders who had once left the Prosperous Dao True Realm, hoping to persuade them to return and protect it.

On the eve of that great war, there had indeed been a Dao Realm being named Yuan Chan, an ancient monk, who had led several disciples to assist.

It seemed that when Qingfeng had arrived in the Nine Heavens, he had also come into contact with this middle-aged man in white.

However, she was not familiar with Qingfeng and had never interacted with him. When the middle-aged man in white spoke these words, Gu Xian’er did not respond.

The middle-aged man in white smiled, seemingly unconcerned, and simply said, “It’s been a long time since any new faces have arrived in the Nine Heavens. You are welcome here.”

With that, he flicked his sleeve, and a rolling fog swept in, instantly hurling everyone present to another mountain range.

Standing atop this mountain range, Gu Xian’er and the others finally beheld the true expanse of this land. Before their eyes lay vast continents floating in the sky, boundless and endless, with surging seas of clouds stretching as far as the eye could see.

Shrouded in mist and radiating divine light, it was exactly as mortals had always sought—a realm beyond the mundane world, a sanctuary for those seeking immortality.

As if sensing everyone’s confusion, Song Yu explained, “Anyone wishing to enter the Nine Heavens must first obtain the consent of the Elder Grave Keeper.”

“The Nine Heavens are vast and boundless, with each of the Nine Layers situated in a different realm. Each layer is separated from the others, stacked one upon another like the tiers of a tower. The place we are heading to now is located in the Ninth Layer.”

And at this moment, at the very pinnacle of the Nine Heavens, where the Ninth Heaven resides,

Within a magnificent palace that stood as if rooted in the vast realm of clouds.

A figure clad in a Da Luo Dao robe, with a profound countenance seemingly shrouded in endless chaotic mist, sat upright within it.

His eyes encompassed the boundless heavens; terrifying visions flickered within them before fading back into silence, like a bottomless abyss that sent shivers down the spine.

This was none other than the Eternal Dao Lord, the current ruler of the Futiang Sect in the Nine Heavens.

"Ji Yu has returned?"

"It seems his scheme has failed, but the person he brought back to the Nine Heavens this time is rather special."

This figure, with eyes of profound depth, swept across boundless dimensions of time and space in an instant, fixing his gaze upon the members of the Crescent Moon Sect who were just leaving that mountain range.

"This woman? Though her aura is chaotic, she is undoubtedly a being who has survived at least seven Celestial Waning Tribulations. Her background is quite complex—is her appearance in the Nine Heavens purely a coincidence?"

“No, her aura bears a certain resemblance to that of Senior West Saint…”

"Could it be her?"

He studied the white-robed woman at the center, seemingly lost in thought.

On another vast ancient land, also situated within the Ninth Heaven of the Nine Heavens.

Cities stood, dynasties rose, various races thrived, and numerous sects and lineages vied for influence—it was a scene of unparalleled prosperity.

The aura of the Dao had faded; every rule and order here was established by the founders of this ancient land. Even the current ruler of the Nine Heavens, the Eternal Dao Lord, would face interference and suppression upon entering this place, daring not to commit any transgression or show disrespect.

In the deepest recesses of this ancient land dwells an incredibly ancient race, whose origins are said to date back to the very birth of the Vast Expanse…

Some even claim that this clan actually evolved and broke away from the Realm of Truth to arrive in this world, and is known as the most ancient clan of the Vast Expanse.

The progenitor of this clan once made contact with the Realm of Truth and, relying solely on his own strength, single-handedly averted a cataclysmic cosmic calamity that would have engulfed the heavens of the Vast Expanse.

There are many similar accounts, but today they are impossible to verify, and it is even harder to distinguish fact from fiction.

This clan was once known as the Ancient Clan.

And now, this clan is known as the Gu Clan.

Deep within the Gu Clan, countless divine islands and majestic ancient mountains rise, while boundless cosmic essence, like soaring true dragons, swirls and permeates the vast void of heaven and earth.

When ordinary people arrive here, nourished by such celestial and earthly essence, they can easily live for thousands or even tens of thousands of years, even without cultivating.

All manner of rare immortal herbs, elixirs of immortality, and immortal divine trees—things rarely found in the outside world—are almost everywhere here.

In the crevices of the cliffs, immortal mushrooms take root; purple mist flows, and the glow of the dawn and dusk converge, resembling fragments of a primordial, primordial land from the deepest depths of chaos.

"The ancestral hall just trembled slightly..."

"What’s going on?"

Suddenly, a boundless, overwhelming aura swept in, as if awakening from the river of time, disturbing the heavens of ages past.

An elderly man with snow-white hair, a stooped and aged figure, and clad in a loose gray robe awoke atop a mountain peak covered in dust and overgrown with withered grass.

His eyes bore the weight of immeasurable time, as if countless eras had withered and faded behind him, as he sat witnessing the collapse of the cosmos and the decay of the ages.
