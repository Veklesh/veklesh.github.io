"What good would it do to tell you? Looking at you now, a mere wandering spirit, do you really think you can seek revenge?"

"Gu Changge spoke in his usual unhurried tone.

Ji Hanjing’s features shifted constantly in the hazy mist, until finally he let his head droop in exhaustion, his expression filled with resentment and resignation.

Indeed, she was now nothing more than a wandering spirit trapped within the Book of Scavengers.

She couldn’t even leave—how could she possibly seek revenge?

Even if she knew who her enemies were, did she even have the power to do so?

Even if she were to rebuild her physical form and foundations, it would take an unimaginably long time to return to her former peak.

"You… can you help me?"

Ji Hanjing suddenly lifted her head, gazing at Gu Changge with eyes brimming with hope and longing.

She was, after all, the mighty Empress of Yao Guang,

a once-mighty empress who looked down upon all worlds with an iron fist,

tribes paying homage, with none but her held in highest esteem, and countless star systems and universes bowing in worship.

When had she ever spoken so humbly, begging another so meekly?

Ji Hanjing had been by Gu Changge’s side for quite some time now.

She didn’t know Gu Changge’s background, but she was well aware of his capabilities. If it were him, he would definitely be able to help her.

“Why should I help you?”

Upon hearing this, Gu Changge merely glanced at Ji Hanjing with a hint of amusement. “As agreed, I will help you break free from the Scavenger’s Book when the time comes, and I can also help you rebuild your physical form. But as for your quest for revenge, that has nothing to do with me.”

Ji Hanjing stared intently at him, her tone resolute. “Once you’ve helped me avenge my ‘543’ great grudges, I’ll hand over everything I possess to you. From that day forward, I’ll obey your every command, serve you faithfully, and go through fire and water for you—even if it means becoming your ox or horse, I’ll spare no effort.”

“I don’t know what plans or grand schemes you have, and I don’t care about them. As long as you help me avenge my enemies, I’ll do anything for you.”

She understood Gu Changge’s character and nature well enough to know he would never act out of mere pity or compassion.

Everything he does is driven by his own plans and logic, as well as his own interests.

If she wanted Gu Changge’s help, she would have to offer a bargaining chip that satisfied him.

“A request from the daughter of the Central Lord, a princess of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court—it’s hard for me to refuse.”

A smile spread across Gu Changge’s face, as if he had known all along that Ji Hanjing would say this.

At this point, she had no other choice.

Ji Hanjing continued to stare intently at him, waiting for Gu Changge’s reply.

She had no idea what Gu Changge was weighing in his mind; those few moments of silence felt as though they had stretched on for an eternity.

"I can help you avenge yourself, but from this day forward, everything you possess will belong to me."

“Have you made up your mind?”

Finally, Gu Changge looked at her, a strange gleam in the depths of his eyes.

As a treasure of Civilization, the Scavenger’s Book possessed power no less formidable than other such artifacts.

However, its original owner, who had found it, never gained the Book of Scavenging’s recognition. Unable to unleash its true power and mysteries, they treated it merely as a “tool” for treasure-hunting and taking shortcuts.

Ji Hanjing still exists as the spirit of the Scavenger’s Book; in truth, she is capable of channeling its power.

Yet, bound by certain restrictions and array patterns, she remains confined within the Scroll of Scavenging, unable to break free.

Gu Changge had actually long planned to have Ji Hanjing use the Civilization Treasure as a vessel to reshape her original physical form.

This is essentially the Civilization Treasure taking on a physical form. From now on, she is the Scavenger’s Book, and the Scavenger’s Book is her; the two are inseparable.

However, Gu Changge had never witnessed a Civilization Treasure taking physical form before, nor had such an event ever been recorded in ancient texts.

He wasn’t certain whether Ji Hanjing would succeed in taking physical form; the key to this lay in whether she could survive the tribulation of transformation.

The transformation tribulation of a Civilization Treasure, by all accounts, is no less severe than the Celestial Waning Tribulation that beings at the Ancestral Dao Realm must endure.

In this world, aside from the Celestial Waning Tribulation, there are also the Tribulations of Natural Disasters and Man-Made Calamities. After all, a Civilization Treasure is not like an ordinary Ancestral Dao Realm; it embodies the entire essence of a civilization, and the karmic consequences it must bear are even more vast and immense.

Ji Hanjing had been trapped within the Book of Scavengers for countless years; her vitality, spirit, and will had long since been exhausted, and she could barely even recall her original past.

Had Gu Changge not awakened her this time, she would likely have remained in that state of utter confusion and oblivion.

She no longer possessed the inherent strength required to weather this great calamity.

"As long as I can take my revenge, I am willing to pay any price."

Ji Hanjing spoke calmly. Beneath her blurred features, her eyes were utterly serene, like a still lake without a ripple.

Afterward, Gu Changge made no attempt to hide anything and directly told her of his plan.

Ji Hanjing did not resist the idea of becoming the Scavenger’s Book from this day forward.

Moreover, this would spare her the lengthy process of separating from the Scavenger’s Book, rebuilding her physical form and bones, and restoring her cultivation.

She herself had never even heard of a Civilization Treasure taking physical form.

If she succeeded, her power would be no weaker than that of those at the Ancestral Dao Realm; in fact, precisely because she was a Civilization Treasure, her power might even far surpass theirs.

She might even hold her own against figures like Mu Fuguang and the Monk of Compassion.

However, according to Gu Changge, this transformation ordeal was perilous and unpredictable, and might even attract the attention of the Realm of Truth.

Fearing the trouble it might cause, he might not be able to help her when the time came, so Ji Hanjing had mentally prepared herself for the possibility of failure—a catastrophe that could result in the complete annihilation of both her body and spirit.

Of course, since Gu Changge had promised her, he would certainly find a way to help her, increasing her chances of success.

Several days later, deep within the Heaven Slaying Alliance, in a desolate sector of the cosmos, a chaotic light shot toward the heavens. Primordial true thunder pierced the vast expanse of space, dense and countless, like an ocean of Daoist thunder.

An ancient, unadorned book floated there amidst the chaos. The misty haze expanded, giving rise to a swirling purple mist—like the primordial purple qi—containing countless profound truths.

Various Daoist energies converged and collided, billowing upward to illuminate the pitch-black, profound cosmos with radiant clarity.

Gu Changge stood in the distance, calmly observing it all.

Various beams of light appeared there, as if striking down from different times and spaces; eventually, heaven, earth, and the cosmos returned to stillness.

However, this state did not last long. Accompanied by terrifying visions—as if the heavens and earth were being torn apart—all manner of eerie entities surged forth from that place.

There were human figures, as well as scenes of all manner of calamities—floods, earthquakes, collapsing skies, volcanic eruptions, and hurricanes—each one capable of destroying an ancient world.

The scene was unbearably harrowing. Amidst it all, sentient beings wailed, prostrated themselves in supplication, and prayed to the heavens—only to be drowned by floods, burned alive by flames, or swallowed by the collapsing heavens and earth. Scene after scene resembled natural disasters that had truly occurred in ancient times. Beyond that, there were various wars, slaughter, and famines…

Countless ghostly figures roamed the realms, their chilling, terrifying aura radiating in all directions.

Time and space shattered; everything became chaotic, making it impossible to discern whether these scenes belonged to the past, or whether they were real or illusory.

In the end, even the faint shadow of the ancient Yao Guang Kingdom reappeared. The once-prosperous realm seemed to revive from the mists of time, poised to reclaim its former glory before the world.

The Book of Scavengers was completely enveloped by thick mist and the eerie spectacle.

Even a treasure of Civilization, under such an eerie calamity, was no different from an ordinary cultivator.

The ancient pages were ignited by pitch-black flames that suddenly appeared out of the void, beginning to fade one by one before turning to ashes.

Gu Changge furrowed his brow slightly, then swept his robe, causing the thick mist to disperse somewhat.

The blurry figure drifting within the Scavenger’s Book was constantly shattering and twisting, and along with the book itself, cracks began to appear.

Ji Hanjing was enduring a pain of bone-molding and flesh-reforming rarely seen throughout history; this transformation was not merely a change on the level of spirit and soul.

It also involved her fusion with the Book of Scavenging, transforming the book into her reborn body—as if a flesh-and-blood form and an object were being fused into a single, living entity.

"However, I did underestimate her willpower. The pain she endured when forging the Book of Scavenging back then must have been no less than what she’s experiencing now. After all, the Book of Scavenging was forged from a part of her own flesh and blood."

Gu Changge withdrew his gaze. He had originally intended to intervene and lend a hand, but now it seemed there was no need for him to act.

He had somewhat underestimated Ji Hanjing’s capabilities.

Several days later, the depths of the cosmos returned to calm. All turbulence and energy had vanished, leaving only silence.

Only a vague, hazy glow floated there, resembling a formless mass of flesh and blood shrouded in chaotic mist, from which various Daoist auras rose and swirled.

Gu Changge had already left this universe several days prior. Once he sensed that Ji Hanjing’s path to taking human form would proceed without a hitch, he departed immediately.

No one would come to disturb Ji Hanjing until she had completed her transformation and departed.

After successfully taking physical form, Ji Hanjing would still need some time to adapt to her new body and powers. Gu Changge naturally had no time to spare waiting for her or guarding her.

Outside, the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance had already begun a massive battle and slaughter.

To be precise, it was the Heaven Slaying Alliance that launched the campaign against the Zhengyi Alliance. The tide of battle was one-sided; the Zhengyi Alliance was forced to mount a defense, but under the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s relentless assault, they could only retreat and shrink their territory.

Several universes bordering the Heaven Slaying Alliance were immediately abandoned by the Zhengyi Alliance. Many civilizations and factions that had originally aligned with the Zhengyi Alliance chose to surrender and submit the moment the Zhengyi Alliance’s army drew near.

The Heaven Slaying Alliance, with its vast resources and immense wealth, paid a colossal price to carve out a stable spacetime passage through the vast void. One ancient warship after another, laden with massive armies, crossed over, their momentum overwhelming and their intent to kill boiling over.

Wherever they passed, they swept through like a bamboo shoot breaking through the ground; the Zhengyi Alliance was routed, utterly defeated, without even a thought of resistance.

Compared to being devoured and slaughtered by the dark entities, reduced to sacrificial fodder, choosing to surrender at this juncture was clearly the wiser course of action.

Consequently, the Zhengyi Alliance faced the invading forces of the Heaven Slaying Alliance with absolutely no ability to fight back, their morale utterly shattered.

Everyone feared death and refused to charge forward to resist; needless to say, the Zhengyi Alliance, now besieged from both front and rear, could not defend both flanks.

Even those at the Dao Realm sought to avoid conflict whenever possible, retreating whenever they could. When all else failed, they chose to submit and surrender—a fate far better than perishing and having their Dao destroyed.

However, there were also those who stubbornly held their ground, refusing to surrender even unto death. Their fate, of course, was grim; in the end, not even their bones could be found, swallowed whole by the vast, surging waves of the Void, leaving nothing behind.

Under these circumstances, the morale of the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s army soared, and they swiftly overran the Zhengyi Alliance’s numerous frontline strongholds and territories.

At this juncture, the Zhengyi Alliance wisely chose to “sacrifice the pawns to save the king,” abandoning the 4.8 territories they could no longer defend, giving up the many vulnerable gaps that were difficult to hold, and naturally relinquishing the weak, fledgling civilizations and realms bordering the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Their territory was instantly reduced to less than one-third of its original size.

At the same time, the Zhengyi Alliance directed all factions and lineages to relocate to the central region as quickly as possible. The True Realm civilizations situated in the heart of the Zhengyi Alliance—such as the Yanyang True Realm and the Shangyin True Realm—were also accepting the arriving forces from all quarters.

While this move proved effective, it also left many feeling disheartened—especially the cultivators still locked in fierce combat with the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s main forces on the front lines.

The pledge made at the Alliance’s founding—to share hardships and resist enemies together—has now crumbled without a fight.

The orthodox factions that had previously rejoiced at joining the Zhengyi Alliance now regretted their decision with every fiber of their being. Who could have known that the Heaven Slaying Alliance would be so overwhelmingly powerful—so much so that even the dark spirits kept their distance, unwilling to attack, and could only

set their sights on the Zhengyi Alliance—it’s like they’re picking on the weakest link.

Faced with the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s relentless onslaught, the Zhengyi Alliance still had a way to retreat.

But against the invading dark spirits, the Zhengyi Alliance had virtually no recourse other than to fight to the death. The most brutal battles, in fact, were taking place outside the embankments and city walls.

Rivers of blood flowed, and mountains of bones piled up. Shattered weapons and corpses drifted through the starry void, and even the endless yellow sands were stained blood-red.

The vast, endless horde of dark spirits still lingered in the distant cosmos, resembling a dark river or ocean, evoking a sense of endless suffocation and despair.
