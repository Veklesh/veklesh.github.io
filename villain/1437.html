According to Emperor Weiyang’s account, when he founded the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty hundreds of eons ago, he was already a puppet of the Master of the Yinxu Cave.

He had no choice in many matters.

To ensure the Emperor of Weiyang would never harbor treacherous intentions, the Master of the Yinxu Cave had deliberately placed a terrifying restriction upon his wife—one that would activate at regular intervals.

The agony was enough to shatter the mind and spirit of even a Dao Realm being; even if one were to shatter one’s own source and reduce one’s soul to countless ashes, it would be difficult to escape.

Emperor Weiyang loved his wife deeply; how could he bear to see her suffer so? Naturally, he dared not harbor any disloyal thoughts toward the Master of Yinxu Cave.

Occasionally, the Master of Yinxu Cave would show a glimmer of mercy, allowing the couple to meet once every so often to ease their longing for one another.

At that time, Jiang Wei Yang had just been born. The Master of Yinxu Cave was certain that Emperor Wei Yang would not dare to rebel against him, so he did not lay a hand on Jiang Wei Yang.

Emperor Wei Yang was utterly exhausted and at a loss. Fearing that his daughter might suffer the same fate as her mother, he sealed her away with his own hands.

It was not until the present era that Emperor Wei Yang, sensing a glimmer of hope in the depths of his being, broke Jiang Wei Yang free from the Dao Source, allowing her to walk the mortal realm.

He had originally had no intention of completely severing ties with the Master of Yinxu Cave, but upon learning from Senior Brother Xingsha that the Master of Yinxu Cave had actually already planned to take action against Jiang Weiyang,

Only then could he no longer contain himself. Following his long-standing plans and arrangements, he had Jiang Weiyang flee the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and the Righteous Unity Alliance.

The emergence of the Heaven Slaying Alliance also gave Emperor Wei Yang a glimmer of hope. He knew that the Master of Yinxu Cave feared Gu Changge’s power and dared not easily probe the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Thus, this place had become the only destination available to Jiang Weiyang—and the only safe haven.

Moreover, since his wife was still in the Master of Yinxu Cave’s custody, he dared not show any outward signs of betraying the Master of Yinxu Cave, nor did he dare harbor any rebellious thoughts.

It wasn’t until Jiang Weiyang arrived safely at the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the restrictions on the jade slip she had left behind were lifted

Only then did he finally confirm that Jiang Weiyang was safe and sound, and that everything was unfolding exactly as he had anticipated. Only then did he dare to appear in the form of a wisp of his true soul to meet her and explain the entire situation.

As for Jiang Weiyang’s eye condition, her mother had actually suffered from the same issue, though it was not as severe in her case.

As long as one is a descendant of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court, the individual with the purest bloodline in each successive generation would inevitably become a cursed one.

They would either die young, be born with physical deformities, or have their facial features disfigured…

Someone like Jiang Weiyang, who suffers only from blindness, is actually quite fortunate.

This curse, which seems to have been passed down from some ancient era, has defied all of Emperor Wei Yang’s efforts to find a solution. He even sought out the Master of the Yinxu Cave.

But even the Master of Yinxu Cave admitted he could not resolve it.

Jiang Weiyang is a true descendant of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court, and this curse of hers originates from the former imperial family of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court—this is the only thing Emperor Weiyang can be certain of at present.

Now, his only remaining hope rests with Gu Changge.

After listening to Emperor Wei Yang recount all of this, Jiang Wei Yang was already weeping uncontrollably, her tears falling like rain.

She never imagined that her father had been carrying such deep hatred and pain all these years.

Her father had never let her know any of this; even though her mother was absent during her childhood, he had never let a single day pass without her being happy.

She also never imagined that her mother had never actually died, but had merely been imprisoned by the Master of Yinxu Cave as a means to threaten her father.

Before this, she had harbored curiosity and longing for the Yinxu Cave, believing it to be a true utopia—untouched by the mundane world and its karma, standing apart from it all.

When the brutal reality was laid bare before her, she finally saw the true face of Yinxu Cave, and felt an icy chill run down her spine.

Gu Changge was not surprised by such underhanded tactics.

Long ago, he had already learned of Emperor Wei Yang’s subjugation by the Master of Fate. He also knew that the Emperor was secretly plotting to break free from his control; otherwise, he wouldn’t have allowed National Preceptor Xing Ming to deliberately “force” him to reveal himself, thereby making the Master of Fate wary and too afraid to personally capture Jiang Wei Yang.

At the time, Gu Changge had played along with the scheme, casually striking out with a palm strike that severely wounded the National Master Xingming, making his performance appear all the more convincing.

Even if the Master of Fate wished to uncover these events, he would have to wait until later.

As for how the Emperor of Weiyang would counter the Master of Destiny and stand up to him in the future, that was no longer Gu Changge’s concern.

Gu Changge had no interest in concerning himself with such matters either.

After recounting all this, Emperor Wei Yang did not linger; he transformed into a wisp of pure energy and returned to the jade tablet edict.

When Jiang Weiyang activates this jade tablet edict, it will also mean that his disguise before the Master of the Yinxu Cave will be completely exposed.

At that point, whether he would live or die was anyone’s guess.

Jiang Weiyang could foresee this outcome, so her spirits were low. She then curtsied gracefully and took her leave.

She had thought that after meeting Gu Changge and having all her doubts resolved, she would feel a sense of clarity. Yet the truth behind so many matters weighed even heavier on her heart, making her feel as though she bore an immeasurable burden on her shoulders.

After Jiang Weiyang left, Gu Changge glanced at her with a touch of curiosity.

For in that moment, he saw a dazzling golden radiance burst forth from Jiang Weiyang. That golden light reflected upon the river of destiny, stirring up boundless ripples.

Vaguely, he saw a future peerless empress emerging from the distant river of fate.

“Has a transformation in her state of mind caused a shift in her destiny’s status?”

“In times of peace, she would be a rare prodigy of her generation. Alas, the Vast Expanse offers no opportunity for her to grow…” Gu Changge withdrew his gaze and shook his head slightly.

As for the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, there was still the pawn known as Ji Hanjing.

Even without the words spoken today by the Emperor of Weiyang, Gu Changge would still have found a way to bring the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty under his control.

The contingency plan arranged by the Master of Destiny could, at best, make the Emperor of Weiyang hesitate to act.

And now, if there was one thing about the Master of Destiny that piqued Gu Changge’s interest, it was the Disk of Transcendence—one of the six treasures of the First Civilization—which he had seized from the Central Lord.

He had stirred up such a commotion, causing the source of the Black Calamity to descend.

On one hand, this was to instill a stronger sense of urgency in the various races and true realms of the Vast Expanse; on the other, it was intended to lure out survivors from the Primordial Era, such as the Master of Destiny.

Judging by the current situation, there is likely more than one major figure behind the Immortal Dao Alliance. As for whether it is the Western Sage, as Emperor Wei Yang surmised, that is actually irrelevant.

As long as the whereabouts of the Holy Maiden of Xi Yuan remained under Gu Changge’s control, he had no concern that the Western Sage would fail to appear—after all, it was merely a matter of time.

At first, Gu Changge did not give the six treasures of the First Civilization a second thought.

Given his current strength, he truly had no need for external aids; he hadn’t even been in a hurry to reclaim the power left behind in the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor, the Source of the Black Calamity, or the Coffin of the World’s Demise.

But ever since the aura of “Constraining Heaven” appeared, he began to wonder if another True Ancestor had become involved in the Vast Expanse’s schemes.

Therefore, he now intends to first ascertain the origin of the blueprints for those six treasures of the First Civilization.

As for the identity of the True Ancestor involved in the schemes of the Vast Expanse, it remains unknown for now, and the other party certainly cannot confirm his existence either.

One could only say that, like him, one could vaguely sense the other’s presence, but it was impossible to determine which of the other two True Ancestors it was—unless one was truly prepared to overturn the table and tear away that thin veil of mist.

The various schemes of both sides intersect across multiple timelines.

Due to the involvement of at least two True Ancestors, the worldlines—which originally followed specific trajectories across the multiverse—have become convoluted and indistinct.

Unless one is unafraid of their own schemes being exposed, many attempts at divination and probing may be obscured by the opposing parties’ auras, rendering them indistinct.

For instance, when Gu Changge investigated the origins of the Transcendence Master Plan, he sensed a faint, lingering mist.

If he had forced a deduction, his own vital energy would likely have alerted the True Ancestor who had left that trap.

At the time, he believed this might be bait deliberately left by a True Ancestor to lure the Vast Expanse.

But later, when he encountered Ye Suyi—who possessed a destiny capable of defying heaven and altering fate—he encountered the same thick fog obscuring his vision while trying to discern her past karma and future destiny.

Wave after wave of vast, impenetrable mist drifted in from nowhere, obscuring the entire river of time and the imprints of reincarnation.

How strikingly similar that scene was.

It could be said to be almost identical to the "Transcendence Framework" that appeared when he was deciphering the notes left by the Eternal Primordial God within the Biyou Heavenly Realm of the Immortal Spirit Civilization—a framework capable of blocking his scrutiny and deductions.

Ye Suyi’s status as one who transcends the mortal realm to become an immortal, defying fate and altering destiny, bears a striking resemblance to the status of the “Divergent One.”

Although the vast majority of such cases are innate, there may also be instances resulting from artificial intervention.

In Gu Changge’s view, he actually suspected that Ye Suyi’s existence was linked to human intervention—much like the “transmigrant anomaly” identity he had fabricated for himself.

"During this period, every aspect of Ye Suyi’s trajectory has been perfectly normal, just as I observed back then..."

Gu Changge, hands clasped behind his back, began to pace lightly through the courtyard.

As his gaze followed, a vast universe unfolded before him. Scenes emerged from the void: a graceful and beautiful figure was meditating within a cave shrouded in intertwining rays of rosy light, completely oblivious to his observation.

"Ever since Ye Suyi appeared, I’ve had my suspicions… especially given the many entanglements we’ve had along the future timeline."

"If I hadn’t encountered her, our paths wouldn’t have crossed in the future timeline. The future I see now is the one that emerged after I made my choice—a future I deliberately allowed a True Ancestor to glimpse..."

"Whether or not I let her follow me, the entanglements of the future will remain unchanged."

"Considering all the events of the present, Ye Suyi’s future has long been predetermined. To be precise, the future—including my current identity—has already been fully foreseen and established."

Gu Changge had come to this conclusion long ago.

This wasn’t necessarily a bad thing; at the very least, it meant his current identity had been crafted successfully. As an Anomaly, he had been accepted and recognized by another True Ancestor without arousing suspicion.

The other party had glimpsed part of the future associated with his current identity, so they deliberately set up Ye Suyi as a contingency to make contact with him.

Heaven’s Will struck a blow, severing the concept of “Heaven Slaying” from the minds of many beings across the True Realm and its Civilizations.

Precisely because the concept of “Heaven Slaying” had been diminished, the emergence of the Heaven Slaying Alliance encountered far fewer obstacles and difficulties.

The other party had long since foreseen some of Gu Changge’s intentions and plans, so they paved the way for him in advance—this could be called a “welcome gift.”

On the timeline they foresaw, he would make a comeback, just as he had in the past—acting in the name of Heaven and carrying out Heaven’s will. They had also calculated that he would reappear as the “Demon Lord.”

The other party foresaw that he might pose a threat to the True Ancestor in the future, which is why they unconsciously “assisted” him in this manner—essentially playing the same game as Gu Changge.

"It is precisely this identity of mine, as I walk through the Vast Expanse, that they are scheming against."

“It’s just a pity that the part of the future I’ve chosen is merely what I want you to see…”

At this thought, Gu Changge suddenly couldn’t help but laugh.

No matter how meticulously they calculated, they overlooked the most crucial detail.

Whether it was the former Heaven's Proxy organization, the current Heaven Slaying Alliance, or the "Demon Lord" alias—they were all merely cogs in his grand scheme.

The other party had calculated up to the third level, but in reality, Gu Changge stood on the tenth.

"What remains to be determined now is whether it is the 'Mysterious' or the 'Divine'..."

“In the future, will you choose to meet me as Ye Suyi? I, for one, look forward to that day.”
