"Weiyang greets the Alliance Leader."

Although Jiang Weiyang was blind, she could sense Gu Changge’s arrival. She hurriedly rose to her feet and curtsied gracefully, but in her haste to stand, she accidentally tripped over the hem of her skirt. With a soft gasp, she stumbled forward.

In that split second, a flurry of humiliating thoughts flashed through Jiang Weiyang’s mind. She never imagined that on her very first formal meeting, she would find herself in such a disheveled and embarrassing situation.

However, the next moment, she did not feel the pain of hitting the ground as she had imagined. A warm hand reached out and steadied her.

Accompanied by a voice as smooth as jade, he said, “Miss Wei Yang, there’s no need for such formal courtesies.”

“I’ve truly made a fool of myself before the Alliance Leader.”

Jiang Weiyang’s cheeks flushed with embarrassment. She quickly steadied herself. At such close range, Gu Changge’s voice was even more mellow and pleasant than she had imagined—like a clear spring flowing over mountain rocks, with the texture of jade.

She shook her head inwardly, quickly quelling the jumble of thoughts swirling in her mind.

The man before her was the Alliance Master of the Heaven Slaying Alliance; even her own father would treat him with the utmost respect, never daring to show the slightest disrespect or negligence.

If he sensed her wandering thoughts, it would be trouble.

Gu Changge seemed to chuckle to himself, then motioned for Jiang Weiyang to sit down beside him. He poured a cup of clear tea and pushed it toward her.

“Miss Wei Yang, there’s no need to be so formal. You are Yao Yao’s friend; just think of me as an elder,” he said in a gentle voice.

Jiang Weiyang held the teacup with both hands, momentarily at a loss for words. All the things she had planned to say on her way here had vanished from her mind the moment she arrived.

She stood there in a daze for a moment, until a faint blush spread across her cheeks. Only then did she take a few deep breaths to gradually calm her nerves.

“It’s my first time meeting the Alliance Leader, so I’m a little nervous…”

True to her upbringing in high-profile settings, she quickly regained her composure and smiled.

Gu Changge waved his hand, his expression unchanged, and said, “It’s all right. May I ask what brings you here, Miss Wei Yang?”

Jiang Weiyang took a deep breath and said earnestly, “Actually, the reason for my bold visit today is to ask the Alliance Leader about matters concerning the Dark Clans. I learned from Yao Yao that the Alliance Leader apparently possesses the means to help sentient beings rid themselves of dark substances, allowing those who have fallen into darkness to return to the world.”

After speaking, she fixed her gaze on Gu Changge. Of course, he couldn’t see her, but she was eager to know how he would respond.

At that moment, she didn’t even realize that as she spoke, her entire body had tensed up, and cold sweat had broken out on her palms.

For this matter carried far-reaching implications; it held extraordinary significance for the entire Vast Expanse clan and its lineage.

If Gu Changge truly had a solution, perhaps the various Daoist lineages and civilizations would not appear so passive and powerless when facing the Dark Clan.

Upon hearing this, Gu Changge seemed to ponder for a moment before nodding. “I do indeed have a way to eliminate the dark substance, but this method is not without its drawbacks. Furthermore, I am currently unable to purge the dark substance from many people at once, as the process itself requires me to use my cultivation to suppress and neutralize it…”

Upon hearing this, Jiang Weiyang understood, and a look of gloom instantly fell over her face.

“Even you, Alliance Master, have no solution?”

she murmured.

Jiang Weiyang thought back to the various rumors she’d heard within the Heaven Slaying Alliance regarding Gu Changge. The most frequently mentioned point was that within the Heaven Slaying Alliance, one need not worry about being attacked by dark entities.

Even if one were accidentally tainted or corrupted by dark matter, the Alliance Master had the means to expel and heal it.

This filled Jiang Weiyang with hope, for she had long suspected and feared that her father, Jiang Yun, might have been corrupted by dark matter at some unknown point—which would explain his drastic change in temperament and the many actions that no longer matched his former character.

This included ordering her to stay away from the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and any place associated with the Righteous Unity Alliance.

At first, Jiang Weiyang didn’t understand, but when the Star-Illuminating National Preceptor later appeared, intending to capture her and take her back, she suddenly realized that perhaps even her father was now acting against his will.

And as his daughter, the only thing she could do seemed to be finding a way to help him expel the dark substance.

“There is a way, but it depends on the circumstances,” Gu Changge said, taking a sip of tea, his expression as composed as ever.

To him, the dark substance was no different from ordinary spiritual energy; whether he chose to expel it or absorb it was merely a matter of a single thought.

He also understood Jiang Weiyang’s meaning: Emperor Jiang Yun was facing a major crisis, and she suspected he might have been corrupted by the dark substance, which was why she wanted him to intervene.

And Jiang Yun’s decision to have Jiang Weiyang come to the Heaven Slaying Alliance was certainly not without this consideration.

However, this offered no tangible benefit to Gu Changge. He still needed Jiang Yun as a pawn to lure the Master of Destiny out into the open.

“Depending on the situation?” Jiang Weiyang murmured to herself. That explanation made sense, but did it really depend on the circumstances?

She didn’t dare to simply tell Gu Changge about her father, Jiang Yun’s, situation outright. It wasn’t that she didn’t trust Gu Changge, but rather that she felt this news was too shocking at the moment and could easily cause panic among all parties.

After all, her father was not only the Emperor of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty but also the Leader of the Righteous Unity Alliance.

If even her father had been tainted by the dark substance, then where in the Vast Expanse could possibly be safe?

“Thank you, Alliance Leader, for clearing this up.”

Jiang Weiyang rose and curtsied gracefully. Beneath her gauze veil, her expression was complex as she couldn’t help but ask, “If, in the future, Weiyang were to ask the Alliance Leader to intervene and remove the Dark Matter, what price would she have to pay?”

Upon hearing this, Gu Changge seemed to be pondering; she did not answer directly, but asked, “Do you have any friends or relatives who have been corrupted by the Dark Substance?”

Jiang Weiyang’s eyes fluttered slightly, her lashes trembling, as she replied in a somewhat somber tone, “I’m worried that such a situation might arise in the future.”

Gu Changge merely smiled and said, “If it’s just a minor matter like that, then it shouldn’t be a problem. Since you’re a friend of Yao Yao’s, I can consider helping you this once.”

“Really?” Hearing this, Jiang Weiyang was overjoyed, her voice trembling slightly.

Realizing that her tone sounded a bit like she was questioning Gu Changge, she hurriedly added, “It’s not that I don’t believe the Alliance Leader’s words; I’m just so surprised. Please don’t take offense.”

Gu Changge sipped his tea and smiled casually. “It’s fine. However, I do have a request of my own.”

Jiang Weiyang replied immediately, “Please speak, Alliance Leader. Whatever request you make, as long as it is within my power, I will agree to it.”

Perhaps sensing that such a statement sounded a bit odd, a faint blush suddenly flushed her snow-white cheeks.

Gu Changge spoke slowly, “You are so intelligent; you must surely understand the current Vast Expanse situation. The Immortal Dao Alliance is about to be established, and the Righteous Unity Alliance may soon join forces with them to collectively oppose our Heaven Slaying Alliance. Although the Emperor of Weiyang is your biological father, judging by his current circumstances, he can barely save himself—so we cannot count on him to do anything.”

“The Weiyang Immortal Court needs a figure capable of stabilizing the people’s hearts. Although an ancient ancestor has recently emerged there, do you really think this person can command the respect of the masses and make the entire Weiyang Immortal Court obey his commands?”

Upon hearing this, Jiang Weiyang was taken aback.

How did Gu Changge know that her father was already in such a precarious position?

However, she quickly grasped the meaning of his second remark. Her dark brows furrowed slightly. After a moment’s thought, she asked tentatively, “Are you suggesting, Lord Alliance Leader, that I should take control of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty in my father’s name?”

Although she was the sole heir of the Emperor of Weiyang, expecting her to win the loyalty of the people was, to some extent, overestimating her capabilities.

After all, she had refused the arranged marriage with the Ninth Prince of the Ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao, which was why she had fled the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty in the first place. Even if she returned now, her position would lack legitimacy.

Moreover, the Weiyang Immortal Court was now, to her, no different from a den of tigers and dragons—countless times more dangerous than the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

She could not forget the incident not long ago when the Star-Illuminating National Preceptor came to capture her and bring her back on orders.

She had also heard about that ancient ancestor—apparently emerging from the ruins of the former Yao Guang Ancestral Court, with a lineage so ancient it was terrifying.

Now that her father was not in the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, all affairs were being managed and controlled by that ancient ancestor.

Under that ancient ancestor’s governance, the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty remained stable and orderly, with no reports of any rebellious rebels emerging.

Gu Changge shook her head and said, “What I mean is, I want you to give the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty a legitimate reason to disregard the Weiyang Emperor’s orders.”

“When your father sent you fleeing the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, he must have given you something, right?”

Although Ji Hanjing was, in terms of lineage, the ancestral progenitor of Jiang Weiyang and the others,

However, the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty was, after all, created by the Weiyang Emperor Jiang Yun himself. He still presides over the Righteous Unity Alliance and has not yet passed away; his authority remains intact.

Even if Ji Hanjing were to insist that Jiang Yun had colluded with the Dark Clans and sought to depose him from his position as Emperor, it would be of little consequence.

As long as Jiang Yun lives, he remains the true master of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty.

And someone as shrewd as Jiang Yun, who devised a plan to cleverly send his daughter Jiang Weiyang to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, must have foreseen the many events to come long ago. Therefore, he must have left some evidence behind as a contingency.

Rather than being the Emperor of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, he is more accurately a puppet of the Master of Destiny, and the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty is merely one of the Master of Destiny’s tools.

Upon hearing Gu Changge’s words, Jiang Weiyang froze once more, then realized what he meant. Her beautiful eyes widened involuntarily, her face filled with an expression of utter disbelief and shock.

How on earth did Gu Changge know all this? She hadn’t told him a single thing.

Apart from her father, only she herself knew about this.

Not even her most loyal maidservants knew.

Emperor Weiyang had indeed given her a silk pouch at the time, and it did contain quite a few items.

Aside from a few life-saving items, there were other objects whose purposes Jiang Wei Yang didn’t fully understand.

Moreover, what truly shocked and astonished her was how Gu Changge could possibly know that her father had actively allowed her to flee the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty?

There was likely not a third person in the entire Weiyang Immortal Dynasty who knew of this matter, yet Gu Changge, who was billions of miles away, knew it inside out, as clear as day.

“You needn’t be surprised. With a little deduction and speculation, one can easily discern the truth of these matters.”

“The Emperor of Weiyang would never have sent his daughter to form an alliance through marriage with the ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao without good reason.” Gu Changge blew on the steam rising from his tea, took a light sip, and remarked casually.

“Nothing can escape the Alliance Leader’s notice.”

Jiang Weiyang quickly regained her composure, though her eyes sparkled with admiration and reverence.
