The Zhe River shimmers in the light, its waters flowing ceaselessly as merchant ships sail downstream.

Waves crashed against the ship’s hull, producing a rumbling sound. Wave after wave was shattered, then collided mid-air, forming a silvery curtain of spray that drifted down in a gentle shower.

A wisp of mist drifted in through the cracks in the portholes, carrying the briny scent of the river—a touch of the mundane, earthly world.

The tabletop was damp, with a few water stains, yet Gu Changge took a leisurely sip of tea, showing no hurry to explain.

Ji Hanjing stared at him blankly, her eyes filled with confusion, waiting for him to continue.

“Aren’t you seeking revenge?” Gu Changge set down his teacup and said slowly.

Ji Hanjing nodded, her eyes burning with hatred. “I think of revenge every single moment. Ever since my sister betrayed me, I’ve longed to turn back the river of time, to go back to the past myself, and make her taste every bit of the pain and despair I’ve endured.”

Her tone was filled with unmasked, searing hatred, as if she longed to grind her own sister’s bones to dust, devour her flesh, and suck out her marrow.

However, just as she spoke these words, she paused.

For she recalled what Gu Changge had told her: her greatest enemy was not her sister, but someone else entirely.

Ji Hanjing had never understood what that meant, and she believed Gu Changge would never deceive her about such a matter.

“I’ve told you before: your greatest enemy isn’t your sister. All these years, you’ve actually been hating the wrong person. But that’s all right. I brought you here today precisely to reveal all of this to you,” Gu Changge said with a meaningful smile.

Ji Hanjing looked at him with growing confusion.

“The Yao Guang Ancestral River is the last remaining trace of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court. You’ve been wanting to defy time and space to return to the past, haven’t you?” Gu Changge smiled faintly.

As his words fell, Ji Hanjing sensed an inexplicable, unfathomable energy emerge.

The entire world fell into an eerie silence. Merchant ships came to a standstill, the waves subsided, and even the misty vapors that had been sweeping toward them now swirled and rose like fog in the air.

The world suddenly grew still and stagnant. The passage of time first blurred, then twisted, and finally came to a halt, as if frozen right before her.

Only the strikingly handsome young man before her continued to sip his tea at a leisurely pace, his gaze serene and undisturbed.

"All traces of cause and effect have vanished; the boundless karmic retribution one must endure when traversing the river of time has also disappeared."

Ji Hanjing’s eyes widened in astonishment, and she couldn’t help but whisper.

In an ordinary universe, or even a typical True Realm, traversing the river of time—whether retracing to the dawn of the ancient past or journeying into the future—would be effortless for her. She would face no interference from karmic forces and need not worry about disrupting her own original spacetime causal threads.

But in the Vastness, even at the peak of her power, she dared not do so—nor could she.

For the higher one’s power and realm, the more terrifying the karmic consequences of such actions become; moreover, one might attract inexplicable, unspeakable gazes from above.

Yet Gu Changge could disrupt the boundless spacetime without the slightest hesitation or qualms.

Ji Hanjing’s gaze fell upon the scene, and the space-time that had just been frozen began to flow once more. Beyond the ship’s windows, the sound of raging waves echoed—boundless surges engulfed the heavens and earth. The spiritual energy surged with such intensity that it was impossible to distinguish whether the waters before them were those of a river or a torrent of spiritual energy.

"The Ancestral River of Shaking Light..."

Ji Hanjing murmured to herself, then rose and pushed open the ship’s window.

A boundless river unfolded before her eyes, seeming to merge with the heavens and earth, with no end in sight. Endless white waves and raging tides crashed and churned across the river’s surface, permeated by an ancient, primal aura.

Along the riverbanks, divine radiance shone brilliantly and immortal light surged. Divine mountains and ancient peaks stood tall, while immortal cities and divine realms were situated there, exuding the utmost prosperity and tranquility.

"In the dawn of time, who imparted the Way? When heaven and earth had not yet taken shape, how could one verify it..."In the distance, Ji Hanjing heard ancient cultivators chanting from afar. A majestic, immortal celestial dynasty stood at the edge of the heavens and earth, situated at the source of the Yao Guang Ancestral River; that celestial dynasty was called Yao Guang.

Her eyes filled with nostalgia as she stepped out, walking far along the undulating river. Gu Changge remained seated by the ship’s window, simply watching her quietly, without disturbing her.

This was the era of Yao Guang Immortal Dynasty’s greatest prosperity, and also the time when Ji Hanjing proclaimed herself Empress.

Ji Hanjing traveled upstream along the Yao Guang Ancestral River, passing through all the traditional sects and factions, all the nations and tribes along the way. No one could see her; she was like a passing traveler, until she finally reached the source of the Yao Guang Ancestral River.

Palaces stretched endlessly, immortal towers hung high, corridors wound like ribbons, eaves soared like beaks, and honeycomb-like water whirlpools rose into the sky, standing tall for who knows how many millions of stories.

Deep within the grand hall, a court lady, dressed in full regalia and holding a jade tablet, stood with dignified grace, her head bowed as she reported something. Ji Hanjing saw her younger self there—full of vigor and vitality, in the prime of her youth, radiating immense majesty and grandeur.

Every minister trembled, bowing their heads as they stood in attendance, not daring to breathe a word.

Yet who could have imagined that in the end, it would be these very ministers of hers—joining forces with the sister she trusted most—who, after she had shouldered the burden of the Eon’s Winter and the apocalyptic reckoning for everyone, would betray her at her weakest moment and force her, alive, into the very heart of the Civilization’s most precious treasure.

A wry smile touched Ji Hanjing’s lips as she drifted past her past self like a ghost.

Would her past self ever have imagined that one day, she would be watched by her present self?

Of course, not even Ji Hanjing could have imagined that it was not just her, but the entire ancient history—indeed, the entire fabric of time and space—that lay under Gu Changge’s gaze.

The past, the future, and even the present—all were under his gaze.

Who exactly is he?

Ji Han Jing couldn’t help but let out a soft sigh. The world’s mysteries and the inexplicable—perhaps only those who have walked to the very end of the path have the chance to glimpse them.

There was a time when she was incredibly arrogant, comparing herself to her parents, looking down on everyone else, and believing her achievements would be no less than theirs.

The Cold Winter of the Era, the apocalyptic reckoning—so what? She had weathered it alone.

But back then, she was nothing more than a frog in a well, believing that the rim of that well was the entire world.

Ji Han Jing walked through palace after palace, seeing many once-familiar faces, though there were some whose names she could no longer recall.

Time is the greatest—and most ruthless—force of all; it erodes memories and temperaments, and she was no exception.

When she was once sealed within the Book of Scavengers, her fury shook the heavens and her hatred knew no bounds. She swore that the day she broke free, she would make those who had betrayed her pay a terrible price, forcing them to live out the rest of their lives in endless despair and agony.

Yet when she saw these people again, her heart was incredibly calm, so much so that she could barely detect a ripple of emotion—for she no longer even recognized some of them.

Finally, Ji Hanjing arrived before a palace. She stood there for a long time before attempting to push open the doors and step inside. But the moment her hand touched the door, she realized:

She wasn’t a person of this era—why should she hesitate?

Inside the hall, a woman who bore a slight resemblance to Ji Hanjing, her gaze gentle, was cradling a swaddled infant by the bedside, softly humming an ancient lullaby.

“Do you have any other plans in reserve?”

Gu Changge, aboard the merchant ship, withdrew his gaze. A thoughtful look flashed in his eyes, and then he took a step forward, leaving this era behind.

Meanwhile, the news that the Dark Beings and the Zhengyi Alliance had reached a peace agreement sent shockwaves throughout the entire Vast Expanse that very day.

Countless civilizations, realms, and ancestral lineages were shaken and horrified, finding it hard to believe.

No one could have imagined that the initial rumors would actually come true and become reality.

In the end, the Zhengyi Alliance had indeed reached an agreement with the Dark Beings, ceding territory and relinquishing vast swathes of cosmic domain.

They also provided the Dark Spirits with vast numbers of living beings to serve as offerings for their grand rituals.

In exchange, the Dark Beings agreed to cease their attacks on the Zhengyi Alliance’s territories, granting the remaining members of the Alliance a brief respite of peace.

In these chaotic and turbulent times, the sensation caused by this news was nothing short of staggering, leaving countless cultivators and sentient beings utterly bewildered.

According to some members of the Zhengyi Alliance, the one currently leading the alliance is none other than Emperor Jiang Yun of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty.

It was through his deliberate efforts that the dark beings reached a peace agreement with the Zhengyi Alliance.

Furthermore, many high-ranking officials within the Zhengyi Alliance have also endorsed this agreement; in fact, such plans had been in the works for quite some time.

It was only the recent incursion by the dark beings that accelerated the implementation of this plan.

This was inevitable. From the very beginning, the Zhengyi Alliance had never cared about the survival of the member factions in the outlying territories; they had merely treated them as cannon fodder.

These reports caused a massive stir, while also sending a chill down the spine—a chill that ran deep.

It even led people to speculate whether the Zhengyi Alliance’s high command had long been infiltrated by the Dark Beings—otherwise, why would they be so humiliatingly eager to make peace?

Isn’t the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty one of the few colossal powers remaining in this vast, desolate world? Why, then, do they lack even the courage to go to war against the Dark Beings?

Furthermore, numerous traditional powers—including the Bosha Sacred Domain and the Ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao—have actually aligned themselves with the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and the Zhengyi Alliance, striking agreements with the Dark Beings.

These revelations were so shocking and unbelievable that people began to suspect the truth behind the earlier reports from the Heaven Slaying Alliance: that the envoys dispatched by the Zhengyi Alliance had indeed intended to assassinate the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

It was not merely a case of “where there’s a will, there’s a way.”

Against this backdrop, the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s actions, in contrast, appeared all the more normal and upright.

After all, when the Dark Beings invaded, the Heaven Slaying Alliance abandoned its plans to continue attacking the Zhengyi Alliance, instead turning its weapons against the Dark Beings. It joined forces with numerous civilizations and sects to fight the Dark Beings, thereby safeguarding their safety.

At the same time, some beings worried that once the Zhengyi Alliance and the Dark Beings reached a peace treaty, would all the resulting conflicts then be directed at the Heaven Slaying Alliance?

However, this worry was clearly unfounded.

After the Zhengyi Alliance and the Dark Beings reached their pact, the Heaven Slaying Alliance showed no signs of worry or hesitation. It remained as formidable as ever, with Ancestral Dao Realm beings from all quarters emerging to command the great army and reclaim the various realms and universes that had been overrun by the Dark Beings.

The appearance of numerous Ancestral Dao Realm figures from the Heaven Slaying Alliance during this great war has drawn the attention and caused a stir throughout the entire Vast Expanse.

Existences at the Ancestral Dao Realm are, without a doubt, the pinnacle of this era; even among Immortal-tier Daoist factions, there are typically only one or two such figures stationed to hold the fort.

Even in the Supreme Realms and Supreme Civilizations, the number of Ancestral Dao Realm beings is unlikely to be large, unless, like the Yuan Hope Civilization, they possess a Civilization Treasure to safeguard them, with foundations of unimaginable depth.

But in the vast expanse of the present, how many Civilization Realms possess a Civilization Treasure to guard them?

There were only six original Civilization Treasures.

Many of the Civilization Treasures forged in later generations as imitations of the original ones have been destroyed in the long river of time, with not even a fragment remaining; those that still exist today can be counted on one hand.

Under these circumstances, the power of the Ancestral Dao Realm becomes all the more critical. Though the Heaven Slaying Alliance has existed for only a few thousand years, the number of beings within the Ancestral Dao Realm it possesses has already reached a terrifying and awe-inspiring level.

In this great war, the few Ancestral Dao Realm beings that appeared from the Heaven Slaying Alliance possessed strength far surpassing that of those who had merely survived eight Celestial Waning Tribulations, leading people to wonder if they were already preparing to undergo their ninth Celestial Waning Tribulation—and thus touch upon the very pinnacle of the Dao.

Where did these beings come from, and why do they now all serve the Heaven Slaying Alliance in unison?

The depth of power displayed by the Heaven Slaying Alliance is increasingly awe-inspiring and awe-striking.
