As for the scriptures and ancient texts—which contained a wealth of knowledge—they too were taken away. The pavilions and palaces throughout the complex were stripped bare, leaving a desolate, eerie silence.

Each group of disciples was led by an elder, but where they would ultimately flee to, and what dangers awaited them ahead, remained a complete mystery.

After Gu Xian’er, Lan Xin, and the others were escorted back to Crescent Moon Sect, they each began packing their belongings, their hearts heavy with sorrow.

It was estimated that within a few days, the dark creatures would breach the city walls and storm into the heartland.

The Crescent Moon Sect was no exception. The recent great battles had already claimed the lives of many disciples and elders; those who had survived were all elite.

Deep into the night, the darkness was thick, the moonlight shimmered like water, and the scent of wine was intoxicating.

They had not chosen to be sent away; instead, they had decided to stay. Many had been born and raised within the Crescent Moon Sect, regarding it as their home.

So, even knowing it meant certain death, they still chose to stay.
