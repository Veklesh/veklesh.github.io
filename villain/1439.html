"With such momentous events unfolding in the Vast Expanse, it is time for the Nine Heavens to step into the mortal world. I just wonder what changes the situation in the Vast Expanse will undergo once the Immortal Dao Alliance is established."

"Judging by the current situation, the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance likely won’t stand in the way of the Immortal Dao Alliance’s formation."

"But why do I keep having this bad feeling..."

"You must have noticed, too, the sudden surge of cultists appearing around the Heaven Slaying Alliance."

"The emergence of the Black Lotus Cult is quite timely, actually. Moreover, neither of us has been able to find any records or origins related to them. The Lord of the Black Lotus that these followers worship is connected to the Dark Clans."

“Could it be connected to the former Heaven's Proxy organization? Their sudden appearance around the Heaven Slaying Alliance—is there some causal link at play?”

The scarred man and the black dog stood in the void, gazing at the vast, unfathomable, and eerie spectacle within the Heaven Slaying Alliance. Both couldn’t help but shake their heads as they speculated.

The Vast Expanse of uncertainty kept shifting. Even beings at the Dao Realm like them felt trapped, their future uncertain, filled with anxiety and unease.

Ordinary beings were likely as fragile as those illusory bubbles—ready to shatter at the slightest touch, not even worthy of serving as cannon fodder.

"Well, even if we wanted to intervene, we have no right to do so. We can only sit back and watch how things unfold."

"The most urgent matter now is to return to the Nine Heavens and seek refuge. I feel that if we continue to linger in the Vast Expanse, we might well meet our end any day now..."

The scarred man sighed, then vanished from the spot along with his dog.

After they had left, Gu Changge of the Heaven Slaying Alliance finally withdrew his gaze, looking somewhat pensive.

In truth, ever since Yao Yao had used that life-saving artifact to “force” him to reveal himself, he had known that someone had been keeping tabs on Jiang Weiyang’s movements all along—and the causal connection behind it all was closely tied to Gu Xian’er.

That was why he had chosen not to intervene, allowing events to unfold on their own.

This man and his dog hailed from the Nine Heavens. To the entire Vast Expanse, the Nine Heavens held a transcendent status, revered as the birthplace of the Immortal Way and the very edge of the heavens.

For countless eons, countless powerful beings seeking immortality and the Way have sought out the Nine Heavens, hoping to enter its realm.

Long before the Prosperous Dao True Realm was renamed, there was a man of great destiny named Qingfeng who left the Mountains And Seas True Realm. Following clues left by predecessors of the Immortal Palace, he set out to find the Nine Heavens, hoping to summon reinforcements to prevent a great catastrophe in the Mountains And Seas True Realm.

Gu Changge was aware of this at the time but did not intervene in any way.

He understood the Nine Heavens better than anyone else; it was not, in fact, the source of the Immortal Way, but rather a celestial graveyard.

From ancient times to the present, whenever a great calamity descended, many overconfident individuals attempted to "Heaven Slay" to block the calamity. The will of the Heavenly Dao within the True Realm’s universe would shatter and fall during those great battles, transforming into the Qi of the Heavenly Dao or fragments of the Heavenly Dao, scattered across the battlefields.

The Nine Heavens is thus a realm composed of numerous shattered Heaven Slaying battlefields.

Many ancient "Heaven Slaying" warriors are buried there, and there are also numerous so-called "heavenly graveyards." Due to their unique aura and chaotic laws, they have been assimilated by the aura of the Great Calamity, transforming the area into a "transcendent" zone immune to its onslaught.

But to Gu Changge, this was merely a “loophole” in the workings of the Way of Heaven amidst the Vast Expanse.

In all the realms of existence, aside from those void-time dimensions, there is no territory that has escaped the baptism of the Great Calamity.

Even the mightiest True Realms, having weathered nine Great Calamities, are no exception. It is merely that, with each calamity overcome, the great tree of their existence grows ever more lush and massive, its influence ever-expanding—thus they no longer face minor calamities so easily.

In the face of the Epoch Cataclysm, which truly settles all accounts, all sentient beings and realms are equal—even the mightiest True Realms.

From ancient times to the present, realms have arisen and perished, having endured countless Cataclysms—too many to count.

If, as the Vast Expanse of sentient beings believes, one is spared from the onslaught of cataclysms after surviving nine of them, then just how many Supreme Realms would there be throughout the entire cosmos?

But for ordinary beings, they are fundamentally unaware of these matters. Throughout their entire lifetimes, they live at most within their own world and that particular universe.

Even when that world withers and shatters, and their race fades away according to the natural course of things, they will never even know what a "calamity" truly is.

It is much like the saying, “It is cooler under a large tree.”

The newly formed and ancient realms that rely on the Supreme Realms, during the span from their birth to their natural demise, never experience the Cataclysm. Naturally, they come to believe that as long as they become realms like the Supreme Realms, they need never fear the day the Cataclysm descends.

Over such an immense span of time, the Epoch Cataclysm arrives, and even the Supreme Realms perish and shatter, as all eras are renewed and begin anew.

Only a very small number of beings—those who understood the truth and were powerful enough—survived by hiding in unknown realms of time and space, lying dormant in silence, hoping to weather the next Epoch Cataclysm.

Under such circumstances, the number of those who engage in “Heaven Slaying” naturally dwindled. Initially, the audacity to “defy Heaven” stemmed from ignorance and fearlessness, but once one truly understood what “Heaven” truly is, all that remained was profound reverence and dread.

Heaven cannot be defied.

This is a law that has existed since the dawn of the Vast Expanse, eternal and unchanging.

Consequently, the Nine Heavens naturally became the refuge for those who had survived the Epoch Cataclysm—the truly ancient beings, resilient enough to endure.

For instance, the true ancestral lands of the Gu family of the Prosperous Dao True Realm are actually situated within the Nine Heavens.

The Gu family ancestor in the Nine Heavens can truly only be called the Ninth Ancestor; the body he currently possesses in the Prosperous Dao True Realm is merely a vessel he has manifested.

His continued presence in the Prosperous Dao True Realm is solely for the purpose of seeking the True Spirit that the distant ancestor of the Gu family once lost.

However, now, the Gu family’s ancestor may have sensed something and is likely to return to the Nine Heavens.

Back then, after the Immortal Palace of the Mountains And Seas True Realm was destroyed, the Gu family’s ancestor had guided some of the palace’s survivors, instructing them to flee to the Nine Heavens.

It is precisely for this reason that the records concerning the Nine Heavens in the later generations of the Mountains And Seas True Realm were actually left behind by the Gu family’s ancestor at that time.

Yet even the Gu family’s ancestor did not know that the destruction of the Immortal Palace was merely a scheme orchestrated between Gu Changge and Qingyi.

Gu Changge did this to make the “Demon Lord” alias even more seamless, ensuring that his identity as one who roams the Vast Expanse would align with the Anomaly.

Of course, the most crucial reason was that after the Ruler of Heaven was sealed, he needed a new identity to serve as a temporary substitute—one that would ensure all logic remained consistent and self-coherent.

Even now that his identity as the Ruler in the Heavens’ Steed has been exposed, when considering the various projections of past and future events, every trajectory of his actions remains logical, seamless, and free of any flaws.

After the Black Calamity ended, he, as the Ruler of Heaven, was sealed and fell into a deep slumber.

Years later, as the power of the seal weakened, he awoke. A strand of his power then broke free from the source of the Black Calamity, taking the name "Demon Lord" and emerging into the Vast Expanse.

At that very moment, the Ji Chan clan of the Ancient Zang Civilization had angered the "Ancestor" of the Realm of Truth because the Epoch Mother Tree had absorbed much of the fortune and primordial essence from the Vast Expanse. The Epoch Mother Tree was struck by a palm from the sky, severing it and cutting off all its life force.

As a result, the Ji Chan clan became the scapegoats of the Ancient Zang Civilization.

With the Eon Mother Tree shattered, the Ancient Zang civilization—where the Ji Chan clan resided at the time—nearly collapsed and faced annihilation, teetering on the brink of extinction.

Thus, to appease the wrath from the other side, the High Priest of Ancient Zang managed to communicate with them and learned that a sacrificial offering was required.

This is why, later on, the ninth princess of the Ji Chan clan, Qingyi, wrapped herself in a scroll to carry the spark of the Immortal Path Civilization and flee.

And that is why, during the Great Calamity, Qingyi faced peril and was saved by Gu Changge, who had at that time transformed into the Demon Lord…

It may seem that every action and reaction is bound by cause and effect, but in truth, all of this fate was predetermined from the very beginning.

Gu Changge had arranged everything long ago.

"Xian’er should have returned to the Nine Heavens by now. Her presence must have been detected by the Gu family there..." Gu Changge’s gaze was deep and inscrutable.

Though he did not wish to admit it, Gu Xian’er had long been a crucial piece in his plan.

Now that Gu Xian’er had been chosen by the forbidden power of “Jiu Tian,” was this not a contingency plan devised by that hidden entity to counter his very identity?

The other party believed they had foreseen his future and his destiny, which is why they had so brazenly struck at his weakness.

In the timeline he had chosen, Gu Xian’er was indeed his Achilles’ heel, his red line—and all along, he had acted as if that were the case.

But was that really the case?

In truth, even Gu Changge could no longer say for certain. Perhaps there was genuine affection, or perhaps it was mostly calculation.

But the human heart is, after all, complex. All this time, he has firmly believed in judging by actions, not intentions.

There has never been a clear-cut definition of good and evil, let alone of selfless love and kindness.

Besides, he isn’t really a human being in the true sense of the word…

For this reason, he has been constantly seeking ways to make amends to those around him—Yue Mingkong, Jiang Chuchu, and others—so that they might feel that thread of warmth.

Meanwhile, in a distant, vast, and ancient land.

On a vast cliff shrouded in swirling white mist.

The group from Crescent Moon Sect, including Gu Xian’er and the woman in white, stood there—some frowning, some gazing intently, some deep in thought—staring at the middle-aged man in white before them.

The middle-aged man in white sat cross-legged in silence, a chessboard set before him. His expression was serene and detached, exuding an air of transcendence.

A mysterious aura swirled around him, as if billions of stars were circling him, rising and falling unpredictably, giving rise to boundless immortal energy.

He looked every bit the part of a true, ancient immortal who had attained the Dao.

This was a majestic mountain range, teeming with surging vital energy and shrouded in primordial chaos. Bathed in auspicious rosy clouds, even the soil glowed, while immortal herbs swayed, filling the air with a heady fragrance.

The middle-aged man in white before them was addressed by Song Yu as the Elder Grave Keeper.

Yet ever since Gu Xian’er and the others arrived here, they noticed he had been staring intently at the chessboard, seemingly motionless since time immemorial, paying them no heed.

Song Yu had been waiting respectfully by his side the entire time, never disturbing him.

"We have finally reached the Realm of the Nine Heavens..."

Beside Gu Xian’er and the others stood a slender woman in a loose, blue robe. With delicate features and long hair pinned back with a wooden hairpin, she surveyed her surroundings, a hint of curiosity in her eyes.
