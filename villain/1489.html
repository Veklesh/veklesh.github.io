Inside the ancestral hall, all the Gu family elders gazed at Gu Xian'er with the utmost reverence. However, as soon as the First Ancestor finished speaking, Gu Xian'er furrowed her brow and a hint of annoyance seemed to cross her face.

"I am not a distant ancestor of the Gu family."

She repeated it once more.

Then, ignoring the assembly of elders, she walked out of the ancestral hall on her own.

Ever since she had been brought back to the Gu family’s ancestral lands, she had not yet learned many of the truths before the First Ancestor of the Gu family sent her into that temporal vortex. This had actually caused a deep-seated resentment to build up within Gu Xian’er’s heart.

If the Gu family’s distant ancestor were to reappear within that temporal vortex and merge with her will, would she still be herself then?

Although that didn’t happen, the First Ancestor of the Gu family certainly knew what was at stake. Sending her there without her knowledge—what was that supposed to mean?

What did that make of her?

All of this left Gu Xian’er harboring a certain degree of resistance and aversion toward the Gu family here.

Even in the Gu family of the former Upper Realm, she never felt a deep sense of belonging. She had always been accustomed to going her own way and had never once considered relying on her clan.

Watching Gu Xian’er leave the ancestral hall, the Gu family elders all wore complex expressions, some seeming to hold back words.

The First Ancestor of the Gu family sighed softly and waved his hand. “Everyone, please step down for now. Our distant ancestor will sort everything out on her own in due time.”

“No one should disturb her right now.”

“Our distant ancestor will always be our distant ancestor. Though she has not yet fully returned, within the Gu family, she remains the supreme authority.”

“Yes, First Ancestor.”

The assembly of Gu family elders withdrew as instructed.

However, Gu Wuwang hesitated for a moment, then stopped in his tracks, not leaving with the others.

He bowed his hands and said, "First Ancestor, I have something to say, but I’m not sure if I should speak."

The First Ancestor of the Gu family looked at him and nodded. "Gu Wuwang, the Ancestor hails from the True Realm of Mountains And Seas, which you were originally tasked with investigating. Logically speaking, since the Ancestor has returned to the Gu family, you deserve great credit and should receive the rewards you are due. Yet, it was not you who found the Ancestor; had it not been for the clan’s message, you likely would still be unaware of the Ancestor’s identity even now..."

Gu Wuwang suddenly felt a bit awkward and said, “What kind of being is the Ancestor? How could I possibly have deduced it? During all those years I spent guarding the True Realm of Mountains And Seas, I never once sensed Xian’er… or the Ancestor’s presence.”“To be honest, this is truly shameful. If not for the news from the clan, I might still be in Prosperous Dao—no, in the Mountains And Seas True Realm…”

The First Ancestor likely still doesn’t know that the Mountains And Seas True Realm has been renamed the Prosperous Dao True Realm.

Because of Gu Changge’s existence, the Prosperous Dao True Realm cannot be divined; the myriad causes and effects related to it are veiled like mist.

In fact, Gu Wuwang suspected that even the First Ancestor standing before him had no memories of Prosperous Dao in his mind and was unaware of what had transpired in the Prosperous Dao True Realm.

In her calculations and perceptions, she might still believe she has been guarding the "Mountains And Seas True Realm" all along, and that the Mountains And Seas True Realm remains, to this day, a newly reborn true realm that has survived two Great Calamities.

The truth as perceived by the First Ancestor through her calculations was not real at all.

Before leaving the Prosperous Dao True Realm, Gu Wuwang had never imagined such a situation could arise…

“This truly is not your fault. However, I do not blame you either. Having been away from our ancestral lands for so many eons, even if you have no merits to your name, you have certainly endured hardships.” The First Ancestor of the Gu Clan nodded in agreement, interpreting Gu Wuwang’s words as a fear of punishment, and immediately reassured him that he need not worry.

Gu Wuwang clasped his hands and said, “First Ancestor, you see clearly. But that is not what I wish to speak of…”

He was quite straightforward, making no attempt to take credit for himself. Back in the Mountains And Seas True Realm, he had actually suspected for a time that Gu Changge might very well be the Gu Clan’s distant ancestor.

Moreover, at that time, he didn’t even know whether the Gu family’s distant ancestor was male or female, and had always referred to them as “He.” It wasn’t until now, upon returning to the Gu family’s ancestral lands, that he realized the Gu family’s distant ancestor was a woman.

Even the first ancestor of the Gu family was a woman.

He didn’t know if there was a bloodline connection between them.

This raises the question: how were the descendants of the Gu family born? The distant ancestor never married in her lifetime, and the First Ancestor, who remained by her side, was the same. So how did the descendants of the Gu family come to be?

The Gu family’s existence is shrouded in far more mysteries and secrets than just these.

The First Ancestor of the Gu family furrowed his brow slightly and asked, “What is this about?”

“What I wish to speak of actually has nothing to do with our distant ancestor Xian’er; it concerns another person.” Gu Wuwang hesitated for a moment before speaking frankly, intending to openly reveal many matters related to Gu Changge.

Now, amidst the Vast Expanse, countless beings and entities long to know the origins of the Alliance Leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Although Gu Wuwang had not yet seen his face with his own eyes, he was certain that the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance was none other than Gu Changge.

When Gu Changge established the Heaven Slaying Alliance within the Prosperous Dao True Realm, Gu Wuwang found it utterly preposterous, feeling it was inconceivable and a grave act of rebellion.

Yet Gu Changge’s power left him awestruck; he even believed him to be a being comparable to their distant ancestors, and found himself unable to utter a word of objection.

Now, the might of the Heaven Slaying Alliance had grown to such an unimaginable extent that it was influencing the Vast Expanse of destiny.

Gu Changge’s power has likely recovered… to a level he could never have imagined.

Indeed, Gu Wuwang used the word “restored” rather than “grown,” for in his understanding, ever since Gu Changge left the Prosperous Dao True Realm, his power had been evolving at a pace that defied human comprehension.

That pace could no longer be described as mere "growth." Throughout history, no matter how extraordinary a figure might be, it was impossible for anyone to attain such power in just a few thousand or tens of thousands of years.

The realms that ordinary people would have to reach step by step seemed to vanish before his eyes.

Apart from calling it a "recovery," Gu Wuwang didn’t know how else to describe all of this.

“Speak,” said the First Ancestor of the Gu Clan, his eyes shining brightly like two eternal moons that never set, as he fixed his gaze on Gu Wuwang.

Gu Wuwang immediately began recounting everything he had seen, heard, and learned within the Prosperous Dao True Realm, without holding anything back.

The moment he spoke of these matters, a terrifying chill instantly engulfed Gu Wuwang’s entire body, causing his hair to stand on end and cold sweat to pour down his back.

"He’s sensed it..."

Gu Wuwang stiffly raised his head. This was still within the Gu family estate; even the most powerful figures of the Fate-Defying Palace could not penetrate this far.

Yet, despite the vast expanse of time, space, and dimensions—the endless distances separating them—merely mentioning his experiences was enough to alert him. And he hadn’t even mentioned his name yet.

The expression on the First Ancestor of the Gu Clan grew unusually grave.

She, too, had just sensed that terrifying aura that made her hair stand on end. Though it had quickly dissipated, the sensation felt as if it had been etched deep into her soul.

How many years had it been? She could no longer recall the last time she’d felt something like this.

“Given how ruthless that man is, I wonder if he’ll even spare a thought for old ties.” Gu Wuwang suddenly felt a twinge of hesitation.

Back in the Prosperous Dao True Realm, Gu Changge had effortlessly renamed it, reversed the celestial tide, and caused its fortune to soar, growing with thunderous momentum. This gave rise to many extraordinarily talented individuals, and to this day, the Prosperous Dao True Realm has produced numerous beings who have attained the Dao Realm.

For a newly born True Realm, this was undoubtedly an incredible miracle and a stroke of divine providence.

And now, he could strike fear into the Vast Expanse, effortlessly stripping away the cultivation of a figure at the end of his path and refining it into elixirs.

Although his strength was not bad, compared to Gu Changge as he was now, describing him as an ant would not be an exaggeration.

"Is this about the Heaven Slaying Alliance Master?"

The First Ancestor of the Gu Clan fell silent for a moment before suddenly asking.

In the Vast Expanse of the present, aside from that one figure, there is likely no other being to be found.

And as for the Ruler of Heaven…

She shook her head.

"Yes, the Heaven Slaying Alliance actually exists in the Prosperous Dao True Realm as well. The person who founded the Heaven Slaying Alliance, as it happens, has deep ties to our Gu family..."

After some thought, Gu Wuwang gritted his teeth and spoke up. After all, even if he didn’t reveal this, there was a high probability that the world would eventually find out anyway.

After all, Gu Xian’er had once lived in the Prosperous Dao True Realm… But if she were a distant ancestor of the Gu family, how would Gu Changge treat her?

Back in the Prosperous Dao True Realm, when he had pampered and protected her so devotedly, was it truly just out of pure kindness, with no ulterior motives or hidden intentions?

As Gu Wuwang continued his account, the expression on the First Ancestor of the Gu Clan’s face grew increasingly grave.

By the end, his pupils were constantly contracting and trembling; even with his composure, he could no longer maintain any semblance of calm.

When she closed her eyes and attempted to divine the future, she found that everything related to the Prosperous Dao True Realm seemed shrouded in mist, making it impossible to see clearly.

In fact, according to her understanding of the past, the Mountains And Seas True Realm still existed. The most powerful faction within it was known as the Immortal Palace, and the Palace Master of the Immortal Palace had deep ties to the Ji Chan clan of the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

During the last Great Calamity, the Mountains And Seas True Realm suffered severe damage, and its Daoist teachings were severed; it now remains in an era of recuperation and renewal.

The entire Vast Expanse seemed to be shrouded by an invisible force, like a nightmare, weighing heavily upon their hearts and distorting their perception of the past.

The past is the foundation of the present; it cannot be altered. The slightest reckless action could trigger unimaginable consequences…

Yet the fate and history of the True Realm of Mountains And Seas have been twisted silently, to the point where even their perceptions have been similarly distorted and altered.

"According to what you’ve said, has the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance grown so powerful as to influence the perception of the past held by the Vast Expanse of life? Or is this the deliberate work of the Heavenly Dao? Are we to understand that it was the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance who altered the established course of the world, and that only by eliminating him can all realms return to their proper path…?"

The First Ancestor of the Gu Clan wore an expression of unprecedented gravity.

Gu Wuwang dared not respond.

He wouldn’t dare to meddle or speak out on such matters.

Outside the ancestral hall, towering mountain ranges stretched into the distance. Among the sacred peaks and islands, silver waterfalls cascaded down, shrouded in ethereal mist, resembling a mythical sanctuary of the immortals.

After Gu Xian’er departed, a golden path appeared beneath her feet, stretching all the way to an island. Among those present were several people who had originally come to the Nine Heavens with her: Lan Xin, Qing Nan, and that white-clad woman who had lost her memory yet possessed unfathomable power.

However, upon her return, she saw only Lan Xin and Qing Nan; she had not yet encountered the woman in white.

Lan Xin was overjoyed to see her.

“The Gu family has been very kind to us, treating us with great care and respect.”

Gu Xian’er felt reassured. Back at the Crescent Moon Sect, Senior Sister Lan Xin had always looked after and cared for her; she had rushed over precisely to check on their safety.

Qing Nan was still dressed in a loose, flowing blue robe, her hair tied back with a wooden hairpin that had slipped slightly. Her features were regular and delicate, and her eyes were exceptionally clear.

“Have you broken through to the Dao Realm?” She seemed to have noticed Gu Xian’er’s transformation at a glance.

Lan Xin was taken aback; she hadn’t paid close attention to Gu Xian’er’s cultivation level before, but now that she looked, she realized she could no longer gauge her true strength.

How long had she been in seclusion that she’d already broken through to the Dao Realm?

Gu Xian’er made no attempt to hide it and simply nodded.

Her gaze swept over Qing Nan, and she was secretly astonished. Previously, her cultivation had been inferior to Qing Nan’s, and she couldn’t see through her. But now, having broken through to the Dao Realm herself—possessing strength comparable to that of a Xu Dao Realm cultivator who had survived three Celestial Waning Tribulations—she still felt as though a mist shrouded Qing Nan.

"Xian’er..."

At that moment, a fiery red streak shot out from deep within the mountains. A crimson bird flapped its wings and flew over, circling Gu Xian’er several times.

Gu Xian’er smiled and held out her hand, allowing it to land on the back of her hand.

The scarlet bird chattered away, speaking in human language, unable to shake its old habit of being a chatterbox.

"Xian'er, something’s off with the Gu family. Is there some secret you’re hiding?" it asked with concern.

“I’ll explain later. Where’s that white-robed elder? Why haven’t we seen her?” Gu Xian’er waved her hand and asked.

Lan Xin and Qing Nan both fell silent, but the Great Red Bird spoke bluntly, “That elder was taken away…”

Gu Xian’er looked puzzled.

Immediately, the Great Red Bird recounted the events that had unfolded in the Nine Heavens in recent days: the Dark Clans had launched a massive invasion, breaching several lower layers of the heavens. Ultimately, it was only through the intervention of several Road’s End-level figures, along with the intervention of a supreme being hidden within the Nine Heavens, that the Dark Clans were driven back.

However, during that incident, the revived Lord of Heaven had also intervened, taking an ancient mirror from her. Following that, the white-robed elder seemed to regain some of her memories and was subsequently taken away by a mysterious figure from the depths of the Nine Heavens.

"Now many are saying that the white-robed elder is actually the former Holy Maiden of the Yuan Hope Civilization. The ancient mirror taken away that day is the Yuan Hope Civilization’s most precious treasure, the Mirror of Reincarnation."

The scarlet bird flapped its wings and chirped excitedly as it explained.

"So that’s how it is..." Gu Xian’er recalled the simple, ancient mirror the white-robed elder had been holding when she first found her.

Who would have thought that the unassuming ancient mirror from back then was actually the Mirror of Reincarnation—one of the treasures of the First Civilization that so many were searching for?

"What a pity that you didn’t claim that mirror for yourself back then, Xian’er..." the Great Red Bird lamented.

Gu Xian’er tapped it on the head and said indignantly, “When have I ever been the kind of greedy person who takes advantage of others’ misfortune?”

The Great Red Bird stammered, “Aren’t you?”

In front of Lan Xin and Qing Nan, Gu Xian’er resisted the urge to beat it up, though a black line seemed to appear on her fair face.

"Times have changed. I’m not the same person I used to be," she said.

The Great Red Bird couldn’t argue with her, so it could only roll its eyes repeatedly.

Seeing this scene, Lan Xin and Qing Nan both smiled. Gu Xian’er had finally emerged from seclusion, and the two seemed genuinely happy for her. After all, they had once come to the Nine Heavens together, fleeing the pursuit of the Dark Clan—they had truly shared hardships.

This was especially true for Lan Xin and Gu Xian’er, who had both once trained at the Crescent Moon Sect.

Afterward, the three of them gathered for a small gathering. In the serene Purple Bamboo Grove, they laid out fine wine and delicious food. During their years at the Gu family estate, Lan Xin and Qing Nan had focused primarily on cultivation and had never left; their knowledge of the outside world came solely from what the Gu family members told them.

It was unrealistic for Gu Xian’er to expect to learn about the outside world through them.

However, she did not mention her connection to the Gu family, nor did she reveal that, in the eyes of the Gu clan, she was their distant ancestor.

"The Heaven Slaying Alliance is growing ever more powerful; it is now unstoppable. There is no one in the Vast Expanse who dares to defy the Heaven Slaying Alliance."

"I’ve heard that the Alliance Leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance personally appeared at the Ancient Hidden Civilization this time. With a flick of his wrist, he could summon clouds and rain; with one hand, he imprisoned and subdued the High Priest of the Ancient Hidden Civilization, and with the other, he refined the Arch-Villain Yun Zhu—who had once caused the destruction of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court—into a single elixir…"

"He has even declared that all factions in the Vast Expanse must submit to him—including the Nine Heavens."

"Avenging the Crescent Moon Sect and my Master... I fear that day is still a long way off."

Inside the Purple Bamboo Grove, white mist swirled, creating an atmosphere of serene tranquility. After a few cups of wine, a faint blush spread across Lan Xin’s cheeks. Her eyes grew misty as she began to share the news she had gathered, her hand clenching the wine cup tightly, as if filled with both fury and helplessness.

Gu Xian’er didn’t know how to respond.

Before the Crescent Moon Sect’s downfall, they had dispatched Lan Xin’s master, Chen Yi, to accompany the Righteous Unity Alliance’s delegation to the Heaven Slaying Alliance for negotiations. However, shortly thereafter, Chen Yi’s whereabouts became unknown, and all contact was lost.

“I’ve heard that the former mastermind behind the Righteous Unity Alliance was none other than the Master of Destiny. The Crescent Moon Sect was destroyed because of the Righteous Unity Alliance’s decisions, and the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance killed the Master of Destiny. Does that count as avenging the Crescent Moon Sect…?” Qing Nan held his wine cup, swirling it gently, and spoke as if in passing.
