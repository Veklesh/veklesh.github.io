"To travel across billions of miles of time and space to bring this to me—you’ve certainly gone out of your way." Gu Changge nodded slightly and said.

"Alliance Leader."

Qi Luo’s voice trembled slightly. She felt a chill run through her body, her limbs grew cold, and her scalp prickled. No matter how slow her reaction, she had finally realized the truth at that moment.

The Lord of the Celestial Host and the Alliance Leader of the Fa Alliance... Xu Ya was one and the same person.

Had she actually, in the presence of the Leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, asked him to investigate his own secrets?

Thud.

Priestess Qiluo’s legs gave way; her face turned pale as she immediately fell to her knees, her entire body trembling uncontrollably, no longer knowing what to say.

"Please... please, Alliance Master... spare my life..."

Her eyes were filled with terror. She recalled the many rumors, and the fates that had befallen the Master of Destiny, the High Priest of the Ancient Hidden Civilization, and others.

“Why should I spare your life?”

Gu Changge’s expression was a mixture of a smile and a frown.

Priestess Qiluo’s face was deathly pale, her full, crimson lips trembling.

In that moment, she had already imagined a thousand ways she might die.

Gu Changge shook his head slightly and waved his hand. A crack appeared, and an indescribable, colossal power surged forth, like a hurricane engulfing Priestess Qiluo.

"During the construction of the Heaven Slaying, we’ll need to split mountains and part the seas. You’ll be heading there to help."

No sooner had he spoken than the hurricane swept up the Priestess Qi Luo, instantly swallowing her into the fissure.

This was a time when every hand was needed.

Although the Qilu Priest harbored some ulterior motives, Gu Changge paid them no mind; after all, he was merely an insignificant minor figure.

"In this current era, progress has indeed reached its peak; the Dao Ancestor is already the ultimate limit."

"Li Zhu is a clever man; he knows that with their strength alone, they can no longer forge a path forward..."

Gu Changge gazed at the empty hall, lost in thought.

The next moment, with a casual gesture, a vast, ethereal river instantly pierced through the boundless expanse of time and space, materializing before him.

This river was immeasurably vast and boundless; though called a river, it resembled an ocean more than anything else. Its countless branches, like winding streams, spread out in a spiderweb-like pattern, connecting billions of universes and infinite spacetime.

It seemed as though some nascent will was brewing within this vast river. As Gu Changge summoned it forth, violent winds and terrifying waves surged and raged within it; yet, as he raised his palm to press down, all the commotion vanished.

"It seems the river of fate within the old cosmic order has also undergone subtle changes."

"In an ordered future, everything is already predetermined. Only by giving birth to disorder can the future become the unknown. From this perspective, the God’s idea is actually correct. If the future remains constant and unchanging, then even a True Ancestor would be unable to break through the essence of their own life and find the power to transcend themselves."

"Whether the Land Of Truth is complete is hard to say. After all, when it was first established, it was merely modeled after the birth and nurturing of the Vast Expanse. In theory, this approach is feasible."

"After all, the Vast Expanse is a perpetual, unchanging cycle—endless and eternal, impossible to break. From the very beginning, the old cosmic order and destiny had already predetermined everything..."

"To this day, no one knows the true reason for the emergence of the Three True Ancestors, but it was precisely because of their existence that this circle was broken…"

"In a sense, the emergence of the Land Of Truth represents a shift from order to chaos—a metamorphosis stemming from the conservation of energy and matter, yet marked by an explosive surge of disorder."

Gu Changge gazed at the river of destiny before him, his thoughts deep in contemplation.

Whether in the spacetime of the old cosmic order or that of the new, logically speaking, the Three True Ancestors should never have appeared.

The reason the Vast Expanse has been able to cycle endlessly, repeating itself time and again, is precisely because it possesses a constant, unbreakable law: the conservation of energy and matter.

The Vast Expanse nurtures all beings and extracts this energy and matter from them, thereby achieving a state of perfect conservation.

Broadly speaking, this can be termed the "Power of All Beings," though it can also be broken down into the power of the mind, the power of faith, the power of destiny, the seven emotions and six desires, honor and disgrace, rise and fall… To perfect the Land Of Truth, enabling it to truly contain the power of the True Ancestors—the "Gods"—the Power of All Beings was further extracted and refined into two forms: Immortality and Creation.

In terms of their names, this actually aligns with the order of the old and new cosmic trajectories.

Immortality represents constancy and unchanging, orderly cycles.

"Creation" represents chaotic change and endless renewal.

When the Heavenly Dao reaches its ultimate limit, that is Immortality.

When the Heretic Dao Realm reaches its pinnacle, it becomes Creation.

Those who argue that the substances of Creation and Immortality serve as the mediums through which they comprehend the authority of the Heavenly Dao are not entirely wrong, but they are not entirely right either. ・ The substances of Creation and Immortality can indeed enhance the cultivation of practitioners in the Dao Realm, but in essence, this is no different from a cultivator absorbing the spiritual energy of heaven and earth.

True Path-level figures who wield the authority of the Heavenly Dao have reached the stage of comprehending the very essence of the spiritual energy of heaven and earth, and even shaping it themselves— this is a transformation from nothingness into existence.

Even a World Ruler who wields the authority of creation cannot achieve this, for it shatters the very laws upon which the Vast Expanse is founded.

To comprehend the Heavenly Dao is, in fact, to comprehend the innate “Heavenly Nature” inherent in all sentient beings of the Vast Expanse. This “Heavenly Nature” is contained within the power of sentient beings, which originates from the sentient beings themselves. Heaven and Earth nurture sentient beings, and sentient beings, in turn, nourish Heaven and Earth, thus forming a self-sustaining cycle.

What the Power Lord and his kind lack in their control over the authority of the Heavenly Dao is precisely this “innate nature.” After the Heavenly Will was reduced to a puppet, this “innate nature” was sealed and erased by the Three True Ancestors.

Consequently, no matter how much the Ruler and his kind study and contemplate, they cannot comprehend the Heavenly Dao through the authority they wield.

As for the Heretic Dao, the King of Anomalies—who would have emerged in the spacetime of the new cosmic order—has already been eradicated by the Three True Ancestors. Until an existence surpassing the King of Anomalies is born, the Heretic Dao is, in fact, also blocked.

This is the current state of affairs in the Vast Expanse.

Unless Gu Changge is willing to expend countless amounts of Creation Substance and Immortality Substance to help Li Zhu break through his current realm, it is essentially impossible for them to advance any further in this lifetime.

But it was clearly out of the question for Gu Changge to engage in such a waste of energy.

The Heaven Slaying scheme can only be considered a crucial part of his plan. He has never imagined that, with the Vast Expanse’s current strength, he could even shake the Land Of Truth, let alone achieve his ultimate objective.

To put it bluntly, even if all the power within the Vast Expanse were combined and multiplied a hundred, a thousand, or ten thousand times over, it would still be incapable of having any significant impact on the Realm of Truth.

"For the Vast Expanse to evolve, the best path remains blood and chaos—to be reborn amidst catastrophe, shatter its innate nature, and forge a new path forward." "The Ancient Civilization has yet to stir. Then I shall lead you there myself."

"Since the Ancient Hidden Civilization has yet to make a move, I shall lead you all."

The next moment, Gu Changge shook his head slightly and raised his palm to release the River of Destiny—the old cosmic order.

His figure flickered, and the void before him split open. Endless fragments of spacetime scattered as he arrived in a Vast Expanse teeming with the vast and boundless—a realm where no living being could ever set foot.

Here, there was no law, no restraint, no rules, no Dao, no traces, and no cause and effect. It was like a vast, profound realm beyond the primordial, existing neither in the past nor the present, and never to be noticed by any being.

To be precise, the concept of size no longer existed here; it was simply impossible to know where its boundaries lay.

Not even the Chaos or the Void could encompass this vast expanse.

In the Vast Expanse, it is simply impossible for any living being to reach this place.

Even a being as powerful as the Ruler would feel oppressed and terrified here, as if constantly watched by some indescribable, inexplicable, and profound gaze.

Here, one feels as insignificant and lowly as a mere insect; if one were to remain long enough, one’s mind would surely shatter and collapse.

Upon arriving here, Gu Changge walked straight toward the deepest recesses of this place.

Just how deep is the deepest part of this place?

This depth was a concept beyond comprehension—a notion so profound that any attempt to grasp it would lead to error and fallacy—and only Gu Changge could truly understand it.

In the deepest recesses of this place, a terrifying will lay dormant. From afar, it resembled a glowing golden cocoon , dazzling and brilliant. Its radiance was as resplendent as the sun, radiating to every corner. At the edges, one could see threads resembling tentacles, piercing through the void and extending into the deeper realms of spacetime.

Each tentacle seemed capable of connecting to a separate super-multiverse. Endless tentacles spread out from this region, too numerous to count.

Fate, causality, time, space—any concept or substance, in the face of these tentacles, seemed as fleeting as bubbles in a stream, evaporating at the slightest touch.

Gu Changge watched this scene from afar, his brow slightly furrowed.

The Heavenly Will has no physical form nor concrete definition; it takes whatever shape people imagine it to be. It is as varied as the people who perceive it—the "Heaven" within each person’s heart is unique.

Even now, everything unfolding before his eyes was merely a drop in the bucket, a mere fraction of the Heavenly Will.

The Heavenly Will remained in slumber; what was now manifesting was merely a portion of its conceptual form materialized into a tangible size.

Yet even with just this fraction, it effortlessly sustains the operation of the Vast Expanse of universes and innumerable spacetime dimensions, governing all fate, causality, and matter.

Gu Changge raised his hand to press down upon it. The area was immediately subjected to immense pressure, and one tentacle after another began to writhe, as if sensing a threat, coiling toward his location.

In the face of these tentacles, a being of Gu’s stature would be pierced through in an instant, with no chance even to flee.

"If you do not awaken now, when will you?"

Gu Changge’s gaze was profound; he offered no resistance, merely standing his ground as the tentacles pierced toward him.

Sizzle, sizzle, sizzle… However, just as the tentacles were about to make contact with him, they reacted as if snow had met the scorching sun, instantly emitting a sizzling sound like hot water poured onto oil.

All the tentacles began to dissipate at a speed visible to the naked eye, then retreated. A wave of fear suddenly filled this boundless, infinitely vast expanse.

Soon, that blazing light—as radiant as the sun, resembling a giant cocoon, neither wholly material nor immaterial, both solid and ethereal—began to contract constantly, as if on the verge of collapse. The tentacles that had spread into the infinite spacetime around it also retracted ceaselessly, converging toward this point.

In the next instant, a terrifying and vast supreme will gradually emerged from within the light.

Then, within it, what seemed to be one shifting face after another began to twist and manifest, while the voices of all beings under heaven, countless races, and infinite Civilizations resounded with a buzzing hum.

It was as if it had encountered a provocation or an affront.

A surge of furious, ferocious emotion surged and swelled throughout this realm, which defied description.

Gu Changge smiled faintly as a fissure materialized before him; not even a force as mighty as the Heavenly Will could prevent this fissure from appearing.

The instant those furious wills transformed into countless, even sharper tentacles piercing toward him, he stepped into the fissure and vanished.

At the very same moment, amidst the Vast Expanse, countless worlds and universes seemed to be struck by some terrifying, fury-filled force.

Across the heavens and earth, the wails of ghosts and howls of wolves rang out. In many realms, massive explosions—unprecedented in ancient or modern times—erupted, and one world after another collapsed and disintegrated.

The world was like a scroll, but now that scroll was burning away bit by bit.

"This is bad—the seal is still shattering..."

He appeared amidst the Ninth Heaven, which was constantly collapsing and cracking, his expression grave beyond measure.
