"The Holy Ancestor is still alive?"

Zhu Xuan, standing among the crowd, froze for a moment upon hearing this, his fists clenching unconsciously.

To the beings who once lived on the Floating Continent, the Southern Venerable held extraordinary significance.

After all, the Southern Venerable was one of the five sages who had once resisted the Black Calamity.

After the Lord of Heaven was sealed by the six primordial treasures of the First Civilization, the Heavenly Mandate Organization subsequently collapsed.

In the long era that followed, the remnants of the Celestial Mandate sought revenge against the major clans, annihilating many ancient lineages and Civilizations.

Among them, the Yao Guang Ancestral Court established by the Central Lord was also betrayed by the Master of Destiny and ultimately destroyed.

The Eastern Sovereign, also known as the Lord of the Pan Clan, founded a family that was once home to Zhu Xuan. This family was besieged by the Dark Clans and eventually vanished into the mists of time.

The Western Saint was extremely mysterious, his whereabouts elusive, and no one could find him.

The Northern Emperor, also known as the Gou Emperor, similarly vanished without a trace, his whereabouts unknown.

Only the Southern Venerable, during those dark years, secretly supported and aided the various tribes that had once resisted the calamity of the Black Plague, rescuing them from the clutches of the Dark Tribes. Ultimately, he established the Floating Continent at the center of the Chaotic Sea of Time and Space, shielding it from the incursions of the Dark Tribes.

The indigenous people of the Floating Continent all reverently address the Southern Venerable as the Holy Ancestor.

To rescue his companions who had been corrupted by dark matter, the Southern Venerable risked everything and set out on a journey to the Nightmare Realm, only to be imprisoned and trapped within it.

Years later, his descendant, Qing Xianzi, returned to the Floating Continent bearing his token, bringing with her the news of the demise of Southern Venerable.

As for who Nan Qing’s father was, no one knew; it has always remained a mystery.

"It seems the Holy Ancestor is still alive, though he has been trapped in the Nightmare Realm all this time. Qing Xianzi has also taken the High Priest of the Dark Clan as her master..."

At this thought, the bitter self-mockery on Zhu Xuan’s lips deepened. His clenched fists relaxed and tightened repeatedly, and after several cycles, he finally calmed his mind.

Had it not been for the final reckoning on the side of the Land Of Truth, he might now be rushing about seeking vengeance for his clan.

Everyone before him was her enemy—including the once-revered and admired Qing Xianzi, and the Alliance Master of the Heaven Slaying Alliance...

"If you wish to see her, then go and see her."

A faint,幽 blue mist once again swirled around High Priestess Wuyue, shrouding her imposing figure.

Only a pair of eyes, as clear and crisp as the morning light, remained visible, devoid of any emotional ripples.

She nodded slightly, her tone flat. Though she appeared to be Nan Qing’s master, there was little warmth in her demeanor.

Nan Qing didn’t mind; she had long since grown accustomed to her interactions with her mentor, Wuyue.

“Who is Nan Qing’s father?”

At that moment, Gu Xian’er suddenly spoke up. She actually harbored some doubts and had a hunch, but she had always refused to consider that possibility.

Ever since she first laid eyes on Nan Qing, she had felt that Nan Qing’s features were strangely familiar, as if she had seen her before.

Especially after Nan Qing had restored her true appearance, that familiar sense of déjà vu had grown even stronger.

Upon hearing this, High Priest Wu Yue did not answer; he seemed unwilling to acknowledge her, appearing aloof and indifferent.

"Only Nan Qing turned around, smiled at Gu Xian'er, and said, 'Why don’t you take a guess?'"

Gu Xian’er’s question also sparked a faint curiosity among everyone present.

After all, according to legend, the Southern Venerable was a remarkable woman with countless admirers and suitors.

Even the rulers of ancient civilizations and the ancient True Realms—the Sons of the Eon—had once courted her; otherwise, she could not have been counted among the Five Sages.

Who, exactly, had quietly impregnated the Southern Venerable and caused her to give birth to an heir?

"Don’t ask any more questions about this."

The High Priestess Wuyue’s eyes grew slightly dark, and her tone turned inexplicably icy; clearly, she had recalled some memories she would rather forget.

To her, whether it was the Southern Venerable or the Nan Qing standing before her now, they were both stains—stains she longed to erase with her own hands.

Because they had defiled the Master’s bloodline.

Especially the Southern Venerable.

To this day, Wuyue still could not fathom why, during the "Rebellion of the Heavens" back then, Southern Venerable had entered the Coffin of the End of the World and, because of a single drop of essence blood, had conceived Nan Qing.

After all, even someone as powerful as the Ancestor Xu could not bear the weight of such overwhelming and terrifying power; he could only exhaust every means to extract and strip it away bit by bit, then slowly refine himself to become a vessel.

At that time, the Southern Venerable—who hadn’t even reached the End of the Path—how was he able to avoid being obliterated by that single drop of essence blood?

This was what Wuyue could not fathom, and it was also the reason she ultimately chose to spare Nan Qing and Southern Venerable.

However, because Southern Venerable had once trespassed into the Nightmare Demon Realm and caused the "Disaster of the Five Lords of Heaven," resulting in the theft of the flesh and blood of the Five Lords of Heaven, she had placed Southern Venerable under house arrest, allowing the mother and son to meet only occasionally.

Seeing that High Priestess Wuyue was displeased, Gu Xian’er did not ask any further questions; after all, she had come to the Nightmare Demon Realm solely for Li Hanying.

Now that the malevolent essence of the Lord of Heaven had emerged, there was no point for her to remain here any longer.

However, they were unable to intervene in the great battle raging on the front lines at the moment.

For a moment, Gu Xian’er felt a bit lost.

In the Nine Heavens, she had inherited the Dao Fruit of the Gu family’s distant ancestor, but she did not believe herself to be the reincarnation of that ancestor. She had always rejected the notion that she was the one destined to face the calamity.

If she truly were the one destined to face the calamity, why was she unable to do anything, and why could she not help Gu Changge in any way?

What good is the cultivation of the Dao Realm in such a time of great upheaval?

With a quiet sigh, she gathered her thoughts.

"Who is it?"

Suddenly, the clear, icy command of High Priest Wu Yue rang out.

She whipped her head around and stared at the Hall of the Celestial Mandate in the distance, which had long since collapsed and crumbled.

With the seal on the Coffin of the Underworld broken, the area lay in utter ruin. The Coffin itself had vanished, now shrouded in a hazy black mist. Terrifying residual energy still permeated the air, deterring anyone from approaching.

The Holy Maiden Xi Yuan, Nan Qing, and the others were also startled by High Priestess Wuyue’s voice and turned their gaze in that direction.

At this moment, who else could have arrived in the Nightmare Demon Realm without a sound and appeared here?

Gu Xian’er, however, paused slightly, sensing a familiar aura.

"Cough, cough, cough…"

From within the spreading, hazy black mist came the sound of coughing, and faintly, a hunched, diminutive figure slowly emerged.

She was alert and vigorous, her silver hair as sharp as needles. Though her face was lined with wrinkles, her complexion was not pale but rather rosy and healthy. Dressed in a patterned linen robe, her eyes were clear and bright—she looked just like the old lady next door.

"Who are you?"

High Priestess Wuyue stared coldly at the old woman approaching her, her tone icy.

Gu Xian’er’s eyes widened in disbelief.

Lan Xin, standing behind her, was equally stunned and shaken, blurting out, “Muen?”

Lan Xin’s master, Chen Yi, had once been an elder of the Crescent Moon Sect, and she too was utterly stunned.

Wasn’t the old woman before them none other than the mad old crone whom the Crescent Moon Sect had once regarded as an ill omen—the one who spent her days acting erratically and rambling incoherently?

Everyone in the Crescent Moon Sect avoided her like the plague.

However, she had learned that on the day the Dark Clans attacked, it was the Mad Old Woman who stepped in, turning the tide and halting the advancing Dark Army, allowing Gu Xian’er and the others to evacuate safely.

Later, while leaving the Crescent Moon Sect to head for the Nine Heavens, this woman repeatedly fell into a deep coma.

Of course, it was Mad Granny who had originally brought Gu Xian’er to Crescent Moon Sect in the first place; the two shared an inextricable bond.

"It was actually her..."

Ji Yu, who had been standing among the crowd, was equally shaken, his mind flooded with memories of the past.

He was not only a disciple of the Eternal Dao Lord but also a bloodline descendant of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court—a legitimate heir of the Central Lord lineage. In his quest to find the legacy of the Ju Tian Sect, he had taken the pseudonym Song Yu and once joined the Crescent Moon Sect.

Within the Crescent Moon Sect lay the ruins of the former Jutian Sect.

In order to restore the Yao Guang Ancestral Court and overthrow the rule of the Usurper, Emperor Wei Yang, he had been searching within the Crescent Moon Sect for the legacy of the Ju Tian Sect.

When he first met Gu Xian’er at the Crescent Moon Sect, he even tried to use this against her as leverage, but was dissuaded by his guardian, Uncle Lan, who warned him that Gu Xian’er’s background was likely extraordinary. Only then did he desist, not daring to offend her.

Subsequent events indeed proved that Gu Xian'er’s background was extraordinary; she actually had a close relationship with the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

While fleeing the Crescent Moon Sect, Gu Xian’er was taken in by that mad old woman, who kept her for a time—presumably to pass on the Jutian Sect’s legacy to her.

Because of this, Ji Yu harbored a lingering sense of resentment for some time, until he gradually came to terms with it.

Now, as they watched the Mad Old Woman appear in the Nightmare Demon Realm, the crowd’s expressions varied—some were shaken, others surprised and bewildered, and still others simply puzzled.

“Do you know her?”

The High Priestess of Wuyue suppressed her hostility, though her gaze remained unfriendly. She glanced at Gu Xian’er and asked coldly.

"The Old Woman saved my life, so of course I recognize her."

Gu Xian’er nodded and stepped forward.

At the same time, she was deeply puzzled.

Why was the Mad Old Woman here?

After they parted ways back then, she had assumed they would never meet again. As for the Nine Celestial Restraints the Mad Old Woman had taught her, even now she could only perform the first few.

This made Gu Xian’er feel she had failed to live up to the Mad Old Woman’s trust.

However, even if she could now perform the Ninth Restriction, she would at most be able to handle a figure at the End of the Path level; as for True Path-level figures above that, she dared not even think about it.

Not to mention that beings surpassing the True Path level have now emerged into the world.

"It seems I didn’t arrive in time, but it’s not too late either."

The Mad Old Woman’s face now bore none of her previous mad, foolish expression; her mind was clear and lucid. A strange domain-field fluctuation surrounded her, as if she were stepping through the gaps of time, causing even the laws of the Dao to step aside.

"I did not mean to intrude; I hope the High Priest will not hold it against me."

She smiled gently and nodded at High Priest Wuyue.

"Someone from the Palace of Defiance?" High Priest Wu Yue fixed his gaze on the fluctuations of energy emanating from the Mad Old Woman.

"We share some history, though I wouldn’t exactly call it that. Rest assured, I mean no harm. I’ve come here only to take Xian’er away." The Mad Old Woman spoke succinctly, then smiled at Gu Xian’er.

Gu Xian’er grew even more puzzled. Had she come specifically to find her, at a time like this?

However, she felt no sense of wariness toward the Mad Old Woman. After all, since arriving in the Vast Expanse, she had faced life-and-death crises, and it was the Mad Old Woman who had come to her rescue and brought her to Crescent Moon Sect.

Later, she had assisted her on numerous occasions and even taught her the Nine Heavenly Restraints—a forbidden technique so taboo it could provoke the wrath of the Heavenly Dao.

"If you want to take me away, then take me away."

“Her whereabouts have nothing to do with the Nightmare Demon Realm.”

Grand Priestess Wuyue said indifferently.

Hearing this, Nan Qing cast a slightly apologetic glance at Gu Xian’er, explaining that her master, the High Priestess Wuyue, was simply that way and asking her not to take offense.

Gu Xian’er didn’t mind, however. She didn’t want to stay here any longer anyway. Although she knew that High Priest Wu Yue and Gu Changge’s predecessor—the Lord of Heaven—shared a special bond, she simply couldn’t bring herself to feel close to him.

Especially given High Priest Wu Yue’s current demeanor… it seemed as though he harbored hostility toward her.

She pursed her lips slightly, then turned to the Mad Old Woman and said, “Grandma, I’ll go with you.”

When the Gu family in the Nine Heavens received the Dao Fruit of their distant ancestor, she had actually inherited quite a few memories, gaining some understanding of the secrets and truths of the world.

Therefore, she was well aware of the origins of the Jutian Sect.

Besides, there were quite a few matters she wanted to verify with Crazy Granny.
