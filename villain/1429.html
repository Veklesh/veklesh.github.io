Beside the bed, a figure clad in white stood there quietly, having appeared without a sound.

Empress Xiyao’s beautiful eyes widened slightly, wondering if she had seen wrong or was experiencing a hallucination.

But at that moment, even Song Youwei seemed to sense something. A flood of memories surged through her mind—memories that did not belong to the present, nor to this era.

She was startled.

"Alliance Leader..." she said, bowing to Gu Changge, then stepped away from Empress Xiyao and withdrew.

“I never expected it to be true, but… this can’t be the real you, can it?”

Empress Xiyao gazed at Gu Changge’s handsome features, mere inches away, and murmured the question. True to her reputation as the Empress of the Demon Realm, though she sensed an aura about Gu Changge that did not belong to this era, she quickly regained her composure.

Nevertheless, she turned her head away, seemingly unwilling to let Gu Changge see her current appearance.

"I have come from five hundred years in the future to see you."

Gu Changge said, walking over to her bedside and slowly sitting down in the spot where Song Youwei had been sitting moments ago.

Although mentioning matters involving future timelines could entangle vast karmic consequences and cause significant upheaval,

But for the Gu Changge of the present, such effects were negligible, and he didn’t care about them at all.

Not to mention, this was merely within the Daochang True Realm, not in the Vast Expanse.

“So, in other words, it wasn’t until five hundred years after my death that you even knew I was gone, right…?” Empress Xiyao paused for a moment, then said softly.

Gu Changge remained silent; he could not lie, nor did he wish to.

Though such words sounded cruel, they were indeed the truth.

If it weren’t for Song Youwei coming to find him, he likely would never have known; nor would he have taken the initiative to investigate or pry into matters concerning Empress Xiyao.

Because in his heart, there had never been a single corner reserved for her.

“The truth is truly cruel and absurd. It wasn’t until five hundred years after my death that you remembered me. It was because you returned to the Daochang True Realm that Youwei sought you out and told you about me, wasn’t it?”

Empress Xiyao’s emotions suddenly surged. She turned her head away, her eyes red and moist, yet her voice remained calm.

As intelligent and perceptive as she was, she had deduced the future simply from these few words.

At that moment, although Gu Changge was sitting right before her, mere inches away, she felt him to be distant and unfamiliar, as if he were in another world she could not reach.

His heart was as cold and ruthless as it had been when he first left the Demon Realm.

All these years, he had not changed, and neither had she—she had remained just as ridiculous and foolish as ever.

Two cold tears slid down Empress Xiyao’s cheeks. She kept her beautiful eyes open, weeping silently.

Countless fantasies, countless hopes, and countless times spent tiptoeing outside the palace in anticipation—all shattered now like a fleeting illusion, leaving behind only desolation and sorrow.

Gu Changge gazed at her head of white hair and her jade-like hands, now marked by wrinkles and the signs of age, and remained silent.

"If that was the case, why did you make those promises to me back then? Why did you make me believe I could rely on you, make me feel that I would finally have a safe haven and a warm embrace..."

"Do you know how envious I was on the day of your wedding? But I also knew that such a scene would never happen to me. You are the Young Master Changge of the Gu family, the ruler of the Divine Realm, the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, and the Demon Lord who once struck fear into the heavens..."

"And I am merely an insignificant empress of the tiny Demon Realm—an old woman who is over six thousand years older than you, yet remains oblivious to it."

"I’m not blaming you for anything; I just feel so foolish for clinging to an empty hope all these years."

Empress Xiyao’s voice grew fainter and fainter, yet every word she spoke was exceptionally clear and calm.

Gu Changge stared at her in a daze, momentarily at a loss for words.

He had spoken in jest back then, yet she had actually taken it seriously and spent all those years yearning for it.

Still, looking back from that time, it made sense. Though she was already an empress, how different was her heart really from that of a carefree young girl?

"I’m sorry..."

Gu Changge said softly. If he could go back to that moment, he still wouldn’t change his decision or his thoughts, nor would he make any different choice.

He had never regretted his actions or decisions. With this “I’m sorry,” he simply wanted to offer a sincere apology for the deception and exploitation he had inflicted upon her.

It might sound a bit ridiculous, but to him, at this very moment, he truly felt a sense of guilt and remorse.

This feeling was so similar to what he had experienced when he had renounced his immortality in Qingshan Village.

Empress Xiyao seemed equally surprised that Gu Changge would ever utter such words.

"You... you really are heartless," she sighed softly, saying nothing more.

In truth, no matter how much one says, it serves no purpose.

Gu Changge said nothing more either; he simply took her hand. He could see everything about Empress Xiyao’s current physical condition at a glance.

However, it ultimately depended on her own will.

When facing the Trial of the Heart Demon, she knew full well that it was a dream and an illusion, that reality and illusion were one and the same, and that the outcome would be as it was—yet she chose to press on without hesitation.

If she wanted to live, Gu Changge would ensure she survived.

But if she was determined to die, Gu Changge would respect her will.

“Don’t worry about me. I’ve lived long enough in this life. In the next, I don’t want to be so exhausted. I don’t want to be anyone’s pawn anymore…” Empress Xiyao seemed to know what Gu Changge was thinking. She turned her head toward him. Though her eyes were still red-rimmed, her expression was calm.

Countless scenes from this life flashed through her mind like a slideshow: being deceived by her mother, questioned by her ministers, the chaos caused by the great demons from all directions, countless assassination attempts, the betrayal of a trusted maid… and even at the very end of her life, she could not even get the man she loved most to pause and look back at her.

If there were a next life, she did not wish to relive any of these experiences…

The bloom of youth had faded from her face, yet before Gu Changge’s eyes now appeared the image of that peerlessly radiant figure in phoenix-patterned robes he had first seen at the True Immortal Academy.

“Could you hold me for a moment? If you did, I believe I would have no regrets in this life.” She asked softly, her eyes fixed on Gu Changge, her voice carrying a humble plea.

Gu Changge looked at her and nodded. “All right.”

Empress Xiyao suddenly smiled, as radiant as ever. With great effort, she propped herself up and leaned toward him.

Gu Changge reached out, embraced her, and let her rest her head against his shoulder.

"This sense of peace... it feels so good..."

Empress Xiyao gently closed her eyes and murmured softly, her voice growing fainter and fainter.

Gu Changge held her quietly like this, feeling her vitality and spirit gradually withdraw from her body, feeling the warmth of her body slowly dissipate and fade away, until finally, all warmth was lost.

Her head finally fell heavily, and the arms wrapped around his neck went limp.

In her final moments, Empress Xiyao breathed her last, her face still bearing a smile—serene and without a single regret.

In the Demon Realm, after reigning for 13,500 years, Empress Xiyao passed away.

The demon race mourned together; the people grieved.

……

Back in the present day, Gu Changge held a pristine white porcelain basin in his arms. The water within was crystal clear, and a beautiful fish swam restlessly, now darting away, now lifting a pair of eyes that seemed both spirited and innocent to gaze at him.

"I’m sorry for what I did to you in my past life..."

"Then let’s start over in this life. I hope that when your memories return, you won’t blame me for being selfish."

He looked at her and said softly.
