Within the Celestial City, cultivators and beings of all races came and went, creating an atmosphere of immense bustle.

Lining the broad streets stood a variety of shops, towers, and palaces, shrouded in swirling clouds and mist. In the distance, one could see radiant divine light emanating from numerous floating divine mountains and islands.

At first glance, it did not differ much from the ancient cities found in the outside world.

Ji Yu led the members of the Crescent Moon Sect to find the inn where they would stay for the night, and then departed with Uncle Lan to pay a visit to the ruler of the First Heaven.

Gu Xian’er and the others gazed at the ancient, rustic banners fluttering outside the inn and couldn’t help but marvel; even the inn itself seemed to exude an ancient, time-worn aura of blood and steel.

The innkeeper was a portly, middle-aged man with a potbelly, whose cultivation level was at the Half-Step Immortal Emperor realm.

If such a figure were to appear in the outside world, it would certainly cause a sensation, but in the Nine Heavens of today, it seemed quite ordinary.

"Truly, this is the Nine Heavens, the land longed for by countless cultivators and sentient beings. It has certainly been worth the journey..."

The elders and disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect were all deeply moved.

Hearing their tone, the innkeeper was also taken aback. It had been a long time since anyone from the outside world had appeared in the Nine Heavens, and he was quite curious about their origins. He then took the initiative to strike up a conversation with them, proving to be an exceptionally good conversationalist.

Several elders of the Crescent Moon Sect also wished to quickly learn more about the Nine Heavens, so they began chatting with the innkeeper as well.

It was then that they learned that, apart from those with special status, once residents of the Nine Heavens Realm left, they could never return.

Even if one memorized the route to the Nine Heavens, it was nearly impossible to return.

This was because, for some inexplicable reason, a strange, almost magical interference would arise, causing their memories to become jumbled. As a result, they would wander aimlessly and eventually find themselves back where they started.

This explains why, despite the Nine Heavens having so many residents, no one from the outside world had set foot here in so many years.

Furthermore, even if someone of special status were to return to the Nine Heavens, bringing an outsider would require the consent of the gravekeeper at the entrance to the Nine Heavens…

All these factors have caused the veil of mystery surrounding the Nine Heavens to grow ever thicker, and the outside world’s understanding of it to dwindle.

However, this does not mean that the Nine Heavens are unaware of events in the outside world.

In recent times, the major events unfolding in the Vast Expanse have already spread throughout the various realms of the Nine Heavens, causing quite a stir.

Whether it be the Heaven Slaying Alliance, the Righteous Unity Alliance, or the Dark Clans, they have all become "terms" known to nearly everyone within the Nine Heavens.

Even the Immortal Dao Alliance, set to be established in the near future, has already spread throughout the Nine Heavens.

However, unlike the anxious and restless mood prevailing among the various races and realms of the outside world, the inhabitants of the Nine Heavens adopted an attitude of detached curiosity, viewing the events as a spectacle that did not concern them, convinced that the flames of war could never reach the Nine Heavens.

The Nine Heavens has always stood apart from the world, untouched by the karma of the outside realm.

Even if the Dark Cataclysm were to descend, it could remain unscathed.

In some of the large gambling houses within the Nine Heavens, one could even see various ethnic factions and powers placing bets, setting up gambling games to wager on which external faction would be the first to fall and be swallowed up.

Many cultivators and sentient beings, during casual conversations over meals, would also discuss events in the outside world and analyze the shifting political landscape, exuding an air of grandiloquence as they offered their insights on the Vast Expanse of the realm.

When the members of the Crescent Moon Sect heard the innkeeper speak of these matters, they were all left speechless and deeply shaken, remaining silent for a long time afterward.

This feeling was complex and hard to fathom, leaving them with a mix of bitterness and bewilderment.

“The courtesan knows not the sorrow of a fallen nation; across the river, she still sings ‘The Flower of the Rear Courtyard’”—perhaps this is exactly what that saying refers to.

Clearly, the situation in the Vast Expanse had become so urgent and turbulent that even those clans and lineages that had lived in seclusion for countless ages were now stepping forward to take sides, fearing that a single misstep might bring about their own destruction and extinction.

That dark realm, which once sealed away the source of the Black Calamity, is now approaching the Vast Expanse; before long, it will border the known worlds…

The reappearance of the Lord of Heaven in the world signifies that boundless darkness will once again shroud the earth.

How could the sentient beings within the Nine Heavens remain so carefree and content? Did they truly believe that calamity would never befall them?

For a moment, the members of the Crescent Moon Sect felt an overwhelming sense of foreboding.

Although, as Ji Yu had described, the Nine Heavens were indeed transcendent—spared from the strife of the outside world and standing apart from all realms—

Yet now, in their eyes, the inhabitants here were nothing more than a group of fools who were born in adversity but perished in ease.

Gu Xian’er remained silent, but the sense of urgency in her heart grew even stronger.

"We can’t rely on this..."

Lan Xin couldn’t help but whisper, her beautiful eyes filled with worry.

"Without having witnessed the power of the Dark Beings firsthand, it is indeed difficult to grasp that sense of despair based solely on rumors and hearsay," sighed the elder of the Crescent Moon Sect.

Before the Dark Tribes reappeared in the Vast Expanse, they had not taken those recorded rumors seriously.

They had even believed that the accounts of the former Black Calamity were somewhat exaggerated, and that the era of the Black Calamity had simply been embellished by later generations.

However, when they witnessed with their own eyes that day the endless tide of darkness surging over the dam’s defenses, everyone deeply understood what it meant to feel oppressed and what true despair was.

And yet, this cruelty and despair fell far short of even one percent of what the Dark Calamity era had truly been.

"Miss Xian'er, be wary of that Ji Yu. On the way here, he kept his connection to the Nine Heavens hidden, refusing to reveal a single detail. Now that we’ve arrived, he’s laid his cards on the table and isn’t even bothering to pretend anymore."

"You must have something he desires. Though the Nine Heavens Realm is transcendent, how different is it really from the True Realms of the outside world? In fact, the inhabitants here are even more arrogant and ignorant."

The private room in this inn resembled a celestial abode, filled with ethereal mist. It was fully furnished with jade beds, tables and chairs, incense burners, and meditation cushions.

The room accommodated four people, so Gu Xian’er, Qing Nan, Lan Xin, and the woman in white shared it together.

Once they returned to the private room and confirmed no one was eavesdropping or spying, Qing Nan spoke softly.

She wore a loose, blue robe, her hair tied back with a wooden hairpin so it didn’t fall loose. Her features were regular and delicate, and her eyes were particularly clear and beautiful, truly striking, giving off a gentle and unpretentious aura.

Along the way, perhaps because she had been saved by Gu Xian’er, she had grown closer to Gu Xian’er and the others, while having little interaction with the members of the Crescent Moon Sect.

Upon hearing this, Gu Xian’er smiled in response. “Sister Qingnan, please rest assured. I understand. Ji Yu has always coveted the legacy that Elder Madam Feng left me and harbors ill intentions. We had a conflict back when we were at the Crescent Moon Sect, and I’ve been on my guard ever since.”

She didn’t know why, but she felt a certain fondness for Qingnan, the woman they had rescued along the way, so she didn’t hold back much when it came to such private matters.

Gu Xian’er also wondered if it was because Qingnan’s features gave her a sense of familiarity; at first glance, she even bore a slight resemblance to someone.

So sometimes, when she stared at Qing Nan, she would inexplicably drift into a reverie.

This always made Qing Nan feel a bit puzzled and confused, prompting her to ask, “Is there something on my face?”

Such a simple, innocent question always caught Gu Xian’er off guard, causing a major awkward moment. Later, when she realized the reason, her cheeks would flush crimson, even leading Lan Xin, who was nearby, to wonder if she had a certain crush.

So, faced with Qing Nan—who seemed so simple-minded, almost a bit slow on the uptake—Gu Xian’er gradually let go of her initial wariness and caution, slowly growing closer to him.

"In the Nine Heavens, where you're in a strange land, it's always wise to keep your wits about you."

“But rest assured, Miss Xian’er. You saved my life; I owe you a debt of gratitude. If Ji Yu ever tries to harm you, I’ll find a way to protect you.” Qing Nan’s tone was earnest, and her eyes, as lustrous as jade, radiated sincerity.

Gu Xian’er felt a touch of emotion welling up inside her. She shook her head and smiled. “Sister Qingnan, there’s no need to worry. No matter how bold Ji Yu may be, he likely wouldn’t dare do anything to me right now. Now that we’ve arrived in the Nine Heavens, you should focus on cultivating diligently and strive to avenge your master as soon as possible.”

Upon hearing this, Qingnan nodded.

That said, Gu Xian’er still harbored many worries. She naturally feared none of Ji Yu’s overt schemes.

But she was worried that Ji Yu might resort to underhanded tactics—such as targeting her senior sister, Lan Xin.

"Elder Chen Yi went to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, and his fate remains unknown to this day. This has caused Senior Sister Lan Xin to harbor deep hatred and resentment toward the Heaven Slaying Alliance. And since I told Ji Yu at the time that I came from the Heaven Slaying Alliance, this situation might very well allow Ji Yu to exploit a loophole..." she sighed softly to herself.

Senior Sister Lan Xin had always taken great care of her, treating her like a real older sister.

But at this moment, she couldn’t assure her of Elder Chen Yi’s safety or even the current state of the Heaven Slaying Alliance in the Vast Expanse—Gu Xian’er knew nothing of it.

"Senior, have you regained quite a bit of your memory recently?"

Gu Xian’er was at a loss. Lately, even the Great Red Bird had become listless, hiding away in the Sumeru Ring, leaving her with no one to confide in and forcing her to keep her innermost thoughts bottled up.

However, the only thing that could be called good news at the moment was that this mysterious woman in white, whom she had saved by accident, was in much better condition than before.

Her cultivation and strength were also recovering at a rapid pace; it was likely that in a short while, she would return to her former peak condition.

However, her memories seem fragmented and incomplete. Her spirit must have been severely injured by someone, causing her to forget many things.

Gu Xian’er often saw her staring blankly into space, as if trying hard to recall something, but each time the pain would cause her memory to snap, leaving her unable to remember anything.

"She has recovered somewhat, but not much."

The woman in white veiled her face with a light gauze; her dark brows were as graceful as distant mountains, naturally lush and green. Her figure was slender and tall, graceful and alluring. Upon hearing this, she merely shook her head, her tone tinged with cool detachment.

"Has Senior managed to recall her origins?" Gu Xian’er asked, her voice tinged with hope.

The woman in white shook her head again. “No, but I have recalled my enemy.”

Hearing this, Gu Xian’er’s curiosity deepened. She speculated, “Your enemy, Senior, must surely be someone utterly vicious and ruthless.”

She had seen the white-clad woman’s true face with her own eyes—a visage as ethereal as a dream, as if it shouldn’t exist in this world. Even the slightest furrow of her brow seemed enough to break one’s heart. Could anyone in this world who could wound her so deeply truly be described as merely “vicious and ruthless”?

If it were her, she doubt she’d have the heart to inflict such pain on this white-robed senior.

"It’s not just ruthless—that person is a complete and utter villain."

The white-clad woman’s usually icy tone finally seemed to stir with emotion; Gu Xian’er could even detect a hint of gritted teeth in her voice.

Her beautiful eyes widened in utter astonishment. What on earth had happened to make this senior—who was so ethereal, detached, and otherworldly—so enraged that she was grinding her teeth?

Lan Xin, standing nearby, couldn’t help but look puzzled.

Even Qing Nan, who had been sitting cross-legged on the jade bed with his eyes closed, couldn’t help but open his eyes and look over.

The woman in white seemed to realize her tone had been a bit off; she quickly regained her usual calm and composed demeanor, saying, “There are many things I don’t remember clearly, but the one thing I remember very clearly is that he subjected me to a great deal of humiliation and torment…”

These words set Gu Xian’er and Lan Xin’s imaginations racing once more. They couldn’t shake the feeling that something was off, yet they couldn’t quite put their finger on what it was.

The next morning, a commotion erupted outside the inn, rousing Gu Xian’er and the others from their meditation.

Many disciples and elders of the Crescent Moon Sect went out to investigate, filled with confusion.

The usually bustling main street had been cleared of people, and at the inn’s entrance, the innkeeper stood with a look of utter astonishment, at a loss for what to do.

Ji Yu looked grim as he stood with his guardian, Uncle Lan, beside a man in a purple robe who had a somewhat effeminate appearance and wore a seven-star crown.

In front of them stood several young men and women, their auras radiant and their attire and demeanor extraordinary. They followed an elderly man clad in a grayish-white robe, his hair white but his spirit vigorous, as if they were all waiting for something.

“Could that be the Palace Master of the Purple Microcosm Palace in the First Heaven, the Great Sage Ziwei?”

"No doubt about it—it’s definitely the Great Sage Ziwei. Hundreds of thousands of years ago, he once preached in a corner of the Celestial Sea. I had the privilege of hearing him then. Though we were far apart, I could see him clearly. That aura is unmistakably his..."

"How could the Great Sage Ziwei appear in this place? And who are these people? Have they come from other Heavens?"

In the distance, countless cultivators and sentient beings witnessed this scene, all of them shaken, astonished, and unable to believe their eyes.

The Grand Sage Ziwei, the ruler of the First Heaven—a mighty being who had survived five Celestial Waning Tribulations and was as elusive as a dragon seen only in glimpses—had actually appeared outside the door of an inn.

How could this not be shocking and awe-inspiring?

Could some peerless master be in seclusion within this humble inn?

Moreover, judging by the situation before them, the Great Sage Ziwei seemed to be overlooked by others. Despite being the ruler of the First Heavenly Realm, he now appeared as if he were an outsider.

"What’s going on?"

Several elders of the Crescent Moon Sect emerged from the inn and, upon witnessing this scene, were equally astonished and shaken.

The elder with the deepest cultivation among them was particularly tense; the moment the smiling old man glanced his way, he felt as though all his secrets had been laid bare.

"Excuse me, is there anyone here with the surname Gu?" The old man, clad in a grayish-white robe, asked with a cheerful smile, his expression amiable and his manner gentle.
