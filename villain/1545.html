The Nightmare Realm, having endured a brutal battle, was now a scene of utter devastation, a landscape of ruin and desolation.

The land was crisscrossed with ravines and fissures, and long-dormant dark volcanoes erupted one after another.

Ash like willow catkins drifted through the sky, and thick dark matter permeated the air, drifting among the mountains in ribbon-like strands, a far cry from its former splendor.

After Gu Xian’er followed the Mad Old Woman out of the Nightmare Demon Realm, the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan, Lan Xin, and the others also departed one after another, choosing not to linger any longer and intending to return first to the Nine Heavens’ current base.

A major battle was also raging in the central region of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, drawn into the conflict by the clash between Yue Mingkong and the six consorts of the Supreme Yin Heavenly Lords.

They were currently unable to return there and could only wait for the situation to become clearer.

At the farthest reaches of the vast, dark heavens, the ancient Altar of Reckoning—reflected from the Land Of Truth—still hung there, like a towering demonic mountain suspended over the hearts of countless living beings, casting a heavy, oppressive shadow.

This final reckoning would affect the Vast Expanse; in truth, nowhere would remain peaceful or tranquil.

"Could it be that something has truly gone wrong with our Lord?"

“Why would a deity harbor such malevolent thoughts…”

Before the shattered Hall of Heaven, the High Priestess Wuyue stood alone, suddenly letting out a faint, lonely sigh. Her injuries were actually quite severe; she had simply been forcing herself to hold on.

"High Priestess..."

"The Lord...?"

Jing Tianyuan approached her with concern.

"Deal with the traitors first. The remnants of the Black Lotus Sect have no place in this world. They are vile thieves of dark power who should have been consumed by the flames of darkness long ago." High Priestess Wuyue waved her hand, her gaze deep and her tone flat.

"Yes."

Jing Tianyuan bowed his head, then transformed into a beam of dark divine light and departed.

The Four High Priests and the other priests all left the Nightmare Demon Realm to lead the Dark Tribes in fierce combat against the enemy on the front lines of the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

Only Wuyue remained behind to guard the Nightmare Demon Realm.

And it was precisely this that gave the Primordial Void and the Formless an opportunity to strike.

This was the sanctuary she had cleared for Gu Changge; no living creature was permitted to set foot here and defile it.

At the same time, on the other side, deep within the Nightmare Demon Realm, Nan Zhe Jing stood amidst the vast void.

Holding a dark token in her hand, she took a deep breath and then made a gentle swipe.

With a humming sound, a fissure spread out before her eyes.

She stepped inside.

Inside, she found herself surrounded by clear streams and lush mountains, the sweet scent of flowers and the chirping of birds—a scene of natural vitality and vibrancy, a stark contrast to the outside world.

In this pleasant, natural mountain range, there was a serene bamboo grove where a woman in white sat quietly in the woods.

An ancient zither lay before her. As her slender hands gently glided across the strings, waves of melodious sounds filled the air, quickly drawing a large gathering of rare birds and exotic beasts.

Surrounded by lakes, it resembled an emerald set in the landscape.

Nan Qing stared at the woman in a daze, her eyes slowly welling up with tears.

At that moment, the woman in white seemed to sense something and suddenly looked up.

Upon recognizing Nan Qing’s face, her body stiffened abruptly, as if in disbelief. Her eyes widened slowly, and she stood frozen in place.

"Qing'er, is that you?"

A moment later, she came to her senses and asked in a gentle tone.

Her gaze was gentle, her features soft and flawless, giving off an aura of infinite beauty. Upon closer inspection, one would notice that her face bore a striking resemblance to Nan Qing’s—in fact, they were seven parts alike.

It was none other than the Southern Venerable, once one of the Five Sages.

"Mother, it is I."

Nan Qing pursed her lips, transformed into a divine rainbow, and descended to the center of the pavilion.

This was a self-contained, isolated realm; only those holding the token of the High Priest of Wuyue could enter.

It could be said that this place was cut off from the world, standing apart from the hustle and bustle of the mortal realm, undisturbed by any living being.

"Qing'er, it’s been a while. You’ve grown even more beautiful. Did you run into any trouble out there…?"

The Southern Venerable’s gaze was gentle as she reached out to touch Qing’er’s face and lips.

The last time mother and daughter had seen each other was tens of thousands of years ago—though for cultivators, that was merely the duration of a single seclusion.

However, time flows differently within the Nightmare Demon Realm; in this isolated world, it had actually been several eons.

For Nan Qing, every reunion was something she cherished deeply.

"Mother, I’ll be able to take you out of here very soon."

Nan Qing said firmly.

This was the very reason she had been striving all along.

"Silly child..."

Southern Venerable’s gaze was gentle as she gently pulled Nan Qing into her embrace, her heart filled with emotion.

Back then, when she discovered a new life had suddenly taken root within her, she had considered refining and erasing it—ending her own life in the process—to nip this being, destined to bring disaster upon the world, in the bud.

For it carried the blood of the Lord of Heaven, as well as her own; if it were to be born, it would surely become an unimaginably monstrous being.

Yet she felt an inexplicable reluctance and tenderness. After all, this was, in a sense, her child; she could sense its life force and the breath of its soul.

She feared the emergence of another terrifying entity as incomprehensible and unfathomable as the Lord of Heaven, one that would bring boundless calamity and upheaval to all realms.

Yet that child was intelligent and obedient, gentle and understanding, and had accompanied her through a long stretch of time within her lonely coffin.

In the end, her maternal tenderness caused her to abandon all thoughts of self-destruction.

Fortunately, to this day, she has never felt the slightest regret; she does not regret giving birth to such a child.

Both mother and daughter cherish this rare opportunity to be together.

Nan Qing shared her experiences and observations from the outside world with her mother, while Southern Venerable Nan Zun smiled and listened patiently.

Only in front of her mother does Nan Qing let down all her defenses, masks, and scheming.

"The world outside is in turmoil now. The Reckoning Envoys from the Land Of Truth have descended, and the Era of Chaos is coming to an end. The Vast Expanse is in chaos—more chaotic and terrifying than even the Age of Black Calamity."

“Figures of the End of the Path tier, beings of the True Path tier—all those who have walked the path of the Supreme Shattering—are emerging one after another…”

Nan Qing briefed him on the situation outside.

“Earlier, the High Priest Wu Yue sought me out to ask about certain matters. I had already guessed this would happen—things are no longer peaceful outside.”

Zun sighed lightly.

"However, I have at least seen my father..."

After a brief pause, Nan Qing’s clear, luminous eyes suddenly burst with a startling radiance, her spirit soaring.

This was the first time she had spoken the word “father” aloud. It felt a bit strange at first, but soon it came naturally.

The Southern Venerable’s expression stiffened slightly. To her, those two words sounded awkward to the ear, carrying an indescribable connotation.

She had originally come into the world to seal the Lord of Heaven’s Succession, yet in the end… she had given birth to Nan Qing, who carried her bloodline.

One could only say that fate had played a cruel trick.

“Has the seal on the Lord of Heaven truly been broken?”

Southern Venerable shook her head and sighed softly, though she did not seem particularly surprised—as if she had known this would happen all along.

“The seal on the Lord of Heaven was actually broken long ago. Now, under a new identity, he roams the Vast Expanse and has established the Heaven Slaying Alliance…”

Nan Qing began to speak of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

"Heaven Slaying Alliance……"

Southern Venerable froze.

Confined here under house arrest, she had no knowledge of what had transpired in the outside world over the years. Although the High Priestess Wuyue would occasionally visit, she never informed her of any events beyond these walls.

"It has all come to pass, just as foretold."

Southern Venerable murmured.

"Mother, what do you mean by that?"

The question caught Nan Qing off guard. What did “it has all come to pass” mean? Could it be that Mother had known all along that this would happen?

"Have you ever met the Western Saint?"

Southern Venerable composed her expression and suddenly grew serious.

In fact, when the High Priestess of Wuyue had come to see her last time, she had already inquired about the Western Saint, but the Southern Venerable had not revealed anything, merely telling her that the Western Saint and the Palace of Defiance shared a deep connection.

The Western Saint was indeed a mysterious figure, and very few beings truly understood him.

“What’s wrong, Mother? What about the Western Saint?”

Nan Qing looked slightly puzzled, then her expression grew serious as well. She said, “This time, the remnants of the ‘Acting on Heaven’—the Ancestor Xu and Wuxiang—attacked,intending to seize the flesh and blood left behind by the Lord of Heaven, they were thwarted by a woman named Li Hanying. That woman possessed a fragment of the Lord of Heaven’s flesh and blood, and it was precisely because of this that the Primordial Void inadvertently released the malevolent intent of the Lord of Heaven’s deity from within the Coffin of the World’s Demise, blocking the Desolate Yin Heavenly Lord who descended from the Land Of Truth…”

“Western Saint… it’s her again…”

Southern Venerable murmured, his tone strangely tinged with fear, and said, “The blueprints for the First Civilization’s Sacred Treasure—used to seal the Lord of Heaven’s Successor back then—were actually produced by her…”

"What?"

Nan Qing was startled.

Amidst the Vast Expanse, various theories have long circulated. Some say these six blueprints of the First Civilization originated from the Land Of Truth.

Of course, there was also a theory that these six blueprints actually originated from the Great Abyss—the place where the Three True Clans slew the Chaos Will, the first chasm to open since the creation of heaven and earth. It was immeasurably deep, so profound that even a True Path-level annihilator could not set foot there.

From within the Great Abyss, various primordial spiritual treasures would occasionally surge forth, including ancient, mysterious arrays—such as the First Killing Array and the Third Killing Array of antiquity.

"The Western Saint is a mystery; the future is unpredictable and ever-changing, yet she alone can see the true future. One may offend anyone, but one must never offend her."

"She has long foretold that one day in the future, that strand of black mist will return once more, and everything will be reversed, completely overturned."

The Southern Venerable’s gaze was complex.

This was the first time Nan Qing had heard such shocking news.

Just what is the Western Saint’s background?

One must understand that this concerns the future trajectory of the Vast Expanse of multiverses—not a single universe or those vast, grand worlds where one might fathom destiny by observing the river of time.

In the Vast Expanse of all worlds, even True Path-level figures who wield the authority of the Heavenly Dao cannot fathom even the slightest glimpse of the true future; at best, they can catch a wisp of future mist or survey a corner of the future.

The timelines and destinies within those vast worlds or single universes can be loosely likened to small puddles. Thus, figures at the End of the Path level need only a single glance to discern the fluctuations in the numbers of the small fish and shrimp within, to fathom their future destinies—everything is within their grasp.

As the small puddle expands into a pond, the number of fish and shrimp increases, the water grows murkier, and various large fish appear one after another. Even for figures at the "End of the Path" level, it becomes difficult to grasp the clear trajectories of time and destiny.

And the Vast Expanse of all worlds is like that boundless, deep ocean—endless and limitless. To grasp the trajectories of time and fate within it—how daunting a task is that?

Not to mention the chaotic interplay of aestheses from peers of all levels within this realm—utter chaos where all destinies are obscured, the river of time vanishes, and everything becomes a blur, shrouded in thick fog. How can one peer into and anticipate the future?

The moment it is disturbed, all peers will sense it.

This is why most figures at the End of the Path level choose to create their own separate super-multiverses—for only within that universe can they truly grasp the fate trajectories of all living beings.

"The Western Saint might be playing a grand game beyond our imagination, with everyone as pawns..."

The Southern Venerable dropped yet another bombshell, leaving Ling Nan completely dumbfounded where she stood.

In truth, there was more she hadn’t said. The spatiotemporal coordinates of the Nightmare Demon Realm had actually been revealed to her by the Western Saint. Now that she thought about it, could it be that all of this was part of the Western Saint’s scheme?
