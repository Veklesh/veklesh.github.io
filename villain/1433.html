In the dark, profound void.

The Divine Ancestor Tai Xuan glowed with a radiant light; layer upon layer of dazzling, brilliant halos surged and expanded behind her, as chaotic energy overflowed and the primordial divine light permeated the air, as if she were creating a brand-new world.

She wanted to break through and tear apart this prison.

Yet, no matter how hard she tried, no matter how many methods she attempted, she could never break free.

Wave after wave of darkness surged from the unknown void, relentlessly crashing down upon her and crushing all her attempts.

Those dark forces seemed to be rooted in the void; no one knew how they had come into being or why they were attacking, yet they were truly omnipresent.

The Ancestral Goddess Taixuan felt as if she were on the verge of madness. For the first time in her life, she encountered such a bizarre and insidious force. Moreover, the speed of this onslaught was far more terrifying and swift than anything she had ever read about the origins of darkness—it was like a plague.

And to think—she was a Divine Ancestor, not some ordinary being, yet she couldn’t even suppress it.

The methods Gu Changge had employed on her severed arm were far more terrifying than the substance of the Dark Source itself.

This sent a chill down the spine of the Tai Xuan Ancestral God, and a terrifying suspicion began to take root in her mind.

On the surface, he was the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, but could he be the true mastermind behind the assault on the entire Vast Expanse in secret…

Could it be that he truly has no connection to the Ruler of Heaven?

And under the influence of this space, the entire celestial realm of the Tai Xuan Divine Realm was collapsing as well.

Endless torrents of black rain poured down in deluge, as if the heavens and earth had burst their banks, flooding every realm.

To all the inhabitants of the Tai Xuan Divine Realm, this was a scene akin to the end of the world; every citizen and deity was filled with terror and despair.

Beyond the celestial canopy, the Multiverse Void Sea trembled, teeming with eerie and foul sights as dark forces invaded and corroded everything.

The Ancestral Goddess of Taixuan wore a face as still as water, her expression grim to the extreme. Though she could not escape, she could observe every situation outside at all times.

With every divine realm that vanished, she felt her power dwindling and weakening.

If her divine realm were to collapse, there would be no good outcome for her. Not only would her divine power be exhausted, but her entire body would likely be eroded and overtaken by that substance—akin to the very source of darkness—causing her to truly plunge into darkness.

This outcome was something she could never accept.

In the next instant, she let out a furious roar. Her true form swelled explosively within this unknown space, her divine power boundless, divine flames surging and swirling around her like countless vast, boundless starry rivers.

She channeled every divine art and Daoist technique from throughout the ages, even burning her own divine essence, in an attempt to shatter this space.

However, the icy prison, seemingly forged from the dark divine metal of eternity, emitted an eerie and terrifying glow—one capable of erasing even the Dao itself.

Needless to say, the divine arts she channeled were instantly obliterated the moment they touched it.

The cage stood towering and immovable, suffused with an aura of eternity, completely sealing her within.

"Is this the Dark Cage that, throughout the ages and endless years, has left even the mightiest beings powerless?" For the first time, the Ancestral God Tai Xuan experienced the true nature of despair.

No wonder those Ancestral Dao Realm beings who had survived eight Celestial Waning Tribulations would turn pale with fear whenever the Black Calamity was mentioned.

Although she had lived through that era, she had chosen to seclude herself within the boundless expanse of time and space, never truly engaging with it.

Originally, she had felt somewhat relieved about this, paying it little mind, believing it had nothing to do with the Divine Path Civilization system and could never involve them, the Ancestral Gods.

But sometimes, calamities are drawn upon us in just this way.

Thud, thud, thud…

Suddenly, a series of eerie and strange sounds echoed through this void.

The Ancestral Goddess Tai Xuan looked in that direction. She could see that outside the cage, a terrifying black cocoon was forming, enveloped layer by layer in surging black mist.

Within it was a consciousness identical to her own, like a beating heart, radiating a surging and astonishing vitality.

This was precisely the consciousness she had sensed earlier.

The Great Ancestral Goddess never imagined that in such a short time, that consciousness had expanded to this extent.

"The old god must die; the new god must rise."

In the next instant, the expression on the Great Ancestral Goddess’s face shifted. In the stillness, she heard a voice as melodious as heavenly music.

That familiar voice made her blood seem to freeze, her expression filled with gravity and apprehension.

Crack...

The terrifying black cocoon began to shatter, cracks appearing one after another. A flawless, slender snow-white foot emerged first—straight and delicate, like heaven’s most perfect masterpiece. Any man who witnessed this scene would instantly lose his senses, utterly captivated by its beauty.

Yet the expression on the Great Ancestor God’s face grew increasingly grim.

The massive black cocoon continued to shatter. As the figure within emerged, the shattered cocoon fibers dissipated, transforming into a sky-filling black mist that she swallowed and absorbed into her body.

It was a figure both ethereal and perfect, standing amidst the boundless void, as if emerging from a multidimensional spacetime.

Ring after ring of divine light swirled and enveloped her form; each layer resembled a nebula, within which lay scattered worlds and universes of every kind.

Within each divine ring, it seemed as though boundless worlds were being nurtured, with myriad celestial realms undergoing birth, destruction, and evolution.

Unlike the Supreme Ancestral Deity, the worlds surrounding this figure were suffused with a thick, dark aura; they were no longer sacred, nor did they radiate light.

Her eyes were closed; her face shifted constantly—at times ancient and indistinct, at times majestic and solemn, at times alluring and graceful, and at times cold as ice. It changed with every passing moment, impossible to truly discern, shrouded in a vast, dark mist.

The entire expanse of time and space seemed unable to contain her true form; even a single divine ring was enough to fill this vast, shadowy void.

"How is this possible..."

The Great Ancestral God gazed upon this figure, nearly identical to himself. The expression on his face could no longer be described as mere shock or dismay; it was a trembling, bone-chilling fear.

"What are you afraid of?"

"I am you, and you are me; we are one and the same."

Suddenly, the figure before the Great Ancestral Goddess opened its eyes. A face identical to hers approached—beautiful yet alluring, with a faint smile curving its lips, and a thick, dark aura swirling around it.

"Where on earth did you come from? How could you possibly have a body exactly like mine?"

Grand Ancestor Taixuan demanded, a wave of uncontrollable fear surging through her, chilling her to the very core.

"Where did I come from? I was created by my master."

"And I am not your body; I am your consciousness. What you see is merely an illusion."

"But now, you are my consciousness."

The Dark Taishanzu God smiled, her beautiful face etched with mockery.

She reached out, her hand passing right through the dark prison to grasp the Great Ancestral Goddess’s neck.

No matter how the Great Ancestral Goddess of Darkness struggled, it was all in vain. The moment her strength passed through the figure before her, it was as if she had passed through a layer of hazy, insubstantial mist, vanishing instantly.

"From now on, I am the Great Ancestral Goddess."

The Dark Taixuanzu God smiled, and his figure vanished directly into the depths of this pitch-black space.

"He truly is the Lord of Heaven..."

Trapped within the dark prison, the Great Ancestral God of Xuan had narrowly escaped death. Her heart was filled with dread, and now that she had finally confirmed the truth, her eyes were filled with fear and her voice trembled.

It seemed that when Gu Changge left that bait behind that day, he had already foreseen this very moment.

He not only intended to strike at the entire lineage of the Vast Expanse True Realm, but had even begun plotting to lay his hands on the Divine Path Civilization.

In the span of a single day, the Divine Realm of Taishuan was engulfed by a deluge of black light.

All the divine realms were reduced to realms of darkness. Yet the Ancestral God of Taixuan, having emerged from the Divine Realm, not only failed to stop this but immediately issued an order: she commanded all the gods to pool their strength and attack the remaining divine realms. Her intent was to plunder resources and forge an even more powerful dark divine essence.

This news sent shockwaves through the Divine Civilization; both the Taosu Divine Realm and the Haoshang Divine Realm were plunged into turmoil and terror.

The fact that the Tai Xuan Divine Realm was being eroded by darkness signaled that the dark factions had already begun to take action against the Divine Path Civilization.

The fact that the Taixuan Divine Realm was deploying its forces so brazenly clearly indicated it had something to rely on. What terrified the other Ancestral Gods even more was the question: when had the mighty Taixuan Ancestral God fallen into darkness?

Meanwhile, on the other side.

At Yao Yao’s earnest request, Gu Changge finally granted an audience to the princess from the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty.

In the courtyard, seated at a jade table, Jiang Weiyang wore a snow-white gown. Though she wore a gauze veil to conceal her true features, she remained visibly anxious. Her pale, delicate hands clutched the hem of her skirt tightly, and she sat with her back perfectly straight, fearful of revealing even the slightest hint of impoliteness or unladylike behavior.

She had been eagerly anticipating the chance to “meet” Gu Changge.

Having just learned from Yao Yao that Gu Changge had returned to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, she once again mustered the courage to ask Yao Yao to pass on a message.

Whether Gu Changge agreed to see her or not didn’t matter.

Fortunately, her efforts were not in vain. She learned from Yao Yao that Gu Changge had agreed to see her. That night, she was so overjoyed, excited, and nervous that she couldn’t sleep a wink.

Early the next morning, she had two maids help her wash and dress, then personally selected several beautiful gowns. She tried them on one after another, comparing them for quite some time, before finally deciding on this pure white, spotless long gown.

The only thing that left Jiang Weiyang feeling a bit regretful was the birthmark she had deliberately left on her face, which had turned half her face a dark, bruised shade. Without special methods, it would be difficult to remove and clean up in a short time.

Under these circumstances, she finally arrived at the courtyard where Gu Changge used to practice his asceticism, led by Yao Yao, and waited for him there.

During her time in the Heaven Slaying Alliance, her understanding of the organization had gradually deepened. Through conversations with the cultivators and other beings around her, she had come to realize that the Heaven Slaying Alliance was not as evil and terrifying as the outside world claimed—a place likened to a den of dragons and tigers.

Only by truly understanding it could one realize that the order and stability within the Heaven Slaying Alliance far surpassed those of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty.

This was the embryonic outline of a new era about to be born.

And among all the stories she’d heard, those concerning Gu Changge were the ones she’d heard most often and found most fascinating. Even in the teahouses within the city, she’d find a corner by the window and sit there; as long as she could overhear cultivators and mortals discussing the Alliance Master, she could listen all day without feeling the slightest bit of fatigue or boredom. On the contrary, she would sometimes even approach them to ask questions.

The more she learned, the deeper her curiosity and reverence for Gu Changge grew. These emotions kept her tossing and turning, unable to sleep all night, longing for her eyes to see the light and witness his true face with her own eyes.

Judging by the few gentle words she had heard when he saved her from the attack by the Star-Bright National Preceptor that day, she could only imagine that he must be a remarkable man, as graceful as jade and possessing the ethereal bearing of a celestial being.

Gu Changge, however, paid no heed to the countless jumbled and strange thoughts swirling through Princess Weiyang’s mind at that moment.

To be honest, although Princess Wei Yang was hailed as the jewel of the Wei Yang Immortal Dynasty, in the current turbulent and unstable situation, without the protection of the Wei Yang Immortal Dynasty, she was, in reality, no different from an ordinary woman.

All her titles and honors were, in Gu Changge’s eyes, utterly insignificant.

The reason he wanted to meet her was for another purpose: Jiang Weiyang’s appearance at the Heaven Slaying Alliance was, in a sense, actually a scheme orchestrated by Emperor Jiang Yun of Weiyang.

Gu Changge had been planning to take action against that ever-restless Fate, and this provided the perfect opportunity to turn the tables on her.
