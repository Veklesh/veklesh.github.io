News that the Black Calamity had once again descended upon the vast realms quickly spread throughout all lands, sending shockwaves of dread through every nation of civilization.

In just a few short days, many realms were attacked and engulfed by the black mist; no living beings or races could escape, and they were utterly buried within it.

One star system after another rapidly dimmed, plunging into eternal darkness.

And this trend continued to spread rapidly outward, a force even the most powerful Dao Realm beings could scarcely halt.

Even within certain realms of civilization, black mist began to seep out, drifting through spatial rifts and spreading across the multiverse, rapidly corroding those star systems.

At this moment, the descent of the black mist seemed to penetrate every crevice.

Some cultivators and sentient beings discovered with horror that, at some unknown point, their spiritual seas had also been tainted by that aura. The black mist had appeared in their spiritual realms—across unimaginably vast distances of time and space—in ways they could not comprehend, seeking to corrupt their will and perception.

These scenes unfolded rapidly across the cosmos, filling countless races and civilizations with dread and terror. It seemed as though there was no safe haven left in the multiverse; no matter where they fled, they risked being swallowed by the black mist.

Numerous supreme realms and civilizations immediately cast aside their past entanglements of self-interest, determined to find a way to halt this dark calamity.

The entire vast expanse was thus thrown into turmoil.

The arrival of the black mist caught everyone off guard. Aside from a few supreme civilizations that had anticipated and prepared for it, many nations were utterly helpless.

They could only do their best to shrink their spheres of influence and move as close as possible to the heart of the Vast Expanse.

In the Yuan Hope Civilization, nearly all races and factions received the explosive news that the remnants of the Black Calamity had once again descended upon the Vast Expanse.

The other supreme civilizations—such as the Dawn Civilization, the Yan Yang True Realm, and the Shang Yin True Realm—immediately relayed this news to the Yuan Hope Civilization upon learning of it.

This was because Xi Nu, the founder of the Xi Yuan Sanctuary within the Yuan Hope Civilization, was one of the sages who had once fought against the Black Calamity, and had even wielded the Mirror of Reincarnation—one of the six primordial treasures of the First Civilization.

Consequently, many of the surrounding supreme civilizations sought to know if the Yuan Hope Civilization possessed any means to counter this Black Calamity that had suddenly descended upon them.

Since the era of the Black Calamity, countless ages and eras have passed. It is unknown how many civilizations have perished in the interim,

, and many Civilizations have endured multiple cataclysmic purges yet remain standing.

However, there are very few supreme realms like the Yuan Hope Civilization that have truly experienced that era firsthand; they have never actually faced the 787 Catastrophe of the Black Calamity, let alone have any means of dealing with it.

Moreover, remnants of the Black Calamity have appeared in the past, but they were ultimately eradicated by the Xiyuan Sanctuary.

The Holy Maiden of Yuan Hope has even led certain orthodox factions within the Yuan Hope Civilization to personally eradicate remnants of the Black Calamity, possessing firsthand experience in this regard.

At this critical juncture, the surrounding superpowers naturally look to the Yuan Hope Civilization for leadership, even though the Yuan Hope Civilization itself has only recently emerged from a major upheaval.

“Why did the Black Calamity suddenly descend out of nowhere? Isn’t this a bit too sudden…” “The remnants of the Black Calamity choosing to emerge at this time likely isn’t without reason. I fear even worse things lie ahead—a greater storm is about to descend.”

Ling Yuling sat upright in the courtyard, her delicate brows furrowed. She glanced at Gu Changge, who remained calm and composed, sipping tea not far away, and a flicker of confusion passed through her eyes.

Upon learning that the Black Calamity had returned to the world, Gu Changge showed only a slight surprise, as if it were not entirely unexpected, and he did not seem the least bit worried.

“The cosmos is so vast. Even if the Black Calamity were to affect the Yuan Hope Civilization, who knows how long it would take? This is not something we need to worry about. After all, if the sky falls, the tall ones will hold it up.” “Besides, hasn’t the Black Calamity already been eradicated once before? Even if it returns, it may not necessarily regain its former ferocity and might.”“Since it cannot be completely eradicated, its return is inevitable. Why then should we be so frightened and anxious?”

Mu Yan lay bored on the stone table, her chin resting on her palm, watching Tian Tian not far away as she practiced the breathing technique Gu Changge had just taught her.

“What you say isn’t wrong, but that’s because you haven’t personally experienced the Age of the Black Calamity. You don’t know how brutal, dark, and hopeless that era was—you don’t know just how terrifying the Black Calamity truly is…”

Ling Yuling couldn’t help but shake her head slightly, thinking Mu Yan was far too laid-back—she had absolutely no concept of just how terrifying the Black Calamity was.

This only served to prove the old saying: ignorance is bliss.

Mu Yan couldn’t help but roll her eyes. “Since you’re so worried, Senior—worrying needlessly—why don’t you try to find a way to stop this Black Calamity disaster?”

Ever since she found out Ling Yuling had beaten her to the punch, she’d taken a strong dislike to her—especially since she’d gone out of her way to speak well of her to Gu Changge back then.

Faced with Mu Yan’s sharp retort, Ling Yuling was left speechless.

If she had such capabilities, how could she have ended up in this predicament?

“Master, what is this ‘dark calamity’?”

Meanwhile, Tian Tian, standing in the distance, blinked her bright, dark eyes, completely baffled, having no idea what Mu Yan and Ling Yuling were discussing.

“You’ll understand once you go out into the world to hone your skills,” Gu Changge replied casually.

The Black Calamity had descended far faster than he had anticipated. It seemed the Celestial Assembly had indeed been planning and lying in wait for many years, having long since made extensive preparations.

However, the current scale was far from sufficient. The Black Calamity was still in its initial stages of eruption, and it was still a long way from reaching the level he desired.

Still, it was enough for him to begin his next move.

Upon hearing this, Tian Tian puffed out her cheeks, her face full of slight resentment.

Gu Changge was becoming increasingly dismissive toward her. He used to explain things patiently to her. Could it be because she had matured recently and wasn’t as cute as she used to be?

“Yu Ling, is there any way to stop this Black Calamity?” “What do you think?”

Gu Changge shot Ling Yuling a glance, paying no heed to Tian Tian’s petty mood swings.

Sooner or later, he would send her out into the world to be tempered by experience—to endure all manner of bloodshed and backstabbing—so she could grow on her own. Naturally, he couldn’t keep pampering her like a flower in a greenhouse.

“As things stand, there’s no solution yet. However, this Black Calamity has struck suddenly and without warning; it’s unlikely to last long. Even during the era when the Dai Tian Organization existed, the Black Calamity spread gradually, eventually engulfing all realms.”“I suspect the remnants of the Black Calamity have other objectives. While they may cause immense panic in the Vast Expanse in the short term, it’s unlikely they’ll significantly alter the overall situation.” Ling Yuling paused in surprise, not expecting Gu Changge to ask her this suddenly. After giving it serious thought and analysis, she replied.

No matter how powerful the remnants of the Black Calamity may be, they could not possibly truly wreak havoc across the entire Vast Expanse at this moment. The realms of Civilization are no pushovers; though the source of darkness is a formidable foe, there are certainly ways to counter and confront it.

What we need to consider now is not how to counter and stop the spread of the black mist, but how to thwart the true purpose of the remnants of the Black Calamity.

After all, although the black mist expands rapidly in the early stages, it will soon begin to wane. The territories it affects and devours will not be extensive; in the end, its expansion will be limited to incremental gains.

Just like a tidal wave: once the crest has passed, the subsequent waves gradually diminish until they subside.

This was the pattern summarized by the sages of their generation.

“You certainly see things clearly,” Gu Changge said with a faint smile. Even he had to admit that Ling Yuling was exceptionally sharp-witted, able to see beyond the surface of the Black Calamity’s sudden return to the world and perceive its true nature.

Ling Yuling gazed at Gu Changge with clear eyes, as if trying to discern whether his praise was genuine or had some ulterior motive.

“After all, I am someone who lived through that era; naturally, I understand more than people do today…” Her long eyelashes fluttered slightly as she spoke softly.

Gu Changge smiled. “Then how do you know that, after all these years, the remnants of the Black Calamity haven’t made any progress at all?”

Ling Yuling suddenly froze.

She recalled the “Zhan” and “Shadows” mentioned by the indigenous inhabitants of the Floating Continent during their time in the Chaotic Spacetime Sea.

Weren’t those the eerie creatures created by the remnants of the Black Calamity?

“Come with me to take a walk in the outside world. The current Xi (bgi) Yuan Civilization still needs you to steady the people’s hearts; things out there are probably in a state of utter panic by now.”

Gu Changge spoke casually, ignoring the dazed Ling Yuling, and walked ahead, vanishing from the valley in an instant.

Ling Yuling snapped out of her daze and, without giving it much thought, transformed into a beam of divine light and followed after him.

Just as Gu Changge had said, the Yuan Hope Civilization was now plunged into panic by the news that the Black Calamity had once again descended upon the world—and the panic was even greater than when similar news had spread before the Xianchu Haotu had been destroyed.

This was because the current news had been confirmed, whereas the report from the Xianchu Vastlands back then had been nothing more than speculation.

Although the various races and lineages had already submitted to the Heaven-Conquering Alliance and become part of it, they were filled with endless dread and fear toward the Black Calamity—a catastrophe described in ancient texts as representing destruction, despair, and utter devastation, a near-insurmountable disaster.

Gu Changge had Ling Yuling stay by his side so that, as a former sage who had fought against the Black Calamity, she could reassure the people across the various cosmic realms of the Yuan Hope Civilization.

No matter how chaotic and panic-stricken the vast multiverse might become, it must not, for the time being, affect the Yuan Hope Civilization where he resided; Gu Changge disliked the atmosphere of widespread anxiety that surrounded him.

Moreover, in the face of this Black Calamity sweeping across all realms, he intended for the Heaven-Defying Alliance to play a

crucial role.

At this moment, Ling Yuling seemed to finally grasp Gu Changge’s intentions. She began tirelessly making appearances across various realms, acting as an envoy of the Heaven-Slaying Alliance to soothe the frightened hearts of the people.

After all, she was a sage who had once fought against the Black Calamity; with this status, her efforts were naturally exceptionally effective. At the same time, Gu Changge instructed Ling Huang, Zhuo Fengwei, and others to follow Ling Yuling and spread the Alliance’s doctrines and principles.

The very existence of the Heaven-Conquering Alliance was to uphold the Way of Heaven, protect all living beings, and purge the darkness.

In the midst of this catastrophic Dark Calamity, only those who place their faith in the Heaven-Conquering Alliance can find protection.

Undoubtedly, many traditional sects and factions were outraged and disgusted by the Heaven-Slaying Alliance’s behavior of “taking advantage of a crisis.” In today’s vast and chaotic world, which faction or Civilization has the audacity and capability to make such a claim?

Is this not simply waving a banner to deceive the masses?

However, such proclamations and propaganda have had a strikingly different, calming effect on the hearts of countless cultivators and sentient beings.

It seemed as though even she believed that the Heaven-Conquering Alliance could protect its followers and believers amidst this dark calamity.

At the same time, a chaotic and terrifying battle was unfolding deep within the Chaotic Spacetime Sea at the heart of the Imperial Immortal Palace.

Outside the Floating Continent, a tidal wave of black mist surged forward, carrying an aura of destruction and decay, intent on dissolving and shattering the light barrier enveloping the entire Floating Continent.

Amidst the swirling black mist, strand after strand twisted, emitting an eerie, bone-chilling laugh that made one’s teeth ache.

Blurred, shadowy beings coalesced from within the mist. At first, they were as black as ink, resembling a pool of molten gelatin, but they quickly took on human form—their features, clothing, and demeanor indistinguishable from those of the natives within the light barrier.

With cold laughter, they summoned a dazzling array of ancient treasures and Daoist artifacts, unleashing a barrage of attacks.

Behind them, black mist coalesced into the forms of primordial beings. Their bodies alone were as vast as stars, their eyes like mountains and lakes, staring intently at the light screen as they relentlessly pounded it, seeking to shatter it.

There were also featherless bone birds, resembling vultures, whose outstretched wings seemed to block out the sky entirely. Their protruding bone claws were shrouded in endless black mist as they repeatedly struck down, emitting a terrifying rumbling sound.

The floating continent shook violently; mountains trembled and the earth quaked, with terrifying cracks appearing everywhere. The dwellings

—houses, fields, palaces, and towers—had long since been reduced to rubble. Mountain ranges collapsed, ancient trees crashed to the ground, leaving a scene of utter devastation.

As long as the black mist did not dissipate, they would never truly die; they would constantly reform and resurrect, an utterly terrifying sight. In the end, they could only be worn down to death, swallowed by the black mist, leaving not even a single bone behind.

This scene filled everyone with utter despair. Now they could only wait for the protective barrier left by the Holy Ancestor to shatter, for those dark creatures to surge forward, and for everyone to fight them to the death.
