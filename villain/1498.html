Amidst billowing black mist, a magnificent black chariot—forged from immortal mystic gold—raced through the vast cosmos, drawn by several ancient ancestral beasts of the Remnant Race that exuded an aura of darkness, heading straight for the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Qi Luo, the Fourth Priestess of the Celestial Assembly, sat quietly in the center of the chariot, appearing serene, though her hand clenched tightly around her sleeve betrayed a hint of tension.

The interior of the chariot was spacious, resembling a miniature world, with dense dark matter intertwining and permeating the air. Seated opposite her was a young woman wearing a gauze hood, with a pure-white True Roar crouching at her feet.

If Zhu Xuan, who had once gone in search of the Pan Clan’s Pool of Creation, were here, he would surely recognize this woman.

After enduring countless life-and-death perils along the way, the group finally reached the former territory of the Pan Clan. Amidst the ruins, they discovered clues left behind by the clan.

As the sole surviving descendant of the Pan Clan, Zhu Xuan, relying on his bloodline, eventually discovered the Dark Flower Pool—left behind by the Lord of the Pan Clan, the former Le Hou—in the deepest recesses of a void-time realm.

Upon returning to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, the group parted ways along the way. This young woman, wearing a gauze hat and carrying a snow-white True Roar, had once helped them repel a horde of dark creatures outside the Floating Continent.

"Why so many questions along the way? Since it was the High Priestess’s order, it must have been done with the Lord’s approval. Otherwise, do you think the High Priestess could have obtained the Mirror of Reincarnation on her own?"

"Of the six treasures of the First Civilization, many have already fallen into the hands of the Heaven Slaying Alliance. I truly cannot fathom why, in an attempt to curry favor, they would hand over the Mirror of Reincarnation."

But with the emergence of super-multiverses from all directions and the influx of numerous beings at the End of the Path, an Ancestral Dao Realm being like her was simply no match for them, and she no longer dared to move about freely.

Back then, the Ruler of Heaven had been sealed by the First Civilization’s Treasure. Now, with even more supreme beings emerging, she suddenly felt a sense of uncertainty and unease, wondering if the Celestial Assembly might not be as powerful as she had once believed.

The Ruler of Heaven was stronger than an End of the Path Being, but even he was likely only at the True Path level.

Whether in the Nine Heavens or the Ancient Hidden Civilization, neither was guarded by just a single True Path-level being.

"Short-sighted."

Seeing Qi Luo’s expression, a trace of derision flashed deep in A Li’s eyes. Whether in the past or the present, it was impossible to claim that merely six treasures of the First Civilization could seal the Acting Ruler of Heaven.

Moreover, the relationship between the Lord of the Heavens and the Alliance Master of the Heaven Slaying Alliance seemed far more complex than the world imagined.

Back when she had left the Floating Continent with Zhu Xuan and the others to journey to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, the moment she laid eyes on the Alliance Leader, a profound sense of dread and trembling had welled up within her heart.

That sensation was even more terrifying than the surging aura she had faced within the Coffin of the Underworld; the dark matter within her body trembled uncontrollably, on the verge of breaking free.

This was the first time in her life she had ever experienced such a sensation. Perhaps that wasn’t the most significant part.

But Nan Qing, the young lady of the Celestial Clan, bore a striking resemblance to the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

The many secrets and schemes of this world were simply beyond the comprehension of a being of her caliber.

Although Qi Luo ranked fourth among the Celestial Clan, she had not been a priestess from the start; rather, she had been chosen by the other chief priests and had gradually risen to her current position.

The Celestial Assembly had once fallen into disarray; had it not been for that incident, the later "Daiqing Rebellion," orchestrated and incited by the progenitor Xu Zai, would never have occurred.

Qi Luo, however, did not have as many thoughts as A Li at this moment.

Her recent unease stems not only from a sense of her own weakness and inadequacy, but more so from the fact that she bears half the flesh and blood of the former Lord of Heaven.

Now that the Ruler of Heaven has revived, why is there still no reaction from the flesh and blood that was once stolen and seized?

She now found herself in a bind, utterly at a loss as to how to handle that fragment of flesh. With her current abilities, she was simply incapable of refining and absorbing it; even bearing a single thread of its vital energy had nearly caused her Dao Fruit to shatter.

Perhaps only figures of the End of the Path tier would be qualified to barely bear it.

Yet the Ancestor Xu’s whereabouts remain unknown, and it is unclear how many remnants of the Eight Clans still linger in the world.

To her, that fragment of flesh from the Ruler of Heaven was a hot potato; she had no idea how to part with it.

"Perhaps I should take this opportunity to deliver the Mirror of Reincarnation and hand over that piece of flesh to the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance."

In some super-multiverses, extraordinarily powerful and brilliant figures have also emerged—so-called prodigies and prodigious talents who attained the Dao Realm in less than an era of cultivation, as well as those blessed with immense fortune who inherited the legacy of fallen masters at the End of the Path.

In this glorious age, the stars shine brilliantly. Amidst the Vast Expanse, battles and clashes of all kinds can be witnessed daily: ancient ruins emerge, ancient secret realms manifest, and forbidden lands that had fallen into the depths of spacetime resurface.

All sentient beings in the Vast Expanse might be reduced to dust, with no living creature spared.

The end of the Heaven and Earth Era is entirely different from any of the Great Calamities of the past or present; otherwise, these super-multiverses would not have stepped forward one after another…
