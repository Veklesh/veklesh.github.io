"What?"

"I don’t think I misperceived it. The power there has weakened. Could it be that the Master intends to let them go?" The gentle woman looked back in confusion at the coffin behind her, which was fading and growing increasingly blurry, then murmured, "But perhaps it’s just my imagination. How could I possibly fathom the Master’s will?"

As the fluctuations of battle dissipated and all laws and order vanished, the entire sky once again plunged into a deep, pitch-black void devoid of light. All dark beings knelt on the ground with eyes filled with reverence and fervor, bowing their heads in homage, while a solemn and eerie silence reigned throughout the cosmos.

At the site of the Crescent Moon Sect, only a void remained; even traces and karmic connections had been erased from the heavens and earth.

If anyone were to attempt to trace the Crescent Moon Sect’s existence at this moment, they would find nothing but a void.

All traces of its existence, every moment of its past, seemed to have truly vanished without a trace, as if it had never existed at all.

“My lady, shall we pursue them?” the maid holding the oil-paper umbrella asked, having regained her composure.

The gentle-mannered woman had also regained her composure. Her bright eyes gazed upon the ruins as she pondered for a long time before shaking her head. “They may have escaped the monk, but they cannot escape the temple. As long as they still exist in this world, they will be found.”

“The young mistress will be arriving here shortly. When she does, simply report everything that happened here truthfully to her.”

“Since the young mistress cares so deeply about the secrets of the Crescent Moon Sect, she will surely find her own way to pursue them.”

With that, she took the oil-paper umbrella from the hand of the maid at her side. Her steps were as light as lotus petals; the space before her blurred, ripples of energy spreading out, and in an instant, she vanished.

Wu Jue had stood respectfully at a distance the entire time, head bowed, not daring to look up or ask a single question.

Only after the gentle woman had departed did he exhale softly, regaining his usual cold, imposing demeanor.

The Crescent Moon Sect had specifically been instructed by Miss Nanqing to preserve every single ruin, down to every flower and tree.

Who could have foreseen this turn of events? That mad old woman was indeed formidable, her methods unpredictable and eerie; even under such terrifying pressure, she was still able to absorb the Crescent Moon Sect and carry it away.

Moreover, that terrifying and eerie gray substance was actually able to stand toe-to-toe with the dark matter, a development that truly caught Wu Yang off guard and exceeded his wildest expectations.

"Could this be the reason Miss Nan Qing specifically instructed us?"

Ever since the defensive line of this territory’s dams had fallen, the dark beings had swept in unchecked. Numerous orthodox sects, including the Crescent Moon Sect, vanished one after another. In the span of a single night, chaos and turmoil engulfed the various cosmic starfields, leaving a trail of desolation and suffering in their wake.

The Righteous Unity Alliance remained indifferent, sending no reinforcements whatsoever. Instead, the vast armies of the Heaven Slaying Alliance rushed in from all directions, facing off against the dark beings from afar. They reclaimed and protected numerous territories, sparing many ethnic groups and factions from disaster and ruin.

News of these events spread throughout the vast realms of the civilizations, sparking immense reactions and upheaval.

Many civilizations that had not yet faced the onslaught of the dark beings were gripped by waves of dread and fear—as the saying goes, “When the rabbit dies, the fox mourns; when one of their kind is harmed, all feel the pain.”

This was especially true for the many reclusive sects and clans that had emerged at this time; they began to hesitate over whether to join forces with the Righteous Unity Alliance.

The overwhelming power and terror of the dark beings were plain for all to see; relying solely on their own strength, it was nearly impossible to resist or stand against them. Therefore, alliance was the only viable path and solution, and now the only two choices were the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance.

The Heaven Slaying Alliance is unorthodox and treacherous—true villains who pay no heed to righteousness or morality—yet they appear reliable in critical moments.

The Righteous Unity Alliance, on the other hand, are hypocrites. On the surface, they preach righteousness and morality, champion justice, and claim to uphold humanity, yet in critical moments, they betray their word and prioritize their own interests.

Under these circumstances, all factions and clans within the Vast Expanse must make a choice. After all, the Dark Beings’ predecessors were the “Representatives of Heaven”—rulers who once governed the Vast Expanse, commanded all realms, and even usurped the authority of the Heavenly Dao.

Their footprints span all realms of the cosmos and countless timelines; there is no time or space, no coordinate, that they do not know.

No matter how vast and boundless the Vastness may be, no matter where one hides, there will come a day when the Dark Beings will discover them.

Given this inevitable trend, even the high-ranking officials of the Righteous Unity Alliance are deliberating on negotiating a truce with the Dark Spirits, willing to pay a tremendous price and sacrifice significant interests to do so.

How could ordinary clans and lineages possibly survive in such a vast and turbulent environment?

The Ancient Realm of Shaking Light: a surging river, rushing and mighty, winding like a serpent as it weaves through towering mountains. Its waves reach the heavens, stretching into a vast, boundless expanse with no discernible edge. A thin mist rises from the river, shrouding everything in an impenetrable haze.

A modest merchant ship drifted leisurely downstream. There were few cultivators or living beings aboard—all were intent on crossing the Yao Guang Ancestral River to reach the opposite shore.

The Yao Guang Ancestral River is the name given to this waterway by the indigenous people of the ancient Yao Guang realm.

It is said that this river originates from the former Yao Guang Ancestral Court, winding its way down from the sacred mountain of the court. Along its course, it flows through plains, cities, and kingdoms, nurturing countless living beings.

In the past, the sects and factions of the Ancient Yao Guang Realm were all established along this ancestral river. Those near the source enjoyed abundant spiritual energy, profound blessings, and flourishing fortune, often standing firm for tens of millions of years—or even longer—as truly immortal lineages.

However, with the collapse and disintegration of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court, the spiritual energy of this ancestral river gradually dried up and dissipated.

By the later eras, it had become an ordinary river; only on rare occasions could one catch a glimpse of extraordinary phenomena, prompting people to sigh with emotion, lean on the railing in remembrance, and reflect on the former might and longevity of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court.

At this moment, in a passenger cabin on the second deck of this merchant ship, a man and a woman sat leaning against the porthole. Before them lay a pot of fragrant tea, its aroma drifting gently through the air, a soothing and pleasant scent.

“Has something happened?”

The woman was tall and graceful, with a delicate and exquisite figure. She wore a light gray gauze gown and a veil, her jet-black hair cascading like clouds, held in place by a wooden hairpin. Her eyebrows were as dark as ink, and her eyes, as black as lacquer, alternated between the depth of the ocean and the majesty of a sovereign.

Across from her sat a man in a loose, dark-gray robe, his fingers gently caressing a teacup as if deep in thought. His eyes held a look that was both intriguing and enigmatic—none other than Gu Changge, who had recently left the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

"It’s nothing."

Upon hearing this, Gu Changge shook his head slightly and set down the teacup.

Just a moment ago, he had sensed an anomaly in his own power—or, to be precise, an anomaly in another part of his power.

Although he had never fully reclaimed that portion of his power, it did not hinder his control or perception of it in the slightest.

Back when he was still the Ruler of Heaven, in order to ensure he could be “sealed” legitimately in the future,

he had divided his power into several parts in advance.

One of those parts remained among the Heavenly Host, sealed away alongside his former physical body.

Additionally, another portion was sealed alongside the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor, and the final portion was sealed by the six treasures of the First Civilization.

In that very moment, he distinctly sensed that a thread of his power within the Heavenly Host had been borrowed, and among the beings that thread of power was intended to confront were those upon whom he had deliberately left a mark.

There were not many people whom Gu Changge had specifically marked; aside from the handful in the Daochang True Realm, there were almost none.

Yue Mingkong, Jiang Chuchu, and the others were all within the Daochang True Realm and had not appeared in Cangmang.

Moving forward, a three-way standoff was inevitable. The remnants of the Black Calamity, the Righteous Unity Alliance, and the Heaven Slaying Alliance would become the three strongest factions dominating the situation in the Vast Expanse.

The remaining clans and lineages would have to find a way to align themselves with one of these three.

This silent game of strategy had begun a very, very long time ago.
