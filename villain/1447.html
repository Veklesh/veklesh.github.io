Meanwhile, beyond the world.

The territory of the Luo Clan.

Once one of the Eight Clans of the Heaven's Proxy, the Luo Clan vanished from public view after the collapse of the Heaven's Proxy.

Even the other members of the Eight Clans knew little of the Luo Clan’s whereabouts.

Furthermore, the Heavenly Host had long been searching for the various factions that had once swept away the essence of the Celestial Realm. Borrowing the power from the Coffin of the Underworld, they continuously sent forces to raid and slaughter, forcing the Luo Clan to wander and flee for a very long time.

It was not until the Luo Clan’s distant ancestor, the Luo Lord, passed away from severe injuries that, in his final moments, he employed supreme means to sever the Luo Clan’s connection with the rest of the world. Only then did the Luo Clan gradually settle down, retreating to seclusion beyond the Vast Expanse and turning a blind eye to the vast affairs of the mortal realm.

Many members of the Luo Clan firmly believed that their ancient ancestor would not perish, but would return at some point in the future. Therefore, every few eons, they would select descendants with the strongest and purest bloodlines to serve as offerings to the ancestor, illuminating his path back from the vast darkness of the Nine Underworlds.

Luo Xiangjun’s decision to flee the Luo Clan and become a mere “bandit” stemmed precisely from her having witnessed firsthand the entire process of her parents and relatives being sacrificed by their own people.

Throughout this process, her parents and others never once resisted; on the contrary, they were utterly captivated by it, viewing their ability to serve as offerings to the distant ancestor as the supreme honor.

Luo Xiangjun believed that the entire Luo clan had gone mad, sacrificing their own people generation after generation for a faith that had long since died, ceased to exist, and was nothing but a phantom—all in exchange for that meager hope.

Such a sick, mad clan would sooner or later vanish completely into the Vast Expanse.

It was a pity that, after fleeing for so many years, she had once again set foot on this land, a place of calamity, filled with despair and desolation.

Especially after learning from the Eternal Primordial God that the Luo clan’s distant ancestor likely did not die, but instead, like him, had deceived the heavens and gone into hiding.

The elders of the Luo Clan had long since received word of Luo Xiangjun’s arrival and were waiting for her in the ancestral hall.

The Grand Elder of the Luo Clan was a man with snow-white hair and a vigorous spirit. Clad in a silver robe, his face bore the marks of time, and his eyes shone with a brilliant silver light.

Two cross-like fissures ran through his pupils, from which strands of silver light radiated, permeated with the essence of time and space—an astonishing sight.

This was a natural trait of the Luo Clan—the innate ability to channel the power of time and space. Luo Xiangjun usually kept this ability hidden; otherwise, given her cultivation and strength, she would never have been able to even approach many of the great tombs, let alone escape from them.

Her daring to accept the key to the Eternal Treasure from Gu Changge’s hands was precisely because of her clan’s innate talent.

"The burial site of our distant ancestors lies deep within the ancestral cemetery. There slumber the heroic spirits of our Luo clan..."

"If you wish to go, please follow me."

The Luo Clan’s Grand Elder led the way, guiding the Eternal Primordial God into the cemetery, while Luo Xiangjun trailed behind, following in their footsteps.

She did not know how the Eternal Primordial God had managed, with just a few simple words, to convince the Grand Elder of the Luo Clan and secure his agreement to grant him access to the resting place of the Luo Lord.

But there must be some connection between the Luo Clan and the Eternal Primordial God.

The Eternal Primordial God was by no means comparable to ordinary Ancestral Gods. Whether it was the Dao of Space-Time controlled by the Lord of Luo or the Dao of Time controlled by the Eternal Primordial God, both were considered the most profound and mysterious Daos in the world.

Even for those at the Ancestral Dao Realm, it is extremely difficult to delve deeply into and comprehend these two Daos of Space-Time and Time.

The Luo Clan’s cemetery is situated deep within the rear mountains. Though called a cemetery, it is in fact a majestic, ancient peak.

The surroundings are filled with towering ancient trees that block out the sky and the sun; their branches and leaves are thick and massive. The charred black earth appears as if it were paved with blood, with wispy strands of blood-red mist swirling and rising. One can see tomb after tomb piled up, stretching endlessly up the mountain, stacked like a staircase.

None of the tombs contained any remains. Upon their death, members of the Luo clan disintegrated into a shower of dust. The tombs here were, in fact, merely symbolic mounds.

On the path to the mountaintop, Luo Xiangjun spotted her parents’ graves.

A flicker of sorrow crossed her beautiful eyes, but she quickly suppressed the emotion.

Her parents had chosen to become sacrifices—no, in fact, every tomb on this mountain, every sleeping spirit of the Luo clan, had done so willingly.

Their souls returned to the embrace of the Lord of Luo; until the very moment of sacrifice, they were at peace and honored.

She paid it no mind and did not grieve for them.

At the highest point of the cemetery stood a magnificent, square tombstone, meticulously restored to towering heights.

Not a single weed grew nearby; the spirit trees planted around it were lush and verdant, their ancient, sprawling branches resembling true dragons coiled here, blocking out the sky and casting a shadow that made the scene appear serene and solemn.

After the Grand Elder of the Luo Clan brought the Eternal Primordial God here, he respectfully withdrew without uttering a word to disturb them.

However, upon seeing Luo Xiangjun, a hint of surprise flashed in his eyes, though he did not take her down with them.

After Luo Xiangjun left the Luo Clan, she was not actually pursued by them. The Luo Clan does not force every clan member to serve as a sacrifice; they respect the choice of every individual.

Luo Xiangjun’s return to the Luo Clan this time came as quite a surprise to the Grand Elder.

After all, he had watched Luo Xiangjun grow up with his own eyes. Among the members of her generation in the Luo Clan, she possessed the highest natural talent and the purest, most potent bloodline—far surpassing even her parents.

"The tomb was completely empty—not even a symbolic grave..."

After the Elder of the Luo Clan left, the Eternal Primordial God shook his head slightly, his gaze pensive. “Based on my understanding of Luo, it’s impossible that he was beaten to death in a mob attack. The Eight Divisions of Heaven's Proxy keep to themselves and rarely interfere with one another; it’s also unlikely they would have acted against him collectively. The Heavenly Host’s objective is to reclaim the former foundations of Heaven's Proxy—they wouldn’t have sought Luo’s life.”

"Then how exactly did he sustain such severe injuries?"

Luo Xiangjun stood silently behind him, saying nothing.

She didn’t care whether Lord Luo lived or died; all she wanted now was for the Eternal Primordial God to spare her. As for the “Outline of Transcendence” he had once promised her, she no longer dared to hope for it.

"Luo is not dead."

"If he were to hide, I doubt I could find him. In terms of research and mastery of the Way of Time and Space, there is likely no one in all the worlds who can match him."

The Eternal Primordial God surveyed the graveyard thoughtfully, his gaze sweeping over every corner.

Regarding the entire Luo clan sacrificing themselves to revive Lord Luo, he neither confirmed nor denied it, nor did he offer his own opinion.

He himself was a primordial being, born with innate knowledge; he had known the meaning of his existence from the moment of his birth and would never go against his own will for the sake of any person or event.

Therefore, he could not possibly feel even the slightest pity or sympathy for these actions.

His sole focus now was on locating the Lord of Luo. In the Vast Expanse to come, it would be difficult for him to survive on his own strength, let alone maneuver within it to secure his own interests.

With Luo Zhu’s assistance, and drawing on their past friendship, he would have the confidence to extract benefits from the situation.

The Great Turmoil of the Vast Expanse—the murkier the waters, the more conducive they are to “fishing in troubled waters.” Those who previously dared only to hide and never show their faces will surely be unable to contain themselves; they would never let such a golden opportunity slip by.

The Eternal Primordial God is no exception.

“Luo once served the former Ruler of Heaven. He must understand that ruler’s style of conduct and would choose to hide early on. It’s likely he foresaw something long ago…”

Suddenly, the Eternal Primordial God realized something, his expression shifting between light and shadow.

He stared intently at the empty tomb before him, then, as if having made a decision, swept his massive hand through the air. The layers of restrictions vanished, the mountain rocks trembled, and a massive fissure appeared, revealing a bronze coffin beneath.

The coffin had long been opened; a blood-red glow swirled around it, and the aura of another dimension permeated the air, shrouding the area in a haze.

As she watched this unfold, Luo Xiangjun hadn’t even had time to process what was happening when the Eternal Primordial God swept her up in his sleeve and hurled her directly into the coffin before them.

Buzz!!!

A tremor rippled through the air, and a terrifying aura suddenly filled the space, only to fade into silence the next moment, leaving behind a fleeting ripple-like disturbance.

"It’s here, just as I thought..."

The Eternal Primordial God let out a cold smile, then strode forward and quickly vanished into the coffin.

In the next instant, the blurry scene before him cleared, revealing a valley filled with birdsong and floral fragrance, its scenic beauty enhanced by a lake reflecting the surrounding mountains.

In the distance, a stream murmured as it gathered into a jade-green lake. Further still, a thunderous roar could be heard—a silvery-white waterfall seemed to crash down from the ninth heaven, stirring up boundless waves. Steam billowed, shrouding the scene in mist.

Throughout the entire expanse of heaven and earth, not a trace of Daoist energy could be sensed; time and space seemed to have completely frozen.

Luo Xiangjun gazed at the scene in bewilderment, standing not far from the Eternal Primordial God.

She had no idea what was happening, but she understood that the Eternal Primordial God must have been using her as a test when he thrust her into that coffin with a single palm strike.

If a deadly restriction had been triggered inside, she would likely have perished instantly, blown to pieces in a cloud of blood.

A flash of coldness flickered in her beautiful eyes, but she suppressed it fiercely. Circumstances were beyond her control; she had no choice but to endure.

"Existing neither in the past, future, nor present; transforming oneself into the Dao and merging with this world—that is indeed a clever tactic."

“In this way, as heaven and earth decay, you remain undying; as all worlds wither, you endure. Even if the Vast Expanse collapses and the ages crumble, you stand apart from it all. Truly, it is only fitting of you, Luo…”

The Eternal Primordial God uttered a sigh of admiration, surveying the world before him with undisguised awe.

At first, he had not anticipated that within that coffin lay a portal to another world. Yet in that fleeting moment, recalling Luo’s past caution and his history of following the Lord of Heaven, the Eternal Primordial God was moved to test the waters.

The moment he pushed Luo Xiangjun toward that coffin, he had already confirmed that there was more to it than met the eye.

Sure enough, upon closer inspection, this realm contained no traces of Daoist laws or cosmic energy; it did not even exist within any known spacetime.

Past, future, or present—not a trace of it could be found.

Even those whose abilities reach the heavens and whose paths have reached the ultimate pinnacle could not hope to discern the true location of Luo.

This technique was even more ingenious than his feigned death to escape.

Who could have imagined that all along, the ancient ancestor of the Luo Clan had been hiding within his own tomb? The so-called most dangerous place was, in fact, the safest.

And as the Eternal Primordial God spoke, the heavens and earth rumbled, as if thunder were rolling.

Suddenly, in the previously clear, cloudless sky, vague ripples spread out, coalescing into a massive, closed-eyed face.

This face was immense, obscuring the entire sky—no, to be precise, the entire sky was this face.

Under the gaze of this face, Luo Xiangjun felt a terrifying, bloodline-suppressing pressure, and his entire body instinctively bowed down to kneel.

"Is that you?"

"Heng?"

Moments later, a terrifying, rumbling sound—as if the heavens and earth were resonating in unison—echoed throughout this world. Luo Xiangjun could no longer withstand the overwhelming pressure of that sound.

Blood and tears streamed down her lovely face as she coughed up fresh blood. She was pinned to the ground, unable to move, her clothes stained crimson with blood, looking utterly wretched.

"Hmm, a descendant of mine?"

The voice seemed to have noticed Luo Xiangjun’s presence, and the overwhelming pressure dissipated.

Luo Xiangjun finally felt a little relief, but fear and horror still lingered in her eyes. This was the aura of her distant ancestor—even a mere wisp of it was enough to annihilate her, a cultivator at the Dao Realm, countless times over.

And yet, such a figure had once willingly followed that Ruler of Heaven.

“It’s been a long time, old friend,” the Eternal Primordial God said with a faint smile, completely undisturbed by the overwhelming pressure.

“As I expected, only you could find this place.”

The void continued to rumble. In the next instant, white clouds coalesced, mountain ranges trembled, and all rivers and waterfalls reversed their flow, surging toward the heavens like blood, coalescing into a silver-haired man clad in a long silver robe, his features sharp and stern.

A vertical silver fissure ran down the center of his brow, and his eyes—also silver—shone with a radiant divine light. He was none other than the Luo Clan’s distant ancestor, the Luo Lord, a primordial being equal to the Eternal Primordial God.

“You’ve come to find me—it seems you’ve run into trouble?”

Lord Luo landed before the Eternal Primordial God, gave him a cursory glance, and spoke in a flat tone.

“Nothing escapes your notice. Are you aware of the current situation in the Vast Expanse?” The Eternal Primordial God asked with a smile, cutting to the chase, his tone unable to hide his emotion and joy.

In the Vast Expanse, nothing brought him greater joy than reuniting with an old friend.

After the end of the Primordial Era, the Vast Expanse was born and took shape. For Primordial beings like them, some died, some fled, some hid, and some were captured; only a handful survived.

His circumstances, however, were entirely different from those of Luo Zhu. He had hidden in various places, severed his Primordial roots, and then reshaped his body into a Post-Heaven form, all out of fear that his Primordial origins might be discovered.

While other beings yearned to defy the Primordial and forge flawless Primordial foundations, beings like them took the opposite path.

At first, Lord Luo had made a choice similar to his own, but later emerged into the world and followed that Ruler of Heaven.

At that time, he chose to remain hidden and lie low, evading the watchful gaze of the True Realm above the Vast Expanse. Even though those three exalted beings had fallen into slumber, their boundless and unfathomable might still manifested as the Eyes of the True Ancestor, acting as the proxy for the Heavenly Dao, surveying all worlds, and eradicating all aberrations.

After the Primordial Era ended, a new Heavenly Dao emerged and began purging those deemed different. Beings like them, born in the Primordial Era, were naturally regarded as “outsiders” by the new Heavenly Dao.

Of course, they did not know whether the new Dao that emerged after the Primordial Era was the manifestation of the will of those three beings, or a consciousness born of the heavens and earth themselves.

During the Primordial Era, heaven and earth had not yet come into being, and the concept of the Way of Heaven did not exist. All sentient beings of every race acted according to their instincts and the meaning they perceived.

If anything existed at that time, it would have been the so-called Great Dao—a unified path from which all things evolved.

The concept of the Heavenly Dao actually did not emerge until the Post-Primordial Era…

“The current Vast Expanse and uncertain situation?”

Lord Luo furrowed his brow slightly, then closed his eyes briefly, as if perceiving and discerning something.

A moment later, he opened his eyes and nodded, indicating he understood, though the expression on his face had grown grave.

"Are you planning to step in?" He looked at the Eternal Primordial God.

The Eternal Primordial God nodded, stating matter-of-factly, “Of course. I have lain in wait for so long—is it not precisely to one day restore the glory of the Primordial Era? That was our era; it must not be buried and severed by the passage of time…”

Lord Luo looked at his old friend and sighed. Though they hadn’t seen each other in countless ages, the other hadn’t changed.

“The Primordial Era has long since ended. No matter what, even if we were to return to the Primordial Age and recreate the Chaos, we could never go back to that time.” His tone was heavy. “As long as the Realm of Origin exists, the Primordial Era can never reappear in the Vast Expanse.”

"The Primordial Era you wish to return to is merely a false Primordial Era..."

The Primordial Era is called the Primordial Era because, at that time, there was no concept of “Heaven.” All living beings acted according to their instincts, free from constraints and unbound by rules.

In truth, according to the grand trend unfolding in the depths of the cosmos, “Heaven” would eventually emerge in later ages. Both the Primordial Age and the Post-Primordial Age are simply manifestations of this grand trend conforming to the Great Dao; there is no real difference between them.

However, within that grand trend, the emergence of the Three Ancestors shattered the Great Dao and destroyed the natural course of events; their will became what is known as "Heaven."

For these beings, the current Post-Heaven Era should, in truth, be called the “Era of the False Heaven.”
