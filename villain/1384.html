A swarm of dark, shadowy creatures surged from the other side of the embankment, as if they could never be completely vanquished.

The sky was pounded by pitch-black mist, wave after wave, a sight so suffocating and despairing it took one’s breath away.

Lan Xin and her fellow disciples and masters had rushed here to hold the front lines, fighting desperately without ever retreating a single step.

But as time went on, she watched as one by one, her fellow disciples and masters fell, their blood staining the sky as they were swallowed by the dark creatures, and then gradually vanished from sight.

The reinforcements they had been waiting for never arrived.

Though Lan Xin did not fear death, her heart was now filled with sorrow and bitterness; it seemed they had truly been abandoned by the Righteous Unity Alliance.

They had been promised that if they held out for seven days, reinforcements from the Righteous Unity Alliance would arrive.

Yet now, half a month had passed, and while news of breached defenses and fallen strongholds spread everywhere, there was still no sign of the Righteous Unity Alliance’s reinforcements.

"Senior Sister, don’t worry. We will survive." Gu Xian’er stood with her sword raised, her clear, radiant face now solemn—a stark contrast to her usual lively disposition.

Waves of sword energy gathered behind her like the Yangtze River. As she swung her sword, the entire sky roared, as if billions of long swords were howling through the air, crisscrossing the cosmos.

Hordes of dark creatures fell before her, collapsing into black mist.

The world was filled with the intent of the sword. At this moment, she resembled a true female sword immortal—fierce and powerful. With a light cry, her sword energy shot forth like a rainbow, piercing the void. It was like lightning and thunder, like the fleeting shadow of a swan, boundless and vast, swallowing up rivers, the sun, and the moon.

This was the first time she had displayed her true, overwhelming power before outsiders; even against ordinary Dao Realm beings, she seemed capable of holding her own.

Although Lan Xin had seen this before, she couldn’t help but feel a deep sense of awe and admiration.

“If I’d known, I wouldn’t have brought you along in the first place. With your talent, you deserve a broader horizon—not to stay here, where we’re desperately guarding the dam, drinking wine and spilling our blood.” She sighed softly, a wry smile on her lips.

As her senior sister, it should have been her duty to protect Gu Xian’er.

But who would have thought that in this great battle, it was Gu Xian’er who repeatedly stepped in to save her—and the rest of their fellow disciples.

Gu Xian’er’s strength was far greater than any of them had imagined.

In ordinary times, she simply kept a low profile and concealed her true abilities, never revealing them to others.

"Senior Sister, don’t say that. The Righteous Unity Alliance may have already decided to abandon us. Why should we 193 still hold our ground here? I sense a terrifying, overwhelming aura approaching from beyond the distant borders. I fear that a major figure among the dark beings is about to arrive."

"By then, even the elders won’t be able to save themselves."

Gu Xian’er approached Lan Xin, her young face heavy with worry as she spoke.

"Why would the Righteous Unity Alliance abandon us? Our Master was killed by them..." The sorrow and despair on Lan Xin’s face deepened.

Gu Xian’er shook her head without speaking; from the very beginning, she had never believed that the Righteous Unity Alliance was truly as righteous and noble as the rumors claimed.

Seeing the Righteous Unity Alliance act this way now came as no surprise.

"Holding our ground here is ultimately not a wise move. If I were capable, I would protect everyone myself, but alas, even I can’t save myself…"

Gu Xian’er couldn’t help but let out a soft sigh.

It wasn’t that she was cold-blooded and indifferent to the lives of her fellow disciples.

It was simply that, in a battle of this magnitude, her cultivation level was utterly incapable of making any decisive difference.

Had this been the Daochang True Realm, she might have had the power to turn the tide.

But the vast expanse of the Cangmang Realm was, after all, not the Daochang True Realm; here, even those at the Dao Realm risked perishing at any moment.

"Without reaching the Dao Realm, one is nothing but an ant."

At that moment, her resolve to follow the Dao grew even stronger, and her desire to break through to the Dao Realm was more urgent than ever.

Boom!

Suddenly, boundless black energy surged forth, like a colossal wave driven by a giant—raging and overwhelming, shattering the stars.

In the distant cosmos, star systems trembled, and meteorites streaked through the void, as if a vast and boundless river had emerged. A figure clad in a battle armor of Nether Iron materialized there.

He gazed coldly, his eyes as icy and distant as two azure moons suspended beyond the world. His true form was immense, as if the entire cosmos could not contain him.

As this being appeared, all the dark creatures let out earth-shaking war cries and prostrated themselves in that direction, filled with boundless fervor.

Every cultivator and living being upon the embankment felt as though the depths of winter had descended upon them; their bodies grew cold, their souls seemed on the verge of freezing, and they trembled with utter terror.

As that terrifying being’s gaze swept across the realm, the heavens and star domains trembled in unison. One after another, the laws of the Dao—thick as chains—appeared at his feet.

With each step he took, the Way resounded in unison, as if a Dark Sovereign were surveying and overlooking the myriad heavens.

"So powerful..."

Gu Xian’er gasped, feeling as though she could barely breathe.

That realm was still very far away—at least a universe away—yet the oppressive aura of that being had already permeated this side, as if the sky itself were about to collapse and crush everyone beneath it.

"My premonition has come true. The dark beings are about to launch a fierce assault."

Lan Xin’s face turned pale as she shook her head. Her flawless, delicate features were etched with sorrow and anguish. “Master, your disciple is coming to be with you.”

In the distant reaches of the cosmos, black mist engulfed the heavens, and terrifying phenomena unfolded as one dark spirit after another emerged.

Each exuded an aura of unimaginable terror, like deities that had existed since ancient times. They seemed to be reciting some ancient ritual text, and the sounds of primordial sacrifices and prayers echoed throughout the heavens and earth.

A vague, blood-red altar appeared in that shattered, distant corner of the cosmos.

Bloodlight shot skyward—like a true dragon drinking water, or billowing smoke—rising from every star system and converging upon that indistinct altar.

These were the souls and cultivators who had perished in this great war; the essence of their blood, diffusing throughout the heavens and earth after their deaths, had become the purest form of energy.

"The Great Sacrifice begins..."

In the haze, everyone heard a ruthless, indifferent voice—like the murmur of an ancient demon—that sent shivers down their spines and chilled their very souls.

Every living being on this side of the dam was now utterly horrified, feeling as though their souls and vital energy were about to fly away, to leave their bodies and drift toward that altar.

Many found their minds growing hazy and unfocused, their bodies no longer under their control, compelled to leave the dam and walk toward it like mindless zombies.

"Run!"

A sudden, commanding shout rang out, carrying earth-shaking fury.

A middle-aged man flew in on a sword, his clear, aloof face now filled with fury.

Lan Xin and the others, who had been momentarily dazed, suddenly snapped back to their senses. Overwhelmed by terror, they nearly dropped their weapons and fled the embankment.

"Elder Zhou…"

Many disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect turned to look at the middle-aged man who had rushed over, their excitement palpable.

He was none other than a renowned elder of the Crescent Moon Sect, whose mastery of the sword was peerless.

"The Righteous Unity Alliance has abandoned us, intending to make peace with the Dark Beings. They have willingly ceded their territory, leaving us to become sacrificial fodder for the Dark Beings."

Elder Zhou’s face was grim, his voice trembling with profound grief.

He looked at all the cultivators and beings still holding their ground here, the scene of utter devastation, and trembled with rage.

These were all disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect and the surrounding civilizations—each a living, breathing soul.

They had been ordered to hold this position to the death, fighting fiercely and shedding blood without retreating an inch, yet now they faced abandonment, betrayal, and a stab in the back from the Righteous Unity Alliance.

"What?"

Everyone who had thought they were receiving reinforcements now stood frozen in place, stiff as statues, unable to believe their ears.

Even Gu Xian’er was no exception; her clear eyes widened in disbelief at the sheer absurdity of it all.

Has the Righteous Unity Alliance truly abandoned everyone? Do they intend to make peace with the dark spirits? To cede territory and lives?

For them, this was simply too cruel.

Many cultivators and spirits who still clung to a sliver of hope stood frozen in place; their last shred of hope shattered, and they nearly collapsed to the ground.

"It's over. Completely over..."

"Hahahaha, this is the Righteous Unity Alliance we fought so hard to defend? What a fine example of justice." Many let out bitter laughter, stumbling about, dazed and disoriented, their eyes vacant.

Others clung to bloodstained garments and shattered armor in the corners, weeping silently.

Their fellow disciples and masters, in a desperate bid to buy time so they might survive, had charged forward like moths to a flame, taking the dark creatures down with them.

Lan Xin’s face was pale, and her body trembled uncontrollably.

Though she had suspected as much, she still couldn’t fathom that the Righteous Unity Alliance could be so cold-hearted and ruthless—that they would truly abandon everyone?

"Everyone, leave now. There’s no need to hold out here any longer."

“The Righteous Unity Alliance has likely already been infiltrated by the dark spirits, who are preparing to initiate the Great Ritual.”

Elder Zhou’s voice was filled with grief, his face equally etched with anger and a deep sense of helplessness.

Upon hearing this news, he too froze in shock, unable to believe his own ears.

The various factions and lineages had been ordered to hold their ground at the dams, risking their lives to fend off the dark spirits’ advance.

Yet the high-ranking members of the Righteous Unity Alliance behind them had turned around to seek peace with the dark spirits, intending to abandon them and cede their territories.

How utterly heartbreaking.

Despair and rage spread across the embankment; everyone had lost their spirit and will to fight. Even if they fled now, where could they possibly go?

Once the dam is breached, how could the lineage and traditions behind them possibly survive? What of their kin, friends, beloved partners, and fellow disciples?

If they retreated even a single step, who would protect those left behind?

"Hurry back to the Crescent Moon Sect. The Sect Master is currently discussing the 'Passing of the Flame' plan with the elders. If our sect’s legacy is to endure, we must preserve the flame. You are all the future hope of the sect, with bright futures ahead of you—do not perish here."

Elder Zhou looked at Gu Xian’er, Lan Xin, and the others, his voice heavy as he spoke.

He had a strong impression of Gu Xian’er. Although this young girl was believed to be tainted by ill omen, her extraordinary talent in the Way of the Sword had even stirred a sense of admiration for her talent within him.

Now that the sect had suddenly faced this calamity, it could only be the will of heaven—a trial destined by fate.

He did not want such a promising sword cultivator to perish here.

She ought to shine brightly in a wider world, soaring with outstretched wings to compose her own glorious chapter.

As for the remaining disciples of Crescent Moon Sect, the fact that they have managed to hold on this long under such dire circumstances speaks volumes about their excellence.

"The Black Tide is coming again."

"You must leave at once. The future of the Crescent Moon Sect rests on your shoulders."

Elder Zhou turned away, saying no more. Leaning on his sword, he gazed coldly at the distant cosmos and slashed through the air—a rift in space-time appeared.

At the same time, he took a step forward. A brilliant sword light swept out, instantly clearing away the surging dark entities in the distance, creating a void.

The rift in space-time continued to expand, forming a gateway. With a flick of his wide sleeves, he instantly sent numerous cultivators and beings through it.

"Elder Zhou…"

The figures of Gu Xian’er, Lan Xin, and the others quickly vanished into the portal.

Watching Elder Zhou’s figure grow increasingly blurry, their hearts were filled with sorrow and grief, and their noses stung with emotion.

Elder Zhou was usually taciturn and solemn. Though he had long admired and pursued Lan Xin’s master, he had never received a response.

Despite this, he never uttered a word, instead consistently safeguarding Elder Chen Yi through his actions.

Even in this dire situation, after learning of Elder Chen Yi’s demise at the hands of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, he remained true to his original vow, continuing to protect Chen Yi’s disciples and the members of the Crescent Moon Sect.

On the other side of the dam, the blood-red altar grew ever more solid and towering, as if it were shrouding that entire universe.

Black mist filled the sky, boundless and far more turbulent than before.

Elder Zhou’s expression was impassive as he strode through the air, taking several steps forward, his Dao Sword—cultivated for countless years—now drawn.

With a soft command, the sword left its scabbard. Its song echoed through the nine heavens, and boundless sword energy—numbering in the billions—filled the cosmic star fields, vast and limitless.

"The heavens are vast and boundless; my Dao grows ever more prosperous."

A crisp, furious shout echoed throughout the heavens and the cosmos. He strode forward, as unstoppable as the sword in his hand, charging into the boundless black mist, determined to tear it apart.

Within the thick, impenetrable black mist, Wu Jue, clad in his Nether Iron battle armor, watched the scene unfold with utter indifference.

At his side, a young girl with her hair tied in a maid’s bun held up an oil-paper umbrella as green as a lotus leaf, shielding them from the boundless black mist pouring down from the heavens.

"The human path is faint and distant, the immortal path is vast and boundless, but the ghostly path is joyful! At the gateway of life, the immortal path values life, while the ghostly path values the end…

Beneath the oil-paper umbrella, a woman of delicate grace—as if hailing from the water towns of Jiangnan—her beautiful eyes slightly curved, her lips as vibrant as flower petals parted gently as she murmured softly.

The news that the Righteous Unity Alliance had chosen to abandon its border defenses and seek peace with the dark spirits quickly shook every corner of the vast land, causing boundless turmoil and upheaval.

No one could have anticipated that the Righteous Unity Alliance would take such a step.

Seeking peace at a time like this, abandoning all the border defenses—wasn’t that tantamount to condemning those civilizations to ruin and sending everyone to their deaths?

How was this any different from the Heaven Slaying Alliance? What happened to the unity and mutual aid we agreed upon? Why

, why go back on their word?

The Righteous Unity Alliance’s actions naturally caused immense upheaval and commotion, while also inviting countless backlashes.

With their prestige and reputation in freefall, they faced a torrent of abuse and condemnation.

Yet even under these circumstances, the Righteous Unity Alliance paid no heed, gradually contracting its territorial reach and relinquishing non-essential territories.

At the same time, powerful figures emerged to negotiate with the high-ranking members of the Dark Beings, seeking to forge a peace treaty—for which they were willing to agree to the Dark Beings’ numerous demands.
