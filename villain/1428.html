Song Youwei bowed her head and reported softly, “The Empress has been plagued by a heart demon. Her cultivation has stagnated for many years, with no progress made. Later, when she attempted to forcibly break through to the Immortal King Realm, she failed. Her Dao Source shattered, and a lifetime of cultivation vanished like water. Overnight, her black hair turned completely white. Even with the various rare treasures and natural treasures gathered by the tribes of the Demon Realm, there was nothing that could save her.”

At this point, she glanced at Gu Changge, who remained silent, and continued softly, “Empress Youyue originally intended to travel to the Divine Realm to seek medicine. She believed that, given the past kindness between you, the Alliance Leader, and the Empress, the Divine Consorts of the Divine Realm would surely be generous enough to bestow divine medicine. However, the Empress refused…”

Empress Youyue was Empress Xiyao’s biological mother. Back in the Demon Realm, Gu Changge had spared her life out of consideration for her relationship with Empress Xiyao.

Now, she holds sway over the Demon Realm.

Gu Changge remained silent. He knew exactly why Empress Xiyao had refused.

In her prime, she had been so arrogant and proud. In just six thousand years, she had risen to become the supreme ruler of her generation, looking down upon countless prodigies of her peers, and becoming an empress whom countless celestial prodigies could only look up to in admiration—an unattainable figure.

How could she possibly humble herself to go beg for help? Especially from those who were once her juniors.

Moreover, the most crucial point was that Yin Mei and the others were Gu Changge’s legally wedded wives.

As she grew old and her beauty faded, to face Yin Mei and the others, begging them for medicine—what kind of torment would that be? Perhaps even more agonizing than death itself.

Seeing that Gu Changge remained silent, Song Youwei did not press the matter further. Instead, she retrieved a scroll from her Sumeru Space, carefully bound with a red silk ribbon.

"We found this while sorting through the Empress’s belongings after her passing. She had always cherished it dearly..."

“I originally intended to follow the Empress’s final wishes and burn it along with the rest, but I took the liberty of keeping it, thinking that perhaps one day I could present it to you, the Alliance Leader.”

As she spoke, she held the scroll gently in both hands and presented it to Gu Changge.

Gu Changge’s gaze fell upon it. In truth, he didn’t need to unroll it to guess what was inside, but he reached out to take it anyway, gently untying the silk ribbon.

It was a scroll painted on the finest Xuan paper, depicting a man dressed in white.

He stood with his hands behind his back, his figure tall and slender, perched atop a sea of clouds. As the mountain breeze blew, his robes billowed and his black hair fluttered in the wind. He was strikingly handsome and elegant, transcending the mundane. As he turned his head, he seemed to be saying something to those below, a faint smile playing at the corners of his mouth.

This was the scene from when he first left the Demon Realm.

Gu Changge was somewhat surprised that Xiyao had actually captured this scene in her painting, but upon closer reflection, it didn’t seem so surprising after all.

In an instant, his thoughts returned to that time.

A multitude of scenes flashed before his eyes.

“Is someone like you worthy of my trust?” By the mist-shrouded pond, Empress Xiyao leaned against him and asked.

Gu Changge smiled and replied, “Why not?”

“But the more I get to know you, the more afraid and uneasy I become…”

“Being alone is so exhausting. I just want to find someone I can rely on to feel at peace. Can I lean on you for a moment?”

"Whenever you want to lean on me, I’ll always be here."

Many years have passed, yet that scene from long ago still seems to linger before his eyes.

Gu Changge remained silent, saying nothing.

He didn’t know what he was feeling at that moment; it seemed he was slightly uneasy, with a sense of heaviness in his heart.

Such emotions were truly rare for him.

She had clearly been nothing more than a pawn he’d used back then—merely a one-night stand—so why couldn’t she let it go?

She was, after all, the Empress of the Demon Realm, ruling over all demonic beings—how domineering and proud she was.

He had merely struck a simple deal with her. Did she really think he’d developed genuine feelings for her?

"She really is pitifully foolish. She’s clearly just a pawn that’s outlived its usefulness—why is she pretending to be so deeply in love…?"

Gu Changge suddenly shook his head, as if sighing lightly, or perhaps explaining it to himself.

Song Youwei froze. Before she could process what was happening, she sensed a vast, boundless, and overwhelming force surging through the space and time before her. Some force and awareness within the cosmos seemed to be shifting.

Her vision grew blurry, and as her sight faded, she seemed to glimpse a shimmering river appearing before Gu Changge. He took a step and walked into it.

The river of time surged, rippling outward, and Gu Changge’s figure appeared in the Demon Realm of five hundred years ago.

In the Demon Realm’s imperial palace, the grand halls were filled with solemnity and oppression, accompanied by an atmosphere of grief.

Empress Youyue stood silently before the palace doors, not stepping inside. Numerous ministers and court ladies of the Demon Realm also waited outside, their hearts heavy.

Deep within the palace, on a bed of jade and crystal, white curtains fluttered like ethereal mist—hazy and dreamlike. A slender figure with snow-white hair lay quietly upon the bed.

Her eyes were closed. Her face bore wrinkles and the marks of time, no longer possessing the peerless beauty of her youth. Yet, in her eyes and features, one could still glimpse the breathtaking beauty she once possessed.

“Youwei, you needn’t waste your magical energy maintaining my appearance anymore. It’s no longer necessary.” Her slightly parched lips moved gently, her voice soft and frail—it was hard to reconcile the woman before her with the once-mighty Empress Xiyao.

Song Youwei said nothing. Sitting by the bed, she continued to hold the Empress’s hand, ignoring her own pallid complexion as she poured her magical energy into her.

“Your Majesty was born with such beauty; even as you depart, you should do so looking your best,” she whispered.

“You say I’m beautiful, yet why has he never once looked back at me…” Empress Xiyao murmured.

Song Youwei remained silent.

Empress Xiyao gave what seemed like a self-deprecating smile. “I’m going to die. This feeling is truly strange—somewhat light, yet somewhat calm, as if I were waiting to fall asleep.”

Song Youwei felt a lump in her throat. Before her eyes, a vision flickered of that peerlessly radiant, imposing, and ruthless empress—seated majestically in the hall, her single command commanding the four seas, her ministers trembling in fear, none daring to speak.

But now, she was as frail as an elderly person in the twilight of her life.

She was clearly still in the prime of her life, with a bright future ahead of her—she could have become a legendary Immortal King and attained even greater heights.

Even though she knew full well that it was a demon of the mind, that it was merely an illusion, not the real him—why did she still choose to wallow in it, allowing her spiritual resolve to shatter…

“Tell me,” Empress Xiyao asked suddenly, “if he knew I was dying, would he come to see me just once?”

Song Youwei didn’t know how to answer.

Empress Xiyao shook her head gently. “Never mind. My beauty is gone; I’m so old and ugly now. I don’t want him to see me like this. It’s better this way. If one day he happens to think of me, he’ll remember how I once looked—not this white-haired version of myself…”

Song Youwei’s nose grew even more sore.

In a daze, she seemed to be transported back to that secluded courtyard many years ago. It was late autumn, with leaves falling and all things withering. Freshly fallen hibiscus petals carpeted the courtyard. Her Majesty the Empress walked over, taking her hand. “Youwei, from now on, you will stay by my side.”

"If there is a next life, I want to be a carefree fish, not someone’s pawn, free and unfettered, drifting with the current."

Empress Xiyao murmured softly, and those words seemed to drain her of all her strength and vitality.

Suddenly, as if sensing something—or perhaps in a final burst of clarity—her eyes snapped open. She forced her head to turn to the side and looked over, then froze in place.
