Chen Yi stared blankly at the white jade vial clenched in her hand. After a long while, she let out a quiet sigh and snapped back to reality.

The ruthless cold poison that had plagued her for so long at the Crescent Moon Sect had left everyone helpless, convinced that her path forward was cut off.

It was precisely for this reason that she had been selected as a member of the Righteous Unity Alliance’s envoy team.

But who could have imagined that, thanks to that small vial of elixir, the vicious cold poison within her body had actually been eradicated and cured? She felt not the slightest abnormality, and it had never flared up again.

It all felt like a dream, leaving Chen Yi utterly speechless.

The peril of this journey was nothing short of a death-defying ordeal; she had already resigned herself to certain death, holding no hope of survival.

Yet who could have imagined that the cold poison within her would be cured as a result?

"I still don’t even know his name..."

Chen Yi and her companions set out once more, transforming into streaks of divine light as they headed toward the territory of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Yellow sand stretched endlessly, the Gobi desolate and boundless, with no end in sight.

The world was bathed in a desolate, dusky glow; only the majestic, towering city walls in the distance revealed their faint outlines amidst the swirling yellow sands.

It resembled a true dragon, sprawling and winding, overlooking the vast expanse of heaven and earth and the myriad living beings.

Chen Yi and his companions, all cultivators at the Dao Realm, reached the outer edge of the city walls almost instantly. The towering walls stood nearly level with the heavens, while the remains of stars drifted through the cosmos.

Massive celestial bodies orbited the wall, each engraved with countless runes that blazed with a light so intense it outshone the sun.

The ancient, desolate city wall was a uniform bluish-gray, covered with scars from swords, spears, and halberds, bearing witness to the great battles that had once taken place here.

The cultivators on the walls had long since noticed Chen Yi and his companions. Flashes of brilliant runes ignited, converging to form terrifying beams of energy, ready to strike at any moment.

Feeling that terrifying aura, Chen Yi and his companions couldn’t help but show a slight change in their expressions.

As the dazzling divine light gathered, poised to strike, one of them soared into the sky and hurriedly clasped his hands, saying, “We mean no harm.”

“These are turbulent times. May I ask where you all come from, and why you have entered the territory of the Heaven Slaying Alliance?”

A man who appeared to be a commander stood atop the city walls, gazing coldly at Chen Yi and his companions as he shouted the question.

Although the Heaven Slaying Alliance had not suffered an attack from the dark beings, they had maintained vigilance throughout the region during this period, preventing anyone with suspicious behavior from entering.

“We come from the Righteous Unity Alliance, acting on the orders of the various sect leaders. We have brought official documents to negotiate with the Heaven Slaying Alliance and discuss how we might jointly resist the impending catastrophe of the Dark Plague.”

The cultivator who had just spoken took a document woven from silk from his bosom and deliberately unfurled it.

A blinding light emerged, coalescing into rows of text that materialized in the void, shimmering with a radiant glow. They resonated with the essence of the Dao, exuding an aura of extraordinary mysticism.

"An envoy from the Righteous Unity Alliance?"

The commander on the city wall’s gaze held a hint of surprise. With a sweeping gesture of his hand, the scroll flew into his grasp. He quickly scanned its contents, then nodded slightly and ordered the gate to be opened to let them pass.

He had long heard rumors that the Righteous Unity Alliance was sending an envoy.

He just hadn’t expected them to arrive so quickly.

Chen Yi and his companions breathed a deep sigh of relief. After expressing their gratitude, they proceeded straight into the city.

Although their strength would have allowed them to force their way in, they dared not act recklessly on the territory of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

To them, every soldier and cultivator stationed on the city walls looked like a fearsome demon radiating murderous intent—all formidable figures best left unprovoked.

Meanwhile, just as Chen Yi and his companions finally set foot on the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s territory and headed toward their headquarters,

Gu Changge had already returned to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, accompanied by Mu Fuguang, Daoist Master Changming, the Compassionate Zen Buddha, and Tong Xian.

During his absence from the Heaven Slaying Alliance, he had entrusted the management of various affairs to the White Bone Ancestral King, Turbid Wind, and others.

As for Ling Yuling, he did not yet fully trust her.

Therefore, he had her and Xi Yin—who was impersonating the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan—traveling across the land to recruit followers and expand the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s influence.

Overall, the Heaven Slaying Alliance remained in a state of thriving, "peaceful coexistence" and stability, unaffected by the dark calamities descending upon the outside world, nor did its expansion trend falter in the slightest due to the emergence of the Righteous Unity Alliance.

Apart from a handful of people, almost no one knew that Gu Changge had left the Heaven Slaying Alliance during this time.

He had already sent Yao Yao away from his side to gain experience on her own, while Mu Yan, with nothing better to do, had been traveling around with Ling Yuling.

When Gu Changge returned to the valley filled with peach blossoms, he stood there, momentarily lost in thought, gazing at the vibrant petals scattered beneath the trees, untouched by a single hand.

The valley had been deserted for some time; with no one to tend to the fallen blossoms, it felt quiet and serene.

"Next, once the envoys from the Righteous Unity Alliance arrive, I’ll have a legitimate opportunity to take action."

Gu Changge brewed himself a pot of tea, leaned against a peach tree, and closed his eyes slightly, feigning sleep while mulling over his next moves and organizing his plans.

This time, upon leaving the Heaven Slaying Alliance, aside from “recruiting” Mu Fuguang and the others, he had actually done something else.

He had personally traversed nearly every corner of the territory surrounding the Heaven Slaying Alliance, pinpointing the locations of several deeply hidden “big fish.” However, since the time to close in had not yet come, he had refrained from stirring up trouble prematurely.

The appearance of the Righteous Unity Alliance played right into his hands, but to uphold the so-called greater good, he would have to be a bit more careful and choose the right moment to strike against the Righteous Unity Alliance.

The arrival of the Righteous Unity Alliance’s envoy delegation now provided him with that very opportunity.

It was also after instructing Chen Yi and the others that he glimpsed a fragment of the future in them, which prompted Gu Changge to act on a sudden impulse and devise the subsequent plan.

Over the next few days, Gu Changge issued an order for Ling Yuling and the others to return. He intended to have Mu Fuguang and the others stay by Ling Yuling’s side for a while.

Upon returning to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, he did not restrict Mu Fuguang and the others’ freedom, nor did he assign them any specific tasks.

Mu Fuguang, Tong Xian, and the Compassionate Zen Buddha possessed strength far surpassing that of ordinary Ancestral Dao Realm beings; trivial matters did not require their intervention, and Ling Yuling’s entourage currently lacked figures of that caliber.

Though she possessed extraordinary strength, relying solely on her own power—even with the prestige of her ancestors who had once fought against the Black Calamity—would be insufficient to intimidate the other civilizations of the True Realm.

It was time for the presence of the Heaven Slaying Corps to resound and strike fear into the entire Vast Expanse.

A few days later, Ling Yuling returned to the Heaven Slaying Alliance as promised. She also brought back some news that she considered quite important and beneficial for the future development of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

"Some of the surrounding Civilizations harbor reservations toward the Righteous Unity Alliance and are reluctant to join, fearing they might incur significant karmic consequences."

"During my travels to a True Realm beyond the boundaries of time and space, I encountered the descendant of an old friend. He informed me that behind the Righteous Unity Alliance lies a vast, shadowy presence—indistinct, vague, and terrifying, resembling a colossal vortex."

"He has inherited his late friend’s legacy, is well-versed in celestial phenomena and the Eight Trigrams, and is skilled at predicting the fates of the void—his methods are unfathomable."

Ling Yuling, her white robes flowing gracefully, appeared ethereal and cool, her skin as white as fresh snow. She walked toward him, carrying a faint, delicate fragrance.

Under the peach tree, Gu Changge sat slightly slouched to one side, gesturing for her to come sit beside him.

Ling Yuling hesitated briefly, but eventually walked over and sat down beside him. Petals of peach blossoms drifted down, floating gently in the air, carrying their fragrance.

“You’ve worked hard. Under your leadership, the Heaven Slaying Alliance has flourished more and more, saving me a great deal of trouble.”

Gu Changge plucked a petal and pinned it to her ear, smiling as he said, “Behind the Righteous Unity Alliance, there is indeed a force supporting and infiltrating it, seeking to gradually spread across the entire Cangmang. That person’s ambition is vast, and his audacity is great, but he lacks the strength to back it up. Sooner or later, he will face the consequences of his actions.”

“As for those civilizations of the True Realm you mentioned, they aren’t opposed to the Righteous Unity Alliance. It’s just that they’re quite distant from it, making it difficult for them to receive its protection, and they’re unwilling to relocate their entire tribes there.”

Ling Yuling’s soft, fair, and translucent earlobes flushed slightly.

"It seems you’ve known all this along. I really have nothing to worry about."

She paused for a moment, then said softly, “If that’s the case, you must have had a plan all along.” Gu Changge smiled. “I do indeed have a plan. Would you like to know what it is?”

Ling Yuling hesitated for a moment, then shook her head. “If you want to tell me, you’ll tell me naturally.”

She didn’t press further; sometimes, knowing more isn’t necessarily better.

Especially since Gu Changge was so inscrutable; though he appeared gentle and flawless at that moment, no one could guess or fathom his true intentions.

“I’ve recruited some powerful individuals this time. I’ll have them work under you later. They’re all very clever; they should save you a lot of trouble.”

Gu Changge rose from beneath the peach tree, took Ling Yuling with him, and left the area, heading for the main hall of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Mu Fuguang, Beici Chanfo, Tong Xian, and the others, who had already received their orders, were all waiting there.

"Greetings, Alliance Leader."

Upon seeing Gu Changge arrive, they all bowed in unison, showing great respect.

Ling Yuling was taken aback. She surveyed this group of powerful figures one by one, her clear eyes betraying her astonishment.

Several of them even gave her a faint sense of danger—one that could threaten her—which spoke volumes about their power.

The Heaven Slaying Alliance currently lacked figures of this caliber.

"From now on, you will serve by her side. I expect things in Cangmang will become much livelier," Gu Changge said.

Mu Fuguang’s beautiful eyes fluttered as she looked at Ling Yuling and said, “This immortal maiden must be the renowned founder of the Imperial Immortal Palace, right? You truly are peerlessly graceful and exceptionally radiant.”

Although they lived in seclusion, detached from the mundane world, they were well-informed about events occurring outside, especially the history of the Yuan Hope Civilization.

The founder of the Imperial Immortal Palace could be called the pinnacle of the Yuan Hope Civilization.

Setting aside those ancient figures and living fossils who hide themselves so deeply that they leave no trace, it would not be an exaggeration to call her the true number one master.

After her initial surprise, Ling Yuling also recognized the origins of Mu Fuguang and the others.

Those within the Restricted Zone rarely become entangled in the karma of the outside world; for a Dao Realm cultivator of such profound cultivation, it is easy to discern their origins.

“I never expected that you fellow Daoists would be invited out of the Forbidden Zone by the Alliance Leader.”

“With your assistance, the day we achieve the great cause of the Heaven Slaying Alliance will draw even nearer.” She smiled gently, her expression warm and gentle, her smile captivating.

Mu Fuguang and the others all looked a bit stiff, their expressions becoming somewhat awkward.

How could they fail to detect the mocking undertone in Ling Yuling’s words?

If not for Gu Changge’s coercion, would they have ever emerged from seclusion, become entangled in the karma of this mortal world, and brought such trouble upon themselves?

Ling Yuling knew this full well, yet she spoke as she did—it seemed she was not quite as detached from worldly affairs and unconcerned with human affairs as she appeared on the surface.

Subsequently, Mu Fuguang and the others briefly explained their origins to Ling Yuling. The forbidden zones behind them were each notorious for their ferocity, their reputations echoing across the vast expanse, representing endless bloodshed and chaos.

Even a Dao Realm being, upon hearing of them, would turn pale with terror and tremble, their eyes filled with dread.

This power was utterly terrifying; even the mightiest True Realm cultivators would tremble with dread in its presence. Yet now, they remained here obediently, serving the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

In front of Gu Changge, they were so docile that they bore no resemblance to the rulers of the Forbidden Zones—those beings whose very names struck terror into the hearts of all worlds.

Thinking of this, Ling Yuling could not help but feel unsettled.

Every time she thought she had a faint grasp of Gu Changge’s true power, that she had touched upon the depths of his capabilities, she would soon discover that it was all an illusion.

He truly seemed… omnipotent, invincible in the entire world.

Half a month later, the Righteous Unity Alliance’s envoy delegation finally arrived safely at Jiangcheng, the headquarters of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Chen Yi and his companions traveled through countless cosmic realms and territories, witnessing the sights of the Heaven Slaying Alliance with their own eyes.

Temples stood in every corner, divine statues towered overhead, clouds and mist swirled, and the surging aura of faith was like a boundless ocean, crashing against the depths of the sky and coalescing into a dazzling Milky Way.

They feared being tainted by the eerie aura within the Heaven Slaying Alliance, lest they be silently converted into one of its own.

Although the Heaven Slaying Alliance was incredibly peaceful, with no strife, and all races and factions strictly adhered to the rules, devoting themselves to quiet cultivation and the pursuit of the Dao, the fanatical expressions of the devotees and followers in those ancestral halls and temples made their hearts tremble with unease. They felt that all this tranquility was an illusion conjured by demons and monsters, making it all the more eerie.

Once this facade is shattered, the scene that awaits will surely be shocking: corpses strewn everywhere, desolation and ruin.

Even they, as cultivators of the Dao Realm, felt such unease and struggled to distinguish reality from illusion—let alone ordinary cultivators.

In their eyes, the Heaven Slaying Alliance was far more dangerous and eerie than the rumors from the outside world suggested. Amidst the turmoil of the ages and the world’s widespread unrest, the Heaven Slaying Alliance had somehow managed to maintain such peace and tranquility.

It was like the eye of a hurricane—utterly still, yet permeated with a thick atmosphere of strangeness and eeriness, completely abnormal.

Perhaps from the very moment they set foot on the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s territory, the sights they beheld were not real.

Reality and illusion, truth and falsehood—it was impossible to tell.

Time ticked by in the chilly side hall where guests were received.

Chen Yi, Layman Huijue, and the others waited restlessly, every one of them on edge, their backs and palms drenched in cold sweat.

Incense swirled through the hall, and mist rose from the incense burner, exuding an aura of tranquility and serenity.

Yet this very atmosphere made it impossible for them to calm down; they were filled with unbearable anxiety.

After the Heaven Slaying Alliance brought them here, they seemed to have abandoned them. As envoys of the Righteous Unity Alliance, they had lost all sense of dignity and respect, treated as though they were invisible.

This left them feeling both helpless and overwhelmed by a wave of sorrow and powerlessness.

Caught between two colossal powers, even those at the Dao Realm were nothing more than insignificant figures.

Suddenly, footsteps sounded from outside the side hall, causing everyone inside to perk up.

But before they could rise to greet the arrival, a blinding golden light shot skyward, rising like a great sun and illuminating the boundless cosmos.

Endless runic power erupted, as if millions of stars were exploding right there; the entire side hall instantly turned to dust, vaporized and obliterated.

"How dare you!"

“How dare you attempt to assassinate the Alliance Leader!”

"You’re asking for death!"

"Seize them!"

A voice filled with fury rang out amidst the endless radiance.

Accompanied by earth-shattering fury, the voice instantly reverberated across the land, causing the entire sky to rumble.

Countless star systems throughout the cosmos trembled violently, surging and churning like a raging sea—as if the heavens themselves were enraged, ready to punish all living beings.

Countless cultivators and living beings trembled in fear amidst this earth-shaking upheaval and furious roar, their eyes filled with terror and dread.

"Is this the 'opportune moment' you spoke of?"

In another hall, Ling Yuling’s beautiful eyes widened in surprise as she turned to look at Gu Changge with utter disbelief.

Gu Changge sat there calmly sipping tea. “Compared to taking direct action, I think this is far more legitimate.”

Ling Yuling was at a loss for words, unable to think of a response for a moment.

“It truly is a case of ‘where there’s a will, there’s a way,’” she said softly.

Gu Changge smiled and said, “To act without just cause is not merely a recipe for defeat, but a path to self-destruction.”“I won’t harm them; at most, I’ll detain them for a while and let them suffer a bit. The Righteous Unity Alliance is full of ambition, acting against the natural order, and foolishly attempting to obstruct the momentum of the Heaven Slaying Alliance—they deserve to be punished.” The news that the Righteous Unity Alliance had sent an envoy to attempt the assassination of the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s leader caused boundless turmoil and a sensation across the vast lands of Cangmang that very day.

Countless cultivators and sentient beings were shocked and found it hard to believe.

That the Righteous Unity Alliance would dare to do such a thing at this very moment made people think they had gone mad.

Some questioned the veracity of the reports.

But regardless of the truth, the Heaven Slaying Alliance was enraged from top to bottom, and a vast army gathered that very day from all corners of the cosmos.
