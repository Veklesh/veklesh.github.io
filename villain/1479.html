"What do you call me?"

Gu Changge seemed to smile as he asked, “Do you think that matters now?”

Ling Yuling froze, unsure how to respond, and finally let out a wistful sigh.

Indeed, the vast and uncertain situation had already become a foregone conclusion.

Did titles and such really matter anymore?

In truth, the moment her memories returned, she had already come to understand many things; the worries she once harbored had finally become reality.

Yet she still had no idea what Gu Changge intended to do.

Rule on behalf of Heaven? Heaven Slaying?

He is the Ruler of Heaven; he is the one who created the Dark Clans; he is the one who brought endless calamity upon the Vast Expanse.

Yet he is also the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, the one who fought against the Dark Clans and unified the Vast Expanse.

She raised her head and gazed at the scene beyond the grand hall. The vast cosmos, the boundless expanse of time and space—no matter how many layers of universes and dimensions there were, none seemed able to escape the concept of “Heaven.”

“Heaven… what exactly is it?” She was lost in confusion.

Gu Changge spoke casually, “Heaven pities the people, Heaven loves the people; Heaven is impartial and selfless, suspended high and indestructible, enduring for all eternity. Heaven… is the law, the iron rule.”

Upon hearing these words, a buzzing sound seemed to ring in Ling Yuling’s mind, giving rise to a deep sense of reverence.

This was the innate reverence for “Heaven” held by countless beings who had lived amidst the Vast Expanse of the cosmos for generations.

Perhaps only Gu Changge dared to speak such words so brazenly.

She looked at Gu Changge and asked, “So, are you planning to change all of this?”

“Change what?” Gu Changge asked.

Ling Yuling replied, “To change these Vast Expanse, boundless rules and ironclad laws that have remained eternal and unchanging throughout endless ages—just as those who once engaged in Heaven Slaying did…”

To all sentient beings, the Realm of Truth exists like the sky—eternal and unchanging.

In the beginning, the Heaven Slaying Ones did not merely appear in the New True Realm or the Ancient True Realm; they were beings that had been passed down from ancient times to the present, like heroic spirits.

They defied Heaven, sought to destroy it, attempting to overthrow the Realm of Truth’s reckoning and “rule” over the Vast Expanse, and to topple that “Heaven” suspended above the Vast Expanse.

"Change? Can it truly be changed?" Gu Changge seemed to sigh softly at that moment.

Ling Yuling looked at him and said softly, “If we uphold the will of all sentient beings—the grand aspirations of all Heaven Slayers throughout the ages—why shouldn’t change be possible?”

“It’s not that simple or easy,” Gu Changge simply shook his head.

“Even you say that?”

Ling Yuling’s feelings toward Gu Changge were complex. She knew full well that he was the former Ruler of Heaven, the very culprit who had once plunged countless beings into despair and cast the Vast Expanse into darkness—the being known as the Source of Darkness.

Yet toward him… she truly could not muster any hatred.

The existence of the Heaven Slaying Alliance had, to some extent, helped her understand Gu Changge’s purpose.

Perhaps bearing the infamy and scorn of one lifetime, a hundred lifetimes, a thousand lifetimes, or ten thousand lifetimes—as long as it could carve out a glimmer of light for future generations and leave behind a faint, visible ember—that was the noble ideal pursued by the countless predecessors of the Heaven Slaying Alliance who had sacrificed themselves one after another.

She knew she herself could not achieve such a feat, yet she sensed… that Gu Changge was acting and making her plans driven by precisely that kind of obsession.

Gu Changge gestured with her hand. Ling Yuling hesitated for a moment but walked over nonetheless. Gu Changge led her, and the two of them vanished instantly from the main hall of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

When they reappeared, a vast, boundless starry expanse stretched before them, its horizon invisible to the eye. Within this expanse, ripples of light undulated; within each wave, countless worlds and universes were born and perished.

These were countless small worlds, each teeming with billions of living beings.

Gu Changge made a casual gesture, and a boundless tidal wave surged into the air before him. The waves drifted and scattered, finally settling into his palm. As he clasped his hands together, the miniature worlds within could be seen collapsing and being destroyed one after another. Then, with a mere thought, those destroyed worlds reappeared once more.

"Are you trying to tell me that, to these countless beings, you are like the heavens themselves? That the gap in power is so vast that, in the eyes of the weak, the strong are the heavens?" Ling Yuling stared at him in bewilderment.

Gu Changge shook his head. “No, I just want to ask you: if you were living in one of those small worlds, and one day faced with all this out of the blue, what would you do?”

"I wouldn’t be able to do anything," Ling Yuling said, then fell silent.

If one were to truly experience life in such a small world—where even the slightest disturbance from the outside could shatter and obliterate it—

That sense of helplessness and despair was beyond words.

Perhaps, for the beings of the Realm of Truth, the Vast Expanse is precisely such a state?

"—An individual truly can do nothing, but what if it were all living beings?"

"The power of the spirit is the one thing that must never be underestimated or disregarded. No one can say just how powerful a single person’s spirit truly is." Gu Changge waved his hand, and the river before them returned to calm. He then led Ling Yuling back to the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Ling Yuling, however, was overcome by a profound sense of awe, reflecting on the words Gu Changge had spoken.

In recent days, the power of the Heaven Slaying Alliance had indeed been growing by the day; the daily changes could truly be described as rapid and transformative.

And all these changes were indeed driven by the doctrines and tenets propagated by the Heaven Slaying Alliance. In some mysterious way, this had indeed caused many powerful figures to emerge within the Alliance, including quite a few beings at the Dao Realm.

The power of faith stems from the purity of the believers and the depth of their devotion…

And that source originates from the heart.

If one relied solely on cultivation, it would take an unimaginable number of eras to break through to the Dao Realm—and even then, one would need to be blessed with countless fortuitous opportunities and possess immense cosmic fortune.

Yet in just a few thousand years, how many more Dao Realm beings have emerged within the Heaven Slaying Alliance compared to the past?

"A single Dao Realm being cannot change anything, but what if there were tens of millions, or even countless Dao Realm beings?"

"Man can overcome heaven."

Ling Yuling seemed to have come to a realization at that moment, her beautiful eyes sparkling with a radiant light.

"Man can indeed overcome heaven."

Gu Changge suppressed the strange light in his eyes, smiled, and reached out to tuck a strand of her hair behind her ear.

"Do you want me to retrieve the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor?" Ling Yuling snapped back to reality, her gaze fixed intently on him.

“No need. I’ll have Luo retrieve it.”

"If you leave the Heaven Slaying Alliance, many matters will be difficult to handle, and I don’t trust the others," Gu Changge said, shaking his head slightly.

“I understand. In that case, I’ll let my younger sister know during this time,” Ling Yuling said softly.

She and Ling Yuxian shared a deep, unspoken bond; just as she had come to know Gu Changge’s true origins, Ling Yuxian would surely learn of them as well.

Afterward, she shared with Gu Changge all the connections and clues related to the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor that she could recall.

Gu Changge gave a single command, and Luo Zhu appeared from outside the hall. With his power at the pinnacle of the Realm, it wouldn’t take him long to retrieve the Six Harmonies Heavenly Abyss Armor.

After Ling Yuling and the others had departed, Gu Changge slowly returned to his seat and sat upright. He gazed at the empty hall and fell into deep thought.

Today, the Heaven Slaying Alliance was at the height of its power, flourishing and magnificent. Across the Vast Expanse of the known world, only the Nine Heavens and the Ancient Hidden Civilization possessed the qualifications to stand against it.

As for the underworld, there were actually very few factions with the True Path Realm standing guard.

Logically speaking, everything was proceeding according to his plan in an orderly fashion, including the numerous powerful figures within the Heaven Slaying Alliance, who were now contributing immeasurable faith energy to him, creating more Immortal Substances and Creation Substances for him.

Yet that faint sense of foreboding that had arisen deep within him showed no signs of abating; on the contrary, it grew ever more intense.

"Does it stem from a chaotic, unknowable future?"

"The past can be glimpsed, but in certain spacetime dimensions, a mist of uncertainty still lingers."

Gu Changge furrowed his brow slightly.

Meanwhile, in the Nightmare Demon Realm, boundless dark matter surged like a majestic river of stars. Before the Hall of the Celestial Mandate, all the High Priests, Priests, and Guardians had bowed and departed, leaving only High Priest Wu Yue standing there.

As that vast and majestic aura gradually subsided and fell silent.

She sighed softly to herself, pushed open the doors of the Hall of Substitution, and walked inside. Upon the grand altar, the Coffin of the World’s Demise floated, its lid already pushed open, yet the interior was completely empty. Within the coffin, only a hazy, blood-red mist, as ethereal as mist, swirled,while the Mirror of Reincarnation lay quietly set aside.

Upon the Coffin of the World’s Demise, a vague yet immense figure could be seen sitting in lotus position. The surrounding spacetime was twisted and distorted, and the gaze of ordinary beings could not penetrate that spacetime to see the ethereal figure.

Rather than a figure, it was more accurately a mere projection.

The true Lord of Heaven has never resided within the Hall of Heaven.

What remains here is merely a fraction of his power.

Within the Coffin of the World’s Demise, guarded by the Heavenly Hosts for generations, his flesh and blood had long since transformed into an intangible, unfathomable mist of power, awaiting the arrival of its true master to claim it.

Wu Yue approached the coffin with the utmost reverence and removed the Mirror of Reincarnation.

Outside the Hall of the Celestial Vicar, a figure had been waiting there all along—none other than Jing Tianyuan, the spirit of the Gate of Eternal Life.

“My Lord, might you tell me when you will grant me an audience?” She looked at Jing Tianyuan, a hint of hope visible on her face, shrouded in the misty vapor.

Jing Tianyuan took the Mirror of Reincarnation and, upon hearing this, merely shook his head. “My Lord said little; he only instructed me to come and take the Mirror of Reincarnation away.”

All the light in Wu Yue’s eyes gradually faded, as she watched Jing Tianyuan’s figure vanish into the endless darkness.

The merging of the Source of Darkness with the Nightmare Realm was, in itself, merely an order from the Lord of Heaven.

Even before the Ruler of Destiny and the Ancestor Xu Shang had arrived, she had already known that the Lord of Heaven had long since broken free and revived. She only regretted that, for various reasons, she could not go to him in person to see him face to face.

Despite countless ages and eras passing, that face had never grown blurry; on the contrary, with the passage of time, it had become increasingly clear.

The great war between the Nine Heavens and the Dark Clans had drawn to a close, yet beyond the Ancient Hidden Civilization, the slaughter continued unabated, the cries of battle shaking the heavens.

Mighty ancient warships tore through space and time, from which emerged countless powerful beings. Led by figures at the Dao Realm, they commanded vast armies that collided like surging torrents.

The major clans beyond the Ancient Hidden Civilization had stepped forward, with Dao Realm beings among them. Each star was vast and boundless, yet at this moment, they had all been reduced to battlefields. The clash between the Ancestral Dao Realm beings directly affected the alternate dimensions; a single wisp of energy spreading out was enough to tear apart the surrounding cosmos.

Jiang Weiyang, Ji Hanjing, and the others stood on the deck of the ancient warship, gazing out at the scene of the battlefield.

"The Ancient Hidden Civilization is deeply enigmatic. Even the races from those external universes alone possess strength that cannot be underestimated."

"Most of them have Dao Realm beings stationed there, which is enough to defend an entire newly formed True Realm..."

“That Ancestral Dao Realm being who intervened just now—we don’t even know its origins. Yet, relying on that power alone, it actually managed to block our path.”

Several ministers spoke up as well—including figures like the Enfeoffed General of the Auxiliary Army and the Heavenly King of the Northern Frontier, who had once commanded major armies—but their expressions were all grave at that moment.

"The Ancient Hidden Civilization merely wants us to back down in the face of difficulty, which is why they’ve only dispatched an Ancestral Dao Realm being."

"If they were to send a few more, we might find it difficult to resist."

State Master Xingming, clad in a pure white robe, spoke up.

As someone who had once followed the Master of Destiny, he could, in a sense, be considered a disciple cultivated by the Master of Destiny.

However, whether it was the Old Woman Xingying from the vast lands of Xianchu, Xing Sha who had once aided Emperor Wei Yang Jiang Yun, or himself standing before them now, all harbored a deep-seated hatred for the Lord of Destiny.

Jiang Weiyang furrowed her dark brows slightly but said nothing.

Ji Hanjing, on the other hand, seemed to have thought of something; a pensive gleam shone in his eyes as he looked toward the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

While a fierce battle raged throughout the outer cosmos, deep within the Ancient Hidden Civilization, inside that magnificent hall, the Lord of Reincarnation and the Lord of the World were working together to refine an object resembling a blue vessel.

The Lord of Calamity, the Lord of Destiny, and the Ancestor Xu had already departed. However, before leaving, the Ancestor Xu had entrusted the Lord of Reincarnation and the Lord of the World with a portion of the flesh and blood he had once obtained from the Lord of Heaven.

A vast and surging energy rippled through the boundless hall, producing a sound as if the heavens and earth were being split apart.

The object before them, resembling a green vessel, suddenly erupted with an aura so immense it defied description. Corrosive-like substances flaked away, revealing an inner hue resembling eternal Dao gold.

Whether it was the Lord of Reincarnation or the Lord of the World, their expressions were grave at this moment, with boundless radiance swirling in their eyes. Around them, countless minute particles, as fine as dust, swirled in their vicinity. Each minuscule particle seemed like a vast, tangible world in itself, from which wispy strands of the world’s essence emanated.

The next moment, with a thunderous roar, the object resembling a green boat shook violently. Clear cracks immediately appeared, rapidly spreading and penetrating its entire surface.

Even though the two of them noticed and tried to stop it immediately, it was already too late. They could only watch as the green vessel became increasingly covered in cracks, seemingly on the verge of shattering and exploding at any moment.

"Have we failed again?"

"This is the strongest substance I extracted from the core of a billion universes, yet it cannot repair the cracks on this green vessel..."

The World Ruler sighed and, in the end, had no choice but to give up.

"It still won’t work."

The Lord of Reincarnation’s expression flickered between light and shadow, then returned to its usual profound calm.The hall where the two stood had collapsed countless times under the impact of that earlier burst of energy, yet under the influence of their auras, it instantly returned to its original state. Even time here seemed to flow backward, as if completely frozen at a single moment.

This also caused the additional cracks on the green boat, which had just been repaired, to heal rapidly, restoring it to its original state.

They possessed the power to reverse the flow of time and space, yet they were unable to fully repair the fissure on the green boat.

The World Ruler said, “Though I cannot fully mend that crack, I can sense a vital energy within it, as if it were a living substance—no longer as lifeless as before.”

The Lord of Reincarnation shook his head. With a wave of his hand, the green boat instantly transformed into a streak of green light, returning to his palm. It appeared no larger than the palm of his hand, bearing a rusted hue, and the origin of that fissure remained a mystery.

When the two of them had first stumbled upon this green boat in the boundless chaos of spacetime, they had nearly been obliterated—body and soul—by the faint trace of energy left within it.

It must be noted that although the two had not yet reached the realm of the “End of the Path” at that time, they were already considered to be standing at the very pinnacle of the Vast Expanse. Yet, faced with this green boat drifting in the chaos, they had no way to approach it.

It was precisely because of this green vessel that the originally hostile relationship between the two gradually eased.

It was only after countless years had passed and they had finally reached the “End of the Path” that they joined forces to attempt to remove it.

The origins of this mysterious green vessel remain a mystery. Even now that the two have become beings of great power, they cannot fathom its source, much less destroy it.

Even the substance from which the green vessel was forged remains a mystery; despite searching every corner of time and space, they have found not a single clue.

They surmised that the cracks on this blue vessel were not formed in the Vast Expanse, but originated from the Realm of Truth.

Perhaps, at some point, an unimaginable being used this blue vessel to “sneak” from the Realm of Reality into the Vast Expanse.

However, the two have now refined it and made it their own.

"The passage we constructed... surely there won’t be any problems, will there..."

The World Ruler waved his robe, and the scepter returned to his hand once more. He also gathered the tiny particles floating in the air and stored them within his robe. Though these particles were minuscule, if released into the outside world, even a single one could easily inflict severe injuries on a being at the Ancestral Dao Realm.
