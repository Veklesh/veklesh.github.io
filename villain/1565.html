The Land Of Truth has its rules: except during the Epoch Reckoning, at the end of each Chaos Heaven and Earth Epoch, the passage between the two sides cannot be opened at will.

Unless there are exceptional circumstances and approval is granted by all nine elders at the Council of Truth, one cannot obtain the barrier that protects against corruption to travel to the other side of the Vast Expanse.

The last being to sneak across is still the subject of much discussion among the various races here in the Land Of Truth.

Of course, that stowaway had never received the Heavenly Seal.

Having never even set foot in the Heavenly Seal Realm, how did it resist the erosion of the surging forces within the passage, and how did it fend off the capture attempt by the Patriarch of the Divine Clan?

It can be said that the most powerful being in the Realm of Truth today is undoubtedly the Patriarch of the Divine Clan. At the dawn of the Zero Era, he received the Summoning of the Kitchen of Heaven bestowed by the True Ancestor.

Now possessing the magical power of thirteen Chaos Heaven and Earth Eras, he has carved out his own Ancestral Realm within the Land Of Truth and is the only being to have attained the title of Heavenly Ancestor.

Even the Patriarch of the Divine Clan was unable to subdue that stowaway, who managed to escape unexpectedly.

Zhen Sanheng spoke, his gaze turning toward the massive figure seated at the head of the table.

"I will personally report this matter to the Patriarch shortly. If it is indeed related to that stowaway from before, the Patriarch will certainly intervene."

Pang Tian’s figure spoke slowly.

All the elders present were well aware that allowing that stowaway to unexpectedly escape from the Land Of Truth and hide back on the Vast Expanse had caused the clan leader of the Divine Clan to suffer punishment—forcing him to kneel in the Hall of True Ancestors for a hundred years.

Of course, such a minor matter would naturally not warrant the attention of the three True Ancestors; it was simply a lapse on the part of the Clan Chief of the Divine Clan, and he deserved to be punished…

"Since this matter is related to the unfathomable power behind that stowaway from back then, it stands to reason that the Heavenly Lord of Absolute Darkness could not have stood up to it. "With your strength alone, you’ll likely fail."

Another elder’s expression also grew much more serious as he said, “Since that unfathomable power is involved, ordinary methods are indeed useless.”

Jueyin Heavenly Lord was completely baffled.

“Jueyin Lou, you shall take the Elder’s Jade Voucher and lead the three other Heavenly Seal Great Clans—Yanhuo, Chongming, and Taimeng—to muster their clan’s strongest warriors and assemble outside the Hexu Abyss.”

“The Absolute Yin Tower will certainly not fail the Elder’s trust on this occasion.”

Jueyin Lou’s expression grew solemn as he accepted the Elder’s jade scroll with the utmost reverence.

This was a decree from the Council of Truth, representing supreme authority capable of mobilizing the forces of all clans within the Land Of Truth.

The other major Heavenly Seal Clans, upon seeing this jade token, could not help but treat it with the utmost reverence, as if they were in the presence of the Council of Truth itself.

Soon, having regained his composure, Jueyin Lou respectfully departed from the Celestial Hall.

The nine elders representing the Nine True Clans, however, did not disperse; they remained seated in their original places.

“It seems our earlier conjectures have been confirmed. It is impossible for a power rivaling the Heavenly Will to have emerged from the Vast Expanse without cause…”

“Some have long suspected that the Chaos Will, slain by the Three True Ancestors back then, did not vanish completely.”

"Perhaps the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance is being aided by that Chaos Will."

"Only the power of the Chaos Will can counter the Heavenly Will. To this day, no being has ever reached that level. Even in the Land Of Truth, to possess power comparable to the Heavenly Dao... according to calculations, it would require the accumulation of no less than nine Chaos Heaven and Earth Eons..."

The murmurs of discussion in the grand hall gradually died down.

Since the Land Of Truth came into being, starting from the Zero Era, only thirteen Chaos Cosmos Eons have passed.

And it takes the magical power accumulated over nineteen Chaos Realm Eons just to barely match the power of the Heavenly Dao. As for how many Heavenly Daos the power of the Heavenly Will actually represents, no one knows.

Therefore, for the sentient beings of the Land Of Truth, reaching a level comparable to the Heavenly Dao would take a very, very long time.

Not to mention the Heavenly Will—that supreme will born from the Heavenly Dao itself, which Lingzhi Shi found tiresome and lacking in righteousness.

The extent of the power the Heavenly Will can command is simply unknown—it is the sum of countless Heavenly Daos.

It is like an ordinary person throwing a punch; the force of that punch represents the power of the Heavenly Dao as understood by the masses, but no one can say exactly how much of that power is contained within that ordinary person.

To them, the difference between the two is roughly the same.

As the second-largest of the Heavenly Seal Clans, second only to the Absolute Yin Clan, the Yanhuo Clan possessed equally ancient roots, though they had received their Heavenly Seal summons later than the Absolute Yin Clan.

In terms of cultivation and power, the clan leader of the Yanhuo Clan was only one era of Chaos Heaven and Earth’s accumulated power behind the Absolute Yin Heavenly Lord.

With each step, lotus blossoms seemed to bloom beneath his feet; he exuded the aura of a Daoist sage and the dignity of a Buddha, as if the ethereal form of an ancient Buddha were coalescing behind him. The Yanhuo Clan’s origins were extremely ancient, traceable to a once-flourishing Buddhist-Daoist Civilization.

There is a legend in the Land Of Truth that the clan leader of the Yan Huo Clan was once the mount of the supreme being of that ancient Buddhist-Daoist Civilization. Later, due to the reckoning of the Great Calamity, he betrayed his original master and chose to follow the elders of the Divine Clan of the Land Of Truth.

The clan leader of the Yan-Huo Clan is also one of the very few beings who endured an entire era within the Purity Pool, having purged all impurities from their being.

The Heavenly Lord of the Absolute Darkness’s power far surpasses his own.

in the other regions of the Land Of Truth, many ancient clans also sprang into action upon hearing the news and rushed over.

Each breakthrough and refinement raises the limit of their lifespan; it is through this very method that the great ancient clans have managed to endure.

Even before Tai Mengyi obtained the Heavenly Seal Summoning Symbol, he was an "End of the Path" cultivator who had broken through his limits eleven times, shattering the limits of his lifespan a total of eleven times.

The woman in the red dress before him, Dao Xian Yun, was a "End of the Path" cultivator who had broken through her limits ten times—a figure who ranked among the very best in the Land Of Truth, with few able to match her.

As for exactly how many breakthroughs and refinements are required to truly reach the level of "Supreme Shattering," no one knows. Each breakthrough brings one ever closer to that realm, yet none have ever truly touched it.

There have been beings who reached the thirteenth breakthrough, but they ultimately perished and their Dao was extinguished during the fourteenth attempt.

It can be said that the cruelty of the Land Of Truth lies precisely here: no matter how dazzling a prodigy may be, or how many times they break through their limits, there is no guarantee they will receive the Summoning of the Heavenly Seal Talisman and enter the Heavenly Seal Realm.

Take the Heavenly Lord of Absolute Yin and the clan leader of the Yan-Huo clan, for example—neither had ever broken through a single limit, yet they were still recognized by the Heavenly Seal Summoning.
