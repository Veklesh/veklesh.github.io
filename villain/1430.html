Upon returning to the Daochang True Realm, Gu Changge set about taking the Divine Realm with him, and he did not hide the matter concerning Xiyao from Yue Mingkong.

Yue Mingkong, however, expressed understanding, for she had always remembered that figure sitting alone in the corner, drinking in silence, during the wedding banquet.

She just hadn’t expected that Empress Xiyao would ultimately choose such an ending. Was she truly so disheartened that she had given up on life?

She couldn’t help but think back to her memories before her “rebirth.” Back then, she must have been much like Empress Xiyao.

“Do you plan to keep her by your side?”

Yue Mingkong looked at the pristine white porcelain basin Gu Changge had brought back. The fish inside was truly beautiful, swimming freely and carefree. Its lustrous, lovely eyes held both innocence and a touch of spirit.

The only question was: once Empress Xiyao regained her memories of her past life and realized that Gu Changge had truly granted her wish—turning her into a fish—would she be utterly dumbfounded?

Yue Mingkong smiled softly, his eyes narrowing slightly; he actually looked forward to witnessing that scene.

“There happens to be a pond in my courtyard. Let’s have her stay there,” Gu Changge said.

Yue Mingkong hummed in agreement and nodded. “I’m going into seclusion again.”

“This time, I’ll stay by your side,” Gu Changge said with a faint smile.

“Mm-hmm.”

“But aren’t you going to check on the Temple of Destiny?” Yue Mingkong asked suddenly, resting her head against Gu Changge’s chest.

Although she had only recently emerged from seclusion, nothing in the Daochang True Realm escaped her notice, and she kept a close eye on all the women connected to Gu Changge.

“Xiao Ruoyin… she’s actually a pitiable figure, and besides, she’s your woman too.” She lifted her clear eyes to look at Gu Changge.

“Are you really not the least bit jealous? You’re so generous.”

Gu Changge smiled. Seeing Yue Mingkong shoot him a glare, as if she were about to reach out and pinch his waist, he said, “I’ll go.”

After what happened with Empress Xiyao, Gu Changge had actually been thinking: if, from the very beginning, he had been a little more cold-blooded and ruthless—if he hadn’t come clean right away but had instead concocted a reason, an excuse—perhaps it would have been a little easier for Empress Xiyao to bear.

Even if those words were nothing but lies.

Actions speak louder than words.

But in the end, his heart softened, and he no longer wanted to continue deceiving her.

As for Xiao Ruoyin’s situation, it was actually quite similar to that of Empress Xiyao; their relationship had started as one of mutual exploitation.

Gu Changge used her to plot against the Creation Immortal Vessel, while she coveted his power and status.

Times have changed, and their circumstances are now completely different. In his eyes, Xiao Ruoyin is nothing more than a stranger with whom he once had a connection—nothing more.

But as Yue Mingkong had said, she was his woman.

The Temple of Destiny was even more desolate than Gu Changge had imagined. As he stepped inside, the woman dressed in a shamaness’s ceremonial robes, her hair tied in a long ponytail, had not yet noticed him; she was still there, lighting candles on her own.

"The Void Constitution of Destiny..."

Gu Changge knew her identity, her background, and the reason Xiao Ruoyin had summoned her.

He made no move to disturb the woman, then walked into the inner chamber, where he saw Xiao Ruoyin sitting cross-legged on a meditation cushion, deep in meditation.

"Putting oneself in a life-or-death situation to find a way out—that takes great resolve. It’s a pity, though; even if I hadn’t come, you would still have failed."

Gu Changge saw at a glance where Xiao Ruoyin’s problem lay: plagued by inner demons, her Daoist heart was tainted. She had already attempted to break through the threshold of the Dao Realm once, and this time she intended to strip away all worldly attachments and eliminate every flaw.

But those flaws had long since seeped into her entire Daoist heart like thick ink. To sever them, she would have to shatter her Daoist heart and start her cultivation anew; otherwise, the outcome would remain unchanged.

To resolve this, she must either reshape her Daoist heart to remove the flaw at its root, or use that flaw as proof of her path. Just as the moon waxes and wanes, the Daoist heart need not be flawless—it all depends on whether she can come to terms with this and accept it.

Since she was never a flawless being to begin with, why torment herself over that single flaw? To accept and acknowledge herself—is that not, in itself, a form of purity of the Dao?

"Your Majesty..."

Xiao Ruoyin’s eyes suddenly opened; her gaze remained calm and unruffled. She was even more composed than Gu Changge had imagined.

Gu Changge made no effort to conceal his presence, so Xiao Ruoyin naturally sensed his arrival.

"I don’t like that title. I still prefer it when you used to call me Young Master Changge." He sat down on the jade stool beside her and casually poured himself a cup of clear tea.

The tea had long since gone cold; who knew how long it had been sitting there.

Xiao Ruoyin watched him, frozen in place for a moment, then rose gracefully. “I’ll go brew a fresh pot.”

“There’s no need,” Gu Changge waved his hand. “I’ll just sit here for a while.”

Xiao Ruoyin nodded, a look of confusion in her eyes as she wondered why Gu Changge had come.

Although she had subconsciously wanted to see Gu Changge and had hoped he would come, now that he was actually here, she didn’t know how to face him or what to say.

“Please sit down; there’s no need to stand,” Gu Changge said with a smile.

Xiao Ruoyin hesitated for a moment, smoothed the hem of her skirt, and sat down on the jade stool beside him. A few strands of black hair slid down her cheek and fell near her ear. Just as she was about to tuck them behind her ear, she felt a warm hand reach out and gently tuck the strands back for her.

She gazed into the gentle eyes so close to her own and froze in place.

“Have you settled in well at the Temple of Destiny? I see you’ve found an heir—are you planning to pass your legacy on to her?” Gu Changge asked casually, as if chatting about trivial matters over tea or a meal.

Xiao Ruoyin gave a soft nod. “Her name is Wu Qi. I found her by chance in one of the lower realms. Since she possesses the same ‘Void of Destiny’ constitution as I do, I sensed her future achievements would surpass my own, so I brought her along to guide her.”

“The Temple of Destiny has been a bit too quiet. Having someone to talk to and relieve the boredom isn’t a bad thing.” Gu Changge nodded.

Xiao Ruoyin looked out toward the inner hall. “Wu Qi is quite diligent. I’ve entrusted her with the daily tasks of sweeping the dust and lighting the candles in the Temple of Destiny. It saves me a lot of trouble, giving me more time to study and fathom the workings of fate…”

Gu Changge nodded in agreement.

Xiao Ruoyin had been cultivating within the Temple of Destiny for years, rarely stepping outside. Even if one asked her about her daily life, aside from seclusion, cultivation, seclusion, and cultivation… she probably wouldn’t have much else to say.

Wu Qi’s presence, however, gave her an opportunity to open up.

Gu Changge then asked her about how she and Wu Qi got along on a daily basis.

Xiao Ruoyin seemed to have forgotten Gu Changge’s earlier request to just sit for a while. She began recalling the vitality and joy that had been added to her monotonous life since Wu Qi arrived at the Temple of Destiny, and so she began to speak…

Gu Changge didn’t interrupt; he listened quietly, occasionally returning a smile whenever her gaze met his.

Later, he asked about Xiao Ruoyin’s cultivation practices and her insights into the Way of Destiny. As she spoke more freely about her areas of expertise, she also took the opportunity to ask Gu Changge about some questions regarding fate that had been puzzling her.

She knew that Gu Changge’s current cultivation had long since reached an unimaginable level.

Although he had not specifically cultivated the Way of Destiny, the Three Thousand Great Dao ultimately converged at the same destination.

Gu Changge answered every question candidly and thoroughly, explaining in great detail to Xiao Ruoyin his understanding of fate, and even went so far as to demonstrate it for her.

On her way to find Xiao Ruoyin, Wu Qi happened upon this peculiar scene.

Xiao Ruoyin, who was usually serious and aloof, now had a faint smile playing at the corners of her lips. Beside her sat a man of unparalleled handsomeness and otherworldly grace. The two were conversing so animatedly that Xiao Ruoyin seemed to have forgotten Wu Qi was even there.

“Is that the current Alliance Leader?”

“He really does look so young—he’s a perfect match for Lady Ruoyin.”

Wu Qi ducked her head. She didn’t even know who Gu Changge was, but seeing Her Majesty finally smile like this made her genuinely happy. She then quietly closed the palace doors and left.

“Sometimes when I return to the Daochang True Realm, time is so tight that I haven’t had a chance to see you. It’s not that I meant to neglect you…” Gu Changge explained calmly.

“I understand, Your Majesty… I am satisfied simply that Young Master Changge has come to see me.”

Xiao Ruoyin quickly corrected herself. Since Gu Changge disliked being called “Your Majesty,” she would revert to her former form of address. For a fleeting moment, she seemed to drift back into a memory of her past self—that woman with a mercenary streak, coveting glory and climbing the social ladder.

As for whether Gu Changge’s words were true or false, it no longer mattered.

What mattered was that he had come.

“In the coming days, I plan to take the Divine Realm with me. At that time, the Temple of Destiny, along with everything else, will be brought into the Vastness by the Heaven Slaying Alliance. If you’re unwilling, I can leave the Temple of Destiny behind,” Gu Changge said.

Xiao Ruoyin paused for a moment, then said softly, “It makes no difference where I am. Whether in the Vast Wastes or Daochang, wherever my heart finds peace is my home.”

Seeing Gu Changge nod and rise to leave, her eyes flickered slightly as she asked, “Could you stay and rest here tonight?”

Gu Changge seemed a little surprised, but then nodded. “All right.”

A faint blush spread across Xiao Ruoyin’s cheeks.

Several days later, the entire cosmic realm where the Divine Kingdom resided—encompassing parts of the Upper Realm’s chaotic regions, an ancient city within the Eight Desolations and Ten Domains, and a village named Qing Shan—resounded with a thunderous roar, enveloped in boundless light.

A hazy passage seemed to appear between heaven and earth, extending from beyond the Daochang True Realm and connecting to an unknown realm.

The Daochang True Realm trembled. All the clans and lineages knew that this was Gu Changge opening a passage from the Daochang True Realm to the Vast Expanse.

From this day forward, the Daochang True Realm would truly border the Vast Wilderness. Whether this would be a blessing or a curse, an opportunity or a danger for all the clans and factions—nothing could be certain.

At the same time, as the Divine Realm rumbled and rapidly receded into the distance, many powerful cultivators sensed the emergence of an unparalleled, overwhelming aura within it. A new Dao Realm had been born, situated right at the heart of the Divine Realm. The Great Dao of Heaven and Earth thundered, and extraordinary phenomena manifested.

Yet no one knew who this being might be.

Where the Divine Realm once stood, only a void remained.

Within the Daochang True Realm, the Heaven Slaying Alliance also began to migrate as the vast passage opened, moving toward the outside world.

"They’ve all left..."

"But why is the aura left by our distant ancestor growing fainter and fainter? Have I overlooked something?" The Gu family ancestor had not left the Daochang True Realm; he remained behind with many members of the Gu clan.

Gazing at the Divine Realm fading into the distance, his brow furrowed deeply; he felt as though he had overlooked something.

After waiting for countless ages, the Ancestor still had not returned.

That being, who once nearly touched the Realm of Truth, had left behind blessings and omens here—there must be a reason for it.

"Should I return to our ancestral lands in the Nine Heavens?" He frowned, lost in deep thought.
