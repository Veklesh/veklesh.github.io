Just as the Heaven Slaying Alliance gathered to report the situation, the fierce battle on another front was drawing to a close. The light that pierced the sun rippled through the various realms of time and space before finally fading into complete silence.

Su En, Qin Yi, Luo Yang, Wang He, and Leng Qiutang—representing the Five Heavens—joined forces. After unleashing an assault equivalent to tens of thousands of times their combined power, they successfully shattered the physical form forged from the Great Avatar of Heaven and then tore it apart.

Streams of the obscure, ethereal essence of the Heavenly Dao converged and surged there, transforming into five streams of incredibly pure, indescribable, and unimaginable energy that flowed into the bodies of the five.

Each of them claimed one of these streams.

Yitian, Shuaitian, Futiān... all were nourished, their auras surging, radiating supreme fluctuations that sent shivers down everyone’s spines.

The Lords of Power and Lords of Restraint of the Ancient Hidden Civilization could not help but frown, sensing a distinct threat.

Although the Five Heavens had defeated the Great Avatar of Heaven, these beings harbored immense ambition and held deep malice toward Gu Changge. If they were to turn their sights on the Heaven Slaying Alliance at this moment, it would likely pose a significant problem.

"They wouldn’t dare to act recklessly..."

"They don’t have the guts."

Yue Mingkong remained clad in armor and wielding his halberd, his gaze cold and piercing, showing not a trace of fear.

“As long as the clash between the Alliance Master and the Heavenly Will remains unresolved, they dare not lay a finger on the Heaven Slaying Alliance. However, with this portion of power derived from the authority of the Heavenly Dao, these Five Heavens have grown even stronger. They have likely already transcended the True Path realm and are now difficult to contend with.”

Lord of Power said in a deep voice.

Previously, the Five Heavens had only emanated True Path-level fluctuations, but now their aura was even more colossal and terrifying—profound and boundless, giving the sensation of facing the direct descendants of the Heavenly Lord of Absolute Darkness.

Of course, this is only the beginning; once the Five Heavens have fully assimilated that power, they will likely become even more formidable.

Judging by the cultivation tiers established in the Land Of Truth, they now possess strength equivalent to at least several levels of the Heavenly Seal Realm.

In the Vast Expanse of today, such power has already broken through all limits.

"There’s clearly a huge piece of meat right before my eyes, yet I can’t get my hands on it. How infuriating! There are nine Heavenly Dao authorities in total, and four of them have yet to be reclaimed by us. Those few are still holding onto the Heavenly Dao authorities."

"If I were to devour them all, I would become even more powerful—perhaps even possessing a sliver of the true might of the Heavenly Will in its prime."

From the shattered battlefield beyond the heavens, Yitian’s voice, tinged with greed, echoed within Su En.

Su En, clad in a golden robe, slowly suppressed the excitement on his handsome face.

He, too, was assimilating this vast, profound, and unfathomable power. Although the authority of the Heavenly Dao contained the Heavenly Will, under Yitian’s control, it had been completely erased and assimilated, becoming a pure energy so profound and mysterious that it defied comprehension.

Under the influence of this energy, Su En’s cultivation base surged continuously.

The Celestial Waning Tribulation, which would normally take an entire eon to overcome, had unexpectedly arrived early. He broke through several cultivation realms in succession, encountering not a single bottleneck.

"This is merely a manifestation of a fragment of the Heavenly Will—a mere tip of the iceberg, a drop in the bucket—yet it has granted me the strength of the Real Dao Realm, sufficient to survive four Celestial Waning Tribulations."

"It’s simply beyond belief. If I were relying solely on natural cultivation, it would take at least several thousand years—or even longer..."

Su En clenched his fists, feeling the terrifying, surging power within him, and grew even more confident.

"You’re right. Of the nine authorities of the Heavenly Dao, only four remain now. What a pity."

Su En took a deep breath and snapped back to reality, finding himself in complete agreement with Chu Shou Shou Tian’s words.

Having realized the importance of the source of the Heavenly Dao, his heart burned with passion. It could be said that no one could resist the thrill of such a cultivation breakthrough; it was addictive, and even the most steadfast Dao Heart would prove useless against it.

Meanwhile, Qin Yi, Luo Yang, Wang He, and Leng Qiutang were silently experiencing their own surges of power. The celestial realms behind them had reaped immense benefits, and they, in turn, received this nourishment, causing their cultivation to rise at a breathtaking pace—a development so extraordinary it could only be described as a dream.

This was especially true for Leng Qiutang, who hailed from the Linglong Miaojie. The fluctuations in her cultivation had already begun to faintly touch the threshold of the Ancestral Dao Realm.

Qin Yi, Luo Yang, and Wang He, however, were still merely at the Void Dao Realm level—far behind her.

The Decaying Heaven, the Corrupted Heaven, and the Chaotic Heaven… were now also coveting the remaining authority of the Heavenly Dao. Their own advancements were equally substantial, to the point where they could already harness the invincible might of the Sunbeam Heavenly Dao.

This power could already influence the causality and laws of the entire Vast Expanse—unlike in the world where they currently resided.

And now, with the true Heavenly Will unable to manifest, the remaining sources of the Heavenly Dao include the Authority of Destiny, the Authority of Yin and Yang, the Authority of Power, and the Authority of Reincarnation.

"The Lord of Reincarnation has perished, his body and Dao utterly annihilated; his authority over reincarnation is now ownerless..."

Soon, the Five Heavens also sensed the fluctuations deep within the Ancient Hidden Civilization and learned of the Lord of Reincarnation’s demise.

Since that is the case, the Authority of Reincarnation is now ownerless—and this is the purest essence of the Heavenly Dao. Who wouldn’t covet it?

Whoosh!!!

In an instant, Qin Yi, Wang He, and Luo Yang vanished, instantly arriving within the Ancient Hidden Civilization. They tracked the fading traces of the Reincarnation Authority, determined to pinpoint its whereabouts and seize it for themselves.

"Haha, go ahead and fight over the Authority of Reincarnation..."

"I’m not that foolish."

Su En sneered coldly, heeding Yi Tian’s advice, and placed himself within the remaining, indestructible core of the Abhorrent.

Though it was a mere drop in the bucket compared to the Heavenly Dao Authority, even a small piece of meat was still meat—how could he possibly pass it up?

At that moment, Yin Tian’s host, Leng Qiutang, was harboring the same thoughts as Su En and chose not to compete with Qin Yi and the others for the Authority of Reincarnation.

Her snow-white gown fluttered in the breeze, giving her an ethereal air. On her usually cold and aloof face, a rare, charming smile blossomed—like a celestial maiden descended to earth—as she turned to Su En and smiled.

A buzzing sound!!!

Su En’s mind was instantly thrown into chaos, as if submerged by a strange, pink river. From within came all manner of seductive sounds—the Ten Thousand Transformations of the Heavenly Demon—causing his mental defenses to crumble. He nearly fell into a boundless illusion, on the verge of eternal ruin.

"That damned Lustful Heaven..."

Yitian, enraged, reacted swiftly. A surge of dense plague energy flooded into Su En’s mind, shattering that strange pink river capable of plunging all worlds into ruin.

Only then did Su En come to his senses, his face flushed crimson. He was furious, yet also filled with dread—after all, he was the host of the Plague Heaven, and yet he had fallen victim to the attack without even realizing it?

"Damn it..."

"She wants to monopolize the Nine Minor Avatars of Heaven."

Watching Leng Qiutang’s figure vanish, Su En hurried after her, determined to seize them by force no matter what.

"When dogs fight, they both get a mouthful of fur."

"The Alliance Leader spared them; it seems he has other plans and intentions."

The members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance did not intervene in the great battle over the source of the Heavenly Dao. The losses in this battle were already heavy; there was no need to provoke another clash with the Five Heavens over it.

Besides, they stood no chance against the Five Heavens even if they tried to compete. Even if they managed to slay the Heavenly Avatar, it would be futile—the Avatar would simply reappear and revive.

It could be said that the Five Heavens’ emergence was more like a contingency plan left behind by Gu Changge specifically to counter the Avatar of Heaven.

After all, the relationship between the two was akin to that of natural enemies.

In the face of the Five Heavens, the immortal and indestructible Avatar of Heaven is like mere sustenance, destined only to be devoured by them.

"The current problem lies in the malevolent intentions of the Heavenly Lord of Absolute Darkness and the deities serving the Lord of Heaven. Regardless of which side prevails, the Vast Expanse of time and the myriad realms will never be healed."

"The Heavenly Lord of Absolute Darkness invoked the Great Purification Ritual for the sake of the Land Of Truth, but the malevolent intentions of the Lord of Heaven are for his own gain."

"Both pose a grave threat to the Vast Expanse."

"Especially now, with the ancient altar of reckoning still active, there is no telling if Reckoning Envoys might descend from the Land Of Truth."

The Lord of Power, Lord of Restraint, and the others still felt a heavy weight in their hearts; they felt no joy at having destroyed the Ancient Hidden Civilization and won this battle.

"Leave a contingent behind to guard the Ancient Hidden Civilization, assess its resources, and treat the wounded before returning to the Heaven Slaying Alliance."

"The Heaven Slaying Ceremony must still be held, and the Heaven Slaying Platform must still be forged."

Yue Mingkong pondered for a moment. Although she had been in a state of slumber and cultivation, that did not mean she was unaware of the current situation in the Vast Expanse.

Before Gu Changge vanished, he had left her quite a few messages.

Now she had stepped forward to take charge of the situation, and no one objected.

"Yes, Mother Kuang."

Gu Changge had always entrusted the crucial task of constructing the Heaven Slaying Platform to Zhuo Fengxie and the others.

Now that Hun Yuanjun was dead, Zhuo Fengxie had no choice but to shoulder the burden alone.

Soon, a large number of powerful figures from the Heaven Slaying Alliance began to withdraw. One ancient warship after another soared into the sky—a dark, overwhelming mass that obscured the sun, endless and vast—carrying vast reserves of resources. They tore through a spacetime tunnel and returned to the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s headquarters.

Following this battle, the balance of power across the Vast Expanse had been settled, and the Heaven Slaying Alliance had undeniably become the strongest faction.

Consequently, when the Ancient Hidden Civilization was liquidated, the vast resources of the Qingtu Civilization were all taken away by the Fatiang Alliance.

All manner of Dao artifacts, divine materials, immortal ores, extraterrestrial ruins, and chaotic oddities—too numerous to count—were swept clean. Even the worlds that had not yet shattered or collapsed were taken away; aside from what could not be carried, almost everything was swept away.

Of course, the super-multiverses that participated in the war—such as the Tathagata Buddha Realm, the Arrow Realm, and the Light and Darkness Divine Realm—also reaped significant benefits. The Ancient Hidden Civilization’s foundations were so profound that everyone could plunder to their heart’s content, more than compensating for the losses incurred in this war.

This was, in a sense, the only piece of good news following the war.

It is worth noting that the Eternal Primordial God, who had previously come to the Ancient Hidden Civilization to intimidate the Ancient Hidden Emperor and others, was imprisoned within a small world, his Dao Fruit sealed—though he was not killed. After the Ancient Hidden Emperor’s demise, many of the sealing runes shattered, and he tore open that small world to step out.

Lord Luo recounted the details of the great battle to the Eternal Primordial God, leaving him deeply moved and sighing with emotion. Though he lamented the immense loss, he also felt fortunate that he had submitted to Gu Changge without hesitation back then; otherwise, his fate might have been the same as that of the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

While the Heaven Slaying Alliance was rapidly plundering the Ancient Hidden Civilization, far away in the depths of the mobile palace and meditation hall where the Lord of Restraint had always resided within the Nine Heavens territory...

BOOM!!!

A vast expanse of nothingness suddenly shattered without a sound, like a mirror that never existed in this world disintegrating in an instant.

Behind it lay a vast, bottomless chasm—its depth unfathomable, its destination unknown—a gaping void that seemed capable of swallowing up all that exists and all that does not.

In the depths of that vast chasm, endless darkness pervaded the air, and from within it emerged an old man decayed to the extreme.

He was emaciated, his eye sockets sunken, his head completely bald, devoid of even a single strand of hair, and his entire being exuded an aura of utter decay.

This stench of decay, however, was unlike that of a rotting sky; rather, it was an ancientness beyond description—an ancientness that had existed since time immemorial, as if it were the very first corpse to have existed since the creation of the heavens and the earth.

……Please support with flowers……

It was as if a being that had been dead for countless ages.

Even a single wisp of that decaying aura was enough to cause the surrounding spacetime and matter to twist and disintegrate, vanishing into nothingness—it could even cause a cultivator’s Dao Fruit to rot away.

"Just as I expected. Will this lead to a new order, or will it all be destroyed and return to the Void, forcing everything to start anew?"

“Leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, are you merely scheming and plotting, or have you truly become entangled with the Heavenly Will?”

The gaunt old man stepped forward and asked in a low murmur.

In his sunken eye sockets, his gaze was profound and luminous, piercing through infinite spacetime to rest upon the depths of the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

Even if the Ancient Hidden Civilization were to perish, the Sea of Reincarnation would still exist, and that boundless Abyss of Heaven would remain.

The celestial light path connecting the Land Of Truth and the Vast Expanse had not vanished either.

On the surface, this battle appeared to be a resounding victory for the Heaven Slaying Alliance, but in reality, they had merely destroyed a dog raised by the Land Of Truth.

With the Heavenly Seal upon him, it was virtually impossible for the Heavenly Lord of Absolute Darkness to perish on this side.

Therefore, this so-called victory, from the perspective of the Land Of Truth, is nothing more than the futile, hoarse roar of a swarm of ants—a sound that Ke would not even hear.

If the Heavenly Lord of Absolute Yin fails to handle the situation, a being far more powerful than him will inevitably descend.

"Unless that passageway is destroyed, but doing so would inevitably cause an even greater upheaval, bringing the Chaos Heaven and Earth Era to a premature end. No, actually, the Chaos Heaven and Earth Era should have already ended; it is merely that the Heavenly Lord of Absolute Darkness has been unable to bring it to a close."

The gaunt old man had been waiting all along, biding his time.

Within the spacetime of the old cosmic order, a prophecy had been left behind: in the future, three Great Ancestors would be born—the Spirit Ancestor, the Earth Ancestor, and the Heavenly Ancestor.

The Vast Expanse has the Heavenly Ancestor, Earth has the Earthly Ancestor, and even the vast and fierce spirits naturally look to the Spirit Ancestor.

News of the Earth Ancestor’s existence has long circulated throughout the Vast Expanse; even beings like the Lord of Power have steadfastly believed that the fragments of relics they obtained originated from the Earth Ancestor.

This included the Lord of Reincarnation and the Lord of the World, who both believed that the shattered green vessel was left behind by the Earth Ancestor, capable of smuggling them back from the Land Of Truth.

The gaunt old man has long been secretly playing the role of the Earth Ancestor, but as Gu Changge discovered when he deduced and captured the truth through Yue Mingkong’s River of Dream Embers back then.

It must be said that the true Earth Ancestor has never once revealed himself.

The gaunt old man was nothing more than a puppet brought forth by the Earth Ancestor.

"Even the Earth Ancestor is wary, daring not to show himself lightly, and has not dared to reveal even the slightest trace of his presence from beginning to end..."

"What kind of existence is truly hidden within the Vast Expanse?"

"What exactly is the Earth Ancestor so afraid of..."

The gaunt old man sighed softly.

Deep within the Nine Heavens, the lake’s surface was calm and shimmering, with wisps of celestial mist drifting about—a truly pleasant sight.

"I didn’t lie to you, did I?"

On a secluded attic with high walls and green tiles, faced with such a question, Wang Zijing fell silent, unsure how to respond.

"Never mind. You wouldn’t believe me anyway, even if I told you I meant you no harm."

The woman reclining on the soft couch across from him gave a faint smile. With a casual wave of her hand, the mist swirling before their eyes dissipated, and the view of the outside world became completely blurred—Wang Mo’er could no longer be seen.

"You really didn’t lie to me. You had no reason to..."

Though Wang Zijing spoke these words, a weight lifted from her heart. After all, the Heaven Slaying Alliance had been founded by Gu Changge. Even if she couldn’t offer much help, she couldn’t stand by and watch it be destroyed.

The outcome of this battle was, after all, a good one.

"Now, the real show is about to begin."

The woman clapped her hands lightly. Before Wang Zijing, the void suddenly split open. A scroll, shimmering with brilliant light and pulsing with boundless spiritual energy, plummeted down and unfolded before her.

Strange and bizarre characters twisted and shifted across the scroll like writhing insects, their movements as enigmatic as the night.

Although Wang Zijing had never seen these characters before, she froze in place at that moment, overcome by an indescribable sense of familiarity.

"What is this?"

she couldn’t help but ask.

“This is something to help you understand yourself. There are some memories you should be able to recall now.”

The woman smiled faintly, then stood up and patted Wang Zijing on the shoulder.

A faint smile also curved her lips; her previously languid expression had vanished, and she now looked exactly like the carefree, unrestrained Wang Zijing of the past.

"You..."

Wang Zijing froze. As she realized what was happening and tried to move, she felt as if a hundred thousand mountains were pressing down on her, leaving her completely immobilized.

“From now on, you’ll study hard right here,” she said, her smile fading as she spoke.

As she spoke, ripples spread before her eyes like waves on a lake. On her snow-white, lotus-root-like bare feet appeared a pair of snow-white, embroidered cloud-patterned clogs. With a graceful stride, she simply walked away.

"That woman—she wouldn’t be planning to take my place and cause trouble, would she?"

Wang Zijing was furious, having vaguely guessed her intentions.

It wasn’t until the woman had vanished that she felt she could move again. Yet the pavilion seemed to be under a spell; no matter which way she turned, she always ended up back where she started, utterly unable to leave.

This left her utterly disheartened, and she could only curse under her breath.
