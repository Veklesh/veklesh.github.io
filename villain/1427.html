Time has flown by, and it has already been nearly a month and a half since Gu Changge returned to the Prosperous Dao True Realm.

During this time, he had found himself with a rare bit of free time and nothing to do; he had long since stopped meddling in the affairs of the Prosperous Dao True Realm.

As for Yin Mei and the others, they had long since stepped back from active management, having found successors long ago. Before Gu Changge’s return, they had been secluded in meditation every day, striving to catch up to his level.

Since his return, they’ve been eager to spend every moment with him.

Even Su Qingge had returned from Qing Shan Village to the Divine Realm to stay by Gu Changge’s side.

Every day, Gu Changge either traveled with them through mountains and rivers or recounted the various cultures, geography, and remnants of Civilization from the Vast Expanse, sharing interesting stories and observations.

This eventually gave him a slight headache, as he didn’t know what else to say. Wang Zijing, however, would often ask him with a smile, hinting around the subject, urging him to admit whether he had met any beauties beyond the Vast Expanse.

For instance, the Holy Maiden of some Civilization, a goddess from the Divine Realm, a princess or empress of the Immortal Court, and so on.

Even Yue Mingkong seemed quite interested in this topic as well. Her beautiful eyes sparkled, the corners of her mouth turned up slightly, and she offered a graceful smile, telling him to speak freely—there was no need to worry about them getting jealous.

Jiang Chuchu’s eyes fluttered as well, gazing at him with a touch of curiosity.

As for Yin Mei and Su Qingge, though they appeared unconcerned, they set down what they were holding, their jade-like ears pricking up slightly, eager to hear what Gu Changge had to say.

Compared to the vast, traditional powers and civilizations beyond, they were far more eager to hear gossip and such.

Gu Changge could annihilate countless universes and erase any spacetime without hesitation, but when faced with this kind of question, he was utterly helpless.

In truth, he harbored little genuine affection for Ling Yuling, Mu Yan, and the others; if anything, he merely used them.

However, saying such things to Yue Mingkong and the others would sound somewhat heartless and cold—after all, they were women, and hadn’t Gu Changge harbored exactly the same thoughts toward them back then?

So, he merely smiled casually and let them guess.

Unsurprisingly, such a dismissive attitude drew a chorus of disapproving glances; even Yue Mingkong couldn’t help but give him a gentle pinch on the waist.

If the members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance were to see Gu Changge like this, they would undoubtedly be wide-eyed and speechless.

In their eyes, Gu Changge had always appeared aloof and inscrutable, as if separated from everyone else by a vast gulf. Even when his expression was gentle, he remained difficult to approach, making it hard for anyone to feel close to him.

Yet when he was with his beloved wife, he was surprisingly warm and natural, shedding all the dignity and air of authority befitting the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, appearing entirely devoted to his wife and daughter.

It was somewhat unbelievable.

But was this truly Gu Changge’s deepest, most genuine sentiment? Perhaps even he himself could not say for certain.

During the time Gu Changge was away from the Prosperous Dao True Realm, aside from Yue Mingkong breaking through to the Dao Realm, Jiang Chuchu, Wang Zijing, and the others remained at the Immortal Emperor realm, still a considerable distance from the Dao Realm.

Yin Mei’s natural talent was not as strong as theirs; though she had always been diligent and hardworking, she remained at the Quasi Immortal Emperor realm, and it was unclear how much longer it would take her to reach the Dao Realm in this lifetime.

Gu Changge did not interfere with them unduly, allowing them to follow their own paths.

After all, even if they were at the Dao Realm now, they couldn’t help him in any way; whether they reached the Dao Realm or not was of no consequence to him.

During this time, Gu Changge took the opportunity to make a trip to Tianlu City. He clearly remembered that Tianlu Xuan Nu had long since broken through to the Immortal Realm. Logically speaking, with the cosmic fortune of the Eight Desolations and Ten Domains bestowed upon her, if she had accumulated enough energy and unleashed it all at once, she should have broken through to the Dao Realm long ago.

Yet, to his perception, Tianlu Xuan Nu had actually been stuck at the Immortal King realm for many years, without making the slightest progress.

Of course, the moment he sensed this, he immediately understood the reason for Tianlu Xuan Nu’s stagnation.

Her constitution was unique, similar to Su Qingge’s Nine Yin Mystic Roar Constitution—it was called the Life-and-Death-Shared Nine Mystic Longing Constitution.

The Nine-Mystic Longing Constitution, a constitution spoken of only in legend, derives from the Red Phoenix of Longing and is a symbol of unwavering loyalty.

The most distinctive feature of this constitution is that it binds them in life and death, making it nearly impossible to break.

In other words, once he had taken Tianlu Xuan Nu’s body, it was as if their lives had become inextricably bound together.

Back when he conquered the Eight Wastes and Ten Realms and captured Tianlu Xuan Nu, she had attempted to use her own constitution to seduce Gu Changge, hoping to bind their lives together and thereby force the Upper Realm to withdraw its troops.

Unfortunately, Gu Changge saw through her scheme at a glance and did not fall for her trap.

This incident even drew a mocking remark from Tianlu Xuan Nu at the time, who called him cowardly. However, in their subsequent interactions, Tianlu Xuan Nu never brought it up again, and Gu Changge naturally let the matter rest.

Though Tianlu Xuan Nu was nominally his woman, in reality, she remained a virgin to this day.

This was also the reason for Tianlu Xuan Nu’s stagnation in cultivation. The very constitution that had once made Gu Changge wary was due to the cold, desolate power it contained—a force capable of assimilating the primordial essence of an Immortal King. When dormant, it was as serene as the Milky Way; yet when unleashed, it was as vast and boundless as the heavens, ready to erupt at any moment.

If the two were to unite, that power would invade his body. Had Tianlu Xuan Nu taken her own life, he—having not yet regained his memories from before his revival—would have been powerless to withstand it.

After all these years, Tianlu Xuan Nu’s cultivation had reached the Immortal King realm, yet this had paradoxically become an obstacle for her. If she could not resolve it, she would likely find it difficult to take the next step.

The moment Gu Changge appeared in Tianlu City, Tianlu Xuan Nu, who was in seclusion within the Tianlu Divine Hall, sensed it instantly.

A hint of surprise flashed across her flawless, jade-like face, and then she smiled.

After all, Gu Changge had made no attempt to conceal his presence.

As he walked, the various restrictions proved as good as nonexistent, unable to stop him. In her quest to break through the Immortal King realm, Tianlu Xuan Nu had long issued a strict order, determined to succeed or die trying.

In truth, she herself did not understand why, with the great era now upon them, fortune surging, and prodigies emerging in endless succession—with so many newly ascended Immortal Kings—

Yet why, with her talent, had she been stuck at the Immortal King realm for so many years, unable to advance?

"It seems you’ve returned to the Prosperous Dao True Realm; you actually have the leisure to pay me a visit."

Tianlu Xuan Nu, clad in a loose, flowing robe, sat cross-legged on a meditation cushion. Her black hair flowed smoothly, her jade-like features flawless and delicate, her skin as tender and snow-white as flower petals.

She opened her beautiful eyes and looked at Gu Changge with interest.

"If I hadn’t come, you’d likely have been stuck at the Immortal King realm for the rest of your life," Gu Changge said, shaking his head slightly.

Tianlu Xuan Nu was taken aback, not understanding the meaning behind his words.

Gu Changge smiled and explained the reason behind her stagnation in cultivation.

After listening, Tianlu Xuan Nu was stunned for a moment—she hadn’t expected this to be the reason. Then, a blush rose to her cheeks, and she glared at Gu Changge, scolding him playfully, “It’s all your fault. Back then, you were so timid—you wouldn’t even let me feed you…”

She recalled the past when she had been defeated by Gu Changge in the Eight Desolations and Ten Realms and taken captive by him.

Back then, she had teased Gu Changge, calling him a proper gentleman with a heart as firm as a rock—unmoved even in the presence of a beauty.

Scene after scene from the past flashed before her eyes. Though so many years had passed, it felt as though it had happened only yesterday.

And yet she was still only at the Immortal King realm.

As for Gu Changge, who knew what realm he had reached? Though he stood right before her, he seemed to exist in a different time and space—vast, profound, boundless, and limitless.

Tianlu Xuan Nu’s gaze suddenly grew complex, and she found herself envying Yue Mingkong and the others. Back then, they had clearly been the younger generation, still a long way from becoming Enlightened Ones.

Yet in the blink of an eye, she had been surpassed by everyone, left stagnating in her old ways.

If she had stayed by Gu Changge’s side all along, might her fate have been different?

"Ah..."

Suddenly, Tianlu Xuan Nu let out a soft gasp, her face flushing even more deeply.

“It’s not too late to eat now.” Gu Changge smiled faintly.

……

Several days later, the clouds dispersed and the rain ceased, and the sound of the stream gradually faded.

Wearing nothing but a thin silk robe, Tianlu Xuan Nu forced her aching body to sit cross-legged on the bed, shrouded in a mist-like veil.

Her entire body glowed; every inch of her skin seemed to exude a delicate fragrance, becoming as lustrous as jade.

Her slender form, her long, flowing hair cascading down, made her appearance utterly captivating.

A vast and torrential power surged within her, as if a boundless universe were being born and evolving.

As expected, she naturally broke through to the Quasi Immortal Emperor realm. With Gu Changge’s assistance, she even managed to condense the radiance of a Quasi Immortal Emperor.

Once this long-accumulated power is fully refined, she should be ready to make a push for the Immortal Emperor realm.

With her talent, once all obstacles and bottlenecks are eliminated, becoming a Dao Realm being is merely a matter of time.

"He just left like that—he could have stayed a little longer."

When Tianlu Xuan Nu had fully stabilized her realm and opened her eyes, Gu Changge was no longer in sight.

She stared blankly for a moment, her eyes growing somber, before letting out a faint sigh.

"I never expected you would even think of keeping me here..."

Suddenly, Gu Changge’s voice, tinged with a hint of a smile, rang in her ears.

He sat on a jade stool in the distance, sipping clear tea leisurely, looking quite at ease.

Tianlu Xuan Nu froze again, then her cheeks flushed slightly as she shot him a sidelong glance.

"I still need your help with my cultivation."

She then composed herself slightly and spoke in a serious tone, but by the time she finished, her ears were already flushed.

The spring breeze knows nothing of sorrow’s taste; it hesitates to speak, then hesitates again.

A few days later, Gu Changge departed.

Tianlu Xuan Nu had no intention of going to Cangmang. To her, Tianlu City was the best place of all; after all, it was the place her master had entrusted her to protect before his passing, and she had always faithfully kept the promise she made to him.

Gu Changge had no intention of forcing her either. Everyone had their own choices to make. Take Su Qingge, for example—even now, she remained deeply resistant to cultivation, and her cultivation level was the lowest of all.

He respected everyone’s choices.

As he was leaving Tianlu City, he noticed that one of his former followers had passed away in meditation several years ago. Gu Changge could no longer recall the follower’s name; he had only learned of this through the discussions of cultivators in the surrounding regions.

That follower had established a powerful clan, and the news of his past association with Gu Changge had spread among the surrounding Daoist factions, becoming common knowledge. As a result, no one dared to underestimate or provoke the clan he had founded.

Gu Changge shook his head slightly, his gaze piercing through the river of time in an instant to glimpse the figure of that follower from many years ago.

He couldn’t help but chuckle wryly.

It turned out that anyone who had stood by his side, marching with the great army he led to conquer the Eight Desolations and Ten Realms, could call themselves his follower.

With a flick of his figure, Gu Changge vanished from the scene.

With matters in the Prosperous Dao True Realm settled, it was time for him to set his sights on his final plan. Although he could not be certain whether the other two Ancestors were also plotting and making preparations, Gu Changge was absolutely certain that another entity was interfering with the vast and turbulent situation—and had been laying the groundwork for a very long time.

In fact, this interference could be traced back to the very dawn of creation.

Before leaving the Prosperous Dao True Realm, Gu Changge intended to take the entire Divine Kingdom with him and, while he was at it, remove the celestial omens that shrouded the Prosperous Dao True Realm. He planned to integrate the Prosperous Dao True Realm into the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

However, just as he was about to begin transporting the Divine Realm, news suddenly arrived from Yue Mingkong, informing him of something.

"The former Empress Xiyao of the Demon Realm passed away in meditation hundreds of years ago. She left a letter, instructing a trusted lady-in-waiting to deliver it to you personally." Upon hearing this news, Gu Changge was indeed momentarily taken aback. Then, after giving it some thought, the image of a noble woman dressed in a magnificent phoenix robe, with a touch of red makeup on her brow, emerged in his mind.

Empress Xiyao?

When was the last time they met? It seemed to have been on the day of his wedding to Yue Mingkong.

Gu Changge’s thoughts swept across the entire Prosperous Dao True Realm in an instant, yet he detected no trace of her presence.

The next moment, he returned to the Divine Realm, where Yue Mingkong personally handed him the unopened letter.

Standing nearby was a woman with an air of scholarly refinement and a gentle, graceful countenance—none other than Song Youwei, the trusted lady-in-waiting of Empress Xiyao and the daughter of the former Grand Tutor of the Demon Realm.

“Greetings, Alliance Leader…” Song Youwei said, bowing respectfully.

Back when Gu Changge first arrived in the Demon Realm, she had looked up to him as an unattainable figure; now, he stood at a height beyond their wildest imagination.

Gu Changge nodded slightly, took the letter, opened it, and scanned its contents.

"I was born before you, and by the time you were born, I was already old; I regret we were not born at the same time..."

In a daze, he saw that woman in phoenix-patterned robes, sitting before her desk, her dark brows slightly furrowed, her lower lip gently bitten, deep in thought.

The Demon Realm had once been in chaos. Though she held the exalted title of Empress and appeared ruthless and merciless, she was, in truth, simple-minded and naive.

Had it not been for the deterrent and suppression provided by her mother, the Demon Emperor Youyue, her rule would likely have been overthrown long ago by the great demons causing chaos everywhere.

Even when a former enemy approached right before her eyes, intending to assassinate her, she failed to notice...

A multitude of thoughts flashed through Gu Changge’s mind, eventually coalescing into a single question: Did she know that I merely viewed her as a tool?

To harbor such longing and admiration yet receive no response—what was the point of such a wait?

Yue Mingkong tactfully took her leave and did not linger. She knew Song Youwei likely had something to say to Gu Changge—or, more precisely, to convey a message from Empress Xiyao.

“How did she pass away?” Gu Changge put the letter away and asked aloud.
