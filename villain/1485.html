Above the Ancient Hidden Civilization, endless spatial-temporal turbulence once surged like the tides.

But now, everything has come to a standstill. That colossal, majestic aura—one that no world could contain—has manifested there, as if suppressing all paths, striking fear into all ages past and present, and bringing an end to all time and space.

The entire Vast Expanse seemed to have fallen into a deathly silence at that moment. All cultivators and sentient beings within the Ancient Hidden Civilization were filled with extreme terror, as if crushed by the will of heaven and earth, utterly unable to move.

The moment those words were spoken, both the Ancient Treasure Emperor and the Primordial Void—who watched quietly from afar—felt their hearts skip a beat.

"This is going to be difficult. When did the Ji Chan clan become involved with this one…?" The Ancient Treasure Emperor’s brow furrowed even deeper; he had not anticipated this turn of events at all.

The rulers of the Five Great Royal Clans also showed signs of unease. Instantly, they began to use their abilities to analyze the situation, trying to determine whether this was true or false. Why had no one previously detected the connection between the Ji Chan clan’s Ninth Princess and that individual?

Yet no matter how they probed and analyzed, the entire chain of causality seemed shrouded in an invisible fog.

Only an indescribable, profound sense of dread filled their hearts.

"Sovereign, this matter is likely to be difficult to handle. The Great Calamity Reckoning is imminent, and the High Priestess holds a very special status—we cannot afford to lose her. We need her to communicate with the other side. If the Reckoning Envoy arrives and discovers that the High Priestess has already perished, the entire Great Calamity Reckoning may be affected."

"No matter what, we must find a way to save the High Priestess. She cannot perish here." They communicated through their thoughts.

Hearing this exchange, Emperor Gu Cang’s features blurred, and the expression beneath his Pingtian Crown shifted several times.

He dared not act rashly. Originally, he had assumed the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance possessed strength comparable to their own—certainly not much greater—but now he felt as insignificant as a mortal facing towering mountains or bottomless abysses.

At that moment, only above the Sea of Reincarnation did Xiao Wangyue’s wailing cries ring out, as if she were pouring out every grievance and heartache she had endured over the years.

The High Priest, clenched within the golden palm that spanned the heavens and earth, looked terrified and frightened, his entire body trembling. He had never imagined that Qingyi, the Ninth Princess of the Ji Chan clan, would be connected to this man.

Had she known all this, how could she have dared to treat Qingyi—and the Ji Chan clan—in such a manner?

But at this moment, no amount of words could make a difference; even if she regretted it deeply, it was useless.

The High Priestess could only pin her last, faint hope on the Ancient Treasure Emperor. As the High Priestess of the Temple of Sacrifice, her divine authority was bestowed by heaven; she represented the supreme will of the heavens and served as the pivotal link to the Realm of Truth. She could not die here.

"Emperor Gucang, save me..."

In that moment, an endless will to survive surged through the High Priestess. She struggled with all her might, but as the golden hand slowly closed in, her limbs twisted and shattered, disintegrating into a sand-like substance that began to seep through the cracks and drift downward.

"Does he dare to save you?"

Gu Changge’s figure descended calmly above the Sea of Reincarnation.

He said nothing, nor did he pay any heed to the Ancient Treasure Emperor or the rulers of the Five Great Royal Clans. He simply gazed at the woman in green, who was silently weeping, then reached out to gently wipe away the glistening tears from the corner of her eye.

The woman in green reached out to grasp that warm, familiar hand and whispered, “I really never expected…”

“I’ll explain everything to you later.”

Gu Changge said. Immediately, a vast, boundless, and indescribable aura surged and erupted within Qingyi, instantly shattering the restraints and shackles binding her, reducing them to a shower of ash.

However, the ashes did not vanish completely. Instead, they swirled in midair for a moment before descending to wrap around the High Priestess, like one barbed thorn after another, piercing brutally into her flesh.

At this level of cultivation, the concept of a physical body should have long since ceased to exist. Yet, under Gu Changge’s mental command, the High Priestess manifested a tangible form of flesh and blood. She let out a terrified cry of agony; her soul’s sensitivity was now a billion times keener than before. Such excruciating pain threatened to shatter her spirit, making death seem preferable to life.

At that moment, every member of the Ancient Hidden Civilization felt this hair-raising, soul-shaking wail of agony—as if suffering torment in the Avici Hell. Merely hearing the sound was enough to shatter one’s spirit and cause it to explode.

The rulers of the Five Great Royal Clans all showed signs of distress, their expressions shifting with shock. The High Priest, like them, was a figure at the pinnacle of power, yet in Gu Changge’s hands, he was no better than a tottering child—merely an ant to be toyed with at will, to be killed however she pleased.

Even Qingyi, watching this scene, showed a hint of reluctance. Kind-hearted and compassionate, she felt that such torment would be better off ended by death.

The fact that she was suffering torment and punishment in the Sea of Reincarnation was, in truth, the consequence of her having evaded her destined fate long ago—a choice that had brought divine retribution upon the Ji Chan clan. It was a punishment she had brought upon herself.

However, the High Priestess’s treatment of her was indeed driven by some personal grudge.

“That vicious woman has finally met her comeuppance. She bullied my sister for so many years, finding every possible way to torment her…” Xiao Wangyue wiped away her tears, her face brimming with satisfaction.

Ji Ziyi looked at Qingyi and said softly, “Xiao Jiu, you needn’t feel any sympathy for her. The High Priestess has always been trying to sacrifice our clan to gain more benefits. If she hadn’t spoken up to the heavens during the ritual back then, our eldest sister wouldn’t have ended up like this.”

"Ahhh..."

"Kill me, kill me..."

"Emperor of the Ancient Treasury, kill me, kill me..."

The golden hand slowly dissipated, but the High Priestess within it twisted into a contorted mass as her physical form disintegrated.Thorn after thorn pierced through her, as if pinning her to the void. At this moment, she possessed none of the noble detachment befitting the High Priestess of the Ancient Hidden Civilization; instead, she was a madwoman trapped between life and death, plunged into delirium. She frantically tore at her own flesh and her own head, her body covered in horrific bloodstains.

This scene sent a shudder through the hearts of the Ancient Zang Emperor, the rulers of the Five Great Royal Houses, and the ancient beings watching from distant realms of time and space. This was a figure of unparalleled magnitude—one who might not appear even once in thousands of millennia throughout all of history.

"I will not kill you. Killing you would be a form of release for you. There are countless ways in this world to make your life a living hell, to make you regret ever having been born."

With a gentle clench of his hand, Gu Changge caused the boundless expanse of the Sea of Reincarnation to split open. From within, a torrent of dense world energy surged forth, instantly coalescing into a tiny bubble that engulfed and enveloped the High Priest.

In the blink of an eye, that tiny bubble devoured the vast power of the world, transforming into a complete universe. Within it, mountains, rivers, lands, oceans, and continents took shape, along with countless suns, moons, and stars evolving to form an ever-changing cosmos.

This universe, resembling an eggshell, fell into Gu Changge’s hands. Within it, the flow of time was entirely different from the Vast Expanse; in the span of a single breath, millions of years passed. Endless torment was inflicted upon the High Priestess, causing her immense agony. Her wails of despair echoed through the crystalline walls of the world, sending a chill down everyone’s spine.

Though only a single breath had passed in the outside world, she had endured tens of millions of years of torment within it.

Even the rulers of the Five Great Royal Clans trembled and instinctively took several steps back, gazing at Gu Changge as though she were a monster.

To casually create a world and control every aspect of its matter, time, space, destiny, and origin—such power far exceeded their imagination. To describe him as a Creator would be no exaggeration.

Moreover, a world capable of containing Lu Jin’s existence had already surpassed the realm of a Supreme True Realm. In other words… Gu Changge could now effortlessly create a Supreme True Realm and arbitrarily alter its rules and everything within it.

The Ancient Hidden Civilization fell into a deathly silence. At this moment, even the Lord of Reincarnation and the Lord of the World—who had been quietly observing the situation without revealing themselves—fell silent.

The Ruler of the World, who held the authority of creation, stared intently at Gu Changge’s palm. Yet no matter how hard he looked, he could not fathom how all that had been accomplished; it was utterly beyond his comprehension. Even if he had witnessed it, he could not have imagined how it was achieved.

This had already surpassed his comprehension and understanding. In that moment, he felt like a toddler trying to grasp the teachings of a learned scholar, yet unable to grasp even the simplest of principles.

Pfft…

The World Ruler suddenly spat out a substance resembling blood—golden and radiant, imbued with dense worldly essence. This substance flowed simultaneously from all seven of his orifices.

"I don’t understand it, but I can confirm that this is indeed a supreme method of creation—one that is beyond the comprehension of those at our level. Perhaps this is precisely the method he uses to create those extraordinary encounters and transformations, from the void into reality..." he said in a low voice.

The Lord of Reincarnation’s expression shifted as well, as if he were trying to discern the nature of Gu Changge’s technique, hoping to glean some truths about the path ahead.

"Fellow Daoist..."

"Your power surpasses ours; we would not be so presumptuous as to attack you. Though we do not know how you reached this stage, the Great Calamity is about to descend. When the heavens and earth undergo great upheaval, the long-dormant Heavenly Will will awaken. No matter how powerful you are, you cannot stand against the Heavenly Will—let alone the even more inconceivable, unimaginable beings that dwell within the Realm of Truth."

"The High Priestess was formed by the Mandate of Heaven; her imperial authority is divinely bestowed. Treating her this way will only invite boundless calamities."

The Sovereign of the Ancient Hidden Civilization finally spoke again, his voice thick with apprehension.

"The Great Calamity is about to descend. Logically speaking, I would not harm the Ancient Hidden Civilization, but since I have come, you must all submit to me."

"As for the Heavenly Will you speak of, it is nothing special in my eyes. If I wish to defy the Heavenly Dao, what of it? I can crush it with a single flick of my wrist." Gu Changge’s expression remained casual, as if he were describing something trivial and simple.

His words caused the expressions of the Ancient Zang Emperor and the others to shift.

“Are you determined to take action against the Ancient Hidden Civilization? Though you are powerful, expecting us to submit so easily is utterly impossible. From the very moment of its birth, the Ancient Hidden Civilization has been loyal to the Realm of Truth; no force has ever existed capable of making it submit,” the Ancient Hidden Civilization Emperor said in a deep voice.

“That is because I did not exist before. If I had, the Ancient Hidden Civilization would naturally have submitted.” Gu Changge seemed to smile as he said, “I do not wish to cause needless bloodshed. In the Heaven Slaying plan, you all have much to contribute. Few have reached the end of the path; every one of you is qualified to charge into battle.”

“You…”

The Ancient Tibetan Emperor’s expression shifted repeatedly—flashes of anger flashed across his face, but he quickly regained his composure. His eyes were deep and abyssal, seemingly devoid of any emotional ripples.

In Gu Changge’s words, they—these beings standing at the very pinnacle of the Vast Expanse—were merely deemed worthy of charging into battle. Did this mean he intended to treat them as cannon fodder?

Though the rulers of the Five Great Royal Clans had long since shed all traces of emotion, Gu Changge’s words now stirred violent ripples within their hearts.

“Are you determined to act, Fellow Daoist? To overturn heaven and earth, to destroy countless spacetime universes? You have likely underestimated the boundless depths of the Ancient Hidden Civilization, accumulated since time immemorial,” the Ancient Hidden Emperor said in a deep voice. As his thoughts shifted, he began to contact the Lord of Reincarnation deep within the Realm of Reincarnation.

He knew the Lord of Reincarnation must be observing all this. If Gu Changge was determined to confront the Ancient Hidden Civilization, then both he and the Lord of Reincarnation would have to resist; they could not allow him to act unchecked and cause boundless turmoil.

In a sense, the Ancient Hidden Civilization served as guardians and custodians of order amidst the Vast Expanse. They were like farmers tending the orchard for a noble lord. Of course, from the perspective of the Realm of Truth, they were far less than farmers—at best, they were merely dogs chained to the ground.

But so what? Even as dogs, within the Vast Expanse, they stand at the very pinnacle, overlooking the succession of eras and the birth and death of countless ages—perched at the very apex of the pyramid.

“I now understand the current state of the Ancient Hidden Civilization. You need not summon anyone; even if they were to appear, they would not dare speak in my presence.”

Gu Changge seemed to chuckle; in that moment, his gaze appeared to pierce through the entire Ancient Hidden Civilization.

As he gently raised his palm, the void before him split open once more. In the next instant, it seemed as though countless dimensions and realms of time and space were tearing apart. Deep within that void lay majestic mountain ranges, scattered islands, and sacred sanctuaries—a scene of unimaginable grandeur.countless figures flickered—numbering in the tens of millions—with various halls standing tall, sacred beyond measure, as billions of divine talismans erupted in divine light.

Clearly, this was a Daoist sanctuary established in an unknown realm of time and space, filled with an aura of boundless depth that firmly concealed this expanse.

But as his massive hand reached in, that expanse of spacetime began to crumble and disintegrate layer by layer. Above the heavens, a colossal, boundless palm slowly expanded; in the blink of an eye, it obscured the entire spacetime and the heavens and earth.

All living beings and cultivators were filled with terror; they roared and charged upward in defiance, but it was utterly futile. They were like fireflies flocking toward ancient mountains, exploding one after another with a series of pops.

In the deepest recesses of that majestic hall, a crack suddenly appeared. A figure, filled with terror, shot out, attempting to tear through space and time to escape.

His features were refined and scholarly, adorned with a tall crown and wide sash, exuding an air of ancient nobility. Yet now he was like a frantic fly, darting wildly in all directions, desperate to break free from the grip of that boundless giant hand.

But clearly, under the shadow of that giant hand, time, space, the laws of the universe, and all matter had come to a standstill; nothing could move anymore. He could only watch as the hand descended from above, just as it had seized the High Priest moments before, grabbing him in a firm grip—leaving him with no means of escape.

"Master of Fate..."

Witnessing this scene, the Primordial Void, hiding in a distant realm of time and space, could not help but tremble slightly. His expression shifted, and without a moment’s hesitation, he turned and fled.

However, Gu Changge seemed to merely glance in his direction before withdrawing his gaze, showing no intention of taking further action.

At that moment, the entire Ancient Hidden Civilization fell into a deathly silence once more. The Emperor of Ancient Hidden Civilization and the rulers of the Five Great Royal Clans all fell silent. The Disaster Lord, who had engaged in numerous conversations with the Master of Destiny—discussions that touched upon the web of cause and effect—trembled slightly, a profound terror enveloping his heart.

Even while hiding deep within the Ancient Hidden Civilization, concealed within countless layers of time and space, the Master of Destiny was still instantly exposed, utterly powerless to resist.

This was a figure who had been laying plans for untold ages, leaving behind countless contingency measures in the Vast Expanse of Time. Behind the collapse of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court, the birth of the Xian Chu Vast Lands, the Great Yang Dynasty, the Wei Yang Immortal Dynasty, the Ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao, and countless other dynasties—his shadow loomed.

Yet now, he was like a fly trapped within the Five Finger Mountain, able only to flail about aimlessly.

The Master of Destiny had finally succumbed to despair. Although he had long suspected that Gu Changge had intentionally let him escape from the Nightmare Demon Realm, using him as some sort of bait, he had always believed he still had hope—that he could make a comeback in the future and eventually stand a chance against Gu Changge.

But it wasn’t until today that he finally understood: the gap between them was like that of a powerless mortal facing an immortal who could soar through the heavens and traverse the earth.

So what if he’d reached the end of the road? A single thought was enough to crush him at will.
