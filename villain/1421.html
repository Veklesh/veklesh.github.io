In ancient times, the black lotus was regarded as a symbol of the end of the world, yet the followers of the Heaven-Substituting Sect once revered it as a sacred object.

The emergence of the Black Lotus Cult was neither an accident nor a coincidence.

Gu Changge could also guess the reason behind the Black Lotus Cult’s sudden emergence: it was nothing more than an attempt to provoke him into action and force him to reveal himself.

For the Black Lotus Cult does indeed have intricate ties to the Dark Clans.

By setting this trap, they were counting on Gu Changge to be wary of the Black Lotus Sect’s ties to the Dark Clans.

Knowing full well that his own identity might be exposed, it was highly unlikely he would send his subordinates to investigate.

Since Chu Xiao and his men had long served under him, they were bound to be quite familiar with Gu Changge.

Therefore, while investigating the Black Lotus Sect, Chu Xiao and his men would likely realize that the Lord of the Black Lotus, whom the sect worships, actually has deep ties to Gu Changge—and might even be Gu Changge himself.

This would, in turn, significantly increase the risk of Gu Changge’s identity being exposed.

Consequently, given these various considerations, there is a high probability that Gu Changge would choose to personally intervene to eliminate the Black Lotus Sect as a source of trouble.

The person who set this trap had precisely these factors in mind, which is why they had Yao Yao relay the news about the Black Lotus Sect back to the Heaven Slaying Alliance at this very moment.

It could also be said that the mastermind behind this scheme possesses an unparalleled “understanding” of Gu Changge’s background and his objectives.

Thus, link by link, piece by piece, this scheme was laid out with such seamless precision and meticulous care.

This even made Gu Changge unable to help but offer a word of praise.

Furthermore, fearing that Gu Changge might not connect the "880" to the Black Lotus Sect, the mastermind had the Black Lotus Sect roam and make their presence known near the borders of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, only to then have Yao Yao "naturally" notice them:

Only Yao Yao remained completely unaware, oblivious to the fact that, unbeknownst to her, she had long since become a pawn in someone else’s game.

Although she possessed the life-saving jade talisman Gu Changge had left her, that didn’t mean she could escape the clutches of Dao Realm beings time and again after witnessing the Black Lotus Sect’s sacrificial ritual with her own eyes.

Once or twice might be acceptable, but three or four times—that couldn’t simply be attributed to good luck.

At this point, anyone with a sharp mind would sense something was amiss and begin to suspect that someone was pulling the strings behind the scenes.

Unfortunately, Yao Yao, a young girl who had only just entered the world, was completely oblivious to this. She was even smug about her good fortune and was thinking about how to take credit for it in front of Gu Changge.

"If it were that sharp-witted Gu Xian’er, she’d have noticed something was off long ago and started figuring out how to expose the mastermind behind it all…"

“Yao Yao is still a bit too green.”

Gu Changge shook his head slightly.

He didn’t intend to blame Yao Yao, though. After all, whatever she’d done was out of concern for him, a desire to help him overcome his troubles.

Besides, he had actually anticipated this trap set by the Black Lotus Sect long ago; he just wasn’t sure what methods they would use to test him.

It seemed those bastards were far more cautious than he’d imagined.

Gu Changge had deliberately let the Tian Shuai and Shao Yin factions know of his reappearance in the mortal realm, and had made it abundantly clear to them that he was no longer at his peak—his power had been greatly diminished. He was no longer the Ruler of Heaven who once struck terror into the hearts of all races across the cosmos.

Now, he dares not even reveal his true identity to the world.

He could only establish the Heaven Slaying Alliance, gradually devouring the various factions, secretly rebuilding his foundations in the shadows. He even went so far as to allow the Heavenly Host to mingle among the masses, drawing the attention of all races across the multiverse to the dark source where the Ruler of Heaven was sealed, thereby allowing him to recover in peace and wait for his return to the pinnacle.

Unfortunately, even during this period when he is at his “weakest in history,” those fellows remain

remained as cautious as ever. Even when testing the waters, they weighed every move against past lessons, ensuring their plans were flawless.

This gave Gu Changge a bit of a headache; the fish were so cunning that no matter how he baited them, they would never take the hook lightly.

Although breaking this deadlock would be a breeze for him now, Gu Changge had no intention of getting involved.

He wanted to see what other tricks those fellows would come up with and who, in the end, could keep their cool.

With the founding of the Immortal Dao Alliance just around the corner, a good show is about to begin.

He wasn’t the least bit anxious; he was simply waiting to see if those fellows could keep their composure, or if they’d be too impatient to start floundering.

"However, there’s another possibility: might they assume that I’m choosing to ignore this because I’ve realized it’s a trap specifically set for me—that this is a sign of guilt or a lack of confidence…"

"If that’s the case, things would get much more interesting." Gu Changge smiled suddenly at the thought.

He didn’t rule out the possibility that some of the fools among them might have such thoughts.

Of course, he also couldn’t rule out the possibility that this was a deliberate tactic designed to mislead him, making him believe they were foolish.

Moreover, if he thought about it more deeply, anyone with the courage and cunning to set a trap for him would certainly have considered and taken into account numerous possibilities.

"If it’s Shizu Xu, he’s meticulous and plans before acting. It’s unlikely he’d use a tactic that appears flawless on the surface but is riddled with flaws to deal with me—or perhaps this is merely a façade designed to mislead me…"

Gu Changge’s brow suddenly furrowed. Within his deep, piercing eyes, a myriad of scenes flashed by—past, future, and present; various timelines; the Three Thousand Daoist Paths; countless mystical arts; and the profound truths of all worlds—all passing before his eyes in an instant.

He sensed that he might have overlooked something. Lately, with everything under his control, he had grown somewhat complacent and careless.

Had he not suddenly performed a calculation just now, he might very well have suffered a setback due to his complacency.

"Ever since regaining those memories, I’ve lost my former habit of planning before acting. I’ve begun to grow arrogant and conceited, looking down on all living beings in the world. This is not a good thing," Gu Changge murmured, letting out a soft sigh.

Whether as the Ruler of Heaven, the Lord of Demons, or his current self, they were all him.

It was simply that each identity brought with it different memories and experiences. Coupled with the restoration of those sealed memories, it was easy for his original habits, conduct, and way of thinking to shift.

Had it not been for that period of living as a mortal in Qing Shan Village alongside Su Qingge, he would likely have long since been overpowered and consumed by his “heavenly” nature.

If the Ancestor Xu were to deal with him, he would certainly have sought to first neutralize the old faction of the "Ruler of Heaven" while setting the trap.

Since he founded the Heaven's Proxy, the Ancestor Xu has been a “veteran” at his side for a long time, thoroughly familiar with his conduct and character.

The Ancestor Xu believed he would commit the sin of arrogance, underestimating the many matters entangled behind the Black Lotus Sect, and it was even less likely that he would personally intervene to resolve the situation after realizing it was a trap set against him.

Therefore, this was not actually a trap set against him, but rather a trap set against the old faction of the Da Tian—that is, the Heavenly Host now led by Wuyue.

Meanwhile, having left the courtyard where Gu Changge usually lived in seclusion to cultivate, Yao Yao’s expression was tinged with a hint of disappointment and despondency.

She was still wondering if she had angered Gu Changge, or if her casual use of the life-saving jade talisman that day had deeply disappointed him.

Even when she returned to the side hall where Jiang Weiyang and the others were waiting for her, she still hadn’t snapped out of it.

“Yao Yao, what’s wrong? You seem distracted.”

It was Jiang Weiyang’s slightly puzzled inquiry that suddenly snapped Yao Yao back to reality.

“Ah, nothing. I was just lost in thought for a moment.” Yao Yao forced a bright, radiant smile and shook her head.

Jiang Weiyang didn’t press the matter. She nodded, then asked with a mix of anticipation and nervousness, “So, Yao Yao, did you mention my situation to the Alliance Leader? How did he

respond?"

Hearing this, Yao Yao was taken aback at first, then realized what was being asked. Her expression immediately turned awkward and embarrassed, and she hurriedly explained,

“When I went to see my Master just now, he was busy and had no time for anything else. I only saw him briefly before he told me to go rest. I didn’t even get a chance to mention your matter to him. I’m truly sorry.”

Ever since the day they met, Jiang Weiyang had been asking Tian Tian to help her secure an audience with the Alliance Leader, Gu Changge.

Yao Yao had agreed at the time, saying she would put in a good word for her with Gu Changge, but whether Gu Changge would see her was not something she could decide.

Jiang Weiyang was delighted and grateful.

And that was precisely why she had followed Yao Yao all the way to the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

However, Yao Yao had completely forgotten about this matter. Just now, in front of Gu Changge, she had been so preoccupied with discussing matters related to the Black Lotus Sect that she hadn’t mentioned a single word about Jiang Weiyang. It wasn’t that she had done so on purpose; she had genuinely forgotten…

Especially after Gu Changge had given her the hint to leave, her mind was filled with worry and turmoil—how could she possibly remember the favor Jiang Weiyang had asked of her?

"It’s nothing."

“The Alliance Leader has countless matters to attend to. Governing and managing the vast Heaven Slaying Alliance is certainly no easy task; it’s only natural that she’s so busy.” Jiang Weiyang also froze for a moment, and the look of anticipation on her face immediately dimmed. However, she quickly recovered, flashing a bright, radiant smile that was both charming and captivating, indicating her understanding.

“I’ll pay my respects to the Master again tomorrow morning and ask about this matter then.” Yao Yao nodded and let out a sigh of relief.

Zhu Fu Yi watched the scene and couldn’t help but let out a soft sigh, feeling an indescribable sense of restlessness and gloom in his heart.

He had been following closely behind Jiang Weiyang the entire time, observing the expressions on her face—from the initial anticipation, nervousness, and anxiety, as she clutched the hem of her skirt.

Then, even when she couldn’t see, she stood on tiptoe to peer ahead, unable to wait.

And now, to the disappointment, resignation, and acceptance.

Wasn’t this exactly how he had felt when he first invited Jiang Weiyang to join him on a trip? The nervousness and anticipation at the start of the invitation, followed by the melancholy and disappointment after being rejected, and then the self-reassurance and sense of release that came with coming to terms with it all.

This process was truly identical, except that now, the cold and aloof goddess—once as distant and unattainable as the moon in the sky—had been crushed into the mud, no longer transcendent.

Zhu Fu Yi’s heart was filled with a complex mix of emotions.

He chose to follow Jiang Weiyang without regret, serving as her groom and protecting her safety—largely because he believed that proximity offered the best chance to succeed.

After all, the Emperor of Weiyang was actively seeking a husband for Jiang Weiyang. If he could win her favor, he would undoubtedly gain a significant advantage in the future.

The arranged marriage between Jiang Weiyang and the Ninth Prince of the Ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao was not set in stone.

Unfortunately, during this time, Jiang Weiyang spent most of her time inside the carriage, rarely speaking to him. Even when they did converse, it was only to express her gratitude, followed by her attempts to persuade him to turn back and not get caught up in this whirlwind.

On the contrary, after the woman in red appeared, the person Jiang Weiyang spoke of and inquired about most frequently was the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

She harbored profound admiration, reverence, and curiosity toward the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance—a figure of mysterious origins and identity, whose power was unfathomable, looming like a terrifying mountain over the vast, boundless realms.

She would often seek out Yao Yao on her own to ask about matters concerning the Alliance Leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, and their conversations would last all day and all night.

Zhu Fuyi drove the carriage, listening to their laughter and conversation, and hearing Jiang Weiyang’s incessant questions about another man, feeling an indescribable mix of irritation and turmoil in his heart.

Of course, he knew that as a minor figure like himself—a mere 5.7—he had no right to feel jealousy or envy toward the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance. Not even his grandfather, or the Emperor of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, possessed such a right.

Yet this emotion still grew uncontrollably within him.

"The leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance must be a figure far older and more powerful than the Emperor of Weiyang.

In his eyes, whether it’s the princess or the Emperor of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, they’re nothing more than a bunch of juniors. I’m just getting too caught up in appearances and overthinking things…”

Zhu Fu Yi could only repeat these words to himself over and over in his heart, trying to comfort himself.

Afterward, Yao Yao led Jiang Weiyang and the others to rest. Many members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance had noticed this young woman wearing a gauze hood, speculating about her identity; yet, out of deference to Yao Yao, no one dared to approach and ask.

As for the carriage holding the Black Lotus Sect’s Holy Child, Yao Yao had assigned men to guard it strictly, forbidding anyone from approaching—an order issued in Gu Changge’s name.

As a result, not even the high-ranking members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance dared to lift the black cover to investigate what lay within the carriage.

Early the next morning, Yao Yao went to the courtyard where Gu Changge usually rested, intending to request an audience with him to inform him of the matter concerning Jiang Weiyang.

However, to her great surprise, Gu Changge—who rarely ever left the courtyard—was nowhere to be found.

The high-ranking members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance were also completely unaware of this.

Ling Yuling had long since grown accustomed to Gu Changge’s elusiveness. Yao Yao sought her out, and she was still pondering the matter of Ji Hanjing, wondering what exactly Gu Changge had said to her in the end.
