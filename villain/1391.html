The beautiful woman in white standing beside her saw this scene, and a flicker of surprise and awe passed through her eyes.

She had long heard of the eerie nature of the Tian Shuai Sect; it was said to be the most mysterious of the Eight Subordinate Hosts.

The Tian Shuai Realm is formless and trace-less, devoid of desire and color; it is the boundless void of heaven, the truly boundless heaven, where sentient beings cannot be sought or seen.

Even if one knew the entrance to the Realm of Celestial Decline, no living being could ever set foot within it, for what one sees and hears might all be illusions—or perhaps, what was real just a moment ago could vanish into a phantom in the next.

"Sacrificial Priestess Qiluo, you flatter me. Such a simple illusion cannot deceive your eyes."

"Ever since the Celestial Division fell apart, the ancient Eight Divisions no longer exist. No wonder they dared to discuss such a risky endeavor with me…"

Qi Luo the Priestess laughed as well, her alluring expression vanishing completely.

Her eyes were as clear and pure as crystal as she fixed her gaze on Wuxuzi, as if she could see right through his soul.

“Priestess Qiluo, you flatter me. The Celestial Decline Clan is merely struggling to survive in the cracks between the great powers. Without some cunning, we would likely have been hunted down and annihilated by the Heavenly Host long ago.”

Wuxuzi gave a faint smile—"217"—and without further words, gestured for her to follow.

Priestess Qiluo’s eyes flashed with intensity. She cast a fleeting glance at the beautiful woman in white beside her and said, “Liu Yun, wait for me outside.”

“Yes, Priestess Qiluo,” the white-robed beauty replied respectfully, bowing her head.

As her words fell, Priestess Qiluo moved with the grace of a lotus, trailing a faint, delicate fragrance as she walked straight toward the foam-like, ethereal substance before her, seemingly unconcerned about any danger within.

Soon, ripples spread out one after another. The foam-like substance shifted and shattered, engulfing and swallowing Priestess Qiluo whole.

Xu Wuzi followed closely behind, vanishing in an instant.

As the ripples faded, calm returned to the scene. The desolate mountain and dilapidated temple reappeared, and the events of moments ago seemed like nothing more than an illusion.

A fleeting flicker of surprise crossed Liu Yun’s lowered gaze, but he remained obediently and respectfully standing there.

She glanced at the villager beside her, who had long since been scared into a daze. After a moment’s thought, she reached out and erased his memory of the incident, then transported him directly to the foot of a mountain hundreds of miles away.

The Heavenly Host and the Eight Subordinate Hosts have always been at odds.

Having inherited the vast majority of the Heavenly Host’s heritage, the Heavenly Host has long regarded itself as the rightful successor of Da Tian. Over the centuries, they have repeatedly sought to eradicate the Eight Subordinate Hosts, exterminating them without mercy.

Qi Luo, the Fourth Priestess of the Heavenly Host, had actually come alone to the entrance of the Celestial Decline Realm at this very moment and entered it.

If one were to claim she had no connection whatsoever to the Heavenly Decline Sect, Liu Yun would not believe it.

Yet Priestess Qiluo displayed all this before her with such composure and boldness, seemingly unafraid of being discovered.

Liu Yun didn’t know whether this was a deliberate test by Priestess Qiluo, or if she simply didn’t care, had no qualms, and didn’t give a thought to the consequences of being discovered.

Did Priestess Qiluo suspect her identity? Did she suspect that she had been sent by the High Priest to spy on her?

Thinking of this, Liu Yun bowed her head even lower.

On the surface, the Heavenly Host appeared to be under the control of the Grand Priestess, but in reality, it was rife with undercurrents and intrigue; every priestess represented different factions and interests.

Even the High Priest’s closest confidants—those few chief priests—might not necessarily stand entirely by his side.

This time, the High Priest, acting on his own, forcibly unleashed the Black Tide upheaval, wreaking havoc across the realms, and insisted on holding the Grand Ritual—a move that had already infringed upon the private interests of many priests.

Were it not for the High Priest’s formidable authority and the deep-seated fear he inspires among the Heavenly Host, who dare not voice any grievances or dissent, this matter would likely have already sparked a backlash.

As far as Liu Yun knows, the reason the Righteous Unity Alliance and the Heavenly Host are signing a peace treaty this time is precisely because several high-ranking priests stepped forward forcefully to negotiate with the Righteous Unity Alliance’s leadership.

That is why this situation has come to pass.

In truth, there are many priests within the Heavenly Host who are unwilling to reveal themselves to the world prematurely at this time, let alone instigate a Black Tide uprising that would sweep across the vast expanse.

If this matter goes too far, the Heavenly Host will also face a united front of resistance and opposition, paying a tremendous amount of effort

consequences.

Although the Heavenly Host has regained much of its former vigor in recent years, it still cannot compare to the "Representative of Heaven" of its heyday.

Despite the Heavenly Host’s formidable strength and its ability to intimidate the various races and civilizations of the Vast Expanse, the premature launch of the Grand Ritual has already depleted much of its previous reserves and resources.

The factions backing the various priests have also paid a heavy price for this.

After all, the counterattacks and resistance from the various tribes of the Vast Expanse are not to be underestimated; they are extremely severe and terrifying.

Yet the Grand High Priestess cares not a whit for the consequences or the cost—her sole obsession is to perform the Great Ritual and resurrect the former Ruler of Heaven.

For this, she is willing to sacrifice everything—even if it means the destruction of the current Heavenly Host.

Her obsession and madness have become a taboo that no one among the Heavenly Hosts dares to mention.

“Priestess Qiluo knows full well that I was sent to her side by the High Priestess, yet she conspires with the Declining Celestials without a second thought. Does this mean she no longer holds the High Priestess in any regard?”

"What was the meaning behind the message Wuxuzi had me convey back then?"

"What is it that he fears will lead to a reckoning?"

Liu Yun stood silently before the ruined temple, her mind churning with thoughts.

The High Priestess had sent her to serve by Qiluo’s side, fully aware that Qiluo would suspect her.

And Qiluo the Priestess never restricted her freedom, nor did she ever mind her presence.

Even when she spoke ill of the High Priest, she didn’t care in the slightest and made no attempt to hide it.

Liu Yun sighed softly to herself, her clear eyes fixed on the dragon-sparrow caged within. What difference was there between her now and that dragon-sparrow? Like a plaything in someone’s palm, she had no control over her own fate.

If she could have regretted it, would she still have chosen to fall into the darkness back then?

For someone like her—who had voluntarily allowed herself to be tainted by the dark substance—was there still a chance for redemption? Was there still room for regret?

As the bubbles dissipated, the layers of illusory scenery before her shattered, and Qiluo found herself in an open field. As far as the eye could see, crisscrossing paths and neatly arranged houses stretched out.

Lush green wheat swayed in the breeze across the fields, exuding a fresh, natural fragrance. In the fields, numerous village women, bent over and wearing straw hats, were weeding and fertilizing—a scene of harmony and tranquility.

No one who witnessed this scene could possibly associate it with the "Realm of Decline" spoken of in legends.

Even Qiluo, the priestess, looked slightly dazed.

But in the very next moment, the scene before her shifted: the rural fields vanished, replaced by the bustling atmosphere of a bustling marketplace.

People were coming and going, traffic was bustling, and the air was filled with the cries of vendors on both sides; she could even smell the aroma of meat buns, a scent she hadn’t encountered in a long time.

"Illusion and reality, truth and falsehood, formless and trace-less, desireless and colorless—the Realm of Celestial Decline truly lives up to its reputation. If I choose to believe in all this before me, will it become real?" Priestess Qiluo laughed, her gaze unchanged.

Yet the scene before her began to collapse layer by layer, as if washed away by the power of time. The entire space disintegrated, shattering into fragments like falling leaves, which then swirled through the air and vanished.

I wonder if this is true or false?”Wuxuzi smiled faintly. “There is no true method of resurrecting the dead in this world. As the scene before her shifted and changed, a thick, billowing mist emerged all around. The mist would at times spread out, at times swell, and at times collapse, as if reenacting the birth of the world and the demise of the universe.
