Life and death, transcendence or immortality—none of it seemed to matter anymore.

Amid the great calamity, the heavens and earth were thrown into chaos, and the entire Vast Expanse was churned into a murky whirlpool by him. Naturally, these “big fish” who had previously hidden themselves could not remain untouched by the turmoil.

His so-called Heaven Slaying grand scheme still relied on these figures at the end of their paths as the backbone of his forces. The next step was to orchestrate this grand drama under the pretext of "Heaven Slaying."

Unless one holds a portion of authority—in a sense, acting as an emissary of the Heavenly Dao, possessing a fraction of its power—one remains unaffected.

But from ancient times to the present, how many beings have actually held such authority?

Beyond that, there are times of great upheaval in the cosmos—such as the emergence of a faction like the Heaven Slaying Alliance—when the Heavenly Dao, operating in the unseen, is disturbed and seeks to restore order. In such moments, the more powerful beings are the more acutely they sense these omens, allowing them to secure advantages for themselves amidst the shifting tides.

The early arrival of the Great Calamity was part of his plan all along. And weren’t those beings who had returned—those who had reached the end of their paths—also drawn out by him?

Many "Road’s End" beings have returned recently. If they truly perceive their celestial destiny, they might unconsciously be drawn by the Heavenly Will, thereby obstructing the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

No matter how powerful the Eternal Primordial God might be, he is still merely a being at the end of the path. His movements and whereabouts are easily noticed by those in positions of power, who may then plot against him.

It has always been this way throughout history: whenever those who defy Heaven and attempt to alter their fate appear, they inevitably draw the gaze of the Heavenly Dao. Acting in accordance with Heaven is the prevailing trend; such actions are rewarded, allowing one to evade reckoning and achieve oneness with the Heavenly Dao.

Since Gu Changge founded the Heaven Slaying Alliance, its influence was initially limited due to its small scale. Yet as the Alliance has grown increasingly powerful—nearing the point of unifying the Vast Expanse—its surging fortune has reached a peak unseen in countless eras, precipitating the early arrival of the Great Calamity.

The cosmic fortune within the Vast Expanse has a threshold; once this limit is exceeded, the Great Calamity will inevitably strike—this is an immutable truth since time immemorial.

Once the Great Calamity takes shape, it cannot be stopped; one can only wait for it to naturally subside and come to an end, then slowly accumulate and gather energy, awaiting the onset of the next Great Calamity. This cycle repeats endlessly, never changing.

Not to mention the Heaven Slaying Alliance—even its name alone reveals how taboo it is. It is a grave transgression and should not exist in this world.

And now, the annihilation and prevention of the Heaven Slaying Alliance is the will of the great cosmic trend born from the depths of the heavens and earth.
