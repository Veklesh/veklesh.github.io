Within the annex, a radiant glow danced in Gu Changshou’s palm.

Vast swathes of Immortal Substances and Creation Substances emerged, radiating a hazy, fantastical glow, within which the indescribable, unfathomable essences of all worlds seemed to intertwine.

His gaze was calm, yet within the depths of his eyes, chaotic substances surged. Immediately afterward, a fissure-like opening appeared before him, within which the turbulence of space and time collided, causing billions of layers of the void to undulate in response.

As he channeled the Immortal Substance and Creation Substance into that fissure, chaotic particles began to form and take shape within it.

In the span of a single breath, a tiny world was created within that void. It then began to expand, devouring Immortal Substance and Creation Substance from the void as it transformed into a medium-sized world…

Gu Changge had been observing this process the entire time. During this, he looked up at the distance and fell deep in thought. Only then did he casually wave his hand, causing all the fluctuations of energy before him to vanish without a trace.

"In the Vast Expanse, it is virtually impossible to carve out a world that is not subject to the will of Heaven and Earth."

“Unless one were to tear this original sheet of paper in half—but such an act would cause far too great a disturbance. What if, instead, one were to fold the original white paper in half?”

Lately, Gu Changge had been trying to leave himself a contingency plan beyond his original scheme.

He had a premonition that unforeseen variables would arise in his future plans, so throughout this process, he needed to leave himself additional means of response.

In doing so, to ensure his plan was foolproof, he had to deceive even himself—keeping the details of this contingency hidden from his own awareness.

Only by severing the relevant parts from all his thoughts and ideas could he ensure there were no loopholes or flaws.

Just as during the period when he walked the world as the Lord of Heaven and the Demon Lord, he had truly concealed his true self deep within his soul.

"However, now is not the time."

In the years that followed, an unimaginably terrifying war erupted between the Dark Clans and the Nine Heavens.

The surrounding cosmos and spacetime were shattered. Though the Grave Keeper’s power had reached the pinnacle of the Path’s end, he was ultimately defeated by the combined might of the Dark Clan’s four High Priests. His blood spilled across endless spacetime, piercing through countless layers of the void, and his true form fell to an unknown destination.

With the Nine Heavens, once guarded by the Grave Keeper, suddenly left wide open, the native inhabitants—who had never experienced war or slaughter—faced such a scene for the first time and instantly lost their composure.

The ruler of the First Heaven perished immediately, struck by a massive black hand that emerged from the endless black mist. His body was torn to pieces, and his true soul, trapped within, was instantly reduced to dust.

The vast First Heaven rapidly collapsed. Although many ancient beings emerged to resist, they were no match for the overwhelming numbers of the Dark Horde, which surged forward like a tidal wave with no end in sight.

Continent after continent shattered. The surrounding stars trembled and exploded into fragments. Billions of living beings, along with their universes, disintegrated completely. Endless rain of blood and dark matter poured down upon every corner of the First Heaven, as if a corner of the heavens and earth had been torn open.

It was a desperate and brutal battle. Ruins and debris lay everywhere, the landscape a desolate wasteland; it seemed as though the heavens and earth were weeping.

Only the tomb in the First Heaven, where the "Heaven" had once been buried, remained immovable as of ancient times, filled with a cold, deathly, desolate aura.

Thick mists flowed, transforming into wisp-like strands that clung to the hills, mountains, and ravines. All laws and fluctuations, upon reaching that place, were instantly dispersed by an invisible force.

Even a Dao Realm being standing there would be affected, stripped of their formidable Daoist arts and cultivation.

After the crushing defeat in the First Heaven, the surviving beings and cultivators attempted to flee toward the Second Heaven. However, the ancient teleportation array had long been destroyed by the Dark Clans. Though some Dao Realm beings cried out for help, no one knew if their pleas had reached the other Heavens.

Amid their screams, the dark creatures surged forward, engulfing them.

Countless beings were overcome by despair. Some had even heard reports that the Nine Heavens had long been prepared to confront the Dark Tribes. The lowest few heavens were merely cannon fodder, intended to buy time; the true forces to meet the Dark Tribes’ main army had already gathered in the Seventh Heaven and above.

Outside the Nine Heavens, amidst the surging, chaotic currents of the void, layer upon layer of spacetime intertwined. The ancient warships of the Dark Clan hovered there, seemingly forged from immortal dark divine metal, radiating an indestructible glow that filled one with dread and despair.

Amidst the vast, sky-piercing black mist, one could discern figure after figure whose auras had reached the Ancestral Dao Realm, standing tall like timeless, unchanging shadows of evil.

After defeating the Gravekeeper, the Four High Priests did not depart but remained to hold their ground amidst the dark army, intent on seizing the momentum to tear the Nine Heavens asunder.

After the collapse of the Second Heaven, the cultivators and sentient beings of that realm were forced to intervene, though their efforts proved futile.

On the side of the Dark Clans, not even those at the Ancestral Dao Realm had moved to intervene; the high-ranking members of the Dark Clans remained on the sidelines, observing rather than joining the fray. This was because, as they had learned from High Priest Wu Yue, the Nine Heavens held far more power than met the eye and could not be underestimated.

Soon, the Third and Fourth Heavens were also attacked. One after another, spacetime corridors pierced through every corner of the Nine Heavens. In the remaining Heavens, supreme beings were roused from their slumber and began to emerge, leading the native inhabitants in resistance.

The great war shook the world, bringing chaos and upheaval.

During this period, the Land of the Dark Source finally bordered the lair of the Dark Beings. The Coffin of the World’s Demise opened, and the sarcophagus within was shrouded in an endless haze of bloodlight. A pulsating rhythm and resounding might, akin to a heartbeat, echoed throughout the Vast Expanse, exploding in the ears of countless cultivators and sentient beings.

A blood-red altar hung high, grand and immense, casting its glow across the Vast Expanse of the multiverse, illuminating the skies of countless realms and civilizations.

During this time, the boundless energy unleashed by the Dark Clans’ grand ritual surged back like crimson star rivers spanning the cosmos, ceaselessly cascading into the void.

Amidst this, a vague figure of a supreme being could be faintly discerned, seated in meditation. The Coffin of World’s Demise hung high above its head, and endless streams of bloodlight cascaded down, completely shrouding it. No one could approach; even from an immeasurable distance, one could still sense that vast and overwhelming might—before it, even beings of the Dao Realm would be crushed.

One ancient world after another floated and sank around it, carrying with them an aura of desolation, despair, deathly silence, and profound depth.

This scene, reflected across the Vast Expanse, filled countless living beings and cultivators with a profound sense of terror and dread, compelling them to instinctively prostrate themselves in that direction.

Everyone knew that the Dark Clans had successfully summoned back the will of the former Lord of Heaven—that very being who had once plunged the entire Vast Expanse into despair and darkness—and that he would once again descend upon the world.

"We humbly welcome our Lord."

Within the Nightmare Demon Realm, the air was filled with the thunderous, fervent cries of worship, shaking layer upon layer of spacetime and the cosmos.

The priests and guardians had also gathered before the Hall of the Celestial Lord, kneeling in worship there.

High Priest Wu Yue raised his head, silently gazing up at the blurred figure shrouded in endless crimson light above him. Beneath his robes, his face was etched with unwavering determination.

“When will I be able to see you again…”

She knew it was merely a phantom, not the true Lord of Heaven, yet she could not hide the deep longing in her eyes.

Within a bronze palace existing in a void of chaotic currents, beyond any realm of time or space.

A pair of eyes opened, filled with murderous intent and indifference, but they soon settled into a deathly stillness as deep as an abyss—as if even the endless ages of eternity could not stir a single ripple within them.

This was a figure reclining in the chair at the head of the grand hall, clad in a simple, faded gray robe, with hair of the same hue, and an aura of lethal intent constantly radiating from his presence.

As his gaze swept across the void, the space before him fractured inch by inch, as if severed and annihilated by some supreme law.

If any being at the Ancestral Dao Realm were present here at this moment, they would undoubtedly be horrified to discover that the spacetime and the flow of time surrounding this figure were in a state of shattering chaos—meaning he existed in no specific point of time or space.

He had annihilated the time and space around him; any presence that drew near would be instantly obliterated.

"What kind of trick is this?"

“Is the path he has chosen different from all of ours?”

The figure in the gray robe gazed silently, watching the changes unfolding at the Dark Clan’s lair, seemingly lost in thought.

As his robes billowed, a wave of gray, murderous energy surged forth. Within the bronze palace before him, several figures suddenly materialized, each enveloped in a thick aura of slaughter—an overwhelming ferocity that suggested they had slaughtered countless beings across the cosmos.

Threads of bloodlight solidified, coalescing upon their bodies to form a kind of Dao-seal.

“Number Three, go to Mount Wangxian,” the figure in the gray robe said indifferently.

In the Hall of the Unborn, aside from the Hall Master, everyone was addressed by their serial number; each number represented both their status and their power.

As the figure in the gray robe descended, a faint, ethereal form within the bronze hall vanished without a sound.

As the Master of the Hall of the Unborn, the figure in the gray robe was one who held the authority over life and death. Over the endless ages, very few things had truly been able to rouse him.

At the same time, in the Ancient Hidden Civilization.

On a barren, lifeless planet, layer upon layer of ripples in space-time stirred, followed by boundless divine light spreading out—like an ancient moon rising, banishing all shadows and darkness.

As the ripples of space-time subsided, a vague figure—bathed in light, crowned with a regal diadem, and holding a scepter—stepped forth, one step after another.

With each step, a substance as fine as particles of light could be seen swirling and rising around the figure.

The once-desolate stars suddenly burst with vitality, as various forms of life began to take shape and flourish.

The figure seemed to notice the commotion in the Nightmare Demon Realm, but after a single glance, it turned its gaze away.

Before him lay a vast expanse of profound and majestic stars, obscured by the vast turbulence of time and space. Yet, with his arrival, a great path unfurled from beneath his feet, stretching all the way into the depths of that star.

The Ancestral Void, the Master of Destiny, and a figure of ordinary appearance—yet with eyes a pale gray and a body shrouded in an aura of calamity—had long been waiting there.

At that moment, sensing a supreme, indescribable aura, the expressions of all three shifted slightly. The figure of ordinary appearance raised a hand, summoning a mysterious treasure resembling a jewel. A misty aura emanated from it, akin to some supreme power, instantly stabilizing the spacetime and cosmos of that location to prevent them from collapsing in an instant.

"This artifact must be the Hun Yuan Pearl..."

"I never expected it to fall into your hands, Fellow Daoist."

The Master of Destiny noticed the artifact and recognized the ethereal aura it contained. Though the Primordial Era lay far in the past, many artifacts from that time had survived to the present. Every Primordial artifact possessed a purity of essence that surpassed anything of the present age, coming closest to that simple, complete Dao.

For it was only after the Primordial Era that the Vast Expanse came into being. Many believe that the Dao within the Vast Expanse is incomplete; only objects formed during the Primordial Era can bear the imprint of that era’s essence.

"Heh, just a stroke of luck." The blurry figure with an ordinary face merely smiled casually.

Beings of their caliber generally possess some Primordial artifacts.

To simultaneously contain the Primordial Void, the Ruler of Destiny, and his world, a vessel is essential. An ordinary universe would have long been crushed by even a single wisp of their presence; if all three were to appear at once, it would instantly collapse into dust.

"I hope you two aren’t trying to pull the wool over my eyes..."

At that moment, a vast, ethereal voice suddenly resounded through the spacetime of this place. The three saw only a flash before their eyes; when they reappeared, they found themselves standing before a figure clad in a sacred robe, crowned with a diadem, and holding a scepter.

"Lord of the World..."

Whether it was the Ancestor of Void, the Lord of Destiny, or the Lord of Calamity, their expressions all grew solemn. The three had long since entered the Realm of the End of the Path, yet to be brought into this realm of time and space without a sound—this was the power of authority.

The so-called End of the Path Realm possessed absolutely no power to resist the True Path Realm.

In the Vast Expanse of the present age, those who wield such authority are few and far between; to catch a glimpse of them is nearly impossible—one cannot even find them.

And the World Ruler before them was none other than a being who wielded the authority of creation. If he did not wish to see them, they would be unable to sense even the slightest trace of his presence, even if they searched the entire Vast Expanse.

"Whether it is true or false, the World Ruler will know the moment he sees it," the Ancestor Xu said.

The World Ruler had once founded the Mountain of Creation and created a race known as the World Clan. That race possessed an innate talent for nurturing worlds, but later, for reasons unknown, they vanished without a trace. Yet the World Ruler has always remained in the world.

The Way of Creation he cultivated focused on the creation of worlds—from the Small Thousand Worlds, to the Middle Thousand Worlds, to the Great Thousand Worlds, the Multiverse, the Super-Multiverse, and finally the True Realm. Each stage corresponded to a specific level of attainment.

The World Ruler has now reached the stage of creating the True Realm. He has always believed that as long as he can create a world comparable to the Vast Expanse of the cosmos, he will be able to transcend his current realm.

In the World Ruler’s philosophy, beyond the Mortal Realm, the Immortal Realm, and the Dao Realm, there actually exists another realm known as the Celestial Realm.

Yet, from the very beginning to the present day, no one has ever attained it.
