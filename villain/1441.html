As the old man awoke, several incredibly powerful auras surged forth from the depths of the Gu Clan, and beams of rainbow light shot out from the palaces atop the various peaks, descending upon this place.

Some were elderly men, others elderly women; there were also handsome young men and women of unparalleled beauty, yet each exuded an air of ancient, weathered wisdom, betraying an age far beyond their outward appearance.

“The ancestral hall did indeed tremble just now…”

“What happened?”

A multitude of figures materialized from different realms of time and space, all landing simultaneously upon this mountaintop.

Following their gaze, one could see deep within the Gu Clan’s territory, amidst the mountain ranges shrouded in endless mist, a mighty force seemed to be surging. The terrain there was more majestic than any mountain range, resembling an inverted plateau.

The plateau stretched across the boundless mist, and faintly visible was an ancient shrine situated there, as if overlooking the entire cosmos. A sense of supreme grandeur permeated the air, while a chaotic waterfall cascaded down like an ocean, warping the surrounding spacetime laws.

At the same time, the place seemed to be the very source of all life in the heavens. Though thick, deathly energy pervaded the air, one could still sense a certain vitality, as if something were being nurtured.

This aura far surpasses that of any single Ancestral Dao Realm being. Even those who have survived nine Celestial Waning Tribulations and approached the End of the Path tier would, upon reaching that place, be crushed until their spines collapsed, unable to advance even a single step further.

Hum!!!

The void twisted and shattered one after another. Ripples spread out, and several figures appeared here silently, joining the others in gazing in that direction.

"Why did the Ancestral Shrine just tremble? Could it be that a distant ancestor is returning?"

Someone asked in a deep voice, their eyes dazzling and startling, as if infinite suns were evolving within their pupils.

"I’m not certain, but the ancestral hall wouldn’t tremble for no reason. Something must have happened." An elderly man in a gray-white robe spoke up from nearby, his voice unable to hide his excitement. He was the very person who had just revived from the pile of weeds atop the mountain.

"Greetings, Fifth Ancestor."

Only then did the gathered Gu clan members get a clear look at the old man’s face and recognize him; many immediately bowed respectfully.

However, at that moment, the Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan had no mind to pay attention to the crowd. He stared intently in that direction, his entire body trembling with excitement, and the beard on his face quivered along with him.

The ancestral hall housed the coffin of the Gu clan’s distant ancestor, and generations of Gu clan descendants had come here to pay their respects and kowtow.

Even in their youth, they had heard countless tales of the distant ancestor’s heroic deeds from their fathers, and they all held him in the highest esteem, taking pride in his legacy.

For the elders of the Gu clan, the distant ancestor represented the very essence of the clan and the reason for their existence.

After the ancient ancestor died to protect all living beings during that cataclysm, the Gu clan has been attempting to revive him by every means possible.

During this time, the Gu Clan dispatched many elders to lead groups of clan members to various regions of the True Realm, searching for the remnants of the Ancestor’s true spirit that had once dissipated into the heavens and earth.

After countless ages of silence, the ancestral hall suddenly trembled—how could this not fill the entire Gu Clan with excitement and cheers?

"The ancestral hall wouldn’t tremble for no reason; there must be a cause..."

The Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan calmed his agitated heart. His eyes shone with a brilliant light as he scanned the ancestral hall, but due to its unique nature, he could not penetrate the chaotic mist shrouding it, leaving him unsure if any unusual changes had occurred within.

He took a deep breath, then seemed to make up his mind. “The ancestral hall is undergoing unprecedented changes. I cannot let this opportunity slip away. Divine omens are fleeting, and relying on my own strength alone, it would be difficult to deduce the full scope of what is happening.”

The thought flashed through his mind, and he hesitated no longer. His robes fluttered without a breeze, and a colossal aura—as if a primordial beast that had slumbered for eons had awakened—suddenly swept across the entire Nine Heavens.

In that instant, the pitch-black, profound expanse of the cosmos seemed to be swept by a ray of eternal daylight, overwhelming everything. Even the barriers separating each of the Nine Heavens became crystal clear.

Every living being and cultivator within the Nine Heavens, regardless of their status, felt a chill run through their very bodies, as if they had been brushed by some terrifying presence.

Even the Immortal Dao Lord, seated high within the Futiang Sect, furrowed his brow. Though his face betrayed displeasure and anger, he could only force himself to endure it.

“What is the Fifth Patriarch doing…?”

The members of the Gu Clan present were equally shaken to the core.

In the next instant, a tomb long buried at the foot of the mountain within the Gu Clan’s territory burst open. A shadowy figure emerged and approached the Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan.

"Fifth Ancestor, what has happened?"

The figure spoke, his eyes bearing the weight of time and age. He appeared even older than the Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan standing before him, as if he had been slumbering for an immeasurable span of time.

The Gu clan members present could not even recognize who this person was—which of the ancient ancestors he might be.

But anyone capable of addressing the Fifth Ancestor as “Old Five” must, by seniority and power, be far above him.

"What is this?"

The next moment, without the Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan needing to speak, the blurry figure furrowed his brow, as if sensing something. Then his entire body trembled, filled with disbelief.

The Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan remained silent. He closed his eyes, as if sensing and calling out to something.

Hum!

Silently and without a sound, the space-time before them collapsed. A figure stepped out—tall and graceful, of unparalleled beauty—with countless fragments of time swirling around her.

At her feet, the shadows of the river of time and the river of the years converged, then gradually faded away.

She was more excited than anyone else present; were it not for the fact that the ancestral hall lay ahead and solemnity was required, she might have rushed forward.

"Could this be..."

“—Our ancestor?”

The Gu clan members gathered here were now truly stunned, unable to believe their eyes.

Some of their voices were even trembling.

Unlike the other ancestral elders of the Gu family, the First Ancestor of the Gu family was a woman, and her status had never wavered.

Even as later Ancestors caught up in cultivation and power, surpassing her, none could ever match or rival this First Ancestor.

Within the Gu clan, there was even a saying that an insurmountable chasm separated the First Ancestor from the Second Ancestor.

There are two types of Ancestors in the Gu Clan: the First Ancestor and the other Ancestors.

From this, one can understand the First Ancestor’s status and power within the Gu Clan.

"The First Ancestor, who has been absent for countless ages and has never even appeared before the clan..."

"Has actually appeared today." A wave of shock and awe swept through the Gu clan members, leaving them unable to calm down for a long time. They finally understood what the location of the Ancestral Shrine truly meant to the Gu clan.

It is likely that even if external enemies were to invade and the Nine Heavens were breached, it would still be extremely difficult to bring these Ancestors out.

As the long ages passed, the Nine Heavens remained aloof from the world, and this was especially true for the Gu Clan.

Not even the entire Gu clan knew how many ancient ancestors remained, let alone their true whereabouts.

"This aura—there can be no mistake. It is indeed the aura of the True Spirit of the Ancestor."

“And it has already appeared within the Nine Heavens…”

One of the Gu Clan’s Ancestors murmured. Her voice sounded slightly hoarse, not at all like the peerless beauty she appeared to be.

"Is this true?"

The Fifth Ancestor of the Gu Clan opened her eyes; though she looked somewhat weary, she could not hide the excitement on her face.

"There can be no mistake."

The First Ancestor of the Gu Clan nodded. Scenes shifted within her eyes as she had already begun to calculate and confirm the truth.

The Nine Heavens were vast beyond measure, and each Heaven was separated from the next by regions such as spacetime turbulence and chaotic star rivers.

Even for beings at the Dao Realm, traversing between them was extremely difficult. However, there were inter-realm teleportation arrays between each layer, controlled by the dominant powers of each realm. These were rarely activated in ordinary times to avoid disrupting the order between the layers.

Led by Song Yu, the members of the Crescent Moon Sect departed the territory of the tombkeeper and set off toward the first immortal city of the First Heaven.

The immortal city stood majestic and towering, its walls stretching endlessly into the distance. No one knew what kind of stone had been used to build them; even the ordinary stars seemed minuscule in comparison.

The surrounding mountain ranges truly embodied the phrase “reaching the heavens.” The Immortal Realms Gu Xian’er had once witnessed in the Upper Realm paled in comparison to the Nine Heavens before her—they were but a mere shadow of this vast expanse.

She couldn’t help but marvel inwardly.

Along the way, she encountered many ancient remnants—beings of overwhelming power, imbued with immortal energy, as if they had undergone countless transformations.

The rulers of certain regions even gave her a sense of dread; she surmised their power might already be approaching the Dao Realm.

Gu Xian’er could not help but acknowledge the extraordinary nature of the Nine Heavens—it was indeed incomparable to any of the True Realms in the outside world.

Soon, the group arrived at the gates of the Immortal City, where a squad of soldiers clad in immortal armor appeared and blocked their path.

However, Song Yu was prepared and presented proof of their identities, allowing the group to enter the city.

Outside every city lay endless, vast ancient forests.

It is said these forests evolved from the ancient Heaven Slaying battlefields. They hold many opportunities, so even cultivators and sentient beings living within the Nine Heavens frequently venture there to seek adventure, explore ancient ruins, and search for hidden caves.

Every few eons, the forbidden zones within these vast ancient forests unleash terrifying waves of beasts to plunder the forces of various cities, a cycle that has long maintained a certain natural balance.

Some rulers of these forbidden zones possess power rivaling even the sovereigns of each of the Nine Heavens.

The Nine Heavens’ ability to remain transcendent and detached from the outside world is, to a large extent, due to this very reason. With its own internal order and the absence of the Dao of Heaven, it maintains a natural equilibrium, unaffected by the outside world and free from the threat of the Great Calamity.

Over time, this place has become the origin of the Immortal Way, a destination sought by countless cultivators and sentient beings.

However, finding the Nine Heavens is exceedingly difficult. Without the necessary karmic connection or a path to guide one there, it is entirely possible to spend one’s entire life wandering aimlessly outside its gates.

"Today, we will rest for the night in the First Heaven. I will pay a visit to the ruler of the First Heaven and ask him to open the teleportation array to the Second Heaven for us tomorrow..."

Upon arriving at the Immortal City, Song Yu finally let out a sigh of relief, his heart no longer in his throat. He offered what he considered a gentle smile and addressed the group.

In his view, they had now reached the Nine Heavens—his own territory.

No matter how extraordinary Gu Xian’er’s background might be, he was fully confident he could get his hands on the Nine Celestial Restraints bound to her.

Hearing Song Yu’s words, a disciple of the Crescent Moon Sect couldn’t help but ask out of curiosity, “Senior Brother Song Yu, what exactly is your background? Along the way, it seemed as though you were very familiar with the Nine Heavens. It must be more than just having lived here for a while.”

The elders of the Crescent Moon Sect were also curious.

Although they had only just set foot in the First Heaven, they had already learned a few things about the Nine Heavens.

Each Heavenly Realm has a ruler, and for ordinary people, even catching a glimpse of them is nearly impossible.

Song Yu clearly wasn’t even at the Dao Realm, yet he was able to directly visit the ruler of the First Heaven—this alone was enough to prove that his status was far from ordinary.

Moreover, they could tell that Elder Lan was, in fact, Song Yu’s Guardian.

He, like Song Yu, had been temporarily residing at Crescent Moon Sect under the guise of an elder.

"To be honest, I feel ashamed to admit that, for certain reasons, I have been concealing the truth from my fellow disciples and elders all this time. My real name is not Song Yu, but Ji Yu. My master is the ruler of the Ninth Heaven and also the Patriarch of the Futiang Sect."

Upon hearing this, Song Yu’s face betrayed a perfectly measured look of shame as he explained.

As he spoke, seemingly worried that the others might misunderstand him, he hurriedly continued,"Rest assured, I harbor no ill will. Bringing you all to the Nine Heavens was solely for your safety—nothing more. The situation outside is chaotic and turbulent now, and the Crescent Moon Sect has been destroyed by the Dark Clans. If we were to wander aimlessly and flee from place to place, we might meet our end at any moment."

“Though the Nine Heavens has always been shrouded in mystery and unknown to the outside world, it is governed by strict rules and maintains perfect order. It has long been a place of peace and tranquility. For the time being, you need not worry about being struck by the calamities of the outside world…”

His face was filled with sincerity and earnestness, and his words certainly did not sound insincere.

Ji Yu truly harbored no ulterior motives or schemes toward the elders and disciples of the Crescent Moon Sect; bringing them to the Nine Heavens was primarily to prevent Gu Xian’er from breaking away from the group.

Hearing this, the members of the Crescent Moon Sect relaxed slightly; after all, they had long suspected as much, so it didn’t come as a great surprise to them.

Moreover, Ji Yu’s candid words struck a chord with them.

The outside world was in such chaos, with factions vying for power and the situation unclear.

The Righteous Unity Alliance, with its hypocritical facade, had abandoned its allies, reneged on its agreements, and acted out of pure self-interest—they were nothing but complete hypocrites.

As for the Heaven Slaying Alliance, they were notorious for their ruthlessness, constantly stirring up chaos and waging war against others—no different from the demonic sects. They were so terrifying that no one dared to approach them.

The delegation sent to the Heaven Slaying Alliance has not been heard from since; it is presumed they perished there.

No matter which faction one looks at, none are forces to be trifled with—and that’s not even counting the Dark Clans, who are watching from the sidelines with predatory intent.

Those orthodox sects of the True Realm that have long stood idly by, concerned only with their own affairs, will likely follow in the footsteps of the Crescent Moon Sect before long.

Neither the Heaven Slaying Alliance nor the Righteous Unity Alliance will tolerate the existence of any faction in the Vast Expanse that refuses to heed their commands...

It is clear, then, that the Nine Heavens is indeed the best refuge available at present.
