The Great Dark Ritual has begun, and worlds continue to wither away. This is an age in which all beings see no hope.

Every day, countless universes are destroyed, and one world after another is obliterated.

Following the collapse of the Righteous Unity Alliance, the territories encompassed by the surrounding realms immediately fell under the control of the Dark Clans.

A vast, surging fog of darkness drifted and spread from there. The Dark Clans descended from the Nightmare Realm, took root there, and established a territory corresponding to that of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

A powerful being among the Dark Tribes referred to that place as the Realm of Eternal Life. Of course, “eternal life” was merely a self-designation of the dark beings, yet it revealed their audacity and ambition.

The Great Ritual continues to be held. The blood-red altar beyond the world stands grand and solemn, drawing ever closer with each passing day.

Meanwhile, the lair of the Dark Clans is gradually manifesting beyond the realms, awaiting the day it will touch and border the very source of darkness.

Black waves surge forth from the sea of spacetime, sweeping up one world after another, engulfing them and carrying them into the lair of the Dark Ones.

Many chaotic realms within the Vast Expanse have fallen victim to the Dark Clan’s incursions; even some races hidden in past timelines cannot escape this fate.

In this vast world shrouded by boundless chaos, the realm stretches endlessly, with mist and clouds billowing and divine light surging. Many races dwell here, and countless lineages stand side by side.

However, as a terrifying great fissure appeared beyond the realm, endless hordes of the Dark Clans, like demons from another realm, charged straight in. A vast, pitch-black mass obscured the sky and blocked out the sun. One after another, dark warships descended with a thunderous roar, completely sweeping through and devouring that vast realm.

Dark beings swarmed in vast numbers, coalescing into an immortal army. Even those at the Dao Realm could only fall in a bloodbath; this was a force beyond resistance.

Scenes like this unfolded every single day.

Before the Hall of Heaven in the Nightmare Demon Realm, High Priestess Wuyue stood in silence. Behind her, the Four High Priests stood in respectful submission.

A vast, ancient altar stood before them, billowing with one black Dao symbol after another.

Every day, vast quantities of crimson energy could be seen converging from every corner of the multiverse, resembling a river of smoke and a starry sky—rolling endlessly as it sank into the altar. The power it contained was sufficient to make every living being in the multiverse tremble with fear, inspiring awe and causing them to kneel and bow their heads.

Among the Heavenly Host of today, only the Grand High Priest and the four High Priests possess the qualification to stand upon the altar.

Any other being, regardless of their cultivation level, would be treated as nourishment the moment they appeared here. They would be obliterated by the symbols within, transformed into pure energy, and absorbed into the abyss.

The four High Priests were all personally selected and created by High Priestess Wuyue. Each carries the bloodline of her Ya tribe within them and has been granted her current power; they can be said to share the same origin and lineage as her, and thus are immune to the altar’s influence.

Moreover, when she was at her weakest and on the brink of starvation, it was by drinking Gu Changge’s blood that she survived. In a sense, his essence remains within her.

"Master, when will His Majesty return?"

"Soon."

Wuyue said softly.

She stood silently, gazing upward toward the source of darkness that was drawing ever closer to the Nightmare Demon Realm. She could clearly sense the resonance emanating from that place, and the Coffin of the End of the World within the Hall of the Celestial Mandate trembled in response.

After waiting for eons, this day had finally arrived.

"Are we to take action against the Nine Heavens and the Ancient Burial Civilization?" asked Feng Cang, the High Priest, holding an oil-paper umbrella.

Due to Wuyue’s orders, the Dark Clans had not yet unleashed their full might against the Heaven Slaying Alliance, instead turning their gaze toward the Vast Expanse of untamed realms beyond.

Currently, the two most mysterious realms within the Vast Expanse are the Nine Heavens and the Ancient Burial Civilization.

It is said that even the true Path of Reincarnation, as well as the Absolute Yin Realm that once appeared in the Vast Expanse, are located within the Ancient Burial Civilization. And behind that Absolute Yin Realm, there seems to be an intricate connection to the Realm of Truth.

Upon hearing this, Wuyue also seemed to fall deep in thought.

Whether it be the Ancient Burial Civilization or the Nine Heavens, neither is an easy target. Behind the Ancient Burial Civilization lies the Reincarnation Tower.

The Tower of Reincarnation is a place akin to the Hall of No-Birth. The being who established the Tower of Reincarnation had usurped the authority over reincarnation within the Vast Expanse.

According to the principles of the Tower of Reincarnation, every time a great calamity approaches in the Vast Expanse, it is actually due to the abundance of sentient beings’ vital energy. Therefore, the Tower acts on behalf of Heaven to harvest this vital energy and reincarnate sentient beings.

At regular intervals, it proactively sends half of all sentient beings into the cycle of reincarnation, maintaining a state where the number of beings is reduced yet kept as balanced as possible, thereby mitigating the arrival of the Great Calamity.

Of course, very few beings in this world truly know of the Tower of Reincarnation. Some rumors even suggest that the Tower’s Master actually descended from the Realm of Truth and is responsible for overseeing the great calamities of all ages in the Vast Expanse.

However, such claims cannot be verified. The Tower of Reincarnation has not revealed itself in the Vast Expanse for a very, very long time, and no one has ever seen the beings within it.

As for the Nine Heavens, Wuyue was well aware that it harbored many members of the Palace of Defiance.

The Palace of Defiance, true to its name, defies heaven and alters fate. Thus, it has gathered countless extraordinary beings from throughout history—those who have sought to break free from the great calamities and defy the heavens.

These beings had once engaged in Heaven Slaying and defied the heavens, enduring the most despairing of cataclysms—the deaths of their loved ones and the shattering of their homeland. Yet, through the intervention of the Palace of Defiance’s founding great power, they survived to this day and united to establish the Palace of Defiance.

It encompasses extraordinary figures from every era, all biding their time to accumulate strength, awaiting the day when they will once again engage in Heaven Slaying and defy fate.

In truth, all this intelligence has been gathered by Wuyue through covert investigations over the course of ten thousand eons. Whether it be the Hall of No-Life, the Tower of Reincarnation, or even the Palace of Defiance, none of these entities can be allowed to surface.

Heaven and Earth cannot permit the existence of such places or such forces.

To uncover the existence of these places requires immense effort—one must painstakingly piece together clues from the traces of karma. Even the most powerful beings at the Ancestral Dao Realm, who guard their respective realms, are unaware of their existence.

To this day, she has only a vague sense of the possible locations of the Palace of Defiance and the Tower of Reincarnation; as for where the Hall of No-Birth might be situated, she has absolutely no clue.

The Vast Expanse is far too immense. Certain chaotic spacetime realms, void domains, and unknown dimensions are nearly impossible to fully probe or comprehend. To truly grasp the secrets of the entire Vast Expanse, one must likely reach the point of wielding partial authority over it.

At present, she was still far from that stage. To be precise, while her cultivation level had reached the required height, she lacked that specific authority.

If she had that authority to assist her, perhaps she could faintly sense the true locations of those places.

"When the Master returns, he will naturally settle all accounts."

"For now, let us take action against the Nine Heavens."

“Qing’er is lying low there; she might prove somewhat useful.”

Wuyue spoke. Her figure, shrouded in mist, vanished from the spot as she took a step. The four High Priests withdrew as instructed.

When Wuyue’s figure reappeared, she was deep within a desolate, pitch-black mountain range.

With a flick of her jade-like hand, a fissure spread open. She stepped inside to find a landscape of clear streams and lush mountains, filled with the songs of birds and the fragrance of flowers—a natural, vibrant world that stood in stark contrast to the outside.

Within this pleasant, natural mountain range lay a serene bamboo grove, where a woman dressed in white sat quietly in the center.

An ancient zither lay before her. As her slender fingers gently glided across the strings, melodious sounds filled the air, drawing a multitude of rare birds and exotic beasts to gather.

Only after Wuyue appeared did the woman in white cease her playing and lift her head.

Her gaze was gentle, her features soft and flawless, giving off an aura of infinite beauty. Upon closer inspection, one would notice that her face bore a striking resemblance to Nan Qing’s—in fact, they were seven parts alike.

"High Priestess Wuyue..."

The woman in white spoke, her voice as melodious as heavenly music, possessing a soothing power that made one want to lose oneself in it.

“Tell me about the Palace of Defiance…” Upon arriving, Wuyue cut straight to the chase.

"The Palace of Defiance, you say?"

“High Priestess Wuyue, why do you suddenly wish to know about the Palace of Defiance? Has something major happened in the outside world?” the woman in white asked, her eyes as gentle as water.

Wuyue replied calmly, “You need not know.”

The woman in white smiled and said, “Let me guess: it’s because, in the current state of the Vast Expanse, there are no other forces capable of standing against the Dark Clans, so you’ve set your sights on the Palace of Reversed Fate.”

“However, I don’t know much about the Palace of Defiance myself—at most, I’ve merely come into contact with people from the Palace of Defiance.”

The woman before him was none other than the Southern Venerable, a former leader of the Sages. She had not died; she had merely been sealed and confined here, unable to leave.

After the collapse of Heaven's Proxy, the Heavenly Host and the Eight Divisions fell into disarray, plunging Da Tian into prolonged internal chaos.

And that period coincided with the time shortly after the Age of Black Calamity had just ended.

Without Heaven's Proxy to keep them in check, the Vast Expanse descended into chaos and turmoil. The lineages and clans established by the former sages each acted independently, and in their struggle for resources and power, they once again plunged into endless bloodshed and conflict.

It was precisely this situation that provided the remnants of the Celestial Authority with an opportunity to lie dormant in the shadows, grow in strength, and seek revenge.

From that era onward, the clans that had once resisted the calamity of the Black Scourge suffered successive assaults and persecution at the hands of the remnants of the Daitian Organization.

Some traditional factions vanished forever, while others—such as the descendants of the sages who once lived on the Floating Continent in the Chaotic Spacetime Sea—chose to flee their homes, constantly migrating and living in seclusion. Southern Venerable was the leader of those descendants of the sages at the time and was revered as the Holy Ancestor by the indigenous people of the Floating Continent.

This was because some of the tribes associated with those sages had been captured and enslaved by the remnants of the Heavenly Replacers, plunging them into eternal darkness.

The Southern Venerable was forced to infiltrate the lair of the Dark Ones—now known as the Nightmare Realm—in search of a solution.

During this process, he encountered unexpected events and was forced to go into hiding within the Nightmare Demon Realm.

Furthermore, at that time, the "Heavenly Host Uprising" erupted within the Celestial Realm. The leaders of the Eight Divisions, vying for the flesh and blood within the Coffin of the Underworld, waged a brutal war in the Nightmare Demon Realm, plunging the region into chaos.

Upon hearing this news, many of the most powerful beings from outside clans took advantage of the chaos to join the fray.

Due to his seclusion and the Ancestor Xu’s schemes, Wuyue failed to detect the abnormalities among the Heavenly Host. Consequently, that chaos dealt a severe blow to the Heavenly Host, whose strength was already severely depleted.

Even the Coffin of the End of the World, enshrined within the Hall of the Celestial Throne, was opened as a result. A crack appeared in the coffin’s lid, and numerous powerful beings fought within, vying for the flesh and blood inside.

In her quest to find a way to counter the Dark Matter, the Southern Venerable also joined the chaotic battle.

Amidst the clashes with numerous formidable foes, she relied on her overwhelming strength to seize the most crucial drop of essence blood. Subsequently, she was surrounded and attacked. To prevent her powerful enemies from seizing that drop of essence blood, she forcibly swallowed it and began to refine it.

Alas, she had vastly underestimated the colossal will and terrifying energy contained within that drop of essence blood from the Lord of Heaven.

The moment that drop of essence entered her body, it took root within her like a seed, violently draining her of all her strength in an attempt to assimilate her.

To resist that will, Southern Venerable took a desperate gamble. Braving the boundless pressure, she ventured deep into the coffin, harnessing the array patterns within the Coffin of the Underworld to try to stay conscious.

During this arduous process, her own will and strength became entangled with that drop of essence. As the two sought to merge and assimilate, an unexpected turn of events occurred.

She discovered a new life form had emerged within her body…

This was a novel experience and situation she had never encountered before. She did not know if it could be considered her child, but since it carried the blood of the Lord of Heaven as well as her own, if it were to be born, it would undoubtedly be an unimaginably monstrous existence.

She had considered refining and erasing it, ending her own life in the process, to nip this being—destined to bring calamity upon the world—in the bud.

Yet she also felt an inexplicable reluctance and tenderness. After all, this was, in a sense, her child; she could sense its life and the presence of its soul.

Then, something happened that sent a chill down her spine and filled her with fear.

The child within her womb seemed to sense her hesitation and inner turmoil, attempting to beguile her and addressing her as “mother.” Even before birth, it displayed a terrifyingly keen intelligence.

To say it was born with innate knowledge or born a saint would still fall short of describing the child’s monstrous nature and terror.

Even while still in the womb, it could effortlessly see through people’s hearts, clearly discerning the myriad thoughts and intentions within them. One must understand that she was no ordinary person; she was one of the five leaders who had once fought against the Black Calamity, possessing profound strength and formidable spiritual power—yet even she could not prevent it from guessing her innermost thoughts.

The Southern Venerable hesitated, struggled, and suffered. He wanted to use himself as a vessel to completely refine and eradicate her, preventing her from ever appearing in the world.

She feared the emergence of another terrifying entity as unfathomable and unpredictable as the Lord of Heaven, one that would bring boundless calamity and catastrophe to all realms.

Yet that child was intelligent, obedient, gentle, and understanding, and had accompanied her through a long stretch of time within her lonely coffin.

In the end, her maternal compassion softened her heart, causing her to abandon all thoughts of self-destruction.

After the chaos among the Heavenly Host was quelled, not even the High Priestess of the Moonless Realm detected any anomaly within the Coffin of the Buried World.

When the Southern Venerable gave birth to that child and decided to raise her within the coffin’s realm,

The Heavenly Host had already gradually faded from the sight of the various realms, beginning to rest and recuperate, while the outside world had also emerged from the chaos that followed the Age of the Black Calamity.

Inside the coffin, the Southern Venerable named that delicate, porcelain-skinned little girl Nan Qing.

To prevent her from straying onto the wrong path, she explained to her about the former "Heaven's Proxy" organization, the "Black Calamity" that had once brought disaster to the Vast Expanse, and everything about the outside world... Because she knew that sooner or later, the High Priestess Wuyue would discover the anomaly within the Coffin of the Buried World.

When that time came, it would be the moment they were separated.

Due to her bloodline, Nan Qing would certainly be safe, but she was different. She had participated in the Heavenly Host Uprising and had seized that single drop of essence blood—the most precious thing at the time. The High Priestess Wuyue would certainly not let her off the hook.

At that point, she would have no choice but to die. She did not wish to fall into darkness like her former companions, becoming the very beings she once hated most. What happened next did not unfold as she had anticipated; it was not that the High Priestess Wuyue had discovered her and her son.

Rather, it was Nan Qing herself who, through her bloodline, triggered the disturbance of the Coffin of the Underworld. That astonishing phenomenon shook the entire Nightmare Demon Realm, causing all dark beings to mistakenly believe that the Lord of Heaven was about to be resurrected and return.

She demonstrated her unique abilities and effortlessly harnessed the power within the Coffin of the Underworld, intending to use it as leverage to intimidate the High Priestess Wuyue and persuade her to spare the Southern Venerable, her mother.

Alas, the power of the Coffin of the End of the World failed to intimidate High Priestess Wuyue, and Nan Qing’s plan came to naught.

However, the High Priest of the Moonless did not harm the Southern Venerable; he merely imprisoned her. Every few eons, he would allow the mother and daughter to meet, but on the condition that Nan Qing become a member of the Heavenly Host and set out to seize the remaining treasures of the First Civilization.
