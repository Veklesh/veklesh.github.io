In the Hall of Eternal Peace, surging mists and chaotic energies filled the air, creating a vast, desolate expanse.

The Emperor of Weiyang wore simple blue robes and no crown.

He was sitting behind a desk made of stacked World Stones, processing memorials, his brow furrowing from time to time.

The Emperor had already noticed Old Lady Xingying approaching the palace, but he did not rise to greet her.

Ye Wudao was left waiting outside the Weiyang Hall by the Old Woman Xingying.

She walked in alone; there were no guards around the Weiyang Hall, making the area appear desolate and quiet.

Given the Weiyang Emperor’s cultivation and power, he had no need for guards to protect him.

“Jiang Yun, you’re quite at ease. At a time like this, you still have the mind to handle memorials.” The Old Lady Xingying entered the hall and cast a casual glance at Jiang Yun. She showed none of the reverence outsiders typically held for the Emperor of Weiyang, appearing quite cold; the two seemed very familiar with one another.

Jiang Yun continued to study the memorial in his hands, not even lifting his gaze, and said, “Senior Sister Xingying, what brings you here?” “It seems you’re not very pleased to see me.”

The old woman Xingying’s expression remained cold, with a smoldering anger hidden beneath her brows.

Even after such an incident had occurred, Jiang Yun remained completely unaware of it to this very moment, having never anticipated any consequences.

“If there is something on your mind, Senior Sister Xingying, you’d best speak plainly. I have quite a few matters to attend to.” Jiang Yun replied in the same detached tone, his attitude equally cold.

Toward the Old Woman Xingying, he made no attempt to hide his distaste or indifference.

Old Woman Xingying snorted coldly, walked over, and placed the sandalwood box she was holding directly in front of Jiang Yun.

"This is a great gift our Master entrusted me to give you. Open your eyes wide and take a good look at what’s inside." "She said in a cold tone, her dazzling silver-white eyes brimming with icy fury.

Upon hearing this, Jiang Yun was suddenly overcome by a sense of foreboding.

Frowning deeply, he looked at the sandalwood box before him. A thick, pungent scent of blood wafted out from the cracks, accompanied by an aura he recognized all too well.

In the next instant, his expression changed drastically. He sprang to his feet from behind the desk, his eyes turning icy cold and filled with murderous intent, his face etched with disbelief.

"What is this?" Even someone as powerful as Jiang Yun spoke with a slight tremor in his voice, as if he could scarcely believe it.

His hand, hidden beneath his robe, clenched so tightly that the sound of cracking bones echoed.

The old woman Xingying did not let him open the sandalwood box himself; with a flick of her sleeve, the lid flew open.

A blood-soaked, wide-eyed head lay exposed right in the center.

The head was covered in a thick beard, and the hair was coiled into a spiral-patterned topknot—it was none other than Xing Sha, who had recently sent him a letter and had shared a hearty feast of meat and wine with him in the Weiyang Hall.

Jiang Yun stared at those eyes, wide open and unable to close in death, his face betraying his grief as his body trembled violently.

Had he not braced himself against the desk beside him, he likely would have lost his balance.

“Senior Brother Xing Sha…” His voice was laced with profound grief and rage.

Outside the Weiyang Hall, the vast starry expanse and the boundless cosmos trembled in unison.

Countless stars trembled and swirled as if being battered by a raging sea, seeming on the verge of plummeting to the ground.

The pitch-black, profound sky seemed to become transparent as blood-red bolts of lightning, as thick as stars, streaked across it, striking down ceaselessly like a divine punishment to destroy the world.

This terrifying spectacle filled countless cultivators and living beings with horror. Unaware of what was happening, they were overcome by sheer terror and prostrated themselves in supplication.

Jiang Yun’s heart was filled with profound grief and deep regret.

He never could have imagined that the senior brother with whom he had been laughing and chatting, and enjoying a hearty meal of meat just a short while ago, would be in such a state when they met again.

“It was you who killed Senior Brother Xingsha. If you hadn’t insisted on testing the Master’s resolve, how could Senior Brother Xingsha have met such a tragic end?” “All of this is your fault. You must pay with your life for Senior Brother Xingsha.” Old Woman Xingying’s eyes were filled with icy coldness, her voice thick with hatred.

If she hadn’t known full well that she was no match for Jiang Yun, she might even have struck out at that moment to avenge Xing Sha.

Unlike the hostile and cold relationship between her and Jiang Yun.

She and Xing Sha had been extremely close—they were fellow disciples.

They had once studied and cultivated together in the Yinxu Cave, and their bond was as close as that of siblings.

When she was weak, Xing Sha looked after her like an older brother, supporting and helping her, disciplining those who bullied her, and finding all sorts of cultivation resources for her, enabling her to reach where she is today.

Although the two parted ways later—Xing Sha was sent by the Master of Yinxu Cave to guard the Flowing Sands Realm and was forbidden to leave without permission—

Whenever she had the chance, she would still travel to the Flowing Sands Domain to visit Xing Sha and bring him all sorts of his favorite foods.

But this time, she never imagined that when she went to see Xing Sha again, she would be carrying a sandalwood box from the Master of Yinxu Cave.

Inside that sandalwood box lay a single sheet of blank white paper.

The moment she opened it, the paper turned into a sword, its sword energy resembling a cascade of stars, shooting skyward. With a thud, it severed Xing Sha’s head.

Now, that bloodstained sheet of paper lies quietly at the bottom of the sandalwood box.

Old Woman Xingying did not know what Xing Sha had done to deserve the Yinxu Cave Master’s death sentence.

But she knew that since the Master of Yinxu Cave had instructed her to deliver this grand gift to Jiang Yun, it meant Xing Sha had died because of Jiang Yun.

"I’ve let Senior Brother Xing Sha down. I’ve betrayed his goodwill and brought about his downfall." Jiang Yun’s expression was blank, his eyes filled with pain and remorse.

He understood that this was the Yinxu Cave Master’s threat and warning to him.

Earlier, Xing Sha had come bearing the Yinxu Cave Master’s edict, but Jiang Yun had not taken it to heart.

As he was leaving, Xing Sha hesitated for a long time before specifically reminding him about his daughter, Jiang Weiyang.

His daughter, Jiang Weiyang, was his Achilles’ heel; naturally, Jiang Yun would not tolerate any danger or mishap befalling her.

At the same time, after much hesitation and deliberation, he had decided to use this opportunity to test the Master of Yinxu Cave’s resolve.

This led to the subsequent series of events surrounding his search for a husband for his daughter.

After all, the matter of selecting a husband was of the utmost importance; it involved not only the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty but also the other civilizations and doctrinal factions within the Vast Expanse.

Moreover, the Master of Yinxu Cave had long remained behind the scenes, avoiding external affairs and unwilling to get involved in any entanglements that might bring about karmic consequences.

Jiang Yun even suspected that the Master of Yinxu Cave had some extremely terrifying enemies.

He had secluded himself in a realm beyond the boundaries, concealing his aura and karmic traces, and shielding himself from all the laws of the Dao—all out of fear that his old enemies might track him down through the threads of karma.

For this reason, Jiang Yun had specifically used his daughter, Jiang Weiyang, as bait to test the Yinxu Cave Master’s resolve, to see if he dared to emerge from seclusion.

The Master of Yinxu Cave was playing a grand game, spanning countless ages and traversing countless civilizations.

Even he was merely one pawn in this game.

Jiang Yun was unwilling to be a mere puppet, at the mercy of others, nor was he willing to see his wife and daughter subjected to coercion and threats, facing mortal danger.

Yet he had indeed grossly underestimated the Yinxu Cave Master’s resolve.

The descent of this dark calamity seems to have prompted the Master of Yinxu Cave to consider stepping out into the world.

No one could shake this resolve.

By executing Xing Sha, who had brought news to Jiang Yun that very day, wasn’t that, in itself, a threat and a warning to Jiang Yun?

Defy him, and this is the fate that awaits you.

A deep chill and sorrow welled up in Jiang Yun’s heart, accompanied by an overwhelming sense of helplessness.

At this moment, he bore no resemblance to the Emperor who once looked down upon all worlds, swept through the ages, and commanded the Nine Heavens and Ten Earths to bow before him; instead, he was a timid, despairing, numb, and powerless puppet.

"If, back in the depths of time and space, I had agreed to that demand and plunged into the darkness then, would the outcome be the same today?" A trace of melancholy and confusion flickered in Jiang Yun’s eyes.

Inside the sandalwood box before him, Senior Brother Xing Sha still lay with eyes wide open, unable to rest in peace. Even at the moment of his death, he had never understood why he had to die.

Old Woman Xingying looked at Jiang Yun; though her expression remained cold, she could not hide the sorrow and pain of "the fox mourning the rabbit’s death."

Today it was Senior Brother Xing Sha—but what about tomorrow? Could it be her? Back in the vast lands of Xianchu, Junior Brother Bai Mei had been cast aside as a pawn the moment he lost his value, left to be captured and imprisoned by the Xiyuan Holy Hall.

Would she, too, be cast aside in the future? Suddenly, Old Lady Xingying felt a wave of despondency wash over her.

She retrieved the bloodstained white paper from the bottom of the sandalwood box, then, clutching the box, transformed into a streak of light and vanished into the distance.

Ye Wudao, waiting outside the Weiyang Hall, had been watching for her to emerge, but the departing Old Woman Xingying didn’t even spare him a glance as she sped away.

"What does this mean?" "Could it be that the Emperor of Weiyang is also connected to the Yinxu Cave?" Ye Wudao frowned in confusion.

“Hah, what a fine Master of the Yinxu Cave, what a fine Master of Destiny—first he kidnapped my wife, and now he’s using my daughter as a threat.” Inside the hall, Jiang Yun watched the characters gradually emerge on the bloodstained white paper. Filled with rage, he laughed bitterly, his face pale and grim.

Wisp after wisp of black mist flashed across his face, only to be swiftly suppressed by him.

The message on the white paper was simple: the Master of Yinxu Cave held Ye Wudao in high regard.

He had mentored him for a time and believed him to be a promising talent who would surely serve a great purpose in the future.

And since Jiang Yun’s daughter, Jiang Weiyang, was looking for a husband,

it would be best for him to act as a matchmaker and bring the two together. A handsome man and a beautiful woman—they would make a fine match.

Reading these words, Jiang Yun could vividly imagine the Yinxu Cave Master’s composed, nonchalant demeanor.

The entire world, all the races of the multiverse, and the masses of living beings—they all seemed to be nothing more than pawns on his chessboard.

Jiang Yun’s expression darkened to the extreme as he personally crumpled the bloodstained sheet of paper into a pile of shreds.

In the past, the Master of Yinxu Cave might have gone through the motions of playing along with him.

But now, he didn’t even bother to hide it.

Outside the Weiyang Hall, Ye Wudao remained waiting there. Ever since learning that the Weiyang Emperor, Jiang Yun, was also connected to the Yinxu Cave, he had completely put his mind at ease.

He didn’t know what had transpired between the Emperor of Weiyang and the Old Woman Xingying, but he wasn’t in a hurry.

After a long while, a sigh echoed from within the Weiyang Hall, followed by a voice that was deep and commanding.

“Come in.”

Upon hearing this, Ye Wudao rose and walked inside.

Inside the hall, Emperor Wei Yang, Jiang Yun, sat upright behind a hanging curtain, surrounded by a haze of chaotic energy that made the atmosphere hazy and indistinct.

A pair of deep, vast eyes seemed to be scrutinizing Ye Wudao, as if trying to see right through him.

This was Ye Wudao’s first time meeting His Majesty the Emperor, whose fame echoed throughout the ages.

However, he showed no signs of panic; he remained calm and composed, bowing with neither servility nor arrogance.

“Truly, the son of a tiger is no dog. The ruler of Nanzhao has spoken of you many times in my presence. ” “Now that I see you, you certainly live up to your reputation as a heroic youth and a young sovereign. ” Jiang Yun’s calm voice carried through, “I intend to marry my daughter, Jiang Weiyang, to you. What do you think?” What?

Ye Wudao was taken aback, almost failing to grasp Emperor Wei Yang’s meaning, but then his face lit up with delight and surprise.

"If Your Majesty is willing to marry the Princess to me, I swear by my Daoist heart that I will never mistreat her or let her suffer the slightest injustice. If I were to gain nine-tenths of the world, I would grant her all ten. " A surge of heroic spirit welled up within him, soaring to the heavens.

It was impossible for him not to be moved by a beauty of Princess Weiyang’s, whose beauty could captivate a nation.

He hadn’t expected that, upon meeting her today, Emperor Weiyang would immediately propose betrothing his daughter to him.

Could this be the “good fortune” the old woman Xingying had spoken of? Jiang Yun showed no sign of being moved by Ye Wudao’s words; he merely nodded and said, “Knowing you have such intentions puts my mind at ease. Once I entrust my daughter to you, if you dare to cause her even the slightest hardship, even if your father were here, I would not let you off lightly.” "Having said this, he waved his sleeve and escorted Ye Wudao out of the Weiyang Hall.

Ye Wudao had wanted to say something more, but it was already too late; his face was filled with a smile and unexpected delight.

From the white jade steps in the distance, a slender, tall figure glided gracefully toward them.

She was beautiful, clad in a moon-white, floor-length gauze gown that outlined her graceful, shapely curves. Her hair was styled like misty clouds, adorned with a slanted hairpin. Her face, as white and translucent as porcelain, bore a cool, serene expression—a vision of unparalleled beauty.

“Princess Wei Yang, we meet again.”

Ye Wudao smiled and stepped forward to greet her.

Jiang Weiyang paused, slightly surprised to hear the voice; she hadn’t expected Ye Wudao to be here.

However, she paid him no mind, her mind heavy with worry. She merely gave a slight nod. “Young Master Ye…” Then she walked straight past Ye Wudao and walked away.

Ye Wudao showed no sign of anger; a smile remained on his face, and he could still catch the lingering, delicate fragrance in the air—as pure and lovely as snow piled upon flowering trees.

“The jewel of the Weiyang Xianchu, you belong to me.” He chuckled and walked away quickly, waiting for the Weiyang Emperor to announce the news.

Upon reaching the entrance of the Weiyang Palace, Jiang Weiyang did not wait for Jiang Yun’s permission but pushed the door open and walked in, her long skirt trailing across the cold floor paved with immortal jade.

She was in such a hurry, her steps so quick, that she didn’t even bother to lift her skirt.

Jiang Yun, however, seemed to have anticipated her arrival; he stood in the center of the hall with his hands behind his back, waiting with his back to her.

“Father.” Jiang Weiyang stepped forward, about to speak, ready to voice all the doubts and worries in her heart.

But Jiang Yun waved his hand to interrupt her, saying, “I have already arranged a marriage for you. This is a silk pouch I prepared for you in advance; you must keep it safe.” As he spoke, a simple yet elegant black silk pouch, no larger than the palm of a hand, appeared in his hand.

Jiang Weiyang froze, unable to fathom the meaning behind Jiang Yun’s words.

"Tomorrow, I will announce to the world your marriage to Ye Wudao, the Ninth Prince of the ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao."

" Jiang Yun said calmly, though the depths of her eyes betrayed unhidden pain and helplessness.

Jiang Weiyang was puzzled; her beautiful eyes widened, yet she could not make out her father’s expression at that moment.

“Since it is Father’s arrangement, your daughter will naturally agree without any complaints…” She recalled the words of General Zhu Cangning, and her face darkened. She bowed her head and spoke softly.

Seeing her so understanding, Jiang Yun’s heart ached. “I’m telling you this not to make you marry Ye Wudao, but to buy you time.” Jiang Weiyang froze again, then suddenly realized.

“Father, are you suggesting I elope and leave the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty?” She shook her head vigorously, like a rattle, “Your daughter will never leave the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty. At a time like this, how could I possibly abandon the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty to save myself? " "No matter what crisis we face, I will stand by your side, Father, and face it together with you. "Jiang Yun shook his head and said calmly, "The Weiyang Immortal Dynasty is currently caught in an invisible, terrifying vortex. Everyone else can stay, but you cannot. I have encountered a terrifying enemy. Only if you leave here can I focus on dealing with him. "All this time, I haven’t told you the truth: your mother isn’t dead. She’s always been here. When you return to the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, I will ensure the two of you are reunited. "Jiang Weiyang was completely stunned, unable to believe her ears.

Her mother was still alive? Hadn’t her mother passed away from illness right after giving birth to her? “I understand, Father, but in this vast and desolate world, where am I supposed to flee? How can I possibly escape a foe that even you find so terrifying?” Jiang Weiyang was not a stubborn or inflexible person.

She understood that at this moment, staying in the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty might only cause trouble for Jiang Yun and tie his hands.

"I don’t know where it’s safe, but you must remember: stay far away from the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and the Righteous Unity Alliance. Go live somewhere he cannot see or reach." Jiang Yun’s gaze was deep, his voice heavy.
