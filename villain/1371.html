This group looked rather strange; taken individually, none of them were unusual, but to encounter them all together in this remote, desolate, and uninhabited fragmented star system...

Chen Yi simply couldn’t think of any reason that would bring such a group together.

Moreover, each of them gave her a sense of being as vast and unfathomable as the depths of the cosmos.

Especially the young gentleman, who appeared to be resting with his eyes closed, casually seated upon a boulder—he evoked in her a profound sense of mystery and enigma.

Whether it was the woman in the vibrant red gown, radiating charm and grace; the young child who looked only a few years old; or the kind-faced old monk—all showed immense respect toward the young gentleman standing nearby, who seemed like an immortal in exile, treating him as their leader.

This left Chen Yi even more astonished and curious.

What could possibly be the reason that such a group of unfathomable, terrifyingly powerful beings would show such profound respect to that young gentleman?

“It was this young… senior who stepped in to teach us back then…” At that moment, the voice of Layman Huijue from nearby reached Chen Yi’s ears, snapping him back to reality.

She had been so preoccupied with wondering about the identities and backgrounds of this group that she had forgotten it was they who had intervened to save them from the attack by the dark spirits.

Without them, they would likely all have perished at the hands of the dark spirits long ago.

Chen Yi was one who valued repaying kindness. Ignoring the pain from her injuries, she hurriedly rose and walked toward the group to bow in gratitude.

Her companions, witnessing this, were not surprised; clearly, she had already expressed her gratitude before waking up.

“Oh, the little girl has woken up.”

The woman in the red dress noticed Chen Yi approaching. She held a scarlet folding fan in her slender, delicate hand, using it to partially cover her face. Her eyes sparkled with a mischievous gleam, and a seductive smile played at the corners of her lips.

Her skin was as white as snow, delicate enough to break with a touch; her eyebrows were naturally dark and lustrous, unadorned yet vivid, with a touch of cinnabar red at the brow’s center; her lips were lush and glossy.

Her crimson dress blazed like fire, accentuating her unparalleled allure, making her seem like a seductive demoness destined to bring chaos to the realm.

As for the others around her, upon hearing this, they merely glanced warily at Chen Yi, appearing not particularly concerned.

Some even kept their eyes closed, resting as if they hadn’t heard the commotion

, like timeless, indestructible boulders that would remain unmoved even through countless eons.

Chen Yi had lived for countless years and was renowned throughout the Crescent Moon Sect as the “Stone Beauty,” yet this was the first time she had ever heard anyone call her a “little girl.”

A look of bewilderment flashed across her cold yet stunning face, and she froze slightly in place.

The woman in the red skirt continued to cover her mouth and giggle. She had delicate features and a slender, graceful figure, reclining against a boulder slightly wider than a person, positioned closest to the young gentleman beside her.

As Chen Yi approached, he could even catch the faint, fragrant scent wafting from her—a fragrance reminiscent of snow-covered lotus roots in a pond, like blossoms and trees blanketed in snow, bright and dazzling.

“What’s your name, little girl? You’re quite a looker.”

“Would you be interested in serving as a maid by my side—bringing tea and water, massaging my shoulders and back?” The woman in the red skirt smiled radiantly, her tone seductive. With a snap, she closed the folding fan in her hand, then reached out to lift Chen Yi’s chin, as if to scrutinize her features closely.

Chen Yi had a graceful figure, eyebrows as slender as willow leaves, and a pair of phoenix eyes whose bright pupils shone like black gems. Her delicate, straight nose was exquisite, her skin as white and tender as fine porcelain, and her full, shapely breasts were delicately curved.

It was no wonder she was pursued by so many elders at the Crescent Moon Sect—her beauty spoke for itself. Yet at this moment, the red-robed woman’s actions left her utterly dumbfounded, as if she were being teased.

A faint blush spread across her porcelain-white face as she stood there, at a loss, unsure how to respond.

Every single member of this strange group, no matter which one you picked, was undoubtedly far more powerful than she was.

She was well aware of this, not to mention that these people had saved their lives.

Under normal circumstances, Chen Yi would have lost her temper long ago, but now she could only grit her teeth and endure the sensation of goosebumps spreading across her body.

It was the first time in her life she’d been teased—and by a stunningly beautiful woman, no less.

"Enough..."

Just as Chen Yi stood there uneasily, struggling to contain her awkwardness and unease,

the young man, who had been resting with his eyes closed, finally opened them and spoke up to stop the woman in the red dress.

His voice was calm and deep, immediately bringing to mind jade bathed in moonlight—a texture that was both warm and refined, with a touch of nobility.

Even under the cover of night, with the campfire flickering on and off, Chen Yi could clearly see those deep, serene eyes—eyes as inscrutable as an abyss.

She stood there momentarily stunned, noticing that those eyes were also studying her.

Their gazes met for an instant, and for some reason, she couldn’t help but lower her head, feeling her cheeks grow hot.

At that moment, the woman in the red dress quickly withdrew her fan and playfully stuck out her tongue.

Not far away, Huijue the Layman and the others noticed that the people behind the woman in the red dress seemed to stiffen slightly at that moment, as if finding her gesture awkward and bizarre.

"I am Chen Yi. Thank you... for saving my life, Senior."

Chen Yi quickly regained her composure, restoring her clarity and composure.

A hint of hesitation and uncertainty flashed across her cool, striking face, though she wasn’t sure how to address him.

For the man in black robes standing before her appeared far too young.

Of course, she understood that many powerful, unfathomable old masters could not be judged by their physical appearance alone.

“It was merely a small favor; there’s no need to be so formal.”

Gu Changge’s tone remained entirely even; he merely glanced at Chen Yi before looking away.

Had he not sensed a karmic connection to Gu Xian’er in this woman, he wouldn’t have bothered to save her.

As for her companions, saving them was merely an afterthought.

The rampage of the Dark Beings across the heavens was his doing in the first place; unless absolutely necessary, why would he go out of his way to intervene against them?

"I am Chen Yi, a junior disciple of the Crescent Moon Sect. I was traveling with my fellow cultivators to join the Heaven Slaying Alliance when our plans were compromised. We were ambushed and intercepted by the dark spirits. Had you not intervened, Senior, we would likely have perished at their hands long ago."

"I truly have no way to repay your great kindness, Senior. Should you ever need anything in the future, I will go through fire and water for you without hesitation."

Although Gu Changge seemed to regard this as a mere trifle, Chen Yi remained earnestly grateful, never forgetting the kindness shown to him today.

The woman in the red dress’s eyes sparkled. Upon hearing Chen Yi mention the Heaven Slaying Alliance, she paused slightly, then grew increasingly intrigued.

The group behind her also seemed to perk up upon hearing this.

Even the few who had kept their eyes closed opened them and glanced at Chen Yi.

So this group was actually heading to the Heaven Slaying Alliance?

That would make things truly interesting.

Of course, it would be a breeze for them to probe Chen Yi and his group’s intentions, but they couldn’t very well do that in front of Gu Changge.

“I thought that after saying you had nothing to offer in return, you’d volunteer to pledge yourself to me,” the woman in the red dress said, covering her mouth and laughing playfully.

Chen Yi was momentarily taken aback, then realized what she meant. Her face flushed slightly, and she grew stiff and awkward, truly unsure how to respond.

In her spare time, she had naturally heard her disciples discuss such matters.

If one were to go out on a training journey, encounter danger, and be saved by someone, how should one repay them?

If the rescuer was handsome, one would pledge oneself to them for this lifetime; if they were plain and ordinary, one would serve them as a cow or a horse in the next.

When she’d heard that back then, she’d just chuckled to herself, never imagining that one day she’d be teased in exactly this way.

Gu Changge had long been aware of Mu Fuguang’s loose tongue and shameless nature; she shot her a glance but didn’t bother to take it to heart.

“Miss Chen Yi, there’s no need to be so formal. I’d go through fire and water for you without hesitation. That we should meet in such a remote corner of the starry realm is fate itself. Seeing you under attack by dark creatures, how could I possibly stand by and do nothing?”

Gu Changge looked at Chen Yi, smiling faintly. His tone remained as gentle as ever, yet his gaze was utterly unreadable.

Chen Yi seemed unable to bear his gaze; she couldn’t help but lower her head slightly, her delicate hands clenched nervously beneath her sleeves.

She didn’t know what had come over her. In the past, when facing the many elders of the Crescent Moon Sect, she had always remained composed, calm, and unhurried—cool and aloof.

Yet today, she appeared so flustered and uneasy, just like her young female disciples.

“I’m afraid I’ve made a fool of myself before you, Senior. Regardless, for the life-saving favor you’ve bestowed upon me, should the opportunity arise in the future, I, Chen Yi, will certainly repay you.” Chen Yi raised her bright, beautiful eyes and spoke earnestly.

Gu Changge seemed to smile but said nothing more.

Meanwhile, Mu Fuguang and the others standing beside him found Chen Yi’s mention of “repaying” somewhat amusing, their expressions tinged with amusement.

Although Chen Yi and his companions came from extraordinary backgrounds—hailing from powerful, ancient Daoist lineages and realms of Civilization—

in the eyes of these beings, Chen Yi and her companions were, in truth, not much different from ordinary mortals.

As for Gu Changge stepping in to save Chen Yi and the others, it came as quite a surprise to them.

After all, in their minds, Gu Changge had absolutely no connection to the concept of mercy.

After Jie Konglian departed, Gu Changge took Mu Fuguang, the head of Jie Kongling, and Changming Daojun to the Temple of Crossing the Underworld, where they subdued the “old bald donkey” mentioned by Mu Fuguang—that is, the Compassionate Zen Buddha.

The Compassionate Zen Buddha was formed from the transformation of three corpses severed by a Buddha of ancient times; it had developed a self-aware consciousness, though it had originally been nothing more than a corpse stripped of all spiritual wisdom.

Later, nourished by the blessings of the Dao’s source and refined over countless ages, it was reborn one night after being bathed in the Sea of Daoist Thunder and the Flames of Buddha’s Light.

On the banks of the River of Utmost Darkness, he established the Temple of Crossing the Underworld, dedicated to guiding the spirits of the deceased. He brought them into the temple and vowed to save all beings in the underworld.

Without a doubt, the Compassionate Zen Buddha was a formidable figure, possessing overwhelming power and a terror that shook the heavens.

The Temple of Crossing the Underworld stood in a forbidden, uninhabited zone beyond the reach of the outside world—a place where the living dared not tread—which initially filled Mu Fuguang with trepidation.

To have been recognized by Gu Changge, the capabilities of the Compassionate Zen Buddha go without saying.

After Mu Fuguang arrived at the Temple of the Underworld, he battled him for days and nights on end. He drained the magical power of countless spacetime dimensions, shattered numerous parallel eras of the past and future, and severed his opponent’s many past and future incarnations—yet to no avail.

An ordinary Ancestral Dao Realm cultivator would not even last a single round against them; the gap in their levels was simply too vast.

They were beings on the verge of enduring their ninth Celestial Waning Tribulation, having journeyed far, far along this path. In the vast expanse of the cosmos, they were truly without equal in the Ancestral Dao Realm. Even those who had just survived eight Celestial Waning Tribulations—including Chu Gucheng, the former ruler of the vast lands of Xianchu, and even the Demon Ancestor Di Huang—paled in comparison before them and would be easily subdued.

Having reached this stage, their cultivation, Daoist arts, and insights are no longer bound by limitations. A single thought or idea from them can evolve and create countless new Daoist arts.

Enveloping an entire extraordinary realm of civilization with a single thought—whether to instantly extinguish and shatter it, or to reignite and recreate it—is as easy as turning one’s hand.

Of course, in the end, it was Gu Changge who intervened. With an overwhelming, unstoppable force, she decisively subdued the Compassionate Zen Buddha, compelling him to submit and follow her.

Mu Fuguang held back, not using her full strength; she was slightly stronger than the Compassionate Zen Buddha, so it wasn’t a case of them being evenly matched.

Gu Changge didn’t mind her concealment; after all, she wasn’t following him wholeheartedly—she had been forced to join

, so it was only natural for her to keep a low profile.

It was just that he had initially promised Mu Fuguang certain benefits, but he hadn’t delivered on them later.

Later, he led Mu Fuguang, the Compassionate Zen Monk, and others through several restricted territories, “recruiting” a number of powerful subordinates along the way.

The vastness of existence, the boundlessness of time, the mystery of past and present, the countless universes, and the endless expanse of space—how many formidable beings have been born? It is simply beyond counting.

Not everyone chooses to remain within those realms of civilization; “madmen” like the White Bone Ancestral King are far from rare, and as for the recluses and those who shun the world in the uninhabited forbidden zones, they are even more numerous.

However, beings like Mu Fuguang and the monk Bei Ci are, after all, few and far between.

The young child, barely a few years old, who now follows Gu Changge is named Tong Xian. He is also a Ruler of a Forbidden Zone, hailing from the Tong Xian Kiln.

His power pales in comparison to that of Mu Fuguang or the Monk of Compassion, yet he is still far stronger than the average being who has survived eight Celestial Waning Tribulations.

Even among Rulers of the Forbidden Zones, there are naturally differences in power levels.

Mu Fuguang and the Monk Beici belong to the top tier, while Tongxian and the others belong to a lower tier—and of course, there are even lower tiers beyond that.

These forbidden, uninhabited zones within the vast expanse may revive from time to time.

To replenish their vitality and restore their energy, the rulers of these forbidden zones will devour the nearest realms of Civilization, unleashing catastrophes akin to the Dark Turmoil.

Once, when Gu Changge left the Daochang True Realm, he was mistaken by the Spirit Emperor and others of the Lingxu True Realm for an awakened Forbidden Zone Ruler.

Of course, even these forbidden, uninhabited zones cannot escape reckoning when the true Cataclysm arrives. Therefore, sealing off their vital energy and remaining in slumber—falling into hibernation—is often their best means of evading judgment.

Although this causes their vital energy to diminish over time, causing their cultivation to gradually fall from its peak, they can quickly recover once they awaken by consuming vast quantities of blood offerings.

As long as their Dao Fruits remain constant and untouched by the corrupting energies of the Great Calamity, they can endure for eternity, standing high above, overlooking the masses of sentient beings.

Gu Changge traversed the forbidden zones and uninhabited regions surrounding the Yuan Hope Civilization, taking the rulers of those restricted areas with him.

In a sense, this was also a blessing for the surrounding civilizations.

Amid the desolate, cold, and shattered stars, a flickering campfire blazed, occasionally emitting a crackling sound

.

The night passed in silence. After thanking Gu Changge, Chen Yi saw that he remained completely unconcerned, and couldn’t help but feel a wave of helpless, wry laughter welling up inside him.

Perhaps, to Gu Changge, saving them was truly just a trivial matter—as if he’d simply rescued a few ordinary people on a whim.

Yet here they were, taking it so seriously, expressing their gratitude with such solemnity and emotion.

As a result, aside from the woman in the red dress, everyone else looked completely unmoved and uninterested.

This left Chen Yi feeling utterly bewildered and at a loss; it was the first time in her life she had encountered such a situation.

After all, she was a Real Dao Realm cultivator who had survived five Celestial Waning Tribulations.

In any Daoist sect or faction, she would be a pillar of strength—a figure that could not be ignored or looked down upon.

Yet, in the eyes of this group, she seemed no different from the ordinary cultivators one might see anywhere along the road.

In the blink of an eye, a faint streak of pale dawn appeared on the horizon, and the morning light began to spread in the distance.

After extinguishing the campfire, Chen Yi, Huijue the Layman, and the others awoke from their rest and recuperation, their complexions noticeably improved.

Though they had narrowly escaped death, they still bore a heavy responsibility: as the envoy team, they must hasten to the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

The attack by the dark creatures was merely a crisis they had long anticipated along the way.

The true, unknown, and unforeseeable dangers, however, still awaited them at the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Chen Yi and the others still felt a heavy weight on their hearts; they had to set out again, not knowing how many dangers lay ahead on this journey.

Even if they endured a grueling journey to reach the Heaven Slaying Alliance, what would await them there?

“Senior, we must set out now. We bear a heavy responsibility, and the journey is long—we cannot delay. Should the opportunity arise in the future…”

With that, Chen Yi, Huijue the Layman, and the others spoke up, intending to bid farewell to Gu Changge and the others.

In a sense, they were all cast-offs, abandoned by the sects and factions behind them for various reasons.

"What exactly are you going to the Heaven Slaying Alliance for?" Mu Fuguang asked with a look of keen interest.
