Ling Yuling wore a snow-white long dress with a pale green gauze robe draped over it. Her expression was serene, ethereal, and cool as she sat upright across from Gu Changge, holding a teacup and sipping it in small sips. She did not appear particularly distressed by being trapped in the void of time and space, appearing utterly unperturbed.

She had been trapped in the dark void for countless ages, had been tainted by dark matter, and had engaged in a battle of wits and courage against immortal spirits corrupted by it.

To her, this minor setback was merely a ripple in the water—hardly worth her attention.

Of course, the most important factor was that Ling Yuling had complete faith in Gu Changge’s abilities; she knew that even without the spatiotemporal coordinates, he would likely be able to bring her back with ease.

So she hadn’t been particularly worried from the very beginning.

Not to mention, she had just heard Gu Changge say that he had actually left a mark on her, so he could step in to save her life should she ever face a crisis.

That void of time and space where she had been exiled was, to him, as clear as a beacon.

Upon first hearing this, Ling Yuling paused in surprise, unsure whether Gu Changge was telling the truth or not, but she still felt a warmth deep in her heart.

"That mysterious man in white is likely on par with me in strength. He hadn’t actually survived the Ninth Celestial Waning Tribulation, as the rumors suggest; he only managed to give me such a crushing defeat by relying on the Fourth Killing Array he’d prepared in advance.

"If I hadn’t underestimated him, the outcome of that battle wouldn’t have been so frustrating. When I saw him activate the Fourth Killing Array, I was genuinely taken aback…" Ling Yuling explained.

She speculated that the man in white might be a descendant of the Central Lord.

But the Yao Guang Ancestral Court had long since been destroyed, vanishing into the annals of history, and there had been no reports of any descendants of the Central Lord surviving into later generations.

Even the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, which now claims to be a descendant of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court, actually has little bloodline connection to the Yao Guang Ancestral Court.

But considering the recent rumors surrounding the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, could they be related to that princess who emerged from the ruins of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court?

ancestral court?

That princess is very likely the daughter of the Central Lord.

As someone from the same era as the Central Lord, Ling Yuling was indeed unaware that the Central Lord had any descendants, and she found it difficult to verify the authenticity of that mysterious woman’s identity.

But if she is truly the Central Lord’s offspring, why would she become entangled with the Zhengyi Alliance, and likely have connections to the dark spirits?

"I already have a rough idea of this man in white’s identity; you needn’t speculate any further." Suddenly, Gu Changge spoke softly, his eyes calm as a still lake.

Ling Yuling looked up at him in surprise and asked, “Is he really a descendant of the Central Lord?”

She didn’t know how Gu Changge had found out.

But if he said so, then it was likely very close to the truth.

“You could say so, or you could say not.”

Gu Changge nodded slightly, then shook his head.

Ling Yuling still looked puzzled. Her cold, flawless face bore a faint frown as she felt that Gu Changge’s words were rather vague and unclear.

“Whether it is or isn’t, that doesn’t really matter.”

Gu Changge smiled faintly and said, “If you feel you’ve been taken advantage of by him and it’s a bit embarrassing, then don’t be so careless the next time you meet him.”

Ling Yuling nearly spit out the tea she was sipping. She gave Gu Changge a reproachful look, desperately wanting to roll her eyes at him.

Was he really going to make light of something like this?

"She’s here."

Suddenly, Gu Changge said softly.

“Who?” Ling Yuling froze for a moment, then sensed something and looked outside the courtyard.

A tall, slender woman with a delicate, snow-white neck, shrouded in a mist of rosy light, walked toward them. Her jet-black hair flowed freely, held in place by a jade hairpin. She wore a brocaded robe embroidered with a celestial phoenix, and her silk-and-jade

boots, untouched by dust, exuded an air of defiance toward heaven and earth, an arrogance that seemed to tower over the nine heavens.

Yet her face was shrouded in a chaotic mist, as if veiled, making her features indistinct. Only her round, fair chin—as smooth as jade—was visible. Even from this outline alone, one could faintly glimpse her peerless grace and breathtaking beauty.

“Who is she?” Ling Yuling’s eyes betrayed her confusion as she scrutinized this unfamiliar woman she had never met before.

The fact that she could enter this courtyard without an announcement clearly indicated she was deeply trusted by Gu Changge and bore a token of his trust.

Logically speaking, aside from the period she spent campaigning against the Zhengyi Alliance, she had never left the Heaven Slaying Alliance; the rest of the time, she had remained by Gu Changge’s side.

When had such a mysterious woman appeared by Gu Changge’s side?

And she was so powerful—judging by her aura alone, even an average Ancestral Dao Realm cultivator would likely be no match for her.

Could she have appeared during the time he was exiled to the Void Realm?

“She is the very person you were just thinking of,” Gu Changge said with a faint smile. He reached out, picked up the teapot, and poured a cup of tea.

The newcomer was, of course, Ji Hanjing.

"Greetings, Senior Yu Ling..."

Ji Hanjing sensed Ling Yuling’s confusion and smiled slightly, though she offered no explanation. After all, when she was still the spirit of the Scavenger’s Book, Ling Yuling had no idea she even existed.

Gu Changge had never told anyone about this matter either.

When it came to understanding Gu Changge and the time spent by her side, Ling Yuling couldn’t compare to her.

Ling Yuling was a smart woman and knew when to hold her tongue; she didn’t ask any further questions and simply returned the smile.

"What business requires me to make a personal visit? You must know I’m not exactly free to slip away these days."

A faint fragrance wafted through the air as Ji Hanjing sat down beside Gu Changge. Without hesitation, she lifted her veil and placed it on the jade table, then picked up the cup of tea he had just poured for her, took a sip, and asked.

“I’d like to prepare a gift for the Zhengyi Alliance—a token of congratulations for attending the ceremony at the Xian Dao Alliance,” Gu Changge said with a faint smile.

“So it seems you don’t intend to take action against the Immortal Path Alliance?” Ling Yuling looked slightly surprised, though she felt a sense of relief.

As long as Gu Changge didn’t take action against the Immortal Path Alliance and didn’t interfere with its establishment, that was fine.

Currently, all corners of Cangmang were closely watching the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s moves, eager to gauge Gu Changge’s stance on the Xian Dao Alliance’s formation.

If the Heaven Slaying Alliance were to disregard all consequences and directly attack the Immortal Path Alliance at this moment, it would likely provoke widespread outrage.

The Zhengyi Alliance would also find a pretext to unite with the various Daoist lineages and civilizations across Cangmang to collectively oppose the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

"Is that the image you have of me—someone who disregards consequences and fails to distinguish between right and wrong?"

Gu Changge smiled and said, “The Immortal Alliance was established to counter the Dark Beings; it bears the heavy burden of the safety and security of the entire Vast Expanse. How could I possibly be so blind to the bigger picture at a time like this?”

At this point, he paused, and the smile faded from his face. “Not only will I not stand in your way, but I will actively support and assist you. Any force that attempts to prevent the formation of the Immortal Alliance will be an enemy of my Heaven Slaying Alliance.”

Ling Yuling didn’t believe for a moment that he was truly being so generous.

It seemed that the benefits of establishing the Immortal Alliance at this time far outweighed the consequences of intervening to block it.

From another perspective, the emergence of the Immortal Alliance could also help the Heaven Slaying Alliance keep the dark spirits in check, allowing them to focus all their efforts on confronting the Zhengyi Alliance without any rear-guard concerns.

Ji Hanjing wasn’t particularly concerned about the Immortal Alliance; she simply wanted to know what Gu Changge wanted her to do next.

After receiving Gu Changge’s telepathic message, she immediately found a way to slip away from the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty and rushed to the Heaven Slaying Alliance to meet him.

Although her status in the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty was now incredibly exalted, and she held great power, presiding over the academy and the court,

that didn’t mean she could leave at will. If she stayed too long and someone discovered she’d secretly slipped out of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty to secretly “meet” with the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, what would the consequences be?

“What is that grand gift?” Ji Hanjing asked succinctly.

Gu Changge shook her head slightly and said calmly, “There’s no need to rush. I’ll tell you what to do in a moment.”

Ji Hanjing nodded, said nothing more, and began sipping his tea on his own.

Ling Yuling, however, fluttered her eyelashes slightly and lowered her gaze. It seemed this matter was quite confidential—was it necessary to keep it from her?

A vague sense of irritation welled up within her, and she suddenly found it impossible to calm her mind.

After all this time, Gu Changge still seemed unable to truly trust her.

"Since the Immortal Alliance has made its decision, I’ll take my leave now to inform the rest of the Heaven Slaying Alliance. By now, they must be as restless as ants on a hot pan, unable to wait any longer." Ling Yuling smiled as she spoke, rising to take her leave.

With her hands tucked into her wide sleeves, she smiled, her appearance elegant and dignified, and she gave Ji Hanjing a slight nod.

Ji Hanjing glanced at her but didn’t notice anything amiss; he simply felt that once Ling Yuling left, he would no longer need to hold back in his conversation with Gu Changge.

“No rush. Didn’t you just want to know whether that man in white is really a descendant of the Central Lord?” Gu Changge smiled, wrapping an arm around Ling Yuling’s waist and guiding her back to her seat.

“Didn’t you say it didn’t matter whether he was a descendant of the Central Lord or not?” Ling Yuling asked with a smile, though she didn’t refuse Gu Changge and sat back down.

For she had noticed that when Gu Changge mentioned the Central Lord, Ji Hanjing—who had been lifting his teacup to drink—had suddenly felt a slight tremor in his hand.

Although it quickly returned to normal, she had still noticed it.

Ling Yuling wasn’t stupid. Connecting this to what Gu Changge had just said, and recalling the ancient, mysterious woman who had suddenly appeared at the ruins of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court not long ago,

Ji Hanjing’s true identity and background were practically staring her in the face.

“Since Han Jing is here today, I was just about to explain the whole story to her. If you’d like to hear it, that’s fine too,” Gu Changge said with a smile.

Originally, Ji Hanjing should have known about this when he was helping her reconstruct the past scene that day

, but due to certain contingency measures set in place by the Ruler of Destiny, Ji Hanjing was forced to remain in that segment of spacetime, unable to proceed further.

Consequently, there were many truths that Ji Han-jing remained unaware of until now.

Gu Changge had originally intended to keep this part of the truth hidden, ensuring Ji Hanjing would faithfully serve him. But over time, judging by her various actions, she was genuinely working for him; aside from her desire for revenge, her heart held no other ulterior motives.

There was no longer any need to keep this part of the truth hidden from her.

Ji Hanjing’s face, shrouded in a haze of confusion, showed no trace of emotion at that moment.

She simply wished to listen quietly as Gu Changge recounted the full story—every detail and the entire sequence of events.

A descendant of the Central Lord?

Her father had only two daughters: herself, Ji Hanjing, and her younger sister, Ji Hanshuang.

She had devoted her entire life to cultivation; even after becoming the Empress of Yao Guang, she remained childless, pouring all her energy into her spiritual practice and the governance of the nation.

In her view, a daughter’s personal desires would only cloud her judgment and disturb her peace of mind; they must be severed like inner demons and karmic obstacles.

Therefore, the descendant of the Central Lord could only be a blood relative of her younger sister, Ji Hanjing.

She had indeed sensed that her younger sister still had living descendants in the world.

"The Central Lord once had a great enemy from the Primordial Era. That enemy called himself the Master of Destiny. After the Primordial Era ended, he lay dormant until the birth of the Vast Expanse. Following the end of the Age of Black Calamity, the multiverse lay in ruins, its vital energy depleted, and all races in decline. The Master of Destiny, having lain dormant for so long, schemed against the Yao Guang Ancestral Court to bring about its collapse and disintegration in order to confront the Central Lord..."

"During the Age of Black Calamity, the Central Lord paid a tremendous price to seal the Lord of the Heavens. Even after the Age of Black Calamity ended, he remained gravely wounded and unable to recover. The Lord of Destiny seized this golden opportunity, attempting to devour the Central Lord’s Dao Fruit and Primordial Source. Unfortunately, the Central Lord possessed the Disc of Transcending Calamity, the supreme treasure of the First Civilization."

"Despite great effort, the Lord of Destiny could not absorb it; instead, he was trapped by the Central Lord within the Disk of Transcending Calamity in the boundless spacetime. To exhaust the Central Lord’s remaining vitality and primordial source, and to gradually erode his

remaining fortune, the Lord of Destiny employed the Art of the Five Aggregates to split off a strand of his physical form to escape. Subsequently, that strand of his physical form took the name Bo Youkang…"

Gu Changge’s tone was unhurried, as if he were simply recounting a trivial matter.

Yet Ji Hanjing’s mind drifted further and further away with every word, as if transported back to that ancient ancestral hall—once flourishing and mighty, vast and timeless, a place where all generations came to pay their respects.

Yet, when Gu Changge finally mentioned the name “Bo Youkang,” Ji Hanjing froze in her tracks, then stood there as rigid as a statue.

She couldn’t believe her ears and found herself muttering, “Impossible… impossible…”

Ling Yuling didn’t know why Ji Hanjing’s reaction was so intense.

Her attention was focused on the Five Aggregates doctrine Gu Changge had mentioned.

The so-called Five Aggregates are the Form Aggregate, the Sensation Aggregate, the Perception Aggregate, the Mental Formations Aggregate, and the Consciousness Aggregate. In certain civilizations, these correspond to the Five Elements of Daoist doctrine, positing that all material phenomena in the world are composed of the combination of these Five Aggregates.

"The Master of Destiny’s form of the Five Aggregates transformed into an ordinary cultivator named Bo Youkao.Through various schemes and stratagems, Bo Youkang skillfully befriended Ji Hanshuang, the young daughter of the Central Lord. The two fell in love and were successfully married. Subsequently, Bo Youkang staged a false accident, creating the illusion of his death and the dissolution of his spiritual essence, whereupon that strand of the Five Aggregates entered Ji Hanshuang’s womb…” Gu Changge’s expression remained unchanged as he continued to speak in a calm tone.
