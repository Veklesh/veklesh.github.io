"I greet Your Highness, the Princess."

General Zhu Cangning, dressed in civilian clothes, had a rugged face with chiseled features and deep-set eyes that betrayed a steely resolve forged by years of hardship.

Upon seeing Princess Wei Yang arrive, a look of immense relief spread across his face, and he bowed in greeting.

“General, there is no need for such formalities. May I ask why you have summoned me?” Jiang Weiyang asked, puzzled.

Upon hearing this, Zhu Cangning did not answer directly. Instead, he looked around to ensure no one else had followed them. Only then did he speak in a solemn voice, “Princess Wei Yang, please do not be perplexed. This old servant will explain everything to you shortly and resolve the many questions that have been weighing on your mind.”

“Within this secluded cottage, this old servant has already deployed the Deceive-Heaven Formation. For the time being, no one will be able to detect or spy upon this place—not even His Imperial Majesty himself.”

“As for your maid, I will find a way to erase her memories of this matter shortly.” Upon hearing this, Princess Weiyang paused slightly, her expression turning grave and solemn.

Zhu Cangning’s grave demeanor spoke volumes about the gravity of the situation.

She had been puzzled lately as well—why had her father been acting so strangely? It didn’t seem like him at all.

So she was speculating that the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty might be facing some kind of crisis.

Or perhaps her father had sensed that some terrible event was about to unfold, which was why he had been acting so strangely.

It was precisely for this reason that she had readily accepted the arrangements and demands of the Emperor of Weiyang, putting the greater good first.

And as the Divine Martial General of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, Zhu Cangning would never resort to deceit or spread rumors to mislead the people.

"Your Highness, since your return from the Ancient Realm of Yao Guang, you must have also noticed some unusual behavior on the part of His Majesty. This old servant has served at His Majesty’s side for quite some time. Even before he unified the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, I fought alongside him in countless battles, earning military merits and establishing a legacy—I know him quite well.His Majesty’s affection for you is plain for all to see. How could he possibly be so reckless in choosing a husband for you…?"

Zhu Cangning sighed, then began to explain.

Jiang Weiyang nodded thoughtfully; she found Zhu Cangning’s words quite convincing.

At the same time, this was precisely what had puzzled her all along.

"General, I understand what you’re saying, but why would Father act this way? Has something happened that I’m unaware of?" she asked.

Lately, the situation in the Weiyang Divine Dynasty had remained calm. It hadn’t been thrown into chaos by the approaching Black Calamity, nor had it joined the Righteous Unity Alliance.

Could this merely be the calm before the storm?

“I suspect His Majesty has… been infected by dark matter.”

Zhu Cangning looked at Jiang Weiyang and shook his head. After a moment of silence, he spoke, his tone incredibly heavy, tinged with a fear and unease he couldn’t quite articulate.

This unease stemmed from a fear of the unknown and a sense of bewilderment.

For even he did not know why even Emperor Wei Yang had become tainted by the Dark Matter.

“What?”

Jiang Weiyang was struck as if by lightning. Her eyes, veiled by white curtains, widened instantly, and she froze in place like a statue, unable to believe what she had heard.

Her father—that wise and valiant ruler, revered by all generations, whose might shook the universe—had actually fallen victim to the Dark Matter?

How could this be? How could such a thing happen to him?

Zhu Cangning had long anticipated Jiang Weiyang’s reaction.

When he first made that conjecture, the shock and horror he felt were no less than what Jiang Weiyang was experiencing now; it took him a long time to calm down.

Of course, he could not guarantee whether this conjecture was true.

But every clue and sign pointed to this possibility.

“General, on what basis did you make this guess?”

Jiang Weiyang forced herself to stay calm, but her voice trembled slightly, tinged with a hint of fear and dread.

To her, this news was like the sky falling—terrifying and impossible to believe.

Zhu Cangning said in a solemn voice, “His Majesty once journeyed deep into various realms of time and space in search of a breakthrough. On one occasion, he encountered a terrifying enemy unlike any he had ever faced before. He suffered severe injuries and nearly did not return. Within the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, only a handful of people know of this, and I am one of them.”

Jiang Weiyang was increasingly shocked and found it hard to believe. If even her nearly invincible father had been gravely wounded and nearly failed to return, just how terrifying must that enemy have been?

Yet Emperor Wei Yang had never once mentioned this to her.

"His Majesty told me at the time that that figure was undoubtedly one of the most terrifying beings he had ever encountered."

"Even then, he speculated that in the future, the vast expanse of the cosmos might face an unimaginable catastrophe. The scene he unexpectedly encountered in the depths of time and space might be connected to the future fate of the entire cosmos.

"A terrifying dark force is brewing in the depths of time and space, like a volcano that has slept for countless eons, waiting for the moment it erupts." Zhu Cangning’s gaze was heavy as he continued.

As he spoke these words, even he felt waves of suffocation and helplessness wash over him; he could smell the thick scent of blood and fire and see endless darkness.

"Are you saying that His Majesty once encountered the source of the dark forces in the depths of time and space?" Jiang Weiyang, quick-witted as she was, quickly grasped Zhu Cangning’s meaning. Her fair complexion turned pale, and her slender frame trembled slightly as she, too, felt an indescribable fear and oppressive weight.

"I don’t know; this is merely my conjecture. I’m not entirely sure why His Majesty chose to tell me this at the time—perhaps he had a deeper purpose."

"If it weren’t for His Majesty’s strange behavior lately, I wouldn’t have even considered that possibility. After all, the injury he sustained back then never fully healed; it flares up frequently, and he has to consciously suppress it."

" Zhu Cangning sighed, her face filled with resignation.

Jiang Weiyang fell silent. If Zhu Cangning hadn’t told her all this today, she likely would never have known.

Of course, this was likely also because, when these events first occurred, she was still sealed within the Daoyuan and had not yet emerged into the world.

Given Emperor Wei Yang’s temperament, he would never have voluntarily told her any of this, causing her needless worry.

"This old servant surmises that His Majesty chose a husband for Your Highness so hastily because he feared that should anything happen to him in the future, there would be no one left to protect you. Thus, he sought to pave the way and ensure your safety in advance. Faced with the Dark Matter, even His Majesty is powerless; we, his subjects, do not know how to assist him either."

“And if this matter were to leak to the outside world, it would be no less devastating to the entire Weiyang Immortal Dynasty than a mountain collapse or a tsunami. The consequences would be unimaginable. After much deliberation, I have decided to inform Your Highness.” Zhu Cangning’s gaze remained heavy.

Jiang Weiyang struggled to recover from this earth-shattering revelation.

She had no idea what to do now; she could only feel a deep sense of helplessness.

After all, she was merely a young woman, inexperienced in the ways of the world. Raised in seclusion within the palace gardens since childhood, she had never faced any major trials or tribulations—she was, in the truest sense, a flower grown in a greenhouse.

At first, aside from sheer panic, her mind went blank; she had absolutely no idea how to handle all of this.

Of course, she understood what Zhu Cangning meant: he was urging her not to be too headstrong, to prioritize the greater good, and not to treat this matchmaking process as a game.

This might well determine her future fate.

"Fu Yi is a young man whom this old servant has watched grow up. His character and disposition are trustworthy, and his talent is beyond question. Just now at the pavilion, Your Highness must have witnessed his abilities."

“In the coming days, Your Highness is likely to face no small amount of trouble. I have taken the liberty of assigning Fu Yi to your side to help you navigate these challenges and discern a suitable husband.”

Zhu Cangning suddenly bowed his hands in respect.

Jiang Weiyang, still reeling from the shocking news she’d just received—her head buzzing and her mind not yet back to normal—was taken completely aback by these words.

What does this mean?

Though her mind was in turmoil, she was no fool. It seemed the General of Divine Valor was offering this advice with good intentions on the surface, but he clearly had ulterior motives.

Was he placing his own offspring by her side so he could have the first shot at her?

“Weiyang thanks the General for his kindness.”

Jiang Weiyang didn’t know what the Divine Martial General’s true intentions were, and at this moment, she found it difficult to refuse his offer.

A look of relief flashed across Zhu Cangning’s face.

Although his words had been somewhat startling, they weren’t entirely groundless; they stemmed from his own speculations and concerns.

Should anything happen to Emperor Wei Yang, the Wei Yang Immortal Dynasty would likely face a catastrophic collapse.

And at that moment, the figure standing behind the Emperor of Weiyang would likely step in.

With that person present, the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty would not collapse, and all problems would be easily resolved.

Having served the Emperor of Weiyang for so long, Zhu Cangning was well aware that behind the Emperor of Weiyang stood a mysterious figure.

That figure has played an indispensable role in the Emperor of Weiyang’s rise to his current position.

When Jiang Weiyang left the Secluded Cottage, she was still somewhat dazed.

Zhu Fuyi, who had been waiting outside, saw this and followed behind her, looking every bit the bodyguard.

From a distance, he saw his grandfather, Zhu Cangning, nod at him.

A surge of excitement and delight immediately flashed in Zhu Fu Yi’s eyes. After all, compared to the other young prodigies, his chances of winning Princess Wei Yang’s heart had just increased significantly.

Having left the banquet, Ye Wudao returned here. He suppressed the arrogant and haughty expression on his face and

and bowed, saying, “Senior Sister Xingying.”

The blind old woman nodded slightly. Her eyes were a silvery-white, as if containing a sky full of stars, dazzlingly beautiful—she was none other than the Star-Echo Old Woman who had once appeared in the Vast Lands of Xianchu.

She had already fled before the Xianchu Vast Lands were destroyed, and had never cared about their fate.

This included her junior disciple, the White-Eyebrow Star Lord, who had once served as the Star Lord of the Vast Lands of Xianchu—she had not cared for him either.

She let him be captured by the Xiyuan Holy Hall, where he remains imprisoned in their Spacetime Prison to this day.

Ye Wudao replied respectfully, “Compared to you, Senior Sister, this level of cultivation is nothing.”

On his way back here, he had already received a telepathic message from this senior sister; she had also arrived at the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty.

Ye Wudao looked surprised at her arrival and the telepathic message.

He rarely had contact with the Old Woman Xingying and the others, but he was indeed a fellow disciple of theirs.

In his youth, he had been guided by a Azure Dragon and carried by a Azure Phoenix to a place called Yinxu Cave, where he cultivated under the guidance of a mysterious figure who claimed to be the Master of Yinxu Cave, reaping numerous benefits.

He could be considered a disciple of Yinxu Cave.

However, Yinxu Cave had a strict rule: it valued karmic affinity and, regardless of one’s background, forbade mentioning anything related to Yinxu Cave outside its walls.

Not even his father, the ruler of the ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao, was aware that he had ever had this opportunity.

The blind old woman before him held a high rank and lineage within Yinxu Cave, and her true strength was unfathomable.

No matter how arrogant and self-important Ye Wudao was, he dared not show disrespect in her presence.

Moreover, in his eyes, the incredibly mysterious Master of the Yinxu Cave was immeasurably more formidable than his own father, the ruler of the ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao.

“Since you’ve returned, come with me to present a grand gift to Emperor Weiyang. Judging by your expression, you must have suffered a setback with his daughter.”

The old woman Xingying rose from her meditation cushion and walked toward the exit of the hall.

Ye Wudao hesitated slightly but followed her anyway, asking in confusion, “I wonder what kind of grand gift Senior Sister intends to present to the Emperor of Weiyang?”

He sensed that something was off about his senior sister’s mood; she seemed to be harboring a suppressed rage.

Besides, with her going over so brazenly, would Emperor Weiyang even grant her an audience?

Could it be that Emperor Weiyang is also connected to the Yinxu Cave?

“No need to ask. For you, what follows will be a fortunate turn of events. This was personally mentioned by our Master; Jiang Yun simply cannot refuse.”

Old Woman Xingying walked ahead, speaking in a calm tone. With a flick of her wide sleeves, the void blurred, and a golden path emerged, piercing through the space and time before them, carrying Ye Wudao away.
