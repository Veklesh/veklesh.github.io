With a flick of her jade-like hand, the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan enveloped herself and Gu Xian'er in two streams of light, leaving this realm of time and space behind. In the very next moment, they appeared in a world as clear and pristine as a mirror, reflecting the mountains, rivers, and white clouds.

A light mist swirled at their feet, and the emerald lake lay as clear as a wash, resembling a pristine paradise beyond the mundane world.

Gu Xian’er gazed at this world, momentarily startled, then quickly realized that this must be the Daoist retreat where the Sect Master resided.

"Aside from thanking you for your care during my time at the Crescent Moon Sect, there is actually another matter I wish to discuss with you." The Holy Maiden Xi Yuan led Gu Xian'er as they walked slowly through this world, speaking softly.

“Is it something to do with the Heaven Slaying Alliance?”

Gu Xian’er was quick-witted and had a hunch that the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan’s memory loss back then was likely due to the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

She had even suspected that Gu Changge might have been behind it.

“It’s not exactly about the Heaven Slaying Alliance. Actually, my master, Xini, wishes to meet with you.” The Holy Maiden Xi Yuan shook her head slightly and said.

"Xiyu?"

Gu Xian’er was taken aback.

She had indeed heard of such a figure—said to be the founder of the Yuan Hope Civilization and one of the sages who had once resisted the Black Calamity, as well as the organizer of the movement.

Many people were more accustomed to calling her the Western Saint, but she was a very mysterious figure who had almost never appeared in the mortal world.

"Senior Xi wants to see me?"

Gu Xian'er remained puzzled.

The Xiyuan Holy Maiden nodded. “It is my Master’s wish. I don’t know why she gave this order either. Rest assured, my Master is a kind person with no ill intent. She likely just wants to ask you some questions about the Heaven Slaying Alliance.”

With that, she led the way ahead, guiding Gu Xian’er as they walked through this world that resembled a mirror.

Gu Xian’er fell silent for a moment, then quietly followed behind her.

The reason the Palace of Defying Fate had chosen to cooperate with the Heaven Slaying Alliance was, in fact, because she had personally assured the Gu family’s ancestors that she would endorse the alliance.

For she was certain that the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance was none other than Gu Changge.

Although the two had not seen each other in a long time—their last meeting had been during the invasion of the Immortal Spirit Civilization in the Prosperous Dao True Realm—

But an inner intuition told her that Gu Changge could not possibly be lying dormant in the Vast Expanse of the cosmos.

And who else could the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance—whose origins remain unknown to the world—be, if not him?

Back in the Prosperous Dao True Realm—or, to be precise, back when they were still in the Upper Realm—Gu Changge’s actions were often criticized by the world, but Gu Xian’er had always believed he had his own reasons and goals.

Just as it had been from the very beginning… In her view, the raison d’être of the Heaven Slaying Alliance was, in fact, no different from that of the Palace of Defiance; the only distinction lay in their distinct styles of operation.

This world was vast and boundless. The lake’s surface was calm, shimmering with ripples, and wisps of celestial mist drifted through the air. One could even see, beneath the water, small worlds as crystalline and dazzling as scattered bubbles. This power to effortlessly create worlds, stabilize the flow of time and space, and establish a realm of its own was, at the very least, a technique belonging to the Realm of the End of the Path.

Deep within, upon the crystal-clear lake, lies a simple courtyard—high walls and green tiles, floating quietly on the surface, with emerald willows and green bamboo, brimming with vitality. Amidst the drifting mist, a slender, graceful figure seemed to be shrouded within. From the pavilion high above, Gu Xian’er wasn’t sure if it was her imagination, but she felt, in a fleeting glimpse, that the figure seemed somewhat familiar, as if she had seen it somewhere before.

"My Master is up in the pavilion; I won’t be going up." Upon arriving here, the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan paused and chose to remain where she stood.

Gu Xian’er nodded and stepped toward the lake, where ripples rippled across the water, making her way up to the pavilion.

"You’re here…"

Before she even pushed the door open, she heard a voice from within, gentle as a clear spring. Though the voice was gentle, Gu Xian’er felt as though she had heard it somewhere before.

Soon, she climbed the bamboo ladder all the way to the highest pavilion railing, whereupon the figure standing there turned around, a faint smile on her face as she looked at her.

"How could this be..."

As the white mist dissipated, the woman’s face came into view before Gu Xian’er. She froze in place, her eyes wide with disbelief.

At the same time, within the boundless domain of the Palace of Defying Fate, where the Ruler resided, after everyone had withdrawn, the Ruler—who had been standing with his hands behind his back—suddenly looked up at the deep, impenetrable void before him and asked calmly, “Are we really going to do this?”

As his words fell, that void suddenly shattered without a sound, like a mirror that had never existed in this world. Behind it lay a vast, abyssal cavern—its depth unfathomable, its origins unknown—that seemed capable of swallowing up all existing and non-existing matter.

"Cough, cough..." After a long while, a coughing sound came from within, as if an old man, decayed to the extreme, were struggling there, trying to break free.

And that stench of decay was so utterly unimaginable that it could not be stopped; even a single wisp drifting out caused the surrounding spacetime to twist and disintegrate, ceasing to exist—it could even cause a cultivator’s Dao Fruit to rot away.

Even the Confinement Master wore a look of apprehension, his brows tightly furrowed as he retreated backward, unwilling to be tainted by the putrid aura seeping out.

"Cough, cough…"

"We have no other choice now. If there is even the slightest chance, we must seize it."

The voice from within was ancient—so ancient it seemed to have existed since the dawn of creation. Hearing it, one could distinctly sense the passage of life and time, and envision scenes of the heavens and earth withering away, the cosmos growing old.

The Cave Master’s brow furrowed even deeper as he retreated, looking deeply apprehensive, as if within that pitch-black, bottomless abyss lay a presence capable of striking fear into the very heavens and earth.

“Do you not even know the true origins of the Alliance Master of the Heaven Slaying Alliance?” he asked again in a deep voice.

“If I knew, why would I have delayed making a decision for so long?”

"Every time that aged voice echoes, an endless tide of decay surges forth from that place, causing the surrounding spacetime to collapse and shatter—utterly unable to withstand its onslaught."

The Master fell silent, then asked again, “Is Gu Xian’er of the Gu family the one you have chosen?”

“Haha, are you worried about something?” That ancient, decaying voice had grown faint and distant.

The Ruler furrowed his brows.

Moments later, a faint, eerie voice finally emerged from the depths of that vast, bottomless, terrifying abyss.

"In the future, a being will emerge who transcends the King of Anomalies. That being will revolutionize everything, unleashing an unimaginable transformation. That being originates from the realm between all living beings and the spirits. Only after his emergence will the True Path and the Path of Supreme Shattering ascend, and only then will the vast expanse of the cosmos glimpse a glimmer of hope."

Upon hearing this, the Ruler asked, “Is the being you speak of—the King who transcends the Anomalies—the Alliance Master of the Heaven Slaying Alliance?”

“Heh, or perhaps…”

"The leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance... whether such a person even exists, or ever has existed, is unknown. You cannot possibly fathom it, nor will you ever understand."

That eerie voice grew low and profound, its aura of decay thickening until it seemed poised to burst forth from this expanse of time and space, spreading throughout the myriad realms of the cosmos.

The Confinement Master’s brows furrowed deeply.

What does this mean?

Could it be that the Alliance Master of the Heaven Slaying Alliance never actually existed? Or was his origin beyond their comprehension and imagination?

"No one knows how he came to be. He never belonged to any realm of the Old Universe or the New Universe. He both exists and does not exist; he is like a figure drifting through the vast, dreamlike void of endless sentient beings..."

The decaying voice murmured, then grew fainter and fainter; the profound abyss within it slowly vanished, as if it would soon fade into oblivion.

"Isn’t the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance also a Spirit Ancestor?"

"Then who is the Spirit Ancestor?"

"Gu Xian'er?"

The Alliance Master gazed at the cavern, which had vanished completely, and fell into deep thought.

Within the spacetime of the New Cosmic Order, there exists a being known as the Spirit Ancestor. He originates from the hearts of all living beings. It is likely that at first, he was not powerful—perhaps even weak—far inferior to the ancient primordial spirits. Yet he would gradually ascend to the highest heights, reaching a pinnacle unattainable by all other beings.

His emergence represents the hope of all living beings, so he will eventually grow to a point where the Heavenly Dao fears him.

For so long, the Palace of Defiance had been awaiting the Spirit Ancestor’s emergence and descent.

Over the next few days, the entire Nine Heavens Realm was thrown into a frenzy. The high-ranking officials of the Palace of Defying Fate gathered and issued an order: the Nine Heavens Tomb would be severed from the realm, and all sentient beings within the Nine Heavens Realm would be relocated and moved far away from this place.

The Nine Heavens Tomb would shatter sooner or later, and the remaining minor avatars of Heaven sealed within would see the light of day once more. With the Nine Heavens’ current strength, it would be difficult to withstand the revived minor avatars of Heaven; the revival of even a single one would plunge the Nine Heavens into great trouble.

After the high-ranking officials of the Palace of Defiance issued their decree, all races and sentient beings across the various continents and living stars of the Nine Heavens began preparing for the migration, taking with them the vast reserves of power accumulated by Luo Yue.

Naturally, the commotion in the Nine Heavens could not be concealed from the Vast Expanse. Many civilizations from the True Realms and super-multiverses were focusing their attention here, eager to discern the Nine Heavens’ next move.

Could it be that, like other super-multiverses, they would carve out a new world for the races and sentient beings within to inhabit?

Each of the Nine Heavens’ realms was immense, boundless, and home to more than a hundred million sentient beings. Among them, life-sustaining planets guarded by beings from the Dao Realm could each be called a great clan.

And in the Nine Heavens, there are as many as ten thousand such great clans.

BOOM!!!

Above the heavens, ancient warships rumbled and shone brilliantly, their aura permeating the air. Vast quantities of resources and spiritual artifacts were being transported away, while powerful cultivators were tasked with opening Sumeru Spaces to store the clan’s resources.

In the shattered Nine Heavens, within a somewhat shabby little tavern, the Grave Keeper sat quietly, gazing at the scene before him with a mix of emotion and nostalgia.

After the great war with Chengtian, the Nine Heavens had essentially been completely shattered. The clans and sentient beings within had all been relocated to the other heavens.

Yet now, regardless of which layer they hailed from, all the clans were about to embark on a journey away from their homeland.

"It really is hard to let go..."

The Grave Keeper tilted his head back and drained his glass in one gulp, then looked around the sparsely populated tavern. The owner was a very old man with a face full of wrinkles, one eye blind, and a noticeable limp.

At that moment, there were only the two of them in the tavern. The owner was carefully and diligently wiping down the tables; though they were quite old, they still looked spotless and immaculate.

"Aren’t you going to leave?" the Grave Keeper asked.

The two clearly knew each other, so his tone was casual and natural.

“Leave…”

"I won’t be around much longer either. If you want to leave, go ahead. I’ll stay here to see them off on their final journey. If everyone else leaves, they’ll probably feel lonely too." The owner sighed softly and shook his head.

"Of all the people from back then, only you and I remain..."

The gravekeeper took a swig of wine, his gaze growing distant. He had once been a refined and affable middle-aged man, but now his hair hung loose, completely devoid of his former air of detachment.

Even if a being from the Ninth Heaven were here now, they could not connect this figure with the towering form that once battled He Chengtian. The innkeeper said little, continuing to diligently wipe down the tables and benches. In a daze, he glimpsed the silhouettes of those comrades-in-arms who had once stood shoulder to shoulder against the heavens.

The tavern owner said nothing more, continuing to diligently wipe down the tables and benches, his mind drifting as he glimpsed the figures of those comrades-in-arms who had once stood shoulder to shoulder against the heavens.
