With the current situation in the Vast Expanse in such chaos and turmoil, the Emperor of Weiyang deliberated at length before finally deciding to pin all his hopes on the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Gu Changge, the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, is the first person he has ever encountered whom he cannot fathom—a figure he finds utterly inscrutable.

Sending his daughter to the Heaven Slaying Alliance was, in a sense, a high-stakes gamble on his part.

The reason he dared to speak these words to Jiang Weiyang right now was because this was the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s territory; no matter how bold the master of Yinxu Cave might be, he would not dare to venture this far.

Therefore, for him, this was actually the safest place.

In the courtyard, Gu Changge sipped his tea leisurely, listening to Emperor Jiang Yun’s words without rushing to respond.

Jiang Yun had not brought up the matter of cooperating with him, which was somewhat unexpected, but from this alone, it was clear that Jiang Yun was a shrewd man.

Moreover, Jiang Yun’s guess was correct: from start to finish, Gu Changge had never regarded the Fate Master or the Righteous Unity Alliance as a threat; his focus had always been on those deeply hidden, high-profile targets.

As for a mere "Master of Destiny," whether Gu Changge chose to deal with him or not was merely a matter of whim.

“Now that you mention it, I am indeed somewhat intrigued.” Gu Changge looked at Jiang Yun, his piercing gaze seeming to probe, as if he intended to see right through all of Jiang Yun’s thoughts.

The Emperor of Weiyang still smiled wryly. “Please rest assured, Alliance Master. This is the only solution I can think of at the moment, so there is absolutely no way I would deceive you.”

“The Master of Yinxu Cave has long believed I dared not resist his manipulation, but in truth, I’ve been plotting for quite some time now, and I’ve learned a great deal about him…”

Jiang Weiyang stood silently by with her hands at her sides; this was the first time she had ever seen her father adopt such a submissive and humble demeanor.

As the ruler of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, the fact that he was speaking this way was proof enough that he had truly reached a dead end and was now in a position where he had nothing left to lose.

"Then tell me your request," Gu Changge said, nodding slightly.

The Master of the Yinxu Cave whom Jiang Yun spoke of was, of course, the Master of Destiny.

In truth, it wasn’t difficult for Gu Changge to learn about matters concerning the Master of Destiny. The last time he accompanied Ji Hanjing back to the Primordial Era, Gu Changge had witnessed many of the Master of Destiny’s schemes.

It could only be said that while such tactics might seem flawless to the Master of Destiny, in Gu Changge’s eyes, they were mere child’s play, no different from a game of make-believe.

Emperor Wei Yang nodded and said in a solemn voice, “I wish to request that the Alliance Leader annihilate the Righteous Unity Alliance.”

Upon hearing this, Jiang Weiyang, standing nearby, was startled at first, but quickly realized that this request was, in fact, entirely reasonable.

But if outsiders were to witness this scene, they would undoubtedly be so shocked and stunned that they would be speechless.

After all, Emperor Weiyang was currently the Alliance Master of the Righteous Unity Alliance, yet here he was asking Gu Changge to annihilate it…

Gu Changge, however, was not surprised. His gaze was deep and inscrutable as he said calmly, “Is that all?”

Jiang Yun’s words were essentially redundant; after all, Gu Changge was undoubtedly going to annihilate the Righteous Unity Alliance—there was no doubt about that.

“I know Your Excellency possesses boundless power and is capable of anything. I only hope that Your Excellency will allow the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty to see the light once more. For this, I am willing to pay any price. From this day forward, the entire Weiyang Immortal Dynasty will follow Your Excellency’s lead. Even if I, Jiang Yun, were to become nothing more than a pawn in Your Excellency’s game, I would have no complaints and would spare no effort.”

The Emperor of Weiyang glanced at Jiang Weiyang beside him, then took a deep breath and spoke earnestly.

His words left Jiang Weiyang with her mouth slightly agape, frozen in place. Then, her nose grew sore, her eyes reddening slightly, and she whispered, “Father, you needn’t go this far…”

She truly hadn’t expected that, in the end, Emperor Weiyang’s request would be this.

All these years, though she could not see, she had actually grown accustomed to it.

Emperor Weiyang’s gaze softened, and he shook his head. “Actually, it is I who am sorry to you. Your blindness was, in truth, caused by me. It has only caused you suffering, forcing you to bear the consequences.”

Jiang Weiyang stood there in a daze, then shook her head gently. “No matter what, Weiyang will not blame Father.”

Gu Changge watched the deep bond between father and daughter, feeling a strange sense of fascination.

Jiang Yun had truly surprised him time and again today, though this kind of parental devotion was something he could never truly understand.

“I do indeed have a way to cure your daughter’s blindness. However, as you said, if the information you provide fails to satisfy me, you should be able to imagine the consequences,” he said calmly.

The Emperor of Weiyang replied in a solemn voice, “Please rest assured, Alliance Master. In truth, I have known for a long time that amidst the Vast Expanse, aside from the Ruler of Destiny who has been plotting all along, there are actually many other beings of equal stature lurking and biding their time. However, those beings are all very clever; they have always hidden themselves in the void beyond the world, barely touching upon the realms of existence.”

“The forces behind the Immortal Dao Alliance are actually connected to those entities. I suspect it is none other than Xi Nü, one of the ancient sages and leaders, known as the Western Sage.”

"The Yuan Hope Civilization is now entirely under the Alliance Master’s control; even the Yuan Hope Sanctuary has not been spared. Yet Xi Nu, the founder of the Yuan Hope Sanctuary who once wielded the Mirror of Reincarnation, has yet to make an appearance.After the Black Calamity ended, the Western Sage vanished for a time before later emerging with the Mirror of Reincarnation to establish the Xi Yuan Sanctuary..."

"Moreover, the current Xiyuan Holy Maiden has been missing for quite some time. I heard that she last escaped from the Alliance Leader’s grasp, and I’ve also considered the possibility that she was rescued by the Xiyuan Woman operating in the shadows."

These speculations, which Emperor Wei Yang spoke of at length, were actually his way of trying to prove his worth to Gu Changge.

Gu Changge’s gaze was deep and inscrutable; he neither confirmed nor denied Jiang Yun’s words.

In truth, he had always known exactly where the Holy Maiden of Xiyuan was. Letting her escape last time was actually a deliberate move—he intended to use her as bait to lure out a bigger fish.

Unfortunately, the big fish behind her was clever. It knew the Holy Maiden of Xiyuan was merely bait and that she carried the Mirror of Reincarnation, yet it remained patient and refused to reveal itself.

The Emperor of Weiyang’s words, however, gave Gu Changge some other insights.

He didn’t particularly care about those leaders who had once challenged the Celestial Sovereign, but those who had survived to this day must have encountered beings from the Primordial Era along the way.

Take the Central Lord, for instance—the father of Ji Hanjing—who was a genuine, authentic Primordial Deity.

The Primordial Era was so distant from the present—so distant that it could be traced back to before the birth of the Vast Expanse. In truth, he had long known that beings like the True Ancestor would surely be aware of the countless futures that would unfold after the birth of the Vast Expanse.

In other words, there were some who slipped through the net—intentionally left behind with the permission of a True Ancestor from that era.

After all, only a True Ancestor can conceal the truth from another True Ancestor.

After the birth of the Vast Expanse, the pure and impure energies separated: the pure energy rose to form the Source World, while the impure energy sank to form the Vast Expanse.

In fact, the origin of the Cataclysm, as later generations have predicted and speculated, has always stemmed from the philosophy of one of the three True Ancestors.

The Source World is not yet complete, so the existence of the Great Calamity is necessary to continuously extract true matter and primordial matter from the Vast Expanse, and to refine pure energy from the impure energy, thereby bringing the Source World closer to completion.

Of course, this is not Gu Changge’s philosophy.

“There are indeed many figures lurking behind the Immortal Dao Alliance, but as for whether it is the Western Sage you speak of, that remains to be seen.” Gu Changge snapped back to the present, casting a glance at Emperor Weiyang. He did not take the emperor’s earlier remarks too seriously.

No matter how formidable the Western Sage might be, he was ultimately just a sage of the past. While his power far surpassed that of Ling Yuling at her peak, it was still limited.

Gu Changge was not one to be intimidated by such a minor figure.

But the one thing that truly concerned him was how, during the Age of Black Calamity, the blueprints for forging the first-generation treasures of Civilization had come to circulate within the Vast Expanse…

The records of the Xi Yuan Holy Hall stated that a figure of the Road’s End tier had moved the heavens, and thus the blueprints had drifted out from the Realm of Truth.

And at that time, Gu Changge was already contemplating how to devise a reasonable plan to make the Ruler of Heaven vanish.

Although the appearance of the blueprints for the First Civilization’s Treasure had helped Gu Changge, he found those records utterly ridiculous.

For whether it was him or the other two True Ancestors, if they were to deal with a variable like the "Ruler of Heaven," why would they need to go to such trouble? The moment they revived, a single thought, a single finger, would be enough to crush him.

Therefore, the claim that the blueprints of the First Civilization’s Treasure had leaked from the Realm of Truth was, from the start, a laughable lie.

People imagined the Ruler of Heaven as far too terrifying and fearsome, believing he was already capable of clashing with the True Ancestors in the Realm of Truth. But all of this stemmed from Gu Changge at the time, who did not understand the true strength of the top-tier powerhouses across the Vast Expanse. Thus, when crafting the character of the Ruler of Heaven, he failed to gauge the appropriate level.

In his eyes, they were all merely ants, differing only in size. Yet in the presence of true ants, the Ruler of Heaven resembled a divine dragon hidden within the vast cosmos.

Of course, this point actually didn’t matter much in the end; Gu Changge had already achieved his objective.

"It seems the Alliance Leader must have had this all planned out long ago..."

Upon hearing this, Emperor Wei Yang was taken aback for a moment before realizing what had been said, and a look of admiration involuntarily appeared on his face.

He had, in fact, wondered how the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan, with her strength, had managed to escape from the grasp of the seemingly invincible Gu Changge.

And now it seemed that all of this had actually been Gu Changge’s plan.

If that were the case, the Holy Maiden Xi Yuan’s whereabouts had likely been under Gu Changge’s control all along—including the lost Mirror of Reincarnation.

Gu Changge nodded slightly and said no more.

Given the current situation, Emperor Wei Yang still held considerable value to him.

As for Jiang Weiyang’s blindness, that was something Ji Hanjing would have to resolve herself. After all, Jiang Weiyang was her bloodline descendant—more precisely, a descendant of her younger sister’s bloodline.

Had it not been for the curse she had cast back then, Jiang Weiyang would naturally not have gone blind.

At this moment, taking advantage of the secluded surroundings and with no fear of being detected by the master of the Yinxu Cave, the Emperor of Weiyang began to explain to Jiang Weiyang the circumstances surrounding her origins and her eye condition.

The Weiyang Immortal Dynasty was founded hundreds of eras ago. Back then, it was not yet known by that name, but rather as the Great Yang Ancient Kingdom. It had maintained friendly relations with the ancient Kingdom of Nanzhao throughout the ages and possessed deep-rooted foundations and great power.

The last ruler of the Great Yang Ancient Kingdom was known as the Yang Emperor.

Emperor Yang was tyrannical, incompetent, and depraved, indulging in wine and women. During his reign, he built “pools of wine and groves of meat,” indiscriminately slaughtered the innocent, persecuted loyal officials, and ruled the people with brutal tyranny. All ethnic groups suffered greatly under his rule, enduring unbearable suffering.

The biological father of the current Emperor of Weiyang, Jiang Yun, was a feudal lord of the Ancient Kingdom of Dayang. His ancestors possessed a trace of imperial blood. Dissatisfied with Emperor Yang’s tyranny, he led his troops in rebellion and later fell in battle on Mount Jiuyin.

Jiang Yun followed in his father’s footsteps, claiming a mandate from heaven and receiving divine weapons sent from the heavens. With the assistance of the remnants of his father’s former allies, he united the feudal lords and various tribes, ultimately storming the capital of the Great Yang Empire, where the Yang Emperor took his own life within the palace…

"In truth, there was no such thing as a mandate from heaven. All of this was nothing more than the arrangement of the Master of Yinxu Cave. At that time, your mother was the Master of Yinxu Cave’s disciple. It was only through her assistance that I managed to storm the capital of the Great Yang Emperor..."

"As our bond grew deeper, so did our attachment. One day, she could no longer hold back and told me everything about the Master of the Yinxu Cave’s arrangements. Though I was furious, there was nothing I could do. In the end, I could only watch helplessly as she was taken away by the people of the Yinxu Cave."

"After that, to ensure your mother’s safety and to be able to see her from time to time, I had no choice but to become the Master of Yinxu Cave’s disciple. I studied magic by his side; he taught me the ways of establishing a dynasty, entrusted me with all the ancient texts from the former Yao Guang Ancestral Court, and had me manage and oversee the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty for him..."As he spoke of these events, Jiang Yun’s face betrayed a hint of anguish, but it was overshadowed by a greater sense of resentment and helplessness.
