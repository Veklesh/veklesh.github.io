Plop.

Ripples spread across the surface of the pond, and the fishing line, gleaming like jade, fell straight into the water.

Ling Yuling watched as Gu Changge resorted to his old trick once more, casting the fishing hook—with the severed arm still attached—back into the pond, remaining silent.

She didn’t know where Gu Changge had obtained this severed arm, which was saturated with divine essence, but it was clear that the “Ancestral God of Tai Xuan” he had just mentioned was undoubtedly a genuine, ancient deity who had survived from the Primordial Era to the present day.

In this current era, true Primordial beings have all but vanished, let alone Primordial deities worthy of being called Ancestral Gods.

One must understand that “Ancestral God” is not merely a title; it denotes an existence far more powerful than even a cultivator at the Ancestral Dao Realm.

Even at the peak of her power, she wouldn’t dare claim she could reliably defeat a Primordial Ancestral Deity.

And that Primordial Ancestral God who has survived to this day is undoubtedly far more powerful than the average Ancestral God. When exactly did Gu Changge engage in a fierce battle with an Ancestral God and sever one of its arms?

Moreover, Gu Changge’s actions today were truly baffling to her.

She had assumed Gu Changge was simply living a leisurely life within the courtyard, unconcerned with anything happening in the outside world.

But now it seemed Gu Changge was still plotting and scheming within a chess game she couldn’t fathom.

“We’ve already startled the big fish. Will using the same bait again actually work?” Ling Yuling couldn’t help but ask softly.

“Fish are rather foolish creatures. As long as your bait attracts them, no matter how many times they’re startled, they won’t learn their lesson—they’ll still rush in without hesitation,” Gu Changge said with a faint smile.

Ling Yuling nodded thoughtfully, then shook her head.

It seemed that in this world, only Gu Changge could so casually refer to a Primordial God as a “fish.”

“So, it seems you’re planning to take action against the Divine Path Civilization?” Ling Yuling asked.

Gu Changge replied nonchalantly, “The Righteous Unity Alliance won’t hold out much longer. Though the Divine Path has declined and retreated, there are actually quite a few Ancestral Gods hiding in the shadows. If I cannot

be put to my use, I cannot allow them to stand in my way.”

Ling Yuling fell silent.

Gu Changge gazed at the pond before him, where mist swirled yet the water remained crystal-clear, his eyes deep and inscrutable.

He used this severed arm of the Tai Xuan Ancestral God as bait to lure the gods—not because he couldn’t locate the Tai Xuan Ancestral God, but simply to avoid alerting the other Ancestral Gods.

Although those divine realms and deities were hidden and concealed within the boundless expanse of time and space, it was actually not difficult for Gu Changge to locate them.

However, doing so would likely alert those ancestral gods prematurely, putting them on guard—which would be detrimental to his subsequent plans and schemes.

Fortunately, he now held a fragment of the Great Progenitor God’s arm in his hand, which gave him an alternative plan.

In his haste to flee, the Tai Xuan Ancestral God had sacrificed this portion of his divine essence. Yet to restore it would require accumulating power over the vast span of at least several hundred eons.

In reality, the primordial deities did not coexist as harmoniously as cultivators might imagine; rather, their battles and struggles were far more brutal and intense. Any primordial deity that has survived from the primordial era to the present day is by no means ordinary.

In ancient times, there was an era when immortals and gods rose together, and there was also an era when the divine system reigned supreme, dominating the cosmos.

In those days, the gods divided the authority over heaven and earth, coalesced into divine essences, and further subdivided those essences, each fulfilling their respective duties.

Mighty deities sat high upon the clouds, overlooking all realms, harvesting the divine essence of weaker deities, while the weaker deities could only spread their faith across the great worlds and universes, refining their divine essence to strengthen themselves.

The divine essence of a powerful deity was precisely the sustenance that lesser deities longed for.

Who knows how many deities secretly covet the divine essence of a single ancestral god?

Having survived from the Primordial Era to the present, the Great Ancestral God Tai Xuan undoubtedly has many mortal enemies.

If those arch-enemies were to learn that His primordial essence has been wounded and His power no longer matches that of his prime, they would surely be tempted to act.

Even those Ancestral Gods with whom He has no prior grievances might be tempted to act; after all, this is a portion of an Ancestral God’s divine essence.

When the Great Ancestral God was at the height of His power, those deities naturally dared not challenge Him, but now that His source has been damaged and He is no longer at His peak,

At this moment, His divine essence is the object of the greatest envy and covetousness among those deities.

No matter how weak they may be, the moment they catch a whiff of blood or spot a suitable opportunity, they will swarm like sharks.

The bait Gu Changge used to lure them was not merely intended to entice the Great Ancestor God.

He intended to bring about the collapse and disintegration of the Divine Path Civilization, and this bait was merely the initial catalyst.

The surface of the pond returned to calm once more.

After brewing tea for Gu Changge, Ling Yuling took her leave; the great battle between the Heaven Slaying Alliance and the Righteous Unity Alliance still required her to oversee the situation.

The senior members of the Heaven Slaying Alliance all regarded her as the deputy leader. Although Gu Changge did not endorse this title, it had already become an accepted fact within the alliance.

As for figures like Mu Fuguang and Tong Xian—those from the Forbidden Zone—they currently obeyed only Gu Changge’s orders. Beyond that, they heeded no one else’s commands and submitted to no one else’s authority.

Only Ling Yuling possessed the means to keep them in check.

The emergence of the Battle Merit Tower has prompted these forbidden beings to begin accumulating and vying for battle merits. However, this has led them to act without regard for the greater good on many occasions, thereby disrupting the entire course of the war.

This has also compelled the high-ranking members of the Righteous Unity Alliance to intervene personally, acting without regard for the consequences, resulting in the tragic deaths of many cultivators and causing unnecessary casualties.

After Ling Yuling hurriedly left, the courtyard fell silent once more.

"The Crescent Moon Sect is indeed somewhat unique. Not only has a power emerged there that even surprised me, but even that girl Xian’er appeared there. It seems this is no coincidence."

Gu Changge gazed at the surface of the pond, deep in thought.

He certainly hadn’t summoned Chen Yi out of sheer boredom.

Chen Yi’s status was somewhat unique—he was both an elder of the Crescent Moon Sect and had once done Gu Xian’er a favor.

Therefore, Gu Changge intended to conduct an experiment on her.

Currently, within the entire Vast Wilderness, very few people knew of his relationship with Gu Xian’er—not even those who had been by his side for quite some time, such as Mu Yan, Ling Yuling, and the White Bone Ancestral King.

If anyone were to try to probe him through Gu Xian’er, he would certainly sense it.

But who would even know that there is a connection between him and Gu Xian’er?

Anyone who knows of the connection between him and Gu Xian’er would never attempt to probe him through her.

It was like a vicious circle with no solution.

But precisely because it was an unsolvable loop, Gu Changge sensed something amiss.

Gu Xian’er’s appearance at the Crescent Moon Sect was no coincidence or accident.

A power had emerged at Crescent Moon Sect that took him by surprise.

And Gu Xian’er, who was connected to him, just so happened to appear there and, by coincidence, came into contact with that power.

Everything in this world follows the path of destiny.

To create so many coincidences, one need only gently nudge the paths of fate, causing them to intersect and touch.

"The power of Confining Heaven..."

"How surprising. Could this be a plan to have Xian'er come after me?"

"I’ll see just what other coincidences there are."

Gu Changge gathered his thoughts. After a moment’s contemplation, he gave a light flick of his wrist. The void before him blurred, transforming into a round mirror that reflected the face of a stunningly beautiful woman, exuding grace and nobility.

It was none other than Ji Hanjing, who had taken the form of a human by cultivating a treasure of Civilization as her foundation.

At that moment, she sat majestically within a grand and imposing palace. Wearing a phoenix crown and feathered robes, with a crown jewel hanging down to obscure her face, her expression was cold and imposing—like an unparalleled empress surveying the world and looking down upon all living beings.

Within this palace, courtiers knelt and bowed their heads to the ground.

Several elderly men clad in dragon robes chanted in unison, paying homage to their ancestor with the utmost reverence.

On the desk before Ji Hanjing lay layer upon layer of ancient texts and classics, stacked high.

Her jade-like features were cold and impassive. With her slender, pale hand, she held one of the volumes, her eyes moving lightly as if perusing its pages. She spoke not a word, seemingly intent on grasping the current era as quickly as possible.

"It seems things are going more smoothly than I anticipated. The Weiyang Immortal Dynasty claims the legitimacy of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court. Now that a living princess of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court has appeared, they will naturally do everything in their power to acknowledge their ancestral lineage and pass the test by any means necessary."

Gu Changge watched the scene unfold with great interest.

Everything unfolding before him was exactly as he had anticipated and planned.

After Ji Hanjing emerged from the ruins of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court, breaking through the Dao Source, she was escorted back to the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty by several elderly imperial uncles, all under the watchful eyes of various Daoist factions.

As for the ruins of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court, the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty had dispatched numerous powerful experts to guard them, forbidding any Daoist sects from approaching.

Had that site been ownerless, the various Daoist factions would naturally have had reason to vie for it—after all, opportunities are predestined, and those of virtue shall claim them.

But who could have known that within those ruins lay an ancient figure sealed by the Dao Source—one suspected to be from the era of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court?

Now, the various Daoist factions naturally had no reason to vie for it anymore, especially not right under the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty’s nose.

Thus, the mysterious woman sealed within the Dao Source was, quite naturally, invited back to the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty by several of its elderly imperial uncles.

Subsequently, through the investigations of various Daoist factions and cross-referencing with ancient texts, the identity of that mysterious woman was finally confirmed.

She was the genuine princess of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court, a venerable leader who had once fought against the Black Calamity, and the daughter of the Central Lord!

Her background was so staggering it left every member of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty’s royal family wide-eyed with shock and awe.

After all, she was a true ancestral figure, with a lineage that no one could rival.

In terms of bloodline, every member of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty’s royal family is her descendant.

This news sent shockwaves through all quarters, causing a massive uproar.

No one could have anticipated that, after countless ages and eras, the princess of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court would reappear in the world in this manner.

This also placed the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty in a rather awkward position.

For those familiar with the history of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty’s founding know that its founder, Emperor Jiang Yun of Weiyang, did not bear the Ji surname—and the Ji surname was, in fact, the imperial lineage of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court.

The predecessor of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty was the Great Yang Ancient Kingdom.

The last emperor of the Great Yang Ancient Kingdom was known as the Yang Emperor.

Emperor Yang was tyrannical and tyrannical, incompetent and depraved, and obsessed with wine and women. During his reign, he indulged in debauchery, indiscriminately slaughtered the innocent, persecuted the loyal and virtuous, and ruled the people with tyranny; all the tribes suffered greatly under his rule, enduring unbearable suffering.

At that time, the biological father of the Wei Yang Emperor Jiang Ming was a vassal prince of a different surname from the Great Jue Ancient Kingdom, named Jiang Sheng.

Jiang Sheng’s ancestors possessed a trace of imperial blood; in terms of lineage, he actually shared some connection with the imperial family of the Great Juegu Kingdom at that time.

However, dissatisfied with the Emperor of Dajue’s tyranny, Jiang Sheng led his troops in rebellion and later fell in battle on Mount Jiuyin.

Emperor Jiang Ming of Weiyang followed in his father’s footsteps, receiving a mandate from heaven and divine weapons sent from the heavens. With the assistance of his father’s former allies, he united the feudal lords and various tribes, ultimately breaching the capital of the Great Yang Empire. The Yang Emperor took his own life within the palace, and thus the ancient Great Yang Empire was brought to an end.

Subsequently, the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty was established upon the ruins of the ancient Dajue Kingdom, taking its place, and a new era began.

The Jiang clan replaced the Ji clan, becoming the imperial lineage of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty.

Now, the Emperor of Weiyang presides over the Righteous Unity Alliance and has no time to attend to matters of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty.

This has left all members of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty’s imperial family—including numerous ministerial clans—daring not to show the slightest disrespect or negligence toward this ancient ancestor. They do not know how to handle the situation, nor do they know how to face her.

For the descendants of the Jiang clan to address those of the Ji clan as “ancestors”? If this were to get out, wouldn’t it invite ridicule? It would only invite laughter.

"I am fully aware of the causes and consequences, as well as the various historical events. You need not be anxious or nervous. The Yao Guang Ancestral Court no longer exists. Whether it be the Great Yang Ancient Kingdom or the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, they are no different in my eyes."

"The same goes for the Ji and Jiang clans."

Within the palace halls, Ji Hanjing closed the ancient text in her hands and cast a cold, authoritative glance at the ministers below, before her gaze swept over the group of elderly imperial uncles from the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty.

Her words caused everyone to exhale deeply, as if a weight had been lifted from their hearts.

They had feared that this ancient ancestor placed too much importance on bloodlines and might fly into a rage and take it out on them.

But in a sense, whether it was the Great Jue Ancient Kingdom or the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, both were far too distant from the era of the Yao Guang Ancestral Court.

The bloodlines of these descendants had long since become so diluted that no one could even fathom the extent of it.

Even if a descendant of the Great Yang Ancient Kingdom’s imperial family stood here, they would likely be no different from the rest.

“Where is Jiang Yun now?” Ji Hanjing asked coldly.

“Reporting to the Ancestor, the Emperor is currently stationed at the Righteous Unity Alliance, overseeing the overall situation and repelling the invasion of the Heaven Slaying Alliance. He has no time to attend to the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty. At present, the various affairs within the Immortal Dynasty are being handled by the State Preceptor, the Chancellor, and others.” An elderly imperial uncle stepped forward and answered timidly.

Facing Ji Hanjing’s gaze, a bead of cold sweat involuntarily formed on his forehead.

As a member of the imperial family of the Weiyang Immortal Dynasty, this was the first time he had ever felt such terrifying majesty—as if he were standing before an empress who looked down upon all worlds.

This venerable ancestor was terrifying in both seniority and power.
