As streaks of light raced through the vast, boundless cosmos, the youth clad in a golden robe finally opened his eyes.

"Su En, do you think we’ll get to see a Divine Messenger this time? It would be even better if we could meet one of the Great Divine Messengers from the Hall. Thank goodness our Tengchuan State has produced a genius like you, who has received the ancient divine revelation."

"The elders of the Hall of Divine Truth should be very pleased..." A young girl’s voice, tinged with a touch of chirping and crispness, came from nearby, brimming with excitement and envy.

The boy named Su En snapped back to reality and glanced at the girl beside him. He seemed to have a vague recollection of her; he gave a faint smile in response.

He wasn’t sure yet whether he had been reborn or had simply gained memories of the future.

If it were true, then the world of the future would be far more chaotic and turbulent than the current situation in Chushixiang.

A world ravaged by the Plague Qi was just one example; others were tainted by the Rot Qi, where all cultivators and living beings walked a path of using the Rot Qi to cultivate power. There were even worlds where the Five Aggregates were muddied, the Six Desires and Seven Emotions were corrupted, and all manner of bizarre auras ran rampant.

But he hoped that wasn’t true. Living in such a future world would be enough to drive anyone mad and rob them of their sanity. Whether it was the plague energy or the corruption energy, in the end, it would all drive people to madness—it was a twisted future.

"If I really have been reborn, what should I do now? Go find the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance and tell him all this? ~Let him change the future?"

"But how could I possibly meet someone like the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance? Even if I told him, would he believe me? -" Sun En furrowed his brow and sighed softly to himself. As a minor figure like him, reborn in this era, he was simply incapable of making much of a difference.

"A mere ant, yet daring to harbor thoughts of shaking the future..."

But just then, the center of Sun En’s brow suddenly began to flicker violently, as an inexplicable, strange power surged within it. At the same time, a cold, ancient voice—one that seemed to span all time and destiny from the dawn of history—echoed in his ears.

"Who are you?"

Su En’s expression changed. As he was pondering his next move, he never expected such a voice to appear in his ear.

Just as he turned to look around, he suddenly realized that this expanse of space and time seemed to have fallen into stillness; time had frozen, and the beings of the Dao Realm of the Holy Truth, who had been guiding them on their journey, appeared to be frozen in place.

Such boundless power caused Su En’s expression to shift dramatically, and an unfathomable thought even flashed through his mind.

"Who am I? I am the omnipotent Heavenly Dao. I exist from the beginning of all things to the end of all things. My existence governs every spacetime and destiny, creating all possibilities and all impossibilities..."

That cold, vast voice echoed, imbued with an indescribable might.

Just then, Su En suddenly felt an itch between his eyebrows, as if something were trying to burrow out from within, accompanied by pain. He tore at it with all his might, but could not tear it away.

"Are you inside my brow?"

Gritting his teeth and drenched in cold sweat, Su En suddenly remembered something. Drawing upon the Plague Qi technique from his future memories, he snapped off his own finger.

As blood gushed forth, wispy strands of blue mist rose, and a crystalline ancient mirror materialized before him. Its smooth, lustrous surface perfectly reflected his pale, bloodless face.

He saw that the space between his eyebrows was actually splitting open. A cold, indifferent eye, encircled by blue patterns, was covered by fine lines bulging like blue veins, forcing its way out vertically from there.

That eye was icy, radiating the detachment of one who surveys billions of living beings, devoid of any emotion. As its gaze fell, wisp after wisp of blue-tinged miasma began to surge forth, infecting this frozen moment in time.

"Who... who are you, exactly?"

Su En’s voice trembled.

"I am the Heavenly Dao. Through my power, you have glimpsed the trajectory of the future to which I belong. Perhaps you may call me Yi Tian." That vast, supremely indifferent voice replied, devoid of any emotion.

Su En’s heart trembled even more. He had never imagined that the so-called Heavenly Dao would attach itself to him, becoming his third eye. Yet this third eye was completely beyond his control; it felt more like another will, lodged within his physical body.

He had become a mere shell—or rather, a vessel.

"To become the host of Yitian is your greatest honor. You may rely on me to become a supreme immortal plague-bearer, enjoying eternal life , immortal and indestructible, existing alongside the Heavenly Dao." Yitian’s voice remained cold and ruthless, yet it was laced with temptation.

“Why are you seeking me out? I’m just an ordinary person.” Su En gritted his teeth, feeling a sharp pain in the center of his brow, as if it were being torn apart completely.

"Only you withstood the power I released; only you are qualified." Yitian replied coldly.

"How did you come to be here? Hasn’t the Heaven Slaying against the Heavens not yet begun in the Vast Expanse? All I know is that the Celestial Tomb of the Nine Heavens is about to collapse, and a minor incarnation of Heaven has already emerged..." Su En asked.

"I am the Heavenly Dao, not an avatar of Heaven. The Heaven Slaying has already begun. The Heavenly Will has been wounded; the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance has injured it. I am a portion of the scattered power that escaped from the Heavenly Will. I severed a river of destiny from the future and became a new Heavenly Dao ."

"The leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance has grand ambitions. His power approaches that of the Heavenly Will at its peak, but the Heavenly Will must divide its power to oversee the Vast Expanse of the cosmos and ensure everything develops in a stable manner. That is why it was wounded by the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance. In the process, I was born. Aside from me, the various manifestations of the Heavenly Will from different spacetime dimensions that you have seen in the future—perhaps he will appear in the future."

"If the Heavenly Will is the ocean, then we are the streams and rivers—yet we are not the same as the Heavenly Will..." Yi Tian spoke coldly. "The Heavenly Dao."

"Could this be the reason for the Heavenly Will’s wrath? Is this what caused the collapse of the Celestial Tomb in the Nine Heavens, and the premature emergence of the Heavenly Avatar…?" Su En was utterly stunned, unable to believe it. The leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance had wounded the Heavenly Will?

Did anyone in the entire Vast Expanse know about this? Did the great figures at the End of the Path know about this?

Moreover, even more terrifying than all this was the realization: just how terrifying was the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, that he had even wounded the Heavenly Will? "The Heavenly Will governs all Heavenly Dao—whether the Minor Avatar of Heaven or any other Heavenly Dao, it is all within its domain."

"It is precisely because of its wrath that the Minor Avatar of Heaven has emerged, and soon the Major Avatar of Heaven will appear as well. I will help you absorb the power of the Heavenly Will and take its place." Yitian’s voice remained cold, yet it was laced with a seductive undertone.

"You… what do you want me to do?"

Su En stopped struggling. He was merely a lowly nobody. The Realm of Sacred Truth was vast, comprising eight thousand states, each containing countless continents and planets. Though he was considered a genius in Tengchuan State—one of those eight thousand states—when viewed across the entire Realm of Sacred Truth, he was completely insignificant.

He had originally believed that the memories and visions in his mind were the true future—the memories of his reincarnated self.

But who would have known that these were merely visions of the future he had seen by harnessing the power of the Plague Heaven? To be precise, they could only be considered one possible future, and that possibility, under the power dissipated by the Heavenly Will, had actually become a new Heavenly Dao.

This was simply an unfathomable, inconceivable occurrence.

"The world you inhabit is called the Realm of Sacred Reason. The Sovereign of this realm is the incarnation of a True Path-level figure who wields the authority of the Heavenly Dao."

"I want you to use the methods of the Plague Qi to permeate the entire Realm of Sacred Reason, allowing the Plague Qi to spread to more worlds, and use the cultivation methods of the Plague Qi you have obtained to usher in an era belonging to me, Yitian." Yitian’s voice remained cold, yet it contained a hint of intensity that sent a shiver down Su En’s spine.

He suddenly had a feeling that the Heavenly Dao was not truly devoid of emotion; it was simply that no interest had yet emerged capable of stirring it.

"I understand..."

Su En’s voice trembled slightly. At the same time, the blue eye between his brows suddenly blinked, and the scene before him returned to normal; no one else around him exhibited this anomaly.

"Plague Qi…"

"To become an existence on par with the Heavenly Dao." Su En’s heart began to race.

Billions of light-years away lay the edge of this vast world.

A majestic and sacred temple, as if formed from primordial chaos jade, stood there.

In this place, the stars seemed as insignificant as dust. The colossal creature guarding the temple—a massive being with the head of a human and the body of a serpent— was shrouded in endless chaotic energy, exuding an ancient, majestic aura that was truly awe-inspiring.

And this is the supreme seat of authority over this vast world: the Hall of Sacred Truth.

Every being that appears here each day is at least at the Dao Realm level. Outside the grand temple gates, there are dedicated divine messengers responsible for receiving these devout worshippers.

Vast, smoke-like silver waves of faith energy converged from every corner of this world, surging here to intertwine and spread like oceans and rivers— surging and surging, radiating brilliantly, enough to engulf the heavens and earth.

"The divine messengers are now receiving the Holy Lord’s revelation: the Realm of Divine Truth is about to be destroyed, and various natural disasters are about to befall it. Only the believers of the Hall of Divine Truth can receive the Holy Lord’s protection, board the constructed Divine Truth warships and airships, and escape this realm to reach the other side."

"The purer one’s faith, the earlier they will depart."

The vast, sacred, and detached celestial voice resounded before the temple, like the deep toll of great bells, shaking the four corners of the world.

All the devout worshippers present fell to their knees in unison, bowing their heads ceaselessly. They formed a vast, dark sea, like a surging river, their lips murmuring gratitude for the Holy Lord’s revelation and divine grace.

"In a moment, you will follow me into the Hall of Divine Truth to receive the baptism of holy water..."

" The several Dao Realm beings who had led Su En and the others to this place gave these instructions from the front, their faces equally radiant with fervor.

Su En followed along with the crowd, head bowed. Beside him were countless promising talents selected from every province of the Holy Reason Realm; as far as the eye could see, their numbers numbered in the hundreds of thousands.

The Holy Water Pool of the Hall of Divine Reason stood at the very center of this vast plaza. The holy water, shimmering silver and bubbling as if boiling, gushed forth from the center, carrying a sacred aura. Several beings clad in luxurious, thick red robes stood there, holding silver vessels, pouring the holy water over the heads of all the believers who had come to bathe in it.

"Holy water..."

Su En gazed at the pool of silver holy water, a mad idea forming in his mind. Between his palms, a wisp of pale blue mist—resembling a plague— swirl and spread.

When his turn came, that wisp of plague-like energy, as expected, spread through the holy water in the pool and merged into it, silently and imperceptibly, without a trace.

………Please support with flowers… "The Age of the Plague is about to dawn…"

That cold, indifferent voice echoed in his mind, tinged with madness.

"Plague, Decay, Rot, Chaos, Lust..."

"Beyond the Five Great Avatars of Heaven, arrange five more aberrant Heavenly Daws. In this way, the Vast Expanse will be driven even further toward a chaotic and unknown future."

A Vast Expanse where no living being could ever set foot.

Here, there is no law, no restraint, no rules, no Dao, no traces, and no cause and effect. It is like a vast, profound realm beyond the primordial, existing neither in the past nor the present, and never to be noticed by any being.

Gu Changge stood in immaculate white robes, silent.

"The leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance..."

"One day, you will regret what you have done."

From the moment Shuai Tian Shi Yi was born, he possessed a fierce will of his own. Through the primordial consciousness of the Heavenly Dao, he had come to understand many secrets and events within the Vast Expanse of the cosmos. The emotions emanating from him were intense, so violent they seemed on the verge of erupting into annihilation.

"Then I shall wait for the day you turn against me."

Gu Changge smiled faintly, unconcerned. The so-called "rearing of venomous creatures" meant throwing a group of beings whose horror surpassed human imagination into the same space, leaving them to slaughter and vie for dominance. Only in this way could the Vast Expanse of the present undergo a faster metamorphosis and leap forward, until they ascended.

For the sentient beings of the Vast Expanse, if the Heavenly Will was too overbearing and invincible, then he would weaken it.

Shuai Tian said no more. Clumps of dense, terrifying gray-black energy swirled ceaselessly, then rapidly collapsed and contracted, transforming into a single, deep and profound gray-black light that instantly darted into the rift in space-time, vanishing in an instant.

As Gu Changge’s gaze followed, he beheld a vast, ancient world, within which stood a magnificent hall, lavishly decorated, where a pale-faced youth lay on a bed, his eyes tightly shut in weakness. A maidservant wept softly beside him, while a beautiful woman stood there shedding tears. A heroic-looking man, who appeared to be the youth’s father, frowned deeply, exuding an innate authority that carried a hint of suppressed anger.
