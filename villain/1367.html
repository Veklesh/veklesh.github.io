Lan Xin had a delicate, captivating face and a graceful figure. She wore a long aqua-blue gown, and her hair, as soft as clouds, was gathered into a cloud-shaped bun with a wooden hairpin, giving her an elegant and dignified appearance.

"Senior Sister Lan Xin," Gu Xian'er said obediently as she walked over and stood beside her.

Lan Xin’s other companions, upon seeing Gu Xian’er approach, couldn’t help but furrow their brows slightly.

Many moved to the side, deliberately avoiding her.

Others, out of respect for Lan Xin, said nothing, though their eyes betrayed their coldness and distaste.

The Xieyue Sect had many disciples, but Gu Xian’er was the only one who had been personally summoned by the Sect Master due to her rare talent and then sent away.

At the time, many elders believed the Xieyue Sect might have produced a prodigy of unparalleled talent.

Yet who could have imagined that after the Sect Master summoned Gu Xian’er, he would not only refuse to accept her but also declare outright that she was tainted by ill omen? He advised against excessive interaction with her to avoid creating karmic entanglements.

This incident caused quite a stir within the Xieyue Sect at the time, creating a huge uproar.

As a result, Gu Xian’er gained notoriety among many young disciples and elders.

Although the Shed Moon Sect’s Sect Master did not wish to take her as a disciple, he was conflicted—unwilling to let such a promising talent slip away. He therefore suggested that the other elders find a way to accept her.

However, at the time, the elders all refused without hesitation; no matter how outstanding Gu Xian’er’s talent might be, they would not agree.

At their level, they placed great importance on the law of cause and effect.

If even the Sect Master, a figure of such cultivation, was unwilling to take Gu Xian’er in, how could they possibly agree?

It might even backfire on them, causing all their years of arduous cultivation to go down the drain, vanishing into thin air.

This incident caused quite a stir within the Slanting Moon Sect at the time.

In the end, only after deep deliberation did the Siaming Sect’s Sect Master reluctantly agree to let Gu Xian’er remain in the sect—but only as an ordinary disciple.

After this incident, the other disciples and elders avoided her like the plague, unwilling to have anything to do with her.

This was because Gu Xian’er had been brought there by that mad old woman from Xieyue Mountain.

The story of that mad old woman was, in fact, common knowledge throughout the Xieyue Sect.

It was said that the mad old woman had once been a powerful figure from ancient times, but for some reason had lost her mind, acting

in a deranged manner, rarely ever in her right mind.

No one in the Xieyue Sect knew how she had found her way to Xieyue Mountain; they only knew that she liked to roam the wilds and desolate marshes, as well as the rugged mountains.

Some of the Xieyue Sect’s ancient elders had once intervened, attempting to expel her from the sect, but all their efforts failed.

Instead, with a single, light flick of her robe, the mad old woman sent them flying millions of miles away, leaving the members of the Xieyue Sect utterly horrified and terrified.

It was precisely for this reason that the members of the Slanting Moon Sect dared not casually drive that mad old woman away anymore.

Later, however, some disciples and elders, sensing her profound cultivation, began to scheme against her. Believing her to be deranged and thus easily manipulated, they frequently sought her out to observe her movements and spiritual aura, and some even hoped to receive her guidance.

Although the mad old woman was not in her right mind, she had never harmed anyone and harbored no ill will.

Occasionally, when her mind was slightly clear, she would casually offer a word of guidance to those disciples and elders, from which they benefited greatly.

However, before long, problems began to arise among the disciples and elders who had interacted with her.

Some failed to break through during seclusion, perishing and losing their cultivation; others were overcome by inner demons, their minds shattered, reduced to walking corpses; still others, while traveling, were caught in the aftermath of battles between supreme beings—collateral damage—and died on the spot; or they were tracked down by enemies and killed on the spot. In short, none met a good end.

They were either crippled or driven mad; those who died outright were actually the luckiest, as they were spared those agonizing, inhuman torments.

This incident sent shockwaves of horror throughout the Xieyue Sect; the events were simply too bizarre.

Even the Ancestor-level figures felt their blood run cold; they shuddered uncontrollably, finding it all too eerie, and found themselves breaking out in cold sweats even in broad daylight.

Disciples and elders who had ever come into contact with the Mad Old Woman were filled with dread, believing it to be an invisible curse that would claim their lives when they least expected it.

Subsequent events confirmed their suspicions: no one was spared. Anyone who had received guidance from the Mad Old Woman or witnessed her Daoist principles and methods ultimately met with disaster and met a grim end.

For a time, the entire Slanting Moon Sect shunned this entity—one they could neither defeat nor drive away—as if it were a venomous snake or scorpion, and everyone began considering whether to relocate or evacuate.

Many ancient beings ultimately concluded that the Mad Old Woman would end up mentally deranged, appearing

—likely because she had attracted and become tainted by ill omens.

And those who came into contact with her would, like her, become tainted by misfortune and ill luck.

As long as one avoids contact with her, there is no need to worry about inviting misfortune.

Gu Xian’er is now shunned by the disciples of the Slanting Moon Sect, avoided like a venomous snake or scorpion, precisely for this reason—no one wants to be tainted by misfortune.

Many disciples and elders would even welcome her early departure from the sect—or her untimely death.

"Senior Sister Lan Xin, we all know you have a kind heart and are a truly benevolent person, but that doesn’t mean you should be so close to that girl."

“Don’t forget, she’s brought misfortune and ill luck upon herself. If you keep spending time with her, who knows when you’ll be tainted by it…”

A woman wearing a pink outer robe over a plain-colored long skirt walked over. She looked at Lan Xin standing beside Gu Xian’er, furrowed her brow, and spoke with a hint of sarcasm.

“Xu Hui, stop it. Junior Sister Xian’er has been a disciple of the Slanting Moon Sect for so long—have you seen any problems with her?”

“Junior Sister Xian’er is obedient and well-behaved, but that doesn’t mean you can bully her like this.”

Lan Xin looked at the approaching woman, her expression darkening, her eyes filled with undisguised loathing.

The young disciples gathered around, seeing this, tactfully stepped back, choosing not to get involved in the confrontation between the two.

The woman, named Xu Hui, let out a dismissive, cold laugh and said, “I’ve never bullied that girl. When I see her, I can’t get away fast enough. She’s such a jinx—she’s an eyesore just being here at Xieyue Sect.”

“If you have a problem, take it out on me. Why are you humiliating Junior Sister Xian’er like this?”

Lan Xin’s face was as still as water, her eyes cold and piercing.

Xu Hui and she had never gotten along; they were practically like oil and water.

Of course, this also had to do with their respective masters.

Their masters harbored a grudge—a deep, irreconcilable one.

However, Lan Xin had always held the upper hand over Xu Hui in the past. Xu Hui would typically step back and avoid her, clearly wary of her—it was unthinkable that she would confront her so directly as she was doing today.

Lan Xin suspected that Xu Hui’s master might be pulling the strings behind the scenes, and that Gu Xian’er had merely been dragged into this innocently.

"Haha, Senior Sister Lan Xin, you sure have a big mouth. But after today, we’ll see if you can still laugh."

A hint of mocking sarcasm flashed across Xu Hui’s face. She chuckled, then, ignoring Lan Xin’s glare, turned and walked away.

As for Gu Xian’er, who stood beside Lan Xin, Xu Hui hadn’t so much as glanced in her direction the entire time, treating her as if she were invisible.

"I’m sorry for dragging you into this, Junior Sister Xian’er. She’s after me." Only after Xu Hui had left did Lan Xin turn to Gu Xian’er with a faintly apologetic expression.

Gu Xian’er still wore her usual demure expression. Upon hearing this, she shook her head and said, “Senior Sister Lan Xin, you’re overreacting. You know I don’t care about these things.”

Lan Xin smiled and said, “I don’t know what got into Xu Hui today—she really said some strange things.”

Gu Xian’er looked pensive for a moment, then said softly, “Senior Sister Lan Xin, don’t you feel like there was a deeper meaning behind what she said just now?”

“It seems like she knows the purpose behind the sect summoning all the elders and disciples here today.”

Hearing Gu Xian’er’s words, Lan Xin finally realized what she meant, and her brows immediately furrowed. “What you say makes sense. What does Xu Hui mean by that?”

Suddenly, a commotion erupted in the distance, abruptly interrupting Lan Xin’s train of thought.

She couldn’t help but look in that direction, and she froze for a moment.

She saw a young man dressed in a white robe adorned with cloud patterns, graceful and exceptionally handsome, walking toward them surrounded by a crowd of young disciples.

He stood tall and straight, his face adorned with a refined smile. His gaze was gentle, his eyes seeming to glisten with a watery light, giving him the air of a noble gentleman, graceful and dignified.

“Senior Brother Xu Yu,” Lan Xin murmured, a fleeting glimpse of admiration and complexity flashing in her eyes.

Xu Yu was, without a doubt, the undisputed leader of the younger generation in the Slanting Moon Sect—possessing exceptional talent, profound blessings, and strength that was unfathomable.

Even Lan Xin—a heavenly prodigy with a proud and confident heart and an unwavering Daoist resolve—felt immense admiration for him, knowing full well she fell far short of his caliber.

As for Xu Yu, he lived up to his name—as smooth and refined as jade, gentle and approachable in his demeanor. He enjoyed an excellent reputation within the Slanting Moon Sect, and nearly all the elders held him in high regard.

"Senior Brother Xu Yu is back…"

"It’s said that Senior Brother Xu Yu went to the incredibly chaotic Yao Guang Ancient Realm for training this time. That place, they say, is so perilous that even supreme beings in the Ancestral Dao Realm might face calamities and meet with misfortune."

"I wonder what kind of opportunity Senior Brother Xu Yu encountered in the Yao Guang Ancient Realm. It feels like his cultivation is even more profound and unfathomable than before."

Many disciples watched the young man walk by and couldn’t help but whisper among themselves, their eyes brimming with admiration and respect.

Even Xu Hui, who had just been locked in a fierce standoff with Lan Xin, couldn’t help but let a hint of admiration flicker in her eyes.

"The top young disciple of Xieyue Sect," Gu Xian’er murmured to herself as she turned her gaze away.

She was well-versed in the art of hiding her true strength, rarely revealing it in public.

Yet, as she had just been sizing up this young man named Xu Yu, a faint sense of danger had begun to stir within her.

It seemed Xu Yu was likely not as simple as he appeared on the surface.

In the main hall square of the Xieyue Sect, as the disciples and elders gathered, the Sect Master of the Xieyue Sect, along with the Grand Elder and others, appeared at the front.

The Sect Master of the Xieyue Sect was an elderly man in his twilight years. He possessed an ethereal, otherworldly air, with a gaunt, refined face. He held a flywhisk in his hand, and an aura of profound mystery swirled around him.

Upon closer inspection, one could see that the void behind him was blurred, with various strange streams of light floating and swirling there.

Standing beside the Shed Moon Sect’s Sect Master was a man with narrowed eyes, clad in a large cloak, and bearing a long, diagonal scar across his face. He appeared to be sizing up the elders and disciples below.

The other elders all appeared quite courteous toward this man.

“This is the envoy from the Righteous Unity Alliance. He has come to the Xieyue Sect today to announce a matter, and that is why I have summoned you all here.”

The Slemon Sect’s Sect Master slowly scanned the crowd before speaking in a weathered tone, explaining the man’s background and purpose.

Now, the onslaught of the Dark Beings has grown increasingly fierce; they are no longer the chaotic, disorganized force they once were, but have begun to act in a well-organized manner.

In recent days, various squads of the Righteous Unity Alliance have successively fallen victim to ambushes by the dark entities, suffering heavy casualties.

There have even been instances where beings at the Ancestral Dao Realm have fallen, ambushed by the combined forces of powerful figures among the dark entities and killed on the spot, leaving the others no chance to come to their aid.

This incident caused a massive uproar within the Righteous Unity Alliance, while also triggering significant turmoil and unease.

In response, after deliberation among the various factions within the Righteous Unity Alliance, it was unanimously decided to dispatch powerful elders from their respective lineages to form an envoy delegation.

Subsequently, this delegation would travel to the Heaven Slaying Alliance to negotiate, setting aside mutual animosity and prejudice to establish a joint alliance and jointly resist the Dark Beings.

After all, relying solely on the strength of either the Righteous Unity Alliance or the Heaven Slaying Alliance would make it extremely difficult to eliminate this

catastrophic black calamity sweeping across the vast lands.

The only way to break this deadlock now is to join forces and stand united against the external threat.

"What?"

After hearing the explanation from the Patriarch of the Xieyue Sect, nearly all the elders and disciples were stunned, standing frozen in place.

"We’re to form an envoy delegation and head straight to the Heaven Slaying Alliance?"

"That Heaven Slaying Alliance?"

Once they came to their senses, the expressions on the elders’ faces darkened, their eyes betraying a hint of fear and unease.

In their eyes, the Heaven Slaying Alliance was not much different from the Dark Beings. Over the past period, the Heaven Slaying Alliance had taken advantage of chaos to plunder, and the number of realms of civilization it had swallowed up was by no means small.

The orthodox factions that opposed them had been nearly annihilated, buried in the river of time, vanishing forever.

Moreover, there were all manner of terrifying rumors and whispers circulating about the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

The Heaven Slaying Alliance Leader possessed immense supernatural powers and divine cultivation, wielding 470 methods that were utterly baffling and beyond imagination.

Any sentient being who comes into contact with him, regardless of their cultivation level or status, will be enslaved and converted by him, reduced to his followers and subjects.

Even the sages who once fought against the Black Calamity have now been converted by him, running about tirelessly on his behalf, fighting and waging war.

Now, throughout the territory of the Heaven Slaying Alliance, shrines and temples can be seen everywhere, enshrining the leader of the Heaven Slaying Alliance.

Incense burns ceaselessly, its smoke rising in billowing clouds.

Even now, as the Black Calamity rages, the Heaven Slaying Alliance continues to wage war and expand its territory.

In the eyes of the other civilizations, the Heaven Slaying Alliance has long been demonized, indistinguishable from a den of evil. Any living being daring to approach it has almost no chance of emerging alive.

And yet, under these circumstances, they still intend to dispatch an embassy to the Heaven Slaying Alliance to negotiate with them? What a joke—to harbor such naive illusions?

How is this any different from sending a lamb into the tiger’s den to meet its death?

At this moment, the elders of the Xieyue Sect wore expressions of utter dismay, their minds filled with nothing but furious curses.

Who on earth came up with this idiotic idea? Could the person behind it be a spy for the Heaven Slaying Alliance? Intentionally sending people to their deaths?

"The Righteous Unity Alliance’s envoy has already arrived; they’re likely selecting candidates right now. There’s no turning back—unless our Xieyue Sect defies the Righteous Unity Alliance’s rules."

“But isn’t sending people to their deaths exactly what we’re doing right now?”

“Is there any hope of survival left?”

The elders of the Xieyue Sect felt their mouths go dry, a bitter taste filling their throats. No amount of anger would help now; all they could do was pray in their hearts that they would not be chosen.

Leaving the Xieyue Sect at a time like this to rush to the Heaven Slaying Alliance—not to mention the risk of being ambushed by dark spirits along the way—even if they made it to the Heaven Slaying Alliance, they would likely never return.

Judging by the Heaven Slaying Alliance’s modus operandi, they had never given the Righteous Unity Alliance a second thought—why would they care about its envoys?

"How could this be?"

“Isn’t this just the various Daoist factions selecting an elder to lead their disciples to their deaths?” Lan Xin, one of the disciples, stood there in a daze, unable to believe it.

She never imagined that the Sect Master had summoned all the disciples and elders today for this very reason.

Gu Xian’er’s clear eyes widened slightly; she too looked dazed, as if she hadn’t quite processed it yet.

This was the umpteenth time she had heard the name "Heaven Slaying Alliance."

Was this "Heaven Slaying Alliance" the same one she knew?

Lan Xin suddenly noticed a mocking gaze. She turned her head and saw Xu Hui not far away, watching her with schadenfreude, her eyes brimming with undisguised derision.

"What does she mean by that?"

A sense of foreboding suddenly welled up in Lan Xin’s heart.

The vast, open square was, unusually, somewhat quiet at that moment.

The elders and disciples all remained silent, not uttering a word.

After finishing his explanation, the Sect Master of Xieyue Sect’s eyes were filled with helplessness and guilt. Finally, he turned his gaze toward a woman standing ahead—her hair tied in a green spiral bun and draped in a veil, dressed in a long, gray gauze gown.
