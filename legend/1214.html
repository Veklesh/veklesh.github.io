We drove the carriage all day long, but even so, reaching the dungeon in a single day was impossible.

Therefore, they were now preparing to camp by the fire, though this campsite was vastly different from the ordinary.

Of course. Elena's carriage could be used as a normal room, and Elk's carriage, while somewhat cramped, was also quite expensive.

And Rei has a magic tent... On top of that, dinner is a freshly cooked meal, still steaming, that Rei pulled out of the Misty Ring.

...If you told an ordinary adventurer this was camping, they'd never accept it.

In fact, it wouldn't be surprising if someone got angry and shouted, "You've got to be kidding me!"

The group enjoying such a luxurious campfire was now passing the time around the flames.

Soon, everyone would retire for the night.

Normally, a watch would be needed, but naturally, Set would take the watch, so there was no need to worry about that either.

"Gamelion, huh? Looks like it's not going to happen this year."

Elk sighed.

Gamelion dishes, practically a seasonal specialty, were naturally a favorite of Elk's.

In fact, he'd even go out hunting Gamelion himself if it were any other year.

"Yeah... But even if we can't hunt Gamelion, we can still eat Gamelion dishes, right? So maybe we should just be satisfied with that this year? It's not like we're struggling for money or anything."

If they could hunt Gamelion themselves, they could eat as much Gamelion meat as they wanted.

They could also choose their favorite cuts of meat.

If that wasn't possible, buying Gamelion meat sold at Gilm was undoubtedly the best choice.

"Or, if you absolutely must get a whole Gamelion, why not just hire someone to hunt one for you?"

Marina continued after Rei's words, savoring the incredibly luxurious soup. It had been simmered slowly until all the vegetables and meat prepared as ingredients had dissolved, and then more vegetables and meat had been added as additional ingredients.

But Elk, hearing the two of them talk, shook his head exaggeratedly.

"You've got to be kidding. The fun of Gamelion is hunting it yourself, right? ...I wish they'd just show up around here."

"Nah, it won't show up here."

Rei muttered in exasperation at Elk's words.

The places where Gamelion appears are generally fixed.

And this area wasn't one of the areas where Gamelion is found.

Of course, Elk knew that too, but perhaps because he was in a good mood, he retorted to Rei's flippant remark.

"That's why it'd be interesting if it did show up. You think so too, right?"

"Well, I'm not exactly short on Gamelion meat myself. ...Though, of course, having extra would be helpful."

"Well, Rei did get a huge amount of Gamelion meat last year, after all."

As expected of the Guildmaster, Marina understood that Rei had taken down a considerable number of Gamelions in the dungeon last year and claimed a significant portion of the meat for himself.

Marina had no intention of blaming him for it, but it was a fact that last year's Gilm ultimately saw Gamelion meat trading at a slightly higher price than usual.

...Had Rei not defeated those Gamelions in the dungeon, the price would likely have been even higher.

"Hmm, that sounds rather interesting. In our territory too, the Gamelion meat that circulated last year fetched a decent price. ...Could it be that Rei had something to do with that?"

As Elena spoke, tearing off a piece of bread and bringing it to her mouth, the eyes of everyone present turned toward Rei.

Lit by the campfire's glow, Rei quietly averted his gaze.

"Come to think of it, there's actually someone researching how to make goblin meat taste decent right now, and I'm helping them out."

A blatantly abrupt change of subject.

But no one had truly intended to blame Rei, and besides, the idea of a method to make goblin meat tasty piqued their interest.

Everyone's gaze shifted, now filled with genuine interest unlike just seconds before.

Goblin meat was, after all, synonymous with inedible food.

Of course, if it were right in front of you when you were starving, some might reach for it, and if you were on the brink of starvation, you'd likely shove it in your mouth immediately.

But even so, it wasn't something one would normally crave. ...Though there were those in the world who loved goblin meat dearly, who would cook goblin meat dishes and even recommend them to others.

At the very least, none of the members here would willingly choose to eat goblin meat dishes.

So when you hear about research to make goblin meat taste good, it's not strange to find it interesting, even if you wouldn't want to eat it yourself.

"So, what about it? The goblin meat I ate before... how should I put it... it had a tremendous flavor."

Elena asked, her words trailing off.

Normally, a duchess's daughter—especially one nicknamed the Princess General—would never eat goblin meat.

But Elena was also a soldier.

Because of that, she had the experience of eating goblin meat. Even for Elena, born into nobility yet accustomed to meager rations as a soldier, the taste of that goblin meat was not something she would ever want to experience again.

Nevertheless, goblin meat was undoubtedly an excellent food source if one could somehow improve its taste.

Goblins exist everywhere, not just in the frontier. They attack people and livestock, and ravage fields.

If these troublesome creatures could be made edible, fewer people would starve even during poor harvests caused by bad weather.

...Of course, even now, most would choose starvation over eating goblin meat. Yet, as the daughter of Duke Kerebel, it was only natural for her to wish to offer them something delicious if possible.

Though Elena and the others present fixed their expectant, curious gazes upon Rei, his only response was to shake his head.

"Using spices could make the meat somewhat edible, but then it wouldn't be something ordinary people could easily afford."

"I see."

Marina nodded in understanding at Rei's words.

She understood where Rei had acquired such a large quantity of spices.

...Then again, it was Marina who had urged Rei to head to Gosh, so that was only natural.

"So, it's still barely finished, then?"

Rei shrugged slightly at Min, who asked while eating fruit.

"Well, yeah. Besides, if goblin meat were that easy to make tasty, someone would've tried it by now."

It's precisely because it's a common ingredient... no, a monster that making its meat palatable could generate a fortune.

Knowing that, the fact it hasn't been done before is proof just how difficult it is.

"By the way, this is a somewhat successful version. It uses a ton of spices, so it's a bit pricey."

What the Misty Ring pulled out was goblin meat.

However, it wasn't ordinary meat. As Rei had tasted, various spices clung to its surface.

It smelled nothing like goblin meat...

If anything, it was an aroma that stimulated the appetite.

(It smells kind of like curry, you know? Well, since curry is made by combining several spices, calling it curry isn't wrong... But... just how much money would it cost to make curry with this Elgin?)

Spices are generally expensive items.

Back on Earth, if I wanted curry, I could easily buy curry roux and make it.

I even experimented with combining different curry roux or adding secret ingredients.

And if I wanted something even more convenient, I could just buy retort pouch curry.

Expensive retort pouches could cost nearly a thousand yen, but cheap ones were available for as little as a hundred yen.

But making curry in this world requires selecting spices yourself and blending them together.

Back when Rei was in Japan, he'd seen a TV show where someone was so obsessed with curry that they even blended their own spice mixes... but unfortunately, Rei couldn't pull off something like that.

(Curry. Just thinking about it makes me hungry. I wish Takumu or Kabajido had left some curry information behind.)

Takumu, who came to this world before me.

Or Kabajido, who was Vihera's brother.

Given their knowledge of Japan, it wouldn't have been strange for either of them to crave curry.

But as far as Rei knew, curry or anything like it didn't exist in Elgin.

(Throw some spices into soup or something? ...No way. I can't imagine that turning into anything edible.)

Trying to cook with his half-baked knowledge would surely end in failure.

With that thought in mind, Rei roughly cut up the goblin meat, skewered it, and stuck the skewers near the campfire.

Then, the aroma of spices began to waft around, stimulating their appetites.

Elena and the others also turned their hopeful gazes toward the skewered goblin meat.

Once it was nicely grilled, he handed out the goblin skewers to each person.

...Of course, Rei didn't reach for the prepared skewers.

He knew there was more to these skewers than just their appetite-whetting aroma. That is...

"Whoa, what is this?! It's disgusting!"

Elk, drawn by the scent, had sunk his teeth into the goblin meat. But the moment he tasted it, he cried out in surprise.

It wasn't just Elk.

Everyone except Rei, who hadn't eaten the goblin meat, reacted the same way.

"Grrr..."

Set let out a pitiful groan as well.

...Set had tasted this goblin meat before and knew it was unpleasant, yet the aroma, heavily spiced to stimulate appetite, had apparently been too tempting to resist.

"This... just the smell makes it seem incredibly delicious, but actually eating it is awful... How should I put it... Yeah, it's a huge trap."

Marina, who usually radiated feminine charm, couldn't maintain that aura after eating the goblin meat.

As a result, for those who only knew her as the guildmaster, this was a side of her they'd never seen before.

"Honestly, I wouldn't willingly eat this."

"Elena-sama, here. You really need to swallow whatever's in your mouth."

Ara handed Elena, who was frowning, a cup of water.

This water, too, was created by Rei using his Water Blade, and it was more than enough to wash away the taste of goblin meat spreading in her mouth.

...Despite having goblin meat in her own mouth, Ara forced herself to swallow it before offering the water to Elena instead of drinking it herself. This demonstrated the depth of her loyalty.

The others also drank the water created by the Waterfall Dagger, successfully managing to erase the taste of goblin meat from their mouths.

Once everyone had settled down, Rei spoke up.

"Well, that's how it is for now. The smell is certainly enough to stimulate the appetite. The taste, though... it still leaves much to be desired."

"No, the good smell just makes the awful taste stand out even more,"

Everyone except Rei nodded in agreement at Elk's bitter mutter.

"That's why I said it's still in the research phase, right? If we could just get the taste to match that smell..."

Everyone sighed at Rei's words.

In the end, that night concluded with the goblin meat issue, leaving a slightly awkward atmosphere as they dispersed.

Elena, Marina, and Ara boarded Elena's carriage, while Elk and Min took their own.

With everyone departing early the next day, Rei watched them head to their sleeping quarters, a bit early though it was. Then he approached Set.

He headed for Set rather than the magic tent because there was something he needed to do now.

As usual, Yellow was perched on Set's back, happily receiving attention from him.

With those two before him... As Rei approached, naturally, both noticed him.

"Grr?"

"Kyuu!"

Gently stroking the two creatures as they each let out their cries, Rei then pulled out the Orc Leader's magic stone from the Misty Ring.

He decided that, for whatever reason, he should have Set absorb the magic stones he hadn't absorbed yet while he still could.

Set seemed to understand this.

He set Yellow down from his back and approached Rei.

He took the magic stone from the palm Rei held out, bit it, and swallowed it...
