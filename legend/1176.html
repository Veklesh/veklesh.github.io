As the werewolf leader's body fell apart, its upper and lower halves landing in separate spots, the remaining werewolves simultaneously lunged at Rei.

"Huh."

Rei greeted them with a hint of surprise, having assumed they would flee now that their leader was dead.

Of course, surprise didn't mean he intended to simply take their attacks. Using his Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left, he countered each one with his dual-spear style.

His ability to wield the dual-spear style effectively even against the werewolf leader likely stemmed from grasping some sort of technique.

Movements that had been impossible before now felt natural, as if he understood how to move his body.

By surrendering to the movements within himself and riding that flow, his two-spear style was undoubtedly superior to before... no, even superior to just a few hours ago.

Of course, becoming aware of that flow didn't mean he had fully mastered the twin-spear style.

At best, he could only say he'd improved somewhat... reached a level slightly above his previous self.

If anything, it was like going from beginner to novice level.

Rei understood perfectly well that there were still many steps to climb above him.

He evaded, parried, and blocked the attacks of the charging werewolves... then shifted into offense.

He deflected claw strikes with the tip of his Twilight Spear and swung his Death Scythe to cleave through torsos.

The attacks Rei unleashed reaped the lives of the werewolves one after another, who had come seeking vengeance for their leader.

The Death Scythe... As if to say, it wasn't difficult for Rei, wielding the Grim Reaper's scythe, to reap the lives of the werewolves.

(Wait, maybe it's not about revenge? If they kill me, the one who defeated their leader, they could take command of the pack themselves...?)

Even as such thoughts crossed his mind, the swung Death Scythe effortlessly sliced through the werewolf, while the Twilight Spear shattered its skull.

Before he knew it, the number of remaining werewolves had dwindled to just two.

Those two were the ones who hadn't joined the attack while the others charged at Rei.

They were the ones who had calmly judged that they had no chance against Rei, who had so easily defeated the werewolf leader.

To the werewolves, they were cowards, but it was precisely that cowardice that saved their lives from Rei's Death Scythe and Twilight Spear.

"...Well then, what will you two do?"

Rei understood they couldn't understand his words, but he called out anyway, hoping they might flee.

Of course, there was the possibility they might flee and harm other humans. However, the fact that they had been so thoroughly overwhelmed by a single human—well, not strictly human, but still—led him to conclude they were unlikely to attack humans again, no matter what they did.

Furthermore, though Rei couldn't comprehend it, the werewolves could communicate with each other.

Therefore, there was a possibility that the strength of humans might spread.

Of course, Rei's thoughts were incomprehensible to the werewolves.

Even so, the fact that Rei, wielding the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, did not attack them was a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity for the werewolves.

Rei swung the Death Scythe wide, threatening them.

Seeing Rei like this, the two werewolves immediately fled the scene, dashing deep into the forest.

"Well, all sorts of monster hordes are rampaging around anyway... Thinking about it that way, this isn't such a big deal. Plus, it saves the trouble of dismembering and skinning them."

Although Rei intended to request dismemberment and skinning services from the guild once this matter was settled, Misty Ring still contained a wide variety of monster corpses.

Considering the sheer volume, two werewolves were practically negligible.

Rei decided there was no need to worry about it that much. He glanced at the numerous werewolf corpses scattered around the area, along with the werewolf leader's corpse mixed among them, then looked around.

The first thing that caught his eye was Vihera, her face glowing with the excitement of battle as she fought the werewolves.

Unlike the werewolf leader that had attacked Rei, this leader was joining the others in attacking Vihera.

Rather than commanding from the rear, she was the type to charge into the thick of battle first.

(Even among werewolf leaders, they're all different, huh... Well, that makes sense.)

If asked whether all humans share the same personality, Rei would likely answer no.

Similarly, it's perfectly natural for werewolves and werewolf leaders to have their own distinct personalities.

Anyway, it seemed Vihera was enjoying herself, which was good... Deciding that, Rei's gaze shifted next to the Cyclops.

But, naturally, the Cyclops was already dead from Set's strike, and now Set stood guard beside the corpse, keeping watch over the surroundings.

Set clearly had no intention of interrupting Vihera's enjoyment.

"Set aside... it looks like Vihera will take a little longer."

He muttered, but he wouldn't dare interfere with Vihera in the middle of combat.

He understood that doing so would surely earn him the wrath of Vihera, who was clearly relishing the act of combat itself.

"Well, in the meantime, I'll get the minimum preparations sorted out."

He lined up the corpses of the two werewolves and one werewolf leader lying on the ground, then stored the other bodies one after another inside the Misty Ring.

"So only two werewolf leaders after all... Vihera!"

Though he hadn't intervened in Vihera's fight, he called out.

At Rei's call, Vihera narrowly dodged the werewolf's attack, drove a fist into its solar plexus, landed a counter, and then turned his gaze.

Of course, he never completely took his eyes off the enemy during that time.

While focusing his awareness on one point, he also kept his senses alert to the entire battlefield.

This was an essential skill for fighting one against many, and naturally, Vihera possessed it.

While keeping his gaze on Rei, he dodged the swinging claws of the werewolf and slammed his fist into the joint of its elbow.

"Gyaaah!"

The pain of a shattered elbow made the werewolf scream.

Hearing this, Vihera reached for the weakened arm of the wolf with the broken elbow, violently swinging it around to slam it into the enemy. As she did so, she spoke.

"Got it. You're the werewolf leader, huh?"

Her tone was surprisingly calm, as if she weren't in the midst of a fierce battle.

To the werewolf, whose elbow had been shattered and whose broken arm was now being gripped and violently hurled, this must have been incomprehensible.

Regardless, Rei and Vihera succeeded in exchanging words.

Rei judged that speaking further might upset Vihera, so he proceeded to extract the magic stones from the two werewolves while keeping a close watch.

Under normal circumstances, attempting such a thing while combat raged nearby would be tantamount to suicide. Yet the pack fighting Vihera focused solely on that battle and made no move against Rei.

Was it because, despite acting as a pack, they lacked any sense of camaraderie? Or was it because the pack leader fighting Vihera was too absorbed in his own battle?

Whatever the reason, it was undoubtedly a saving grace for Rei.

After extracting the magic stones from the werewolf and the werewolf leader, he stored the three corpses in the Misty Ring as well.

By that time, Vihera's battle was already nearing its conclusion, with only the werewolf leader and three werewolves remaining alive.

Despite the heavy losses, the warwolf leader still refused to admit defeat.

(The Lizardman Leader showed his belly and surrendered, though.)

Rei found himself thinking about the Lizardman Leader for some reason, but right then, Vihera's kick struck the Werewolf Leader's torso.

Feeling the sensation of ribs breaking through his greaves, he withdrew his leg and turned to face the remaining werewolves.

And naturally, the blown-away werewolf leader flew straight toward Rei's position.

(Did he aim for that?)

Thinking that, Rei fixed his gaze on the wolf-man leader lying on the ground and raised the Twilight Spear.

"Gururu..."

Facing the tip of the Twilight Spear being swung down toward its head, the werewolf leader let out a short cry.

That became its final cry as the Warwolf Leader's life faded away.

"The others... did they escape?"

The remaining werewolves, seeing their leader dead, immediately fled.

Vihera, seemingly having lost the will to fight, showed no intention of pursuing the fleeing enemy. She silently watched the backs of the three disappear.

And so, only two people and one beast remained at the scene: Rei, Vihera, and Set.

"That was more enjoyable than I expected,"

Vihera said this, but she certainly didn't look satisfied.

It was only natural. While the werewolf leader was a higher-ranking species, it was still only one step above a regular werewolf.

Had they been truly formidable beings like Generals or Kings, Vihera might have felt genuine satisfaction. This time, however, it was merely a matter of finding it more enjoyable than anticipated.

"Anyway, the request to exterminate the werewolf pack is complete. ...Just absorb the magic stones and we're done?"

"Yeah, but before that, we should stow all the corpses. We've already extracted the magic stones."

Rei muttered while scanning the surroundings.

The thick scent of blood flowing from the werewolves would undoubtedly attract nearby monsters.

Since a werewolf pack was in this forest, the number of other monsters probably wasn't that high, but even so, he didn't want to fight goblins or the like here.

(Well, it's not just goblins—there's definitely a chance other monsters are here too.)

But if asked which monster in Elgin was the most prolific, Rei would undoubtedly answer goblins.

As individuals, they are extremely weak.

But they compensate with strong reproductive ability, capable of breeding not only with humans but also with female monsters or even female animals.

Of course, since capturing human women is easier than capturing other monsters or animals, humans are still the most commonly used for goblin breeding.

"Right. Well then, shall we go?"

Vihera probably didn't want to fight goblins after dealing with the werewolf either. She nodded immediately at Rei's words and proceeded to help him retrieve the corpse of the werewolf she had defeated into his Misty Ring.

Of course, she didn't forget to store the cyclops with its destroyed head in the Misty Ring as well, just to be safe.

Its body bore multiple gashes torn by the werewolf, making it unsuitable as food in that sense.

As raw material, it lacked the most valuable parts like the eyes or horns, so a high price wasn't to be expected. ...Still, since the Cyclops was a fairly high-rank monster, even if sold at a bargain price, it would fetch a decent sum.

After quickly finishing the recovery, Rei and Vihera mounted Set's back as they had when they arrived, clinging to his forelegs as they departed.

While Rei straddling the back was manageable, Vihera clinging to the front legs was extremely difficult to time in a forest like this.

In a normal grassland, they could have had Set take flight first, then turn around so Vihera could reach out and grab onto his legs.

However, in a forest like this one, where Set couldn't fly freely, Vihera needed to grab his front legs the instant Set took off.

Of course, if Set pushed himself, he could fly up above the forest and then dive down to pick up Vihera... but Vihera didn't want to do that.

After all, he was already asking too much of Set, who normally only carried Rei.

Seizing the moment Set took flight, she grabbed onto his front leg. And so, the two humans and one beast left the forest behind them, a place thick with the heavy, iron-rust stench.
