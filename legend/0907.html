Several weeks had passed since the duel between the Baron of Sisne and the Earl of Eriell.

As the new year approached, Gilm remained bitterly cold with snow continuing to fall.

Perhaps because the snowfall started late, Gilm had accumulated an unusually large amount of snow this year.

Residents were all busy shoveling snow, and some even hired adventurers for the task.

For the adventurers themselves, snow shoveling was a welcome job, despite the low pay.

Adventurers still working at this time of year were, naturally, those who had failed to earn enough funds to get through the winter.

In other words, they certainly can't be called skilled adventurers.

That's precisely why the chance to work safely within the city, without having to venture outside Gilm for snow-bound slaying or gathering quests, is such a big draw.

That said, some deliberately take extermination requests, aiming for a big payoff rather than earning little by little.

"...Yeah, that's what I thought. What are you doing here?"

Leaving the Dusk Wheat inn to meet Set, Rei saw Mireine shoveling snow at the entrance and muttered in disbelief.

For a moment, Rei thought he'd mistaken her, but the person standing before him was undoubtedly Mireine, leader of the C-rank party, Scorching Wind.

"Ah, ahahaha. Well, I kinda ran a little short on cash..."

Perhaps because of the snow shoveling, Mireine wasn't wearing her usual leather armor, but rather regular clothes topped with winter gear, and she explained shyly.

The Scorching Wind, known as a rising star and among the most skilled young parties, naturally had no trouble finding work.

In fact, seeing that party members Ecuril and Surnin were absent made it clear that only Mireine was short on cash.

And Rei immediately understood what had caused Mireine to squander her money.

"...Set?"

Mireine froze at the short word that escaped Rei's lips.

It was nothing short of hitting the nail on the head.

"Look, I'm happy you're giving food to Set, but what are you going to do if you end up struggling to make ends meet?"

"But, but it can't be helped! If I don't give Set-chan food, Johanna will!"

"I suppose so."

Even to Rei's eyes, Mireine and Johanna were alike.

Not just in how they doted on Set, but in their personalities too.

That's precisely why they inevitably clashed... and likely spent the funds meant for surviving the winter out of competitive spirit.

For Set, it must have been a happy time every day, getting to eat delicious food, but naturally, this caused financial damage to both Mireine and Johanna.

The result was Mireine shoveling snow in front of Dusk Wheat inn.

"Wh-what is it? Just so you know, Johanna was eyeing this snow shoveling job too. I managed to win it out from under her."

Mireine insisted, seemingly spurred by Rei's gaze.

Why she and Johanna fought over clearing the snow at Dusk Wheat inn was obvious.

After all, shoveling wasn't just about the entrance to the Dusk Wheat inn; it also included the area near the stables.

Mireine's desire to spend even a little more time with Set had undoubtedly reached Rei.

...Though, to be honest, it would have been harder to overlook it.

"Well, I know you're a full-fledged Rank C adventurer now, Mireine, so it's not really my place to say anything... but try not to cause too much trouble for Surnin."

As Rei spoke, recalling Surnin's face—the perfect fit for terms like Mireine's brake, her stopper, her external conscience—Mireine fell silent, her expression guilty.

Seeing her like this, Rei understood she must be keeping it a secret from Surnin that she'd used up all their winter funds.

Even so, it was fortunate for Mireine that it hadn't become fatal by this point, well into the new year.

If she could just hold out for another two months, three at the latest, she'd manage to see spring.

(Though, considering her stubborn rivalry with Johanna, even if she earned money now, she'd probably spend it all immediately.)

As if sensing Rei's thoughts, Mireine thrust her shovel into the snow, a beautiful smile on her face as she spoke.

"Leave it to me. I'll make sure Set-chan gets something delicious to eat."

"No, that's not what I meant. ...Well, there's no point arguing about it now. By the way, Mireine seems to have spent all her money, but what about Johanna? She was lavishing quite a bit on Set too, right? If Johanna wanted to do this snow shoveling too, does that mean she's short on cash as well?"

Mireine aside, Johanna was guarding Muet during that duel incident a few weeks back, so she probably isn't that strapped for cash... That's what I thought as I said it, but the response I got was Mireine's smug smile for some reason.

"Heh heh. Well, I guess I do have some money set aside, but the people I live with forbade me from using any more than what I've saved up? And if I want to buy something extra for Set, they told me to take on jobs and earn it myself."

"...I have absolutely no idea why you look so pleased about this."

"Well, Johanna and I both can't buy souvenirs for Set-chan. But I get to be right here by Set-chan's side like this. I'd say that's already a win for me, wouldn't you?"

"Wait, why are you even competing? Why?"

Though he muttered in exasperation, seeing her unchanged demeanor somehow brought Rei a sense of relief.

"Well, I gotta go now. Keep up the good work shoveling."

He said, starting to walk toward the stables... but the next moment, Mireine shot past Rei at incredible speed and circled around to the front.

"Hold on. There's only the stable over there, but are you going to see Set-chan?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah, I am."

"I'm enduring this snow shoveling even though I want to see Set-chan, and Rei just gets to see him so easily?"

"No, you're shoveling snow because it's your job, right? I didn't sign up for any snow shoveling work."

"That's that, and this is this."

Mireine stood tall and proud, and Rei sighed before speaking.

"You're refreshingly self-centered. ...More importantly, is it okay for you to be talking to me?"

Mireine tilted her head, wondering what Rei meant, as he muttered while catching sight of a scene that had suddenly entered his field of vision.

But the situation wouldn't wait. A figure returning from the stables placed a hand on Mireine's shoulder.

"So it was you, Mireine. The snow clearing isn't done yet, but you have time to play around? Should I contact the guild?"

"Hii! I'll get right on it!"

At Lana's voice, Mireine hurriedly left Rei's side and resumed shoveling snow.

Watching this, Lana let out a small sigh before bowing her head to Rei.

"I'm sorry, Rei-san. I'm putting you to trouble."

"No, don't worry about it. Mireine and I know each other, so it's fine to chat a little."

"Still, you're here on a snow shoveling job, so you need to work properly."

Lana glanced again at Mireine, who was shooting Rei a resentful look.

Catching that look, Mireine redoubled her efforts at shoveling snow.

"Well, I'm off then."

"Yeah, Set was playing in the snow in front of the stables."

Realizing that the stable was where Rei was headed, Lana immediately understood his purpose. She called out to him.

After offering a brief word of thanks to Lana, Rei left Mireine behind and headed toward the stables.

Mireine shot Rei an envious glance, but when Lana looked at her, she turned back to shoveling snow.

"Grrrrrrrrr!"

As Rei walked along the snow path Mireine had likely made towards the stables, that sound reached his ears.

Most residents of Gilm would recognize whose voice it was.

The cheerful-sounding whinny came from the stable, perhaps because he was free to come and go as he pleased.

Of course, ordinary horses or familiars wouldn't be allowed such freedom, but thanks to Set's intelligence and Rei being a regular customer at the Dusk Wheat inn, he was granted a special exception.

When Rei arrived in front of the stable, he found Set playing a game where he'd flop down into the snow, leaving his imprint behind.

"Grrr? Grrrrrrrrrrr!"

Set noticed Rei and growled happily.

Seeing Set like that, Rei smiled as he approached.

"Been doing okay?"

It might have been a bit dramatic since they'd seen each other just yesterday, but Rei still called out to Set.

Set, rising from the snow, ran straight toward Rei.

His favorite person, Rei, had come to see him! Purring happily, he rubbed his face against Rei.

"Want to play? Want to play together?" Set seemed to ask. Rei smiled back and stroked him.

"Yeah, let's play together. ...But how exactly are we going to play? With Set, we can't do things like snowball fights or anything like that."

"Grrr..."

"No good?" Set looked up with those round eyes. Just seeing that scene, he could only be mistaken for a pet... no, a beloved animal.

(A snow hut... might be a bit tricky. So then, a snowman?)

After a moment's thought, deciding that Set could probably manage a snowman, Rei rolled a snowball and placed it before him.

"Okay, Set. Roll this snowball around to make it bigger. Roll it into a ball."

"Guru?"

Set didn't understand what Rei wanted him to do, but since it was Rei speaking, he began rolling the snowball.

As he rolled it, the snowball grew larger and larger. Finding this amusing, he deftly moved his front paws to keep rolling it, and its size steadily increased.

Even though Mireine had shoveled a path to the stable, it was only a limited one.

In other words, outside the path, there was still a lot of snow piled up.

It was in such a spot that Set was making what could be called a "Set拓" (Set imprint), rather than a fish拓.

He kept rolling the snowball, thoroughly enjoying the fresh snow... until it grew to about a meter in diameter.

Not wanting to be outdone by Set, Rei also rolled a snowball, increasing its size.

Even so, his snowball remained smaller than Set's. While part of it was because Set was trying too hard, the main reason was that Set was building the torso, while Rei was building the head.

The finished snowballs turned out exactly as Rei had planned: Set's was a good bit larger.

(Come to think of it, do they even have a snowman culture in Elgin? Well, it doesn't really matter if they don't. Something like this could easily catch on.)

He scanned the area, searching for tree branches.

Fortunately, several trees grew behind them, and he found branches lying on the ground, half-hidden by the snow.

For Rei, who had planned to retrieve firewood from the Misty Ring if worst came to worst during the request's camping phase, this could be considered a stroke of luck.

"Grr?"

What are you doing with that branch? Set asked, his gaze following Rei's movements. Rei placed the snowball he'd made on top of the one Set had created.

Then he broke the branch into manageable pieces and buried it into the snow around his face.

"Ideally, we'd have a bucket or some oranges, but..."

Aside from the bucket, the fruit substitute for the mandarin was inside the Misty Ring, but Rei had no intention of wasting food.

He stuck another branch into the torso section to make arms.

"There! Now the snowman is complete."

"Gurrrrrrr!"

Set growled happily and immediately began running around the snowman.

"I made it myself, so it feels weird to say this, but I didn't expect him to be this happy."

"Guruu"

Seeing Set's happy expression, a smile also appeared on Rei's lips.

After all, it felt good to see someone happy with something he'd made, no matter what it was.

(If he's this happy, maybe I should make a snow hut too?)

He thought this as snow flurries drifted down.

But a snow hut big enough for Set to fit inside would have to be quite large.

Considering the effort involved, the physical strain aside, the mental fatigue would be immense.

If I were serious about making one, I'd need to bring Mireine and Johanna along.

(Those two would gladly help if I said it was for Set's sake. ...But then again, a snow hut means no mochi. If I had glutinous rice, I could make mochi...)

Being in the countryside, Rei's house back in Japan had a mortar and pestle properly stored away.

Having used them to pound mochi before, Rei naturally knew how to make it.

But the key ingredient, glutinous rice, doesn't exist in Gilm.

(I've never seen it in stores either.)

As she pondered this, she suddenly noticed commotion near the entrance of Dusk Wheat inn as dusk fell.

Wondering why people had gathered, she and Set started to head that way, when... A loud voice came from the entrance.

"Rei, are you there? I've come to see you!"

Yes, that voice sounded vaguely familiar.
