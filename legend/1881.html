“...Oh, the magic stone.”

Rei had been watching the giant dragon’s head vanish, but suddenly realized something and muttered that.

Setting Gaiska aside, the magic stone belonging to the creature—presumably the monster that had tried to break the seal—had been swallowed by the dragon’s head that Elena had created using dragon-language magic alongside Gaiska, and was no longer there.

Rei realized this only now… but since there hadn’t been any other means of attack in that situation, he resigned himself to giving up on the magic stone.

Since it was the magic stone of what appeared to be a monster that could only be sealed, not defeated, there’s no doubt that Rei had been hoping—well, actually, he’d been counting on it quite a bit.

“I’m sorry. But I couldn’t very well unleash a Breath of Light right here.”

“Ah… I see. No, don’t worry about it. If I’d used Deep Flame to completely burn that flesh cocoon to ashes, I wouldn’t have had to trouble Elena.”

Rei understood that this incident was caused by his own lack of thoroughness, so he had no intention of blaming Elena.

…To be precise, it wasn’t so much Rei’s lack of thoroughness as it was being outmaneuvered by Gaiska’s unexpectedly strong vitality.

“More importantly, how’s the Misty Ring doing?”

“Hm? Oh. …No problem.”

At Elena’s prompting, Rei pulls the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

If he’d had this, the battle against Deotres would have been much easier… but if he can’t use it when it matters most, then… I’m curious about what kind of abilities that flesh cocoon possessed.

(I never thought it would block the Misty Ring’s effects. Or maybe it was sealed precisely because it was capable of something like that?)

As he thought about that, Rei suddenly remembered.

The ability to manipulate space.

And this space, too, had been created by that ability.

Now that the one who created it was dead, there was no guarantee this space would remain intact forever.

“Elena, let’s get out of here quickly and go back to that house. If that flesh cocoon was what was forming this space…”

By the time he said that much, Elena must have understood what Rei was getting at.

She nodded quickly and turned her gaze toward the passageway they had entered through.

In that instant… as if waiting for that very moment, a sharp crack echoed around them.

It was a faint sound, yet Rei and Elena didn’t miss it.

“Let’s go!”

Shouting that, Rei started running without waiting for Elena’s reply.

Naturally, Elena followed right behind Rei.

Although the space was quite large, with Rei and Elena’s physical abilities, that distance was no problem at all.

Listening to the sound of the space being torn apart—a series of sharp, cracking noises—Rei and Elena entered the passageway.

Naturally, since that passageway had also been expanded by the influence of that flesh cocoon, they didn’t expect to come out unscathed.

Rei and Elena ran through the passageway at full speed.

The passage was narrow, so naturally, running at full speed presented various difficulties, but since there was a risk of being caught up in the collapse of the space if they slowed down, they had no choice but to push on.

Crack, snap, snap, snap… Those sounds came one after another.

It seemed certain that this space would be destroyed before long, but their top priority was simply to escape.

Fortunately, both Rei and Elena possessed physical abilities that could only be described as superhuman.

The speed at which they were running through the corridor at full speed was far beyond what any ordinary person could possibly achieve.

Yet Rei and Elena showed no sign of being bothered by it and kept running… Eventually, they came upon the spot where they had entered the passageway—the location of the hidden door leading to what appeared to be a study or something similar.

The hidden door was already open, so they were able to run straight through without having to go through the trouble of opening it again.

“Huh!? …Eh? Lady Elena? Rei?”

Ara, who had been reading documents at her desk, half-instinctively raised her Power Axe against Rei and Elena as they suddenly burst out of the secret passage, but she immediately realized they were people she knew and lowered her guard.

But Rei and Elena shouted desperately at Ara.

“Ara, get outside! This mansion is probably about to collapse!”

“Rei’s right! Just grab whatever papers you can! Let’s go!”

With that, Rei and Elena grabbed as many documents as they could from the desk and rushed out of the study.

……Under normal circumstances, she wouldn’t be able to believe something like this right away.

But… for Ara, the option of not believing Elena’s words simply didn’t exist. She immediately grabbed her Power Axe and a handful of documents, then rushed out of the study after Rei and the others.

The reason neither Rei nor Elena checked on Ara’s reaction was… likely because they understood that, being Ara, she wouldn’t hesitate at a moment like this.

Leading the way for Ara and Elena, Rei ran at the front.

He was acting with the intention of dealing with any traps set within the house himself the moment they were triggered…

(There are no traps?)

As he ran down the hallway, Rei found himself wondering.

The distance to that study wasn’t particularly long, but there had been countless traps set along the way.

Given that, he had assumed countless traps would go off just as he was escaping the house... but in reality, not a single trap had been triggered, and he was able to run without any trouble.

While running, Rei stowed the documents in his hand into his Misty Ring as he thought… and eventually came to a conclusion.

Most likely, Celestan had possessed some sort of key item that controlled the magic item—the house—which generated these traps one after another.

However, Celestane had everything he possessed absorbed by Gaiska, who harbored intense hatred toward him.

Perhaps, at that point, there was a key item of some sort within Gaiska—no, within that lump of charcoal—that controlled the countless traps in this house.

However, the lump of charcoal that held Gaiska’s will was devoured and killed by the dragon’s head that appeared through Elena’s dragon-language magic.

In other words, whatever that key item might have been no longer exists in this world.

To begin with, Rei doesn’t even know where it goes when something is devoured by that dragon’s head.

(Even if I asked Elena… she probably wouldn’t know either.)

In any case, Rei concluded that it was precisely because the key item was lost along with Celestine that the traps set up in this house had stopped activating.

That was merely Rei’s own speculation, and the reality might be different.

But for now, Rei decided it would be easier to understand if he just assumed that was the case.

“Let’s head outside!”

To begin with, the house where Celestine used to live wasn’t exactly the size of a mansion.

That’s why, if Rei and the others ran as fast as they could, they could make it outside in less than a minute.

However… just as Rei and the others were about to step outside, as if they’d been waiting for that exact moment, the door swung open and several soldiers appeared.

For a split second, Ray wondered if these might be the soldiers from the Duke of Kerebel’s household that Elena had brought with her… but the ones who actually entered the house were soldiers from the Marquis of Seisor’s household.

Come to think of it, it made perfect sense. The soldiers of the Seisor Marquis’s household had been trying to break through the traps and enter the house even before Rei and the others had entered.

In that situation, what would happen if they realized that the traps had stopped working because of Celestine’s death…? The scene before Rei and the others’ eyes was the answer.

“What!? Get back! Don’t come inside! This house is about to blow up!”

Rei didn’t know exactly what would happen. He didn’t know… but from behind, he could hear crackling, creaking, and other sounds that were hard to describe.

What’s more, the sounds never seemed to fade or move away—it was as if they were chasing after Rei and the others.

Despite this, the soldiers who had entered the house—perhaps elated at being the first of the Marquis of Seisor’s troops to enter—showed no reaction to Rei’s angry shout.

…To be precise, there were soldiers who entered the house before those men, but all of them had fallen into a trap and escaped.

However, Rei could feel the sound of destruction coming from behind him growing louder and louder. Realizing that staying here any longer would be absolutely disastrous... and that there was no point in trying to reason with the soldiers, he decided to force his way through.

“Get out of the way!”

Shouting once more, Rei charged toward the soldiers gathered in front of the door.

Naturally, seeing Rei and his group charging at them like that, the soldiers had no choice but to respond.

Although he wasn’t armed, Rei was charging straight at them.

Given that, they realized they would collide if they stayed put… but with so many soldiers coming up from behind, they couldn’t step back through the door.

In that situation, naturally, they had to do something. Some tried to rush inside the house to get out of Rei’s path… and others, for whatever reason, interpreted Rei’s actions as a hostile act against them and tried to draw their longswords.

Of course, in a situation where people were crowded together and could barely move, there was no way they could pull a longsword from its sheath at their waist, and in the end, even if they tried to draw it, they couldn’t.

Rei charged straight at those soldiers.

Naturally, crashing straight into the soldiers caused them all sorts of injuries, but there was no other way.

(I don’t have time to break through the walls… and above all, given that this house is full of traps, it wouldn’t be surprising if the walls were sturdier than they look. …And will this phenomenon—which appears to be the collapse of this space—really be confined to the interior of this house?)

Although Rei had fought the flesh cocoon within the space inside this house, there was no guarantee whatsoever that the phenomenon resembling the collapse of space would be confined solely to this house.

Given that, the priority was to get out of this house as quickly as possible… and then out of the property, and if possible, out of the slums as well. Depending on the situation, the option of escaping from Anesis itself could not be ruled out.

If this house were larger—on the scale of a mansion—the doors leading outside would be wide enough that she could have used Sleipnir Boots or some other means to run right over the soldiers’ heads.

...In that case, aside from Elena, who like Rei possessed Sleipnir Boots, there was also the problem of how to get Ara—who didn’t have them—to safety.

In any case, while such a stunt might have been possible in a mansion, this house—though slightly larger than average—was by no means on the scale of a mansion.

That’s exactly why the entrance is no different from an ordinary house, so we can’t use a method like running over the soldiers’ heads to escape outside.

(It can’t be helped. Don’t blame me. Besides, if I blow you away, you’ll end up leaving this house anyway, so you should be fine.)

Having made up his mind... Rei charged straight into the crowd of soldiers from the shoulder.

Looking at Rei’s stance, it wasn’t hard to figure out what he was about to do.

Naturally, the expression of the soldier who would be the first to collide with Rei contorted violently.

To make matters worse, that man knew exactly who was charging toward him.

Though he was small in stature, he was about to collide head-on with an adventurer of renown.

Naturally, being rammed by such a person… and on top of that, considering the fellow soldiers behind him, it goes without saying just how much damage he would sustain caught in the middle.

But… Rei was already right in front of him, and there was nothing he could do about it now.

All the man could do now was try to avoid getting seriously injured—no, to avoid dying—even if he was knocked flying by Rei.

And in the next instant, Rei’s consciousness, as if told that such resolve was useless, simply fell into darkness.
