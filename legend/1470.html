Only a faint light filled the room.

Lively voices could be heard from outside.

Gilm's nights were quite noisy, partly because it was summer, and partly because the workers doing the expansion were making a racket.

By the time the date changed, things would quiet down somewhat with work looming the next day... but now was still the height of the evening, with no sign of it settling down.

Laughter, cheers, shouts, and screams echoed from all directions.

Insects buzzed around the room, and the air remained stiflingly warm despite nightfall, causing the people inside to grow increasingly irritable.

"Hey, how's the headcount coming along? With the current size of Gilm, you shouldn't be having trouble gathering women, right?"

One man among several in the dimly lit room asked.

"We've gathered a decent number. The problem is where to lock them up. It's fine for now, but they might be found eventually... The smell is a problem too."

The man who was asked sighed and muttered, but a third man in the room spoke up.

"You idiot. The smell is what's good, right? It's their body odor!"

"...I just don't get your kinks. Think about it—if this were winter, fine, but it's summer now! Locking multiple women in one cell in this heat? They're using pots for their business, not wiping themselves down, so the sweat smell... fine. Got it. Now stop staring at me with those gleaming eyes."

The second man noticed the third man staring at him with shining eyes, full of expectation. Before he could say anything else, he cut off the conversation.

He couldn't quite keep up with the other man's fetish—where a woman's scent was a welcome treat—especially since he himself only had ordinary sexual preferences.

And certainly, he couldn't agree when they happily discussed the smell in such detail.

The first man, who had been silently listening to their exchange, spoke up as if to cut off the pointless topic.

"Anyway, we still don't have enough women, right?"

"Yeah... We should've been able to gather them more easily. I never expected security to be this tight."

The other two nodded in agreement at the man's irritated mutter.

It was a Gilm where many gathered for the expansion work.

Naturally, with so many people, there were bound to be many unfamiliar faces.

That's precisely why these three had come to the guild to gather people, but Gilm's security was surprisingly good.

In a village where most residents knew each other, it might be different, but in a town or city of this scale, many guards would turn a blind eye to crimes for money.

But there were none of those among Gilm's guards.

(Well, people are creatures of desire. It's not like there aren't any such scum. If that's the case, then it's simply that we haven't been able to find them yet.)

Being on the frontier, perhaps the corruption in Gilm was simply more sophisticated.

The man thought this, clicking his tongue in disgust.

In the end, they couldn't secure women from places where many gathered. Consequently, they established connections with a criminal organization based in the slums, who supplied women to them.

Naturally, the criminal organization didn't provide women without expecting something in return.

They took a hefty sum from the men in exchange for the women.

This meant costs inevitably rose, making it difficult for the men to gather the number of women they had initially planned for within Gilm.

In fact, the funds allocated from above for operational expenses had already dwindled to about half.

And this despite not having gathered all the women they were aiming for yet.

"Still, it would be nice if they didn't care who the women were, as long as they were women."

"Ah... well, I can't say I don't understand that feeling. Ugly ones are out of the question, and at least average-looking women... what does that even mean? Well, better-looking women are nicer to smell, at least when it comes to body odor and stuff."

Ignoring that last remark, the men continued their conversation.

"Well, what can you do? We need the women to give birth to children over there. And as many women as possible."

"...It's not about taking clients and then making them have kids. It's about making them take clients so they can have kids. ...Doesn't feel great, does it?"

"Personally, I kinda like the smell aspect, though."

"Shut up, pervert."

A moment of slight calm passed between the three men after the last one spoke.

Amidst this... one man finally spoke up.

"Come to think of it, didn't you say yesterday you were going on patrol in town today?"

"...I was planning to. Figured maybe I could handle some of the women on the main street. But the guild asked me, so what can you do?"

"You could have just refused."

"Don't be stupid. I've got a reputation for being pretty serious in the guild, you know? There's no way I could turn down a request proposed by the guild itself."

"Ha! Ajas being serious? ...What kind of joke is that?"

The man... At Ajas' words, the two men in the room each let out a laugh, as if they'd heard some amusing joke.

Honestly, if Ajas was serious, wouldn't that mean there'd be no unserious people left in the world?

Even that thought crossed their minds as the two men laughed.

"Levjef, Hastun. Honestly, I'm not happy about you laughing at me. Especially you, Hastun. You're a pervert who loves the scent of women, aren't you?"

"Calling it perverted is a bit much. It's definitely a refined taste that others can't understand, though."

"...A refined taste, huh? It's all in how you phrase it. Anyway. Back to the point. I was assigned to patrol Treant Forest today..."

A hint of unease mingled with Ajas's words as he spoke.

The other two seemed to pick up on it too. They waited for Ajas to speak.

"Rei showed up for guard duty in Treant Forest."

"Huh? Rei? That Crimson one? You're kidding, right?"

Levjef's voice sounded almost dazed.

But Ajas's words carried enough shock to make it seem entirely plausible.

After all, Rei had been assigned a great deal of work concerning the current expansion of the Gilm.

Why would Rei, of all people, deliberately take time out of his busy schedule to come to Treant Forest?

Neither Levegef nor Haston could fathom the reason.

"Well... the timber from Treant Forest is considered an important material for this expansion... but even so, with the woodcutters there, I can't imagine Crimson going."

"Yeah. Normally, he'd have plenty more work to do."

Hastun murmured in agreement with Levegef's words.

It wasn't a particularly unique opinion; anyone who knew how capable Rei was would likely agree.

Clearly, assigning Rei to guard Treant Forest was a poor use of resources.

"Yeah. ...And besides, maybe, just maybe... I think Rei's target was me."

"...Hey."

Unlike his lighthearted demeanor just seconds ago, a low voice escaped Levegef's lips.

His gaze, too, was no longer the familiar, easygoing look; it had sharpened.

"You didn't go and do something stupid and get caught, did you?"

"No, that shouldn't be the case. Since arriving in Gilm, I haven't made any particular moves."

Ajas could say that with confidence.

In truth, since arriving in Gilm, he had worked as diligently as possible as an adventurer.

He could state with certainty that he hadn't drawn attention to himself.

Still, the words Rei had spoken stirred Ajas's wariness.

(Killed a merchant? ...Which merchant? There shouldn't be any witnesses left in the first place. There was a survivor... or something like that, right?)

Looking at the snake tattoo on his left hand—or more accurately, the tattoo carved into his left hand—Ajas felt a bad premonition.

The tattoo on his left hand was extremely conspicuous.

If anyone had survived, this snake tattoo would easily make him identifiable.

What troubled him most was not knowing which merchant Rei was referring to.

Ajas had killed countless people... no, dozens of people.

Naturally, several of them had been merchants, and Ajas had absolutely no idea which incident Rei was referring to.

"So, what now? Crimson... no, if Rei's glaring at you, you can't make any rash moves right now, can you?"

"Yeah. I'd better lay low for a while. You guys will have to work harder to make up for it, though..."

"You owe me."

"Yeah, you got a debt. But if you hook me up with a woman who has a strong body odor, I'll let it slide."

Huston's words eased the tension in the room, at least a little.

"Alright, I'll let it slide. Forget about the body odor nonsense. I'll arrange a woman for Huston later."

Of course, "preparing a woman" didn't mean introducing her—it meant forcibly snatching her away.

What they were discussing was nothing short of criminal, yet upon hearing this, Huston broke into a broad smile that seemed to blow away the earlier tension and spoke up.

"Are you sure it's okay?"

"Yeah. It's payback for the trouble I caused."

"Well... I guess having a friend like you is priceless."

"A woman for Haston? Then I can expect all sorts of things too, right?"

"Yeah. Expect away. ...Anyway, back to the matter at hand—how to deal with Rei... Honestly, I was thinking maybe I could just keep Rei occupied like this, and you guys could handle the woman. What do you say?"

"...I see. Word is Rei has a nose for trouble. Seems plenty of crooks have gone down because of him."

"Thinking about it that way, while Ajax keeps Rei occupied, we can relax a bit. ...Though, there are some sniffing around Ajax lately, so we can't make too many loud moves either."

Huston muttered resentfully.

Officially, Ajas, Levjef, and Haston are strangers to each other.

Each had come to Gilm as a solo adventurer.

That's precisely why Levegef and Haston noticed. That someone had been investigating Ajas recently.

If the investigator was skilled, it was naturally difficult for the subject—in this case, Ajas—to notice.

But for someone in a neutral position, spotting the one investigating Ajas wasn't difficult.

Of course, this isn't something ordinary amateurs could manage, but Levjef and Huston, having come to Gilm specifically to abduct a large number of women, possessed skills that could be called first-rate in the conventional sense.

Precisely because of that, when they happened to encounter Ajas in the streets, the guild, taverns, shops... places like that, they noticed the presence of those probing him.

And once Levjef and Huston knew, the information naturally flowed to Jas as well.

"I suspect it's someone working for Rei... but from what I know, Rei shouldn't have that kind of organization."

There was a connection between Rei, Marina, Daskar, and Egg, but Ajash probably didn't know about that.

Then again, since they were aware they were doing something shady, it wouldn't be surprising if someone was investigating them.

"Logically, the most suspicious ones would be the guys in the slums, right?"

"But we're their business partners, right? Would they really do something to make us suspicious? It'd only hurt them."

Hastun countered Levjef's words.

But Ajas shook his head at Haston's words.

"They might be suspicious of us too. ...Besides, normally, they wouldn't go to the trouble of gathering this many women. Why they need that many is probably something they're curious about too."

"...Probably hoping to get a piece of the action themselves."

"That pisses me off."

The words "infuriating" that came from Ajas's mouth were echoed in agreement by both Levjef and Hastun.

Even though they'd made a deal with us and were making a killing... they still want to get involved?

"Should we just wipe them out and approach another organization? With all the people flocking to Gilm now, gathering women shouldn't be a problem, right?"

Levjef suggested, but Ajas shook his head in refusal.

"Let's hold off. We don't have solid proof it's them. If we pull something like that and it turns out they're not involved at all, we'll be in a sticky situation. ...If we do anything, we need solid evidence first."

The other two nodded in agreement.

Thus, perhaps feeling uneasy about engaging in shady business, Ajas and the others fell into a mild state of suspicion.

...As for Rei, well, that was one thing. But for Ilse, whose true identity remained unknown, it was probably fortunate.
