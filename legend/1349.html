The inn employee guided Rei and the others to where the carriage was parked, then immediately returned to the inn.

Even if it was only Precious's room, the fact that guards were searching meant they needed to calm the other guests staying at the inn and explain the situation.

With so many tasks demanding their attention, they simply didn't have the bandwidth to spare for Rei and her companions.

That said, if Rei and his group had been unknown individuals, the staff wouldn't have simply left them alone until the carriage inspection was thoroughly completed.

The staff allowed it solely because Marina, who had been the guildmaster until just the other day, was among them, and because Rei himself, as an adventurer with a nickname, commanded a certain level of trust.

Anyway, after confirming the employee had left, Rei called out to Set, who was looking around with keen interest.

"Set, this carriage. Can you detect Precious's scent?"

"Grrrrr... Grrr!"

Set nuzzled his face against the carriage, taking in its scent, then let out a low growl.

What was there was a sense of relief.

Seeing Set, Rei breathed a sigh of relief.

"I see. We can track it. ...Then, let's pursue Precious right away."

"Rei, shouldn't we tell the guards?"

Vihera, who had been watching Rei and Set's exchange, asked, but Rei shook his head.

"They're moving on their own, and we should move on ours. Besides, moving with the others would likely slow us down."

The members of Crimson Wings had spent the winter together and trained in combat, so they understood each other's actions, if not perfectly, then at least somewhat.

But if someone like the guard—a complete stranger in terms of combat experience—got involved, there was a risk their reactions would be delayed when it really mattered.

Vihera seemed to understand this. His question was merely a formality; he didn't press further.

"Alright, let's go. ...Set, count on you."

"GRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a happy growl and left the place that could be called a hangar.

Several carriages stood around them, each slightly different from the others.

Rei found them somewhat intriguing, but catching up to Precious took priority for now.

(Maybe I'll ask to see the storage area at Dusk Wheat inn later.)

The high-class inn where Rei and the others were staying, the Dusk Wheat inn, naturally had a storage area for carriages, given the large number of merchants and adventurers who used it.

After all, the stable where Set usually stayed was also a stable for the horses that pulled those carriages.

...Lately, thanks to Rei making Dusk Wheat inn his regular lodging, Set had been treated almost like the stable master.

Anyway, deciding that if they were curious about the carriage, they could look at the one at Dusk Wheat inn later, Rei and the others moved straight on to tracking Precious.

Set, who had been sniffing near the inn's entrance, must have eventually picked up Precious's scent from the odors drifting in the air. Showing no sign of hesitation, he began walking.

And so, Rei and the others followed Set.

The guards watched them with complicated expressions, but Rei and the others seemed unconcerned, simply ignoring the stares.

Meanwhile, the guards who had initially been scattered around the inn had already been gathered inside. They were searching the rooms Precious used and questioning other guests for information.

Some, having confirmed Rei and the others had left, were now inspecting the carriage.

(Well, do your best. We'll take advantage of that opening to move freely.)

Glancing back at the inn, Rei followed Set.

After walking for a while...

"Ah, this really is a slum district, isn't it?"

The first to notice this was, unsurprisingly, Marina, who had lived in Gilm the longest among them.

She must have understood that the direction Set was heading was toward the slum district.

However, it was a different route than when Rei and the others had headed for the slums before.

This area... Precious must have chosen it to try and confuse the pursuers, even just a little.

"Even if it is the slums, or even outside Gilm, there's no way he'll escape Set's nose."

Rei, who placed great trust in Set's sense of smell, muttered proudly.

He reached out to stroke Set's body, but quickly withdrew his hand, realizing he mustn't disturb Set while he was tracking a scent.

In truth, Rei's deep trust in Set stemmed largely from Set's proven track record in scent-based tracking.

His Scent Enhancement skill was still at level four, not yet reaching level five where the skill would be significantly boosted.

Even so, thanks to his inherently sharp griffin sense of smell, he was performing admirably.

"Now then, I wonder how far Precious intends to run. With Gifnan captured, there's no more room for excuses. If that's the case, then it should have ended the moment he was caught..."

"Is that so? Seeing that Rei took Gifnan away, she left the inn immediately. That means she abandoned Gifnan, right? Then, she might insist this whole thing was Gifnan's own decision and action?"

Vihera stated this in response to Marina's words, but Marina, who had been listening, shook her head.

"You're a former princess too, Vihera. You should understand. A subordinate's failure reflects on their superior. And Gifnan wasn't even an adventurer belonging to the Spier Trading Company. He was an adventurer Precious hired privately, right? So she can't escape responsibility for that."

"...Hmm. That sounds pretty tough."

While this conversation unfolded, Set steadily tracked Precious's scent, and soon Rei's group entered the slum district.

"Well, we should be catching up soon... which is why you lot showed up, right?"

About ten minutes after entering the slums... Rei drew Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring and readied them, Marina nocked an arrow to her bow, Vihera assumed a fighting stance, and Byune readied White Cloud.

Appearing before the group were faces familiar even to Rei and the others... Yes, Precious's guards.

"...I didn't expect you to reach this far so quickly. I'd be happy if you just turned back now, but I doubt you'd listen to that. Still, I can't let you pass through here."

The man who seemed to be the leader among the guards, a man in his thirties, muttered while holding his longsword.

His expression was bitter.

He didn't believe they could defeat Rei and the others in a fight.

Still, to save Precious, they needed to buy time here.

(Well, even if we stall them here, who knows how much distance that'll actually buy Precious to escape?)

After Gifnan was taken by Rei, Precious hesitated a little before deciding to leave the inn.

She arrived in the slums, but never imagined Rei would pursue them so quickly.

The promise Rei made to several people in the slums—to alert her if anyone with a griffin came—had paid off.

Even so, the guards standing before Rei now were barely holding themselves upright, barely suppressing the fear within them.

"So, you're standing in our way. Looks like you're aware you've committed a crime, Precious. ...The more you interfere with our capture, the heavier your guilt becomes."

"...I can't just nod and agree to that, you know. Unfortunately."

The man shouted, clenching his arms tightly to keep the longsword in his hand from trembling.

"I see. ...Then I suppose we'll have to respond accordingly."

Even as he said this, Rei had absolutely no intention of killing the people before him.

No, rather, he knew they were opponents he could never defeat, yet they still chose to fight him for Precious' sake.

That loyalty was something even Rei found admirable.

...That said, it didn't mean Rei was kind enough to indulge them.

He felt a certain respect for the people standing before him, but letting Precious escape here was not an option.

"Well... let's go."

Those words served as the signal to begin the battle, and the Crimson Wings launched their attack.

Rei stepped forward, Death Scythe and Twilight Spear at the ready.

As the distance closed with the leader of the guards, he leapt to evade the man's swinging longsword.

Simultaneously activating Sleipnir Boots, he kicked off midair and landed behind the man.

Turning in mid-air, he swung the hilt of Death Scythe, cutting at the man's legs.

Without breaking his turn, he strikes the man, who is about to fall to the ground after having his legs swept out from under him, with the hilt of the Twilight Spear.

That single blow sent the man flying several meters sideways, crashing into a building before collapsing to the ground.

The impact of his back hitting the wall knocked him completely unconscious, but considering the difference in strength between Rei and the man, that was only to be expected.

Confirming he had only knocked him unconscious and not killed him, Rei turned his gaze to the other battles.

Vihera danced through the air, effortlessly dispatching about three guards. Marina kept them at bay with her bow while using spirit magic to rob them of consciousness.

Only Byune was struggling somewhat against an opponent who, despite the presence of White Cloud, possessed considerable skill. Still, he ultimately created an opening by throwing a transparent long needle, then drove the hilt of White Cloud into the opponent's solar plexus, seizing their consciousness.

As for Set, he struck with a blow deliberately restrained to avoid killing his opponent, effortlessly seizing their consciousness.

...The entire battle lasted less than three minutes.

The longest fight, unsurprisingly, was against Byune, the weakest member of the Crimson Wings.

"Yeah, we didn't kill everyone. ...Well then, Set. Shall we pursue Precious again? They probably don't think they can handle us with this force. If that's the case, they might have already fled."

"Considering the battle time, it's doubtful how far they could have fled in such a short span. It would be different if they had horses, but the carriages were left behind at the inn."

Vihera's words made Byune nod.

Rei and Marina agreed with that assessment, but they couldn't rule out the possibility he still had some means of escape.

Then, rather than risking a reckless move, it would be wiser to capture them quickly.

"Grrrr!"

"Alright, let's go!" Set growled as he advanced through the slum district.

Then, Rei and the others vanished... Presumably, they judged this area safe. The slum dwellers emerged, attempting to strip the unconscious guards of their weapons, armor, clothes... and any gold or jewels they might carry.

They approached the unconscious men, but...

"Hey!"

The man who was unconscious... or rather, who should have been unconscious, opened his mouth. At that voice, the slum dwellers froze.

The slum dwellers had misunderstood one thing: even though they'd been crushed by the Crimson Wings, Precious's guards were skilled fighters to begin with.

The only way to explain why they suffered this fate was that they had simply faced a formidable opponent.

Moreover, given the manner of their defeat, their injuries weren't severe, and physically, they still had enough left... at least enough to fight the men standing before them.

Hearing the men's voices, the slum dwellers stopped in their tracks, seemingly taken aback. Realizing the gap in strength between themselves and the men, they simply walked away.

Watching them go, the man pondered what to do next... what should be done now that Precious had been captured.

"Gurrrrr!"

Set ran through the slums, growling.

His speed was clearly faster than when he was outside the slums.

It was proof that he was closing in on Precious.

Guided by Set, Rei and the others advanced... but suddenly, Set stopped in front of a building—a very old one.

"Is this it?"

"Guruu"

In response to Rei's brief question, Set let out an equally short growl.

Seeing Set confirm with his growl, Rei and the others didn't question it.

"Let's go."

"Yeah."

Rei and Vihera exchanged brief words, while Marina and Byune nodded silently.

Since Set couldn't go inside, he naturally stayed outside the building, keeping watch to make sure Precious didn't escape.

And so, when Rei and the others entered the building... unexpectedly, Precious greeted them with a somewhat composed demeanor.

Considering how she'd escaped the inn, the sofa Precious sat on wasn't something she'd brought with her; it must have been here originally.

"I'm surprised. When you sent your guards over here, I thought you'd already escaped."

"...Unfortunately, I didn't think I could escape from Rei alone, Rei."

Realizing escape was impossible, Precious surrendered without the slightest resistance, her capture ending as abruptly as it began.
