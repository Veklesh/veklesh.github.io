Once a decision is made, Rei acts quickly.

Well, I suppose that’s only natural, since he doesn’t really need any preparation to begin with.

Basically, almost all of Rei’s gear is stored in his Misty Ring, so there’s no need to go back for anything… or anything like that.

I did wonder if I should let Elena and the others know about the pirate hunt, but seeing the hopeful looks from the villagers and thinking it wouldn’t take that long, I decided I could wait until dinner tonight to tell them.

“Besides, these are pirates in the middle of nowhere. I can’t imagine they’re all that strong, combat-wise.”

“Guruu?”

At Rei’s words, Set turned his head back, asking, “What’s wrong?”

I patted Set on the back and told him it was nothing… and soon, the island we were looking for came into view below us.

It had been only a few minutes since they took off from the village... though, of course, that was only possible because of Set’s speed; if they were traveling by ship, it would take a fair amount of time.

“Hmm… Judging from the view from the sky, there seem to be quite a few people there.”

On the opposite side of the village, a simple—really, very simple—harbor had been constructed.

It was likely built using the trees growing on the island, but even a makeshift harbor requires a fair amount of effort to construct.

Since the pirates on the island had managed to do that, there was no doubt they had a decent-sized crew.

Above all, since there were two ships in that makeshift harbor, it’s safe to assume they’re a pirate crew of considerable size.

(Well, both of them look pretty old, though.)

Even from the air, there were visible signs that the ship’s deck had been repaired in several places.

The sails are currently folded up, so I can’t tell what condition they’re in, but judging by the deck, it’s highly likely they’ve undergone repeated repairs as well.

That in itself isn’t a problem. It isn’t, but… one of the main reasons Rei wanted to defeat these pirates was to get their hands on their ships.

Once the expansion work currently underway in Gilm is complete, Gilm’s next project will be research to enable ships to move on land.

The basis for this is the sand-walking ship, but at the same time, there aren’t many people in Gilm who possess the know-how to build ships.

It’s not that there are none at all, but it is a fact that there are very few.

For that reason, a training ship to study shipbuilding would be essential, even when converting a sand ship into a land ship.

Rei’s idea was to use a ship captured from pirates as that training vessel.

Since they’ll ultimately be worn out as training vessels, old ships are fine… but honestly, even if we can’t get a brand-new ship, I’d prefer to have something a bit sturdier.

(No, I suppose it’s better to have a training ship at all. Besides, if the ship is too new, we’ll have to ask Lord Daskar to pay a higher price when he buys it back. …Come to think of it, maybe a rickety old ship is actually better?)

Rei was lost in thought, examining the ship, when Set turned around and asked, “What are you going to do?” snapping him back to reality.

“Right. Well, I guess it’s time to attack. For now… to make sure they can’t escape by ship, could you guard the ship, Set? If there’s anyone on board, attack them just enough to subdue them without killing them.”

“Grr!”

In response to Rei’s words, Set growled, “Got it!”

After petting Set, Rei asked him to go downstairs.

“Let’s make a big show of it. Otherwise, it’ll be a pain to hunt down and take out the pirates one by one.”

“Grr! Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrr!”

Obeying Rei’s words, Set descended to the ground, letting out a roar that seemed to echo across the entire island.

Naturally, hearing such a roar from above, the pirates immediately notice Set’s presence.

Several men emerged from a house—or perhaps it should be called a shack—built in a spot hidden from view.

Although the shack was built in a spot invisible from the outside of the island, it hadn’t been designed with flying creatures like Set in mind. From where Rei was perched on Set’s back, it was completely visible.

“Well then, I’ll leave the ship to you, Set.”

“Grr!”

Patting Set’s back lightly—partly to encourage him as he let out a growl—Rei jumped down from his back.

Set was in mid-descent, hovering at a height of about fifty meters above the ground.

Under normal circumstances, falling from this height would undoubtedly be fatal.

But… in Rei’s case, it’s different.

He activated the Sleipnir Boots, kicking the air several times mid-fall to slow his descent.

And where she landed was...

“Whoa! You… you bastard! Where the hell did you come from?!”

It was right in front of the pirates who had just emerged from the hut.

The pirate standing right next to Rei was startled by the sudden appearance of a stranger, but given the situation, he must have recognized Rei as an enemy.

He immediately grabbed his weapon and launched an attack before even asking who he was.

That was a natural reaction for a pirate.

Especially since this happened right after Set’s cry just a moment ago—there was no way he could have recognized him as an ally.

“Whoa!”

It wasn’t a battle axe… but rather a blow from a woodcutter’s axe swung at Rei. However, since it was a reflexive strike, it lacked any real sharpness, so there was no way it could hit Rei.

Rei easily dodged the axe blow and immediately swung the Twilight Spear in a horizontal slash.

“Guh!”

It wasn’t a slash with the blade, but a blow delivered with the hilt.

Under normal circumstances, it might have been possible to block it, but the one who delivered that blow was Rei.

There was no way a pirate from such a backwater could block that.

The bandit who took Rei’s blow was sent flying sideways by the force of the impact, colliding with a comrade and coming to a halt.

If his comrade hadn’t been there, he would have flown several meters through the air and ended up rolling around on the ground.

In that sense, the pirate who took Rei’s hit might have been lucky, after all. …Though, of course, being attacked by Rei in the first place is hardly something you could call lucky.

“What!?”

Seeing their comrade get blown away, the other pirates watching let out cries of astonishment.

The man who was blown away wasn’t exactly the strongest among these pirates…

But he wasn’t weak, either.

As pirates, they were naturally accustomed to rough and tumble.

So when a man like that was blown away by someone as small as Rei, the other pirates could only stare in disbelief.

A few of the pirates wondered if he might have just been faking it, but judging by the way he was lying there, that seemed highly unlikely.

“What’s the matter? Just going to stand there and watch? Unfortunately, I don’t have much time. Come at me already, and let me finish you off.”

Those were the taunting words that came from Rei’s mouth.

No, those weren’t just provocative words—they were meant to provoke them.

Words meant to enrage his opponent and prevent them from escaping.

What was troublesome for Rei wasn’t that they were attacking him, but that they might escape from this place.

This deserted island was quite large, and there were plenty of places for people to hide.

If the pirates were to scatter in all directions, it would take time to track them down.

The reason I described finding them here as “time-consuming” rather than “impossible” or “difficult” is likely because Set is here.

With Set’s keen senses, finding the pirates wherever they hide wouldn’t be all that difficult.

……Though, it’s undeniably true that it would take time and be a hassle.

In any case, Rei’s provocation succeeded brilliantly in getting the pirates’ blood boiling.

Axes, clubs… some even attacked with their fists, perhaps because they couldn’t find anything else that could serve as a weapon.

Of course, not everyone had only such makeshift weapons; some carried their own proper weapons used when raiding other ships or villages.

But… trying to overpower Rei with sheer numbers, without any coordination whatsoever, was nothing short of a mistake.

“Woooooooooah!”

“Do you really think you can take me down with attacks like that?”

He dodged a blow from a club and struck back with the hilt of his Death Scythe.

I held back as much as possible to avoid breaking his bones, but... even so, the blow had enough force to send the pirate flying.

Just like the first pirate, the one who was sent flying came to a stop only after crashing into his comrades.

The other pirates also lunged at Rei, but they were beaten down without managing to land even a scratch.

And so, within minutes of Rei landing on the island, every single pirate had lost consciousness and collapsed.

“...Well then.”

Glancing at the unconscious pirates, Rei pulls a rope from his Misty Ring.

He then proceeded to tie up the unconscious pirates’ limbs one by one and secure them to a nearby tree so they couldn’t escape.

The fact that they remained unconscious even after all that was likely a testament to just how powerful Rei’s single strike had been.

Once he had finished tying up the pirates, Rei looked up at the sky as if to say, “I failed…”

Summer was coming to an end, but perhaps because they were by the sea, a summery blue sky stretched out above them.

“Nah, I guess it doesn’t really have anything to do with being by the sea. Though, emotionally, it has a lot to do with it.”

Muttering to himself, he shifted his gaze from the sky to the pirates hanging there.

What Rei saw in that view was mostly a feeling of annoyance.

He’d felt the same way when they’d taken down the bandits around Sabrusta—it took longer to tie them up than to defeat them.

That said, while he’d learned how to tie them up so they couldn’t escape and had gotten faster at it thanks to that experience, Rei wasn’t quite sure if he should be happy about it or not.

“Anyway, on to the next one. First…”

Rei’s gaze fell on the shack from which the pirates had burst out.

Even though they were fighting loudly outside, there was no sign of anyone coming out of the hut.

Given that, there was likely no one else in the hut, and as far as he could tell by sensing for any presence, that was certainly the case.

But there might be someone capable of concealing their presence, or the possibility of some kind of treasure wasn’t entirely out of the question.

Although the chances were slim, he decided to take a look inside just in case.

But all they found inside were things like sake, dried meat, and dried fish—nothing of particular value.

Rei couldn’t bring himself to steal a pirate’s half-drunk liquor or half-eaten dried meat and fish, so he continued peeking inside the hut one after another.

“I thought there might be at least one bandit in there.”

Among the bandits around Sabrusta, there were some who preferred to capture their victims rather than kill them.

Ransom, selling them into slavery, a target for venting frustration, a sexual partner… for various reasons, these captives were also quite useful.

That’s why Rei had thought it wouldn’t be strange to find similar captives on this island, which served as a pirate hideout… but fortunately—or perhaps unfortunately—there were no captives to be found anywhere.

Deciding to ask about that later once the pirates woke up, she set out to search for the other pirates who were likely still on the island.

When viewed from above the island, the only place with a hut was where Rei had just landed, but come to think of it, it was daytime now.

Under normal circumstances, they would undoubtedly be doing some sort of work; in fact, it would be rare for them to be inside a hut at this time of day.

“Well, from what I could see from above, there were actually quite a few of them moving around the island. …Now then, with that in mind, where should I head next?”

He wondered how the people working on the island would react if they heard Set’s cry from above.

Thinking about that...

“Hey! What’s going on with you guys!?”

From a distance—from the spot where they’d just tied the pirates to the tree—that shout reached them, and Rei muttered, “I guess so.”

If they’d assumed Set’s cry meant a monster attack or something, it was only natural for them to think they should head back to their base for the time being.

(Speaking of bases, since they’re pirates, some of them might have headed for the ship… but that’s just too bad for them.)

From what Rei had seen in battle, these pirates—appearance aside—weren’t particularly skilled.

In fact, there were even more skilled bandits around Sabrusta than these pirates.

Judging that such men had no chance of infiltrating the ship Set was guarding... Rei set off toward the direction the voice had come from, intending to hunt down the consciousnesses of the newly gathered pirates.
