"Well, what should I do now?"

Rei murmured, releasing his grip from the World Tree's trunk.

Just seconds ago, the World Tree had been near death, its state almost deserving of being called withered. Yet now, lush green leaves sprouted from it, completely transforming its appearance from mere moments before.

The same was true for the light sphere flickering beside Rei. The light, which had been on the verge of vanishing after being drained of magic and sap by the giant cicada, now shone brilliantly.

That orb of light fluttered happily around Rei, darting about him.

"Calm down. ...Marina said that sudden, drastic recovery isn't good for the body, but it seems fine for now."

At Rei's words, the orb stopped moving and began flashing intensely, signaling it was fine.

Relieved by the orb's state, Rei's gaze turned skyward.

There, a battle between a giant cicada and Set was unfolding, visible even from their current position on the ground.

Flames, ice, and wind blasted forth, painting the sky.

Seeing this, a fleeting thought crossed Rei's mind: If only they had defeated it while it was attached to the World Tree... But without knowing what abilities the giant cicada possessed, charging in without a second thought and giving it their all from the start would be nothing short of foolish.

At the very least, Rei had no intention of being that foolish.

Especially with the World Tree already weakened, such a move could have been fatal.

(The World Tree, the World Tree. It's a bit late now, but setting aside the fact you can't see the World Tree from the outside, how is it that you can see the outside scenery clearly from inside? ...Well, if it were completely cut off from the outside, it might affect the Dark Elves' health, and they probably couldn't farm either.)

Watching the sun in the sky, he pondered such things, then immediately shook his head.

Now was not the time for such thoughts.

Even as I stood here, Set was still battling that giant cicada, and above all...

"It was bad that the barrier's protective field vanished, even temporarily, right?"

As Rei muttered, the orb flickered as if apologetically.

Noticing the orb, Rei gently reached out as if addressing Set... but his hand passed right through it.

"Whoops. ...Well, no need to worry that much. The Dark Elves are doing their best to protect you, right? Plus, there's Elena, Marina, and Vihera too."

Rei stopped speaking there and turned his gaze back to the sky.

There, he could still see numerous attacks being launched.

Facing a giant cicada ten times his size, Set was holding his own almost evenly.

Well, to be precise, it wasn't exactly even—it was more of a stalemate.

The giant cicada, having absorbed Rei's magical power, evaded Set's attacks using its enhanced physical abilities fueled by that magic, or blocked them with magical ultrasonic waves and barrier shields.

Set, seemingly having already anticipated the giant cicada's ultrasonic attacks, evaded them without being hit.

The battle rages on, but Set refuses to retreat even an inch before an opponent vastly larger than himself.

There was no doubt that the settlement remained safe only because of Set's fight. If Rei were to call out to Set now, even slightly, Set's attention would shift toward her. And if he tried to pick her up, the giant cicada would be free to move.

(Leaving the settlement would be fine, but it shouldn't attack the settlement... No, even if it leaves the settlement, letting something like that run wild would be bad.)

In other words, Rei absolutely should not have done anything to draw Set's attention to himself in the current situation.

"Then..."

His gaze shifted, not to the sky, but to the settlement.

The barrier had vanished, at least temporarily, due to the giant cicada's earlier impact.

And since he'd heard reports before arriving at the World Tree that countless monsters were gathering around the settlement, he judged that dealing with that was the priority.

(Besides, there was no guarantee monsters hadn't entered the settlement while the barrier was down.)

The Dark Elves capable of combat were undoubtedly outside the settlement, engaged in battle.

There were no Dark Elves capable of combat left in the settlement now, or if there were, they were extremely few.

In reality, those injured in battle had been brought back to the settlement, so it wasn't that there were no capable fighters at all.

But still, once they were injured, rendered unable to fight, and sent back to the rear settlement, they couldn't be counted on for combat strength.

"So, I'm the only one who can move freely? If the barrier's protective field was still gone, we probably should've dealt with that first..."

His gaze fell upon a light sphere flickering energetically.

Thanks to the massive infusion of Rei's magic power, it was surprisingly vibrant.

(Forcing magic into a dying old man... or was it life force in this case? Anyway, it felt like he'd poured power into it to revive it... If that's the case, then I can understand what Marina meant when she said it wasn't good for the World Tree.)

The orb, seemingly unaware of Rei's thoughts, flickered violently as if enraged.

Driven by the orb's urging, Rei thought for a moment, then kicked off the ground, heading toward the settlement with the Death Scythe in hand.

He decided that right now, action was more important than thought.

As if unwilling to be left behind, the orb flickered and followed after Rei.

Even as he ran along the path leading to the settlement, the sounds of Set and the giant cicada battling above echoed in layers.

To someone listening, it might have sounded like the signal for a festival, but no one in the current situation was thinking about such things.

(Well, I don't know if they have fireworks over here or not. ...But they have magic, and Japanese people like Takumu have visited in the past, so fireworks wouldn't be out of place. Wait, they didn't seem to have udon either, so maybe it's pointless to hope.)

Holding the Death Scythe, he kept running and arrived at the settlement.

Naturally, those capable of fighting had headed outside the barrier's protective circle to defend the settlement as best they could, leaving almost no one inside.

But "almost none" doesn't mean none at all...

"Whoa, whoa! Don't come here! Don't come over here!"

A familiar voice reached his ears, and Rei immediately sprinted toward the sound.

He reached the spot in less than ten seconds. There were nearly ten children there.

Among them were familiar faces like Ian, Karuja, and Myrth. Lugdos, in particular, was swinging the dagger he'd grabbed to protect the other children, holding off the approaching goblins.

Three goblins closed in on the children.

Each carried what could barely be called clubs—mere wooden sticks, or rather, branches broken off and fashioned into makeshift weapons—and they surrounded Lugdos and the others, grinning hideously.

The children outnumbered them, and some among them could use spirit magic or had learned weapon handling.

But... that was merely practice; they had never actually fought in battle.

Moreover, they were alone now. The situation was extraordinary—the World Tree was withering, the barrier was breaking, and monsters were gathering.

Asked if they could fight monsters in such a situation, the answer was no.

Especially since Lugdos had just been struck on the head by a goblin's club in the forest and was injured.

Perhaps traumatized by that incident, Lugdos's hand trembled with fear as he gripped his dagger.

"Dammit, don't come any closer! I said don't come any closer! If you come any nearer, I won't let you off easy!"

Even so, as the leader of the pack, Lugdos desperately put on a brave front, protecting the younger children while trying to hold off the goblins.

"Gyaa! Gyaa! Gyaa!"

"Gya-gya-gya!"

"Gya-gya!"

The goblins seemed to see right through Lugdos' bluff. Each one laughed while grabbing wooden sticks...

"That's enough!"

The moment those words echoed around them, a sudden gust of wind seemed to blow, and all three goblins were cleaved clean in two, upper and lower halves severed.

"Huh? What?"

Seeing the goblins die the instant the words were uttered, Lugdos looked around in confusion, unable to comprehend what had happened.

At the end of his gaze stood a man wielding a scythe larger than himself.

Lugdos, who lived in this settlement, naturally knew who that was.

The adventurer who had come to heal the World Tree.

And for Lugdos, though he'd never say it aloud, a formidable romantic rival—yet also the benefactor who had saved him last night after he'd lost consciousness in the forest.

(I'll never let Marina-chan fall into his hands!)

The older woman who had stolen his heart at first sight. Muttering her name silently, Lugdos opened his mouth to speak...

"Big bro! Big bro!"

From among the children, a particularly small one began running toward Rei.

"Hey, wait, Myrth!"

"Thank you, Rei-san."

"Thanks, Rei-nii!"

Following Myrs, who had dashed off first, Karuja and Ian also broke away from the group and started running.

These three had been saved by Rei in the forest last night and had witnessed his power firsthand.

Myrth especially looked up to Rei, who had saved him. It was hard to believe that just moments ago he'd been on the verge of tears facing the goblins, now he wore a bright smile.

"Are you okay?"

Rei placed a hand on Myrs' head, as if to calm him.

Realizing Rei wasn't someone to fear, the other children all ran over to him too.

"Hey, hey, big brother, you're so strong!"

"How did you defeat him just now? That wasn't a move any ordinary human could pull off..."

"You idiot. This guy fought and beat the Elder! There's no way he's just a normal human being!"

"Oh, come to think of it, Mom said this guy Rei is human, but not really human."

"...? Human? Or not?"

Watching them each talk freely, Rei gently placed a hand on each Dark Elf child's head before heading over to Lugdos, who stood a little ways away.

"This is the first time we've met while you're fully conscious. It feels strange to say this, but it's been a day since yesterday."

"...Hmph!"

Caught looking foolish in front of his love rival. Unable to bear it, Lugdos turned his face away from Rei.

"What? Did I do something to make you dislike me?"

"Nothing."

Her words said one thing, but her attitude screamed dissatisfaction with Rei.

(Why does he hate me so much? This is actually the first time I've talked to him, right? He was unconscious yesterday.)

Unaware that his relationship with Marina was being questioned, Rei could only feel puzzled by Lugdos's attitude.

Still, staying here was dangerous, and he couldn't just leave the children behind.

"Anyway, staying here risks more monsters showing up. The barrier's active now, but there's a real chance monsters slipped in when it wasn't up earlier. We were actually in danger."

The children gasped softly as Rei muttered this, glancing at the goblin whose torso had been cleaved in two.

They knew all too well what would have happened to them if Rei hadn't come this time.

"Why the hell were you guys out wandering around at a time like this? Shouldn't you have been hiding at home or somewhere?"

"Hmph! W-we weren't just playing around! Look!"

What Rugdos pulled out of the bag he was carrying were several potions.

And they weren't low-quality ones either—the engravings on the containers made it clear these were high-grade stuff.

"...Why you guys? Weren't the adults with you?"

"Everyone's busy, so we came to get them!"

"Even so, it seems like a few adults could have come along too."

Hearing Rei's muttered words, Lugdos quietly averted his gaze.

That alone made Rei understand. ...He had figured it out.

That Lugdos and the others hadn't been sent by the adults to get the potion, but had come on their own initiative.

"You guys... coming out at a busy time like this..."

It wasn't that they were bored and decided to go out. Rei understood that they had taken this approach because they wanted to help the adults in any way they could.

Sure, there might have been some boredom from being cooped up for so long, but at its core, it was the desire to help the adults... to help the injured people of the village.

Even so, the actions taken by the Lugdos were by no means commendable.

Just as he opened his mouth to speak to Lugdos... a shout of anger, not Rei's, echoed through the area.

"Found you, you lot! What are you doing in a place like this?! ...Ah, you..."

The dark elf woman's eyes widened in surprise, clearly not expecting Rei to be with the children.

Seeing this, Rei decided he didn't need to scold her himself and spoke up.

"Well then, sorry, but I'll leave the kids to you. I still have things I need to do."

"Huh? Oh, yes. Understood."

Caught off guard by Rei's abrupt words, the woman could only respond that way.

Leaving her standing there, Rei took his Death Scythe and walked away.
