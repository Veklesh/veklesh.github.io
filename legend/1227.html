"Haa, haa, haa, haa..."

"Gasp, gasp, gasp, gasp..."

Only the sound of such ragged breathing echoed throughout the room.

The sounds came from Rei, who had released the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, as well as Elk and Ara. Similarly, Elena, holding Mirage in a distant location, was also breathing heavily due to the extreme use of magic.

Marina and Min could only stare speechlessly at the Silver Lion collapsed on the floor.

A deathmatch. It was a battle truly worthy of that name. Even for Min, who had fought countless powerful foes as part of a Rank A party, and Marina, who had endured countless battles during her long adventuring career, a battle this fierce was a first in their lives.

And yet, they had defeated a Rank S monster.

The moment they realized this, joy welled up inside them... explosive joy, even.

This might allow them to restore the consciousness of Vihera and Rodos.

That thought was undoubtedly true.

"Haa..."

Rei's breathing finally calmed as he leaned against the corpse of the silver lion he'd just defeated, glancing around.

Behind him stood a silver form.

Given the Silver Lion's massive six-meter frame, its body pressed against the wall behind him felt like a wall itself to Rei.

Shifting his gaze slightly, the next thing that came into view was the silver lion's face.

The handle of the Death Scythe extended from its mouth, and the handle of the Twilight Spear extended from its eyeball.

That enormous face... Could it still be alive? That thought lingered within Rei.

But blood tears flowed from the eye pierced by the Twilight Spear and the other, unharmed eye, and its tongue hung limp from its mouth, dangling onto the floor.

Seeing that, it was clear beyond doubt that the Silver Lion was dead.

"Ow!"

Seeing the head, Rei finally accepted that the Silver Lion was dead. But that realization must have broken his concentration.

Simultaneously, pain surged through his entire body.

Furthermore, his face and head bore numerous wounds. Blood flowing from his forehead poured into his right eye, staining his vision crimson red.

"Grrrrrr..."

Concerned by Rei's cry of pain, Set leaned closer, asking, "Are you okay?" But Set himself was no better off... no, compared to Rei, who wore the Dragon Robe, Set's body bore multiple slash wounds, even more than Rei's.

The final action the Silver Lion had taken, using its tail to create what could be called a barrier.

The injuries he sustained were from charging into that barrier to save Rei.

Despite bearing more wounds and bleeding more than Rei, Set growled softly, watching Rei with concern.

"I'm fine. Look, I even defeated the Silver Lion. ...But honestly, this is the first time I've been injured this badly in battle."

Rei had experienced countless battles before.

The sheer density of combat he'd faced was something beyond what ordinary adventurers experienced.

Even so, it was rare for Rei to be injured in those battles.

His most severe injury came during the civil war in the Bestir Empire, in his battle against the Rank S adventurer, Immovable Noiz.

(An S-rank adventurer versus an S-rank monster... If Noiz had fought me seriously back then, would I have ended up with injuries like these? No, this victory only came because of the support of many others. What would have happened if he'd fought me for real?)

Enduring the pain of his wounds, Rei turned his gaze toward the Silver Lion, but his thoughts diverged slightly.

Even though they're lumped together under the S-Rank category, technically, it's all beings above Rank A that are grouped as S-Rank.

This applied to both adventurers and monsters. A weaker Rank S monster might be defeated by a Rank A adventurer, while a powerful Rank S monster could withstand a hundred Rank A adventurers.

Even among S-rank monsters, there was that much difference.

Furthermore, the Silver Lion was a monster renowned for its defensive prowess due to its thick coat, making it no surprise that defeating it proved difficult.

It was particularly ill-suited to Rei's preferred attack methods, like slashing and stabbing, as most of them were rendered ineffective.

Still, it was fortunate for Rei and the others that its attack power wasn't particularly high, despite its defensive prowess. ...Even so, Rei had sustained injuries that could be called the worst he'd suffered since arriving in Elgin.

"Rei, are you okay!?"

"Rei, are you okay!?"

Elena and Marina ran over to Rei, who was leaning against the Silver Lion's corpse.

Seeing Rei's face badly swollen, blood flowing from several wounds, and one eye completely covered in blood, they panicked and rushed out of the room.

Grabbing the potion left there...

"Elena? Mari... Guildmaster too?"

A sudden voice called out.

Of course, Elena and the others understood they were in a dungeon, but they were so focused on treating Rei as quickly as possible that they hadn't noticed the source of the voice.

Turning toward the source of the voice, she saw one of Johanna's companions standing there.

"Why are you here?"

"No, I came to check what happened because I stopped hearing the Silver Lion's voice that I'd been hearing until just now."

The man replied briefly to Marina's words.

To be honest, he absolutely didn't want to go anywhere near a place where an S-rank monster might be lurking.

Still, he judged that if the Silver Lion had been defeated, it would be best to bring Vihera and Rodos here quickly, which was why he had come to check.

Understanding the man's feelings... and likely Johanna's intentions in sending him, Elena and Marina both let out a slight sigh of relief.

Simultaneously, seeing the blood-soaked Rei, they calmed from their state of confusion. They exchanged glances and nodded to each other before Marina spoke to the man.

"Yes, we worried you. But please rest assured. We've already defeated the Silver Lion."

"Huh!? ...I knew it. Seeing the two of you out here, I figured as much. And there's that corpse over there too. ...Still, really... Rei, you're something else."

The shock must have been immense.

He didn't even notice he was referring to Rei as "Captain" again like before, staring blankly toward the entrance of the room where the Silver Lion must have been.

"But quite a few people got injured. We're treating them now. Could you bring Vihera and Rodos over while we're busy?"

"Huh? Oh, yes! I'll bring them right away!"

Nodding at Marina's words, the man hurried back the way he came.

Watching his retreating figure, Elena and Marina returned to the room, each holding a potent potion.

"It feels odd to say this after coming to get potions, but now that I think about it, couldn't we have just used the potions in Rei's item box?"

"I guess that just shows how flustered we were. Anyway, let's get back to Rei. Since we're already here, we should take the potions with us."

Leaving something as incredibly valuable as a World Tree Potion lying out in the open was unsettling for Marina, too.

Of course, she didn't think Johanna and the others would steal it, but she worried that someone else... some adventurer who had come to this lowest level for whatever reason might see those potions lying on the floor and take them as a stroke of luck.

(In that sense, it was probably good we could retrieve the potions right after the battle ended.)

With these thoughts in mind, she entered the room where the Silver Lion lay.

And once again, she was struck by the Silver Lion's sheer size.

Even compared to Set, the largest among them, its size was clearly different.

The sight of Rei leaning against the Silver Lion's body could only be described as small in contrast to its massive frame.

(Even so... without Rei, defeating the Silver Lion would have been nearly impossible. That much is true.)

Their support, provided by Elk, Ara, and the others, was undoubtedly an effective force in defeating the Silver Lion.

But... if asked whether they could have defeated the Silver Lion without Rei and Set... Marina would shake her head.

In truth, it was Rei who ultimately took the Silver Lion's life at the very last moment.

The Silver Lion possessed fur with formidable defense against both magic and physical attacks, but its eyeballs and the inside of its mouth didn't possess defense as strong as its fur.

...Even so, it possessed enough defense to remain completely unharmed even when Rei's Deep Flame adhered to its face.

In any case, shoving a weapon into the Silver Lion's mouth like that was something no ordinary person could do.

Perhaps Elk could have managed it, but unfortunately, the Axe of the Thunder God would have only scorched and sliced the Silver Lion's mouth slightly.

It was precisely because it was the Death Scythe—a massive weapon with a two-meter shaft and a blade nearly one meter long—and precisely because the Twilight Spear had pierced its eyeball that the Silver Lion's brain could be destroyed.

Not many possess a weapon with a shaft that long.

(Ah, but perhaps Elena could have managed it?)

Recalling the astonishing power Mirage displayed late in the battle, Marina smiled.

If the Mirage's blade had become whip-like... and if it could deliver a blow with that much power, perhaps Elena could have defeated the Silver Lion.

Marina glanced briefly at Elena beside her, but decided that treating Rei's injuries was the priority for now.

"Rei, um... are you okay?"

"Ah... well, it's not as bad as it looks. I mean, it hurts, of course. But I'm not going to die right away, so I'll be fine."

His face was covered in his own blood... and above all, the Silver Lion's blood, staining it bright red.

Anyone seeing Rei now, unaware of the situation, would likely faint on the spot.

"First, even if we use a potion, we need to wash the blood off your face. ...Can you get the Running Water Dagger?"

"Yeah."

True to his words, Rei's wounds weren't as bad as they looked.

At the very least, he could still move his body. Taking the Waterfall Dagger from the Misty Ring, he began to wash the blood from his face.

...Though, a dull ache definitely shot through his body when she did, and he did slightly frown.

Marina channeled magic into the Water-Flowing Dagger she'd drawn, creating water to wet the cloth Elena had produced, then began wiping Rei's face.

Most of the blood covering Rei's face wiped away easily with the damp cloth.

This was likely because not much time had passed since defeating the Silver Lion.

But at the same time, even after wiping the blood away, fresh blood flowed from the wounds.

The wound on his forehead, in particular, was quite deep, showing no sign of stopping.

"That's a wound from the Silver Lion's fangs,"

Rei murmured thoughtfully. One wrong move, and it could have been his skull crushed or his eyeball gouged out by those fangs.

If things had gone badly... no, even without things going badly, it could have been a life-threatening wound.

"Honestly... always doing such reckless things..."

Muttering thoughtfully, Elena gently wiped the wound and applied a potion.

Fortunately, though the wound itself was fairly deep, it healed without leaving any particular scar.

A cheap potion might have left a scar, but what Elena used now wasn't quite a World Tree Potion... but it was a fairly high-grade potion nonetheless.

She wiped the wounds on his face, applying potion to each one and making them disappear.

Fortunately, most of the wounds on her face were caused by the Silver Lion's tail and weren't particularly deep.

Elena and Marina both breathed a sigh of relief that no scars remained on Rei's face.

The potions the two had brought were used elsewhere too.

Ara applied it to Set, and Min to Elk, and so on.

Ara, who was using the potion on Set, had sustained some injuries herself. Fortunately, or perhaps not so fortunately, she had only joined the battle later on.

Thanks to that, she wasn't as badly injured as the others,

though she certainly wasn't unscathed. Even so, a light application of the potion healed most of her wounds.

"Grrrrrrr"

After Rei, the most severely injured was undoubtedly Set.

His large body meant he bore multiple wounds from the Silver Lion's tail attacks.

Still, the tail's attacks were essentially whip-like strikes, not deep wounds.

Though Set bore such wounds all over his body, he showed no particular sign of suffering.

This resilience was likely due to being a high-rank monster like a griffin.

Even in that state, it seemed he still found it pleasurable to have potions applied and his wounds heal.

Set purred happily...
