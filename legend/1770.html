After finishing his conversation at the guard post, Rei stepped outside.

Incidentally, the elderly woman who was the victim in this incident had already finished giving her statement and had long since left the guardhouse.

The guard likely chose to put off speaking with Rei because he thought giving her some time would help her cool down.

At Gilm—Rei’s base of operations, known by the nickname “Crimson”—there have been a series of crimes committed by people wrapped in red cloth… specifically, crimes like robbery and theft.

Seeing such people, there was no way Rei wouldn’t feel resentful.

The guards understood that sentiment, but they likely anticipated that if they let an enraged Rei go out into the streets, he would immediately set out searching for those with red cloth wrapped around their arms.

In fact, if Rei had left the guardhouse while still agitated, it couldn’t be ruled out that he might have taken such action.

However, thanks to the guard’s judgment, Rei’s irritation had subsided, if only slightly.

“Grr?”

Set noticed Rei coming out of the guardhouse and purred happily.

Surrounding Set were several people of all ages and genders, each petting him or feeding him, showering him with affection.

When Set stood up upon seeing Rei arrive, those people didn’t try to stop him any further, letting him do as he pleased.

...Though a few of them looked a bit disappointed.

“Anyway, let’s take a walk around town for a bit. I saw that guy with the red cloth for the first time today. Maybe I’ve just been overlooking him all this time.”

“Grr!”

Set purred happily at Rei’s words.

Set knows that the reason they’re walking through town is to look for those people with red cloth wrapped around their arms.

But for Set, being with Rei is the most fun thing of all.

Of course, he also loves being doted on by all sorts of people, just like he was a moment ago.

Anyway, that’s why Rei and Set were just wandering around town…

“Huh.”

Spotting a food stall selling steamed buns, Rei let out a sound of admiration.

That stall was the very one Rei had gotten involved with a little while ago.

To be fair, the ones who had given advice to that stall—which sold meat buns that were neither particularly good nor bad—were the people who had been using the training ground nearby at the time.

Although it was the same stall selling those meat buns, it was no longer near the training ground; instead, it was situated on a busy stretch of the main street.

Rei had heard that for a while, the stalls’ locations were determined by popularity or sales volume, but looking at its current spot, it was clear that the stall’s sales were going well.

In fact, the fact that there were a few customers in line was proof of that.

“Is this really goblin meat?! I tried it back when I first became an adventurer, but it was totally inedible!”

“Yes, it really is goblin meat. We get goblin meat that’s been specially processed by Percy’s Tool Shop, and that’s what we use.”

“...So does that mean goblin meat is actually edible now?”

“Um, I’m afraid that’s not going to happen. I heard they’ve managed to mass-produce this goblin meat to some extent, but apparently, they can’t quite do that yet.”

“Is that so? That’s a shame. I thought if goblin meat could be this tasty, it would really help out new, broke adventurers.”

He hears such comments, but Rei believes it will be a long time before goblin meat becomes as widespread as the man—who appears to be an adventurer—suggests.

To make goblin meat edible—or, more accurately, to make it taste good—several ingredients are needed, with Gamelion’s innards being the main one.

Of those, the biggest problem is, of course, the Gamelion’s innards.

A monster that only appears in the fall is, by definition, that much more precious.

If this were a monster that appeared frequently, like goblins, then they could mass-produce delicious goblin meat.

“He seems busy for now, so I guess I don’t need to say hello.”

“Grr…”

At Rei’s words, Set growled softly, sounding a little disappointed.

To be honest, Set probably just wanted to eat a little of the meat bun.

Unlike the meat buns from before, they’re not exactly “top-notch,” but the fact that they’ve reached a level where you can say they’re reasonably tasty is a big deal.

"We’ll save the meat buns for another time."

Rei said that, but the time was drawing near for him to head to the Duchy of Kerebel with Elena.

Naturally, that meant he wouldn’t be able to eat meat buns for a while… but he figured he could always have some once he returned to Gilm after the New Year.

In Rei’s mind, meat buns were something to be eaten during the cold season—specifically, in autumn and winter—and that perception had spread along with the meat buns themselves.

Of course, Rei had no particular objection to selling meat buns in spring or summer, but out of gratitude toward him for popularizing them, that was how things had come to be.

However, that was strictly true only in Gilm, where Rei was based; if the dish known as the meat bun were to spread to other villages, towns, and cities, it wouldn’t be strange for shops to start selling them even in spring or summer.

Even so, Rei had no intention of voicing any complaints.

After all, eating meat buns in cold weather was simply a matter of Rei’s personal preference.

In fact, if you went to a place like Chinatown, they sold meat buns year-round, and it wasn’t uncommon to see people buying them.

...Though, as Rei recalled seeing on TV before, sales do tend to drop on days when the temperature exceeds 30°C.

“Well, let’s head somewhere else. I need to find those idiots quickly. If they keep causing all this nonsense, it’s going to be a hassle for us.”

Seeing how busy the steamed bun stall was, Rei felt a little calmer.

He still couldn’t forgive those who were still ostentatiously wrapping red cloth around their arms, but he no longer felt the urge to “kill on sight” or engage in a “search and destroy” mission.

That said, he had absolutely no intention of letting them off the hook if he found them.

Still, while she’d originally planned to kill about eighty percent of them, she’d calmed down enough to think she’d let them off with just a beating.

“Oh, isn’t that Rei? What’s up?”

As I walked through the town, someone called out to me like that.

Looking in the direction of the voice, I saw Vihera, Byune, and a few others who looked like adventurers.

“I’m looking for something… no, someone, actually. By the way, are you on patrol?”

“Yes. It seems all sorts of commotions just keep happening.”

“...Could those disturbances have anything to do with that guy who wraps a red cloth around his arm?”

“A red cloth?”

At Rei’s words, Vihera seemed to recall something… and eventually nodded in response.

“Come to think of it, there was a man like that during the fight. Why do you ask?”

Rei felt a sense of unease at her unexpectedly casual response.

That was because there was no trace of dissatisfaction in Vihera’s tone.

If she had actually seen a man with a red cloth committing a snatch-and-run robbery, as Rei had, she would never have spoken in such a tone.

Vihera loves a good fight, but she could never bring herself to like someone who uses force to take things from weaker opponents.

That was the source of his unease.

“A fight, huh? What was that man in the red cloth like?”

“Well, what do you mean…? When we told him to stop fighting, he stopped right away. He even apologized, saying he was sorry for causing us trouble.”

“...The man in the red cloth, you say?”

“Yes.”

Vihera nodded simply at Rei’s words.

Seeing that, Rei felt a new doubt arise.

There was no way this could be the same opponent that Rei—or, more accurately, Set—had defeated.

(So… did the guy know Vihera was my ally and apologize right away?)

It is widely known in Gilm that Rei and Vihera are in a party together.

And Vihera, a stunning beauty clad in the sheer garments typically worn by prostitutes and dancers, is instantly recognizable as Vihera.

Thinking about it that way, it’s entirely plausible that they recognized Vihera as Rei’s companion, apologized sincerely, and tried to avoid drawing attention to themselves.

(If that’s the case, does that mean that red cloth was really meant for me? …But why? Well, I’m not saying I haven’t earned anyone’s grudge…)

Far from having no enemies, considering everything Rei has done so far, there are surely countless people who hate him.

He’s used force without hesitation even against nobles, killed many bandits, sold people off as criminal slaves, and in the war, countless lives were lost at the hands of Rei and Set.

There are also those who hold a grudge against Rei for various other reasons.

To those people, Rei is someone they could hate as much as they want and still not hate enough.

That said, having experienced firsthand what happens to those who rashly lay a hand on Rei, there aren’t many who would dare to do such a thing.

“What’s wrong?”

“Well… I was just a little concerned about that guy wrapped in red cloth.”

Rei answered Vihera’s question and explained how the old woman had nearly been mugged and how Set had stopped it.

“I see. Since he went out of his way to wrap a red cloth around his arm, there’s no way the guy he was fighting and the guy Set beat up are completely unrelated.”

“Hmm.”

It wasn’t Rei who agreed with Vihera’s words… but Byune.

Rei was quite surprised that Byune would assert herself here of all places.

No, it wasn’t just Rei. The other adventurers traveling with Vihera and Byune were also staring at Byune in surprise.

“Um… why did you agree with Vihera just now?”

As if pushed by the expectant gazes of the others, Rei asked Vihera.

Rei had known Byune for quite some time now, but even so, he couldn’t pretend to understand what Byune was trying to say as well as Vihera could.

...Still, he has come to understand her to a certain extent—really, to a certain extent.

“Well, I suppose it’s only natural since there’s someone wrapped in a red cloth.”

“I see. So you had the same impression as me. …Anyway, if anyone finds that red cloth, let me know.”

He decides that there’s no point in trying to figure out Byune’s reasoning here, so he chooses not to think about it any further.

Normally, I wouldn’t help but think that I should be able to understand Byune’s words and actions a little better by now… but unfortunately, there are things I need to do before I can think about that.

Perhaps Vihera understood how Rei felt, because she didn’t try to force the conversation to continue.

“Sure, I’ll let you know as soon as I find it. …But if the person wrapped in that red cloth is causing trouble, we’ll have to arrest them ourselves, okay?”

“That’s fine by me. …Oh.”

After responding to Vihera’s words, Rei suddenly realized something.

He realized he didn’t need to go searching for someone wrapped in a red cloth like this; he could have just asked that man—the one who tried to snatch the old woman’s bag and got knocked out by Set—for information.

The fact that Rei hadn’t realized something so simple must mean that the blood had really gone to his head.

In his heated state, he’d completely forgotten about it, and even after he’d calmed down somewhat at the station, he still couldn’t recall it.

“What’s wrong?”

“Nothing. I guess I was more worked up than I realized. I thought I’d be able to get information on the red-cloth gang much more easily than this.”

“I see. …But be careful, okay?”

At first, Rei thought she was saying “be careful” as a joke or something, but judging by Vihera’s expression, it was anything but a joke—she looked completely serious.

“Do you really think I’d lose to a bunch of nobodies like that?”

“I don’t. But they probably feel the same way, right? They surely don’t think they can win by fighting you head-on. Yet they’re blatantly provoking Rei. Doesn’t that make it reasonable to assume they’re up to something?”

“……Now that you mention it, that’s a possibility.”

Anyone even slightly familiar with the situation wouldn’t think of fighting Rei head-on, just as Vihera said.

Yet the fact that they’re acting this way means there’s definitely some kind of plan behind it.

“You’re right. We’d better be careful.”

Saying that, Rei expressed his gratitude to Vihera.
