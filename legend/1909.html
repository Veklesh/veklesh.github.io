At the mention of wanting information about Rei, Suu-chi speaks up.

“So, what kind of information do you want? I don’t know all that much, you know. I’m not exactly an informant or anything.”

“I figured. I wasn’t expecting much in that regard anyway.”

At the mention of “not expecting much,” Suu-chi’s expression turns sour.

From Suu-chi’s perspective, it’s one thing to say it herself, but being told by someone else that they aren’t expecting much from her is probably not very pleasant.

However, even from Rei’s perspective, Suu-chi was a complete amateur when it came to combat, and yet here she was attempting the reckless feat of trying to defeat a kobold—she certainly didn’t strike him as the type to be an informant.

Still, the reason Rei approached Suu-chi was that, since she was from the slums, he figured she might have some information that could only be obtained there.

Since the slums were a place Rei rarely visited, it wouldn’t be strange for Suu-chi, who lived there, to have some kind of information.

Also, the fact that Rei didn’t care if the kobold’s corpse was left lying there was the reason he could casually ask Suu-chi for information like this.

“Well, I don’t think you need to go out of your way to ask me…”

Even as she said that, perhaps thinking it was a good deal to get two kobold corpses—originally three, but one had been pierced through the chest by a spear thrown by Rei—from which she could strip the magic stones, Suu-chi opened her mouth, though she looked displeased.

“So, what kind of information do you want, exactly? I can’t tell you anything unless I know specifically what you’re looking for.”

“Right. …First off, there’s this massive outbreak of kobolds. Do you have any idea why that might be? Specifically, are they using some kind of magic item or something?”

Naturally, that was the first thing he asked.

After all, Rei was operating under the assumption that there was a magical item controlling the kobolds, so that was only natural.

……She herself wasn’t entirely sure if such a magic item even existed, but once she’d made up her mind, she wanted to be thorough about it.

But… naturally, Suu-chi shook her head at Rei’s question.

“How would I know? Besides, if I knew there was a magic item like that, I’d sell it to someone.”

Given her financial struggles, Su-chi’s attitude was only natural.

Magic items… even if they’re only of kobold-level quality, if there were items capable of endlessly gathering resources or launching raids like this, it would be only natural for anyone of a certain standing to want them.

With a magic item like that, there would be countless ways to use it.

The first possibility that comes to mind would be to have kobolds raid the territories of rival nobles.

Since they could inflict damage on a rival noble’s territory without having to do anything themselves, it’s only natural that they would want one.

There are various other uses as well.

……Given how rare such a magic item is, it’s likely that attempting to sell it carelessly would be dangerous.

“I figured as much. But I heard some information in the slums… though I suppose it’s probably just a rumor.”

“Rumors? Hmm…”

Since he wasn’t looking for detailed information but just a rumor or something, he began to think if there was anything.

Fortunately, there were currently many people dismantling kobolds in the slums.

Since they were working in large groups, naturally many people were chatting, and rumors frequently came up in conversation.

Suu-chi, too, had naturally joined in such work at first to earn even a little money to feed the children she lived with.

She tried to recall if she’d heard anything back then, but nothing immediately came to mind.

Well, to be precise, there were plenty of rumors that seemed relevant, but the question was whether it was safe to even mention them.

“When it comes to rumors about magic items, there’s a mix of truth and fiction—there are plenty of them, but…”

If this were an ordinary village or town, there probably wouldn’t be that many rumors.

But this is Gilm. It’s a town on the very frontier, where many rare materials for magic items can be found and harvested.

Given that, it’s only natural that all sorts of rumors about magic items—a real mixed bag, so to speak—would be circulating.

“Well then, for starters… let’s see, are there any rumors about magic items that summon monsters or anything like that?”

That’s where the conversation inevitably led, but in response to Rei’s question, Suu-chi shook her head.

“I haven’t heard anything like that.”

“Then… what about someone who possesses that kind of skill?”

Rei, for his part, had hoped this particular incident involved a magic item.

However, since there were no leads on magic items here either, he decided he needed to look into skills as well.

...To be honest, if a magic item was out of the question, he really hoped it would be a rare or high-tier species.

If it wasn’t a magic item, there was a possibility that by defeating such a rare or high-tier species, obtaining a magic stone, and using Beast Magic, he could acquire the skill.

“Hmm, even if you say ‘control,’ what exactly do I have to do to control a kobold? If we’re talking about monsters in general, maybe… just maybe it’s possible, but specifically a kobold? Besides, if something like that were possible, wouldn’t it be a skill to control other monsters instead?”

That was a fact she couldn’t deny.

Take orcs, for example.

Their materials and magic stones sell for a decent price, and their meat is quite delicious.

If there were a skill that allowed one to freely control orcs, that person would never have to worry about meat for the rest of their life, and they could even sell the meat to make money.

Or, even beyond orcs, there are plenty of monsters more useful than kobolds.

“Ah… well, then. You know, around the time before winter, or right after winter started, there were these guys called the ‘Red Cloth’ running wild—did you know about that?”

“Of course I know about them. For a while, they were even causing trouble in the slums.”

“...For a while? So, did they stop causing trouble right away?”

What Su-chi said came as a surprise even to Rei.

To begin with, the Red Cloth gang were a particularly nasty bunch, and they had caused quite a few disturbances.

For a group like that, why did they stop causing trouble so quickly in the slums—where they could move about freely—rather than on the main streets, which were teeming with adventurers and guards?

To Rei, it was nothing short of a mystery.

But Suu-chi continued as if it were only natural.

“On the main streets, there are strong adventurers and guards, and if they catch you doing something bad, you’ll get arrested and punished—but that’s all according to the established laws, right? But in the slums, of course, there are no laws or anything like that…”

Saying this, Suu-chi seemed to recall that incident, her expression turning slightly sour as she continued.

“Those Red Cloth guys messed with a few organizations in the underworld. As a result, that idiot was killed in a pretty gruesome way as an example to others.”

“Ah… I see. You don’t need to say any more.”

In other words, they’d learned the hard way—through the death of one of their own—that they were no match for their opponents.

I concluded that, as a result, they must have stopped messing with the slums.

In reality, even if they intended it as a harmless prank, there are many cases where the other side doesn’t see it that way.

That’s probably exactly what happened with Akabu.

Since they hadn’t faced severe punishment when caught on the main streets, they probably went into the slums thinking they could get away with it… and ended up being made an example of.

“Well, if guys like that ever clash with people who are truly living on the edge of death… they probably won’t try anything like that again. No, actually, it’s surprising they kept acting that way on the main street even after going through something like that. …Hm?”

Perhaps something in his own words struck a nerve, because Rei paused there and fell deep in thought.

(If the Red Cloth guys were that much of a bunch of cowards, would they really be able to pull off the same stunt on the main street after one of their own was killed? No, since there were so many of them, it wouldn’t be strange if a few could do it… but there shouldn’t have been that many. If that’s the case… they couldn’t stop it? Why? Were they being ordered by someone?)

But if that were the case, it would raise the question of why they were given such an order in the first place.

“What’s wrong? Anyway, if you don’t have any more questions, I suppose I can keep that kobold’s corpse as payment for the information, right?”

“Ah… yeah. Go ahead. I don’t have any particular objections.”

He felt like he was on the verge of figuring something out, but just couldn’t quite get there. Feeling that frustration, Rei replied to Suu-chi.

Seeing Rei like that, Suu-chi looked as if she wanted to say something, but since she’d already received permission to keep the kobold’s corpse, she decided to prioritize that for now.

If asked which to prioritize—her own curiosity or food for the children—it was undoubtedly the latter.

She drew a knife—also slightly rusty—from the sheath at her waist, slit open the kobold’s chest, and extracted the magic stone.

This magic stone was the most valuable material among monsters, and it was precisely to obtain it that Suu-chi had pushed herself to come here.

Therefore, letting it slip away is not an option.

“Huh.”

Rei was slightly impressed… and felt a touch of jealousy and envy, because the way Suu-chi extracted the magic stone from the kobold was more skilled than even a mediocre adventurer.

That said, there are probably plenty of people in the slums today who can do the same thing as Suu-chi.

After all, she’s been dissecting hundreds of kobolds almost every day, so it was only natural she’d become accustomed to it, whether she liked it or not.

...But since those skills were gained solely from repeatedly butchering large numbers of kobolds, they were essentially limited to kobolds—she could only handle them that smoothly.

“...What?”

Perhaps noticing Rei’s gaze fixed on her, Suu-chi asked with a puzzled look.

Since she knew enough about Rei from rumors, she never expected him to do something like steal the magic stones stripped from the kobold carcass she’d given him. Still, having him stare intently at her while she was butchering wasn’t exactly pleasant.

Precisely because she knew the rumors about Rei, she wasn’t particularly frightened even when she saw Set watching the surroundings from nearby.

When she’d left the slums on errands, she’d seen from a distance on several occasions various people—adventurers, merchants, residents of Gilm, men and women of all ages—admiring Set… and that, in this case, was likely a major factor.

“No, I just thought you were pretty good at butchering.”

“Of course. How many kobolds do you think I’ve butchered this winter? I’m not quite at the point where I can do it with my eyes closed… but I’m pretty used to it by now.”

As if to prove those words were no lie, she continued butchering even as she spoke with Rei.

She checked her hands a few times, but there was nothing clumsy about her movements.

“I’ve butchered quite a few monsters myself, but I couldn’t pull off something like that with such ease.”

Rei’s butchering skills have improved dramatically since he first arrived in this world. They have improved, but… even so, Rei still couldn’t exactly say he was good at butchering monsters.

He could do it at an average level, but that was all he could manage.

From Rei’s perspective, Su-chi’s dissection skills were… Just as he was thinking that, an idea suddenly struck him.

“I’ll put in a good word for you. Why don’t you try helping with the Giganto Turtle dissection?”

“……Huh?”

What on earth was he talking about, out of the blue?
