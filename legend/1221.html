After using the Seed of Admonition, Rei retrieved a Paired Orb from the Misty Ring.

In front of Johanna and the others, who were puzzled that they showed no change despite the magic being used, he channeled magic into the Paired Orb.

Immediately, the Paired Orb reflected Grim's form... a skeleton appeared.

"Hmm, Rei. You seem to have arrived rather quickly."

"Is that so? The other adventurers are already deep inside the dungeon."

Even as she said this, she understood that from Grimm's perspective on time, it probably wasn't particularly strange.

As she spoke with Grimm, she noticed an odd silence behind her. Turning her gaze, she saw Johanna and the others frozen in place.

(What's going on?)

Rei noticed Johanna and the others looking puzzled, but decided it wasn't worth worrying about right now. He turned his attention back to Grim, reflected in the Paired Orb, and spoke.

"For now, I want to head to the lowest level. Can you prepare the transfer?"

'Understood. We'll be right there.'

"...Go?"

The moment he murmured that in response to Grimm's words, a powerful presence abruptly materialized behind him.

"Huh!?"

Rei gasped, turned around, and saw a skeleton clad in robes, holding a staff, and wearing a crown... Grimm, or rather, the figure that should be called a Lich Lord.

"Fofofo. The Paired Orb aside, when was the last time I stepped directly before humans like this?"

Grim laughed cheerfully, but the impact his presence had on those around him was immense.

Only Rei and Elena, who had met Grimm before, appeared calm.

In a similar sense, there was also Ara, but she was so overwhelmed by the difference in power... no, presence between herself and Grimm that she couldn't even utter a word, let alone move.

True to their rank as A-rank adventurers, Elk reached for the Axe of the Thunder God on his back, and Min pointed her staff.

Marina also nocked an arrow on her bow, ready to fire at any moment.

But... even those who had taken up a combat stance against Grimm understood that if it actually came to a fight, they would have almost no chance of winning.

Even so... even so, as if urging himself on, he steeled his resolve, refusing to accept death here...

"Enough!"

Rei's voice echoed around them, dissipating the tension that had been ready to explode—at least for Elk and the others.

"Have you forgotten? Grimm came here to help us. So why are you taking such a hostile stance?"

As if finally remembering their current situation at Rei's words, the weapons aimed at Grimm were lowered.

Still, the fear and suspicion lingering in their eyes when they looked at Grimm was perhaps unavoidable, given how utterly extraordinary his very existence was.

Rei was interacting normally, but undead beings were fundamentally incompatible with humans.

Especially a powerful undead like Grimm—it wouldn't have been strange if his mere presence had stolen the consciousness of someone weak-willed.

The one standing before them was the one who could restore the consciousness of Vihera and Rhodes.

Even understanding that, they couldn't stop themselves from instinctively bracing for battle.

Even in such a situation, they managed to nod in response to Rei's words, and Elk and the others released their weapons.

Johanna and the others, having heard Rei's words, also managed to break free from their frozen state.

Whether it was Elk, Min, Johanna, or her companions... they had heard the stories, but seeing a Grimm directly with their own eyes made them realize it was a presence beyond imagination, forcing them to freeze.

But still, if Rei says so...

That thought alone allowed them to steady themselves and cease their hostile stances.

Without their trust in Rei, some might have attacked the Grimm out of sheer terror.

If that had happened, what would have become of them?

Given how fond Grimm was of Rei, perhaps those who attacked wouldn't have died.

But conversely, they might have been killed, and even after death, toyed with.

Thinking about it that way, it was impossible to casually interact with Grimm like Rei did.

(Thinking about it, the reason Rei can interact so casually with that monster Grimm... is it because she's used to interacting with Set-chan normally?)

It must be because she's unsettled. Still unaware that she'd addressed Rei as "Captain," Johanna murmured to herself.

Right in front of Johanna and the others, Rei and Grimm chatted amicably, as if they were old acquaintances.

In truth, Rei held a special place in Grimm's heart for many reasons.

The Zepairu Clan—a group of geniuses among geniuses, whom even he, who was called a genius by others, admired and could never reach no matter how hard he trained.

And Zepairu himself, who led that Zepairu Clan, had chosen Ray as his successor.

Though Grimm had all but lost the ability to feel emotional fluctuations, his feelings toward Rei still wavered.

And within that emotional fluctuation lay not the negative feelings other undead harbored toward the living, but positive ones.

Grim himself might not want to admit it, but it was perhaps akin to the gaze of a grandfather watching his grandchildren.

Otherwise, there was no way Grimm would lend his power to humans so readily.

"Hmm, you lot... I see."

As Grimm murmured, his gaze shifting to others besides Rei, Elena, and Marina—those he had previously met through the Paired Orbs—Elk and the others tensed again, their expressions betraying their surprise.

He was there. Even just his presence radiated overwhelming pressure, but the instant he directed his gaze—or rather, his skull, since he had no eyeballs—at them, a chill ran down their spines.

It felt like ice had been shoved directly into their spines—a sensation so deadly it felt almost fatal.

Elk didn't consider himself particularly clever.

Compared to his wife, Min, the difference in their intellect was like that between an adult and a child.

But... precisely because of that, his instinctive intuition far surpassed Min's.

In fact, during their time in the party known as the Axe of the Thunder God, there were numerous instances where Min saw no problem, but Elk's intuition warned of danger... and as a result, they escaped crisis more than once.

That same instinct was now telling Elk that he absolutely must not make the Grimm before him an enemy.

No matter what means we use, we cannot win.

"Calm down."

It was Rei's voice that snapped Elk out of his confusion.

That voice, which entered his ears with uncanny clarity, restored calm to Elk's heart.

"Huh..."

With that deep exhalation, his attitude toward Grimm shifted completely.

Yes, this Grimm was not an enemy to him.

On the contrary, he was the benefactor who had restored Rhodes' consciousness.

Elk averted his gaze from Grimm and turned his eyes toward Set, who carried Rhodes on his back.

Above Set's head, Yellow was desperately trying to hide from the overwhelming presence emanating from Grimm. Seeing Yellow like that, Elk's confusion subsided even further.

And... above all else, it was undoubtedly Rhodes, carried on Set's back.

He had acted all this time to save the unconscious Rhodes.

His efforts might finally be rewarded today.

If that was the case, he absolutely could not afford to make a fool of himself now.

"...I'm sorry. I'll be relying on you today."

Elk bowed his head to Grimm.

Ming bowed his head as well, matching Elk's gesture.

Grim, who had been watching the two, showed a slightly surprised expression upon seeing Elk and Min bow to him.

'So you two are the parents of the one who wants to regain the other's consciousness?'

"That's right. The one on Set's back is my son. ...Please, do whatever you can to bring him back."

"That's fine. With the Silver Lion's heart, it's likely possible. However... Needless to say, the woman who knows Rei takes priority."

Grim stated this as if to emphasize his point, but Elk eventually nodded in return.

"I understand that situation from Rei. In that case, we'll simply do our best. However... if it proves impossible to restore Rhodes' consciousness with this Silver Lion, I'd appreciate your cooperation in obtaining the heart of another Rank S monster."

At Elk's words, Grimm, with his skeletal face, thought for a moment before nodding in return.

'Very well. This time it's at Rei's request, but if you manage to obtain the heart of a Rank S monster from now on, I promise to lend a hand just as I did this time.'

"Really?! Thank you, that's a huge help!"

Even if this attempt failed, a thread of hope remained.

With that thought, Elk shouted happily.

Johanna and the others could only stare blankly at Elk's face, now beaming with a full smile.

They understood that Grimm was an acquaintance of Rei's and not someone who would attack them.

But even so, facing someone as powerful as Grimm—someone whose very presence felt like a tangible embodiment of their own deaths—Johanna and the others couldn't be as detached as Elk.

If Grimm were to harm them on a whim, their lives could be ended in an instant.

As this thought crossed her mind... Johanna suddenly realized.

(In terms of overwhelming power, isn't this undead Grimm the same as Rei?)

With Johanna's level of skill, she couldn't tell whether Rei or Grimm was stronger.

But even so, there was one thing she knew for certain.

Namely, that if either Rei or Grimm were to act in earnest, her life would vanish in an instant.

Therefore, since she felt no fear toward Rei, perhaps she needn't fear Grimm either.

While Rei and Grimm differed vastly—a living person versus an undead monster—Johanna had no mental space to dwell on that distinction right now.

Or rather, it was more accurate to say she was trying not to think about it.

In any case, Johanna seemed to have sorted things out to some extent within herself. After taking a small breath, she finally opened her mouth.

"Um, Mr. Grimm! ...Please take care of Lord Vihera!"

Johanna bowed deeply.

Grim shifted his gaze from Elk to Johanna, his expression equally intrigued.

He was well aware of what kind of being he was.

And he was also aware of the presence he unconsciously radiated.

Yet, despite sensing that, she still offered words of gratitude toward him.

Of course, someone like Elk, a strong individual among humans, could manage to speak with him.

After all, he had just done so moments ago.

But... Johanna, who now stood before Grimm's gaze, could hardly be called a strong one.

Well, within the category of humans, she might be considered strong. But through Grimm's eyes, she possessed only a level of strength that could hardly be called formidable.

For such an opponent to bow her head to him without hesitation, it was impossible not to be intrigued.

'Since it's a request from Rei, I'll do the job properly if I have the materials. But that's only if I have the Silver Lion's heart. If not, don't expect me to lend a hand.'

"Yes. I'm sure Rei and the others will manage! ...Though I can't participate in the battle myself."

Johanna and the others' job was solely to look after Vihera and Rodos while Rei and the others fought the Silver Lion.

Given that, she couldn't see what they could possibly do in the current situation.

Rhodos aside, she felt frustrated that she couldn't do anything for the respected Vihera.

'Don't worry, young one. You are still young. From my perspective, you're barely out of the cradle. That being the case, you still have a long road ahead.'

Johanna flashed a slightly awkward expression as the undead offered comfort.

Johanna's companions wore similarly awkward expressions.

Watching this, Rei finally spoke up.

"Grim, it's time to head to the lowest level... I'm counting on you."

"Very well. Then... let's go."

The moment he murmured this, without any sense of dissonance whatsoever... before they knew it, Rei and the others found themselves on the dungeon's lowest level.
