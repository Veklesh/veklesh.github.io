Time rewinds a little.

Rei watched from the darkness as Levegef and Gestal exchanged words.

Besides Rei, Byune and Ilse were present.

The rest of the group had scattered to surround the transaction site.

Ilse was by Rei's side because it was the safest place for her.

The reasoning was that since Rei was the strongest among them, it would be acceptable for Ilse to stay with him.

...However, having only Ilse guard Rei was too risky in many ways. Therefore, Byune, who was stronger than Ilse but possessed the lowest combat ability among the Crimson Wings, was assigned as her escort.

Even so, though Byune was the weakest within the Crimson Wings, she possessed above-average combat strength as a thief.

Considering that, she possessed sufficient strength to serve as Ilse's escort.

"Guh..."

Ilse struggled to suppress her voice, knowing the enemy who had slain her family was right there... within her line of sight.

At last... truly at last, she could avenge her kind family. The thought made it impossible for her to hold back.

She wanted to leap out right now.

She struggled to suppress that urge within herself.

"Calm down."

Rei spoke to Ilse, though he didn't truly believe those words would calm her.

But if it helped Ilse remember her current situation even a little... That was the thought behind his words.

(But if this keeps up, she'll likely reach her breaking point. Maybe I should make my move before that happens.)

Rei shifted his gaze to the numerous ruins surrounding Ajax and the others.

Most were constructed from a combination of wood and stone, though some buildings were made entirely of wood or stone alone. All possessed an age that made them worthy of being called ruins.

Holes dotted the walls and roofs everywhere, ruins so dilapidated it seemed impossible to live there.

Indeed, no one lived in those ruins. No one... except right now, where several individuals who appeared to be Jestal's guards were lurking there.

Of course, not all those lurking were Jestal's guards. Rei understood from what Dahl had told him that there were people from various factions present: members of other organizations, informants, those hoping to reap some kind of windfall...

Normally, they would need to wait until everyone was fully prepared.

But waiting that long risked Ilse making some kind of mistake.

Having made that judgment... then, as if waiting for that very moment, Yellow appeared.

Yellow, who was supposed to come to him once all preparations were complete.

"Yellow is here. Does that mean preparations are complete?"

"Kyuu!"

At Rei's words, Yellow let out a short cry.

Of course, Rei couldn't understand Yellow's words. ...Since Set, who was of a different species, could understand him, it wouldn't be strange if Rei could too, he thought. But for now, he simply couldn't comprehend Yellow's speech.

Even so, even without understanding Yellow's words, it wasn't difficult to grasp what Yellow was trying to say at this particular moment.

"Alright, let's get started then."

Muttering, Rei glanced at Byune.

Receiving that gaze, Byune gave a small nod in return.

She understood what Rei was asking of her.

To prevent Ilse from acting strangely in her excitement, and to protect her from the others if things turned chaotic.

Confirming Byune understood, Rei moved away from the spot.

His top priority was to deal with the guards surrounding Jestar, Ajax's business partner.

If he rashly attacked the guards here and stormed the transaction site directly, the guards might rush over and allow Ajax and the others to escape.

To prevent that, the absolute priority was to reduce the number of reinforcements that could come to their aid.

He approached the ruins a short distance away and quietly peered inside.

Inside, two men had already drawn their weapons, preparing to storm the scene at a moment's notice should any trouble arise during the deal.

(Perfect timing. Let's take care of these guys first.)

Making that decision, Rei drew only the Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring and charged straight into the ruins.

The two men heard the noise and realized someone had broken in, but by the time they noticed, Rei was already poised to thrust the Twilight Spear at them.

Their luck lay in the fact that Rei had been instructed by Dahl to capture them alive if possible.

Therefore, instead of thrusting the tip of the Twilight Spear, Rei thrust the butt end.

...Well, it was fortunate they didn't die, but it hardly counts as fortunate when they were left suffering in agony.

The guards, required to act as swiftly as possible, wore no armor.

Not only metal armor, but even leather armor made from monster hides carried considerable weight.

When their boss was attacked by the Ajax, moving without armor allowed them to rush to his aid as quickly as possible.

The difference that weight makes is a matter of seconds... maybe a dozen seconds at most.

But in this case, those few seconds to over ten seconds could mean the difference between life and death for Gestal.

This time, however, it backfired completely.

Following the first man who lost consciousness, the other man also had his senses taken away.

Given the role of bodyguard, and specifically assigned to protect the organization's boss, Jestal, he was undoubtedly one of the more skilled fighters within the group.

That much was certain, but unfortunately, he was no match for Rei.

"...Alright, next."

Leaving the two unconscious men behind, Rei departed the scene.

Left behind in the ruins were only the two unconscious men.

He assumed the intelligence unit would arrive soon to capture them.

Of course, he'd heard from Dahl that the number of intelligence unit members involved in this incident wasn't large.

Given that, there was also the possibility that members of another organization, or someone monitoring the transaction for some reason, might arrive and secure these two before the intelligence unit captured the unconscious pair.

However, Rei decided that was their problem, not his, and left the scene.

If luck was on their side, the intelligence unit would handle it.

With only that thought in mind, Rei headed for the next location.

Along the way, he faintly heard someone's scream. There was no need to wonder whose voice it was; it was obvious.

(Elena, Marina, Vihera, and Ara are holding their own too. Especially Vihera—she mentioned there might be a strong opponent there.)

Thinking such things, Rei also took down several more.

That said, not all those Rei defeated were Jestal's guards.

Some belonged to other organizations, but since Rei couldn't tell them apart, he certainly wasn't going to bother asking them.

As a result, a fair number of non-Gestal subordinates also had their consciousnesses taken by Rei... but Rei paid it no mind, continuing to take them down one after another.

After defeating over ten people, Rei came upon a slightly larger ruin.

Naturally, there was a hole in the wall, so he peered inside.

True to its size, five figures were inside.

However, two of them had daggers at their waists, but they were sheathed.

This was completely different from the others, who had already drawn their weapons, ready to act at a moment's notice if anything happened.

(Well, once these guys hear the screams of the others, they'll probably start keeping a closer watch on their surroundings.)

As it was a summer night, the sounds of insects echoed around them.

Additionally, the fact that they were each fighting at a sufficiently distant location meant the men hadn't heard their comrades' screams.

If they had senses as sharp as Rei's, they might have heard...

(Is this probably the command post? Or something like that? If so, those five... no, two seem to be the liaison personnel, so one of the remaining three must be commanding the guards... Thinking about it that way, it's pretty impressive that no one's reporting being attacked.)

Normally, if attacked, they'd report it to the command post.

But the fact that this hadn't happened even now made it clear the others were incapacitating the guards to prevent them from escaping after an attack.

Then again, considering Elena and the others' abilities, that might not be so surprising.

(Ara... she's fighting barehanded, not with the Power Axe. Using that could easily kill someone instead of just knocking them unconscious.)

Still, even though she couldn't use her weapon, Rei wasn't worried about Ara.

She possessed strength worthy of being called superhuman to begin with, and she had received proper training in unarmed combat.

Given that, the chances of her being seriously injured by the organization's guards were extremely low.

(Well, anyway, I should probably deal with the guy inside first.)

Since the ruins were a fairly large building, he figured he could use Twilight Spear as well as Death Scythe. He drew Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

Thus, Rei, now wielding his usual two-spear style, charged straight into the building.

Up until now, she had skillfully concealed herself, minimizing noise and presence to avoid detection by those inside. But charging in like this would inevitably alert them.

Moreover, given that the guards included command-level personnel, those present were undoubtedly skilled fighters.

"Enemy attack!"

That's precisely why one of the guards noticed Rei entering and shouted... but the moment he yelled, Rei had already struck both of them in the solar plexus with the pommels of both the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, knocking them unconscious.

Three remained.

One of them—whether he recognized Rei as Rei or not is unclear—still judged her to be an opponent he couldn't defeat.

Without a word, he immediately tried to flee the scene.

"Think you can get away? Penetrate!"

The Death Scythe's pommel, wrapped in wind, was unleashed, instantly robbing the man of consciousness.

That leaves three.

Of the remaining two, one charged at Rei with a look of near-despair.

The other, seeing an opening while Rei attacked the man who had charged him, tried to finish Rei off. He followed behind the man, staying out of Rei's line of sight.

Still channeling Penetrate, Rei flashed the Twilight Spear, slashing the first attacker sideways and sending him flying. Without a moment's hesitation—indeed, using the momentum from the Twilight Spear strike—he spun in midair and swung the Death Scythe.

Having been blinded by the man's advance, he couldn't avoid the sweeping horizontal strike of the Death Scythe.

No, it would be more accurate to say that the blow, delivered while spinning, was completely invisible to the man before he even had a chance to think about dodging.

The sudden strike from the side left the man no opening to dodge, sending him flying.

If Rei made a mistake at this moment, it was in the direction he sent the man flying.

The building itself was already a ruin, ready to collapse at any moment.

That's why the man, blown away by Rei's Death Scythe strike, smashed through that wall and continued flying without losing momentum... ultimately bouncing several times on the ground and rolling all the way to the spot where Gestal and Ajax were conducting their deal.

"Did I go a bit too far? Nah, but timing-wise, it shouldn't be too much of a problem."

Muttering, Rei followed the man out through the wall he had destroyed.

Upon arriving at the transaction site, those present naturally noticed his presence.

Even in a guild like Gilm, where many adventurers gathered, there was only one reckless soul who wielded both a scythe and a spear simultaneously.

"Rei...?"

Rei turned his gaze toward the voice that reached him. There stood Gestal, his face frozen in utter astonishment.

"Ah, I'm your enemy. Though there are a few others too."

As if in response to Rei's muttered words, a building a short distance away shattered, and a man was blown flying toward them.

Following the man's trajectory appeared a woman clad in a thin garment like a dancer or courtesan.

The fact that she wore greaves and greaves set her apart from dancers and prostitutes.

Then, Marina, Elena, and Ara appeared, as if pursuing Vihera.

Furthermore, Set, who had been flying through the night sky, surveying the surroundings, descended.

「Crimson Wings……」

Though Elena and Ara were different, Gestal murmured those words nonetheless.
