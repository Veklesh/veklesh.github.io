A deafening roar, and the village is engulfed in flames.

Using fire in the forest on the second floor of the dungeon is tantamount to suicide.

However, unlike the surrounding forest, the trees in that area had been thoroughly cleared, leaving the ground bare.

And using the wood that must have once grown there, several simple hut-like structures stood in a row.

How many goblins were there who suddenly realized their settlement was on fire?

Most of them likely perished in the flames without even realizing what was happening.

Luckily… no, perhaps unluckily in this case, since they saw their own settlement burning with their own eyes and realized they couldn’t escape their fate of being burned alive, a few goblins were running around inside the settlement in a frenzy.

But all the huts were ablaze, and there was nowhere to escape.

Even the goblins who tried to make their way out of the settlement were immobilized by arrows and stones flying at them from all directions, and eventually met their end in the flames.

Stunned by the sight of the burning settlement, Relieu turned her gaze to the boy standing next to her... Rei.

Although Relieu was a Rank A adventurer with a nickname, his true strength lay strictly in his role as a warrior.

Although she can use various skills, even for someone like Relieu, a fire spell powerful enough to burn down an entire village—albeit a small one—is something she rarely gets to see.

Noticing a goblin trying to escape the village, Relieu throws a stone to take it down, then turns to Rei and asks again.

“Hey, listen. Are you sure this is okay? If the fire spreads to the surrounding trees and we end up burning to death, that’s no laughing matter.”

“Is that so? If this fire spreads, it’ll clear out the second floor of the dungeon, making it easy to find the stairs to the third floor. Ah, but it’d be a shame if that tree with all the different fruits on it burned down.”

“Hey, wait a minute. Are you… really sure this is okay?”

As Relieu shot him a suspicious look, Rei nodded in assurance while hurling the spear he’d pulled from his Misty Ring—not the Twilight Spear, but a spear that was practically scrap metal.

“Calm down. Marina’s spirit magic has everything under control. It won’t spread, so don’t worry.”

Saying that, Rei turned his gaze toward the goblin settlement.

Looking closely, a thin membrane of water could be seen covering the settlement.

The water membrane was quite thin; stones, spears, and arrows fired by Rei and his companions would easily pierce right through it.

However, even that amount of water was enough to prevent the flames burning the goblin settlement from spreading.

……Under normal circumstances, it wouldn’t be strange for that amount of water to evaporate easily.

But what covered the goblin settlement wasn’t just ordinary water—it was water created by Marina’s spirit magic.

If all the flames burning the goblin settlement had been created by Rei’s magic, that would be one thing, but while the initial flames were indeed created by Rei’s magic, the ones spreading were ordinary flames.

This is likely the result of adjustments Rei made when casting the spell, thanks to his intuitive ability to structure magic.

Rei’s reputation… that he possesses the ability to create a whirlwind of fire and burn an entire army to the ground—of course, Relieu knows this as well.

As an adventurer with a nickname, it’s only natural that he would gather information on newly emerged nicknamed adventurers from the same base.

Even so, perhaps he hadn’t expected Relieu to be capable of such fine-tuned control—leaving aside large-scale destruction—and Relieu cast a look at Rei that seemed to express renewed admiration.

Even as she watched him, she continued to pick off goblins trying to escape the burning settlement with stones.

Though he was aware of Relieu’s gaze, Rei looked up at the sky.

There, Set flapped his wings… flying through the sky as if to declare that he alone was the ruler of the heavens.

Although this forest was home to many bird monsters like the Yellowbird and the Grimbow, so far, no one had attempted to attack Set as he soared through the sky.

Was it because they understood Set’s strength, or was it simply a coincidence?

In any case, from above, Set was unleashing various skills—Wind Arrow, Ice Arrow, Earth Arrow—down upon the ground.

……Naturally, the goblins bearing the brunt of these attacks were the ones suffering the most.

The few surviving goblins in this settlement were taking heavy damage from the arrows raining down from above.

“It’s nice not having to worry about saving the ones who are trapped, isn’t it?”

As he hurled another spear, Rei muttered.

Hearing Rei’s words, Relieu agreed wholeheartedly, from the bottom of her heart.

If this goblin settlement had been outside the dungeon… or if adventurers were allowed to freely enter the dungeon, there might have been women being held captive within the goblin settlement.

But this was inside the dungeon.

And as far as anyone knew, Rei and his group were the first to enter.

...It was possible that someone had entered the dungeon before them, but Rei was certain that anyone capable of doing so would never make the mistake of getting captured by goblins.

That said, since Set hadn’t found any signs of human presence, Rei judged that there was no need to worry about that.

For that reason, if the goal was simply to wipe them out, this goblin settlement wouldn’t be particularly difficult.

It’s not exactly a trade-off, but since they burned down the settlement along with everything else, it’ll be difficult to recover the goblin subjugation proof parts and magic stones.

For Rei and his group, goblin magic stones weren’t something they particularly wanted.

In fact, the effort required to extract the magic stones outweighed the benefits, so it wasn’t really an issue.

That said, for Rei—who was helping with research on how to cook goblin meat deliciously (though he only dropped by occasionally)—the chance to obtain a large quantity of goblin meat might have been a pretty tasty opportunity in its own right.

(No, if it’s just a matter of collecting goblin meat, I could just hire some random adventurers in Gilm. There’s no need for me to go out of my way to take it there myself.)

Watching the goblin settlement as the flames began to die down, Rei hurled his spear once more at the goblin that had fled, engulfed in fire.

The spear flew at a speed that made it impossible to believe he’d thrown it so casually; it shattered the goblin’s skull… and, piercing the torso of the club-wielding goblin behind it, it stuck diagonally into the ground, pinning the goblin in place.

That said, the goblin had already died instantly the moment its torso was pierced.

Time passed… and after about half an hour, judging that all the goblins had been wiped out, Marina used her spirit magic to extinguish the fire that was still burning.

“I wonder how many other goblin settlements there are. Kobolds, too. …If it were an orc settlement, I’d actually welcome it.”

Stepping into the burnt-out settlement, Rei muttered.

For Rei, orc meat was something he ate on a daily basis.

Precisely because of that, Rei went through it quickly, and his honest desire was to secure as much meat as possible.

As for kobolds, he’d decided treating them the same way as goblins would be fine.

(Ah, but if there were any rare or high-tier species... Hmm, I wonder if there are any. It’s a bit of a toss-up. It would be a big help to us if there were.)

Even with a goblin’s magic stone, if it comes from a rare or higher-tier species, there’s a high chance of learning a skill.

Given that, it goes without saying that if such monsters were around, it would be a huge help to Rei… and to Set as well.

Of course, higher-tier species aside, they’re called “rare” precisely because they don’t spawn that easily.

“Orcs, huh. …If we burn down an orc settlement like this, the smell of something appetizing is bound to waft through the air.”

“Is that so? It might be at first, but I have a feeling it’ll eventually just smell like burnt meat.”

As Relieu and Rei walked and talked, the others who had scattered to different locations gradually began to gather.

“Rei, what are we going to do now?”

“What do you mean, ‘what are we going to do’? If there are other settlements like this one, we’ll just keep wiping them out. There’s no way we’ll finish exploring this floor in just one day. So, I’d like to avoid running into goblins or whatever by surprise as much as possible. …Well, even if we kill every last goblin, the dungeon’s core will probably just summon more.”

There’s no doubt that goblins are troublesome monsters, even if they’re weak in battle.

Perhaps the dungeon’s core knows this too, because goblins are monsters that appear frequently throughout the dungeon.

They’re the perfect monsters for overwhelming an enemy with sheer numbers.

……Though, if they realize their opponent is strong, they’ll run away immediately.

“It’s not like the dungeon core can summon a massive horde of goblins right away, is it? As long as they don’t show up until we’ve explored this second floor and found the stairs to the third, that’s fine.”

Rei nodded in response to Relieu’s words.

In fact, Relieu’s words weren’t wrong.

Rei and the others don’t need to completely clear this second floor—there’s no need to go to every single spot, collect rare materials, or defeat every single monster.

As for Rei, as long as there’s a chance to obtain magic stones from unknown monsters, he’d like to wipe out every monster in this forest… well, maybe not quite that, but he does want to defeat every type of monster.

Of course, since he hasn’t taught Relieu or Byune about Beast Magic, he can’t exactly say that outright.

However...

“If possible, I’d like to collect magic stones from unknown monsters.”

He never forgets to mention that.

Even if his knowledge of Beast Magic is a secret, the fact that Rei has a hobby of collecting magic stones is fairly well known.

Rei muttered this, thinking that since Relieu was gathering information on him, she would naturally know about that as well…

“As long as you’re not wiping them all out, killing one or two shouldn’t be a problem.”

Sure enough, she said that, and told him there was no problem with Rei fighting the monsters.

However, Rei was bothered by the word “two” that had come out of Relieu’s mouth.

In order for Death Scythe and Set to learn their skills, they each need one magic stone… a total of two.

Because of that, when Rei tells people he has a hobby of collecting magic stones, he often explains that he acquires two stones—one for display and one for storage—but… judging by what he just said, it seems he might even know about that.

(Though it’s highly likely I’m overthinking this.)

Rei was thinking about this while watching Relieu, when suddenly, Set, who had been flying in the sky, began descending toward the ground.

Yellow was by Set’s side, but perhaps finding it difficult to keep up with Set’s speed, he had been left behind in the air.

“Grrrrrr!”

Set’s growl carried a warning that an enemy was approaching.

The moment they heard that sound, Rei and the others immediately went on high alert.

And then, emerging from the nearby bushes was...

"Pugii!"

It was a pig.

...Well, to be precise, it would be more accurate to call it a creature somewhere between a wild boar and a domestic pig.

Though, of course, wild boars and pigs don’t usually have wings like birds.

On top of that, the monster that appeared was barely knee-high to Rei.

No matter how you looked at it, it seemed like a baby monster.

“What is it? Just a baby?”

Saying that, Relieu lowered the tip of the longsword he had been holding at the ready. …But he immediately noticed something was off.

That was because he was the only one who had dropped his guard.

“Hey?”

Relieu asked in puzzlement, but Rei and the others, knowing how keen Set’s sense of danger was, weren’t about to let their guard down just because the monster in front of them was a child.

“P… Pghii”

The monster looked up at them with teary eyes.

At first glance, it looked so adorable that one might easily describe it as such, but Relieu turned her gaze back to Rei and the others.

“Hey, what the…!?”

The moment he looked away from the monster, Relieu reflexively swung his longsword.

The fact that he reacted instantly to the killing intent he sensed was exactly what you’d expect from a Rank A adventurer with a nickname.

But the blow Relieu struck… was blocked by the monster’s fangs, which had grown to be as large as Set’s without Relieu even noticing.
