If it were Marina, the former Guild Master, she would surely know what the guild’s guidelines entail.

Considering that, Rei’s honest feeling was that there was no need for a guild staff member to go all the way to a place where Kobolds were likely to be.

Thanks to the earthen walls, there’s no risk of more Kobolds appearing, but conversely, that also means the Kobolds currently inside Gilm… while it’s not impossible for them to escape given the height of the walls, it would certainly be a struggle.

In response to Rei’s remark questioning whether it was really necessary for guild staff to go out of their way to such a place, Lenora smiled and spoke up.

Even after the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle is finished, Suchi and her companions will need to return to the slums for a while.

Given how busy the guild already was, having to deal with such matters undoubtedly placed a significant burden on them.

Currently, many are out on jobs—mostly hunting Kobolds—but by the time the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle is finished, many other adventurers will be returning to the guild.

Considering that, even if they don’t enter the guild directly and just gather in front of it, having about fifty slum dwellers there was clearly a recipe for trouble.

“Well, well… I mean, I expected as much, but I didn’t think it would come to this.”

The site where the Giganto Turtle was being butchered, located a short distance from Gilm.

What Rei saw upon arriving there was a massive pile of Kobold corpses.

It was clear that since Rei had built an earthen wall, they couldn’t enter Gilm, and had therefore been driven this way.

There were also onlookers who had come to watch the adventurers defeat the monsters drawn by the Giganto Turtle’s dismemberment and the smell of its blood, but upon seeing the massive pile of Kobold corpses, they looked visibly queased.

They had seen Kobold corpses like this yesterday as well.

But even though they were the same kind of Kobold corpses, the number was different today.

That is likely why so many people feel uneasy, frightened, or disgusted by the sheer number of Kobold corpses.

(It’s not a big deal if there’s just one bug, but if there are dozens or hundreds of them… it starts to feel gross, doesn’t it?)

As residents of Gilm, they naturally knew that Kobolds had invaded Gilm, and some of them were likely anxious about it.

In this world, quality can easily outweigh quantity, but that applies only to a select few, such as those with special titles.

For ordinary people like the onlookers here, being attacked by dozens of Kobolds is nothing short of a death sentence.

So even if they feel threatened by the Kobolds now, it’s a little too late, just as Rei thought.
