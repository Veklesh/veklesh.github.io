"Well, I hope he's still safe..."

A few minutes after the meeting with Daskar using the Paired Orbs ended... Rei was in the sky.

Of course, Rei wasn't flying directly; as usual, he was traveling aboard Set.

And at the tip of Set's foot was the Set Cage containing Elena and the others.

Originally, he hadn't intended to bring Ryutas and the others to the battlefield.

From Rei and the others' perspective, Ryutas and his guards were merely stronger than ordinary people, but not skilled enough to be called true warriors. They were practically a hindrance.

If they were to head to the battlefield with Rei and the others, the honest truth among the Crimson Wings, including Rei, was that they wanted at least someone with Byune's level of skill.

Despite this situation, bringing Ryutas along was ultimately due to his position.

Daskar had given them an additional request: since Ryutas was a versatile asset, they were to protect him from those like the assassins of Jaya.

If Rei and the others were returning to Mejogo, they couldn't leave Ryutas and the others behind in the forest, considering the possibility of assassins arriving.

The battlefield isn't exactly safe either, with Jaya soldiers and the Resistance fighting... Still, it was judged safer for them to stay with Rei and the others than left behind in the forest. That's why they're traveling together like this.

(If we keep them near Marina in the rear guard, they'll have their original escorts too, so it should be safe enough.)

That was part of the calculation.

"Ah, there it is. ...Troublesome. Well, maybe it's only natural."

Within minutes of taking flight from the forest, the Mejogo came into view.

Seeing the Mejogo meant, naturally, seeing the battle raging near its gate.

Seeing this, Rei's brow furrowed slightly—likely because the battlefield had descended into chaos.

When Rei and the others escaped Mejogo, pulling off that stunt of dropping the carriage from the air, the opposing forces hadn't yet made contact.

That's precisely why they could pull off dropping the carriage.

But now, it had devolved into a chaotic melee where the opposing forces were intermingled.

In other words, if Rei were to use his signature area-of-effect annihilation magic here, it would inevitably harm not only Jaya but also the Resistance and the women.

As someone tasked with bringing the Resistance—or more accurately, the women led by the Resistance—to Gilm, Rei couldn't possibly do such a thing.

If he did something like that, the Resistance and the women would never trust Rei again.

To prevent that, he couldn't possibly resort to burning them all to ashes.

"No choice. Set, descend to a spot slightly away from the battlefield."

"Guruu!"

Responding to Rei's words, Seto let out a cry of understanding and began descending toward the ground to lower the Set Cage.

After setting the Set Cage down on the ground, he returned to the sky before descending once more.

With the Set Cage, its camouflage ability would have made such a form inconspicuous.

But because he placed the Set Cage on the ground, some people noticed Set's form even from within the battlefield.

Whether they were Resistance fighters or Jaya soldiers, it made no difference.

...Had they known Rei and his group were the ones who brought down the carriage, it would have caused significant confusion among the Jaya soldiers too.

But when Rei brought the carriage down from the air, the Set Cage was present.

The scene visible to the Resistance remained hidden from the Jaya soldiers.

Therefore, at this point, the Jaya soldiers had no understanding whatsoever that it was Rei and his group who had inflicted heavy casualties and chaos upon them at the start of this battle.

Under the gaze of such men, Rei and his group moved into action.

Of course, if they had barged onto the battlefield without a word, they risked being treated as enemies by both sides.

To avoid that, they needed to make arrangements beforehand.

"I'll go talk to Sura, who leads the Resistance. Marina, stay here and guard Ryutas while providing support. Elena, Vihera, and Byune, the three of you head to the battlefield. But remember, you're only attacking the Jaya soldiers."

"How are we supposed to tell them apart?"

Elena's gaze was fixed on the battlefield as she asked.

Of course, the resistance forces had many women.

But not all of Jaya's soldiers were men, nor were all of the Resistance fighters women.

Given that, there was a real risk of attacking Resistance soldiers instead of Jaya soldiers if we weren't careful.

"Why not just ask them? Also... it's safe to assume the beautiful ones are more likely to be on the Resistance side."

Naturally, beautiful women are popular as prostitutes.

While personality matters if you're looking for a lover, a prostitute is fundamentally a one-night companion, so beauty is absolutely essential.

Moreover, since Jaya could brainwash them with slave collars, personality wasn't an issue.

In other words, they must have prioritized taking away those considered beautiful or cute.

(Being considered beautiful by them... must be rather complicated.)

Watching the three rush into the fray, and Rei riding Set toward the Resistance headquarters, Ryutas couldn't help but think that.

Elena and the others, who were searching for beauties themselves, were incredibly beautiful—so much so that even in the midst of battle, one's gaze would be drawn to them.

Being sought out as a beauty by such individuals could easily wound a woman's pride.

In that sense, being sought by Byune was somewhat preferable to being sought by Elena or Vihera.

But that was only because Byune was still small and had an appearance that should be called cute rather than beautiful. What she would become in the future was unknown.

(In that case, Marina not going over there... was perhaps the only saving grace.)

Watching Marina use spirit magic to slice through several men, presumably Jaya soldiers, with wind blades, Ryutas kept a watchful eye on the surroundings with his guards, ready to respond to anything that might happen.

"Hey, what the hell was that?!"

Sura, commanding the resistance, gasped in shock at the sight of Set suddenly descending onto the battlefield.

Of course, she knew what Set's presence signified, but his unexpected return to the battlefield was clearly unforeseen.

While she hadn't considered Rei an enemy, given their cooperative relationship with Rei and the others, Rei and his group had nonetheless left once before only to return to the battlefield now.

It was only natural to assume there was some significance to this. Perhaps... just maybe, for some reason, they had decided to cooperate with Jaya. That possibility wasn't entirely out of the question.

That's why he'd been on guard, but then he saw Rei, riding Set, arrive at the Resistance headquarters where he was stationed. For now, he judged there was no hostile intent.

If they were hostile, they would undoubtedly be armed.

...Though, in Rei's case, he could pull weapons from the Misty Ring at any moment. And above all, even unarmed, his inhuman physical abilities granted him vicious lethality.

Above all, considering the capabilities of the Set he rode, it would be naive to assume he wouldn't be hostile even unarmed.

Even so, Sura restrained her allies who were preparing to intercept Rei and Set as they approached. It was likely because she had at least some degree of trust in Rei.

"Let them through! That griffin is an ally!"

Technically, it wasn't confirmed as an ally yet, but Sura shouted anyway.

Her cry stemmed from the fear that the sudden appearance of a high-rank monster like a griffin might cause confusion.

In truth, only the Resistance members knew that Rei and Seta were coordinating their actions.

The many women liberated from prostitution present now hadn't been told about the cooperation with Rei.

Given that, they might mistakenly assume the high-rank monster was attacking them, panic... and in the worst case, even attack Rei.

That was why Sura shouted.

Fortunately, Sura's cry reached the ears of those nearby before they could panic, and those in Set's path quietly moved aside.

The sight of a griffin—a rank A monster—passing right beside them made everyone break out in a cold sweat, despite the summer heat.

But naturally, Set didn't attack such people, arriving before Sura without incident.

"Looks like you're still safe, huh?"

"...Safe? I was suddenly charged by someone riding a griffin out of nowhere!"

Sura complained, but Rei responded with a defiant smile.

"When we escaped from here, didn't we provide you with backup?"

"Yeah. That was a big help."

Indeed, the damage inflicted on the Jaya soldiers by several carriages plummeting from the sky was substantial.

It wasn't just the direct damage; being caught off guard from a completely unexpected direction had also dealt a heavy psychological blow.

What if they faced another such attack mid-battle? The thought alone made even the uninjured Jaya soldiers unable to focus fully on the fight at hand.

That was undoubtedly one reason why the Resistance, despite being outnumbered and somewhat on the defensive, was still holding their own.

"But if you're truly on our side... Laura, Sharia's unit is about to be surrounded! Go support them!"

Even while speaking with Rei, Sura's gaze darted constantly across the battlefield.

Issuing that command, she turned back to Rei.

"As you can see, we don't have time for leisurely conversation. Tell me frankly. Why did you come back?"

"To cooperate with you."

"That..."

Sura faltered for a moment at Rei's words.

Among those present, many who knew Rei's true identity showed expressions of joy.

It was only natural. An adventurer powerful enough to take on an entire army single-handedly was offering to help them.

It would be no exaggeration to declare victory in the battle right then and there.

But... Sura, leading the resistance, couldn't simply trust Rei's words so easily.

"I appreciate your help, but then why didn't you just join us when you attacked them earlier?"

She implied he must have some ulterior motive.

Since that wasn't entirely wrong, Rei nodded without hiding anything.

"Yeah. A message from my employer. Looks like you're trying to escape Mejogo, but where do you plan to go with this many people?"

"Well..."

Sura stumbled over her words.

It was only natural. None of them had ever imagined this situation would arise.

They never imagined all the slave collars would suddenly be destroyed, and the women forced into prostitution would snap back to their senses...

Though, to be fair, it was Rei who orchestrated things to turn out this way.

"Even if we escape this place, there's nowhere for us to go. But staying here in Mejogo is also problematic in many ways. Am I wrong?"

"...That's right."

In the end, Sura acknowledged Rei's words.

In truth, many within the Resistance shared that view, but the majority of the women themselves simply didn't want to stay here.

Beyond that, the risk of being attacked by Jaya's forces while staying here was also significant.

That said, it was undeniably true that escaping Mejogo offered nowhere to go.

"Well, I have good news for you lot."

"Good news?"

Sura looked at Rei curiously at his words.

"Yeah. Since you asked me to be blunt, I'll be straightforward. Lord Daskar of Gilm... of the Kingdom of Mireana, has said he'll welcome you. And not just the freed women. All of you, including the Resistance."

"Why?"

The quick reply was the question Sura felt upon hearing Rei's words.

"Why would a noble of the Mireana Kingdom go that far?"

The terms seemed far too generous.

Sura's question echoed the thoughts of the others listening nearby.

"Well, yeah. Honestly, I think so too. But there is a reason. I don't know if you're aware, but Gilm is currently undergoing expansion work. Because of that, they can never have enough hands. Also, I can't go into detail, but even after the expansion work is finished, there will still be plenty of work."

Those words were undeniable facts.

Dismantling the Giganto Turtle, refurbishing the Sand Ships, new manufacturing projects, and so on.

Plus, since Gilm is expanding with the construction, they naturally want more residents.

"...Well then, what will you do? It's up to your answer from here."

At Rei's words, Sura thought for a moment... then, as if making up her mind, she opened her mouth.
