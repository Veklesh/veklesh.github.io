"Ahahahahahahaha! Cheers, cheers, cheers!"

"Ah, cheers to the World Tree!"

"To our settlement, cheers!"

As the moon rose in the night sky and stars twinkled, many Dark Elves gathered at the training grounds within their settlement to feast.

Numerous bonfires illuminated the surroundings brightly, and everywhere, Dark Elves drank and made merry.

They had feasted and celebrated when the World Tree temporarily recovered, but this feast was on a far grander scale.

Moreover, the variety of dishes being served was undeniably more luxurious than at the previous feast.

...Though it must be said, the ingredients for these lavish dishes were largely the meat of the monsters that had attacked the settlement today.

The meat of the orcs, who could be called the dark elves' natural enemies, was especially popular. It had become a strange situation where orcs, relentlessly attacked by dark elves while alive, were welcomed by them after death.

While this place was normally where the Dark Elves conducted their combat training, today it had been transformed into a place of happiness by the many gathered here.

That happiness stemmed from the dishes made with monster meat, starting with the orc meat, bringing bliss to the palate... a feast for the senses.

"Still, who would've thought that light orb was the World Tree's will... Grandpa, has anything like this ever happened before?"

One of the dark elf men asked his grandfather—a dark elf younger than Optis, though they didn't appear to differ much in age—while eating a skewer of roasted orc meat.

"Nah. I've lived in this settlement for ages, but this is the first time I've seen it."

"...Then why did it do that?"

"Well... Probably thanks to that man named Rei, I suppose..."

The dark elf nodded in agreement at his grandfather's words.

The reason behind the recent series of events—the World Tree disturbance likely caused by the giant cicada—was already common knowledge. Because...

"He's the kind of guy who could take down a giant cicada like that..."

He muttered, glancing toward the giant cicada's corpse placed in the corner of the banquet hall.

The giant cicada's corpse had been stored in Rei's Misty Ring, but Marina had requested it be brought out so everyone could clearly see what kind of foe it had been.

Some of the Dark Elves were so stunned by its sheer size they nearly fell over, yet most still sent looks of respect toward Rei and Set for defeating such a colossal foe.

At this point, it was safe to say the fearful glances directed at Rei had almost vanished.

Some still harbored doubts regarding the magic incident, but having been saved by them, their pride as Dark Elves wouldn't allow them to maintain such an attitude.

Normally, the corpse of the giant cicada that had caused this World Tree disturbance would not have been out of place at the center of the banquet table.

So why was it placed outside the banquet hall? Because of its sheer size.

If the giant cicada's corpse were in the center of the banquet hall, those feasting would have to maneuver around it to move about.

A monster a few meters long wouldn't be that much trouble, but this one was twenty meters long... Even cut in half, that meant two pieces each about ten meters long.

It might have been fine for viewing, but it was deemed too much of an obstruction for holding a banquet, so it ended up being placed in a separate location.

"Son-in-law, you really saved us this time."

"No, I told you, I'm not your son-in-law."

Not being much of a drinker, he replied to Optis while sipping fruit water that the dark elf had chilled for Rei using spirit magic.

Optis wasn't entirely serious either; he was probably teasing Rei half the time.

The proof was the amused smile playing on Optis's face.

He was thoroughly enjoying Rei's apparent dislike of being called "son-in-law."

Knowing this, Rei decided that arguing back would only amuse Optis further. He quietly averted his gaze from Optis and turned his eyes to Set, who was eating stuffed oak meat beside him.

It was a dish where the innards were removed from an oak tree trunk, stuffed with herbs, nuts, fruits, and vegetables, bound with thread, and then slowly roasted over time.

This time, lacking time, they'd resorted to using spirit magic for cooking, so the flavor inevitably fell short compared to when they'd put their full effort into preparing it.

But... even so, it seemed delicious enough for Set, who was happily eating the stuffed oak.

"Looks a bit rough, but it smells delicious, doesn't it?"

Even with the oak's face and limbs attached, and despite being prepared so it was perfectly edible, it still couldn't shake off a certain grotesque quality.

(They say the more bizarre the food, the tastier it is... I guess this is one of those things.)

Sighing, Optis was about to speak up, still trying to tease him about something...

"Elder... no, Grandfather. You seem to be enjoying yourself quite a bit. It appears you've been causing quite a bit of trouble for Rei since earlier."

The voice addressed Optis.

Optis froze for a moment at the sound of that voice, but the next instant, he smiled... not the teasing smile from before, but a smile meant to cover up what he was saying, and he opened his mouth.

"No, no, I'm not causing Rei any trouble. Just having a little chat, you see? Right?"

Optis' gaze shifted toward Rei, who he'd been talking to.

As Optis pleaded with his eyes for her to cover for him, Rei opened her mouth to speak...

"Is that so?"

Marina shot her a sidelong glance, her eyes crinkling with amusement.

"You were definitely pestering him, huh?"

At first, Rei briefly considered covering for Optis, but when Marina's gaze fell on her, she instantly changed course and offered Optis up as a scapegoat.

"What?! Rei, you're abandoning me!?"

"Well, what can I say? It's true I was inconvenienced."

"Grandfather. Might we have a word?"

Sensing something dangerous in those words, Optis set down the wine glass he was holding and immediately grabbed his beloved cane, trying to flee...

"Oh dear, Grandfather. Where do you think you're going? You're supposed to be coming this way, aren't you?"

Marina closed the distance in an instant and grabbed Optis by the collar.

"What!?"

It was Optis who cried out in surprise.

Even if it was half in jest, he never imagined he'd be caught so easily.

"Marina... What happened to you?"

Optis' tone held none of the playful air from mere seconds before.

Being her own granddaughter, Optis understood Marina's capabilities well.

While Marina possessed exceptional skill as an archer and spirit mage, as a reaction to that, like most dark elves, her close combat abilities were far from strong.

Yet the way she had closed the distance between them just now was not the movement of someone who was weak in close combat.

That very fact made him wonder how Marina had acquired such power.

But... as Optis fixed her serious gaze upon her, Marina opened her mouth with a radiant smile.

"Right now, something that came from Rei's body is circulating within me. That's probably why."

When Marina said that, movement around them froze for an instant.

It was... far too suggestive a statement. No wonder.

Whether they imagined something or had simply drunk too much... Dark Elves of both genders who had been eavesdropping flushed crimson.

Naturally, Marina's words reached Elena and Vihera as well. The two swayed to their feet and began walking toward Marina.

Swaying, swaying.

Their movements, reminiscent of ghosts or something similar, made even those still frozen in place after hearing Marina's earlier words feel their hearts pound violently.

The sight of the two women swaying and swaying as they advanced, their beauty all the more terrifying for its sheer strangeness, instilled a deep dread in the surrounding dark elves.

"Marina. I'd like to have a word with you."

"Right. I'd like an explanation for that remark just now and that kiss earlier, if possible."

"Oh? Well, if you want to hear it, I'll tell you everything. But this isn't the place. We need to have a woman-to-woman talk, so shall we step outside?"

Marina stated this without much resistance, turning her gaze toward Optis.

"So, Grandfather. I'll be stepping away for a moment, but please don't get too carried away for someone your age. I'll hear about it later from someone else."

Marina smiled seductively, a faint smile playing on her lips, then stood up and walked away.

Watching his granddaughter's retreating figure, Optis shot a somewhat exasperated glance at Rei, who was nearby.

To Rei, who had been enjoying the meal with Set, seemingly completely unfazed by the earlier exchange between the three women.

"You... are going to have a hard time."

"What?"

Rei, his mouth stuffed with stewed oak meat, responded to Optis' words.

With his mouth full, it was practically meaningless.

"Hmph, well, whatever. ...Everyone. The disaster has passed. Now let us drink and eat heartily, and celebrate our safe survival!"

Optis raised his wine cup loudly, trying to break the frozen silence.

Taking that as a cue, those around him raised their cups too, chattering away as they tried to forget the scene they'd just witnessed.

"The World Tree's back, so we should drink and make some noise today, right?"

"Yeah. ...But hey, wait—that 'disaster' Optis just mentioned... Could it be..."

"Don't say it. Just don't say it. Don't say another word. Absolutely don't say it. Got it? The disaster is that giant cicada over there, split clean in two from head to tail. It has to be. Nothing else. ...Understand?"

The dark elf man nodded in response to his friend's insistence that he must not utter another word.

Everyone understood. The calamity spoken of by Optis referred not only to the giant cicada, but also to the conflict between the three beautiful women.

But voicing it felt like inviting new misfortune upon themselves, so they couldn't bring themselves to say it.

"But... what was that light orb Rei was carrying? It felt strangely familiar."

Perhaps wanting to avoid further talk of calamity, the dark elf man nearby abruptly changed the subject.

The others around them, looking exasperated, explained it was the will of the World Tree.

The shift in topic felt somewhat deliberate, but many likely wanted to divert the conversation quickly. The discussion moved in that direction as if no one had any objections.

He glanced over at Rei, who was eating with Set, but the orb everyone was talking about was nowhere to be seen.

It had left Rei's side when they returned to the settlement after defeating the giant cicada.

That's precisely why some people hadn't seen the light sphere Rei had been carrying.

...Actually, Rei had the orb with her even before she started causing trouble near the barrier's perimeter. She had it with her when the village first started getting attacked by monsters and headed for the World Tree. So, plenty of people saw it then too... but back then, most were too preoccupied with the monster attacks to pay much attention.

Quite a few people thought, "Maybe I saw it?" but very few remembered it clearly.

The Dark Elves each claimed, "I saw it," "I didn't see it," "I touched it," "I stroked it"... but a few were called out by their companions for bragging.

It was only natural. Especially the dark elf man who claimed he'd stroked the orb—he was met with looks of utter disbelief from those around him.

They knew the light sphere couldn't be touched.

"So, is that light sphere really the will of the World Tree?"

"If you're curious, why not ask it yourself?"

"Would it tell us? We're talking about the one who took down that giant cicada, right?"

"Nah, that's irrelevant. Considering Rei's personality, if he thinks it's okay to tell us, he'll spill the beans right away."

"...Then you go ask him."

"Yeah, yeah! Go on, ask him!"

They say loose lips sink ships, and sure enough, the Dark Elf who suggested asking Rei drew stares from the others before eventually standing up.

Perhaps because he hadn't been particularly afraid of Rei to begin with, he simply walked over to her.

Still, he didn't suddenly ask about the light sphere. Instead, he started talking about the dish Rei was eating.

"Rei, is that dish tasty?"

"Hm? Oh, it's delicious. So dark elves eat stews with offal and beans too, huh?"

"Ahaha. That probably varies by household. ...So, I was wondering, would it be okay to ask what that light sphere was?"

"That light sphere? That was... something like the will of the World Tree."

'......'

Hearing Rei's words, spoken without any attempt to hide their meaning, the dark elves listening fell silent. Inside, they thought, "Well, of course."
