After stepping out of the office, several minutes passed... Rei stood leaning against the wall.

Normally, if he'd gone back downstairs, he could have gotten a cup of tea from the guild staff.

But Rei wasn't in the mood for that right now.

He never imagined Ambris would attempt teleportation, suddenly appearing beside Vihera and then seeping into her body... But the fact remained that he hadn't managed to defeat Ambris before such an action could be taken.

No matter how many attacks he unleashed to destroy Ambris's body, the troublesome enemy would immediately resurrect... Still, if he had kept Vihera farther away during the fight, this situation could have been avoided.

As these thoughts crossed my mind, the office door suddenly swung open.

And the face that appeared was, unsurprisingly, Marina's.

For a moment, Rei had hoped, just a fleeting hope, that perhaps Vihera might casually appear instead. That made his disappointment all the greater.

Marina, who had appeared, invited Rei into the room without any particular sign of anger, despite his disappointed expression.

Normally, it wouldn't be pleasant for Marina to see the man she loved worrying about another woman.

Yet, if the object of his concern was Vihera... or Elena, Marina strangely didn't feel jealousy to that extent.

Of course, this wasn't because she loved Rei less than the other two... but because she understood that all three of them were destined to be with Rei.

If he were worrying about Vihera or Elena... or anyone else besides her, she might have felt some jealousy.

Thinking such things, Marina escorted Rei into the office.

Vihera's figure lying on the sofa was unchanged from when Rei had left the office earlier.

The thin garment—one hesitates to even call it clothing—that had been removed to examine Vihera's condition was now back in place.

Still, there was no sign of Vihera regaining consciousness.

The only silver lining, if you could call it that, was that the unconscious Vihera showed no signs of distress.

To anyone unaware of the situation, Vihera simply appeared to be in a deep sleep.

If agonized moans had been escaping Vihera's lips, Rei would have been unable to stand still.

Averting his gaze from Vihera's face, which looked as if she were merely asleep, Rei asked Marina.

"So, how is Vihera? Do you have any idea when she might wake up or anything like that?"

At those words, Marina shook her head and opened her mouth regretfully.

"I don't know. There are no physical abnormalities, and his magic power shows no disturbance. ...Honestly, it wouldn't be surprising if he woke up at any moment. From what I've seen, I don't think there's a problem..."

Marina paused, then turned her gaze back to Vihera.

She couldn't do anything about it. Seeing Marina's resigned attitude, Rei took a potion from the Misty Ring.

"Then... would using this potion be pointless?"

This potion was no ordinary potion.

It was a rare potion made using materials from the World Tree.

Its effects were vastly different compared to the potions sold in Gilm.

A potion of that caliber might just heal Vihera.

Marina shook her head at Rei, who spoke with that thought.

"Unfortunately, I don't think so. If he's injured, it could heal him in an instant, but..."

Marina's words were hesitant.

Marina had never seen anyone in Vihera's current state before, so she couldn't be certain whether the World Tree Potion would actually be ineffective.

Even to Marina's eyes, Vihera truly looked as if he were merely sleeping.

It wouldn't be the least bit surprising if she woke up yawning at any moment.

"We could have a healer or doctor examine her... but what should we do?"

It would probably be pointless.

Marina implied as much, but considering the slim possibility Vihera might regain consciousness, there was no way Rei could refuse to have her examined.

"Then, can I ask you? I know you're busy with all sorts of things right now."

The "busy" in this case referred not to Marina, but to the healers and doctors.

The matter concerning Ambris currently unfolding... no, that had unfolded around Gilm.

The leader species that had been created formed packs, and several of these packs were roaming around Gilm's vicinity... more accurately, the borderlands.

Many adventurers have accepted bounties to defeat these packs, and naturally, many are getting injured.

For Gilm, letting adventurers die from lack of money for treatment would reduce their defensive strength. Thus, Daskar declared he would cover the medical costs himself.

Thanks to this, even if you're injured fighting a pack... as long as it's not life-threatening, you can be healed quickly.

Of course, free treatment is limited to adventurers hunting the hordes or searching for Ambris.

"Yes, I'll bring him right away."

Even as she said this, Marina's tone held no hint of expectation.

She probably anticipated that even bringing in one of Gilm's most skilled recovery mages wouldn't help.

Still, clinging to that one in a million chance, she left the room.

She needed to have a guild official fetch a healing mage.

She also needed to inform Lord Daskar that Ambris had been defeated.

However, even if Ambris was defeated, the Leader Species it had created would not vanish.

While no more Leader Species would be created, the Gilm members would still need to defeat the hordes led by the already-created Leader Species.

...Depending on the time and circumstances, the packs might clash and fight each other, or they could be overrun by a more powerful monster.

Conversely, it couldn't be ruled out that a leader species' pack might absorb another pack and grow even larger.

"Alright, wait here for a bit,"

Watching Marina leave the office after saying that, Rei sat down on a different sofa from the one where Vihera was sleeping.

"...Haa..."

A deep sigh escaped him as he turned his gaze to the blue sky outside the window.

There was the bright blue sky, typical of summer.

It was completely different from his state of mind... almost the exact opposite. Staring at that blue sky, Rei sighed again.

As long as they were active as adventurers, Rei... and Vihera too, had prepared themselves for facing danger within their quests.

Still, seeing Vihera lying there unconscious like this, he couldn't help but feel something.

He watched Vihera's face, which looked as if she were simply sleeping soundly, for about ten minutes... until finally, a knock sounded on the office door, and it opened.

Entering the office alongside Marina, who looked as radiant as ever, was another woman.

She appeared to be in her mid-twenties. Her green hair was cut to shoulder length for ease of movement.

Her well-proportioned face bore a gentle expression; one could certainly call her beautiful.

However, since Rei was accustomed to seeing beauties like Elena, Vihera, and Marina—women who could be considered representative of their era—on a daily basis, he wasn't particularly surprised by the sight of this woman.

The woman first directed her gaze toward Vihera, who was lying on the sofa, then turned her eyes to Rei.

"Nice to meet you, Rei. I'm Lunamiria. ...Well, actually, we've met several times before, but this is the first time we've formally greeted each other like this."

"We've met before?"

Looking closely at the face of the person who introduced herself as Lunamiria, Rei immediately recalled where he had seen the person before.

It was a face he'd occasionally seen among those who doted on Set, feeding and petting him.

"Oh, right, Set's..."

"Yes. Set-chan has played with me several times. ...I never imagined I'd meet you, Rei, in a place like this."

Lunamiria flashed a smile, but her expression quickly hardened as she turned her gaze toward Vihera.

"I'd like to talk about Set-chan too, but for now, this gentleman's treatment comes first."

"...I'll leave it to you."

Nodding at Rei's words, Lunamiria approached the unconscious Vihera and gently placed her hand on his face.

In her left hand, not touching his forehead, she held what appeared to be a wand, a magical catalyst.

She murmured an incantation... and soon her hand was enveloped in light, which then flowed into Vihera.

Marina gently took Rei's hand as he watched the scene.

Rei, too, squeezed Marina's hand back.

Through the warmth of each other's hands, they understood they were firmly present... and eventually, the light faded from Vihera's body.

Perhaps Vihera would awaken at any moment.

With that hope in mind, Rei looked toward Lunamiria... but Lunamiria, who had been concentrating on the magic, opened her eyes, looked at Rei and Marina, and slowly shook her head. ...Not up and down, but side to side.

"Huh!?"

Both the thought "Why?" and the thought "I knew it" existed within Rei's heart.

Facing an unknown entity like Ambris, he hadn't expected things to be resolved so easily.

But... even so, he had held onto the hope that maybe, just maybe, something could be done.

"I'm sorry, but it seems beyond my abilities. ...Before that, based on my examination, there are no abnormalities in Ms. Vihera's body. I'd say she's in perfect health."

"...Perfectly healthy? But..."

As Rei tried to protest, Lunamiria nodded back, as if to say she understood.

"Yes. Even if she's healthy, she won't regain consciousness. In other words, the reason Ms. Vihera remains unconscious isn't a physical problem, but a mental one."

"...So you're saying using a potion made from World Tree ingredients would be pointless?"

"Well... Ordinary potions are generally meant for physical recovery, so I could say it's pointless..."

"But since it's a potion made from the World Tree, that incredible substance, we can't say for certain it won't have any effect..."

Marina joined the conversation between Rei and Lunamiria.

Marina was undoubtedly the one among them who understood the true nature of the World Tree best.

Not only was she born and raised near the World Tree, but Marina is descended from its bloodline.

That's precisely why she understands the immense power inherent in ingredients derived from the World Tree.

"So, do you think we should try it?"

"Using the potion won't harm your body. So it should be fine... right?"

"Yes. However, it might end up wasting a potion made from the World Tree."

Lunamiria answered Marina's question in a polite tone.

While she had spoken to Rei in a relatively casual tone as his master, she naturally adopted a more appropriate demeanor toward Marina, the Guildmaster.

Marina seemed accustomed to such things, showing no particular concern as she turned her gaze toward Rei.

"It seems using the World Tree potion won't cause any problems. Why not give it a try? Though, it's more likely to end up being a waste."

Marina gave him a challenging look, as if asking, What do you say?

World Tree materials were extremely rare.

It wasn't something you could simply buy with money.

Are you willing to waste it just to try? Marina's gaze asked this question. Without hesitation, Rei pulled the potion from the Misty Ring.

It was the sort of item that, if offered in the right place, could easily be traded for gold coins.

Lunamiria, too, found herself captivated by the potion's inherent prestige, if that's what you'd call it.

Rei brought the potion to Vihera's lips... but, of course, Vihera, unconscious, couldn't possibly drink it.

Well, forcing it into her mouth might make her swallow it, but there was a chance she'd reflexively spit it out.

While the World Tree potions weren't limited to the one Rei currently held, they weren't exactly abundant either.

Rei pondered how to get her to drink it... and a scene from a manga or anime he'd seen back in Japan flashed through his mind.

(Guess I have no choice. I don't like doing this to someone unconscious, though.)

Resolved, he poured the World Tree Potion into his own mouth.

Then, placing his hand on Vihera's face, he poured the potion directly into her mouth.

He kept their lips pressed together for several seconds to ensure she wouldn't spit it out.

Confirming Vihera's throat moved to swallow the potion, he gently pulled his lips away.

Under normal circumstances, Marina would be teasing about this scene... but now wasn't the time for that.

Rei, Marina, and Lunamiria all watched Vihera intently.

But... there was no sign of her waking up. She remained asleep.

"No good... huh?"

"...Yeah."

Rei murmurs briefly in response to Marina's words.

Trying to encourage Rei, Marina looked up at his face... but what she saw wasn't a dejected expression, but one filled with resolve.

"Vihera... I will wake you up. Absolutely."

She murmured.
