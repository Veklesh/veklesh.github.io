After the minor commotion at the stables ended, Rei and Isceld, having parted ways with Set, were heading toward the staff quarters.

"Was that Set? I thought he'd throw a fit when we parted ways, but he was surprisingly easygoing."

Rei shrugged slightly at Iskeld, who spoke while gazing toward the staff quarters visible in the distance.

"Being on the same grounds probably made a big difference. Sure, it's a bit farther away, but it's not much different from staying at an inn. Plus, if Set really wanted to, he could come find me anytime."

"With almost no one knowing about Set, having a griffin roaming the grounds is problematic in many ways. We'll need to inform them about that later..."

Iskeld muttered quietly to himself about how to handle Set, but that too ended once they arrived at the staff dormitory.

"I saw it earlier, but seeing it again like this, it's really impressive."

Though Rei had visited places with similar historical weight before, he still murmured with deep feeling at the building before him.

(Well, knowing the castles of the Bestir Empire, I'm not that surprised.)

Compared to the castles he'd visited several times in the imperial capital, the staff dormitory before him wasn't particularly astonishing.

"I suppose so. The Officer Academy is a school with quite a bit of history, after all."

As he spoke, a man emerged from the dormitory entrance.

He appeared to be in his thirties, but his physique was so thin it was hard to believe he was a teacher at the officer academy.

Skin and bones... well, that might be a slight exaggeration, but he certainly didn't look like someone working at the officer academy.

(Well, not all staff at the military academy are necessarily well-trained. There must be administrative staff and such too.)

Still, in Rei's mind, the image of an officer academy staff member was strongly tied to that of an instructor, so he couldn't shake a certain sense of incongruity.

"Nice to meet you, Rei. I'm Samaloon. I'll be your attendant this time."

"Attendant?"

At the words of the man who introduced himself as Samaloon, Rei turned his gaze toward Iskeld.

Iskeld nodded in response to that questioning look, as if it were only natural.

"I'm quite busy with my duties as Knight Commander. Today is an exception. Precisely because of that, you'll need someone to consult if anything comes up, won't you? That's this man."

"As Iskeld said, if you encounter any difficulties, please consult me. I can't promise I'll be able to help with everything... but I will do my utmost within my capabilities."

"I see. Well then, if anything comes up, I'll be counting on you."

Samaroon smiled and nodded in response to Rei's words.

"Yes. For me, too, being able to be with someone as renowned as you, Rei the Great Mage, is a great pleasure."

"Grand Mage?"

Caught off guard by the unexpected title, Rei tilted his head, momentarily unsure what had just been said.

This was because Rei himself didn't recognize himself as a mage.

It was only natural. It was difficult for someone who wielded a scythe far larger than themselves and charged headlong into battle to see themselves as a wizard.

Rei saw himself as a magic warrior.

That was precisely why Samaloon's unexpected words caught him off guard.

But Samaloon showed no sign of noticing Rei's confusion, continuing with a delighted smile.

"That's right. A fiery tornado powerful enough to annihilate enemy forces. I can't even begin to imagine what kind of magical composition or how much mana it would take to achieve something like that. I've also heard you can use one mana-intensive spell after another. And above all..."

"Ah... hold on, hold on."

Before Samaloon could finish his sentence, Iskeld cut him off.

"What is it!?"

"...You really get worked up when it comes to magic, don't you?"

His casual tone, clearly familiar with the other person, showed these two weren't just meeting for the first time or having a few encounters—they were solid acquaintances.

"Anyway. Sorry to interrupt your fervor, but I need to get back to work. Believe it or not, I'm the Knight Commander, so I've got a lot on my plate."

"Now that you mention it, yes. Honestly, I can't believe they entrusted someone like Iskeld with being the Knight Commander. What was Duke Quent thinking?"

"Well, I am, after all, a popular Knight Commander among the people."

Hearing the word "popular," Samaloon sighed and shook her head.

It was a gesture that couldn't have been more infuriating to anyone watching.

In fact, Iskeld's cheek twitched as he shot a glance at Samaloon.

"Understand? Do not attempt to pair a brute-strength warrior with a wizard wielding profound magic."

"Ho, ho... What a delightfully cheerful attitude you're putting on."

"Amusing? Well, yes. I feel like I'm being forced to participate in a ridiculous spectacle. Don't you agree?"

Samaroon turned his gaze toward Rei, implying, "You'd understand, wouldn't you, as a mage?"

"Ah... Well, I'm more accurately a warrior who uses magic. As for mages..."

"Why not?! Magic is the most sacred power in this world! It's not like the strength of some muscle-bound brutes!"

"...You, even someone as mild-mannered as me could get angry at this."

"Heh heh. Gentle, huh? ...That's interesting."

"Well, explain to me properly what you find so amusing."

Unable to bear the increasingly tense atmosphere that seemed to grow worse just from talking, Rei interjected.

"Iskeld's got a lot on his plate, right? And Samaloon said he'd show me to my room. Don't you think we should stop wasting time here?"

Wondering why he felt compelled to speak up despite it not being his style, Rei still addressed the two squabbling before him.

Whether they understood Rei's sentiment or simply hated wasting any more time, the two fell silent and stared at each other without a word. ...No, glared at each other.

They both looked away simultaneously, signifying their breathing was perfectly in sync.

"Fine. I'll save face for Rei and call it quits here."

"That's my line. If Iskeld stays here, the muscle-brains will get infected."

"Hey, don't go calling people muscleheads without permission!"

Sigh. As the exchange started up again, Rei let out a sigh, as if to say there was nothing to be done.

(Still, Iskeld sure has a lot of different sides to him.)

That was what Rei had been thinking while watching Iskeld all this time.

When he greeted Rei upon his arrival at Glacial, his demeanor could be described as dignified. While escorting Rei to the officer academy, he had a slightly more relaxed air. And in his interactions with Samaloon, it was like dealing with an old acquaintance.

People change their attitude depending on who they're with, but seeing it shift so noticeably was quite interesting.

"Hmph. Remember this. Someday I'll make you regret it."

"You too. Don't keep sending out brainless muscleheads like that."

After exchanging words that sounded like nothing but insults to Rei, the conversation seemed to come to a pause.

Iskeld turned his gaze toward Rei and spoke.

"Well then, I'll be taking my leave. The rest, though it pains me, is left to my bones. You'll have to handle it. Needless to say, as an instructor, don't let things like whether someone's a noble's child or a commoner affect your training. If someone raised that way joins the knights, they'll be more trouble than they're worth."

"...Even so. I'm only supposed to be an instructor here until spring, remember? At best, two months... Even if it drags on, I shouldn't be here more than three months."

"I know. But you're a powerful instructor. Your influence won't be insignificant. And besides..."

Iskeld hesitated, struggling to find the words, but Samaloon cut in.

"We won't hold back for nobles, so it's certain to affect the instructors and staff too."

"...Yeah, I'm looking forward to seeing the results. Samaloon, make sure you eat properly."

With only that said, Iskeld walked away.

Watching his back, Samaloon muttered with a sour expression.

"Hmph. That muscle-head thinks as long as I eat properly, I'll be fine."

And yet, unlike before, Rei's tone felt somehow softer. It definitely wasn't just Rei's imagination.

But that atmosphere vanished in mere seconds.

As if to say, "Was that just an illusion or something?"

"...My apologies, Rei-san. Whenever I see that muscle-head, I just end up like that. Now, if you'll allow me to show you to your room."

"Yeah, please do."

Led by Samaloon, they entered the staff dormitory.

Probably because classes were in session. There was hardly a soul in sight.

They climbed the stairs to the second floor.

The room located right at the top of the stairs. That was the room prepared for Rei.

"Here you go."

Prompted, he entered. The room contained the essentials: a bed, desk, chair, a sofa likely for guests, a water jug, and so on.

"Huh..."

"What do you think? I tried to prepare it reasonably well."

"Ah, it's fine. Most of my necessary belongings and tools are already packed in this."

"...Speaking of which, I heard rumors that Rei-san carries an item box..."

Seeing the Misty Ring embedded in Rei's outstretched right arm, Samaloon muttered in stunned silence.

Rumors were rumors, and it seemed he hadn't believed the talk about Rei having an item box at all.

"An item box is truly remarkable. ...Hm?"

Samaroon, who had been staring intently at the Misty Ring on Rei's right arm, suddenly shifted his gaze toward the window.

There, a small bird was visible.

If that were all, it would have been dismissed as just a bird lacking caution.

In fact, Rei had felt a slight unease about the bird—it didn't try to flee even though people were present—but that was all.

But Samaloon seemed different from Rei. Seeing the bird, she let out a small sigh.

"The Headmaster? ...So, he wants me to bring Rei?"

Samaroon asked, not to anyone in particular... no, to the bird. The bird gave a small nod in response.

Then, as if urging them to hurry, it repeatedly stamped its feet on the spot where it stood.

To an observer, it almost looked like the little bird was stamping its feet in frustration.

"Alright, I'll go right away. ...I'm sorry, Rei. I had planned to let you rest today and bring you to the Headmaster's office tomorrow, but the Headmaster has requested you urgently. If you don't mind, would you come with me to the Headmaster's office now?"

"Well, I don't mind... Is it a familiar or something?"

As Elena recalled Yellow, the familiar created by her dragon language magic, she asked. Samaloon sighed and nodded.

"Yes. The Headmaster is quite skilled in magic and occasionally uses familiars like this to give instructions to staff. Honestly, some people aren't used to it."

"Samaroon seems used to it, though?"

"Yes. Believe it or not, I'm a magician too."

Samaroon flashed a slightly smug smile, but to Rei, it wasn't so much "despite appearances" as it was perfectly fitting for his outward appearance.

Unlike Iskeld, whose body was packed with muscle, Samaloon was slender.

He certainly didn't look like someone capable of close combat; with his staff, he was unmistakably the quintessential wizard.

(Or maybe a scholar?)

Considering his exchange with Iskeld, he was probably a wizard... Just as that thought crossed his mind, a small bird fluttered its wings, as if urging him to hurry.

Seeing this, Samaloon sighed resignedly.

"I understand, so please don't rush me so much. Let's go, Rei. The Headmaster seems quite eager to meet you soon."

As if dissatisfied with those words, the bird fluttered its wings again.

"...Well, I suppose. A familiar is rather intriguing. Maybe it's worth hearing what he has to say."

"But you already have a griffin, Rei."

Even as he said this, Samaloon seemed to understand that keeping the Headmaster waiting any longer would cause trouble. He left the staff quarters, taking Rei with him.
