The day after Rei and the others returned to Gilm... After finishing breakfast at the usual spot, the Dusk Wheat inn, they were about to head out to buy some souvenirs for Elena and Ara, who were leaving in a few days. That's when a guard appeared.

"Sorry to bother you, Rei. It's about yesterday. Could you spare a moment?"

"Well, I don't mind..."

What should I do? He glanced at the four women behind him—Elena, Vihera, Ara, and Byune—and all four nodded reluctantly.

Byune hadn't been involved in yesterday's incident, so she didn't really need to come along. But perhaps she decided it would be boring to stay behind alone, or maybe she had something else in mind... Either way, Byune also headed toward the guard post with Rei and the others.

"Grrrr..."

The one looking slightly dissatisfied was Set, who had wanted to get souvenirs with Rei too... and who, despite having just finished breakfast, was already thinking about eating something else.

Meanwhile, Yellow, still sleepy, curled up on Set's back and slept.

"Hey, once we're done talking, I'll make sure you get something to eat, so cheer up."

"Grrr?"

Set looked up with wide, round eyes, asking "Really?" Rei smiled and gently stroked him.

"Yeah. Since the guards are taking up our time like this when we're heading out, they'll probably have food and some sort of compensation ready for us over there. Probably... Like Gamelion or something."

"GRRRR!"

At the mention of Gamelion, Set let out a delighted growl.

...But unlike Set, the guard's cheeks twitched slightly.

Not giving Set any food after this exchange would be rather inconvenient in many ways.

And as Rei said, it was true that there were several skewers of Gamelion meat in the guard post right now.

To be precise, they were meant for the guard's meal who had come to fetch Rei and the others.

(Why is there Gamelion meat here? Could it be... my body smells of it?)

The guard had oddly arrived at the correct answer, but immediately dismissed it with disbelief.

And so, with it decided that Set would get to eat the Gamelion meat... feeling a bit down, they arrived at the guard post.

"Huh? Ranga's not here today either?"

"Captain Ranga has some business to attend to and isn't in Gilm right now. Well, I can't say exactly what it is. ...So, what's up with this guy?"

A figure holding a fairly high position within the guard squad turned his gaze toward the guard near Rei and the others, who was looking slightly downcast... the man whose Gamelion skewers he had been looking forward to eating had been taken by Set.

"Hmm, I wonder what's wrong. I'm not quite sure myself. Though, when I came to the guard post, I did see him feeding the skewers to Set."

"You... how can you even say that..."

The dejected guard addressed Rei directly.

Realizing what had happened from the situation, the guard who had spoken to Rei first decided getting further involved could lead to trouble and quickly got to the point.

This quick judgment—was it because he worked as a guard in a place like Gilm, or because he'd known Rei long enough?

"The reason we summoned everyone, starting with Elena, concerns yesterday's incident."

"Yesterday's incident... you mean that botched attack?"

Elena, whose name had been mentioned, asked, and the guard nodded in response.

"Yes. I would have preferred to explain after calling Guildmaster Marina as well, but I hear she's been quite busy and couldn't spare the time... so I've forwarded the report to her instead."

'Ah.'

Hearing this explanation, not only Elena, but also Rei, Vihera, and Ara each responded with that word.

Only Byune remained silent, simply observing the situation.

It was already a season that could be called winter, or perhaps just a little more... depending on how you looked at it.

For Gilm, winter meant things would get quite busy.

For a guildmaster, this season was just as busy as spring, when adventurers truly began their activities.

And during such a season, the guildmaster had been away for several days.

It probably wasn't as hectic as when he'd gone to the dark elf settlement for the World Tree incident, but Marina still had a considerable amount of paperwork to handle.

Since yesterday, Marina had been handling that paperwork, along with documents related to the guildmaster's handover regarding workers (individuals).

Given Marina's current state, Elena and the others understood perfectly well that she was too busy to spare any time to come here.

Only Byune showed no particular reaction, largely because she had barely met Marina and was naturally expressionless.

"We understand Marina isn't here. So, what about the explanation for yesterday's incident?"

At Rei's words, the guard nodded before speaking.

"First, I want to report that we successfully apprehended the person responsible for yesterday's incident."

"...Huh? That was quick."

Of course, Rei knew that Gilm's security force was an excellent group.

It was only natural, given they maintained order in Gilm, a place where many skilled adventurers gathered.

Still, resolving it so quickly, just one day after it happened, was nothing short of astonishing.

"Ah, well, yeah. Unfortunately, it wasn't us who resolved this matter. It was the Knights."

"The Knights?"

Rei had interacted with the Gilm Knights several times before.

Especially during the war against the Bestir Empire a few years back, he'd traveled in the same carriage as the Knights, so he knew some of them fairly well.

Still, compared to adventurers or guards, his interactions with them were inevitably limited.

Because of that, Rei couldn't quite grasp what the guard was saying.

"What do you mean the Knights are involved?"

"...Are you serious? Forget about you, an adventurer. Think about the others involved. If anything happened to Lady Elena, our current friendly relations with the Noble Faction would collapse instantly. And besides..."

The guard stopped speaking there and shifted his gaze toward Vihera.

That alone was enough for Rei to grasp what the guard meant.

"Well, it could easily become a diplomatic issue."

"..."

Rei's words had hit the nail on the head. The guard remained silent.

Relations between the Kingdom of Mireana and the Bestir Empire were far from bad.

The Emperor of the Bestir Empire was ambitious, but he knew full well the strength of the Mireana Kingdom's forces—more specifically, the capabilities of Rei, who excelled at commanding armies—from the war and the subsequent civil unrest that followed.

And as for the third prince, the heir to the throne, he practically worshipped his sister, Vihera.

If such a sister were attacked by someone... it could easily lead to war breaking out again.

In reality, he wouldn't interfere in her private affairs, but that's something you can't grasp from secondhand information.

(Well, speaking of which, if it got out that I was unconscious for nearly two months over the Ambris incident... especially if it got out that it happened while I was with her... who knows what she'd say the next time we met?)

As these thoughts ran through his mind, an unpleasant premonition suddenly struck Rei, and he changed the subject.

"So, who the hell did that, anyway? I'm not exactly short on targets, but..."

Rei asked the guard in a slightly mocking tone.

Everyone in the carriage back then had reasons why it wouldn't be strange if they were attacked.

Even Ara, the coachman, could have been a target, given his noble status.

But there's a huge difference between being someone who could be targeted and actually being targeted.

Everyone in that carriage possessed skills above a certain level... no, skills worthy of being called first-rate.

The mere presence of one such individual lowers the success rate of an attack. So who would be foolish enough to launch an assault against multiple skilled individuals, starting with Rei?

In response to Rei's question, the guard's expression hardened slightly as he uttered the name of the mastermind.

"Viscount Linus Marnino."

You know the name, don't you?

Rei was implicitly asked this as the guard's gaze fell on him, but his response was merely a tilt of his head.

And it wasn't a feigned look; it was genuine confusion, as if he truly didn't know.

The guard who had been listening to Rei was a seasoned veteran.

He was confident he could spot someone trying to hide something unless they were exceptionally skilled.

Especially since he'd known Rei for quite some time, he understood that Rei was not someone who excelled at such underhanded tricks.

That's precisely why he couldn't hide his surprise at Rei's attitude.

"You know, right?"

"No, I don't think I've heard anything about it."

Rei said it so naturally that even the guard wondered for a moment if he genuinely didn't know.

But according to information coming down from above, Linus had tried to request a targeted hit on Rei and been refused. As a result, he suffered significant losses and harbored resentment, which led to yesterday's crime.

If that information was accurate, then it meant Rei was the one covering something up.

"Really? You genuinely don't know?"

"Ah... well. Now that you mention it, I do recall hearing something..."

Seeing Rei's attitude, the guard probably judged he wasn't trying to hide anything. The guard opened his mouth, looking slightly exasperated.

"According to our records, you met with Viscount Marnino just recently, didn't you?"

"...When?"

"Before you left Gilm for the dungeon. Apparently you tried to request a specific job and were turned down."

"Ah!"

Once Rei put it that way, it wasn't hard for Rei to recall Linus.

Rei wouldn't normally make such a gaffe, but this time, progress on the Vihera matter had come immediately after meeting Linus. The shock of that must have made him forget completely.

"You really forgot... He's a noble, you know?"

"Nah, a lot happened after that. So, that noble Linus ended up holding a grudge over getting turned down and hired those guys yesterday?"

"That's the gist. But apparently, he went to great lengths to cover his tracks. He used several middlemen, and what's more, he hired mercenaries, not adventurers."

"...I see."

Mercenaries and adventurers are similar, yet fundamentally different.

Mercenaries were those who participated in acts involving people, like war... or at the very least, conflicts.

In the sense of participating in war, adventurers are no different, so perhaps mercenaries could even be called a subcategory of adventurers.

"But even mercenaries, if not as much as adventurers, must have heard rumors about Rei, right? There aren't that many people who stand out this much."

Vihera asked, looking puzzled.

Rei's partner, Set, and the weapon Rei favors, the Death Scythe. Plus his recently famous twin-spear style using the Twilight Spear.

There's plenty of information to recognize Rei as Rei.

While he often doesn't carry the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear in town, the existence of Set is impossible to hide.

If you understood who Set was, it was obvious how suicidal that would be.

"Looks like they gathered a bunch of people completely clueless about that sort of thing. They probably never seriously thought they could harm Rei. Just getting to annoy him was enough for them."

"...What am I supposed to say to that?"

All Rei could do was stare in disbelief at the guard's response to Vihera's question.

"I understand how you feel, Rei, but they probably never imagined anyone would find out it was them."

"Then why was it resolved that very same day?"

While Rei had no doubt about the guard's abilities, he didn't place excessive trust in them either.

If multiple people were involved, he couldn't imagine it being resolved in just one day.

"...Well, we've got our own issues too."

As guards, it wasn't exactly pleasant that an incident they should have handled themselves had been resolved by the Knights, even if it was with the help of the Grassland Wolf.

Rei tilted his head at the guard's words, but Elena and a few others seemed to understand the situation. They smiled slightly.

After listening to the brief explanation... In the end, Rei was left with an indescribable feeling, but this matter had been resolved in this way.
