Byune Fraut. A girl around ten years old descended from the Fraut family, once considered a noble house within the labyrinth city of Exil. However, the Fraut family itself fell into decline about three hundred years ago, and their influence within Exil is now negligible.

Even so, she became involved in the turmoil when Rei and Elena went to Exil last year. Ultimately, she ended up under the protection of Bosk Silwa, the head of the Silwa family—another renowned noble house within Exil, much like the Flout family...

For some reason, right now, that very Byune was being attacked by bandits alongside Vihera.

"Byune, don't push yourself too hard."

The fist that swung with those words, reinforced by the gauntlet, easily shattered the bandit's jaw.

It was certain he wouldn't be able to eat anything but liquid food for quite some time. ...Though, to be fair, Vihera had no intention of letting him eat even that.

The follow-up strike delivered just before the bandit hit the ground easily snapped his neck.

"Mm."

Byune parried the thief's longsword attack with her dagger while responding curtly to Vihera.

"Don't you dare show off, you little brat!"

Perhaps Vihera disliked Byune's attitude. Or maybe the fact that Vihera had taken the lives of so many of his comrades played a part.

The thief roared in rage, raising his longsword high...

"Hmph."

With that single word, Byune leapt backward, swiftly swinging his hand.

"Gyah!"

An instant later, the thief's scream, pierced by sharp, intense pain, echoed around them.

Several thieves surrounding Vihera turned their gaze toward the screaming man. There, piercing the palm gripping the longsword, was a slender needle.

"Wh-what... a needle!?"

The thief blurted out in surprise at the unexpected sight, but that reaction proved a fatal opening. Before he knew it, the woman who should have been surrounded now stood before him.

Then came the impact.

The last thing the man saw was her voluptuous thigh.

Seeing that, he didn't even realize he'd been kicked and his neck snapped. His life vanished without a trace.

"Oh dear, you thought you could take me down with that kind of strength? I'd heard there were plenty of thieves near a town called Saburusta, so I was actually looking forward to a fight... But this? The Holy Light Church was more of a challenge."

"Hmph!"

At Vihera's words, Byune protested with a short cry.

Unlike Vihera, who enjoyed combat as a personal inclination, Byune didn't find much value in fighting.

Unlike before, she no longer desperately needed to earn money, so her primary concern was simply eliminating any enemies blocking her path.

This was partly because Byune was originally a thief and not particularly skilled in combat.

For someone like Byune, no matter how close or indebted he was to someone, Vihera's obsession with combat was a bit hard to grasp.

For Byune, who dived into dungeons purely for the money, enemies were simply targets to be taken down as easily as possible.

"Dammit! Why are these bastards so damn strong?! Who the hell decided to attack us?!"

"Shut up! Now's not the time to pass the buck! We gotta deal with these bastards first!"

One of the bandits shouted irritably, and the others surrounding Vihera retorted.

With spring bringing increased traffic on the roads—merchants, travelers, and the like—it naturally became prime earning season for the bandit gangs.

Many bandits had spent the winter holed up quietly in their hideouts, lacking prey.

Adventurers, even without winter savings, could always take on guild quests as a last resort—they had ways to earn money.

But with few exceptions, bandit gangs couldn't pull off making money in towns.

Considering this, they faced a far harsher winter than adventurers.

Having endured that harsh winter, this band of thieves finally targeted a merchant's carriage as spring arrived and they sought prey.

The woman guarding the carriage was said to be a beauty unlike any they had ever seen before, so they attacked the carriage with high spirits... but the result now spread before the thieves was an unbelievable, an unbearable sight.

About twenty bandits attacked this carriage.

But now, fewer than ten remained standing, and among those still upright, almost none were unscathed.

The bandits were completely collapsing.

Within the first few minutes of the attack, they had understood that the woman clad in sheer garments, who seemed to be seducing men... Vihera, was no ordinary person.

Still, she was ultimately a woman. They had made the fatal mistake of being blinded by Vihera's beauty and misjudging the situation, thinking they could overwhelm her with sheer numbers.

After that, the number of bandits falling to the ground increased in just a short time, leading to the current situation.

"Try a little harder. Then, you might just be able to have me?"

Occasionally, words of provocation escaped Vihera's lips.

They were unleashed whenever the thieves tried to flee, her body writhing seductively to stir the men's base desires.

Just like her attire, she danced like a courtesan.

Just a little more... if they pushed just a little harder, they might make that woman theirs.

The bandits were convinced of this, and as a result, their numbers had dwindled to this point.

"Hmph!"

Moreover, among the guards were not only Vihera, but also Byune.

They weren't killed with a single blow, but instead, long needles flew at them, and their limbs were stabbed and slashed by daggers.

Even now, needles fired by Byune pierced the men's hands, drawing cries of pain.

And above all else...

"Dammit! If it's come to this, I'm outta here!"

The bandit who was about to voice the decision to flee was suddenly pierced through the torso by a flying spear, pinned to the ground.

Though he wore cheap leather armor, the spear flew through as if it didn't matter, piercing the thief's body along with the armor.

"What?!"

The surviving bandits let out cries of horror at the sight of their comrade suddenly impaled before their eyes.

However, the only ones who cried out in shock were the thieves and those in the merchant's carriage.

Byune wasn't one to speak much to begin with, and this wasn't the sort of thing to make him show emotion. As for Vihera, he immediately understood who had arrived from the spear attack launched from above.

「Rei！」

Vihera shouted, a broad smile lighting up her face.

His face no longer bore the seductive charm he'd displayed while provoking the bandits moments before; instead, it revealed pure, unadulterated joy.

It was a smile that could even be described as innocent.

Seeing Vihera's smile now, it was hard to recall the seductive image she had projected moments before.

Though, of course, that was only her expression. Her body remained provocative, reminiscent of a dancer or courtesan.

And it was the bandits who reacted with extreme sensitivity to Vihera's demeanor... or more precisely, to the name that had escaped her lips.

"Rei! The Rei who devours thieves!?"

One of the surviving thieves shouted, and the others seemed to grasp who Rei was.

With a speed unimaginable just moments ago when they were trying to capture Vihera, they turned their backs on her and fled the scene.

This was near Subursta, not far from Gilm.

Naturally, they knew all too well about a certain Rei residing in Gilm.

Especially the phrase 'Thief Eater' uttered by one of them—it could only portend an unfortunate end for the bandits.

What was unfortunate for the bandits was not only that they had been spotted by Rei, but above all, that they had no horses.

When they first attacked, they had horses, but the thief riding one was the first to lose his life, thanks to the actions of Vihera and Byune.

The horse had also fled the scene at that time, leaving the bandits with no choice but to run on foot.

Even their weapons hindered their running, so they tossed aside the weapons they held and tried to flee the scene in one swift dash. At that very moment...

Slash!

With that sound, deep gashes tore into the ground where the bandits had aimed to flee.

They were fortunate that the rampage occurred slightly off the road itself, sparing the roadway from damage. ...Though the bandits certainly couldn't call it good luck.

Seeing the ground they intended to flee across slashed open for several meters, the bandits instinctively stopped in their tracks.

Their experience as thieves and the instinct honed from surviving countless dangers made them understand that ignoring this warning and fleeing would surely cost them their lives.

Having halted their flight and momentarily secured their lives, the bandits... the very next instant, found the source of that deadly strike appearing before them.

"GRRRRRR!"

The figure rode upon a griffin named Set.

A Rank A monster—no ordinary thief could hope to defeat such a foe.

Set descended from the sky, blocking the thieves' escape route.

"Where do you think you're going? You don't seriously believe you can escape me, do you?"

Riding Set's back and wielding a scythe taller than himself, Rei presented a figure that seemed a fitting symbol of death for the thieves.

Thief Eater... One of the thieves suddenly recalled the words his comrade had shouted earlier.

Normally, thieves prey on merchants, travelers, and sometimes even adventurers or mercenaries to secure their daily sustenance.

But there are those who prey upon thieves themselves.

They prey on thieves, taking the treasures the thieves have hoarded for themselves, and if the thieves are still alive, selling them as slaves.

And yet, precisely because their targets are thieves, they are thanked by the residents of nearby towns and villages. To the thieves themselves, they are a nightmare.

Of course, just because someone is somewhat skilled doesn't mean anyone could pull off such deeds.

It is only possible because they possess exceptionally high combat prowess as individuals.

The fact that such an opponent now stood before them felt somehow unreal.

No, I vaguely understood that my instincts were probably doing this to ease the fear I felt toward the opponent before me.

Fighting meant certain defeat. Even fleeing was nearly impossible, given the opponent rode a griffin.

"Well, well. Just a moment ago, he was desperately seeking me out. Now he seems a bit cold, doesn't he?"

The voice that reached them from behind the bandits was Vihera's.

Caught between two opponents they couldn't possibly defeat, the only choice left for the bandits was to quietly drop their weapons and surrender...

"Ugh, uwaaaaaaaaaah!"

Believing in a miraculous possibility, their only remaining option was to somehow incapacitate Rei, who possessed the means to fly.

Following the first bandit who charged at Rei, the others judged that if they didn't act now, they'd either die or, at best, be sold off as slaves. They lunged at Rei.

"Fools!"

Rei muttered, dismounting from Set's back to stand on the ground and meet the thieves head-on.

(Better to keep them alive than kill them.)

That was Rei's thought, but it wasn't born from any noble sentiment about valuing life or wanting to avoid killing others if possible.

Partly, it was because fewer people meant fewer potential informants about the hideout's location. Also, handing them over to the guard squad to be sold as slaves was easier with a larger group.

He dodged the first attack aimed squarely at him by the man with the longsword by shifting his body slightly, then thrust the stone tip of the Death Scythe into the man's solar plexus.

The thief wore leather armor, but unless it was made from the hide of a high-rank monster, the kind a thief could afford offered no defense against a thrusting blow.

"Guh!"

Even so, Rei's strike was deliberately restrained. The thief collapsed, vomiting stomach acid rather than blood.

Several more thieves attacked afterward, but every single one was incapacitated by Rei's strikes.

The last one standing was a thief wielding a curved blade.

Seeing his comrades fall before his eyes, he froze in shock and terror.

(That's unusual.)

While long swords and spears were common sights, curved blades were something even Rei had rarely seen since arriving in this world.

He couldn't use it himself, but he decided it would be better to acquire it than destroy it. He swung Death Scythe horizontally, driving the hilt into the bandit's torso.

The thief flew away and lost consciousness instantly.

"That should be all of them."

Looking around, there were no more bandits standing.

Confirming this, Rei held out his hand toward Vihera, who was approaching him, and spoke.

"Long time no see."

"Yes, it has. I'm glad to see you're well. ...I missed you..."

Vihera murmured with a smile, sealing Rei's lips with her own.
