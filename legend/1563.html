Elena and the others watched Rei approach the black crystal, but since they didn't know what it truly was, they couldn't just act freely.

Especially if what Rei had told them was true, the Black Crystal might even be capable of reviving the giants.

None of them present could grasp the theory behind such a feat.

...Then again, since there were no alchemists present, it was perhaps only natural that they didn't know the detailed theory behind magic items.

Still, for those watching, it was purely questionable whether the black crystal Rei was heading towards was truly a magic item.

Fundamentally, what Elena and the others were staring at was, after all, just a crystal... it looked no different than a mineral.

If it were a magic item, it wouldn't be strange for it to show more signs of human intervention.

Amidst these doubts... eventually, Rei gently touched the black crystal.

"...Nothing's happening?"

Elena had expected some kind of change to occur when Rei touched the black crystal. Unexpectedly, however, nothing seemed to be happening.

Truly, it was just Rei touching the black crystal.

"What's going on? Could it be that the black crystal has some kind of will and is afraid of Rei?"

Vihera muttered, half-jokingly, but considering the black crystal's special abilities, it wasn't a possibility that could be entirely dismissed.

The fact that the other three couldn't immediately dismiss Vihera's words suggested they too felt an eerie presence emanating from the black crystal.

Rei touched the black crystal, and over ten seconds passed... Even after that much time, Rei showed no sign of moving.

Finally, growing impatient, Elena shouted.

"Rei, what's going on!?"

Elena shouted, but Rei showed no sign of reacting to her voice.

"Could it be... he can't hear us?"

"Listen, Vihera. The distance from here to where Rei is isn't that great, right? And while there's a chance a giant could be rampaging around... that's not happening either."

Vihera nodded in agreement at Marina's words.

But at the same time, she wondered why their voices weren't reaching Rei.

It wasn't just Vihera; the others must have been wondering the same thing.

What should we do? They exchanged glances... and Marina was the first to speak.

"I'll try using spirit magic to make our voices reach Rei. ...It's underground, so there aren't many wind spirits around."

Marina's spirit magic was almost universally effective when she used it, but naturally, it had its limitations.

For example, trying to use water spirit magic in the desert is difficult unless you're near an oasis or similar location.

Of course, by morning, even the desert might have some moisture, or there might be water in a canteen... so it's not entirely unusable... but naturally, you couldn't use the kind of powerful spirit magic Marina always uses in such places.

Stories exist about casting magic powerful enough to create a tsunami in the desert... but that's another matter entirely.

At the very least, Marina couldn't pull off something like that right now.

At the same time, even in underground facilities with little airflow, using water spirit magic isn't as difficult as trying to use it in the desert, but it's still quite challenging.

"Still, it's not impossible, right?"

At Vihera's words, Marina smiled and nodded.

Told by her comrades that she was certain Marina could do it, Marina couldn't bring herself to deny it.

"Alright. Wait a moment. I'll give it a try."

She announced, then addressed the wind spirit.

Compared to when she used it on the ground, the sensation was considerably weaker, but she still managed to successfully call upon the wind spirit.

"Rei, can you hear me? Rei. What's wrong? Did something happen? You touched the black crystal and just froze. Can you hear my voice?"

Marina's murmured voice reached Rei's ears.

Of course, no one else could tell if he could truly hear Marina.

But precisely because they understood the immense power of Marina's spirit magic, they showed no particular concern.

...The problem, though, was that no matter how many times Marina called out, Rei, who was touching the black crystal, showed absolutely no movement.

Even if he was intensely focused from touching the black crystal, showing no reaction at all was strange.

"He should be able to hear us, yet he shows no reaction at all. ...What do you think?"

"Even if you say that... If watching from afar like this won't help, then perhaps we should move closer to Rei ourselves and speak to him directly?"

Vihera nodded in agreement with Elena's words.

Suddenly, Byune nudged Vihera's body and showed her the small stone she held in her hand.

"Huh!"

"...You mean throw this and see what happens?"

"Mm."

Given their long history together, Vihera must have immediately understood what Byune meant.

But even after being handed the pebble, she looked around at the others with a troubled expression.

"What do you think?"

"Well, now that you mention it... Why not just give it a try? Of course, don't throw it as hard as you would to actually attack an enemy. Just at a speed where Rei might notice something hit him."

At Elena's words, Vihera reluctantly took the pebble.

He couldn't help wondering what if he threw the pebble and it ended up hindering Rei? Still, if it could break this current stalemate... that's why he thought it.

"Ready? I'm throwing."

Confirming everyone nodded at her words, Vihera flicked the pebble with just a flick of her wrist.

If this were an attack against an enemy, she would have thrown it with all her strength, but attacking wasn't the goal this time.

For now, it was enough if the pebble reached Rei.

The pebble flew through the air and safely reached Rei, striking her head.

Though, since he was wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, it hadn't actually hit Rei's head directly.

......

Everyone, including Vihera who threw the pebble, held their breath and watched the situation unfold.

But Rei showed no sign of moving, as if he were a stone statue or something.

Another ten seconds passed in complete silence...

"What do you think is going on?"

Vihera, who had thrown the pebble, asked, seemingly to no one in particular.

But no one could answer; only silence filled the air.

But... Elena finally spoke up, thinking they couldn't just stay like this forever.

"For now, there's no point in us just standing here like this. Since things have come to this, why don't we go down the stairs and move to the fifth basement level?"

"Right. Calling out to him doesn't work, and neither does throwing pebbles. This doesn't feel like a normal situation at all. ...So, we should probably go near Rei and try talking to him directly."

Marina agreed with Elena, and the entire group began descending the stairs.

"In hindsight, it's a good thing we took out all the giants. ...Though I never expected there'd be this many right after shipment."

"True. Though I fought well enough, so it was fine. ...Still, if only I'd been stronger, I wouldn't have had anything to say."

Vihera looked satisfied, yet still slightly dissatisfied.

Elena and Marina exchanged small smiles at Vihera's reaction.

Byune, however, showed no particular change in expression, as Vihera's attitude was nothing new to her.

They conversed with each other, yet remained vigilant, ready to react instantly to any movement from Rei.

Descending the stairs in this manner, they eventually reached the fifth basement level.

But, as expected, there was nothing around them. Only the black crystal and Rei remained.

"...Even looking at it like this, it doesn't seem like there's anything special about it."

"Yeah. I think Vihera's right... In that case, it's definitely that black crystal affecting things. ...Let's go."

At those words, everyone nodded.

Step by step, they approached the black crystal... or more precisely, Rei, who remained motionless while touching it.

But even when they reached right beside Rei—close enough to touch her with an outstretched hand—neither Rei nor the black crystal showed any sign of movement whatsoever.

"Nothing even now that we're this close, huh... Well then, what should we do? Do you think it's okay to touch Rei now?"

"I wonder. Probably not, but let's try speaking to him anyway. Spirit magic didn't work, but maybe our actual voices... there's a chance."

Marina said this, and everyone spoke to him up close.

"Rei, what's wrong?"

"Rei, did something happen?"

"Rei, look at us already!"

"Hm!"

Despite their calls, Rei showed no sign of waking, as expected.

"If even my voice doesn't work, we don't have many options left... but I suppose we need to try touching her directly. Who's going to do it?"

"I'll do it."

Elena immediately spoke up at Marina's words.

Vihera was also about to volunteer, and Marina, who had made the suggestion, had intended to touch Rei herself, but Elena had declared it first.

Seeing Elena, Marina and Vihera exchanged glances... then nodded slightly to each other before stepping back a few paces.

Byune, who was concerned about Rei but saw him as a comrade rather than a man, followed suit and stepped back as well.

Confirming the other three had stepped back, Elena gently reached out her hand to Rei.

She was aware she was doing something foolish.

Rei, who had stopped moving after touching the mysterious black crystal.

Touching Rei like that meant there was no guarantee she wouldn't suffer some harm from the black crystal herself.

But even so... even so, she didn't want to let anyone else take the action of helping Rei.

Elena understood that this was an action born of petty female pride and jealousy.

For now, acting as part of the Noble Faction was necessary, allowing her to stay with Rei.

But that was only for the current situation. Once this matter in the Kingdom of Reburina was resolved, she would have to part ways with Rei again.

Of course, even when separated from Rei, she used the Paired Orb to speak with him every few days, if not daily.

Even so, it was only natural that she envied Marina and Vihera, who were able to act directly alongside Rei.

"Rei... Rei?"

Aware of the feelings stirring within her, Elena gently reached out a hand toward the Dragon Robe Rei wore.

The moment her hand touched the Dragon Robe, she thought something might happen.

That's what Elena thought... but in reality, nothing special happened.

The sensation of Rei's body within Elena's hand was unmistakably there.

It wasn't certain that touching Rei would cause something to happen, but still, the fact that absolutely nothing did occur left Elena with a sense of doubt and a hint of dissatisfaction.

"Elena, how is it?"

Vihera asked Elena, who was touching Rei, but Elena shook her head.

"No good. No reaction at all. ...What do you think is going on?"

"That's exactly why we're stuck like this. ...Marina, can't you do something with spirit magic?"

"Look, spirit magic isn't something you can just use for anything,"

Marina told Vihera with an exasperated tone.

Listening to the two of them, Elena shook Rei's body, which was still touching the black crystal.

"Rei, Rei...!?"

At that instant... Elena couldn't grasp what exactly triggered it, but suddenly, a massive surge of magic erupted from Rei's body. The magic coiled around him, gradually transforming into a deep Crimson aura.

Elena knew exactly what it was, of course.

The Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour. Among Rei's skills, it was an extremely powerful skill that combined offense and defense.

Yet, despite the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour activating, Elena, who was touching Rei, didn't have a single burn.

No, it wasn't just Elena. Marina and the others nearby were unaffected as well.

And so, before the astonished eyes of Elena and the others, caught off guard by this sudden turn of events, the black crystal was consumed by the Crimson magic... leaving not a single fragment, not a speck of charcoal behind. It was utterly annihilated.
