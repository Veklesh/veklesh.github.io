Rei and the others emerged from Treant Forest, but their journey to Gilm wasn't exactly smooth sailing.

"Dammit, this sucks! Why the hell do we have to run into a goblin horde right here?!"

Using his magic-infused Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, Rei's strikes felled goblins one after another.

The blade of the Death Scythe sliced through several goblins at once... along with their weapons—clubs and rusty longswords—while the hurled Twilight Spear pierced through several goblins before returning to Rei's hand.

Set attacked with area-of-effect skills like Fire Breath, Water Sphere, and Wind Arrow. Marina's arrows pierced goblin heads one after another. Vihera rampaged, using the magic blades generated on her gauntlets whenever she landed a hit.

It was a barrage of attacks worthy of being called a mere brush against the armor sleeve, yet the goblin horde that had somehow appeared continued to attack Rei and the others without learning their lesson.

(Why won't they run?)

Intercepting a goblin attempting to attack from behind with the butt of the Twilight Spear that had returned to his hand, Rei wondered.

Goblins were reckless monsters that would charge even when they saw Set, unable to grasp the difference in power.

But even so, if they actually fought and found their opponents stronger, they should have fled without hesitation.

Despite this, this goblin horde didn't flee even as their comrades were killed one after another.

Instead, they charged headlong, as if the word "retreat" didn't exist in their vocabulary.

(Well, maybe they really don't know the word "retreat.")

He swung the Death Scythe wide, slicing several goblins cleanly in half from top to bottom.

"Fight the goblins together! Never try to handle this alone!"

That voice reached Rei's ears.

Looking in the direction of the voice, he saw Fekts giving orders to his fellow woodcutters as they fought the goblins.

Was it the influence of the battle in Treant Forest? Or did working as a woodcutter outside Gilm mean he had experience fighting goblins...? Either way, Fekts and the others seemed to handle the goblins with a certain degree of confidence.

(They're surprisingly good.)

Judging by how the others followed Fekts's commands, it was clear they trusted him.

From Rei's perspective, Fekts had been a man who'd run wild on his own selfish assumptions... that was the image he'd had. But seeing him give orders to his comrades like this made him think he might have to reconsider his opinion a little.

"Well, that doesn't mean this whole incident just disappears, though...!"

He thrust the Twilight Spear forward, piercing the goblin's skull.

"GRRRRRRRRRR!"

Along with Set's cry came a Crystal Breath.

It alone couldn't finish them off, but the goblins, their bodies covered in thin crystal, froze.

Set then charged straight at the immobilized goblins.

Unable to evade Set's strike, the goblins fell one after another.

Given Set's large frame, each strike shatters the bodies of many goblins.

About ten minutes passed... Then, the last three are pierced together by the Twilight Spear thrown by Rei, and the battle finally ends.

"Haaah, haaah, haaah..."

After the battle ended, Fekts sat down on the ground, panting heavily.

While giving orders to his comrades, Fekts himself had been swinging his axe.

It wasn't an axe suited for combat—not a battle axe, as it were—and the strain from the fight in Treant Forest and this battle with the goblins had taken a heavy toll.

The blade was coated in sap, blood, and grease, and its sharpness was far from ideal.

"Come on, get up now! I know you're tired after the fight just ended, but if we stay here too long, we might get attacked by monsters again!"

At Rei's words, the woodcutters, including Fekts, grumbled in protest... yet they rose to their feet.

They couldn't deny Rei's warning that staying here might lead to another monster encounter.

This time, they had managed to handle the goblins.

But... if they had to fight other monsters here, especially ones stronger than goblins, the woodcutters would undoubtedly suffer casualties.

Knowing this, they had to force themselves to get up now and return to Gilm as quickly as possible.

Most of the woodcutters already deeply regretted coming to Treant Forest to outwit the adventurers.

No, perhaps it was strange that, after all they'd experienced, there were still those who didn't feel deep regret.

Regardless, those who managed to get back on their feet followed behind Rei.

Their formation was as follows: the woodcutters gathered in the center. Rei led the way ahead of them, Marina and Vihera flanked the sides, and Set brought up the rear.

Normally, having Set lead would be optimal, but in this case, they judged that being attacked from behind by monsters posed a greater danger. Thus, Set, possessing the sharpest senses and the ability to sense magic, took the rear position.

Several of the woodcutters watched Set's position with a hint of unease.

What they felt was awe.

Of course, as residents of Gilm, including Fekts, the woodcutters present knew Set.

Even before that, they had seen him come to Treant Forest countless times during the day today, carrying Rei. Some had even petted him or fed him then.

But... even so, having witnessed Set in combat with their own eyes, it was difficult to treat him the same as before.

The woodcutters had been forced to witness that spectacle right before their eyes.

That said, conversely, it also meant they were protected from behind by a Set possessing that level of power, which brought a sense of security.

After defeating the goblins, Rei and his group continued along the road.

Fortunately, after the goblin horde's attack, they encountered no further monster assaults. Eventually, the silhouette of Gilm, illuminated by moonlight, came into view in the distance.

(Was the goblin attack just a coincidence? No, even if the attack itself was random, goblins that didn't flee... especially since they weren't led by any higher-ranking or rare species...)

Seeing Gilm ahead brought a sense of relief.

The goblins they had just fought flashed through Rei's mind.

In the end, because they were in a hurry, they hadn't bothered with collecting proof of slaying parts or stripping magic stones, leaving the corpses behind.

(As for turning them into undead... I could just burn them tomorrow when I go to Treant Forest. ...Well, considering today's events, I don't know if I'll even be doing logging work in Treant Forest tomorrow.)

With the Fekts incident, he'd seen the dangers of Treant Forest at night with his own eyes.

Of course, I'd heard reports about the dangers from Slesha, and Rei had been told as well.

But still, hearing about it and seeing it firsthand are completely different.

It truly is the case that seeing is believing.

(Though, according to Slesha, there were supposed to be several other types of monsters besides Treants... But today, only Treants and vines appeared. I don't know what kind of monster that vine was either... Surely it wasn't some kind of Treant, right?)

Moreover, even after defeating the Treant, they didn't obtain any magic stones.

By common sense alone, this means the Treant Rei and the others defeated wasn't a monster.

Of course, there's no way such an existence could be anything but a monster, yet still, no magic stone appeared.

(As a timber supply source, it's undoubtedly an excellent location... but it leaves me uneasy. For now, the forest only attacks at night. But that might not always be the case. There's a real possibility it could bare its fangs at us even during the day.)

As Rei approached Gilm, an unpleasant feeling washed over him.

Of course, the adventurers guarding the woodcutters possessed a certain level of strength.

Given that, dealing with a Treant attack shouldn't be too difficult.

At the very least, they shouldn't be reduced to running around helplessly like Fekts and the others.

...Though, if they were focused solely on logging for the extra reward, assuming no monsters appeared, that would be a different story.

While these thoughts swirled, Rei and the others finally arrived before the main gate.

"Hey there, it's Rei! I've brought back the woodcutters who went into Treant Forest! Open the gate!"

Shouting that from outside, the main gate opened immediately.

The swift opening was likely because the guard who had dealt with Rei when they left Gilm was present.

Using documents bearing not only Guildmaster Walker's signature but also Lord Daskar's seal, this response was only natural.

However, the gate didn't swing open fully like it did during the day. It only opened just enough for a person... no, for Set to squeeze through.

Considering it as a monster countermeasure, this was a perfectly reasonable precaution.

Without a single complaint, they all entered Gilm.

Once everyone was inside Gilm, the doors closed immediately.

No monsters appeared, but nights in the frontier saw all manner of monsters moving about actively.

Considering that, the guards' actions deserved praise, not criticism.

"We're here..."

The moment they entered Gilm, one of the woodcutters let out a sigh of relief from deep within and sat down on the floor.

It seemed somewhat pathetic for such a sturdy man, a lumberjack by trade, to do... but no one laughed at him.

Even for adventurers, operating outside the Gilm at night was intensely nerve-wracking, draining both mentally and physically.

Considering that, it was hardly surprising the woodcutters were utterly exhausted after facing death in Treant Forest at the hands of monsters, only to be attacked by a goblin horde afterward.

Physically, they might be tougher than the average Gilm resident, but ultimately, they were still ordinary people.

Of course, they could be attacked by monsters while cutting wood, but in those cases, they had adventurers as guards.

In truth, they rarely had to handle such situations entirely on their own.

"...So, what do we do now? Should we take them to the guild?"

Vihera asked Rei, looking at the lumberjacks slumped on the ground.

Rei hesitates slightly at the question.

He had accepted this job as an emergency request from Kenny.

But she hadn't been told at all where to take the lumberjacks she'd brought back.

"The guild should be fine, right? We need to complete the paperwork since the request was successfully finished."

Marina's words carried deep persuasive weight, coming from the former guildmaster.

"Yeah, that's probably the quickest way. ...Let's go!"

At Rei's call, the woodcutters mustered their last strength and stood up, their bodies exhausted.

To begin with, the woodcutters, including Fekts, had been working as usual during the day... no, they'd been working just as hard as the adventurers, who'd left their guards behind to focus on logging.

After finishing their daytime work and returning to Gilm, they took a brief rest before heading straight back to Trent Forest to resume logging. During that work, they were attacked by treants and vine monsters, facing mortal danger.

Even after being saved by Rei, they were attacked by goblins and moved while constantly anxious about when other monsters might strike.

It would be strange if he weren't exhausted by now.

But... even so, Fekts summoned every ounce of physical and mental strength left in him to stand up.

"Come on, everyone. We're almost home where we can rest. Give it your last bit of strength!"

At Fekts' words, all the woodcutters rose to their feet.

Even if Fekts had made several mistakes during this incident, it seemed undeniable that he remained the central figure among the young woodcutters.

This fact surprised Rei just a little.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn't be strange for them to harbor resentment toward Fekts for dragging them into such a situation.

...Though to be fair, Fekts did invite them, but since they chose to go along, it was closer to resentment.

Regardless, seeing everyone rise at Fekts' command, Rei and the others exchanged a few words with the guard before setting off toward the guild.

The guard watched them go, silently hoping no further trouble would arise.
