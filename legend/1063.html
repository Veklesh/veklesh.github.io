While eating, a sudden pulsation arose.

This pulsation had also occurred when Rei first entered this settlement, which is precisely why he didn't panic this time when he felt it.

"Whoa! What... what was that just now? Huh? Wait... did something just happen? Like... thump!"

Earth, feeling the World Tree's pulse for the first time, frantically looked around.

It wasn't just Earth. Poro, who had been pulling meat off a skewer right beside Earth, reacted the same way.

"Poloo!? Pololooo!?"

Polo frantically looked around, still holding the meat in his hand.

But seeing that everyone else, aside from himself and Earth, seemed startled but not quite as flustered, he relaxed a little and shook his nine tails.

Leaving the confused human and fox to their first pulsation, Rei and the others exchanged glances.

"You felt that, right?"

"Of course we did. Honestly, if anyone didn't feel that pulse, that would be the real problem."

The first to respond to Rei's question was Vihera , who placed the bread she was holding onto a plate.

Elena, Marina, and Ara nodded silently.

Only Byune nodded without pausing her hand mid-salad bite.

"Do you know what happened?"

"...No, we can't tell here. We need to go to the World Tree. But that pulse just now... it felt kind of like... well, it was similar to the pulse when Rei first entered the settlement, yet different somehow."

"Different?"

While Rei understood the pulse as a pulse, he couldn't quite grasp the difference.

No, it wasn't just Rei. The others felt the same way.

The only one among them who truly understood it was Marina, whose bloodline held a deep connection to the World Tree.

"Anyway, to the World Tree...!?"

Let's go. Just as Rei was about to say that, he toppled the chair he was sitting on and leapt backward.

Those around him wondered why Rei had suddenly done such a thing, but Rei had no time to answer them.

Because he felt that gaze again—the one he'd been sensing repeatedly since arriving at this settlement.

Under normal circumstances, he wouldn't have paid it much mind. Well, completely ignoring it was impossible, but he certainly wouldn't have reacted this sensitively.

But... now, at this moment. Feeling those eyes upon him at the very instant the World Tree's pulse struck the settlement—he couldn't dismiss it as coincidence.

Even so, the reason I didn't draw the Death Scythe from Misty Ring was probably because the gaze... or rather, the consciousness directed at me, held no trace of hostility.

"Rei? What's wrong? All of a sudden..."

Seeing Rei kick his chair back and assume a combat stance, Vihera asked, though her own gaze darted frantically around the room.

The same was true for Elena, Marina, Ara, Byune, and even Earth.

Despite his appearance, Rei was an adventurer known by the Crimson epithet.

The fact that Rei was acting like this now meant something was definitely up.

Everyone present understood that.

Each rose from their chair, ready to react instantly to whatever might happen.

Even Set, who had been eating a large chunk of meat, adjusted his posture so he could immediately back up Rei if anything happened.

Thump. As if timed perfectly, the pulsation occurred once more.

But this pulse felt distinctly different from the previous ones. And above all...

"What the hell is that?"

It was Rei who murmured this.

At the end of his gaze... a sphere of light had appeared in the corner of the garden, seemingly out of nowhere.

In the night, it might have seemed like a fantastical sight.

But... this orb appearing now was clearly connected to the pulsation and the gaze Rei had sensed.

(But I still don't sense any hostility towards me... right?)

Watching the orb flicker irregularly before him, Rei half-instinctively understood it held no hostility toward him. He simply walked closer.

"Hey, is that okay?"

In the silence, only Earth spoke up.

Everyone shared the same concern for Rei's safety, yet they held their words back precisely because of their trust in Rei as a person.

The difference between those who'd known Rei for quite some time and Earth, who'd only met him less than a week ago, was starkly evident.

That difference was starkly evident.

"Don't worry. He'll probably be fine."

After telling Earth that, Rei slowly... very slowly approached the sphere of light.

All the while ready to respond immediately if anything unexpected happened.

The light sphere didn't take any particular action toward Rei as he approached.

It simply waited for Rei to approach, its light flickering.

And then... Amidst the silence that had fallen over everyone, including Earth, Rei reached out... and touched the orb.

Thump. The pulsation began again.

But this pulse was vastly different from the previous ones.

What radiated from the point of contact was a pure plea for help. And the terror of a life threatened.

It wasn't a clear transmission of will, but more like a vivid image itself being forced into his mind.

"Huh!? Haa, haa, haa..."

Releasing his grip on the light sphere, Rei exhaled roughly.

「Rei！？」

Seeing Rei like this, Elena started to draw Mirage, but Rei quickly reached out, signaling with his body language that he was fine.

"I'm fine! ...I'm fine. It was just my first time experiencing that sensation, so I was a little startled."

"Are you really okay?"

Nodding to Elena's concerned question, Rei turned his gaze back to the light sphere before him and spoke.

"You... are connected to the World Tree, aren't you?"

At Rei's words, spoken with conviction, the orb flickered.

Perhaps pleased that its nature had been understood, the flickering became quite intense. So intense, in fact, that it hurt Rei and the others' eyes to look at it.

"Calm down."

At Rei's words, spoken with slightly narrowed eyes, the orb's flickering subsided.

Though they couldn't exchange words, Rei and the orb clearly understood each other.

Rei and the ball of light attracted different reactions from those watching around them.

Marina, who had a particularly deep connection to the World Tree, reacted most intensely.

Because of her bloodline, she was certain the light sphere now before Rei was connected to the World Tree.

(But I've never heard of such a light sphere appearing before... Does that mean this is the first instance?)

Though her mind was filled with questions, Marina gave nothing away.

This restraint likely came from her years of experience as a guildmaster.

"So, you want me to save you from something, is that it?"

The orb flickered in response to Rei's muttered words.

"...Something dangerous is threatening the World Tree. That's correct, isn't it?"

Another flicker.

Since the orb couldn't speak, communication wasn't always smooth.

Still, thanks to the image flashed through his mind earlier, Rei understood that some crisis was indeed approaching the World Tree.

"Understood. There's no point standing here talking about it. Let's head to the World Tree."

Saying only that, Rei turned and began walking toward the garden exit.

Set followed after Rei, while Elena, Marina, Vihera, Ara, and Byune entered the house without another word, seeking their equipment.

Only Earth watched them silently. Realizing he was the only one left in the garden, he immediately headed for the house Rei was borrowing.

He couldn't accept being left behind alone. More than anything, seeing the communication between Rei and the light orb, he understood this settlement was fraught with danger. He also realized something was amiss with the World Tree visible from their current location.

It wasn't exactly a debt of gratitude for lodging and a meal, but still, for Earth, this Dark Elf settlement was a place that had welcomed him warmly.

Even if it stemmed from the premise of a request from Marina's acquaintance, remembering how they'd been received by the exclusive village, he felt nothing but gratitude. He didn't want to abandon this place just because it was dangerous.

(As someone aiming to be a hero, I couldn't do that here, and more than anything, I didn't want to. I don't know how much use I am right now, but...)

Becoming a Rank C adventurer at Earth's age was undeniably impressive.

But everyone here was stronger than Earth. Normally, one would think getting involved in this matter with Earth's current strength would be nothing short of suicide.

Even so, Earth headed for the house Rei was borrowing to retrieve his weapons.

His partner, Polo, was on his left shoulder. Even if his own attacks were ineffective, Polo's lightning strikes could still be a combat asset.

(Plus, his magic reflection ability held the potential for a sudden reversal.)

Running towards Rei's rented house, Earth cast his usual look of trust towards his partner perched on his left shoulder.

「Set！」

"Gurrrrrrr!"

While Earth was heading toward the house Rei was borrowing, Rei was leaping onto Set's back and racing through the settlement.

Rei chose to ride Set rather than run because it was faster, and no one tried to stop him.

Everyone in the settlement was still shaken by the pulsation that had occurred earlier.

(I could move faster using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour... but I can't exactly use that inside the settlement.)

As the scenery flowed by before his eyes, Rei fixed his gaze on the World Tree, visible even from here.

Then, he suddenly felt something off when comparing it to the World Tree he'd seen the day before.

(What is it? ...Something's...)

Beside Rei, who felt this question, the light sphere still floated, flickering.

Set's running speed was quite fast, but keeping up with this pace was no problem at all for the light sphere flying through the air.

Rei shifted his gaze to the orb, then back to the World Tree, and finally realized what had been causing the sense of unease.

"The top... it's withered!?"

At Rei's words, the orb flashed intensely as if in agreement.

...Just as he said, the leaves growing on the upper parts of the World Tree had visibly begun to wither.

"Gurururuu, Gurururuu!"

Set growled, increasing his running speed even further.

The fact that the World Tree was withering was, of course, visible to the Dark Elves as well... and, naturally, they were shocked.

"No... the World Tree..."

"Why?! Yesterday it was weak, but still alive! In fact, it was definitely stronger than before! So why... why?!"

"It's over... This settlement is finished..."

The Dark Elves cried out in despair at the change in the World Tree, their spiritual anchor.

The shock was all the greater precisely because it had been so close to full recovery just once.

And then... even more despair struck.

"Enemy... Monsters are attacking!"

The dark elf's voice, filled with utter shock, echoed around them.

Those who heard the words looked toward the edge of the settlement, momentarily unsure what had been said.

There, indeed, were monsters.

Goblins, kobolds, orcs—monsters that had abnormally proliferated between the barrier's protective field and the confusion field—along with many other monsters.

Every single one of those monsters, as if they had lost all reason, was smashing the weapons they held into the barrier.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn't be unusual for fights to break out between monsters.

But now, it was as if the other monsters didn't exist, as if only they themselves existed in this world. They were relentlessly attacking the barrier in front of them.

"Monsters?! Damn it, troublesome creatures at the worst possible time... Why now of all times?"

On Set's back, Rei frowned.

For a moment, Rei hesitated over which to prioritize... but the light sphere flying beside him began to flicker violently.

It flickered as if indicating which to prioritize.

"Elena, Vihera, and Marina are there. The monsters should be fine left to them... Set, the World Tree! Go to the World Tree!"

"GRRRR!"

In the end, Rei chose to head for the World Tree.

Dealing with the attacking monsters was necessary, but he believed Elena and the others could handle it no matter what. It was a decision born of that trust.

He held the conviction within his heart that since they were people he could trust, it should be fine to leave it to them here.

Seeing Rei like this, the orb of light flickered violently.

Riding on Set's back, Rei, along with the orb, headed straight for the World Tree.

Through the chaos and panic of the Dark Elves, relentlessly.
