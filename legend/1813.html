The moment he saw the stage, Rei was surprised by the larger-than-expected crowd gathered there.

After all, what was about to take place was merely a mock battle.

In fact, with food stalls and street performers around, Rei even wondered if most people were there simply to enjoy the festival atmosphere.

But now that he was actually standing on the stage, he saw that there were not just a hundred or two hundred people, but over a thousand… no, even more spectators.

To be honest, Rei was surprised that so many people had gathered; but even more surprised than Rei was likely the opponent he was about to face in the mock battle.

Most of those gathered were people who had accepted requests from nobles—often out of a sense of curiosity—after hearing they could spar with an adventurer known by a nickname.

Now that these people had actually arrived at the venue for the mock battle and found such a large crowd waiting for them, it was only natural that those unaccustomed to such scenes would be taken aback.

Of course, not everyone was surprised in that way...

A few who appear to be high-ranking adventurers show no signs of being intimidated, even with so many spectators around them and all eyes on them.

They are likely accustomed to being in the spotlight.

However, such individuals are still in the minority.

Especially among the newcomers who have only just graduated from being beginners, many are cowering under those stares.

(I didn’t volunteer to participate in this mock battle, so I’m not going to go around feeling sorry for them.)

With that thought in mind, Rei parted ways with those who had accompanied him this far and made his way onto the stage.

That said, it wasn’t exactly a grand stage.

It was a truly makeshift setup, consisting merely of the stage area being packed down so that the ground wouldn’t get slippery even if it snowed.

It was likely the result of a decision that it only needed to be usable for today.

The people standing on that stage didn’t direct hostility toward Rei when she appeared… in fact, some even looked at her with gratitude.

This was likely because they realized that, with Rei appearing alongside Set, the audience’s attention had shifted from them to Rei.

Of course, not everyone looked at Rei that way; some gave him a defiant glare, while others, for whatever reason, shot him hate-filled glances.

But... Rei, receiving such gazes, felt slightly deflated.

(The person whose gaze I felt on the way here... isn’t here. Or are they hiding their true strength and blending in with that crowd?)

He knew there were a few strong opponents here, but that was about it.

To Rei, it seemed that the owner of that eerie gaze he’d felt on his way to this stage wasn’t among the people he could see before him.

“Rei, this way.”

A man standing in the center of the stage calls out to Rei.

Among the spectators watching from the sidelines, reactions to the words of the man entrusted with the role of referee vary: some look convinced, as if to say, “I knew it”; others look skeptical, as if to say, “No way”; and still others look down on him, as if to say, “Just a kid.”

The gazes of all these people are fixed on Rei.

Although voices openly mocking Rei could be heard, he didn’t seem particularly bothered by them and turned his gaze toward where the nobles were gathered.

There, they were likely keeping warm with some sort of magical item. Despite it being the time of year when the year was about to end, no one was dressed particularly warmly.

Among those nobles, there is only one person… Gaiska alone is glaring at Rei with hatred.

(No matter what, I don’t recall doing anything to deserve this much hatred… Why on earth am I hated so much? Is this just because of that argument back then? Well, I’ve heard there are nobles who never forget a grudge, so maybe it’s not that strange after all?)

While enduring Gaiska’s glare, Rei’s gaze shifted next—likely because the chair was small. It fell on Brewitt, who looked slightly cramped.

As always—though it’s only been a few days since they last met—he has a muscular, well-built physique, and it’s clear those muscles aren’t just for show, but for actual combat.

Also present were Teres, Ranigus, Ara, the Duke and Duchess of Kerebel, who ruled over Anesis, and, above all, Elena.

There were also quite a few nobles Rei had never seen before, and every single one of them had come here to watch Rei’s mock battle.

“Well then, Rei. First, let me introduce Set.”

Prompted by the words of the referee—or perhaps I should say the emcee—Rei calls over Set, who is waiting a short distance away.

“Set,” he said. Just by hearing his name, Set approached as if he had sensed exactly what Rei wanted.

Among the spectators surrounding them, some who were seeing Set for the first time were so frightened they couldn’t even speak.

But those who had seen him while Rei was touring Anesis with Set likely understood that he was docile. They showed no signs of being particularly frightened.

“Everyone, take a look. This monster is a Rank A monster… no, actually, the Guild has certified it as equivalent to Rank S due to its rarity—it’s the griffin, Set!”

The announcer’s words echoed throughout the venue… and the audience, who had been making a racket just seconds ago, fell silent.

It was only natural. Set had just been introduced here as a monster equivalent to Rank S.

And since he was introduced as an S-rank equivalent monster, it was only natural for them to be surprised.

Even a Rank A monster is something an adventurer might encounter once in a lifetime, if at all.

For the audience—who weren’t adventurers at all—it might as well have been a creature straight out of a fairy tale.

For that very reason, to be honest, even when told that Set was a Rank S-equivalent monster, it just didn’t feel real.

It’s fine for now, but as time goes on, everyone will likely start to react with genuine surprise at the phrase “Rank S-equivalent.”

Just as Rei was thinking this, the male host spoke up, as if he had read her mind.

“But please rest assured. As you can see, Set has a very friendly personality as the familiar of this adventurer… Rei of Crimson! …Would you mind if I pet him?”

Addressing the audience, the male host asked Rei.

Although it wasn’t evident in his voice or expression, Rei noticed that the host’s legs were trembling slightly.

The male host must also feel a sense of dread at the thought of petting a high-rank monster like Set.

Yet he hides it—perhaps because he understands that if he shows fear, the audience will be frightened too.

In any case, Rei felt a sense of admiration for the host, who, despite his fear, was trying to touch Set on his own initiative.

“Ah, it’s fine. Set is used to being petted, so as long as you don’t stroke him too roughly, you’ll be okay.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Set, who was standing next to him, turned his round eyes toward the host.

“Are you going to pet me? Are you going to play with me?” That hopeful gaze made the host feel silly for having been so nervous.

He knew that the creature standing before him was a Rank S-class monster, but as far as he could tell, it looked just like a dog or cat that was used to people and was showing through its behavior that it wanted to play.

The host gently reached out his hand toward Set.

His hand, extended slowly, touched Set’s body… and stroked him.

“Whoa!”

At the host’s actions, gasps of astonishment escaped the audience.

Even though the host had said it was safe, they never expected him to actually pet it himself just to prove it...

But as they watched the host, those cries of astonishment gradually subsided.

Because they could see the tension rapidly melting away from the host’s face.

It felt so pleasant to the touch that it made one want to keep stroking it forever.

Several minutes passed... and even after all that time, the host showed no sign of stopping. Not only the audience, but even the nobles began to look slightly irritated.

Those gathered on this stage had come to see if Crimson Rei’s abilities truly lived up to the rumors. They had gathered to confirm that, not to watch the host’s relaxed expression.

(If this keeps up, things are going to get messy.)

Having come to that conclusion, Rei addressed the host, who was still petting Set.

“Hey, how long do you plan on doing that?”

“Huh!?”

Rei’s words snapped the host back to his senses. He hurriedly pulled away from Set and addressed the crowd.

“Well, he has a wonderful texture. With a texture this soft and such a friendly nature, I can understand why he’s loved by everyone in Gilm, where Rei is based.”

Despite the large crowd, not a single person misses the host’s words.

The reason he was able to do that was likely because the host was using some kind of magic item.

Unfortunately, it was obvious to the eye... he wasn’t holding anything like a microphone, so even Rei couldn’t tell what kind of magic item he was using.

(But I’d like one. Maybe I’ll check out the magic item shops in Anesis later?)

It was clear that a magic item with a megaphone-like effect would be incredibly useful in many situations.

Of course, it wouldn’t be surprising if such a magic item came with some sort of restriction, but as long as it wasn’t too severe, Rei was willing to accept it.

While Rei was thinking about magic items like that, he was giving a commentary on Set in an attempt to make up for his earlier blunder.

That said, it could also be interpreted as the host being even more infatuated with Set.

“So, if you happen to see Set in town, there’s no need to be surprised. This friendly fellow will purr happily if you pet him. Also, the power of those round eyes is sure to captivate just about anyone in an instant. It’s almost enough to make you think he possesses a charm spell or skill.”

Ah, I see.

Rei, who had been listening to the male host’s words, suddenly found himself nodding in agreement.

Of course, Rei understood that Set didn’t actually possess such magic or skills, but seeing how friendly Set was and how easily everyone took a liking to him, it was only natural to think that way.

“Well then, now that we’ve introduced Set, it’s finally time for today’s main event. As you all know, we have a lineup of the strongest here—including Rei, known by the alias ‘Crimson,’ adventurers based in Anesis, and soldiers and knights who serve the nobility!”

“Waaaaaaaaaaah!”

At the emcee’s rousing words, the audience erupted in cheers.

It was as if they had completely forgotten what had happened just moments ago when he was petting Set.

"Grrr?"

“Is it okay for me to stay here?” Set asked, clearing his throat toward Rei as he listened to the audience’s cheers.

While petting Set’s head, Rei told him to move off the stage.

Set showed no resistance to Rei’s words and quietly left the stage.

Some of the audience and even Set’s sparring partner noticed Set’s behavior, but for now, no one seemed inclined to say anything about it.

It wasn’t that the audience didn’t notice Set’s presence, but they were clearly more interested in the mock battle—the main event of the day.

As for those gathered to face Rei in the mock battle, most felt relieved that they wouldn’t have to fight Set, who was equivalent to an S-rank.

There were a few who actually wanted to fight Set… but those were only the high-ranking adventurers and those with nicknames—those confident in their own strength.

In any case, even as some turned their gaze toward Set, the emcee continued speaking.

“As you know, this is a mock battle. Pre-selected groups will take turns fighting Rei, but since this is a mock battle, you must not do anything that could kill your opponent. Normally, we would need to prepare weapons with blunted blades for a mock battle, but…”

The emcee paused there and looked at the adventurers who would be Rei’s sparring partners.

Among them were some wielding massive battle axes, others with extremely curved blades, and still others carrying weapons Rei had never seen before.

Furthermore, even with the weapons Rei possesses, aside from the spear, it would be difficult to blunt the blade of his scythe to create a similar weapon for the mock battle.

Moreover, since both are magic items, there’s no point in making mock battle versions of them.

“Since everyone has their own weapons… we’ve agreed that everyone will be careful not to kill their opponent. We’ve also arranged for healers to be present, and we have rare potions on hand that can heal even fatal wounds, so the chances of dying are slim. …Now, the group that will be the first to participate in the mock battle, please remain here, and everyone else, please step off the stage for now!”

The male emcee’s voice echoed across the stage.
