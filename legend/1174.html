"The second time... no, this is the third time, flying through the sky like this."

Holding onto Set's front paws, Vihera murmurs.

Far below lay a summer-green meadow.

A little further away, the outline of a highway could be seen.

Though, calling it "a little distance away" was only possible because she was clinging to Set, who was flying at an altitude of 100 meters; ordinarily, one wouldn't be able to see the road.

Looking up at the sky, there was nothing but clouds stretching endlessly.

Vihera gazed with fascination at the clouds, closer than she had ever seen them before.

They weren't rain clouds, but having clouds filling her entire field of vision like this still gave Vihera a strange feeling.

(It's been quite some time since I fled the Bestir Empire... But since joining forces with Rei, I've been able to see things I've never seen before and fight powerful enemies I've never faced. ...Joined forces, huh?)

Her cheeks flushed faintly at the word "together" she had murmured to herself.

She understood that "together" was a word typically used when referring to marriage.

(But Rei's main wife is Elena, and Marina and I are just secondary wives or concubines, aren't we?)

For a common woman, being one of several wives might be a source of dissatisfaction.

But unfortunately, Vihera was no common woman. Though she had already fled, she was originally of the Bestia royal family.

Having been raised in a culture where polygamy is the norm, she harbors no aversion to such an arrangement.

Moreover, if the woman who loves Rei as much as she does were clearly a worthless woman, Vihera might have considered eliminating her.

But neither Elena nor Marina could be described as hopeless in any way.

Ability, appearance, personality. In all these areas, they were above average... no, one could even say they were close to perfect.

And above all, the reason Vihera had no resistance to accepting the other two was undoubtedly because Elena and Marina possessed strength that matched his own.

Appetite, sexual desire, sleep.

Humans possess these three fundamental desires, but in Vihera's case, there existed an additional urge that could be called a desire for combat.

Considering Vihera's desire for battle, Elena and Marina were the perfect companions.

And above all, there was Rei.

(If we could live together as the four of us, it would be fun, wouldn't it? ...But will that day ever come?)

Feeling the summer breeze, she surrendered to a fleeting fantasy, but quickly remembered how difficult that would be.

She herself was fine. She had already fled the Bestir Empire and was now active as an adventurer. If Rei were to go anywhere, she could follow him anytime.

Marina held the position of Guildmaster, but that was all it was.

Of course, the position carries weight, but if Marina truly wanted to, resigning wouldn't be difficult.

In fact, Marina is currently considering stepping down as Guildmaster to return to adventuring and is searching for someone to take her place.

She also has a lineage that could be called that of a World Tree Priestess, but since that isn't widely known at present, it shouldn't be an issue.

But... compared to Marina, who could easily regain her freedom, Elena couldn't simply walk away.

She is the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel, who leads the Noble Faction, and holds the title of Princess General, a name that symbolizes the Noble Faction.

She absolutely cannot just abandon her position and say, "Yes, that's right."

Above all, Elena is the sole daughter of the Kerebel Duke family and holds the position of heir.

(I wonder how long it will take before Elena can move with us...)

Watching sunlight stream through gaps in the clouds, she suddenly found herself pondering that question.

And yet, she never doubted for a moment that Elena would ultimately join them.

"Hey, Rei."

"Hmm?"

Rei, who was straddling Set's back, answered Vihera's call.

They were still not used to conversing like this while straddling either side of Set.

Still, perhaps understanding that they'd have to get used to it if they were going to keep traveling with Rei like this, Vihera didn't complain.

(She's nothing like Hester. ...Well, that's to be expected, I suppose.)

Realizing that comparing Hester and Vihera was pointless, Rei was once again impressed by Vihera's incredible... decisiveness.

"It's a werewolf pack, but do you think there are more than two werewolf leaders?"

"Hard to say. Based on past experience, it wouldn't be surprising if there were multiple leaders."

The packs Rei had fought so far were goblins, kobolds, and lizardmen.

Among them, only the kobold pack had multiple leaders.

(Though it was somewhat questionable that the kobold pack, which usually acted in groups, had been alone.)

Still, if someone said it was just a coincidence that they happened to be acting alone, she could almost accept that.

"Surely... no other adventurers are being attacked this time... right?"

Vihera's muttered words, recalling their battle against the Lizardman leader's pack, reached Rei clearly.

"That time, it was an adventurer party investigating another monster pack that got attacked, right? I can't say it's impossible... but I wonder."

"I've heard werewolves fight in packs, so considering that, it's entirely possible, isn't it?"

"...Grrrr."

It wasn't Rei who answered Vihera's words, but Set.

His gaze was fixed on a spot far from Gilm... a place deep within the woods, roughly a day's journey by carriage.

Though given its size, it would be more accurate to describe it as something between a grove and a forest.

Even though it was a day's journey by carriage, it wouldn't take long for Set, who could fly.

Of course, since Vihera was clinging to Set's front legs for the ride, they weren't moving as fast as when it was just Rei and Set, but it made almost no difference.

"That forest, huh... Rei, what about it?"

The moment she saw the woods, Vihera spoke to Rei.

Rei understood perfectly well what that question implied.

Because inside the woods, a battle was raging so spectacularly it was impossible to hide.

Could adventurers be under attack again? Rei recalled the earlier conversation, but that thought vanished instantly.

Because the one fighting there was a cyclops, as large as the surrounding trees.

"...A cyclops, huh?"

A hint of disappointment mingled in Rei's muttered words, and it was probably unavoidable.

Both Set and Death Scythe had already absorbed the Cyclops' magic stone, so defeating it offered no real benefit.

No, the Cyclops itself was a fairly high-rank monster, its meat was correspondingly delicious, and its materials fetched a high price.

In that sense, it was undoubtedly a tasty prey. ...If you could defeat it, that is.

Possessing strength commensurate with its massive frame, a single swing of its fist could easily snap any tree growing nearby.

Indeed, even now, as Rei watched, the Cyclops' swinging arm effortlessly snapped trees in its path.

But even this Cyclops wasn't unscathed.

Numerous werewolves clung to its body, tearing chunks of flesh with their fangs before pulling away.

Of course, not every werewolf could manage such a feat. Those that bit too deeply, their fangs unable to tear through the flesh, were easily crushed by the Cyclops' blow—as if swatting a mosquito resting on its body—shattering their bodies... and most crucially, their skulls.

The blood flowing from the Cyclops itself, and the blood spilled by the crushed werewolves.

The Cyclops' body was drenched in both kinds of blood.

"Wow... What can I even say?"

Even for Vihera, who relished combat, the sight of werewolves swarming the Cyclops seemed to give her pause.

"Considering the numbers, it seems safe to assume there are multiple werewolf packs."

"Yes. ...But charging into that mess doesn't sound very appealing."

"Really? I thought Vihera would be the first to charge in."

"Listen, what kind of person do you think I am?"

Vihera muttered slightly displeased at Rei's words, though contrary to her tone, the corners of her mouth curved into a faint smile.

She enjoyed fighting powerful foes one-on-one, but she didn't dislike a chaotic melee either.

(She's probably happy about it.)

Sitting atop Set's back, he observed Vihera's expression while shifting his gaze back to the ground.

There, more werewolves than just seconds before were now clinging to the cyclops.

If time continued to pass like this, regardless of who won, they would lose the opportunity to reap the benefits.

That thought crossed Rei's mind, but as usual, he couldn't jump down from Set's back.

The trees growing in the forest made it impossible to pinpoint where the target werewolf leader was located.

Of course, he didn't think jumping down now would let it escape, but still, considering what might happen, he wanted to take down the werewolf leader with one strike if possible.

"Hey, what are you going to do?"

"Well... I'd have preferred to take it down in one hit, but... maybe I should take out the Cyclops first so it doesn't interfere with the fight against the werewolves?"

There was a risk that while fighting the werewolf pack, the Cyclops could attack from behind and land a painful blow.

Upon hearing Rei's reasoning, Vihera immediately agreed.

While he would have liked to fight a Cyclops in peak condition, the current one was covered in injuries and hardly seemed worth the effort.

Above all, while they had fought a Cyclops before, this was their first battle against a werewolf.

Their tactic involved using speed to close in, attacking with sharp fangs and claws, then immediately retreating.

And they did this in a pack. There was no doubt they were extremely troublesome opponents.

But while they might be troublesome for ordinary people, it was different for Vihera.

"Well then, I suppose I'll have my fill of fun."

The words about taking down Rei's Cyclops and Vihera's eagerness for combat.

Feeling both of these things radiating from his body, Set flapped his wings and descended toward the ground.

"Set, sorry, but you take care of the Cyclops. Vihera and I will handle the Werewolf Leader."

"GRRR!"

Rei let out a sharp cry at his words. As the ground drew near, Rei leapt from his back, and Vihera cut the rope binding her to Set and descended.

A height that would normally be fatal.

But as usual, Rei drew the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring, then used the Sleipnir Boots to slow his descent. Vihera used tree branches to slow hers.

Both landed safely in the forest almost simultaneously with Set unleashing a strike from his forepaw at the Cyclops.

The combined effects of his falling speed, Set's own brute strength, and the magic item, the Bracelet of Might... meant that the swing of his forepaw easily crushed the Cyclops's skull, even without using a skill.

Landing on the ground, Rei muttered inwardly, 'Ah...'

The cyclops' eyeball and horns were materials that could fetch a decent price.

However, there were issues: Rei hadn't specifically told Set to defeat it without damaging the head, and the werewolf's attack had already caused some damage near the eye and on the horn.

(Well, can't be helped. The real target this time was the werewolf leader anyway.)

Holding the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left, Rei scanned his surroundings.

There, the werewolves stood on guard against the sudden intruder.

"Gurrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

The werewolf let out a growl that sounded similar to Set's, yet distinctly different.

Baring claws and fangs stained with Cyclops blood, they watched for an opening in Rei, who had appeared before them.

Even facing this pack of werewolves, Rei showed no particular sign of tension. Instead, he even smiled slightly as he spoke.

"What's the matter? Come on. I took care of the Cyclops that was your prey. Since you understand that, you can't forgive me, right? ...Well, it wasn't me who defeated the Cyclops, it was Set."

With a smile meant to provoke, Rei faced the werewolves, who each let out threatening growls as they charged at him.
