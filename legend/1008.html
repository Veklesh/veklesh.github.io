When Rei and the others began fighting in the slums... Ara waited idly with Set in front of the magic item shop.

Originally, Elena had told her to wait here and show the guard the stairs leading underground if they arrived, but the guards hadn't shown up at all.

"Honestly, I heard Gilm's guards were excellent... They're taking quite a while to arrive, don't you think?"

"Grr..."

Grumbling, Ara stroked Set's head.

Whether Set was listening to Ara or not, he simply narrowed his eyes at the sensation of being stroked and purred contentedly.

It had been quite a while since Elena and the others had gone down the stairs leading underground.

Considering they still hadn't returned from below...

"I guess that staircase doesn't lead to a room or anything after all. It must connect somewhere else."

Ara had thought several times about heading down the stairs herself. If it were just a room, that might be one thing, but if it was a passageway leading somewhere, she knew she'd inevitably follow Elena.

If that happened, there'd be no one left to explain the situation to the guards when they arrived, and the guards would be confused.

"If only Set could talk..."

"Guru?"

What's wrong? Set tilted his head slightly.

It was an undeniable fact that Set understood human speech, but it was difficult for him to convey his thoughts to humans.

"Nothing. ...Still, this shop has all sorts of interesting things, doesn't it?"

While stroking Set, Ara's gaze shifted from the entrance into the shop interior.

Numerous items clearly identifiable as magic items were present, and even Ara, who wasn't particularly interested in magic items, found herself captivated by their sheer number.

The axe Ara carried on her back was indeed a magic item called a Power Axe, but there were several others that looked far more expensive.

"It's a bit odd that there are hardly any weapons, though... I suppose that's just the kind of shop it is."

Inside the shop are various magic items that would be convenient for everyday life.

Of course, there is demand for that sort of magic item too.

Particularly among nobles and wealthy merchants, many prefer such magic items over weapons.

That said, considering how many trust their loyal subordinates with magical weaponry, it's also true that many still require such items.

"That sofa, for instance... I remember seeing a merchant bring a similar one to Lady Elena's house, the Kerebel residence. It automatically adjusts to the perfect shape when you sit down and even gives you a massage. I think it cost several platinum coins..."

There were several other magic items I recognized, but some were completely unfamiliar.

"Is that pot a magic item too?"

As someone skilled at brewing tea, Ara was intensely curious about what kind of magic item that pot was.

Still, she couldn't just go using the magic items in the shop without permission, so all she could do was look.

As she kept glancing subtly toward the shop interior, she soon heard footsteps approaching.

Turning toward the sound, she saw three guards standing there.

"Finally..." Ara opened her mouth to address the guards.

"You're awfully late. I thought you'd arrive sooner."

She must have been quite stressed from not being able to pursue Elena. Though she didn't intend it, her tone carried a slight edge.

Even so, the guards bowed their heads quietly, showing no sign of anger.

"Our apologies. A problem arose. We needed personnel to handle it over there."

"...A problem?"

If she'd been met with a confrontational response, Ara could have handled it accordingly.

But with such a polite apology, Ara couldn't very well come across as high-handed.

Whether the guard knew Ara was nobility, or perhaps knew she was Elena's... the princess-general's attendant, his respectful treatment had paid off.

Though, to be clear, Ara saw herself less as a noble and more as a knight serving Elena.

"Yes. Well, the suspect Rei and the others captured used some strange magic item when he escaped. The commotion caused quite a few men to be called out to deal with it..."

"I see."

Ara gave a short nod, deciding it was pointless to blame the guards further.

"So, there's a staircase leading underground in this shop, and Rei and the others have already headed that way..."

"...I see. And you, Ara?"

"I needed to tell you about this place, so I waited. Now that's done, I suppose it's fine for me to go too?"

"Understood. We'll send two more from our side. We don't know what lies beyond those stairs leading underground, but it's strange that there's been no response since Rei and the others headed down."

"Are you sure?"

Ara's expression betrayed a hint of surprise.

She had expected to go alone, so having the guards accompany her was unexpected.

Considering the guards' skill level, she judged they wouldn't be much of a hindrance and nodded in return.

In ordinary towns or villages, guards weren't typically that strong, but this was Gilm.

Numerous adventurers, including high-rank ones, gathered here. Those adventurers held feasts night after night in taverns or frequented brothels in the pleasure district.

Adventurers tend to be hot-blooded and short-tempered, so it wasn't unusual for such individuals to cause trouble.

It was also common for them to quarrel over dividing the rewards from completed quests. Trying to sell materials obtained from quests for a higher price by dealing directly with shops instead of the guild, and then getting into disputes over the negotiations, was nothing unusual either.

It falls to the guards to quell the disturbances caused by adventurers, including those of high rank.

Naturally, their skill level must meet a certain standard.

It was precisely because she understood this that Ara accepted the offer to monitor the guards' movements.

"Yes. Maintaining order in Gilm is our duty."

"Right, then let's go."

The guards nodded at Ara's words and entered the shop, leaving only one person behind.

"Set... no, his size makes it impossible. He could probably squeeze into the building itself, but the stairs leading down to the basement..."

"Grrr..."

Set growled regretfully at the guard's words.

Truthfully, if breaking the door wasn't an issue, getting inside the shop wouldn't be difficult.

And since he was considered a rare griffin species, using his size-altering skill would mean he wouldn't even need to break the entrance.

But with the stairs leading underground, there was no telling what lay beyond.

Sending someone ahead to scout was an option, but given the current situation, it was difficult to say if that was feasible.

"Alright then, Set. I'll go check it out. Wait here for me."

Ara stroked Set's head as she said this, then descended the stairs leading underground with the guard.

"Now then, entertain me!"

Near a shack deep within the slums.

There, the battle was about to begin.

Among Rei's group, the first to charge toward the enemy was, naturally, Vihera.

He closed in on Dias, the man whose face was covered in beard, channeling magic into his gauntlets to extend claws.

Dias, waiting expressionless with his claymore weapon in hand, prepared to meet the attack as Vihera drew nearer.

Seeing this, Vihera judged he had accepted the challenge and put more power into the foot that kicked off the ground... but in that instant, a chill ran down his spine and he leapt sideways, kicking off the earth.

With a sound that sliced through the air, an arrow pierced the spot where Vihera had been just an instant before.

"Huh? You dodged that? As expected of Lord Vihera, you truly are a skilled fighter...!"

Adrius spoke with a tone of admiration laced with provocation, then, just like Vihera seconds before, kicked off the ground and leapt sideways.

Then, just like the hidden weapon arrow he had fired at Vihera, something pierced the spot where Adria had been standing.

It was the tip of a longsword connected by steel wire treated with multiple magical processes.

The only difference was that unlike the arrow Adria had fired, the blade had been pulled free from the spot it had pierced when Elena, wielding the weapon, flipped her wrist, returning it to her hand.

The Linked Sword, Mirage. It was a magic item Elena excelled with, also known as the symbol of the Princess General.

"I'll be your opponent. ...You address Vihera with honorifics, so you must be from the Bestir Empire, correct?"

"Well, I wonder? But really, between that incident in the city and this time... you keep getting in the way strangely. Honestly, it's not fun... at all!"

Adriana pointed the hilt of her longsword toward Elena and channeled magic into it.

But before its effect could manifest, Elena moved.

"Don't think a hidden weapon will work on someone who's seen it before!"

Having witnessed that weapon used in the surprise attack on Vihera, Elena understood that the weapon Adria held fired arrows stored inside its hilt using magic.

And considering the thickness of the hilt, the number of arrows it could hold was two... maybe three at most, no matter how hard she tried.

That's precisely why she tried to close the distance in one swift move, aiming to seize the initiative before Adria could act...

"Oh, what a pity."

Facing Elena approaching with the Mirage in hand, Adria showed no sign of panic, instead even wearing a smile as she waited.

In that instant, Elena instinctively spun away.

Simultaneously, arrows flew from the hilt of the longsword gripped in Adria's hand.

Not just one or two, but three, four, five... One after another, the arrows were released, ultimately exceeding ten as they bared their fangs toward Elena.

"What!?"

Elena instantly swung Mirage, using its chained sword properties to swing it in a circular arc before her.

Every arrow fired by Adria was sliced cleanly by Mirage's blades and fell to the ground.

"Huh... Not bad reflexes for a sudden reaction."

"...What's going on? That clearly exceeds the number of arrows that should fit inside the hilt."

Elena, who had been using the Mirage as a shield, brought it back to her hand and returned it to its longsword form, asking with a puzzled look.

But Adrienne smiled at Elena and spoke.

"Don't you think I'd be foolish to reveal my abilities to an enemy? If you're so eager to know, why don't you try solving the mystery yourself!"

As she shouted, she fired arrows one after another from the tip of the hilt, closing the distance to Elena in a straight line.

It wasn't because Elena's weapon was a chain sword, making close range advantageous...

"Adriana! Why are you closing the distance?! With that weapon, you could attack from range!"

Behind Paul, the magic item shop owner who was holding his shield toward Rei, Zubo shouted at Adria.

Even as he yelled, Zubo tried to seize any opening to hurl the glass vial from his belt at Rei.

"Flying Slash!"

Not exactly countering a counter, but Rei swung his Death Scythe, sending a slash flying.

The slash sliced through the air, but as Paul channeled magic, ice formed around his shield.

The Flying Slash cuts through the ice... but the act of slicing the ice alters its trajectory, sending it flying past Paul and Zubo.

"A shield that creates ice, huh? Tsk, what a troublesome trick."

"I know you specialize in fire magic. And as an alchemist, do you really think I'd face someone like you without any countermeasures? Rei... I intend for you to see hell on earth before you die."

The murderous intent in Zubo's eyes as he stared at Rei was beyond what a mere alchemist could muster.

Meeting that gaze head-on, Rei shouldered his Death Scythe and wondered.

(Why does he hate me this much? I'm certain this is my first meeting with that alchemist. If so, it must be because of the war last spring, or perhaps the civil unrest in the fall... Hmm, which one could it be?)

Beyond that, he had fought assassins sent by the Bestir Empire and thwarted plots before they could be carried out.

To the hardliners of the Bestir Empire who opposed the Mireana Kingdom, Rei was nothing short of a plague god.

Unable to grasp why he was so hated, Rei decided to confront him directly.

"So, you seem to hate me quite a bit... but we've never met before, right? Why do you hate me so much?"

"Because you... you're the one who killed His Highness Kabajid! That's why!"

Zuboz shouted, his usual polite tone blown away, his voice thick with hatred.
