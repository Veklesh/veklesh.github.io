"Well, at least this means I don't have to worry about anything else for now... but what should I do?"

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

Taking Rei's words as a provocation, the red cyclops bared its fangs and glared at him.

Its left hand was clenched into a fist, poised to strike at any moment.

Even just clenched, it carried the sheer power of a five-meter-tall cyclops monster—enough to easily crush a human body... no, shatter it.

In fact, considering it possessed enough strength to effortlessly swing the hammer lying on the ground, Rei couldn't help but think taking a human life wouldn't be difficult.

(Huh? Wait... What...?)

Facing that red cyclops, a question arose within Rei.

Just moments ago, it had displayed what could only be described as absurd healing abilities—regrowing severed limbs instantly, even after being sliced off at the shoulder by a flying slash. Yet, the red cyclops standing before Rei now seemed to be shielding its right hand.

No, it wasn't just appearing that way—it was actually protecting it.

This only deepened Rei's confusion.

Why wasn't the right elbow healing, despite having demonstrated such extraordinary regenerative abilities?

Having his right elbow crushed was certainly a serious injury.

But compared to having its torso severed, it should be a relatively minor injury.

(So, there's definitely something fishy going on here, huh?)

Though seemingly unaware of Rei's thoughts, the red cyclops grasped the hammer handle lying on the ground with its free left hand.

Instantly, the blood flowing from the red cyclops's right elbow—shattered by Death Scythe's blow—stopped, and the bone piercing through the skin retracted back into the body.

"I see. So that's the seed of your immortality."

As if answering Rei's question, the red Cyclops revealed the seed of its abnormal regenerative ability.

Though Rei questioned both the secret behind this high regeneration ability and the act itself, once he understood that the intelligence of a Cyclops was never particularly high, he found himself rather convinced.

In other words, it was likely trying to show off the secret of its power to instill fear in Rei.

"Seems even with great power, you couldn't become clever. ...Revealing your own magic trick like that? That's third-rate magician stuff, you know? Well, I suppose you couldn't pull off something as dexterous as a magician anyway."

"GAAAAAH!? GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAH!"

Even if it couldn't understand Rei's words, it seemed to grasp the provocation, and the red cyclops let out a roar of rage.

Driven by rage, the red cyclops switched the hammer from its left hand to its right, swinging it back as it closed the distance to Rei.

But perhaps its rage was too intense. The blow it swung with all its might toward Rei became an oversized strike.

"I'm not dumb enough to take a hit like that!"

Shouting, Rei, Death Scythe in hand, leapt diagonally to the right... to the left from the red cyclops's perspective.

The oversized hammer swing missed Rei entirely, crashing into the ground instead. Lightning crawled across the earth, creating a crater.

The red cyclops had always believed that even against somewhat agile opponents, its lightning could neutralize their speed.

It likely believed from past experience that even if its opponent pulled off some petty trick, it could still inflict damage with lightning. But the situation was different now that Rei was in the air, not on the ground.

The magic item that allowed him to tread the air: the Sleipnir Boots.

For Rei, who excelled in speed-focused combat, this magic item had saved him countless times in battle.

Relying on that magic item once more, he kicked off the wall of air and swung his Death Scythe, channeling magic power in a triangular leap.

"Power Slash!"

The same strike as before.

The difference this time was that instead of the blade of the Death Scythe, he aimed the handle at the red cyclops' torso.

If it were the blade, the wound would likely heal as quickly as before.

But he judged that using a blunt strike to knock it back would ultimately be more efficient.

...Yes. Not here in this forest, but to the clearing-like area a short distance away, where every tree had been uprooted by the Cyclops.

"Waaah!"

The Death Scythe weighed around 100kg.

Combined with Rei's brute strength and the power of the Power Slash, even a five-meter-tall red Cyclops couldn't withstand it.

The overwhelming mass transmitted through the handle of the Death Scythe.

The sheer mass of muscle, combined with the weight of the hammer gripped in his right hand, meant even Rei couldn't blow the opponent away without a full-power swing.

"Gahh!?"

With a cry of disbelief, the red cyclops was sent flying through the air.

Never in its wildest dreams did it imagine being blown away by such a tiny creature.

A cry that seemed to refuse to accept that fact.

Yet the fact remained: it had been knocked back. By the time the red cyclops regained its footing after bouncing several times on the ground, the surrounding trees were already gone.

It was returned to the spot where it had ordered the five cyclopes to uproot the trees, making the area easier to move around in.

"...Well then, with no trees around, I can fight to my heart's content. Honestly, using corrosion takes time, but destroying that weapon isn't difficult..."

Even a powerful magic item is meaningless if one cannot wield it properly.

The red cyclops possessed immense strength, capable of wielding a hammer with one hand.

But even so, it was merely swinging it around recklessly.

With no technique whatsoever, the hammer swung purely by brute strength would deliver a fatal blow if it connected.

But an attack like that, relying purely on brute force with no technique whatsoever, had no chance of hitting Rei.

(Well, if he could actually wield it properly, I probably would've had to resort to corrosion. But since he can only attack with brute force like just now, it made me want to try and take that hammer from him.)

Being a massive chunk of metal, its raw offensive power as a hammer was naturally high. But what caught Rei's attention most was its effect as a magic item.

One was the ability to generate lightning where it struck, and the other was granting the user an almost abnormal regenerative capacity.

If asked whether Rei could use it in actual combat, its sheer size made it unwieldy, and regular use seemed impossible.

Moreover, the power to grant its owner an abnormally high regenerative ability seemed slightly unsettling to Rei.

(If you think about it cynically, the low intelligence of this creature—likely a rare or higher-tier species—could be considered a side effect of this hammer. ...Well, being a Cyclops, it's actually quite possible it was just dumb to begin with.)

The red cyclops likely judged the small opponent before it to be someone it couldn't afford to underestimate, given the fact that this creature had blown it away like this.

Gripping the hammer, it growled, bared its fangs, and let out a mighty roar.

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHH!"

For anyone who wasn't an adventurer... no, even for a newly minted adventurer, that roar would make their legs tremble, their hearts grip with terror, and their movements slow.

Some might even die of shock from the sheer impact.

It was a roar that radiated such savagery, yet Rei brushed it off like wind through willows, even having the composure to glance around and survey the battlefield.

After listening to the entire roar, Rei, Death Scythe in hand, even smiled slightly as he spoke.

"Well, is the performance over? If you have something more entertaining to show, I might stick around a little longer..."

"GAAAAAAH!"

His roar, which should have terrified and broken the will to fight in anyone who heard it before.

Facing an opponent who remained utterly unfazed by it, the red cyclops finally began to grasp, however faintly, that the being before him transcended his understanding.

Yet, due to his position commanding other Cyclopes, he could not admit it.

The creature before him was nothing more than his prey.

He forced himself to believe it, and as proof, he crushed its body with a single blow, then raised his hammer...

"You're too naive."

With that muttered warning, the red cyclops was overcome by primal fear and stumbled back several steps.

Before he knew it, something was present around the overwhelmingly smaller being.

Invisible, yet undeniably present.

Cyclopes normally lacked the ability to sense magic power, but whether it was the influence of wielding a magic item hammer, or because it was a rare or higher-tier species, the red cyclops could sense magic power compressing and concentrating around Rei. ...It could sense it.

"Gahh!? GAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

Something beyond its comprehension was happening.

Judging this, the red cyclops swung its hammer, closing the distance, determined to deal with the small presence before it... but in that instant, it halted again, overwhelmed by intense heat, and retreated.

Suddenly realizing, the small being before it was now shrouded in something red, gazing at it with an aloof expression.

The only unusual thing was that, despite merely being covered in red, its very presence seemed amplified.

The red cyclops instinctively sensed that merely approaching it would expose him to the heat, and direct contact would likely result in more than just a burn.

If this had been an ordinary cyclops, fleeing the scene might have been an option.

But the red cyclops, confident in its own power and proud of having dominated other cyclopses through strength, could not bring itself to make such a pitiful escape.

"GAAH! GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAH!"

A roar, as if to inspire itself.

Simultaneously, the hammer held by the red cyclops pulsed with a thud.

Then, veins became visibly prominent on the surface of the hand holding the hammer...

(No!?)

What he'd thought was veins for a moment spread across the red cyclops' body as if eroding it... no, more like taking root within it.

"Gah, Gahh!? Gahhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh

The red cyclops must have noticed it too. Whether it was a scream, terror, or the excruciating pain of roots taking hold inside its body, it let out a voice that echoed throughout the area.

But this scream wasn't like the battle cry it had been moments before; it was unmistakably a scream of terror.

"What is it?"

Rei was confident he could defeat the red cyclops before him at any moment if he chose to.

The one screaming before Rei's eyes must be a rare or higher-tier species of cyclops.

But regardless of higher-tier, a rare species possesses strength equivalent to one rank above its base species.

In other words, this red cyclops was a monster equivalent to rank B.

...Rei was indeed a rank B adventurer, but he wasn't just any rank B adventurer. He possessed the strength to go toe-to-toe with rank S adventurers.

That's precisely why, even against a B-rank equivalent monster, he believed defeat was impossible as long as he didn't let his guard down. He observed the red cyclops's agony.

The hammer clenched in its hand was clearly the cause of the current phenomenon, but even to Rei's eyes, it was a sight he had never seen before.

Left unchecked, the Red Cyclops would undoubtedly grow stronger.

But since magic items were involved, Rei wasn't about to let this sight go unseen, even if it meant taking some risk.

"Is it transforming because of a magic item's effect? It's not like it's the final boss of a game or something."

Rei muttered, recalling games he'd played back in Japan, but the red cyclops seemed to ignore such thoughts, continuing its transformation.

The muscles in its right hand, gripping the hammer, swelled even larger, appearing abnormal even compared to its already massive body.

Roots pulsed from the hammer across its entire body, each beat causing the roots beneath the skin—presumably deep within—to flicker with a dull, reddish-black glow.

"GAAAAAAAAA... GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

The light of will vanished from the giant eye of the red cyclops as it screamed like a death cry... Then, as if remotely controlled, it took a step toward Rei.

"So this is the result. Thinking about it, I guess it was good I didn't touch that hammer earlier."

Grateful for his luck, Rei waited, Death Scythe in hand, as the red cyclops awkwardly approached him.
