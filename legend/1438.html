Rei was led to the guildmaster's office by Lenora and sat down on the sofa as Walker, the guildmaster, suggested.

"Sorry to take up your time."

"No, it's fine. I couldn't very well explain this request at the counter, could I?"

"True. If we did, it could lead to all sorts of complications. ...So, getting right to it, could I have the documents confirming Daskar's request is complete?"

Nodding at the request, Rei retrieved the documents from the Misty Ring and handed them to Walker.

Glancing over them, he confirmed Daskar's name was properly written at the end. Nodding, he rose from the sofa and placed them into a box for sorting documents inside his desk.

"Confirmed. I acknowledge the request as completed. ...Still, your travel speed is as fast as ever. I never expected you to return in such a short time."

"Well, Set's here. Besides, it's more convenient with me around, right?"

"You bet."

Rei said this half-jokingly, but he clearly hadn't expected such a serious nod in response.

But before the surprised Rei, Walker nodded thoughtfully and spoke.

"The biggest thing was definitely transporting the timber from Treant Forest."

"Ah... Kenny told me about that. Sounds like it was quite a hassle."

"Yes. There were also some adventurers who started acting unusually cocky because Rei wasn't around, but it was nothing serious, so I don't think it's worth worrying about."

"Getting cocky just because I'm not around... Not like I'm exactly upholding adventurer discipline or anything."

Honestly, Rei tends to go on rampages pretty often.

Most of it was because he was provoked, but the fact remains that Rei caused trouble in town.

So when you say Rei was following the rules, it's only natural I'd be confused.

"Well, I don't think you need to worry about that too much. More importantly, starting tomorrow..."

"Yeah, I'm listening. I hear you've figured out how to break through the wall."

"Yes. It took quite a while, but we've managed to figure it out."

"Craftsmen tend to be stubborn, you know."

While there are some flexible craftsmen, to Rei it seemed most were stubborn precisely because they were confident in their skills.

"That was one issue, but there were also problems with the materials. So, what do you say? Could you assist with the construction using earth magic?"

"Well, if you ask me to. Fortunately, I don't have anything else pressing right now. ...Though I'd rather be working on dismantling the Giganto Turtle..."

"Ah, about that matter. We might actually be able to help with that."

"Hm?"

"The expansion project won't require adventurers indefinitely, and... well, more adventurers seem to be gathering than we anticipated. Some even claim to have come from the Bestir Empire and neighboring countries."

"...From the Bestir Empire? Could it be they've been ordered to come scout out what Gilm's expansion will look like?"

Previously, agents of the Bestir Empire had infiltrated Gilm, causing several disturbances.

And Rei, caught up in those disturbances, ended up opposing the Bestir Empire and ultimately participating in the war.

Of course, with pro-Mireana Kingdom factions holding significant power within the current Bestir Empire, war isn't necessarily imminent.

But that doesn't mean they won't investigate the inner workings of the Gilm, where Rei—a person of concern even within the Mireana Kingdom—is present.

Even if they are not currently hostile, the fact remains that this is a nation that waged war just a few years ago and remains a potential enemy state.

Given that, it's only natural to gather intelligence when a central figure of the Neutral Faction—one of the three major factions within that country, and someone who knows firsthand the actual military strength they possess from the war and the subsequent civil unrest—shows significant movement.

"That's both correct and incorrect. They likely don't intend to openly antagonize Gilm. But at the same time, it's not surprising to think that confirming those movements is necessary."

"I don't really get the complicated stuff, but does that mean we don't need to worry about internal sabotage or anything like that?"

Breaching the wall meant the possibility of monster attacks at night.

If strange movements occurred in such a situation, it would undoubtedly create a very troublesome situation for Gilm.

But Walker nodded, dispelling Rei's concern.

"Of course, we won't leave them completely unchecked. We plan to assign our own people to monitor them for any suspicious activity. ...Though, they're probably aware of that too."

Since they're openly declaring they're from the Bestir Empire, they're probably just aiming to gather information within the scope of what ordinary adventurers can do.

Walker's explanation made sense to Rei.

"Well, I'll leave that to the guild. If that guy tries anything shady with us, that's a different story."

"Hahaha. That probably won't happen."

Few nations understood Rei's capabilities as well as the Bestir Empire.

Rei's name is more famous among the people of the Bestir Empire than in the Mireana Kingdom, where he belongs.

...Though, unlike the Mireana Kingdom where he's essentially a national hero as their own adventurer, in the Bestir Empire he's practically synonymous with terror.

Even so, due to his exploits during the civil war, some aren't all that afraid of him... in fact, some even hold him in respect.

Typically, these individuals were either members of the guerrilla unit Rei led during the civil war or those who witnessed his power up close.

"Fine then. ...More importantly, what's the schedule starting tomorrow?"

"...Ideally, I'd like you to rest for a few days, but unfortunately, we don't have much time to spare. Given that, I'd like to ask you to handle the matter of Treant Forest and the earth magic immediately."

"When do you think the wall will be destroyed?"

"I can't say exactly, but probably within a few more days."

"A few days, huh? Looks like I came back at a pretty good time then."

"Indeed. The craftsmen demolishing the wall are highly skilled. They precisely determine where to destroy it and meticulously break only that section. They possess that level of expertise."

"Huh."

Hearing Walker's words, Rei recalled a scene he'd seen on TV back in Japan.

A program about demolishing buildings in America by blowing them up all at once.

(Well, Walker said they're only demolishing part of the wall, so it's probably less dramatic than that.)

The idea of using explosives to demolish an entire massive building all at once had amazed Rei back then.

Despite being such a spectacular, large-scale explosion, actually pulling it off required extremely precise calculations, a thorough understanding of the building's structure, figuring out the direction it would fall... and so on.

(I don't know what the craftsmen's skills are like, but I'll look forward to it a little.)

Like watching some kind of attraction, Rei eagerly awaited the day the wall would be destroyed.

The destruction of the wall meant, of course, that come nightfall, there would be nothing left to keep the monsters out.

Furthermore, since the barrier normally maintained by Gilm exists along the wall, things wouldn't be the same as before.

Of course, the barrier itself won't vanish.

There was no way they could choose not to prepare against monsters attacking from the sky in the frontier.

However, even so, destroying part of the wall meant it wouldn't function at full capacity as usual.

Given that, while vigilance against monsters breaching the destroyed wall takes priority, vigilance against aerial assaults remains necessary.

"Anyway, once the wall is breached, there'll be no rest. We need to push forward with repairs as quickly as possible. The key to that will be the cooperation of those who use earth magic, starting with Rei-san."

"...Well, I suppose so."

Of course, it wouldn't be impossible to build new walls without leveling the ground first.

But compare a wall built without leveling the ground to one built after leveling it.

If asked which is sturdier, the leveled one is clearly superior.

Also, while unlikely, it's not impossible that there might be some kind of cavity beneath the ground.

The area was surely thoroughly investigated when Gilm was built, but a long time has passed since then.

It wouldn't be surprising if some monster or animal had burrowed through the ground near Gilm during that time.

In that sense, it was only natural that earth mages were called upon to investigate.

(And then...)

Walker looked at Rei before him once more.

Walker himself had never seen it, of course, but he recalled reading in a report that Rei's earth magic wasn't limited to leveling the ground; it could be manipulated with a certain degree of freedom.

In other words, it meant it could somewhat shorten the construction time for building new walls.

If they were to resolve the situation of having no walls as quickly as possible, Rei's cooperation would be indispensable.

"I know this will cause you a lot of trouble, but I appreciate your help."

Rei gave a small nod to Walker, who bowed deeply once more.

The expansion of Gilm was something Rei welcomed too.

Given that, he had no hesitation in cooperating.

...Of course, if he were asked to work for free, he would refuse.

This time, however, he was working on a formal commission, so there was no need for such concerns.

Walker wouldn't dream of letting such a highly capable adventurer slip away from the guild.

Though, that capable adventurer does cause all sorts of problems.

"Ah, I understand. Expanding Gilm's reach is something we should welcome, after all."

"That's a relief to hear. Anyway, I'd like to get back to discussing this current request. Is that alright?"

"Hm? Ah, sure."

Come to think of it, I only handed over the completed request paperwork. With that thought, Rei proceeded to explain the retaliation against Relduct to Walker.

Perhaps because the details were similar to what he'd told Daskar, Rei found himself speaking much more smoothly than he had earlier with Daskar.

"...I see. So the mines too..."

"Did it go badly?"

Seeing Walker seem to hesitate, Rei wondered if something was off and asked.

But Walker shook his head at Rei's words.

"No, not at all. You were just fulfilling the client's request, Rei."

To be precise, the mine collapse wasn't requested by Daskar, but by Gale, who was also part of the Neutral Faction.

However, Walker didn't hold it against him.

He wasn't entirely without reservations about Rei's actions, but he'd likely decided to consider it within the scope of this particular request.

"What about payment for this job? I can pay you right away."

"Ah... no, just combine it with the payment for the Gilm expansion. I'll get it all together later."

"Well, if that's what Rei says, it's a relief for us. Most adventurers would want their payment as soon as possible, though."

Walker's words weren't wrong, but in Rei's case... well, more accurately, in the case of high-rank adventurers including Rei, that didn't apply.

For starters, Rei wasn't exactly strapped for cash, so there was little urgency to receive payment immediately.

Besides, since he planned to receive this reward along with other minerals like fire ore, getting them all together later would be less hassle.

"Well, I'll be heading out soon. I still haven't told Marina, Vihera, or Byune I'm back."

"Yes, understood. ...Once the details regarding the wall are finalized, I'll contact you."

Rei nodded at Walker's words and rose from the sofa.

(Well then, Marina's handling the timber transport from Treant Forest, while Vihera and Byune are patrolling the town. ...I suppose I should go see Vihera first, after all.)

He thought to himself as he walked away.
