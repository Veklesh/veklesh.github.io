"Well, Set. Can you pick up the scent?"

At Rei's words, Set, who had been sniffing the corpse of the assassin brought from the lab in Daldora's shop, hesitated slightly before making a low growl.

Only Rei understood that this growl conveyed Set's resolve that he was ready.

The sun was already sinking, and twilight was falling. In these conditions, they had placed the corpse on a simple board in the courtyard of Daldora's shop.

To an outsider, they certainly wouldn't look like a sane group.

And with the griffin sniffing the corpse, it only made matters worse.

But... even in such an eerie situation, everyone understood this was something that had to be done.

That was precisely why, when Set was sniffing the corpse, no one said a word about it.

"So, what's the verdict? Can Set track the scent?"

Rei nodded to Augusto, who had come to ask on behalf of the group.

Seeing Rei's gesture, everyone breathed a sigh of relief.

After going this far, if it turned out he couldn't actually track the scent... well, that would be hopeless, so it was only natural.

If he couldn't track the scent now, it meant trying tomorrow morning would absolutely fail too.

"And what about the Ryubrand side?"

Rei muttered, shifting his gaze. Daldora immediately nodded in response.

"The letter has already been sent. I also conveyed that it needs to be read urgently, so even if something happens, the guards shouldn't interfere with us."

Though Daldora usually had a nervous expression, she now wore a satisfied smile.

It seemed to mean that the arrangements for this time were perfect.

"Understood. ...Then, Set. I'm counting on you."

"GRRRRRR!"

Set let out a growl, signaling his acceptance of Rei's request.

Just as they were about to leave Daldora's shop... there stood the figure of an adventurer.

It was someone Rei recognized.

The most skilled among Daldora's guards, the one who led them.

"Narsus, are you done already?"

"Yes, thanks to the potion, my wounds are fine. ...Well, I probably won't be able to properly taste food for a while, though."

Narsus muttered with a wry smile, and Augusto, standing a short distance away, returned the wry smile.

It was a sympathy only someone who had also drunk a potion during the daytime attack, and whose sense of taste was similarly destroyed for a time, could feel.

"My apologies, Daldora-sama. Normally, as your hired escort, I should be guarding you while Rei is out on the raid..."

"It's fine. I originally planned for Narsus to join the raid with Rei anyway. ...Besides, even without Narsus, my guards are all skilled fighters. Right?"

The guards where Daldora's gaze fell all nodded back with confident smiles.

"...Thank you. So, will it just be Rei and me participating in this raid?"

"No, I'm going too."

The voice belonged to Zarust, hired as a bodyguard for Augusto.

Rei had half-expected it, but Narsus couldn't hide his surprise as he turned to look.

"You? ...What about Augusto's guard?"

"That's already been settled. Daldora... and her guards will head to Augusto's residence. It's on the outskirts of Gosh, so it'll be easy to spot strangers there."

"But the enemy can disguise themselves freely, right? Then I don't think that'll be of much use..."

"I considered that too. But I already have permission from these two regarding this matter."

Zalust's gaze naturally fell upon Augusto and Daldora.

Glancing their way, Zarust stepped forward, long sword in hand.

Though Zaruust wielded both spear and longsword with equal skill, he'd chosen the longsword this time—likely because it was an assault... an assault on the enemy's hideout.

Zalust's gaze conveyed that no amount of further argument would make him budge.

Even though the assailant had blended into the crowd, the fact that they had inflicted a fatal wound on Augusto was something Zarust could never forgive.

Pure luck... pure luck that Rei happened to have a potion capable of healing even a fatal wound, saving him. Without Rei, Augusto would undoubtedly have died.

It was a mistake from Zarust that could be called fatal.

For Zarust, who owed a deep debt of gratitude to Augusto's father... and to Augusto himself, it was a mistake so profoundly regrettable it could truly be called fatal.

To atone for that mistake, Zarust was now participating in the attack on the assassin.

As if he couldn't forgive himself otherwise.

(Saying more... is pointless.)

Seeing the resolve in Zarust's eyes, Rei realized deep down that further persuasion was futile.

(But, going to their mansion to protect Augusto and Kathy is fine... but what about Daldora's family in that case?)

Rei didn't know if Daldora lived with her parents... or if she was married, or living with a lover. He didn't even know if she had children.

Still, Daldora was the foremost merchant in this town of Gosh.

Rei thought such a person couldn't possibly live alone, but Daldora said nothing in particular.

And since Daldora said nothing, Rei didn't press the matter.

"So, it's decided: these three and Set are going. ...The rest will reinforce the guard at Augusto's mansion, correct?"

Everyone present nodded at Rei's words, which served as confirmation.

Among Daldora's guards, some wanted to join Rei on the attack side, but they likely felt it was too much to ask of themselves to act alongside Rei and Narsus, Zaruust aside.

They showed some reluctance, but ultimately said nothing more.

Thus, Rei, Set, Zarust, and Narsus headed into the night city, while Augusto, Daldora, and her guards made their way to the outskirts.

It was a scene that could be described as nothing short of a perfect example of enemies forced to share the same boat.

Even though it was called the night city, the sun had only just set.

Naturally, the streets were crowded, and with that, Set—who had become famous overnight in recent days—Zalust, the adventurer close to Augusto, and Narsus, leading Daldora's guards, inevitably drew attention.

It was common knowledge in Gosh that Augusto and Daldora were rivals.

So, it was only natural that the person serving as their guard would stand out, walking around with Set.

Surprisingly, the least conspicuous among the three people and one beast was Rei.

This was largely because the hood of his Dragon Robe concealed his distinctive feminine features.

Still, being by Set's side meant she inevitably drew a certain amount of attention.

"So, Set. Are you picking up the scent?"

"Grrrrrrr!"

Set responded to Rei's words with an energetic growl. But... what Rei and the others found after following Set was a street stall selling skewers grilled with generous amounts of spices.

"...Hey, Rei. This can't possibly be their hideout, can it...?"

Zalust said, sounding somewhat doubtful.

Narsus nodded in agreement with Zarust's words.

In this case, the poor stall owner was the one to feel sorry for.

Suddenly, a griffin appeared right in front of him, and then it started growling.

Rei, accustomed to Set, understood the growl meant "I want to eat that" or "That looks delicious." But to the stall owner, who knew almost nothing about Set, it sounded like a threat.

Rei must have noticed the stall owner's reaction. He hurriedly pulled out a silver coin and handed it to the owner.

"Give me whatever skewers I can buy with this."

"...Huh? Oh, sure. Just a moment."

As if he hadn't been scared just seconds ago, the man swiftly reached for the skewers.

You could say this is typical merchant behavior.

Receiving more skewers than he could hold in both hands, he handed them to Set, ate some himself, and then passed them to Zarust and Narsus.

The two had been focused on the impending raid on the assassins' hideout, but when told it was to boost morale before battle, they couldn't very well refuse.

Though somewhat reluctant, they all began devouring the skewers.

Unsurprisingly, Set ate the most skewers, which even surprised the shopkeeper present.

"Hey, Rei. Are we really going to make it to our destination like this?"

Zalust asked, looking slightly doubtful. Rei shook his head.

"I don't know. That part is completely up to Set, so asking me is a bit of a problem."

"Well, if not Rei, who should we ask about Set?"

Zalust muttered in exasperation. Narsus, who had been silent, nodded in agreement.

His determination to fight had been so strong just moments ago that his frustration now felt all the more intense.

Pressured by the two of them, Rei spoke to Set, who had just finished eating his skewered meat and looked satisfied.

"Hey, Set. Shouldn't we start moving soon? If we keep this up, we'll just be wasting time, right?"

"Grrr..."

Set let out a slightly apologetic growl.

Even to an outsider, Set's demeanor was enough to evoke sympathy.

"Come on, calm down. We'll be fine if we get serious from here on out. ...Well, sorry for the commotion."

After briefly informing the stall owner, Rei started walking again with Set in tow.

Behind them, Rei and Narsus followed, still slightly uncertain if it was okay to keep trailing after Set like this, but they pressed on.

Of the group, Narsus harbored the most doubts about Set, while Zarust didn't feel quite as strongly.

This was largely because Zarust had seen Set's power with his own eyes.

"Are we really going to be okay like this? ...I can't quite trust this situation."

Even so, Zarust voiced this doubt because of Narsus's suspicious gaze fixed on Set.

He asked before Narsus could, hoping to ease some of that suspicion and discontent. Hearing Zarust's words, born of that intention, Rei shook his head.

"I think I mentioned this before, but whether we can track a scent depends on whether it's still there. ...Well, we ended up arriving at that stall as if guided by that scent."

"And that's supposed to reassure me? I can't possibly trust that."

"Well, what can I say? Since we have no other means, we have no choice but to rely on Set, right? I mean, we could try the old-fashioned way—asking around to find their hideout... but everyone understands how difficult that would be."

Given that, Zaruust could only fall silent, for it was the truth.

After all, with only Set's sense of smell as a clue, relying on him was indeed the only option.

"Alright. ...But please, be careful. Even as we speak, Mr. Augusto might be targeted by assassins."

"With danger also threatening Daldora, we can't afford to be overly optimistic either."

Worried about Augusto and Daldora, Zarust and Narsus urged Rei on.

(I get what you're saying, but blaming me now... Well, it is Set we're talking about, so maybe it's not so strange that I'm the one being blamed.)

Rei stroked Set, who was sniffing the air for assassins while surveying the surroundings.

Set seemed pleased by the petting, happily checking his surroundings.

He must be using one of his skills: heightened sense of smell. Suddenly, Set fixes his gaze in one direction and growls.

"Did you find something?"

"Guruu!"

Set responded to Rei's words, and Zarust and Narsus, hearing this, also lit up with excitement.

Despite all the talk, somewhere deep down, they hadn't truly believed Set could actually track a scent.

It was almost a flaw in Rei, an adventurer with a nickname, who usually gave off no such vibe whatsoever.

But... as they followed Set's lead, Zarust and Narsus... and even Rei began to wonder.

"Hey, wait. If we keep going like this..."

"Probably, yeah."

Zalust and Narsus exchanged words, and Rei too began to question the direction Set was leading them...

"GRRR!"

Set growled at the main gate of Gosh.

Which meant the assassins' hideout was outside Gosh.
