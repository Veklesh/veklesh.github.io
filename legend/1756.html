The news that Sura and her group had arrived in Gilm naturally became the talk of the town.

Not only had they arrived in a procession of over a hundred carriages, but Lord Daskar had come out to greet them... Furthermore, since word had already been spread that Sura and her group were refugees—though strictly speaking, that wasn’t entirely accurate—it was only natural that they would attract attention as soon as they entered Gilm.

Even on Gilm’s main street, with over a hundred carriages passing through, it was impossible to simply weave through the crowd.

Naturally, the main street was temporarily closed to traffic… but Sura fully understood that they were outsiders who had come to Gilm.

Given that, they needed to be accepted by the residents of Gilm as quickly as possible.

In that situation, the quickest way for Sura and her group to be accepted was to use whatever they had at their disposal.

And so...

“Whoaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa! No way! What the hell is that group of beauties?!”

“No, seriously, what is that? Amazing… amazing…”

“Eeek! Miss, look this way!”

From the carriage currently traveling down the main street, women—all of whom were above average in looks and possessed features worthy of being called beautiful, cute, or adorable—were waving from the carriage windows, walking alongside the carriage, or even sitting on the roof, charming everyone around them.

These were women who had been taken from neighboring countries to serve as both the wombs that would bear giants and as prostitutes to bring in money for Jaya.

Naturally, they were selected primarily for their attractive features, and in this parade-like display, many of them served as the show’s highlights.

...That said, the fact that only beautiful and cute women were gathered here meant that while they were popular with men and some women, they also drew resentment.

Sura likely decided that, even if it meant incurring some resentment, it was necessary to do so for now.

In fact, the effect is tremendous, and she is being cheered on by the crowd.

“Eeek! Set-chan, Set-chan, Set-chan!”

Among them, there were some who were actually there for Set—not the women, but Set, who was following behind them alongside Rei (a man).

However, at the same time, by acting this way, Sura and the others clearly demonstrated that they had a connection with Rei and Set.

There was hardly anyone in Gilm who didn’t know Rei or Set, so the likelihood of the women being subjected to strange advances should have decreased significantly.

……That said, among those who had come to Gilm for the expansion project, many were unfamiliar with information regarding adventurers.

Naturally, such people didn’t know Rei, and if they were unlucky, things could end very badly for them.

(At any rate, this has served as an introduction for Sura and the others. There’s no doubt that the number of people who would think of doing something foolish will decrease. …Though it’s a bit painful that they won’t disappear entirely.)

Walking at the rear of the parade alongside Set, Rei looked around.

At first, many people looked on, wondering what all the commotion was about, but once they saw Sura and the others in the parade, they were surprised and became engrossed.

This cycle repeated itself… and amidst it all, at the food stalls selling snacks meant to be eaten on the go, vendors were desperately raising their voices to attract customers, knowing this was their chance to shine.

“Hey, wait, Rei! What’s this?!”

Startled by the sudden sound of his name, he looked up to see Fron—the man he’d teamed up with a few days ago for the Gamelion hunt—staring at him with a look of astonishment.

But if Rei said anything in this situation, it would undoubtedly draw attention.

Rei simply gave Fron a slight nod and kept walking without saying another word.

Seeing Rei like that, Fron must have sensed something too. Though slightly bewildered, he sighed as if to say, “Oh well,” and without another word, he watched Rei… and the parade pass by.

They continued down the main street... and as they eventually reached the area under construction, the number of people watching the parade naturally began to dwindle.

By this point, the majority of people were workers on the expansion project, so that was only natural.

Since it wasn’t Sura’s intention to disturb those working, the parade came to an end.

...Though, even though they called it a parade, in reality they’d just been moving down the main street without any music or anything.

Rei wasn’t sure if it could really be called a parade, though.

For Rei, who had lived in Japan, the word “parade” inevitably conjured up images of something much more extravagant.

Like when the local baseball team wins the national championship, or a local player shines at a world tournament... that sort of thing.

“Well then, I’ll show you to your accommodations.”

Following Sarajiros’ instructions, Sura and the others moved along quietly, unlike before.

Mind you, this was a group of nearly a thousand people, the vast majority of whom were women.

Since the majority of those working on the expansion project were men, it was only natural that their eyes would be drawn to such a group.

If it were just a matter of being captivated, there would be no problem.

But… several people ended up dropping the luggage they were carrying onto their own feet.

In addition, some dropped their belongings onto the feet of their coworkers, and this even led to arguments.

“Um, is it okay to keep going like this?”

It must have been because she could see that kind of exchange happening. Sura asked Sarajiros, but he simply nodded, indicating that it was no problem at all.

“That’s true. But if we linger here, we’ll inevitably draw everyone’s attention. Given that, I think we should move on as quickly as possible.”

Hearing that, Sura agreed… and so they made their way through the crowd of people busy with their various tasks.

Eventually, they arrive at a spot where the construction work is already mostly finished, and naturally, there are fewer workers there.

Among the group, there were quite a few who didn’t like being the center of attention… no, who were actually quite uncomfortable with it, and that’s why some of them felt relieved.

“It’s quite… different from what I expected.”

“Is that so? I’m used to Gilm being this kind of place, so I don’t really feel that way.”

In response to Sura’s words, her boyfriend, Rocks, replied.

His slight grumpiness was likely because Sura had been treated almost like a sideshow attraction.

That said, Rocks was the only one in a bad mood; Sura herself didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

In any case, the group arrived at a nearly deserted spot where many buildings stood in a row.

However, since it was impossible to build homes for all a thousand people in such a short time, the area consisted of several structures that could best be described as large huts.

Despite their appearance, these huts—built using wood from the Treant Forest—were far sturdier than they looked.

The lumber harvested from the magic-enhanced Treant Forest had excellent insulation properties, so the warmth inside barely escaped.

Furthermore, the craftsmen who built the huts spread out before Sura and the others were, naturally, skilled carpenters, so there were no drafts or snow falling through gaps into the huts.

“There might not be quite enough huts for everyone… but still, it’s better than freezing to death, and above all, it’s vastly superior to the people who come here to work and have to sleep in shabby shacks.”

Sura and the others say nothing in response to Sarajiros’s confident statement.

Strictly speaking, they were being treated as refugees.

Given that, they were more than grateful just to have been provided with these huts.

Especially this time of year, sleeping outside at night is tantamount to suicide.

If they could stay in a town or village, that would be one thing, but when they couldn’t, everyone used to sleep in the wagon.

There was no doubt that simply being able to stretch out their limbs and sleep was more than enough to satisfy them.

“Thank you.”

Sarajiros, moved to utter words of gratitude from the bottom of his heart, was momentarily at a loss as to how to respond.

He didn’t let it show on his face, but he knew from intelligence that he possessed the strength to easily wipe out this group if they were just a run-of-the-mill band of thieves.

That’s why he’d expected they might complain about not having a house each… but the unexpectedly positive reaction made Sarajiros feel relieved.

“No. I’m glad you’re pleased. Also, as you might expect, this house is strictly temporary. Once we’re able to start expansion work next spring, we plan to ask for your input and provide a house to anyone who wishes to have their own. At that time, we’ll be reusing the lumber from these huts, so I’d appreciate it if you could try not to get them too dirty…”

“If there are many people who prefer communal living rather than living alone, is it okay to keep using the huts as they are?”

Sura asked.

From Sura’s perspective, she likely feels that living in a group is safer than having just one or two women living alone.

Also, having traveled this far and lived in a group for several months, she’s grown quite accustomed to that lifestyle.

If they were going to live in an unfamiliar land anyway, it would be reassuring to live with people they knew well.

At first, the group was little more than a loose collection of various people simply traveling together, but after traveling side by side for months—literally spending every waking moment together—they inevitably grew close, whether they liked it or not.

Of course, on the way to becoming close, we’ve clashed, fought, and argued countless times.

As a result, a sense of unity as a group emerged, and they came to understand what each other needed to do with perfect synergy.

From Sura’s perspective, having led such a group, she expected that—while she wouldn’t say everyone…—most of them would likely continue living together even after spring arrived.

...Though, to be honest, Sura herself is considering living with Rocks in the future.

However, Sura didn’t expect that to happen right away next spring.

She planned to do so only after everyone else who had come to Gilm with her had established a foothold and could manage on their own.

Rocks had been told of Sura’s plan in advance and had no particular objections.

“Um, so about the carriage…”

“Ah, I’ll take care of that. I hate to say it, but acquiring this carriage and the horses to pull it was quite an expense for the Zolge Trading Company. We’ve been given permission to use it for our activities in Gilm.”

At Gimekara’s words, several of the women wore looks of dissatisfaction.

After all, they had spent several months traveling by carriage.

They had complained about the cramped sleeping quarters, but even so, the carriage had become like a home to them.

And as for the horses pulling the carriage, after traveling and caring for them together for months, they had grown attached to them.

The fact that the Zolge Trading Company was unilaterally taking away that carriage and those horses left many of them feeling resentful.

……Perhaps because they’d gained enough strength to wipe out any band of bandits they came across, the glares directed at Gimekara were sharp.

So much so that it wouldn’t be surprising if ordinary people were to lose their will to fight immediately.

But even when met with such stares, Gimekara doesn’t back down an inch.

And naturally so. No matter what the women might think, it was Gimekara—and the Zolge Trading Company to which she belongs—who provided the carriages in the first place.

From Gimekara’s perspective, she intended to lend the carriage and the horses pulling it only temporarily, and objectively speaking, it’s not unreasonable to view it that way.

“Wait a moment.”

It was Sura who spoke up in that tense, explosive situation.

With Sharia and Rocks flanking her on either side, she spoke with an air of authority—a dignity she had seemingly acquired without realizing it while leading a group of nearly a thousand people.

“It was Gimekara who prepared the carriage. Given that, it’s only natural for Gimekara to claim ownership.”

Judging by those words alone, she was clearly on Gimekara’s side.

But, Sura continued.

“But, Gimekara. Since it’s the carriage we used and the horse we cherished, I’m sure you understand that we’re attached to it, don’t you?”

“Well, I suppose I can understand that…”

“Right, if you understand, that’s fine. So, if we were to say we want to buy the carriage and the horse, would you sell them to us?”

At Sura’s words, Gimekara’s eyes widened slightly.

Given that Gimekara was a capable merchant, this reaction showed just how surprised he was.

“The horse and the carriage. Both come at a considerable price if you’re going to buy them. Is that all right with you?”

“Yes, if we truly want them, I think that’s only natural.”

In the end, that’s how the matter was settled.
