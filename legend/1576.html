After defeating the soldiers standing before the massive gate, Rei surveys his surroundings.

Naturally, his attention is focused on the area beyond the gate.

Since it was certain that Jaya's people were beyond this gate, he figured they would immediately check outside the gate if they sensed anything unusual.

He had taken care to eliminate the soldiers without making noise, but if for some reason they had managed to confirm the situation outside the gate, there was a possibility enemies were lying in wait inside the gate itself.

Of course, he was confident he could handle even Jaya soldiers lying in wait.

But if a fight broke out inside the gate, the facilities within could be destroyed.

That meant something important... like research materials on gigantification or black crystals... could be destroyed.

Also, though he wasn't expecting it, there might be some sort of magic item inside.

(Well, if that magic item was just a replica of the Black Crystal or something, I definitely wouldn't want it.)

I recalled touching the black crystal on the fifth underground floor of Mejogo.

The moment I touched it, I suffered a mental attack from the Black Crystal.

Exactly how long I was subjected to that mental assault by the Black Crystal? Rei has no idea.

It could have been mere seconds, or hours, or perhaps days, months, even years.

Ultimately, Rei regained his senses thanks to Elena and the others' voices. He then activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and burned the black crystal to ashes, ending the mental assault.

Even so, the black crystal's mental assault was something Rei never wanted to experience again. Even if a replica of that black crystal existed, he couldn't possibly want it.

Moreover, given the unpredictable chaos that could erupt if it were carelessly disseminated, he considered destroying any replica that might exist, if possible.

"Seems like there won't be any problems after all."

"Yeah. ...But seriously, hasn't the performance of Rei's Sleipnir Boots improved since before? I was pretty much on the edge just reaching this far, but Rei seemed to have more room to spare than me, even stepping on air?"

He probably didn't need his beloved Mirage to take down opponents of a soldier's caliber.

With her bare hands, Elena addressed Rei.

Rei nodded in agreement at her words.

"True... but didn't you say before that my Sleipnir Boots had been enhanced?"

Rei said, recalling he thought he had mentioned it when they spoke via the Paired Orb... Elena shook her head.

"No, that shouldn't be the case."

Except for times like this, Rei and Elena basically lived apart.

Elena's joy came from communicating using the Paired Orb.

Moreover, her innate abilities were already high... and above all, having inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone, her abilities had increased dramatically.

If Elena put her mind to it, recalling what Rei had said wouldn't be difficult.

Given Elena's nature, if Rei had told her about enhancing Sleipnir Boots, she certainly wouldn't have forgotten it—especially since it was a magic item she herself used.

Seeing Elena's expression as she looked at him, Rei realized his misunderstanding about the Sleipnir Boots and apologized to her.

Incidentally, long-distance relationships naturally exist in this world too.

They do exist... but unless you're nobility, they almost always break up along the way.

Depending on the distance, it could take months for a letter to reach its destination... sometimes even half a year or a full year.

And considering the possibility of being attacked by bandits along the way, there was no guarantee the letter would even arrive safely.

Maintaining romantic feelings for each other under such conditions was incredibly difficult.

(Thinking about it, Japan really is suited for this kind of thing, with email, phones, SNS, and all that.)

Even so, Rei had read somewhere that long-distance relationships were highly likely to fail, even when both partners were in Japan.

...The fact that this "something" wasn't something he heard from a friend, but rather information from TV, anime, or manga, was typical of Rei.

Fundamentally, the biggest factor was that Rei lived in the countryside, where there simply weren't people who did long-distance relationships.

Or if there were, they were people completely unrelated to Rei.

"Rei? What's wrong?"

Marina, who had come up near the door, spoke to him. Rei shook his head, signaling it was nothing.

"No, I was just wondering what might be on the other side if I opened this door."

"Oh? ...Well, I'll just let you keep thinking that for now."

Marina said she'd accept that explanation for now, then turned her gaze to the door Rei was looking at.

The door was quite large, built to allow giants to pass through.

Beyond that door, Ryutas had mentioned, were several individuals responsible for the current commotion.

Whether that was true or not, she couldn't say, but it was certain that someone belonging to Jaya was in there.

Then... Just as that thought crossed her mind, Rei instinctively backed away from the door.

No, not just Rei. Everyone—Elena, Marina, Vihera, Byune—stepped back from the door.

Because a tremendous sound came from the door.

It sounded like something had been hurled at the door with tremendous force.

"...What is it?"

For a moment, they thought the soldiers they had defeated in front of the door had realized it and were taking some kind of countermeasure... but despite that, the door showed no sign of opening.

Worse still, the metal door sealed the other side so tightly that even with Rei's superhuman hearing, she could barely make out what sounded like screams.

"Well, I can't think of what could have happened..."

"What is it? I hear something like a scream."

"Yeah. Maybe something went wrong? Or the Resistance snuck in somehow?"

Following Rei's words, Elena, Marina, and Vihera each murmured.

Only Byune, the thief who should have been the first to notice any disturbance, tilted her head expressionlessly.

Like Rei's body, created using Zepairu's technology, the other three also possessed extraordinary abilities.

Elena, who inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone; Marina, descended from the World Tree's priestesses among the Dark Elves; and Vihera, who absorbed Ambris.

All three possessed unique powers in various ways, and even now, their hearing was as sharp as Rei's.

"No way. The Resistance's main force should have been annihilated. By the giants' assault, no less."

"...But that doesn't mean the entire main force was killed, right? Then, we can't rule out the possibility that some survivors remain?"

At Vihera's words, Rei tilted his head. Huh?

He considered whether that possibility was actually likely... and concluded it probably wasn't.

"If something's happening behind that door, opening it will tell us what it is right away."

Laying his hand on the door, Rei nodded in agreement with Elena's words as she spoke while looking at it.

Normally, it would be difficult for one person to open such a door, but if it was Rei opening it, that changed the story.

He pushed the inward-opening door... and it began to open, slowly at first.

In that instant, the familiar smell of rusty iron wafted through the crack.

And then, the screams of several people... or more.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

"Don't come! Don't come! Don't come over here!"

"Stop it! You idiot! I said stop, stop!"

"Stop! Don't eat me! Don't eat meeeee!"

"Dammit! 'No problem with defense forces because we have giants'!? Who the hell said that?!"

Such a scream, filled with despair, as if spitting blood.

Of course, it wasn't just screams that reached my ears.

"Dammit! We don't have enough troops! Why the hell did you send reinforcements to Mejogo?!"

"What choice did we have?! Forget that now! Just focus on protecting everyone!"

"Dammit! That giant won't budge from the door!"

"Aaaaaah! Dammit, let go! I said let go! Obey my orders! Ah, stop... Don't eat me, don't eat me! I'm sorry, I'm sorry... Mom, help meee!"

The sound of bones cracking, like a predator devouring a small bird, came through the crack in the door.

Realizing what that sound meant, Rei gently closed the door, which had been slightly ajar.

Turning around, he found Elena and the others standing there, completely baffled by what had just happened.

Though Rei was equally clueless.

No, Rei knew exactly what was happening.

The giant beyond the door was probably rampaging for some reason.

And that instead of sending reinforcements to Mejogo, they had prepared the giants as defensive forces.

They possessed overwhelmingly more resilient physiques than ordinary soldiers, yet obeyed their commanders absolutely.

For those left behind here, they were undoubtedly far more useful as combat forces than the average soldier.

But... this time, it backfired completely.

For some unknown reason, the giants suddenly began to rampage.

Some tried desperately to contain them, but such individuals were the first to be devoured by the giants.

And as the soldiers desperately tried to stop the giants, the result was that beyond the door where Rei and the others now stood, a literal bloodbath was unfolding.

"What do we do?"

"...What do you mean, 'what should we do'?"

Rei, who was asked by Vihera as she turned her gaze to him, hesitated, unsure what to do.

A somewhat rare sight unfolded there: Rei, stunned and dumbfounded, never having imagined things would turn out like this.

It was only natural. Not long ago, the giants had been completely under Jaya's control.

The people beyond the door had allowed the giant into their living quarters precisely because they were confident in that control.

But now, that giant had suddenly broken free from control and gone berserk.

No, it wasn't just rampaging. Judging by the voices coming from inside, it seemed people were being devoured.

"We have no choice but to help them."

Reluctantly... truly reluctantly, Rei made his decision.

He had been told that those beyond this door were the ones involved in creating the giant.

Given that, he couldn't possibly want to help them under normal circumstances.

And the idea of risking his own life to save such people? He couldn't possibly accept that.

But... to get information about the Titans and the Black Crystal, it was obvious he needed them alive.

(Getting eaten by the giants they created is just karma, I suppose.)

Thinking this, Rei turned his gaze toward Elena and the others.

There they were, unsurprisingly wearing displeased expressions.

Yet, despite their displeasure, they likely understood that rescuing those inside was a necessary act.

They were preparing to storm inside.

"There's no helping it if we want to hear the story from the people inside. Naturally, it won't be pleasant."

Elena's attitude clearly showed her dissatisfaction, but she had resigned herself to the fact that it was necessary to obtain information.

Relieved by this, he turned his gaze to the others.

Marina and Vihera both looked reluctant, but they nodded back at Rei's gaze.

Only Byune returned the nod expressionlessly, though this wasn't because she was happy to help those inside; it was simply her usual lack of facial expression.

"Well then, let's go."

He announced, slowly pushing open the door, which had been slightly ajar.

He opened it just wide enough for one person to pass through and stepped inside the room.

And the first sight that met his eyes was a scene truly worthy of being called a hellscape.

People torn apart and devoured by the giant.

Bodies with only their upper or lower halves eaten, discarded on the ground as if discarded after losing interest.

No, perhaps those corpses were fortunate, spared further pain and terror.

The truly tragic ones were the many still alive, their bodies torn apart piece by piece... yet not fatally wounded.

Watching them, Rei drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and closed the distance to the nearest giant.
