Set's front paws, still transparent from the attack meant to save Rei, lunged toward Vicente.

As if instinctively sensing the blow, Vicente kicked off the ground in an instant... only to be sent flying through the air the next moment, accompanied by a violent metallic clang.

Even with his strength halved by the Sanctuary's effect, the force of Set's foreleg strike was easily enough to send a person flying.

"Guh!"

Gasping in pain, Vicente somehow regained his balance mid-air and landed on the ground.

The metal armor provided as Stigma-specific equipment... crafted using vast quantities of light ore, a magical metal, this armor weighed about the same as leather armor despite being metal, yet possessed strong magical and physical defense. ...Thanks to that light armor, the Sanctuary's effect, and above all, Vicente's own skill, the blow had not been fatal.

But Set's attack didn't end with that single blow.

"GRRRRRRRRRRGRRRR!"

Another roar echoed throughout the area. Simultaneously, the still-transparent Set kicked off the ground and launched a follow-up attack toward Vicente.

The matchup between Set and Vicente was the best possible scenario for Set, and the worst possible scenario for Vicente.

If Vicente had possessed any kind of weapon, he might have had a chance.

But Vicente's weapons were his fists, and the only things that could be called weapons were his gauntlets and greaves made from light ore.

Since armor made of the same magical metal was easily destroyed, it was certain that attacking Set with gauntlets and greaves would cause him more damage than it would inflict on Vicente.

If these were ordinary weapons, only the weapon itself would be destroyed when broken.

But gauntlets and greaves are, quite literally, things worn on the hands and feet.

If they were destroyed, damage would naturally be inflicted on his hands and feet.

No, if the opponent is a formidable enemy like Rei, sustaining some injury oneself wouldn't matter.

But right now, the battle with Rei was in full swing.

If I sustained injuries severe enough to hinder combat at this point, it would undoubtedly make executing divine punishment afterward much more difficult.

In other words, all Vicente could do now was somehow minimize the damage, get past Set, and break the current stalemate alongside the old man.

Feeling Set's approaching presence, he retreated once more while gauging the likely power of the impending blow... Suddenly, he noticed Rei, where his gaze had fallen, concentrating intensely with a large scythe in hand.

Understanding what this preparation signified... and further, knowing that Rei could unleash powerful attacks even within this sanctuary, Vicente instantly grasped what Rei intended to do.

He already possessed prior intelligence on what Rei excelled at most.

Combine that with the current situation... While the sanctuary greatly reduced Rei's physical abilities, for some reason, his magical power remained fully functional.

Combining these factors, it wasn't difficult to understand what Rei was about to do and who it targeted.

'Flames, spread forth, displaying crimson fire. Become a carpet of flames, following my will.'

With those words, a mass of flame materialized at the tip of the Death Scythe... The next instant, as the incantation completed, the flame born at the tip pierced the ground.

'Thin Flames!'

As the magic activated, flames spread out in a circle, with Rei at its center.

What was strange was that the flames gave off no heat whatsoever.

At the very least, compared to the heat radiating from the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour that Rei wore, it felt completely cool.

Because these flames weren't meant for attack, but for exploration.

The flames generated around Rei quickly spread throughout the surrounding area... and soon detected the presence of something not far from Rei, specifically behind her.

Of course, the flames didn't stop at merely detecting the presence; they spread further, increasing their area.

Vicente was startled by the presence of the flames.

He didn't know the flames were meant for exploration, flames that wouldn't burn him if he touched them. Seeing the flames heading towards him, his surprise was only natural.

Having seen Rei’s Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour up close, his wariness was exceptionally high.

For a moment, he even worried that Set, who was likely attacking him, might also suffer damage from the flames. However, reasoning that as Rei's familiar, Set probably possessed some kind of magic item granting resistance to fire, he decided it would be unwise to continue facing attacks from both the invisible Set and the flames. He leapt backward once more, trying desperately to escape the situation.

"Damn!"

Vicente's leap backward and the advancing flames arrived almost simultaneously. The twenty-second effect of the optical camouflage Set had used expired, and the griffin's form came into Vicente's view at nearly the same moment.

Seeing Set touch the flames without flinching, he thought, "He must have equipment to increase his fire resistance..." But the next instant, the spreading flames reached beneath Vicente.

Normally, he might have relied on his armor's magical defense, but Set's strike had crushed it, leaving many sections shattered.

But against the flames unleashed by the formidable enemy, Rei, he steeled himself against any cry of pain, determined to endure it and minimize the damage. Yet, no matter how long he waited, the expected searing pain of burns never came.

Panicking, he looked around and saw flames already spreading everywhere, engulfing him.

Despite this, he felt no heat whatsoever from the flames touching him.

Caught off guard, a split-second opening.

It was less than a second, but for Set, who had been in a defensive posture to withstand the flames, it was more than enough of an opening.

"GRRRRRRRRRR!"

A roar accompanied the strike of his forepaw.

That strike, weakened considerably by the Sanctuary's effect, lacked its usual power.

However, Set understood that within this peculiar space called the Sanctuary, the power of his own attacks was also consistently diminished.

Precisely because he understood this, the skill Set chose for his current attack wasn't one that relied on direct offensive power.

"Wooooooooo!"

The fact that he managed to dodge Set's front paw strike aimed at his face at the last possible moment, even though he was caught off guard, was likely only possible because he was a member of the Stigma, said to possess strength equivalent to a Rank A adventurer.

Furthermore, the fact that he was attacked on his left cheek, not the right cheek where the Stigma resided, was precisely why Vicente had been forced to dodge the attack with desperate, life-or-death urgency.

Even so, he couldn't avoid it completely. Several wounds remained on his left cheek, inflicted by the sharp claws extending from Set's forepaw.

...Yes, the wounds from that claw strike were there.

"A mere monster... to me...?"

He didn't finish the words, realizing something was wrong with his body.

Normally, within the sanctuary, his physical abilities should have been heightened.

Yet, for some reason, even standing like this was now agonizing.

It was something absolutely unthinkable under normal circumstances, but it was easy to imagine that the earlier blow was the cause.

"You... what did you do to me...?"

"GRRRR!"

Facing Vicente, who glared at him, Set let out a threatening growl.

As the growl echoed, Vicente wiped the blood trickling down his cheek with his hand and brought it before his eyes.

The color of the blood was not normal. It was a color one might even describe as dark and murky.

Seeing it, Vicente finally understood. That blow had injected poison into his body.

(What a petty trick. ...No, I knew the griffin was a rare species. It's my own fault for forgetting that. Fortunately, we're within the sanctuary. No matter how much poison I take, I won't die so easily.)

A chill and lethargy assaulted his body. Feeling symptoms as if he'd fallen ill, Vicente nevertheless stood his ground.

He knew the creature before him was a griffin, a Rank A monster... no, a rare species, making it equivalent to a Rank S monster.

But even so, this was within the sanctuary, and he possessed the power of the Sacred Light Goddess.

Given that, he couldn't afford to lose easily, no matter what kind of monster he faced.

Moreover, he understood that even against an S-rank monster, he had a chance to win within this sanctuary.

"Grrrrr?"

Set gave Vicente a suspicious look and growled.

The poison claws he had just used were a Level 4 skill, the highest level skill among Set's many abilities.

To say he wasn't confident would be a lie.

And yet, the man before him now seemed somewhat off his game, but ultimately, that was all it amounted to.

"Grrr..."

Resigned, Set kicked off the ground to create distance from the man.

Vicente was momentarily surprised by Set's action, but he quickly judged that letting Set gain distance would be bad. He pursued Set as he retreated backward.

Under normal circumstances, even a moment's hesitation would have meant he couldn't match Set's physical prowess and would have been forced to give ground.

But this was within the Sanctuary.

A place where anyone without a Stigma was at an overwhelming disadvantage. And Vicente, having activated the Sanctuary, could reap its benefits—a battlefield overwhelmingly favorable to him.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set let out a loud roar, opening his beak to unleash a fire breath... but at that very instant. Vicente's figure had already slipped deep into Set's embrace.

"A mere monster dares to interfere with me!"

A swinging fist and a fire breath unleashed at close range.

Both struck their target simultaneously...

"GRRRRRRRR!"

It was Set who was blown away.

Struck on his left shoulder, Set cried out in pain but braced his limbs to land on the ground.

That action alone sent a dull ache through his battered left shoulder, but fortunately, Set's griffin physique meant Vicente's punch hadn't broken any bones.

Though Set had been pushed back, Vicente wasn't unscathed either.

Vicente, whose exceptionally handsome features and the stigma on his right cheek had lent him a mystical charm, now bore a deep, vivid mark from Set's claws on his left cheek.

The wound, torn open by Set's poisoned claws, had turned a dark blue-black. On top of that, the fire breath Set had just unleashed left various large and small burn marks across his face.

...And yet, the stigma on his right cheek remained completely unaffected. Had Rei witnessed this entire exchange, he might have reconsidered the power of religion.

Regardless, the current situation favored Vicente, partly due to the influence of the Sanctuary's domain.

"Grrrrrrr!"

"Hmph! Even if you're an S-rank monster, you're still just a monster! How could you possibly defeat me, who has the protection of the Holy Goddess!"

Set growled, while Vicente reaffirmed his faith aloud, simultaneously kicking off the ground.

Neither was completely battered and bruised... but it was true that both had sustained some injuries.

However, as a high-rank monster, Set could ignore that level of pain, and Vicente could also ignore some pain through the power of his faith.

The one and the beast advanced straight toward each other. As the distance closed, Set opened his beak.

"GRRRRRRRRRR!"

Along with the roar, a Crystal Breath erupted from his beak.

The breath struck Vicente, who was advancing straight toward him, and in an instant, it began encasing Vicente's body in crystal.

But Vicente wasn't about to be stopped by an attack like that.

He forcibly shattered the crystal hindering his movement, closing the distance to Set.

Originally, the Crystal Breath was only level one, possessing only the effect of covering the target in thin crystal.

It might have been enough to stall a low-rank monster, but it lacked the power to hold Vicente back.

Even so, for an instant... just an instant, Vicente's movement did halt.

"GRRRRRRRRGRRRR!"

Next, the Shock Magic Eye slammed into Vicente.

The attack itself was extremely weak, but an impact is an impact.

Vicente took the blow to his body, caught off guard, and halted his movement once more.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

The distance between Set and Vicente had already shrunk to about two meters, but next... no, finally, Set unleashed another Fire Breath.

BOOM!

With that sound, the fire breath unleashed from Set's beak engulfed Vicente's body.

But such an attack couldn't possibly work on Vicente now.

He simply ignored the flames, closing the distance with Set until he was finally within striking range! Just as he thought that...

Slash!

A fierce impact struck his torso.

Somehow, the next instant, Vicente found himself floating in the air. His gaze locked onto the figure of his arch-enemy, Rei, who had swung the Death Scythe horizontally, and his own lower half, severed from the waist down.

Realizing he had been cleaved in two along with his armor, Vicente screamed almost reflexively.

"You bastard! In the name of the Holy Goddess of Light, bring down divine punishment upon youuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuuu

Spinning through the air with only his upper body, blown away, Vicente continued to voice his hatred toward Rei until his final breath.
