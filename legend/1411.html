The four of them—Rei, Marina, Vihera, and Byune—entered the forest and retraced the path Rei and Byune had just taken.

After watching Byune lead the way, Rei glanced up at the sky through the gaps in the branches.

For just an instant, he caught sight of Set flying through the sky. Feeling relieved at that, Rei continued deeper into the woods.

"Still, a cave... It's a bit unusual to find one in a forest like this."

Marina murmured, perhaps recalling the information she'd heard from Rei, who had scouted ahead.

Even as she spoke, she remained alert to her surroundings—typical of the skilled Marina.

Normally, if anything unusual happened, Set would be the first to notice—before the thief Byune, before even Rei with her heightened senses.

But Set wasn't here now.

Well, to be precise, since Rei had spotted him earlier when looking up at the sky, it was more accurate to say he wasn't nearby.

Therefore, they walked cautiously, alert to their surroundings, trying not to be spotted by thieves... and not to be spotted by monsters.

"Whoa!"

Amidst this, Rei suddenly thrust the thorny spear he held into the bushes.

"Gyupii!"

A deathly scream echoed through the area.

When Rei pulled the thorn spear from the bushes, what he saw was a rabbit monster with three horns.

...If you could even call a creature with fangs a rabbit.

(A Gamelion's child... No, that can't be right. Still, it's the first monster I've ever defeated. ...It was in the Monster Compendium, I think...)

As Rei contemplated the dead rabbit monster impaled on the tip of his spear, Marina spoke up.

"Oh, isn't that an Owl Rabbit?"

"Ah, that's it."

Owl Rabbit. A Rank E monster characterized by three horns and fangs.

Though rabbit-shaped, its fangs clearly marked it as a carnivorous monster.

It attacks with its three horns growing from its forehead and its fangs, relying on its sturdy legs and swift movements, but it wasn't an exceptionally powerful monster.

However, like orcs, it possesses meat that's tastier than its rank suggests, making it a popular monster in that regard.

"Anyway, we'll gut this one later. Gotta bleed it out and all."

Muttering, he pulled the thorn spearhead from the Owl Rabbit's carcass and stored it in his Misty Ring.

He decided that since the meat tasted good, it would make a perfect souvenir for Set, who was flying overhead alone.

"Alright, let's move on. Byune, I'm counting on you."

"Mm."

Byune gave a small reply to Marina's words, and they continued on through the forest, now bathed in the evening sun.

(I really want to finish this up and get back as soon as possible. ...I wonder how it'll go.)

From what Rei had seen during reconnaissance, the private soldiers they were to fight this time weren't particularly strong opponents.

Rei felt sorry for Vihera, who was looking forward to it, but he suspected this wouldn't be a satisfying fight.

(Well, the guy they called captain seemed pretty strong, so maybe I should just leave him to it.)

If these were monsters, he couldn't afford to let anyone else handle them, since he needed the magic stones absorbed by his beast magic.

But this time, the enemy was human.

Since defeating them wouldn't yield any magic stones, there was no need for him to forcefully take on the strongest enemy.

So, handing that formidable foe over to Vihera posed no problem whatsoever.

"Hmm."

After walking through the forest for a while...

Suddenly, Byune, who was walking at the front, let out a short sound.

It sounded no different than usual, but the underlying caution was palpable to Rei and the others.

And given the current situation, if Byune was on guard, there was no need to wonder who the target of that vigilance was.

"...It's here."

Marina murmured softly, and Rei, Vihera, and Byune each nodded.

Byune, as a thief, Marina with her dark elf's keen eyesight, and Vihera with the heightened vision gained from absorbing Ambris—all three were observing the target.

Yes, several men keeping watch around the hideout.

If this were a forest... considering it a monster's habitat, it was only natural.

If they were in the woods without any guards, they'd never know when monsters might attack.

To prevent that, watchmen were necessary.

Though, Rei and Byune had slipped past those watchful eyes a little while ago, infiltrating the hideout and escaping from within.

"What should we do?"

"I'll take care of it."

Rei muttered, holding the thorn spear.

The other three silently nodded in agreement.

Everyone knew Rei was the strongest among the four.

If Rei was moving, they felt it must be the right call.

"Alright. Then, once we take out the guard, we move all at once. There's no way there's only one guard, so they'll sense something's wrong soon anyway."

"I can take care of the enemy captain, right?"

Rei nodded in response to Vihera's questioning look.

Satisfied, Vihera's expression changed the next instant, a savage grin spreading across her lips.

She was likely anticipating the battle ahead.

Understanding this, Rei prayed the fight would satisfy Vihera as he leapt out from behind the tree where he'd been hiding.

"Huh!?"

The guard, realizing something had burst out, tried to scream.

But Rei's lunge had already brought the man within his range before the sound could escape the man's throat.

Moreover, Rei wielded a spear. In terms of weapon reach, it held a clear advantage over the man's longsword.

While long-handled weapons are difficult to use in the forest, a spear, which relies primarily on thrusts, was a different matter.

In the end, the guard couldn't even cry out. He took a blow to the solar plexus, lost consciousness, and collapsed to the ground.

Still, it was fortunate for the man that Rei's thrust had struck with the butt of the spear, not the tip.

However, Rei didn't spare him out of any reluctance to kill or similar sentiment.

It was simply a matter of thinking that having more sources of information would be better for extracting even a little detail about who was behind this private army.

...Though it was undoubtedly true that capturing him alive would likely increase the reward.

From there, without exchanging a word, Rei and his men advanced toward the enemy's hideout.

If they could launch a surprise attack first, they could take them all down in one go.

That's precisely why they took out the lookouts silently... but the effect was less than Rei had anticipated.

Suddenly confronted by unfamiliar faces... the moment they saw Rei, those inside the hideout immediately grabbed their weapons and launched their attack.

(Huh.)

Rei was slightly impressed by their quick reaction.

They hadn't seemed to notice him when he snuck in, but once they spotted Rei, they didn't hesitate—they instantly judged him an enemy and attacked.

Of course, given that they had set up their hideout in this forest, even with guards, they never knew when monsters might attack.

Given that, it was only natural to be prepared for such an emergency.

"Enemy..."

But... even so, it was too slow.

Enemy attack, or perhaps "enemy arrived"?

Either way, before he could shout about Rei's arrival, the man was struck in the side by Rei's thorn spear and sent flying several meters sideways.

It was unfortunate for the man that the hideout was spacious enough to allow a sweeping thrust with the spear.

Though whether it's better to lose consciousness from being stabbed through the body or blown sideways unconscious probably depends on the person.

Even so, the unconscious man's scream echoed through the hideout.

Hearing it, those present each judged it an enemy attack and rushed out to intercept, weapons drawn.

"What!? You say it's not a monster!?"

"Kid!?"

Seeing Rei wearing the hood of the Dragon Robe, several voices raised in confusion.

But that hesitation proved fatal for the men.

Rei, paying no heed to the men's reactions, stepped forward.

And the thorn spear swung.

...Had Set been present, he might have recognized Rei as the adventurer known by the Crimson epithet. Unfortunately, Set was currently in the sky.

In the end, the man who had called Rei a brat was blown away by the thorn spear's strike before he could even comprehend what had happened, crashing into another man standing beside him.

With the commotion reaching this level, it was only natural that activity around them intensified.

But Rei wasn't the only one fighting.

Marina used her bow to pierce the enemies' limbs, rendering them unable to move further.

Vihera took down enemies with a single strike while searching for the captain she targeted.

Unlike the other three, Byune moved stealthily to avoid detection, launching long needles and striking with White Cloud when openings appeared to support the others.

"Calm down, everyone! Form up! The enemy is outnumbered—don't panic!"

The captain shouted this upon seeing his subordinates being knocked unconscious one after another by Rei and the others.

His voice seemed to snap them back to their senses. The private soldiers rapidly regained their composure.

To begin with, this private army possessed a level of training worthy of being called elite.

Given that, it wasn't particularly difficult for them to regain their composure once they'd fallen into confusion.

"Huh."

The voice expressing admiration belonged to Rei.

While using his thorn spear to sever the consciousness of those appearing one after another, he turned his gaze back to the scene around him.

There, Vihera, who had keenly spotted the enemy captain who had just shouted, was attacking him.

"Captain!"

Seeing the captain like that, someone nearby tried to provide support, but...

"Gah! Dammit!"

An arrow pierced his leg.

Naturally, there was no way they could support the captain now.

Before the man's eyes, fists, feet, and weapons flew at a speed barely perceptible to the naked eye.

Even if the man shot through the thigh tried to back up the captain, he would undoubtedly only get in the way.

And when the man finally realized it, Rei was already standing before him...

"What!?"

The instant he realized someone was before him, a strike from the thorn spear knocked him unconscious.

Watching the man collapse to the ground, Rei surveyed the area, thorn spear still in hand.

(Considering I didn't use the Thorn Spear's special ability, maybe the Twilight Spear would have been fine as a weapon? ...Well, I suppose I should use the Thorn Spear occasionally too.)

As he thought this and scanned his surroundings, he noticed several men entering a cave within the hideout.

...To be precise, it wasn't a cave within the hideout; it was more accurate to say they chose this location precisely because of the cave.

Anyway, as Rei, who had infiltrated this hideout earlier, he knew what was in there.

After all, that's why Rei and the others were hired in the first place—to retrieve those materials.

Seeing those men enter the cave where the materials were stored in this situation naturally gave him a bad feeling.

「Rei！」

A short call.

But when Rei turned his gaze toward Marina, who had called out, he understood what she meant the moment she looked toward the cave.

Most of the people already in this hideout had been incapacitated.

Many remained conscious, but most of them had their limbs pierced by Marina's arrows and were barely able to move.

Given the situation, Rei judged it safe to pursue the person who had entered the cave. Nodding slightly, he stepped toward the cave entrance.

"Ahahahaha, not bad at all. More! More quickly, more strongly, more sharply!"

He ignored the delighted voice of Vihera echoing behind him.

(Well, I guess I was lucky enough to have the skill to satisfy Vihera...)

Thinking that, he followed the men into the cave.

He was careful to keep his presence hidden, partly to gather as much information about the men as possible, and partly because a surprise attack would make victory easier.

"Hey, what are we gonna do with this stuff?!"

"Burn the combustible materials, and render the rest unusable. Or, if that's too much, just make them hesitate to use it!"

From their conversation, Rei realized the men had entered the cave for exactly the purpose he'd anticipated, and a small smile touched his lips.

Given the current situation, the reason they'd enter a cave stockpiled with supplies was surely to prevent it from being recovered.

When Rei scouted earlier, they'd mentioned something about coming to retrieve it, but if that wasn't possible, they'd been told to destroy it.

Having confirmed that, it was more than enough... No, if he listened any longer, he judged the supplies would be ruined, and opened his mouth.

"That's a bit of a problem."

"What!?"

Facing the stunned men at the sudden outburst, Rei lunged at them.
