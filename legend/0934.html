Damn it! I managed to let the young master escape, but there are way too many of these bastards! How the hell did Graciall manage to muster this many men!?

The man shouted in frustration as he swung his longsword, parrying the blade of the opponent lunging at him before instantly reversing his own blade to cut down the assassin.

Even using the same weapon, the skill difference between wielders was so stark that any observer would have marveled at the disparity.

Not that the guard's skill was exceptionally high, but rather that the assassin's skill was low.

"That's because numbers were the priority! Quantity over quality, you know!"

The man drawing his bow and firing arrows shouted back, equally displeased.

Yet the unbroken speed of his arrows proved this man possessed skill beyond mere quantity.

"We let Lord Eland escape, but some of us went after him. We gotta take care of these guys and pursue him."

Everyone nodded at the spearman's words.

Though Eland possessed a certain level of strength as a noble, it was still only a certain level.

If asked whether he could adequately fight multiple assassins, he couldn't confidently nod in agreement.

After all, the assassins attacking them relied on their numbers.

For someone like Eland, with little combat experience... especially little experience fighting humans, it was entirely possible he could make an unexpected mistake.

That was precisely why they wanted to defeat the assassins as quickly as possible and catch up to Eland. But they were only five, while the enemy still numbered nearly twenty.

It was at that moment that Eland, accompanied by Rei... or rather, Eland being led by Rei, appeared at this location.

"Eland, I'm charging at them now. Follow right behind me. If possible, meet up with your escort and hand you over to them."

"So I'm just dead weight, huh?"

"Yes."

Had these been Eland's original guards present here, they would have hedged their words and avoided saying outright that she was a hindrance.

But Rei declared it bluntly, as if it didn't matter, and drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

Though they likely hadn't heard Rei and Eland's exchange, several assassins suddenly turned around.

At the end of their gaze stood a small figure wielding a scythe taller than himself, and Eland, who should have been the target.

The men who saw Rei and Eland wondered for a moment if what they were seeing was some kind of illusion.

It was only natural. After all, the target they had let escape was now returning, and staying here meant certain death.

Despite the fact that eliminating the target should have been their top priority, most of those present had chosen to stay, unable to abandon the bodyguard.

...Though it must be said, the bodyguard's provocation of the assassins played a significant role.

A true first-rate assassin wouldn't have fallen for such a cheap provocation. But the ones here couldn't be called first-rate at all... they were third-rate, not even worthy of being called second-rate.

In the end, they took the cheap bait and were now putting on a show in this back alley. But even such men would remember their original purpose if the target appeared before them.

"Damn it, there he is!"

Unfortunately for them, Eland now had a bodyguard named Rei.

The assassin who'd been about to shout Eland's name first was sent flying by a single swing of Rei's Death Scythe, crashing into the back of another assassin already fighting Eland's guards.

"It was a Death Scythe strike, but don't worry, it was a glancing blow. He's probably got five or six broken bones, though."

"Well, it's not so much the edge as the hilt he's hitting him with."

Aeland murmured softly at Rei's words.

But that was of no consequence to the assassins.

All that mattered to them was that Rei, who had suddenly appeared, was a threat.

"What? The target's here!? Hey, you guys in the back! We'll handle these guards, so hurry up and kill the target! That should make these guys give up!"

"Don't be ridiculous! What do you expect us to do against a monster like that?!"

Several of those near Rei shouted reflexively.

A man nearly as tall as himself had been sent flying several meters by a single swing of a scythe. Straight out to the side, flying through the air.

The sheer force required to do that was beyond comprehension.

Fighting meant death. ...Even if it didn't go that far, getting blown away like that man earlier wouldn't end with just a broken bone or two.

Knowing this, they shouted desperately, but those who hadn't seen Rei fight directly grew impatient, even irritated, wondering what they were so scared of.

"Just do something already! It's been ages since the fight started here. When will the guards... Dammit!"

Taking advantage of the shouting man's distraction, one of Eland's guards fired an arrow at him.

Still, he was clearly the leader here, giving orders to the others, and he must have some skill. He sliced the arrow aimed at him cleanly with his longsword.

"Everyone, you're safe!"

Taking advantage of the chaos, Eland shouted, boosting his guards' morale.

Under normal circumstances, the strong feeling would be why the person they were protecting had returned.

But now, Rei stood beside Eland.

Rei, known by the nickname Crimson, possessed more than enough strength to be considered a significant combat asset in this situation.

Moreover, the guards couldn't simply leave the person they were sworn to protect in the hands of an outsider, no matter how skilled Rei was.

As a result, the guards rallied and charged at the assassins.

Front and back. Caught in a pincer movement from both sides, the assassins were easily thrown into confusion.

Had they been thinking clearly, they would have realized that even caught in a pincer attack, the sheer difference in numbers wouldn't immediately lead to fatal casualties.

Moreover, if they had attacked Rei, who had come from behind, he was, after all, alone. A certain number of them should have been able to escape from this spot.

Even in an alleyway... no, precisely because it was an alleyway, Rei couldn't simply use his signature wide-area annihilation magic.

But almost none retained enough composure to make such a judgment, and even if they did, they couldn't calm the panicked people around them.

As a result, it didn't take long for nearly twenty assassins to be wiped out.

"You're safe, young master!"

"Ah, I encountered Rei while fleeing. Fortunately, he lent me a hand. Thanks to that, I'm safe and sound."

True to his words, they must have noticed he truly had no injuries anywhere. The guard who had been talking with Eland—the same one who had spoken with Rei when the carriage wheel got stuck earlier—checked to see if Eland was genuinely unharmed and breathed a sigh of relief.

"That's good. It seems you really are unharmed after all."

Hearing that, the other guards finally relaxed too.

"So, what are we doing with these guys? Hand them over to the guards? Personally, I'd appreciate it if you could do that."

"Huh? Why?"

The guard who had been talking to Eland shot Rei a puzzled look.

The rumors the men had heard about Rei included that he knew no mercy when dealing with enemies.

So why would a man like Rei, with such a reputation, say he wouldn't kill the assassins and hand them over to the guards instead?

(Or maybe the rumors are just rumors, and he actually values life? ...He doesn't exactly look like a humanitarian though.)

Unaware that such thoughts were even being considered, Rei opened his mouth, jabbing the tip of the Death Scythe into the ground.

"Actually, since I came to Glacial, I've been targeted by someone myself. I figured someone from the same underworld might have some information on that sort of thing. In fact, I told an acquaintance to turn in the guy who followed Eland after I took him down."

"An acquaintance?"

When the guard asked, it wasn't Rei who answered, but Eland.

"Instructor Glink."

"...Come to think of it, I noticed earlier too. Calling Glink 'Instructor'... Could it be you were a cadet at the academy?"

"Yes. Due to various circumstances, I took a break for a while, but I'm scheduled to return tomorrow. ...Wait. You asking about Instructor Glink like that—could it be...?"

Rei asked, seeking confirmation, having grasped something from Rei's words.

Rei wasn't one to miss the implication. He nodded and spoke.

"Until spring, I'm teaching mock battles. ...Well, only for the third and fourth years in the S Class, though."

"...I see. Should I say I'm relieved, or should I say I'm disappointed? I'm in the second-year S-Class, you see."

"I see. That's unfortunate."

Rei and Eland exchanged a few brief words like that, but then the guard man called out to them.

"Young master. If you intend to turn these individuals over to the guards, it'd be best to summon them quickly. Right now, they're all either dead or unconscious. The dead are one thing, but the unconscious could wake up at any moment."

"You're right. Then let's get the guards here quickly..."

Just as Eland finished speaking,

Rei felt a chill run down his spine and spun around reflexively.

Simultaneously, a powerful impact struck.

Before he knew it, Rei's body was flying through the air. Realizing he'd crash into the alley wall if he kept going, he managed to twist his body.

The intense shock he felt through the soles of his feet made it clear that if he had been blown into the wall as he was, even wearing the Dragon Robe, the impact would have been devastating.

Though Rei managed to land safely on the ground, he had no idea what had just happened.

No, he understood he'd been attacked by someone, but he never expected to land a hit on an assassin of that caliber... nor did he expect to be knocked back this far.

Fortunately, the Dragon Robe had prevented fatal injuries, but it couldn't completely absorb the impact.

It protected him from the direct blow, but it couldn't completely absorb the resulting shock.

"Guh... Dammit... What just happened?"

Even after taking a blow powerful enough to send him flying, he hadn't let go of the Death Scythe he held. It was instinct, understanding that this was his lifeline.

Judging it too dangerous to stay crouched, he forced himself to stand despite the pain in his ribs.

And what met Rei's gaze as he stood were three figures.

One man in his twenties, one in his thirties, and one elderly man who looked to be in his sixties.

All three were men, each clad in gleaming golden metal armor. Each bore a single tattoo-like mark: the man in his twenties on his right cheek, the man in his thirties on the back of his left hand, and the man in his sixties on the back of his right hand.

One of the three, the man in his twenties, swung his fist. Ray watched it and murmured with admiration.

"Well now, you can get right back up after taking my punch? You certainly live up to the reputation that earned you this assignment."

His tone was as polite as Glinck's.

But while Rei might have been able to respond, Eland and the guards who heard those words couldn't even move slightly.

They understood instinctively. The one standing before them was an opponent they could never hope to defeat.

It was a relationship akin to absolute predator and prey.

And there were three such beings.

No matter how they struggled, it was meaningless. Like trying to stand alone against a landslide—utterly hopeless. An overwhelming... something beyond even a difference in strength.

"May I ask who you are?"

Rei gripped the Death Scythe, fixing a sharp gaze on the man in his twenties who had spoken.

He had a tattoo on his right cheek, but even accounting for that, his features could be considered handsome.

The man in his thirties possessed a rock-solid physique, while the old man was so small he seemed like he might break if touched... his build was actually not much different from Rei's.

"Yes, you may ask. Though whether I choose to answer is another matter entirely."

His expression was calm, his tone gentle. Judging by that alone, he looked like nothing more than a nice young man, but no one present saw him that way.

"...I see. Then I'll have to force you to tell me who you are. I suppose I owe you that much for landing a blow on me. ...Eland, step back with the guards for a moment. I'd tell you to leave, but they don't seem inclined to let you. Make sure you don't get caught in the crossfire of my attack."

「Rei？」

Ignoring Eland's words, Rei concentrated his mind to activate the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour. At that very moment... An arrow suddenly fell from the sky, piercing the ground as if to sever the connection between Rei and the tattooed men.
