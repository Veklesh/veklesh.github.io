The day after completing the extermination of the three Lizardman Leader-led packs, Rei finished his meal and walked through town with Vihera and Set, just as he always did.

As usual, whenever they spotted a stall giving off a delicious aroma, they bought food for themselves and Set to eat as they went.

"Are you sure it's okay? I could treat you to this much."

Rei's gaze turned to Vihera.

They had bought several skewers of kobold meat from a stall selling them, but for some reason, Vihera hadn't ordered any.

Rei asked her about it, puzzled, but Vihera just gave him a look of exasperation.

"Look, eating street food with Rei and the others is fun and I'm happy to do it, but I am a woman. You know what happens if I eat more than I move, right? Especially in this outfit."

Vihera's outfit, as always, was a sheer, thin garment that revealed what lay beneath, proudly displaying her stunning figure.

Even without her almost aggressive beauty, any man would want to embrace her, and any woman would envy her figure.

It was supple like a carnivore's yet flaunted femininity to the utmost degree.

"...Nah, I don't think you need to worry about it that much. Right?"

"Hot! Huh? Ah, ah. Yeah. Of course I think so too."

The stall owner, grilling skewers, found his gaze drawn to Vihera's body. He let out a cry of surprise and pain as a spark flew up.

But true to his role as a skewered meat vendor, he quickly seemed to forget the pain and agreed with Rei.

Being a young man in his twenties, he was naturally not yet jaded.

That very fact meant that when faced with the sight of a beauty like Vihera—whose figure deserved the adjective "peerless"—standing right before him, it was only natural for his consciousness to be stolen away.

Whether it was good luck or bad luck that the result was only a minor burn was unclear.

"Hmm, ahem. Anyway, it's a huge help having Rei and Set here. It's only been a few days since the monster hordes started rampaging, but all sorts of problems are already cropping up."

The shopkeeper said this, as if to cover up his momentary fascination with Vihera's form.

At the end of the shopkeeper's gaze stood several merchants and their guards.

They looked visibly displeased, each arguing with the others.

The merchants were telling their adventurer guards they wanted to leave Gilm immediately, but the guards opposed it.

From the merchants' perspective, they wanted to get out of Gilm as quickly as possible, where multiple monster swarms were appearing simultaneously. However, from the perspective of the adventurer escorts, if they were attacked by a swarm, it would be difficult to protect the merchants completely.

Moreover, even as escorts, they had no desire to willingly charge into a place where death was highly likely.

The merchants' perspective versus the guards' perspective.

The discussion—or rather, the near-argument—between them echoed throughout the area.

"I've told you! Under these circumstances, there's no way we can protect you if we leave Gilm now!"

"But what about us? We're here in Gilm for business too! If we stay like this, we'll just waste expenses and end up in the red. Besides, there probably aren't any particularly powerful monster groups around yet, right? So then..."

"Don't be ridiculous! Even if they're just goblins or kobolds, whether we can handle them depends entirely on their numbers!"

"But we're not here in Gilm for fun either. If we don't deliver this cargo by the contract deadline, we'll have to pay compensation!"

"Which is more important to you—your life or your money?"

"Both, obviously!"

"...If you absolutely, positively must get out of Gilm, I can only offer one suggestion. Instead of a carriage, ride a horse and keep galloping at full speed. That way, you won't be attacked by monsters while you're moving."

That implies monsters will attack if the horse isn't running.

And horses can't run forever.

After running for a while, it needs to rest, and when that happens, the chance of being attacked by monsters naturally increases.

Of course, if you're traveling on horseback alone, the chances of monsters spotting you are lower than if you were traveling by carriage.

But if you are spotted by monsters in that state, there's no escaping. You'll be killed and eaten.

Merchants who come to the frontier town of Gilm understand this, as one would expect. I cannot agree to going by horse alone.

The biggest issue is that if you go by horse alone, you'd have to leave the wagon and its cargo behind in Gilm.

Some merchants could stay behind in Gilm, but that would create other problems.

"Isn't that exactly why we need escorts? To handle these very issues!"

"Don't be unreasonable. There are limits to what we can and cannot do."

Similar exchanges echoed from several other locations.

Noticing Rei's gaze, the skewered meat vendor seemed to understand what he was looking at. He opened his mouth, looking slightly troubled.

"As you can see, many people can't leave Gilm because of this commotion. Especially merchants—a lot of them have contracts for supplies and such. Some merchants come here for direct trade, but even they have deadlines for transporting the goods they buy."

"But that adventurer's point is valid, isn't it? Forcing a move now, given the current situation, would just make us easy prey, right?"

"Yeah. I get that part... But the fact is, some people have actually made it through the swarm and reached Gilm. Seeing that, it's not unreasonable for them to think maybe they could make it too, right?"

The shopkeeper's words were undeniably true.

After all, a caravan that looked like it had just arrived in Gilm was moving down the main street.

The reason it was clear they'd just arrived in Gilm was the numerous gashes on their wagons.

Moreover, the horses pulling the wagons bore slash wounds, clearly indicating they had been attacked by someone.

"Seeing people like that, it's only natural for merchants to wonder if maybe..."

"Yeah. ...But considering they're trapped in Gilm, the people in this town seem surprisingly calm."

"Well, something this large-scale is rare, but monster swarms appearing isn't unusual."

The shopkeeper man smiled as he replied, likely delighted to be speaking with Vihera, a woman whose beauty could rightly be called peerless.

While the situation was indeed serious, as Vihera had said, for the shopkeeper—who chose to live in Gilm rather than leave it—it wasn't that much of a bother.

"Normally, if we were trapped inside the town, we'd have to worry about food supplies... but we don't need to worry about that. In fact, we have plenty of food left over."

That's why I'm running this shop, the shopkeeper continued.

Hearing this, Rei and Vihera looked convinced.

With so many monster groups present, it was only natural that they would need to be defeated.

The corpses of the defeated monsters were naturally brought back to Gilm as various materials and food.

The fact that the monsters forming these packs were humanoid, and no powerful monster packs had been discovered yet, was a major factor.

...Of course, should such a powerful monster pack be discovered, high-rank adventurers would be dispatched immediately.

Just like the lizardman pack Rei and Vihera were sent to deal with.

In that sense, the shopkeeper's claim that Gilm actually has a surplus of food isn't wrong.

"The real hassle is the non-food issues, though. ...Like luxury goods. We've got plenty of meat, but things like flour and such are getting pretty tight. Still, it's a relief not to have to worry about starving."

"Well, I don't have to worry about bread or anything like that."

Inside the Misty Ring, there was plenty of bread.

And it's freshly baked, fragrant, incredibly delicious bread.

Seeing Rei like that, the stall owner shot him an envious glance.

For a vendor selling food, an item box that holds endless supplies and where time stands still inside is something they'd kill for.

"Anyway, Rei, we should get going soon. Maybe the guild has some information?"

Rei nodded at Vihera's words, who hadn't eaten the skewered meat.

It was fine to talk here, but as Vihera said, the guild might have some information about this recent orc pack.

Especially for Rei, who was eager to hunt down the orc horde, wanting to get information as soon as possible was only natural.

"Grrr?"

Beside Rei, Set growled.

As if conceding to Set's unspoken question of "Leaving already?", he bought a few more kobold skewers and left the spot.

"Hmm, sorry, but we haven't received any information about the orc horde yet."

Kenny shook her head at Rei's words upon arriving at the guild.

Normally, he should have asked Lenora, but she was currently assisting another adventurer.

Fortunately for Rei, the adventurer Kenny was assisting had just finished their business, allowing him to speak to Kenny instead of Lenora.

For Kenny, it could be considered good luck.

This wasn't because she'd treated the adventurer she'd been assisting before Rei arrived poorly; it was purely a matter of timing.

"I see. What about the other groups?"

"Quite a few goblin groups are out. Some kobolds too. And unusually, there's even a werewolf pack."

"...That's unusual."

Werewolves. As the name suggests, they're humanoid monsters with wolf faces.

They possess a high level of social organization, like lizardmen, but they are by no means friendly toward humans.

They are monsters that resemble beastmen, yet are fundamentally different.

Calling a werewolf a beastman is like calling a human a monkey.

For dog-type beastmen... especially wolf beastmen, it was an insult severe enough to potentially spark a duel.

"True. However, werewolves are inherently pack-forming humanoid monsters. In that sense, when a higher-tier species like this one appears, they become significantly stronger all at once. That's also why, unlike other monsters, there are many packs led by leader species."

"Even if lizardmen are said to be social, they mostly fight alone in battle. Thinking about it that way, werewolves, with their wolf-like pack hunting traits, would be formidable opponents."

Vihera added to Kenny's words.

He had his thoughts about Vihera, but he kept work and personal matters strictly separate.

Nodding at the explanation, he spoke again.

"That's right. Since they normally act in packs, it's extremely troublesome when a higher-ranking species appears. Even so, if it's the weakest leader type among the higher-ranking species, we might still manage somehow."

"As meat... isn't it that tasty?"

"Just as Rei said. Rank D monsters like orcs and lizardmen, but apparently the meat tastes quite foul. Though I hear a skilled cook can turn that foulness into flavor."

"The stench becomes delicious, huh... Not really interested, are you?"

Vihera, meeting his gaze, thought for a moment before nodding.

This was because Vihera hadn't fought a werewolf yet either.

While Vihera personally preferred fighting powerful enemies one-on-one, she wasn't averse to battling foes that worked together.

Rei, on the other hand, was intensely curious about what kind of delicious dish that beastly-smelling meat could become... And so, the two found themselves in agreement.

If Byune had been here, she might have stopped them... but unfortunately, only Rei and Vihera were present.

Kenny had been unsure which adventurers to assign to the werewolves, who sometimes outnumbered other packs due to their effective coordination and leader species command. Having Rei and Vihera take this request was a huge help.

"Speaking of which, Marina said she'd look into this matter. Have you heard anything yet?"

"No. I haven't heard anything. Since it involves the Guildmaster, I think they'd handle it immediately if they found out anything, so I assume they haven't discovered anything yet."

Rei answered Rei's question. When asked if they should meet with her, Rei decided it would be rude to interrupt, so he headed off with Vihera to hunt down the werewolf pack.
