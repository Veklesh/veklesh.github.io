After Ajax left to fight the goblins, Rei headed to Lugurno's place with Ilse.

Rugurno, who was quite skilled and never overbearing but always courteous to others, naturally became the representative figure among the adventurers.

Of course, some adventurers resented the idea of placing themselves under someone else's command. But when asked if they wanted to handle the tedious tasks themselves—like negotiating with woodcutters and the guild, or dealing with other adventurers—they also refused.

Ultimately, pride and the hassle... but above all, the fact that Rugluno was well-liked by Rei was a major factor. In the end, everyone accepted Rugluno as their representative.

"Hey, Rei. What's up?"

Ruguruno, who had been giving instructions—or rather, requests—to the surrounding adventurers about who would go defeat the goblins and who would stay behind to continue guard duty, was slightly surprised to see Rei approaching him.

"Ah, I needed to talk to you. This is Ilse."

"Ah, well... Nice to meet you, I'm Ruguruno. So, what's the matter with this young lady?"

"Ah... How should I put this? Actually..."

Rei was about to explain why she and Rugluno were here, but Ilse cut her off.

"Rei-san, please let me explain. We're here because of my reasons, after all."

For a moment, Rei hesitated, unsure what to do about Ilse's words. Ultimately, though, he accepted it, thinking that if Ilse said so.

Ruguruno, too, seemed to grasp from their exchange that something troublesome was afoot. After giving instructions to the surrounding adventurers, he motioned for the two to step aside to a more secluded spot.

Once they were in a spot where the other adventurers couldn't hear, Rugluno spoke again.

"So, is this going to be a hassle?"

"Well, troublesome it is. ...Ilse."

Prompted by Rei, Ilse spoke up.

"Nice to meet you, I'm Ilse. Actually, the reason Rei brought me here today is because I believe the person responsible for my parents' deaths is here..."

Having stated this, Ilse briefly explained the situation.

Of course, she didn't go into the deeper details, but even so, Rugluno seemed satisfied.

"I see. I was wondering why an adventurer suddenly joined us..."

"You're not angry?"

For Rugluno... and for those working in this Treant Forest, it felt like they'd been dragged into a vendetta that had absolutely nothing to do with them.

With Rei standing right there, it wouldn't have been surprising if he'd at least been a little annoyed, even if he didn't yell at Ilse. So why was he so calm?

Of course, his temperament—not as hot-blooded as many other adventurers—played a part.

But that wasn't the only reason Ruluguno was so calm.

"Regardless of why new adventurers were assigned here, the fact is monsters have been appearing in Treant Forest more frequently lately, albeit gradually. Considering that, it actually worked in our favor."

Considering goblins had actually infiltrated Treant Forest, Ilse couldn't deny his words.

Of course, Rugluno probably wasn't thrilled that their workplace was being used.

But he knew complaining to Rei would only make things worse, so he was trying to stay positive.

"I'm glad to hear you say that. Now, about Ajas... After talking it over, it seems settled that he'll be Ilse's avenger."

"...I see. That's unfortunate. He was a capable adventurer, easy to get along with and always serious about his work."

True to his words, Rugluno murmured with genuine regret.

He shook his head immediately and spoke again.

"So, what happened with Ajas?"

"He heard about the goblin attack and jumped at the chance to head over there. Well, that's why we ended up here."

"I see. So, what should I do?"

As Rygurno asked this, Rei tried to answer, but his words halted for an instant.

He noticed the gaze fixed on him.

No, if it was just being looked at, he probably wouldn't need to worry about it.

After all, Rei knew he stood out.

He was used to people looking at him.

But... the gaze directed at him now was one filled with fear and dread.

And in the current situation, the number of people who would direct such a gaze at him was limited.

(If it were that woodcutter... I forget his name, but the one who fell for Marina at first sight... well, that would be kind of funny.)

As I thought this and scanned the area for signs of others, the source of the gaze pulled away, as if timing it perfectly.

(Damn, let him slip away. Well, given the situation, that gaze was definitely Ajash's.)

Feeling the presence recede, Rei spoke up.

"Sorry, but could you wait a moment?"

"Huh? Oh, sure. Well, I don't mind, but..."

"Rei-san?"

Both Ruguruno and Ilse nodded in response, though clearly taken aback by Rei's abrupt request.

Leaving the two behind, Rei exited Treant Forest.

「Set！」

Calling out to the sky like that, Set, who had been flying overhead, naturally didn't miss the call and descended to the ground.

Those already accustomed to seeing Rei and Set were one thing, but there were also those who hadn't seen them much yet.

Those people watched in undisguised surprise as Set, who had been soaring high in the sky, descended at Rei's single call.

"Grrrrrrr"

Set descended, purring happily, and rubbed his face against Rei's.

Play? Play? As Rei stroked Set's head, who seemed to be pleading for it, he spoke.

"Sorry, but playtime will have to wait. Just now, someone escaped from Treant Forest... no, returned to Gilm, right?"

He deliberately avoided saying "escaped" here. Knowing that only Rei, Ilse, and Rugurno were aware of Ajas, he didn't want to carelessly upset the workers by saying something reckless.

...Though that caution was immediately undermined by Rei's next words.

"Follow that escapee. But you don't need to capture or kill him. See where he's headed... Ah, yes. If he's heading toward Abuero, then capture him after all. And once he returns to Gilm, find out where he's hiding."

"Guruu!"

Rei stroked Set, who let out a cry at his words. As if that were the signal, Set took a running start, then flapped his wings and soared skyward.

"...Huh?"

As Rei watched Set depart, he suddenly snapped back to reality and noticed the strange looks the woodcutter and adventurers were giving him.

"Hey, wait. Didn't you say you were going to capture him?"

"The one who left here was Ajas, right? Said he wasn't feeling well or something. ...What did that guy do?"

"Whoa, pissing off Rei? That's nothing short of suicide."

Realizing several people were looking his way and having that conversation, he remembered what he'd asked Set to do and flashed an "Oh crap..." expression.

But the fact was, saying anything now was pointless.

Given that, I decided not to try to cover anything up. I just let their glances pass over me and walked straight into Treant Forest.

(Ah, but if I send Set away, won't that cause trouble for me when I return to Gilm?)

The thought crossed his mind, but he needed to check where Ajax had fled first. Honestly, since Set had already flown off, there was nothing he could do about it anyway.

...Though, if it's Set, there's no denying the possibility that if Rei calls out loudly, his keen hearing might pick up her voice and bring him back.

"Ah... um, guess I'll head back for now."

He muttered to no one in particular, then turned and headed back into Treant Forest, almost as if escaping the stares gathering on him.

There, naturally, Ilse and Rugurno were still waiting, talking about something.

"Sorry, did I keep you waiting?"

"Ah, Rei-san. No, it's fine, but what happened?"

Ilse asked, wondering if Rei had some information about Ajas.

But Rei, understanding what Ilse was thinking, shook his head.

"No specific news on Ajas right now. I just asked Set to track him down, so we should find out where he fled."

"Huh? Escaped? Without reporting anything to me?"

Rugurno was startled by the two of them talking like that.

After all, he was in charge of coordinating the adventurers guarding the woodcutter.

He was likely shocked to hear that they had left Treant Forest without informing him, even if only as a formality.

Though, Rei personally believed it was only natural that Ajas fled.

If he'd been targeted and still remained in Treant Forest, that would show some guts, he thought.

"That's how it'll be. Probably back to the base in Gilm... or maybe the secret hideout. Personally, I'd prefer it if he went to the secret hideout."

Rei's words showed he hadn't considered the possibility of finding Set at all.

Though Rei trusted Set unconditionally, he didn't hold that belief without reason.

He simply couldn't imagine anyone spotting Set flying at a hundred meters altitude.

"You know where he usually stays in Gilm, right? Then I really want you to look for the secret hideout."

Ilse said this with a faint smile, but the aura emanating from her body, though clumsy, could easily be called a killing intent.

"Yeah, that'd be a big help. ...But they must know who I am... no, who Ilse is. They'll definitely be on high alert."

"Well, if they're wary of us, that's fine by me. ...But more importantly, what are we going to do now? If we sent Set out, then to get back..."

Ilse said this, but unlike before, she looked slightly happier.

Clinging to Set's front paws to fly here must have felt almost traumatic for Ilse.

The absence of Set meant she could hope to avoid the same grueling journey back to Gilm she'd endured coming here.

Rei was surprised by how quickly her attitude changed, but for Ilse, the time spent getting here with Set must have felt like hell.

To begin with, not many beings in this world could experience flying.

Dragon Knights, summoners who contracted with large monsters, tamers who had tamed creatures... There were probably a few other ways, but broadly speaking, that covered it.

In that sense, Ilse had experienced something extremely rare... but for her, it was far from something she could welcome or be thrilled about.

"Ah, in that case, I was thinking of borrowing a carriage. Of course, once I arrive in Gilm, I'll return it here. ...Is that alright?"

"Huh? Hmm, well... It's fine for you and the others to ride it for travel, but I'm a bit uneasy about the driver returning here alone. Ideally, we'd need to arrange for an escort..."

The number of adventurers guarding the woodcutters had increased due to the Ajas incident.

But even so, unlike before, with goblin-like monsters increasingly appearing in Treant Forest as seen earlier, there wasn't exactly a surplus of adventurers.

Given that Ajas had returned on his own accord, Rugluno preferred not to reduce their numbers any further if possible.

That said, Rei didn't want to walk or run all the way to Gilm either.

If it were purely a matter of stamina, it wouldn't be a problem at all, and if it were just Rei, that might be acceptable. But this time, Ilse was with him.

Ilse, who primarily handled gathering-type requests, wasn't particularly physically capable.

Sure, she was probably above the average person in that regard, but still below the average adventurer.

And right now, Rei needed to get back to Gilm as quickly as possible. He couldn't slow down to match Ilse's pace.

(Maybe I should just carry her?)

That's what Rei was thinking, but before he could voice it, Rugluno spoke up.

"Understood. If it's just one carriage's worth of people, we can split into other carriages. It'll be a bit cramped, but we can manage. Please take one carriage."

And so, Ilse was spared the humiliation of being seen by everyone being carried by Rei, who was shorter than her.
