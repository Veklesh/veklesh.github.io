"Hey, did you hear? They called in a special instructor for our mock battles starting today."

"Yeah, I heard. But what kind of guy is he? If they went out of their way to call him, he must be pretty confident in his skills."

"Hmph. From my perspective, any opponent is the same. Though if I could choose, I'd prefer it not be some lowly scum."

"Damn it, it's Insula again."

"Leave him be. Honestly, he's got the skills to back up his talk. He's already got a spot lined up in the knight order after graduation. ...Why not try buttering him up now, while you can?"

"No thanks. Why would I want to deal with some rotten noble? Besides, I'm becoming an adventurer after graduation. I couldn't care less about the Knights."

"Hey, keep your voice down! Look, Insula's glaring at us."

"Let's change the subject. ...So, what kind of person is this mock battle opponent anyway?"

"We haven't changed the subject. If anything, we're back to it."

This conversation was happening inside one of the buildings on the military academy grounds.

It was essentially a gymnasium, used for exercise during winter and rainy weather.

Being the academy that trained knights for the duke's household, the gymnasium was heated by magic items, creating a comfortable space that made it hard to believe it was winter outside.

Most of the students present were in their late teens, though a few in their twenties could be seen as well.

This situation arose because they had entered at different ages.

Being S-Class meant this academy gathered students of exceptional ability, but that very fact sometimes led them to overestimate their own power.

Of course, this overconfidence was confined to the school grounds; once they graduated and became knights or adventurers, they would often face harsh realities.

"They're here!"

One of the noisy students shouted sharply.

Simultaneously, all eyes focused on the gymnasium entrance.

Two figures had arrived.

One was Glink, one of the mock battle instructors.

His shaved head and muscular build made an immediate impression of strength.

And the other one... As everyone's gaze followed Grink to the person accompanying him, audible sighs of disappointment rose.

No, some even wore expressions of mockery.

Could such a small person really teach them anything in the mock battle?

It was the typical reaction upon seeing Rei.

If there had been someone skilled enough, they might have been able to gauge his strength from his demeanor.

But those present were skilled fighters only within the confines of their own school.

No one present could grasp the sheer magnitude of the power gap between them and Rei.

"Hey, hey, is that guy really the new instructor? For the mock battles?"

"...What? That guy?"

"Well, he's wearing a robe, so he's probably an adventurer or something... but he doesn't look particularly strong."

The students exchanged whispers, their voices barely audible to those around them.

It wasn't out of consideration for Rei, but for Glinck.

Contempt, doubt, superiority. Knowing full well the various stares directed at Rei, Glink still said nothing as he led Rei to stand before the students.

Glink understood that anything he said here would be largely meaningless.

Therefore, rather than speaking himself, letting them see his strength with their own eyes would be the quickest way for Glink to prove Rei's abilities.

He glanced over the lined-up students, confirmed no one was absent, and opened his mouth.

"Well then, it seems all the S-Class students are present. Before we begin today's lesson, let me introduce this person."

Hearing the word "this person" from Glink's mouth, every student's face showed surprise.

It was only natural. Even though only S-Class students were present, none of them had ever defeated Glink.

For Glinck to introduce someone so formally like this was unprecedented.

Despite his gentlemanly nature, which belied his appearance, he rarely made introductions like this.

"For the next few months... specifically, for the short period until spring, I'll be co-teaching the mock battle class with Rei."

Even hearing that, no one could react immediately.

They could only wonder who it was at the end of those gazes.

"Rei, please."

"Understood."

Prompted by Glink, Rei lowered his hood and stepped forward.

The moment they saw his face, most of the students were at a loss for how to react.

It was only natural. The one at the end of their gaze was their own age... no, clearly younger.

Though all the confused stares were directed at Rei, he didn't seem particularly bothered. He'd encountered people reacting this way to his appearance countless times before.

Admittedly, some girls found Rei attractive, but they kept those feelings hidden.

...Certain privileged noble students, their sense of entitlement laid bare, felt irritated by Rei's demeanor, but Rei didn't care about that as he spoke up.

"I'm Rei. ...Well, just saying that might not make it clear what kind of person I am. Rank B Adventurer, Crimson Rei. That should tell you who I am."

The moment the word "Crimson" left Rei's lips, the students listening to him began to murmur.

A murmur that seemed to forget Instructor Glink was present.

It was only natural, now that they knew the man before them was a living legend.

"Hey, Crimson... Is that for real?"

"I don't know about that. But from what I've heard, Crimson was supposed to have a griffin, right? I don't see it anywhere."

"If you're going to mention it, wasn't it also said he wields a great scythe? He doesn't have that either, right? So, is it all just nonsense?"

"That's the last thing I'd expect. Do you think Professor Grink would deal with someone impersonating him? Besides, if he's standing here as our teacher, the Headmaster must have met him too. Fooling that Headmaster... you don't seriously think that's possible, do you?"

"So he's not a teacher, just an instructor... well, whatever. You mean he's been fooling around without the headmaster noticing?"

"...Are you serious? Besides, even if you tried something like that, the Headmaster's familiar would spot you in a heartbeat."

It was common knowledge that the headmaster had a bird familiar that flew around the academy grounds.

He claims he's just patrolling the grounds, but how many people actually believe that?

Whatever the reason, it was a fact that the headmaster's familiar bird flew around the school grounds.

Ask if shady characters could sneak past its watchful eye and boldly enter the academy grounds? Anyone familiar with the Headmaster would almost certainly say no.

In fact, there was a track record of suspicious individuals attempting to enter the school grounds several times a year being apprehended.

Considering all that, it was hard not to conclude that the person standing before them was indeed the adventurer known by the alias Crimson, just as he had claimed.

Some wondered if he was using a different name in front of the headmaster and had adopted the Crimson name here, but they quickly dismissed the idea, reasoning it would serve no purpose.

Glancing at the students, who could be described as being in a state of semi-confusion, Rei spoke again.

"Well, it seems many of you still can't believe I'm the real deal, even seeing me like this. ...Honestly, I don't blame you. So here's what we'll do. To quickly convince you I'm the genuine article, I want to hold a mock battle. Since I'll be your mock battle instructor, I also want to properly gauge your abilities as students."

The students fell silent for a moment at Rei's words, but quickly showed their displeasure.

Even those who didn't voice it clearly wore expressions that were anything but friendly.

Surprised by their unexpectedly stubborn resistance, Rei wore an expression of astonishment.

Though, admittedly, that surprise also contained a significant amount of astonishment at the students' inability to gauge their own strength.

"Very well. Then let me be your first opponent. If you truly are Deep Crimson, that would be a dream come true for me. But... if your skills don't match your big talk, an apology won't be enough!"

The one who shouted was a man who looked like he was undoubtedly a noble. Rei didn't know him, but the students called him Insula.

Because the metal armor he wore was clearly not something a student would typically carry.

It was gear a Rank C adventurer could easily be seen wearing.

(Though it's meaningless if your skill doesn't match it.)

He must be confident in his skills, even among those present. That much was clear from his speech just seconds ago.

But... even so, his skill was hopelessly inadequate.

"Very well, then. I'll take you on. Grink, where are the mock battle weapons?"

"Over there... Guys, sorry, but could you bring them over?"

Glink instructed a student, who then gathered several others nearby to fetch a basket stored against the gym wall.

Inside were several weapons: longswords, spears, battle-axes, halberds.

Of course, being weapons for mock combat, the blades were blunt.

...Even with the blades blunted, metal weapons were heavy, making them blunt instruments in their own right.

If Rei swung one with his strength, it would become a deadly weapon.

Holding such a weapon... he glanced at the man who had just challenged him to a mock battle and suddenly realized.

"Huh? What's up? I thought I challenged all of you to mock combat?"

'What!?'

At Rei's casually muttered words, all the students let out gasps of astonishment.

Of course they would. They were the fourth-year S-Class.

Meaning, they were the most skilled individuals in this entire academy.

They believed that if the entire class attacked together, they could even defeat Grink.

And that was indeed true.

And yet, the man standing before them now had just told them to come at him all together.

To the students, it was unbelievable.

Though, to be fair, only the students thought that. Glinck silently agreed with Rei.

Here lay the weakness of those who had never truly experienced real combat.

Even if they had experienced combat before, it was mostly in situations where they held overwhelming advantage.

It wasn't even the frontier, so monster numbers weren't high, and bandits near prosperous places like Graciall were cunning, rarely showing their tails.

Under such circumstances, there inevitably became a shortage of worthy opponents to fight.

"Don't be ridiculous. There's no way we can take on this many..."

"No problem."

From the basket the student brought, Rei pulled out a spear.

It's no secret that Rei's most proficient weapon is the scythe, the Death Scythe.

But such a niche weapon wouldn't be prepared for a mock battle, so he took the spear he used most often after the scythe.

That said, Rei primarily used his spear for throwing, and he didn't have much experience fighting with it in the conventional way.

Still, he judged that he could handle a man wielding a mock battle longsword like the one before him, and he readied the spear in his hand.

"Come on. I'll show you with my own strength why fighting me alone is pointless."

"Don't you dare!"

His patience must have reached its limit at Rei's words. Insula swung his longsword high, preparing to bring it down on Rei... but before he could, he suddenly realized the tip of Rei's spear was pressed against his face.

"Huh?"

Insuara let out a dumbfounded gasp, clearly bewildered by what had just happened.

He himself couldn't comprehend what had just happened, and neither could the others watching the sequence unfold around them.

In the blink of an eye, something had moved swiftly, and before anyone knew it, the spearhead gripped in Rei's hand was pressed against Insula's face.

"What? Is it over already?"

"Huh!? No! That was just me getting careless!"

"I see. Then let's try again."

Pulling the spear back toward himself, Rei took a few steps away from Insula.

Realizing this was the signal to restart, Insula readied the longsword in his hand once more.

However, the expression on his face was not the same anger as before.

He didn't know if the man before him was truly the one called Crimson, but he understood this opponent possessed considerable skill.

Given that, underestimating him would only put him at a disadvantage... Insula shifted his mindset.

"Haaaaaaaah!"

With a battle cry, he lunged forward, swinging his longsword down toward Rei.

"Too slow."

But what Insula had swung with all his might was, to Rei, barely worth a yawn... well, that might be a slight exaggeration, but it certainly wasn't a powerful strike.

The spear tip gently caught the blade of the descending longsword, absorbing the shock as it wrapped around it... By the time Insula realized it, the longsword's hilt was no longer in his hand. The sword, now entangled by the spear, danced through the air.

In the same fluid motion, the spear's tip was thrust before his eyes... and Insula experienced complete defeat.
