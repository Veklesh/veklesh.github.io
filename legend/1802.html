After satisfying their cravings with baked goods, Rei and his companions return to exploring Anesis.

That said, in this world, travel for the sole purpose of sightseeing is rarely undertaken.

Given that, even if you ask Miranda or Leodanis, “Are there any tourist attractions in Anesis?”—since travel isn’t generally practiced here—there’s no way such things could exist.

Well, to be precise, they might exist, but since seeing them is part of everyday life, people probably don’t think of them as tourist attractions.

In fact, there was an episode of a TV show Rei watched back in Japan where someone who had visited a place with a crystal-clear cobalt-blue sea raved about how wonderful the scenery was, yet the locals—who had seen that view since birth—looked puzzled, as if they couldn’t understand what was so special about it.

And so, Rei and the others wandered through the streets of Anesis without encountering any tourist attractions... but just as they turned left at an intersection, a massive building suddenly came into view, and Rei stopped in his tracks.

“What’s that huge building?”

“Huh? That one? Oh, that’s the library. You’re right. Now that you mention it, they say there aren’t many libraries on this scale, so you could probably call that library one of Anesis’s landmarks.”

“...It’s huge.”

Listening to Miranda’s explanation, Rei was once again struck by the sheer size of the library.

The library in Gilm that Rei had visited a few times was also quite large.

Since arriving in this world, Rei had visited various villages and towns, but none of them had a library larger than the one in Gilm.

In fact, there were many villages and towns that didn’t even have a library to begin with.

Yet, Gilm had a library—and it was the largest Rei had ever seen.

In fact, the library in Gilm was larger than the city library Rei had visited back in Japan.

Of course, it would be a different story if we were talking about a prefectural or national library, but unfortunately, Rei had never been to such places, so he had nothing to compare it to.

In any case, the fact that Gilm’s library was so enormous wasn’t simply a matter of showing off…

Of course, vanity likely played a role, but Gilm’s location on the frontier was the primary factor.

Precisely because it was a frontier, there were not only unknown monsters, but also plants, minerals, and countless other as-yet-unknown entities.

Naturally, when it comes to researching these things, the more reference materials available, the better.

The guild maintains a certain amount of resources, but if you want to look into something in greater detail, going to the library is the best option.

The ancestors of the Daskar family, who have ruled Gilm, understood this and collected books voraciously... but the library currently before Rei’s eyes is undoubtedly larger than Gilm’s.

(Is this the difference between a town and a city? Ah, but if that’s the case, does that mean Gilm’s library might also expand once the construction is finished?)

As Rei was captivated by the library’s size, Miranda asked, perhaps sensing his thoughts.

“Want to take a quick look inside?”

“Uh… I’m interested, but I’ll pass for today. It’s already getting late.”

Winter evenings fall early.

Besides, Rei and the others didn’t leave the mansion until after noon.

There was no way they could stop by the library now just to read a book.

Well, maybe I could read one or two books, but naturally, I’d want to read a variety of books if I’m going to do this.

That’s exactly why Rei says he’d prefer to come to this library when he has more time.

“Ah, in that case, if you get a permit from the master, you can use the library for free. Of course, since that permit is essentially a sign of the master’s trust in you, if you damage a book, people will say the master didn’t know how to judge character.”

“……Ah, I’ll think about it.”

Of course, Rei has no intention of deliberately damaging any books.

But considering what might happen if something went wrong, he couldn’t just casually ask for a permit.

Leodanis, who had been watching their exchange from the side, felt relieved while trying not to let it show on his face.

This was because, while he didn’t mind moving his body, he simply didn’t enjoy the act of reading books.

Leodanis’s honest feeling was that, if he had to choose, he’d much rather be moving his body than reading a book.

...And yet, since he isn’t exactly bad at paperwork, it seems that what he likes and what he’s good at are two different things.

“Is that so? Well, to use the library, you’ll need to pay an admission fee and a deposit in case you damage a book, so please don’t forget that when you come.”

“So that part is no different from the library in Gilm.”

Since he uses the library in Gilm fairly regularly, Rei felt reassured that he wouldn’t be confused when using this one.

(That said, I won’t be in Anesis for very long. I probably won’t have many chances to visit this library.)

Feeling a touch of regret at that thought, Rei sets off walking through the town again.

Set, Leodanis, and Miranda followed after Rei.

As usual, some people look confused at the sight of Set, but thanks to Leodanis’s—or, more accurately, the Knights’—authority, they manage to resolve any issues as they walk. Suddenly, a voice reaches Rei’s ears.

“Hey, hey, you think you can get away with just that after picking a fight with me? You’re the ones who started this, so show a little more guts, will you?”

“H-h-hey… s-sorry. It was our fault for picking a fight with you. So please forgive us! Okay? Please. Ah, I don’t have much, but I do have some money…”

“Sigh… What a pathetic bunch. Do you think you can win just because there are more of you? Ah, forget it. Get lost. You’re an eyesore.”

After hearing that exchange, several men emerged from a street a short distance away from Rei and the others.

They left the area, leading what appeared to be injured comrades—as if fleeing—though they were likely actually fleeing.

“A fight?”

Leodanis muttered upon noticing the men, but the others walking around merely glanced at them with a hint of surprise—and that was all.

In a crowded place like Anesis, there are bound to be a fair number of hotheads, so it’s probably nothing worth making a fuss over.

In fact, Leodanis shows no sign of chasing after the fleeing men to ask what happened.

“There were a lot of them. …Just so you know, don’t go causing any trouble, okay? If Rei starts causing trouble, I won’t be able to stop him.”

I’m slightly surprised by the words that come out of Leodanis’s mouth.

I never expected him to admit that he wasn’t strong enough to stop me himself.

The Leodanis that Rei knew was someone who had absolute confidence in his own strength.

After all, at such a young age, he belongs to the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights, considered one of the elite forces in the Kingdom of Mireana, so it goes without saying that he possesses the strength to match.

But Leodanis’s confidence was shattered in an instant when he was completely overwhelmed in a mock battle with Rei, unable to do almost anything.

That said, considering Leodanis’s future, it was probably fortunate that he learned this in a mock battle where there was no risk of death.

If he’d found that out in a real battle, he probably… no, he would have definitely died.

“Listen, could you please stop talking like that as if you’re assuming I’m going to cause some kind of trouble?”

Rei said this while petting Set, but Leodanis couldn’t stop casting a suspicious glance at him.

“...Given what I’ve heard about Rei, it does seem like you’re assuming I’m going to cause trouble.”

At Leodanis’s words, Rei tried to retort… only to realize, quite casually, that he couldn’t.

Considering everything he’d done so far, it was only natural that people would think that of him.

That said, it wasn’t as if he regretted any of it.

“Oh, someone’s coming out.”

Miranda said this, hiding behind Leodanis.

The reason she chose Leodanis over Rei to hide behind was likely because Leodanis was a member of the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights, whereas Rei was merely a guest.

Was her ability to quickly assess the situation a result of her instinct as a maid?

In any case, realizing they couldn’t keep arguing any longer, Rei and Leodanis turned their gaze toward where the men had emerged.

……However, Rei wasn’t wary of them.

Since Set, who is usually sensitive to any kind of hostility, hadn’t reacted at all, she assumed there was probably no problem.

Of course, just because they aren’t hostile now doesn’t mean they won’t become so once they meet and talk, so she wasn’t completely letting her guard down.

(The guy who took down that many people, probably all by himself. …Well, even if a bunch of thugs band together, it doesn’t mean much in this world.)

In this world, quality easily surpasses quantity.

That’s why Rei can easily wipe out a band of thieves and even trample the armies of great nations.

When you think about it, defeating a group of thugs isn’t all that unusual.

Gilm... no, if it were Gilm before the expansion, many adventurers would have been able to do just that.

Though, of course, that’s only because most of the adventurers who gather at Gilm are skilled fighters.

What appeared before Rei’s gaze—half filled with curiosity—was a man of considerable stature.

That said, a giant in and of itself isn’t all that rare in this world.

The reason Rei was surprised—albeit only slightly—was that he understood at a glance that this man possessed the kind of skill that wouldn’t be out of place even in Gilm.

Since Anesis is the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, it’s not surprising to find skilled fighters there.

So why, then, did it still come as a surprise?

Rei’s question was answered the very next moment.

“Lord Brewitt!? Why are you doing this in a place like this!?”

Leodanis, who had been keeping watch beside Rei, let out a cry of astonishment.

No, Leodanis wasn’t the only one surprised. Even Miranda, who had been hiding behind him, was staring in shock at the man called Brewitt.

“You… is that armor from the Kerebel Duke’s Knights?”

“Yes. I am Leodanis, a member of the Kerebel Duke’s Knights.”

Seeing Brewitt say this, Rei vaguely realized the identity of the giant standing before him.

Considering the way he addressed him earlier, there was almost no doubt...

(He’s a noble. And likely a high-ranking one, or someone with influence.)

Though he thought that, the man’s attire was hardly what a noble would wear.

It didn’t look dirty, but it was still the sort of thing an ordinary person would wear.

Yes, it was certainly not the kind of clothing a knight like Leodanis would pay respect to.

“Oh? That’s a griffin over there. So, are you Rei of Crimson?”

Brewitt, having averted his gaze from Leodanis, looked at Set and then at Rei standing next to him and asked.

Rei was momentarily unsure how to react to Brewitt’s attitude, but eventually spoke up as usual.

“Yeah. I’m Rei, an adventurer in Gilm. And you? Judging by Leodanis’s reaction, you must be some big shot… But why on earth would a big shot like you be getting into a fight with someone like that in a place like this?”

As he says this, Rei looks at Brewitt’s physique.

He’s wearing a cloak to ward off the cold, but even so, it can’t hide the impressive muscles on his body.

And yet, the fact that there wasn’t a single scratch or speck of blood on his face, body… or even his cloak meant he hadn’t allowed those thugs from earlier to land a single blow.

Or perhaps he moved so quickly that he avoided all the blood splatter.

(No, considering his massive build, he likely prioritizes the power of a single strike over speed. …Though that raises its own questions.)

When fighting by using one’s own strength and weight as a weapon, movements inevitably become sluggish.

Of course, he’s probably faster than the average person… but even so, not fast enough to avoid the blood spatter from his punches.

Or, if he were capable of breaking bones with a single blow, he wouldn’t have to worry about blood splatter… but I confirmed that one of those thugs from earlier was clearly bleeding from the face.

(Hmm... I’m starting to get interested.)

Glancing at Brewitt, Rei waited for his reply, looking forward to seeing what kind of response he would give.
