In the end, Rei and his group managed to wipe out the flock of Yellowbirds with almost no casualties.

There was no doubt that Rank D monsters were a nuisance for the adventurers in Golz, but for Rei and his group, they weren’t exactly formidable foes.

Of course, the fact that they could fly made them extremely troublesome, but Rei’s group included Set, who could fly freely through the air; Rei, who could move about the skies with considerable freedom thanks to the Sleipnir Boots; and Elena, who, though not quite as powerful as Rei’s enhanced version, also possessed the Sleipnir Boots.

And with many others in their group possessing abilities to attack flying targets—such as Marina, who used spirit magic—the Yellowbirds were no real challenge to Rei and his companions, even if they came in a flock of dozens.

It was extremely difficult to locate the Yellowbirds that had emerged from the dungeon, but since they returned to the dungeon where Rei and the others were—presumably due to some sort of homing instinct—wiping them out wasn’t a difficult task.

“Well, anyway, we’ve defeated the Yellowbirds…”

Watching the Golz adventurers who had been there before Rei and her group arrived gather the Yellowbirds’ corpses, Rei spoke up.

The one he addressed was Relieu, known by the nickname “Gale.”

“That’s right. For now, the emergency quest is complete. …What’s up?”

Since he’s wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, Rei can’t clearly see his expression, but for some reason, Relieu notices that Rei is giving him a strange look.

“Why are you looking at me like that?” Relieu asked, and Rei realized she hadn’t grasped what he was trying to say.

“That flying slash that attacked the Yellowbird. What was that?”

“Hm? Oh. That. Come to think of it, you were using a similar attack, Rei. Is that why you’re curious?”

“Yeah.”

Since it was true that he was curious, Rei nodded back without showing any sign of being flustered.

Relieu was slightly surprised by Rei’s reaction, but since there was nothing to hide, he spoke frankly.

“That skill is kind of my trademark.”

“……So it is a skill, after all. I was actually wondering if it might be some kind of Magic Sword.”

Like the Magic Palm used by Vihera, it’s a unique technique—a skill—that utilizes magical power, even though it doesn’t rely on traditional magic.

Rei’s assumption was that since Relieu didn’t use wind magic, it was likely a skill.

Of course, there are Magic Swords that can unleash such special effects by using magical power.

However, from what Rei could see, while the longsword Relu held was certainly worthy of being called a masterpiece, it didn’t appear to be a Magic Sword.

As someone whose hobby is collecting magic items, Rei’s judgment was reasonably accurate.

……That said, this was only in comparison to an amateur; he was still far from the level of a professional alchemist.

In any case, since it wasn’t a Magic Sword, he immediately realized it must be a skill of some sort.

“This is just a regular weapon. That skill is Wind Slash. It’s my specialty.”

“Wind Slash, huh. It’s exactly what it sounds like.”

“Well, if you’re going to say that, what about your Flying Slash? That spell is pretty much the same thing, isn’t it?”

“……I won’t deny that.”

For a moment, following the flow of the conversation, Rei was about to say that Flying Slash was a skill, but then he remembered that he’d classified Death Scythe’s skills as his own magic, so he nodded at Relieu’s words.

“It seems we can both use similar abilities, even if we call them magic and skills.”

“Yeah. Plus, those kinds of skills are actually pretty handy.”

At Rei’s words, Relieu nodded thoughtfully.

In fact, for Relieu, who wields a longsword, the Wind Slash skill is incredibly useful.

So much so that it had practically become his trademark.

“I’ve finished collecting the Yellowbird corpses. What should we do now?”

As Rei and Relu were talking, one of the adventurers called out to them.

At those words, Rei looked over toward where the Yellowbirds had been gathered, and just as the adventurer had said, the bodies of all the slain Yellowbirds were piled up there.

Since the Yellowbirds themselves are fairly large, the sight of dozens of them all lying dead formed a rather massive pile.

“Well, since you guys helped out, you can each take one of the corpses.”

“……Huh? Really?”

Had they not expected to receive such a benefit? The adventurer who had come to report to Rei—and the other adventurers who had heard Rei’s voice—all broke into delighted smiles.

If the Yellowbird’s meat were delicious, Rei wouldn’t have been so generous.

However, while Yellowbird meat is edible, its flavor isn’t particularly special.

Of course, it wasn’t bad, but it didn’t have the exceptional flavor of, say, an orc.

Given that, it’s fine to secure a few, but when the number reaches dozens, it’s only natural to realize that the effort of butchering them would be a hassle.

After all, now that it’s certain he’ll get two magic stones, everything else is just a bonus for Rei.

“Ah, you guys helped us out in all sorts of ways. Just think of this as your reward.”

“I don’t really remember helping that much, though. As for throwing stones at these guys, we were the first ones to throw them, and we barely hit anything anyway.”

That’s what the man said, but from Rei’s perspective, being able to ask about the local terrain, what kinds of monsters fall from what appear to be dungeons, and how often they appear… that kind of information was incredibly valuable.

Although he had heard about the situation from Walker, there was no doubt that getting information directly from someone who had experienced it firsthand was the best approach.

The men didn’t feel they’d been particularly helpful, but they figured that since he was an adventurer with a nickname, it wouldn’t be out of character for him to be this generous, so they gratefully accepted the Yellowbird.

For Rei and the others, the Yellowbirds weren’t exactly a tasty catch—beyond just the meat—but for the adventurers from Golz, they were likely a more than satisfying prize.

“Well, we’ve taken out all the Yellowbirds for now… By the way, there’s no chance there’s another flock of Yellowbirds out there besides this one, is there?”

“Huh? Oh, yeah. Nope. That’s all of them.”

“I see. Then there’s no problem. …Well, now we just have to figure out what to do next.”

Relieved by the man’s words, he turned his gaze toward the dungeon carved into the cliff face at the end of his mutter.

It wasn’t even sunset yet, so while it would be impossible to fully explore the dungeon at this hour, there was no harm in just checking out the area around the entrance.

Since we’ve come this far, maybe we should take a quick look around, he thought…

“Rei, we should hold off today.”

Elena said this, stopping Rei.

To be honest, Elena also wanted to see what the dungeon on the cliff face looked like.

However, they had originally come here on a mission to defeat a flock of Yellowbirds, so she told Rei that it would be best not to get involved in anything else.

It’s probably safe, but since it’s an unknown dungeon, there’s no doubt that something might be lurking inside.

“Ah.”

As if to interrupt Elena’s words, a male adventurer muttered softly.

At the sound of his voice, everyone present—including Rei—instinctively looked toward the dungeon, where they saw several goblins falling toward the ground.

They thrashed wildly in midair, but since they couldn’t fly, there was nothing they could do.

As a result, the goblins were smashed against the ground and killed.

“……I see. So that’s how they fall. There’s no way they’d survive that.”

Since it was the first time he’d seen a monster fall from the dungeon and die, there was a somewhat bittersweet tone to Rei’s words.

Whether the dungeon possesses something akin to a will, Rei couldn’t say.

But looking at the dungeon’s core, he thinks it wouldn’t be surprising if it did.

If that’s the case, why did it build a dungeon in a place like this?

That was the only question on his mind.

Rei harbors this question, but it is one he can only ask because he has experienced several dungeons up to this point.

For the residents of Golz, since this is the first dungeon they’ve ever seen, this must be what they consider normal.

In fact, even after seeing this scene, they didn’t seem particularly moved and called out to Rei.

“Um, if it’s okay with you, I’d like to take the magic stones and the proof-of-extermination parts from the goblin corpses. Is that alright?”

For Rei… no, for most adventurers in Gilm, setting aside the goblin’s magic stones, the proof-of-extermination parts weren’t exactly something they’d go out of their way to want.

Of course, since they’ve already killed the goblin, some might think it’s a waste not to take them, and it’s a different story for those who’ve made it to Gilm despite still being low-rank for some reason, or those who became adventurers right here in Gilm.

In any case, as far as Rei was concerned, he had no desire to go out of his way to the location of the goblin’s corpse just to strip the magic stone and proof-of-extermination parts from it.

“Ah, do whatever you want. Just be careful—there might be other monsters falling from the dungeon. It’s one thing to die fighting a monster, but dying because you got hit by a falling monster would be ridiculous, wouldn’t it?”

“Got it!”

With a cheerful shout, the man headed toward where the goblin had fallen.

“What’s with you? You’re acting pretty worried. Do you like him or something?”

Rei, who was generally not at all good with people, had even gone so far as to warn him to be careful.

It wasn’t surprising that Marina was giving him a slightly teasing look.

“I don’t really mean it that way. But there’s no doubt I’m grateful for all the information he gave me. So, it’s only fair to give him a little advice, right?”

Even as he said that, the man who had gone to retrieve the goblin’s magic stone and proof of defeat parts seemed quite accustomed to rummaging through the corpses of monsters that had fallen from the dungeon.

Even without Rei going out of his way to warn him, he’d naturally be keeping an eye on his surroundings.

(Well, if what’s lying on the ground is a monster that’s worth more money than an orc or a goblin, there’s a good chance his attention is focused on that instead.)

Even though he understood that monsters could fall from above, he simply couldn’t help but focus his attention on those valuable monsters.

“Hmm. …If Rei says so, then I guess it’s fine. It’s a big help to us that the adventurers from Golz are cooperating with us. …In that sense, I think we did a great job accepting the Yellowbird extermination request and taking them all down on the same day.”

At Marina’s somewhat meaningful remark, Rei gently averted his gaze.

“Y-you’ve already defeated them!?”

When Rei returned to the Golz Guild and reported that the quest was complete, the receptionist’s voice echoed throughout the room.

Given that the guild had expected this group of flying bird monsters to take not just a day, but several—or even over ten days—to defeat, they likely never imagined the quest would be resolved within the same day.

The reason they were able to resolve the quest so quickly was that the Yellow Birds had returned to the dungeon due to some sort of homing instinct… in other words, they had come to where Rei and the others were on their own, like moths flying into a flame. So, while it was more a matter of luck than actual skill…

(“Flying into the fire”… or rather, it might be more accurate to say the duck came carrying the green onions, the pot, and all the other ingredients on its back.)

Thinking that, I placed the Yellowbird’s beak and magic stones—the parts designated as proof of defeat after butchering it by the cave—on the counter, excluding the two that Death Scythe and Set would absorb.

I didn’t forget to add the bag of feathers and the raptor-like claws as materials, either.

“Also, I gave the adventurers near the cliff one carcass each of this monster… the Yellowbird. That’s not a problem, is it?”

“Huh? Oh, yes. Since those were monsters Rei and her group defeated, we have absolutely no problem with that.”

Saying that, the receptionist began processing the completion of the request and the purchase of the materials and magic stones.
