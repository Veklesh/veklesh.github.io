Spotting Rei entering the guild, receptionist Kenny flashes a smile.

She thought it was lucky that Rei came when Lenora wasn't around because he had business with Guildmaster Walker.

...But when she saw the beautiful green-haired woman accompanying Rei, her expression turned slightly displeased.

But Rei seemed completely oblivious to Kenny's displeasure as he headed for the guild counter... Since Lenora wasn't there, he naturally went to where Kenny was.

"Kenny, where's Lenora?"

"Lenora? She's with the Guild Master. Seems she had some business to attend to. ...So, Rei. Who exactly is that beauty over there? A client?"

If she's a client, that's fine.

That thought crossed Kenny's mind as he asked Rei, but Rei shook his head in response.

"No, not really. ...I came because I wanted to ask Lenora or Kenny for a favor."

"...Something you need? Could it be about recruiting new members for Crimson Wings?"

Kenny asked, glancing at Ilse.

The dissatisfaction had already vanished from Kenny's face, replaced by a look of curiosity.

As the guild receptionist, Kenny had seen many adventurers.

He possessed an eye that could gauge their strength to a certain degree.

To Kenny's eyes, Ilse, who was with Rei, simply didn't seem to possess the ability necessary to belong to the Crimson Wings... or even a Rank B party.

Then again, if we're talking about not being worthy of their rank, Byune was also in that category, but in Byune's case, she possessed a reasonably high level of combat strength.

Even Kenny could see that Byune's combat strength was undoubtedly higher than Ilse's.

That was precisely why he couldn't bring himself to fully support that person joining Crimson Wings.

That's what he thought.

But contrary to Kenny's thoughts, Rei shook his head.

"No, it's not about a new member for Crimson Wings. It's just that I definitely have a favor to ask regarding Ilse."

"...Something best kept private?"

"If possible."

Seeing Rei's expression, Kenny must have realized this wasn't some kind of joke.

Glancing around, he called out to the receptionist nearby.

"Sorry, but I need to step away for a moment. I have something to discuss with Rei."

"...Hmm. Well, I'm not busy right now, so it's fine. But come back as soon as you can."

With most adventurers busy with their own work, the number present at the guild during the daytime was low.

That said, this was only a comparison to mornings or evenings. Compared to the usual daytime crowd before the expansion, the number of adventurers present was clearly higher.

"Yes. I'll be back as soon as possible. ...Also, when Lenora returns, could you tell her we went upstairs?"

After leaving that message, Kenny headed upstairs with Rei and Ilse.

"Come on in."

The room Kenny led them to wasn't the large conference room they'd used several times before, but a small one—about the size of a six-tatami-mat room, in Rei's estimation—suitable for a single party.

Inside were a conference table and several chairs.

"Sorry about this. I really wanted to use the bigger room, but... the other one's a bit out of commission right now."

"Unavailable?"

"Yes. You see, it's because of the expansion work we're doing right now..."

Hearing that, Rei understood.

He didn't know exactly who was using it, but he figured it was probably occupied by people involved in the expansion work.

"That's fine. I'm not the type to complain about not having a big room. ...Honestly, it feels a bit much for just the three of us to use such a large room."

Kenny smiled and nodded, clearly pleased by what Rei had said.

"I'm glad to hear you say that. ...So, what was this business you needed?"

Kenny gestured for them to sit down and talk. Rei and Ilse sat down and spoke up.

"Actually, there's something I need your help with."

"Help? Well, if it's a request from you, Rei, I'd probably agree to most things. ...Is it dangerous, by any chance?"

Kenny, the receptionist, knew full well how skilled Rei was as an adventurer.

But since Rei himself was asking for her help, she could easily imagine it wasn't something trivial.

Could it be that some major conspiracy was brewing again?

Kenny asked, her heart heavy with unease. Rei, however, spoke without any sign of tension.

"Do you know an adventurer named Ajash? Rank D?"

"...Ajash? Hmm, hold on a sec."

Caught off guard by the sudden mention, Kenny closed his eyes, trying to recall the name.

After a moment's thought... he seemed to remember, and a smile spread across his face.

"You mean the guy with the snake tattoo on his left hand?"

"Yes, that's the one."

When the words "snake tattoo" came up, Ilse, who had been listening beside Rei, showed a momentary flicker of tension. But whether she decided to leave this to Rei... or feared that mentioning anything about Ajas would make him furious, she remained silent for now.

"So, what about Mr. Ajas? He doesn't seem particularly noticeable, nor is he exactly a skilled fighter, is he?"

In a village out in the sticks, a Rank D adventurer could easily be the best fighter around.

But this was Gilm, deep in the frontier.

In this land where skilled adventurers gather in droves, a Rank D adventurer is considered average at best... and in some cases, it's not uncommon for them to be treated as below average.

The fact that Kenny remembered such a figure—one you could almost call a nobody—was likely due to the large snake tattoo on his left hand.

"About that Ajas guy. I'd really like to team up with him on a job sometime. Is there any way to make that happen?"

"Huh? Isn't that impossible?"

Kenny dismissed Rei's suggestion with surprising speed—almost reflexively, you might say.

"No good?"

"Yeah... I think it'd be a bit difficult. After all, Rei is one of the most famous people in Gilm, right? What possible reason would someone like Rei have to team up with a Rank D adventurer?"

Faced with that, Rei couldn't argue.

Realistically, it was quite unusual for a high-rank adventurer with a nickname to team up with a Rank D adventurer.

Of course, if the other party was an adventurer Rei already knew, teaming up through connections wouldn't be unusual, but this time was different.

Rei knew about Ajas from what Ilse had told him, or from the information Egg and the others had gathered, but Ajas didn't know about Rei.

Well, to be precise, he probably knew of him, but it would be more accurate to say they had no direct acquaintance.

For that reason, pairing Rei and Ajas is difficult... Kenny couldn't help but agree.

"Can't you work something out around that?"

"Hmm... Well... I don't think it's possible with just Ajas alone, but if we could gather several other adventurers and have them act together with Rei-kun, it might be doable. However, in that case, I think it would become quite troublesome, you know?"

"Hassle?"

Rei tilted his head at Kenny, who spoke as if something were stuck between his teeth.

But inwardly, Kenny let out a happy scream at Rei's reaction, though she hid it as she continued her explanation.

"Yes. Adventurers who've been in Gilm for a long time know what kind of personality you have, Rei, so I don't think many people would do anything foolish anymore."

That he didn't say "none" meant Rei's appearance still had an impact.

Whether he was aware of it or not, he just let out a sigh tinged with resignation and said nothing more.

Smiling at Rei's demeanor, Kenny spoke again.

"But among the people who came for this expansion project, many only know Rei-kun through rumors... rumors of Crimson. That's precisely why some might act strangely based on their own preconceptions."

"Yeah, probably."

In fact, Rei had already been approached by several adventurers, or even woodcutters who weren't adventurers.

The Crimson Rumor was that he had single-handedly burned the entire Bestir Empire Army to the ground, and that he showed no mercy, even to nobles.

Hearing those rumors, no one in their right mind would think of messing with Rei.

But... among those who came to Gilm, there were still some who ultimately dismissed them as mere rumors.

Among them, a small number still believed the rumors were either spread intentionally by Rei himself... or that all of Rei's achievements were solely due to the griffin, Set.

What such people would do if they ever encountered Rei... well, that was something Kenny preferred not to think about.

Admittedly, part of it was concern for Rei, but more practically, even the fools entangled with Rei were essential labor for the current Gilm.

When it came to Gilm's expansion project, labor was never enough... the more, the better.

The more hands, the faster the expansion of Gilm would be completed.

That said, the sheer number of people arriving was already causing problems with finding places for them to stay.

Since the goal was to gather as much labor as possible, Daskar naturally couldn't just ignore that issue.

Currently, several locations within Gilm were urgently preparing buildings to serve as lodging.

Unused vacant houses were being repurposed as temporary lodgings.

It was a rather forceful tactic only possible because Daskar was the lord of Gilm.

...In any case, with that many people gathering in Gilm, it was impossible to treat Rei the same as those who had been in Gilm from the start.

You could say it was a matter of those who had actually experienced Rei's terrifying nature, which didn't show on the surface, versus those who hadn't.

"But there's no need to worry. Unless they do something truly reckless, we'll handle it peacefully too."

"...But with Rei-kun, I just can't believe that 'peacefully' part."

Even Kenny, who held strong affection for Rei, couldn't fully trust Rei's words.

This was also due to the impact of Rei's past actions... in a way, it was Rei's own doing.

"Anyway, if it's just about getting Ajas into the group, that's fine, right?"

"Yes. Fortunately... though I hesitate to call it that, we've recently heard there's a shortage of manpower in Treant Forest. If it's just leading them there, I think we can manage."

"The Treant Forest? There are plenty of woodcutters over there, and adventurers chop wood when they're not busy, right?"

They'd get paid for escorting the woodcutters, and if they cut trees on top of that, they'd earn even more money.

For adventurers, that meant substantial rewards.

Rei understood Treant Forest was one of the popular spots.

When asked about it, Kenny shook his head in denial.

"It used to be like that, but that was assuming monsters didn't appear in Treant Forest, right?"

"So you're saying it's different now?"

"Well, yes. More accurately, it's changing... that's probably the right way to put it. Little by little, monsters have started appearing. So naturally, adventurers have to provide escort services as requested, right?"

"I suppose so."

"...Well, some adventurers figured they didn't need to do the escorting and got absorbed in cutting down trees instead..."

"Whoa."

Kenny's words made Rei gasp in surprise.

Their job as woodcutter escorts was their primary duty as adventurers; tree felling was merely a side task.

Yet, to be blinded by the promise of extra pay and neglect their primary duty was, by any normal standard, unthinkable.

"Naturally, that adventurer can no longer accept commissions in Treant Forest, though."

Even so, there were plenty of jobs outside Treant Forest, and even adventurers like that had to be used... had to be utilized effectively. That was the current state of affairs in Gilm.

Of course, if it were a truly terrible individual... someone like Ajas, who is believed to be the enemy of Ilse, the subject of this current discussion, then Gilm himself would have to take various measures.

"What do you say? Personally, I think this is fine for now."

"...I beg you."

At Rei's words, Ilse bowed deeply.
