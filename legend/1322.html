When Rei, Marina, Vihera, and Byune entered the tavern, the gazes of the patrons inside naturally turned toward them all at once.

This tavern, tucked away on a back street, was the kind of place where everyone was basically familiar with each other.

While regulars sometimes brought friends, increasing the crowd, Rei and the others were different.

Everyone was visiting this tavern for the very first time.

Well, Vihera had actually been here once before... but either no one present knew that today, or they simply didn't mention it.

...Meanwhile, Set was sprawled out near the tavern as usual.

Anyway, under normal circumstances, it wouldn't be surprising if someone approached complete strangers like this... especially Rei, wearing a party dress with a plunging neckline, and Vihera, clad in what could be described as the thin, revealing attire of a dancer or courtesan suited to this kind of tavern.

Even the obviously small, childlike Byune would normally be ignored if she entered a tavern like this.

But... even now, seeing Rei and the others enter the tavern, no reckless fool dared to hassle them.

The regulars here... meaning those who'd been in Gilm long enough to know.

They knew what kind of people Vihera and Marina were... and who was with them.

No one seemed to notice Byune's presence, but in a way, that was probably inevitable.

Anyway, the four of them sat down at an empty table without anyone bothering them.

Though Rei and the others had come to speak with the informant introduced by the security squad, they figured it was only natural to order something since they'd entered the tavern.

The other patrons, observing Rei and his group, likely concluded they hadn't come to this tavern looking for trouble.

Judging that getting involved carelessly would lead to trouble, they averted their eyes from Rei and his group, enjoying drinks among themselves.

...Though perhaps they were just being extra cautious not to attract attention by being too rowdy, drinking a bit more quietly than usual.

"What would you like to order?"

A tavern attendant approached Rei and the others' table and asked.

Unusually, it was a male attendant instead of the female staff commonly seen in taverns.

Whether this was the norm for this tavern, or if it was because it was still daytime... or perhaps because it was Rei and his group, was unclear. When asked for their orders, Rei and his group didn't seem particularly bothered and simply stated what they wanted. Marina and Vihera ordered wine, Rei ordered wine diluted with water, and Byune ordered milk.

Normally, ordering wine diluted with water or milk would likely draw unwanted attention... but naturally, no one reckless enough to bother them here would show up.

Beyond that, this type of restaurant often features stewed offal and beans prepared with their own unique seasoning.

They casually ordered skewers, stews, braised meats... and bread.

"We'll bring it right away, please wait a moment."

The server who took our order headed toward the kitchen, looking slightly surprised.

...Well, that's to be expected. The dishes we ordered weren't particularly special.

They're standard fare at this kind of tavern.

But what probably surprised the server was the sheer volume of food ordered.

It was several times the amount usually ordered by patrons drinking and eating normally in this tavern.

The stewed offal and beans varied in taste depending on the establishment, but since most places' versions suited Rei's palate, he ordered enough for ten people.

He also ordered quite a lot of other dishes. Consequently, the men unlucky enough to be seated near Rei's table couldn't believe their ears... and inwardly muttered, "Go to a cafeteria, not a tavern!"

Then, as several pre-made dishes were placed on the table, Marina asked the waiter.

"Do you sell flowers that bloom on moonlit nights?"

Twitch.

At Marina's words, the waiter froze, then finally spoke.

"Come dawn, we might just get a shipment in."

"So... I should come before the moon sets and the sun appears?"

"...No. That won't be necessary. I can show you to a room where we can talk right away. What do you say?"

"Right. Could you show us there after we finish eating?"

"Understood. We'll arrange it that way. We take pride in our cuisine, so please enjoy your meal."

After exchanging the code phrase the guard had given her and arranging to meet the informant after finishing her meal, Marina reached for the bread on the table.

"Oh?"

The bread felt softer than she'd expected. Marina voiced her surprise.

It wasn't freshly baked... she could tell that much since the bread wasn't warm.

But still, the fact that it felt soft to the touch was undeniable... and that was what surprised Marina.

Of course, soft bread isn't rare in Gilm, even if it's not freshly baked.

But finding such bread served in a tavern tucked away on a back street was completely unexpected.

Surprised by Marina's reaction, Rei reached for the bread while savoring the stewed fangboar.

And he felt the same surprise as Marina.

"For what it looks like, this tavern sure serves some delicious food."

Vihera murmured in surprise as she savored the Fangboar stew.

Even though it's called stew, it's not simply a matter of throwing meat into a pot and boiling it.

Various preparations are necessary beforehand: seasoning the meat, searing the surface when appropriate, tying it with string to maintain shape, or scoring it with a knife to ensure even cooking...

The actual stewing process also demands skill—controlling the heat, seasoning, simmering time...

In that sense, the stewed fangboar on the table now seemed far too refined to be the kind of dish you'd expect from a backstreet tavern.

Even the skewered items brought out as an extra were perfectly cooked—crisp and fragrant on the outside, yet carefully prepared so the inside wasn't overcooked.

Of course, compared to professional restaurants like Manpukutei or Kogane no Pan-tei, it fell slightly short.

But even so, as fare served in a tavern like this, it was undoubtedly top-notch.

(So that's why this backstreet place has so many customers.)

When Rei and the others entered, there had been a few empty tables, but now the additional customers who had arrived were sitting at those tables and ordering food.

The newcomers were likely regulars at this tavern. They didn't draw stares like Rei and the others did.

That said, they did seem slightly surprised by the unusual atmosphere inside the tavern.

That surprise vanished when they saw the table piled high with food that Rei and the others had ordered.

Time passed as they ate their food, and Marina and Vihera had a light drink... Eventually, when most of the food on the table was gone, Rei called over a server and settled the bill.

The bill was quite high, but considering the quality of all that food, it was a reasonable price.

"Please follow us this way. There's someone waiting for you."

Nodding at the words, Rei and the others were guided by the staff to a private room at the back of the restaurant.

"Hm?"

"Hey... don't forget why we came here in the first place."

Vihera muttered exasperatedly at Byune, who seemed to be thinking, "Are we just going to leave like this?"

Seeing Vihera like that, Byune finally seemed to remember why they'd come to this tavern. She stopped complaining and let herself be led into the private room.

The private room Rei and the others entered wasn't particularly spacious.

It was less a room rented out for parties by wealthy patrons and more a private room for confidential meetings.

After all, what Rei and the others were doing here wasn't a banquet, but buying information from an informant. Thinking of it that way, it wasn't wrong at all.

"You lot are customers? What do you want to know?"

The man drinking ale while seated in one of the room's chairs asked them upon seeing Rei and the others.

The man who had guided Rei and the others to this room had stepped outside and was not present.

This served two purposes: to keep watch for anyone approaching the room, and to ensure no one overheard the information broker's conversation with Rei and his group.

Many had lost their lives after overhearing information they shouldn't have.

This was likely a self-defense measure to prevent that from happening.

"We want information on a person named Precious from the Spier Trading Company."

"...I see. The Spier Trading Company, the one making headlines lately."

"Talk of the town?"

Rei responded to the informant's words.

If the Spier Trading Company was that much of a hot topic, it wouldn't be strange for it to reach Rei's ears.

This wasn't because Rei was actively gathering intelligence; it was simply information he picked up while buying snacks, overhearing shopkeepers or passersby chatting.

Of course, this information came in the form of casual chatter and rumors, so it lacked certainty.

But that very lack of certainty meant it was incredibly fresh information.

"Ah. Though, when I say 'talk,' I mean among the underworld crowd, you understand. Spier Trading Company does some pretty aggressive business. You know that, right?"

"I've heard about it."

"I see. And they make sure to properly inform the underworld figures too. Most trading companies do that much, but Spier Trading Company spends a lot of money building connections within the underworld."

"Is that... normal?"

It was news to Rei that most trading companies engaged in such practices.

But this isn't entirely a bad thing.

It's not unusual for a merchant guild to have various dealings with underworld figures.

Of course, if underworld figures were to appear openly, the guards wouldn't stand idly by... but such occurrences are rare.

"I wouldn't call it normal... but it's not exactly rare either. Anyway, that's how the Spier Trading Company has become fairly well-known in Gilm's underworld."

"I see. Well, I understand about the Spier Trading Company. And what about Precious?"

"Unfortunately, I don't have any information on that person."

Hearing that, Rei remembered that the informant before him, while a guard's personal informant, wasn't exactly a top-tier source.

(Come to think of it, that's true... Still, I managed to get some pretty useful info even so.)

It surprised Rei that it wasn't uncommon for trading companies to have connections to the underworld, not just Spier Trading Company.

But given that security wasn't as good as in modern Japan, it was only natural they'd feel the need to protect themselves.

Knights and guards existed, but their numbers were limited.

Moreover, during spring and late autumn, large crowds gathered in Gilm, and places like this one became too chaotic for the security forces or knights to handle.

That's when the underworld figures prove extremely useful.

"Then, could you gather information on Precious?"

"...You asking for information, Rei? This must be related to that incident in the Noble District, right?"

The informant stated this, but Rei showed no surprise.

After all, the man from the underworld earlier already knew about it.

In fact, if someone like that knew about it but the informant didn't, it would undermine the credibility of the man's skills as an informant.

"Yeah. It's related... I think. The circumstantial evidence definitely points to that guy, but I don't have solid proof."

"Hmm, I see. ...Honestly, it seems rather complicated, but I'll look into it. But naturally, the fee will be commensurate, right?"

Even though he was the security squad's go-to informant, he wasn't formally employed by them.

Since he had to earn his own money through honest work, he needed to work properly as an informant.

That said, in this case, the client was Rei, who wasn't exactly short on cash.

In fact, it was almost certain that after this matter was resolved, the Spier Trading Company would pay him some money as an apology and proof of settlement. Paying the informant in front of him a little extra wouldn't hurt his pocketbook at all.

"Alright. I'll pay your asking price."

"Your asking price? Are you sure about that?"

If it was his asking price, then even the most worthless piece of information could command several gold coins... or even platinum coins, or light gold coins.

But Rei nodded back, indicating it was fine.

"You're the guard's own informant. You might ask for something unreasonable, but you won't demand something completely unreasonable without discussion, right?"

This implied that if the informant tried to rip him off, word would get back to the Guard.

The informant silently raised both hands at Rei's words.
