Since the dungeon only had two levels, Rei had little trouble making his way back to the entrance.

Goblins appeared several times, but they too were wiped out by Rei.

As before, he left the corpses behind, but Rei figured the slime would handle them one way or another.

Besides, he couldn't rule out the possibility that other monsters still in the dungeon might eat the goblin corpses out of hunger. And even before that, if the dungeon collapsed, it wouldn't matter anyway.

(Goblin undead... probably not much demand for those, though.)

He pondered these things as he climbed the stairs leading outside.

Soon, the rock crevice Rei had passed through when entering the dungeon came into view... What Rei saw upon exiting was the already dimmed sky and a bonfire burning to ward off the cold.

"Gurrrr!"

Set, who had sensed Rei's presence and magic as he emerged from the rock crevice—or more accurately, as Rei moved from inside the dungeon toward the outside—ran toward him, purring happily.

Rei gently stroked the head rubbing against him as he purred.

Whether he hadn't noticed the skill yet or simply didn't care, he showed no sign of displeasure whatsoever.

When a giant like Set moved, the soldiers nearby naturally noticed.

After all, he'd seen Set react first and take down monsters emerging from the dungeon with a single blow on several occasions. It was only natural they'd be wary of his movements.

"Rei? The dungeon..."

Seeing Rei playing with Set, Ranga asked with relief that no monsters had appeared.

Rei stroked Set's head while pulling the dungeon's core, split clean in two by Misty Ring, from his hand.

At first, Ranga probably didn't understand what it was either. He stared intently at the dungeon core held in Rei's hand for a few seconds... then, realizing what it was, he opened his mouth hesitantly.

"Rei, could that possibly be..."

"Yeah, it's the dungeon's core. We've cleared it."

"...Grr?"

Dungeon core? Set growled upon seeing the spherical, metallic hemisphere Rei held in his hand.

"Sorry, Set."

With Ranga present, he couldn't voice what he felt sorry about, but Set seemed to understand what Rei meant. He rubbed his face against Rei's leg, growling softly as if to say, "Don't worry about it."

Set was disappointed he hadn't gained a skill from the dungeon core either, but he didn't think Rei would deliberately destroy it just to be mean to him.

Even Set could vaguely grasp that some unavoidable circumstance must have led to this outcome.

Set rubbed his head against Rei's, as if to comfort him, showing he didn't mind.

Rei seemed to understand Set's feelings. He smiled and stroked Set back.

"So, Rei. I heard you cleared the dungeon... Could you tell me a bit about that?"

Ranga, who had been watching Rei and Set play, seemed to understand things had settled down. He addressed Rei.

At Ranga's words, Rei nodded in response.

"Sure, no problem. Though there's not really that much to say."

"Well, I suppose so. This dungeon hasn't been around that long, so it probably isn't very deep. ...Which is probably why monsters still come out of it."

Ranga's gaze shifted to the side of the rock that served as the dungeon's entrance.

There, goblin corpses were piled up, their number easily exceeding twenty.

Seeing crystals attached to several of the corpses, Rei turned his gaze toward Set, whose round eyes were fixed on him, begging to be stroked.

It was no longer necessary to hide the fact that Set was a rare species.

Still, he preferred not to draw attention to the Crystal Bracelet that trapped its wearer inside a crystal.

Crystals were useful materials for alchemy and could also be used for accessories.

If someone was blinded by greed, they might consider messing with Set.

(Well, it was my mistake for not telling Set not to use the Crystal Bracelet... No, if someone were to target Set anyway, they'd likely be after Set himself rather than the crystal or whatever.)

I tore my gaze away from the goblin's corpse and returned to my conversation with Ranga.

"So, what do you think? If you notice anything while clearing the dungeon, I'd like to hear about it."

"...Hmm, just one thing. Have you heard that Gamelions are really hard to catch in Gilm this year?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah, they are hard to catch. Gamellion meat is pretty expensive even when you buy it at the store. Since Gamellion is only available this time of year... Wait a minute. You bringing that up here... Could it be?"

Ranga, who had been casually responding to Rei, suddenly widened his eyes mid-sentence as if he'd just realized something.

The other soldiers, who had been listening silently, reacted similarly, all turning their astonished gazes toward Rei.

Rei, meeting their gazes, nodded and recounted the scene he'd witnessed in the hall at the dungeon's core.

"Yeah, the space at the dungeon's core was packed with Gamerians... well, 'packed without a gap' might be an exaggeration, but it was so dense you'd want to say that. I didn't count them properly, so I can't say for sure, but there were probably over fifty."

"...What? Could this be the reason for the low Gamellion numbers this year?"

Hearing Rei's words, not only Ranga but the other soldiers all wore expressions of shock.

Gamellion meat is something you can only eat during this season.

Of course, it could be processed into dried meat or the like, but that inevitably fell short of the fresh taste.

That's precisely why many had been feeling sad, thinking they might not get to eat much Gamellion meat this year...

"So... so then... could we consider sending the gamellion meat to market?"

Knowing just how voracious Rei and Set's appetites were, Ranga asked timidly.

Rei pondered Ranga's question for a moment, then turned his gaze toward Set. After a pause, he reluctantly nodded.

"About ten, maybe."

Rei had initially planned to sell about half at the market, but as they discussed it, his greed had grown slightly.

The fact that Gamellion meat was surprisingly delicious for a Rank C monster had influenced him.

"Hmm... ten. Well, considering the size of Gamerians, it should have some effect..."

"Well, since we've cleared the dungeon, no more Gamerians should be summoned. So, even if it's a bit late, I think we can hunt them from now on. Besides, considering I only got about fifty in the end, it's entirely possible they're just appearing later this year."

"Is that also because of the dungeon?"

"Probably, but I can't say for sure."

The conversation about Gamerians ended there, shifting to other topics from the dungeon.

"Still, that was one dungeon packed with goblins, huh?"

"Yeah, definitely. Probably because it's brand new. I'm sure other monsters will show up as time goes on."

"There were ogres, but they seemed much shorter than the ones I know."

"That's probably because it's a newly created dungeon too."

"As a fighter, that was fine... but the materials are cheap, huh?"

Ranga gave a wry smile and nodded in response to Rei's questioning look.

"From what I've heard, that's how it is. They say even if you gather tons of goblins, the materials are practically worthless."

Hearing Ranga's words, Rei felt deeply relieved he hadn't gone gathering goblin materials.

"Ah, right. I need to give you that, Rei."

Several soldiers called by Ranga approached, each carrying a palm-sized leather pouch.

Handed the pouch, Ray peered inside, puzzled. Inside were crystal shards.

Most were only fingertip-sized, but even so, this quantity was sure to fetch a decent sum.

Glancing at Set, the source of the crystals, Rei returned the leather pouch to the soldiers.

"No, you guys keep this."

"...Are you sure?"

"Yeah. Seems like Set caused you some trouble. Consider it a token of thanks for looking out for him."

"I see. If you say so, Rei, we'll gladly accept it. With the money from selling these crystals, once we get back to Gilm, we'll hit the tavern. It should cover a night's worth of drinks for all of us. Thank you."

Ranga bowed his head slightly, but when he raised it again, a sharp gleam shone in his eyes.

It was the expression of someone in charge of Gilm's guards, not the usual stern face of a kind-hearted man.

"So, Rei. Could it be that Set is some rare or higher-tier species of griffin?"

For Ranga, this was something he absolutely had to ask.

Depending on the answer, he might need to report it to Daskar.

He'd even considered that if it was being hidden, he'd have to find a way to pry it out...

"Huh? Oh, yeah. Set is a rare breed of griffin."

Rei answered Ranga's question without hesitation.

This seemed to surprise Ranga too. His eyes widened slightly as he turned his gaze toward Rei and opened his mouth again, as if to confirm.

"Um, just to be sure, one more time. Is it correct to understand that Set is a rare species of Griffin?"

"Yeah. ...Oh right, I guess I'll be here for a while. Well, no wonder you didn't know. I've already reported this to Lord Daskar. When I joined the civil war in the Bestia Empire, Set used his skill in front of a large crowd. I'd been hiding it until then, but after that happened, keeping it secret was impossible."

"...Huh?"

Though Ranga had a stern face, the way his eyes widened like he was stunned gave him an unexpectedly endearing charm.

Seeing Ranga like that, feeling a certain warmth wash over him, Rei continued.

"Probably... maybe it's not just Lord Daskar. Could it have spread within Gilm too?"

The reason he thought this was because of the existence of the prospective settlers who had come to Gilm with Rei.

The former guerrilla fighters had seen Set using his skills normally, and even those connected to them had witnessed Set in battle when they were attacked by bandits or monsters during their journey.

Normally, such information shouldn't spread so easily within Gilm, but the network of those who cherish Set is extensive.

Naturally, it's highly likely several Set admirers reside within the lord's manor itself, making it entirely plausible for information to leak from there.

And once information leaks outside the lord's manor, it would spread quickly to those in Gilm who also like Set.

Of course, even those within the lord's mansion wouldn't leak information if it were important to Gilm.

However, since Rei himself was no longer trying to hide information about Set, its confidentiality wasn't particularly high.

Hearing Rei's explanation, Ranga and the other soldiers nodded in agreement.

"...I see. That's true."

Living in Gilm, they likely knew just how much influence those who favored Set held.

"Anyway, I understand that part. If Lord Daskar knows about it, I don't particularly intend to say anything either way. So, what are you going to do from here, Rei?"

"What will I do?"

"It's getting dark already. If we head back now, the gates might be closed... Ah, right. With Set's speed, we might still make it."

Looking around, it had still been faintly light when they emerged from the dungeon, but now it was completely dark.

He checked the pocket watch he'd taken out of his misty ring. It was past five in the afternoon.

Returning now, at Set's speed, would undoubtedly be plenty of time.

Should he spend the night here and head for Gilm tomorrow morning, or ride Set back immediately?

He considered which to choose... and made his decision without hesitation.

"I'm heading back to Gilm. While the magic tent would make this cold bearable, I'd much rather sleep soundly at the Dusk Wheat inn. Plus, I want to report the dungeon's conquest to the guild as soon as possible."

"Hmm, you're right. ...Well then, I'm afraid I'll need you to wait a moment. I'd like you to take this report to Lord Daskar informing him the dungeon has been cleared."

"Understood. Do it as quickly as possible."

Hearing Rei's words, Ranga nodded and immediately began drafting the report.

Fortunately for Rei, it wasn't a formal report but merely the initial notification that the dungeon had been cleared, so it didn't take too long to prepare.
