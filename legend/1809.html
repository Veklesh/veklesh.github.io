Rei, who was thoroughly enjoying the meal Georgima had prepared, didn’t initially understand what Liberte had said.

After sharing several meals together, Rei had grown so accustomed to dining with Duke and Duchess Kerebel that he no longer felt particularly self-conscious.

Well, it’s not that he had ever really been bothered by it to begin with, but it would be more accurate to say that he had now become quite relaxed and was able to fully savor the food.

It was during such a pleasant dinner that those words suddenly came out of Liberte’s mouth.

Swallowing the tender, simmered whitefish he had in his mouth, Rei simply repeated what Liberte had said.

“Um, a mock battle?”

“Mm-hmm. Ahead of the New Year’s party, I’ve received a unanimous request from the nobles already staying at Anesis to see Rei’s mock battle.”

“……Why on earth?”

“I wonder. I find it strange too. Of course, it wouldn’t be unusual for them to know that Rei is here in Anesis if they’ve gathered enough information… but would they normally say something like that?”

Arcadia also voiced her confusion.

Logically speaking, it would be a breach of etiquette for the leaders of their own factions—some of whom were nobles from the Royal Faction—to approach Rei, their guest, and suggest he participate in a mock battle.

That said, Rei was not only a guest of the Duke of Kerebel’s household but was also regarded as the right-hand man of Daskar, who led the Neutral Faction.

Given that, it was only natural that nobles from the Noble Faction and the Royal Faction would want to see Rei’s strength.

“Apparently, the reason for this mock battle was that Rei’s name came up during a gathering of the nobles. Well… I hate to say it, but Rei doesn’t exactly look like he’d be very strong.”

Liberte looked at Rei, seeming a bit hesitant to say it.

Although he was wearing the Dragon Robe, he had naturally removed his hood, as was customary in such a setting.

Because of that, Liberte was able to get a clear look at Rei’s face.

A face that could best be described as feminine.

And compared to the members of the Knights’ Order, starting with Philma, Rei’s build was slender.

If someone who knew nothing about Rei were to judge Rei solely by his appearance, they would never in a million years conclude that he was a skilled adventurer with a nickname.

……Though, if the person making that judgment had the ability to see through his opponent’s strength, that would be a different story.

“Father, if it’s just the idle chatter of someone who can’t see through Rei’s strength, is there really any need to listen to it? Or, since Rei has been doing mock battles with Philma and the others lately, perhaps you could have them show you that instead.”

As far as Elena was concerned, she knew exactly just how strong Rei was.

Given that, she felt there was no need to take seriously the words of someone who had gone out of their way to bring up the topic of mock battles with her father.

Liberte could understand his daughter’s reasoning.

However, this time, more people than he had anticipated were expressing concern about Rei’s abilities, and even for Liberte—who had been tacitly accepting the relationship between Rei and Elena—he couldn’t simply let the matter rest.

The biggest concern was that if this were left unchecked, suspicion toward Rei would spread among the nobles, potentially leading to trouble.

And if problems did arise, it was impossible to rule out the possibility that some nobles would claim Rei was indeed lacking in ability and stir up some kind of commotion.

……If the target weren’t Rei, Liberte probably wouldn’t have been so concerned.

However, Rei has a personality that shows no mercy whatsoever, even when dealing with nobles.

Depending on the situation, a massacre of nobles could occur at Anesis, and given that possibility, Liberte could not simply ignore the nobles’ requests.

Even if Rei were to refuse the mock battle, she needed to be informed in advance that the nobles were voicing their suspicions for that very reason.

“If we were to reject this request outright, you can imagine how the nobles would react… Elena, you understand, don’t you?”

“Well…”

Even Elena, who wasn’t particularly close to the nobles, understood what her father was getting at.

Even if Liberte were to reject the nobles’ request without saying a word to Rei, the nobles wouldn’t voice any complaints directly to Liberte.

That said, that merely meant they wouldn’t voice their complaints directly; in reality, they would harbor resentment.

And that pent-up frustration would eventually boil over.

Given that such a scenario was inevitable, it was absolutely necessary to inform Rei that such a discussion had taken place.

Judging that Elena had accepted his explanation, Liberte turned his gaze toward Rei.

“So, what will you do? As for this decision, there’s nothing I can do about it. It all depends on what you decide, Rei.”

“Now that you put it that way… I suppose I can’t very well refuse.”

There were various reasons why Rei said that.

First, until the New Year’s party began, Rei didn’t have anything specific he had to do and was at a loss for what to do with himself.

And then, who had spread such a rumor?

Since arriving in Anesis, Rei hadn’t met that many nobles.

Given that, the first person who came to mind as someone who might spread such a rumor was Gaiska.

Given that this man had shown such intense hostility toward him, it wouldn’t be surprising if he were to speak of Ray’s lack of ability.

That said, there’s no point in bringing it up here.

(Though I was a bit surprised he was capable of pulling off something like laying the groundwork with Gaiska.)

Judging by his attitude toward Ara, the Gaiska Rei had observed was someone who looked down on anyone with a lower title than his own… no, than his family’s.

Naturally, Rei thought there wouldn’t be many people willing to cooperate with such a person.

(Or maybe there are other collaborators besides Gaiska.)

As Rei was thinking this, Liberte spoke up, as if to confirm something.

“So, you’re really okay with taking on this case? There’s nothing in it for you, after all.”

“It’s fine. I knew even before coming to Anesis that there were people who didn’t approve of my relationship with Elena.”

“Uh… I see.”

Liberte looked as if he wanted to say something in response to Rei’s words, but since many of those involved in this matter were from the Noble Faction, he held his tongue.

Instead, he brought up the topic of the mock battles.

“I hear that those who’ve been asking to see Rei’s true strength are hiring their own subordinates or adventurers to face her in mock battles. Is that okay with you?”

“I can only say it’s probably fine. A city as large as Anesis—it wouldn’t be unusual to find people with nicknames or high-ranking adventurers here, would it?”

While it may not be on par with the border towns of Gilm or the capital of the Mireana Kingdom, Anesis is the second-largest city in the Mireana Kingdom.

It wouldn’t be strange at all for skilled adventurers to gather here.

“Adventurers with aliases or high-ranking adventurers, you say. Of course there are some, but I don’t think anyone like that will be involved in this particular incident.”

“……Why is that?”

If the goal is to test one’s own strength, it would only make sense to face a skilled opponent rather than a weakling.

In response to Rei’s questioning gaze, Liberte took a sip of soup before speaking.

“If you’re an adventurer too, Rei, you should know. Generally speaking, most adventurers take the winter off. If you were to hire such people, you’d naturally have to offer a higher reward than usual.”

“True,” she added. But she swallowed the rest of her words.

Under normal circumstances, that would be fine. But this time, it’s a mock battle with Rei, known by the nickname “Crimson.”

Perhaps some might find it amusing and participate regardless of the cost, she thought.

At Liberte’s subtle hint, Rei’s expression flickered with a hint of interest.

Until just a moment ago, he had absolutely no motivation.

That said, if the opponent were something like the Kerebel Duke’s Knights, it might be worth holding a mock battle.

It’s not that he has any particular complaints about his battle becoming a spectacle… well, not exactly, but it’s not something he’d dwell on.

However, from what Liberte had said, it seemed that most of the nobles who had brought this proposal were looking to hire people who could safely be called “small fry.”

That’s why he’d been unmotivated, but if the opponents were people with nicknames or high-ranking adventurers, that would be a different story.

I hesitate to say “fortunately,” but Rei, currently at Kerebel Duke’s residence, doesn’t really have anything specific she needs to do.

Well, he does chat with people like Elena and Ara and attend tea parties, but that isn’t something he does all the time.

Elena has her duties as the Duke’s daughter and the Princess General, and Ara has her duties as the captain of the Royal Guard.

Especially in Ara’s case, while she’s active in combat and training, when it comes to administrative work, she’s practically just a figurehead.

Still, as the commander of the knight corps, there were documents she needed to review and approve.

Since she had to process the paperwork that had piled up while she was away from Anesis, Ara couldn’t very well go off with Rei.

“Father. Have you forgotten that Rei is also an adventurer? If you’re going to have Rei participate in a mock battle, I think you should promise him some sort of reward.”

Observing Rei’s demeanor and concluding that he had become enthusiastic about the mock battle, Elena addressed Liberte.

Elena, of course, was well aware of just how popular she was with men.

That’s precisely why she could easily imagine that this mock battle was the work of those who had their own reasons for bringing Rei along.

Although, to be fair, there were others involved as well.

In any case, since she was dragging him into a mock battle for the sake of some noble’s petty pride, she felt that, given the circumstances, she should at least offer him some kind of reward.

Liberte, too, nodded in agreement with Elena’s words, thinking, “If we’re going to make Rei participate in this mock battle for our own convenience…”

“Hmm, you’re right. Through the guild… no, this matter is strictly personal. In that case, perhaps we should make it appear as though I commissioned Rei directly, bypassing the guild. …Would that be all right?”

Normally, requests that bypass the guild are considered somewhat suspicious, and since any problems that arise must be resolved on one’s own, most adventurers avoid them.

Of course, it was a different story for those who possessed the ability to handle any situation on their own, or those who hoped to increase their reward even slightly by bypassing the guild.

“Um, I was planning to do this even without a reward, so I don’t really mind, but…”

“That won’t do. Even if you don’t intend to, what would others think… that’s the problem.”

“……I see.”

From Rei’s perspective, he’d judged there wouldn’t be any particular problem, but from Liberte’s perspective—who was hosting Rei as a guest—it was only natural that she wouldn’t want to risk being suspected or looked down upon by the other nobles.

Although Liberte was the Duke of Kerebel, the leader of the Noble Faction, his position was by no means unshakable.

Naturally, not everyone within the Noble Faction was united, and some believed they themselves should be the ones leading it.

Especially since he began adopting a cooperative stance toward the Neutral Faction, the number of those who felt this way had been growing.

For that very reason, Liberte absolutely could not afford to show any weakness.

While he couldn’t go into the details of that situation, he explained to Rei that paying her a reward for this incident was absolutely necessary, given that they were luring her into a mock battle.

Rei surmised that Liberte hadn’t explained everything, so he didn’t press for further details.

“So, what kind of reward would you like? Money, as usual? Or… I heard you’re collecting magic items—would you prefer that? Of course, I can’t give you anything that’s been passed down through my family for generations…”

At the mention of magic items, Rei looks slightly pleased.

But before Rei could say anything, Elena spoke up.

“How about a Wind Magic Ore? You mentioned you received a Fire Magic Ore before…”

If Flame Ore is a stone imbued with the magic of fire, then Wind Ore is one imbued with the magic of wind.

Rei wonders if, in that case, Flame Ore could just as well be called Fire Magic Ore or Flame Magic Ore… but I suppose it comes down to how it sounds or the taste of whoever first discovered it.

“Wind Magic Ore? Does that even exist?”

“Mm-hmm. I can’t tell you exactly where it is, but there is a mine.”

And so, the reward Rei would receive was decided.
