"Hmm. This arrow was meant for Rei...?"

Erinde picked up the arrow placed on her desk and addressed the two figures before her—Rei and Glink.

Both nodded in response.

"Yes. Rei managed to dodge it, but that arrow was fired at such speed by someone completely unaware. If I had been the target, I would have been hit without a doubt."

"And one hit would have been the end... huh? Considering the potency of this poison, even a graze could be fatal."

"Do you know what kind of poison it is!?"

Rei had come to the Headmaster's office seeking clues, but he surely hadn't expected to get information this quickly.

He turned his astonished gaze toward Erinde and asked.

"Hmm. This poison is called Heaven's Tears. Its main ingredient is basilisk blood. It's concocted by mixing that with magic power, poisonous herbs, and monster materials. Those poisoned by it suffer such intense agony that, true to its name, it makes Heaven weep in pity. They writhe in torment for three days and three nights before finally dying."

"...Quite a delightful poison. Never imagined you'd target me with something like that."

Rei hadn't expected it to be such a vicious poison either. He shifted his gaze back to the arrow Erinde held.

"Is such a vicious poison really that easy to come by in Glacial? ...This place is far more dangerous than I imagined."

"Of course not!"

Erinde shouted, unwilling to let him misunderstand the city she called home.

Surprised by Erinde's behavior, so different from her usual self, Rei spoke again.

"Then why is that poison there?"

"It can only be prepared by someone targeting you, Rei. Of course, if someone were caught carrying such a dangerous substance upon entering Graciall, they'd be questioned immediately. But properly identifying this sort of thing requires considerable knowledge."

"So the guards missed it."

Erinde reluctantly nodded at Rei's blunt statement of the outcome.

"That's right. Other possibilities are that the ingredients were brought in separately and mixed into poison within Graciall, or that some other method was used... Regardless of the reason, having poison this potent brought into the school poses all sorts of problems."

"Moreover, whoever possesses these poisoned arrows has the speed to escape from Set."

"Set... the griffin familiar of Rei, right?"

"Yes. Compared to ordinary animals or monsters, Set's senses are exceptionally sharp. Especially his sense of smell."

To be precise, it's because of Set's skill, Enhanced Sense of Smell, that he can demonstrate such acute olfactory abilities. But Rei has no intention of revealing his hand like that.

Besides, it's true that even in his normal state, Set's senses are sharp, befitting a high-rank monster.

"A person capable of escaping a griffin's pursuit... Hmm? And yet, a bow... No, surely not. That would be far too obvious, and I can't imagine him resorting to such a pathetic trick. And using poison, of all things..."

Erinde murmured as if she'd noticed something, but immediately dismissed the thought herself.

Rei, puzzled by Erinde's behavior, turned his gaze toward her. She shook her head before speaking.

"No, it's just that I know a skilled archer. I rashly connected the bow to that person. Don't mind it."

"...So you're certain that acquaintance of yours is truly innocent?"

"Yes. Absolutely. Besides, they shouldn't even be in Graciall right now."

"That actually sounds a bit suspicious to me. Well, never mind. Next topic... though since I already know the answer, maybe I shouldn't ask?"

"Huh? What are you talking about?"

"No, I was just thinking—since you always use birds to patrol the academy grounds, I wondered if you might have spotted anyone suspicious."

Rei had wanted to ask if that meant she hadn't seen anyone suspicious, but he knew from their previous exchange that wasn't going to happen.

Erinde seemed to understand what Rei was getting at. She nodded back with a stern expression.

"That's right. I used the birds to patrol the academy grounds again today. That's a fact. If there were any suspicious people there, I'm sure I would have spotted them..."

"Considering that Set lost track of them partway through, there's probably some kind of trick involved."

As he said this, Rei vaguely understood what that seed might be.

Because Set had actually used it... or more accurately, been used by it before.

That meant...

"A Transfer Stone, huh?"

"A Transfer Stone? Wasn't that a magic item supposedly created by alchemists of the Bestir Empire? You mean whoever used this arrow had one?"

"Yeah. That poison called 'Tears of Heaven' is created using alchemy too, right? Considering that, I don't think it's that far off symbolically."

"...So you're saying an alchemist is involved in this? Or is it the Bestir Empire?"

At Erinde's words, Rei tilted his head.

He couldn't imagine the Bestir Empire would come after him.

The Third Prince faction, currently holding significant power within the Bestir Empire, knew full well the extent of Rei's abilities.

It might be a slight exaggeration to say they survived the recent civil war thanks to Rei's power, but it was undeniably a major factor in their victory.

Knowing the extent of that power, it was hard to imagine them turning hostile toward Rei now.

Indeed, those in the Third Prince faction, who had witnessed and truly felt the extent of Rei's power up close, likely understood it better than anyone from the Mireana Kingdom.

Given that, they had no reason to attack Rei without cause.

It wasn't impossible that it was the Third Prince's father—the current Emperor—who made the move, but given the current situation, it seemed unlikely he'd consider doing anything to make Rei an enemy.

(Could it be that my involvement in the civil war has caused various problems, or something like that? No, that can't be it. If that were the case, it would make more sense that someone with a siscon complex was trying to prevent me and Vihera from acting together once spring came...)

Thinking it through, Rei couldn't help but conclude that possibility was entirely plausible.

But he immediately dismissed it.

After all, he couldn't believe they wouldn't realize the sheer stupidity of making an enemy of someone like himself.

Even if Mercurio lost his mind and made such a decision, there was also the expectation that Theorem would stop him.

After all, antagonizing Rei could easily result in him riding Set and unleashing a barrage of fire magic from the air at will.

If that happened, the Bestir Empire would suffer immense damage.

(Surely he wouldn't... do something like that?)

If that were the case, it would be troublesome... While Rei was thinking this, Glinck, who had been listening to Rei and Erinde's conversation until now, spoke up.

"If we're talking about an alchemist, it might not be limited to the Bestir Empire, right? While the Bestir Empire has become famous for alchemy recently, the true origin lies in the magical city of Ozos. It's possible someone came from there."

"That possibility can't be ruled out. But the transfer stones were developed in the Bestir Empire, right? If they used those, then the Bestir Empire remains the prime suspect, doesn't it?"

Erinde asked, her gaze fixed on the arrow resting on the desk, but Glink shook his head.

"The Bestir Empire may have developed the magic item known as the Transfer Stone. But conversely, that's all they did—develop it. While Ozos has seen its alchemy technology rapidly overtaken by the Bestir Empire in recent years, it still possesses the accumulated technical expertise gained from centuries as a mage city."

Glink paused there, then continued as Rei and Erinde turned their gaze to him.

"In other words, if they knew such a thing existed, wouldn't it be possible for them to create something similar?"

"...Yeah. Grink has a point. Ozos does have a long history as a prosperous magical city. Considering that history, it's entirely possible they could have created something similar, just as Grink says. If they could get their hands on an actual Transfer Stone, Ozos would undoubtedly be able to replicate it."

As Erinde spoke, tapping her fingertips on the desk, Rei let out a faint sigh of relief.

For Rei, the Bestir Empire was no longer merely a complete enemy state.

He had come to know many faces there, and they had become opponents he would prefer not to fight.

Of course, if they attacked first, he would repel them.

After all, he disliked the idea of being attacked unprovoked, no matter what.

"Still... Ozos, huh? I've never been there."

"Oh? You, Rei, who favors magic items? Ozos has all sorts of magic items. Alchemy is advanced there, and as befits its name as a mage city, research into magic is also well developed. I think you should visit at least once."

"...If you mean magic research, wouldn't Samaloon be better than me?"

"Ah... In his case, if he went to Ozos, he might just never come back. He seems to know that himself. Even though he's interested, he hesitates. I guess, despite everything, he has an attachment to this country, his birthplace."

Hearing Erinde's words, Rei wondered, Oh?

Samaroon's passion for magic is truly remarkable.

It's almost like he's running wild with it.

That's precisely why it's puzzling he doesn't go to the place called the Mage City.

After all, watching Samaloon, Rei had thought it wouldn't be surprising if he casually abandoned his birthplace.

"Anyway, we can't just ignore this matter. No, more than that, we can't just handle it within the academy. Rei, you are a guest invited to Duke Quent's domain. Since that guest was nearly harmed, we must inform Duke Quent as well."

Though she conveyed this to Rei, a hint of worry lingered in Erinde's expression.

As the academy's headmistress, Erinde didn't want to burden Duke Quent... no, her friend Ronald, with such troublesome matters if she could help it.

Though the head of the duke's house and an elf were of different races and status, the friendship between them was undeniably genuine.

Erinde understood perfectly well that her friend was extremely busy at the moment.

Though the very cause of that busyness was the person standing right before her now.

"I believe you haven't met with Duke Quent yet, right, Rei?"

"Hm? Oh, it was actually scheduled for the day after I arrived here... but it seems he's been rather busy with various things. Besides, I'm not too fond of formalities myself."

"...I suppose so. Considering that, Rei and Duke Quent might not be the best match. Still, while he's strict about etiquette, he's definitely not a bad person. I'll vouch for that."

"That sounds like you're telling me to meet Duke Quent, doesn't it?"

"Exactly. I think it'd be best to meet him at least once to discuss this matter."

Erinde's unexpected words made Rei's brow furrow slightly.

As someone who didn't hold a particularly high opinion of nobility, Rei had hoped things could just be handled informally and that spring would come so he could return to Gilm.

If he had to meet someone, he'd prefer it to be someone like Maruka, someone he could interact with casually.

"I know you don't hold a high opinion of nobles, Rei, but why not meet without those preconceptions?"

As if sensing this was the moment to push, Glinck, who had been observing the conversation without speaking until now, also urged Rei.

"...Like I said before, I know next to nothing about proper etiquette towards nobles. If someone like me were to meet Duke Quent, a key figure among the royalists, it would definitely cause a stir."

"You're quite particular, Rei. ...Alright then. How about this? If I tell Duke Quent that he can meet with you without worrying about etiquette or formalities, would you agree to meet him?"

Rei pondered Erinde's words for a moment... then nodded.

"Alright. Do that. But I'll say it again—I don't want much to do with nobles. If you truly understand that, and still say you won't mind the etiquette and such, then I'll agree to meet him."

"Alright, I've got your word."

Whether she sensed an advantage or not, Erinde smiled at Rei's words and declared this.

...What Rei hadn't accounted for was how close friends Erinde and Duke Quent truly were.
