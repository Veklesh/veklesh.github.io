"Whoooooo! It's Rei! Rei's here!"

The voice of an adventurer echoes through the Forest of Treant.

That voice is filled with joy.

In fact, for the adventurers stationed in the Forest of Treants, Rei was nothing short of a savior.

Even when it comes to felling trees, he can slice them cleanly in two with his Death Scythe.

Felled trees can also be stored in an item box and transported.

And even if they were attacked by the monsters that had recently begun inhabiting the Treant Forest, he possessed overwhelming combat power.

For those working in the Forest of Treants, it was certain that he would be a major asset.

“Oh, Rei, you’re finally here!”

Not only the adventurers, but the lumberjacks were also delighted by Rei’s arrival.

No matter how many trees they cut down, transporting them was inevitably slow, so work had been stalling lately.

In fact, dozens of felled trees were piled up in the Treant Forest.

The logging itself was proceeding smoothly, and the forest had shrunk considerably since Rei had last seen it.

But that meant the felled timber hadn’t been collected.

(I think we could solve this if we just assigned more people to this side. The fact that we can’t… is there some reason for that? Well, there’s probably work to be done at the expansion site too, and since I made significant progress here before I went to the Kingdom of Reburina, we might still have some leeway…)

Stepping down from Set and looking at the fallen trees around him, Rei immediately stows a nearby tree in his Misty Ring.

Seeing that Rei had started working, Set flapped his wings and headed back into the sky.

To keep watch over the area and defeat the monsters that have taken up residence in Treant Forest.

“I’ll be stowing away the trees that have been haphazardly cut down over here, so the rest of you guys just do your own jobs. Now that I’m here, I’m not going to let any wood go to waste!”

“Ooooooh!”

At Rei’s shout, the woodcutters somehow all shouted in unison.

Among the woodcutters were some who had previously had trouble with Rei.

But even they must welcome Rei, a capable worker, with open arms.

Especially now, they needed to cut down as many trees as possible, including those needed for construction during the winter.

If they cut corners here and ran out of lumber for the winter expansion project, the worst-case scenario would be having to come all the way out here in the winter just to cut down more trees.

For the lumberjacks, that was absolutely out of the question.

Given that Gilm is on the frontier, it’s not uncommon for powerful monsters to appear even near the town.

And come winter, it wouldn’t be surprising for monsters specific to that season to appear.

Although the woodcutters were more skilled than the average person, that didn’t mean they could handle monsters.

So, to meet their quota, the lumberjacks immediately set to work cutting down trees.

(Well, if we really do run short on lumber in the winter, I’m sure they’d ask me to help out.)

Sending lumberjacks out in winter means we’ll need escorts, and we’ll also need people to haul the felled trees.

Doing all that in the snow would be extremely difficult.

But if I send Rei, he can fell the trees with his Death Scythe and store them in the Misty Ring. As for traveling back and forth to Gilm, if he rides the Set, he’ll arrive in just a few minutes.

No matter how you look at it, sending Rei is much quicker than dispatching multiple lumberjacks or adventurers.

...The problem is whether Rei will agree to do it.

“Wow, that’s amazing… No way. He’s stowing those trees away one after another.”

“Oh, you’ve never seen him before? That guy is Rei. Crimson.”

“I know that much. He’s riding a griffin, so there’s no one else but Crimson who’d be doing that. …Still, for such a small guy…”

A short distance away, the adventurers stationed in the Treant Forest as the woodcutter’s guards were chatting.

(Until recently, the adventurers were cutting down trees to make money too… but did they stop doing that because they couldn’t carry the wood anymore?)

Even as he wondered this, the felled trees were being stowed away in the Misty Ring one after another.

“Grrrrrrrrrrr!”

A sudden cry rang out.

Rei, who knew it was Set’s cry, wasn’t particularly surprised, but several woodcutters and adventurers looked startled by the sudden sound.

“That’s just Set’s cry, so don’t worry about it!”

A strange misunderstanding… To prevent anyone from mistaking it for a monster attack, Rei shouted to those around him.

Since Set’s cry is so distinctive, most people didn’t mistake it for a monster attack, but a few, upon hearing Rei’s words, visibly let out a sigh of relief.

Even for those who knew Set, if they were only familiar with his usually docile self, it was no wonder they were surprised to see him fighting an enemy.

A little while later...

“Grrrrrr…”

Flapping his wings, Set returns.

Clutched in his front paws was the corpse of an orc.

“An orc, huh? …Troublesome.”

As far as food goes, orc meat is delicious and a real lifesaver.

But considering the nature of orcs as monsters, having one this close by is by no means something to be happy about.

Perhaps realizing this, many of those who saw the orc’s corpse Set had brought back wore stern expressions.

The female adventurers, in particular, wore especially grim expressions.

That’s only natural. For a man, it might just end with being killed, but if a woman is captured by an orc, there’s nothing but the worst possible outcome.

Glancing around at the others, Rei speaks to Set.

“Set, thanks. I’ll take care of the orc for now.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set lets out a happy growl.

Stroking Set as he rubbed his head against Rei’s lap, begging for praise, Rei spoke up.

“So, about the orcs… were there any others? They haven’t set up a settlement or anything, have they?”

"Grr!"

To those watching from the sidelines, Set’s meow was incomprehensible, but his partner, Rei, understood exactly what it meant.

……It was probably much the same as Vihera, who clearly understood Byune’s words.

In any case, Rei nodded back at the people watching them closely from the surrounding area.

“It’s okay. For now, it seems there aren’t any orc settlements around here. Of course, just because there were orcs doesn’t mean we can completely let our guard down.”

At Rei’s words, everyone who had been listening let out a sigh of relief.

If you consider orcs as monsters found around Gilm, they aren’t particularly powerful.

However, in terms of their ferocity and behavior, they are among the worst of the lot.

That said, since orc meat is quite tasty for its rank, they’re also, quite literally, a “delicious” monster.

“Alright, Set. If you spot any orcs, keep hunting them down aggressively. Got it?”

“Grr!”

Set let out a short growl in response to Rei’s words.

The others didn’t know what Set was saying, but even so, they understood what he meant now.

In other words, they understood that he was saying he would actively hunt orcs.

Perhaps realizing that his response had pleased those around him, Set purred happily.

(Yeah, that’s fine… but now that this has happened, I won’t be able to go back there right away.)

Rei had been planning to return to Sura and the others once the expansion work was wrapped up, but now that he knew the orcs were in Treant Forest, he couldn’t just do that right away.

Besides, for Rei, orcs were also prime, tasty prey.

That said, the Treant Forest is vast.

Since he didn’t know how many monsters—like orcs and goblins—had infiltrated the area, it seemed impossible to completely wipe them out.

No matter how sharp Set’s senses were, if more monsters kept pouring into the Treant Forest, there was nothing he could do.

After all, it’s only natural that the number of monsters would increase even while Set is busy defeating them.

“For now, leave the monsters in the Treant Forest to Set and just do your own jobs. That’s more efficient than us running around the forest trying to defeat them, right?”

That’s what Rei said, but the real reason he didn’t assign them to handle the monsters was that the adventurers assigned to guard this area weren’t exactly skilled fighters.

There are plenty of other jobs for skilled adventurers.

Above all, even while Gilm is carrying out these expansion works, the guild is still accepting regular requests.

Escorts, monster hunts, gathering, reconnaissance… and so on.

Naturally, such jobs couldn’t be entrusted to those who had come to Gilm specifically for this expansion project.

As a result, skilled adventurers who had been in Gilm for some time naturally tended to take on those other jobs, and a sort of natural division of labor had begun to emerge.

That said, this was merely a general trend; there were times when a self-proclaimed expert who had just arrived in Gilm would take on a guild job and fail… or, if they had the skill or luck, succeed.

Conversely, there were also times when adventurers who had been in Gilm for a while would help out with expansion projects on a whim or just to make a little extra money.

In that sense, the adventurers here today are the typical type who came to Gilm seeking work on expansion projects.

Rei isn’t the type to be so carefree as to ask such people to hunt monsters in the Treant Forest.

(Well, monsters like goblins probably wouldn’t be a problem… but if a monster appeared that was beyond these guys’ capabilities, they’d pay for their inexperience with their lives.)

Rei certainly doesn’t want them to go through something like that.

“Grrrrr!”

“Alright, do your best, Set.”

“If you find any monsters that look tasty, bring them over here too.”

“You idiot, ‘tasty’ monsters are basically high-rank monsters, you know? There’s no way we could handle something like that.”

“If it’s an orc or something, maybe even I could handle it!”

“Don’t be ridiculous. One might be manageable, but two or three… you don’t even need to think about what would happen, do you?”

"Guh, w-well, that’s..."

Faced with such a crushing rebuttal, the man couldn’t argue any further.

After all, since we don’t know what will appear, there’s a chance we could end up facing high-rank monsters like Rank B monsters.

(Well, I guess I don’t really need to worry about Rank A monsters, though.)

Including griffins like Set, Rank A monsters are creatures you’d almost certainly never encounter in a lifetime if you lived an ordinary life.

Even for an adventurer, seeing one even once in a lifetime would be considered a stroke of luck.

Of course, Rank A monsters are, by definition, incredibly powerful. If an ordinary adventurer were to encounter such a monster, it would almost certainly mean certain death.

In that sense, it might truly be a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

“Hey, stop talking and get to work! Get to work! Your job is to guard us, isn’t it! What are you going to do if we get attacked by monsters while we’re cutting wood?!”

One of the woodcutters shouted in a low voice.

His voice carried such intensity that even the adventurers entrusted with their protection seemed to flinch.

That shout brought the conversation about the monsters in the Treant Forest to an abrupt end.

Satisfied with that, the woodcutter turned to Rei.

“Rei, once you’ve gathered all the trees you’ve cut down, get them to Gilm right away. Things are still fine here, but I hear the supply of wood is getting pretty low over there.”

The woodcutter spoke to Rei in the same low voice he’d used when yelling at the adventurers.

Rei nodded at his words and began loading the remaining logs into the Misty Ring one after another.

Seeing Rei like this, the other adventurers also return to their respective tasks.

It’s unclear whether “fortunately” is the right word in this case, but word had been properly passed on the day before that Rei would be coming to Treant Forest for a while starting today, so they wouldn’t have to do jobs like transporting the felled wood to Gilm by carriage.

While this also meant there were few adventurers present, the fact that they didn’t have to perform physically grueling tasks like loading and unloading was a stroke of luck for them.

And so, rejoicing at Rei’s arrival, the adventurers set about their work with renewed dedication.
