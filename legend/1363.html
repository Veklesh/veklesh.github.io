Time rewound. Several hours had passed since Rei stored the trees he cut down in Treant Forest into his Misty Ring and left the area with Marina and the others... It was already nearing evening.

Near Treant Forest, four adventurers could be seen.

"Hey, are you sure this is okay? Didn't the guild warn us not to go near this forest?"

"It's fine. Besides, if we keep taking the guild's warnings at face value, we'll never rank up any further. So, even if it's a bit dangerous, we need to make a name for ourselves."

The man with the spear replied to the woman wielding a longsword.

The other two, wielding a battleaxe and a club respectively, nodded in agreement with the spearman's words.

This party, the Third Eye, was a Rank D party that had arrived in Gilm last spring... around this very season.

But even after nearly a year of activity in Gilm, they hadn't achieved anywhere near the success they'd initially hoped for.

Of course, parties that achieve results within a year are not that common.

Still, for the man who believed they could do it... the results of this past year were far from satisfying.

What further fueled the man's impatience was the rapid increase in the number of adventurers arriving at Gilm this spring.

Though that was merely his perception; in reality, the numbers increased at the same rate every year.

After all, the Third Eye had arrived in Gilm last spring alongside other parties.

But for the men, this was their first spring in Gilm.

To be precise, they had spent last spring in Gilm too, but they had arrived well past the midpoint of the season then, so this was undoubtedly their first proper spring there.

And among the newcomers who arrived, there were clearly some who were far more skilled than themselves.

Under such circumstances, it was only natural for the Third Eye members to feel anxious... It was then that they heard a rumor at the guild tavern.

It said that a party of adventurers heading for Gilm had been attacked and wiped out by a forest that shouldn't have existed there.

And as if to back up the rumor, a notice was posted on the request board warning that there was nothing in this forest and advising people to stay as far away as possible.

Given this, perhaps something was there after all.

It was only natural to think so.

Perhaps there was something there... something they didn't want others to see.

If they could get their hands on it, their rank might rise, and their names could become known.

Driven by such thoughts, the Third Eye had come to Treant Forest.

If it truly didn't want anyone to know, then the guild wouldn't have made the Treant Forest incident public in the first place. But the Third Eye members hadn't thought that far ahead.

Even when verifying information, the Third Eye is a party of four warriors.

When it came to handling information, they inevitably fell short compared to thieves.

...Then again, becoming a thief, while not as rare as becoming a mage, was still uncommon to begin with.

A party consisting solely of warriors wasn't particularly rare.

But in this case, it could be said that very fact led to the worst possible outcome.

Up until now, they'd managed to gather a fair amount of information even without a thief, but at the crucial moment, their lack of knowledge about that information became a fatal flaw.

"...Even so, there really isn't any sign of monsters or animals in this forest. For the Treant Forest, the trees growing here are just ordinary trees."

The woman wielding the longsword murmured as she touched a tree glowing red in the sunset.

If the tree she was touching truly was a Treant, as its name suggested, it wouldn't be strange for it to attack at any moment.

But the tree the woman touched was, after all, just an ordinary tree, showing no sign of movement whatsoever.

In truth, the very tree she touched was being eyed as construction material for the Gilm expansion, but for now, none of the Third Eyes knew this.

No, even within the entire Gilm, only a handful knew.

That was precisely why the Third Eye did nothing, even when faced with a tree that could fetch an extremely high price.

Even if they knew this wood could fetch a high price, the Third Eye couldn't have done anything about it anyway.

Even if someone with a battle axe cut it down, they'd have no way to carry it back.

A lumberjack might manage, but mere adventurers... especially those of Rank D... couldn't do anything with a felled tree.

Having walked all this way, they naturally had no carts or wagons. If they wanted to take it back, they'd have to cut it down to a size they could carry by hand.

But even if they brought back wood cut to a size a person could carry, it was doubtful it would be useful as building material.

Of course, they wouldn't be completely useless.

"True. ...But time is what a pity right now. I want to uncover at least some of this forest's secrets before night falls. Surely it can't just be a place with trunks, like its name suggests."

The other members nodded in agreement at the words of the party leader with the Third Eye, the spear-wielding male adventurer.

Only the female adventurer wielding a longsword looked uneasy, but she likely understood that anything she said now wouldn't change her companions' minds.

She quietly followed her companions into the forest.

"Looking at it like this, I still don't think there's anything unusual about this forest."

Evening fell... Despite it being a time when both animals and monsters were reasonably active, not a single disturbance could be heard.

The woman found this odd, but it ended up easing her companions' tension.

"Right. So that means we don't have to worry about being attacked by anything, then."

The man with the axe said this, but the woman couldn't lower her guard even after hearing his words.

"Listen. It's evening now, and there's still nothing happening in the forest? How can that possibly be reassuring? There's probably something ahead. Stay on your guard."

"But as far as we can see, there's no need to worry, right?"

"...In a forest like this, the absence of anything unusual is itself unusual. You should think that way."

The woman muttered in exasperation, but since there was no particular danger in their current situation, they all continued walking through the forest while talking.

The sight of the forest glowing crimson in the sunset would have been genuinely mesmerizing if this had been an ordinary forest.

Or perhaps it reminded them of childhood days playing outside, heading home as evening fell.

It was a scene that stirred a certain nostalgia, but even so... or rather, precisely because of that, it gave them plenty to think about.

"...There are nuts and berries, so we probably don't need to worry about food."

The man carrying the spear, the party leader, murmured with a hint of relief.

True to their status as a Rank D party, the Third Eye's finances were far from comfortable.

Of course, it was enough to live normally, but saving where possible was only natural.

In that sense, this forest, abundant with nuts and berries, was perfect.

"The lack of meat is one thing, but the absence of wild vegetables is also concerning."

The man wielding a club muttered as he surveyed the surroundings.

Meat... They knew beforehand there were no animals or monsters here.

He hadn't truly believed it until he saw it with his own eyes, but now that he had, there was no doubt.

But... it was spring, the season for wild vegetables, and yet there were none to be found. This was unexpected even for the club-wielding man.

Many wild greens require preparation like boiling out the bitterness, but plenty can be eaten raw.

He knew wild greens grew elsewhere—he'd gathered them in a different forest, not this one—so it wasn't that they didn't grow in the wilderness.

"I really wanted to eat seasonal food, but oh well."

"Well, if push comes to shove, we can always go back to Gilm and eat there. ...We've pretty much covered the area now. Let's camp here tonight and thoroughly search first thing tomorrow morning."

Following the party leader's words, the Third Eye members left the forest.

Only the woman wielding the long sword felt uneasy about this forest where neither animals nor monsters existed... In fact, despite it being spring, not even insects were present.

But she knew no one would listen if she mentioned it here, and besides, they were already set on leaving the forest. So she said nothing more and stepped outside.

They immediately began preparing camp. Though spring had brought considerable warmth, nights still grew quite chilly.

They prepared layers of cloth they had brought, then ate a meal of gathered nuts and berries, dried meat, and hardened bread.

The sunset that had been floating in the night sky just moments ago had now completely disappeared, and darkness enveloped the surroundings.

"Hey, I'll ask just in case, but you don't plan on going back to Gilm, do you?"

Even though she knew it was probably a lost cause, the woman asked.

Fortunately, with the forest nearby, firewood was no problem.

Even now, the Third Eye members huddled around the campfire, each engaged in their own conversations.

Her words came out amidst this, but were predictably shot down by everyone.

"What do you think we came all this way for? There's definitely something in this forest. It's essential for us to rise up."

"...Right. Sorry. I guess I was feeling a bit weak-hearted."

As the woman apologized, the party leader's stern expression softened into a magnanimous nod.

"Well, this forest is definitely creepy. Considering the circumstances, it's only natural to feel uneasy."

"Yeah. ...We should probably have extra vigilant watch duty tonight at camp."

The party leader nodded in agreement.

The fact that this forest was an eerie place remained unchanged.

They decided there was no such thing as being too cautious.

After talking for a while longer, night deepened, and they eventually retired to sleep.

Normally, the rule for Third Eye encampments was that one of the four remained awake. However, this time, to be extra cautious, they decided on two-person watch rotations.

First, the two carrying the battle axe and club took watch, while the party leader and the woman with the longsword each went to sleep.

"...Hm..."

How many hours had he slept?

Camping usually meant shallow sleep, but tonight, for some reason, she had slept soundly.

She casually glanced toward the campfire... and saw it had gone out. The woman, still somewhat groggy, rapidly snapped awake.

"Huh!?"

Almost reflexively, she reached for the weapon nearby...

"Huh?"

Surprised that her hand wouldn't move as she intended, she tried to utter a question... but even that voice came out slurred.

To begin with, while reaching for the weapon, she had also tried to push herself up with her hands on the ground, but those wouldn't move either.

The way he couldn't move, as if paralyzed, must have looked to an observer like a worm writhing on the ground.

The woman understood something was wrong with her, but she didn't know what it was.

Determined to assess her surroundings, she strained her still-functional eyes to scan the area... only to have her mind go completely blank at the sight that instantly filled her vision.

Because there were two of her party members... the very two who had been on watch before her.

...That alone wouldn't have been particularly surprising.

But... what truly shocked her was recognizing them as her companions, no, as human beings.

Because normally, those two had bodies larger than hers, yet now they were emaciated, reduced to little more than skin and bones.

Their weight, which should have been double, triple... no, nearly five times hers, was now less than a third of hers.

What was truly astonishing was that they were still alive. ...Though whether you could call it living was debatable, given they were forcibly kept alive while every fluid in their bodies was drained, their flesh and organs dissolved, and even that was sucked up as nourishment by the vines growing from the ground.

Agony, terror, confusion, despair... Those emotions had already completely extinguished the light in their eyes.

Seeing them like that, she searched for the other companion... the party leader. There, she found him, his upper body torn away by a giant carnivorous plant.

And then... despite being paralyzed, the last sensation she felt was something piercing into her body with a sharp thud. With that, the woman's consciousness faded away.
