The room containing the dungeon's core, which they had approached before.

Each time they drew nearer, an inescapable tension welled up within Rei and the others.

Still, it wasn't paralysis-inducing tension; it was more like a manageable level of nerves, likely due to their accumulated experience.

Johanna and the others, who had taken Vihera and Rodos under their care, remained in a more distant location... the place Grim had teleported them to.

(Leaving Johanna and the others behind was the right call.)

Gripping Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, Rei murmured inwardly.

His palms gripping the handles felt slightly sweaty at this point.

This was partly due to the tension of challenging the Silver Lion, but also because he knew he had to defeat it to restore Vihera's consciousness.

(Win. No matter what.)

As he refocused his concentration, a hand suddenly rested on Rei's shoulder.

For a split second, Rei thought, Enemy!? But he quickly realized it was Elk's hand and exhaled.

"Calm down. If you're that tense, the others won't be able to fight properly either. You're the leader of this party, after all."

"...Huh? Me?"

Elk's words made Rei look utterly surprised.

Truthfully, Rei had no awareness whatsoever that he was the leader of this party.

He'd always thought someone more experienced, like Elk or Marina, should take the lead.

"Of course. This whole thing only became possible because of Rei."

"No, but that doesn't mean I have to be the party leader. Considering experience, Elk and Marina are..."

"It's fine. It's our unanimous decision that Rei should lead this party. ...Right?"

"Yeah."

Marina, meeting Elk's gaze, nodded with a smile playing on her lips.

"Rei's going to need to keep pushing himself as an adventurer from here on out. It's only fair, right?"

"Mm. I agree with Marina."

Following Marina, Elena nodded as well, and the others like Min and Ara nodded back as if it were only natural.

"Grrrrrrr!"

Then, Set growled, seemingly pleased that Rei was being praised.

(It's not like he's actually being praised, though.)

Thinking that about Set's behavior, Rei still felt pleased that he was being appreciated.

Since he held the Twilight Spear, he gently stroked Set with the back of his hand instead of his palm.

Seeing Rei like this, smiles spread across the others' faces too.

As he stroked Set, the tension within Rei gradually faded away.

And so they continued walking... until the room they were heading for came into view.

Even before entering the room, he felt an intense pressure.

(I don't recall feeling this kind of pressure when I came to this dungeon before... Could it be because I understand the Silver Lion is inside?)

Rei murmured to himself inwardly, but ultimately, what needed to be done remained unchanged.

Standing before the room, Rei spoke as if making a final confirmation.

"Got it? If the Silver Lion's roar comes, handle it individually. If you absolutely can't counter the roar, take the hit, then get out of the room, recover, and come back in. Same if you get injured."

Rei didn't expect everyone to emerge unscathed from the Silver Lion battle.

Therefore, while minor injuries were manageable, if anyone sustained wounds that hindered combat, the best course was undoubtedly to retreat to the safety of the room outside and recover.

"I'll leave potions here. If you think your injuries are problematic, feel free to use them."

Saying this, Rei pulled potions out one after another from his Misty Ring.

Most were potions available for purchase in Gilm, but among them were some crafted from World Tree materials—potions so rare they could only be bought with light gold coins.

While they couldn't restore Vihera's consciousness, these potions could heal ordinary severe injuries in an instant.

In fact, even if someone had their limbs severed, using these potions could likely reattach them.

Elk and Min stared in astonishment at how effortlessly such potent potions were produced.

Marina had witnessed Rei administer the World Tree Potion to Vihera by mouth, and Elena and Ara, having known Rei for a long time, understood he wouldn't hesitate to use such a rare potion in a situation like this.

"H-hey. Are you sure this is okay?"

"Yeah, it's fine. If we lost to the Silver Lion because I was stingy with potions, that would be pretty irreparable."

Nodding at Elk's words, Rei opened his mouth again.

"Well then. Looks like they're waiting for us over there... Let's get going."

With Rei's words, everyone shifted their focus from the potion to defeating the Silver Lion.

Entering the room where the Silver Lion lay, what Rei and the others saw was a lion covered in silver fur, true to its name.

Even to Rei's eyes, accustomed to Set's size exceeding two meters in length, its form was clearly massive.

Even without its tail, it was about four meters long—roughly twice the size of Set. Including its long tail, the lion's total length would likely be around six meters.

A rich mane, signifying it was a male lion, surrounded its face, and true to its name, it too was silver.

It possessed a presence truly worthy of being called the king of beasts.

The space the silver lion occupied was large enough for its six-meter frame to move about freely.

"Grrrrrrr"

The similarity to Set's roar was likely due to their shared lion heritage.

(Though, in Set's case, only his lower half is that of a lion.)

Majestic, imposing, bizarre. Various words flashed through Rei's mind, yet he realized he wasn't particularly nervous.

He felt certain he could handle this, that he could reliably unleash his full power.

And it wasn't just Rei.

Elena, Marina, Elk, and Min each gripped their weapons, their fighting spirit rising. Even Set, who needed no weapon, showed no sign of flinching before the silver lion nearly three times his size. But...

"..."

In that she didn't speak, she was the same as Rei and the others.

But what Ara felt was undeniably awe.

Ara had seen beings worthy of awe countless times before.

Elena was one such being, and so were Rei, Set, and Elk.

She had seen other similar beings before... but for Ara, the Silver Lion was not an opponent she could face head-on.

Unconsciously, her legs, her hands, her entire body trembled.

Staying before the Silver Lion meant losing her life.

Instinctively, she sensed it.

"GAAAAAAAAAAH!"

The Silver Lion's roar was different from just seconds ago.

The moment she heard it, Ara collapsed to the ground, her legs giving way beneath her.

"Ara, you wait outside the room for a bit. Then, once you can move again, join the fight."

Though Rei had been elevated to party leader, he still understood what he must do.

He understood that forcing the current Ara into battle would only get her killed by the Silver Lion. That was precisely why he spoke those words.

Under normal circumstances, if Elena were joining the fight, Aara would have stubbornly insisted on joining too.

But for Ara, facing the Silver Lion now, that was impossible.

...It might sound harsh, but if this incident meant Elena regaining consciousness, Ara could have fought the Silver Lion without any problem.

Of course, it wasn't that Ara disliked Vihera.

But still, for Ara, Elena and Vihera were not of equal value.

"I-I understand."

Her voice trembling, Ara stepped back.

Watching Ara like that, Rei and the others stepped forward.

"Let's go."

Rei murmured, simultaneously activating the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour... and each of them moved forward at once.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

As if to deter Rei and the others, the Silver Lion roared.

Simultaneously, Set planted his feet firmly and unleashed the King's Intimidation, roaring as well.

Normally, the King's Intimidation possessed the ability to seal an opponent's movements.

However, Set's roar couldn't even match the Silver Lion's, only slightly weakening its power.

Still, it was fortunate that it weakened the roar's power at least somewhat.

Thanks to wearing the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, Rei was almost entirely unaffected by the roar.

Elk and the others also succeeded in dampening the Silver Lion's roar's power, each using their own methods.

Their own strength, magic power, magic items, the power of spirits...

Using these powers, they couldn't completely nullify the roar, but they managed to limit the damage to a level that wouldn't hinder their next action.

"Flying Slash!"

The first strike launched was one of Rei's specialties.

Having reached Level 5, the skill's power had increased dramatically compared to before... but naturally, Rei didn't expect that strike to be effective against the Silver Lion.

The Silver Lion possessed strong resistance against magic and slashes. This Flying Slash was, above all, meant as a strong deterrent.

If this strike could draw even a little of the Silver Lion's attention toward Rei, that would be enough.

That was the thought behind the strike, but...

"GRRRR!"

To Rei's complete surprise, the Silver Lion effortlessly deflected the flying blade with a single flick of its tail.

(Damn it. I knew this kind of attack wouldn't work... but still, I never imagined it would be blocked so easily with just a tail swipe.)

Troublesome.

That seemed to be what Rei was thinking, but it was undeniable that this strike had succeeded, even if only for an instant, in forcing the Silver Lion to act.

In that split-second opening, the others besides Rei had each moved to their own actions.

Elk closed the distance with the Silver Lion, wielding the Axe of the Thunder God. Elena whipped her Mirage into a whip-like form to restrain the Silver Lion's movements. Marina unleashed a wind shockwave at the Silver Lion using spirit magic. Min chanted a spell. Set flapped his wings and soared into the sky.

After confirming everyone's actions, Rei stepped forward.

Because he moved while deploying the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, even though Rei acted last, he arrived before the Silver Lion faster than any other ally.

"Take this!"

A single strike from the Death Scythe, utilizing the maximum physical enhancement granted by the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

The magic-infused scythe swung as if to reap the Silver Lion's life, true to its name... but with a sharp metallic clang, it was deflected by the lion's fur.

"Huh!?"

Shouting involuntarily, Rei used the momentum from the deflected Death Scythe to spin on the spot, thrusting the Twilight Spear in his left hand toward the Silver Lion's face.

However, the tip of the Twilight Spear only struck the silver lion's forehead, sending another piercing metallic clang echoing through the air.

Simultaneously sensing something hurtling toward him, Rei leapt instinctively... only to be struck by a violent impact and sent flying.

"Guh!"

Twisting mid-air, he landed on the floor, but the sheer force of the Silver Lion's strike sent him sliding several meters along the floor, feet still planted.

Regaining his footing, he readied Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, but no follow-up attack came.

He understood why almost immediately.

Immediately after Rei was blown away, Elk arrived beneath the Silver Lion and swung down the Axe of the Thunder God.

The Silver Lion swiftly twisted to dodge Elk's Axe of the Thunder God, swung with all his might.

Marina's wind shockwave, fired as a diversion, and Elena's whip-like Mirage strike slammed into him.

Yet, as if to say such attacks weren't even worth dodging, the Silver Lion didn't move, merely glaring at Elk.

(I knew non-physical attacks wouldn't work, but seriously? Not even a Death Scythe strike infused with magic... and one using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour? That's just too damn bad, even as a joke.)

Currently, the Silver Lion is only wary of Elk, who wields the Axe of the Thunder God.

That's likely because the Silver Lion instinctively understands that a strike using the Axe of the Thunder God can damage it.

In other words, the Silver Lion believed that as long as it focused its vigilance solely on Elk, it couldn't be defeated.

Despite feeling a dull ache in his ribs from Silver Lion's earlier strike, Rei took a small breath.

"I'll show you... that your thinking is wrong!"

Gripping Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, he planted his foot firmly on the floor, closing the distance to the Silver Lion in one swift motion.
