"See ya later!"

"I'll come visit you again at Gilm!"

"Next time, let's talk about magic, okay?"

"Set! Next time, I'll make sure you get to taste my cooking!"

Hearing farewells like that from the ground below, Rei waves his hand as he flies through the sky with Set.

Though clad in his Dragon Robe, he had lowered the hood, allowing the warm spring air to brush against his face.

Even so, the wind grew cold once they reached a hundred meters above the ground, but Rei let out a small smile, thinking that too was part of the spring air.

"Grr?"

What is it? Set turned around, flapping his wings.

To Set, Rei smiled and stroked his head, saying it was nothing.

"A few days to Gilm. The journey's easier than when we came here, so let's enjoy this fine weather as we go."

"Gurrrr!"

Set purred happily at Rei's words.

For Set, being with Rei was undoubtedly the happiest thing of all.

After petting Set and gazing at the grassland that would turn into a green carpet come summer, Rei lifted his eyes to the blue sky.

It was perfect weather for setting out on a journey—clear and sunny, what you might call a May day.

...Though it was still early spring, hardly May at all.

(Based on his memories of Japan, this weather would easily pass for May.)

For Rei, who had lived deep in the mountains of Tohoku, early March or April was still a season between winter and spring where snow could fall and the high temperature barely reached ten degrees.

That's precisely why experiencing such warmth at this time of year, even though she'd felt it last year too, still brought a slight surprise.

"There are quite a few people coming and going... but I suppose that's normal. It might be different in a frontier like Gilm, but this place has a good location."

Watching the many merchants, travelers, and adventurers walking the road, Rei murmured to himself before suddenly pulling a small container from his Misty Ring.

Inside the container, about the size of his index finger, was a bright red liquid.

"Honestly... why do you have to do things you're not used to?"

What flashed through Rei's mind as he muttered this was the farewell party held the previous day.

It was the item Insla had suddenly appeared at the party to give Rei, having been in the S-Class for three or four years.

That was the container now clutched in Rei's hand, its contents a concentrated potion.

Concentrated potions naturally contained less liquid, making them more convenient to carry.

The technology itself had been developed decades ago, but it never became commonplace due to the difficulty in handling it.

While it would still have some effect if diluted with plain water, the potency would be drastically reduced.

To achieve proper efficacy, one must use water imbued with magic power specifically suited to the concentrated potion, achieved through alchemy.

The difference in effectiveness was stark: using properly treated water could heal even what would be called a serious injury, while using ordinary water might barely heal a small cut from a knife on the fingertip.

Given how inconvenient to use such potions are, it's no wonder the technique of concentration never caught on.

After all, if you have to carry both the concentrated potion and the magically treated water separately, you'd be better off just carrying a regular potion.

The reason they chose a concentrated potion—made with such an inconvenient, nearly extinct technique—as a gift for Rei was likely because they'd heard somewhere that Rei enjoyed collecting magic items as a hobby.

Hearing this, the students pooled their money, found a concentrated potion as a rare magic item, and decided to give it to Rei as a present.

"It's true I collect magic items, but I collect ones that are actually usable in combat,"

Even as he said this, Rei carefully stowed the concentrated potion he held in his hand into his Misty Ring.

He might say all sorts of things, but anyone who'd gotten used to spending time with Rei could tell he was embarrassed.

"...Well, I'll take it for now. Potions and such never hurt to have, and you never know when they might come in handy."

"Grrrr..."

Set let out a teasing growl at Rei's muttering.

Rei gave Set a light pat on the back of his neck.

"Hey, you're ten years too early to be teasing me. ...Well, ten years would probably fly by for us anyway."

"Guruu!"

Exchanging such banter, Rei and Set flew through the sky toward Gilm.

Several people walking along the road looked up, but flying at a height of about a hundred meters, they couldn't tell if it was a bird or a monster.

Of course, if the travelers were seasoned adventurers, they might have recognized it wasn't a bird.

Of course, even if it was a monster and not a bird, ordinary adventurers wouldn't stand a chance against it. ...That is, ordinary adventurers.

"Whoa!"

Suddenly realizing a whistling sound was heading straight for him, he instinctively drew his Death Scythe to intercept it.

But the whistling sound wasn't fast at all. Rather, it seemed deliberately designed to make noise, moving at a visibly slow speed.

Before swinging the Death Scythe, Rei recognized the whistling sound approaching him as an arrow with a rounded tip. Seeing paper tied to the arrow, he grabbed it with his bare hand just as it passed right beside him.

"What is this?"

"Guru?"

Not only Rei, but Set seemed interested in the arrow too, turning his head and growling curiously.

"What is it?" Rei had said aloud, though the very fact it was an arrow made its origin clear.

"Sardat could have just come see me directly instead of going to all this trouble."

"Grrrr..."

Set growled in agreement with Rei's words.

Their first encounter hadn't been friendly, but Sardat hadn't been hostile toward Rei or Set either, so Set quickly relaxed.

Now, he didn't dislike being petted by Sardat at all.

Compared to Rodos, whom he still disliked precisely because they had once been enemies, the difference was stark.

"...A letter. So this is an arrow message? Maybe we have something like this too... No, if you use bows and arrows, it's something you'd naturally think of."

Thinking like that, he opened the letter.

Inside were brief farewell words, followed by a simple request: to introduce him to a girl the next time they met.

"Honestly, I think it's a bit much that the part asking me to introduce a girl is longer than the farewell itself."

Sighing, he nevertheless didn't throw the letter away. Instead, he stored it alongside the Death Scythe in the Misty Ring.

(Introduce a girl... As an S-rank adventurer, I doubt he'd have trouble with women. Or have the bad rumors spread that far? ...It's possible. I remember hearing rumors about Sardat a few times even at the academy.)

Feeling a bit uneasy about the difference in how Noiz and others were treated, Rei flew with Set, enjoying the spring air.

Occasionally, they spotted adventurers battling monsters below, but they passed by without intervening.

He might have intervened if an adventurer seemed in trouble, but none appeared to be.

Especially now, with spring just beginning, it wasn't uncommon for adventurers to have already exhausted their winter reserves.

Stealing the prey of such adventurers would likely only earn him their resentment.

So, for Rei, unless an adventurer was in mortal danger...

"Ah."

...there was no need to help.

That's what Rei had been thinking, but right then, he spotted an adventurer about to be attacked by an orc in the woods below.

The longsword in his hand had been knocked aside and was now stuck in a nearby tree trunk. The adventurer was desperately trying to create distance from the orcs.

The most unfortunate thing was that the adventurer was solo... and a female adventurer at that.

A female adventurer defeated by orcs. Rei already understood what that meant.

But adventurers were responsible for themselves, and the consequences that followed were theirs alone.

As long as one chose to be an adventurer, that was an undeniable truth, and Rei saw no need to go out of his way to save the female adventurer.

Even if he pretended not to notice and flew past the orc and the fallen adventurer, no one would complain.

That was precisely why...

「Set」

"Guruu!"

As if understanding what Rei meant from that brief exchange of calling his name, Set let out a short growl and dove straight down toward the ground.

(Yes, if I just flew past overhead like this, no one would complain. But if I let that happen, I wouldn't be able to forgive myself.)

Even as Set descended toward the ground, the orc continued approaching the female adventurer, reaching out to tear off the leather armor she wore...

"Hmph!"

The spear, drawn from the Misty Ring, flew from Rei's hand.

Normally, Rei would use his arm strength and body twist to throw the spear, but he was currently on Set's back.

That meant his arm strength was one thing, but the transfer of force through body twist was nearly useless.

...However, riding on Set's back as they descended toward the ground meant he could transfer that speed to the spear.

Of course, such a feat required perfect synchronization between them, but with Rei and Set, that was never a concern.

Rei's arm strength and Set's descent speed. The spear launched by the combination of those two forces flew straight toward the ground...

"Buhhii, buhihihi... buh..."

It pierced the head of an orc focused solely on tormenting a weaker opponent, shattering and piercing through it before plunging into the ground, snapping the shaft.

The spears Rei used for throwing were meant to be disposable anyway, so most were on the verge of being discarded.

Not all spears were like that, but it was true that the overwhelming majority were on the verge of being discarded.

Given that, it was only natural that a strike powerful enough to crush an orc's skull would cause the shaft to snap.

"Kyaah! ...Huh? What? What just happened?"

To the female adventurer, the sight of an orc attacking her, only to find its head gone and the creature dead when she came to, elicited a cry of bewilderment.

The adventurer woman looked around, but all that surrounded her were the shattered remains of the orc's head—blood, flesh, bones, and brain matter—and she couldn't find the one who had saved her.

Had she looked up at that moment, she might have spotted Set. But after hurling their spears at the orc, Set and Rei had quickly regained their footing and left the scene.

They hadn't helped her expecting anything in return, nor did they intend to see her through to the end.

They had only intervened because it would have been unpleasant to wake up to death within their sight. That was precisely why they chose to leave without even addressing the female adventurer.

"I... was saved...?"

The female adventurer looked around again. Just when she thought she'd somehow overcome the threat to her life and virtue, she looked around once more.

"...What about the proof of slaying...?"

The proof of slaying an orc was its right ear, worth three silver coins.

But what lay before the female adventurer was the corpse of an orc with its head destroyed.

She couldn't possibly find the right ear, nor did she want to look for it.

For a few seconds, she scanned her surroundings, her gaze searching. Realizing there truly was no one who had come to her aid, she let out a small sigh before walking over to the headless orc corpse.

"Even if the right ear's impossible, magic stones and materials should be fine, right? ...I'd like to finish stripping it before other monsters show up... Ugh. Why didn't I bring someone with me?"

Reflecting on her reckless actions, she began stripping the orc.

...Of course, she remained ready to flee the moment any other monsters appeared.

This female adventurer successfully stripped the orc of its materials, gathered as much meat as she could carry, and made it back to Glacial. Later, she would come to be known as the Orc Slayer.

"Well then, time to move on again today."

"Grrrrrrrrrrr!"

Set growled in agreement at Rei's voice.

Several days had passed since leaving Glacial. On this day, as they neared Gilm, Rei began their journey as usual, riding on Set's back.

However, that day's journey did not last very long.

Because, less than an hour after setting out, they spotted figures fighting bandits on the ground below.

That alone wouldn't have startled Rei so much.

...But the story was different when he saw that the woman wore clothing made of thin fabric, like that worn by dancers or prostitutes, and had bracers on her arms and greaves on her legs.

And beside that woman stood a single, petite girl.
