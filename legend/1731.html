"These skewers are delicious."

"Yeah, you're right. Since it's grilled oak, I thought it would be more expensive in a place like this."

Rei replied to Elena as she muttered those words while eating the skewered meat.

In fact, in places like Gilm where orcs appear fairly frequently, orc meat is quite cheap.

However, that’s only possible because there are orcs in the vicinity; if there were no orcs nearby, it was only natural that the price of orc meat would be higher.

There are meats preserved to last longer, like salted or dried meat, but such meats are inevitably more expensive than fresh meat. In some cases, they become too salty to improve shelf life, requiring extra effort like desalting before cooking.

“Logically speaking, it’s probably because of the dungeon we cleared.”

“Hmm, I know that’s the most likely possibility, but even so, I don’t think the orcs that fell to their deaths from the dungeon alone could supply all of Golz’s meat.”

“Hmm… There were quite a few orc settlements inside the dungeon, so maybe a large group was moving together and fell off that cliff?”

Even as he voiced this unlikely theory, Rei popped a piece of orc meat on a skewer into his mouth.

The seasoning was simple—just salt and herbs—but it suited the grilled orc meat perfectly.

“Well, whatever the reason… I think it’s a blessing to be able to eat delicious skewered meat like this in this kind of weather.”

“Heh, you’re right.”

At Rei’s words, Elena looked up at the sky while savoring the flavor of the skewered meat.

There, she sees a blue sky worthy of being called a clear autumn day, and the sun casting a light onto the ground that is much softer than in summer.

It was truly the kind of weather you’d call perfect for a date.

"Hm? Elena, what’s wrong?"

Rei called out to Elena, who had stopped moving and was still looking up at the sky.

Snapped back to reality by his voice, Elena shook her head, saying it was nothing.

“I was just thinking how nice it is that it’s sunny.”

“Yeah. If it had rained, we wouldn’t have been able to do the demolition today. And even if we waited until tomorrow, it’d definitely be harder to work with the ground still wet.”

“That wasn’t exactly what I meant.”

She muttered this with a hint of dissatisfaction, probably because she had hoped Rei would say that today was perfect for a date.

However, while Rei looked puzzled by Elena’s attitude, he said nothing more and headed toward the next shop.

The shop Rei had his eye on was a variety store that mainly sold various tableware, along with all sorts of other items.

Forks, knives, spoons, tableware… there were many items made of metal, as well as wooden tableware.

Among them, what caught Rei’s eye was a wooden cup.

It had no handle—it was truly just a cup for drinking water or alcohol—but to Rei, it somehow looked like a nice piece.

(Is this what they mean by “it has character”? Well, it wouldn’t hurt to have a cup like this for everyday use.)

Having made up his mind, he picked up the cup. Elena, who had been watching nearby, looked puzzled and picked up another cup placed nearby—a metal one.

“If you’re going to buy one, wouldn’t this kind of cup be better?”

“Hmm. That might be nice, but this one seems easier to use… Metal is good for cold drinks, but when drinking something warm… or hot, it might be a problem.”

“Hmm, now that you mention it, you’re right.”

As Rei and Elena were talking like that, a store clerk appeared.

The female clerk, who looked to be in her forties, paused for a moment when she saw the two of them, but immediately broke into a smile and spoke up.

“Oh my, are you newlyweds? Or maybe a couple living together? If so, we’ve got tableware that’s easy to use, even if it doesn’t look fancy.”

“What!?”

At the clerk’s words, Rei and Elena both let out a cry.

It was a cry that could be interpreted as surprise, embarrassment… or perhaps even joy.

In that moment, Elena was the first to come to her senses.

“W-well, Rei and I aren’t… um, we’re not in that kind of relationship yet!”

“Oh… I see. I’m sorry about that.”

The clerk bowed her head slightly in apology at Elena’s words.

Seeing the clerk’s reaction, Elena calmed down a little, but…

“But you’re ‘not yet’ in that kind of relationship, are you? Hehe, I wonder what will happen in the future then.”

“Huh!?”

“W-w-what!? …Never mind. Here’s the payment. I’m taking this!”

With her embarrassment reaching its limit, Elena grabbed the wooden cup Rei was holding and the metal cup she had been holding herself, handed the clerk a gold coin as payment, and dragged Rei out of the shop.

The female clerk watched Elena’s awkward departure in astonishment… but then, glancing at the item in her hand, her face twisted in disbelief.

Most of the tableware sold in this shop costs only a few copper coins… even the most expensive items are only worth a few silver coins at most.

They certainly didn’t carry anything expensive enough to require a gold coin.

“Wait, wait, customer! That’s too much, that’s way too much!”

The clerk frantically chased after Elena... and somehow managed to get the gold coins back and collect the proper payment.

“Um, well. Elena? Hey, Elena. Are you listening?”

Since she hadn’t actually blown herself up, Rei managed to snap out of it relatively quickly and called out to Elena.

But Elena doesn’t seem to be hearing a word Rei says.

“Seriously… I mean… basically… anyway…”

Rei wasn’t entirely opposed to watching Elena like that a little longer, but if the passersby and others around them were focusing their attention on her, he couldn’t just let things continue that way indefinitely.

“Come on, Elena. Pull yourself together.”

“Huh!? …Oh, I’m sorry. I guess I was a little confused.”

Though surprised by Rei’s appearance, Elena answered while trying not to show it.

……If someone who only knew the usual Elena—the Princess General, to be precise—were to see her now, even though her appearance was the same, it wouldn’t be strange for them to think, “Who is this?”

That’s how different Elena was right now compared to her usual self.

Since Elena makes her living fighting, it’s only natural that the mention of a weapons shop would pique her interest.

That said, if Marina were to see this scene, she might scratch her head in confusion, wondering why they’d go to a weapons shop on a date. Or perhaps she’d just shrug and think, “That’s just like Rei and Elena.”

But Rei and Elena paid no mind to the shopkeeper’s reaction and immediately began looking around the store.

After all, even though Golz is the most prosperous town in the area, it’s still a small-town at the end of the day.

Not many monsters appear here, and there aren’t that many bandits either.

Five spears, mostly with chipped tips.

The Twilight Spear, which Rei often uses alongside the Death Scythe.

A magic item spear with the special ability to return to his hand when he wills it… a so-called magic spear, but there are enemies who he’d rather not use such a spear against.

Preserved foods, which could be called a specialty of Golz.
