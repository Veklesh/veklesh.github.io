When Rei and Set headed to the cafeteria meeting spot, both Vihera and Byune were already waiting there.

Since gathering information from people was essentially Vihera and Byune's job, Rei wasn't surprised by that... Instead, he sighed when he saw four unconscious men lying near the table where Vihera and Byune were seated.

What's more, the cafeteria waitresses and other customers weren't giving Vihera and Byune reproachful looks... rather, they were looking at them with gratitude. So, it was no wonder he couldn't quite grasp the situation.

"Oh, Rei. You're late."

"Sorry, stuff came up. ...Ah, Set is out front. Just give him something to eat. And get me something simple too."

Rei hands a silver coin to the waitress who approaches to guide him and says this.

He headed straight for the table where Vihera sat, noticing the envious, jealous glances from the surrounding customers.

"So? What kind of trouble did you get into this time? I assume those four passed out on the floor are the ones you were causing a scene with?"

"Oh, these guys? They were drunk and bothering other people. Then they started bothering me, so I just put them to bed."

"...Quietly?"

Hearing Vihera's words, Rei shot a skeptical glance at the four men sprawled on the floor.

Their bodies were clearly well-trained, making it easy to guess they were adventurers or mercenaries.

Yet these four well-trained men lay completely unconscious on the floor.

Yet, there was no blood coming from their mouths or noses, and no visible injuries on their faces or bodies.

"Yes. Quietly. Do you have any objections?"

Though a smile played at Vihera's lips, her eyes held no trace of amusement.

Rei, judging that continuing the conversation would undoubtedly lead to trouble, gently averted his gaze from the four lying on the floor and sat down facing Vihera.

Then the waitress brought bread, soup, skewered meats, stewed dishes, and other foods, placing them on the table.

"Thank you. I'm sorry, but I have something important to discuss. Could you keep people away?"

"Y-yes, of course!"

The waitress shot Vihera a look mixed with respect and admiration before forcibly moving the surrounding customers to other seats.

However, the customers forcibly moved didn't give Vihera resentful looks—though they did give Rei looks mixed with jealousy—and obediently changed seats.

"You've become quite the popular figure, haven't you?"

Rei muttered with a wry smile at the stares directed at him. Vihera merely shrugged her shoulders slightly and returned a silent smile.

"...Well then. I've had some hits and misses over here. How about you?"

"Not exactly certain, but I did find something worth mentioning. But first, can I hear Rei's story?"

Rei nodded to Vihera, who was spooning green fruit into her mouth.

He wondered if what Vihera was eating might be an avocado?

"After capturing and interrogating some bandits active around Gilm, I got intel that two suspicious individuals headed upstream along the river flowing through the forest where we fought the Cyclops."

Rei continued, explaining they'd followed that lead upstream. What they eventually found, however, was a place filled with the stench of decay and the smell of blood, littered with the corpses of numerous monsters.

There, they acquired the magic item incense burner and continued upstream, eventually arriving at the cave where Set had teleported.

However, deep within the cave lay a magic circle, presumably intended to collapse the cave, and a magic item in the form of a jar.

They managed to escape from there, but then a golem emerged from the collapsed cave ruins. After defeating it, they returned to Gilm and discovered a magic item that seemed to have been created by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire...

"Well, it seems we ended up in the same place after all."

"Huh? What do you mean?"

Rei interrupted Vihera mid-sentence, but all he got in return was her meaningful smile as she raised her ale to her lips.

Beside Vihera, Byune was also enjoying a dish of tomato-like vegetables stuffed with cheese.

"As you know, we've been gathering information both inside and outside Gilm. During that process, we heard rumors about several magic items recently appearing that outperform those made within Gilm. We even managed to speak with a few people who possess these items."

"...So, the source of these magic items—or rather, the ones who made them—were alchemists from the Bestir Empire?"

"Correct. Apparently, they're selling magic items in Gilm... not exactly flashily, but discreetly enough."

"But why would they do something like that? Even if they say it's inconspicuous, selling magic items with higher performance than usual will inevitably draw attention, won't it? Is that part of the plan?"

At Rei's suggestion that it might be a diversion, Vihera tilted her head.

"I wonder. Honestly, even if it's a magic item supposedly made by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire, we can't be sure it's the same person causing all this trouble now."

"So we'll have to gather intel by visiting the shops selling them, huh?"

"Hm?"

Byune, who had been silently eating until now, muttered briefly, implying, "Are we leaving already?"

Before they knew it, most of the meal Rei had ordered had vanished, ending up inside Byune's stomach.

Of course, Rei had been eating while talking with Vihera, but the fact remained that most of the dishes had been eaten by Byune.

"...Well, whatever. Byune's got a healthy appetite."

Saying it almost like a sore loser, Rei stood up from his seat.

"Well then, let's go to that magic item shop to walk off our meal. Rei, do you know where it is?"

"I asked the stall owner. Apparently, it's in a back alley or something like that, so it might be a bit hard to find. They say it's decorated with a goblin skull, so you shouldn't mistake it for another shop."

"That's what my intel said too. Alright, let's go."

And so, Rei and Vihera, along with Byune and Set—three people and one beast—left the cafeteria and headed toward the magic item shop.

"...We arrived without incident, unexpectedly."

Rei muttered.

Before Rei now stood the magic item shop Seymour had told them about.

The goblin skulls decorating the place were in extremely poor taste, making it impossible to mistake the shop.

"What? Did you think something would happen?"

"Not so much something happening... More like, I was expecting something to happen. Not that I'm bragging, but for some reason, I tend to get caught up in trouble."

"...Now that you mention it, yeah. Being with Rei, I've gotten caught up in quite a few messes."

"Hmph!"

As Vihera muttered, recalling their time together, Byune remembered the countless incidents they'd encountered while in Exil.

Both understood that Rei was prone to getting caught up in trouble not just intellectually, but through lived experience.

Though if you asked Rei, he'd say Vihera attracts trouble with her appearance and clothing that tempt men, while Byune gets dragged into trouble because people underestimate her due to her small stature.

"Grr."

Set purred soothingly, rubbing his face against her.

Though she thought Set's presence also attracted trouble, she stroked his head and spoke.

"I don't think I'm the only one to blame. More importantly..."

Realizing continuing this conversation would put him at a disadvantage, Rei forcibly steered the topic back.

"We're not getting anywhere standing here. I'm going in soon. Set, wait outside as usual."

After saying this, he watched Set purr and Vihera and Byune nod before opening the door in front of him.

Instantly, a sweet scent wafted out from inside the shop.

Not the sweetness of fruit, but something unnatural.

"...What is that smell?"

"I don't know. From what I can see, it looks like a normal magic item shop."

"For security?"

While mass-produced items were one thing, given that they carried expensive goods like the ones Seymour used, Rei thought it wasn't unreasonable for them to take some kind of security measure. Vihera tilted her head at him.

"Is that so? I don't think this kind of thing could really provide security."

Just as Vihera murmured this, a man suddenly appeared from the back of the shop.

He looked to be in his fifties, but his physique was unmistakably that of a trained warrior.

He seemed slightly surprised to see Rei—perhaps because he knew her from living in Gilm?

(A former adventurer or something?)

Having just met Seymour, who was fond of magic items, Rei couldn't help but wonder.

"What sort of magic item are you looking for?"

His gentle voice, combined with the muscles visible in his arms beneath his clothes, evoked the image of a quiet mountain.

His lack of reaction even upon seeing Vihera was likely another reason for that mountainous impression.

But he quickly shook his head, realizing this wasn't the time for such thoughts, and stated his purpose for coming to the shop.

"Actually, Seymour told me he could buy magic items made by alchemists from the Bestir Empire at a pretty good price here, so I came to check it out. Believe it or not, collecting magic items is my hobby."

"Oh?"

At the mention of collecting magic items as a hobby, the man flashed a delighted smile.

It was the kind of smile someone might have upon finding a kindred spirit.

Given how expensive magic items are, it probably meant few people actually came here to buy them.

"I see, collecting magic items is your hobby. ...The one Seymour bought definitely came from this shop."

"Huh, I see. ...Still, how did you even get your hands on a magic item made by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire?"

"They're usually hard to come by. Luckily, a merchant brought a whole batch not too long ago. You know how there was that commotion in the Bestir Empire last year? Seems they flowed in around that time."

"...I see."

Rei felt a slight letdown, realizing it was a magic item leaked from the incident he'd been involved in.

But he quickly realized he couldn't be certain if that was actually the case.

Even if the seller claimed that, he couldn't completely rule out the possibility they were lying to cover something up.

"So, who was the person who came to sell these magic items?"

"Well, since this is our livelihood too, if you're going to buy magic items, we'd appreciate it if you'd buy them through the shop."

The shopkeeper frowned at Rei and said this, perhaps judging from Rei's mention of collecting magic items and his request for information about the seller that Rei intended to buy the item directly from that person.

Rei immediately tried to deny it to the shopkeeper, but if asked why she wanted to know, she couldn't give a good answer.

"I wanted to know specifically who made the magic item, since it was created by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire. I'm from the Bestir Empire, so I was a little curious."

Vihera spoke up for Rei, who had stumbled over her words.

The shopkeeper, seemingly suspicious of Rei and the others, shifted from his earlier somewhat talkative demeanor. He now eyed them like they were shady characters before speaking.

"If you're collecting magic items, you should know this: alchemists are those who can create magic items. If what you want is information about alchemists, I'm afraid I can't tell you that. It's for business reasons."

"Can't you make an exception?"

Normally, if a woman like Vihera—a beauty clad in the light garments of a dancer or courtesan—said this, it wouldn't be strange for someone to nod in agreement.

But the shopkeeper didn't seem to notice Vihera's expression at all, shaking his head.

"No means no. I'm running a business here. I can't just hand out information to anyone. ...Sorry, but you'll have to leave. Since Seymour referred you, I won't say anything more."

The shopkeeper declared this, then turned his back on Rei and the others, seemingly determined not to listen any further.

No matter what Rei and the others said after that, he didn't respond.

For a few minutes, Rei and the others tried desperately to coax information out of the shopkeeper. But when they finally realized it was futile, they simply left the shop.

"...Hmph."

Watching them leave, the shopkeeper muttered under his breath before shifting his gaze to a nearby magic item... the very one they had been searching for: a magic item crafted by an alchemist of the Bestir Empire.
