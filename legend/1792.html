The first person chosen to spar with Rei was a man who looked slightly older than him.

That said, although several years have passed since Rei arrived in Elgin, his appearance hasn’t changed at all since then.

Normally, someone in their mid-teens would grow significantly in height within a year, but there was no sign of that at all. …That said, since Rei was also an extremely powerful mage, this wasn’t considered a particular issue.

In any case, since he appeared slightly older than Rei—who was in his mid-teens—that meant his opponent was likely in his late teens… probably not yet twenty.

To be a member of the Duke of Kerebel’s elite knight corps at that age means they are undoubtedly a talented individual, likely with a promising future ahead of them.

However, it’s only natural for someone of that age to get a big head because of their talent… In that sense, Rei was probably the perfect match for Philma.

The man wielding the longsword is completely immobilized in the face of Rei’s Death Scythe and Twilight Spear.

While there is a difference in skill, the overwhelming presence emanating from Rei’s two weapons is likely the primary reason the man hesitates to attack.

A dozen or so seconds pass... and eventually, Rei speaks.

“If you’re not coming over here, I’ll come to you.”

Saying that, he takes a step forward.

At Rei’s words and actions, the man instinctively tries to step back, but…

“Leodanis!”

Instantly, Philma’s voice echoed around them.

She certainly hadn’t shouted, yet her voice still carried through the area—a result of her continuing to issue orders to her allies even amidst the clamour of battle.

And the man—Leodanis—snapped back to his senses at Philma’s voice… He raised his longsword and lunged forward, trying to close the distance between himself and Rei in one swift motion.

“Aaaaaaargh!”

Though it was a cry bordering on a scream, his lunge was far sharper than that of the average adventurer.

...However, the fact that he was still slower than his usual speed was likely due to the cold of winter—which made his movements slightly sluggish even after running for a while—and the light snowfall that had slightly dampened the ground.

For Rei, getting the upper hand on Leodanis was no difficult task.

But since this was a mock battle, he needed to let Leodanis gain some experience.

That was precisely why Rei kept his eyes fixed on Leodanis’s movements… and eventually, he effortlessly deflected the longsword swung at him with his Death Scythe.

A sharp metallic clang echoed around them, and in the next instant, the longsword Leo Danis had been holding flew away, spinning through the air. By the time Leo Danis realized what had happened, accompanied by a tingling sensation in his hand, the tip of the Twilight Spear was already thrust before his eyes.

“Enough!”

“……Huh?”

Philma’s voice echoed through the training ground.

The voice that came from Leodanis’s mouth was one of complete bewilderment.

Only his intensely numb hand indicated that he had lost.

And it wasn’t a loss after a close fight—he had been literally swept aside.

The moment Philma’s voice rang out, a murmur rose from the other knights who had been watching Leodanis’s battle—though it was hard to even call it that.

“Hey, did you see that? No, did you actually see it?”

“...That scythe... it was swung without us even noticing.”

“And the spear—when did he thrust it out?”

Such voices echoed around them, but Philma’s voice rang out, as if to drown them out.

“Next! Sharisla!”

“Me!? O-okay!”

At Philma’s command, a woman in her mid-twenties holding a spear stepped forward in front of Rei.

As the woman called Sharisla approached, Leodanis picked up his longsword from the ground and stepped back.

Leodanis, who had been so confident in his own strength, probably never imagined he would lose so easily.

Even as he walked away, his expression suggested he still couldn’t quite grasp whether he had truly lost.

“Begin!”

At the same moment Philma’s voice rang out, Sharisla lunged forward.

By hesitating to make his move, Leodanis had handed the initiative to Rei.

Given that, he reasoned it would be better to move before Rei and seize the initiative.

That was likely the reasoning behind her actions.

As a strategy against Rei, it wasn’t necessarily wrong.

But... to wrest control from Rei, Sharisla’s skill, stamina... in short, her overall ability was simply not enough.

“Haaah!”

Sharisla’s thrust, unleashed with a sharp cry.

The fastest and most basic attack with a spear.

And, as befits a member of the Kerebel Duke’s Knights, the speed of that thrust was incomparable to that of ordinary adventurers.

However...

“Huh?”

The next moment, a somewhat dazed voice, similar to the one Leodanis had uttered, escaped Sharisla’s lips.

That said, it wasn’t just Sharisla who let out such a sound; the other knights watching the battle around her did the same.

It was only natural. After all, where the knights’ gazes were fixed, Sharisla’s spear and Rei’s Twilight Spear were connected in a straight line.

What had happened was clear enough just by looking at the result right before their eyes.

In short, Rei’s thrust had met Sharisla’s thrust—that was all.

However, the fact that the tips of Sharisla’s spear and Rei’s Twilight Spear collided without the slightest misalignment… it was a result that could easily be described as miraculous.

If the tips had been even slightly—just the tiniest bit—out of alignment, the spears would have slid along the opponent’s tip or shaft.

Seeing that this did not happen, and that both spears remained completely motionless, anyone could understand just how much skill was required to perform such a divine feat.

The fact that they could grasp this was likely proof that every member of the Kerebel Duke’s Knights possessed a certain level of skill.

“Let’s go.”

With the tips of their lances locked together, Rei muttered briefly.

The moment she heard it, a chill ran down Sharisla’s spine as she wondered what was about to happen… but, perhaps judging that she would lose without doing anything if she stayed like this, she quickly pulled her spear back.

It was precisely because both of them were putting equal force into their spears that the balance was maintained.

Of course, this didn’t mean Sharisura and Rei were equally matched; it was certain that, since this was a mock battle, Rei had been holding back to match Sharisura’s level.

In any case, if Sharisla withdrew her spear in that situation, it wouldn’t have been strange at all for Rei to lunge forward.

It wouldn’t have been strange… but Rei showed no sign of moving, not even a twitch.

However, in the short time they’d spent together, Sharisla had come to fully understand just how superhuman Rei’s abilities were—truly worthy of being called “supernatural.”

Given that, she didn’t expect Rei to lose his balance just because she suddenly let her guard down.

Rather, judging that this was to be expected, she launched another thrust.

“Maybe he’ll block it the same way again?” The thought did cross her mind in the back of her head… and when Rei actually launched another thrust, Sharisla thought, “Just as I expected.”

However, the impact of the spear being parried did not reach her arm, and she instinctively felt a sense of doubt.

She had been wondering why Rei—of all people—would fail to meet the spearhead.

But the fact that there was no reaction in her hand now... Just as she thought that, a slight recoil remained in Sharisla’s hand, and the spear had vanished.

And before she knew it, just as with Leodanis earlier, she found herself gazing at the tip of the Twilight Spear thrust before her eyes. The moment she saw her own spear spinning through the air out of the corner of her eye, she instinctively understood what had happened.

When Sharisla launched her thrust a second time, Rei did not make a move to parry it with the tip of his spear, as he had the first time.

Sharisla couldn’t tell if this was intentional or simply the result of a failure, but she surmised that Rei had timed it to the moment the spearhead was deflected, entangling Sharisla’s spear and flinging it upward.

If the spear—Sharisla’s primary weapon—were to vanish from her grasp, there would be nothing she could do.

No, she did have a dagger or similar weapon prepared for emergencies, but since it was merely a backup, her skill with it was naturally inferior to that with the spear.

There was no way she could defeat Rei in such a situation… no, there was no way she could even land a single blow in retaliation, so Sharisla spoke before Philma could say anything.

“I concede.”

Hearing Sharisura’s words, Rei turned his gaze to Philma. After seeing Philma nod, he withdrew the spearhead he had been pointing at her.

“Hmm, I knew it, but I didn’t expect it to be this overwhelming.”

Those words came from Philma’s mouth, but they contained not disappointment in her subordinates… but rather praise for Rei.

Since they train together almost every day, Philma understands that his subordinates are by no means weak.

It might seem as though they were easily defeated by Rei, but that was purely because of Rei’s extraordinary skill.

It would be a different story if the two of them had been holding back during the mock battle, but judging by their movements, there was no sign of that, nor did they have any reason to do so.

(He’s clearly gotten stronger compared to when I saw him before.)

What Philma is referring to is the time he fought while concealing his identity during the Azott Trading Company incident.

Given that several years have passed since that battle, it was only natural that Rei had grown stronger.

Still, the extent of his growth was unexpected. Now that he no longer needed to hide his identity, Philma found himself wondering just how far he could push himself against Rei in a situation where he could fight at full strength.

(So I was a knight after all... Is that what this means?)

The moment he became aware of the burning passion within him, Philma spoke up.

“It seems I’m too strong when facing Rei. Of course, fighting a formidable opponent is good training in itself… but since this is a rare opportunity, I’d like to have a mock battle with Rei next.”

A murmur ripples through the room.

The knights who heard this began to murmur.

For the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights… no, since Philma is one of the strongest figures even within the Duchy of Kerebel, that reaction was only natural.

That said, she is merely one of the strongest—not the absolute strongest—but even so, the knights who train with her day in and day out knew exactly how formidable Philma’s abilities were.

Philma, who demonstrated that strength day in and day out, and Rei, who had just displayed a power worthy of his “Crimson” moniker right before their eyes.

Naturally, everyone present was curious to know which of the two was stronger...

“Wait a moment!”

Someone stepped in to stop Philma’s actions.

This person was in his mid-forties, older than Philma.

Even so, since he was training like this, his physical strength must be considerable.

However, this knight’s primary role was to support Philma; he held a position akin to that of the knight’s order’s deputy commander.

Rather than fighting on the front lines, he excelled at commanding his subordinates in battle, and precisely because of that position, he had decided that he could not allow Rei and Philma to fight here.

Philma, who could be called one of the strongest in the Duchy of Kerebel, was, so to speak, a symbol of the military.

Although she is less well-known than Elena, who could be called the symbol of the Noble Faction, Philma, who has served as the commander of the Duke of Kerebel’s Knight Corps for many years, is viewed by many as a symbol of strength.

The knights present here today view Philma in exactly the same way.

It is certainly true that they want to see who is stronger, Rei or Philma.

But at the same time, how many of them can actually imagine a scenario where Philma and Rei fight, and Philma loses?

It didn’t seem to have sunk in yet, but if they actually saw Philma lose with their own eyes, they would surely be shocked.

Or perhaps, if the opponent were Elena… or even just someone from the Duchy of Kerebel, it might be different.

However, Rei is obviously not from the Duchy of Kerebel, let alone a member of the Noble Faction.

For Philma—who, in a different sense from Elena, is the very symbol of the Duke of Kerebel’s martial prowess—to lose to such a person could lead to the worst possible outcome.

That is precisely why I stopped it...

“It’s fine.”

“That’s right,” Philma tells her older subordinate.

“But!”

“Rei is someone Elena brought with her. You understand what that means, don’t you?”

“That means…”

Moreover, given that the Duke and Duchess of Kerebel had effectively tacitly approved of his relationship with Rei at last night’s dinner, the man falls silent at Philma’s implied suggestion that holding a mock battle here would pose no problem.

Confirming this, Philma turns to Rei, flashes a savage grin, and asks.

“Well then, Rei. A mock battle with me… will you accept?”

Rei responded to Philma’s question with a silent nod.
