Stepping outside Gilm, Rei was surprised by the number of people gathered before him.

Given what happened with the kobolds, he had thought that if a few dozen—say, fifty—people showed up, that would be a success. But the number standing before Rei now was several times that—nearly two to three hundred people.

Not all of them were adventurers… no, in fact, the majority were people who weren’t currently working as adventurers.

But this is Gilm.

There are plenty of people here who hold the title of “former adventurer,” and even among those who aren’t former adventurers, many have experience butchering monsters for one reason or another.

Or there are former guild employees who are skilled at butchering, as well as those who handle monster meat and are skilled at butchering.

Considering all these factors, it’s hardly surprising that so many people have gathered here.

While the reward for butchering a Giganto Turtle is low, the agreement is that the meat is shared to some extent, so I suppose many were drawn by that prospect.

“Still, I really didn’t expect this many people to show up. …Right?”

“Grr!”

Set let out a growl in response to Rei’s words.

...Though, Set wasn’t looking as bewildered as Rei; it seemed he was simply responding because Rei had spoken to him.

The atmosphere was somewhat brighter than when they first arrived at the guild, and Rei was surprised by the large crowd in front of him. Perhaps hearing Rei’s voice, a guild staff member who had been giving various instructions nearby smiled and called out to him.

“It’s not even close yet. It’s like this now, but since it’s busy in the morning and some people can’t make it yet, there are those who’ll be arriving a bit later. Once they get here, the numbers will go up even more.”

As he said this, the guild staff member turned his gaze toward the adventurers—no, the people participating in the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle—standing ahead.

Among them, I could spot a few familiar faces, starting with Johanna and Hasta.

(Oh, that’s the cook from the diner I’ve been to a few times. …So people are coming from places like that, too?)

Upon spotting the chef from the restaurant that served delicious stew, Rei noticed several other chefs from his regular haunts as well.

It was easy for Rei to imagine that what those cooks were after wasn’t the reward, but the Giganto Turtle’s meat.

“What’s wrong?”

“Nothing, really. …So, how are we going to handle butchering the Giganto Turtle? I feel like we’re too close to Gilm here.”

Rei and the others were currently just outside Gilm.

If they were to bring out a Giganto Turtle—which was larger than the city walls—in a place like this, there was a high probability that Gilm’s walls themselves would be destroyed.

The guild staff member understood this as well and nodded immediately.

“Of course. Listen carefully: under no circumstances should you attempt to bring the Giganto Turtle out here. Absolutely not, no matter what happens.”

“……Now that you mention it, I kind of want to summon it.”

He said this while vaguely recalling a variety show where the hosts keep saying, “Don’t push it! Don’t push it!”

But seeing Rei’s attitude, the guild staff member probably thought he might actually summon the Giganto Turtle right there. Panicking, he grabbed Rei’s hand and dragged him to the designated work area.

Set followed Rei and the guild staff… and the others participating in the dismantling work followed them as well.

“Hey, wait a minute. I was just joking. It’s a joke. I’m not seriously planning to summon a Giganto Turtle in a place like that!”

“In Rei’s case, given his past experience, it seems like he could do it—and the fact that he can is a big deal.”

Well, that’s true.

Hearing the guild official’s words, Rei seemed to accept it without thinking.

Rei knew how others saw him.

That’s exactly why, even when the guild staff member said that, he had no choice but to accept it.

While Rei was unconsciously nodding in agreement, the guild official continued moving, keeping a proper distance from Gilm.

……If they strayed too far here, it would take unnecessary time to return to Gilm, and it would also take time to flee back to Gilm if something happened.

That’s why it was important not to stray too far from Gilm.

“Come to think of it, there was still some snow left in the city—so it really did snow last night.”

When walking through town, the person walking ahead of Rei had already trampled the snow, so what remained on the path wasn’t exactly what you’d call fresh snow.

But since Rei and the others were, of course, the first to walk in the areas slightly off the main road today… that meant they could enjoy the sensation of stepping on fresh snow.

The sensation of Sleipnir Boots pressing into the snow.

For Rei, winter was basically never a season he could welcome.

Back in Japan, as was typical of the Tohoku region, there was heavy snowfall, requiring snow removal almost every day, and the ground was frozen, so if you weren’t careful, you could easily slip and fall.

Also, the snow on the roads had been compacted into a slushy mess by countless passing cars, and it wasn’t uncommon for snow to fly up when a car drove by nearby.

Not all snow is like that, and just like the scene spread out before him, there have been countless times when Rei has experienced stepping on fresh snow for the first time.

“That’s right. To be honest, I’d prefer it if it didn’t snow at all.”

The guild staff member probably doesn’t have many fond memories of snow either.

Though his expression didn’t change, he said this with an air of being fed up with the snow.

“That said, if it doesn’t snow, there’s less meltwater, which means there’s less water in the mountains, forests, and woodlands from summer through fall. If that happens, Gilm might naturally face a water shortage too… which would be a real problem in its own right.”

“Ah, I see. Water shortages are a matter of life and death, after all.”

There are, of course, magical items that can generate water, like the flowing dagger Rei possesses.

However, such magical items aren’t infinite in number, and with a population rivaling that of a city—not to mention the fact that construction is underway, further increasing the number of residents—it’s unlikely they could produce enough water to quench the thirst of everyone in Gilm.

Well, if it were just a matter of magical power, Rei alone could handle it.

But considering the effort and cost involved in distributing that water, it’s simply not realistic.

While meltwater isn’t the only water source, Rei could understand that having multiple sources is better.

That’s because, back when I was in Japan, I saw a TV report about how dam storage levels were dropping and people would no longer be able to use water freely.

Large-scale water storage facilities like dams do not exist in this world.

Well, maybe they do exist, but at least there weren’t any in Gilm.

As they talked like that, once they had put some distance between themselves and Gilm, the guild official spoke up, looking slightly tense.

There was no doubt that the conversation up until now had been partly meant to ease his own tension.

Precisely because of that, now that he was actually about to say those words, the tension he couldn’t hide—and hadn’t managed to hide—was showing.

“Alright then, Rei. This should be a good spot. Please take out the Giganto Turtle.”

“Got it. Okay, then? I’m bringing it out, okay?”

As I asked for confirmation, I made sure the adventurer following behind me didn’t step in front of me… and that Set, who was enjoying the sensation of stepping on the fresh snow, was far enough away… then I took the Giganto Turtle out of my Misty Ring.

Just a moment ago, there was definitely nothing there—a grassland… no, a snowfield.

But the next moment, a wall stood there.

Well, strictly speaking, it wasn’t a wall. It was just that the Giganto Turtle was so enormous that anyone seeing it up close would mistake it for a wall.

Giganto Turtle。

True to its name, the Giganto Turtle is an incredibly massive creature.

Reactions to seeing such a Giganto Turtle vary.

Those who participated in the battle in the Treant Forest and actually saw the Giganto Turtle with their own eyes wore expressions of understanding, as if to say, “So that’s how big it was.”

Those who did not participate in that battle are simply astonished to see the corpse of a monster of this size up close.

Beyond that, some look on with curiosity, while others—perhaps because a comrade was injured or killed during the incident in the Forest of Treants—gaze at it with hatred in their eyes.

“What should we do about the head?”

During the battle with the Giganto Turtle, Rei had severed its head.

As a result, the Giganto Turtle’s body and head were stored separately inside the Misty Ring.

Wondering if he should take out the head as well, Rei asked the guild clerk... and the clerk snapped out of his daze of astonishment.

“Huh? Oh, yes. Um… well… not yet. We’re going to start dismantling the torso first, so please leave the head for later.”

“But the head is smaller. Wouldn’t it be better to start with the head instead of the body?”

“No. The head is usually a crucial part and contains valuable materials. So, I’d recommend against disassembling it right away. But if you absolutely insist on starting with it, Rei, then go ahead.”

Perhaps having calmed down a bit while speaking, the guild clerk finally looked at him and asked, “So, what will you do?”

Rei thought for a moment at those words, then nodded.

“Just do whatever you think is best. After all, the Guild is probably more used to handling situations like this.”

“……We’ve never disassembled a monster this huge before, though. If we’re talking about disassembling giant monsters, I’d say Rei is the one who’s more used to it, since she disassembled that Remrace that appeared in Emoshion.”

Hearing that, Rei could do nothing but nod.

That said, while the Remrace was a giant monster, the Giganto Turtle is even larger than that.

If asked to take command of butchering that colossal beast, Rei would likely shake his head and say it was simply impossible.

After all, the terms of this job weren’t for Rei to do the dismantling himself, but for the guild to gather the manpower to do it.

“Well, just do your best for now. More importantly, should I give the order soon? It looks like a lot of people are confused.”

At Rei’s words, the guild staff member finally seemed to remember the current situation. He gave Rei a slight bow, then shouted loudly toward the personnel who had gathered to dismantle the Giganto Turtle.

“Alright, we’re about to begin dismantling the Giganto Turtle! Please split into the groups as previously instructed and get to work! Your rewards will be determined by your contribution to the dismantling!”

At the guild official’s shout, even those who had been transfixed by the Giganto Turtle seemed to snap out of it, and they split into groups to begin the dismantling work.

Rei watched the dismantling work while petting Set, who had returned satisfied after playing in the snow.

That said, even when starting the dismantling, given the Giganto Turtle’s size, they couldn’t just jump right into it.

They needed to reconfirm who would be handling which parts, and some stood watch from a distance to ensure no one stole materials during the process.

In the end, the actual dismantling didn’t begin until about thirty minutes had passed.

It wasn’t yet fully light out, but that dimness only served to heighten the eeriness of the Giganto Turtle’s immense size.

“Grr? Grrrr…”

“Is it okay that we’re taking so long to butcher this?” Set growled at Rei, who was petting him, but Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered and just kept petting him.

“We’re dissecting a monster this huge, so it’s only natural that we’ll run into some trouble at first. Well, starting tomorrow… or maybe after a little more time, we’ll get the hang of it. There are quite a few people here who aren’t adventurers, after all.”

In any case, he doesn’t expect the dissection of the Giganto Turtle to be finished by the end of this winter.

That being the case, I’d rather they take their time and do a thorough job, rather than rushing through it and doing a sloppy job.

“Hey! First, we’re going to skin this leg, so the people in charge, come over here!”

“Hold on a sec, I’ll be right there! More importantly, we need to do something about the shell—which team’s handling that?”

Listening to that exchange, Rei and Set watched the dismantling work for a while, eating the sandwiches Lana had made for them.
