Sandrine brought the bread dough and handed it to Rodrigo.

"Here you go, Dad. This."

"Ah, thank you. So, Mr. Rei. Should we just wrap this meat filling in the dough and then steam it... is that right?"

Rodrigo asks for confirmation, and Rei nods.

"Yeah. But I'm not sure about the steaming time, so you'll have to adjust that part."

When Rei was in Japan, he had bought meat buns sold at the supermarket.

But lightly spraying water on those buns before microwaving them was clearly different from actually steaming them in a steamer like this.

After all, supermarket meat buns are just pre-made items that are meant to be eaten as is, and you're just warming them up.

"Hmm, I see. Well, I'll try adjusting that part. Alright, let's get started..."

"No."

"Huh?"

Rei corrected Rodrigo as he tried to fold the dough over the meat filling after rolling it into a rectangle.

"Um, I think..."

Recalling the method for wrapping meat buns from a cooking show, Rei reached out.

"Like this, you put the meat filling in the center of the dough, then wrap it up and gather it at the top... Ah, it's not working."

"Um, like this?"

Rodrigo seemed to grasp the gist of Rei's explanation. He wrapped the meat filling in the dough, shaping it into the form Rei recognized.

Being able to shape it properly just from hearing the explanation was typical of a chef running a shop on the main street.

"Yeah, yeah. That's pretty much the shape of the meat buns I know."

Under Rodrigo's hands, the shape of the meat bun Rei knew well was completed.

Though Rei just didn't know it, some meat buns involved spreading the dough into a circle, placing the filling inside, and then folding it into a half-moon shape...

"Hey, Rei. Does this shape mean anything? It takes quite a bit of effort to make it like this. Couldn't we just wrap it like the bread Dad made earlier?"

Sandrine voiced what was surely a natural question.

Since this shop gets many customers, it definitely makes sense to minimize the effort required.

"I wonder? This is the shape I know, so I suppose it probably has some meaning. I'd like you to experiment with that too. It might affect the steaming process and such."

Rodrigo nodded in response to Rei's answer to Sandrine's question.

Indeed, it was an undeniable fact that the shape affected the steaming process and consequently the texture.

Furthermore, since the dough adjustment and the ingredients added to the meat filling also affect these aspects to some degree, research was undoubtedly necessary.

"Understood. It seems there's plenty to experiment with, which is excellent."

Rodrigo might seem timid, but when it came to his work, his enthusiasm was different.

Or perhaps it was simply because his hobby and work had merged.

"Anyway, let's give it a try. We won't know what to do until we see exactly how it turns out."

Rodrigo said this as he swiftly wrapped the meat filling in dough and placed it into the steamer.

The steamer itself wasn't much different from the ones Rei knew.

Since steaming is the cooking method, it was only natural that the shape would be similar.

(Using magic items could make it look completely different... but fortunately, or unfortunately, this shop's steamer is closer to a traditional bamboo steamer.)

Even while the dumplings steamed, Sandrine didn't forget to gather information.

Rodrigo kept his eyes fixed on the steamer, staring intently at it, wondering when it would be done.

"Hey, Rei. You said meat buns are the basic dish among steamed buns, but what else is there?"

"Right. Well, from what I know, there are ones with different fillings inside the meat bun... Other than that, I think there are ones where cheese is kneaded into the dough and steamed, things like that."

Rei recalled the steamed buns his mother used to buy from the supermarket back on Earth as he explained.

"Cheese? ...I see. That sounds pretty tasty."

"Well, I think we can decide what kind of dish to make right here at this shop. Personally, I think meat buns are best."

"...Hey, Rei-san."

Suddenly, Sandrine looked at Rei as if something had occurred to her and spoke up.

"Hm? What is it?"

"Rei-san, you know about steamed buns... no, this dish called meat buns, only in theory, right?"

"Yeah, that's true."

"But listening to the way you just described it, it sounds like you've actually eaten it before."

"......"

Rei fell silent at the sudden remark.

Considering his own words and actions since he started making the meat bun... no, since he started explaining it, it wasn't surprising Sandrine felt that way.

(Had he been a little too fixated on the meat buns?)

While Sandrine was sharper than Rei had anticipated, he still had to admit his own words and actions had been careless.

"Well..."

He got that far, but then hesitated, unsure what to say next.

If he admitted he'd actually eaten this dish before, he'd likely be asked where he'd eaten it and why he'd kept it a secret.

Of course, covering it up wouldn't be difficult.

Rei had traveled to various places in Set, and his background story was that he had lived as an apprentice magician before coming to Gilm.

But even so, Rei wanted to avoid carelessly making excuses here.

"Sandrine, it doesn't really matter where this dish called 'meat bun'... or steamed buns come from, does it?"

Rodrigo, who had been staring intently at the steamer, offered Rei a helping hand as he stumbled over his words.

"Father."

"Rei has been a tremendous help with this matter. Considering that, it doesn't matter if this dish was actually made somewhere else. ...No, rather, if it's actually being eaten, that means it's accepted."

Rodrigo's words were undeniably true.

If meat buns were actually eaten as a dish, it meant the dish itself was accepted.

"But..."

"Sandrine, Rei is an adventurer. A high-ranking one with a reputation, at that. You understand there are things he can and can't discuss with others, right?"

Rodrigo spoke softly, but honestly, he'd rather not get involved in the kind of troublesome situations that come from digging too deep into the unspoken circumstances of a high-ranking adventurer like Rei.

But that wasn't unreasonable.

If he were an adventurer or mercenary confident in his skills, it might be different, but Rodrigo was, after all, just a cook.

It was only natural he'd prefer to stay out of such matters.

Especially with a daughter and wife like Sandrine.

Whether she sensed this or simply decided it was too dangerous to pry further, Sandrine gave up asking Rei any more questions.

(Well, it wasn't that dangerous a topic, really.)

Of course, Rei couldn't reveal his secret... he couldn't talk about Japan. But it wasn't the kind of secret that would warrant killing someone if they asked.

Still, if she could brush off his own slip of the tongue, that was something Rei was glad about.

Silence filled the kitchen for a moment.

It was Rodrigo who broke the silence.

"It's ready. ...I think the steaming time is probably fine now."

He announced, removing the steamer from the heat and placing it before Rei and the others.

The moment he lifted the lid, steam billowed out into the kitchen.

(Since the meat filling is inside the dough, there's hardly any smell. ...With meat buns, you get a slightly sweet aroma, though.)

As the steam cleared, they could clearly see what was inside the steamer.

"Huh?"

It was Rei who voiced the surprise.

Her murmur seemed to carry a hint of surprise. Sandrine opened her mouth, a bad feeling creeping over her.

"What's wrong, Rei? Is something off?"

"Ah... Doesn't it look kind of flattened?"

Just as Rei said, compared to when it was placed in the steamer, it had visibly flattened out.

At least, the steamed buns Rei knew didn't flatten out like this.

Though Rei was puzzled by this, Rodrigo shook his head, insisting it was fine.

"This is our first attempt at cooking them, so it's only natural to have a few mishaps. In fact, I think we got off easy with just a little flattening. It probably happened because we used the same bread dough we'd use for regular baking."

"Meaning?"

Rodrigo smiled at his daughter's question and began to explain.

"If we keep experimenting with different dough recipes, we'll eventually find one that works well for steaming."

"Dad, you make it sound easy, but isn't it really hard?"

"It'll be fine. I'm good at this kind of work. Plus, Mom will help too."

Hearing about Mom helping, Rei was slightly surprised.

Since only Rodrigo and Sandrine were present, he had assumed his mother was no longer with them for some reason.

Still, he kept quiet, thinking voicing that thought might lead to trouble.

"Alright, let's taste it then."

Rei took the meat bun, which had flattened more than he remembered, and split it in two.

The aroma of the meat filling, made with oak meat, wafted around them.

The aroma was appetizing, and considering the ingredients, it was easy to predict it would be delicious.

Rodrigo was a skilled cook to begin with.

Given that, it was unlikely to be so bad it was inedible unless something truly terrible had happened.

Drawn by the scent, Rei took a bite into the bun.

First came the heat... then the flavor of the filling, a blend of oak meat and various vegetables, and finally the soft sweetness of the bread dough enveloped it all, mingling in his mouth... but.

"Hmm..."

Rodrigo swallowed the meat bun in his mouth and grumbled in dissatisfaction.

Sandrine tilted her head slightly, looking a bit perplexed too.

"It's... meh."

Then Rei voiced his honest opinion of the taste.

...Yes, to be honest, the taste was indeed subtle.

The meat filling was subtly bland, its flavor diluted by the moisture from the vegetables, making it a bit watery.

The bread dough itself wasn't bad, but it still had an odd texture.

Its softness also fell short compared to the texture of the meat buns Rei knew, leaving it somewhat crumbly.

It was like the filling was watery and the bread dough was crumbly.

Overall, Rei's description of it being "subtle" was spot on.

It wasn't exactly bad, but if asked if she'd willingly eat it again, she couldn't honestly say yes.

"Hmm, well... But for a first attempt, I think it turned out pretty good, don't you? Now it's just a matter of how to improve it from here."

Rodrigo seemed to see potential in the meat bun's flavor, smiling as he said this.

"Is that so? ...I see."

After taking a bite or two, Rei tilted his head.

But since a professional chef said so, he concluded it must be true.

If it were up to Rei alone, he had no confidence he could make this meat bun... no, this meat bun-like thing—that description seemed more fitting—any tastier than it already was.

It was probably a comment born from his confidence in his own skills as a chef.

"Yes. It's not that delicious right now, but it shows potential. And above all, since the steamed bun method was something I never imagined before, I'm looking forward to seeing how it turns out."

For Rei, it was hardly a success, but Rodrigo seemed to see it differently.

Are you okay? Rei looked at Sandrine with that concern, but she nodded back, signaling she was fine.

"Honestly, it's true it's not tasty as it is now, but if Dad says he can do it, he'll definitely figure something out. He's usually timid and indecisive, but when it comes to cooking, you can trust him."

"Sandrine... that's a bit..."

Was that praise or criticism?

As a father, he probably took it as a put-down.

Rodrigo addressed his daughter with a slightly pathetic expression.

"What are you talking about? Come on, let's first figure out how to finish this dish."

Watching the affectionate exchange between parent and child, Rei suddenly spoke up.

"Hey, about these meat buns... Do you think I could let Set try one?"

After being told there was no particular problem with that, Rei tried feeding Set the meat bun imitation... only to hear a rather awkward cry.
