The group woke up at the inn in Subursta, ate breakfast a little early, and left the inn.

...At that moment, Rei's hand was still rubbing his sleepy eyes. This was likely due to his general dislike of mornings, but it could also be attributed to the fact that he had been talking with Elena using the Paired Orb late into the night.

"Hm!"

Instead of riding in the carriage, Rei walked through the town alongside Set. He gave a small nod in response to Byune's words, spoken from the carriage window as if urging him to perk up.

(Kids sure are energetic from the morning, aren't they? Makes me jealous. When I was little, I'd go to elementary school early and play on the playground too. ...Looking back now, I wonder what gave me all that energy back then?)

Thinking about how different he was as a child compared to now—including becoming Rei—he headed toward the gate, but upon arriving near it, he noticed some commotion.

"Hey, hey, no need for a commotion here."

The gruff merchant on the driver's seat muttered, but there was no way around it if they wanted to leave Subursta.

Rei, too, felt a vague sense of unease as he stroked Set and moved toward the gate. He saw several guards surrounding someone.

The merchant, wanting to avoid any trouble, probably wished to steer clear of the commotion. However, since it was happening near the main gate, they had no choice but to pass close by to leave Subursta.

And just as the group passed near the commotion...

"Master! Take me as your disciple!"

The unexpected cry echoed around them.

Master? Startled by the unexpected words, Rei tilted his head and turned toward the sound.

...That was probably a mistake. Because there, staring at him, was Magita.

Even for Rei, forgetting someone he'd only met yesterday was unthinkable.

He understood that this person was the one he had fought yesterday, but he still wondered why they were calling him Master.

Considering yesterday's events, he felt strongly that she should be resentful instead.

But in Magita's eyes fixed on him, there was no trace of resentment whatsoever.

How should he react to Magita? While he hesitated over this, several of the guards surrounding Magita approached Rei and the others.

"Excuse me, but could we ask you a few questions?"

The request wasn't exactly high-handed, so Rei and the others felt it would be awkward to refuse.

Besides, they hadn't done anything they needed to hide, so there was no real reason to refuse.

...The two merchants, now facing a delay in leaving Subursta due to this incident, wore slightly displeased expressions. However, since they were the ones who had asked Rei to fight Magita last night, they couldn't very well refuse.

In the end, after hearing about last night's events and learning Magita had lost to Rei, the guards looked satisfied.

Being Subursta guards, some of them recognized Rei's face, which probably helped.

Those guards, however, turned their gaze toward Magita with a look of genuine interest.

To challenge that Crimson One, they seemed to say.

Magita, too, wore a look of astonishment upon hearing that Rei was a Rank B adventurer known by the alias Crimson. Yet, he quickly replaced it with a smile that seemed out of place with those sharp eyes.

"I see. So my master really was strong, huh?"

"No, I'm not saying I want to take you on as a disciple. Besides, the very meaning of seeking strength is different for you and me, isn't it?"

"The meaning behind seeking strength?"

Rei sighed, realizing Magita genuinely didn't understand, and spoke up.

"Remember what you said at the tavern yesterday? You said you wanted to show off your strength to others and bask in that sense of superiority, right? Unfortunately, I can't agree with that kind of philosophy. I'm not interested in training someone like that."

His refusal stemmed partly from the fact that until recently, he'd been an instructor for mock battles at the officer academy.

Master and disciple might be subtly different from instructor and student, but they were undoubtedly similar.

Rei also felt a bit bored at the idea of teaching someone how to fight again.

If he had the teacher-like personality that enjoyed instructing others, he might have taken Magita under his wing and tried to reform him.

But Rei was absolutely not that kind of person.

He actually holds a similar view to Magita's – that opposing enemies should be crushed by force, and that showing off one's power brings a sense of superiority.

"No, Master! Please, train me!"

"I told you, I'm not your master. Besides, if you want to get that strong, you should go somewhere with more fighting. Not necessarily Gilm... but somewhere like Abuero."

Compared to Gilm, stronger monsters naturally appear in places closer to the frontier, like Aburo.

In terms of fighting humanoid foes like bandits, Saburusta might be more suitable. But if you genuinely want to fight powerful enemies, Abuero is better than Saburusta.

"That's... because I thought there was no one stronger than me."

"I don't know what made you think that, but from my perspective, it looks like you ignored all the ways you could have gotten stronger and just focused on defeating opponents to bask in a sense of superiority. Besides, what do I gain from training you?"

"I'll help you with your work as a master."

Those words, spoken instantly, would have been considered favorable terms for an ordinary adventurer... one roughly as strong as Magita.

After all, Magita was somewhat famous as a reasonably skilled adventurer, even if she did cause trouble in Suburusta.

Understanding this himself, Magita probably thought that helping Rei would be his condition...

"No thanks."

Once again, Rei flatly rejected Magita's proposal.

"What?! Why, Master?!"

"I told you I'm not your master. Besides, even though I'm traveling with Vihera and Byune this time, I'm basically a solo adventurer. And I've got this guy as my partner."

"Gururu?"

Gently stroking Set's back, a pleased sound came in response.

Magita knew the griffin was with Rei, but she'd only had eyes for Rei herself. Her gaze shifted back to Set, and an expression of surprise... didn't appear.

"What is this monster?"

"Huh?"

At the words that escaped Magita's mouth, everyone present—not just Rei and Vihera, but even the guards and the two merchants—uttered the same muttered response in unison.

It was only natural. As adventurers, they assumed everyone knew famous monsters.

No, even common folk, not just adventurers, knew the name Griffin and what it looked like.

"...My familiar. This is Set, the griffin."

"Gluu!"

The cry that escaped Set's beak was by no means meant to intimidate.

Still, his beastman's wild instincts must have sensed just how powerful the monster before him was.

He instinctively took a few steps back.

(He can't gauge strength just by seeing Set's form, but he retreats upon hearing the cry... Huh. He's definitely different in many ways. Not being surprised by Set is a plus, but then again, that might be natural considering he didn't know Set... didn't know griffins.)

That peculiarity piqued his interest somewhat, but it still wasn't enough to make him go out of his way to take a disciple.

"When traveling in large groups like this time is different, but basically I fly through the air riding Set. So what would you do? By the way, basically only I can ride Set. Well, I could take a kid along with me, but someone as big as you? No way. ...I could carry you by hooking you onto my claws, but would you want that?"

The suggestion that came out of Rei's mouth was clearly an impossible option for Magita too. He shook his head immediately.

Among the humans living in this world, those who experience flying are surprisingly few, except for exceptions like dragon knights.

Even fearless Magita... flying through the air? And being hooked onto Set's claws to fly? She absolutely wanted no part of that.

Seeing this, Rei nodded with satisfaction.

"See? That's exactly it. I won't teach you how to fight. If you really want to get stronger, you should go to Abuelo... no, to Gilm. There are plenty of guys over there stronger than you. Besides, even if we ignore Byune, Vihera is stronger than you, right?"

"Hey, why's my name coming up here?"

"Vihera likes to fight, right? So I figured she might be a good match for Magita."

At Rei's words, Vihera glanced at Magita for a moment, but immediately shook her head.

"Sorry, but that's not the kind of strength I'm looking for."

"What did she say?!"

Magita, clearly displeased by Vihera's words, immediately turned to glare at her.

In Magita's eyes, Vihera was nothing more than a dancer or a prostitute... at best, Rei's mistress.

While captivated by her beauty and figure, Magita could not tolerate her words and actions that clearly placed her beneath Magita.

But before Magita could take a step toward Vihera, she was stopped by a spear thrust abruptly before her.

Everything about that spear, from blade to shaft, was a deep, solid green. There was something about it that seemed capable of stealing one's consciousness just by looking at it.

"That's why I say you're still green. Didn't you hear what I said? Vihera is stronger than you. Not just a little stronger, but overwhelmingly so. If you challenged her, you'd be lucky to last a few seconds. Well, if we're talking about the same fighting style, Vihera might actually be better suited to train you."

"...Me? More than this woman?"

"Yeah. Definitely."

Magita shot him a look that said she wasn't convinced, but Rei, returning the thorn spear to the Misty Ring, stated it plainly.

"Look, you use hand-to-hand combat as your weapon, right? So why did you even think about becoming my disciple? My weapon is..."

Rei glanced at the guard who recognized him, likely deciding it was better than wasting time here. Confirming the guard's nod, he drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

Magita's eyes widened at the sight of the scythe suddenly appearing before him, a weapon taller than Rei himself.

"The weapon I normally use is the Death Scythe. What good would it do for a fighter like you to become my disciple when I wield such a long weapon? If you absolutely must become a disciple, then you should choose Vihera over me."

"...Hey, Rei. Trying to pass me off?"

Realizing from Rei's words that he was being thrust into the role of Magita's master, Vihera fixed his gaze on Rei.

"Well, what can I say? Like I just said, fighting is nothing more than a pastime for me. So, asking Vihera, who knows real combat, is the best choice, right?"

"...I suppose so. It might be the best choice. If you don't consider my circumstances at all."

"Don't you think it'd be nice to have someone you can spar with casually?"

"I don't think so. At this level of strength, I'd rather just train alone."

Magita could no longer contain himself at Vihera's words, which dismissed his own skill as irrelevant and implied she was clearly his superior based on their exchange.

"Master! Let me fight this woman! If I win, you'll have to accept me as your disciple!"

"...Well, I suppose so?"

At Magita's words, Rei turned his gaze toward Vihera.

Seeing Magita clearly determined to fight, Vihera eventually sighed.

"Fine. I'll fight you. Let's go outside the city. Staying here any longer will only get in the way of others."

The area near the main gate was fairly spacious; there was no reason they couldn't pass through even with Rei and the others present.

But drawing attention in such a place was inevitable.

Especially since Vihera was clad in thin garments that accentuated her alluring figure, and Rei had Set nearby.

...The least conspicuous among them was Byune, but even Byune, only about ten years old, was fully armed. Seeing how flawless her bearing was, it was clear she only blended in because the other three stood out so strongly.

For now, everyone nodded at Vihera's words, and the group, along with Magita, would be leaving Subursta.
