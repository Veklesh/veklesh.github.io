"Dammit! Even if they were lured by the flute, there are way too many of them!"

The dark elf man shouted as he fired an arrow.

The arrow flew straight from the bow, piercing the torso of a goblin bursting out of the bushes.

It wasn't an instant kill like a headshot, but it was enough to severely slow its movements.

It had been about ten minutes since the dark elves and Rei had joined forces and started moving toward the settlement.

But for the two dark elf men, if not for Rei, those ten minutes felt like an hour, or even two.

The problem was the goblins, appearing as if they were endlessly spawning.

The flute Rei had first used, blowing it several times while moving to signal their presence to others.

Like fish drawn to bait, the goblins appeared one after another at the sound of that whistle.

Though not even ten minutes had passed, the number of goblins they had slain already exceeded thirty.

It was true that the endless stream of goblins was taking a mental toll.

But there was another, even bigger problem. That was...

"Damn it, I'm running low on arrows! How about you over there?!"

"I'm running low too!"

Yes, as the two dark elf men shouted in frustration, the dwindling number of arrows was the biggest problem.

Since bows were their primary weapons, paying attention to arrow count was essential.

But the truth was, with goblins appearing endlessly, they couldn't afford to worry about conserving arrows.

"Once you've used up your arrows, switch to guarding the children. I'll handle the remaining enemies."

"Even if we run out of arrows, we still have spirit magic!"

To the dark elf who shouted that they couldn't afford to play around in this situation, Rei paused briefly before yelling back.

"Just don't drain your magic dry!"

Shouting, Rei fired the arrowheads created by Nebra's eyes at the goblins that had materialized.

If the dark elves knew what kind of magic item Nebra's Eye originally was, they would be extremely envious.

For archers, the number of arrows they carried was a matter of life and death.

But carrying more arrows meant their weight slowed their movement.

Dark Elves had spirit magic as an alternative to bows, but even that had limits on the number and types they could use, given the finite nature of their magic power.

Furthermore, some dark elves couldn't use elemental magic due to innate limitations.

Not everyone possessed seemingly inexhaustible magical power like Rei.

(If only there were a few more, I could entrust Lugdos to them and move Set freely...)

Lugdos, unconscious, obviously couldn't be handled roughly.

He'd been struck on the head, after all, so safety was paramount.

Protecting the children was also a factor, so keeping him on Set was still the best option.

But... if more Dark Elves were joining us, we could have them escort the children and carry Lugdos.

That would free up Set to move freely, dramatically boosting the party's combat strength. Though...

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set's roar echoed as his Wind Arrows struck the newly appeared goblins one after another.

Even now, purely focusing on ranged attacks, he was undoubtedly a formidable force.

Still, Set's... the griffin's true strength lay in close combat, leveraging its exceptional physical prowess.

Especially now, Set possessed the skill Power Crush, capable of shattering even large boulders with a single blow. ...Though, against goblins, using a skill of that caliber was nothing but overkill.

As Rei and his party advanced through the night forest, slaying goblins, the bushes ahead rustled once more.

"Enemy! We're out of arrows, so I'll use spirit magic!"

"Me too!"

The two dark elf men shouted, each unleashing earth and water elemental magic.

An orc bursting from the bushes had its instep pierced by an earth spear sprouting from the ground, sending it tumbling to the earth.

The orc beside it suddenly found its face enveloped in water, causing it to panic. It swung the longsword it held, wounding another orc that had fallen.

With the two leading orcs in such a state, the orcs behind them naturally couldn't advance.

Worse still, some fell over and were trampled by their comrades, screaming in pain.

"Flying Slash!"

The flying slash aimed at the clustered orcs cleaved several orcs in two, while also inflicting cuts of varying severity on several others behind them.

"Let's go! Charge straight through!"

Shouting only that, Rei left the confused orcs behind and began running.

"Guruu"

Hearing Set growl as if lamenting the wasted orc flesh, Rei nodded inwardly.

(I would have liked to salvage the meat, but there's no time for that now. ...Well, I did manage to salvage a few bodies, so I'll call it good.)

The orc meat was delicious, a shame to waste, but there was no time to leisurely stow the corpses in their current situation.

Doing something like that would undoubtedly attract other monsters.

If it were just Rei and Set, such a thing might have been possible, but now he needed to protect the children.

With such a vulnerable presence among them, enduring the loss of orc meat was simply unavoidable.

As they ran, leaving the orc group behind, Set suddenly glanced toward the branches of the trees growing on the right.

A few seconds later, Rei sensed something approaching them.

(Again? And on a branch? A troublesome monster... Huh?)

Rei considered launching a preemptive Flying Slash, but realized the approaching presence wasn't a monster and breathed a sigh of relief.

Seeing Rei like this, a Dark Elf man running nearby asked curiously.

"What's up?"

"Reinforcements. Over here."

As Rei muttered this, a Dark Elf leapt down from a branch to the right, landing and immediately running alongside Rei and the others.

"Did I keep you waiting?"

The one asking was the dark elf man who had spoken with Rei before this expedition.

Seeing him approach as casually as ever, a smile touched Rei's lips.

"Yeah. Thanks to you, we ended up fighting tons of goblins, orcs, and kobolds."

"Same here. It's the night forest, sure, but why the hell are there so many goblins and such just today?"

The man sighed as he muttered, his quiver already less than half full.

He hadn't just been shooting arrows from a distance; he'd been fighting up close too. His leather armor was splattered with monster blood everywhere.

"Marina said monsters that entered the Labyrinth Barrier while the World Tree was weakened got trapped inside when it recovered. That must be it."

"...Still, this abnormal increase is a bit much, don't you think?"

"That's undeniable... Huh?!"

While talking with the dark elf man, Rei fired an arrowhead created from Nebra's eyes into the bushes ahead.

"Gyaaah!"

What followed was a dog-like yelp.

"A kobold?"

As if answering Rei's mutter, a kobold burst out from the bushes.

It had likely intended to ambush them, but it lacked the strength to hide its presence from Rei.

No, even if it had fooled Rei, it couldn't have fooled Set running right behind her.

'WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Realizing their ambush had been discovered, the kobolds let out a unified war cry and charged at Rei and his companions.

The dark elves countered, firing arrows from their bows and using elemental magic to intercept them one after another.

Set also took them down with ranged attacks like Wind Arrows, Ice Arrows, and water balls.

Amidst this, Rei naturally used Flying Slash to slice through the kobolds one after another.

(The current situation is definitely close to the worst possible, but the one good thing is I can unleash Flying Slash to my heart's content.)

His Flying Slash, now at level five, had seen its power skyrocket.

In terms of mastering Flying Slash, having endless targets was definitely not a bad thing.

(I've also gotten a good feel for adjusting its strength through these battles. The range of the more powerful Flying Slash, and how effective it is against different numbers of enemies.)

He unleashed Flying Slash at the kobold climbing a tree to attack from above, cleaving its body in two along with the branches.

The kobold, suddenly showered with its comrade's entrails from above, was confused, unable to comprehend what had happened... and that's when Rei and the others charged in.

"Haaaaaaaah!"

One of the Dark Elves unleashed a flurry of thrusts with his rapier.

In an instant, the thrusts came twice, thrice in succession. They pierced the torso of a spear-wielding kobold, and as the impact forced its head downward, the thrusts pierced its skull.

The movement was undeniably that of a formidable fighter; to Rei's eyes, this dark elf was clearly a skilled warrior.

(So even among Dark Elves, there are those skilled in close combat, not just magic and bows.)

Suddenly, the image of an elf who had fought in last year's Bestir Empire tournament flashed through Rei's mind.

An opponent worthy of an A-rank adventurer, possessing both powerful spirit magic and formidable close-quarters combat skills.

(I'm not quite at that level, though. ...Maybe Rank C? They live longer than humans, so they have more time to train. That makes sense.)

Rei swung his Death Scythe at the approaching kobold.

"Gyaaah!"

A blow delivered with pure brute force, no technique involved.

Yet that single blow shattered the kobold's longsword and, with unabated momentum, struck its face.

A monster like a kobold couldn't possibly withstand the impact of the Death Scythe, which weighed around 100kg.

The kobold, screaming as it was blown away, had its skull crushed and lost its life at that very moment.

When he tried a Pain Burst with the return blade, the kobold that took it let out a scream unlike any he'd ever heard before and lost consciousness.

"Honestly, shouldn't we be arriving at the settlement around now?"

"Yeah, we're definitely getting close. Honestly, I never expected this many monsters to appear just because it's a nighttime forest."

The dark elf man positioned beside Rei replied to his mutterings while drawing his bow taut.

"Seems that way. I thought this was just normal for this forest."

"Even so, this is too many. If it's always like this, there's no way we could live in this forest."

"So it's related to the forest's disturbance after all?"

"Probably."

Though they exchanged words, their hands never stopped, swiftly dispatching one kobold after another.

The battle continued like this for several minutes. Every kobold that had attacked lay dead on the ground.

While they fought the kobolds, the nearby dark elves gradually joined the fray, steadily increasing Rei's party's combat strength as time passed.

Conversely, the kobolds weren't particularly powerful monsters to begin with. They had managed to hold out solely due to their sheer numbers... but once that threshold was crossed, their annihilation was swift and, in a sense, an inevitable outcome.

"...Our numbers have grown."

"Yeah. At this rate, we shouldn't have to fear monster attacks anymore."

"That's true... but if it's not too much trouble, could you take the four on Set's back? That way, Set could freely join the fight."

"No, but that... I think that griffin is already contributing enough to our forces as it is? Why go to the trouble of having us take the children now?"

From the perspective of the dark elf man talking to Rei, Set was already fulfilling his role perfectly by firing ranged shots while carrying four children on his back.

And if they took the children, whoever was watching them would obviously be unable to participate in the attack.

While spirit magic could be used to attack, after multiple battles since entering the forest, few had ample magic power left.

Therefore, it was only natural to think the current situation was the best course.

Rei tried to retort, but another Dark Elf spoke up first.

"We're approaching the settlement!"

Hearing that, it was only natural for Rei to conclude that taking in the children now would only be a hindrance. In the end, they decided to proceed to the settlement as they were.

And then... the forest ended, and soon the barrier's protective circle came into view.
