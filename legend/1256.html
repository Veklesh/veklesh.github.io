Confirming the Silver Lion's magic stone absorption and terrain manipulation skills... or rather, several days after the day Set played in the snow... Rei stood before the shoe shop he had visited before.

As usual, Set was beside Rei.

Though the day Mireine and Johanna had spent together had ended, several townspeople who had witnessed it asked Rei if they could play with Set too. So, something like a "play in the snow with Set" gathering was held in the backyard near the stables of the Dusk Wheat inn. But that too was over now.

Well, to be precise, it ended naturally because Rei was heading to the shoemaker's, and Set wanted to go with him.

"Wait here for a bit. I don't think it'll take long."

"Grrr."

At Rei's words, Set obediently let out a growl and lay down near the shoe shop.

Snow had been falling intermittently since Rei last visited the shoemaker, and now it had accumulated quite heavily.

It was now fully winter, and most adventurers, except those who hadn't managed to save up enough funds to get through the season, were allowing their bodies to rest for a brief respite.

Because of this, places like the dining halls and taverns were now experiencing what could be called the busiest time of the year.

Rei gave Set one last pat before entering the cobbler's shop.

"Welcome. ...Ah, Rei. Your shoes are ready. Just wait a moment."

The shopkeeper greeted Rei upon seeing him, then disappeared into the back of the store.

Without saying a word, Rei simply watched as things proceeded swiftly.

After all, she had come today specifically to pick up her shoes, so the clerk's attitude wasn't exactly wrong.

Still, he thought, couldn't he have at least listened to what I had to say before acting?

That's what he thought, but he quickly decided it wasn't worth getting angry over and sat down on a chair.

Around him, just like when he'd come a few days ago, rows of shoes were displayed.

(Looking at it like this, it really does feel like I'm at a shoe store...)

As I waited, looking at the shoes around me, the craftsman who was the salesperson's father eventually came out from the back of the store.

To Rei's eyes, his stubborn-looking appearance perfectly embodied the image of a craftsman.

And what the craftsman held in his hands were two separate pairs of shoes.

One pair for everyday wear in town, and another pair with steel plates embedded for when he acted as an adventurer.

"Huh? I thought I ordered ten pairs of each?"

"Ah, right. But all the shoes are made the same as these. If you wear these and they're fine, then they should be fine."

Though blunt, his words clearly conveyed his confidence in his work, something Rei understood perfectly well.

That's precisely why she changed into the new pair without any particular complaint.

"Huh..."

The word that escaped Rei's lips was one of admiration at the comfort of the shoes.

Setting aside Azimov's shoes, which were clearly too big for her feet, these were noticeably more comfortable than the pair she'd bought at this shop before.

Wearing shoes that truly fit his feet like this made him realize just how precious the custom-made Sleipnir shoes prepared for him really were.

(Actually, how did Elena get her pair of Sleipnir shoes? If they don't fit properly, that seems like it would cause all sorts of trouble...)

The question popped into my head, but I decided I could ask later when using the Paired Orb. I walked lightly around the shop, still wearing the shoes.

They must be designed to minimize strain on the feet when walking. The sensation of my feet touching the ground was significantly less than with the shoes I'd been wearing earlier.

"How are they?"

"Hmm, I just put them on, so it's hard to say, but I think they're probably fine."

"...I see. Just in case, if you have any problems wearing those boots, come back. We'll adjust them again. ...Next up is these."

Next, he handed me a pair of boots with steel plates, meant for adventuring.

Naturally, these fit perfectly too, just like the first pair.

I don't know how they made them, but they didn't seem much heavier than the first pair Rei wore.

It should be heavy with steel plates, after all.

Rei tilted his head in puzzlement, but even when he directed a questioning gaze at the craftsman, the man remained as unfriendly as ever, showing no sign of responding.

Deciding it was pointless to ask, Rei concluded these boots were certainly not bad for him. He gave a small nod and spoke.

"Yeah, these are fine too. I can wear them normally."

"...Alright. If you feel any discomfort wearing those, bring them back. I'll readjust them for you."

With just that, he stood up as if his business was done and walked back into the depths of the shop.

Still, satisfied with his work, he offered a faint smile, just the corners of his lips curving slightly beneath his gruff expression—a gesture typical of this craftsman.

"Alright, hold on a moment. I'll bring the rest of the shoes right away."

The shop assistant said this and headed toward the back of the store, as if following his father.

Rei switched to the shoe without the steel plate and checked its fit again.

While he was doing this, the merchant soon returned from the back of the shop with the remaining shoes and handed them to Rei.

"That completes the transaction. If you need anything else, come back."

Nodding at the words, he stored the shoes he'd been given, the first pair with the steel plate, and the shoes he'd been wearing until today into his Misty Ring before leaving the shop.

The price was fairly steep, but Rei felt it was well worth every penny.

"Gurrrrrrr!"

Hearing Set's cry, Rei turned to see Milein feeding him a sandwich.

But even seeing that scene didn't particularly surprise Rei.

After all, it wasn't unusual for residents of Gilm to feed Set, and having Mireine—Johanna would likely object, but—the foremost Seto enthusiast present was hardly a rare occurrence.

(Come to think of it, last winter Marka showed up while Milein was playing with Set too. ...I hope he doesn't come this year too?)

Recalling the person who always said "Noja," Rei glanced around just in case.

Could it be...? That thought prompted his action, but naturally, Marka was nowhere to be seen.

Feeling a mix of relief and subtle loneliness at that realization, he approached Set and Milein.

"Set, Milein."

"...Ah, Rei. You're back already? I wanted to play with Set-chan a little longer."

Milein must have known from Set's cries that Rei was approaching.

But even so, her honest desire was to play with Set for as long as possible.

"Since you're here anyway, wanna come with me for a little walk around town?"

"Huh? Really!? ...Well, I'd love to say yes, but—"

At Rei's words, Milein flashed an expression that clearly conveyed disappointment... the kind that felt genuinely heartfelt.

"Actually, I have plans with Sulunin and Ekril. I can't just leave them hanging to play with Set-chan."

"...Huh."

Milein's words made Rei look slightly surprised.

He'd assumed that, knowing Milein, she'd choose to be with Set over ignoring her plans with Sulunin and the others.

But Milein chose to prioritize Sulunin and the others.

Considering Milein's personality—a Set enthusiast... no, calling her a Set-first advocate would be more fitting—it was surprising enough to make even Rei look astonished.

"What could I do? They said if I was late again because of Set-chan, they'd make me do all sorts of punishments."

"Ah... yeah. I see."

It wasn't a choice she made willingly, but one born out of fear of Sulunin's punishment.

(Well, I guess it's understandable.)

Sulunin usually had a quiet, mature personality, but precisely because of that, angering him must be terrifying.

They say the quieter the person, the scarier they are when angry, and Sulunin was the perfect example of that.

(Besides, since Sulunin understood Mireine's personality, if he were to punish her, it would likely be something related to Set.)

Being in a party with Mireine, Sulunin naturally understood her personality well.

In fact, he probably understood her better than Milein understood herself.

"Well then, Set-chan. Let's play again sometime. That day before was fun, and I'll look for some delicious food to prepare."

"Gurururuu"

Set purred happily at Milein's words.

After petting Set and watching Milein leave reluctantly, Rei started walking through the town with Set.

"Guru?"

Looking at Rei's shoes, Set let out a meow. Rei gently stroked him and spoke.

"These are the shoes I'll wear until Sleipnir's shoes are reinforced. You were wearing different ones just now, right?"

"Guru..."

For Set, who fundamentally didn't wear things like shoes or clothes, Rei's behavior must seem a bit strange.

(Come to think of it, is Set actually naked every day? ...If Milein or Johanna heard that, they'd probably nose-bleed and faint.)

Or maybe his body hair serves as clothing... While pondering such trivial things as he walked down the main street, a pleasant scent suddenly wafted from ahead... An appetite-whetting aroma drifted toward him.

"Whoa, smells good. ...Should we grab a bite?"

"Gurrr!"

At Rei's words, Set happily cleared his throat and headed straight for the food stall.

"...And, well, normally we'd have happily returned to the inn and relaxed in our room, but..."

"I'm terribly sorry, but we've been waiting for a chance to contact you for quite some time, Mr. Rei."

After enjoying a hearty soup with Set, Rei returned to the Dusk Wheat inn... only to be addressed by the person standing before the reception desk.

A man in his thirties, more accurately described as overweight than imposing.

He was taller than Rei, but even so, he could be considered short by the standards of this world.

Though smiling with his mouth, his eyes held no warmth. He addressed Rei in a cheerful tone.

The reason Rei remained quiet in front of such a person was likely the Gamelion cuisine spread across the table.

Stews, grilled dishes, steamed dishes... various foods filled the table.

Of course, Rei hadn't ordered this meal; it was the person sitting across from him who had.

Although Rei had eaten a hearty soup at a street stall, he still had plenty of room left.

Enough, in fact, to finish every single dish before him.

"So, you say you've been waiting for a day when you could contact me?"

"Yes. Actually, I'm Tris, a merchant belonging to the Speer Trading Company."

"...Ah."

Rei had somehow understood that Tris, sitting before him, was a merchant.

Her physique was too flabby to be an adventurer.

There was a chance he might be an envoy for some noble, but that possibility vanished with her last statement.

"So, what does this merchant from Spier Trading Company want with me? I don't have any business with merchants right now."

"Yes, I realize it was rude of me to take up your time, Rei... but I have matters to attend to as well."

Rei directed his gaze at Tris, who spoke with a smile, urging him to continue.

...Though, Rei had a vague idea why the man before him had come.

Or rather, the most likely reason a merchant would go out of his way to visit Rei was undoubtedly the Silver Lion matter.

Not wanting to waste time here, Ray reached for the Gamelion skewers while voicing his guess.

"The Silver Lion matter?"

"Yes. ...What do you say? The Silver Lion material you possess, Rei. Would you consider selling it to us?"

"Unfortunately, I have no intention of doing so at this time."

Rei shook his head immediately at Tris's words.

Several other merchants had come as well, but he had turned them all down.

Given that, he had absolutely no intention of selling the materials to the person standing before him.

"I see. ...Well, I suppose there's nothing to be done. If you change your mind, please let us know."

"Huh?"

Ray had assumed negotiations would begin here, but Tris, having said that, stood up as if his business was done.

Unexpectedly, Rei could only watch Tris walk away in silence.
