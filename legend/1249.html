Byune had returned to the labyrinth city of Exil, but she came back to Gilm the very next day after Rei and the others had left Gilm for the dungeon.

She had managed to resolve the issues that arose in Exil and returned to Gilm... but what awaited her was the news that Vihera had fallen unconscious.

She considered heading to the dungeon herself, but worried she might miss them and judged she'd likely only be a hindrance if she went. So she waited quietly at the Dusk Wheat inn as dusk fell.

And today, Vihera and Elena had returned.

"Oh, Byune. I thought you wouldn't return to Gilm until spring... but you're back much sooner than expected."

"Mm."

Nodding at Vihera's words, Byune looked up at her partner's face, positioned considerably higher than her own.

As always, Byune's expression remained almost unchanged, but even so, Vihera, who had known him for a long time, seemed to understand what Byune wanted to say. She gently stroked Byune's head.

"Seems I caused you worry. I'm fine, you know. See? Nothing's wrong with me, right?"

"Mm..."

Even as his head was stroked, Byune shot Vihera a slightly worried glance.

He noticed her eyes had changed color, now silver instead of their usual hue, but he knew mentioning it would only make Vihera worry about him.

Having known Vihera for so long, she understood her personality well.

Of course, the same was true for Vihera. Because they'd known each other so long, she understood exactly what Byune was thinking.

Though Byune only ever used the word "Hmm," Vihera was the only one who could accurately grasp its meaning.

"Byune, how have you been?"

After their exchange with Vihera concluded, Elena addressed Byune.

For Elena, who adored Byune, his presence helped her forget, at least a little, what Vihera had said to her in the carriage.

Moreover, Byune knew Elena doted on her, so she responded with her usual expressionless nod.

"Mm!"

"I see. You're doing well. Considering the distance from Exil to Gilm, did you come alone?"

"Mm."

She shook her head at Elena's next question.

In truth, Byune had traveled with adventurers heading from Exil to Gilm.

Of course, being adventurers chosen by Bosk Silwa, Byune's guardian, they were a bit rough around the edges but wouldn't harm Byune.

While there had been some confusion in communication, they had still arrived safely in Gilm. In terms of skill, they were certainly no less capable than the adventurers of Gilm.

That adventurer was already heading toward the guild, eagerly searching for any good quests.

They had brought a fair amount of money, but prices differed considerably between Exil and Gilm.

Things that were cheap in Exil were expensive here, and things that were expensive in Exil were cheap here.

Taking that into account, his current funds felt a bit iffy for getting through the winter.

Byune knew this, but he didn't seem particularly concerned as he engaged Elena in conversation.

"Lady Elena, talking like this here might disturb others. Wouldn't it be better to talk inside?"

"Hm? ...Ah, I see. Well then, let's do that. Ara, regarding the carriage..."

"Yes, I'll hand it over to the inn staff, so please don't worry. Rei, I'll take care of Set too."

While Elena spoke with Ara, Rei noticed Byune, who had moved closer to Set, gently stroking his head. He nodded at Ara's words and replied.

"Is that so? If it's okay to leave it to you, then I'll leave it to you."

"No problem. More importantly, at this hour, the dining hall should be getting crowded soon, so we should hurry."

"Hmm!"

It wasn't Rei or Elena who responded quickly to Ara's words, but Byune.

For Byune, who had lived a poor life, meals were incredibly important.

Moreover, the Dusk Wheat inn was known for its delicious food, and the cafeteria was open to non-guests as well.

The thought of possibly missing out on that food was absolutely unacceptable to Byune.

"Hey, wait a minute, Byune. I get it, I get it!"

As if she'd forgotten she'd been petting Set just moments before, Byune pulled Vihera toward the dining hall.

Seeing Byune like this, Rei and Elena couldn't help but smile as they followed behind.

"Huh! Huh!"

"Hey, Byune. You don't have to pull so hard, okay? Look, it's fine. It's not that crowded yet. Hey, Rei, Elena!"

Vihera shot them a pleading look, but Rei and Elena didn't particularly feel like helping and just followed after them.

It was true she'd drunk fruit water in the carriage, but that didn't satisfy her hunger.

Well, maybe if she'd just drunk a huge amount of fruit water, it would be different, but there was a cafeteria right there—why not use it?

"I'm craving Gamelion cuisine. ...Seems the Silver Lion meat situation has finally settled down."

"Hm?"

Hearing Rei's words, Byune turned her gaze toward him with interest.

Being the picky eater that he was, Byune's interest was piqued.

The Silver Lion was fairly well-known as an S-rank monster, especially around this area of Gilm, where it was famed as a dungeon boss monster.

Whether Byune knew this or not, she now looked up at Rei with a hopeful gaze, thinking: If it's a rare dish, I want to try it too!

If it were someone who liked little kids, they might have fallen for Byune's trap instantly. Unfortunately—or perhaps fortunately—Rei wasn't the type to fall for that look.

...Though it probably helped that there wasn't the height difference between Rei and Byune that there was with the others.

"Another time. That dish is dangerous to eat carelessly in certain places."

Dangerous? Byune tilted her head slightly, expressionless. But seeing Elena and Vihera nod in agreement at Rei's words, she couldn't outright dismiss it.

Truthfully, the meat was delicious beyond compare. But precisely because it was so delicious, its flavor lingered in your mouth indefinitely. The downside was that for days afterward, no other dish could satisfy you.

"Well, well, well, well!"

Just as Rei was about to explain the Silver Lion meat, that voice rang out.

Recognizing the voice, Rei turned his gaze in that direction. There, as he had expected, stood the person he anticipated.

It was Lana, the plump proprietress of the Dusk Wheat inn, standing there genuinely delighted and surprised, a smile on her face.

The focus of Lana's gaze was Vihera.

When Vihera had been unconscious, she hadn't been taken to the hospital. Instead, she had been laid to rest in the room she rented at the Dusk Wheat inn.

Because of that, Lana had found time to visit Vihera several times.

For someone like Lana, seeing Vihera conscious like this—no, actually laughing and walking around normally—it was no wonder she was in such a state now.

Vihera seemed to understand why Lana looked so incredibly happy. She too smiled and spoke.

"Seems I've caused you quite a bit of worry. But as you can see, I'm fine now, so please don't worry."

"Oh, oh, oh."

Whether from surprise or joy, that was all the sound that came out of Lana's mouth.

Still wanting to show her joy through her actions, Lana hugged Vihera tightly.

"I'm so glad... I'm so glad for you."

"Yes, thanks to Rei and the others, I'm able to move safely like this now."

Vihera returned Lana's embrace, gently patting her shoulder.

That gesture seemed to further stir Lana's joy. After one last strong hug, she pulled away, her face beaming as she declared.

"Alright, today's on me! Eat your fill and get your strength back!"

"Is that okay?"

Vihera asked this because she wondered if it was okay for just them to receive special treatment.

The dining hall of Dusk Wheat inn was crowded with customers, and quite a few were focused on the exchange that had just taken place at the entrance.

She wondered if it was okay to show favoritism to just them in such a place. But to that Vihera, Lana opened her mouth without hiding her delighted expression.

"Besides, you and Vihera have been loyal patrons of this inn for a very long time. This much is fine."

Rana, who had just moments ago spoken in a tone that could be considered somewhat familiar, returned to her usual manner, though she couldn't quite suppress the smile as she said this.

She was clearly delighted that Vihera had perked up so much.

Moreover, it was an undeniable fact that Rei and Vihera were regulars at the Dusk Wheat inn.

This inn was among the higher-end establishments in Gilm, and as such, its lodging fees were higher than average.

Vihera had been staying at such an inn solely because Rei was there, and Rei had made it his regular lodging for several years since coming to Gilm, partly due to the matter with Set and partly for the meals.

...Furthermore, quite a few people came hoping to see Set because Rei was staying there, and most of those people ended up dining in the restaurant.

The profits Rei and Set bring to this inn are by no means insignificant.

It's precisely because she understands this that Lana is treating them like this. ...Still, the biggest reason is probably that Vihera has recovered.

"Now, now, please take a seat at the table. For drinks, Rei would prefer something other than alcohol, correct?"

Pushed along by Lana, they were guided to an empty spot in the dining hall.

Once seated, she immediately asks for their orders.

"What would you like to eat? Today's special is definitely the Gamelion dish. Unlike last year, it's widely available now, so you can eat as much as you like."

A smile flickered in Lana's eyes as she turned her gaze toward Rei.

The meaning behind it was obvious.

Her gaze told him that the reason last year's Gamelion meat was scarce was because of Rei.

The reason more Gamelion meat was available this year compared to last was likely because the adventurers still remembered last year's incident vividly.

They were undoubtedly working hard to gather as much Gamelion meat as possible.

"Hmm, well then. I'll have the Gamelion stew, please. And a Gamelion meat sandwich."

"Then I'll have the Gamelion sauté and the Gamelion steamed meat."

"Huh! Huh!"

After Vihera and Elena placed their orders, Byune insisted she wanted some too.

Watching her point to one menu item after another, you couldn't help but wonder if her tiny body could actually handle all that food.

But Byune always finishes everything.

Rei also casually orders some Gamelion dishes for Lana.

"Ah, and I think Ara will be here soon too, so just order something for her as well."

"Yes, understood. Please wait a moment."

Lana, smiling happily at the size of the order, cheerfully walked away.

(Is she a good person, or just too nice...? It makes me happy, but is she really okay?)

Watching Lana's retreating figure, Rei felt a little uneasy. Of course, Lana didn't treat everyone like this.

The fact that she'd known Rei for several years, a fairly long time, played a big part.

While she didn't interact much with Elena, Vihera, and the others, her experience as the proprietress of the Dusk Wheat inn gave her confidence in her ability to judge people.

That she judged Rei to be problem-free with that confident eye—was it because Lana was magnanimous, or because her eye for people was simply kinder than most?

But while it was true Rei had his issues, he had never caused trouble for the inn himself.

...Sure, he'd had thugs chasing Rei show up at the inn, or fought an Elk in the backyard, but Lana seemed to find that within acceptable limits.

In any case, Rei waited eagerly with Elena and the others for the Gamelion cuisine to arrive.
