“Keep moving forward! The right flank, keep firing arrows while cutting off the bandits’ retreat! Left flank, there aren’t many of them! Attack them from the side!”

Following Sura’s orders, dozens of wagons move into position.

Seeing this, the bandits realize their escape route has been blocked by the wagons and fall into confusion.

“Hey, they’ve flanked us from behind!”

“Aaaah, arrows! Arrows!”

“Damn it, who the hell suggested we attack such a vicious gang?!”

The bandits’ curses echo through the air as they use their nearby comrades as human shields to block the torrent of arrows raining down from the carriages that have flanked them.

……The bandit used as a shield had tried to complain at first… but once several arrows pierced his face, there was no way he could survive.

“Who cares! Tell the boss that there aren’t any bandits around, even though the area near Sabrusta is the best place to make a killing!”

As arrows rained down from all directions and his comrades fell one after another, one of the surviving bandits shouted... but the next moment, his face twisted in horror as he spotted a group of carriages charging straight toward them.

“Those guys are charging us! Everyone, scatter!”

Just as the bandit had shouted, the carriages—dozens of them—charged straight toward the bandits.

The bandit’s instinctive shout was by no means wrong. It wasn’t wrong, but… if the bandits were to scatter and flee at that moment, there was no doubt they would be allowing themselves to be trampled underfoot—a process known as “picking them off one by one.”

Precisely because they knew this, the bandits hesitated over what to do… and as a result of that hesitation, they allowed the carriages to charge through, leading to their annihilation.

Even those who managed to escape were killed one after another by arrows fired from the carriages… It didn’t take long for the bandits—who had recklessly attacked simply because nearly the entire group consisted of women—to be wiped out.

“So, what was the damage?”

“There’s no way there’s any, really.”

At the words of Sura, the leader of this group, the female wolf beastman replied in exasperation, and the others nodded in agreement.

After the “battle” with the bandits—which was really just a massacre—the key members of this group had now gathered here.

All around them, the bandits’ corpses were being gathered into a single pile and burned to prevent them from turning into undead, while others were stripping them of weapons, armor, and any valuables.

Normally, they would have followed the example of Rei, who had saved them, by extracting the location of the bandits’ hideout from them and then raiding it… but since the surviving bandits had revealed that these bandits had apparently just arrived today and their hideout did not yet exist, that step was skipped.

To the bandits, the sight of over a hundred carriages, the horses pulling them, and the fact that nearly the entire group consisted of women with striking features made this a truly mouth-watering target. They attacked Sura and her group in an attempt to loudly announce their arrival near Sabrusta—the bandits’ most lucrative hunting ground—but the result was the complete annihilation of the bandit gang.

The attacking bandits likely never imagined it.

They surely never imagined that nearly everyone aboard these more than a hundred carriages was combat-ready.

Even among the combatants, few were capable of close-quarters combat with weapons like longswords or spears.

In fact, that applies only to the adventurers hired as escorts and a small number of the women.

On the other hand... nearly all of the women knew how to use a bow.

Since they had only begun practicing archery partway through this journey, their skill was by no means high.

In fact, there were quite a few who could barely shoot an arrow straight ahead.

But… if they can shoot an arrow forward… and if several, a dozen, or even dozens of them shoot their arrows forward in unison… it would be no exaggeration to call it a veritable rain of arrows.

As a result, the hail of arrows fired as a preemptive strike completely threw the bandits into confusion… and in the end, they were unable to put up a proper fight and were subjected to a one-sided attack.

“Now, according to the information I heard at the place we stopped by a little while ago, this area was supposed to be safe… what do you think?”

Saying this, Sura looked around at the others present.

Sharia, a wolf beastman who had been abducted by Jaya and forced into prostitution in Mejogo, but whose slave collar had come off for some reason; later, with Rei’s mediation, she joined the Resistance and became one of its most formidable fighters.

Gimekara of the Zolge Trading Company, who provided financial support to this group and supplied them with their greatest weapons—bows and arrows, wagons, and escorting adventurers—as well as various other necessities.

Rocks, an adventurer affiliated with Gilm, who leads the group of guards hired along the way; it would be no exaggeration to call him the core of their fighting force.

And then there is Sura, who, having led the Resistance in Mejogo, naturally became the leader of this group as well.

There are a few other fairly important figures as well, but for now, the people present here are the ones who should be called the core members of this group.

“That’s right. From what I know, there were quite a few bandits around Sabrusta.”

As an adventurer affiliated with Gilm, Rocks was quite knowledgeable about the local situation.

Hearing Rox’s words, everyone wore expressions that seemed to say, “I knew it.”

That said, there was no trace of despair or resignation in their expressions.

……That was only natural. After all, they had just managed to wipe out the bandits who had attacked them without a single casualty.

“So, what should we do? Should we hurry a bit more to get to Sabrusta?”

At Sharia’s words, Sura frowned in thought.

“Since there were bandits near Sabrusta, there’s no guarantee there aren’t others lurking nearby, right? If we rush, we’ll inevitably let our guard down… Why not just keep doing what we’ve been doing?”

“I agree. There aren’t any branches of the Zolge Trading Company left in this area. However, since there are other trading companies we have connections with, I don’t think we’ll be completely unable to obtain any information…”

“There’s no certainty,” she concludes.

The fact that they have connections simply means they’re a different guild from the Zolge Trading Company.

In that case, it’s only natural that they would prioritize their own guild’s interests over those of the Zolge Trading Company… and depending on the situation, there’s no guarantee they wouldn’t try to set a trap for the Zolge Trading Company.

That’s precisely why we can’t trust this information as completely as we would information from a Zolge Trading Company branch.

“I’ve heard there are plenty of people in Sabrusta with ties to bandits, too. …Would it be worth having the Guild look into the situation a bit?”

At Rox’s words, Gimekara nodded, though with a hint of hesitation.

“That’s true. Since we’re getting closer to the frontier, the Guild’s influence should naturally be growing stronger. …Well, though, it’s not as if there aren’t people within the Guild who have ties to bandits either…”

Though Gimekara’s words were vague, deep down he was certain that several people were indeed connected to the bandits.

That said, the more information you have, the more reliable it becomes.

In that sense, gathering information from multiple sources is by no means a mistake.

Given that, there was no reason for Gimekara to object to Rox’s words.

“Anyway, Rei should be waiting for us once we arrive in Gilm. Until then, we’ll just have to do our best.”

At Sura’s words, everyone present nodded in agreement.

Just then, a woman appeared.

She wasn’t exactly an enemy… nor was she a stranger to Sura.

“Sura, I’ve finished looting. The bodies have been disposed of, so we can leave right away.”

“Right. Well then… let’s move on. For now, we just need to get as close to Gilm as possible. Though, once we pass this point, we won’t have to worry about bandits anymore, will we?”

Sura’s gaze turns to Rocks, and the man she’s looking at opens his mouth with a slightly troubled expression.

“I’m not so sure. Honestly, before the expansion work began, I could have nodded with confidence. But now that so many merchants are passing through, there’s no guarantee there aren’t bandits targeting them. Especially at this time of year, there are many merchants who come in a hurry and leave in a hurry. Since those merchants often don’t hire escorts, they make easy prey for bandits.”

To Rocks, traveling through the frontier without an escort seemed like nothing short of suicide.

It seems impossible, but... merchants who want to spend as little money as possible, or those who’ve spent too much on purchasing goods to begin with and can’t afford to hire guards, aren’t uncommon at this time of year.

For that reason, it’s not impossible that there are those who venture deep into the frontier hoping to strike it rich.

That said, thanks to all this training, this group has become something more like a “carriage cavalry” than a “mounted archery unit.”

And with this many people, there are naturally former hunters and adventurers among them, as well as a fair number of people who, though they’d never used a bow before, actually possess a talent for archery.

Given that, Rox predicted that even if there were bandits beyond Sabrusta, it would likely end up as a one-sided massacre, just like this time.

“Anyway, we need to hurry now…”

...we should keep moving.

Just as Sura was about to say that, Gimekara spoke up.

“Wait a moment. It seems the best choice right now is not to leave immediately, but to rest here for a bit.”

Gimekara said this abruptly, but his gaze was fixed on the sky.

And the fact that this group was looking up at the sky and saying such a thing implied a certain reality.

Realizing this, Sura and the others reflexively turned their gaze toward the sky.

And there, just as expected... was Set, flapping his wings.

Since Set wasn’t carrying his Set Cage today—perhaps because he’d come alone with Rei—they could clearly make out his figure even from the ground.

“It seems Gimekara’s choice was the right one after all.”

Everyone nodded at Sura’s words.

Naturally, with the leaders of this group looking up at the sky like that, the others followed suit and looked up as well.

Under those circumstances, it wasn’t hard for word to spread that Rei and Set had arrived.

And… this group included quite a few Set fans, and cheers erupted at the sight of Set, whom they hadn’t seen in a long time.

“Eeek! It’s Set-chan! Set-chan!”

“Set-chan, over here! Come on, come over here!”

“We’ve got some delicious jerky over here! Homemade jerky we made ourselves!”

Each of them tried to get Set’s attention, beckoning him to come their way… but Set, as expected, landed right next to Sura and the others.

It was because Rei had asked him to, but the women could only feel disappointed about it.

That said, since they knew Set adored Rei, they weren’t exactly devastated.

...On the contrary, the women were racking their brains, wondering if there was any reason they could use to go over to where Sura and the others were.

“Is there some kind of commotion going on?”

Rei, having dismounted from Set, asked this while vaguely imagining what kind of commotion it might be.

“Yes. Bandits. …I heard that the adventurers, including a certain someone, had already taken care of most of the bandits around here…”

As she said this, a hint of reproach flickered in Sura’s eyes.

Still, the fact that it was only slightly reproachful was likely because she understood that some time had passed since Rei and the others had defeated the bandits here.

If it had been right after they’d defeated them, that might be one thing, but quite some time has passed now.

Sura understood that blaming them for bandits appearing under those circumstances would be nothing but selfish.

Besides, defeating bandits brings benefits in various ways.

First, there’s the loot from the defeated bandits… though, since this group primarily relies on bow attacks, any melee weapons they acquire would likely only be useful enough to sell.

Searching the bandits’ hideout… Some bandits have valuables, and there might be captives there.

Selling captured bandits as slaves… since most of this group were women, this carried various risks, so it was limited to cases where there was a town or village nearby.

...As a result, the cost of the arrows used ends up being higher... but it was a fact that groups trained by Rei—famous for being Bandit Eaters—were gradually beginning to emerge.
