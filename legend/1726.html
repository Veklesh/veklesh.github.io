Since there was a ceiling, the actual speed of the fall wasn’t that great, but even so, there was the power of the Brace of Might, the Power Crush skill, and above all, Set’s innate strength as a creature over three meters tall.

The combined force of these three elements shattered Garangiga’s head… and the headless body collapsed to the ground.

Seeing this, everyone was about to breathe a sigh of relief... but just then, despite having lost its head, the Galangiga moved its massive body.

“Damn it!?”

The first to react was Rei.

Whether by chance or due to the vengeful spirit of the headless Garangiga, he swung his Death Scythe as he watched the colossal form—spikes sprouting from its bristling scales—advance straight toward him.

“Power Slash!”

Prioritizing the sheer power of the strike over cutting power, the blow didn’t quite blow the Galangiga’s body—which had been closing in on Rei—away… but it did succeed in deflecting it.

After sending the Garangiga’s body flying, he hurriedly put some distance between himself and it.

When he glanced at the others, they had fortunately seen the movement with which the Garangiga had just attacked Rei and judged that even a corpse was dangerous. The members in the vanguard hurriedly put distance between themselves and the Garangiga’s corpse.

(Wait, seeing it moving around so energetically like this, is it really fair to call it a corpse?)

They kept their distance from Garangiga’s rampaging body, watching warily to see if it would attack them again.

However, the Galangiga’s body made no move to attack Rei and the others from a distance; it simply continued to thrash about in place.

“...So the Garangiga no longer has any will of its own and is just rampaging mindlessly. Is that the effect of losing its head?”

Relieu, who had sustained a faint cut on his cheek—likely from the battle—muttered to himself.

The fact that she had sustained only such a minor injury while facing a monster believed to be Rank A-equivalent was a testament to Relieu’s skill.

That said, in that sense, Rei and the others had also sustained only minor injuries, such as scrapes and light bruises.

The ground had been shattered by Garangiga rampaging with its massive body, and many of their injuries were caused by small stones and debris scattered in the process.

But fundamentally, Garangiga’s attacks were extremely powerful; had they failed to dodge something like a Poison Water Cutter, they would have sustained damage that could be called fatal.

With a poisoned Water Cutter, there is no option to take moderate damage; it’s either completely dodge it or suffer a fatal wound.

Even if you managed to dodge it by the skin of your teeth, given the potency of the poison that melts rocks and the ground, it was certain you would be hit head-on by the spray.

In that sense, it was only natural that there were only a few people with minor scrapes and bruises.

“I’ve heard that certain animals have such strong vitality that it’s not uncommon for their bodies to keep moving even after losing their heads—I suppose this is similar to that.”

As he said this, Rei recalled a TV program he’d seen back in Japan.

It was a show about carp cuisine, where the fish was still alive even though its entire body had been cooked... a scene that, by any normal standard, would be utterly impossible.

But having actually seen it, Rei found himself convinced by his own words.

Especially in the case of this Garangiga—such a massive snake, likely a Rank A-class monster.

Naturally, it wouldn’t be the least bit surprising if its body still harbored such strong vitality.

“Ah, I’ve heard of that. …But how long are we supposed to keep this up?”

Relieu gazed at the thrashing body of the Garangiga with a look that was part exasperation.

It had been several minutes since Set had crushed its head.

Despite that, the Rangigiga’s body shows no signs of calming down.

Given that, there was no way they could simply stow the body away in the Misty Ring, nor could they do anything about the dungeon’s core at the bottom of the subterranean lake.

(Well, I suppose we could ride Set and approach from above, or take a wide detour to reach the dungeon’s core, but if that ends up stimulating it in some strange way and causing trouble, that’d be a hassle.)

Since I don’t know what effect the dungeon’s core has on the boss monster, I want to avoid carelessly provoking it here.

That was Rei’s honest feeling.

“Grr!”

Set approached Rei’s side, letting out a happy little cry.

It was hard to believe this was the same creature that had just crushed Garangiga’s head with a single blow, scattering flesh and bone fragments all around—it was rubbing its head against Rei’s, begging to be praised.

Rei stroked him firmly to praise him... Just as the two of them were engaging in this physical affection, Relieu spoke up.

“Hey, um… during that fight with the Garangiga just now, if I’m not mistaken, it looked like Set got smaller.”

“Yeah, you’re right. He did get smaller. But the fact that Set can use skills is nothing new, is it? I don’t think it’s really that surprising.”

Rei said that, but unlike skills like Fire Breath or Wind Arrow that are immediately obvious, the ability to change one’s body size is a very rare skill.

Rei could understand why Relieu was surprised by that.

Of course, Relieu understood what Rei was getting at… but the reason he felt compelled to point it out to Rei was likely because of the significant fact that Set had shrunk.

Everyone present knew that Relieu was one of Set’s biggest fans.

She had initially tried to hide it, but with Set right there, there was no way she could keep it a secret.

In the end, she had even come to accept it.

For someone like Relieu, the fact that Set’s body was shrinking held great significance.

Of course, if you asked her whether the current three-meter-tall Set isn’t adorable, she’d deny it immediately.

But at the same time, it goes without saying that Set looks even more adorable when he’s small, and as a Set enthusiast, it was only natural for him to want to see Set like that.

……Furthermore, as a Set enthusiast, Relieu was the first to learn that Set possessed the ability to shrink his body and to actually witness it with her own eyes.

In fact, as a Set enthusiast, she had learned of and witnessed this before even Milein and Johanna—the so-called “Big Two”—who were renowned in Gilm… If word of this got out, it wouldn’t be surprising if a rain of blood fell.

That said, since Relieu is a Rank A adventurer with a nickname and possesses strength that far surpasses that of Milein and Johanna, it’s not as though a bloodbath would happen so easily… yet, whenever Set is involved, one cannot say it’s entirely impossible—and that was the astonishing… and terrifying aspect of Milein and Johanna.

(Well, I don’t know to what extent Relieu himself actually believes that, though.)

As he thought this, Rei noticed the rampaging Garangiga moving from a spot where terrain manipulation had created a step, so he used terrain manipulation again to return it to the original ground.

He could have left it as is, but the uneven ground could have caused someone in Rei’s group to get injured, and it might have gotten in the way of their movement, so this was the best course of action.

“Come to think of it, I heard you used earth magic to help with the expansion work on Gilm. Was that what you just did?”

“Yeah.”

“...So you can use earth and wind magic too.”

He muttered thoughtfully—after all, Rei was famous for his fiery tornadoes.

Even among those somewhat well-informed, while it’s common knowledge that Rei uses a wind magic called “Flying Slash,” the fact that he uses earth magic is almost certainly unknown.

That’s likely why Relieu was so surprised when Rei suddenly used that earth magic, and why it reminded him of the Gilm incident.

Of course, from Relieu’s perspective, she probably never imagined that the same magic used for Gilm’s expansion project would be employed in combat like this.

“Well, it’s just so-so, I guess.”

“No, honestly, calling it ‘pretty decent’ after using magic like that is a bit much. When facing a large group, even at that height, if walls suddenly appear or pitfalls open up, it’d be a huge hassle, wouldn’t it?”

What Relieu was murmuring to himself about was likely war.

In a war where tens of thousands—or even more—soldiers are mobilized, what would happen if Rei used the same earth magic he used against Garangiga?

Precisely because he could imagine it, he felt a sense of threat in Rei’s power.

That said, when it comes to threatening power, Relieu—a Rank A adventurer with a nickname—would be treated in much the same way.

In Elgin, where it is not uncommon for quality to surpass quantity, Relieu possesses power that is certainly formidable.

“Hey, Rei. Can you do something about the water in the subterranean lake with that earth magic you used earlier?”

Vihera was also familiar with beast magic, but she likely asked that because Relieu and Byune were present.

“It’s not that simple. …That’s an underground lake of that size, you know? It’d be tough to handle with magic. Actually, wouldn’t Marina’s spirit magic be easier to use?”

“Really? Like, to drain the water from the underground lake out…”

“Definitely this land… land? Well, I think the underground lake is actually bigger than the land.”

As Rei spoke, looking around the space, Vihera simply replied, “I see,” and said nothing more.

Vihera probably said that knowing full well it was a long shot. She was just hoping it might work out, if it did.

“Marina, what do you think?”

“I don’t know what to say to that. …I can’t exactly go around messing with the water in an underground lake, can I?”

“Then, what about dealing with the water all the way to where the dungeon’s core is?”

“I don’t think it’s impossible.”

“Then, I’ll ask you to do it right away. …It looks like Galangiga’s body has finally stopped moving.”

Just as Rei had said, Galangiga’s torso had finally stopped moving.

Although the Galangiga’s torso had been rampaging for quite some time after losing its head, it was likely unable to keep moving for that long in such a state.

Feeling relieved by this, Rei and the others headed toward the subterranean lake.

Naturally, they didn’t forget to stow the Galangiga’s torso in the Misty Ring along the way.

(I can’t tell Set this, but that third eye on its forehead… I bet it was some pretty valuable material.)

Monster eyeballs have always been in demand as materials.

For that reason alone, the eyeball possessed by what appeared to be a rare or high-tier variety of Garangiga was undoubtedly a rare material.

And considering that it’s material equivalent to a Rank A monster…

With these thoughts in mind, I moved on and arrived at the edge of the underground lake.

With Galangiga—the lord of the underground lake and this dungeon’s boss monster—gone, it wouldn’t be surprising if a major commotion broke out in the lake as time went on.

That said, if you were to ask whether that would have any bearing on Rei and the others, it’s a bit of a gray area.

“Marina, please.”

At Rei’s words, Marina casts a water spirit spell.

In the blink of an eye, the water recedes from the area around the dungeon’s core, revealing a clear path.

“...Rei, please hurry a bit. Maybe because this is where the dungeon’s core is protected, the resistance is pretty fierce.”

Rei nodded at Marina’s words and, riding on Set’s back, began racing toward the dungeon’s core.

The path, created by moving the water with spirit magic, led straight toward the dungeon’s core.

“Got it. Just hold on a little longer.”

Calling out to Marina, who was using spirit magic behind him, Rei surveyed the surroundings from atop Set’s back.

The scenery blurred by Set’s speed, but even so, Rei’s eyes could make out the underwater scene.

(There used to be places like this aquarium in Japan, didn’t there?)

He even had the leisure to think such thoughts.

Even as he thought this, Set kept running... and eventually, the pedestal where the dungeon’s core was installed came into view.

Rei didn’t know if the dungeon core possessed any kind of will, but since it was right there before his eyes now, he figured there was no problem… And just like that, the dungeon core drew closer before his eyes…
