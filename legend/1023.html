Rei looked surprised at the words coming from Marina's mouth.

He knew that since Marina was a Dark Elf, she naturally had a homeland.

But Rei never imagined he'd be invited there, and the fact that this homeland contained a tree called the World Tree also caught him completely off guard.

"Except for a few rare exceptions, basically no one outside the elf race is allowed into the forest... or so I read in a book once."

Surprised by Marina's words, he reached for the sandwich filled with meat.

The sandwich, filled with meat grilled and then coated in sauce, was clearly too heavy a dish for a simple tea snack.

Leafy greens were sandwiched between the meat and bread. In his mouth, the soft texture of the bread, the juices overflowing from the meat, the rich umami of the sauce, and the crisp bite of the fresh vegetables blended together perfectly, delighting Rei.

There were other vegetable-based sandwiches too, and even some unusual ones like dried fish flakes mixed with sauce. Each one was incredibly delicious.

Savoring such a sandwich, Rei turned her gaze toward Marina.

Marina took a sip of her tea before nodding at Rei's gaze directed at her while she ate.

"Yes, that's not wrong. Whether elves or dark elves, their individual abilities surpass ordinary humans, but they're vastly outnumbered. So then... you understand, right?"

Having read about such matters, Rei naturally understood.

Elves and Dark Elves fundamentally possess greater abilities than humans. And above all, an overwhelming majority possess exceptionally beautiful features.

Indeed, Marina was a beauty described as peerless, and while Audban's allure differed in nature from Marina's, she was undeniably well-featured.

Because elves and dark elves are few in number, their rarity is high.

So what happens?

They fetch high prices as slaves, so slave traders wouldn't overlook them.

Not the properly licensed slave traders, but those known as the dark or underground slave traders.

History records instances where nobles, dazzled by an elf's beauty, sent troops from their own lands to attack the forest.

Most such nobles suffered heavy losses at the hands of the elves and retreated, but even so, it was not uncommon for elves to be captured.

This has led to the unfortunate consequence that many elves and dark elves harbor resentment toward humans.

That said, there are still quite a few who harbor no hostility toward humans, like Marina or Feilma, who was with Rei during his rank-up exam.

Regardless, the reason Elves and Dark Elves attacked by slave traders and noble lords could stand against such overwhelming numbers lay in their individual prowess and, above all, the barrier enveloping the forests where Elves dwell.

The source of that barrier is the World Tree, which could be called the lifeline of elves and dark elves.

"World Tree healing... I'm not exactly a doctor, you know?"

As he told Marina this, Rei suddenly recalled the profession of arborist he'd seen on TV back in Japan.

Tree doctors, as the name suggests, were people who treated plants... mainly trees.

But of course, Rei didn't possess the botanical knowledge required to be one.

Having lived in the Japanese countryside, he could identify wild mountain vegetables, but that certainly didn't mean he could treat the World Tree.

His parents were farmers, and he had helped them out, but even that was just doing what he was told—he didn't possess any clear knowledge.

(If the World Tree were some kind of wild vegetable... No, wait, I don't even understand Elgin's wild vegetables, so that's impossible anyway.)

Marina and Audban both nodded in agreement at Rei's obvious statement, and Marina spoke up.

"Of course, what we're asking isn't what you're thinking, Rei. Well, to put it simply, we want you to infuse the World Tree with your magic. It seems the cause of its weakness is a lack of magic power, so if we give it some of yours for now, it should be fine for a while. During that time, we want you to figure out the cause of its illness."

"Infuse it with magic? And that will cure the World Tree? It's called the World Tree, after all—it must be some kind of special tree, right?"

Back in Japan, whenever the World Tree appeared in games, manga, or novels, it was almost always depicted as a gigantic tree, almost like a sacred tree.

For Rei, the term "World Tree" conjured an image so immense that even looking up, one couldn't see its top.

Hearing Rei's words, filled with that very image, Marina smiled radiantly and spoke.

"Indeed. The World Tree... well, to be precise, it's more accurate to say that elves settled where the World Tree exists. There's always one in the forests of the elf tribes—elves, dark elves, high elves. Part of why the elf tribes manage the forests is for the sake of the World Tree. And besides..."

"Lady Marina."

Audban murmured, cutting Marina off.

His tone was sharp, utterly unlike the man who had been joking moments before.

To Audban, Marina's words must have been something he couldn't let pass.

Marina tried to tell him that Rei and the others would be fine, but Audban had only met them today... just moments ago.

Given that, it was only natural Audban couldn't trust Rei as much as Marina did.

"Right. I may have gone a bit too far. ...Anyway, our request for Rei... specifically, the request for the adventurer Rei and Vihera , Byune is as I said earlier."

"Huh? Wait a minute, Marina-sama. The one channeling magic into the World Tree is Elena over there, right? So why are you requesting others?"

"...Listen. Were you even paying attention to what I said? And it's not Elena-san, it's Elena-sama. At least while you're here, you'll address her that way. Anyway, Elena-sama isn't an adventurer. So even if we tried to request her, it wouldn't make any sense."

"So... did we come here for nothing?"

"Not at all. With Rei here, we have no shortage of magic power—neither in quality nor quantity—compared to having Elena-sama."

"Huh? What?"

Audban was completely lost in the conversation, but Marina didn't blame him.

Even Marina, who possessed the ability to sense magic, couldn't detect any of Rei's immense magic power when he wore the New Moon Ring.

"Rei, could you do me a favor?"

Marina turned her moist eyes toward Rei, evidence speaking louder than words.

Faced with Marina's gaze, Rei felt no particular need to refuse and nodded in response.

"Audban. Watch Rei closely. Okay? I won't tell you not to be surprised. But don't faint or anything."

"...Huh?"

He couldn't understand what she was saying.

Audban directed that look toward both Rei and Marina.

The magic power emanating from Rei wasn't exactly low, but it certainly wasn't high either.

Compared to Elena's, it was clearly less. In fact, it was so small it wasn't even worth comparing.

What on earth was she talking about in this situation? Wondering this, yet unable to believe Marina would say something meaningless, he kept his gaze fixed on Rei.

Feeling Audban's gaze, Rei saw Marina nod and gently slipped off the new moon ring that had been on his finger.

In that instant...

"Hii!"

Audban had thought feeling Elena's magic earlier was the peak of surprise, but the magic he sensed from Rei now far surpassed even that.

In fact, it was incomparable in the opposite sense from before.

Unconsciously taking a few steps back, Audban was finally snapped back to reality when Marina, seated at her desk chair, grabbed his hand.

He then sensed Rei's magic anew.

It gave the impression of a vast, endless blue sky stretching out in every direction. Or, though he'd only heard it in stories, the image of an endless ocean spread out before him seemed a fitting description for the magic Rei possessed.

"M-My Lady Marina... What exactly is this?"

Audban asked timidly, but Marina decided it was enough and nodded toward Rei.

At that nod, Rei slipped the new moon ring back onto her finger.

Instantly, the overwhelming magic that had been assaulting Audban vanished. He felt relief, yet at the same time, he fixed a look of awe upon Rei.

"Who... who are you? It's unbelievable that a human could possess such power. Such... such power..."

"Calm down. Rei isn't going to attack you. Here, take a deep breath."

At Marina's words, Audban took a deep breath in and out, in and out.

After repeating this several times, she finally seemed to calm down. She turned her gaze back toward Rei.

"That ring... could it be..."

"Yeah, probably as you guessed—it's a relic from the ancient magical civilization."

"An artifact..."

As Audban muttered in stunned silence, Marina offered a mischievous smile and spoke.

"Understand? With Rei here, there's absolutely no need to worry about the World Tree's magic supply. Well, actually, with Lady Elena here, there's no need to worry at all... So, what do you say? Will you come with us?"

To Marina, Elena was a talent far too valuable to let slip away here.

Her magic power was exceptionally high, and her personal skill was undoubtedly top-tier, with an extra "super" attached. Yet, unlike most high-rank adventurers, she also possessed a common-sense side.

While her title meant nothing in Marina's home forest, the fact that she was a symbol of the noble faction, nicknamed the Princess General, would undoubtedly prove effective should anything happen before they reached their destination.

While that ulterior motive was present, Marina also understood, from her own experience as a woman, that Elena would likely be saddened if she couldn't be with Rei.

"Well, before you ask me that, I haven't actually decided to accept that request yet."

Rei interjected, noticing the conversation had somehow progressed on the assumption he would accept the request.

Marina turned a seductive gaze toward Rei and spoke.

"Oh? You won't accept? The reward for this request is something you'd probably be very pleased with, Rei."

"...I'd be delighted?"

To be honest, Rei was inclined to accept Marina's request.

Rei was someone close to him, and he had no intention of refusing unless it was an utterly unreasonable request.

Still, Rei had spoken spitefully because the conversation had been proceeding as if he would accept the request without any real choice.

But Marina continued speaking as if absolutely certain that once Rei heard her request, he would accept it.

"Yes. As I mentioned earlier, what I want to ask of Rei is for the World Tree to channel its magic into healing. As payment, I intend to offer you branches and leaves from the World Tree, its sap, and several pieces of amber."

Rei couldn't grasp just how precious those things were.

Still, he grasped that since they were all parts of the tree called the World Tree, they must be extremely rare items in alchemy.

(Amber... wasn't that fossilized sap? ...But the World Tree still exists, so how does it turn into amber? Well, I guess it makes sense in a world where magic is commonplace.)

He wasn't exactly an expert on gems, but he did recall seeing amber with a mosquito trapped inside on TV back in Japan.

(World Tree amber must be incredibly valuable, and surely a top-tier material. Even branches would be coveted by wizards for wands. Leaves and sap might be used for more potent potions?)

I ponder all this, but ultimately, all I can grasp is that it's an extremely rare material.

"What do you think? I reckon there's a lot for Rei to gain here. Plus, with the World Tree's illness, all sorts of monsters seem to be gathering in the forest. For someone like Rei who collects magic stones as a hobby, you can't just leave it be, can you?"

Hearing the word "magic stones," Rei, already drawn to the World Tree's materials, made his decision.

"Alright, I'll take the job. ...What about Elena and Vihera? Since I'm the one accepting this, I don't expect you to tag along..."

Rei was about to say that, but Elena spoke first.

"I came here to see Rei in the first place. If Rei is going, then naturally I'll go with him. Fortunately, my magic seems useful for the World Tree matter too."

"Rei's going, and Elena's going, right? Then of course I'm going too."

"If Lady Elena is going, then naturally I shall accompany you as well."

"Mm."

Everyone nodded... And so, Rei accepted Marina's request, accompanied by Elena, Vihera, Ara , and Byune.
