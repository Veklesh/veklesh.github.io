The party name "Scarlet Wings" that Rei suggested was registered without any particular issues.

Occasionally... truly only occasionally, a party name might be rejected because it already existed, but fortunately, it seemed no other party in Gilm bore the name Crimson Wings.

Of course, if you searched the entire Kingdom of Mireana... or indeed the whole of Elgin, you couldn't guarantee the same party name didn't exist. But fundamentally, as long as you weren't in the same base, having the same party name wasn't a problem.

"Then... with this, Rei and your party, Crimson Wings, is officially formed. Furthermore, based on your respective ranks and past achievements, Crimson Wings is classified as a Rank B party."

At Lenora's announcement, it wasn't just Rei who wore a look of surprise—Vihera and Byune shared the same expression.

Only Marina, likely having anticipated this outcome as Guildmaster, showed no surprise, instead wearing a smile.

"Is that okay? When you first become an adventurer, no matter how strong you are, your rank stays low."

"Yes. It's only natural there's a difference in treatment between someone who just became an adventurer and someone who's accumulated numerous achievements since becoming one."

"Besides, with a nickname like yours, Rei, you're hardly ordinary. And Vihera has done quite a lot in the Labyrinth City too. And... well, Marina, need I say more?"

"Hmph!"

Kenny continued Lenora's explanation, prompting Byune—whose name hadn't been mentioned—to voice a slight complaint.

"Well, Byune-chan... she's adorable, but when you think of her as an individual adventurer, her achievements and skill level are... you know?"

"Could you not pass the buck to me? ...Byune's prowess as a thief is, for better or worse, merely standard. Of course, having that level of skill at her age is impressive, but the guild ultimately prioritizes current ability."

Hearing that, Byune couldn't voice any further complaints.

Within this party, Byune understood better than anyone that her own skills were decisively lacking.

"Hmm..."

Though her expression remained unchanged, Byune muttered a single word, sounding disappointed.

Seeing Byune like this, Lenora shot Kenny a sharp glance before changing the subject.

"Anyway, that completes the formation of Crimson Wings. ...So, what do we do now? If we can take on a request right away, that would be helpful for us too."

It was already spring.

During winter, few venture outside Gilm unless there's a special reason—like needing money, specific materials, or particular ingredients.

But that also meant monster hunting was practically nonexistent during winter.

Various other reasons existed too, resulting in a massive... truly enormous number of request forms being posted on the guild's request board the moment spring arrived and adventurers began their activities.

Even now, many adventurers stood before the board, eagerly scanning the requests for something worthwhile.

Kenny, who had interrupted Rei and Lenora's conversation just moments ago, was now accepting request forms brought in by adventurers.

Rei and the others had only just formed their party, but they were a group of skilled individuals.

In terms of pure combat skill alone, they had three individuals who were among the very best in Gilm, plus they had Set, an exceptionally excellent means of transportation.

Considering all that, she hoped they would take on some kind of request.

As the guild receptionist, it was only natural for Lenora to feel that way.

But Rei shook his head at Lenora.

"Sorry, but we have somewhere to go before taking on any requests."

"Somewhere to go? Well, I suppose I can't insist then..."

"Well, I'll take care of my business first. If I still have time afterward, I'll stop by the guild again."

"I see. Understood."

At Rei's words, Lenora looked slightly disappointed but said nothing more.

As a receptionist, it was only natural she couldn't force anything on an adventurer.

"Well then, see you later."

Rei said, and Vihera and Marina also smiled as they bid farewell. Only Byune murmured briefly before leaving the spot.

...Just before exiting the guild, Marina glanced back at it one last time.

The place where she had worked as guildmaster until today... and the place she would now use alongside Rei as an adventurer.

Surveying the guild interior, she realized this anew.

She had no regrets whatsoever about having worked as Gilm's guildmaster for so long.

No, quite the opposite—it had been deeply fulfilling.

Of course, being in the remote town of Gilm and dealing with skilled adventurers meant there were various hardships.

...Though, the greatest hardship in recent years had been dealing with Rei himself.

But Marina taking over the guild's management with her strong leadership wasn't all good news.

Because Marina is here, everything will be fine.

It was true that a small but growing number of guild staff held that belief.

If things continued like this, regardless of the present, it would certainly not be good for the guild in the future... especially if she ever needed to step down as guildmaster for some reason.

Marina's decision to step down as Guild Master stemmed not only from her desire to live with Rei, but also from this very reason.

Even so, Marina knew that the moment she stepped away from this place, she would cease to be the guild master entirely. Thinking that brought a profound sense of emotion.

「Marina？」

Lost in these thoughts, Marina turned at the sound of Rei's voice right behind her.

And seeing Rei's face... realizing she would now live her life alongside him, a smile naturally spread across her own.

"No, it's nothing. ...Well then, shall we go?"

Still smiling, Marina left the guild alongside Rei.

All the guild staff bowed deeply to see Marina off.

...Even Kenny, who envied Marina for forming a party with Rei, refrained from showing any envy or jealousy just this once. He bowed respectfully to Marina, who had led the guild for so long.

"Gururu?"

Waiting for Rei and the others as they emerged from the guild was Set, who had been receiving attention from several adventurers and residents.

Noticing Marina seemed different than usual, Set purred, "Are you okay?"

Those who had been paying attention to Set quietly moved away, seemingly unwilling to disturb him.

"Yes, I'm fine. I've just been feeling a bit sentimental after everything that happened."

Seeing Marina gently stroke her own body, Set must have decided there was no particular problem. He narrowed his eyes contentedly, purring for more strokes.

"Hehe, Set's still as adorable as ever. ...So, Rei. You're heading to Azimov's place now, right?"

Marina asked while stroking Set, and Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah. Seems the Sleipnir Boots are finally done. ...Well, actually, they were finished a few days ago, but it took a few more days to thoroughly check them."

"...Well, Azimov does seem quite particular about alchemy, after all."

Marina had known about Azimov before—a skilled individual... though one could also call him an eccentric.

She had met him and spoken during the Ambris incident, gaining some understanding of his character... but now she was seeing his eccentricity firsthand, even more pronounced than the rumors suggested.

Though perhaps it was more accurate to say he tended to run wild because he'd turned his hobby into his job.

In any case, Azimov was intensely particular about his work.

He was skilled, but his reputation wasn't great, partly because he rarely took on work that didn't interest him.

For someone like Azimov, Rei, who generously allowed him to use rare materials without hesitation, was the best client imaginable.

The Twilight Spear was the prime example.

That's precisely why Rei even made the kiln he casually requested.

...Though it's true that the deciding factor was definitely Ray's permission to use Silver Lion materials.

And now, the Sleipnir Boots, for which he requested reinforcement using Silver Lion materials.

Esta Nord of the Zepairu lineage, known as the greatest alchemist in history.

To actually see the Sleipnir Boots, crafted by that very person with the pinnacle of his artistry.

For Azimov, double-checking everything was only natural.

"Thanks to that obsession, I don't have to deal with getting stuck with some weird defective product, so I'm actually grateful. ...Well, I'd be happier if you could somehow manage a little more time, though."

"A master craftsman naturally takes the time required for their work."

Vihera, who had been listening to Rei and Marina's conversation, interjected to mediate.

As a former princess of the Bestir Empire, she'd seen countless top-tier items, so her opinion carried weight.

"Well, I trust your skill completely."

Rei wasn't seriously disparaging Azimov either.

Honestly, though, he wished he could have finished it a bit sooner.

The four people and one creature walked along the road, chatting in the spring-like weather.

Though calling it spring-like was a stretch; it wasn't quite spring yet.

Half... no, maybe seventy percent spring, but mornings and nights were still bitterly cold.

"Ah, Set-chan! Hey, Rei. Can I play with Set-chan?"

"Sorry. I've got something to do right now, so I can't. Play with Set another time."

After responding to the child playing nearby, the child looked disappointed but resignedly accepted it.

"Hehe, Set's quite popular, isn't he?"

"Yeah. Honestly, I don't know when he started getting this kind of attention."

Marina's words prompted Rei to stroke Set thoughtfully as he replied.

When Rei and Set first came to Gilm, people were terribly frightened of them simply because they had a griffin with them.

But... now, even small children approached him, wanting to play with Set.

This was partly because Set had never caused senseless trouble in town, and partly because he was born with a childlike personality, quickly bonding with anyone who paid him attention.

Also, though Set looked very frightening at first glance, upon closer look, he had round, adorable eyes. The fur growing from his body felt like silk, making one want to stroke it forever.

Deep in thought about everything that had happened since arriving in Gilm, he walked on until he finally reached his destination... Azimov's house.

He knocked lightly, but there was no response from inside.

"What should we do? Well, he's probably inside, engrossed in some research or something."

"Hmm."

Though Vihera and Byune hadn't met Azimov that many times, they seemed to have grasped his personality fairly well from those few encounters.

Rei nodded in agreement at their straightforward words.

"Yeah, then maybe we should be a bit more forceful... Hey?"

Rei was about to knock on the door again, but as if overcome by his own momentum, the door opened automatically.

...Right. It hadn't been locked.

"Pretty careless, huh?"

Had it been unlocked the last time he came?

The thought crossed Rei's mind, but right now, it was better to go inside and warn Azimov directly.

Thinking this, he opened his mouth just in case.

"Hey, Azimov! You there? I'm coming in!"

"The door being unlocked... isn't really that strange, is it? He's at home, after all."

Vihera tilted her head, slightly puzzled by Rei's actions.

"Well, some people lock their doors even when they're home. Besides, Azimov sometimes brings snacks."

"...I see. So, should we go inside?"

"Yeah. ...Set, sorry, but you'll have to wait here. It doesn't look like you can come inside."

"Grr."

Set growled disappointedly at Rei's words.

But Set not being able to enter the building was nothing new.

In the end, he lay down in front of Azimov's house, in a spot that wouldn't obstruct the path.

Then, as if to say "Have a good trip," Set growled.

With Set seeing them off like that, Rei and the others entered the house.

But... once inside, Rei felt something was off.

There wasn't anything definitive.

Still, he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off.

Driven by that instinct, Rei started running.

His destination was the laboratory where Azimov always stayed.

Within mere seconds, Rei arrived there. What he saw... was Azimov lying on the floor, bleeding from his body.
