"Hmm... I think we should be seeing fishing villages or port towns by now."

"Grrrr..."

At Rei’s words, Set let out a happy growl.

Ever since yesterday—or more precisely, ever since the Water Sphere’s level went up thanks to Guztos’s magic stone—he’d been in such a good mood.

That said, Rei wasn’t entirely unfamiliar with that feeling.

Whether it’s the Death Scythe or Set, skills acquired through Beast Magic really come into their own once they reach Level 5.

In fact, the Water Ball at Level 5 had become significantly more powerful compared to Level 4.

Specifically, the Level 4 Water Ball allowed him to summon two water balls, each with a diameter of 40 cm, and manipulate their trajectories to a certain extent. As for its power, if it hit a rock, the water ball would explode, causing the rock’s surface to shatter.

But at Level 5, the Water Ball skill allows him to summon four water balls, each about one meter in diameter. Not only that, but its controllability has clearly improved, and its power is now sufficient to shatter the rock itself, let alone just its surface.

(It was the same with Flying Slash, but once you reach Level 5, it becomes incredibly powerful all at once. …Well, it’s not like there aren’t any drawbacks.)

The speed at which the water balls flew, however, was almost exactly the same as it had been at Level 4.

Of course, it wasn’t so slow it made you yawn… but since it was only about as fast as a baseball, it wasn’t difficult to dodge if you tried.

(I suppose the maneuverability is there to compensate for that.)

As I flew through the sky thinking about that, Set eventually spotted a village in the distance and let out a cry.

It was a small village, likely home to fifty people or fewer, but considering the goal of obtaining Rei’s net, that was actually preferable.

If word got out that there was an adventurer with a griffin as a partner, it would undoubtedly cause a huge commotion.

To prevent that, the best course of action was to keep the information itself from spreading.

……That said, since it’s a small village, it’s highly likely their fishing skills are rudimentary, so it’s hard to say if they even have nets.

“Well then… let’s go. I’m hoping they have some decent fishing gear. I’d at least like to find a harpoon or something.”

The tool Rei currently uses to catch large fish is a spear.

But it’s just an ordinary spear… no, considering it’s a spear on the verge of breaking, it’s actually less than ordinary.

As such, the spear has no barbs or barbs of any kind, so even if it hits a fish, it simply slips right out.

So far, the fish had died from the spear’s attack, so it hadn’t been a problem, but if the spear didn’t kill it… or if the seawater caused the spear to get stuck in a strange place, there was a chance the fish could escape.

However, if it were a harpoon with barbs, it wouldn’t just slip out like a spear even if it pierced the fish.

Of course, if handled roughly, it would likely come out even after piercing the fish… but that would depend on Rei’s skill.

(I’d also like something like a fish-version of a bug net—the kind you use to scoop up fish when reeling them in. Since Set’s water ball has become quite powerful, it could stun fish swimming in relatively shallow areas of the ocean with the impact.)

That’s a simplified version of the fishing method Rei and Set used to do in the river—hitting a rock to stun the fish with the shockwave.

Rei hasn’t actually tried it yet, but he’s certain it will work.

While he was thinking about that, the village drew closer, and eventually, Set landed a short distance away from it.

Rei left Set there and headed toward the village.

Normally, it wouldn’t be strange for Set to throw a tantrum wanting to go with Rei, but this time they weren’t planning to stay in the village; they only intended to ask for some fishing gear.

Other than that, they might buy some fish if there were any left over.

For that reason alone, since they wouldn’t be staying in the village for long, Set agreed to wait quietly in a spot away from the village.

……Though, there’s no doubt that the fact he’s been spending all his time with Rei lately, playing in the ocean and such, has had an influence on that.

(He’s gotten quite a bit bigger, but Set’s clingy habits still haven’t gone away. Well, I’m happy about it, so it’s fine.)

Thinking that, Rei made his way toward the village while keeping a watchful eye on his surroundings.

And so, less than ten minutes after dismounting from Set, the village eventually came into view.

Since Rei could see the village, it was only natural that the villagers could see him as well.

However, what came as a complete surprise to Rei was that, as he drew closer, several people began gathering at the village entrance.

What’s more, the villagers were holding harpoons, wooden sticks, farming tools… and clearly looked wary of Rei.

(Well, it is a typical rural village. It wouldn’t be strange if they were a bit insular.)

Even as he thought this, Rei didn’t stop in his tracks.

Showing no sign of being flustered, Rei continued toward the village.

Seeing Rei like that, it was actually the villagers who were the ones thrown off balance.

And no wonder. Despite more than ten people waiting with weapons in hand, Rei walked toward them without showing the slightest sign of fear.

However, once they could clearly see Rei approaching, the villagers, who had been bristling with hostility, gradually calmed down.

Rei isn’t particularly tall, and he certainly isn’t visibly muscular.

Furthermore, since he generally keeps his belongings stored in his Misty Ring, he doesn’t appear to be carrying any weapons.

That said, since Rei was wearing the Dragon Robe, it wouldn’t have been strange for people to suspect he was hiding some kind of weapon underneath.

In any case, judging by Rei’s appearance, he certainly didn’t look like someone capable of harming the village.

Once that became clear, the villagers, who had been on guard with weapons in hand, finally began to relax.

(Well, it’s fine that they’re relaxing, but I can’t quite accept that they’re basing it solely on my appearance. Besides… what if I’m actually a scout for some bandit gang or something? What are they planning to do then? Isn’t that a bit too careless, no matter how you look at it?)

Even so, since this was undoubtedly to Rei's advantage, Rei decided not to say anything for the time being and simply kept walking.

As he finally neared the village... soon, a man in his twenties emerged from within and approached Rei.

“What business do you have in this village?”

He must have judged from Rei’s appearance that he wasn’t an enemy, but there was still a hint of wariness in his tone.

“Before we get into that, just to confirm—this is a fishing village, right?”

“……Huh? Oh, yeah. Well, I guess so.”

Rei nodded, looking somewhat bewildered by Rei’s words.

The man, who probably wanted to say, “You can tell just by looking,” had already completely let down his guard toward Rei.

It seemed Rei’s appearance had worked in his favor.

“I see. Good to know. …Actually, I’ve been fishing and playing in the ocean a little ways from here, but catching small fish is a bit tough without a net. If this is a fishing village, I was wondering if you sell nets for fishing.”

“……Huh? Eh?”

The man looked as if he didn’t understand what Rei had said.

But that was only natural.

The very fact that someone like Rei is fishing outside the village or playing in the ocean is dangerous in many ways.

There are plenty of monsters around here, and plenty of wild animals too. And above all… right now, there are even more dangerous beings out there.

“Are you crazy?! If someone like you’s wandering around here alone—let alone sleeping out in the open like that—you’ll definitely get spotted by them!”

Hearing Rei’s words, the man snapped back to his senses and shouted that in a panic.

It was a shout of anger directed at Rei… or, to be precise, a shout born of concern.

If there were anyone here capable of recognizing Rei’s strength, they would have realized that the man’s worry was almost entirely pointless.

But in this backwater of a backwater—a village that, in a different sense from Gilm, truly deserves to be called a frontier—there was no way anyone that skilled could be found.

To the villagers, Rei appeared to be nothing more than what he looked like on the surface.

(Should I have brought Set along?)

Rei thought that for a moment, but if he had brought Set here, there’s no doubt it would have caused an even bigger commotion.

Yes, for example, as if every single villager were to challenge him to a fight with their lives on the line.

Normally, one wouldn’t expect that to happen, but just a moment ago… when Rei first came into view, he had seen with his own eyes the villagers gathering, each clutching whatever they could use as a weapon. Given that, he couldn’t dismiss that concern.

Thinking that, he concluded that leaving Set behind had indeed been the right decision. With that in mind, Rei spoke up.

“I don’t know what kind of opponents those guys are… but don’t worry. Believe it or not, I’m a Rank B adventurer.”

“Don’t lie.”

Rei was taken aback by the man who, without a moment’s hesitation, immediately declared that Rei’s claim to be a Rank B adventurer was a lie.

He was used to being underestimated because of his appearance.

But even so, to be told so instantly that he was lying...

“No, it’s true. Look, here.”

He pulled out his guild card from the Misty Ring and showed it to the man, but the man merely tilted his head in confusion.

In fact, he didn’t even react when he saw her take the guild card out of her Misty Ring.

He probably has no idea what an item box is.

“Hmm… I don’t know about that. Is this what a guild card looks like? The last time an adventurer showed me one was four… five years ago? It’s been that long… Hey, anyone! Is there anyone here who can identify a guild card!?”

He calls out to the villagers in the distance, but adventurers rarely ever come to a village in such a remote area.

After all, even merchants only come by once every few years, if that.

Given the circumstances, there was no way to know for sure if the guild card Rei was holding was genuine.

Still... a few villagers came over and took a look at Rei’s guild card.

“I think this is the real thing…” Though his words were a bit hesitant, the man who had just guaranteed that this was a guild card—and thus provisionally recognized Rei as an adventurer—now looked at Rei with a gaze reserved for something strange.

“So, this adventurer went out of his way to come all the way to this village just to sell fishing gear and stuff like that? That’s quite…”

He didn’t finish the sentence, but Rei could easily guess that the word he was about to say was “eccentric”—or something along those lines.

“If you could just think of it as part of being an adventurer, that would be helpful.”

If an ordinary adventurer had heard that, they would have shouted, “Don’t lump me in with a guy like Rei!” But unfortunately—or perhaps fortunately—there was no guild branch in this village, and since Rei was the only adventurer present, he didn’t have to endure such a retort.

If anything, many people actually accepted it precisely because he was an adventurer.

“So, I’d like to get right down to business… but is there a problem?”

At first glance, this village looks like nothing more than an ordinary fishing village in the countryside.

In fact, the very idea that everyone would go so far as to prepare for battle just because someone like Rei approached them… even to Rei, that seemed odd.

“Just a little. Pirates came by the other day and stole our food and supplies.”

“Ah… yeah, I see.”

In the past, this would have been the kind of situation where bandits were said to be active, but here, it was pirates.

Then again, given this village’s location right on the coast, pirates are probably a more common threat than bandits.

“I get why they were so wary of me. But… I hate to say it, but can this village really handle pirates on its own?”

“……”

At Rei’s words, the villagers fell silent.

The villagers, too, had been thinking that it would be difficult to deal with pirates on their own.

If there were only about ten pirates, the village’s forces alone might be enough to handle them.

Bandits might be a group of just a few people, but pirates, because they need to use ships, naturally tend to have larger numbers.

In fact, it’s not uncommon for them to form groups of over a hundred.

(But… was I lucky, then? A ship, treasure, and pirates. Especially the ship—there’s a chance Lord Daskar might buy it from me for a high price to use as a land vessel.)

With that in mind... Rei smiled and spoke up.

“Would you consider hiring me?”

He asked this with a smile on his face.
