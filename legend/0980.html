As Rei entered the guild, it was nearly evening, and the place was bustling with adventurers.

Some had completed quests, while others had slain monsters and come to exchange their proof of defeat parts, magic stones, and materials for gold.

Even so, it wasn't quite full evening yet, so the crowd wasn't as thick as it would be later in the evening.

Many adventurers who had received their rewards headed to the attached tavern, and the number of patrons there was far greater than during the day.

If Rei were among such a crowd, he would naturally blend in.

He wasn't particularly tall to begin with, and with the hood of his Dragon Robe pulled up, few would recognize him as Rei.

If the former mercenaries who escaped the Cyclops were already in Gilm, he might have pushed his way through to the receptionists he knew, like Lenora or Kenny. Fortunately, Ranga had told him he was the first to arrive at Gilm.

(This crowd... This is exactly why I hate coming to the guild in the evening or early morning.)

Rei sighed inwardly as he joined the line for the receptionist.

Rei cast curious glances his way.

He looked small and frail, hardly the build of an adventurer who worked solo.

Some wondered if someone had gotten annoyed with waiting in line and dumped it on Rei, but no one voiced it.

It was considered bad manners to meddle in another party's affairs.

Unless it was some kind of severe abuse, just seeing someone made to wait in line wasn't necessarily evidence of such.

Had she pulled down her hood, they might have recognized her as Rei, but in this crowd, no one felt like doing that.

Unaware he was being watched like that, Rei scanned his surroundings.

Since the sheer number of people was unavoidable, she tried to kill time until her turn came.

As she scanned her surroundings, Rei suddenly noticed that the lengths of the various lines differed.

(Why?)

Rei was puzzled, but the answer came quickly.

Because he overheard the words of an adventurer standing in the line next to Rei.

"Today's the day I'm going to ask Kenny out... for dinner tonight..."

"Seriously. You need to give it up already. Maybe before, but Kenny-chan these days isn't someone you can just casually invite to dinner. And even before that, trying to invite someone to dinner when there's this long line? That's just going to bother everyone else. Honestly, it'll only make Kenny-chan think less of you."

"Hmph, then what am I supposed to do?"

"If you ask her when the receptionist isn't busy, it probably won't bother anyone."

"No, but during the day we're out taking requests too, right?"

"That's exactly why—on days off when there are no requests."

The adventurer's eyes widened, clearly surprised by the suggestion.

"Oh, right. So we can come to the guild even when we're not looking for jobs?"

"Well, that's obvious now. You're always hanging out at the tavern and stuff."

From the conversation he overheard, Rei understood that the length of the line in front of the receptionist reflected her popularity.

Of course, a long line doesn't necessarily mean she's popular.

Even with a long line, if she handled reports and currency exchange quickly enough, the line wouldn't stay that long.

As he pondered the popularity of Lenora and Kenny, the line eventually moved forward, and it was Rei's turn.

"...Huh? Rei?"

Lenora, recognizing the person who appeared before her as the Rei she knew, flashed a moment of surprise before immediately reverting to her receptionist demeanor.

"Correct. I was worried you might not notice me."

With a smile, he pulled down his hood, revealing, unsurprisingly, Rei's face.

Those standing directly behind Rei and the adventurers handling various procedures at the adjacent counter all wore expressions of surprise.

...Naturally, Kenny at the counter next to Lenora was too.

"Well, we've known each other for quite some time now. So, what's up? I thought you were in the forest handling that Cyclops business?"

"Ah, that was resolved safely. But there were cyclopes other than the five reported before we arrived, and Mireine and the others sent people to Gilm to deal with them. I was going to tell them we didn't need that anymore..."

At Rei's words, Lenora hurriedly reached for the documents nearby.

Her action stemmed from the thought that perhaps someone had already handled that matter.

Originally, in this cyclops extermination mission, Dietz had reported that cyclops, which normally act alone, were moving in groups, and there were various oddities about it.

That's why she acted, wondering if another receptionist might have received that report... But Rei called a halt to Lenora's actions.

"Don't worry. I confirmed with Ranga when I entered Gilm. It seems the person who came to report on that matter hasn't arrived in Gilm yet. Thanks to Set, I suppose."

"I see. ...Yes, it seems that way. The reports we've received here don't mention anything about that either."

After briefly skimming the important report for confirmation, Lenora breathed a sigh of relief.

"That's precisely why I was quietly standing in line like this."

"Rei, if that's the case, wouldn't it be okay for you to join my line?"

Kenny, who had been dealing with other adventurers next to Lenora, called out to Rei.

Even so, he wasn't striking up a conversation like usual, probably because there were still many adventurers ahead of him in line.

Even with Rei present, Kenny was doing his job properly without losing control—perfectly normal behavior for a receptionist. Seeing this, Lenora thought to herself, Kenny's finally being serious!

Boobs... boobs... boobs... boobs...

"Yikes! Hey, wait a minute, Lenora. Why are you glaring at me like that?!"

Kenny wasn't an adventurer, just a receptionist. But whether it was the wild instinct of a cat-kin or just plain female intuition, she sensed something like murderous intent coming from Lenora.

Rei, too, looked shocked as Lenora's demeanor suddenly changed right before his eyes.

"Oh, oh? Sorry, Kenny. I guess remembering everything just now made me..."

"You're not fooling me, you're not fooling me at all!"

Kenny blurted out, but since he had to deal with the adventurers, he couldn't say any more.

Leaving Kenny to return to his work, Rei turned to Lenora and began speaking as if starting over.

"So, about that Cyclops business... there's something that's been bothering me."

"Something that concerns you? What exactly is it..."

"There was a red cyclops that seemed to be commanding the regular cyclops. I think this one was probably either a rare species or a higher-tier species..."

"A rare or superior species of Cyclops? That would indeed be troublesome. ...But since you fought it, Rei, you must have defeated it, right? Rare species magic stones are quite uncommon."

Rei had a hobby of collecting magic stones, and Lenora didn't seem particularly concerned.

Rei had told her she collected magic stones as a hobby to secure the stones needed for his beast magic, and she was surprised to remember that.

"Ah, no. Yeah. I was going to, but actually, that Cyclops had this weird magic item hammer."

"...A Cyclops with a magic item?"

It must have been quite unexpected. After a brief silence, Lenora repeated herself.

The adventurers lined up behind Rei, the one handing Kenny the completed request documents, and even the one opposite Kenny all subtly listened to Rei's words. Whether they showed it on their faces or not, they were all inwardly surprised.

Cyclopes were monsters specialized in physical attacks, and even the limited magic they could use was confined to shockwaves fired from their single eye.

Yet, the idea that one could wield a magic item was simply unbelievable.

"Above all, the red Cyclops was considerably larger than a regular Cyclops. And the magic item seemed perfectly suited for that large Cyclops to use. A regular Magic Sword would be too small and awkward for a Cyclops to wield, so I sensed something a bit contrived about that."

"That's true. Logically, it shouldn't be possible for a cyclops—especially a rare or high-ranking one—to have magic items tailored for its use. If that's the case, then the deliberate nature Rei mentioned does raise questions."

Lenora murmured, seeming convinced by Rei's words.

"Right? I figured we should report that part. I brought that magic item too. What do you say? If you want to see it, I can show it to you."

Where's that magic item? That question never crossed Lenora's lips.

She knew full well Rei carried an item box.

"Then, could you please do that? Maybe the guild staff can look into it and find out something."

"That's fine, but it's cramped in here, you know? It's pretty big and heavy, just like the red cyclops had it. If I carelessly took it out here, it might even damage the floor. ...Oh, and since this magic item was on the red cyclops I defeated, it's mine, right? I'd be in a bit of a bind if I had to submit it to the guild or something."

"Using a magic item whose origin and maker are unknown... No, that's perfectly normal, isn't it?"

At Lenora's troubled words, most of the adventurers present nodded in agreement.

Of course, it's true that using magic items you know for certain were made by an alchemist is best, but magic items are fundamentally expensive.

It's a different story for something like a Magic Sword—barely even worthy of the name—where a regular longsword has just a light infusion of magic. But a proper Magic Sword comes with a hefty price tag.

That's precisely why, even if a magic item's origin is unknown, those who acquire it often happily claim it as their own.

...Even if there's a chance it might be cursed.

Moreover, magic items found in dungeons are prime examples of items whose creators are unknown.

Even so, finding one represents a golden opportunity for a fortune, and naturally, the finder claims it as their own.

"Yeah. Besides, I'm not going to use it as-is anyway."

"Right. It's a magic item perfectly sized for a Cyclops to use, so it'd be a stretch for Rei-san to use it..."

"Well, I probably could use it if I tried, but I don't really want to go to that trouble. Besides, I've never even used a hammer before. It's better to put it to good use, right?"

"Put it to good use, you say? Um, if it's not too much trouble, could you tell me more?"

As he started to nod at Lenora's words, he noticed the attention he was drawing.

Worse still, the guild hall, usually bustling with noise, had fallen completely silent.

The tavern area was still noisy as usual, so it wasn't complete silence, but nearly everyone on the guild side... even the guild staff behind the counter were focused on Rei's words.

He shrugged, deciding it was best to dodge the question for now. If he explained how he intended to use the magic item in this situation, many would likely try to get a piece of the action.

"I'll keep that a secret. But if it works out, it could lead to some interesting things. Whether it'll actually work is another matter."

Using an existing magic item as part of the materials to create another magic item.

Normally, it seemed like a pretty impossible feat, but then again, transforming Noiz's Magic Sword into a spear was essentially the same thing.

Of course, in the case of this hammer, the difficulty was higher since it involved synthesizing it with a Magic Sword.

(Besides, if even just part of that hammer could be used as material for magic items, it should be possible to create a considerable number of them.)

A hammer so large it didn't look small even when held by a cyclops nearly five meters tall.

Naturally, its mass was considerable, and Rei hoped that even using a portion of it to carve a spear would yield sufficient results...

"...Understood. However, I'd like to see that hammer. So, could you come to the guild's storage area? There, we can appraise it without drawing attention. Ah, of course, only if you're okay with it, Rei."

Rei immediately nodded at Lenora's words.

Even if it was to be used as material for a magic item, it was essential to thoroughly check whether there were any issues with that hammer.

If the guild would handle that for him, it was practically a dream come true.
