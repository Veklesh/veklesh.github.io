"Hmm... I don't think there are many magic items of that sort around here."

"Grr..."

In response to Rei’s words, Set lets out a growl and looks disappointed.

The construction site for the expansion in Gilm.

A short distance away, a battle between adventurers and kobolds was still raging.

But Rei didn’t seem to care at all, instead inspecting the construction site.

...That said, building materials were piled up in several spots, and many were covered with magical cloth to protect them from the snow.

The trees felled in the Treant Forest have already been processed into lumber after undergoing magical treatment, so they’re only covered with makeshift cloths as a precaution.

The stone materials, on the other hand, were left almost completely exposed to the elements.

He looked around the gaps between these building materials, checking to see if any magical items had been placed there… but, as expected, he couldn’t find any clues.

“Set, please.”

Noticing the presence of a kobold approaching him, Rei calls out to his partner.

Hearing Rei’s words, Set immediately swings his front paws at the kobold approaching them.

“Grrrrrrrrrrr!”

The kobold charged right at Set without showing the slightest sign of fear, but just because it wasn’t afraid of him didn’t mean the decisive power gap between a kobold and a griffin could be bridged.

Set’s forepaw swings toward the kobold.

That single blow effortlessly crushed the neck of the kobold, who was holding a rusty longsword—wherever he had found it.

……The kobold’s blood, bodily fluids, flesh, bones, teeth, and even its brain were scattered all around.

Normally, one shouldn’t go so far as to crush the head just to secure the right ear, which serves as proof of the kill.

But that’s only an issue for those who want the proof of kill part; for Rei and Set, who don’t care about it, it’s no problem at all.

That said, since the magic stones are still there, anyone who finds this kobold’s corpse in the slums would be lucky.

“Ah… well, it’s a bit dirty, but the snow and rain will clean it up by spring, right? Besides, from what I can see, the ground is just as stained with blood where the others are fighting.”

Looking around, Rei saw an adventurer defeating a goblin with a club-like weapon.

Some were even crushing the goblins’ heads with a weapon called a morning star—a spiked metal ball connected by a chain.

Compared to those, the corpse of the kobold Set had killed probably wasn’t all that different. Reaching that conclusion, Rei headed toward the next suspicious spot with Set.

The stonework a short distance away was stacked firmly to prevent it from collapsing, but precisely because of that, he wondered if some kind of magical item might have been set up to hide it from view.

That was the reasoning behind their actions, but...

“A miss, I suppose. Well, even if someone were to set up a magic item, they wouldn’t go about doing it so brazenly in such an easily spotted location, would they?”

It’s a rare magical item capable of controlling kobolds.

If it were to break, the consequences would certainly be irreversible.

When I think about it, placing it here—in a place where battles with kobolds are bound to happen—seems nothing short of reckless.

(There’s also the question of just how far the magic item’s effect actually reaches. …Well, considering they’ve been gathering kobolds endlessly from this area, there really’s no need to go out of their way to place it at a construction site.)

That was Rei’s line of thinking, though he chose to set aside the idea that this might not be a magic item at all, but rather the result of some other means.

After all, Rei was searching for magic items like this mostly just to kill time; he hadn’t accepted it as a formal request.

(In that case, rather than freely controlling the kobolds, it’s gathering them here… Could it be a magic item that acts like a moth-to-a-flame trap? Actually, I’ve heard there haven’t been any kobold-related incidents in the streets of Gilm.)

While speculating, Rei focuses his sense of smell, searching the area for any strange odors—any scent that might attract kobolds.

But all Rei could detect was the smell of kobold blood.

That’s only natural, since the battle between the kobolds and the adventurers is still raging nearby.

That leaves Rei with few options.

“Set, could you check the area for any strange smells?”

“Grr?”

"Smell?" Set tilts his head in confusion.

Rei continued speaking as he stroked Set’s head.

“That’s right. Maybe… just maybe, there’s something giving off a scent that attracts kobolds. A scent so faint that even Set can’t pick it up right now.”

Set’s senses, including his sense of smell, are overwhelmingly sharper than Rei’s.

Although Rei said there might be a scent even Set couldn’t detect, Set possessed a skill called “Scent Enhancement” that sharpened his sense of smell.

If he used that skill, he might be able to detect a scent he currently couldn’t pick up.

In response to Rei’s request, Set happily activated the skill.

"Grrrrr!"

With his senses even sharper than usual, Set tries to detect whether there is any scent that might be attracting the kobolds...

"Grr..."

After sniffing the scent lingering in the air for several dozen seconds, Set eventually let out a apologetic growl toward Rei.

However, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered and simply stroked Set’s head.

She hadn’t asked Set with any real certainty to begin with; she’d just asked him in the hope that he might find something.

Given that, it was only natural, in a sense, that Set wouldn’t be able to find anything.

“Don’t worry about it. Besides, what Set just did wasn’t a complete waste. In fact, it actually meant a lot to me.”

“Growl?”

“Really?” Set tilted his head adorably.

Rei stroked Set, as if to prove that what he’d said wasn’t a lie or a cover-up.

The fact that Set couldn’t pick up any scent meant Rei could be certain there weren’t any magical items in this area emitting a scent that would attract kobolds, so it was a relief for him to know he didn’t need to search any further in that direction.

While petting Set, Rei thinks about what to do next.

The most likely possibility was, after all, that there was something here attracting the kobolds.

However, since even Set’s keen sense of smell couldn’t detect a magical item emitting a scent that attracts kobolds, it was safe to say there wasn’t one here.

No, actually, even if it were here, since he couldn’t find it, he couldn’t very well go trying to get his hands on it now.

“Well, if that’s the case… we’re back to square one. A method that attracts only kobolds without affecting other monsters, starting with goblins… if there’s any other way to do that…”

Gazing at the kobold corpses lying on the ground, Rei pondered ways to gather kobolds other than through scent.

(Cobolds… Cobolds are monsters that look like dogs or wolves. That’s exactly why I doubted the idea of gathering them by scent… But if it’s not that scent… then sound?)

Thinking of dogs, Rei remembered dog whistles.

He’d seen it on TV back in Japan; it was a device that allowed dogs to clearly detect and hear sounds inaudible to humans.

Rei didn’t think the dogs he knew and the kobolds were the same creatures, but there was no doubt they resembled each other in having dog-like faces.

If that’s the case, perhaps they aren’t gathering them with scent, but are controlling the kobolds with some kind of magic item similar to a dog whistle.

(Even humans can’t hear certain sounds depending on their age, after all. Thinking about it that way, it wouldn’t be strange if such a magical item existed… would it?)

Even as he thought this, Rei’s senses were sharper than those of an ordinary human.

If even Rei’s ears can’t pick it up, does that mean it’s emitting a sound that only kobolds can hear… or…

“Set, this time it’s not a scent—it’s a sound. Do you hear anything strange?”

“Grrrr…”

In response to Rei’s words, Set silently shook his head.

Seeing that, it was clear that no particular sound was echoing here.

Or perhaps there was a sound even Set, a griffin, couldn’t hear… but Rei wasn’t particularly worried about that.

That was because he had such strong trust in Set.

(Besides, a sound that a kobold can hear but a griffin can’t… that’s just not plausible, is it?)

Biologically speaking—though he is a monster—a griffin is far superior to a kobold, so it was only natural for Rei to think that way.

That said, just because a creature is of a higher rank doesn’t mean it’s superior in every single ability, so the possibility that a sound is echoing around them—one that a kobold can hear but Set cannot—couldn’t be ruled out.

“If they can’t hear the sound either… other than smell and hearing, what else could it be… taste? No, that would be difficult unless they actually tasted it themselves, and I can’t really think of anything regarding touch either. That leaves sight? No… but sight… what do you think?”

“Grr?”

Rei knew asking Set probably wouldn’t make any difference in this case, but he asked anyway, clinging to the faint hope that maybe… just maybe…

But, as expected, all that came back from Set was a meow that seemed to say, “What’s wrong?”

“Is there something only kobolds can see? …Hmm, I wonder. Compared to smell or sound, it just feels like a much smaller possibility. …If that’s the case, what’s the next possibility…?”

A magic item that interferes with the five senses to manipulate the kobold.

That’s what Rei had thought, but when he actually looked around, there was nothing that fit the description.

No matter what he did, it would be incredibly difficult to find anything in the current situation.

“Marina’s spirit magic… no, actually, given Marina’s personality, she’s probably already checked this area for anything suspicious, right?”

“I’ll ask her about that over dinner tonight.”

With that in mind, Rei and Set began checking suspicious spots one after another.

Naturally, doing something like that would draw attention.

And while Rei might be one thing, Set is a griffin. It would be impossible for him not to stand out.

Most people didn’t bother to speak to Rei and Set, simply going about their business of hunting kobolds… but naturally, there were some eccentric adventurers among them.

“Hey, Rei. What on earth are you doing? I thought you were supposed to be butchering a Giganto Turtle?”

A man in his early twenties, wielding a longsword and traveling solo, called out to Rei.

The main reason he felt compelled to speak up was probably that his curiosity got the better of him… but seeing the scene of someone lifting the cloth covering the wood to check inside also played a big part in sparking his curiosity.

Rei, having been addressed, poked his head out from under the cloth, looked at the man, and spoke.

“I don’t really need to be here for the Giganto Turtle disassembly. I’ll come back later to collect what didn’t get finished today, but until then, I’m free.”

“...Are you really coming all the way here just because you’re bored? To you, Rei, a kobold isn’t even worth the effort to defeat.”

“That’s true.”

Rei nodded thoughtfully at the man’s words.

For Rei, a Rank B adventurer with a nickname, kobolds aren’t worth the trouble of fighting.

If Rei showed any sign of hostility… no, if Set even showed his face, the kobolds were so weak they’d run away on the spot.

That’s precisely why the man couldn’t understand why Rei would go out of his way to come here.

Above all, he couldn’t imagine a kobold hiding in a narrow space like the one Rei had been looking at earlier.

In other words, he surmised that Rei had come here for something other than the kobolds.

“So? If you don’t mind, could you tell me what’s going on? Even if it’s something you don’t understand, I’ve been fighting here for days, so I might have an idea.”

“I think the reason the kobolds keep attacking endlessly is because someone is using a magic item to artificially trigger it. So, I was just looking around to see if I could find that magic item.”

“……A magic item, huh. I see.”

It’s a fairly well-known fact in Gilm that Rei has a hobby of collecting magical items.

For that very reason, the man found Rei’s explanation convincing… but in the end, he had no leads. Having satisfied his curiosity, he returned to the task of hunting down the kobolds.
