The researcher had been about to say something in response to Rei's words, but there was no time to engage with him.

For Rei and his group, the giants were opponents they could handle without much trouble. But ordinary soldiers... soldiers with the skill level of those present here, couldn't defeat the giants.

That much was obvious just by looking at the area around Rei and the others right now.

The corpses of soldiers—no, the scattered fragments of flesh that should be called remains—were strewn everywhere.

And in the time it took Rei and her group to reach this spot, the soldiers hadn't taken down a single giant.

Of course, the soldiers themselves were likely shaken, never expecting the giants they had completely subdued to suddenly go berserk.

Regardless, Rei's honest feeling was that they couldn't afford to concern themselves with those present here now, given the urgent need to reach the location of the Titans.

"Wait a moment! Is it true... the black crystal was destroyed!?"

Despite having the tip of the Twilight Spear thrust at him, the researcher shouted.

The Black Crystal he and his colleagues had worked together to research. And the giants born from that Black Crystal.

Because he had been involved with it, the words "the black crystal has been destroyed" coming from Rei's mouth were impossible to ignore.

But... that was solely the man's concern. Rei had no need to care about his situation.

"Shut up. Be quiet for a moment."

He said curtly, stepping forward and swinging the Twilight Spear sideways.

He chose to sweep rather than thrust because he suspected the researcher might hold vital information.

Survivors were already scarce, so it was necessary to keep the researcher alive if possible.

Especially since, as far as Rei could tell by scanning the area, the man before him was the only one who fit the description of a researcher.

That was precisely why Rei struck not with the tip, but with the shaft, sweeping the researcher man aside.

The blow must have been a significant shock to the man. His body slammed into the wall of a nearby building, and he collapsed to the ground, unconscious.

"Marina, make sure that man doesn't get away."

"Oh? I'm staying here?"

"That's how it'll be. Normally, I'd want spirit magic support..."

The spirit magic support Marina could provide was arguably the pinnacle of what the term "support" could entail.

It was regrettable not to receive that support, but in reality, either Marina or Elena needed to stay here.

And since this retaliation against Jaya was an operation being carried out in cooperation with the Noble Faction, taking Elena along was only natural.

Moreover, she was undoubtedly a reliable ally purely as combat power.

She might not be as skilled at support as Marina, but using Mirage allows her to attack from mid-range, and she's also proficient in wind magic.

(Well, I can use magic too. For support, I've got Death Scythe skills and Twilight Spear throws. Actually, I have more long-range attack options. ...If they're just causing trouble, maybe using flashy magic to wipe them out would be quicker. Vihera would probably complain, though.)

Rei knew full well that using magic could easily take down... no, kill the giants en masse.

But doing something like that risked collapsing the cave.

The underground facility in Mejogo was built specifically as an underground facility from the start, so using a bit of magic there wouldn't be a problem.

But the place they were in now was a natural cave.

Even though it had been somewhat modified, like the area Rei was in now, using magic powerful enough to take down a thousand giants at once could easily cause the shockwaves to collapse the cave.

(Should I just collapse the cave and bury them all alive?)

To say such a thought never crossed Rei's mind would be untrue.

But if he were to attempt such a thing, he would obviously need to get everyone else out of the cave first.

He needed to get all his companions out of this treacherous terrain.

The Jaya people—soldiers aside—the researchers needed to be brought out too, to extract various information.

He would have to move through that treacherous cave with a burden in tow.

If he wasn't careful, the giant might escape from the cave before Rei could act.

Considering that, the idea of collapsing the cave along with everything inside was undoubtedly just too much trouble.

(We might even need to dig up research materials and such.)

Now that the black crystal has vanished, even Rei couldn't say how valuable any research materials left here might be.

However, there are still many unresolved aspects to this incident, and having research materials would undoubtedly be beneficial.

Of course, there's also the possibility that those research materials might prove useful for something else later on.

While it's unknown where the Black Crystal came from or how it arrived here, its disappearance doesn't necessarily mean all traces of its existence have vanished.

(Well, one life can only produce one giant. If you need numbers, it'll definitely be flashy, so it'd be easy to spot.)

If the goal is to generate massive profits while mass-producing giants, Mejogo is undoubtedly the optimal method.

Of course, since this optimal method involves forcibly taking women from other nations and sacrificing their lives as payment, from a common perspective, it can only be described as an unholy practice.

"Ah, but now isn't the time for such thoughts. Elena, Vihera, Byune, are you ready?"

After lightly shaking her head, Rei asked. The three nodded, indicating no issues.

"Marina, you're in charge here. If anyone tries to escape, kill any soldier-like types. For those researchers lying unconscious, just cut off one limb and let them go."

He spoke deliberately loud enough for the surrounding soldiers to hear.

Those words resonated with the soldiers with unparalleled intensity.

Of course. These men had fought a battle worthy of being called a massacre against giants they themselves couldn't lay a finger on.

To defy such an opponent would mean certain death.

Above all, to leave this space, they needed to exit through the door.

In other words, they had been made painfully aware that if any of Rei's group were standing in front of that door, overcoming them would be virtually impossible.

The only possible breakthrough was that Marina had a bow in her hands... but several soldiers had seen her use spirit magic during the battle against the giants with their own eyes.

Given that, they couldn't believe they could handle such an opponent on their own.

After all, some among them had seen magic for the very first time just now.

There was no way such men would obey an order to charge at someone wielding spirit magic.

...No, it was possible they didn't even recognize that magic as spirit magic in the first place.

In any case, the soldiers who understood Rei's words abandoned the idea of escaping from here.

They understood implicitly from Rei that if they tried such a thing, they would lose their lives.

Glancing at the soldiers, Rei judged they understood the meaning of his words. After exchanging a few final words with Marina, he stepped outside the door.

Having seen Rei off, Marina knew the figures before her were members of Jaya... beings who had literally devoured the women they'd forcibly taken. Given that understanding, there was no way she could be friendly toward them.

"I'll permit actions to rescue your comrades. But don't do anything strange. As Rei said, I can't guarantee your lives."

Marina declared, glancing at the soldiers.

Her features were exceptionally well-defined, and the cold stare she directed at them carried an overwhelming presence.

It was clear enough that her statement about not guaranteeing their lives wasn't a threat or anything else, but an undeniable fact.

"Now, as for what Rei and the others will do with the giants... well, I suppose there's no need to worry about that."

Standing before the door was a beautiful woman dressed in a party gown that seemed utterly out of place in this setting.

The sight was undeniably jarring, but the soldiers knew they were facing an opponent of monstrous strength. Thus, they quietly tended to their injured comrades.

Rei and the others emerged through the door and began running through the cave.

Having already traversed this path once, they weren't particularly wary of traps or ambushes, but the cave's terrain was far from ideal.

Even so, they weren't walking like they had on the way in; they were running, making their speed of movement extremely fast.

...Along the way, they passed by guards who had either fainted or died, but they took no particular action upon seeing them.

Thanks to this, the time it took them to reach the first fork in the cave was surprisingly short.

"Ryutas... isn't here. Outside the cave? Or did he already retreat from here...? Either way, since he's not here, I don't need to worry about him for now."

"Probably. Anyway, right now, we absolutely need to confirm as quickly as possible whether the giant's rampage was limited to the one over there, or if it involves all the giants."

Rei nodded at Elena's words and immediately turned his gaze toward a passageway different from the one they had come through.

With the path forming a Y-junction, there was no need to wonder which way to go.

Glancing at Elena, Vihera, and Byune, it was clear they still had plenty of stamina left.

...While Elena and Vihera were expected, Rei was slightly surprised that even the petite Byune still had reserves. But he quickly reminded himself that as specialized combatants, it was only natural for thieves.

"Alright, let's go. Just keep in mind, since this is a path giants use, we can generally assume there are no traps. We'll head out at the same pace we came back here. Is that okay with you?"

When Rei asked if they were physically okay, Elena, Vihera, and Byune each nodded in response.

After confirming this, Rei set off down the unseen path... the path leading to a place where countless giants likely existed.

Their running speed remained unchanged, just as he'd said.

Still, since this was their first time here, they kept a watchful eye on their footing just in case.

But that caution soon became less intense.

This was likely because the giants had moved the ground numerous times.

The ground was fairly even, and while there were a few spots where he might have tripped, he could basically run across it without any problems.

(Considering nearly a thousand giants walked here, it's no wonder it's like this. Plus, seeing about twenty giants over there, it seems they don't just pass through once and never return.)

Thinking this way, he figured it was probably fine, but he didn't forget to stay alert so he could react immediately if anything happened.

The cave scenery flowed past in rapid succession.

Though Rei couldn't tell since he was wearing the Dragon Robe, the air was so cool it was hard to believe it was summer.

...Though that's just because this is a cave, not because of the giants.

"Hey, Rei. What if the giant starts causing trouble?"

Vihera, running behind Rei, asked.

Without even looking back, Rei could imagine Vihera wearing a delighted smile.

"Well... we'd have to take them down, I suppose. A few might be manageable, but releasing nearly a thousand giants into the forest would be far too dangerous."

Rei didn't know exactly where the forest and cave they were in were located.

Well, of course he knew it was somewhere near the Kingdom of Reburina or Mejogo, but he had absolutely no idea how far away it actually was.

But he could guess the forest surrounding the cave wasn't that deep.

After all, the Kingdom of Reburina needed the giant herd as a military force. If they were too far away to be useful when the time came, that would be no laughing matter.

In other words, this forest was probably near Mejogo, and likely near the capital, Rossi.

It's not hard to imagine the devastation if a herd of uncontrolled giants rampaged in such a place.

Especially after seeing those giants devouring the soldiers earlier with my own eyes, it's even harder.

"Right. If that's what you intend to do, Rei, I have nothing to say. As for the rest..."

Just as Vihera spoke, a dull sound came from the direction Rei and the others were heading... a sound like metal striking flesh, yet incredibly massive.
