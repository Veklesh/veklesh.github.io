The moment they saw it, most of the men gasped.

They felt threatened by the other adventurer who had appeared to prevent their escape, but now, an even greater threat stood before them.

At first glance, Rei had seemed like nothing more than a child who had just become an adventurer, but that impression was now completely shattered.

He caught the blow of a club swung by a large man with one hand, and though it looked like nothing more than a light kick, it sent the man tumbling several meters across the ground.

Displaying such extraordinary physical prowess, and above all, the massive scythe that had appeared out of nowhere.

The moment most saw that scythe, they understood the true identity of the figure before them.

And above all else...

"Grrrrrr!"

Emerging from behind Rei was a griffin.

It growled unpleasantly, fixing its gaze on the men.

Though its eyes were usually best described as round, the gaze it now fixed on the men was sharp—the look of a predator eyeing its prey.

As far as the men knew, only one person in Gilm wielded a scythe like that.

That one person was, within Gilm... no, likely the only one in this entire world who had a griffin as a familiar.

There was no way the men living in Gilm's underworld didn't know the identity of such an opponent.

A man who shows no mercy to enemies, wielding his power without restraint.

One of the men spoke the name of the figure who carried the Death Scythe over his shoulder, with Set at his side.

"Crimson... Rei."

A murmur rippled through the room. The moment the name was spoken aloud, the men stirred.

They already knew from the current situation that the one before them was Rei.

But hearing it spoken aloud made them realize once more that the figure standing before them was an utterly hopeless foe.

Several of them, grasping this truth, quickly scanned their surroundings.

With Rei's presence revealed, the ambush was as good as failed. Given that, they judged they should leave this place as quickly as possible... indeed, before suffering any injuries.

But Rei's allies, surrounding Keep and the others, brandished their weapons ostentatiously, keeping them at bay.

"Well... you've played right into my hands. I get that this was the quickest way to erase the duel's outcome... but still, that was way too short-sighted. Since you came here to attack, you're finished now."

Rei swung his Death Scythe ostentatiously.

The sound of it slicing through the winter night air made those who heard it think of their own end.

"What will you do? If you just let us capture you quietly, it'd save us the trouble and be easier for us."

Knowing full well he'd never comply, Rei declared.

His words were directed at the men, but also at Keep.

Rei and Set were here, Fron and Brass were here, and even former guerrilla members like Sergio, Johanna, and Deets were present.

There was absolutely no way they could escape safely from here.

With no escape route, fighting here was an option, but given the forces gathered here, it would be nothing short of suicide.

Given that, surrendering quietly would mean avoiding injury... Logically, that was indeed the best choice.

But for these men, surrendering meekly without a fight would wound their pride. ...Even if it was pride in the wrong sense.

For Keep, it was simpler: he could never bring himself to surrender to mere adventurers.

Pride played a part, but above all, it was a matter of self-preservation.

Today's duel had already dragged Keep's noble status through the mud, covered it in filth, no, practically drenched it in excrement.

Even as things stand, punishment from his family is certain. If he were caught attempting to attack the Baroness Sysne's household now, disinheritance would be the least of his worries.

In fact, Keep expected disinheritance to be the lightest possible punishment.

As Keep's subordinates, the knights would face the same punishment... no, given their failure to prevent this situation, they might face even harsher penalties.

In other words, those present absolutely could not pretend to obey Rei's orders.

"...Damn..."

Amidst the silence following Rei's words, that voice suddenly echoed around them.

The voice belonged to Keep.

Rei faced Keep, ready to swing the Death Scythe at any moment, and spoke.

"What's that supposed to mean? Could you please articulate yourself more clearly?"

"I said don't mess with me! You're the one who caused all this in the first place! If you hadn't interfered back then, I wouldn't be in this mess! If only you weren't here... If only you weren't here!"

As he spoke, Keep grew more agitated, swinging the spear in his hand repeatedly.

"Why the hell do people like you even exist? Dammit! Listen up, all of you! If we don't deal with these bastards here, it won't just be me ending up in the worst possible situation—it'll be all of you too! Make up your minds! We kill these bastards right here!"

Keep's cry, thick with resentment, made the knights each raise their longswords.

The other men, too, seemed swept up by Keep's words and readied their weapons.

Surveying the group, Rei sighed.

"Hey, Rei! They chose to fight us, so that's that! Just let us fight already!"

Fron shouted at Rei, his irritation plain, his expression eager to beat the hell out of the enemy.

Fron's speech might be masculine, but she's actually quite caring.

That much was clear from how she'd taken care of Rei when they first met, since Rei wasn't particularly skilled at dealing with people.

But right now, Fron seemed to have forgotten all about that kindness, her irritation plain for all to see.

It was understandable. He'd flaunted his status and power to get his hands on a woman, and when that failed, he'd forced a duel. Then, perhaps because he'd learned Rei's true identity, he'd repeatedly attacked the Baron Sisne household. When that too ended badly and he lost the duel, he'd led a large force to attack like this very same day.

It was only natural Fron despised him.

"Hmph. ...Rei, Fron's words are harsh, but at this point, I doubt such men would surrender quietly. If we waste time here, who's to say they won't pull something else? It's better to capture them all at once, don't you think?"

Rei sighed softly and nodded at Blasso, who held the Earthquake Hammer as he spoke.

To be honest, fighting the Keepers, who had no strong opponents, was a hassle, so it would have been best if they'd just surrendered quietly...

"Well, what can you do. All units, move out. Capture them. However, do not kill the Keep or the knights. The rest, killing them is acceptable in the worst case. Still, these are the ones who tried to raid a noble's mansion. Capturing them means they'll definitely be enslaved, so the reward will be somewhat higher."

Rei's words served as the signal, and the battle commenced.

The first to move, as expected, was Fron.

Sword in hand, he lunged at the nearest man.

The man held a spear, but the shaft was cleanly severed as Fron parried the thrust, simultaneously slicing through the man's body.

The wound was clearly fatal at a glance, but Fron paid him no mind and moved on to the next opponent.

For Fron, the feeling of dislike toward the men before her was stronger than the desire to recoup the money spent on selling them into slavery.

Elsewhere, fighting had also begun.

Fron was the only one who felt it was acceptable to kill his opponents. The women among the former guerrilla fighters, however, likely had their own reservations about this situation. While they didn't go so far as to kill, they beat their opponents mercilessly.

Despite possessing the skill to knock them unconscious with a single blow, she deliberately inflicted pain without finishing them off.

For the men, being repeatedly beaten without being able to lose consciousness must feel like hell itself.

The men vastly outnumbered them, nearly three times as many.

Despite this, it was the men who were suffering one-sidedly.

Glancing at the scene, Rei, Death Scythe in hand, turned to the Keep before him and spoke.

"...Well, it seems they've started. I suppose it's about time we did too."

As Rei took a step forward, Keep, whose earlier bravado seemed like a lie, took several steps back.

Following Keep's lead, the knights also retreated backward.

Rei took another step forward, and Keep and the others retreated... After repeating this several times, Rei opened his mouth in exasperation.

"Hey, how long do you plan to keep running? You're nobility, aren't you? At least show some noble pride for once. Besides, you can't run any further now, can you?"

Hearing Rei's words, Keep reflexively turned around.

The knights, drawn by Keep's movement, also looked behind them.

That was a fatal opening when facing someone like Rei.

Even so, Rei showed no sign of attacking. He simply let Keep and the others look behind them, forcing them to realize that the men he had brought were fighting Fron, Blasso, and the former members of the mobile unit, and that they couldn't retreat any further.

"You understand you can't escape now, right? Well then, shall we get this over with? I'm not one for wasting time either. ...Set, you take care of those three knights over there."

"Grr!"

Set, who had been advancing in step with Rei as he took a step forward, growled at Rei's words and turned his gaze toward the knights.

The knights, naturally facing a Rank A monster for the first time, flinched under the pressure emanating from Set... yet they still guarded their positions, swords drawn, facing Set.

(Huh.)

Rei, who had assumed the knights would abandon the keep and flee, wore a look of surprise at this unexpected turn of events.

"Well, do your best. I'll handle things over here."

Rei approached, Death Scythe in hand.

Keeping, likely trying to deter him, swung the spear he held.

...However, it was not the movement of someone trained in spear techniques; it was merely a reckless, aimless flailing.

Against an opponent unfamiliar with combat, such an approach might have been difficult to counter. But against Rei, it was utterly meaningless... in fact, it only created a huge opening.

Ray swung the Death Scythe, striking the spear.

In that instant, a shrill metallic clang rang out as the spear was knocked from Keep's hand, flying straight upward.

"Gyaaaah! It hurts! It hurts! It hurts!"

Keep crouched on the spot, wailing in agony from the impact of the spear striking the Death Scythe.

Watching this, he extended the hand not gripping the Death Scythe... and in the next instant, the spear flung skyward was clutched in that hand.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

When Rei turned his gaze toward the sound of Set's growl, he saw the three knights lying unconscious, struck down by water balls and wind arrows.

Whether they understood Rei's words before the battle—that it would be best to avoid killing them if possible—the knights were injured, but none fatally.

Many of the men led by Keep, too, had fallen one after another, either unconscious or dead on the ground, the difference in skill starkly evident.

"There. You're finished. Surrender quietly. The men you brought are nearly all wiped out."

Hearing Rei's words, Keep, who had been clutching his arm and wailing, frantically turned his gaze behind him.

The scene unfolding there matched Rei's words exactly.

All of his knight escorts had already lost consciousness, and despite the large number of men, most of them were already lying on the ground.

"See? Your scheme failed. Just come quietly and let us capture you."

"...Shut up! What grudge do you hold against me to do this?! To defy me, a noble?! Damn you!"

Drenching his fear of Rei in his own hatred, he drew a dagger from his robe and lunged forward.

He flung the dagger's sheath aside and thrust the blade toward Rei's face... toward the spot not covered by his Dragon Robe.

Keep himself was completely unaware, but this was actually the best option available to him at that moment.

The Dragon Robe, crafted from dragon hide and scales, boasted extremely high defense.

Indeed, a mere weapon couldn't even scratch it.

However, this protection only applied to the areas covered by the Dragon Robe itself. It did not extend to a magical barrier or shield, nor did it cover Rei's body itself like the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

In other words, areas not covered by the Dragon Robe—such as the face and abdomen—remained vulnerable to normal weapon attacks.

A single blow to the face, in particular, would be fatal.

Though...

"That's if it hits me, though."

With that single remark, he waited for the keep and swung the Death Scythe.

Two silver lines sliced through the night air.

The next instant, Keep's shoulders and arms were severed, flying into the night sky before falling back to earth, illuminated by the moonlight.
