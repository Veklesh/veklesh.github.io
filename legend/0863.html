Walking down a back alley not far from the library in Gilm, Rei soon spotted the building he was looking for.

"Well then, Set."

"Grr."

Responding to Rei's call, Set purred in understanding before lying down in a spot hidden from view, tucking his body away.

Lying there like that, if you ignored its size and the season, it might look like a cat curled up.

...Though it was a cat with wings sprouting from its back.

Glancing at Set like that, Rei opened the door to the building.

Instantly, air warm—no, hot—no comparison to the cold outside enveloping Rei.

Furrowing his brow slightly at the air, he confirmed no one was visible before calling out loud enough to be heard even in the back of the shop.

"Pamidor, are you there?"

"Yeah, I'm here! ...What is it, Rei?"

Emerging from the back of the shop as he spoke was a man with a face that, at first glance, could only be described as criminal.

Not only did he have a vicious-looking face, but his physique was so muscular he could probably lift someone like Rei with one hand. He looked more like a thief than most actual thieves.

Sweat was pouring from his forehead, probably from working as a blacksmith, making him look even more intimidating.

The heat that poured out when he opened the door was likely due to that.

Knowing exactly what kind of person Pamidor was, Rei didn't let his appearance faze him as he called out.

"What do you mean, 'What'?" he asked. "I'm a customer here, after all. ...Is Kumito not here?"

"Kumito's out. What's this? For someone who says they're a customer, you came looking for Kumito, not me."

"No, that's not it. I was just asking."

"Fine. So what's this about? Kumi told me you were back, but you're not the type to come back just to say hello, are you?"

At Pamidor's words, Rei shrugged slightly and drew the magic sword from Mistling.

Well, since the blade was broken in half, it should probably be called the original demon sword split into two.

Anyway, Rei handed the demon sword to Pamidor.

"Hey, this thing... it's quite something, isn't it?"

"Yeah, I know that. It's a magic sword used by an S-rank adventurer, so it's not just any old thing."

"Rank S!? Where from?"

"The Bestia Empire."

Hearing Rei's blunt statement, Pamidor immediately recalled a name in his mind.

"Immovable Noise, huh?"

"Yeah. I had some business in the Bestia Empire, and that's when I fought him."

"...So, the fact that the demon sword's broken means... you won?"

"Nah, more like I was let off."

"Huh. So even you couldn't do anything against a Rank S opponent?"

"I managed to put up some resistance, but... if he hadn't backed off..."

I probably would have lost.

Pamidor inwardly admired Rei for swallowing those words.

Rei was indeed strong. So strong, in fact, that ordinary adventurers stood no chance against him.

Pamidor himself was just a blacksmith, but as a skilled one, he understood Rei's strength.

He'd also heard about Ray's strength during the Azoth Trading Company incident, though he hadn't witnessed it firsthand.

Given that level of strength, Pamidor understood how difficult it must be for him to admit his own weakness.

"Still, to destroy that immovable magic sword? That alone feels like quite the feat."

He stared intently at the fragment of the magic sword in his hand.

Seeing the blade snapped clean in two, he grasped the sheer power of the blow that had struck it.

"For now... I'm still just a blacksmith. To forge a demon sword, I'd need help from an alchemist or a mage knowledgeable in such matters. What do you say? Leave it with me for now? Once this job wraps up, I plan to visit an alchemist."

"Hmm... Yeah, that's true. I'd actually like to come along too, but I was thinking of heading to the dungeon soon."

While responding to Pamidor's words, Rei also found the idea of meeting an alchemist appealing.

Being a frontier region, Gilm attracted many alchemists seeking rare materials.

Of course, compared to the Bestia Empire, which actively promoted alchemy as a national policy, or Ozos, known as the Mage City, their level was probably lower.

Still, Rei wanted to meet a proper alchemist.

...Though, speaking of unqualified alchemists, he had encountered one recently during the Azoth Trading Company incident.

"Hmm, I won't force you. In that case, I'll handle things here. Drop by sometime tomorrow or later."

"Got it. I'll stop by after I get back from the dungeon. Count on me."

Despite his villainous appearance, Pamidor had a strong sense of loyalty.

Knowing he was skilled, Rei had no hesitation entrusting him with the matter of the demon sword.

Though, that was largely because the cursed sword was merely a side item he'd acquired.

If it had been something like the Death Scythe... well, not quite that, but if it had been something he used regularly, like the Thorn Spear, the Waterflow Dagger, or Nebula's Eye, he would have definitely gone to see the alchemist with Pamidor.

And if it had been an Estanoll creation, like the Dragon Robe... he would have headed straight for the Imperial Capital of the Bestia Empire or Ozos, knowing it couldn't be fixed here.

As for Noise's cursed sword, since it was purely a chance acquisition, Rei felt it was good enough as long as it worked.

"Alright, leave it to me. A magic sword used by a Rank S adventurer? Sounds like a good job for me too."

Hearing Pamidor's voice behind him, Rei stepped outside.

"Grr?"

Spotting Rei emerge from the shop, Set approached, purring as if to ask, "How was it?"

Rei patted Set's head and urged him onward.

"It was a long shot, but he said he'd show it to an alchemist. Anyway, business is done... Let's head to the dungeon. No guarantee we can clear it today, though."

"Gurrrrr!"

Set growled happily at Rei's words.

He understood he might not be able to enter the dungeon himself, but he was still thrilled at the prospect of flying through the sky with Rei.

Walking through town with Set like this, he naturally drew attention wherever they went, but they eventually reached the gate.

"Hey, Rei. Set too? Heading out of town?"

The guard who had processed Rei's return to Gilm called out to her.

The busiest time for those passing through the gate had already passed, so he was probably bored now.

In truth, it wasn't that no one was processing to leave the gate, but there were only a handful of people present.

"To that dungeon I heard about the other day..."

Ugh. The guard grimaced at that.

"You really are a curious one. I told you what kind of place it is, didn't I?"

"Yeah, I gathered all kinds of other info too. Still, I just want to go see it once."

"...Well, you are a high-rank adventurer with a reputation. My warnings are probably pointless... Be careful. Or maybe not even that necessary in that dungeon."

"Not really. Dungeons are places where you never know what might happen."

He handed the guild card he'd taken from Mistling and the familiar necklace he'd removed from Set's neck to the guard, requesting the necessary procedures.

With so few people around, the process wrapped up quickly.

"Well then, good luck."

"You guys must be struggling out there in the cold too, but hang in there."

Calling out to the guards enduring the bitter cold where snow could fall at any moment, Rei exited Gilm with Set.

Normally, they would have been asked not to fly near the gate, but fortunately, there was hardly anyone around now.

"Set, let's go then."

"GRRRRRRRR!"

At Rei's words from his back, Set let out a high-pitched cry. After a few strides, he flapped his wings and soared into the sky.

It was less like flying and more like dashing through the sky.

The few guards near the gate were speechless at the sight of Set, utterly captivated.

"Set, the dungeon is over there. Head straight ahead."

Heading in the direction Rei indicated, Set flapped his wings.

With each flap of his wings, he covered a considerable distance.

By flapping them continuously, his speed increased even further.

Normally, at this time of year, the sky above would be bitterly cold. It wouldn't be strange to start shivering instantly, but Rei, protected by his dragon robe, and Set, being a griffin, found this level of cold completely unproblematic.

Watching the scenery below rush past, Rei was reminded once again of just how incredible Set truly was.

When traveling on the ground, one would typically follow the roads. However, roads do not stretch out in a straight line.

They detour around mountains, detour around valleys, and detour around rivers until they reach a narrower stretch.

You could even call them winding.

But when Set flies, none of that matters. He can fly straight ahead.

Moreover, its speed was incomparably faster than traveling on the ground.

"Ah, Set. Turn right at that pile of stones on the road."

"Gurrrr!"

Hearing Rei's words upon seeing the pile of stones placed as a marker to veer off the road, Set growled and changed direction.

Continuing straight off the road, they soon saw several tents pitched on the ground. This was their destination, the dungeon.

The tents were likely used by the soldiers stationed here.

What would normally take several hours by carriage was reached in about ten minutes after taking off from Gilm, demonstrating just how fast Set could fly.

And that was while enjoying a leisurely flight through the air, not even at full speed.

"Tents, huh. ...Staying in tents this time of year must be pretty rough unless they're magic tents or something."

With a magic tent, you wouldn't feel the cold outside air.

But ordinary tents certainly didn't have that capability, meaning proper cold-weather gear was essential even for sleeping.

Several people near the tent, gathered around a campfire, noticed something—or someone—approaching from the sky.

The soldiers reflexively grabbed their bows, but upon recognizing the flying creature as a griffin, they immediately lowered them.

...That said, they didn't leave their bows on the ground. If the approaching griffin wasn't the Set they knew, they'd be ready to intercept it immediately.

Of course, if it truly was a griffin other than Set approaching, the bows and arrows the soldiers carried would be useless... or rather, they'd only provoke its wrath.

Seeing Rei riding on Set's back as he descended to the ground, the soldiers finally relaxed their guard.

Facing those soldiers, Rei dismounted from Set and spoke.

"I'm Rei the adventurer. I'm here to clear the dungeon..."

"Rei!?"

Before he could finish, a voice called out.

Turning toward the source of the voice, he saw a stern-looking, bearded middle-aged man.

It was Ranga, who commanded Gilm's guards and was currently leading soldiers here to watch for monsters emerging from the dungeon.

Ranga had just stepped out of a tent, perhaps taking a break. Rei smiled and spoke up.

"Long time no see."

"Grrr!"

As if echoing Rei's words, Set growled.

Finding the scene amusing, Ranga also smiled and raised his hand slightly.

"Long time no see. From what you said earlier, I guess you're here to conquer the dungeon?"

Ranga's words drew a nod from Rei.

"I heard it's a shallow dungeon, so I thought I'd give it a try. If we can clear it today, great. If not, I still want to experience what kind of place it is. It's not off-limits or anything, right?"

Rei had heard adventurers could normally enter dungeons, but he asked just to be sure.

Langa nodded in agreement at Rei's words.

"Of course it's fine. But I've heard this dungeon doesn't pay well... If you're okay with that, though."

"I heard about that when gathering info on the dungeon, so it's fine. Alright, let's get started... Oh, Set will have to wait outside. Is that okay?"

"Huh? Oh, I see. That dungeon entrance is probably too narrow for Set to get through. Yeah, that's fine. Just... sometimes monsters come out of the dungeon, and when that happens..."

Understanding what Ranga meant, Rei gave a small nod in response.

"When monsters appear, I'll have Set fight too. He's got that covered without me having to say anything."

"Gururu!"

Leave it to me! Set growled, and not only Ranga but the other soldiers breathed a sigh of relief.

Living in Gilm, everyone naturally knew about Set.

That's precisely why they were relieved that things wouldn't get too hectic outside for a while.

The monsters emerging from the dungeon weren't particularly strong. No, they were clearly weak, but you never knew when they'd appear... Plus, being on the frontier, monsters attacking from the surrounding area were actually tougher than those coming from the dungeon.

They understood that with Set around, they could rest easy.
