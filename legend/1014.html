Zuboz suddenly let out a scream.

But the disturbance wasn't just that scream.

Rather, it was within Zubo's own body that a major change was occurring.

"Guhh, gah gah gah... Gah!"

Zubozu screamed, groaning in pain.

The roots stretched across his body, visible on his face, arms, and the backs of his hands, writhed. The light they emitted shifted from a dull glow to an increasingly blinding radiance.

This was clearly a different reaction than before, and at the same time, Zubo was visibly suffering.

"What is this? ...A runaway reaction?"

Holding the Death Scythe, Rei murmured, recalling that Zuboz had said he wanted to make a few more adjustments before drinking the liquid in the glass bottle.

In other words, the medicine Zuboz drank had gone haywire due to insufficient adjustment, and the result was the Zuboz now writhing in agony.

Thinking that way, it wasn't entirely unreasonable.

As Rei watched, Zoboz's transformation continued.

His body kept swelling. His height, which had reached about two meters after first drinking the liquid, now stretched to nearly three meters.

Despite this, his muscle mass hadn't decreased—it had increased. His entire body was now covered in dense, solid muscle.

Naturally, the sheer size of his body tore all his clothes to shreds. All that remained were scraps barely covering his waist.

The transformation didn't stop there. With his clothes torn away, his entire body was exposed, revealing roots spreading not just across his face and hands, but all over his body.

Visibly writhing within his body and emitting an intense glow, these roots looked as if he were harboring dozens, even hundreds, of long, serpentine creatures like snakes or earthworms inside him.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

A scream... or rather, a cry of agony that could hardly be called a roar.

Along with the scream, frothy saliva dripped from his mouth, and tears of blood streamed from his eyes.

Zubo's consciousness must have already vanished completely. There was no glimmer of intelligence in his eyes.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Screaming as he endured the agony in his own body, Zubozo suddenly shifted position in an instant.

His movement was clearly faster than before.

Simultaneously, his previously predictable movements had become erratic.

The fact that his movements were not optimized for attack meant there was waste in them.

But it was also true that this very wastefulness concealed the true intent of his movements. Precisely because of this, he appeared to Rei as a far more difficult opponent than before.

"Damn it."

Rei activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour once more and readied his Death Scythe.

But... the moment Rei activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, Zubozo forcibly changed the direction he kicked off the ground, altering his course.

The destination of the half-runaway Zubozu was Paul, positioned not far from Rei.

He was the one immobilized, his body wrapped in thorns created by the thorn spear.

"For someone running wild, you're pretty sharp!"

Using the heightened physical abilities granted by activating the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, Ray moved at incredible speed to stop Zuboz from dealing with the thorns binding Paul.

That was the thought as Rei pursued Zubo...

"Gaaah!"

"What!?"

Seeing Zuboz open its mouth wide—wide enough to fit a human head inside—Ray felt a bad feeling.

He thought it might be preparing to breathe fire and incinerate the thorns in one go.

However, Zuboz's action was completely unexpected.

Because instead of breathing fire from its wide-open mouth, Zuboz pointed its teeth—sharp enough to be called fangs—directly at the pole.

Using the heightened physical abilities granted by the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, Ray closed the distance in an instant, determined to stop Zuboz.

His speed was so great that an ordinary person would barely be able to see Rei.

Zuboz probably hadn't anticipated Rei's speed either.

No, if this had been Zuboz before it went berserk, having gathered information on Rei, it might have been able to respond.

But the current Zuboz, while exhibiting some semblance of intelligence in its actions, was fundamentally in a state of rampage.

That's precisely why the shock Rei delivered the instant he closed the distance and attempted to bite off Paul's head was completely unforeseen.

Hot... no, burning hot. The instant he felt it, Zoboz was sent flying, scorched by the blow from the handle of Rei's Death Scythe.

"Guh!"

The cry that rose was Paul's.

With his Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour deployed, Rei could control the heat to a certain extent.

But conversely, it was only to a certain extent; it wasn't perfect.

Moreover, this time he needed to inflict even a little damage on Zubo, deliberately exercising almost no control over that aspect.

And since Zuboz was trying to bite off Paul's head, Paul was naturally close to where Zuboz had been blown away.

As a result, the intense heat from the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour that hit Zuboz also engulfed Paul, who was right there.

"Whoops, sorry about that."

Realizing this, Rei quickly moved away from Paul.

Rei had been beside Paul for less than three seconds.

Even so, in those mere three seconds, multiple burns had formed on Paul.

It could be considered a silver lining for Paul that the thorns covering most of his body were the effect of the Thorn Spear.

Thanks to these thorns absorbing the heat from Rei's Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, Paul's burns were clearly less severe.

Without these thorns, the number of burns would undoubtedly have been several times greater.

"Uoooooh!"

Paul had barely managed to pull away when, in the next instant, another scream echoed through the area.

Rei turned his gaze toward the source of the scream and saw Zuboz tearing off Dias's right arm, leather armor and all.

Rei had just blown away the Zuboz that had tried to devour Paul, but the repelled Zuboz must have kicked off the ground and headed straight for its next prey.

And the one chosen was Dias.

(But why Dias?)

Vihera, enraged, swung the claws extending from her gauntlets at the Zuboz that had torn off the right arm of the opponent it had fought.

The claws, formed from magic, sliced open the bloated belly of the Zoboz, but the wound healed before its innards could spill out.

Watching this, Rei scanned the surroundings once more... Seeing Byune and Azimov unharmed brought relief, yet also deepened his suspicion.

He wondered what had driven the rampaging Zuboz to attack them.

If it was targeting the easiest prey, then Byune and Azimov would be easier targets than Dias.

While Byune could handle combat to some extent, she was ultimately a thief by trade. Rei couldn't imagine her being able to do anything against the current Zubo.

As for Azimov, he was an alchemist who usually devoted himself entirely to research.

If the current Zuboz attacked him, he could take his life even more easily than Byune's.

In truth, at that moment, Dias—knocked unconscious by Vihera's blow—was the most defenseless target. But that was only true if Dias acted alone.

Indeed, Vihera was now attacking the Zuboz that had struck Dias.

For Vihera, who had thoroughly enjoyed the fight with Dias, Zuboz's actions were absolutely unforgivable.

(No, now is not the time for such thoughts. I must deal with Zuboz first. If I carelessly let him escape here, the slums themselves could become his feeding ground.)

Grasping the Death Scythe, he fixed his gaze on Zoboz, who was fighting Vihera.

Vihera's fists and feet struck Zuboz's bloated body again and again.

But that very swollen body seemed to possess considerable durability. While it absorbed the impact of the attacks, none of them were anything close to a fatal wound.

But Vihera is a top-tier fighter for a reason. Seeing his strikes go unanswered, and worse, witnessing the wounds from his gauntlet claws and leg blade attacks heal one after another, he had to realize half-hearted attacks were useless.

"It has a troublesome healing ability, doesn't it? ...But how about this!"

Zuboz crouched to dodge the swinging arm. The moment he saw it pass overhead, he kicked off the ground and closed the distance in an instant.

Though Zubo had grown massive, if it still possessed proper consciousness, that might be one thing. But now it was close to a rampage state.

In such a state, it couldn't move its body efficiently, inevitably making its attacks crude and imprecise.

...Well, even if Zubozu had consciousness, he had almost no combat experience, so his attack methods probably wouldn't have changed much from what they were now.

Even using combat reflexes, its fighting style lacked originality, just as Rei had said, making its attacks extremely predictable.

Against a skilled fighter like Vihera, there was no way he could possibly prevail.

And so, slipping into his guard, Vihera gently reached out and touched Zubo's body...

"How about this!?"

Through the palm that made contact, she unleashed a shockwave of magic power directly into the depths of Zubo's flesh.

It was a skill Vihera excelled at, known as the Infiltrating Magic Palm.

Even if the target wore armor, the attack sent the shock directly into their body, rendering the armor useless.

...Yes, normally, but.

"Huh!?"

Noticing the unusual sensation after unleashing the Immersion Palm, Vihera froze in surprise for just an instant. The next moment, she instantly kicked off the ground and leapt backward.

Then, an arm that could only be described as a massive, muscular arm swept past the spot where Vihera's body had been just an instant before.

(What was that sensation just now?)

Watching the arm pass right by her nose, Vihera was puzzled, but then she realized Zuboz hadn't taken any damage at all.

Meaning the Immersion Palm had failed to land.

Though puzzled by Zuboz's condition, Vihera's feet, which had leapt backward, landed on the ground. He kicked off again, moving forward.

He wanted to confirm whether the failure of the Immersion Palm was his own mistake... or if it simply had no effect on Zuboz.

As if spurred by Vihera's movement, Zuboz swung his fist again.

He wasn't swinging at Vihera because he recognized him; it was more like he was swinging at any moving target.

Even a fist capable of shattering rock with a single blow—a slow, blunt strike lacking speed or sharpness—wasn't difficult for Vihera to evade.

With fluid movement, he evaded the punch and slipped into Zuboz's guard.

Then, touching the now completely exposed torso...

"Hah!"

With a sharp exhalation, he unleashes the Immersion Palm.

For an ordinary opponent, the shock delivered directly into their body would likely shatter their internal organs.

But this strike, infused with even more magic power than before, vanished as if absorbed by something before the shock could even generate within his body.

"The Immersion Palm still doesn't work!?"

A fist swung once more toward the motionless Vihera.

He lightly leapt, gently landing on the fist coming toward him. Riding the momentum of Zubo's swing, he successfully created distance.

"Back off, Vihera! Your skills won't work on that thing!"

Rei's voice, of course, reached Vihera as well.

He also knew that Rei couldn't launch a supporting attack precisely because he was near Zubo. Yet, Vihera had no intention of relinquishing this fight. He closed the distance again with Zubo, who had momentarily pulled back.

Initially, his actions stemmed from an unforgivable feeling towards Zubo, who had harmed Dias, who had fought him well. However, the unexpected strength of his opponent had already sparked an obsession within Vihera to fight Zubo.

(Forget the power and speed of a single strike—the way he attacks, with no logic or technique whatsoever, is no different from a Cyclops. Though his defense and recovery are on a whole different level compared to a Cyclops.)

Closing the distance, Vihera's mind raced.

Direct blows were ineffective, and while claw attacks could slice through skin and flesh, Zuboz's sheer size meant they never proved fatal. Worse, it possessed astonishing healing abilities.

Her trump card, the Demon-Infused Palm, was inexplicably ineffective.

(But...)

Driven by the thrill of battle, Vihera approached with a seductive smile. Zuboz swung his right fist again.

She dodged the blow... up to that point, her actions were the same as before, but what followed was different.

Dodging Zuboz's fist, larger than her own face, Vihera immediately reached out.

Instead of stopping the fist's motion, she deflected its trajectory as if parrying it... and timed it perfectly with the swing of Zubo's fist, applying force to the hand making contact.

The next instant, Zuboz slammed into the ground as if he'd thrown himself there, sending a thunderous roar echoing around them. And then...

"Sorry, but I've got this kind of attack too...!"

Grasping Zoboz's arm as he lay on the ground, she instantly applied leverage to his elbow... A bone-crunching sound, smaller than when Zoboz had been slammed into the ground seconds before, yet still evoking pain, echoed throughout the area.
