"Damn it!"

That curse echoed through the room, and in the next instant, the sound of shattering glass rang out.

But the man who had shouted—Gaiska—didn’t seem the least bit relieved by that.

Because the situation he currently found himself in was that dire.

That was the money Gaiska had set aside to hire the Black Wolf.

He’d scraped together money from all sorts of places, and when that still wasn’t enough, he’d even put up the Marquis of Seisol’s mansion in Anesis as collateral to secure the funds…

“Why? Why does Father know about this? Could it be… has someone betrayed me? No, there aren’t many people who know about this in the first place, and there’s no way Deotres would betray me!”

Gaiska’s cry echoed through the room.

...Yes, the fact that he had secretly used the mansion as collateral to borrow money—something he absolutely had to keep hidden—was, for some reason, known to his father.

It was unclear exactly how his father had found out, but naturally, once he learned of such a thing, he couldn’t just let it slide.

Especially since this mansion was prepared in a city ruled by a figure leading the Anesis Noble Faction; if it were to fall into the wrong hands, the reputation of the Marquis of Seisol’s family would be dragged through the mud.

It was precisely because he understood this that Gaiska had tried to keep it a secret from others… but in the end, the only outcome awaiting him was house arrest.

And even that confinement is only due to the current situation; once the party—attended by the majority of the Noble Faction—is over, the head of the Seisole Marquis family will undoubtedly take disciplinary action against Gaiska.

Whether that punishment would mean remaining under house arrest, being expelled from the Seisole Marquis family, or—in the worst-case scenario—being killed,

Gaiska didn’t know what would happen, but no matter how it turned out, he couldn’t imagine it leading to a favorable outcome for him.

“What should I do? What should I do…?”

Normally, if he were unsure, Deotres would offer advice, but Deotres was nowhere to be seen here.

In other words, he had to decide everything on his own.

“Damn it, what should I do… This is all Rei’s fault to begin with. If that bastard Rei had shown any respect for the nobility, none of this would have happened. Damn it… Damn it!”

He tries to throw another glass, but since he’d already thrown one earlier, no one has cleaned it up or placed a new glass in front of Gaesca, so there’s nothing there to throw.

If there were a servant here, he could have taken it out on them… but naturally, no one would even think of approaching Gaiska right now.

They knew exactly what kind of person Gaiska was, so they wouldn’t dare try to approach him so easily.

And now that Gaiska’s father had arrived, Gaiska was no longer the most powerful person in the mansion.

With her father here—though he’s not in the mansion right now, having gone to the party—if she were to assault the servants with punches and kicks like she used to, it would be a fatal mistake.

“What should I do… what should I do…”

Gaesca muttered words similar to those of Count Caseres, who had attacked Set, but just like Count Caseres, no help would come. …Instead, what arrived was…

He heard a knock on the door, and Gaiska glanced toward it, looking as if he didn’t know what had happened.

He couldn’t imagine anyone coming to see him while he was under house arrest, and for a moment, Gaesca even wondered if the knocking was just his imagination.

But… a few seconds later, the sound of knocking came from the door again.

It was clearly the sound of knocking coming from there; it was definitely not just his imagination.

Who on earth would come to see him in this situation?

As he pondered this, he decided that things couldn’t possibly get any worse than they already were, and Gaiska stood up.

Her legs wavered for a moment, but she told herself it was just the alcohol and made her way to the door.

When he opened the door, he saw the face standing there and opened his mouth in astonishment.

“Why is someone like you here?”

“Heh heh, don’t say that. I heard Lord Gaiska was in a terrible situation, so I rushed right over.”

The man laughing as he spoke was the intermediary Gaiska had contacted regarding the Black Wolf.

Even seeing the man smiling like that, Gaiska—who, to be honest, had absolutely no idea why he was there—remained wary of him.

And rightly so. The only reason the mediator had been cooperating with Gaiska was because of the reward he stood to receive from the Black Wolf.

Although he had already handed over a portion as an advance, it would be extremely difficult to come up with the amount due as a success fee after the Black Wolf killed Rei.

If he’s come to see me for some reason, it’s undoubtedly regarding a breach of contract.

Under normal circumstances, he’d say he couldn’t care less about a contract with a man like this, but in his current situation, he couldn’t afford to act that way.

Gaiska may be in decent physical shape, but that’s only to a certain extent.

As someone from the underworld, the middleman was undoubtedly used to rough business.

Given that, if the man in front of him were to attack, there’s nothing Gaiska could do.

Normally, Deotres would be here at a time like this… but Deotres isn’t by Gaiska’s side right now.

Gaesca would never admit it, but the fact that he instinctively took a few steps back was likely because he felt fear toward the mediator.

Seeing Gaiska like that, the mediator shot him a contemptuous glance, though he took care not to let it show on his face.

To the mediator, it seemed ridiculous that a man who had looked down on him so thoroughly up until now was now trying to suppress his fear right in front of him.

“Are you saying you came here for my sake? To begin with, my father effectively controls this mansion. How on earth did you manage to get in here?”

“Well, given my usual circumstances, I have various connections and methods at my disposal.”

The mediator’s words were the kind he normally would never have let slip by.

And yet, here he was, actually showing up in my room—this room tucked away deep within the mansion.

By any reasonable standard, the likelihood of being assassinated if something went wrong was extremely high.

But Gaiska either didn’t realize this, or simply wasn’t aware of the danger; he didn’t seem particularly concerned.

“I see. So, what do you want with me? Since you’ve come here, you must know what kind of situation I’m in right now, don’t you?”

“Yes. Father found out about the matter with us, and I’ve been placed under house arrest. …But is that really okay?”

“What?”

At the words of the man acting as an intermediary, Gaiska wore a puzzled expression.

He didn’t know what the man was getting at. He didn’t know… but he must have sensed something ominous in the air.

“Lord Gaiska, you may have been a bit reckless. But you acted that way because you judged it to be in the best interests of the Marquis of Seisol’s household, didn’t you? If that’s the case, you deserve praise, not a scolding… certainly not a scolding in front of your servants, followed by an order of house arrest. In fact, you should be commended and told you did well.”

“Well…”

By any reasonable standard, what the mediator was saying made no sense at all.

After all, using the mansion—especially one located in a major city like Anesis—as collateral to borrow money is something that could never be forgiven.

But for Gaiska, who was currently cornered, the intermediary’s words were like sweet poison.

Even knowing that, it was like a tempting fruit he simply couldn’t resist reaching for.

“That’s right. You’re absolutely correct. I acted with the best interests of the Marquis of Seisol in mind. My father should have acknowledged that.”

He stated it with conviction.

What Gaiska had done was, if handled poorly… no, if it became public knowledge, something that would tarnish the name of the Seisole Marquis family.

But he immediately seized upon the words of the mediator standing before him—words implying that he himself was not at fault.

(Heh, this is easy. Even someone like this is of the Marquis’s bloodline. That means there are plenty of ways to use him. I wasn’t sure what to do when the head of the family told me to go into seclusion… but he really is something else.)

The fact that this mediator came to see Gaiska wasn’t, of course, entirely his own decision…

Naturally, this intermediary also belongs to the organization behind the scenes.

That’s only natural, considering his ties to the Black Wolf.

The man who serves as the mediator’s superior in that organization went to see Gaiska and ordered him to somehow persuade him and bring him back.

At first, given Gaiska’s personality, I thought persuading him would be absolutely impossible… but once I actually met and spoke with him, the persuasion was completed far more easily than I’d expected.

To be honest, I was even a little taken aback by how easily it went.

In any case, the intermediary successfully made contact with Gaiska and managed to persuade him.

All that was left was to take Gaiska to the location his boss had specified...

“Lord Gaiska, how many people are still in this mansion? I’d like you to go into hiding for the time being.”

“Hide? Are we leaving this mansion?”

“Yes. We need to convince my father of just how much you’ve done for the Seisol Marquis family. But if you’re standing right in front of him at that moment, the Marquis of Seisol simply won’t be able to honestly admit his mistakes.”

At those words, Gaiska felt a momentary pang of doubt.

But before Gaiska could think anything through, the mediator spoke again.

“Come, let’s leave here as soon as possible. That way, the Marquis of Seisole will come to understand just how wonderful Lord Gaiska has been. Whatever we do, we must first bring the Marquis of Seisole back to his senses.”

“Is that so? …I see.”

Gaiska was about to doubt again, but he chose to believe what suited him best.

This was because Gaiska harbored a desire to believe his actions were right, and he was easily swayed by that sentiment.

“Yes, of course. So, getting back to my earlier question, do you know how many people are still in the mansion? I’m fairly confident in my skills, but even so, if there are a lot of them… I suppose that wouldn’t be a problem for the Black Wolf, though.”

“I need the Black Wolf to kill Rei to prove that my actions were correct. I can’t burden him with something this trivial. …So, about the number of people. Since Father is here, there are more than usual. Though, the skilled ones are probably heading to the party as Father’s guards.”

“Hmm. So there are a lot of people, but no skilled fighters, is that it? In that case, maybe I can manage…”

Gaiska felt a slight sense of unease toward the mediator who muttered this.

Perhaps this man intends to kill those remaining in the mansion.

It was a strange thought, but Gaiska—who routinely abused the servants and, in some cases, wouldn’t stop beating and kicking them even after they’d lost consciousness—was actually worried about such a thing.

Of course, it wasn’t out of concern for the servants’ lives… but rather because he judged that it would be a major problem if any of his father’s subordinates—the head of the Seisol Marquis family—were to die.

“Are you going to kill them?”

“Huh? Oh, no. Of course I wouldn’t do such a thing. If it might put Lord Gaiska in a bad position, I couldn’t possibly do something like that.”

“...I see.”

At the man’s words, Gaiska seemed unaware of it himself, but a look of relief flashed across his face.

The mediator wore a servile smile, but inwardly, he was clearly irritated that things had turned into such a hassle.

He had thought this would be a simple job, but he never imagined it would turn into such a hassle.

Still, since he had to deal with the situation as it was, he began to devise a plan to escape the Marquis of Seisol’s estate without encountering the servants… or the guards and knights.
