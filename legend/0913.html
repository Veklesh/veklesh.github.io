Rei nodded in response to the man who suddenly stepped down from the carriage, stowing the Death Scythe into its Misty Ring.

His ostentatiously luxurious attire made it easy to guess he was likely a noble.

Still, Rei's demeanor remained unchanged.

After all, the only noble he usually encountered was the intimidating Daskar, so he simply couldn't feel intimidated by the noble standing before him.

His recent clash with Keep was likely influencing his thoughts too.

...Though, having met nobles like Maruka, it wasn't that he disliked all nobles.

Still, it was an undeniable fact that nobles didn't hold a particularly favorable place in Rei's mind.

"Ah, I'm heading to Graciall in the Duchy of Quent on a request."

"...I see. Well, sorry to spring this on you suddenly. Actually, I'm heading to Graciall myself right now. It's unfortunate we both end up traveling during this season."

Rei felt a hint of surprise at the man who told him this with a smile.

He'd assumed trouble was brewing again, but from their exchange, the man seemed friendly.

(This is unexpected. Well, I certainly don't think all nobles are like that. Actually, if all nobles were like that, the Mireana Kingdom would be finished.)

The image of a country where every noble was a playboy flashed through his mind, and he shook his head frantically.

Seeing Rei like this, the man looked puzzled and spoke up.

"Hm? Is something wrong?"

"No, nothing. More importantly, since you're heading to Graciall, does that mean this road definitely leads there?"

"Mm. Though it'll still take a while. This road definitely leads to Graciall."

Rei breathed a sigh of relief at the man's words.

He had suspected it was correct, but this was still a road Rei was traveling for the first time. Some anxiety was only natural.

"What's wrong? You don't seem lost or anything."

"Ah, no. I know the route well enough."

To be precise, he had a map, but considering its rarity, he couldn't just casually mention it to people, so he glossed over it.

"It's just... as you can see, I'm traveling with Set... flying through the air on a griffin. It's just different from moving around on the ground."

At those words, the man must have noticed Set lying down a short distance away from Rei. He gave a look of understanding... but the next moment, his expression turned puzzled as he spoke.

"Well... to my eyes, it looks like he's lying down in the snow..."

"Yeah, it looks that way to me too."

"...Is that okay?"

Rei tilted his head and spoke to the man who asked for confirmation.

"What is?"

"Well, lying down in the snow like that. Won't the griffin catch a cold?"

"...If it catches a cold from a little snow, it can't be called a Rank A monster."

"I see. So that's how it is."

Rei felt somewhat thrown off by the man's expression of understanding at her explanation.

Anyway, if this road led to the Duchy of Quent, there was no reason for him to stay here any longer.

"I'm glad you confirmed this road is the right one. If you're heading to Glacial, we might even meet up over there."

"Hmm, I wonder about that. Unfortunately, I won't have much freedom to move around over there. Still, well... I suppose if luck is on our side, we might meet... I'll keep that in mind."

It wasn't hard to sense there was more to that statement.

Still, that didn't mean Rei intended to pry any further.

The man before him seemed like a decent enough noble. At least, that was how he appeared to Rei.

But that was all it was.

If they were attacked by a monster or something here, he'd gladly lend a hand.

But if asked if he wanted to stay with Rei and protect him constantly, Rei would say no.

After all, Rei was currently under commission from Maruka.

Leaving that request unattended to travel together all the way to Glacial made no sense.

Maruka had sent Rei ahead precisely because having him travel with her would only slow them down. ...Even though it was snowing.

Considering that, Rei felt no desire to actively involve himself with the noble before him.

(Besides...)

He shifted his gaze toward the area around the carriage.

There were the men who had first spoken with Rei.

They appeared relaxed, but he could tell they were keeping a close watch on him.

If Rei were to do anything strange, they would protect this nobleman, even at the cost of their own bodies.

Despite knowing just how strong Rei is, there was someone they were willing to go that far to protect.

If he has guards sworn to such loyalty, there's no need for Rei to intervene.

"Well, the carriage should be ready by now. I'll be heading off soon... If fate allows, we'll meet again."

"Hmm, yes. Fate, huh? To be perfectly honest, I can't think of anything more unreasonable."

Rei judged that if he listened any longer, he'd inevitably get dragged into the man's problems. He gave the man a light wave and approached Set, who was lying sprawled across the snowfield.

"Grrr?"

Set looked over, asking with his eyes if it was okay now. Rei nodded. Seeing this, Set stood up and turned his back to Rei.

Straddling Set's back, Rei turned his gaze back to the man and spoke.

"Well then, I'll be praying for your safe arrival at Glacial."

"Mm. You're out here in this snow too. Flying through it seems dangerous, but do your best."

After exchanging these few words, Set took a few running steps, flapped his wings, and soared skyward.

The griffin flying through the swirling snow evoked a somewhat fantastical feeling in those watching from the ground.

But that was only natural. While Set's presence had become routine in Gilm, he was fundamentally a Rank A monster—a creature that even high-ranking adventurers might only encounter once in a lifetime.

In terms of absolute safety, those who encountered Set this time could only call themselves fortunate.

"Crimson Rei and Set, huh... I hope we meet again in Glacial."

The man who saw Set and Rei off murmured softly.

"Young master. We should be going soon. Until now, having Crimson with us gave us peace of mind, but we don't know when pursuers might arrive. If possible, I'd like us to gain some distance while we still can."

"...You're right. Let's go then. Be extremely careful. My uncle's men are skilled fighters."

"Yes, rest assured. We'll deliver you to Gracial even if it costs us our lives."

"You fools. Your lives aren't something to be thrown away so easily. I won't permit you to die without my permission. Keep that in mind."

At the man's words, the others listening nearby smiled.

Their smiles were warm, filled with the resolve that they were the ones protecting this man.

"Hmph. Come on, let's go. ...Fortunately, the horses seem to have shaken off the tension and fear from facing Set and can walk again."

He said this as if to hide his embarrassment, then climbed into the carriage.

No, not as if to hide his embarrassment—he was genuinely hiding his embarrassment.

After all, the man's cheeks were faintly flushed as he climbed in.

Watching their master board the carriage, one man murmured while preparing for departure.

"Hey, maybe we should've tried to get Deep Crimson to join us after all?"

"I wish we could have... but from the looks of it, she seemed to be rushing to Graciall. She probably didn't have the time to lend us a hand."

"...But an adventurer like Shinku rushing like that? Did something happen in Graciall? If so, is it really safe to go there now? They might have already taken precautions."

"We've discussed this countless times. Graciall is the safest place right now. They won't find us there anytime soon. ...Until spring. If we can just hold out until spring and protect the young master, victory is ours. We'll make it work somehow."

"Hmph. Just a few months ago, there was civil war in the Bestir Empire. Don't tell me the next one will be in the Mireana Kingdom."

"It won't escalate to civil war. At worst, it'll end as a dispute among nobles. ...If we're lucky, it might just be a family feud, but don't count on it."

"Ugh. All this talk about royalists and whatnot, and this is the result? The nobles would be appalled if they heard."

"Hey, idiot. If the young master hears you—"

"Hey!"

As if they'd been listening the whole time, a voice came from the direction of the carriage.

From the man they were supposed to protect, their master.

The men froze at the perfect timing.

They wondered if he had heard their conversation.

Fortunately, however, the voice directed at them was not harsh.

"Hey, what's going on? Aren't we supposed to be leaving soon? Didn't you say we should cover as much distance as possible in this snow?"

"Ah, yes! We'll be on our way immediately!"

Relieved that their conversation hadn't been overheard, the men prepared to depart.

Several days after leaving Gilm, the weather fortunately hadn't turned too harsh, and since it was winter, they hadn't encountered any bandits. Rei was riding Set towards Glacial.

...Or rather, they had been heading there.

For now, before Rei's eyes lay a town surpassing Gilm in scale... or more accurately, a city.

"That's Graciall... right?"

"Guru?"

Gazing at the clear sky, a stark contrast to the weather of the past few days, Rei muttered.

He was fairly certain it was, yet he couldn't definitively declare the city before him to be Graciall. This hesitation likely stemmed from Rei's occasional, utterly hopeless sense of direction.

Still, based on what he'd heard from the first carriage he encountered and the villages and towns he'd passed through during the few days since leaving Gilm, he felt confident it was correct.

...There had been a few minor incidents along the way, but since no one had suffered any major harm, Rei decided it was fine for now.

"Still... this is pretty incredible."

Rei murmured, his gaze fixed on the wall encircling what appeared to be the city of Glacial.

While not quite as imposing as the walls of Gilm in the frontier, the structure before Rei was still remarkably tall, and its thickness was easily imaginable.

"They don't have vicious monsters like in the frontier, so having walls like this... probably means they're expecting human enemies."

Was it to guard against bandits, hostile nobles... or perhaps in case the Mireana Kingdom was attacked?

Rei couldn't tell which was the reason, but he understood one thing: the city of Graciall had exceptionally strong defenses.

"...If this weren't Graciall, that would be interesting in its own way. Anyway, let's go. Set."

"Gurrrrrrr!"

Responding to Rei's call, Set growled and began descending toward what they believed to be Graciall.

Those who were in the process of completing entry procedures for Graciall were startled by the sight.

Due to the winter season, few people were going through the entry procedures for Graciall.

But few didn't mean none; in a city with this many residents, people naturally came and went even in winter.

Unlike the frontier town of Gilm, this area was less prone to vicious monsters.

As these people stood shivering in the cold, waiting their turn to complete the procedures, a monster suddenly descended from the sky. It was inevitable that chaos would erupt.

"Ugh, waaahhhhh! A monster! A monster attack!"

"Contact the guards! No, the knights!"

"Evacuate us! Get us out of here!"

"It's coming! It's coming! It's coming!"

The people in line began to panic, on the verge of chaos, when suddenly—

"Quiet!"

A loud voice echoed throughout the area.

Strangely, it seeped into the chaos, and gradually, the commotion subsided.

"That is not a monster. To be precise, it is a familiar. It belongs to Gilm, the rank B adventurer known as Crimson. He is here on a direct request from Lord Maruka."

The man who declared this stood well over two meters tall, his body so muscular it could be described as being made entirely of muscle.
