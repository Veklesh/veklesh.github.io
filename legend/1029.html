About an hour after parting ways with the slave traders, the carriage had arrived at the forest.

Normally, it wouldn't have been surprising if it had taken several more hours, but fortunately, the carriage carrying Rei and the others was incomparably more high-performance than an ordinary carriage. Above all, the horses pulling it had been specially trained since they were young.

While they were undoubtedly animals in the sense of being horses, they possessed strength that made monsters like goblins no match for them.

With the carriage now stationary, everyone disembarked.

A few traveling merchants traded with the dark elf settlement, and some even came all the way from Gilm.

Naturally, this meant the ground had been trampled into a path.

Though the path was wide enough for Elena's carriage to pass, they stopped and dismounted because the three Dark Elves insisted on seeing and sensing the current state of the forest for themselves.

"This... I thought it even from afar, but it's looking rather weak. And as those men said earlier, it seems monsters are running rampant."

Marina wore an expression that defied description as she gazed upon the sight of her homeland, returned to after so long... truly, after a hundred years.

It was simply impossible to believe this was the place where she was born and raised.

"Yes. Even compared to when I went to fetch Lady Marina, the forest's vitality has visibly diminished."

"Hmm, I haven't been away that long, so it doesn't seem particularly different to me."

Rei and the others watched the three Dark Elves conversing from a distance.

"To my eyes, the forest looks remarkably vibrant..."

The others nodded in agreement at Elena's words.

It was probably because they didn't know the forest's original state, but even so, looking at the forest right before them, it didn't feel particularly weakened.

"If it's weak like this, I wonder what would happen if the World Tree were healed. I kind of want to see that."

As Vihera spoke, Rei touched a tree in the forest. It felt solid and sturdy.

It offered a solid, firm resistance to the touch, showing none of the hollowed-out or rotten insides one might associate with disease.

As she enjoyed the feel of the tree, she seemed to grow satisfied. Marina returned to Rei and the others, bringing Audban and Jusla with her.

"Sorry to have kept you waiting. Well then, shall we be going? It'll take a little while to reach the location of the Lost Barrier, but once we're deep in the forest, monsters could attack at any moment, so be careful. ...Though normally, you wouldn't need to worry about monsters."

As she murmured, Marina's gaze fell upon Set, who had already dashed into the forest and was running around with Yellow.

As she said, monsters rarely approached a griffin on their own.

Most monsters were nothing more than prey in front of a rank A monster like a griffin.

Moreover, Set wasn't just any Griffin; he was recognized as a rare species.

Rare species are usually treated as one rank higher than the monster they're based on, meaning in that sense, Set was equivalent to an S-rank monster.

...Though strictly speaking, Set was a monster created through Beast Magic, so monster ranks weren't entirely reliable.

Regardless, logically speaking, with such a high-rank monster present, other monsters wouldn't attack.

However, information from the slave traders encountered on the way to the forest indicated that the monsters within had become ferocious.

Monsters that rarely fight among their own kind were now attacking each other regardless. If that was the case, they might even attack a Griffin.

Given that, when advancing through this forest, we couldn't rely solely on Set. Everyone needed to stay alert and watch their surroundings.

"How long will it actually take to get from here to where Marina lived... to the World Tree's barrier? If it's going to take a while, I think it'd be better for us not to go out all together, but to split our forces in half."

Marina paused briefly before answering Rei, who was observing the forest from outside.

"Hmm. To the Labyrinth Barrier... if we travel by carriage instead of on foot, it should take about four or five hours. But... with this carriage, wouldn't we be safe even if monsters attacked? It's faster than a regular carriage too. I think it's better for everyone to ride in the carriage and dash through quickly, rather than all of us moving forward while keeping watch."

"Is that so? Hmm... Elena, what do you think?"

"Me? Well, spending extra time here increases the chance of a monster attack. So, I think we should move quickly by carriage. Fortunately, this carriage was made for me to ride in. It's not weak enough to be damaged by ordinary monsters' attacks. Besides..."

Elena's gaze shifted away from the carriage and toward the horses pulling it.

"Any ordinary monster wouldn't even have time to attack before being trampled underfoot."

Compared to ordinary horses, these two were clearly giants.

Lower-rank monsters would undoubtedly be crushed beneath their hooves without a fight.

After hearing Elena's words, Rei thought for a moment before nodding and speaking.

"Understood. Then let's go with Marina's suggestion. Besides, even if monsters appear, we have Set."

"Grrr!"

Set growled happily at Rei's words.

"Kyuu! Kyuuuuuuuu!"

Yellow, perched on Set's back, let out a cry as if to say, "Don't forget about me!"

That said, Yellow's cries were best described as adorable. No one in their right mind would rely on Yellow in battle.

While Yellow did possess several abilities useful in combat, its appearance was utterly incapable of suggesting such utility.

"Yellow, you're staying with me in the carriage."

"Kyuu..."

At Elena's words, Yellow let out a disappointed cry and flew off from Set's back.

From here on, as Marina had mentioned, they would be moving at considerable speed.

Yellow could fly, but his speed was still nowhere near Set's.

Of course, Elena didn't think there were many threats capable of harming Yellow, with his high defense, even if he got separated here. But there are always exceptions and what-ifs.

Given that, they couldn't just leave Yellow outside the carriage.

"Grr..."

Set cast a glance toward Yellow's figure, his throat making a slightly lonely sound.

Set was an important friend to Yellow too, and he probably wanted to stay with him if possible.

"Come on, Set. You can protect Yellow, right? Huh?"

Patting the lonely-looking Set, Rei climbed into the carriage.

The others boarded as well, leaving only Ara seated on the driver's platform.

"All right, we're leaving!"

With Ara's voice, the carriage began to move.

Slowly at first... but gradually picking up speed.

The two horses pulling the carriage seemed to understand this was a dangerous area. It wasn't quite full speed ahead without regard for consequences... but it was clearly faster than when they had been heading into the forest.

This wasn't a properly maintained road, but a path formed over years of being trodden.

Many traveled by carriage, and while it wasn't welcome news for the dark elves, slave traders also frequented the route, so it naturally had enough width for carriages to pass.

Still, it was ultimately just a hard-packed path.

It had its bumps, of course, and riding at speed meant the ride inside the carriage was far from comfortable.

Moreover, when the horses pulling Rei and the others' carriage picked up speed, they were faster than even an average horse at full gallop.

Given that, the carriage interior should have been jolting violently up, down, left, and right...

"Hmm, these dried fruits are delicious. The sweetness isn't overpowering, and they have just the right amount of tartness."

"Really? They're just leftovers from the Dark Elf village... I'm glad Lady Elena likes them."

Audban smiled happily at Elena's words.

For Audban, who delighted in the sight of beauties and maidens, Elena's smile held a value that could easily be described as supreme compared to the smiles of Marina or Vihera.

Indeed, he felt it was worth offering her the dried fruit he'd been carefully saving just to see that smile.

"Still, this carriage is incredible. The scenery outside is rushing by at an astonishing speed, yet sitting inside like this, I don't feel a single jolt."

"Hmm."

Vihera murmured in admiration, gazing out the window. Byune , who was slowly savoring the dried fruit she'd also received from Audban, gave a short reply.

Though Byune usually tried to eat as much fruit as possible, she wasn't that desperate this time.

She did like sweets, but she wasn't the type who felt she had to monopolize everything offered to her.

She knew when to eat hastily and when not to.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Several people turned their gaze toward the window at the sudden sound of Set's voice.

Out there, Set's front paw strike was smashing an orc's skull.

"Oh no, an orc showed up... Hang in there, Set. Wipe them all out!"

Jusla's cheers echoed inside the carriage.

Her words were light, yet the hatred in her eyes was intense.

Jusla looked completely different from her usual self, but for a Dark Elf... no, for any woman, it was only natural to know what orcs represented.

Orcs, who used women of various races for breeding, were naturally despicable enemies, even to the dark elves, who were all considered beautiful.

And if there were orcs in this forest, it stood to reason they were targeting dark elves.

Considering that, even Rei couldn't bring himself to stop Jusla.

...Though, then again, dark elves also killed orcs and ate their flesh, so the mutual hatred remained unchanged.

After all, orc meat was surprisingly delicious for its rank, literally a tasty monster.

Watching Jusla, Rei suddenly felt regret that he couldn't salvage that orc meat.

(Set could probably bring back one or two orc corpses... but he probably doesn't have the time for that.)

It was hard to tell since the carriage wasn't rocking, but even as he idly pondered this, the two giant horses were pulling the carriage at considerable speed.

Indeed, the scenery visible through the window was flowing by in an instant.

(Even so... even so, if it were Set, maybe...)

That thought crossed Rei's mind, but he was also strongly convinced it was ultimately impossible.

"Orcs. ...They are troublesome monsters. Every year, they cause no small amount of damage."

As a fellow woman, she probably had her own thoughts on the matter. Elena sighed gloomily and muttered.

In the carriage, where everyone except Rei was female, they all silently agreed with Elena's words.

Even Byune was no exception, nodding with an expression she rarely showed.

(Wait, there was someone else besides me?)

Rei's gaze shifted to Yellow, who was nibbling on a nut a short distance away.

Sensing Rei's gaze, Yellow tilted her head, looked over, and soon flew into the carriage.

Landing on Rei's arm, he offered the nut, asking, "Want some?"

"Kyu?"

"Nah, you can have that. I'm good for now."

As Rei stroked Yellow's slightly cold scales, the sensation must have felt good, because Yellow rubbed against Rei, asking for more.

Seeing Yellow like this, the unpleasant feelings Elena and the others had been harboring toward the orcs began to ease.

The conversation drifted away from the orcs, and time passed... until suddenly, Marina, Audban, and Jusla all stopped speaking at once, their gazes turning outside the carriage.

As if representing the three, Marina spoke up, as expected.

"It seems we've arrived. ...Ara, could you stop the carriage for a moment?"

Those words meant nothing less than they had arrived at the most crucial location within the forest inhabited by the Dark Elves... the place where the first barrier of the World Tree was erected.
