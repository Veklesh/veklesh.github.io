Several days had passed since everyone began traveling by carriage... Compared to when they were traveling on foot, the difference in distance covered was astonishing, and they had covered considerably more ground than Rei had anticipated when they left Mejogo.

But... precisely because of that, Rei thought it would be wise to rendezvous with the envoy from Gilm soon. After dinner, during their free time, he brought up the topic with Sura.

"Huh? So tomorrow morning, only Rei is leaving?"

"No, at the very least, Elena and I need to go. And if possible, I'd like Marina to come too."

Elena was acting as support from the Noble Faction... or at least that was her intention.

But from the Noble Faction's perspective, she was also expected to play a role in keeping track of what Rei and the others were actually doing.

She understood that herself, but in reality, she prioritized spending time with Rei and hadn't put much effort into that aspect.

Rei was aware of the situation and mentioned her name considering Elena's position.

The matter of the Paired Orb was also significant.

Mentioning Marina's name was likely due to her name value.

In Gilm, Marina, the former guildmaster, was a very famous figure.

Given her close ties to Daskar, it would be nearly impossible for the envoy members to make unreasonable demands of Rei and the others.

Furthermore, when using the Paired Orbs to negotiate with Daskar, Marina's presence served as a trump card in those talks.

In that sense, Marina's presence was necessary.

"So, should Byune and I stay here then?"

The lack of tension in Vihera's words likely stemmed from her feeling that even if she went along, she wouldn't face opponents strong enough to warrant a fight.

In reality, the envoy's escort should have included adventurers, soldiers, and knights, but Vihera likely deemed such opponents unworthy of her time.

Of course, many adventurers were strong. And among knights, there were those of exceptional strength.

But Vihera simply couldn't imagine such individuals being present as escorts.

...Given that, his words stemmed from the judgment that it would be better for him to stay here and guard them rather than go along himself.

Rei understood that it would be problematic for all of them to leave this place, so while he appreciated Vihera's words, he held no resentment.

"Right. Then, I'll leave it to you."

"Yes."

"Mm."

Vihera and Byune both gave short replies to Rei's words.

With that settled, they immediately began preparations.

Of course, since departure wasn't until tomorrow morning, there wasn't all that much work to do right now.

Not much, but not nothing either.

They needed to transfer meals for a thousand people—lunch, dinner, and an extra breakfast just in case—onto the wagons. Furthermore, since they couldn't use the sand ships as lodging if Rei didn't return by tomorrow night, preparations for that were also necessary.

Especially at night, they had to be wary of monsters and bandits, so the guards needed to patrol more rigorously than usual.

"Understood. I'll inform the others about the situation... but I'd really appreciate it if you could return as soon as possible."

For Sura, Rei was the pillar... or rather, the central figure of this group.

That wasn't wrong. He possessed combat strength to respond immediately if anything happened, carried large quantities of food, and allowed them to avoid sleeping outside at night by using the sand ship.

Set and Yellow, meanwhile, had become beloved mascots for the women, helping them forget the hardships of the journey.

(Now that they had a carriage, they didn't need to worry about that much anymore.)

For the women, unaccustomed to travel, not having to walk all day was nothing short of a blessing.

"Yeah. We're not exactly traveling with the envoy group anyway. Once we finish exchanging information and talking with Lord Daskar, we plan to head right back here. ...If things go smoothly, I think we could be back by tomorrow afternoon. Well, I can't say for sure since I don't know how far the envoy group has progressed yet."

"...Rei, in that case, shouldn't we leave more than just a day's worth of supplies? If we can't meet up with the envoy smoothly, it might take several days."

At Marina's words, Rei turned his gaze to Sura, asking, What do you think?

Even though autumn was gradually approaching, the days were still hot and distinctly summer-like.

At such temperatures, leaving regular food out would surely spoil quickly.

That meant what we'd need would be preserved foods like dried meat.

Unlike regular meals, preserved foods didn't take up much space.

Rei's Misty Ring contained a large quantity of such food, and there were also provisions supplied by Gime Kara.

For the time being, there was little chance of running out of food while Rei was away and before he returned.

Furthermore, additional supplies prepared by Gime Kara were stored in towns and villages, and it should be possible to eat freshly prepared meals there.

As for clothing, everyone was now wearing ordinary clothes, albeit somewhat plain, rather than the prostitutes' attire.

With the carriages, horses, drivers, and guards all gathered, there was little of urgent need in their current situation.

Although everyone had been provided with adequate clothing, fabric was still in demand, and those skilled in sewing continued to make garments.

This was likely because, even in such circumstances, many still desired to wear cute or beautiful clothes if possible.

However... previously, those exempted from walking and riding in carriages would perform tasks like sewing clothes instead.

But now, there was no obligation to perform such labor, and those individuals gained various benefits by making clothes.

For instance, they receive priority access to meals and water produced by the short swords, or they get priority seating in better carriages... things like that.

"Yeah, I guess we should leave extra food for now."

With that, they decided to prepare... and the night passed.

The next day, when it was explained that Rei and the others would be temporarily leaving the group, some naturally felt uneasy or dissatisfied.

For the Resistance members and women who had witnessed Rei's strength time and again, it wasn't just Rei's departure that caused unease—the absence of Elena, Marina, and the others was also a source of anxiety.

The issues of food and water weren't a problem thanks to the preserved food Rei had stored in the Misty Ring and the water created by Marina's spirit magic...

...but the fact that the water produced by the flowing dagger, usable only at night, and above all, Set, the healer of hearts, would also be gone, caused considerable backlash.

That said, Rei and the others weren't about to compromise here. Under various scrutinizing gazes, Rei retrieved the Set Cage from the Misty Ring, which had just stored the sand ship.

Those watching from a short distance away initially had no idea what it was for.

It was only natural. Moving in such a manner was something ordinary people couldn't comprehend.

If anyone had been awake during Rei's air raid on Mejogo, they might have seen the Set Cages. But those who lived as prostitutes were fast asleep at that hour.

When escaping Mejogo... when Rei dropped the carriage he'd acquired from the sky, the Set Cage was also used, but it should have been hidden by the surrounding scenery and invisible.

Then, when they returned to Mejogo again at Daskar's request, they landed far from the battle site. For one reason or another, almost no one present here has ever seen the Set Cage.

"Alright, Vihera, Byune, Sura, Gime Kara. I'm counting on you guys. I'll be back as soon as I can."

"Leave it to us. Since Set isn't here, I'm hoping some powerful monster will come attack us."

"No, pray we don't get attacked."

Rei couldn't help but retort at Vihera's words.

But for Vihera, who possessed not only the three basic human desires—appetite, sex, and sleep—but also a desire for combat, a battle against a formidable foe was always something to be welcomed.

Of course, given that this wasn't the frontier but rather the small, rural kingdom of Reburina, the likelihood of high-rank monsters appearing was low.

But... low probability doesn't mean zero possibility.

Perhaps, just maybe, the possibility of such a powerful monster appearing was extremely low, but it existed.

Vihera accepted staying here, pinning his hopes on that possibility smaller than a grain of sand.

That said, it was only because Rei was returning to this group.

If Rei had simply joined the envoy and headed straight for Gilm, he absolutely would not have made such a move to stay behind.

"Heh heh, that's right. Praying alone won't accomplish anything, after all."

"...Just don't go praying to some Holy Goddess of Light or anything like that."

Vihera responded to Rei's warning—spoken while recalling the troublesome religious group they'd clashed with before—with a broad smile instead.

As if to say she'd welcome it with open arms if the Holy Light Church came attacking for any reason.

"Well, I'm sure those guys would definitely satisfy you."

Recalling the Holy Light Church members they had fought before, it was clear they were significantly stronger than the giants they had battled in the Kingdom of Reburina.

Of course, this doesn't apply to all Holy Light Church members—only to the select few elite fighters within the church.

"Right. I'm waiting for opponents like that too."

Though Rei and Vihera exchanged only a brief conversation, Sura, Gimekara, and Ryutas, who had been listening nearby, could not possibly miss it.

Sura didn't know the Holy Light Church existed, but Gimekara, a powerful figure within the Zolge Trading Company, and Ryutas, considered a candidate to succeed Jaya, both knew its name.

Still, since the Holy Light Church was ostensibly a benevolent religion, they couldn't immediately believe what Rei and the others had said.

At first, they even suspected it might be a fabricated story meant to deceive them.

Rei and the others knew they were being overheard, yet showed no sign of being flustered.

Still, their keen intuition, honed by experience, told them these words held significance.

Leaving those three behind, Rei, Elena, and Marina waved lightly to Vihera and Byune.

Meanwhile, Set greeted those two... and the women who doted on him with a purr, then immediately picked up Rei and dashed off with him on his back.

During the short run-up before takeoff, Elena and the others climb into the Set Cage.

Seto, having made a wide detour in the sky, grabbed the Set Cage Elena and the others were in... and flew off.

The scene looked almost like a hawk hunting its prey.

...Though in reality, it was vastly different.

Sura, Gime Kara, and Ryutas watched Set's departure... along with the women who had cherished him.

The former showed their resolve to protect this group, while the latter showed their sadness at Set's departure.

Even if it was only for a day or so, the absence of Set felt to the women like losing a pillar of support.

(This requires some thought.)

Watching the women, whose morale was visibly low, Gime Kara frowned in thought.

Ryutas and Sura shared the same expression.

Indeed, if this situation continued, it was certain to lead to various problems.

Morale isn't just needed during war; it's essential at any time.

Especially in a situation like this, where a motley crew is gathered and heading toward a destination, if morale drops... well, trouble is almost guaranteed.

People wouldn't cooperate; they'd just start doing whatever they pleased.

No, even that would be tolerable. In the worst case, they might even start killing each other over the slightest provocation.

Of course, such extreme actions are rare, but they are not impossible.

(I need to consult with the two of you... and above all, with Vihera-san.)

Gimekara murmured to himself, wishing with all his heart that Rei and the others would return as soon as possible.
