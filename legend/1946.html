"I wonder, what on earth is this story about?"

I can smell blood.

Hearing this, the middle-aged man tilted his head in puzzlement, looking as though he didn’t quite understand what was being said to him, and turned to Rei.

Seeing the middle-aged man, the guards hesitated for a moment, unsure whose words to believe... but immediately turned their stern gazes toward him.

To the guards, Rei was someone they’d interacted with on numerous occasions in the past, and they’d even worked together to solve cases.

Calling him a “comrade-in-arms” might be a bit of an exaggeration, but many of them certainly thought of him that way.

Moreover, regardless of Rei’s personality, his abilities were undoubtedly among the very best in Gilm.

Since Rei—a man of such caliber—said he could smell blood on the middle-aged man standing before them, the guards had no choice but to believe him.

“I’m sorry, but I’d like to take a quick look around the mansion.”

The guard made his request, but the middle-aged man shook his head.

“I’m sorry, but I cannot do that. I am here at the master’s request. As such, I cannot possibly let a stranger—even if they are a guard—into the mansion without an invitation…”

“Absolutely not.”

Saying this, the middle-aged man made it clear that he was absolutely determined not to let the people present into the mansion.

“Huh. Could it be that there’s something inside this mansion that… you don’t want us guards to see?”

“Not at all. There is nothing of the sort. However, I cannot allow such behavior in my master’s mansion without permission.”

“I suppose you’ll just have to accept that because we’re guards.”

“I’m afraid I can’t do that. I’ll speak with the master when he returns, so please go home for now.”

Saying this, the middle-aged man bowed his head.

Just as the guard was about to say something to him, he was suddenly blown sideways.

“What!?”

The guard was completely bewildered by the sudden turn of events, but when he saw Rei, standing next to him, reaching out his hand, it became clear why this had happened.

One of the guards tried to say something to Rei, but someone else acted first.

“Hah!”

“You’re too soft!”

The middle-aged man, who had been bowing his head, let out a sharp gasp and delivered a swift, powerful kick.

It was a kick aimed at the head of the guard standing next to the one who had been knocked away by Rei.

It was a kick with such power and precision that, had it connected, it would have—if not caused the head to explode—certainly fractured the skull.

But the kick was easily stopped by Rei’s hand, which had stepped forward to shield the guard.

The sound that echoed around them was like the sound of flesh being slammed against concrete, amplified several times over.

“...Using a kick as an attack while wearing greaves, huh? I haven’t seen that much outside of Vihera... but there sure are some menacing butlers out there.”

Realizing that the middle-aged man was wearing leg guards under his pants, Rei remarked teasingly.

But even as he said this, he didn’t let go of the man’s leg.

The middle-aged man desperately tried to shake off Rei’s grip, but his leg showed no sign of breaking free.

He was only holding on lightly, but that was only “lightly” from Rei’s perspective; for the middle-aged man, it was a different story.

It was only when things reached this point that the guards finally seemed to realize what had happened.

Before they could even react in confusion, and before they could assess the situation, they pinned down the man whose foot was being held by Rei and tied him up so he couldn’t move.

When an amateur ties someone up, even if they think they’ve done it securely, someone with the knowledge of a thief can often escape with ease.

However, for guards, it is not difficult to tie someone up in such a way that they cannot escape even if their joints are dislocated.

Perhaps because they were professionals, in the blink of an eye—at a speed that even Rei found astonishing—the guards had tied up the middle-aged man and were now scanning the surroundings warily.

“Guh…”

The middle-aged man, perhaps never expecting to be captured so easily, could only utter a sound of surprise.

“Well, this at least confirms that this mansion is suspicious… but I’d like you to tell me who’s using it.”

Rei asked the bound middle-aged man, but the man showed no sign of opening his mouth.

(Huh. He’s quite loyal, isn’t he? …Though he doesn’t seem to be afraid of his superiors, at least.)

Rei muttered in admiration as he observed the middle-aged man before him.

In fact, although he lost easily because his opponent was Rei, this middle-aged man’s skill was actually quite high.

As proof of that, setting aside the first strike he tried to land, even the guards couldn’t react to his second kick, which was aimed at the head.

If Rei hadn’t been there, the man’s strike would undoubtedly have shattered the guard’s skull.

In other words, depending on the circumstances, the guard might have died.

That said, being a security guard is a life-or-death job.

They don’t let the fact that their lives are being targeted stop them from doing their jobs properly.

...Though I suppose after work is done, they probably drown their sorrows in alcohol to numb the fear of death.

“For now, this man doesn’t seem likely to say anything, no matter what we ask him. However, the fact that he attacked us like this suggests there’s probably something inside this mansion… As you all said earlier, there’s no doubt there’s something here that they don’t want anyone to see.”

Rei’s words were agreed upon by the guards.

That makes sense—if there really wasn’t anything they couldn’t show us, he wouldn’t have attacked even if the guards had held their ground.

It was precisely because they saw the guards’ resolve and judged they would never back down that they took the initiative to try and reduce their numbers, even if only by one, which led to that kind of action.

“That decision wasn’t wrong, though. If it weren’t for Rei, depending on the situation, we might all have been wiped out right here.”

One of the guards muttered this, looking at Rei.

Although the guards were used to facing adventurers and had undergone rigorous combat training, there were still times when they couldn’t handle an opponent who was simply too strong.

And the middle-aged man they were facing this time was exactly that kind of opponent.

For a man like that, his worst mistake was probably assuming that the only ones in front of him were security guards.

Perhaps he didn’t possess the skill to gauge his opponent’s true strength; the fact that he couldn’t recognize Rei as Rei even when looking at him—thanks to the Dragon Robe—was a critical oversight.

If Set had been there, he might have recognized Rei as Rei, but unfortunately, Set was currently on guard with the other guards, watching for anyone trying to escape the mansion.

“The real problem is inside the mansion. If there are others as strong as this guy, it’s going to be a little… no, actually, quite a hassle.”

One of the guards muttered, looking frustrated.

Part of it was probably that they hadn’t expected a sudden ambush, but there was no doubt they hadn’t been able to react to the middle-aged man’s strike.

At the very least, it was impossible for any of the guards here to defeat the middle-aged man in a one-on-one fight; they would need to gather more men.

If that’s the case, searching this mansion will undoubtedly take time.

Given the scale of the mansion, there’s a high probability of hidden passages, secret staircases, and secret rooms.

To find them, they naturally need to identify suspicious areas, and that requires manpower.

However, because of the presence of that middle-aged man, it has become difficult for the others—Rei aside—to operate alone or in pairs.

(Or was this guy acting with that very intention? If so, he’s definitely going to be a pain.)

“Is this what they call the wisdom of age?” Rei thought, turning his gaze toward the middle-aged man who was bound.

Perhaps sensing Rei’s gaze, the middle-aged man looked back at him, still gagged.

Since he couldn’t speak, Rei had no way of knowing what he was thinking, but from Rei’s perspective, he seemed to have a look of satisfaction, as if he’d done what he had to do.

Of course, that was merely Rei’s impression; the reality might be different.

In any case, there was no doubt that this middle-aged man’s actions were a nuisance to both the guards and Rei.

“What should we do? Should we just call for more guards? If we do that, I think we can handle it with our numerical advantage.”

“That’s one option. But… with this case, we need to gather evidence as quickly as possible. …Actually, it might be best if we could get Set inside the mansion.”

The guard said this, but he probably understood just as well as anyone that it was impossible.

Rather, it was undoubtedly just a joke meant to put everyone, including Rei, at ease.

“Well, Set’s senses are sharper than mine. If we could get him inside, it would definitely be helpful. …That said, there’ll probably be a lot more things getting broken in this mansion.”

Using Size Change might reduce the damage to some extent, but Rei had no intention of doing such a thing.

Compared to Fire Breath, Size Change isn’t one of Set’s most well-known skills.

In other words, it serves as a last resort for emergencies. …Though, to be honest, there probably aren’t that many situations where changing one’s body size carries such significant weight.

“I see. Well, I guess there’s no helping it. We’ll just have to investigate this place on our own after all. Besides, the task of finding the person who escaped from the mansion is important.”

With Set’s keen senses, it would be nearly impossible for anyone to slip past him.

“But then, what about searching the mansion? If someone like him shows up, there’s nothing we can do. It’d be a different story if it were Rei, though.”

The guards’ gazes turned toward Rei.

Since it was Rei who actually defeated the middle-aged man, no one could deny it.

...Though for those who knew Rei’s true capabilities, it was only natural to conclude there was no need to deny it.

“Ah… Well, then, let’s go with a compromise. Rei goes alone. The rest of us… we’ll stick together in groups of two or three. It’ll take longer than if we all searched separately, but if we get attacked, we should be able to handle it to some extent. …And while we hold them off, the others will come to our aid. Is that okay?”

In this case, the offer to help was clearly directed at Rei.

Rei understood this, so he nodded in response to the guard’s words.

“Got it. In that case, I’ll check out the central area of the building. That way, even if someone gets attacked somewhere, I won’t be too far behind. …Though, normally, the center of a mansion would likely contain important rooms or something. I’m not sure if it’s a good idea for me to be the one checking that area, though.”

There’s no doubt that Rei has gained a wealth of experience as an adventurer.

But naturally, compared to professional guards like these, his skills fall short.

That’s why Rei said what he did, but the guards nodded in agreement, saying it was fine.

“We’re fine with that. If there’s something important there, it’s entirely possible that whoever’s there is strong. In that sense, having Rei search that area is definitely the best choice.”

Everyone nodded at the guard’s words, and as a result, Rei ended up searching the central area of the mansion.

The remaining guards split into groups of two or three and entered the mansion.

Also, just in case… just in case someone might try to escape from here, and above all, since we need to keep an eye on the middle-aged man we captured, a few of us will stay here as well.

“Rei, let us know right away if you find anything.”

“If I find any important-looking documents or anything, I’ll store them in my Misty Ring. That way, we can check that area later, right?”

“...Yeah, that makes sense. But if you forget to take them out and they just stay in there, we won’t know about it, so be careful.”

Rei’s Misty Ring is exclusive to him; only Rei can put things in or take them out.

It was precisely because he knew that the guard told Rei this.

“I know. I’ll keep that firmly in mind.”

Nodding, Rei made his way deeper into the mansion.
