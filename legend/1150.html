The day after the farewell party... Rei was standing before Gosh's main gate.

At the main gate stood figures like Augusto and Kathy.

Augusto had actually wanted to bring Günter here too, but unfortunately, Günter was the gatekeeper at Augusto's mansion.

If there had been other people, it might have been different, but currently, Günter was the only gatekeeper.

Therefore, the formal farewell had already taken place when Rei left Augusto's residence.

...Though to be fair, Günter hadn't said anything particularly thoughtful; he'd just briefly told him to come back sometime.

Instead... well, not exactly instead, but waiting before the main gate was someone Rei hadn't expected to see.

"Leaving already? That's rather sudden. I wanted to discuss things with you, like that village matter..."

The one addressing Rei like this was Zarust.

The reason Zarust, who should have been at the village of the Hundred-Faced Ones, was here... was purely coincidence.

Originally, Zarust had been acting alongside Narsus and Rei to defeat the assassins targeting the lives of Augusto and Daldora.

As a result, they had secured the village serving as the Hundred-Faced Ones' base, and Zarust remained partly to protect that village.

However, among those Rei brought aboard the Sand Ship were a fair number of adventurers. This meant Zarust, who had made no preparations, no longer needed to stay in the village.

Of course, even without any preparations, Zarust and Narsus were skilled adventurers.

Camping? Hardly. There were houses, and since people had lived there until recently, supplies were available.

In a place like that, living there wasn't difficult.

They could have easily settled there permanently.

But... just because they could live there didn't mean they could actually live there.

Even if we could live in that village, it's only natural to want to use things we're accustomed to.

And using someone else's clothes? That was something I'd only do if absolutely necessary. If I had my own clothes waiting back at Gosh, I'd rather not.

That's why, when part of the survey team traveled by carriage to bring the information they'd gathered back to Gosh, Zarust hitched a ride.

Of course, he wasn't just tagging along for free. Traveling through the desert, even for a short time, meant he could serve as protection against monsters or sand pirates.

And then, when they returned and were about to go through the procedures to enter Gosh... Rei, Set, Augusto, and Kathy appeared right at the main gate.

Naturally, Zarust, having heard that Rei was leaving Gosh today, was surprised. He didn't quite go so far as to blame Rei outright, but he did voice his dissatisfaction.

"Well, I came to Gosh in the first place more by circumstance than any real reason anyway."

To be precise, he did have the reason of escaping the persistent merchants regarding the Twilight Spear, but Rei had no intention of mentioning that.

He wanted to keep it at that – that he'd come to Gosh purely by chance, without any particular reason.

"Then why not stay a little longer? Honestly, I owe you more than a few favors, so I'd like you to stay until you're repaid."

"I've got things to do too. I can't just stay in Gosh forever. Besides..."

"Besides...?"

"...I'd rather be in Gilm during this season."

Being in the desert during summer... Even though the Dragon Robe kept him perfectly cool, the mere image of it conjured up some pretty grim impressions.

Slightly exasperated by Rei's words, Zarust let out a small sigh.

For Zarust, born and raised in Gosh... and for Augusto and Kathy, the desert was something that remained constant.

Of course, it wasn't completely barren. While it didn't have distinct seasons like Gilm, considering the year as a whole, the temperature and scenery did change somewhat.

"Besides..."

"And?"

Zalust prompted Rei, who had stopped mid-sentence again, but Rei shook his head as if to say it was nothing.

(Getting a decent amount of magic stones was nice for us too. Well, there are probably other monsters unique to the desert... but we can deal with those when we come back to Gosh again.)

Though Rei had no intention of ever living in Gosh, he didn't hesitate to visit occasionally.

Especially since things like Gosh's unique spices and ingredients cost a fortune in other towns.

(Direct from the source... Wait, in this case, I'm actually coming here myself to buy them, so that's different, right?)

Memories of his time in Japan surfaced in Rei's mind.

Winter's specialty, the sandfish. Buying it at the supermarket was expensive, but buying directly from the fishermen often meant getting it for less than half the price.

(Well, pickling the hatahata sushi was a lot of work, though.)

Helping her parents pickle hatahata sushi was an annual tradition for Rei.

Though it wasn't the kind of sushi you make with rice pressed into shapes; it was more like rice sushi. The pickled sushi was stored in the freezer and could be eaten year-round.

Of course, Rei also enjoyed eating the hatahata sushi.

(...Huh? Why am I thinking about sandfish?)

Realizing her thoughts had somehow drifted completely away from Gosh, she shook her head slightly... It must have been because she remembered something. Rei suddenly felt an urge to eat sandfish.

But he didn't even know if sandfish existed in this world.

Even if they did, hatahata were deep-sea fish, not the kind Rei and Set could easily catch if they tried.

(Wait, Set can fly through the air while carrying a bear. If that's the case, maybe if we used a net...?)

As Rei pondered this, Zarust sighed, looking somewhat exasperated.

"Rei, it's fine to think things through, but it's a bit much when you suddenly get so absorbed in your thoughts while we're talking."

"Huh? Oh, sorry. I just got a sudden craving for fish."

"...Fish, huh?"

Gosh did sell fish caught from the oasis, but naturally, the quantity wasn't large. It was extremely rare... and therefore expensive.

Even an adventurer like Zarust could only afford it if he stretched his budget a bit.

"Honestly, being able to eat fresh fish in the desert is pretty amazing in itself."

"I understand that."

From there, for some reason, for a few minutes, Rei and Zarust... along with Kathy, who enjoys cooking as a hobby, and Augusto, who loves eating delicious food, the four of them chatted animatedly about fish dishes.

"Hmm, so Gilm has such fish dishes... Cathy, can you make it?"

"I wonder. But if Augusto wants to eat it, I'll definitely try making it."

"I envy you, Augusto. Having such a skilled wife in the kitchen."

As they listened to the three of them chatting, bored, Set rubbed his head against Rei.

"Grrr..."

Is this going to take forever? Set tilted his head questioningly, and Rei apologetically stroked his head.

"Sorry. I wanted to chat a bit longer, but Set seems to have reached his limit. I should be heading soon."

Hearing Rei's words, the one who looked the saddest was, of course, Kathy.

Augusto also wore a disappointed expression.

Zalust was disappointed too, of course, but as adventurers, encounters and partings were unavoidable.

Zalust understood this, but it was undeniably disappointing that Rei, to whom he owed a debt, would be leaving Gosh.

"Rei, please... please come visit again. Augusto and I will always welcome you."

Kathy, holding back tears, stroked Rei's head.

Though she was still wearing the hood of her Dragon Robe, she stroked him over it. Still, Rei seemed to feel the warmth of Kathy's hand touching him.

"Yeah, I'll come back. Your cooking was amazing, Kathy."

Rei said with a smile, and Kathy returned it, nodding happily.

"Rei, if anything happens, please rely on me. I'll help you as much as I possibly can. ...Though, I do wonder how much use my abilities would be to you."

"Well, to rely on Augusto's help, I'd have to go all the way to Gosh anyway."

Rei nodded at the somewhat lighthearted words from Augusto and Zarust.

"Yeah. If anything happens, I'll be sure to ask for your help. ...Well then, see you."

After a brief farewell to the two, Rei mounted Set's back.

"Alright, Set. Let's go."

"GRRRRRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a high-pitched cry of joy.

...The monsters near Gosh who heard that sound sensed the presence of an overwhelmingly powerful being and quickly scattered, though that was likely beyond Rei and Set's awareness.

After a few paces of running, Set flapped his wings and soared into the sky.

Augusto, Kathy, and Zarust watched him go, seeing Set's form gradually shrink in the cloudless sky.

Augusto regretted the departure of the reliable adventurer. Cathy, who had regarded him almost like a son, could no longer hold back the tears that fell. Zarust, though younger, deeply regretted the loss of Rei, whose superiority made comparison pointless.

All three missed Rei in their own ways, but perhaps Zarust grieved the most—not for Rei the person, but for the loss of a Rank B adventurer bearing that legendary title.

Though Zarust was known as a skilled adventurer in Gosh, that reputation held only within Gosh.

Honestly, he wanted to learn more about adventuring from Rei, whose reputation was legendary even in the Mireana Kingdom and the Bestir Empire.

...That said, Rei was an adventurer specialized in combat prowess.

In the general sense of the term, he was by no means outstanding.

No, of course he possesses superior skills compared to ordinary adventurers, but even so, viewed as a Rank B adventurer, he's probably just average... or perhaps slightly below.

In Rei's case, he essentially overpowers his average adventurer skills with his physical prowess, extraordinary magical power, the presence of his griffin, Set, and his numerous magic items.

It's not wrong to say he can do that precisely because he possesses the strength to force his way through.

"...Hm?"

Zalust, who had been regretting wanting to spar with Rei, noticed several figures hurrying toward them through the open main gate.

Realizing who they were, Zarust frowned slightly.

Because those figures were Daldora and her guards.

Augusto and Daldora had been targeted by the Hundred-Faced One and had cooperated regarding the village incident, but one reason for that was Rei's existence.

If Daldora tried to eliminate Augusto, she and her guards would inevitably clash with Rei.

And precisely because she understood there was no chance of victory against Rei, Daldora had adopted a conciliatory stance toward Augusto.

But... considering the current situation where Rei is gone, it's fair to say it's questionable whether he'll maintain that attitude.

"Oh my, I came in a bit of a hurry... but it seems I didn't make it in time. Had Rei already departed?"

"...Your ears are remarkably sharp."

Augusto asked back, his guard clearly up, wondering where Dali had gotten the information that Rei was leaving Gosh.

Like Zarust, now that Rei was gone, there was no need for him to ally with Auguste.

No, considering the interests of the Hundred-Faced One, Augusto was a liability with no upside whatsoever.

He couldn't imagine her attempting anything in plain sight like this... but considering Daldora's past actions, it was still possible.

Augusto was inwardly tense, but as if reading his mind, Daldora smiled and spoke.

"Rest assured. I wouldn't dare lay a finger on you now, Augusto. If something like that were to reach Rei's ears..."

Even without finishing the sentence, everyone present understood the future Daldora was hinting at.

Of course, whether Rei would actually return to Gosh again was uncertain.

But... if she were to eliminate Augusto, she'd have to live in constant fear, wondering when Rei might arrive.

Daldora, already a nervous sort, could never endure such days.

As a result... Daldora reluctantly decided to team up with Augusto.
