As the sun set, darkness reigned over the forest.

The only light source was the moon reigning like a queen in the night sky, but even that moon was now obscured by clouds, casting only a faint glow upon the earth.

Moreover, the forest trees blocked the moonlight, so it could be said the ground was enveloped in darkness.

But... within that darkness, a large number of Dark Elves continued to run as if the darkness posed no obstacle at all.

Dark elves possessed excellent night vision to begin with.

Even with only the faintest moonlight filtering through, they could see almost as well as in daylight... well, not quite, but their vision wasn't significantly impaired.

And racing through the night forest at a speed that rivals even those dark elves is Rei , riding Set.

Both Set and Rei possessed night vision as sharp as the dark elves'.

And a short distance away, Elena could also be seen.

Having inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone, Elena's night vision surpasses even that of the Dark Elves.

The only ones in Rei's group without night vision are Vihera , Ara , and Earth.

Both Byune and Polo (one person and one beast) possess sufficient night vision. However, Byune struggles with rapid communication with others, and Polo never leaves Earth's side. Thus, the four people and one beast explored the forest near the settlement under Byune's guidance.

Marina was stationed near the settlement entrance with Optis, coordinating the other Dark Elves.

"Alright, I'll leave it to you."

"Yeah."

The dark elf man who had been talking with Rei until Marina arrived said this to Rei, then gradually walked away.

If someone possessed the power to see through tree branches from above, they might have glimpsed a somewhat distorted, radiating spread.

(Being Dark Elves, they're well-accustomed to nighttime operations, aren't they?)

Watching the Dark Elf recede into the distance, that thought crossed Rei's mind.

"Guru?"

"Can we go a bit faster?" Set asked, turning his gaze toward Rei.

Rei gently stroked Set's back, granting permission for him to increase speed.

The next instant, Set surged forward at a speed that could only be called explosive acceleration.

This was the result of unleashing the speed he had been holding back while traveling with the Dark Elves.

The nighttime forest scenery rushed past Rei's eyes.

"Set, goblins. Find goblins, no matter what. They're probably the most numerous creatures in this forest right now."

"GRRRRRR!"

Set let out a high-pitched growl at Rei's words.

Normally, any monster hearing that sound would understand it stood no chance against him and would retreat from Set.

But...

"Tch, showing up so soon!"

As Set tilted his body slightly, something passed through the spot where Rei's body had been just an instant before.

What had passed by became clear the moment he turned his gaze forward again.

It was a stone.

A fist-sized stone, at that.

While possessing high lethal potential, such stones were commonplace, lying scattered everywhere. In the context of a nighttime forest, they could even become deadly weapons. ...Provided, of course, that one couldn't see them coming or evade them.

Set and Rei possessed keen night vision. Furthermore, their physical abilities allowed them to easily evade attacks at the speed they had just witnessed.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

As Set let out a sharp growl, multiple wind arrows materialized around him.

But the one who threw the stone just now would be unable to spot those arrows.

Being arrows made of wind to begin with, they were difficult to see even in daylight. And now it was night.

No matter how good their night vision was, recognizing those wind arrows was virtually impossible.

And then the wind arrows were released.

Their speed was incomparably faster than the stones hurled at Set and Rei earlier, making evasion impossible for anything in their path.

"Gyaaah!"

"Gyaaah!"

"Gyo gyo gyo!"

Those who had hurled stones at Rei and Set were pierced and sliced by the Wind Arrows one after another, screaming in agony.

Some, their necks cleanly severed, lost their lives without even realizing they were dead.

"Goblins, after all."

Though Rei had wondered if they might be orcs, given their use of stone-throwing tactics, the attackers were goblins said to breed here.

"Wait, Set! The kids are okay, right!?"

"Gluu!"

What if the missing children were with the goblins? Had they been injured in the attack? Rei shouted, gripped by that fear. But Set responded to Rei's concern with a confident growl, assuring him it was alright!

Though he didn't know how, Rei understood that Set had acted knowing the Dark Elf children weren't where the attack had landed. He let out a small sigh of relief atop Set's back.

If they, who had come to rescue the dark elf children, ended up hurting the very ones they were supposed to save, the goodwill they'd gained by restoring the World Tree in the settlement would surely plummet before their eyes.

At the same time, he worried it might worsen Marina's position, so the fact that only goblins lay ahead was welcome news to Rei.

"Flying Slash!"

Shouting as he swung the Death Scythe, Rei stood atop Set's back.

What was unleashed was a flying slash, now possessing power orders of magnitude greater than before, thanks to reaching Level Five.

It sliced through the tree branches blocking Set's vision ahead, flying straight forward.

For a moment, Rei worried Marina might scold him for pointlessly cutting branches, but he decided defeating the goblins came first and shelved that thought.

"Gyaa Gyaa Gyaa!"

"Gyaaaagaaaa!"

"Gyo gyo gyu!"

The goblins that had survived Set's Wind Arrow were now screaming as their bodies were sliced apart by the flying slash.

「Set！」

"Grrr!"

Quickly grasping the meaning of Rei's call, Set charged straight ahead.

To reach the goblins beneath him as quickly as possible.

The branches that had stretched out before Set, as if to block his path, were mostly severed by the Wind Arrows and Flying Slashes.

Thanks to that, Set didn't need to worry about the branches and ran straight ahead...

"GRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

The moment the goblin came into view, he let out a high-pitched, intimidating growl.

What was unleashed was one of Set's skills: King's Intimidation.

Level 1—it was far from powerful enough to be called formidable—yet it still had a dramatic effect on the goblin in Set's line of sight.

The moment they heard that roar, the goblins' fighting spirit—already shaken by the preemptive strikes of Wind Arrow and Flying Slash—was completely shattered.

Some frantically tried to flee, others were so shaken they didn't know what to do, and still others were rendered unable to move at all by the effect of the King's Intimidation... Their actions varied, but they all shared one thing in common: they couldn't come up with an effective countermeasure.

All the goblins could do as Set and Rei approached was cry out in panic.

The last few seconds left to the goblins passed like this... until the beings bringing death revealed themselves.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set swung his limbs wildly, sometimes using his tail, sometimes his beak, to dispatch goblins... Rei leapt down from Set and swung his Death Scythe, reaping goblin lives as casually as plucking weeds.

Thanks to the actions of this one man and one beast, the goblins who had attacked by hurling stones at Set and Rei were wiped out in a mere dozen seconds.

"...Well, goblins are pretty much that, huh?"

Rei murmured as he lightly swung the blade of the Death Scythe, flicking away the blood that clung to it.

Considering Rei and Set's combat prowess, they should never have struggled against mere goblins.

The initial stone-throwing had startled Rei, but once they understood the nature of the attack, dealing with it proved straightforward.

"Grrrr..."

Set nodded in agreement at Rei's words, then looked around, his throat making a sound that seemed to say, "Let's hurry?"

If they had killed orcs, they might have taken the time to store the bodies in the Misty Ring for meat. Fortunately—or perhaps unfortunately—only goblin corpses lay here.

...Even so, considering the possibility of them becoming undead, it would be better to dispose of them properly. But there was no time for that now.

With the need to find the dark elf children, starting with Rugdos, they couldn't afford to waste time here.

(Besides, if this were a road or something, fine, but in the forest, other monsters and animals will clean up the corpses. ...Though I really can't imagine wanting to eat goblin meat.)

As that thought crossed my mind, the phrase "goblin meat" suddenly struck a chord.

(Goblins are basically everywhere. To the point where you don't even need to go looking for them. If that's the case, wouldn't it be pretty groundbreaking if I could figure out how to make goblin meat taste good?)

Rei still couldn't figure out what method would make goblin meat tasty.

But dried goblin meat was something most new adventurers relied on.

After all, it's cheap.

It was incredibly unpalatable, but the fact that it was cheap to eat made it an undeniably welcome staple for adventurers.

If he could develop a way to make goblin meat palatable, it would undoubtedly be a groundbreaking achievement.

...Of course, what Rei was thinking about, others had naturally thought of too. Countless people—tens, hundreds, thousands—had tried and failed to improve the taste of goblin meat.

Unaware of this, Rei decided he'd give it a try once the World Tree matter was settled.

For now, he stored the single goblin corpse lying nearby into his Misty Ring for later use, then climbed back onto Set's back.

Set wondered why Rei had gone to the trouble of storing the goblin corpse in the Misty Ring, but decided that finding the children was the priority for now and kicked off the ground.

"The goblins are getting more active now that night has fallen. That means the danger to the children grows too, so we have to search thoroughly."

"Grrrr."

Set let out a short growl in response to Rei's words and raced through the night forest.

Both Rei and Set could clearly sense battles erupting throughout the forest.

The clang of swords, the screams of monsters, and even what sounded like the cries of dark elves reached their ears.

"I hope the damage isn't too bad... Huh?!"

"Guruu!"

At the end of his words, Rei swung the Death Scythe.

Simultaneously, a stone hurled from the darkness was cleaved clean in two by the blade of the Death Scythe.

"Damn it, goblins again!?"

Rei shouted in annoyance, but the sound coming from ahead wasn't a goblin's scream—it was a howl like a dog's.

"WAAAAAAAAAAGH!"

That sound made Rei instantly realize what kind of monster had just attacked them.

"Not goblins... kobolds!?"

"Grrrrrrr!"

Set barked in response to Rei's cry.

But the kobolds, who would normally flee the moment they heard Set's growl, showed no sign of retreating. They continued hurling stones at Rei and Set.

"Dammit!"

Frustrated that every stone they threw was blocked or dodged, several kobolds hurled their weapons—spears, longswords, daggers—at them.

Rei, expecting only stones, was somewhat surprised but sliced them down with Death Scythe.

Or rather, because they were throwing what resembled weapons, they couldn't throw them properly, and some even flew off in unexpected directions.

「Set！」

"GRRRRRRRRRR!"

Without needing to hear the rest, Set understood what Rei wanted and let out a high-pitched growl aimed at the kobolds.

It sounded similar to the roar he had let out just seconds before, yet it was fundamentally different.

The king's intimidation, used during the battle with the goblins.

It was unleashed with full force, directed squarely at the kobold.

As a low-rank monster like the goblins, the kobold had absolutely no means to counter this roar.

Just like the goblins before, they split into two groups: those frozen in place and those thrown into confusion...

"GRRRRRR!"

The roar echoed for the third time, and simultaneously, the water spheres created around Set flew straight toward the kobolds.

The kobold struck by the sphere had its skin torn and flesh shattered, screaming in agony.

The screams of their comrades in this situation where they could barely move.

In their excited minds, they finally began to realize that the enemy they were facing might be far beyond what they had anticipated... But then, Set and Rei charged in. Just like with the goblins, within mere seconds, all dozen or so kobolds lost their lives.
