“...What a hassle.”

"Grr."

“Hm? …Ah, I see. I’m not saying Rei is wrong, but… didn’t you try a little too hard in the mock battle?”

Rei, Set, and Brewitt—two humans and one creature—who had been hopping from one food stall to another, muttered upon sensing the presence of someone following them.

Though, it seemed Brewitt only realized it because of Rei’s mutter.

No, rather, the fact that Brewitt was able to sense that presence because of Rei’s mutter is proof that he’s more battle-hardened than your average noble.

(At least it’s a relief that it’s not the owner of that eerie gaze.)

The people chasing them were clearly inferior to the owner of the gaze Rei had sensed several times.

For starters, they couldn’t completely suppress emotions like killing intent or malice… in fact, they didn’t even seem to be trying to hide them.

No matter how you look at it, they deserve to be called scum.

(Though it’s hard to imagine those nobodies would actually attack me—someone who showed that much power in a mock battle—or Set of the Griffins.)

Even someone incapable of gauging an opponent’s true strength should have realized, after watching that mock battle, that Rei was undoubtedly strong.

Despite that, Rei thought, "Why me? Don’t they know their place?" But then, he glanced up at the giant standing next to him.

He had assumed the attack was directed at him, but now he wondered if perhaps Brewitt was involved instead.

At first glance, Brewitt doesn’t look like a noble, and Rei had seen him getting into fights in town.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange if someone held a grudge against Brewitt, she thought.

“Hey, maybe… the people chasing us aren’t after me, but Brewitt?”

“Huh? Me? …Ah, I guess I can’t rule that out.”

For a moment, Brewitt looked confused, as if wondering, “Why me?” but then, perhaps recalling his own actions up to that point, he looked convinced.

“Ah… So, does that mean I’ve dragged Rei and the others into this?”

“I don’t know. There might be some guy who’d try to hurt me after seeing that mock battle… but if I think about which scenario is more likely…”

“So, it’s me after all.”

“Grr.”

Set let out a cry as if to agree with Brewitt’s words.

Even so, the fact that Rei and Set didn’t look at Brewitt with any blame likely stemmed from the fact that they themselves often found themselves in similar situations.

Brewitt smiled happily at Rei and Set’s words.

He’d thought they might accuse him of dragging them into a pointless commotion, but he was probably relieved that they didn’t actually do that.

“Anyway, we can’t enjoy good food with that crowd hanging around. We should probably take care of them somewhere out of the way. Do you know of any such place?”

Brewitt was asked by Rei where to fight, but this festival had been decided on the fly to begin with, and the food stalls set up there had also been chosen more or less by chance.

Naturally, since it hadn’t been carefully planned or prepared, there were plenty of spots that could be called blind spots.

That was true... but Brewitt had been watching Rei’s mock battle until just a little while ago, and they’d come here specifically to enjoy the food at the stalls after it ended.

Naturally, he wasn’t at all familiar with this area.

“Well… I mean, the stalls are set up in a pretty chaotic way, so if we look around, there might be a spot like that, right? It’s better to make our move before those guys start pulling any funny business in this crowd.”

“I’d have liked to enjoy the food a little longer, though. Right?”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set growled in agreement.

From Set’s perspective, he probably wanted to prioritize eating food that he could only have right now.

That said, the food stalls here are the same ones usually found in Anesis.

Naturally, if you were determined to find them, you could probably track them down.

……That said, since it’s a festival today, they are serving dishes slightly different from usual, so I can’t deny that there are some dishes you can only eat today.

In any case, deciding they wouldn’t be able to leisurely browse the stalls like this, Rei and the others set out to find a place where they could run wild without being noticed or causing a commotion, and where they wouldn’t break anything.

Normally, moving around while being followed by people approaching them with ill intent would be mentally draining… but this is a group consisting of Rei, Set, and Brewitt.

Accustomed to rough situations, Rei and the others continued walking through the festival grounds without seeming particularly bothered.

Rei would have liked to stop at a food stall along the way to buy something to eat, but given the current situation, that was out of the question.

Especially since the mock battle had just ended, there were many people looking to satisfy their hunger at the stalls, and the lines were quite long.

And naturally, Rei—who had just fought in the mock battle—and Set, who had appeared earlier, were drawing attention from the other patrons.

(If this keeps up, the guys chasing us will either lose their patience and attack us here... or give up on attacking. The latter would be fine, but if it’s the former, it’d be a total disaster. We need to find a secluded spot ASAP.)

Seeing how visibly irritated the group chasing them looked, Rei felt a slight sense of panic…

“Grr…”

Suddenly, Set growled and tugged at Rei’s Dragon Robe.

Rei didn’t know where Set was trying to take him, but he knew things would go badly if they stayed put, so he let Set lead him away.

Brewitt, who was traveling with Rei, followed after Rei and Set.

And so, Set led Rei and the others to a place with few people. Specifically, it was the area where the leftover materials from this festival were stored.

Until this morning, this place must have been bustling with festival preparations, but now that the festival was underway, there were few people around.

...The reason a few people are still here even though the festival has started is likely because they’re acting as guards to prevent the leftover festival supplies from being stolen.

That’s why the people still there cast suspicious glances at Rei and the others when they suddenly appeared… but upon seeing Set, they stopped in their tracks, looking bewildered.

Seeing a griffin out of the blue, that reaction was only natural.

Especially since the main event of this festival is both Rei’s mock battle and Set’s introduction.

In other words, if Set is here, that means one of the people with him must be Rei.

“What on earth is someone like that doing here?” they wondered… but before one of the men could say anything, Rei spoke up.

“Sorry about this. I was looking for a quiet spot and ended up here. Things are about to get chaotic, so if you don’t want to get hurt, you’d better hide or move somewhere else.”

Normally, I should probably have someone call the guards, but it’s a hassle to go to that trouble with someone who seems to hold a grudge against Brewitt.

Or rather, in this case, it would be a hassle to explain the situation to the guards after the commotion was over.

“Huh? What the hell are you doing all of a sudden…?”

From the perspective of the men in charge of this area, there was no way they could just nod and agree to that.

That’s exactly why they were trying to get the details… but before Rei could even respond to the man’s words, things started to move.

More than ten men emerged in a group from the direction Rei and the others had come from.

Among the men who arrived, the one at the front spotted Rei and his group. He looked around and broke into a delighted smile.

“Heh, it looked like you were trying to run away from us, but I never thought you’d end up running into a place with no escape from us. Bad luck.”

The man laughed smugly, but the look Rei gave him was one of utter exasperation.

If it were just Brewitt, that might be one thing, but here was Rei, who had shown such strength even in a mock battle, and above all, Set was present.

In a situation like this, why could he be so cocky…? From Rei’s perspective, it was completely incomprehensible.

“If anyone’s unlucky, I’d say it’s you guys, not us. To begin with, do you really think you can take us on with such a small group?”

“Huh? That’s obvious, isn’t it?!?”

For some reason, the man shouted with all his might at the end of his sentence.

Watching the man, Rei sensed the presence of the men who would normally have remained behind as guards drifting away. Relieved that no bystanders had been drawn into the conflict, he continued speaking.

“Why the sudden scream? What’s wrong? Did Brewitt really give you guys such a hard time? I don’t know what you guys had a fight with Brewitt over, but there’s no need to get that angry.”

“……Huh?”

The man, who had been screaming just seconds ago, flashes a genuinely puzzled expression, as if he doesn’t understand what Rei just said.

"Huh?" Rei felt a momentary pang of confusion at the man’s reaction.

Why is he looking so puzzled right now?

Logically speaking, in this situation, it wouldn’t be strange for him to react by either rebelling against what Rei said or, depending on the case, agreeing with her.

But even so, the fact that he was making that particular expression right now was unexpected, even to Rei.

“……”

Rei and Brewitt exchanged silent glances.

Rei’s gaze seemed to accuse him, asking if he wasn’t the cause of this, while Brewitt’s gaze pleaded that while he had thought so, it was actually not the case.

And so, eventually, Rei turned to the men in front of him and asked.

“Just to be clear, is your target Brewitt… this guy right here?”

“Huh? We’ve got no use for a big guy like that. What we want is your life!”

Seeing him state it so bluntly, Rei’s face takes on a subtle expression, a mix of surprise and understanding.

Having made such a conspicuous entrance, Rei could tell that his opponents weren’t planning any particular kind of trick.

But… since they were here, they must have known that Rei had defeated several opponents in the mock battle.

(No, maybe they came here after the mock battles ended and didn’t see them… but even so, that leaves a lot of questions. Setting me aside, why doesn’t he seem the least bit intimidated by Set?)

If he hadn’t watched the mock battles, it wouldn’t be strange for him not to be afraid of Rei.

But with Set—who stood over three meters tall—right there, the fact that he showed absolutely no fear of him was clearly abnormal.

“Come on, just die already. Then we can all be happy. Man, we’re lucky we only have to kill one guy like this.”

At the man’s words, the others chimed in as if to agree.

(It seems like they’re communicating, but maybe not? Well, that’s pretty common with scum like this, so it’s not surprising.)

I couldn’t help but think that, but there was something off about it.

It wasn’t that they were speaking over Rei’s words; it was more like they genuinely weren’t hearing him at all—that was the sense of something off.

“Turns out it wasn’t me after all—it was you.”

“I guess so. …But honestly, what’s up with the fact that they don’t seem the least bit intimidated by Set? Normally, I don’t think anyone could possibly say something like that in front of Set.”

Brewitt couldn’t help but agree with Rei’s words.

For a moment—just a split second—he wondered if those men might actually be skilled enough to defeat Set, but he immediately dismissed the thought.

Judging by the way they moved their bodies and wielded their weapons, he could tell they had no chance of fighting Set—let alone standing a chance against a Rank D adventurer considered a veteran.

“Are you guys out of your minds? You think you can beat me with skills like that?”

“Uuuu… uu, u… Shut the hell up!”

The moment he heard Rei’s words—which could hardly even be called a provocation—the man suddenly screamed.

Rei didn’t understand why he’d snapped like that so suddenly, but the other men reacted to his scream, each grabbing a weapon and charging straight toward Rei and his group.
