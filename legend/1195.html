The day after his conversation with Elena, Rei was asleep as usual on the bed in the room at Dusk Wheat inn.

But Rei, who had been in a deep sleep, suddenly opened his eyes.

Normally, it would take Rei several minutes... or even over ten minutes to fully wake up from his drowsiness. Yet, despite having just awakened, his consciousness was already clear.

He sat up immediately, his gaze sharply fixed on the door... and almost simultaneously, the door was pounded violently.

"Please wake up, Mr. Rei. I'm sorry, but we need you to come to the Guild immediately!"

The voice coming from beyond the door was likely that of a guild employee.

Usually, if something happened requiring Rei specifically, it was often his assigned handler, Lenora... he thought, but seeing how it was still dim outside the window, he understood the reason.

Simply put, Lenora probably hadn't started her shift yet.

(But why the rush? It's still dark outside. That must mean it's something urgent.)

After lighting the magic item to brighten the room and putting on the Dragon Robe to get ready, a sudden bad feeling crossed his mind.

(Could something have happened to the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear? If so, I'd understand why she's in such a rush.)

He was borrowing cherished weapons from adventurers... magic items, no less. If they were stolen, it would be no wonder he was this panicked.

Of course, that "no choice" is from the guild's perspective. For Rei, who might have had her magic items stolen, "no choice" is no excuse.

(Now then, what could this be about?)

Opening the door, there stood a guild employee, just as expected.

Rei had seen him a few times at the guild before.

(Huh?)

But upon meeting him, Rei felt a sense of unease.

The expression on the guild official's face before him was certainly not one of someone contemplating an apology to Rei.

On the contrary, there was even a look of joy on his face.

(This isn't about a magic item being stolen or anything like that?)

Puzzled, Rei addressed the person before him.

"So early in the morning... or is it late at night? Anyway, what brings you here at this hour?"

"Yes. Actually, we need you to come to the guild, Rei."

"...May I ask why?"

"We'll discuss that on the way. A carriage is waiting outside the inn."

Judging from the guild employee's hurried demeanor that he'd understand the reason once they got there, Ray nodded.

"Understood. ...And it's just me? I'm traveling with Vihera too."

"A female staff member is already on her way to meet her. She should be at the carriage shortly."

"...She will?"

Rei knew women took time to get ready.

Especially in Vihera's case, given her naturally striking features and the various specialized garments she wore, it took even longer than usual. He knew this from their time traveling together.

"If it takes too long, we'll have her join us later... For now, let's just go. Please."

With the guild employee bowing like that, Rei couldn't refuse, even though he didn't have any particular business.

Moreover, while it seemed the worst-case scenario of his magic items being stolen had been avoided, that very fact made him intensely curious about what had actually happened.

"Understood."

In the end, he gave a short nod to the guild clerk's words and stepped out of the room.

Descending the stairs to the first floor, he found an unfamiliar face at the inn's reception desk.

Who is that? he wondered for a moment, but since they were at the front desk, he assumed they were an employee of the Dusk Wheat inn.

Indeed, the person had accepted the guild official's apology for the disturbance caused that morning... no, that started in the middle of the night, without question, so it must be correct.

Stepping outside the inn, he saw the carriage he had seen several times before.

The person at the driver's seat was someone Rei had never seen before, but they were wearing a guild uniform, so they were undoubtedly a guild employee.

"Well then, please come in. We've prepared some simple food and drinks inside, so if you're hungry or thirsty, help yourself."

"Huh? Aren't you going to explain the situation?"

"Yes. But I need to check on Mr. Vihera first. If it takes too long, as I mentioned earlier, we'll have to go ahead and send another carriage."

"...Well, fine. But before that, can I go check on Set? Leaving the inn at this hour without a word will probably worry him."

"Huh? Oh, sure. Of course you can... But wait, do Tamers... can they sense each other's location even when apart?"

Rei's words prompted the guild employee to ask curiously.

It wasn't because of Tamer... but rather, precisely because of Beast Magic, that Set could sense Rei's magical power and detect his location... but he couldn't say that.

"Well, Set and I have been together since we were kids. We share a deep bond. Especially since Set is a rare breed among griffins."

In the end, he resorted to his usual excuse to gloss over it.

The guild clerk didn't question Rei's explanation.

Well, perhaps they did have doubts, but it was more accurate to say they didn't voice them.

Standing before the guild staff was a high-ranking adventurer with a notorious reputation.

If they did anything to upset him, who knows what disadvantages might arise?

"Understood. However, I'd like to hurry as much as possible, so I don't have much time... Is that alright?"

"No problem. Then, let's get started."

Saying only that, Rei left the guild clerk behind and headed toward the stables.

Even if some were early risers, they'd likely still be asleep at this hour.

As such, Rei reached the stables without encountering anyone.

"Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

Set must have sensed Rei's arrival. The moment the stable door opened, that deep, rumbling whinny echoed through the space.

Horses inside the stable that were accustomed to Set either remained asleep upon hearing the sound or opened their eyes briefly before drifting back to sleep. However, horses not yet accustomed to him couldn't do the same, their bodies stiffening in fear.

It was fortunate that, due to the Ambris incident, fewer people were coming to Gilm, meaning most of the animals in the stable were already accustomed to Set.

Even though it was still dim, Set noticed Rei had arrived and greeted him with a happy purr. As Rei stroked Set, who was rubbing his head against him demanding attention, Rei spoke.

"I've been called to the guild. Sorry, but I need to step out for a bit."

"Gururu?"

"Are you okay?" Set tilted his head slightly.

Set probably understood that being summoned at this hour meant something significant had happened.

Stroking Set's head reassuringly, Rei spoke.

"Since Marina is the Guild Master, I don't think she'd do anything strange. At first, I thought maybe the magic item we left with her had been stolen, but apparently that's not the case either."

"Grrr..."

Hearing Rei's explanation, Set felt a little relieved.

Still, he seemed lonely at the thought of being left behind, a sad gleam in his round eyes.

But he understood that throwing a tantrum now would only cause trouble for Rei. Set didn't insist any further.

"Well, I'm heading out. Get some rest, Set. You'll probably have to move around a lot tomorrow... no, today too."

As a griffin, Set possessed a physiology that could easily go without sleep for days on end.

But needing no sleep and choosing not to sleep were entirely different matters.

Besides, perhaps due to being created by beast magic, Set didn't dislike sleeping at all.

He especially loved sleeping under the spring sun, in the shade of summer trees, or in the crisp autumn air. His sturdy griffin physique even let him sleep normally in winter snow.

He does enjoy sleeping, but he enjoys being with Rei even more.

When Rei camps, Set stands guard. If anyone who might harm his beloved Rei approaches, he springs into action instantly.

"Grrrrrrr."

Giving Set one last pat as he growled "Be careful," Rei left the stable.

Returning to the inn's front... where the carriage was parked, he found Vihera just emerging, freshly dressed and ready.

Though she should have been barely awake, as far as Rei could tell, Vihera looked exactly as she always did.

Wearing her usual light robes, she looked every bit the Vihera.

...Though, having gotten ready in such a short time, there were a few places that weren't quite as usual... Fortunately or unfortunately, Rei didn't notice them.

The joy of not having her beloved see her untidy parts, and the frustration of wanting him to notice even the smallest changes.

Feeling the conflicting feminine emotions within herself, Vihera spoke.

"Looks like you're ready over there."

"Yeah. Getting a man ready is easy enough when you put your mind to it."

Of course, that was only true for adventurers like Rei.

Even those of higher standing, like nobles or great merchants, could usually get ready faster than women, barring exceptional circumstances.

"Right. Then let's board the carriage. You'll explain the situation during the ride, won't you?"

Vihera said, glancing behind her.

At the end of her gaze stood the male guild employee who had come to wake Rei, and the female guild employee who had likely gone to wake Vihera.

"Yes, I'll do that. So let's hurry."

Following the male guild employee's voice, Rei climbed into the carriage with Vihera.

The guild must have realized it was unreasonable to summon Rei and Vihera at this hour. Whether it was Marina's instruction or another guild employee's, the carriage they boarded was exceptionally well-equipped.

Reaching for the sandwiches already prepared, Rei asked the guild employee.

"So, I think it's about time you explained the situation, don't you?"

"Well... To put it simply, we identified remnants of Ambris from the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear we borrowed from you, Rei. From those remnants, we've completed what you might call an Ambris detector—a magic item."

"...Huh?"

That was the dumbfounded sound that escaped Rei's mouth at the guild clerk's explanation.

Vihera, though silent, couldn't hide her astonished expression.

Rei, who had paused his hand reaching for a sandwich, finally spoke.

"If that's true, that's incredibly fast. It hasn't even been a day since I handed over the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, you know?"

"Yes. I understand how you feel, Rei. Honestly, the developments were so sudden even for the guild. When we received the report, it caused quite a bit of confusion."

The male guild employee's words seemed to say he understood Rei's feelings. The female guild employee beside him nodded in agreement.

Normally, just creating a magic item takes an enormous amount of time.

Just think about how long it took to create the Twilight Spear that Rei possesses.

Of course, the Twilight Spear required gathering various rare materials, so it undoubtedly took longer than a typical magic item.

"Is it real? You didn't just say you completed a magic item that doesn't exist to cover something up, did you?"

Vihera, having calmed down slightly after drinking the fruit water prepared in the carriage, asked the guild official.

The guild clerk understood why she might be suspicious. Nodding at Vihera's words, he spoke.

"Naturally, when we first heard the story, the guild suspected the same thing. However, after investigating, we concluded that this magic item is definitely not a fake."

"Seems? Why so vague? If it's truly a magic item that can find Ambris, why not just try using it once?"

"Yes. The guild thought so too, of course... Unfortunately, we couldn't activate it."

"Huh?"

Seeing Rei's expression of complete confusion, the guild employee gave a wry smile and continued.

"According to the creator of that magic item, they succeeded in extracting the remnants of Ambris, but they couldn't purge the magic power from the objects that contained those remnants... the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear. Of course, it could be done given enough time, but right now, every moment counts..."

Hearing this, Rei finally understood why he had been summoned.
