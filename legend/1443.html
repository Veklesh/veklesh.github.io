Several days after Rei returned to Gilm and the party was held... On a day when the summer heat grew increasingly intense, and moving during the day caused sweat to pour out in buckets... Rei's figure was within Gilm.

Of course, it wasn't just Rei. Marina, Vihera, Byune, and Set—the entire Crimson Wings crew—were all gathered there.

Though, even within Gilm, the space was vast.

Rei was at the spot where they planned to destroy the wall next.

When Rei first appeared, several craftsmen unfamiliar with Gilm had looked displeased. But after being told what kind of being Rei was... and above all, after seeing Set up close, no one voiced any complaints about Rei anymore.

At least outwardly, anyway.

"Now then, we begin demolishing the wall! Once it's gone, monsters could appear at any moment. Adventurers, please be ready to respond immediately. And please clear the debris as quickly as possible!"

One of the craftsmen, forced into the role of explainer, shouted to everyone near the wall.

At his words, the many adventurers gathered there, besides Rei, shouted in response.

The adventurers' tasks, as the craftsman man shouted, were to deal with any monsters that might approach due to the wall being destroyed and to clear the rubble.

Neither task was more important than the other; both were crucial.

If the rubble wasn't cleared quickly, it would take extra time to rebuild the town and reinforce the walls. But they couldn't afford to relax their guard against monsters either.

"Alright, let's get to it!"

As the official shouted this, the wall demolition finally began.

As the dwarf craftsman made his move, a sound emanating from the wall echoed around them in perfect sync.

The sound, which seemed to resonate directly in one's gut, gradually grew louder... until eventually, countless cracks appeared in the wall.

Cracks spread across a significant portion of Gilm's wall, and the sound emanating from it grew louder as time passed.

Then, in the next instant... with a sound that seemed to reverberate throughout the entire Gilm, the wall began to collapse.

Rei had no idea what means had been used to pull off such a feat.

But even so, the scene currently unfolding before Rei's eyes was incredibly powerful.

First, a fragment of wall fell from above onto the ground, and soon other sections began collapsing as well.

The walls shattered and fell to the ground... over a very wide area.

That very fact gave the scene enough impact to threaten Rei.

It started slowly, but the next moment, the walls collapsed at an accelerating pace. It looked exactly like a rock avalanche, a landslide—nothing else.

"Whoa..."

That sound reached Rei's ears, but he couldn't tell who had uttered it.

No, it was probably the minority who even heard it.

The surrounding area was filled with the deafening roar of the walls being destroyed... no, collapsing on their own.

A roar so immense it felt like every soul in Gilm must have heard it.

Among those around Rei, some were covering their ears, likely because it was too loud.

The rabbit beastman adventurer—a male, mind you—even bent the ears protruding from his head backward.

(Doesn't that hurt?)

Rei thought that as he glanced at the rabbit beastman man, but quickly turned his gaze back to the wall.

Strangely, none of the debris from the collapsed wall seemed to be falling onto the Gilm side.

Every single piece landed outside of Gilm.

It could be said this scene was born from the meticulous calculations of those who carried out this destruction, or perhaps from years of experience and intuition as craftsmen.

How long had the wall actually been collapsing...? Watching it, Rei felt it could have been seconds, tens of seconds, or even minutes.

Then, as part of the wall covering Gilm... no, a surprisingly large section collapsed safely. While everyone stood stunned, one dwarf shouted.

"Hey! The wall's down! Get back to your damn jobs!"

Though short in stature, the muscles covering his body made him look anything but small.

In fact, his physique seemed far more robust than that of many adventurers whose only claim to fame was their bodies.

His voice, as loud as the wall's crashing roar, finally snapped the others back to their senses.

"Hey, let's go! Get that rubble cleared out fast! And those in charge of defense watch, get to your assigned areas immediately!"

"Ralda, Zebii, let's go! The more rubble we clear, the more our profits grow!"

"Everyone, let's go! It's daytime now, but with that much noise echoing around, monsters are likely to come! Be careful not to push yourselves too hard."

Amidst the various voices echoing around them, Rei called out to the party members nearby.

"Alright, then, let's get moving. Everyone knows what they need to do, right?"

Marina, Vihera, and Byune each nodded in response to Rei's question, while Set made a guttural sound.

Rei's first task was clearing the massive debris blocking the exit from Gilm. After that, he prepared the ground by raising and lowering it as instructed by the craftsmen, laying the groundwork for constructing a new wall.

...Of course, Rei wasn't the only one working to level the ground.

Looking around, Rei could see several figures who appeared to be mages.

Among them were several spirit mages, including Marina.

「……Rei？」

One of those spirit mages caught Rei's gaze and murmured in surprise.

And Rei understood exactly who that spirit mage was.

"Feirma?"

He asked for confirmation, probably just to be sure.

An elf who primarily wielded a bow and spirit magic, she was a classmate from when Rei took his first rank-up exam.

An elf who wielded spirit magic and used a bow as her weapon.

Considering her combat style, she was very similar to Marina.

The difference was that Feilma was an elf, while Marina was a dark elf.

"Yeah, it's been a while."

"Yeah. ...I'd love to catch up, but I don't have time right now. How about grabbing a bite together after we finish today's work?"

For Rei, these words came purely from wanting to talk with Feilma.

He had seen the three who took the rank-up exam with him—Culotte, Arogan, and Skola—fairly often, but he hadn't seen the elf Feilma at all... nor the swordsman Spelvia.

That was precisely why he'd spoken up, wanting to renew old ties after so long.

It had been several years, so strictly speaking, it might not yet qualify as old acquaintances, but Rei and the others were adventurers.

In a profession where danger is ever-present, it's not uncommon for acquaintances to die not just after years, but after months... or even days if things go badly.

Given that, it wasn't unusual for Rei to invite someone he hadn't heard much about since the rank-up exams to grab a meal.

But to those unaware, it could only look like Rei was hitting on Feilma...

"Hmm."

It was Vihera who spoke up, looking slightly displeased.

Marina, the former guildmaster, seemed particularly unfazed, likely because she knew Feilma, who had taken the rank-up exam alongside Rei.

Of course, she wouldn't normally remember every single person who took the rank-up exam, but Feilma was a fairly rare elf even within the Gilm guild, and above all, the fact that she took the exam with Rei had left a strong impression.

That's precisely why Marina understood what kind of person Feilma was.

"Anyway, work comes first right now. Let's talk about it tonight."

While Vihera's glare was intimidating, a glance around made it clear that wasn't the only reason she said it.

Dozens, hundreds... more than a hundred adventurers, craftsmen, and even day laborers were moving toward the wall outside.

In such a situation, it was only natural that those standing still like Rei and the others would be in the way.

In fact, some of those heading outside the wall were shooting them looks that clearly said they were in the way.

"Yeah, you're right. I guess we should get to work for now. Oh, I'm staying at the Dusk Wheat inn. Drop by tonight if you're free."

"You're still staying at that inn? I'm jealous,"

Feilmah said this with a wistful tone. For her, the Dusk Wheat inn was undoubtedly a luxury inn whose price tag made her hesitate to stay there.

Rei had stayed there when he first came to Gilm and it had become his regular lodging, but that was something only Rei could manage.

Even with a certain level of confidence in one's skills, rank-ups weren't something ordinary people could achieve so easily.

That said, it wasn't that Feerma's rank-up speed was slow; it was simply that Rei was exceptional.

Anyway, after making plans with Rei, Feerma simply walked away.

"Well then, I suppose we should get moving too."

"...I have a lot I want to ask you, but I'll put that off for now."

Vihera said this and headed out of town with Byune.

Vihera, who relished combat, and the thief Byune naturally joined the group tasked with keeping watch for monsters outside.

Though, it was daytime now.

Powerful monsters were still more likely to appear at night.

"Well then, let's go too."

"Yeah, you're right."

"Grr."

As Vihera and Byune departed, the remaining two—or rather, two people and one beast, including Set—also left the spot.

Stepping outside through the collapsed wall, they found a large crowd already hard at work.

Among them, the largest group was undoubtedly those carrying away the rubble created by the collapsed wall.

Some carried it in wooden baskets, others carried it by hand, perhaps because they were alone, and still others carried it in leather sacks... Among them, a fair number were preparing carts.

(Well, it'll probably take a while longer before carts can pass through.)

Right now, with countless pieces of rubble scattered everywhere, it's impossible for carts to pass. However, the wall was destroyed over a fairly large area, and the rubble is being cleared away steadily.

While they gather the rubble onto carts, it's certain that one or two carriages will be able to pass through.

"Well, once we clear that pile of rubble over there, carriages should be able to pass through."

"You guys work on that. I'll go handle the ground leveling."

"Grrrrrrr!"

At Rei's words, Marina said that and went over the wall, with Set following behind.

That said, Set wasn't going to be leveling soil; he'd be patrolling the surrounding area from the sky.

Since flying enemies were extremely troublesome foes, considerable expectations inevitably fell upon Set.

...Though, to Set, it was just another day of flying around as usual.

"Do your best."

Calling out to the one person and one beast, Rei also crossed the spot where the wall had once stood and stepped outside of Gilm.

(The guards and the adventurers who took the request... they're going to have their hands full, huh?)

Touching a nearby piece of debris about four meters in diameter and storing it in his Misty Ring, Rei pondered the hardships the guards and adventurers were about to face.

Now that the wall was destroyed, it was only natural that anyone drawn to Gilm would set their sights on it.

Entering Gilm without going through official procedures would be a significant advantage, at least in terms of criminal activity.

(Well, I don't need to worry about the Noble Faction... but could the Royal Faction show up?)

Due to the Lerdact incident, Rei recognized that the possibility of the Noble Faction getting involved in this expansion project was now virtually zero...

But even Rei didn't know how the Royal Faction, the largest of the three major factions, would react.

If they made a move, it was certain some sort of disturbance would occur at the planned wall construction site.

"Ah, Rei, excuse me, but could you handle that pile of rubble over there?"

A nearby adventurer asked him, and he looked over at the rather large... probably six-meter-tall pile of rubble.

While they generally tried to break things down into smaller pieces, exceptions inevitably occurred.

The reason Rei was asked to handle large debris in the first place was precisely because of that situation.

"Yeah, got it. I'll take care of it right away."

Saying that, he stored it in the Misty Ring... truly clearing it up immediately, Rei moved on to stow the next piece of debris into the Misty Ring.
