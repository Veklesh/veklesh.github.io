After requesting that the Snow Ape be dismantled, Rei heads to the guard post with Set.

Naturally, this was to ask if there had been any progress regarding the sentry at the outpost that Grange had mentioned yesterday.

Arrangements had been made so that if there were any major developments, Rei would be notified through the guild.

However, it would not be unreasonable to assume that if the progress was minor, there would be no need to go out of their way to contact Rei.

……In fact, even if there were movements that didn’t seem particularly significant, going out of their way to contact Rei through the guild would be a burden for both the guards and the guild.

In that sense, it’s certainly not wrong for Rei to come here himself to hear the report directly.

On the way to the guardhouse, he told those who saw him that he had no time to play around today… yet, since he was casually buying food from the food stalls along the way, it ended up taking longer than expected to finally arrive at the guardhouse.

“Alright, Set. I’m going to have a quick chat, so I’ll leave this to you.”

“Grr!”

In response to Rei’s words, Set let out a growl to signal he understood, then lay down on the ground a short distance from the guard post.

Rei placed a few of the meat buns he’d bought from a food stall on the way in front of Set. Glancing sideways at Set happily moving his beak as he ate the buns, Rei knocked on the headquarters door.

The door was knocked on, and after a few seconds, it opened.

“...Rei? About yesterday’s incident?”

“Yeah. I just wanted to ask how things turned out. …Considering I haven’t heard anything through the guild, it seems there hasn’t been much progress.”

When Rei said this, the guard nodded and motioned for him to come inside the guardhouse.

The first thing Rei noticed upon entering the guardhouse was how few guards there were.

But that was only natural. They needed to deploy personnel to keep watch over what appeared to be the enemy’s stronghold.

“As Rei said, there hasn’t been any significant activity so far.”

The guard said this as he handed Rei a cup.

Inside the wooden cup was warm water.

It was what is commonly known as plain hot water.

“Sorry, this is all we’ve got.”

“No, don’t worry about it. In this cold, even something like this is enough to warm you up.”

Rei replied to the guard, though thanks to the Dragon Robe’s abilities, he wasn’t feeling the cold at all.

Still, out of consideration for the guard’s kindness, he didn’t refuse and brought the hot water to his lips.

(Couldn’t they have made tea from ordinary leaves? I seem to recall there being persimmon leaf tea… Well, I don’t know if persimmons even exist in this world. Thinking about it, it seems like they could dry ordinary plant leaves to make tea. This is the frontier, after all, so it wouldn’t be strange if there were some kind of special plant here.)

Even as he thought that, Rei didn’t actually know how to make tea.

It’s just that I feel like I’ve seen it on TV shows—leaves being sun-dried or roasted.

“So, about their base over there—I get that there aren’t any major movements, but aren’t there any minor ones either?”

“Unfortunately, no. It seems they’re being extremely cautious over there. …Which is only natural.”

At the guard’s words, the other guards nodded in agreement.

In fact, that wasn’t an exaggeration at all.

Even for a Kobold, bringing a monster into the city was a serious offense. If they were found and captured, the death penalty was certain.

No, actually, if it were simply the death penalty, that would almost be considered lenient.

In the worst-case scenario, they could end up being tortured endlessly, only to be healed with potions or recovery magic… and the cycle would repeat itself.

“If that happens, what do we do? Sure, I’m blocking the Kobolds’ entry with an earthen wall, but it’s not exactly foolproof. As proof of that, when I checked yesterday, I saw a few adventurers fighting Kobolds.”

“The problem is how long that earthen wall will hold out. Yesterday, it just so happened that Rei ran into Grange and the others, so there weren’t any particular issues, but there must be other former Red Cloth members out there, right? I don’t know exactly how many are left, though.”

"Sending people over there... that might be difficult."

"Yeah. If we were to try and guard the earthen wall, we’d end up fighting the Kobolds who managed to climb over it first. We guards are generally good at fighting people, but we’re not that great at fighting monsters."

That was only natural, considering the nature of a guard’s job.

Generally speaking, the people guards are supposed to fight are those who get drunk and cause trouble in town, or those who commit some kind of crime.

They’ve undergone combat training for such opponents, but when it comes to monsters, it’s a different story.

……Of course, there are summoned beasts, and if monsters were to band together and attack Gilm, the guards would have no choice but to fight them… but the opponents we’re usually meant to fight are, after all, people.

That said, when I say “people,” there are various races, such as humans, elves, dwarves, and beastmen.

“In that case, adventurers? If they’re the type who’d gladly go hunt down Kobolds, they might actually be happy to take on guard duty at the earthen walls.”

Another guard in the room suggested this, but Rei shook his head.

“If someone’s happy to fight Kobolds, they’d probably want to fight even more of them—and might end up breaking the earthen wall. It’s not a wall that can be destroyed that easily, but I still want to avoid any risk if possible.”

That said, the walls have been made quite sturdy by Meru and Marina’s spirit magic.

Even someone with only enough strength to fight Kobolds wouldn’t be able to break them that easily.

“Is that so? But sending someone from the guards is still out of the question. If that’s the case, we’ll have no choice but to leave the earthen wall as is—is that really okay?”

“Hmm… you’re right. Maybe I’ll ask Marina if she can do something about it with her elemental magic.”

Even to Rei, the breadth of Marina’s application of spirit magic was clearly extraordinary.

That said, it wasn’t simply because spirit magic was inherently that flexible.

Rather, it was the high level of adaptability that came solely from the fact that it was Marina using it.

(When Marina isn’t home, spirits guard her house and stop any strangers who try to break in. …In fact, they go so far as to capture them or, in some cases, even attack them. So, couldn’t they do something similar with the earthen walls?)

Rei thought this through and decided to suggest it to Marina over dinner tonight.

“Guild Master… no, former Guild Master, I suppose. To be honest, I genuinely think it’s amazing that you can get along normally with someone like that.”

One of the guards who had been listening to Rei’s words looked at him with admiration… and at the same time, a look of intense envy.

For those living in Gilm, Marina is someone many admire, and many see her as an unattainable ideal.

However, very few people are truly capable of courting Marina.

Part of it is her exceptional ability, and part of it is her beauty. But Marina wasn’t just a pretty face—she possessed a beauty imbued with a powerful, feminine allure.

Since Marina could be described as almost too perfect in some ways, the only people capable of making such advances were either those with extreme confidence in their own abilities or those who simply didn’t understand anything at all.

In any case, given that Marina was such a person, the fact that Rei was teaming up with her—and, on top of that, dining at her house almost every night—was astonishing to many.

Moreover, anyone with even a modicum of insight into romantic affairs could easily discern the meaning behind the way Marina looked at Rei.

“Hmm, well, I know Marina’s pretty amazing in a lot of ways, but when you’re with her, she’s just a normal girl.”

In a way, calling Marina an “ordinary woman” here was almost absurd… but unfortunately, she herself never seemed to notice.

“Don’t do it. Rei’s the kind of guy who has plenty of other beauties just as stunning as Marina at his beck and call, you know? There’s no point in saying something like that.”

“...No, the phrase ‘keeping them around’ is a bit off, don’t you think?”

From an outsider’s perspective, “keeping them around” might be the appropriate phrase, but Rei himself didn’t intend it that way.

That’s precisely why Rei voiced his dissatisfaction, but the guard merely shot him a look of exasperation.

Faced with that look, Rei realized he was at a disadvantage in this situation and changed the subject.

“So, have you found out anything about the enemy base you’ve been watching? Like, who owns that building, for instance?”

“Ah, we looked into that. But… unfortunately, the owner isn’t currently in Gilm. We don’t even know if they’re aware of it. It’s possible they know the owner isn’t in Gilm right now and are using the place as a base without permission.”

“...Without permission, huh. If they’re pulling a stunt like that, I’m sure the truth will come out eventually. Anyway, is it okay if I ask who owns that building? Oh, that is, if you’re willing to tell me, of course.”

“That’s fine. The building is owned by the Viscount Cobbs, a noble family aligned with the Royal Faction.”

When he heard the name “Viscount Cobus,” Rei tried to recall if it sounded familiar, but unfortunately, it was a name he’d never heard before.

That was only natural, since Rei wasn’t particularly close to the nobility to begin with.

That said, he was acquainted with a few influential nobles, starting with Daskar.

“I’ve never heard of them.”

“Well, I suppose so. They aren’t exactly a famous noble family, and besides, the person who was in Gilm wasn’t the head of the Viscount Cobus family—it must have been a relative.”

In the noble district of Gilm, besides the Neutral Faction led by Daskar, there are also mansions belonging to nobles aligned with the Royal Faction and the Noble Faction.

But, naturally, the head of the family wouldn’t live among members of a different faction—in other words, a group that’s practically hostile to them.

Since the main objective is gathering intelligence on the Neutral Faction, anyone with a certain level of ability would suffice… The member of the Cobus family in Gilm was, after all, a relative of the head of the family.

“You mentioned the Royal Faction, but what exactly is their rank? The only member of the Royal Faction I know of is Duke Quent himself.”

To be precise, it’s not Duke Quent himself, but rather that I’m close to his granddaughter, Marka Quent.

Thanks to that connection, I’ve met a few nobles from the Royal Faction… but could there be someone among them who has ties to the Viscount Cobus family? When Rei voiced this suspicion, the guard shook his head.

“No, no. The Viscount Cobus family isn’t all that significant—just as the title ‘Viscount’ suggests.”

“...If they’re not a major house, I can’t imagine them plotting something as audacious as this. So, does that mean someone—someone from somewhere—is using that building without permission? Come to think of it, it was the Cobus Viscount family, wasn’t it? Even if no one was sent directly from them, aren’t there still people connected to them there?”

“No, there’s no one. In fact, everyone—including the servants—left around the time the expansion work on Gilm began. The reason given was that with the expansion, a large number of strangers would be coming to Gilm, which could lead to a decline in public order.”

“I can’t deny that.”

In fact, regardless of whether they were adventurers or not, large numbers of people came to Gilm seeking work related to the expansion project.

And, naturally, with that many people gathered, there were bound to be some with bad behavior mixed in.

In fact, the guards’ patrols alone weren’t enough, so they even hired adventurers to help maintain order.

Among Rei’s acquaintances, Vihera and Byune were involved in that kind of work.

Even under those circumstances—and there were indeed various problems—there were several groups besides the Viscount Cobus family who decided to leave Gilm temporarily out of concern for public safety.

Considering that, Rei couldn’t deny the reason the guard had mentioned.

“In that case, is it safe to assume that the Cobus Viscount family isn’t involved in this incident after all?”

“I’m not sure. I don’t know for certain about that. I suppose I could say they probably aren’t involved…”

Hearing the guard’s words, Rei wasn’t sure how to react.
