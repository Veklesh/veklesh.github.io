Rei certainly didn’t have a high opinion of a man named Granger.

He tried to keep a low profile as much as possible, attempting to tear down the earthen wall with the help of the men in red cloth.

Not only did he dislike that behavior, but the way Granger treated the men in red cloth he led as mere pawns also strangely got under Rei’s skin.

“Well, I get that even though you’re a Red Cloth, you’re not the one who hired them. But that doesn’t change the fact that you’re still my enemy, does it? So, standing in front of me like this, what exactly do you plan to do?”

“Grr.”

Perhaps sensing Rei’s bad mood, Set growled in annoyance as he looked at the men in red cloth.

Normally, Set was praised by the residents of Gilm for his round eyes and was adored by many for his cuteness, but that was only because they were people who doted on him.

When faced with enemies, he naturally adopts an attitude to match.

And as for the men belonging to the Red Cloth… no, since they’re no longer active as part of the Red Cloth, perhaps I should say they used to belong. In any case, those men aren’t particularly strong adventurers to begin with.

In fact, most of them are likely former adventurers who dropped out along the way.

...And even if they couldn’t gauge Rei’s strength, facing the current Set—who looked ferocious at a glance—they naturally cowered.

But even in such a situation, Granger kept a smile on his face and silently looked at Rei.

“Even if you ask me what to do… well. Now that Rei has found us, there’s no way we can break through the earthen wall. So, if we just quietly leave as is, could you let us go…? Is that not possible?”

Rei smiled back at Granger, who asked this while still smiling.

That said, it wasn’t the kind of smile one would offer a friendly acquaintance; it would be more accurate to describe it as a savage grin.

“Do you really think that’ll work? No, the other Red Cloth guys might be fine with that, but there’s no way I’m letting you—who knows who hired these guys—get away.”

“Ah, I figured as much. Well then, what if I gave you information about those guys? Would you let me go?”

“Rejected. I don’t know if you’re telling the truth, and more than anything, you seem like a bad influence. If I let you go now, it’ll probably cause unnecessary trouble for Gilm. …In that case, I’d better arrest you here and hand you over to the guards or the knights.”

“That’s honestly a problem for me. …You might not believe me, but I just arrived in Gilm a little while ago, right before winter, and I haven’t done anything wrong, you know?”

“And you expect me to believe that? Besides, if you really had nothing to hide, why on earth would you get hired by those guys from the Red Cloth?”

The person hired by the group employing the Red Cloth to control the Red Cloth.

There was no way that person could be someone without any hidden motives.

And above all, the fact that he’s standing here smiling in front of Rei and Set makes him look pretty suspicious.

“Haha. Now that you mention it, that puts me in a bit of a bind. But I can’t just let myself get captured like this…”

He probably intended to say “I can’t let that happen,” but in the middle of his sentence, Granger suddenly moved his hand.

It was the exact same kind of movement Rei used when firing the arrowheads created by Nebula's Eye.

It was launched at a speed that made one wonder how many people in the room had even noticed it, but Rei’s senses were far sharper than those of an ordinary human.

He tilted his head slightly, and something small—no bigger than a fingertip—flew past the spot where Rei’s face had been just a moment ago.

“Huh!?”

He probably never expected to be dodged.

Granger gasped in surprise, but realizing there was no time to hesitate now that he’d already made his move, he immediately moved on to his next action.

“Gyah!”

“Hot!”

"Ouch!"

“Damn it, what the hell are you doing all of a sudden?!”

Screams erupted one after another from the mouths of the men in red cloth.

There was no need to say who had done it.

Granger, who had attacked Rei and then immediately fled the scene, had slipped through the gap between the men and slashed at them one after another with a dagger he’d drawn from the sheath at his waist.

Rei immediately understood why he had done such a thing.

He’d done it to slow down his own pursuit, even if just a little.

(If it had been just me, I might have done the same. But...)

Watching the men in red robes, thrown into confusion by the sudden attack, Rei shouted.

「Set！」

“Grrrrrrrrr!”

With just that single word, Set must have understood what Rei was trying to say.

Set let out a sharp cry, took a few steps to build up speed, flapped his wings, and took to the sky.

However, instead of flying at his usual altitude of about 100 meters, he flew directly over the men who were in an uproar after being cut off.

Naturally, Rei didn’t pay those men any particular attention either; using Sleipnir Boots, he kicked off from the air above the men in red cloth and moved on.

“Those guys are the Red Cloth crew, so if we take them to the guards, we might even get a reward!”

He shouted this to the other group.

This didn’t really mean much.

It’s just a matter of “if we can catch them, we’ll be lucky…”

It wouldn’t be strange to just let them escape, and given the current situation, Rei doubted the Red Cloth guys would go break down the earthen wall even if they did escape. He also wondered if they’d even bother going back to the place where they’d been used and discarded.

After all, Granger had gone so far as to stab them one after another with a dagger just to escape.

If there were anyone who could tolerate that… at the very least, they wouldn’t be acting like this, teaming up with the Red Cloth guys here, but would be spending their time on something more meaningful.

In any case, listening to the commotion behind him as those who had leaped over using the Sleipnir Boots made a racket, Rei followed Set… or, more accurately, he followed Set, who was chasing Grange.

“...Another nuisance.”

He muttered this because Grange was running through narrow spaces—the gaps between supplies, for instance—places that Set, standing over three meters tall, couldn’t fit into.

Setting Rei aside, that was undoubtedly the best choice for escaping Set, and in that sense, it wasn’t entirely incomprehensible… but from the pursuer’s perspective, it naturally wasn’t exactly fun.

(Set can change size, but there are limits, after all.)

Set’s size-changing ability isn’t powerful enough to let him squeeze into the kind of places Granger was running through—places barely wide enough for a person to pass through.

Though things might be different if his level went up by absorbing magic stones.

In any case, since he can’t pull off a stunt like that in the current situation, whenever Granger flees to a place Set can’t enter, he pursues him from the sky.

This construction site, piled high with building materials, was the worst possible battlefield for Set.

If the goal were simply to defeat or kill Granger, he could attack along with the construction materials, but this time, the objective is strictly to capture Granger. Moreover, knowing how hard the residents of Gilm worked to prepare these materials, he certainly couldn’t bring himself to do such a thing.

“Set, you just need to call out his position from above. I’ll corner him while following you!”

“Grrrrr!”

Perhaps hearing Rei’s words, Set let out a growl.

Hearing that sound from above and behind, Grange, who had been weaving his way through the construction materials, frowned slightly.

Naturally, there was no trace of the smile that had been on Grange’s face when he was in front of Rei.

Instead, his expression was one of intense concentration, focused on how he could escape from Rei and Set—two of the most troublesome opponents even within Gilm.

(This is a hassle. It was supposed to be a very simple job. …Why is Rei even here in the first place? I think I’m generally a decent person.)

He managed to squeeze through the pile of stones, twisting his body sideways.

“Under normal circumstances, I’d definitely have shaken them off by now…” As she thought this, Grange looked up at the sky, confirmed Set’s presence there, and felt a wave of annoyance wash over her.

The biggest problem of all was that the main reason he was able to escape like this was because this place was stacked with a massive amount of materials for the expansion project.

In other words, he couldn’t possibly leave this construction site with Set still hovering above him.

That said, Granger had no way to deal with Set flying in the sky, and to make matters worse, there was no doubt that not only Set but also Rei was chasing him from behind.

In such a situation, he needs to completely shake off Rei and Set, escape the construction site, and then vanish without drawing attention from those around him.

For Granger… no, for anyone, that would undoubtedly be an extremely difficult task.

But... even so, despite thinking it was a hassle, Granger didn’t think he’d get caught.

He was confident in his ability to run, and he believed that if he could run around and hide in a place with such a large amount of construction materials, he wouldn’t be found.

If he’d known that Set’s senses were sharp and that his skills included Enhanced Scent, he might have panicked more… but since that sort of information isn’t widely known even in Gilm, it was only natural that Granger didn’t know about it.

(For now, I’ll just hide so I don’t get found… There are plenty of ways to get back to Gilm from here, so I don’t need to worry about that.)

And so, after confirming that Set’s figure had vanished from the sky before she knew it… she still didn’t move immediately.

He probably knew that if he panicked and moved now, there was a high chance Set would spot him.

A few minutes passed in the gap between the construction materials. After confirming that all sounds and signs of movement had vanished from the surroundings, he clenched his hands—chilled stiff by the cold—and stepped out from the gap...

“Gah!”

In an instant, before he could even process what was happening, his neck was yanked, and a small scream escaped Granger’s lips.

Granje was confused, but being thrown to the ground caused snow to touch her body and face, and the cold helped her regain her composure.

Lying face down with her neck pinned, she couldn’t look up, but she could still move her gaze to the side… and what she saw there was a robe that looked like any ordinary one.

But setting the robe aside, it was easy to tell just by looking at the shoes that these were no ordinary shoes… and unfortunately, Grange recognized exactly whose shoes they were.

They were the magic item known as Sleipnir Boots, worn by a person of interest here in Gilm.

“W-why…?”

“Hm? What do you mean, ‘why’? Did you really think you could escape me with a move like that? If so, you’ve really underestimated me.”

Granger’s neck was grabbed from behind and pressed against the ground as Rei replied.

The recipient had a lot on his mind regarding Rei’s words, but before he could say anything, Rei continued speaking.

“Still, you were lucky to come out this way. If you’d come out the other side, Set would’ve been waiting for you.”

“Wha—”

Rei and Set. There was no doubt he would have been caught either way, but if he was going to be caught anyway, it was clear that Rei was the better option than the griffin, Set.

Feeling a slight sense of relief at that… and then a single question arose.

“Why on earth are you here? There shouldn’t have been any signs of anyone around…”

“I’m not exactly an expert at hiding my presence, but it’s not like I can’t do it. Of course, if you were more skilled, you probably would’ve sensed me.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Granger felt the gulf in their abilities more acutely than ever.

Starting with this Red Cloth incident, he’d handled plenty of shady jobs in the past.

That’s precisely why he was skilled at sensing and concealing his presence—yet to be caught so effortlessly in a situation where he was completely helpless was something he had never anticipated.

“...I give up. I won’t resist anymore, so could you let me up?”

Convinced of his defeat… Granger spoke those words to Rei, who was pinning him down.
