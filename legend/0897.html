After gazing at the magic stone and spending a leisurely time, it was an hour when he would normally have been asleep. Yet Rei was receiving a visitor in his room.

Rei understood that he was, to some extent, a well-known adventurer.

That's precisely why he knew it wasn't unusual for someone to come calling at this hour—though it was still a bit early to call it late at night.

(Some urgent request... or someone trying to make a name for themselves by defeating me... or something else entirely. Now then, who could it be?)

Prepared to handle whatever came his way, he approached the door and called out to the person on the other side.

"What's the matter?"

"I have a confidential matter to discuss. Might I come in, if you don't mind?"

The voice was unfamiliar even to Rei.

Probably someone he'd never met before... he thought as he opened the door.

And standing before Rei, who had opened the door, was, unsurprisingly, someone he didn't recognize.

"...Who are you?"

At the words that came from Rei's mouth, the man—mid-twenties, average build and height—bowed deeply.

"Nice to meet you, Rei. My name is Siest. Actually, I've come bearing a proposal for you from a certain individual. Would you be willing to hear me out?"

"...Fine. Well, this isn't the best place to talk. Would the cafeteria be okay?"

"The cafeteria, you say? I'd prefer not to have others overhear our conversation if possible."

"Well, that's how it is. If we're talking about another place, then my room is the only option, right?"

"Yes, that's fine. No, actually, I'd be most grateful if we could. To be allowed into the room of the renowned Rei would be the greatest honor."

Laying an exaggeratedly delighted Siest a somewhat exasperated look, Rei invited him inside.

Letting a man she'd just met into her room was extremely careless.

But in Rei's case, she was confident she could handle any unexpected situation immediately if it were just an ordinary person.

That is, unless the person was someone like Noiz, the Rank S adventurer who fought in the Bestir Empire.

"Alright, come in. Unfortunately, I don't have anything like a sofa, so please sit in that chair."

Rei instructed as she invited Siest in and sat down on the bed herself.

Siest's expression stiffened for a moment, but the next instant, he smoothed it out and spoke.

"Thank you. I'll take my leave, then."

Saying that, Siest entered the room and sat down on the single chair inside, just as Rei had instructed.

Once he confirmed this, Rei immediately spoke up.

"So, what brings you here at this hour... well, not exactly late, but still? And to a stranger like me, no less?"

"Yes. Actually, Rei is Keep-sama, the opponent you're dueling in three days..."

By the time Rei got that far, Rei felt a vague sense of unease.

He didn't voice it, however, instead urging her to continue with his gaze.

"He says he wants to recruit Rei. And he's offering several platinum coins as a signing bonus."

"I knew it."

Hearing the conversation unfold as he'd vaguely anticipated, a sigh escaped Rei's lips along with those words.

Hearing Rei's words, Siest seemed confused. He looked at Rei with a puzzled expression.

"So, they realized they couldn't beat me in an actual duel, so they tried to buy me off? And they were kind enough to offer platinum coins, no less."

A platinum coin was undoubtedly a high-value currency for the average person.

But for adventurers of a certain caliber, it was by no means a rare commodity.

And above all, Misty Ring contained a considerable number of platinum coins.

The most recent source was likely the Gamelion hunt just the other day.

During that, Rei rode in... or more accurately, he seized platinum coins from adventurers who tried to steal the former guerrilla squad's carriage.

For Rei, being told to betray Muet for a few platinum coins was laughable.

"First off, if I win this duel, half of the Eriell family's fortune becomes the property of the Sysne baronial house. If that happens, the amount paid to me as the victor of the duel won't be just a few platinum coins, would it?"

"Well... but the opponent is the Count of Eriell's family, right? If we go through with this duel, it could cause all sorts of trouble for you too, Rei..."

Siest was about to say that when Rei smiled and spoke up.

"You think there would? Well, I hate to say it, but as long as I'm in Gilm, I don't see any inconvenience for me. Things would be different if I went to the capital, of course. And if it really came to that, I could simply vanish from the Kingdom of Mireana. After all, I'm a free-spirited adventurer."

To be precise, it probably wouldn't be that simple.

For starters, if it came to actually fleeing the Kingdom of Mireana, it would definitely become harder to meet with Elena.

It wasn't every night, but I did talk to Elena fairly often via the Paired Orbs. Still, it was only natural to want to see her in person whenever possible.

But Siest, unaware of this, could only stare in astonishment at Rei, who casually declared he'd abandon the kingdom.

"Well, that's all I have to say. Now, go back. I can guess who your master is from what you just said, but I won't pry."

Since he'd been told to serve as a retainer to Keep, it was easy to guess whose man Siest was.

But he knew pointing it out would only cause trouble, so Rei let it slide.

(Still, I never expected him to come after me first. I thought he'd attack the Baron Sysne household... Was I wrong?)

Watching the man before him, Rei felt a complex mix of relief at his miscalculation and disappointment.

To be precise, they had already launched an attack, and it was precisely because that had failed that they resorted to trying to buy Rei off. But Rei had no way of knowing that.

"You... you're serious!? The target is the Count of Eriell family!? They're nobles with considerable influence even among the royalist faction! And yet, you refuse a request from their next head!?"

Siest's eyes widened in disbelief at the words coming from Rei's mouth.

But what Rei returned to such Siest was a savage grin.

"Why the hell should I go out of my way to serve some guy like that? If you want me under your command, show me your own strength, not your parents' power or your family's money. Then I might consider it. ...Though, let's be honest, it'll take more than an ordinary vessel to contain a water like me."

Smiling as he spoke, Rei couldn't even begin to imagine himself serving a noble.

Fundamentally, he often acted alone in ways that could be seen as high-handed. He was only truly effective when operating as an individual. He strongly believed that if he had to coordinate his actions with other knights, he wouldn't be able to fully unleash his abilities.

"...Very well. If you insist, I shall take my leave here. But understand this: such an opportunity is not something that comes along often."

Giving the person before him a look that said he simply couldn't comprehend them, Siest rose from his chair, bowed, and left the room.

Watching him go, Rei removed the Dragon Robe, stored it in the Misty Ring, and flopped onto the bed.

"Well, trying to buy me off means you've figured out who I am. Feels like you're a bit slow on the uptake, though."

Thinking about his actions starting tomorrow, Rei took a book from the Misty Ring and began reading.

"...What? Say that again."

The room echoed with words that seemed to say he didn't understand what the other person was saying.

Hearing those words, Fultos wiped the sweat rising on his face with his sleeve.

The beads of sweat weren't caused by the magic item heating the room, but by sheer tension.

"Rei refused to serve Lord Keep, he said."

"...What? What the hell is going on? I'm the next head of the Eriell Count's family! And some adventurer punk has the nerve to refuse an offer to serve me? Who the hell does that man think he is?!"

His fist, raised in anger, slammed onto the desk.

Skin broke and blood flowed, but Keep, consumed by rage, seemed to feel no pain as he shouted.

"Don't you dare mock me! I'm offering you a position of service, and you refuse it! Damn it! To dare play such tricks with a noble! I will never forgive you!"

To Keep, it felt like the hand he'd extended to the adventurer had been bitten.

He... no, his position as a noble had been insulted by a mere adventurer, and he could never forgive it.

"Fultos! Prepare a duelist... someone who can kill that lowly wretch named Rei! He'll pay for insulting me with his life!"

He commanded Fultos with a furious glare, but Fultos's response was a simple shake of the head.

"Have you forgotten, Lord Keep? We originally sought to recruit Rei precisely because we couldn't find anyone capable of defeating him. That hasn't changed. Gilm is fundamentally a neutral faction's stronghold. We cannot fully leverage our influence as supporters of the king or as the House of Count Eriell."

"Guh... Then what do you want me to do?! Find strong adventurers in this Gilm... Wait, hold on! If we can't hire strong adventurers here, why bother clinging to this Gilm? I recall there's another town nearby... Aburo and Saburusta, or something..."

Kip smiled, thinking he'd come up with a last-ditch strategy, but Fultos responded once again by shaking his head.

When he thought he might have to fight Rei, Fultos had considered it too.

But he had to dismiss that idea immediately.

"Lord Keep. This is Gilm. It's a place where adventurers gather, some as strong as those in the capital city itself, within the Kingdom of Mireana. If you ask whether adventurers from other towns would duel someone like Rei, who's renowned as a powerhouse even within Gilm... Besides, we barely have any time left!"

"Don't be ridiculous! We still have to do something about this! If we don't, half our fortune will be taken away!"

Keep shouted, but from Fultos' perspective, he wanted to ask why he'd ever bet half his fortune in the first place.

(If only it hadn't been a legal agreement, but just a verbal promise, he could have wriggled out of it somehow...)

While he muttered bitterly to himself, Keep's angry shouts echoed through the room.

Ignoring it, Fultos pondered ways to break the deadlock when suddenly, as if struck by an idea, Keep froze.

Though a bad feeling stirred within him at his master's sudden change, Fultos spoke up.

"My lord, what is it?"

"...The reason I'm suffering like this is because there's a document with my blood seal under the Marquis of Larks, right?"

"Huh? Well, yes. It's true that document exists, so it's true we can't wriggle out of this situation."

"Then why not just get rid of that document? Like, have some bandits sneak in and steal it or something?"

"Impossible."

Fultos held back the urge to shout, "Don't be ridiculous!" and stated firmly.

"We're talking about the lord's residence, right? Security is obviously tight. Especially the knights of the Marquis of Larks' domain—they're renowned for being elite. There's no way we could sneak in and slip past their watchful eyes."

Fultos didn't voice it, but he also suspected Daskar might have already figured out their movements.

If it were Keep, such a rash move wouldn't be out of character.

"Then what do you want me to do?!"

"Well..."

"You complain like you know everything, yet you don't have a single alternative! But if attacking the lord's manor is too difficult, why not just destroy Gilm itself? Hire people to set fires throughout the town all at once. That would make a duel impossible!"

"Stop it! If we do something like that, the Count of Eriell's house will be ruined!"

"So if you want to complain, come up with an alternative!"

Even when yelled at, no alternative plan came from Fultos' mouth.

But even so... he absolutely could not allow them to sneak into the lord's mansion or set fire to Gilm.

Such actions would surely endanger not only the Keep but his own life as well.

To save his own life, there was only one thing Fultos could do.

"I understand. I'll find someone strong enough to defeat Rei. So please, refrain from any rash actions."

Only a few days remained until the duel.

In that time, he had no choice but to prepare someone who could stand against Rei.

Keep nodded in satisfaction at Fultos' words.

If he could find someone who could defeat Rei, there was no need for worry.

Of course, Keep couldn't grasp just how difficult that would be to achieve.

That was precisely why he opened his mouth, convinced Fultos's suggestion could be fully realized.

"You should have said so from the start. Well then, I'll leave it to you."

...And so time passed, and finally, the day of the duel arrived.
