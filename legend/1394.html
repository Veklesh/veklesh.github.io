The moment he spoke, the puppet moved, raising its right arm while shaking the tree roots that formed its body.

Rei, watching from close by, didn't understand what it meant.

Still, he could guess that since it had taken action like this, there must be something to it, so he stared intently at its movements.

Prepared to counterattack immediately if it launched an assault, Rei watched as the puppet extended its raised right arm toward her.

Had it attacked with force, Rei would have countered.

But there was absolutely no sense of aggressive intent in the arm extended toward Rei.

Rather, it gave off an aura as if it were seeking something...

The arm appeared to be made of tree roots. It seemed capable of extending itself by rearranging the roots composing its right arm, without moving its body.

As the arm extended toward him neared the point of touching his body... Rei hesitated for a moment, unsure what to do.

There was no hint of malice. That was a fact.

But at the same time, it was also true that this forest of Treant was a dangerous place that had claimed the lives of many people.

This place, deep within the heart of Treant Forest, held a puppet made of tree roots.

It was utterly inconceivable that it had no connection whatsoever to Treant Forest.

The Treant Forest openly displayed hostility toward people, yet he felt no hostility from the doll before him.

Faced with this contradiction, Rei hesitates, unsure what to do... and then, while he hesitates, the hand made of tree roots touches Rei's body.

「Rei！」

"Gluu!"

Vihera and Set had assumed Rei would never allow the doll to touch him. Yet Rei, without even the slightest hint of resistance, let the doll touch his body.

One person and one beast cried out in surprise at this, but Rei, touched by the doll, was in no state to care.

It wasn't as if he was under attack.

But from the point of contact, the doll's thoughts were being transmitted to her.

No, this probably shouldn't even be called thoughts. It was more accurate to describe it as something closer to instinct.

'Reproduce, spread, grow, endlessly, endlessly. Expand your very existence, and consume anything that stands in your way. Spread, spread, until you cover everything with yourself.'

It wasn't heard as clear words, but rather an image hammered into Rei's mind. If forced to put it into words, it would be something like this.

"Huh!?"

Feeling, seeing, hearing... whatever it was, Rei understood it as something akin to an image language. Instinctively, she leapt backward from her position.

Vihera and Set, who had been watching Rei, saw her suddenly leap backward the instant the tree root puppet seemed to touch her, and their tension heightened.

"Rei, are you okay!?"

"I'm fine, don't worry. Nothing actually happened to me."

Even as he shouted, a fleeting doubt crossed Rei's mind: Was that really true? Yet he understood, as if it were obvious, that nothing had actually been done to him.

Lowering the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear he'd held in both hands, he turned his gaze back to the tree root puppet.

There it was, frozen with its arms outstretched.

It wasn't performing any hostile actions.

"What was that just now..."

Even as he spoke, Rei approached the doll seated on the throne made of tree roots once more.

Then, as if waiting for this moment, the doll stretched out its hand once more.

Rei gently touched the outstretched hand.

'Reproduce, spread, grow, endlessly, endlessly. Until you cover and absorb everything with your own existence.'

The words were slightly different from before... an image language, yet Rei understood exactly what she had received.

"...I see."

She murmured, gently withdrawing her hand from the tree root doll and creating distance.

Watching Rei's actions, Vihera and Set approached cautiously, keeping an eye on their surroundings.

Unfortunately, the battle Vihera had hoped for—against opponents worthy of being called Treant's elite—didn't happen. But right now, Rei was the greater concern.

If this had been combat with the tree roots, it would have been business as usual, and Vihera wouldn't have given it a second thought.

But what had happened this time wasn't a battle.

In fact, it was merely a slight touch.

That fact alone made the abnormality stand out.

"Are you okay?"

Though he had just spoken those words himself, the same words came from Vihera's mouth.

Beside Vihera, Set also gazed at Rei with concern.

Facing the one person and one creature before him, Rei spoke.

"Didn't I tell you not to worry? I'm not hurt or anything. It's just... this tree root doll isn't exactly a clear enemy... No, actually, this tree root doll doesn't even feel like the main body either."

When the image language flooded into Rei's mind, some of the other party's information flowed in as well.

Most of it was incomprehensible gibberish to Rei, but he grasped one thing.

Namely, the root doll before him was likely equivalent to a terminal within this Treant Forest.

In fact, every monster in this Treant Forest was equally equivalent to a terminal, just like this tree root puppet.

(No wonder there are no magic stones. Essentially, this entire Trent Forest is one giant monster, and the Treants appearing here are all terminals... like limbs, if you will.)

Killing a goblin would yield the magic stone within its body.

But destroying the goblin's limbs... no, considering the scale of the Treant Forest, even destroying parts like goblin body hair or claws won't yield magic stones.

That's exactly what Rei and the other adventurers had been doing.

That's precisely why, despite defeating countless monsters including Treants, magic stones had never appeared before.

"Not the main body? What do you mean?"

Vihera asked Rei, who was staring intently at the tree root doll.

By exchanging words like this, it became clear that Rei hadn't suffered any particular attack from the tree root doll... nor had he been subjected to any mental interference.

That's why, unlike when he'd spoken words of concern for her earlier, he could now ask with a slightly calmer demeanor.

"Exactly as you said. The entity that rules this Treant Forest... no, wait. It might be more accurate to say it is the Treant Forest itself. That entity is separate. These tree roots, and even the Treant that appeared so many times, are all apparently like hair or nails to that Treant Forest."

"What the hell is that... I've never heard of such an existence."

"Yeah, me neither. ...But this Gilm is the frontier. Don't you think it's possible for such an exceptional existence to exist here?"

"Well..."

Considering this is the frontier, it makes sense that any kind of existence could be here.

Vihera seemed convinced by Rei's words.

Though she looked slightly dissatisfied, she eventually nodded and spoke.

"So, did you learn anything else from touching that tree root doll?"

"Well, it didn't show any clear hostility toward us."

"...Huh?"

It must have been a completely unexpected statement. Hearing Rei's words, a dumbfounded sound escaped Vihera's mouth.

Given what the Treant Forest had done up until now, it seemed impossible to think it harbored anything but malice toward humans... and toward Gilm.

"Malice... or rather, in a case like this, it's probably more accurate to call it acting on half-instinct. Anyway, the information we got from this tree root is that its primary goal is to expand the Treant Forest. Everything else seems pretty much irrelevant."

"...Doesn't care, huh. Well, I suppose that's accurate if you mean the instinct to preserve the species."

The fact that one could communicate with monsters wasn't particularly surprising to Vihera either, so he made no special mention of it.

After all, seeing Rei and Set, the idea of communicating with monsters didn't seem all that unusual.

When Rei explained what could be called an image language, Vihera did show a slightly intrigued expression.

"But why is this monster heading toward Gilm? If it just wants to expand the forest, wouldn't it make more sense to go somewhere without towns in its path?"

Vihera voiced the obvious question.

The reason Trent Forest was deemed dangerous in Gilm was partly because Slesha's party had been attacked there, but the biggest factor was that it was expanding towards Gilm.

...While the Treant forest drew attention for various reasons—being recognized as a promising source of building materials for Gilm's expansion, or the discovery of monsters lacking magic stones—the fact it was spreading toward Gilm was undoubtedly the primary cause.

In other words, had the forest expanded not toward Gilm... nor even toward Avelo, the closest settlement to Gilm, it wouldn't have caused such a huge commotion.

Of course, if it had been seen as a source of increased timber, it would undoubtedly have become an ideal workplace for the woodcutters.

"Hmm... I wonder. I haven't heard about that part yet. Is it okay not to ask?"

Since this exchange was more like an exchange of imagery than words, it felt more accurate to say he was sensing the conversation, accepting it, rather than hearing it.

Feeling slightly uncertain about that, Rei turned his gaze toward the tree root doll.

It seemed like it would answer if asked.

But at the same time, part of him wondered if communicating through that kind of incomprehensible image language was really wise.

He knew from using the image language twice before that it wasn't dangerous... yet somewhere inside, the thought lingered: Was it really safe?

He recognized the contradiction within himself, but he couldn't help feeling that way.

Regardless, just because it felt dangerous didn't change the fact that this was the embodiment of Treant Forest, an entity he had finally managed to make contact with.

It's only natural I'd want to find a way to communicate with it, to prevent further harm to the adventurers.

"Fine, I guess. I'll try again, so keep an eye on the surroundings. Well, considering what's happened so far, I don't think we need to be that cautious. Still, just in case."

"Is that okay? ...Alright. Be careful."

"Grrrr!"

Following Vihera's words, Set growled, "Leave it to me!"

He shot a sharp glance at the surrounding trees, signaling he'd attack immediately if anyone tried anything against Rei.

Seeing the two of them like that, Rei gave a small nod and approached the root doll once more.

This time, instead of passively receiving the other's will, he reached out his hand and sent his own image, imbued with strong intent.

Still, Rei wasn't entirely sure if this was the right way to respond to the image language the other side was using.

Still, he had a hunch this was probably the right answer.

The image he sent forth was a question: Why, Gilm... why are you expanding the forest toward where people are?

'Magic, Presence, sustenance'

Unlike the first time, the response was simply a few words strung together.

But even from those words, he could understand that the one manipulating this tree-root puppet was scheming something deeply unsavory.

Magic and existence. That was fine. But the last word, sustenance, was something Rei could never tolerate.

(Are they treating Gilm as food!?)

For Rei, it was both surprising and, in a way, understandable.

Gilm had many skilled adventurers.

Among them, there were surely those possessing vast amounts of magic power.

After all, even though Rei currently hides his magic power with the New Moon Ring, he possesses immense magic power.

People aren't bait.

Desperately trying to make them understand this, he sent out mental images...

'No, no, no'

Only absolute denial came back.

No, perhaps they never intended to listen to Rei's opinion from the start.

Such was the absolute rejection that made him think that.

And so, we exchanged messages repeatedly through the language of images...

'Interference, bait, sustenance'

With an unprecedented strength of will, the exchange of imagery ends.

Then, before Rei knew it, the wooden root doll before him had risen to its feet.

"...Ah, sorry. Seems like I failed to persuade it."

Announcing this to Vihera and Set, Rei fixed his gaze on the tree root puppet before him while readying his Death Scythe and Twilight Spear.
