"Ugh... mmm... mmm..."

Laying on the bed, Rei rolled over, and the movement woke her up.

She scanned the room, sensing something different from usual, but found herself in the familiar twilight-hued room of Dusk Wheat inn, nearly devoid of personal belongings.

Her half-asleep mind searched for what was different, but Rei couldn't quite grasp it.

She looked around, then froze for a few minutes... until her mind finally cleared of sleepiness, and she realized what was different about the room.

Yes, normally when she woke up in the morning, the room would be filled with sunlight streaming through the window. Today, there was none of that.

It was clear from looking out the window that it wasn't raining either.

And the conclusion Rei finally reached was...

"I overslept, huh?"

He muttered that alone, his mind still not fully awake.

Judging by the sun's position seen through the window, it must be close to noon, he vaguely thought.

And in that hazy state, he recalled last night.

After the battle in the slums ended, things got hectic once Ara and the guards rushed over.

They pulled the thorn spear out of Paul's right arm, freeing him from his immobilized state. Then, they led the right arm-less Dias and the bound Adria, who had been restrained by Byune, out of the slums.

Meanwhile, the man who had attacked Byune as one of Dias's subordinates had vanished without a trace.

Rei and the others concluded he had either regained consciousness and fled, or been taken away by his comrades. However, since they needed to hurry to the police station, they decided it wasn't worth searching for him too diligently and left it at that.

Fortunately, word of the battle Rei and the others had fought had spread, albeit from a distance, by those who had witnessed it. Consequently, they faced almost none of the attacks they had endured when they first arrived in the slums.

...Yes. Almost none, but not none at all. That was the nature of the slums.

Fighting off attackers as they advanced, they finally exited the slums. Everyone, including the guards, breathed a sigh of relief, and Rei felt the same sense of relief.

Their next destination was the guard post.

While explaining what had happened to the guards, including Ranga, someone from the knight order arrived, forcing them to recount the story from the beginning once more.

After giving further detailed accounts, Rei and the others were finally released at midnight.

Naturally, there were no food stalls left, and even the taverns had closed by that hour.

Even so, the Dusk Wheat inn, being a high-class inn, welcomed Rei and the others warmly.

While at the guard post, Rei had asked the guards to bring Set from the magic item shop to Dusk Wheat inn. Rei went to meet him there and ended up soothing Set, who was sulking because he'd been left behind.

After that, when they returned to the inn, Elena, Ara, Vihera, and Byune each complained of hunger. However, the kitchen fires had already been extinguished. So, they made do with a light meal using the food stored in Rei's Misty Ring... By the time everyone returned to their rooms, the sky was already brightening.

Having gone to bed so late, oversleeping was only natural.

"...I'm hungry."

Recalling last night's events and the vegetable and ham soup they'd eaten as a late-night snack must have triggered it. Rei's stomach growled loudly, making its presence known.

He pulled a watch from the Misty Ring to check the time. As expected, it was already past morning; calling it just before noon would be more accurate.

"Guess I'll head to the cafeteria."

Perhaps still feeling off from waking at an unusual hour, Rei slowly got ready and headed downstairs to the cafeteria.

"Oh my, you're taking your time. ...Are you really okay?"

Vihera, who was eating a sandwich in the cafeteria, called out to Rei.

Though Vihera's tone initially sounded teasing, she must have quickly realized Rei wasn't in her usual state. She asked, looking slightly flustered.

At the same table as Vihera, Elena sat, silently watching Rei with a look of concern.

Of course, Ara was beside Elena, and though not as visibly as Elena or Vihera, she too wore a worried expression toward Rei.

To these four... well, actually three, since Byune was eating her sandwich expressionless as usual... Rei waved his hand dismissively, signaling it was nothing.

"I just woke up at a different time than usual, so don't worry about it. Things should be back to normal in a little while,"

He said this and sat down at the table where Elena and Vihera were seated.

The dining hall of the Dusk Wheat inn was certainly not cramped, befitting its status as a high-class inn. In fact, it would be better described as spacious.

But now, most of the gazes from the guests eating their slightly early lunch in that spacious dining room were fixed on Rei's table.

It was only natural. Elena and Vihera were stunning beauties, each in their own way.

Ara, while not quite matching her mistress Elena, possessed sufficiently refined features.

She usually stays by Elena's side, so Ara doesn't stand out much, but she was certainly beautiful enough that if she wanted to, she wouldn't have any trouble attracting men.

Even Byune, eating her sandwich expressionlessly, was a young beauty with plenty of potential.

So naturally, a man sitting down at their table drew attention.

And since that man was Rei, famous even in Gilm, it was impossible for him not to draw attention.

Already accustomed to such attention, Rei showed no concern for the surrounding stares as he casually ordered food from Lana, the innkeeper.

...Though "casually" was relative; since the hungry Rei needed to fill his stomach, he ordered enough for several people.

"So, you're heading to the lord's manor later today, right?"

"Yeah. To report on this matter."

Judging from the amount of food Rei ordered from Lana, Vihera apparently decided there was no immediate cause for concern. She asked Rei.

"Hmm, then I suppose I should go along too."

"What about me, Elena-sama?"

"As for Ara... she arrived after the battle ended, so it shouldn't be an issue, right?"

"But we can't just send Elena alone..."

Though she asked about what to do with Ara, her true desire was likely to go with Elena.

Whether she sensed this or simply decided it was better to act together, Elena paused briefly before nodding.

"If you think it's better for Ara to accompany me, I won't refuse. But are you sure? It's only been a day since yesterday. Don't you think Ara might want to rest a little?"

"No, it's fine. I feel more at ease being with you, Elena-sama."

"Well, well, you're quite devoted to her, aren't you?"

Ara shot Vihera a complicated look as she said this.

She already knew from Elena what kind of person Vihera was.

Honestly, Ara was unsure how to behave toward the person before her.

Should she show respect as the former princess of the Bestir Empire? Or should she adopt a hostile stance toward the princess of a long-standing enemy nation? The only thing she knew for certain was...

(This person might have a bad influence on Elena...)

The basis for this thought was the sheer clothing Vihera wore, the kind typically worn by prostitutes or dancers.

That sheer fabric, so thin it might as well be called underwear rather than clothing, naturally drew the gazes of everyone in the tavern... especially the men's. To Ara, it seemed to be the primary reason for all the attention.

In truth, Elena's beauty was also a major reason for the attention, but when faced with Vihera, Ara completely forgot about that.

Habit, it seemed, was that powerful.

In any case, to Ara, a noble and a knight, Vihera's attire could only be described as scandalous.

And according to what Ara had heard from Elena, Vihera also harbored romantic feelings for Rei.

Having been raised as a noble, Ara didn't feel particularly uncomfortable with the idea of a man taking multiple wives.

She did have some reservations, but she also judged it wasn't her place to interfere.

But... if being around Vihera caused Elena to start wearing similar clothes, influenced by him... Honestly, just thinking about it made her want to scream and grab the power axe in her room to go on a rampage.

(Ugh, really. What am I supposed to do!?)

Faced with a situation she'd never encountered before, Ara inwardly clutched her head in frustration.

Perhaps her inner turmoil showed on her face, because Elena swallowed a piece of freshly baked bread kneaded with nuts and opened her mouth.

"...Not getting enough sleep?"

"Ah, no. I was just thinking about something. It's not like anything's wrong or anything..."

Elena was puzzled by Ara's flustered explanation, but as she took a sip of tea, she slightly... very slightly, but still noticeably, furrowed her neatly shaped brows.

The first to notice Elena's reaction, naturally, was Ara, who had spent her entire childhood with her.

"What's wrong? Is something wrong with that tea...?"

Could it be poisoned? Ara wondered for a moment.

Elena, known by the nickname Princess General and a symbol of the aristocratic faction. There were undoubtedly those both within and outside the country who found Elena a nuisance, and even within the aristocratic faction, there were those who wished to eliminate her.

She had faced assassination attempts many times before, but Elena had always thwarted them all with her innate knowledge and skill.

And now, having even inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone, Elena was far beyond being harmed by ordinary means.

Still, as the commander of the knights serving Elena... and above all, as her childhood friend, he could never overlook anyone who threatened her.

That said, he wasn't feeling quite that serious right now.

If the tea truly had poison in it, Elena would have reacted more clearly.

And indeed, Elena responded to Ara's words as if nothing was wrong... yet with just a hint of disappointment.

"No, I just thought this tea was inferior compared to what Ara brews. Once you get used to Ara's delicious tea, you tend to be dissatisfied with most other teas."

"Well..."

Hearing Elena's words, feelings of delight and guilt wrestled within me.

"Oh? Is the tea that girl brews really that delicious? Then I'd love to try it myself."

"Hmm!"

It was precisely because it involved food and drink. Byune immediately chimed in with agreement at Vihera's words.

Elena smiled happily at the two of them.

Seeing Ara praised made Elena happy too.

"Well, yes?"

"If you don't mind my tea..."

"Rei hasn't had Ara's tea in a while, has she?"

"That's true. The tea she made me before was delicious."

Rei's words carried a deep, lingering sentiment.

She did use magic items, but only for things like turning water into hot water—nothing more.

Beyond that, the flavor of the tea was purely elevated by Ara's skill.

(If Ara brewed tea using water created by the Waterfall Dagger, that would surely be the finest tea imaginable.)

Rei's thoughts drifted to the magic item inside the Misty Ring.

While it was originally a magic item for water-based attacks, Rei's innate aptitude for fire meant he couldn't use it as a weapon.

The only thing he could do was consume magic power to create water... Yet, the water created using Rei's immense magic power tasted like something worthy of being called heavenly water, even though it was just plain water.

Ara briefly glanced toward the kitchen, but Elena stopped her.

"Ara's tea would be a shame to miss, but isn't it about time? We're meeting Lord Daskar, so we can't afford to be late."

"...My apologies, Elena-sama. You're right, we don't have much time to spare."

Elena nodded at her words, and Vihera also looked at Ara with interest.

(Seems reasonably strong... I'm somewhat interested.)
