"Huh," muttered Rei.

The moment those words were heard, many of those around him felt a chill run down their spines and instinctively took a few steps back.

Rei hadn't actually done anything.

He truly had only muttered it... yet for those present, it struck like a formidable threat.

Especially those with more than a passing familiarity with combat would have recognized the killing intent emanating from Rei.

"Just to be sure, let me ask again. Who is that man over there?"

"Of course... he's an old friend of mine."

Precious replied to Rei, trying not to change her expression, but cold sweat was beading thickly on her back.

The fact she didn't let it show on her face was probably why she was a top-tier merchant.

...Though he couldn't quite suppress the faint tremble of his catfish-like beard.

(In that sense, Tris was better—he completely hid it in his attitude and expression, regardless of what he felt inside, even when he sensed my killing intent.)

Thinking this, Rei suppressed the killing intent emanating from his body.

Even while Rei was radiating that killing intent, Marina, Vihera, and Set showed no particular reaction.

Only Byune remained expressionless, gripping Vihera's thin robe.

The silent interval continued.

Rei silently looked at Precious, then at her guards, then at the man who was Slain's body double.

Silence alone filled the air around them... and then, Rei spoke.

"I see. Friends are important, aren't they?"

Rei murmured this, finally ending the silence, and simultaneously, the killing intent emanating from him dissipated like mist.

The silence had lasted less than thirty seconds, at most.

But for those present... especially those other than Marina, Vihera, Byune, and Set, those thirty seconds felt much longer... to some, it felt like minutes, ten minutes, even half an hour.

Leaving those who breathed sighs of relief at Rei's words, Rei spoke again.

"Yeah. Friends are important. That's precisely why... if a friend is harmed, I need to retaliate accordingly. Don't you agree?"

"Yes, perhaps you're right. Mr. Azimov, was it? I hope a tragedy like his never happens again."

"...Huh. Didn't expect Azimov's name to come up here. Seems you know quite a bit about the case I'm currently involved in."

"Didn't we discuss it briefly at the branch office before?"

"Ah, that's right. ...Still, don't you think whoever attacked Azimov and then tried to steal my magic item was being pretty stupid?"

Rei's words were almost provocative.

While there was a hint of hoping he might slip up, it was more purely a provocation, hoping to draw out even the slightest hint of something.

To such words from Rei, Precious's guards and Slain's subordinates—who would normally show some reaction—remained silent.

But given the killing intent Rei had just unleashed, even they couldn't utter a word now.

"Well, yes. It would certainly be considered a foolish act. But do you remember what we discussed when we met at the branch office before?"

"Huh? Something about how there's more than one way to look at things?"

"It's slightly different... but yes. Broadly speaking, you're not wrong. Why not keep that in mind as you think about it?"

Deep down, Precious likely had various thoughts and feelings, but fortunately—or perhaps unfortunately—she managed to keep them hidden while speaking.

"I see. But changing your perspective doesn't change the outcome. The one who plotted this deserves proper retribution, though."

Even after hearing about changing perspectives, that was the first thing Rei insisted on.

He couldn't just leave those who opposed him unpunished.

As he said this, Rei's gaze fell not on Precious or her guards... but on the man who had served as Slain's body double and the others guarding this building.

His gaze contained no trace of killing intent whatsoever, and precisely because of that, those who had just been drenched in that earlier killing intent felt uneasy under Rei's calm stare.

He must have decided it was better to take control of the situation before Slein's subordinates made any foolish moves.

After all, if any of Slain's men blurted out something strange, it could prove fatal for him as well.

"Is that so? It's a pity for anyone who makes an enemy of you, Rei."

"Do you think so?"

"Yes. Making an enemy of an adventurer with a nickname is practically tantamount to suicide, I'd say."

Seeing Precious state this matter-of-factly, Rei couldn't help but tilt his head.

If she truly considered him an enemy, wouldn't it be strange for her to be this calm?

But at the same time, there was no doubt that Precious was the most suspicious person in this whole affair.

Of course, he considered the possibility that Tris was skillfully using him to pit him against Precious.

Even so, the fact remained that Precious was the most suspicious figure in the current situation.

"I suppose so. I show no mercy to those who oppose me. ...If I find the one who plotted this, they'll certainly pay the price. ...By the way, I hear the noble who opposed me before had both shoulders and arms disappear? Divine retribution is a scary thing."

What Rei meant by "divine retribution" as he pulled the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and flaunted it was clear.

And who would deliver that divine punishment.

And considering Set's flight speed, escaping that divine punishment would be virtually impossible.

"Divine punishment, huh? ...That sounds terrifying."

"Right? Unfortunately... it's impossible for divine retribution not to fall upon those who have already become my enemies. Regrettably."

As he muttered, Rei's gaze fell not on Precious, but on Slain's subordinates.

Several of those met by Rei's gaze couldn't withstand the intensity of his stare and collapsed unconscious to the ground.

...He wasn't exactly radiating killing intent, but that very lack of intent seemed to be the source of their terror.

"Huh? What's wrong? Looks like we've got some weaklings here. How do you expect to survive in this slum?"

"I-It's fine. I just pushed them a bit too hard at work."

The one who answered Rei's words was not Precious... but the man standing beside her, Slain's body double.

"And you? An old friend of Precious'?"

As if only now noticing the man's presence, Rei spoke. The man forced his instinct to flee from the person before him back under control with sheer willpower before opening his mouth.

"Yes. Precious helped me out in various ways before, and we've been acquainted ever since."

"Huh."

Still sticking to that story, huh?

That's what Rei's words seemed to say, but for the man now, he had no choice but to push through with the story that he was Slein and that Precious was an old friend.

Exposed to the atmosphere Rei radiated, he still stated it so firmly. Only someone entrusted with being Slain's body double could do that.

"Flying Slash!"

Rei muttered, swinging the Death Scythe.

Normally, it was a powerful skill, an attack capable of easily slicing through a person without issue.

But Rei wouldn't do such a thing here. The slash released was significantly weakened, flying straight ahead to carve a sharp gash into the ground right next to Precious.

'Huh!?'

Everyone present wore expressions of utter shock at Rei's sudden action.

Marina, Vihera, Byune, and Set—the three humans and one beast—didn't seem particularly bothered, thinking it was just Rei being Rei...

"Whoops, sorry about that. I spotted a rat over there. It looked like it was targeting Precious, so I attacked before I could say anything... Was that too sudden?"

"...Yes. It was sudden, so I was a bit startled. But if there really was a mouse targeting me, I thank you for driving it off."

Precious said this, though of course she didn't actually believe Rei's action was because a mouse was really targeting her.

His action was clearly a show of force, a blunt expression of how Rei felt about them.

But there was no way she could voice that thought in the current situation.

The aura emanating from Rei's small frame alone dominated the entire area.

That was precisely why, even if what Rei was saying was clearly unreasonable... even if it was a threat against her, or a warning, she couldn't voice it.

"When rats scurry around your feet, it's bound to be annoying for whoever's nearby. Don't you agree?"

"I suppose so. Even from a merchant's perspective, rats that ruin food and goods are troublesome creatures."

"I see. Glad you understand. But while that might be true for merchants... what happens when a cat spots a mouse?"

As he spoke, Rei stroked Set, who was nearby, with his left hand—the one not gripping the Death Scythe.

Set was a griffin—a monster with the lower body of a lion and the upper body of an eagle.

And since lions are, of course, felines, it wasn't entirely wrong for Rei to call Set a cat.

Set seemed pleased to be stroked by Rei, purring contentedly like a true cat.

But... even though Set had a side that was practically a pet to Rei and the other members of Crimson Wings, it was different for Precious and Slain's subordinates.

Considering what Rei just said, it was essentially the same as declaring that mice belong to cats... meaning, anyone hostile to them would be attacked by Set.

Of course, if a familiar caused trouble in town, the tamer would be held responsible.

"Grrr?"

What's wrong? Set purred, rubbing against her. Rei shook her head, as if to say it was nothing, and continued stroking him.

"Now then, I think you understand... When a cat faces a mouse, it doesn't hold back. It hunts with full force, you know?"

Smiling as he spoke to Precious, Rei recalled a phrase he'd learned back in Japan.

The saying that even when a lion catches a rabbit, it gives it its all.

Though apparently, there's also a theory that lions don't actually use their full strength when hunting...

Regardless, Precious hid her inner thoughts at Rei's words, yet still smiled as she spoke.

"Is that so? I have various countermeasures against mice myself, so I'll be fine."

Those words served as a warning to Rei as much as they were to herself.

It was clear that the "mice" she mentioned referred not only to Tris, who had been investigating her, but also to Rei himself.

Realizing this, Rei showed a hint of surprise before smiling and replying.

"I see. The rats are probably trying their best too, so I suppose we all need to do our best."

"Yes, I suppose so. ...Let's both do our best. Well then, I have some business to attend to, so I'd like to take my leave. Is that alright?"

"Ah, I just happened to be here too, and I don't really have that much business either."

They exchanged those words.

Both knew that wasn't entirely true, but outwardly, they revealed nothing.

"Well then, I'll take my leave here. Come on, let's head inside."

Slayne's body double said this, then entered the building with the others.

After seeing them off, Rei spoke once Precious was out of sight.

"Well, I guess we should head back too. ...Our business is done, after all."

"Heh heh, I suppose so. ...Still, they were quite bold over there, weren't they?"

Marina responded with an amused smile at Rei's words.

Marina had met Precious at the Spier Trading Company branch before, but back then, they'd barely had time for conversation.

Considering that, having this time to talk with Rei was undoubtedly important for getting to know Precious as a person.

"...Yeah. From talking with her like this, it seems they're not done with this matter yet. We need to find some evidence before Tris does."

He muttered this to himself while pondering what to do next.
