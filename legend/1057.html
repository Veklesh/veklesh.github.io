Rei and Set, who had slaughtered the kobolds that had frozen in place under the King of Set's intimidation, scanned their surroundings to confirm the dark elf children were gone.

Then, just as he had done with the goblin earlier, Rei stored one of the kobolds into his Misty Ring. He let out a sigh that was a mix of relief and disappointment.

It could be considered fortunate that the missing dark elf children weren't here, sparing them from witnessing the cobolds' slaughter.

But at the same time, the fact that they still hadn't found the Dark Elf children was enough to leave Rei feeling disappointed.

"Next stop. I'd like to find some kind of clue soon."

"Grr..."

Set growled in agreement at Rei's words.

Honestly, Rei feared that before they found the dark elf children, they'd end up defeating nearly every monster in the forest...

Not that he disliked the idea, but given the situation, securing the children should be the top priority.

"Anyway, we should keep searching the area as much as possible while taking out monsters along the way... right?"

With those words, the Death Scythe swung.

Simultaneously, a sensation like cutting through something soft lingered in Rei's hand through the Death Scythe.

"What was that?"

Rei tried to look at whatever he'd cut, but before he could, he noticed something else being hurled toward them and swung the Death Scythe again.

That soft resistance returned.

It felt exactly like cutting through cloth or something similar.

(What the hell? What am I cutting?)

He didn't believe such a soft thing could harm him, but the fact remained that it was attacking him with hostility.

He couldn't just ignore the attack, even if he thought it was probably harmless.

But since I didn't know what the enemy's attack meant, just passively defending indefinitely wasn't exactly an interesting prospect either.

(Damn it. I really wanted to avoid using it at night because it's too conspicuous.)

Muttering resentfully to himself, Rei activated Death Scythe's skill.

"Magic Shield!"

With that word, a shield of light materialized, capable of blocking any attack—once.

...Its effectiveness was extremely high, making it a skill that could be called Rei's trump card in defense...

"Still too conspicuous."

Just as Rei said, the Magic Shield was a shield of light, and naturally, in the night... especially in a forest where even the moonlight was obscured by clouds, it stood out abnormally.

In other words, it made for an excellent target for the enemy...

As the enemy's attack was unleashed once more, Rei did nothing but wait, motionless.

The projectile, arriving without even the slightest whistling sound, collided with the Magic Shield and stopped moving.

Simultaneously, the Magic Shield vanished in a burst of light.

But... Rei's eyes did not miss what had collided with that magic shield.

"Feathers, huh?"

Yes, it was feathers.

The true nature of what had been silently attacking Rei and Set all this time was a feather.

The absence of even the sound of air being sliced like an arrow was likely due to the soft parts of the feathers muffling the wind noise.

"Hoo! Hoo!"

Realizing its attacks had been blocked, the monster that had been sending feathers echoed its cry.

"An owl-type monster. ...Well, it probably isn't just a regular owl, though."

Though Rei had excellent night vision, once it blended into the thick foliage, spotting it became nearly impossible.

Even so, what he could faintly make out definitely resembled an owl.

Of course, since it attacked by flinging feathers like this, it was undoubtedly some sort of monster, not just an ordinary owl, as Rei had said.

In fact, Rei had seen the third eye on the owl's forehead, and horn-like protrusions growing from its body.

There was no way it could be an ordinary owl.

The owl's size wasn't as large as an Aerowing either; it was only slightly bigger than a regular owl.

That made it all the more adept at disappearing from Rei and Set's sight by hiding among the branches in this densely wooded forest.

Rei and Set had good night vision, but even so, they couldn't track it with their eyes when it hid among the branches.

Furthermore, unlike Aero Wings, this owl monster possessed the unique trait of making no sound when flapping its wings.

To be precise, it wasn't completely silent, but the sound was astonishingly faint. Rei's ears couldn't detect the flapping of an owl's wings.

Set could barely make out the sound, but it was only faint, not enough to clearly understand where it was.

"Damn, troublesome..."

Tongue clicking, Rei tried to recall the name of the owl-type monster he'd glimpsed for just an instant.

It should be in the Monster Compendium... The thought brought it back to him immediately.

Rank C monster, Del Troy.

As you can see, it's an owl-type monster. While classified as a Rank C monster, it's only strong enough for a single person to defeat.

Pure combat power alone would place it at Rank D level, but its characteristics—only active at night, moving silently, and possessing stealth abilities that allow it to blend into the surrounding darkness—along with its extremely high stealthiness, such as launching feather attacks (which Rei had blocked numerous times), earned it its Rank C classification.

The tail feather is the designated proof of defeat.

Its beak, claws, third eye on its forehead, and several types of internal organs are traded as materials.

"...Del-Troy, huh? For a three-eyed owl, it sure has a pretty cool name, doesn't it!"

The Death Scythe swung the instant something moved again, slicing through the feather Del Troy had launched.

(If it keeps firing feathers like this, won't it run out eventually? Maybe I should just drag this out into a war of attrition... but that's not really an option.)

They had deliberately entered the dangerous night forest to search for the Dark Elf children. They needed to defeat Del Troy, who was right before their eyes, as quickly as possible and return to the task of finding the children.

In other words, deciding he needed to defeat Del Troy as quickly as possible, Rei swung his Death Scythe as if to keep him at bay.

"Flying Slash!"

The slash he unleashed flew straight toward where Delthroy was believed to be.

However, though it sliced through several thick branches as it flew, Del Troy was no longer in the path of the Flying Slash.

"Ouch!"

Rei, who had been looking around, thinking the Flying Slash had missed, suddenly felt a faint pain behind his right shoulder.

To be precise, it was more of a slight tingle than actual pain.

His intense focus on locating Del Troy had made him sensitive enough to perceive even a slight touch as pain.

Holding the Death Scythe in his right hand, he brought his left hand around to the back of his right shoulder. There, he found one of Del Troy's feathers.

Del Troy had likely moved behind him before Rei launched the Flying Slash earlier, then seized the opening after the attack to release the feather.

For Del Troy, it might have been intended as a one-hit kill, but unfortunately, his calculations hadn't factored in the element of the Dragon Robe Rei was wearing.

As a result, it failed to pierce the Dragon Robe, managing only to deliver a slight shock to Rei.

"You moved behind me? ...You're playing a nasty game."

Rei spun around instinctively, but naturally, Del Troy was already gone.

He had vanished into the darkness, blended into the tree branches, and disappeared from the spot.

"Grrrrr."

Facing an attacker who struck without showing themselves, Set growled in his throat, his irritation plain.

Of course, if he didn't care about the means, there were plenty of ways to defeat it.

Set's fire breath or Rei's flame magic would surely finish it without even giving it a chance to dodge.

But... launching such an attack in this forest would undoubtedly mean a full-scale confrontation with the Dark Elves.

As someone who didn't want that situation, Rei naturally couldn't resort to such methods.

"Set, if your ears are useless, use your nose!"

"Guruu, grrrrrrr!"

At Rei's command, Set growled in acknowledgment and activated his heightened sense of smell skill.

With his heightened sense of smell, he sniffed the surrounding scents... then immediately fixed his gaze on one spot and opened his beak wide.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

What erupted from his mouth wasn't flames... but countless bubbles, large and small.

One of Set's skills: Bubble Breath.

It had no direct offensive power, but when the released bubbles burst, they transformed into a highly adhesive liquid that splattered around.

Many of those bubbles hit the branches within range, bursting and transforming into the sticky liquid that adhered to the surrounding branches.

However... the countless scattered bubbles varied in size.

Some slipped through gaps between branches, others passed through spaces already filled with liquid from earlier bursts... eventually, several bubbles reached beneath Del Troy.

"Whoa! Whoa!?"

He probably never imagined that someone like him, who made the night forest his territory, could find his location so easily.

After all, Rei's flying slash had been aimed at the spot where Del Troy had vanished.

Was it complacency? Del Troy showed no sign of noticing Set's Bubble Breath until nearby branches became sticky, and he was ensnared by the bubbles.

His misfortune deepened when he tried to take flight the instant the bubbles touched him while perched on the branch.

The bubbles hitting its feet and the branch instantly transformed into a sticky liquid, bonding the branch and DelTroy's feet together.

Convinced he could fly, Del Troy silently flapped his wings, trying to take off from the branch... only to realize the next instant he couldn't fly, letting out a cry of panic.

"Alright!... Wait, in this situation, I can't just rush over either."

Set's Bubble Breath was indeed the means to break this stalemate.

But at the same time, the areas touched by the bubble breath were coated in sticky liquid, making it impossible for Rei to advance carelessly.

Scanning the area and confirming no other enemies were present, Rei made a quick decision.

"Set, keep watch here for a while."

"Guruu!"

Set reacted swiftly to Rei's words, growling in his throat as if to say, "Leave it to me!"

Laying a trusting gaze upon Set, Rei then left the spot.

His destination was not the direction Del Troy was headed... but to the right.

His goal wasn't to leave this spot entirely, but to take a detour towards Del Troy.

The Bubble Breath's line of fire was essentially a straight line in the direction Set was facing. Therefore, the idea was that by passing through areas without Bubble Breath, he could avoid contact with the sticky liquid.

(There was a scream, so he's probably immobilized, his wings tangled in Bubble Breath. If that's the case, taking a slight detour should ensure he doesn't escape.)

Thinking this, Rei ran through the night forest, Death Scythe in hand.

Branches whipped at him, but wearing the Dragon Robe with its hood pulled low, they were hardly a hindrance.

He ducked to avoid longer branches that threatened his face, pushing deeper into the woods. Soon, his target, Del Troy, came into view ahead... but his momentum halted.

"...I should have anticipated this happening."

Rei murmurs with a sigh. Where his gaze falls, Del Troy hangs from a tree branch.

Rei briefly wondered if this might be intentional for an owl-type monster, but seeing Del Troy struggling desperately to free himself, that thought vanished instantly.

Despite its feet being stuck to the branch by Bubble Breath, it hadn't noticed and tried to fly off, resulting in it being forced into this hanging position.

"How should I put it... pitiful."

"Hoo! Hoo!"

Del Troy must have heard Rei's mutter. He let out a threatening hoot to intimidate him, but... in his current state, there was nothing he could do.

Even so, it tried feigning flight by flapping its wings. But for Rei, blocking feathers launched from visible range posed little difficulty... and with a single strike of the Death Scythe, its neck was cleanly severed.
