The trees growing in Treant Forest are generally spaced apart to some degree.

Indeed, the spacing was generous enough for a three-meter-tall Set to walk around freely.

However... the area where Rei and the others were currently advancing was clearly a place worthy of being called a path.

Of course, it wasn't paved with stones or compacted into a path.

Nevertheless, the area where they walked was free of grass, and despite being deep in the forest, the ground—or more precisely, the spot where Rei and the others walked—showed no exposed tree roots.

It had been carefully maintained so as not to obstruct Rei and the others' passage.

Anyone who saw this and claimed it wasn't a path would have to be described as a real curmudgeon.

It was the kind of path that made you think that way.

Rei and the others were currently advancing along it.

Given how blatant it was, the possibility of some kind of trap had already vanished from Rei's mind.

Of course, they weren't completely careless; they remained ready to react instantly if anything happened.

"When it's this blatant, it almost feels silly to doubt it,"

Vihera, walking at the rear, muttered with a hint of exasperation.

Rei agreed with her assessment, and as he glanced at the trees lining the path, he replied.

"Usually, when it's like this, it turns out that tree was actually a Treant or something."

Back in Japan, in manga, novels, anime, and games, those statues standing in meaningful spots were actually golems, right... Thinking that, Rei scanned the trees growing around them.

"Hmm... Well, wanna check it out?"

Hearing Rei's words, Vihera approached a nearby tree.

Vihera had been keeping proper formation at first, but since nothing had happened since entering this path, she must have been bored.

She touched the tree trunk and even tried scraping it a little with the claws extending from her gauntlets... but even doing that didn't cause anything noteworthy to happen.

"Oh, what a shame."

Without seeming as disappointed as her words suggested, Vihera returned to Rei and the others.

"Listen... this is enemy territory, so be careful, okay?"

Trusting Vihera's judgment, Rei offered a cautious warning.

He was prepared to rush to her aid if anything happened, but even so, he wanted her to avoid reckless actions in a situation like this, where they had no idea what might occur.

(Well, calling this enemy territory is a bit of a stretch—it's more like we're inside the enemy's belly.)

Given the high likelihood that something from Treant Forest lay ahead, one could literally consider this place the enemy's guts.

Of course, both Vihera and Set surely understood that, so he didn't bother saying it out loud.

"Grr."

Set growled, urging them to hurry. Rei and Vihera nodded to each other, agreeing that moving forward would clarify things better than standing here talking. They began walking again.

And so they walked along the path created by whatever lay ahead, for about ten minutes.

After walking for that long...

"The exit is finally coming into view."

Rei nodded back at Vihera's voice, tinged with discontent, coming from behind.

Of course, riding Set would have gotten them here in mere minutes.

But the scenery, which seemed to blatantly scream "trap," made such a move far too dangerous to attempt.

It wasn't just Rei who sensed it; Vihera must have felt it too.

...Considering how vigilant they'd been about their surroundings, only to find nothing particularly trap-like all the way to the exit, it was no wonder Vihera felt dissatisfied.

(Besides, while we're walking like this, the battle must still be raging in front of Treant Forest. ...I hope the casualties over there aren't too heavy.)

What crossed Rei's mind weren't the adventurers he'd met on this quest... but the researchers.

Alchemists, magic researchers, scholars... it was a gathering of various researchers, but without exception, they were all driven by intense intellectual curiosity.

After all, only such people would have come on this investigation in the first place, and that was only natural.

But prioritizing their intellectual curiosity over the battle happening right before their eyes—while perhaps correct for researchers—was nothing short of troublesome for the adventurers tasked with guarding them.

Even while under their protection, the researchers were discussing their respective hypotheses about Treant Forest.

If this were somewhere far from Treant Forest, it wouldn't be such a burden for the adventurers... but in reality, they were doing this right next to where combat was happening.

It's no wonder the adventurers felt like telling them to cut it out.

And that very battle continues even now, as Rei and the others walk this path.

(We need to resolve this as quickly as possible.)

Watching the path's exit, Rei thought this... and eventually, Rei and the others finally reached the deepest part of the path.

"This..."

It was naturally Rei, the first to see the scene spread before them, who murmured at the sight.

A remarkably vast space.

It was a space so vast that it would be impossible to miss if viewed from above.

Grass grew on the ground, illuminated by moonlight. Had it been daytime, the sight would have been worthy of being called a green carpet.

Thick trees stood in a circle, surrounding the vast space like pillars.

Furthermore, at the very center of the space, intertwined tree roots formed something resembling a throne... And upon that throne of roots sat a figure shaped like a person, also made of roots... No, an object.

"Hey, what do you think that is?"

Vihera, who had been looking around in astonishment, murmured as she gazed at the throne made of roots and the human-shaped thing.

"I wonder. But... considering how blatantly it seems to have been waiting for us like this, I'm sure it's connected to Treant Forest."

Rei murmured, staring intently at the figure made of tree roots.

Since it was so blatantly waiting for them, it must intend to communicate.

That's what he thought, but facing the tree root doll, Rei hesitated.

Should he just speak to it... or should he just take it down right now?

He hesitated, but realized that lingering here would only increase the burden on the adventurers currently fighting. So, he moved forward while speaking.

"Do you understand my words?"

He asked, but the tree root doll showed no reaction.

If it moved even slightly, he'd know it heard him, but since it didn't even twitch, Rei was at a loss for how to respond.

"...Could it be... that doll isn't actually a monster or anything like that? Could it really just be a regular doll?"

"Who would make a doll like that here?"

Getting this far would undoubtedly be a considerable feat for any ordinary adventurer.

And to outmaneuver Rei and the others to get here and create a scene like this? That wasn't something just anyone could pull off.

On top of that, this vast space definitely didn't exist when Rei and the others were in the sky.

Which means, if someone really did create this, they must have made it while Rei and the others were walking the path to get here.

(Which would also mean they made that path too.)

Rei looked at the figure ahead and concluded that was highly unlikely.

If that was the case, then the scene unfolding before them must undoubtedly hold some meaning.

Even so... even if it did have meaning, the doll showing no sign of reacting at all when Rei called out to it was nothing but creepy.

"So, a chair... no, it's pretty big. A throne? If they made a throne like this to wait for us, it wouldn't be strange to think they have knowledge about humans."

"But there are goblin kings and orc kings, right? Couldn't the knowledge have come from them?"

"Ah... yeah, well, maybe that's true."

His confident answer was easily dismissed, and Rei looked away as if trying to hide something, stroking Set.

What's wrong? he wondered, watching Set purr. Regaining his composure, Rei spoke again.

"Anyway. Whether it's goblins, orcs, or even ogres... whatever they are, I just wanted to say they're not creatures rampaging purely on instinct. They have intelligence."

"I know that much. ...So what are we going to do about it?"

"Well, what do you expect me to do?"

Sighing, Rei looked once more at the throne made of tree roots and the doll.

Having gone to all this trouble, he couldn't believe it meant nothing.

But if asked what that meaning was, he had to admit he couldn't help but tilt his head in confusion.

(Worst case, I could try destroying it... but doing something like that in the current situation would definitely be a bad idea.)

If he carelessly destroyed it, there was no telling what might happen.

If that doll actually held the secret to the Treant Forest, and destroying it stopped the forest from spreading further... well, that would be the best possible outcome.

But conversely, that doll might be the very thing holding back the Treant Forest's expansion. Destroying it could cause the forest to spread explosively at an even faster rate than before.

"I'll go check it out for now. Set and Vihera, be ready to respond immediately no matter what happens."

"Is that okay? Or rather, are you leaving me behind?"

Vihera's words carried a hint of dissatisfaction.

Why leave her behind when there might be a fight...?

"From what we can see, we don't know what might happen. ...Besides, we can't rule out the possibility that the trees here are Treants."

At Rei's words, Vihera turned her gaze to the trees lining the edge of this space.

Most trees growing in this Treant forest possess a certain minimum size... thickness.

But the trees growing here were even thicker and larger than those.

If these trees were Treants, they were undoubtedly more powerful monsters than any Treant they had fought before.

At Rei's words, Vihera glanced around at the surroundings.

Vihera had experienced battles with Treants in this forest before.

From the feel of the fight, they weren't exactly formidable foes, which was precisely why Vihera had stopped fighting the Treant and moved to battle the giant flower growing deep within the forest.

(But this place is undoubtedly important to the Treant forest, right? Since they're guarding it, it makes sense that the Treants appearing here would be stronger than those found on the forest's outer edges...)

What crossed Vihera's mind at that moment was the difference between a regular soldier and a guard soldier.

Even among soldiers, there are ordinary ones and powerful ones.

Perhaps this Treant was, in that sense, a guard soldier.

The term 'guard' had surfaced here, likely because, however imperfectly, something resembling a throne did exist.

Moreover, this space clearly felt different from the Treant Forest they had been walking through until now.

"Well, I suppose there's no helping it."

In the end, Vihera nodded at Rei's words and prepared herself, ready for whatever might happen.

Set watched Rei with concern, but still gave a small growl, accepting that it was Rei's decision.

Accompanied by the farewells of one person and one beast, Rei approached the throne at the center and the doll seated upon it.

Of course, he was fully prepared to respond immediately if anything happened, holding the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left.

Only the sound of his footsteps crushing the grass beneath him echoed around him.

By this point, even Rei's keen ears could no longer hear the sounds of battle raging outside Treant Forest.

...If any adventurers were using explosions, perhaps he might have heard them... Fortunately or unfortunately, no such individuals existed.

Well, Marina, who could use spirit magic, could probably cause an explosion if she wanted to.

She didn't do it because the guild placed great value on this forest.

(Still, if push came to shove, Marina would make the decision, though.)

Thinking such thoughts, Rei continued walking... until he finally arrived before the throne.

He stared intently at the doll fashioned from tree roots.

It was larger than Rei, but still not enormous—not three or four meters tall.

Facing that root-made doll... Rei remained vigilant, ready for anything, and spoke.

"Do you understand my words?"

The instant he spoke, without any wind or movement, the puppet twitched its arm.
