Rei stared wide-eyed at the food in front of him.

Ara had shown him to the guest room, and they had been chatting for a while. When Rei mentioned he was a little hungry, Ara headed to the kitchen and soon returned with the dish now spread out before him.

...It was round, steaming, and filled the air with a savory aroma.

The aroma was so appetizing that one could easily imagine the rich flavor of the seafood it contained.

The only downside for Rei was that the sauce drizzled over it resembled demi-glace.

He also found it a bit disappointing that there were no toppings like bonito flakes or green seaweed.

That said… just looking at it, it was easy to tell it would be more than delicious enough.

“Why is there seafood okonomiyaki here…?”

Rei muttered, his surprise evident as he looked at the seafood okonomiyaki right in front of him.

“Looks like I’ve managed to surprise you,”

Seeing Rei like that, Ara smiled amusedly.

Since Rei rarely shows surprise, in that sense, Ara must have witnessed something quite rare.

Seeing Ara’s smug expression, Rei wondered if he had mentioned seafood okonomiyaki in passing at some point before…?

“This dish, which became popular in Emoshion, can serve as a meal on its own, and once you’ve prepared the ingredients, you can easily make a large batch. That’s why it’s become quite famous these days.”

“So, how did a dish that became popular in Emoshion end up in the Duchy of Kerebel?”

The Duchy of Kerebel and Emoshion are quite a distance apart.

Even within the Kingdom of Mireana, it’s not a distance you can just casually travel.

That’s precisely why Rei wondered why seafood okonomiyaki had appeared here.

“The chef at this estate is quite ambitious when it comes to perfecting flavors. In fact, the reason he decided to work for the Duke of Kerebel in the first place was so he could cook to his heart’s content using the Duke’s wealth and connections, and gain access to rare ingredients. …Apparently, he holds you in quite high regard, Rei.”

“Me? Why on earth?”

“It’s only natural—just look at this seafood okonomiyaki, or Gilm’s udon, meat buns, and pizza. Considering how he’s come up with one dish after another that we never would have imagined before, it makes perfect sense. Anyway, please help yourself. It’s a special treat, so let’s eat it before it gets cold.”

In front of Ara, who said that, the same seafood okonomiyaki as Rei’s was prepared.

In fact, the reason she wanted to eat it before it got cold was probably because that’s exactly what Ara was thinking.

Rei understood that, but since it was a freshly made dish, he could also understand Ara’s desire to eat it before it got cold.

Nodding at her words, he picked up a knife and fork.

(Come to think of it, eating okonomiyaki with a knife and fork... Well, usually you use chopsticks, but the Kingdom of Mireana doesn’t have them. That TV special... was it a spatula? Maybe I could ask them to make one of those.)

Although it felt a little odd, I cut the seafood okonomiyaki with a knife, just as I would a steak, and brought it to my mouth.

The first thing that hit my palate was the refreshing taste of the sauce.

Based on my past experience, I’d expected a richer flavor… but while surprised by the unexpected taste, I savored the seafood okonomiyaki in my mouth.

The crisp texture of the shrimp, the vegetable with a bite similar to cabbage, the white fish that melts in my mouth, and the shellfish cooked just right—not overcooked, with a perfect spring to it.

The flavors, filling my mouth, blended seamlessly to delight Rei’s palate.

“Delicious…”

The taste of that seafood okonomiyaki must have been delicious enough for Ara as well.

With a hint of surprise in her voice, Ara murmurs.

Of course, Ara knew that the chef who made this dish was highly skilled.

Still, tasting it firsthand like this, she found herself captivated by the flavor.

"Yeah, it’s quite different from what I make, but it’s still really good."

“Huh? Is it different from what you came up with, Lord Rei?”

“I didn’t come up with it—I just made it… but whatever. Ah, well. Though, it’s just the little details that are different; the overall concept is pretty much the same.”

That’s what Rei said, but in reality, the seafood okonomiyaki right in front of him was of a higher quality than the one Rei had made at Emoshion before.

Of course, since the seafood okonomiyaki Rei had made was based solely on the knowledge he’d acquired as a culinary amateur while in Japan, it wouldn’t be surprising if a professional chef found it lacking in various ways.

Also, a major factor was the lack of other ingredients and seasonings that, while not strictly essential for okonomiyaki—such as bonito flakes, mayonnaise, and green laver—definitely make it taste better when included.

However, since Rei was the only one aware of the lack of such ingredients and seasonings, the chefs who discovered seafood okonomiyaki at Emoshion had been making their own improvements on their own.

Several years had already passed since Rei introduced the seafood okonomiyaki… Naturally, thanks to these improvements, the seafood okonomiyaki had come to taste even better than the version Rei had taught them.

And then, a skilled chef employed by Duke Kerebel refined it even further… resulting in a dish so delicious that even Rei would be amazed.

“The chef who prepared this dish would like to meet Rei later. Would that be all right?”

While Rei was savoring the seafood okonomiyaki, Ara suddenly asked him this.

When Rei looked at Ara at those words, her plate was already empty.

She must have remembered to ask Rei about it just as she finished eating the seafood okonomiyaki.

But upon hearing this, Rei was a little taken aback.

“Well, I guess I could meet him, but there’s no particular dish I can teach him just because we meet. I mean, there are a few things I vaguely remember, but that’s really just a vague recollection.”

“Would that be okay with you?”

“……Well, if that’s what you say, Ara, I don’t really mind.”

For the time being, Rei would be staying at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

Given that, meeting someone who’ll cook for him during that time wouldn’t bother Rei at all.

If meeting him meant he’d cook delicious meals for him, Rei would be more than happy to see him as often as possible.

“I see. Then I’ll let him know later. Um… he’s quite passionate about cooking, but he can be a bit rude at times, so I hope you’ll bear with him…”

Seeing how Ara seemed a bit hesitant to say this, Rei could vaguely guess what kind of person she was.

For someone who has mastered—or is in the process of mastering—a single craft, it’s natural for other aspects of life to become somewhat neglected. Having met many different people over the years, Rei could understand that.

“That’s fine by me. It’ll be easier than dealing with some snobby noble who thinks he’s something special.”

“Hahaha.”

Since she could imagine all too well exactly who Rei meant by a “deluded noble,” all Ara could do was laugh it off.

Despite Ara’s reaction, Rei said nothing more and simply savored his seafood okonomiyaki.

About ten minutes later, Rei’s plate was completely empty.

After the maid Ara had called cleared the plate and left the room, Ara proceeded to brew some tea for Rei.

Ara’s skill at making tea is exceptional—even Elena looks up to her.

Sipping the tea, Rei chatted casually with Ara.

“So, what about Elena?”

“Well, she’s just returned to Anesis, so I imagine she’s quite busy with various things.”

“...And you, Ara, aren’t busy? You’re the captain of Elena’s guard, aren’t you?”

At Rei’s words, Ara gently looked away.

Rei could vaguely understand what that gesture meant.

Still, he decided that bringing it up wouldn’t be pleasant for Ara either, so Rei decided not to press the matter further.

(After all, now that we’re back in the Duchy of Kerebel, Ara probably has plenty of work to do. It’s okay to let her escape reality for a little while.)

With that in mind, he changed the subject and continued talking… when suddenly, there was a knock at the door.

At the sound of the knock, Ara, who had been drinking her tea, froze for a moment.

She couldn’t help but think that perhaps they had come to summon her regarding the Knights’ work.

Since Ara had frozen in place, Rei replied that he could come in for now.

At Rei’s words, Ara shot him a slightly resentful glance, but… the next moment, the person who opened the door and stepped inside was not the one Ara had hoped wouldn’t come.

A man about 180 cm tall with a sturdy build.

He looked to be in his forties.

But his eyes held a strong curiosity, which made him look somewhat younger than his actual age.

“...Who is that?”

Naturally, Rei had no idea who this man was, so he glanced at Ara, wondering, “Could he be one of Elena’s bodyguard knights?”

Ara, who felt Rei’s gaze upon her, wore an expression that was half joy and half confusion.

“Lord Georgi, what on earth are you doing here…?”

“Well, that’s obvious, isn’t it? I wanted to meet this Rei guy everyone’s been talking about. Hey, are you Rei? Have you been coming up with all kinds of dishes?”

“Ah… I see.”

With those words alone, Rei understood exactly what kind of person Georgiima, standing before him, was.

In other words, he must be the chef at Duke Kerebel’s mansion that Ara had mentioned earlier.

“Huh? What’s wrong? You’ve been coming up with recipes, right? It’s amazing for someone so young. I’d love to know how you come up with them.”

“Ah… yeah. Georgiima, was it? You’re definitely the chef at Kerebel Duke’s mansion, right?”

“That’s right.”

In response to Rei’s words, Georgiama replied with a confident expression.

He must have that much confidence in his skills as a chef.

“I see. You seem to know, but I’ll say it anyway. I’m Rei. Now, regarding the cooking… I don’t know if you know this, Georgiama, but I was raised by a wizard deep in the mountains when I was little. The recipes I’ve shared are ones I remember reading in books back then—I didn’t come up with them myself.”

Rei makes his usual excuse, partly to keep Georgi in check.

In reality, Rei isn’t all that knowledgeable about cooking, so he couldn’t answer if someone brought up specific culinary topics.

“Flambe? Isn’t that the one where you pour in wine or something and burn off the alcohol?” That’s about the extent of Rei’s culinary knowledge.

No, actually, just knowing the name of the cooking technique “flambé” is probably a big deal for Rei.

Rei tried to fob Georgima off like that, but...

“Ha ha ha. Don’t worry about it. Even if it’s just knowledge, you’ve used it to spread the word about countless dishes. That achievement deserves praise, not criticism.”

Seeing him laugh so heartily, Rei let out a sigh.

That said, the sigh wasn’t directed at an unpleasant person; it was, to some extent, a sign of goodwill.

“Anyway, I love eating good food, but I don’t know much about cooking techniques. If that’s okay with you, I’ll listen to what you have to say. What do you say?”

“That’s not a problem. Actually, hearing what you just said has made me want to hear even more detailed information.”

As Georgiama spoke with curiosity shining in her eyes, Rei looked at Ara, unsure of what to do.

Feeling his gaze, Ara addressed Georgiima with a touch of hesitation.

“Lord Georgi, have you finished preparing dinner for today? I heard we were having a small party to celebrate Elena’s return and to welcome Rei.”

“Huh? As for that, most of the preparations are already done. That’s exactly why I’m here.”

“Ah… I see. I suppose I didn’t need to mention that to you, Lord Georgiima.”

Georgima is the kind of person who pours her entire being into cooking; it’s not uncommon for her to even join adventurers in hunting monsters for ingredients.

Thanks to Kerebel Duke’s wealth, he no longer does such things these days, but his obsession with cooking remains strong.

“Yeah. I’d never cut corners when it comes to cooking!”

As Georgiama declared this with such confidence, Rei suddenly felt a mischievous urge.

“Alright then. If there’s a dish at today’s party that I can honestly say tastes delicious from the bottom of my heart, I’ll teach you one recipe I know. …But keep in mind, I only know the general method. You’ll have to figure out the details through trial and error. That’s how udon and other dishes became what they are today. Can you do it?”

At Rei’s words, Georgima fixed her gaze sharply on him… and nodded with a fierce grin.
