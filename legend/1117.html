Rei entered the mansion and parted ways with Set at the entrance before being shown to the guest quarters.

Set, who had parted ways at the entrance, had been instructed by Mebastian to ensure the mansion staff were given proper food—not just scraps—so he should have filled his stomach before Rei by now.

In this case, the truly pitiful ones were those ordered to feed Set.

After all, a man not as thick-skinned as Mebastian would have approached Set... the griffin... with terror at seeing him up close, cautiously and fearfully.

...And so, they ended up asking the cooks serving the Count Salmas household to prepare numerous dishes and bring them to Set.

The cooks, who were busy preparing the meal for Ryu Brand and Rei while also having to make dishes for Set, were probably the busiest people in the mansion right now.

"Huh..."

Rei took a sip of the tea Mebastian had the maid prepare and was slightly surprised.

He had assumed it would be ordinary black tea, but upon tasting it, he found a slight spiciness.

For a moment, he wondered if it might be poison or something, but he quickly realized it was some kind of spice.

Not being particularly knowledgeable about cooking, Rei didn't know the name of the spice.

But after the faint spiciness, a rich milk flavor spread across his palate.

(Back in Japan, I saw something called chai, an Indian milk tea, on TV once. Is this similar? But in a desert like this, it's probably not cow's milk... but milk from some other animal... or maybe a monster.)

The first sip surprised him, but with the second and third sips, he found it surprisingly suited Rei's taste.

As he sipped this chai-like beverage, a knock soon sounded at the cabin door.

"Excuse me, Lord Rei. Your meal is ready. Lord Ryu Brand requests your presence."

"Understood."

Rei nodded at Mebaschan's words and rose from the sofa where he'd been sitting.

For a moment, he wondered if this attire was appropriate, but then he reminded himself that he'd meet Daskar, the lord of Gilm, dressed just like this.

...Well, in Daskar's case, his unconventional personality meant he wouldn't care what anyone looked like unless something truly extraordinary happened.

"Then lead the way."

"Yes, this way."

Mebastian bowed and led Rei out of the reception room.

They walked down the hallway for a few minutes before stopping in front of a room.

The door was larger than those of the guest rooms, likely because this was the dining room.

Looking at the door, which was a size or two larger than the guest room doors, Rei pondered.

It must be large for when they gather many guests for meals, or to bring in dishes on large platters.

And perhaps because he had just recalled Daskar, he remembered the door to the study in Daskar's mansion.

A door so luxurious it could be called a work of art in its own right.

To Rei, it was a door she would have been afraid to knock on, but those serving Daskar were likely accustomed to it. They reached for the door without any particular hesitation.

Thinking this way, I entered what appeared to be the dining room, and my guess proved spot on.

A large rectangular table stood there, and at one end of it sat a man.

A man of medium build and height, around forty.

At first glance, Rei didn't recognize him as a Ryu Brand.

For better or worse, most of the nobles Rei had met so far were quite distinctive in various ways.

Even those less distinctive usually had some memorable aspect.

But... the man now in Rei's line of sight possessed none of those distinctive traits.

He was the kind of person whose defining feature was having no defining features.

(Is this really Ryu Brand? Could he actually be a body double?)

That's the kind of doubt Rei had, but while a country like the Kingdom of Mireana might use a body double, he couldn't imagine a small country like the Kingdom of Solrain doing so.

(So then, is this guy the real deal? No, but... Hmm, what's going on here?)

As Rei agonized over whether the man before him was genuine, Ryu Brand spoke.

"So you're Rei? I hear you're an adventurer with a nickname. I'm glad to meet you. Come, take a seat. It's a bit early, but let's have lunch."

Before Ryu Brand lay several dishes.

Particularly eye-catching was the fish caught in the oasis.

Though an extremely precious ingredient in Gosh, perhaps his position as the lord of Gosh allowed him to procure it so easily.

(Well, Augusto had fish prepared too, so it probably isn't priced completely out of reach for commoners.)

Gazing at the steamed fish dish, Rei sat down in a chair as Ryu Brand urged him to.

"We've received quite a few reports about you. I hear you defeated Sand Serpents and sand pirates with sand ships, even taking down a Sand Lizardman. True to your nickname, you seem quite skilled."

Rei hesitated over how to address the man before him. After a moment's thought, and considering they weren't yet clearly enemies, he replied in a polite tone—polite for Rei, anyway.

"Fortunately, I have an excellent partner."

"Oh? Is that so? It seems the Griffin truly is a splendid monster. ...Well then? I hear that Griffin is your familiar. Would you be willing to part with it?"

For a moment, Ray wondered if that was the reason he'd been invited. Yet, despite that implication, there was no greedy glint in Ryu Brand's eyes that suggested he desperately wanted the griffin.

He says he wants it, but it doesn't feel like he truly desires it from the bottom of his heart.

(What? He really is a type I've never seen before. What's going on?)

Rei was puzzled, but that didn't mean he could just accept Ryu Brand's proposal here.

"I'm afraid not. Set is a monster who grew up with me since he was small. You could say he's my friend... no, more like family. Would you, Lord Ryu Brand, hand over your family if someone asked for them?"

"I would."

"Exactly. So, I too..."

Rei's gaze shifted to Ryu Brand as those words slipped casually into her sentence.

Meeting Rei's gaze, Ryu Brand continued as if surprised by something.

"Listen, for nobles, the existence of the house itself is more important than the individual. I'm no different. That's precisely why I married a wife ten years my senior."

"...But you don't seem to have one?"

If you're married, wouldn't it be natural for your wife and children to be here in this dining hall? To Rei's remark, Ryu Brand gave a small smile and shrugged his shoulders.

"My wife and son are currently away on business in Larnid."

"...Lanaid?"

Though unfamiliar, Rei guessed it must be some city. This time, however, Ryu Brand looked surprised at Rei's reaction.

"You don't know? It's the capital of the Solrain Kingdom."

"Ah... now that you mention it, I think I've heard of it."

He thought he might have heard it from Marina... Hiding his embarrassment, he reached his fork toward the steamed fish.

The delicate white fish melted softly in his mouth, paired with a sauce rich in spices.

It was undoubtedly a dish that could be described as quite delicious...

(Cathy's cooking skills are definitely better.)

The food Cathy made at Augusto's house was definitely superior in flavor.

(In this case, should I praise Cathy for her superior cooking skills compared to the manor's chef...? No, but with Cathy, she'd probably just say something like, "It's the power of love," even if you praised her.)

Having witnessed her fiery passion countless times, Rei couldn't help but let a faint wry smile touch his lips.

Noticing Rei's wry smile, Ryu Brand spoke up.

"What's the matter? This steamed fish dish is quite a luxury item here in Gosh. Of course, for Rei who lived in the Mireana Kingdom, it might not be that special... Did it not suit your taste?"

"No, it's delicious. I'd never eaten fish from an oasis before coming to Gosh."

Those words were absolutely true.

The fish Rei had eaten until now were basically river fish. And sea fish bought in the port town of Emoshion.

Fish raised in an oasis? He'd only ever tasted it once before, in a dish served at Augusto's house.

(I've never eaten fish from lakes or such, but is this what they're like?)

That thought flashed through Rei's mind, but before he could dwell on it further, Ryu Brand spoke up.

"I see, I see. Hearing you say that makes me happy too. Normally, I'd serve a more elaborate dish... but this time it was sudden."

"Well, that's certainly true."

Mebastian had come to see Rei, which was how they ended up at this lord's residence.

Considering that, it was quite impressive that they'd prepared this much food in such a short time since Rei arrived.

(If that's the case, then since Kathy took her time cooking, it doesn't necessarily mean she's better than the cook here, does it?)

Ryu Brand's words made Rei realize he'd been mistaken.

It wasn't that anyone could make tastier food given enough time, but still, with a certain level of skill...

The time Rei had spent waiting in the guest room after arriving at the lord's mansion wasn't particularly long.

If, during that time, she had prepared several dishes—from steamed fish to stew, stir-fries, and salads using vegetables and fruits—then she was undoubtedly top-notch.

As Ray savored the array of dishes before him once more, Ryu Brand, who had been watching him with satisfaction, suddenly spoke up.

"Now then, Rei. I summoned you here partly because I wished to meet an adventurer with a nickname, but there are other reasons as well."

"...Reasons, you say?"

Rei asked while sandwiching something resembling meat simmered in oil—a dish that could be called meat-based oil sardines—between slices of bread. Ryu Brand nodded in response.

"Yes. You've only been in Gosh for a few days, yet you've already become quite famous. You understand that, don't you?"

"Well, that's..."

He was close with Augusto, who opposed Daldora, the head of the influential Erema Trading Company that commanded griffin Set as a familiar here in Gosh. Furthermore, he had even seized the sand ship Daldora possessed. ...Though nominally, it was presented as having been taken from sand pirates.

It was only natural that someone like you would become a topic of conversation in Gosh—a city that, while fairly large within the Kingdom of Solrain, isn't as big as Gilm.

Few have actually witnessed Rei's true strength firsthand, but those who have—like those who received protection from Augusto alongside Zarust, or the four members of the Sand Claw—certainly exist.

And above all, the reason Rei was the talk of the town was undoubtedly due to Set's presence.

The very existence of a griffin carried that kind of intense impact.

"Let me get straight to the point. Are you opposed to planting Tira trees around Gosh to counter monsters?"

"Huh? Um, why bring that up all of a sudden?"

Of course, Rei knew opinions were split right now in Gosh over whether to use Tira trees to repair the walls or as a monster countermeasure.

While divided, the fact that Daldora supports using Tira trees means that side holds an overwhelming advantage.

Even so, to say the faction supporting Augusto's plan to use the existing barrier is powerless would be completely wrong.

Besides Augusto, there are others who, to some extent, operate independently beyond the reach of the Elema Trading Company.

Augusto is fighting alongside such individuals to oppose the use of Tira wood, but naturally, they were at a disadvantage.

But... what if someone like Rei were to join their cause?

Not only would it add pure combat strength, but it would also generate significant buzz.

For Ryu Brand, allied with Daldora, this was absolutely unacceptable.

Rei paused, considering the Ryu Brand staring intently at him.

Should he get involved in this matter concerning Gosh?

Since Marina had urged him to come here, he knew there must be some reason, but he didn't know if this was it. Precisely because he didn't know...

"I don't know yet,"

That was his answer.
