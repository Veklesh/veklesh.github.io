In the countryside... in a village like any other, Zakunya.

Though it was a small village with a population of about fifty, quite a commotion was currently brewing within it.

"Move aside, Father! I have to go find Shira!"

"No way! It's already getting dark! If you leave the village at night in this state, you'll definitely get into trouble. Think about that!"

"But Shira!"

The man was about to dash out of the house to search for Shira... his beloved who had left saying she was going to gather nuts and never returned.

His father desperately tried to stop him, but the age difference meant the boy dragged his father along as he burst out of the house...

"Calm down, you fool!"

With those words, a fist came crashing down on the man's skull.

"Gah!"

Sparking pain erupted before his eyes, and the man collapsed to his knees.

The person who caused the sparks... the person waiting for the man in front of the house, seeing him crouched there, opened his mouth in exasperation.

"You don't need to risk your life for that stupid girl."

"What the hell? She's my own daughter! You can't talk to me like that!"

"Because she is my daughter. ...It doesn't have to be you who goes looking for her. I'll go."

"...Huh?"

The man, who had been holding his head, looked up again at the man... at Shira's father, wondering what he'd just said.

But seeing Shira's father clad in leather armor made from monster hide, a massive greatsword in his hand, he couldn't help but understand the true meaning behind those words.

He'd heard stories about him being an adventurer in the past, but he'd never seen him look this much like one before.

That's precisely why the man shouted.

"Are your knees okay?!"

At the man's words, Shira's father smiled defiantly and spoke.

"Hmph, it doesn't matter. They probably won't hold up in a long battle, but that's still better than letting my future son-in-law die."

"Wh-what!? A stepson...?"

What that meant was clear.

In other words, the man standing before him acknowledged him as Schiller's husband.

...Though Shira was somewhat of a natural. It would likely take time before she understood and accepted the man's feelings.

That was precisely why Shira, despite being past twenty—an age often considered "left behind" in this village where many married in their mid-teens—had never been in such a relationship with anyone.

While that was a relief to the man, it also meant that most of his intense advances were brushed off, which wasn't entirely positive.

Still, since Shira's father had come to understand the man's feelings, it wasn't entirely wasted effort.

"Just wait. I'll find Shira and bring her back..."

He'll come.

Shira's father was about to finish that sentence, but before he could, someone else appeared, running toward them.

Already in what could be called adventurer mode, Shira's father raised the greatsword in his hand, ready to strike at the intruder. But the runner turned out to be someone he knew.

This village was small.

That meant it was safe to say all the villagers knew each other.

In such a village, it wasn't hard to understand that the person running was a resident.

"Hey, Shira's back! She's safe! Relax, Regula, Jazz!"

The one called Regula was a man who harbored feelings for Shira.

The one called Jazz was Shira's father.

Both men widened their eyes at the words of the villager who had run all the way here.

Their expressions were mostly joyful, but there was also no shortage of confusion.

They questioned why Shira had returned at this hour... despite it being summer, the sun had set, and it was now fully nighttime.

Of course, they were happy Shira had returned, but still, they wondered how he had managed to come back safely at such an hour.

"Did Shira seem strange in any way?"

Jazz asked, seeking confirmation. The man who had come to report Shira's return nodded hesitantly.

"Ah, yeah. Actually, it seems an adventurer was with her. Apparently, she rescued someone who'd been taken by orcs..."

"Orcs!?"

At the word "orcs," Regula's voice cracked as she cried out.

She knew exactly what it meant for a woman to be taken by orcs.

That was precisely why she couldn't help but think that the person she cared for had suffered such a fate.

"Seems like you don't need to worry about that. The adventurer defeated the orc and rescued her before anything happened to Shira."

Hearing this, not only Regula but also Shira's father, Jaz, breathed a sigh of relief.

They must have feared the worst for their daughter.

"So, what about that adventurer? Since he saved my daughter, we should thank him, right?"

"Ah... well, that..."

Seeing the villager suddenly stammering, the three who'd heard the story—Regula, Jaz, and Regula's father—felt a pang of doubt.

Something had definitely happened, but they couldn't figure out what it was.

If Shira had been taken hostage or something, the man who came to tell them would have looked much more serious.

The fact that he wasn't meant it couldn't be anything critically bad, that much was certain...

"Alright, I'll go."

He didn't know what had happened.

But for now, Jazz, a former Rank C adventurer, could handle most situations, no matter what.

Jazz's decision stemmed from that kind of mindset.

And as the strongest in the village, Jazz. Regula and her father accepted it.

...Unnoticed by them, the man who brought news of Shira's safe return wore a slightly awkward expression.

Watching the three hurry away, the man spoke up.

"No matter how strong Jazz is, I don't think he could handle a Griffin."

Those words faded softly into the night's darkness.

"Shira! Are you... safe...?"

Regula called out to Shira, whom she found at the village entrance, but her words trailed off mid-sentence.

Because where Regula's gaze fell, Shira was happily stroking the massive body of a griffin.

The villagers gathered around, seemingly unsure how to interpret the situation, couldn't bring themselves to speak to Shira.

"Um... huh? What's going on? Shira?"

"Oh, Regula? Why are you so flustered? And Dad too. You hurt your knee, you shouldn't be pushing yourself. Bringing out weapons from your adventurer days like that..."

What came out of Shira's mouth wasn't a voice of relief, nor one of joy at their reunion.

Regula felt a strange sense of relief at that.

This. This felt like the real Shira.

Feeling relieved, she still decided she needed to hear the explanation now. She turned her gaze to the griffin Shira was stroking... and only then did she notice another person besides Shira and the griffin.

Of course, that person, Rei, had been there from the start.

But seeing Shira, whom he thought had disappeared, and Set, who delivered an impact far surpassing that, was shocking enough.

Faced with such a creature and its companion, it was no wonder he hadn't noticed Rei.

...Though, if Rei had been without his Dragon Robe hood, his feminine features might have caused some surprise.

In any case, Jazz, Regula, and Regula's father all breathed a sigh of relief that Shira was safe.

Naturally, the next concern was Rei and Set.

"Shira, um... who are that person and that griffin?"

At her father's question, Shira opened her mouth with a beaming smile.

"Remember when that bard came before and sang about it? The adventurer who distinguished themselves in the war against the Bestir Empire. They earned the nickname 'Crimson' for their exploits back then. That's this child right here."

"This one," she said. Shira referred to Rei as a child, but Rei didn't say anything in response.

Of course, if someone took such an attitude toward him with the intent to belittle him, Rei would respond in kind.

But in Shira's case, there was no particular malice; she simply acted that way toward Rei without a second thought.

...Whether that was amusing or not was, well, slightly not amusing.

"I see. So that Crimson saved your daughter. I'm Jazz, Shira's father. Let me thank you for saving my daughter. Thank you."

Jazz bowed deeply.

Following Jazz, Regula also bowed.

Shira looked puzzled, wondering why not only his father but Regula bowed too, but then thought, Well, I guess that's natural if they're friends.

"Hey, what's with all of you? I mean, I know it was bad that I got taken by the orcs, but..."

"Yeah! And why were you out of the village alone anyway? You're always told to go out in groups!"

Regular shouted, lifting his head at Shira's words.

For Regula, it was a statement born of deep concern for Shira, but Shira couldn't understand that.

She puffed out her cheeks in displeasure.

"What could I do? Everyone was busy."

"Yeah, but busy or not... Ugh. Anyway, I'm just glad you're safe. Jazz was about to head out of the village to look for you any minute now."

Regula stated this without mentioning that Jazz had stopped him from leaving the village himself.

"You're the one saying that?" Several people who knew Regula's personality shot her a look, but she seemed completely unfazed by their stares.

Actually, Regula was in a complicated state: relieved to see Shira was safe, yet irritated by Shira's current utterly unconcerned attitude. She simply didn't have the mental bandwidth to notice those glances.

"I'm sorry. But don't worry, Rei helped me. More importantly, I want to bring Rei into the village. Is that okay?"

"Hmm, I suppose it's fine."

The one who answered was an old man who had arrived a little while ago and had been watching Shira and Regula's exchange.

"The Village Chief."

Someone among the villagers murmured.

With so many villagers gathered, it wasn't surprising the Village Chief had come.

"Indeed. ...You mentioned Rei? I wish to express my gratitude for your assistance to our villagers. As you see here."

He bowed deeply, just as Jazz and Regula had done earlier.

"No, don't worry about it. It was just a coincidence for me too, and orcs are literally tasty prey for me as well."

"I-I see. ...But still, we must repay you for saving Shira. However, as you can see, this Zakunya is a small village. We doubt we have anything worthy of a renowned adventurer like Rei..."

"Ah, I already know what I want."

Hearing Rei say this, the village chief tensed, though he tried not to show it.

He didn't know what Ray wanted.

As he himself had said, Zakunya was only a small village of about fifty people.

If something like that existed here, something a renowned adventurer like Rei would desire...

Whatever he imagined, the village chief's gaze shifted toward Shira.

Though somewhat older, Shira still possessed above-average looks.

If he desired Shira's body... The village chief likely thought this because most adventurers he'd met before were unsavory characters.

...Shira's father, Jaz, was also an adventurer, but he was likely seen as one of the rare exceptions.

Regula was the one who instantly understood the meaning behind the village chief's gaze.

Having romantic feelings for Shira, she was extremely sensitive to that kind of gaze.

"What!? Village Chief!?"

Blood rushed to Regula's head as she tried to say something, but before she could, Rei spoke up.

"So, this village has some famous dish, right? You're saying you'll feed it to me and Set until we're stuffed?"

"Huh?"

The words that came out of Rei's mouth were completely unexpected. Not just the Village Chief, Regula, and Jazz, but every villager who had been listening murmured in unison, as if caught off guard.

A Rank B adventurer with a nickname.

They wondered if it was really okay to put such a person to work and pay them with food.

But Rei, whose gaze was fixed on the village chief, wasn't joking at all... He was completely serious.

Realizing this, the village chief eventually let out a hearty laugh and invited Rei and Set into the village.
