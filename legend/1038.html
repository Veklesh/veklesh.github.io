After checking on the World Tree, Marina returned to the settlement, where Byune was dismantling the Aerowings.

Rei , Elena , Vihera , Ara , and Jusla—who had come for the Aero Wing—followed Byune's instructions as they proceeded with the dismantling.

Though Byune's instructions were precise, the sight of them dismantling the craft using only her characteristic terse commands was quite an unusual spectacle.

But since Byune possessed the highest dismantling skills among those present, this situation was only natural.

Simultaneously, Vihera, seemingly interpreting Byune's intentions, relayed instructions to the others.

And since he was also doing his own work properly, it could be said that Vihera, while not quite at Byune's level, possessed a certain degree of skill in disassembly as well.

(I wonder what on earth happened to the former Imperial Princess?)

Marina briefly pondered this question before approaching to assist with the dismantling herself.

Though Marina now held the position of Guildmaster in Gilm, she had originally worked as an adventurer.

Naturally, that included dismantling the monsters they defeated, and she considered herself reasonably skilled at it.

"May I join you?"

That's why she announced herself and began working on the Aerowings' disassembly.

"Lady Marina!?"

Jusla was naturally the one most surprised by Marina's actions.

But the others accepted Marina without any particular surprise.

"Well, you're late. Have you finished your business?"

"Yes. I'll talk to you after dinner."

Marina gave a brief reply to Vihera and immediately began dismantling the Aerowings.

The first task, which the others were also doing, was plucking the feathers.

Aerowing feathers were valuable not only as decorative items but also as raw materials.

Considering the Aero Wing as a whole, they were probably the second most valuable material after magic stones.

But... as Vihera plucked feathers from one wing, she frowned slightly.

"The quality of these feathers is rather poor, isn't it?"

"I think it's probably due to the World Tree incident..."

"I wonder..."

Marina tilted her head at Vihera's words, then reached for the feathers... Even for such a large monster, with this many people working on the dissection, it would be finished in no time.

In the end, the magic stone went to Rei as she'd insisted, while the rest of the meat was cooked and eaten by everyone.

...As for the feathers, Vihera and Marina ended up splitting them between themselves.

And so, they set about cooking... but once again, it was Byune and Marina who took the lead.

It wasn't that Rei couldn't cook, but he could only manage truly simple... bold, or to put it bluntly, rather rough-and-ready man's cooking. As for Elena and the others, most of them had no cooking skills either.

They could manage to make something edible, but creating something delicious was difficult.

Jusla could cook to some extent, but she still couldn't hold a candle to the likes of Byune and Marina.

In the end, those two took the lead, preparing dishes using the Aero Wing's meat.

Despite its size, the Aerowings yielded surprisingly little meat. Still, under normal circumstances, this amount should have been more than enough for everyone to eat their fill.

But... there were big eaters like Rei and Byune here, and Set ate even more than those two.

In the end, the meat wasn't enough, so they ended up eating dishes taken from Rei's Misty Ring.

Yellow aside, Set's size made it difficult for him to enter the house, so today's meal was held in the garden Elena and the others were borrowing.

The Aero Wing meat transformed into various dishes: skewers, stews, stir-fries, and more.

And now, facing the array of dishes Rei had prepared... Marina raised her glass and spoke.

"Well then, to celebrate arriving safely... safely? Well, to celebrate arriving safely at the settlement, cheers!"

"Cheers!"

Marina hesitated momentarily, remembering the pulsations and the square when she said "safely," but she quickly continued.

Lifting her cup, each of them raised it to their lips.

"Ah... Delicious. Why does alcohol taste so good after a hard day's work?"

Jusla declared after downing her cup in one gulp, and the others nodded in agreement.

Then they immediately reached for the Aerowings' food.

After finishing her fruit water, Rei reached for the skewered meat first.

The stewed and stir-fried dishes looked delicious too, but he decided that if he wanted to savor the meat simply, the skewered meat was the best choice.

Specifically, not the kind drenched in rich sauce, but the simple kind seasoned only with salt.

With each bite came a crisp texture, followed by a juicy burst of meat juices filling his mouth and the satisfying chewiness of the meat. The skewering also imparted a savory aroma, making it a flavor he could eat endlessly.

"Damn, this is good."

The words murmured from Rei's mouth conveyed his genuine feeling.

Hearing those unadorned words, Elena gently reached for the skewered meat.

The salt seasoning perfectly highlighted the natural savory flavor of the Aerowing meat.

Its simplicity meant there was no hiding its deliciousness, making it only natural that Rei was satisfied.

"Grrr..."

Set had his share of meat too, but considering his size, it was still too little.

It was tasty, but Set's throat rumbled, wishing he could eat more.

His gaze drifted toward the sky, now turning dusk—perhaps he was considering hunting down a few more Aero Wings himself.

And so, the lively meal of delicious meat came to an end. Jusla left, saying she had business to attend to, and after Ara placed the after-dinner tea she'd brewed in front of everyone, Marina spoke up.

"Now then, first, I have some good news. That pulsation that caused this whole incident? It seems it wasn't a bad thing for the World Tree after all."

The sudden, astonishing statement drew the gaze of everyone present toward Marina.

Even Set, who had been resting and lying down a short distance away, and Yellow, who had been perched on Set's back, turned toward Marina.

Amidst this, Vihera was the first to speak.

"Is that true?"

"Yes. After we parted ways in the square earlier, I went to take a look at the World Tree. And I saw that the World Tree had regained a tiny bit of its vitality. It's so slight that I don't think anyone but me could notice it."

"...So what? You're saying that Rei being confined and Marina being blamed by that Ragdo guy meant absolutely nothing? Is that it?"

Elena murmured, her brow slightly furrowed, radiating an atmosphere as displeased as her words.

Of course she was. Even if they didn't know it was for the World Tree's sake, they had publicly berated Rei and Marina relentlessly.

If asked whether it was forgivable, it was absolutely not something she could forgive.

But... Marina smiled at Elena and spoke.

"I'm glad you're angry for me... no, for Rei and me. But Ragdo, despite how he seems, still cares deeply about this village. It's just that his methods are a bit problematic."

"If we don't figure that out, won't he keep interfering whenever we try to do something?"

At Vihera's words, Elena, Ara, and Byune nodded in agreement.

Only Rei remained silent, perhaps because his time under house arrest had been relatively comfortable, showing no sign of blame.

"It'll be fine. I plan to have the World Tree channel magic through me tomorrow, and I intend to ask Ragdo to be present for that."

"Huh!?"

Marina's unexpected words seemed to catch everyone off guard. Ara gasped, her eyes widening.

But glancing around, she realized she was the only one surprised. She closed her mouth, looking slightly embarrassed.

(Did Byune understand what Marina was thinking too?)

Could it be that I'm less perceptive than Byune? The thought suddenly crossed Ara's mind. But Byune seemed completely unfazed by Marina's words, busily peeling the fruit prepared as a tea snack.

Ignoring Ara, who breathed a sigh of relief at this sight, Vihera opened her mouth as if she understood.

"I see. So that's why you went to check on the World Tree."

"That wasn't the only reason. I wanted to see for myself if there really was nothing wrong with that pulsation."

"Even so, dragging out that man Ragdo was part of the plan from the start, wasn't it?"

Continuing Vihera's words, Elena turned her gaze toward Marina.

All three possessed beauty worthy of being called among the most stunning in a nation... no, even in this entire Elgin. Seeing them silently confirm each other's intentions with their eyes felt strangely unsettling.

(I bet there are plenty who'd trade places with me right now.)

Taking a sip of tea, washing away the lingering sweetness of fruit with a faint bitterness, Rei thought.

Those who coveted his position—men who could easily part with a few gold coins for it... or women, for that matter.

As Rei pondered this, the silent exchange among the three seemed to conclude. All three nodded, smiles spreading across their faces.

"So Ragdo becomes the convenient fool, then."

Elena spoke with a spiteful smile, but neither Vihera nor Marina moved to deny it.

Calling him a clown was perhaps a bit harsh, but since Ragdo would be forced to watch Rei heal the World Tree right before his eyes, despite his strong doubts, they decided it was fair enough to label him as such.

"Well, anyway, I just need to channel magic into the World Tree tomorrow, right?"

"Yes. If you do that, I'm certain the World Tree will recover."

"...Right. So, does that mean you'll be leaving this settlement tomorrow?"

"No, I'd like to observe things for a few days if possible. Besides, there's also the matter of the strange phenomenon spreading through the forest because of the World Tree's disturbance, right?"

"Come to think of it, you did say you wanted that resolved too. But if the World Tree's illness is cured, won't that problem disappear as well?"

It was only natural for Vihera to think that if the forest's disturbance stemmed from the World Tree's weakness, curing the World Tree would resolve it.

But Marina shook her head at Vihera's words.

"If enough time passes after the World Tree's treatment, the disturbances might resolve themselves. But it will undoubtedly take a very long time. We're talking years, decades, even longer. If we can resolve it here before that happens, we should."

Marina's voice held a sincere tone.

She needed help because resolving the forest's anomaly on her own would take too long.

Faced with that gaze, Elena, Vihera, and even Rei each found it difficult to refuse.

If this had been someone issuing orders arrogantly, they would have refused outright.

With tomorrow's plans roughly settled, the conversation naturally turned to something else...

Elena and Vihera, who had been drinking tea, each placed their cups on the table.

The table had originally been inside the house, but bringing it outside was easy for Rei, who could use the Misty Ring.

...The reason he used Misty Ring was because when he first tried to carry it outside, he realized the table was larger than the doorway.

Rei had wondered how the table had gotten inside the house in the first place, but figured, well, she was a Dark Elf, so she'd probably used magic to manage it. He didn't seem particularly bothered by it and simply stored it away in Misty Ring.

Anyway, after finishing their meal at the table brought out that way, they were now enjoying their after-dinner tea...

"Marina. Let me ask again—what was the meaning behind what you did to Rei today?"

"Well, I suppose we need to make sure that's properly addressed."

Serious gazes were directed at her from Elena and Vihera.

Even Ara and Byune were looking at Marina with interest.

Marina understood perfectly well what this meant.

After all, purely in terms of experience as a woman, Marina had literally lived through more years than Elena or Vihera.

Naturally, she had also learned deeply about the subtleties between men and women.

...But precisely because of that, Marina shook her head at the two of them.

"To prevent Rei from being treated unfairly. That's my only intention."

That was her explanation.
