Rei, Vihera, Pamidor, Azimov, and Byune arrived at a tavern tucked away in a corner of Gilm.

It was already evening, and people throughout the town—adventurers included—were relaxing after finishing their work for the day.

Amidst this, the five sat at a table toward the back of the tavern.

As for why Byune, who had no connection to this matter whatsoever, was with them, it was likely because Rei and Vihera were the only familiar faces she knew.

Byune preferred being with Rei and Vihera rather than with unfamiliar adults, so she had tagged along with the four.

Rei and Vihera understood Byune's skill as a thief and harbored the thought that they might need to ask her for something during this mission.

They figured it would be better to explain everything together now than to have to explain the situation again later.

Another reason they didn't need to go to the guild was that the Cyclops extermination job wasn't originally accepted by Rei and the others, and they hadn't been specifically requested to provide reinforcements either.

...Though for Mireine and Johanna, who were now staring at Set outside the tavern with a look of utter regret as he waited for his food to arrive, it might have been nothing short of a tragedy.

"Is this girl really such a skilled thief? She can't be much older than Kumito, right?"

While they waited for their drinks and food to arrive, they couldn't engage in deep conversation, so they each made light small talk.

Amidst this, the words that came out of Pamidor's mouth after hearing Byune's explanation were those.

"Hmm."

However, having grown accustomed to being underestimated due to her age, Byune didn't get particularly angry, merely muttering a single word.

...Being underestimated based on age or appearance was something Rei also experienced often, but even compared to Rei, this was a mature response.

"Ever heard of a place called Exil?"

"Ah, I know it. It's a labyrinth city. Pretty good materials come out of there, so the alchemy level is high too."

Azimov answered immediately upon hearing Vihera's words.

As expected of a skilled blacksmith like Pamidor, he possessed knowledge about labyrinth cities.

"One of several labyrinth cities in this country. Why?"

"Before meeting me, this child was soloing the dungeons in that labyrinth city. Though ultimately, they ended up clearing it with me in a party."

"...This child, you say?"

Pamidor shot her a somewhat suspicious look, but considering Byune's appearance, it wasn't surprising at all—it was actually quite natural.

After all, a child around ten years old had been tackling dungeons solo.

But Vihera nodded without hesitation at Pamidor's words.

"Yes, of course. I vouch for his skills."

"...Guarantee his skills is all well and good. But who are you? Seeing you with Rei, you must have some kind of story too."

"Hey, hold on. Why does just being with me mean I have a complicated past?"

Rei's words seemed to say he was offended, but Pamidor just gave him a look that said, "What's so surprising about that?"

"You're with him, so of course there's something going on."

"Did you say that? You said it outright?"

He retorted, but given that Vihera was indeed a complicated person, his words held little persuasive power.

Rei considered retorting something else, but seeing the waitress's near-miraculous skill—carrying multiple glasses in her hands while balancing several plates of food on her wrists and arms—made him fall silent.

"Here you go, sorry to keep you waiting. Two ales, one wine, two fruit waters. Plus stir-fried oak meat, braised jalm, and a few other things."

Rei's eyes turned to the dishes placed on the table.

He gave the stewed Jalum dish a particularly interested look.

The Jalm was the second monster Rei had encountered since arriving in Elgin, resembling a flying squirrel.

This dish involved plucking the Jalm's fur, removing its innards, preparing it for easy eating, parboiling it in plenty of water, and then simmering it in sauce with vegetables.

Removing the Jalm's bones during preparation allowed the entire creature to be eaten whole.

"Enjoy your meal."

The waitress smiled at Rei, who promptly reached for the Jalm stew, and then left.

After a few minutes of trivial chatter, confirming no one was paying attention to his story, Vihera spoke up.

Incidentally, the reason Vihera wasn't drawing attention despite being present was likely because nearly everyone in the tavern was either a resident of Gilm or knew a great deal about it, and the griffin outside the tavern had already noticed Rei's presence.

...It was undoubtedly also related to the presence of Pamidor, whose vicious face made him look like nothing less than the leader of a band of thieves.

Even if someone was interested in Vihera, no one among the tavern patrons was reckless enough to mess with a group that included those two.

If there had been, someone would have stopped them.

"...Well, shall we get down to business?"

"Nah, first tell me why you're involved in this. When Azimov told Rei about the hammer, we decided it'd be best to talk about it right where you are."

Pamidor's gaze followed Azimov, who was reaching for the skewered meat.

"Come to think of it, I haven't introduced myself yet. I'm Vihera, former Second Princess of the Bestir Empire. Pleased to meet you."

"..."

Pamidor silently shifted her gaze to Rei as Vihera stated her name with a smile and a casual tone.

Rei returned Pamidor's gaze with a nod.

"The world sure is something else..."

"Well, that's how it is. You understand why I thought it best to call Vihera for this matter, right? ...Now then. Azimov."

"Mmm... phew! Why do ale and skewered meat go so well together? Huh? Oh, you mean that hammer was made by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire?"

"That's right. ...But first, how can you be so sure that hammer was made by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire?"

"That's simple. Alchemy, while varying slightly by individual, inevitably reflects the influence of what one has learned up to that point. And since the alchemy of the Bestir Empire has developed so rapidly in recent years, its characteristics are particularly pronounced. Anyone can tell just by looking at magic items made by an Ozos alchemist and a Bestir alchemist."

"...Anyone? But the guild staff couldn't tell when I showed them theirs?"

Rei asked, suddenly curious at Azimov's words, but the man himself immediately shook his head.

"Correction. Not anyone, but any alchemist with a certain level of skill."

"That's not really 'anyone' anymore. What level of skill are you using as the standard?"

Sighing at Azimov's words, which seemed to use himself as the standard, Rei spoke again.

"So, that hammer showed the characteristics of an alchemist from the Bestir Empire, right?"

"Yeah. If you'd like, I could explain those characteristics—or rather, alchemy in detail—but you're not interested, are you?"

"It's not that I'm not interested, but... well, I suppose we don't have time for that now."

Hearing that alchemists from the Bestir Empire were involved, Vihera's gaze sharpened slightly, momentarily overwhelming Azimov.

While Azimov fundamentally believed alchemy was everything and cared little for others' status, there seemed to be something in Vihera's gaze.

"So, you're absolutely sure there's no mistake? If it becomes a problem later and you say, 'Actually, the Bestir Empire wasn't involved after all,' that won't be funny at all, you know?"

"That's absolutely correct. Ask any other alchemist, and they'd likely say the same. ...But what I'm trying to say is, this is merely the work of an alchemist from the Bestir Empire... or more precisely, someone who studied alchemy in the Bestir Empire. If you ask me whether they're currently affiliated with the Bestir Empire, I can't confidently say yes."

"Ah, I see. So even if someone becomes an alchemist in the Bestir Empire, they don't necessarily have to serve the empire now?"

At the words that came from Rei's mouth, Vihera let out a faint sigh of relief.

Even though he had fled the Bestir Empire, he hadn't lost his affection for his homeland.

Especially since the next emperor was Mercurio, who held deep affection for Vihera.

But what escaped Pamidor's lips was a cruel truth for Vihera.

"But that hammer was made recently. It couldn't be more than a year old... no, it wouldn't be surprising if it was only a month or two old."

Given that he had been introduced as a skilled blacksmith, Pamidor's words weighed heavily on Vihera's heart.

"...Right. If that's the case, things might get a little interesting."

As Vihera murmured, Rei opened his mouth in agreement.

"When I heard that hammer was made by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire, I remembered Rodos, who fought during the civil war. Come to think of it, there are many similarities—like granting powerful regenerative abilities or stripping away will."

"So, the alchemist who served my brother is here? As for why... well, I suppose that goes without saying."

"Exactly. Not to brag, but my exploits during that civil war were pretty remarkable."

"...Civil war? You mean the one that happened last year in the Bestir Empire? But you fled, didn't you? So you were involved in the civil war?"

Pamidor, who had obviously heard about last year's civil war, asked with a menacing expression and wide eyes.

"Well, things happened. Anyway, the First Prince, who became our enemy in the civil war, acquired an alchemist. Given how things unfolded, I suspect that alchemist is involved. What do you think?"

Rei asked Azimov, but the man he questioned only wore a troubled expression.

"Asking me about that won't help. That hammer was definitely made by an alchemist from the Bestir Empire. But if you're asking if it's in the First Prince's hands, there's no way I'd know."

"...I suppose you're right."

Hearing this while drinking ale, Rei couldn't help but agree.

While it was clear the hammer came from an alchemist of the Bestir Empire, not all of them had joined the civil war on the side of the First Prince.

It was possible that an alchemist who hadn't joined the civil war had come to the Kingdom of Mireana for some reason... But the moment that thought crossed his mind, Rei immediately shook his head.

"That's impossible. An alchemist from the Bestir Empire just happens to come near Gilm, just happens to create a magic item that only a huge monster like a cyclops could use, and just happens to have it acquired by a red cyclops... How many coincidences would have to pile up for something like this to happen?"

They say coincidence becomes necessity when it happens three times in a row, but to Rei, this incident clearly felt like it had been deliberately orchestrated.

"This is bad,"

The one who muttered that was Vihera.

Naturally, the word "bad" that just came out of her mouth referred not to the food, but to the current situation.

At least for Rei, none of the dishes on the table could be called bad; if anything, they were quite tasty.

Of course, being a pub, it wasn't top-tier cuisine, but it certainly wasn't the kind of taste you'd call bad.

"An alchemist?"

Rei asked, just to be sure. Vihera nodded as she brought a plate of steamed vegetables tossed in nut dressing to her mouth.

"Yes. As you probably know, Rei, relations between the nobles and neutrals of Mercurio and the Kingdom of Mireana are good following last year's civil war. ...Well, it could change drastically depending on my father's whims, but for some reason, he seems to be letting Mercurio do as it pleases for now. But if this incident truly involves an alchemist from the Bestir Empire..."

Vihera paused there, but for those listening, the rest was not difficult to imagine.

An enemy that had been hostile for years committing an act that could only be seen as malicious toward a nation currently in a friendly relationship.

The result would undoubtedly have a detrimental effect on the relationship between the Kingdom of Mireana and the Bestir Empire.

"I suppose we should speak with Lord Daskar about this. ...Even though it's only been a day since yesterday."

Muttering, Vihera rose from her chair...

「Rei？」

Vihera turned her gaze to Rei, who had stood up following her for some reason.

"Hmm?"

Following Rei, Byune also stood up.

"Well, Vihera has helped us out in many ways. Besides, more combat power is better, right?"

"Mm."

Byune nodded in agreement with Rei's words.

Seeing the two of them like that, Vihera looked surprised for a moment, but quickly brought a smile to her lips.

"You fools."

Though she said it, Vihera's joy was plain to see.
