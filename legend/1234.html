The Silver Lion's disassembly went more smoothly than expected.

Grim had cast a spell to sharpen the knives for the task, but the sheer number of people involved made a far greater difference.

Even though the Silver Lion was about six meters long, with this many people... especially with Min, who was experienced in butchering high-rank monster materials like the Elk and possessed extensive knowledge in butchering, and above all, with Grimm offering crucial advice at key moments, it went remarkably well.

(I should probably throw a barbecue party for this group later, using the Silver Lion's meat.)

Most said they didn't need any materials, and even Elena and the others who did want materials only took a small portion. As a result, nearly the entire silver lion went to Rei.

If he had taken down the Silver Lion alone, it wouldn't have been a problem at all. But defeating it required the combined strength of the many people present.

Given that, taking almost the entire lion felt deeply unfair to Rei.

Therefore, since it was such a rare opportunity, it seemed only right to at least treat everyone to the exquisite meat of the Silver Lion.

That was Rei's honest feeling.

"Hmm, well... what remains is the dungeon's core, I suppose."

As the dissection of the Silver Lion concluded and Rei distributed the fruit water he'd retrieved from his Misty Ring to everyone, Grimm's voice echoed around them.

...Naturally, as an undead being, Grimm couldn't drink the fruit water.

Seeing Grimm, who could even defeat a Silver Lion, watching Rei and the others drink the fruit water with a hint of envy was a somewhat surprising sight for Rei.

Anyway, as the Silver Lion's disassembly reached a pause, the room connected to this one where the fierce battle with the Silver Lion had unfolded was where Grimm's gaze was fixed.

Not the passageway Rei and the others had come through, but another connected room.

What lay there, of course, was known to everyone present.

The very thing that made a dungeon a dungeon... the room containing the dungeon's core.

"But... um, are you sure? I know it's not my place to say, but if we destroy the dungeon's core here, this place will obviously cease to be a dungeon."

Rei said this because he knew one of Grimm's laboratories was located within this dungeon.

If this ceased to be a dungeon, all sorts of problems would likely arise in Grimm's laboratory too.

Understanding that this was what Rei meant, Grimm let out a laugh that echoed around them.

It was a laugh that sounded nothing like something an undead creature would produce, a laugh that was somehow reassuring.

It was the kind of laughter one might offer to a close relative.

"Ho ho ho. It doesn't matter. My laboratory isn't only in this dungeon. I have several others, and if push comes to shove, I wouldn't be particularly troubled even if this dungeon were to disappear. You know that, don't you? My laboratory is in this dungeon, yet it is not of this dungeon."

Only three people could grasp the meaning of those words: Rei, Elena, and Ara. ...Set might have understood too.

In any case, Grimm's laboratory existed within this dungeon... or more accurately, in a place that could be called the reverse side of this dungeon, or perhaps a slightly overlapping world.

The fact that Rei and the others had managed to enter that laboratory before was almost entirely by accident.

Recalling that, Rei and the others exchanged expressions of understanding.

"Ah... I see."

Elk and the others tilted their heads, unsure why Rei understood, but they judged it best not to ask about it now... or rather, that it was better not to pry carelessly. They kept their mouths shut.

Grim might be putting on the airs of a kindly old man now, but his true nature remained that of a lich... no, a powerful undead worthy of being called a Lich Lord.

What would happen if they did something to upset such an entity?

The answer was plain to see in the corpse of the Silver Lion, whose formidable defenses had been utterly meaningless when its belly was so easily ripped open.

"So, you needn't worry about that. If you require it, destroy the dungeon's core. If not, leave it be."

If you don't destroy the dungeon's core here, eventually another monster will be summoned here—one that could be called a boss monster, or even a master.

Whether it would be another Silver Lion or a completely different monster... or even whether it would be stronger or weaker than the Silver Lion... none of that is certain.

(Even if it's not quite Rank S, if it consistently summons Rank A monsters, it'd be an unbeatable money-making spot... But surely, such a thing is impossible, right?)

Even defeating the Silver Lion required this much firepower.

If an even more powerful monster were to guard the dungeon's core, dealing with it would be anything but easy.

Considering the possibility that it could eventually become unmanageable, destroying the dungeon's core here was undoubtedly the best course of action.

Moreover, Rei had a reason he couldn't overlook this situation.

Previously, when Rei severed a dungeon core with the Death Scythe, the skill had been acquired.

And not just any skill—it had learned skills from both cores it had severed, despite the rule that skills couldn't be learned from the same monster's magic stone.

One core wasn't a complete dungeon core, so perhaps it was a different type of monster... or rather, it might have been recognized as a dungeon core.

To verify that possibility, Rei absolutely wanted to destroy the dungeon core here with Death Scythe.

Of course, if this ceased to be a dungeon, it would affect more than just Grimm.

The most severely affected would naturally be the numerous buildings constructed around this dungeon.

Already more like a town than a village, its scale was such that the shops built here were naturally based on the assumption that the dungeon existed, as were the people who had gathered.

If this dungeon were to vanish, there would be no further development here.

Well, if you considered it a frontier outpost, it wouldn't lose all value, but with Gilm present, it would definitely hit a ceiling.

To begin with, there were no protective barriers of any kind. Even now, monsters, wild animals, and occasionally even bandits would sometimes climb over the fences and enter.

It was simply impossible for this place to develop beyond what Gilm offered.

In other words, if Rei destroyed the dungeon's core, those who earned their livelihood here... and even the merchants who came seeking dungeon-sourced materials would suffer.

(But it's also true that if we don't destroy the dungeon's core here, we won't get another chance.)

Developing this place like the labyrinth city Rei once created would be difficult in this remote frontier.

If he gave in to momentary mercy here and didn't destroy the dungeon's core... and as a result, that became the cause of the dungeon spiraling out of anyone's control, Rei would be left with nothing but regret.

"Are you sure?"

Rei turned his gaze to Marina, asking as if for final confirmation.

This dungeon falls under Gilm's jurisdiction, and naturally, Guildmaster Marina is the ultimate authority.

"Yes. I've already informed Daskar about this matter. From the moment I heard the Silver Lion had been defeated, I knew the dungeon would vanish. After all, since we've already defeated the Silver Lion, even if we don't touch the dungeon's core here..."

The rest of her words didn't need to be spoken for them to understand.

If Rei and the others left this place without doing anything to the dungeon's core, there was a risk that whoever came here next might tamper with it.

And if the person who tampered with the core came forward, that would be one thing. But what if they vanished without a word?

Considering that, it was indeed better to make it clear who had destroyed the dungeon's core.

Furthermore, while the disappearance of the dungeon would certainly cause various problems for those gathered here, the dungeon wouldn't vanish immediately just because its core was gone.

It would take time, gradually ceasing to function as a dungeon.

In other words, even if Rei destroyed the dungeon's core, it wouldn't immediately cease to be usable as a dungeon.

For a while... Marina couldn't say exactly how long, as it depended on the dungeon's size, the core, and various other factors. Still, the fact that there would be a certain period of time wasn't necessarily a bad thing for those in the surrounding areas.

"Understood."

After deliberating... the choice Rei ultimately made was to destroy the dungeon's core.

Some had their thoughts about Rei's attitude, but none voiced dissent.

They understood who had contributed the most to defeating the Silver Lion.

"Decided? Then it's best to act quickly."

Rising slightly into the air, Grimm led the way forward, with Rei and the others following behind.

Only Set and Yellow remained, keeping watch outside the room where the battle with the Silver Lion had taken place, waiting for someone else to arrive.

That said, the entrance to the room offered a clear view into the chamber housing the dungeon's core, so there was no risk of Set losing sight of Rei.

The room they followed Grimm into contained the dungeon's core.

"This is... the dungeon's core..."

Who murmured that?

Naturally, few present had ever seen a dungeon core before.

Even to Rei's eyes, one of the few exceptions, the dungeon core here was different from any he had seen before.

The dungeon cores Rei had seen before were either cores from before the dungeon was created or cores from dungeons that had only just come into being.

Compared to those, this one was clearly different in size, color, luster, and above all, the very atmosphere emanating from the core itself.

『Rei』

While everyone's eyes were fixed on the dungeon core, Grimm's voice echoed once more.

Hearing it, Rei gave a slight nod... took hold of the Death Scythe, and moved forward to stand before the dungeon core.

Understanding that everyone's gaze was fixed on her, she readied the Death Scythe.

(If I can't learn the skill with this, it'll be the worst.)

Thinking this inwardly, Rei swings the Death Scythe without changing his expression.

A sharp strike that seemed to cleave not just the air itself... no, the very fabric of space itself.

The blade of the Death Scythe passed through... and soon, the dungeon's core was severed diagonally, its upper portion sliding smoothly to the ground.

[Death Scythe has acquired the skill 'Terrain Manipulation Lv. 3']

Simultaneously, an announcement message echoed in his mind.

It was the signal that the dungeon core had granted a skill.

(Yes!)

Skill acquisition through a magic stone—this time, the dungeon's core.

Having succeeded, Rei silently cheered inside.

To be honest, she wanted to shout her joy aloud.

The terrain manipulation skill could only be leveled up using the dungeon's core for now.

In other words, if the level increases, it might even become possible to manipulate terrain to the point of creating a dungeon.

The current situation is incredibly inefficient, but he understood from several examples, starting with the Flying Slash, that reaching skill level five would dramatically enhance the skill.

If that's the case, terrain manipulation might eventually reach level five too, and who knows? Maybe I could do what Rei imagines.

(Well, to do that, I'd probably need to destroy at least two more dungeon cores. ...Ideally, I wish I could learn terrain manipulation using monster magic stones.)

As those around him fixed their gaze on the dungeon's core, Rei was lost in such thoughts when suddenly, Set's cry echoed through the area.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

'Huh!?'

Hearing only Set's warning growl, which was meant to alert them if someone entered the room, they held their breath, wondering if someone had arrived.

Yet, amidst the tension, only Rei remained expressionless.

He understood that Set's growl sounded pleased, meaning it was an expression of joy regarding the level-up in terrain manipulation.

But those unaware wore tense expressions.

"Relax. Set's cry just now was one of joy. It wasn't to warn of danger from someone coming."

Hearing Rei's words brought relief, likely because they knew the strength of the bond between Rei and Set.

Everything they needed to do here was finished... All that remained was to return to the surface.
