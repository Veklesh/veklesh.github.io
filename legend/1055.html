Several dark elves rushed into the garden Elena and the others were borrowing.

However, these dark elves were only about as tall as Byune and were clearly still children.

"Marina-nee! It's bad! Lugdos and the others aren't coming back!"

"I told them not to go! But they insisted they'd be fine..."

"Please, Marina-nee. Help Lugdos and the others!"

Because each was shouting their own concerns, it was hard to grasp the full story immediately.

But... even so, he grasped that several children had gone somewhere and hadn't returned.

Watching these dark elf children, Rei suddenly felt a pang of doubt.

(Marina left this settlement nearly a century ago. ...If that's the case, why are these kids so attached to her? Surely, a hundred years ago, these kids weren't even born yet... right?)

Considering the lifespan of dark elves, it was possible they had actually been born then.

That's what Rei thought, but the truth was that Marina had actually met them today while researching the World Tree within the settlement.

Among the dark elf children, there were troublemakers and a self-proclaimed leader. These kids had picked on Marina, who was treated as an important figure in the settlement... but of course, Marina wasn't going to just take it lying down. In the end, the little leaders got thoroughly beaten.

"Then, Rugdos and the others said they'd show them what they were made of, so they left the settlement and went into the forest!"

"Wh-what...?"

Marina's face twisted at those words.

Of course she would. Normally, there were hardly any monsters between the Barrier of Confusion and the Barrier of Protection, so it was relatively safe to let them leave the settlement.

But... now, because the World Tree had recovered the previous day, many monsters were effectively trapped between the Barrier of Confusion and the Barrier of Protection.

And there was a high possibility goblins had infiltrated in large numbers.

What would happen if several Dark Elf children, lacking proper combat skills, were sent into such a place... It wasn't hard to imagine.

"Damn it!"

Marina raised her gaze to the sky.

The sunset was already sinking, and night would fall before long.

And once night fell, the monsters would become even more active. Even for dark elves, it would ultimately become a situation beyond what children could handle.

Marina hesitated for a moment, but quickly turned to Rei and the others and spoke.

"Rei, Elena, Vihera , Ara , Byune, Earth... Could you lend me your strength?"

Originally, the request Rei and the others had accepted was to heal the World Tree and resolve the forest's abnormalities.

They had absolutely no obligation to save the dark elf child here, and choosing not to would not have brought them any blame whatsoever.

But...even so, this was Marina's homeland for Rei and the others. If Marina was in trouble, it was only natural they would want to save her.

Unlike Rei and the others, Earth's reasoning was simpler.

He was aiming to be a hero, so abandoning a child was simply not an option. More than that, Earth had once been the leader of the kids in the village where he grew up.

For that very reason, abandoning the bully who went into the forest was never an option for him, and he understood that bully's feelings.

Rei was the first to speak up.

"Alright, I'm in."

Hearing Rei's words, everyone else agreed to go into the forest.

"...Thank you. Then, let's go. Hey, you guys. Did you tell the other adults about this?"

At Marina's questioning words, the children all shook their heads in unison.

Perhaps they thought they'd be scolded if they did, or maybe their impression of Marina when they met her was just that strong.

In any case, learning that the other adults still didn't know about this, Marina addressed the children.

"Understand? Go immediately to Grandfather... the Elder, and tell him exactly what you just told me. He should be able to mobilize the others right away."

"But..."

One of the children opened their mouth timidly.

"If this gets out, we'll get in trouble..."

"It's already blown up this big. There's no way we can keep it hidden now. So wouldn't it be better to tell them quickly and go look for the missing children? If we're going to get scolded, it's because we did something wrong. It can't be helped. ...Or are you going to abandon the missing children just because you don't want to get scolded?"

Marina asked with a smile that seemed playful... yet her eyes never crinkled in amusement. The children shook their heads vigorously.

They absolutely did not want to do anything that would abandon Lugdos, who was also their leader.

Sure, he was a troublemaking ringleader who caused all sorts of problems like this time, but he was also genuinely caring and well-liked by those under him.

Seeing the children's expressions, Marina's face broke into a full smile, replacing the one without warmth from just seconds before.

"Alright, then go. We'll head into the forest to look for Lugdos and the others too."

"Uh, yeah! Got it!"

Shouting that, the children hurriedly ran off.

Watching them go, Marina spoke.

"Alright, everyone, I'll need your help. I'll get ready quickly too, so let's meet at the village entrance."

At Marina's words, everyone sprang into action.

Some headed to the house Elena was renting, Marina went to her own home, and Earth went with Polo to the house Rei was renting.

Amidst this, only Rei remained behind, seemingly without any particular task.

For Rei, who used Misty Ring, preparation was fundamentally unnecessary.

Of course, purchasing consumables was necessary, but fortunately, there was nothing currently lacking.

With that settled, Rei headed straight for the entrance to the settlement.

"Grrrr..."

Beside Rei, Set growled.

(Well, I suppose it's more accurate to say he's brimming with motivation.)

Just like in Gilm, Set was basically lenient with children.

Of course, it wasn't unconditional leniency; he would retaliate lightly against those who played pranks on him. Still, overall, it was undeniable that he was lenient towards children.

That's precisely why the fact that a child had gone into the forest... and into a forest believed to be overrun with goblins, and hadn't returned... must have stirred up all sorts of thoughts within him.

As he walked with Set toward the entrance of the Dark Elf settlement, Rei's gaze drifted to the wooden fence surrounding the village.

This settlement was protected by the barrier of the World Tree's shield, meaning there were no walls like those found in Gilm.

The wooden fence served only as a token barrier, which was likely why the bully kids like Rugdos could so easily slip outside.

(Normally... when the World Tree was healthy, it wouldn't have been a problem.)

But now, with the World Tree's problems, the forest had become dangerous.

Adults would naturally have warned the children about this, but children often take pleasure in breaking the rules adults set for them.

This tendency is especially strong among the ringleaders.

"But that doesn't mean... it's okay to just let kids die, you know."

"Gluu!"

In Rei's muttered thoughts, the fact that the opponent was a child didn't change the fact they were a Dark Elf... meaning the possibility that they were actually older than Rei was completely gone.

Regardless of their actual age, since they looked like children, treating them as such was only natural.

It was almost like a defense mechanism, a way to avoid thinking about Marina's true age.

In any case, Rei and Set arrived at the meeting place.

And seeing several dark elves already gathered there, they wore expressions of surprise.

Well, if they were just gathered, it wouldn't have been particularly surprising. But were they gathered here for some reason? If someone said it was for a banquet, he might have been somewhat convinced.

But... the Dark Elves gathered here, almost all of them holding bows in their hands, certainly didn't look like they were about to have a banquet.

(Besides, the World Tree hasn't fully recovered yet. There's no way they'd be having a feast. So many were shocked by what happened.)

So why were so many Dark Elves here? Rei's question was answered the moment he overheard their conversation.

"Listen up. If you find a goblin, the first thing you do is check if any Lugdos are nearby."

"But we're talking about a boy, not a girl! And he's a child. Do you really think goblins would find a dark elf like that and just leave him alone?"

"Even so. It's not impossible, right?"

"...I hope he's still alive..."

The worried words spoken were expressions of concern for the children, starting with Lugdos.

Meaning they were likely gathered here, just like Rei, to search for Lugdos and the others.

(They're surprisingly early.)

After Marina left the house, Rei should have left immediately.

But when Rei arrived, a large group of Dark Elves were already gathered there.

"...Huh? Are you here to help too?"

One of the dark elves, seeing Rei, called out to him.

With no intention of hiding anything, Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah. Marina asked me to. ...But seriously, I only heard about this a moment ago. You guys got here pretty fast."

"A child from the settlement is missing. Of course we're gathering."

The one talking to Rei was a dark elf who looked about twenty years old.

With a quiver on his back and a bow in hand, his appearance perfectly matched Rei's image of a dark elf.

"Should I say you're quite the team player?"

"Is that so? I think it's normal. ...Well, having someone like you help out is nice for us too, though."

The dark elf man, who had heard from acquaintances capable of sensing magic about the extent of Rei's magical power, smiled happily upon hearing Rei would cooperate.

What remained was no longer fear or dread toward Rei.

His decision to head into the forest to save the dark elf child was certainly a factor.

Also, the fact that this man himself lacked the ability to sense magic played a significant role.

In fact, several people who had sensed Rei's magic at the training grounds had quietly averted their gaze the moment they saw her.

Still, what they felt wasn't fear but awe... Both meant he was feared, but the absence of malice likely stemmed from his proven track record of healing the World Tree.

"Gururuu"

"Whoa!"

Perhaps feeling lonely since Rei had only been talking to the Dark Elf man, Set appeared, growling softly.

Seeing Set like this, the dark elf man wore a look of surprise—though it was surprisingly mild for someone unaccustomed to Set.

"...You don't seem very surprised."

"Well, of course. He's with you, the one who healed the World Tree. It's easy to imagine he wouldn't just rampage pointlessly."

The dark elf man speaking with Rei said this, but among the other dark elves, many were clearly trying to keep their distance from Set.

Seeing this, it was clear not all Dark Elves accepted Set like the man did.

"Hey! Get off me! Get off!"

"I told you to wait! It's almost nightfall! What do you think you're doing, going into the forest alone?! It's better for us all to work together than for everyone to split up!"

Hearing those voices suddenly, Rei turned his gaze in that direction.

There, several dark elves were trying to stop one from heading into the forest.

No, upon closer look, several others were also trying to head into the forest and being stopped.

"What's that?"

"...Ah. That's Lugdos's brother. The others are probably siblings or relatives of the ones who went into the forest with Lugdos."

"I see," Rei nodded.

If told that their own relatives were in the forest now... the forest between the Barrier of Confusion and the Barrier of Obstruction, it was only natural they'd be that frantic.

"But if everyone just acts on their own whims, it'll only waste time finding Lugdos and the others. Even knowing that... they're just that desperate, I guess."

Just as Rei was about to nod in agreement with the man's muttered words,

"Everyone, pay attention here. I'm about to assign each of you a specific area to search. Please follow those instructions. As you know, there are many monsters in the forest right now, so under no circumstances should you act alone."

Marina's voice echoed throughout the area.
