The day after using the Paired Orbs to speak with Daskar, Rei and the others received a visit from a certain individual.

They had just finished breakfast in the cafeteria and were about to check on Mejogo's status, planning to attack if everything looked okay.

Since Mejogo was most active at night, the current morning hours were undoubtedly like the middle of the night for those working regular jobs.

That's precisely why Rei anticipated their resistance forces would be weaker than if they attacked at night.

Of course, just because it was that time of day didn't mean the defensive forces were non-existent.

If this hour was close to midnight for Mejogo's inhabitants, it was only natural they'd be on guard.

There had been talk of cooperating somewhat with the Resistance, but since Daskar had told them last night to do as they pleased, they didn't intend to prioritize that.

However, if Rei and his group attacked Mejogo, it would undoubtedly cause a major disturbance.

Marina also voiced the opinion that, in terms of minimizing casualties from such a disturbance, the cooperation of the larger Resistance force was essential.

Indeed, Rei and the others possess combat prowess worthy of being called one-man armies, capable of taking on thousands. But in terms of numbers, they are undeniably few.

Given that, if an incident occurred in a city as vast as Mejogo, could they really manage to prevent accidents? The answer would be no.

Marina's spirit magic possesses power that could be called all-purpose, but even so, it cannot make the impossible possible.

Perhaps the Resistance could handle that aspect.

It was during this conversation that a visitor arrived.

"Thank you for yesterday, Rei. I never imagined you were an adventurer with a nickname... It took me a while to find you."

The person who said this, bowing with a friendly smile, looked familiar to Rei.

The man who had been attacked by thieves yesterday and whom he had rescued... Ryutas was standing there.

"Ryutas, huh. ...Still, how did you know I'd be here?"

Rei hadn't revealed his identity when he saved him yesterday. More precisely, he hadn't mentioned he was an adventurer from the Kingdom of Mireana, known by the epithet Crimson.

Judging solely by appearance, the concealment effect of the Dragon Robe made it impossible to connect Rei to Crimson.

And he certainly wasn't accompanied by his partner, Set, who was practically his symbol.

(Was it because I showed him the Misty Ring? But if you asked whether he could tell the Misty Ring was a genuine item box... that would be difficult.)

Magic items that could be called inferior versions of item boxes were fairly rare, but they did exist.

Seeing the Misty Ring Rei possessed, it should have been impossible to easily determine if it was an Item Box or a degraded version.

For those reasons, Rei simply couldn't understand why Ryutas had found him...

"Well, I am a merchant, after all, so I gather all sorts of information. That made finding you, Rei, not particularly difficult. ...You stand out in all sorts of ways, you see."

"Is that so? Well, I get that I stand out, but I still think it would be hard to find me based on that alone."

Rei's muttered words weren't wrong.

If it were beauties like Elena, Marina, or Vihera, finding them wouldn't be difficult at all.

But in Rei's case, wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, he was hardly noticeable compared to the other three.

Given that the Dragon Robe itself was inconspicuous due to its concealment effect, spotting Rei specifically as Rei should have been quite difficult.

"That's precisely why we have the merchant's information network, though. It seems we managed to surprise them, which is the best outcome."

Rei nodded in understanding at Ryutas' words, delivered with a refreshing smile, thinking that was just how merchants were.

However, only Elena and Marina didn't fully believe Ryutas' words.

A merchant's network undoubtedly surpassed that of even mediocre nobles... nay, even royalty.

But naturally, there was a vast difference between the network of a merchant from the small country of Reburina and that of a major merchant from the Kingdom of Mireana.

That discrepancy likely caused their unease.

"Hmm. Merchants are impressive. ...So, what brings you here today? Could it really be just about yesterday's matter?"

"No, no. Of course, as a merchant myself, I've come seeking a fair profit. ...By the way, Rei. Why is a renowned adventurer like you in such a small country? If there's anything I can assist with, I'd be happy to help."

Lytus asked with a friendly smile, but Rei shook his head.

"No, I came because I had a small request, but that's already taken care of. I've just been busy with various things lately, so I'm taking a little break now. No need to worry about it."

"Is that so? Rei saved my life yesterday. I wanted to repay that debt... My apologies. Ah, but if anything comes up, please come to my shop. I'll help you as much as I can."

Saying this, Ryutas pointed out the name of a shop located a short distance from the main street.

Of course, Rei and the others had only just arrived in Rossi, so they didn't know the exact location Ryutas had described; they only had a general idea of the area.

"I see. I'll stop by if I need anything. Is that alright?"

"Yes. Of course, I'll charge you, but I'll do my best to keep the cost reasonable."

With that, he bowed, stood up, and left Rei's room.

Watching him go, Rei turned his gaze to the others in the room.

"Well, what do you think?"

The muttered words were only one sentence, but the meaning behind that single sentence was profound.

"Hmm, Ryutas, was it? He himself didn't seem particularly trained, though."

Vihera was the first to speak.

She probably didn't find him particularly interesting since he wasn't a strong opponent.

"Yeah. I hear he's been moving around towns and villages, so he's probably more trained than someone living in the city... but that's about it."

Marina murmured in agreement with Vihera's words.

However, Marina's tone carried a hint of doubt.

Noticing this, Elena spoke up with a seductive smile.

"It wasn't that anything seemed suspicious... but there was definitely something that caught my attention."

"Yeah, that's about right. It's just a hunch, though."

Marina nodded in agreement at Elena's words.

Intuition... There are many kinds—a woman's intuition, an adventurer's intuition—but that sense of intuition isn't entirely meaningless.

Anyone who risks their life for a living, adventurers foremost among them, understands this instinctively.

Given that, she couldn't definitively say that Ryutas was a safe person.

Of course, relying solely on intuition to decide everything was impractical in many ways.

But... for Rei and the others now, if Ryutas seemed suspicious, the most likely connection would be...

"Jaya, huh?"

"We don't have proof, but I don't think it's unlikely."

Everyone present nodded at Marina's words.

After all, they had entered Rossi so brazenly. It was expected from the start that Jaya would learn of their existence.

Hadn't Jaya, sensing this, sent Ryutas to observe Rei and the others?

No one objected to Marina's explanation.

"So saving Ryutas when he was attacked by bandits was exactly what they wanted?"

"Well... I'm not so sure. I don't think so. Though, since Rei and Ryutas met because of that incident, it's not impossible that Ryutas was sent because of that."

Rei nodded in understanding at the explanation that Ryutas had been sent precisely because he happened to know Rei.

"Anyway, now that they've recognized us, dragging our feet will only make it harder to move. We really need to take action as soon as possible."

"Hmm. So you still won't cooperate with the Resistance?"

"I'm not sure. If it takes them time to act, then cooperation might be an option. Considering Nocor started moving yesterday, it wouldn't be surprising if they brought an answer soon."

It was only yesterday, but Rei didn't know where the Resistance was operating.

No, given how much emphasis they're placing on the Mejogo incident, it stands to reason their main activity is centered there.

But, being the Resistance, it's difficult for Rei and the others to make contact unless they reach out first.

(Should I talk to Unchu? ...Would that actually be the quickest way to get results?)

For Rei and the others, the only person with connections to the Resistance and who could be contacted immediately was Unchu.

He's an informant, but since he's hostile towards Jaya, the biggest advantage is not having to worry about information leaking.

"I'm heading over to Unchu's place. What about you guys?"

"Well, if you put it that way... I don't really have anything to do here anyway. I'll come with you. What about Elena and the others?"

"I'll come too. I need to report this matter to the family anyway. If it's important, I want to know about it."

"I'll pass. It was late last night, and I'm still a bit sleepy. I'll just sleep in my room."

"Mm."

As Vihera spoke, Byune muttered her usual brief response.

Rei and the others didn't understand the meaning, but Vihera seemed to grasp it perfectly.

She opened her mouth, looking slightly exasperated.

"It seems Byune wants to buy and eat various things at the food stalls. ...Well, it shouldn't be a problem, right?"

Rei nodded in response to Vihera's question, which carried a hint of confirmation.

Byune was a thief, but one specialized in combat prowess.

While opponents as strong as Rei and the others might be another matter, there was confidence that she could handle anyone in this country.

Leaving the inn where Rei and the others were staying, Ryutas continued walking down the road with a smile on his face.

Though he was simply walking, one by one, men began appearing around him.

Even so, Ryutas maintained his calm smile, showing no sign of fear toward the men around him.

Of course not. Those men weren't his enemies; they were his guards.

"So, young master. How did it go?"

One of the guards asked timidly.

For the guards, making enemies of Rei and his companions was an absolutely avoidable situation.

They knew full well that even half... no, even a tenth of the strength rumored to flow from Rei was something they couldn't possibly handle.

They even wondered if it wouldn't be better to pick a fight with the surrounding nations of Reburina rather than antagonize Rei.

...Of course, doing something like that would mean no guarantee of survival. None whatsoever... Yet, it still seemed far preferable to fighting Rei and his group.

But the guard's wish was swiftly dismissed by Ryutas.

"It's only a matter of time. Probably soon... maybe even within days, they might make their move."

At those words, not only the man who had spoken to Ryutas, but the expressions of the other men darkened as well.

However, Ryutas' mistake at this point was likely that he had calculated the time until Rei and the others would act based solely on his own common sense.

For groups like the Jaya, to which Ryutas belonged, gathering supplies, recruiting people, gathering intelligence, and then planning an assault took time.

In comparison, Rei and his group possessed combat strength sufficient to handle an entire town on their own. Being few in number meant they required almost no supplies, and they also had the Misty Ring, a storehouse of resources.

As for strategy, they possessed enough raw power to force their way through most situations, making elaborate planning largely unnecessary.

This difference in perspective likely stems from the contrast between Ryutas, who lived in the small nation of Reburina, and Rei and the others, who lived in the large kingdom of Mireana.

That said, Rei and his group did recognize that collaborating with the Resistance was preferable, as they disliked recklessly attacking and harming innocent parties. It was fortunate that this perspective prevented Ryutas's approach from becoming a fatal mistake.
