That evening, after Rei and Ilse had formed an alliance with Dahl and the others... Ajax waited in his room for his two companions to return.

Outside, the sun had already turned to sunset, and it was nearly time for work to end.

With nothing to do, Ajax couldn't help but feel anxious.

What if Rei stormed into the inn before his two companions... Rebejev and Haston... returned?

Of course, logically speaking, it would be Rei who would be blamed if he did such a thing.

While circumstantial evidence strongly suggested Ajax was responsible for Ilse's family's death, there was no definitive proof.

He had abandoned his work and rushed back to Gilm the moment he heard the news, but he could probably get away with it by insisting he was feeling unwell.

Naturally, such behavior would damage his standing with the guild.

But now that Ilse had appeared, Ajax didn't think he could continue working as an adventurer any longer.

That wouldn't change whether he went to other villages, towns, cities... or even other countries.

Guilds communicate with each other, and through those connections, it's possible to have someone placed on a wanted list as a bounty target.

Having walked many dangerous paths himself, Ajax was well aware of such matters.

"Damn it. Being an adventurer was a pretty sweet gig."

He mutters grumpily while lying in bed.

In truth, adventuring had been the most lucrative job imaginable for Ajax.

Of course, that "lucrative" didn't mean lucrative in the typical adventurer sense. For Ajax, who operated as a sort of thief, it meant lucrative in the sense of being an invaluable source of information.

If he learned of a bounty on some bandits, he could leak the info to them for a reward, or tell the guild where their hideout was and earn high contribution points on the bounty hunt.

He also played the part of the failed escort, pretending he couldn't protect the merchant he was guarding when bandits or monsters attacked... and then killing the merchant himself. Of course, this wasn't something he could pull off too often, risking suspicion from the guild.

Or, when a request for caravan escorts was posted, he'd leak details to the bandits about which adventurers were joining and how many.

Beyond that, the title of adventurer brought Ajax... and even Levjef and Haston... all sorts of benefits.

Now, Ajax found himself regretting the decision to abandon it.

But clinging to it just because it was what a pity to let go was clearly impossible, given his current situation.

"Damn it, I'd love to hit the tavern, but that's not an option..."

No, I can't.

Ajax couldn't quite finish that thought.

The door burst open abruptly, revealing Levjef and Huston standing there.

The door opened without a knock, causing Ajax to reflexively reach for the longsword nearby... but seeing it was his two companions entering the room, he breathed a sigh of relief.

Still, the abrupt, knock-free intrusion immediately put him in a foul mood, and he opened his mouth.

"You two, barging in like that... What's going on?"

Ajax had initially intended to complain, but seeing the serious expressions on their faces, he was suddenly overcome by a bad feeling.

Could it be that not only his own identity, but also those of Levjef and Haston, had been discovered by other adventurers?

Even so, he doubted it could have happened on the same day as him... He couldn't help but wonder.

(Could it have been Ilse? Was that woman closing in on me part of a coordinated plan with these two?)

He considered it for a moment, but fortunately—or perhaps unfortunately—Haston spoke up to deny it.

"Ajax, this inn is being watched."

"Huh!? But... weren't we already being investigated by someone? Considering that, wouldn't it make sense that those same people are investigating us now?"

Ajax was startled by Huston's sudden words, but he recalled being told before that someone was looking for him and stated as much.

Yet, even as he said it, Ajax also felt that possibility was probably low, considering today's events.

The inn was being watched on the very day he was accused of killing Ilse's family.

Considering that the inn hadn't been watched when he'd said he was being tracked, it was unreasonable to dismiss it as coincidence.

"No... the guy watching us didn't have that kind of vibe."

"If Huston says so, that's definitive."

Huston's words drew a weary sigh from Ajax.

Haston had an intense fixation on smells—especially women's scents—but his sense of smell could do more than just cater to his hobby; it could detect danger.

Since danger itself doesn't have a smell, it was closer to a special ability, but Ajax and the others had been saved by Huston's sense of smell countless times before.

Given that, they couldn't doubt Huston's words now.

"How dangerous exactly?"

"...Extremely high. If things continue like this, I have a feeling the worst possible outcome awaits us."

After a moment's hesitation, Huston stated this.

Huston's words made Ajax's expression stiffen.

Because the words that came from Huston's mouth were ones he had never heard before, even after committing countless misdeeds together.

Despite Ajax and his crew committing numerous crimes, they had never been caught red-handed by other adventurers, guilds, guards, knights... or any other suspicious parties. While their own caution played a role, Huston's keen sense for danger had also been instrumental.

"What do we do?"

Without a hint of doubt in Huston's words, Levjef asked.

While no one within the group was formally designated as the leader, Ajax often took the lead in deciding their course of action.

"We have no choice but to escape now. What about the deal...?"

"We won't know for sure until we contact them, but given the current situation, it's unlikely all the people we want are available."

"...Probably not."

At Levegev's words, Ajax scratched his head.

He had already requested they expedite the deal at a time when he was already under suspicion.

With barely any time having passed since then, even if he said the deal was happening today, the other side couldn't possibly be ready.

"The only saving grace is that they know we're in a precarious position too. At least they won't try to jack up the price."

"Well, if we stall and get caught, they'd be in danger too. Thinking about it that way, it's only natural."

In reality, if they tried to jack up the price here, Ajax and his crew would have no choice but to pay whatever amount demanded.

The funds allocated from above for this operation are substantial.

That's only natural. After all, the women Ajax brings in are precious assets to the higher-ups.

It might sound harsh, but you could call them resources.

Given that they're being used for such unsavory purposes, it's impossible to openly recruit people.

That's precisely why Ajax and various others are ordered to bring women.

Consequently, even if the organizations dealing with Ajax demand somewhat unreasonable sums, it's not that they can't afford it.

...However, since whatever money remains after this job is completed becomes part of Ajax's reward, they naturally wanted to spend as little as possible.

Of course, that's all in the past tense. Given the current situation, they couldn't afford to be stingy with money.

To be stingy with money now would be to trade that money for their lives.

Ajax was ruthless, but it wasn't stupid.

If the deal could be settled with money, they judged it best to wrap things up quickly and leave Gilm.

"Alright, I'll make contact with the organization. The deal is tonight. And once the deal is done, we leave Gilm immediately. ...Is that understood?"

Levjef addressed Ajax and Haston, his tone less a question than a demand for confirmation. Both nodded without hesitation.

"Then I'll go get some food and water from the inn's dining hall. Three days' worth should be enough for now, right?"

"...Yeah. But if possible, buy as much as you can. Shops are still open outside now, so if the inn staff go shopping, the dining hall should manage somehow."

Since it was already evening outside, many people would be eating in the inn's dining hall.

Buying food from such a dining hall would naturally deplete the ingredients for the dishes they serve.

Fortunately, it was still evening, and the sun hadn't fully set yet.

That meant it was still possible to go buy ingredients from shops that were still selling them.

Moreover, requesting three days' worth of meals for three people wouldn't cause the dining hall's supplies to run out.

But... when Ajax and the others moved, they needed to take the women they'd acquired in Gilm with them in the carriage.

Given that, we had to provide them with at least minimal meals.

After all, it would be pointless to let them starve to death after buying them from the organization, and we've been instructed to hand them over in a healthy state.

(Well, if they were just skin and bones, you wouldn't want to hold them anyway.)

Considering what the higher-ups might say when we arrive, we couldn't reduce the women's rations.

Fortunately, the slave collars meant they wouldn't run away or cause trouble, so we didn't have to resort to such tricks as cutting their rations to weaken them.

Anyway, feeding dozens of people for three days would require a lot of the food in the mess hall.

I'll just have to rely on Huston to negotiate that part skillfully.

(I suppose I could go myself, but it's probably best I don't move for now.)

Naturally, the cafeteria was crowded.

Given that, there was a possibility someone who recognized Ajax's face might be there, or someone might come looking for him.

Of course, Ajax hasn't explicitly committed any crime yet.

To be precise, he is a criminal, but it's more accurate to say that it hasn't come to light yet.

For that very reason, it was best for Ajax to stay indoors as much as possible right now.

"Understood. I'll go then."

Levjef announced, and Haston also left the room to attend to his own duties.

Watching the two of them leave, Ajax thought about what lay ahead.

If the deal was suddenly happening today, the number of women gathered would naturally be fewer than originally planned.

That was a painful reality for Ajax too, but given his current situation, it couldn't be helped.

"Still, if things go well, we can escape Gilm today. If that happens, we'll just have to find the missing women somewhere else—another town or village."

The original plan had been to gather the missing women in Aburo or Saburusta, but Ajax had no such luxury now.

Operating in places like Abuero or Saburusta carried a high risk of being spotted by Rei, Ilse, or whoever was currently watching the inn.

(Was it fortunate or unfortunate for me that it wasn't Rei or Ilse keeping watch?)

The fact that Rei wasn't here meant that even if they took action, they wouldn't be immediately dealt with.

But while that was good news, the presence of a new faction also meant more forces would be hunting them down.

A mixed blessing... That was Ajax's honest feeling.

But there was nothing to be done about it now.

All he could do now was hope his actions would come together in time.

That meant Levjef skillfully gathering supplies and Haston maintaining smooth communication with the organization.

The real danger, of course, wasn't gathering food, but Huston negotiating with the underground organization.

Still, Ajax trusted both Levjeff and Huston.

He believes both of them will do their jobs properly.

"Damn it, I never thought I'd run into something like this today. If they were going to find me, they should have waited until after I'd accomplished my goal."

Cursing his own bad luck, Ajax prayed he could somehow escape Gilm safely.
