The merchant who witnessed the scene didn't understand what had happened at first.

No, he understood. More accurately, he didn't want to understand.

The two women hired as carriage escorts—Vihera and Byune... or rather, one woman and one girl.

Byune was a peculiar individual who barely spoke, communicating only through broken phrases, yet normal communication was still possible with her, and above all, she was skilled.

And then there was the other one... Vihera, who acted like Byune's guardian. With her striking beauty and provocative attire, the merchant simply couldn't tear his eyes away.

Though he'd seen his fair share of beauties in his years as a merchant, he couldn't deny she was the most beautiful woman he'd ever encountered in his life.

Add to that her easygoing manner toward him and others, and it was only natural for him to feel affection.

That this fondness transformed from admiration for Vihera's beauty into romantic affection... well, that was only natural.

He wasn't the only merchant harboring feelings for Vihera. His traveling companion, the other merchant in the carriage, felt the same way, and it wasn't uncommon for them to get excited discussing her when they lay down to sleep at night.

Though perhaps a bit hasty, he had even intended to ask her directly to hire him once they arrived in Gilm, bypassing the formalities of a commission.

This decision was by no means based solely on Vihera's beauty. They had been attacked several times by monsters and bandits along the way, and her skill in dispatching them all with ease was more than sufficient to make her a reliable escort.

And in the future, I wanted her to become my wife and walk through life with me.

He had even harbored such feelings, but seeing the scene before him now, he couldn't help but realize it was a fleeting dream.

He had seen Vihera smile countless times while she guarded him, but he had never seen the kind of smile she wore right now, the one directed at someone else.

Knowing who that smile was directed at, he couldn't even imagine becoming a rival for her affection.

Though seeing him directly was a first, as a merchant, he naturally knew who this man was – the one riding a griffin and wielding a scythe larger than himself.

He was a figure who had become famous overnight in the last year or two, and it was said that during last year's war with the Bestir Empire, this man alone had nearly destroyed the Bestir Empire Army.

Rank B adventurer, Rei, known by the epithet Crimson.

(Goodbye, my first love...)

The merchant gently averted his gaze from the two lips pressed together, realizing his first love had ended so abruptly.

Turning to his companion, he saw the sorrowful expression on his partner's face and resolved to drown their sorrows in drink tonight.

Leaving those pitiful merchants behind, Vihera beamed with joy, delighted to reunite with Rei alongside Byune.

"So, Rei. Why are you here? I'd be happy if you came to pick me up."

Having exchanged a kiss and seemingly satisfied for the moment, Vihera questioned why Rei was here.

It was only natural. She had told him beforehand she was going to Gilm in the spring, but even so, appearing here so perfectly timed was something even Vihera found hard to believe.

(If he really did sense I was coming and came here for that reason, then perhaps it is fate. ...Though Elena would probably be angry.)

Vihera harbored the fantasy that he was a knight in shining armor who had come to rescue her from bandits, but it was nothing more than a fantasy.

True, she had been attacked by bandits, but she had fought them off, and when they showed signs of fleeing, she had taunted them to stop them in their tracks. Casting Vihera as the damsel in distress was a bit of a stretch.

Considering Rei's words and actions, and the fact he wielded a massive scythe called the Death Scythe, he hardly fit the prince charming mold either.

"Well, what can I say? It really was just a coincidence that I happened to be passing by."

"By chance? ...Were you somewhere else?"

"Yeah. I took on a job over the winter, teaching at a military academy called Glacial."

"...Well, that's quite an unexpected turn of events."

Laying a vague smile over Vihera's gaze, which seemed to say, "You've done something again, haven't you?"

He knew things wouldn't have turned out this way if not for the duel incident in Gilm, but what was done was done.

As he thought this far, he suddenly recalled the matter concerning the Holy Light Church.

If he was targeted as a major enemy, then it wouldn't be strange if those who acted alongside him, like Vihera and Byune, were also targeted.

The moment Rei realized this and opened his mouth to speak, Byune, who was nearby, tugged at the hem of his Dragon Robe almost simultaneously.

"Hmph!"

Byune gave his usual short warning and pointed. The thieves lay sprawled on the ground.

Several were dead, but most were either unconscious and collapsed or conscious yet unable to move.

Leave the thieves alone and stop flirting! That was what Byune seemed to be saying, and it was a perfectly valid point.

Those immobilized by pain would regain movement given time.

Even if they regained mobility, with people like Rei, Set, Vihera, and Byune around, they probably wouldn't be able to do anything. Still, they couldn't ignore the possibility of a worst-case scenario.

"Right. Then let's tie up the thieves for now. ...Ah, yes. We were supposed to escort those merchants to Gilm. What about you, Rei?"

"...What do you mean, 'what about me'? I don't have anything else to do, so if it's okay, I'll come along with you."

The request for an officer academy instructor was technically a designated request through the guild.

But there are political considerations involved, so it's not difficult to make various accommodations.

Like, for instance, if it takes Rei and Set a bit longer to get back to Gilm.

"Oh? Well then, I'll go make the request. Can you wait here for a bit?"

Pleased that Rei would accompany her, Vihera ran towards the carriage.

As she did, the light fabric of Vihera's spring dress fluttered in the breeze, revealing her voluptuous figure to Rei. He gently averted his eyes, pretending not to see.

"Hmph!"

Byune tugged at Rei's Dragon Robe.

Seeing her point toward the bandit, he immediately understood what she wanted.

"Set, I'll tie up the thief. Keep an eye on the surroundings for now."

"Guruu!"

Set growled at Rei's words. Seeing this, Byune's expression barely changed, though the corners of her eyes softened slightly.

Having gone through the Exil experience with Set, Byune had grown fond of him too. She was probably glad to see he hadn't changed.

"Alright, Byune. For now, I'll tie up the living bandits."

"Mm!"

Rei pulled out a rope from his Misty Ring and announced. Byune snapped back to reality and nodded immediately.

They proceeded to select the bandits who were either unconscious or immobilized by damage, binding them up with rope.

Some of the thieves tried to seize an opening to escape.

But Set, who had been put on watch, wouldn't allow it. Besides, their injuries were too severe to move properly, so they ended up being bound by Rei and Byune.

While this was happening, they must have come to an agreement. Vihera approached Rei with a pleased smile.

"How did it go? ...Well, judging by your expression, I guess I don't need to ask."

"Yeah, basically no problem. Seems they knew about Rei too. But they want to talk a bit. Could you go? I'll handle things over here."

"Got it."

Without a moment's hesitation, Rei nodded at Vihera's words.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn't be strange for Rei to say something to express concern for Vihera, but in Rei and Vihera's case, they understood each other's personalities and abilities well enough.

Handing the rope he was holding to Vihera, Rei headed toward the carriage parked a short distance away.

Waiting there were two men in their mid-twenties.

One wore a friendly expression, while the other looked slightly displeased.

"I heard you wanted to see me?"

"Yes. I understand you're offering to join our escort... In that case, we'll be traveling together for the short journey to Gilm. Given that, I thought it necessary to know a bit about your character."

The friendly-looking man stated this, but the sullen-looking man cut him off.

"To be perfectly honest, your reputation isn't exactly stellar. Well, it's true you have quite a few good points too. But considering all that, I can't place my full trust in you like I do with Ms. Vihera and the others."

"I can't deny that."

After all, Rei had repeatedly attacked thieves for fun, antagonized nobles, and even clashed with the Azoth Trading Company, one of the most powerful groups in Gilm.

(...Huh? Thinking about it, am I actually... no, am I quite a dangerous presence? From the outside, it's no wonder people would want nothing to do with me...)

As Rei suddenly reflected on his past actions, he found himself thinking, Well, maybe it's only natural people are wary of me like this? He was actually starting to accept it.

"I see. I'm glad you understand. ...Well, considering you didn't resort to violence even after hearing this from mere merchants like us, maybe the rumors are exaggerated."

"See? I told you. Since you're acquainted with Vihera, there's no need to be so wary. Besides, an adventurer with a nickname like yours wouldn't normally be hireable by us. This is a good opportunity."

"...If you say so, then fine."

The grumpy merchant said that and stopped complaining.

To be honest, he didn't want to travel with Rei.

Considering the gaze Vihera directed at Rei, bordering on love at first sight, the journey from here to Gilm felt like nothing but agony.

Having to watch the person you like flirt with their lover right in front of you. That was practically mental torture.

The only saving grace was that neither merchant had confessed their feelings to Vihera yet.

Had they, it would have made things unbearably uncomfortable, no doubt.

"I see. Well then, it's only for a short while, but I'll be counting on you."

Rei, seemingly completely unaware of the two merchants' feelings, turned his back on them and headed toward Vihera, who was interrogating the bandits.

"...You're sure?"

"Yeah. Of course. What's the point of saying anything now? Besides, getting protection from him... the adventurer with the Crimson Nickname... is still a better deal than nothing."

"You're surprisingly bold, aren't you?"

"Is that so? Well, they say first loves never last. This much is fine, isn't it?"

The two merchants teased each other about their lost loves, each sealing their feelings for Vihera deep inside.

"So? Did he spill the beans about the hideout?"

"Yes. When I asked nicely, he told me right away."

Vihera reported with a smile, but seeing the indentations shaped like hands and feet in the ground near the bound bandits, it wasn't hard to imagine what kind of "gently" she meant.

That said, Rei had no intention of reproaching her for it.

Considering the likely treatment those bound bandits had endured before, Rei thought Vihera was right—it had been gentle... no, too gentle an interrogation.

(Though, once handed over to the guard squad, they'll be interrogated again. Thinking about how harsh that interrogation will be, I can't help but feel pity for them. After that, they'll probably be slaves. ...It's their own fault, though.)

They must realize what treatment awaits them in their future. Glancing at the dejected bandits with their heads bowed, he addressed Vihera.

"So, where's the hideout? Well, since it was Vihera and the others who got attacked this time, I won't demand a share of the loot. ...Ah, but the spear we used in the initial attack broke, so it'd be helpful if you could at least let us replace that."

"There should be spears the bandits were using, right?"

Following Vihera's gaze, the spears the thieves had used lay discarded on the ground, just as he'd said.
