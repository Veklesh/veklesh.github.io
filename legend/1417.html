At dusk at the Dusk Wheat inn... After parting ways with Marina, Vihera, and Byune, Rei returned to his room. Inside, he spoke with the person reflected in the Paired Orb placed on the table.

"Hmm, so you're busy over there too, huh?"

"Of course. Actually, it's more accurate to say things have gotten even busier because of all the complications involving Gilm's movements."

The other person... Elena placed the cup of tea she was holding on the table and murmured dreamily.

"Huh?"

"Nothing. There, Yellow should just play over there."

What's wrong? The small black dragon on the other side of the Paired Orb tilted its head, but Elena gently stroked it as she said this.

Yellow glanced at Rei's figure on the other side of the Paired Orb, then used its tiny wings to take flight from the table.

When she first contacted the other side, Yellow had seemed delighted to see Rei... but upon learning Rei was inside the building, he let out a disappointed cry.

That was probably because he understood that being inside the building meant Set wasn't there.

Though still young, Yellow was reasonably sharp.

At least enough to understand that Set wouldn't be there because it was inside a building.

And Yellow and Set were practically best friends.

It might have something to do with the fact they were both created beings, but anyway, the two had gotten along incredibly well from the moment they first met.

That's precisely why Yellow, upon learning Set wasn't here, seemed a bit down at first.

Still, when Rei told him that next time he contacted them, he'd do it together with Set, Yello's spirits managed to lift.

Yellow walked away from the table, chirping softly.

"Playing? What exactly does Yellow do when he plays?"

As far as Rei knew, Set liked being petted by everyone.

In slightly larger spaces, he often runs around playing with the children.

Of course, within Gilm, Set wouldn't fly with a child on his back.

Fundamentally, when flying, Set could only carry Rei on his back. Still, if the rider was a child, he could carry them while flying.

But naturally, he couldn't pull such stunts within the Gilm.

"Hmm, shall we take a look?"

At Rei's words, Elena moved the Paired Orb slightly.

And where they moved, there was Yellow chasing a ball made from rolled-up cloth.

Of course, this ball wasn't like the sturdy ones sold in Japan that Rei knew...

(Well, with magic existing here, I suppose they could make something like that if they wanted to.)

Unlike back in Japan, magic existed here. So, to Rei, it didn't seem strange at all that something as simple as a ball could be made.

As Rei thought this, Yellow chased the ball rolling across the floor, using its short tail to nudge it along and then chasing after it again.

Being a somewhat misshapen ball, its path when rolled was quite erratic.

But that very unpredictability made Yellow utterly engrossed in chasing it.

That erratic movement must have been stimulating Yellow's wild instincts.

"He seems to be having quite a good time playing. Maybe I should try making something like that for Set too."

'Set... honestly, I'm not sure. If he were as small as Yellow, this might be fine, but...'

"You mean Set's too big for this?"

'Probably. I hate to say it, but Set is older than Yellow, right? He grows faster too, so honestly, it's doubtful he'd be thrilled with something like this.'

"Is that so? I'd rather think he'd be happy playing with it."

To Rei, even though Set was over three meters tall, he still felt like a child.

Of course, in reality, Set wasn't actually a child.

While he had a friendly disposition, he attacked hostile opponents without mercy. If left free outside the Gilm, he hunted prey as he pleased and ate it.

Viewed as a beast or monster, he was undoubtedly a fully grown individual.

But even so, Rei treated Set like a child.

That was likely due to his friendly nature.

He loves to eat, loves to nap in the sun... or in the shade of trees this time of year, and gets completely absorbed playing with the other children of Gilm.

Seeing Set like that, it's impossible not to treat him like a child.

Hearing Rei's words, Elena must have recalled Set's personality too.

"...Maybe you're right about that."

She murmured, seeming somewhat convinced.

Rei and Set were strangely in sync.

Or rather, for Elena, who knew Rei's secret, it was only natural.

Created through beast magic, Set was, in a sense, akin to Rei's child.

It wasn't strange for such a being to cherish his parent, Rei.

No, it was precisely because he was in front of Rei that he could truly act like a child.

Of course, Elena would never voice that thought.

She knew that if she did, it would undoubtedly upset Rei.

Even though Set is like a child to Rei, Rei is still only in his mid-teens.

Of course, Elena had heard that before coming to this world, Ray had been a little older than he was now, but even that was ultimately just 'a little older'.

Being told he was a parent at that age would naturally make him unhappy.

(At least I would be.)

Though Elena was older than Rei, already in her twenties, she wouldn't be happy to be called a parent either.

...Though in Elena's case, inheriting the Ancient Dragon's magic stone during the succession ritual had made her nearly ageless.

And so, for a while, they engaged in small talk.

The topics weren't anything dramatic.

It was just the usual things that happened, but for Elena, talking with Rei for the first time in days, it was an incredibly happy time.

"I see. So, are meat buns really that popular in Gilm?"

"Well, they're traditionally a winter dish... but that's just my impression. Apparently, they're sold year-round in places like Chinatown."

Of course, Rei had never been to Chinatown.

It was only natural, since there were no such shops near where Rei lived.

That's probably why Rei's image of meat buns was so strongly tied to winter.

"Chinatown?"

"Ah, I guess that doesn't ring a bell? Well, it's a place in the world I came from where Chinese restaurants line the streets. Meat buns are one of those Chinese dishes."

"Hmm, sounds delicious. Do you know any other dishes from that Chinese cuisine?"

Elena's gaze held a hint of expectation... but Rei's response was simply a shake of his head.

"I know the names of some dishes and what they are, but how to make them..."

When he heard "Chinese food," the first thing that came to Rei's mind was shrimp in chili sauce.

But he didn't know how to make it.

After all, while the dish it was based on was Chinese, Sweet and Sour Shrimp was formally conceived as a Japanese dish.

It's common knowledge among those familiar with the details, but Rei didn't know that either.

So when asked how to make that shrimp chili, there was no way she could answer.

(Huh? Ah, but ramen... How exactly is ramen different from udon?)

It suddenly occurred to him, but he couldn't grasp that difference either.

But, whether it was fortunate or not, Rei knew how to make kiritanpo hot pot.

Back in Japan, his father raised chickens for cockfighting. When a chicken lost a fight or got too old, they'd dispose of it by making kiritanpo hot pot.

Rei had helped with this several times. It was also a regional specialty dish, so it made a big impression when she participated in a "hot pot field trip" event during middle and elementary school, where they cooked kiritanpo hot pot outdoors.

(I want kiritanpo hot pot... but there's no rice in this world. Well, maybe there is, but as far as I know, it's not sold around here.)

For some reason, Rei had been thinking about kiritanpo hot pot while eating ramen. Suddenly, he noticed Elena across the Paired Orb giving him a look that seemed to mix exasperation with something else.

"Sorry, what was it?"

'Rei gets really absorbed when it comes to food. Not that it's surprising or anything.'

Of course, Elena knew that Rei loved eating.

Still, even if it was through the Paired Orb, when she was with him, she wanted him to look at her, if possible.

It was only natural for a woman in front of the man she loved to feel that way.

Though Elena never really showed it outwardly.

"Oh? Well, I never disliked eating to begin with. Besides, if I didn't enjoy it, I wouldn't have gone out of my way to teach you all those dishes."

The dishes Rei had taught her... udon, okonomiyaki, nikuman, pizza.

Mostly, he taught them because she asked him to, but there was definitely an element of wanting to eat them himself.

There were plenty of other dishes he wanted to try, but unfortunately, he didn't know the recipes for most of them.

(I'd like to make fried stuff... but oil is expensive. Plus, there's the egg problem too.)

Being young, Rei naturally loves meat. And he loves fried food too.

However, in this world of Elgin, oil is fundamentally expensive, and while eggs aren't quite as costly as oil, they're still classified as a fairly high-end item.

Of course, nobles have the funds to manage such things, so it's probably not a problem for them.

Rei isn't exactly struggling for money either, so if he really wanted to, he could probably manage it... but even with money, you might not necessarily have the ingredients.

(If I get the chance, I could try making tonkatsu... no, maybe oak meat cutlets, or Gamelion karaage... wait, you need potato starch to make karaage... right?)

His knowledge of Tatsuta-age and karaage was a bit mixed up, but to Rei, there wasn't much difference between the two.

Both are delicious, and they're both favorites of mine, so it doesn't change anything.

"So, Rei. How's the expansion work on Gilm coming along?"

Rei snapped back to reality at those words and opened his mouth hesitantly.

"Well, it's still in the preparatory stage right now, so I can't really say... but it seems to be progressing reasonably well. They're putting me to good use, too."

"Hmph. Even saying that, Rei doesn't look the least bit displeased, does she?"

Laying a small smile, Elena's words seemed to say she saw right through Rei. Feeling embarrassed, Rei quietly looked away.

Truthfully, Rei even felt a sense of joy at Gilm growing larger.

After all, Rei had spent several years in Gilm.

He'd interacted with many of the residents living in Gilm, to the point where he considered it his home ground.

That's precisely why he was happy to see Gilm growing like this now.

It was only natural he wanted to help with this endeavor as much as possible. That's precisely why he was doing what was, for Rei, a simple job—transporting timber cut down in Treant Forest—without complaint.

"Now that you mention it, you might be right."

He muttered, not quite admitting it outright, but somehow evading the point.

Elena watched Rei with a smile.

To Elena, Rei was always a person vastly different from the others around them.

That had always been the case, but living apart like this made her feel it even more keenly.

...Though, even when apart, they communicated every few days using their Paired Orbs.

Eventually, the conversation turned to the current situation in Gilm, where many adventurers, craftsmen, merchants, and others seeking work had gathered.

Elena had asked about that particular area for some reason.

"Hmm, so no major disturbances have occurred, then?"

"With so many people gathering, there are plenty of skirmishes and such... but yeah. Though the guards and knights are running around Gilm every day. Or should I say flying around?"

'Flying around is Rei and Set's job, isn't it?'

Elena smiled, tracing a beautiful arc.

Watching Elena with interest, Rei proceeded to recount the events in Gilm.

Suddenly curious, Rei asked, "Why are you so eager to hear about Gilm?" But...

"Hehe, you'll find out soon enough... very soon, in fact."

That was all the reply he got.
