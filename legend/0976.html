"Haa, haa, haa, Eeeh! You're stubborn, aren't you?! Even though your whole body's covered in wounds, why won't you just die?!"

Mireine gripped her longsword, glaring hatefully at the cyclops before her.

How many times had she already sliced through that skin?

The blade of Mireine's longsword had sliced into the Cyclops' body more than ten or twenty times, yet it still wouldn't fall, bleeding profusely from every wound.

"Mireine, that skill? It seemed to deal quite a bit of damage to it."

Johanna, standing beside Mireine, asked while holding her spear at the ready.

Fortunately, the cyclops's single eye remained injured, and its vision hadn't returned. It rampaged wildly, destroying the surrounding trees indiscriminately.

The fact that its eye was blind meant they could safely recover to some extent by keeping their distance. In that sense, it was an easier opponent to fight, which brought a sense of calm to Mireine and Johanna.

After catching her breath at Johanna's words, Mireine shook her head.

"No way. I'm out of magic. I never had that much magic to begin with. That's why I developed the Shock Wave in the first place."

"Can't use it."

"...Hey, you can't land effective attacks on a Cyclops either, can you? If you say it's unusable... Ouch!"

Mireine tried to retort to Johanna's words, but her voice must have been too loud. Hearing it, the Cyclops, though its vision blocked, charged toward the source of the sound.

"You idiot! Your voice is too loud!"

"Shut up. You're the one who started this fight!"

Though they argued back and forth, their timing was perfect as they split cleanly to the left and right, their movements perfectly in sync.

Then, a fist came crashing down where Mireine and Johanna had been standing just seconds before.

Though the punch carried enough force to kick up dust and debris, it meant nothing if it didn't land.

"Enough already, just die!"

Johanna's spear, thrust forward with utter weariness, pierced the Cyclops's side where the fist had struck.

Simultaneously, Mireine's longsword swung from the opposite direction tore through the Cyclops's side, slicing through flesh.

The Cyclops screamed in agony.

But even hearing that scream, Mireine and Johanna didn't let their guard down, immediately creating distance.

The instant they pulled back, the Cyclops's arms swung wildly, sweeping through the space where Mireine and Johanna had been just moments before.

Even if the blow missed, the sight of arms as thick as Mireine and Johanna's torsos roaring through the air sent a chill down their spines.

Even so, neither Mireine nor Johanna halted their advance, despite the terror running through their spines.

The instant the colossal blow passed before their eyes, they seized the opening, drawing the Cyclops into their weapons' range and launching their attacks.

Mireine stepped forward and swung her longsword, while Johanna thrust her spear forward from a position where she could put all her strength into the spearhead.

Their movements were unbelievable for two girls who were always fighting.

As the Cyclops screamed in pain, the wind arrow fired by Surnin struck its mouth.

Prioritizing speed, the wind arrow's attack power itself was low. However, it possessed sufficient force to slash through the Cyclops's mouth.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAHHH!"

Ignoring the pain inside his mouth, the Cyclops roared in agony and frustration.

Blinded, its body slashed and pierced, its frustration reached its peak...

"GAAAAAH!?"

Suddenly, it halted its roar, crouching down as if clutching its right leg.

Hearing the scream-like cry, the three understood it was a cry of pain from an attack.

But who...? Mireine and Johanna turned their gaze to Surunin, who possessed long-range attack capabilities, but even Surunin wore an expression that suggested he didn't understand what had happened either.

"Who?"

Mireine muttered, her gaze shifting to the leg the Cyclops was clutching... and she couldn't help but frown.

What came into view was the sight of a needle stuck between the nail and skin of the big toe on the right foot.

Needless to say, piercing the space between the nail and skin with a needle caused excruciating pain.

Compared to being sliced by a longsword or stabbed by a spear, the pain from this was overwhelmingly worse.

Indeed, the Cyclops was clutching his foot, unable to move.

"Hmph!"

That sudden sound reached Mireine and Johanna, who had been looking around in confusion.

The source of that brief sound was a tree branch growing a short distance from where Mireine and the others were fighting.

There sat a figure of small stature—still a girl, or perhaps even a young child, depending on the perspective.

(Who!?)

Mireine wondered for a moment, but deciding that an unknown person here who would help her was probably Johanna's acquaintance, she charged straight at the Cyclops, which was groaning in pain.

Johanna did the same, likely deciding that child was someone Mireine knew, and charged at the Cyclops spear in hand.

A swinging longsword and a thrusting spear.

Surnin also launched a spear-shaped blast of solidified earth toward the cyclops.

The child on the branch... Byune also swiftly threw needles to provide support.

"The main event arrives at the very last moment!"

Dietz's strike, swinging his longsword wide from behind the Cyclops, joined the fray... From there, the concentrated assault piled damage upon the Cyclops, leaving it unable to retaliate.

Though Ekril, the scorching wind ally, was absent, the assembled force more than compensated: Johanna wielding her spear, Byune providing support with needle throws, and Dietz, strong by virtue of being male. It was only natural that the blind Cyclops' life would be snuffed out.

The Cyclops collapsed to the ground with a heavy thud.

Knowing the Cyclops' formidable vitality, Mireine and the others remained vigilant, never taking their eyes off it.

Seconds passed, then tens of seconds, then dozens of seconds, yet the Cyclops showed no sign of movement. Mireille turned her gaze to Johanna, who stood beside her, spear at the ready.

At that very moment, Johanna's gaze also met Mireine's, their eyes locking mid-air.

They both wanted to say the same thing.

Namely, go check if the Cyclops is dead.

"Johanna has her spear, so she should be fine. She could just poke it a little, right?"

"Rei could probably tell just by punching it barehanded. Don't push it onto someone else."

While they exchanged these few words, Byune, who had been poised with her needle on a tree branch, leapt down and approached the Cyclops.

"Hey, young lady, that's dangerous!"

"Don't get too close!"

But Byune seemed to ignore the warnings entirely, closing in on the cyclops and delivering a light kick to its head.

The Cyclops didn't move at all. It was completely dead.

"Johanna's acquaintance sure has some guts, doesn't she?"

"...Huh? Isn't she Rei's acquaintance?"

Only then did they realize neither knew who Byune was, and both their gazes turned to Dietz, who had been standing there oblivious.

'Who?'

A simple question, yet one asked in perfect unison.

Watching the two of them, Dietz thought they seemed to get along somehow, and shifted his gaze to Vihera, who was fighting the Cyclops.

Only two Cyclops remained alive now. One of them had both arms broken at the elbows and was currently trying to crush Vihera underfoot.

"Hey!"

Mireine cried out in alarm at the sight, but Johanna, Dietz, and Byune watched Vihera's fight without the slightest sign of panic.

Surnin also flashed a momentary look of alarm, but seeing the other three's composure, he must have concluded it was fine. He judged that Vihera possessed the strength to handle five Cyclops alone.

The clearest proof lay beside the figure called Vihera: the corpses of three Cyclops.

And one of the two surviving Cyclops already had both arms rendered useless.

"Who... is she?"

"I'd like you to explain that along with the other one over there."

Johanna hesitated at Surnin and Mireine's words.

Should she simply state that Vihera was the former Second Princess of the Bestir Empire?

Johanna hesitated for a few seconds, but ultimately decided to postpone her answer and turned her gaze toward Byune.

"More importantly! What on earth is that girl?! She's surprisingly skilled despite her looks, and she casually does things like sticking needles between her nails!"

Johanna deliberately raised her voice to ask, trying to deflect Mireine's question.

Dietz, who had become the target of her words, scratched his head and spoke up.

"That child is Byune. She seems to be Vihera's partner."

"...Lady Vihera's!? Huh? Well, she is skilled, but still, a child like that?"

Johanna, her eyes filled with disbelief at Dietz's explanation, turned to look at Vihera again. At that very moment, she froze.

Mireine, puzzled by her reaction, followed Johanna's gaze and also froze.

Because at the end of that gaze was...

"GRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

The very being Rei and Johanna loved dearly was roaring.

Simultaneously with the roar, multiple Wind Arrows appeared around Set and fired toward the Cyclops.

"Gaaah!?"

The cyclops whose arms had been broken screamed as it took several wind arrows in its back.

No, that scream wasn't just from the pain of the wind arrows; it seemed to be partly because it had seen the griffin, a being vastly superior to itself.

The other Cyclops, the only one still unharmed, must have sensed the powerful presence approaching as well. Yet, instinctively aware that any lapse in concentration would mean an instant death at Vihera's merciless assault, it couldn't stop moving.

...It didn't stop moving, but it was only natural that its movements would slow in the presence of a being like a griffin. Seizing that opening, Vihera's fists struck repeatedly, concentrating their blows on the cyclops's right knee.

Was Vihera refraining from using his Demon-Infused Palm purely to savor the fight?

But even that battle rapidly became tedious as his opponent's movements slowed...

"Enough. Sleep."

With those words, the Immersion Palm was unleashed upon the Cyclops, whose right knee had been shattered by the final blow, taking its life in one strike.

Then, against the Cyclops whose arms were already useless, he unleashed the Immersion Palm onto its right knee, shattering it in one blow. As it knelt, he unleashed the Immersion Palm once more, taking its life with surprising ease.

And so, the battle unfolding here came to an end...

"Grrrrrrrr..."

Set let out a growl as he flapped his wings and descended to the ground.

Seeing Set, Mireine and Johanna started to cheer, but their movements froze when they noticed he was clutching something.

The first to speak was naturally Mireine, who instantly recognized who Set was gently holding in his front paws to avoid injury.

"Ecuril!?"

At that word, Surunin, who had just escaped the nightmarish situation of facing seven Cyclopes combined and was feeling relieved, also turned his gaze toward Set.

And there, indeed, was Ecuril.

Set, who had been flying, flapped his wings and slowly descended to the ground, careful not to transmit the shock to Ecuril, whom he held in his front paws.

"Ecuril!"

Mireine cried out and ran toward the spot, followed by Sulnin, Johanna, Dietz, and the others.

"...He's okay. He's injured, but it's nothing life-threatening. He just seems to be unconscious."

Surnin, having checked on Ekril laid on the ground, reported this, and everyone who heard it breathed a sigh of relief.

"Honestly, acting as our rear guard alone to let us escape... You always do such reckless things."

Mireine's muttered words, tinged with both anger and relief, echoed around them.
