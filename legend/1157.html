Normally, Set would have been asked to land a little way off from Gilm, but this time was special.

Because, while it wasn't a matter of life and death—thanks to Hester's potion—Pump was undoubtedly seriously injured and needed treatment as soon as possible.

Therefore, despite it being somewhat reckless, Rei had Set land near Gilm.

...If it were purely about helping Pump, Rei could have just used the potion he carried in his Misty Ring... Unfortunately, Rei had no intention of doing so.

If it had been someone Rei was even somewhat close to, Rei would have used a potion. But he'd only met Hester today, and as for Pump, they'd barely exchanged a word.

If Hester hadn't had a potion, Rei might have used one of his own, but even those weren't particularly high quality.

Anyway, Set suddenly descended right before them, but Gilm's guards wouldn't be thrown into chaos by something like that.

They were somewhat on alert, aware that something was approaching from the sky, but upon realizing it was Set... and that he was carrying Rei on his back, while his front paws held a bundle tied with rope—Hester bound to it, clutching Pump—they breathed a sigh of relief.

Of course, since he was carrying something unlike usual, they understood something had happened.

Still, knowing it wasn't an aerial monster attack was a definite relief.

"Hey, where's Captain Ranga?"

"He's over in the Noble District today. You know, that idiot who caused trouble the other day."

"...Ah, that. Damn it. Well, can't be helped. We should at least inform Captain Ranga, who's in charge of Rei."

"Ah, damn. I should've gone to get him when Rei came back."

"Nah, that wouldn't work. It's not like Rei caused any trouble or anything."

Hearing that conversation right in front of him, even Rei felt like sighing.

"Listen, how can you two talk about this so openly in front of me? More importantly, we have an injured person. Get him processed inside immediately."

At those words, one of the guards must have recognized the figure bound by the rope bundle Set was holding. He opened his mouth, looking taken aback.

"You're the one who told us about the goblin leader... Come to think of it, you left with Rei, so you'd be coming back with him too. So, you're the injured one?"

To keep him as warm as possible during the hundred-meter flight, Pump's body was wrapped in cloth.

Because of that, the guard probably couldn't tell how badly Pump was injured.

But even so, as he approached, being a guard, his sense of smell was naturally keen to the scent of blood...

"Hey, doctor! Wait, there was someone who could use healing magic, right? Anyway, get ready to treat him!"

Hearing that voice, the surrounding guards must have realized this was no ordinary situation. They sprang into action swiftly.

They cut the rope binding Hester and Pump to prevent them from falling from Set... and likely realized Pump's condition. The guards each harbored anger toward the one who had inflicted such pain on a person.

Those waiting near the gate for entry procedures into Gilm had some discontent, but none dared voice complaints in the current situation.

"Please, help Pump!"

Hester shouted as she entered Gilm with the guards.

The entry procedures would likely be handled after the guards had accompanied them inside Gilm.

"I'll report this to the guild, so don't worry about us!"

Calling out to Hester's retreating back, her voice must have reached him. He glanced at Rei for just an instant, bowed once, then ran off.

Watching him go, Rei turned his attention back to the guard remaining at the scene and pulled out his guild card.

"So, I'll need you to handle the paperwork. I have to inform the guild about this incident."

"...Alright, understood."

Responding briefly to Rei's words, the guard began the paperwork and spoke up.

"So, was that really... the work of the goblin leader?"

"Yeah. When we attacked, it was being used as a nice little toy."

"Damn it! That's why goblins are such a pain in the ass. Damn them!"

This guard wasn't exactly on good terms with Pump.

Still, perhaps because they lived in the same Gilm, he felt a sense of camaraderie.

Feeling a certain comfort in the guard's demeanor, Rei completed the procedures and received the familiar's necklace.

"Don't worry, the goblin leader and the whole pack were wiped out. A few survivors might be out there, but what can a handful of goblins do anyway?"

"...Yeah. Still, I can't stop being pissed off."

Exchanging those final words, Rei and Set entered Gilm.

Naturally, several people tried to speak to Set or offer him food while they walked, but seeing Rei's expression, they silently let them pass, perhaps judging it wasn't the time for that.

Rei arrived at the guild, parted ways with Set, and entered the building.

The moment Rei entered, several pairs of eyes turned toward him.

Among those staring was, of course, Vihera .

However, there was no trace of concern for Rei in Vihera's gaze.

It wasn't that she was heartless; she simply hadn't thought for a moment that Rei would be troubled by a mere goblin leader.

It wasn't just Vihera sending such glances; Lenora and Kenny were doing the same.

...No, in fact, not a single person present believed Rei would be outmatched by a mere goblin.

Under those gazes, Rei nodded at Vihera to indicate everything was fine, then headed straight for the counter.

"I've taken care of the goblin leader's pack."

"Thank you. I believed you would complete the request safely, Rei."

Lenora exhaled in relief at Rei's words.

As she did so, she began processing the paperwork she had prepared after Rei left...

"Um, as per procedure, could I see the goblin leader's body?"

"Sure."

It was a scam where they pretended to defeat the target when they hadn't, then swindled the reward.

It was a scam tactic used before by another guild.

Of course, considering the presence or absence of proof-of-kill parts, it wouldn't cause that much of a fuss.

Still, it's not uncommon for someone to be unable to cut off the proof of defeat during the heat of battle.

They exploited that for fraud, but naturally, such a scheme couldn't last forever.

But once an adventurer got a taste of it, they repeated the same thing over and over... and naturally, the guild found out about it.

As a result, that adventurer was sold into criminal slavery and ultimately died, filled with regret for their actions.

Unaware of such past incidents, Rei spoke while looking at the Misty Ring.

"Hester's comrade was still alive, so I rushed back to treat them. That's why, while the body is here, it hasn't been dismembered or stripped yet... If that's okay with you, would you like to check?"

"Yes, please."

Lenora's words surprised Rei a little.

He had expected her to show at least a little reluctance.

But he quickly understood—she was the guild receptionist, after all.

After all, purchasing materials was part of a guild receptionist's job.

And among those materials were entrails, eyeballs, brains... The dismembered remains of monsters, skinned and brought in.

She was used to seeing materials; there was no reason she should be shocked by a corpse.

"Alright, I'll get it out then."

After announcing that, she retrieved the goblin leader's corpse from the Misty Ring.

The torso and the severed head.

The corpse, clearly larger than ordinary goblins, was unmistakably that of a Goblin Leader, a higher-ranking goblin species.

Since it was already common knowledge that Rei possessed an item box, those watching nearby didn't seem particularly surprised by the act itself.

Their surprise stemmed from another reason.

Moreover, since those present were adventurers active in Gilm, many possessed the strength to easily defeat a goblin leader.

...That was true only when facing the Goblin Leader alone, however. Encountering a horde of nearly a hundred goblins led by one would likely lead many to the same fate as Hester and the others.

Even so, it took Rei roughly an hour from accepting the goblin leader slaying request at the guild to returning here.

Completing the request in such a short time after accepting it was truly a speed that could only be described as astonishing.

Normally, just getting ready and reaching the forest where the Goblin Leader and its pack were hiding would take several hours.

Then, entering the woods and searching for the target... that would take several more hours... no, if things went badly, even days.

Furthermore, it was necessary to separate the goblin group from the leader, requiring either waiting for the right moment or setting up a diversion.

Even without being an extraordinary figure like Rei, some Rank B adventurers or Rank A adventurers could probably crush a goblin horde head-on, but such adventurers were few and far between.

Alternatively, adventurers could gather enough numbers to match the goblins' strength, but whether that's feasible... Rewards for goblin hunts are already low, and materials and magic stones don't fetch high prices.

On top of that, the more people involved, the smaller the share of the reward becomes... Under those circumstances, it would be impossible for a large group to take on such a job without some compelling reason.

Unless, of course, a goblin horde were to attack Gilm itself.

In other words, what had the adventurers in the guild astonished was that it had taken Rei only about an hour from accepting the request to resolving it.

"Confirmed. Goblin leader. Um, is it okay if I cut off the right ear as proof of slaying?"

"Yeah... No, I'll do that."

He pulled a butcher's knife from his Misty Ring, swiftly sliced off the right ear of the decapitated goblin leader, and handed it to Lenora.

Compared to a regular goblin's ear, it was large, matching the size of its body.

However, the shape itself wasn't much different from a regular goblin's, making it difficult to identify it as a goblin leader just by looking at the ear alone.

At least, from Rei's perspective, it was undeniably true that he could barely discern any subtle differences.

"Well then, confirmed. Now, Rei. The Guildmaster is waiting. Do you have a little time now?"

"I don't mind, but..."

Rei's gaze shifted to Vihera, who stood a short distance away.

Feeling that gaze, Vihera approached the counter.

...Seeing Vihera approach, Lenora instinctively took a step back.

Vihera was flaunting her ample bosom, and for Lenora, who harbored insecurities about her own bust size, Vihera was an enemy every bit as formidable as Kenny.

(It's okay. My bust size isn't small by any means. It's perfectly average for a human. Yes, I won't let anyone call me flat-chested.)

She reassured herself mentally, then took a small, deep breath before speaking.

"I understand Ms. Vihera is also invited, so I don't see any problem."

"Is that so? Well then, I suppose I'll take you up on that."

Unfazed by Lenora's attitude, Vihera stated it plainly.

Kenny, too, was inwardly displeased by the behavior of his romantic rival... especially since Vihera was one or two steps ahead of him.

Still, nothing about the situation changed... Rei stowed the corpse of the goblin leader, its right ear severed, into the Misty Ring, then entered the back of the counter with Vihera.

Watching the two depart, Lenora began gathering the documents for this designated request.

Since it was such an urgent request, she'd prepared most of the paperwork while Rei was handling the request, but there were still some documents she couldn't complete until she properly verified the proof of extermination parts.

While writing those papers, Lenora thought she needed to hear Hester's side of the story too... just as she noticed Kenny sighing beside her.

To a man, it might have seemed like a melancholy sigh, but unfortunately for Lenora, who had known Kenny for a long time, she couldn't possibly think so kindly of it.

"What's wrong?"

"...Nothing. Lenora's been undermanned from the start and isn't even considered a threat, but since I'm somehow able to fight back, I have a lot to think about."

What did "not a threat" even mean?

It was obvious when you saw Kenny standing there, emphasizing her chest with both arms.

Seeing Kenny like that, Lenora felt the veins on her forehead throb.
