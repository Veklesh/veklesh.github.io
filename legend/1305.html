That day, Galahad was handling paperwork at his residence as chairman of the Azoth Trading Company.

He never had much knowledge in that area to begin with, and after becoming chairman, he struggled with various things for a while.

He believed he had endured his share of hardships as an adventurer, but these were different struggles.

Meeting with the bigwigs from other trading companies, or even meeting Daskar, the lord of this very guild... That's how he came to experience a different kind of hardship than before.

Of course, it wasn't as if he'd never met such important figures before.

But those encounters had always been as an adventurer, not as the actual head of the Azoth Trading Company.

As I handled these duties, I often found myself impressed that Bornter, despite being a problematic elder brother, had managed to keep the Azoth Trading Company together.

Still, after years as chairman, he naturally grew accustomed to the work.

The current Galahad had become fully capable of handling the duties of chairman.

Today, once again, he reviewed various documents submitted from below, signed them, and asked questions of the responsible parties when necessary.

Working like that, and feeling a bit tired, he was taking a break.

(Spring, huh. For adventurers, this must be the busiest season.)

Having been an adventurer himself, Galahad gazed out the window with a pensive look, thinking to himself.

He recalled his adventurer days and felt a touch of nostalgia.

He had been reasonably confident in his abilities, but Bornter, who knew this, had used him well.

But still, for the sake of his family... That's what Galahad had told himself to endure it. The result was his current situation... and Bornter's whereabouts, unknown to him now.

It wasn't him, who had endured his brother's mistreatment for years, who had done it. It was Rei, who had been picked on by his brother. So, it was only natural to find it a little amusing.

Even though he and Rei didn't have any overt dealings now, their current relationship could still be considered reasonably good.

"...Hm?"

Galahad was lost in thoughts of Rei when suddenly, footsteps rushing into the office reached his ears.

Since they were making such noise, it was certain they weren't an assassin.

Still, just to be safe, he reached for the long sword he couldn't bring himself to part with, even now that he held the position of Chairman.

Of course, he didn't truly believe it would be necessary.

But having spent more time as an adventurer than as Chairman, old habits die hard.

Galahad thought this to himself as he looked toward the door...

"Galahad, it's terrible!"

It was Murt who burst into the office, practically kicking the door open.

He was someone who looked up to Galahad like a real older brother, and had gotten involved alongside him when Azoth Trading Company had a dispute with Rei.

"What's wrong, Murt? Why are you so flustered?"

"Haa, haa, haa... G-Galahad-san... Rei is here."

"...Rei?"

It was surprising even to Galahad that Rei would show up at precisely this moment, when he was thinking about him.

After all, Rei was supposed to be avoiding any overt involvement with the Azoth Trading Company right now.

So why? He wondered for a moment, but decided she must have some business and opened his mouth.

"I see. Then send him to the guest room. I'll be right there..."

"No! That's not it!"

Murt shouted, cutting Galahad off.

At that, Galahad must have noticed something was off with Murt.

Furrowing his brow slightly, he spoke.

"What's wrong? What happened?"

"According to the gatekeeper who brought me the report, Rei seems pretty angry. Fortunately, it looks like no one at the gate has been harmed yet."

"...What?"

It was someone who had worked at the Azoth Trading Company for a long time, and it was safe to say that hardly anyone would think of picking a fight with Rei.

Naturally, Galahad had no recollection of giving any orders that would provoke a confrontation with Rei.

In other words, the current situation was a complete surprise... and knowing exactly what Rei's anger could bring, Galahad's cheek twitched.

"Did someone lay a hand on Rei?"

"I doubt anyone would be that reckless..."

The reason Murt couldn't say that with absolute certainty was likely because several years had passed since Rei and Azoth Trading Company had last clashed.

It wasn't impossible that someone who had joined the company since then had decided they could handle Rei and made a move...

Of course, those who had been with Azoth Trading Company from the start had been warned not to antagonize Rei, but it couldn't be ruled out that some of the younger members possessed that characteristic youthful sense of omnipotence.

That was precisely why he had been vague.

Though Azoth Trading Company had scaled back its operations compared to before, it remained one of the major trading houses within Gilm.

Given that, it was only natural that the number of people employed there was enormous.

"Anyway, if Rei has come, show him to the guest quarters. I don't know what's got him so angry, but I'll hear him out."

The news that Rei was angry was not easy on the stomach.

Still, from past dealings, I knew Rei wasn't someone who refused to listen altogether.

If he were the type to lash out without warning, this mansion would likely already be in ruins.

That was why Galahad could manage to stay somewhat calm right now.

...Though, having to talk to an angry Rei was quietly inflicting some mental damage on Galahad.

Murt must understand that too. He nodded immediately and left the office without another word.

The new guard who came to report was in no condition to be of any use whatsoever.

Furthermore, when explaining the situation to Rei, it would be better for someone who knew her at least a little to speak directly, rather than someone who knew nothing.

That was the reasoning behind his actions.

(My stomach...)

Though it was over his leather armor, Murt gently rubbed his stomach.

While not as severe as Galahad's, this incident had apparently dealt significant damage to Murt's stomach as well.

When the Azoth Trading Company had clashed with Rei before, Murt had initially taken hostile actions against Rei. Now, however, he understood just how reckless that behavior had been.

...Though, his hostility toward Rei was largely because his respected Galahad had been treated with contempt by Rei.

It wasn't hostility without reason.

And as the troubles with the Azoth Trading Company continued, Murt came to understand just how capable a person Rei truly was.

(My stomach hurts. Why on earth is Rei so furious?)

Murt left the mansion and headed toward the gate.

He didn't show it on his face, but seeing Rei standing before the gate made the stomach pain he'd been feeling earlier feel even worse.

Still, he forced himself not to show the pain. Reaching the gate, he opened his mouth.

"Long time no see, Rei."

"Yeah. With everything that's happened, we never got a chance to meet face-to-face like this until now."

"Hahaha. Well, even so, being in Gilm, I hear about your exploits, Rei. Seems you're making quite a splash."

He said this, trying to ease the tension between Rei and himself.

But it wasn't flattery.

It was the honest truth that news of Rei's exploits had reached me.

"So, are those beauties and young ladies you're with the ones who've been hanging out with Rei lately?"

"Yeah. They just formed a party today. ...But on the very day they formed, some bastard critically injured an alchemist I know and stole the magic item I'd left in his care."

At those words, Murt understood why Rei was angry. ...He understood.

Rei was merciless to enemies, but deeply loyal to his comrades.

Given what had just happened, it was only natural he was this furious.

Above all, the fact that they stole Rei's magic item was significant.

The Azoth Trading Company had previously clashed with Rei because they'd tried to seize the magic item he possessed.

Considering that, Rei coming to Azoth Trading Company for a similar reason this time might be a kind of fate.

(No, I don't need that kind of fate!)

Rejecting that fate with every fiber of his being, Murt opened his mouth, careful not to provoke Rei... and careful not to offend the others present either.

"Well... there are some idiots out there. Though it seems unlikely anyone in Gilm doesn't understand what happens when you make Rei your enemy."

Considering the context of Gilm, making an enemy of Rei could be considered a fatal blow in the most violent sense.

Furthermore, many of those who cherished Rei's familiar, Set, showed no mercy to anyone who opposed Rei, who had Set as his familiar.

But... if Rei was here and in a foul mood, it was obvious who the target of that fatal blow would be.

(Who the hell did something this stupid!? ...No, maybe it's some kind of misunderstanding...)

Given that Rei had come this far, that possibility was frighteningly low.

Understanding this, yet still clinging to a thread of hope, he spoke.

Yet, at Murt's words, Rei fixed him with a gaze worthy of his nickname, "Crimson," brimming with overwhelming pressure, and spoke.

"Yeah. I thought so too. Especially since it was that merchant guild that got their asses kicked doing something similar before, and the current head of that guild is Galahad, the very person responsible back then."

"No!"

Before Rei could finish his sentence, Murt cut him off.

It was reflexive.

That's precisely why Rei felt something off about Murt standing before him.

Wasn't he trying too hard, considering he'd chosen to oppose him?

Of course, Rei knew exactly how much power he possessed.

Given that, he could understand why Murt would confront him head-on like this. But Murt's expression right now didn't look like acting at all.

He did wonder if Murt might actually possess exceptional acting skills, but observing him now, that possibility seemed unlikely.

These were words born from genuine, heartfelt concern for Galahad.

But... that momentary lapse in vigilance lasted only an instant.

"Then why is the scent of the one who attacked the alchemist and stole my magic item heading toward this mansion?"

"...What?"

A stunned utterance escaped Murt's lips.

Since Rei had come to the Azoth Trading Company, he was certain there was some reason behind it.

But the fact that the person who did it was inside this mansion... that was a bit unexpected.

"Is that true?"

"You doubt Set's sense of smell? I'm not bragging, but Set's sense of smell surpasses not only humans, but even dogs."

A lion-based monster, and dogs? Murt wondered for just a moment, but now was no time to dwell on such details.

He hurriedly turned his gaze to the gatekeeper and shouted sharply.

"Was anyone inside the mansion before Rei and the others arrived!?"

"Um, yes. Mr. Coris said he had business or something, a little while ago..."

"How long ago, specifically?"

"...Just a few minutes before Rei and the others arrived."

"That must be it."

Rei murmured at the gatekeeper's words.

The person who originally attacked Azimov had fled the scene around the same time Rei and the others arrived at Azimov's house.

After that, they healed Azimov's injuries with potions, left him in Marina's care, and then followed the scent with Set, Vihera, and Byune. Considering the timing, it wasn't that strange.

"By the way, did anyone besides Coris come by?"

"Well... about an hour ago, Mr. Sols came by."

"We can probably rule him out. ...Anyway, since this Coris is the one who stole my magic item, I'd like to hear his story."

"Calm down, Rei."

Rei declared, pressing forward toward Murt, but Vihera gently restrained him, placing a hand on his shoulder.

"If he's inside this mansion, he can't escape. And with Set here, we can assume the Sleipnir Boots are already back, right? ...Hey, you said your name was Murt, right? Until this matter is resolved, could you please make sure no one, including that Coris fellow, leaves the mansion grounds?"

All Murt could do in response to this request was silently nod, to show that they... that Azoth Trading Company, had no intention of opposing Rei.
