Their lunch break, during which they had witnessed the heartwarming sight of Yellow rescuing a squirrel, eventually came to an end, and Rei and the others decided to resume their exploration of the forest.

Unlike before, rather than just wandering aimlessly through the forest, someone suggested that since there was a river right in front of them—even if it was more of a stream—they might as well follow it upstream.

With no particular objections, and since the presence of a river would make it easier for Marina to use her specialty—elemental magic, particularly water magic—they decided to head upstream.

If there had been a clear way to explore this dungeon-like forest, they might have prioritized that… but since this was Rei and the others’ first time tackling this dungeon, they didn’t have any specific guidelines to follow.

“The river’s gotten quite a bit wider, hasn’t it? I was sure that if we headed upstream, we’d reach the source right away.”

Spotting the silhouettes of fish swimming in the river, Vihera murmured.

With a width and depth that allowed fish to swim freely, the river was now too large to be called a mere stream.

“I’m not sure if ‘tributary’ is the right word given where we were just now, but I suppose that’s what it is. The river getting wider gives me more options in battle, so I’m actually glad about it.”

“Grrr!”

The moment Marina mentioned having more options in battle, Set let out a sharp cry, as if it were a signal.

The moment they heard Set’s cry, everyone readied themselves to respond immediately, no matter what happened.

“Since Marina said so…”

“Oh, is it because of me? Well, I suppose so. Since we’re here anyway, I’ll use my water spirit magic to…”

Before Marina could finish her sentence, Rei sprang into action.

She had seen the surface of the nearby river swelling unnaturally.

Emerging as if breaking through the water’s surface was a horse.

(Horse, water, horse… A kelpie!)

The Kelpie, a Rank C monster that often inhabits places like rivers, swamps, and lakes.

Rei had heard stories about them and even eaten them in dishes before, but this was the first time he’d actually seen a Kelpie in person.

The kelpie had only its head above the water, and it was facing Marina.

Seeing a kelpie like that, it would be impossible not to be suspicious.

In fact, the moment Rei stepped in front of Marina, water spurted from the kelpie’s mouth.

A water jet, or perhaps a water cutter?

In any case, as the water was fired toward Marina in an instant, Rei swung his Death Scythe, infused with magic.

There’s no doubt the water attack launched by the kelpie was quite powerful.

A surprise attack… especially since it was lying in wait, fully prepared, right in the river—its own territory.

But such an attack was never going to work against Rei, who held the Death Scythe; the water fired by the Kelpie was easily blocked by the blade.

Perhaps not expecting her attack to be blocked in such a prime opportunity, the Kelpie paused for an instant with her head still above the water’s surface… and that moment proved to be her undoing.

The Mirage unleashed by Elena took the form of a whip and wrapped itself around the Kelpie’s neck, which was protruding from the water’s surface… and severed it clean off.

"Hihihihihiiin!"

As a torrent of blood gushed from the severed Kelpie’s neck, another Kelpie emerged from the river’s surface a short distance away.

Family? Friends? Or perhaps a lover?

No one present knows what kind of relationship the two Kelpies share.

But there was no doubt that the newly appeared kelpie felt anger toward Rei and the others, and, driven by that rage, it tried to attack Elena, who had killed the other kelpie...

“Grrr!”

The moment Set’s voice rang out, the face of the kelpie—which, like the first one, had been about to spit water—snapped unnaturally as if struck by something, and the water was flung in a random direction.

One of Set’s skills: the Magic Eye of Impact.

The attack itself wasn’t particularly powerful, and the Kelpie that took the hit didn’t seem to have suffered any damage.

But as a move to catch an opponent off guard in the middle of nowhere, it was quite effective.

“Hiiihhh!?”

The kelpie, having experienced a blow meant to catch it off guard being deflected in an unexpected direction by an unforeseen attack, looked back at Elena in a panic... or rather, the moment it tried to look, it was struck by a blow from Set, who had instantly moved right in front of it, causing its head to explode.

It was “Power Crush,” one of Set’s skills.

Having reached Level 5, that strike had become terrifyingly powerful—and it certainly wasn’t going to stop at just breaking the Kelpie’s neck.

On top of that, Set was equipped with a Bracelet of Strength.

The combined effect of that magic item, which boosts physical strength, and Power Crush caused the Kelpie’s head to explode.

(Should I say something like, “That’s some nasty fireworks”?)

Watching as Marina used her spirit magic to pull the Kelpie’s corpse out of the river, Rei found himself thinking that.

Of course, even if he said that, no one would understand him.

“Two kelpies, huh. Kelpie meat is delicious, so this was a welcome ambush.”

Relieu must have eaten kelpie meat before, too. True to his words, he wore a delighted smile.

“I didn’t think there were monsters in the river. Well, come to think of it, it wouldn’t be strange if there were.”

“What should we do? Should we keep heading upstream? There might be other monsters waiting for us in the river, you know?”

Rei hesitated slightly at Vihera’s question.

After all, he didn’t even know where the stairs leading to the third floor were.

Given that, even if they continued upstream, there was a very real possibility they’d be attacked by monsters like the Kelpie—creatures that made the water their domain—just as they had been.

But after thinking it over for a moment, Rei decided to head upstream again, fully aware of the risks.

“Since we don’t know where the stairs are, it doesn’t really matter which way we go. Besides, I know the river monsters are a pain, but we can handle them just fine.”

“...I won’t argue with that.”

The reason Vihera seemed slightly dissatisfied was likely because, if the monsters were in the river, she wouldn’t be able to fight them.

Of course, it wasn’t impossible for her to fight if she pushed herself, but fighting while in the water would be a considerable burden even for Vihera.

There’s no way she’d be able to perform at her usual level.

Moreover, since Vihera specializes in close-quarters combat that relies on her agility, fighting in a situation where she can’t move freely in the water simply wouldn’t allow for a proper battle.

From Vihera’s perspective, it’s only natural that she’d want to explore the forest, where she can move freely and fight.

“Well, even if we go upstream, it’s not like all the monsters we encounter will be confined to the river itself. There are plenty of monsters active along the riverbanks, and since water sources naturally attract large numbers of monsters, why not focus our efforts there?”

While there are some monsters that don’t need to drink water—a concept Rei finds a bit hard to grasp—for the vast majority, water is, of course, essential to their survival.

If you head upstream along the river, it’s not hard to run into monsters that have come to drink.

Of course, when animals and monsters are drinking, they tend to let their guard down, and if a threat approaches, they often flee immediately.

(I guess what Vihera’s hoping for is that a monster, annoyed at being interrupted while drinking, will attack us.)

Rei thought this as he watched Vihera nod in agreement at his words.

In fact, since Vihera seemed convinced by Rei’s words, his prediction was spot on.

“Anyone else have any objections? I won’t listen to anything you say later.”

After confirming that Vihera was on board, he asked the others, but no one seemed to object.

Elena had Mirage for mid-range attacks and wind magic for long-range, and in the worst-case scenario, she could use Sleipnir Boots to fight even enemies in the river.

Marina, who specializes in wind and water elemental magic, actually prefers moving along the river.

Byune isn’t the type to fight on the front lines; her main role is providing support from a distance. While it’s somewhat difficult to fight when the enemy is in the river, she still possesses effective means of attack.

As for Relieu… well, she’s a Rank A adventurer known as the “Gale,” and since she has a skill called “Wind Slash” that’s similar to Rei’s “Flying Slash,” she’ll have no problem.

Set and Yellow can fly, so that’s not an issue either. …Though Yellow doesn’t actually participate in combat.

(Actually, I suppose Sleipnir Boots are more necessary for Vihera than for me or Elena.)

I can’t say I don’t agree, but the greaves that come as a set with Vihera’s boots are also magic items capable of generating magical blades from the heels, and they play a significant role in Vihera’s fighting style.

For Vihera, who specializes in close combat, the blades on the heels of the greaves provide valuable offensive power. …Though, to be honest, her “Magic Palm” possesses far more devastating offensive power than those blades.

Furthermore, Sleipnir Boots—which allow one to walk on air—are the very kind of magic item that even non-adventurers would desperately want to get their hands on.

While not quite as rare as the Item Box, there is no doubt that it is an extremely rare item.

It’s not something you can just ask for and have immediately.

(If I could imbue the greaves with the effects of Sleipnir Boots, that would be the best solution.)

Rei heads upstream along the river, hoping that Sleipnir might appear.

Sleipnir, a monster in the form of an eight-legged horse, is extremely rare, partly because it can gallop through the sky.

But she clings to a faint—truly faint—hope that perhaps a Sleipnir might be lurking within this forest.

That said, the reason for this hope is simply that she encountered a horse-like monster called a Kelpie in the river, leading her to wonder, “If there’s a Kelpie, might there also be a Sleipnir?” It’s a hope so faint it barely qualifies as hope.

Still, as long as the possibility isn’t zero, there’s no harm in hoping.

“Alright, let’s get moving.”

With Rei’s words, the group began moving upstream again.

Along the way, they spotted a few deer—non-monster animals—but they didn’t make any move to hunt them.

It’s not that venison tastes bad, but when you consider the flavor of the meat itself, the meat of a magical monster is definitely tastier.

There was no need for Rei and the others to go out of their way to spend time hunting deer here.

“Kyuu…”

For some reason, only Yellow, perched on Set’s head, seemed disappointed that they couldn’t hunt the deer.

“Deer, huh. There must be plenty of other wildlife in this forest too—boars, foxes, raccoon dogs… maybe even bears, depending on the situation.”

“Judging by this forest, Rei’s words seem to be correct. But… why is a newly formed dungeon this vast? Looking at this forest, it’s hard to believe it was just created.”

At Elena’s words, Rei… no, everyone else turned their gaze to their surroundings.

In fact, this forest is undoubtedly larger than the cliff where the dungeon was located.

Does it connect to some other space from those stairs... or is the interior of the cliff simply vast, or is it something else entirely?

Rei didn’t know the reason, but he could still understand that this dungeon was no longer a newly formed one.

“Probably the correct answer is that the dungeon grew without anyone noticing, and for some reason, it just happened to connect to that cliff…”

“I suppose so. Of course, there are still so many mysteries surrounding dungeons, so it’s possible this dungeon was this size from the very beginning.”

“……At least, when I challenged a newly formed dungeon near Gilm before, it wasn’t this big.”

“Ah, the one in Gamelion.”

Rei’s words must have immediately brought back memories of that time. Marina flashed an amused smile.

Partly because the Gamelions had gathered inside that dungeon for some reason, the Gamelions that year were unable to line the pockets of adventurers as they usually did.

Even now, a large number of those Gamelions remain inside Rei’s Misty Ring, delighting Rei and the others’ palates.

As someone who lives in Gilm, Relieu also seemed to have many memories of Gamelion, and as they talked, they made their way upstream along the river.
