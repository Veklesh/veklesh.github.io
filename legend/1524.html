The day after Rei went to Mejougo... well, strictly speaking, since he'd fallen asleep near dawn, it should be called the same day, but for Rei, who considered the moment he woke up to be the next day, it was indeed the next day.

Anyway, since he'd fallen asleep around dawn, it was only natural that Rei woke up close to noon.

He got ready and headed straight to the dining hall, where a fair number of guests were already eating lunch.

...The reason the dining hall wasn't packed was largely because only lodging guests could use the Silver Fruit Inn's dining hall; unlike the Dusk Wheat inn, it wasn't open to the public.

Even so, the number of guests increased significantly in the morning and evening, but now it was noon... daytime.

The guests staying at the Silver Fruit Inn were also currently outside the inn, each going about their own work.

Therefore, they likely didn't need to return all the way to the Silver Fruit Inn just to eat lunch; they probably ate with those who were working.

But... precisely because there aren't that many people in the dining hall, the strangeness stands out.

One of the many tables in the dining hall.

Many of those currently in the dining hall, regardless of age or gender, were directing their attention to that table.

Even those who weren't looking directly couldn't help but keep their awareness on that table while eating.

(Well, I suppose it's understandable.)

Rei, entering the cafeteria, observed the scene around him and thought to himself with a sigh.

Naturally, the table drawing the gazes and attention of the other patrons was occupied by four people: Elena, Marina, Vihera, and Byune.

Rei wondered why Yellow wasn't there, but concluded he was probably still asleep or heading to meet Set.

As Rei made his way toward Elena's table, his presence couldn't help but draw attention. Naturally, the other patrons turned their eyes toward him.

Among those gazes were some that mocked him, implying that a kid like him could never possibly charm such beauties.

Given that Elena and her friends were all beauties and young beauties, it was only natural that if it were evening, some men would consider trying to chat them up.

Fortunately, however, no one in the current cafeteria was attempting such a thing.

That very fact made Rei's actions stand out all the more, making the mocking stares he received seem only natural.

But Rei ignored the stares around him as if it were natural and approached the table.

Elena and the others, eating their lunch—or perhaps a late breakfast—at the table, noticed Rei's approach.

The other customers watched with a hint of malicious curiosity, wondering how Rei would be rejected... but upon seeing him, Elena and the others each broke into smiles.

Only Byune continued eating without changing her expression, as usual.

However, that smile seemed more like one born from knowing Rei's secret.

Seeing the three of them wearing expressions that seemed somehow filled with confidence, Rei vaguely realized the reason for their smiles—the memory of Yellow that Elena must have told them—but he deliberately chose to ignore it for now.

"Rei, you slept in pretty late today, didn't you?"

"...Well, I went to bed late, so what can I say?"

Responding to Elena's teasing words, Rei sat down at the same table as Elena and the others without asking permission.

The expressions that appeared on the faces of the surrounding customers who witnessed this scene could only be described as astonishment.

They had expected the presumptuous sight of him trying to chat up the beauties only to be flatly rejected... but the outcome was the complete opposite, so their reactions were understandable.

Worse still, he was casually chatting with the beauties. It was impossible not to feel jealousy.

"Wait, is that face... female?"

"His voice is male, right? His demeanor is male too."

"...But that face, though?"

"Well, I get where you're coming from."

"Wow... it's rare to see a man that beautiful."

"Yeah. But he's not my type. Guys need to be dependable, after all."

Since it was a cafeteria, when they sat down, seeing Rei's face after he took off his Dragon Robe hood caused another small commotion.

Seeing his bare face, they probably couldn't tell if he was a man or a woman for a moment.

And the Dragon Robe hiding his body's contours definitely influenced that judgment.

"So, what are you doing today? You need to get this done as soon as possible, right?"

Rei thought about it after casually ordering food from the waiter, while Vihera reached for the basket of bread and asked.

"Right. Since we know where Mejougo is, I want to ride Set and fly over Mejougo first. Ideally, I'd like to get an aerial view of the entire place to see what it looks like."

After asking Marina to use spirit magic to mask their voices, he murmured this.

Hearing Rei's words, Elena and the others all nodded in agreement.

If they were going to settle the score with the organization called Jayah that had provoked Gilm, it was highly likely combat would occur in Mejougo, which was surely an important location within Jayah.

Therefore, it made perfect sense to investigate Mejougo's specific layout beforehand.

(Fortunately, Mejougo is an entertainment district. That means most of them should be asleep from midday until evening. Flying over the city then would likely go unnoticed... and I didn't see any signs of protective barriers either. Worst case, we could just land directly in Mejougo?)

That was Rei's initial thought, but he quickly reconsidered.

As evidenced by the slave collar he received from Sharia, it's best to assume Jaya possesses considerable alchemical prowess.

If that was the case, the possibility that some kind of special magic item, unknown to Rei, had been installed in Mejougo could not be ruled out.

Of course, for Rei, such magic items were undoubtedly things that would warm his pockets the more he had.

But this was a magic item.

As seen with the slave collar Sharia was given, it's entirely possible it possesses unexpected effects even for Rei.

Therefore, it cannot be definitively stated that there isn't a magic item capable of catching Rei off guard.

"Hmm. Well, for now, let's just leave Rei be... What about us?"

"Vihera, just so you know, I'm not getting involved in any fights."

Marina warned Vihera, who had come to discuss today's plans, as if hammering home her point.

But Vihera responded to Marina with a dissatisfied look.

"Well, I'm not going to pick fights with just anyone, you know. There don't seem to be that many strong opponents in this country anyway."

"No, that's not true."

Rei interjected, cutting off Vihera's words.

Vihera looked at Rei expectantly and spoke up.

"What? Did you find someone strong in Mejougo?"

"Yeah. Though I think they're stronger than the adventurers in this country, if it's strong enough to satisfy Vihera... Honestly, I'm not sure how strong they really are."

What flashed through Rei's mind was the orc-like woman he'd seen at Mejougo's headquarters.

There was no doubt she possessed strength unimaginable for this small country... but if asked whether that would satisfy Vihera, Rei couldn't help but tilt his head in doubt.

Vihera already possessed high combat ability, but absorbing Ambris had further increased her fighting power.

Truly, to satisfy Vihera from the bottom of her heart... it would take strength on the level of Rei, Elena, or Marina.

...Though, among the names mentioned just now, Marina's means of attack were ultimately limited to her bow and spirit magic. While that might suffice against ordinary opponents, facing a formidable foe like Vihera in close combat would be quite difficult.

"Hmm. ...But it sounds more interesting than just sitting around doing nothing here in Rossi."

Back in Gilm, Vihera could have mock battles with Elena and Rei at Marina's house—since it wasn't life-or-death—and that satisfied her to some extent. But she couldn't pull that sort of stunt here in Rossi.

No, this Silver Fruit Inn was one of Rossi's most luxurious lodgings, so naturally, there was space to move around.

But if Vihera were to exercise there, crowds would undoubtedly gather just to see her physique.

That could lead to all sorts of troublesome complications. More importantly, it would reveal exactly what kind of power Vihera possessed to those around her, someone whose name was barely known in Rossi.

Honestly, Rei wanted to keep the true abilities of people like Vihera and Marina—whose names hadn't spread this far into Rossi—hidden.

That was precisely why Rei had repeatedly warned Vihera—and the others—to avoid moving their bodies in conspicuous places.

That said, neither Elena nor Marina were as combative as Vihera.

As for Byune, honestly, if she didn't have to move, she'd rather stay still and eat.

Of course, if she didn't move her body for a long period, it would inevitably become sluggish, so this was strictly conditional on it being short-term.

"The problem is that thing being in Mejougo. ...We can't exactly take Vihera to Mejougo."

"Ah... you're right. If we tried something like that, it would definitely cause a huge commotion."

At Marina's words, everyone present... even Byune, who had been focused on eating, nodded.

Truthfully, Vihera's outfit was the kind that could easily be mistaken for a prostitute's.

If someone like that were in Mejougo, naturally, a large number of people wanting to buy Vihera would appear.

That would inevitably cause a commotion, which would reach the ears of Jaya's people... and there's no doubt their presence would be exposed.

"The final blow... If it's time to deliver a powerful strike to Jaya, then having Vihera in Mejougo might be acceptable... The problem is how long that will take."

They had been told to return as soon as possible, but that didn't mean they could force the situation.

If damage spread to completely unrelated areas, it would only worsen the Daskar's standing within Gilm... and the Neutral Faction's position as well.

Moreover, as evident from Elena's cooperation in this matter, the Noble Faction is also assisting.

If a major mistake were made here, it would mean Daskar would owe the Noble Faction a huge debt.

Retaliation must be swift, yet executed with the utmost precision to avoid error.

The situation is dire on many fronts, but it is precisely because it is so dire that Daskar entrusted this to Rei and the others.

"If only we had someone who knew more about the situation, things would be easier for us. ...Unfortunately, it's not that simple."

Elena murmured as she placed the spoon she'd been using to drink soup back into the bowl.

"Having someone like that would make things easier for us... but would such a person just come forward so easily?"

Considering the actions of the organization known as Jarya, it was certain that a significant number of people harbored grievances.

But just because they're dissatisfied doesn't mean they'd risk exposing it—that would surely lead to trouble.

Given that, there probably weren't many who openly showed their resentment toward Jaya.

Even if Rei and the others tried to contact such individuals, finding them would be extremely difficult if they were hiding.

If this were familiar territory like Gilm, we could ask an informant to handle it...

"Not being able to use informants is a real pain. ...Marina, can't you get the guild to introduce us to one?"

"No way. Well, they could probably introduce us to one, but whether that informant is truly trustworthy... that's a bit iffy. Didn't I explain that before?"

"...You did. But with no other leads, isn't that the quickest way? We should be prepared for some danger, but to get moving as soon as possible..."

Marina hesitated at Rei's words.

In truth, the guilds themselves weren't that powerful in the Kingdom of Reburina.

They do take on jobs like escorting people or hunting down thieves, so they have some capability, but it's nothing compared to a frontier like Gilm.

Given that, it wasn't impossible that someone had connections to Jaya, either because Jaya's eye was on them or because they sought profit by linking up with Jaya themselves.

But it was also true that things wouldn't progress if they stayed like this.

Considering all that... eventually, albeit reluctantly, Marina nodded in agreement.
