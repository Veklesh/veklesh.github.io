The first to notice Rei and the others was one of the researchers who had managed to keep some distance from the giants and was observing the outside from inside a building.

This place, similar in size to the space where the giants were sleeping, even contained several buildings.

It was undoubtedly the size of a small village.

Hiding within one of those buildings, a man in his twenties who was searching for a way to survive noticed a door that was slightly ajar.

But finding that alone was useless.

A giant stood blocking the doorway.

Giants possess no clear intelligence.

A giant... despite its appearance, which should be called that of a huge human, possessed only goblin-like intelligence... no, perhaps not even as much as a goblin.

Their intelligence was barely above that of wild animals.

Why is such a giant standing in front of the door?

Wild animals wouldn't do such a thing...

In any case, with that giant acting as a gatekeeper, escape was impossible. Consequently, most of us here were forced into a desperate battle.

Even in such a situation, the man was scanning his surroundings, thinking if there might be some way to escape...

(Who is that? It's definitely not the soldier who was at the gate. So, did the ones who went to the giant's nest come back? This quickly? Was there even such a small one there in the first place?)

The man hid himself so he couldn't be seen from outside the house, staring intently at that figure.

He didn't know why he was watching that figure so intently. He didn't know... but still, for some reason, he couldn't help but be curious.

...The giant walked, biting down on an arm he'd likely forcibly torn from a soldier outside the house. For better or worse, the man didn't notice this.

It was a display of that researcher-like concentration where focusing on one thing made everything else disappear.

Anyway, at the end of that man's gaze, the robed figure... Rei pulled a massive scythe out of nowhere.

With a swing of the scythe, the giant standing before the door had both knees severed in the next instant.

"Huh?"

For a moment, the man couldn't comprehend what had happened.

It was only natural. The man possessed deep knowledge of giants.

Their bodies were incredibly resilient, their bones even harder than their flesh.

There was no way such a seemingly casual blow could so easily sever both knees.

(No, even before that... where did he pull that scythe from? ...A scythe? Hey, wait a minute. Could it be...)

Only then did the man finally comprehend who stood before him.

That Crimson was here... a person who could pose a threat to Jaya. And that the Rank B party, Crimson Wings, had arrived in Rossi, the capital of the Kingdom of Reburina.

And the fact that Crimson possessed an Item Box was a rather well-known event.

If it was just the item box, a simplified version existed, albeit extremely expensive.

But the fact that he possessed a Great Scythe confirmed that the robed figure was indeed Rei.

A scythe looked like a very vicious weapon, but it was actually quite difficult to use effectively in combat.

Therefore, few wield such a weapon.

While some might be tempted to use it based on its appearance, mastering it in actual combat requires considerable skill.

In fact, using a standard longsword or spear would ultimately make one stronger than wielding a scythe.

For that very reason, the mere fact that someone wielded a scythe made it easy to guess who they were.

And the skill Rei displayed was truly worthy of that reputation.

No, it wasn't just Rei who demonstrated skill.

The four who entered right behind Rei, with the exception of the smallest one, also demonstrated more than sufficient strength.

Seeing the strength of Rei and his group, I judged that I might be saved.

Given that adventurers from Gilm had come to a place like this, it was clear they hadn't stumbled upon it by accident.

But still... it was true that being saved was better than being devoured by giants.

(Please, hold on. I need your strength to get out of here alive. If I survive, I'll do anything I can for you. So please, I'm counting on you.)

The man prayed desperately for Rei and the others to win, all for his own survival.

"Next!"

After severing the knee of the giant that had initially blocked the gate and then decapitating it to ensure its death, he launched an attack on the nearby giant.

Fortunately, the space beyond the door was quite vast, with several buildings.

With that much space, wielding the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear posed no problem whatsoever.

Thus, Rei was currently fighting the giants in his usual two-spear style, wielding the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left.

Of course, Rei wasn't the only one fighting. Elena, Marina, Vihera, and Byune were also engaged in battle.

"They're ferocious, but that just makes them easier to fight!"

Dodging a giant's hand reaching out to devour that soft flesh, Vihera used the claws extending from her gauntlets to slash the giant's neck.

Where the black crystal existed, the giants could heal even severe wounds effortlessly, but unfortunately, there was no black crystal here.

Then again, they didn't heal during the battle on the fourth basement floor either, so healing in a place this far away is probably impossible.

"There aren't that many enemies, either!"

Elena attacked the giant, swinging Mirage more like a hammer than a sword.

The whip-like Mirage functioned literally as a metal whip.

...The giant who took the hit screamed in pain.

"What are you doing? You shouldn't be eating that!"

An arrow fired from Marina's bow pierced the giant's body as it lunged toward a soldier.

Whether from the pain or simply the shock... either way, the giant stopped moving.

Another arrow shot struck the giant's hands, which had been holding the soldier back. The soldier, now free from the giant's grasp, glanced briefly at Marina before quickly moving away.

Naturally, such dramatic movement drew the attention of the soldiers—or rather, the researchers besides the soldiers—who had been fighting the giant or fleeing.

"Who are you people?! Reinforcements!?"

One soldier asks, his voice tinged with no small amount of hope.

Since these were faces they hadn't seen here before, it was certain they weren't just ordinary reinforcements.

Still, seeing them take down the giant that had overwhelmed them one after another, it was undeniable they possessed power beyond anything the soldiers could fathom.

Rather than foolishly antagonizing them here, cooperating—or more accurately, being unilaterally saved—and surviving this situation was the absolute priority. That was the obvious conclusion.

"Get out of the way, you dead weight!"

But even so, being met with this level of hostility... no, outright murderous intent, was completely unexpected.

Of course, we understood we were the villains.

Still, we thought this might be a situation where we could cooperate.

Then again, the soldiers had been thoroughly beaten by the giant horde.

Honestly, even if told to back off, it was hard enough to put up a front and refuse.

As a result, the soldier who had spoken to Vihera quietly retreated... moving away toward the side without giants.

If asked if it wasn't frustrating, he'd answer immediately that it was.

But if asked which was more important—life or getting back at Vihera—life was undoubtedly more important.

That soldier wasn't the only one who heard Vihera's voice.

The other soldiers, barely clinging to life, heard it too, as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

Vihera shot looks of contempt, scorn, and above all disgust at those soldiers, then launched his attack at the remaining giant.

Naturally, with Vihera and the soldiers—those nuisances—out of the way, the others intensified their assault on the giants.

Within less than ten minutes, every giant present had lost its life.

"Well, with this many giants, this is about what you'd expect."

Rei murmured as he watched the last one collapse to the ground, its skull crushed by Twilight Spear.

After confirming all the giants were dead, he looked around again.

(I've thought it several times before, but this place really is vast. How could such a large space exist deep within a cave... And giants are gathering on the other path, right? What's going on... Huh? A giant?)

Suddenly, Rei's gaze shifted to the giant corpse lying on the ground.

What lay before him... no, what was there, was a giant's corpse.

And at the end of the other path... a massive number of giants. Probably over a thousand.

What if this giant rampaging wasn't just a coincidence?

"What's wrong, Rei? Weren't we supposed to make contact over there?"

Rei turned his gaze toward Elena, who wore a puzzled expression.

There, he saw a serious expression.

Why so serious? Elena wondered, but before she could speak again, Rei opened his mouth.

"What if this giant's rampage isn't a coincidence at all... but something happening to all the giants?"

"Wh-what!?"

Shouts of surprise erupted not only from Elena, who had spoken to him, and Marina and the others who had gathered around Rei, but also from the soldiers.

It was only natural. About twenty Titans had killed roughly seventy percent... no, eighty percent of the soldiers present.

In such a situation, being told that nearly a thousand more Titans might be rampaging was enough to send shockwaves through anyone.

(Why did the Titans suddenly start rampaging in the first place?)

Yes, when Rei opened the door, the Titans were already rampaging.

Given that, the reason for their rampage remained completely unknown.

Could there be some other reason?

Clinging to a sliver of hope, he called out to a soldier standing a short distance away.

"Hey, what caused these giants to start rampaging? Did we do something weird? Tell me honestly."

The soldier's expression turned slightly displeased at being spoken to in a commanding tone.

But he understood exactly how much power Rei and the others possessed—he'd seen it with his own eyes.

They had effortlessly... truly effortlessly defeated the giants they themselves couldn't touch.

That meant it was best to avoid antagonizing Rei and his group if at all possible.

Calming himself, the soldier opened his mouth.

"No, we didn't do anything in particular. We really didn't do anything, yet it suddenly started rampaging. If there's a reason, I'd like to know it too."

"...I see."

He saw no trace of deception in the soldier's eyes. He was half-convinced the man was telling the truth.

If that was the case, there was no need to waste any more time here.

But if they just headed toward the giant like this, there was a high chance the people here would flee.

Considering that, going over there together wasn't an option.

(Well, what to do. ...Vihera would want to fight the giant, and Byune is out due to combat strength issues. That leaves Elena and Marina, or perhaps I need to stay here.)

After a brief pause, Rei spoke up.

"If a giant rampaged here, doesn't that mean giants might be rampaging at their gathering place too? And judging by this situation, all of them..."

"Nonsense!"

The researcher-like man shouted sharply at Rei's words.

Naturally, Rei didn't know the man, but he was the one who had been hiding inside the building, observing the giants.

He had come out after confirming all the giants were defeated, intending to hear the details from Rei and the others.

In that instant, he heard something impossible... no, something he absolutely refused to believe, causing him to reflexively shout.

"Who are you?"

"I'm a researcher studying the giants. ...And about the giants..."

The man tried to continue, but Rei thrust the tip of his Twilight Spear at him, forcibly cutting him off.

"Sorry, but I can't be friendly toward you. I'll be blunt. The black crystal breaking was probably what caused the giants to go berserk."

"What!?"

Caught off guard by Rei's sudden words, the man could only stare, eyes and mouth wide, unable to comprehend what was being said.
