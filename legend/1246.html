"...Hey, what do you think?"

After completing the formalities at the main gate and entering Gilm, Rei and the others were heading toward the usual spot at the Dusk Wheat inn.

They were traveling by carriage, but suddenly, several men surrounded them along the way.

Of course, the reason wasn't that they wanted to admire Set, who was traveling with the carriage... The long swords and spears the men held in their hands proved that much.

Furthermore, all of them wore masks, perhaps to hide their faces.

(But would they really attack so brazenly in the middle of town?)

What baffled Rei was that this wasn't some deserted back alley along the highway, but a main street.

It was simply not the kind of place where people would normally attack someone.

Yet, the masked men were currently surrounding the carriage Rei and the others were riding in.

"What do you think? Honestly, all I can say is... Are they out of their minds?"

Vihera gazed out the carriage window, murmuring with a hint of exasperation.

Gilm was large enough to be considered half a city, complete with slums.

In such a place, it wouldn't be strange for people to attack others even in broad daylight. But right now, Rei and the others were on a main street.

Indeed, as Rei looked out the window, he could see not only the masked attackers surrounding the carriage, but also others watching from a distance.

The guard squad would undoubtedly arrive before long.

Whether the attackers understood this or not, Rei couldn't tell.

"Come out here! I'll kill you!"

"You don't want this fine carriage wrecked, do you?! Get out here now!"

The loud, angry shouts came from outside the carriage.

Each man shouted while brandishing their weapons.

"...I agree with Vihera's opinion about sanity. Why would they attack us with Set here? Normally, it's unthinkable."

Elena murmured, gazing out the window.

Yes, by any normal standards, this could only be called abnormal.

Set was right beside the carriage.

To the residents of Gilm, he might be treated like a pet, but he was still a high-rank monster.

He possesses the strength to easily defeat the very foes currently surrounding the carriage.

Did those surrounding the carriage understand this, or not?

No, with Set right in front of them, it was impossible for them not to realize it.

Then why, in this situation... why can they be so bold, even with S-rank monster-equivalent Set present? Rei couldn't understand it at all.

If Set were to get even slightly serious, those men would lose their lives in a matter of seconds.

(Don't they want to live? ...But they don't seem reckless or resolved either.)

Even to Rei's eyes, the men weren't exactly skilled fighters.

If you think about it as adventurers, they're probably Rank E... or at best, Rank D.

Honestly, it's hard to believe they possess the strength to hold any real sway in Gilm.

"That's strange,"

Marina murmured, sharing the same question as Rei.

No, given her position as Guildmaster, Marina knew more about adventurers than Rei did.

Therefore, she should understand better than Rei that the current situation is abnormal.

"Hey! I said come out! Get your ass out here now, or I'll kill this horse too!"

The man shouted while swinging his spear, but Elena's lips curled into a sneer at his words.

The horse pulling this carriage had undergone rigorous training since it was a foal.

For men merely shouting around the carriage, it would be easy for the horse to turn on them.

"What shall we do, Elena-sama?"

The door connecting to the driver's seat opened slightly, and Ara asked Elena.

With Ara's skill, defeating the men currently surrounding the carriage would be easy.

But she hadn't done so because she didn't know what purpose the men had in surrounding them.

Normally, surrounding a carriage would naturally mean they were targeting someone or something inside.

But that was only under normal circumstances.

This wasn't the kind of thing that happened in a place like this—Gilm's... and on a main street, no less, where there were countless eyes watching all around.

That's precisely why acting rashly was unwise—the consequences were too uncertain. That's why he ended up asking Elena.

"...Right. If we wait here, the guards will probably arrive. If we make a rash move here, it could endanger the people watching from the sidelines."

"So, we wait and see for a while?"

"Yes, please do. However, if they actually attack us, you have permission to fight back. ...Rei, is Set okay?"

"They seem fine."

Rei replied to Elena's words while watching the situation outside the window.

Outside, Set stood guard beside the carriage.

Even if the men attacked, he could likely blow them away with a single swipe of his front leg.

But perhaps sensing something off about the situation, he showed no sign of moving for now.

The men surrounding the carriage could only speak so boldly precisely because Set remained still.

Yet, they only shouted threats without actually attacking. Perhaps they didn't want to cause a major disturbance.

Or perhaps causing this disturbance itself is their objective.

"What the hell is their goal?"

"I don't know?"

Marina tilted her head at Rei's muttered words, spoken as if in surprise... At that moment, the situation shifted.

"Stop it! Don't bully Set!"

From the crowd watching the commotion, a child—a child who looked to be only five or so—dashed out.

The child didn't see the carriage Rei and the others were riding in.

All he saw was his friend Set being bullied by masked adults.

That's why he couldn't stand by and had to break through the crowd to save his friend.

It was undoubtedly brave...

But it was also undeniably reckless.

"What the hell, kid... Hey! Get out of the way!"

One of the masked men shouted as he kicked at the child charging toward them.

No, it wasn't really a kick with any real force behind it.

It was more accurately just a violent swing of the leg.

Even so, an adult's strength was no joke for a five-year-old.

Especially when the men were clearly no strangers to rough work, as evidenced by the weapons they carried.

"Whoa!"

The child collided with the man's leg and was sent flying, tumbling across the ground.

Screams rose from the crowd watching nearby.

They probably never imagined such a thing would happen on a busy street like this.

Some might have even thought it was some kind of performance or trick.

Feeling pleased with the reaction, the man who kicked the child opened his mouth.

"Hah! What the hell... huh?"

He was about to yell at the people around him.

But what he saw the next instant was the sight of Set, who should have been beside the carriage, somehow standing right before him.

And not just standing there, but with his front leg raised, poised to strike.

For a moment, the man couldn't comprehend what had happened... but the next instant, he was slammed to the ground with a violent impact and lost consciousness.

Fortunately for him, Set had no intention of killing the man.

Though that was purely because he didn't want to cause a commotion, not out of any concern for the man's life.

Normally, taking a direct hit from Set would easily turn a head or upper body into a pile of flesh.

But the man who kicked the child was unconscious from the impact of being slammed into the ground. Seeing his limbs twitch slightly, it was clear he was still alive.

"What!? Damn it! He did it!"

Seeing his comrade unconscious, another man raised his longsword, ready to attack Set...

"That's enough."

Rei, seeing the situation shift due to Set, leaped out of the carriage.

Deciding weapons weren't necessary, he swung a punch into the gut of the man who had been heading towards Set.

In terms of raw physical strength, Rei was inhuman, despite his appearance.

That fist literally sank into the man's stomach... through his leather armor, and he collapsed to the ground, unconscious and unable to utter a word.

The other men surrounding the carriage must have noticed their comrade had been knocked out so easily. Rather than attacking all at once, they cautiously watched for Rei's next move. ...But that was a mistake.

If Rei had been the only opponent, that might have been acceptable.

But Elena and the others were also present.

The carriage door wasn't large enough for them all to burst out at once, but still, within seconds of Rei emerging, Elena, Marina, and Vihera appeared.

And then...

"Guh!"

Ara on the driver's seat moved.

Though they had been observing until now, seeing Rei move, they likely judged it best to act themselves.

The men, who had been focused solely on Rei... or more accurately, on Set, were suddenly attacked from a direction they never expected.

Still, considering they were inside Gilm, it was only natural that Ara attacked with bare hands rather than his power axe, judging by the looks on the surrounding people's faces.

Adventurers might be accustomed to human blood, but this was Gilm... and the main street at that.

Many ordinary people present were completely unaccustomed to such things.

What would happen if someone split a torso cleanly in half vertically, or horizontally, or worse, shattered an upper body and scattered flesh fragments everywhere in such a place? It didn't even need to be considered.

"Gah!"

"Gyah!"

"Bwah!"

Perhaps Elena and the others understood this too, for they only knocked people unconscious rather than killing them.

The sight must have amused those watching nearby. Each of them cheered.

...Of course. Who would find it entertaining to watch someone kick around children?

In fact, among the onlookers were adventurers, and even they launched attacks at the masked men who had been about to assault Rei and the others.

Of course, they weren't wielding their longswords to slash; they fought by striking with their blades still sheathed.

Rei and the others launched their attack all at once, and what's more, other adventurers nearby joined in to help fight... Once that happened, the battle was settled extremely quickly.

It ended in what could only be described as the blink of an eye. The masked men lay unconscious on the ground.

"...So, Rei. Who do you think these guys are?"

"Even if you ask me... Can't you guess, Marina?"

Asked by Vihera, Rei turned to Marina nearby.

Though Marina primarily wielded spirit magic and a bow, she was also exceptionally skilled in unarmed combat, as befitted a former Rank A adventurer.

She certainly wouldn't be outmatched by opponents like the unconscious men before them now.

"I can't imagine any adventurer in Gilm who'd mess with Rei. With Set here, it's obvious that whoever's in this carriage is either Rei or someone connected to him."

"Grr..."

"Ahahaha, it's okay. See? He's fine now."

The child who had been blown away by the masked man earlier let out a happy cry as Set rubbed his face against him.

The child playing with Set had sustained a few scrapes from rolling on the ground, but nothing serious.

Rather, the child seemed delighted to be interacting with Set, letting out happy squeals.

Among those watching this scene, there were naturally some who could be called Set's admirers.

Those people watched the child playing with Set with envy... but they didn't interfere.

That child could play with Set to their heart's content because they were the first to go help him.

They themselves, despite making various excuses, hadn't been able to take that action.

Considering those circumstances, this was a natural reward.

Of course, if asked whether they were envious, they would answer that they were extremely envious.

Some among those who feel this way find relief that Milein and Johanna—the two names most often mentioned as Set enthusiasts—are not present when he is surrounded by masked men.

...Because if those two had been here, it might have ended in bloodshed.

Watching the guards rush over, he thought this from the bottom of his heart.
