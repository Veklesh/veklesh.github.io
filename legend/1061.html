Emerging from the forest, Rei and the others arrived before the settlement to find an unexpected sight.

By the light of the bonfires lay corpses, corpses, corpses. ...A scene that could truly be called a mountain of corpses.

Yet, the reason Rei and his companions did not grieve was likely because every single corpse belonged to monsters they had fought countless times in the forest: goblins, kobolds, orcs.

There were also corpses of several other monsters Rei had never seen before. Upon seeing them, Rei wore a look of disappointment.

The Death Scythes held in the hands of Set and Rei, who had been brought into this world through Beast Magic, grew by absorbing the magic stones of monsters.

However, there was a constraint: either Rei or Set had to participate in combat against a monster possessing a magic stone to synchronize their magical wavelengths with it.

Synchronizing magic wavelengths didn't require anything particularly special, but participating in the battle itself was essential.

In other words, they couldn't simply accept a guild request to deliver magic stones, easily obtain them, and use them for growth...

(That wolf with three horns... Oh, and that monkey with four hands over there—first time seeing one of those. And is that water? Ah, a slime. But a black slime? Never seen one before. ...I only managed to defeat one unknown monster, but over here...)

Rei felt a twinge of envy, but now wasn't the time for that. He approached the group of Dark Elves standing near the barrier's protective circle.

Optis and Marina were among them, so it was clear they were commanding the forces here.

(Optis, for her part, was probably participating in the battle despite her age.)

Watching the tip of Optis' staff stained with blood, Rei opened his mouth.

"The four children, including Lugdos. I brought them back safely. Set."

"Guruu."

At Rei's call, Set stepped forward, carrying the four children on his back.

Marina's expression twisted into relief at the sight.

No, Marina wasn't the only one relieved. All the dark elves gathered there wore expressions of relief.

"Lugdos!"

"Ian!"

"Karuja!"

"Myrth!"

Each of them must be one of the four families. Calling out their names, they approached Rei.

Normally, they might be afraid of the griffin, but now their minds are focused solely on their children. They approach without any concern for Set.

They embraced the three on Set's back, scolded them, and rejoiced. ...Yes, the three of them.

"Lugdos! What on earth happened to Lugdos!?"

The dark elf, presumably Lugdos's mother, screamed upon seeing the unconscious Lugdos.

Blood was flowing from his head, and he was unconscious—it was only natural.

Rei addressed the distraught mother.

"Looks like he shielded those three over there and got hit by a goblin with a club or something. I thought about healing him with a potion, but since he was hit in the head... I wasn't sure if it was okay to heal him without permission, so I left him be. It's probably best to have someone with detailed knowledge examine him before healing him."

"...Thank you."

Her eyes had been fixed only on Lugdos. The mother finally noticed Rei and gasped for a moment.

This mother, possessing the ability to sense magic, had been present when Rei removed the New Moon Ring.

That's why she knew exactly how much magic power Rei actually possessed, and she even felt a sense of awe.

But... even so, she wasn't so impolite as to not express gratitude to the one who had saved her son.

"Don't worry about it. I did it because Marina asked me to. ...More importantly, you should hurry back to the settlement and get treated."

At Rei's words, the mother and the father standing nearby bowed deeply, then carried Lugdos back into the settlement.

Watching them go... Ian, Karuja, and Myrus, the three family members, suddenly approached Rei and began expressing their gratitude.

"Thank you. Because of you, Karuja..."

"Please allow us to thank you for Ian as well."

"Thank you for saving little Myrth. If anything had happened to this child, I truly don't know what I would have done..."

As the expressions of gratitude came one after another, Rei found himself slightly unsure how to respond.

Rei wasn't used to receiving thanks from others in the first place.

He hesitated for a moment, unsure what to say... then let out a small sigh before speaking.

"Don't worry about it. Like I said before, I only took this on because Marina asked me to. Besides... I got something out of it too."

Hearing Rei's words, everyone around him wore puzzled expressions.

But Rei's words were absolutely true.

His greatest gain this time was obtaining the magic stone from the owl-type monster, Del Troy, which he had encountered for the first time.

He'd also been able to use his enhanced Flying Slash multiple times, and while Pain Burst had seen less use, he'd still had opportunities to deploy it. It wasn't perfect, but he was starting to get the hang of it to some degree.

(And above all, we managed to rescue the children safely. ...Well, considering Lugdos' injury, it's not like everyone was completely unharmed, but still, it was definitely fortunate they weren't devoured by goblins.)

In Rei's mind, the final tally was positive, so this incident was settled.

Because of that, being praised so lavishly felt a bit awkward.

"Oh, the others are back too."

It probably wasn't meant to cover for Rei's discomfort, but still, one of the dark elves suddenly spoke up.

As those present turned their gaze toward the forest, several dark elves appeared.

Most had empty quivers, and many bore injuries of some sort.

Still, none had injuries that could be called fatal, and the dark elves near the settlement breathed a sigh of relief.

As Rei watched them approach, carrying potions, drinks, and light food, he suddenly sensed someone drawing near and turned his gaze that way.

There stood Marina, dressed as usual in a party dress with a deep neckline and open back. She smiled and spoke to Rei.

"Yes, good work. ...Thanks to you, Rei, we were able to rescue those children safely. ...Thank you."

"No, I—"

Just as Rei opened his mouth to speak, the hem of his Dragon Robe was suddenly tugged.

Looking down, Rei saw Myrth, who should have been clinging to his parents just moments ago, now grabbing onto the Dragon Robe.

"Big bro, big bro!"

"Ah... yeah. What is it?"

"Daddy and Mommy said they're taking me—"

"Me?" Rei tilted his head at Myrs' words, but when he looked back at Myrs' parents, they bowed deeply once more, just as they had before.

(Ore... Ah, thanks.)

Seeing this, Rei finally understood what he meant and simply gave a small nod in response to Myrth's parents.

Then, gently pushing Myrs' back, who was still tugging at the hem of his Dragon Robe, he guided him toward his parents.

"There, go ahead and spoil yourself with Dad and Mom today."

He swallowed the words "though you might get scolded" and said this. Mylus, of course, couldn't possibly grasp Rei's true meaning. With a smile that made anyone watching feel happy, he ran off toward his parents.

"You're quite used to dealing with children, aren't you?"

"...Is that so?"

Marina murmured in surprise, and Rei tilted his head.

He didn't think he was particularly skilled at handling children, let alone good at it.

But to Marina's eyes, Rei seemed perfectly adept at handling children.

This was purely a difference in how each perceived things.

"Anyway... thank you for helping the children of this village. This matter wasn't related to the World Tree's request, yet you still helped them. I'm grateful."

Marina's expression, showing pure gratitude rather than her usual seductive smile, made Rei look slightly surprised before he spoke.

"Don't worry about it. Since it was a request from Marina, something like this is no big deal."

"Oh, Rei. Is it really okay for you to say that? You might have forgotten, but I'm the Guildmaster of Gilm. If you're willing to do anything I ask, I might end up making all sorts of unreasonable requests, you know?"

Marina's mischievous smile, which made one wonder what had become of the one she'd worn just moments before, finally brought Rei back to his senses, and he smiled back.

"This time is special. If you want to make a request, prepare a fitting reward."

"...Very well. When I do ask for something, I'll make sure to prepare the finest reward. One truly unparalleled in this world... you understand?"

That smile, radiating feminine allure, drew the eyes of many watching Marina and Rei from the surrounding area.

Seeing husbands and lovers mesmerized by Marina, angry women stomped their feet and pinched their bodies, resulting in a mass production of screams.

"Hmm. This is unexpectedly... well, perhaps not so bad. It might make for a suitable playmate for me."

Optis watched Rei and Marina's exchange, a pleased smile spreading across his face.

To Optis, Rei was quite an interesting opponent.

No, in terms of keeping him company, he was arguably the best choice as a son-in-law.

But he immediately remembered Rei was human and shook his head slightly.

(The lifespan difference between a Dark Elf and a human is too great. Especially Marina, who carries the bloodline of her clan more strongly. Even if they were together, in the end, Marina would only be left grieving...)

Precisely because Optis didn't know what kind of person Rei was, if he truly wished for his granddaughter's happiness, a temporary relationship might be tolerable, but marriage was absolutely unacceptable.

Yet she couldn't bring herself to speak harshly to Marina. Seeing Rei, she sensed something.

Outwardly, he was unmistakably human, yet the magic power dwelling within him was utterly unlike anything human.

That unnatural quality meant even Optis couldn't definitively call Rei just a human.

(Well, possessing that much magic... He probably won't die after a few decades like ordinary humans. So, there's no need to decide right now what kind of being Rei truly is. Besides...)

Optis turned his gaze to his beloved granddaughter and saw Marina's face as a woman.

Having lived for so long, Optis knew full well that saying anything here would likely lead to nothing good.

In stark contrast to Optis, however, there were many men glaring at Rei with obvious resentment.

Rei possessed immense magical power, had temporarily restored the World Tree, ventured into the forest to search for a missing child, and ultimately rescued four children.

With such achievements, he could easily be called the savior of this settlement.

But... even so, the fact that Marina, an incredibly attractive woman, was close to Rei was something that, as men, they simply couldn't help but feel jealous of.

Some had known Marina before she left the settlement, while others were born after she departed.

Marina drew the attention of all these men, yet she herself seemed completely unconcerned.

She understood she was more beautiful than most, and had been courted countless times since her adventurer days.

Some had even tried to force themselves on her, but she had rebuffed them all.

Now, mere glances from men didn't stir any particular feelings in her.

...Though, if those gazes held hostility or murderous intent, that would be a different story.

Ignoring the gaze directed at her, Marina spoke up.

"Well, the children are safe now. Those with nothing to do should return to the settlement. I'll wait here for the others still in the forest to come back."

"Then I'll wait too."

Marina smiled at Rei, who spoke while stroking Set.

"Oh, is that alright?"

"Yeah. Elena and Vihera haven't come back yet either. The others too."

Hearing Rei say this, the surrounding dark elf men shot him envious glances... yet, perhaps knowing they'd only be in the way if they stayed any longer, they turned and headed back to the settlement.

And so, Rei waited at the entrance to the settlement for Marina, Set, Optis, and the others, including Elena, to return.
