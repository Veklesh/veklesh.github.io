"Waaah, waaah!"

“...What is this little guy?”

After finishing their meal of various fish dishes at the Full Belly Inn, Rei and Kenny stepped outside, but just then, an owl appeared with a screech and perched on Rei’s shoulder.

Kenny gave the owl a suspicious look, but Rei recognized it and wasn’t particularly surprised.

This was especially true since, on their way to the Full Belly Inn, they had witnessed a person connected to this owl engaged in negotiations.

“What happened to your master?”

“Gyaa?”

Rei asked the owl perched on his shoulder, but unlike Set, he had no way of knowing what the owl was trying to say.

However, just by looking at the owl’s demeanor, he could immediately tell that it had no hostile intentions toward him.

“Vivian, where on earth did you go!?”

As that voice echoed around them, the owl’s owner—Bistol—appeared almost immediately.

“So, Vivian, huh. …Is that the name you’ve been given?”

“Eek!”

Whether she was happy to have been given the name Vivian or not, Rei couldn’t tell from her cry, but she eventually waved her arms vigorously at Bistol, who had appeared from down the road.

“Hey, Bistol. Over here!”

“Oh my, Rei-chan? My Vivian-chan must have caused you some trouble, hasn’t she?”

“Huh!?”

It was Rei, who had been watching the situation unfold beside Rei, who gasped when he saw the person Rei had called out to—Bistol.

Working as a receptionist at the guild, she often saw all sorts of unusual people.

After all, many adventurers go out of their way to make themselves stand out.

……That said, those who stand out without any real ability usually realize it quickly and go back to dressing normally, while those who can’t do that tend to disappear from Gilm—or even this world—before long.

Even Kenny, who was used to seeing such attention-seeking types, was surprised by Bistol’s appearance… and above all, by his mannerisms.

From the mouth of a massive, muscular man came a distinctly feminine way of speaking.

And when you saw his mannerisms, which also had a vaguely feminine air to them, it was far beyond what one would normally tolerate.

Of course, that’s only from a “normal” perspective; Kenny, who deals with all sorts of people daily as the guild’s receptionist, quickly snapped back to reality.

“Rei. Who’s that over there?”

“Hm? Oh, this is Bistol. He came with me when I went to the sea. And on the way back, while we were taking shelter from the rain, we rescued this owl… was it Vivian?

“Well, well, Rei-chan is quite the character, isn’t he? I never would have guessed there was a good person like him besides those three.”

With those words, Kenny’s impression of Bistol completely changed.

“Hehe, I’m flattered to hear you say that, but unfortunately, Rei and I aren’t in that kind of relationship yet.”

With a formal demeanor, Kenny addressed Bistol.

If anyone were watching Kenny as he usually is, they’d be surprised by his choice of words right now.

That said, Kenny isn’t exactly the type to be casual with just anyone.

In this case, since he was dealing with someone Rei knew—someone he didn’t quite understand—he probably adopted this attitude.

“Oh my, is that so? Rei-chan, you’re really slow on the draw, aren’t you?”

Rei had been teased for being quick on his feet before, but he’d never been told he was slow. He hesitated a moment, unsure how to respond to Bistol’s remark… but deciding that continuing the conversation would undoubtedly lead to trouble for him, he changed the subject.

“Looks like your fish negotiations are going well over there. Did you make a decent profit?”

“That’s right. Thanks to you, Rei-chan, there’s no doubt we made a pretty good profit. How about I treat you to something as a thank-you?”

Bisturu, of course, knew exactly how much Rei could eat.

After all, she’d cooked for him countless times while they were at sea, so she’d inevitably come to understand the situation, whether she liked it or not.

Since Bistoll was offering to treat Rei, there was no need to wonder how much profit he’d made from this deal.

For Bistol, this was fish obtained with almost no necessary expenses… and even if it wasn’t exactly fresh, it was a preserved food—dried overnight—that was as close to fresh as possible.

It’s fish of a value that’s incomparable to the salted fish that comes into Gilm.

Moreover, thanks to Bistol’s overwhelming presence and his own business acumen, they were able to make the deal at a fair price without being ripped off.

Precisely because he was able to make such a deal, Bistol was deeply grateful to Rei for taking him out to sea.

“Hmm, but I just finished eating.”

“Oh, really? …Ah, a date, huh?”

Seeing Kenny, Bistol muttered this, but Kenny, upon hearing it, gave a slightly embarrassed smile.

Logically speaking, since a man and a woman had eaten together, calling it a date wouldn’t be wrong.

But… perhaps it’s a shame to say this, but the place where Rei and Kenny had eaten wasn’t some atmospheric restaurant, but rather a working-class diner where laborers gathered.

Of course, all the dishes served were delicious, but Kenny’s honest impression was that it wasn’t so much a date as it was a tasting event.

That said, he was certainly happy to have had the chance to eat with Rei.

In fact, considering it was a more unusual experience than a typical date, he even thought it was a good thing.

Seeing Kenny like that, Bistol held out his hand to Vivian.

“Come on, Vivian. Come over here. We’re about to leave, so don’t bother Rei.”

Vivian, who had been perched on Rei’s shoulder, obediently moved when she heard Bistol’s words.

“Huh… So you decided to go with the ‘tamer’ approach after all?”

Just by looking at the collar Vivian was wearing, it was immediately clear what that meant.

After all, Set and Yellow were wearing similar ones.

“Yes. It seems this little one has taken a liking to me, too.”

Bistol, smiling happily, even seemed to be brimming with maternal love.

...Though, to be honest, seeing a burly, muscular man radiating maternal love does feel a bit off in many ways.

“I see. Well then, do your best. It’s about time another big business opportunity came up in Gilm.”

What exactly did he mean by “business opportunity”?

Bistol, who was by no means incompetent as a merchant… and Kenny, the guild receptionist, immediately understood what Rei was talking about.

“The Gamelion season is almost here, after all.”

Yes, Gamelion—a seasonal staple and a signature ingredient of autumn in Gilm.

It’s not that Gamelions don’t live outside the Gilm area; there are places in other regions where you can commonly see them.

But… due to its size and ferocity, a Gamelion possesses enough strength that it wouldn’t be unusual for several adventurers to be hired to hunt it down.

The fact that they’re treated as ordinary ingredients is likely because this is Gilm, a place where skilled adventurers gather.

In any case, for merchants coming to Gilm, Gamelions are a highly sought-after commodity as both raw materials and food ingredients.

As for food, transporting raw Gamelions over long distances is difficult without special magical items like refrigerators, but even as dried meat, their deliciousness, rarity, and above all, the fact that they are a seasonal delicacy, ensure a substantial profit.

As a merchant himself, Bistol certainly wouldn’t let such a golden business opportunity slip by.

...As Rei was talking with Bistol, he noticed a gloomy expression on Kenny’s face.

Bistol noticed it too, but considering the relationship between Rei and Kenny, he decided it would be best not to interfere. Instead, he smiled and spoke up.

“Well then, I’m going to get back to work. My next client is waiting. …Hey, Rei. You should really take some time to talk to that girl, don’t you think?”

“Huh?”

Kenny, who had been suddenly addressed like that, let out a cry of surprise.

He hadn’t the slightest idea he’d been wearing such an expression.

Still, he quickly regained his composure and gave Bistol a grateful smile.

Being able to talk with Rei at his leisure right here wasn’t a bad thing for Kenny at all… in fact, it was something he should welcome.

“See ya.”

Saying this with a smile, Bistol took Vivian and walked away.

Neither Rei nor Kenny knew for sure if there really was a business meeting coming up, but they both immediately understood that Bistol had been thoughtful.

“Why don’t we buy some fruit juice over there and talk in the shade?”

Rei said this as he looked at a food stall a short distance away, and Kenny nodded in agreement.

“Gamelion is a delicious monster in many ways. I won’t deny that. But that’s only true for adventurers in Gilm.”

Having said that, Kenny took a sip of the fruit juice but frowned slightly at its warmth.

However, the reason Rei’s fruit juice was cold was simply because he’d taken the juice he’d bought in the summer and left it outside in the winter to chill… or so it seemed.

After all, there aren’t many shops that own expensive magic items like refrigerators.

“I see. So, does that mean the adventurers who’ve come to Gilm for the expansion project are also heading out to hunt Gamelions?”

“Yes. Of course, adventurers are basically responsible for themselves. So even if someone insists on going Gamelion hunting, the Guild can’t force them to stop. But…”

Kenny cut himself off mid-sentence, but Rei understood what he was getting at.

In other words, she was likely thinking about the many adventurers who would challenge Gamelions without the necessary skills, get injured… or, in the worst case, die.

“Actually, if things turned out like they did before—when a Gamelion was found in some dungeon—maybe there’d be fewer casualties this year.”

“Coming from Rei—the guy who single-handedly took down almost all the Gamelions that had infiltrated that dungeon—that doesn’t sound very convincing. Or rather, maybe it is convincing because you’d make a killing if that happened.”

She must have remembered what happened before. Kenny shot Rei a sidelong glance.

That said, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered by it and just kept drinking his fruit juice.

“But if that happens, I think fewer adventurers will get killed by Gamelion, don’t you?”

“That’s certainly true… but I never would’ve thought another dungeon would appear, and right near Gilm, no less… That’s impossible, right?”

“I don’t know. All sorts of strange things have been happening in Gilm lately.”

Take the new dungeon, for instance, or the Forest of Treants.

That said, since this is the frontier, these are the kinds of anomalies we just have to accept.

“True. But I can’t use that as an excuse to stop adventurers. No, if they say they’re acting as adventurers precisely because of these anomalies, then I have to accept that.”

To Kenny, adventurers are basically nothing more than customers.

And they’re the kind of customers who often act rudely.

That said, if you were to ask whether he didn’t care if those customers died, the answer is, of course, no.

Given that, it was only natural for him to want to do something about the adventurers who were heading off to their deaths to obtain Gamelion.

“Stop the adventurers, you say? What about lowering the purchase price for Gamelion?”

“No good. Even if the price drops at the Guild, there are plenty of other places to sell them.”

Basically, adventurers have the freedom to choose where to sell the materials they obtain.

In Rei’s case, he sells everything to the Guild because he finds haggling a hassle, but adventurers who are good at negotiating, or those who have exclusive merchants willing to pay top dollar, don’t sell their materials to the Guild.

Of course, selling materials outside the Guild means there’s a chance they’ll be haggled down to a lower price, depending on the negotiation.

Still, as Rei said, if the Guild were to offer a low price for Gamelion, adventurers would undoubtedly choose to sell to other merchants rather than the Guild.

Moreover, many adventurers would view the Guild with suspicion if it were to intentionally buy materials at low prices.

Since it’s the only guild in this frontier town, the adventurers probably wouldn’t go so far as to openly oppose it… but even so, tensions would undoubtedly rise, and it was certain that all sorts of trouble would arise later on.

“If that happens… I can’t quite think of a solution.”

“Really? Well, I suppose the guild is in a bind too.”

Saying that, Kenny offered a troubled smile.
