Upon entering Viscount Gale's mansion, it was only natural that Set could not enter.

Nevertheless, the guards showed no fear toward Set, so Rei left Set in their care and entered the manor.

The maid led him not to an office like at Daskar's, but to a reception room.

The moment Rei entered the room, a man seated on the sofa stood up with a delighted smile.

Near that man stood figures who appeared to be bodyguards.

This wasn't necessarily because they were wary of Rei; it was probably just standard procedure.

Seeing the guard give Rei a small bow, Rei could have guessed as much.

"Hey, Rei. Glad you could make it."

Late twenties to early thirties.

As nobility go, he wasn't too young, nor was he particularly old... or so one might say.

He was a bit on the heavy side, but not so much that it hindered his movements.

That man greeted Rei with a broad smile.

This man was undoubtedly Viscount Gale, and Rei bowed his head.

"I apologize for taking up your time."

"Not at all. Thanks to Rei, we suffered almost no casualties in the war against the Bestir Empire. Given that, you're practically my benefactor. ...Now, please sit down."

Prompted by Gale, Rei sat down on the sofa and immediately pulled a letter from his Misty Ring.

Unlike the letter of introduction from Daskar he had given the guard earlier, this one contained detailed information about the recent incident.

"It's from Lord Daskar."

"Hmm."

Gail took the letter from the envelope and began reading it immediately.

As Rei watched this scene, the maid who had earlier guided her into this reception room prepared tea and placed it before her.

After a slight bow, the maid exited the room.

Glancing at Gale reading the letter, Rei drank his tea.

The guards said nothing either, and silence filled the reception room.

A little time passed... and when Gale finally finished reading the letter, she sighed.

A sigh that could only be described as bitter.

"So... that's how it is. ...To do such a thing. I don't know what possessed you to reach out to Gilm... no, to be precise, I do know, but I refuse to accept it."

Even with the backing of the Noble Faction, attempting to lay hands on Gilm—whether that backing would prove useful afterward was, most likely, no.

For the Noble Faction, Lerdact is clearly not an entity worth protecting at the cost of openly antagonizing the Neutral Faction.

Of course, among the Noble Faction, there are those with an excessive pride in their bloodline who would insist on protecting Lerdact even if it meant clashing head-on with the Neutral Faction.

But such a move would only benefit the unscathed Royal Faction.

As for whether Duke Kerebel would accept that outcome... he likely would not.

"...So, this letter says Rei has been entrusted with retaliating against the Earl of Lerdact. What do you intend to do about it?"

"I have a general idea, but I thought I'd gather some information on that front myself."

"I see. The letter did mention wanting information, didn't it? ...Alright, what do you want to know?"

"Specifically, if you could tell us where the property is located within the Earl of Lerdact's domain... particularly any magic items, that would be helpful to us."

Hearing the term "magic items," Gale nodded in understanding.

While it wasn't widely known that Rei had a hobby of collecting magic items, it wasn't surprising for someone reasonably well-informed to know about it.

Therefore, there was no way Gail, belonging to the Neutral Faction, wouldn't know about it.

And the fact that Rei was asking for this kind of information made his intentions clear.

Magic items ranged from commonplace ones like lanterns and fire starters sold in shops, to rare items like Rei's Misty Ring, of which only a handful existed in the world.

And what Rei was asking about now was clearly the latter.

"When you ask where... the first place that comes to mind is definitely the Earl of Lerdact's mansion. As for somewhere within the mansion itself, I'm afraid I don't know."

That answer was more or less what Rei had expected.

After all, the Neutral Faction and the Noble Faction had been long-standing rivals.

Given that, it would be strange for Lerdact to know where the treasure was hidden within his own mansion.

...Though it was possible the Lerdact side might deliberately spread false information.

"So, we need to sneak in first, huh?"

"Ah... Rei. Just a heads-up, but there's a very real possibility the magic items there aren't worth the effort it'll take to get them."

Lerdact certainly doesn't hold a high position within the Noble Faction.

Even Gale, whose territory borders his, knows that.

He's not exactly eating cold rice, but his abilities are probably only on par with the masses.

That's precisely why, if asked whether he possessed any magic items Rei might covet, one couldn't help but tilt one's head in doubt.

Of course, for ordinary adventurers, there are plenty of magic items they'd want no matter what.

But in Rei's case, he fundamentally wants magic items he can actually use.

He has no interest in magic items that are merely for show.

Even so, since acquiring magic items was just a side goal this time—something nice to have if possible—Rei nodded in agreement with Gale's words.

"Yeah, that's possible. But this time, that's just a side thing. Whatever it is, losing a magic item is a big loss for them."

"Ah... well, that's..."

Rather, the fact that the treasured magic item stolen from him was something the thief had no particular interest in.

Knowing that, while there was damage from the theft itself, the psychological shock to Relduct would likely be even greater.

(I think doing something like that would just make Rei the target of resentment... but in Rei's case, he has the power to casually deflect whatever the other side throws at him.)

Rei had already demonstrated just how much power he possessed during the war with the Bestir Empire.

Given that he possesses enough power to hold his own against an entire army, Lerdact, who lacks significant presence even among the Noble Faction, would be utterly helpless against Rei.

(Besides...)

Being part of the Neutral Faction, Gale understood how significantly Rei had contributed during the Bestir Empire's civil war.

Not only in the war, but also in the civil war that erupted within the Bestir Empire afterward, and through various other commissions he's undertaken, he's made a name for himself.

It was plain to see that he possessed the strength befitting an adventurer with a nickname.

(Thinking about it that way, even if Count Lerdact used every ounce of his power to challenge Rei, the only outcome I can see is defeat. Though... if that happened, it would undoubtedly cause a huge uproar.)

If a noble were to challenge a single adventurer, using every ounce of power at their disposal... and then suffered a crushing defeat, it would undoubtedly cause a major uproar for both the nobles and the adventurers.

For the noble, it wouldn't be unreasonable to feel their honor had been sullied. For the adventurer, it wouldn't be unreasonable to feel the noble had used their status to oppress adventurers.

While there are undoubtedly complex underlying circumstances, looking only at the surface, it's only natural that many would interpret it that way.

And if things escalate further, the ultimate outcome would be a confrontation between nobles and adventurers... It could even lead to civil war, or worse, the Adventurers' Guild might withdraw its support from the entire Kingdom of Mireana.

Whether it would truly escalate to such a massive situation is uncertain, but the possibility is certainly there.

"Rei, please try not to make things too flashy."

"Huh? Oh, right."

Rei himself hadn't thought that far ahead, but he probably understood on some level that going too far could lead to all sorts of unpleasant consequences.

He nodded in response to Gale's words.

"So, about the information in the Lerdact County..."

"Yeah, I don't know if you're aware, but taxes in the Lerdact County are extremely high."

"I know that. The father of someone I happened to help on my way here was from the Lerdact County."

"Hmm, that's... not exactly rare, I suppose."

"I suppose not."

Once you become an adventurer and see places beyond where you live with your own eyes, you'll understand that our lives are far from privileged.

Especially when you consider that in the Viscountcy of Gale, right next to the County of Lerdact, the tax rate isn't exactly low... but it's not high either.

Life here versus life in other noble-ruled territories.

For those who've seen both, it's hardly strange to want to escape that life.

Many didn't want to abandon the land where they were born and raised, but those who became adventurers often didn't hold such deep attachments.

As a result, people were gradually leaving the County of Lerdact.

And to maintain the same level of revenue despite fewer people... the solution was to raise taxes.

With an already high tax rate becoming even higher, the people of the Lerdact County found themselves barely scraping by just to survive.

"I think lowering taxes a bit might actually bring more people back... But since our place is where those fleeing from there end up, the hostility from the other side is absolutely immense."

A weary sigh escaped him, and Gale offered a weak smile.

Being singled out for hostility by Lerdact, despite bearing no fault, undoubtedly took a mental toll.

(And judging by this, Viscount Gale definitely isn't the strong-willed type. ...No, if anything, he's the kind of person who's easy to get along with.)

Watching Gale, who looked exhausted before him, Rei spoke again.

"Is there any other information you might need regarding the Earl of Leldact's domain?"

"Hmm, yes. ...I hear there are several mines in the Lerdact County."

"Mines, you say?"

Depending on their reserves, mines can bring great wealth.

In fact, depending on the ore deposits, they could even make it possible to aim for a higher title.

And if it's a rare ore like fire ore, well, that goes without saying.

"If you could make those mines unusable, that would be a huge help. What do you think?"

He said it casually.

Those words, delivered as if it were nothing, made Rei recognize that the person sitting before him, no matter how kind-hearted he might seem, was indeed a noble.

"That doesn't sound like a proposal I'd expect from someone who claimed to care about the lives of the people over there."

"True. I sympathize with their plight. But that doesn't mean I want to risk strengthening the military power of Count Lerdact."

A noble who views me as an enemy using ore mined from the mines, or the gold earned from selling it, to strengthen his military power.

That could mean acquiring more powerful weapons and armor, or hiring more formidable mercenaries and adventurers with that gold.

While I worry about the people in the neighboring lands, I can't risk endangering my own territory.

As a noble, that was only natural.

"The mines, you say. Do you know their specific location?"

"No, I only know the general area. Getting information from over there is quite difficult, you see."

"I imagine so."

Rei nodded in agreement as Viscount Gale explained that since they were dealing with an adversary, entering the Earl of Lerdact's territory from the Viscount of Gale's domain could raise suspicions.

"What do you think? Can you manage it?"

"...If it's a mine, I think they're doing fairly large-scale excavation work, so searching from the air might work. Also, as I mentioned earlier, there's talk that the brother of the person who fled from the Lerdact County is still there, so if we can get information from that side..."

Hearing Rei's words, Gale relaxed visibly... Then, for about an hour, they exchanged the information each possessed.

Rei was particularly eager to learn about Hazlue, the town where Jazz's brother, Dostrite, was said to live. Fortunately, this information was readily available.
