The moment that voice rang out, every guild employee inside the counter—indeed, even the few adventurers reviewing request forms—turned their gaze toward its source.

Their reaction stemmed from knowing exactly who that voice belonged to.

Brown skin and silver hair. A beauty that captivated men relentlessly.

And yet, covering that body men find irresistible was a party dress with a plunging neckline, and from the deep slit, her voluptuous thighs were faintly visible.

A woman whose beauty could easily bewitch any man—or even a woman—yet her eyes shone with a light of high intelligence.

Women beautiful only on the outside? The Kingdom of Mireana was surely full of them.

But a woman possessing both beauty and intellect was rare indeed.

This woman was one of those rare few, and her pointed ears and brown skin proved she was a Dark Elf.

"Guildmaster."

Kenny murmured her title.

Marina Alianza. Guildmaster of the Gilm Adventurers' Guild, located on the frontier of the Kingdom of Mireana.

"Kenny, I'm sorry, but could you show them to my room? I'll hear them out."

"...Understood."

Kenny had many things he wanted to say, but he couldn't possibly defy Marina, the Guildmaster, when she spoke like this.

Marina smiled slightly at Kenny's reaction and addressed Maruka again.

"Then, shall we talk in my room?"

"Hmm. That would be most helpful to me as well."

With their agreement, Maruka, Koan, and Rei entered the area behind the counter.

"Lenora, tea, please. And some light snacks for Rei as well."

"Yes."

"My apologies. Well then, shall we go?"

Thus, the three were guided by Marina toward the office.

"Please sit down."

Marina gestured to the sofa and addressed Maruka and the others.

"Hmm. ...So you are Marina. I didn't meet you when I visited before and only heard rumors, but it seems the rumors were actually more subdued than reality."

"Heh heh. If you're going to say that, then what about you? A genius girl who wields the three elements of wind, earth, and light, representing the next generation of magic in the Kingdom of Mireana. Your name is famous even among adventurers, isn't it?"

"I'm still far from perfect. Proof of that is I can't even defeat Rei."

"...Frankly, I doubt there are many who could defeat Rei. If such individuals existed in large numbers, the Mireana Kingdom would be crushing the Bestir Empire single-handedly."

They smiled at each other, exchanging compliments.

After conversing for several minutes, Lenora entered the room carrying light refreshments like tea and sandwiches.

"Thank you for your trouble. Please leave them over there."

"Yes. Um... about the request documents, do we still need to prepare them?"

"Yes. I'll take care of that here, so don't worry about it."

"I see. Well then, excuse me."

Bowing, Lenora exited the office.

As she did, she shot Rei a worried glance, but she didn't make any move to say anything about it.

Once Lenora was out of sight, Marina picked up the tea on the table and crossed her legs.

Her brown, voluptuous thighs came into view for Rei and Koan, but neither of them seemed to pay it any mind.

Not that she seemed disappointed by this, Marina turned to Maruka and spoke.

"Now then, regarding this matter with the royalist faction, we've received quite a bit of information ourselves. The reason you want to make him an instructor at the officer academy is because you don't want to involve Rei in this affair, isn't it?"

"Well, well, the Guildmaster of Gilm is indeed sharp. Your ears are quick."

Maruka murmured with apparent admiration, but Marina smiled and shook her head.

"Well, the matter of the Count of Eriell's household is quite famous in Gilm. ...You know, for various reasons."

"...I see."

He must have understood the meaning implied by the word "various." Maruka gently averted his gaze.

He knew exactly what his faction, the Royalist Party, had done in this city. That knowledge shaped his attitude.

(Though, it was precisely because of those various things that Father and the others took action like this.)

Even so, the fact that the Royalist faction was feeling the squeeze in Gilm was not something Maruka could welcome.

It made their work in Gilm far more difficult: gathering intelligence on the neutral faction's movements, obtaining rare monster materials only found in the frontier, scouting powerful adventurers drawn to the frontier, and all the other tasks the Royalist faction needed to handle.

Fortunately, not many people directed such attitudes toward children like Maruka, and with someone like Koan around, they couldn't afford to act recklessly.

But it was true that the other royalist members at the Gilm guild were feeling rather uncomfortable.

"Anyway, then. As for me, I'd like to welcome Rei as an instructor at the officer academy, at least for this winter. We'll make arrangements here during that time. What do you say? Isn't that better than having strange disturbances caused at Gilm?"

"I can't deny that. Normally, I'd say you couldn't pull strange stunts in Gilm, but..."

Marina set her teacup on the table, her eyes clouding over with melancholy.

Rei understood why she hesitated.

Ranga, who currently commanded Gilm's security force, was absent, having departed for the Earl of Eriell's residence.

Of course, it wasn't that the guards were incompetent, so disturbances wouldn't happen easily in the city. Still, it was true that the guards' vigilance was somewhat—compared to when Ranga was present, at least—reduced.

"Right? Then there shouldn't be a problem, should there?"

"No, there are plenty of problems, aren't there? To begin with, even if I were to teach at the officer academy in Glacial during the winter, I couldn't just leave Gilm today and arrive tomorrow. Normally, it should take about twenty days, shouldn't it? Furthermore, considering it's winter and snowing now, I think it would take a month."

Marina paused there, her gaze drifting toward the window of the office.

Outside the window, snow was falling.

It was clearly falling harder than when Rei and the others had arrived at the Guild.

"Even if he takes that long to reach Glacial and then starts teaching... I don't think there'll be much time left, do you? Maybe a month... two months at most. Do you really think the cadets at the academy could learn anything from Rei in that short time? Besides, I doubt Rei could effectively teach someone he's just met anything worthwhile."

"Don't worry about that."

Maruka countered Marina's words head-on.

That attitude seemed unexpected even to Marina. She turned her gaze, urging an answer.

"As the Guildmaster said, normally it takes about twenty days to reach Glacial. But what if it's not normal? The carriage was created as a magic item, and what pulls it isn't horses, but tamed monsters. In fact, it took me only ten days to travel from Glacial to Gilm, you know? And that was through the snow, mind you."

How about that? Maruka puffed out her tiny chest.

She was likely boasting, but to an outsider, the scene looked like nothing more than a child putting on airs.

Koan, standing behind Maruka, seemed to understand this. Uncharacteristically troubled, he hesitated, unsure whether to interject.

But leaving Koan behind, Maruka continued speaking.

"And besides, if push comes to shove, we could always put Rei on Set and send her ahead to Glacial."

"No, what about that?"

Rei couldn't help but interject.

"Hm? Is there something wrong with that? If we use Set, who can fly, we could bypass those winding roads and even cross mountains. I'm certain it would be faster than going by carriage with me."

"But it's cold up in the sky, isn't it? And it's winter now. We'd end up stranded out there!"

Marina stated, but Rei shook his head at that.

"That won't be a problem. The Dragon Robe has the ability to maintain a constant temperature, and I'm not weak to the cold either."

After all, this body was created by Zepair and the others. With the Dragon Robe, surviving a snowy mountain in the dead of winter wouldn't be difficult.

Set the griffin wasn't weak enough to be affected by that level of cold either, so the cold itself wasn't an issue.

"Then what exactly is the problem?"

"First and foremost, whether they'll trust us if we go to that town called Glacial? Or is it a city, since it's Duke Quent's stronghold? Anyway, just us two, so we'll definitely arouse suspicion."

"That shouldn't be a problem if I write a letter. After all, adventurers traveling with a griffin are practically unheard of except for Rei. Impersonating Rei would be difficult too."

"...Whether the gatekeeper can recognize Maruka's handwriting or not aside, that does sound like it might work. The other problem is whether I can actually get to a place I've never been before. Especially if, like I said earlier, we end up taking shortcuts off the main road..."

He trailed off.

If I had maps, traveling along the main roads wouldn't be difficult.

But in places with no roads at all... like mountains, for instance... you inevitably need some sense of the terrain.

If it weren't winter, asking hunters or adventurers out in the wild wouldn't be too hard.

Unfortunately, it was winter now, and few ventured out.

Asked if they could safely reach their destination under such circumstances, Rei couldn't help but tilt his head.

Especially in Rei's case, she often made unintended detours while hunting down bandits.

So, if asked whether he could reach a town he'd never been to before, without a map...

"Probably not. If I had a map, I could ride Set and fly along the roads, so that would be different."

"We have one."

Maruka stated simply in response to Rei's words.

"You have one?"

Maps were extremely rare items, practically classified as confidential.

Given that, it was only natural for Rei to doubt Maruka's words.

"Yes. I had planned for Rei to proceed ahead with Set, if possible. Given that, I figured a map would be necessary. My father gave it to me!"

"Pretty amazing, huh?" Maruka puffed out his chest again.

"So, does that mean Duke Quent is aware of this? A map of the Duchy of Quent and its surrounding areas?"

"Yes. It shows how seriously my father views this matter. However, if this map is stolen, shown to anyone, or worse, sold, appropriate measures will be taken. ...Koan."

"Yes."

At Maruka's command, Koan pulled a folded map from his pocket.

But it was not yet handed over to Rei.

"This map will be given to you, Rei, only after you have formally accepted the commission. While we trust you won't misuse it, should you do so, it would place the young lady in a very difficult position. Please be extremely careful."

"...I suppose so."

Marina nodded at Koan's words.

A detailed map of one's own domain carried such weight.

To lend it to someone... not exactly a stranger, but certainly not a member of her family or an associate.

For Marina, it was only natural that Duke Quent would take a firm stance against Rei if the map were misused.

Even knowing they stood no chance in a direct confrontation, they might still raise an army.

She entrusted the map to Rei with that level of resolve.

It wasn't so much trust in Rei... but rather, trust in Maruka's eyes was stronger.

"So, is this acceptable? Are we to understand that Rei will accept my request?"

"If Rei says it's fine, I have no complaints. What do you say?"

"No problem. The reward is attractive too."

"...Reward? I don't think Rei is short on money, so if that's the case..."

The word "reward" coming from Rei's mouth made him pause for a few seconds, then he likely recalled Rei's hobby.

After all, that was the method used when I asked Rei for an unreasonable favor.

"A magic item as payment?"

"Hm? Close, but no cigar. The correct answer is fire ore."

"Fire Ore? ...Ah, I see."

The reason Rei earned the nickname Crimson.

Rei's trump card: the Flame Tornado.

Marina instantly grasped what would happen if Flame Ore were used in that Flame Tornado.

(This isn't just vicious—it's downright dangerous.)

Marina felt a twinge of worry, but since the formalities of a designated request were in order, and Rei seemed satisfied accepting it, she accepted it too.

"So, Rei's okay with this, right?"

"Yeah. It'll be done by spring, right?"

Maruka nodded back at Rei, who was looking at her for confirmation.

"Mm. That won't be a problem. Father can't afford to take too long either. ...But you seem strangely fixated on spring. Is there something?"

"Ah. I have various things to attend to once spring arrives."

What flashed through Rei's mind were Elena coming to meet him to remove the 'Seed of Warning' and Vihera, who would surely arrive from Exil.
