The day after hearing about Ambris from Marina, Rei, Set, and Vihera flew through the sky as planned, searching for its form.

Knowing only that it was a black, mist-like presence, they hadn't expected to find it immediately, and naturally, they hadn't been able to locate it.

They were utilizing Set's keen senses—especially his hearing, smell, and sight—to the fullest, yet still couldn't locate Ambris.

(Since its body seems made of mist, I doubt it makes any sound when moving. Likewise, being mist, I don't think it has any scent. If that's the case, sight is probably the most useful sense. It's a shame my newly leveled-up sense of smell can't be used.)

Still, even mist might carry some scent. A faint hope flickered within Rei.

"Guruu!"

It probably wasn't because he sensed Rei's thoughts, but suddenly, Set growled.

What? Following Set's gaze, Rei saw a group of kobolds and goblins moving along side by side.

"This is troublesome. So even different monster groups cooperate now? I really wish I'd gotten intel on this area too."

According to what Marina had told him, there was no record of different monster species cooperating during the upheaval that occurred three hundred years ago in the Valley of a Hundred Demons within the Bestir Empire.

Was that because such cooperation never actually happened, or because any instances that did occur weren't recorded?

But Rei immediately shook his head, dismissing the latter possibility.

Three hundred years is an incredibly long time for humans.

But for long-lived races like the elves, it felt like just a little while ago.

Therefore, it wouldn't be strange if some information about the Valley of a Hundred Demons remained, or if someone remembered it directly.

(It's possible the elves weren't in the Valley of a Hundred Demons back then... right? Not that the Bestir Empire actively excludes elves or beastmen or anything.)

For instance, when Rei traveled to the Bestir Empire to participate in the tournament held there, he saw many beastmen, and elves as well.

After all, in the main tournament itself, Rei fought an Elf adventurer who was a Rank A adventurer and had a nickname.

If it were a country that excluded elves and beastmen, that wouldn't have happened.

However, this was partly due to Rei's narrow perspective.

Even within a country, while the capital area might not discriminate against elves or beastmen, it's quite common for discrimination to exist in the countryside.

The Bestir Empire's territory is vast, and customs vary greatly by region.

Considering the nation's founding through successive invasions and annexations of surrounding small countries, differing customs by region were only natural.

"Rei, what are we going to do?"

Vihera, hanging from Set's front paw, asked.

Rei's response was brutally short, yet concise.

"Burn it."

Fortunately, there were no adventurers, merchants, or travelers nearby—no monsters other than goblins and kobolds.

The area where the monster horde was walking was grassland, with hardly any flammable trees nearby.

Having assessed the situation, Rei drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and chanted the spell.

'Flames, take my magic as your source of power. Become arrows that consume all. Rain down like a storm, ceaselessly, uninterrupted.'

This spell was an enhanced version of one Rei had used before.

Normally, it would produce around fifty flaming arrows, but now nearly five hundred flaming arrows hovered around Rei.

The countless flaming arrows hovering around Set, Rei, and Vihera bared their scorching fangs as Rei activated the magic.

'Rain of Flaming Arrows!'

With those words, the Death Scythe was swung down, and obeying Rei's command, the flaming arrows rained straight down toward the ground.

Flying at their usual altitude of 100 meters, the goblins and kobolds advancing on the ground hadn't noticed Rei and the others overhead at all.

Furthermore, the summer sun high in the sky made it easy to overlook the brightness of the flaming arrows.

If any of them possessed the ability to sense magic, it might have been a different story. Unfortunately, only ordinary goblins and kobolds were present here.

There was absolutely no sign of any such individuals.

As a result, the goblins and kobolds were caught completely off guard by the countless flaming arrows raining down from above, unable to defend themselves.

Pierced by the flaming arrows, the goblins and kobolds' bodies burned one after another.

Still, some lucky ones survived—using allies as shields or simply avoiding the arrows by chance.

But... that fleeting luck turned to despair in the very next instant.

'Flames, your power is my power. Burn with my magic as I command, scorching the enemy. Your nature is spreading fire, hellfire. Use my magic as fuel to increase your fierceness.'

As the spell was chanted, fireballs materialized around Rei.

Ten of them.

Each emitted intense heat, so scorching that merely approaching them without caution could likely incinerate one.

'Ten Fireballs'

As the spell activated, those ten fireballs rained down upon the ground like a final blow.

Goblins and kobolds that had somehow survived the flaming arrows were instantly engulfed in flames by the fireballs falling from above, burned alive.

Moreover, the fireballs that struck the ground exploded as expected, scattering explosive flames around them.

Even the goblins and kobolds that had been damaged by the flaming arrows but had survived with only injuries were consumed by these explosions.

A few minutes... no, in reality, it was only about a minute of attack, but that was enough to completely wipe out the horde of goblins and kobolds, a group of over a hundred monsters.

The one who accomplished this was Rei, riding atop Set as he flew through the sky.

Without even touching the ground, he had annihilated the horde of monsters with such ease it could truly be called a mere side task.

For the goblins and kobolds that were attacked, it was undoubtedly an event they couldn't possibly comprehend.

"What about materials?"

"We already got the magic stones from both the goblin leader and the kobold leader. As for getting magic stones or proof-of-kill parts to sell... it's not worth the effort for the profit. Besides, judging by the state of things, the magic stones are probably burned too."

All that remained on the ground were the charred, carbonized corpses of goblins and kobolds.

Luckily, a few individuals might still be alive, but it was certain they wouldn't survive much longer.

(Wait, can you really call it luck when they survived after most of their bodies were burned?)

It would be different if they had potions or healing magic, but goblins and kobolds wouldn't have such things.

In other words, the goblins and kobolds still alive now would suffer terribly until their final breath.

Moreover, before that happens, adventurers who saw the scene from afar might arrive, or monsters and beasts might attack the dying goblins and kobolds for food.

Considering that, it's hardly a matter of luck.

"Well, anyway, what we're doing today isn't defeating a horde of monsters, it's finding Ambris."

Even as he said this, Rei would undoubtedly act to obtain the magic stones if any unknown monsters were present.

Conversely, this also meant he didn't particularly want the magic stones from monsters they'd already defeated.

"Right. ...Whether we capture them or not is another matter."

Vihera murmured softly, likely recalling what Rei had said yesterday.

The number of victims affected by this incident wasn't large, but they definitely existed.

For those people, the existence of Ambris, who caused the massive swarm, was absolutely unforgivable.

If Rei really did something like that, it was highly likely he would be blamed by those around him.

Vihera didn't particularly want to see that kind of scene.

(Besides, Rei wouldn't just let something happen to him. If anything did happen, he'd definitely fight back.)

Worst case, he might not be able to stay in Gilm anymore.

Honestly, considering that, I wish Rei would give up on capturing Ambrith.

"...Still, he's nowhere to be seen. If it really were black mist, you'd think it'd be pretty obvious if he were flying through the sky."

"Is that so? But if he's flying, that's one thing. If he's in woods or forests... or worse, if he's blended into the grasslands, finding him quickly would be difficult, wouldn't it?"

What Vihera said was undeniably true.

Given it's a mist-like opponent, it's reasonable to expect it could take any form.

If it were moving by weaving through the grass growing on the ground, even Set would have trouble spotting it.

No matter how sharp one's eyesight, it's meaningless if you can't see it in the first place.

"If it were emitting magic..."

Rei murmured thoughtfully.

An Amblis is a being born from magical energy abnormalities.

Given that, he had initially thought that anyone with the ability to sense magic power could spot them immediately.

But that suggestion, voiced on a whim by Rei, was swiftly dismissed by Vihera.

When it happened in the Valley of a Hundred Demons three hundred years ago, those who uncovered how the Ambris came to be naturally assumed that, since it was born from a magical anomaly, anyone with the ability to sense magic could find it immediately...

While those possessing such abilities were rare to begin with, considering Ambris's nature of continuously producing higher-tier beings, it couldn't be ignored. Consequently, many individuals with the ability to sense magical power were gathered.

But... no matter how they used those individuals, they ultimately failed to locate the Umbris.

Its high stealth capability, born precisely from its origin in magical energy anomalies.

Because of this, finding Ambrith proved extremely difficult.

The only way to confirm its presence was to see it directly, but its body was made of mist.

That made spotting it incredibly difficult.

"Well now, what on earth should we do?"

"Grrr."

Rei growled in agreement with her words, though if anything, it was Set who seemed more annoyed by the prospect of finding Ambros than Rei.

After all, the star of this search was Set, with his keen senses and wings.

"Ah, Rei. Look over there."

At Vihera's words, they looked down to the ground and saw a trio that looked all too familiar.

Flying at a height of 100 meters, they shouldn't have been able to spot Set from below... Yet, for some reason, one of the three suddenly began looking around as if sensing something.

After scanning the area for about ten seconds, that person suddenly looked up into the sky. From that height, Set should have been no bigger than a speck, yet they spotted him clearly and waved their hand vigorously.

"...What do we do?"

"We have to go. Besides, we need information. ...Still, how did Vihera even recognize Mireine and the others from this distance?"

Yes, the one waving to Rei and the others on the ground... or more accurately, to Set, was Mireine, leader of the Rank C party, Scorching Wind.

"I'm using magic to enhance my vision. It's a skill I've been practicing lately."

"If you're using magic to enhance something, wouldn't physical enhancement be better?"

Since Vihera fights hand-to-hand, wouldn't physical enhancement be more useful than visual enhancement? Rei pointed this out, but Vihera shook her head... Then, remembering she was hanging from Set's front legs, she opened her mouth.

"Just enhancing my vision is giving me quite a hard time. Physical enhancement is still way off. I'm practicing to enhance my senses one by one, starting with sight."

Rei trained in the Two-Spear Style, while Set and Death Scythe grew stronger by absorbing magic stones.

Vihera, who loved combat, refused to be left behind by the man she cherished.

That's why she devised skills like the Immersion Palm, and now she was pushing herself to advance even further beyond that.

Of course, she never voiced the reason—to avoid being left behind by Rei.

It was a woman's pride... she wanted Rei to see only her good points, if possible.

And sure enough, Rei accepted Vihera's explanation without question.

"I see. Well then, for now... let's head down to the surface. If we keep ignoring Mireine like this, things could get complicated when we meet her next."

"I suppose so."

Vihera knew exactly how deeply Mireine felt for Set.

Given Mireine's personality, she could only agree with a smile at Rei's words.
