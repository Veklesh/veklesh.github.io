The day after receiving the request from Daskar, Rei, Vihera, Byune, and Set immediately headed outside Gilm to find the alchemist... or so it seemed. Instead, they waited by the gate for Mireine and the others.

The reason was the corpse of a cyclops stored within Rei's Misty Ring.

There were seven of them.

Most were slain by Vihera, but among them were also the bodies of Cyclops defeated by Mireine and the others.

In truth, Byune had contributed significantly to defeating those Cyclops, but she had relinquished that right.

Considering the number of cyclopses defeated by Vihera, who could be considered Byune's guardian, it wasn't strange at all.

"But are you sure this is okay? I mean, I'll be saved, but..."

While waiting for Mireine and the others to arrive, Rei addressed Vihera beside him.

It was past nine in the morning, and the number of people leaving Gilm was considerably less than during the busiest part of the morning.

Even so, given Gilm's location, the number of people leaving at this hour was still far from small, and many were captivated by the Vihera, who could easily be mistaken for dancers or prostitutes.

Some men were so utterly captivated by the Vihera that they were being stepped on by their girlfriends or female companions.

Despite such stares, Vihera nodded to Rei without seeming particularly bothered, as if it were nothing unusual.

"Yes, it's fine. It's true that Set helped us defeat that cyclops, and it's only the magic stone from that one, not all the materials, right? I'm not exactly short on money either."

"One magic stone is no problem at all," Vihera continued.

"Sorry about that."

"It's fine. But are you sure just one magic stone is enough? If you want, I could share the magic stones from the other Cyclops?"

"No, one is enough. Besides..."

"Besides...?"

The absorption of magic stones requires either myself or Set to participate in combat.

Rei, who had been about to say that, clamped his mouth shut.

He remembered he hadn't yet told Vihera about the magic beast technique.

(If Vihera is going to stay in my party like this, maybe it's about time I told her. ...Elena said something like that the other day too.)

Rei recalled Elena's words from their conversation via the Paired Orb a few days prior, but that thought vanished cleanly from his mind the very next instant.

"Set-chan, Set-chan, Set-chaaaan! Long time no see. Aren't you hungry?"

The moment that voice reached him, Mireine threw herself into Set's arms.

"Sorry, my Mireine..."

Surnin bowed his head, resignation already evident on his face.

Ekril, standing beside Surnin, looked at Rei, hurriedly bowed to Surnin, then opened her mouth.

"Um, thank you for helping me yesterday. If things had gone on like that, I'm sure I would have died. I really wanted to thank you yesterday, but..."

As Ekril trailed off, Rei recalled yesterday's events.

Upon hearing that an alchemist from the Bestir Empire was involved in this incident, he had immediately dragged Vihera away the moment he encountered Mireine and the others returning.

His actions were so swift there was absolutely no chance for Ekril to speak up, and in the end, she hadn't been able to thank Rei.

So, after returning from the lord's mansion yesterday, before eating, he tried to ask the guild to deliver a message... but before that, Mireine had already delivered the message.

But that was only natural. Having accepted the request to slay the Cyclops, they needed to submit proof—like the Cyclops's head or its magic stone—to verify the completion of the quest.

But since the Cyclops' corpse was still inside Rei's Misty Ring, the request hadn't been completed yet.

That said, word had reached the guild that Rei had completed the slaying while heading to reinforce the reinforcements, and with about a week still left until the request's deadline, Mireine and the others weren't exactly in a desperate situation.

"Don't worry about it. I don't want to see an acquaintance die either. I'm just glad you're safe."

"...Thank you."

Ecuril bowed her head again at Rei's words.

Then, as if timed perfectly, voices echoed around them once more.

"Hey, Set-chan, how've you been?"

The content was similar to before, but the voice belonged to someone entirely different.

"Gah, Mireine, Rei."

"Hmph. You got here first yesterday, but today I beat you to it."

"Guh..."

Right in front of Johanna, who was about to hug Set, Mireine deliberately hugged him instead.

"Grrrrr..."

Set let out a somewhat troubled growl at Mireine and Johanna.

"Hey, cut it out. Set's clearly uncomfortable. Besides, we don't have time to waste here today. You two offered to help with the Cyclops skinning, remember?"

At Rei's voice, everyone present nodded seriously.

Among them were those who had fled separately before Rei joined them yesterday, proving everyone had arrived safely at Gilm.

Though a few had minor injuries, Rei breathed a sigh of relief upon seeing none with serious wounds.

(Well, I trained them, so it's only natural they wouldn't get injured by monsters encountered while fleeing, though a Cyclops might be another story.)

Rei muttered to himself, hiding his relief behind a blush.

"Rei? What's up?"

Noticing Rei's expression, Dietz asked.

"Nothing. Anyway, we should get going soon. You guys should finish your quest as quickly as possible too, right?"

Everyone nodded at Rei's words and headed straight for the gate together.

With this many people moving together... especially with prominent figures like Rei, Set, and Vihera among them, it was only natural they'd stand out.

Especially now that the busiest part of the morning had passed and things had calmed down a bit, they couldn't help but stand out.

"Rei, this group... Could it be about yesterday's incident? Or maybe..."

Ranga asked, wondering if something had happened regarding the Cyclops. Rei shook his head, seeming to imply there was no particular issue.

"The Cyclops thing is fine now. I was thinking of stripping materials from the Cyclops. They're so big, and there are so many of them, it'd take too long with just one or two people."

Hearing Rei's words, Ranga breathed a sigh of relief.

As the captain of the guard, he had naturally been informed by Daskar about the alchemist situation.

From Ranga's standpoint, the existence of a malicious alchemist was nothing but a nuisance.

But since he didn't know the face of the enemy, he couldn't possibly subject every single person entering Gilm to a thorough interrogation.

In that sense, the guards handling entry and exit procedures throughout the city at Gilm were currently under the most strain.

Furthermore, Daskar's request to keep this matter quiet made it difficult to delegate the task to subordinates.

Yet, not a trace of such thoughts showed on Ranga's face. To Rei's eyes, he looked exactly the same as always.

"I see. Well then, be careful. Especially when dismantling multiple monsters the size of a Cyclops, there's always the possibility other monsters will be drawn by the scent of blood."

After this brief exchange, they waited for everyone to finish their procedures before exiting through the gate into the outer world of Gilm.

"Alright, we're heading to the forest you went to yesterday. But don't worry, it's not that deep in; it's quite close from here."

The reason it took them so long to return yesterday was because they had gone deep into the forest, but the place Rei was aiming for wasn't that far from Gilm.

Johanna and Dietz, former members of the guerrilla squad who had only just arrived in Gilm, flashed a momentary look of reluctance upon hearing about the forest, perhaps recalling yesterday's incident.

But upon hearing it was a nearby location, they breathed sighs of relief.

Compared to these former guerrilla fighters, Mireine and the others, who had lived in Gilm for a long time, immediately understood where he was talking about.

Thus, the group began walking toward the forest, drawing the attention of those around them.

Rei scanned the crowd with his eyes, trying to spot the alchemist responsible for the Cyclops incident among those watching them, but the hostile stares were numerous.

Considering Rei's actions, it wasn't surprising he drew hostility, but this time, the glances were particularly filled with jealousy.

(Probably... no, definitely because of Vihera.)

Rei sighed under his hood, glancing at Vihera walking beside him.

As usual today, she wore a thin garment that seemed designed to flaunt her figure in a way that could only be described as provocative. Walking beside such a Vihera, it was only natural that Rei drew jealous glances from the men.

Deciding it would be impossible to spot an alchemist even if one were present, Rei quickly stopped worrying about the stares and focused his attention on heading into the forest.

Warm sunlight streamed through the trees, and the sounds of the nearby river, the lush green foliage, and the gentle breeze all reminded him that it was spring.

An overwhelming scent of green and fresh air filled the surroundings, something she couldn't have smelled while inside the Gilm.

...Though, for Mireine and Johanna, who had been in the mountains the day before yesterday and yesterday, smelling spring now was hardly anything special.

(Wild mountain vegetables... if only we had things like fiddlehead ferns, bonna, or aiko. Especially since we have flour and oil, we could make tempura too.)

Rei had lived in the mountains back in Japan.

Naturally, mountain vegetables—the bounty of the mountains—were a seasonal delicacy, and tempura made from fiddlehead ferns, often called the queen of mountain vegetables, was one of Rei's favorite dishes.

(Ah, but did tempura batter have something besides flour? Wouldn't it just be wheat dissolved in cold water for the coating? And we don't have tempura dipping sauce either... Well, we have salt for seasoning, so that should be fine.)

Just remembering the taste of fiddlehead tempura made Rei feel hungry.

(We have udon noodles, so maybe tempura udon? ...Ideally, tempura would go better with soba noodles, but I've never seen soba in this world. Buckwheat grains... yeah, I've never seen them in shops either.)

By the time he arrived at the river, his mind still on the tempura, Rei's stomach was already making its presence known.

But he couldn't very well say they should rest now, having just arrived at the river.

Suppressing the feeling of hunger, he pulled out two Cyclops.

One was the cyclops Mireine and the others had defeated themselves, and the other was the cyclops Set had attacked from behind.

"Alright, split up and start dismembering them. Like I said before, I'll put the dismembered parts in my item box and carry them, so don't worry about it."

Following Rei's instructions, they split into two groups and immediately began dismembering the Cyclops.

Two Cyclopes, each nearly four meters tall, lying side by side on the ground took up a considerable amount of space.

"Seeing them like this again, they really are huge, aren't they?"

Mireine, forcibly pulled away from Set by Surnin's hand due to the start of the dismantling, murmured as if regaining her composure.

Nearby, the former guerrilla squad members were similarly pulling Johanna away from Set as they started dismantling their own Cyclops.

"...Set, sorry, but please handle perimeter security as usual."

Judging that this would only take too much time, Rei asked Set to keep watch over the surroundings.

This wasn't unusual, though.

Whenever Rei did anything outside of Gilm, Set usually used his keen senses to keep watch.

"Guruu!"

So, Set gave a short growl in response to Rei's words and vanished into the nearby bushes.

He probably understood that staying here would only get in the way of disassembling the Cyclops.

"Ahh... Set-chan..."

"Come on, Johanna. Hurry up. The sooner you finish stripping the materials, the more time you'll have to play with Set."

At her companion's voice, Johanna's eyes, which had dimmed for a few seconds, regained their light.

"Come on, there's no time to waste. Let's finish dismembering this Cyclops already!"

Shouting loud enough for all to hear, she grabbed the butcher's knife and strode toward the Cyclops corpse lying on the ground.

Seeing this, the others also began dismantling the Cyclops, smiling wryly at Johanna's typical behavior.

Mireine, Rei, Vihera, Byune, and the others, too, seemed drawn by Johanna's lead as they walked toward the cyclops's corpse.
