“Lady Elena, wouldn’t you mind coming back to Gilm soon?”

Some time had passed since they entered the Kingdom of Mireana. Though summer still lingered, the season was beginning to shift toward autumn. On a night when the weather had gradually become more pleasant, Rei and the others heard those words from Daskar, who appeared in the Paired Orb.

It was almost a daily occurrence for Ara and Elena to converse through the Paired Orbs at night.

It had started out that way today as well, but suddenly Daskar came into Ara’s room and spoke to her through the Paired Orb.

“That’s sudden again. Well, I was actually thinking it might be time for me to head back soon, too.”

Seeing Daskar appear, Rei, who had been relaxing in the magic tent, came over to Elena and said that.

Hearing Rei’s words, Daskar, trying his best to look apologetic despite his stern face, spoke up.

“Up until now, we’ve managed by using a lot of people, but perhaps because Rei isn’t here, the expansion work is falling behind schedule. If possible, I’d like to get it to a certain point before the snow falls.”

“Ah… So, you’re planning to keep working through the winter, right?”

“That’s right. But you never know what might happen with the cold and snow. I want to get the difficult parts done now and save the easy ones for winter. Though, that’s just the plan, of course.”

Even Rei, who was born and raised in a snowy region, understood what Daskar was getting at.

After all, he’d spent many winters in Gilm.

Given that, he naturally knew how much snow fell in Gilm every year.

That’s precisely why he’d probably prefer to avoid doing the expansion work on those critical—and difficult—areas during winter, when it’s cold and the snow makes things slippery.

“That’s true. If construction gets delayed now, it might be a problem. Besides, we’ve got a decent number of guards assembled by now, so even if we go over there, I think… really, just maybe, we’ll be fine.”

Right? Rei asked the people around him—Marina, Vihera, and Byune, who had come because Daskar had been projected onto the Paired Orb—with a glance.

The first to respond to Rei’s gaze was Vihera.

“That’s true. The guards train every day, and some of the others have a natural talent for combat, so some of them are actually stronger than your average adventurer.”

Although the training was with opponents who had absolutely no combat experience, it seemed to be surprisingly enjoyable for Vihera.

She says this with a beaming smile.

Daskar, who knew of Vihera’s fondness for combat, looked slightly surprised at her demeanor.

However, deciding that anything he said now would only give Marina ammunition to tease her with, Daskar avoided pressing the issue about Vihera and changed the subject.

“So, could you head over there tomorrow?”

“I suppose so. I might be a little late, but I’d still like to head over there as soon as possible.”

“I’d appreciate that. …Also, regarding the Giganto Turtle… what do you think? Personally, I’d prefer not to dismantle it just yet, if possible.”

“Ah, are you saying you don’t have enough people right now?”

“That’s right. There’s no way we can spare any extra personnel.”

Hearing Daskar’s words, Rei nodded in agreement.

If Rei and the others had stayed in Gilm, they could have dismantled the Gigant Turtle with those spare personnel.

Rei, with his Misty Ring, and Marina, who uses spirit magic—those two alone are a significant force.

Though not quite as powerful as those two, Vihera also held significant value in leading others to patrol the area and check for any abnormalities.

A woman… and one dressed like a prostitute or a dancer at that—Vihera moving about in a conspicuous manner.

Anyone reckless enough to make a move upon seeing her would get a taste of her wrath and learn to be more cautious in the future, and the women would find it easier to ask Vihera for help than a tough-looking man.

Byune, too, uses her small frame to move about quickly; if there’s a problem, she can resolve it, or if she can’t, she can at least alert the adults nearby. …Though, as always, Vihera is the only one who can fully understand Byune’s words.

Elena, however, is the only one who, due to her status as a representative of the Noble Faction, cannot be seen helping with the construction work.

Still, if word spreads that the Princess General has come to Gilm, she can subtly imply that anyone acting suspiciously risks being arrested by the guards, which makes it possible to deter those who might cause trouble.

(Ideally, I’d have liked to get the Gigant Turtle’s magic stone sooner. …Well, it’s not like a commotion is going to break out immediately, so if I just hold out a little longer, it shouldn’t be impossible… right? Besides, since I’m asking Lord Daskar to research sand-sailing ships as well, I suppose I’d better not ask for the impossible.)

After weighing the pros and cons in his mind, Rei nodded to Daskar.

“Understood. Since we’re short on manpower, I can’t ask for the impossible. It might even be a good winter project. Fortunately, things don’t rot as easily in the winter.”

“True. …That’s one option. Even if it’s not enough to justify an expansion project, there might be some folks who want to earn a little extra cash.”

“That’s true. Besides, I think we should prepare as many winter jobs as possible this year.”

Rei’s words were not spoken half-jokingly, but with complete seriousness.

In fact, many people have gathered in Gilm right now.

For residents of nearby towns like Abuero and Saburusta, returning home for the winter would be possible.

But since there were also people who had come from villages and towns much farther away than those, it was only natural that some would choose to spend the winter in Gilm rather than return home.

That being the case, if asked whether their current earnings would be enough to get them through the winter... many would likely find themselves in a tight spot.

With so many people in Gilm right now, there are plenty of shops to cater to them.

There are surely quite a few who don’t save the money they earn but spend it all right away in the shops.

It was Daskar’s duty as the lord of Gilm to provide more work than usual for such people.

“If that’s fine with you, I’ll prepare accordingly… but are you really okay with that?”

“Yes, no problem at all. It saves us the trouble, after all. …Just make sure to handle it in a way that prevents any strange ideas from arising.”

“I understand. I’ll send people over to handle that as well. I’ll contact the Guild too, so you should start thinking about the rewards.”

Rei nodded at Daskar’s words of caution.

The dismantling of the Giganto Turtle would be carried out strictly as a request from Rei.

However, since the Guild would be the one paying the reward, negotiations with the Guild were necessary.

(Still, it’s a relief they’re sending someone—probably a guard. If some idiot with strange… foolish ideas shows up, it’ll be a hassle for us too.)

For example, there’s no guarantee that some unscrupulous adventurer wouldn’t try to hide the meat or materials from the Giant Turtle’s disassembly and keep them for themselves.

No, Rei didn’t really want to think about it, but he could definitely expect there to be a few such individuals.

That’s exactly why having guards dispatched to catch such people was a huge help to Rei.

With ordinary monsters, it might be manageable, but when it comes to butchering a gigantic—no, an enormous monster like the Giganto Turtle, there’s no way Rei could keep an eye on every single part.

(Considering that, it might be better to leave it entirely up to the Guild.)

Even if they couldn’t be watched in Rei’s presence, they might try to steal meat or materials, but if asked whether they’d dare do such a thing where Guild staff were present… there would be some who did, but there were surely very few of them.

Of course, relying on the guild for that would increase the financial burden, but the Misty Ring has a massive amount of money.

It’s not at all strange for Rei to decide that spending a little more of that money is worth it if it makes his life easier.

“Well, next…”

After uttering those words, Daskar and Rei continued to exchange various reports and hold consultations.

The next day, after breakfast while preparing to depart, Rei gathered the core members to explain what Daskar had told him the previous day.

However, upon hearing this, Gimekara, Sura, Rocks, and the others showed no particular sign of distress and accepted it.

“Understood. So, when do we leave? If possible, I’d prefer to avoid leaving right away—we need to explain this to everyone first. Plus, we have to load all the supplies onto the wagons.”

“I thought you’d be a little more surprised. You’re surprisingly calm. Well, it’s a huge relief compared to having people cry and scream, though.”

In response to Rei’s words, which carried a hint of surprise, Gimekara smiled and spoke up.

“I’d already heard that Rei and the others would be leaving eventually. We’d been mentally preparing for it, you know. Right?”

Sura and Rox, who had been looking at Gimekara, nodded at her words.

That said, while Rox was fine, Suura wore a slightly anxious expression.

Having been with Rei and the others ever since Mejogo, it was only natural for Suura to feel anxious about them leaving.

Compared to Suura, Gimekara had joined them partway through… and since Rox had only joined a short while ago, they didn’t feel quite as anxious about Rei and the others’ absence.

However, if there’s one problem…

“Um, since Rei and the others are leaving, that means they’ll be riding on Set, right?”

Gimekara asked Rei, watching Set purr happily as the women stroked him.

What she displays is not the reassuring confidence of someone who has completely put her earlier worries aside, but unmistakable anxiety.

Where that anxiety comes from… it’s obvious without even having to think about it.

For those who rely on Set as their emotional pillar, his departure is a momentous event.

In fact, Gimekara knew that when Rei and the others had gone to meet the delegation from Gilm before, the group’s anxiety had grown considerably.

Fortunately, since Rei and the others were able to encounter Ronitas immediately, they returned quickly, and the anxiety within the group was swiftly dispelled… but if things were like that even then, what would happen if it were to last for a long period?

It’s not at all difficult to imagine.

Perhaps Sura shares Gimekara’s concerns, for she wears a slightly anxious expression.

Although Rocks was in charge of organizing the escort, since he had originally been acting as an escort for Ronitas and the others, he wasn’t part of this group at the time.

Therefore, although he had heard stories about it, he had never actually seen with his own eyes just how much morale would drop.

And among those present here, there were two others—Vihera and Byune—who had witnessed that scene with their own eyes.

That said, setting aside the naturally taciturn Byune, Vihera wasn’t the type to say anything.

There was no doubt that without Set, the journey to Gilm would take a considerable amount of time.

Precisely because he understood that, he didn’t make a move to say anything here.

“That’s right. Without Set, getting around would be a problem. After all, our movements are based on the assumption that we’ll be traveling via Set.”

“Is that so? …But, um…”

Gimekara’s gaze turned once more toward the women.

Rei understood exactly what that meant.

“I understand how you feel, Gimekara. I do… but unfortunately, this is a done deal. If Gilm still had some leeway, we could have stayed and acted together a little longer. It’s a shame, but we can’t do that under the current circumstances.”

No matter what Gimekara said, if he was told it was already decided, there was nothing more he could do.

All Gimekara could do was pray that he could somehow help the women, who relied on Set as their emotional anchor, stay positive.

That said, when she considered just how much impact her voice would have on them… she concluded it was so difficult as to be nearly impossible.
