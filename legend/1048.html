The day after the World Tree's treatment ended and the family feast concluded.

Given that last night, people had gathered all over the settlement to drink and revel late into the night while gazing at the World Tree, it was now a time when someone would normally be waking up. Yet the settlement's residents still slept soundly.

Naturally, Rei was no exception... though he specialized in eating rather than drinking, he had eaten late into the night and was still fast asleep in the borrowed house.

Inside this house, besides Rei, Earth's figure could also be seen.

They couldn't very well kick him out of the settlement after the banquet ended, but they also couldn't have Elena and the others put Earth up in the house they'd just met him at. By process of elimination, he ended up staying at the house Rei had rented.

While there weren't many rooms to spare, there was a living room, so Rei didn't particularly object to having Earth stay and agreed to it.

Amidst this... it was still early morning. Despite it being a time when Rei would normally still be asleep, he abruptly sat up in bed.

It wasn't like he jumped out of bed, but anyone familiar with Rei's usual grogginess upon waking would have been surprised by how alert he was.

Usually, after waking up, Rei would be dazed for ten or more minutes... sometimes even half an hour. But not now.

"What is it? ...No, who is it?"

Muttering, he pulled the Dragon Robe from Misty Ring, donned it, slipped on the Sleipnir boots, and left the room.

The fact he hadn't retrieved the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring was likely because the reason he'd woken—that sensation he'd felt while sleeping—wasn't entirely unpleasant.

Yet he had fully awakened. Partly because his sixth sense detected something urgent about the current sensation, and partly because of a premonition, or a gut feeling, if you will.

What was that sensation just now? Wondering about it, he suddenly realized.

"Was that similar to the gaze I felt after the World Tree's treatment ended?"

To be precise, it wasn't exactly a gaze, but rather an indescribable sensation. Still, calling it a gaze seemed the closest and most fitting description.

But since I couldn't figure out what that feeling was telling me, there was nothing I could do about it.

Still, for some reason, I couldn't stay still. I left my room and headed straight for the living room, where I saw Earth sleeping on the sofa.

And Poro, who had been using his own nine tails as a pillow beside Earth, lifted his head, seemingly awakened by the sound Rei made.

"Poloo?"

Still looking a bit drowsy, Polo turned his gaze blankly toward Rei.

Is it okay for a monster to be in that state right after waking up? Rei thought, completely ignoring his own situation.

But considering the gaze he'd felt earlier, he had no time to deal with Polo right now.

His gaze shifted to Rei, who lay completely unaware on the sofa. Laying his hand on Rei's shoulder, he spoke softly, careful not to wake him.

"I'm going out for a bit. You can stay asleep if you want."

"Polooo?"

At Rei's words, Polo promptly fell back asleep, using his own tail as a pillow once more.

(Polo is one thing, but honestly, as an adventurer, isn't it a bit of a failure that he doesn't wake up at all with me, someone he just met yesterday, right here?)

He glanced at Earth, who was in a deep sleep without even snoring, then left the living room.

Opening the door and breathing in the refreshing spring morning air, he soon sensed someone approaching from Rei's direction.

Could it be related to something I sensed? Thinking that, I turned my gaze toward the approaching figure...

"Huh?"

The figure was completely unexpected. Rei's expression flickered with surprise.

Because the person standing there was not someone Rei particularly liked, and he understood that the other person certainly didn't like him either.

The other person... Ragdo must have noticed Rei too. Increasing his running speed even more, he came up in front of Rei and slowed down.

"Haa, haa, haa. ...How did you know I was coming?"

"I didn't know. I just happened to step outside. So, what are you doing out so early in the morning..."

He glanced up at the sky and noticed the sun had already risen.

"Well, that's not exactly it, but what are you doing out at a time when most people are still asleep?"

"...I thought it would be better if you saw it for yourself rather than just hearing about it. I'm sorry, but please come with me."

Ragdo muttered, fixing a serious gaze—one utterly devoid of deception—on Rei. Rei understood that Ragdo's words held no falsehood.

"Alright."

Without unnecessary questions, he immediately nodded in agreement.

Fortunately, all of Rei's belongings were stored in the Misty Ring.

There was no need to go back to his room for anything; he simply left the house with Ragdo.

"Is Marina okay?"

"Of course she's needed. That's why we're heading there now."

"Huh? Then we're going to the wrong place."

Ragdo was heading toward Optis's house.

Noticing this, Rei spoke up, and Ragdo gave him a puzzled look.

"What do you mean?"

"Marina is over here."

Rei headed toward the house Elena and the others were renting.

The day before yesterday, Marina had slept at her own house... the house where Optis lived. But yesterday was different.

They'd partied late into the night at the banquet, so she'd decided to stay at Elena's rented house instead.

There had been other things discussed among the girls... specifically, they'd gotten quite animated talking about Rei, but Rei didn't know that.

Still, he knew Marina had stayed at Elena's rented house, so he headed there with Ragdo.

Ragdo grumbled a bit under his breath, but he probably understood that wasting time here was pointless.

He obediently followed Rei toward the house Elena and the others were renting.

Naturally, given the emergency, they didn't walk; both Rei and Ragdo ran.

What surprised Rei during the run was how unexpectedly fast Ragdo's legs were.

Given Ragdo's appearance, which could be described as that of a gentle young man, Ray had assumed he was terrible at physical activity...

That was what Rei thought, but he quickly dismissed the notion.

After all, he was a Dark Elf. There was no reason he should be unfit for physical activity.

Of course, not every Dark Elf was skilled at physical activity.

Just as everyone has their strengths and weaknesses, it was only natural that some dark elves might be physically uncoordinated.

Still, the reason Ray thought Ragdo was good at physical activity was solely because he worked under Optis.

It wasn't really a reason, but knowing who Optis was made it strangely understandable.

Soon, they arrived at the house Elena and the others were renting. Ragdo knocked on the door without hesitation.

It was less a knock and more a bang.

Fortunately, there were no other houses around the one Elena rented, but if there had been, the noise would have been a nuisance to the neighbors.

(Knock... no, it wasn't even a knock, more like a punch, right?)

The sound reverberating through the area must have reached inside the house too. Rei could hear movement and sense activity within.

Still, they didn't come out immediately, probably because they knew it was Rei outside.

If it had just been Ragdo, it might have been different, but wanting to show themselves in their sleepy state to someone they had feelings for... especially the day after a party? It was only natural for a woman not to want that.

Several tens of seconds passed after Ragdo stopped pounding on the door.

As Ragdo's frustration boiled over and he raised his fist again—the same fist reddened from his earlier actions—Rei stopped him.

"What are you doing?! Right now, we need to get to Lady Marina as quickly as possible..."

"Calm down."

"Hm?"

Ragdo froze momentarily at the small murmur that accompanied Rei's words.

Turning his gaze toward the source of the voice, he saw a girl who looked about ten years old.

Ragdo recognized her, of course.

She was the girl who had been with Rei and the others.

She hadn't stood out much among the three women boasting striking beauty—Elena, Vihera, and Marina—but he certainly wouldn't have missed someone in the group.

(Come to think of it, there was another woman wielding a battle axe too.)

Even as he pondered this, the conversation between Rei and Byune continued. ...Though whether it could even be called a conversation was debatable.

"Marina stayed here last night, right?"

"Mm."

"Then, sorry to ask, but could you go get Marina? Ragdo says something serious has happened."

"Mm."

Nodding, Byune headed back inside the house.

Watching him go, Rei turned his gaze back to Ragdo and spoke.

"So, what's going on that's got you in such a rush? We've got time before Marina comes out. Could you fill me in on the situation while we wait?"

"...Well, I'd rather explain everything at once, but... is that what you'd prefer?"

Ragdo must have realized that continuing like this would only waste time.

He weighed the effort required versus explaining the situation to Rei first, and ultimately chose the latter.

"Actually, I went to the World Tree this morning... and when I did... no, when Rei poured his magic into the World Tree yesterday, it should have been regaining its strength. But it was clearly weakened."

"...What?"

This was unexpected news even for Rei.

His infusion of magic should have compensated for its magic deficiency and restored it.

After all, it had emitted an intense light after he finished pouring his magic into it.

A dazzling radiance, as if it were declaring itself full of vitality.

That was precisely why both the Dark Elves and Rei's group had concluded the World Tree's treatment was complete, and that while it might take time, the forest's abnormalities would gradually diminish...

"If the World Tree weakened, does that mean its illness wasn't cured after all?"

Faced with Rei's obvious question, Ragdo frowned, struggling to find words.

Even to Ragdo's eyes, the dazzling radiance the World Tree had emitted yesterday suggested it had fully recovered.

And indeed, if one considered only yesterday, the World Tree had undoubtedly been in a state that could only be described as fully recovered.

Yet for some reason, when Ragdo went to the World Tree this morning, it was clearly weaker than yesterday.

Thinking about it that way, it was actually more plausible that it hadn't fully recovered.

But... even so, seeing the World Tree yesterday, it was impossible to believe it hadn't actually recovered.

And above all, Marina, who shared a deep connection with the World Tree, had stated yesterday that it had recovered.

The Ragdo who first met Rei might have suspected Marina was covering for him, perhaps even lying to protect him.

But the Ragged of today has seen Rei's power up close and understands it better than anyone.

Even so, the fact that the World Tree he saw this morning was clearly weakened was undeniable... and that internal contradiction made Ragdo hesitate to nod in response to Rei's question.

After a few seconds of hesitation, Ragdo finally shook his head.

"No. Honestly, based on what I saw of the World Tree yesterday, it certainly didn't look like the disease hadn't been cured. ...That's precisely why it's abnormal."

"W-What do you mean? Do you have any idea what could be causing this?"

Rei, who had been talking with Ragdo, shifted his gaze to behind Byune, who had just appeared in the doorway.

Standing there with a stern expression was Marina.

It wasn't exactly early morning, but it was still a time when most of the villagers from last night's banquet were still asleep.

Despite the hour, Marina wore a different party dress than yesterday.

She had also worn a party dress when they went exploring in the forest, albeit a different color and style, so for Marina, whether it was early morning or deep in the woods, wearing a party dress held no sense of incongruity.

Marina hadn't heard all of Ragdo's story.

Even so, she'd grasped the general situation just from hearing part of it.

When Rei asked her, Marina shook her head.

"No, I have absolutely no idea. At the very least, when Rei and I poured magic into the World Tree yesterday, he was definitely fully cured and had regained his strength."

"Then something happened between when we finished channeling magic and this morning? Did someone suspicious approach the World Tree?"

"Impossible!"

Ragdo immediately refuted Rei's words.

This wasn't out of concern for his comrade. It was purely because last night, the Dark Elves had held a celebratory feast all night long in a spot where they could see the World Tree.

If anyone had approached, they would have definitely noticed.

"So the only possibility is the short window after the feast ended and before Ragdo went to the World Tree. ...Anyway, Marina seems ready too. Let's go check the World Tree."

"Rei, we'll come with you."

Elena and Vihera each announced this as they appeared.

Both were already fully prepared; Elena carried the sheath holding Mirage, while Vihera wore gauntlets and greaves.

Considering it a waste to waste time arguing, and above all, judging that these two combatants wouldn't be a hindrance, he suddenly noticed someone missing from the scene.

"Where's Ara?"

"Seems she drank too much,"

Elena's words, accompanied by a wry smile, made it clear she was still asleep.

"Got it. Let's go then."

Saying that, Rei and the others started running. Byune waved them off without changing her expression... then let out a small yawn and simply went back inside the house.
