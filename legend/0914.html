"Lord Iskeld... It's Lord Iskeld!"

"Wh-why is the Knight Commander of the Quent Duchy here!?"

"How should I know? Maybe he came to defeat that monster?"

"Wait, didn't he mention something about a request?"

"Yeah. He definitely did. I heard him."

"So then, that monster... the griffin isn't an enemy after all?"

"Probably. And even if it were an enemy, don't you think with Lord Iskeld here, we could handle anything?"

"I won't deny that. He's one of the strongest even among the royalist faction."

"Actually, the fact that there are multiple people strong enough to go toe-to-toe with Lord Iskeld just shows how powerful the royalist faction is, huh?"

Hearing the surrounding voices, Iskeld breathed a silent sigh of relief.

He didn't particularly like being the subject of rumors, but it was fortunate that the residents had escaped their confusion.

His massive frame, easily exceeding two meters, might appear like a giant to some.

It wasn't just his height; his body was packed with dense muscle, and his face bore features that could rightly be called ruggedly handsome.

Though only in his mid-thirties, his aura readily suggested he'd survived well over a hundred battles.

It was no wonder everyone was startled when such a man, the commander of the Duke Quent's Knights, appeared at the gate.

Even Rei, approaching the gate alongside Set, could easily sense his presence.

(Huh. ...Strong. He seems to possess power on par with Rank A.)

The fact that he could remain calm even when facing an opponent of Rank A caliber was likely because he had experience fighting Noiz of an even higher rank... Rank S.

While he felt he had essentially been overlooked, the fact remained that he had faced Noiz head-on.

That's precisely why he could address Iskeld without particular agitation, even with the entity standing before him.

"I'm Rei. I came here on a request..."

"Hmm. I've heard about it. That's precisely why I came out to greet you like this, to avoid any commotion. Follow me."

Iskeld glanced at Rei and, even with Set's presence close by, showed no sign of agitation as he spoke.

Both understood the other's strength, yet they exchanged words as if nothing unusual had happened.

"...What about the entry procedures?"

"I'll handle it. Ah, show me your guild card, just in case."

"Understood."

He handed the guild card he'd taken out from within the Misty Ring to Iskeld, and the procedure was completed without fuss.

He placed the familiar necklace, handed over along with the guild card, around Set's neck. The two humans and one beast entered the Graciall.

"Sorry about that. I didn't expect everyone to be watching us like this."

"It's fine. When you're with Set, this kind of attention comes with the territory."

"Gururu?"

Hearing his name called, Set turned his gaze toward Rei and Iskeld, asking with his round eyes, What is it?

Even knowing he was a griffin, Isceld couldn't feel any hostility toward Set, who looked straight at him with those round, innocent eyes.

Just looking at him, one instantly understood his lovable nature. Iskeld let a small smile touch his stern face as he spoke.

"They said 'Deep Crimson and her familiar, the Griffin,' so I was expecting some kind of monster... I never imagined such an adorable creature would show up."

"Set is basically friendly, you know. It's different with enemies, but maybe he's like me in that way."

"...If you understand that, you should have shown a little more restraint. This incident was indeed caused by the royalist faction... I hate to say it, but it was the arrogance of those royalist nobles. But isn't part of the reason it escalated this far also down to you?"

"Well, I suppose so. But I'm certain that if I hadn't intervened, the King's faction noble's infamy would have spread throughout Gilm. Apparently, they'd already caused quite a stir before clashing with me."

As they exchanged words while walking, they eventually passed through the gate and finally arrived inside Glacial.

"Huh. ...It's developed quite a bit."

"Of course. It is Duke Quento's stronghold, after all."

Even as he answered, he clearly didn't mind having his home praised. Iskeld wore a proud smile.

In terms of sheer scale, the city of Graciall was clearly larger than Gilm.

First and foremost, the number of people was different.

Gilm had flourished more than adequately as the sole city on the frontier, but what spread before Rei's eyes seemed to hold several times Gilm's population.

(The difference between a Marquis and a Duke, the difference between the frontier and a peaceful place—that much makes sense, but honestly, this is impressive.)

While admiring Graciall's appearance, Rei suddenly remembered the letter he'd received from Marca before leaving Gilm.

"Come to think of it, Marca gave me a letter to pass to the gate guard. Guess it wasn't needed since someone as important as you showed up. Um... Iskeld, was it?"

"...Ah, I haven't introduced myself yet. I'm Iskeld. I serve as the Knight Commander of the Duke of Quento's Knight Order."

"I'm Rei. I'm a Rank B adventurer in Gilm."

As Rei continued his introduction, Iskeld gave him a wry smile.

His face usually had a stern look, but when he smiled wryly, he looked surprisingly young.

"I know you. In fact, if there's anyone in the Kingdom of Mireana today who doesn't know Rei, they'd be considered a fraud."

Rei was momentarily surprised by the shift in tone, which had lost its stiffness compared to just seconds ago, but he didn't comment on it and replied.

"...But then, it seems you didn't know about Keep, who belongs to the King's faction?"

Iskeld frowned slightly, clearly stung by the remark, and opened his mouth.

"He's an exception in every sense. To begin with, using such an unworthy individual as a wedge to infiltrate Gilm is a conspiracy of the lowest order. ...If it were a conspiracy meant to damage the King's faction, then perhaps he could be considered competent."

Iskeld stated this with deep conviction. Yet, as they walked the streets, many recognized Iskeld's true identity: soldiers stood at attention and saluted, adventurers wore expressions of surprise, and citizens gazed at him with admiration.

Nodding to them, raising a hand lightly, returning smiles—all while doing so, Rei and Iskeld exchanged words.

...Though it wasn't just Iskeld who stood out; Rei and Set being present played a significant role too.

While Gilm might be familiar, this city of Graciall was a first for both Rei and Set.

Naturally, nearly everyone seeing a griffin was a first-timer, and under normal circumstances, chaos would be expected.

Yet the fact that there was hardly any commotion at all was likely because Iskeld was with them.

"If someone like that were around, it would be quite troublesome. ...Though thankfully, it didn't come to that this time."

"Yeah. Though if there were someone like that, I'd recommend Duke Quento hire them."

He exchanged words with Rei while also greeting those who approached him—proof he was accustomed to this sort of task.

And for the residents of Gracielle, seeing Rei and Set alongside their proud Knight Commander naturally drew plenty of attention from others.

"Hey, that griffin you're bringing along..."

"No mistake, it's Crimson."

Adventurers and merchants with keen eyes for information quickly recognized its identity...

"Male? Female? What do you think?"

"Can't tell with that hood, but with that build, it wouldn't be weird if it were a woman, right?"

"...A woman commanding a griffin? She looks like a formidable villain."

"Really? To me, she feels like a mysterious woman."

"A woman!? A beauty!? A gorgeous woman!?"

"Wow, your true feelings are totally showing. Honestly, I wouldn't want to get close to someone like that."

"Wait, wasn't the rumor that Shinku was a man?"

Rei, Isceld, and Set continued on, sowing various topics of conversation. Suddenly, Rei's gaze stopped at a certain food stall.

It was a food stall selling something that looked unfamiliar, yet somehow familiar—a dish he knew.

They were using udon noodles.

However, it wasn't the kind of udon commonly served in Gilm with soup poured over it.

Instead, it featured something like a meat miso paste poured over boiled udon noodles—a dish that didn't exist in Gilm... or if it did, it was one Rei had never seen before.

(Like jjajangmyeon or dry dan dan noodles?)

Of course, it probably wasn't the same minced pork paste Rei knew. The seasonings were different to begin with.

But it looks similar to those dishes... and for a moment, something sharp flashes through Rei's mind.

(No. It's not jjajangmyeon or dry dan dan noodles. I'm sure I saw it on TV... yes, something called jjajangmyeon, a dish from Morioka. It's close to that. ...I only saw it on TV, I've never actually eaten it.)

That dish is one of the Three Great Morioka Noodles, alongside wanko soba and hiyashi soba.

Of course, Rei's knowledge was limited to what he'd seen on TV; he didn't know what it was really like.

Precisely because of that, Rei's desire to try the dish at the food stall now in his line of sight grew stronger.

She turned her gaze toward Isceld, but he seemed completely oblivious, walking down the main street.

"Grrr..."

Set, walking beside Rei, seemed interested in the dish too.

Set was quite fond of udon noodles himself, often eating them from stalls in Gilm.

Especially warm udon eaten amidst the swirling snow in winter was one of life's greatest pleasures.

"Aren't you eating? Hey, aren't you eating? I want some," Set pleaded with his round eyes. Rei, who had already been tempted to try the dish himself, couldn't resist...

"Iskeld, can I talk to you for a moment?"

"Hm? What is it?"

"I'd like to make a little detour."

Rei's gaze was fixed on a food stall.

Iskeld looked slightly surprised at Rei's words, but then, as if suddenly remembering something, he smiled and spoke.

"Well, that dish was created by Lord Maruka when he visited Gilm last year. It might be interesting to ask Rei, who came from Gilm, about its taste."

"Well, it's more like it's becoming a famous dish in Gilm, right?"

"That's what I've heard. Though apparently it's quite different from the one in Gilm."

"I won't deny that."

Thinking of Gilm, Rei felt he should deny it, but his curiosity to try it won out.

The sight of numerous other stalls wafting all sorts of delicious smells only intensified his desire.

(Then again, this world doesn't have copyright or anything like that... Besides, it's just food. It's not like it's rare in Japan for dishes to be adapted into something different.)

Convincing himself... or forcing himself to accept it, he followed Iskeld toward the food stalls.

What surprised him at that moment was probably the stall owner.

Iskeld, a famous figure in this Gracia, was heading straight for his stall.

And he was bringing Rei and Griffon with him.

"Old man, three cups, please."

"H-heh."

Still, being a businessman, he quickly snapped back to reality and began cooking.

The cooking itself wasn't difficult at all.

Just boil the udon, drain it, and top it with what looked like a meat-based miso seasoning.

(I wish I had chopsticks...)

Eating the udon with a fork, Rei thought deeply.

If it were regular udon where you just pour soup over it, a fork wouldn't be a problem. But with this type, where you mix something like meat miso into the noodles, a fork just isn't as good for stirring as chopsticks.

Though, in Set's case, it wasn't about chopsticks—he was just sticking his beak right into the bowl and eating.

"Gurururuu"

Set happily gurgled as he ate his udon, but his beak was stained with the meat miso seasoning.

(I'll have to wipe it after he finishes eating.)

Thinking he'd have to get a cloth from the Misty Ring... he finished stirring the udon with his fork and brought it to his mouth.

It felt like eating pasta with meat sauce, but the taste of this udon was quite different.

What looked like meat miso was more likely a sauce made by reducing something down, giving it the richness of a thick demi-glace sauce.

It mixed with the water still clinging to the noodles after draining, making it cling easily to the udon.

The rich sauce flavor combined with the savory meat umami created a distinct, deep taste that spread through my mouth, different from jajamen or dry tantanmen.

The udon noodles themselves, perhaps because they were cut thicker, held their own against the seasoning.

Its deliciousness was so compelling that I could have eaten it forever, and the noodles kept disappearing into my mouth one after another.

(Though... if the same flavor kept going, I'd probably get tired of it. Maybe some kind of condiment would be nice.)

Thinking such thoughts while slurping the noodles, Rei finished the bowl.

"...That was delicious."
