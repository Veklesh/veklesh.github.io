"Okay, that should do it."

"Grr!"

At Rei’s words, Set let out a cry as if to agree.

If you looked at that scene alone, it was quite heartwarming... but right next to that man and his companion, a Snow Cyclops’s corpse—its head severed—hung upside down from a tree, and a considerable amount of blood was trickling down the trunk from the severed neck onto the ground, creating a rather gruesome sight.

This was a grove located some distance from Gilm—though “some distance” was relative, given that Set had flown there.

In the middle of that grove, he had hung the Snow Cyclops’s corpse from a particularly large tree and was draining the blood.

Originally, they had intended to hang the corpse from a branch, but unfortunately, a branch could never support the massive body of a Cyclops—five meters tall… though it was a bit smaller now that its head was missing.

As a result, the method Rei chose was not to support the Snow Cyclops’s carcass with a single branch, but to thread a rope through several branches and hang the carcass along the tree trunk to bleed it out.

And just as Rei had planned, the bloodletting of the suspended Snow Cyclops was proceeding smoothly.

“Still, I never expected a high-rank monster like this to show up. …Well, this is the frontier of Gilm, so it wouldn’t be strange if one did, but until now, it’s mostly been Kobolds.”

“Guruu? …Guruu!”

Set, who had been surveying the surroundings, replied to Rei’s words.

That said, even Rei couldn’t tell what, if any, intention lay behind Set’s reply at that moment.

If anything, it sounded like a half-hearted reply.

Still, as long as the scent of the Snow Cyclops’ blood lingered in the air, the possibility that monsters might be drawn here by it could never be ruled out.

For Set, it was only natural to focus his attention on that.

Rei, too, knew the reason behind Set’s actions, so he said nothing more and simply watched the Snow Cyclops continue to bleed.

(Come to think of it, since I don’t know which parts of this monster can be used as materials, I should probably leave the butchering to the guild after all. That guild employee said something along those lines, too. Though, before that, I’ll need to extract the magic stone first.)

Since the Snow Cyclops was a monster they’d never seen before, its magic stone would naturally serve as fuel for Rei’s—or, more accurately, Death Scythe and Set’s—beast magic.

Lately, with everything going on, I haven’t been absorbing any magic stones, so this will be my first time in a while.

...Just as he thought that, Rei recalled what happened yesterday.

“Ah, come to think of it, Set absorbed a Kobold’s magic stone yesterday. …Though in the end, he didn’t learn any new skills.”

“Grr?”

“Did you call me?” Set growls at Rei, but Rei shakes his head, indicating it’s nothing.

As he did so, he turned his gaze back to the Snow Cyclops’s corpse… and seeing that blood was still flowing from its neck, he let out a small sigh.

Even for a five-meter-tall giant, isn’t that a bit too much blood? he thought to himself.

“Well, it is a monster… so I guess it wouldn’t be strange for that to happen, right?”

Of course, Rei had no idea exactly how much blood a monster actually contained.

No, come to think of it, if you consider how many people actually know the details of that, probably no one does.

(Goblins… no, there’s no point in raising goblins. That said, if there were someone farming Orcs—which have tasty meat and are highly fertile—then that would be a different story.)

As he pondered such things, after a dozen or so minutes… the blood flowing from the Snow Cyclops’ neck finally stopped.

“That monster really bled a lot. …Well, whatever. Now then, Set. Let’s at least extract the magic stone for now. After all, you’re the one who’s going to absorb it.”

“Grr?”

At Rei’s words, Set tilted his head, as if to ask, “Is that okay?”

Rei stroked Set’s head and nodded, as if to say, “Of course.”

“This guy’s name is Snow Cyclops. And he used a blizzard… an Ice Breath, too. Given that, Set, who’s learned Ice Arrow, is better than Death Scythe, who hasn’t learned any ice-based skills. …Well, it might not be Ice Arrow—he might learn a different ice-based skill, or he might not be able to learn any skills at all.”

Although the chances were slim, if there was even a possibility that Set’s Ice Arrow might be strengthened, choosing that path was only natural.

(Set’s Ice Arrow is currently at Level 4. If that’s the case, if things go well… really well, that is—there’s a chance it could reach Level 5. And once it reaches Level 5, that skill will be strengthened more than it has been with any previous level-ups.)

That’s right—Beast Magic skills become significantly more powerful once they reach Level 5.

If there was even a chance of that happening, there was no way he wasn’t going to take it.

“Alright, Set. I’m going to take out the magic stone, so just wait a moment.”

Rei muttered as he looked at the Snow Cyclops, which he had cut loose and laid on its side on the ground.

Given its massive size, laying it on its side was a bit of a struggle, but he pushed through, determined to get the magic stone.

Bending his knees and adjusting his position so his hands wouldn’t bump into the surrounding trees, Rei took a knife out of his Misty Ring as he looked at the Snow Cyclops, now safely lying on the ground.

And it wasn’t just any old knife he used for butchering monsters—it was a top-quality, extremely sharp blade.

It was clear from cutting it with the Death Scythe and even just touching it now that the Snow Cyclops’ skin was incredibly tough.

It was a hassle that it remained so tough even in death, but if he convinced himself that this was simply because it was a high-rank monster, the thought that Set might be able to acquire a new skill by absorbing its magic stone grew stronger.

While this is merely a general trend and not an absolute rule, the higher the rank of the monster, the more likely it is that Set will acquire a skill upon absorbing its magic stone.

Considering that, this Snow Cyclops was a monster I could definitely count on.

“Huh. It’s putting up quite a fight.”

The knife Rei was holding was a high-end model, even among butchering knives.

As such, it was extremely sharp—so much so that if handled carelessly, it wouldn’t be surprising to lose a finger or two.

Even with a blade like that, cutting into the Snow Cyclops’ chest met with considerable resistance.

Rei felt it might be quicker to just use the Death Scythe, but since it would be a hassle to do that at this point, he focused on making a clean cut right through the heart.

Normally, blood would have flowed, but that was likely because he had bled it out thoroughly. A little… just a tiny bit of blood flowed, but that was all.

Rei reached into the Snow Cyclops’ body through the wound, feeling around for the heart… eventually, his hand touched something with a familiar firmness, and he pulled it out.

It was probably because he hadn’t managed to extract just the magic stone. A sensation like tearing flesh transmitted to Rei’s hand, but even so, he didn’t seem particularly bothered by it and successfully removed the magic stone from the body.

“Phew, that was quite a hassle. …Still, I can’t exactly let someone who knows nothing about it see me absorbing a magic stone.”

He could have had the people dismantling the Giganto Turtle extract the magic stone for him, but then the question of where to absorb it would have arisen.

Of course, he didn’t have to watch the Giganto Turtle’s disassembly the whole time, so he could have simply taken the magic stone and moved somewhere else with Set to absorb it.

But if I’m going to go to all that trouble, it’s actually quicker to just extract the magic stone myself like this.

I washed the extracted magic stone with water created by the Dagger of Flowing Water and tossed it to Set.

Set catches it with his beak and swallows it whole...

[Set has learned the skill "Power Crush Lv. 6"]

That announcement echoed in my mind.

“……Oh?”

It’s fine that he learned the skill.

After all, that was exactly what Rei had expected.

However, if the skill he’d acquired had been an ice-based skill like Ice Arrow or even Ice Breath, Rei would have been satisfied… but “Power Crush” was clearly unexpected, even for Rei.

“Huh?”

Set, too, had been told there was a high probability he could learn an ice-based skill from Rei, so he showed a slight look of confusion that it turned out to be Power Crush.

Watching Set’s reaction and the corpse of the Snow Cyclops, from which the magic stone had been extracted, Rei felt a sense of understanding.

Come to think of it, it made perfect sense: before Rei and the others arrived, the Snow Cyclops had been killing and eating the Kobolds with its brute strength.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange for his Power Crush level to go up instead of an ice-based skill.

“Ah… yeah. Well, it was a little different from what I expected, but rather than not learning any skills at all, I guess I should just be glad that Power Crush’s level went up.”

At Rei’s words, Set thought for a moment… and eventually, he made a sound as if to agree.

It was true that he would have preferred Ice Arrow’s level to go up, but as Rei had said, it was certainly better than nothing happening at all.

“Anyway, we’ve absorbed the magic stones, so let’s head back to the Giganto Turtle dissection site. We can have them handle the dissection of the other parts over there.”

Saying that, Rei stored the Snow Cyclops’s corpse in the Misty Ring and took off, riding on Set.

“Ah, Rei. You’re back. It seems to have taken quite a while for a simple bloodletting…”

A guild employee approached Rei, who had landed on the ground riding Set, and said this.

...Behind the guild employee, several adventurers, civilians skilled in butchering, and residents of the slums led by Suchi were busy butchering the Giganto Turtle.

Meanwhile, a short distance away, the guards were fighting the Kobolds...

“It seems there was more Snow Cyclops blood than we expected. It took a while to finish draining it. That’s why there are so few Kobolds left.”

He says this to a guild official.

Normally, since they’d built an earthen wall to keep them out of Gilm, the number of Kobolds coming here should have increased significantly compared to yesterday.

But as far as Rei could see, there weren’t that many Kobold corpses lying on the ground.

And the number of adventurer guards currently fighting the Kobolds wasn’t that high either.

In other words, there were far fewer Kobolds than Rei had expected.

“Huh? Oh, right. Probably, the Snow Cyclops killed most of the Kobolds before we got here this morning. …There weren’t many Kobold corpses, but that’s probably…”

He didn’t need to finish his sentence; Rei understood what he was getting at.

After all, Rei had seen the Snow Cyclops tearing the Kobolds to pieces with his own eyes.

“But if that’s the case, it means that Snow Cyclops must have eaten almost all of the Kobolds that appeared in such large numbers… Is that even possible?”

“I’m not sure. However, when we left Gilm, there was only one Snow Cyclops here, but that doesn’t mean there was only one here during the night. It’s possible that the others returned in the early morning, and that one remained here for some reason.”

Hearing that, Rei had no choice but to agree.

That said, it was undoubtedly because he’d been hoping—if possible—that more Snow Cyclopses would appear… or at the very least, that one more would show up so the Death Scythe could absorb a magic stone.

“I don’t know if any Snow Cyclopses will appear tomorrow… I wonder.”

“Well, we’ll just have to wait and see tomorrow. …Speaking of which, about that request to build an earthen wall…”

“Ah, that’s right. …Well, we might not need one for Kobolds this size, though.”

In fact, perhaps partly because we’d significantly increased our escort numbers after the Kobolds stopped entering Gilm, we were currently able to defeat them without any trouble.

I couldn’t help but wonder if we really needed to build an earth wall with just this many, but when I considered whether things would stay this way indefinitely, I concluded that it would still be better to build one.
