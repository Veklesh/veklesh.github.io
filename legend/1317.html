He was caught up in the power struggle at Spier Trading Company.

Even upon hearing those words from Tris's mouth, Rei strangely did not show any anger.

Rei himself found it strange, yet at the same time, he understood.

Of course, if Tris standing before him had orchestrated this incident, Ray would have drawn his Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and struck in an instant.

But the person now before Rei's eyes was apologizing to him.

That was proof Tris wasn't the one behind this.

Still, not knowing for certain, Rei asked Tris.

"A power struggle within the Spier Trading Company? So, which side are you on? Did you know nothing about this... or..."

...were you the one who plotted it?

As Rei asked this, he gave off no aura of murderous intent or anger.

He wasn't emitting it... but precisely because of that, Tris, who had witnessed it, couldn't bring himself to lie or deceive the current Rei.

"Of course, I know of your power, Rei. No one would be so reckless as to lay a hand on you. While there's no clear evidence, I suspect the one who plotted this was Precious—the one you encountered in the corridor earlier."

The moment that name was spoken, Rei felt not surprise, but rather a sense of confirmation, almost like "I knew it."

Terry, Tris's subordinate, had reacted so strongly. If Precious were claimed to be completely unrelated now, that would have been the real surprise.

"Fighting for power within the Spier Trading Company is your business. But why do we have to get dragged into it? Or rather, what were you thinking dragging us into this? Should I ask that instead?"

"As you know, Spier Trading Company began full-scale operations in Gilm this spring."

"Yeah. I heard about that before."

The Spier Trading Company's merchants had been active in Gilm before.

But most of that was procurement; they didn't have branches in Gilm and weren't conducting large-scale operations.

A frontier like Gilm naturally has endless business opportunities lying around.

After all, there are countless items available only in Gilm.

Even so, the number of shops with branches in Gilm isn't particularly high.

...Though "few" might be an understatement. Considering how many trading companies exist in the Mireana Kingdom, that "few" is only relative to the entire kingdom. In reality, quite a few companies do have branches in Gilm.

For that reason, establishing a branch in Gilm is also a goal for many trading houses.

"...So, Precious was displeased that Tris was entrusted with the Gilm branch. Is that it?"

For members of the Spier Trading Company, being entrusted with a branch in Gilm is naturally an honor, and at the same time, proof that one's abilities have been recognized by superiors.

Beyond that, being entrusted with a branch in Gilm brings immense, truly enormous benefits—both as a merchant and as an individual.

Given that, it's hardly surprising that someone might covet Tris's position.

...Though, it was precisely because Tris tried a little too hard that Mayo's company, who was busy researching how to cook goblin meat deliciously with Rei, ended up getting caught in the crossfire.

In any case, it wouldn't be surprising if some people disliked Tris's enthusiastic efforts.

"Yes, that's correct."

Tris nodded, the flesh on her cheeks jiggling, though given the subject at hand, it was hardly a laughing matter.

"I hate to say it myself, but I consider myself fairly competent within the Spier Trading Company."

"...I suppose so. Since you're being entrusted with a branch of Gilm, you absolutely have to be competent."

Having worked as Guildmaster just hours ago, Marina knew exactly what it meant to be entrusted with a branch office at Gilm.

Tris, praised by such a figure as Marina, nodded before speaking again.

"Yes. But Precious, whom you all met earlier, is also known as a capable merchant within the Spier Trading Company. ...She seems to have a tendency toward somewhat forceful deals, but apparently it's not enough to be a problem."

Tris sighed, but to Rei, it felt like she was being rather harsh.

Rei had asked Pamidor to forge a White Cloud using Silver Lion materials.

He had practically barged into Pamidor's smithy demanding they hand it over.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, Tris apparently didn't know who would be using the White Cloud. Once he learned it was Rei... or more precisely, Byune (the female member of Rei's party) who would be using it, he backed off quietly.

Considering that, Tris probably wasn't forcing an unreasonable deal after all.

"So that Precious one... ultimately wanted to drag you down from your current position?"

To Vihera's question, Tris nodded in response... and right at that moment, Terry returned to the office accompanied by the maid.

Light snacks like tea, sandwiches, dried fruit, and sweet baked bread were laid out on the table.

The one whose eyes lit up at the sight was Byune, who had been listening impassively yet bored.

Perhaps the sandwich she'd eaten in the carriage earlier hadn't been enough, for she immediately reached for the sandwiches on the plate.

Several maids smiled at Byune's antics, but once they finished their preparations, they left the room with Terry.

Rei briefly wondered why Terry had left the room too, but decided it wasn't worth worrying about for now. Reaching for a sandwich himself, he spoke again.

"Let's get back to the point. So, the reason Precious orchestrated this whole affair..."

"Simply put, she wanted to cause trouble for me, the one in charge of the Gilm branch. And the bigger the trouble, the better. Like creating a reason for Azoth Trading Company, which holds significant influence even within Gilm, to clash with that notorious adventurer."

"That... but wouldn't that be disastrous if it failed? And even if it succeeded, wouldn't it be pointless?"

Marina and Vihera nodded in agreement at Rei's tilted head.

The fact remained that even if it succeeded, Tris... or more accurately, the Spier Trading Company, would still have made the Azoth Trading Company an enemy.

If Precious was thinking of taking Tris's position overseeing the Spier Trading Company's Gilm branch, it was utterly misguided, no matter how you looked at it.

What would happen to a trading house that had just opened a branch in Gilm if it drew the ire of Azoth Trading Company, which still held considerable influence in Gilm, albeit not as strong as before?

Logically, the only option would be to withdraw from Gilm.

So why make such an obvious move?

Tris set down the tea she was drinking on the table and shook her head at that question.

"Unfortunately, I don't know the answer to that either. And as I said earlier, the idea that this was Precious's doing is based solely on circumstantial evidence. There's no definitive physical proof. I was thinking of clarifying that point earlier, but..."

"I see. So you didn't let us pin anything down, and then you met with us on the way back."

"Yes. Unfortunately, that's how it is. After all, we can't do anything about Precious based on circumstantial evidence alone."

Seeing him sigh, it was clear Tris was lamenting his own lack of power.

And he was clearly resentful of Precious's meticulous planning, which deserved the label "well-prepared."

"Would it be... impossible for me to take action?"

Rei knew how others perceived him.

If that's the case, wouldn't it be okay to force contact with Precious, even if it's a bit aggressive?

He said this, but Tris silently shook her head.

"This is Precious we're dealing with—she's planned everything meticulously. She's undoubtedly factored Rei in. If that's the case, she's definitely prepared countermeasures."

"Looks like they've got some firepower too."

"The ones who attacked Azimov?"

"Yes."

Marina and Vihera exchanged brief words.

A merchant with considerable power wouldn't be unusual to have their own guards, nor would it be strange for them to have someone handling behind-the-scenes matters discreetly.

If they had that kind of firepower, even if Rei and the others did something, it wouldn't necessarily mean they could handle it... but they could buy time and call the guards.

Rei wondered if calling the guards wasn't suicide for Precious too. But they'd caused all this trouble without leaving a single piece of evidence.

He was undoubtedly confident he could handle any investigation that came his way.

"Then what do you suggest we do? We know Precious orchestrated this whole thing. Are we just going to leave it at that?"

To those who knew her personality, Rei's tone as she asked this seemed uncharacteristically subdued.

But... precisely because he was quiet, the anger simmering beneath was deep and intense.

"Well, yes. Personally, I'd prefer to do just that... but judging by your reaction, Rei, it seems you can't choose that path. In that case, we need to make a different choice."

"I suppose I agree. I'm not particularly fond of just sitting around waiting for the other side to make their move."

"Hmm."

Vihera and Byune agreed with the proactive approach.

Given Vihera's naturally proactive nature, she dislikes waiting for the other side to make the first move.

It's not that she couldn't do it if she wanted to, but she probably decided it wasn't necessary this time.

"If we make a clumsy move here, I think it could lead to all sorts of unpleasant consequences."

Marina voiced a cautious opinion to the two.

Marina also tends to favor aggressive tactics, but if everyone were on the offensive, the balance would be off.

That's precisely why she was taking the cautious stance now.

"If we just stay quiet here, wouldn't there be a chance they'll get a taste for this situation and act even more violently?"

"I don't think we need to worry about that,"

Tris dismissed Rei's doubt.

Why? When Ray's gaze demanded an explanation, Tris continued as if it were obvious.

"Precious must know that meeting with you and Rei means their information has reached us. Therefore, they should understand that any reckless actions here would only strengthen suspicion against them."

"...Well, I wish he hadn't acted strangely in the first place, then."

Anger still lingered, but within Rei, a sense of utter bewilderment grew stronger.

Rei had expected this incident to be a plot targeting either him or the Azoth Trading Company... or both.

But in reality, it turned out they'd just been dragged into a power struggle within the Spier Trading Company. Well, what can you do?

Of course, if asked whether he could forgive Precious for using him, the answer was no. Combined with the matter involving Azimov, Rei had already decided how he would ultimately deal with it.

As if sensing Rei's anger, Tris's smile vanished for an instant... but the next moment, it returned – a smile without warmth in the eyes.

"Your anger is entirely justified, Rei. But... regarding this matter, might we be allowed to handle it ourselves?"

For Tris, this incident felt like Precious had openly challenged him to a fight.

Given that, he wanted to handle it himself if possible.

As the person entrusted with this Gilm branch, it was only natural to think that way.

It wasn't wrong. It absolutely wasn't wrong.

Except... that was assuming the person sitting before Tris right now wasn't Rei.

"Hmm?"

It was calm, not loud at all. One might even say it was close to a whisper.

Yet the instant Tris heard those words, a chill ran down his spine. ...Like ice formed in the dead of winter, even though spring had already arrived.

Still, she didn't let her unease show on her face or break out in cold sweat. That was likely down to her experience.

It was a situation ready to explode at any moment... Facing someone who could erupt violently if provoked, Tris managed to speak.

"We'll make any concessions we can. And if you demand something as an apology for this incident, we'll pay whatever is possible. So... please, can we make a deal?"

"...Understood."

After a few seconds of silence, Tris exhaled in relief at the words that came from Rei's mouth.

He judged his opinion had been accepted...

"So, the Spier Trading Company chooses to oppose me. Is that it?"

At those words from Rei's mouth, he froze.
