Rei, Samaloon, and Set walk through the grounds of the military academy.

It was a winter night, and though no snow fell, a cold wind howled.

The moon in the sky was hidden by clouds, and the moonlight wasn't particularly bright.

Though it was the kind of night no one would normally want to be outside, Samaloon was the only one shivering in the winter cold.

Rei had his Dragon Robe, and Set, riding his griffin, didn't care about this level of cold at all.

But Samaloon, being an ordinary human, couldn't help but shiver in this cold.

"Ugh... After feeling warm from Rei-san's skill earlier, this feels even colder now."

"You look pretty well-dressed. Still cold?"

Rei asked Samaloon, who was muttering coldly.

From Rei's perspective, judging by how puffy his robe was, he was undoubtedly wearing several layers underneath.

Even so, Rei is still cold? Rei wondered, but given Samaloon's naturally thin build, he probably just couldn't handle the cold well.

"From my perspective, it's amazing you're not cold at all wearing just that one robe, Rei."

"Well, this is a magic item, you see."

"I envy you... I envy you so much."

Perhaps feeling pity for Samaloon, who was chatting away as if to distract himself from the cold, Rei gently stroked Set's back.

That alone seemed to tell him what Rei wanted. Set purred and moved from beside Rei to closer to Samaloon.

"Huh? What's this... Rei?"

"It'll be cold until we reach the building with the Headmaster's office. If you touch Set, you should stay a bit warmer. Riding him... well, Set would have to allow it."

"Um, well... is that okay? Really?"

Samaroon timidly asked Rei and Set, but Set purred, his attitude clearly indicating it was fine.

Understanding that this throat-clearing wasn't meant to be intimidating, Samaroon finally broke into a delighted smile.

For Samaloon, who held a deep fascination with magic, Set—or more accurately, griffins—were incredibly intriguing creatures.

High-rank monsters could effortlessly manipulate magical power.

That very fact made them invaluable subjects for magical research, and it was true that high-rank monsters had long piqued his interest.

However, opportunities to encounter rank A monsters... or even higher rank S monsters, were few and far between. In that sense, the griffin Set was practically a godsend for Samaloon.

"Grrrr..."

Set urged Samaloon to touch him, as if saying it was okay.

Seeing Set like this, Samaloon timidly reached out... and eventually touched Set's back.

Her eyes widened at the smooth feel of Set's fur, and she began stroking his back repeatedly.

About a minute passed like this. Before he could even think about how they'd be late arriving at the Headmaster's office, Rei, who could sense, hear, and see the small bird flying above them, felt sorry for Samaroon but spoke up.

"Samaroon, stop that. We should head to the principal's office soon, or we'll get even more scoldings than usual."

Rei knew just how pleasant the feel of Set's skin was.

Even though Rei had stroked Set so many times it was ridiculous to count, he never grew tired of that touch.

Given how strangely captivating Set was, this first time Samaloon got to stroke him must have been an absolutely sublime experience.

"Ah... I've gone to the stables several times before to check on Set, but I only ever watched from a distance. Looking back now, why didn't I stroke him and enjoy this feeling? I feel like an idiot."

"You really think so? ...Well, it's not unreasonable."

Even those two female adventurers who are huge Set fans claimed that sensation was nothing short of supreme pleasure.

By normal standards, Samaloon's words were probably just common sense.

But perhaps precisely because I'm always with Set, I don't truly appreciate how precious he is.

That thought suddenly crossed Rei's mind, but the chirping of a small bird overhead snapped him back to reality.

"Come on, let's hurry up and go. If we stay here, we'll probably get lectured until morning."

Rei called out to Samaloon, who was once again focused on stroking Set's fur.

Though Rei didn't know Erinde that well, he could tell just by looking at the bird that she was pretty angry about this whole situation.

Or rather, the interaction between the bird and Samaloon.

Precisely because of that, he decided it would be best not to do anything to make her angrier. He half-dragged Samaloon, who was still lost in the sensation of Set's fur, toward the building housing the Headmaster's office.

"...Huh? Wait? Rei-san? Wait? What about Set? I was definitely petting Set..."

Once inside the building housing the principal's office and separated from Set, Samaloon finally snapped back to reality.

In his own memory, he should have been petting Set outside, but somehow... truly, somehow, time seemed to have skipped ahead, and before he knew it, he was inside the building. It was only natural he was surprised.

Normally, no matter how pleasant Set's fur felt to touch, he wouldn't lose himself like this.

But this time, various factors had contributed to her losing herself: her intense interest in Set, the cold of being outside on a winter night, the smooth feel of Set's fur, and the warmth of Set's body heat.

Furthermore, witnessing the skill "Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour" just moments before—a technique that, while not magic itself, made heavy use of raw magical power—might have also played a part.

In any case, Samaloon kept looking around with that look of surprise on her face.

But Rei dragged Samaloon away without a word.

"Come on, let's go. If we keep Erinde waiting like this, things could get messy."

Fortunately, once she confirmed Rei and Samaloon had entered the school building, the bird moved away from them.

She probably trusted Rei enough to believe he wouldn't run away now that they'd come this far.

"Ah, yes. ...Come to think of it, there was something like that."

"No, that sort of thing... even towards the headmaster of this academy?"

"No, I respect the Headmaster, you know? That's an undeniable fact. But still, it's just... how should I put it? I'd rather not get yelled at too much, if possible."

"I feel the same way,"

Rei said that, but regarding this incident, Samaloon was completely caught in the crossfire.

Rei had been using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour to provoke an attack or lure out whoever was watching him, but Samaloon hadn't known any of that.

He'd only come along out of interest when Rei mentioned training with magic-based skills...

That said, since Samaloon had gotten to see the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, he had almost no regrets.

"Alright, we're here. ...Are you ready?"

"Not really... but I guess I have no choice, huh?"

"Yeah. I'd rather just pretend I don't know about this, but that's not an option, is it? Alright, let's go."

With that, he knocked on the door. Almost immediately, a voice from inside told them to come in.

Inside the principal's office, waiting for Rei and Samaloon, was Erinde. She sat there, her expression sullen, her gaze fixed on them.

Though she was usually an elf whose gender was hard to discern, seeing her like this now made it even more difficult to tell.

(She's angry... I knew she was angry, but... I never imagined she'd be this furious.)

Erinde, looking furious, set the bird chirping on her shoulder onto the desk. She then fixed a cold stare on Rei and Samaloon and spoke.

"Now then, Rei. I trust you understand why I summoned you here... First, let me hear this. Why did you do such a thing?"

Erinde asked, her gaze urging Rei to speak.

Meeting her gaze, Rei opened his mouth.

"I think you know why, more or less. But if you insist I spell it out... well, deterrence, enticement, intimidation, a warning, I suppose."

"I suppose so. I figured as much. Not that I'm saying it's wrong, but still, if you were going to do something, couldn't you have at least given us a heads-up beforehand?"

"I do feel bad about that part. It's just... I only thought of it a moment ago."

"Just because you thought of it doesn't mean you should casually pull something like that. Forget about that skill for now, but what's up with your magic power suddenly exploding like that? I ended up spitting out the mulled wine I was drinking."

Erinde sighed heavily as she grumbled.

Seeing her like that, Rei wondered if she wasn't as angry as he'd expected. But the one who understood the situation the least right now was Samaloon.

"Excuse me, but I'm afraid I don't quite understand what you two are talking about. Would you mind explaining it to me in more detail?"

"...Rei. Did you bring Samaloon here without explaining the situation at all?"

Erinde shot him a reproachful look, and Rei quietly averted his gaze as he spoke.

"I wasn't sure how much information I should share. Besides, it's quicker to get the details out here, right?"

"Sigh, fine. I understand. I'll explain. ...All right, Samaloon. Rei is being targeted by someone. Someone who would resort to using poisoned arrows during class, no less."

"Huh? Um... is that... really true?"

Samaroon exclaimed, clearly confused by what Erinde was saying.

Of course. This Graciall is practically the stronghold of the Duke of Quent's family.

Attacking Rei, who Quent is sheltering here, is an act that would completely destroy Quent's reputation.

Under normal circumstances, no one would dare do such a thing.

Which means someone unusual is doing it...

"Rei, do you have any idea who it might be?"

"...Well, I have several suspicions. So many, in fact, it'd be a pain to count them all."

"Ah..."

Indeed, Samaloon couldn't help but nod in agreement.

Considering Rei's personality, it stood to reason he had countless enemies.

Most recently, it wouldn't be surprising if nobles loyal to the king, like those under Duke Quent, were targeting him.

And it wouldn't even be at Quent's behest—just his subordinates acting on their own initiative.

"Well, even if Rei were targeted by ordinary opponents, he'd probably manage. The problem is the poisoned arrows they used, right?"

"You mentioned that earlier, didn't you? But poisoned arrows... I mean, aren't they commonly used in assassinations?"

The words that came from Samaloon's mouth were an undeniable fact.

Poison has been used in assassinations since ancient times... In fact, it could be called one of the most common methods of assassination.

While poisons derived from plants or animals are the norm... in this case, the poison itself was the problem.

"That's right. If this poison weren't Heaven's Tears, I wouldn't have been so concerned about this incident. As you well know, Samaloon, this poison called Heaven's Tears is an extremely potent venom. It's so potent that even a graze could prove fatal. At the same time, it's incredibly labor-intensive to produce."

"Tears of Heaven!?"

Samaroon reflexively turned his gaze toward Rei, likely because the words that came out of Erinde's mouth were unexpected.

"Is that true?"

"Unfortunately, I'm not knowledgeable about poisons, so I can't say for certain. But if the elf Erinde says so, it must be an undeniable fact."

"That's the situation. Anyway, Rei, you're being targeted by those who can prepare and use Heaven's Tears. Considering that, you understand why I don't want you making careless moves, right? In other words, your actions this time were a bit... no, quite... highly unilateral."

Erinde fixed her gaze firmly on Rei once more. After this, she proceeded to lecture both Rei and Samaloon for nearly an hour.

...Samaroon was completely dragged into this by Rei.
