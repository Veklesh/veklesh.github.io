Several days had passed since receiving the secret request from Daskar.

Just before the six o'clock bell rang, Rei and the others gathered before the main gate of Gilm.

The five heading for Rossy in the Kingdom of Reburina—Rei, Elena, Marina, Vihera, and Byune—along with the two beasts, Set and Yellow.

And Ara, who had come to see off Rei and the others... or rather, Elena and the others in this case.

Daskar, the client for this job, and his guards.

Amaros, the alchemist who crafted the cage.

With such a gathering of people, and a huge cage large enough to hold several individuals, it was impossible not to attract attention.

Especially since Gilm was currently bustling with construction workers and merchants, who were repeatedly bringing in large quantities of goods, seeing it as prime earning season, only to leave the guild again.

It was impossible for them not to stand out.

"You understand how to use the Mirage Cage, right?"

Before the cage—or rather, the cage Marina and the others had decided to call the Mirage Cage—Amaros asked the others besides Rei.

Of course they did. Since Rei was riding on Set's back, it was essentially Elena and the others who would be operating the Mirage Cage.

That said, the explanation wasn't particularly difficult.

When the magic stones ran low, the box that held them would alert them, and all they had to do was put more magic stones in.

Once the magic stones are added, the Mirage Cage activates automatically. Crafted from the skin of a Murder Chameleon, the cage blends seamlessly into the surrounding scenery.

To be precise, it changes to the scenery from about a minute prior, so it's useless anywhere but the sky.

For example, if Set had been flying through a blue sky a minute ago and was now flying beneath the clouds, the cage would appear as a patch of blue sky within the clouds—a subtly awkward design flaw.

Furthermore, while the Mirage Cage could blend into the surroundings to deceive, Set carrying it couldn't be hidden separately.

...Still, the fact remains it makes him harder to spot, so it is useful.

"Then, Rei. I'm counting on you."

While Elena and the others listened to Amaros explain the Mirage Cage's usage, Rei was addressed by Daskar.

"Yes, leave it to me. I'll give those who tried something strange on Gilm their just desserts. ...They seem to have quite a few magic items too."

While Rei felt Gilm had been disrespected and provoked, the primary reason he accepted Daskar's request was undoubtedly the magic items.

Because Ajax had prepared dozens of slave collars.

This meant the organization Ajax belonged to either possessed the financial resources to procure that many magic items or the technological capability to create them.

Given such an opponent, it was only natural that they would be a target worthy of Rei's covetous gaze.

How many magic items could he obtain now...? That thought burned strongly in Rei's mind.

Right then, the six o'clock bell tolled, and the merchants and adventurers who had been watching Rei and the others began leaving Gilm.

With the gates just opening, the number leaving Gilm was large.

With the large Mirage Cage in tow, Rei and the others waited until the flow of people leaving the city slowed... confirming the numbers had thinned before stepping outside Gilm.

Several soldiers lifted the Mirage Cage and carried it away.

Rei briefly wondered if using Misty Ring to transport it would have been faster, but he felt it would be rude to point that out to those who were going out of their way to carry it for them. So, he simply followed them out the main gate.

Naturally, such a spectacle drew attention, and they attracted even more stares than before the gate opened.

Still, Rei knew full well how conspicuous they were, so he didn't really care.

Once outside Gilm, they moved a short distance away from the main road. Daskar then addressed Rei again.

"Rei, while you're gone, I'll make sure the Gilm expansion project runs smoothly. So don't worry about that."

"The way you put it makes it sound like I'm the one in charge of the Gilm expansion, not you, Daskar-sama."

"Hm? Is that so?"

Daskar tilted his head at Rei's words, but the guards and subordinates all nodded in agreement, confirming Rei's statement.

Observing the reactions around him, Daskar gave a small cough.

Regaining his composure, he spoke again.

"Anyway. It's undeniable that without you, the expansion work would face numerous delays. And that goes for the others too, besides you."

Daskar muttered, glancing toward Marina and Vihera, who were a short distance away, touching the Mirage Cage and listening intently to Amaros.

The role the members of the Crimson Wings, led by Rei, had played in Gilm's expansion work was quite significant.

With Rei and the others gone, it was only natural that the expansion work would inevitably slow down or encounter problems from now on.

But even knowing that, sanctions must be imposed against the Kingdom of Reburina... no, more accurately, against the clandestine organization operating within its capital, Rossy.

"Well, we got the map, and Marina and the others are here, so I don't think we'll get lost. It shouldn't take too long."

Fundamentally, maps are treated as something that should never leave the premises... they're strategic assets worthy of that designation.

Rei had borrowed one from Marina before, but that had been a highly risky endeavor.

If Rei had lost that map, it would undoubtedly have led to a life-or-death situation.

It was that important an item, but this time it was handed over to ensure they reached the Kingdom of Reburina as quickly as possible.

That said, what they received was only a copy of the section from Gilm to the Kingdom of Reburina.

Rei and Set were both somewhat directionally challenged to begin with, and it wasn't uncommon for them to get distracted by anything interesting they came across.

As a result, they'd been late arriving at their destination on several occasions.

...Even so, considering Set's speed, they still arrived at their destinations overwhelmingly faster than if they had traveled by horseback.

This time, they were urged to reach the Kingdom of Reburina as quickly as possible, settle the matter at hand there, and return to Gilm as soon as feasible. The map copy was given to them to aid in that endeavor.

Furthermore, Marina had previously... while still active as an adventurer, she had traveled near the Kingdom of Reburina on a quest, so there was no concern about getting lost.

"Right. Well, with Marina around, I suppose we don't need to worry about that."

Marina was a seasoned adventurer with a wealth of experience.

If asked who was the most reliable person in the Crimson Wings as an adventurer, Daskar would choose Marina, even setting aside their complicated history.

If it were purely about combat strength, he might choose Rei, but when judging overall adventurer capability, he had to conclude Marina was superior.

Furthermore, her experience as a guildmaster adds a significant boost to her trustworthiness.

"Speaking of which, we've already dispatched an envoy here in a specially made carriage. They'll arrive later than Rei and the others... but please remember that much."

"Understood."

"Rei! We're ready over here!"

Though taken aback by the sudden call, Rei nodded and waved vigorously at Vihera.

Nearby, Elena, Marina, and Byune were also looking at Rei.

Set was running around a little ways off, with Yellow perched on his head.

Glancing at her companions, Rei addressed Daskar.

"Well then, I'm off."

"Alright. Count on you."

Daskar's words carried complete trust.

Indeed, Rei had proven himself worthy of such trust time and again, so the guards and subordinates surrounding Daskar raised no objections to his attitude.

After confirming Daskar's nod, Rei called to Set and mounted his back.

Elena and the others accompanying Rei were already inside the Mirage Cage, fully prepared.

The top part of the Mirage Cage had handles attached for Set to grip easily.

Furthermore, the top could be closed if rain started falling during flight, and shock-absorbing material was incorporated between the Murder Chameleon leather and the cage to withstand falls from a certain height.

Of course, since Amaros had rushed its construction, there were still several areas lacking in refinement.

Elena and the others understood this, and didn't seem particularly bothered by the uncomfortable seating.

"Lady Elena, be careful!"

Hearing Ara's voice from outside, Elena opened her mouth.

"Well then, I'm off. I'll leave the matter with Gilm to you."

Watching this exchange out of the corner of his eye, Rei straddled Set's back.

"Well then, let's go. ...You can do this, right, Set?"

"Gurrr!"

At Rei's words, Set growled, took a few running steps, flapped his wings, and soared into the sky.

Those remaining on the ground, including Daskar, watched Set's departure with dazzled eyes.

The morning sun hung in the sky, and the sight of Set flying toward it held a certain fantastical quality.

After gliding lightly through the sky, Set dove steeply toward the mirage cage on the ground.

"I'm counting on you."

"Guruu!"

Responding to Rei's words, Set let out a spirited cry, locked onto his target... and in the next instant, he grabbed the cage's handle and flapped his wings again.

Those watching from the ground thought Set's movement seemed to slow for an instant as he seized the mirage cage's handle, but the next moment, he was soaring energetically through the sky again.

The Daskar watched intently until Set vanished from sight.

"Set, isn't the Mirage Cage heavy?"

"Grrrrrrrrr!"

At Rei's words, Set growled in his throat, "I'm fine!"

There was no sign of strain whatsoever, and Rei felt reassured, stroking Set's neck.

"What about you!? Any problems!?"

While petting Set, Rei then called out into the Mirage Cage.

The ceiling, or rather the lid, was currently open, allowing a clear view inside.

Inside the cage, Elena and the others sat on cushions that had been prepared beforehand.

The cage wasn't particularly large, so it felt quite cramped compared to their usual living space.

Fortunately, they weren't strangers. Everyone riding in the Mirage Cage was someone familiar and close to them.

That's precisely why the close quarters weren't particularly problematic.

This was only possible because of this particular group. If even one unfamiliar person of the opposite sex had been present, the atmosphere wouldn't have been as peaceful as it was now.

"It's okay, no problem at all!"

Hearing Vihera's voice from inside the cage, Rei let out a slight sigh of relief.

He trusted Amaros's skill, given that he was Daskar's subordinate, but still, it felt like they'd barely met.

Given that, he figured he'd need to be ready to step in immediately if anything went wrong.

(If only it were a bit bigger... Ah, maybe I should have brought the magic tent? Nah, it wouldn't fit in here.)

The space issue could be solved with Elena's carriage or Rei's magic tent.

But the carriage obviously wouldn't fit inside this cage, and Rei's magic tent was larger than this cage.

If I'd known about the Mirage Cage a bit earlier, I could have requested that sort of thing... But I reminded myself that the current Mirage Cage was still just a prototype.

(Once this matter is settled and it gets improved, I can just ask them to make it big enough to hold the magic tent. Besides, there's also research on sand ships, so when traveling in large groups, that might become the main means of transport anyway.)

Sand ships... no, once the land ships are complete, they'll carry more cargo than carriages, and moreover, the land ships themselves can serve as lodging for Rei and the others.

Without needing magic tents, a land ship with rooms for everyone would be the ideal mobile base.

(Though how fast it can go will probably be an issue too.)

If the land ship's speed were slower than a carriage... or worse, in an extreme case, slower than a person walking, it wouldn't be useless, but its applications would be severely limited.

Since it has a far greater carrying capacity than a horse-drawn carriage, in extreme cases, it would be perfect for use as a supply train.

Merchants, too, would surely find value in its ability to transport massive quantities of goods at once, even if its speed was lacking.

(A land ship... I'm looking forward to it. For that reason, I hope this current request can be wrapped up as quickly as possible.)

With these thoughts in mind, Rei set off for the Kingdom of Reburina alongside Set, and Elena and the others inside the mirage cage.
