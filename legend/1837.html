The moment he saw the figure appear before him, Rei unconsciously held his breath.

That’s how breathtaking the scene before him was.

Put simply, it was nothing more than Elena wearing a party dress.

Normally, since one rarely has the chance to see a party dress, it wouldn’t be strange for one’s attention to be drawn to the fact that she was wearing one.

But fortunately—or perhaps unfortunately—Rei knew of a woman named Marina who wore party dresses all the time.

And not just any party dress, but one with a plunging neckline and a low-cut back—a dress that was both glamorous and incredibly alluring.

Since Marina—who is already in a league of her own when it comes to feminine allure—is wearing such a party dress, it’s only natural that the men around her are often captivated by her.

And as Rei was paired with Marina at the party, he naturally found himself interacting with her frequently.

But… even for Rei, Elena’s appearance in that party dress right in front of him was enough to catch his eye.

“Um… what do you think? I’m a little shy since I’m dressed differently than usual.”

Saying that, Elena lightly swept her golden hair back.

Watching Elena, all Rei could manage was... to utter a single word.

“Yeah, it suits you.”

It was a rather ordinary compliment, and by no means sufficient to express his admiration for Elena.

But even so... for Elena, coming from the person she most wanted to hear it from, she couldn’t help but be happy.

“Is that so? …Is that so?”

Elena murmured in a pensive tone, then suddenly looked at Rei and spoke.

“Um, your outfit looks good on you, too, Rei. I can’t believe you put it together in such a hurry.”

“...Is that so? Personally, I’m not really a fan of this kind of outfit, though.”

As far as Elena could tell, looking at Rei, she couldn’t understand why he disliked his current outfit so much.

Still, since he had to attend today’s party, she needed him to put up with it.

“I’m sorry, but you’ll just have to put up with that outfit today.”

"Yeah, I get it. At the very least, I need to wear something that won’t make me look like a fool standing next to Elena."

“……Hmm.”

At those words, which came out of Rei’s mouth so suddenly, Elena’s cheeks flushed slightly.

From Elena’s perspective, he had told her he wanted to be by her side.

She understood. She understood, but… even so, it was impossible not to feel embarrassed.

Even though she was known as the “Princess General” and feared by the surrounding nations, Elena was still a young woman at heart.

“Lady Elena, Lady Elena! Pull yourself together!”

Ara, who had been standing by her side, tried her best to snap Elena out of what could only be described as “maiden mode.”

That said, Ara, who was standing by Elena’s side, was also dressed in a party gown—a departure from her usual attire.

Although Ara often serves as Elena’s bodyguard, she is actually of noble birth.

Naturally, unlike Rei, she is accustomed to wearing party dresses, and to the trained eye, it would be no surprise if she were to catch anyone’s attention.

...However, since she is currently by Elena’s side, people’s eyes inevitably turn to Elena rather than Ara.

However, Ara doesn’t hold a grudge against Elena over this.

If anything, she was actually delighted to be by Elena’s side.

“Hm? Oh, I’m sorry. I’m fine.”

Snapped back to reality by Ara’s voice, Elena immediately replied.

“So, Rei. Are the party preparations all set?”

“Nah, I didn’t really have anything to do anyway.”

To be precise, Miranda had told him which nobles to watch out for, but he had far fewer responsibilities than Elena and the others from the Kerebel Duke’s household.

“Is that so? I’ve been pretty busy myself. Lots of people have come to see me, and above all, getting dressed takes time.”

Elena looked a little tired, and indeed, her makeup, hairstyle, and getting into her party dress—all of it took time.

In Rei’s case, he did apply a light layer of makeup, but that was strictly to make him look good under the party lights—nothing too elaborate.

In contrast to Rei, Elena was thoroughly made up.

...Even so, Elena wasn’t merely wearing makeup; she was applying it. In other words, she wasn’t being used by her makeup; she was mastering it. That was the impression Rei got.

In fact, Elena’s beauty—even beyond the party dress—was undoubtedly elevated to a whole new level by her makeup.

Elena was often called a goddess of beauty even in her daily life, but now her beauty surpassed even that.

Rei felt undeniably lucky to be able to see Elena like this.

He disliked applying makeup himself, but there was no doubt that he enjoyed watching others—especially Elena—apply it.

“I’m happy I got to see Elena like this, though.”

“……”

At Rei’s words, Elena’s movements stopped once again.

But this time, she snapped back to her senses before Ara could say anything.

“I-I see. I’m glad you’re happy, Rei. …If possible, I’d like to talk a little longer, but…”

Elena’s gaze turned toward Ara, but Ara, meeting her gaze, shook her head.

“I’m sorry, but you still have a lot to do, Lady Elena. There isn’t much break time left.”

“R-right.”

“Ah… well, I suppose that can’t be helped. Just by us talking like this, I can tell you’re really stretching yourself to make time for this.”

Even as he said this, Rei wore a slightly disappointed expression.

That’s because there are still a few hours left before the party really gets started.

If Rei were dressed in his usual Dragon Robe, he probably wouldn’t be this bored.

After all, he could have just gone out for a bit to play around outside the mansion.

But in his current state—with makeup on and wearing a custom-made suit for the party—there was no way he could do something like that.

If he were to go see Set in this state… or do something like that, there was a high chance his clothes would get so dirty that attending the party would be out of the question.

A mock battle with the Kerebel Duke’s Knights would be out of the question. …Though, on this day—said to be the busiest of the year—the Kerebel Duke’s Knights were naturally busy with security and escort duties, so even if Rei had been in his usual Dragon Robe, there likely wouldn’t have been anyone available to hold a mock battle anyway.

In any case, he said goodbye to Elena, who mentioned she’d be busy from now on, promised to come back before the party started, and left the room.

Incidentally, the reason Rei would visit this room again before the party wasn’t simply to kill time, as he had done this time, but because Rei was to serve as Elena’s so-called escort.

This wasn’t Elena’s decision alone; she had obtained proper permission from both Liberte and Arcadia.

Liberte’s objective is likely to use Rei’s escorting of Elena to make it known to the other nobles exactly what kind of relationship Rei and Elena have.

Rei doesn’t particularly care for being put on display like that, but if it means Elena will be approached by other men—well, not completely, but at least significantly less—then he has no choice but to accept it.

Besides, when it comes to being a spectacle, Rei has already grown accustomed to it through the mock battle incident and the matter with Set.

In that sense, Rei had no reason not to accept the role of escorting Elena.

“Well, now that that’s settled, the question is how to kill time. …Is there anything?”

Leaving the room, Rei asked Miranda.

Miranda, who had been waiting for Rei outside the room, thought for a moment before speaking.

“Hmm. This mansion has a library, so why don’t you go there? There’s quite a large collection, so I don’t think you’ll get bored, Rei. And you won’t have to worry about getting your clothes dirty either.”

“There’s a library in the mansion? …That’s amazing.”

In fact, considering the price of books in this world of Elgin, having a library attached to the mansion was quite a luxury.

It was something only the Duke of Kerebel, holder of the title of Duke and ruler of Anesis—the kingdom’s second-largest city—could afford.

At the very least, while Daskar’s mansion in Gilm had a room that could be called a study, it wasn’t large enough to be called a library.

“Yes. The Master is an avid book collector, so while it may not compare to the libraries in the capital, it should still be quite impressive.”

“I see. Well then…”

Let’s go to the library.

Just as Rei was about to say that, someone suddenly called out to him.

“Hey, isn’t that Rei? I thought you weren’t here, but I guess you were in a place like this.”

The owner of that voice spoke in a manner that made it a bit of a stretch to call him a noble… but he was undoubtedly a noble, and someone Rei recognized.

「Brewitt」

Rei called out the man’s name… but there was a subtle hint of doubt in his voice as to whether the person standing before him was actually Brewitt.

Brewitt Exoris.

He was the heir to the Exoris family and possessed a rare personality that got along well with Rei.

...Though, to begin with, the fact that he was wandering around town without even a single bodyguard raised questions about his suitability as a noble.

The reason Rei hesitated, even for a moment, to confirm whether this person was truly Brewitt was clearly because his appearance had changed since they last met.

When Rei had seen him before, he was dressed like a commoner—or perhaps in clothes slightly finer than those of a commoner—but even so, they weren’t the sort of clothes a noble would wear.

Rei had recognized the person wearing those clothes as Brewitt, but the Brewitt who now stood before him had dressed his massive frame in attire befitting the upcoming party, and his hair was neatly combed.

Since Rei was clearly different from the Brewitt Ray knew, it would be unreasonable to expect him to recognize him immediately upon seeing him.

That said, if we’re going to point that out, Rei himself was also dressed and styled differently than usual.

“Hm? What’s wrong? Why the puzzled look? You haven’t forgotten who I am, have you? No, wait—you called my name, so that can’t be it.”

Judging by Rei’s puzzled expression, he must have had some doubts. Brewitt asked Rei, but since he had called his name, he concluded that Rei hadn’t forgotten him right away.

Rei quickly shook off her surprise and shot Brewitt a look tinged with mild exasperation.

“I don’t think there are many people who’d believe the Brewitt I met in town and the one standing here right now are the same person… At least, I didn’t recognize you for a second.”

“Come on, if you’re talking about how my appearance has changed, isn’t that exactly the same for Rei? So why do I have to be the only one getting told that?”

Although he voiced his dissatisfaction, Brewitt’s expression was dominated by amusement.

It was clear he wasn’t actually blaming Rei.

Rei probably knew that too. He smiled and continued speaking.

“It’s fine—I don’t have much experience with places like this.”

“Still, I wish you’d at least understood who I am.”

“Don’t be ridiculous. …So?”

“So?” he replied because, when Brewitt appeared, he’d spoken as if he were looking for him, wondering why he was in a place like this.

Maybe he was looking for me because he needed something from me, I thought.

“Hm? Oh, no. It’s not like I had anything specific to ask of you or anything. I was just getting a little bored here, so I was looking for a familiar face. And I heard a rumor that Rei was attending this party too, so I figured you’d probably be here.”

“……You didn’t have to go out of your way to look for me. As Brewitt, don’t you have acquaintances among the nobility?”

At Rei’s question, Brewitt gently looked away.

He was well aware that, as a noble, he was an anomaly.

That’s exactly why there weren’t many people who chose to associate with him… and as a result, the only ones who approached Brewitt were those after his influence.
