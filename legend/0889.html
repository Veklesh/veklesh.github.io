"I never thought you'd show up so soon after yesterday."

"Grr?"

Hearing Rei's words, Set, walking beside him, growled as if to ask, "What's up?"

Rei shook his head at Set, as if to say it was nothing, and stroked his head.

The day after receiving Gamelion's meat, it seemed Lenora had already contacted Baron Sisne saying he could come anytime. Early this morning, word reached the guild, and a guild liaison told him to head to the Baron Sisne residence before noon.

Of course, Rei didn't have any particularly urgent business—though he did have plans to pick up Rent's cheese—so he readily accepted.

With the Gamelion matter settled and the snow now falling in earnest, Rei had nothing pressing to do.

If he had to name something, it would be enjoying a second nap in bed or wandering the food stalls to eat warm dishes while snow swirled around him. But it wasn't something worth turning down a request from the Baroness Sisne's household for.

"Grrrr."

Even now, as they walked through the falling snow, Set, walking beside Rei, purred contentedly.

Being with Rei was certainly one reason for his good mood, but a major factor was also the Gamelion cuisine served for dinner last night and breakfast this morning.

The Gamelion meat Rei had brought back yesterday, cooked at the Dusk Wheat inn, had delivered a taste that could only be described as delicious to both Rei and Set.

Incidentally, much of the meat Rei had brought back had been cut into small pieces by the Death Scythe.

For the guild, selling larger cuts of meat at the market fetched a higher price, and for Rei, it saved the trouble of chopping it up with a knife. It was a mutually beneficial arrangement.

Though Set did enjoy grilling large chunks of meat and tearing into them whole.

Anyway, Rei brought the meat, already cut into manageable pieces, straight to the Dusk Wheat inn and had them prepare dishes using it for himself and Set.

"Gamelion cuisine is really going to hit its stride from now on, so even the food from the stalls should get tastier."

"Guruu!"

Set purred happily at Rei's mutter.

As they walked like that, naturally, many people gathered around wanting to play with Set, touch him, or fuss over him.

Still, perhaps due to the snow falling now, there were considerably fewer than usual.

Continuing on while politely declining their advances, they soon arrived at the Noble District, lined with aristocratic mansions.

Guards were just passing through the entrance to the Noble District, but those guards were adventurers too. There was no way they didn't know Rei.

"We're going in, okay?"

"Sure thing. Just don't cause any trouble in the Noble District. You're already pretty conspicuous as it is... Well, even if someone knows Rei, they probably won't give you any weird hassle."

Wahahaha, laughed the adventurer man as he spoke, only to have the woman beside him—a bow-wielding archer, it seemed—smack him on the head.

"Idiot! Do you really think we could handle it if something like that happened? I'm not having it."

The man, whose frame exceeded two meters in height, nodded in agreement at the woman's words.

"You're right. If Rei went berserk, the Noble Quarter wouldn't leave a single speck of ash behind."

"...What's the deal with the rumors about me?"

Rei mutters in response to the man's words.

But the man merely smiled at Rei before speaking.

"Ha! What are you talking about? I fought in the Spring War and saw you create that fiery tornado with my own eyes! Don't lump me in with those idiots running around panicking over rumors."

"I see. Well, whatever. Do as you please."

Rei seemed to decide there was no point in saying more. He said only that and entered the Noble District.

The adventurers watched Rei go without saying anything in particular.

About a minute passed after Rei disappeared from sight. Then, the man who had spoken to Rei first opened his mouth.

"Still... summoning Rei like that... I hope it doesn't cause any real trouble, right?"

"Surely not. In today's Gilm, there's no one who would antagonize Rei... no one... right?"

The archer woman's words seemed to unsettle him. He turned his gaze to his colleague.

The burly man nodded in response.

"If anyone in Gilm doesn't know how terrifying Rei is, it'd only be someone who just arrived. But the family that recently came to Gilm... Ah..."

"There was one. ...I have this strangely unpleasant feeling about it."

The archer woman's mind flashed to the noble who had arrived in Gilm just a few weeks ago.

The noble rumored to have been sent as a check against the neutral faction and the noble faction, who had recently grown closer due to the civil war in the Bestir Empire.

Adventurers usually didn't pay much attention to such matters, but since she'd accepted the request to guard the Noble District, she needed to be aware of the situation.

"They wouldn't seriously turn the Noble Quarter to ashes, would they?"

The massive man muttered, his voice trembling with an anxiety that seemed disproportionate to his size.

A noble who could rightly be called a petty villain.

He was a classic noble in the worst sense, someone who looked down on us with contempt.

As someone sent as a deterrent, it was obvious the plan was to cause trouble here and use it as an excuse to impose some punishment on the neutral faction.

What would happen if such a man encountered Rei?

The adventurers present could vividly picture this noble district reduced to ashes.

"...What do we do?"

"What do you mean, what do we do? He's not someone we can handle ourselves... All we can do is report it to our employer."

"Got it. I'll go handle it right away. You guys hold down the fort here."

The man who had spoken with Rei first announced this, then swiftly ran off.

Hoping he wouldn't do anything reckless.

"Nothing's changed."

Meanwhile, contrary to the adventurers' worries, Rei arrived at the Sysne Baron's residence without incident.

Just like last year, there were no gatekeepers or guards in front of the mansion.

"Still careless as ever."

"Grrr..."

Set growled in agreement with Rei's words, then turned his gaze toward the mansion. Just then, the door opened, and a maid appeared.

Recognizing the maid, Rei raised his hand lightly and called out.

"You're Ashié, right?"

The maid called Asie also bowed slightly upon seeing Rei.

"It's been a while, Rei. Please come inside. I'll call the master and young master right away."

She must have already been informed that Rei was coming today. She said this without any particular surprise at seeing him.

"Ah. Is it okay if I let Set play in the garden?"

"That's fine, but... in this weather? Won't he be cold?"

Ashie said, casting a worried glance toward Set. But Set himself turned his round eyes toward Ashie, tilting his head slightly as if to say, What's wrong?

His adorable expression was something that, had Mireine or Johanna been present, would have instantly knocked them out cold.

Of course, Asie wasn't averse to cute things, but she wasn't about to abandon her work for it.

"Don't worry. Set doesn't mind this kind of cold at all. In fact, he doesn't mind playing in the snow either."

"Is that so?"

Ashie turned to Set, seeking confirmation of Rei's words, but Set was merely purring contentedly.

She still wasn't entirely convinced, but seeing the way Set looked at her, she decided Rei wasn't lying. She led Set out into the garden and then invited Rei inside the mansion.

Once inside the mansion, Rei was led to the reception room.

"I'll go inform the master and young master of your arrival, Mr. Rei. Please wait here for a moment."

With that, he placed herbal tea and dried fruit on the table.

"Ah, understood. I'll take my time then."

"Excuse me, then."

Watching Asie bow and leave the room, Rei looked around the room.

It wasn't furnished with anything particularly expensive, but the room was clean and tidy.

(Seems he's still strapped for cash... but strangely, it's a place where you can relax.)

Compared to places like the lord's office or the guildmaster's study, this was clearly a space designed for rest.

A handmade-looking scented pouch sat on a shelf, and a painting, likely depicting the house's garden, hung on the wall. It wasn't by a famous artist, but it somehow suited the room perfectly.

Gazing at the painting, presumably depicting a spring garden, Rei lifted the herbal tea to his lips.

(This herbal tea too... I'm pretty sure she mentioned before that she makes it herself. It's surprisingly delicious for an amateur's homemade stuff. If that's the case, then naturally these dried fruits must be homemade too. ...That's pretty impressive in its own right.)

Speaking of dried fruit, the one Rei was most familiar with was dried persimmons.

That was because when she lived in Japan, there was a persimmon tree in her family's garden.

While sweet persimmons are commonly eaten in the Kanto region, in Tohoku, even sweet persimmon trees produce astringent fruit. So, they're basically either dried into persimmon chips or treated with shochu to remove the astringency before eating.

Anyway, for that reason, when Rei thought of dried fruit, she thought of dried persimmons. But dried persimmons are black.

To someone unfamiliar, they certainly don't look like food.

But the dried fruit now prepared as a tea snack before Rei had indeed shrunk from drying, yet its color remained vivid.

(Is this just the variety, or is Asie's skill in making them so good? ...I suspect the latter. Price-wise.)

In Rei's understanding, fruits that retained such vivid colors even after drying seemed like expensive varieties.

Considering that, Rei suspected it was likely Asie's skill that preserved such vivid colors.

Placing a piece of dried fruit in his mouth, a rich sweetness spread.

Just as the sweetness filled his mouth, he took a sip of the herbal tea.

Surprisingly, it washed away the sweetness cleanly and beautifully, leaving only a faint hint of tartness as an aftertaste.

As she savored that simple sweetness, a knock suddenly sounded at the reception room door, and a child appeared.

The child, around ten years old, spotted Rei drinking herbal tea in the reception room. Eyes sparkling with delight, they opened their mouth.

"Rei-san, it's been a while!"

"It has been a while, Baslero. ...Huh?"

After exchanging a few brief words, Rei nodded at Basleru with an impressed look.

His body itself hadn't changed much compared to a year ago.

Well, he had grown taller, but he hadn't gained extreme muscle mass or anything like that.

And yet, compared to a year ago when he was practically an amateur when it came to moving his body, his movements were undeniably more refined.

It was clear he'd trained diligently over the past year.

Of course, that didn't mean he was immediately at a level where he could fight monsters. The answer to that was no.

"Looks like you weren't just goofing off this past year."

"Yes. I trained diligently every single day. While I didn't have someone as skilled as Rei to train me, I did occasionally get training from adventurers."

Basurelo, responding to Rei's words, was as extremely polite as he had been a year ago.

Honestly, from Rei's perspective, it was hard to believe he was still a child.

(A noble's child, huh. Even among nobles, there are all sorts. Elena and Lord Daskar are nobles too.)

Though Rei generally held a poor impression of nobles, it was true that some nobles commanded his respect.

"So, did you call me this time because you wanted me to train you again?"

"Ah, no. That part..."

Just as Baslero was about to respond to Rei's words, a voice called out at the perfect moment.

Turning his gaze in that direction, he saw Muet, the head of the Baron Sysne family, and the maid, Asie.

To Rei, Muet still looked somewhat frail, yet he sensed a core of strength within him—likely because the light in his eyes was different.

"Long time no see, Muet."

Rei stood up and bowed his head.

Anyone familiar with Rei's usual demeanor would surely be astonished by this sight.

After all, this was the same Rei who treated nobles of even higher rank with arrogant disdain, yet here he was bowing his head, though his tone remained unchanged.

This change was undoubtedly due to last year's first impression.

There was no doubt this attitude stemmed from recognizing him as a figure worthy of respect.

"Long time no see, Rei. It's been a year, but I'm glad to see you're doing well. I've heard about your various exploits. Getting a nickname? That's just like you, Rei. Well, please sit down."

Prompted, Rei sank into the sofa.

Muet and Baslero sat down across from Rei, and Muet immediately spoke up.

"Actually, the reason we asked you to come today is partly because Baslerro wanted to see you, but also because we wanted to see just how much your skills have improved. Unfortunately, I'm completely useless in that department."

"...I see."

Rei turned his gaze to Baslerro. Seeing the look of expectation in Baslerro's eyes, Rei's expression softened into one of understanding.
