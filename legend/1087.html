Not a single cloud graced the sky—a deep blue expanse stretching endlessly high, the very picture of summer. And from the sun poured forth intense rays, equally quintessential to the season.

Though summer hadn't fully arrived yet, the sunlight pouring down onto the earth sapped people's strength with its intense heat.

Travelers on the road frowned involuntarily at the mirage distorting the scenery in the distance.

Many travelers and adventurers glare hatefully at the sun, which seemed to float in the sky as if it owned the place.

Having one's stamina sapped by the heat is an extremely painful experience.

Amidst this... An adventurer tasked with gathering a flower that blooms only under the summer sun, like his party companions, glared at the sky with irritation... and there, he spotted something strange.

(A monster?)

He murmured, yet did not immediately assume a defensive stance because the monster seemed completely unaware of their presence, merely flapping its wings.

Flying monsters weren't particularly common, but they weren't nonexistent either.

If it were heading their way, they'd have to either flee or engage, but the monster flying where the man's gaze now rested showed no interest in them whatsoever, simply soaring gracefully through the sky.

He couldn't imagine what it felt like to fly in this intense sunlight, but he supposed it must be cooler than the sun-baked ground below. He found himself wishing he could fly too...

His gaze was drawn skyward, captivated, until his companions called out to him... His startled return to reality made him scramble to catch up with them.

"If there were just a few more clouds, I could never get tired of this view."

"Grr."

Completely oblivious to the exchange happening below, Rei flew through the summer sky alongside Set.

The sunlight pouring down from the summer sun was intense, even compared to the ground, but fortunately, Rei had his Dragon Robe, which functioned like a simple air conditioner.

As a Rank A monster, Set could easily withstand this level of sunlight without issue.

The reason Rei was flying with Set so far from Gilm was because they were heading for Gosh, a desert town Marina had suggested.

Rei had been hounded by numerous merchants over the matter of the Twilight Spear, and Gosh was the refuge Marina had chosen until that situation settled down.

The neighboring country of the Mireana Kingdom... a small nation, practically a vassal state, called Solrain, had much of its land covered in desert.

Moreover, unlike the rocky desert Rei had previously encountered in a dungeon, most of it consisted purely of sand.

Gosh, the city Rei and Set were heading towards, was one of the top five most developed cities in this nation of Solrain.

It was a city that had developed around an oasis, hot year-round... a place you definitely wouldn't want to visit in summer.

Yet, Rei was heading there with Set.

It was a trip Marina had suggested, and under normal circumstances, he would have declined.

He accepted it partly because he didn't have any urgent business requiring his immediate attention.

While he and Mayo were researching goblin meat, Mayo was currently experimenting with the aging process Rei had mentioned, and Rei was told he didn't need to do anything right now.

Rei understood this was Mayo being considerate, knowing Rei was being hounded by merchants over the matter of the Twilight Spear.

With nothing pressing for Rei to do at present, and the possibility of encountering unknown monsters in the desert—unlike the dungeons of the Labyrinth City of Exil—Rei decided to go to Gosh.

He was worried Vihera might be angry upon his return since he'd been away accompanying Byune on her trip home, but Marina offered to speak for him, so he set off with peace of mind.

Going to the desert in summer seemed like madness to those suffering the heat in Gilm.

For someone as overweight as Mayo, that feeling was likely even stronger.

But... fortunately, Rei possessed a Dragon Robe, and above all, his body was the culmination of the Zepairu clan's techniques.

Naturally, it possesses a certain degree of resistance to both heat and cold, and with the Magic Tent, he could stay quite comfortable.

There was also the thought that in a neighboring country, Rei's name wouldn't be as widely known as it was in the Kingdom of Mireana.

Of course, given his exploits in the war against the Bestir Empire and the tournament, it wasn't as if his name was completely unknown.

At the same time, there was a good chance information about Rei had reached the Adventurers' Guild... Still, he figured it was better than being persistently hounded by merchants.

"The desert, huh. I wonder what kind of magic stones we'll find there."

"Gururu!"

At Rei's words, Set purred happily.

Having acquired several skills from the World Tree incident, he probably wanted to try them out.

Especially a skill like the King's Intimidation—it was so powerful that opportunities to use it lightly were few and far between.

Several days had already passed since leaving Gilm. Unusually... truly unusually, Rei and Set continued their carefree journey through the skies without encountering any particular trouble.

They hadn't been attacked by bandits while camping, hadn't seen any carriages being ambushed by thieves or monsters while flying, and hadn't gotten lost either. They were making steady progress toward Gosh.

Marina couldn't lend them a detailed map due to her position, but she had given Rei a rough map to Gosh and explained the general route.

...Even with all that caution, trouble usually finds Rei, but strangely enough, the journey had been completely problem-free so far.

(Did trouble think, "With this heat, I don't feel like moving either"? ...No way.)

Gradually, the surrounding scenery shifted from ordinary landscapes to something closer to a rocky desert.

Gosh lay within a sand desert. Once we passed through this rocky desert area, the surrounding scenery would shift to that sandy landscape.

"Grrrr?"

Suddenly, Set, who had been flying, growled.

Wondering what was wrong, Rei looked toward Set, who was staring down at the ground.

Following his gaze, a caravan came into view.

Unlike the ones Rei usually saw, being attacked by bandits or monsters, this one was moving at a leisurely pace through the rocky desert.

Wondering what had caught Set's attention about this caravan, Rei stared intently at the caravan below.

Then, he immediately understood what was different from the caravans he had seen before.

It was the carriages. Normally, as the name suggests, they would be pulled by horses, but the carriages in this caravan were being pulled by animals with humps on their backs... camels.

(Is it still a carriage if camels are pulling it?)

The thought crossed Rei's mind, but having seen tamed monsters pulling wagons before, he decided it was probably still a wagon.

While Rei was observing the caravan on the ground with these thoughts, several members of the caravan must have noticed Set flying overhead. They frantically began preparing to intercept him.

"Huh, spotting us from this distance means they've got some pretty skilled guards. ...Set, let's go."

"GRRR!"

Responding to Rei's voice, Set flapped his wings and took off from the spot.

After flying on for several minutes, Rei noticed Set focusing his attention back down to the ground.

Thinking perhaps another caravan with camels was present, Rei also looked down. But what spread out below was a landscape resembling a mix of rocky desert and sandy desert.

If asked what made this place different from before, he could only say it was just that kind of difference.

(Set seemed to be thinking about trying camel meat... but that worry was unfounded, I suppose.)

From Rei's perspective, camels didn't look like something you could eat.

But since he preferred monster meat, Rei reasoned it probably wouldn't be strange for him to eat a camel either.

And while eating camel was beyond Rei's common sense, he had seen dishes using camel meat on TV back in Japan. He also knew that dairy products made from camel milk—cheese, milk, yogurt, ice cream—were apparently extremely expensive in some places.

(I think I saw somewhere that camel humps are considered a luxury ingredient in Chinese cuisine... though apparently they're just lumps of fat.)

For some reason, while thinking about camel dishes, Rei was bothered by the fact that Set was still staring at the ground. She turned her gaze back that way.

Rei had been carefully observing the ground, thinking there must be a reason Set was watching it so intently... but suddenly, he saw the ground bulging and moving.

It looked as if something was wriggling beneath the surface... and indeed, Rei's hunch proved correct.

Something long and thin was moving beneath the surface. From the air, he could only make out a rough shape, but it was something long and thin, like an earthworm or a snake.

"An earthworm? No, surely not..."

If earthworms this huge existed, this must be a land beyond human comprehension.

It was as if Rei had forgotten that even the Mireana Kingdom, where he usually resided, was considered one of the realm's most remote frontiers. But he quickly recalled the contents of the monster encyclopedia he had seen in the library before leaving Gilm.

It was the sandworm, a monster he had encountered before in the dungeons of the labyrinth city of Exil.

But the sandworm he'd encountered before had legs. The one currently moving across the ground—or more accurately, through the ground—didn't appear to have legs.

Of course, since it was moving underground, it was possible that its legs were simply not visible from above...

"It's small."

Rei's muttered words upon seeing the monster, likely a sandworm, running underground, sounded like disappointment.

Compared to the sandworm he'd encountered before, this one was clearly larger.

Normally, a monster five meters long would undoubtedly be considered enormous. However, for Rei, it had only been about a month since he fought a twenty-meter-long... a giant cicada that freely flew through the sky.

Moreover, the World Tree's size was also astronomically larger than ordinary trees, making him somewhat desensitized to enormous monsters.

"Anyway, it'd be a shame to let it get away here. ...Set."

"GRRRRRR!"

Understanding exactly what Rei meant from his words alone, Set spun on the spot and gave chase after the monster that had just passed by, likely a sandworm.

Given Set's speed, catching up to a monster moving underground shouldn't be too difficult... but Rei, realizing what lay ahead in the monster's path, frowned slightly.

He understood what the monster was aiming for.

At the end of his gaze was a caravan of camels pulling wagons... yes, the very people Set had just overtaken.

"Ugh, another troublesome situation..."

Rei muttered, but the caravan must have also noticed the approaching monster. They hurriedly prepared to intercept it.

The adventurers numbered about twenty. Considering it was a caravan with five camel-drawn wagons, the number seemed a bit low, but this was related to the fact they were traveling through the desert.

Traveling through the desert naturally required carrying large quantities of water, but more people meant higher water consumption.

Therefore, it was generally standard practice to hire a small number of highly skilled individuals.

Of course, this was merely the standard practice; many chose different approaches.

In any case, the caravan on the ground seemed to have chosen to meet the attack head-on rather than flee, and the guards formed up.

Then, as if waiting for them to complete their defensive formation, the monsters emerged from the sand.

Seeing them, Rei's expression flickered with a hint of surprise.

Because the monsters that appeared were not sandworms.

Having assumed it was a sandworm, he was caught off guard.

In appearance, it resembled a sandworm, much like Rei had mistakenly thought. But unlike a sandworm, it had scales covering its body.

Not like an earthworm, but like a snake.

"A Sand Serpent?"

He recalled the description in the Monster Compendium.

A Rank C monster—specifically, the kind of Rank C monster you defeat as a party.

The five-meter-long Sand Serpent's head gazed down from above at its prey below, flicking its tongue to intimidate the guards.

The guards weren't just any skilled fighters either, each trying to counter it... but Rei smiled, thinking it was rather perfect.

"Twilight Spear... allow me to test it."

With those words, a Crimson spear materialized in Rei's hand.

He channeled magic into the spear drawn from the Misty Ring... and hurled it.

Flying straight, slicing through the desert air, the Twilight Spear... in the next instant, cleanly shattered the Sand Serpent's skull, ending its life.

"...Huh? Did I... go a bit too far?"
