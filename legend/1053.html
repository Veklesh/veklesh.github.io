Set, drenched in Aerowings' flesh, bones, and entrails, couldn't bear the situation, his senses all too sharp.

Set turned his gaze toward Rei , his throat making a sorrowful sound. Rei silently drew the Water-Flowing Dagger from his Misty Ring.

Most adventurers carry a leather water bag... or if they have a carriage, many keep a barrel of water on hand.

In the forest, there might be a river, but that wasn't guaranteed either.

And humans, elves, dwarves, beastmen, and other rare races fundamentally cannot survive without water.

The reason I said "basically" is because some rare races don't need water at all.

In any case, carrying water is essential for adventurers.

In that context, Rei is quite a rare case.

Because he possesses the Misty Ring, he can carry as many barrels of water as he wants.

And indeed, several barrels of water are currently stored within the Misty Ring.

However... when Rei uses water, he primarily employs a magic item known as the Short Sword of Flowing Water.

Originally, it was a dagger with the ability to manipulate water as a weapon according to its owner's will. Yet, despite possessing enough magic power to fully heal the World Tree, Rei, who specializes in the element of fire, can only produce a small amount of water.

Yet the water created using Rei's magic possessed a sweetness worthy of being called heavenly nectar.

It was truly among the finest waters in all of Elgin.

Without hesitation, Rei poured this water over Set.

If anyone who knew the deliciousness of the water created by Rei's flowing water dagger had seen this scene, they would have screamed.

(Come to think of it, if I sold this water, I'd never have to worry about money again. ...Well, I've never tested how long it keeps its flavor, so I don't know.)

Feathers, blood, bits of flesh and bone, and fragments of internal organs clinging to Set's fur gradually washed away. They washed away, but... Set let out a disappointed growl.

Because the water flowing from the Water-Flowing Dagger was barely trickling—far too little to wash away the grime from his body, which stretched over two meters.

What was needed here wasn't the quality of the water, but the quantity.

Realizing this, Rei poured water over Set with a troubled look and spoke.

"Hey, Set. At this rate, we'll never get you properly washed off, right? I'm pretty sure there was talk of a spring or river between the barrier's protective field and the confusion barrier. Why don't we go look for it?"

"Gururu?"

Set tilted his head, wondering if it was really true.

Under normal circumstances, it would be an adorable gesture, but unfortunately, Set was currently covered in the remains of the Aerowings.

In his current state, even tilting his head looked somehow eerie.

But Rei showed no sign of being intimidated by Set's appearance and continued speaking.

"Jusla said she was taken while napping in a place like that, so I'm sure it exists. I just don't know where."

"Grrrrrrr! ...Gyaaah!"

"Leave it to me!" Set growled, but the next moment, a scream erupted.

He'd used his Enhanced Sense of Smell skill, but since he'd activated it here... in this place littered with Aero Wing wreckage, the unexpected stench had made him scream.

(He tried to smell water and ended up sniffing blood? ...And his sense of smell is already sharp. The Aerowings couldn't beat Set at all in a fair fight, but who'd have thought they'd get one last shot in here? Well, I doubt he's happy about it.)

Even if told that fragments of his own corpse damaged Set's sense of smell, Rei couldn't possibly be pleased.

After all, if he was dead, getting one shot in back at him would be the end of it.

(Surviving is what truly matters, after all.)

Surviving was what gave it meaning.

That was an undeniable truth within Rei.

"Grrr..."

"Whoops, sorry. Well, let's move a bit away from here then. Also, if you're looking for a river, besides the smell of water, you can also look for the sound of water. ...Well, you need flowing water, so finding a spring or something like that would be tough."

"Guruu!"

As if trying to make up for his earlier failure, Set let out a spirited growl.

He immediately closed his eyes and began focusing his hearing, trying to pick up the sound of water.

(He has a skill that enhances his sense of smell, but he doesn't have one that enhances his hearing. So from here on out, it's entirely up to his natural abilities. Still, considering it's Set's natural ability, I'm not worried.)

To avoid disturbing Set as he concentrated on his hearing, Rei silently watched over him.

Glancing at the wreckage of the Aerowings scattered around them, insects had already begun gathering there... including enormous ones about ten centimeters long, as well as smaller groups of ants.

(The fact they don't seem afraid of Set probably means they aren't that intelligent. Wait, if their intelligence isn't developed, that means they're wild. In that case, it wouldn't be strange if they sensed Set's threat, right?)

For the insects, the Aerowings' flesh and organs were nothing short of a feast.

Especially since the flesh was already torn apart, sparing the smaller insects the trouble of tearing it into smaller pieces.

(I just hope eating Aero Wing meat doesn't turn them into monsters...)

Only insects gradually increase in number; no other animals exist.

Perhaps because many here sense fear toward Set.

"Guruu!"

As Rei watched the small ants forming a line, carrying away the meat fragments one after another, Set's cry echoed in his ears.

Considering the cheerful tone in that cry, he understood it probably meant Seto had heard the sound of water or something similar.

"Did you hear the sound of water?"

"Guruu!"

Set growled in agreement, then tugged at the Dragon Robe with his beak, signaling they should hurry.

Set probably didn't want to linger here either, where Aerowings' meat scraps were scattered everywhere.

(There's a saying about a sea of wine and a forest of meat, but while this might not be a sea of wine, it sure is a forest of meat.)

Watching the Aero Wing's entrails dangling from a nearby tree branch, Rei left the scene alongside Set.

"Guruu... Gurururuu..."

Walking through the forest, Set used his tail to shake off pieces of flesh and entrails clinging to his body.

Rei assists Set, removing bits of flesh and organs from places his tail can't reach.

Occasionally, bone fragments got tangled in Set's fur. If they didn't comb through it carefully instead of just roughly brushing, bones could pierce Rei's hands or pull out Set's fur.

"What the hell are we even doing?"

"Grrr..."

In response to Rei's words, Set gave a small, apologetic purr.

It was true that Set had been elated by the power of the Power Crush.

On top of that, he'd unleashed the Strike of Might while falling. In a way, this state was only natural.

Seeing Set's sad expression, Rei realized that even though he hadn't meant to, his words might have sounded like he was blaming Set. He hurriedly stroked Set.

...Blood and bits of flesh stuck to his hand, but he didn't seem particularly bothered by it now as he spoke to Set,

"Everyone makes mistakes, so don't worry about it. Besides, you did it because you thought Power Crush's level would go up and benefit me, right? So, I'm not blaming you at all."

Laying his hand on Set's head as he spoke, Rei stroked him for several minutes. As they walked on, Set seemed to gradually recover, his stride gradually filling with renewed energy.

They kept walking for over ten minutes. Finally, the sound of flowing water reached Rei's ears.

"There was a river, wasn't there?"

"Gurrr!"

At Rei's mutter, Set purred happily.

Then, as they emerged from the forest trees... the next moment, a river appeared before Rei and Set.

It wasn't a particularly large river, though.

The depth ranged from ankle-deep to knee-deep at its deepest point.

The opposite bank was about seven to eight meters away.

It was too shallow for swimming, but it was perfectly fine for washing Set's body.

"Grrr..."

Set let out a slightly disappointed growl, perhaps wishing he could dive into the river and play.

The idea of diving to chase fish seemed appealing, likely because Set was a griffin with elements of a bird of prey. His fondness for fish might also stem from his lion... or more accurately, his feline heritage.

"Come on, Set. You're going to wash yourself, right? Come here."

Rei, now unencumbered after stowing the Dragon Robe and Sleipnir's Boots in the Misty Ring, called to Set.

"Grrrrr!"

Responding to Rei's call, Set immediately heads toward her, preferring to be washed by Rei rather than diving to catch fish.

Even though he'd managed to remove some meat fragments and bits of entrails during their journey, it still wasn't completely clean.

If he charged in like this, Rei would end up covered in bits of flesh and organs too... So she quickly began splashing river water over Set to wash him down.

"Gururururu!"

Whether it was because Rei's hands washing his body felt good, Set let out a happy growl.

Watching him like this, Rei realized a smile had slipped onto her own lips without her noticing.

Seeing someone happy because of something you did was just that satisfying.

"Hey, don't struggle so much. ...This area here, the blood's starting to clot. Just hold still while I pour a bit more water on it."

"Gurrr!"

Nodding, whether out of habit or griffon instinct, Set shook his body like a dog when wet.

Naturally, this meant water splashed onto Rei, who was nearby...

"Whoa!"

Rei let out a small yelp from the large amount of water sprayed on him.

Still, thanks to it being spring, the river water wasn't that cold.

It was sunny today, and even in the forest, it felt a bit warm. The coolness of the water was actually quite pleasant.

Still, getting soaked wasn't exactly fun for Rei either, so she scooped up some river water and splashed it at Set.

"Grrr! ... Grrrrrrr!"

Set was startled by the sudden splash of water, but in the next instant, he slammed his front paw into the water's surface and splashed water back at Rei.

"Whoa, nice one, Set. Well, I'll return the favor!"

And so began the water fight in the river.

One human and one dog became completely absorbed in splashing each other.

...Both Rei and Set possessed physical abilities far surpassing the average human. They dodged the water aimed at them, immediately countering to splash their opponent... only for the other to dodge again. This exchange involved such high-level physical skill that it could hardly be called mere play.

(Isn't this the kind of thing you do with a lover or something?)

Playing... no, fighting with Set, a classic scene from a manga Rei had read back in Japan suddenly flashed through his mind.

It wasn't a river, but the ocean or a pool, where lovers would splash each other with water, exchanging clichéd banter like, "Eii!" "Kyaa, gotcha! Now it's my turn, Eii!" "Whoa, you're good."

(Why am I playing around with Set like this? ...Not that I mind, though.)

Thinking such things while splashing water, his gaze suddenly fell... He noticed a small crab at the bottom of the water.

"Oh, a crab. Set, there's a crab!"

"Grrr?"

A crab? Intrigued by Rei's words, Set stopped splashing water at him and approached.

It wasn't like this was the first time Set had seen a crab.

He'd eaten them several times when he went to the port town of Emotion.

(Well, I'm more of a shrimp guy than a crab guy, though.)

Watching the tiny crab, no bigger than a fingertip, Rei murmured to himself.

It was fine just looking at it like this, but when it came to eating, Rei much preferred shrimp over crab.

Above all, the texture of shrimp suited Rei's taste perfectly, and he felt he could eat shrimp endlessly.

Precisely because of that texture preference, Rei preferred larger shrimp. Small shrimp, even though they were shrimp, just weren't to her liking.

(I'd love some fried shrimp right now.)

Thinking such thoughts, Rei enjoyed playing in the river with Set.

...Completely forgetting about investigating the forest's disturbance.

It would be a little while longer before the one person and one creature noticed.
