The guild where Rei showed his face for the first time in a while presented the scene he had expected.

While the tavern area was packed with adventurers, the quest board area was nearly deserted.

The few who were there were likely those who had failed to save enough money to get through the winter.

The drinkers in the tavern naturally noticed Rei as he entered the guild.

"Hey, isn't that Rei over there?"

"Hic... Huh? Whoa, it really is Rei. Why's he at the guild this time of year?"

"Maybe he came to eat? I mean, he's always eating at stalls with Set, right?"

Hearing that, the men drinking at the same table... no, even those at nearby tables nodded in agreement.

In the adventurers' minds, a successful adventurer ate delicious food, slept with beautiful women, and gambled lavishly...

Among them, Rei was the adventurer who specialized in eating.

Then again, with stunning beauties like Vihera around him, he probably did enjoy the company of beautiful women... they thought, tinged with envy.

Still, that was merely the perception of these adventurers; in reality, adventurers came in all shapes and sizes.

Anyway, despite all eyes being on Rei, contrary to the adventurers' expectations, he headed not toward the bar but toward the request board... or more precisely, toward the reception counter.

"Ah, Rei! Over here, over here!"

Kenny, who seemed bored at the counter, spotted Rei and waved her arms wildly to call him over.

Lenora, who was usually beside her, was nowhere to be seen. Perhaps she was busy with some other task?

With that thought in mind, Rei approached the counter and addressed Kenny.

"I came because I was summoned... but from what I can see, it doesn't look like anything urgent, does it?"

Rei had been lounging in his lodgings reading the Monster Compendium when the guild summoned him, prompting this hurried visit... Yet looking around, there was no sign of any particular rush or urgency.

Having assumed something like the previous Magic Fever outbreak had occurred, Rei felt a bit deflated.

(Good thing I didn't bring Vihera.)

When Rei was summoned to the guild, Vihera initially wanted to go with him.

But he had asked Rei to prepare in case something happened and they needed to act quickly... Seeing the inside of the guild now, it seemed like a waste of effort.

Still, the only consolation was that if he could store the prepared items in the Misty Ring, then the preparations wouldn't be entirely wasted.

(Someone like Byune would probably eat the food I prepared...)

Recalling Byune, who loved eating just as much as himself... or perhaps even more, Rei let out a small smile.

Seeing Rei, Kenny bowed his head in surprise.

"Ah, sorry. It's not like I had some urgent business like last time. It's just... this time of year, you know?"

Hearing Kenny's explanation, Rei couldn't help but agree.

For most adventurers, the only reason to come to the guild this time of year was to visit the tavern.

Some adventurers still stood before the request board, agonizing over choices. But when it came to funds for winter survival, Rei possessed enough wealth to live off for decades, perhaps even a century.

Selling materials like Silver Lion pelts, Set feathers, or body hair would bring in a fortune beyond calculation.

If someone had business with Rei like that, they'd naturally go see him themselves... or else, they'd need to get him to come to the guild.

Kenny would have preferred to go see him himself, but being in the middle of work made that impossible.

That's precisely why he sent another guild employee to run the errand.

"Ah... I see. Well, it's not like I had anything urgent to do anyway, so it's fine. I just wish you'd told me it wasn't urgent."

"...I'm sorry. That was clearly my mistake."

Rei flustered slightly at the sight of Kenny bowing his head.

As Rei had said, he didn't actually have anything urgent to do right now.

He'd only given a casual reminder, never expecting Kenny to get so down about it.

"Ah, don't worry about it. I'm not really bothered by it either. ...Anyway..."

To change the mood, Rei cleared his throat softly before speaking again.

"So, what exactly did you call me here for? I'd like to hear the details, if possible."

"Right. ...Well, to put it simply, there's been a request specifically for you, Rei."

"...A designated request? At this time of year?"

The displeased expression on Rei's face was undoubtedly because the previous named request incident was still fresh in his mind.

He'd been told he couldn't refuse once he heard the details, but when he did refuse the request, mercenaries were hired and sent to attack the carriage Rei was riding in, almost like harassment.

...The miscalculation for the nobles and mercenaries was that the carriage Rei was riding in wasn't an ordinary carriage, but the one Elena was riding in.

Because of that incident, a slight tension now exists between the Noble Faction and the royal faction.

Normally, the royalist faction, which holds greater power, would have the upper hand over the Noble Faction. But this time, it was simply too much.

The mercenaries who attacked the carriage carrying Elena, the symbol of the Noble Faction and the woman known as the Princess General.

And the fact that it was revealed Viscount Linus Marnino hired those mercenaries was significant.

This was the result of action taken by those known as the Grassland Wolves, under Daskar's command. Consequently, the Royalist faction found itself in a position where it could offer no plausible excuse.

Naturally, upon learning this, the royalists immediately discarded Linus, as if cutting off a lizard's tail.

But could the Noble Faction, whose symbol had been attacked, possibly accept that? Of course not.

The result was the current standoff.

In that sense, the Neutral Faction stands to gain the most from this incident.

After all, the King's faction and the Noble Faction, both wielding greater influence than themselves, were now locked in conflict.

Setting aside such factional strife, precisely because of that incident, Rei was not eager to accept the request for an appointment.

"Don't worry. This request has nothing to do with nobles or anything like that."

"Really? ...Well, I suppose I could hear you out a bit."

Rei felt a slight relief at Kenny's words and fell silent, ready to listen.

Seemingly pleased to be talking to Rei, Kenny smiled as he spoke.

"As you know, Rei, Gilm gets pretty quiet in winter."

"Yeah, I guess so."

From spring to autumn, Gilm bustles with merchants, travelers, and newly arrived adventurers. But come winter, the merchants disappear, and the adventurers who came but judged their skills insufficient leave.

As for the travelers, some leave before winter sets in, while others stay in Gilm.

Either way, it was undeniable that fewer people were seen in Gilm compared to spring through autumn.

...Though, to be fair, Gilm was already small enough to be considered a town. Losing a few people didn't make it feel truly deserted or lonely.

"So, the restaurant near the guild asked me to help develop a new dish."

"...Did you mention fewer people earlier? Is that relevant now?"

"I suppose it does. That's what they said."

At Rei's words, Kenny shrugged his shoulders slightly.

As he did, the cleavage visible through the guild uniform's wide-open neckline swayed heavily, full of substance.

Though Kenny usually layered up in bulky winter clothes when going outside, inside the warm guild hall, heated by magic items, she wore only her usual guild uniform.

Averting his gaze from Kenny's appearance, Rei spoke up.

"So, what kind of new dish do you want? Something udon-based?"

Rei had taught the owner of the Manpukutei restaurant, and which had spread from there.

It had already become one of Gilm's signature dishes and spread to other towns.

Some dishes, like grilled udon, had even evolved into unique creations.

Rei wondered if they wanted him to come up with a new udon dish, but inwardly, he scratched his head.

Rei wasn't particularly knowledgeable about cooking to begin with.

Of course, he'd made some dishes during cooking classes at school, and he had experience making what might be called "guy food" at home, starting with curry.

But... that was about the extent of it.

He enjoyed cooking manga to a certain extent, but if asked if he remembered every dish featured in them, he'd shake his head immediately.

(Curry udon... but there's no curry, right? Putting udon in stew? I've seen that before. Kama-tama udon? That might work, but then there's the issue of whether he can safely eat raw eggs, or if he can even get a steady supply of them every day...)

Rei hesitated inwardly, but Kenny shook his head at her assumption.

"No. Not existing udon dishes. Something completely new, like a brand-new dish no one's ever eaten before."

"...What do you expect from a mere adventurer?"

"Well, you're a mage, right? If you could invent udon, surely you could invent a new dish too?"

"I don't know. Nothing's coming to mind. Curry... that'd be tough."

"Curry?"

"Yeah."

Since we have udon, if curry catches on, we could eat curry udon.

That's what I think, but Rei doesn't know how to make curry.

The curry she made back in Japan used curry roux. She knew a little about secret ingredients like chocolate, coffee, ketchup, sauce, and soy sauce, but that was about it.

Plus, making curry obviously requires multiple spices, which would also bring up the issue of cost.

(What was it... There was a cooking manga centered around curry, right?)

I think back to my time in Japan, but while I remember the general story, I don't recall at all what ingredients they used to make the curry.

(If only I'd remembered this stuff properly back then...)

I regret it, but it's too late now.

"Rei, what's wrong?"

"Nothing. I was trying to remember how to make curry, but all I recall is that you use a ton of spices—like dozens of different kinds."

"Ah... that sounds pretty difficult. Even if you could make something like that, only a few people would be able to eat it."

"Right? Plus, I have no idea how long it would take to actually make it properly."

"Can't you think of anything?"

"Even if you say that..."

The only other thing that immediately came to mind was okonomiyaki, but I'd already taught that as seafood okonomiyaki somewhere else.

(Any other unusual dishes...)

As Rei thought this far, he suddenly remembered the curry bread-like thing he'd eaten just the other day... No, considering it didn't use curry powder, maybe it should be called a piroshki-like thing.

Of course, without curry powder, you can't make curry bread, and it wasn't exactly something you could call baked piroshki-like either.

Something you could make using existing cooking methods, yet still be easy to eat. And something that would be especially welcome when it got cold.

"Steamed pork buns?"

"Steamed pork buns? What's that?"

Rei blurted out his idea, but Kenny was the only one listening.

It probably had something to do with the season, meaning there were fewer receptionists on duty.

"You know what steamed buns are?"

"Bug bread? ...Hey, Rei. You're not planning to use bugs, are you?"

The idea of eating insects divides people extremely sharply.

Kenny, it seemed, was firmly in the dislike camp.

Sensing this, Rei hurriedly shook his head.

"No. Not bread made with bugs. It's steamed, like steamed dishes."

"...Steaming? Bread? Not baking?"

"Yeah."

"I think the deliciousness of bread comes from things like that toasty aroma and soft texture."

"Well, the toasty aroma aside, in terms of texture, steamed bread holds its own. Actually, it might even be better."

"Hmm, I can't quite picture it. At least, I've never tried it."

"Then..."

Even without hearing Rei's words to the end, Kenny seemed to understand what Rei was getting at.

He nodded immediately.

"Yeah, I think it'll work."

"Then, please proceed with accepting the designated request."

And so, Rei accepted the request to create a new dish.
