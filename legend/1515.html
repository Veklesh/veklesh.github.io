Rei turned around, puzzled by the voice that had called out to him from behind.

His question was why they had gone out of their way to speak to him.

Though Rei was clad in a Dragon Robe, its magical properties included a concealment effect designed to make it inconspicuous as a magic item.

In other words, to the naked eye, Rei currently appeared to be nothing more than a person wearing an ordinary—albeit somewhat flashy—robe.

Moreover, Rei was shorter than the other patrons and looked unmistakably like a child.

Although Rei had come to Mejougo ostensibly for social studies, he certainly couldn't be called a VIP customer compared to the others.

So why call out to him? It was only natural to wonder.

Turning around with that question in mind, Rei's gaze fell upon a young prostitute... one who appeared to be in her late teens.

She wore light makeup, but her features were more cute than beautiful.

"Me?"

Rei replied to the woman who had spoken to him.

Seeing Rei's reaction, the woman smiled and nodded.

"Yes, that's right. You're probably new here, aren't you? So, before you get caught up in some shady place, I thought maybe you'd like to try me. Believe it or not, I just started working as a prostitute not too long ago, so you can enjoy a fresh, untouched body, you know?"

Even if she was young, a prostitute was still a prostitute.

The woman cast a seductive glance at Rei and asked.

For someone who claimed to be new to the trade, her approach was surprisingly practiced. Unfortunately, Rei, who had never bought a prostitute before, seemed oblivious to her attitude.

"Well, the friend I came with did recommend a place..."

"Oh, no, no. Don't you think a place chosen through a prostitute's eyes is more likely to be a good find than one picked from a customer's perspective?"

"Well..."

Rei quietly averted his gaze at the prostitute's words.

In fact, even considering the kind of restaurants Rei liked, there was a difference between places that satisfied ordinary customers and those that satisfied the chefs actually preparing the food.

When it comes to which truly satisfies you, it's definitely the latter.

Of course, customer satisfaction is the fundamental premise—though those with self-serving ideas are a different story.

In any case, if you're choosing a recommended brothel, asking a prostitute is undoubtedly the best way.

...Though, Rei hadn't come to Mejougo intending to buy a prostitute. He'd come to see what fate awaited the women who'd been forcibly taken away.

"Hmm, that sounds interesting too... but I want to explore Mejougo more thoroughly first, to properly understand what kind of place it is. After that, I might buy a prostitute... but unfortunately, it can't be right now. Besides..."

Seeing Rei pause mid-sentence, the woman tilted her head in puzzlement.

"Besides? What is it?"

"Well, considering what you said earlier, the place you'd show me around would be your own establishment, right?"

"Uh, w-well..."

Apparently, he'd hit the nail on the head. The prostitute gently averted her gaze from Rei.

But she recovered quickly, which was proof of her resilience.

"But my place is pretty famous around here. I work hard, you know. What do you say?"

"Ahaha. Well, I'll look around a bit... and if I'm satisfied, I might come to your place. ...Oh, by the way, could you tell me your name? I'm Ren."

"Ren?"

The instant Rei said "Ren"... just for a split second, the prostitute wore a puzzled expression.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn't be surprising if one missed that fleeting moment.

But considering Rei normal was, to begin with, far too much of a stretch.

The moment he sensed the woman's abnormality, the wariness within Rei rapidly intensified.

Still, the fact that he didn't let his guard being up show on his face was likely down to the experience gained from surviving countless battles.

"Yeah, I'm Ren. What about you?"

"Huh? Me? Oh, I didn't introduce myself? I'm Sura. Nice to meet you."

Whether delighted to have introduced herself to Rei or not, the prostitute calling herself Sura beamed a broad smile.

But Rei, who had seen that fleeting moment of doubt earlier, already suspected that this woman named Sura was suppressing her true feelings, wearing a mask as a new prostitute before him.

(That expression earlier... Could it be she acted that way knowing I was Rei? Well, I did use my guild card normally when entering Rossi, and people were surprised by me then, so it wouldn't be strange if word got out.)

Thinking that, Rei couldn't help but wonder if he really needed to be so wary. Still, he decided caution was necessary for now.

After all, if she'd approached him specifically from the start, he had no idea what she might be after.

"Right, Sura-san. Nice to meet you."

"I don't mind you saying 'nice to meet you,' but I'd rather you buy me. ...No? Really no?"

"Like I said earlier, it's not possible right now. I want to look around this Mejougo more. Besides, pretty ladies are easy on the eyes."

The moment he said that, Rei felt something stir inside the Dragon Robe. Flustered, he hurriedly added,

"Well, then, I'll take my leave. If we meet again, I'd appreciate it."

"Huh? Ah, wait... Got it. Well, if we meet again, come hang out!"

Sura tried to say something to Rei, but he had already moved away from her.

She must have decided that persistently pursuing such a Rei would only leave a bad impression.

Sura watched Rei walk away, a hint of disappointment visible on her face.

Once she was sure Rei was completely out of sight, Sura slipped into the alleyway between two brothels nearby.

While the main street had numerous lights, they were merely for the customers' enjoyment.

Naturally, places like back alleys had no such lights.

Even so, the moon floating in the midsummer night, unclouded, cast a decent amount of light onto the ground.

Sura walked through that faint moonlight, but the pleasant smile that had been on her face moments before was gone.

The word "expressionless" couldn't fit anyone better; her face showed absolutely no emotion.

Dressed in revealing clothing befitting a prostitute, the expressionless Sura almost seemed like a doll or something.

"So, what do you think? Was it definitely Rei?"

"Yes, probably. ...But he seemed suspicious of me partway through the conversation. I thought we were talking normally until then."

"...You? Did she suspect you?"

"Seems so. Sharp intuition, I suppose... Is that the ability of a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname?"

"So what do we do? Make contact? You know we're severely outnumbered. ...One of our bases got hit yesterday too. Those bastards again."

The man's voice tried to suppress his emotions, but it wasn't entirely successful.

No, the more he failed to hide it, the more hatred seeped into his tone.

"I know. But if we move carelessly, we'll be crushed immediately. ...After what happened in the spring, you should understand that well enough, shouldn't you?"

The spring incident.

The man who heard this reflexively wanted to shout something back, but realizing where they were, he forced himself to speak.

"I know. ...That incident decimated our numbers. But if we don't act now, they might go after Rei. Or Rei might go after the organization... after Jaya's people."

The moment he uttered the name of the organization, Jaya, his words were imbued with unmistakable hatred.

But seeing the man like that, Sura thought it was only natural.

His lover had been taken away, forced into prostitution... and ultimately, his lover's life had been lost.

On top of that, the reason for that loss.

It was simply something he couldn't possibly tolerate.

"Yeah. If Jaya gets involved, they'll just end up getting hurt themselves... Still, why do they have to show up right when we're about to take action?"

"...Hmph. Who knows? Maybe Jaya's the one messing with Rei? I don't know why he'd deliberately target Gilm, Rei's base of operations."

"Would he do something like that? Even with all his forces, this country is still just a small one, isn't it? There are plenty of places to attack, like villages out in the countryside of the Mireana Kingdom, right?"

"Gilm is undergoing expansion work, right? Maybe it's because people gather there... or something like that?"

News of the expansion work in Gilm must have reached the Kingdom of Reburina. The man stated this matter-of-factly.

Though the Kingdom of Reburina lay over a month's journey by carriage from Gilm, the fact that news reached them so reliably showed just how widely known the expansion project was.

That's precisely why people are flocking to Gilm from all over the country... no, even from neighboring nations, seeking work.

"But... no, never mind. Anyway, it's clear that Rei isn't employed by those Jayah people. ...When I first heard that Crimson had come to Rossi, I panicked, thinking maybe he'd been hired over there."

As she said this, Sura's expression softened slightly, revealing a hint of relief.

Even without this, there was a huge disparity in strength between the Jayah, who had deep connections to the upper echelons of the nation, and their own resistance.

If even a famed adventurer joined the Jaya side, the gap in strength would become hopelessly wide.

Such a thing absolutely could not be allowed.

"Rei came to Rossi with a bunch of women, right? If they learn what Jaya's doing, maybe the women would cooperate, even if Rei himself wouldn't? Being part of Rei's party means they must have considerable strength."

In situations like this, women who rely entirely on men... well, sometimes the opposite pattern isn't unheard of either.

But regarding Rei's party, the Crimson Wings, I recalled hearing from a merchant who came from the Kingdom of Mireana that this wasn't the case at all—every single member was sufficiently strong.

And once they understood the situation, he expected they would likely... no, almost certainly agree to cooperate with them.

The man surged forward.

It was only natural. If the one who had truly arrived was the Princess General, he believed she would undoubtedly not overlook what was happening in this country... and what was happening here in Mejougo.

"If the rumors about the Princess General are true, she'll be fine. But what about the people behind her? Horanz knows this too, right? The Princess General is the symbol of the Noble Faction. The nobles. Now, do you really think they'll just let what Jaya is doing slide once they find out?"
