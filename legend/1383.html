At first, Rei and the others thought they were enemies, but they relaxed when they recognized the familiar figure that appeared.

The same seemed true for the other side. Seeing Rei and the others, they lowered the weapons they had been holding.

"See? I told you they weren't enemies!"

"You didn't say that!"

Runo muttered in exasperation, prompting a reflexive retort from his fellow adventurer.

In truth, Runo had noticed something ahead of them thanks to his magic-seeing eyes.

He had informed his companions of this, but he hadn't said a word about it being Rei and the others.

"Huh? Was that it?"

"Yeah. Damn... Well, at least it wasn't a monster."

Runo gave an ambiguous smile to the adventurer who muttered with a clear sense of relief, trying to brush it off.

They both felt relieved knowing they weren't enemies, but in this case, Runo and his group were probably the ones feeling more relieved.

After all, when they cleared the branches blocking their path and moved forward, the ones waiting for them were the members of Crimson Wings.

Fortunately, Rei and the others quickly dropped their battle stances. But if they had fought, misunderstanding each other's intentions... well, it was only natural to feel genuinely relieved.

And it was only natural that Runo's tone grew a bit harsher when she realized she hadn't informed them about Rei and the others.

"It's a relief we're all safe and sound."

Vihera spoke up, trying to dispel the awkward tension in the air.

Though the adventurers had faced mortal danger mere seconds ago, they couldn't help but be pleased to be spoken to by a beauty like Vihera.

While remaining alert to their surroundings, their tension eased somewhat.

...Though a few of them relaxed a bit too much.

"Ah, I'm glad you're safe too. Though, to be honest, I didn't find anything particularly noteworthy when I looked around."

"Yeah, same here. ...There were a few interesting things, but nothing that important either."

"Yeah, exactly. It was the same yesterday when I came into this forest as a woodcutter's escort. There's absolutely nothing that looks promising. ...I never thought it would be like this, even after going so deep into the forest."

"Come to think of it, you got here surprisingly fast, didn't you? I thought we were the ones who'd made the most progress..."

"Ah... well..."

In response to Vihera's question, the adventurer who had been speaking turned to the researcher assigned to their group.

Glancing around, he saw the researcher watching Set with an interested gaze, waiting for the adventurers to finish talking.

"Once he realizes there's nothing left to investigate, he just keeps pushing deeper into the forest without hesitation. And then he just keeps going..."

That's how he caught up to Rei and the others.

The adventurer man explained.

Hearing this, Vihera turned her gaze toward the researcher.

He was watching Set, but didn't seem inclined to force anything.

Some researchers, upon learning of Set's existence, might insist on keeping him for themselves based on their own theories. Fortunately, this researcher didn't seem to be that type.

That was fortunate for Rei and the others... but even more so for the adventurers guarding the researcher.

They knew full well what consequences awaited if they angered Rei and the others here.

If word spread that they'd bullied Set, who had become something of a mascot for Gilm, life in Gilm would become unbearably awkward.

Given that, the adventurers hoped the researcher would behave himself, and for now, that hope was being fulfilled.

A short distance away from Vihera and the others engaged in that exchange, Rei was also talking with Runo and Slesha.

"I see. Even Runo's eyes couldn't tell?"

"Yeah. I kind of expected that when I came yesterday. ...Still, it's a bit surprising that every single tree in this forest possesses magic. I don't sense any magic from branches lying on the ground, though."

"Huh. So broken branches are recognized as dead, then?"

"I wonder."

"Honestly, it feels a bit unnatural for branches to break and fall to the ground in this forest where there are no animals or monsters."

Being in Treant Forest, where every other party member except himself had been killed, was probably affecting him.

Even to Rei's eyes, Slesha looked extremely tense.

(She's probably just being cautious about ambushes... but she's so tense it might actually be counterproductive.)

Even as he thought this, a part of Rei understood it was only natural to feel this way when arriving at the place where his comrades had been killed.

Even if it was just an acquaintance, seeing an adventurer he knew—and didn't dislike, he assumed—die wasn't pleasant. So, he addressed Slesha in a casual tone.

"Slesha, I get you're pumped up, but if you tense up too much, you might freeze up when something actually happens, or overreact and put us in danger."

"I... I know that..."

Even when cautioned by Rei, who was clearly younger than her, Slesha didn't get angry.

She understood that despite being younger, his adventurer skills and rank were overwhelmingly superior to hers.

But even knowing that, if asked whether she could actually follow his advice, the answer was no.

Slesha understood she needed to relax her grip. She understood it, yet somehow, without even realizing it, her muscles would tense up.

Because she knew exactly what kind of place this was.

"...I do understand, though."

"Even so, you're much more relaxed than you were at the start."

Runo, who had been listening to Rei and Slesha's conversation, said this, but upon hearing it, Rei tilted his head.

"Why? If you're coming to Treant Forest, you were here yesterday too, right?"

"Yes. ...But yesterday was merely escorting the woodcutters. Compared to that, today is a proper investigation, so I couldn't help but..."

...I can't help but tense up, he thought.

As Rei considered that it couldn't be helped, the researcher man who had been watching Set spoke up.

"Excuse me, but could we wrap this up soon? Since we have to leave Treant Forest before evening, I'd like to get as much done as possible."

"Ah... I see. Sorry about that."

At Runo's words, Rei shook his head.

A little further away, the adventurers who had been talking with Vihera and Marina ended their conversation, looking slightly disappointed.

"Well, nothing should happen during the day, but be careful just in case."

"Yeah. Rei and the others too."

Rei and Runo exchanged a few brief words before heading off to join their respective groups.

Watching Runo and the others wave lightly and depart, Rei and his group also started walking off in a different direction.

"Still, it seems they haven't found anything either. ...During the day, it really is just an ordinary forest. No animals or monsters, though."

As Rei murmured while walking through the forest, Vihera, walking beside him, nodded.

"Yeah. If nothing's appearing like this, maybe we should just leave the forest? Save our strength for nightfall. Or get our bodies moving in preparation for battle."

Vihera's suggestions were two extremes, but it was obvious which one reflected her preference.

For Vihera, a battle-crazed warrior, training in combat right here at the forest's edge felt far more fulfilling than trudging through this empty woods.

Moreover, many adventurers of considerable strength besides Rei and the others had gathered here.

...Though, Rei couldn't say how many adventurers actually wanted to fight Vihera and lose.

It had been quite some time since Vihera arrived in Gilm.

And given Vihera's appearance and her dancer-like, prostitute-like attire, the number of men who'd tried to hit on her could be counted on more than two hands... no, even using both hands and feet wouldn't be enough.

For that very reason, it was fairly well known that Vihera was strong.

So, when someone was invited to spar with such a Vihera and accepted...

(probably exist)

While no one wants to get beaten to a pulp by Vihera, those who do accept get to see her limbs up close.

Moreover, depending on their own skill, they might even get to touch Vihera's body.

If asked whether one could resist such temptation, it would be difficult for any man to turn it down.

"Grrr?"

What should we do? Set's growl echoes from his throat. After a moment's thought, Rei speaks.

"Well then, let's look around the area a bit more. If nothing turns up, we'll head back. Besides, I don't feel like eating lunch in a place like this."

Normally, eating in a spring forest would be like enjoying a picnic.

But not in this Treant Forest. ...Or rather, it's more accurate to say he wouldn't even consider it.

Honestly, if they were going to eat here, they'd rather wait until they were out of the forest.

The others seemed to share Rei's sentiment. None voiced any objection.

Even Byune, who was as greedy about eating as Rei or Set, felt the same way. That alone showed just how eerie the Treant Forest was.

Normally, the dappled sunlight filtering through the trees was a spot Rei liked to relax in.

"Looks like no one objects. Let's walk around a bit more."

Rei muttered as he started walking, and everyone followed.

Even as they walked through the forest, Rei remained vigilant. But with Set present, it was far more likely that Set would sense danger before Rei ever felt threatened.

Given that, it was only natural that his guard would slip.

"Oh? ...Hey, Rei."

Marina suddenly spoke up.

"What is it?"

"Look, that vine... Does it look familiar?"

Following Marina's gaze, he saw vines wrapped around a tree.

To Rei's eyes, the green vine looked like nothing special—just an ordinary vine.

But if Marina, a dark elf knowledgeable about the forest, had noticed it, it surely held some meaning.

Rei stared intently at the vine, but after several minutes passed, she shook her head.

"No good. I don't get it. What is that vine?"

Not just Rei, but Vihera, Byune, and Set also turned their gaze to Marina, urging an answer.

Under their combined gaze, Marina sighed and spoke.

"Well, yesterday when we came into this forest, the Fekts that were fleeing were attacked by the Treant in front and the vines from behind, right? This is that vine."

"...This?"

Since the vines had been entirely left to Marina, this was the first time Rei had clearly seen them.

That's precisely why, even when told such vines suddenly appeared before them, it felt somehow unreal.

What's more, even though Rei and the others were standing right in front of it now, the vine showed absolutely no sign of moving.

It looked just like ordinary vines.

(Well, if Treant's true form is just an ordinary tree, then this must be ordinary vine too.)

Thinking this, he reached out to touch it, but even when Rei touched it, the vine showed no sign of moving.

It's not part of a monster, let alone the monster itself.

Still, Rei didn't think Marina had mistaken this vine.

As a Dark Elf, she was knowledgeable about plants. Before becoming Guildmaster, she'd spent years adventuring and completed numerous quests. And as Guildmaster, she'd undoubtedly gained even more knowledge.

And above all, since it was Marina who said it, Rei had no reason to doubt her.

...Of course, if she was clearly mistaken, that would be another matter, but the likelihood of that seemed extremely low.

"Hmm, even touching it like this, it feels no different from ordinary vines."

Vihera murmured as she touched the vine.

Beside her, Byune followed suit, touching the vine as well.

"Hm?"

But like Rei and Vihera, even touching the vine, she couldn't tell if it was truly a monster.

She tilted her head, murmuring softly.

"So, what should we do with this vine? Should we just take it with us?"

"Yes, if possible, I'd appreciate it. ...Ah, but please don't just cut it off anywhere. Try to keep it as long as possible."

"...This vine, you say?"

"Yes."

He stared at the vine. It was quite long.

Finding what could be considered its base would undoubtedly take some time.

But he couldn't possibly refuse Marina's request—it might be a crucial clue about this forest of Treant... So in the end, Rei found himself searching for the base of the vine.
