"Huh. So Gilm is really that different?"

Ranigus murmured in admiration at Rei’s explanation of Gilm.

At first glance, it didn’t look like acting at all.

Actually, since Ranigus wasn’t acting at all and was listening to Rei’s story with genuine surprise, that was only natural.

Even at the Musey Baron family, Ranigus’s family home, they naturally gather information on Gilm.

The Musey family itself isn’t a particularly large house, given that it is a baronial family, but as the largest faction—the Royal Faction—its influence in that regard is significant.

Numerous nobles from the Royal Faction have dispatched subordinates or relatives to Gilm, and they share information through these contacts.

They don’t share absolutely everything, and since there are several sub-factions within the Royal Faction itself, the information they obtain varies depending on those circumstances.

Even so, a great deal of information is available, but hearing the story directly from Rei like this provides a different perspective.

“Hey, Rei. Is it true that a huge forest suddenly appeared near Gilm?”

Teres, who had been listening to the conversation with Ranigus, suddenly asked that.

Logically speaking, discovering a forest that was already there would be one thing, but for a forest to suddenly appear near a town is simply impossible.

But… in a frontier region like Gilm, anything is possible.

In fact, if a forest were to appear near the town, it wouldn’t be all that surprising.

It wouldn’t be strange for someone to have that impression.

“Ah, it’s been created. It’s called Treant Forest, and the timber from that forest has been magically treated using alchemy and is being used for the current expansion work.”

“Huh… I guess that’s how things are in the frontier. Sounds kind of interesting. Maybe Ara could really shine in that forest?”

Teres said this while looking at Ara, likely because Ara uses a Power Axe as her weapon.

Since it’s shaped like an axe, as the name suggests, she’d likely be able to excel in the forest.

“I wouldn’t deny that.”

“Oh?”

Teres, who had assumed Ara would take offense at her words, gave her a surprised look.

However, Ara didn’t take Teres’s remark about excelling in the Treant Forest as a dig.

Well, Teres probably meant it that way, but since Ara knew the history of how her weapon had evolved, she didn’t think much of it.

“That said… if we’re talking about logging, Rei alone should be more than enough.”

Ara hadn’t seen it firsthand, but she knew from what she’d heard.

While it’s not uncommon for even an ordinary lumberjack to take several tens of minutes—or even an hour in some cases—to fell a single tree, Rei, wielding the Death Scythe, can finish the job with a single flash.

Compared to someone like Rei, even with a magic item like the Power Axe, she couldn’t possibly claim to be of any use.

“Oh.”

Having expected her to snap at him as usual, Teres muttered with a hint of disappointment.

Still, intrigued by Ara’s remark that “Rei is enough,” Teres turned her gaze toward him.

“Can Rei really fell a tree that easily?”

“If you ask me if I can, I suppose I’d have to say yes. That said, my main job wasn’t actually cutting down trees—it was carrying the ones the lumberjacks had already cut.”

“Ah, I see. Considering that Rei has an item box and the weight of the wood, that would be more efficient, wouldn’t it?”

Ranigus nodded, looking convinced.

“So that means carrying the felled wood is harder than actually cutting it down, right?”

“That’s right. Think about it, Teres. Which is harder: cutting down a tree or carrying it all the way to Gilm? It’s true that Treant Forest was formed near Gilm, but it’s not right next to Gilm. It just means it’s closer compared to other places.”

Ranigus’s words were correct.

In fact, Treant Forest isn’t close enough to reach on foot in just a short while.

And if you have to load the felled timber onto a custom-made wagon and haul it that distance, it wouldn’t be surprising if it took a few hours.

But for Rei, who has an item box and a means of transportation called Set, reaching Gilm takes only a few minutes.

Teres nodded at Ranigus’s explanation, but then she suddenly realized something.

“Wait a minute… So, if Rei can use the Item Box, wouldn’t it make no difference if he did the logging himself instead of just transporting the wood? He could start logging right away, couldn’t he?”

“Then why doesn’t Rei just do it alone?”

As Teres looks at him with that question in her eyes, Rei lowers the hand that had been eating the sandwich the maid had brought him.

By the way, this sandwich wasn’t made by Georgima…

Having been taught about ramen, Georgima had immediately set about trying to make it herself, so she was far too busy for that.

So, she had brought a sandwich made by Georgima’s subordinate—or perhaps I should say apprentice.

There’s no doubt the taste is inferior to last night’s or this morning’s meals, but that’s only when compared to Georgima’s own cooking.

It’s clearly no match for the sandwich prepared for breakfast… but even so, since it was made by a chef from the Duke of Kerebel’s household, it’s clearly superior in taste to the sandwiches served at your average bakery or diner.

Sandwiches are, after all, a simple dish that even Rei could make, so he was a little curious as to why the taste differed so markedly, but for now, answering Teres’s question took priority.

“I can cut down trees, but to put it bluntly, I’m just using brute force to chop them down. A professional lumberjack would consider things like which trees are suitable for building materials before cutting them down. That said, I’ve cut down every single tree growing in Treant Forest.”

“If that’s the case, then there’s no point in even judging the quality of the wood, is there?”

“I won’t deny that. But… I’m not just helping out with the expansion project. I have other work I need to do as well.”

In fact, while helping with the expansion project, Rei was also traveling to the Kingdom of Reburina, escorting those who had escaped from there, going out to sea to catch fish... and engaging in all sorts of activities.

And if Rei were to be absent—or, to be precise, since Set was also gone, leaving just one person and one beast—they wouldn’t be able to secure building materials.

If that happened, the expansion work itself could come to a standstill… so it was only natural to establish a system that allowed the construction to proceed even without Rei.

“Hmm. So Rei’s been busy, huh? I bet he didn’t even have time to spend with Lady Elena, did he?”

“Huh!?”

Cough.

Startled by the words that suddenly came out of Teres’ mouth, Elena managed to suppress a cough, though she narrowly avoided spitting out her tea.

Still, perhaps some tea had gone down the wrong way, as she choked a few times… but thanks to the abilities enhanced by the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone, she quickly recovered from the situation.

...The Ancient Dragon whose magic stone had been inherited by Elena probably never imagined that the one who inherited its stone would use its power for such a thing.

In any case, having managed to recover, Elena glared at Teres, who was wearing an amused smile.

Still, the fact that there wasn’t the same kind of pressure she’d directed at Gaiska meant that Elena was exercising proper self-control.

“Teres, what are you suddenly talking about?”

“Oh, I was just curious about that sort of thing. You were too, weren’t you?”

When Teres turned her gaze toward him, Ranigus thought for a moment before nodding in response.

“That’s true. As for the nature of your relationship… I’ve heard various rumors about it, and I’ve heard it’s often a topic of conversation among the Noble Faction. If that’s the case, I’d like to hear the truth behind those rumors, if possible.”

“Even you, Ranigus… That sort of thing isn’t something to be discussed in public, so unfortunately, I’ll have to remain silent.”

Even as she said this, seeing the faint blush on Elena’s cheeks made it obvious—without even needing to think about it—what kind of feelings Elena harbored toward Rei.

Teres and Ranigus would normally have wanted to hear the full story from Elena herself, but judging by her current demeanor, they understood the general situation and decided to stop pressing her there.

They could have pressed her more insistently, but they judged that doing so at this moment would likely upset Elena.

“Well then, let’s blatantly change the subject…”

The fact that he deliberately said “blatantly” out loud revealed just how mean-spirited Teres was.

But before Elena or Ara could say anything in response, Teres continued.

“Did anything unusual happen when Rei made that request? Something interesting would be fine, too. Living as a noble, I just can’t seem to get enough of that kind of excitement.”

Rei, having been asked the question, wasn’t sure how to respond to Teres.

When it comes to excitement, Rei has taken on all sorts of requests over the years, and he’s even gotten involved in incidents outside of official assignments.

However, many of those experiences were things he couldn’t casually share with others, and many of the assignments involved other people’s privacy.

"Avoiding those kinds of things..." As he pondered this, what came out of Rei’s mouth was an activity that, in a sense, had become one of his hobbies.

“How about hunting bandits or something like that?”

“...Bandit hunting? Is that exactly what it sounds like?”

Teres looked at him with interest.

For Teres, a noble, she had never actually seen a bandit with her own eyes.

They were merely something she’d heard about from others, or perhaps just a figure from fairy tales.

“That’s right. Normally, we’d be the ones getting attacked by bandits who prey on merchants and the like… but some bandits have amassed quite a fortune, and if we capture them alive, we can sell them as criminal slaves, so it’s actually quite a lucrative business.”

That’s what Rei said, but since he used to travel with Set, it was difficult to bring captured bandits back to towns or villages alive, so they usually ended up killing them all.

However, if they happened to be traveling by carriage for some reason, they would sometimes sell the captured bandits to slave traders.

“Hahahaha. So instead of bandits attacking us, we attack the bandits? Doesn’t that make Rei the bandit?”

“No, if you think of it as bandit suppression, there are actually quite a few requests for adventurers. …Though there are hardly any bandits around Gilm, so the Gilm Guild doesn’t get those kinds of requests.”

That said, merchants visiting Gilm sometimes hire adventurers as escorts, and when that happens, it’s not uncommon to fight bandits along the way.

For bandits, adventurers from Gilm are the worst possible opponents… Conversely, for adventurers, bandits are lucrative targets.

Of course, that’s only true if they have the skill to defeat the bandits in the first place.

“Bandits attack ordinary people, and adventurers attack bandits. When you think about it that way, are adventurers the most important ones?”

“I don’t know. Adventurers are doing work commissioned by those ordinary people, and without ordinary people, life would be pretty tough in a lot of ways. Isn’t it just a case of give and take?”

Some adventurers possess various production skills, starting with alchemists.

Take, for example, those whose families run restaurants or bakeries—they often help out at home from a young age, so they naturally pick up those skills.

However, relying solely on such people is nowhere near enough to satisfy everyone in the world.

Given that, it’s only because ordinary people do those kinds of jobs that adventurers can go about their daily lives.

In that sense, you could say adventurers are the most important... though I wouldn’t go so far as to say they stand at the pinnacle.

“Actually, if we’re talking about that, wouldn’t the nobility or royalty be the most important?”

“……Now that you mention it, you’re right.”

“Though, as a poor noble like us, I don’t really feel that way.”

Ranigus interjected as Rei and Teres spoke.

The Ranigus family is a baronial house, and in terms of rank, it is overwhelmingly low.

There’s no doubt they weren’t of a rank that would allow them to enjoy tea like this at a duke’s residence.

……Though he himself doesn’t show any sign of it.

“Is that so? I’ve heard there’s quite a buzz around their pottery and tableware, though.”

“I’d be delighted if Lady Elena said so, but they aren’t really that expensive.”

And so, the group enjoyed small talk, completely oblivious to the fact that Gaiska had vanished.
