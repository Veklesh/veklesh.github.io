With his wrists bound behind him, Rei flaunted the Death Scythe before the twenty or so bandits before him.

Unable to move, they faced a massive scythe with a blade nearly a meter long.

It goes without saying how terrified the bandits must be.

It should be noted that the bandits were bound only at the wrists; their legs remained free.

...Considering they would be taken to Gilm afterward, it made sense not to bind their legs, as that would have completely immobilized them.

Of course, to prevent them from escaping individually, their hands, bound behind their backs, were tied together with a single long rope.

This meant that if the bandits tried to escape, they would have to coordinate their movements perfectly, all fleeing in the same direction and matching each other's pace.

There was no way thieves like them could manage that. Escaping was impossible unless something extraordinary happened.

"Hiiii! Wha—what the hell?! Who the hell are you?!"

"Don't you know who I am? ...Seems you're just low-level thieves."

"Well, being a thief already makes you low-level, doesn't it?"

One of the guards overheard Rei's mutter and interjected, looking as if he couldn't help himself.

"Is that so? ...Well, I suppose you're right."

Though Rei knew of the existence of Egg, the Robin Hood-like figure, he had no desire to mention it here.

Even Egg, leading his men, now served Daskar in a spy-like role.

(I figured anyone running a bandit operation near Gilm was either a skilled bastard or a reckless fool... Looks like it was the latter. Wait, if these bandits didn't know me, maybe they weren't even from around here to begin with?)

Even if they weren't active around Gilm, any bandits operating near Aburo or Saburusta would naturally know about Rei.

Even if they didn't know Rei personally, adventurers who command griffins are known as untouchable entities.

Rei, known as the Bandit Eater—a thief who steals from thieves—is that famous around here.

No, his fame isn't confined to this area alone. Rei's notoriety has spread quite far.

He stands at the apex of a food chain, so to speak, with common folk at the bottom, thieves in the middle, and Rei at the top.

Yet, the thief before him knew nothing of Rei. That meant he was out of the loop.

(Was this a mistake?)

What Rei wanted was information on the alchemist who was giving magic items to monsters.

He captured these thieves assuming someone among them might have some knowledge, but now he wonders if it was all for nothing.

(But then again, whether it's the Cyclops, the Kobolds, or the adventurer pinned down before Gilm's gate—all of them were near Gilm. And overwhelmingly on Gilm's side, not Abuero's. Abuero should have received word from Lord Daskar too...)

Though troubled, he decided hearing their side of the story first was paramount and addressed the thieves.

"I'm going to ask you a few questions. Answer honestly, and I won't harm you. If I get answers that truly benefit me, I might consider letting you go."

At Rei's words, the thieves' eyes lit up with hope. At the same time, however, the men guarding them wore expressions that screamed, "What the hell is he thinking?"

The guards didn't voice their discontent because they knew that capturing these bandits was only possible because Rei and Set were there. Without them, things would undoubtedly have ended disastrously.

With all eyes on him—the thieves, the guards, and the two women—Rei swung the Death Scythe in his hand, severing their expectations.

A sharp slash that seemed to cleave the very air itself.

Seeing it, the bandits imagined that blow aimed at themselves and stifled screams in their throats.

"If any of you try to escape by lying... you know what happens, right?"

The meaning behind Rei's words was crystal clear after witnessing that slash from the Death Scythe.

If it were merely a matter of losing their lives, that might be bearable. But in the worst case, they would be subjected to torture using that great scythe...

Only after that fear had seeped into every one of the thieves did Rei finally speak.

"Now then, I have a question for you lot who've been operating around here. Haven't you seen any strange individuals around lately? ...'Strange individuals' is a bit vague, but specifically, I mean those operating alone or in small groups of just a few people."

That alone didn't seem to make it clear what Rei was getting at, so he continued.

"Those guys probably can't get into towns like Gilm or Abuero, so they're likely sleeping outside the city like you lot. They've probably set up a base somewhere. And it's probably a fairly large place."

Silence fell over the group at Rei's words.

Rei glanced over at the guards, but all he got in return was an apologetic shake of the head.

Being guards, they could enter towns, so they rarely needed to camp out around here. It was understandable they weren't familiar with that kind of information.

"So? What about you guys? Anyone got any intel?"

At Rei's words, the bandits exchanged glances, but none spoke.

"Do you genuinely not know anything, or are you just keeping quiet? ...By the way, if this keeps up, you'll end up being sold as slaves. You'd better think carefully about that."

"We know!"

The moment the word "slave" was uttered, one of the thieves shouted loudly.

"...Really? If this is a lie just to escape your current situation... you understand, right?"

The sheer force of the Death Scythe being swung, combined with its size, made it clear that if Rei wanted to, he could sever his body in an instant.

Better to be a slave than die for lying, right? Facing Rei's blatantly threatening stance, the man hurriedly opened his mouth.

"W-Well, it's not exactly certain! It's just information suggesting it's probably them, but is that okay!?"

"Probably, probably... Yeah. Without proof, it feels like you could say anything. ...Right? What do you think? Is the guy claiming to have info someone trustworthy?"

Rei asked the man tied up next to the one who claimed to have information. The man frantically nodded.

"He's... he's famous among us as the guy with the intel. So I don't think he's lying!"

Rei glanced at the thief, who was shouting in the hope that revealing useful information might save him. Then, turning his gaze back to the man who claimed to have information, he spoke.

"I'll hear you out. If you're trustworthy, I'll consider it as promised. ...Speak."

"Y-yeah. Um, do you know a forest a little ways away from this grove?"

"...Yeah."

Hearing the word "forest" from the thief's mouth gave Rei a glimmer of hope.

Because the Cyclops incident, the Kobold incident, and the man rampaging in front of Gilm's main gate getting his dagger... all of it had happened in the forest.

Which meant there was some credibility to what the man was saying.

"There's a river running through that forest, and a few days ago, I saw two guys heading upstream. No idea if they're the ones you're looking for, but..."

"...Could those two just be adventurers?"

If they were adventurers, it wouldn't be strange for them to enter the forest on some kind of request.

After all, Mireine and the others had entered the forest on a quest to defeat a cyclops.

But at Rei's words, the man who claimed to have seen suspicious figures shook his head.

"They probably weren't adventurers. Well, maybe one of them was an adventurer or something, but the other one definitely didn't look like an adventurer at all."

"What made them not look like adventurers?"

"They weren't wearing armor or any gear."

"...If you mean not wearing armor, then I'm the same, aren't I?"

What Rei wore was a Dragon Robe, boasting far superior defense compared to any ordinary metal armor.

Still, it wasn't armor.

Hearing Rei's words, the thief hurriedly continued.

"But man, that guy had like a bunch of glass bottles hanging from his belt, right? What adventurer in their right mind would strap something fragile like glass bottles to their waist? They'd shatter just from bumping into a tree before any real fight even started!"

"Glass bottles, huh?"

If they were ordinary glass bottles, they'd shatter instantly upon impact, just as the thief said.

But if the glass was reinforced with magic, that was a different story.

It wasn't uncommon for magic-enhanced glass containers to be used for preserving materials like monster organs or eyeballs.

For adventurers... well, anyone with even a little knowledge, even a thief, would likely know about this sort of thing. Yet the man who spoke to Rei, despite being reputed to be well-informed, didn't know.

(Well, this whole bandit gang seems to have overestimated their own abilities and come out to the frontier. So this is what they mean by 'well-informed'? Still, this info is pretty valuable. Carrying several pieces of magic-enhanced glass on their belts is very alchemist-like. A river... and upstream at that?)

Just as Rei was pondering this, something caught his attention. His gaze drifted to Set, who was keeping watch over the bandits nearby, and his question was answered.

(Right. When those Bestir Empire guys infiltrated Gilm before, they tried to escape using a transfer stone, but Set charged in and accidentally teleported them to that cave. Could this be it? If they originally planned it as an escape route, it wouldn't be strange for them to have supplies and facilities to live off of.)

The cave they'd visited before regarding Set's incident flashed through Rei's mind.

He didn't remember the exact route since they'd flown, but knowing it was upstream meant Set, who could fly, wouldn't take long to get there.

"That's some pretty good intel."

"R-Really?! Then you'll help me out, right!?"

The male bandit clung desperately to the single thread dangling before him, shouting.

Hearing his words, the women who had been attacked by the bandits moments before slightly frowned.

However, unlike those women, the bodyguard showed no particular change in expression.

Knowing that Rei was feared as the Bandit Eater, they couldn't possibly let the bandit escape, even if he had provided information.

"Well, even if I let you go, you probably couldn't do much alone. But considering you're an informant, there's a chance you could cause more trouble... huh? Sorry, but I really can't just let you go like this."

"Hey, wait a minute! What's that supposed to mean?! You said you'd help me if I gave you information!"

"Save you? I said I'd consider letting you escape, but I never promised I definitely would, right? So, after considering whether to let you go, I decided it's better not to. Letting you escape would just cause unnecessary damage."

Hearing Rei's words, the thief couldn't accept it and shouted, "You've got to be kidding me!"

"No, help me! Please, I beg you! I gave you the info you needed, right?! So, keep your promise and let me go!"

"I never said I'd help you. I only said I'd consider it."

"You've got to be kidding me... You've got to be kidding me! What the hell is that?! How the hell am I supposed to accept that?!"

Laying a sigh before speaking to the shouting thief, Rei said,

"Acceptable, huh? Well, were all the people you've attacked so far acceptable? If so, I might consider listening to what you have to say."

"...Ah, yeah. Of course. We don't take lives needlessly! We know our limits!"

"Well, that's a bit odd, considering you were trying to beat the crap out of me when I showed up?"

"That... well, it was just an act!"

"I see. Then tell the guards that during their interrogation. ...Can I leave the rest to you?"

Ignoring the thief still shouting in protest, Rei addressed his guards.

"Ah, yeah. But the money from when these guys became slaves..."

"You lot can split it. Looks like the carriage was wrecked, and your gear's pretty beat up too."

The guards, who had been under attack by the bandits until Rei arrived, had considerable damage to their armor and weapons.

Laying out that repairing it would cost a fair bit of money, Rei's words left the guards momentarily stunned, but they quickly bowed their heads.

"This is a huge help."

"Don't worry about it. Also, it might be worth trying to find out where the bandits' hideout is. ...Set."

"Gurrrr!"

Rei called out, and Set came running.

Leaping onto his back, Ray took hold of the reins. With a few strides and a flap of his wings, Set soared skyward.
