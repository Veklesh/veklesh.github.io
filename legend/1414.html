The second floor of the guild serves as a meeting room for adventurers.

There are several large conference rooms for multiple parties to use, and several smaller conference rooms for single parties or small groups.

Lenora led Rei and the others to one of the smaller meeting rooms.

"He seems like a very responsible person."

"Ah, ahaha. Yes, that's true. He has quite a strong personality in many ways, but I think his sense of responsibility is strong."

Hearing this, Marina tilted her head slightly, looking a bit puzzled at Lenora, who seemed somewhat hesitant.

Lenora's evasive tone seemed to catch Marina's attention.

"What's wrong?"

"Well, I think you'll understand once you meet them. Look, we're here now."

Just as Lenora finished speaking, Rei and the others were standing before the door.

"Excuse me, Mr. Bistor. Would you have a moment?"

"Sure. What is it?"

"...Huh?"

A voice from inside the conference room drew a questioning sound from Rei's lips.

The voice that reached them was unmistakably male... a deep, almost gruff voice.

Despite that, the tone of speech was distinctly feminine.

It was impossible not to be suspicious.

Marina, sitting next to Rei, couldn't quite hide her puzzled expression, though she didn't voice it.

"......"

Rei and Marina exchanged a silent glance.

Watching them, Lenora vaguely anticipated what was to come... and opened the door.

As Rei stepped inside, he momentarily imagined the room was some elegant garden where afternoon tea was being enjoyed.

Though, the one enjoying that afternoon tea wasn't some noble lady... but a man whose bulging muscles were obvious just from his appearance.

Despite his physique, he was inexplicably wearing women's clothing.

Of course, even if they were women's clothes, it wasn't something you'd find in a size suitable for someone like the muscle-bound figure Rei was looking at... well, he couldn't say for sure, but it was definitely extremely rare.

"Ugh..."

It was only natural, perhaps, that Rei let out a groan like that when she saw such a person.

Marina, standing beside Rei, didn't show outright surprise on her face, but even Rei could see her movements had frozen.

"Well now. Lenora-chan, what about those two over there? Or rather, judging by their appearance, could they be members of the Crimson Wings?"

Bistol asked this while raising her little finger for some reason, but her observation was correct.

(Marina, I get. She's famous, after all. But... how did she recognize me too?)

Rei was wearing his usual Dragon Robe hood.

Seeing him—someone whose face she shouldn't be able to confirm clearly—and still identifying him as a member of Crimson Wings... no, as Rei himself, was unexpected.

"Hehehe. The Crimson Wings are a famous party even within Gilm. Especially with the current expansion work, the fact that Rei-chan is playing a major role is pretty well-known, y'know."

"...-chan?"

He clearly hadn't expected to be addressed with the diminutive suffix. Rei stared intently at the person standing before him.

"Oh dear. If you keep staring at me like that, I might fall in love."

More like, your appearance is intense, you know.

Resisting the urge to say that, Rei opened her mouth.

"Fine. Call me whatever you like."

"Oh, then 'Darling' then... No, sorry. Stop giving me that look, it was a joke! I won't say it again."

Bistol hurriedly explained, looking at Marina instead of Rei.

Until now, her personality could easily be described as bold, but now sweat beaded on her forehead.

That was how much Marina's current gaze affected him.

But when Rei turned his gaze to Marina, all he saw was her usual smile.

Of course, she was undeniably beautiful.

Even so, the moment Bistor saw Marina's smile, a chill ran down his spine.

Was it a woman's intuition—or at least her instincts—or the small-animal-like instinct of one being stared down by an overwhelmingly powerful opponent... Either way, it was firmly etched into his mind that it was best to refrain from making that kind of joke to Rei.

Completely unaware of this exchange, Rei opened his mouth as if to say, "Anyway..."

"So, I need you to check the supplies I got back, okay?"

"Huh? Oh, right. That's true. I completely forgot about that. Well then, let's head to the supply yard right away. We're supposed to deliver it to Gilm tomorrow, but I got permission to leave it at the supply yard today."

Bistol said this, sounding as if she were trying to cover something up.

"Well then, I'll be taking my leave. Oh, Bistor-san. Once you've checked the materials, please sign here and hand it over to Rei-san."

"Got it. Leave that to me."

Bistol took the documents, smiling and winking. Seeing this from the side, Rei instinctively took a few steps back.

"Ah, ahahaha. Bistol, maybe you should focus more on your bodyguards from now on."

"Hmm... Well, the people who came this far with us have been working together for quite some time, so it's hard to suddenly switch gears... But considering the job, I suppose I can't really say that."

Stroking his stubble, Bistol murmured.

Yet, the sadness in his eyes came from the genuine grief he felt deep down over the loss of several guards.

Seeing Bistor like this, Rei reconsidered the figure before him—a being worthy of being called a monster in a different sense than the one Rei knew.

"Ah... about those bodies at the attack site? I had them burned over here."

"Huh? Really!?"

Though slightly taken aback by Bistol's energetic approach, Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah. It's near the road, and it's getting pretty hot now. If we'd left them there, it would've definitely led to all sorts of trouble."

"Thank you... Thank you so much... I really wanted to do something about it myself, but at the time, all I could do was run for cover at Gilm..."

Despite her appearance, Bistol was genuinely grateful to Rei.

Though at first glance, his appearance could easily be mistaken for that of a monster, his heart was deeply compassionate, and his nature was exceptionally gentle.

You could even call him sensible.

She wiped her tears with a handkerchief she took from her pocket. Though her eyes still held a trace of sadness, she spoke.

"Well then, shall we really be going soon? It's getting quite late, and Rei and the others must be busy from here on out, right?"

"That would be a huge help. I'm getting hungry too."

"Listen... when Lenora asked you to do that job, you were eating, and before leaving Gilm, you bought all sorts of snacks, right? And you seemed to be eating something on the way back too... How come you're still hungry?"

Marina shot him a look of utter exasperation, but Rei just shrugged his shoulders slightly in response.

Truthfully, his enormous appetite was one of Rei's most significant flaws.

Right now, Rei possessed enough wealth to be called filthy rich, so it wasn't a problem. But if Rei had been just an average adventurer, he might have starved to death.

"Well, whatever. They say kids who eat well grow up strong, right? Oh, right. If you don't mind, besides the reward for this job, I'll treat you to dinner tonight."

Naturally, it was Rei whose eyes lit up at the mention of Bystle treating her.

Of course, Rei had enough wealth stored in the Misty Ring to live off for a lifetime... no, enough to last several lifetimes over.

But still, being treated felt good, and there was the chance to enjoy cuisine Rei hadn't tried before.

Since Bistor had only arrived in Gilm today, the chances of her knowing a delicious restaurant unknown even to Rei were slim.

"Ah... um, Bistol-san. Maybe you should reconsider that..."

"Oh? Why's that? You went out of your way for me this time, Rei-chan. Plus, my friends were honored by your condolences... Isn't this much the least I can do?"

As she'd mentioned when meeting Rei, Bistor possessed information about the Crimson Wings, and by extension, about Rei.

But that was purely adventurer-related information; she had no details about his everyday habits.

Yes, he didn't know how much Rei usually ate.

Listening to Rei and Marina's conversation, he could guess she probably ate reasonably well, but he didn't know exactly how much.

Moreover, he didn't know that besides Rei, the Crimson Wings included Byune, who ate disproportionately for her size... and above all, he didn't know that Set, the griffin, was there.

Well, he probably knows about Set, of course, but even so, he likely wouldn't think he'd be included among those being treated.

"...Well, I suppose so. If Mr. Bistol says so, I won't argue. But, you know... If you're treating, I'd recommend the Crystal Pavilion. I hear it's cheap, generous portions, and delicious."

Lenora suggested a place that would at least minimize the damage to Bistor's wallet.

He probably didn't understand Lenora's intentions, but then again, Bistol didn't know many shops in Gilm.

He'd heard some stories from business associates, friends, and acquaintances who'd been to Gilm, but that was the extent of it.

That's precisely why Bistor, having received the latest shop information, happily thanked Lenora.

"Thanks, Lenora-chan. I'll get you some nice cosmetics next time. I use them myself, so the effects are guaranteed, y'know?"

"Uh, um... ah, ahahaha. Well, I'll look forward to it."

Even if she said it was the cosmetics Bistol used, what exactly they were... She was interested, but part of her also felt a little scared.

But Bistor seemed used to such reactions. She gave a small wink and then turned to Rei.

"Well, shall we go? We need to check the supplies first."

"Ah... yeah. Right, let's go."

Rei had plenty he wanted to say to Bistol, but he understood that bringing anything up now would just waste more time.

In the end, he said no more, and led Marina out of the room.

"I'll leave this to you."

After leaving the guild, Bistor, Rei, Marina, and Set headed for the supply depot where Bistor was originally supposed to deliver the materials.

The situation had likely already been explained through the guild. Guards were stationed there.

Normally, guarding supplies was a job left to adventurers, but given the recent bandit attack, they were taking extra precautions.

To be precise, they weren't thieves but some private militia group, though the details of that situation hadn't been made public yet.

The plan was to extract information from the prisoners Rei and the others had captured, then have the higher-ups, including Daskar, make a decision.

Though, according to what Rei heard from Marina, depending on who orchestrated this incident, it might never be made public at all.

If it had been a country other than the Kingdom of Mireana, the matter wouldn't be so complicated.

They could simply issue a statement condemning the offending nation and demand they hand over those responsible for orchestrating this incident.

Whether they would comply quietly was another matter entirely, but at least it would be possible to publicly condemn them.

However, if the perpetrators were a hostile faction within the Kingdom of Mireana itself, openly condemning them becomes difficult.

Of course, Daskar wouldn't simply let it go; they would retaliate in some way.

If Daskar does nothing and lets it slide, it will only reinforce the perception that they won't retaliate no matter what happens, encouraging more adversaries to encroach.

After all, the frontier of Gilm is a place others would kill to get their hands on.

To prevent such imitations, it was necessary to retaliate firmly, even if not publicly.

(Well, that's none of my concern, though.)

Thinking this, Rei, urged by Bistol, retrieved the stored supplies from within the Misty Ring.

...About thirty percent of those supplies were items even Bistor didn't recognize, proving that others besides Bistor and his men had been attacked by the private soldiers.
