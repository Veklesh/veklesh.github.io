After finishing his business meeting with Azimov and Pamidor, Rei stepped outside to find the scene he had expected unfolding before him.

People of all ages—from small children to adults and the elderly—were lying on the ground around Set.

Despite it being Azimov's residence, a place people usually avoided, crowds had gathered as if that mattered not a whit.

"This was definitely unexpected,"

Amidov's surprised murmur, unaware of Set's existence, could be heard. Pamidor and Rei, however, wore smiles, seemingly unfazed.

For these two, people gathering where Set was present wasn't particularly unusual.

Especially since Rei and Set had been away from Gilm for a while, many people wanted to play with Set.

Things had finally calmed down a bit recently, but it wasn't completely settled yet. It was only natural that people would gather where Set was, drawn to him.

"Well, it's telling you to pay a little more attention to what's going on outside. Besides, if you'd understood about Set sooner, you might have gotten his materials a bit earlier, right?"

"...Hmph."

As the two exchanged words, Rei started to step forward... then turned back and spoke again.

"For now, I'll leave the matter of the demon sword to you. Let me know once you have a rough plan. I'll basically be at the Dusk Wheat inn. As for the dungeon's core, you can use it if you can... but don't use it for anything other than the demon sword."

That last remark was directed not at Pamidor, but at Azimov.

He understood Azimov's fervor for alchemy well, but precisely because of that, he suspected Azimov might do anything to satisfy his own thirst for knowledge.

Indeed, it seemed Rei had hit the nail on the head. Ajimov gasped for a moment at the words, then reluctantly nodded in response.

"Yeah, I get it. I'll let you know when I have something concrete. That's fine, right?"

"Do that. ...And Pamidor too, please."

"Leave it to me. I'll make sure to keep a close eye on him so he doesn't do anything stupid."

Pamidor immediately understood Rei's meaning.

Azimov shot Pamidor a slightly displeased look.

If he didn't emphasize it here, he'd probably mess something up.

While observing Azimov's demeanor, Rei also thought that it was precisely because he was this kind of man that he was such a highly skilled alchemist.

"Alright then, I'll leave it to you."

Leaving only those final words, Rei made his way toward the crowd gathered around Set.

"Alright, alright, move aside. Set's got to move on now, so let's save the fun for another time."

At Rei's words, those present reluctantly made way.

Some probably still wanted to play with Set.

But they understood that interfering with Rei would also sadden Set, so they obediently made way.

Some might prioritize their own feelings above all else, but fortunately, none of those gathered here seemed to be that type.

"Grrr!"

"Let's play again!" Set barked cheerfully, standing up and rubbing his face against Rei's as he approached.

Rei stroked Set back before leaving the spot.

Watching Set like that, the people who saw them off wore expressions of regret, yet somehow also seemed satisfied.

"Wow... Even against a Rank A monster, to pull off something like that. Unbelievable."

Azimov, who had been watching the scene from a short distance away from the group that had seen Set off, murmured deeply.

By Azimov's standards, the idea that someone could interact so familiarly with a high-rank monster like a Griffin was an utterly unbelievable sight.

Unusually, this was true even by ordinary standards.

However, while it might have been correct by ordinary standards, it simply didn't fit the norms of Gilm.

"The fact that you've never ventured outside is fatal in more ways than one. Even I, who haven't been in Gilm that long, know this. For you, who've lived here since before, to be unaware... that's a bit problematic, isn't it?"

"...I can't deny that's true."

His dissatisfied reply likely stemmed from the thought that if he'd known about Set earlier, he could have obtained various griffon materials.

Azimov kept his displeased expression, but eventually spoke.

"Still... thanks to Pamidor, I got to meet Rei and Set. That was pure luck for me. Who knows? I might have gone years without even realizing a Griffin existed right under my nose."

"Oh oh, show your gratitude to your heart's content. ...Well, jokes aside. Since Rei entrusted me with the job, anything less than top-notch won't satisfy my pride as a craftsman."

"Yeah, you're right. As an alchemist, I've got the ultimate material—a dungeon core. And if I can get griffin materials too? Other alchemists would grind their teeth with jealousy if they heard about it."

"...Thinking about it, it's strange, isn't it? There are plenty of other alchemists in this Gilm besides you. Why haven't any of them made contact with Rei until now?"

"I dunno. If you ask me, I can't fathom why they didn't reach out before, especially with such prime materials available."

Azimov dismissed it with a single sentence, but naturally, some alchemists within the guild had tried to contact Rei.

But... Rei was always swamped with commissions, so he wasn't around the Gilm much.

And while they couldn't reach Rei, the number of people favoring Set grew, creating an atmosphere where it became difficult to casually ask for materials. Furthermore, it led to a major uproar when the Azoth Trading Company, wanting Set, clashed with Rei.

What proved fatal was that word spread among Gilm's alchemists that alchemists had cooperated with the Azoth Trading Company. This made it utterly impossible for alchemists to approach Rei.

As time passed, an atmosphere developed where alchemists felt they should refrain from contacting Rei.

This was likely significantly influenced by Rei's merciless nature towards those who opposed him.

Now, among Gilm's alchemists, initiating contact with Rei is considered taboo—unless Rei makes the first move.

It was almost ironic that Azimov, who knew nothing of these circumstances, was the first Gilm alchemist to make contact with Rei.

He himself was completely unaware of it, but perhaps that was for the best.

Of course, this information would undoubtedly spread among Gilm's alchemists before long. In that sense, it might be unfortunate that he'd be questioned by other alchemists about the circumstances.

As soon as he parted ways with Set near the entrance and stepped into the guild, Lenora spotted him and smiled.

Simultaneously, the knight beside Lenora noticed her gaze shift and turned to look at Rei, a smile spreading across his face.

Why is a knight at the guild? Rei wondered, but before he could voice it, the knight dashed over to him.

"Rei-sama, this is perfect timing. I was looking for you, but I couldn't find you anywhere and was getting lost."

Rei tilted his head at the words of the knight, who appeared to be in his thirties.

It wasn't that he was a complete stranger.

He was one of the knights serving Daskar, and had accompanied them as an escort when they headed for the Bestir Empire, so Rei recognized him.

Still, he wondered why such a knight would be searching for him.

(Because I cleared the dungeon? No, but Ranga should have sent a report on that matter, and he himself returned here today to report directly...)

So he couldn't have business with me, right?

That's what Rei thought, but his assumption was far too naive.

Even if it was a newly opened dungeon, clearing it in just a few hours would naturally make Daskar want to hear the details.

Even if he'd already informed the guild and they'd relayed the report, that wouldn't change anything.

"Lord Daskar wishes to speak with you regarding the dungeon matter."

The knight who delivered the message seemed somewhat apologetic.

He was in his mid-thirties, significantly older than Rei.

His aura was imposing, befitting a knight.

Yet he addressed Rei with polite language, likely because he recognized Rei's true strength.

Of course, things would be different if Rei were someone who flaunted his abilities, but at the very least, Rei had never acted that way toward Daskar.

On the contrary, he had a proven track record of completing various requests for Gilm, the Marquisate of Larks, and above all, for Daskar.

Given that, it was only natural for the knight to treat Rei with respect.

Unaware of the respect directed his way, Rei tilted his head at the knight's words.

...A certain feline beastman cheered inwardly at that gesture, but managed to hide it successfully.

"Regarding the dungeon, hasn't Ranga already submitted a detailed report?"

"Yes. Lord Ranga's report has been received as well. However, I still wish to hear directly from the person involved."

Rei paused again, considering the knight's words.

Rei had come to the guild wanting to know how far things had progressed regarding Gamelion, but the conversation had veered completely off course.

But as Rei hesitated, Lenora, who had been speaking with the knight, interjected.

"Rei, the guild would also prefer to receive a direct report from Daskar-sama..."

"Hmm... Yeah, I understand. But before that, can I ask what happened with the Gamelion matter?"

Realizing that question revealed why Rei had come to the guild, Lenora bowed apologetically.

"I'm sorry. We're gathering people to dismantle Gamelion, but it's still in progress since it was only yesterday..."

"I see."

"It should have progressed further by now, but... well..."

Rei shook his head, signaling he didn't mind, as Lenora hesitated, struggling to find the right words.

"It's fine. I'm not in such a rush to eat all of Gamelion's meat right away. Besides, I knew full well that fewer adventurers are active this time of year when I asked."

"Well, that's part of it, but..."

"Huh? Is that not it?"

"Yes, well... Actually..."

"Well, yesterday, Rei mentioned something about Gamelions possibly being properly huntable from now on, right? A lot of people who heard that have headed over there. I mean, think about it—it's more profitable to take down a Gamelion themselves than to just dismantle one, right? Well, some couldn't go because of rank restrictions, so those folks are helping with the dismantling."

The one who announced this was Kenny, who had been waiting beside Lenora to jump into the conversation.

"Hey, Kenny!"

"What? There's nothing to hide, right? Besides, Rei doesn't seem to mind either."

Lenora let out a small sigh as Kenny turned his gaze toward Rei.

"That might be true, but... even so, we took it on, and now you're just casually saying it's not done yet? What's up with that?"

"Oh my, do you really think Rei-kun would care about such a small thing? Small is someone's chest... No, never mind. Why don't you lower that clenched fist?"

Kenny tried to tease her as usual, but sensing a definite threat in Lenora's clenched fist, he trailed off.

"Oh? Really? Shouldn't you just say it all then? Come on, Kenny? What exactly is wrong with me?"

He was clearly annoyed at being teased so much. Only the corners of his mouth curved into a smile, his eyes showing no warmth at all.

Kenny, meeting Lenora's gaze in that state, seemed to sense the clear threat. He forced a flustered smile and opened his mouth.

"Ah, ahahaha. See? I didn't say anything, did I? Anyway, Lenora, you should put that fist down. Right?"

While addressing Lenora, Kenny shifted his gaze toward the knight and Rei...

"...Then, um, Rei. Would you come with me?"

"Yeah. Right. That's probably best. Let's get going."

Rei nodded simply at the knight's words, delivered with a gentle averted gaze.

With their mutual understanding settled, they turned on their heels.

"Ah, wait, Rei!? Don't abandon me! ...Ouch!"

Tears welling up from the blow of the fist, Kenny turned his gaze toward Lenora.

Lenora wiped the smile off her face, sighed, and spoke.

"Now, stop saying such foolish things. Kenny, you should go dismantle the Gamelion too."

"Huh? Wait, why me?"

"...Go?"

"Yes, understood!"

As if pushed by an inexplicable force, Kenny's cry echoed through the guild.
