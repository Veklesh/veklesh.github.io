"So, that wraps up the third set of treasures, then."

“Rei, shouldn’t we head to Gilm soon? The main gate stays open late at night, but it’s better to get there a little early if possible. If we wait any longer, it’ll definitely get crowded.”

Marina spoke to Rei, who had just stored the treasure the bandits had hoarded in the Misty Ring.

The slight exasperation in Marina’s eyes was likely because they’d hunted too many bandits.

It had been a few hours since they arrived in Sublusta… or rather, since they first spotted the bandits operating near Sublusta.

In just those few hours, he’d wiped out three entire bandit groups, so it was no wonder Marina was looking at him that way.

...Of course, wiping out three bandit groups means taking the lives of the bandits who belonged to them, but there’s no reason to blame her for that.

Elena, Marina, Vihera, and Byune.

All four of them have had their fair share of experience, and they don’t possess the kindness—no, the naivety—to think that, just because they’re bandits, they shouldn’t take lives.

Depending on the situation, taking them to Sublusta as slaves might have brought in more money, but since the goal this time was to instill fear in the bandits in this area, they did not do that.

They killed the bandits, but spared a few to extract information; these survivors will be released to spread the word that the bandit gang has been wiped out.

That way, even if it’s quite some time from now, I’ll ensure the safety of Sura and the others when they pass through this area.

“Hmm, that’s right. It’s getting late, and considering all that, I guess this is as far as we can go.”

It had been a few hours since they decided to hunt down the bandits.

Hearing that three bandit groups had been wiped out in just that short time, the bandits in this area should have been too afraid to make any careless moves.

That said, the actual time spent fighting during those few hours didn’t even add up to twenty minutes for all three groups combined.

Most of the time was spent not on interrogation... but on getting them to confess where they kept their loot, and in some cases, guiding us there, so we could stow the treasure in the Misty Ring.

(If there had been any prisoners, it would have taken even longer, but fortunately, there weren’t any.)

Rei felt relieved about that, but it wasn’t a matter of luck or anything; it was simply because, for the bandits in this area, selling their captives into slavery carried too great a risk.

If they sold someone as a slave, there was a chance that, if things went wrong, the authorities could trace the trail back to them.

If the profits from attacking merchants were meager, they might have had to resort to such methods.

But since attacking a merchant alone yields a substantial profit, they likely judged there was no need to unnecessarily increase the risk of getting caught.

Of course, even in such cases, there are times when people do get captured.

After all, young women are essential in a different sense.

Fortunately, there were no such individuals among the bandits Rei hunted down this time.

If there had been any, that would have been a real headache in terms of what to do next.

“So, shall we head to Gilm?”

Nodding at Elena’s words, Rei left the forest, boarded the Set Cage, and set off for Gilm.

“Oh, Rei. It’s been a while.”

The person in charge of Rei at Gilm’s main gate was a man with a stern face… normally, it would have been Ranga, who led the security force.

“Same here—I haven’t seen you in ages… What have you been up to?”

He was someone with whom Rei had a connection, but he hadn’t seen him at all recently.

...To begin with, the fact that Rei had been in the Kingdom of Reburina was one reason they hadn’t met, but it was true that they had hardly seen each other even before the expansion project began.

“I was away from Gilm for a bit on business.”

“Come to think of it, something similar happened before.”

As Rei spoke, recalling how Ranga had been dispatched as a Daskar envoy to handle an issue involving a certain nobleman Rei was involved with, Ranga nodded with a smile on his stern face.

“Come to think of it, that did happen.”

To anyone unaware of the situation, Ranga would certainly look like a bandit.

In fact, a man who appeared to be a merchant, standing a short distance away from Rei, was trembling at the sight of Ranga talking to her.

Another merchant who knows Ranga explains to the first one that, despite his appearance, he isn’t actually that scary—as long as you haven’t done anything wrong.

“Whoa, I don’t want to keep you waiting here. The paperwork’s all done, so you can go in now.”

Perhaps noticing the stares from those around him, Ranga hurriedly says this to Rei.

Rei gives Ranga a brief nod in return, takes the familiar’s necklace, and enters Gilm alongside Elena and the others.

“I don’t know where Ranga went, but now that he’s back, does that mean some big commotion is about to happen…?”

“I wonder. Maybe he was just out on a mission for Daskar. Actually, as a guard, Ranga is quite capable. It wouldn’t be surprising if someone asked him to provide backup somewhere else.”

Hearing Marina’s words, Rei nodded in understanding.

Her words were certainly not a lie; it was not uncommon for Ranga to be dispatched when a noble belonging to the Neutral Faction faced some kind of problem—particularly one related to a crime.

Ranga himself enjoys handling the paperwork for those coming and going from Gilm, but this is a case where his personality and his abilities don’t necessarily align.

“I see. Ranga has it rough, too. …So, what should we do now? Do you still think we should go greet Lord Daskar first?”

“That shouldn’t be necessary. Last time, I had to meet Daskar to explain the situation with the Kingdom of Reburina, but this time there’s nothing in particular to explain, right?”

Hearing Marina’s words, Rei pauses to think, “Is that so?”

But if asked whether there was actually anything to report, there wasn’t really anything.

Just how much work had piled up during the few days Rei and the others were away? That would become clear once they actually went to the site.

When they next joined up with Sura and the others, they would need to consult with Daskar to some extent regarding the supplies to bring and the number of guards to take along…

“Leave the details of the negotiations to me.”

Hearing Marina’s words, Rei decides to take her up on the offer.

In truth, Rei is by no means skilled at negotiations.

He excels at the kind of negotiation he used with the bandits—one that relies heavily on force and borders on intimidation—but he certainly couldn’t pull that kind of stunt with the Daskar.

In that sense, it was indeed best to leave the negotiations entirely to Marina.

...Though for Daskar, who knows about Marina’s past—which could be called a “dark history” in many ways—the fact that Marina is the one negotiating would surely give him pause.

If the negotiations were to be detrimental to Gilm, Daskar would never back down, even if his own embarrassing past were brought up.

But conversely, that means if the negotiations aren’t to Gilm’s disadvantage—or better yet, if they’re to his advantage—they’ll almost certainly proceed at Marina’s pace.

Naturally, Rei was well aware of this, and it was with that in mind that she asked Marina.

“Well then, I’ll go talk to Daskar right away. …What about the Set Cage?”

“We don’t really need any adjustments or anything like that, do we? Last time, since it was our first time using it, we needed to work on those details. But actually, there weren’t any particular issues.”

The Set Cage, which had been adjusted and modified last time, has been working without any issues.

Given that, Rei’s opinion was that there was no need to go out of our way to bring it this time.

Perhaps Marina had asked Rei just to be on the safe side, because she didn’t delve any deeper into the matter and simply turned her gaze toward him.

And as for what Rei did when she looked at him...

“For now, shall we grab something to eat?”

At Rei’s suggestion, everyone—especially Byune—agreed.

In fact, since they’d been busy hunting bandits today, they’d actually been moving around more than when they were escorting Sura and the others.

It was only natural that they’d be hungrier than usual.

(Cooking, huh. I suppose it’s about time I restocked the ingredients as well.)

The cost of the meals provided to Sura and the others is, naturally, treated as a necessary expense.

But just because it’s a necessary expense doesn’t mean they can get food immediately just because they have the money.

If it were just a meal or two, that would be one thing, but what Rei wants is a huge pot of stew and thousands of loaves of bread.

There were nearly a thousand Suuras, and while stew and other dishes might be manageable, one loaf of bread would definitely not be enough.

Moreover, since they’ve been exerting themselves physically during combat training lately, it’s only natural that they’d be even hungrier.

Although Rei had stockpiled various dishes up until now, there’s no doubt that his supplies have dwindled significantly due to this recent situation.

Besides, the food stored in Rei’s Misty Ring isn’t just from any restaurant.

After all, it’s only food from restaurants that Rei personally found delicious, so the quantity is inevitably limited.

“Full Belly Inn… At this time of day, it’s probably pretty crowded.”

Cheap, delicious, and generous portions—Full Belly Inn is the ultimate restaurant for adventurers.

But, naturally, such a place is popular and always crowded.

With the expansion work bringing in more people, it was easy to predict just how crowded the place would be.

If Rei and the others showed up to eat right now, it was certain to cause quite a commotion.

The woodcutter would be linked to Rei, the craftsman to Marina, and those with something to hide to Vihera... and so on.

And considering that Elena—undoubtedly the most famous of them all—was there as well, such behavior would be tantamount to throwing themselves into the thick of the commotion.

“In that case, why don’t we just eat at my place? There won’t be any unnecessary interruptions there, and Set can relax too.”

Marina’s gaze fell upon Set, who had appeared out of nowhere and was playing with the children.

Given the current situation, it was certain that taking Set into a nearby restaurant would cause a commotion.

“You’re right. I agree. …If Johanna shows up, things are bound to get noisy again.”

Although Milein was away in Gilm serving as an escort for the special envoy from the Kingdom of Reburina, Johanna was still there. The last time they returned to Gilm, she had sensed Set’s presence out of nowhere and appeared.

To prevent that from happening again, going to Marina’s house in the Noble District now was undoubtedly the best choice for everyone.

“Set, let’s get going!”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set growled and moved away from the children he had been playing with.

The children, too, have known Set for quite some time.

Although Set plays with them, they seem to understand that Rei’s words take priority, so they quietly step aside.

Occasionally, there are children—and sometimes even adults—who throw a tantrum, insisting, “I still want to play with Set!” But today, there was no one like that, and Set quietly made his way over to Rei and the others.

“It’s fine for you guys to go into my house, so please head home first. I’ll wrap up the negotiations with Daskar first.”

“Is that okay?”

“Yes. Rei and the others have come to my house countless times before, haven’t they? I’ve set it up so that I deal with trespassers using spirit magic, but with Rei and the others, there shouldn’t be any problem at all.”

Rei nods in response to Marina’s matter-of-fact statement.

He was slightly intrigued by the idea of using spirit magic for security, but realizing it would be problematic to ask about such a thing in a public place, he said nothing on the matter and asked something else instead.

“Got it. Then I’ll go ahead and do that. So, what do you want for dinner tonight? If there’s anything you’d like, I can prepare it here.”

“Hmm, I guess pizza. The pizza Rei makes is delicious.”

“……If that’s fine with you, it’ll make things easier for me. Are you sure?”

Rei’s pizza is made in a magical pizza oven.

Since the pizza oven is a magic item, heating it up is easy, and pizza itself isn’t exactly a labor-intensive dish.

“Since you’ve gone to the trouble of coming back to Gilm, wouldn’t you prefer something even more delicious?” Rei asked. Marina flashed a smile radiating an intense feminine allure and replied.

“I want to eat something you’ve cooked yourself, Rei. …Is that okay?”

With that, tonight’s dinner was decided.
