The Axe of the Thunder God swung down by Elk was swiftly dodged by the Silver Lion moving sideways.

The Axe of the Thunder God slammed into the floor, shattering it and sending fragments flying while simultaneously unleashing a bolt of lightning.

Yet the Silver Lion crawled across the floor, seemingly unconcerned by the lightning flowing toward it. Baring its sharp fangs, it lunged at Elk.

For the Silver Lion, the most troublesome opponents were Elk and the griffin, Set, who wielded the Axe of the Thunder God capable of dealing effective damage.

Therefore, it was only natural that it would first try to take down Elk, one of the troublesome opponents.

Elk kicked off the floor to evade the Silver Lion's attack while pulling the Axe of the Thunder God back to his side.

Even so, he couldn't completely evade the fang strike. A slash wound, as if cut by a blade, appeared on his cheek, and a single drop of blood flowed.

Even though he dodged the fang strike, he was still within the Silver Lion's attack range.

Should he land a blow with the Axe of the Thunder God, or buy some distance? Elk's hesitation lasted less than a second.

But that brief moment was all the Silver Lion needed to act.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR

A roar that echoed throughout the area.

Hearing it directly would rupture eardrums, while shockwave blades sliced skin. Elk, hit at point-blank range by this signature Silver Lion attack, was sent flying as if punched by the shockwave.

Like Rei earlier, he scraped his feet along the floor but immediately readied the Axe of the Thunder God to confront the Silver Lion...

"GRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

With that roar, Set, who had been flying in the sky, brought his front paw down in a strike toward the ground.

Level 5 skill: Power Crush.

For the Silver Lion, this strike might deal even more lethal damage than the Axe of the Thunder God.

Though not quite reaching the hundred-meter altitude of the sky, it still came from a considerable height. Moreover, it was a strike enhanced by the power of the Magic Item, the Bracelet of Might, which boosted his arm strength.

Realizing this, the Silver Lion halted its pursuit toward the Elk... attempting to dodge the forelegs descending from above.

"GRAH!?"

But the silver lion paused its movement only for an instant because water, which had crept up to its feet unnoticed, had just wrapped around its legs.

Though the Silver Lion possessed strong resistance to magic, this did not mean it could nullify spells entirely.

If the water touched the silver lion's fur and then acted like a water snake... or a rope, binding its movements, it could be effective.

Though it was a water-based binding spell created by Marina's spirit magic, it only managed to halt the silver lion's movement for an instant.

Set was still several meters above, and for a Silver Lion, evading or intercepting him was hardly difficult.

Even among lion-like monsters, there was a difference between the pure lion-type Silver Lion and the Griffin, whose upper body was that of an eagle.

As if to demonstrate that difference, the Silver Lion bared its fangs, preparing to meet Set head-on... but then, growling irritably, it leapt away from the spot.

Then, several arrows of wind pierced the spot where the Silver Lion's body had been.

It was Min's magic.

Though the Silver Lion possessed strong resistance to magic, Min likely judged that if it took this spell while attempting to intercept Set's attack, it would lose its balance midair.

That was not an incorrect judgment.

The spot the Silver Lion had dodged to... where the wind arrows had struck... the next instant, Set's front paw came crashing down, shattering the floor over a wide area.

"Power Slash!"

Rei charged toward the spot where the Silver Lion had temporarily retreated.

Deploying the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, his physical abilities enhanced, he unleashed the Death Scythe skill... specifically not one that sharpened the blade, but Power Slash, which increased the sheer power of the strike itself.

"GRRRR!"

The silver lion must have sensed danger from Rei's strike.

Instead of absorbing Rei's attack with its body like before, it countered with a strike from its front paws.

The silver lion's forepaw and the Death Scythe collided midair.

The result of that collision was Rei being blown away.

This wasn't because the Power Slash lacked power; it was simply the difference in their respective weights... their mass.

On one side, Rei, whose physique could aptly be described as petite. On the other, the Silver Lion, whose total length, including its tail, reached about six meters.

What would happen when those two collided was self-evident.

Of course, this outcome wouldn't occur against just any opponent.

The Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour possessed capabilities that justified its name.

But... even so, when the opponent was a Rank S monster like the Silver Lion, it was a different story.

"Guh!"

Blown away and landing on the floor once more, Rei let out a groan of pain, likely from the dull ache in his side where he'd first been struck.

Yet he immediately swallowed that cry, driven more by the need to hide his weakness from the Silver Lion.

"Raaah!"

He channeled magic into the Twilight Spear, maximizing the effect of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, and hurled it with all his might.

The Twilight Spear, released at a speed barely perceptible to the human eye as it sliced through the air, struck the Silver Lion's torso. The beast had been attempting to dodge Elk's Axe of the Thunder God, swung to halt its pursuit of Rei.

Yes. It didn't pierce it, nor did it even penetrate it... it merely collided.

For most monsters, being struck by the Twilight Spear, imbued with magic while wearing the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, would likely result in complete annihilation, leaving no trace behind.

Yet despite taking such a devastating blow, the Silver Lion suffered no wounds that could even remotely be called fatal.

But... fortunately, even if it couldn't wound it, the Twilight Spear's strike undoubtedly traveled at high speed and succeeded in transmitting the full impact of its collision with the Silver Lion's body through its frame.

The Silver Lion's movement halted for an instant due to the Twilight Spear's impact.

It was then struck by Elk's Axe of the Thunder God.

The surrounding air echoed with the same high-pitched metallic clang as when Rei first swung the Death Scythe.

Up to that point, it was the same as with Rei. The difference was that the Silver Lion let out a cry of pain.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR

Even so, the blow from the Axe of the Thunder God apparently didn't inflict what could be called a fatal wound, and it swiftly leapt away from the spot.

"Gwaah!"

Leaving Elk's agonized scream behind.

The tail swung in tandem with its leap easily blew Elk away, despite his body being densely packed with muscle unlike Rei's.

Moreover, the tail's strike had left a gruesome gash in Elk's leather armor.

Not a slash or a blow... but a strike that seemed to shave it away.

The fur growing on the Silver Lion's tail had literally shaved it off.

「Elk！？」

"I'm fine!"

Elk shouted back at his wife's worried voice, driven by a stubborn refusal to be beaten. He stepped forward again.

Set, who had soared back into the sky after delivering that earlier blow, also waited for an opening to strike the Silver Lion.

And Rei, holding the Twilight Spear close at hand and poised with the Death Scythe, also stepped forward.

Against the two nicknamed warriors stood Set, a rank S-equivalent opponent.

From behind, Elena whipped her chained blades like a whip to keep the enemy at bay, Marina alternated between her bow and spirit magic, and Min unleashed spells.

Attacks that would surely kill any ordinary monster in the vicinity relentlessly assaulted the Silver Lion.

But... even after taking such attacks, it doesn't die. In fact, it calmly counterattacks. That's precisely why it's an S-rank monster... and not just S-rank equivalent like Set, but a clearly defined S-rank monster.

"Huh!?"

The instant Rei saw the Silver Lion tense its body, a chill ran down his spine.

It wasn't based on any reason; it was purely an instinctive sense.

It wasn't just Rei; Set, Elk, and the others... even Elena and the others farther away felt it too.

"Back off! Magic Shield!"

Shouting on the spur of the moment, Rei activated Death Scythe's skill, Magic Shield.

Though it could only be used once, the skill, which blocked any attack, performed its role flawlessly. ...However, Magic Shield could only block an attack once; it was weak against attacks launched in succession.

Yes, the fur sprouting from the Silver Lion's body—a body that blocked both attacks and magic like metal—was vulnerable to attacks unleashed in rapid succession around it.

"GAAAAAAAAAAH!"

The first silver lion hair... no, the first hair needle of that omnidirectional attack could be blocked by the Magic Shield.

But the next instant, the Magic Shield vanished, and one hair needle after another assaulted Rei.

Almost instinctively, he used the Dragon Robe as a shield, but even that couldn't protect his entire body.

He brought his arm, wrapped in the Dragon Robe, up in front of his face, which wasn't covered by the hood, but even that couldn't completely block the attack.

It felt less like pain and more like intense heat.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, he had leaped backward, so he didn't suffer any fatal wounds.

But the silver needles slipped through the gap in his hastily extended arm, mercilessly tearing multiple wounds across Rei's face.

Still, thanks to extending his arm, there was no fatal damage.

...Which, conversely, meant he sustained numerous injuries that weren't fatal.

It was only because he wore the Dragon Robe, a powerful magic item, that Rei got off with that much.

However... unlike Rei, Set and Elk, who took the Silver Lion's attack at close range, did not escape with such minor injuries.

Elk, in particular, had suffered the worst.

Though Set was lower-ranked than the Silver Lion, he was a Griffin with inherently robust physical strength and agility.

Unlike Rei and Elk, he had no armor, and barbed feathers pierced his body, but the wounds, while painful, did not incapacitate him.

In contrast to Set, Elk wore leather armor, but it was gear prioritizing mobility.

Furthermore, as expected, the leather armor allowed free movement at the joints.

Like Rei, Elk also shielded his face, thrusting the Axe of the Thunder God forward as a makeshift shield.

However... even leather armor worn by a high-rank adventurer like Elk didn't possess the formidable defensive power of Rei's Dragon Robe.

Silver Lion's barbed needles pierced Elk's body one after another.

Those that struck the Axe of the Thunder God were deflected off, but the shield wasn't large enough to cover his entire body.

The fact that none proved fatal was likely because, while needles are highly effective at inflicting pain, their pure lethality is surprisingly weak.

Of course, if one were to pierce the head, instant death would be unavoidable.

Even if they were embedded all over the body like they were in Elk now, unless they pierced vital organs, it wouldn't threaten his life.

...That said, long hair needles... needles large enough to be used as weapons, piercing his entire body... the pain must be excruciating.

And as long as those hair needles remain embedded, they naturally hinder Elk's movement.

Even when trying to move, the needles would hit his body and get in the way.

"Guhhhhh!"

Elk wasn't just taking a beating one-sidedly.

Despite having hair needles piercing his entire body, he still forcefully swings the Axe of the Thunder God down toward the Silver Lion.

"GRAAAH!"

Did the Silver Lion not expect Elk to attack him with the momentum from his own strike? It took the full force of Elk's Axe of the Thunder God and was sent flying.

This was likely the first proper damage Elk had inflicted on the Silver Lion since the battle began.

But Elk was far from at full strength, his body covered in barbed needles.

Still, the fact that a Silver Lion over six meters long was blown away spoke volumes about the sheer power of Elk's strike.

"Dammit! How is it that I knocked out its fur and it hasn't lost any health? ...Elk, get out of the room and recover! In your current state, you'll only slow us down!"

Rei shouted.

Since Elk was the one who landed a solid hit on the Silver Lion, his words could be seen as arrogance.

But the truth was, Elk was bleeding from every orifice. While he might hold out for a few minutes, enduring a prolonged battle? The answer was no.

Elk understood that about his own body, too.

He understood that stubbornness here would only slow everyone down... meaning it would hinder them from obtaining the Silver Lion's heart. He shouted.

"Got it! Then I'm counting on you guys for a little while! Don't get taken down before I get back!"

"Hah! By the time you get back, we'll have already taken down the Silver Lion! If you don't like that, you better get back here fast!"

Rei shouted back... Elk, wearing a mischievous grin like a prankster, left the room.
