The summer heat subsides considerably once night falls.

Of course, it doesn’t get suddenly cold just because it’s nighttime, but there’s no doubt that it’s become much more comfortable compared to the nights just a short while ago.

If you listen closely, you’ll notice that the sounds of insects around you have changed somewhat.

Listening to those sounds of insects signaling the approaching autumn, Rei and the others were having dinner in the garden of Marina’s house.

“Come to think of it, it feels like it’s been quite a while since we last ate here.”

“Really? I don’t think it’s been that long. I mean, wasn’t it before we went to the Kingdom of Reburina?”

Conversation flowed as they ate stew packed with meat and vegetables and reached for slices of freshly baked pizza.

There were also several other dishes on the table, such as skewers, stews, and grilled fish.

It might be a stretch to call it a lavish meal, but there was no doubt that the food was delicious.

“Hmm, this fish pizza is delicious. Did you catch the fish before returning to Gilm?”

As she savors the simple pizza—made with river fish, cheese, and a few summer vegetables—Elena lets out a sigh of admiration.

When it comes to delicious food, Elena, Marina, and Vihera have had countless opportunities to taste dishes of the sort served at noble parties.

But for these three, the home-cooked meal Rei made using the magic pizza oven tasted better than any feast they’d ever had.

“Ah, that’s because we have plenty of river fish. I used that. But since it’s just river fish and vegetables, the flavor inevitably ends up being on the lighter side. I’d like to try making it a bit more flavorful, though.”

“Is that so? I’m perfectly satisfied with this light flavor, though.”

“Hmm, I think I agree with Rei—I’d prefer a slightly stronger flavor.”

In response to Elena’s words, Vihera voiced her own preference.

“Oh, I think it’s delicious just the way it is.”

“Hmm…”

Marina agreed with Elena, while Byune agreed with Vihera.

Opinions were split right down the middle, but Rei didn’t let it bother her as she baked a new pizza.

Well, considering that pizza is topped with oak meat simmered in a sweet-and-salty sauce, he probably is paying a little attention after all.

“So, how did your day at work go? We’ve managed to haul all the trees from Treant Forest over here, at least.”

“For Rei, that kind of work isn’t a burden at all, is it?”

Rei’s Misty Ring is exceptionally good at that kind of work.

No matter how much stuff—even things that would require a tremendous amount of effort for a person to carry—she can easily stow it all away in her Misty Ring.

“Yeah, that’s true. Anyway, the biggest problem on my end is taken care of. It’s just… it looks like quite a few monsters have made their way into the Treant Forest.”

“Surely they’re keeping an eye on that area, right?”

Upon hearing Vihera’s words, Marina put the pizza she was eating back on her plate before speaking.

“Look. No matter what, there’s no way we can completely seal off Treant Forest, right? No matter how hard we try to block it off, the monsters slip through the cracks and make their way into the forest.”

“...Why go to such lengths just to get into Treant Forest?”

As far as Vihera knew, there were hardly any animals in Treant Forest.

There seemed to be a few birds flying in the sky, but even so, their numbers were small.

Vihera’s question was whether they’d actually have trouble finding food if they settled in a place like that.

“There are people there, and other monsters too, right? They won’t have any trouble finding food.”

Even as she said that, Marina likely didn’t find the idea of monsters inhabiting the Treant Forest all that amusing.

As a former guild master, that reaction wasn’t surprising.

“So, does that mean Rei is going to go around exterminating the monsters in Treant Forest starting tomorrow?”

Elena asked Rei, looking slightly envious.

Although Elena was staying at Marina’s house with Ara, as a representative sent by the Noble Faction, she had to hold meetings with various people, including Daskar.

Of course, she wouldn’t do anything to let her meeting partners sense that, but even so, Elena probably didn’t particularly enjoy those kinds of meetings.

That was precisely why she envied Rei, who could be out and about.

“I think that’s probably how it’ll go. Fighting monsters isn’t a bad thing for me, either.”

There’s a chance we might get magic stones from unknown monsters, and even if not, it wouldn’t hurt to get some monster meat.

And the woodcutters and adventurers working in the Treant Forest would no longer be attacked by monsters.

When you think about it that way, Rei’s actions could be called the best course of action.

……Though, for Rei, magic stones and meat have already become the more important factors.

Vihera, who had been listening to Rei and Elena’s conversation, wiped the food crumbs from the corner of Byune’s mouth and spoke up, looking puzzled.

“From what I’m hearing, are monsters the only things in the Treant Forest? What about animals?”

“Unfortunately, it seems there aren’t any animals. It wouldn’t be strange if there were some, though.”

“Since it’s such a lush forest, it wouldn’t be strange for herbivores that eat the plants, and carnivores that eat the herbivores, to gather there.”

Saying this, Rei enjoyed his pizza topped with sausage, onions, and mushrooms.

“Well, putting me aside for now… how did things go for Marina and Vihera?”

“I was working at the construction site, but it seems they’re running a little behind schedule compared to what we were told beforehand.”

“Why? It’s not because they’re short on building materials, is it?”

The main reason Daskar had asked Rei to return was to handle the transport of the felled timber from Treant Forest, but that didn’t mean no timber had been transported at all.

Also, the amount of wood Rei had transported before heading to the Kingdom of Reburina was substantial, so it was unlikely that they would run out of building materials.

“No. It’s not a materials issue. The problem is that too many skilled craftsmen have gathered.”

“...You mean the problem isn’t that skilled craftsmen didn’t gather, but that too many gathered?”

If there weren’t enough skilled craftsmen, I could understand that the work would be delayed because they couldn’t handle the workload.

But why would there be too many skilled craftsmen…? As he thought this, he immediately remembered.

Come to think of it, there had been a similar commotion before the expansion work even began.

“Is it because they’re skilled that they’re stubborn and can’t accept other craftsmen’s methods? No, but wasn’t that already resolved before construction began?”

“That’s right. In terms of the general direction, it had been resolved.”

“Oh, could that mean…”

At Marina’s words, Ara, who had been enjoying her pizza, muttered as if she’d just realized something.

Seeing Ara like that, Marina nodded in agreement.

“I think you’re right, Ara. The craftsmen had agreed on the general plan, but when it comes to the details, they each have their own methods. And since they’re all seasoned craftsmen, there’s a proper reason behind each of those methods.”

“So, why hasn’t this been an issue until now?”

“Of course it has! But until recently, it wasn’t such a big deal. However… it seems that lately, these issues have been piling up more often.”

“The construction has started to fall behind… I see. Well, rather than just compromising and sweeping things under the rug to keep the project moving, it might be better to have a proper discussion and move forward only after we’re both satisfied.”

“That’s true. If we just go along with things here and push the construction forward, we know there’ll be a similar uproar later on… Thinking about it that way, I suppose we really should clarify those points. Especially now that we’re heading into fall and winter…”

She said nothing more, but everyone present understood exactly what Marina was getting at.

The current season is fine. Even in the fall, there’s probably no need to worry that much.

But winter... if we’re working in the snow and an accident happens due to some kind of mistake, there’s a high chance it could be irreversible.

To avoid regrets later, there’s no doubt we should thoroughly discuss the finer details of the construction on-site.

“Do the higher-ups know about these circumstances?”

“Of course. It’s just… no matter how much we say, we’re dealing with craftsmen. I mean, if they were unskilled workers, that would be one thing, but since we’re gathering skilled craftsmen for this project, there’s nothing we can do unless they’re convinced themselves.”

“...I get the feeling that, if things go wrong, the extension project itself might just end up getting finished. Or is that just my imagination?”

If we discuss things and the craftsmen work well together, there’s no doubt they’ll be a tremendous asset on the job site.

But that’s only if they work well together.

It would be great if that happened, but if it didn’t, I couldn’t rule out the possibility that most of the craftsmen would walk off the job.

Especially since there are many dwarves among the craftsmen, and their stubbornness is beyond the ordinary.

"That’s a possibility… but I’m sure we’ll manage somehow."

As Rei understood her words, yet she said this with a certain air of confidence—perhaps because Marina had some sort of ace up her sleeve.

Though he harbored some doubts about that, Rei decided not to ask any further, thinking, “If Marina says so…”

“So, if that’s the case, what about Vihera?”

“Me? Hmm, well. To be honest, nothing’s really changed from before.”

Vihera, who was eating roast venison cooked in a pizza oven with a sauce made from tart fruits, replied to Rei.

“No different from before… huh. From what I heard, it sounded like you took down quite a few people?”

Rei spoke the information he’d heard from Kenny, but Vihera smiled at him and replied.

“Yes, that’s why I said it’s no different from before, didn’t I?”

She said this with a nonchalant expression, but that meant nothing less than that she had been forcibly silencing large numbers of people with her brute strength all along.

That said, Rei recognized that anyone who would be treated that way by Vihera must be a particularly malicious opponent.

After all, no matter how hungry Vihera is for a fight against a strong opponent, she wouldn’t take such a hardline stance against someone who isn’t that malicious.

“If that’s the case, it means more despicable scum are infiltrating Gilm than I’d anticipated. …Honestly, this is a pain. I just hope it doesn’t cause trouble down the line.”

As Rei said this, Marina handed him a glass of fruit water chilled with spirit magic and spoke in a consoling tone.

“There’s nothing we can do about that. No matter what, people like that are bound to slip in among the adventurers. If anything, I think you should praise Vihera for taking them down so decisively.”

“I don’t think Rei needs to worry about it that much. It’s not like Rei is the representative of all Gilm’s adventurers, right? Setting aside Rank S adventurers, there are plenty of other Rank A adventurers and those with nicknames in Gilm.”

“I didn’t realize Vihera knew so much about Gilm’s adventurers.”

Judging by her tone, there was no doubt that Vihera had gathered some information about the Rank A adventurers and those with nicknames in Gilm.

As far as Rei was concerned, the only Rank A adventurers and adventurers with aliases he knew were the Elk group from the Axe of the Thunder God.

Well, Elena does have a nickname, but since the premise here is strictly that they’re adventurers affiliated with Gilm, she probably doesn’t count.

“Of course! Rei really should learn a bit more about the adventuring scene. Besides… don’t you think it’s only natural for me to gather information on such powerful adventurers?”

“I won’t deny that.”

Actually, considering Vihera’s personality—she loves fighting strong opponents—it makes perfect sense for her to gather information on people rumored to be powerful.

After all, she never knows when she might face such an opponent.

Failing to prepare for that could have resulted in a truly fatal loss for Vihera.

“Hehe, next time I’ll tell Rei all sorts of things about that, so look forward to it.”

Saying that, Vihera broke into a broad smile.

However, when it comes to information on adventurers, former Guild Master Marina is no slouch either, and Elena, thanks to her wide network, knows a few people with nicknames.

And so, the dinner table grew increasingly lively.

Watching the scene unfold, Rei realized that he had truly returned to Gilm.
