After walking along the path trampled by the giants for about ten minutes,

In that short time, Rei and the others had figured out where the giants were.

"Ah... I see. No wonder aerial reconnaissance wouldn't spot them. The surrounding forest is completely hidden by the trees."

The place Rei pointed to was what could be called a cave.

Indeed, the entrance was about five meters tall, easily large enough for a giant to enter.

Its width was just barely enough for two giants to walk side by side... but even so, a single giant could enter without any trouble whatsoever.

Surrounding the cave were numerous trees. As Rei had said, these trees provided such dense cover that the cave remained hidden even from aerial reconnaissance.

That said, it wasn't because the Jaya people had deliberately planted the trees to achieve this effect; it was purely coincidental.

Still, the fact remained that the cave entrance was concealed. When Rei scouted Mejogo from the sky aboard Set, he hadn't noticed it at all.

Whether by accident or not, it was undoubtedly an unpleasant development for Rei.

"But does it really go on beyond this? I mean, this cave... it doesn't look that big, does it?"

As Vihera pointed out, the rocky mountain housing the cave entrance itself didn't appear particularly large.

Of course, one could guess from the outside that it was probably spacious enough to hide several people, or perhaps a dozen or so.

But if asked whether it could conceal hundreds... or even thousands of giants standing three meters tall, the answer was no.

Rei tried to say something in response to Vihera's words... but suddenly stopped moving.

No, it wasn't just Rei who stopped. Though slightly behind him, everyone else also froze, focusing their awareness on the presence approaching from within the cave... or more accurately, from inside the cave towards them.

"As for that explanation... wouldn't it be best to ask this guy?"

Rei's muttered words, spoken while looking at the cave, must have been clearly heard by whoever was inside.

Hesitating for a moment at having his presence discovered, he nevertheless emerged from the cave immediately.

"Well, well, I never expected to be found so easily. That was completely unexpected."

Hearing the voice of the person who spoke, Rei felt a moment of doubt.

It was a voice he recognized from somewhere.

Where had he heard it before... Before he could recall, the figure emerged from the cave.

A man who seemed to come from a good background.

Seeing the man's face, Rei finally recalled his name.

"Ryutas... was it?"

"Do you know him?"

Judging from Rei's reaction, Marina concluded he was probably an acquaintance. Still, seeing him appear in a place like this, she couldn't imagine he was an ally.

"Yeah, unfortunately... though I hate to say it that way, but we definitely know each other. Remember when I came with Set to scout Mejogo before? I told you about helping that merchant who was being attacked by bandits, right?"

"You did mention it."

Elena murmured, as if recalling Rei's words.

"But from what I heard, the person you saved was a merchant, right? At the very least, it definitely wasn't someone who'd be coming out of a Jaya facility like this."

"Hahaha, thanks for the harsh opinion. But hey, could you cut him some slack? I used to hide my identity too."

"Kept your identity hidden, huh? Well, considering you had several guards... no, still have them, I figured you weren't just some ordinary peddler."

Ryuutasu flashed a slight look of surprise at Rei, who spoke while gazing toward the depths of the cave.

...Yes, not just noticing, but creating.

The fact that the presence of his guards was noticed was likely within Ryutas's expectations.

"With powerhouses like you two, my guards are useless. They should be quite skilled, though."

Ryutas's demeanor was subtly different from when he had encountered Rei before.

Then again, that encounter had been accidental, and he'd undoubtedly been putting on an act to conceal his true identity. It was easy for Rei to imagine that the Ruyutas before him now was the genuine article.

"True. For someone from the Kingdom of Reburina, he seems reasonably skilled."

Even if he was skilled, it ultimately only meant something within the small nation of Reburina. In a major kingdom like Mireana, he wouldn't be considered that strong.

As Rei implied this, a murderous intent flared from the guards.

(Was it dissatisfaction at having his own strength belittled? Or was it to prevent harm coming to his charge, Ryutas, due to the insult? I couldn't tell, but it was immature, that's for sure.)

Of course, Rei and the others were aware the guards were hiding.

The other side likely understood that too, yet revealing it here was an action that could only be called rash.

Whether he understood this exchange or not.

Ryutas opened his mouth without seeming particularly concerned.

"Just adequate, huh? He's among the best in Jaya, you know. Yet to an adventurer with a nickname, he's just adequate?"

Ryutas seemed slightly shocked, but was he genuinely shocked, or was he simply putting on that act?

Even Rei couldn't tell.

The only thing he knew for certain was...

"I never expected you to admit so readily that you're one of the Jaya. I thought you'd try to cover it up a bit more."

Hearing the phrase "among the best in Jaya" made his affiliation immediately obvious.

Hearing those words come from Ryutas' mouth was surprising to Rei as well.

"Is that so? But since we've run into each other here, I don't think there's any point in pretending anymore."

"I won't deny that."

Ryutas was present in this cave, believed to be the shipping point for the giants.

Even if he claimed he had nothing to do with the Jaya, it was utterly impossible to believe.

"But you're still trying to cover it up, aren't you? And why come out the front entrance? If you were trying to escape, there must be other hidden passages or something."

"Oh? Gathering intel? You're surprisingly sharp. ...Well, since it's already out in the open, I'll tell you. Unfortunately, this is the only entrance and exit to this cave. Well, of course, there used to be a few others before... You know what I mean?"

Ryuutas didn't finish his sentence, but his gaze demanded understanding. Rei grasped what he meant immediately.

"Giants, huh?"

"Correct. The passages leading outside weren't all wide enough for giants to pass through, but even those narrow enough for humans could be easily destroyed if a giant rampaged through them. That's why there's no other entrance or exit leading outside besides this one."

"...That's important information. Are you sure it's okay to just tell me so casually?"

The information that slipped from Ryutas's mouth was quite unexpected for Rei.

Of course, he couldn't just take that information at face value.

Still, it was undeniably crucial information.

So why reveal such vital information? It was only natural to wonder.

Elena and the others, watching the exchange from a step back, shared that same thought.

But Ryutas continued speaking, even smiling at Rei's words.

"First off, skilled operatives like Rei and her crew have made it this far. Trying to hide anything more would just get discovered immediately. So, wouldn't it be better to just spill what you can, get captured, and then hope they go easy on you?"

"Youngster!"

One of the guards hiding behind a cave rock emerged, saying this.

They clearly hadn't expected Ryutas to reveal their organization's secrets so casually.

A stern-faced man in his forties or fifties glared at Rei and the others while giving Ryutas a sharp look from the corner of his eye.

"What on earth are you saying to such people?! Young Master carries the future of Jaya on his shoulders! You must be fully aware of that!"

"Well, that's easier said than done. Now that Rei and the others have come this far, I don't think there's much we can do. Even if I tried to fight back here, do you really think I could do anything about Rei and the others?"

"Well..."

The man who addressed Ryutas was, after all, a bodyguard, skilled at gauging an opponent's strength.

Even to his eyes, it was clear that Rei and the others possessed power far surpassing his own and that of his subordinates.

If Ryutas didn't leak the information here, a fight would undoubtedly break out.

And if it did, their defeat was certain.

Yet, as a member of Jaya, he couldn't simply obey Ryutas's words.

"Young master, I understand what you mean. But I was raised in Jaya. I cannot accept such a thing."

"...I suppose so."

At the man's words, Ryutas flashed a momentary look of disappointment.

But it vanished just as quickly, replaced by his usual faintly amused expression.

"So, what are you going to do? You know that even if all of you gang up on them, there's nothing you can do about Rei and the others, right?"

"I understand that, but... even so, I..."

The man cut himself off, drawing a dagger from its sheath at his waist and assuming a fighting stance.

It was a clear act of hostility, but the blade was aimed not at Ryutas, but at Rei.

(Yeah, right.)

Rei felt a sense of relief at that.

Ryutas seemed likely to follow them quietly and would probably provide detailed information about the situation.

They wanted to avoid targeting such a person if possible.

If he himself were the target, he was confident he could handle it however necessary, so this turn of events was actually what he wanted.

"So? What about the others? If you want to go with this guy... well, I'm not exactly stopping you."

As if obeying Rei's words, one by one, they revealed themselves.

To Rei's eyes, it was clear every single one of them was more skilled than Jaya's elite fighters.

"More than I expected are coming along for the ride. ...You, surprisingly, don't have much popularity, do you?"

"How rude. ...Though seeing this scene, I can't deny it, unfortunately."

In truth, the number of auras Rei sensed totaled about ten.

And only two remained by Ryutasu's side.

In other words, eight had abandoned Ryutas and stood before Rei.

It was no wonder Ryutas lost confidence in his own lack of popularity.

The sweat on Ryutasu's forehead was definitely not from the summer heat; it was undoubtedly from his lack of popularity.

At least, that's how it appeared to Rei.

"Well, for now... if we deal with these guys, we'll get the information, right?"

"That's right. Though, we've got our own issues too, so it's not like they'll spill everything."

Ryuuta's words reminded Rei of the man who had died the instant he tried to say something in the underground facility.

"Ah... I see. So that curse-like magic... you have it too?"

"..."

Rei muttered suddenly, but for some reason... yes, for some reason, Ryutas looked at Rei with a stunned expression of utter shock.

It was the face of someone who had seen something utterly unbelievable, or someone unimaginable.

For Ryutas, who usually kept his expression composed and never showed his true feelings, this was an extremely rare sight.

"What's wrong?"

That's precisely why Rei, puzzled by Ryutas's reaction, turned his gaze toward him.

"Don't underestimate me!"

Rei, who had shown an opening before him that couldn't have been any bigger, must have wounded the man's pride.

Grasping his dagger, he closed the distance to Rei in one swift motion.

He was confident in his own skill.

That was precisely why he had been entrusted with Ryutas's protection—he took pride in that role.

But... facing such a man, Rei's attitude—as if he didn't even exist—was absolutely unforgivable.

That step forward, fueled by such fury, was undoubtedly the man's decisive move. There was no doubt about it.

But... the fact that the one standing before him was Rei was the man's sole and greatest miscalculation.
