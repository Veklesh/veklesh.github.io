The dinner party—where the food was delicious but we couldn’t fully savor the flavors, leaving us unsure whether to call it a blessing or a curse—was still going on.

Strictly speaking, since we were still in the middle of the appetizers, I suppose one could say the meal had only just begun.

“Rei, I’d like to ask you something. How was Elena during her time in Gilm?”

Duke Kerebel suddenly asked Rei, who was savoring a potage made from a vegetable that resembled a potato yet was clearly different.

Rei, who had been sipping his soup quietly in accordance with table manners, was at a loss as to how to answer the sudden question.

He was unsure how to answer.

Hesitating, Rei glanced at Elena, and only after seeing her nod did he speak.

“They cooperated quite actively. Thanks to that, well… the strange interference has also decreased.”

The “strange interference” he referred to, of course, was the actions of the aristocrats in the Noble Faction.

Duke Kerebel was well aware of this, of course, but he made no move to reveal it and simply nodded.

“I see. That’s the best news of all. I’ve been interacting with Margrave Larks quite a bit lately. Precisely because of that, I was worried there might be someone trying to hinder the expansion of his stronghold.”

“Thanks to Elena, it’s a huge relief that those concerns are gone.”

“……Elena, is it?”

At Rei’s words, Duke Kerebel murmured that slowly.

His gaze was naturally fixed on Rei, but his eyes held a powerful intensity.

Seeing Duke Kerebel like that, Rei finally realized his mistake.

Although Rei usually addressed Elena by her first name, this was no ordinary occasion.

Yes, this was a place where Elena’s parents were both present.

Calling Elena by her first name in such a place carried deep significance.

At the very least, no one had ever done such a thing before.

...Not even those who sought marriage out of desire for Elena’s beauty and strength, or the wealth and power of the Duke of Kerebel’s family, had ever dared to do such a thing.

That’s how much the Duke of Kerebel was feared; the sheer pressure he exerted was so intense that one might even feel physical pressure if one made a misstep.

Rei, too, realized he had made a mistake, but there was no way he could take back the words he had already spoken.

Besides, he had no intention of trying to do so.

That is why, even as he felt the pressure emanating from Duke Kerebel once more, he nodded in response.

“Yes. Elena has been a great help in many ways. …Though, of course, her assistance with the matter concerning the Kingdom of Reburina and other things has also been significant.”

A slight twitch.

The moment Rei uttered the words “Kingdom of Reburina,” an aura resembling murderous intent emanated from Arcadia, who stood beside Duke Kerebel.

It was something entirely different from the aura emanating from Kerebel Duke.

Sensing that aura, Rei opened his mouth, looking somewhat timid.

“Lord Arcadia, is something wrong?”

“...No. I’ve heard about the matter concerning the Kingdom of Reburina as well. Including exactly what was being done and how.”

Though a smile played on Arcadia’s lips, his eyes were completely devoid of humor.

Given Arcadia’s enigmatic nature, seeing him like this sent a chill down his spine.

It even felt as though saying the wrong thing here might lead to the worst possible outcome.

“Mother, could it be that there were victims even where you were…?”

“I’m not sure. I’ve heard that some people have gone missing, but that doesn’t necessarily mean they were all caught up in the incidents Elena and the others were involved in.”

Though Arcadia said this, her eyes revealed what she was truly thinking.

“You. There was talk of sending a delegation to the Kingdom of Reburina. What’s the status of that?”

It seemed like a casual inquiry, but the pressure behind her gaze was intense.

Still, as Arcadia’s husband, Duke Kerebel spoke without seeming particularly bothered.

“Matters like this require a lot of groundwork. You know that, don’t you, Arcadia? The official delegation will likely depart in the spring.”

“……I’ve heard that the Neutral Faction left quite some time ago, though?”

“That can’t be helped. After all, this matter came to light in Gilm. If we were to sit idly by here, they might well take advantage of our weakness. Still, we took retaliatory action before they could act, and having Elena with us saved face for the Noble Faction.”

In that sense, it was the Royal Faction that fell furthest behind regarding the matter of the Kingdom of Reburina.

That said, the Royal Faction wasn’t directly involved in the matter of the Kingdom of Reburina.

Still, as the largest faction, there’s no doubt their prestige has been damaged.

Furthermore, they struggled to select personnel—specifically, who would travel to the Kingdom of Reburina—and as a result, the dispatch of an embassy was postponed until next spring or later.

But true to their name as the Royal Faction, the one leading them is the King of the Kingdom of Mireana himself.

In other words, those who depart next spring or later will constitute the official delegation from the Kingdom of Mireana.

“Even so, won’t that fail to ease the grievances of those who were taken away?”

“Mother, as far as I know, the survivors among those who were taken have gone to Gilm…”

“I know.”

Arcadia replied casually to Elena’s words.

But upon hearing that, Rei felt a chill run down her spine once more.

The incident involving Sura and the others had, in fact, become quite well-known in Gilm.

But even though it was only a recent occurrence, why did Arcadia know about it?

Of course, the nobles of the Noble Faction maintain estates in Gilm to gather all sorts of information.

That’s why it wouldn’t be surprising if he knew about the incident involving Sura and the others… but even so, the speed at which the information had spread was clearly too fast.

Paying no heed to Rei’s reaction, Arcadia continued speaking.

“But the fact that they’re there means they have no intention of returning home… or perhaps they can’t return. In the villages and towns I know, no one has ever come back. That being the case… of course, I don’t necessarily think that everyone who goes missing has been abducted.”

Countryside, cities, the frontier… People live in all sorts of places, but in this world, it’s not uncommon for someone to vanish for one reason or another.

By bandits, illegal slave traders, or monsters.

Young, beautiful women, in particular, are vulnerable to such dangers.

Arcadia probably understands that, but still, when she thinks of her acquaintances suffering such a fate, she must have her own thoughts on the matter.

“Arcadia, let’s leave it at that. I know what you’re thinking, and I understand, but there are things I can and cannot do. Besides, right now, this is a welcome party for Rei… the boy Elena brought here for the first time. Please don’t get too carried away.”

The moment she was told this, Arcadia muttered a quiet “I’m sorry” and returned to her meal.

...That said, as for Elena, who had heard this, her face had turned crimson at the words spoken by Duke Kerebel.

Elena didn’t hide the fact that she loved Rei from Marina or Vihera, but that was strictly in front of her friends.

Or with close friends she’d known since childhood, like Ara, or fellow party members like Byune.

Even if it wasn’t said directly, it was only natural for her to feel embarrassed when her father said something like that to her.

“……”

Elena’s cheeks and ears, as she looked down, were visibly flushed.

Seeing Elena looking down, seemingly unable to speak, her parents smiled.

Not a teasing smile, but one that showed their joy at their daughter’s growth.

……Incidentally, the fact that Elena showed no interest in boys was something both her parents had been worried about.

Elena’s appearance was undeniably beautiful.

So much so that it wouldn’t be strange to call her a goddess of beauty.

Yet, Elena showed absolutely no interest in men and devoted herself solely to training.

As the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel, she did make appearances in society, but that was strictly just that—appearances.

The fact that Elena had taken an interest in a man was undoubtedly a cause for celebration for the Duke and Duchess of Kerebel.

(That said...)

Duke Kerebel had been watching his daughter with a smile, but the next moment, he turned a sharp gaze toward Rei.

“Rei, do you understand the position Elena is in?”

The sudden question.

But, of course, Rei understood exactly what that question implied.

“She is Kerebel Duke’s only daughter, isn’t she?”

“That’s right. In the future, will Elena inherit this house, or will the man she marries into the family inherit it? Or perhaps adoption is an option.”

“I think there’s a more certain way to handle this, though.”

At Rei’s words, Duke Kerebel smiled with amusement.

“Hmm, a more certain method, you say? That piques my interest. I’d very much like to hear about it.”

“It’s simple. Just give Elena a younger brother or sister, and that will solve everything.”

“Huh!?”

Perhaps Rei’s words were completely unexpected, for not only Duke Kerebel but even Arcadia gasped for a moment… and once they realized what kind of act he was suggesting, Arcadia’s cheeks flushed as red as Elena’s had been moments earlier.

Duke Kerebel didn’t react as blatantly as Arcadia; rather, he fixed a stern gaze on Rei, who had uttered such a thing.

“Isn’t it rather impertinent to meddle in the relationship between my wife and me?”

“Perhaps. But considering Elena’s situation, isn’t this the best course of action? As it is, Elena has already inherited the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone at the Altar of Inheritance. As a result, I believe her lifespan is now equal to that of an Ancient Dragon, rather than a human’s.”

Though the words were unfamiliar and his delivery slightly awkward, Rei spoke while maintaining eye contact with Duke Kerebel.

He uses polite language when speaking with Daskar as well, but for better or worse, Rei has known Daskar for quite some time.

That’s why, while he’s somewhat accustomed to speaking with Daskar by now… this is his first time speaking with Duke Kerebel.

It was only natural that he felt a bit nervous.

...Or perhaps the fact that he is Elena’s father is also having a strong influence.

“The inheritance of the Ancient Dragon’s Magic Stone, is it? Even though it is a long-cherished wish passed down through the Duke of Kerebel’s family for generations… no, perhaps that’s beside the point now. In any case, I understand what you’re trying to say, Rei.”

Now that she has inherited the Ancient Dragon’s Magic Stone, Elena will undoubtedly live longer than humans… and even longer than elves.

If such a person were to become the head of the Duke of Kerebel’s family, there would surely be no shortage of people who would take issue with it.

In the worst-case scenario, it could even lead to something resembling a family feud.

The position of head of the Kerebel House must be that important… and yet, incredibly appealing.

“So?”

Probably not.

Even as I thought that, I asked just in case… but, as expected, the response was a shake of the head.

“It’s not that simple. To begin with, Elena is a special figure even among the Noble Faction. You of all people should understand that best, don’t you, Rei?”

“Well…”

Rei was at a loss for words.

In fact, within the Noble Faction, Elena—with her nickname “Princess General” and her beauty—is a figure truly worthy of being called a symbol.

...Which is probably why there’s no end to the men who keep trying to woo her, undeterred.

Precisely because she is Elena, abandoning her status as heir to the Duke of Kerebel’s house—let alone, though he didn’t say it aloud here, serving under Rei—is something the Noble Faction simply cannot tolerate.

“Whatever I do, I can’t make a decision right away.”

“Is that so?”

I knew it all along, but I still can’t help feeling disappointed.

“However…”

Whether he felt pity for Rei or had intended to say this all along, I don’t know, but the Duke of Kerebel smiled faintly and spoke.

“At the very least, until the expansion of Gilm is complete, barring any unforeseen circumstances, I intend to have Elena keep an eye on things in Gilm to ensure the Noble Faction doesn’t pull any shenanigans, so please rest assured. Of course, if anything happens, I’ll have you come back.”

Hearing Duke Kerebel’s words, both Rei and Elena felt a sense of relief.
