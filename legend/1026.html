Rei's Death Scythe strike sent the attacking man flying sideways.

Struck by the hilt rather than the blade of the Death Scythe, the blow wasn't fatal, but several bones were undoubtedly broken.

"Rei, how are you... No, I suppose I don't need to ask."

Ara , wielding her Power Axe, asked, but Rei shrugged.

"Just a minor foe. ...Still, this is the hideout? It certainly looks the part."

Rei's gaze fell upon a cave carved into the mountainside.

Seven bandits lay unconscious or dead around it.

"Well, it can't be helped. Bandit hideouts are all the same. I'd be surprised if there was a mansion built up in the middle of these mountains."

As Ara declared this while wiping blood and flesh—presumably from the bandits—from the blade of her Power Axe, anyone unaware of the situation might well wonder which side was actually the bandits.

But Rei didn't seem to care about that as he spoke.

"If the information we got from the bandits is true, only these seven should have been left in the hideout. There shouldn't be anyone else."

The image of the bandits who had attacked them flashed through Rei's mind.

Five days had passed since leaving Gilm. Just as Marina had predicted, once they left the frontier town of Gilm, bandits began attacking in place of monsters.

The carriage pulled by a horse of such obvious, massive stature proved no deterrent against bandit attacks.

Well, if they had had escort cavalry, it might have been different. Unfortunately, Rei and the others were traveling with only Elena's carriage, making them prime targets for the bandits.

The fact that only Ara was in the driver's seat might have also played a role.

And above all, the fact that Set and Yellow were flying through the air chasing each other and weren't near the carriage was undoubtedly fatal for the bandits.

The outcome awaiting those bandits was nothing short of disastrous.

Nearly everyone aboard the carriage possessed high combat ability, making them opponents the attacking bandits simply couldn't handle.

As a result, all but a few lost their lives.

If there had been a town nearby, they might have considered handing the bandits over as slaves. But naturally, no such town existed close by. Transporting the bandits would have taken time, and Marina, mindful of the World Tree incident, didn't want to waste any more time.

Based on that judgment, Rei's suggestion to annihilate the bandits was chosen. Figuring they might as well go all out, they decided to raid the bandits' hideout... and now, the bandits are suffering the misfortune of their hideout being stormed.

"Audban was against it, but I'm glad we came."

The image of Audban opposing the bandit hunt, citing lack of time, flashed through Rei's mind.

But Marina, who was also the Guildmaster, had no intention of simply overlooking the bandits, even if this wasn't her assigned territory.

After all, the Dark Elf Forest, home to both Marina and Audban, wasn't far from here.

This meant that among those attacked by the bandits operating here, there were bound to be quite a few who had gone to the Dark Elf Forest.

Generally speaking, the Elven race—not limited to ordinary Elves or Dark Elves—tends to be closed-off, though there are exceptions.

Those who conducted business with such insular people were naturally few. She judged that attacks on such individuals would ultimately harm Marina and Audban's homeland.

Even so, Audban insisted they should hurry onward, but in the end, Marina's opinion prevailed, and Rei would demonstrate the full extent of his prowess as the so-called Bandit Eater.

"She has a stubborn streak about her, doesn't she? ...I don't know why, though."

"Ah... yeah, that's true."

Hearing Ara's words, Rei recalled the look Audban had given him.

The expression he'd worn when she removed the New Moon Ring was probably only natural for someone who'd witnessed such immense power firsthand.

In fact, Rei had even thought it remarkable that Audban hadn't fainted or made a scene despite directly sensing his magic.

As a result, when Audban saw the griffin, Set, with his own eyes, he showed almost no surprise, perhaps because he'd built up a tolerance through Rei.

However, how Rei perceived others and how others perceived Rei were entirely separate matters, and this discrepancy was precisely what fueled Audban's current aversion toward Rei.

Marina trusts Rei, which is precisely why Audban hasn't taken any particular action. But if Marina weren't here, Audban would have left the group long ago.

(Well, if it means treating the World Tree requires enormous magical power, they can't exactly cut me loose, I suppose.)

After a moment's thought, Rei shook his head and spoke again.

"Anyway, let's search this hideout... Huh?"

Rei muttered that much before suddenly realizing something.

According to the bandit who attacked Rei, there were supposed to be seven bandits left in the hideout. But...

"There's still another presence in the cave?"

At Rei's words, Ara, who had just finished wiping the blood and flesh from the blade of her Power Axe, frowned slightly.

"Is that true?"

Though she asked, there was no hint of doubt in Ara's tone toward Rei.

Ara knew all too well from past experience just how extraordinary Rei's abilities were.

(Thinking back, attacking Rei when we first met... Honestly, saying I got lucky with my life doesn't quite cover it.)

Suddenly, she recalled the moment she first met Rei.

He understood now: if Rei had been serious back then, he wouldn't be alive today.

Seeing Rei wield his power without hesitation against nobles time and again only reinforced that conviction.

Rei, seemingly unaware of Ara's thoughts, nodded silently, his gaze fixed deep into the cave.

"Yeah. But seeing how he hasn't shown himself even after all the bandits were killed... either he has some reason, or he's just playing hard to get."

A vision suddenly flashed through Rei's mind: the cave's deepest recesses, where the bandits' big boss awaited the intruders.

...That the big boss was Pamidor was probably because of that vicious face.

"What should we do? We can't just go back now, can we?"

Ara asked Rei as if it were a given, but Rei nodded in agreement.

"We've come this far. Turning back now would be pointless. The Dark Elves... no, Marina's clan is likely to suffer."

While he claimed it was for Marina's sake, the truth was he also harbored hopes of finding some treasure.

Rei wasn't usually overly concerned with money, but sometimes thieves possessed things money couldn't buy.

Also, it was easier to get a large number of spears in a place like this than to buy multiple throwing spears at a weapons shop.

Especially for Rei, who primarily used spears as throwing weapons. Even spears with chipped tips were perfectly serviceable as disposable weapons.

"...Alright, let's go then."

Rei spoke to Ara, who sighed and muttered under her breath.

"You don't have to force yourself to come along, you know? You could even go back ahead if you want."

"No. Lady Elena instructed me to accompany Rei."

While true, Elena hadn't assigned Ara solely as Rei's escort.

Well, it was partly for that purpose, but more significantly, it was to prevent Rei from doing anything strange.

For instance, finding a magic item in a bandit hideout and becoming so engrossed in examining what it was, or trying it out and getting completely absorbed.

There was likely concern that once he became engrossed in something, he might forget everything else.

Especially now, as they were heading to the forest where the dark elves, starting with Marina, lived to treat the World Tree. While defeating the thieves was necessary, they didn't want to waste time on anything else.

For that reason, accompanied by Ara, who had been assigned as his supervisor, Rei headed straight into the cave.

Fortunately, the cave didn't seem to branch off too complexly; there were only a few places where the path split into two.

Guided by the sensations he picked up, Rei advanced through the cave.

Ara walked quietly behind Rei, but her expression betrayed doubt about whether anyone was truly there.

Of course, Ara could also feign sensing human presence.

Especially on the battlefield during chaotic combat, lacking such skills could lead to being ambushed from behind. As such, many who stand on the battlefield possess a certain ability to sense presence, whether consciously or unconsciously.

However, that ability only extended to sensing things within a certain distance from oneself. If asked whether she could sense someone deep within the cave, the answer was no.

Still, the reason they followed behind Rei without complaint was likely because, given his skill, they thought... maybe. And indeed...

"Here."

Rei stopped and murmured. Before him stood a prison cell.

It was likely a place to hold captives taken during raids, to be used as slaves or playthings.

Currently, only one person was imprisoned inside. They lay bound by chains, blindfolded, gagged, and sprawled on the floor.

"How awful..."

Ara muttered, her face flushing crimson with fury the next instant.

She had likely realized from the length of the bound figure's hair that it was a woman.

"Rei, is it alright?"

"Yeah. I wouldn't have come this far if I didn't intend to help in the first place."

Rei nodded without hinting at the thought he'd had upon entering the cave—that perhaps someone was leading the bandits.

Ara didn't seem particularly concerned about that either. She searched for a key to open the cell, but there was none to be found.

"Hm? Hmmmmmm!"

The bound woman let out a groan, seemingly alerted by Rei and Ara's voices.

This only made Ara more determined to break down the cell quickly, but no matter where she looked, the key was nowhere to be found.

"...Well, I guess I have no choice. Can you hear me? I'm going to break this cell open now, so if you could, please move a little closer to the wall?"

Hearing Ara's voice, the woman, still blindfolded, crawled toward the wall.

Once she confirmed the woman was far enough away, Ara raised the Power Axe in her hand... and swung it down forcefully toward the cell.

Ara's own immense strength combined with the power axe's destructive force. Together, they bent the metal cell frame severely with a single blow.

Perhaps dissatisfied that it hadn't shattered with one blow, Ara swung the power axe again, bringing it down with even greater force than before.

A high-pitched metallic clang echoed throughout the area.

Not only that, but the metal itself was forcibly bent, successfully creating a gap.

(Still that same brute strength... Though, I guess I can't really talk.)

Rei was impressed by Ara's brute strength.

But then he realized that description—brute strength, or sheer idiotic power—applied to himself just as much, and he couldn't help but smile wryly.

After forcibly creating a gap large enough for a person to squeeze through, Ara attempts to enter the prison cell...

"Rei-sama, I'm terribly sorry, but just a moment..."

At those few words, Rei understood what Ara meant.

A woman wouldn't want a man seeing her like this.

"Understood. I'm searching for the thieves' treasure, so I'll leave her care to you."

"Yes."

After exchanging those words, Rei left the room containing the prison cell.

After that, he casually searched the thieves' hideout, finding weapons and valuables to stow away in his Misty Ring.

That said, for a hideout, there wasn't much in the way of significant treasure. Mostly it was food and alcohol... and even that wasn't the kind of stuff that would satisfy Rei. It was the sort of thing where cheap was fine as long as there was plenty of it.

As for the alcohol, Rei wasn't much of a drinker, so he couldn't say for sure, but considering the food was cheap stuff... even including things like goblin jerky, it was clear there was no way the alcohol alone would be anything special.

The only saving grace was that there were nearly twenty spears.

"...Quite the poor bandits. Or maybe they'd already disposed of their loot?"

Grumbling, Rei decided it was about time to head toward where Ara was. But when he saw the figure standing where the cell had been, his eyes widened in surprise.

Brown skin, pointed ears. The hair color was slightly different from Marina's or Audban's—a reddish silver, perhaps—but there was no mistaking the undeniable fact that the being before him was a Dark Elf.
