Hearing the shop owner's voice coming from the kitchen, Rei and the others exchanged glances as if to say, "What the hell?"

The strange, angry-sounding shouts coming from there sounded nothing like someone cooking.

"Hey, what do you think?"

Even for Rei, who was usually arrogant and haughty... well, maybe that's a bit of an exaggeration, but who was generally unfazed by anything, this was a scene that couldn't help but surprise him.

"You know, top-tier people often do things that ordinary folks just can't understand."

Rei murmured, reaching for a skewer of roasted oak meat, seemingly unfazed.

For some reason, Marina and her companions nodded in agreement as they looked at Rei.

"...Huh?"

Rei muttered, feeling like he'd been treated incredibly unfairly, but for Johanna and the others, it seemed like this was exactly the kind of thing they expected him to say. They all looked away together... or rather, it would be more accurate to say they looked away in perfect unison.

Even though they looked away... or rather, precisely because they looked away, it was clear what Johanna and the others meant.

"There, Rei, calm down. This dish is delicious too, you know? Here, open wide."

Vihera, who had been watching the exchange between Rei and Johanna and the others, said this while offering Rei a forkful of stir-fried oak meat... Despite being seasoned simply with salt, the meat was incredibly flavorful thanks to the careful preparation.

"Mmm."

The aroma wafting from the oak meat and the dripping juices made Rei move toward Vihera's fork almost reflexively, taking a bite of the oak meat.

'Ah'

Seeing Vihera like that, Elena and Marina both let out a gasp.

"Hmph."

As for Vihera, she wore a triumphant smile directed at the two of them.

"...Hey, Johanna. What do you think of those four?"

"Well, what do I think? If you get carelessly involved, you'll definitely get hurt here. Isn't it better not to get involved?"

"In your case, you just want to keep Set all to yourself, right?"

Johanna puffed out her chest as if to say she had nothing to hide, responding to her companion's sighing remark.

"Oh dear, did I do something? I'm just admiring little Set here, you know? Oh, look, Set-chan. This vegetable stew over here is delicious too, you know?"

"Grrr."

The silver lion meat carried off to the kitchen was intriguing... extremely intriguing. But even more than that, Set was captivated by the array of dishes right before him.

Every dish before him was exquisite, and until the silver lion meat arrived, Set felt a strong desire to savor these dishes fully.

...Though Set understood perfectly well that once dishes made with the silver lion's meat arrived, his focus would undoubtedly shift to those.

Having seen that the quantity of meat itself wasn't particularly large, Set also anticipated he probably wouldn't get to eat much of the silver lion's meat.

Beside him, Yellow was also happily eating the oak meat dish. However, Yellow wasn't particularly large to begin with... rather, he was small, so it seemed he filled up quickly on just a little amount, and his eating pace had slowed.

"Still, seeing it all laid out like this, there sure are a lot of meat dishes."

Elena, having settled her fight with the woman named Vihera, murmured as she looked over the array of dishes on the table once more.

"Well, the place is called the Orc Stew Inn, so isn't that to be expected?"

Ara, who had been enjoying the food while the women's fight was happening, finished dipping the last of her stew into bread and replied.

"Besides, this is a dungeon... It's only natural that adventurers are common here, and when you're moving your body, you crave meat."

"Hey, Vihera. Some adventurers actually prefer vegetables, you know?"

Marina told Vihera.

In truth, that wasn't entirely wrong.

Of course, as Vihera had said, it was true that many adventurers preferred meat dishes.

This was likely influenced by the fact that young men made up a large proportion of adventurers.

But naturally, some also enjoy fish, vegetables, fruits, nuts... there are many things they like.

"I know that, but this is Oak's Stew House, right? Naturally, most people come here for the meat dishes, don't they?"

"Hey, my place isn't exclusively a meat restaurant. We have a decent selection of vegetable dishes too... You can see that just by looking at the tables."

The one who said this was the owner, who had been shouting and making strange noises in the kitchen just moments ago.

Unusually... well, aside from Rei and the others who had come to this shop for the first time today, even Johanna and the others who frequented the place regularly found him unusually cheerful, smiling brightly.

The reason was undoubtedly the plate he held in his hand.

On the plate were skewered grilled items.

It was a surprisingly simple dish considering the time invested, but the owner's smile proved just how good it was.

"Hold on a sec. First up, Silver Lion skewers. Honestly, I wanted to get more creative with it. But this is S-rank monster meat, and I haven't quite figured out its properties yet. To really understand the texture, I kept it simple – just skewers. Seasoned only with salt. Give it a try for now."

The shopkeeper's words made perfect sense to Rei and the others.

After all, in this entire area... no, in the entire Kingdom of Mireana... hell, even looking across all of Elgin, there were almost no chefs who had ever cooked Rank S monster meat.

While "almost none" implies there are a few, even a little... even among S-rank monsters, their quality varies wildly.

The question of whether a cooking method suited for one Rank S monster would be appropriate for another Rank S monster is simply no.

For C-rank... or even B-rank monsters, since they're hunted in reasonably large numbers, some information about how to cook each type has been passed down.

...Though, in most cases, such information is treated as secret recipes, important matters that ordinary cooks couldn't possibly know.

Anyway, since this was the first time the shop owner had cooked a Silver Lion, he needed to start with a simple dish to understand the meat's properties.

"It's seasoned only with salt, but I think it's a good choice to bring out the meat's natural flavor."

As the shopkeeper spoke these words, everyone's hands—and beaks—reached for the plate on the table.

Rei was the first to grab a skewer.

Next was Set... and one after another, they all grabbed skewers of Silver Lion.

"Hey! There's no need to rush like that! There's enough for everyone, so calm the hell down!"

The shopkeeper's angry shout echoed through the shop, but Rei and the others let it go in one ear and out the other as they brought the skewers to their mouths.

The first thing they tasted upon taking a bite was the savory aroma and the perfectly charred surface of the Silver Lion meat.

Given the owner's confidence in his meat dishes, the cooking of the meat was nothing short of perfect.

Some might prefer it cooked more thoroughly, while others might like it closer to rare.

But the majority undoubtedly preferred the current level of doneness.

After biting into the surface layer, filling their mouths with the savory aroma of charred meat, what spread next was a surprisingly rich, deep meat flavor.

Perhaps due to the cut of meat, the juices themselves weren't particularly abundant, yet the feeling of "meat!" remained fully intact.

Moreover, it wasn't tough at all, offering an incredibly tender texture.

(Silver Lion... I've never eaten lion meat, but surely lions on Earth aren't this delicious? ...Probably not. After all, the reason the Silver Lion tastes this good is because it's an S-rank monster.)

Even as such thoughts crossed his mind, most of Rei was simply overwhelmed by the pure taste of the grilled Silver Lion skewers.

Even for Rei, it was hard to put into words exactly what made it so delicious.

It was simply delicious. That single word was all Rei could say.

That flavor, making him want to eat it forever, was truly worthy of Rank S monster meat.

It wasn't due to any special spices or seasonings; it was purely the taste of the ingredient itself.

The others, besides Rei, also seemed speechless, their eyes fixed on the skewers they were nibbling.

Set, Yellow, and the others were no different; everyone was simply silenced by its deliciousness.

Even Elena and the others—as the daughter of Duke Kerebel, former royalty of the Bestir Empire, and the guildmaster of a dark elf who had lived for ages—had tasted countless delicacies, yet they too were rendered speechless by this overwhelming flavor.

For those like Johanna, it was simply too intense.

'…………'

Unable to speak, their consciousness completely consumed by the meat's deliciousness, their state would be utterly incomprehensible to an outsider.

Johanna, while not quite on par with Elena and the others, possessed features generally considered beautiful.

Her family ran a fairly large business, so she had experience with decent cuisine... yet even Johanna, like the others, wore an expression as if her soul had been sucked out, her usually beautiful face taking on a somewhat amusing look.

"This is..."

The shopkeeper was the first to speak. Running a place like this probably gave him some sort of tolerance for delicious food.

Taking a bite or two, he shifted his gaze back to the meat skewered on the stick.

His expression clearly showed he was pondering how best to prepare this meat for maximum flavor, but right now, no one, including Rei, had the luxury of such contemplation.

He had lived as a chef for many years.

He had more than enough confidence in his own skills.

But... right now, he couldn't think of a single way to cook the meat before him.

Well, to be precise, he could easily imagine that no matter how he cooked this meat, it would become an exquisite dish.

But that wouldn't be bringing out the true flavor of the meat.

A dish that truly makes the most of this supreme ingredient, Silver Lion meat... Even thinking about it, nothing comes to mind for the owner.

(So I was still immature, huh?)

Had I become arrogant? Had I lost my drive to improve? Had I grown complacent with the status quo?

Thinking such thoughts, feeling disgusted with my own immaturity, I bring the Silver Lion skewers before me to my mouth.

It hadn't been long since it was cooked, so it wasn't cold.

The taste of the silver lion meat filling his mouth was truly heavenly... he could say that.

(And while it is good meat, the fat itself is surprisingly minimal. Of course, that probably depends on the cut.)

The owner hadn't asked which cut this meat was.

But from his experience handling various monster meats, he guessed it was probably the inner thigh, like beef.

It's lean, red meat. For purely enjoying the taste of the meat itself, it's a superior cut compared to places with more fat.

The rich, fatty cuts favored by nobles taste delicious in small bites—one or two mouthfuls—but eating too much causes indigestion and even makes you feel sick.

Compared to that kind of meat, lean cuts are different from fatty ones in that you can eat a lot of them.

...Of course, there's still a limit to how much you can eat.

However, the meat on the skewers the shop owner held now was a kind of miraculous contradiction—extremely tender even compared to fatty cuts, yet still allowing you to fully savor the rich flavor of the meat itself.

He hesitated over how to cook it... but that hesitation vanished in mere seconds.

After all, I'm a cook. I decided the only way was to try every dish I knew how to make, one after another.

"I'll bring the next dish!"

Announcing this, he took a bite of the remaining Silver Lion meat on his own skewer and headed back into the kitchen.

That day... countless dishes featuring Silver Lion meat were served. Each time, everyone ate and was left speechless, stunned. This happened over and over. Rei had them prepare a few more portions of meat, and among those, he slyly stored his favorites away in Misty Ring.
