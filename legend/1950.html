For now, Rei and the others return to searching the mansion, pondering where the corpse lying before them was created… and why there was any need to create such a corpse in the first place.

Although it isn’t as spacious as the mansions in the aristocratic district, its proximity to that area means it’s still far larger than an ordinary home.

For that reason alone, searching the mansion is quite a hassle… and the most troublesome part of all is that there are still people inside the mansion targeting Rei and the guards.

For that reason, while Rei could manage on his own, the other guards had to move in groups of two or three, making it impossible to proceed with the search smoothly.

“Well, there’s still a chance there’s something in that study, so it’s only natural for the guards to check it out.”

As far as Rei could tell, there seemed to be nothing else in the study where he’d found the wall switch.

But if the guards were going to check it, it was only natural to hope that something might turn up.

So, having left the study to the guards, Rei continued searching the mansion for other places where he might find a clue.

“Oh, Rei. What’s up?”

As he was walking past a room, someone called out to him.

He turned his gaze toward the voice and saw three guards standing there.

Rei immediately realized they had been searching inside that room.

“I was just looking for any clues. I’ve been checking all over, but I just can’t seem to find anything.”

“Well, whoever’s behind this is definitely a cautious one. They wouldn’t leave evidence lying around that easily.”

“I know that. Still, there are just too few clues. …I did find what looked like some documents in the study, though.”

“You should’ve said so sooner. Let me see.”

At Rei’s words, the guard who had been searching the room’s expression changed—not drastically, but he spoke with obvious interest.

Rei hesitated for a moment, but figuring there were no other leads anyway, he took the documents out of his Misty Ring and handed them to the guard.

Honestly, he should have looked at the documents with the other guards after leaving that place—which had essentially become a mountain of corpses—but he’d completely forgotten in the face of that bizarre scene.

But... as the guards read the documents, their faces were filled with confusion.

“What is this? …I can’t make any sense of this at all.”

“You too? It feels like some kind of text that isn’t really text, doesn’t it?”

“It’s a code.”

The third guard looking at the documents muttered that.

It wasn’t a particularly loud voice, but it definitely reached the ears of all three.

Naturally, the voice reached Rei’s ears as well.

“A code? …Another hassle. Do you think you can crack it?”

“I can’t do it right here and now. I need to examine it more closely.”

“Ah… Since it was inside a hidden compartment, I’m sure it contains some important information.”

Saying that, Rei also peered at the document, but... all that was actually written there were a string of incomprehensible words and numbers jumbled together in no particular order. There was no way he could make sense of what was written on the paper.

"Yeah, I don’t really get it."

To begin with, Rei wasn’t particularly good at deciphering codes.

If it had been something simple—like a text on a piece of paper with a raccoon drawing on it, mixed with a ton of “ta” characters—or something that barely even qualified as a code, that might have been manageable. But this wasn’t the kind of thing that could be solved with child’s play.

“Right? Yeah, I think I should just have Rei hold onto this for now. Once we’re done exploring the mansion, I’ll hand these documents over when I report back to the higher-ups.”

“...Are you sure? Some of the guards...”

The guards who had been listening immediately understood what Rei was worried about.

He must have concluded that there was a high probability of a traitor among them.

In fact, given everything that had happened so far, it was only natural to reach that conclusion, and the guards who had come here must have been half-convinced of it themselves.

“I’ll make a copy of these documents here. That way, even if something happens to the originals, there’s no need to worry. Right?”

“Unless there’s some kind of trick hidden in the paper itself.”

As far as Rei knew, a trick like a hidden message revealed by heat using orange peel was the first thing that came to mind.

Documents found in a hidden compartment, documents written in code, and an opponent who was more cautious than anyone they’d ever faced before.

Considering all that information, it wouldn’t be surprising if there were some kind of trick built into the paper itself.

“……I see. That’s a possibility. But if that’s the case, I’m really at a loss. I certainly can’t hand the copy over to my boss.”

“I don’t know about that, so I’ll leave it up to you. Anyway, these are all the documents. The rest is… this tool.”

After stowing the returned documents inside the Misty Ring, Rei proceeded to pull out what appeared to be several tools.

But just by looking at them, naturally, no one could tell what they were used for.

At first, Rei thought they might be magic items, but at least for now, channeling magic into them didn’t produce any noticeable changes.

“What… exactly are these tools for? Since they were in the secret compartment, I don’t think they’re just decorations or something completely meaningless…”

“For now, since we don’t know how to use them, it’s best if Rei keeps them. We’ll probably need them for something…”

“It’ll come in handy.”

The guard was about to say that, but Rei’s ears picked up faint sounds of screams and angry shouts coming from the distance.

It was precisely because Rei’s hearing was sharper than usual that he could make out those faint sounds.

In fact, the guards standing in front of Rei didn’t seem to hear those sounds at all; they were busy discussing the tools they were holding.

But even without hearing the sounds directly, they must have immediately realized that something was different about Rei.

The guards looked at Rei with suspicion, but... by then, Rei had already sprung into action.

“I’m just going out for a bit.”

Leaving only those words behind, he left the room and started running toward the direction the voice had come from.

Naturally, the guards present, realizing something was amiss, immediately gave chase to Rei.

...That said, even for well-trained guards, there was no way they could bridge the vast gap in physical ability between them and Rei.

Rei’s figure kept moving further and further ahead of the guards running down the hallway, leaving them behind.

“Damn it! I knew this, but is there really nothing we can do about this gap in physical ability!?”

Frustrated by the gap in ability between Rei and themselves, the guards shouted in exasperation even as they ran desperately.

Of course, Rei could hear those shouts coming from behind him.

But right now, rather than responding to them, his priority was to go help the person whose voice he could hear ahead of him.

A few minutes had passed since Rei started running down the hallway.

Gradually, the screams and shouts reaching Rei’s ears grew louder and more intense.

It was clearly the sound of a battle, though there was also the possibility that it wasn’t a battle at all, but simply a one-sided attack.

(No, since I can still hear voices like this, it can’t be just a one-sided attack. If that’s the case, I can still make it in time!)

What Rei recalled was the figure of a man pinned to the wall in the room with the secret door, his arms and legs impaled on stakes as if displayed next to a painting.

If the goal was simply to silence him, there was no need to go that far.

If that’s the case, whoever created—or staged—that scene did it simply because they enjoyed it.

It was a contradictory existence—creating such a scene just to silence someone—but even so, looking at the wound where his throat had been slit, it was immediately clear that the perpetrator was skilled.

...Of course, there was no guarantee that the person fighting where Rei was headed was the one who had carried out the impalement.

“Found it.”

He spotted the scene of the battle ahead.

What surprised Rei slightly was that the one fighting the three guards was a woman… no, someone of an age that should be called a girl.

In this world, there are many whose appearance and strength are not proportional.

Rei himself was a prime example of that.

Still… in the battle unfolding before Rei’s eyes, a petite girl who appeared to be in her early teens was holding a longsword and holding her own—or even gaining the upper hand—against three guards… adult men. It was only natural to be surprised.

No, to be precise, it wasn’t so much that she was holding her own as it was that she was launching a one-sided attack against the guards, who were solely focused on defense and barely managing to avoid fatal wounds.

Are they holding back because their opponent is a child?

Rei couldn’t help but wonder the same thing, but from what he could see, the girl’s combat skills were undeniably superior to those of the guards.

As proof, the girl—her face twisted into a smile that could only be described as one of a killer—was about to bring her longsword down on the guard’s head…

“I won’t let you!”

He activated Nebula's Eye at his waist, generating several arrowheads.

Rei swiftly hurled the magic-forged arrows.

The launched arrows struck the longsword that was about to come crashing down on the guard’s head… diverting its path from his head to his left shoulder.

It was a blow that nearly severed his left shoulder, but there was no doubt that it was a far lighter injury than being killed by an attack to the head.

Then, the girl, her smile from a few seconds ago vanishing as she realized her attack had missed—no, had been forcibly deflected—glared at the intruder with obvious irritation… and in the next instant, immediately fled the scene.

Although the girl was confident in her own strength, perhaps she didn’t think she could go head-to-head with Rei, or maybe she had simply been told to run away if Rei showed up.

Rei didn’t know the reason, but the fact that the enemy had fled in this situation was undoubtedly a relief, as it meant he could tend to the guard’s injuries.

That’s what Rei thought, but…

“I’ll handle the treatment here, so you go after him, Rei! He’s someone who might lead us to a clue, so if possible, capture him without killing him!”

With the guard, who’d been slashed on the left shoulder, telling him that, Rei couldn’t just stop here.

He tossed a reasonably potent potion from his Misty Ring to the guard who was still unharmed, then set off in pursuit of the fleeing girl.

He could hear the guards hurriedly treating the wounded soldier behind him, but Rei deliberately ignored it and kept chasing the girl.

“Don’t come any closer!”

The girl must have noticed Rei chasing her as well. She turned around and quickly hurled the longsword she was holding at Rei.

But such a half-hearted throw… and especially a longsword, which isn’t even meant for throwing, had no chance of flying accurately toward Rei.

The longsword spun through the air and flew off in a completely different direction, even without Rei making any special effort to dodge it.

(It’s true that it’s hard to run while holding a longsword, but if she throws it, she won’t have a weapon to use when I catch up to her.)

Or, there’s a possibility he’s carrying a dagger or something similar as a backup weapon, but even so, there’s no doubt that a longsword would be more advantageous when fighting Rei.

(Well, if he’s going to mess up his own attacks, that’ll make things easier for me… right?!)

The spearhead is thrown again.

However, the problem is that we’re inside a mansion.

In other words, the hallway doesn’t run straight; there are plenty of places where it bends and turns.

That’s exactly why the arrowhead Rei threw doesn’t hit the girl as she turns the corner, but instead embeds itself in the wall.

“I won’t let her get away!”

He quickly chases after the girl, rounding the corner of the hallway... At the same time, Rei catches sight of a door closing a short distance away.

But even after moving to the door, Rei doesn’t make a move to open it right away.

The fact that the door had closed right before Rei's eyes was clearly suspicious.

In fact, it wouldn’t be surprising if some kind of trap were to activate the moment Rei opened it.

That was precisely why Rei stopped in his tracks.

That said, just standing silently in front of the door wouldn’t allow him to catch the girl inside.

Unsure of what to do, Rei eventually reached out gently toward the door… and opened it, ready to react immediately to whatever might happen.

He had expected that an arrow or something similar might fly out at any moment, but when he opened the door, there was no one there—not even the girl.
