While Rei and the others were making their way through the underground facility, Set was running wild at the entrance to the facility.

"Dammit! Take down that griffin! Attack its wings and bring it down to the ground! That's our only chance!"

The one shouting this was the highest-ranking person among the Jaya present.

He was the one who had sensed the enemy had directly invaded the underground facility and was leading the reinforcements who had come to aid them.

But few could respond to the man's orders.

After all, those present had come specifically to attack the rear of Rei and the others who had breached the underground facility.

Inside the underground facility, direct attacks were more effective than ranged weapons—to avoid mutual destruction—so few had brought bows.

Moreover, even those with bows weren't professionals, so the arrows they shot were easily dodged by Set.

The others were throwing stones lying around, but naturally, none seemed likely to hit Set. On the contrary...

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set let out a high-pitched cry in midair, and ten ice arrows materialized around him.

"A Griffin skill?! Dodge! Everyone, dodge!"

A man who noticed shouted, but in the midst of their desperate attack, there was no way they could escape immediately... The next instant, the ten ice arrows struck the men.

"Gyaaah!"

"Guh!"

"Ow!"

Not all the ice arrows struck the men, but several still suffered injuries.

Some suffered a single blow, like an eyeball pierced by an ice arrow, and died instantly.

Amidst such fierce combat, some took advantage of Set flying overhead and attempted to leap into the underground facility through the entrance.

What made Set troublesome was his ability to fly. And the fact that he used skills a griffin normally couldn't employ, attacking them one-sidedly from a place beyond their reach.

Given that, once inside the underground facility, Set should have been unable to attack or pursue them.

Well, if he landed on the ground, he might be able to pursue them, but that would mean abandoning his greatest advantage: the ability to fly.

He probably figured that if he did that, he might be able to handle even a Rank A monster.

At the point they were using skills, any adventurer would likely recognize them as a rare species, equivalent to the rank above—a rank S monster. But the ones here weren't adventurers.

That's precisely why they couldn't fully grasp the situation.

Those attempting to enter the underground facility entrance were suddenly engulfed in flames unleashed from above, their bodies consumed.

Luckily... or rather, unluckily, the power of Set's Fire Breath wasn't as high as the flames handled by Rei.

They weren't reduced to charcoal in one blow, but instead suffered the torment of being burned alive.

The woman who happened to be shielded from the flames by her companions tried desperately to enter the building... but the next instant, she was seized by Set's front paws as he descended rapidly, flapping his wings. The very next moment, she was already in the sky.

Set could easily carry even the corpse of a giant bear.

Carrying a single woman posed almost no burden at all.

"Huh... What?"

The momentary sensation of floating left her disoriented, unsure where she was. Yet, seeing the hooked claws gripping her torso... and feeling the wind against her cheek, she realized where she was.

"Kya... KYAAAAAAAAAAHHH!"

Even as she screamed, her hand reflexively reached for the sheath of the dagger at her waist. She hadn't seen as much combat as the elite guards who had initially defended this place, but she had survived her share of battles nonetheless.

But before her hand could draw the dagger, that sensation of floating returned.

What made this sensation fundamentally different from before was... Set wasn't holding her anymore. She'd been released from a height of over ten meters.

The woman, falling straight toward the ground, struggled frantically to regain her balance despite the confusion.

Under normal circumstances, she might have managed to regain her balance.

But having just been grabbed and carried through the air by Set, her mind was too confused to move her body normally.

Moreover, she wasn't even on solid ground—she was suspended in midair, where movement was already difficult.

As a result...

"Nooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooo

Unable to regain her balance in midair, the woman's forced movement only altered her falling trajectory. Consequently...

"Whoa! Hey, don't come over here! Don't come over here!"

The man who had rushed here with her screamed, desperately trying to avoid her.

However, the woman didn't stop flailing in midair. By chance, she changed course toward the direction the man had dodged... ultimately causing both of them to collide while screaming.

Though the fall was only from about ten meters high, the woman's weight, amplified by her leather armor and equipment, proved a deadly weapon, inflicting fatal damage on the man.

Both of them collapsed to the ground, screaming.

Whether it was good luck or bad luck, both the woman and the man were still alive.

However, they had sustained injuries that could easily be called life-threatening, making it utterly impossible for either to return to this fight.

For these two, the worst was yet to come.

Unable to move due to the pain, the battle raged on around them.

Allies ran frantically around them, weapons flew through the air, and various attacks—like the ice arrows and wind arrows Set had unleashed—rained down on their surroundings.

...As a result, unable to move, the man and woman were caught up in the battle and lost their lives.

"Dammit! Throw anything! Anything you can get your hands on! Knock that griffin down to the ground!"

The man in command shouts, his voice laced with frustration.

But those already holding bows were attacking until their arrows were nearly gone, and there weren't exactly countless things lying around that were just right for throwing.

Of course, this was Mejogo, a place truly worthy of being called Jaya's domain.

If they moved just a little away, replenishing arrows would be possible, and even supplementing the few bows currently in use wouldn't be difficult.

But they couldn't do that.

Because Set prioritized attacking anyone trying to enter the underground facility or escape from here.

The fighters understood they were in a death trap, yet they couldn't bring themselves to flee.

...Especially since they belonged to the criminal organization known as Jaya.

If any of them tried to escape alone, even if they managed to survive here, it was easy to imagine what their life would become afterward.

"GRRRRRR!"

Set let out a high-pitched Mejogo... as if demanding it echo throughout the entire battlefield... The moment that sound reached their ears, every movement of those present froze.

Set's skill: King's Intimidation.

That roar possessed the power to intimidate and immobilize opponents of lower rank than Set.

Of course, not everyone is completely immobilized, but this time, everyone was frozen in place.

"Eh... ah..."

The man who had been giving orders tried to say something.

But neither his body... nor his mouth could move. He could only watch silently as Set opened his massive beak wide in midair... and unleashed a fire breath from within.

"Don't go over there! Stay in a safe place for now!"

"Sura, where the hell is a safe place?! If we send them to a building, it might get destroyed!"

One of the Resistance members shouted this at Sura, who was giving orders.

Indeed, even from a distance, it was clear that buildings near where Set was rampaging were being destroyed.

Given that, taking shelter in a building seemed like nothing but a mistake.

But Sura shouted back at her comrade.

"It's fine! He's only rampaging near the city center. If you're going to evacuate, move to the outskirts, not the center! Everyone, guide them that way!"

The fortunate thing for Sura's resistance group was that while the slave collars Jaya used could forcibly alter the wearer's will, they couldn't command them to resist or attack resistance fighters like Sura and her group.

Or perhaps it wasn't that they couldn't command them, but rather that they underestimated the resistance and deliberately chose not to issue orders. Either way, it was undoubtedly a relief for Sura and the others right now.

That said, it wasn't exactly a trade-off, but the evacuation was barely progressing.

It was currently daytime... a little past noon, but for the prostitutes who slept in the early morning, it was the time for deep slumber... by normal people's standards, it was a time close to the dead of night.

Most of the brothel's residents were asleep, with only a handful awake.

In this situation, they had to rouse the residents of the brothel in the city center and evacuate them.

It's simply impossible for the current number of resistance fighters to accomplish.

And what's more...

"Who the hell do you think you are, bossing us around?! I'll kill you!"

The guards dispatched from Jaya, prepared for when customers caused trouble, intervened.

Well, from the guards' perspective, they'd suddenly heard a deafening roar, and then these intruders had appeared out of nowhere, rousing the prostitutes and ordering them to evacuate.

For those tasked with guarding the brothel, the question was whether they could trust such people.

The timing was just too perfect, which probably made the guards suspicious.

In truth, it was precisely because Sura and the Resistance knew about this attack in advance that they could react so swiftly. The guards' judgment wasn't wrong.

But there was one, fatal miscalculation...

"Shut up. If you keep this up, you'll get caught up in that chaos and die too, you know? Just follow our orders and evacuate."

As she spoke, Sharia drove her fist into the guard's solar plexus.

Caught off guard by the sudden action, the man who had been yelling at Sura collapsed, unconscious, without uttering a word.

The other guards watching this scene were too stunned by the sudden act to utter a sound.

The guards in charge of this establishment all possessed roughly equal strength.

In other words, seeing the man taken down without warning, they instantly understood they had no chance against Sharia.

Amidst this, several guards noticed the slave collar around Sura's neck.

Strictly speaking, it was merely an imitation designed to look like one, but expecting the guards to tell the difference was impossible.

And Sharia also wore the same slave collar around her neck.

Of course, Sharia's slave collar, like Sura's, was also just a fake.

As a wolf beastman, she absolutely refused to wear such a collar normally. However, since they had to operate within Mejogo during daylight hours this time, if Sharia moved around without the collar, someone would inevitably notice.

Therefore, despite her reluctance, Sura managed to persuade her.

In fact, thanks to the slave collars, Sura and Sharia managed to successfully lead the people inside the building to another location without arousing any particular suspicion from the guards.

After that, they headed toward brothels and taverns near where fighting was likely occurring. They roused the sleeping patrons and took them directly to buildings on the outskirts of Mejogo—places where they wouldn't be harmed.

Naturally, such actions could easily draw attention, but despite the ongoing fighting, it was still daytime within Mejogo. Most people were asleep, so it hadn't caused a commotion.

"This way! Come this way! It's dangerous to go toward the city center!"

Among the awakened prostitutes, some offered to help Sura and the others.

Though their wills were bent by slave collars, forcing them into prostitution against their will, their very natures hadn't changed.

Some naturally offered to help those who had come to seek refuge.

(Fortunately, it doesn't seem like Mejogo itself will vanish completely, as I'd feared beforehand... but please, let's end this as quickly as possible.)

Sura gazed at the griffin Set flying through the sky, silently wishing for that.
