"...Huh? You want to invite me to hunt Gamelion?"

Several days after informing Daskar about the dungeon's conquest, Rei was eating breakfast at the Dusk Wheat inn as usual.

He had been planning to pick up the Gamelion meat he'd requested from the guild sometime around today when an adventurer who asked to share his table said that.

"Ah. I hear Set can fly, so what could be better for finding a Gamelion? Plus, I hear you're a renowned adventurer with the strength to take one down solo."

"What do you say?" asked the man, who looked to be in his mid-twenties and sported a scruffy beard.

Whether he was staying at the Dusk Wheat inn or had come specifically for the dining hall was unclear, but at least from his appearance, he didn't look like an adventurer who could afford to stay at the Dusk Wheat inn.

Then again, if you were to point that out, Rei himself looked like nothing more than a novice mage—short in stature and certainly not built for strength.

If you knew what kind of person Rei was, it might be different, but knowing nothing, some might find it hard to believe Rei was staying at the Dusk Wheat inn.

This was especially true for those who had just arrived in Gilm and were seeing Rei for the first time... Yet, to Rei, the man now inviting him to hunt Gamelion didn't seem to be fooled by appearances.

But in another sense, you could say the unshaven man before him lacked discernment.

(Well, I only reported the Gamelion matter to the guild; it wasn't widely publicized, so maybe it's not strange? Though being out of the loop on info is a bit disappointing for an adventurer...)

Rei pondered this while watching the man eating bread and waiting for his reply.

He opened his mouth to refuse...

"Besides, you've already gotten plenty of Gamelion meat, haven't you? So, wouldn't it be okay to help those guys out?"

"Those guys?"

He tilted his head at the man's suggestive words, pausing his refusal and urging him to continue.

The man smiled faintly, set down the bread he was holding, and spoke.

"You brought the people who want to move to Gilm, didn't you?"

"...So that's what this is about."

"Whoa, I'm not trying to threaten you or anything. It's just... I heard some adventurers were talking about going to look for Gamelion, so I figured I'd help them out."

Even as he said this, he anticipated that telling Rei this story would make her highly likely to cooperate... no, he had likely brought up the topic precisely because he was aiming for that.

He was counting on the fact that searching for Gamelion from the air would make it easier to find.

"What do you say? Since I'm the one who brought those guys here, shouldn't I at least lend a hand?"

"...Even if I were to help hunt Gamelion, what's in it for me? Sure, I brought those guys here, but I'm not exactly their guardian or anything."

"Hey, hey, that's cold. Don't you have any comradeship? Well, I guess it's understandable since you're an adventurer. Fine, then. How about this?"

The man pulled something from the leather pouch at his waist. At first glance, it looked like nothing more than cheese.

"Cheese?"

"Yeah, cheese."

"Look, I don't really need cheese or anything..."

You can buy that at any shop around here.

Rei was about to say that with exasperation, but the man cut him off, thrusting the cheese toward him.

"Well, complain after you eat it."

"...It's not poisoned, is it?"

"Well, who knows? Maybe it is poisoned... but are you afraid to eat it?"

Rei hesitated for a moment at the man's provocative words, but reached for the cheese.

He was confident that if it were an ordinary poison, he'd know the moment it touched his tongue. He was also confident that his body, forged by the combined technical prowess of the Zepairu clan, wouldn't be affected by ordinary poisons.

And above all, he found himself deeply intrigued by the cheese before him.

"If you insist, I'll eat it. But if this turns out to be just some cheap cheese you find anywhere, you'd better be prepared."

"As you wish."

His shrug at the threatening words radiated confidence.

Wondering what gave the man such confidence, Rei brought the cheese to his mouth.

But... the moment he put the cheese in his mouth and chewed, his movement stopped.

A rich umami flavor spread across his tongue.

Then the cheese's intense aroma spread throughout his mouth and escaped through his nose.

He wanted to savor it forever. This cheese, which made him feel that way, could easily be called the most delicious cheese Rei had ever tasted... even compared to everything he'd eaten in Japan.

Rei didn't have particularly sharp taste buds.

He could recognize delicious food as such, but he didn't possess the kind of knowledge to explain why it was so delicious—like the origins of ingredients or anything like that. He could only describe it as delicious.

And yet... no, precisely because he was like that, Rei could honestly voice his reaction to the cheese he'd just tasted.

"Delicious."

A simple word.

And precisely because it's so simple, it's overflowing with pure, honest feeling.

"Right?"

"...This is the first time I've ever had cheese like this. Where's it from? I've never tasted cheese this good, even among the ones sold in stores."

"Heh heh heh. Well, of course. This is a special cheese made at my family home. It's labor-intensive, so we can't make much of it. It's only distributed to nobles and major trading houses we have contracts with in advance."

"So, you mean it's impossible to get normally?"

"Exactly. Now, back to what we were talking about earlier. If you help us take down Gamelion, I'll pay you with this block of cheese as your reward. But you'll have to give up on Gamelion. What do you say?"

It was the most delicious cheese Rei had ever tasted.

Receiving it as a reward meant Rei had no complaints.

Still, he didn't nod immediately. He fixed his gaze on the man before him and spoke.

"Before I answer, there are a few things I'd like to ask."

The gaze Rei shot with those words was sharp, sending a chill down the man's spine.

Still, he couldn't back down here. He urged Rei to continue.

"Fine. Ask me anything. I'll answer what I can."

He was determined to draw Rei into this Gamelion matter at any cost. To the man consumed by that resolve, Rei's first question was...

"You mentioned a chunk of cheese. How big exactly was it?"

It was about the cheese he would receive as payment.

Of course, it was about the reward he himself would receive. It was only natural to ask for details about that, but even so, it must have been completely unexpected for the man.

He'd expected a different kind of question, given how seriously Ray had asked.

That made him feel a bit let down, but asking about the cheese chunk also meant Ray was interested.

Choosing to interpret it positively, the man replied.

"Well... would it help if I said it's about the right size for Rei to carry and lift?"

"That's a bit vague. Like, about half the size of this table?"

The tables in the dining hall of Dusk Wheat inn were rectangular. A standard-sized person could sit across from another, with two more seated on either side, making a total of six people.

"Well, it's definitely not that big. Hmm... maybe about a quarter of this table?"

"...I see. That's still quite large."

It wasn't enough to eat to your heart's content, but it was certainly a substantial amount.

Of course, that was only because it was Rei and Set.

Cheese is typically eaten in small portions.

Like pairing it with wine, cut into small pieces.

"Right? I'll use this as payment. Will you take on the Gamelion case?"

"Well, I suppose I could take it... but one last question. If this cheese you're giving me isn't sold on the market at this quality, it should be worth a hefty sum. More than what you could earn in Gamelion, in fact."

The guild's purchase prices for materials are usually fixed, but exceptions are made for urgent situations.

Like when Gamelion supplies are low due to this dungeon run, or recently with materials for the medicine against the demonic fever Rei was sent to gather.

But... even so, the price is different for cheese that isn't even sold on the market, the kind only nobles and major merchants can afford.

From Rei's perspective, it seemed like the man before her was getting the raw end of the deal.

That's precisely why she wondered if there might be some shady circumstances behind it.

Especially since all the prospective settlers had lived in the Bestir Empire before. Could a faction hostile to the Daskar have orchestrated some scheme? Even someone as clueless as Rei wouldn't be unreasonable to think that.

Yet at Rei's words, the man inexplicably hid his cheeks and averted his gaze.

Yes, as if trying to hide his embarrassment.

(...Huh?)

Completely caught off guard by this unexpected reaction, Rei inwardly questioned it.

Still, he didn't want to keep staring at a man with stubble and flushed cheeks. He asked bluntly.

"You... you're not plotting something shady or anything, are you...?"

"Aaaahhhhh, yeah! That's exactly it! I fell head over heels for that Johanna chick at first sight!"

The man slammed his fist on the table and shouted.

Despite the bustling cafeteria, his voice echoed strangely loud throughout the room.

‘...’

For a moment... no, several seconds, the cafeteria was undoubtedly enveloped in silence.

Realizing this, the man glared at those around him who were staring as if to say, "Got a problem with that?"

Those he glared at quietly averted their eyes. ...Though they still had smiles on their lips.

But unlike those around him, Rei stared at the man with a somewhat exasperated look.

He naturally remembered the woman named Johanna.

And why wouldn't he? She was the woman who doted on Set more than anyone else in the guerrilla squad, leaving an incredibly strong impression on Rei.

She was the one Rei felt strongly must never be introduced to Mireine, who also doted on Set... perhaps even adored him.

"...Yeah, well, I get how you feel. It's gotta be tough, but hang in there."

"Don't look at me with those kind eyes! Don't look at me like that!"

The man shouted at Rei's words.

At the cafeteria entrance, Lana peered out and sent the man a gaze filled with compassion.

Noticing this, the man fell silent, saying no more.

Inside the cafeteria, conversations gradually resumed as the man fell silent.

But at the table where Rei and the man sat, the silence lingered for a while longer.

After about five minutes of silence... Rei decided it was time and spoke up.

"So, getting down to business... Well, if you say there's nothing shady about it, I don't mind helping out. Actually, considering how delicious that cheese was, I'd say I want to help out."

At Rei's words, a smile returned to the man's face, which had looked displeased just moments before.

Watching that smile, Rei hesitated, unsure whether to speak or not. Still, he decided it was better than staying silent and finding out later, and opened his mouth.

"I don't know if you're aware, but Johanna adores Set. Like, she even says things like why wasn't she born a griffin herself. So, what do you think would happen if you brought Set to someone like that? She'd definitely become obsessed with him and stop paying you any attention, right?"

"Yeah, probably. But to find Gamelion, getting help from Set, who can fly, is still the best option. Plus, if I show her me fighting Gamelion, Johanna might take an interest in me too."

The fact he could state that so confidently meant this man was likely a Rank C adventurer himself, either operating solo or as part of a Rank C party.

(Looking at him without preconceptions, you can tell he's physically fit. You can see it in even his minor movements. Hmm...)

Knowing the man before him possessed considerable strength piqued Rei's interest. He extended his hand.

"Alright, if you're okay with it, I'll cooperate. Again, I'm Rei, a Rank B adventurer."

"Alright. That's a huge help! I'm Rent, a Rank C adventurer. Nice to meet you!"

He firmly shook the offered hand, and thus, Rei's plans for the day were set: Gamelion hunting.
