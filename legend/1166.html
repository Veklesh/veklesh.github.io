"I see. Several goblin leaders. That means a considerable number of goblins were heading toward Gilm, then."

After hearing Rei's explanation, Lenora muttered gloomily.

Currently, the guild was filled with the sight of numerous adventurers, and a buzz of activity filled the air.

But what made it different from usual was that the buzz wasn't the usual excitement after finishing a job; it was mixed with unease.

Many who had taken requests and ventured outside Gilm had seen swarms of monsters, including goblins, with their own eyes.

In fact, much of the guild's knowledge about monster swarms came from reports by such individuals.

Of course, information also came from sources other than adventurers, such as merchants and travelers.

It was precisely because of information gathered from such sources that a goblin horde heading towards Gilm had been discovered, leading to the dispatch of adventurers for interception.

"I knew you'd be fine, Rei... but you encountered the kobold leader too, right?"

Kenny, listening to the report beside Lenora, looked at Rei with concern. "Are you okay?"

She probably believed Rei would be fine given his skill, but her worry stemmed from being a lovesick maiden.

"That part was fine. It wasn't just me; Vihera was there too. ...Speaking of which, where's Vihera?"

"Unfortunately, Ms. Vihera hasn't returned yet. ...That's strange. She should be back by now."

Vihera had headed for a goblin horde closer than the one Rei had gone to.

Thinking about it that way, it wouldn't have been strange for her to finish the fight and return by now.

Even so, Rei didn't harbor any thoughts that Vihera might have...

He understood Vihera's strength and knew she could handle any number of goblins.

Of course, it was true that even Vihera had physical limits.

Even though he didn't wear armor like ordinary warriors and didn't wield weapons like longswords, spears, or hammers, if he kept fighting, his stamina would eventually run out.

That's precisely why he traveled with other adventurers—to prevent that from happening—so there was no need for worry.

(It's unclear if goblins can judge human beauty or ugliness, but even setting that aside, Vihera is undeniably a tempting target for them.)

If the mother is exceptional, the goblins born to her will naturally possess high abilities.

In that sense, Vihera must be a mother body goblins would kill to have.

(Well, just because goblins want Vihera doesn't mean Vihera will become exactly that, though.)

Vihera's strength is not something that can be overcome no matter how many goblins gather.

That thought lingered within Rei.

And then... as if reading Rei's thoughts, Vihera appeared the moment the guild doors opened.

The only difference from usual was that several adventurers followed in Vihera's wake.

And they weren't just walking alongside her; they moved as if they were Vihera's attendants.

But Vihera paid no particular attention to those following behind her. Spotting Rei inside the guild, she smiled and approached.

"Well, well, Rei. You're early."

"I've got Set with me, after all. ...More importantly, who are those adventurers?"

As Rei asked, glancing at the adventurers behind Vihera, she let a wry smile touch her lips.

"I helped them out a bit during their fight with the goblins. Then..."

...this happened.

As Vihera explained, the men behind her frantically shook their heads.

"It wasn't just a little help! If left to our own devices, we would have been wiped out by those goblins! Since you saved us, we want to repay the favor!"

Their voices echoed through the guild.

The volume was loud enough that other adventurers nearby, who had been observing Rei and Vihera, naturally heard it too.

"Ah... yeah, I see. Well, alright."

Though the passionate words felt stifling to Rei, she somehow understood the men's personalities and simply replied with that short acknowledgment.

The adventurers probably had their thoughts about Rei's attitude, but they likely knew about his relationship with Vihera. They said nothing more and fell silent.

"So, how did it go for you, Rei?"

"Nothing much. There were a lot of goblins, but we took care of them all with magic and Set's skills."

"...I suppose so. At times like this, I envy your power, Rei. For us, the fighting was one thing, but the real hassle was dealing with the goblin corpses."

Vihera muttered with obvious disgust, and Rei understood exactly what she meant.

Rei had incinerated his group with magic, but Vihera couldn't use magic.

That meant she had to gather and dispose of the hundred corpses that made up the goblin horde.

(Ah, I see. That's another reason why Vihera took so long to return.)

By any normal measure, dealing with a hundred goblins was incredibly troublesome and labor-intensive.

Moreover, since multiple groups were heading towards Gilm, it was possible he had to deal with several groups, just like Rei.

Thinking about it that way, you could even say Vihera returned rather quickly.

"So, what should we do now?"

After hearing Vihera's account, Rei turned his gaze to Lenora, wondering what they should do now. Lenora, however, offered Rei a slightly weary smile before speaking.

"I think we can do nothing today. ...Honestly, I don't think anything major will happen that would require Rei and Vihera to go out... I hope nothing happens."

Rei, whose exploits were worthy of being called one-man armies, and his comrade, Vihera.

If these two were called out, it would naturally mean a major incident had occurred.

At the very least, given the current situation, it would undoubtedly involve a horde of monsters.

While she hoped such a situation wouldn't arise, Lenora didn't forget to add one final remark.

"But... just maybe... just maybe... I can't say the possibility of something happening is absolutely zero. ...No, given the current situation, the possibility of something happening is actually very high. So, it would really help us if you could stay somewhere where we can reach you immediately."

"Ah, that's fine. Both Rei and I are staying at the Dusk Wheat inn. ...Well, we have separate rooms, though."

Staying at the same inn.

The moment he heard that, Kenny's expression turned dissatisfied.

Ideally, Kenny would want to stay at the same inn as Rei... no, more than that, he even wanted to have Rei stay at his own house.

But the former was difficult on his salary, and the latter was impossible for various reasons.

Being a guild receptionist meant she earned significantly more than others her age.

The actual work wasn't as easy as others might think, but even accounting for that, she earned a high salary.

Even with such a high salary, she couldn't afford a room at the Dusk Wheat inn.

Well, for one or two nights, it would be a bit extravagant, but not impossible.

But could she afford it long-term... enough to make it her regular lodging? The answer was no.

If she were an adventurer, a fairly high-ranking one at that, staying at the Dusk Wheat inn as a regular lodging would be possible.

(An adventurer, huh... But I haven't even received proper combat training. And joining a guild in the frontier of Gilm would be difficult anyway.)

For a moment, Kenny envisioned himself wielding a longsword... and standing beside Rei. But reality quickly snapped him back.

Working here as a receptionist for several years.

During that time, he had seen countless adventurers fail to return from their quests.

Because of that, Kenny probably knew better than most adventurers themselves just how dangerous their work truly was.

Lenora glanced at Kenny's expression and promptly nodded back at the two before her.

"Understood. I'll contact you if anything comes up. Take care."

After receiving Lenora's farewell, Rei and Vihera, having collected their reward for this quest, left the guild.

The adventurers who had been saved by Vihera followed after them... leaving only Lenora and Kenny behind.

Strictly speaking, of course, they weren't alone. Many adventurers filled the guild, and in the tavern, those who had returned after completing the goblin swarm extermination request were drinking.

Inside the counter area, guild staff bustled about their various tasks.

Still, Lenora and Kenny couldn't help but feel like they were the only ones left behind.

Exhaling softly, Lenora tried to shift her mood.

She decided she couldn't get any real work done like this, and acted on that judgment.

After shifting her mood, she rolled up the papers she was holding and tapped Kenny, who still looked disappointed.

"Yikes! Hey, Lenora. What are you doing all of a sudden?"

"It's not 'what are you doing,' is it? Right now, we need to work instead of spacing out like that. ...Here."

Kenny was about to say something else, but when he looked in the direction Lenora was staring... there was his boss, glaring at him.

If he stayed like this, he'd get scolded.

Realizing this, Kenny hurriedly returned to his work.

Of course, Lenora wasn't about to let Kenny get ahead of her.

After all, what good would it do to warn Kenny if she ended up getting scolded herself?

(Still... what on earth is happening? If it were just goblins, I could understand them forming a mob and causing trouble like this... but this? Including all sorts of different monsters?)

Lenora felt uneasy, but she believed that if the adventurers in Gilm joined forces... and with Daskar, who ruled this land, present, they could overcome a challenge of this magnitude. With that thought, she returned to her work.

"So, I hear a goblin horde tried to attack Gilm?"

"Yeah. A merchant caravan I know spotted the goblin horde."

"But they're just goblins, right? So there's no way the adventurers of Gilm would lose... I hope."

"I know the adventurers in Gilm are skilled, but still... facing a hundred or two hundred goblins alone..."

The dining hall on the first floor of the Dusk Wheat inn.

The merchants conversing there fell silent upon seeing Rei and Vihera appear at that very moment.

They were merchants well-versed in intelligence. Naturally, they knew the extent of Rei's power and understood the feats he'd accomplished during the war against the Bestir Empire.

They also possessed intelligence that the woman accompanying Rei—who at first glance looked like nothing more than a prostitute or dancer—was a highly skilled adventurer herself.

With these two, even hundreds... thousands... tens of thousands of goblins wouldn't be a problem.

That thought crossed his mind.

...And that thought was not wrong.

In fact, Rei, who specialized in wide-area annihilation magic, was confident he could handle tens of thousands of goblins. Vihera, too, would kill every last goblin without hesitation, as long as her stamina held out.

Though the merchants in the dining hall turned their eyes toward them, they were accustomed to being the object of curious stares.

Ignoring the stares from those around them, they made their way to an empty table.

Almost immediately, Lana arrived.

They hired waitresses during busy times like mornings and evenings, but it was still early evening, not yet the rush hour.

That was precisely why Lana had come to take their order.

"You're back early today."

As Lana placed ice-cold water in front of Rei and Vihera using a magic item, Vihera smiled and spoke.

"Yes, I just finished a goblin extermination request. I couldn't be bothered to do anything else after that."

A goblin extermination request.

At those words, Lana's expression showed a hint of surprise.

She didn't raise her voice, likely because she was the proprietress of the high-class inn, the Dusk Wheat inn.

"That must have been... quite a lot to handle. Please rest now."

"Yes, I'll do just that. Thank you."

Vihera smiled and expressed her gratitude, and Rei followed suit... Soon after, they ordered an early dinner to ease their fatigue.
