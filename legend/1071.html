"...Guess there really was no point in me coming here after all."

Though Rei had been released outside the barrier, Optis was still rampaging with glee in the direction of his gaze.

Rampage, unstoppable, slaughter... Various words flashed through Rei's mind, but as he tried to voice them, his own past actions came back to him, and he ultimately remained silent.

"No, there was meaning in coming here, right? You can't enter or leave this barrier's protective field without a Dark Elf."

"...Ah, right. That's true. If the Dark Elf hadn't been here, I would've been trapped inside the settlement."

While Rei could probably force his way out of the barrier's protection if he pushed himself, doing so would place a renewed burden on the World Tree, which had only just recovered.

The World Tree was already weakened, having had its magic power and sap absorbed by that giant cicada.

...Worse still, it had even seemed on the verge of withering away completely at one point.

Even though Rei's magic forcibly restored it, it still couldn't be called fully recovered.

While its magic power was replenished by Rei's, the sap—essentially the World Tree's bodily fluid or blood—had been absorbed in vast quantities by the giant cicada.

Furthermore, though Rei was unaware, the roots of the World Tree itself had been ravaged when the giant cicada was still a larva.

This meant the World Tree was currently only managing to stay in a somewhat healthy state through Rei's magic alone. Placing any further burden on it could be considered fatal.

Given that, there was no doubt that Rei's arrival at this very place held significant meaning.

Right beside Rei, the light sphere flickered as if expressing dissatisfaction.

This orb was essentially the will of the World Tree itself.

Naturally, it could easily have allowed Rei through the barrier's protective field, and it had intended to do so.

But in the end, it was the Dark Elf who passed through the barrier's protective sphere, and now the sphere could do nothing else.

"Huh? What... is that? Somehow, it feels strangely familiar."

Noticing the flickering orb drifting near Rei, the dark elf man asked while shooting arrows at the goblin trying to escape Optis's threat.

"Ah... yeah. Kind of."

Rei couldn't very well declare it the will of the World Tree right here, so he fell silent.

If he even hinted at such a thing here, the dark elves would undoubtedly start a commotion.

With monsters attacking right now, that would only lead to chaos. He wanted to avoid that if possible.

(Well, knowing it was the World Tree's will might boost morale...)

Still, weighing whether morale or avoiding chaos was more important in the current situation, Rei ultimately chose the option that wouldn't cause chaos.

If Optis weren't here, he might have chosen the other option.

But on this battlefield, Optis was currently rampaging freely. There was no need to deliberately choose the path that risked causing chaos.

Deciding his presence here was pointless, and also to divert the conversation, Rei called out to the Dark Elf shooting arrows.

"The barrier's been redeployed, so I'm not needed here anymore. Do you know of any places short on manpower?"

"Hard to say. We're all clustered together fighting like this, so I have no idea what other areas are like... Whoa!"

Rei nodded in understanding at the Dark Elf's words, who had just swiftly taken down five goblins in rapid succession.

"If no reinforcements are requested, does that mean things are under control over there?"

"Yeah. So, the fact that no reinforcements are being requested probably means everyone's holding their own against the monsters, right?"

Laying his bow to his shoulder as he explained, Rei thought for a moment before speaking.

"Got it. Well, there's no point staying here. I'll circle around the perimeter through the barrier for now. If I find any areas in trouble, I'll jump in to help."

"That would be a huge help to us too."

The Dark Elf speaking with Rei here had also participated in last night's battle in the forest.

And he had seen with his own eyes just how much power Rei possessed.

Given that, the thought that Rei's power might be insufficient... simply didn't occur to them.

Rather, they judged that with Rei's power, even a battlefield on the verge of total annihilation could be easily turned around.

"Don't worry about it. This is my job too."

Healing the World Tree and resolving the forest's anomaly were Marina's requests.

Defeating the giant cicada, which seemed to be affecting both, and dealing with the monsters it likely summoned, was exactly what Rei wanted.

(Though if it turns out the giant cicada wasn't involved in the forest's abnormality after all, I'd be utterly humiliated.)

The anomaly causing monsters in the forest to become agitated and the current state of the monsters seemed somehow connected.

But that was merely Rei's speculation; there was no concrete evidence whatsoever.

That very uncertainty left Rei with some doubts, but he said nothing more and sprinted away from the spot.

As he did, Optis shot Rei a meaningful look, but Rei pretended not to notice.

He knew that talking to Optis now would only waste time. But more than that, he had an absolute certainty that it would lead to even more trouble.

Optis regretted losing his playmate, but as an elder, he could only feel gratitude toward Rei.

Still, he couldn't hide the boredom from his expression. For Optis, Rei was a valuable presence in many ways.

But Rei showed no concern for Optis's feelings. He began moving, tracing the barrier's perimeter with his Death Scythe in hand.

(If I use the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour... No, there might be risks in the forest. Then, for now, just stick with the monsters as they are!)

As this thought crossed Rei's mind, his gaze fell upon three kobolds running alone for some reason.

"Waaaaaah!"

"Wauwau!"

"Brrrrrrrrr!"

Their frantic cries as they ran desperately gave Rei a sense of their desperate struggle.

In truth, these three kobolds were desperately trying to escape their death trap, so what Rei sensed was not wrong at all.

Their misfortune, however, was undoubtedly encountering Rei during their escape.

Suddenly, they realized they were surrounded by goblins, orcs, ogres, and numerous other monsters, fighting desperately for their lives.

Driven by the terror that staying meant certain death, they fled desperately, joining other kobolds who shared the same thought and escaping together... only to find waiting for them the very embodiment of death itself for kobolds: a figure wielding a Death Scythe.

The moment they saw Rei running toward them at a speed they could never match... the kobolds flopped over onto their backs, exposing their bellies to him.

Their only luck was having discarded any cumbersome weapons during their flight.

Now barehanded, they rolled onto their backs, curled their tails, and averted their gaze from Rei.

It was a gesture of unconditional surrender.

...A gesture of unconditional surrender.

Rei's mind went blank for a moment in shock when the three kobolds in his path suddenly made such a gesture.

Though kobolds were monsters with dog-like faces, he never imagined their gestures would mirror those of dogs.

And yet, despite his shock, Rei kept running, passing right by the dog... no, the kobold in its surrender posture.

(Ah)

He snapped back to reality after passing them. Glancing back for just an instant, he saw the kobolds fleeing for their lives.

It was as if they believed staying here meant certain death.

In truth, that conclusion wasn't entirely wrong.

If the kobolds had still been willing to fight, Rei wouldn't have let them escape like that.

(Ah well.)

Judging by how utterly terrified the kobolds looked, they clearly felt intense fear toward humans and dark elves. Rei figured they wouldn't be attacking dark elves in the forest anytime soon.

Rei reasoned that it was fine to leave it be for now, and he lost interest in the kobold rapidly disappearing into the distance and turned his gaze forward.

After running for about a minute, the sounds of battle reached him from ahead.

(I thought battles might be happening everywhere along the barrier's perimeter... But actually moving around like this, it seems the fighting is concentrated in specific groups, just like that dark elf said earlier. ...Is this also the giant cicada's doing?)

Running while tilting his head in confusion, Rei soon saw not just the clamor but the actual sights of battle unfolding.

While the absence of an exception like Optis likely played a role, the battlefield showed the monster and dark elf forces fighting almost evenly matched.

That said, the even match was largely due to the monsters fighting amongst themselves.

The dark elves' attacks were likely directed at those who had strayed from the monster fights.

The three kobolds Rei had encountered earlier were the ones who had managed to escape the Dark Elves' attacks while straying from the pack.

As the battlefield rapidly drew nearer, a familiar face appeared within it.

The man, with a nervous expression, was desperately directing the dark elves to minimize their own casualties while inflicting as much damage as possible on the monsters.

"Huh... not bad, Ragdo."

Yes, it was Ragdo desperately directing the fight, and the surrounding dark elves followed his orders without hesitation.

Though Ragdo's influence had waned after being outargued by Marina, seeing this scene now, no one would think that.

"Rei!? Why are you here...?"

"The World Tree's treatment... treatment? Well, anyway, we succeeded in identifying the cause of the World Tree's weakness and removing it. The World Tree has recovered considerably now, so I thought I'd deal with the monsters. Then..."

The image of Optis dominating the monsters flashed through Rei's mind.

It was truly a sight worthy of being called the hero of the Dark Elves.

...While Rei felt some doubt about a figure in the position of an elder being a hero, part of her accepted it because it was Optis.

"Then what? ...Goblins are trying to break through from the right flank! Prioritize taking them out!"

Even while conversing with Rei, he surveyed the battlefield, analyzed the situation, issued commands to the Dark Elves, and simultaneously attacked with his bow and spirit magic.

(Could Ragdo be seriously impressive?)

He'd glimpsed flashes of it before, but even so, his current performance was nothing short of a one-man army.

Perhaps because his first impression had been poor, Rei had always tended to underestimate Ragdo... or even look down on him. But seeing him now, he couldn't possibly think that way anymore.

"Rei? What's wrong?"

"Nothing. Just that Optis was fighting to his heart's content at the place I went to first."

"Ah, the Elder..."

Ragdo seemed to grasp the situation from Rei's words. He let out a small sigh before resuming giving orders to the other Dark Elves.

Watching this, Rei opened his mouth, Death Scythe in hand.

"So, do you need help? From what I can see, it looks like an even fight."

"We need it. We absolutely need it. We need it no matter what."

Rei was momentarily taken aback by Ragdo's immediate response.

He must have been wearing such a serious expression...

"Alright, calm down. So where should I attack? I can handle both ranged and close-range combat."

"Please attack from a distance. Everyone is currently focusing on ranged attacks. If you recklessly charge into the enemy lines here, it'll make our allies hesitate to attack."

"I see. Got it. ...Well then, let's go a little flashy. It'll boost morale better that way, right?"

"Well... yes, I suppose. Honestly, I'm worried that going too flashy might draw the monsters' attention to us, but with Lord Rei here..."

He no longer doubted Rei's strength.

In fact, considering just how powerful Rei truly was, Ragdo's honest feeling was sheer relief that he had arrived here at this very moment.

They were evenly matched now, but what would happen next was completely unknown.

If the monsters fought each other and reduced their numbers, it would be advantageous for them.

But if, conversely, the monsters still had enough numbers and turned their attention to them, the sheer difference in numbers would put them at a crushing disadvantage.

That Rei had arrived at such a moment meant there was nothing more reliable.

Feeling Ragdo's gaze, Rei gave a small nod before raising his Death Scythe... and swung it wide.

"Flying Slash!"

The flying slash shot straight toward the monsters, slicing through several goblins, kobolds, and orcs, or inflicting large wounds upon them.
