"W-wait! We surrender! We can't beat you! We'll cooperate fully with the interrogation, and we'll hand over all the treasure we've hoarded! So please, don't kill us!"

Shouting this, the bandit throws his weapon to the ground.

Seeing the bandit, Rei wondered what to do.

Without a single battle, the moment the bandits saw Rei appear, they shouted that and surrendered.

If it had been just one of the bandits acting that way, Rei might have accepted it.

But the man who had just surrendered in front of Rei looked, to all appearances, like a man of some standing among the bandits.

If such a figure were to suddenly surrender... it would be natural for anyone, not just Rei, to be taken aback.

(No, is this just a ruse to catch me off guard?)

He had that thought, but with the man having dropped his weapon and raised both hands, he surely couldn’t make a move to exploit any opening.

I couldn’t help but wonder if he was using himself as a decoy while someone else was aiming a bow or something from somewhere else, but as far as I could tell by sensing the area, that wasn’t the case.

“Ah… well, that was a surprisingly easy surrender. I thought you’d put up a bit more of a fight.”

“You’re Rei, the Bandit Eater, right? There’s no way we could beat you in a fight. So, we’d better just surrender. It’ll make a better impression during questioning, too.”

“You’re being quite magnanimous. Do you realize that? If things continue like this, there’s a high chance you’ll end up as slaves. And depending on the decision, there’s not zero chance you’ll be executed either.”

At Rei’s words, the bandit nodded without showing any particular sign of distress.

“Like I said before, I don’t think we can fight you and get away, let alone win. So, even though it’s not exactly fun for us, surrendering is probably the smartest choice. …So, we’d like to hand over the treasure we’ve hoarded.”

“I see. But given the current situation, if I were to enter your hideout, you might try to escape, right? Considering that, I can’t just trust you that easily.”

Bandits’ hideouts are, naturally, caves, shacks they’ve built themselves, or in some cases, holes dug into the ground.

If Rei were to go into a place like that right now, it would naturally be the perfect opportunity for the bandits to make a run for it.

Even though the bandits had surrendered the moment Rei appeared, it was only natural that they would try to escape if given the chance.

If things continued this way, even with the best of luck, they would end up as slaves… or, if things went badly, they might even face execution—exactly as Rei had said.

Given that, they certainly wouldn’t let this opportunity slip away.

“But… then what are we going to do? Are we just going to sit here quietly until someone comes?”

“No, that’s not it. …Well, since you know who I am, I’m sure you can guess.”

Still holding the Death Scythe—the very weapon that had allowed the bandits to recognize Rei at a glance—he spoke.

「Set！」

The word that escaped Rei’s lips seemed to vanish, as if swallowed up by the surrounding trees.

Then, after a dozen or so seconds…

“Ugh, whoa!”

One of the bandits let out a scream as Set suddenly appeared from between the trees.

He knew Rei had a partner named Set, but he probably never imagined he’d actually see a griffin up close.

Screaming, he backed away almost instinctively.

Seemingly oblivious to the panicked bandits, Set approached Rei, purred as usual, and rubbed his face against her.

“Sorry for calling you out on such short notice. …Anyway, I’m going to search the bandits’ hideout now, so Set, you keep an eye on them here. If they try to escape, feel free to attack them. It’d be more convenient if they stayed alive, but I don’t really need to worry too much if they die.”

Rei’s words weren’t a threat or anything of the sort; his tone made it clear he was speaking the absolute truth.

Realizing this, the bandits felt a chill run down their spines.

They understood that if they made a careless move, they would truly die.

The other bandits felt the same way and froze in their tracks.

Leaving the motionless bandits behind, Rei entered the bandits’ hideout.

It didn’t seem to be a place where they slept, but rather a storage area for looted supplies and valuables, so it wasn’t very large.

Naturally, there was no one being held captive, and it didn’t take long to gather up the items inside.

(Come to think of it, what happened to the wagon? There were no horses, so did they carry all this stuff here by hand? And besides, to move this stuff somewhere else, they’d have to transport it again. If that’s the case… no, rather than thinking about it here, it’d be faster to just ask. Hmm? Come to think of it, those scoundrels didn’t have a wagon either. Or did they hide it? And the horses, too, somewhere? …Well, whatever.)

Having made that decision, Rei quickly heads out of the hideout.

He looked for the first person who had surrendered to Rei… but there was no need to search; seeing them in the same spot as before, he approached.

“Hey. How were you guys planning to transport this when you sold it? Specifically, where’s the carriage or something?”

“Well, um… we have a cart, but no carriage.”

Slightly taken aback by the unexpected answer, Rei pauses for a moment.

“Really? So, when you go to sell it, you’re going to go out of your way to transport it in a handcart?”

“People from the underworld come to buy them from us. Most of the bandits around here usually sell off the treasures and supplies they’ve stolen that way.”

“...Huh.”

Faced with yet another unexpected revelation, Rei muttered under his breath.

Rei had assumed the bandits went out themselves to sell their loot, so the idea that merchants actually came to them was completely unexpected.

(Well, many gems and such lose value if they get scratched. So, if they think having bandits handle the transport is risky in various ways, it makes sense for the merchants to come out themselves… that’s a plausible explanation.)

Even if they operate behind the scenes, they’re still merchants at heart.

It’s only natural that they’d want to avoid, as much as possible, having expensive goods turned into cheap ones just because they were transported by bandits.

“So? When is that merchant coming next?”

In Rei’s view, since merchants who buy goods from bandits are essentially bandits themselves, he’d concluded it would be fine to capture that merchant and seize his money and goods…

“Merchants are sharp when it comes to information. Especially those in the underworld—their instincts in that regard are probably sharper than those of merchants on the surface. I think word has already gotten out that we’ve been captured, so I don’t think he’ll come here.”

“They’re a troublesome bunch.”

As for which is easier to hunt—bandits or merchants—the bandits right in front of them were proving that point with their very existence.

As he thought that far, Rei suddenly wondered if his current line of thinking was rather bandit-like… but then he quickly dismissed the thought, reasoning that since they were scoundrels themselves, it wasn’t really that strange.

Since he was doing business with them knowing full well they were criminals, that merchant must naturally be a criminal too.

“I get the gist of it. Well, since we’re done here, let’s be on our way.”

Saying that, he used a rope he pulled out of the Misty Ring to bind the bandits’ hands.

With the bandits chained together, even if one tried to escape, they couldn’t do so unless the whole group tried to escape together.

(I suppose I should tie their legs too, but it would make it hard to walk through the forest, and that would take too much time.)

That thought wasn’t out of any mercy on Rei’s part… of course not; it was purely because he found the extra time it would take to move around a hassle.

For the bandits, I suppose you could say they were lucky.

Though, of course, you couldn’t exactly call it good luck once they’d been found and captured by Rei.

(Still, these guys were really good at disguising themselves. If it weren’t for Set, I would have completely missed them.)

While leading the bandits away, Rei looked around and thought that.

In terms of the five senses, Rei’s were far sharper than those of an ordinary human.

But even with Rei’s extraordinary senses—even his vision as he scanned the forest from above on Set’s back, searching for thieves—he hadn’t been able to spot these thieves.

It was Set, whose senses were even sharper than Rei’s, who found them.

Given that he found this band of bandits using not just his sight, but his hearing and sense of smell as well, Set’s ability to detect enemies completely surpasses Rei’s.

Not only does he naturally possess senses superior to Rei’s, but whenever they camp out, Set always takes the watch.

Naturally, it stands to reason that his senses—including his sixth sense and even his ability to sense magic—would become increasingly acute.

Thanks in part to Set, Rei, having captured the bandits, heads back to the spot where he had a run-in with Tarania.

Upon emerging from the forest, several adventurers had already gathered there, along with the bandits, the people who had likely been held captive, and the treasure the bandits had hoarded.

The adventurers there had been on high alert the moment someone emerged from the forest, fearing an ambush by the bandits—or perhaps other bandits coming to steal the captured bandits or their hoard—but they breathed a sigh of relief upon realizing it was Rei and his group.

They reasoned that if they were on their own, they’d be helpless against a large group of bandits, but with Rei and his party there, they could easily handle any number of bandits that might attack.

For that reason, the man leading the adventurers waved happily at Rei and called out to him.

“Hey, looks like you guys managed to catch some bandits too.”

“Yeah. They were good at hiding, so it took some time to find them, but once we did, they surrendered without a fight. Actually, it took longer to tie them up with rope and bring them here.”

“It’s reckless to even think about fighting against Rei and Set. Still, so are all the bandits going to be sold as slaves? There are quite a few of them, so that should bring in a decent profit.”

The adventurer talking to Rei looked at him—and then at the bandits Rei was leading—with a hint of envy.

Rei had captured the bandits, and the bandits he had captured seemed to know the bandits the man had captured, as they exchanged glances with one another.

They weren’t glaring at each other or anything like that, and the fact that no trouble had broken out meant things were easier for Rei and his group to handle—which was certainly a stroke of luck.

If the bandits had started fighting among themselves here, that would have been a real headache.

“This job was actually pretty easy for us. …But the real problem is how many bandits there are. No matter how many times we defeat them, if there were already a significant number to begin with and they’ve been increasing lately, there’s a high chance we won’t be able to keep up.”

“Ah, that’s right. Considering what happened earlier, there’s no doubt a significant number of bandits are still lurking around here. Plus… was it Tarania? With that guy’s situation, it’s certain there’ll be all sorts of problems popping up in Sublusta too, right?”

Those words were an undeniable truth.

A direct subordinate of the magistrate of Sublusta had been in cahoots with the bandits.

Naturally, there’s no way his superior, the magistrate, wouldn’t know about it, and if that’s the case, the investigation would inevitably extend to the magistrate himself… and to the noble who dispatched him.

Under normal circumstances, it would be difficult for Daskar, a member of the Neutral Faction, to take on a member of the Noble Faction or his subordinate magistrate.

But this time, Elena was traveling with Rei and the others.

What’s more, she had been instructed by Duke Kerebel, the leader of the Noble Faction, not to interfere with the expansion work in Gilm.

Since she took such action knowing full well this, the price she’ll pay is sure to be high.

It is possible that the nobles were unaware of what the magistrate and Tarania were doing, but a magistrate is, after all, someone who governs a town or village as the noble’s representative.

If that representative causes a disturbance, naturally, the nobility who appointed the magistrate also bears responsibility.

(Was it ‘appointment responsibility’? I heard that term quite a bit on TV back when I was in Japan, too.)

When he was in Japan, Rei was a high school student living in the countryside and had almost no interest in politics.

However, he remembers vividly how, whenever it made the news, the opposition party would scream “accountability for appointment! accountability for appointment!” as if they’d just bagged a monster.

That’s probably why that phrase came to mind in this situation as well.

That said, for Rei, this whole affair is, after all, nothing more than a matter for the Noble Faction.

He could have predicted there would be some back-and-forth with Daskar of the Neutral Faction, but since he had no intention of getting involved—nor any need to—all he could say was, “Good luck with the trouble.”
