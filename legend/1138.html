When they saw Set growling in the empty space, the three people present—including Rei —didn't understand what he was doing at first.

It was only natural. They had left Gosh and traveled through the desert at night, searching for the assassin's hideout... or perhaps what they thought might be their base. Yet Set had stopped in the middle of nowhere.

Could there be something nearby? With that thought, Zarust and Narsus scanned their surroundings.

But all they could see from where they stood was the desert stretching out beneath the soft moonlight.

"Underground, maybe?"

Narsus suddenly wondered, glancing down at the sand, but there was no sign of an entrance.

But if the enemy's hideout was here, the only possibility was underground.

"But... how are we supposed to search for it down there? Are we gonna have Set find it for us too?"

Zalust muttered, but Narsus couldn't possibly answer that.

Watching the two of them, Rei glanced around to make sure there was nothing suspicious nearby before turning his gaze back to Set.

Catching Rei's gaze, Set rubbed his head against Rei's body, as if begging for praise.

Judging by Set's demeanor, it was clear he wasn't just unable to pick up the scent anymore; his attitude unmistakably indicated this was indeed the place they had been aiming for.

(If that's the case, the assassins' base should be here... but where is it?)

Stroking Set's head as he rubbed against him, Rei looked around curiously.

In Rei's mind, it was already a certainty that the assassins' base was here.

That's how strong Rei's trust in Set was.

But... even someone like Rei couldn't find the assassins' headquarters by simply looking around.

He couldn't help but wonder if, as Zarust and Narsus had suggested, the base was underground.

Yet, what Set was observing was not merely the sand on the ground, but the surrounding scenery.

In other words, if Set senses something, it's not underground, but in the space above ground.

(What is it? ...Something I can't perceive, but Set can sense... Magic power, perhaps? If so, is it some kind of magic item or spell making him invisible to us? Magic power... Magic power?)

After gathering his thoughts internally, Rei decided on his next course of action.

But before taking that action... clinging to a sliver of hope, he addressed Zarust and Narsus.

"Just checking, but neither of you happens to have an eye for magic, or the ability to sense it, or anything like that, right?"

At Rei's question, both shook their heads as if it were obvious.

"If we had such a rare ability, we'd be way stronger by now."

"There were a few like that before I came to Gosh, but I'm not one of them. I don't think any of the guards hired by Lord Daldora had that ability either."

"I see. So, I guess I'll have to figure it out myself. ...Not that I'm very confident about it, though."

Muttering to himself, Rei drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring—his very symbol.

Seeing Rei wield the great scythe, Zarust and Narsus each reached for their own weapons.

Zalust usually wielded a spear and a longsword, but since they were launching an assault on the assassins' hideout this time, he held only the longsword.

...He probably never imagined he'd end up outside Gosh after saying he was going to attack the hideout too.

Narsus also held a longsword.

However, the quality was visibly different from Zarust's... one might say the inherent rank of the weapon itself was distinct.

This difference was likely the result of the high-priced reward he'd received from Daldora.

Daldora's guards were originally hired from skilled adventurers across neighboring nations.

Among them, Narsus held a position commanding the guards, and naturally, the reward he received from Daldora was substantial.

"Are you ready? Whatever happens, don't let surprise make you lose your guard."

Hearing Rei's words, they must have finally understood that what was about to happen was no ordinary affair. The two silently nodded in response.

After confirming their readiness, Rei channeled his magic into the Death Scythe.

That magic was the kind a typical mage could only muster by straining their very life force.

...Though, equipped with the New Moon Ring, which concealed his magical output, even if someone could sense or see the magic, they wouldn't understand Rei's actions.

Even though an amount of magic close to the limit for an ordinary mage was being channeled, the Death Scythe remained unaffected.

If this were just some ordinary magic item lying around, it would undoubtedly have caused some sort of disturbance.

"What lies in the direction Set is looking is, at first glance, empty space. ...But the fact that Set sensed something means there is something emitting a scent... and something only Set can perceive."

The words slipped from Rei's mouth as he raised the Death Scythe.

With those words, the Death Scythe was raised...

"If it's the same magic, then with my magic... I can cut through it!"

With those words, the Death Scythe came crashing down.

True to the name of the Grim Reaper's blade, it was a strike that seemed to lead all things... and all beings to death.

The effect of that strike was overwhelming.

What should have been a desert illuminated only by the moonlight suddenly cracked open as if a fissure had appeared in space itself... and in the next instant, the space shattered with a sound like glass being dropped.

And what appeared in the shattered space was a village.

Before that village stood nearly twenty human figures.

They were ordinary-looking people, dressed not in the leather armor typical of adventurers.

That very fact made the sight of the group before the village, each wielding longswords, daggers, spears, and other weapons Rei had never seen before, seem strangely out of place.

"Just as I thought. ...I have no idea how they managed to completely hide a village inhabited by such a group from human eyes, but it seems beyond doubt that this was the assassins' base."

Since the village itself was concealed, it couldn't possibly be a temporary hideout.

As Rei spoke these words, one of the figures before the village stepped forward.

Amidst the mostly average builds of medium height and weight, this man stood out as clearly abnormal, easily exceeding two meters in height, perhaps even reaching two and a half meters.

In his hand, he gripped a chain, the end of which was attached to a massive iron ball nearly a meter in diameter, as if to demonstrate his strength.

"Well done finding this place. I am Resonance, the one leading these men. ...But what do you intend to do now that you've found this village? I didn't expect the barrier protecting it to be destroyed, but even so, if you hadn't returned, the village's existence wouldn't have been revealed."

The man calling himself Resonance appeared to be in his thirties or forties, yet his tone sounded uncharacteristically old.

Feeling a sense of incongruity at the gap between his appearance and his speech, Rei opened his mouth.

"So, this is definitely the headquarters of the assassin group, right?"

"Hmm. Even if I said no, you wouldn't believe me, would you?"

The words that came from Resonance's mouth acknowledged nothing other than Rei's statement.

Facing Resonance, Rei shifted his gaze to Set.

"My partner tracked the scent of the assassin who attacked us all the way here. So if you tell me this isn't it, I find that hard to believe. Especially since, however you did it, you managed to hide the entire village."

"Gurrrr!"

Set growled happily at Rei's words.

Rei, who trusted him this much, was an extremely important person to Set.

He understood that trust existed, but voicing it must carry significant weight.

"...A griffin, huh? You're the worst incarnation imaginable for us."

Resonance muttered this with a sigh, showing no sign of fear despite facing the Griffin.

No, that probably applied to the others as well, aside from the large Resonance speaking with Rei.

Standing before Rei and the others were about twenty individuals in total.

In terms of sheer numbers, they vastly outnumbered Rei and her group. Yet, in terms of combat strength, Rei's group, embodying the very essence of a small but elite force, clearly held the upper hand.

In other words, even though they understood that if it came down to a head-on battle, Rei and his group would undoubtedly win, there was still no trace of fear on their faces.

(What? They're not flinching at all in this situation? What's going on?)

Listening to Rei and Resonance, Zarust reached for the scabbard at his waist, ready for combat at any moment.

Facing them directly, it was impossible not to grasp that the individuals before him were abnormal.

This was true not only for Zarust, but for Narsus as well.

Both men kept their hands on their weapons, ready to draw at any moment, watching the direction of Rei and Resonance's conversation.

"You might see them as the worst possible enemies, but to me, they're reliable partners. ...Now then. Assuming you are assassins... why target both Augusto and Daldora?"

Having said that much, Rei shook his head immediately and spoke again.

"No. Targeting Daldora alone makes perfect sense. He seems to have done all sorts of despicable things. But why drag Augusto into it?"

Narsus's cheek twitched for just an instant at the bluntness of the words.

He probably wanted to argue that it was only natural Daldora, whom he was protecting, was targeted because of his own wicked deeds.

Of course, Narsus knew Daldora had been up to all sorts of shady business.

After all, handing over the sand ships to the sand pirates and having them attack Augusto was hardly something to be praised.

But... even someone like Daldora was good at looking after those he took under his wing, and above all, it was an undeniable fact that he had contributed to Gosh's development.

But to Rei, Daldora was an enemy of Augusto, someone he was only temporarily allied with.

The only reason Rei might feel any goodwill toward Daldora is probably because he gave Rei the Sand Ship.

...Though Daldora himself had absolutely no intention of giving the Sand Ship to Rei.

If anything, he felt more like he'd been robbed of something he'd lent to the Sand Pirates.

Even so, he doesn't complain... or rather, can't complain. It's probably because he can't afford to have it become public knowledge that he lent it to the Sand Pirates—though it remains an open secret among those in the know—and above all, he doesn't want to make Rei an enemy.

In this regard, he was quite different from that certain guild leader who had previously tried to acquire Set and Misty Ring.

"Involve... Hmm, involve him. I see. So that is how you feel, then."

Rei inwardly tilted his head at Resonance's expression of surprise, as if taken aback by an unexpected remark.

(What? It's like he didn't realize I was thinking that way until he said it... Could I have misunderstood something? No, but considering his actions so far...)

Holding the Death Scythe, Rei silently concluded that anything he said now would only give away information to his opponent.

Resonance, facing such a Rei, twisted the corners of his mouth into a smile.

"I see, I see. Now that you mention it, from an outsider's perspective, it wouldn't be strange for it to appear that way. My apologies. How careless of me... But do you really think I'd deliberately give away information?"

Resonance asked, still smiling. Rei, too, smiled as he opened his mouth.

"Well, you know, before I die... or something."

"Heh heh, well said. You never had any intention of dying in the first place, did you?"

Resonance clenched the chain connected to the iron ball, suppressing laughter bubbling up from deep within.

Facing such a Resonance, Rei also smiled and tightened his grip on the Death Scythe.

Clinking the chain as he pulled it closer, Resonance spoke with a smile.

"Very well. Then let's do this. Try defeating me... no, defeat us, the Hundred Faces. If you succeed, I'll tell you why we attacked Augusto too."

As he spoke, Resonance pulled the chain taut... and in the next instant, the iron ball floated into the air and began to swing.

"So it comes to this after all. Well, fine. I understood this would lead to a fight anyway. ...But since you said it, if we win, you'll tell us properly, right?"

"Ha ha ha. Do as you please... very well!"

As he shouted, Resonance swung his arm wide... and following that motion, the iron ball circling in the air lunged toward Rei.
