After finishing his final class at the academy, Rei cleaned himself up before boarding a carriage and traveling through Glacial.

Set, Rei's partner, was nowhere to be seen beside the carriage.

Rei had wanted to bring Set along, but considering where they were headed, he couldn't really insist.

Set seemed disappointed not to be able to stay with Rei, but having exercised during the mock battle probably helped relieve some of his stress. He returned quietly to the stables.

...Part of it was probably that he didn't want to burden Rei too much with his own issues.

"It's been a while since we traveled by carriage like this."

"Hmm? Do you rarely use carriages, Rei?"

Rei, sitting beside Erinde in the carriage, shot her a puzzled look at his unexpected remark.

Rei smiled faintly at that look and nodded.

"Even when I have somewhere to go, riding Set is clearly faster. Traveling by carriage is usually just when I'm moving alone like this, or for getting around town."

"How enviable. I'd love to ride Set myself."

Marka, seated across from Erinde, cast an envious glance toward Rei.

Marka had always wanted to experience what it felt like to fly through the sky, but naturally, as a duchess's daughter, opportunities to fly didn't just come her way.

Admittedly, someone as small as Marka could probably be squeezed onto Set somehow, but Coan would never allow it.

"Why not become a dragon knight? ...Or since you're good at magic, Marka, you could be a flying wizard."

That was precisely why the words that came out of Rei's mouth were almost half-joking.

Caught off guard by this seemingly random suggestion, Marka's eyes widened.

"...Young Lady?"

Coan, standing beside Marka, asked with a hint of unease.

He'd seen Marka act like this several times before.

And without fail, such moments were always followed by major upheaval.

...Like when she had gone to Gilm with almost no attendants for some reason.

Thanks to that, the Quent Duke's household gained a connection to a man named Rei, but that was merely hindsight.

Coan, who had been dragged along because of it, felt immense fatigue despite his devotion to Marka.

Recalling past hardships, Coan turned his gaze toward Marka. Yet Marka, seemingly unaware of Coan's presence, sat with a serious expression, murmuring softly to himself as he pondered something.

Inside the carriage, after that last glimpse of Marka, silence naturally filled the space without anyone saying anything... and soon, their destination came into view.

"This is... incredible."

It was Rei who broke the silence.

His gaze fell upon a remarkably massive mansion.

The largest building he had ever seen was probably the castle in the imperial capital of the Bestir Empire.

While clearly smaller than that castle, a mansion and a castle were fundamentally different things. To Rei's eyes, this was a mansion of a size he had never seen before.

Daskar's mansion in Gilm was also quite large, but even that seemed small compared to the mansion now before Rei's eyes. It couldn't even compare to the mansions in the noble district.

Then again, that was only natural. The mansion before him belonged to Duke Quent, while Daskar's... well, the Marquis of Larks held a title that was literally different in rank.

"Hm? Ah, it's not that bigger is necessarily better, you know. Using a carriage to move your own house is nothing but a hassle."

"...A carriage..."

Hearing that, Rei turned his gaze back to the mansion.

Considering the mansion's size, it made sense that moving around on foot would take time.

"Well, if it's that big..."

In Rei's mind, it was like using a car to move around the grounds where his own house stood.

Regardless, while his attention was consumed by the mansion, the carriage continued onward and arrived at the gate.

As befitted the Duke of Quent's residence, the personnel guarding the gate were truly first-rate.

Merely showing Marka's face allowed them to enter smoothly, and soon they arrived at one of the several buildings within the grounds.

"We're here. Now, Rei. I'll go fetch my father, so you wait in your room with the maid and Erinde! Let's go, Coan!"

"Wait, Miss! Wait!"

Coan chased after Marka, who had immediately dashed off after opening the carriage door.

Left behind, Rei and Erinde exchanged glances without a word and stepped down from the carriage.

There, as expected, the maid was waiting for Rei and Erinde.

"Rei, it's a pleasure to meet you. I am Cammy, the maid. I will be attending to you and Mistress Erinde during this time. Though it will be brief, I look forward to serving you."

The woman named Cammy, dressed in a maid's uniform, appeared to be in her mid-twenties. Serving the Duke of Quent household, she exuded a sense of cleanliness and had very well-defined features.

"Nice to meet you, Cammy."

Erinde, seemingly acquainted with her, said casually and started walking.

Rei followed the two of them inside the building.

"Huh..."

A glance at the room Cami led them into drew a gasp of admiration from Rei.

Numerous paintings adorned the walls, and various pieces of furniture filled the space.

Each piece was more than enough to delight the eyes of whoever waited here, and indeed, Rei found himself captivated by the sight.

"The fact that Rei was shown to this room means Duke Quent must be quite considerate toward you, don't you think?"

Erinde, seated on the sofa, turned her gaze to Cammy and asked. Cammy smiled as she placed freshly brewed tea on the table and replied.

"I was simply instructed to entertain you both."

"Hmm... Rei, don't get too comfortable with Cammy. She may look like this, but she used to be a Rank B adventurer."

"Oh, that was quite some time ago. I don't think there's any need to be that wary."

"Is that so? Well, I'm employed by Duke Quent, so I don't think there's anything to worry about. But Rei, you should be careful."

Erinde said this, but Rei showed no particular surprise at the words, merely nodding lightly before returning to admiring the room.

Even to Rei's eyes, it was clear the maid tending to them possessed considerable skill.

Since Rei wasn't exactly at odds with Duke Quent, he felt there was little need for particular caution.

Seeing Rei like this, Cammy shot him a slightly surprised look.

Even without hostility, normally someone with skills like Cami's would naturally be wary if someone of that caliber was nearby.

But even after learning Cammy's true identity, Rei showed almost no sign of concern.

Of course, he wasn't completely indifferent; he was paying at least minimal attention. Still, he wasn't paying any more attention than that.

(Is it because he believes he can handle anything that might happen? ...That's probably where he differs from me, even though we're both Rank B. Me, who reached Rank B as my limit, and him, the one with the alias for whom Rank B is merely a stepping stone. I find myself feeling a little jealous.)

Keeping his inner thoughts hidden, peaceful moments passed in the guest room.

About twenty minutes into this, Rei noticed the footsteps of several people approaching the room. Cami and Erinde noticed them a moment later.

"Looks like they're here. I thought it would take a bit longer."

Erinde murmured as she placed the tea on the table. Simultaneously, a knock sounded at the door. After a brief exchange, the one who entered the room was none other than Ronald Quent, the head of the Quent family.

Beside him stood Iskeld, presumably as his guard, along with Marka and Coan.

Beyond the door, several other presences lingered.

(The Knight Commander himself as an escort? Is he short-handed? ...No, considering the Stigma matter, it's rather to be expected.)

Rei satisfied himself with this reasoning, but he had no idea he was actually one of the targets Iskeld was guarding against.

While Rei and the Quent family were currently on friendly terms, Rei was fundamentally seen as neutral.

Moreover, given Rei's personal history with the royalist faction, Iskeld had no choice but to remain wary of him.

...Though, spending time with Rei makes such thoughts seem ridiculous, and above all, Rei has no intention of harming Duke Quent himself.

"Did I keep you waiting? My apologies, I was a bit swamped with work."

"Not at all. A little wait is nothing. So..."

"Hmm, I understand. ...You've worked hard these past two months. I hear the cadets at the academy were greatly inspired by you. Their skills in mock combat have also improved significantly. I summoned you due to my own circumstances, but I am grateful."

Ronald delivered this with his stern expression unchanged, but I'd heard from Marka before that he was actually pleased...

While being unable to show emotion easily might be advantageous for a nobleman, it could easily lead to misunderstandings in situations like this.

"I'm the one who gained valuable experience from this."

This was not flattery directed at Ronald, but Rei's genuine sentiment.

The people Rei had trained up until now were either Basleros, whose fundamentals weren't quite solid, or former guerrilla fighters who already possessed a certain level of skill.

Compared to them, the cadets at the officer academy had some foundation, but they didn't possess the solid skills of the former guerrilla fighters.

They were positioned somewhere between Basler and the former guerrilla fighters.

Considering that, it was only natural for Rei to think that even if it didn't help now, it might prove useful in the future.

"I appreciate you saying that. ...Well then, let's get the reward settled first. I believe you requested fire ore, correct?"

"Yes. As you know, for someone like me who specializes in fire magic and skills, fire ore is a very versatile material."

The Fire Tornado... the fire whirlwind... had already become something of a signature move for Rei.

Knowing this, Ronald listened to Rei's words and readily agreed.

"This time, our mishandling caused you quite a bit of trouble. As I mentioned before, I've included extra compensation for that inconvenience in your payment. ...Come in."

As Ronald spoke, four men entered the room.

Each stood over two meters tall, their bodies visibly bulging with muscle.

Each carried a barrel about one meter tall, which they placed on the floor.

"Your Grace, is this acceptable? It is quite heavy, though..."

"It doesn't matter. For that man, this kind of weight is nothing."

As if thinking their strength had been underestimated, the representative of the four men flashed a momentary look of displeasure.

But they couldn't afford to keep such expressions on their faces around Ronald, and they quickly returned to their usual demeanor.

Ronald, of course, didn't miss their reaction.

Judging it unwise to leave their discontent festering, he addressed Rei in front of the men.

"Here is your reward—three barrels of fire ore."

"...But there seem to be four barrels?"

"Hmm. Those are for commemorating our meeting, and for the trouble Marka caused you."

Ronald's words caught Rei off guard.

Originally, the reward was supposed to be two barrels of fire ore.

But that had been reduced to three barrels because they'd allowed Stigma's attack. He certainly hadn't expected to receive anything beyond the fire ore on top of that.

"Is that really okay?"

"I'd be delighted if you accepted it. ...The other barrel contains Wind-Thunder Ore. It's quite rare. Do you know of it?"

"...Yes, of course. I never expected to receive something like that."

If Flame Ore is an ore imbued with the magic power of fire, then Wind-Thunder Ore is an ore imbued with the magic powers of both wind and thunder.

While the name "Fire Ore" implies an ore imbued with fire and stronger flame attributes, magic ores containing separate attributes are even rarer and have a wider range of applications.

(If I incorporate it into a fire whirlwind, the wind magic will likely make the whirlwind itself larger, and the thunder magic might even make it crackle with lightning... I think. Plus, naturally, if I bring it to a blacksmith or alchemist, it could be used as material for magic items. ...Come to think of it, what ever happened to turning that longsword I took from Noiz into a spear? Maybe I'll drop by when I get back to Gilm.)

With such thoughts swirling, Rei approached the barrel.

"I'm glad you're satisfied. Now, please take it."

"Yes, thank you. I never imagined I'd receive something this valuable."

Responding to Ronald, he touched the barrel and stored its contents within the Misty Ring.

The men who had carried the barrel watched with wide eyes, astonished, then nodded in understanding at Ronald's earlier words.

And so, the handover of the reward concluded... leaving only the farewell party that night as the remaining event.
