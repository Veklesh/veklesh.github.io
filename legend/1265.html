Several days had passed since talking with Mayo... yet Rei had taken no action against Spier Trading Company.

This was partly because he'd been told it was their problem, and if Rei got involved carelessly, it would lead to all sorts of troublesome complications.

Rei himself understood that he was, in many ways, a public figure.

He also understood that this particular matter was not something he should get involved in.

If the Spier Trading Company had used some unreasonable justification to try and bring Mayo's company under their umbrella or absorb it, Rei wouldn't have stayed silent. But this was ultimately a dispute between trading companies... and a legitimate one at that.

Given that, it was an impossible option for Rei to stick his neck into it.

Rei understood very well how much power he wielded.

But... precisely because of that, he understood it wasn't something he could wield recklessly.

So what was Rei doing now...?

"Ah, Rei-kun. How about this one? It might be a bit mature for you, but I think it suits you quite well."

"Eh, Kenny. I think this one suits Rei-san better, actually."

For some reason, she was walking down the main street with Lenora and Kenny, browsing the displays in various shops.

(Window shopping, huh? Well, there's no glass, but...)

Inside the shops were piles of clothes.

However, every single piece was secondhand... so-called vintage clothing. This was different from the window shopping Rei knew.

Then again, new clothes were basically impossible to afford in Elgin.

While Lenora and Kenny, earning decent wages as guild receptionists, could probably afford them if they stretched their budgets a bit, today they had ended up at a secondhand clothing store.

(Besides, some of these secondhand clothes look just like new. ...I wonder how they ended up in this shop?)

Clothes that looked indistinguishable from new naturally cost more than others.

In fact, some were several times more expensive... some even close to ten times more.

"I was actually looking for a tailor to make clothes,"

He muttered under his breath, tilting his head in confusion at how things had come to this.

The clothes made from silver lion fur, as suggested by Azimov.

He had intended to look around for a skilled tailor to consider that option... but along the way, he encountered Lenora and Kenny, who happened to be off duty today, and ended up tagging along with them.

Not that Rei disliked Lenora or Kenny.

In fact, it could even be said he felt fondness toward them.

Even Rei himself didn't understand what kind of affection it was.

Even so, walking around and browsing various shops with the two of them like this was by no means unpleasant for Rei.

...Though, to an outsider, aside from Lenora, probably not many people would recognize Kenny as Kenny, what with him being so bundled up.

"Ah, Rei. Let's go there next. I hear it's a shop famous for serving delicious herbal tea."

Saying that, Kenny pulled Rei along, leading him to a shop a short distance from the vintage clothing store.

"Hey, Kenny. Wait a moment. ...I'm sorry, Rei-san. Kenny just got so excited about finally getting to go out with you again after so long."

Lenora bowed apologetically to Rei, who was being pulled along by Kenny.

"Don't worry about it. I was thinking of buying something for Set as a souvenir anyway, so this works out perfectly."

The image of Set playing in the snow with the children near the stables flashed through Rei's mind.

Originally, Set was supposed to come play today too, but since the kids had asked to play with him, they ended up splitting up.

Set had been torn between choosing snow play or going out with Rei, but Rei's suggestion to play with the children led to them splitting up.

Though Set was drawn to the snow play, he ended up going his own way. Thinking about bringing him a souvenir... well, that was typical of Rei.

"A souvenir for Set-chan, you say? ...Come to think of it, that shop has delicious bread with dried fruit kneaded into it. That might be nice."

"Exactly. It's a bit pricey, but the taste is worth every penny."

Kenny, who had been tugging on Rei's hand, nodded in agreement at Lenora's words.

"Bread with dried fruit kneaded in? I think I had that somewhere before, and it was pretty tasty. Not that shop, though."

Hearing them talk, Rei recalled the taste of that bread from before.

Even before coming to Elgin, he'd bought and eaten bread with dried fruit—mainly raisins—kneaded into it from the supermarket many times.

But there was definitely a difference between bread made by machines and bread made by hand by an artisan.

Compared to the bread he'd eaten in Japan, the bread he ate here was superior in flavor.

(Well, even in Japan, bread from bakeries tastes better than the regular stuff sold everywhere.)

Raisin bread was one of Rei's favorite foods.

Even after coming to Elgin, bread kneaded with dried fruits and nuts remained one of Rei's favorite treats.

"That's right. While the specific ingredients kneaded into the bread vary by shop, many places do it. I've heard some even bake bread with vegetable juice added?"

"Other unusual things... Come to think of it, I once saw a bakery somewhere advertising a 'luxury meat bread' and baking oak meat."

At Lenora's words, Kenny suddenly chuckled as if remembering something.

What's so funny? Rei wondered.

To Rei, bread wrapped around ham or bacon wasn't exactly rare, even if it didn't involve kneading meat into the dough.

In fact, many bakeries sold such breads, and Set certainly didn't dislike them either.

"Did something weird happen? I think it's a pretty common kind of bread."

"No, Rei. I'd never seen bread like that before, and frankly, I didn't want to eat it."

"...Huh? You don't want to eat it? What do you mean?"

Seeing Rei's completely baffled expression, Kenny suppressed a smile and explained.

"I think you're imagining bread wrapped around seasoned oak meat and baked, but that's not it. What that shopkeeper did was squeeze baked oak meat, then mix the meat juices into the bread dough before baking it."

"...What... is that supposed to be?"

Instead of wrapping the meat and baking it, they kneaded the meat juices into the bread dough.

Hearing this, Rei tilted his head in confusion.

He couldn't say for sure since he hadn't actually tasted it, but even so, Rei couldn't imagine such bread being tasty.

He couldn't help but think it would make for a pretty greasy loaf.

Seeing Rei's expression, Kenny nodded in understanding.

In fact, that bread had been sold as a trial product, but people only bought it once out of curiosity and never looked at it again.

Considering that, it wasn't hard to imagine what kind of bread it was.

As they talked like this, Rei and the others kept walking and eventually arrived at their destination.

As expected of a shop specializing in herbal teas and light snacks, there weren't many men around.

Of course, it wasn't completely devoid of them, but the majority of customers were still women.

"Wow..."

Given that the majority of customers were women, Rei muttered in a way that seemed almost involuntary.

However, while she herself didn't seem to feel it much, Rei, with her feminine features, could easily be mistaken for just another woman when she was with Lenora and Kenny like this.

This was likely influenced by Rei being petite and wearing the Dragon Robe that hid her figure.

Naturally, among the patrons were adventurers and others who recognized Rei for who she was, for various reasons.

But for now, they seemed focused on their own enjoyment, and no one appeared particularly interested in bothering Rei.

"I get why the herbal tea is popular, but I never expected this many customers."

"Like I said earlier, it's not just the herbal tea—the bread made with dried fruit is famous too. ...Come on, let's sit down."

Guided by the staff, Rei, Lenora, and Kenny sat down.

They ordered a set of herbal tea and dried fruit bread. Kenny finally let out a sigh of relief and began taking off several layers of clothing.

Kenny's body, which had been bulky from layering, became somewhat slimmer.

Of course, he couldn't possibly take off all his outer layers inside the shop, so he was still bundled up compared to usual.

"Phew, warmth really is nice. ...If only they could warm up the entire inside of Gilm."

"Listen, how many magic items would you even need to pull something like that off? And it's not about barriers or anything—you'd need to physically cover the entire city, right?"

"I know. But lots of people hate the cold besides me, so I bet many would agree, right? Hey, you hate the cold too, don't you, Rei?"

Kenny looked away, and Rei hesitated over how to respond.

Of course, Rei didn't like the cold either, but wearing his Dragon Robe meant he didn't have to worry about it. Besides, his body wasn't like that of an ordinary human.

He was far more resilient to cold than Kenny felt.

...Even so, based on his experiences in Japan, Rei still didn't like the cold.

Besides, if Rei really put his mind to it, warming the entire Gilm wasn't impossible.

Considering Rei's immense magical power and his flame-specialized attributes, it might have been natural.

Of course, it carried the risk of accidentally burning Gilm to the ground, so it wasn't something he could do lightly.

Unless something like a major cold wave struck, it wouldn't be something he'd actually do.

"Here you are, your herbal tea set."

The waiter approached and placed the herbal tea and bread before the three of them.

The bread consisted of only two pieces, each bite-sized, which was a bit unsatisfying for Rei.

In the end, she ordered some more bread and sipped her herbal tea while chatting with Lenora and Kenny.

The moment it touched her tongue, a refreshing coolness spread through her mouth.

The sensation, similar yet different from mint tea, was by no means unpleasant to Rei.

Next, she naturally reached for the main attraction this time—at least for Rei—the bread kneaded with dried fruit.

Perhaps they had arrived just as it was baked, for the bread was warm to the touch... not scalding, but still pleasantly hot.

As her teeth sank in, the surface offered a crisp bite as the bread entered her mouth, filling it with the aroma of wheat... and in the next instant, the sweet fragrance of the dried fruit exploded throughout her mouth.

Chewing the bread in his mouth, the sweet texture of the wheat mingled with the sweetness of the dried fruit spreading throughout his mouth.

(Huh)

The sweetness and texture filling her mouth made Rei realize the dried fruit kneaded into the bread wasn't just one kind.

At least two kinds... Even with her less-than-keen palate, that much was clear. Still, she understood this bread was delicious.

She would have loved to buy a large quantity... but when she placed her additional order earlier, she was told there was a limit to how many one person could purchase, and she had already ordered up to that limit.

Considering she needed some for gifts for Set, she couldn't eat all the bread here, leaving her feeling disappointed.

"As always, the bread at this shop is delicious. The herbal tea too."

Kenny happily ate the bread and sipped his herbal tea.

Lenora nodded in agreement at Kenny's words, smiling as she enjoyed her tea time.

Having a sweet tooth must be a feminine trait after all. ...Though, to be fair, Rei, being a man, also has quite the sweet tooth.

(I think I read somewhere that not drinking alcohol makes you crave sweets... or maybe not? Or was it something else?)

Rei wondered.

In Rei's case, though, growing up in Japan with a father who drank, dinner often included dishes meant to accompany alcohol. So while he doesn't drink himself, he does enjoy snack-like foods.

This day passed without any particular commotion... He spent a leisurely time chatting over tea with Lenora and Kenny, enjoying their company.
