“...Um, Walker. What about you?”

Rei had decided to head to the dungeon, so he was busy with handover duties and preparations—especially stocking up on large quantities of various dishes to eat inside the dungeon. He was about to leave for Goltz, where the dungeon was located, early in the morning, but for some reason, just as the six o’clock bell rang and he was about to step out the main gate, Walker called out to him.

The last time Rei had seen him, Walker’s desk—and even the floor—had been piled high with paperwork, and he’d seemed incredibly busy processing it all. But as far as Rei could tell now, Walker looked healthy and didn’t seem to be leading a busy life at all.

And the man Walker was accompanied by was in his late twenties.

He wasn’t exactly muscular… but just by looking at his appearance—and the way he moved—it was immediately clear that he’d trained his body with an emphasis on agility.

He had a longsword in its sheath at his waist, so there was no doubt he was a front-line fighter.

But what surprised Rei most of all was… the man’s strength.

He possessed a strength that was simply incomparable to that of an adventurer who had come for the construction project.

No, even considering him as an adventurer from Gilm, there was no doubt he possessed strength that would place him firmly among the top ranks.

“Huh.”

It was, of course, Vihera who murmured this, flashing a smile that could only be described as lewd as she looked at the man.

“Ah, Vihera, calm down.”

Rei tried to stop her, but perhaps sensing something in Vihera’s smile, the man reflexively took a few steps back and reached for the longsword at his waist.

“Mr. Relyu, please calm down. She’s not a problem.”

“But… that smile just now…”

The man called Relu looked as if he couldn’t accept Walker’s words and tried to speak, but eventually decided it would be pointless to say anything here and now, so he let go of his weapon with a resigned expression.

“So? Why did you bring such a skilled fighter here, anyway? And at this early hour, no less.”

Relieu, the man called the expert, flashes a slight smile and speaks.

“I’m your backup.”

“...Reinforcements?”

At those words, Rei turned to look at Walker, who nodded as if it were only natural.

“Yes. You probably don’t know this, Rei, but he’s a Rank A adventurer known as ‘Gale.’ I think he’ll be quite reliable as a combatant, don’t you?”

“No, that’s not what I mean. Why do we need to go out of our way to increase our numbers? I hate to say it, but our party is already pretty strong in terms of combat power, you know?”

“I don’t think ‘pretty high’ even begins to cover it.”

At Rei’s words, Relieu muttered almost reflexively.

Although Relieu bore the nickname “Gale,” standing before the members of Rei’s Crimson Wings, the only opponent he could say he could definitely defeat was someone smaller than himself… specifically, Byune, whom one could only call a girl.

With that much firepower assembled, there was no way the phrase “overall combat strength is quite high” could suffice.

That said, she didn’t intend to lose so easily either.

“If our forces are sufficient, why go to the trouble? And hire someone with a moniker, no less?”

Naturally, hiring an adventurer with a nickname requires a commensurate reward.

In fact, depending on the situation, money alone might not suffice; it could require some kind of special magic item—something that normally couldn’t be bought so easily with money.

“It’s cooperation from Lord Daskar. As you know, Goltz is a city located in a territory ruled by the Neutral Faction. On top of that, it seems that the Neutral Faction is quite close to Lord Daskar… so, if we’re going to take on the dungeon, it’s best to bolster our forces as much as possible.”

“...Huh.”

“Did you know about this?” Rei asked Marina with a glance.

Since Marina was the one who negotiated with Daskar regarding this matter, it wouldn’t be strange for her to know that additional reinforcements were coming. Maybe she hadn’t told them to surprise them… That’s what Rei thought, but seeing how surprised Marina looked, he could easily guess that she hadn’t known either.

In fact, the words that came out of Marina’s mouth next were filled with surprise.

“I didn’t know. But… I’d heard he was a close friend of Daskar’s. Even so, I never imagined he’d hire Relieu to accompany us.”

Naturally, Marina, who had been a guild master before joining Walker, knew of Relieu, who went by the nickname “Gale.”

Seeing Marina’s reaction, Rei concluded that Relieu was likely a trustworthy ally for the time being.

“So, I can trust him?”

Rei asked Walker, not Marina.

In response to Rei’s words, Walker nodded, indicating there was absolutely no problem.

“Yes. He’s a man you can trust. Besides… in his case, there’s one reason why it’s absolutely fine for him to act alongside the Crimson Wings.”

“A reason why it’s no problem?”

“That’s right. The Lady Elena—who is with the Crimson Wings… and you all—is an incredibly beautiful woman. So much so that, under normal circumstances, it wouldn’t be strange for someone to harbor impure thoughts.”

What came out of Walker’s mouth was, in a sense, only natural.

Even Rei, who had been traveling with Elena, Marina, and Vihera for quite some time, could never deny it.

Of course, since making a move on any of the three would be a matter of life and death, very few people would be foolish enough to try it.

Even so, while Elena and the others were used to being the object of lustful gazes, that didn’t mean they didn’t find it unpleasant.

……That said, if it were Rei casting those lustful glances, it would be a different story.

“He’s married. He’s head over heels for his wife. To the point where he absolutely cannot see any woman other than his wife in that light.”

“Hey, hey, just so you know, that doesn’t mean he doesn’t see you as women, you know?”

That’s what Relieu said, but there was no trace of desire in the way he actually looked at Elena and the others.

Elena and the others, who were being watched, understood this clearly.

Seeing Elena and the others nod, they must have concluded that what Walker was saying was certainly not a lie.

“I get it… but we’re probably heading to a dungeon, right? It hasn’t been confirmed yet, but it’s almost certainly the case. And if it is a dungeon, I don’t know how big it is, but depending on the situation, it could be pretty cramped.”

“Ah… well, I get what you’re thinking, Rei, but we won’t know for sure until we actually go there.”

Rei hesitated slightly at Relu’s words.

After some thought... he finally spoke up.

“Just to be clear, if I take you with me, how will we split the materials from the monsters we defeat in the dungeon, the magic stones, and any other treasures we might find?”

This was Rei’s final confirmation.

The main reason Rei and the others were heading into the dungeon this time was to obtain the dungeon’s core, which was necessary to raise the level of Death Scythe’s terrain manipulation.

But naturally, they were also aiming for monster materials and magic stones.

Especially in a dungeon, they might be able to obtain types of magic stones they haven’t been able to get before, and in that sense, Rei was looking forward to it as well.

That said, the monsters that have fallen off the cliff and lie dead on the ground appear to be goblins, kobolds, and orcs, so in that sense, there’s a good chance they’re just run-of-the-mill monsters.

Still, monsters that have fallen from the dungeon to their deaths on the ground are likely among the weaker ones in the dungeon... that’s a high probability.

In reality, since there are orcs among the dead monsters, it’s probably not that likely that they’re all just the weaker ones.

In any case, no matter how reliable they might be as combatants, if they force themselves to tag along and demand a share of the treasure found in the dungeon, there’s absolutely no benefit for Rei and the others in bringing them along… well, not exactly, but the disadvantages clearly outweigh the benefits.

However, when asked this with such reservations, Relieu shook her head without seeming particularly bothered.

“Ah, I don’t really need anything from the dungeon.”

Hearing those words spoken so casually, Rei was naturally surprised.

“Then why are you going into the dungeon?”

“Taking on a dungeon naturally means risking your life. That doesn’t change, even for an adventurer with a nickname. And yet, you’re saying you don’t want anything?”

“That’s right. I’ve already received my reward from Lord Daskar.”

“...If you don’t mind, may I ask what that reward is?”

A reward substantial enough for him to declare, despite facing the dungeon, that he needs nothing else.

Hearing that he’d already received it from Daskar, Rei found himself wondering.

Of course, it’s not that he absolutely… had to know at all costs, but he still wanted to know if possible.

In response to Rei’s curiosity, Relieu nodded honestly, as if there were no problem with it, and spoke.

“A little while ago, Shumine… ah, that’s my wife’s name. Shumine fell seriously ill. It was a pretty nasty illness, but Lord Daskar had the ingredients needed to make the potion to cure it.”

“Ah… I see.”

Hearing that much, Rei could easily understand why Relieu felt indebted to him.

Given Daskar’s character, there was no way he would refuse to help cure the illness of Relu’s wife—a valuable asset to Gilm—even if he had the means to do so.

If this had been an ordinary… say, just a Rank C or Rank B adventurer, Daskar might not have handed over the ingredients for the potion based on his judgment as a lord.

But an adventurer with a nickname is a rare commodity—a true trump card with exceptionally high combat prowess.

If such a figure feels affection for Gilm, then a little expense would certainly be within acceptable limits.

Of course, there are some who don’t even recognize a favor when they’re given one… but in Relieu’s case, fortunately, he had the sense to properly appreciate the favor.

(But even so... if we can use an adventurer with a nickname for free, is it really worth it to go out of our way to have them tag along on our dungeon exploration? Honestly, what’s the point?)

Watching Relieu, Rei couldn’t help but think that.

If they had the chance to freely use an adventurer with a nickname, wouldn’t it be more beneficial for Gilm to assign them a different task?

Yes, it was only natural for Rei to think that.

“So, it’s settled that he’ll be accompanying us? I have work to do, so I’ll be taking my leave now.”

Seeing Walker like that, Rei briefly considered calling him back.

But considering the mountain of paperwork in Walker’s office, and the fact that he’d taken time out of his work—or perhaps even before starting it—to come see him like this, Ray couldn’t just casually call him back.

“Rei? Are you sure this is okay?”

Elena asks, having seen Rei stop himself just as he was about to speak, but after a moment’s thought, Rei decides it’s fine.

Since Daskar had arranged it and Walker had personally escorted the person here, there was no need for suspicion.

If they were to carelessly add a man to this group, there was a high chance romantic entanglements would cause problems, but judging by how devoted and faithful to his wife Relieu seemed, it didn’t look like that sort of issue would arise.

Given that, since they were about to tackle what appeared to be an unknown dungeon, having more combat power wasn’t a bad thing.

...That is, assuming the dungeon is spacious enough.

“He’s got a nickname, so he can’t be completely unreliable, right? …Well, I’ll leave it to you for now.”

“Yeah. But I’d like to get back to Gilm before winter, if possible, so please take care of things. I don’t want to worry Shumine.”

“True. I want to stay in Gilm during the winter, too. Though, depending on the dungeon, I hear there are plenty of places where it’s actually more comfortable inside than out, even in winter or summer.”

For example, the dungeon Rei first explored was like that.

That dungeon, which he explored with Elena, maintained a constant temperature, making it an ideal refuge during both summer and winter.

That said, since we’d already cleared that dungeon, hardly anyone used it anymore.

“That’s a relief to hear. Well, shall we go? Let’s clear this dungeon as quickly as possible. Actually, I’ve tried tackling dungeons a few times, but I’ve never actually cleared one.”

“I figured.”

Clearing a dungeon is difficult even for adventurers with a reputation.

Knowing this, Rei gave a nod filled with genuine understanding in response to Relieu’s words.
