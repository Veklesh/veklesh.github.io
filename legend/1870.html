After parting ways with Brewitt, Rei and Elena had made their way directly to Elena’s room inside the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion.

That said, there were several rooms belonging to Elena in this mansion, and this was one of the rooms used to receive guests.

...In reality, it was the finest of the guest rooms, but Rei didn’t seem to notice.

“Please, come in.”

“...Come to think of it, it feels like it’s been a while since I’ve seen this.”

Rei muttered this as he looked at Ara, who had served him tea while he sat on the sofa.

In fact, since arriving at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, Rei had had almost no opportunity to meet with Ara.

As the captain leading Elena’s knightly guard, Ara had a mountain of work to do in the literal sense, and she had been busy dealing with it.

As a result, while they had crossed paths a few times, the frequency was alarmingly low compared to how often he saw Elena.

Perhaps Ara understood this, for she smiled.

“That’s right. I’ve been so busy processing paperwork that I’ve inevitably had fewer chances to see you, Rei…”

“So, is it okay now? Well, I’m happy because I get to drink tea brewed by Ara.”

Ara’s skill at brewing tea is, quite literally, exceptional.

From Rei’s perspective, it didn’t seem all that different from what others did, but the taste of the tea was distinctly different.

Someone more knowledgeable about tea would probably be able to discern all sorts of subtle differences... but in Rei’s case, while he’ll drink tea when it’s served to him, he doesn’t actually brew it himself.

Even when he was in Japan, the most he ever did was drink tea from tea bags. And even that was only a few times.

He had, however, drunk his fair share of the canned or bottled tea sold in vending machines—what one might call “tea-flavored beverages.”

Even for someone like Rei, Ara is the one who can brew tea that he can confidently say is delicious.

To be honest, I can’t help but think that Ara’s true calling might be working in a café-like shop rather than holding the position of captain of Elena’s Guard.

That’s what I can’t help but think.

That said, the main reason Ara’s tea-making skills have improved this much is simply because she wants Elena to enjoy a delicious cup of tea.

Given that, it didn’t seem likely to Rei—who knows just how much Ara cares for Elena—that she would take on a job that would keep her away from Elena’s side.

“Hearing you say that, Lord Rei, makes all my hard work worthwhile. …So, Lady Elena. May I listen to what you’re about to say?”

When Ara asked Elena this, Elena turned her gaze toward Rei, as if to ask, “What do you think?”

“It’s fine if Ara stays.”

If anyone other than Ara had been here—someone Rei didn’t know well, or someone he wasn’t particularly close to—he certainly wouldn’t have given his permission for her to listen in.

In fact, even with someone reasonably close, like Leodanis or Brewitt, he would have turned them down.

But since he’d known Ara for several years now and was fully aware of both her personality and her abilities, refusing her wasn’t an option.

Perhaps sensing Rei’s thoughts, Ara sat down next to Elena with a slightly pleased look on her face.

……For a moment—just a split second—Rei wondered if Ara’s cheerful expression wasn’t because of what he’d said, but simply because she’d managed to sit next to Elena. But he quickly told himself that wasn’t the case and spoke again.

“First of all, I was being targeted by an assassin known as the Black Wolf.”

The moment the words “Black Wolf” left Rei’s mouth, Ara’s expression—which had been so happy just moments ago as she sat next to Elena—froze.

As someone from Anesis, which was like a second home to her, Ara naturally knew the name Black Wolf.

It was precisely because she knew just how dangerous an assassin he was that Ara’s expression froze like that.

“To be honest, it’s a miracle you’re still alive.”

“Yeah. Black Wolf was actually strong. But even though being an assassin is his true calling, for some reason, when he attacked me, he came at me head-on. If he’d just stuck to being a typical assassin, I’m sure I’d have been in real danger.”

That’s exactly the problem—you never know what methods he might use to target your life in everyday situations.

Under those circumstances, it wouldn’t be strange for even Rei to be mentally drained.

Yet, Black Wolf didn’t use such tactics; he challenged Rei head-on.

Even Rei doesn’t know why he did that. Maybe even Black Wolf himself didn’t have any solid reason.

(Maybe he instinctively realized I’d come from Japan because of the steamed bun incident? …It’s a possibility, but I wonder.)

In the end, now that the Black Wolf himself had died without saying a word about those circumstances, there was no point in speculating about them.

“I guess I was just lucky.”

“Yeah. I won’t deny that. Since Black Wolf challenged me head-on, I was able to win.”

In response to Ara’s words, Rei replied briefly, then continued.

“And since that Black Wolf was an assassin, there’s no doubt he was acting on information received from someone. So I asked Elena to check with the Kerebel Duke’s household to see if they had any information on that.”

“...Is it okay for me to hear that?”

In response to Ara’s question, Elena nodded in agreement.

After all, to Elena, Ara was her best friend since childhood and the person she trusted most.

And given that she held the position of captain of the Royal Guard, acting as Elena’s right-hand woman, Elena had decided it would be best for Ara to hear what she was about to say.

“Please do. …Well, to put it simply, it seems there’s someone who specializes in acting as an intermediary with the Black Wolves. I’ve also heard where that person is based.”

“Impressive. …But if you knew that much, wouldn’t it have been better for the House of Duke Kerebel to take action themselves?”

It was true that Elena—arguably their greatest asset—wasn’t present, but there was Philma, who led the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights.

It was unthinkable that they lacked sufficient combat power.

Given that situation, why did they leave the Black Wolf to run wild?

It was only natural for Rei to think that.

But Elena shook her head at Rei’s words.

“The one acting as the Black Wolf’s intermediary is a member of the largest faction within the underground organizations in Anesis.”

“Ah… I see.”

Anesis is known as the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, after the capital.

Naturally, when it comes to the most powerful faction operating behind the scenes in such a city, they can’t simply be dealt with so easily.

It is an undeniable fact that, precisely because it is an underground organization, it naturally commits various crimes, but at the same time, it is precisely because that organization exists that the city does not descend into chaotic disorder.

If no organization were to unify Anesis’s underworld, it would descend into a state of warring factions, with numerous organizations clashing in an attempt to seize hegemony over Anesis’s underworld.

Furthermore, it served as a deterrent against criminals coming from outside Anesis.

Governing Anesis, the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, is not something that can be managed with mere idealism.

Even in Gilm, where Rei is based, there are several such underground organizations, and the lord, Daskar, tolerates their existence.

Having vaguely sensed the situation, Rei couldn’t argue with Elena’s words.

Of course, he had his own thoughts, but given the incident with the Black Wolf, voicing his dissatisfaction wouldn’t change anything. …It would be a different story if Rei were to go and set up an underground organization here in Anesis, but naturally, he had no such intention.

However, given the current situation, there was something he found problematic.

Namely, if the House of Kerebel had been turning a blind eye to that organization until now, wouldn’t it be problematic for Rei to suddenly move in on it?

“Don’t worry. This time, they’ve gone too far, no matter how you look at it.”

As if she had read Rei’s thoughts… no, not as if, but because she actually had, Elena said this.

This incident had gone beyond what the Duke of Kerebel could tolerate.

Rei was, after all, a guest of the Duke of Kerebel’s household.

And Rei had tried to kill him.

This was completely beyond what the Duke of Kerebel’s household could tolerate.

This time, Rei happened to defeat the Black Wolf, but that was only because it was Rei.

If it hadn’t been Rei who was targeted by the Black Wolf, that person would likely have died.

If that had happened, the prestige of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, which had welcomed that person as a guest, would have been completely ruined.

“So?”

“Hmm. That’s part of the reason it took so long to get my father’s permission. …We’re going to strike. I don’t know what they were thinking when they did this, but that doesn’t mean we have the option to just sit back and do nothing. …Are you ready?”

Rei nodded in agreement at Elena, who asked this even though she already knew the answer.

He had fought to the death with the Black Wolf and engaged in a mock battle with Brewitt.

Even so, Rei still had plenty of stamina left, and if told he had to fight right away, he would have answered without hesitation that he was ready.

“Of course.”

“Hmm, I see. …But rest for now.”

“……Huh?”

Rei had assumed they’d be heading out immediately, but what came out of Elena’s mouth was the unexpected command to rest.

Why on earth? It was only natural for him to feel puzzled—and even a little annoyed.

“Are you kidding me?”

“No. Think about it. If we cause a commotion while it’s still light out, naturally many people will see it. If that happens, some might take unnecessary action. To prevent that, we’ll strike at night.”

Rei felt somewhat unconvinced by Elena’s words, but as long as they were in Anesis—the Duchy of Kerebel—he couldn’t ignore her decision.

No, he might have been able to get his way if he’d really tried, but he decided it would be better to follow Elena’s instructions for now.

Still, he voiced the doubts he felt regarding Elena’s words.

“This operation is, so to speak, meant to serve as a warning, isn’t it? Yet, is it really okay not to reveal that it’s the Duke of Kerebel carrying it out?”

“We’ll make it clear. However, at the same time, we don’t want to cause a commotion among the general public. That’s precisely why we’re planning to strike at night. I hesitate to call it ‘fortunate,’ but the place we’re planning to raid tonight is apparently quite famous in the underworld. It seems members of other organizations have posted lookouts there so they’ll know if anything happens.”

“Ah… I see. So we’ll have those guys let us know what’s going on.”

“Exactly.”

Even without explicitly announcing that the men of the Kerebel Duke are attacking, the people watching over the target location will spread the information on their own.

On top of that, the information spread by such people generally tends to get more sensational as it spreads.

If that’s the case, just how sensationalized will the news of the Duke of Kerebel’s invasion become as it spreads throughout the underworld of Anesis?

Upon hearing this, Rei found himself looking forward to it a little, even though he knew it was somewhat inappropriate.

……Of course, the fact that Rei was participating in that battle meant that rumors about Crimson Rei would naturally spread as well, but that was old news to him by now.

Given that rumors about Crimson had already spread due to the war with the Bestir Empire and various other reasons, it was only natural to conclude that worrying about it was pointless.

“I get the gist of it. …So, who’s participating in this raid? Surely it’s not just me, Elena, and Ara, is it?”

“Basically, that’s correct. However, to prevent anyone from escaping the target location, we’ll have soldiers surround the area. …Though, given the nature of the target location, it’s highly likely that secret escape routes have been prepared, as one would expect.”

“Ah, that’s definitely something you’d expect. What are you planning to do about that?”

When Rei heard the word “escape route,” what came to mind were scenes like exiting to a house far from the underground passage or connecting to a dry well.

Then again, given that magic and magical items exist in Elgin, it wouldn’t be surprising if there were escape methods different from what Rei had in mind.
