A short distance from the forest where Rei and the others were.

There, a canopy had been set up, and nearly twenty soldiers were present.

The tents were meant to provide some relief from the midsummer sun, whose rays were softened by the trees in the forest. Yet, these tents were exceptionally pristine... so much so that one might suspect they had scarcely seen any actual combat use.

Beneath that canopy, a man shouted impatiently.

"Dammit! What about the report?! It's been three hours since the soldiers entered the forest! Why haven't we received a single report yet?!"

The one shouting was a stout man whose build surely exceeded 100 kilograms.

He had a mustache under his nose, but his round face, with its sagging cheeks, gave him no sense of authority.

...No, even before that, there was an aura of slackness about his entire body, which only served to further diminish any sense of authority.

"Lord Rajalas, they are commoners after all. Perhaps they are struggling in the forest?"

One of the men surrounding the fat man—the one called Rajalas—said this, his voice dripping with a sense of superiority.

Several other men nodded in agreement.

...The soldiers guarding outside the tent listened to Rajalas and his entourage with irritation, though they hid it from their expressions.

Normally, if they were protecting Rajalas, they should guard him inside the tent.

But to Rajalas, a noble of the Kingdom of Reburina, commoners were nothing more than trash.

Having such trash share the same tent as him was absolutely unacceptable.

If he could keep that attitude bottled up inside himself, it might be tolerable. But he was blatantly showing it.

Naturally, the guard soldiers stationed around him found this anything but amusing.

Moreover, the soldiers assigned as guards here were all skilled fighters who had excelled during training.

Naturally, since they guarded the squad leader commanding the unit, the soldiers accepted that skilled men were assigned. They accepted it... but even so, they couldn't possibly accept being commanded by someone like the figure before them.

(This orc is our commander?! Give me a break. And seriously, why is the guy in charge of the unit so damn fat?)

One of the escort soldiers watched Rajalas ranting, unable to contain his own irritation.

It took considerable effort to keep his own irritation from showing on his face.

"Why the hell do I have to wait in this sweltering heat anyway?!"

"Well, that's because we need Lord Rajalas's strength to command this unit, after all."

"Exactly! These soldiers are nothing but dim-witted commoners. We nobles... Your Highness Rajalas must command them. Otherwise, what on earth would they do?"

Hearing his entourage say this, his pride was somewhat satisfied.

Rajaras relaxed the irritated expression he'd worn mere seconds ago, if only slightly.

...Naturally, the soldiers who overheard this conversation found it anything but amusing.

But Rajalas was a noble. ...And a noble in the worst sense of the word.

If they made Rajalas unhappy here, who knew what kind of retaliation might come?

After all, he was merely a noble from the small country of Reburina... specifically, a viscount whose rank was lower than most. Precisely because of this, Rajalas clung stubbornly to his sense of nobility, and it was this very trait that had caused him to stir up various troubles over the years.

Some soldiers had even been forced to leave the army because of Rajalas.

That's precisely why, no matter how irritated one might be, defying Rajalas was absolutely forbidden.

(Damn noble. Wish a stray arrow would hit him and kill him.)

Just as one soldier thought this, he noticed a figure running out of the forest.

The soldier thought it might be an enemy at first, but upon recognizing the figure as someone familiar, he breathed a sigh of relief.

It wasn't just the soldier cursing the nobleman inwardly who noticed the soldier emerging from the woods.

The other guard soldiers noticed him too, as if it were only natural.

And within tens of seconds of the soldiers noticing... the soldier emerging from the forest finally reached the vicinity of the tent, causing Rajalas and his entourage to finally notice him.

"Huh? Who are you? Whose permission did you get to show your face here..."

"Hold on, Lord Rajalas."

Rajaras muttered unhappily upon seeing the soldier's face, but his entourage stepped in to stop him.

As an associate, he didn't want to incur Rajalas' displeasure.

They suspected the soldier had returned from the forest bearing some information... perhaps even a report that his mission had been completed.

It was obvious that cutting down a soldier who might have such a report would lead to all sorts of trouble later.

Of course, as Rajalas's entourage, they had no intention of showing mercy to commoners. But punishing a soldier without any particular reason would clearly cause problems later.

"What is it?"

"This man likely brought a report that the mission was completed. Since it was carried out under Lord Rajalas' command, the first to receive that report would be Lord Rajalas himself. Therefore, this commoner, knowing his place, probably ran here to deliver it."

"Indeed, as expected of Lord Rajalas. Your command is truly masterful. Just your presence alone makes a difference in the soldiers' morale."

At the words of his entourage, Rajalas looked at the soldier, who was panting heavily.

The look on his face now was one of satisfaction, unlike just seconds ago.

(Idiot.)

Seeing Rajalas and his entourage like this, the soldier who had been voicing his dissatisfaction inwardly moments ago once again made his discontent clear.

It was only natural. If they had truly annihilated the enemy in the forest and returned victorious, the soldiers' faces wouldn't be pale and twisted with fear.

Therefore, something had definitely happened.

Something that, despite sending hundreds of soldiers, was utterly beyond their control.

A sickening, deeply unsettling premonition gripped the escort soldier as he watched the panting soldier.

"Haa, haa, haa..."

"Dammit, how long are you going to stand there like that?! Report what happened immediately! And don't you dare tell me it's good news for me!"

Unable to tolerate the soldier's state any longer, Rajalas shouted, his irritation plain.

Not necessarily responding to Rajalas' words, but having somewhat caught his breath, the soldier finally spoke.

"The allies who entered the forest... they're all... wiped out!"

Silence.

Everyone fell silent, unable to comprehend the meaning of the soldier's words.

The sound of birds chirping could be heard from somewhere.

Even the guard soldier who had been showing irritation toward Rajalas since earlier couldn't comprehend what had been said.

The enemy had been wiped out, but the casualties were heavy.

Or perhaps the enemy was strong and they were struggling.

If that had been the report, the guard soldier might have accepted it.

But... the report the soldier brought was the annihilation of their own forces.

There was no way he could accept it properly.

"N-no, maybe... maybe some are still alive. But that bastard... that man... Ah... AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAHH!"

The moment he tried to speak, the soldier couldn't form coherent words.

It was as if the terror he'd been desperately holding back had finally broken free.

(What? What happened?)

The escort soldier watched the suddenly screaming soldier, confused.

What could possibly cause this?

He couldn't comprehend it at all.

And the others were just as baffled.

No, what Rajalas found most unforgivable was things not going his way.

"Dammit, stop screaming! Stop screaming like a fool and explain what happened, now! You useless piece of trash!"

He snatched a longsword from one of his entourage and swung it down at the soldier screaming in terror.

Of course, Rajalas had no intention of killing the soldier.

The sword was sheathed; it might serve as a blunt weapon, but it was useless as a sword.

A dull thud echoed as it struck flesh.

But... that wasn't enough to snap the soldier back to his senses. No, he couldn't snap back.

The sight the soldier had witnessed must have been overwhelming.

Rei, dancing through the blood, wielding the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear.

To the soldier, that sight was nothing but a symbol of dread and terror.

"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Rajaras repeatedly brought the scabbard of his longsword down upon the soldier, who was tearing at his own head.

"Lord Rajalas, stop there! If you kill him now, we won't get any information!"

He was about to kill the soldier for real.

His entourage, realizing this, frantically tried to restrain him.

The death of a single common soldier posed no problem—not just for Rajalas, but for his entourage as well.

But if they killed this soldier now, they wouldn't get a single piece of information about what had happened in the forest.

His entourage understood the situation well enough to realize that would be bad...

"Hmph... But this man couldn't even properly fulfill the role assigned to him. Then punishment is necessary, isn't it?"

Rajaras' words carried discontent.

While punishment for the soldier before him was part of it, what bothered him most was the man's incessant whining right now. He also took a certain pleasure in beating such a nuisance into submission.

But... Rajalas should have properly assessed the man's condition before acting.

He shouldn't have acted without understanding what it meant to attack a soldier who was half-mad and screaming.

Being mad meant the soldier had already completely forgotten what common sense was.

...Yes. Under normal circumstances, even if attacked, if the assailant were a noble... Rajalas would know he was an opponent he absolutely could not strike back against.

But with this soldier, now devoid of common sense, it wouldn't be strange for him to counterattack against someone who attacked him.

"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

"Enough, shut—"

Shut up, he thought.

Rajaras, shouting that, tried to slam the soldier with his longsword back in its sheath, but the next instant, he was hit by a powerful impact.

"Guh..."

He had no idea what had happened.

Before he knew it, his face was pressed against the ground... meaning he had been knocked down.

"What the—" He tilted his head, trying to sit up, and in that instant, pain shot through his cheek.

"Guh!"

It hurt.

He reached for his cheeks, which had become flabby and sagging from his usual lifestyle.

Then, in the next instant, the pain struck again... intense pain assaulted Rajalas.

"Ow! What is this? What the..."

Despite the hindrance of his belly fat, Rajalas managed to get to his feet. What he saw was a soldier being restrained by the guarding soldiers.

He was screaming violently, just as before.

Seeing Rajalas finally rise, his dazed entourage snapped back to reality, still unsure what had happened.

"Lord Rajalas, are you all right?!"

"You wretch! How dare a commoner lay hands on a noble! What do you think you're doing?!"

"Kill him! Kill him!"

Seeing his entourage shouting like this, Rajalas finally understood what had happened.

He had just been struck by that soldier... a mere commoner had struck him, sent him flying, and made him kiss the ground.

The moment he grasped this, blood rushed violently to his head.

A commoner, he thought.

His head nearly exploded with rage, but Rajalas scanned his surroundings.

What met his eyes was an intricately decorated scabbard and the long sword drawn from it, its blade exposed.

The moment he saw the light reflecting off that blade, he lost himself and reached for the longsword.

"You... I'll kill you!"

It was a crude killing intent, but he seemed utterly unconcerned. He strode toward the soldier being restrained by the guards, sword in hand.

One of his entourage must have noticed this. He shouted in alarm.

"Lord Rajalas! We need to hear your explanation first... Guh!"

"Shut up. Don't stand in my way!"

Rajaras pulled the longsword from the bodyguard's gut and shouted.

But... the next instant, something flew out of the forest and blew off Rajalas's right hand, the one holding the longsword.
