The Labyrinth City of Exil.

The numerous disturbances that took place there, involving aberrations and the Holy Light Church.

Amidst all this, a figure was working behind the scenes: Pri Marschel, head of the Marschel House—one of the three Houses ruling Exil at the time.

This man, who was also the sworn enemy of Byune—a member of Rei’s party—was capable of sealing magic into gems and unleashing it instantly at will.

Given that he had acquired such a technique, it was only natural that even Rei would be surprised.

……Even so, the reason he didn’t immediately regard Lemuria as an enemy was likely because Elena had categorically stated that sealing magic into a gem did not require any kind of sacrifice.

“Is what Elena said true?”

“Yeah, it’s true.”

In response to Rei’s question, Lemuria nodded, still using her usual peculiar way of speaking.

“So, no sacrifice of any kind is needed?”

Remuria nodded again in response to Rei’s follow-up question, which sounded like he was seeking confirmation.

Rei didn’t know how she had acquired the techniques Pri possessed, but that was likely due to the power of the Duke’s House.

And by further refining that technology, they’d managed to seal magic without needing any kind of sacrifice.

Hearing that, even Rei had to admit it was impressive, and the fact that they could use magic without chanting spells in an emergency was incredibly helpful.

(But there are some issues with using it, too.)

The reason Rei couldn’t simply rejoice was precisely because of that problem.

Rei’s companion, Byune, had lost her parents to a sacrifice performed by a Priest.

Even though sacrifices were no longer required, Rei didn’t know if she could remain indifferent seeing them use what might be called “gem magic”—a form of magic developed from Pri’s research and technology.

No, thinking about it logically, there’s no way she could remain indifferent.

If that’s the case, even if she were to obtain a gem imbued with magic, she wouldn’t be able to use it so easily.

After all, unlike Rei, Elena, Vihera, and Marina, Byune was, after all, just an ordinary human.

Moreover, since it’s already been decided that she’ll return to Exil in the future, she’ll eventually leave the Crimson Wings—whether that’s in a few years, a decade, or even longer, she doesn’t know.

Once that happens, she’ll likely be able to use the gem freely.

Or, while traveling separately from Byune, there might be a situation where she needs to use magic for some reason.

“...I understand. Let’s make a deal.”

“Zoi? Are you sure you’re okay without hearing the details, zoi?”

Did she not expect Rei to accept her proposal so readily? Lemuria asked, looking surprised.

Normally, since it’s something that benefits her, she wouldn’t need to ask.

To Rei, the fact that she chose to ask anyway seemed to reveal Remuria’s good nature.

“Just by asking like that, I don’t think you’re trying to deceive me. Besides… if the head of a Duke’s House were to try to deceive a business partner, what would happen…? I suppose that goes without saying, doesn’t it?”

It’s not uncommon for ducal houses to possess military strength on par with small nations.

But when facing someone like Rei, military strength on the scale of a small country would be annihilated in an instant.

And if Set were to join the fray, that annihilating power would be combined with mobility, making even a major power unable to handle them.

If one could foresee just how much harm attempting to deceive such an opponent in a transaction would cause, there’s no way they’d even try it.

“...I’m grateful.”

“Save that thanks for when Set decides it’s okay to give up the feather. Along with the gem. Just so you know, I don’t intend to force Set into anything. If Set says he doesn’t want to give up the feather, that’s the end of it.”

Even as he said this, Rei felt a faint sense of unease.

He wondered if Set’s feather would truly cure the illness Lemuria spoke of.

It wasn’t that he doubted Lemuria’s knowledge—or the opinion of her personal alchemist or whoever—but rather, it was purely about Set’s existence.

To cure Remuria’s family’s illness, they need Set’s… no, a griffin’s fresh feathers. Rei had no objection to that. He accepted it as simply the way things were.

The biggest problem here is that Set is not an ordinary griffin, but one created by Rei through beast magic.

In other words, while Set is a griffin, he is not the kind of griffin commonly known in Elgin.

Neither Rei nor Set had given this any thought at all, but since it involves human lives—or, to be precise, people who are struggling to lead normal lives, though their lives are not in immediate danger—it’s a different matter.

But there was no way he could possibly say something like, “Could it be that Set was created using magical beast magic…?” Rei felt a slight panic, but he quickly remembered an excuse to bring up the matter.

“Also, Lemuria. Before we get to the matter of the wings, there’s something I should mention.”

“What is it?”

Lemuria had been looking pleased at the unexpected possibility of easily obtaining Set’s feather, but seeing the serious expression on Rei’s face, she grew solemn.

“I don’t know if you’re aware of this, but even though Set is a griffin, he’s not an ordinary one. He’s a rare species. In other words, in terms of rank, he’s higher than an ordinary griffin—equivalent to Rank S. If what you’re after, Lemuria, is the feather of an ordinary griffin, then perhaps…”

He continued, explaining that if she used Set’s feathers to make medicine or something similar, it might not produce the intended effects.

If Rei were thinking only of his own interests, this would have been information he’d be better off not sharing.

Since Set is a griffin, if he simply agreed to the request when asked for his fresh feathers, then even if the medicine made from them didn’t work, he wouldn’t have any reason to be blamed by anyone.

But even so, the reason Rei went out of his way to say that was partly because Lemuria had been friendly toward him, and partly to avoid any future complaints.

“A rare species, huh? I’m aware of that matter. I looked into it thoroughly to confirm, and it appears there are no particular issues in that regard.”

“……I see. If it’s not a problem even though it’s a rare species, then I don’t have any issues with it. The only question is whether Set will accept it… What do you say? Shall we go right now?”

At Rei’s question, Brewitt, who had been listening to the conversation from the side, burst out laughing as if he couldn’t help himself.

“Hah, uh, look, Rei. As you can see, we’re currently at a party. If we go to the stables in a situation like this, won’t our clothes get dirty?”

“Nope, we’re going.”

“……What?”

As guests at this New Year’s party, leaving now to go to the stables risks getting our clothes dirty, and if the other nobles see us like that, we might be looked down upon—that’s not good.

That’s what Brewitt said, but Remuria immediately declared that she was heading to the stables.

“Um, well… is that okay? Like Brewitt said, I feel like there might be all sorts of problems if we leave the party to go to the stables.”

“It’ll be fine. I’ve already greeted Duke Kerebel, and I’ve finished greeting everyone else I needed to, you see. As for anyone else who might have business with me, they’ll likely come to the mansion tomorrow or later.”

“...No, but if that’s the case, even if I get Set’s feather, it’ll just sit there until Lemuria leaves Anesis, so it’s not exactly going to stay fresh, is it?”

Since she wanted a fresh feather—though Rei still found that expression a bit odd—it stood to reason that the feather she obtained from Set would need to undergo some sort of magical treatment or something as soon as possible.

However, even after this party ended, Lemuria wouldn’t be able to return to her domain immediately.

As the head of the Duke of Fuldar’s House—a noble family wielding significant power even within the Noble Faction—there were surely countless social engagements and negotiations she needed to attend to.

“Ah, don’t worry about that. Once I get the feather from Set, I plan to have the mage I brought with me use summoning magic to transport it to my domain right away.”

“Summoning magic? That’s quite rare.”

“Not really. There are wizards who can use summoning magic in cities, towns, and elsewhere. I’ve used it myself before. …Though, to be clear, those who can summon such things are limited to bird-like creatures, not monsters—so if you were attacked by a monster, you’d be finished in an instant.”

Hearing Elena’s words, Ray recalled hearing that some high-end inns offered such services.

That said, the “Dusk Wheat inn” in Gilm—Rei’s regular inn—didn’t employ any mages capable of summoning magic.

“Hmm. The mage I employ is quite skilled, even among summoners. That’s why I can use the griffin’s feather—or the monsters I summon—to return to my territory immediately.”

“So there’s no problem then? In that case, I’ll head out right away… but is it okay to go like this?”

“I’ve already told my people to come immediately if I call, so if you send someone to summon them, there won’t be any problem.”

With Remuria saying that, Rei’s next course of action was decided in an instant.

“Well then, I’ll be counting on you.”

The mage summoned by Lemuria bowed his head without a moment’s hesitation upon seeing Rei and the others.

……If the person bowing had been Elena, he would have thought it only natural.

Given that Brewitt was the heir apparent to a noble house, it wouldn’t have been strange for him to bow to her.

But for some reason, it was Rei who was bowed to.

Given that Rei was also famous as an adventurer with a nickname, it wasn’t all that strange for him to be bowed to.

It wasn’t strange… but still, the fact that he bowed to me first, before Elena or Brewitt, was something I couldn’t quite wrap my head around.

“Uh… just lift your head for now. I appreciate the respect, but I’m not quite used to being treated that way.”

“I-Is that so? Um… You won’t call me rude and cut off my limbs, will you?”

“...I might need to look into what kind of rumors are actually circulating.”

Rei was momentarily confused as to where such a story had come from, but when he thought about it, since there had been rumors circulating that he would sever the limbs of nobles, it wouldn’t be strange if that rumor had been slightly distorted into him having a “hobby” of severing the limbs of mages.

As far as Rei was concerned, it wasn’t funny at all.

“Uh, um… yes. Well, I’ll leave it to you.”

The mage looked up timidly. Seeing that Rei wasn’t holding a Death Scythe in his hand, he felt a slight sense of relief, and the tension in his body eased ever so slightly.

“Ho ho ho.”

Lemuria had been watching Rei and the mage she’d hired with amusement, but realizing there was no point in lingering any longer, she headed straight for the stables.

As for Elena, who wanted to stay with Rei, that was one thing, but Rei was puzzled as to why Brewitt had tagged along as well.

They ran into a few nobles and servants along the way, but the nobles didn’t approach them since Elena and Remuria—two big names—were together, and the servants didn’t feel the need to speak to them since Rei and Elena were together.

…Though, setting the nobles aside, it might be more accurate to say the servants were too busy with party preparations to have the time to speak to Elena and the others.

In any case, Rei and the others proceeded without being spoken to or reprimanded by anyone, leaving the mansion and arriving at their destination—the stables that served as Set’s private quarters while he was there.

“Grrrrrrrrrrr!”

Perhaps sensing Rei’s presence, Set’s delighted voice could be heard from inside the stable.

Still, unlike yesterday, he didn’t break through the stable walls or doors to come out.

(Oh, the stable wall... I guess it’s already been repaired?)

Seeing the wall that had been damaged during yesterday’s commotion while repelling Count Caseres’ men, Rei thought this to himself, yet, as if pushed by Lemuria’s gaze, he opened the stable door.
