Rei and Vihera, at the point where Set's gaze landed, that individual—which had risen from an ordinary goblin to become a goblin leader—let out a fierce cry... no, a battle cry.

Then, as if drawn by that sound, the goblins came into view.

What's more, even though they passed right near Rei, the goblins showed no sign of noticing her or her companions, continuing to advance toward their leader.

The number of goblins that gathered was about fifty.

Considering that the groups led by the goblin leaders Rei had defeated so far were usually around a hundred, this was only about half that number.

"What do you think?"

"Hmm. Several goblin groups have vanished recently. Could it be that the goblin population in this area has actually been dwindling?"

"It's possible, but do you really think the goblin population could be decreasing?"

"...The scary part is I can't say it won't."

Goblins breed from humans, beastmen, elves... hell, even from the females of other monsters, and even from female animals.

Rei simply couldn't believe that such goblins would become so scarce they couldn't form groups anymore, just because a few were killed.

Vihera felt the same way; her words were probably just a tentative suggestion.

"If that's the case, then the other goblins will probably take a little more time to gather."

"Probably."

Vihera agreed with Rei's words and turned her gaze back to the goblin horde.

There, goblins were gathering around the goblin leader.

Even watching now, the black mist no longer lingered on the goblin leader's body.

The remnants of Ambrith, if you could call it that, had completely vanished.

"Chasing after the goblin horde like this probably won't accomplish much. They're likely heading for Gilm anyway."

"Grrrr..."

At Rei's words, Vihera turned her gaze toward the direction the goblin that had evolved into the goblin leader had come from.

As if in agreement, Set growled.

"Yeah. We'll have a better chance of finding Ambrith if we check that way. Alright, let's go. Wait, there's something we need to do first."

Muttering, Rei drew the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring and chanted a spell.

'Flames, you shall exist only within the domain I designate, and shall not exist in any other domain. As the price for your brief life, feed upon my magic within that domain and sublimate your life in an instant.'

Simultaneously with the incantation, he thrust the pommel of the Death Scythe into the ground.

As the pommel pierced the earth, a crimson line began to run across the ground, radiating outward from the point of impact.

That crimson line traces a circle, encircling the goblin horde centered around the Goblin Leader...

'Fire Spirit Dance'

As the words flowed from Rei's mouth, the magic activated. A crimson force field erupted from the line tracing the ground, ultimately transforming into a red dome.

The crimson dome swallowed the goblin horde whole, then proceeded to generate fire spirits shaped like lizards.

Countless lizards were spawned within the crimson dome, their numbers far exceeding those of the goblins.

The goblin who had become the leader screamed while looking around frantically, but his voice was inaudible to Rei.

And with their leader failing to properly command them, it was only natural that the other goblins were thrown into chaos.

Inside the crimson dome, the goblins screamed. Naturally, some individuals vented their frustration on the lizards within the dome.

But... that very act sealed the fate of the goblins within the crimson dome.

The lizard that seemed about to be struck by a goblin exploded, shattering part of the goblin's body.

Moreover, the shockwave from that explosion triggered other lizards to explode, shattering goblins as yet another lizard exploded... and so on. One after another, the goblins were caught in the chain of lizard explosions and died.

The lizards themselves detonated from the shockwave, which in turn detonated other lizards... creating a chain reaction of explosions.

The chain reaction of lizard explosions quickly filled the crimson dome with explosions.

"This is... incredible,"

Vihera, seeing this magic for the first time, murmured in awe.

The explosions occurring within the crimson dome would normally be considered utterly cruel.

But outside the crimson dome, no sound from within could be heard.

Yes, not the sound of the explosions themselves, nor the screams and cries of agony from the goblins being killed by them.

All visible from the outside was the pure explosion itself.

The silent explosions occurring within the crimson dome were undoubtedly fascinating to Vihera as well.

Though, since Rei knew about fireworks, seeing the explosions within the red dome didn't particularly surprise him.

Besides, this wasn't the first time he'd used this magic.

(Should I say something like... 'Damn, that's some dirty fireworks,' or something?)

Rei muttered to himself while watching the results of his magic. Eventually, all the lizards created inside the crimson dome must have exploded and vanished. The crimson dome itself began to fade away.

Simultaneously, a thick, pungent smell of rust began to permeate the air.

After the red dome vanished and the remnants of the explosion were carried away by the wind, what remained was ground that had been reduced to a paste—flesh, bone, and organs alike—so thoroughly pulverized by the blast that nothing recognizable remained.

It didn't matter if it was a goblin or a goblin leader—it was all reduced to meat paste.

(If it had been a monster more powerful than goblins, it probably wouldn't have been this spectacular. Well, if the corpses weren't recognizable, they couldn't become undead, so easier cleanup is a plus.)

Even if there were only about fifty of them, considering the effort saved in collecting and burning the goblin corpses, using this magic was by no means excessive.

After confirming not a single survivor remained, including the Goblin Leader, Rei called out to Vihera, who stood beside him, her expression slightly displeased by the goblin blood's stench.

"Vihera, stop spacing out. We're going to find Ambris, right?"

"...Oh, right. I completely forgot seeing that scene just now."

"Grrrr."

Sensing something in Vihera's demeanor, Set purred and rubbed his face against hers.

Set smiled at him and stroked his body.

As she stroked Set, her mood gradually shifted. Before a minute had passed, Vihera spoke.

"Well then, shall we go? We need to find any clues we can about Ambris right now."

With that, Vihera started walking in the direction the goblin leader had come from earlier.

Naturally, Rei and Set followed suit, trailing after Vihera.

As they continued walking through the grasslands, Rei soon spoke up, looking troubled.

"Hey, Rei. The goblin who walked this way before becoming the leader had a pretty shaky gait, didn't he? If that's the case, heading straight back the way the goblins came might be pointless."

"...Now that you mention it, you're right."

Recalling the goblin's unsteady gait, which could aptly be described as staggering, Rei agreed with Vihera's words.

But unlike Vihera, who seemed slightly hesitant, Rei didn't appear concerned at all.

Because even in this situation, Rei knew an effective way to track them.

"Set, I need your help."

Set understood exactly what Rei was asking for with just that, and he purred happily.

As if to prove that being relied upon by Rei was something that made Set very happy indeed.

And so, Set activated his skill: Enhanced Sense of Smell.

His sense of smell was already sharp enough to lead them to the Hundred-Faced Ones' base outside the city before.

Now, in this short time, it had risen to Level Four, making Set's sense of smell even sharper than before.

Though about fifty goblins gathered in the end, tracing the path of just one specific one back to where it came from was no difficult task for Set.

"Grrrr..."

Set growled, signaling "This way," stepping in front of Vihera and leading the two forward.

Vihera glanced at Rei, who nodded reassuringly.

Seeing this, Vihera felt reassured. She followed Set, moving forward with Rei.

"I never knew Set had this kind of tracking ability."

"Well, it's not exactly a flashy ability."

It wasn't a flashy skill like Fire Breath, but it was undoubtedly extremely useful nonetheless.

Set didn't possess many skills usable outside of combat, but heightened sense of smell was one of the rare exceptions.

Like Water Sphere, combat skills that could also be used outside of battle weren't exactly rare. ...Though, since Rei acquired the Water Dagger, Water Sphere had seen far less use for hydration purposes.

Anyway, with Set's current heightened sense of smell, heading to where the goblin who became the goblin leader had come from—that is, the place where he made contact with Ambris—was easy.

Following Set's guidance, they soon spotted a small grove ahead.

It was truly a small grove, so small that one could tell at a glance it was unlikely to harbor many monsters or wild animals.

"...Is this it?"

"Gluu!"

Set growled confidently in response to Rei's questioning look.

Following the path taken by the goblin who had become a Goblin Leader, reaching this spot was an undeniable fact, even by Set's keen sense of smell.

"So even goblins living in a place like this can evolve into goblin leaders, huh?"

"No, where they live doesn't really matter, does it?"

"Really? Don't they need to gain various experiences to evolve? If so, could a goblin living in a place like this even gain those experiences?"

The words that came out of Vihera's mouth were something even Rei could understand.

This forest, too narrow for high-rank monsters to inhabit, might actually be a surprisingly good dwelling place for goblins.

Of course, if it was livable for goblins, it meant other low-rank monsters could live there too.

But if there were no absolute powerhouses, it was undeniable that the goblins' chances of survival increased.

"...But then, that means there's a possibility other low-rank monsters live here too. Couldn't we also be denying the possibility that Ambris's ability has made them a leader species?"

"That's possible. But Ambrith can only make humanoid monsters into leader species, right? If that's the case, and the others are different kinds of monsters..."

"So there are no leader species, huh?"

"Yes. ...Though, making humanoid monsters into leader species was a characteristic specific to the Ambrith from three hundred years ago. It's not necessarily true for this Ambrith either."

"But right now, all the Leader Species leading packs are humanoid, aren't they? So I think it's safe to assume this time is the same."

The moment Rei uttered what could be called an optimistic remark, Vihera opened her mouth in a reprimanding tone.

"Look, Rei. This is only the second sighting of the Ambrith, right? So, there's a possibility it could be something completely different, isn't there?"

That was undeniably true.

With only one precedent, it wouldn't be strange if an Ambrith with entirely different abilities appeared.

Still, Rei wasn't overly concerned.

In fact, all the swarms created so far were composed entirely of humanoid monsters.

If this Umbris was different from the previous one, the monster that produced the leader species should have been entirely different as well.

Of course, he wasn't being careless, but he still didn't believe this Amblis was a completely different entity.

"Gururuu"

Set growled, interrupting Rei and Vihera's conversation.

Was it moving across the ground? That thought crossed Rei's mind, but the next movement Ambrith displayed wasn't to remain on the surface—it was to sink into the ground.

Even mist normally cannot burrow into the ground.

It proved that while it appeared to be black mist at first glance, it was not mist at all.
