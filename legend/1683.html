The sight of the sunset sinking into the sea evokes a deep sense of wonder, even though there is nothing particularly special about it.

For those who have lived by the sea since childhood, such a sight might be one they’ve seen countless times.

But for Rei, who grew up in the mountains—or rather, deep in the mountains—and only visits the sea a few times a year, the sight of the sun sinking below the horizon was overwhelmingly moving.

Even after coming to Elgin, Rei has made the inland town of Gilm his base.

Of course, it’s not as if he’d never been to the sea, like Emoshion… but even so, he can’t help but be captivated by the current scene.

Set, who is carrying Rei on his back, seems to understand Rei’s feelings; he doesn’t make any noises like a growl.

On the contrary, he flies through the sky as slowly as possible.

About ten minutes passed like that... and eventually, perhaps satisfied with the sight of the setting sun, Rei gave Set’s back a light pat and spoke.

“Set, to Galanka… Ah, no. Since there’s no one here anyway, maybe I should finish absorbing the magic stone while I still can.”

The magic stone from the monster that looked like a black sea bream, which they had just obtained today.

Naturally, he didn’t want Bistol or Byune to see him absorbing that magic stone.

Given that, there was no doubt that absorbing the magic stone now, while no one was around, was the best course of action.

“Grr?”

Set turns around to look at Rei and asks, “What are you going to do?”

In response, Rei thinks for a moment... and eventually makes a decision.

“Alright, before we go to Galanka, head down to the surface. Let’s absorb the magic stones from the daytime monsters.”

“Growl!”

"Got it!" Set let out a cry and began descending toward the ground.

It wasn’t even a forest—it was more like a patch of trees, so sparse that one would hesitate to call it a grove.

Since they hadn’t decided on a specific spot, they simply landed there.

Once they landed, Rei surveyed the surroundings with a somewhat half-hearted air.

Though, given that Set was there, the area had already been thoroughly scouted.

Rei, whose senses were inferior to Set’s, didn’t even think he could spot an opponent that even Set couldn’t find.

She didn’t think so… but still, she scanned the surroundings with a sense of “just in case.”

This wasn’t because he didn’t trust Set; it was simply something Rei did to put his own mind at ease.

“Yeah, it’s clear. …Well, that leaves… whether Set or Death Scythe will absorb the magic stone… what do you say?”

“Grr…”

Set let out a meow, sounding apologetic, as if to say he wanted to eat the magic stone himself.

He probably understands that since he ate Guztos’s magic stone, he should let Rei—or more accurately, Death Scythe—eat the magic stone this time.

But right now, Set somehow felt like he wanted to absorb more magic stones.

Was it because he’d seen the sunset over the sea, or had the battle with the monster given him something to think about?

Rei couldn’t say for sure, but there was no doubt that Set wanted to absorb the magic stone.

“No?” Set looked at him with those round eyes, and Rei paused for a moment… just a few seconds.

And in the end, he gave in to Set’s request.

That said, Rei had various means of attack besides the Death Scythe skill.

Even a standard Death Scythe strike possessed devastating power, and as a dual-spear user, he had the Twilight Spear, not to mention magic.

There were also other options, like throwing disposable spears or using Nebula’s Eye.

Thinking about it that way, he concluded that it would be better to use the magic stone to strengthen Set after all.

“Alright. Here you go, Set. Take this.”

Saying that, Rei handed the magic stone to Set.

Set caught it with his beak...

[Set has learned the skill "Ice Arrow Lv. 3"]

Yes, the announcement message echoed in his mind.

“Ice Arrow… Well, I guess it makes sense since it was firing scales at me and it’s a fish monster… right?”

“Grr?”

Mimicking Rei’s puzzled tilt of the head, Set tilts his own head as well.

But it wasn’t because he, like Rei, had doubts about the skill he’d just learned… it was simply because he was imitating Rei.

Well, of course, Set does have his doubts about having learned Ice Arrow, but it’s more accurate to say he’s more concerned about Rei’s reaction.

“Well, whatever. For now, let’s check exactly how much Ice Arrow has been strengthened. …It’s only Level 3, so I don’t think it’s that powerful.”

Since Water Ball had just reached Level 5, where its performance skyrocketed, the fact that Ice Arrow was only Level 3 left him feeling, “Hmm…” to be honest.

“Grr!”

In response to Rei’s words, Set let out a cry that seemed to say, “Leave it to me!”

(Five arrows at Level 1, ten at Level 2... So, logically speaking, that would mean about fifteen at Level 3, right?)

As Rei pondered this, Set activated Ice Arrow.

The number of ice arrows floating around Set was exactly fifteen, just as Rei had predicted.

Satisfied that his prediction had been correct, Rei nodded, but he also wondered what would happen as the level increased.

If the number increases by five per level, that would mean twenty at Level 4.

That’s easy to predict, but as seen with Water Ball, the skills learned through Beast Magic become significantly more powerful at Level 5.

(If that’s the case, it won’t just be a simple increase to twenty-five ice arrows… will it?)

As he pondered this, watching the ice arrows floating beside Set, Set looked at Rei as if to ask, “What’s wrong?”

The way he looked was so adorable that if Milein—famous for her love of Set—had been there, she would undoubtedly have hugged him.

Rei felt like stroking that adorable Set, but he held back and spoke up.

“Yeah, for now… well, try shooting at that tree over there.”

“Grrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl and fired an Ice Arrow.

The Ice Arrow flew through the air, slicing through it. Its speed was slightly faster than before—in fact, it was a bit faster than the one he’d used earlier today when fighting that fish monster.

But... the Ice Arrows, striking the tree trunk in rapid succession, managed to shatter the trunk before any of them could actually embed themselves.

“Ah… I see. Well, I suppose that makes sense. It was pretty powerful to begin with, so I guess this is what happens.”

Even a Level 2 Ice Arrow had enough power to shatter rock if five of them hit.

So if fifteen were fired, it’s only natural that a tree trunk would be easily destroyed.

“Grr?”

As Set turned to look at him, asking, “What are we going to do?” Rei stared at the shattered tree trunk and the fallen tree… Eventually, resigning himself to it, he stowed the broken branch in his Misty Ring and returned to Set’s side.

“Well, let’s head to Galanka for now. I think we’ll call it a day on the Ice Arrow experiments.”

At Rei’s words, Set nodded without objection.

With that, Rei climbed back onto Set’s back and they rose into the air.

Just as before, the setting sun sinking beyond the horizon seemed, in a way, to be consoling Rei for his failure.

Though, to be fair, Rei himself didn’t consider the whole sequence of events a failure.

After all, Set’s Ice Arrow had indeed been activated, and it was clear that its power was by no means weak.

If that was the case, what exactly constituted a failure...

(Exactly how much damage would have been dealt if all fifteen Ice Arrows had hit their mark... Was it a bit of a failure that I didn’t know that?)

He couldn’t help but think so, but for the time being, he convinced himself that his actions hadn’t been a failure as he flew through the sky… and eventually, by the time the sun had sunk more than halfway below the horizon, he arrived at Galanka.

Next to Galanka stood an earthen prison created by Marina using spirit magic.

However, since the pirates already had slave collars on, they couldn’t just start rampaging around pointlessly.

For that reason, the earthen cell is designed so they can come and go as they please.

That said, the pirates wearing the slave collars have been ordered not to leave.

As Rei looked at the earthen cell, he seemed to feel nothing in particular.

Considering he’s a former pirate and what he’s done up to this point, it’s not even a case of “you reap what you sow.”

No, since it’s clear that the pirates will be sold to the Daskar, their treatment is actually quite decent compared to ordinary pirates.

They’re given decent meals, and their movements aren’t strictly restricted.

There’s no doubt that the slave trader, Lilrose, thought to herself: if she brought Gilm back and he was emaciated and useless, she had no idea what kind of backlash she’d face.

If things went well, it was a business opportunity as big as it could possibly be, but precisely because of that, there was a high chance of ruin if things went wrong.

Even as he watched the earthen dungeon housing those pirates, Set descended toward the ground.

Perhaps still unaccustomed to Set’s presence, several villagers who first spotted him looked startled, but they quickly snapped out of it and began moving.

It was a relief for Rei that their actions didn’t resemble a defensive battle.

As Set, having descended to the ground, approached the Garanka, a man eventually emerged from within.

To Rei, too, this was a familiar face within Garanka… Pastra.

“Glad you came. Considering what we talked about this morning, I thought you’d arrive a little sooner.”

“I’ve had my own share of trouble. So, how’s the catch?”

“Oh, I’ve caught plenty. And there are plenty of the shellfish Rei asked for, too.”

A pleased smile spread across Pastra’s face as he said this.

That was only natural, since Rei had agreed to buy every single fish he’d caught today.

To the people of Galanka, Rei was truly a source of wealth.

Even the villagers, who had been afraid of Set at first, couldn’t help but break into happy smiles when they heard what Rei and Pastra had to say.

“Well then, let’s take a look right away. So, where are they? It’d be better if I went to get them myself rather than having you bring them to me, right?”

At those words, the villagers, including Pastra, nodded and led Rei toward the sandy beach within the village—toward the hut where Rei had previously been shown the nets and harpoons.

Since it’s right by the sea, the fish they’ve caught must be kept there.

As they walked, Rei asked Pastra,

“What do the caught fish look like? I can’t store them in my Misty Ring if they’re still alive.”

“Since the fish were caught with a harpoon, I think they’re mostly dead… but how do you kill the shellfish?”

“Well, I don’t know how to answer that. You guys are the experts on that, right?”

At Rei’s words, Pastra looked around, looking troubled.

But all the others can do in the face of Pastra’s gaze is simply look away.

Actually, killing a shellfish isn’t that difficult.

If you simply put them in boiling water or roast them while still in their shells, they’ll undoubtedly die.

However… that merely means they die during the cooking process; you can’t actually kill them intentionally.

With fish, you can easily kill them by snapping their necks, but clams, of course, have no bones.

If you were to break the shell, the clam would undoubtedly die, but that would undoubtedly make cooking it quite a hassle.

...Besides, removing the shell fragments one by one is quite a hassle.

“What should we do?” The villagers exchanged glances, their faces filled with worry.

“For now, I’ll just take them as they are. Once we get there, I’ll ask Marina if she can do something about it with spirit magic. If that doesn’t work, I’ll store the ones that have been grilled or boiled in the Misty Ring.”

“If that’s fine with Rei, then it’s fine with us too. …Are you really sure about that?”

“Yeah. Of course, keep catching clams tomorrow too. I’ll buy them just like I do the fish.”

At Rei’s words, several of those traveling with him let out sighs of relief.

They were likely the ones who’d been catching shellfish instead of fish.

Led by Rei, they arrived at the sandy beach.

There, several nets were submerged in the sea, and there was no doubt that they contained clams and fish.

“Alright, well, putting the clams aside for now, let’s start gutting the fish! Everyone, give me a hand.”

At Pastra’s words, the villagers who had been traveling with them nodded… and others who were free came over as well, each taking a fish to gut.
