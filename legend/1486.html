In a room within a building in the slums, Levegef now faced the man before him.

Seated on a luxurious sofa that seemed out of place in the slums was a man in his fifties.

He possessed an undeniable commanding presence, entirely convincing as the leader of an organization, showing no sign whatsoever of diminished abilities due to age.

He didn't have any visible slash wounds on his face, yet the aura he emitted was distinctly different.

Levyev had seen his share of battles, yet he sometimes felt overwhelmed by the aura emanating from the man before him.

Naturally, since he was speaking directly with the organization's boss, bodyguards were present in the room, and Levjef had been disarmed before entering.

"You think you can walk all over me?"

Several minutes passed after Levegef voiced his demand.

The silence was finally broken as the boss spoke.

His tone carried a hint of displeasure. Sensing this, the guards stood ready, their weapons drawn, poised to attack Levegef at a moment's notice.

This was to ensure they could react instantly if negotiations broke down or if Levegef made any strange moves.

Having spent years as an adventurer and a rogue, Levjef was confident in his own skills.

Even so, if the guards attacked him, survival would be difficult.

The guards possessed the skill to make him think that.

(Especially that beastman guy... cat? Leopard? He's troublesome. His skills are definitely above mine.)

He briefly glanced at the man among the guards who seemed the most skilled, but quickly refocused his attention on the boss.

"Of course not. I'm indebted to you, Jestar-san."

At Levjef's words, the boss... Gestal frowned for a moment.

Whether he was irritated by the blatant flattery or his thoughts turned elsewhere,

Even Levjef, receiving that displeased gaze, couldn't tell.

"Yeah, I figured. That's exactly why I went along with your unreasonable demands and forcibly gathered the women. Even with that sudden deadline shortening. But... what now? Finished today? What's with that attitude, after asking me to do the job?"

He took a sip of wine, but the lukewarm liquid only soured his mood further.

"Of course, I understand why you're upset, Gestal-san. But we have our own circumstances too."

"That goes without saying. And just as you have your reasons, I have mine. Especially since gathering women is necessary, I absolutely have to prioritize my own circumstances."

"We certainly understand your situation. So, the women gathered so far are sufficient. We'll pay the fee as originally agreed. ...How about it? Would that satisfy you?"

At that condition, Gestal suppressed a slightly displeased expression.

Normally, Levjef's proposal would be nothing but advantageous for the organization.

They could receive the pre-contracted payment without expending any further effort.

But... even understanding that, Gestal did not nod in agreement on the spot.

Bringing such a sweet deal meant there was definitely something behind it.

"...What did you lot do?"

Setting his wine glass on the table, Gestal stared intently at Levegef.

"You told us to cut short the job we were hired for precisely because you messed something up. If that's the case, you must have a reason you need to hurry, right?"

"Well..."

Levegef hesitated for a moment, considering whether to evade the question. But if he tried to dodge it and they caught him, things would definitely get messy.

Therefore, he decided to speak without hiding everything, concealing only the crucial details—the information that would make them want to back out.

Since they were leaving Gilm tonight, he figured it wouldn't matter too much if some trouble broke out after they were gone. That was his reasoning.

"As you can understand, given that we've been dealing with underground organizations like Gestal's and that the nature of those deals involves gathering women, we too have our own shady business. In fact, we've been involved in all sorts of crimes up until now."

Everyone listening to Levjef's words wore expressions that seemed to say, "Well, that's obvious."

After all, anyone without skeletons in their closet wouldn't have contacted them in the first place.

"And naturally, that includes killing people. ...And those who are killed naturally resent the ones who killed them, right?"

"You bet."

Gestal nodded back, as if that too was only natural.

Gestal, too, had endured many bitter experiences.

It wasn't just once or twice that acquaintances or subordinates had been killed by members of other organizations.

In such cases, he had naturally sought revenge against the perpetrators, so he understood the feeling of vengeance all too well.

Though in Gestal's case, beyond the hatred for having acquaintances killed, there was also the aspect that he couldn't afford to be looked down upon if he was to survive in the underworld.

In any case, having experienced revenge many times, Gestal understood it well.

And from Levjef's words, he understood why he'd insisted on carrying out the deal today, even though the deadline had already been shortened.

(This guy's got some skill too. ...If that's the case, then the one who showed up to take revenge must have been a real expert? Well, skilled fighters aren't exactly rare in Gilm.)

Gilm had always been a gathering place for many skilled adventurers.

Thinking about it that way, it wasn't surprising that the person who came to kill Levegef and his companions was a skilled fighter.

"I see. Fine, I'll accept that you're not looking down on me."

Jestal's mistake at that moment was probably not asking who had come to avenge Levjef and the others.

Of course, knowing full well what would happen if he mentioned Rei's name, Levjef wouldn't have named Rei even if Gestal had asked; he would have only mentioned Ilse's name.

But if the conversation had gone that far, there was a good chance Gestal would have questioned Levjef's words.

And if he had felt doubt, he might have investigated the circumstances... and reached Rei. The possibility was slim, but it existed.

But Gestal neglected to do so.

No, Levjef's manner of speaking, the way he spoke of Levjef or his associates as enemies, suggested he was someone who had only recently arrived in Gilm. In other words, he gave the impression he wasn't someone who had been in Gilm for a long time.

This was partly due to Gestal's carelessness, but more so because Levegef was skilled at steering the conversation.

As a result, Gestal stopped prying further into who was pursuing Levjef and his group.

His existing dislike for Levjef was undoubtedly a major factor.

Deciding he didn't want to be dragged into Levjef's affairs any further, he spoke up.

"Alright. I'll make a deal with you based on your situation. But I don't have that many people gathered. You understand that, right?"

"Yes. I suppose that can't be helped. So, the deal will be tonight... right. Around midnight. And, as I mentioned before, the method to get us out of Gilm with the carriage..."

"Huh? That's impossible, obviously."

Relieved that the deal would proceed smoothly, Levjef moved on to the next topic.

They had always planned to escape Gilm tonight. Given that, they certainly couldn't just bolt out the main gate.

Moreover, they'd been told Jestar had a plan to slip past the guards and escape outside Gilm, and Levjef and the others were counting on that.

...After all, it was impossible to openly lead dozens of women, especially women wearing slave collars, out through the main gate.

But Jestar, without even seeming to consider it, flatly declared it impossible in response to Levjef's words.

"Why? Leaving Gilm was part of our original contract, wasn't it?"

"True. It was in the contract... the original, initial contract. That contract didn't stipulate it would end this quickly. You forced us to change the transaction date on a whim. We can't just bend everything to suit your convenience."

Jestal's words drew silent nods from the surrounding guards, who intensified their glances at Levjef.

It served to pressure Levjef into accepting Gestal's words, while simultaneously keeping him on guard against Levjef losing his mind and attacking Gestal.

"But originally..."

"Listen, it might have been that way originally. But you're the one who forcibly changed the schedule. Arranging things properly isn't something you can just do on the spot."

Hearing that, Levegef couldn't help but show some understanding of Gestal's words.

Originally, the deal with Gestal was supposed to be handled patiently until the requested number of people were gathered.

They had demanded it be done as quickly as possible for their own convenience, insisting on conducting the transaction tonight.

Naturally, Gestal hadn't anticipated that proposal, and it was difficult for him to arrange the escape plan immediately.

But given the current situation, it was also true that they absolutely had to make that impossible demand work somehow.

After all, even if only the women were handed over, failing to escape from Gilm would draw significant attention.

It was simply impossible for Levjef and his men to hide dozens of women, even while lurking in the slums.

"Can't you make that work?"

"...If you ask whether it's possible or not, of course it is. But doing so will cost considerably more than originally planned, you understand?"

"Understood. We can add that amount to the reward."

Levjef's expression was stern as he said this, but inwardly, he was relieved.

He had anticipated the cost increasing for this particular job.

Considering that, this development was also as expected.

Seeing Levjef readily agree to the increased payment, Gestal paused to consider for a moment before finally speaking.

"Five platinum coins, in addition to the original payment."

"What!?"

Levjef understood that additional payment would be necessary.

But five platinum coins was unexpected.

"That's far too much, no matter how you look at it!"

That Levjef, who had been calm throughout the discussion, shouted like this clearly showed how absurd the amount Jestal had named was.

Watching Levjef's reaction, Gestal took another sip of the now-warmed wine.

It seemed as if he were silently telling Levegef to calm down.

"Still, you see. We were already facing a time constraint, and now you're rushing us like this. We need a fair amount to prepare. Let me be clear: our profit is practically nonexistent in those five platinum coins. Almost all of it is necessary expenses."

There was no way for Levegef to tell if those words were true.

Was it really true that nearly all five platinum coins covered necessary expenses, or was this a calculated attempt to squeeze more money out of them now?

But still, since it was Levjef and his group who demanded a drastically shortened deadline for the deal and insisted it be done tonight, they had no choice left.

No, they could still abandon the deal entirely and vanish with Ajax and Haston.

But... Ajax could be stripped of his adventurer status, and the incident involving Ilse's family could come to light, potentially leading to him being named a wanted criminal.

And if the Guild investigated, they could easily uncover the possibility that Levjef and Haston had been secretly connected to Ajax.

And this latest incident... If word gets out that they forcibly abducted dozens of women and even went so far as to put slave collars on them, Levjef and Huston would undoubtedly become wanted criminals too.

If he were to throw away the deal itself now, he'd face assassins not only from the guild hunting bounties, but also from the organization Levjef and his men currently belong to, and even from Gestal standing right before him.

To prevent that, they had to at least complete the transaction... In the end, all Levjeff could do was nod to the amount they demanded.
