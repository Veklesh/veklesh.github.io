The butler and Deotres both underwent a rapid physical transformation due to the blood vessels—or perhaps tentacles—that had been driven into the back of their necks from the flesh cocoon.

To be specific, their bodies nearly doubled in size, and their muscles swelled accordingly.

Their ears elongated, fangs grew, and scales and fur sprouted across their faces.

Furthermore, a tail even sprouted from their waists... and their appearance had become such that it was simply impossible to believe they had been human just moments ago.

The transformation wasn’t yet complete; it was still ongoing, and the creature continued to change form.

Rei and Elena attacked the two of them… or perhaps they should already be called “the two of them.”

The first thing fired was an arrowhead created by Rei using Nebra’s eyes.

Flicking that arrow with his finger... in a motion akin to a finger bullet, he fires them one after another at the two monstrous forms.

Under normal circumstances, this would only inflict damage equivalent to a slight prick, making it an attack useful only for holding the enemy at bay.

But that’s only under normal circumstances—Rei’s physical abilities are far from ordinary.

The projectiles pierced the bodies of the two, who had not yet fully completed their transformation, one after another.

A series of strikes—tearing through skin, gouging into flesh, and shaking the very bones—were unleashed… yet the two monstrosities showed no sign of being bothered by attacks that, had they been in their pre-transformed state, would have caused them to scream in pain.

On the contrary, they remained silent, as if they weren’t even feeling any pain.

“Do they have no sense of pain!? Elena!”

“I know!”

Realizing that firing more arrows was pointless, Rei called out to Elena, who was running behind him.

Elena, hearing his call, quickly replied to Rei’s words and swung her whip-like Mirage.

Taking on the form of a whip—one of the two forms characteristic of the Linked Sword—the Mirage lunged at the two monstrous creatures.

It was a sharp strike that seemed to slice through not just the air, but space itself.

In fact, the blade of the Mirage sliced through the torso of one of the two monstrosities—the butler—and… before long, just as it seemed to have severed the torso in two, it continued to slice through Deotres’s torso, albeit with slightly diminished force.

However, having severed the butler’s torso, its momentum had likely diminished. After cutting through about thirty percent of Deotres’s torso, Mirage’s movement came to a halt.

That said, just because its movement stopped didn’t mean Elena’s attack was over.

Maintaining the momentum of the strike with Mirage, Elena pulled the hilt she was gripping back sharply the next instant.

It was the act of pulling the whip-like blade of Mirage back toward her, and naturally, the blade returned to Elena’s hand. …Yes, from the state where it had sliced through about thirty percent of Deotres’ torso and come to a halt.

The blade of the Mirage returned to Elena’s hand, slicing through Deotres’ torso—bones, flesh, skin, and internal organs.

Once she had Mirage’s blade back in her hand, Elena transformed it back into a longsword, swung it once to shake off the blood, flesh, and fragments of entrails clinging to the blade, and shouted.

「Rei！」

Rei, called by Elena, moves to attack—the opposite of when he had called out her name moments earlier.

The arrowhead formed from Nebula’s eyes was truly nothing more than a distraction, but that was strictly true only at long to medium range.

Once they got close—within striking distance of Rei’s fists—he could choose to use his own fists and feet as weapons.

They hadn’t exchanged a single word, yet for Rei and Elena, it was easy to coordinate their attacks without even looking at each other.

This was proof that there was a solid bond of trust between them.

“Haaah!”

Deotres had feigned having his torso sliced open and his internal organs destroyed, but then Rei’s fist came crashing down on him.

Physical abilities surpassing those of an ordinary human, and movements honed through battle.

All of that was concentrated into Rei’s fist… and the blow he unleashed succeeded in sending Deotres—a figure far larger than Rei himself, one who could truly be called a giant—flying.

What Rei felt through his fist was a sensation as if he’d punched a thick rubber tire… and the feeling of shattering ribs with his fist.

Had Rei possessed a technique or skill like Vihera’s—the ability to drive the impact directly into an enemy’s body—he could have inflicted even greater damage on Deotres.

Unfortunately, Rei was unable to do such a thing.

Even so, Rei’s powerful strike succeeded in landing a blow that could be considered fatal to Deotres… yet, despite being sent flying, Deotres came to a halt mid-air.

This wasn’t because Rei used his Sleipnir Boots, nor was it because he forcibly stopped himself in midair using some kind of psychic power.

Purely, it was because the tentacle extending from the back of Deotres’ neck had reached its maximum length that his movement stopped in midair.

Knocked back by Rei’s power, his movement was forcibly halted in midair by the tentacle.

Naturally, this dealt a considerable blow to Deotres, but it meant nothing to him now that his pain receptors were numb.

Watching the massive body slammed into the ground as the tentacles reached their limit, Rei did not launch a follow-up attack on Deotres... but instead turned toward another figure... Celestine.

It was clearly Celestine who was controlling the flesh cocoon… and the tentacles.

Given that, and having already rendered the butler and Deotres unable to fight, it was only natural to attack Celestine here.

But... even as Rei approached, Celestine, still gently touching the flesh cocoon, showed no sign of fear.

Could it be that he still has another trick up his sleeve?

Rei entertained that thought for just a moment, but considering the current situation, he decided that if he could take Celestane down before he could use that ace up his sleeve, that would be best. He clenched his fist...

「Rei！」

Elena’s voice rang out unexpectedly from behind him.

At the same time, realizing that something was being fired at him, slicing through the air, Rei abandoned his attack on Celestan, changed direction, and put distance between himself and Celestan.

A moment later, something passed by the spot where Rei had been standing.

While putting distance between himself and Celestine, Rei turned his gaze in that direction… and, unusually, was left speechless.

Because what was there… or rather, because he was able to clearly see where the thing that had attacked Rei had come from.

It was the butler, his torso nearly severed.

The intestines protruding from that severed torso... were being wielded like a whip.

To protect his master from Rei.

But such a butler… no, considering that, like Deotres, his body had swollen to the size of a giant, it would be more accurate to call him a “former butler.”

In any case, upon seeing the butler’s transformed form, Rei felt bitter that his attack on Celestine had been interrupted… but in the next instant, a sudden doubt crossed his mind.

(Is the torso still partially connected?)

Yes, that was the very nature of his doubt.

The butler’s body should have been severed in two—top and bottom—by the whip-like Mirage strike Elena unleashed while he was in the midst of growing to gigantic size.

That was a fact Rei had clearly confirmed with his own eyes.

If the severed torso had been right next to him, he might have overlooked it, thinking it hadn’t been completely severed.

But Rei saw with his own eyes that the butler’s torso, severed in two, had been flung in opposite directions by the force of the Mirage.

And yet, the butler’s torso was still connected like this.

Rei was startled by the butler’s appearance, but when he considered the nature of the being he was facing, he realized this sight wasn’t all that surprising.

“Hahahaha, this is incredible. I never thought it could go this far… Mirales, are you still conscious?”

“G… G…”

In response to Celestan’s words, the butler—the being called Mirales—gave a faint reply.

One could argue that this form was merely a coincidence, but seeing him nod back at Celestan clearly indicated that he possessed his own will.

(Even in this state, he still obeys his master’s orders? …Is this loyalty, or should I rather call it blind faith?)

Rei had been watching the two of them, but at the sound of the wind cutting through the air that reached his ears once more, he leaped from where he stood.

The next moment, the spot where Rei had been was occupied by the monstrous form Deotres had transformed into. With his hands clasped together, he slammed them into the ground, creating a small crater.

But by then, Rei was already in the air and suffered no damage from that blow.

Naturally, in such a situation, Elena wasn’t just standing there watching either.

With Mirage transformed into a longsword in her hand, Elena charged toward Mirales.

As far as Elena was concerned, she believed she had already defeated Mirales.

Despite this, Mirales managed to reattach his severed torso and resurrected himself.

This was unexpected for everyone, and it wasn’t due to any mistake on Elena’s part.

Still, Elena likely saw it as her own mistake, which is why she acted to defeat Mirales.

Rei says nothing in particular to Elena and turns to face Deotres.

The wound on Miralles’ torso, inflicted by Elena’s Mirage, was rapidly regenerating as countless bubbles rose from the wound.

Even witnessing such a sight, Rei wasn’t particularly surprised.

After all, he’d already witnessed her effortlessly reattach her torso after it had been severed in two, so expecting him to be surprised by something like this now would have been unreasonable.

“It seems that their butler… that guy named Mirales… is conscious, but are you conscious too?”

Rei asked the figure before him not only out of genuine curiosity, but also because he needed time to figure out how to kill him.

Without the Death Scythe, he couldn’t use the skills he’d acquired through magic stones, and since the Death Scythe also served as a magic catalyst, casting spells was impossible as well.

Even so, he was confident he could take down any ordinary monster using Nebula’s eyes and Rei’s own physical abilities, but given that ridiculous recovery—no, regeneration—he didn’t have many options for defeating him in his current state.

That said, “not many” simply means there are still options available.

(The Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour… should I use it?)

It’s a skill that possesses exceptionally high offensive power, even among Rei’s diverse array of attack methods.

Unlike Death Scythe’s skills, it can be used without relying on magic stones… it’s a skill in the truest sense of the word.

No matter how high his regenerative ability is, it surely isn’t infinite, so I figured I could handle it however I wanted if I used the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

(That’s right. If I keep fighting like this, I won’t lose, but winning will be difficult. Besides, since what they’re doing is essentially stalling for time, I need to defeat Deotres as quickly as possible and deal with that seal… that flesh cocoon. The problem is… whether they’ll stay put until I activate the skill.)

After all, what stood before me was Celestan’s puppet… no, it would be more accurate to call it a puppet of the flesh cocoon that Celestan was controlling.

Given that, I wanted to avoid wasting time here as much as possible.

But the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour isn’t a skill I can activate the moment I decide to use it; it requires a little time.

That very delay was the reason I couldn’t use the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour even when fighting the Black Wolf.

“Go… gi…”

Deotres uttered a short sound in response to Rei’s words, but even Rei, who had asked the question, couldn’t tell if it was truly a reaction to his words.

It was even possible that Deotres himself didn’t realize it.

But for now, he needed to buy time… and in the meantime, build up his magical power to activate the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

And so, even as Rei spoke with Deotres, the magical power he emitted grew rapidly more intense.

If Deotres—or perhaps Mirales or Celestan—had the ability to sense magic, they might have realized Rei’s intentions.

However, Rei had calculated his chances in this regard.

Up until now, most who had seen or sensed Rei’s magical power had been surprised and unsettled.

That was precisely why he acted this way—believing that if Deotres and the others could see or sense Rei's magic, they would react the same way.

And then... the moment Deotres, standing face-to-face with him, sensed something, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour activated.
