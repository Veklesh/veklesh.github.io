"Set, I doubt it, but there might be some kind of barrier set up. Keep your eyes peeled around there."

"Guruu!"

Two hundred meters above the destination, Mejogo.

Set usually flew at around 100 meters, but this time, aiming for a surprise attack from above, he was being extra cautious and flying at nearly double his usual altitude.

Even at 100 meters, though, the Set Cage allowed him to blend into the surroundings, making detection unlikely.

Still, just in case, just in the unlikely event, he was being cautious.

"Hey, I just thought... If there's a barrier or something set up, wouldn't we hit it before Set does?"

Hearing Marina's words from inside the Set Cage, Rei recalled the current situation.

The Set Cage, true to its name—though strictly speaking, it was a Mirage Cage—was the cage Set held with its four legs.

Meaning, if they descended like this, they would undoubtedly enter Mejogo before Set did... In that sense, if there was a barrier or something similar, it would undoubtedly be extremely dangerous.

"...Damn it. I didn't consider that."

The Set Cage was originally a magic item created for the purpose of being carried by Set for transportation.

That purpose did not include infiltrating enemy strongholds...

"For now, let's descend slowly. Once we cross the boundary, if there are no problems, we can just keep moving. If some kind of barrier is in place, we'll have to find a way to force our way through."

Ultimately, with no other means available, that was the only course of action.

Still, the reason he didn't opt for a temporary retreat was likely because he considered the possibility of a barrier being in place extremely low.

Descending slowly like this was purely a precaution.

Neither Marina, who had called out to Rei, nor the others seemed to object to his suggestion.

And so, flapping his wings, Set slowly descended... and eventually succeeded in entering Mejogo without any particular problems.

Though Rei had been confident it would be fine, he still felt relieved upon experiencing it firsthand and confirming there were no problems. He then asked Set once more.

"There. You see it, right? Land near that building."

From atop Set's back, Rei pointed toward a building.

It was the building Rei had noticed during his reconnaissance of Mejogo.

After gathering various pieces of information, Rei was certain that building was probably... no, definitely connected to an underground facility.

"Grr."

Perhaps out of consideration for the prostitutes who were likely sleeping now, Set let out a low growl instead of his usual loud cry.

Then, flapping his wings, he headed toward the building Rei had indicated.

Fortunately, the area surrounding the building resembled a fairly spacious plaza.

This wasn't necessarily a space to relax those working in the underground facility... but rather, likely simply for practical reasons in operating the facility.

As Set descended toward the plaza, Rei gazed once more at Mejogo.

The scene he had witnessed upon arriving last night seemed like a lie; the city itself was now quiet.

It wouldn't be wrong to say Mejogo itself had fallen asleep.

It wasn't that no one was out, but the number of people was astonishingly small compared to what Rei had seen when he first arrived in Mejogo.

(The ones moving... are they Resistance? Or Jaya's guards? Or perhaps they have some unrelated business... That's a possibility too. Well, the situation has already begun. There's no point dwelling on that now.)

If possible, he hoped it meant the Resistance was preparing.

That's what Rei was thinking, but the next moment, a smile touched his lips.

Because, at the end of his gaze, he spotted a familiar figure moving through the city streets.

A female wolf beastman... meaning Sharia.

It wasn't night, so she wasn't dressed as a prostitute.

Even so, Rei could clearly recognize that figure as Sharia.

And since the proposal for Sharia to cooperate with the Resistance had been made right before Rei's eyes, he naturally remembered it.

He couldn't entirely dismiss the possibility she'd caused trouble within the Resistance and been expelled, but worrying about that would be endless.

(Alright. Anyway, the Resistance is moving to protect the residents in line with our actions. If that's the case, we can proceed as planned.)

Of course, even if the Resistance hadn't acted according to Rei's wishes, he wouldn't have called off this raid.

But... in that case, I might have held back from rampaging out of concern for those somewhere in Mejogo who weren't involved in this incident at all... who were, in fact, victims of Jaya's actions.

That said, even if it's safe to rampage, if Rei were to go all out, there's a risk Mejogo itself could be reduced to ashes. So, in truth, we can't just rampage freely.

"We've confirmed the presence of what appear to be Resistance members."

Hearing Rei's words muttered loud enough for the Set Cage to hear, Elena and the other women showed expressions of relief.

Even if the commotion grew somewhat larger, the Resistance would surely handle it.

...However, since the actual combat was taking place inside the underground facility, damage reaching the surface was fundamentally unlikely... but that fundamental logic didn't apply to Rei and the others.

Set descended, its altitude dropping below the surrounding rooftops.

At this altitude, they could no longer observe Mejogo from the sky as they had before.

Not that it was compensation, but at this low altitude, the Set Cage's camouflage ceased to function.

Well, to be precise, there wasn't an actual malfunction in the camouflage system itself. The Set Cage's camouflage function reflected the surrounding scenery from about a minute prior.

In open skies, that might be manageable, but in a city like Mejogo, that one-minute gap was fatally significant.

It's enough to make anyone feel an overwhelming sense of something being terribly wrong at a glance.

Above all, having descended to an altitude equal to or below the buildings, it was impossible for Set to remain undetected.

And this was a location that could be called Mejogo's highest-security zone; naturally, there were quite a few guards stationed there.

It wasn't as busy as Mejogo's peak nighttime hours, but there were still plenty of guards on duty.

"Enemy... attack! Enemy attack!"

The cry echoed through the surrounding area from one of the guard posts set up in the plaza-like space.

Others had also noticed Rei and the others descending.

But even so, the utterly unexpected sight had caught them completely off guard.

Given their responsibility for security here, the guards were undoubtedly among the elite within Jaya. Yet even for such men, the existence of enemies descending directly from the sky was something they never imagined.

No matter how much influence an organization held over a nation, the fact that this nation was the small country of Reburina, which didn't even possess a single Dragon Knight, proved fatal.

They knew dragon knights existed, but had never actually seen one. They possessed knowledge of their specific powers, but lacked any real sense of their actual capabilities.

And while Jaya likely knew that Rei was in Reburina, the idea that this Rei would descend from the sky riding a Set was something those in charge of security here could never have anticipated.

The moment the Set Cage touched the ground, several men emerged from nearby small huts.

In their hands were various weapons—long swords, clubs, spears—and it was clear their intent was not to capture, but to kill.

"Wooooooooooh!"

"Kill him! Kill him! Kill him!"

"Bows! Bring bows! They can fly!"

"Call for reinforcements!"

"Damn it! Do they have no sense of common sense, charging straight in here like this?!"

The moment one of the men shouted, several figures leapt out of the Set Cage.

Elena's linked sword, Mirage, whipped through the air like a lash, slicing through those around her one after another. Wind blades created by Marina's spirit magic tore through the enemy. Vihera punched and kicked opponents away.

Byune hurled sharp needles that pierced bodies, causing some to scream in agony.

Amidst the chaos, Rei leaps down from Set's back, stowing the Set Cage into the Misty Ring to expand his maneuverable space, then draws the Death Scythe.

The sheer presence of that enormous scythe alone was enough to strike fear into the hearts of those who saw it.

The guards stationed here were naturally among the most skilled in Jaya, and they took pride in their own abilities.

And yet... or perhaps precisely because of that, the sight of Rei wielding the Death Scythe froze them in terror.

And to pull such a stunt in front of Rei... was nothing short of suicide.

"Die."

With that single word, the Death Scythe swung, severing the torsos of five men. Their entrails spilled out, filling the air with the pungent stench of blood.

Five grown men, severed at the waist in a single blow.

It was only natural they would die after such an act. Those who witnessed... who had seen that scene, realized Rei was an opponent beyond their comprehension, and took several steps back.

"GRRRRRR!"

But of course, he wouldn't let them escape so easily. After lowering the Set Cage and Rei, Seto, who had been flying overhead, charged straight into them.

A colossal form over three meters long was charging.

Naturally, anyone struck by such an entity wouldn't escape unscathed. They were blown away, their bones shattered throughout their bodies.

"What the hell... What the hell are you people?!"

Unfair.

Faced with a scene that could only be called utterly unreasonable, one of the guards shouted.

His cheek was smeared with the undigested contents from the stomach of his comrade—a man who, just minutes ago, had been joking and laughing with him. His face and body were thickly coated with blood, fragments of entrails, and bodily fluids.

Facing that man, Rei held the Death Scythe and opened his mouth.

"Who am I? Given who you are, I assume you already know. Or has the intel been blocked higher up?"

Even as Rei spoke, a battle raged around them, one so brutal that calling it a rout felt like an understatement.

Of course, it was Elena and her group doing the crushing, and Jaya's people were the ones being crushed.

The man understood that, but the sudden shift from peaceful times to a battlefield—especially the sight of his own people, who should be the stronger ones, being utterly crushed—made attacking Rei impossible.

"Wh-what are you talking about?"

"Well, if you don't understand, then don't. It doesn't matter who we are; the outcome you face will be the same either way."

"Wait!"

The moment Rei tightened his grip on the Death Scythe, the man, seemingly realizing he couldn't survive this, frantically dropped the longsword he held to the ground.

"I surrender! I surrender, I beg you! Please, just spare my life!"

"...If you were truly just common thieves, I might have granted that plea. I might have spared your lives, perhaps selling you into slavery instead. But... considering what you've done, do you really think I could just nod and say 'Yes, of course' when you ask for mercy?"

The words that came out of Rei's mouth were astonishingly cold.

It was as if he were saying that he himself had been foolish to think he could be saved just because he'd dropped his weapon.

Still, the man who didn't want to die watched Rei approach, Death Scythe in hand, desperately trying to think.

He racked his brain like he'd never done before in his life... and finally, finding a breakthrough, he shouted as if driven by it.

"Information! Don't you want information?! ...Huh?"

The moment he spoke, a sharp wind brushed against the man's neck and vanished.

The man didn't understand what had happened, but suddenly realized a massive blade existed right beside his own neck.

...Yes, Rei's Death Scythe.

The man couldn't believe his eyes.

Just an instant ago, the massive scythe that should have been in Rei's hands was now pressed against his own throat.

If he had opened his mouth just a few seconds... no, just a few moments later, his neck would undoubtedly have been severed, and he would have died.

As this thought struck him, overwhelmed by the sheer relief of having survived, the man felt his consciousness being swallowed by darkness.
