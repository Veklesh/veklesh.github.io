At first, Lerdact didn't understand what was happening to him.

He even thought his own right arm, spinning through the air, was some kind of joke or prank.

He never imagined that someone would dare to harm him so brazenly, especially since he was a noble.

He knew Rei was a dangerous opponent who attacked without restraint, even against nobles. Yet he had convinced himself... that he, Rei, was different.

That Rei, belonging to the Neutral Faction, would attack him, a noble said to be forming friendly ties with the Noble Faction...

But Lerdact only considered what suited him, and several crucial pieces of information had vanished from his mind.

And perhaps that was understandable.

Truthfully, the idea that Rei would directly attack his territory... let alone launch a direct assault on the lord's manor... was completely unexpected, even for Lerdact.

It wasn't that he had underestimated Rei...

He simply hadn't imagined someone could pull off such a feat alone.

Of course, individuals possessing that level of ability do exist.

But there's a huge difference between being capable of doing something and actually choosing to do it.

Why?

That was what Lerdact thought as he watched his own right arm flying through the air.

But... such doubts wouldn't last forever.

The next instant, blood spurted from his right shoulder, and excruciating pain overwhelmed Lerdact.

A pain so intense he had never experienced anything like it in his entire life.

It felt like red-hot iron was being pressed against his body.

Of course, Lerdact had never experienced such agony before, but what he felt now was closer to intense heat than pain, strictly speaking.

Without even realizing it, he clutched his bleeding right shoulder and rolled around on the floor.

"Gah!"

The next instant, a violent impact sent Relduct flying into the air.

...Unbeknownst to him, it was Rei who had struck him with the hilt of the Death Scythe. Through his tear-blurred vision, he could make out Rei saying something... The next instant, the searing pain returned, and a scream erupted.

Rolling around on the floor, tears, snot, and drool streaming from him, Lerdact failed to notice that the blood flowing from his severed right shoulder, cut off by Rei moments before, had stopped.

The overwhelming heat and excruciating pain. These two forces had robbed Lerdact of any mental capacity to think about anything else.

Glancing at Lerdact, who was writhing on the floor, screaming in agony, Rei sighed in exasperation.

"You've had plenty killed on your orders, yet losing one arm reduces you to this? I'd expect a more noble, dignified demeanor from someone of your station. Especially since I went out of my way to cauterize the wound to prevent you from bleeding out."

Though Rei's words carried disbelief, Lerdact showed no sign of reacting.

After watching Relduct like this for several minutes, Rei finally clicked his tongue softly. He took a potion from the Misty Ring and poured it over Relduct.

It was by no means a high-quality potion, merely a cheap one sold in shops.

Even so, it must have eased some of the pain Lerdact was feeling.

Tears, snot, and drool still clung to his face, but the pain had subsided enough for him to finally turn his gaze toward Rei.

"Don't think this is enough to atone for what you've done. People died because of you."

"...Even if they died, they were just commoners! How dare you do this to me, a noble..."

"Unfortunately for you... and for the people of Gilm, merchants are more important than nobles like you. ...Well, relax. I've been told not to take your life."

At those words, Lerdact undoubtedly breathed a sigh of relief.

He understood that at least his own life was safe... and in the next instant, the Death Scythe swung, severing everything from Lerdact's left shoulder down.

"Eh? ...Ah, ahhh, aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!"

Having already lost his right arm, he instantly realized his left arm was gone. Without the initial delay, Lerdact comprehended what had happened and screamed in agony.

Then, to stop the massive flow of blood, Rei's magic cauterized the wound again... Yet he never grew accustomed to the pain. His face was a pitiful sight, covered in tears, snot, and drool.

Even if someone claimed he was a noble of noble blood, it would be impossible to believe.

Moreover, having suddenly lost both arms, he could no longer maintain his balance properly.

As a result, all Reldact could do was crawl pitifully across the floor like a worm.

"What... didn't you say you wouldn't kill me?!"

"True. The one who hired me specifically said not to kill him. ...So I haven't killed him, have I?"

Only then did Lerdact finally realize.

The person before him held no respect or fear for his noble status.

If they got in the way, he would simply slice them apart with his scythe as easily as cutting weeds.

Lerdact knew, as information, that Rei saw no value whatsoever in nobles—or more accurately, in nobles who opposed him.

But knowing it as information and actually feeling it were two entirely different things.

For Lerdact, who had lived within noble society, someone who saw no value not only in him but in the very existence of nobility itself was a completely incomprehensible being... literally nothing but a monster.

Only now did Lerdact truly grasp the danger Rei represented.

Watching Lerdact like this, Rei opened his mouth.

"Even if you lose an arm or two... you're not dead. Right? What? Relax, you still have two legs, don't you?"

Realizing Rei wasn't joking at all, that he meant every word, all that remained for Lerdact was overwhelming terror.

Only now did he understand why the men entrusted with guarding him had fled without putting up a proper fight.

Well, to be precise, Lerdact had been unconscious when the guards fled, but even so, seeing Rei now, it was clear the guards wouldn't have fought him either.

Rei before the attack, the very picture of nobility, was nowhere to be seen.

All that remained was a single man desperately trying to get past the overwhelming monster before him.

Glancing at Lerdact's pitiful state, Rei opened his mouth.

"Now then, I've got a question... Since you hold the title of Count, you've got magic items, right?"

He flinched.

Lerdact froze at Rei's words.

His eyes held the look of someone staring at an unbelievable opponent.

Rei understood exactly what Rei was demanding.

Anyone gathering information on Rei would quickly learn that collecting magic items was one of his hobbies.

And since Rei was attempting to lay hands on Gilm, there was no way Relduct could be unaware of information concerning Rei, who was considered Daskar's right-hand man.

But even knowing that, Lerdact did not immediately nod in agreement.

Magic items are extremely valuable.

Everyday magic items might be a different story, but those collected by nobles naturally held high rarity value.

Should he really give one to Rei?

That thought made him hesitate to respond to Rei's words.

"Hey, not answering my question in this situation... you know what that means, right?"

If this continues, I'll be killed.

"I see. Then, perhaps you could show me the way. Think of it as an apology for messing with Gilm. Not such a bad deal, right? Though it depends on whether you value your life or your possessions more."
