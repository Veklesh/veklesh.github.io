When Rei was invited to dine at the lord's mansion, Ryu Brand asked him whether he would side with Augusto... or more precisely, whether he opposed planting the Tira Tree to counter the monsters.

And Rei's reply was...

"I don't know. I wish you could have given me an answer right away."

"Even so, I've only just arrived in Gosh. Above all, I know almost nothing about this Tira tree. Therefore, whichever side I choose, I believe I need to thoroughly investigate the Tira tree first."

Rei understood his own power to a certain extent.

He was absolutely certain, no joke, that if the Daldora faction and the Augusto faction came to blows, whichever side he chose would win.

He was confident he could even win against the city of Gosh itself if it came to that.

That's precisely why he couldn't clearly state here which side he would take.

In his heart, Rei still wanted to side with Augusto.

But that was merely Rei's impression based solely on what he saw of Augusto. Without understanding Daldora at all, he couldn't make a decision.

(Though it's true that Daldora is a despicable person...)

The exchange between Daldora and Augusto that Ray witnessed when he first arrived in Gosh.

Based solely on that, Rei's honest feeling was that he and Daldora would never get along.

But she couldn't let her own feelings alone decide everything.

"...Hmm. As a noble, I'd like you to cooperate with Daldora. Is that still no good?"

"Yes."

He answered that part without hesitation.

For Rei, the status of being a noble didn't hold much meaning to begin with.

If that was true even for the nobles of the Mireana Kingdom, he certainly had no reason to show deference to the nobles of a vassal state... especially one as weak as Solrain.

But that attitude of Rei's probably didn't sit well with Ryu Brand. He frowned slightly.

"To state that so boldly in my presence shows you have some nerve. I wouldn't advise doing anything to anger me here, would you?"

Having assumed no one would dare show such insolence to a noble, Ryu Brand let his displeasure show, his tone tinged with a hint of threat.

But... that was a bad move.

"If you wish to oppose me, so be it. If you intend to deal with me by force, I shall respond in kind. Yes, for example, the destruction of Gosh..."

Rei's expression as he said this was utterly serious.

Ryubrand understood that too. He tried to laugh it off, but couldn't.

"Well, it would only be an unfortunate event for the other residents too. I wouldn't do such a thing."

He said it with a smile, as if to say, "Where did that serious expression from a moment ago go?"

Seeing Rei like this, Ryu Brand let out a sigh of relief...

"Well, completely obliterating this entire mansion... reducing it to ashes would be easy enough."

he declared.

"...What?"

Ryubrand couldn't comprehend what had just been said and asked again.

He had assumed nothing could harm him, no matter what happened, so he couldn't comprehend what Rei was saying.

"I said I could burn this entire mansion and everyone living here to death anytime. Didn't you hear me?"

"Are you serious?"

"Yes, absolutely. In fact, I'd like to ask you something. Why do you think I couldn't do it? I've survived countless battles. Wars, even. The lives I've taken in those battles aren't just a hundred or two hundred. Adding the people living in this mansion to that number is nothing."

Is this man before me insane?

Ryubrand thought this as he looked into Rei's eyes. But Rei's eyes, clearly visible beneath his lowered hood, seemed perfectly sane.

(So this man... is he serious!?)

The man before him... didn't think much of him... of nobles.

He had heard the story from Daldora, but he had always thought it was just a rumor... an exaggerated rumor.

But that information was true. If Rei standing before him now chose to, his life could be extinguished in an instant.

Realizing this, Ryu Brand noticed his hands were trembling without him even knowing.

"I-I see. That's quite bold. But perhaps you shouldn't casually mention killing people so lightly?"

"Unfortunately, I'm an adventurer. Killing is my daily routine. Whether it's monsters... or people."

At the word "human," Ryu Brand's legs began shaking so violently he could no longer hide it.

It was fortunate for Ryu Brand, who valued his noble dignity, that the trembling was hidden beneath the tablecloth.

Facing such a Ryu Brand, Rei smiled and spoke.

"Oh? I meant it as a joke to liven up the meal. Was it not amusing?"

"...I suppose so. It's hardly a suitable topic for mealtime. I'd appreciate it if you refrained from such subjects in the future."

Though half-joking to Rei, those words made Ryu Brand understand just how dangerous a person Rei truly was.

The fact that it was half-joking meant that for Rei, half of it was also true.

(Dangerous... This man is truly dangerous.)

He finally grasped it deep in his heart. Ryu Brand, seemingly unconcerned about the conversation they'd just had, turned his gaze to Rei, who was eating a meat dish cooked with extra spices.

Yet, while feeling terror at Rei's presence, he also found himself nodding in understanding. So this was the kind of person they called an adventurer who had a griffin as a familiar.

(Dangerous. He doesn't even regard nobles as nobles... No, worse than that. The sheer abnormality of knowing they are nobles and yet being able to say he'd kill them without hesitation. According to what I heard from Daldora, the Marquis of Larks holds him in high regard...)

How on earth can they place such trust in a dangerous individual like this? Ryu Brand thought deeply.

Distracting his thoughts from the person before him had probably been for the best. Before he knew it, the trembling in his body had ceased.

Understanding this, Ryu Brand spoke again.

"Now then, as for you... no, you're not the one facing that high-ranking adventurer with the alias. So it's settled that Rei hasn't decided what he'll do next, correct?"

Perhaps because he now understood what kind of person Rei was, Ryu Brand addressed her properly by name instead of "you." ...Yet he still omitted honorifics. Was it because he disliked using them for Rei, who wasn't nobility?

But Rei showed no sign of noticing Ryu Brand's manner, simply nodding in response.

"Yes, that's right. To begin with, my coming to Gosh was mostly just a matter of circumstance. I might even leave tomorrow, or the day after."

"...I see. I understand. I can't say anything about what you want to do. But remember, neither I nor Daldora are pursuing the matter of the Tira Tree as some sort of game."

Rei nodded back at Ryu Brand, who was staring intently at him.

"Understood. I'll keep that firmly in mind."

"Good. Well then, enough of the difficult talk. Shall we eat? It's a shame to let such fine food get cold."

Normally, the food would have cooled by now during their conversation, but whether there was some sort of device on the table holding the dishes, they showed no sign of cooling.

Rei's expression flickered with slight surprise at the warmth of the soup he reached for.

(Is this a magic item too?)

A table with a heat-retaining function.

It would be convenient and enviable to have one, but if you asked if it was necessary in the desert, you'd have to tilt your head in confusion.

(Well, it does get cold at night, so it's not exactly useless...)

Even so, it was customary to eat dinner before the sun fully set. While it wasn't exactly true that no one ate after temperatures dropped near freezing—as one might expect in the desert—it was certainly rare.

Especially for a lord ruling over Gosh, it wasn't as if emergencies never arose.

In such moments, having this table—which seemed like a magic item—would undoubtedly be convenient.

"Hm? Oh, you noticed? Right, this table is a magic item."

"You have quite a convenient magic item, don't you?"

"Ha ha ha. All thanks to Daldora."

"...I see."

Rei understood it was essentially a bribe, but there was no hint of blame in his tone.

In this Elgin, bribery isn't exactly illegal.

Of course, excessive bribery would be punished, and it's certainly not a praiseworthy act, so some people would find it distasteful.

Moreover, many despised bribes, like Daskar, the lord of Gilm where Rei had his base.

Judgment on such matters varied greatly from person to person, which was precisely why Rei had no intention of condemning Ryu Brand or Daldora.

Had they demanded bribes from Rei, he would have taken a firm stance then.

(That's probably why Augusto is always playing catch-up on this point too.)

Ray pictured Augusto's kind-looking face in his mind.

Purely in terms of business acumen, Augusto certainly wasn't inferior to Daldora.

But Augusto's honesty held him back, while Daldora had built his current position by acting without regard for means.

"This bread has grilled fish from Gosh's Oasis sandwiched inside. I'm not averse to this sort of dish myself... though the cook scolds me for being ill-mannered. My wife's also rather particular about such things."

"So today is special." Saying this, Ryu Brand reached for the bread sandwiching the fish fillet.

(He's got a pretty decent personality, you know. Likes this kind of fast-food style dish. Well, since it's fish sandwiched in bread, it must be a luxury dish in Gosh. Still, does the cook make it even though he gets scolded?)

Watching Ryu Brand eat, Rei also reached for the same dish.

The moist bread held grilled, flaked fish and sauce.

Seeing the sauce slightly soaked into the bread, it was clear that if left for too long, the sauce would soak in too much and make it difficult to eat.

Finely chopped vegetables with an onion-like texture, mixed with the fish, stimulate the palate and delight the mouth.

"Well? Pretty good, huh?"

"Yes, it is. This dish is delicious. Though it's not exactly something you can eat casually..."

It's too expensive to eat casually, and if you leave it sitting after making it, the sauce soaks into the bread and makes it hard to handle.

Thinking about it that way, calling this dish fast food was a bit of a stretch.

(If I laid lettuce or some leafy greens under the fish... or maybe spreading a thin layer of mayo or olive oil would prevent the bread from getting soggy?)

Rei, who had been thinking about such things for some reason, eventually realized it and couldn't help but smile wryly.

It wasn't just Augusto who had a strong fascination with food; he was like that too.

"Huh? Something wrong?"

Ryubrand asked, seeing Rei suddenly smile.

"No, nothing. I was just thinking how delicious this dish really is."

"...I see."

For the next ten minutes or so, Rei and Ryu Brand ate together.

After finishing their meal, with the fruit brought by the maid as dessert before them, Ryu Brand spoke again.

"Rei, I want to ask you one last time. Are you willing to cooperate with me and Daldora?"

Facing Ryu Brand's question, Rei shook his head silently.

He must have decided further words were pointless. Ryu Brand turned his attention to the dessert before him, savoring it with relish.

"Rei, thank you for granting Lord Ryu Brand's request today."

After the meal, as they exited the lord's mansion gates, Mebastian bowed deeply to Rei.

Rei shook his head at Mebastian, signaling with his attitude not to worry about it.

Beside Rei, Set, also in high spirits after eating plenty of delicious food, happily purred.

"Well, today's affair wasn't a bad thing for me either."

"Is that so? ...Well then, thank you very much indeed for today."

Leaving Mebastian behind with his deep bow, Rei departed the scene with Set.
