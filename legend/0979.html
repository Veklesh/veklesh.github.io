Set flies, the green carpet spread out below him.

From the ground, his form, flapping his wings wide, might appear as either bird or beast.

Though soaring through the spring-like blue sky, Rei's gaze remained fixed on the ground below.

He was searching for any sign of those who had escaped the forest. However, the Cyclops had been dwelling deep within the forest, and simply exiting that dense woodland would likely take considerable time.

Moreover, the fact that they had split up meant that if any of them encountered monsters in the forest, they would have to handle it alone.

Still, their chances were undoubtedly better than facing a Cyclops alone head-on.

"GRRRR!"

Set let out a sharp cry, his gaze fixed on a grassy area slightly removed from the forest, still some distance from the road.

Hearing the cry, Rei turned his gaze in the direction Set was looking... and saw the figure of a lone adventurer running across the ground.

"That's..."

The familiar figure was, of course, one of the former rangers.

Even after escaping the forest, he was still running desperately, pushing beyond his physical limits because he needed to reach Gilm as quickly as possible to warn them of the Cyclops threat.

「Set」

With just that word, Set understood what Rei wanted to say and what action he expected. Flapping his wings, he descended toward the ground.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set let out a cry, designed to catch the attention of those running on the ground.

Naturally, the sound reached the ears of the man running while keeping a watchful eye on his surroundings.

Startled by the sudden sound, the man immediately stopped and scanned his surroundings.

But he must have recognized the voice immediately. He looked up at the sky with an expression of disbelief.

And there, as if it were only natural, was Set, with Rei on his back.

"Rei-san! Set!"

The man shouted, his face beaming with joy.

His leather armor was quite dirty in places from running at full speed through the forest, but his expression radiated a joy that seemed to say such dirt was nothing.

And rightly so. The former guerrilla fighter had witnessed Rei's prowess countless times, and it was etched into his heart.

"Actually, in the forest..."

"You mean the Cyclops, right? Don't worry, that's taken care of. ...Well, I can't say it's completely resolved since there might be more Cyclops out there besides the ones you saw, but Johanna and the others are all safe."

"...Huh?"

The sudden words from Rei's mouth seemed unbelievable. Eyes turned to him, asking what on earth he was talking about.

After all, Rei's words seemed to imply he'd anticipated their actions, so it wasn't strange for the man to think that.

But quickly, they accepted it, their expressions shifting to one of understanding—it wouldn't be surprising if Rei did something like that.

To clear up the man's misunderstanding, Rei spoke up.

"When I went to the guild, Dietz showed up. So I listened to what he had to say, and..."

"...Dietz made it in time."

The man breathed a sigh of relief.

He had been worried that, left unchecked, the Cyclops could damage Gilm, and more importantly, that they themselves might not survive. So the fact that Dietz had returned to Gilm and brought reinforcements safely... and that those reinforcements were none other than Rei, the most powerful ally imaginable... that was more than enough to ease the man's mind.

However, the next words out of Rei's mouth made the man freeze.

"Ah. We had Vihera too, so we had more than enough firepower."

"Vihera!? Um, that Vihera is the Vihera I know, right? From the Bestir Empire..."

"That's right. I don't know anyone else named Vihera besides the one you're talking about."

"Why would Lord Vihera... Oh, come to think of it, when we were heading to Gilm, he mentioned something about coming once spring arrived."

The man understood why Vihera was in Gilm and felt grateful for the coincidence.

Thanks to Vihera, they had been saved.

Of course, his gratitude wasn't just for Vihera; he was also thankful to Rei and Set standing before him.

Unconcerned with the man's state of mind, Rei continued speaking.

"Anyway. You don't need to worry about the Cyclops for now. You don't need to do what Mireine said about informing Gilm. I rode Set here specifically to tell you that, but... aren't the others still scattered?"

"Huh? Yes. Everyone scattered and fled separately. ...In that sense, it's surprising you managed to find Mireine, Rei. Did you find her fleeing through the forest like me?"

For a moment, Rei looked as if he didn't understand what the man was saying, but he quickly grasped it.

Meaning Mireine and the others hadn't told the others anything, acting as rear guard to prevent the Cyclops from pursuing their comrades.

"No, I met Mireine, Surnin, and Johanna. They were holding off the Cyclops, right?"

"...Huh?"

Rei wore an expression similar to the one she had just moments before, one of not understanding what the other was saying.

But as a former guerrilla fighter, his mind worked quickly.

He immediately grasped the meaning of Rei's words... understood why Mireine and the others had stayed behind. He looked up at the sky, unable to say anything more.

Spreading out before him was a spring-like blue sky.

"Honestly... what a soft-hearted fool,"

He muttered, a wry smile playing on his lips.

Several seconds passed before he finally shifted his gaze from the sky to Rei and spoke.

"So, the three of them are safe, right?"

The certainty in his words came from his trust in Rei and Vihera's abilities.

Rei nodded in response to the question.

"Yeah. They've got some scrapes and bruises, but nothing life-threatening or major injuries."

"I see. ...Ah, that's a relief. Once we get back to Gilm, I'll treat you guys to a meal. I'm not exactly rolling in cash, though."

"Do that. Anyway, I'm heading to Gilm, but..."

"Ah, yes. That's probably best. I might not be the first one out of the forest, so it makes sense. Forming an extermination party costs money too."

The fact that Rei could respond so quickly meant the man understood the issues involved in forming an extermination squad.

"Well, I'm heading out soon. If you manage to meet up with the others, could you explain the situation to them?"

After telling the man this, Rei climbed back onto Set's back.

Set gave the man a single cry of encouragement before beginning his run-up.

After a few strides, he flapped his wings and soared into the sky.

Watching them from below, the man shifted his focus as Set and Rei grew smaller in the distance. He started walking toward Gilm.

Praying he could meet up with the comrades who had fled in another direction.

"Well, if I can meet up with the others, it'd be easier for me since I wouldn't have to go all the way back to Gilm... But yeah, probably not that simple."

"Guruu!"

Set murmured in agreement to Rei, who was muttering while looking down at the green grassland.

As they continued flying through the sky, flapping their wings, the sudden sound of a rumbling stomach reached their ears.

Come to think of it, it was already past mid-afternoon, nearing evening.

But they'd left Gilm in a hurry to help Mireine and the others, leaving no time for lunch.

Thinking about it that way, it wasn't strange that Rei's stomach was now demanding food.

They had bought snacks while guiding Vihera and the others, but it still hadn't been enough to fill Rei and Set's stomachs.

"Growl..."

Hearing Rei's stomach growl, Set seemed to remember his own hunger and whined that he was hungry too.

He turned around, his round eyes pleading with Rei, who sat on his back, to feed him.

Faced with that gaze, Rei also felt the urge to give Set something to eat.

Though it probably had a lot to do with the fact that Rei was hungry too.

"Once we finish our business in Gilm, we'll eat somewhere... but this will tide us over until then. Here."

What he pulled out from the Misty Ring was a sandwich.

Ideally, Rei wanted to feed Set something like stewed meat in a pot or a hearty stew, and she wanted to eat it herself too.

But such dishes were basically purchased already cooked in pots.

In other words, if they absolutely wanted such a dish, they'd have to either descend to the surface and make time for lunch, or, worse, be prepared to spill the pot of food onto the ground while trying to serve it from Set's back.

Wasting food... especially ruining a meal Rei had approved and bought in bulk just to eat now? The answer was no.

Besides, since the sandwiches were freshly made and stored in the Misty Ring, there was no loss of flavor.

Freshly made sandwiches combine the soft texture of the bread with the various fillings inside, making your hungry stomach demand more, wanting to eat even more.

Resisting the urge to keep eating, Rei offered the sandwich to Set.

Set deftly picked up the sandwich from Rei's hand with his beak, chewed it in his mouth, and then let out a satisfied gurgle at its deliciousness.

"Gururururu!"

Just like Rei's stomach, Set demanded more and more sandwich.

While feeding Set and eating himself, Gilm soon came into view.

Considering Set's flight speed, it was only natural. At Rei's signal, Set flapped his wings and descended toward Gilm's gate.

...Even then, he was still eating the sandwich. That's probably why he's Set.

As expected, people were going through the entry procedures in front of the gate.

As it was nearing evening, a fair number of adventurers had finished their quests.

Thanks to that, almost no one seemed surprised when Set descended to the ground.

Though...

"Rei! I asked you to land a little way from the gate, didn't I!?"

Ranga, who had come out upon hearing the commotion, gave him a scolding.

"Sorry, Ranga. I had something urgent to do. ...Well, maybe it's good timing since you came?"

Langa must have understood that Rei's expression as he muttered this was anything but playful.

Ranga's expression shifted from anger and confusion to pure question.

"What's wrong?"

"You know I went to help with the Cyclops hunt, right..."

"Yeah, of course. ...Are you going to tell me you let the Cyclops escape or something?"

A murmur rippled through the crowd.

The moment Ranga spoke, the adventurers watching the exchange between Rei and Ranga began to murmur.

The expressions on their faces were ones of utter astonishment.

It was only natural. Among the adventurers based in this Gilm, there were almost none who didn't know the strength of Rei and Set.

If anyone did, it would be someone who had just arrived at Gilm, who had heard rumors about Rei but had never actually seen him.

...Even such individuals would hardly mistake Rei if he had Set with him.

"No, that's not it. I already dealt with the Cyclops. It's just that another problem has arisen."

At Rei's words, Ranga's expression darkened slightly.

From their past dealings, he knew that problems involving Rei usually became major ones.

That experience made him suspect something troublesome had happened.

Ranga's unease was both right and wrong.

To be precise, the problem was... well, there was the red Cyclops, and the fact that there were actually seven Cyclopses, and possibly even more out there. But most of that had already been resolved.

Though the possibility of more Cyclopes existing remained unchanged.

"So, what exactly is the problem?"

"There were seven Cyclopes, not five, and one of them appeared to be a rare or higher-tier species."

"Huh!?"

Even though I'd expected some kind of problem, hearing it directly still made me jump.

After all, they were told there were seven Cyclops—Rank C monsters—and one of them was likely a rare or higher-tier species.

Those around them who had been listening to Rei and Ranga's conversation reacted the same way.

"Ah, don't worry. We took care of all those Cyclopes. It's just... it seems the extermination team scattered before we arrived. They might be coming to report that the Cyclopes were beyond their capabilities."

"Ah, I see. You want to stop them."

"Exactly."

Ranga exhaled in relief at Rei's words, then paused before speaking.

"Then you can relax. None of the members who returned after you left were part of the group that went to hunt the cyclops. If that had happened, we would have noticed immediately upon their return."

Hearing this, Rei exhaled in relief... and decided it would be best to inform the guild as well. He proceeded to enter the Gilm hall.
