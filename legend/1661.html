A few days had passed since Rei had told Gimekara about natto… and there they were again, standing before Gilm.

“Lady Elena, please be careful. I’ve heard there are monsters in the sea that are completely different from those on land.”

“I know. It’s not like this is my first time going to the sea, so don’t worry about it so much.”

Elena had previously fought a kraken alongside Elk and the Axe of the Thunder God, as well as other adventurers, knights, and soldiers.

Precisely because of that, she was a bit at a loss as to how to respond to Ara’s words, which made it sound as if she were going to the sea for the very first time.

Seeing Elena like that, Rei gave a small smile, but then she noticed a man approaching her and turned her gaze in his direction.

The man, perhaps noticing that Rei had looked at him, smiled and bowed his head.

“Rei-chan, thanks for taking me along today.”

As he said this, closing one eye, it was a man Rei knew… at least, he looked like a man.

He was short, and while he didn’t look muscular at first glance, his body was covered in muscles that Rei found enviable.

He was one of the merchants Ray had met not long after the expansion of Gilm had begun.

Although he was technically the leader of a caravan, this time he had left his caravan in the care of his companions and decided to travel with Rei and the others... his name was Bistol.

He must have heard somewhere that Rei and the others were heading to the sea, and he asked to be taken along.

It wasn’t because of Bistol’s hobbies or a desire to go out and have fun; it was purely business-related.

“If it’s Bistrol, he won’t get any funny ideas, so it shouldn’t be a problem. …At least not for us.”

Although Bistoll had a muscular, burly build, his personality was that of a woman. In fact, as far as Rei could tell, he could even be called a maiden.

Given that Bistol was like that, Rei predicted that even if they traveled with Elena and the others, nothing problematic would arise.

And, in fact, he wasn’t wrong.

Bistrol’s type was tall men with toned muscles.

In that sense, there was no one in Rei’s group who fit his type.

……That said, the fact that there are no problems applies strictly to Rei and his group.

For the men in the villages and towns they were heading to, it couldn’t be ruled out that Bistol might be seen as a predator hunting its prey.

(After all, I do have this image of men in fishing villages and port towns having toned bodies. …Wait, I don’t even know if we’ll go to places like that in the first place. Ah, but I’ll need to gather information to find out what kind of fish can be caught.)

Rei and the others are heading to the sea.

However, this isn’t some kind of vacation or anything like that; they’re going to the sea purely to catch fish.

Given that, it’s not exactly a beach trip—it’s more like fishing.

“Wow, I’m really lucky to have run into Rei and the others at a time like this.”

Saying this, Bistol stroked the pouch at her waist with genuine delight.

Although the pouch’s construction and appearance differed greatly from the one Elena carried, it was undoubtedly what one would call a magic pouch.

It was a device that could be called a lower-tier version of the item box that Elena also possessed.

However, even as a lower-tier version, it was an extremely expensive item.

Rei wondered why Bistrel possessed such an item, but when she was told, “It’s a secret,” with a wink, he couldn’t press the matter any further.

Well, if it were truly necessary, he wouldn’t let the matter drop just like that, but considering Bistol’s personality, he couldn’t imagine it had been obtained through illegal means.

...That said, he couldn’t completely rule out the possibility that he’d defeated an attacker and happened to get his hands on the magic pouch the person was carrying.

Whatever the reason, once Bistrol had the magic pouch, he learned that Rei and the others were going to the beach and decided to put some fish in the pouch and bring them along.

Given the magic pouch’s capabilities, Rei wondered, “Why fish?” but seeing how satisfied Bistul was, he didn’t intend to say anything more.

(Besides, in Gilm, which isn’t on the coast, seafood is undoubtedly quite expensive. It’s a solid business, after all. With people gathering here for the expansion project, if they hear there’s fresh fish—not just dried fish—some would surely be willing to pay a fair price for it.)

That said, not everyone is grateful for the fish.

Currently, people from all over have gathered in Gilm.

Naturally, some of them have come from the coast. To people like that, it’s probably hard to understand why anyone would be so grateful for fish.

In fact, for them, the meat of the monsters that can be hunted around Gilm is far more valuable.

“Well, there’s no point in standing around like this forever. …Let’s get going!”

At Rei’s words, Elena, who had been talking with Ara, and the others gathered around Rei.

When he takes out the Set Cage, Bistol’s eyes light up the moment he sees it, and he strokes it.

“Is that the Set Cage? I’ve been looking forward to riding in it. I can’t wait to see what it’s like!”

The way she said this, sounding so excited, didn’t seem like she was just being polite or joking.

Her attitude and demeanor made it clear that she was genuinely looking forward to it from the bottom of her heart.

Of course, it was only natural for Bistol to feel that way.

Basically, in Elgin, people rarely get to experience flying through the sky.

Either you become a dragon rider, tame a flying monster, or summon one using magic.

In Rei’s case, it’s officially listed as taming… but very few people possess that kind of talent.

That’s probably why Bistol is so thrilled about flying through the sky in the Set Cage.

(There aren’t any windows to speak of, and even if you look up at the only open part, all you can see is Set’s belly. …Maybe I should have them add some sort of window next time?)

Even so, the Set Cage was crafted from various precious monster materials, and whether such a modification would even be possible was a bit of a long shot... or so Rei suspected.

“Bistol seems to be looking forward to it too, so I guess we should get going.”

“Grr!”

Set let out a happy growl. While it was true he was delighted to be going to the sea... his joy stemmed more from the prospect of eating seafood.

At least, that’s how it seemed to Rei, and there was no doubt that was exactly what he was feeling.

“Kyu! Kyu-kyu!”

On top of Set’s back, Yellow was also chirping happily.

Yellow seemed happy too, but unlike Seto, it wasn’t because of the fish—he was just genuinely happy to be going out with everyone.

(He used to compete with the river crabs, didn’t he? Does that mean he might end up competing with crabs or hermit crabs at the beach too?)

As he thought this, Rei saw Yellow, called by Elena, leap off Set’s back and climb into the Set Cage with the others, and he gave Set’s back a light tap.

Understanding exactly what Rei wanted with just that, Set crouched slightly to make it easier for him to climb onto his back.

Then Rei climbed onto Set’s back… and with a few steps of a running start, Set flapped his wings and soared into the sky.

Set’s takeoff, which took place a short distance from Gilm’s main gate, drew the attention of those going through the entry procedures.

To those familiar with Set, it wasn’t particularly eye-catching behavior, but among the people present, there were some seeing Set for the first time.

To them, Set was undoubtedly a rare sight in many ways.

Drawing the attention of those around him, Set flapped his wings widely in midair... and eventually, grabbing the Set Cage that had been placed on the ground with his four legs, he soared back into the sky.

Holding the Set Cage, Set vanished from sight in the blink of an eye, and those present were unable to witness the Set Cage’s effect of blending into the surrounding scenery.

“Hmm… It’s another beautiful day today.”

“Grr!”

Even though summer was drawing to a close, the weather and temperatures during the day were still distinctly summery.

As far as temperature was concerned, it wasn’t something Rei—who was wearing the Dragon Robe with its simple air-conditioning function—needed to worry about.

Still, being able to enjoy the summery air from atop Set’s back like this was a tremendous luxury—and a great pleasure.

Several cumulus clouds, looking almost like cotton candy, drifted across the sky, as if insisting that it was still summer and too early for autumn.

The intense sunlight pouring down from the sun seemed to be cheering on the cumulus clouds’ antics.

(There’s late autumn and late winter, but late summer… Hmm? I feel like I’ve heard that before. So, is there actually a term for ‘late summer’?)

As Rei gazed at the summer sky and pondered such things, a thought suddenly crossed his mind.

Late summer—a sign of summer’s end.

Rei wondered if such a term even existed, but when he actually tried to picture the phrase “late summer” in his mind, he realized he’d seen it before in manga, novels, and games.

Rei had been slightly puzzled by the term “late summer,” but he quickly pushed the thought out of his mind.

Now wasn’t the time to be thinking about such things; he should head to the beach as soon as possible.

That was his decision.

“Alright then, Set. …Let’s pick up the pace toward the ocean!”

“Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrr!”

Perhaps just hearing Rei’s cheerful words made Set happy too, for he let out a roar that echoed throughout the area.

...Hearing Set’s roar, the monsters and animals on the ground felt a deep-seated terror and hid, trying not to be spotted by him.

Merchants, adventurers, and those seeking work who were traveling toward Gilm also looked up at the sky, seemingly without thinking.

That said, some of them recognized the roar as Set’s, and those people didn’t seem particularly frightened.

Several hours passed like this… Set had been flying toward the sea…

“Damn it! Why the hell are they charging at me instead of running away when Set is here!?”

With a burst of frustration, Rei pulled the Twilight Spear from her Misty Ring and hurled it.

The spear flew through the air... piercing the flock of harpies ahead.

Watching as numerous harpies, their bodies and wings pierced, fell to the ground, Rei summoned the Twilight Spear back to her hand.

It was precisely because of this ability—the ability to recall the spear—that she was able to fight on equal terms against a swarm of harpies numbering well over a hundred.

“Grrrrrrr!”

Set’s fire breath blocks the harpies’ advance.

Some harpies even launched wind blades or sharp, pointed feathers, but even with Set Cage, such attacks were useless against Rei and Set.

Even when they tried to launch their signature attacks with their claws, the harpies’ numbers dwindled one after another from a single strike of Rei’s Twilight Spear or a skill unleashed by Set before they could even get close.

“Anyway, that sheer number is a nuisance, so let’s just whittle them down.”

Muttering to himself, Rei sheathed the Twilight Spear, which had returned to his hand, into the Misty Ring and drew the Death Scythe.

In this case, what’s needed isn’t the Death Scythe as a weapon, but as a magic catalyst.

“Flames, become arrows that burn everything to ashes and rain down like a storm.”

As he chanted the spell, fiery arrows were generated one after another near Rei, who was riding Set.

There were about fifty of them.

Once all the flaming arrows were in place, she cast the spell.

“Raining Fire Arrows!”

The moment she spoke the spell’s name, the nearly fifty flaming arrows behind Rei flew toward the harpies all at once.

The flaming arrows then set the harpies they struck ablaze, one after another.

Normally, he wouldn’t want to do such a thing for the sake of magic stones or materials, but given their current situation heading toward the sea, he simply didn’t have time for that—that was Rei’s honest truth.

One after another, the harpies were pierced by the flaming arrows and engulfed in flames.

Even so, as Rei watched the harpies still trying to attack them, he wondered, “Could it be that we’ve gotten too close to their territory or nest?”

However, all they could see on the ground was grassland; there was simply no place nearby where harpies would live.

(So, did we enter a harpy’s territory instead of a nest? But this has never happened before.)

There are harpy nests near Gilm as well.

The place where they previously obtained—or, more accurately, created—the fire ore is the prime example of this.

But even when Set flies around Gilm, the harpies never attack him.

Concluding that it was likely just a quirk of the harpies in this area, Rei once again flew toward the sea with Set.
