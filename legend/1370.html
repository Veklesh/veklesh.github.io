When Rei arrived at Treant Forest with Set, an astonishing sight awaited them.

Numerous trees lay felled, all facing outward toward the forest's edge.

Normally, this wouldn't be considered particularly strange, but given that this was Treant Forest, it was undoubtedly a mysterious sight.

"Grrr?"

"Yeah, head down to that area where all those trees are fallen."

What are we doing? Rei answered Set, who looked at him with round eyes.

Set then began descending toward the ground.

Even as he did, the sound of axes striking tree trunks echoed all around.

And it wasn't just one or two places—the sound came from all around.

(Well, I was supposed to be guarding the lumberjacks, but... ...Well, since there aren't any monsters around, I guess it can't be helped.)

His task, for now, was to carry the felled logs close to Gilm.

With that thought, Rei landed and addressed the gathered woodcutters and adventurers.

"I'm supposed to take the felled trees. So, is it okay if I only take the trees that are already down?"

"Huh? Oh, yes, that's right. But we'll be cutting more trees from now on..."

We'd appreciate it if you could come back.

A man who seemed to be the representative of the woodcutters said this to Rei.

The adventurers also wanted Rei to come back, since cutting trees meant more money.

...However, the adventurers knew what kind of person Rei was, and since none of them were particularly close to him, they kept it to themselves.

Some newcomers to Gilm only knew Rei through rumors. Yet, regardless of Rei's appearance, seeing the griffin he rode made them understand this wasn't a situation for careless talk. They watched Rei and the woodcutter's exchange in silence.

Unlike those adventurers, however, the woodcutters did not view Rei as someone to fear.

It wasn't uncommon for them to feed Set food when they met in town, and on such occasions, they would exchange a few words with Rei, however brief.

The attitude toward Rei differed completely between the adventurers and the others.

There was no doubt that Rei's usual conduct influenced this difference.

"Alright. After I haul this tree... well, I'll be back here in a few hours or so. Oh, and I'll take the felled trees as they are, so mark them so I can tell who cut which one."

Rei made it clear he didn't want any arguments later about which tree he cut and which one someone else cut.

The woodcutters and adventurers probably understood that.

Each began marking their logs—some carved simple symbols into the trunks, others wrote their names clearly on paper and attached it.

Once preparations were complete for each tree, Rei touched them one after another, storing them away in the Misty Ring.

It wasn't unusual for Rei to use Misty Ring.

In fact, he frequently does things like ordering food in town and then storing the entire pot, plate, or frying pan... into the Misty Ring.

But... even so, seeing an entire tree vanish like this was astonishing to those who witnessed it.

Even those familiar with Rei felt this way, so the shock of someone new to Gilm was beyond ordinary.

"Whoa, storing that huge tree whole... So the rumor about Crimson having an item box is true after all."

"Yeah, that's what I hear. ...Well, it's obviously her own power, but she also has a griffin as a familiar. Even if she had an item box, there aren't many reckless fools who'd try to steal it."

"But they do show up sometimes."

An adventurer who'd been active in Gilm for several years told the newcomer.

"Ugh, really? Are there really such fools who'd dare mess with someone with a nickname?"

"Yeah. But even though he has a nickname, Rei earned the Crimson Nickname during the war with the Bestir Empire. Before that name really spread, even with his griffin, Rei was often underestimated."

Hearing this explanation, the adventurers listening all wore expressions of disbelief.

Now, Rei's true strength is widely known.

Rumors aside, many had witnessed Rei in action during the war against the Bestir Empire and other battles.

Given that, unless there was some clear reason, no one would willingly choose to antagonize Rei.

"So, who was the one who messed with Rei?"

At the man's timid question, the one explaining about Rei made a gesture as if slicing his own throat.

Seeing that, it wasn't hard to imagine what had become of the person who had crossed Rei.

"Better not mess with Rei, huh?"

"Yeah."

Their voices remained low... so low they could only be called whispers.

But in that instant, as their eyes met and understanding passed between them...

"Yeah. If you could avoid antagonizing her as much as possible, it'd be a big help to me too."

Rei, who had been storing felled trees into his Misty Ring a short distance away, called out.

The two adventurers, who hadn't expected their conversation to be overheard, froze for a moment.

They turned to look for the adventurer who had told them about Rei, hoping for help... but the person who had been there just seconds ago was nowhere to be seen.

(He got away!?)

That terrifying sense of danger... While they thought that, it was only natural to feel abandoned.

Still, Rei showed no sign of saying anything further to the two adventurers, reaching out instead to a nearby fallen tree.

"Having trees gathered like this nearby makes gathering them convenient."

"Yeah. Though, since we're cutting them from the outside in, this is only natural. ...But still, this forest seems to be getting bigger every day, doesn't it?"

The woodcutter who felled the tree Rei had stored muttered in puzzlement.

"Hm? Oh, yeah. At this rate, I bet trees will be growing here again by tomorrow."

"Exactly. It's fine that the forest expands overnight... No, normally that's impossible, but considering this is the frontier, it makes sense. But the fact that every tree growing in the expanded forest is above a certain thickness... What's up with that?"

As a woodcutter, that detail inevitably caught his attention.

To Rei, the fact that the forest was expanding and trees were growing here was already abnormal enough. The fact that all the trees were above a certain thickness was just a minor detail.

"Is it really something to worry about?"

"...As a woodcutter, it bothers me."

For the woodcutter conversing with Rei, it was genuinely a matter of concern.

He fixed his serious gaze on Rei as he asked.

"Well, what can I say? I'm not exactly an expert on this Treant Forest either. All I know is that it probably gets dangerous at night. In fact, yesterday I found what looked like the remains of a party wiped out somewhere ahead."

Slesha's party and the party whose belongings were scattered at what Rei thought was yesterday's campsite.

Based on those two incidents, I'm just guessing that nighttime is probably dangerous.

"Rei, is that true!?"

Rei's muttered words echoed with gasps of shock from those around him.

It wasn't the woodcutter who had been talking to Rei just seconds ago... but Slesha's voice, coming from a little further away.

Until just now, she had been watching Rei stow the wood in the Misty Ring, seemingly speechless, but she wasn't about to miss the words that came out of Rei's mouth.

"Slesha? And Runo too... So you two took this job as well? But it doesn't look like you've cut any trees."

"Yeah. I was helping Slesha out a bit. ...You understand, right?"

It was Runo, standing beside Slesha, who answered Rei.

But Slesha, who had been standing right next to him, ignored Runo's words and stepped forward.

The words that had just come out of Rei's mouth undoubtedly held significant meaning for her.

There was no doubt she had been attacked at night.

But... that alone didn't prove she'd be attacked again at night.

No, logically speaking, monsters are most active at night, so it wasn't that strange... Still, without proof, it was impossible to say for certain.

That's precisely why she couldn't afford to miss a single word Rei said now.

"Is that really true?"

"Huh? Yesterday... no, the day before yesterday, another party was attacked? Well, there wasn't any evidence, but it's probably true."

The reason Rei couldn't definitively say they'd been attacked was because Set hadn't been able to detect the scent of blood at the location where the luggage had been.

Given that Set was a griffin with enhanced olfactory abilities, if even a single drop of blood had been spilled, there was no way Set wouldn't have detected it.

Yet, at the location of the luggage, Set detected no scent of blood.

That meant there was something in this forest that could completely erase smells... something so effective that even Set couldn't detect them.

For Rei, who relied on Set's sense of smell when searching for something, this was a very troublesome opponent indeed.

"Is that so?"

Suresha murmured softly at Rei's words, unable to say anything more and falling silent.

Is something wrong? Rei wondered for a moment, but considering Slesha's circumstances, he couldn't say anything more.

The annihilation of a party member was a matter of immense gravity.

If Rei's own party... Marina, Vihera, and Byune... had been killed, he wouldn't know what he'd do either.

(Well, it's hard to imagine those three dying so easily, though.)

Marina had years of experience as a Rank A adventurer, and as a Dark Elf, she excelled in spirit magic and archery.

Vihera seems risky since she loves combat, but her wild instincts—or perhaps you could call it a woman's intuition—instinctively avoid lethal danger.

Compared to the other two, Byune's various abilities are lower, but she's still a thief, so her crisis detection skills are sharp.

(Huh? Thinking about it that way, could it be that the one with the lowest danger sense in the Crimson Wings is actually me? Well, in my case, I have Set...)

Watching Set's expression at the end of her gaze, Rei shook her head slightly.

Though confident in his own abilities, Rei didn't believe he could surpass Set when it came to sensory perception.

That Set was currently being petted and fed dried meat by the woodcutter and several adventurers, looking thoroughly pleased.

Even the adventurers who harbored various thoughts about Rei seemed different around the friendly Set.

Though this puzzled Rei a little, he decided that for now, storing the fallen tree in the Misty Ring was the priority and focused on that.

Even as he worked, the sound of axes striking tree trunks echoed through the forest, along with the thuds of branches snapping and trees crashing to the ground.

However, unlike the lumberjacks whose job it was to fell trees, the adventurers weren't quite as skilled at cutting them down.

Every tree felled by the woodcutter fell cleanly outward toward the forest's edge.

Precisely so that Rei could easily store them in the Misty Ring. ...Originally, this was done to save effort when loading the wood onto the cart.

But compared to the trees felled by the woodcutters, the trees cut by the adventurers were far from falling neatly.

Even if they fell toward the forest's edge, their directions varied wildly.

That was probably the better outcome.

Some trees weren't even falling toward the forest's edge, but inward instead.

Many trees couldn't fall to the ground because of others still standing, so they were leaning against them.

(Is this the difference between professionals and amateurs? ...Well, actually, felling a tree in the desired direction isn't as easy as it looks.)

When he was in Japan, Rei had helped his father clear fields by cutting down trees.

They weren't as thick as the trees growing in Treant Forest, but even so, he couldn't fell them exactly as he wanted.

(Well, since I can store them in Misty Ring anyway, it's not really a problem.)

After stowing all the wood the lumberjack had felled, Rei headed next toward the area where the adventurers had cut down their trees.
