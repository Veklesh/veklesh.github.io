A grassland littered with countless goblin corpses... There stood the hunters dispatched for this mission, including Rei and Set.

The stench of goblin blood, entrails, and bodily fluids permeated the air. The summer afternoon sun beat down, heating the air and intensifying the foul odor.

Had it been spring or autumn... or even just a cool morning, the stench wouldn't have been this overwhelming.

Despite the foul stench, the adventurers gathered without seeming particularly bothered.

Set, whose sense of smell was the keenest among them, let out a faint, displeased whimper, but even so, compared to the undead like zombies, it was still bearable.

"So, this is how it is... What do we do?"

The first to speak was Rei, the youngest present... and the highest-ranked adventurer.

Normally, having Rei, still only in his mid-teens, take the lead in the discussion wouldn't be amusing to the others.

But everyone present knew exactly who Rei was. And even if someone didn't, they'd seen him attacking the goblin horde from behind with fire magic and Set's fire breath.

Once they saw that, they couldn't deny who was the most powerful among them.

"What do you expect me to say... We can't just leave it like this, right? If we do, it'll definitely rot and stink, and monsters or animals might come looking for the goblin meat. Worst of all, there's the risk it could turn into undead."

The adventurer holding the hammer muttered, and the others nodded in agreement.

"Then let's burn it."

At Rei's words, everyone nodded again.

Every adventurer here was skilled in their own right.

Naturally, they understood that stripping the goblin's materials would yield little income relative to the effort.

"Looks like no one objects... Oh, right. I'll take the goblin leader's magic stone. Is that okay with you all?"

No one voiced any objection to Rei's question.

They all understood just how much work Rei had put in against the goblin horde.

"If it's just the goblin leader's magic stone, no one can complain if Rei takes it."

One of the adventurers nodded... and Rei began stowing the corpses of several goblin leaders into his Misty Ring.

These goblin leader corpses would be submitted to the guild as proof of completing this request.

"But is the magic stone alone enough? Goblin leaders have some decent materials, right?"

"Nah, the magic stone's fine for me. You guys can handle skinning these corpses. The reward from the guild is enough for me."

Hearing Rei's words, the other adventurers seemed slightly pleased.

To be honest, Rei was just passing off the hassle of dismembering the goblin leader's corpse to the others... but the other adventurers showed no sign of understanding that.

"Alright, we've stowed the goblin leader's corpse. Let's clear away the goblin corpses. Is that alright with everyone?"

After confirming no one objected, Rei took hold of the Death Scythe and began weaving a spell.

'Flames, feed upon my magic and consume the dead. Purify their unresolved grievances with my fire. Resentment, bitterness, envy, hatred. All are meaningless before my magic. Flames burn even grudges to ashes. Therefore, return to the heavens with my magic.'

Blue flames ignited at the tip of the Death Scythe.

He thrusts the tip into the ground... and the spell is complete.

'Flames of Mourning'

Blue flames spread across the ground, enveloping the goblin's corpse. Soon, the goblin's body crumbled within the flames.

Watching this scene, Rei felt the vast amount of magic power within him being depleted.

Originally, this spell contained elements of the Holy attribute in addition to the Fire attribute.

Precisely because of that, Rei, who only possessed aptitude for the fire attribute, was forcibly using this spell, resulting in an immense drain on his magic power.

Even if we only consider raw magical power, Rei's reserves were unmatched in this world. Yet, using this spell just once depleted a significant portion of them.

It was like a magician with aptitude accomplishing something with the effort of a single finger flick, while Ray was using the maximum power output of a nuclear power plant just to manage the same feat... that might make it easier to understand.

"Haa, haa, haa..."

He exhaled roughly, struggling to steady his breathing.

Seeing Rei like this, Set rubbed his face against her, his throat making a worried sound.

Are you okay? Set seemed to ask with his attitude.

The surrounding adventurers showed no sign of noticing Rei or Set's state, their eyes instead drawn to the spectacle before them... captivated by the blue flames.

It was only natural for them to associate flames with the color red.

To such people, blue flames were nothing but a strange sight.

Was it just his imagination, or did the collapsing goblin corpses look somehow... content?

"H-hey... this..."

One of the adventurers, who had been mesmerized by the blue flames, finally managed to utter just that.

That voice snapped the others out of their trance.

"What should we even call this... Fantastic. That word doesn't even begin to cover it."

"Yeah."

Watching the goblin corpses vanish one after another, each of them let out a gasp of awe.

A horde of goblins led by several goblin leaders.

With each pack numbering around a hundred, that meant hundreds of goblin corpses.

Some goblins managed to escape, but their numbers weren't particularly large.

In other words, nearly hundreds of goblins were burning... no, being purified by blue flames before the adventurers' eyes.

With that many burning blue like this, it wouldn't be an exaggeration to describe it as fantastical, as one of the adventurers had said.

But such a fantastical sight vanishes once there is nothing left to burn.

When the blue flames finally died out, not a single trace of goblin remains could be found in the surrounding area.

...Well, that might be an exaggeration, since goblin weapons and armor were scattered about.

"Well, now that we've dealt with the goblins, shouldn't we head back to Gilm? We need to report to the guild, and there's a chance another horde might be approaching the city."

Rei's voice snapped them back to reality. The adventurers hurriedly prepared to leave and headed for the carriage.

"Rei, you... wait, don't you need to ride in the carriage?"

One of the adventurers glanced over at Rei, who was stroking Set, and spoke before nodding to himself.

After all, he had seen with his own eyes that Rei could travel by riding Set.

"Yeah, go ahead. I'll be going separately with Set."

Just as he said that, he suddenly remembered Hester.

(He hated goblins, so it wouldn't have been strange if he'd taken this request. Well, it was daytime, so maybe he was busy with his own job.)

"What's wrong?"

"Ah, no. Nothing. If anyone wants to hang onto Set, I could bring them along. What do you think?"

"...Huh? Hang on? Not ride?"

At Rei's words, the man briefly let out a cry of joy, thinking, Could I ride Set too?! But he quickly realized Rei hadn't said "ride," but "hang on."

"Yeah. Unfortunately, Set basically won't let anyone but me ride on his back. Instead, he can carry you by grabbing your shoulders with his front legs."

"Ah... no, I'll take the carriage."

"Is that so? Well, I won't force you."

The adventurer man chatting with Rei seemed to prefer not to be carried by being grabbed by the front legs and dangled along for the ride. He simply stated that.

The other adventurers listening to Rei and the man exchanged hopeful glances, perhaps thinking they might get a ride on Set too? But it seemed they too preferred to avoid being grabbed by the front paws and dangling along for the ride.

One by one, they boarded the carriage.

Once everyone was inside, they gave Rei a quick farewell before departing.

Watching this, Rei stroked Set with a hint of dissatisfaction.

"I don't think being carried by the front legs is so bad, you know? Huh?"

"Grr?"

"Huh?" Set tilted his head slightly.

For Set, carrying people was something he could do, but if he didn't have to, he probably didn't mind.

"Well, whatever. Vihera might be back soon anyway. Let's head back."

"Guruu!"

The goblin horde approaching Gilm wasn't just the ones Rei and the others had fought here.

Considering that the goblin leader Rei and the others had defeated had several subordinates, that was only natural.

Vihera, who possessed combat prowess higher than the average adventurer just like Rei, had been dispatched to deal with a different goblin horde.

Vihera had intended to join Rei's group, but since she couldn't ride Set, she had to travel by carriage. The guild didn't have a surplus of carriages available for loan either.

Especially since Rei had already headed there, the destruction of that goblin horde was practically guaranteed. Sending additional forces there risked creating idle soldiers.

Of course, it was a guild request, and as the lord, Daskar had also dispatched his knights.

Given the sufficient combat strength, Vihera could have borrowed a horse instead of a carriage and gone alone to join Rei... but he judged it unnecessary to go that far. Thus, Vihera obediently headed for another goblin horde.

And for Rei, he couldn't imagine Vihera managing anything against a goblin horde... precisely why he figured it wouldn't be strange for her to be heading back to Gilm by now.

"Alright, let's go."

Laying that down, Rei mounted Set's back.

Set purred happily at Rei's presence, then took a few running steps before leaping into the air.

(Come to think of it, the goblin corpses were left behind when we went with Hester. Is that okay? ...Well, leaving them there would've been dangerous for Pump, so it couldn't be helped. I can only hope they're being eaten by beasts or other monsters.)

Besides, since it was in the woods, Ray also figured that even if it became undead, it probably wouldn't harm Gilm.

Rei's guess was it would probably be taken down by a higher-level monster.

"He'll manage over there somehow. ...Oh, I can see it now."

Flying through the sky, Set naturally arrived at Gilm far faster than those traveling by carriage.

Landing a short distance from the main gate, guards approached from the gate's direction.

Normally, the guards would wait at the main gate, but the fact that they were moving meant they were interested in Rei's actions... specifically, the goblin horde approaching Gilm.

"Rei's back, so that means you've already taken care of the goblin horde, right? How did it go?"

His tone conveyed absolute conviction that Rei, facing goblins—even a horde—could never be outmatched.

Then again, that was only natural for those who understood Rei's abilities.

In fact, facing a vast, chaotic horde was Rei's most favored combat style.

"Several goblin leaders each commanded their own packs, and they'd gathered together. I brought back the goblin leaders' corpses, but I burned all the goblins."

To be precise, it was to prevent them from becoming undead, but Rei didn't feel it necessary to explain that much.

If he were explaining to the guild, it might be different, but for the guards, this much information should suffice.

"You... burned them all... I heard there were quite a few... You truly live up to your nickname, Crimson."

The guard, upon hearing that every single goblin had been burned, was momentarily stunned, but quickly wore an expression of understanding.

Considering Rei's strength, it was only natural.

"Still, a goblin horde. If it were one large horde, it'd be easier to deal with. But when small groups like this keep appearing, it gets troublesome."

Rei nodded in agreement at the guard's words.

If there was just one large army somewhere, then sending Rei to wipe it out in one fell swoop would be the solution.

But with multiple small groups moving freely like this, they'd have to locate each one individually before taking them down.

(They call it the folly of spreading your forces thin, but this is a nasty tactic in its own right.)

He pondered this while talking with the guard, completing the procedures to enter Gilm.
