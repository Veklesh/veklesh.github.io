"What should we do?"

“Since they haven’t found us yet, we can handle them however we want.”

Hearing the guard say this, Rei hesitates, unsure of what to do.

In reality, if they decided to fight, subduing the other side wouldn’t be that difficult.

After all, their forces consist of only one person, and that one person is leading the men in red cloth with a rope.

Judging by his movements, he clearly possesses a certain level of strength, but it’s only enough for a security guard to subdue him.

Given that, if he intended to capture them, he should have been able to do so without fail.

Nevertheless, the reason Rei didn’t make an immediate decision to capture him is simply because there are still many unknowns surrounding this incident.

Moreover, judging from what I’m seeing right now, there’s no doubt he’s trying to use the men in red to accomplish something.

As a result, it’s certain they’ll end up as nothing but skin and bones, just like the corpses found in this mansion…

(No, letting them die right in front of the guards would probably cause a lot of trouble.)

As far as Rei is concerned, even if the Red Cloth members die, he wouldn’t feel all that much regret.

If someone told him it was the result of their own choice to act as Red Cloth members, he’d even accept it.

But that’s strictly Rei’s opinion; the guards feel differently.

Given that they tend to be a group of compassionate people—though there are some hardliners who believe criminals deserve to die—there’s absolutely no way they’d choose to let a Red Cloth member die here when the odds are so high.

“Let’s capture him. If he’s the one holding that rope, he probably knows a lot of information.”

In the end, that was what Rei chose.

Of course, if the one holding that rope knows something, all we have to do is get it out of him, so in the end, it probably amounts to the same thing.

“Got it. But how do we catch him?”

The guard said this because the man had entered from a spot that was quite a distance away from where Rei and the others were standing.

In fact, even if they sprinted over there right now, there’s a high chance the man with the rope would escape in the worst-case scenario.

They might be able to catch him if they chased him, but there was also a chance he could get away.

So, what should we do…? After thinking it over, Rei came up with a simple solution.

“It looks like he’s heading toward the center of this space, so how about we advance to a spot where he can’t escape even if he runs, and then go in to apprehend him?”

“That’s probably the safest bet. But do you think he’ll head back toward the stairs he came down? There’s also the possibility he might head toward a different set of stairs…”

“If that happens, it really comes down to how quickly we can catch him. …Instead of chasing him as a group, maybe we should each act separately?”

At Rei’s words, the three security guards nodded.

They must have judged from the man’s demeanor—and the fact that he was holding a rope—that they could capture him on their own.

“Alright, then let’s wait a little longer before we move. …I wonder why I’m the one in charge.”

Rei muttered to himself, suddenly struck by that thought.

Normally, apprehending the suspect should have been the guards’ job, not Rei’s.

But for some reason, it was Rei who was in charge right now.

He found this puzzling himself, but one of the guards, as if to say not to worry about it, gave Rei a light pat on the shoulder.

“All things considered, when it comes to making quick decisions in situations like this, Rei has more experience than we do, so he’s better at it. In that case, it’s faster if Rei takes charge.”

“Is that so? The suggestion I made was just to move him to a place where he couldn’t escape and then catch him—I think it’s the kind of thing anyone could come up with.”

“Even so. It’s a huge help to have someone like Rei around who’s used to situations like this.”

Rei tilts his head at the guard’s words.

If we’re talking about being seasoned, the guard should encounter various crime scenes almost every day, so it wouldn’t be strange if he were more seasoned than Rei.

He had that thought, but figured, well, if that’s what the guard says, then so be it. He didn’t say anything more on the matter and brought up something else.

“Well then, let’s get moving for now. They’ve already made quite a bit of progress through this underground space.”

Normally, if beings like Rei and her group were in the same space, there’s no way they wouldn’t notice them.

However, Rei and the others had been hiding near the stairs they’d come down, and thanks to that, they hadn’t been spotted.

Of course, it would be a different story if the man in front of Rei and the others had keen observational skills.

In any case, the guards nodded at Rei’s words and began to move.

At first, they moved slowly to avoid being spotted, heading toward the man advancing toward the center of the underground space.

The guards other than Rei each headed toward the man from different directions—specifically, toward staircases other than the one they had come down.

And so, Rei proceeded while engaging in this contradictory behavior—trying to draw the man’s attention while also trying not to be spotted—but…

“Hmm, hmm, hmmm.”

The man holding the rope seemed completely oblivious to Rei’s presence as he approached, focused entirely on his humming.

Perhaps, if the men in red cloth were in their right minds, they might have alerted him to Rei’s presence… but unfortunately, the men in red cloth, bound by rope, possessed no sense of self.

Because of this situation, the man with the rope showed no sign of noticing Rei approaching him, nor the guards.

He likely believed this underground space to be an absolutely safe place.

In fact, considering that there was a staircase leading to this underground space behind a hidden door in the mansion where Rei and the others were staying, it was undoubtedly impossible to find this place so easily.

That is precisely why the man with the rope thought the same way… he believed this place was absolutely safe, felt no sense of danger, and never imagined that people like Rei and his group would be here.

...If word had gotten out that Rei and his group were conducting a forced search of the mansion, the outcome might have been different.

Unfortunately for him, that information hadn’t reached him—which was his misfortune.

As a result, Rei approached very close to Akabu and the others, who were bound with rope... and only then did the man, who had been focused on humming to himself, notice Rei’s presence.

And it wasn’t even the sound of footsteps that alerted him; he was humming contentedly and happened to glance over to check on the men in red cloth, only to make eye contact with Rei by chance—that’s when he finally realized Rei was there.

Had Rei not gotten so close, he might have been able to make a run for it.

However, having them get this close was completely unexpected, and that’s precisely why, in the current situation, the man had absolutely no way to escape.

“What!?”

All the man could do was let out a cry of surprise.

The next moment, Rei pounced on the man and easily pinned him to the ground.

The impact of being thrown to the ground caused him to let go of the rope, but the Red Cloths made no move to flee; they simply stood there.

This clearly demonstrated that the Red Cloths lacked any sense of self.

“Damn it! What the hell are you doing?! Let me go!”

The pinned man shouts in frustration, but of course, Rei has no intention of letting go.

However, from the man’s perspective, he has no idea who is holding him down, which is precisely why he shouts so defiantly.

“You bastard, do you know who I am!? Do you think you’ll get away with this?”

“Well, I don’t know who you are, so if you don’t mind, I’d like you to tell me exactly who you are.”

“What!? You’d better not regret this!”

In response to Rei’s somewhat provocative words, the man shouts in frustration.

Under normal circumstances, the man wouldn’t have so readily taken the bait of Rei’s provocation.

But being attacked like this in a place that should have been completely safe had sent him into a near-panic, and that was what had led to the current situation.

“Just tell me. Who are you? And why would I regret anything? It’s hardly likely that you—who got caught without even noticing me approaching—would make me regret anything, is it?”

“Guh!”

Realizing he’d been captured by Rei so easily, the man fell silent.

But that lasted only a few seconds.

Driven by a strong desire to stand up to his captor and make him regret it, he says something he shouldn’t have.

“Don’t say you didn’t hear me! I serve a faction within the Royal Faction that wields immense influence! If you get that, let me go right now! Otherwise, even your acquaintances will meet the worst possible fate!”

Hearing those blunt words, Rei even begins to wonder if this might be some kind of bluff.

It was clearly too convenient to reveal his own affiliation to such a suspicious-looking opponent—especially one who was investigating this very incident.

“What do you think?” Rei asked, turning his gaze toward the approaching guard.

The guards must have heard the man Rei was holding down as well. They looked slightly doubtful, but eventually shook their heads.

That likely meant the man Rei was holding was telling the truth.

Rei and his team were here strictly for the investigation.

But the man being held couldn’t possibly know that.

It wouldn’t be strange at all if he thought they were just petty thieves or something, and that if he mentioned his backers, they’d get scared and back off.

(Still, the Royal Faction? Another headache.)

Rei knew a few people in the Royal Faction.

But he couldn’t possibly believe that those acquaintances had orchestrated this incident…

To begin with, the Royal Faction is the largest of the so-called three major factions.

Precisely because of that, Rei knew from someone he’d spoken to before that there were several sub-factions within the Royal Faction.

It was probably the work of a faction within the Royal Faction that Rei had never had anything to do with.

Even so, Rei couldn’t fathom what could have motivated them to pull a stunt like this.

“The Royal Faction, huh. …But for the Royal Faction—the largest of the three major factions—to have Kobolds attack Gilm? What’s that all about?”

“Shut up! Their souls are just trash—what else can we do?!”

Rei flinched slightly at those words and looked at the mindless red cloths while holding the man down.

Rei had mentioned the Kobolds partly as a test.

But the man took the bait perfectly and uttered a decisive statement...

(Souls... so does that mean they intended to use these red cloths as some kind of sacrifice?)

At this point, the assumption that the Kobold was being drawn in or controlled by a magic item—as Rei had suspected—had completely collapsed.

Of course, the magic item theory was merely something Rei had hoped for—nothing more than wishful thinking.

Considering that, while the idea of a sacrifice came as a bit of a surprise to the other guards, it was still a plausible explanation.

To begin with, even for a Kobold, controlling such a massive number of monsters is no ordinary feat, and the sheer number of Kobolds is the biggest factor of all.

Despite kobolds being killed day and night, almost every single day, the number of kobolds arriving in Gilm shows no sign of decreasing.

There must have been a fair number of Kobolds in the area around Gilm, but given the number that had been killed so far, it wouldn’t have been surprising if all the Kobolds in this region had been wiped out.

And yet, even today, Kobolds are gathering in Gilm once again.

It would be virtually impossible to pull off something like that using conventional methods.

But if they’re performing some sort of sacrifice, then perhaps… just maybe, it wouldn’t be strange if they were able to bring Kobolds here from somewhere else.

“Hmm. The quality of the soul, you say. Does that affect the types of monsters that appear around here?”

“Of course. Since we’re at it, I’d prefer stronger ones… Wait. Who the hell are you guys? What are you doing in a place like this?”

He must have thought something was off about Rei and the guards.

The man tried to catch a glimpse of Rei’s face as he held him down, looking at him with suspicion.
