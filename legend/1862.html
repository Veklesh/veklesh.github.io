Black Wolf. He is an assassin who is after Rei’s life.

But for some reason, he never actually goes so far as to seriously target Rei.

In reality, he has attacked Rei a few times, but neither of those instances felt like a serious attempt; they were more like him testing the waters.

That Black Wolf is currently watching Rei from a rooftop quite a distance away—more than 300 meters, to be exact.

The fact that he was able to discern that it was a person, and furthermore, identify it as the Black Wolf, was likely due to the fact that Rei’s body, created by Zepair, possessed keen senses. But…

(A black wolf? Is it really a black wolf?)

The black wolf that had made contact with Rei several times recently—and which he’d practically fed like a pet with steamed buns—had silver hair.

But the black wolf Rei was looking at now had black fur.

That was the drug addict who attacked him after the large-scale mock battle.

When he defeated those guys, their hair color was the same as when he first encountered them.

Because he had interacted with them directly, Rei had a strong image of the Black Wolf as having silver hair, but the Black Wolf currently in his line of sight undoubtedly had hair that was worthy of being called the Black Wolf.

(Well, come to think of it, if it had silver hair, it wouldn’t be called a Black Wolf—it’d be called a Silver Wolf, right?)

Thinking that, Rei recalled his previous encounters with the Black Wolf and suddenly realized something.

(Could it be that his hair turns silver at night? And black during the day? ...Well, it’s possible. This is a world where magic and such exist.)

Did he come here again just because he wanted to eat a meat bun?

As if trying to coax him, Rei turned her gaze toward the Black Wolf… and their eyes met.

“Huh!?”

In that instant, Rei felt an overwhelming killing intent.

It felt as though all the rapport they’d built up until now had vanished completely.

No, it was a killing intent so intense that she couldn’t even spare a moment to think about such things.

What was truly terrifying, however, was that the black wolf’s killing intent was directed precisely and solely at Rei, while the others around him seemed completely oblivious to it.

Even for someone not accustomed to combat, feeling the Black Wolf’s killing intent like this—regardless of whether they could recognize it as such—would likely cause some physical reaction.

Yet everyone around Rei was going about their lives as usual.

In other words, it meant that the killing intent was being directed specifically at Rei.

However… that would only be the case for ordinary people…

“Hmm, this killing intent… seems to be coming from a rather skilled opponent.”

For someone like Elena, who was standing right next to Rei, the situation was different.

Also, since the killing intent was directed specifically at Rei, the fact that she was right next to him likely played a significant role.

The drunkenness he had felt just moments ago vanished instantly the moment he sensed the killing intent directed at Rei.

Rei couldn’t tell if the drunkenness had truly vanished, or if his body had simply entered combat mode, temporarily making him forget it.

He didn’t know… but even so, there was only one choice for Rei in this situation.

“Sorry, Elena. That guy… the Black Wolf… is my enemy. Don’t get involved.”

“What?”

Black Wolf.

The moment that word was spoken, Elena’s gaze followed Rei’s.

Because she inherited the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone, Elena’s physical abilities have become far superior to what they used to be.

In fact, they were on par with Rei’s.

Elena’s gaze naturally fell upon the Black Wolf at the end of Rei’s line of sight… a figure with black hair instead of silver, whose appearance was truly worthy of the name “Black Wolf.”

“That’s… the Black Wolf.”

As a resident of Anesis, Elena naturally knew the Black Wolf’s name.

However, naturally, she had never seen the Black Wolf in person before, so in that sense, she could consider herself lucky to be seeing him with her own eyes right now.

That said, it wasn’t so much a matter of luck in seeing something rare, but rather the fact that she was able to recognize the face of a skilled assassin in advance.

“But why? He’s an assassin. And not just any assassin. I don’t think there’d be a problem if I lent Rei a hand, would there?”

Elena’s voice was at a perfect volume—low enough that the passersby around them couldn’t hear, yet loud enough that Rei could hear it clearly.

Given that they were having such a dangerous conversation about assassins, she must have judged it too risky to speak loudly enough for others to hear.

In fact, while Rei was wearing the Dragon Robe and a hood, Elena wasn’t doing anything in particular to hide her identity.

Since the new year had just begun, many of Anesis’s residents were still in a festive mood.

Though, to be fair, Rei—who had turned the mock battle at the end of the year into a near-festival-like commotion—had actually played a part in that as well.

In any case, if other passersby were to overhear talk of assassins in the current situation, it would lead to serious trouble.

Elena also harbored concerns that, depending on the situation, the Black Wolf might attack her along with the bystanders.

Unlike Rei, who has met the Black Wolf numerous times and has even gone so far as to feed him, today was the first time Elena had encountered the Black Wolf directly—though, given the distance, strictly speaking, she had only seen him.

Given that, Elena had no basis for judgment other than the rumors about the Black Wolf.

Rumors that it was an extremely skilled assassin.

That was precisely why Elena suspected the Black Wolf might act strangely.

That was the reasoning behind Elena’s actions.

Seeing Elena like this, Rei was about to tell her she was overthinking it… but the next moment, the Black Wolf’s killing intent was directed at Rei once more, and he was unable to say another word.

The Black Wolf now is clearly different from the one Rei has dealt with until now.

If I had to put it into words, he felt similar to the Black Wolf Ray first encountered during the attack by the drug addict.

In any case, the Black Wolf currently in Rei’s line of sight clearly seemed like a different entity from the one Rei had dealt with before—the one he’d tried to lure with a meat bun.

“I’ve had some dealings with that Black Wolf, even if only briefly. If possible, I’d like to handle this myself.”

“Rei… this isn’t a game. If the opponent is a Black Wolf, it’s come out of its own accord. In that case, we should work together to defeat it right here.”

Elena’s words were absolutely correct.

After all, a skilled assassin is, by definition, a very troublesome opponent.

Precisely because of that, Elena’s words—that they should defeat him when they have the chance—were correct.

But even so... from Rei’s perspective, having dealt with the Black Wolf many times before, he wanted to see for himself what had become of him, if possible.

“I’ve brought Black Wolf food a few times before. He seemed to have taken a liking to me, so maybe I can get him to surrender without a fight.”

“It’s impossible.”

Elena stated this so categorically because she, too, could sense the killing intent emanating from the Black Wolf.

It was a killing intent that made it impossible to believe the Black Wolf would surrender.

In fact, it was so intense that she could easily believe the Black Wolf wasn’t planning to surrender at all, but was instead preparing to challenge them to a fight.

“...Even so, please.”

Normally, Elena would be right.

Even though he knew that, Rei—who, for one reason or another, had become involved with the Black Wolf—still made that plea.

In reality, he hadn’t spent much time with the Black Wolf, so it wouldn’t be strange if someone asked why he was so fixated on him.

Still, it was an undeniable fact that Rei had come to feel that way when dealing with the Black Wolf.

Whether she understood Rei’s feelings or simply judged from his demeanor that anything more she said would be pointless… eventually, Elena let out a sigh before speaking.

“If the Black Wolf were to harm those around us… if he were to harm the residents of Anesis, I would intervene myself. As a member of the Duke of Kerebel’s family, that is one thing I cannot compromise on. Is that acceptable to you?”

Elena’s words conveyed a resolve that she would absolutely not compromise on that point.

Rei, too, nodded in agreement without any objection to Elena’s words.

“I understand. If you’re willing to stand by and watch, that’s fine with me.”

“Hmm. Well then, shall we go? From the looks of things, they seem to be waiting for us to arrive.”

As she said this, Elena’s gaze shifted to the black wolf on the roof, which was staring intently at Rei.

But even though the black wolf is aware that Elena is looking at him, he shows no reaction whatsoever.

It kept its gaze fixed solely on Rei, its prey.

“...He’s completely fixated on Rei. It’s a bit unusual for me to be ignored like this.”

As Elena laughed, it seemed to Rei that she was still slightly tipsy.

Of course, Rei could predict that once the battle actually began, the power of the Ancient Dragon within Elena would completely sober her up.

(Well, that’s beside the point…)

Right now, it’s better to think about the Black Wolf than about Elena.

Having made that decision, Rei began walking while watching the Black Wolf in the distance.

Fortunately, Rei had his Misty Ring, and like the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, the weapons he used on a daily basis were stored inside it.

Since that was the case, he wouldn’t have to go through the trouble of returning to Duke Kerebel’s mansion just to retrieve his weapons.

(Speaking of going back for weapons, I wouldn’t be surprised if the Black Wolf would wait for me. …Though, that would be the silver-haired Black Wolf, not the black-haired one.)

Thinking about the opponent he was about to face, Rei walked along the path.

Having walked through Anesis several times before, he had a general idea of where he was.

That said, in a city as vast as Anesis, the more intricate areas are truly labyrinthine.

In a place like that, anyone visiting for the first time would inevitably get lost… but Rei, strangely enough, shows no signs of being lost as he makes his way forward.

When flying through the sky with Set, he would occasionally take a wrong turn, but the Rei of the moment showed no sign of that at all.

Elena, walking behind Rei, wondered, “How is Rei able to navigate such a place without getting lost?” But she remained silent, knowing that even if she asked Rei now, he wouldn’t tell her.

The two continued walking in silence for about ten minutes.

Suddenly, the view opened up before them.

It opened up in a truly mysterious way, revealing a clearing about the size of a small park.

In the center of it all, the black wolf—who should have been on the roof of the building just moments ago—was waiting.

“What?”

Seeing the black wolf, Elena turned her gaze toward the building where it had been standing just moments ago.

But since the black wolf was here, there was obviously no way it could still be on the roof.

Rei could tell Elena was confused.

After all, Rei had been through the same thing himself.

“It seems to be the Black Wolf’s skill. …Though I don’t know exactly what kind of skill it is.”

Muttering so Elena could hear him, Rei took another step toward the Black Wolf.

“Well, it’s been a while. Looks like you didn’t come here just to eat a meat bun again today…”

Even now, the Black Wolf was radiating a murderous aura toward Rei.

It was such a powerful killing intent that it wouldn’t be surprising if an ordinary person were affected by it.

However, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered by the Black Wolf’s killing intent as he pulled a meat bun out of his Misty Ring.

The last time he showed the steamed bun, the Black Wolf had fixed his gaze on it the very next moment, but now there was no sign of that, and the killing intent directed at Rei hadn’t ceased.

Unlike before, the black wolf shows no sign of emotion as it fixes its gaze entirely on Rei, who puts the meat bun he was holding back into his Misty Ring.

“Looks like he’s not in the mood for discussion. …But I thought we were talking normally just the other day. Why did things suddenly turn out like this?”

Rei asked, his hands empty, but the Black Wolf didn’t offer him any kind of answer.

Well, since Rei had never actually seen the Black Wolf speak before, that wasn’t particularly surprising.

Still, Rei asked the black wolf.

“If you don’t like meat buns, maybe you’d like some other kind of meat? Hmm, how about a stew made with orc meat?”

Perhaps because he’d been feeding him, Rei really didn’t want to fight the Black Wolf if he could help it.

After all, he used to be so innocent that you’d never have guessed he was an assassin, so why is he now directing such a murderous intent at me?

Since he hadn’t known the Black Wolf for very long, it was unreasonable to expect him to fully understand its motives.

He took a step, then another, toward the Black Wolf… and in the next instant, the killing intent he’d been sensing grew even more intense, causing Rei to leap back almost reflexively.

Then, right behind the spot where Rei had been just a moment ago, the Black Wolf appeared… and swung its sharp, dagger-like claws at the spot where Rei’s body had been.
