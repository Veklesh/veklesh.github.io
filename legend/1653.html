Those bandits were a group that had begun operating around Sublusta about a year ago and called themselves "Pure White."

Although the name sounded like that of a group of heroes, contrary to its name, the actions of "Immaculate White" were extremely brutal.

When they attack caravans, they try to capture people without killing them whenever possible, but of course, this isn’t out of any humanitarian concern… rather, the main reason is that they enjoy having someone to scream and cry while they vent their frustrations.

Those captured were subjected to treatment that made every single one of them regret ever being born.

They were forced to drink boiling water, used as targets for throwing daggers, had their fingernails torn out, their eyes gouged out, and their teeth forcibly pulled out... and so on.

If these acts were a form of torture intended to extract information, the victim would likely find relief by revealing what they know.

But... the members of "Immaculate Purity" commit such acts purely for their own amusement.

...The silver lining, perhaps, was that the thieves belonging to the Immaculate White were, after all, merely sadists—people who took pleasure in inflicting pain—and had no interest in tormenting women in a sexual sense.

That said, if you were to ask whether that really counts as good luck, I’m sure everyone would say no.

Even if they aren’t assaulted in a sexual sense, they are still assaulted physically.

And all for the sake of hearing her screams and cries.

Today, too, the members of "Immaculate Purity"—just to kill time—pierced the hand of the only surviving woman with a dagger, pinned her to a tree trunk, and then all of them began hurling stones, daggers, and tree branches lying around at her, reveling in her screams.

But perhaps because her screams were so pleasing to the ear, they got too carried away and the woman died... Just as they were discussing whether they should go hunt for another victim, that voice echoed throughout the area.

“Grrrrrrr!”

For those of immaculate purity, that was the beginning of a nightmare.

At first, they thought it was just some monster flying through the sky that had let out a cry.

If that had been all, honestly, they wouldn’t have needed to worry all that much.

In fact, the place where the Immaculate Whites currently used as their base was well-concealed using the surrounding trees, and they were highly confident that there was no risk of being discovered.

One of the main reasons the Immaculate Whites—known as a vicious band of thieves—had never been spotted by adventurers or guards was precisely because their concealment techniques were so masterful.

But while they could deceive human eyes, they could not deceive the eyes of Set… the Griffin.

The moment that cry echoed through the area, the one who truly brought nightmares into being revealed himself.

“You lot aren’t even worth selling as slaves. …Fortunately, it seems this is your base, so there’s no need to extract information from you. Therefore… die.”

The figure who had suddenly appeared and killed one of the bandits spoke those words, then, as if pulling it out of thin air, hurled a spear using sheer brute strength.

Launched as if slicing through the air itself, the spear flew at a speed so incredible it was hard to believe it was a spear… a speed that could easily be mistaken for an arrow shot from a bow. It pierced through several bandits at once and finally came to a halt after piercing the trunk of a nearby tree.

“N… Now! He threw his spear, so that scythe is his only weapon left! Let’s all charge at once! Let’s show them the power of our immaculate purity!”

One of the bandits shouted, still shaken.

If he hadn’t been so flustered, he would have realized who his attacker was the moment he saw the scythe.

There are many adventurers, but those who wield scythes are a very small minority.

And if that person could wield both a spear and a scythe, there was no way they wouldn’t have known who it was.

And to challenge someone to a fight without knowing who they are is, in this case, nothing short of a fatal mistake.

If the people present here had been able to figure out Rei’s true identity, they wouldn’t have made the mistake of needlessly challenging him to a fight; they would have fled immediately.

In fact, it wouldn’t have been strange at all for them to vanish into the forest at top speed, without a second thought for their comrades, just to save themselves.

But… precisely because the attack was worthy of being called a surprise assault, the pure-white ones were unable to realize in that split second that Rei was Rei… the man with the nickname “Crimson,” feared by bandits as the “Bandit Eater.”

They were the ones who always launched surprise attacks; they likely never imagined they themselves would be ambushed.

Furthermore, their confidence in the effectiveness of the camouflage used to hide this base had led to the complacency of those immaculate, pure-white bandits.

To Rei, such thieves weren’t even enemies… they were nothing more than targets.

Facing such targets, Rei swings his Death Scythe, effortlessly reaping their lives.

With a single swing of Rei’s Death Scythe, the hands, legs, torsos, and necks of several bandits are severed, leaving them utterly helpless.

Some, possessing a keen sense of danger and realizing that as outlaws, they would surely face execution if caught, fled accordingly.

This band of thieves is, after all, a group of outlaws, and very few of them possess any sense of camaraderie.

It was those very people who fled first...

“Grrrrrrrrrr!”

The blow from Set’s forepaw, as he plummeted from the sky, easily crushed the bandits’ heads and torsos.

As they were crushed, they slammed into nearby trees, and the impact shattered their bones.

“Ugh… AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAGH!”

Seeing the death of their comrade—or more accurately, the way he was so easily killed by Set—the bandits let out a scream.

Combined with Set’s earlier cry, several of the bandits finally turned their gaze toward him.

“G… It’s a griffin! Aaaaaaah!”

That single word proved fatal.

They were already off guard, and now they were being ambushed by a vicious creature like Rei, with their comrades being killed one after another.

On top of that, with a griffin now appearing, these bandits—who had spent their lives preying on weaker opponents to satisfy their sadistic urges—lacked both the ability and the resolve to recover from this situation.

And what sealed their fate above all else was...

“Wait a minute. A griffin? And the adventurer who attacked us was wielding a scythe… Wait, that’s Crimson, the Bandit Eater!”

Neither Rei nor the bandits knew who had shouted that decisive line.

The only thing they knew was that the figure that had appeared before them was a being that was nothing short of fatal to the bandits.

It wasn’t an opponent they could defeat in battle; it was a being that would bring absolute death upon them, just like the Death Scythe Rei wielded.

“Ugh… Aaaaaaah!”

The moment they realized who Rei was, the spotless, pure-white bandits scrambled to flee.

It was a flight born of the thought that, rather than anyone daring to challenge Rei, they just needed to escape—and escape successfully.

“If you’re going to run, then you shouldn’t have been a thief in the first place!”

The Twilight Spear, unleashed along with that angry shout, pierced several fleeing bandits through the back.

Among the bandits pierced from behind, by some strange coincidence, there were some who remained impaled on the Twilight Spear.

Seeing the tip of the Twilight Spear stuck in a tree trunk, with several bandits impaled through the back along with it, Rei was briefly reminded of the phrase “a shrike’s snare.”

Naturally, the bandits who had suffered such a fate were already dead, and when the Twilight Spear returned to Rei’s hand through its power, they collapsed from the tree trunk to the ground.

“Come on, don’t run away! You go by a ridiculous name like ‘Immaculate White.’ Show me some real power!”

“It’s a waste to use the Twilight Spear on the likes of you,” Rei said, thrusting the Twilight Spear into a nearby tree trunk before pulling a spear from his Misty Ring.

It was the weapon Rei had used before obtaining the Twilight Spear—one with a chipped tip and a handle on the verge of breaking, something that should normally be treated as scrap.

Holding that spear, Rei hurled it with a twist of his body.

Since he didn’t take a running start and wasn’t throwing with full force, both the speed and power were far inferior to the strike from the Twilight Spear he had just fired.

However, while it might not be enough against a truly formidable opponent, it possesses more than enough power to attack the hopeless bandits currently in Rei’s line of sight.

The spears, one after another, sliced through the air and pierced the bandits—one for each spear thrown.

The bandits who died with their heads crushed could be said to have been lucky, as they died without feeling any pain.

Some had their sides pierced, their thighs shattered, or their arms crushed...

Those individuals were knocked down by the impact and ended up rolling around on the ground.

Naturally, dirt sticks to their wounds, creating unsanitary conditions unsuitable for treatment… but for Rei, who has no intention of treating the bandits and has decided to kill them all, seeing their plight does not move him in the slightest.

If anything, he casts a cold, dismissive glance at them—as if to say, “Go ahead and become food for monsters or animals”—then sets off in pursuit of the one who managed to escape.

(Well, I wouldn’t exactly call that good luck, though.)

Even if they manage to escape from here, that doesn’t mean they’ve truly succeeded in their escape.

No matter how accustomed they are to moving through the forest as bandits, that doesn’t mean they can possibly escape from Rei… or from Set, Rei’s partner.

“AAAAAAAAAAAAAAGH!”

Indeed, a scream echoes through the forest.

A scream utterly out of place in the forest, where the branches of the trees block the summer sunlight and dappled sunlight filters through.

Turning toward the source of the scream, Rei retrieved his Twilight Spear, stowed it in the Misty Ring along with the Death Scythe, and began to move forward.

After walking for only a few minutes, Rei’s sense of smell—sharper than that of an ordinary person—detected the scent of rusted iron.

There was no need to wonder whose blood it was; it was obvious.

In fact, as Rei walked, he immediately spotted a bandit who had lost both legs, rolling on the ground and enduring the pain.

Looking closer, he saw two legs—presumably the bandit’s—lying on the ground a short distance away.

It wasn’t a clean, sharp cut like the one made by Rei’s Death Scythe, but a single blow that had forcibly shattered them with brute force.

As for who had done it, that, too, was obvious to Rei.

“Ugh, aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah! Don’t come, don’t come, don’t come!”

“Eeeeeeeeeek! I’m sorry, I’m sorry. Forgive me, forgive me, forgive meee!”

“Mom, Mom! Where are you? I’m right here! Help me, please! Don’t come, I beg you!”

Such screams were rising from all over the forest.

“...I’ve made Set angry.”

The Griffin is feared and known as the Grim Reaper of the Skies.

But as you can tell from its lion’s lower body, it’s not just a master of the skies—it’s no slouch on the ground either.

And this is the forest, after all. For monsters and wild beasts, it’s a place that could truly be called their home turf.

What would happen if you did something to anger Set in a place like this? ...The screams currently echoing through the forest were the answer.

The reason for provoking Set… was obvious.

Leaving the rest of the bandits to Set, Rei walked back to the spot where he had just landed.

There, he found the corpse of a woman, completely naked, her hands pierced by daggers and pinned to a tree trunk.

It was obvious at a glance that the woman was already dead.

After all, if she were alive, there was no way she could remain so still with multiple daggers piercing her body, and above all… with a dagger buried up to the hilt in her forehead, it was hard to believe she could still be alive.

The only reason he could recognize her as a woman was because she was completely naked.

Otherwise, I would never have recognized that creature—with wounds all over her face, starting with her forehead—as a woman.

“...I should have made her suffer a little longer before killing her.”

Recalling the bandits he had just impaled with his spear, Rei muttered with a look of disgust.

As he did so, he took a cloth from the Misty Ring, drew the dagger from his palm, laid it on the cloth, wrapped the body in it, and then stored it back in the Misty Ring.

It couldn’t store living creatures, but it could store corpses.

“At least I’ve avenged her,” he murmured to himself… and then entered the cave that served as their immaculate, pure-white base.
