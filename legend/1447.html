"Wow... It's hot again today, even in the morning. If only the clouds would do a little more, it'd be a big help for us."

Rei muttered, gazing up at the sky.

The cloudless blue sky seemed to stretch endlessly high, higher, and higher still.

The sun floated in that blue sky, asserting itself as if to say, "Here I am!"

It silently declared itself the undisputed star of this summer sky.

Of course, Rei was wearing a Dragon Robe with built-in air conditioning, so his situation was quite different from those doing other jobs... no, the difference in their working conditions was like heaven and hell.

"With a space this vast... there's simply no escaping the heat, you know."

Responding to Rei's mutter was not the dark elf Marina... but the elf Feilma.

Last night, they'd celebrated their long-awaited reunion at the Dusk Wheat inn, having a reasonably good time.

Vihera, who initially had reservations about Feirma, had since learned that Feirma's feelings toward Rei weren't romantic but rather those of friendship. Since then, their relationship had improved considerably.

"Can't you do something with spirit magic? At this rate, some of us might get too hot to move!"

"Impossible. Covering an area this vast with spirit magic would require far more mana than I possess. Lady Marina... you agree, right?"

"Unfortunately, that's how it is."

Marina, meeting Feilma's gaze, smiled without breaking a sweat despite the heat as she acknowledged this.

What Marina was doing was easy for Feilma to guess.

She was using wind spirit magic to create a comfortable space around herself.

"Marina... only yourself..."

Feilma voiced her dissatisfaction. Rei tilted her head in puzzlement and spoke.

"If you envy Marina's situation, why don't you do the same, Feilma?"

"Impossible."

Feilma immediately shook her head at Rei's question.

"The skill level in spirit magic itself is vastly different between me and Marina-sama. And the amount of magic power we possess... If I tried to do what Marina-sama does, I'd run out of magic power during today's work."

Feirma explained, and Rei nodded in understanding, as if to say, "Oh, I see."

For Rei, who possessed immense magical power, running out of mana was an experience he had rarely encountered.

Rei’s trump card, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, consumed vast amounts of magic power, yet he had never before experienced it depleting his reserves to the point of exhaustion.

Feilma seemed to understand his feelings. She sighed and shot an envious glance at both Rei and Marina.

"Talented people..."

"Rei! Rei! Rei!"

An old man approached Rei, interrupting Feilma's words.

Who it was... Recalling yesterday's events, Rei understood immediately.

A look of displeasure flashed across Rei's face.

The old man who had seen Rei's... well, more accurately, Death Scythe's skill up close must have developed a strong interest in Rei.

Yesterday, during the break, he had approached Rei and tried to talk about various things. Even after work ended, he had attempted to call out to Rei.

Perhaps realizing his persistence wasn't well-received, he had quietly backed off yesterday... But with the new day, that realization seemed reset, and he approached Rei directly.

"Ah... plenty of folks are already wilting in this morning heat, but you're looking pretty lively yourself."

"Don't be so formal with someone like me... Call me Marutsu."

"Well, it's not like we're strangers. We just met yesterday. Why are you acting so familiar all of a sudden? ... Fine, I guess it's okay if you call me by my name."

The old man... Marutsu spoke, and Rei responded with a slightly displeased expression.

"Oh, thank you. ...So, about that. I'd like to accompany you today as your assistant, Lord Rei. What do you think?"

Marutsu asked with eyes shining brightly, leaving Rei wondering what to do.

His tone wasn't the typical old man's voice from their first meeting either; it felt somehow rejuvenated.

Was it that his curiosity had been piqued by the very existence of Rei... causing him to feel rejuvenated? Or had this been his natural tone all along, and he'd adopted that other manner to project authority?

He couldn't be sure of the details, but regardless, this tone was undoubtedly easier for Rei to interact with.

Considering yesterday's incident, it was obvious to Rei what Marutsu was aiming for by saying such things.

The terrain manipulation skill Rei had wielded with the Death Scythe must have struck a chord with Marutsu.

According to what Rei heard from Marina after work yesterday, Marutsu was a fairly well-known figure as an earth magic user... and as a researcher.

For someone like Marutsu, Rei's terrain manipulation skill had apparently been quite shocking in many ways.

He'd been acting in ways that could easily be described as stalking Rei since yesterday.

Fortunately for Rei, but unfortunately for Marutsu, Marutsu had been properly assigned his own designated area.

He couldn't very well leave his assigned area on the first day, so he approached Rei during breaks or after work ended...

"What about your assigned area?"

"Ah, I finished that last night."

"...Last night?"

Hearing that matter-of-fact statement, Rei looked at Marutsu again.

It wasn't particularly strange that his robe was the same as yesterday's, but the fatigue visible in his body wasn't just Rei's imagination.

"You... have you been here since yesterday?"

"No, I had some errands to run, so I stepped away for a bit..."

"No, you should have rested properly."

As expected... or perhaps even more than expected, Rei muttered weakly.

The fact that Marutsu showed no signs of magic power depletion even after working all night couldn't be explained away simply by him possessing higher magic power than ordinary humans.

Even if he possessed high magical power, it might be understandable if he had an enormous amount like Rei. But for an ordinary mage to use magic all night long and still be this energetic was a bit abnormal.

But the secret was about to be revealed.

"Hey! Who the hell tossed an empty potion bottle here?! And... this is a mana-restoring potion, isn't it?! What the hell, using it now when construction just started!"

The voices of the craftsmen echoed around them.

And Rei, Marina, and Feilma all fixed their gaze intently on Marutsu.

They understood. Who had used that potion.

"Marutsu... you..."

"Ah, I saw Rei coming, so I just..."

Rei looked at Marut, who somehow showed no sign of remorse whatsoever, and felt puzzled.

"What about that potion?"

"What do you mean, 'what happened to it'?"

"Wasn't that the one prepared by Gilm for use in this construction project?"

If that was the case, he'd be in for a lot of trouble.

As Rei continued speaking, Marut shook his head to deny it.

"No, no. Of course this is our own stuff. We prepared it ourselves when we took this job."

"...Hey, I know it's a bit late to ask, but why has your tone changed since the beginning?"

"My tone, you say? Well, I suppose it's because I'm eager to learn more from you, Rei. It wouldn't be right to speak arrogantly to someone I'm seeking guidance from, would it?"

Hearing this, Rei was slightly surprised.

Not just that he'd change his tone to seek guidance from someone younger than himself, but that he'd thought it through so thoroughly and actually acted on it.

Considering that this showed just how serious Marutsu was, and with the matter of the potion to restore magic power, there was no choice but to accept.

If this person had been someone who acted arrogantly, Rei could have easily cut them off—not in a physical sense, of course.

"Ah... well. Are you really done with your work, Marutsu?"

"Yes. There's no doubt about that."

"...Alright. Well, I don't have anything specific to teach you, but if you want to come along, that's fine too."

"Really?! Thank you so much!"

Bowing deeply to Rei, Marutsu immediately walked away, presumably to prepare something.

"Is that okay?"

Marina asked Rei, but Rei nodded, indicating it was fine.

"He's not teaching me any secrets or anything. I'm just watching, so it should be fine."

"Is that so?"

"Yeah. I don't plan on doing anything. ...If they figure something out from watching me, that's fine too."

Feirma, who had been listening to Rei and Marina's conversation, looked slightly surprised as she turned to Rei.

"Huh, you're surprisingly tolerant. I thought you'd be much stricter."

"...Yeah, if they'd come at me more aggressively, I'd have responded accordingly. But since they're acting like that, I'm just going to let them see it. I don't mind."

As Rei, Marina, and Feilma talked like this, Marutsu, who had apparently finished his business, approached Rei and the others.

"Sorry to keep you waiting."

"No, it didn't take that long, don't worry about it. ...Alright, I'll take Marutsu with me. You've got a lot on your plate today too, but hang in there."

Being the two most skilled in spirit magic, even if not quite at Rei's level, they were expected to lay solid groundwork as part of the wall's preliminary processing.

In fact, compared to Rei, who was restricted to one meter, Marina, who had no such limitation, could be said to be expected to do even more in some respects.

Though, due to magic power limitations, she inevitably has a usage cap unlike Rei.

"Yeah, you too."

After exchanging a few light words, Rei, Marutsu, Marina, and Feilma each headed to their assigned locations.

"Well then, shall we go?"

Marutsu said to Rei, who nodded in agreement and headed toward the spot where he had last used his terrain manipulation skill yesterday.

"Hey, you're here! Been waiting for you!"

The craftsman man in charge of this area greeted Rei cheerfully upon seeing him.

But upon seeing Marutsu standing behind Rei, he wore a puzzled expression and spoke up.

"Hey, wait a minute. What's up with that old guy over there? I thought he was a mage, right?"

Marutsu was the oldest among these earth mages, which was likely why the craftsman remembered him.

"Ah, he finished today's work, so he's tagging along with me. ...He said his work was done anyway, so it should be fine, right?"

"Hmm... Well, if he's done with his work, I guess I can't really say anything about it."

If the work is done, then. The craftsman said no more.

If it turned out to be just talk and the work wasn't actually done, Marutsu would likely face some kind of penalty later.

(Though...)

Watching the craftsman, Rei turned his gaze to Marutsu beside him.

He thought it unlikely he'd do such a thing... yet, knowing Marutsu's extraordinary curiosity about himself and the Death Scythe, it wouldn't be surprising if he did.

"Anyway, I understand that part. In any case, I know it's sudden, but please start the work right away. It looks like it'll be another hot day today, so I want to get as much done as possible while it's still cool in the morning."

"Got it, let's get started. ...Just to confirm, we need to lower the excavated area over there, right?"

Looking at the spot where the ground had been dug out quite deeply—about fifty centimeters—probably by magic yesterday, Rei asked.

"Yeah. That'd be a big help. Once that's done, we'll have our guys take over and finish it. ...Still, it's a pain that such a powerful spell can't move that spot any further once it's used."

"Yeah, I guess so."

Responding, Rei drew the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring.
