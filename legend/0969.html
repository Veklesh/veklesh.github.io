After leaving Gilm, Rei and the others first moved toward the forest following Dietz's lead while discussing their next course of action.

Vihera's horsemanship was exceptionally skilled; despite having Byune riding in front of him, there was not a hint of instability.

Deets wasn't as skilled a rider as Vihera, but he still didn't fall from the galloping horse.

During the civil war, when selecting members for the guerrilla unit, a certain level of horsemanship was required for swift movement. Deitz was among those gathered for that reason, so it was only natural.

Vihera and Deets each led a spare horse for emergencies, and alongside them, Set rode with Rei on his horse.

These were well-trained horses; seeing Set did not frighten them or hinder their movement.

"So, what's the plan now? If you tell me the location, I can ride Set ahead."

This was precisely what Set, capable of flight, could do. Pinpointing Set and Rei as combat assets should have been an extremely effective strategy against five Rank C monsters like the Cyclops.

But as he urged the horse on, Deets shook his head.

"Considering Captain Rei's strength, I'd love to do that... but the place is a bit hard to find. We've been moving through the forest since yesterday, staying overnight."

"Hard to find? Me and Set go into that forest pretty often. Unless something crazy happens, it shouldn't be a problem."

What flashed through Rei's mind was that time long ago when a Bestir Empire operative used a transfer stone, and only Set had been transported.

Set had been teleported deep into a cave in the forest, and Rei had followed him here.

Fortunately, they'd met up with the Scorching Wind back then, so Set hadn't been left alone...

Remembering that incident, Rei gently stroked the neck of the Set before him.

Even now, remembering the moment Set vanished before his eyes sent a chill down his spine.

"Guru?"

What's wrong? Set looked back while running. Rei shook his head, signaling it was nothing, then spoke to Deets.

"Besides, even though we've gotten used to being together, Set is a griffin. His senses are far sharper than any ordinary animal's. He can sense magic too, so he'd know if Surunin of the Scorching Wind used magic."

"Come to think of it, Set is a griffin, right? I'd completely..."

The rest of that sentence was probably better left unsaid.

Set growled slightly, seeming a bit annoyed, and Deets hurriedly bowed his head.

"Ah, sorry. ...So, if Set's abilities can locate Johanna and the others, could you go ahead? Five Cyclopes? That's no joke. If Rei's squad... Rei and Set go, we'd be safer even if the Cyclopes attack."

"Rei, you know fire magic is off-limits, right? In winter, the snow might have helped prevent wildfires, but considering the season now..."

Rei nodded in agreement as Vihera relayed this while riding her horse.

It was true that spring had arrived, the snow had melted, and fires spread more easily.

In such conditions, what would happen if Rei used her signature fire magic in the forest was obvious.

"I know. Even if I use magic, I won't use anything with a wide-area effect. Something with a narrow range is fine, though."

"...I think it'd be best to avoid even those with a narrow range, if possible. There's a real chance the cyclops could move around and cause the flames to spread."

"I'll keep that in mind. Anyway, Set and I will go ahead. Vihera and Byune, follow Deitz's lead."

With that, Rei tapped Set lightly on the back without waiting for a reply.

It was precisely because he knew this was a matter of urgency.

Deets and Vihera, aware of this, watched them go without comment. Byune, held in Vihera's arms, waved a small hand as she saw Set and Rei off.

"...Is that really okay?"

Watching Set's figure soar high into the sky, Deets murmured.

Hearing only those words, one might think Dietz was worried about whether Rei had the strength to fight a Cyclops. But Dietz felt no such anxiety on that point.

As a former member of the Roving Squad, having witnessed his abilities up close, he had no doubts whatsoever.

However, the unease Dietz felt now wasn't about Rei's strength. It was purely the fear that his comrades might be spotted by the five Cyclopes.

No matter how strong Rei was, it meant nothing if they weren't even present for the fight.

"Don't worry."

As Set and Rei vanished from sight, a voice suddenly called out over Deets's shoulder as he rode his horse at full speed toward their destination.

There was no need to wonder whose voice it was.

Besides himself, Byune, and Vihera, there was no one else present at that moment.

"Considering Set's flight speed, he should reach us without any trouble. And while I don't know about those 'Scorching Wind' people, the rest are the guerrilla fighters from the civil war, right? Trust your comrades."

"...Yeah, you're right. There's no point in me freaking out here anyway."

To be honest, it wasn't like he could relax immediately.

But it was undeniably true that fretting now wouldn't change anything.

And panic easily leads to mistakes, ultimately wasting precious time.

Considering that, fretting now wasn't just pointless—it could directly endanger my comrades.

"Alright, I'll pick up the pace a bit more. ...Sorry, but hang in there just a little longer."

He directed the latter part of his words to the horse he rode, gently stroking its neck.

Perhaps pleased by this, Deets' horse let out a neigh and picked up speed even more.

"Why on earth did a group of Cyclopes come all the way near Gilm?"

Mireine murmured softly as she watched the Cyclopes gathered about fifty meters ahead in the forest.

The cyclops in her line of sight tore apart a deer it had killed with a single blow to the head using a club, then sank its teeth into the thigh without even bothering to remove the hair.

Blood, hair, and bits of flesh clung to its mouth, but the cyclops, ravenously devouring the raw meat, seemed utterly unconcerned.

No, rather, it seemed to suggest that this was the finest spice, as it licked the area around its mouth clean with its tongue.

The deer had been a decent size, but even so, five Cyclopes devoured it in no time.

After devouring one leg, the Cyclops seemed to realize there was almost nothing left to eat. It reached for the only remaining part—the deer's head—and carelessly tossed it into its mouth, crushing it with its sturdy fangs.

Seeing the deer vanish in mere minutes, Mireine frowned slightly.

Of course, Mireine ate venison too—she might even call it a favorite.

But that was properly prepared and cooked venison.

To want to eat it raw... and worse, to want to tear the carcass apart by hand like the Cyclops had done? She couldn't imagine it.

"Well, I wonder why. Considering the season, it's likely that it wandered into this area hungry because there was no food in winter, and it was only found now..."

Surnin, who had been observing the Cyclops alongside Mireine, muttered while holding his staff.

Even though they were about fifty meters away from the Cyclops, they were tense, probably because they didn't know when they might be discovered. There was no softness in his voice.

"Still, it's interesting how they eat deer raw, yet they're smart enough to pull up trees to make their own living space more comfortable."

Mireine nodded in response to Ekril's muttered words from within the thicket.

"Eating aside, they've got enough sense to properly prepare the place they live in. ...Which is a pain."

"And we've been eating dried meat and such since yesterday to avoid using fire..."

"...Do you want to eat raw deer meat, Ekril? Untreated meat, at that?"

"Um... yeah, yeah. We really want to get back to Deets as soon as possible, but what do you think?"

Though they exchanged words while shifting their gaze evasively, they took care not to shake the bushes they were hiding in and make careless noises—a testament to the C-rank party's professionalism.

Mireine shook her head at Ecuril's words.

"We'll arrive at Gilm, seek reinforcements at the guild, and then return. ...It won't be possible to come back immediately."

"...If only Set wasn't involved, he'd be so competent..."

"Did you say something?"

"No, nothing."

The words she'd meant to mumble under her breath seemed to have reached Mireine clearly.

Ecuril, caught by her gaze and asked, hastily shook her head.

"Calm down, both of you. ...More importantly, it's almost time for the shift change. Look."

At Surnin's words, they turned their gaze behind them. There, Johanna—Mireine's natural enemy, not in the sense of an unbeatable opponent, but as an adversary destined by the heavens—was approaching them, leading three other men.

That four people could move almost silently spoke to Johanna's skill.

Mireine's group of three, Johanna's group of four arriving now, plus the two others and Deitz guarding the horses and carriage in the rear while watching for other monsters approaching—ten people total had come for this Cyclops extermination mission.

Originally, only one cyclops had been sighted. Not only should they not have needed this many people, but Mireine's group alone, with their Scorching Wind, should have been more than enough to defeat it.

However, upon entering the forest to search for the Cyclops, they discovered the carcasses of wild animals, including bears, as well as the remains of Orcs.

That in itself wasn't a problem, but upon examining the partially eaten corpses, they found not just one set of teeth marks, but several different kinds.

Realizing there was more than one cyclops, Mireine decided then and there to send Deitz to Gilm for reinforcements.

Normally, given the significant discrepancy from the information they had been given beforehand, they could have simply returned to Gilm as a group.

However, this forest was not far from Gilm, and knowing that five Cyclopes were present in such a location meant they couldn't simply ignore it.

Additionally, the fact that it was just spring and funds were tight, coupled with the fact that they had spent the night moving through the forest, likely played a role.

Monsters like Cyclopes were worth a decent sum even if they only defeated one.

If they could defeat all five, it would mean a substantial income, especially considering the request for reinforcements.

Driven by both duty and greed, Mireine, Johanna, and the others kept watch on the Cyclops, observing them while preparing to strike swiftly once reinforcements arrived.

"So, what's the situation over there?"

Johanna asked in a low voice. Mireine shifted her gaze to one of the Cyclopes.

It was the one that had just killed a deer with a single swing of its club.

"The cyclops holding that club—its shoulder twitches just before it attacks. That tell makes dodging their strikes pretty straightforward."

Though Mireine and Johanna often fought over Set, they weren't doing anything foolish like that now.

Partly because they knew how to separate work and personal life, but more importantly, they were facing five Cyclopes. If they started bickering now, they'd pay for it with their lives.

Neither Mireine nor Johanna wanted that to happen.

"Huh. Easy-to-read opponents like that are nice to fight. ...Anyway, I'll take over watch duty. You two rest up. Mireine aside, Surunin-san over there is a valuable mage. If push comes to shove, we'll need her as our main force."

"I know that. Anyway... wait a second."

Mireine tried to retort to Johanna, but suddenly noticed something odd about the five Cyclopses in her line of sight.

Just moments ago, they'd been savoring the aftermath of forcibly dismembering and devouring a deer with brute strength, yet now they'd stopped moving.

Their gaze shifted toward an unexpected direction—exactly opposite where Mireine and Johanna were hiding...

A sudden, unpleasant feeling flashed through Mireine's mind, and even though they were about fifty meters away, she held her breath more tightly than before.

Seeing Mireine like this, the others must have sensed something too. Following her lead, they blended into the surrounding trees with even greater caution.

And then... the next instant, emerging from the forest was a Cyclops. Unlike the others' green skin, this one had red skin and was larger than usual.
