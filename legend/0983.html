Rei and Pamidol arrived at Asimov's workshop and knocked on the door before them.

As usual, this was a place with few people around, so the sound of their knocking echoed throughout the area as if to say, "Here I am!"

But there was no sign of the door opening.

"...Damn it, again. Is he engrossed in his research, or just sleeping? Either way, we're not getting anywhere like this. Rei, break down the door."

"Huh?"

Rei turned to look at Pamidor, wondering what he was suddenly talking about, but there was no trace of a joke in his tone.

He was absolutely serious about demanding the door be broken down.

"No, that's not okay. This is someone's home, after all! If the guards or someone saw us destroying this door, we'd get arrested."

"It'll be fine. This isn't the first time I've broken down this door."

His tone implied he'd broken down doors several times before.

"I did get caught by a guard once before, but they helped me out."

"...Really? A guard helped you break down a door?"

"Yeah. ...Well, back then, there'd have been all sorts of problems if we didn't break the door and get inside."

He gently averted his gaze and stared into the distance, his expression radiating an aura that made it impossible to ask what had happened.

Though they'd only met once, Rei knew something of Azimov's character.

Given that, it was easy to guess something terrible had happened.

"Anyway. After that incident, no one said anything anymore when they saw me breaking Azimov's door. ...Of course, it's not like anyone can do it. It's limited to me and a few others. So, get to it."

Rei, meeting his gaze, sighed, realizing he had no choice but to do it.

"Grr?"

Seeing Rei like that, Set purred and turned his gaze toward him.

His round eyes conveyed nothing but genuine concern for Rei.

"Don't worry. It's nothing."

Gently stroking Set's head, Rei placed his hand on the door... and forcibly pulled himself away from the house.

The sound of wood being forcibly split echoed around them, but there was hardly anyone nearby.

Even when someone passed by occasionally, those who knew it was Pamidor simply accepted it with a resigned "Oh, him again..." Those who didn't know him quickly moved on, wanting nothing to do with the intimidating Pamidor.

Naturally, some knew Set, and a few knew Rei.

But more than that, those moving about this area knew this was Azimov's home and workshop, so their desire to avoid involvement was likely even stronger.

"Hey, Azimov! You there?! Hope you haven't kicked the bucket or anything!"

Watching Pamidor enter through the open door, Rei spoke while stroking Set's head.

"Alright, Pamidor and I are going inside. Keep watch here for anyone coming. If the door stays open, some weirdo might wander in."

"Grr!"

Hearing Set's response, Rei left him sprawled in front of the house and followed Pamidor inside. As he did, he suddenly felt his words to Set had sounded a bit criminal.

(Nah, it's just my imagination. Besides, no one heard me, so I'm safe for now.)

Making excuses to himself, he proceeded through the building and arrived at the room he'd been shown before. There, Pamidor was staring at Azimov's back with an exasperated look.

Despite having intruders in his house, Azimov showed no sign of noticing the two of them.

And what was he doing, seemingly oblivious? To Rei's eyes, it looked like nothing more than a suspicious experiment.

In reality, it wasn't particularly suspicious; he was conducting an experiment related to alchemy. But to Rei's eyes, it looked like nothing other than a suspicious experiment.

Pamidor's fist struck the back of Asimov's head.

Not that she was hitting him with full force.

Pamidor's muscles, honed by years of blacksmithing, were incredibly strong.

Compared to a novice adventurer fresh out of training, his attack power was overwhelmingly superior.

Azimov, too engrossed in his experiment to even notice Rei and Pamidor behind him, finally registered their presence when a fist came crashing down on the back of his head.

"Ow! Who's there?!"

"It's me, me. And Rei. ...Seriously, focusing on your experiments is fine, but notice when someone comes to visit."

"...Oh, it's you, Pamidor. And Rei too. Unfortunately, the spear isn't ready yet. It's progressing smoothly, but transforming a broken Magic Sword into a spear isn't something you can pull off just like that."

When he turned around and saw Pamidor and Rei, especially Rei, Azimov immediately thought he was about to get a complaint.

Then again, only about a winter had passed. If it were some job where cutting corners was acceptable, that might be one thing. But this time, reforging a Magic Sword... specifically, a Magic Sword wielded by a top-tier master, into a spear? That was an incredibly difficult task. He figured they'd understand that.

Especially since the Magic Sword's blade was broken.

While it might be obvious to anyone knowledgeable in alchemy or blacksmithing, he didn't know if Rei possessed that level of expertise. That's precisely why he'd expected complaints...

"I see. That's a relief."

"What?"

Caught completely off guard, a rare look of confusion flashed across Azimov's face.

It was only natural. While he'd been scolded for delays before, he'd rarely received words of gratitude for it.

That's precisely why Azimov questioned Rei's words.

"What do you mean?"

"Actually, recently... or should I say today. I got my hands on a little magic item. And since it has some pretty good abilities, I was wondering if it might be possible to incorporate it into the spear."

"...Hmm."

His expression changed completely from just seconds ago, and Asimov wore a look of genuine interest.

"That's quite intriguing. Might I ask what kind of magic item it is?"

"Yeah. That's why I came here—I wanted you to see it too. When I showed it to Pamidor, he probably said it was impossible."

"What?"

Azimov turned his gaze toward Pamidor at Rei's words.

His eyes reflected a look that said, What kind of nonsense is he spouting?

But Pamidor, meeting that gaze, shook his head.

He knew that no matter what he said, they wouldn't understand until they actually saw that hammer.

(A small magic item like an ornament could be incorporated into a spear, but not that hammer...)

After all, ordinary humans couldn't wield a hammer like that.

Preempting Azimov, who seemed about to speak, Pamidor opened his mouth.

"Before you say anything to me, take a look at the magic item Rei actually obtained. If you decide you can incorporate it into a spear after seeing it, then I won't say a word."

"...Alright. Rei, please."

"Ah... I appreciate you saying that, but it's a bit impossible here. We need a wider space."

"A wider space?"

As Azimov asked, Pamidor nodded beside Rei and spoke up.

"Yeah, Rei's right. It's too cramped here. ...I think this house has a backyard, right? Let's go there."

"Huh? Wait, what does that mean?"

"Just come on. It's quicker to see for yourself than listen to explanations."

At Pamidor's words, Azimov reluctantly nodded, stood up, and left the room.

(Honestly, if that magic item Rei brought turns out to be worthless, I won't stand for it.)

He harbored this dissatisfaction inwardly.

Still, this was Rei, the one who possessed the Noiz Magic Sword. It was easy to imagine that whatever magic item he'd brought would be something equally formidable.

"Backyard, backyard, huh... Yeah, seeing this, the term 'backyard' seems like the perfect description."

The backyard of Azimov's house.

Frankly, calling it a backyard felt like a stretch given the scene before him.

Spring was certainly a factor, but the grass was overgrown, and several tree branches lay scattered on the ground, likely blown in from somewhere during the winter snowfall.

It was exactly the image of a backyard Rei had in her mind, so she had no complaints, but she did have a few thoughts.

But Asimov ignored it, turning his gaze to Rei and speaking.

"Look, we've come this far, so enough already. Just show me that magic item of yours."

Rei nodded at Azimov's words and pulled the hammer from the Misty Ring, just as he had done when retrieving it from Pamidor's shop—or rather, workshop.

"Whoa! ...Now that's... huge!"

The hammer Rei pulled out was unexpectedly large, causing a gasp of astonishment to escape Azimov's mouth.

Pamidor, observing Azimov's reaction, wore a faintly sympathetic smile.

After all, Pamidor had been startled when she first saw this hammer, so she was probably glad to find someone sharing a similar reaction.

"Yeah. As you can see, it's huge. We took it from a rare or higher-tier Cyclops species after defeating it."

After stating this, Rei proceeded to explain the hammer's abilities to Azimov.

The mention of lightning drew Azimov's interest. Hearing it granted its wielder powerful regenerative abilities, he stared intently at the hammer. When told it took root within the user's body and stole their will, he frowned.

Though Ajimov was seen by those around him as a dangerous individual, his sensitivity to magic items was undeniably ordinary.

"So, what do you want to do with this hammer?"

"At first, I thought about melting part of this hammer or something and having it used in the spear I requested... but Pamidor said that was impossible. So, I thought maybe you could do it, Azimov, and brought it here."

"I see. Well, I can't say I don't understand why Pamidor would say that. But that's because he's thinking from a blacksmith's perspective. With my skills, it'll take some effort, but I can manage it."

At those casual words, Pamidor frowned and spoke up.

"Hey, you can't just say 'I tried and it didn't work'! This is a magic item, after all. Just because Rei finds it hard to use doesn't mean you can mess it up..."

"Hmph. That's the opinion of an ordinary person, I suppose. Well, leave that part to me."

As Azimov spoke with brimming confidence, Rei continued, as if seeking confirmation.

"You really can do it?"

"Yeah. ...But like I said before, it's gonna be a job that takes some time and effort. As payment, I want the unused portion of this hammer. Oh, and don't worry, just because I'm taking it doesn't mean I'll only use a little bit of the hammer for the magic item. I won't do that."

Hearing Azimov's words, Rei turned his gaze toward Pamidor.

His gaze asked, Is that true?

Pamidor, receiving that gaze, nodded back at Rei while wearing an expression of surprise at Azimov's words.

Though Azimov spoke with an air of superiority, Rei knew his talent in alchemy was genuine, and there was no deception there.

"Understood. Then I'll leave everything concerning that hammer entirely to you. Also, if there are any materials we're missing, just let me know. I'll gather them as best I can."

The words that came out of Rei's mouth were absolutely sincere.

With Set around, even if the materials were in a somewhat distant location, he could go get them immediately.

Rare materials often existed in remote, uninhabited areas, and obtaining them required the resolve to fight powerful monsters.

But for Rei, facing such monsters was actually a way to obtain rare magic stones, making it more like a dream come true.

(Come to think of it, I never absorbed that magic stone I got in the dungeon. With everything that happened, I completely forgot about it. ...Wait, can I even absorb a Cyclops' magic stone? If Set participates in battle, it should be possible to absorb it, but... Maybe I'll ask Vihera and the others about that when they get back.)

Rei was looking forward to learning some new skills, but suddenly, Azimov's mutterings reached her ears.

"Still, what on earth possessed an alchemist from the Bestir Empire to create such a magic item?"
