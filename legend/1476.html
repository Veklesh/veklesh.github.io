The day after asking Marina to use spirit magic so he could sense Ilse's danger in an emergency, Rei finished his usual tasks at the construction site as usual, then headed toward Treant Forest with Ilse.

"Aaaaaah! Wait, I'm falling! Falling! I'm falling, Rei-san!"

"Grrrrrr..."

Hearing the sound that was somewhere between a scream and a cry coming from beneath his feet—literally—Set turned his head and growled at Rei, who was riding on his back, asking if he was okay.

Rei stroked his neck to calm him down and opened his mouth.

"It's fine. Ilse's an adventurer too. Holding onto Set's leg for a few minutes won't be a problem."

"It is a problem! I said it is a problem! I may be an adventurer, but I have no confidence in my physical abilities!"

Even in her near-panic, Ilse must have heard Rei's voice. Clinging to Set's right front leg, she screamed as if abandoning her usual composure.

Yes, the only one Set could carry on his back and fly with was Rei.

To be precise, he could carry one or two children besides Rei, but carrying an adult besides Rei was impossible.

Gliding down from a high place... that might be possible, but Seto couldn't reach the altitude needed to glide with Ilse on his back, and there weren't any buildings in Gilm tall enough for that.

As a result, Ilse ended up flying by clinging to Set's front legs, just like Marina and Vihera had done... but Ilse herself wasn't particularly confident in her combat prowess—or more accurately, her physical abilities.

That's precisely why, as an adventurer, she'd primarily taken gathering requests rather than extermination ones.

So why was Ilse hanging onto Set? Simply put, traveling to Treant Forest by ground would take far too long.

Ilse could ride a horse, but even so, flying with Set was overwhelmingly faster than riding a horse on the ground.

Moreover, reaching Treant Forest would take only a few minutes at Set's speed, so it was just a matter of enduring that short time... Thus, Rei decided to carry Ilse on Set.

Ilse initially insisted on traveling separately from Rei, but the wind spirit attached to her had been instructed by Marina to draw magic power from Rei.

In other words, staying close to Rei meant the effects of the spirit magic Marina had used would last longer.

This attempt was a test to find a way to minimize the burden on Marina, and fortunately, it worked out well.

Admittedly, it was a rather tricky workaround that required two conditions: finding a spirit willing to heed Marina's request and that spirit being drawn to Rei's magic. Fortunately, it seemed to have worked this time.

In any case, that was the reason Ilse needed to stay as close to Rei as possible, and it was also why things were the way they were now.

Furthermore, while Marina or Vihera could easily hold onto his forelegs even at Set's flying speed, Ilse, lacking confidence in her physical abilities, couldn't manage it.

As a result, Set was flying considerably slower than usual.

(You'd think slowing down would mean more time clinging to Set's front legs while in the air. Well, if Ilse's okay with it, I've got no complaints.)

Thinking this, Rei looked around at the scenery.

Flying was nothing new, but today's altitude was low.

Usually they flew at around 100 meters, but now it was only about 50 meters—half the usual height.

This too was at Ilse's request...

(Considering what happens if you fall, whether it's 100 meters or 50 meters, it probably doesn't make much difference.)

Unless you have Sleipnir Boots like Rei or Elena, which let you use the air as a foothold, or can manage it with spirit magic like Marina, falling from fifty meters is almost certainly fatal.

(Well, maybe Vihera, having absorbed Ambris, might manage somehow... but anyway, in Ilse's case, I suspect she'd die even from a ten-meter fall, let alone fifty meters.)

Listening to Ilse's screams as she still clung to Set, Rei checked the Treant Forest ahead.

Thanks to the efforts of woodcutters and adventurers, the forest had thinned somewhat.

Even now, where Rei was looking, he could see a tree fall, likely cut down by a woodcutter.

From the way the tree fell, Rei could generally tell whether it had been cut by a woodcutter or an adventurer.

Of course, it was only a rough guess, not a certainty.

Trees felled by lumberjacks, likely due to their expertise, fell in a way that wouldn't obstruct paths.

They were also adjusted to avoid hitting other trees growing nearby.

Compared to that, the adventurer's felled trees were inevitably rougher around the edges.

They'd fall in places where people walked, or sometimes they'd hit nearby trees, breaking them.

"Eek! Let's get down quickly, Rei-san!"

For Ilse, getting back down to the ground quickly was clearly the priority, far more important than the sight of the felled tree.

Seeing Ilse's expression, which seemed to say nothing more than "I want to get down to the ground faster than that," even as she observed the path left by the Giganto Turtle's movement, Rei signaled to Set.

"Set, get down."

"Guruu!"

Maybe we should have sped up and gotten here sooner?

Seeing Ilse's state, Rei thought that again, but since Ilse herself had refused it, there was nothing to be done.

Besides, for Rei and Set, even going slowly didn't mean a huge time difference anyway. He decided not to dwell on that for now.

After all, they had arrived here in Treant Forest... to the place where Ajas, the one believed to be Ilse's killer, was.

Given that, neither Rei nor Ilse could remain in the same mood they'd had while flying through the sky.

...Though, right now, Ilse could only think about getting down to the ground as quickly as possible.

As Set descended toward the ground, Ilse let go at about a meter above the surface and landed...

"Yaaah!"

Unable to adjust her feet to the speed of her landing, she tumbled straight onto the ground.

Watching Ilse's predicament, Rei jumped off Set's back.

Her innate physical abilities were different, and perhaps because she'd jumped off Set's back countless times before, she landed as usual.

Set, having set both of them down, landed on the ground as well.

"Grr?"

Set turned on his heel, walked over to Ilse who had just stood up, and growled softly, "You okay?"

Ilse patted Set's head, assuring him she was fine, and began walking toward Rei.

Relieved that while she had fallen, she hadn't twisted her ankle or suffered any injury, she opened her mouth.

"...Let's go."

Ilse's expression as she said this was so serious it was hard to believe she had been screaming just moments before.

Of course. The place where her family's killer was believed to be was right there.

Rei nodded at Ilse's demeanor and led the one person and one beast toward Treant Forest.

At the edge of Treant Forest, several adventurers stood watch, likely wary of monsters.

Those adventurers stared curiously at Ilse, who was with Rei.

It was only natural, since she had flown through the sky on a griffin with Rei.

...Though in reality, she'd been screaming the entire time they were flying.

And when they landed on the ground, she ended up tumbling awkwardly instead of landing gracefully.

She certainly wasn't the type of adventurer who would travel with Rei, she thought... though the fact that she was quite beautiful probably played a part too.

"Good work. Keep it up."

I gave the adventurers a small bow. In response, they bowed back, not entirely displeased.

(Are these guys really going to be okay as escorts?)

Watching the exchange, Rei felt a twinge of unease.

But the fact remained that this group had made it this far without any major incidents. So, without saying anything more, Rei entered Treant Forest.

"Ah, Rei!"

One of the woodcutters spotted Rei and called out cheerfully, waving his hand.

While a few of the woodcutters disliked Rei, the majority were friendly toward him.

It was only natural. Thanks to Rei hauling away the felled timber, the woodcutters didn't have to worry about transporting it at all; they could just focus on cutting down the trees.

"How's the logging going?"

"Yes, we've got a decent amount. So, um... how about you?"

The woodcutter seemed curious about Ilse's presence and asked.

"My name is Ilse. Today, I'm accompanying you to observe Rei's work."

"Huh... Rei's? That's quite a demanding thing to be doing, isn't it?"

"Ahaha. Well, I suppose you could call it experience as an adventurer."

While Ilse and the woodcutter exchanged words, Rei stored the felled tree nearby into the Misty Ring.

"Ilse, we're heading deeper in."

"Ah, yes."

Ilse responded lightly to Rei's words, but if the woodcutter had been a bit more observant, he would have noticed her tension.

Though she smiled and chatted with the woodcutter, her hands were clenched tightly, her grip firm.

Ilse's strength wasn't particularly great, so it wasn't a problem... but if she had been stronger than average, it wouldn't have been surprising if her nails had torn through the skin of her palm and drawn blood.

"Calm down."

Precisely because he understood Ilse's state, Rei cautioned her as they walked side by side through the forest.

"Ah, ahahaha. I know... I know. But I just can't help it..."

She was about to face her family's enemy.

Of course, attacking here and now was out of the question.

But still...

With that thought, Ilse walked through Treant Forest alongside Rei.

Along the way, they encountered several woodcutters and adventurers. Ilse reacted momentarily each time, but upon realizing they were different people, she immediately smiled and greeted them.

And so they walked through Treant Forest for about ten minutes...

"Hey? Rei? You came again today?"

A voice called out.

Ilse stopped in her tracks at the voice.

The voice sounded familiar.

Father, mother, brother... Ilse had heard that voice when her family was killed.

It was from years ago... a voice that, under normal circumstances, might well have been buried in her memory. But Ilse could never forget the voice of her family's killer.

Unconsciously, she clenched her back teeth.

But seemingly oblivious to Ilse's state, Ajas, who had addressed Rei, continued speaking with a smile.

"Huh? Who's that woman over there? You've brought quite the beauty with you again, haven't you?"

"Ah. The guild asked me to let Ilse experience the work. That's why we're traveling together like this."

"Huh. ...Well, even if she watches Rei work, it's questionable whether ordinary adventurers could learn anything from it... Hm?"

Ajas paused there, staring intently at Ilse's face.

Ilse, too, stared back intently, though she avoided glaring at Ajas.

However, the emotions each harbored were entirely different.

"Hey, you... Ilse, was it? Haven't we met somewhere before? Somehow, I feel like I recognize you."

If asked normally, it wouldn't be strange for it to be taken as a pick-up line.

But Ilse, upon hearing it, struggled to suppress the urge to scream.

She couldn't let him know who she was right now... that she was the survivor from that time.

Compared to when her parents were killed, Ilse had grown considerably and seemed more mature, yet traces of her former self remained.

Ilse's family, who were traveling merchants, had been attacked and killed by Ajash, whom they had hired for escort during their journey.

Fortunately, Ilse had been playing in a flower field a short distance away when her parents were killed... or so the story went, so she had escaped death.

"Is that so? This is my first time meeting Mr. Ajas."

"...Hm? Oh? Hmm... Is that so? Did I get that wrong?"

"I think so?"

Suppressing the vengeance burning within her, Ilse exchanged words with Ajas, who wore a puzzled expression.
