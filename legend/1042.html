Emerging from the bushes was an old man. And for Rei ... and for the others as well, he was a familiar face.

Speaking for Rei and the others, Marina addressed him.

"Grandfather!?"

Yes, the one who pushed aside the bushes and appeared was Marina's grandfather, the elder of the dark elf settlement in this forest... Optis, who served as something like the village chief.

But as Rei looked at Optis, he felt a twinge of doubt.

Rei had spoken with Optis yesterday, which was precisely why he understood that the voice calling out to Polo earlier was not Optis's.

Marina likely felt the same.

After all, she had known Optis longer than Rei did, so it was only natural.

That's precisely why Marina stepped out of the bushes and asked Optis.

"Grandfather, that voice earlier wasn't yours, was it? Whose voice was it?"

"Marina? You did well with the World Tree matter. Even from afar, I could see the World Tree regaining its strength. Now then, about that voice earlier. Allow me to introduce you. This is Earth."

Along with Optis' voice, a man appeared from behind him.

In appearance, he looked to be in his late teens, slightly older than Rei.

He was about a head taller than Rei, and compared to Rei's feminine features, he had a rugged... masculine face.

His brown hair, as if made from the earth itself, was striking.

He wore a chest guard made from monster hide, likely for ease of movement.

The choice of a chest guard over full leather armor likely prioritized greater mobility.

He carried a bow and quiver on his back, likely magic items, and a sheath holding a dagger at his waist.

Overall, his equipment emphasized lightness, clearly indicating a combat style that relied on swiftness.

"Huh!? Old man Optis, these people..."

The man called Earth saw Rei and the others... or rather, upon catching sight of Set standing beside Rei, he gasped for a moment.

But the fact that he didn't seem flustered by Set's presence spoke to the man's composure.

"Hmm, I did tell you, didn't I? This is my granddaughter, Marina, and her husband, Rei. This is Earth. He's an adventurer who came to investigate the forest's disturbance. We just happened to meet him in the woods. At first, I thought he was an enemy, but after talking, he turned out to be a decent fellow."

"Grandfather..."

Marina's gaze, filled with exasperation, fell upon the staff Optis cherished.

After confirming there was no blood on it, Marina exhaled in relief before calling out to Earth, who was captivated by Set.

"It seems Grandfather was taken care of by you... Earth, was it? Is that carbuncle your familiar?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah, that's right. Honestly, I thought you'd disappeared, but you were just hanging out with someone else in a place like this?"

"Pororo!"

At Earth's words, Pororo let out a cheerful cry.

For Polo, talking with monsters like Set and Yellow must have been incredibly enjoyable.

Seeing Poloro like that, Earth gave a resigned smile and bowed his head to Marina.

"Seems my Polo's gotten a bit attached..."

Earth addressed Marina without a hint of hesitation, causing several people nearby to show expressions of surprise.

Rei, in particular, couldn't hide his astonishment at Earth's demeanor.

Marina was undeniably a stunning beauty, the kind of person you wouldn't casually approach.

Well, maybe with effort one could manage it, but feeling nervous was still the norm.

But... right now, under Rei's gaze, Earth was conversing with Marina without any particular air of self-consciousness.

(Could it be he's used to being around beauties like Marina, Elena, and Vihera? If so, maybe he's just accustomed to it...)

That's what Rei was thinking, but when Earth suddenly turned his gaze toward him, Rei had to abandon that line of thought.

Not only did he look at Rei, but the next moment, he started walking straight toward Rei.

"Hey, you're Rei... Crimson, right?"

"...Yeah."

Compared to when he'd spoken with Optis and Marina, Rei answered curtly, his tone slightly rough.

Even so, beneath the Dragon Robe, he remained alert, ready to activate Nebula's eyes at any moment.

With Optis present, it was certain he wasn't an enemy, but she couldn't fully trust a near-stranger.

Whether sensing Rei's tension or simply speaking at his own pace, Earth reached out his hand toward Rei as if offering a handshake.

"I'm Earth. Rank C adventurer. Meeting the legendary Crimson is a pleasure for me too."

"I'm Rei. A Rank B adventurer belonging to Gilm. And you're Set, right?"

"Guruu? Grrrr!"

At Rei's call, Set approached Earth, purring happily.

Normally, a griffin approaching would be nothing but terrifying.

...But that was different for Gilm's residents. That was simply because Gilm was too unique.

"Huh? What is it? What's wrong? Come on, come on."

Earth showed no sign of fear toward Set, reaching out to stroke his head.

"Grr, grrrrr!"

Set rubbed his face against Earth's, radiating an air of pure bliss.

His completely relaxed demeanor surprised even Rei.

Rei had seen Set become attached to people many times before.

Set was naturally affectionate and fundamentally wouldn't neglect anyone who showed him kindness.

Given that, such a scene shouldn't be unusual... but to Rei's eyes, it was vastly different from the ordinary.

Even when it came to affection, there were degrees to it.

For instance, someone who lightly strokes his head versus someone who gives him food – inevitably, Set would prioritize the latter.

By that measure, the affection Set showed Earth right now was quite profound.

It was practically the same level as his affection for Mireine or Johanna.

As if Earth's body emitted a pleasant scent, the way he gently rubbed his face against it looked utterly relaxed to an observer.

(What? Could it be some kind of special magic item? Or is it just his constitution?)

While pondering this, Rei's gaze drifted toward the other monster present besides Polo and Set... Yellow.

But Yellow showed no particular reaction; he simply returned to Elena and let her stroke him silently.

Compared to Set, who was clearly attached to Earth, Yello's demeanor seemed distinctly different.

"There, there. How's that? Here? Does this spot feel good? See?"

At the end of Rei's gaze, Earth smiled as he stroked Set.

Watching that playful interaction, it was hard to believe Earth was using some magic item to charm Set...

Uncharacteristically unsure of what to do, Rei stood there. Seeing his hesitation, Marina addressed Earth.

"Is it about time? We're near a Dark Elf settlement, after all. You said you came to investigate the forest's disturbance, but could you tell us a bit more? Of course, since Grandfather brought you here, I doubt you have anything to hide. ...Ah, yes. I forgot to introduce myself."

Pausing briefly, Marina smiled faintly and spoke.

"I'm Marina. Marina Ariansa. That gentleman over there... I'm the granddaughter of the elder of the Dark Elf settlement in this forest."

"Huh? The elder!?"

Earth, who hadn't heard about this from Optis, stared at him in utter shock.

Seeing this, Marina concluded that Optis and Earth must be meeting for the first time.

"Yes, the elder. And by the way, I'm the guildmaster of the Gilm Adventurers' Guild."

"What!?"

Marina's words were apparently unexpected too. Earth stopped petting Set completely and turned his gaze to the others around him.

"So, so, could it be that the others are important figures too?"

"Well, yes. Over there is Elena Kerebel. ...You might know her as the Princess General."

Earth turned his gaze toward Elena, already too stunned to speak.

Earth's features were sharp enough to be called ruggedly handsome, but seeing him like this, eyes wide with astonishment, revealed a face that was somehow endearing.

(This kid's interesting. Earth... Come to think of it, about five years ago, there was an adventurer named Earth who conquered some dungeon. Oh yes, they said he was a tamer, and seeing him with that rare type of carbuncle called Polo, there's no doubt about it.)

As I spoke with Earth, I recalled hearing that name at a guildmasters' meeting before.

"Um, so... could it be that the beauty over there is also famous?"

Earth could meet Elena and Marina's gaze head-on without looking away, but he couldn't bring himself to look directly at Vihera.

The reason, of course, was the sheer garment Vihera wore.

Such sheer garments, almost transparent, were certainly not the sort of attire an adventurer would wear.

It was the sort of thing worn by those in professions like prostitutes or dancers, and on top of that, Vihera possessed the most stunning beauty Earth had ever laid eyes on.

Earth's limited experience with women made it impossible for him to look directly at such a beautiful woman in such a revealing state.

(I never thought anyone could be more beautiful than Liv.)

The image of a woman who was more than a friend but less than a lover—someone he was quite close to—flew through his mind.

Suddenly, he imagined Liv giving him that familiar, unchanging expression... yet the kind of knowing, knowing look only someone close to her could give. Earth shook his head frantically, trying to shake off the guilty thought.

Marina could easily see through Earth's actions. She smiled slightly before speaking.

"Well, yes. There's a lot to it, so I can't say exactly, but it's certain he's an important person."

"...Anyone else?"

"Well, I wonder. Maybe. Maybe not. ...Anyway, that aside. You said you came to investigate the forest's disturbance. Whose request was it? We're supposed to basically leave these woods alone."

"It was a request from a merchant from Cross."

"...I see. A merchant from Cross. I have a pretty good idea who that is. That makes sense, then."

Cross is a village not too far from this forest.

Marina understood why it was a merchant from that village because the dark elf settlement did conduct trade with Cross merchants, albeit on a small scale.

The lord ruling Cross probably wanted to trade more extensively with the dark elves, but the dark elves themselves preferred to avoid contact with humans as much as possible.

That's why the trade remained small-scale.

Given that the previous lord of Cross had attempted to raid the dark elf settlement, suffered heavy damage in the ensuing counterattack, and ultimately fell from power—with the current lord's family arriving afterward—the present lord refrained from any forceful actions.

That was precisely why they provided Cross with generous protection, but never resorted to coercion.

The dispatch of Earth this time was part of that policy.

(Though technically, it's more like paying the reward on their behalf, right?)

Marina surmised as much.

While some villagers in Cross do trade with the Dark Elves, it remains a meager endeavor.

If asked whether such villagers had enough savings to hire a Rank C adventurer, one would have to tilt their head in doubt.

In other words, it was highly likely that a portion... or perhaps the entire reward for investigating the forest's anomaly came from the lord.

"So, while we were investigating the forest, that guy Polo suddenly took off running... and when I chased after him, I ran into old man Optis."

"You're lucky you made it back alive,"

The one who said this was Rei, who had been suddenly attacked by Optis yesterday.

But at Rei's words, Earth tilted his head as if he didn't understand what Rei was talking about.

Seeing Earth's expression, Rei concluded he probably wouldn't start a fight, and shot a glare at Optis.

Optis, meeting Rei's glare, quietly averted his gaze.

Rei fixed her gaze on Optis, but Optis kept looking away.

"Sigh... Both of you, cut it out. We're heading back to the settlement for now. We found Grandpa, after all. ...Can Earth come too?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. I don't mind, but... is it okay to just leave those two alone?"

"It's fine. They're just playing around."

With that, Marina sighed and turned her gaze back toward the settlement.
