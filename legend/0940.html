After finishing their third-year S-class lessons and lunch, the break ended and afternoon classes began.

Though Rei normally had no afternoon classes, she was now inside the gymnasium alongside Glink.

Before Rei's eyes, the third-year Class B students were engaged in mock battles against adventurers.

However, their skill level couldn't be said to be high, even compared to the third-year S-class students.

(Well, it's only natural since S Class gathers the elite.)

As Rei muttered this to himself, one of the adventurers approached and spoke to him.

"Ah, so you're Crimson. I've heard about you. Who'd have thought someone would actually want to observe this class?"

Rei was slightly surprised by the words of the man, who was older than her, probably in his late teens.

Despite knowing his alias, Crimson, the man acted completely normal.

Most other adventurers present seemed to be avoiding any interaction with Rei, as if they were saying, "Better safe than sorry."

"So, what do you think of the class?"

"Well... first off, I'm surprised by how many adventurers there are. In my S-class lessons, it's just me against the whole class. Compared to that..."

He paused, glancing around the gymnasium once more.

There, adventurers were engaged in mock battles in various combinations: one-on-one with students, multiple adventurers against multiple students, and even one-against-many or many-against-one scenarios.

"It's refreshing to see so many different kinds of mock battles happening simultaneously."

"Ah, yeah. Though, this isn't just about the students' side; it's got to do with our circumstances too."

"Huh?"

When Rei looked at him, confused by the man's words, he opened his mouth with a wry smile.

"For famous adventurers with nicknames like you, it's one thing. But for us? We took this job because we need the money."

"...Oh."

That brief explanation made it clear to Rei why so many adventurers were serving as mock battle instructors.

(So, it also meant trying to save as many adventurers as possible who hadn't saved enough money to get through the winter. That way, the students could keep practicing mock battles without any waiting time.)

Continuing mock battles without breaks must be pretty grueling for the students too. But purely in terms of training efficiency, it seemed like a decent option.

(Though I imagine constant physical exertion alone increases the risk of injury, so moderate breaks are probably necessary.)

I pondered this while watching several students in the corner of the gym, kneeling on the floor and breathing heavily.

"Well, anyway, that's why the mock battles are happening. ...So, classes aside, what exactly did you come here for?"

"Well, how should I put it... there might be a bit of trouble brewing. It might not get resolved within the academy, but there's a chance it could. I thought I'd give you a heads-up in case you get dragged into it."

At those words, the adventurers nearby who had heard him—including the man who had been talking to Rei—all wore displeased expressions.

It was only natural. Nobody wants to get dragged into someone else's trouble.

Still, the reason they didn't complain was largely due to the infamous reputation of the nickname "Crimson."

While she herself found it somewhat irritating, the rumor had spread that Rei mercilessly crushed anyone she disliked.

The adventurers who took that rumor seriously probably figured they'd be in for a world of hurt if they complained to Rei here.

Amidst this, the male adventurer who had been talking to Rei finally spoke up.

"Ah... so basically, we're getting dragged into your mess, right?"

"Maybe. But you know..."

"Even if you call it a possibility, the fact you're here makes it almost certain, doesn't it?"

Rei nodded back at the man who looked at her for confirmation.

"Yeah. Probably... no, definitely, most likely... pretty much guaranteed. Of course, it's possible nothing actually happens."

"Hmm... Well then, since we're getting dragged into this, how about you grant me a small favor?"

"...Money?"

If a little money was all it took to get him to act, that would be perfect for Rei.

That was his thought as he asked, but the man silently shook his head.

His gaze was fixed on the adventurers and students engaged in the mock battle.

What the man meant to say... well, there was no need to ask again.

"Quite the peculiar hobby, isn't it?"

"Is that so? If I can spar with an adventurer with a nickname in a mock battle where there's no real danger to my life, I'd be willing to pay for it myself, wouldn't you agree?"

The man asked another adventurer nearby. The one asked seemed to share the opinion, though slightly hesitant, and nodded silently in response.

"So, what do you say? If you're up for it, would you spar with us too? Getting a spar in with Crimson? You could brag about it to your buddies."

"Yeah, yeah. I'm all for a mock battle too. Never thought I'd get to spar with an adventurer with a nickname. Bet those guys who turned down this job would be kicking themselves if they knew about this?"

Another adventurer nearby chimed in, and quite a few others agreed.

As he said, sparring with Rei was incredibly appealing to the adventurers.

After all, it wasn't something you could easily do with a renowned adventurer under normal circumstances.

Moreover, seeing Rei, some wondered if he truly possessed the strength befitting a renowned adventurer.

The number of those casting such looks of contempt or doubt was small, but precisely because of that, they stood out.

Then again, this sort of gaze wasn't unusual for Rei. He paid it little mind, turning his attention toward Glink.

When that gaze asked if a mock battle was acceptable, Glink hesitated slightly before nodding.

He knew that students outside the S-Class from third and fourth years had expressed interest in seeing just how powerful an adventurer with a nickname truly was. More than that, Glink himself wanted to witness the sight of Rei sparring with an adventurer, not a student.

"Understood. Watching Rei's mock battle alone should be educational. Is that alright?"

Glink's gaze fell upon the instructor supervising the third-year Class B mock battle.

The instructor, unable to resist the temptation of seeing Rei—a man of renown—fight, and genuinely believing the mock battle would benefit the students, smiled and nodded.

"Yeah, no problem. I'll get to see Rei's true strength with my own eyes too."

And so, somewhat swept along by the flow of events, Rei ended up participating in a mock battle against adventurers.

Of course, being a mock battle, Rei couldn't use his true weapon, the Death Scythe.

In his hand was the spear he'd wielded countless times in recent mock battles.

And the man held a claymore.

"...Hey, wait. Considering the weight of that weapon, doesn't it defeat the purpose of a mock battle if it doesn't even have a blade?"

"Hah! If you're going to say that, then your weapon's pretty much the same deal. It might be lighter than this claymore, but it's a mock battle spear wielded by someone with a nickname, you know? There's no way a normal person could block it."

The adventurers gathered around to watch the mock battle nodded in agreement at the man's words.

The students, however, looked slightly perplexed. It was probably because of Rei's appearance.

"Hey, are you sure this is okay? That Rei guy... I heard he's got a nickname too..."

"Hmm, probably. Look, all the adventurers have serious expressions. Like that guy over there."

"Oh, you're right. He's usually so unprofessional..."

"Plus, they say Crimson might be the second Rank S in the Mireana Kingdom? If she's that strong, she's definitely got it covered."

The students' gazes were fixed on the fox beastman female adventurer.

The ears sprouting from the top of her head twitched busily, clearly showing her intense curiosity about what kind of fight Rei would put on.

Even to the students' eyes, Rei didn't seem particularly strong.

He was short, and he didn't have any noticeable muscle definition.

In fact, it seemed like he'd be crushed in a fight against an adventurer like the one standing before him now.

...That said, some students had connections to third-year S-class students and had heard that Rei was what you might call deceptive in appearance.

Those who had heard such stories found themselves nodding in agreement at the sight of Rei, spear in hand for the mock battle, thinking "He really is deceiving in appearance."

But that perception was limited to the students. The adventurer facing Rei, and the other adventurers watching the fight nearby, were acutely aware that Rei was far from what he appeared to be.

The man facing Rei felt this most keenly.

Even though it was a mock battle spear, his own favored weapon, the claymore he was accustomed to wielding daily, felt incredibly heavy.

No, it probably wasn't actually that heavy, but just standing there facing Rei was rapidly draining his physical and mental strength.

And this was despite Rei showing no killing intent or fighting spirit, merely standing there with his spear poised.

"What's the matter? This is a mock battle, right? We won't get anywhere just standing here facing each other. If you're not coming to me, I'll come to you."

It wasn't quite a provocation, but it was an invitation.

Hearing this, the man judged that if he didn't make his move now, he'd lose without doing anything. He lunged forward in one swift motion.

"WAAAAAAAAAH!"

Pumping himself up, the man roared to drown out the fear within him. Wielding his claymore, he closed the distance to Rei.

Using his height advantage over Rei, he swung his claymore down with all his might in a single, simple strike.

Seeing this, Rei decided it was a perfect opportunity to try out the counter technique he'd been practicing lately. He kicked off the floor, spear in hand.

That speed must have been completely unexpected, even for the man preparing to swing his claymore.

Before he knew it, Rei was already within striking distance. Worse, he passed right beside him and headed toward his back... The instant Rei's movement stopped, he suddenly realized the spear tip wasn't coming from his left side where Rei had just dashed past, but was extended and thrust from behind his right side.

Had the spearhead been real instead of a training weapon, and had Rei even slightly intended it, his carotid artery would have been severed instantly.

And if it had been Rei's Death Scythe, moving while the blade was already at his throat would have sent his head flying.

"...I give up. Being taken down this completely leaves me no choice but to surrender. I've learned the hard way that the nickname 'Crimson' isn't just a rumor—her skill is the real deal."

"Did you doubt me?"

The man continued speaking, inwardly relieved as the spear moved away from his face, though Rei's words seemed to imply offense.

"Well, yeah. To be blunt, it'd be weird if anyone could tell you were a nickname-holder just by looking at you."

"...Even this country's Rank S adventurer is like that?"

The man gasped involuntarily at Rei's softly muttered words.

Yes, this S-rank adventurer of the Mireana Kingdom, Saldat the Skybow, was practically the polar opposite of what one would typically imagine when hearing the term "S-rank adventurer."

(Well, maybe I think that way because the first Rank S adventurer I ever met was Noiz.)

Perhaps in the Kingdom of Mireana, the mention of a Rank S adventurer conjures up an image of an elf with a lighthearted personality like Saldat.

For a moment, Rei thought that might be the case, but the man in question shook his head sideways, looking offended.

"Well, I get that she's strong, and it's true she's good at looking after people—women, at least. As for whether she's respected or not... I'd say she is."

"Huh? I heard Saldat was based in the capital. Does he come here often too?"

"Huh? Didn't you know? He's friends with the headmaster here. He drops by occasionally."

"Come to think of it..."

The exchange in the academy director's office flashed through Rei's mind.

It was true they seemed quite friendly, and frankly, they couldn't casually ask him to be their bodyguard without some level of familiarity.

"Oh! Are you done talking!? Next, please... please spar with me!"

As if she'd been waiting for the man's conversation to end, the female adventurer declared this to Rei.

No, it wasn't just her. Many other adventurers, and even students, were now directing expectant gazes toward Rei, hoping for a mock battle with him.

Seeing this chain of events, they must have understood that Rei possessed the skill worthy of his nickname.

What should I do? Rei turned to Glink for guidance, but all he got in return was a helpless shake of the head.

In the end, Rei sighed and resigned himself to participating in the mock battle until class ended.
