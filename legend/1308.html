The incident where Azimov was attacked and his shoes were stolen by a male named Srei Punil.

That's why only Murt, with his mediocre skills, reacted to Rei's anger.

Based on this incident alone, it's still unclear who was the primary target.

But if they pull a second or third stunt like this, it'll become clear whether it's Rei or Azoth Trading Company they're after.

(Though even if Azoth Company is the target... I won't let it slide that easily.)

Using me is unforgivable, but above all, the act of attacking Azimov itself is utterly unacceptable.

It's unclear what motivated them to pull this stunt, but I intended to give them a proper response.

"I suppose so. The reason this situation has become so complicated is likely because the adventurer involved is Rei."

Given that Rei commands the griffin Set and his reputation as the "Rei" is known even in neighboring countries, it wouldn't be surprising if he'd incurred grudges anywhere and everywhere.

Moreover, there are likely those harboring grudges without cause, and it's also true that Rei's personality tends to make enemies easily.

Considering all that, it was still impossible to clearly determine whether the hostile party was targeting Rei or the Azoth Trading Company.

"Set, can't you track the scent on the Sleipnir Boots any further?"

While the exact location where the Sleipnir Boots were hidden in the carriage remained unknown, if Set's sense of smell could pinpoint that spot, it might be possible to track the perpetrator's movements after they parted ways with the carriage.

"Grrr..."

If the same situation arose again, he'd probably do the same thing.

I'd asked Marina to take Azimov to a safe place before informing the guards.

Therefore, the guards who came here might know something about Azimov's condition.
