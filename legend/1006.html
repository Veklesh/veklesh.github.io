The order for advancing through the slums was Elena, Rei, Byune, Azimov, and Vihera from the front.

"It feels just like exploring a dungeon," was Vihera's observation.

Indeed, even with Elena leading the way, Rei, Elena, Vihera, and Byune could sense the slum dwellers watching them—not monsters, but still observing their every move.

Though Azimov had almost no combat experience, he still understood that the surrounding situation was becoming increasingly unsettling.

Walking through the slums like this, Vihera called out to Azimov ahead.

"If you weren't confident in combat, we could've just gone back to the magic item shop through that underground passage earlier. Why did you bother following us?"

"You're chasing an alchemist, right? Well, my knowledge might come in handy... That's the official line. The real reason is I'm interested in the magic items and materials that alchemist probably has."

"...You're quite honest. But if it's magic items and materials you want, I thought there were plenty in that shop earlier?"

What came out of Vihera's mouth was factual, but not necessarily the whole truth.

Knowing this... no, anticipating it, Azimov snorted and spoke.

"True, that shop and basement had various magic items and rare materials. But the truly rare magic items and materials? They're probably held by the alchemist we're heading to, right?"

"That's true... but since we've been asked to apprehend the alchemist, any materials or magic items he possesses will likely be handed over to Lord Daskar for the time being, right?"

After all, this whole incident started because someone was giving magic items to monsters.

Since it was an alchemist who was doing that, those magic items must hold some secret. I never thought Daskar would hand them over to someone else.

"...I understand."

Though he seemed to accept it verbally, Vihera didn't miss the hint of dissatisfaction in his gaze.

"Just so you know, unlike Rei, I've barely spoken to you. We haven't known each other long enough to build trust. If you start thinking strange thoughts, you'll end up regretting it."

As soon as she finished speaking, she channeled magic into her gauntlet. Instantly, long claws extended from it, whizzing past Azimov's face.

It was an obvious threat, but Azimov, far from being deterred, fixed his gaze on Vihera's gauntlet and murmured with apparent admiration.

"Huh, quite the piece of work. Creating claws with magic? Now that's rare. And seeing how perfectly it fits your arm, it's clearly not off-the-shelf—a magic item made directly by an alchemist, right?"

"...Yes."

It was incredibly difficult to deal with someone who was being threatened yet showed absolutely no awareness of it.

In fact, since she seemed pleased, threatening her held no meaning for Vihera either.

"For now, don't get any strange ideas. Not about the alchemist, and not about Yellow either."

She said this while watching Yellow flying right beside Elena, then focused solely on keeping watch over their surroundings.

"Still, there are some pretty hot-blooded bastards out there,"

Rei, walking behind Elena, muttered in exasperation.

Several slum dwellers had tried to attack, but each time, Rei had shot them a murderous glare to deter them.

Given that they were in the slums, a place where violence never ceased, the residents' reaction to the killing intent Rei emitted was swift.

They judged they had no chance of winning and gave up on attacking.

It was a situation where they couldn't bring themselves to attack such prime prey.

Emerging from the hidden passage, Rei briefly wondered if he should have used the same tactic on those who attacked him earlier. Yet, he somehow felt that even if he had projected his killing intent then, they still would have charged.

Among them, Rei sensed several had fled the scene. He concluded they must have sensed the difference in strength and run.

"Well, as long as they don't bother me, I couldn't care less!"

Mid-sentence, Rei reached out.

The stone hurled at his face never fulfilled its purpose, landing safely in Rei's palm.

"Hmph!"

With a sharp exhale, he flicked the stone from his palm toward the direction it had come from, using only a flick of his wrist.

Unlike when it had been thrown at Rei, the stone flew with a speed that seemed to slice through the air. It struck the torso of the person who had thrown it mere seconds before, shattering ribs.

He couldn't win in a fair fight. Given that, he'd likely chosen to attack from a distance, out of reach of Rei and the others' attacks... Unfortunately, against Rei, that choice was nothing but a bad move.

Then again, perhaps it was fortunate that he hadn't been subjected to Rei's signature spear-throwing.

"So, Elena. How long until we reach our destination? I don't want to keep moving while worrying about getting ambushed like this."

Probably because the ones who threw stones had been easily counterattacked. Those who had been thinking similarly stopped moving, creating a strange situation where they were now moving while the slum dwellers maintained a wide-ranging encirclement around Rei and the others.

It wasn't that the slum dwellers had given up on attacking Rei and the others; they were simply watching the situation unfold because, as things stood, they stood to suffer greater harm themselves.

This wasn't exactly giving up; it was more like biding their time, waiting for an opportunity.

Even so, the situation felt considerably safer than before, so Rei asked Elena. What came back, however, was the rare sight of Elena looking slightly perplexed.

"Yellow was tracking that woman from the air. That's precisely why he couldn't find her..."

"Ah, I see."

Upon hearing Elena's words, Vihera was the first to voice understanding.

"Things are different when you're on the ground versus in the air, right? Especially in a maze-like place like this slum."

"Exactly."

Rei also nodded in agreement at that explanation.

Whether it was the nature of the slum or not, the buildings here seemed to lack any sense of planning, constructed in a chaotic, disorderly fashion.

Probably because people prioritized convenience for themselves, the result was a slum that, as Vihera said, was practically a maze.

"So we need a guide. Damn."

Rei frowned slightly because he had caught the stone thrown at him earlier and immediately thrown it back.

As a result, the slum dwellers would likely now view Rei as someone to be wary of.

...Though, that realization felt incredibly belated.

After all, Rei had already been on their guard since the moment he'd radiated killing intent to keep the slum dwellers at bay.

"Well, I suppose I have no choice. ...Byune, could you help us?"

"Mm."

When Vihera asked Byune, the most skilled at stealth among them, Byune gave a short reply and left the scene.

"Vihera, where did Byune go? If a child like Byune acts alone in this slum district, it could lead to trouble."

"It's fine. Byune just went to look for a guide."

Vihera replied to Elena's worried question with a faint smile.

She understood that Byune couldn't possibly handle someone who seemed to be watching them now.

Elena understood that too, of course. But unlike Vihera, who saw her partner and Byune, Elena recognized Byune's abilities while also viewing her as someone who needed protecting.

...That said, neither perspective was inherently correct.

While both acknowledged her skill, it was somewhat unusual to consider a girl of about ten as a partner by general standards. Yet, many adventurers showed exceptional talent from a young age.

And in fact...

"Hm?"

Byune emerged from behind cover, leading a man with her.

Perhaps because he lived in the slums, a stale body odor wafted from him.

His beard was completely unshaven, his hair grown wild and untamed, and grime clung to his skin.

That man, with the tip of a dagger pressed against his thigh, was now staring fearfully at Rei and the others gathered there.

This man was one of those who had surrounded Rei and the others, but having witnessed the extent of Rei's power with his own eyes, being dragged out like this made him feel like he was living on borrowed time.

Trying to appear weak, he opened his mouth with a frightened expression.

"Wh-wh-what the hell? What are you gonna do with me like this? Just so you know, I don't have any money!"

At those words, Vihera turned his gaze toward Rei.

Rei understood exactly what he meant, and stepped closer to the man.

He understood himself best suited to negotiate—or rather, threaten—the man in this situation.

Not only was there the matter of his killing intent, but also how he had caught the stone earlier and immediately thrown it back.

Though Rei was usually looked down upon, after demonstrating his skill, his small stature only made him seem more unsettling.

"Now then, you have two choices. One is to refuse our request and lose your life right here."

As Rei spoke, Byune buried the tip of his dagger slightly into his leg.

It must have felt like a needle prick, but for the man, already tense from being dragged before Rei, it was more than enough to jolt him.

"Hii! Wh-what is it?! What do you want?!"

His fear was half-acted, yet half-genuine.

But Rei had no mercy for someone who had been ready to attack them at the first opportunity.

"The other option is to quietly comply with our demands..."

He flashed a silver coin from his Misty Ring at the man before him.

"Or take this reward."

A single silver coin wasn't particularly high-value for ordinary life in Gilm.

Even low-ranked adventurers could earn that much.

But... in the slums, it was a different story.

A single silver coin held enough value that a fight to the death over it wouldn't have been surprising.

With such an item thrust before him, and a threat of death if he refused, the man had only one choice.

"I'll take it! Just tell me what you need. I'll do whatever I can... no, even if I can't, I'll find a way!"

Rei's response was far more eager than Rei had anticipated.

For a moment, Rei's expression showed surprise, but the fact that the other party was eager meant he could skip the extra effort of persuasion or threats.

"Elena, the place Yellow saw..."

"Understood. The place we want to go is a small hut. It looks quite old from the outside, but the interior has been solidly constructed."

The man was momentarily captivated by Elena's beauty, but quickly snapped back to focus, thinking of the silver coin he'd receive.

Still, pinpointing the exact location with only that information was difficult.

"Um, is there anything else that might be a clue? Like the specific shape of the hut, or anything distinctive around it?"

"Hmm, now that you mention it, there were hardly any buildings around it. It was like a square. And..."

As Elena described the surroundings of the building from Yellow's memory, the man nodded at first, but his face gradually turned pale.

Because he knew that place.

Or rather, he knew that a being of monstrous strength resided there.

He had witnessed it before: when a group of about twenty slum dwellers had once attempted to raid that building, they had all been slaughtered by a single man.

After the attack, he had considered looting the place, thinking there might still be something usable left. But that memory remained a deep wound within him.

He wanted the silver coins. But life mattered more to him than that. He opened his mouth to refuse... but then he remembered the words that had just come out of Rei's mouth, the words about life and death.

"Well, I know where you're headed, but it's a dangerous place. So, um... I'll guide you close to it. Would you let me off the hook for that?"

"Dangerous?"

Rei's questioning words made the man nod repeatedly.

"Yeah. The guy guarding that building is on a whole different level of strength compared to me. So, you know? Please."

"A man guarding the building, huh. Looks like we still have someone to fight."

Elena muttered, and Rei nodded in agreement.

Vihera smiled happily at the prospect of fighting a strong enemy, while Byune, as usual, showed no particular reaction.

Azimov remained completely detached... And so, having secured the promise to be led only to the vicinity of their destination building, Rei and his group followed the man's guidance along the path.
