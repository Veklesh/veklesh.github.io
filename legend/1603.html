As Rei had predicted, the group led by the former prostitutes arrived before Rossi during the night.

Well, perhaps "as expected" isn't quite accurate.

Rei had thought they would arrive at Rossi around 8:00 PM, but when he checked his pocket watch upon their actual arrival, it was nearly 10:00 PM.

The nearly two-hour delay was, as expected, due to the sheer number of people.

However, it wasn't due to trivial reasons like the complaints voiced by some of the women initially; it was purely a matter of physical stamina for those affected.

Among those brought along were all sorts of people.

Among them were those who had scarcely left their homes before being taken by Jaya... and those who couldn't leave their homes at all.

Those who had never left and those who couldn't leave.

The words sound similar, but the reality was vastly different.

The former were what you might call sheltered girls, while the latter were those who couldn't go outside due to illness or similar reasons.

At least, that was the story as far as Rei understood it.

Was she a prostitute despite being ill? he wondered. But apparently, wearing the slave collar enhanced healing abilities, and her illness had healed naturally.

Hearing this, Rei understood that the power of the black crystal wasn't entirely bad.

For that reason, there were inevitably those who walked slower than normal, and such individuals ended up slowing down the group's overall pace.

(Somewhere, a carriage or something... Damn it. I shouldn't have tried to capture a carriage while the Resistance and Jaya were fighting. Or I should have taken the carriage left in the lowest level of the giant's nest...)

It was too late to dwell on such thoughts now, but he considered stopping at villages or towns along the way... No, perhaps that wasn't necessary?

His gaze fixed on the capital of the Kingdom of Reburina: Rossi.

And those in power in Rossi, who were undoubtedly manipulating the nation, were clearly hostile towards Rei and his group.

Though, to be precise, the upper echelons of the nation had no choice but to oppose Rei and his group present there, as they needed to establish a common enemy—Jaya—with those likely arriving from the Kingdom of Mireana.

(In other words, forget the common folk in the city—the upper echelons of the nation can consider me their enemy. So... if I procure supplies here, they won't complain, right? Fortunately, with Set's power, pulling one or two wagons—or even more—should be easy.)

Reaching this conclusion, Rei instructs one of the nearby Resistance members to fetch Elena.

To be precise, the resistance members weren't Rei's subordinates and weren't obligated to obey his orders.

But everyone accepted that Rei was currently leading this group.

Indeed, if Rei were to disappear from this place... and his companions too, this group would undoubtedly be at the mercy of Jaya and the people of Reburina.

After sending someone to fetch Elena, a few minutes passed... Elena approached Rei from within the walking group.

"What's wrong? Did something happen? From the looks of it, nothing particularly troublesome seems to have occurred."

"Ah. I just had an idea. At this rate, our movement speed is too slow. So, I think we should prepare a carriage."

"...A carriage? Ah, I see."

Elena flashed a momentary look of puzzlement, but when she saw Rei stroking Set, who was walking beside him, she nodded in understanding.

Perhaps pleased by the attention, Set purred softly.

In any case, Elena understood what he meant about bringing a carriage. She swept her golden hair, which shimmered beautifully even under the moonlight, back from her face and spoke.

"I feel some sympathy for the people of Reburina... though, considering what they've done up until now, it's hardly possible to truly sympathize."

To have their carriage stolen without warning... and given that it was Rei doing it, it would likely be taken completely. Elena felt pity for the people of Reburina.

But considering how the upper echelons had colluded with Jaya, Elena judged there was absolutely no room for sympathy.

She wasn't alone in this. In fact, within this group, there were probably few who could forgive the Reburina upper echelons who had allied with Jaya.

"Well, that's why we're going to make a little intrusion."

"That's fine... but what about the barrier?"

In Mejogo, no barriers of any kind were in place, so they could simply enter from the air.

But Rossi was still the capital, even in its decay.

For emergencies, a barrier was undoubtedly in place.

"True. There's no doubt a barrier is up. But... I suspect that barrier is likely designed to alert us to intrusions."

This wasn't just a thoughtless remark.

Barriers like the one protecting Gilm, designed to prevent enemy intrusion, are powerful... but the catalysts required to maintain them are correspondingly expensive.

Even for a capital city, a small country like Reburina would likely struggle to afford such a barrier.

Furthermore, since flying monsters rarely ever came, it didn't seem likely to Rei that they would set up such an expensive barrier.

That said, even if by some mistake such a powerful barrier had been set up, he was confident that Set could easily break through it.

"Alright. I won't say another word. We do need a carriage, after all. Though I do wonder what we'll do about the carriage while Rei is gone..."

"Didn't you say you wouldn't say anything more? ...Well, we'll just have to figure something out in the villages and towns along the way."

Since Rei's Misty Ring couldn't store living creatures, they couldn't take horses from the castle to pull the carriage.

He couldn't help but think, If only it could store living creatures too... but he decided dwelling on the impossible was pointless and switched gears.

"If possible, I'd like the cavalry to pursue us. That would save the trouble of preparing horses."

"Heh heh, you're right. That would be rather amusing."

Rei returned Elena's smile as she smiled, then left the spot with Set.

"Sura, I'm going to infiltrate Rossi for a bit. You guys keep moving forward without worrying about us. If you stop moving carelessly, it'll cause us trouble."

"Huh? Oh. Got it... but are you serious?"

Being nearby, Sura had naturally overheard Rei and Elena's conversation.

She had heard it, but... she never imagined he'd say he'd sneak into the capital so easily.

She'd even half-expected it to be some kind of joke.

But since he was actually saying they'd sneak into the capital like this, it couldn't possibly be a joke.

"Yeah. Well then, I'll leave the rest to you, Set."

"Gururuu"

At Rei's words, Set let out a short growl and lowered his body slightly.

As soon as Rei leapt onto Set's now more accessible back, Set began to run.

After a few strides, Set flapped his wings and soared skyward. The sight possessed enough power and grace to captivate the eyes of those watching from behind.

"That was quick. ...I knew it would be."

Riding on Set's back and looking down at the ground below, Rei murmured.

Considering Set's flying speed, it was only natural, but in the blink of an eye, Rei and Set were above Rossi.

Below them lay Rossi... specifically, not the town, but the castle.

Yes, Rei and Set were now directly above the castle.

"The problem is how to find the carriage, right? Should we assume it's near the stables?"

Come to think of it, Rei had rarely had to consider where the carriage was stored.

That was because, when traveling, they usually rode on Set rather than using a carriage.

They did occasionally use carriages provided by the guild, but in those cases, the guild handled the arrangements.

Unfortunately, Rei had no idea where the carriages were stored.

"So then... I guess I'll have to search for it by force. I'll start near the stables where the horses are. Ideally, I'd like to seize about ten of them."

"Grrr!"

"Leave it to me," Set growled, purring.

Stroking Set's neck, Rei spoke.

"Alright, I'll leave it to you. Once we pass through the barrier, they'll probably notice we've entered Rossi immediately. When that happens, soldiers and knights will naturally start searching for us."

"Though spotting Set descending from above would be difficult.

They might be spotted if they kept flying, but since Rei and the others were landing directly on the ground, it should be even harder to find them.

And it's night now, making it even harder to spot us.

"GRRRR!"

Set let out a cry, then began descending toward the ground, flapping his wings.

Perhaps... just maybe... Rei had considered the possibility of a barrier like Gilm's being in place... But as if mocking Rei's resolve, Set descended straight toward the castle.

The brief sense of unease he felt must have been proof he'd passed through the barrier.

But even after passing through what seemed to be the barrier, nothing unusual happened for now.

Finding this strange, he wondered if it could perhaps alert specific places or people without being heard by intruders.

With that thought, Rei landed near what appeared to be stables while still mounted on Set.

"Set, keep watch around us! If they attack, you can fight back, but don't kill them!"

This "don't kill them" order wasn't because Rei was particularly humanitarian...

It was purely because more manpower would be needed to transport the wounded.

Since they wouldn't be staying long, this approach would also be the quickest way to reduce the enemy's forces.

"Guruu!"

Set, seemingly aware of their current predicament, let out a short growl inaudible to others before moving away and hiding behind a building.

...Given that Set now stood over three meters tall, even if he was keeping watch, carelessly wandering around would inevitably get him spotted immediately.

Though Rei and the others didn't grasp it, if soldiers or knights searching for intruders from the sky found him, he would undoubtedly be deemed a suspicious individual... or more accurately, an intruder.

That was precisely why Set hid behind the building.

Leaving Set behind, Rei ran toward the building he had his eye on.

The first building that caught his eye was one near the stables.

The vague impression it gave him of a garage... was that because Rei had lived in Japan?

Anyway, he figured that if there was a carriage, it would naturally be near the stables where the horses that pulled it were kept...

"Bingo... I guess you could say."

The building wasn't locked at all and could be opened freely.

Since this was within the castle grounds, the guards likely relaxed their vigilance, assuming no one would steal a carriage from such a place.

Entering the building, Rei smiled as he looked around.

There, lined up, were nearly twenty carriages—exactly what Rei had been searching for.

Among them were carriages adorned with quite luxurious decorations, likely meant for those of high rank... or perhaps even royalty.

Rei hadn't seen the garishly tacky carriages of nouveau riche during his rank-up exam before, but the carriages he saw were luxurious and possessed a refined elegance.

"Didn't expect to find it on the first try. ...Well, given the building layout, I figured it was probably the right place."

It wasn't luck; it was simply his guess being correct.

There was no way Rei would think cautiously about taking just a few carriages... He simply stored every carriage he could find into the Misty Ring.

Along with that, every last bit of the small items used to tether horses to carriages—ropes, metal fittings, and what seemed like repair parts—was also stored in the Misty Ring... and within a minute, all the carriages and parts that had been piled inside the building vanished completely.

"Honestly, it'd probably be better to cause a commotion by using some magic here... but even I wouldn't go that far."

Since not everyone in the castle was Rei's enemy, burning innocent people alive wasn't something Rei particularly relished.

So, I guess I'll just head back. The moment he thought that...

"GRRRR!"

"WAAAAAAAH!"

Set's howl and screams echoed throughout the area.

"Found us, huh?"

Since his business was already done, Rei had no reason to linger. He leapt out of the building.
