By the time the sun was halfway down, Rei and the Crimson Wings party were traveling along the highway.

Rei rode Set, while Marina, Vihera, and Byune each rode horses. The remaining horses were led by their reins as they moved along.

Along the road Rei and his group traveled, many others hurried to reach Gilm before the main gate closed.

Thanks to Gilm's recent expansion, the main gate now remained open considerably later into the night. Some individuals, possessing special permission, could even enter Gilm in the dead of night.

Still, it was only natural that most people wanted to reach Gilm before nightfall, especially if they knew the nature of the frontier.

The biggest reason is probably that monsters become active at night.

It's no wonder people hurry, thinking they'd rather not fight such monsters.

This tendency was especially strong among merchants and those heading to Gilm seeking work.

...Though there were a few who, eager to make a name for themselves as adventurers in Gilm, actually wanted to hunt monsters themselves.

Regardless of their various reasons, their goal of reaching Gilm was the same. Yet, when such people saw the members of Crimson Wings, their eyes widened involuntarily.

It was only natural. Nearly thirty men were bound by ropes, and worse, they were all chained together like beads on a string to prevent even one from escaping.

While their feet weren't bound to hinder movement, their hands were tied behind their backs, preventing them from gaining speed even if they tried to run.

With that many people walking together, they couldn't possibly go unnoticed.

"Hey, wait... are those guys being sold as slaves?"

"Nah, that's not it. Those are the guys from Crimson Wings. If that's the case, they're probably bandits or something."

"...Bandits? Out here in the frontier?"

"Yeah, come to think of it, I think I've heard rumors about it somewhere, or maybe not..."

"Which is it?"

Overhearing this conversation, Rei tilted her head slightly.

Normally, he wouldn't be able to hear voices of companions talking, but Rei possessed senses far sharper than ordinary, allowing him to catch their exchange.

(What's going on? This should be the first time the merchants' attack became public knowledge.)

That was precisely why Rei had been dispatched on an emergency mission for this incident.

But judging from what he'd just overheard, it seemed as if previous attacks were already tacitly known.

Of course, Rei knew that was probably true.

Because the materials found inside the cave were far beyond what a single merchant caravan could possibly carry.

Stroking the neck of his own horse, Set, he turned his gaze once more to the horses ridden by Marina and the others at the front of the line.

This horse had been tethered at the hideout that Rei and his group had raided.

He could also make out carts nearby.

Since they were attacking merchants to seize supplies, it only made sense they'd prepare to transport them.

They had hidden the horses inside the hideout because they understood that leaving them tied outside would make them easy prey for monsters.

For that reason, Rei had the Misty Ring retrieve the cart and confiscated the remaining horses as well.

However, since the Misty Ring couldn't store living creatures, they naturally had to lead the horses back themselves.

They couldn't just let them loose, so Rei and the others ended up moving while leading the horses.

Naturally, the horses were terrified of Set.

That's precisely why the horses Marina and the others rode led the way, while Rei rode Set, moving behind the bandits... no, behind some private soldiers from somewhere.

In terms of keeping watch to prevent the mercenaries from escaping, it worked out perfectly.

As Rei and the others continued walking, their progress was naturally slower compared to other merchants and adventurers.

They were passed one after another... and finally arrived in Gilm some time after night had fully fallen.

"Oh, Lady Marina, and the others too. We've been waiting for you!"

The guard spotted Marina and announced cheerfully.

He called Marina's name instead of Rei's, the party leader, likely because Marina had been leading the way.

As for Rei, the crucial one, he was slowly making his way at the very back of the line, riding on Set.

...Incidentally, dried meat was stuffed in Set's mouth, and Rei held a piece of dried meat in his hand as well.

Though Rei and the others had eaten before leaving Gilm, Set hadn't had a meal yet. Feeling hungry, he whined at Rei.

Unable to resist Set's whining voice, Rei ended up taking some dried meat from the Misty Ring and giving it to him.

Though it was one thing to give it to Set, the fact that he was eating it himself too was... well, typical of Rei, I suppose.

"Hey, Rei. Come over here."

Rei, who had been enjoying the dried meat with Set while leaving the negotiations to Marina, was summoned by that voice.

He would have preferred to leave it to Marina, but since he'd been called over, he couldn't very well ignore it.

"What is it?"

"We're handing over the people we captured in this incident, and we need your signature, Rei."

"Ah... Yeah. I figured."

With no other choice, Rei headed toward Marina.

...Which meant Set would naturally pass near the horse, and the horse stiffened whenever Set came close.

(Well, at least it's not bolting.)

The horse's honest feeling was probably that it didn't even have the energy to bolt, but for Rei, if it meant less hassle, there was no problem at all.

"Oh, Rei. Looks like you've been pretty busy today."

"Ah... yeah."

His attitude was quite different compared to when dealing with Marina, but considering everything Marina had accomplished at Gilm up to this point, it wasn't surprising at all.

Rei didn't particularly mind the other person's attitude and signed the documents he received.

Then, handing the papers to the guard, he asked about something that had slightly puzzled him.

"These documents were drafted assuming we'd bring back prisoners from the start, weren't they?"

"Yeah, that's right. ...Well, since I entrusted this job to Rei, I figured bringing a prisoner was a given, right?"

The clause about the reward increasing based on the number of prisoners was clearly written with Rei's actions in mind.

(If they're offering a reward, I might as well take it.)

Normally, capturing bandits would mean selling them as slaves.

That would bring in additional income, but the prisoners Rei brought back this time weren't to be enslaved; the knight order would take them.

Where did they come from? What were they after? What actions had they taken up until now? ...It was necessary to extract as much information as possible.

Particularly crucial would be who they worked for and any weaknesses they possessed.

Given the need to extract such information, selling him as a slave was out of the question.

The conditions for taking them in were set with this in mind.

Moreover, the reward offered was considerably higher than what selling him as a slave would have fetched.

Since there was no loss for Rei, there was no need to hesitate here.

And so, the procedures were completed, and the captured individuals were led away by the guards...

"Wait a minute!"

Rei called out to the guard.

"Huh? What is it? ...Ah, never mind me. Just take these guys away."

After telling his fellow guard that, the guard who had handed Rei the documents approached him again.

"Hey, what about that horse? And we've got the pack wagon they were using secured over here too?"

"The horse? Ah... I didn't hear anything about that. The paperwork didn't mention it either. You can probably just dispose of it however you like?"

"Even so..."

Hearing the guard's words, Rei looked at the horse, seeming perplexed.

As for the cart, if push came to shove, it could be loaded onto Set and dropped from above as a mass weapon, or used as firewood.

...The fact that the idea of simply using it as a regular cart didn't occur to him was probably because Rei had the Misty Ring.

Watching Rei like this, Vihera stroked the horse and voiced a suggestion.

"Why not sell the horse? ...Well, worst case, you could always process it for meat."

Vihera's gaze fell upon the scene behind them: Set being stroked by adventurers who seemed to be either curiously observing the group processing their entry into Gilm or who had likely been based there for some time.

At Vihera's words, Rei thought the horse seemed to tense up for a moment... but he dismissed it as probably just his imagination and turned his thoughts back to the suggestion of selling it.

The horses brought by the private soldiers weren't exactly thoroughbreds.

But conversely, they weren't worthless either.

As long as they were perfectly serviceable mounts, buyers would surely be plentiful.

Especially now, with Gilm's expansion, there was never enough of the means of transportation, like horses and carriages.

"Ah... I see. Got it. We'll go with that for now."

"Do that. Oh, and take the materials to the guild, not the storage yard. They want to inspect them properly before accepting delivery."

With that instruction given, the guard departed.

Only Rei and the others remained, along with those still processing their Guild membership applications and the guard handling the paperwork.

"Well then, let's head over too. ...Vihera, could you handle the horses?"

"Sure. What about the price?"

"Leave it to me. Just make sure they don't try to haggle you down."

"Oh? Who do you think you're talking to? I'm quite skilled at that sort of negotiation, you know?"

Vihera's words were brimming with confidence.

Before meeting Rei and the others... back when she was traveling with Byune in the Labyrinth City of Exil, shopping for consumables and such was naturally Vihera's job.

Of course, in her hometown of Exil, Byune could communicate to some extent with certain people, but even so, it was still quicker for Vihera to go shopping.

Having heard about this situation from Vihera before, Rei raised his hand slightly in apology and spoke.

"Got it. I'll leave it to you then."

With that, Rei, Marina, and Set split off from Vihera and Byune, each group going their separate ways.

"Shall we go too? It's getting dark, and things should be calming down at the guild... I mean, that's what I'd normally say,"

Marina said with a troubled smile, likely because it was already dark outside and the usual peak busy time at the guild had passed.

But that was only 'normal' under ordinary circumstances. With Gilm's expansion drawing adventurers in droves, the current situation was far from what could be called 'normal'.

There were numerous requests: demolishing buildings in the planned expansion area, transporting construction materials, preparing timber, stone, metal, and other materials into usable shapes so they could be assembled immediately once construction began... Adventurers were working late into the night on these tasks.

Of course, lacking specialized knowledge, they're mostly doing basic tasks anyone can handle.

In any case, many adventurers are working late.

Of course, many finish their work by evening.

Thanks to those adventurers, the guild should have a decent number of people right now.

"Well, that can't be helped. Anyway, since we completed the request, we have to hand it over."

And so, with the increased number of people bringing more energy to the streets, Rei and the others walked toward the guild.

"Rei! The guard squad contacted us, but it seems you've already resolved it!"

After parting ways with Set in front of the guild, Lenora quickly spotted Rei entering with Marina and happily waved, calling out to them.

Inside the guild, as Rei had expected, a fair number of people were lined up at the counter to collect today's rewards. But Lenora ignored them and called out to Rei and Marina.

"Hey, wait! What about my reward?!"

Naturally, the male adventurer who had been waiting for Lenora to process his paperwork voiced his dissatisfaction.

Flustered, Lenora asked a guild clerk nearby who was handling another task and came over to Rei.

"Good work today. Now, let's get moving—the client is waiting."

"...Waiting?"

"Yes. The Crimson Wings are a skilled party, so they definitely want to wait for your return. ...I never imagined you'd be back this soon, though. They're waiting in the conference room upstairs now, so let's go."

With that, Rei and Marina were led upstairs by Lenora.
