Should I go back? Once Rei made up his mind, he acted quickly.

Still, he couldn't just leave unconditionally.

After all, having gotten involved in the events that unfolded here in Gosh, ignoring them and just returning to Gilm... if he tried something like that, Marina would definitely be furious.

Rei didn't want that to happen either, so he would properly inform Augusto of his decision.

(The various incidents in Gosh... including the matter of the Hundred-Faced One, have already been resolved. There's still the question of what to do about the Tira Tree, but as far as this particular matter goes, I'm hardly involved anymore.)

Both Augusto and Daldora fully understood the dangers of the Tira Tree, especially given the Hundred-Faced One incident.

Given that, how to protect Gosh wasn't really a problem for Rei to solve.

Thinking this way, Rei declined Zarust and Narsus, then flew through the sky on Set's back, eventually returning to Gosh, which had just departed a short while ago on the sand ship.

When they had departed by sand ship, it had been early morning.

However, after arriving at the Hundred-Faced One's village, watching the unnecessary husband-and-wife comedy act there, and then wandering around the village aimlessly afterward...

It only took a few minutes to get from the village of the Hundred-Faced Ones to Gosh, but even so, it was no surprise that by the time he returned to Gosh, it was no longer early morning.

「……Rei？」

That's precisely why the guard responsible for Gosh's entrances and exits couldn't hide his surprise at Rei's sudden appearance.

Why was Rei back already, just hours after leaving?

But he knew about the events of the previous night from the shift handover, and unlike then, it was daytime now.

Given that, there was absolutely no reason to hinder his entry.

After checking his guild card and handing over his familiar's necklace, the procedure itself was completed quickly.

Once inside Gosh, the first thing Rei did was visit various shops to buy all sorts of dishes and ingredients unique to this town.

As a result, on this day, certain shops within Gosh saw several days' worth of sales in a single day... or worse, some shops had their entire inventory purchased, recording months' worth of sales.

Of course, Rei didn't always buy in such large quantities.

Being a desert town, Gosh had many ingredients and seasonings unique to the region that weren't sold in Gilm.

The combination of the desert's intense daytime heat, freezing nights, and the presence of oases meant a remarkable variety of spices were sold here.

While common spices could be obtained in Gilm, the ones sold in Gosh were extremely rare and seldom made it all the way to Gilm.

Even if they did reach Gilm, the price would be ten times higher than what you'd pay in Gosh... or even dozens of times more, if you weren't careful.

Though Rei had ample funds, he still didn't want to make unnecessary expenditures.

That's why he was buying up items unique to Gosh like this.

Adventurers who visited other towns, cities, or even countries sometimes did this too, but what set Rei apart was the sheer volume and variety of goods he bought.

With the Misty Ring, Rei bought more than the average adventurer... or even merchants.

Spices and such would spoil and become unusable after years passed, and even if usable, most would lose their flavor and aroma.

However, as long as they remained inside the Misty Ring, the passage of time ceased to exist.

This meant that no matter when he used the spices, they would be fresh.

And the Misty Ring's properties also come into play because Rei's purchases include dishes.

Being able to eat Gosh's unique dishes whenever they're freshly made must be incredibly enviable for those without an item box.

"Well, thanks again!"

The shopkeeper gasped for breath as he thanked Rei.

Skewers generously seasoned with spices made in Gosh.

That's why he bought every last one the shop had.

The sight of skewers being grilled one after another and immediately stored in the Misty Ring as they were finished could almost be described as bizarre.

The shopkeeper was out of breath, partly because he'd grilled today's entire supply in a fraction of the usual time.

Honestly, Rei had told him it was fine just to grill them without pushing himself so hard... but to the shop owner, it must have sounded like a kind of challenge.

He threw himself into grilling them all, and now he stood there, gasping for breath.

"Well, thanks. I'll savor every bite of these skewers."

After paying a little extra, Rei and Set walked on, eating one skewer each.

As they did so, they repeatedly bought various items and stored them in the Misty Ring. Finally satisfied, Rei contacted the guild and headed for Augusto's mansion.

The mansion on the outskirts of Gosh, unlike when they had come last night or this morning, showed no sign of guards whatsoever.

Only Günter stood guard at the gate.

"Oh? What's up? You're back pretty early. Did something go wrong?"

Rei shook her head at Günter, who asked with a slightly concerned expression.

"No, nothing really. I sent the guys to investigate the village over there. They should be checking things out around now."

"...Then why are you here, Rei?"

"Just had some business to take care of. Besides, with Set here, it's only a few minutes one way from the village to Gosh, so it's not that big of a deal for us."

At Rei's words, Günter turned his gaze back to Set.

Although Günter had the capacity to accept Set's existence from the start, he still didn't fully understand Set's abilities.

He had seen Seto arrive at the mansion last night and again this morning, but there had been a gap of several hours between those appearances.

He'd assumed most of that gap was spent traveling... but if it was actually only a few minutes, Günter couldn't help but be astonished.

Watching Günter stare wide-eyed at Set, Rei asked about something that had been on his mind.

"What about you? Compared to this morning, you don't have any guards besides Günter."

"Ah... well, those adventurers were hired by Daldora anyway. Besides, we took out the original assassins, right? So I told them there was no need to hire any more unnecessary guards and ended the contract."

"That was a pretty quick decision. You can't completely rule out the possibility of stragglers remaining, so I thought you'd keep the heavy security for a while longer."

"Well, Daldora already has skilled guards. These adventurers hired by the dozen in Gosh... whether they're here or not makes no difference... no, having them around just costs extra money and gets in the way."

Rei's expression showed understanding at the words coming from Günter's mouth.

Augusto had sent Zarust, whom he employed as a guard, so there were no immediate bodyguards present. But Daldora needn't worry about Augusto.

Their alliance had always been somewhat like forced coexistence.

Now that concern was resolved, it was obvious there was no need for Augusto and Daldora to keep cooperating.

"So, in the end, the guard is back to just Günter?"

"...Well, Augusto doesn't have much financial leeway. Still, I imagine this whole affair will make him somewhat wealthier."

"I suppose so."

Even if it was in cooperation with Daldora, they defeated the majority of the legendary Hundred-Faced Ones who had long been revered in this country and destroyed their stronghold.

And Rei played a major role in that, so he's essentially a guest of Augusto now.

Furthermore, Zarust is unquestionably an adventurer hired by Augusto.

Considering all that, Augusto's contribution regarding the Hundred-Faced Ones outweighs Daldora's.

That said, even if Augusto deserves more credit, Daldora's assets are absolutely essential to make the Hundred-Faced One's village usable.

And in the world... especially among merchants, it was self-evident that those with money held the power.

"So, what exactly did you come back for, Rei? Judging by your demeanor, it doesn't seem like you have some urgent business like yesterday, right?"

"Hmm, I'm not sure. It's true it's not urgent, but still, there's something I wanted to tell Augusto and Kathy."

"...Something you need to tell them?"

Rei's somewhat evasive words made Günter frown slightly.

Perhaps, even at this moment, Günter had already guessed what Rei had come to tell them.

"Yeah. Can I come in? Augusto is here, right? Or did he go to the shop?"

"No, he's inside. ...Well, it's fine if it's you. Just... I'd prefer it if you didn't make those two sad, if possible."

Rei shrugged slightly at Günter's words.

What he was about to tell them would undoubtedly make Augusto and Kathy sad.

(No, Augusto might not be that upset. But Cathy would definitely be sad. Or rather, I don't think she'd accept it.)

Cathy was the one who treated him like a child, even though they couldn't have children together with Augusto... though that probably wasn't the reason.

If Rei simply said he was returning to Gilm, she wouldn't accept it so easily.

Still, Rei couldn't stay in Gosh forever.

(It had been quite some time since the Twilight Spear incident too.)

Rei figured those troublesome merchants probably didn't have time to bother with him anymore.

Entering the grounds, Rei parted ways with Set, who headed toward the stables as usual, and made his way into the mansion.

Unlike yesterday, there was no sign of anyone inside the mansion.

Since only Augusto and Kathy lived in this mansion to begin with, that was only natural.

Still, the sense of loneliness he felt was likely because so many people had been inside yesterday.

Heading towards the room used as the living room, he found Augusto and Cathy there, sipping a drink resembling milk tea and chatting, enjoying a leisurely moment.

"Augusto, is it okay for you not to be busy moving around right now? Daldora is already on the move, right?"

He'd been so busy since yesterday he'd completely forgotten, but now that he thought about it, this was exactly how these two were when together...

His approval rating had gone up in a way Daldora probably wouldn't be happy about if she found out, but Rei didn't let that bother him as he spoke up.

"Actually, um..."

But now that Kathy was right in front of him, it somehow felt awkward to say.

"Huh!?"

I hadn't said anything yet.

"We lived together for a little while, after all. There's no way I wouldn't know something like this, right?"

Is that how it is? Rei wondered, but since she'd actually guessed it like this, it must be an undeniable fact.

"...Yeah."

"I don't know, but maybe as soon as tomorrow."
