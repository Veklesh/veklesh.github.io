Rei — or rather, Death Scythe — and Set had finished absorbing the magic stones and were walking toward the house Elena and the others were renting.

Even now, Set still couldn't shake that slightly disappointed look, and even to Rei's eyes, he seemed down.

(Did he want that Aero Wing magic stone that badly? ...Well, I can understand why.)

Similar to Death Scythe's Flying Slash, several of Set's skills also possess wind attributes.

Wind Arrow and Tornado were two of them.

Wind Arrow was weak in power but excelled in attack speed. Being an arrow made of wind also made it difficult for enemies to spot.

Though Set possessed numerous skills, Wind Arrow was arguably the ranged attack he used most frequently.

And above all, Tornado.

The fire whirlwind that earned Rei the nickname "Crimson."

This attack method, possessing the power to sweep away everything around it, has two origins: Rei's fire magic and Set's Tornado skill.

By infusing it with more of Rei's skills and magical power, it grows, fuses, and ultimately becomes a tornado made of flames... a fire whirlwind.

In other words, strengthening Tornado directly enhances the Fire Whirlwind.

That's precisely why Set coveted the Aerowings magic stone. Yet, the prospect of Rei also being able to raise the level of her Flying Slash proved irresistible. In the end, Set succumbed to Rei's persistence and relinquished the Aerowings magic stone.

"Grrr..."

Of course, Set didn't object to Rei's... Death Scythe Flying Slash becoming more powerful.

No, he was actually delighted by it.

Seeing his beloved Rei grow stronger naturally made Set happy too; he had no complaints about that.

Still, deep down, Set couldn't help but wish he could absorb the Aero Wing magic stone himself.

As these thoughts crossed Set's mind, Rei, walking beside him, gently reached out a hand.

"Hey, the Aerowings are still in the forest. Let's look forward to exploring the woods this afternoon. Right?"

"Grrrrr."

Rei's words seemed to lift his spirits a little, and Set growled more energetically than he had a few seconds ago.

With a sandwich from his Misty Ring in his hand, Rei handed it over to Set.

It hadn't been long since lunch, but for Set, eating one or two sandwiches posed no problem.

He happily finished the sandwich Rei gave him... and now, seemingly almost back to normal, Set started walking beside Rei.

(It's a bit awkward for me to say this after giving him the sandwich, but isn't he cheering up a bit too easily?)

Rei understood that Set was weak when it came to food, but seeing him cheer up so easily over a sandwich still made him feel a little uneasy.

What if, someday, he gets lured by food and it leads to trouble?

...But Set doesn't accept food from just anyone.

He used his senses, sixth sense, and ability to perceive magic to judge who was friendly toward him.

He never shows affection, trust, or accepts food from those approaching him with hostility... or those trying to use him or Rei.

"Gurrr, grrrr... grrr?"

Set had been purring contentedly, but as they neared the house Elena and the others were renting, he suddenly fixed his gaze ahead.

What met his gaze were two small figures approaching straight toward him.

One was Polo, running across the ground with his nine tails fluttering in the wind.

The other was Yellow, flapping his wings wide as he flew through the sky.

Both of them were approaching Set at full speed.

One running on the ground, the other flying through the air.

Normally, the one flying through the air would be faster, but in this instance, it was Polo, running on the ground, who held the speed advantage.

But... even so, the one who ultimately reached the goal—Set's back—first was Yellow.

For Polo, running on the ground, reaching Set's back required either climbing up from Set's legs or using Rei as a wall to leap up in a triangle jump.

However, Rei's position wasn't suitable for Polo to perform a triangle jump, forcing Polo to choose the option of climbing up Set's body.

In comparison, Yellow, flying through the air, only needed to land directly onto Set's back.

While it needed to slow down before landing, Yellow could easily reduce its speed by spreading its wings to create air resistance.

Consequently, despite Polo having the advantage in speed, it was Yellow who ultimately secured the victory.

"Kyuuu! Kyu, Kyu!"

"Polooo..."

In stark contrast to Yellow, who was happily purring, Polo looked dejected.

Polo, who had been so confident in his speed, surely never imagined he would lose.

What remained was a stark image of clear victor and vanquished.

"Gururururuu"

Set growled at the two of them.

It wasn't a growl of blame for racing each other to his back as the finish line, but rather one that seemed to urge them to get along better.

Indeed, upon hearing that growl, Yellow and Polo stopped fighting each other and lay down together peacefully on Set's back.

(He's acting like the older one...)

In reality, Set was older than Yellow but younger than Polo... but neither Rei nor Set knew that.

The fact that Polo is only slightly bigger than a squirrel probably adds to that misunderstanding.

"Polo suddenly took off running, so I wondered where he'd gone..."

"Looks like he picked up Set's scent and moved with Yellow."

As they spoke, Elena and Earth emerged from the grounds, both equipped to a degree that could be described as fully armed.

Behind them, similarly equipped figures could be seen: Marina, Vihera , Ara , and Byune .

"You're all ready to go."

Watching the six of them, Rei murmured.

"Well, isn't that the same for you, Rei? ...Though, in your case, you didn't really need to prepare equipment to begin with."

Marina said to Rei, looking slightly envious.

The others also directed similar glances at Rei.

"Even if you say so. My weapon just takes up space and gets in the way if I carry it normally."

The Death Scythe Rei carried had a handle over two meters long, and the blade itself was over a meter in size.

Carrying such a massive weapon by hand while walking was extremely conspicuous.

And above all, no matter how light the Death Scythe was in Rei's hands, its sheer size meant it was bound to be cumbersome.

Furthermore, while spear-throwing was one of Rei's favored attack methods, he couldn't very well carry around throwing spears just for the sake of it.

If he did that, Rei would be carrying several to over a dozen spears, plus the Death Scythe in his hand – an outfit hardly suitable for walking through populated areas.

"Heh heh, that would be interesting in its own way. I'd love to see Rei like that sometime... though spears would probably just get in the way during a fight."

Since Vihera herself favored lightness—wearing thin robes as armor—she likely had an aversion to heavy equipment.

Earth, who also prioritized lightness and wore only a leather breastplate instead of full leather armor, agreed with Vihera.

As they exchanged these words, Marina finally spoke up.

"Well, it's about time we headed into the forest. Even if we're inside the Lost Barrier, I want us to properly investigate the forest's surroundings... as part of looking into the anomaly occurring there."

"I know we've already prepared, but isn't the World Tree's healing complete by now? If so, is there really a need for us to go out of our way to investigate and resolve this anomaly? Won't it resolve itself naturally as time passes?"

As Elena slid her finger along the sheath holding Mirage, Marina shook her head.

"True, now that the World Tree has recovered, it might resolve itself over time. But it might not. As long as that remains unclear, I'd prefer to deal with this anomaly now if possible."

No one disagreed with her words.

Even Earth, the only one among them who had just met Rei and the others, shared the sentiment that resolving the forest's anomaly was the best course of action, since they had come to investigate it in the first place.

Marina looked around at the group, confirming no one had any objections. She gave a small nod and spoke.

"Then, shall we decide on the team assignments?"

"...Teams?"

Rei murmured, wondering why that phrase had come up.

Rei nodded as if it were obvious and continued.

"Understand? Almost everyone here possesses more than sufficient strength as combatants."

At Marina's words, nearly everyone present nodded in agreement.

Those confident in their own abilities, like Rei, Elena, and Vihera.

Ara also had a fair amount of confidence purely in terms of combat strength.

Earth, too, as a Rank C adventurer, had reasonable confidence in his abilities alongside Polo.

...Only the thief, Byune, remained silent, lacking much confidence in her own combat abilities.

Marina looked around at the group and continued.

"Since we have people this strong, it's inefficient for everyone to stick together. But acting alone makes it hard to contact others if something happens. Given that, it's better to split into groups of two or three."

"...Does that include me?"

The one who voiced this was Rei.

Rei herself was confident in her combat ability, and she also had Set.

It was certain that just the two of them—him and the beast—would be more than enough fighting power.

Marina seemed to understand that too. She looked at Rei and Set, thinking for a moment.

She understood there was no issue with their combat strength. But if Rei were to become immobilized in an emergency, even if Set came to alert her, there would be no one to communicate with. That was a serious weakness.

(Oh, but would anything really happen to Rei?)

That thought came precisely because she knew Rei's capabilities.

She didn't think Rei could defeat anyone or anything.

Still, considering Rei's strength, she hadn't imagined he wouldn't be able to handle even somewhat powerful monsters appearing in this forest.

(Besides...)

She noticed the gaze directed at her and looked toward its source.

Since he was the one speaking, it was natural the gaze was directed at him, but that gaze... those two gazes were stronger than the others'.

That is, Elena and Vihera.

(They probably think I'll use this chance to act together with Rei, don't they?)

As the guildmaster, as the granddaughter of this settlement's elder, and as Marina.

She considered what choice she would make... and finally turned toward Rei and spoke.

"Well, yes. If it's Rei and Set, getting through any problems shouldn't be too difficult. Just be careful not to go too high when flying. It's fine while you're inside, but if you go too high, you might touch the confusion barrier deployed up there."

Well, Rei would probably just break through the confusion barrier or something like that, Marina added jokingly. Hearing this, everyone present let out a small smile.

Because they understood that statement was true.

But at the same time, they also understood that Rei would never actually do such a thing.

He came to heal the World Tree; there's no way he'd try to mess with it.

"So, Rei will be with Set. The others remaining are..."

Marina's gaze shifted to the remaining members.

The remaining members were Elena, Vihera, Ara, Byune, Earth, and herself.

(Including Polo, that's six people and one creature... So should we split into two groups of three? Or three groups of two?)

Marina hesitated for a moment, but ultimately decided it would be better to cover as many directions as possible and chose three groups of two.

"Elena and Ara, Vihera and Byune, me and Earth. ...It's a bit safe, but how about this combination?"

That pairing was exactly as Marina had said—a combination that could rightly be called safe.

But at the same time, it was also a combination well-suited for Ara and Byune, who were accustomed to supporting Elena and Vihera, to fully demonstrate their abilities.

(Now, if I can just coordinate well with Earth...)

And so, the team assignments were finalized without incident.
