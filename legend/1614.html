Hmm, I see. Things seem to be going smoothly for now. But... we don't have unlimited funds either. We need to cut back a bit more.

"Well, isn't it better than negotiating to save money only to end up with shoddy goods, or worse, having the deal canceled altogether?"

In the Paired Orb illuminated by the bonfire before the Magic Tent, Daskar looked at his conversation partner, Marina, with displeasure.

"But like I said before, we don't have that much leeway in our budget either. Especially now, with the expansion work, our expenses are huge. You understand that, Marina, right?"

"True. The expenses are high, but... the income coming in is also substantial, right?"

Pop. The crackling sound of fire leaping in the bonfire echoed around them.

Amidst the chorus of insects chirping from all around, Daskar, seated across the Paired Orb, remained silent.

"Oh? What's wrong? Did I say something wrong?"

'Grr...'

Daskar growled briefly at Marina's words.

He wanted to retort. But he knew that if he said anything back here, it would come back threefold, fourfold.

Knowing this, the Daskar could only growl.

Smiling at Daskar's reaction, Marina spoke again.

"Anyway, that's why we're gathering carriages and horses while returning to Gilm. If we manage to gather them smoothly like this, we might even arrive over there sooner than planned. Though, once we're somewhat organized, we'll probably return to Gilm for a while."

"Regarding that, Marina probably knows more about adventurers than I do. So, you make the call on that front."

"How the hell should I know? I didn't give those guys convenient things like Paired Orbs. They did leave much earlier than you, though, and their carriage is specially made. It should be considerably faster than normal travel... Still, considering the distance to the Kingdom of Reburina..."

Traveling normally by carriage, the distance from Gilm to Rossi, the capital of the Kingdom of Reburina, is roughly about a month.

And since Rei and the others arrived in the Kingdom of Reburina, hardly any time has passed yet.

Considering that, if Rei and the others kept up this pace, encountering the envoy party should have been some time off.

No matter how specially made the carriages and horses the envoy group used were, catching up immediately should have been impossible.

"I suppose so. Once we meet up with the envoy, I'll contact them. We'll need to exchange information, after all."

'Do that.'

A quiet atmosphere returned to the surroundings... or so it seemed.

"Someone's approaching."

Rei muttered, turning his gaze toward the road.

Though the summer sky had been cloudless during the day, now some clouds had appeared, obscuring the moon.

Even so, Rei detected the approaching presence thanks to his exceptional physical abilities and... his accumulated experience.

If Set were here, he would likely have noticed the approaching presence much sooner, but unfortunately, Set was inside the captain's cabin of the sand ship.

For now, no one with strange intentions had entered there, but it was still necessary to keep someone in the captain's cabin just in case.

"Who could it be? ...Well, approaching on a night like this, they must have some reason."

Vihera murmured, her voice tinged with a hint of anticipation at the possibility of combat.

The others also began preparing for battle, albeit half-heartedly.

However, since only one presence approached and there were no signs of hidden allies nearby, Rei suspected it was unlikely to be bandits.

(Could it be one of Jaya's assassins who followed Ryutas...?)

Rei glanced briefly toward the sand ship where Ryutas slept, but soon the faint moonlight allowed him to clearly make out the figure approaching.

However, upon seeing the figure, Rei and the others immediately relaxed their guard.

Of course, they weren't completely relaxed; they maintained a minimum level of vigilance.

The approaching figure, too, likely didn't expect to go unnoticed—after all, why would someone like him deliberately approach on a night like this?

Stopping a short distance away, they opened their mouth.

"Excuse me, may I have a word?"

The voice belonged to a young man.

However, to Rei, that soft voice suggested good breeding.

Since Rei was the only man in their group, he needed to speak to the man to avoid being looked down upon.

"What is it? If you're looking for the road, it's not here—it's where you came from."

"Yes, of course I know that. But my business isn't with the road—it's with you gentlemen. Besides, would it be alright if I came a little closer? Since I've finally managed to meet you, Mr. Rei and the others, I'd like to see your faces clearly while we talk."

"...Huh."

Despite not having introduced himself, Rei narrowed his eyes slightly at the mention of his name.

Then again, for the past few nights, they'd been deploying the sand ships as lodgings. And above all, a group of nearly a thousand women—many dressed as prostitutes—was moving through the area.

Such a group was bound to generate rumors.

In that sense, it was later than Rei had expected for a man like this to make contact.

Merchants had already approached them the very next day after they first used the sand ship.

However, those were merely individuals who, upon seeing the sand ship, judged it more profitable to approach Rei and his group on the spot. They were definitely not the sort who seemed dispatched by some organization, like the ones standing before him now.

"So, what do you say? Of course, I won't say it's free. I've prepared a small token of appreciation."

"A gift, huh. Well, if you put it that way, I don't particularly plan to refuse. ...But let me warn you, it's for your own good not to get any funny ideas."

He briefly flashed a hint of lethal intent, causing the man's approaching footsteps toward the bonfire to falter.

That he didn't stop walking was likely because Rei understood that as long as he didn't act against his own interests, he wouldn't be harmed.

Eventually, though walking slightly slower, the man appeared near Rei and the others... within the light of the bonfire.

He appeared to be in his mid-twenties. His features gave off a gentle impression, though he now looked slightly tense.

The reason for that tension was, needless to say, the killing intent Rei had emitted... but Rei showed no sign of caring about it at all as he stated.

The man, seemingly well aware that he looked suspicious, opened his mouth hurriedly.

"Of course. I mean, I have no hostile intentions whatsoever. I just... thought I might be able to help you all."

"Help us, huh? Specifically?"

"A carriage and horses. Plus food and clothing... If you wish, I can arrange for guards as well."

"...What?"

The man's words were completely unexpected, even for Rei.

That's precisely why Rei, who had been watching the man, froze for an instant.

What the man had offered was undoubtedly exactly what Rei and his companions needed.

In fact, Rei and the others had planned to purchase these things in the towns and villages they would likely stop at.

The reason they hadn't prepared it in a single village or town was precisely because the quantity Rei needed exceeded what any single village or town could supply.

But the man standing before them had just offered to provide it.

Marina, surprised by the man's words, asked him instead of Rei.

"Are you from the Kingdom of Reburina? I hate to say it, but I can't imagine a small country like Reburina could prepare the supplies we need."

To be precise, if the country were truly committed, it could probably prepare that much immediately.

But that was only because it was a nation. A merchant guild couldn't just easily arrange it.

Well, maybe some trading companies could manage it if they tried hard enough, but it was hard to imagine a trading company doing something that wouldn't benefit them.

That's precisely why I couldn't believe a Reburina merchant guild would lend a hand to Rei and the others.

(Above all, even in Reburina, all the major trading houses should have been under Jaya's strong influence. So, for us, who are hostile to Jaya... no, for Jaya to lose to us, and then for the country's upper echelons to try to cut ties with Jaya... I see.)

Quickly gathering her thoughts, Marina looked at the man with her usual smile.

...Though it was her usual smile, it was only her usual smile to Marina.

In other words, it was a smile that radiated a woman's intense allure.

The man couldn't possibly remain calm when faced with such a smile.

The moonlight streaming through the gaps in the clouds and Marina's figure illuminated by the bonfire created an atmosphere that intensely emphasized her femininity to an almost overwhelming degree.

Even for a man with strong resistance to such... so-called honey traps or seduction tactics, Marina's feminine allure was not something he could easily handle. He struggled to resist being captivated by her beauty.

He was here representing the trading company.

This deal must succeed at all costs. With that thought in mind.

"Yes. I am Gime Kara of the Zolge Commerce Guild from the Kingdom of Reburina."

Marina asked Gime Kara directly, without hiding anything about who they were—a group that had escaped from Mejogo... and a group hostile to Jaya.

She understood that since he had approached them, he must be acting with full knowledge of who they were.

"Hmm. What exactly would make that happen?"

"Well... To be perfectly honest, I suppose it's because I have no intention of boarding a sinking ship."

With that single statement, Marina... and the others too, understood why Gimekara had approached them like this.

Gimekara said nothing in response to Rei's words... Only the chirping of insects and the crackling of the bonfire echoed around them.
