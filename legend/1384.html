The air was filled with an aroma that whetted the appetite.

After noon, Rei and the others left Treant Forest to eat lunch.

Normally, adventurers eating on the job would have dried meat or hardened bread.

...That is, when undertaking long-term quests.

When traveling close to their base town and using a carriage, like this time, they had a bit more leeway for proper meals.

Indeed, the members of Crimson Wings were enjoying a variety of dishes, starting with the freshly made stew Rei had taken out of the Misty Ring. Even the coachmen, who were in charge of the carriage, were eating proper meals like sandwiches and fruit.

Even so, there was no contest between sandwiches made some time ago that morning and the freshly prepared dishes Rei had retrieved from the Misty Ring—the latter clearly won out.

Several of the coachmen cast envious glances at the food Rei and the others were eating.

And eventually, they couldn't stand it anymore.

One of the coachmen approached Rei and spoke up.

"Um, Rei. If it's not too much trouble... would you mind selling me a few of those delicious-looking skewers?"

"Sure, no problem."

"...Huh? Really?"

Though he'd asked, the driver, who'd probably expected to be refused, was slightly surprised.

He hadn't expected to be sold them so readily.

But for Rei, skewers were something he could buy in abundance at Gilm.

If he kept them in his Misty Ring, he could enjoy freshly grilled skewers anytime he wanted.

That was precisely why Rei handed the skewers over to the coachman without hesitation.

"Hmph!"

When her portion seemed smaller, Byune let out a slightly dissatisfied sound, but when Rei pulled out a new skewer from the Misty Ring, she immediately nodded contentedly and returned to eating.

"Um... Sorry about that. How much?"

"No, money's fine. I'm making a profit one way or another anyway."

The reward Rei would likely receive for this Treant Forest incident would probably be in the form of fire ore.

Even Rei couldn't predict how much that would amount to.

That's how significant the role Rei... or rather, Rei and the members of Crimson Wings including Set, had played.

The reward would undoubtedly be substantial.

Plus, they sold Treant's corpse—devoid of magic stones—to the guild, so that should also bring in a decent sum.

Considering all that, treating them to skewered meat wouldn't be a major expense.

Rather than begrudging a small expense, it's better to maintain good relations with the coachmen.

Especially since all the coachmen are guild employees, in that sense, treating them to skewers isn't a problem for Rei.

"You guys come too. You can have a skewer or two each."

When Rei called out like that, the coachmen approached them with smiles so delighted they seemed almost greedy.

"Help yourselves. ...Ah, you guys can have some too."

As an afterthought, Rei called out to the adventurers who remained behind to guard the carriage and coachmen.

They were part of the survey team sent to investigate Treant Forest, but it wasn't feasible for everyone to enter the forest, leaving the carriage and driver unattended.

While monsters didn't appear in Treant Forest during the day, that was merely an empirical rule.

And since Treant Forest itself was an irregular entity, it wouldn't be surprising if monsters appeared at any time.

Furthermore, while monsters didn't appear from within Treant Forest, they appeared normally elsewhere.

There was no guarantee such monsters wouldn't attack the carriages left behind here. While unlikely, the possibility of bandits appearing was also very real.

Considering these possibilities, it was only natural that the carriage and its driver needed escorts... and indeed, some members of this party had been hired specifically for that purpose.

Naturally, their pay was lower than those sent to investigate Treant Forest, but the likelihood of encountering monsters was low, and with little prospect of actual combat, it was effectively a cushy job where they could collect pay for doing practically nothing.

In fact, many adventurers who had entered Treant Forest as part of the survey team had requested to serve as guards.

Those guards happily reached for the skewered meat at Rei's words.

Though they abstained from alcohol, it became a lively lunch... and as several hours passed, the survey teams began gradually returning from Treant Forest.

Those returning saw Rei and the others outside the forest having what could be called a banquet without alcohol and voiced their dissatisfaction... but when Rei handed them ice-cold fruit water, their complaints immediately ceased.

Incidentally, this chilled fruit water wasn't something Rei had taken from the Misty Ring...

It was fruit water stored in the carriage, chilled by Marina's spirit magic summoning water spirits.

It wasn't icy cold enough to give you a headache, but it was still as cold as water chilled in a river.

Even under the spring sunshine, for those who'd been walking through the forest and returning, there was no greater treat.

When they drank the fruit water from the cups handed to them, they'd make a dissatisfied face, yet be surprised by the coldness on the first sip, confirm the chill on the second sip, and then down the rest in one gulp on the third sip before demanding another cup.

What followed was the smile of a stunning beauty like Marina or Vihera.

No one could be dissatisfied with this.

...Of course, many still harbored some dissatisfaction, and some wished they could get closer to Marina or Vihera.

But they all knew those two were members of Rei's party, so they couldn't afford to make any careless moves.

By the time evening approached, the entire investigation team that had entered the forest had returned.

"Surprising, I suppose."

"Oh, really? Monsters don't appear during the day, and there are no beasts. Thinking about it that way, it's not strange that everyone returned safely unless they got lost, right?"

Marina spoke up, perhaps hearing Rei's muttered comment about everyone returning.

"Yeah. Normally, you'd think so... Hmm, maybe it's just my imagination?"

"I don't know. I can't say for sure about that part. But logically speaking, wouldn't that be the case?"

"But... we don't know what kind of monsters are in this forest... or maybe they aren't monsters at all. Anyway, they must know we're here, right? So I don't think they'll just let us investigate quietly."

Being investigated meant the forest's secrets could potentially be uncovered.

In reality, we still know almost nothing about it. Precisely because of that, whatever exists within this forest surely doesn't want its secrets known.

The unknown alone is enough to instill fear in others.

(They say seeing a ghost's true form is like seeing a withered pampas grass... but honestly, as the investigator, I'd much rather it be withered pampas grass than some unidentifiable something lurking in this forest.)

While pondering metaphors Elgin would never grasp, Rei glanced at the survey team members who had moved straight from lunch preparations to dinner preparations.

During the daytime investigation, no one had actually discovered anything significant.

It was expected from the start, but still, some members likely had various thoughts.

Many wore disappointed expressions when they emerged from the forest—especially the researchers—but now everyone was working together to prepare dinner.

It was probably just what they needed to shift their mood.

Near the forest, some adventurers had started cooking over ordinary campfires.

Some were simmering nuts and wild herbs, likely gathered from Treant Forest, to make stew.

"Hey, is that stuff you picked from Treant Forest? Didn't I tell you it's dangerous and you should stop?"

"It's fine. I know my plants pretty well, you know. What I gathered isn't the kind that only grows in the Treant Forest."

"...Still, I think you should stop. We packed plenty of food into the wagon, right? No need to risk danger for this."

"It just looked so tasty."

Such exchanges could be heard, but the other adventurers didn't seem particularly bothered, each enjoying their meals and conversations.

Of course, since they were about to tackle the nighttime forest survey—in a sense, the main event of this expedition—no one was fooling around with drinking.

That was only natural for adventurers.

"Can we eat the food Rei brought too? If so, maybe if we offer something from our side, we could get some of the freshly cooked food?"

"Hmm... I see your point, but there's a lot of us. Won't Rei run out of food?"

"Oh, you didn't know? They say Rei's item box holds enough food to feed thousands for years."

"No way."

Hearing the words as they walked, Rei couldn't help but interject with a look of disbelief.

It was undeniable that the Misty Ring contained a massive amount of food.

But even so, it definitely didn't hold enough food to feed thousands of people for years, as they'd just heard.

Even if they counted the untouched monster meat as food, there was no way there was that much.

Especially with people like Rei, Set, and Byune who ate more than the average adult, it was doubtful how long it would last.

"Ugh, Rei!? Wait, were you listening?"

The female adventurer who had been talking to the man was startled by Rei's presence and asked.

"Yeah. Just happened to be passing by nearby. ...Just so you know, I don't have that much food."

"R-right. Yeah, I figured as much... I just heard a rumor."

The female adventurer tried to cover up her words in a panic, but Rei wasn't angry.

Shaking his head dismissively, he said,

"More importantly, things will get busy once night falls. You might not even have time to rest properly then, so you should relax while you can now."

"...Huh? But from what I heard, only Treants come out, right? So then..."

"Well, we fought Treants and... that vine monster, but that doesn't mean that's all there is, right? There's a good chance there are plenty of other monsters out there too."

If they'd only encountered the Treant, she could nod in agreement with the female adventurer's words.

But... the fact was, Rei and the others had seen vine monsters besides the Treant.

"This vine, huh?"

Keeping information to yourself and then getting attacked would be a nightmare.

Reaching that conclusion, Rei pulled out the vine Marina had told him to collect from his Misty Ring.

Seeing the vine suddenly appear before her, the female adventurer's eyes widened in silence.

The male adventurer who had been talking with the female adventurer moments before reacted similarly, staring intently at the vine in Rei's hand from a short distance away.

It wasn't particularly thick.

But perhaps due to its length and the preconception of it being an attacking vine, it somehow seemed serpentine.

"This... is what attacks?"

Rei nodded in response to the female adventurer's timid question.

By then, several other adventurers besides those who had been talking to Rei had also focused their attention on the vine in Rei's hand.

It was only natural. After all, they could see with their own eyes what might be the monster they would have to fight.

"Rei, mind if I touch it?"

One of the adventurers nearby called out.

Then, as if following suit, others approached Rei, pleading to be allowed to touch the vine.

Normally, not many adventurers actively approached Rei, but when their own lives were on the line, it was a different story.

Such adventurers were not people Rei disliked.

Rather, they were welcome.

That's precisely why he complied with their requests and handed over the vines.

"Ah, but this is the one I need to submit to the guild, so don't damage it,"

He didn't forget to say that.

And so, a peaceful moment passed... Eventually, the sunset vanished completely, and darkness enveloped the surroundings.

...Yes, the night had arrived—the true main event of this occasion.
