By the time they had finished butchering the monsters they’d caught at sea—including Baeros—it was past 3:00 p.m. when Rei and Kenny returned to Gilm.

They had to defeat goblins several times—goblins that had been drawn to the smell of the monsters being butchered, starting with Baeros.

Although there are various monsters in the forest, every single one that attacked Rei and the others this time was a goblin.

Seeing this, Rei remarked that it was because of his good behavior, but... Kenny couldn’t bring herself to nod in agreement and instead shot him a look of exasperation.

No matter how much Kenny liked Rei, he simply couldn’t agree that Rei’s daily conduct was good.

Rei, however, seemed somewhat unconvinced by that look.

Rei would never go out of his way to pick a fight with an ordinary, law-abiding citizen.

At least, that’s what Rei himself believes.

...When Kenny thought about it that way, he tilted his head, wondering if he’d ever actually heard of Rei picking a fight with someone for no reason at all? But even setting that aside, Rei’s past actions certainly couldn’t be called virtuous.

Even from Kenny’s perspective, Rei—who could casually sever a noble’s limbs—could by no means be described as having good daily conduct.

In any case, they ate a simple lunch of sandwiches and skewered meat taken from Rei’s Misty Ring, but while that might have been enough for Kenny, it certainly wasn’t enough to fill Rei’s stomach.

“So, what are we going to do?”

“Hmm. I was thinking we should head to the Full Belly Inn—including Baeros—and have them whip up something to eat.”

“Um, can I come too?”

“Sure, go ahead. You helped me butcher the monster, so… not that this is exactly a thank-you gift, but…”

“That’s fine. …But wait, is it okay to go to the Full Belly Inn? At this time of day, aren’t they on their break after lunch?”

She asked this with a hint of concern, likely because Kenny knew just how busy the Full Belly Inn was every day.

It’s delicious, cheap, and the portions are huge.

There’s no way a place like that wouldn’t be packed; in fact, it’s full almost every day, and they even set up tables and chairs outside the dining hall.

Just how busy it is—well, that goes without saying.

(Do they call this an “open terrace” even at a diner like this, not just at a coffee shop?)

For Rei, the image of an “open terrace” was something only stylish restaurants would have.

In reality, it’s not that uncommon a style, but when Rei was in Japan, there weren’t any open-air terrace restaurants in the area where she lived, and the only way she could learn about them was on TV.

Naturally, the TV specials on open-air terraces featured trendy establishments, so it wasn’t surprising that Rei had formed that impression.

Anyway, they’re so busy… and right now there aren’t many customers, so this is a precious break—is it really okay to interrupt them? Hearing Kenny’s words, Rei thought for a moment and nodded.

“Dishot, who runs the Full Belly Inn, has a very strong passion for exploring cuisine. If someone like Dishot were to see ingredients from a monster like Baelos, it would undoubtedly spark his creative inspiration.”

Rei was absolutely certain of that.

As far as chefs go, there are probably many in Gilm who are in a similar position to Dishot.

But as far as Rei knew, there weren’t many who approached cooking with as much sincerity as Dishot.

That’s exactly why, if he were shown an unknown ingredient—especially a fish monster that would be impossible to obtain in Gilm—he would undoubtedly pounce on the opportunity with glee.

(It might be interesting to show him not just Baelos, but even ordinary fish.)

Including the monsters like Baeros, the total amount of seafood Rei had acquired during this vacation amounted to several tons.

Even without counting what Rei caught, adding in what he bought in Galanka easily brought the total to that amount.

With that much seafood, under normal circumstances, they wouldn’t have to worry about running out for several years… no, unless they ate seafood every single day, they might even go even longer without running out.

However, considering that the ones eating them are Rei, Set, and Byune, there’s a non-negligible chance they might consume it all in less than a year… or even within a few months.

“Is that so? I see. Well then, once I return the carriage, I’ll head to the Full Belly Inn.”

Kenny said this with a smile.

Rei nodded, got out of the carriage... and the two went their separate ways.

To be honest, Kenny would have preferred a more atmospheric restaurant rather than a place like Full Belly Inn if she were dining alone with Rei… but since she could eat with him, she didn’t voice her dissatisfaction.

To begin with, the main purpose of this meal was to eat the seafood Rei had caught.

Given that, they needed a place where they could bring their own ingredients and cook them… and the kind of atmospheric restaurant Kenny had in mind wouldn’t allow for that.

To be precise, there might be some places that do allow it, but at least Kenny didn’t know of any.

“Ah. Kenny helped me out with a lot of things today. I’ll treat you.”

“Hehe, I’m looking forward to it.”

Saying that, Kenny headed toward the guild with his carriage.

When Rei said Kenny had helped him, it wasn’t flattery or anything like that.

In fact, if Kenny hadn’t helped with butchering the monster, there was a high chance the meat would have been completely unusable as ingredients.

Or perhaps the edible parts would have remained attached to the bones.

Thanks to Kenny’s decent cooking skills, they were able to minimize food waste.

In that sense, for Rei, there was simply no option but to thank Kenny.

After seeing Kenny’s carriage off, she started walking down the street, intending to head to Full Belly Inn first…

“Hey, take a good look at this dried fish! I made it myself, you know!? And yet, what’s with this price?!”

“If people knew you made it yourself, nobody would buy it! Hey, you! Don’t come at me with your muscles flexed!”

……Suddenly, a familiar voice and screams drifted in from somewhere. Rei glanced in the direction of the sound, confirmed it was the person he expected, and walked away without saying another word.

He acted on the judgment that it would be best not to interrupt Bistol’s business negotiations.

From the perspective of the person negotiating with Bistoll, if Rei had spoken up, they probably would have thought of him as a godsend… but unfortunately, that wasn’t going to happen.

(Well, good luck.)

Rei knew just how hard Bisturu had been working, making dried fish almost every night.

That’s precisely why, praying that all that effort would pay off… he simply headed straight for Full Belly Inn.

“As expected, there aren’t many people around at this hour.”

Rei arrived at a spot where he could see the Full Belly Inn and looked around, but there weren’t many people in sight.

Still, compared to before—before they started the expansion—there were quite a few more people.

Still, compared to midday or early evening, the number of people in the area wasn’t high.

A slightly late lunch… or an early dinner. Or perhaps just a quick snack—there were quite a few people heading for Full Belly Inn, the nearby diners, or the food stalls for that very reason.

And so, upon entering the Full Belly Inn...

“Oh, hello, Rei-san!”

Saying this, Dishot’s wife, Enedra, stood up happily upon seeing Rei.

To Enedra, Rei was the benefactor who had saved the shop.

Of course, when Rei first became involved with Full Belly Inn, the shop wasn’t exactly on the brink of closing down.

Still, it was a fact that, in terms of profits, the shop was clearly doing worse than it is now.

However, thanks to the udon recipe Rei taught them, the shop’s sales skyrocketed.

...It was unexpected that the number of customers would grow so much that the family alone could no longer run the shop.

On top of that, with the expansion of Gilm, many people have come to work there, and thanks to them, the shop is raking in unprecedented sales almost every day.

It’s so busy, in fact, that one might think they wouldn’t have the stamina to keep up.

Despite the situation, Enedora showed no sign of fatigue as she greeted Rei.

There were still a few customers inside the diner, and upon hearing Endora’s words, they all turned their gaze toward Rei, wondering what was going on.

“Long time no see. I got my hands on some rare ingredients, so I came by to show them to you…”

“Let me see it.”

Before Rei could finish his sentence, Dishot emerged from the kitchen and said that.

He was as gruff as ever, but Rei didn’t seem to mind Dishot’s attitude as he took several fish, shellfish, seaweed, octopus, and other seafood out of the Misty Ring.

As Dishot watched, his usually surly expression gradually gave way to one of surprise... and when Ray finally produced a slice of Baeros, his astonishment became plain for all to see.

“This meat… no, fish. Given its size… is it a monster?”

“Yeah. It’s a fish monster called a Baeros. Apparently, it’s a pretty rare monster; I finally figured it out after looking it up in the library. The meat is supposed to be quite delicious.”

“...Can I touch it?”

“Sure. I’d be happy if you could cook something with this. I’ve been butchering it until just now, so I skipped lunch.”

To be precise, he had eaten some sandwiches and skewers, but to Rei, that was merely a snack, not a proper meal.

“Got it. I’ve actually got a dish I’ve been wanting to try. Just give me a sec.”

“Someone else is coming later, so please make enough for them too.”

When Rei said that, Dishot nodded, grabbed the ingredients, and headed back to the kitchen.

“Hehe, it’s been a while since I’ve seen him look so happy.”

At Endora’s words, the customers in the shop—including Rei—who had witnessed the exchange, inwardly retorted, “A happy face!?”

Though they didn’t say it out loud, there was no doubt that Rei and the customers were all thinking the same thing.

Even if Rei and the others, who hadn’t known him long, couldn’t understand it, his wife, Endora, probably could.

After Rei half-convinced himself, the shop door opened and a customer walked in.

“Ah, Rei. Sorry to keep you waiting.”

“No, I haven’t been waiting that long. Dishot’s in the kitchen cooking, so please wait a little longer.”

From the conversation between Rei and the customer—Kenny—Enedra realized who the companion Rei had mentioned was, and she placed a glass of water for Kenny on the table where Rei was sitting.

The few customers in the shop shot Rei jealous glances when they saw Kenny.

Rei was used to seeing women of rare beauty, starting with Elena, but even to him, Kenny’s features were undeniably those of a beauty, so he didn’t seem particularly bothered by those glances.

“So, what kind of food can we have?”

“I don’t know. I’ve left that entirely up to Dishot, so I won’t know until it comes out. …Oh, look, it’s already here.”

Just as Rei said, Dishot approached their table with a plate in hand.

Normally, serving food would be Enedra’s job… but since this is a test dish, he probably wants to hear Rei’s opinion.

“Give it a try. I haven’t decided on a name for it yet, but it shouldn’t be bad.”

Despite his words about it not being bad, D-Shot’s expression betrayed his confidence in the dish.

As if urged on by Dischot, Rei looked at the plate…

“Huh.”

He looked at the dish on the plate and was impressed.

Classification-wise, it was probably a soup.

What he could see was thinly sliced fish wrapped around udon noodles.

Judging by the bite-sized pieces of udon, it was easy to guess that they had been rolled up like a seaweed roll and then cut.

When I put the filling in my mouth, the flavor of the fish and the texture of the udon... along with the seaweed, all blended together seamlessly.

(Seaweed?)

Curious about the texture in my mouth, I used a spoon to break apart another piece of the filling on the plate.

As I did so, the ingredient—which I suppose you could call a “fish-wrapped udon roll”—fell apart, revealing several strips of seaweed inside.

Realizing that the udon hadn’t been wrapped in a fish fillet, but rather in seaweed, which was then wrapped in fish, Rei enjoyed the soup—no, the udon—with admiration.
