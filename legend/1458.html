"Um, then I'll have the stewed orc meat and a sandwich, along with the summer vegetable salad and fruit water."

"I'll have the same, please."

After briefly introducing themselves, Rei and Ilze entered the cafeteria. They sat down at the table vacated by customers who had just finished eating and left the shop, promptly placing their order.

The waitress swiftly cleared the dishes and utensils from the previous customers, wiped down the tables, and within mere seconds, the tables were spotless.

Though slightly surprised by this, Rei concluded that their professional skills were impressive and sat down.

...Meanwhile, Set had already returned to his aerial lookout post.

Naturally, Set would have preferred to stay with Rei, but Rei judged that Ilze's financial situation simply didn't allow for the luxury of treating Set.

(Well, it's clear not everyone is like Milein or Johanna.)

They possessed considerable power, yet they adored Set.

They'd spend the money they earned from commissions without hesitation just to show their affection for Set... Rei recalled the faces of those two, but quickly refocused his gaze on the person before him.

He'd followed along because she'd offered to treat him to a meal, but he could easily guess she wasn't actually planning to treat him out of apology or gratitude for yesterday.

Hoping it was something that could be dealt with before their food arrived, Rei spoke up.

"So? You didn't really invite me to dinner just because of yesterday, did you?"

"You figured it out, didn't you?"

Ilze probably didn't think she could get away with that excuse either.

She readily agreed with Rei's words.

...Meanwhile, quite a few people in the cafeteria were watching Rei and Ilze, but Rei, perhaps accustomed to attention, ignored the stares.

Ilze, perhaps aware she possessed beauty that drew men's attention, brushed back her shoulder-length green hair to ignore the stares.

Though she did suspect the nature of those gazes was different.

And indeed, that assumption wasn't entirely wrong.

For those who knew what kind of being Rei was, seeing him with an unfamiliar woman—one beautiful enough to be called a beauty—was enough to stir up all sorts of thoughts.

There was the gossipy curiosity of what Marina or Vihera (the female one), who were always around Rei, would think if they saw him talking to such a beauty.

Or jealousy over why only Rei could be so close to such a beauty.

There was also jealousy directed at Ilze, who, though few in number, appeared close to Rei.

Though Rei usually wore the hood of his Dragon Robe, his bare face was so well-proportioned it was often called feminine.

Among those who knew Rei's appearance, some felt a protective urge toward the small Rei despite his striking features.

...Though almost no one ever showed it outwardly.

After all, Rei was famous in Gilm in many ways.

His wild antics were widely known.

And with stunning beauties like Marina and Vihera always by his side, no one dared approach Rei carelessly.

"Oh?"

Ilze, sensing a different gaze than usual, tilted her head slightly... Probably deciding it was just her imagination, she looked back at Rei and opened her mouth to speak... But seeing the waitress bring the food, she stopped herself.

It took only a few minutes from ordering to the food arriving.

They'd ordered stewed oak meat, sandwiches, a summer vegetable salad, and fruit water.

The stew was obviously something that could be made in large batches and stored, and the salad was likely the same. The sandwich wasn't made in-house but sourced from a contracted bakery, and fruit water was naturally something that could be prepared ahead of time during this season.

In short, since all the dishes were already prepared, the only time needed was for plating.

Thinking about it that way, it was only natural they brought it out in minutes.

"Um... well then, let's eat first."

Ilse said, and Rei nodded, reaching for the sandwich.

As evident from the satisfied customers leaving, it was a reasonably tasty sandwich.

The slightly stricter evaluation might be due to the buckwheat noodles he'd eaten earlier.

That just proved how delicious the udon had been.

The fruit water not being properly chilled left Rei feeling a bit dissatisfied.

Magic items capable of chilling things like a refrigerator inevitably became expensive.

Considering the price of that earlier udon dish was about three times the usual cost for a single meal, you can imagine just how expensive they are.

Anyway, the other dishes served were decent enough, so he ate without any particular complaints.

As they ate, Rei addressed Ilze.

"So, what exactly did you need to talk to me about?"

"Hmm... munch... well, you see..."

After swallowing the stewed oak meat in her mouth, Ilze fixed Rei with a serious gaze.

Ilze would have preferred to discuss this in a more formal setting, but she considered herself fortunate to have secured this opportunity to speak with Rei at all and opened her mouth.

"Well... I heard that you, Rei, don't particularly care if someone is a noble. Is that true?"

"I can't really say anything because I don't know what you mean by 'don't care'."

"Ah, sorry. Right. I mean, normally, people hesitate when they come into conflict with nobles for some reason, but I heard Rei doesn't hesitate to oppose them?"

"Yeah. That's basically right. Of course, it depends on the person too."

The whole "nobility" thing was just a starting point for the conversation.

Still, the fact that Rei showed no sign of backing down even when facing nobles was something Ilze found pleasing.

Seeing Ilze like this, Rei wondered if she had some particular feelings about nobles.

Of course, if the enemy were someone like Elena or Daskar, he wouldn't be so quick to oppose them outright.

"I see. ...Well, if it's okay, could I ask what criteria you use to judge friend from foe?"

"What criteria? Well... I treat those friendly towards me with friendship, and those hostile towards me with hostility. That's about it."

Just as he'd expected... no, even simpler and clearer than he'd imagined. Ilze was slightly surprised by this straightforward method of distinguishing friend from foe, and at the same time, he felt a touch of envy.

Normally, even if someone was hostile, if they were a noble, one would likely play it safe and avoid antagonizing them. At least, Ilze would do that.

If you foolishly defied them and got killed... or even if it didn't go that far, if you were crippled or suffered injuries that made daily life difficult, you wouldn't be able to take revenge.

Responding with hostility to a hostile opponent was ultimately something only someone with immense power like Rei could afford to do.

That's precisely why she found herself envying Rei from the bottom of her heart for possessing that power.

"Is that so? ...To be honest, I envy you, Rei."

That must be why she spoke words she never needed to utter.

And once spoken, Ilze couldn't stop her own mouth.

"You may not be able to do everything, but you possess the power to handle most opponents however you wish. And yet, you can live exactly as you please. Truly, I envy you from the bottom of my heart."

With those words, she brought the sandwich to her mouth as if wishing it were her enemy, and... bit into it.

After swallowing the sandwich in her mouth, Ilze spoke again.

"If only I had power like Rei-san's... Then I could kill that man right now. Kill him. Kill him. Kill him. Kill him."

Deep hatred burned in her eyes as Ilze thought of her enemy.

The enemy of her kind parents and brother... her family.

Normally, she would never utter such words aloud. Yet, she found herself spilling them out to Rei like a complaint. Part of it was envy for Rei's power, but more significantly, it was the realization that her enemy was here in Gilm.

And yet, knowing the enemy was here but being unable to act was putting more stress on her than she had imagined.

"...Seems you've got quite the complicated situation going on."

After listening to Ilze's entire story, Rei continued eating without the slightest concern, even though the hate-filled glare directed at him wasn't meant for him.

"Huh!?"

At Rei's words, Ilze seemed to realize she had spoken her true feelings... feelings she would never normally reveal.

She gasped softly, then covered her mouth.

Moreover, this dining hall was filled not only with craftsmen and laborers working on the expansion, but also with many adventurers.

Several of those adventurers sensed the murderous intent emanating from Ilze and turned their gaze toward her.

But even those individuals, upon realizing the woman speaking was Rei, simply returned to their meals and conversations.

They likely acted this way because they figured, with Rei present, it wouldn't escalate into a major disturbance.

Rei, who had been judged thus, noticed several adventurers looking his way and showed a slightly displeased expression, but it vanished quickly.

He decided that listening to Ilze was the priority right now.

Not that Rei had any particular interest in Ilze—specifically, as a woman.

Regardless, Ilze, who had caught Rei's gaze, took a deep breath, seemingly unaware that she had been unconsciously radiating killing intent.

Perceiving killing intent was something only possible once one's skill had reached a certain level, and Ilze didn't possess that kind of power yet.

"Well, um. I wanted to ask you something, Rei. What do you think about revenge?"

Originally, she had planned to ask about revenge in a more roundabout way, like she had when she asked about nobles.

But since she'd already voiced the truth in her heart, it was too late to beat around the bush now.

(I drank fruit water, not ale or anything like that... And even if it had been ale, I wouldn't get drunk from that amount, right?)

Ilze fought the urge to roll around on the floor in frustration over her own blunder as she waited for Rei's answer about revenge.

Rei, showing no signs of tension or deep thought, simply opened his mouth.

"Well, I guess it's fine?"

"...Huh?"

Rei's casual words came as a surprise.

She had casually asked others what they thought about revenge before.

But most had spoken negatively about it, saying revenge was meaningless, that it wouldn't make anyone happy, that it was a bad thing.

Not everyone felt that way, of course. A few had spoken positively about revenge... Still, she hadn't expected someone like Rei to affirm the act of revenge so readily.

That's precisely why Ilze stared intently at Rei before her once more.

...Normally, being stared at intently by a beauty like Ilze would make anyone feel flustered... but in Rei's case, perhaps because Elena and the others were nearby, he didn't feel particularly flustered.

"Do you really think that?"

"Yeah. I've done my share of revenge myself, so I can't exactly stop someone else from seeking it."

That said, the revenge Rei spoke of wasn't the kind involving someone close to him being killed... it wasn't that serious.

He didn't know what kind of revenge Ilze meant, but even so, Rei was tolerant of the act of revenge itself.

(People who say revenge is fundamentally wrong—could they still say that if they were in the same situation as someone contemplating revenge? If they could just take it and endure it, then saying revenge is wrong might have some credibility...)

From Rei's perspective, most who spoke that way were people saying it from a place completely unrelated to the revenge itself.

Worse still, it wouldn't be surprising if some shameless person even said revenge was wrong after causing the whole mess in the first place.

Ilze must have thought something upon seeing Rei like that.

In the end, she said nothing more. After settling the bill, she left the cafeteria.
