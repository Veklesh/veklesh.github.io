"Well... you really went all out this time, didn't you?"

Marina looked at the earthen wall and murmured in admiration.

She probably never imagined he’d built a mud wall this elaborate.

“Wow…”

Marina’s student, Meru, also let out a voice of admiration as she looked at the earthen wall spreading out before her.

From Meru’s perspective, she was amazed at just how much effort it must have taken to build a mud wall of this scale—and, indeed, at Rei’s magical prowess.

...In reality, it wasn’t magic at all; it was a terrain manipulation skill that Death Scythe had acquired through Beast Magic.

Marina understood this, but Meru wasn’t exactly Rei’s ally, and on the way here, she’d heard that Marina’s apprenticeship would only last until spring.

In other words, since he wasn’t someone she should confide Rei’s secrets to, she had to avoid mentioning anything about Death Scythe’s skills and let him believe it was Rei’s magic.

“Being praised like that makes me a little embarrassed. …From what I can see, there aren’t any kobolds climbing up the earthen wall right now, are there?”

To change the subject, Rei says this.

Of course, that wasn’t the only reason; he actually needed to know the situation in that area as well.

As far as he could tell, there were no signs that any kobolds had scaled the earthen wall.

...Though, since this is strictly limited to what Rei can see right now, it can’t be called definitive.

In any case, now that Marina was here… and since Meru had arrived, it was certain that this earthen wall would become even more fortified.

“It seems so. …Though, it looks like there are a few of them on the other side of the earthen wall.”

Whether due to her spirit magic or her Dark Elf senses, Rei nodded in agreement at Marina’s words.

“I suppose so. But if they can’t climb this earthen wall, they’ll head straight for the Giganto Turtle. …Things might get rough over there, though.”

“Huh? Are there kobolds on the other side of here!?”

Unlike Rei and Marina, Meru hadn’t realized there were kobolds on the other side of the wall. Hearing their conversation, he looked around in a panic.

“No, I said on the other side of the earthen wall. What are you looking at us for?”

Seeing Meru’s reaction, Rei said this with a touch of exasperation.

Once again, doubts welled up inside Rei as to whether this person was really up to the task, but honestly, as long as Marina said he was fine, he had no choice but to trust her.

It wasn’t as if she had read Rei’s thoughts, but Marina spoke as she stroked Meru’s head—which was about a head shorter than her own.

“Well then, shall we give it a try? …There, don’t be nervous. I’ve told you many times, but Meru is definitely a fully-fledged spirit mage. All that’s left is to make sure she can use that ability naturally in everyday life. …Okay?”

“...Yes.”

Meru gave a quiet reply to Marina’s words and focused her attention.

Rei and Set watched her, but Marina took Rei by the hand and led him away from Meru.

Naturally, Set followed Rei, and soon no one remained around Meru.

“Why are you moving away?”

“She can’t concentrate very well when people are nearby. I think we’ll have to work on that little by little.”

“We’ll talk about that later. …Just out of curiosity, is Meru an adventurer or something?”

“Yes. Though, as you can see, she’s extremely shy, so she can’t form a party and works solo instead.”

“Wait, solo… isn’t that impossible?”

From what Rei could tell, Meru was someone who specialized in spirit magic.

To be precise, it’s unclear just how well she can wield that spirit magic, but even if she’s not exactly a complete novice when it comes to physical combat… she doesn’t exactly look like someone who excels at close-quarters fighting.

(If she could cast spirit magic instantly like Marina, relying solely on spirit magic wouldn’t be a problem. Though, well, Marina can use a bow too.)

But from what I can see, it takes Meru a fair amount of time to cast her spirit magic.

Being a solo adventurer in a situation like this... it’s not impossible, but it must be incredibly tough.

(If I could only use elemental magic like Meru, I’d probably have to hide somewhere safe and cast spells to take down enemies without getting attacked… or, better yet, I’d probably just refuse all bounty quests and specialize in gathering requests instead.)

In Rei’s case, due to her personality, the amount of money she makes, and above all, her need for magic stones, she couldn’t take that route. But if you were solo and only had Meru’s abilities, that would likely be the best option.

“Rei, look.”

It was Marina’s voice that interrupted Rei’s train of thought.

At the sound of her voice, he turned his gaze toward where Marina was looking, and there, Meru’s spirit magic was finally beginning to activate.

First to appear was a mass of water.

It was as if a slime or something similar were writhing in the air—about a meter above the ground.

“Wow.”

Seeing the water floating in the air, a gasp of admiration escaped Rei’s lips.

The mass of water was quite substantial; to Rei’s eyes, it looked like enough to fill a 25-meter pool to the brim.

Rei had heard from Marina that she was talented, but honestly, he never expected it to be this impressive.

Seeing Rei’s reaction, Marina turned her gaze toward him with a smile.

Though she didn’t say it out loud, her gaze seemed to say, “I told you so.”

Rei returned a small nod to Marina, reflecting on his own lack of discernment.

Set, standing beside Rei, was also gazing at the water floating in the air with keen interest.

Where the two of them and the dog were looking, Meru asked the water spirit to move the water floating in the air.

When the water lands on the earthen wall, it is absorbed into it as if being sucked right in.

That said, the amount of water Meru had summoned with her spirit magic was nowhere near enough to saturate all the earthen walls.

Meru likely realizes this, but the amount of water she can summon with her current power is simply the limit.

“Phew… Um, Lady Marina. What should we do next? Should we let the water soak into all the earthen walls for now? Or would it be better to summon a wind spirit to freeze just the water we have now?”

“A wind spirit to freeze it?” Rei wondered, but considering how cold it feels when the wind blows on wet skin, she figured it wasn’t entirely wrong in terms of freezing it, so she decided to wait and see for now.

“I suppose so. Since our interaction with the water spirit went reasonably well, please show me the wind spirit next.”

“Y-yes!”

In response to Marina’s words, Meru replied as such and began calling out to the spirit with renewed concentration.

Then, even faster than when summoning water, a strong, cold wind began to blow around them.

“Compared to the water spirit, the effect is kicking in much faster. Is this because Meru has good chemistry with the wind spirit?”

“No.”

Marina flatly asserted in response to Rei’s words.

Rei, who hadn’t expected such a definitive answer, shot Marina a questioning look.

But Marina, who was looking right back at Rei, didn’t seem to be joking at all.

“What do you mean, ‘no’? The wind actually started blowing faster than it did with Water, didn’t it?”

“That’s certainly true. But if you ask me which—wind or water—works better with Meru, it’s water. …It’s just that I’ve gotten a little used to it now, since I used to be nervous and flustered about using spirit magic in front of Rei.”

“Getting used to it?”

“Yes. I guess I just wasn’t used to using spirit magic in front of Rei yet. …As a result, it took me a little while to properly focus my mind and speak to the spirit.”

“……Are you really cut out to be an adventurer? If it takes you this long to attack just because people are watching, I don’t think you’d stand a chance if you were actually attacked by monsters or bandits.”

Rei was now convinced of the reason why Meru was a solo adventurer, but honestly, he still thought this was pretty bad.

Since Marina was coaching her, her abilities themselves probably weren’t bad.

But Meru’s biggest problem wasn’t her ability—it was her timidity.

In fact, it was highly doubtful whether he could even manage something as simple as talking to a guild employee. It wasn’t just Rei’s imagination that made him think that.

“That’s true, but if you want to, you can change your personality, right? If Meru really wanted to, she should be able to change her personality too.”

Even as the two of them spoke, the wind blowing around them grew rapidly colder.

Rei had his Dragon Robe, and Marina could use spirit magic, so they didn’t seem particularly bothered, but if there had been anyone here who couldn’t ignore the temperature around them, they would likely have been shivering from the cold.

Though they couldn’t see it, it was almost certain that the earthen walls had frozen solid.

And a few minutes later… Perhaps judging that he had succeeded in freezing the earthen walls, Meru turned his gaze toward Marina and, unusually, broke into a broad smile.

Although Meru usually wore a timid expression, his handsome features meant there was no doubt he warmed the hearts of those who saw him.

“What did you think? I worked pretty hard on it…”

“Instead of asking me what I think, how do you feel about it yourself? Do you think it went well?”

“Um… yes. I could tell the spirit had granted my wish.”

“I see. Well, that’s good enough. …Now, if you could just work on your personality a bit, there wouldn’t be any problems.”

“Ugh.”

Just as I was feeling happy, those words came out of Marina’s mouth.

It brought Meru back to reality.

“Well, that… I’ll get to that later…”

“I see. Fortunately, Dark Elves live longer than humans, so ‘later’ might be fine. …But if you keep saying ‘next time,’ ‘the time after that,’ and ‘the time after that again’… you’ll never actually change, you know?”

Hearing Marina’s words, Rei suddenly recalled her time in Japan.

Homework assigned by the school during long vacations.

“I’ll start today,” “No, I’ll start tomorrow,” “Actually, the day after tomorrow…” Thinking like that, summer vacation would eventually draw to a close, and he’d become so busy he wouldn’t even have time to play.

The situation with the email now and Rei back then might seem similar, but they’re actually quite different.

“Well, I think you did a good job today, at least. …Now, I was actually planning to have Meru handle the other areas too, but it looks like it’ll be faster if I do it myself. It seems Meru has picked up a few tips on spirit magic as well.”

“Stay back,” she said.

After telling Rei and Meru that, Marina moved over to the earthen wall and concentrated.

The action itself wasn’t much different from Meru’s.

But what set her apart from Meru was the time it took from focusing until she spoke to the spirit.

Marina was overwhelmingly faster than Meru.

You could say that difference was a direct reflection of their skill in spirit magic.

“Wow…”

A word of admiration escaped Meru’s lips.

She could only marvel at the speed at which the spirit magic was being wielded—a speed she herself could never hope to match.

“Please.”

With that brief command, water appeared.

And at a glance, it was clear that the amount of water was distinctly different from what Meru had produced.

Eventually, the water took the shape of a straight rod… no, more like a water snake or something, hovering above the earthen wall… and then it landed.

As a result, the water seeped into the earthen wall without any waste.

When Meru did it, the water never seeped in so neatly, and some of it even leaked onto the ground outside the earthen wall.

Considering that, it’s safe to say that Marina’s mastery of spirit magic is incomparably superior to Meru’s.

The scale was also vastly different.

While Meru could only make the water seep into a portion of the earthen wall, Marina made the water seep into the entire wall all at once.

Once she determined that the water had soaked in sufficiently, Marina’s next step was to call upon the wind spirits.

...This, too, was different from Meru.

In Meru’s case, the cold wind reached those around her—even Rei and the others, who were standing a short distance away.

Fortunately, since Rei, Marina, and Set didn’t particularly mind a breeze of that magnitude, it had no effect on them.

In any case, the wind spirit magic Marina used completely froze the entire earthen wall without affecting the surroundings at all.
