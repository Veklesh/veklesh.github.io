Inside Rei’s room at night.

There stood the Black Wolf, who should have been out to kill Rei.

The hunter and the hunted.

Despite the two of them being in the same room, there was no sign of a fight breaking out.

In fact, quite the opposite—the two were even talking to each other in a calm manner.

……If someone who knew the circumstances were to witness this scene, it wouldn’t be strange for them to harbor strong doubts about why they were able to remain like that.

That said, even if Rei were to fight the Black Wolf, he had absolutely no intention of losing in a head-on battle. That was likely why he could afford to act this way.

“So, now that your skills are clear, I want to ask you something. Why are you following me around? For someone who’s been doing that, you haven’t even tried to kill me. …Well, we did have a brief skirmish on the balcony, but that wasn’t exactly a serious fight either.”

“……”

In response to Rei’s question, the only reply he received was, as expected, silence.

Rei had no idea what the Black Wolf was thinking to act this way, but honestly, he was at a loss as to what to do.

(Should I just call Elena or something? No, but I don’t know if the Black Wolf would stay silent even if he saw Elena.)

Although the Black Wolf didn’t show any particular reaction in front of Rei, that didn’t necessarily mean it would be the same in front of Elena.

In fact, there was a chance he might overreact and start a fierce battle.

If that happened, it was certain to become a real hassle.

So, naturally, since Rei didn’t want to go through that, he wanted to handle the black wolf on his own.

“The fact that you’re nodding at what I’m saying means you actually understand what I’m saying, right?”

It was a question asked for confirmation, and the Black Wolf nodded obediently at Rei’s words.

“In that case… could it be that you simply can’t speak, or something like that?”

At the next question, the Black Wolf tilted its head.

It wasn’t a denial, nor was it an affirmation.

It was neither black nor white—more like a shade of gray.

(How am I supposed to interpret this? Does he understand what I’m saying, or not?)

Rei was troubled by the black wolf standing before him, but considering the gaze—one that could even be called innocent—with which it was looking at him, he wasn’t sure what to say in response.

After all, even though the black wolf was looking at him with an innocent gaze, that black wolf was an assassin who had killed countless people up to this point.

……That said, when it comes to killing people, if you include the wars of the Bestir Empire, Rei has taken tens of thousands of lives.

In any case, the fact remained that the wolf standing before Rei was a highly skilled assassin—a person who had even gone so far as to use drugs to make others attack Rei and her companions.

Given that, the very fact that he’s looking at me with such an innocent gaze is, in many ways, strange.

It is strange… yet, even so, Rei did not immediately feel the need to do anything about the Black Wolf standing before him.

That said, it wasn’t as if he intended to just let things slide with the Black Wolf… He fully understood that eventually—indeed, ultimately—the time would come to settle the score.

But even so… even so, Rei couldn’t bring himself to do anything to the black wolf right now.

(It’s not like I’ve grown attached to it just from talking to it… or so I think.)

He didn’t intend to, but perhaps his feelings had naturally grown.

With that question in mind, Rei reached into her Misty Ring and pulled out a chilled fruit drink.

Normally, it’s winter now.

Instead of cold fruit juice, something warm—like soup or tea—would be more appropriate.

But this is the Kerebel Duke’s mansion, where magic items with air-conditioning-like effects are used as a matter of course.

Since the effects of these magical items reached nearly every corner of the mansion—a structure so vast it could easily be mistaken for a castle at first glance—it demonstrated just how many… or how high-performance these magical items must be.

Consequently, the room where Rei was staying was naturally kept comfortable; based on Rei’s perception, the temperature was around 20°C.

It wasn’t a temperature that would make one hesitate to drink cold fruit juice.

“……?”

When the black wolf was first handed a glass of fruit juice, he wore a puzzled expression, as if to ask, “Why me?” But he was likely drawn in by the sweet-and-sour scent wafting from the glass. Eventually, he gently brought the glass to his lips.

Gently.

A broad smile spread across the black wolf’s face.

Was this the first time the Black Wolf had ever tasted fruit juice…? Or perhaps it was his first time drinking it cold.

(No, but... isn’t he a skilled assassin capable of killing even those with aliases? And he’s hired for exorbitant fees. If that’s the case, I don’t think he’d ever be short on money.)

Based on the rumors about Black Wolf, that’s what Rei thought… but judging by what he sees here, Black Wolf certainly doesn’t look like someone who indulges in luxury.

The clothes he wore were practical—in this case, for combat—but they weren’t exactly high-end.

Of course, highly practical clothing naturally comes with a corresponding price tag.

And when it comes to clothes worn by a master assassin like Black Wolf, the materials naturally need to be of a certain caliber, causing the price to skyrocket.

...That said, from what Rei could see, the clothes the Black Wolf was wearing didn’t seem to be made from particularly rare materials.

“Want another drink?”

Black Wolf nodded in response to Rei’s question.

As far as Rei was concerned, this fruit juice was something he had stocked up on during the summer and stored in his Misty Ring, so he was used to drinking it.

In reality, there are quite a few stalls selling fruit water, and the taste varies greatly from shop to shop.

The quality of the fruit they source, the combination of fruits, and the ratio of juice to water.

These factors intertwine in complex ways, and even though it’s just a drink made by squeezing fruit and diluting it with water, the taste varies greatly from shop to shop.

That’s precisely why it’s so hard to find a flavor you like, but at the same time, it’s fun to treat it like a treasure hunt.

There are also stalls that sell fruit water chilled using magic items designed for cooling.

……Though the fruit water in Rei’s Misty Ring was cooled using Marina’s spirit magic.

In any case, it seems the fruit water Rei prefers was also well-received by Black Wolf.

“Have you never had anything like this before?”

“……”

Rei’s question, posed in a casual tone.

He’d asked it in the hope that it might help him understand the Black Wolf’s background—or at least his daily life—to some extent… but the Black Wolf showed no particular reaction to it.

Was it because he had sensed Rei’s intent, or was there some other reason?

Whatever the reason, the fact remained that the Black Wolf hadn’t reacted to Rei’s words.

This made Rei wonder for a moment if this strange lull was about to end and the battle would resume, but fortunately, the Black Wolf showed no such signs and was simply savoring the fruit juice.

(Could it be... that he’s just so absorbed in the fruit juice that he didn’t hear what I said... or something like that? No, right?)

Rei had that thought, but since the Black Wolf wasn’t gulping down the fruit juice all at once but was sipping it slowly, savoring it, he couldn’t pretend to see through the situation.

(If he’s this happy about the fruit juice, does that mean there’s a chance he might let his guard down if I give him some food?)

With that thought, Rei pulled a meat bun out of his Misty Ring.

“Huh!?”

But upon seeing the meat bun, the Black Wolf reacted far more strongly than Rei had expected.

It was as if he were staring intently at the meat bun—just as his name, “Black Wolf,” suggested—as if he were eyeing his prey.

Surprised by the black wolf’s behavior, Rei began to understand, just a little, why the figure standing before him was called the “Black Wolf.”

After all, the reaction he’d just shown upon seeing the meat bun, with its appetite-whetting aroma wafting through the air, seemed almost identical to that of a wolf when it spots its prey.

(Though, since he hasn’t even cut into the bun yet, I don’t think the bun itself is really that appetizing.)

What wafted from the meat bun was a faint, sweet scent.

It was the scent of the dough.

That scent alone was enough to whet one’s appetite, but compared to the aroma that would waft from the filling once the bun was split open, it was clear which was more appetizing.

“Want to eat it?”

When Rei asked that, Black Wolf hesitated for a moment before finally nodding in response.

From Black Wolf’s perspective, he probably didn’t understand why Rei was going out of his way for him like this.

But perhaps unable to resist the meat bun’s allure, Black Wolf gently reached out and took the meat bun from Rei.

(Well, I guess from his perspective, he might be thinking, ‘Why on earth is the person I’m after giving me food?’ or something like that.)

As Rei watched, the Black Wolf was eating the steamed bun with obvious relish.

What surprised Rei just a little was that the Black Wolf was using both hands to eat the steamed bun.

That said, if we’re talking about using his hands to eat, he used both hands just fine when he drank that fruit juice earlier, so I suppose it’s not really that surprising.

Munch, munch. Watching the black wolf eat the meat bun in a manner that perfectly matched that sound, she honestly felt a wave of uncertainty wash over her for the umpteenth time, wondering what she should do next.

After all, even though she’s currently feeding him meat buns like this, the person right in front of her is actually an assassin out to take her life.

Given that, Rei knows she can’t just keep things going like this forever.

I know that. I know it, but... for some reason, I just can’t bring myself to do anything about the current situation.

“……”

As Rei pondered what to do about the Black Wolf, the Black Wolf, having finished his meat bun, turned his gaze back to Rei.

Even so, the fact that he says nothing is something Rei has long since grown accustomed to.

“Uh… want another meat bun?”

The Black Wolf nods in response to Rei’s question.

Rei doesn’t know what the Black Wolf is thinking or why he’s acting this way. He doesn’t know… but even so, for some reason, Rei finds himself wanting to take care of him.

(Training him... Yeah, I guess if you looked at it from the outside, this is probably training him. ...Though the fact that the one being trained is Black Wolf is a bit of a tricky part.)

After handing the Black Wolf another steamed bun, Rei brings one to his own mouth.

Compared to the Japanese meat buns Rei knows, the taste is indeed somewhat different.

But Rei knew exactly why the taste was different.

The ingredients—the various seasonings and spices—are different here in Elgin compared to Japan… no, compared to Earth.

The problem is that even knowing this, there’s nothing Rei can do about it.

He wasn’t particularly skilled at cooking to begin with, and whether it was these meat buns or other dishes like udon, all Rei could recall were the finished products—and even then, he only remembered the recipes in broad strokes.

And Chinese cuisine uses a wide variety of spices.

There was no way Rei could remember all of those spices, so as a result, the meat bun he was eating, while technically a meat bun, had turned into something that was, well, not quite a meat bun.

(Well, that doesn’t mean they’re bad, though.)

Because I know the taste of meat buns from Japan, there’s something a bit off about them, but for the residents of Elgin, this is the only kind of meat bun they’ve ever known.

In other words, for the people of Elgin, this is what a meat bun tastes like.

“……Huh? What’s wrong?”

While savoring the meat bun and pondering the taste that still felt slightly off, Rei suddenly noticed the Black Wolf staring intently at it.

“Just so you know, there’s no poison in it or anything like that, so you can relax. Besides, I’m eating it too, right? …Well, since we’re not eating the exact same meat bun, you might not be able to trust me completely, though.”

Rei said this, but the Black Wolf continued to stare intently at the meat bun, as if he hadn’t heard a word of it.

It was as if he were looking at prey… the same gaze Rei had seen when he took out the meat bun earlier.

(What is this?)

Rei was puzzled by the Black Wolf’s behavior, but the wolf showed no sign of noticing his gaze, continuing to stare intently at the meat bun.

What on earth was going on?

With that question in mind...

“Huh?”

Just as Rei was about to speak, the Black Wolf vanished from right in front of him.
