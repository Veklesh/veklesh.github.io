Rei gazed at Tris and smiled.

The smile he wore beneath the hood of his Dragon Robe was so sinister it gave the viewer an ominous foreboding.

The recipient of that smile was Tris, dispatched by the Spier Trading Company.

What was astonishing was that Tris showed no sign of being unsettled by Rei's smile. On the contrary, he even returned the smile.

Then again, standing before Rei right now probably wasn't easy for Tris either. A bead of sweat formed on his forehead.

"A rare material brought to Pamidor's shop, you say? ...Quite an intriguing story. Might I ask where you obtained that information?"

"...No, I'm afraid I can't divulge that information to others."

Overwhelmed by Rei's presence, Tris managed to reply.

Watching Tris from a distance, Vihera wore an impressed expression.

It was only natural. Vihera, who had acted alongside Rei and, above all, had fought against him, understood all too well the sheer magnitude of the pressure Rei projected.

"Oh? So you're saying it's a secret you'd rather keep hidden, even if it means incurring my displeasure?"

"Yes. It would violate a merchant's code of honor."

"...I see."

Rei had been staring intently at Tris, but gradually, the pressure emanating from his body weakened.

He knew Tris would never speak further, no matter what he said. More importantly, he could roughly guess where the information had leaked from.

The reason Rei, Vihera, Byune, and Set had come to Pamidor's shop today was because yesterday, in the backyard of the Dusk Wheat inn, when Pamidor brought it over, Byune had said the handle was too thick.

Furthermore, before Pamidor arrived, Rei and Byune had been doing combat training, and several people had been watching them from the Dusk Wheat inn.

Rei concluded that among them was likely someone connected to Tris's information network.

They hadn't particularly hidden it at the time, so it wasn't difficult to see the dagger with their own eyes.

...Yes, that dagger with its white blade—something unimaginable under normal circumstances.

"It seems I've gotten myself involved in something I shouldn't have."

Perhaps sensing the pressure from Rei had lessened, Tris murmured with relief.

He couldn't possibly reveal the source of the information, so it was a relief that Rei wouldn't do anything further to him.

The main reason Tris had come to Pamidor's shop today was because he didn't know what the material of the dagger he'd had yesterday was.

If it was an unknown material, she thought, it could be a big business opportunity.

...She never imagined that material would be from a Silver Lion.

Given Rei's involvement, it wasn't unreasonable for her to have realized that, but unfortunately, all Tris had been told was that a man named Pamidor possessed an unknown material.

Rei's name wasn't mentioned.

Considering that, he had some reservations about the source of the information. Still, learning that using Silver Lion material could forge a white blade was significant.

What kind of weapon could be forged from Silver Lion material was completely unknown information.

Since there were witnesses seeing Pamidor hand over that unknown weapon to Rei... or more accurately, to Rei's companion, Byune, those with sharp wits would likely catch on.

Even so, it was an undeniable fact that only a few people possessed that knowledge.

"Yeah, you know... sticking your neck into strange business will get you hurt, you know?"

Rei, perhaps sensing Tris's situation, said this in a slightly softer tone than before.

"Yes. But if you ever need to supply weapons made from Silver Lion materials anywhere, please do consider us."

Rei nodded at Tris, who delivered this with his usual smile that didn't reach his eyes.

"If the opportunity arises, that is."

Though Rei currently had no intention of supplying items made from Silver Lion materials to anyone else, that didn't mean it would always be that way.

While Rei rarely struggled with money, the possibility of such a situation arising was still very real.

In such a case, he vaguely thought, he might consider relying on Tris, who stood before him.

Of course, as evident from the lack of warmth in his eyes, Tris was not someone he could fully trust.

Likewise, the Spier Trading Company he belonged to probably wasn't much different in that regard.

But... seeing how he'd ended up at Pamidor's shop, it was undeniable that he was a capable individual.

Moreover, Tris knew Rei's reputation and character.

If he tried to deceive them, it was obvious what kind of disaster would befall him and the Spier Trading Company.

Precisely because he understood this, Rei judged that while Tris might not be trustworthy as a merchant, he could be relied upon.

Tris probably knew how Rei viewed him. Without asking for more—neither to sell him Silver Lion materials nor to sell him weapons forged by Pamidor using those materials—he bowed his head.

"Well then, I'll take my leave now. ...Ah, Mr. Pamidor. Even without using Silver Lion materials, the weapons you forge are truly first-rate. So next time, I'll be sure to stop by for your supplies."

"...Hmph. Do as you please."

Pamidor replied curtly, clearly displeased by Tris's words.

Tris, having learned the problematic dagger was made from Silver Lion materials, had backed down without fuss. That likely earned him some measure of respect. Though clearly displeased, he hadn't outright refused without discussion.

For Pamidor, this was likely the maximum concession he could make.

Understanding this, Tris flashed his usual smile that didn't reach his eyes, gave a small bow, and left the shop.

(Even at times like this, her eyes don't smile? ...Could it be that Tris's eyes not smiling is just a habit or something like that? ...No way.)

After seeing Tris off, Rei found himself pondering such things. He confirmed they were now the only ones left in Pamidor's shop before speaking.

"That was a disaster."

"Yeah. Damn it, I don't like those kinds of guys. They're too persistent. ...Well, at least he seemed to know basic manners, so that's something."

Pamidor sighed, looking slightly displeased. His expression seemed even more menacing than usual, and Rei thought that if someone unaware encountered him on the streets at night, they'd likely scream in terror.

Not that he'd read Rei's thoughts, but Pamidor addressed Byune.

"I'll bring the adjusted dagger right away. Just wait a moment."

"Mm."

To Byune, Pamidor's fierce expression didn't seem to bother her much; she replied curtly as usual.

Perhaps finding Byune's lack of fear toward his own face unusual, Pamidor's earlier sullen expression vanished. He flashed a small smile and headed toward the back of the shop... the forge.

"You said his name was Pamidor, right? I've hardly spoken to him, but he's quite a distinctive character."

Watching Pamidor head toward the forge, Vihera murmured softly.

"Huh? Actually, I'm more surprised you've ever spoken to Vihera. You two don't have any connection, do you?"

"Oh, but we have! Remember that party we had a little while back? The one where we all ate Silver Lion meat? I had a chance to talk to him a little there."

"Ah, that time."

Given how many guests had come for the party, it wasn't strange for Vihera and Pamidor to have spoken.

Rei accepted that explanation, but he couldn't help wondering what they had talked about.

They shared interests—or hobbies—like combat and blacksmithing, but Rei still couldn't imagine their conversation flowing easily.

Still, he figured even if the conversation didn't flow, at least Vihera wouldn't be scared just by looking at Pamidor's face.

"Hey, sorry to keep you waiting. Here it is, this."

Intrigued, yet slightly afraid to ask, Rei was about to inquire what they had talked about when Pamidor emerged from the forge carrying a dagger.

The dagger Pamidor held didn't look much different from the one Ray had seen yesterday, at least to an outsider.

But that was only natural. For the child Byune, the only issue was the thickness of the handle; the blade length hadn't changed from yesterday.

Byune took the dagger handed to him and silently drew it from its sheath.

What emerged was the same pure white blade he'd seen yesterday... a blade as white as fresh snow.

(More than a weapon, it's an art piece.)

For nobles or merchants with an eye for beauty, it wouldn't be strange at all if they offered any amount of money to buy that pure white blade.

While captivated by the blade's beauty, Rei's next thought inevitably turned to its length.

(Longer than a dagger, shorter than a longsword... A middle sword? No, it leans slightly more toward a dagger... Is it about the length of a wakizashi?)

Rei pondered this while gazing at the pure white blade, though he didn't actually know the precise length of a wakizashi.

His impression came solely from seeing them a few times in period dramas back in Japan.

"Here, give it a swing. If it feels fine, then it's finished."

"Hmm."

Prompted by Pamidor, Byune moved to a spot a little further away... somewhere where swinging the pure white dagger wouldn't hit anyone, then swung the blade.

A sharp sound, like slicing through air.

It didn't match the sound produced when Rei... or any other high-rank adventurer swung their weapon, but it was still a sound the old Byune couldn't have produced.

Ever since it was decided they'd form a party in the spring, the results of the combat training Byune had undergone were showing.

She continued swinging and thrusting the dagger several times... until several minutes passed.

"Hmm."

Byune, who had been swinging the dagger, stopped her movements and nodded to Pamidor with satisfaction.

Though she usually needed Vihera to interpret her words, this time he understood her meaning without needing anything said.

"I see. You're satisfied?"

A smile appeared on Pamidor's face.

...Even that smile could be described as sinister, yet Byune showed no fear, bowing his head slightly.

"Mm."

Though he didn't say it aloud, everyone watching could understand that Byune was expressing his utmost gratitude to Pamidor.

"I see. I'm glad you're pleased. ...Well, truth be told, I was actually making it for Rei."

Perhaps embarrassed by the gratitude of someone like Byune—a girl who, despite her expressionless face, was undeniably beautiful—Pamidor flushed slightly and looked away.

"Strengthening Byune's combat capabilities was the biggest concern when forming this party. So I'll be thanking you too."

"Of course, I'd like to thank you too."

Hearing words of thanks from both Rei and Vihera, Pamidor's face flushed not just on her cheeks, but all the way to her ears and the nape of her neck.

Pamidor and Rei had known each other for quite some time.

Precisely because of that, he understood Rei's personality well. That's why hearing Rei express gratitude like this made him both happy and embarrassed.

Besides, even though he loved his wife, Pamidor was still a man.

There was no way he wouldn't be pleased to hear words of thanks from a beauty like Vihera.

(Is there really a market for a man's tsundere personality... especially a man who's like the big boss of thieves? Well, I guess there must be, since Pamidor managed to get married...)

Watching Pamidor like that, Rei pondered such things.

If Pamidor knew what Rei was thinking right now, it would probably... no, definitely lead to a brawl right here.

"Hmm."

As if to break that tense standoff, Byune held out a small cloth bag to Pamidor.

"Huh? What is it?"

Perhaps embarrassed that his flushed face had been seen, Pamidor gruffly took the cloth bag and peered inside.

Inside were numerous coins.

There were no gold or platinum coins, but counting the silver coins mixed in with the many copper coins, there were probably over ten.

In other words, it was practically the same as if gold coins were inside.

"Hey, what's this supposed to mean?"

"Hm?"

"It's the least I could do... to show my gratitude,"

Pamidor hesitated slightly at Vihera's interpretation... but eventually concluded it was Byune's sincerity. He accepted it quietly.
