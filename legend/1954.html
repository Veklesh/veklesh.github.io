The man, who has finally realized how strange the men named Rei (all male) holding him captive are, says this, but Rei and the security guards have no obligation to engage with him.

Or perhaps, if the three men other than Rei had been wearing security uniforms, the man might have immediately recognized their true identities... but for this particular incident, they’d changed into plain clothes just to be safe.

That’s precisely why the man doesn’t know who is standing before him… or what they were after.

“What should we do? Can we leave this guy to you? If we’re trying to extract information, that’s your specialty, after all.”

“If that’s the approach, aren’t you the one who’s more used to it?”

The guard deliberately avoids mentioning Rei’s name as he says this.

If they made it clear right now who they were—and who was holding the man down—it would be like handing the information to him.

In a situation like this, they likely know that the fact the man has no information at all serves as a weapon to unsettle him.

Rei, too, seemed to have caught on to the guard’s reasoning; he gave a slight nod before speaking.

“It’s not that I’m bad at extracting information, but if I do it, things will get pretty rough. It’ll be something close to torture.”

Rei’s words were by no means wrong.

After all, when extracting information from bandits, the quickest way is to demonstrate one’s seriousness by severing their fingers or limbs before prying the information out of them.

...Lately, though, word has spread that Rei is known as a “Bandit Eater,” and people often give him the information right away without him having to resort to such tactics.

In any case, Rei isn’t capable of extracting information through persuasion.

(Was it the good cop and the bad cop? I feel like there was something like that, but I can’t pull it off on my own, and security guards can do that kind of thing as a matter of course, so it’s useless knowledge right now.)

The man who had been listening to their conversation must have realized that they weren’t trying to deceive him, but were simply speaking honestly.

He opened his mouth, looking flustered.

“Wait! Like I said before, laying a hand on me is the same as declaring war on the Royal Faction! Do you really understand that!?”

“That’s easier said than done. To begin with, if you’re being made to do a job like bringing Akabu all the way here, you’re clearly not some important figure in the Royal Faction, are you? Are you planning to say, ‘Actually, I’m a member of the Duke’s household’? Well, if you work for the Duke’s household, then maybe…”

“Guh!”

Rei’s words must have hit the nail on the head.

The man seemed at a loss for words, unable to say anything.

Or perhaps… That was the thought behind Rei’s question, but unfortunately, it was clear the man was by no means a member of the Duke’s family… no, even without going that far, he was clearly not a noble.

“Anyway, moving on. It looks like we can get a lot of information out of this guy for now…!?”

Let’s take him to the interrogation room and make him spill what he knows.

Rei was about to say that, but he let go of the man he’d been holding back—almost reflexively—and leaped away from the spot.

“Get back!”

Ignoring the cold sweat on his back, he shouted.

They must have sensed the intense sense of urgency in Rei’s voice. Without hesitation, the guards retreated from the spot and moved to Rei’s side.

Left behind in the center of this underground space were the man who, just seconds ago, had been pinned to the ground by Rei, and the men in red cloth, bound by rope and devoid of any sense of self.

The mindless men in red cloth show no particular reaction to whatever happens.

However, the man who had been restrained just seconds ago was startled by his sudden release… no, at first he was confused, not understanding what had happened, but he quickly realized he could move freely again, and a look of doubt crossed his face… and when he realized where he was, an expression of disbelief spread across his face… and he began to run away from the spot.

He tried desperately to get even a little distance away from where he was... but his actions were fatally too slow.

It was almost simultaneous: the moment Rei drew the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring and readied them, and the moment the space in the very center of the underground chamber split open.

It was as if the space were tearing like paper... and in the next instant, dozens upon dozens of pink tentacles, drenched in some kind of liquid, burst out from the rift.

“Waa, waaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!”

The man ran while screaming, but whether it was his screams or his frantic movements that caught the tentacles’ attention, they pursued him relentlessly.

“H-help me! Help me, please! Help me!”

The man pleaded desperately, but of course, the tentacles weren’t about to listen to his cries and come to his aid… Moving through the air at arrow-like speed, the tentacles touched the man’s body with their tips.

The tip of the tentacle wasn’t particularly sharp, nor did it resemble a mouth in any way… yet, the tentacle had its effect.

Thud.

Thud, thud, thud, thud.

As that sound echoed, the tentacle writhed as if a snake had swallowed something whole.

At the same time, the man’s body rapidly withered away… and in less than ten seconds, it collapsed to the floor, reduced to nothing but skin and bones.

...That corpse was the same one Rei had seen while searching the mansion.

(I see. I’d been wondering how such corpses were created, but this is how they’re produced.)

The three guards standing nearby gasped in shock, but Rei’s sense of urgency—which he had felt just seconds ago—had strangely vanished, allowing him to calmly observe the scene before him.

Of course, that was only because he had been able to clearly identify that unknown entity… and while Rei knew full well that the pink tentacles were by no means an opponent he could let his guard down against,

Still, the fact that he was calmer than the guards was likely due to all the experiences he’d had up to this point.

That said, seeing those pink tentacles—not just one or two, but dozens of them emerging from the rift in space—made it clear that this was no entity they could easily deal with.

Rei stepped forward, ready for the tentacles to attack at any moment.

The guards were clearly shaken, and that was precisely why Rei wanted to assert his presence and calm them down before they panicked and started acting erratically.

“Calm down.”

He murmured to the guards, keeping his voice low so the tentacles wouldn’t hear.

Whether the guards could hear Rei’s words or not… or perhaps even if they could hear them, their minds couldn’t process them—unfortunately, they showed no sign of reacting to her words and remained unable to speak.

(Actually, to avoid drawing the attention of that pink tentacle, it might be best if they just stayed silent… but the problem is, can they really stay silent?)

The corpse of the man, reduced to nothing but skin and bones, fell to the floor, and the pink tentacles moved through the air as if searching for something… or rather, as if signaling that they still weren’t satisfied.

Watching those tentacles, he pondered what to do... and then, in the next instant, the tentacles suddenly stopped moving, only to spring into action all at once.

Just as before, they flew straight ahead like arrows slicing through the air... but their targets weren’t Rei and the others—it was the mindless Red Cloths.

Rei wondered why they were targeting the red cloths instead of them, but the likely explanation was that the red cloths were simply closer to the spatial fissure.

However, since they lacked self-awareness, the red cloths showed no particular reaction even when they saw the pink tentacles flying toward them.

They simply watched the tentacles approach in silence... and the next moment, upon contact, just like the man before them, their internal organs, bodily fluids, and everything else were absorbed by the tentacles’ tips.

And it wasn’t just one or two of them; every single Red Cloth present silently accepted the tentacles… and eventually, just like the first man, every last one of them collapsed to the ground as corpses, nothing but skin and bones.

Rei watches this scene without feeling anything in particular.

Well, he had hoped that this would be enough to satisfy them and that the tentacles would vanish back through the spatial rift, but he realized that was a naive thought when the tentacles didn’t return to the rift—instead, their tips were directed straight at Rei and the others.

“Damn it, just run for now! Get back to the mansion!”

The tentacle was targeting them.

Realizing this, Rei shouted quickly. Fortunately, the guards had apparently come to their senses, and without hesitation, they began running toward the stairs they had descended.

This situation was likely completely unexpected for the guards, but having served as guards up until now, they didn’t freeze in shock at the sight before them.

But… naturally, the tentacles weren’t about to let their prey escape.

Ignoring Rei, who was still nearby and not trying to escape, the tentacle first reached out toward the guard who was attempting to flee.

As before, the tentacle moved swiftly to capture its prey, but...

Slash!

In the next instant, the tentacle, which had been stretching out straight, was severed by the blade of the Death Scythe held by Rei.

However, there was a hint of dissatisfaction on Rei’s face after severing the tentacle.

(It’s tough. No, maybe I shouldn’t say tough—should I say it’s hard to cut through?)

If the pink tentacle were just—though that’s not quite the right way to put it—a tentacle, the Death Scythe would have sliced right through it without any resistance at all.

Yet, the blade of the Death Scythe that Rei swung met with resistance against that tentacle.

Even though it was a Death Scythe infused with magic.

There was no doubt that this tentacle could not be damaged by the longswords wielded by the average adventurer.

Rei was certain of this, but before his eyes, the tentacle severed by the Death Scythe thrashed about like a snake or something… and eventually crumbled into dust and vanished.

It was clearly an abnormal sight.

Then again, it was obvious from the moment a rift appeared in space and the tentacles began to emerge that this was far from ordinary.

“...It would be easier for us if you’d just give up willingly.”

The disgusted tone in Rei’s voice was likely because a new tentacle had emerged from the spatial rift and appeared in this world.

And there was no doubt that the new tentacle had set its sights on Rei—the only one left here—as its prey, rather than the guards who had already disappeared after climbing the stairs.

“For now, there’s no doubt the Royal Faction summoned this troublesome thing. What a pain…!”

As if to cut Rei off before she could finish her sentence, all the tentacles were launched toward her.

No matter how fast the tentacles were, as long as they moved in a straight line like before, there wouldn’t have been a problem.

But… perhaps having learned from the tentacles being severed earlier, the ones flying toward Rei now included not only those moving in a straight line but also some that changed their trajectory mid-air.

“Troublesome! Flying Slash!”

He fired Flying Slashes at the tentacles that had changed course to keep them at bay, while simultaneously severing them with the return of his blade or using the Twilight Spear in his left hand to cut them down.

Perhaps because he couldn’t simply slice them cleanly with the Death Scythe, even a single strike with the Twilight Spear left a strong resistance in his hand.

Even though the tentacles were severed midway, those still connected to the rift in space kept attacking Rei one after another.

In fact, the length of the tentacles was replenished exactly as much as Rei severed, and the number of tentacles extending from the spatial rift only increased further.

“Multi-Slash!”

Following the Death Scythe slash, two additional slashes were unleashed as a skill effect.

A single slash severed several tentacles, and the follow-up slashes cut through other tentacles near the ones struck by the first blow.

Even as he feels the enemy’s resistance with each strike of the Twilight Spear, he continues to sever tentacles one after another.

However, the number of tentacles emerging from the rift in space shows no sign of decreasing.

(What should I do? Physically, I still have plenty of stamina left... but I can’t keep this up forever.)

While pondering this in the back of her mind, Rei’s Death Scythe and Twilight Spear continue to sever the pink tentacles one after another.

Normally, the severed tentacles would pile up on the floor, and it wouldn’t be surprising if there were no room left to stand.

Yet she was able to move without issue because the tentacles that fell to the floor were instantly turning to dust.

That’s precisely why Rei hadn’t accidentally stepped on a tentacle and lost her balance…

“Rei, over here! Come back!”

Hearing that voice suddenly, Rei continued fighting the tentacles while slowly making her way back toward the stairs she had descended.
