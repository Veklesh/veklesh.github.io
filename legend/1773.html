“So? You’re saying you still haven’t found the mastermind behind all this red cloth being handed out today, then?”

As Vihera speaks while savoring the summer vegetable soup, Rei nods while eating freshly baked bread.

Incidentally, the reason they were able to enjoy fresh summer vegetable soup at this time of year—when it was already winter—was, naturally, because they were using ingredients stored in Rei’s Misty Ring.

It was precisely because they had purchased and stockpiled large quantities during the summer that they were able to eat summer vegetables that were still fresh even now in winter—so fresh, in fact, that one might believe they had been picked just this morning.

The soup Marina made using those summer vegetables was nothing short of exquisite.

……Though, if you asked her, she’d claim it tasted better than usual because it contained a secret ingredient called “love.”

“Apparently, the guy handing out the red cloths is pretty cautious. There are plenty of people who’ve received one from him, but when you ask where he actually lives or which inn he’s staying at, no one has a clue.”

“I don’t know if this is the right way to put it, but it’s what you’d call a fleeting relationship.”

“Isn’t that a bit off the mark?”

Vihera interjected at Marina’s remark, which she made while enjoying a dish of white fish wrapped in herbs and baked in a magic item oven.

“Whether it’s a passing acquaintance or whatever, you haven’t forgotten that we’re leaving in three days, have you?”

“Yeah. Anyway, I don’t really have anything I need to pack.”

At Elena’s words, Rei looked at his right arm.

Although Rei was staying at the Dusk Wheat inn, there were almost no personal belongings in his room.

Normally, if you stay at the same inn for a long time, your personal belongings tend to pile up in the room... but in Rei’s case, he keeps almost all of his personal belongings stored inside his Misty Ring.

That’s why his room is so clean.

...And precisely because the room is so clean, it makes it very easy for Lana, the proprietress of the Dusk Wheat inn, and the other staff to clean, which is one of the reasons they’ve come to look favorably upon Rei.

“If we’re going, I could leave right this very minute. …Right, Set?”

“Growl? Grrrrrrr!”

"Leave it to me!" says Set, who is happily devouring a whole roasted Yellowbird.

Next to him, Yellow, who is also eating, glances briefly at Rei with a “Huh?” but immediately returns to his meal.

The whole roasted Yellowbird isn’t just a simple roast, as the name suggests.

Rei had asked a familiar diner to prepare it; they stuffed the bird with mushrooms, nuts, and vegetables before slowly roasting it over a low flame.

The surface of the Yellowbird is coated with a honey-based sauce, and it’s a dish that took a great deal of time to prepare, over and over again.

Naturally, given all that time and effort, the price was commensurate with the cooking fee.

But take just one bite, and you’ll undoubtedly agree that the flavor is well worth the price.

Just look at how eagerly and wholeheartedly Yellow is devouring it—it’s obvious.

“At times like this, I envy someone like Rei who’s in such a carefree position,”

Watching Rei and Set, Elena murmured thoughtfully.

As someone known by the nickname “Princess General,” Elena knew she needed to properly explain her situation if she were to leave Gilm, even if only temporarily.

Well, if she could leave Ara behind like she did when she was traveling with Rei and the others, she wouldn’t have to worry so much… but this time, she needs to officially leave Gilm, and that includes Ara.

Given that, she had to inform not only the lords governing Gilm, such as Daskar, but also the members of the Noble Faction staying in Gilm of her departure, and she also needed to speak with the relatives of the Royal Faction’s key figures.

...I imagine this is an incredibly tedious process for Elena, but if she were to cut corners here, it would lead to her father, Duke Kerebel, being looked down upon, so she simply cannot afford to slack off.

“That’s different for everyone, isn’t it? In fact, some people actually enjoy that kind of groundwork. …Though I don’t really understand it myself.”

As a former princess of the Bestir Empire, she had seen more than her fair share of nobles before she fled.

Among those nobles, there were some who actually enjoyed laying the groundwork.

Perhaps engaging in such behavior allowed them to feel more strongly that they were indeed nobles.

To be honest, Vihera’s true feeling was, “How on earth can they be bothered with such a hassle…?”

“I know that, but still…”

Elena trailed off and took a bite of her sandwich.

Though she didn’t say it outright, everyone understood what Elena meant.

Ultimately, Elena’s true nature was that of a warrior, not a noble. …Though she reverts to being a woman whenever Rei is involved.

Still, whether it was good luck or bad luck for Elena that she could handle her duties as a duchess’s daughter so flawlessly was anyone’s guess.

“Come to think of it, the snow’s been late this year.”

Perhaps feeling sorry for Elena, Rei blatantly changed the subject.

To be precise, it was already snowing—at least enough for light flakes to drift—but he was referring to snow that actually accumulates.

That said, since Rei wasn’t particularly fond of snow, he actually preferred it this way—it was less of a hassle.

“Snow, huh. It’s falling now, but at this rate, it doesn’t look like it’ll stick.”

Marina gazed into the distance.

Thanks to Marina’s spirit magic, the courtyard is protected from the cold, snow, rain, and wind.

If I ever want a little breeze, I just ask Marina, and she’ll have the wind spirits blow a breeze, so I can control that aspect quite freely.

As for lighting, since we’ve brought magical items that provide light, we can see outside the space sealed off by her spirit magic even at night without any trouble.

Beyond that space, there was a scene of light snowflakes drifting down—a sight that had become a common occurrence over the past few days.

But, as Marina said, it’s nowhere near enough to accumulate.

It would undoubtedly be melted by tomorrow morning.

“When it snows, it causes all sorts of trouble. If possible, I’d really like things to stay just like this.”

At Rei’s words, everyone present nodded in agreement.

If she were a child, a young Byune, she might be happy that it was snowing... but Byune, having struggled since she was little, showed no sign of joy even when it snowed.

Then again, the Byune family—the Fraut estate—is quite spacious, and since they naturally have to shovel the snow whenever it falls, it’s not surprising that Byune has come to dislike it.

“Why don’t we just use Marina’s spirit magic to make the whole of Gilm look like this garden?”

“Listen, Vihera. Don’t ask me to do the impossible. If it were just the size of this garden, that might be one thing…”

At Vihera’s words, Marina immediately retorted, “Don’t ask for the impossible.”

That said, Vihera hadn’t really meant to suggest that she could actually do such a thing; she’d merely brought it up as a topic of conversation.

……Though if Marina had said she could do it, she might have asked her in earnest.

Watching the two of them, Rei also wondered if there was any way to deal with this snow.

If this were just a place he happened to be staying at, Rei probably wouldn’t have given it much thought.

But this was Gilm, Rei’s base of operations.

If it would make life here more comfortable, that would be ideal.

That said, he couldn’t think of a single way to deal with the falling snow.

(Like a dome, maybe—covering the whole of Gilm with a roof? No, that’s not feasible. Besides, if I covered it with a roof, not only would snow and rain be blocked, but wind and sunlight wouldn’t get in either. So, a retractable one?)

Rei pictured a massive roof hovering over Gilm, opening up come spring… but while imagining it was easy, he realized actually pulling it off would be incredibly difficult.

Especially since the current Gilm is scheduled to expand by 50% through ongoing construction.

Rei couldn’t possibly imagine that it would be feasible to cover every part of such a place with a roof.

In fact, if handled poorly, it would likely be even more difficult than the current expansion project, and technical problems would surely arise.

“Is it impossible?”

“Hm? What’s impossible?”

Perhaps having overheard Rei’s mutter, Marina suddenly asked.

…Though part of it was probably just that she’d grown tired of going along with Vihera’s story.

“No, I just thought that if we were going to deal with snow and rain, we might as well put a roof over the entire Gilm. But I figured doing something like that would be even harder than the expansion work.”

“Well… I suppose so. I just didn’t think there was anyone who would come up with something like that.”

Perhaps Rei’s suggestion was so unexpected that Marina let out a gasp of both exasperation and admiration.

The others—Elena, Ara, Vihera, and Byune—were also looking at Rei with similar expressions.

“Don’t look at me like that. I just thought of it. Whether it’s actually feasible is a whole other story.”

“I suppose so. As for whether it’s feasible… well, if we didn’t consider the effort involved, it might be possible. But then, while it would keep out the snow and rain, sunlight and wind wouldn’t get in either, right? That would cause all sorts of problems.”

“We could make the roof foldable or something—so it can be stowed away—and only put it up in the winter… I know, I know it’s not possible. That’s why I said it, didn’t I? It was just an idea.”

Seeing her gaze turn back to him—and clearly with more exasperation than admiration—Rei cut himself off before finishing his thought.

“Yeah, but even if it’s impossible to put a roof over all of Gilm, wouldn’t it be possible to cover just the main streets or something?”

As Rei said this, an image popped into his mind: the shopping street in his hometown back in Japan.

It was exactly the kind of street commonly known as an “arcade shopping district.”

Even if covering the entire city of Gilm with a roof was impossible, Rei felt it might be possible if limited to just the main streets.

And as for something like deploying the arcade roof only on rainy or snowy days—it was probably… really, just probably, but it shouldn’t be impossible.

“Hmm… Well, if it’s just the main street, we might be able to make it work if we push ourselves… but I think a retractable roof would be a bit difficult. It would be too much trouble. We’d probably have to hire adventurers, like mages, to handle it.”

There might be some who’d do it for free, but if that became the norm, it could cause problems for adventurers and the guild in the future.

In that case, we’d still have to hire adventurers.

But hiring adventurers every time it rains or snows is a hassle, and above all, it requires payment.

If we could expect sales to increase enough to cover it, we might be able to put up with that… but for many shops, customer traffic doesn’t change much whether it rains or snows, so from their perspective, there’s no point in going out of their way to pay for it.

“...I feel like if the shops don’t sit down and discuss this properly, all sorts of problems are bound to arise in the future.”

Having reached the same conclusion as Rei, Vihera murmured.

“There are quite a few former adventurers around, so I get the feeling we’ll be able to work something out.”

“Ah, there are people like that, too.”

Being an adventurer is, fundamentally, mostly physical labor.

Naturally, as they get older, some of them can no longer push themselves as hard.

Of course, this isn’t true for everyone—there are some who continue working on the front lines as adventurers even into old age—but those are merely a few exceptions.

Beyond that, it’s not uncommon for people to lose limbs during a quest, or even if it doesn’t go that far, to become unable to move due to the aftereffects of an injury.

What happens to those who quit adventuring for such reasons?

Some may return to their hometowns, but others are unable to do so for various reasons.

It’s not uncommon for such people to use the money they earned during their adventuring days as capital to start some kind of business, and some get hired by a shop.

That’s why, if we’re talking about whether what Rei suggested is possible, it’s probably doable.

“Yeah, I suppose so. I’ll try talking to the Guild about it. Of course, it ultimately depends on what the people on Main Street decide, and we’ll need to ask the carpenter if it’s actually feasible.”

Marina, who seemed slightly more enthusiastic, said this.
