"Oh, Rei. Finally got here. Took longer than I thought it would."

As Rei entered the guild's warehouse—now better described as a makeshift alchemy lab—that voice reached his ears.

Rei and Vihera both turned their gaze toward the source of the voice.

There, as Rei had expected, stood Azimov.

He wore an extremely satisfied expression, likely delighted to be fully immersed in his work—or rather, his hobby.

"Judging by that look, so it was Azimov who built the detector to find this Ambris after all?"

The success of creating the Ambrith detector, as he'd heard about in the carriage.

Though Rei had been surprised upon first hearing it, it wasn't surprising that Azimov, capable of creating a powerful magic item like the Twilight Spear, could find the remnants of Ambris and build a detector from them.

At least, that was Rei's understanding, and judging by the current state of the warehouse, his prediction wasn't too far off.

"The Death Scythe was a bit beyond me, but the Twilight Spear was a magic item I made myself. Working from that wasn't all that difficult."

"...I'd still think that's plenty difficult enough. But you mean only I can use it?"

"Yeah. If I'd had more time, I could've adjusted it so others could use it too... but unfortunately, the priority was getting a working prototype as quickly as possible."

As Azimov muttered regretfully, the surrounding alchemists wore similarly disappointed expressions.

(They're all the same.)

The thought flashed through Rei's mind, but he decided now wasn't the time for that and spoke up.

"So, summoning me at this hour means you want to confirm whether that detector actually works?"

He felt a touch of exasperation, but at the same time, he understood it was probably unavoidable.

Given the current situation in Gilm, Ambrith was the top priority threat that needed to be dealt with.

Given that, if the detector was ready, it was imperative to confirm as soon as possible whether it functioned normally... and if it didn't, to determine why.

And if it worked without issue, the next task was to make it usable not just for Rei, but for others as well.

(Come to think of it, the fact that Vihera and I were just waking up meant these guys had pulled an all-nighter? ...Well, maybe that's not surprising.)

Since they're people who turned their hobby into their job, pulling all-nighters probably isn't too much of a struggle for them.

"Ah, right. So, could you test it out for me right away?"

With that, he handed me a magic item about the size of a palm.

Its shape resembled a flattened crystal, as if it had been crushed.

However, inside the crystal was a black mist... something similar to the remnants of Ambrith.

"Another strange shape... So, how do I use this?"

"Just channel your magic into it. After that, the black stuff inside the crystal will move toward where the Ambris is. Follow it, and you should reach the Ambris."

"...But what if there are multiple Ambris? What happens then?"

"It should indicate the location of the closest Ambris... I suppose."

The slight hesitation in Azimov's voice was likely because he hadn't actually tested it himself.

The fact that only Rei could use this magic item was holding them back.

"So, you're telling me to try using this."

When Rei asked, Azimov and the other alchemists nodded.

"Understood. ...Alright, I'll do it?"

With that declaration, he channeled magic power into the detector in his hand.

The remnants of ambrosia inside the detector slowly began to stir.

"Whoooooo!"

The alchemists watching all cried out in unison.

They probably had confidence that it would work properly, since it was a magic item they had made themselves.

But they hadn't actually tested it... it was essentially nothing more than a theoretical concept.

That very fact seemed to make their joy at seeing the magic item actually working all the stronger.

(Huh... So that's how it works.)

Looking at the magic item in his hand, Rei pondered with admiration.

Inside the flat crystal, the black mist moved diagonally forward to the right.

And within that flat crystal, it gradually shifted from the right diagonal front... slowly, very slowly, but steadily moving toward the center.

This meant the detector had found Ambris moving from right to left.

"So Ambris is still moving even at this hour. ...And, is it okay for me to take this detector? It uses Ambris residue, right? Meaning, if I take this, they won't be able to make another detector, will they?"

I recall him saying that while it's currently only usable by Rei, eventually other adventurers will be able to use it too. I ask him about this, and Azimov responds with a confident smile and a nod.

"What's inside that detector isn't all the Umbrus residue extracted from the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear. We've secured a sufficient amount here, so don't worry."

"You're thorough. ...So, I can take this detector with me, right?"

"Yes. Since only you can use it, honestly, it doesn't serve any purpose here. Well, I suppose I could revisit it for reference purposes, but..."

As if to say that if the detector had worked, he no longer needed you, Azimov spoke.

Though Rei felt a slight irritation at that exchange, before he could say anything else, Azimov spoke up.

"There's the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear over there. Take them. The Death Scythe was a bit beyond even my capabilities, but I made some detailed adjustments to the Twilight Spear. Well, it's not like I added new functions or anything, more like maintenance."

"...Weren't you busy with the Ambrith situation?"

"Hmph. This kind of thing? Anyone could handle it, even without me. More importantly, hurry up and go. You still have things to do, don't you?"

Azimov said this while abruptly looking away from Rei. However, the female alchemist who had been standing a little distance away approached Rei.

"Heh heh, Mr. Azimov, despite appearances, he actually cares quite a bit about Rei, you know? When separating the remains of Ambrith from the borrowed magic item, he insisted on doing it himself instead of letting someone else handle it."

"Hey! Waa-sha! Stop talking nonsense and hurry over here! We're starting work!"

"Yikes! I-I know, I know! Don't yell at me!"

Startled by Azimov's loud voice, the female alchemist... the woman called Washa... grumbled at Azimov but headed over.

Thus, the alchemists, led by Azimov, discussed how to make the detector usable by anyone, each voicing their own opinions: this won't work, that might work.

The alchemists huddled together, each voicing their own opinions, and naturally, this left Rei's surroundings quiet.

"Hey, do you think there was any point in me coming?"

Vihera murmured softly.

She had been summoned at this hour to determine whether Rei could operate the detector.

In other words, despite being roused from a deep sleep and brought to the guild, Vihera had absolutely nothing to do.

Just being there. Being brought to the guild at this hour just to be put in that situation was something she couldn't quite accept.

"But if they'd only called Rei, you would've gotten angry, right?"

Rei and Vihera turned at the sudden, familiar voice from behind them.

There stood Marina, dressed as usual in her party dress.

Had she, as Guildmaster, not been asleep yet? Or had she been woken up early in the morning like Rei and the others?

Rei couldn't tell which it was, but Marina, who had appeared, didn't look tired at all.

Vihera smiled and nodded at Marina.

"Of course! If you're calling Rei, you have to call me too."

"Then don't complain. ...So, I just arrived, but judging from the situation, the detector didn't go off?"

Marina asked, looking at the alchemists fiercely clashing, but Rei shook his head.

"No, that part worked fine. We confirmed the detector functions normally. Now we're discussing how to modify it so others can use it too."

"...Honestly, even if we build a detector to find Ambrith, who appeared once three hundred years ago and has now reappeared, I don't think it'll be very practical."

"True. But we can't be sure Ambrith only appears here, right? If it shows up somewhere else, this could be useful then. Besides..."

Marina flashed a seductive smile and turned her gaze to the detector Rei was holding.

"It doesn't have to be limited to detecting only Ambris, does it? Right now it can only find Ambris, but what if it could detect other monsters too? What do you think would happen then?"

"Hunting requests would get easier."

"Yes. And since you can head straight to the location of the monster you're targeting, you won't have to deal with unnecessary encounters along the way."

"That... would be convenient."

When it comes to what's tough about extermination requests, defeating the target monster is one thing, but above all, it's finding the monster itself.

Unless you're trying to find them sleeping, monsters are usually on the move.

Whether you can successfully encounter a moving monster depends heavily on the adventurer's tracking skills and luck.

But if a detector could pinpoint where the monster is, it would definitely boost the success rate of the quest.

"...Well, it'll probably be a long time before that actually happens."

Marina murmured softly, her voice audible only to Rei and Vihera.

"Huh? Why? If this magic item could be mass-produced for use beyond just Ambris..."

"That's the tricky part,"

Marina asserted in response to Rei's words.

Marina smiled as she spoke to Rei and Vihera, who were looking at her with puzzled expressions.

"That detector Rei has... It uses several extremely rare materials to make. Not just white gold coins, but if you're unlucky, it could even reach the level of light gold coins."

"...Really?"

With a hesitant look, Rei glanced at the detector in her hand.

Judging by the palm-sized, flat crystal, it didn't look like it contained such rare materials.

But Marina wouldn't lie in this situation. So, the detector in Rei's hands must indeed be that valuable.

"Light gold coins? ...I get that using something that valuable is necessary when crafting magic items, but..."

What flashed through Rei's mind was the Twilight Spear.

Many of the materials used in it were rare. If one didn't bring their own materials and instead had an alchemist prepare everything, it would certainly cost enough to require light gold coins.

(Well, if you were actually paying cash for a Magic Sword like Noiz used, light gold coins would be perfectly normal.)

Thinking about it that way, the Twilight Spear ended up being incredibly cheap to make precisely because he'd supplied the materials himself.

"You understand, right? Mass-producing something at that price is difficult, and even if it were possible, only a handful of people could afford it."

"...What about the guild lending it out?"

"I understand Vihera's point, but that would be rather difficult. If this were worth at least silver coins... no, even a few gold coins, lending it might be possible. But lending out a magic item costing light gold coins? That's simply not feasible."

There's the risk of it being stolen, destroyed in battle, or simply lost.

Gilm's guild gathers rare materials due to its geographical location on the frontier.

That's why it has more financial leeway than guilds in other towns, but that doesn't mean it's rich enough to throw away light gold coins freely.

"If we could make it a bit cheaper, that might be a different story. ...Anyway, right now, Ambris is the top priority. Not immediately... but once we can get out of town, could you handle it?"

"Yeah."

Nodding at Marina's words, Rei shifted his gaze from the detector in his hand... to the key for defeating Ambris.
