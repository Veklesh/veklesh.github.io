Needless to say, eliminating the bandits around Sublusta wasn’t something that could be done in a day or two, so Rei and his group spent about five days relentlessly hunting them down.

That said, while Rei and his group finished in five days—though they only stopped there to deliver supplies to Sura and the others—the other adventurers are still continuing their hunt.

Naturally, with such a large-scale hunt underway, unlike the bandits who had fled quickly based on information from the survivors of the first bandit group Rei had defeated, many of the bandits who had optimistically assumed they were still safe seemed to sense the danger and began fleeing as well.

How well-connected are the bandits’ intelligence networks? Even in such a situation, bandits kept arriving one after another, making it a veritable feeding frenzy.

(No, or is it more like a game of cat and mouse? Or perhaps the newly arrived bandits were made scapegoats by those who tried to flee the area around Sublusta... is that a possibility?)

With such thoughts in mind, Rei took supplies out of the Misty Ring and began piling them on the ground.

Since these were luxury items like dried fruit, the women present scrambled to load the supplies onto their own wagons first, but Sura’s sharp voice rang out around them.

“I hope there’s no one here who thinks only of themselves? I’ll need to have a serious talk with anyone like that later.”

At those words from Sura, the women who had been swarming toward the dried fruit froze in their tracks.

“She’s got things under control, doesn’t she?”

“Well, I did lead the Resistance, after all, so this much is to be expected.”

“...So? I heard from Gimekara that the numbers have dropped a bit again—it’s not like anyone died, is it?”

“That’s right. Some found work or met women at places we stopped along the way, and others left because they were getting close to their hometowns. Our numbers have dropped by more than ten.”

“Huh. …Hmm?”

It wasn’t that she was genuinely interested, but even so, something about what she’d just heard caught Rei’s attention.

Over ninety percent of this group consists of women.

That was only natural, given that this was a group of people who had been brainwashed in Jaya and forced into prostitution.

But one of the reasons Sura just mentioned for leaving the group...

“Wasn’t it because you found a man?”

“Hehe… Unfortunately, you’re not wrong. It’s because I found a woman.”

To Sura, who spoke with a slightly haggard expression—one that betrayed mental exhaustion—Rei handed her a fresh piece of fruit—not a ration, but a personal item.

Taking it, Sura—in a manner unbecoming of a woman… or rather, quite manly—bites into it without even peeling the skin.

“I don’t know if they were just naturally inclined that way or if they turned to that path because they hated men. I’m not entirely sure about that, but there were a few people who decided to find a female lover and live with her…”

“Ah… yeah. Well, I suppose that could happen, right?”

In response to Sura’s words, that was all Rei could manage to say.

Of course, Rei knew that there were homosexuals in the world.

When Rei was in Japan, there were no such people around him; they were merely figures that appeared in manga, novels, and video games.

“Well, it’s not like they forced anything or anything—they just naturally ended up in that kind of relationship, so I don’t have any complaints.”

Even as she said this, Sura still looked somewhat exhausted, and she ate the fruit Rei had given her, as if to cheer herself up.

The women had been fighting over the dried fruit, but they didn’t even glance at the fresh, raw fruit Sura was holding.

They likely understood that if they tried anything like that in the current situation, it would end badly in more ways than one.

Normally, at this time of day in the summer, juicy fruit would be something they’d definitely want.

Even though autumn is gradually approaching, it’s still quite hot during the day.

So much so that if there were a lake or river nearby, they’d want to go swimming.

……In fact, the reason Rei and the others joined this group that Sura and the others were with was because they found them taking a break by the river.

“If the folks who left are living happily, then that’s fine, isn’t it? …Changing the subject, how’s your training going? It’s been a little while now, after all.”

Rei blatantly changed the subject, but Sura didn’t seem particularly bothered by his words and simply nodded in response.

“Everyone’s taking their training seriously. …I suppose it’s because they remember being dragged here against their will.”

If only they had the strength, they wouldn’t have had to go through this.

There were probably many who thought that.

And because they didn’t want to regret their lack of strength ever again, the women threw themselves into their daily training.

Above all, since the entire group traveled by carriage, the fact that their travel speed didn’t slow down even when they trained to the limits of their physical endurance was a major advantage for this party.

“Well, fine. Worst-case scenario, even if there’s no work in Gilm, we can always take on jobs as adventurers. …Though, whether that’ll actually work out is another story.”

As expected of a town on the frontier, powerful monsters roamed the surrounding area.

In fact, it’s not unheard of—though certainly rare—for Rank C monsters to venture as close as the outskirts of Gilm.

Adventurers who managed to reach Gilm through chance, luck, or the help of others lack the strength to fight in places where such monsters might appear.

In that sense, even though the women in the group have undergone combat training, they aren’t strong enough to operate in Gilm.

That said, there are a few among the women who have experience as adventurers, so if they form a party centered around those individuals, they might just manage... it’s a possibility.

Furthermore, many of the women have learned how to use bows and train almost every day.

Even if the monsters are strong, there is a chance they can be defeated by attacking from a safe distance, and even if they cannot be defeated, it is possible to create an opportunity for the comrades fighting on the front lines to escape.

In that sense, it’s certainly not a mistake that most of them are training to use bows.

“Still, it’s better than nothing, right? Besides, I heard there’ll be work on the expansion project for a while, so I don’t think we need to go outside the city.”

“Well, it’s better than doing nothing. Anyway, this is all the supplies I brought. The rest is up to you…”

“Just divide it up however you like.”

Rei was about to say that when a voice suddenly interrupted him.

“Rei, can I talk to you for a moment?”

“……Gimekara? What’s up? I didn’t think there was anything urgent I needed to talk to you about.”

“No, that’s true. But I actually have something I’d like to discuss with you.”

“A favor? I’ve already handed over all the supplies, and there’s nothing left for me to do, so sure. …Is that okay?”

He glanced at her as if to confirm, and Sura nodded in agreement.

“So, it seems you’re okay with it. What’s on your mind?”

“Come this way. It’s something I’d rather not have others hear.”

“……Just so you know, I don’t take on shady jobs like assassinations, okay?”

“That’s not it! What do you take us for, Rei!?”

As Gimekara shouts in indignation, Rei recalls what the Zolge Trading Company has done in the past.

Forcing women they’d dragged along to become prostitutes with slave collars, making them bear the children of giants.

The Zolge Trading Company was the one doing business with Jaya.

What’s more, even during negotiations with Daskar, Gimekara had gone so far as to declare that once they’d solidified their foothold in Gilm, they’d start dealing with underground organizations.

“No, considering what you lot have done so far, I think it’s a reasonable decision.”

Faced with that, Gimekara couldn’t come up with a rebuttal.

Well, she could probably argue if she wanted to, but she understood that arguing pointlessly here would only upset Rei.

“Anyway, it’s not anything shady, but there’s something I’d rather not have others overhear. So, is that okay?”

“Ah… I see.”

It wasn’t Rei who looked convinced by Gimekara’s words—it was Sura.

Nodding as if to say she could already guess what the matter was, Sura nodded again at Rei.

“It’s fine. I guarantee it’s nothing shady. So why don’t you just listen to what she has to say?”

With Sura saying that, Rei couldn’t imagine Gimekara was up to anything suspicious in this situation.

“Alright. Let’s go, then.”

And so, Rei was led by Gimekara toward a less crowded area.

In the distance, they could see Elena, Vihera, and Byune... along with Rocks, who was overseeing the group’s guards, training the women... and Marina teaching archery to a large group of women. As they watched, Rei and Gimekara made their way toward the shade of the trees.

“Sorry for the sudden request. …Actually, I think we talked about this briefly before—it’s about that dish that only you know how to make, Rei.”

“……Ah……Yeah. That matter. I wondered what it was about since you went out of your way to bring me all the way out here. Why all of a sudden?”

“No, it’s just that the food I ate at the village we stopped at a little while ago was surprisingly delicious…”

“So, you got a little anxious?”

“It’s not that I’m panicking, but delicious food is a real selling point in business.”

“Well, I can see that.”

Restaurants serving dishes Rei came up with—like udon, steamed buns, and pizza—are all seeing their sales go up across the board.

People can’t live without eating, so it’s only natural that if they’re going to eat, they want to eat delicious food.

The profits from food might be small compared to deals with nobles or with Jaya, but in terms of being a daily source of income, they’re significant.

Thinking about it that way, Rei could understand why Gimekara was so desperate.

He understands, but… the problem is whether understanding Gimekara’s feelings will immediately result in a dish that can actually be made.

Thinking that far, he suddenly remembered the matter of the goblin meat.

He’s been too busy to visit lately, but Rei had been researching ways to make goblin meat taste good with a certain person.

(Wait, is that a bad idea?)

Rei is collaborating with a member of the trading company.

Even though Rei has contributed some money and provided ingredients and spices, if they manage to make goblin meat palatable, it’s only natural that the trading company would be the one to market it.

Given that, I can’t very well bring Gimekara into the picture now.

Given that, the first thing that comes to mind is… something I thought of a little while ago…

“Natto”

The moment those words left Rei’s mouth, Gimekara’s gaze sharpened like that of a bird of prey that had spotted its prey.

“So, what kind of dish is this natto?”

“Well, if you ask me what kind of dish… It’s not really a dish, is it? More like an ingredient?”

Rei didn’t know the official difference between a dish and an ingredient, but since there are various dishes made with natto, he figured calling it an ingredient probably wouldn’t be wrong.

However, perhaps the ingredient itself was more interesting to Gimekara than the dish, as she turned to Rei with a beaming smile.

“Not a dish, but an ingredient? That’s interesting. But… can you actually get that around Gilm?”

“Oh, no problem. Since it’s an ingredient made from beans, it should be easy to make.”

At this point, realizing it would be more trouble not to teach her, Rei resigned himself to the situation and continued his explanation, recalling everything he knew about making natto.

“What you need are beans… specifically soybeans, but since it’s hard to explain exactly what kind of beans they are, just try experimenting with various kinds of beans for now.”

Edamame is a type of soybean, and Rei knew that… but unfortunately, since he’d never seen anyone eating edamame in Elgin, it was unclear whether that knowledge would be of any use.

“You’ll also need straw. …That’s all.”

“...Straw?”

Hearing that, Rei suddenly realized something.

All he knew was rice straw… but what about other kinds? Could natto be made with wheat straw?

But since he didn’t actually know the detailed method for making natto to begin with, he decided to have them try using wheat straw for now, and if it didn’t work, they’d just give up.

“Ah. First, boil the soybeans, wrap them in straw, and leave them alone. After some time has passed, the beans will start to form strings—that’s natto. …I think that’s roughly the right method. But since this is just a rough guide, you’ll just have to learn the details by actually making it yourself.”

“When you say ‘forming strings’… um, doesn’t that mean it’s going bad?”

“Technically, it’s fermentation. It’s similar to cheese. Well, I hear people tend to have strong opinions about it, so you should just take your time and give it a try.”

Upon hearing that it wasn’t rotten but fermented, Gimekara finally let out a slight sigh of relief.
