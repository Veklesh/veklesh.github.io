The bread and soup must have revived her. The beastwoman relaxed her guard somewhat toward Rei, who had given her food—freshly baked soft bread and soup packed with vegetables, which, depending on how you looked at it, was quite a luxurious meal.

Still, she hadn't completely let her guard down, likely due to the inherent caution inherent in her nature as a woman.

"Alright, alright. I'm Sharia. As you can see, I'm a wolf beast-kin."

Sharia proudly displayed her slightly above-average bust, but her clothes were stained with dirt and dust, hardly a figure to boast about.

(A wolf. ...Good thing I didn't say 'dog beastman'.)

Hearing that the woman before him was a wolf beastkin, Rei silently praised himself for not calling her a dog beastkin when he first saw the ears sprouting from the top of her head.

"I see, Sharia. I'm Rei. So... where should I start? Why are you hiding here in the first place? Judging by your clothes, you came here as a prostitute, right?"

While he was certain she'd been forcibly brought here by Jaya's hand, Rei didn't let it show.

After all, she might genuinely have come here wanting to be a prostitute.

Of course, hiding like this meant she definitely had some kind of story.

(How should I proceed?)

As Rei watched, Sharia opened her mouth, her eyes filled with anger.

"This is no joke! Why should I be dragged here against my will and forced to sleep with a man I don't even like?!"

Sharia screamed, unable to tolerate Rei's words.

Seeing Sharia like this, Rei nodded in understanding.

She understood that this was the person Rei was looking for—the one forcibly brought to Jaya.

First, what should he ask... While pondering that, Rei remembered the most important question to ask first.

"I'm sure there are others besides you who were dragged here against their will. So why are the rest of them satisfied with this life as prostitutes?"

"That... is probably because of this."

Saying this, Sharia held out a collar.

It was beautifully adorned, but even Rei could tell it was a slave collar.

But at the same time, he wondered.

For instance, if they used a slave collar to command someone to stand there or fetch something, that would be straightforward enough.

But, for instance, commanding a reluctant woman to live as a prostitute... such an order would obviously be fraught with problems and couldn't completely restrain her actions.

Moreover, from what Rei could see, all the prostitutes appeared to be working of their own free will.

At the very least, Rei couldn't believe that such a thing could be achieved with these mass-produced slave collars.

"What? You think I'm lying?"

"No, I don't think that. In fact, the reason I ended up coming to the Kingdom of Reburina was because Jarya's people tried to take someone away from the town where I lived."

"...Did you come to rescue the people who were taken?"

Sharia's eyes held a hint of envy as she asked Rei that question.

If Sharia had been taken, no one would have come to rescue her.

Or perhaps, even if someone wanted to help, they didn't know who was responsible for such a thing...

"No. Call it a silver lining, I suppose. I managed to rescue them before they were taken. I came here to settle the score with Gilm for what he did."

"Gilm... that Gilm!?"

Sharia knew Gilm's name too, showing surprise at the word Gilm coming from Rei's mouth.

"I don't know what that Gilm is, but if you mean the Gilm in the border town of the Mireana Kingdom, then yes, that's the one."

"Gilm... Gilm... No way..."

The word "Gilm" must have delivered a powerful shock to Sharia.

She was simply... stunned... not even noticing the bread she held fall to the ground as she repeated the word "Gilm."

Seeing Sharia like this, Rei was naturally surprised too.

No, the name of the town Gilm was extremely famous, so knowing it wasn't that strange in itself.

But that she would be so utterly shocked just by hearing the word "Gilm" was completely unexpected.

"Calm down. Here, drink this for now."

Though he'd just finished his soup, he handed her the fruit water he'd taken out of his Misty Ring, partly to help calm Sharia down.

The fruit juice Rei handed her... or rather, Sharia noticed the cup holding the fruit juice was cold, and instantly snapped back to her senses.

She wondered when she had even received this cup, but given this season worthy of being called a tropical night, its chill was incredibly appealing.

And so, almost instinctively, Sharia drank the chilled fruit water. She finally seemed to calm down, exhaling softly.

Seeing this, Rei spoke again.

"So, the story is that I'm from Gilm, and the Jayah folks messed with Gilm, so I'm here to settle the score... is that okay?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. ...I wonder what those Jaya folks were thinking, trying to mess with Gilm like that."

Gilm is a city ruled by Daskar, a central figure among the Neutral Factions, and both his subordinates and adventurers are elite.

What's more, I hear Gilm is home to many famed adventurers, like Crimson and the Axe of the Thunder God.

For Jayah, merely a clandestine organization within a vassal state, to meddle in such a place... Sharia wondered if they'd lost their minds.

...Actually, the Rei standing before Sharia was the very Rei known as Crimson. Yet, he carried neither the Death Scythe synonymous with Crimson nor the recently famous Twilight Spear. And without his partner, Set, it was hardly surprising Sharia failed to recognize him.

Had she noticed the bread, soup, and fruit water being taken out of the Misty Ring, things might have been different.

"I don't know if Sharia is aware, but Gilm is undergoing expansion work right now. Because of that, a lot of people are gathering looking for work. Not just adventurers or craftsmen, but even ordinary folks seeking day labor. That guy who belonged to Ajax... Jaja probably understood that too."

"Ah, I see. If someone who'd lived there for a while suddenly vanished, it would cause a stir, but if someone who'd just arrived disappeared, it wouldn't be such a big deal...?"

"Correct. That's precisely why Gilm was such an ideal hunting ground for Ajax. Actually, we only realized it because of another incident. If that hadn't happened, we probably wouldn't have noticed."

Even as he thought this, Rei also suspected that, in reality, an Intelligence Unit could have uncovered Ajax's scheme eventually, even if it took time.

At first, the Intelligence Unit didn't suspect Ajax either.

It's impossible to say how things would have unfolded under normal circumstances... but considering the number of people currently gathered in Gilm, it was probably unavoidable.

Of course, that was likely exactly what Ajax was aiming for.

But Ajax's misfortune was that someone was targeting him as the enemy of their family.

From there, his scheme was exposed like pulling on a string of potatoes, and he ultimately lost his life.

"Anyway, that's the gist of my situation. So, is it okay if I ask about Sharia now?"

"...Yeah."

At Rei's words, Sharia nodded hesitantly.

Having heard Rei's story, she probably felt she couldn't just keep her own circumstances to herself.

Especially since the person before her wasn't trying to take her as a prostitute. There was no one else she could turn to for more help.

"What do you want to know first?"

"Well... this is what's been bothering me the most. Earlier, that guy who was brought here because of that collar said he was working as a prostitute of his own volition, right?"

"...Yeah. When you wear this collar, how should I put it? You start thinking that being a prostitute is a wonderful profession, your reason for living, that you were born to be a prostitute... something like that."

Rei was astonished by Sharia's explanation.

Naturally, the commonly used slave collars possess no such effect, one that could even be called brainwashing.

If Sharia's words were true, then it could hardly be called a slave collar anymore—it would be another kind of magic item.

(What's going on? Even if Jaya has an abundance of magic items... isn't this a bit too far-fetched?)

Rei shifted his gaze back to the collar Sharia held—or rather, its remains—and spoke.

"If it's okay, could I borrow it for a moment?"

Sharia hesitates slightly before handing the collar over to the outstretched hand.

For Sharia, that collar was both a symbol of the hatred that had bound her actions and proof of what... exactly was happening here.

In other words, it was evidence of the harm inflicted by the very entity that had subjected her to this ordeal.

That was precisely why she had kept it, even though it was a detestable magic item that had twisted her will.

It was only natural to hesitate when asked to lend it.

Though, her hesitation lasted only a moment.

After all, even if he possessed this magic item now, it served no purpose whatsoever... In fact, it was not hard to imagine what would happen if the people of Jarya found it.

...It was fortunate for Sharia that the brothel owner entrusted with her care hadn't yet informed his superiors that she was gone, likely to cover up his own failure.

"...Looking at it like this, it doesn't seem to have such complicated properties. Well, you can't really trust appearances when it comes to magic items anyway."

Rei muttered, examining the collar handed to him.

Having used various magic items himself, Rei understood how difficult it was to gauge their capabilities based on appearance alone.

"But!"

Hearing Rei's words, Sharia, perhaps feeling her explanation wasn't being believed, reflexively tried to speak up.

But before she could, Rei nodded, signaling he understood.

"I told you looks aren't reliable. ...But if there's a magic item like this, I think it could be used for more than just making money as a prostitute."

Even just off the top of his head, Rei could think of soldiers, craftsmen, farming... any of those paths would come with their own hardships.

To prevent her from giving up midway, to make her believe she was born for this purpose... If it could make her believe that, then for those in power, there probably wasn't a more useful magic item.

Then why...

That was the question Rei pondered, but Sharia couldn't possibly know the answer.

"Anyway, I think we should look into this... Unfortunately, I don't have any acquaintances here who know much about that sort of thing."

Gilm might have acquaintances he could rely on for that sort of thing, but the Kingdom of Reburina was practically an enemy nation to Rei.

Moreover, Reburina was a small country, and its level of alchemy and such could hardly be called high.

Taking it to an incompetent party wouldn't just fail to uncover the collar's effects... they might even inform on Jaaia.

(What about Marina... could she figure something out?)

Elena, Vihera, and Byune could use magic items, but they probably couldn't investigate their detailed structure or performance.

Naturally, the same applied to Rei. The only possible candidate was Marina, who had lived for a very long time.

Though it felt like a long shot, almost a last resort.

"What should we do? Should I keep this, or should Sharia hold it? Honestly, in terms of figuring out its capabilities, I don't think it makes much difference either way. But in terms of safety, it's better for me to have it. But we've only just met. I know it's hard to trust or rely on someone so quickly. So, you decide."

"Alright. I'll leave it with you, Rei. But in return, could you bring me food every few days? Not every day, but every few days? In my current situation, getting food and water is really tough."

Surprised by how readily Sharia trusted him, Rei nodded to her proposal.
