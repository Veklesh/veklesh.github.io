"A snake tattoo on your left arm? Hmm, well... I've never heard of that before. Even when I was Guildmaster, it was different for someone like Rei who was just generally unconventional. A tattoo alone never caused any issues."

Marina replied to Rei as they headed toward the site of the expansion work.

Hearing this, Rei sighed regretfully.

Yesterday, when he returned to the Dusk Wheat inn, Ilse was waiting for him. After hearing her story in Vihera's room, he learned that someone in the guild bore the same tattoo as the person who killed Ilse's family five years ago. Thinking that might be it, Rei decided to ask Marina, the former guildmaster.

But all she got back was that Marina had no recollection.

(So maybe I should ask the receptionist instead?)

Unlike the guildmaster, who wouldn't hear about adventurers unless they were exceptionally distinctive, the receptionists interacted directly with adventurers.

Naturally, they interact with many adventurers and would remember distinctive individuals well.

And Rei had two receptionists he was close with: his assigned handler, Lenora, and Kenny, who was friendly with him.

Given that, there was no reason not to reach out to them.

"So you're investigating that person... Ilse, was it? You decided to help avenge that girl?"

"I haven't decided exactly yet. But if the person Ilse saw at the guild really is the one described, things could get messy, right?"

"That... would be true."

The kind of person who would kill a family of traveling merchants.

I'd heard Ilse survived because the rest of her family desperately tried to get her away, but it was close to total annihilation.

No, Ilse only did the simplest jobs within that merchant family.

In that sense, you could say the entire merchant family was wiped out.

After all, the peddling business Ilse's family ran hasn't been carried on since.

In any case, if such an individual truly had come to Gilm, it was highly likely they were plotting something.

Knowing that, there was no way Rei, who cherished Gilm, could just let it slide.

At first, he couldn't help but think that perhaps it would be quicker and easier to handle it himself, secretly. But in the current Gilm, Rei was attracting a lot of attention.

At the very least, acting openly would be problematic in many ways.

Moreover, the role Rei was playing in the current Gilm expansion project was significant in many ways.

While minor actions might be possible, the undeniable truth was that there simply wasn't enough time to take serious action.

"As for the guards..."

"I'll at least try to talk to Daskar about it."

The ease with which she could mention speaking to the local lord here likely stemmed from the comfort of an old acquaintance.

The guards were already barely managing with the help of adventurers... no, even then, they were short-handed, and several incidents had already occurred.

She must have judged that spreading unreliable information and having the guards investigate based on it would be problematic in many ways.

"You want Egg and his crew to handle it?"

Egg, who once led the Prairie Wolves, a band of former outlaws, now commands Gilm's intelligence unit.

By any standard, it could be called an extraordinary rise in rank.

...Though whether he himself is happy about being so heavily utilized is another matter entirely.

Regardless, Egg and his team were undoubtedly reliable when it came to gathering intelligence.

However, a large number of people had gathered in Gilm now... truly from all corners.

Among them, there are undoubtedly those scheming something, and as an intelligence unit, focusing on them is undoubtedly necessary.

(In fact, since there are fewer of them than guards, Egg and the others might actually be busier right now. They probably can't afford to hire adventurers or anything like that.)

That's what Rei thought, but he agreed with Marina's words: leave gathering intel on the enemy to the Eggs, since they're the experts.

"Yeah, best leave that to the professionals."

"Rei-sama! Rei-sama! Rei-samaaa!"

As if timing it perfectly for when Rei and Marina had finished their discussion, that voice reached Rei's ears.

In truth, he'd been walking while talking with Marina, so it probably wasn't deliberate timing.

It was probably just coincidence... but to Rei, it felt as if someone had deliberately timed it.

The person running toward him with an energy unimaginable for an old man, moving at a pace that could rival a youngster... Glancing at Marutsu, Rei sighed.

It wasn't that he disliked Marutsu, but Marutsu, unable to leave a question unanswered, kept talking to Rei about all sorts of things.

He meant no harm, but the pitying looks the others gave Rei made it clear just how often this happened.

"Hehe, do your best, okay?"

"Hey, Marina!"

While it was true that most of Marutsu's interest was focused on Rei, that didn't mean he wasn't interested in Marina either. Marina used spirit magic on a massive scale... truly, on a scale and with a precision that would be utterly unbelievable under normal circumstances.

Marina probably knew that too, which is why she made her escape so quickly.

Watching Marina's retreating figure... her deep green party dress exposing a large portion of her smooth, tanned back... Rei was about to voice his dissatisfaction.

But before he could, Marutsu stepped in front of Rei and spoke cheerfully.

"Rei-sama, regarding the type of magic you use..."

"Ah... For now, I'm off to work."

Rei had already finished using terrain manipulation to sink the ground.

In that sense, there was no need for Rei to come all the way to the spot where he was building the outer wall... but that didn't mean there was nothing else for him to do.

"Is that so? Understood. ...But Rei, just because you carry an item box, aren't you being taken advantage of? Carrying the corpses of defeated monsters, transporting building materials..."

Marutsu muttered discontentedly at Rei's words.

No, not just dissatisfied-looking, but genuinely dissatisfied.

Even to Marutsu, it was unbelievable how he wielded magic and handled magic items that seemed as valuable as an item box itself.

To make Rei do what he considers menial tasks...

Though, to be fair, Ray himself didn't seem to care about any of that.

After all, Rei was currently assisting with Gilm's expansion project through a designated request.

Given that, he didn't seem particularly bothered by using the item box.

...If he had obtained the item box after enduring great hardship, things might have been slightly different.

But since Rei had obtained the item box that was prepared for him immediately upon arriving in Elgin, he understood its rarity yet felt no hesitation in using it.

Especially since it wasn't a magic item with a limited number of uses, after all.

"Ah, it's Rei! Rei's here!"

The adventurers assigned to night guard duty cheered upon seeing Rei.

They were happy Rei had arrived... but more accurately, their voices were drawn to the Misty Ring he carried.

The adventurers each marked the corpses of the monsters they had slain to prevent other adventurers from claiming them.

Rei stored the monster corpses one after another into the Misty Ring.

Most of the corpses were goblins, so after securing the magic stones and the right ear as proof of defeat, the rest would be disposed of by the guild.

However, there were a few monsters of some value, starting with the orcs. The adventurers who defeated these monsters seemed genuinely pleased, perhaps because they felt their work was done.

Seeing Rei like this, Marz looked slightly displeased.

For Marutsu, who held strong respect for Rei, the attitude of the others towards her was likely unwelcome.

But he quickly hid that expression and approached Rei.

"Rei, is there anything I can help you with?"

"Ah... no, there's nothing specific I need help with today. Once this is done, I have to head to Treant Forest. I can't exactly take you with me to Treant Forest, can I?"

"Well..."

Marutsu nodded regretfully at Rei's words.

His spirit was still quite youthful, but his physical decline was undeniable.

Clinging to Set's front legs to move, like Vihera and the others did, was simply impossible for him.

If he tried something like that, he'd definitely fall to the ground midway.

(Huh? Ah, but from Gilm to Treant Forest, at Set's speed, it's only a few minutes. For that short a distance, clinging to Set might not be impossible... right?)

He considered it while looking at Marz, but quickly dismissed the idea.

Maltz's arms simply didn't seem strong enough to support his own weight.

Maybe he could manage for a few minutes, but maybe not.

It was only natural he didn't want to risk such a gamble in this situation, especially when there was nothing at stake.

"Well, that's the situation. Besides, you came to Gilm as a mage, right? You should focus on that instead."

"...I see. Understood. Well then, it's unfortunate, but I suppose I'll just focus on my own work."

"Yeah."

Rei briefly wondered what kind of expression "focus on my own work" was, but decided it would be trouble to voice it and let it slide.

After seeing off Marz, who had returned to do his own work, Ray proceeded to stow away the monsters one after another.

Once that was done, Rei headed next to the guild.

The adventurers who had taken the night watch duty returned to their lodgings, starting with the inn, just as the adventurers taking the day watch duty began their shift.

Even though the monsters that appeared weren't particularly powerful, staying up all night to guard was still extremely tough.

Moreover, while no exceptionally powerful monsters had appeared this time, that was largely due to chance or luck.

After all, this was Gilm... the frontier.

It wouldn't be surprising at all if a Rank C monster or even a Rank B monster appeared out of nowhere.

Of course, if a monster beyond the adventurers' control had appeared, more powerful adventurers would have rushed to their aid.

The fact that no such battle occurred was fortunate for the adventurers.

Those returning to their beds fully understood that good fortune and wanted nothing more than to surrender to it and sleep soundly.

...Though some were already contemplating heading to the brothel, even though it was barely morning.

The adventurers arriving to replace them looked sleepy, given the early hour, but most were bursting with energy.

However, even those feeling energetic were only so because it was still morning.

As noon approached and the afternoon wore on, the sun's rays grew increasingly intense, and the time spent enduring that heat would continue.

Whether it was the heat of the day or the drowsiness of the night... whichever they chose, it was certain that the escorting adventurers wouldn't be able to work in a comfortable environment.

(Well, for now, I suppose I can leave Egg to Marina... I should probably just go about my business normally.)

Since he was drawing attention in various ways, it was probably best for him to act normally so Marina and the others could move more freely. Thinking this... Rei confirmed all the adventurers assigned to the night guard had vanished before setting off toward the guild.

On the way, watching the many people working, he realized anew that Gilm's expansion was truly underway...

"Ooh, that looks tasty. Give me one."

"Sure thing. Working again today, Rei? Keep at it."

He bought a skewer from a familiar stall owner and walked while eating.

The stall catered to adventurers and craftsmen working at the construction site, so the flavor was a bit on the salty side. But for Rei, that actually made it a nice accent.

If he got thirsty, he could always buy some fruit water or pull out the Waterfall Dagger from his Misty Ring.

Thinking like that, he kept walking... and soon, Rei arrived at the guild.
