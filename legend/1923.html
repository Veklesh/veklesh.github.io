"Well then... um, I'll be right back."

Suchi bowed to the guild official and headed for the slums with her friends from the slums... and the adventurers the guild official had arranged to escort them.

Rei, who had seen them off, glanced at the guild clerk standing next to him, who was smiling happily as he watched Suchi and the others depart, and spoke.

“You handled that nicely.”

The guild official gave a slightly embarrassed smile at Rei’s words.

The various words the Guild official had spoken at the Giganto Turtle’s disassembly site were more than enough to win over the hearts of the slum residents, including Suchi.

As a result, while Rei was stowing the Giganto Turtle in the Misty Ring, the discussion wrapped up in no time, and most of them ended up accepting the Guild official’s proposal.

...However, there were some who were confident in their own abilities or who didn’t want to leave the slums; those individuals chose to participate in the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle but decided to continue living in the slums as before.

So, the Guild staff returned to the Guild to handle the necessary paperwork, and Suchi and the others were escorted back to the slums.

“That’s right. From the Guild’s perspective, I think this matter went better than expected. Above all, even though Suchi and the others are from the slums, they work diligently and responsibly. It’s a big deal that we were able to see that.”

The guild staff member was committed to providing full cooperation regarding the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.

Naturally, the rewards for those who participated in the dismantling were provided by the Guild and Daskar, which is precisely why they needed to keep expenses to a minimum while ensuring their actions benefited the Guild.

In that sense, Suchi and her group were truly a stroke of luck.

“Well, getting hired by the Guild is probably a relief for Suchi and the others… but you’d better not even think about trying to trick them into working for you, okay?”

“Of course. Basically, it’s menial labor and the pay isn’t that high… but even so, there’s no doubt they’ll be able to live a better life than in the slums.”

Hearing those words, Rei felt a little relieved.

He didn’t think the guild staff would try to deceive Suchi and the others, who had come to work on Rei’s recommendation, but there was always a chance.

If that were to happen, it would be something Rei—who brought Suchi here—could never forgive.

“Got it. Well, I won’t say anything else then. Also, I’m heading back soon—is it okay if I don’t stick around until the very end?”

“Yes, that’s no problem. When I checked with the guild earlier, they said the cleanup of the warehouse was mostly finished. Plus, they’ve brought in magical items for heating, so we’ll be able to live in the warehouse without any issues.”

It might be bearable in the summer, but now that it’s winter, it must be quite cold inside the warehouse.

I suppose they brought in those magical heating items to mitigate that... but is it really going to be okay? That thought lingered in Rei’s mind.

Back in Japan, portable heaters were sometimes set up in the gym for winter school assemblies… but considering the size of the gym, it never really got warm at all.

It might have been warm for those standing right next to the heaters, but for the students lined up in the gym, it was completely useless.

The guild staff had mentioned a warehouse, and if fifty people—no, if they brought along their families, friends, and partners, it would easily be over a hundred people staying there. Naturally, a warehouse large enough to accommodate that many people wouldn’t be out of the ordinary.

“Can it really heat a space that big?” Rei wondered, but then again, since it was a magic item, perhaps it could handle something like that with ease.

Also, even though it was a warehouse, since it was a guild warehouse, there wouldn’t be any drafts.

Given that, even if it was a bit chilly, it would undoubtedly be warmer than sleeping in the slums.

“I see. Well then, I’ll leave it to you.”

Rei wasn’t entirely opposed to asking more questions about the warehouse, but he had a feeling that inquiring further might lead to some trouble, so he said only that and left.

He called out to Set, who was playing with a few adults and children a short distance away, and Set immediately came over to Rei.

“Grr!”

With Set purring happily by his side, Rei walked away.

The guild staff member bowed deeply to Rei and Set.

He was likely just that happy that Rei had entrusted Suchi and the others to him.

For someone working at the guild, establishing a personal connection with Rei held great significance.

……Though, strictly speaking, when it came to a connection in the purest sense, Lenora and Kenny were by far the closest to him.

Still, for the guild staff, Rei’s words and attitude were that much more welcome—that was the point.

“So? It seems Rei was quite busy today… I wonder if he managed to accomplish his goal?”

As we were having dinner in Marina’s backyard as usual, Vihera asked that.

Of course, since Vihera had also been at the Giganto Turtle dismantling site today, she must have heard about all that… bringing the slum dwellers over, getting the Guild to make various concessions so Suchi and the others could stay by clearing out a warehouse, preparing magic items to make their stay comfortable, and arranging escorts to go pick up the families living in the slums.

Even so, the fact that she went out of her way to ask Rei about it like that suggests she probably intended to use what Rei had done as a topic of conversation with Elena, Marina, and Ara.

“Oh, and there was that incident with the mud wall, wasn’t there? …It seems you and Meru didn’t get along very well.”

“Meru? …You mean, Marina is teaching spirit magic?”

Elena tilted her head at the name that came out of Marina’s mouth.

It was probably because she’d never heard the name before.

But she quickly recalled the story about Marina teaching spirit magic to the dark elf and muttered as if it all made sense.

“Yes. …She’s a bit timid, or maybe shy around strangers—that sort of girl. But when it comes to compatibility with spirits, which is essential for spirit magic, she’s quite exceptional. Even Rei, who’s seen her up close, would agree, right?”

“Ah… yeah. I guess so.”

“Hey, what’s with that vague answer?”

Perhaps taking offense at Rei’s words, Marina said with a hint of dissatisfaction.

From Marina’s perspective, Meru is a Dark Elf with considerable talent.

She’s still a bit raw right now, but if she keeps growing like this, there’s no doubt she’ll achieve great things in the future.

...That said, setting aside her talent for spirit magic, I really think she needs to do something about that timid nature of hers.

“Well, even if you say that… I see Marina’s spirit magic all the time…”

Rei, who had been spoken to in that dissatisfied tone, replied to Marina.

In fact, as far as Rei knows, he witnesses Marina’s spirit magic—which possesses the highest level of skill—almost every day.

For example, even though it’s cold enough for snow to be falling right now, the temperature in Marina’s backyard is warm enough to eat outside, and the snow doesn’t even make its way into the yard.

All of this is thanks to Marina’s spirit magic.

To anyone familiar with spirit magic, it’s a massive display of power—so vast that it wouldn’t be strange for them to think, “What a waste…”

Since they’re eating here in a place where such large-scale spirit magic is being used, Rei—no, Elena, Vihera, and Ara—inevitably end up using the same standard for their spirit magic as Marina.

Since no one other than Rei has seen what kind of spirit magic Meru used, they can only nod in agreement with Marina’s explanation, but Rei is different.

Having actually seen Meru’s spirit magic with his own eyes, he finds it hard to fully believe Marina when she says he’s exceptionally talented.

“You know, that’s only natural. Meru and I have been using spirit magic for very different lengths of time.”

Marina said this, but Rei could guess that the fact she was a Priestess of the World Tree probably had something to do with why her spirit magic was so skilled.

“Ah, yeah. Well, I suppose that makes sense.”

Rei doesn’t know how old Meru actually is.

But Marina, standing right in front of him, possesses the longevity typical of a dark elf and has a long track record as an adventurer before becoming a guild master.

Given that experience, it’s only natural that her skill with spirit magic would be exceptionally high. …Of course, that’s also thanks to the talent required to become a Priestess of the World Tree.

Putting Marina and Meru in the same category is, in a way, like telling a talented rookie who’s just become an adventurer to demonstrate more power than Rei.

There’s no way they could possibly work together.

(Asking a woman about her age is supposed to be taboo... but then again, Marina mentioned her years of experience herself, so maybe I should just let it slide.)

Deciding it would be best not to dwell on the matter any further, Rei changes the subject.

“Anyway, while I can’t say for certain that this will stop Kobolds from entering Gilm… it will definitely reduce their numbers. If that happens, more people should be able to join in dismantling the Giganto Turtle.”

“Hmm. But… since Rei has used earthen walls to block the Kobolds from entering Gilm, won’t that mean more of them will head toward the Giganto Turtle?”

“Probably. So the guys hired as guards for the dismantling should get busy. Of course, whoever takes it down can claim ownership, so if they think about that, I’m sure the guys assigned to guard duty will work hard since it’s a chance to make some money… but…”

Rei said this, but he also had some concerns.

When it came to eliminating the Kobolds that had infiltrated Gilm, the construction site had been set up with building materials arranged in a simple maze-like pattern.

Thanks to that, even if a certain number of Kobolds invaded Gilm, dealing with them wasn’t particularly difficult.

However, the fact that the earthen walls now prevent them from entering Gilm means the Kobolds will all move out onto the open ground at once.

The question is whether the guards hired to protect the demolition site can handle such a sudden, coordinated attack.

In one-on-one combat, it’s not uncommon for adventurers to take down a Kobold in an instant.

Even in a two-on-one or three-on-one situation, victory is possible with sufficient skill.

But when it comes to ten-to-one, twenty-to-one, or thirty-to-one… and if that keeps happening over and over, it’s a different story.

“...Should we build a low earthen wall near the demolition site to split up the Kobolds to some extent?”

After a few seconds of silence, Rei murmured.

It wouldn’t need to be as large as the earthen wall built to keep Gilm out, but even a wall about a meter high would be a major obstacle for the Kobolds.

And it wouldn’t be just one or two—Rei could create as many of these earthen walls as he wanted within the range of his terrain manipulation.

Given that, the kobolds’ formation would be disrupted, and they should be unable to advance as a massive horde.

The downside was that the earthen walls would also get in the way of his guards when it came time to defeat the Kobolds… but if they simply waited for the Kobolds to climb over the walls on flat ground, there shouldn’t be a problem.

(Well, there might be a few who insist on taking down the Kobolds themselves, or who want to kill as many as possible to make more money... but I don’t need to worry about those guys.)

That’s my conclusion.

After all, they were hired as guards, strictly to intercept attacking monsters—I wasn’t encouraging them to actively go out and kill enemies on their own.

Given that, Rei decided there was no need to pay attention to anyone who chose to charge into the earthen wall of their own accord.

“Oh, that sounds kind of interesting. …Though the fact that we’re fighting Kobolds is a bit of a downer, but if that’s the case, I might as well join in.”

“...Well, with Vihera’s strength, I don’t think you need to worry that much... but just in case, be careful, okay?”

Rei said that and went back to focusing on finishing her dinner.
