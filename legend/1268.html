Rei dodged the long sword's strike as it came at him, slicing through the air, by checking his footing, taking a step back, and lowering his right side.

But the opponent wielding the longsword likely didn't believe that strike alone would defeat Rei. The next instant, he launched a slashing upward strike from the downward swing.

Though the blade of the longsword came toward him, Rei swung the Twilight Spear without any sign of panic.

While fighting with both the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear was Rei's usual style, this time, due to the handicap, he fought with only the Twilight Spear.

In midair, the longsword's blade clashed with the Twilight Spear's tip... In that instant, Rei flipped his wrist, wrapping the longsword's blade around the spear's tip and flinging it skyward.

"Ah!"

Such a sight wouldn't have been unusual between long-handled weapons like spears.

But the sword's wielder never expected such a maneuver from a spear and a longsword. Startled, they froze for an instant... only to realize the Twilight Spear's tip was now pressed against their face.

"...I give up. You win."

The woman who spoke—Milein—admitted defeat and sighed bitterly.

Hearing this, Rei obediently lowered the tip of the Twilight Spear.

"For a strike wielded in this snow, it was quite powerful. But, perhaps because you're still mindful of the snow beneath your feet, the sharpness of your slash lacks the edge it once had. ...Well, I suppose not moving your body much during your recent life has something to do with it."

"I suppose so. That's why I asked Rei to train with me."

Milein's gaze swept around the surroundings.

All that lay before them was a vast silver world.

No one else was in sight besides Rei and the others.

That was only natural. Even though it wasn't snowing today, the outside temperature was still bitterly cold.

In such conditions, it was hard to imagine many people wanting to go out of their way to do combat training at the guild's training grounds.

Rei wouldn't have come all the way to the guild either if Milein hadn't invited him.

"Hm!"

As Milein left in front of Rei, Byune appeared in her place.

It was her turn now. Holding a dagger, she fixed her gaze on Rei.

Vihera watched Byune's actions with amusement.

...Well, more accurately, it was because she was next after Byune and was looking forward to it.

"So, are you ready?"

"Mm!"

At Rei's words, Byune nodded.

Byune seemed unchanged from usual... or so it seemed. But unusually for the normally expressionless Byune, her eyes held a glint of fighting spirit.

Though usually almost expressionless, Byune was now filled with motivation ahead of her battle with Rei.

For some time now, Byune had been receiving combat training from Vihera.

This was the moment to show the results. That's what she must have been thinking.

(Given Byune's combat style, which prioritizes mobility, I doubt he can fully demonstrate his abilities in a place like this.)

Feeling the snow beneath his feet through the soles of his boots, Rei readied his spear.

Currently encasing Rei's feet were not the Sleipnir Boots... but rather the boots he had custom-made at the shoemaker for this particular mission.

They were made from monster hide and reinforced with iron plates.

In Rei's estimation, they were closest to safety boots, but compared to the ones he wore during his rice delivery part-time job at a transport company back in Japan, these were more comfortable and sturdier.

Of course, the ones Rei wore in Japan were mass-produced items, while the ones he wore now were custom-made by a skilled craftsman, tailored to the shape of his feet.

Additionally, the materials differ—synthetic leather versus monster hide—and in terms of comfort, the pair Rei currently wore was clearly superior.

He had tried them on several times since receiving them, but this was still his first time using them in actual combat—albeit a mock battle—so Rei was understandably nervous.

However, as expected of a craftsman possessing high skill even by Gilm standards, while they didn't quite match Sleipnir Boots, they posed absolutely no problem.

"Alright. ...Come on!"

"Mm!"

That single word served as the signal, and Byune began her action.

Just as Rei had thought earlier, moving on snow alone meant Byune couldn't reach her full speed.

Still, perhaps because he was a thief, Rei closed the distance to Byune at a speed far beyond that of an ordinary amateur.

Byune must have realized that simply closing the distance with Rei offered no chance of victory. With the hand not holding the dagger, she swiftly drew a long needle and hurled it.

The long needle sliced through the air, hurtling straight toward him.

A strike so fast it would normally be impossible to track with the eye. Yet, as if it were the most natural thing, Rei caught it within his field of vision.

And then, swinging the Twilight Spear...

"What!?"

Feeling a sudden unease about the multiple long needles flying towards him, he didn't strike them down with the Twilight Spear. Instead, he leapt right to avoid them.

(What was that feeling just now?)

He pondered this mid-air, but Byune must have seen this movement as the perfect opening, for she hurled more long needles once more.

...At that moment, Rei saw it.

Byune's left hand, raised to strike him.

Among the long needles clasped between her fingers, one finger held nothing... no, something extremely hard to see.

It was especially hard to see because the other fingers held ordinary long needles.

But even so, Rei's eyes, possessing senses far sharper than any ordinary human's, could faintly but unmistakably see that something was there.

If this had been an ordinary human, they would never have been able to discern the invisible hour hand Byune was holding.

"Huh!?"

And the released long needle flew straight toward Rei, including the visible objects.

While she could see the needle when Byune held it, once released like this, judging its trajectory became difficult.

Instead of using the Twilight Spear to deflect it, Rei chose evasion.

The long needle pierced the space where Rei's body had been just an instant before.

Even a long needle not sharpened for mock combat would hurt if it hit.

Especially since it wasn't thrown by an amateur, but by Byune, who was highly skilled in wielding them.

Rei certainly didn't want to be hit by it.

Of course, he had the Dragon Robe, but there was still the possibility it could pierce through the gaps and strike his bare skin.

"Hmph!"

Confirming that Rei had dodged the long spear's strike, Byune stepped forward, dagger in hand.

Even in the snow, her speed was faster than that of an ordinary warrior.

This was partly because Byune was a thief accustomed to swift movements, but her extremely petite frame and light weight also played a role.

Her combat training with Vihera in the snow also played a significant role.

"Do your best, Byune!"

Vihera's words of encouragement for the one she had trained—though not exactly her student—echoed across the training grounds.

That voice, naturally, reached Byune as well. The speed at which she closed in on Rei increased further.

Rei braced himself, Twilight Spear at the ready, awaiting Byune's advance.

In terms of distance, Rei held an overwhelming advantage.

But if she could break through that distance, the advantage would shift to Byune.

And yet, even as Byune closed the distance, Rei made no move to block her advance or maintain his spear's reach. He simply waited.

He felt disdained.

Feeling this, Byune suddenly increased his speed even further, swinging his dagger not at Rei's torso... but at his thigh.

A strike that seemed to slice through the air itself.

A strike maximizing Byune's swiftness headed for Rei's thigh...

"Too easy."

With that single word, Rei used a flick of his wrist to spin the spearhead of Twilight Spear halfway around, sweeping under Byune's leg.

Because Byune prided herself on her speed, the effect of that strike was significant, sending her tumbling across the snow.

Still, she managed to regain her footing... The next instant, what Byune saw was the tip of the Twilight Spear thrust toward her face.

"...Hm..."

Though only a word, Byune murmured it with a hint of disappointment.

Yet, her expression remained almost unchanged—a truly impassive face.

As Byune lowered her dagger in defeat, Rei also withdrew the spearhead.

Byune stood up and eventually made her way back toward Vihera.

"Well, Byune tried her best... but she still needs to hone her skills a bit more."

Vihera murmured, lightly stroking Byune's hair.

Not to encourage her, but simply stating the facts, Vihera's words made Byune nod slightly.

"Well, I'll avenge you, Byune. Don't worry."

"No, I'm not dead."

Milein blurted out as Vihera stepped forward, crunching through the snow.

"Oh? Well, I just need to beat Rei, right?"

"...Vihera, could it be that you just want to fight?"

Milein's tone toward Vihera was anything but that of someone addressing a former royal.

But Vihera seemed to actually enjoy it.

After all, Vihera saw herself not as royalty, but as an adventurer. It was only natural.

"Ready? Let's go."

"Yeah."

This mock battle was the first one for Vihera since regaining consciousness in the dungeon.

He had conducted combat training with Byune, but that was strictly combat training.

Well, in terms of combat training, this mock battle was similar, but the opponent was different.

He undoubtedly thought of Byune like a sister, but when considering her as an opponent to fight, it was only natural to feel dissatisfied with her.

In contrast, Rei was the most suitable opponent one could ask for.

After all, even when Vihera fought at full strength, he had never been able to defeat Rei.

Thinking about it that way, this mock battle was truly the perfect opportunity.

Enjoying the feel of the snow beneath his feet, Vihera stepped forward to face Rei.

No further words passed between them... until a gust of wind swept up the untrodden fresh snow piled on the ground.

As if waiting for that moment, Vihera took a step forward.

A speed surpassing even the famously swift Byune by several degrees.

As if to say it was merely a light greeting, a flash came from the claws extending from his gauntlets.

The blow slashed through the air in a horizontal arc, but Rei, capable of easily evading such a movement, dodged the magical claw strike at the last moment, just out of its reach...

"What!?"

The moment the magic claw passed by Rei's chest, it extended. By over a meter.

The instant he saw that movement, Rei instinctively swung Twilight Spear to deflect the magic claw.

The Twilight Spear and the gauntlet that produced the magic claws.

Both were magic items in that regard. Furthermore, both the gauntlets and greaves worn by Vihera were masterpieces crafted with utmost dedication by an alchemist possessing superior technology compared to other nations, back when they were still in the Bestir Empire.

But... even so, compared to the Twilight Spear, forged using materials from numerous rare monsters, they inevitably fell a notch below.

As a result, the clash between the Twilight Spear and the Magic Claw ended with the spearhead slicing the claw clean off.

Though Vihera's attack was blocked by a split-second reaction, he must have been startled nonetheless. Rei's eyes, hidden beneath the hood, fixed Vihera with a gaze as sharp as a predatory bird spotting its prey.

"You've become quite the skilled performer, haven't you?"

"Heh heh, really? This is all thanks to Rei, you know? I never thought I'd be able to do something like this either."

"...I see."

With that single word, he understood why Vihera could now perform such feats.

The reason her eyes had turned silver was the same.

Because she absorbed the heart of the Silver Lion, packed with its most concentrated magic. Because she absorbed the very essence of Ambris.

"Now, let's move on!"

With eyes glistening with lust—or rather, combat lust—Vihera shouted.

Most men would find their primal instincts stirred just by seeing Vihera now.

Translucent garments that seemed to reveal what lay beneath danced through the air, each movement accompanied by the swing of Vihera's hands and feet.

At first glance, her arms and legs certainly didn't look capable of striking anyone, yet each blow possessed the power and sharpness to easily knock down even a large man.

Such blows were unleashed without pause. Rei evaded them, parrying with the Twilight Spear.

It was less a battle between two combatants and more like a dance... and it continued like that for nearly an hour.
