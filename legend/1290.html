The year changed, and the new year arrived... yet nothing particularly changed; time simply flowed on.

Rei spent more time with Set than usual, but he was enjoying these days without any real commotion, which was rare.

Mornings were spent eating breakfast, then either heading out into town, playing with Set, or conducting combat training with Byune... Afternoons involved walking around eating lunch with Set, followed by shopping or visiting the library.

Come evening, they'd return to the Dusk Wheat inn, snacking along the way, and have dinner in the dining hall.

At dinner, he was usually with Vihera and Byune, though Marina would occasionally show up too.

...Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, most of those currently staying at the Dusk Wheat inn knew about Rei and the others.

When a beauty like Marina appeared, some tried to hassle her, but they quickly gave up once they learned she was an acquaintance of Rei's.

Of course, if they knew Marina was the Guildmaster, they wouldn't have tried anything like that in the first place... But since she rarely appears in public, it's not uncommon for people to see Marina and not recognize her as the Guildmaster.

About a month into the new year, Marina came to eat dinner with Rei and the others at Dusk Wheat inn as dusk fell.

"So, how's the handover to Walker going? Spring isn't exactly right around the corner... but it's only about two months away now."

"Ah, that part is almost completely sorted out. Walker was a quick learner to begin with. I was worried because he joined the guild a bit late, but most of the handover is already done, so it's fine."

Marina said this while enjoying the stew made with dried oak meat—a dish you could find anywhere, yet one that tasted remarkably different, likely due to the considerable time and effort put into it.

"Huh. So there's no problem then. If things keep going smoothly like this, we should be able to properly form a party by spring."

"Yes. I'm keeping my body in good shape too... Honestly, we could start the party tomorrow and it wouldn't be a problem at all, you know?"

With a smirk, Marina announced this. Vihera reached for the bread and opened his mouth.

"Hmm. Then, how about sparring with me tomorrow?"

For Vihera, who loved combat, a fight with Marina was something he could look forward to.

Adventurers often rest their bodies during winter, but Vihera trained in combat with Byune almost daily, so she never grew sluggish.

"Cut it out. If Vihera and Marina fought seriously, whether it's the courtyard or the guild training grounds, it'd definitely get pretty destructive."

muttered Rei, eating his egg, vegetable, and ham sandwich, looking exasperated.

If it were just Vihera, the fight wouldn't be that spectacular.

Being skilled in close combat, Vihera's fighting style itself isn't flashy.

...At the very least, compared to when Rei fought at full strength, starting with the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, it could be said to be terribly tame.

But Marina is different.

Using spirit magic and a bow... and relying primarily on spirit magic, Marina's skill made her battles spectacular.

If you've ever seen Marina use spirit magic to clean the house or shovel snow as effortlessly as breathing, you needn't doubt her skill.

(There was that guy who once made a dragon with water spirit magic... but Marina's probably better than him.)

Of course, Rei wasn't particularly knowledgeable about spirit magic.

It was merely an impression, but even so, Rei had absolute confidence in his instincts.

But for Marina to fight Vihera, she'd obviously need to get serious.

At that moment, Rei tried to imagine the extent of the collateral damage... but the next instant, he dismissed it as pointless and opened his mouth.

"Besides, Vihera doesn't fully grasp her own power yet, does she? What if she accidentally goes all out and damages the surroundings... or worse, gets hurt herself? What then?"

"Hmm... I suppose I have no choice."

Revived by the Silver Lion's Heart... or more accurately, having regained consciousness, Vihera still didn't understand her own limits.

She wanted to know them immediately if possible, but Grimm, who had performed the ritual, had warned her not to push herself until the magic power settled into her body.

After all, it was the magic of a Silver Lion... a Rank S monster. It couldn't possibly integrate into her body that easily.

Even now, it was still incomplete.

The combat training with Byune that had been happening since winter began was meant to train Byune, but it was also meant to help Vihera gradually, even if only little by little, get used to her current situation.

Understanding this, Vihera obediently followed Rei's words.

...Though, to anyone who knew Vihera even a little, the fact that she'd so readily given up on combat was surprising.

Indeed, several adventurers listening to Rei and the others in the cafeteria couldn't hide their expressions of astonishment.

Even as they saw these individuals conversing with their eyes, Rei, Vihera, and Marina continued their conversation without seeming particularly bothered.

They understood, in many ways, that they stood out.

"Still, we really need to decide on a party name soon, don't we? It's been about two months now."

"Even if you say so..."

For Rei, who lacked confidence in his naming skills, deciding on a party name was an extremely difficult task.

If it were just a matter of slapping on a random name like the Set Squad, that would be a different story.

"Well, saying there's only two months left also means there are still two months left... We can decide before we actually submit the party formation form to the guild, so I won't rush you. I don't really care about the party name anyway."

"I'm the same as Marina—I don't really care either. ...Just don't make it too weird, though."

"...I'll take care of it."

The truth was, "anything is fine" was the most troublesome thing for Rei too.

(I've seen it in manga too—when someone asks, "What do you want to eat?" and they say "Anything's fine," and then "Anything's fine" turns out to be the hardest thing to deal with... It's kind of like that.)

Thinking that, Rei ate his dinner.

Fortunately, the conversation about the party name seemed to end there.

"So, how's Byune's training going? Vihera's been training him more than Rei, right?"

As the conversation naturally turned to Byune's combat strength, Byune, who had been drinking soup with single-minded focus, lifted his face from the bowl and gave a small nod.

"Mm."

Marina wasn't sure if she could call it confidence, but when she looked over at Vihera, who was in charge of Byune, he nodded back.

Seeing that, Marina understood he must have some degree of confidence in his own abilities.

She understood... but since it was unreasonable to completely trust Byune's self-assessment alone, she asked Vihera again.

"So, what's the actual situation?"

"...Well. Byune was originally a thief, so her attack power isn't that high to begin with. If we're talking strictly about holding the line, I think she could be a decent combat asset. ...Though we'd need to be careful about various things when facing high-rank monsters."

Huh.

Marina looked at Byune again, struck by Vihera's high regard for her.

Vihera, who enjoyed combat, approached fighting with a sincere attitude due to that very preference.

Even after years of knowing each other, she wouldn't overestimate Byune's abilities beyond what they truly were.

As if continuing Vihera's words, Rei spoke up.

"Besides, there's also the dagger made by Pamidor."

There was no need to wonder what kind of dagger that was.

Forged from the materials of a Rank S monster, the Silver Lion, it's a weapon that wouldn't look out of place in the hands of a high-ranking adventurer like a Rank A.

But the reason he didn't mention it was made from Silver Lion materials directly here was probably because this was a tavern.

In front of the adventurers in the dining hall and the merchants who were sharp on intelligence, such a weapon... especially one carried not by Rei, Marina, or Vihera, but by the still-childish Byune, could easily spark ill intentions in someone.

Normally, no one would consider such a thing because of Rei's presence, but this time it was a dagger made from the materials of an S-rank monster.

It wouldn't have been strange if someone lost their mind over it.

"Ah, I've heard about it. Seems like a pretty high-performance dagger, huh?"

Marina understood what Rei was referring to and joined the conversation.

In truth, a dagger crafted from Silver Lion materials possessed exceptional sharpness.

Except for S-rank monsters, it seemed capable of cutting through any A-rank or lower monster without fail, barring some extraordinary exception.

Of course, the user's skill was essential to that, so if Byune's technique was poor, it would be meaningless.

"That's right. Thanks to it, I have a means to deliver effective attacks against most monsters. Plus, the transparent long needle I started using recently is pretty handy too."

Rei agreed with Vihera's words.

Transparent long needles, released mixed in with ordinary ones, are a far more troublesome thing than one might imagine.

While Rei could detect them through hearing or sight, it would be difficult for ordinary people.

"I see. So, does that mean we don't need to worry about Byune's combat strength?"

"I think she's generally fine. ...What about Rei?"

"I agree with Vihera."

Rei nodded in agreement with Vihera's words.

Hearing this, Byune relaxed her eyes slightly, looking a little pleased as she sipped her soup.

...Only Vihera seemed to notice.

"Strictly speaking, as a thief's role... if we're only considering things like distracting enemies and such, I think Byune has decent ability."

With both Vihera and now Rei vouching for Byune's abilities, Marina finally nodded in return.

"Right, then there's no problem. ...So, how about we all head out of Gilm together sometime soon? We can wait until spring to officially form a party, but we'll need to get used to working together."

"I'm fine with it... but are you okay, Marina? Even if the handover to Walker is mostly done, aren't you still busy with all sorts of things as Guildmaster?"

The duties of a guildmaster aren't limited to just the handover.

There are many other duties as well.

Marina nodded reassuringly to Vihera, who asked if it was really okay to venture outside the city under such circumstances.

"If it were spring, my duties as Guildmaster would be heavier, but it's winter now. Of course, it's not like I have no work at all, but it's manageable. Besides, Walker is here too."

"...Walker isn't officially Guildmaster yet, right? Is that okay... confidentially speaking?"

"It's fine. In fact, he needs to get properly accustomed to the work while I'm still here. Once spring comes, I won't be Guildmaster anymore, and there will be more documents I can't show him."

Rei nodded in agreement upon hearing that.

Even as a former Guildmaster, there were naturally documents she couldn't show him now that she wasn't the current one.

Considering that situation, it was indeed essential to thoroughly teach him how the work was done while she was still around.

"Still, Walker has experience running a guild branch office in the dungeon. It's far easier than teaching someone completely new to the job of guildmaster. The work itself isn't that different, after all."

Marina's words carried strong persuasive power.

It was the kind of persuasiveness that made you nod in agreement without thinking.

"Alright. If Marina says so, I guess I'll head out of Gilm and hunt some monsters. As for when exactly..."

"Any day that works for Marina is fine. We don't really have anything else we need to do anyway."

Rei nodded at Vihera's words, and Byune nodded back... And so it was decided. They would venture outside Gilm in winter—something only certain people did, like adventurers who needed to earn money.
