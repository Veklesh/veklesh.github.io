Even if a cicada pupa has developed a crack on its back and is about to complete its emergence, a single strike from the Death Scythe can inflict significant damage.

Furthermore, if the pupa is still in its pre-emergence state, a powerful strike before it fully emerges might be enough to finish it off.

Such thoughts flashed through Rei's mind.

A barrier appeared as if to protect the pupa, but in the next instant, it was easily destroyed by the Death Scythe's strike.

A sound like shattering glass reverberated around them, and simultaneously, the barrier was sliced apart like wet paper tearing, vanishing into nothingness.

Though the barrier had even withstood a Flying Slash, it was utterly no match for the Death Scythe's strike.

Maintaining the momentum of the strike that had cleaved the barrier, the blade of the Death Scythe continued straight toward the giant cicada in the midst of molting...

The moment something warm and soft enveloped Rei, he suddenly found himself looking up at the blue sky.

"...What...?"

What happened? He had no idea what had happened to him.

The only thing he knew was that he was somehow lying on the ground.

As he tried to sit up, his memory completely blank, pain shot through his entire body.

"Ow!"

Surprised by the pain I hadn't felt in so long, I wondered if I'd been injured, but the Dragon Robe wasn't torn or damaged.

But I remember a flowing sensation. When I brought my hand to my cheek, there was a wet feeling there.

Wiping it away and holding my hand up to my eyes, I saw a crimson liquid—a color worthy of the nickname "Crimson" given to Rei.

"...Blood?"

Yes, there was no doubt about it—the liquid on Rei's finger was blood.

"Grrrrrrr"

Set's cry could be heard right near Rei.

No, not right beside Rei. It was coming from beneath Rei's body.

Then the light orb flew straight toward Rei as if concerned, flickering violently.

"What happened?"

Rei muttered as he stood up, but his entire body ached, making him frown.

It wasn't just one spot; his entire body ached uniformly.

Still unable to comprehend what kind of attack could cause this, Rei stood up and turned his gaze toward the World Tree. His eyes widened in shock.

The scene unfolding there was completely unexpected.

Centered on the World Tree, shockwaves radiated out in all directions... a scene that could easily have been mistaken for an explosion.

Grass growing from the ground was bent outward.

"Did we get attacked?"

"Grr."

Set growled in agreement with Rei's muttered question.

Rei looked at Set again, but there stood Set, bearing several wounds just like Rei.

His torso was unharmed, but his limbs and other extremities bore numerous cuts...

It was fortunate that he had been positioned behind Rei to launch a follow-up attack after Rei's strike.

"Ow!"

The slightest movement sent a faint wave of pain through his body.

It wasn't pain severe enough to be called fatal, but his entire body ached uniformly.

"What the hell? What kind of attack did I take?"

He had no memory of the moment he'd been hit.

The moment something warm had gently enveloped Rei's body, the next instant he'd been blown away like this.

Gritting through the pain, Rei's gaze shifted back to the World Tree... to its trunk.

Why hadn't it launched a follow-up attack after knocking him away and knocking him unconscious, even if only for a few seconds?

For a moment, Rei wondered if it hadn't even considered him an enemy. But then, if that were the case, it wouldn't have blocked the attack with a barrier or used any means to attack in the first place.

But seeing the giant cicada pupa still clinging to the World Tree, its body about to emerge from the crack in its back, made him think it wasn't that it hadn't pursued him, but that it couldn't.

(Has it not finished molting yet? Then what was that attack earlier? ...An explosion? No, that doesn't explain why I have no burn marks. Aside from the areas covered by my Dragon Robe, it's strange that there are no burns on my face or anywhere else at all.)

Instead of burns, there was a slash wound on his cheek.

The rest of my body is covered in bruises, not cuts.

(What kind of attack did I really take?)

Confused by the fact that despite receiving the same attack, the nature of the damage was so different, Rei gripped the Death Scythe once more.

The enemy's attack method remained a complete mystery. Still, he couldn't afford to just wait and see.

Compared to before Rei lost consciousness, more leaves of the World Tree had withered, clearly showing its weakening state.

If they just waited passively for the giant cicada to emerge, the fate of the World Tree was all too clear.

"Guess I have no choice,"

He had no clue what kind of attack the enemy had launched, but even so, waiting for the pupa to emerge was not an option for Rei.

"Besides, a monster of that caliber. If I get its magic stone, I'll definitely be able to learn its skills. There's no way I'm letting it slip away here."

Gripping the Death Scythe, Rei stood up.

A dull ache still lingered faintly throughout his body, but ignoring pain of that magnitude wasn't difficult to force himself to do.

Once the fight started, the excitement and tension would likely make him forget the pain anyway.

"First, I need to understand what kind of attack it's going to use... Magic Shield."

With those words, a shield of light materialized near Rei.

A Magic Shield that could block an attack once should be able to defend against even the unknown attack he had just endured.

That was the defensive strategy born from Rei's calculations.

"Set, I'm sorry, but this time stick to providing support from a distance. Since we don't know what kind of attack they used, if you're carelessly close by, there's a chance you could get caught up in it again."

"Grrr..."

Set growled worriedly at Rei's voice.

Set didn't fully understand what kind of attack had just been launched either.

But even so, it was an attack that could blow Rei away without her even knowing when it hit, so it was clearly no ordinary attack.

Rei gently stroked Set's head.

"It's okay. Have I ever lost a fight before?"

"GRRRR!"

Set shook his head.

Truthfully, in Set's memory, there was no instance where Rei had ever clearly lost a battle.

There were battles where victory was conceded, but even then, they had fought evenly until that point.

If he fought, he won. That had been Rei's record up until now.

"Right? Then this time will be fine too. Leave it to me. Besides... it seems like we don't have much time left."

The pupa clinging to the World Tree had already fully opened its back, and the time remaining until its molting was complete was certainly not long.

Given that, what was needed here was swift action over careful deliberation.

(If I had my way, skillful speed would be best, though.)

Moving the Magic Shield, he muttered to himself before shifting his gaze toward the light sphere.

The orb flickered in response to Rei's gaze.

Though Rei couldn't understand what it was trying to say at all, it was undeniable that its brightness had diminished compared to earlier.

On that front too, it was now imperative to free the World Tree from the giant cicada as quickly as possible.

"You stay back with Set too. Don't get dragged into the fight."

The orb flickered briefly several times, as if nodding in response to Rei's words.

(Damn, it's weaker than I expected. ...Which means we need to deal with that pupa as quickly as possible.)

Rei understood perfectly well that time was running out.

Even as they stood there, the giant cicada continued to absorb magic power and sap from the World Tree.

"But I... I can't just stand by and let it kill the World Tree!"

He kicked off the ground, closing the distance straight toward the World Tree... toward the giant cicada clinging to its trunk.

The Death Scythe was channeling magic power just like before... no, even more than before, making its power even more formidable.

"Haahhhhhhh!"

He activated Sleipnir's boots, leaping step by step into the air.

He swung the Death Scythe, slicing through the barrier's protective field with almost no resistance.

Up to this point, it was the same as before, no problems whatsoever.

(Now it begins!)

The moment he screamed inwardly was almost simultaneous with the massive cicada, its metamorphosis nearly complete, beginning its attack as its body emerged from the slit in his back.

A piercing sound seemed to ring out for just an instant, and the next moment, the Magic Shield dissolved into particles of light.

Once more, an attack was launched, radiating out from the World Tree.

Seeing the magic shield vanish and the surrounding attacks, Rei finally instinctively understood what kind of assault he had endured.

"Some kind of sonic attack!"

Of course, it wasn't pure ultrasound.

A giant cicada-like monster spanning twenty meters in length... and one that had grown by absorbing Rei's magic power—the very magic power that earned him the name "non-human"—and the sap of the World Tree.

The ultrasonic-like waves it emitted were naturally not ordinary either, likely amplified by magic.

Though it was an attack that could be called a barrage of countless sonic blades, it couldn't cut through the Dragon Robe.

Still, the shockwave transmitted through the Dragon Robe to Rei's body.

This was the true nature of the attack that delivered a shock evenly across Rei's entire body.

"But once the seed cracks open, an attack like that won't faze me!"

Earlier, before he could even swing his Death Scythe, Rei had been blown away by the all-around ultrasonic attack.

This time, however, thanks to the Magic Shield, he had successfully weathered that attack.

In other words, it was impossible for the giant cicada to block the single strike of the Death Scythe unleashed by Rei. ...Yes, it should have been impossible.

Yet the Death Scythe that came down did not give Rei the satisfying feel of slicing through a pupa.

Instead, a sharp metallic clang echoed throughout the area.

"What!?"

Rei's eyes widened at the sight before him.

The strike from the Death Scythe, which should have sliced through the pupa... or the likely soft body of the newly emerged giant cicada, had been blocked by something extending from within the pupa.

To anyone who saw it, what it was would have been immediately clear.

Rei, too, knew what it was, having seen it countless times back in Japan. It was none other than...

"The leg of a giant cicada?"

Yes, it was undoubtedly the giant cicada's leg that had caught the Death Scythe.

Despite being newly molted, it showed no weakness or softness whatsoever as it absorbed the Death Scythe.

And this giant cicada was a monster. Not just any cicada, but a monster. And it had more than one leg...

"Chii!"

As if bursting from a chrysalis, Rei unleashed a strike. Leaving the Death Scythe still caught by the first leg, he thrust the stone tip forward toward the leg.

"Penetrate!"

A skill that wraps wind around the spike, increasing the thrust's power.

But such a strike was no match for the leg that had blocked the Death Scythe's magic-infused blow. Once again, a fierce metallic clang echoed around them.

(What the hell is up with a creature's leg making a metallic clang when it hits something?!)

There had been enemies who had blocked the Death Scythe before.

But using a freshly molted leg to block... that was a first even for Rei.

Rei laying back to gain distance from the giant cicada using the momentum from the deflected Penetrate.

Even so, the moment he touched the ground, it surged forward again.

Though its molting wasn't yet complete, it still possessed enough strength to block Rei's strike.

If it were allowed to finish shedding completely, it would undoubtedly become a major threat to him... and to this settlement.

It was precisely because he understood this that he pressed forward.

"Set, cover me!"

"GRRRRRRRR!"

A roar immediately answered Rei's call.

Simultaneously, multiple arrows of wind and ice flew in from behind.

Only Set, Rei's partner, could understand Rei's speed and fire them to avoid hitting him.

But... even that barrage of attacks was deflected by something transparent that suddenly emerged from the cocoon... its wings deflecting the arrows wildly off course.

"What a nuisance!"

Shouting as he closed the distance, Rei swung his Death Scythe again. Simultaneously, the feather quivered sharply, unleashing a gale-force wind around him.

There was no way Rei, suspended in midair, could withstand that gale; he was blown away.

While he took no damage, the distance between him and the giant cicada widened once more... and that distance was hopelessly vast.

Yes, by the time Rei landed and absorbed the momentum of his blowback, the giant cicada had finally emerged completely from its chrysalis.
