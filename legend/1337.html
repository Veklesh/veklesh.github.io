While Rei and the others were conducting mock battles in the courtyard of the Azimov residence... Tris was listening to a report from a subordinate in the Spier Trading Company's office.

"I see. So Precious still hasn't shown its hand?"

"Yes. The people surrounding Precious are proving more troublesome than anticipated."

Hearing his subordinate's words, Tris sighed while sipping his ice-cold fruit water.

For ordinary people, magic items that chill things were extremely expensive, but for Tris, who managed the Spier Trading Company's Gilm branch, they were something he could afford with a little stretch.

Moreover, she believed that if it meant the members of the trading company could work comfortably, an expense of this magnitude was no problem.

But right now, Tris had no leisure to savor that cold fruit water.

The plot to pit Rei against the Azott Trading Company and frame it as Tris's failure.

Progress on the investigation into it had been almost nonexistent.

The primary cause, as mentioned in the latest report, was the people surrounding Precious.

The individuals Precious brought along as her bodyguards and personal aides are obstructing Tris's investigation.

Of course, they don't interfere blatantly.

Their subtle interference was nothing short of extremely troublesome.

"Would it be impossible to turn them to our side...?"

"Yes."

Tris murmured the question as a confirmation, but the subordinate who brought the report simply nodded.

Those surrounding Precious were... Precious's... more accurately, they were all alumni of the orphanage Precious's family had run for generations.

For orphans, death was more common than life.

Moreover, some within the orphanage saw the orphans as nothing more than tools for their own business.

They made them work like slaves, pocketing the profits for themselves... Some even treated the orphans as objects of desire.

Compared to such orphanages, the one run by Precious's family provided proper meals and spared them from unjust treatment.

For that reason, those who came from orphanages felt a strong trust in Precious.

...Though it's also true that the education they received at the orphanage had a strong influence on this.

For that very reason, the possibility of the orphans betraying Precious and siding with Tris was nonexistent.

"I knew it would be troublesome, but it still is."

"We are conducting investigations around Precious and her guards, keeping a low profile..."

His vague words were proof the investigation wasn't progressing as smoothly as hoped.

That said, Precious herself found even Tris's subordinates' investigation troublesome, prompting her to take the step of requesting Slain's cooperation.

"Well, it can't be helped. Please keep pushing forward. Given the current situation, we can't afford to let Rei cause trouble. We need to resolve this before it comes to that."

"Yes. We'll do our best. ...But the slum district..."

Tris nodded in understanding at his subordinate's hesitant words.

Tris had naturally received the information that Precious had pointedly headed for the slums a few days prior.

Likewise, he knew Rei had gone there too.

When he received that information, he wondered if Rei might be planning to make a move against Precious and the others in the slums. However, at that time, he had no subordinates available to send to the slums, putting him a step behind.

He immediately dispatched someone hired through Gilm who knew the slums well... but by then, the situation had already concluded.

Still, from the information gathered, he learned that what happened in the slums was merely a warning shot fired by Rei, and no actual harm had come to Precious.

"We're working on gathering personnel for that side as well. We'll assemble them as quickly as possible and send them over, so please do your best with the current situation for now."

"Yes, understood. Then..."

Just as he started to say that, the man abruptly stopped speaking.

He looked around warily, as if alert to something.

Seeing his subordinate like this, it wasn't hard for Tris to grasp what was happening.

Tris was, after all, a merchant, not someone who put himself in the thick of battle.

But even so, being entrusted with a branch of Gilm meant he'd navigated his fair share of dangerous situations.

Just from his subordinate's reaction, Tris understood the current situation was far from good.

"Precious?"

"I'm not entirely sure yet. But given the circumstances, I'd say it's probably correct."

Pulling a dagger from his waist, the man moved closer to Tris, ready for anything, while keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

Tris must have realized there was no time for leisurely fruit juice here either. Though he couldn't directly confront the enemy himself, he stood up, careful not to obstruct his subordinates.

"Is anyone there? Come out!"

The shout of his subordinate echoed through the area, and immediately two men entered through the door.

They were the two men who had been standing guard at the door.

Swords in hand, their expressions stern and focused, they checked Tris to confirm he was unharmed, their faces briefly relaxing in relief.

But... at that very moment, a violent shock shook the branch office, accompanied by screams.

It was fortunate that nothing had happened in Tris's room, but the screams now were definitely not ordinary.

"Damn. They weren't after Tris, but targeted this place instead. ...Ruscur, you go check on the shop. South stays here with me to guard Tris. There's a good chance that scream was a diversion."

"Yes, sir!"

"Understood!"

The man called Ruscur swiftly exited the room, while the man called South remained on standby beside Tris.

"Edistol, what do you think? Is this still Precious's doing?"

At Tris's words, the man who had been giving orders to the two guards... Edistol, nodded swiftly.

"It's probably correct. Unless, of course, Lord Tris has incurred someone else's grudge and was attacked by them instead."

"...We can't rule that out either, can we?"

The Spier Trading Company... no, in this case, it should be said Tris, is aware it engages in somewhat forceful dealings.

For that very reason, if asked whether they had no enemies, they couldn't honestly nod in agreement.

But at the same time, if asked whether they were hated enough for someone to directly attack the shop like this, he couldn't honestly nod to that either.

(Could it even be an accident or something unrelated to an attack?)

Even so, Tris's instincts, honed over years as a merchant, told him the screams that echoed at this moment were unlikely to be anything but an attack.

"It seems rather early for Precious to take action... What do you think?"

"I don't know. Perhaps they judged that the incident in the slums had completely turned Rei against them and decided to strike first."

"...It really stings that we were too slow to send people to the slums."

"It couldn't be helped. We never suspected Precious had connections to the slums in the first place. Besides, Precious only arrived in Gilm after spring had come. That was quite some time after you, Lord Tris."

"Even so, the reason she established contact with figures in the slums, aside from Precious and her subordinates being competent... must be that it offered significant benefits to the slum dwellers as well. She probably offered generous rewards."

Edistol nodded at Tris, who muttered while jiggling the flesh on his cheek.

The next instant, the office door swung open and Ruscur appeared.

"It seems a carriage crashed into us. The horse apparently panicked over something... but—"

Mid-explanation, Ruscur's words abruptly halted... and a horn suddenly sprouted from his forehead.

No, it was only for an instant that he thought it was a horn.

As Edistol realized it wasn't horns, but rather the slender tips of weapons specialized for thrusting—rapiers or estoc—he stepped forward to protect Tris.

A moment later, South must have realized his partner had been killed by an attack from behind.

Watching Ruscur collapse to the floor, eyes rolled back, he followed after Edistol.

"Heh heh, I'm afraid my role ends here. I hope we don't meet again, eh?"

A woman with black hair cut straight across her shoulders.

Her features were fairly well-proportioned, but she wasn't exactly a peerless beauty.

This woman, who could truly be found anywhere, smiled as she swung her rapier.

Brain matter, blood, and bodily fluids clinging to the tip of the blade splattered onto Edistol's face.

Yet, Edistol showed no sign of caring, closing the distance between himself and the woman.

But... the next instant, the woman slammed something onto the floor, filling the area with smoke.

"Guh!"

The smoke halted Ejistor's movement for an instant, but that split second proved fatal in this case.

When he came to his senses, the smoke had completely vanished for some reason, and at the same time, the woman's figure had also disappeared.

"Damn it, did I let her get away?"

"Edistol!"

Ejestol nodded at South's words and turned his gaze back to Tris.

"I never expected them to make a move at this stage... What should we do?"

"Yes, you're right. This whole incident is a bit suspicious. They should understand that using force would make it easier for Rei to come out. So why did they pull this stunt? ...Edistol, just asking, did you recognize that woman earlier?"

At Tris's question, Edistol shook his head.

"I recognize most of Precious's people, but not all of them. It's highly likely she's someone not openly associated with them. Or..."

Edistol glanced at Ruscur lying on the floor and spoke again.

"I asked earlier, but I think there's a possibility it could be someone not affiliated with Precious. What do you think?"

"I don't have any specific suspicions about someone who would act so blatantly... but that doesn't mean it's impossible. More importantly, please treat Ruscur with respect."

His association with Ruscur hadn't been particularly long.

He'd hired him after arriving in Gilm, so it had only been a few months.

Still, as a fellow member of the Spier Trading Company, even if only for a short time, he was a comrade they had shared experiences with. Burying his body with respect was Tris's way.

Egestor understood this and gave a small nod.

"Damn it! Who did this?!"

South slammed his fist on the floor.

He hadn't been part of the same party as Ruscur, but they had still been friends for several years.

That very fact made it impossible for him to remain calm after seeing him killed like this.

Tris glanced at South's state and once again wore a troubled expression.

(Seriously, why do this? Having gone this far, Precious can't back down now. Did they know that and still choose to act?)

While it wasn't yet certain that this incident was definitely Precious's doing, Tris was half convinced.

Yet, she also harbored a question.

Why resort to such a violent act?

Why attack the shop so blatantly in the first place... But then, the screams from earlier came back to her.

Ruscur, who had gone to check on the shop, had his life taken by the assassin before he could explain everything.

"Egestor, please go check on the shop. That scream earlier is troubling me too."

"But..."

Normally, Edistol would obey Tris's orders immediately, but now he hesitated.

...Understandably so. Ruscur, who had just died, and South were of comparable strength.

One of them hadn't noticed the sneak attack from behind and was killed with a single blow.

If that woman appeared again while he was gone, who would protect Tris?

They had already fled the scene, but the possibility that the enemy had feigned retreat only to return for another attack couldn't be ruled out.

After an attack just ended, it was only natural for tension to fade.

Tris seemed to understand what Edistol was getting at. She thought for a moment, then spoke.

"All right, then I'll come with you."

"Wait, hold on!"

Tris shook her head at Ejistol, who was telling her to absolutely not go out into a place where she'd be an easy target for assassins.

"I trust Edistol. Come on, let's go. If something's happening at the shop, I, as the person in charge, have to go..."

"Tris!? Dammit, South, we're going too!"

"Huh? Oh, yes! ...See you later."

After a brief word to Ruscur, South followed after Edistol.

What they saw was a carriage smashed into the store.

Fortunately, aside from a few people with minor injuries like bumps and scrapes, there seemed to be no fatalities or serious injuries.

"Everyone, stay calm. First, treat the injured. Make sure the customers are safe. And those who can help, get the carriage out of the store."

Tris gave the orders, and the surrounding chaos gradually subsided.
