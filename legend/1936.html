"So, could you bleed it out for me, just like you did with the Snow Cyclops yesterday?"

“Sure, that’s fine.”

Standing before the numerous corpses of snow apes, Rei replied to the guild staff member’s words.

In reality, while draining the blood is one reason, they’re also splitting up because they don’t want anyone to see them extracting the magic stones or absorbing them.

……One reason was that while hanging them from a tree was the quickest way to drain their blood, there just weren’t any suitable trees around here.

“What about Vihera? It looks like Byune is helping take down the Kobolds. Do you want to come with me?”

Looking in that direction, he could see the adventurers fighting the Kobolds that had once again escaped the earthen maze Rei had created using terrain manipulation.

Among those adventurers was Byune, and Rei asked what Vihera—who served as Byune’s partner and guardian—was going to do.

At Rei’s words, Vihera thought for a moment… then shook her head.

“I’ll pass. If I stay here, other powerful monsters might be drawn by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood. Besides, someone needs to be able to communicate with Byune if anything happens, right?”

Hearing that, Rei didn’t press the matter any further.

After all, even though Rei had known Byune for quite some time and could generally tell what he meant from his gestures, he wasn’t able to fully understand Byune’s intentions the way Vihera could.

“I get it. Then I’ll handle the defense over here. …Though if it were Vihera, she’d probably say she’d welcome powerful monsters with open arms.”

“Oh, you get it, don’t you? As for powerful monsters, I don’t mind how many show up.”

Saying that, she wore a dreamy expression, as if lost in thoughts of the battle.

Seeing her in her usual, typical Vihera manner, Rei smiled... and, watching the guild staff being overwhelmed by the aura Vihera was giving off, called out to Set.

“Hmm… I was sure monsters would be gathering here, drawn by the scent of the Snow Cyclops’ blood, but there’s nothing here. Or maybe they went over to the Giganto Turtle?”

When they arrived at the spot where they had drained the Snow Cyclops of blood and extracted its magic stone the day before, there were no monsters of any kind to be found.

He would have preferred to find an unknown monster, but deciding it was better than finding only Kobolds and goblins, he descended to the surface.

“...Ah, but monsters did come after all.”

Noticing several footprints in the snow that had fallen last night, I muttered that to myself.

Still, since there were no monsters around at the moment, I had to prioritize draining the Snow Ape’s blood.

Since there were ultimately more than ten corpses, I bled them all.

Fortunately, since they were less than half the size of yesterday’s Snow Cyclops, finding a place to hang them wasn’t a problem.

Rei took a rope from the Misty Ring and began hanging the snow monkeys’ corpses with their wounds facing downward.

Since many of the corpses had not only had their necks severed but also their torsos cut off, the number of bodies to hang ended up being quite a few after all.

In any case, while Rei was busy hanging the bodies like this, Set stood a short distance away, keeping a watchful eye on the surroundings.

He reasoned that since they were draining so much blood so openly, it wouldn’t be surprising if monsters drawn by the scent showed up.

The fact that he was keeping watch on his own initiative, without Rei even having to say a word, suggested that this sort of thing was second nature to Set as well.

(Well, even if there were monsters nearby, it’s more likely they’d be drawn here by the scent of this Giganto Turtle’s blood.)

The Giganto Turtle and the Snow Cyclops.

If asked which of the two would be more attractive to a monster, it would undoubtedly be the former.

In terms of rank, that was the deciding factor.

In reality, it’s just that the opponent is tough; normally speaking, there’s no doubt that the Snow Cyclops’s blood and flesh would be a more than sufficient feast for the monsters in the area.

“Anyway, there’s no doubt I’ll have some free time while I’m bleeding, so I wonder what I should do.”

Muttering to himself, Rei surveyed his surroundings.

The pungent smell of the Snow Ape’s blood hung heavily in the air.

Although the amount of blood per individual was less than that of a Snow Cyclops, there were many of them.

Because of that, the total volume of blood is substantial, and as a result, the pungent, rusty smell wafting through the air grows stronger.

"Hmm... Is this enough now?"

The Snow Ape, severed at the torso, naturally bleeds profusely, and above all, the sight of its internal organs—such as its stomach and intestines—hanging from the cut surface is extremely unsettling.

That said, it’s been several years since I came to this world of Elgin.

Having dissected monsters countless times, I was already used to sights like this.

“I only need two magic stones, so for now, I’ll take this one.”

He slit open the chest of the snow ape, which was now just a torso, and extracted the magic stone from within.

Since the blood hadn’t completely drained yet, the magic stone was covered in more blood than usual, staining Rei’s hands.

But Rei immediately wiped the magic stone with a cloth she pulled from her Misty Ring… and turned her gaze to another snow monkey hanging from a nearby tree.

This snow monkey’s neck had been slashed by the magical claws extending from Vihera’s gauntlets, and it had already lost a considerable amount of blood by the time the battle ended.

As a result, a great deal of blood had already flowed, and while not as much as the snow monkey whose torso had been split in two by the Death Scythe, the amount of blood remaining was already quite small.

He slashed open its chest in the same way to extract the magic stone...

「Set！」

Calling out to Set in a slightly louder voice, Set—who had been keeping watch on the surroundings—approached Rei with a happy expression, as if to ask, “Did you call me?”

While being called by Rei was a major reason for his joy, the prospect of absorbing the magic stone was also a significant factor.

Absorbing the magic stone of an unknown monster means Set gets stronger, so he’s probably happy about that too.

“Well, here’s the magic stone you’ve been waiting for… What kind of skill do you think it has?”

“Grr…”

In response to Rei’s question, Set growls as if to say he doesn’t know.

In fact, while he’d often acquired skills that the monster in question was known for, there had also been times when he wondered, “Why this skill from that monster’s magic stone?”

That’s precisely why Set probably didn’t know what specific skill he would acquire—or if he wouldn’t acquire anything at all—by absorbing the Snow Ape’s magic stone.

Of course, since even Rei didn’t know what kind of skill could be acquired from that magic stone, the only way to find out was to actually absorb it.

“Can’t you make a guess, Set? …Well, since it’s called a Snow Ape, maybe an ice-based skill? After all, during the battle with Vihera, it did wrap ice around its claws and fight.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a cry, as if to say, “I hope so.”

As for Set’s ice-based skills, it would likely be Ice Arrow.

Or, though it’s water rather than ice, there’s also the skill Water Ball.

(If possible, I’d really like his Ice Arrow level to go up.)

Currently, Ice Arrow is at level four.

In other words, if it levels up here, it will reach level five.

And once it reaches level five, the skill will be strengthened to a degree that’s incomparable to what it was before.

“Alright, Set. Here.”

As soon as he said that, Rei held out one of the magic stones he was carrying to Set.

Set swallowed the magic stone with his beak...

“...No good, huh?”

“Grr…”

Feeling disappointed that the usual announcement didn’t play in his mind, Rei muttered.

Set made a gurgling sound in his throat, as if to say, “I’m sorry,” to Rei.

“Don’t worry about it. You got the skill from yesterday’s Snow Cyclops, so there’s no need to get that down. …Well, in that case, with the Death Scythe…”

Petting Set to encourage him, Rei pulls the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring and tosses it into the air.

In the next instant, he swings the Death Scythe...

[Death Scythe has acquired the skill "Ice and Snow Slash Lv. 1"]

The announcement echoes in his mind.

“Oh.”

“Growl!”

As Rei lets out a cry of surprise, Set purrs happily, as if to say, “Congratulations.”

“Ice Snow Slash, Ice Snow Slash… huh. Considering how Snow Monkeys fight, I can pretty much imagine how effective it’ll be… Set, step back a bit.”

Saying that, and only after seeing Set obediently step back, Rei swings the Death Scythe.

“Ice and Snow Slash!”

The moment she activated the skill, the blade of the Death Scythe grew slightly larger.

That was because the blade of the Death Scythe had been enveloped in ice.

The blade made of ice looked sharp enough. It did, but...

“Hmm… It seems like it would be more powerful if I just channeled magic through the Death Scythe and swung it normally… Well, maybe it’s effective against enemies whose weakness is ice? It’s helpful that the attack range has widened, though.”

Since the blade is covered in ice, the Death Scythe’s attack range has expanded, albeit slightly.

That is an undeniable fact, but if asked whether there’s a need to intentionally use this skill… Rei could only tilt his head in confusion.

That said, for Rei, who can only use fire magic, an ice-based attack method is quite valuable.

In that sense, there might be an opportunity to use it.

“For now, it’s a skill that won’t be very useful during the winter. …It might come in handy around summer, or if I go to a volcano or a desert. Oh, but would desert monsters actually welcome the ice?”

With a slight doubt lingering in his mind, Rei deactivated Ice Slash with a hint of satisfaction and sheathed the Death Scythe in the Misty Ring.

“Grrrrr!”

Set, who had been watching Rei nearby, let out a growl as if to congratulate him, as if he’d forgotten that he hadn’t been able to learn the skill himself.

From Set’s perspective, seeing his beloved Rei master a skill like this was a joyous occasion.

So much so that he let out a joyful growl before he even had a chance to feel sad about his own failure.

“Thanks, Set. I’m glad you’re happy for me.”

Seeing how happy Set was, Rei smiled and stroked his head.

Amidst the lingering scent of the snow monkey’s blood, Rei and Set played together for a while… Eventually, once all the blood had drained from the snow monkey’s corpse hanging from the tree, they took it down and began to pack it away.

In about a minute, all the snow monkey carcasses hanging from the surrounding trees had been stored inside Rei’s Misty Ring.

(A feast of wine and flesh… Well, there’s no wine, so it’s not exactly a “pool of wine,” but in the sense of a “forest of flesh,” I suppose that’s accurate…? Though to an observer, it would likely be a forest of flesh that’s utterly uninteresting and nothing but eerie.)

In Rei’s mind, “a feast of wine and women” meant… essentially a banquet held by those in high positions where they gather large numbers of women and wine.

The actual meaning might be quite different, but in Rei’s mind, that was exactly how he saw it.

“Well, now that the ‘meat grove’ is taken care of, I suppose it’s time to head back. I need to have this snow monkey dismantled, too.”

“Grr?”

"Meat forest?" Set growled in puzzlement.

Even though Set understood Rei, it was only natural for him to be confused when a term like “meat grove” suddenly came up.

“Nah, never mind. Let’s head back for now.”

Was Rei looking a little embarrassed because he’d said the word “meat forest”?

In any case, Rei hopped onto Set’s back and took flight.

“Then, please leave the dissection of this Snow Ape to us.”

Rei, who had been told this by a guild official right next to where the Giganto Turtle was being butchered, nodded and spoke up.

“You did a pretty clean job butchering that Snow Cyclops yesterday, too. I’m not doubting your ability to handle this Snow Ape.”

Just as Rei had said, the dissection of the Snow Cyclops he’d requested yesterday had been done magnificently… in fact, it had been dissected even more neatly than Rei himself could have done.

Given that, it was only natural that Rei had no concerns about the dissection.

...That said, since the Snow Cyclops had been butchered by someone who happened to have a relatively free hand while others were working on the Giganto Turtle, it wasn’t necessarily the case that the same person who butchered it yesterday would be the one to butcher the Snow Ape today.

In any case, Rei replied to the guild staff member’s words and immediately began retrieving the Snow Ape’s corpse from inside the Misty Ring.
