The plan to attack Mejogo today has, of course, been communicated to Daskar in advance.

Given that, the Daskar, naturally eager to know the outcome immediately, had arranged for Ara, who was entrusted with Rei's Paired Orb, to come to the lord's mansion.

That's precisely why, the moment Elena used the Paired Orb, a response came immediately from the other side.

"Lady Elena, it seems you were safe after all."

Seeing Elena's image reflected in the Paired Orb, Ara breathed a sigh of relief from the bottom of her heart.

Of course, Ara knew exactly how much power Elena possessed.

Even so, as someone devoted to Elena, seeing her safe would naturally bring relief.

"Ah, it's not exactly that there were no problems here... but still, the planned actions were completed safely. There were a few unexpected results, though."

"Unexpected results, you say?"

Ara couldn't see Ryutas and his guards standing a short distance away.

Elena shook her head reassuringly at Ara, who asked the question with a hint of concern.

"Didn't I say everything here is fine? There's no lie in those words. However, a matter has arisen that requires some consultation with Lord Daskar. Ara, are you currently at the lord's manor?"

"Yes, I was told to stay here today."

Had this been the words of a noble who only thought of his own interests, Ara would not have listened.

But Ara knew Daskar wasn't that kind of noble.

It was only natural, given how often they ended up involved in things together.

Elena, seemingly understanding Ara's feelings, smiled and spoke.

"I see. Well then, without further delay, please take over for Lord Daskar."

"Understood."

As she said this, Ara's expression held a hint of disappointment.

As someone deeply devoted to Elena, Ara probably wanted to talk with her longer if possible.

But now, unlike usual, there was no time for lengthy conversation.

"We can talk with Ara tonight, right?"

"Yes!"

Elena's words must have pleased Ara. She nodded with a beaming smile, then immediately called for someone and asked them to inform Daskar that Elena had sent word.

Normally, Daskar was extremely busy with his duties as a lord.

If this were an ordinary town, it might be different, but Gilm is a town located on the frontier.

What's more, due to the ongoing expansion work in Gilm, he had even more tasks than usual.

Nobles and merchants also frequently sought meetings, hoping to gain some profit from the construction.

Meeting Daskar under the current circumstances should have been extremely difficult.

But... After Ara sent someone to inform him that Elena had contacted her, Daskar appeared in Ara's room within minutes.

He had been meeting with a merchant, but it was merely a courtesy call, not a particularly important discussion, which likely contributed to his availability.

Normally, a merchant might feel displeased if their meeting partner left during the session, but since the merchant was eager to make a favorable impression on Daskar, they wouldn't protest under ordinary circumstances.

In fact, by insisting Daskar not worry about him, he had even succeeded in making a favorable impression.

Though, the merchant did manage to secure a promise to meet again as soon as possible, which was quite merchant-like.

In any case, Daskar, who had interrupted his meeting with the merchant to appear, sent a grateful glance to Ara for giving up her seat and sat down before the Paired Orb.

By then, Elena had learned of Daskar's arrival and switched places with Rei.

The task of retaliating against Jaya was the job Rei had received from Daskar.

Given that, it was likely his judgment that he, merely an assistant, couldn't report on it himself.

"Good work, Rei. Now, without further ado, I'd like to hear your report."

"Yes. First, regarding the retaliation against Jaya, I believe we achieved sufficient results."

Having stated this, Rei began recounting the events from when he infiltrated—though "stormed" would be a more accurate description—the giant's nest.

The report that came from Rei's mouth was nothing short of horrific.

The giant offspring born by tearing through their mothers' wombs were particularly horrific.

Beyond that, there was the black crystal, the rampage of the giants within the nest, the deprogramming of the prostitutes, and the battle between the Resistance and Jaya.

After hearing these explanations, Daskar paused briefly before speaking.

"Rei, did you ask what the prostitutes who escaped from Mejogo plan to do now?"

"No, after providing support during the skirmish with Jaya, they came straight here..."

Hearing Rei's words, Daskar thought for a moment... then spoke.

"Rei, about those prostitutes... Are you certain they have nowhere to go?"

"With that many of them, probably. They could stay in Mejogo, but most likely wouldn't want to."

Mejogo was the place where the prostitutes had been forced to submit to men they didn't love, against their will.

Those who would want to stay in a place holding such memories were, if not nonexistent, surely a minority.

"Hmm, in that case... Rei, I'm afraid I need you to do one more job. Protect those prostitutes and the resistance members over here."

"Are you sure? That means meddling in Reburina's internal affairs. They'll probably complain."

"What are you talking about? Those Jaya people tried to take women from Gilm too! We managed to stop them then, but that might not be the first time. There's a good chance they've already taken others before we even knew about it."

Rei couldn't deny the truth in those words.

But Rei understood that what Daskar had said wasn't his true objective.

"So, you mean gathering evidence?"

"That too. Besides, we might be able to acquire some capable personnel. There's no need to sit idly by here."

So that's the real goal.

That's what Rei thought, but even so, something still didn't quite sit right with him.

Considering this single incident, the Resistance seemed to be filled with capable individuals.

Indeed, even from Rei's perspective, having made contact with Sura who currently leads the Resistance, that was an undeniable fact.

But... the Resistance's main fighting force had already been crushed by the Giants. In terms of pure combat strength, they were by no means superior.

Besides, no matter how many Daskars secured for the Resistance, if asked whether anyone would actually leave the country of Reburina where they were born and raised to come to Gilm... no, to the Kingdom of Mireana, it was a dubious proposition.

Rei's question was: what good would it do to offer favors to such people?

It certainly wasn't solely because he felt sorry for the prostitutes and wanted to help the resistance... though that wasn't the only reason, that much was clear.

Of course, this wasn't because Daskar thought prostitutes were expendable.

Daskar himself undoubtedly pitied the prostitutes, but the Daskar of today was, above all, a lord.

Because of that position, it was an undeniable fact that he couldn't judge matters based solely on his own feelings.

(Which means there's definitely something else going on... but what?)

To resolve that question, Rei asked bluntly.

"What's your purpose?"

"Manpower. ...As you know, expansion work is currently underway in Gilm. But with a project this large, we can use all the manpower we can get. Of course, if anyone doesn't want to come to Gilm and wishes to return to their hometown, we won't force them."

"So, if someone wants to return home, they'll just leave midway?"

'From our perspective, either is fine. They can part ways immediately, or they can save money at Gilm before returning to their hometown.'

From Daskar's perspective, it seems his judgment is that as long as he can bring a certain number of people to Gilm, that's sufficient.

Upon hearing this, Rei eventually nodded.

The women protected by the Resistance were all victims of Jaya.

Given that, he naturally wanted to help them if possible. Still, if they decided to return on their own, Rei had no intention of saying anything.

"Understood. I'll cooperate with the Resistance here and relay Daskar's proposal. And one more thing... Ryutas."

At Rei's words, Ryutas, whose name had been called, appeared.

On the other side of the Paired Orb, Daskar wore a puzzled expression at the sight of the unfamiliar face.

Daskar, whose imposing appearance was fitting for his reputation, somehow looked humorous with that puzzled expression.

"Pleased to meet you, Daskar Larks, Marquis of the Borderlands. I am Ryutas, currently under Rei's protection."

'Under Rei's protection? So there's some reason for that?'

If Ryutas were just some ordinary person, Rei wouldn't have gone out of his way to protect him.

At that judgmental remark, Ryutas smiled.

Of course, this smile wasn't born from genuine joy or amusement; it was calculated, as always, to avoid causing the other person discomfort.

"Yes. Actually, despite appearances, I am the son of the one currently leading Jaya."

"Oh?"

Daskar murmured briefly at Ryutas' words.

However, his expression was different from before. He fixed his gaze on the man before him—or more precisely, before the Paired Orb—as if trying to see right through to the depths of Ryutas' soul.

Faced with Daskar's gaze, Ryutas held his breath for a moment.

Though skilled at controlling his expression, the intensity he sensed in Daskar's gaze held that kind of power.

Still, the fact that he was only overwhelmed for an instant was perhaps because it was Ryutas.

'Using the phrase "son of the leader" implies you're not the successor, is that correct?'

"Yes. There are several other children considered potential successors besides me. ...Though, in my case, I wasn't expected to be one from the start. And now that I've defied Jaya by cooperating with Rei like this, I can't return to that position of successor anyway."

'Why did you do such a thing? From your position, betraying Jaya should be fatal, shouldn't it? Though, if it's true that Jaya was abandoned by the country, then I suppose you could say you accurately read the situation.'

"The main reason was that I couldn't follow my father's methods anymore. After all, he's been discarded by the country like this."

Daskar gave a small nod at Ryutas' words.

To begin with, the very act of forcibly taking women from another country—even if Jaya served as the official front—was reckless.

Until now, the Black Crystal and the slave collars had prevented major upheaval, but that Black Crystal had already been destroyed by Rei, freeing the women... and worse, even the giant—the trump card of the nation of Reburina—had gone rogue.

That said, the rampaging giant had already been annihilated by Rei and her group.

If they sided with Jaya, they would have to face the army of the Kingdom of Reburina... and the nation itself.

If they sided with Reburina, they would likely find themselves at odds with the Kingdom of Mireana, not to mention Jaya. And they would do so without their trump card, the giant.

The people of Reburina are trying to fabricate the existence of Jaya as a common enemy with the Kingdom of Mireana, but Rei has already reported this to Daskar.

Given that, their scheme—if it can even be called that—is bound to fail.

Therefore, the best choice under the current circumstances is undoubtedly to side with Rei and his group.

Daskar firmly met the gaze directed at him, laden with that meaning.

An exchange of glances between two men.

Everyone present remained silent, not wanting to interrupt the two... until finally, Daskar broke the silence.

"Understood. We'll protect you here."

He declared in a deep, resonant tone.

Having drawn those words from him, Ryutas let a small smile touch his lips.

Had Daskar declared he didn't need Ryutas here, Ryutas... and his guards would have had nowhere to go but the afterlife.

Just avoiding that outcome made Ryutas feel his life was worth risking.
