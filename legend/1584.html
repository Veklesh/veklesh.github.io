Giants emerged one after another from behind the destroyed gate.

Behind them, Elena and Vihera continued to rampage, trampling the giants beneath their feet.

Unable to fight those two, the giants emerged from where the door had been and charged toward Rei outside.

However, the space where the door had been was far from wide. It was impossible for even three or four giants to exit together.

...Yet, perhaps overwhelmed by hunger, the giants couldn't think that far ahead. For now, they were desperate to get outside and devour Rei.

Thus, the giants that emerged one or two at a time from the room had their lives taken away in an instant by Rei's Death Scythe and Twilight Spear.

However, these giants stood about three meters tall, truly massive creatures.

With such creatures being killed one after another, their corpses inevitably began piling up around Rei.

Unlike where the giants had originally been, the space where Rei now stood... and where Byune and Yellow were keeping watch a short distance away, was by no means spacious.

In fact, compared to the area where the giants had been waiting, it was extremely cramped.

With giant corpses piling up one after another in such a confined space, it was only natural that there would be no room left to step.

Giants losing their balance by stepping on their fallen comrades' corpses was probably a relief for Rei too.

However, just like those giants, Rei also found himself in a situation where there was no place to step due to the giant corpses.

Under such circumstances, it naturally became difficult for Rei to evade the giants' attacks. Consequently, he found himself experiencing a delicate busyness: fighting the attacking giants while simultaneously using the Misty Ring to recover their corpses.

He kept up this fight for over ten minutes.

In that very instant, as he dodged a blow from a drooling giant reaching out, its mouth watering with hunger, and crushed its skull with a thrust of Twilight Spear, Byune behind him called out.

"Hmph!"

It was a sound meant to grab Rei's attention from the fight.

Hearing it, Rei leapt backward, leaving the fallen giant's corpse behind.

A look of mild surprise flashed across Rei's face.

Placing Byune behind Rei for vigilance had been purely precautionary... truly nothing more than that.

Or rather, it was, expressed in the past tense.

In the other room—though it was far too spacious to be called a room—Marina had been left behind.

The rampaging giant had already devoured most of those inside that room, and of the survivors, the majority were injured.

Given that, it was unlikely anyone could mount a counterattack against Marina, and even if they did, she would handle it easily.

That's precisely why the worry about her back was nothing more than a precaution...

(What happened? No, who's coming?)

Leaping backward, Rei threw the Twilight Spear the instant he landed.

This time, he threw it from his left hand, not his usual throwing arm.

Its power was several levels lower compared to when thrown with his dominant right hand, but even so, with Rei's strength and the Twilight Spear's performance, taking down a giant was easy.

...Though, to avoid carelessly piercing too many giants' bodies and endangering Elena and the others fighting in the room beyond, he needed to pull it back immediately after piercing just a few.

"Who's coming?"

Rei voiced his question as he pulled the Twilight Spear back to his hand.

But instead of answering, Byune stared intently at the path leading to the entrance... and the next moment, still holding Yellow, she sprinted toward it.

In the end, only Rei remained behind. Judging that Byune's actions were probably correct for now, he resumed fighting the giants.

...Rei didn't entertain any worries that Byune had abandoned the scene and fled.

It had been quite some time since they formed their party.

That very fact gave him absolute confidence that Byune would never do such a thing.

And above all, the sound of someone running towards Rei's location was clearly audible.

There was absolutely no room for thought like trying to be quiet—it was the sound of someone running at full speed, relentlessly.

From the sound alone, it was clear they were running for their life, heedless of anything else.

Because this was a cave, and because it was a single path, there was no way to miss the sound of those footsteps.

And approaching those footsteps were Byune's footsteps.

They would likely meet within moments.

Thinking this, Rei hurled the Twilight Spear... And indeed, within minutes, Byune returned with a man.

"Haa, haa, haa... Thank goodness, you were here."

The man, panting heavily, was someone Rei recognized.

The man who had been by Ryutasu's side as his guard.

As someone trained as a guard, he shouldn't have been panting like this after just a short run.

Yet this man was panting because he had run at full speed... no, beyond full speed, driven by the need to inform Rei of the situation outside as quickly as possible.

Beside the exhausted guard, Byune showed no signs of breathlessness, likely because she hadn't run as far. She sat with Yellow perched on her head, looking at Rei.

"So, you're the one who was with Ryutas, right? What the hell did you come here to do...!"

With that voice, the Twilight Spear was hurled once more.

The guard was startled by the sudden action right before his eyes, but his expression quickly shifted to utter astonishment.

The spear that had pierced the giant reappeared in Rei's hand the very next instant.

"Huh? Ah..."

He couldn't comprehend what he was seeing.

The guard looked utterly bewildered, but Rei paid him no mind, repeatedly throwing the Twilight Spear and snatching it back.

"Hm?"

Through the doorway once filled with giants, he saw several of them collapse to the ground.

And atop the fallen giants' corpses stood Elena, Mirage in hand.

"Rei? That person over there..."

"Ah, he's Ryutas's guard. He had some business..."

"They're enemies!"

The guard's shout cut off Rei's words.

Right now, they needed to explain the situation in this cave as quickly as possible and get help for Ryutas as soon as possible.

That was precisely why he had interrupted Elena and Rei's conversation.

At the word "enemy," Rei paused his conversation with Elena and turned his gaze toward the guard.

"Enemy? More giant reinforcements? Or is it Jaya's?"

"N-no. The enemy is from this country... soldiers of the Reburina Kingdom! And likely over a hundred... no, if things go badly, possibly hundreds!"

"...What?"

Rei's movements froze for an instant at the unexpected words.

The only reason it didn't matter was because Elena was systematically taking down the nearby giants.

Brushing the blood from Mirage's blade, Elena spoke.

"I see. So you judged that the commotion we caused made it impossible to keep this giant incident hidden any longer. And to bury everything in darkness..."

"No, but... Could the soldiers of the Kingdom of Reburina even handle the giants in the first place?"

"The kingdom's higher-ups probably don't even know the giants are running wild. ...Not that it's surprising, though."

Only a select few should know the black crystal was destroyed.

Those who were heading through the underground passage to Mejogo's subterranean facility were wiped out by Rei and the others.

Some might have survived with injuries, but they certainly wouldn't have the luxury to report back... and above all, they'd have no means to do so.

Anyone trying to enter from Mejogo is being blocked by Set.

The only possible candidates are those who were already inside Mejogo's underground facility.

When Rei and the others stormed Mejogo's underground facility, almost none of the Jaya personnel, including the researchers, were left.

A few remained, but the number Rei and the others encountered was limited to that extent.

Based on Rei's estimates, there could have been dozens, or even hundreds, still in that underground facility.

With that many people, it wouldn't be surprising if someone discovered the Black Crystal had been destroyed.

(The question is, how did they report it to Jaya's higher-ups... No, it wouldn't be strange if there were other secret passages or something like that. Did it reach the Kingdom of Reburina from Jaya? But that seems a bit hard to imagine.)

Regardless of the attack on the underground facility, the destruction of the Black Crystal was something Jaya would absolutely want to keep hidden.

Would Jaya really just casually inform the Kingdom of Reburina about it? That was questionable. And even if it had been known, the time it took for soldiers to reach this cave was far too short.

Moving military forces is fundamentally a massive undertaking.

You can't just say "We're deploying" and expect a simple "Yes, understood."

You have to gather soldiers who might be training, working, or even enjoying their days off, arm them, and above all, move them from Rossi to this forest where the cave is located.

Considering the time that would take, it made more sense to believe they were attacked here, regardless of whether Rei and the others destroyed the black crystal or not.

"So? Did Ryutas tell me to report this?"

"Yes. And he said they'd buy us time, so he'd like reinforcements if possible."

"...Huh."

Rei let out a slightly impressed sound at the words from the guard's mouth.

He'd assumed they'd simply relayed the information that soldiers from the Kingdom of Reburina had arrived and then fled themselves.

Of course, Rei understood why Ryutas had deliberately stayed behind, risking his own life to buy time.

He wanted to do Rei a favor, even a small one.

That was the gist of it, he figured.

Even so, the fact that he would actually risk his own life to that extent was unexpected, even for Rei.

You could say he was pleasantly surprised.

(He must have had a plan to win, but still... putting his life on the line like that... well, that does make him somewhat trustworthy.)

But just because he risked his life doesn't mean he's truly trustworthy.

But between someone who just gives orders from behind and a man who steps into a life-threatening situation himself...

When asked which was more trustworthy, Rei's view was the latter.

Of course, if it were someone in a position like a feudal lord, things might be a bit different.

(No need to even think about what to do.)

Gathering his thoughts in an instant, Rei addressed Elena.

"Can I leave this place to you?"

"That's fine... but are you going to go yourself?"

At Rei's words, Elena frowned disapprovingly, her beautiful brows knitting together.

The son of the man leading Jaya was Ryutas.

Knowing that, it was only natural Elena wouldn't feel kindly toward Ryutas.

"Well, yeah. Ryutas should be useful in various ways for this matter. He seemed dissatisfied with Jaya's current methods too... Don't you think we should help him? Besides, honestly, I'm not needed here, right?"

At Rei's words, Elena thought for a moment and sighed.

Her gaze shifted back to the giant emerging from the room where Vihera was rampaging.

"Alright. Then I'll handle this situation. After all, the leadership of this matter lies with the Neutral Faction... and Rei. As a collaborator, I should follow their lead."

"Sorry about that."

"It's fine. And take Byune with you. Byune's power might not be enough to handle the giant, but it should be more than sufficient against the soldiers."

"Hm?"

"Me too?" Byune tilted her head in confusion. Elena, holding Mirage in its longsword form, nodded without looking at Byune and spoke.

"Yes. Byune, take Yellow with you too."

The guard who had come to call for Rei as reinforcements looked at Yellow... the black dragon cub perched on Byune's head, wondering why they'd bother bringing such a monster.

A black dragon... a dragon's child. He couldn't help but think it might pointlessly stir the soldiers' desires.

It was a natural question, unless one knew about Elena's ability to see Yellow's memories.

And because Rei understood Elena's thoughts, he nodded.

"Got it. Then Yellow, Byune, and I will go reinforce Ryutas. Leave this place to you... though I doubt you need to worry."

"Hehe, but it still makes me happy that you worry about me, Rei."

With a radiant smile like a large, vibrant rose, Elena grabbed her Mirage and ran toward the giant. With Elena's back to him, Rei led Byune toward Ryutas.
