The moment Rei and the others entered the guild, the gazes of the adventurers inside turned toward them, as if repeating the previous day's events.

Admittedly, it was still daytime, so there were considerably fewer people than yesterday.

Even so, it was only natural that beauties like Elena, Marina, and Vihera – women one wouldn't normally get to see – would attract attention. Several people had spotted Rei and his companions yesterday, and those same people were now surprised to see Vihera, who hadn't been there the day before.

Naturally, some of those people shot envious glances at Rei, who was traveling with them, but they didn't act on it.

In just these two days, yesterday and today, word about Rei has spread far and wide. ...Though, of course, the fact that he has a griffin with him makes it obvious who Rei is.

Of course, there were a few who didn't recognize him, but they knew about Elk and wouldn't dare antagonize Rei.

Ignoring the stares from those around them, as usual, Rei and the others head straight for the counter.

Seeing Marina arrive like this so soon after yesterday, the receptionist at the counter immediately understood the purpose of their visit.

"Welcome, Marina-sama. Do you have business with the Worker Representative?"

"Yes. Could you tell me if there's been any progress regarding yesterday's matter?"

"Yes, right away."

The receptionist nodded, and one of the guild staff members inside the counter immediately headed toward the back.

Watching them go, Rei recalled that the person in charge here was named Worker.

Of course, Rei had met Worker before when she came to the Altar of Succession with Elena, and they had introduced themselves.

But she couldn't possibly remember someone she'd met years ago completely, and it was only through this exchange that she finally recalled.

"Rei, you'd be wise not to forget Worker's name. He's bound to become famous someday."

"...Famous?"

Hearing the words come from Marina's mouth, Rei tilted his head slightly.

Seeing Rei like that, Marina smiled.

Elena and Vihera also looked puzzled, unsure what Marina meant by her words.

"Hehe, that's a secret. For now, just remember the worker's name."

"Well, if Marina says so, I guess it's fine."

Rei and the others' conversation was naturally heard by the receptionist standing nearby.

However, she couldn't make out what Marina was saying and merely exchanged glances with another receptionist nearby.

Several minutes passed like this, and eventually the guild employee who had gone into the back earlier returned.

"The worker representative wishes to see you. Please follow me."

"Yes. Then, let's go."

"Elena, I'll wait here. It's likely to be a complicated discussion."

Just as Rei and the others were about to head toward the back of the counter, Ara suddenly addressed Elena.

"Oh? I don't think the workers would mind if Ara came along."

Marina said to Ara, but Ara shook her head slightly.

She judged that the conversation about to take place would likely be significant in many ways, and that it would be better for her not to hear it in her current state.

Realizing it was pointless to pressure Ara, Rei and the others left her behind and headed inside the counter area... Marina, continuing from yesterday, and Rei and Elena, meeting the workers face-to-face for the first time in years.

"You returned from the dungeon remarkably quickly. ...It's been a while since we last saw you, Rei. And you, I believe, is meeting us for the first time?"

The worker stood up to greet Rei and the others as they entered the office.

Though the worker had appeared young when Rei had seen them before—still considerably older than Rei and Elena—their appearance seemed unchanged even after several years.

In their twenties... or at most, early thirties, even being generous.

(Could it be... they have elven blood or something? ...Though their ears look perfectly human, if anything.)

They didn't have the long ears characteristic of elves; they looked like ordinary human ears.

Rei wondered about this, but then reasoned that this was another world... a fantasy world, after all. It wouldn't be strange for certain people to live longer than average.

"Yes, I'm Vihera. If I say I'm the cause of this whole situation... that might make it easier to understand?"

"Oh, you're... I heard you'd been unconscious for quite some time. Are you all right?"

The worker looked puzzled at Vihera's words, but Vihera smiled and spoke.

Though Vihera wore a sheer garment reminiscent of those worn by dancers or courtesans, the Worker showed no particular surprise or lecherous expression upon seeing her—a testament to his competence, no doubt.

"Thanks to Rei and Marina. ...Ah, and Elena too."

Elena, mentioned as an afterthought, flashed a slightly displeased expression, but she didn't say anything more about it.

After everyone settled onto the sofa and exchanged brief introductions over the next few minutes, the Worker finally opened his mouth to get down to business.

"So, His Highness Vihera regaining consciousness means the outcome the Guildmaster mentioned yesterday?"

Referring to Vihera as "Your Highness" meant the worker knew Vihera's true identity.

Then again, given that she hadn't particularly acted to conceal her identity, including during the civil unrest in the Bestir Empire, it wouldn't be difficult for someone reasonably well-informed to figure out who Vihera was.

"Please stop calling me 'Your Highness'. I've already run away, so just call me Vihera."

"...Understood. Then, to clarify. Since Vihera has regained consciousness, the Silver Lion has been defeated and the dungeon's core destroyed. Is that correct?"

The Worker stared intently at Marina as he asked.

He was the Worker dispatched to this land after the dungeon's creation to manage the guild.

Even if it was treated as a branch office rather than a full-fledged guild... no, precisely because of that, he had grown attached to this land.

Marina, who understood the Worker's feelings, nodded simply before speaking.

"Yes. The Silver Lion was defeated, and the dungeon's core was destroyed. ...Rei."

Prompted by Marina, Rei extracts the dungeon's core from the Misty Ring.

It possessed a strange charm, seeming to draw the gaze of all around it simply by being there.

"So this is the dungeon's core... I've never seen one before."

"I suppose so. After all, seeing a dungeon core isn't something you get to do very often. It was a good experience for you."

The worker, told this with a smile, stared intently at the dungeon core in Rei's hand and opened his mouth.

"Um, would it be okay if I touched it a little?"

"Sure, go ahead. The guild's going to hold onto it temporarily anyway, right?"

"That's likely how it will go. Rest assured, we won't confiscate it outright. Ideally, I'd like you to leave it with us today..."

"That's a bit of a problem. I plan to return to Gilm soon. I'm not really keen on coming all the way back here just to get it back."

"Oh, that shouldn't be a problem. The real investigation of the dungeon core won't happen here, but back at Gilm."

Marina replied to Rei's words, but Elena spoke up, looking puzzled.

"If you're going to examine it in Gilm, then isn't there any need to leave the dungeon core here today? I think it would be perfectly fine to do that after you return to Gilm."

Vihera and Rei nodded in agreement with Elena's words, but the worker shook her head at them.

"No, the full investigation will be done at Gilm, but we can get the basics here. That way, even after checking at Gilm, we can return it to Rei, sooner."

"That's true. Besides, since the dungeon is disappearing, the people gathering around here will naturally dwindle. The buildings erected here will likely be demolished soon too. Once that happens, this branch office will gradually scale down its operations."

A meaningful gaze shifted from Marina to the worker.

It was a gaze that suggested something suggestive, but Marina wasn't doing it intentionally; it was a natural reaction.

And the worker seemed to understand what Marina's gaze meant. He let out a small sigh before speaking.

"I think being the guildmaster of Gilm is a bit too much for me, though."

"Huh!?"

The words that came out of the Worker's mouth made Rei, Elena, and Vihera all exclaim simultaneously.

Their gazes all turned to Marina, who was watching them with a radiant smile.

She must have understood why all eyes were on her, yet she kept smiling as she spoke.

"Oh? Is something wrong?"

"What do you mean, 'what's the matter'? What was that about just now? If the Worker becomes Guildmaster, what about Marina?"

Yes, Marina was currently the Guild Master of Gilm.

If a worker were to become the new Guild Master of Gilm in her place, it naturally meant Marina would no longer be Guild Master.

"What do you mean, 'what about her'? She'll resign as guildmaster, won't she?"

"Can you just quit that easily?"

Vihera was puzzled by Marina's matter-of-fact statement.

No, it wasn't just Vihera. Everyone else shared the same doubt.

Needless to say, Gilm was the only frontier territory within the Kingdom of Mireana.

Naturally, its value was immense, which was precisely why Daskar, who governed Gilm, could lead the Neutral Faction.

It was precisely because Daskar led them that the Neutral Faction could maintain its presence despite its small numbers.

The reason Daskar could command such influence was precisely its frontier location.

Numerous rare materials obtainable only in the frontier.

Materials stripped from monster carcasses, and various plants and ores that grow due to its unique ecosystem.

Additionally, the large number of merchants who gather to obtain these materials available only in the frontier.

Alchemists, herbalists, and blacksmiths who seek to acquire these materials as cheaply and in as large quantities as possible.

And above all, adventurers who can earn substantial rewards by gathering these materials.

While various factors play a role, the reason Daskar holds such strong influence as the lord of Gilm is precisely because adventurers exist... that is, because the guild exists.

Skilled adventurers often possess eccentric personalities in many ways.

Marina's immense strength was crucial in keeping such adventurers in line, preventing them from causing major upheavals that could collapse Gilm, even if they did stir up minor disturbances.

Even if Marina announced she was stepping down as Guildmaster, it wasn't something that would be accepted so easily.

"Ah, it's fine. I've already properly discussed it with Daskar. Besides, it's not like I'll be completely cutting ties with the guild, right? Well, I'll hand over the guildmaster position to a worker, but I think I'll stay involved with the guild in some capacity—as an advisor or a consultant or something like that."

"Advisor?"

Marina nodded at Vihera, who repeated the word "advisor" with a somewhat vague expression.

"Yes. After all, I don't think it's right for me to just abandon the Guild of Gilm like this. I want to be able to step in immediately if anything happens."

"...Then why resign as Guildmaster at all?"

"True. But... if I stay Guildmaster, unlike Vihera, I can't be with Rei, right? I was already thinking about stepping down as Guildmaster and returning to being an adventurer. Besides..."

Her words trailed off as Marina's gaze shifted toward Worker.

The worker seemed to understand the meaning behind that look. He exhaled softly before speaking.

"The guild is running perfectly fine right now. But... you're saying we rely too much on the guildmaster, aren't you?"

"Yes. Of course, as a Dark Elf, I live far longer than humans. But I can't remain Guildmaster forever. If something were to happen and I were gone... the guild might not even function properly."

Understanding the meaning behind those words... everyone grasped why Marina was considering stepping down as Guild Master.

Though, wanting more time to be with Rei was also undeniably a genuine part of her reasoning.
