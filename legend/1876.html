When Rei and Elena emerged from the secret passage, what they saw was a rather spacious basement—one so large that it wouldn’t have been surprising if it were about 100 tatami mats in size.

Not only was it vast, but the ceiling was so high—certainly over ten meters—that it was hard to believe they were in a basement.

It was such a vast space that it was simply impossible to believe it was connected to the house where Rei and the others had been just moments ago.

That said, considering the time they’d spent walking through the secret passage, both Rei and Elena had more or less anticipated that the spatial layout would be somewhat off.

In any case, when Rei and Elena emerged into such a vast basement, the scene that unfolded before them could be described as completely unexpected.

The first thing that caught their eyes was something resembling a mass of flesh situated near the center of the roughly 100-tatami-mat space.

No, in this case, it would be more accurate to describe it as a cocoon of flesh rather than a lump of meat.

On the surface of that cocoon of flesh were vessel-like structures in various colors—blue, green, yellow, and red—pulsating rhythmically.

The oval-shaped cocoon of flesh rested atop what could be called a similar flesh-like pedestal on the ground, and a pillar of flesh stretched from the apex of the oval up to the ceiling.

Presumably, anyone who saw it would find it repulsive.

Surrounding that flesh cocoon were three figures.

One was Celestine, the owner of the house where Rei and the others had been until just moments ago, and the leader of an organization connected to the Black Wolf.

Then there was the butler who served Celestine… and the remaining figure was Deotres, who had been feigning service to Gaiska while eagerly biding his time for an opportunity for revenge.

Naturally, Rei didn’t recognize any of the three, but he understood who the person he was looking for was based on the information Elena had given him beforehand.

“You must be Celestine, right?”

“Yes, that is correct. I never imagined that you, known by the epithet ‘Crimson,’ would know my name… It is an honor.”

In response to Rei’s words, Celestane replied without showing any particular sign of unease and bowed gracefully.

Beside Celestane, the butler bowed just as his master had.

“...So that’s your butler, I suppose. And? Who’s the other one?”

“A subordinate of Gaiska. I’ve seen him before.”

It was not Celestane, nor the butler, nor Deotres who answered Rei’s question, but Elena, who stood beside him.

The name Gaisca was not someone Rei could easily forget.

After all, he was essentially the root cause of this whole incident.

“So, Gaiska is here after all. But then, why was the House of Marquis Seisor trying to storm this house? Was it to stop Gaiska?”

“Heh heh, ha… ha ha ha ha ha ha ha… ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha!”

Upon hearing Rei’s words, Deotres suddenly burst into such laughter.

It seemed as though he was saying he hadn’t expected what Rei had said to be this funny.

But, naturally, Rei had absolutely no idea why his words were being laughed at.

“I’d really appreciate it if you could tell me exactly what was so funny about what I just said.”

While getting ready to respond should a battle break out at any moment, Rei spoke to Deotres.

However, Deotres seemed completely unfazed by Rei’s actions and continued to let his laughter ring out.

Watching Deotres like that, Elena frowned slightly.

The Deotres Elena knew was not the sort of person who would laugh so loudly and boisterously.

(Is that man really the man I know? ...And if he’s here, where on earth is Gaesca?)

Yes, it made sense that Deotres was here.

Since it was Gaisca who hired the Black Wolf, it was only natural, in a sense, that his subordinate, Deotres, would be here.

But there was no sign of his master, Gaiska, anywhere.

“What happened to Gaiska? He doesn’t seem to be here.”

Elena asked quietly, addressing Deotres, who was laughing—a laugh that could almost be called maniacal.

Her voice wasn’t particularly loud, but perhaps the mere mention of Gaiska’s name carried enough weight. Deotres stopped laughing and turned his gaze toward Elena.

Deotres’ expression bore a smile so eerie—one that would make any ordinary woman recoil the moment she saw it—but unfortunately for him, Elena was no ordinary woman. She was a woman known as the “Princess General,” who had experienced countless battlefields.

Even when Deotres fixed her gaze on her, Elena did not back down a single step; rather, she returned his gaze with confidence.

Perhaps sensing something in Elena’s gaze, Deotres flashed a delighted smile and turned her eyes toward the flesh cocoon.

“Gaiska… If you’re looking for Lord Gaiska, well, there he is. Right there. Oh well, I suppose it can’t be helped if you can’t hear him. It’s gotten to the point where he can’t even speak anymore.”

Deotres says this, his eyes fixed on the flesh cocoon.

Neither Celestine nor the butler said anything in response to those words.

That meant Deotres’ words were correct.

Rei and Elena looked at the cocoon of flesh once more, observing it for a few seconds before finally realizing something.

In the wrinkled parts of the cocoon... hidden there, as if trying to hide, was a face.

The fact that only the face is visible there implies that the body is inside the cocoon of flesh.

Given the situation, it was impossible to believe that the person inside was unharmed.

In fact, that face was nothing but skin and bones, and to an outside observer, it was impossible to believe that the person was still alive.

(No, the fact that it can survive in such a state probably means that the flesh cocoon is keeping that person alive. And, judging by Deotres’s condition, the one buried in that flesh cocoon is likely Gaiska.)

Based on their previous exchanges, Rei makes this assumption and tries to recall Gaiska’s face.

But perhaps because he had seen her trapped inside that cocoon of flesh, he couldn’t easily recall Gaiska’s face no matter how hard he tried.

“Just to be sure, is it correct to assume that the one buried in that flesh cocoon is Gaiska…?”

Deotres nodded matter-of-factly in response to Rei, who asked on Elena’s behalf.

“That’s right. Did you know? Apparently, the House of Seisor used to take on jobs like sealing away foes they couldn’t defeat on their own, going back in history. That earned them the nation’s recognition and a place among the nobility, and now they hold the title of Marquis. In other words…”

Deotres made a point of drawing it out, but hearing that much, Rei could grasp the general situation.

“So that flesh cocoon is something Gaiska’s ancestors sealed away, too?”

At those words, Deotres nodded… or so it seemed, but then he shook his head dramatically.

“Too bad. It’s true in the sense that it was sealed, but it seems it wasn’t the ancestors of the Seisor Marquis family who sealed it—it was someone else. Still… even if it was someone else, the fact that it was sealed remains the same. So, if we use someone from that bloodline… breaking the seal shouldn’t be too difficult.”

Mind you, since we used a rather forceful method, Gaiska literally felt like he was going to die countless times.

As he said this, Deotres wore a smile that seemed to come from the bottom of his heart.

It was as if he were saying, “There’s nothing in the world that makes me happier than this.”

Seeing Deotres like that, Rei decided he wouldn’t get any useful information out of him, so he turned his gaze to Celestan.

“So? Why on earth did you pull a stunt like this? You’re the one who’s been running the underworld of Anesis, right? What are you trying to accomplish by doing something that destroys Anesis?”

Rei didn’t know exactly what the Flesh Cocoon was.

But the fact that it had been sealed meant, naturally, that it was a dangerous entity, and going out of one’s way to break that seal was, so to speak, like a parasite killing its host.

Of course, even though they’re currently active within Anesis, Rei doesn’t think that the organization itself would cease to exist if Anesis were to disappear…

Even so, wouldn’t the downsides outweigh the benefits for the organization led by Celestine if they went ahead with something like that?

(I don’t know exactly how dangerous this flesh cocoon is, but based on my impression from talking to it, it feels like it would be treated as a nuclear weapon.)

That’s just the impression Rei got, though; the reality might be different.

However, judging by Deotres’s demeanor, he didn’t think he was all that far off.

“If you ask me why… well, I guess I just wanted to try it?”

“...You did it just because you wanted to? I don’t know exactly what that flesh cocoon is, but I can still understand that it’s dangerous. In fact, if you’re not careful, you might even get caught in the crossfire and die along with it.”

“Ah, there’s no need to worry about that. Naturally, I’ve put on shackles to prevent myself from doing anything.”

“Shackles,” he said. That was what Celestan told Rei, but there was no way Rei could believe it.

Based on his past experiences, he could easily imagine that even if she claimed to have restraints on such an opponent, they would be broken in an instant.

“……To be honest, this isn’t exactly why I came to this house in the first place.”

“I see. Then, what is your purpose?”

Celestain asked, seeming somewhat intrigued by Rei’s mutter.

Facing such an opponent, Rei opened his mouth without any hesitation.

“You were acting as an intermediary for the Black Wolf, weren’t you? To obtain documents and such related to that relationship… evidence, so to speak. Though, I don’t need that anymore.”

Rei said this while gazing at the figure believed to be Gaiska, embedded within the flesh cocoon.

Given the matter of that flesh cocoon, there was no doubt that the household of the Duke of Kerebel could not simply leave Celestine and the others alone.

(Honestly, I have absolutely no idea what they were thinking when they pulled a stunt like this. I don’t know… but even so, leaving them be is simply not an option.)

To Rei, the Duke of Kerebel’s family was not just another run-of-the-mill noble house.

Not only is it Elena’s family, but—though it might sound strange to say “unusually”—they are capable individuals, even in Rei’s eyes.

They weren’t exactly unfriendly either. …Though when Rei first met them, they did give off a certain air of intimidation—or perhaps it was just the aura of nobility.

“Well, I wonder what that means. …Though there’s no doubt I harbor ill will toward you. I also want to avenge the Black Wolf.”

For Celestine, the Black Wolf was both a tool of her trade and, without a doubt, an irreplaceable presence.

He should have concealed the news of the Black Wolf’s death as much as possible, and considering that no one witnessed that battle, Celestain shouldn’t have known about it. He shouldn’t have… but since he explicitly mentioned avenging the Black Wolf, it was certain that Celestain knew.

(There was definitely no one at the site of that battle. If that’s the case… is there a traitor within the household of the Duke of Kerebel? Well, even within the household of the Duke of Kerebel, not everyone who works there swears absolute loyalty.)

As far as Rei knew, most of those working for the Duke of Kerebel were content with their positions and had sworn their heartfelt loyalty to the family.

But that was only the number of people Rei knew of.

“The Black Wolf’s killer, huh. …It would be a big help to me if you could tell me how you found out that information.”

“Did you really think I’d just come out and say it myself? If so, you’ve seriously underestimated me.”

“...I knew you’d say that. Well then, let’s change the subject. The traps in this house, and the secret passage you used to get here from that room. You’d know about those, wouldn’t you?”

“Yes, of course. As for the traps, this house itself functions as a kind of magical item. As for the secret passage…”

Celestan stopped speaking there and turned his gaze toward the flesh cocoon.

He didn’t say a word, but it was obvious without even having to think about it what he meant.

“I see. …And so, this is my final question. You prepared that flesh cocoon… and it seems you’re going to break the seal, but what do you plan to do after that?”

In response to Rei’s question, Celestan merely offered a faint smile and said nothing in reply.
