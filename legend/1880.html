“……Huh……”

The moment Rei used Deep Flame on the flesh cocoon, a dazed exclamation escaped from Celestan, who had almost reflexively moved away from it.

But Rei and Elena probably didn’t even hear that sound.

Though Rei and Elena possessed senses sharper than those of ordinary humans, the Flesh Cocoon was now burning with a thunderous roar, and that sound completely drowned out the single word that had escaped Celestine’s lips.

……It was only natural that such a sound would escape Celestine’s lips.

After all, the flesh cocoon he had been desperately trying to revive had just been burned the very moment he thought the seal had finally been broken—after using his long-time subordinate as a disposable pawn to buy time.

Thanks to Rei’s skill, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the flames possessed the very nature Rei had intended: Deep Flames.

Thanks to that Deep Flame, the flesh cocoon was now completely engulfed in flames.

Rei watched the flames with satisfaction.

From Rei’s perspective, since the seal was about to be broken, he had no intention of waiting around for it.

His actions stemmed from the idea that he might as well defeat the enemy in one fell swoop before the seal was fully broken.

Or, if he had been in a state where he could freely use the Misty Ring, then perhaps… just perhaps, he might have waited for the seal to be lifted.

In any case, that was merely a hypothetical scenario, and now that it had been burned to ashes, there was nothing to be done.

Celestan looked over there, hoping for some kind of salvation—hoping, in fact, that even if Deep Flame was used, the flesh cocoon might withstand the flames—but what met his eyes was the sight of the flesh cocoon crumbling into charcoal and falling to the ground, starting from the edges.

Clearly, that flesh cocoon had been burned by the Deep Flame and had sustained significant damage.

“...Oh, I never thought it would go this smoothly.”

Watching the flesh cocoon being consumed by the Deep Flame unleashed using most of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, Rei murmurs softly.

As he himself had said, even though the seal was on the verge of breaking—no, even though it was already half-broken—he probably hadn’t expected to be able to burn it this effectively when he unleashed the Deep Flame attack upon it.

The one who felt this most keenly was likely Rei, who had actually done it.

And that single word from Rei’s mouth brought Celestan’s consciousness back.

“Ah… ah… aaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!”

What came out of Celestan’s mouth was a sound that could only be called a scream.

Letting out that anguished cry, Celestan takes one step, then two, toward the cocoon of flesh that continues to burn under the effects of the Deep Flame.

But she cannot get any closer than a certain point.

This was because the Deep Flame had trapped intense heat within a certain radius of the flesh cocoon.

It was heat so intense that if Celestan were to approach carelessly, her body might well catch fire.

If Celestan were to charge into that heat, I would have to stop him.

With that in mind, Rei had been preparing to intervene, but fortunately, Celestan didn’t seem to have any suicidal tendencies. He didn’t venture any closer than that certain distance; he just stood there.

“Well, with that settled, Celestain’s plan—to revive whatever was sealed away—has failed. So, what are you going to do now? If you’d just surrender quietly, we could offer you some favorable terms.”

“……”

Rei spoke to him, but Celestane showed no sign of opening his mouth.

No, if anything, he seemed dazed, almost as if his mind had wandered off.

(What’s going on? At the very least, judging by Celestane’s demeanor just a moment ago, he didn’t seem like he was… well, like he was willing to risk his life to break the seal. It seemed more like he was just interested, and figured, “Well, since the seal looks like it’s about to break anyway, why not?”)

From what Rei could see, that’s how it should have been, but for some reason… yes, for some reason, Celestan now wore an expression as if he had despaired of everything in this world after failing to break the seal of the flesh cocoon.

“What do you think?” Rei asked, turning his gaze toward Elena.

But even with Rei looking at her like that, Elena had no way of knowing why Celestan had suddenly become like this.

Watching Elena silently shake her head, Rei removed the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

This was because the flesh cocoon had already turned into a lump of charcoal, unrecognizable from its original form, and it was clear it would crumble completely at any moment.

As Rei released the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the Deep Flame was released at the same time.

This was likely because the Deep Flame’s effect had abruptly ceased. The flesh cocoon, struck by the impact, fell to the ground as a lump of charcoal and was destroyed; the portion connected to the ceiling also turned to charcoal partway down and fell to the ground, and the flesh cocoon itself fell to the ground and shattered.

The sight of the lump of charcoal shattering gave Rei the fleeting illusion that it was like glass falling to the ground and shattering...

“Ah…”

A small word escaped Rei’s lips without her realizing it.

But in the end, she did nothing more than silently gaze at the being that had turned into a lump of charcoal.

“Come to think of it, if this was a monster, maybe it had a magic stone or something? If so, I’d like to get my hands on it.”

The next moment, those were the words that came out of Rei’s mouth.

Elena, who had heard this, looked momentarily taken aback, but the next instant, she couldn’t help but burst out laughing.

“Haha… Seriously, you were looking so serious for once, I was wondering what you were thinking…”

“No, but you know that too, right, Elena? That I have a hobby of collecting magic stones…”

Rei had started to say “need,” but even though he was in a daze, he decided it would be better not to say such a thing in front of Celestan and hurriedly changed his wording.

Seeing Rei like that, Elena smiled again.

“That’s true. I don’t know if that sealed entity is really a monster, but there’s still a high chance it is. If so, there’s a possibility it has magic stones. Why don’t you check it out? I’ll keep an eye on Celestan.”

“You’re right. In that case, I’ll take a look.”

As he replied to Elena, Rei approached the charred, flesh-like cocoon—or rather, what used to be a flesh-like cocoon.

However, given the sheer size of the completely charred mass of flesh, Rei hesitated to touch it directly with his own hands.

No, even if it hadn’t turned to charcoal and remained a cocoon of flesh, Rei would still have avoided touching it directly with his own hands.

Given that he was witnessing the sight of blood vessels pulsing in various colors, Rei’s decision was only natural.

(But I can’t exactly use the Twilight Spear on something like this… I’m glad I bought a disposable spear for times like this.)

The Twilight Spear, one of Rei’s main weapons alongside the Death Scythe.

He certainly couldn’t use the Twilight Spear just to deal with a lump of charcoal, so, figuring he might as well, Rei reached into his Misty Ring to pull out a throwing spear…

“Huh?”

But what came out of Rei’s mouth was a dumbfounded exclamation.

That’s because, no matter how hard she tried, she couldn’t pull the disposable spear out of the Misty Ring.

The flesh cocoon that had sealed off the use of the Misty Ring had now turned into a lump of charcoal right before Rei’s eyes.

Given that, it wouldn’t have been strange if she could already use the Misty Ring.

And yet, even now, when he tried to use the Misty Ring, he couldn’t.

It took less than a second for him to realize he couldn’t use the Misty Ring when he actually tried.

Realizing what this meant, Rei instinctively leaped backward from where he stood to put distance between himself and the lump of charcoal.

“Watch out, Elena! This guy’s still alive!”

The moment Rei shouted, Elena’s face betrayed her surprise.

It was only natural. Enveloped in deep flames, with his entire body now reduced to charcoal, it was impossible to believe that the flesh cocoon was still alive.

Still, it was impressive that, a split second later, Rei took a stance with Mirage in hand, ready to face the charred mass he had distanced himself from.

……One reason they were able to react so instantly was likely that both Rei and Elena had just witnessed the extraordinary regenerative abilities of Deotres and Mirales, whom they had been fighting moments ago.

Then, as if reacting to Rei and Elena’s vigilance, the charred mass stirred… and in the next instant, a single tentacle burst forth, piercing through the charred mass.

Naturally, the tentacle wasn’t unscathed either… no, in fact, several sections of it had partially turned to charcoal, and the fact that it could still function as a tentacle was a sight that astonished even Rei and Elena, who were watching.

Yet, even in that state, the tentacle shot swiftly through the air… and in the next instant, it pierced the chest of Celestan, who was sitting on the ground in a daze.

“...Huh?”

Once again, it was Rei who voiced his confusion.

It was only natural. Rei, who was the primary reason Celestine had been instantly turned to charcoal the moment the seal was broken, and Elena, who had been fighting Mirales—Rei’s ally who had been manipulated by the flesh cocoon.

If the attack had been directed at those two, it would have made sense.

But… the one the tentacle had just pierced wasn’t Rei or Elena—it was Celestine.

“Why?” It wasn’t strange that the two of them were wondering that.

Especially since, according to Celestine, that sealed entity had been programmed by the shackles to obey her commands. She had boasted about it, hadn’t she?

“Guh… gah…”

A sound that wasn’t quite a voice escaped from Celestine’s mouth.

And then, a look of utter astonishment—as if asking “Why?”—was directed at the lump of charcoal… From within that lump of charcoal, a single ray of light was visible… Despite the fact that most of its body had been turned to charcoal, they noticed a presence glaring at them with hatred.

It was the head of Gaiska, whose body had been encased in a cocoon of flesh.

Though there was no trace of his former self left, the hatred in those eyes was unmistakably Gaiska’s.

Why Gaiska’s consciousness—which, until just moments ago, could have been said to have lost all sense of self—had returned in this way was unclear.

Perhaps it was the effect of the Deep Flame used by Rei that brought his consciousness back, but that remains a mystery, and I cannot say for certain.

Above all, Celestan no longer had the luxury of time to ponder such things; his torso had been pierced, and the blood, flesh, bones, and internal organs within his body were being sucked out by the tentacles that had penetrated him, as if drinking juice through a straw.

In the blink of an eye, he was reduced to nothing but skin… and by that point, Celestan’s consciousness had completely vanished.

「Elena！」

“I know.”

Naturally, Rei and Elena weren’t just standing there staring at the sight of the flesh cocoon… no, the lump of charcoal… no, Gaiska killing Celestan.

But with Gaiska still alive and the Misty Ring unavailable, Rei didn’t have many options left.

Moreover, he had already used his ultimate move, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, and expended much of his magical power to incinerate the flesh cocoon with Deep Flames.

Of course, his depleted magical power would recover to some extent over time, but there was no way he could possibly use the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour again right now.

In that case, the most effective option here is Elena… and precisely because Elena knows this, she immediately began chanting her own trump card: Dragon Language Magic.

And the moment the tentacles launched by Gaiska absorbed every last trace of Celestan—even the single remaining piece of skin—Elena’s Dragon Language Magic activated.

From behind Elena, the head of a gigantic dragon appeared.

It wasn’t the dragon itself that appeared, but only its head.

This was a perfect demonstration of the true nature of Dragon Language Magic.

The dragon’s head that appeared in this manner acted as if it had a body, arching its upper body as though it were actually there… and then, in the very next instant, thrust its head forward in one swift motion.

The speed was such that even Gaiska, who had absorbed Celestane himself, could not possibly react in time.

Before he knew it, Gaiska’s body—which had been slowly beginning to regenerate from a pile of charcoal—was seized by the dragon’s jaws. That single fang, taller than Rei himself, crushed Gaiska in the next instant and swallowed him whole.

And just like that, the dragon’s head—which had bitten Gaiska to death… no, devoured him—vanished the very next moment, as if it had never been there at all.

...Naturally, after the dragon’s head vanished, there was no trace of Gaiska’s body left behind.
