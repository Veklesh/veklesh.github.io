About ten days had passed since his date with Kenny, and Rei arrived at the guild as usual.

During those ten days, Rei had gone once with Elena and the others to deliver supplies to Sura.

This was likely because they were all traveling by carriage. They were already quite close to Gilm, and at this rate, there was no doubt they would reach Gilm well before winter—indeed, likely by early autumn.

That said, in the ten days or so since Rei had returned from the sea to Gilm, the season had been rapidly shifting from summer to fall.

If that’s the case, Sura and the others might arrive in Gilm a little later than Rei had anticipated.

In any case, whenever Rei enters the guild, he can’t help but overhear the adventurers talking about Gamelion.

With autumn approaching, it was only natural for such topics to come up... but the problem was that even the adventurers who had come to Gilm seeking work for the current expansion project were talking about Gamelion.

(Things are turning out just as Kenny predicted.)

Many of the adventurers who are active in Gilm on a regular basis understand just how strong Gamelion is.

However, among the adventurers who came to Gilm specifically for work on the expansion project, while not all of them are like that, there are many who can by no means be called skilled.

If such people tried to defeat Gamelion, there’s no doubt that casualties would mount.

In some cases, it could even cause delays in the expansion project itself.

Given the current situation, where we want to make as much progress on the construction as possible before winter, any delay in the work would be completely counterproductive. That said... even with that risk in mind, the benefits of defeating the monster Gamelion are significant.

Naturally. While there is no shortage of work on the expansion project in Gilm, the pay itself isn’t particularly high.

Of course, the pay isn’t so low that one can’t make a living, but compared to the reward for defeating Gamelion, it’s overwhelmingly low.

This is only natural given that this is a quest involving the risk of death, but some adventurers reasoned that since they had managed to make it all the way to Gilm, they could surely handle any monsters in the area.

In reality, there is a significant difference in difficulty between coming to Gilm before the expansion work began and coming now.

However, given the fact that they’ve actually made it to Gilm, there are quite a few adventurers who convince themselves that their skills will hold up here as well.

...In that sense, ordinary people who have come looking for work—rather than adventurers—are probably better suited for the expansion project.

Of course, even among those ordinary people, there are certainly some who overestimate their abilities and go so far as to register as adventurers...

That said, naturally, those who have just become adventurers cannot accept the kinds of requests typically handled outside the city… but for better or worse, Gilm is currently in a very chaotic state, so it’s not as if there are no ways to pull the wool over people’s eyes in that regard.

Since they’ve come to Gilm to make money, it’s only natural that their attention would turn to jobs that offer higher rewards.

And so, they end up in a situation they can’t handle, only to regret it when they’re on the brink of death… but by then, it’s already too late.

(I wish there were some way to handle that sort of thing.)

Even if someone unrelated to him dies, Rei feels a little sadness, but he doesn’t dwell on it.

After all, being an adventurer is, above all, a matter of personal responsibility.

If you die because you misjudged your own abilities, it’s nothing but your own fault.

Still, I do feel that it’s better not to die.

Part of that is because Kenny was worried about him.

But there’s no doubt that, in reality, there’s nothing he can do about it.

After all, if we were to go so far as to forcibly ban Gamelion hunting, that would only create problems.

(I guess what it comes down to is that I need to assess my own abilities objectively...)

With that in mind, Rei approached the counter where Lenora was sitting to ask about today’s schedule, but…

“Rei-san!”

For some reason, the moment Lenora saw Rei, she called out happily.

It was usual for Kenny to cheer when he saw Rei, but it was rare for Lenora to act like this.

In fact, a male adventurer who was in the process of accepting a request from another receptionist at a nearby counter was staring at them in surprise.

Lenora must have noticed the stares from those around her. Her cheeks flushed slightly, but she still beckoned to Rei.

Next to Lenora, Kenny was handling the paperwork for another adventurer’s request, but he glanced at her for just a moment with a look of envy.

“So, if she called out to you like that, does that mean there’s a problem or something?”

“No, well… I mean, it is a problem, I suppose… The Guild Master is asking for you, so could you please come to his office?”

“...Walker’s?”

It was only natural that Rei got a bad feeling the moment he heard those words.

Being summoned to the office specifically in this situation meant something troublesome had undoubtedly happened.

(I wish I’d brought Marina with me.)

Since they worked in different areas, Marina, Vihera, and Byune each headed straight for their respective workplaces.

The reason Rei comes to the guild almost every morning is that, given his involvement with the Misty Ring and Set, there are simply many tasks he can handle.

In this case, you could say that worked against him.

That said, Rei doesn’t really have any particular feelings toward Walker.

So, being summoned to the office isn’t a problem in itself, but if he’s going to be asked to take on a request, he’d honestly have preferred Marina to handle the negotiations… that’s the truth of the matter.

Lately, Rei had been leaving that sort of negotiation entirely up to Marina. It seems the consequences of that have come back to haunt him.

(Well, if something really bad had happened, they wouldn’t have waited for me to show up—they would have come to the inn to get me. …If that’s the case, it’s probably not such an urgent problem… or so I’d like to think.)

Considering everything that’s happened so far, it’s probably… likely… surely not that big of a problem. …I hope not, anyway. Even as he thought that, Rei stepped behind the counter and, together with Lenora, headed up the stairs at the back to Walker’s office.

Normally, this time of day is busy because adventurers often come in to accept quests, but the fact that Lenora called Rei to the Guild Master’s office suggests the issue at hand is fairly urgent.

“Excuse me, Guild Master. I’ve brought Rei with me.”

“Ah, please come in.”

A voice came from behind the door, and Lenora opened it.

“Whoa…”

Rei stepped into the office and let out that exclamation upon seeing the scene inside.

But it was only natural for her to react that way.

There were several piles of papers on the desk, completely obscuring the face of Walker, who was supposed to be working.

And seeing that there were piles of papers not just on the desk but even on the floor, it was no wonder a sound that could be interpreted as either surprise or pity escaped Rei’s lips.

“...Prepare some tea.”

She stood up from her chair, and Walker’s face finally came into view.

At Walker’s words, Lenora wore a worried expression, bowed, and left the room.

“For now… this spot is fine, so please have a seat.”

Walker pointed to the sofa in the office and said this, then stretched slightly as he walked over to Rei’s side.

Even with this much work on his plate, as far as Rei could tell, there was no sign of fatigue on Walker’s face.

(In a way, maybe work is his hobby.)

With that thought in mind, Rei sat down on the sofa, and just then, Lenora arrived carrying a plate with tea and some small sandwiches.

“Oh, is that okay?”

“Yes, the Guild Master seems to have a lot on his plate. He needs to eat something tasty to keep him going. …Rei, please help yourself if you’d like. It’s homemade, so it’s nothing to brag about, but…”

Saying that, Lenora left the office.

Lenora’s homemade sandwiches, which have quite a following.

If any of her fan-adventurers heard about this, they would undoubtedly envy Rei and Walker.

Depending on the situation, there might even be someone who would challenge Rei to a duel over it.

Rei brings the sandwich to his mouth.

The crunch of the vegetables and the tender texture of the stewed meat, combined with a slightly spicy yet refreshingly tangy sauce, spread through his mouth.

Compared to sandwiches sold by professional chefs or bakers, the taste is naturally several notches below.

But even so, no one could argue that the sandwich isn’t delicious.

It’s not just Rei eating the sandwich; Walker is too.

After finishing the simple sandwich of cheese, ham, and vegetables with obvious relish and taking a sip of tea, he lets out a small sigh, as if he’s finally come to his senses.

After taking a sip of tea himself, Rei asks Walker.

“So? You went out of your way to summon me to your office, so there must be a reason, right?”

Rei certainly didn’t think he’d been summoned all the way to the Guild Master’s office just because Walker wanted someone to talk to… or for a reason like that.

If she just wanted someone to talk to, there were plenty of other people besides Rei who would be suitable.

At Rei’s words, Walker nodded and spoke.

“I’ll get straight to the point. Rei, how about we try clearing a dungeon?”

“……Huh?”

What on earth is he talking about?

That was the first thought that crossed his mind when he heard Walker’s words.

To begin with, Rei had already cleared both dungeons in the vicinity of Gilm.

Given that, the fact that he’s bringing up clearing a dungeon now means… Thinking that, I recalled a conversation I’d had with Kenny a little while ago.

“Could it be that a new dungeon has been found near Gilm?”

If that were the case, it would be a mixed blessing for Gilm.

Dungeons undoubtedly bring great wealth, but they also bring extremely high danger.

It’s truly a case of high risk, high reward, and especially given the current situation—where many adventurers who wouldn’t normally come to Gilm are staying here—the risks outweigh the rewards.

Kenny had been worried about the Gamelion situation as well, but in Gamelion’s case, if you’re lucky—or unlucky—you might not even encounter one.

But when it came to dungeons, it was a different story.

Dungeons vary so widely depending on the location and floor that they truly deserve to be called “as diverse as can be,” but even so, the likelihood of encountering monsters is high.

Moreover, since adventurers from Gilm have fought Gamelions countless times before, their attack patterns are well known.

Of course, individual monsters can vary greatly, but there’s not much you can do about that.

In contrast, dungeons aren’t places where only one type of monster appears, and you never know what kind of monsters might show up.

It was by no means a place where you could send an adventurer whose abilities were in question.

However, Walker shook his head at Rei’s words.

“No, there’s no doubt a dungeon has been found, but it’s not near Gilm.”

“...Then, Abuero or Sabrusta?”

Both are towns located near Gilm.

If a dungeon were found in a place like that, it would undoubtedly cause a huge commotion.

That said, Rei hadn’t heard anything about it either.

Well, Rei doesn’t have many ways to obtain information, but Elena, the symbol of the Noble Faction, and Marina, the former guild master, have highly reliable intelligence networks.

Vihera doesn’t have quite as extensive a network as those two, but she has her own sources.

Since Rei dines with those three every night, if such information existed, he would undoubtedly have heard about it.

But Rei hadn’t heard anything of the sort.

That means either such information doesn’t exist… or, if it does, it hasn’t spread that far.

However, Walker shook his head at that as well.

“No, that’s not it. Do you know the town of Goltz?”

Rei thought for a moment at Walker’s words, but eventually shook his head, indicating he didn’t know it.

“Is it a famous town?”

“Not really. It’s just an ordinary town—nothing particularly notable, though it’s the most prosperous one in the area… But apparently, on the face of a mountain cliff a short distance from Goltz, something that looks like a dungeon has suddenly appeared.”

"Something that looks like it?" Rei turned his gaze toward Walker, as if urging him to continue.
