The person who suddenly spoke to her was exactly as Marina had described—the very embodiment of the word "ordinary."

Taller than Rei, but not strikingly so.

His weight wasn't skinny, but he wasn't fat either.

Even with that average build, he didn't have visibly bulging muscles, yet he didn't look unreliable either.

His clothes had a springtime feel, but they were the kind you'd see on anyone walking down the street.

His face wasn't so ugly it was unbearable to look at, but it wasn't strikingly handsome either.

His short, neatly trimmed brown hair was the kind you'd see on countless others just by glancing down the main street.

He was as ordinary as could be, and it wouldn't be wrong to say that very ordinariness was his defining characteristic.

"Huh..."

The one who uttered that impressed-sounding remark upon seeing the person who had spoken to her was Vihera.

Given the plot to pit Rei against Azott Trading Company, she naturally remained vigilant of her surroundings.

Amidst this, she hadn't sensed the man's presence until he was quite close.

Of course, she could sense him once he entered her personal space, but conversely, that meant she hadn't been able to detect him until then.

"Well, you came out rather quickly. I thought you'd be a bit later."

Marina addressed the man who spoke to her, her words accompanied by a smile.

Based on their initial encounter, she had anticipated he would appear once they made their move, but she hadn't expected him to show himself this quickly.

"I heard you were looking for me?"

"Heard, huh. I'm a bit curious who told you... but even if I asked, you wouldn't tell me, would you?"

"Yes, unfortunately."

Marina wasn't particularly disappointed by that answer, as she'd expected it.

"Right. So, could you tell me more about this matter? Of course, only what you can share."

"Well... I'd like to discuss that, but my master wishes to meet with you all. If you don't mind, would you accompany us? Of course, my master knows more about this matter than I do."

"...Master?"

Marina asked back, clearly surprised that the name came up so easily here.

Rei and the others were equally surprised, staring intently at the nondescript figure before them.

Perhaps not expecting such scrutiny, the man before them nodded slightly, looking a bit flustered.

"Yes. My master wishes to meet you all."

"...What should we do?"

Marina asked, her gaze fixed on him, but Rei couldn't help but nod at the man's words.

With no real leads to go on at this point, they couldn't just refuse the other side's proposal here.

"We have no choice but to go. We need to give proper thanks to whoever orchestrated this ridiculous affair."

"I agree with Rei. This whole thing is just making fools of us."

Vihera nodded in agreement with Rei's words.

Byune, who was riding on Set's back, showed no particular inclination to speak, seemingly indifferent either way.

"So then, shall we be shown the way?"

"Yes, the carriage is ready. This way."

The man led Rei and the others out of the alleyway.

Several pairs of eyes from within the alleyway followed Rei's departure.

Rei noticed the stares but didn't seem particularly bothered. He called out to the man leading the way ahead.

"So, what's your name? You seem to know ours, so I'd like to hear yours too, if possible?"

"I doubt I'll have many opportunities to meet people like Rei... My name is Terry."

"...Terry, huh. Yeah, got it. Well then, thanks for the tour, Terry."

"Yes."

Learning that not only his appearance but also his name was commonplace... not particularly unusual, Rei thought he was quite thorough. However, Terry himself seemed completely oblivious to Rei's thoughts as he continued walking down the path.

Walking behind Rei, Marina once again turned her gaze to the back of the person who had introduced himself as Terry.

(Did he use this incident to connect with Rei...? If so, that's fine too.)

As they left the Noble District, several people approached them, trying to establish a connection with Rei... no, with the Crimson Wings.

Rei and his group had refused them all, but if providing information in this case meant establishing a connection... that was a different matter.

If that was indeed the goal behind this incident, then Terry's master must be quite well-informed.

In fact, if the flow of information after this incident occurred was exceptionally swift, it signifies possessing considerable intelligence-gathering capabilities.

(But still, it's only natural to assume this incident is somehow connected to whoever's been messing with Rei.)

While there were various thoughts swirling around, considering the current situation, it was certain Terry's master was involved in this incident.

Perhaps, on top of that, they also want to form a connection with Rei...

Anyway, upon exiting the alley, a carriage was already waiting there.

Even to an outsider, it looked expensive and impressive.

Moreover, the horses pulling it showed no sign of being startled by Set.

It was clear these were exceptionally well-trained horses.

Naturally, a horse subjected to such rigorous training was an extremely expensive commodity.

Rei had ridden in carriages belonging to several nobles before, including Elena, and the horses pulling those carriages were just like the rigorously trained horse now standing before him.

"Huh."

Vihera murmured in understanding upon seeing the horse.

He probably thought that Terry's master must be quite a person to be able to provide such a horse.

"Please get in. I'd have preferred to keep it a secret that Rei was riding in this carriage, but... that seems impossible now."

Terry smiled slightly at Set.

Most residents of Gilm knew that if Set was present, it essentially meant Rei was there too.

Though they did occasionally split up, so it wasn't an absolute rule.

"If you absolutely must go separately, I could leave Set with someone else?"

"No, I don't think worrying about that is pointless anymore, and my master said you don't need to be concerned about it either."

"Gururu?"

Set tilted his head at Rei and Terry's conversation.

...For some reason, Byune, who was riding on Set's back, tilted his head too.

Watching this, Vihera silently stroked Byune's head.

"You seem to get along well."

"Yeah, I won't deny that. We've got our own issues too."

After exchanging those few words, Rei and the others boarded the carriage.

Byune reluctantly climbed down from Set's back and entered the carriage as well.

Terry then sat on the driver's seat and set the carriage in motion.

Set followed behind the carriage, letting out a short snort, but naturally, such behavior made him stand out considerably.

The carriage itself was quite high-quality, so standing out was only natural.

"Hey, that carriage... If Set's there, does that mean Rei's riding in it?"

"Wow... I'd love to ride in a carriage like that, even just once."

"You idiot. If commoners like us tried riding in a carriage like that, we'd be too worried about scratching the stuff inside to relax. That kind of thing is for people who know how to ride properly."

The man found himself agreeing with his friend's words.

Truth be told, he had said he'd love to ride in a carriage like that just once, but if he ever actually found himself in one, he'd undoubtedly be too nervous to enjoy it.

Besides those two, many others were also watching the carriage.

The carriage itself was impressive, but more than that, the sight of Set moving alongside it was undoubtedly the biggest reason it drew attention.

"...I feel like I'm getting a lot of attention."

"Like you didn't notice before?"

When Rei muttered this inside the carriage, Vihera replied with a hint of exasperation.

Rei considered retorting, but since the remark was undeniably true, there was really no point in arguing.

"Still, I'm starving. ...Want something to eat?"

Trying to change the subject, Rei pulled out a few sandwiches from his Misty Ring and asked the other three.

The first to receive a sandwich from Rei was, naturally, Byune.

She happily bit into the sandwich filled with stewed oak meat and plenty of vegetables.

Vegetables had been expensive during the winter, but now it was spring.

Prices hadn't completely stabilized yet, but they were still much more reasonable compared to winter.

And best of all, spring meant an abundance of wild mountain vegetables, allowing them to enjoy sandwiches made with seasonal ingredients.

"This slight bitterness is just right. It holds its own against the rich, robust flavor of the oak meat."

Vihera murmured her thoughts as she ate the sandwich Rei had given her.

It tasted similar to butterbur shoots, or 'bakke' as they were called where Rei lived.

But naturally, this butterbur was different from the one Rei knew; it was red in color.

It was chopped, so it was impossible to tell the exact shape of the wild vegetable, but at the very least, the color was different from what Rei knew.

Despite being inside a luxurious carriage, Rei and the others didn't seem to mind at all.

Normally, one might worry about eating such a sandwich and dirtying the carriage... but Vihera, being a former princess, and Marina, a well-known guildmaster, were both accustomed to riding in carriages of this sort.

Byune was focused on savoring the delicious sandwich given to Rei and showed no concern for the carriage.

And Rei, having seen Elena's carriage, showed no surprise at this level of luxury either.

Thus, a brief period of satisfying hunger continued.

The sandwiches Rei had brought out easily served ten people... yet even that wasn't enough to completely fill the stomachs of everyone present.

To begin with, Rei and Byune ate more than usual, and Marina and Vihera, being adventurers, also consumed larger portions than average.

It was only because Rei and Byune ate so extraordinarily much that the other two weren't perceived as eating that much.

After finishing the sandwiches, Rei pulled out fruit water next.

He would have preferred to drink water from the running blade dagger, but he likely decided it was better not to do such a thing inside the carriage.

Even so, the fruit water refreshed his mouth... and with his hunger somewhat sated, he moved on to the matter at hand.

"What do you think?"

"Well... Logically, he should be on our side, but... I can't fully trust him."

"I figured."

"Mm."

Vihera, Marina, Rei, and Byune each murmured.

An opponent this well-connected. It's certain they're involved in this matter somehow, and there's no way you could fully trust someone like that.

That was a shared sentiment among Rei and the others.

Under normal circumstances, they wouldn't follow someone this obviously suspicious.

But... right now, they simply had no information.

That's precisely why, even if they found him a little... no, quite suspicious, they needed to make contact with Terry's boss.

"Whoever this guy is, we'll get some information out of him, more or less. If he decides to team up with us after that, fine. But... I wouldn't like it if we ended up being used without a word."

"I don't think there are many reckless people who'd try to use Rei for their own ends."

"I agree with Vihera. For better or worse, Rei is famous in Gilm. Anyone who does a little digging can find out what kind of person she is. Do you really think someone like that would try to use Rei?"

As Marina finished speaking, the carriage came to a stop.

A moment later, Terry lifted the carriage door, and Rei and the others stepped out... to see a sign hanging on the building before them reading "Spier Trading Company."
