Storing the Cyclops' corpse takes mere minutes.

After all, it only required touching the corpse, so there was no reason for it to take long.

Having finished storing the corpse, Rei addressed Mireine, who could be considered the person in charge here.

"Mireine, what do we do now? Should we just head back to Gilm, or wait for the others to return? If there are no corpses besides the Cyclops, that means everyone else is alive, right? But it seems like we didn't run into anyone before Dietz and the others got here."

Hearing Rei's words, Mireine stopped admiring Set and nodded in response.

"Right. I asked everyone to return to Gilm and report this incident... but I suppose we should go back to Gilm first and tell them we've dealt with the Cyclops. Though, considering the time, they probably haven't arrived at Gilm yet."

"What should we do? I could head back to Gilm ahead of you."

"...That's an option, but a group of Cyclopses has started appearing, you know? If anything happens, I'd be glad to have Rei here as our trump card."

Speaking of trump cards, there's also Vihera, who took on five Cyclopses.

But for Mireine, it's probably more reassuring to have Rei, someone she knows well, than Vihera, whom she'd only just met.

...Though it certainly can't be said that the reason for Set being there is entirely absent.

Whether he knew it or not, upon hearing Mireine's words, Rei turned his gaze toward Vihera and spoke.

"Having Vihera here means we shouldn't have any problems with combat strength. Vihera should be able to hold her own against that red Cyclops."

"The red cyclops?"

Perhaps concerned by Rei's words, Vihera shot him a questioning look.

"Yeah. The one believed to have been controlling these Cyclops. Not sure if it's a rare species or a higher-tier one... but it was a troublesome opponent either way."

"How interesting."

"...I knew you'd say that. Well, we've already defeated it anyway, so it's better to save the details for when we get back to Gilm. So, what do you say?"

The last part of his sentence was directed at Mireine.

Whether heading to Gilm or searching the forest for the scattered former members of the guerrilla squad, having Set with his ability to fly made both options viable.

Having come this far, Rei felt either option was acceptable and directed a look toward Mireine, signaling he'd leave the decision to her.

Entrusted with the decision, Mireine hesitated slightly... Her gaze swept over the others in turn—Dietz, Byune, Vihera—before she finally spoke.

"Right. I'm sorry, but could Rei head towards Gilm first? Since Set can fly, she'll definitely arrive before the others who are moving on foot. We need to properly report that this battle is over, or else they might form an expeditionary force."

Rei immediately nodded, seemingly having no objection to Mireine's suggestion.

Indeed, with seven Cyclopes present, and the possibility of rare or higher-tier species near Gilm, the likelihood of an extermination squad being formed was high.

And forming an extermination squad requires gathering adventurers, collecting supplies, preparing wagons—it costs both money and time.

If, after going through all that trouble, it turned out they'd already been defeated, it would literally be a waste of effort and a complete loss.

If any Cyclops remained, that would be one thing, but to have already wiped them all out... Just as he thought that far, Rei suddenly recalled the red Cyclops he had defeated, then remembered the seven Cyclops corpses here, and spoke up.

"Are we really okay reporting that we've defeated all the Cyclopes? The info I first got from Dietz said there were five Cyclopes. But in reality, there was a red Cyclops, plus seven regular ones. Could there be more out there?"

"Well..."

Mireine couldn't immediately refute Rei's words.

The others felt the same way, and the sounds of birds and beasts enjoying the spring forest echoed around them.

The silence was first broken by Surunin, who had been observing Ekril.

"We should probably explain that to the guild too. Probably as if we wiped them all out. And... that hammer the red cyclops had is concerning."

At those words, the image of a massive hammer flashed through the minds of those who had actually seen the red cyclops.

A hammer that didn't look small even in the hands of a five-meter-tall red cyclops, as if it had been crafted specifically for it.

"Ah, I recovered that hammer. ...It's a pretty troublesome piece of equipment."

"Troublesome, you say? You mean troublesome in the sense that it's too heavy to lift or something?"

Though he probably didn't think so himself, Rei shook his head at Surnin's question, asked for clarification.

"That hammer itself felt like it possessed some kind of consciousness. When the red cyclops I was fighting started getting pushed back and realized it couldn't turn the tide, it extended root-like tendrils from the hand gripping the hammer into its body and took over the red cyclops' consciousness... No, since it never clearly displayed consciousness outwardly, erasing its consciousness might be more accurate. Plus, it has regenerative abilities, so it's troublesome even in a normal fight."

Vihera, listening to Rei's explanation, frowned slightly.

"If the Cyclops used a magic item, that hammer must have been huge, right?"

"Yeah. It was considerably larger than the clubs ordinary Cyclopes wield."

"...I wonder who prepared that hammer and how. Honestly, I can't think of a way. Well, if it's just about preparing it, you could have an alchemist make it, but is that really necessary? They're already Cyclopes, with low intelligence. If you approached them saying, 'Here, take this magic item,' wouldn't they just think bait was coming?"

"I've been wondering about that too. It's unlikely that a cyclops that size just happened to find a magic item it could use somewhere. ...By the way, it's about this big."

Deciding it would be wise to show it to Vihera, Byune, and Dietz as well, Rei pulled out the hammer.

The sudden appearance of the enormous hammer before them made everyone present—even those who'd seen it before like Mireine, Surnin, and Johanna—widen their eyes in shock.

Among them, Surnin was the first to regain his composure and cry out.

"That hammer has the ability to take over someone's consciousness, doesn't it!? Isn't it dangerous to just casually bring it out like that?!"

At Surnin's cry, everyone took a few steps back.

But Rei, showing no particular sign of tension, touched the hammer's handle and spoke.

"Relax. There's no consciousness left in this hammer anymore. When I tried to store it in my item box, it tried to take over my consciousness. Probably because it's a magic item, it tried to use magic to do something. Instead, it got swallowed up by my magic and its consciousness completely vanished."

"...That's possible, I suppose."

Though Mireine and Surunin didn't know exactly how much magic power Rei possessed, having witnessed his battles countless times, they understood it was far beyond ordinary levels.

Johanna and Dietz, moreover, had witnessed firsthand the creation of fire whirlwinds, the Overlord’s Armour, and the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

These were phenomena on a completely different scale from what ordinary mages could produce... sights that could truly be called natural disasters.

Of course, mastery of magical techniques was essential, but above all, immense magical power was required to unleash such phenomena.

And it was absolutely impossible that Rei lacked that magical power.

"Well, that's why this hammer is a pure magic item... though calling it that might be a bit odd. It doesn't have the ability to control people or anything like that."

"...If that's true, it's a pretty powerful magic item, right? Capable of generating lightning, plus granting its wielder high regenerative abilities... wouldn't that make it a superior version of Lord Elk's Axe of the Thunder God? Well, it's a hammer, not an axe, so that's a big difference."

Surprisingly, Surnin seemed a bit excited, but Mireine spoke up, as if pouring cold water on his enthusiasm.

"True, just looking at the abilities, that might be the case... but who could possibly wield this? I mean, ordinary humans couldn't even swing this thing around..."

She paused, then turned her gaze toward Rei.

Rei's usual weapon was a large scythe.

True to its name, it was a large scythe, with a shaft about two meters long and a blade about one meter long. It was also a weapon that would be difficult for most people to master.

No, forget mastering it—even swinging it properly was difficult.

Given that Rei wielded such a weapon, Mireine's gaze suggested the possibility that perhaps she could also wield this magic item hammer. However, Rei shook her head at that suggestion.

He was confident in his strength, and if he wanted to, he could probably hold this hammer.

But that was the limit.

Compared even to the Death Scythe, this hammer was far too massive. While she could hold it, its sheer size would make swinging it impractical.

There was no way the smaller-than-average Rei could swing it effectively.

"I don't really feel like using it. Besides, compared to Elk's axe, I don't think it's an upgrade. Purely in terms of performance. If we're talking about versatility, maybe it could be considered an upgrade... but to me, it feels like a downgrade."

"Hmm... Now that you mention it..."

Surnin sighed regretfully, finally snapping back to reality at Rei's words.

"But, Rei-san. If you can't use it, why did you bring it at all?"

While petting Set, Johanna asked, and Rei inquired as he stowed the hammer into the Misty Ring.

"Leaving it there like that, other monsters might use it. Considering its size, normal monsters probably couldn't wield it, but we don't have any guarantee we've taken out every last Cyclops either."

"Are there still Cyclopes left?"

Rei understood Johanna's urge to sigh.

Originally, it was supposed to be just one cyclops. But upon arriving in the forest, they found five. Worse, a red cyclops—likely a rare or higher-tier species—was present, wielding a magic item. Then, when they tried to escape, two more cyclops were rampaging near where the horses and carriage had been, destroying the carriage and killing the horses.

If she were told there were even more cyclopes beyond that, Johanna would certainly find it all rather unpleasant.

"I'm not sure. But since we took down that red cyclops, I doubt the regular ones will stick together anymore."

"That's still a problem. A Rank C party might manage, but if ordinary travelers or low-rank adventurers encounter a Cyclops, they'll die for sure."

"...Once this business is settled, we should probably report it to the guild and suggest they look into it. Anyway, I'm heading to Gilm ahead of you. What about you guys? We do have horses, though."

Rei turned his gaze toward Dietz.

When Rei and the others left Gilm, Vihera and Dietz had brought spare horses along with their own mounts.

Since Byune was riding with Vihera, there were currently two horses available.

And here were Mireine, Johanna, Ecuril, and Surnin.

Of course, having two adults ride one horse was challenging in many ways, but it wouldn't be a problem as long as they didn't ride at full speed.

The borrowed horses were also highly trained and exceptionally capable, which was a significant advantage.

Hearing Rei's words, Mireine and the others whispered among themselves, discussing what to do, and soon reached a decision.

"Well then, if possible, we'd like to use the horses, if that's alright? We can't just leave Ecuril unconscious like this, and carrying her would be quite difficult."

That's how it ended up.

"Alright, I'll leave that to you. Even if another Cyclops survivor shows up, having Vihera with us should be reassuring."

"Heh heh, I hope so. Personally, since we've come all this way to the frontier, I'd be thrilled if a stronger monster showed up. Like that red Cyclops Rei fought, for instance."

Somehow relieved by her unchanging battle-crazed nature, Rei headed straight for Set.

"Well, you won't run out of enemies to fight in Gilm. It wouldn't be surprising if even more powerful monsters showed up eventually. ...Set."

"GRRRR!"

He climbed onto Set's back as the beast roared, rising to his feet as if to say, "I've been waiting for this."

Set growled back, conveying "See you later," then took a running start, flapping his wings and soaring skyward.

...Watching them depart, Mireine and Johanna looked on regretfully as they saw off the one person and one beast.
