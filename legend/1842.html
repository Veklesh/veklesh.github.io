A tense atmosphere swept through the room.

Brewitt shot an irritated glance at Azuel’s words.

To begin with, Brewitt, who roams the streets without a bodyguard, could be called an anomaly among the nobility.

Naturally, when someone of Brewitt’s build walks around town, it’s not uncommon for him to get dragged into a fight.

Even if he has no intention of it, there are those who simply can’t stand Brewitt’s arrogant—or, to put it another way, self-assured—attitude. Moreover, Brewitt isn’t the type to turn down a challenge; on the contrary, it’s not uncommon for him to stick his neck out and get himself into trouble.

That’s precisely why, when someone blatantly provokes him like this, he accepts the challenge immediately.

……That, too, is likely one of the reasons he’s considered an outsider among the nobility.

“Hey? Why can’t I go to Count Fanrail’s territory? It’s not like we’re rival factions or anything—that shouldn’t be a problem, right?”

“Brewitt. You’re the next Count, aren’t you? If so, what do you think would happen if someone like that were to die on your territory? At the very least, I couldn’t possibly get along with someone who might have killed my own successor.”

It’s not uncommon for nobles to have a public facade that’s completely at odds with what they’re thinking on the inside.

But even so, the general consensus is that one cannot maintain friendly relations with someone who killed one’s successor—especially a son by blood.

Given the high likelihood of that happening, it’s only natural that Count Fanrail wouldn’t be pleased by Brewitt’s visit.

No, if Brewitt were coming to an ordinary village or town, it wouldn’t be such a problem.

But the town Brewitt is hoping to visit is one near a forest teeming with monsters, a town frequented by adventurers.

In such a place, the likelihood of encountering trouble is naturally higher than in ordinary villages or towns, and there is no doubt that the nature of that trouble is also more dangerous than what one would find in ordinary villages or towns.

Azuel’s words carried that implication.

Upon hearing this, Brewitt thought for a moment... and then let out a deep sigh.

“Ugh, fine. It’s not like I’m looking to pick a fight with just anyone. I spoke without thinking.”

“Huh,” he said.

Rei, who had been watching, raised his opinion of Brewitt a notch.

Even for a noble... no, perhaps precisely because he was a noble, it’s usually quite difficult to honestly admit one’s mistakes—especially when pride is at stake.

But Brewitt did so without hesitation.

If it were purely a noble’s judgment, there might be an issue, but at the very least, from Rei’s perspective, Brewitt’s attitude was more than enough to be viewed favorably.

Rei’s opinion of Brewitt was already high to begin with, but this raised it another notch.

Azuel, too, seemed slightly surprised by Brewitt’s words, but eventually smiled and nodded in agreement.

“I’m glad you understand. I don’t want any unnecessary strife brought into the Noble Faction.”

“Ah, I was being rash, thinking as I usually do. …Still, in that sense, I do envy Lady Elena.”

“Huh? Me?”

Elena, who had been watching the exchange without saying a word, tilted her head at Brewitt’s remark.

That simple movement caused her golden hair to glisten in the light of the magic items illuminating the party hall, drawing the attention of Brewitt and the others.

Still, I was able to snap back to reality right away, probably because I’d seen Elena at parties a few times before.

That said, perhaps because Rei was there today, Elena seemed to be shining even more brightly than usual.

“Ah, right. Lady Elena is just like me… no, wait, she’s more important than me to the Noble Faction, isn’t she? Yet, unlike me, she can go wherever she pleases.”

“Hmm. Now that you mention it, that’s true. But that’s only because she has such a grand title as ‘Princess General.’ If Brewitt wants to be in a position like mine, he’ll have to distinguish himself on the battlefield or something—he’ll need to earn a title of his own.”

“...But there aren’t any battlefields left, you know.”

Until a few years ago, wars with the Bestir Empire were a frequent occurrence.

Of course, most of those were skirmishes, but in the large-scale war that took place a few years ago, the Bestir Empire suffered heavy losses.

Since then, the Bestir Empire has been plagued by civil strife, and ultimately, those who currently hold the reins of power in the Bestir Empire are a faction that could be called pro-Mireana Kingdom.

That said, it’s not so much that they genuinely feel any affinity for the Kingdom of Mireana; the main reason is that their beloved older sister is serving under Rei in the Kingdom of Mireana.

(Well, the prince aside, it’s a bit surprising that the Emperor is behaving so meekly and maintaining friendly relations with the Kingdom of Mireana.)

At Brewitt’s words, Rei recalled the image of the Emperor of the Bestir Empire he had met before.

It was clear just by looking at him that he had a personality brimming with ambition.

And yet, such a man had refrained from waging war against the Kingdom of Mireana—even though it was the will of his own successor.

The Bestir Empire is a great power on par with the Kingdom of Mireana, but it has one major weakness: it has no access to the sea.

That is precisely why they had been waging endless wars all this time, seeking to seize the ports held by the Kingdom of Mireana.

Given that, even Rei found it hard to believe that the Emperor would back down so meekly.

“If you’re looking to earn a nickname, there are other ways besides war with the Bestir Empire—like leading a large-scale campaign against bandits, standing up to rampaging monsters, or challenging supposedly strong opponents to mock battles and winning every time…”

Saying that, someone else interrupted Rei and the others’ conversation once again.

When he turned his gaze toward the source of the voice, he saw an old woman standing there.

Naturally, Rei didn’t know who the old woman was… or rather, unusually enough, he did know who she was.

That’s because this old woman is one of the most influential figures within the Noble Faction.

In this regard, it seemed that Miranda’s last-minute lessons about several important nobles had paid off.

Of course, this is a world without things like photographs or video cameras.

Well, perhaps such items exist as magic items, but unfortunately, there was no way Miranda—the heiress of a poor Baron’s House, and not even the eldest daughter—could have access to such things, so unless it was a distinctive figure—like this rare old woman—she wouldn’t have been able to tell who it was right away.

That was also why Rei didn’t recognize Zascal, the head of the Seisor Marquis family, the moment they met.

Above all, that distinctive way of speaking left no room for error.

“You’re the Duke of Fuldar, aren’t you? I’m Rei, an adventurer. Pleased to meet you.”

“Ho ho ho. You seem far more polite than I’d heard. I am Remuria, head of the Duke of Fuldar’s House. Pleased to meet you.”

Remuria said this with a cheerful smile.

Duke of Furlundal.

That was the name of a noble who wielded particularly strong influence even among the Noble Faction.

In the Kingdom of Mireana, the highest noble title is that of Duke, and apart from the king—the sole exception—no one else can issue orders to them.

...In reality, it’s not quite that simple, but the fact remains that they are near the pinnacle of the Mireana Kingdom.

What makes Lemuria particularly remarkable, given his position, is that despite being in his seventies or eighties, he still serves as the Duke of Furundal.

The duties of a head of the house are quite demanding, and it is common practice to pass the title to a successor in one’s fifties—or even earlier, in some cases.

Yet, in the House of Fuldar, the elderly Remuria still served as head of the family.

(I heard there were various disputes within the House, and as a result, this old woman, Remuria, is still serving as head of the House... but now that I think about it, I’m a little curious as to what kind of commotion actually took place.)

Miranda had told me about the general situation, but she hadn’t gone into specifics about what exactly caused the conflict.

I had other things to focus on… and, above all, I didn’t have much interest in the nobility, so I’d just let that information go in one ear and out the other.

“Still… aren’t you a bit too hot-blooded?”

The look in Lemuria’s eyes as she gazed at Brewitt was one of utter exasperation.

For Lemuria, who had lived as a noble for many years, Brewitt’s words—which seemed to hope for a fight to break out—made her frown.

Brewitt, caught in her gaze, couldn’t reflexively pick a fight like he had when Azuel spoke to him, so he scratched his head to cover up his discomfort.

The problem with Remuria wasn’t just her title as a member of the Duke’s House—it was, above all, her age.

If Brewitt were to lunge at Lemuria here, he might very well end up dead if he wasn’t careful.

Brewitt was generally hot-blooded, but even so, he couldn’t possibly imagine wanting to do something like kill an old woman.

“No, but still. You do admire people with nicknames, don’t you? It’s got nothing to do with being nobility or anything like that. Besides, if you had a nickname, people wouldn’t complain even if you went out alone.”

“……That’s true. There’s no doubt about that. But do you have any idea how few people actually manage to earn such a title…?”

Rei was slightly bothered by the way she ended her sentence, but what Lemuria was saying wasn’t wrong at all.

There are probably countless people who wish to have a nickname.

Especially among those who have just become adventurers, almost everyone must surely think that one day they too will want to have a nickname.

But among them, only a handful can actually become one.

No, perhaps even fewer than a handful.

It’s not as if everyone who aspired to have a nickname died, but there were certainly quite a few who died from pushing themselves too hard or taking unnecessary risks in their quest to earn one.

I suppose Lemuria didn’t want Brewitt to die that way.

But the more impetuous a young person is, the more they convince themselves that they’ll be fine.

Even now, Brewitt couldn’t understand Remuria’s feelings.

That said, he probably understands that it would be rude to simply reject the idea outright without discussion.

He managed to gloss over it and spoke up.

“Uh, well, you know. Anyway, I don’t plan on pushing myself too hard, so don’t worry. Besides, I’m pretty confident in my skills.”

“Ho ho ho. I’ve heard rumors about that myself. But there’s no point in pushing yourself to the point of death, is there?”

“You’re right. I’ll be as careful as I can.”

Though Brewitt said that, Rei couldn’t help but feel it was a bit of a stretch to believe he’d actually be careful in the truest sense.

It was probably just a line to get out of the situation.

That said, Rei had no intention of blaming Brewitt for it.

After all, Brewitt isn’t the kind of person Rei needs to look after every little detail.

Since he’s already acting on his own judgment, if anything happens, it’ll be his own responsibility.

…Though, even from what Rei could see, Brewitt clearly possessed sufficient strength, so he didn’t really think anything would happen.

(Besides, if something did happen, I’d probably end up helping Brewitt anyway.)

For Rei, Brewitt is someone he actually likes—which is rare for a noble.

That’s precisely why, if Brewitt were to ask for help, he would help him.

That said, Brewitt is also very proud. He wouldn’t ask Rei for help so easily, and if he did, it would only be when he was truly at his wits’ end.

“Right then, be careful, you hear?”

Rei couldn’t tell if Lemuria had determined that Brewitt was satisfied, but since Brewitt said he’d be careful for now, Lemuria didn’t seem inclined to say anything further.

“Well then. The reason I’ve spoken to you both like this… is partly because of that monk over there, but more than that, I have a small favor to ask of you.”

With that, Remuria’s gaze fell upon… as expected, Rei.
