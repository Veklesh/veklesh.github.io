Rebels, resistance fighters... they are those who have raised their banners against those who rule the land.

Considering the heavy taxes imposed in the County of Lerdact, it's hardly surprising such people would emerge.

"...Well, even with heavy taxes, they leave us enough to survive. But it's really just the bare minimum to live on. In normal times, that might be okay, but if a family member gets sick or injured, their livelihood becomes endangered all at once."

The man muttered as he ate the bread offered by Rei.

It was unclear whether he no longer felt the pain from being blown away by the Death Scythe or was simply enduring it, but he showed no sign of pain on his face.

Around him, the other unconscious people had also regained consciousness and were eating the bread Rei had offered.

Some regained consciousness while Rei and the man were talking. They looked at Rei as if they wanted to complain, but their nearby companions told them to help themselves to the bread. They took bread from a nearby bag and began eating.

(They sure are eating it like it's delicious... It's not freshly baked bread or anything, just preserved, hardened bread.)

Watching the men like this, Rei reached for a piece of bread and put it in his mouth.

Bread suited for long-term storage, used by adventurers, soldiers, merchants... those who travel.

Given that it's baked hard, it's not meant to be eaten as is. Normally, you'd dip it in soup to soften it before eating.

Of course, if soup isn't available, it's sometimes softened with water or hot water.

It wasn't inedible as is, but it was hard and hardly delicious.

But... right now, before Rei's eyes, every single rebel was desperately eating that hard-baked bread.

Seeing that, you can tell just how hungry those men were.

"For people who say they can survive, you lot sure look hungry."

"Well, we are... beings who can't be seen in public."

They couldn't openly act as rebels, so they had to move without being seen.

Naturally, that meant they struggled for food too.

"Villages and towns are barely surviving with the taxes they pay. We can't ask them to give us enough to live on. And if we lived in villages or towns, we'd be struggling just to pay our taxes... Changing the current situation is simply impossible."

That's precisely why we're struggling for food, the man explained. Rei shook her head weakly.

"Isn't that putting the cart before the horse in a lot of ways?"

"Even if you say that... In the end, someone's just gotta figure it out."

The man muttered bitterly.

The man who had been talking with Rei all this time was the leader of the rebel army.

That he was the first to be blown away by Rei's attack... perhaps it was just bad luck.

If he hadn't been knocked unconscious first, they might have coordinated better to deal with Rei and Set. ...Though it probably would have just delayed the inevitable.

"Anyway, I get the story. ...But if you're struggling for food, I don't think this is the time to be worrying about rebellions or whatever."

"...I know. But like I've said before, we're backed into a corner too. We have no choice."

The man declared he would never waver from his convictions.

And it wasn't just him; those around him eating bread felt the same way.

"I see. ...Well then, that might actually work out perfectly."

Rei gave the man a puzzled look at her straightforward response.

"No, the reason I came to this Lerdact County in the first place was to take revenge on Count Lerdact for something he did. And the revenge I have in mind is to steal a magic item and make sure they know it was my doing."

"Killing is forbidden, though," Rei added. The man's eyes widened dramatically.

The man already knew who stood before him, having heard it from Rei himself.

If such a person were to take revenge against Relduct, wouldn't that also benefit them?

"H-Hey!"

"Let me be clear: I won't be joining forces with you. But... if after I do something, you happen to try something and storm into Count Lerdact's mansion, and by chance the enemy forces are gone, and by chance you find the incapacitated Count Lerdact... I won't be responsible."

"...Hah, a series of coincidences? That sounds kind of interesting."

"But. Don't go taking Count Lerdact's life."

"Why the hell not?!"

One of the man's companions shouted at those words.

Considering all the suffering they'd endured, taking Lerdact's life seemed justified.

Though they shot him looks that seemed to say just that, Rei shook his head.

"If you absolutely insist on taking Count Lerdact's life, then I'll have to render you incapable of moving for several days right here. ...Are you still willing to do that?"

"That..."

The man faltered at Rei's words.

Even though they vastly outnumbered him, they had just experienced firsthand what would happen if they actually fought.

Still, they couldn't stomach Rei's attitude. Another man shouted, his face twisted with fury.

"Why the hell is that? Why the hell is a famous adventurer like you sucking up to nobility?!"

"I don't mean to. As for Count Lerdact, normally I wouldn't care if he died."

"Then why?!"

"Because that's the request I was given. I can't say who gave it, but that person wants revenge on Count Lerdact, not his death."

"...Why? Why?!"

One of the men shouted, unable to accept Rei's explanation.

"Well, if you can't agree with my opinion, you don't have to force yourselves to act with me. You guys do your thing, I'll do mine. If you can take down Count Lerdact on your own, go ahead. It's not like I'm forced to stick with you guys."

Faced with that, the men could only fall silent.

After all, it was undeniably true that their reliance on Rei's power had grown stronger.

"Anyway, it's fine if you want to use me to achieve your goals. But if you try to push your own agenda without listening to my demands... then next time, I'll be the one seriously dealing with you."

The men who had voiced their complaints to Rei earlier froze, a chill running down their spines.

They knew they wouldn't survive if Rei truly targeted them.

At this point, the rebels had several choices.

Could they defeat Lerdact with Rei's help, yet refrain from killing him?

Or challenge Lerdact without Rei's aid, facing overwhelming odds?

Hold off on a full-scale offensive against Lerdact for now and wait a little longer.

Or... ignore Rei's warning, use Rei's power to kill Lerdact, and become enemies with Rei.

(Damn it, this sucks. It looks like there are several choices, but in reality, there's no real choice, is there?)

There are several options, but only one can be clearly called the right answer.

Well, waiting it out isn't the best option either, but it's certainly not a bad one.

Still, the prospect of defeating Lerdact was an incredibly tempting option.

(But if we mess up, we could end up killing Count Lerdact. If that happens, based on how Rei acted earlier, he'll definitely come after us to kill us.)

The men had indeed gotten involved in rebellion, but what they'd done so far amounted to little more than skirmishes.

Of course, they'd feared being killed in battle before, but compared to the absolute murderous intent Rei had just radiated, those fears felt like child's play.

Team up with Rei... or rather, use her, as Rei suggested. Either way, in that case, ignoring Rei's demands would be impossible.

He understood the questioning looks from his comrades around him, wondering what he would decide, yet the man remained uncertain.

It was Rei who spoke up, temporarily putting the man's worries on hold.

"Well, you can decide that once I actually start moving. More importantly, do you know where that mine in the Count of Relduct's territory is?"

"Huh? Oh. I know where it is... but what are you going to do about it?"

"First, I'll have them destroyed."

"You fool!"

The one who said that wasn't Rei, nor the man talking to Rei, but another man present.

Of course. The mines were one of the few export industries for the Lerdact County.

If it were destroyed, the people of the Lerdact County would naturally suffer.

"I understand how the one who just shouted feels, but you know the Earl of Lerdact is building up his military, right? And that he's planning to invade the neighboring Viscount of Gale's territory."

Conflicts between nobles weren't exactly rare.

Disputes over mines or water with neighboring nobles are... well, not exactly commonplace, but they do happen frequently.

Of course, there are various unspoken agreements involved.

If the nobles suffered greater losses, it would mean a decline in the national strength of the Kingdom of Mireana.

For the Kingdom of Mireana, bordering the great nation of the Bestir Empire, weakening itself through internal strife was something to be avoided.

However, the military buildup currently being undertaken by Lerdact was far beyond the scope of such tacit understandings.

At least, that's what the intelligence from Gale indicated.

"But! ...And there are people working in those mines! Destroying the mines means killing them too, doesn't it?!"

"Relax. Even if we destroy the mines, we don't plan to do it during the day. Making the mines unusable is all we need. We have no intention of killing the men working there. ...Though what happens to Count Lerdact's men is another matter."

"...At night, huh?"

Though Reldaxt was eager to increase his wealth, he didn't force the miners to work nonstop all day.

If he did that, it would only fuel more resentment against him... Not only that, but he probably feared that making such a conspicuous move might draw Gale's attention.

Whatever the reason, the mines being inactive at night was fortunate for Rei.

There was no worry of involving the workers.

"Right. Night. ...So, it would be helpful if you could tell me the location of the mine. You don't want Count Lerdact's men getting better armed either, do you?"

"True. ...But it's been quite some time since mining began. There's no doubt Count Lerdact's men have significantly reinforced their armaments by now. In fact..."

The man paused his conversation with Rei and shifted his gaze to the weapons they held.

There, lying on the ground, were weapons used by the rebel army.

"Huh?"

Unable to understand the man's actions, Rei also turned his gaze to the tools.

Seeing Rei's reaction, the man smiled wryly and spoke.

"These weapons, actually, were taken with the help of our comrades infiltrating under Count Lerdact. Though 'taken' might not be the most accurate term. It'd be more precise to say we brought weapons that the Knights had stopped using and were stored in a warehouse."

"Ah... I see. So, it's basically hand-me-downs from Count Lerdact's subordinates, huh?"

At the word "hand-me-downs," those around him visibly grimaced with genuine disgust.

But it was undoubtedly true that without resorting to such measures, they wouldn't have been able to assemble their weapons.

Weapons are fairly expensive items.

Moreover, trying to gather weapons in this very Lerdact County meant not only the cost, but also the fact they couldn't be easily obtained.

In that sense, seizing weapons no longer in use was probably the quickest solution.

The only drawback was that, being hand-me-downs, their quality naturally fell short compared to weapons currently in use.

Rei nodded to the man, then handed him several spears taken from the Misty Ring—not the broken ones used for throwing, but ones seized from bandits and the like.

"If you tell me the location of the mine, I'll give you these as payment for the information. The quality isn't that great, but they should still be usable. You can never have too many spare spears, right?"

"...Are you sure?"

For the rebels, spare weapons were never too many.

Rei nodded at the man who asked like that.

"Getting the info here saves us the trouble of going all the way to that town."

Rei decided it was more advantageous to get the information here than to go out of his way to meet a man he knew only by name.
