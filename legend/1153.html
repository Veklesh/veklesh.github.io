Damn it, damn it, damn it! Why? Why the hell do we have to run from goblins like this?!

Shedding tears of frustration, the adventurer man continued running toward Gilm.

The other adventurer running beside him—a man in his thirties who exuded more experience than the twenty-something who'd spoken—shouted at his companion.

"Shut up! Right now, just focus on getting back to Gilm! If we waste our stamina here, they'll catch up!"

Because he was the veteran, the man understood the gravity of the situation unfolding.

Normally, for adventurers active in Gilm, goblins were barely worth considering as enemies.

But... that was only true when dealing with one or two at a time.

Facing five at once meant getting hurt if you let your guard down.

If there were more, the severity of the injuries you'd sustain would only increase...

Facing over a hundred goblins at once made survival difficult even for Rank D or Rank C adventurers.

Even if pure strength isn't an issue, living beings have physical limits.

And once stamina is depleted, movements slow down, making victory difficult even against goblins.

Unless there's some kind of exception, that's certain... Unfortunately, these two adventurers were not that exception.

As a result, they were told to inform their remaining comrades about the goblins, and so these two are heading towards Gilm, sacrificing their comrades as rearguard.

It was supposed to be a relatively simple request: search for a harpy's nest in the woods a short distance off the road... but in reality, it had led to this crisis where the party was on the verge of being decimated.

"I see it! Gilm!"

The sight of Gilm, their base, came into view. The veteran man shouted, and a look of joy spread across the other man's face...

"Guh!"

A strange sound suddenly reached their ears. Glancing sideways, they saw a man with an arrow lodged in his forehead.

A veteran adventurer should be able to handle an arrow flying from behind... especially one shot by a goblin.

After all, the man possessed that level of skill.

But... the mental turmoil from seeing the flood of goblins, the shame of fleeing from mere goblins, the responsibility of having to look after the younger man in the party, the sense of relief at seeing Gilm...

All of these emotions mingled together... and he couldn't dodge the arrow fired by the goblin archer pursuing him from behind.

Right before the man's eyes, the light faded from the eyes of the man who had just been encouraging him moments before. He collapsed to the ground, carried by the momentum of his running stride.

The man who remained behind watched this, yet he could not stop his feet.

Because the goblins were still chasing them from behind.

"Damn it! Damn it! Damn those bastards!"

He screamed, consumed by the bitter realization that he was now the last one left in the party... and that it was the goblins who had killed everyone else.

His luck lay in the fact that his relentless running had brought him closer to the highway.

And on the road, he spotted several adventurers. Seeing them, the pursuing goblins judged further pursuit too dangerous and withdrew.

...This was something a normal goblin would never do.

Goblins attack any enemy in sight, and if defeated, they flee without hesitation.

They couldn't gauge the difference in strength between themselves and their opponent, and they wouldn't hesitate to attack even a griffin.

And yet, once shown true strength, they scatter and flee in disarray.

For such goblins to retreat mid-attack, judging it potentially dangerous, is nothing short of an anomaly.

Some of the adventurers on the road noticed the goblins' unusual behavior.

Seeing the group of goblins chasing the man, they were naturally on guard.

The man, who had literally narrowly escaped death, felt uneasy about the goblins' behavior. Nevertheless, he stepped onto the road and began running toward Gilm.

The man, now the sole survivor of his party, continued running straight ahead, seemingly oblivious to the questioning glances directed his way by others traveling the road.

Arriving at Gilm's main gate, he practically lunged at the guard processing entry procedures and blurted out.

"Listen to me!"

"Whoa! What the hell!? ...Ah, the procedures are complete, so you may enter."

The guard, who had been processing the person just seconds ago, turned back to face the man who had grabbed him.

"Goblins! A group of goblins appeared!"

"...A group of goblins?"

If this weren't Gilm... if it were a normal town, not on the frontier, he might have thought, "What's the big deal about goblins?"

But this was Gilm, the frontier.

Hearing that a goblin horde had appeared, one thing came to mind.

"Which are leading them—the higher species or the rare species?"

The guard's expression had changed completely from when he'd been processing the person who'd just entered Gilm earlier. It was sharp now.

He clearly understood the danger at hand.

"Probably a goblin leader. There were over a hundred goblins."

"...A hundred?"

Hearing this, the guard's expression softened slightly.

Hearing that a large number of goblins had appeared, he had assumed a swarm in the thousands or tens of thousands.

Of course, even monsters like goblins become a significant threat when there are a hundred of them.

A skilled high-rank adventurer might manage, but for low-rank adventurers or merchants, they're a hopelessly desperate situation.

(Well, it's only a hundred or so. Is this guy right about it being a goblin leader? I guess we should be thankful it wasn't an orc that showed up as a higher-tier or rare species.)

Both goblins and orcs are the same in that they use human women for breeding.

But their individual strength differs greatly.

A single ordinary orc can take on several goblins... or even more than ten if things go badly.

Given how stupid goblins are, that gap can only widen, never narrow.

(We should be thankful it was only a leader.)

Higher-ranking commanders... like Generals leading thousands of goblins, or Goblin Kings commanding tens of thousands... when such entities appear, not just adventurers, but knight orders and guard units must also be deployed.

The settlement led by the Orc King, which once existed not far from Gilm, had fewer members. This was partly because fewer orcs had gathered there, but also because goblins and orcs inherently differ greatly in their reproductive capacity.

Compared to such an Orc King, a Goblin leader equivalent to a human squad leader is something a group of adventurers could handle if they gathered enough strength.

It might be difficult for a single party to handle it alone, but considering the skill level of the adventurers in Gilm, it was by no means impossible.

While it would need to be reported to higher-ups, it was understood that it wouldn't escalate into a major issue.

The guard, having made that judgment, checked the man's guild card and swiftly completed the procedures.

"I'll inform the higher-ups about the goblin matter too. But I expect the guild will handle it directly."

"I know! But let me tell you, I don't think it's just a regular goblin leader!"

He must be in a terrible hurry. Spitting out the words curtly, the man walked straight into Gilm.

"...Goblins, huh. Hope this doesn't turn into a hassle."

Goblin leaders didn't appear frequently, but they weren't exactly rare either.

But... for some reason, the guard couldn't shake a bad feeling in his chest.

He thought he needed to make sure to report the man's remark—that it wasn't just any goblin leader—as a special note.

"A group of goblins has appeared!"

The adventurer man entered the guild and headed toward the counter, informing the receptionist.

Fortunately, it was daytime, not a busy hour for the guild.

Because of this, the man didn't have to wait in line and could speak to the receptionist immediately.

"A goblin horde!? How many are there!?"

Lenora , who had been organizing documents, asked the man in surprise.

Then, turning her gaze toward the man, she realized he was someone she knew.

It was Hester, one of the younger adventurers.

He was the one who had recently joined the Rank D party, Drifting Cloud, and was starting to make a name for himself as a promising new adventurer.

But the man before her now bore no resemblance to someone you'd call up-and-coming.

He looked like he'd barely escaped with his life.

"Over a hundred. ...Everyone else but me."

I see, Renola thought.

Lenora understood why Hester was giving off this vibe.

If the party she'd just joined was wiped out, this was bound to happen.

Though she felt pity for the man before her, Lenora, the guild receptionist, had to do her job.

"Over a hundred? Then it must be a goblin leader."

Lenora expected him to answer yes to that. But Hester hesitated to nod.

"Hester?"

"No, I know that only a Goblin Leader would command a hundred goblins. But for that, the goblins were strangely clever."

"Smart?"

"Yeah. Like, they sensed danger when they got close to the road and cut off their pursuit midway."

"That's..."

Lenora, working as a receptionist at this guild, had gathered a great deal of knowledge about monsters.

Her knowledge was probably superior even compared to some clumsy adventurers.

Even for someone like Lenora, she'd never heard of goblins acting that intelligently.

"Could it be... a higher-ranking one? Like... a Goblin General? No, even then, it's hard to imagine them acting that intelligently..."

"Lenora, shouldn't we report this to the higher-ups right away? If goblins are pulling stunts like this... it could mean we're dealing with a rare species of goblin leader."

Kenny , who had been listening beside Lenora, spoke with a slightly grave expression.

"Yeah, you're right. Even if we don't know, the higher-ups... worst case, the Guild Master might know something."

"Yes. Given how long they've lived, it wouldn't be surprising if they knew something like that."

"...Somehow, doesn't that feel a bit... prickly?"

"Really?"

As they exchanged these words, Lenora rose from her seat.

She headed straight for her superior, who was stationed deep inside the counter area, and relayed the details Hector had shared.

"I see. It's correct to assume it's the goblin leader. But... so that's how it is."

The man, who appeared to be in his fifties, groaned thoughtfully after hearing Lenora's explanation.

Based on what he'd heard, it was undoubtedly a case of a goblin leader emerging.

But considering the goblins' behavior on the road, it was far beyond the judgment a goblin leader could make.

"This bothers me a bit. Is there any adventurer free and able to move immediately?"

"Even if you say so..."

It was daytime, so most adventurers were out fulfilling quests.

There are adventurers in the tavern too, but those are just the ones celebrating a job well done or taking the day off to rest.

A few adventurers were gathered near the request board, ready to take on new jobs, but most of them weren't exactly seasoned veterans.

If this were a true crisis—a matter of whether Gilm itself would be wiped out—it might be possible to summon skilled adventurers from around the city. But a goblin leader and a hundred or so goblins weren't quite that serious a threat, making it impossible.

Fortunately or unfortunately, this Gilm is located in the frontier, so there are many skilled adventurers.

Honestly, if you felt like it, you could probably find one just by stepping outside the guild and searching for a few minutes.

(Well, most adventurers are out on quests right now, so that might be difficult too.)

Since it wasn't that urgent, the worst-case scenario would be asking an adventurer returning from a job in the evening to investigate.

That's what Lenora was thinking, but just as she was about to voice it... a familiar voice reached her from the counter.

"Ah, Rei! I heard you were in the Kingdom of Solrain, but you're back!?"

Hearing the voice of her friend... no, her troublemaking buddy, Lenora couldn't help but thank the heavens for this stroke of luck.
