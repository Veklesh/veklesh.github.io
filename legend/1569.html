"Here they come, you bastards! Don't hold back! Attack with everything you've got! But don't kill them!"

Attack with everything you've got, but don't kill them.

The order that came from Zelsutri's mouth was undoubtedly unreasonable.

But still, if it came from their superior—and specifically the third-highest-ranking figure in this facility that could be called a giant holding area—the soldiers had no choice but to obey.

Some wondered why he'd lied about being in charge here... but regardless, the soldiers ordered to attack the targets before them had no time to ponder such things.

Fortunately, the people before them didn't seem like particularly strong opponents.

In fact, three of the five were beauties beyond compare.

And Zelsutri's interest wasn't in those three, but in one of the remaining two—a girl who could only be called a child.

It was a fairly well-known fact around here that Zelstri had such tastes.

Given that, once the five before them were defeated, the three beauties should be his to claim.

Under normal circumstances, he might harbor such feelings toward such suggestive individuals... well, he would harbor them, but he wouldn't act on them.

But fortunately, this time he had orders from his superior, so there was no hesitation here.

"Haah!"

The soldier who moved first targeted the figure clad only in gauntlets and greaves for armor, wearing thin garments like a dancer or courtesan.

She flaunted an incredibly alluring figure, directly provoking the desires of men.

"Oh? Targeting me first? But... no."

With those words, Vihera effortlessly parried the thrust from the man's spear.

"No killing intent whatsoever. And thrusting with the butt of the spear instead of the tip... Honestly, I doubt you even have the spirit to fight."

Speaking with apparent exasperation, Vihera parried the spear strike with her gauntlet and immediately stepped forward... driving her fist into his solar plexus.

The soldiers, seemingly unprepared for anyone actually attacking here, each held weapons but wore no armor beyond leather armor.

They were completely caught off guard.

After all, this place had never been attacked before, so a lack of tension was perhaps understandable.

They had received word that the Giant's Nest had been attacked, but the idea that the Jaya, the largest and strongest organization in the Kingdom of Reburina, could lose never crossed their minds.

And with reinforcements sent, victory seemed assured.

But... those men's hopes... no, their delusions crumbled effortlessly before reality.

It was as if they had built a castle in the sand.

Starting with Vihera's strike, the battles began one after another.

Yet, the only ones suffering casualties were the soldiers of Jaya.

And it was utterly one-sided.

"Hmph!"

The one particularly excelling in this battle is Byune.

The soldiers, armed but unarmored, were nothing but prime prey for Byune, who specialized in the Long Needle.

If these soldiers were elite, they might have been able to parry a long needle strike or block it with armor... but unfortunately, the men had no such luxury.

"Uwaah!"

"Gyah!"

"Ow!"

Screams rose one after another at the pain of the long needles piercing them.

It was far beyond any pain the soldiers could endure.

"Well then, I suppose I'll step in. ...After all, the more spirited the prey, the more enjoyable the hunt."

Saying this, Zelstri stepped forward.

Zelstri's target, naturally, was Byune, who stirred his desire.

To capture that lovely maiden with his own hands and make her the prey of his desires.

That was the thought driving him forward...

"I suggest you refrain from making strange advances toward my party members."

Rei blocked his path, halting Zelstri's advance.

"...What the hell, you? Kid? I've got business with that woman. I've got no interest in scum like you, so get out of my way."

With the Dragon Robe hood removed, Rei's face alone could easily be mistaken for a woman's.

But Zelstri paid Rei no mind whatsoever.

That was likely because, within Zelstri's mind, he had determined that the Rei standing before him was not a woman.

Realizing this, Rei frowned in displeasure.

"You think I'll just say 'Yes, sir' and back off? If you really want to deal with me, then you'll have to deal with me by force!"

"Oh? Then... I'll grant your wish and force my way through!"

With that shout, Zelstri closed the distance to Rei in one swift motion.

The speed of his lunge was somewhat surprising, even to Rei.

...Though, it was only a slight surprise.

For someone from a small country like Reburina, and specifically within a single organization, he could be considered quite skilled.

But compared to those Rei had fought before, that speed instantly became ordinary... or even below ordinary in some cases.

He evaded the fist swung toward him by stepping back a single step.

Rei gently reached out his hand to the fist passing mere centimeters before his eyes, applying just enough pressure to alter its flow of force.

"Guh!"

Dragged along by that shift in force, Zelstri's body flew through the air.

To an observer, it would appear as though he had gracefully parried the enemy's attack.

But... the next instant, a bone-crunching, dull sensation returned to Rei's hand.

Though the surrounding chaos of battle muffled the sound, the sensation was unmistakable to Rei, who gripped Zelstri's wrist.

"Ah."

Rei muttered something that clearly meant "Damn it."

It was only natural. He'd intended to parry Zelstri's attack like aikido and throw him, but he'd misjudged the force and ended up breaking the bones in his wrist.

Zelstri, his wrist bone broken mid-air, couldn't even break his fall properly and slammed into the ground.

Given Zelstri's reputation for strength, the blow naturally carried tremendous force.

In the end, it had come back to haunt him—nothing short of sheer bad luck.

Especially since it was Rei's mistake that broke his wrist bone in the first place.

"Guh... Guhnnoooooo!"

The impact of being slammed into the ground on his back, his own strength amplified by Rei's, and the broken bones in his wrist, shattered by Rei.

The dual agony left Zelstri unable to do anything but groan in pain.

And that groan... or rather, that scream that echoed out stopped the movements of the soldiers fighting around him.

To the soldiers, Zelstri's strength was absolute.

That Zelstri would be defeated so easily... Was this some kind of dream?

Thinking this, the soldiers turned their gaze toward Rei.

If the one who defeated Zelsutri had been someone of comparable stature, they might have been able to accept it.

But Rei was different. He was clearly smaller than Zelsutri, and in a normal fight, it seemed impossible he could possibly stand a chance against him.

Because of this, some soldiers even wondered if Zelsutri had just tripped or something.

But... seeing Zelsutri lying sprawled on the ground, it was obvious there was no possibility he had merely stumbled.

"Well then, what now? If you still want to fight, I'm game."

Rei's muttered words echoed strangely well throughout the area.

Part of it was likely the disbelief that Zelsutri, the strongest among them, had been defeated so easily, causing the fighting to naturally subside... but there was also something about Rei's words now that compelled them to listen.

They probably understood that if the fight continued, they wouldn't get off scot-free either.

(Party cocktail effect? No, the name feels slightly off, but I'm sure I saw or heard something like that in Japan.)

To be precise, it wasn't the Party Cocktail Effect, but the Cocktail Party Effect.

It's the phenomenon where, even amidst a crowd of people talking, you can pick out your own name or things that interest you. It's slightly different from what Rei was thinking, but he didn't seem particularly bothered by that.

In any case, the soldiers are left wondering what to do after Rei's words.

They no longer believed they could win a fight.

But that doesn't mean they can just quietly surrender either.

They were in a situation where retreat was impossible.

"What's the matter? If you're going to do it, do it quickly. We don't have all day to deal with you lot."

As Rei stated this again, the soldiers gradually dropped their weapons, one by one.

After a glance at this, Rei and the others moved toward the door.

It was a large door, wide enough for two carriages to pass side by side and tall enough for a giant to walk through.

A wall had been built beside the door, meaning those emerging from the hidden passage had no choice but to pass through it to exit.

Which meant Rei and her group were in the same position...

"Would you open the door? Otherwise, we'll have to break it down."

At Rei's words, the soldiers hurriedly opened the door.

They did so because they believed that if it were Rei and his group, they could break even this massive door.

Of course, if they had let Byune search, finding the mechanism to open the door wouldn't have been difficult.

But it was important to make them believe that Rei and her group hadn't simply barged through here on their own, but that they themselves had let them pass. That was the judgment they had made.

Elena and the others didn't seem to have any particular objection to Rei's attitude either, showing no sign of interjecting.

As they stood there, the closed wooden door creaked open.

(Huh)

As expected, a path lay ready beyond the opened door.

It wasn't a well-maintained road like a highway, though. It was a path, naturally formed and trodden down.

Considering it was a path trodden by many giants, that was probably only natural.

This path, compacted by their footsteps, continued deep into the forest right beside the door Rei and the others had emerged from.

No, the correct explanation was probably that they had cleared a path through the forest, however narrow, to create that door... or rather, what could be called a simple checkpoint.

Impressed by this, Rei still harbored a question.

(I don't know exactly how far this place is from Mejogo, but judging by the travel time, it shouldn't be that far. If that's the case, I think we should have noticed something unusual when we came to scout Mejogo on Set before.)

That question.

Even if they were using magic to create illusions, Rei might not have noticed, but Set certainly should have detected it.

And yet, he had absolutely no recollection of any facility gathering the giants that must lie ahead.

"Well, maybe we'll find out once we get there."

"What's wrong, all of a sudden? Did you notice something strange?"

Marina asked, apparently hearing Rei's mutter.

Rei shook his head at Marina's words and replied that it was nothing.

In truth, he judged that even if he voiced what he felt, it would likely be meaningless.

Well, not that it wouldn't matter, but he still judged that he probably wouldn't understand unless he went ahead and saw for himself.

"No, I was just wondering what kind of place that facility up ahead is. ...In that underground facility, the black crystal seemed to be providing some kind of energy to the giants. But the number of giants shipped out so far is probably a hundred... a thousand, maybe even more than that."

"Now that you mention it, that makes sense. Being giants, they must eat a lot. I wonder how they manage the food supply?"

The mention of eating reminded her of the scene when a giant was born, tearing through its mother's womb, then devouring the mother's body as she died from the pain and shock... Marina furrowed her beautiful brow.

But considering the actual size of a giant's body, it was undeniable that they required a tremendous amount of food.

And then there's the sheer number of giants.

It was obvious that food bought normally from stores or gathered in any other way wouldn't be nearly enough.

Well, it might be possible if they acted recklessly, but such behavior would naturally draw attention.

Just because Jaya was the largest underground organization in the country of Reburina didn't mean they had no enemies.

And since one person alone couldn't defeat an organization like Jaya, seeking reinforcements elsewhere was only natural.

How were they gathering food without drawing attention while under the watchful eyes of such people...? Everyone, including Rei, shared that same question.
