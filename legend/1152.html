Rei , Vihera , and Set headed to a restaurant that was fairly upscale even by Gilm standards.

That said, neither Rei nor Vihera were short on money, so entering a restaurant of this caliber posed no particular problem.

Rei, in particular, had wiped out several bandit groups on his way back from Gosh, and the treasure he'd acquired amounted to a considerable sum.

Vihera, confident in her own abilities, could easily earn money by accepting monster extermination requests if she wanted to.

In fact, ever since returning to Gilm and hearing that Rei had gone alone to the desert nation of Solrain, she'd been accepting extermination requests almost daily, partly to vent her frustrations.

"Hmm. A battle with a Sand Serpent sounds somewhat interesting. I've fought similar monsters a few times before."

Vihera replied briefly after hearing about Rei's battle with the Sand Serpent—though only the part about the Twilight Spear being thrown.

For Vihera, skilled in hand-to-hand combat, a large opponent would normally be considered extremely difficult to fight.

Indeed, the reason she used weapons like longswords and spears instead of relying solely on hand-to-hand combat was related to the opponent's size and the resulting distance.

And since hand-to-hand combat relies on one's own limbs for attack, the offensive power inevitably becomes lower.

...Though, in Vihera's case, she could use magic item gauntlets and greaves to generate sharp claws or blades.

However, since the weapons thus created are still used in close combat, their reach isn't particularly long.

In other words, while they can slice through an opponent's skin and flesh, inflicting damage beyond that is generally impossible.

Even so, Vihera finds the battle with the Sand Serpent interesting because she has a plan to win.

Inflicting a magical shock directly within the opponent's body... Vihera's skill, Magic-Infused Palm.

With this skill, even the toughest armor, most supple skin, or thickest muscles become nearly meaningless.

The more confident an opponent is in their defenses, the more likely Vihera's strike will be fatal.

"Vihera might actually enjoy it. ...I took it down with a single strike from the Twilight Spear, though."

"Did you hear about that? That spear caused quite a big fuss, didn't it? And in the end, Rei had to evacuate temporarily or something. ...If he'd come when I was here, I would've given him a fun little punishment."

Seeing Vihera smile like that, I wondered what kind of punishment she would have given... thinking about how lucky the missing merchants were, I reached for the food on the plate.

The food bought from Gosh's stall and the dishes Cathy made were delicious, of course.

But still, Rei had spent the longest time in this world here in Gilm, and eating Gilm's food gave him a comforting feeling, like returning home.

"So, what are you going to do now, Rei?"

"...From here? Well, I don't really have anything specific in mind."

If asked if he wanted to go somewhere or do something, Rei would probably hesitate a bit and shake his head.

For now, he'd focus on gathering magic stones from unknown monsters around Gilm.

The remote Gilm, naturally, still held many monsters Rei had never fought before.

In fact, the monsters he had fought were few and far between.

The monsters in the desert were certainly intriguing, but he wanted to defeat the monsters closer to home rather than those in other regions.

For Rei, that wasn't strange at all.

(Eventually, the Demon Forest too...)

As Rei pondered this, he noticed Vihera looking oddly displeased at the end of his gaze.

No, sulking was a more accurate description than being displeased.

"What's wrong?"

"...Nothing. It's just... Rei seemed to be thinking about something else while you were with me."

"Other things? But you were the one who asked what we should do next, weren't you?"

"I know. ...Still, you should try to understand a woman's feelings a little."

Rei tilted his head.

Of course, the person standing before Rei was someone worthy of being called a stunning beauty.

He knew it was generally strange to think about a woman's heart with someone like her.

But if that person was Vihera... Honestly, Rei felt it would make more sense if she asked him not to fight.

Vihera, perhaps sensing with a woman's intuition that he was thinking something strange about her, fixed a slightly steady gaze on Rei.

Vihera was well aware she wasn't an ordinary woman.

Even so, it was only natural for a girl in love to occasionally wish to be seen as a woman by the one she liked.

"This dish is delicious."

The topic was abruptly changed.

Rei must have realized that continuing this conversation could lead to trouble.

Seeing Rei like that, Vihera tried to say something for a moment, but she must have decided that anything she said now would be meaningless. Without saying anything further, she reached for the food.

And so, while eating, Rei and Vihera drifted into casual conversation.

Rei understood that pressing further would only put him at a disadvantage, so he avoided delving deeper into matters of a woman's heart. Perhaps Vihera also felt it pitiful to blame Rei any further, so she went along with his words.

"Yeah. It is a bit of a shame we can't try the food of the Bestir Empire."

"Don't be unreasonable. If this place were near the Bestir Empire, that'd be one thing, but it's a distant, ordinary frontier, you know?"

"...Ordinary frontier is a term that raises all sorts of questions."

"I won't deny that. But the fact remains we're far from the Bestir Empire. Given that, expecting to eat Bestir Empire cuisine in Gilm is a bit much, don't you think?"

"I know that, though."

They continued their meal while exchanging such conversation.

When Vihera first entered this restaurant, she was radiating anger. But continuing to eat with Rei like this seemed to have calmed her considerably. Even the waitstaff bringing dishes to the room weren't as tense as they had been initially.

"Here you are, the stewed Baicon and Kelpie."

The two stews, arguably the main dishes of this meal, were placed on the table.

The bicorn is a subspecies of unicorn, possessing two horns unlike the unicorn with only one.

Their temperament is also vastly different from the generally docile unicorn; they are extremely vicious and will attack anyone they see.

Kelpie are horse-like monsters that live in water, known for excelling at water-attribute magic.

Both are known as fairly powerful horse-like monsters, and serving their meat for comparison is something only a high-end restaurant like this could manage.

"Both meats are tender yet have a satisfying chewiness—delicious. ...Though the Kelpie might be slightly more tender."

"That's true. The Biacon is a monster that lives on land, while the Kelpie lives in water. That difference in meat texture probably comes from that, right?"

Rei and Vihera shared their impressions while tasting each type of meat.

(Horse meat... Hmm. It brings back memories.)

Memories of the horse meat dishes his mother occasionally made back in Japan flashed through Rei's mind.

A stew made with horse meat, onions, shirataki noodles, and sliced ginger to cut the gaminess.

He loved eating it over freshly cooked rice, like a horse bowl instead of a beef bowl.

(In this world, there might be similar dishes, I suppose.)

Thinking such thoughts, he finished his meal... and finally, as dessert, chilled fruit was served.

Magic items like this aren't cheap, but I suppose that's precisely why they're used in high-end establishments.

The refreshingly tart fruit seemed to make me momentarily forget the summer heat... Perhaps because it was chilled, its sweetness felt even more pronounced.

After finishing the meal, Vihera seemed to have calmed down somewhat. Her anger had completely subsided.

After settling the bill and stepping outside, Set was waiting there.

His cheerful demeanor was likely because he'd been eating the dishes brought out from the restaurant while Rei and Vihera dined.

Feeding him in front of the shop rather than in the stables meant they wanted everyone around to know that Set... and Rei, were patrons of their establishment.

The sight of Set enjoying the food heartily, while perhaps commonplace elsewhere, warmed the hearts of everyone in Gilm.

This served as tremendous publicity for the shop... and undoubtedly had a very high customer attraction effect.

"Gurrrrrrr!"

Having just finished his steamed river fish, Set purred at Rei and Vihera, a tiny bit of fish skin still clinging to his beak.

Seeing Set like this, Vihera, who had calmed down but was still a little angry, relaxed. She gently removed the fish skin from his beak.

"Rei has Set, and Elena has Yellow... I'm a little envious."

Though Vihera loved combat above all else, it didn't mean she had no interest in other things.

She felt a little envious of familiar spirits and familiars like Set and Yellow, who were both beloved and adorable, yet also demonstrated high combat prowess.

She did wish she had such a presence herself... but unfortunately, Vihera possessed neither the talent of a tamer nor the magical ability required to create a familiar.

Of course, if she tried desperately enough, it wasn't impossible.

For instance, I've heard that some rare tamers actually sell the monsters they've tamed.

But... even so.

Vihera stroked Set while silently shaking her head inside.

It wouldn't be easy to travel together unless it was not only adorable but also genuinely useful in battle.

"Hmm, well... Acquiring a familiar or a servant spirit is difficult... If there's any possibility, there's the dragon knight-style method of raising monsters yourself from eggs or newborns."

"I suppose that's the only option. Though, as an adventurer, taking care of eggs or babies is quite a hassle, you know."

"...I suppose so."

As it is, the Vihera before us has a penchant for fighting.

In that state, hatching eggs or caring for newborn monster babies would be difficult.

"Isn't there some kind of magic item for that? Rei the mage might know something?"

While scratching Set's neck as he purred contentedly, Vihera asked. Rei recalled a book he'd seen in the town library before.

"Hmm, well... They're not living creatures per se, but... for beings created using magic, like golems or gargoyles, I don't think you'd need to raise them."

"Golems and gargoyles, huh... I suppose it's possible, but it doesn't quite click for me."

Was Vihera picturing herself clinging to a gargoyle's leg, flying through the sky? Or perhaps perched atop a golem's shoulder?

Whatever the image, given that Vihera herself was a beauty without equal, it couldn't possibly look out of place.

"Well, even if we're talking about creating them with magic, I can't do it myself. We'd need help from someone like an alchemist, right?"

The two figures who created the Twilight Spear crossed Rei's mind.

However, asking them to create another golem or gargoyle so soon after crafting the Twilight Spear felt rather risky.

Of course, the danger lay in the risk to the health of those two, who had pushed themselves to the limit creating the Twilight Spear.

While pondering this, Rei suddenly spoke up, sounding a bit joking.

"In Vihera's case, there's Byune , right? She's got more than enough skill as a thief, so she probably wouldn't be a hindrance."

That much was clear, considering their experience in the battle fought in Marina's home forest.

It was true that Byune didn't possess combat skills worthy of being called a one-woman army or a force to be reckoned with, but she still had the ability to not interfere with Vihera's fight and protect herself.

Well, to be precise, it wasn't so much protecting herself as staying hidden and avoiding detection.

On top of that, she was undeniably cute, perhaps because she was still small.

In that sense, Byune was probably the perfect candidate for what Vihera was looking for.

It wouldn't be strange to think that way.

"Listen... Byune isn't really a familiar or a servant, is she?"

As Vihera said this with an exasperated look, her gaze fell upon... an adventurer rushing toward the guild with a bloodshot face.

"Did something happen?"

"...Being with Rei never gets boring, does it?"

Meeting each other's gaze, the two people and one creature began walking toward the guild.
