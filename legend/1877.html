Celestine remained silent, a faint smile playing on her lips.

Seeing Celestine like that, Rei turns his gaze toward Elena.

It was a look that said, “I’ve asked what I wanted to ask, but what about you?”

That said, it was clear to Rei that Celestine was targeting him as the Black Wolf’s enemy, and the circumstances surrounding that… Rei was intrigued by the nature of the relationship between the man leading the organization and a mere assassin, skilled as he might be.

Especially since the Black Wolf, like Rei, was from Japan.

Whether Celestine knew the details of that situation or not… if possible, Ray had wanted to ask her about it, but judging from their earlier exchange, he couldn’t possibly expect her to speak honestly.

That was precisely why Rei decided to let Elena take the lead.

However, Elena silently shook her head at Rei’s gaze.

She must have thought that if she tried to ask anything here, she would likely—no, she would undoubtedly just be brushed off.

If Celestane had been willing to answer his questions properly, there were plenty of things he would have wanted to ask.

“Oh? Is there nothing else you want to ask? I would have liked to talk a little longer, if possible.”

Celestane muttered regretfully, and with those words, Rei realized why Celestane had been answering his questions all along.

“Buying time, is it?”

“Yes, exactly.”

The moment she said that, Ray sensed an inexplicable intensity emanating from Celestine, who had been wearing a gentle expression until then.

It was as if his previous behavior had all been a mask—that was the impression it gave.

“Unfortunately for us, this seal still won’t break. …Honestly, according to our calculations, the seal was supposed to be lifted a little sooner… Perhaps the bloodline of the Seisor Marquis family, which the man named Gaiska—whom we sacrificed—carries, isn’t quite as strong as we thought. Well, since he’s a direct descendant but not the legitimate heir, that might have something to do with it.”

The way Celestine looked at the flesh cocoon was less like looking at a human and more like examining some kind of material.

“But, well… judging by the way it looks, the seal seems to be about to be broken. I suppose we can consider our attempt to buy time a success.”

The seal is breaking.

As if to prove those words, the blood vessel-like structures that appear to be entwined around the flesh cocoon begin to pulsate more strongly.

Compared to when Rei and Elena first entered this space, the pulsation is clearly stronger.

There’s no doubt it’s getting stronger…

"I see. So you wanted to buy time and wait for the seal to be broken. But... doesn't that also mean the seal hasn't been broken yet? Considering we're standing right here, isn't that a bit too careless?"

Rei states this.

Even Rei doesn’t know what kind of entity is sealed within that flesh cocoon. But given that they went to the trouble of sealing it like this, it must undoubtedly be powerful.

But… no matter how powerful it is, it cannot demonstrate its true strength while sealed.

In other words, in this situation, for Celestine to protect the flesh cocoon, she, the butler, and Deotres—just the three of them—would have to face Rei and Elena, two individuals with such formidable reputations.

Objectively speaking, there was no doubt that this was an impossible task.

Despite that, even after hearing Rei’s words, Celestine showed no sign of being shaken… she even had a smile on her face.

Rei was concerned by Celestine’s demeanor, but even from his perspective, she didn’t look strong.

Compared to the average person, she seemed to be in decent physical shape, but that was about it.

Deotres possessed a decent level of strength, and the butler was even more agile than Deotres.

However, even if the butler was the strongest of the three, that was only true when compared to Deotres and the others.

That level of strength was practically within the margin of error for Rei and Elena.

(Could they be hiding their true strength? No, but... judging by what I see here, I really can’t imagine that.)

At the very least, from Rei’s perspective, it didn’t seem like Celestine and the other two were hiding their true strength.

If that’s the case, the fact that they aren’t showing any signs of strain even in this situation… means they’re either bluffing, or he should assume they have some kind of trump card up their sleeves.

And given that they lead the largest organization in the underworld of Anesis, the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, Rei believed the former possibility was extremely unlikely.

(That said...)

Right in front of Celestine and the others, Rei reached into his Misty Ring to pull out the Death Scythe...

“Huh?”

Normally, he should be able to pull the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear—no, in fact, anything and everything stored within the Misty Ring on his right wrist—out of it. But he couldn’t.

“Oh my, what’s the matter? Aren’t you going to pull out the great scythe that’s synonymous with your nickname, ‘Crimson’? Or… are you unable to pull it out?”

A smile played on Celestine’s face as she spoke.

She must understand what is happening to Rei.

...Rei is strong, but there are many reasons for that strength.

One of those reasons is the Misty Ring.

A true item box—a rarity even in this world—capable of storing anything and retrieving it with ease.

But if the Misty Ring—the very source of Rei’s strength—were to be sealed… and especially if that were to happen while he hadn’t even obtained a weapon yet?

That alone wouldn’t render Crimson Rei completely powerless, but the absence of the Death Scythe, which serves as both a magic catalyst and the vessel for his Beast Magic skills, was undoubtedly a significant blow to his combat effectiveness.

However… even though Rei understood that his combat power had been significantly reduced, his unease was quelled in an instant.

It was a painful blow that the Death Scythe, the Twilight Spear, the disposable spears she used, and various other weapons and magical items had been sealed.

Even so, he was confident he could defeat all three opponents standing before him, and on top of that, Elena was there.

For that reason alone, there was absolutely no way he could imagine losing this battle.

(Having my Misty Ring sealed is a blow… but remembering that they said the spatial anomaly we’ve been experiencing since the secret passage was caused by that flesh cocoon, it’s likely that it’s also the one sealing my Misty Ring. If that’s the case, then if I can just deal with that flesh cocoon…)

The seal on my Misty Ring should be broken, he thought.

Rei came to this conclusion, albeit half-intuitively.

He didn’t have any concrete proof, but he was almost certain of it—perhaps because he could feel the power emanating from that pulsating flesh cocoon.

“Misty Ring… I was a little surprised that you sealed the item box. But what are you going to do now? I can’t use my weapons anymore, but that doesn’t mean I can’t fight without them. Besides…”

Elena, who had caught Rei’s gaze as he stopped speaking, drew Mirage from the sheath at her waist.

As a linked sword capable of transforming into both a whip and a longsword, Mirage is also a symbol of Elena.

It was a unique weapon with a different kind of difficulty to handle compared to Rei’s Death Scythe… but Elena wielded Mirage with perfect mastery.

That said, while many had heard rumors and admired Rei, attempting to use a large scythe like the Death Scythe, there were none who tried to use Elena’s articulated sword… well, not exactly none, but very few.

Come to think of it, this is only natural; a large scythe isn’t that difficult to make if you set your mind to it. In fact, one could even say its construction is rather simple.

However, when it comes to the linked sword, which takes the form of both a longsword and a whip, while the longsword part is one thing, transforming it into a whip requires an extremely complex construction.

Given that, it’s difficult to even attempt making one… no, rather, there are overwhelmingly few blacksmiths who can actually make one.

Or, if they force one to be made, it ends up as junk that’s practically useless.

In any case, very few people thought, “Given the characteristics of the weapons I have, I’ll use the same one as Elena!”

...The fact that I said “almost” implies that even in such a situation, there are still those trying to use the linked sword somehow.

“I’ll admit I’m genuinely surprised that they managed to seal Rei’s weapon. But… since they can’t seal mine, I think I understand what Rei is trying to say, don’t you?”

In reality, even for Elena alone, defeating the three people near the flesh cocoon wasn’t all that difficult.

Naturally, they must have known that too, yet despite it, they didn’t show any signs of frustration or anything of the sort.

In response to Elena’s words, Celestan even smiled as he spoke.

“That’s right. As things stand now, we can’t win. But… we don’t really need to win. All we need to do is buy time until the seal is broken. …Deotres, please fulfill your contract.”

“I understand.”

As if his earlier maniacal laughter had vanished, Deotres stepped forward with a serious expression.

And it wasn’t just Deotres who stepped forward. The elderly butler also stepped forward to stand beside him.

What those two were thinking was clear enough just from the fact that they had stood in front of Rei and Elena.

It was nothing less than a resolve to buy time until the seal was broken.

“Do you really think the two of you can hold me and Elena back?”

As he said this, Rei’s thoughts turned to the weapon strapped to the belt at his waist, hidden beneath his Dragon Robe.

Nebula’s Eye, a magic item that allows him to create arrowheads for a few seconds to a few dozen seconds by channeling magical power.

Whether it was the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear, Rei generally preferred polearms, so those weapons were usually stored in his Misty Ring.

That was precisely why the only weapon Rei could currently use was Nebula’s Eye, equipped on his waist belt.

Since it is a weapon designed solely for throwing projectiles, it lacks significant power and is essentially only a tool for holding the enemy at bay.

However, even with such a weapon, when hurled using Rei’s superhuman physical abilities, it was certain to deliver a strike that, while not quite a one-hit kill, would still be quite devastating.

“Ho ho ho. If my master tells me to do it, I have no choice but to comply, given my position. Besides… I suggest you don’t underestimate it.”

The butler said this with a smile on his face.

His composed demeanor suggested he had some sort of trump card up his sleeve.

Deotres, meanwhile, held his longsword and stared at Rei and Elena, his face completely expressionless.

“Well then, shall we begin?”

It wasn’t Rei, Elena, Deotres, or the butler who spoke… it was Celestine.

Before they knew it, Celestane had quietly reached out toward the flesh cocoon nearby.

And in the next instant... two writhing blood vessels shot out from the flesh cocoon like whips.

The moment they saw this, Rei and Elena wondered if it was an attack aimed at them, but the blood vessels extending from the flesh cocoon were headed toward...

“Guh!”

A groan-like sound escaped from the butler and Deotres’ mouths.

The red blood vessel pierced the butler, and the blue one pierced Deotres.

The blood vessels embedded in the back of their necks pulsed even more violently than before.

Rei and Elena were startled by the sight for a moment, but... the next instant, Rei lunged forward.

Following Rei, Elena also took Mirage in her hand and stepped forward.

At first glance, stabbing a blood vessel into an ally looks exactly like an attack on a teammate.

However, it was hard to believe that Celestine would do such a thing in the current situation; in other words, this action must mean that there was some other reason behind it—not an attack on an ally.

In other words... to bolster their combat strength.

It was precisely because Rei had read so many manga and novels in Japan that he was able to anticipate that move.

Closing the distance, Rei activated Nebula’s Eyes and hurled arrowheads one after another, partly as a deterrent.

Launched with just a flick of his fingers, the arrows pierced the bodies of Deotres and the butler one after another… and thus, the curtain was raised on the final battle that would unfold in Anesis.
