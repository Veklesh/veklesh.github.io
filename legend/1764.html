The day after accepting the request for a mock battle—no, the day after accepting the request—Rei immediately headed to the training ground they had visited the previous day, accompanied by Vihera and Byune.

However, Set was nowhere to be seen with Rei today.

Set was supposed to spend the day playing leisurely in Marina’s backyard with Yellow.

When Rei asked what he was going to do today, Set seemed a little unsure… but since Yellow let out a cry asking to play with him, Set must have decided to play with Yellow today.

There’s no doubt that being with Rei is the most important thing to Set, but his desire to play happily with Yellow, a friend in the same situation as himself, is certainly not wrong either.

“Over there. …I wonder what kind of people are there. I hope there’s at least someone worth seeing.”

Spotting a stall selling steamed buns, Vihera muttered.

The fact that the meat buns serve as a landmark for the training ground means that, once again, the stall is set up there today—which, in turn, implies that yesterday’s sales weren’t exactly stellar.

(I did give her a few suggestions for improvement, though. …But expecting her to fix things overnight, just one day after yesterday, is probably asking too much.)

Rei had advised him to try adding ingredients with different textures or using bite-sized pieces of meat instead of just ground meat, but it’s difficult to put that advice into practice the very next day just because someone told you to.

A professional chef with years of experience could probably make those kinds of adjustments, but unfortunately, the owner of the meat bun stall doesn’t have that kind of experience.

“Hello. Um… thank you so much today.”

When the stall owner spotted Rei and the others, he stepped out from behind the stall and bowed.

Even though this request had nothing to do with Rei and his friends, he was deeply grateful that they had agreed to help.

That said, in Rei’s case, to be precise, it was for his own sake… he’d only agreed to this because he wanted to be able to casually eat meat buns at the stall.

“Um, here. Please, help yourselves. …The taste is pretty much the same as yesterday, though.”

Saying this, the shopkeeper handed one meat bun to each of them.

Vihera and Byune, having received them, immediately took a bite... but made a somewhat skeptical face.

If it’s bad, they could certainly say so outright.

If it were delicious, they could naturally and honestly say so.

This meat bun wasn’t bad, nor was it good… it had an extremely ordinary taste, making it difficult to evaluate.

The shopkeeper must have sensed their mixed feelings. As if trying to cover something up, he scratched his head and laughed.

“Hahaha. So it didn’t work out, did it? I tried changing the seasoning a little, though.”

“...That’s a different approach from what I suggested yesterday.”

What Rei had suggested yesterday was to try changing the size of the ingredients to enhance the texture...

But the stall owner chose to change the seasoning ratio instead.

That said, Rei’s opinion wasn’t exactly gospel, and the stall owner was, after all, a professional selling food—though Rei wasn’t sure if that was the right way to put it.

“Anyway, once today’s mock battle is over, it seems we’ll be able to get help from someone who really knows their way around cooking, so please bear with it a little longer until then.”

Rei ate a meat bun as well and headed toward the training ground.

Naturally, Rei could only describe the meat bun he’d eaten as “ordinary.”

In any case, when he entered the training ground, it turned out to be a fairly impressive dojo.

Of course, since this was a place for combat training in this cold weather, it wasn’t going to be outdoors or in a drafty spot.

But anyway...

“Whoa.”

That exclamation slipped out of Rei’s mouth, seemingly without thinking.

That’s because nearly thirty men and women had gathered there.

On top of that, every single one of them was staring intently at Rei and the others.

Despite being a training ground, not a single person was currently training.

They were simply staring intently at Rei and her group.

Eventually, one of them—a man who seemed to be the leader of the group gathered there today—stood up.

He was in his thirties, and while it was clear he was reasonably muscular, he wasn’t showing off pointless bulk; rather, he had a physique with lean, toned muscles that emphasized agility.

“Thank you for coming. Welcome. My name is Edwar, and I’m in charge of this training ground. I apologize for imposing on you today at Cheval’s insistence. However, all of us have been very much looking forward to experiencing Rei’s power firsthand.”

Rei, who had fully expected him to speak in a much more brusque manner, was slightly taken aback by the man’s attitude.

“Ah, don’t worry about it. I’m doing this because it benefits me in various ways, too.”

“Yes, I understand. It’s about the food stalls set up in front of the training ground, isn’t it? Besides Shuval, there are a few others who were born in the cafeteria or used to work there, so I’ll have them help out there as well.”

Rei immediately realized that “Schwar” was referring to the skinhead man from yesterday.

So, he offered his thanks for the man’s words.

“Thanks, that’s a big help.”

“Not at all. Since Rei, known as ‘Crimson,’ is going to spar with us, this is nothing. So, those two over there…”

Eduardo’s gaze shifted to Vihera and Byune, who were with Rei.

The two didn’t seem particularly bothered by his gaze; Vihera looked back at him with keen interest, while Byune, as usual, returned the gaze with her expressionless face.

“It’d take too long to spar with just me, and I figured it’d be better to keep the number of idle people to a minimum. I think a lot of you know Vihera, but I can say her strength is the real deal. As for Byune, she’s several levels below Vihera in skill, but she’s still stronger than your average adventurer.”

At Rei’s words, a few of those listening began to murmur.

Even though they knew how strong Rei and Vihera were, they probably had no idea how strong Byune was.

After all, Byune patrols Gilm alongside Vihera.

There was no doubt she possessed the ability to be permitted to do so, and those who actually knew how capable Vihera was in combat accepted this without any murmurs.

“I see. Understood. Well then… if you’re ready, I’d like to get started right away. Is that all right?”

Rei nodded at Edward’s words, and Vihera and Byune nodded as well.

Once he had confirmed this, Edward gave instructions to the group who had been looking forward to the mock battle with Rei.

There were about thirty adventurers present, and Rei’s group consisted of three.

Under normal circumstances, each group would naturally take on ten people, but the people currently present had gathered specifically to spar with Rei.

Realizing that if left to their own devices, everyone might line up in front of Rei, Edward divided them up appropriately among Rei, Vihera, and Byune.

A few looked dissatisfied, but since Rei had firmly declared that he would definitely spar with everyone, their complaints subsided.

Some even volunteered to step back, wanting to observe Rei’s techniques a little before engaging in a mock battle.

And so, the sparring matches began immediately...

“You’re first?”

Looking at Edvard standing before him, a sigh of exasperation escaped Rei’s lips.

However, Edward merely smiled at Rei’s words and replied, “Of course.”

“Being the first to fight in a situation like this is the very reason I’m here leading everyone,”

Hearing Edward say this so confidently, Rei found himself nodding in agreement.

The number of people gathered here was quite substantial.

Trying to coordinate all of them would be a huge hassle for Edvard.

Precisely because he understands this, it’s no surprise that Edward holds the privilege of fighting first in situations like this.

“As for weapons, what should we do? Well, I do have a spear with the tip blunted, but as for the large scythe that Rei is most skilled with… that’s a bit much, isn’t it?”

“I suppose so.”

Rei accepted Edwar’s hesitant words without getting angry.

To begin with, those who use a scythe are extremely rare.

Among those who had heard rumors of Rei’s exploits or had witnessed her battles firsthand, there were some who considered using a scythe just like her.

There were many such individuals, especially among those who had just become adventurers, but despite its menacing appearance, the great scythe is extremely difficult to handle.

Most people quickly give up, realizing it’s not a weapon they can use.

Precisely because of this, none of the people gathered here use a scythe… and there’s no way they could have prepared one overnight.

It was precisely because Rei understood this that he didn’t make any accusations.

“Well then, if you have a spear with a broken tip, that’ll do for now.”

As soon as Rei said that, one of the adventurers brought over a spear with a blunted tip.

Of course, just because the tip has been blunted doesn’t mean it’s completely safe.

Since it’s a normal spear aside from the blunted tip, it still packs enough power to be used as a weapon.

And besides, it’s Rei who’s going to be using it.

With that spear, he could easily kill ten or twenty monsters.

Perhaps realizing this, Edvard, too, held a longsword with a blunted blade and fixed Rei with a serious expression.

As the others around them dispersed, watching the two of them intently from a distance...

“Nooooooooooooooooooo!”

With a piercing scream, Edward closes the distance between himself and Rei in one swift motion and swings his longsword wide.

Its sharpness was greater than Rei had anticipated. Still...

“Hmph!”

It came down straight at him... and while there was no doubt he would have been crushed if he hadn’t dodged it, Rei effortlessly evaded that strike, even though the blade had actually made contact with him.

Yes, it was merely faster than Rei had anticipated.

Using the momentum from that evasion, he swung his spear with just a flick of his wrist.

If he’d used his whole body for the strike, he could have delivered a powerful blow. But in exchange for that power, the strike would have been a wide, sweeping motion.

In comparison, the strike Rei unleashed using only his wrist was overwhelmingly harder to anticipate than a standard attack.

...And yet, thanks to Rei’s physical abilities, that strike—despite being delivered with nothing but a flick of the wrist—was an extremely powerful blow.

“Huh!?”

Faced with a horizontal slash aimed at his legs, Edvard instinctively leaped out of the way...

“Guh!”

Because his jump was just a fraction too early, Rei adjusted her wrist movement again to alter the spear’s trajectory, and the blow struck him around the thigh.

“You reacted too quickly. Depending on the situation, you might get countered like this, so be careful. …Well, can you still stand? I didn’t put that much power into that strike.”

At Rei’s words, Edvard managed to get back on his feet.

Even so, his legs were trembling slightly—a sign that the blow had hit harder than he’d expected.

(Huh? It shouldn’t have been that powerful of a blow.)

That’s what Rei thought, but his definition of a “light blow” was based solely on his sparring sessions with Elena and the others.

No matter how skilled he might be, there’s no way Edvard could be as strong as Elena and the others.

That’s precisely why Rei’s strike had dealt such significant damage to Edvard.

“Ha, ha, ha… I-I never thought a single blow would affect my leg this much.”

“I aimed directly for your thigh. …In that sense, it was a safe attack.”

As Rei said this, he recalled an interview with a martial artist he’d seen on TV while in Japan.

When fighting an opponent weaker than yourself—especially one who’s just started martial arts—a low kick to the thigh is more than enough.

By kicking the thigh, you can disable your opponent without causing life-threatening injuries or leaving lasting damage.

He wondered if the words of the fighter in that interview were true.

Watching Eduardo, who had managed to get back on his feet but could barely stand as his legs trembled, Rei found himself thinking about that now, belatedly.
