Rei and the others stepped outside the shack, which looked like it could collapse at any moment, and cautiously surveyed their surroundings while considering their next move.

Rei, Vihera, and Byune had followed the alchemist they were tasked with capturing through the underground passages all the way to the slums. That much was fine, but they were now stuck, unsure how to track him further from here.

If Set were here, they could have tracked the scent like they did with Adria, but unfortunately, Set was supposed to be waiting with Ara in front of the magic item shop.

"Rei, Gilm is the city you use as your base, right? So, have you been to the slums?"

"I wouldn't say never, but I've hardly ever been here. This place is completely unfamiliar to me too."

Responding to Vihera's question, Rei turned his gaze to the other Gilm resident present... specifically, the person who had lived in Gilm even longer than he had.

"Huh? Me? I've never been to the slums at all. Besides, I hardly ever leave my house."

"...Yeah. I was an idiot asking Azimov."

Of everyone here, Rei knew best just how deeply Azimov was immersed in alchemy.

That's precisely why he asked about the spear when introduced to Pamidor.

Even if it was Pamidor's recommendation, if the person lacked the skill or didn't seem trustworthy, Rei wouldn't have made the unreasonable request to reforge the Magic Sword into a spear.

Of course, Pamidor wouldn't have made an introduction without considering that.

"So what do we do now? Seems like a lot of people are interested in us."

Vihera asked, scanning the surroundings.

Even as they spoke, figures lurking in the shadows—likely residents of the slums—were watching Rei and the others.

To the slum dwellers, Rei and his companions appeared to be tasty prey in more ways than one.

Rei, hooded and seemingly carrying nothing of value due to the Dragon Robe's influence, appeared particularly unremarkable. Yet his small stature suggested he was likely not yet an adult, and those seeking such individuals as slaves were by no means few.

Asimov, being male and seemingly carrying nothing of particular value, was likely to be killed before anyone made a fuss.

But the other three women were a different story altogether.

Even Byune, the youngest of the three, possessed a face beautiful enough to be called attractive, despite being a child.

Those who preferred such a girl were likely more numerous than those who preferred Rei.

And above all, Elena and Vihera could enjoy her themselves, while simultaneously selling her as a slave would undoubtedly fetch an extremely high price.

While they were keeping the others at bay, acting as if the best prey had arrived for them, Elena's words set the situation in motion.

"Well, it seems we won't need to get lost on our way to where we're going?"

As Elena murmured while gazing up at the sunset sky, Rei and the others also lifted their eyes upward.

There, reflected in the sky, was something black floating against the crimson sunset.

Everyone except Azimov knew what it was.

"I see. It's Yellow."

"That's right. Thanks to it, we can easily find out where that woman escaped to earlier."

Normally, humans would be the ones tracking down fugitives.

In more unusual cases, one might employ beasts with keen senses of smell. But in a twilight cityscape where countless scents mingled, even a somewhat sharp sense of smell proved useless.

In such conditions, tracking by flying through the air like Yellow proved an exceptionally effective method.

In this world, beings capable of flight were not particularly numerous.

Well, many monster species can fly, but controlling them is something only extremely rare beings like tamers or summoners can do.

Adriana knew full well of Set's existence, but a giant like him flying through the air was impossible to miss. Even if Adriana hadn't noticed, some passerby surely would have.

After all, Set was so famous in Gilm that almost everyone knew him.

Compared to such a prominent figure as Set, Yellow had the advantage of being smaller and thus harder to spot.

"Kyu! Kyu-kyu-kyu! Kyuu!"

Yellow descended to the ground, caught by Elena, who held him close as he let out a cry.

The sight of a tiny dragon like Yellow chirping was incredibly endearing, stirring a protective urge... but such feelings seemed irrelevant to the slum dwellers trying to earn food money to survive today and tomorrow. Soon, several people emerged from behind the buildings.

Until just moments ago, they had been warily watching each other, but the appearance of Yellow, a baby dragon, changed everything.

Even the slum dwellers knew how rare and, consequently, how valuable dragons were as raw materials.

They silently agreed to cooperate in the attack and split the profits evenly. The thought of fighting amongst themselves and letting Yellow, Elena, or Vihera escape was unthinkable.

...Moreover, it wasn't just the slum dwellers who were surprised by Yellow's presence and watching him closely; Azimov, one of Rei's companions, was equally focused.

No, for Azimov, a skilled alchemist, dragon materials were probably something he'd kill for. His focus on Yellow was even more intense than that of the slum dwellers.

"Kyuu!? Kyukyuu! Kyuu!"

Perhaps frightened by Azimov's gaze, Yellow clung desperately to Elena.

"...Azimov, I'm sorry, but could you please not look at Yellow like that?"

"Huh!? Eh, ah, ah. Sorry."

Elena's words seemed to snap Azimov back to his senses. He hurriedly apologized.

"No, it's fine as long as you understand. I'm well aware of what Yellow means to an alchemist. ...Now then, Yellow. I'm sorry, but show me the memories you saw."

Saying this, Elena lifted Yellow, who was clinging to her, with both hands and brought their heads together.

Elena's action seemed to be the trigger. The slum dwellers surrounding Rei and the others began moving in unison without a word.

"Well then, shall we deal with these people?"

Rei and Byune nodded at Vihera's words, while Azimov moved to Elena's side.

As an alchemist, Azimov's combat prowess was never particularly high.

Well, some alchemists are reasonably skilled in combat, but Azimov was an alchemist specialized in research. In terms of combat strength, he was nowhere near as powerful as the term "magician" might suggest.

Of course, using magic items crafted with alchemy, he could defend himself to some extent.

However, the reason Azimov was traveling with Rei and the others in the first place was solely to investigate the origins of the magic items... specifically, who had created them.

Therefore, he had brought only the bare minimum of defensive magic items.

Still, the fact he wasn't completely empty-handed showed he was living properly in Gilm.

(Though the look in his eyes when he gazed at Yellow felt slightly dangerous...)

Vihera, waiting barehanded for the slum dwellers as usual, suddenly thought this.

Perhaps he might... well, more accurately, perhaps Yellow might start causing trouble for Elena.

The time he spent with Azimov was extremely brief. No matter how much Rei trusted Azimov, that alone wasn't enough to grant him unconditional trust.

She was Elena's romantic rival, a fellow countrywoman, a friend—their relationship was complex in many ways.

Even so... no, precisely because of that, if there was someone who might cause trouble for Elena, he couldn't just stand by and do nothing.

"Byune, please escort Elena."

"Mm."

Byune, too, felt a certain affection for Elena, though not as strong as Vihera's.

That's precisely why he vaguely understood what Vihera was worried about, and he had no objection to keeping an eye on Azimov to prevent him from getting any strange ideas.

If the attacking enemy had been stronger, there wouldn't have been such leeway, but this time the opponent was a slum dweller.

Even if they were accustomed to attacking people, these were adventurers whose profession was combat... specifically, Rei and Vihera, whose combat prowess was incomparable to that of ordinary adventurers. That meant he could watch the fight with peace of mind.

"WAAAAAAAAAH!"

Screaming, the slum dweller lunged first at Rei.

Vihera, at least, wore gauntlets and greaves. Seeing Rei, seemingly unarmed and small, the slum dweller likely intended to capture him as a hostage.

Judging solely by Rei's appearance without Set, it wasn't unreasonable to think he'd be an easy target.

Even in the slums, this was Gilm.

Many likely knew who Rei was, but with his hood up, his identity was impossible to confirm by appearance alone.

That was the greatest misfortune for the slum dwellers.

"No need for weapons."

Rei muttered only that to the slum dweller lunging at him before charging forward in one swift motion.

Such attacks weren't uncommon, and the movements of those attacking Rei were extremely practiced.

But... even so, their movements were incomparably slower than the monsters Rei had fought before. Even considering them as fellow humans, their agility was only on par with, or slightly above, that of a low-rank adventurer.

Casually closing the distance to such an opponent, Rei slipped inside the range of the man swinging his club overhead—as if to capture him—and thrust his fist forward before the club could come down.

With Rei's strength, a genuine punch could crush flesh and shatter bone, inflicting severe injury.

But Rei felt no need to go that far right now.

If this were a common thief, he could capture him, force him to reveal his hideout, and seize the hoarded treasure. But the man before him was a slum dweller... it was highly unlikely he possessed any treasure worth mentioning.

Since they had attacked important figures like Vihera and Elena, turning them over to the guards might result in them being sold as slaves. But considering the hassle of the paperwork, it would be more trouble than it was worth.

Besides, they were the ones who had invaded the slums.

That's precisely why Rei's fist, when it swung, grazed the jaw of the club-wielding man, taking only his consciousness, not his life.

Through that sequence of movements, Rei likely judged that he was not someone to be underestimated, despite his appearance. Several men attacked simultaneously, but...

"Useless."

One after another, the slum dwellers lost consciousness under Rei's relentless barrage of punches and kicks.

Those who charged at Vihera met the same fate. Those who had hoped to taste the exquisite body beneath her thin clothing found themselves with broken limbs, collapsing to the ground without having done anything.

The reason she didn't just knock them unconscious like Rei did, but went so far as to break their bones, was likely because she had no intention of letting those who targeted her body as a woman off scot-free.

If they had been strong enough, she might have considered them worthy opponents to fight. But those present now were beings incapable of understanding Vihera's power, or even grasping the vast difference in strength between themselves and her.

And so, in less than a minute, nearly twenty slum dwellers all lost consciousness and fell to the ground.

"Hmm, perfectly timed."

Elena murmured, having been peering into Yellow's memories.

"Well? What about the woman who escaped?"

"No problem. She's in a hut a short distance from here. However... it seems she has several accomplices."

In Yellow's memory, someone had come to pick up Adria.

A man whose beard connected his chin and sideburns, his entire face covered in facial hair.

Even to Elena's eyes, his movements suggested considerable strength, making her wonder why such a person would be in the slums.

But he quickly recalled that not all powerful individuals remained in the open world.

In fact, those who sank into the underworld precisely because of their strength were too numerous to count.

"I see. So while cornering him might be possible, capturing him outright seems difficult."

Rei, having somewhat grasped the situation from Elena's demeanor, murmured to himself.
