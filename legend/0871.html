The sun had completely set, and the moon was hidden behind clouds, leaving only a faint light in the sky.

At that moment, a single shadow flapped its wings and flew through that night sky.

Needless to say, it was Rei riding Set.

Having conquered the dungeon, Rei was heading for Gilm.

Ranga, who had been waiting to deal with any monsters emerging from the dungeon, suggested that tomorrow morning would be fine... but Rei, wanting to rest at an inn if possible, decided to push on and return to Gilm.

The dungeon itself wasn't located far from Gilm to begin with.

That's precisely why, at Set's speed, they would arrive in Gilm in no time at all.

"Grrrrrr"

Set growled happily as the lights of Gilm came into view.

While not many places used light magic items, it was still past five in the afternoon, and perhaps because many were still awake, the number of lights was overwhelmingly greater than on a summer night.

Beyond magic items, many were likely building bonfires to ward off the cold.

The lights were clearly visible to both Set and Rei, and they headed straight towards them.

As the main gate of Gilm came into view, Set flapped his wings and descended toward the ground.

On the ground, the guards stiffened momentarily at the sight of a shadow descending from the sky. But recognizing it as Set, they exhaled in relief and relaxed their vigilance.

The darkness of night made it difficult to distinguish Set.

Set landed on the ground and began walking toward Gilm.

Rei dismounted from Set's back, and the two of them soon arrived at the main gate of Gilm.

"Rei and Set, huh? Try not to startle us so much. It gets dark early enough as it is this time of year."

One of the guards addressed Rei.

Near the main gate, perhaps for vigilance, numerous magic items for light and bonfires were present, so darkness wasn't an issue.

"We rushed over, but it's still okay, right?"

The guard who had seen them out of Gilm had likely already finished his shift. Rei addressed another guard.

That guard, likely recognizing Rei, immediately nodded and began the entry procedures into Gilm.

The other guards, too, were petting and doting on Set, offering him dried meat and sandwiches.

"Gurururu!"

Some even gave Set warm soup, and his happy sounds echoed around them.

"It must be tough out here in the cold."

"What? This is just part of the job. Besides, monsters that only appear this time of year show up. If one of those monsters attacks, you won't be complaining about the cold."

The guard smiled as he quickly checked Rei's guild card and processed their entry into the town.

"Besides, it's not that cold here, is it? Haven't you noticed?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. Now that you mention it, you're right."

Rei hadn't noticed, but a magic item that emitted heat was placed beside the gate.

It wasn't exactly warm, though; its effect was merely to slightly lessen the chill.

His Dragon Robe functioned like air conditioning, warming him when it was cold and cooling him when it was hot, so Rei probably hadn't noticed the warmth.

"Well, we'll close the gate soon and warm ourselves properly inside Gilm. Nothing beats a hot wine on a night like this."

While they exchanged these words, the formalities proceeded, and Rei and Set entered Gilm.

...What struck Rei was how the guards seemed disappointed they couldn't pet Set a little longer.

Of course, it wasn't just because they wanted to show affection for Set.

Many likely simply found Set's warmth comforting and wished to touch him for as long as possible.

Understanding this, Rei felt a twinge of guilt as he left the scene.

Once inside Gilm, their first destination was the Adventurers' Guild.

Rei proceeded, thinking it only proper to report the dungeon's conquest to the guild first. Even though it was already dark, it was still only a little past five in the afternoon.

Naturally, the streets were bustling with people, and the food stalls were busier than ever, capitalizing on the crowd.

With Set walking among them, it was only natural he would attract attention.

Many residents relaxed at the sight of Set, offering him bread, skewered meats, and other treats.

The stall owners, seemingly aware that customers would increase if they saw Set eating their food, sold items to Rei at a discount, and many even gave Set food for free.

Receiving enough food to last not just dinner but likely tomorrow's meal for an ordinary person, Rei and Set arrive in front of the guild.

"Set, here, eat this and wait a bit, okay?"

"Gurrr!"

Laying the food they'd been given on the way before Set, who was sprawled out as usual in the carriage and familiar space, Rei entered the guild.

Watching him go, Set savored the sandwich before him... purring contentedly as he stroked the bodies and heads of the people who had gathered around him to play, enjoying the pleasant sensation.

"Set-chan, feeling good?"

"Ah, yes. Here, have this too, Set-chan."

"Oh, Set. There sure are a lot of people here today."

Hearing voices call out to him like that, Set felt happiness wash over him.

...It was a shame that Rei, whom Set adored, wasn't here.

What Rei heard upon entering the guild was a commotion—or rather, a racket.

However, the noise was coming from the tavern attached to the guild.

It being evening, the adventurers were all over the place—some eating, some celebrating successful quests, others reflecting on failed ones—and the noise was deafening.

The guild itself also had more people than when Rei had come earlier that morning.

Those who had completed today's quests had likely returned.

Even so, this season, with winter approaching, the number of active adventurers wasn't particularly high.

Most adventurers have already finished preparing to see out the winter.

Those still taking on quests now were either not yet ready for winter or, like Rei, were acting for reasons beyond the reward.

Especially this year, with fewer Gamelions around, many were out hunting for Gamelions' meat.

"Hey, how'd it go over there?"

"No luck. Not a single Gamelion in sight. Damn it, what's going on? There's never this few Gamelions around at this time of year."

"Yeah, right. Plus, the Gamelion season is almost over. If we're unlucky, they might just head back to the Demon Forest like this?"

"...So what are you saying? Are you saying we have to go all the way to the Demon Forest to get Gamelions? You've got to be kidding me. Sure, I like Gamelion meat, but it's not worth risking my life for."

"Yeah, I guess. ...Even if we did go Gamelion hunting, there's almost nothing to gain from it, so the pressure from the party would be intense."

"Ah, I get it, I get it. I'm in a similar boat. ...So, what do we do?"

"Gamelion? Hmm, let's see. It's been five days already. If we still haven't found one after five more days, we'll give up. ...Honestly, I'd like to secure some Gamelion meat, but pushing any further is just too much."

"Yeah, I get it. Same here. ...At least we can take comfort in the fact that winter preparations are already done. As for Gamelion, it's almost like a hobby at this point."

"Huh? Really? How envious..."

"...Hey, you... you weren't chasing after Gamelion even though you hadn't finished your winter preparations, were you?"

"No, but Gamelion fetches a high price."

"You, man..."

Ignoring the conversation among these so-called Gamelion hunters, Rei headed toward the counter.

Lenora, who was organizing papers at the counter, noticed the figure approaching her.

Kenny, who was also sorting papers beside Lenora, noticed the figure just as she did.

"Ah, Rei. Oh? Weren't you supposed to be in the dungeon?"

"Welcome back, Rei. Silly Lenora. Of course Rei would be back from conquering the dungeon."

Kenny was as biased toward Rei as ever, but this time, his words were spot on.

Rei stepped in front of Lenora, who could be called his partner, and pulled out the dungeon's core, split clean in two by Misty Ring.

At first, both Lenora and Kenny tilted their heads, unsure what Rei had retrieved.

And no wonder. A dungeon core isn't something you get to see every day.

That's precisely why Lenora asked Rei with a puzzled expression.

"Rei, what is this?"

"It's the dungeon's core."

『……』

Needless to say, Rei is a famous figure in Gilm.

Given that, a fair number of people were listening intently to the conversation between Rei, Lenora, and Kenny.

Those people gasped at the words coming from Rei's mouth.

It was only natural. Possessing the dungeon's core meant nothing less than having conquered the dungeon itself.

Some knew Rei had left Gilm earlier that day, and they realized he had conquered the dungeon in less than a day... no, in just a few hours to half a day.

"...Huh?"

Lenora and Kenny were likely just as stunned.

This morning, Rei had heard information about the dungeon from Lenora, and then headed for the dungeon.

Yet to hear that he'd already conquered it? Unless Rei himself said it, neither Lenora nor Kenny—who favored Rei—could believe it. They'd have dismissed it as a joke.

But considering what Rei had accomplished so far, this was no joke.

"Um... Rei, did you really clear the dungeon?"

Caught off guard, Lenora fell silent, so Kenny asked instead.

Most of the people at the guild were focused on what Rei would say, and both the adventurers and the guild staff had fallen silent.

Even in this tense atmosphere, shouts could be heard coming from the tavern. It was no wonder anyone witnessing this scene felt something was off.

"Yeah. As you know, that dungeon was brand new and pretty small. Even I didn't find it that hard to clear."

Hearing those words, most adventurers inwardly retorted, 'What do you mean, "even me"?!'

Though she hadn't heard their silent retorts, Lenora snapped back to reality and spoke up.

"It's true we heard the monsters in the dungeon weren't that strong... but still, to accomplish that in such a short time..."

"Well, never mind that. I have something to explain—or rather, report—about the dungeon."

"...What is it?"

"In the room containing the dungeon's core, there were a large number of Gamelions. Probably over fifty. I think that's likely the reason why the Gamelions were so extremely scarce this year."

The adventurers murmured the moment they heard Rei's words.

And no wonder. There are quite a few adventurers hunting Gamelion.

Even among those currently at the guild, there were over ten such individuals.

"Gamelion? ...I see. Because of the dungeon's core, then?"

"Probably. So, I'm thinking of distributing some to the guild..."

"That would be a huge help, but are you sure? Didn't you look forward to Gamelion meat too, Rei? And you, Set-chan?"

"Yeah. Ranga asked me too, and having fewer Gamelions isn't good for the Gilm either. ...But I have a request too. I'll sell you ten Gamelions. In exchange for the payment for some of them, I'd like you to skin my share of the Gamelions... Is that possible?"

Lenora considered Rei's words for a moment.

But seemingly unable to decide alone, she turned her gaze toward her superior behind the counter.

The boss, meeting her gaze, nodded without hesitation.

This was partly influenced by the fact that the boss enjoyed Gamelion dishes, but more significantly, it was judged to be in the guild's best interest.

In truth, Gamelion was fairly rare in the current Gilm, and its purchase price had risen significantly compared to previous years.

If they could save on those costs, there was no hesitation in gathering guild staff skilled in butchering to strip the Gamelion.

"Understood, Rei. I'll accept your proposal then."

Lenora, having gained her superior's approval, turned to Rei and announced it with a smile.

...While the Gamelion shortage was part of it, the main reason for that smile was likely because she'd been utterly captivated by Gamelion cuisine.

Incidentally, the letter containing the report entrusted to her by Ranga would also be delivered to the lord's mansion via the guild.
