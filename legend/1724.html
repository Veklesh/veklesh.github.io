He attacks the Garangiga living in the underground lake from the air and drags it up to the surface.

Entrusted with this fundamental task—the very prerequisite for fighting a boss monster—Set was flying through the sky with Rei on his back.

Originally, Set had planned to go alone, but carrying Rei made almost no difference to his flying speed or agility, and having Rei’s hands meant they could block or intercept any attacks from the Garangiga. …And above all, the fact that Set felt most emotionally stable when he was with Rei was the biggest deciding factor in this situation.

“Set, it seems the Garangiga’s only long-range attack is spitting venom, but that’s only true for ordinary Garangiga. I can’t possibly believe that Garangiga is the same as the others, so be careful.”

“Grrrr!”

As Rei spoke, stroking his neck, Set growled in response, as if to say, “Leave it to me!”

Watching Set like that, Rei looks once more at the underground lake ahead—the place they were about to head toward.

The underground lake is the Garangiga’s boss room, and the area above it is also classified as a boss room.

That was, strictly speaking, only Rei and the others’ assumption.

But based on the atmosphere surrounding this underground lake, Rei had a hunch that their assumption was likely correct.

In other words, if they plunged straight into the airspace above the underground lake, there was a very high chance Garangiga would attack.

(Still… no matter how you look at it, that’s cheating, isn’t it?)

Even from their current position, they could make out the silhouette of Garangiga—or perhaps it should be called a serpentine shadow—swimming freely through the underground lake.

Their size was completely different from the Garangiga Marina had described.

You could literally say it was in a completely different league.

(Normally, rare or high-tier species are one rank higher… but this doesn’t look like that at all.)

According to what Rei heard from Marina, the Galangiga is a Rank C monster.

In other words, according to the classification, the Garangiga currently swimming leisurely in the underground lake should be treated as a Rank B monster.

But looking at the Garangiga right in front of me, there’s no way anyone would recognize it as a Rank B monster.

(Maybe I should just try throwing a spear at it from here?)

That way, I could attack it one-sidedly from a safe spot.

He wasn’t entirely opposed to the idea, but Marina had asked him not to do it if possible.

She said that precisely because she knows just how powerful Rei’s spear throw is.

Her reasoning was that if he were attacked and the enemy was within his range of movement—and not in the boss room—there was no telling how the Garangiga would react.

Mind you, when she told him that, Rei thought attacking from above the underground lake might be much the same thing… but in any case, thanks to Rei and Set’s actions, preparations for an intercept were already in place.

Given that, it was only natural to Rei that he couldn’t possibly change his attack strategy on a whim right here.

“Well then… shall we go?”

At Rei’s words, Set growled and finally plunged into the air above the subterranean lake.

And the very next moment after they entered the airspace above the subterranean lake, the movements of the Garangiga swimming within the lake clearly changed.

It hadn’t attacked Rei and the others yet.

However, it had clearly stopped moving and turned its face toward where Rei and the others were.

It was likely because the underground lake was so clear, with moss glowing like illumination, that they could see this even from above.

(There’s no doubt it’s focusing its attention on us, but it doesn’t seem to be attacking. …I suppose this is just a state of alert for the Garangiga?)

Holding the Twilight Spear, Rei focused his attention on the Garangiga, which was watching him from above in the underground lake.

It wasn’t necessarily a reaction to Rei’s attitude, but the Garangiga deftly twisted its body as it swam through the water, moving directly beneath Set.

Clearly, it was a move aimed at them.

Realizing this, Rei channels magic into the Twilight Spear to gain the upper hand.

What came as a surprise to Rei was that the moment he channeled magic into the Twilight Spear, Garangiga’s movements changed.

It was clearly reacting to Rei’s magic… or, more precisely, to the very act of Rei channeling magic into the Twilight Spear.

Then, the Garangiga, which had been underwater, rose rapidly to the surface with the force of a leap, breaking through the water to reveal itself.

Its massive head—though he had realized as much from a distance—was clearly larger than Rei had anticipated. …To be precise, it was Garangiga’s sheer presence that made it appear so enormous.

“Take this and die!”

“Grrrrrrrr!”

Just as Rei hurled the Twilight Spear, infused with magic, Set let out a high-pitched cry, summoning twenty wind arrows that were fired all at once.

Having thrown the Twilight Spear, Rei thought that he would at least be able to inflict some damage on Garangiga... and if things went well, perhaps even pierce its body and deal a near-fatal blow.

But… Garangiga twisted its body swiftly in midair.

The Twilight Spear pierced the spot where Garangiga’s body had been just a moment before, but in the end, it pierced not the body but empty space.

Admittedly, the strike from the Twilight Spear, imbued with magic and strengthened, did not deal a fatal blow to Garangiga, but it did succeed in slashing the scales on its body where the spear had passed.

...It was as if a strike meant to pierce the opponent’s body had instead merely left a shallow cut on the skin.

Naturally, it did not inflict a wound that could be called a fatal blow to Garangiga.

And the twenty wind arrows Set fired...

“SHAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!”

As Garangiga let out a sharp scream, a third eye appeared on his forehead—a spot that hadn’t existed a moment before—and the instant that eye opened, all the wind arrows were shattered.

The wind arrows themselves aren’t particularly powerful.

However, due to their speed and near-transparent appearance, they are extremely difficult to intercept.

Despite that, Garangiga simply opened his third eye and obliterated them all at once.

No, the wind arrows weren’t the only ones hit by that third eye’s attack.

The attack from the third eye also reached Set, who was in the air firing the wind arrows... and Rei, who was straddling Set’s back.

“Guruu!?”

Fortunately, perhaps because its power had been significantly reduced by destroying all the wind arrows, by the time the attack reached them, it no longer possessed enough power to inflict major damage on Set or Rei.

Even so, enough power remained to throw Set, who was flying in the sky, off balance, and he flapped his wings to regain his footing.

Rei used the special ability of the Twilight Spear to pull it back to her hand and swap it with the Death Scythe that had been inside the Misty Ring.

The fact that he was able to pull off such a maneuver while Set was struggling to regain his balance in midair was likely due in part to it being a largely instinctive reaction.

“Set, you’ve done your part. To shore!”

“Grr!”

Having somehow regained his balance, Set quickly nodded at Rei’s words, flapped his wings, and headed for the shore.

Then, a liquid of some sort pierced the spot where Set had been just a moment ago.

Watching the shore rapidly approach, Rei, overcome by a bad feeling, looked up... at the ceiling.

The ceiling of the cave Rei saw was clearly beginning to melt... and there were even traces of it having been sliced open.

(He said he’d spit venom, but can he really compress it this much to fire it!? Isn’t that a water cutter!?)

It was sliced open by water pressure, and yet, if you touched the splashing droplets, they would corrode you.

That attack was no longer simply spitting venom; it was something that should be called a venom-infused water breath—or perhaps a water cutter.

There was no way to counter such a vicious attack—the only option was to dodge it.

Moreover, if you tried to dodge it at the last possible moment—the kind of close-call evasion Rei excels at—you’d still take damage from the toxic spray.

It’s a strike that requires evading with ample… indeed, almost exaggerated… room to spare.

「Set！」

“Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrr!”

At Rei’s call, Set lets out a sharp growl and flies toward the shore.

Trying to support Set, Rei unleashes a slash toward the rear.

(Damn, maybe I should’ve just thrown the spear instead!?)

Even as he thought that, there was a split-second delay when retrieving an item from the Misty Ring.

Against ordinary enemies, this wouldn’t be a problem at all, but when facing a monster like the Garangiga currently chasing them, even a split second could be a major opening—and there was a high chance it could prove fatal.

The only consolation for Rei was that the Garangiga was pursuing Set exactly as planned.

The Flying Slash he unleashed had seen its power skyrocket upon reaching Level 5, but... against the Garangiga, it hadn’t even managed to chip a single scale.

Still, the attack executed just as the Garangiga was about to strike contributed to Set’s ability to fly away unscathed.

(That impact that blew Set’s Wind Arrow away at the start… if that’s the same type of attack as Set’s Shock Magic Eye, it’s going to be a real pain.)

Set’s skill, “Shock Magic Eye,” is an extremely useful ability that deals shock damage to the opponent the moment it’s activated.

However, in exchange for that convenience, the actual damage dealt to the opponent isn’t all that significant.

Compared to Set’s “Shock Magic Eye,” the similar attack launched by Garangiga not only wiped out all twenty Wind Arrows with a shockwave but also inflicted some degree of aftershock on Rei and the others.

Even though it is a skill of the same type as Set’s “Shock Magic Eye,” its power is vastly different.

That said, right now, the eye on Garangiga’s forehead is closed, and even if someone told him there was an eye there, he wouldn’t know it.

It’s a skill that would be extremely troublesome if fired repeatedly in the current situation, but since he isn’t doing so, there must be some reason for it—that’s what Rei thinks, though it’s a somewhat optimistic assumption.

And thanks to Set’s flight speed, they reached the shore in the blink of an eye.

However, there was no sign of Elena and the others right by the shore.

Since they’re luring the Galangiga onto land to fight, it’s only natural they’d try to engage it as far away from the underground lake as possible.

“Set, speed up!”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set spreads his wings wide and slows down.

Normally, slowing down while being chased by a Garangiga would be nothing short of suicide.

He slowed down knowing full well that, at this speed, there was a chance Set would crash into the narrowing walls or ceiling of the dungeon before they reached the far end of the shore—where Elena and the others were waiting.

And so, Set lands at the farthest point of the shore, the spot furthest from the underground lake.

Since he had spread his wings and slowed down considerably, he touched down on the ground and completely stopped his momentum with just a few steps.

Using his lion’s powerful legs, he immediately spun around.

At that moment, Rei also leaped down from Set’s back, drew the Twilight Spear from her Misty Ring, and assumed her usual dual-spear stance.

Just as expected, Garangiga emerged from the subterranean lake and was heading straight toward Rei and the others.

It moves across the ground, writhing its body with a speed that belies its massive size.

“Damn, its speed doesn’t change much even after coming up to the surface, does it?”

Facing the approaching Garangiga, Relieu says dismissively.

Honestly, since it usually lives underwater, she probably had hoped its movements would be slower on land.

“It’s pointless to expect that. …Anyway, I’m attacking!”

With that, Marina unleashes the wind blade she had prepared while Rei and the others were luring the Garangiga.

But she didn’t stop there; she nocked arrow after arrow onto her bow and fired.

Elena also conjured a wind blade with her magic and released it.

While they were both wind blades, there was a significant difference between spirit magic and ordinary magic.

Although Elena’s wind blades were fewer in number compared to Marina’s, each one was larger.

Set fires another wind arrow, Rei unleashes a flying slash, and, not to be outdone, Relieu fires a wind slash.

The barrage of attacks is fired straight at Garangiga...

“Duck!”

Seeing Garangiga open its mouth as it closed in on Rei and the others, Rei recalled the scene from earlier and shouted instinctively.

The fact that everyone reacted instantly to Rei’s words was a testament to the fact that they were, after all, a group of elite fighters.

...Although Byune was being held and dragged along by Vihera.

And just then, a water cutter filled with venom sliced through the spot where Rei and the others had been standing just a moment ago.
