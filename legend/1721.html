It was about twenty minutes after Byune had disarmed the string traps set up inside the cave that Rei mentioned a spell called “Thin Flame,” which was useful for disarming traps.

This was because Byune, having disarmed several traps, was beginning to look a little tired.

Naturally, this wasn’t physical fatigue, but rather mental exhaustion.

Disarming traps naturally requires a great deal of concentration and is mentally exhausting.

That was precisely why Rei had asked Byune if she wanted him to take over using magic...

“Hmm!”

Surprisingly, it was Byune herself who was the first to reject Rei’s offer.

“What does that mean?” Rei asked Vihera with a look.

Seeing Rei’s expression, Vihera smiled and spoke up.

“You’ve probably heard the saying that a single battle is worth months of training, right? Though, of course, the exact number of days varies greatly from person to person.”

“I suppose so. …So, what Byune is doing is essentially the same thing?”

“That’s about right. As a thief, Byune possesses considerable combat prowess. However, when it comes to the essential skills of a thief, she’s not exactly bad, but she’s not particularly good either. …You could say she’s average.”

From an outsider’s perspective, Byune is probably what you’d call a combat-specialized thief right now.

That’s not necessarily a bad thing, and there are plenty of other thieves with a similar style.

But Byune doesn’t think of herself as a thief specialized in combat.

No, she knows that’s how she’s perceived, and she understands that it can’t be helped under the current circumstances, but even so, Byune doesn’t consider herself specialized solely in combat… She wants to be an ordinary thief who possesses proper skills as a thief.

For someone like Byune, the experience of discovering and disarming traps in a place like this is an asset beyond compare.

That is precisely why she refused Rei’s suggestion to use magic to disarm the trap all at once.

“What should we do? Since Rei is the party leader, you decide.”

“Even if you say that…”

Told by Marina to decide what to do, Rei looked at Byune, who was staring at him expressionlessly... and eventually nodded.

“Alright. It’s not like we have to rush through this dungeon. I’ll leave it to Byune for a while. But only for a while, mind you.”

Setting Rei’s personal feelings aside, when considering the best interests of the entire “Crimson Wings” party, there was no downside to Byune improving her skills as a thief.

...That said, from the perspective of Relieu, who was hoping to clear the dungeon as quickly as possible so he could return to his wife in Gilm, there seemed to be a slight hint of dissatisfaction.

Still, since he was merely here as a helper this time, he didn’t object to Rei’s words.

(Shumine… I’m sorry, it looks like I’ll be late coming home. Still, I’ll make sure to be back before winter sets in.)

Relieu sighed, but when would he realize that wasting an hour or two here today was nothing more than a minor setback?

Given that Relieu tends to lose all sense of perspective when it comes to his wife, it was more likely he wouldn’t realize it until the very end.

“Hmm!”

As for Byune, the one who mattered most, she seemed pleased by Rei’s words, her lips curving into a faint smile. As usual, she uttered just a single word before immediately beginning to scan the surroundings for any potential traps.

The others, having decided to go along with Byune’s plan, had no complaints and quietly followed her as she moved forward, carefully surveying their surroundings.

They continued on like that for about an hour.

Along the way, they disarmed several traps, and Rei and the others were still inside the cave.

Since it was nearly Gamelion season, Rei took a skewer of grilled Gamelion out of her Misty Ring and began to eat.

Naturally, seeing Rei eat the grilled Gamelion, Set growled with hunger, wanting some too, and the others took an interest as well.

“Since this is last year’s Gamelion, it’s really something, isn’t it?”

Although the skewers were seasoned with nothing but salt—an extremely simple preparation—that very simplicity brought out the flavor of the Gamelion meat, making them incredibly delicious.

The fat of the meat and the salt.

When these two combine in your mouth, one bite isn’t enough—you want another. Still not enough—another bite. Still not enough—yet another bite.

Before you know it, all the meat on the skewer has vanished, leaving only the skewer behind.

“An item box, huh? I’m jealous. With that, food doesn’t go bad, right? And considering all the meals we’ve had so far, it’s only natural that everyone wants one.”

Muttering this, Relieu also eats some of Gamelion’s skewered meat.

For Relieu, who works solo, the Item Box must be a highly coveted item.

Rei looked at Relieu with a puzzled expression.

“Relieu, you’re a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname. You can afford to buy a magic bag or a magic pouch, right?”

Magic items—which could be considered simplified mass-produced versions of item boxes—are extremely expensive, but for an adventurer with a nickname, buying one isn’t difficult.

After all, having a nickname means possessing the ability to back it up.

However, in response to Rei’s words, Relieu shakes his head.

“I’ve thought about it a bit myself, but time still passes even when things are stored inside. Even if I put freshly made food that Shumine made for me in there, like Rei does, it’ll get cold right away. That’s just too wasteful. I want to eat the food Shumine went to the trouble of making while it’s still delicious.”

“Is that your only criterion?”

Rei couldn’t help but retort, but no one could really blame him for that.

“You don’t have to stick to hot food—wouldn’t something like a sandwich work?”

“I thought about that too. I did, but… I’d just get sick of eating nothing but sandwiches.”

“Huh. You’d get bored of food made by your beloved wife? So that’s the extent of your love, Relieu.”

“Huh!?”

At Vihera’s words, Relieu froze.

“My… my love is that shallow? No, that can’t be. I love Shumine more than anyone in the world. So… anything Shumine cooks should taste delicious to me. There’s no doubt about that.”

“Then it doesn’t really matter if it’s not in the item box, does it? Sandwiches might take up some space, but they’re not that heavy.”

“No, but… if I’m going to eat Shumine’s cooking, isn’t it only natural that I’d want to have something like hot soup too?”

"There’s nothing we can do about that. If you absolutely… really, absolutely have to eat hot food, then isn’t that exactly what Shumine would do? You’d have to turn Relieu’s wife into an adventurer and form a party with her, wouldn’t you?"

“There’s no way I can do something like that.”

Without even a moment’s hesitation, Relieu immediately dismissed Vihera’s suggestion.

Of course. Shumine, Relieu’s wife, wasn’t a former adventurer—she was just an ordinary resident of Gilm.

…Though, from the perspective of people in other towns and villages, simply being a resident of Gilm meant they were treated as something special in a way.

After all, Gilm was located in the absolute farthest reaches of the frontier, and for those unfamiliar with it, it wasn’t uncommon for its residents to be perceived as peculiar simply for living there.

But in reality, even if you live in Gilm, you’re just an ordinary person, no different from those living in other towns.

Perhaps because Gilm is so remote, it possessed the open-mindedness to readily accept someone like Set.

“Well then, just give it up. You’re happy enough with just the sandwich, aren’t you?”

“I won’t deny that. …Huh?”

Relieu had been talking with Vihera, but when he suddenly glanced over at Byune, he exclaimed in surprise.

“Huh… Look at that. That thief of yours is even disarming traps like that.”

Where Relieu’s gaze fell, Byune was disarming a trap where sharp blades—longswords so long they seemed to lack handles—were falling from above, and was gathering those blades.

It goes without saying where the collected blades were headed.

“Hmm!”

Being careful not to cut her hand on the blade, Byune handed the collected blade to Rei.

Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered; he took the blade and sheathed it in his Misty Ring.

Since these were blades Byune had obtained by disarming the trap herself, they belonged to her.

It was a brief exchange, but that was the underlying assumption.

……That said, Rei was still a little curious about how Byune would use that blade.

Byune’s fighting style fundamentally prioritizes speed.

He uses daggers and long needles, and although he doesn’t use it now, he used to use a crossbow as well.

Given that, there are others besides Rei who wonder how she intends to use a blade that is essentially the blade of a longsword itself.

“Hey, Byune. How do you plan to use that blade? If you don’t mind, could you tell me?”

“Hmm… hmm!”

Byune replied to Vihera immediately, but Rei, who had been watching the exchange, couldn’t make out what Byune was saying.

However, Vihera, as if it were obvious, understood the gist of the conversation.

“I heard she’s planning to sell it once we get back to Gilm?”

“...So that’s it.”

Rei didn’t understand how that conclusion followed from their earlier exchange, but at any rate, he understood how the blade was used.

(Technically, since she started traveling with us, she shouldn’t be short on money.)

Even so, the fact that she’s selling the blade made Ray wonder if Byune might actually be short on money.

That’s what Rei thought, but he also felt it might be inappropriate to ask about it given the current situation.

In the end, without asking any further questions, Rei and the others began moving forward again.

“Byune, don’t you think you’re getting faster at spotting and disarming traps?”

Marina remarked as she watched Byune moving while scanning the cave floor.

When Rei looked over at Byune after hearing that, he noticed that, well... it was only noticeable now that she’d pointed it out, but Byune’s movements did seem to have sped up slightly.

This mission hadn’t dramatically improved Byune’s skills as a thief, but it was proof that, albeit gradually, her skills were definitely improving.

(As I thought, nothing beats real-world experience. …Though that doesn’t mean we should neglect training, of course.)

Thinking this, Rei looked over at Vihera.

Although Vihera enjoyed sparring with Rei and the others, he thought that perhaps this could be considered a form of training as well.

However, in Vihera’s case, it would be more accurate to say she engages in mock battles not for training, but to satisfy her own thirst for combat.

You often hear stories about people whose hobbies became their jobs… and he wondered if this was something similar.

In fact, Rei’s thinking wasn’t wrong at all.

At least, that’s what Rei himself believed.

“What? Is something wrong?”

Vihera naturally noticed Rei’s gaze.

Since it was the gaze of the person she had feelings for, there was no way Vihera—who was already sensitive to the stares of others—could fail to notice it.

However, it was easy to predict that there was no way anything Vihera was hoping for would happen inside the dungeon.

And in fact, Rei didn’t say anything that would meet Vihera’s expectations.

“No, it’s nothing. It’s just that, watching Byune, I was thinking maybe from now on, we should have him train as a thief, not just in combat.”

It was something he blurted out on the spot, but it was a surprisingly accurate observation.

As for Byune’s combat training, while people like Rei and Elena do help out, it’s still Vihera who spends the most time with her.

This was partly because she’d known Byune the longest, and partly because it suited Vihera’s temperament—or perhaps I should say her inclinations—as someone who loved combat.

“That’s true. …If it’s just combat training, it’s fine for now, but it’s obvious that she’ll eventually hit a wall as a thief. She should probably apprentice under a thief. …A skilled one. Do you know anyone?”

“...Are you saying that knowing my social circle?”

Rei, who generally struggles with social interactions, prefers close, deep relationships.

As a result, while many people know of Rei through his reputation, there aren’t that many people he actually knows personally.

In the end, Vihera shot Rei a exasperated look as she struggled to figure out how to find someone to teach Byune the ways of a thief.
