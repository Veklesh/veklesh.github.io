For the time being, after stocking up on plenty of preserved food—not exactly "iburigakko," but something similar—Rei and Elena headed out of Golz.

Since it wasn’t even noon yet, it was a given that the butchering of the kelpies and garangigas hadn’t finished, but the reason they went outside the city anyway… was, as one might expect, the announcement message that echoed in Rei’s mind.

That said, even as they headed out of Golz, Rei had a pretty good idea of what had happened.

After all, there had been a previous instance where an announcement message had played while Rei was separated from Set, stating that he had acquired a new skill.

In other words, Set, who was acting separately from Rei, must have engaged in combat with some kind of monster and acquired a new skill by consuming the magic stone of the monster he defeated.

Although he understood that, what worried Rei a little was... what kind of monster he had defeated.

Rei doesn’t fully understand the criteria by which Beast Magic determines which skills to acquire.

However, having acquired numerous skills so far, he’s managed to grasp the general pattern.

That said, it’s really only a general idea at best.

The general rule is that the higher the rank of the monster or the rarer the species, the easier it is to acquire a skill from its magic stone.

Conversely, this also means it’s difficult to acquire skills from the magic stones of weak monsters.

In other words, the fact that Set’s Earth Arrow had leveled up likely meant he had fought a monster of considerable strength.

That is precisely why Rei and Elena went outside Golz.

Just in case there was a chance Set might bring back a monster’s corpse.

“Rei, do you really think Set will come?”

“Probably. …If he brings a monster carcass, I’d like him to dissect it while he’s at it.”

At Rei’s words, Elena turned her gaze toward the people butchering the Kelpies and Galangigas.

There, many adventurers were still frantically butchering monsters.

That said, one of the Kelpies had already been completely butchered, and the other was so far along that it would likely be finished in less than twenty minutes.

In contrast to the Kelpie, the Galangiga… due to its size, they were still in the process of peeling off its scales.

That was to be expected. Even the scales of a standard Galangiga serve as materials for alchemy and medicine.

The same goes for the spines hidden beneath the scales.

In other words, they had to peel off each scale one by one without damaging them, and pull out the spines beneath without breaking them.

And the Galangiga they were butchering was a massive beast… It was clear to everyone that the Kelpie’s dissection had been nothing more than a warm-up act.

“Judging by the Galangiga, I don’t think we’ll have time to handle that one.”

“It seems so.”

Watching the adventurers carefully peel off the scales and carry them to the collection area, he concluded there was absolutely no way they could spare the time to dissect a new monster.

Perhaps noticing the two of them, a female guild employee in her twenties, who had been giving instructions to the adventurers nearby, approached them.

“Is something wrong? Do you need something? If you’re here to watch the dissection, that’s fine, but…”

“Hm? Oh, no. It’s just that Set seems to have defeated a monster. He might bring the carcass over, so I came to see if we could handle an extra dissection…”

Upon hearing that Set had defeated a monster, the female guild employee looked puzzled.

That’s only natural. After all, she had clearly seen Set fly off alone just a moment ago.

And yet, for some reason, Rei was convinced that Set had indeed defeated a monster.

Well, since Set is a griffin, it’s not exactly surprising that he’d hunt monsters.

She understood that, but even so, why was Rei so certain of it… and on top of that, the idea that he would bring the carcass here was extremely puzzling.

However, she didn’t think asking about it here would get her an answer, so the guild clerk simply convinced herself that, since he was a tamer, they could probably communicate somehow, and opened her mouth.

“Well, it depends on how big the monster is. If it’s about the same size as a Kelpie, there shouldn’t be any particular problem… but, um, was it a Galangiga? If it’s about that size, it might be a bit…”

“Ah… I suppose so.”

Considering how massive a Galangiga is, I can’t imagine the people handling it have much leeway.

Just as Rei was nodding in agreement with the guild clerk’s words…

“Rei, he’s here. That’s good news.”

At Elena’s words, Rei looked up at the sky.

There, flapping its wings, was Set.

And just as Rei had expected, Set was carrying what appeared to be a monster’s corpse in his front paws.

“Is that… a bear? It has two tails, and its fur is yellow, though.”

As far as Rei knew, bears usually had brown or black fur.

There are bears with white fur, like polar bears, though.

In any case, as far as Rei knew, there were no bears with yellow fur.

Also, since it looks slightly larger than Set, that means it’s over three meters long.

“That’s rare. That’s an Argos Bear. I never expected to find one around here… Or, considering the Griffin’s speed, did you actually hunt one from far away?”

The guild clerk said this, but upon seeing the corpse of the monster called the Argos Bear, Rei suddenly had a bad feeling.

Since it’s a bear-like monster, its habitat would typically be woods, forests, mountains… that sort of thing.

However, judging by what the guild clerk said, it implies that this isn’t a monster that typically inhabits this area.

And since Set had acquired a skill, it likely possessed a certain level of strength.

Based on all that, the conclusion Rei reached was...

(Could it be... the monster from that dungeon?)

Monsters like goblins, kobolds, and orcs fell to their deaths from the dungeon formed on the cliff face.

This implies that there was a forest on the second floor of the dungeon, and the monsters were able to move freely between the two.

The residents of Golz had been obtaining materials from dead monsters, proof of defeat, and magic stones, but that doesn’t mean there aren’t any monsters that survived the fall from the dungeon.

In other words, that monster might be an Argos Bear.

(The question is, could it survive a fall from that height…? It doesn’t have wings like a Yellowbird. Hmm? Yellowbird… yellow? The Argos Bear’s fur is yellow too. …Is this just a coincidence?)

Rei was pondering this when Set descended to the ground.

Naturally, the people who had been dismantling the Garangiga also noticed Set’s presence…

“Whoaaaaaaaaaa… Huh? Wait, what? What’s that?”

“Whoa, the Griffin is holding a bear monster. …Holding it? How can he even hold something like that? They’re about the same size, aren’t they?”

“Hey, wait a minute! I don’t care about that, but hold onto that end properly! You’re going to peel off the scales all weirdly! It’s got spikes too—it’s dangerous!”

Everyone let out cries of surprise at the sudden appearance of Set. …Though, there were a few who were fussing that it would interfere with the disassembly of the Galangiga.

“So, that Argos Bear, was it? Are you going to include that monster in the disassembly too?”

“Understood. Leave it to me.”

Rei shot a surprised glance at the guild staff member who had declared this so casually.

Even though it was an extra job, he didn’t seem the least bit hesitant… in fact, he’d nodded without even consulting his superior.

“I know I’m the one asking, but are you sure this is okay? If you’d like, you could consult with Banillas…”

“No, the Guild Master told me to accommodate Rei-san and the others’ requests as much as possible. So, it’s no problem.”

Saying this, the guild clerk smiled.

That smile held a strong sense of goodwill toward Rei and his group.

The dungeon that suddenly appeared near Golz... Even though they were half-convinced, they couldn’t be sure if it was really a dungeon until Rei and the others actually entered it.

But… in reality, it was a dungeon—and one that didn’t look like it had just been created at all.

It was the kind of place where Golz’s adventurers—and not just them, but even ordinary people who weren’t adventurers at all—would approach, seeking materials from monsters that had fallen and died in the dungeon.

Fortunately… truly fortunately, no major problems arose while the residents of Golz were venturing near the dungeon.

There was the Yellowbird incident, but that too was resolved that very day thanks to Rei and the others.

That was precisely why Vanillas was deeply grateful to Rei and the others, and had thoroughly explained the situation to the guild staff.

Given that it was a request from Rei and the others, it wouldn’t be a problem if the number of monsters to be butchered increased by one or two.

Admittedly, it might have been a bit difficult if a monster the same size as a Galangiga had been added, but fortunately, the Argos Bear that Set brought wasn’t that large.

In reality, it was about the same size as Set, over three meters in length, but having seen the size of the Garangiga, he probably judged that the difference in size meant it would be fine.

“Is that so? Alright, then, count on you, Set!”

“Grrrrrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a joyful growl and began descending toward the ground.

Mid-descent, he dropped the Argos Bear onto the ground, causing a dull thud to echo through the area.

Those who had been watching Set descend while taking a break from dismantling the Galangiga gasped at the sight—which could only be described as an Argos Bear “bombing raid”—as the ground actually caved in slightly from the impact. But Set paid no mind to that, approaching Rei and rubbing his face against hers.

Rei stroked Set’s head and praised him for defeating the Argos Bear.

“You did a great job. Yeah, yeah. Set is really strong, after all.”

At Rei’s praise, Set purred proudly.

To an outside observer, it was a grifon over three meters long—a monstrous beast of the most vicious kind—but seeing how it was now so affectionate toward Rei, it appeared incredibly endearing.

Faced with such an incongruity, the adventurers who had been butchering the Galangiga are left speechless.

Should they be afraid of Set’s presence, or should they smile at the sight of him being so affectionate toward Rei?

They were torn between conflicting emotions.

The one who broke the awkward silence was the guild clerk who had been talking to Rei just moments ago.

“Alright, we have an additional task. We’ve decided to butcher that Argos Bear over there as well. I doubt anyone is free… but please have about five of you move over to that one. If you don’t know how to butcher it or which parts are usable, ask a guild staff member. Above all, do not act on your own judgment.”

“Well, I get that, but… is this the Argos Bear? It’s got a huge, gaping wound in its chest—a fatal one, actually… What’s the deal with its internal organs and stuff?”

“Grr?”

Perhaps hearing the adventurer’s words, Set, who had been squinting his eyes as Rei stroked him, turned his gaze toward the Argos Bear.

The male adventurer, now facing Set head-on, reflexively took a few steps back, but that was all he did.

For an adventurer unaccustomed to Set, he certainly had guts.

“W-what?”

The fact that his voice trembled even slightly was likely because he understood what a griffin truly was.

If Set had stayed in Golz a little longer, perhaps he would have been recognized not as a griffin monster, but as the endearing being known as Set… but unfortunately, it had only been a few days since Rei and the others arrived in Golz.

And they’ve spent most of those days outside Golz… mainly in dungeons.

Given those circumstances, it would be difficult for them to get used to Set the way the residents of Gilm have.

Set knows this, of course... but even so, for the friendly Set, it was a little sad to be feared by someone who wasn’t an enemy.

“Ah, don’t worry about it. Set probably just pulled out the magic stone. It’d be a big help if you could dismantle the Argos Bear after removing the magic stone.”

Saying this, Rei stroked Set, who looked a little disappointed.
