The door connecting to the space that had confined the giants was, naturally, large enough for the giants to come and go freely.

But... even if the giants could pass freely, it was obviously impossible for nearly ten of them to try to exit at once.

The bodies of the other giants got in the way, literally causing a bottleneck.

Did they even understand that, focused solely on satisfying their hunger, nearly ten Titans couldn't all rush toward Rei's location at once?

Or perhaps they thought that if they were the ones to break through, they could devour Rei all at once.

Watching these giants, Rei swung his Death Scythe at the immobilized giants.

Though not quite as powerful as the Power Slash that blew the door off its hinges, the Death Scythe, imbued with sufficient magic, unleashed a force worthy of its name, the Grim Reaper's Scythe.

The giants, who had been jammed together and unable to move, were blown away en masse.

Of course, it wasn't merely a knockback; the bodies of the giants present sustained various injuries.

The worst injuries were undoubtedly the slash wounds.

The giant whose torso was cleaved in two died instantly, as expected.

Beyond that, many giants suffered broken bones and ruptured internal organs from the sheer impact of the Death Scythe's swing.

Among them, the unluckiest were likely the giants crushed by the giants blown away by Rei's single strike of the Death Scythe.

Even for giants boasting immense strength, having nearly ten giants with bodies not much different from their own blown away and land on them was devastating.

That was undeniably a weapon worthy of the name.

"Well, that's about it. ...Though I don't expect them to give up just like that."

The giants displayed a frenzy worthy of the name.

Naturally, they wouldn't calm down just because a few comrades had been slightly injured.

Rather, they instinctively sensed that their fallen comrades meant enemies lay ahead—targets to devour.

Is this enough, if it means helping Elena and the others?

Thinking that, Rei looked at the new giant that had appeared.

Not far from where the previous one had been blown away, this giant moved swiftly to its next action. Unlike before, it wasn't blocked by the bodies of its fallen comrades, unable to escape.

It had cleverly outmaneuvered its comrades.

Rei couldn't tell if the giant possessed enough intelligence to form such thoughts, but seeing it lick its lips at the sight of him, he could roughly guess what it was thinking.

Watching the giant that saw him as nothing but food, Rei took a step forward without any particular sign of tension.

The giant, as if waiting for that very movement.

It reached out, trying to devour Rei.

(Even if you try to eat me, I'm wearing a Dragon Robe, so I doubt you'll manage it that easily. I wonder how that'll go?)

The Dragon Robe, made from dragon hide and scales, wasn't something a giant could just bite through.

Then again, Rei didn't think the giant could possibly understand that the robe he wore possessed such formidable properties.

"Anyway, you... die right there."

Dodging the outstretched hand, Rei's swung Death Scythe cleanly severed the giant's neck.

But the giant, its head severed, seemed unaware of its fate, taking several steps forward headless.

Then, as if puzzled, it moved its arms a few times... and the next instant, blood spurted violently from its neck as it collapsed to the ground.

"Well, that's about it."

Rei muttered, but another giant attacked him.

It wasn't a thought of avenging its comrade... it was a movement purely driven by appetite.

Precisely because it was driven by hunger, its movements showed no hesitation.

But... precisely because it was pure instinct, it made no feints or tricks, only the simple, relentless motion of trying to grab Rei's body.

To Rei, such movement was equivalent to standing still.

Of course, it wasn't actually stationary.

Quite the opposite. Being driven by pure instinct, there was no hesitation whatsoever—it moved to devour Rei.

"Don't think you can devour me that easily!"

Using the Twilight Spear, he dodged the hand movement and countered with a thrust.

Given the giant's linear movements, there was no way it could evade the thrust launched in counterattack.

The tip of the Twilight Spear easily crushed the giant's skull.

Starting with that giant, the corpses of giants began piling up one after another.

The giants' only advantages were their massive size and... above all, their sheer numbers.

An overwhelming number, exceeding a thousand.

...However, the preemptive strike launched by Rei and Vihera drastically reduced their numbers.

Even now, Elena and Vihera, who had charged into the space where the giants remained, were defeating them one after another, and their numbers were decreasing at an accelerating rate.

Though the giants held the advantage in numbers, when they appeared before Rei, the size of the space where the door had been meant only one... or at most two could show themselves.

Three or four giants would be blocked by each other's bodies, preventing them from escaping the doorway.

Once that happened, those immobilized giants were nothing but targets for Rei.

Consequently, the giants had only two choices: either challenge Rei one or two at a time, or attempt to emerge all at once, get stuck, and be treated as targets by Rei.

If the giants possessed greater intelligence, they might have prioritized evasion when facing Rei, using the gaps in his defense to lure more giants in...

But fundamentally, giants are creatures ruled by instinct. In terms of intelligence, they are inferior even to goblins, who know how to cooperate with their comrades.

As a result, the giants fell one after another to Rei's Death Scythe and Twilight Spear.

While Rei and the others fought the giants inside the cave... Ryutasu, stationed near the cave entrance, stood with his cheeks twitching from tension.

Just moments before, he had been wearing his usual smile.

Of course, it wasn't a genuine smile; it was one crafted to hide his true feelings from those around him.

Wearing that smile, Ryutasu contemplated what to do next.

It was clear his position was far from strong right now.

After all, he had revealed much of the information he was permitted to discuss—about this facility, Jaya, the giants... Given that, even as one of the sons of the man leading Jaya, he was certain to be treated as a traitor.

That was precisely why he needed Rei and the others to thoroughly crush Jaya, and why he needed the Kingdom of Mireana to handle matters within the Kingdom of Reburina. Until then, he had to either go into hiding somewhere or flee the country and seek refuge in another nation... That was the sort of thing he was contemplating.

One of the guards keeping watch around him returned to Ryutas.

He brought news: someone had entered the forest.

...If it had been just a few people arriving, Ryutas wouldn't have worn such a grim expression. But...

"Soldiers from the Kingdom of Reburina... hundreds of them... and reinforcements are certain to follow, you say?"

Ryuutas repeated the information he'd just heard from the guard, and even that alone made him understand the danger of his current situation.

No, to be precise, it wasn't his own danger, but rather the danger to those within this cave and its surroundings... that was the correct way to put it.

If he were only concerned for his own safety, escaping from here would be the solution... but that wasn't an option.

It was clear that the soldiers surrounding this forest had no intention of letting anyone escape.

If Ryutas truly wanted to, and with the strength of his guards, escape might indeed be possible.

But what if he disappeared right now, and in his place, soldiers from the Kingdom of Reburina stormed into the cave and turned hostile towards Rei and the others?

(At worst... no, they would definitely conclude I sold out Rei and the others. That's not just unpleasant... I can only imagine the worst possible outcome. So, what's the best course of action? A choice that saves me, avoids Rei's hatred... and if possible, even earns their gratitude?)

Ryutas pondered the best course of action in his mind.

Risk versus reward, his own safety versus what comes next... after Jaya is crushed.

He weighed all these factors... then glanced toward the cave and let out a small sigh.

"Guess this is it..."

He muttered, his expression clearly displeased... truly displeased.

Though Ryutas often wore masks, anyone seeing his expression now would know without a doubt it was genuine.

It certainly didn't look like a feigned expression.

"Someone go into the cave and relay a message to Rei. Ah, no. If possible, don't just relay a message—bring reinforcements back with you. We desperately, truly lack combat strength."

"...I don't mind doing that, but are you planning to stay here? To fight the soldiers?"

One of the guards asked Ryutas, looking utterly incredulous.

Ryutas was also considered one of the candidates to succeed the criminal organization known as Jaya.

Naturally, he possessed a certain level of skill.

But that was merely above a certain level; it wasn't the kind of power that set him apart from others, like Rei and the others.

In other words, with Ryutas's skill level, he couldn't pull off what Rei and the others did—where quality overcomes quantity.

He was certainly stronger than the average soldier, but against opponents who relied on sheer numbers, he simply wasn't enough. His strength was hopelessly inadequate.

It was precisely because he understood this that one of the guards asked Ryutas that question.

...Naturally, the guards are stronger than Ryutas, but they too lack the strength to handle hundreds, and likely more, soldiers.

Ryutas fully understood his own strength and that of his guards. Understanding this, he nodded at the guard's words.

"That's right. I'll stay here and fight the soldiers of the Kingdom of Reburina."

"You'll die, you know?"

The guard didn't hedge his words with "You might die," but stated flatly, "You will die."

In truth, that statement was probably not wrong.

Ryuutasu understood that too, but that didn't mean he could just run away alone.

(Well, it's undoubtedly the next best option. But if I hold off the soldiers of the Kingdom of Reburina trying to enter the cave here, it'll show my sincerity towards Rei and the others.)

It was sincerity steeped in calculation, but sincerity was sincerity nonetheless.

Of course, soldiers from a small country like Reburina wouldn't stand a chance against Rei and the others.

Still, it would undoubtedly be a hassle.

If Ryutas and the others managed to hold off those soldiers, Rei and the others would undoubtedly feel at least some gratitude toward them.

To be precise, that was Ryutas's hope.

Having decided to cooperate with Rei and his group, Ryutas's stance was to go all out in their support, not to do things halfway.

When he explained this point and stated his desire for them to be seen as friendly toward Rei and his group, the guards wore slightly awkward expressions.

They understood Ryutasu's feelings.

But the guards' main concern was the sheer magnitude of the risk involved.

If Rei and the others arrived while they were still alive, there would be no problem.

But of course, no one could know for certain if that would actually happen.

In fact, the possibility that Rei and the others would arrive after they were dead... was far more likely.

Inside the cave, the path was straight ahead except for the first fork.

Both the location of the Jaya people and the gathering place of the giants were a considerable distance from the fork.

Since only one messenger can be sent, heading in the wrong direction would result in a potentially fatal loss of time.

But sending two messengers would undoubtedly leave us severely weakened in terms of combat strength.

"Why not send the young one as the messenger?"

As he said this, the escort man found himself thinking it might actually be a good idea.

There were no traps inside the cave, so there was basically no danger.

...Though in reality, a giant was running amok. But since they were outside the cave, they had no way of knowing that.

If Ryutas encountered the giant, the worst possible outcome awaited him.

Though, with Rei standing in front of the giants, that worry was probably unnecessary.

"Rejected. With me here, the soldiers might hold back a bit. ...More importantly, look, they'll be here soon. Send the messenger."

Even Ryutas could now detect the presence and approaching sounds of the intruders. He informed his guards that there was no time to waste.
