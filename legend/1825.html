Inside the Seisol Marquis’s mansion, Gaiska set down the glass of wine he had been drinking on the table and turned his gaze toward the man sitting across from him—the man who had acted as an intermediary with the Black Wolf.

His expression was far from pleasant; no one working in this mansion would dare approach him on their own initiative.

The intermediary stood before Gaiska, looking at him with an air of deference.

However, Gaiska was unaware that the mediator’s attitude was nothing more than a facade.

To the intermediary, Gaiska was someone he believed he could easily deceive.

And, in fact, he wasn’t wrong.

“So, what was all that commotion today? I specifically ordered that only the Black Wolf be hired. …Do you really think a bunch of pathetic fools like that could do anything to Rei?”

Although Gaiska looked down on Rei and even hated him, he had to acknowledge his strength.

After all, Rei had demonstrated that level of power during today’s mock battle.

But precisely because of that, his hatred for Rei only grew.

When Gaiska was told by Deotres, standing beside him, that Rei had distinguished himself once again—and that it was likely the Black Wolf’s doing—he summoned the man acting as the intermediary to hear his side of the story. …No, “grilled” would be a more fitting description.

“Even if you ask me that, I’ve got no answer. I’ve already entrusted this job to the Black Wolf. Once that’s done, there’s nothing more I can do to get involved. I mean, if he were taking actions completely meaningless to the job, that’d be one thing, but I don’t think that’s the case this time, is it?”

“Why not? You should have fully understood Rei’s power during today’s mock battle. If that’s the case, there’s no need to wait and see anymore. Just tell him to kill him already.”

At those words from Gaiska, the mediator shot him a look of contempt—just for an instant, a mere split second.

That said, Deotres was the only one who noticed that look; Gaiska didn’t seem to have noticed it at all.

“Well, I’m afraid I don’t quite follow your logic there… but I suppose it wouldn’t be unreasonable to conclude that, in the end, a mock battle is just a mock battle.”

“Then why on earth did we go to the trouble of staging such a farce in the first place!”

With his frustration plain to see, Gaiska slammed the glass of wine he had been drinking down on the table.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn’t have been out of the question for him to have thrown it at the man acting as mediator, but the fact that he didn’t do so—was it because he’d managed to restrain himself to some extent, or simply because the man in front of him wasn’t worth such an outburst?

“After all that groundwork, all we ended up doing was boosting Rei’s reputation!?”

“Please calm down. I don’t think that mock battle was meaningless, either. To begin with, isn’t it possible that they caused that commotion as a finishing touch precisely because they assessed Rei’s strength during that mock battle?”

“……The opponents in that mock battle weren’t just adventurers; there were knights and soldiers hired by nobles as well. What’s more, there were even others with nicknames just like Rei’s. Though, seeing how they lost without even putting up a proper fight against Rei, I’m honestly suspicious whether those ‘nicknames’ are even real.”

As Gaiska spoke with genuine distaste, the mediator took care not to let his exasperation show.

After all, what took place today was nothing more than a mock battle.

It wasn’t a real, life-or-death battle, and both sides had gone to great lengths to avoid attacks that might affect the area outside the stage.

He couldn’t help but think, “Don’t they understand something so simple?”

Still, he managed to hide it through sheer willpower and continued the conversation.

It’s difficult to have a proper conversation with an idiot like this, but I kept telling myself that this, too, is part of the job.

“So, for now, I take it you’ve accepted the Black Wolf’s actions. Is that correct?”

“If I keep talking to Gaiska like this, I’ll just be wasting time.”

Having come to that conclusion, the mediator spoke up to bring the conversation to a close.

That said, the mediator hadn’t expected him to understand so easily.

But… contrary to the mediator’s expectations, Gaiska waved his hand impatiently, signaling for him to leave.

The mediator, who had fully expected to be subjected to a barrage of complaints, was slightly taken aback... but, as if to say he had no intention of staying here and being subjected to more unnecessary nitpicking, he made to leave the room...

“Hey.”

At the words that came from Gaiska’s mouth, his movement stopped.

He wore an exasperated expression, but fortunately, the mediator had his back turned, so Gaiska couldn’t see his face.

Gaiska called out to the man’s back in a voice laced with irritation.

“Listen up. I’m paying you guys a lot of money. Don’t you dare screw this up.”

At the sound of his voice, the middleman turned around and nodded obsequiously.

“Of course.”

Perhaps satisfied with the man’s reply, Gaiska says nothing more, signaling with his attitude that he should get lost.

It was exactly the kind of gesture one would make when shooing away a stray dog.

In fact, to Gaiska, the middleman was probably no different from a stray dog.

Even though the mediator knew this, he still found it unpleasant.

Still, the fact that he managed to keep Gaiska from realizing this until the very end was partly due to his skill in such matters… but more than anything, it was probably because, to Gaiska, the mediator simply wasn’t worth worrying about.

“Hmph,” he snorted, looking at the door through which the mediator had vanished, clearly displeased.

“What a fool. Utterly useless. In that case, wouldn’t it have been better to find someone more capable?”

Gaiska muttered grumpily, glaring at Deotres.

It was unpleasant enough just to be dealing with someone who wasn’t even a noble to begin with, but for that person to be incompetent as well—it was bound to be no fun.

But seeing Gaiska looking so displeased, Deotres spoke up to calm him down.

“Young master, let’s calm down a bit. After all, the guys Rei defeated today weren’t exactly your men, were they?”

“Of course not. There’s no way someone who’s a drug addict would be one of my men.”

Gaiska snorts, just as he did before.

But the emotion behind it was vastly different.

Earlier, he had met with the mediator directly, so his irritation had turned into dissatisfaction and spilled out.

However… the assumption that drug addicts were his subordinates certainly made him feel irritated, but since it was still just an assumption, that irritation was smaller than the frustration he felt toward the intermediary.

“Right? Then, there’s no reason for the young master to get that angry over today’s incident, is there?”

“Now that you mention it, you’re right. But… what if we waste our forces like we did today, and as a result, we don’t have enough strength to fight Rei?”

“Ah… Young Master. Wasn’t the plan for this mission supposed to be the assassination of Rei? Since we don’t necessarily have to fight him head-on, I don’t think you need to worry about running out of manpower.”

To begin with, if we were to fight Rei head-on, defeating him would be difficult… no, even calling it “difficult” is just wishful thinking.

Realistically speaking, I’m not saying there’s no one with the ability to handle Rei, but finding such a person is difficult to begin with.

Even if we did find such a person, there’s the question of whether we could actually hire them.

To begin with, hiring Black Wolf was already a huge stretch, and hiring a new assassin is something Gaiska simply cannot afford right now.

(Was hiring Black Wolf really the right choice? If we were going to spend the money anyway, surely there were more effective ways to use it?)

This recent incident has made me feel that way all the more deeply.

If we fail to kill Rei as planned, what will happen…? That would undoubtedly be fatal for Gaiska.

After all, even this mansion is being used solely to collect rewards.

Could it be that Black Wolf, whom we hired at such great cost, is actually useless?

Gaiska’s thoughts bring on a wave of intense anxiety.

“……”

Having known Gaesca for quite some time, Deotres naturally understands what Gaesca is thinking and what anxieties he is harboring. Even though he understands… Deotres still makes no move to say anything to Gaesca.

From Deotres’ perspective, he actually welcomes Gaiska’s emotional instability.

In fact, he even finds himself hoping that Gaiska will become even more anxious.

That said, he makes absolutely no move to show it.

“Young master, let’s calm down for now. Since there’s nothing we can do at the moment, we have no choice but to trust Black Wolf.”

Gaesca still looked slightly dissatisfied at Deotres’ words, spoken without the slightest hint of his true feelings, but he forced himself to shift his mindset, reasoning that if his trusted subordinate said so, then so be it.

“Anyway, that ridiculous mock battle is over now. …Which means the next event is the New Year’s party.”

“Aren’t you looking forward to seeing what kind of outfit Rei will show up in?”

“I won’t deny that. An adventurer showing up at a gathering of nobles. I’m really looking forward to seeing what kind of commotion he’ll cause.”

At Deotres’ words, Gaiska laughed happily—truly happily—as he raised his glass of wine to his lips.

“Um, like this?”

“No. That’s not how you move your feet. You’re focusing too much on your right foot.”

At Elena’s correction, Rei shifted his focus from just his right foot to his entire body.

...That said, focusing his attention on his entire body naturally meant focusing on his face as well... which meant he was looking closely at Elena’s face, right in front of him.

Rei and Elena had kissed several times before, but even so, seeing Elena’s goddess-like beauty so close to his own face inevitably made him feel a bit shy.

Unlike Rei, who was self-conscious, Elena, the object of his attention, seemed completely oblivious to his feelings and continued to give him instructions one after another.

Dancing is, of course, a staple of any party.

Since he was attending the party, Rei was naturally bound to have the opportunity to dance. …Though, it was also an undeniable fact that Elena wanted to dance with Rei.

For Elena, dancing with Rei at the New Year’s party holds various meanings.

As a girl in love, it’s only natural to want to dance with the one she likes, and if she dances with Rei, she won’t have to worry about being hit on by random guys.

At the same time, she also wanted to show everyone around her—if possible—that she and Rei shared a special relationship.

Though not quite to Elena’s level, Rei was also quite handsome.

Since he’s in his mid-teens, some might not be interested in someone younger, but conversely, there was a possibility that others might be strongly interested in someone younger.

In terms of keeping such suitors at bay, Elena’s beauty was a powerful asset.

That was precisely why Elena was putting so much effort into her dance training with Rei.

It was far too intense to be called “after-dinner exercise,” but even so, Rei continued practicing the dance, though he was still bewildered by his sudden closeness to Elena.

Given that, Rei, who was naturally athletic,

His dancing skills improved rapidly, and gradually, the number of times Elena had to correct him began to decrease.

Just how long had they been practicing, anyway…? They had danced nonstop, without even taking a break, and before they knew it, they had become so skilled that no one would believe they were beginners.

Although it was a dance with no music, relying solely on their shared rhythm, Rei and Elena were naturally well-suited to each other, so they were able to continue dancing without any problems at all.

To an uninformed observer, the sight of them dancing so passionately might even make them wonder for a moment if they were at a ball.

Even as they danced like that, the two showed no signs of running out of breath.

In fact, they were barely even breaking a sweat.

In that state, the two continued to dance on and on... until Ara came to fetch them.
