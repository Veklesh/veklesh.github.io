"What!? Wait, Rei, are you serious!?"

Lenora’s voice, filled with surprise, echoed through the guild.

Setting aside the times she teases Kenny, Lenora is usually a capable person who calmly gets her work done.

Since such a composed Lenora suddenly shouted out loud, the guild staff inside the counter—who knew her personality well—naturally turned their gaze toward her, wondering what had happened.

...That said, once they realized it was Rei talking to Lenora, they quickly assumed it was just business as usual and went back to their work.

Unlike the adventurers—many of whom are currently on leave, though those out dismantling Giganto Turtles or hunting Kobolds are an exception—the guild staff are busy with various tasks, starting with the expansion construction.

Unless there was some kind of emergency, they simply didn’t have the bandwidth to pay much attention to him in the current situation.

Seeing this, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered by Lenora’s surprised exclamation and continued speaking.

“I’m serious. Besides, the Guild was having trouble with the kobolds to begin with, right? So, since I’m offering to reduce the damage, I don’t think there’s a problem. …Well, I can’t say it’s completely safe, since there’s no guarantee that a kobold won’t manage to climb over the earthen wall I build.”

“That’s…”

Rei’s words were an undeniable fact.

Although the kobold attacks were continuing almost daily as an unexpected development, if they could be dealt with, it would undoubtedly benefit the guild… and above all, Gilm.

“But… are you really sure it’s okay? The area where the expansion work is being done is pretty large, isn’t it? If that’s the case, I think it’d be tough if you were to handle it all by yourself, just in case something happens.”

Kenny, who had finished organizing the documents next to Lenora, asked, sounding concerned for Rei’s safety.

Hearing Kenny’s words, Lenora also turned a worried gaze toward Rei.

However, despite their concerned gazes, Rei nodded to indicate there was no problem and spoke up.

“Don’t worry. Besides, I’m not alone—Set is with me. So even if a monster shows up while I’m using magic, I can usually handle it.”

Hearing that, and knowing that Set possesses strength far beyond his cute appearance, she had no choice but to accept Rei’s words.

“……I understand. I’ll go consult with the higher-ups, so please wait a moment. However, I don’t think the Guild alone can make a decision on this matter, so I’ll need to run it by Lord Daskar and the others. That means I don’t think we’ll be able to build the wall today.”

“Ah… well, now that you mention it, I suppose you’re right. Still, this shouldn’t be a bad thing for Lord Daskar either, so if possible, please contact him immediately and get his decision.”

At Rei’s words, Lenora nodded firmly and left the room.

Lenora, too, is a resident of Gilm.

Moreover, while she possesses some ability to defend herself, it is, after all, only limited.

That’s precisely why, even though she’d been fine until now, if the kobolds were to pass by the construction site and make their way into the town… When she thought of it that way, it was only natural for her to feel anxious.

If even Lenora felt that way, it wouldn’t be strange for the residents of Gilm—who aren’t guild officials or anything of the sort—to feel even greater anxiety than she did.

I will definitely get permission.

With that resolve, Lenora headed toward her boss’s office.

Watching her back, Rei noticed Kenny looking at her with a mischievous smile.

“What’s up?”

“Oh, nothing. It’s nothing, really. I just thought it was funny that Rei is so busy, even though he just requested the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.”

“Ah… I won’t deny that.”

“Right? Someone just came from the Giganto Turtle dismantling site a little while ago, and they hired a few adventurers on the spot and went off somewhere.”

Rei knew exactly what those words meant.

(I guess he gathered people to clean out the warehouse. …If that’s the case, does that mean he got permission to let Suu-chi’s family and the others stay at the warehouse too?)

For now, Rei felt relieved that there was a possibility of increasing the workforce for the Giganto Turtle’s dismantling.

“The Giganto Turtle was a tasty monster in more ways than one. In that sense, there’s no doubt that being busy is a welcome development for me.”

“Huh, I see. …But…”

“Sorry to keep you waiting!”

“……Oh?”

It hadn’t even been a few minutes since he started talking to Kenny.

Despite that, Lenora was already running toward Rei.

“...That was quick. I thought our discussion would take a little longer.”

“So the Guild Master was aware that the current situation is precarious.”

“Then, what about the earthen wall?”

“The Guild Master will report to Lord Daskar immediately and seek his permission later. So, Rei, he wants you to build the earthen wall right away to prevent any more kobolds from invading.”

“...Is that okay?”

“We’re sending someone to the lord’s mansion to get permission right away, so please wait just a little longer.”

Rei had fully expected to hear that, so being told outright that he could go ahead and build the earthen wall was clearly unexpected.

However, seeing Rei’s reaction, Lenora nodded with a serious expression and spoke.

“It means the guild’s higher-ups… and the Guildmaster, too, understand that the current situation is dangerous. Of course, that’s probably because they realize that building an earthen wall to prevent the kobolds from invading—even if it’s not a perfect solution—would be beneficial for everyone in Gilm.”

“I suppose so. Logically speaking, no one would welcome kobolds running rampant in the town they govern…”

It’s not as if there’s absolutely no one who would want that…

In fact, if someone had absolutely no interest in governing their territory and was just playing at being a lord for fun, it wouldn’t be surprising if they did something like that.

...Of course, Daskar was no such fool.

Rei was convinced of that, but the next words to come from Lenora’s mouth convinced him even more.

“Besides, Rei, you’re going to freeze the earthen walls with Marina’s spirit magic, aren’t you? If Marina appears, even Daskar is no match for her.”

“……I won’t deny that.”

Rei recalled the conversations he’d witnessed between Daskar and Marina on several occasions.

Marina had known Daskar since he was a child, and she held over him even what could be called his “dark past”—such as the time he proposed to Marina, a beautiful woman older than him.

If Marina’s actions were to harm Gilm, Daskar would surely not be able to tolerate it.

His responsibility as the lord of Gilm versus his own embarrassing past.

There’s no need to even think about which one he’ll prioritize.

But this time is different. Marina is stepping in strictly for Gilm’s sake.

To be precise, Marina hasn’t actually appeared yet at this point, but since Rei said he would ask Marina to reinforce the earthen wall, there was no doubt she wouldn’t refuse.

“So, I’ll handle getting the permission over here. Rei, please take care of the earthen walls.”

“Good luck, Rei.”

Following Lenora’s words, Kenny also cheers Rei on.

To be honest, Kenny would have preferred to stay and talk with Rei.

However, considering what Rei was about to do, there was no option to hold him back.

That’s why, unusually for Kenny, he didn’t press the issue or try to talk a little longer; he simply said that.

Rei nodded at the two of them… and not only that, but he also received expectant glances from the adventurers and guild staff still remaining at the guild.

As for the adventurers, aside from the few who were desperate for money because of the kobold incident, none of them were particularly pleased with the current situation.

Still, the reason they’d come to the guild to accept the request to exterminate the kobolds was that if they didn’t, the town of Gilm itself would suffer damage.

As for the guild staff, the kobold situation had significantly increased their workload, so they were more than happy for it to be resolved.

“Alright, I’m off. First, I’ll look for Marina… no, actually, I should build an earthen wall before looking for her. That way, the number of kobolds getting in will be reduced, at least a little.”

Saying that, he leaves the guild.

Set, who had been playing with a few people in front of the guild, noticed Rei and purred happily.

“Sorry, but Set and I have something we need to do. Please save playing with Set for another time.”

Hearing Rei’s words, the people who had been playing with Set shrugged it off, showing no signs of throwing a tantrum, and walked away.

They probably knew that trying to force Set to play with them now would only make him dislike them.

And so, Rei heads toward the construction site with Set.

Maybe they’d run into Marina along the way.

He held onto that hope, but unfortunately, they couldn’t spot Marina or the Dark Elf she was supposedly teaching, and Rei and Set arrived at the construction site.

“Wooooooooooooooooooah!”

Rei turns his gaze toward the sudden roar.

There, an adventurer stood, raising a greatsword to strike down at a kobold.

But the kobold easily dodged the greatsword’s blow and lashed out with its sharp claws.

“Ow! Damn it, that kobold’s getting cocky!”

Watching the battle between the adventurer and the kobold, Rei stopped in his tracks.

The adventurer wasn’t exactly a powerhouse, but he wasn’t a complete novice either.

It was clear from his movements that he possessed a decent level of strength—enough to easily defeat a kobold or two.

Despite possessing such skill, he was currently struggling against a single kobold.

He was wielding a greatsword, which naturally didn’t pair well with a kobold—a creature known for its swift movements.

Even so, the way this battle was unfolding came as a surprise to Rei as well.

“Is it a rare species or a higher-tier one? …I don’t think it looks that different from a regular kobold.”

Simply put, a kobold is a humanoid with a dog’s face.

But in this case, the fact that it had a dog’s face was proving to be a major problem.

Just as there are many different breeds of dogs, each with its own coat color, individual kobolds vary greatly in this regard.

If these were orcs or goblins, most individuals would look nearly identical, but kobolds were different in that regard.

That’s precisely why it’s difficult to tell whether the differences you see in a kobold are due to individual variation or because it’s a higher-ranking or rare species.

At the very least, the kobold the adventurers were fighting right where Rei was looking didn’t seem all that different from the kobold corpses lying around nearby.

Anyway.

The adventurer in the distance is fighting the kobold alone.

I don’t know if they usually go solo or if they normally travel in a party but just happened to be alone today.

I don’t know… but facing a kobold that might be a rare or higher-tier species, Rei simply couldn’t bring himself to abandon him.

...It’s an undeniable fact that he was more interested in the unknown magic stone than in helping the adventurer, though.

“Hey, do you need help!?”

That said, snatching the prey the adventurer was fighting would likely lead to all sorts of trouble, so he shouted loudly enough for the adventurer wielding the greatsword to hear.

The adventurer with the greatsword, who had been completely outmaneuvered by the kobold’s speed, was startled by the sudden shout. He instinctively leaped backward and yelled.

“Please! This guy isn’t your average kobold!”

After confirming that he was genuinely asking for help, Rei quickly hurled the spear—which looked like it could break at any moment—that he’d pulled from his Misty Ring, using only the movement of his hand.

The spear, propelled with full force, flew straight through the air, slicing through it… The kobold, sensing the spear’s approach, tried desperately to dodge, but his movement was just… and fatally… too slow. In the next instant, the spear shattered the kobold’s skull, and the shaft snapped, rolling across the ground.
