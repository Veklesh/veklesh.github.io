After talking with Ajas for about ten minutes, Rei decided that staying any longer would arouse suspicion. Eventually, glancing around at the surrounding forest, he spoke up.

"Well, I need to go check on the others too, so I'll be taking my leave around here."

"Huh? Oh, I see. I want to make sure I check the surroundings thoroughly too. That'll be a big help. Honestly, having someone famous like Rei around here just distracts me no matter what I'm doing."

"...Distracting, huh? I never really thought of it that way. Well, having a skilled adventurer like you here in Treant Forest as a guard is a big help for us too. I'd be happy if you could stay on guard here a bit longer, not just for today or tomorrow."

"Hahaha, that depends on the pay. If I can cut down trees while guarding, that's money, right? Normally, you'd think it's a pretty lucrative job... But if it were that lucrative, they wouldn't be recruiting extra help, would they?"

"True. There's no doubt monsters have been increasing lately. ...Actually, it was abnormal that there were so few monsters in Treant Forest until now."

To be precise, it's more accurate to say the Treant Forest itself attracted monsters, and as a result, it wiped out all the surrounding monsters.

But new monsters were gradually moving into the areas where the old ones had vanished, and that was the reason the number of monsters in Treant Forest had started increasing recently.

(Well, it's true the number of woodcutter guards has decreased, but it's not exactly hopeless. This whole incident is also meant to probe Ajash while putting pressure on him.)

Looking at Ajas before him, Rei suddenly shifted his gaze to Ajas's left hand.

A snake tattoo, as if climbing up his arm.

That distinctive tattoo was the primary reason Ilse believed Ajas to be the enemy of her family.

Of course, Ilse hadn't forgotten Ajas's face, but even so, memories inevitably fade over the years.

Ajas's tattoo was the decisive factor in that situation.

"Huh? What is it? Oh, this tattoo? It's unusual, isn't it?"

Seeing Rei's gaze, he must have understood where she was looking.

Ajas didn't hide the tattoo on his left hand... he flaunted it, almost as if showing it off.

(Even if he didn't know Ilse was targeting him as an enemy... still, if he'd done something wrong before, would he really flaunt such a conspicuous tattoo like this? Or does he have absolute certainty that even if he has done something wrong, it will never be discovered?)

He couldn't figure out the reason, but seeing Ajas display the tattoo with such confidence was completely unexpected for Rei.

"Ah, that's unusual. I've seen plenty of adventurers over the years, and some had tattoos, but one this flashy is rare."

A fleeting image of the elite members of the Holy Light Church flashed through Rei's mind, but he dismissed it as irrelevant.

(Wait, could it be... Ajash is also connected to the Holy Light Church?)

He pictured Ajas uttering the Holy Light Church's stock phrase about the Goddess of Holy Light... but Rei couldn't quite picture it.

Daskar certainly knew the dangers of the Holy Light Cult.

He never imagined Daskar would deliberately bring the Holy Light Cult into Gilm.

(Well, lately I haven't heard much talk about the Holy Light Church anyway.)

Given past events, the Holy Light Cult has become difficult to operate within the Kingdom of Mireana, at the very least.

Of course, banning religion outright would only drive them underground... Still, the nobles likely judged it better than allowing the Holy Light Cult to spread openly.

In any case, for Rei, the Holy Light Cult was nothing but a troublesome presence.

He immediately banished the thought from his mind and refocused his gaze on the tattoo on Ajas's left hand.

"I see? Well, that makes sense. I'm probably the only one with a tattoo this impressive."

Ajas, who had been acting cool, seemed pleased to have his prized tattoo praised. He smiled and said that.

"Yeah, I've never seen anyone else with a tattoo like yours. ...Actually, are there really no others out there with the same tattoo? I mean, you don't know everyone who has one, so I can't say for sure... but I've never seen it before."

"I don't know every single tattooed guy out there, so I can't say for sure... but still, I haven't seen it."

"Hmm... I figured."

If he knew someone with the same tattoo, would he brag about it, or say he didn't know?

Rei couldn't tell which one Ajas was, but even so, it didn't seem to him that Ajas was lying.

(It's still not certain if there are others with the same tattoo. But... does that mean the possibility that Ajas is Ilse's killer has increased? If so, maybe I should put a little more pressure on Ajas.)

Having made that judgment, Rei opened his mouth, feigning nonchalance.

"I forget how many years ago it was, but there was some commotion about a guy with a tattoo killing a peddler or something... Do you know anything about that?"

Upon hearing Rei's words, Ajas flinched. Just for an instant... yes, definitely just an instant, but his eyes betrayed a flicker of unease.

That alone was sufficient proof for Rei. He concluded the person before him was almost certainly the one Ilse was searching for.

Of course, incidents involving merchants being killed were common occurrences around Gilm, in the Kingdom of Mireana, and indeed throughout the world of Ergin.

It was also far from uncommon for the perpetrator to be someone with tattoos.

But... if one had any inkling of suspicion about oneself, it was only natural to think that one was being referred to.

That, he thought, was probably why Ajas had been unsettled.

"What's wrong? You seem unsettled. Could it be... you know something about this?"

"Huh!? ...No, nothing. The tattoo's shape is different, but I just thought it was a shame someone with a tattoo like that would resort to becoming a thief."

"I see. Well, I hear they're still investigating that case, so catching the culprit might not be too difficult. If that happens, it'll obviously be tough for them to keep adventuring."

Ajas assumed the culprit was a thief, while Rei assumed it was an adventurer.

In that sense, their conversation was completely mismatched, but Ajas either didn't notice or chose not to say anything.

"Ah, sorry for taking up your time. Well, I need to check on the others, so I'll really have to take my leave this time."

With that, Rei turned and left.

Ajas stared intently at Rei's back... at the back of his Dragon Robe.

"Well then, let's see how this plays out. It'd be easier if he just quietly showed his hand. But that's probably unlikely."

From a safe distance away from Ajas, Rei murmured to himself while surveying the forest.

To anyone watching, Rei's actions clearly put pressure on the situation. Yet, the one who did it wondered if maybe... it wasn't enough?

He was clearly unsettled, but he hadn't made any fatal mistakes.

If he'd at least tried to flee reflexively, Rei could have clearly determined that Ajas was Ilse's killer.

Of course, Rei had seen the flicker of unease in Ajas's eyes with his own.

Of course, having lived as an adventurer, he couldn't claim to have a completely clean slate.

But... even so, that fluster looked far too suspicious to Rei's eyes.

"Ah, Rei-san! Where'd you go!? Over here, over here!"

As Rei walked through the forest, that voice reached his ears.

Turning his gaze toward the sound, Rei saw Rugluno teaching other adventurers about Treant Forest.

When he first started working, many looked down on Rugluno. But now... at least those around Rugluno didn't seem to think that way anymore.

(Guess he silenced them with his skills?)

A fair amount of time had already passed since the expansion work on Gilm began.

During that time, Rugurno, who had been guarding the woodcutters in Treant Forest, must have gained considerable expertise.

That said, the Treant Forest itself had changed considerably in this short period.

Initially, no monsters appeared at all, making the woodcutter escort duty almost nominal. In reality, they mainly worked alongside the woodcutters felling trees.

But gradually, monsters began to increase, and adventurers changed their tactics accordingly.

It was precisely because he possessed that know-how that Rugurno earned the respectful gazes of the other adventurers... or at the very least, he was no longer looked down upon.

"Seems things are going well over there. That's the most important thing."

"Ahaha. They're all skilled adventurers. If you explain things clearly and logically, they understand perfectly."

At Ruguruno's words, several adventurers listening nearby subtly averted their gaze.

Those were likely the ones who had looked down on Ruguruno simply because he had been humble.

But Rugluno had earned their respect through his actual skill.

"I see. Then the woodcutter's escort should go smoothly too."

"Yes. I hope the others see it that way too. ...Now, if only the others would come over here..."

Ruguruno muttered regretfully, his gaze turning toward the sound of an axe cutting wood.

If it were just a woodcutter chopping wood, the sound would be more rhythmic.

After all, the sounds coming from several locations had a steady rhythm.

Rei could easily guess who was producing the off-beat sounds.

It was likely those who had entered Treant Forest first earlier.

There was no doubt they were cutting down trees to earn a little extra money.

(Saying it's unfamiliar work... is easy, but they must just want the money that badly. However... the problem is, judging by this sound, they seem to be focusing solely on cutting the trees.)

Their original job was to guard the woodcutters; cutting trees was merely a side task.

Yet, the fact they were focusing on that side job made it seem dangerous to Rei in many ways.

No, if only the adventurers were getting caught up in danger, Rei wouldn't think much of it—they'd have only themselves to blame.

But if those adventurers caused the woodcutters to get attacked by monsters, the fallout would land on every adventurer who took this job.

Especially since Rei had pushed the guild to arrange this situation specifically to investigate the Ajash incident.

Given that, the guild's scrutiny of Rei would inevitably grow harsher.

Of course, there have been times before where lumberjacks got injured because monsters weren't completely defeated.

That's precisely why, even if the woodcutters were attacked, it wouldn't all be Rei's fault... But given Rei's position, and above all, the desire to finish the Gilm's expansion as quickly as possible, having the woodcutters suffer damage here was not ideal.

(Though, judging by their attitude earlier, I doubt they'd listen even if I warned them. ...Well, I'll just make sure they regret it later.)

In this case, the regret wouldn't involve Rei doing anything.

It simply meant that after the job was done, the woodcutters would report this incident to the guild.

If the woodcutters weren't harmed, the guild wouldn't punish adventurers for abandoning their assigned work to pursue personal gain.

But from then on, even if a lucrative job came along, those adventurers would likely find it difficult to get hired.

That was only natural. For the guild, prioritizing adventurers who diligently completed their work over others with comparable skill was only logical.

"Rei. Anyway, let's join the escort group too. Others are providing protection, but I don't think that alone will be enough personnel."

"...Is that so? Well, we've got our own things to handle over here. Let them do as they please over there."

Adventurers operate on a self-responsibility basis.

Whatever they gain is theirs to keep... but naturally, any major failure is theirs to bear as well.

And so, Rei and the others began moving, each to attend to their own tasks.
