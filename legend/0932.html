Two days had passed since Rei used the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour at night. Today was the academy's weekly day off, so Rei was walking through the streets of Glacial with Glink.

Originally, Rei had intended to go out alone to sightsee, but Erinde had insisted it was out of the question for Rei, who was being targeted, to venture outside the academy alone. Negotiations led to a compromise: Rei would go out with Glinck.

Glink's primary duty was classroom support, and daily life matters fell under Samaloon's purview... Unfortunately, while Samaloon was a mage, his physical abilities themselves weren't particularly high.

When it came to pure protection, Glinck, who trained his body as a warrior rather than relying on magic, was clearly the better choice. That's why Glinck was selected over Samaloon...

(Well, it's good that I can ask him about Graciall's unique rules if anything happens. ...But I really wanted to bring Set.)

Truthfully, if we're talking pure bodyguard duties, Set would be better than Glink.

However, bringing Set around Graciall carried a high risk of causing various problems and disturbances, so Set was left waiting at the stables today.

Set looked lonely upon learning he couldn't play with Rei, but he was eventually persuaded when told Rei would return before evening.

Of course, in exchange, I had to promise to bring back some delicious food as a souvenir.

"I'm pretty hungry right now. Any good places around?"

Asked by Rei, Glinck tilted his head, deep in thought.

Incidentally, Glinck was dressed as an adventurer today, likely because he saw himself as the escort. He wore leather armor and carried a long sword in a sheath at his waist, giving him the appearance of an adventurer at first glance.

Though taller than Rei, Glinck was still below average height. Yet no one dared bother him.

That was likely because his body was visibly covered in muscle.

Somehow, seeing Glink like that gave Rei the impression of a small tank...

This small tank of a man, Grink, spoke in his usual tone, polite and refined despite his appearance.

"Well, if you go straight down this main street, you'll find a section with several food stalls lining the roadside. Fortunately, the weather is good today, so there should be quite a few stalls out, don't you think?"

Not a cloud existed overhead, the sun blazing brightly. As Grink spoke, gazing at the clear winter sky, Rei also lifted his eyes toward the heavens.

The dazzlingly bright sky seemed to say it didn't care if Rei was being targeted by someone.

"Well, whatever. Let's go then. I'd like some warm food if possible."

"Aren't there any stalls serving cold food this time of year? It's cold, after all."

"Is that so? Precisely because it's cold, heating up the room and eating cold food in that warmth is actually quite nice, don't you think?"

"...Normally, such extravagance isn't possible. If anyone could afford that, they'd probably be nobles or royalty, wouldn't they?"

Hearing Glinck's words, Rei murmured to himself, "Now that you mention it..."

Back in Japan, when winter came, Rei would often warm himself by a wood stove in the living room or an electric heater in his own room, then eat cold ice cream in a room where the temperature was close to thirty degrees Celsius. But in Elgin, only a very select few could afford such luxuries.

However...

"Is that so? Well, I can do something similar thanks to my skill with fire magic. I can keep cold dishes in my item box, no problem."

Yes, Rei was an exception. Even in winter, he could drink chilled fruit water or eat well-chilled fruit.

To ordinary people, it was an incredibly enviable... no, perhaps because it was beyond imagination, it was hard to even tell if it was enviable or not.

"That is enviable. If Rei ever runs short on money, couldn't she make a business out of that?"

"If you ask if I could do it, I probably could... but business isn't really my thing."

Shrugging his shoulders slightly, he continued down the road until they reached the street Glinck had mentioned, lined with numerous food stalls.

"Wow... this is incredible."

Faced with the countless stalls before him—certainly over fifty in number—Rei let out a gasp of admiration.

Gilm had streets with many stalls too, but this clearly surpassed it in number.

(Still, the real issue with food stalls is the taste. No matter how many there are, if the food tastes bad, it's pointless.)

He muttered this bitterly to himself, likely because, deep down, Rei instinctively understood that Gilm was like a second home to him, a place he belonged.

"If there's anything delicious you'd recommend, could you tell me?"

"Hmm... For something warm, soup would be best. Especially soups packed with ingredients—they warm you up and fill you up nicely."

"Alright, I'll go with that."

Following Glinck's advice, Rei ordered several bowls of soup.

He had eaten a proper breakfast before leaving the staff dormitory, yet Rei kept ordering soup after soup, drinking it all down, finishing it completely, and then moving on to the next stall.

Naturally, such behavior drew attention, but Rei himself didn't seem to care at all.

He believed he could always handle whatever came his way, and besides, Glinck was there if anything happened.

...Though Glinck was actually troubled by how conspicuous the person he was supposed to protect was being.

Understandably so. The best course right now was for those targeting Rei not to discover them.

But the fact that his charge didn't seem to care at all about that was practically like telling the enemy to come find them.

Ignoring Glinck's worries, Rei heads toward the next food stall.

Glink had joined Rei for the first few bowls of soup, but now he was already full and just followed behind.

"Oh, you made it to my place? You're something else. ...Brother? Sister?"

"If I had to pick, I'd say big bro. ...One for me."

"Got it."

After exchanging a few words, the soup that arrived was quite different from anything Rei had tasted before.

The aroma wafting from it was different too.

"This... I've never seen this soup before."

"Ah, it's a specialty from my hometown. It's got a bit of an interesting flavor, so it's best to start by taking small sips at first."

Intrigued by the mention of an interesting flavor, he used the spoon provided to taste just the soup.

Instantly, Rei's eyes widened in surprise. She had lowered her hood to avoid it getting in the way while drinking.

"This... is it sour? No, spicy? Sour and spicy? It's a taste I've never had before."

"Right? It really divides people. But those who like it, love it. Well, that's why my stall is still going strong."

If Rei had any knowledge of Thai cuisine, he might have recognized it as Tom Yum Goong.

But having lived deep in the mountains of Japan, there was no way Rei had ever tasted tom yum kung. All he could do now was be amazed.

Though, generally speaking, tom yum goong is a dish with shrimp in it. What Rei was drinking now was a soup made with dried meat, so strictly speaking, it wasn't tom yum goong.

If anything, it resembled tom yum gai, made with chicken.

Having never eaten that type of dish before, Rei stared intently at the soup in the bowl.

The milky white broth made it impossible to see what ingredients were inside.

Still, it was undeniably a strangely lingering flavor.

(As for bread... Hmm, I wonder. It might go well, or it might not... If anything, noodles seem like they'd pair better.)

The sourness and spiciness both assert themselves fiercely, but the flavor of the base soup itself isn't bad at all.

No, rather, it was precisely because the fundamentals were solid that even a soup this sour and spicy could feel genuinely delicious.

"Yeah, it's good. I'm actually pretty into it."

"I'm surprised,"

It was not the shop owner who responded to Rei's words, but Glinck, standing behind him.

True to his words, he wore an expression of genuine surprise.

It was unusual for Glinck, who usually spoke politely but rarely showed much emotion.

"Is that so? ...Judging by your reaction, Grink?"

"I'm afraid I don't really..."

He stopped himself there and gave a small bow to the stall owner.

He probably thought the vendor might be offended, having been told the taste of his hometown was bad, albeit in vague terms. But the vendor just laughed and shook his head.

"Don't worry about it. Like I told that guy earlier, I know full well this flavor's got a strong kick. ...Well, back in my hometown, everyone drank it normally, so I was surprised to see it here in Graciall."

"Yeah, that happens."

Rei nodded at the stall owner's words.

Rei himself had lived in the countryside his whole life, but he had still gone on a school trip.

He remembered being shocked by various things back then: the chawanmushi wasn't sweet, the potato salad wasn't sweet, but the tamagoyaki was sweet.

...While sweet tamagoyaki often appears in manga and novels, so seeing it didn't surprise him that much, actually eating it felt quite strange.

It had a peculiar taste, different even from the egg used in sushi.

And what surprised Rei the most was probably the potato salad.

At Rei's house, sweet potato salad was the norm.

That's why the salty potato salad felt like a kind of culture shock.

As Rei recalled these memories and shared them, the shop owner must have sensed something familiar. He gave Rei a look, as if seeing a kindred spirit.

"Seems like your hometown has some dishes that aren't exactly mainstream elsewhere, huh?"

"I don't think it's that big of a deal... I guess it just comes down to where you grew up and stuff like that. Anyway, this soup is delicious. Can I get another bowl?"

"Sure thing."

Even if the taste isn't easily understood by others, it must still be gratifying to see the flavors of one's hometown appreciated. The shopkeeper smiled as he ladled out the soup, about to hand it to Rei...

"Get out of the way! Please, get out of the way!"

With those words, the stall shook, and the shock sent the bowl the shopkeeper was holding crashing to the ground.

Naturally, the soup spilled onto the ground too...

"Ah!"

Who uttered that word?

Was it Glinck, watching the unfolding events from behind? Or a passerby who'd been shoved from behind and crashed into the stall? Or the shopkeeper, whose plates hit the ground from the impact? Or perhaps... Rei, whose second helping had been swallowed by the earth instead of him?

In any case, the fact remained that the soup Rei had intended to drink was gone...

"Hmm."

One word.

The word that came from Rei's mouth was truly brief, yet everyone who heard it sensed something ominous.

It certainly wasn't imbued with any emotion.

Just a muttered word.

And yet, not only the stall owner and those nearby who had witnessed the entire sequence of events, but even Glinck himself sensed something ominous in that utterance.

"A grudge over food is terrifying. I'll teach you this saying the hard way. Glinck, sorry, but I'm going ahead."

Saying only that, he handed the stall owner a slightly larger amount of copper coins and ran off.

Glink could only watch the sequence unfold, but seeing Rei vanish before his eyes snapped him back to reality.

"Huh!? Rei-san!"

As Glinck vanished in pursuit of Rei, the stall owner—still reeling from the turn of events—and the surrounding crowd were left behind.

And only the disappointed expressions of the stall owners, who would likely have been Rei's next customers had this commotion not occurred.

"Haa, haa, haa... To think he'd send assassins all the way to Graciall. That uncle of mine... he really has no sense of restraint."

A man—or rather, someone somewhere between a boy and a young man—darted through the crowded streets, running as fast as he could to avoid dragging anyone else into his mess.

...No, to be precise, he was being cornered.

Every time he tried to escape, daggers flew as if anticipating his move, guiding him relentlessly in one direction.

Still, to survive, he had no choice but to follow that lead.

Running through the crowd, he soon found himself in some back alley.

The man's gaze fixed on the wall.

Completely cornered at the dead end of the alley, the man drew his longsword from its scabbard at his waist, trying to buy time and hold out until the guards who had been separated from him could catch up.

"Heh, don't get cocky, kid. Give it up."

"Yeah, yeah. Give us your life for our money."

"Kill him? Kill him? ...Kill him!"

"Hey, hey, calm down, you guys. Let's finish this quickly and get out of here. We've caused quite a commotion. They've probably seen our faces."

The four men declared this, weapons in hand, closing in on their target. At that moment...

"Well, well. I wondered who it was. That face looks familiar. ...Still, revenge over food is a terrible thing."

The voice echoed through the alleyway.
