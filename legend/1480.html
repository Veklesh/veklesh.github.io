"Ilse!? Why are you with Rei?"

Rei and Ilse, who had returned to Gilm from Treant Forest, gave the coachman a silver coin as a thank-you for letting them return the carriage. They debated whether to summon Set or go to the guild to report on Ajas...

It was then that they encountered... Melan, who had been commissioned to patrol Gilm.

Unfortunately, today's Melan seemed to be with a different group than Vihera, as she wasn't present.

(Well, if Vihera had been here, Melan might have picked on us even more. Though, considering the current situation, it probably wouldn't have made much difference. Still... Melan...)

Hearing that name, Rei suddenly remembered melons.

Not far from where Rei had lived in Japan was one of the region's premier melon-producing areas.

Every summer, he'd receive melons from a friend of his parents who lived there.

Seeing Melan and remembering melons was probably because it was summer now.

Of course, it wasn't just a one-sided gift; it was an exchange for vegetables from Rei's family farm that were misshapen, slightly bruised, or otherwise unsuitable for shipment.

Incidentally, the melons they received were naturally of the same non-marketable variety.

Incidentally, what surprised Rei when she heard about it before was melons... specifically the muskmelons grown in that region.

The more intricate the pattern on the outside of a muskmelon, the more expensive it becomes.

Indeed, it's not uncommon for the most expensive ones to cost tens of thousands of yen each.

But... that price difference is solely about the pattern. When it comes to actually eating them, even melons with patterns worth less than a thousand yen taste no different from those costing tens of thousands.

In other words, he was shocked to learn that the price difference was based solely on the outer pattern.

Of course, the taste probably varies depending on the farmer growing the melon, and some people might claim the pattern affects the flavor. But at least from the experience of tasting the high-end melon—albeit one with blemishes that made it unsellable—that Rei's parents' friend let them try, she couldn't detect any difference in sweetness.

It probably had something to do with Rei's tongue not being that sensitive to taste, though.

"Hey! Are you even listening?!"

Tired of listening to Melan, Rei had been lost in thoughts about melons, but the call snapped him back to reality. He turned his gaze toward the voice.

There, staring at him with obvious irritation, was Melan, of course.

Rei had been thinking about melons because he found Melan's words tedious, but that clearly didn't sit well with Melan.

He glared at Rei with intense eyes.

"Hey, Melan. Cut it out. I don't want to get into a fight with Rei."

"Yeah. Vihera told you too, didn't she?"

Several adventurers traveling with Melan told him, as if trying to reason with him.

This wasn't just out of concern for Melan...

They knew Rei showed no mercy to those he considered enemies.

If Melan got labeled as an enemy by Rei, and they got dragged into it and labeled as enemies too...

they understood that, given their own abilities, there was absolutely no way they could stand up to Rei.

That's precisely why they didn't want to get dragged into this with Melan. That was likely the honest motive behind trying to stop her.

But Melan might feel irritated by his comrades' attitude, yet he could never accept it.

"Don't mess with me! If Rei hadn't dragged Ilse around in the first place, none of this would have happened..."

That one line was probably the mistake.

To Ilse, Rei was a skilled adventurer with a reputation, even if younger than her.

Moreover, Rei was someone helping her with her revenge despite having nothing to gain from it.

Today, Rei had no obligation whatsoever to take Ilse to Treant Forest.

It was because of today's incident that Ilse became certain Ajash was her enemy.

To be precise, she had suspected him from the moment she first saw him at the First Sight Guild, but she lacked proof.

But thanks to Rei, she gained clear confirmation today that Ajas was indeed Ilse's enemy.

Moreover, it was significant that Rei, who had harbored doubts about whether it might be a case of mistaken identity, was also shown evidence—circumstantial, but evidence nonetheless—that Ajas was Ilse's enemy.

Despite everything Rei had done for her, Melan was blaming him.

And she was doing it because of him.

Even if they stayed at the same inn, in the end, that was the extent of their relationship.

That's precisely why Ilse couldn't forgive Melan's attitude.

"Shut up!"

The words that came out of Ilse's mouth weren't that loud.

After all, the people walking around weren't paying that much attention to Rei and the others.

...Though a few did gather nearby, thinking some sort of trouble had started, watching the scene unfold as if it were an amusing spectacle.

In any case, it was Melan who was surprised by the sharp retort that followed—not loud, but all the more cutting for it.

"Ilse, what the hell are you so angry about? I just think you're being dragged around by Rei against your will..."

He couldn't understand why Ilse was glaring at him so sharply.

If Melan had to put his honest feelings into words, that would be it.

Given Melan's fundamentally strong sense of justice, he had been blaming Rei based on his perception that Ilse was being forcibly dragged around by him.

Yet, he never imagined he'd be blamed by Ilse, the victim in this situation.

While he had a somewhat self-righteous streak, Melan had rarely been rejected this outright before.

That's precisely why Melan couldn't understand why Ilse was acting this way toward her.

But... to Ilse, Melan was nothing more than an obstacle getting in her way.

Especially now, with the presence of Ajas before her eyes, that feeling was likely amplified.

That's precisely why, under normal circumstances, Ilse might have been able to brush off Melan's words. Unfortunately, however, Ilse was somewhat agitated and had become aggressive.

...No, perhaps it's strange that she's only this upset, considering she just saw her family's killer.

"Don't interfere. What if Ajas escapes because of you?"

"...Ajas? Who's that?"

Though he was Ilse's enemy, Melan had never heard the name Ajas before.

That's precisely why Melan asked, "Who is that?" but...

"My family's enemy."

Quietly... Ilse murmured in a voice that those watching nearby couldn't hear, yet sent a chill down the spine of anyone who did catch it.

Fortunately, Set wasn't present.

That meant few could recognize Rei as Rei, dismissing it as just another quarrel between adventurers. Few were watching.

Had anyone been paying close attention to the exchange, they might have clearly heard Ilse and Melan's words.

In any case, it was fortunate for Ilse that few around her were observing the situation.

But... if they continued talking excitedly here, they would definitely draw attention from those around them.

Rei, realizing this, shifted his gaze toward the tavern a short distance away.

(Ideally, I wouldn't want anyone else to hear us, so we should probably talk in a room at the inn... but I can't imagine either of them accepting that.)

If he suggested going to his room, Melan would undoubtedly object. If he suggested going to Ilse's room, given her current state, he doubted she'd accept Melan. If he suggested going to Melan's room, Ilse might well refuse.

As a compromise, Rei suggested a neutral location... though calling it neutral might be a stretch—the tavern.

"Yeah, that's right. Yeah, I think that's a good idea too."

"Yeah. If we talk in a place like this, the guards might come... Plus, I don't think it's right for outsiders like us to intrude."

They probably didn't want to get involved in Ilse and Melan's conversation, and especially not in the trouble Rei was tangled up in. The other adventurers who had been patrolling the town with Melan all agreed.

The other adventurers, who hadn't actually spoken up, nodded in agreement with those words.

Anyway, with the others saying so, and though still in the middle of work, Melan decided to take a short break and headed for the tavern Rei had been staring at.

Leaving work unfinished wasn't exactly Melan's idea of fun either... but she must have decided it wasn't the time for that now.

"Huh... So this is how it is."

Perhaps because it was Rei's first time entering this particular tavern, Rei let out a slightly surprised gasp upon stepping inside.

That said, the interior wasn't much different from other taverns.

Despite it being daytime, a few customers were present, but that too wasn't an unusual sight in Gilm.

(Guess it was a good call to make this a tavern instead of a restaurant.)

Given it was daytime, if asked which would be busier, the tavern or the cafeteria, the cafeteria would likely be the answer.

That's precisely why he'd chosen this tavern when it caught his eye, and it seemed the number of patrons was lower than Rei had expected, which was a relief.

"What would you like to order?"

Probably because it's still not busy in the daytime. Instead of a waitress, the tavern owner himself came to take the order from Rei and the others at their table.

"No alcohol. Fruit water or tea, and something light to snack on for three people."

Normally, if you didn't order alcohol at a tavern, you'd often get hassled by the other patrons.

Fortunately, it was daytime, the tavern was sparsely populated, and no one was drinking excessively.

Also, even if those patrons didn't know Ilse and Melan, they knew Rei.

Picking a fight with Rei here would be asking for trouble.

Understanding this, they didn't dare make any foolish moves.

...Though, it seemed some were curious about what Rei, Ilse, and Melan were talking about, and a few were listening intently.

The owner placed the fruit water and roasted nuts on the table before returning to the counter.

After watching him leave, Melan was the first to speak.

"Ilse, I thought I heard something about revenge earlier?"

Melan, having had some time to calm down since their earlier conversation, reached for the fruit water and asked Ilse.

Ilse nodded without hesitation.

"Yes. I found the enemy of my family, someone I've been searching for years."

"Just to be clear, Ilse, you're not thinking of doing something foolish like revenge, are you?"

Melan asked cautiously, almost fearfully. But Ilse immediately replied.

"What are you talking about? Of course I am."

"Y-Yeah, right. That's going too far..."

Melan breathed a sigh of relief at Ilse's words, but froze at her next statement.

"Of course I'm going to avenge my enemies. That's why I became an adventurer in the first place."

"Wh-what?! ...Th-that's wrong! Revenge is wrong! You can't do it!"

Melan's voice echoed through the surroundings.

Naturally, it reached the other patrons in the tavern. Several of them turned their gaze toward Melan... and then toward Rei and Ilse.

Seemingly unaware of the stares, Melan stopped the hand reaching for her fruit water and slammed her fist on the table.

A dull thud echoed around the room.

Fortunately, this tavern was frequented by rough types like adventurers, so the tables were sturdy.

They were sturdy enough that even when Melan slammed his hand down with all his might, they wouldn't break.

But Ilse, showing no particular change in expression toward Melan, opened her mouth.

"Why?"

"Revenge is pointless. Besides, there's no guarantee the family, friends, or lover of the person you avenged won't seek revenge on Ilse in turn."

Those words were true. True, but... they were merely the opinion of someone whose family, friends, or lover had never been killed by another's malice.

In truth, Ilse found Melan's argument shallow.

In response to Melan's words, some patrons in the tavern nodded in agreement, while others wore expressions of scorn.

And Ilse...

"So what? I've decided to seek revenge. This resolve will never waver."

...she declared.
