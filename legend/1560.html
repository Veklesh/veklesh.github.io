The giant horde showed no concern whatsoever for their slain comrades... no, rather, they used the deaths of their own as fuel for their rage, blood boiling in their heads.

No matter how many of their own fell, they attacked without flinching.

If facing such a horde were an ordinary opponent, victory would be difficult.

But the ones facing these giants were the Crimson Wings led by Rei – or more accurately, with Elena added to their ranks.

No matter how much rage and murderous intent consumed the giants, they couldn't defeat opponents whose strength was absolutely beyond their reach.

Of course, overwhelmed by such powerful emotions, they wouldn't abandon their attacks even after taking a half-hearted hit.

Even so, against opponents of such vastly superior skill, the outcome was never in doubt.

No, rather...

"Ahaha, ahahahahaha! Fine, fine! Try enduring more!"

Attacking her never made her give up easily, and that fueled Vihera's high energy.

Vihera's attacks carried immense weight with each strike.

Even without using the Magic Palm, they could easily shatter an opponent's body.

But the giant who took that blow staggered and rose again.

Broken bones tore through flesh and ripped skin, yet the pain did not halt their movement.

For Vihera, they were nothing more than a good punching bag.

No, a punching bag doesn't attack back, so perhaps the term "sparring partner" is more accurate.

To those watching the scene, the giant could only be seen as pitiable.

"Whoa!"

Rei, who had been watching the fight between Vihera and the giant, noticed the giant, who had been lying on the ground with both knees shattered a short distance away, getting up.

More accurately, it would be described as the giant pushing himself up with both hands on the floor.

Regardless, the giant crawled toward Vihera on his hands.

The reason it was moving toward Vihera was likely purely because Vihera was the one who had shattered its knees.

Or perhaps it was simply because Vihera was the closest.

Whatever the reason, now that Rei had spotted it, letting it slip away was not an option.

Of course, Vihera wouldn't fail to notice an enemy crawling toward him, but there was always the possibility of something unexpected happening.

"Stay put and sleep!"

The Twilight Spear, released once more, sliced through the air, heading straight for the giant crawling along the ground... and in the next instant, it shattered the giant's skull.

Just like the first throw, the Twilight Spear's power didn't stop there; it embedded itself completely into the wall.

The first throw had slowed its speed somewhat, likely because it had dealt fatal blows to several giants.

But this time, only one giant had been felled, so its momentum was scarcely diminished... Ultimately, thanks to its sharp tip and Rei's brute strength, the Twilight Spear was effortlessly embedded into the wall.

Normally, extracting it would be difficult, but with the Twilight Spear, that worry was unnecessary.

The moment Rei focused his will, it returned to his hand.

"What's next... Are they still coming?"

The fifth basement level, the lowest floor of the underground facility housing the black crystal.

From the staircase leading there, another giant appeared.

Watching Marina fire wind blades created by spirit magic at the giant, Rei wondered.

(There should have been about fifty giants on the fifth basement level. And our preemptive strike should have killed about half of them, or at least left them with injuries severe enough to immobilize them. But...)

Glancing at the fourth basement level, now a battlefield against the giant horde, Rei saw a vast number of giant corpses.

Many had been reduced to piles of flesh, making it impossible to count exactly how many bodies lay there.

Even so, it was clear they had already taken down nearly twenty.

In other words, it should have been strange if the giants weren't already close to being wiped out by now.

Of course, since he hadn't counted them precisely, there was bound to be some margin of error.

Even so, the flow of Titans emerging from the fifth basement level showed no sign of stopping.

"Rei, something's wrong!"

Sharing the same doubt as Rei, Elena shouted while cleaving a giant in two with her Mirage-transformed longsword.

The sight of a giant over three meters tall split cleanly in two was shocking in many ways... yet no one present seemed particularly moved by it.

Everyone present knew just how powerful Elena was.

"Yeah, I agree. Ideally, I'd like to check out the fifth basement level..."

His words trailed off, not because another giant appeared on the stairs... but because Vihera was already fighting one to his heart's content near the staircase.

If anyone tried to interrupt Vihera's enjoyment of the fight, they might be mistaken for an enemy and attacked.

...Of course, it's not that Vihera would do that on purpose; it's more likely to happen purely due to the momentum of the fight.

Or, it was entirely possible that Vihera, high on the excitement of battle, would get carried away by the momentum...

But given the high likelihood that something was happening on the fifth basement level, ignoring it was simply out of the question.

"Well, what can you do? I'll go check on the situation down below. Fortunately, you should be able to handle the giant without me, right?"

At Rei's words, Elena nodded slightly, holding Mirage.

"No problem. Rei, I want you to check down below. Though, there's a good chance you'll get attacked by the giants on the way...!"

The whip-like Mirage lashed out, striking a giant positioned away from Vihera.

The giant, its right leg tendon cleanly severed, limped along... or rather, that limping likely drew Vihera's attention.

Vihera, magic claws sprouting from her gauntlets, closed the distance in one swift move, leaping to slash the giant's throat.

Naturally, the giant wasn't going to be taken down that easily. Blood gushed from its severed neck, yet it still tried to slam its fist down at Vihera, who was mid-air from the attack... But like a cat—or a leopard—Vihera twisted mid-air and kicked the giant's arm as it passed right beside her.

Even then, it didn't forget to slice the arm with the magical blade extending from the heel of its foot armor.

Landing silently on the floor, Vihera charged toward the giant, who showed no sign of dying even with his neck severed.

"Alright, now!"

Vihera moved from where she had been fighting earlier, chasing the giant from the staircase entrance.

Now, she could head to the fifth basement level without provoking Vihera.

Rei made that judgment and, without a moment's hesitation, began running at full speed.

The ability to seize a fleeting opportunity is absolutely essential for a top-tier adventurer... but Rei excelled in this particular skill.

He sensed opportunities almost instinctively and acted without delay.

This time was no different.

Sprinting toward the stairs, Rei weaved through the giants still standing.

Naturally, the giants would try to react if they saw something running beneath their feet...

"You think I'll let you?"

Marina's arrows pierced the giant one after another.

The giant, pierced by multiple arrows, turned its gaze not toward Rei, who had passed beneath its feet, but toward Marina, who had attacked it.

Given their already enraged state, it was only natural for the giants to direct their aggressive impulses toward the one provoking them further.

Similarly, Marina darted beneath the feet of several other giants and fired arrows at them too.

By the time all the giants hit by arrows had shifted their attention to Marina, Rei had reached the stairs leading to the fifth basement level.

But...bad timing. Another giant appeared from the stairs once more.

"Damn!"

With the distance to the stairs now negligible, slashing directly was faster than throwing the spear.

Making that judgment, Rei held Twilight Spear in his left hand while drawing Death Scythe from Misty Ring in his right.

Adopting his usual dual-spear stance, Rei closed the distance to the giant whose upper body was now visible and swung the magic-infused Death Scythe in a single flash.

He hadn't used any particular skill, but the blow from the Death Scythe possessed enough power to sever the giant's upper body.

In fact, the severed upper body rolled onto the fourth basement floor, while the lower half tumbled down the stairs.

As a result, the other giant that had been chasing the severed giant up the stairs collided with its fallen comrade's torso and tumbled down to the fifth basement level.

Taking many of their comrades down with them.

Reaching the base of the stairs, Rei fortunately descended the now-giant-free steps.

However, Rei's goal wasn't to move to the fifth basement level, but to observe the situation there.

He didn't descend the stairs all the way... but the sight that met his eyes the next instant made him visibly startled.

Because the number of giant corpses scattered across the fifth basement floor was clearly far too few.

He should have killed at least twenty or more with his preemptive strike, yet now fewer than ten corpses lay scattered across the floor.

Those fewer than ten corpses had also, for some reason, been moved very close to the black crystal.

For a moment, Rei wondered if surviving giants had carried the corpses there. But the scene before him immediately disproved that thought.

Because just seconds ago, the lower half of the giant Rei had severed moments before moved as if drawn toward the black crystal.

For a moment, Rei wondered if the momentum from tumbling down the stairs had carried it near the black crystal. But the way the giant's lower half moved was clearly unnatural.

It was as if it were being pulled by invisible strings or something... that kind of movement.

(What the hell?)

Fortunately, due to the giant's lower half falling earlier, the giants' bodies were no longer on the stairs.

Thanks to that, Rei could observe the giant corpses gathered—or rather, gathered together—near the black crystal without worrying about his surroundings.

Suddenly, black powder—no, something more like black snow—began to emerge from the black crystal.

That black snow fell upon the giant corpses... and in the next instant, necks sprouted from the giant corpses whose heads had vanished.

Yes, the expression "a neck sprouted" was indeed accurate.

Bones extended from the neck to form a skull, followed by the birth of a brain, eyeballs, teeth, and flesh. Finally, skin and body hair were produced.

Before I knew it, the corpse that should have lost its head had already become a beautiful form that hardly seemed like a corpse at all.

And the black snow continued to fall... until, gradually, the giant's fingers twitched slightly.

The giant, which should have been a corpse, began to move again.

At first, Rei thought it might be some kind of zombie-like undead, but from the looks of it, the giant didn't seem undead.

It seemed to him that the giant had been resurrected as a giant, nothing less.

(Bringing the dead back to life? Well, that's quite a rare magic item. If people knew about this effect, it wouldn't be surprising if folks from all over the world flocked here, or if someone tried to claim it for themselves. ...Well, it hardly seems like the predictable sort of thing, though.)

From Rei's perspective, that black crystal certainly didn't seem capable of resurrecting the dead.

Then again, with a giant actually being resurrected right before his eyes, that skepticism didn't hold much weight.

(If that black crystal's effect created the giant, then probably... it can only revive that giant, or something like that. There's likely some restriction.)

But... As this thought crossed his mind, Rei shifted his gaze toward the stairs leading up to the fourth basement level.

Numerous giant corpses currently lay on that fourth basement level, yet none showed any signs of revival.

(Meaning, the black snow is essential for the giants to revive. ...Then, for now, the top priority is to bring all the giants up to the fourth basement level and dispose of them.)

Without corpses on the fifth basement level, revival is impossible.

If that was the case, the task was simple.

Whatever that black crystal was, whether we destroyed it or seized it, the giants themselves were an obstacle.

Therefore, we should bring all the giants up to the fourth basement level and exterminate them there.

(The question is whether that black crystal possesses some means of self-defense... but from here, I can't tell anything about that. Whatever it is, separating the giants from the black crystal's vicinity comes first.)

Having reached that conclusion, Rei confirmed the giants that had been knocked down earlier were attempting to climb the stairs again. He cast one final glance at the black crystal before heading back down to the fourth basement level.
