"Huh?"

Upon hearing Rei's words, the third-year S-class students all looked back at him as if to say they couldn't comprehend what they'd just heard.

Though, even as they did so, the students' gazes were fixed on Set, who was sprawled out on the gym floor beside Rei.

Having played to his heart's content with the fourth-year S-class students this morning—from Set's perspective, at least—and feeling reasonably satisfied, Set now lay sprawled out without any particular excitement.

That said, this was also because Rei was speaking now. If told it was okay to play, he would have gladly gotten up.

Regardless, what the third-year S-class students should be thinking about now was the words spoken by the person standing before them, rather than Set.

The recently arrived instructor at this military academy, a Rank B adventurer with a notorious nickname.

The man who shattered the students' somewhat arrogant attitudes, not just breaking them but reducing them to splinters.

It hadn't been that long ago that they hadn't been able to land a single hit and felt their spirits nearly crushed.

Though, to be fair, it had only been about five days since Rei had arrived at this school.

But conversely, five days... With mock combat training held once daily, they'd been utterly crushed one-sidedly five times already.

If they hadn't been told that it wasn't just them suffering this fate, but that even the seniors in Class S of the fourth year hadn't managed to land a single scratch on Rei, they would have been completely broken.

"So, like I said earlier, today's mock battle class won't be in this gym. We're doing it outside. To be precise, it's not a mock battle—it's a snowball fight."

Finally, they seemed to understand Rei's words, snapping out of their frozen state of thought.

One of the third-year S-class students timidly raised their hand and spoke up.

"Um, well... sorry, Instructor. We came here for class, not to play around."

"Of course I know that. ...Just so you know, this isn't just any snowball fight."

Hearing that, the students frowned again, a sense of foreboding returning. They waited for Rei's next words.

(So this world has snowball fights too, huh. Well, I guess it's only natural to think of it as a game when there's snow around.)

He had heard about snowball fights from Glink and confirmed they existed.

Though Grink had been somewhat reluctant about the snowball fight... meaning using play instead of a mock battle.

Still, when Rei persuaded him, he eventually relented, albeit grudgingly.

"First, the enemies in this snowball fight are each other."

"Does that mean... we won't have to fight against Rei, Mr. Rei?"

Rei nodded in response to the same student asking the question again.

"That's right. You don't have to fight me."

Though his words clearly hinted at an ulterior motive, the prospect of having to ask any more questions—lest they risk being forced into a mock battle with Rei—made the student and the others fall silent.

"However... the team that came in last in the snowball fight will face rigorous training. Be prepared for that."

『...』

Hearing this, most students didn't look sad, but rather showed expressions of understanding.

It was only natural. They knew full well how grueling Rei's training was.

They had anticipated from the start that there was no way everyone would simply enjoy the snowball fight and call it a day.

"The rules for the snowball fight are simple. Everyone will form teams of ten, different from usual, and set up bases on the field. Each team will be given a flag. This one."

Saying this, Rei produced a flag: a wooden stick about one meter long with cloth attached.

"The team whose flag is knocked down or captured loses. With forty of us, we can form four teams. First, Team A versus Team B, then Team C versus Team D. The winners of the first round advance to the finals, and the losers compete for last place."

After explaining the simple tournament format, Rei glanced at the students, checking for any complaints.

But by then, the students were already exchanging glances, discussing who to put on their teams and what tactics to employ.

"Looks like you're all set. Alright, form your teams."

At Rei's words, everyone immediately sprang into action to assemble their teams.

Watching them, Rei stroked Set.

"This is quite a mean lesson, isn't it?"

Glink called out to Rei.

Initially, Grink had been somewhat opposed to the idea of incorporating snowball fight-like play into mock battle training. Still, he likely concluded that adding rules like preparing a flag and either capturing or knocking it down would make it effective group combat training. In the end, he accepted it.

"Is that so? Well, this is a lesson, after all. We can't just play around, can we?"

"...I suppose so."

Glink shifted his gaze to Set, who was stroking Rei.

There was no trace of fear in his eyes toward Set.

Normally, it would be impossible to shed fear so quickly when facing a griffin, but the fact that he could do it meant that, despite everything, he was indeed worthy of being entrusted with the role of mock battle instructor at this academy.

Then again, seeing how Set was being affectionate with Rei, it was easy to understand why there was no need to be so afraid.

While petting Set and conversing with Glink, the teams steadily formed... and within minutes, all teams were finalized.

After confirming this, Rei promptly distributed flags to each team and headed outside.

Naturally, Set came along too.

"Grrrrrrr"

Set purred happily at the falling snow.

He growled, wanting to run around in the snow himself, but Rei stopped him.

"Just wait a little longer. I'll let you play to your heart's content later."

"Grrr..."

Set growled disappointedly at Rei's voice, but remained obedient, likely because he understood Rei would never break his promise.

After patting Set's head, Rei casually assigned team names: A, B, C, and D.

"First, A and B fight. Next, C and D. Only snowballs are allowed for attacks. Direct physical attacks, magic, and skills are all forbidden. Anyone hit by snow is immediately eliminated. However, if you knock it off with your hand, you won't be disqualified. I'll judge whether it counts as elimination."

Simple, but precisely because of that, the rules were easy to understand.

Those who could use magic complained, but Rei ignored them and spoke up.

"Time limit: twenty minutes. If both flags remain after time's up, the team with more survivors wins. You get five minutes to set up your positions and decide your strategy. Begin."

With that, both Team A and Team B charged toward the field.

They planted their flags, then built walls of snow to protect them, or constructed fortress-like barriers to hide themselves.

Five minutes wasn't enough to build anything truly satisfying, but it was still far better than nothing.

Then, five minutes passed...

"Time's up. Begin!"

After a glance at the pocket watch taken from the Misty Ring, the group combat training, now renamed a snowball fight, commenced.

Of course, just because the starting signal was given didn't mean both sides would immediately spring into action.

Since the side with more survivors at the end of the match time would win, it was only natural to choose a combat strategy of holing up in their own territory and waiting for the enemy.

Furthermore, since they hadn't fully fortified their positions within the initial five minutes, reinforcing them was also a major objective.

As a result, even after the match started, neither side showed any signs of moving.

"So it came to this, huh?"

"Yeah. Just as Grink predicted. It's not like this choice is wrong, but..."

Perhaps because it unfolded as expected, Rei watched the situation develop without saying much.

So, both sides finished building walls of snow to protect their flags, yet still, no active engagement occurred.

Glancing at his pocket watch, he saw that ten minutes had already passed since the snowball fight began.

After checking the time, Rei addressed the snowball fighters.

"If nothing happens and time runs out, we'll consider both sides lacking sufficient fighting spirit. As punishment, we'll hold a mock battle. And for that mock battle, the opponents won't just be me... Set will be there too. If you're aware of that and still choose to act this way, then fine."

The moment those words left his mouth, both teams in the snowball fight sprang into action simultaneously.

They understood that the words just coming from Rei's mouth were not empty threats.

In every lesson held so far, everything Rei said he would do had been carried out exactly as stated.

Even if it seemed utterly ridiculous to most people, if Rei said it would happen, it would happen.

Their actions stemmed from that understanding.

Each side, armed with plenty of snowballs, advanced toward the enemy.

Once they closed the distance somewhat, they built a wall of snow as a makeshift shield.

Of course, neither wanted the other to pull such a stunt, so they threw snowballs to stop it. But having been in the S-Class for three years, they possessed skills that could be called elite even among third-years.

Moreover, their method of attack wasn't bows and arrows, but snowballs thrown with bare hands.

Naturally, their speed wasn't particularly fast, and even while building the wall, they could easily dodge them.

The adverse conditions of scattered snow likely played a part, but time passed without either landing a decisive blow.

Of course, not everyone remained unscathed.

A few were hit by snowballs due to moments of carelessness, slipping on snow-covered ground, or snow falling into their eyes and blocking their vision.

Still, time just kept passing like that...

"That's enough!"

The moment those words left Rei's mouth, Team A had eight survivors and Team B had six. Victory smiled upon Team A.

"We did it! We made it through! At least we're not dead last now!"

"We did it! We did it! We survived!"

The Team A students shouted in joy.

And in stark contrast to the victorious Team A stood the defeated Team B.

"Nooooooooo! This can't be! This can't be!"

"Calm down! As long as we're not last, it's fine! We still have a chance. We just need to win the next battle!"

"Y-y-y-yeah. C-c-calm down. If you get so worked up..."

"First of all, you calm down."

The students of Team B, their faces drained of color, grieved over imagining what would happen if they lost the next snowball fight... no, the next battle.

Watching this scene of mixed emotions, Rei shifted his gaze toward Teams C and D.

"Alright, Teams A and B, move to the sides. Next up are Teams C and D."

Following Rei's instructions, each took the action they should.

Twenty students faced each other on the snow-covered field.

Watching them, Rei shouted loudly.

"The match begins!"

At his command, both teams charged forward simultaneously—all except the flag bearer and one escort.

They likely understood from the match that any attempt to stall for time would result in a painful penalty.

They immediately grabbed handfuls of snow and hurled them at their opponents.

Some students didn't throw snowballs themselves, focusing solely on making them.

(They've become quite efficient at this.)

Rei murmured inwardly, impressed. But if the students knew, they'd likely shout loudly, 'It's all your fault!'

Or perhaps, "Thanks to you."

It was unclear which it would be, but there was no doubt that Rei was the reason they were acting this way.

"Dammit! We'll win! We absolutely will win! We won't lose!"

"Go ahead and lose! It's fine, even if we lose here, there's still another match!"

"If you say so, then you guys lose!"

"DIEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEEE

"Die, you bastards!"

"We won't lose! We won't lose! We won't lose! We won't lose!"

The battle, fought in an atmosphere so grim you'd never guess it was a snowball fight, was going well for Team D, whose members were focused solely on making snowballs.

Team C dwindled: one, two, three, four, five.

Of course, Team D wasn't unscathed either.

Several had already been hit by snowballs and dropped out.

Especially those who tried to flank the flag directly, thinking the enemy's numbers were dwindling, only to be caught in concentrated fire the next moment and eliminated.

And so time passed...

"Alright, twenty minutes. That's it..."

That's it. Just as Rei was about to say that, he twisted his body almost reflexively.

Simultaneously, an arrow flew in from nowhere, piercing the space where Rei had been a moment before, embedding its tip deep into the snow.
