The words that came out of Marina’s mouth.

Upon hearing Rei say that the core of the dungeon he had created was undoubtedly from the dungeon generated on Golz’s side, Vanillas finally let out a small sigh.

……If Rei had been the one to state this with such certainty, Vanillas wouldn’t have been so quick to believe it.

It wasn’t that Rei wasn’t trustworthy; it was simply that Marina, the former guildmaster, commanded such high credibility.

“So this is the dungeon core. I’ve seen pictures of it before, but this is the first time I’ve actually seen one with my own eyes. …But why is it broken?”

“Because I cut it.”

“……Did you have to cut it?”

“I think it probably had to be. Also, now that we’ve brought the dungeon’s core here like this, we don’t know what will happen to that dungeon, so we should be careful. The biggest concern is that the space might become distorted, which could cause the cliff to collapse.”

At the mention of a cliff collapsing, Vanillas’ expression twisted.

That’s only natural. If a cliff that massive were to collapse, the damage to the surrounding area would be quite severe.

Besides, depending on the situation, it could even affect the ecosystem around that cliff.

“Right. In that case… no, before that, could you tell me what the dungeon was like inside? I heard there were those rock plants and an area that looked like a forest, but…”

Vanillas had been thinking about the impact the dungeon’s disappearance would have on the surrounding area, but he must have decided that gathering information about the dungeon itself came first.

With a serious expression, Vanillas fixed her gaze on him, and Rei began to recount his experiences inside the dungeon.

The monsters he encountered in the forest, the space filled with countless rock plants, the hidden door, and the passageway leading from it that was rigged with a multitude of traps.

And then, the massive underground lake and the Galangiga, a monster that, despite being a Rank C, possessed the strength of a Rank A monster.

Despite the Garangiga’s formidable strength, thanks to their combined combat power, they managed to defeat it without any fatalities or serious injuries, though they did sustain some minor wounds.

In the midst of the battle with Garangiga, they discovered the dungeon’s core at the bottom of the subterranean lake. After defeating Garangiga, they used Marina’s spirit magic to drain the lake’s water and successfully destroyed it.

After that, since the dungeon was spatially distorted, they didn’t know what would happen now that the core had been destroyed, so they hurried to escape… and that’s why they’re here in Golz now.

It was quite a rushed explanation, but after explaining what had happened in the dungeon, Rei took a sip of the tea the receptionist had brought over mid-story.

“Well, that’s about it.”

“Right. …I thought this the last time I heard about it too, but from what you’re saying, there’s no way that dungeon could have been newly created.”

“It was that big, and there were forests and everything. …I’ve cleared a dungeon that was genuinely brand-new before, but compared to that one, it was incredibly easy to clear.”

“Ah, I’ve heard about that. Last year… wait, was it the year before last? A dungeon appeared near Gilm, and for a while, guards and such were reassigned there. Did Rei clear that dungeon?”

Relieu, who had been listening silently to Rei’s story, spoke up as if suddenly remembering.

“Yeah. Thanks to that, I managed to take down a ton of Gamelions in the dungeon, too. Come to think of it, it was a pretty lucrative dungeon.”

“...So that Gamelion incident was your doing, too?”

Relieu looked exasperated, but Vanillas clapped her hands lightly to draw Rei and the others’ attention, then spoke.

“The information about the dungeon was a big help. So… you brought back the Galangiga’s corpse, right?”

“Yeah. We went through the trouble of defeating it, after all. It looks like it’ll be pretty useful in all sorts of ways.”

Two Kelpies and one Galangiga.

Honestly, considering learning skills through Beast Magic, Rei would have preferred to have more monster magic stones.

That said, since there’s no denying that spending too much time on it would have led to all sorts of trouble, she figures it can’t be helped, even if she is a bit dissatisfied.

“Oh, Kelpies too? …I heard there was a forest, so that makes sense. So, what are you planning to do about skinning and butchering the monsters?”

“Well, what can I say? For now, I was thinking of placing a request at the guild… Gilm’s guild later, as a winter job.”

Of course, I’ll have to extract the magic stones first before posting the request.

Just like the Garangiga, the Kelpie is actually quite large.

For Rei and the others to handle the butchering and skinning on their own… it wouldn’t be impossible, but it would definitely take a long time.

Given that, the decision to have them butchered at Gilm along with the other monster carcasses that have been piling up is surely not a mistake.

Since the guild is covering the costs to have the monsters processed anyway, I figured Walker would probably be okay with including the Kelpies and Galangigas in the batch.

After all, this dungeon run was based on a request—no, a favor—from Walker, so a little flexibility should be allowed.

That’s what Rei thought, and if that didn’t work, he planned to have Marina handle the negotiations and see what he could get out of them.

(We managed to acquire quite a few orcs, after all.)

The orc settlement in the dungeon forest ended up being quite a sizeable group, all things considered.

To ordinary adventurers—especially those active in Golz—an orc settlement is nothing but a threat.

But to Rei and his group, an orc settlement was nothing more than a place to restock on meat.

Of course, it would have been a different story if the settlement had contained high-ranking or rare species, such as the Orc King… Fortunately, the settlement in the forest was home only to ordinary orcs.

As for Rei, he did have a secret hope that an Orc King might be there.

Rei had only defeated a single Orc King so far, and Set had absorbed its magic stone.

So, he wanted the magic stones that the Death Scythe would absorb… and above all, an Orc King’s meat is incredibly delicious.

Even the meat of an ordinary orc is quite delicious, so just imagine how delicious the meat of an Orc King—a monster of a much higher rank—must be…

It’s such exquisite meat that it wouldn’t be surprising if he wanted to go searching for nothing but the Orc King.

There’s a term called “carnal desire,” but in this case, it’s not in the general sense—it’s an opponent that makes you feel a carnal desire in the sense of appetite.

“Would you mind letting us handle the butchering of the monsters you defeat in the dungeon?”

“...To the adventurers of Golz? Why on earth?”

Rei didn’t really mind the idea of entrusting the butchering of monsters to the adventurers of Golz.

But since there’s no real need to do something like that in Golz, Rei had been thinking he’d wait until they returned to Gilm… which is precisely why Banillas’ proposal came as quite a surprise.

It seemed Rei wasn’t the only one who felt this way; the others also shot questioning glances at Vanillas, wondering what she was thinking when she said such a thing.

“Why, you ask…? Rei and his group cleared a dungeon that we couldn’t handle on our own, didn’t they? So, I think it’s only natural to show our gratitude. Oh, and since this is an act of gratitude, there’s no need for a fee or anything like that.”

“...What do you think?”

Marina looked at Rei, who was asking her, thought for a moment, and then spoke.

“I don’t really mind, you know? It’ll just mean we’ll get back to Gilm a little later, though…”

“Huh?”

Relieu spoke up, cutting into Marina’s words.

Naturally. Returning to Gilm later meant that his reunion with his wife, Shumine, would be delayed.

For Relieu, it was a stroke of luck that they had cleared the dungeon—their original objective—far sooner than expected.

Although he had been clearing the dungeon alongside Rei and the others at Daskar’s request, it was still painful to have to live apart from his beloved wife, Shumine.

However, as a result, the dungeon run ended far sooner than Relieu had initially anticipated.

Relieu had hoped to return to Gilm before winter… before the snow fell, but because he had finished the dungeon so quickly, he thought he would be able to return to Gilm around the middle of autumn, rather than after the snow had fallen.

Yet, if he had to dismantle the Kelpie and the Garangiga here… it would delay his return to Gilm by a day or two… or possibly even longer.

For Relieu, that was a situation he wanted to avoid at all costs.

...Of course, that was purely Relieu’s situation; for the others, there were many benefits to accepting Vanilas’s offer.

For Rei, in particular, the ability to secure Kelpie and Galangiga meat immediately was a major advantage.

Rei had eaten kelpie meat a few times before, and its unique, fluffy texture was an addictive flavor.

If they were to wait until returning to Gilm to butcher them, they’d have to wait until winter, when adventurers’ work dries up.

If he really wanted to eat it, there was always the option of butchering the Kelpie himself… but once he returned to Gilm, Rei would be quite busy and would need to focus on the expansion construction.

Given that, Rei concluded it would be best to leave it to them if they could butcher it here.

(Though, if I take too long, Relieu might explode with frustration, so I’ll have them butcher only the kelpie and the garangiga in Golz, and the orcs in Gilm. Ah, but since they’re doing the butchery for me anyway, I suppose it wouldn’t hurt to hand over an orc or two.)

There were orc corpses that had fallen from the dungeon, but there was no doubt that the demand for orc meat was still high.

In that case, handing over one or two orcs—who have plenty of corpses to spare—wouldn’t be much of a problem.

In fact, if they’re willing to go out of their way to butcher them for just one or two orcs, that’s exactly what Rei would want.

“Sorry about this, Relieu. I think I’ll take you up on that offer.”

“Ugh… Fine, I guess I have no choice. Just make sure you get it done as soon as possible, okay?”

The reason he didn’t say he’d go back on his own was simply because using the Set Cage to travel would be overwhelmingly faster than running to Gilm himself.

Even if Relieu went back alone now, the ones who would end up arriving in Gilm first would still be Rei and the others, once they’d finished dismantling the Kelpie and the Garangiga.

Knowing that, Relieu quietly backed down.

……Finishing the dungeon run unexpectedly early probably gave Relieu a little breathing room, too.

“Ah. And, you’re Shumine, right? Why don’t you buy your wife a souvenir? Since you’ve gone all the way out of Gilm anyway.”

“Oh? Oh, now that you mention it, you’re right. A souvenir, huh. Hmm, what do you think she’d like?”

At Rei’s suggestion that he should buy a souvenir, Relieu immediately began to agonize over it.

It wasn’t as if Relieu had never bought any kind of souvenir when he went outside Gilm before.

But for Relieu, a devoted husband, buying a souvenir that would please his wife, Shumine, was clearly a major event.

“Well, don’t ask me. I don’t even know this Shumine person, so there’s no way I could possibly know what kind of souvenir would make her happy. How about buying something that’s a local specialty of this town? Even if it’s food, it won’t go bad since we have Misty Ring.”

“That’s it!”

At Rei’s words, Relieu laughed with genuine joy.

She must have thought that if she bought some kind of souvenir in Golz and brought it back, Shumine would probably be happy.

(She’s easy to handle… but with Relieu, if you misjudge the balance, there’s a chance she’ll explode.)

For example, if someone tried to take Shumine hostage, Relieu would undoubtedly lose control and either kill the person responsible… or, depending on the situation, do something even worse than killing them.

Even if it didn’t go that far, there was no telling how she would react if someone carelessly made contact with Shumine.

With that in mind, Rei decided not to return immediately, but instead to get Relieu’s approval to dismantle the Kelpie and the Garangiga in Golz.
