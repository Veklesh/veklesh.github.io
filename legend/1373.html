"Rei, are you here!?"

The voice echoed through the dining hall of Dusk Wheat inn.

Outside, twilight was beginning to fall—the perfect hour for dinner or banquets. ...Though, to be fair, some had been drinking since noon.

Still, the Dusk Wheat inn was fundamentally a dining hall, not a tavern.

Of course, they served alcohol, but it was strictly as an accompaniment to meals.

The rule at Dusk Wheat inn was that those who wanted to drink and make noise should go to the tavern and do as they pleased.

Even so, when mealtime rolled around, customers beyond the inn's lodgers gathered at the dining hall. That was surely because the food served at Dusk Wheat inn was simply delicious.

Even now, many customers were enjoying their meals.

It was to such a place that Kenny arrived.

Among the surrounding patrons who heard the cry were adventurers, and naturally, some recognized Kenny.

Kenny was originally a receptionist who enjoyed immense popularity. Few adventurers wouldn't recognize her face.

"Kenny-chan, what's up? If you're up for it, grab a drink with me..."

"Yeah, yeah, sorry. I can't right now. Where's Rei?"

The adventurer who'd tried to invite Kenny for a drink wasn't met with her usual easygoing response. Instead, she looked desperate, her face flushed slightly from alcohol but wearing a serious expression.

As an adventurer, he had plenty of opportunities to drink. That's precisely why a small amount of sake like what was served at the Dusk Wheat inn wouldn't get him drunk, even if he drank it.

...Though, naturally, the alcohol was affecting his thinking.

"Something happen?"

"Yeah. So, what about you, Rei?"

As if deciding he couldn't say more, the man silently directed his gaze toward the back of the dining hall.

Following his gaze, Kenny saw Rei, Vihera, Byune... and even Marina sitting together, eating amicably. His eyebrows twitched.

Since Rei and the others formed the party Crimson Wings, it wasn't strange for them to be eating together.

Especially since everyone except Marina was staying at this inn.

(But then, why is Marina here?)

Kenny knew Marina owned a mansion in the Noble District.

So why Marina? He couldn't help but feel a little annoyed.

Even if she understood the logic, as a maiden harboring feelings for Rei, the sight before her eyes stirred various thoughts.

But she quickly remembered that it was her—not Lenora, but her cat beastman self with superior athleticism—who had been dispatched. She moved closer.

Lenora wasn't exactly clumsy, but in terms of pure physical ability, she still couldn't match Kenny, the cat beastman.

And by nightfall... meaning this time when adventurers returning from work outside the city began crowding the streets, using a carriage wasn't an option.

That was precisely why Kenny, purely because he was the fastest on foot, had been asked to go to this location.

He had his own thoughts about the current situation, but fulfilling his job came first.

"Rei-kun?"

"...Hmph. Kenny? What's up all of a sudden?"

Rei, who had swallowed meat that had been simmered for a long time and fell apart the moment he chewed it, spoke up when he saw Kenny approach the table.

Normally, Kenny would smile and engage in conversation when Rei spoke to him.

He wouldn't forget to show off his charm either.

But the Kenny standing before Rei now did none of that. He opened his mouth with a serious expression.

"Rei, I have an urgent request."

Hearing Kenny's serious tone, Ray must have realized this wasn't a situation for joking around.

Rei also adopted a serious expression and spoke again.

"Sounds like a pretty dangerous request. ...What exactly is it?"

For a moment, Ray wondered if it was okay to discuss an urgent request in this crowded cafeteria. But Kenny spoke regardless of the surroundings.

It seemed there was no time to worry about that now.

"The woodcutters left Gilm a little while ago. They're probably headed for Treant Forest, so I need you to go meet them immediately."

"...Huh?"

Rei let out a dumbfounded sound at Kenny's words.

It was common knowledge that monsters became more active at night.

And this was Gilm, on the frontier. The monsters lurking nearby were incomparable to those found in ordinary villages or towns.

But what shocked Rei most was that the woodcutters had headed for Treant Forest.

"Didn't you tell them about the dangers of Treant Forest?"

"Of course I did. But... either the woodcutters didn't believe it, or they think they can handle anything that happens..."

Seeing Kenny silently shake his head, Rei couldn't say anything more.

Even though they understood the danger, why would they do something so foolish?

Rei couldn't comprehend it at all.

Of course, being woodcutters who wielded axes daily, their strength was above average. Strong, yes... but that strength was for cutting wood, not for fighting.

Moreover, Treant Forest became an extremely dangerous place at night.

After all, two parties had already been wiped out.

And that was only what was currently known.

In reality, it wouldn't be surprising if many more adventurers had been attacked... no, devoured by the forest.

"Anyway, that's why I need you to hurry and rescue the woodcutters. Normally, the main gate would be closed by now, but the Guildmaster granted special permission."

The document handed over stated, as Kenny had said, that the gate would be opened with the Guildmaster's permission.

Helpfully, it even bore Daskar's signature.

(Well, opening the main gate at this hour... Walker couldn't have done it alone... not that I'm saying he couldn't, but better safe than sorry, I suppose.)

Staring regretfully at the stewed meat before him, Rei stood up.

Honestly, he had mixed feelings about the woodcutters who'd thrown themselves into a death trap.

But still, when considering what to do with Treant Forest going forward, it was absolutely undeniable that a decrease in the number of woodcutters was a bad thing.

To begin with, the number of woodcutters in Gilm wasn't that high compared to ordinary towns.

This was because the woodcutters' work—felling trees—required them to venture outside Gilm.

Monsters appearing in ordinary forests and woodlands during the day, let alone the Treant Forest, were not uncommon occurrences.

While not as dangerous as adventuring, few were willing to take on such work.

For that very reason, losing even a few of the already scarce woodcutters in a place like this was something to be avoided.

It wasn't that he didn't think it was foolish, but right now, saving those woodcutters came first.

As Rei stood up, Marina, Vihera, and Byune also rose to their feet.

"Huh? You're coming too?"

"Yes. After all, we're going to escort the woodcutters, right? Or is it a rescue? Either way, more people are better, don't you think?"

When Vihera asked Kenny, he immediately nodded in agreement.

"From what the guard at the main gate said, it seems over ten woodcutters went out."

"See? It'd be tough for just Rei and Set to handle that many. Luckily, Marina and I can cling to Set's legs to move. ...Byune, you stay behind."

"Hmph!"

At Vihera's words, Byune protested.

He was likely dissatisfied because he had expected to go too.

But Vihera continued, speaking to Byune as if to persuade her.

"Marina and I have tried clinging to Set's legs and flying several times before. But you haven't flown clinging to Set yet, have you, Byune?"

"...Hmm."

Unable to counter that, Byune fell silent.

(Someone as small as Byune could probably ride on Set's back and fly... but maybe it's better not to say that.)

In terms of pure combat strength, Byune wasn't particularly strong.

Well, after winter training, Byune had become reasonably capable as a thief, but that was only compared to the average thief.

Compared to Vihera, Marina... and above all, himself, Rei felt Byune's combat strength was practically negligible compared to the other thieves.

If the enemy monster were clearly identifiable, it might be different, but the Treant Forest was notoriously ambiguous in that regard.

From what Slesha had said, it seemed likely that the enemy was indeed a Treant, as the name "Trent Forest" suggested... but considering the existence of the party that had apparently been attacked yesterday, it was difficult to think that way.

That they'd been attacked yet managed to avoid alerting Set to their presence wasn't something a Treant could pull off.

"Sorry, Byune. You stay here. If any new information comes in, I expect word will reach this inn, so I'll need you to receive it."

I had doubts about whether Byune could actually receive such information... or more accurately, whether she could communicate with whoever brought it. But for now, at least, I decided to tell Byune that.

Whether Byune noticed Rei's attitude or not... he couldn't tell, but even so, she didn't voice any objections and simply nodded in response.

Seeing this, Rei stood up again.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, neither Marina nor Vihera had drunk any alcohol yet today.

Since Rei himself didn't care for alcohol, it was probably for the best that Marina and Vihera also tended to drink sparingly.

Rei wouldn't dream of taking anyone who'd had even a little to drink into Treant Forest, where anything could happen.

With that settled, Rei addressed the adventurers seated at a nearby table—men who made the Dusk Wheat inn their regular haunt and were acquaintances of his.

"If you don't mind, you guys can have this food over there."

"Huh? Is that okay? Well, if you're offering, we'd be happy to take it."

The food on the table where Rei and his companions had been sitting hadn't been touched much.

Some dishes hadn't been touched at all.

Some people absolutely refuse to eat food that's been touched, but fortunately, these adventurers weren't particularly picky about that sort of thing.

After all, sharing food with others is commonplace when working as an adventurer.

In fact, anyone who disliked that sort of thing probably couldn't function as an adventurer.

"Ah. Unfortunately, we have business to attend to. ...Everyone, get yourselves ready. Marina..."

Rei cut himself off.

Marina was currently dressed in her usual party dress.

She wasn't wearing pants like she had when she and Rei had clung to Set's legs heading into Treant Forest before.

Of course, unlike daytime, night was dark.

Even if she flew through the air in a party dress, there was no worry of being seen from below.

...Even so, her reluctance to fly through the sky in a party dress was likely a matter of Marina's pride as a woman.

Moreover, a party dress is simply unsuitable for flying, even for a short time.

But if she were to change clothes here and head to Marina's mansion in the noble district, it would take a considerable amount of time.

At the very least, with the woodcutters' lives on the line, spending time on such things was something to be avoided.

Rei hesitated slightly, but Marina shook her head at him.

"I have several outfits stored in Vihera's room, so I can get ready quickly too. I carry my bow and arrows with me as well."

While she had worked as a guildmaster before, Marina was now an adventurer.

Given that, it was only natural she'd be ready to act immediately when the need arose.

"Alright, then. ...Kenny, who's leading those woodcutters?"

"His name is Fekts. He's a central figure among the younger woodcutters, well-respected and skilled at his craft. ...But..."

Kenny hesitated, finding it hard to speak, but deciding it was necessary to share the information since they were about to go help this Fekts, he opened his mouth again.

"Well, he's apparently notorious for hating adventurers."

"...Ah."

Kenny's words brought back memories of that day.

He remembered the woodcutter who had glared at the adventurers.

He also recalled being called "Fekts" by his companions, confirming his suspicion that it was indeed that man.

"Got it. I remember his face, so that's fine. Though, if he really hates adventurers, it could get complicated..."

"I hear it's okay to be a bit forceful about it. Make safety your top priority."

Nodding at Kenny's words, Rei began taking action to prepare.
