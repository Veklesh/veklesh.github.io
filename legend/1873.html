When Elena and the others arrived in front of the house, they saw several people lying on the ground.

Unsurprisingly, there were far more people who appeared to be members of the organization than soldiers or knights.

On one side were soldiers and knights who underwent rigorous combat training on a regular basis; on the other were members of the underworld who, while accustomed to rough and tumble, did not train every single day.

It was only natural that such a clash would lead to this outcome.

“That’s strange,”

Elena murmured softly as she looked at the scene before her.

Rei and Ara and the others realized what Elena was getting at and nodded in agreement.

“That’s true. Even though it’s such a one-sided battle… for some reason, that house still hasn’t fallen.”

Rei murmured.

In fact, there was certainly no mistake in those words.

From an outsider’s perspective, there was no doubt that the Seisor Marquis family’s military strength was overwhelming.

After all, the Seisor Marquis family holds significant influence even within the Noble Faction, so while their military strength may not match that of the Kerebel Duke family, it is still among the largest among the nobles of the Noble Faction.

They haven’t brought their entire force with them to Anesis this time.

However, precisely because of that, many of the soldiers and knights who came along as escorts could be called elite.

It’s not as if every soldier is like that, though—as was made clear by the soldier who tried to stop Elena and the others from proceeding just a moment ago.

“Who’s there!”

He must have noticed Rei and the others. A sharp voice rang out, just like the soldiers’ earlier.

The voice belonged to a knight who appeared to be in his mid-thirties.

Thanks to the moonlight and the bonfires burning all around, even someone without Rei and Elena’s keen night vision could make out his face.

He was likely a high-ranking figure among the forces of the Seisor Marquisate attacking this place.

For that very reason, it was only natural that upon seeing prominent figures like Elena and Set, he immediately recognized who they were. …Though Rei probably only realized it because Set was there.

“Lady Elena… Crimson? My apologies. But why are you in a place like this? And with such a large entourage, no less.”

“My objective is that house as well.”

Hearing this, the knight’s face twisted in a grimace.

He had suspected as much.

Otherwise, there would be no reason for famous figures like Elena and Rei to be here like this.

Well, not that there couldn’t be another reason, but that was still the most likely explanation.

“But, as you can see, we’ve already eliminated most of the people in this house.”

“...Yet, for all that, it doesn’t look like you’ve managed to take the house yet?”

“Guh…”

Perhaps he’d been hit where it hurt; the knight was at a loss for words.

In fact, Elena’s words were not wrong.

As for those surrounding the house to defend it, although the Marquis of Seisor’s forces had suffered some casualties, the situation could be described as a near-total victory.

But… that’s where the problem lies.

The moment they entered the house—no, the moment they stepped onto the grounds—various traps were triggered.

Soldiers and knights were, after all, meant for frontal combat and were incapable of disarming traps like a thief.

Well, perhaps some who had been adventurers before becoming soldiers or knights might have possessed such skills… but unfortunately, there were none such among them.

As a result, unable to disarm the traps, they fell into them one after another.

Furthermore, even if they triggered a trap once, that wasn’t the end of it.

I don’t know what method is being used, but a trap that has been triggered once is reset the very next moment.

Sometimes it wasn’t the same trap at all, but a completely different one; other times, it remained exactly the same.

As a result, many soldiers were wounded simply by trying to enter the house.

The trouble is that while these injuries are severe, they are not fatal.

Thanks to this, while there have been no fatalities, a significant number of troops are tied up transporting the seriously wounded to the rear or treating them.

The honest truth was that this had led to a shortage of manpower, leaving them stuck, unable to storm the house.

A few lucky ones managed to reach the house, but the moment they opened the door and stepped inside, they were severely injured by the traps. They either died on the spot or somehow made it back to their comrades to have their wounds treated.

As a result, the current situation is nothing short of an overwhelming struggle for the forces of the Marquis of Seisor.

“If that is the case, I don’t see any problem with us going out, do you? After all, we are supposed to be acting on orders from my father… Duke Kerebel. Given that, any attempt by Anesis to hinder our actions would not serve the interests of the Seisor Marquis family.”

Faced with Elena’s assertive statement, the knight was at a loss for words.

This was because Elena’s words were, in fact, correct.

If they were to forcibly—literally use force—to stop Elena’s actions here, it would undoubtedly be a major disadvantage for the Seisor Marquis family.

It was unreasonable to expect the knight—who was merely one of the commanders, not the one in overall charge—to make such a judgment.

Well, he might be able to force a decision if he tried, but he absolutely did not want to be held responsible for such an action later on.

Leaving the knight, who couldn’t say a word to Elena, as he was, Elena addressed the forces of the Duke of Kerebel’s household.

“From now on, the three of us—me, Rei, and Ara—will storm that house. The rest of you should surround the house to ensure no one escapes if anyone does manage to get out. And…”

Having said that much, Elena glanced at the knight who had fallen silent, then continued.

“Also, there are many injured among the Marquis of Seisor’s men. Please tend to them as best you can.”

“Is that all right?”

In response to Elena’s words, Ara asked briefly.

From Ara’s perspective, this incident amounted to nothing less than the Seisor Marquis family running amok.

Despite that, was it really okay to help them like this? It was only natural for her to think so.

But Elena replied that there was no problem with Ara’s concern.

“Regardless of this incident, it wouldn’t be good for the Seisor Marquis family’s fighting strength to be weakened. Given that, it’s better to treat them. …Besides, it’ll earn us some goodwill.”

Hearing that, Ara had no reason to refuse.

She immediately took over for Elena and began issuing instructions one after another.

(Huh.)

Seeing Ara like this, Rei is slightly surprised.

The Ara Rei knew was someone who was absolutely head over heels for Elena.

But seeing her now, issuing orders one after another, he thinks to himself, “No wonder she’s the one leading Elena’s knightly guard.”

However, Rei wasn’t just watching Ara; he also needed to give Set some instructions.

“Set, you keep an eye on the ground from above. If anyone tries to escape from this house, capture them. …Can you do that?”

“Grr!”

“Leave it to me!” Set growled.

Set already had excellent night vision, but now, in addition to the moonlight, there were bonfires lit by the soldiers of the Marquis of Seisor’s household on the ground.

With those lights, he could see the ground even more clearly than usual, so Set had absolutely no intention of letting anyone who tried to escape get away.

Once Rei had finished giving orders to Set and Ara had finished giving orders to the soldiers, Elena, who had been watching, spoke up.

“Well then, shall we go?”

Elena’s expression as she spoke was fierce yet beautiful.

It was a sight truly befitting her nickname, the Princess General.

Rei, following behind her, lacked Elena’s sheer dignity, but the aura he projected was overwhelming.

Compared to those two, Ara inevitably seemed a step below, but that was simply because she was up against the wrong opponents.

The knights of the Marquis of Seisor’s household could not stop the three of them as they advanced onto the estate grounds, even if they wanted to.

To fulfill their duty, they absolutely had to stop them… but even if they tried, they didn’t think they’d stand a chance.

Furthermore, they had no way of knowing how the soldiers of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, stationed nearby, might react if they attempted to intervene.

In the end, the knights were unable to move until Rei and the others had entered the estate grounds.

“Whoa! Right off the bat?”

Rei, who had naturally taken the lead, slashed down an arrow that flew out of nowhere the moment he stepped onto the ground, using the Death Scythe he’d drawn from the Misty Ring.

It’s true that Rei took the lead partly because he’s a man… but a major reason is simply that, wearing the Dragon Robe, he possesses high defensive capabilities.

Of course, his ability to use the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear to defend against incoming arrows like this was also a major factor.

That said, if it were just the latter, Elena—who wields the Mirage, a linked sword—could do it too, but in Elena’s case, she’d need to give orders to Rei and Ara if anything happened.

In that sense, having Elena in the center and Ara at the rear in case of an emergency was by no means a wrong formation.

“Rei, I think you’ll be fine, but just be careful.”

“I know. Arrows like these are no problem… Huh!?”

Arrows aren’t a problem.

That’s what Rei was about to say, but the moment he took a step forward, the ground began to move like a bear trap, trying to clamp down on him.

Rei hadn’t expected to encounter such a trap the moment he stepped away from the arrow trap… but by swinging his Death Scythe, he severed the earthen trap—which, unbeknownst to him, had even been fitted with sharp blades.

“...That was a surprise.”

Those were words born of Rei’s honest surprise.

That trap had undoubtedly remained undetected by Rei until the very moment it activated.

Though Rei was no match for a professional thief, he still had some confidence in his ability to spot traps.

Yet, despite that, he hadn’t been able to detect this trap at all.

Then again, considering that a trap that had already been triggered immediately reset itself and transformed into a different trap… it was clear that this was no ordinary trap.

(Should I force my way through? No, if I do that, the traps might trigger one after another. If that happens...)

Rei, Death Scythe in hand, called out to Elena behind him.

“Elena, if we keep going like this, there are just too many traps to deal with. How about we just burn them all—along with the ground—using magic? That way, even though we don’t know what methods they’re using, I think we can eliminate a good number of the traps at once.”

“Hmm… but won’t that cause casualties among the people of the Seisor Marquis family?”

“I’ll handle that part carefully.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Elena thought for a moment… recalling their actions regarding the recent series of traps, she nodded in agreement.

“Then, please proceed as you suggest. However, as I just said, please be absolutely careful not to harm anyone from the Seisor Marquis family.”

“Yes.”

Nodding, Rei infused his Death Scythe with magic and began chanting the spell.

“O Flame, you shall exist only within the realm I have designated, and shall not exist in any other realm. As the price for your brief existence, feed upon my magic within this realm, and in an instant, let your life be sublimated.”

As the incantation progressed, a red line began to draw itself, connecting Rei and his companions to the target house.

Then the red lines form a transparent dome... and fire spirits in the shape of red lizards appear by the dozens, hundreds, and thousands.

"Fire Elemental Frenzy"

The spell was completed, and the magic activated.

The lizard-shaped fire spirits exploded, triggering the others to explode as well... and the chain reaction of explosions continued within the dome.

Since the transparent dome itself extends only from where Rei and the others are standing to the house, it isn’t that large.

That is why, although the fire spirits’ explosions were massive, the explosions themselves eventually came to an end.

As the explosions ended, the transparent dome vanished… and all that remained was the ground, churned up by the blast.

They couldn’t find any traps there, and even when Rei poked the ground with the tip of his Death Scythe, there was no sign of any new traps being triggered.

“...Well, with that settled, there are no more traps here for now. That leaves the house... what do you think? Should we just burn it down right here?”

“That’s an option, but we have a lot to investigate, so let’s not do that.”

Saying that, Elena moved forward across the ground with Rei and Ara… and eventually, without incident, they arrived in front of the house.
