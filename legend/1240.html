After muttering "Delicious," Rei simply kept shoveling the oak meat stew into his mouth.

It wasn't just Rei; the others were doing the same.

The overwhelming presence of the meat... wasn't the only thing.

Though the stew was dominated by the strong presence of the orc meat, the vegetables also held their own, creating flavors that matched the meat's intensity.

Though this was supposed to be a celebratory feast where lively chatter over food wouldn't have been out of place... now, they were all solely focused on savoring the stew with single-minded devotion.

When the stew in the dish finally vanished... perhaps marking a pause, Rei spoke up.

"Man, this is seriously delicious."

Rei's muttered words seemed to be the trigger, as the others began to speak up one after another.

"As Rei said, this stew... it's a flavor you don't get to experience often."

"Indeed. The dishes served at that dinner hosted by Count Males, the so-called Gourmet Count, were also delicious, but..."

"The Gourmet Count? What's that? Sounds intriguing."

Vihera immediately responded to Elena and Ara's words.

"Ah, Count Mears. I recall receiving a letter from him before, asking us to let him know if we came across any delicious dishes eaten by dark elves."

"A letter to the Guildmaster... No, he's a noble, so isn't that a bit strange?"

"It's not strange at all. In fact, he wouldn't hesitate to write to the Emperor of the Bestir Empire for something delicious."

Johanna's companions were astonished by the very act of writing to the Guildmaster, but hearing he'd also written to a neighboring country—a longstanding enemy at that—left them utterly speechless.

Listening to this, Rei found himself developing a slight interest in that gourmet count... Count Mears.

While Rei generally held the strong belief that most nobles, save for a few exceptions, were hopelessly useless, his interest in Count Mears likely stemmed from that epithet—though calling it an epithet was difficult—the Gourmet Count.

Both Rei and Set enjoyed eating.

Enough, in fact, to want to spread the dishes he remembered.

...But considering that Rei had been a high school student back in Japan, he basically couldn't teach most dishes.

He might be able to teach cooking if he had the ingredients, but he didn't know how to make those ingredients.

For example, potato starch is different from wheat flour, but he didn't know how to make potato starch.

With the knowledge, you could easily make it from potatoes, but without that knowledge, there was nothing he could do.

To be precise, I did learn it back in elementary school, but I'd completely forgotten it.

"The ham and sausage I ate first were pretty tasty too... so I can expect other dishes to be good as well."

Recalling the stew's flavor, Rei next reached for a dish made by boiling oak meat to remove excess fat, then coating it in a sauce from strongly acidic fruit.

The fruit used for the sauce was probably also gathered from the dungeon.

The oak meat itself had a subtle seasoning, likely from the careful preparation during boiling.

The seasoning was exquisite—any stronger, and it would have clashed with the sauce's tang.

Additionally, the stir-fried vegetables from the dungeon used a spice similar to pepper yet subtly different. Cooked over high heat, they retained a satisfying crunch, making them perfect for cleansing the palate between bites of the oak meat-based dishes.

"This is delicious. Especially this stewed vegetable dish—it's absolutely exquisite."

True to the restaurant's name, most dishes featured oak meat.

However, the other dishes are no less impressive, satisfying even Marina's discerning palate, honed by years of experiencing diverse cuisines.

As a dark elf, she had lived a long life, working as an adventurer and later as a guildmaster.

With such experience... and possessing beauty that never fails to draw attention, Marina naturally received many invitations to dine, giving her ample opportunity to sample diverse cuisines.

Among all those experiences, the dishes she's eating now undoubtedly rank among the very best.

"It's not just the meat. This fish, too—it's a white fish that practically melts in your mouth the moment you take a bite."

Ordinary fish couldn't possibly be sourced in a place like this; this fish undoubtedly came from the dungeon.

Is it from the forest layer after all? Rei pondered as he rinsed his mouth with fruit water and brought the fish to his lips.

Just as Vihera had described, it melted in his mouth... yet it carried a different kind of richness than the meat of an orc. This fish reminded Rei of the fish he had eaten back in Japan.

(It's similar to hokke. Though, the flesh is much firmer and more plump than hokke, and it's richer in fat too.)

Whether that was simply because the species was different, or due to the preparation before grilling, or some other reason entirely, Rei couldn't tell. But the undeniable truth was that it was delicious.

After that, they kept repeating the cycle of eating other dishes and exclaiming how delicious they were. Still, after a certain amount of time passed and they'd tasted most of the dishes, their comments on the meal settled down, and the conversation shifted to this dungeon.

"Still, that Silver Lion was incredible, wasn't it? I only saw the corpse, but to think such a massive monster existed... Even though Set-chan is big, compared to that Silver Lion, it was like an adult and a child—or maybe even more than that?"

Johanna murmured thoughtfully, stroking Set as she recalled the Silver Lion's corpse.

Her companions seemed to agree with Johanna's sentiment, each nodding and voicing their astonishment at seeing the Silver Lion's corpse for the first time.

"Yeah. Honestly, facing an opponent that size... on top of that, incredibly agile, and with defense that was even higher. If its attack power had been as strong as its other abilities, who knows what would've happened."

Recalling the fierce battle with the Silver Lion, she simultaneously remembered the pain in her ribs from that fight.

It had improved considerably since the battle ended, but even now, a slight ache surfaced at unexpected moments.

(By tomorrow, the pain will probably be almost gone... Should I have postponed the celebration until tomorrow? Nah, momentum is key with stuff like this.)

He endured the faint ache, yet couldn't resist the temptation of the food before him and reached out.

"With that many of you together, and still struggling? Honestly, an S-rank monster is nothing short of a monster itself."

One of Johanna's companions murmurs thoughtfully.

As former members of the mobile unit, they knew Rei and Set's capabilities well, and naturally, they knew Vihera's strength from sharing the battlefield.

Having lived in the Bestir Empire, they naturally knew Princess General Elena, and Elk's name was also known as a Rank A adventurer with a nickname.

Beyond that, with figures like Guildmaster Marina, Elena's attendant Ara, and Elk's wife and party member Min all present, what Johanna and her companions saw after the battle ended was Rei and the others covered in wounds.

Only those who had stood at the front lines bore wounds, and even those were not severe enough to be called critical injuries, but rather minor ones.

Considering all that, they couldn't help but feel that even if they had participated in the battle, they would have only slowed everyone down.

"Yeah. Honestly, I get it now—Rank S monsters really are a bunch of freaks. Well, this time, things just didn't go our way either."

It might sound like sour grapes, but it wasn't a lie.

Rei's Death Scythe weighed around a hundred kilograms.

But even when he landed a blow with the Death Scythe while using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, he couldn't cut through its fur.

The only effective attack was Death Scythe's skill, Power Slash.

Though ultimately, we resorted to targeting areas without body hair... thrusting the Twilight Spear into the eyeball and destroying the Silver Lion's brain through the upper jaw.

"Compatibility, huh? If that's the case with Rei-san, it's really good we didn't join the fight. Right, Set-chan?"

"Guru?"

Johanna murmured while stroking Set, responding to Rei's words spoken from the bottom of his heart.

Stroke by stroke, Set glanced toward Johanna for a moment before promptly returning to his meal.

Despite Set's attitude, Johanna gently stroked his body with a pleased expression.

"Silver Lions, huh... Yeah."

Thinking of the Silver Lion, Rei's gaze fell upon the numerous dishes arranged on the table.

If they could make such delicious dishes with oak meat, he couldn't help but wonder what they could do with Silver Lion meat.

And once the thought took hold, there was no stopping it.

The news that the Silver Lion had been slain and the dungeon's core destroyed would be officially announced by the guild soon enough, but that didn't mean they absolutely couldn't spread the word right now.

That was precisely why, even though the owner was away from the counter, they were discussing the defeat of the Silver Lion right here in the shop.

Amidst this, Rei placed a roughly one-kilogram chunk of meat onto an empty plate on the table.

Yes, it was the meat from the silver lion they had defeated just a few hours ago.

Though he placed it casually, that alone drew the gazes of everyone around him.

That's how compelling this chunk of meat was.

"Since we're here, I was wondering if you could cook this meat for us. What do you say?"

'Agreed!'

At Rei's words, everyone... even those accustomed to fine cuisine like Elena, Marina, and Vihera... all voiced their agreement in unison.

There was not a hint of hesitation.

Only those who craved the meat existed.

Of course, Rei was no different from the others.

Given the culinary prowess of this restaurant's owner, there was no doubt he could properly prepare the meat of a Silver Lion, a rank S monster.

"Alright then, since no one objects, let's order right away?"

After confirming no one objected, Rei spoke up.

"Owner, can I have a word?"

"Huh? I've got plenty to do myself... Hey, what is this meat?"

The owner was about to snap back, "What is it? I'm busy preparing the extra dishes!" but stopped mid-sentence upon seeing the unfamiliar meat on the table.

If it had been ordinary meat, or meat found anywhere, it would have been something the shopkeeper had seen countless times before, and he wouldn't have bothered asking like this.

But the meat sitting there had a different kind of presence.

A distinct presence, clearly not like any ordinary meat.

"It's the meat of an S-rank monster."

Rei stated it succinctly, and with that alone, the shopkeeper understood the meat before him was that of the Silver Lion, the creature guarding the dungeon's core within this labyrinth.

If the shopkeeper had been working somewhere in the kitchen where he could hear voices, he might have overheard what Rei and the others were saying.

Unfortunately, the shopkeeper had holed up in the back room where the spices were stored.

Then again, if he was completely focused on cooking, he might not have heard even if called out to, so perhaps he wouldn't have noticed even if he had been in the kitchen.

But... the shopkeeper knew.

That what lay before him now was the meat of a rank S monster, the Silver Lion.

And with such a truly premium ingredient at hand, it was only natural for a chef to want to cook it.

"Let me cook this meat. I'll waive today's fee. In return, I want to taste the dish you make with this Silver Lion meat."

He wanted to cook it himself and taste it.

When the shopkeeper said this, Rei naturally didn't refuse... and simply nodded in agreement.

Because that was precisely what Rei had been hoping for.

"Understood. Then I'll leave the preparation of this silver lion meat to you. It's the finest ingredient, so make the finest dish you can."

"Hmph, who do you think you're talking to? Leave it to me. As a chef, there's no way I'd waste ingredients this good!"

The shopkeeper, wearing a manly grin, took the plate of silver lion meat and disappeared into the kitchen.

Watching him go, Rei and the others turned their gaze toward the kitchen, eagerly anticipating what kind of dish would emerge...

"Woooooah!"

"Wooooo!"

"Guh!"

"Gorrah!"

"Get out of here!"

Despite hearing shouts from the kitchen that sounded like nothing but a fight, all we could do was exchange glances.
