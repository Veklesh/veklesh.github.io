The giant flower that had been scattering seeds collapsed, only for several more similar giant flowers to sprout from the forest of Treant the very next moment. Seeing this, Rei swung his Death Scythe with displeasure.

Flowers bursting from the ground were cleanly severed and fell to the earth.

"Damn, troublesome... What do you think we should do about this?"

Perhaps that flower was the boss of Treant Forest.

Though he had no proof, Rei had held onto the hopeful assumption that it was, but that wish was quickly dashed.

Marina, who had been firing arrows without pause, filling the Venus flytraps with them, answered Rei's irritated words.

"Well, that giant flower is still a major force for Treant Forest. If that's the case, if we keep taking it down, won't they eventually give up?"

"...That might be true for ordinary monsters."

To begin with, the monsters existing in Treant Forest, even limiting it to those Rei and the others had fought so far, didn't possess magic stones.

That meant this monster was no ordinary monster.

It wasn't unreasonable for Rei to wonder if the methods he used to defeat ordinary monsters would even work here.

But the fact remained that, given the current situation, it was the only quick solution available.

Well, in the worst case, Rei could burn the entire forest to the ground. But doing something like that would undoubtedly result in the entire Treant Forest... or at the very least, a significant portion of it being consumed by flames.

Of course, trees could potentially grow back from the burned areas, but since it was only a possibility, it was difficult to actually carry out.

That was precisely why Rei refrained from using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, whose power against plant life was downright vicious.

Swinging Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, Rei pondered what to do next. But with almost no detailed information about this forest to begin with, there was little he could do.

But... as if hearing Rei and Marina's conversation, one of the researchers exchanging opinions despite the situation looked at Rei and spoke up.

"I believe it's certain that something lies at the center of this forest... or perhaps at the opposite edge, considering how this forest only extends toward Gilm.

"...The problem is, whether dealing with that something will actually resolve this whole situation, you know!"

Rei shouted while severing the stem of the plant that had hurled the strange fruit at him with his Death Scythe.

Incidentally, the thrown fruit scattered a liquid that melted the surrounding grass upon hitting the ground.

She was momentarily startled by a monster said to dwell in Treant Forest attacking grass that could be considered its comrades, but then realized that monsters rarely consider different types of monsters as allies.

"It's entirely possible something's in the center of the forest... but I still don't know what it is?"

The water spirit moved along the ground, snatching up vines that were trying to sneak closer, hidden in the night's darkness and the shadows of the grass. In the next instant, it twisted them all together and snapped them off.

Several adventurers watching gasped in surprise.

The adventurers present naturally knew what kind of person Marina was.

They knew she had been a skilled adventurer in the past, but even so, mastering such powerful spirit magic to this extent was likely unexpected.

...Seeing the severed vines, several men instinctively pulled back or took a step inward.

They were likely men with their own thoughts and concerns.

"That's true. But if we stay like this, we'll either have to retreat... or keep fighting endlessly until morning. It seems to be an undeniable fact that monsters don't appear during daylight hours."

Marina turned her gaze to Rei, asking silently, What do we do?

After all, Rei was the party leader of the Crimson Wings, the party Marina belonged to.

Given that, it was only natural that Rei would make the party's decisions.

Of course, if it was clearly wrong, she might voice her objection.

"Understood. Then I'll..."

Go.

That's what Rei was about to say, but before he could finish, he saw several adventurers who had been fighting monsters stagger... with steps like drunks or something, trying to enter the forest.

Strangely, the monsters around them didn't attack these adventurers.

They simply passed right by them, as if they weren't even there.

"Hey, Saurthard! Where are you going?! Come back here!"

"Sharan, come on, I said come back! Hey, you can hear me, right?!"

Several such shouts could be heard from around them.

Each one was directed at the adventurer who had started walking deeper into the forest.

But the person being called paid them no mind whatsoever, continuing toward the forest.

"Huh!? This is bad!?"

The first to notice was Marina, alerted to the disturbance by the wind spirits.

She immediately used her magic, pleading with the spirits.

'Water spirits, spread thinly and swallow the harmful presence in the air!'

With those words, water spread out dramatically, forming a vast surface that moved through the air.

What the hell is she doing? Some wondered at Marina's actions, but judging it wasn't the time for that, the adventurers held back their comrades trying to head deeper into the forest while fighting nearby enemies.

But... when Marina began manipulating the water spirit through the air, suddenly no one dared stagger toward the forest's depths anymore.

"What happened?"

Rei, who had just sliced through a Treant trunk with his Death Scythe, moved closer to Marina and asked.

Rei, however, kept her gaze fixed on the water she had summoned to the ground and spoke.

"Look."

It was a rather blunt request for Marina, but it was still enough to indicate what she wanted him to do.

When Rei turned his gaze to the water on the ground, he frowned slightly.

The object within the water... he saw yellow powder.

"What's this?"

"Well, it's probably pollen. ...Those who inhaled it ended up like that and started walking."

Watching the adventurers secured by her companions before they could enter the forest, Marina murmured. Rei looked at the water on the ground again, a look of surprise on his face.

"...This is troublesome."

"Yeah."

They exchanged brief opinions.

"What do we do?"

"Knowing this exists, we can handle it. We could gather them with water like now, or blow them away with wind... Burning them all is risky in many ways, though."

"Honestly, I think we should just burn the whole lot at once... Hey!"

He deflected the sharp-spiked fruit flying through the air with his Death Scythe and hurled the Twilight Spear.

Launched at a speed incomparable to the flying fruit, the Twilight Spear pierced and shattered the trunk of what appeared to be a Treant subspecies.

Watching the monster fall to the ground split clean in two, he deflected the seeds raining down from a new giant flower with his Death Scythe... and felt a twinge of doubt.

(Somehow, the amount of seeds falling seems low... Even though the number of those sunflower-like things has increased.)

From where Rei stood, he could see at least four giant flowers.

Considering the areas obscured by the trees, there should have been even more flowers.

But as far as Rei could tell, the amount of seeds falling hadn't changed from when there was only one flower. ...No, it even seemed to have decreased.

Rei couldn't tell if that was a good thing or a bad thing, but he decided to convince himself it was good for now.

(After all, Vihera did this. Thinking that way, it can't be a bad thing. ...I hope.)

While Rei was wrestling with these thoughts, one of the researchers shouted again.

"Never mind the pollen! It's the center of the forest! No, wait, maybe it's the edge!"

The researcher's words reminded Rei of what they'd been discussing before the pollen issue.

He had intended to go there himself and had actually been about to say so.

But then, Rei remembered how many times she and Set had looked down upon this forest from above.

And once he remembered that, he couldn't simply nod in agreement with the researcher's words.

"As you know, I've flown over this Treant Forest many times on Set. But there wasn't anything particularly unusual around the center, was there? At least, definitely nothing like a meaningful gaping hole or anything like that. ...I don't know about the edges, though."

Where the giant flower grew, there was indeed a gaping space, just as Rei had described. But Rei couldn't know that now.

"Hmm. Considering how vast this forest is, it wouldn't be surprising if there were some sort of distinctive landmark or marker."

The researcher murmured regretfully at Rei's words.

"If that's the case, then it must be towards the edge of the forest, right?"

"Even if you say that, where exactly is the edge? Unlike the center, doesn't the entire opposite side count as the edge?"

"Well... I suppose that's true."

He replied, but it wasn't exactly information that would be immediately useful in their current situation.

What they needed now was information that could be used immediately.

The center of the forest or the edge?

Either way, they had to figure out how to navigate the Treant Forest toward one or the other... or retreat from the forest's edge altogether.

But retreat wasn't an option in Rei's mind.

After all, with Set and Vihera inside the Treant Forest, that was only natural.

Retreating now would mean abandoning Vihera and Set.

(So then... either wait here until Vihera and Set return, or go myself, huh?)

Even as he considered this, Rei knew the answer was already decided, given his own nature.

"I'll go."

At those words, Marina's expression became a mix of joy and disappointment.

She was glad her prediction that Rei would never abandon Vihera and Set had proven correct, but given the current situation, she couldn't leave this place.

Marina wanted to charge into the forest with Rei if possible, but she couldn't think of anyone else present who could command the situation more clearly than she could. More importantly, the adventurers' advantage in their fight against the monsters emerging from Treant Forest was entirely due to Marina's spirit magic.

It wouldn't be an exaggeration to say Marina was favored by the spirits.

That was precisely why she could wield spirit magic so skillfully.

If Marina were to leave, the adventurers would undoubtedly lose this battle.

To prevent that, Marina simply cannot leave this place.

That was the reason for the regretful expression on her face.

"Yes, be careful."

In the end, all Marina could do now was say that and see them off.

If she were to act selfishly here, following Rei might be an option.

But given Marina's nature, she couldn't do that.

...If this were Vihera, she might head to Treant Forest with Rei, thinking she could fight a formidable foe.

"Yeah, leave it to me. For now, if possible, I'll meet up with Vihera first, then head for the center of Treant Forest. ...Hope it's not on the other side."

"Mm!"

"Byune stays here on guard."

"...Mm."

Though Byune declared she wanted to go too, Rei immediately refused.

He knew Byune was attached to Vihera, but the place they were heading to was completely uncharted territory.

They had entered Treant Forest during the day before, but the stark difference between day and night was obvious given their current situation.

He couldn't take Byune to a place like that.

While she had gained some combat ability through winter training, it was ultimately only to a certain extent.

At the very least, she wasn't at a level where she could be taken to an unknown place that might also be dangerous for Rei.

And certainly not...

"Please take me with you too!"

"Of course not, you'd just slow us down."

The researcher was out of the question.

Rei immediately dismissed the researcher's plea to be taken along.

Taking along a researcher who, despite their high intellectual curiosity, couldn't even properly protect themselves would be nothing short of suicide.

Leaving the researcher behind, visibly shaken by being called a liability, Rei swung his Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, cutting down enemies as he entered the forest.
