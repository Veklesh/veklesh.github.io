The next day, I went out into the streets of Gilm early in the morning as usual to defeat the monsters that had gathered in search of the blood and flesh scraps left over from the previous day’s butchering…

“Ugh.”

It was Vihera who let out a sigh of exasperation.

I was sure a powerful foe would show up again today... but what was there was just a horde of goblins.

“...That’s strange.”

As he looked at the goblin horde, Rei felt puzzled.

Up until now, there should have been Kobolds here.

Yet, the only ones who had shown up were goblins.

Given the recent strange occurrences in Gilm—and the incident with the Kobolds—this was clearly abnormal.

“Anyway… is there anyone here who wants to fight these goblins?”

Rei asked this, having decided that wiping out the goblins took priority over thinking things through, but no one agreed.

For those who had come here as guards, fighting goblins would be nothing short of a waste of energy.

For those hired for their butchering skills, getting injured in a reckless fight with goblins would hinder their work.

As for the people from the slums, while they could technically eat goblin meat, they certainly didn’t have any desire to do so.

Moreover, since they could receive a fair reward simply by performing the butchering—and get Giganto Turtle meat, which was in a league of its own compared to goblin meat—there was no way they would lay a finger on the goblins.

In the end, since no one volunteered, Rei asked Set to wipe out the goblins.

The sight of him launching one attack after another from above—fire breath, water balls, wind arrows, ice arrows, and so on—was truly a scene worthy of being called a rampage.

Then, using the terrain-manipulation ability of the Death Scythe, he buried the goblins’ corpses underground. Once preparations were complete, he retrieved the Giganto Turtle from the Misty Ring.

Since they hadn’t actually started the dissection that long ago, the process itself hadn’t progressed very far.

...That is precisely why those in charge of the dissection set to work one after another.

“Still, I never would have thought there’d be nothing but goblins.”

Vihera murmured beside Rei, who was watching the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.

“True. To be honest, I was surprised there weren’t any Kobolds.”

“……Do you think yesterday’s incident has anything to do with this?”

When she heard “yesterday’s incident,” the first thing that came to Rei’s mind was, of course, the pink tentacles.

Since the man who brought Akabu had said it was related to the Kobolds, the fact that there were no Kobolds here was surely connected.

“If you think about it logically, it’s probably related. The most likely possibility is that it has something to do with the damage I dealt to the tentacles.”

Including the person connected to the mastermind behind this incident, everyone—including Red Cloth—was devoured by the tentacles… or perhaps I should say, sucked to death.

In any case, even though more people were killed than originally planned, the fact that no Kobolds have appeared means it’s only natural to look for the reason in that area.

“Hmm… Hey, Rei. When we investigate that mansion, I’m coming along this time.”

Vihera didn’t ask to be taken along; she simply stated that she was going.

Rei nodded in response to those words, which were so typical of Vihera.

To be honest, ever since she heard about the tentacles, Ray had known that Vihera wanted to fight them, so it was hardly a surprise.

“But those tentacles are a bit more troublesome than the enemies we’ve fought so far. You’re aware of that, right?”

“Yes. Just touching the tip drains all sorts of things from you, right? I’ll make sure not to touch that part, so I’ll be fine.”

Vihera says this with a voice full of confidence.

Rei watched Vihera like that and smiled.

He knew Vihera loved a good fight, and he felt that her attitude right now was exactly what made her Vihera.

“Got it. Well, I’m heading to the headquarters after this is over today. Want to come with me?”

When he’d stopped by the guild before coming here, Lenora had asked him to drop by the headquarters if he had some free time.

That’s why Rei invited Vihera like this, but…

“Hmm… today, huh?”

The reason she hedged her words was likely because Vihera felt she was something of a guardian figure to Byune.

That said, Vihera’s heart was strongly set on the battle against the tentacles.

Part of it was that there had only been goblins here this morning, but more importantly, Byune was getting stronger.

In fact, with Byune’s current strength, she could likely trample over goblins and Kobolds without suffering a single scratch, using her overwhelming power.

Even so, the main reason she didn’t immediately agree to Rei’s invitation was likely because, with this escort mission, there’s no telling what kind of monsters might appear.

Depending on the situation, it wouldn’t be surprising if a Rank B… no, even a Rank A monster appeared.

After all, the scent of a Giganto Turtle’s blood and flesh is that alluring.

Considering the worst-case scenario, if possible, I didn’t want to stray far from Byune.

That was what Vihera was thinking...

“Hmm.”

Byune came up in front of Vihera and muttered briefly, as usual.

Rei didn’t quite grasp the exact meaning of those words.

But even so, he could somehow understand what she meant.

In other words...

“Isn’t Byune just saying, ‘Don’t worry about me, just go ahead’?”

“I guess so. …Hey, are you really okay?”

“Mm!”

Without changing her expression, Byune showed through her demeanor that she agreed with Rei.

Seeing Byune’s words and actions, Vihera thought for a moment… and eventually nodded.

“I understand. Well then, I suppose I’ll take Byune up on her offer today.”

“Mm.”

Byune’s expression made it clear that was fine with her.

Vihera gently stroked Byune’s head, filled with gratitude.

Although Byune didn’t show it on her face, she seemed pleased and silently accepted the gesture.

“Just so you know, even though they’ve asked me to come to the headquarters, that doesn’t necessarily mean we’re going straight to the mansion from yesterday. And even if we do go, it doesn’t mean there’ll be a fight.”

Even as he said this, Rei’s prediction was that, given what happened yesterday, a fight would likely be unavoidable.

Considering the attackers who had come at them without warning, there was no guarantee that similar threats didn’t lurk in other estates as well.

(If anything, Vihera is probably the kind of person who welcomes that sort of opponent.)

Rei recalled the people he’d fought yesterday, but if asked whether Vihera would be satisfied with their strength, honestly, it was a bit of a toss-up.

It was true they were undoubtedly stronger than the guards, but even so, they were only strong enough to defeat Rei without him putting up a fight.

“Alright, wait here for a moment.”

Saying that, Rei stepped away for a moment and spoke to a guild staff member.

“Vihera and I are going to the guard post. Is it okay if we leave this to you?”

“Sure, I’ve heard the story, so that’s fine. As for this side… for some reason, no Kobolds have shown up today, so it shouldn’t be as busy as it was until yesterday. It’s a shame that maze you built isn’t coming in handy, though.”

The guild clerk’s gaze fell on the maze set up between the site where Gilm and the Giganto Turtle were being dismantled.

That said, it wasn’t a particularly elaborate maze.

Its sole purpose was to scatter the Kobolds when they attacked in groups.

Given how makeshift the maze was, even seeing the guild staff’s disappointed expressions left Rei at a loss for words.

“I’d be happy to hear you say that… but well, if it’s not useful in that regard, then so be it. Anyway, I’ll leave this to you. It’ll be tough, but I’m counting on you.”

The guild staff member involved in dismantling the Giganto Turtle is in charge and needs to respond immediately if any problems arise.

In that sense, the work of a guild official is by no means easy.

It is precisely because Rei understands this that he is telling the guild staff to take care of things from here on out.

For the guild staff as well, since this job serves as a form of compensation for Rei, they would never even think of cutting corners.

Even under these circumstances, the fact that Rei took the time to speak to them like this was something that made them, as the ones actually doing the work, very happy.

After speaking with the guild staff for a few minutes, Rei heads toward Gilm with Set and Vihera.

...As he did so, he noticed the spectators who had come to watch the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle looking a little disappointed, and he understood why.

For the spectators, while the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle was certainly a draw, seeing Vihera—a woman of unparalleled beauty—was likely one of their main attractions as well.

In that sense, he could understand why so many people were casting envious or jealous glances his way.

That said, Rei was used to that kind of attention, so she didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

“Hehe, I wonder if this counts as a date, too?”

Walking beside Rei, Vihera laughed happily and took his arm.

Feeling Vihera’s ample breasts press against his elbow, Rei gently looked away and spoke.

“It feels a bit too dangerous to call this a date, though.”

“Hmm?”

"Is that so?" Set, walking on the opposite side from Vihera, growls.

From Set’s perspective, being able to go out with Vihera and Rei is a huge joy, so he probably doesn’t mind the danger all that much.

“Well, we don’t know yet if anything dangerous will happen. You just got a message asking you to come to the station, right? So, it might be an interrogation… though I’m not sure if that’s the right word for it.”

Vihera says this with a smile, but deep down, since they’re calling Rei, she’s absolutely certain that something dangerous is about to happen.

After finishing the formalities at the main gate, they head to the guardhouse they visited yesterday.

A few passersby wanted to play with Set, but unfortunately, Rei turned them down and kept walking.

And since Rei kept walking, Vihera naturally had to keep walking too… Just like before, envious and jealous glances were directed at Rei, who was linked arm-in-arm with Vihera.

If this were anywhere other than Gilm—especially a place they’d never been before—there would surely be people who’d try to hassle Rei.

But among the residents of Gilm, there was no one—well, not exactly no one, but certainly no one present at that moment—who would be so reckless as to do such a thing.

“Ah, Rei. …Vihera’s here too…”

The guard standing in front of the guardhouse looked pleased to see Rei... then, upon seeing Vihera, his expression turned complicated.

For the guard, Vihera was a very difficult person to deal with.

In fact, he’d been called out to deal with the commotion Vihera had caused countless times.

And yet, in most of those cases, the source of the problem was not Vihera but the other party, so all the guards could do was issue a warning.

He had warned her countless times that her attire—which resembled that of a dancer or a prostitute—was the cause of these incidents, but when she claimed it was a magic item or that it was suited for battle, the guard couldn’t deny it.

“Well, if there’s going to be a fight, Vihera will definitely be a valuable asset. More importantly, is it okay for me to come into the guardhouse? Besides, in this situation, the more combatants we have, the better. Especially having more people who can handle those tentacles—that’ll definitely be a huge help.”

When we fought yesterday, there weren’t that many tentacles.

No, even so, they’re normally a difficult opponent to handle, but with Rei’s skills, it wasn’t a problem.

But that was only true as of yesterday.

If more tentacles appeared, it would naturally be a huge help to have more personnel to deal with them.

……Though, of course, that was only true if we were to storm the mansion again, as we did yesterday.

The guard standing in front of the command post hadn’t participated in the search of the mansion yesterday, but he’d probably heard about it. He nodded reluctantly.

“Alright, let’s go inside.”

“Ah. …Come to think of it, what happened to the people who participated in the search yesterday? Did they face any punishment?”

“No. Though they’ll probably get some kind of warning. But that’ll likely happen after this whole affair is over.”

Relieved to know there were no immediate issues, Rei let out a sigh of relief.
