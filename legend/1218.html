Marina, who had business with the Guildmaster, informed the receptionist... Soon after, she was promptly escorted to the Guildmaster's office.

Naturally, some of the guild staff recognized Marina, and it seemed one of them had informed their superiors.

Because of this, Marina disappeared into the depths of the guild without having to wait at all.

Rei and the others had come to the guild with Marina to ensure she didn't get tangled up with any strange individuals.

Given that purpose, there was no need for them to accompany her further. They waited for Marina at the tavern attached to the guild with Johanna and the others.

...While ordering a mountain of food, of course.

Rei, Elena, Ara, Elk. Plus Johanna and her companions.

With that many people, one table was obviously insufficient. Rei, Elena, and Johanna sat at one table, while the others sat at tables near Rei's to keep an eye on anyone trying to eavesdrop on their conversation.

Naturally, everyone in the tavern was paying attention to Rei and his group, but Elk and the others kept those people in check, preventing them from eavesdropping.

"So, why are Johanna and the others here anyway?"

As I asked while eating a sandwich filled with stewed boar meat and vegetables, Johanna opened her mouth with a slightly serious expression.

"Well, we heard that rare, highly effective potions drop in that dungeon..."

"Potions? Why on earth... Wait, is it Vihera?"

"Yes."

Johanna immediately nodded in response to Rei's words.

Seeing Johanna like this, Rei understood immediately.

Johanna and the others were currently affiliated with the Kingdom of Mireana, but they were originally born and raised in the Bestir Empire.

Furthermore, during the civil war, they had acted alongside Vihera.

Furthermore, Vihera had enjoyed immense popularity within the Bestir Empire, and many among Johanna's group harbored a strong sense of admiration for him.

Learning that Vihera was unconscious due to the Ambris incident, it was only natural that some would want to do something about it.

And so Johanna and the others searched for a way to restore Vihera's consciousness.

First, using magic themselves... that was impossible.

Among those who had migrated to Gilm, there were a few who could perform simple magic. However, if such basic magic could restore Vihera's consciousness, Rei wouldn't have struggled this much.

They tried going to those skilled in healing magic and asking alchemists to make a consciousness-restoring potion, but Rei had already tried both, and they refused as soon as they heard about Vihera.

They considered various other options too, but naturally, any method Johanna and the others could think of had already been tried by people like Rei and Marina.

And the last option left was...

"Magic items found in dungeons, huh?"

"Yes."

When Rei stated his conclusion at the end of the explanation, Johanna nodded simply.

Inside dungeons, it was sometimes possible to find magic items and potions that defied ordinary expectations.

For example, Ara's Power Axe, glaring menacingly from a distant table, was also found in this dungeon.

Therefore, the possibility of finding a potion with extremely potent effects wasn't entirely out of the question.

Of course, if asked whether they could actually obtain the specific item they sought, the chances were undeniably slim.

Still, that was about all Johanna and the others could do for Vihera at this point.

So, for about a month now, Johanna and the others had been coming to this dungeon in search of potions.

Admittedly, a major reason they could do this was that preparations for winter survival were already complete.

That's precisely why they could afford to search this dungeon for a potion capable of restoring Vihera's consciousness.

Furthermore, venturing into the dungeon inevitably meant encountering monsters, yielding magic stones, proof of slaying parts, and depending on the floor, other materials as well.

Even if they didn't find potions, they could sell these items, so they weren't exactly coming up empty-handed.

"The other day, a lizardman had a pretty decent halberd, you know. ...Well, no one could use it, so I sold it off,"

Johanna said with a smile.

It must have sold for a hefty price. The others also wore pleased smiles.

"...I see."

"So, why are you and Rei here in this dungeon? Looking for potions too, like us?"

Johanna's tone as she asked this seemed to imply she already knew the answer.

Having fought through that civil war together, she naturally understood the feelings Vihera held for Rei.

Therefore, she assumed they were here to restore Vihera's consciousness... that was the basis for her question.

But Rei shook his head at Johanna's words.

"Huh?"

Unable to comprehend Rei's actions, Johanna asked back in puzzlement.

It was only natural. She had assumed Rei and the others had come here seeking potions, just like they were.

She had asked, but it was more of a confirmation than anything else.

Given that her question was met with a negative action, it was only natural for her to be puzzled.

Slightly... just slightly, Johanna's gaze toward Rei hardened.

"You didn't just come here to make money... right?"

"Yeah."

Johanna's question met with a straightforward agreement.

Knowing the relationship between Rei and Vihera, Johanna this time truly looked perplexed at Rei's words.

"Um, so what exactly did you come here to do, Rei?"

"To bring Vihera back to consciousness, of course."

"No, but..."

Johanna murmured, clearly unable to fully grasp the situation.

Watching Johanna's reaction, Elk opened his mouth, looking slightly exasperated.

"Rei, maybe you should stop there? This young lady doesn't understand a word you're saying."

"Young lady..."

Johanna wanted to protest being called "young lady," but since it was Elk who said it, she couldn't really complain.

Johanna had only been in Gilm for a year or so, but naturally, she knew the face and name of Elk, a famous Rank A adventurer with a nickname even in Gilm.

There was no way a newcomer like her could offer any opinion to such a figure, and she reluctantly fell silent.

...Though, if the topic had been about Set instead of herself, she might have snapped back.

To calm herself slightly, she took a sip or two of the ale sitting on the table.

Once settled, Johanna guessed what Rei was about to say... and her expression betrayed utter shock.

"Could it be... you found a way to restore Vihera's consciousness besides a potion?"

"That's right. ...Though it's a method with various problems. That's what I was discussing with Marina earlier."

Rei recalled what Marina had told her to discuss earlier.

Gulp. Johanna swallowed her saliva, not ale, and fixed a serious gaze on Rei.

"Please tell me. How can we restore Vihera's consciousness?"

At Johanna's words, Rei glanced around.

Confirming that Johanna's companions had formed a protective wall around them, Rei finally spoke.

"I know an undead... a Lich Lord. He says if he uses the heart of an S-rank monster, he could restore Vihera's consciousness immediately."

"...Huh?"

She couldn't comprehend the words that had come out of Rei's mouth.

She didn't understand what he'd said.

Johanna looked back at Rei like that.

"Um, well... Rei, I'm not quite getting what you mean."

"Yeah, I figured."

Elk agreed with Rei's words.

Nodding as if to say he understood Johanna's feelings, he spoke up.

"I get how you feel, kid, but what Rei's saying is true. ...Well, I haven't actually seen this Rich Lord thing myself yet."

"Rich Lord... Huh? Oh, a Rank S monster? So that's why they're in this dungeon?"

Johanna was surprised by Rei's claim that he knew the Rich Lord, but she still managed to figure out why Rei and the others had come to this dungeon.

If they just wanted treatment from the Rich Lord, there was no need to come to the dungeon.

They could have just had him heal them somewhere secluded in the woods.

But if they needed the heart of an S-rank monster? Well, such rare beasts weren't exactly lying around everywhere.

But this dungeon had Silver Lions.

In terms of obtaining an S-rank monster's heart, there was undoubtedly no better place than this.

"So, what should we do? I mean, it feels weird to say this to Rei-san, but we're no match for an S-rank monster..."

Johanna murmured sadly, perhaps feeling pathetic herself.

Deep down, she wanted to do anything for Vihera.

But Johanna knew all too well that she didn't possess the strength to fight an S-rank monster.

Johanna muttered bitterly.

Her voice carried to her companions nearby, who also wore expressions of frustration.

"Don't worry... though saying it like that might sound a bit dull. We already have more than enough firepower to take on the Silver Lion. What I need you to do is watch over Vihera and Rodos in the lowest level of the dungeon while we fight the Silver Lion."

"...Ah, I see."

Johanna's expression shifted to one of understanding, though tinged with slight disappointment at Rei's words.

"I know there are no monsters other than the Silver Lion on the lowest level, but just in case something happens, I want someone to stay with them."

That was something Johanna could understand.

Since they were challenging a Rank S monster with a small, elite team, they couldn't afford to pull any extra personnel from that group.

The fact that no monsters other than Silver Lions existed on the lowest level was already common knowledge.

That's precisely why he felt strongly about hiring someone reliable, even if their skills were only average.

(Besides, my presence probably plays a big part too. There's no way they'd entrust the unconscious Vihera to a man.)

Since the others were also from the Bestir Empire, they held a deep sense of loyalty toward Vihera.

But it's also true that Vihera possesses a captivating beauty and physique that could steal anyone's gaze at first sight. What would happen if such a person lay unconscious before them...?

It was undeniable that they couldn't completely guarantee they could suppress the desires welling up inside themselves.

"So, it's sort of like escort duty...?"

"Yeah. Though in practice, it'll probably be more like an escort."

"And my son, Rhodes, too."

Elk stated this, but the moment Rhodos' name was mentioned, Johanna and the others' faces stiffened for an instant.

It was only natural. Rhodos was an enemy they had fought during the civil war.

Few had fought him directly, yet many had witnessed his intense fixation on Rei.

For that very reason, many had strong feelings about the name Rodos.

But if Rei, the one Rodos was most obsessed with, said nothing and gave his permission... Johanna nodded.

"Understood. While I can't go as far as Lady Vihera... I'll do everything possible."

"Yeah, that'll do. I've heard about your relationship with Rhodes, and doing that much is more than enough for us. ...Thanks."

Elk knew what his son had done.

Knowing that, he honestly thought it would be understandable if Johanna refused.

But still, she had firmly agreed to provide the escort. For that, he could only offer words of gratitude.

Elk's words of thanks must have dissolved the lingering resentment he still held toward Rhodes, however slight it was. Johanna returned a smile to such an Elk.

Then again, no matter what Rhodes did, Johanna never thought he could beat Rei.

That was likely why her resentment toward Rhodes was so small.

For Johanna and the former guerrilla fighters, Rei was the symbol of ultimate strength.

He single-handedly burned enemy forces to the ground and even defeated an S-rank adventurer. ...Though, Rei doesn't acknowledge defeating Noiz.

"Ah, speaking of which, Rei-san. The reward for this request... I can expect it, right? I heard you granted Milein the right to spend a day with Set-chan or something."

He recalled the incident where his rival—or rather, his natural enemy, in the sense of a foe sent by the heavens—had boasted about it to him.

Because of the Vihera incident, that right hadn't been used yet... but conversely, that meant every time she met Milein, she'd get bragged about it.

When she'd get to spend it, how they'd spend it... Johanna, who got asked about it every time they met, naturally wasn't amused.

"Fine. I'll give you the right to spend a day with Set too. The others are negotiable."

Saying that, Rei reached for the skewered meat.
