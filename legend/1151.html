Several days after leaving Gosh... Unfortunately, or perhaps inevitably, Rei had gotten lost several times along the way, but he had somehow managed to return safely to Gilm.

If Rei hadn't ridden Set, but instead had to rely solely on the same travel methods as ordinary adventurers—like carriages, horses, or walking—he probably wouldn't have made it back to Gilm until winter... or maybe even the following year.

No, considering the location of the Kingdom of Solrain, it could have easily taken years.

Rei had always been the type to get caught up in trouble.

On top of that, he had a fiery temper and couldn't resist a fight when one was offered.

Especially since hunting bandits was practically a hobby for him... it was even possible that every bandit in the area had been wiped out in his wake.

Of course, that would be something ordinary people would rejoice over.

In any case, it was sheer luck that Rei and Set, who were prone to getting lost, managed to return safely to Gilm.

Amidst the heat of midsummer, Rei walked the road alongside Set.

Given that, while Rei, clad in his Dragon Robe, might be one thing, Set stood out like a sore thumb.

And anyone in Gilm knew exactly who was with Set.

"Ah, Set!?"

"Huh? Really? It is Set. Set's here!"

"Set-chan, you disappeared so suddenly, I was worried! Oh, but I figured you probably went with Rei on some kind of request or something."

"Hmph, long time no see, Set. Here, eat this for now. ...Just so you know, I didn't prepare this for you!"

"Set-chan? Wow... It feels like it's been ages since I last saw you."

That voice spread around in an instant.

And everyone started petting Set, offering him food, spoiling him rotten.

Of course, those who noticed Set also noticed Rei...

"Oh, Rei. I hear you ran away from those merchants chasing you? Don't worry, those merchants aren't in Gilm anymore."

"Even an adventurer with a nickname has opponents they can't defeat, huh?"

"The crime of stealing my time with little Set deserves death a thousand times over. So it's only right those bastards got kicked out. You should thank me, Rei, and make more time to spend with little Set."

Many people call out to Rei like that.

(What exactly is the difference between him and Set?)

Sighing, Rei walked with Set, eventually stopping at a familiar food stall to buy something to eat as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

Compared to Gosh, it was unbelievably comfortable.

...Though, since even in Gosh he wore a Dragon Robe that functioned like a makeshift air conditioner, there was practically no difference in how Rei felt.

(Still, those merchants were kicked out. I bet I'm not the only one who wants to say 'serves them right.')

Given how those merchants had swarmed around him, it was easy to imagine they'd caused all sorts of trouble for the other folks in Gilm too.

Honestly, that made Rei feel refreshingly relieved.

...However, that pleasant mood lasted only until the very next moment.

"Oh, Rei. Welcome back. Looks like you went on a little trip somewhere without me... Had quite the fun, didn't you?"

The voice belonged to someone Rei knew well.

A woman of unparalleled beauty and allure, wearing a sheer robe that seemed to reveal what lay beneath, adorned with gauntlets and greaves.

Such a beauty stood smiling in the direction Rei was heading.

If that smile had been genuine, Rei would have smiled back and exchanged greetings with the woman before him... Vihera.

But while a smile played on Vihera's lips, none existed in her eyes.

And overflowing from her captivating form was undeniable anger.

(At least it's not murderous intent, so that's something.)

Even those who weren't adventurers could sense the anger radiating from Vihera's body.

Most of the people around Rei fell silent, barely able to utter a word.

They fell silent... but among them, two men who must have recently arrived in Gilm, unaware of Rei or Vihera, happened to pass by and addressed Vihera.

"Hey, sis. Pretty sexy outfit there. Too bad you're wearing gauntlets and greaves though."

"Huh, well now... Sis, which brothel are you from? Oh, how much?"

Those two were sorely lacking in the ability to sense danger.

What was most fatal, however, was that they had addressed Vihera at that very moment.

If they'd just glanced around, they'd have seen plenty of people keeping their distance from Vihera.

Yet those men, completely oblivious, addressed Vihera... and on top of that, judging by her appearance, it was understandable, but they mistook her for a prostitute.

...If Vihera hadn't been in a foul mood, they might have gotten away with a bit of teasing.

But unfortunately for the men, Vihera's mood right now was nothing short of terrible.

"Would you mind not touching me? Sorry, but I'm not idle enough to entertain the likes of you."

As she dodged the hands reaching for her shoulders, cold words came from Vihera's mouth.

The two men froze for a moment, seemingly unsure what they'd just heard... but they quickly realized they'd been mocked. Their faces flushed red as they prepared to yell back...

"Didn't you hear me? I don't have time to deal with the likes of you right now."

With those words, before anyone could react... before anyone could even notice, Vihera's hands had shot out and seized both men by the throat.

She wasn't crushing their throats.

Her fingers merely touched their throats.

Slender, white fingers that certainly didn't look like those of someone skilled in combat.

They touched their throats... and they couldn't make a sound.

No, since the fingers were merely touching them, they should have been able to speak without issue if they tried.

But for some reason, the men couldn't make a sound.

They were men who had rarely been involved in rough business, so they couldn't understand.

The power of Vihera, who possessed the strength to fight, however imperfectly, against extraordinary beings like Rei and Elena .

Seeing their gaze shift from lust to confusion, then to fear of an incomprehensible foe, Vihera finally withdrew her fingers.

In that instant, the two men collapsed to the ground, unable to comprehend what had happened.

Glancing at the two men, Vihera turned her gaze back to Rei.

...Beside Rei, Set was visibly frightened, but to the onlookers witnessing this scene, it seemed only natural that he would be afraid.

If Rei were a clear enemy, Set wouldn't be scared like this.

But, whether it should be called unfortunate or not, Vihera standing before Rei now was not an enemy.

While anger toward Rei existed, hostility did not.

That was precisely why Set wasn't acting like he was protecting Rei.

Gently stroking Set, Rei opened his mouth to calm Vihera.

"What are you so angry about? Of course, I feel bad I didn't take you with me, but Vihera wasn't even there when I headed for Solrain in the first place, right?"

"Yes, that's right. Unfortunately, by the time I returned, several days had already passed since Rei left Gilm."

"Right? It's true I couldn't wait for Vihera in that situation, so you don't need to be so angry, do you?"

"...I suppose so. I know this is almost like taking it out on you, right? But isn't it a bit unfair that only Rei has to fight desert monsters?"

Oh, that's what it was about! Everyone nearby who'd been listening to Vihera's story couldn't help but scream inwardly.

They'd all assumed it was some romantic squabble, but the idea that she was angry because she wanted to fight the desert monsters too... was completely unexpected for everyone present.

The only one who understood why Vihera was angry was Rei, who grasped what might be called her combat drive.

Appetite, sleep, sex... Beyond these three so-called basic human desires, Vihera possessed something that could be called a combat urge.

An unknown monster appearing in the desert—a place even Vihera had scarcely ventured into, dungeons aside.

The fact that he couldn't participate in the fight against such a monster, even if it was unavoidable, meant nothing but sorrow and lamentation for Vihera... literally, nothing but grief.

Rei stood before him as the target for those complex emotions churning within his chest.

"Well, what can I say? You never imagined things would turn out like that, did you?"

Rei had his own reasons.

He couldn't just wait around for Vihera while dealing with a merchant who wouldn't stop pestering him.

Besides, Vihera had gone back to Exil with Byune (the woman).

He couldn't afford to wait around for someone whose return date was uncertain.

If he had done that, he might have ended up with a mass production of merchants with severed limbs.

"I know that... but still."

Perhaps understanding something in Rei's words, the anger emanating from Vihera eased slightly.

Taking advantage of that momentary opening, the two men who had been harassing Vihera moments before crawled away from the scene, their legs giving out beneath them.

Under normal circumstances, some might have laughed at such a sight.

But no such person existed in this place at this moment.

"More importantly, what about Byune? Did she finish her homecoming in Exil?"

"Yes. But there's a bit of a problem. She says she'll stay in Exil until that dispute is settled. Once it's over, she'll come back here again, apparently."

"Is that okay? It's quite a distance from Exil to here, isn't it?"

At the very least, even Rei thought it was unreasonable for a child like Byune to travel alone from Exil to Gilm.

Vihera must know that too... Thinking this, he turned his gaze toward her. Vihera, catching his look, nodded as if it were obvious.

"It's fine. They understand perfectly well over there, so they won't send Byune alone. They're hiring proper escorts to come with her."

"I see."

Was it really necessary to go that far? That thought crossed his mind, but if Vihera said so, it probably wasn't just nonsense.

"So, what kind of monsters were in the desert? You can at least tell me that much, right?"

"Right. Well, we can't just keep making a fuss here forever... Shall we move somewhere else?"

As she talked with Rei, her anger finally seemed to subside.

Vihera considered Rei's suggestion, glancing around before nodding.

The place where Rei and Vihera were facing each other was the main street leading to the guild.

Such a wide road was now completely blocked for traffic because of Rei and Vihera.

While it was possible to navigate around them, the two stood right in the middle of the road. If they stayed like this, it would inevitably cause a traffic jam.

Vihera's anger had subsided somewhat, and the congestion had eased a little... but only a little.

If they kept talking here, guards might come, accusing them of obstructing the passage.

Rei's honest feeling was that he wanted to avoid having to deal with the guards so soon after returning to Gilm.

"So, where are you going? I'd prefer somewhere quiet to talk properly, somewhere without unnecessary interruptions."

As she spoke, Vihera's gaze was fixed on the direction the men who had approached her earlier had fled.

Vihera knew she attracted men, and she understood that her clothing accentuated that desire.

Even so, she wanted to avoid being interrupted while talking with Rei... especially when discussing a battle against an unknown monster.

"Yeah, you're right. The food stalls probably won't suit Vihera's tastes... So, how about we find a restaurant? I've been craving a proper meal from Gilm's kitchen for ages."

"Guruu!"

Rei stroked Set, who growled "Me too!" as he spoke. When Rei looked over, Vihera nodded in agreement.

"Alright, let's compromise on that. ...Though enjoying a leisurely meal alone with Rei was rather appealing too."

With a seductive smile, Vihera murmured.
