"...Rei? What on earth are you doing in a place like this?"

About ten days had passed since the incident with Stigma, and just as things were finally settling down within the academy, Rei was unusually walking around the academy grounds alone.

While the perpetrator who used Heaven's Tears against Rei remained unknown, most other matters had been resolved, allowing him to stroll about with ease.

Fortunately, mock battle training for both third and fourth-year S-Class students ended in the morning, leaving the afternoons completely free. He was killing time walking when he was suddenly addressed.

Rei turned toward the voice, but felt no need for caution.

The voice was familiar.

And there, at the end of Rei's gaze, stood Eland, the son of a noble.

"What are you doing here? Aren't you still in class?"

"No, actually, I got called away on some sudden business, so I'm doing self-study now."

Seeing no sign that he was hiding anything, Ray concluded he wasn't actually skipping class.

Then again, he never really suspected Eland of skipping class in the first place, as it seemed unlikely for someone like him to do such a thing.

"...Speaking of which..."

Amidst their silence, Eland suddenly spoke up.

"Hmm?"

"No, it's just... I dragged Rei into my mess before. I figured I should at least let you know."

Rei heard this, but looked at Eland with a puzzled expression, clearly unsure what he meant... Then, a few seconds later, it finally clicked. He remembered the time they had reunited.

"Ah, that time..."

"Did you forget? An event where you were attacked by so many people?"

"Well, just like you had your share of things going on, I had mine too."

Rei shrugged at Eland. Being attacked by a group of assassins, even if they weren't particularly skilled, had become a relatively minor event for him.

(I really am a magnet for trouble, aren't I? Remembering being attacked by assassins like it was just another day... Well, if I may offer an excuse, the three Stigmas who appeared right after left far too strong an impression compared to that rabble.)

Rei let out a small sigh at his own misfortune of getting caught up in trouble wherever he went, then turned his attention back to Eland and spoke.

"So, what happened to you after that? You haven't been targeted by assassins... or anything like that, I hope?"

"Hmm, you're sharp. True, I haven't been attacked by assassins since that day. That's precisely why I avoid leaving the grounds."

"...I see."

Though Rei replied thus, having heard from Headmistress Erinde that individuals suspected of being assassins had killed officers' school guards, he couldn't simply nod in agreement at those words.

"I can only feel profound gratitude toward Duke Quent."

"Huh? Why? Well, I suppose I am grateful for the shelter here..."

"Of course that's part of it. But what saved me more was that, thanks to Duke Quent's power, the one who was after my life was brought down. And stripped of his title too."

"Ah... I see."

Rei understood exactly what that meant.

It must be related to what Rei had just been thinking about moments ago—the assassins sent to the military academy.

To send assassins against the officer academy, a place meant to nurture the next generation of talent. If they'd only been after Eland's life, that might have been one thing, but such an act would naturally lead Ronald to conclude they were hostile towards him.

The knight who orchestrated this incident had been half-forced into it by Stigma, but the underlying circumstances were what they were. Knowing that wouldn't make Ronald go easy on them.

As a result, the organization targeting Eland's life had been utterly destroyed.

From there, it spread like wildfire, reaching Eland's uncle... and the outcome was precisely what Eland had just described.

(Personally, I would have preferred to see the Holy Light Church completely wiped out.)

Considering the Holy Light Church matter, a sigh escaped Rei's lips.

After the Stigma incident, Rei had initially harbored hopes that the Holy Light Church might be purged from the Kingdom of Mireana... but those hopes quickly turned to disappointment.

It was another incident following last year's chaos in the Labyrinth City of Exil, yet for some reason, no punitive action against the Holy Light Cult had been taken whatsoever.

This was despite causing such a commotion right in the stronghold of the King's faction, the most powerful group within the Mireana Kingdom itself.

Of course, this was something Rei could not overlook either.

However, according to a letter Ronald sent through Coan, the Holy Light Church had extended its roots quite deeply within the Kingdom of Mireana.

In fact, rumors had even surfaced that the death of the saint known as Stigma within this city was being viewed as problematic. Even from Rei's perspective, it was clear that leaving things as they were was untenable.

(If the Kingdom of Mireana is swallowed whole by the Holy Light Church... I might need to leave Gilm and relocate my headquarters elsewhere.)

Gilm, where Rei had established his base, was located in the frontier. With the title of Daskar's Marquis of the Frontier, it possessed a strong degree of autonomy.

Even so, it remains a territory belonging to the Mireana Kingdom, and that fact doesn't change.

Given that, if the Kingdom of Mireana were to be eroded by the Holy Light Church, it would inevitably be affected.

(I recall something similar happening in the other world too, back in the Middle Ages or something, where religion took over the state... Is this going to be like that?)

If that happened, I—someone the Holy Light Church had designated as a major enemy—could only face the worst possible outcome, no matter how you looked at it.

If I were to flee, should I escape to the Bestir Empire? Or head for the magical city of Ozos, which I'd only heard about but never visited? Or perhaps choose another country entirely.

Even amidst such dark thoughts, Rei was cheerfully imagining a bright future. But suddenly, he noticed Eland beside him staring at him with an exasperated look.

"You're lost in thought while talking to someone... It's fine with me, but if you did that to a regular noble, it would lower their opinion of you. Worst case, they might take offense... No, wait, who would dare do that to you, Rei?"

What flashed through Eland's mind was the scene of a noble drawing his weapon to punish Rei for disrespect, only to be knocked flying by Rei's fist instead.

Well, getting knocked flying might be the best-case scenario. It could easily be life-threatening.

"...Be careful, okay? Really, be careful?"

Those words weren't spoken out of concern for Rei... but rather for the nobles who might fall victim to Rei's hand.

After all, Eland wouldn't be pleased if the number of royalist nobles dwindled because they recklessly laid a hand on Rei.

Especially lately, with the crackdown on discipline, many nobles were losing their very status, like his uncle who had recently tried to kill him.

Amidst this dwindling number of royalist nobles, a further reduction in their ranks was by no means a matter of indifference to Eland.

"Hm? Well, I never really had much to do with nobles to begin with... Hm, no, wait..."

Eland shot Rei a genuinely concerned look as he stammered.

Rei, meeting that gaze, quietly looked away.

He himself didn't particularly enjoy dealing with nobles and had no desire to be involved with them, yet somehow he often found himself entangled with them. He was desperately trying to push that fact far into the recesses of his memory.

"Anyway, my business is settled. Since I caused you trouble, I figured I should at least let you know."

"...You got paid properly, so I don't think you need to worry about it."

A few days after the day Eland was attacked, Eland visited Rei to deliver his payment.

Since he'd received his payment in full, Rei couldn't understand why Eland was so concerned.

Not that it felt bad to be the focus of such concern, mind you.

(It's precisely because there are nobles like this that you can't just say, 'May all nobles perish!'...)

The faces of several nobles Rei could call acquaintances flashed through Rei's mind.

There was Elena, Daskar... and even Vihera, who was no longer a noble of the Mireana Kingdom and had already renounced her imperial status.

The faces of several other nobles also flashed in and out of his mind.

"Even if you say that, this is just my personality, so what can I do? ...So, um. That..."

Seeing Eland looking like he wanted to say something but couldn't, Rei opened his mouth, puzzled.

"What is it?"

"Well, you see... I know this is a bold request, and I'll pay whatever reward is necessary. So... would you spar with me?"

"That's a pretty sudden request to bring up."

"...No?

It wasn't quite a plea, but still, that pleading look made Rei hesitate.

But given his current position as an instructor at the officer academy, he decided it would be best to comply whenever possible, even if the request came from someone outside his assigned class.

"Alright. Luckily, no class should be using the gym right now. Let's go."

"Ah... yeah!"

Eland knew he was asking a lot.

A mock battle with Rei was something many would pay any amount to experience.

Asking him to do a mock battle, even with some connection, was pushing it, so feeling a bit guilty was only natural.

Watching Rei fight the assassin, he understood just how strong he was.

...Well, to be precise, he understood Ray was vastly stronger than himself, but he hadn't grasped just how vast that gap truly was.

I wanted that strength for myself. Since I was going to live as a noble from now on, strength was absolutely necessary.

Especially if it meant standing on the battlefield as a noble who had graduated from the military academy.

Clenching his fists, Eland followed Rei into the gymnasium.

"Haa, haa, haa..."

The sound of heavy breathing echoed through the gymnasium, empty except for Rei and Eland.

Naturally, the sound came from Eland, who sat slumped on the floor. Rei stood silently beside him, holding the mock combat spear he always used.

Several minutes passed like this. Finally, as Eland's breathing began to steady, Rei spoke.

"Come on, let's continue. You were the one who asked for this mock battle. You didn't think it would end after just this, did you?"

"Of course!"

Though his breathing had steadied, Eland's legs still trembled slightly as he stood up, gripping his mock battle longsword.

Of course, the trembling wasn't from fear of Rei, but from sheer exhaustion.

Though less than ten minutes had passed since the mock battle began, Eland's body was already utterly exhausted.

Under normal circumstances, he wouldn't be this exhausted after just ten minutes of fighting.

But when facing Rei, it was a different story.

Even his most casual swings carried immense power.

When Eland first parried Rei's spear thrust with his longsword, he was literally blown away, unable to comprehend what had just happened.

Beyond mere technique, the fundamental difference lay in overwhelming physical prowess.

He knew firsthand how formidable Rei was, having witnessed his fight with the assassin up close. He'd also heard stories from his third- and fourth-year seniors, who were also S-Class.

But seeing and hearing... it was vastly different from experiencing it firsthand.

I didn't take Rei's spear aimed at me head-on, nor did I parry it; I focused solely on evasion. And yet, for the unavoidable strikes, I deflected them.

Somehow enduring Rei's attacks like this, he would strike with his longsword, as if seizing the occasional opening Rei showed in his attacks... but even that strike was mercilessly parried by Rei's spear.

"There's a big difference between having an opening and deliberately creating one to show. It's a useful tactic to lure an enemy into attacking from your desired position, but don't make the mistake of falling for it so easily. Fight while observing carefully. If you carelessly play into their hands..."

Rei paused mid-sentence, and Eland, sensing a bad feeling, desperately pulled the parried longsword back to his side... The next instant, before he could even comprehend what had happened, his body was flying through the air.

The pain from the impact was real, but he had no clue how or why it happened.

Still, he refused to be slammed onto the floor like a ragdoll. Somehow, mid-air, he managed to regain his posture. Though it was an ugly sight, he succeeded in avoiding being slammed onto the floor on his back.

Seeing him roll across the floor multiple times, few would believe he was a noble.

Still, Eland managed to avoid losing consciousness. Staggering on unsteady legs, he pulled himself to his feet.

He then aimed his longsword at Rei... and leapt forward once more.

As Rei lived through days like these, time passed... and eventually, the day arrived when the instructor's contract ended.
