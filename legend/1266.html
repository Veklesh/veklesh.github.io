"Huh... So this is Marina's mansion."

"Grrrrrrr"

At Rei's mutter, Set purred happily.

...Though that delight wasn't because he got to see Marina's mansion, but rather because he saw the falling snow.

"She said it wasn't that big, but I guess she was being modest."

"Mm."

Vihera, murmuring beside Rei, received a nod of agreement from Byune.

Compared to other mansions in the Noble District, it was undoubtedly small.

But even so, compared to buildings outside the noble district, it was undoubtedly large enough to be considered substantial.

At the very least, it could be considered too spacious for Marina to live in alone.

It was about the size of Johanna's residence, only slightly smaller...

Of course, it had a courtyard, and though seemingly unused now, stables were also visible.

From that mansion, its owner—Marina—appeared and began walking toward Rei and the others.

Wearing her usual party dress, the red of her gown stood out vividly against the white snow piled up around her.

She must be using spirit magic. Wherever Marina walked, the snow melted away cleanly.

(It's convenient, isn't it? No need to shovel snow or anything.)

Rei was deeply impressed by the convenience of spirit magic, but suddenly thought, Couldn't I use my own magic to melt the snow?

He thought about it... but ultimately decided that even if he tried, the melted snow would just freeze again, creating all sorts of hazards, and quickly gave up on the idea.

Marina's spirit magic merely moved the snow itself, so there was no particular concern. However, if Rei used fire magic to melt the snow, it would become water vapor.

If he tried to completely evaporate even that moisture... he risked causing a steam explosion if he wasn't careful.

Taking such risks just for shoveling snow was something Rei simply couldn't bring himself to do.

...Though, it was probably also because doing such a thing would disappoint Set, who enjoyed playing in the snow almost every day.

"Welcome. ...Come to think of it, it feels like it's been ages since I said something like this in this mansion."

"Oh? Not many people come here?"

"Well, yes. I mostly greet guests at the guild. Honestly, I only come back to this mansion to sleep. ...Frankly, if it's just for sleeping, I think an inn would be fine."

"Well, a guildmaster living in an inn? Honestly, what kind of situation is that?"

At Marina's words, Rei muttered with a hint of exasperation.

Of course, inns range from cheap lodgings for newly minted adventurers to high-end establishments frequented by wealthy merchants.

But for Marina, in her position as Guildmaster, staying at an inn—especially in her own home city, rather than when visiting another town—would indeed give people the wrong impression.

Given the prevailing sentiment that those of high standing should live accordingly, a guildmaster and former Rank A adventurer like Marina needed at least this much of a mansion to reside in.

Understanding this, Marina responded to Rei's words with a seductive smile and a shrug.

...At that moment, her brown twin peaks swayed vigorously within the wide-open neckline, making a bold statement. Rei merely averted his gaze gently.

Seeing Rei like that, Marina flashed a smile that was alluring in a different way than just seconds before.

"Well, there's no point standing out here in the snow talking. Come inside. As for Set..."

"GRRR!"

At Marina's words, Set turned his gaze toward the mansion's courtyard.

There lay the snow that had fallen and piled up.

Fresh snow, untouched and untrodden, fallen and piled up just as it had come down.

For Set, who'd recently become obsessed with playing in the snow, that courtyard was the ultimate playground.

Seeing Set like this, Rei and Vihera smiled, while Marina wore a slightly perplexed expression.

However, while Set could enter Marina's mansion, it wasn't spacious enough for him to run around freely.

Considering that, if Set wanted to play in the courtyard, she had no intention of stopping him.

"You can play in the courtyard as much as you like. Just don't break any trees, okay?"

"Guruu!"

"No problem!" Set growled, then plunged straight into the courtyard, crunching through the snow.

Marina's spirit magic had only piled snow from the entrance to the main gate.

Seeing Set enjoying the feel of fresh snow beneath his paws, Rei and the others couldn't help but smile.

His innocent demeanor hardly resembled that of a high-rank monster like a griffin.

He looked more like a small child playing.

(Well, that feeling isn't wrong, though.)

Rei found himself agreeing with that thought inwardly.

After all, it hadn't even been five years since Set was born through beast magic.

By normal standards, it was still young enough to be considered a child.

...Of course, being monsters, many reach adulthood within mere months of birth.

Regardless, while Seto charged straight into the courtyard, trampling the snow, rolling around on it, and leaving his body imprint, Rei and the others were guided by Marina into the mansion.

(Set was rolling around in the snow... but are his wings okay? I hope he didn't hurt himself.)

Rei pondered this as he entered the mansion.

The mansion was deathly quiet.

There was no sign of maids or butlers, yet it was so clean it seemed not a speck of dust could be found.

"Wow, this is incredible."

"Mm."

"Really?"

Vihera let out a gasp of astonishment, and Byune agreed.

But Marina showed no particular reaction to their surprise.

"Of course. This mansion is pretty spacious, yet it stays this clean... There don't seem to be any maids or anything. How do you manage it?"

"Oh, didn't I mention it before? With spirit magic, this is easy."

"...Marina, what on earth are you using spirit magic for?"

Magic in general is rare, but spirit magic is exceptionally so.

While it's primarily used by elves and dark elves, those races rarely appear in public.

Some humans could wield spirit magic too, but such individuals were naturally even rarer than mages.

So why on earth would someone use such rare spirit magic for cleaning...? That was the tone of utter bewilderment.

"Oh? But you didn't say anything when I cleared the snow, did you?"

"That's because it's useful for taking on requests. Honestly, I'm incredibly jealous."

Snow is surprisingly troublesome to walk through.

You slip, lifting your feet takes more energy than usual, and when it melts, it soaks the ground, making it even harder to walk.

With carriages, wheels can get stuck in ruts, and on fresh snow, wheels might not turn at all.

For those reasons, using spirit magic to clear the snow and make walking easier made perfect sense for an adventurer.

But... cleaning?

Sure, it's better than nothing, but even so... the feeling of "seriously?" was stronger.

"Marina, you, really..."

"What? It's convenient, isn't it?"

Seeing Marina state this so casually, Vihera decided it was pointless to say anything more.

She also felt it was inappropriate for others to comment on how she chose to use her abilities.

"I suppose so. If it's fine with you, Marina, then it's fine."

"Right? Well, the room is this way. I'll get drinks ready right away. Tea is fine, right?"

Rei and the others nodded at Marina's words and were led to the reception room.

The reception room, naturally, was spotless, not a speck of dust in sight. The moment they entered, Rei and the others widened their eyes slightly.

But having already seen it once, they weren't about to be startled again by the same thing. They settled onto the prepared sofa.

"Just wait here for a moment,"

Marina said, then left the room.

Rei and the others watched her go, then looked around the room again.

Marina had said she was only returning to sleep, but she had clearly made thorough preparations in case someone came.

Several landscape paintings adorned the walls, including one depicting the World Tree.

The reception room contained a heating magic item, and it had already been warmed before Rei and the others entered.

"This room's surprisingly comfortable."

"Hmm."

Vihera murmured, and Byune nodded as she surveyed the surroundings.

Beyond the paintings on the walls, several pieces of furniture in calm, tasteful colors were present, making it clear one could relax comfortably in this room.

"Sorry to keep you waiting. Here you go."

As Rei and the others looked around the reception room, Marina returned with tea.

Setting the tea on the table, Marina sat down on the sofa and spoke.

"Well, it's just a humble home, but please make yourselves comfortable today."

Marina hadn't invited Rei and the others to her home—her mansion—today for any particular reason.

She simply wanted to talk with them at ease in her own home.

If she had to put it into words, she wanted to talk a little about how things would be when she acted together with Rei and the others after spring... after handing over the position of Guild Master to Walker.

She would become an advisor, while simultaneously becoming an adventurer herself... essentially a half-guild employee, half-adventurer.

With this group gathered, it was only natural that the conversation would turn to that topic.

"Marina joining us shouldn't be a problem for the guild, right?"

"Yes, that's right. Of course, the guild won't give me any special treatment just because I'm here."

"...But Marina has been acting as Guildmaster up until now, right? Doesn't that mean there's a chance guild staff might unconsciously..."

"Well, I can't deny that possibility. However, Walker tends to be quite strict about that sort of thing. I'm also mindful of preventing such situations."

Marina has been Guildmaster of Gilm for many years.

Her track record is universally acknowledged, and as a former Rank A adventurer, her skill is undeniably high. Above all, she embodies the very essence of feminine allure.

In terms of popularity, she undoubtedly ranks among the top guildmasters in all of Elgin.

Because of that allure, female adventurers who pride themselves on their charm instinctively judge they stand no chance against her and avoid her, but she is deeply admired by most others.

Given that, it was impossible to completely dismiss the possibility that, almost unconsciously and without even realizing it, he might show Marina some consideration.

"I think Lenora or Kenny would probably be fine, though."

Hmm, huh. Hearing Marina's words, Vihera murmured in admiration.

Of course, Vihera knew exactly what kind of people Lenora and Kenny were.

...Of course she did. After all, Vihera was acting alongside Rei.

It was practically inevitable that he would meet Lenora, who was in charge of Rei, and Kenny, who harbored feelings for Rei.

Especially since Kenny was clearly interested in Vihera—no surprise, given Kenny's own reputation for being quite the charmer.

Though, lately, Rei had mostly seen Kenny bundled up in layers.

Even when he took off his coat at the herbal tea shop, he was still somewhat bulky.

"More importantly, shouldn't we decide on a party name?"

Vihera suggested, even as she thought about one of her love rivals.

Now that she mentioned it... Rei and the others agreed with that suggestion.

Up until now, Rei, Vihera, and Byune had been acting together, but they hadn't specifically formed a party.

It was more accurate to say they'd just been together.

But if Marina was joining them, it was definitely better to formally establish a party.

Otherwise, now that Marina had returned to adventuring, even if only part-time, countless people would undoubtedly try to recruit her.

"Hmm, but Marina's half-guild employee, right? Can she join a party?"

"Ah, it'll be fine. Even if she's an advisor, she doesn't actually get paid for it. ...Though, knowing Walker, he'll probably get strict about things to avoid any favoritism if I join the party."

"That shouldn't be a problem."

"...The most dangerous one seems to be Rei, though."

Marina muttered in a slightly exasperated tone at Rei's immediate response.

But Rei was serious.

If push came to shove, she could quit being an adventurer... and just become an ordinary traveler, she reasoned.

"As for Byune... well, I suppose she'll leave the party when it's time to return to Exil?"

"Mm!"

Byune nodded at Vihera's words, and they continued chatting over tea until evening.
