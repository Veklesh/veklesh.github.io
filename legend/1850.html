"Hey, wait a minute. Where the hell are you taking me? Why on earth are we in a place like this…?"

Gaiska’s words echoed down the dark path.

It was a street lit only by the moon and stars, with not a single magical item like the ones in Gaiska’s mansion to provide any light.

On top of that, perhaps because this area was close to the slums, there was a sense that many people were still awake, even though it was already past midnight.

Gaiska was trained to a certain extent, but that was all it was.

If he were as well-trained as adventurers… or soldiers—those who make a living from combat—he wouldn’t have felt particularly intimidated by the figures lurking nearby.

But that was only true if he had been trained; expecting the current Gaiska to act that way was simply unreasonable.

No matter what the situation, someone like Gaiska rarely has to handle things on his own.

It was limited to situations where he wanted to avoid being noticed by others—like when he hired the Black Wolf.

For that very reason, walking down a path he had never taken before was something he wanted to avoid at all costs.

“Heh heh heh. Is someone of Lord Gaiska’s stature actually afraid of a little darkness like this? Look, there’s moonlight and starlight here, so I don’t think it’s anything to worry about.”

The words that came from the intermediary’s mouth had a somewhat provocative tone to them.

Normally, directing such words at Gaiska would be pointless… or rather, it would only serve to anger him.

Indeed, if this were the Seisor Marquis’s House, no one who obeyed Gaiska’s orders—Deotres included—would ever tolerate such an attitude.

...Of course, Deotres had already vanished from the Seisor Marquis’s House, but unfortunately, Gaiska was unaware of that.

Had he known, Gaesca might have sensed something amiss.

In any case, despite his irritation at the words spoken by the guide, Gaesca resolved not to make a further spectacle of himself and spoke once more.

“There’s no way I’d be afraid of a little darkness like this. All I’m saying is, why on earth would you bring me—a noble member of the Seisor Marquis House—to a place like this?”

“Oh, man…”

At Gaiska’s words, the guide muttered quietly under his breath.

He hadn’t expected Gaiska to say something like that here.

Needless to say, this was a slum. In other words, there were many people here in dire financial straits, and to them, the fact that Gaiska was a noble meant he was seen as someone they could kidnap and extort money from.

Under those circumstances, saying something like that here would normally be nothing short of suicide.

...That said, that would only be true if Gaiska were the only one present.

“Lord Gaiska, we must hurry. To the people here, you are like a precious gem. If you linger here carelessly, there’s no telling what lengths they might go to in order to get their hands on you.”

Perhaps feeling flattered by the comparison to a jewel, Gaiska completely wiped away the slight trace of fear that had been on her face moments before and smiled contentedly.

“Is that so? I’m a jewel, am I? …Well, I suppose so. To those who live in a place like this, it wouldn’t be strange for them to see me as something as brilliant as the sun.”

“...I suppose so.”

Perhaps he hadn’t expected his own words to put Gaiska in such a good mood; the man struggled slightly to hide his inner exasperation.

(A jewel, huh. What a nasty jewel. At the very least, it’s a jewel I wouldn’t want.)

Despite these thoughts, drawing on his experience as a mediator, he refrained from letting them show on his face, instead returning a nod with a smile.

“Anyway, I can’t very well give a gem to people like those over there, so let’s hurry.”

“Hmm, is that so? I thought a gem like me absolutely had to be shown to as many people as possible.”

Gaiska nodded contentedly at the man’s words, but upon hearing them, the slum dwellers began to stir, their irritation clearly evident.

Given that they were in a place like this, it was no surprise that many harbored nothing but ill will toward the nobility.

And yet, faced with such behavior, it would be nearly impossible for them to simply accept it.

Several of them tried to step forward to vent their frustration at Gaiska... but stopped just before they could move.

It was a danger that only the slum dwellers could understand.

If they had stepped forward here, they would almost certainly have been killed.

It wasn’t Gaiska who would do that… but the shabby-looking man standing beside him.

Realizing this, the others—though still feeling irritated with Gaiska—ultimately said nothing more, fell silent, and hid back inside the building.

Gaiska probably had no idea that such an exchange had taken place.

The man acting as the intermediary thought it was a hassle, but since he was the one who had brought up the matter of the jewels, he had no intention of saying anything further.

“Well then, let’s be on our way. There are people waiting for you, Lord Gaiska.”

Hearing those words, Gaiska, who had been in a good mood, felt slightly put off.

This was because, despite having lavished praise on him by calling him a “jewel” and the like, the man clearly implied that the person he claimed was waiting for him was of a higher rank than he was.

However, just as Gaiska was about to voice his dissatisfaction to the intermediary, he managed to hold back at the last moment.

……If Deotres and the others had known about this, they would undoubtedly have been surprised.

After all, the Gaiska that most people knew was not the sort of person capable of such restraint.

All things considered, it must have been that much more unsettling for him to be in a place like this without a reliable partner like Deotres by his side.

“Hmm. I see. Well then, let’s hurry. I don’t want to stay in a place like this forever either. …But just to be sure, the place you’re taking me isn’t going to be like this, is it?”

“Yes, of course not. It’s fit for Lord Gaiska… though I’m not sure if I should say that, but I’ve made every possible preparation. I don’t think you’ll be disappointed.”

At those words, Gaiska shot him a suspicious glance for just a moment.

That said, the look of suspicion wasn’t one that suggested the intermediary was deceiving him; it was simply a doubt as to whether he could truly provide hospitality befitting Gaiska.

The intermediary man understood that he was being looked at that way.

But rather than worrying that he was truly being suspected, he took that look—if that was all it was—and accepted it with delight.

In any case, the two of them continued down the road… and as they passed through a street lined with numerous shacks, a single House eventually came into view.

It wasn’t large enough to be called a mansion, but it was the kind of house you would never expect to find in a slum like this.

Given that the surrounding structures—though I hesitate to even call them that—were indeed buildings, the House stood out strikingly.

“This House… is this the place you were talking about?”

“Yep. Someone here is waiting for Lord Gaiska. They’re asking if he’ll lend a hand with this recent incident.”

What exactly did “this matter” refer to? It was obvious without even having to think about it.

In other words, it must be about preparing the reward to pay the Black Wolf.

Or perhaps he was referring to the very act of opposing Rei.

Even Gaiska didn’t know exactly what he was referring to. He didn’t know… but even so, for Gaiska right now, there was only one person he could rely on.

Of course, Gaiska himself didn’t see it that way.

If anything, it wouldn’t have been strange for him to want to say, “You should be grateful that I’m lending you my strength.”

...Still, he possesses enough sense not to voice such thoughts here.

That said, the fact that he still can’t completely hide his true feelings is probably just typical of Gaiska.

“Lord Gaiska, it’s easy to get in, isn’t it?”

“Yeah.”

Nodding at those words, Gaiska heads to the House.

But on the way, Gaiska suddenly notices something.

That was… because there were no guards to be seen anywhere.

Not even a gatekeeper was in sight.

If this weren’t a slum, that wouldn’t have been so strange.

But this is the slums.

There were plenty of people struggling financially here, so the fact that there were no guards at any of the Houses was nothing short of strange.

“Hey, don’t you have any guards here?”

“No, there aren’t any fools who’d even think of breaking into this mansion. If there were… it would probably only be someone who’s just arrived in Anesis.”

There was a strong sense of confidence in the man’s words.

His attitude seemed to declare with absolute certainty that no one here would ever attack the House, no matter what.

Though Gaiska felt a slight doubt at those words, he said nothing more.

He likely understood that whatever he said now would be meaningless.

“I see. Well then, who is in the House? I’ll look forward to finding out.”

It was partly bravado, but even so, it seemed to work this time.

That was merely Gaiska’s own impression, however; he had absolutely no idea whether it would actually work.

In any case, having come this far, Gaiska had no choice but to enter the House.

Since he had already harmed those inside the Seisor Marquis’s House, he couldn’t possibly return to the Seisor Marquis’s House empty-handed.

In reality, even if Gaiska returned to the mansion with a gift, Zascal would never forgive him.

And that was only natural.

He tried to sell the mansion in the important location of Anesis to hire a skilled assassin to attack Rei, and after that was discovered and he was placed under house arrest, he slipped out of the mansion with an unknown companion.

And he did so while harming the residents of the mansion—those who served the Marquis Seisor’s House.

Gaiska had ordered him not to kill anyone, but while the man outwardly obeyed those words, he intentionally took several lives.

This was a scheme to prevent Gaiska from returning to the House of Seisor, but unfortunately, Gaiska remains unaware of it.

He never once suspects that the man guiding him might harm him.

No matter what he does, he is convinced that he—a man of noble blood—is absolutely in the right, and that this man must obey him.

“Well then, please come in.”

Opening the door, the man welcomed Gaiska inside the House.

Seeing the look on the man’s face, Gaiska felt a chill run down her spine, but she told herself it wasn’t because she was afraid of the man before her… or of the House itself, but simply because it was winter and cold, and she took a step forward.

And when he stepped inside the House, the scene that unfolded before him was quite unexpected.

Well, given that this house was located in a slum, it was unexpected in many ways to begin with.

However, judging by the scene before her now, she couldn’t act rashly no matter what. That thought surfaced in Gaiska’s mind.

Of course, that was merely Gaiska’s own perception; if he were determined to act, he could certainly do so.

That said, whatever he decided to do, he couldn’t just stay cowering in fear like this.

He took a small breath in and out, trying to calm himself.

In his current state, Gaiska had a strange conviction that no matter what he tried to do, he wouldn’t be able to move properly.

Therefore, he needed to calm himself down.

And so, Gaiska turned her gaze once more to the scene before her.

A collection of furnishings so magnificent they seemed out of place in a slum… in fact, so magnificent that even Gaiska had rarely seen anything like them.

It was the kind of scene where, if someone told him this was an art museum, he wouldn’t be surprised to believe it…

“...Am I dreaming?”

Gaiska’s muttered words drifted off into the void… as if they had been swallowed up by the myriad furnishings.
