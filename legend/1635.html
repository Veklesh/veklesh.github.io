It had been about ten days since we passed through the checkpoint between the Kingdom of Reburina and the Kingdom of Mireana… As we passed through several villages and towns along the way, our escort grew larger with each stop.

Even so, since they’d been chosen more for their character than their strength, there weren’t that many who could truly be called skilled fighters… which is precisely why some were trying to sell themselves to us by any means necessary.

“Come on, just hire me! Looks like it’s all women here, right? Well, I’ll take the job, so just leave it to me!”

At a guild in one of the towns we stopped at along the way, such a voice rang out.

Perhaps due to the increasing number of reasonably skilled adventurers—unlike in the Kingdom of Reburina—more people had recently been approaching her with such confidence in their abilities.

……If they’d let Set into town, there probably wouldn’t be anyone making such a fuss.

Furthermore, the fact that they’re only moderately skilled—not skilled enough to recognize Rei’s strength—complicates matters in this case.

And above all, the fact that Marina and Vihera are part of Rei’s party is likely a major factor.

If things went well, they might even get close to these two beauties.

It was only natural for the men to think that way.

(The only consolation is that Elena wasn’t there. …No, wait—since Elena might be recognized, perhaps the commotion wouldn’t have happened if she had been there?)

Almost as if to escape reality, Rei recalled Elena, who had been discussing something with Ara regarding the Noble Faction.

But to the man demanding to be hired, Rei’s attitude was apparently unforgivable.

“Hey, are you even listening?! I’m telling you to hire me!”

“……Even if you say that. We’re looking to hire someone with a good personality, even if their skills are just average.”

“Then I should be just fine, shouldn’t I?”

The man replied to Rei’s words without a moment’s hesitation.

There was no doubt he genuinely believed he had no issues and possessed a good personality.

Seeing the man like that, Rei was momentarily dumbfounded… but the next instant, he shook his head.

“Looking at you right now, I can’t for the life of me see you as having a good personality.”

In fact, the man was casting lustful glances at both Marina and Vihera.

Furthermore, seeing his eyes light up upon hearing that the group he’s to guard consists of nearly a thousand women makes it clear exactly what he’s hoping for.

If he were courting her out of genuine affection, that might be one thing, but seeing him display such blatant lust, Rei has no intention of hiring someone like that as a bodyguard.

As it is, the majority of Rei’s group consists of people who were brainwashed by slave collars and forced into prostitution against their will.

Given that, it’s only natural that they’d be sensitive to the men’s leering glances.

The reason Set is so adored by so many women is partly due to his inherent charm, but more significantly, it’s because he is an animal—or, to be precise, a monster—and doesn’t look at them in that way.

What would happen if a man like the one currently clamoring to be included in Rei’s personal guard were to be introduced into such a group?

He would undoubtedly cause trouble.

That’s exactly why Rei said what he did, but in this case, his timing was poor.

Making a remark that seemed to belittle himself in front of two beauties like Marina and Vihera.

And above all, the fact that it was Rei who made that remark.

……If Rei had been carrying his Death Scythe, the man might have realized who he really was… but he certainly couldn’t just pull out his Death Scythe in the middle of town, especially when they weren’t even in a fight.

For those reasons, it wasn’t unreasonable for the man to assume that Rei was simply tagging along with Marina and Vihera… in other words, that he was a fox borrowing the tiger’s might.

He couldn’t imagine such a person having a good personality… In other words, if someone called him mean-spirited, it was only natural that the blood would rush to the head of a man who was already rough-and-tumble by nature.

“What did you say, you brat! Do you even know who you’re talking to!?”

The man shouted menacingly and reached out toward Rei.

In the past, most people would have been rendered speechless by a man’s intimidating outburst.

Rei was likely one of them.

But judging by the situation around him, there’s no doubt he holds significant influence within the group he leads.

If he could intimidate a man like that and make him obey his orders, he’d be in for a treat. Hell, he might even be able to make Marina and Vihera his own women.

That was his intention... but the next moment, as if struck by a sudden impact, the man found himself leaning against the wall.

“Gah… ugh…”

Even through leather armor, the impact of such a hard collision couldn’t be completely absorbed.

Without understanding what had happened, the man lost consciousness right then and there.

“Um… uh… isn’t this usually where I come in?”

Watching the man who had hit the wall and lost consciousness, Rei muttered in confusion.

He had intended to counterattack if the man tried to punch him.

But before Rei could do anything, the man had already been knocked flying by someone else.

From Rei’s perspective, it felt as if his prey had been snatched away from him.

He couldn’t help but think, Isn’t this exactly what they mean by “having your fried tofu snatched away by a kite”?

“No, I’m sorry! My idiot said some stupid things and did some stupid stuff… he’s really an idiot.”

How many times did he say “idiot”?

Thinking that, Rei looked at the person bowing to him.

He was even shorter than Rei, yet the arm that had struck the man was visibly muscular.

Rei didn’t know who this person was—a man with a thick beard stretching from his chin down to his chest—but he could tell exactly what kind of person he was.

“A dwarf?”

“Yeah, I’m Sinarama, the guildmaster of this guild. I’m truly sorry for what my idiot did. …And, well, I’m also sorry to Marina…”

“Huh?”

Seeing Sinarama like that, Rei couldn’t help but voice his confusion.

That was because Sinarama was clearly showing signs of nervousness in front of Marina.

Well, if it were just nervousness, that might not be so strange, given that Marina has been a guildmaster for so many years at Gilm.

But the Shinara standing before Marina was not only nervous; his face was also beaming with joy.

It was as if his very demeanor was saying that he was overjoyed to have met Marina.

It’s not as if dwarves and elves are at odds with each other here in Elgin, as is often the case.

Of course, with so many dwarves and elves around, there are bound to be some who don’t get along.

But since they aren’t enemies by race, a scene like the one before her isn’t all that strange.

(It’s not strange, but… it still feels a bit off.)

In the manga and novels Rei used to read back in Japan, elves and dwarves were usually portrayed as being on hostile terms.

That’s precisely why seeing a dwarf looking at Marina with such admiration feels so out of place.

He’d seen elves and dwarves getting along in Gilm a few times before… but the way Sinarama was acting now seemed to imply something more than that.

“Oh, you… you’re Sinarama, aren’t you? I see. I heard you became a guild master, but I didn’t realize it was here.”

“Yes. I caused you quite a bit of trouble in the past, Lady Marina… and yet, I’m sorry to have caused you trouble again today.”

The way he bowed deeply made it clear at a glance that Shinara held Marina in the highest regard.

And… it was the adventurers in the guild who were surprised to see Shinara like that.

Some were hesitating whether to help Rei and the others who were being harassed, others were thinking it served them right, and still others were looking on disinterestedly as if it were business as usual… Many of them were staring at Shinara, who had bowed his head, with looks of disbelief.

To the adventurers of this guild, Shinrama was a guildmaster who could be called a strong, reliable older brother.

He was famous in these parts for never backing down when a guild adventurer had a run-in with a noble in the past; on the contrary, he had consistently defended the adventurer and even went so far as to force the noble to apologize.

Given that such a man as Shinara was bowing his head, the honest reaction of anyone witnessing the scene would likely be that it was simply unbelievable.

“It’s fine. There are plenty of adventurers with that kind of personality, after all.”

“I’m sorry. I’ll make sure to discipline him properly.”

Saying this, Shinara turned her gaze toward the man she had just knocked down.

The man had been knocked unconscious by the impact of his back hitting the wall, but if he were conscious, he would surely be trembling at Shinara’s glare.

Those around him looked at him with pity.

They understood what fate awaited him from tomorrow onward.

Considering Shinara’s personality… and the fact that he’d tried to pick a fight with someone Shinara respected and liked so much.

To be precise, it wasn’t Marina—whom Shinrama respected—but her companion, Rei, whom he had tried to harass… but the result would likely be the same.

No, in fact, given that he directed his lustful gaze at Marina, his punishment might actually be even harsher.

“Hey, listen. That dark elf… who is he? I can’t quite believe Shinrama-san is being so deferential to him.”

“I’d love to know myself. Shinara-san’s a big shot around here, right? And yet, to bow his head like that in front of everyone…”

“My lady, you’re wonderful.”

“Wait, hey. Why are you suddenly calling her ‘Sis’?”

Amidst such voices, the conversation between Shinrama and Marina continued.

“So, how many adventurers here have no personality issues? I don’t know if we’ve received any intel yet, but we’re currently on the move with a group of nearly a thousand people. We need adventurers to serve as our escort… specifically, ones who won’t cause any trouble.”

As she explained, Marina’s gaze turned toward Gimekara.

Originally, the plan was for Gimekara to hire escort adventurers right here and now.

However, because the man from earlier had accosted Rei in the middle of it, those discussions had come to a halt.

“Gimekara, can I handle the negotiations? It’d probably be awkward for Sinarama to deal with me directly.”

Setting aside the fact that demonstrating one’s work in front of someone they respect is one thing, actually negotiating with that person is something some people would prefer to avoid if possible.

And Marina knew that Shinrama was exactly that kind of person.

That was precisely why she had entrusted the negotiations to Gimekara.

……That said, since the Zolgue Trading Company had already agreed to provide support for Rei and the others’ journey, it was only natural for Gimekara to handle the negotiations here.

“Yes, leave it to me. …First off, our destination is Gilm. So, if there are any of you who wish to go to Gilm, I’d recommend the escort service to you. We’ll cover all expenses—lodging, water, and food—and the meals we serve are so good you’d never think you’re camping out.”

“Hmm, Gilm, huh. There are quite a few folks who’d like to go to Gilm, but…”

When Shinara turned his gaze toward the receptionist, she seemed to understand immediately what the Guild Master was getting at. She spoke up right away.

“As for their requirements, they say they’re looking for adventurers with decent skills and a good personality. Since they’re traveling with nearly a thousand women, I suppose that aspect inevitably becomes an issue.”

“Ah… that’s true. There are plenty of skilled adventurers, but when you add good character to the mix, the number drops drastically.”

As he said this, Shinrama glared at the adventurers staring at him.

Regardless of their skills, the adventurers who’d been told they had character issues hurriedly looked away.

In truth, they knew full well that, skill aside, they had various character flaws.

That was precisely why they had averted their eyes from Shinrama.

Seeing the adventurers’ reaction, Shinrama let out a sigh.

“As you can see. Of course, it’s not as if there’s not a single one, but at least among the people here right now, there isn’t a single one who fits your criteria.”

It was almost noon.

Despite the time of day, the fact that they were at the guild without accepting any quests meant they couldn’t exactly be called serious adventurers.

That said, the number of days an adventurer takes to rest after finishing a single quest varies from person to person.

Since there are people like that here too, it doesn’t necessarily mean they’re all hopeless.

“Alright, then. So, assuming we’re choosing based on personality, what about the reward…?”

“Hmm, I’m not quite convinced by that. Raise the reward a bit more.”

“But we’re providing almost everything, starting with meals…”

The others present watched in silence as the sudden negotiation began.
