In one of the most magnificent buildings in Gosh... Dalidra sat in a room, drinking ice-cold water while listening to a report from a subordinate.

Outside, the temperature approached forty degrees Celsius, yet the room Dalidra occupied remained a mere thirty degrees.

Of course, even that would be hot enough for most people, but Dalidra was also from Gosh... a man born and raised in the desert.

Thirty degrees felt rather comfortable, almost the perfect temperature.

"So? You haven't interfered with Rei's actions, have you?"

"Yes. As instructed, we haven't interfered at all... but are you truly certain this is wise? Couldn't that man become an obstacle to you, Dalidra -sama?"

"Well... If we're talking about being an obstacle, he absolutely is one. A super-sized one at that."

"Then, even if defeating him is impossible, shouldn't we at least devise a way to drive him out of Gosh?"

Though the words came from a man who had served the Elema Trading Company since the previous generation, Dalidra immediately shook her head.

"No! Consider this: laying a hand on him means certain ruin!"

Though Dalidra usually strove to remain calm, her subordinate's words made her involuntarily emphasize her words with force.

She possessed some information about what kind of person Rei was.

But... what made her recognize Rei as a major threat was the advice from the very men she employed as her guards.

She had spent a fortune to gather these bodyguards—not just from Gosh, but from Solrain and neighboring nations.

They were guards worth the enormous sums paid to hire them, and the very fact that he employed such guards served as a significant source of power for the Elema Trading Company.

They warned that antagonizing the Elema Trading Company meant making those bodyguards your enemies.

This was no joke. Dalidra genuinely believed that with his guards, he could easily destroy a village.

And these were adventurers he trusted implicitly... even including high-ranking B-rank adventurers among them. Yet, every single one of them had come to tell him the same thing.

That they absolutely should not antagonize this person named Rei.

Worse still, they declared that if he insisted on antagonizing Rei, they would resign as his guards, even if it meant paying a penalty.

Given the high fees he paid them, the penalty would naturally be astronomical.

Even so, the guards insisted it was better to settle it with money than risk losing their lives.

Given the words of adventurers whose abilities he trusted, Dalidra couldn't force them into battle. ...That said, having obtained information on Rei, Dalidra himself had never considered any reckless actions that might antagonize Rei.

Even so, hearing his own guards speak that way was nothing short of shocking.

Though Dalidra hadn't intended to oppose Rei, the guards' words only strengthened her resolve.

"...Is that how it is?"

Dalidra nodded deeply at her subordinate's words.

"Yes. From what I've heard, he possesses the strength to single-handedly determine the outcome of a war. Take last year's conflict between the Kingdom of Mireana and the Bestir Empire... That battle started evenly matched, but through his actions alone, he killed a third of the Bestir Empire's forces."

"What!? ...Is such a thing... truly possible?"

Though it came from his master, the man declared it hard to believe. Dalidra finished the lukewarm water in his cup and continued.

"Of course, this is likely an exaggerated rumor. But according to the intelligence I've gathered, it seems true that he performed feats decisive enough to alter the course of the war."

"...A human? Even if it were the dragon I've heard rumors about, taking human form..."

"Heh heh, a dragon? Well, yes. Among the information I gathered, there were also reports of someone commanding a dragon... but it seems it wasn't a dragon after all, but a griffin."

To be precise, the rumor spread because he was seen with Yellow, a child of the species known as Black Dragons or Black Dragons.

Had I gathered more information on Elena , a renowned figure even within the Kingdom of Mireana, I might have learned that she used Yellow as a familiar... Unfortunately, that information hadn't reached Dalidra .

"A griffin, you say. Honestly, it's my first time seeing a real one. ...I never imagined it would be that impressive..."

The man must have seen Rei walking with Set. He murmured thoughtfully.

Just looking at its appearance, it seemed incredibly friendly, and indeed, he had seen it getting along well with the merchants and adventurer guards of the Auguste caravan.

But... even so, to the man's eyes, a griffin seemed like an utterly formidable creature.

"Yes. What's the status on the intel from over there? I believe I instructed them to find out when Rei will be leaving this Gosh."

"Unfortunately, nothing yet. Should we hurry them along?"

Hearing the man's question, Dalidra thought for a moment and shook her head.

"No, let's hold off. True to his nickname, Rei is sharp. If we push him too hard here, they'll realize he's one of ours."

"So, regarding the intel from the other side... proceed without pushing too hard... as usual, then?"

Dalidra nodded at the man's words, deciding to adopt a wait-and-see approach toward Auguste and Rei for the time being.

At the counter of the guild in Gosh, the receptionist gasped in surprise at the sight before her, but quickly composed herself with a look of understanding.

What lay before her was sand serpent material... a folded piece of skin.

The skin of a Sand Serpent approximately five meters long—though effectively around four meters since Rei had crushed its head.

Despite its size, it was neatly folded, allowing it to just barely fit on the counter.

Naturally, the existence of a Rank C monster was rare, but the receptionist knew the person before her was the famed Rank B adventurer, Rei the Crimson.

If any adventurer in this town brought in Sand Serpent materials, they'd be surprised.

After all, with few exceptions, Gosh's adventurers were by no means exceptionally skilled.

And even among those exceptions, most are adventurers employed by the Elema Trading Company. Purely Gosh-born adventurers who are truly skilled are extremely rare.

"Sand Serpent materials, you say? Please wait a moment."

Precisely because this receptionist had been the one Rei told yesterday that he was Crimson, she quickly recovered from her confusion and began preparing to appraise the purchase. Just to be sure, she turned to ask Rei.

"So, is the Sand Serpent material just the skin?"

The Sand Serpent's meat, organs, and bones are also useful materials.

Particularly, some internal organs contain poison-producing glands, which are in high demand as poison against monsters.

Additionally, parts like fangs, eyeballs, and tongues could fetch a decent price... Unfortunately, those had been shattered by the Twilight Spear that Rei had unleashed.

"Ah, yeah. I'd like to keep the meat for Set to eat, but the bones and organs can be sold. Since the head was blown off, we don't have any materials from that area though."

"Blown off... I see. Understood. I'll purchase any materials that can be bought."

"It's a bit tricky to extract the bones here... Is it okay to just hand them over as-is?"

"No, I'm sorry, but could you come to the warehouse instead? It would be easier if you could hand them over there."

Nodding at the receptionist's words, Rei moved on.

The sight of the two of them naturally drew the attention of everyone in the guild.

Jealousy, admiration, discontent, affection, curiosity... Under a barrage of varied gazes, Rei and the receptionist left the guild.

...It was typical of the guild that among those casting jealous glances, some harbored feelings not about Rei's skill, but about him being seen with the receptionist.

After all, receptionists are generally hired for their attractive features, designed to be well-liked by adventurers.

"I'm jealous. I'd love to be in a position like that someday."

One muttered discontentedly, prompting a nearby adventurer to open his mouth with a wry smile.

"Then you should go defeat a Sand Serpent solo like that guy. Easy, right?"

"Ha! Like that's something a normal human could do! I'm a Rank D adventurer, you know? There's no way I could beat a Sand Serpent, which is ranked near the top of C-rank monsters, even fighting in a party!"

"If you lack the skill, just improve it. Slacking off and then envying someone like... Rei-san? That's just pathetic."

"Huh?"

The man accused of being pathetic for his jealousy glared at the man who had spoken to him so rudely.

But the man whose gaze had been met showed no sign of flinching, staring right back.

He hadn't been an adventurer for very long. Normally, he should be overwhelmingly stronger, yet for some reason, he couldn't bring himself to be aggressive toward the man in front of him.

He understood that if he tried anything, he'd undoubtedly be crushed.

This adventurer, too, had been just an ordinary one until a few days ago... though perhaps "ordinary" wasn't quite the right word. In any case, he was just one of the many adventurers you'd find anywhere.

He was full of enthusiasm and actively took on quests, but he was still just a rookie adventurer without any particularly noteworthy abilities... The main reason the man's demeanor changed so drastically was witnessing Rei's battle up close.

A rank C monster, the Sand Serpent. He knew he couldn't defeat it alone... hell, he doubted he could even defeat it with his party members.

When it appeared before him, he braced himself for death.

But... in the end, that resolve became utterly meaningless, rendered pointless by a single strike from Rei.

Rei had dispatched the Sand Serpent, likely one of the higher-ranked C-rank monsters, with surprising ease.

As a new adventurer, it was impossible not to be inspired by that sight.

And what cemented the man's resolve were the sand pirates who attacked using a sand ship.

Rei's familiar, the Griffin, let out a single cry. That alone rendered the sand pirates unable to move.

Wanting to be like Rei, the man pondered how he could achieve that. He resolved to push forward aggressively, no matter what.

Ideally, he'd have wanted a weapon like Rei's Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear that had killed the Sand Serpent in one strike, but the man, who had only recently bought a longsword, couldn't afford such luxuries.

He'd figure out that part later. For now, his priority was accepting extermination requests and getting stronger.

"Tch."

Seeing the man like that, the adventurer who had been jealous of Rei walked away.

Watching him go, the man turned his gaze to the quest board, searching for the highest difficulty quest he could possibly take on.

(One day, I'll become an adventurer like Rei. I'll absolutely make it happen.)

This adventurer, who admired the younger boy, would go on to experience many things... eventually rising to become a Rank B adventurer, the highest rank within Goush.

"Alright, I've confirmed receipt of the materials. Here's your payment."

Receiving a bag of mixed gold and silver coins from the receptionist, Rei immediately stowed it away in his Misty Ring without checking the contents.

"Rei-san!? Um, that... Are you sure you don't want to check the contents?"

"Ah, I trust the guild. Besides, if they tried to pull any tricks, we'd take appropriate measures."

The receptionist gasped briefly under the gaze directed at her from beneath the hood.

It wasn't just that the gaze directed at her was sharp; more than that, a chill ran down her spine for reasons she couldn't fathom.

Facing Rei's intense stare, the receptionist hurriedly nodded in response.

"Of course. There's no way I'd do such a thing. I'll handle it properly."

"That'll put my mind at ease when selling the materials."

"About that matter... There were no magic stones among the materials... I heard the proof of slaying parts vanished along with the head, but what happened to the magic stones?"

Rei understood the receptionist's intent to change the subject, but he decided it was necessary to address the matter and replied.

"Collecting magic stones is my hobby. One for preservation, one for display. So, I basically won't sell magic stones until I've defeated at least three monsters."

"...Huh. Collecting magic stones, huh? Um, well... that's... quite a refined hobby you have there. Hobbies can be hard for others to understand, after all."

She was trying to gloss over it, but there was no mistaking that the receptionist thought Rei's hobby was unusual... that he was a bit of an oddball.
