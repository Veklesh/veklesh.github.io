After completing a simple forest survey, Rei returned to the guild, completed the entry procedures, and headed to the guild with Marina and the others who had been waiting outside.

"So, what was the forest like?"

"Well... If I had to sum it up in one word, it'd be 'abnormal'. That word fits best."

Marina, who had asked about the forest, raised her well-shaped eyebrows in surprise, clearly not expecting such a word from Rei's mouth.

Vihera reacted similarly, though she directed a pleased gaze at Rei, filled with anticipation for battle.

"Abnormal? How so?"

"Well, Marina, being a Dark Elf, you'd probably feel the same way if you went into that forest. After all, I couldn't find a single animal in there. And it wasn't like they were hiding; there really wasn't a single one."

"...That's..."

For Marina, a Dark Elf and above all a priestess of the World Tree, if what Rei said was true, it was a place that could hardly be called a forest at all.

There was no way she could possibly recognize it as a forest.

"No monsters? It's a forest, so what about trents and such?"

Unlike Marina, Vihera sounded disappointed, implying that without monsters, there would be no battles to look forward to.

But Rei shook his head at Vihera's words.

"Slesha's companion was attacked by something, so there's definitely something out there. ...Well, whether it's the kind of enemy that would satisfy Vihera, I don't know."

Even as they spoke, Rei and the others kept moving forward.

Several tried to speak to Set, but seeing how serious Rei and the others looked, they understood they were discussing something important.

They stopped calling out and simply watched over Set silently.

When the group arrived at the guild, Set walked away without Rei needing to say anything, and Rei and the others entered the guild.

Perhaps because it was already close to noon, there were hardly any adventurers inside the guild.

Instead, the tavern had a fair number of people eating lunch.

(Ah, I'm hungry. What should I eat after the report? ...Ah, udon? Or maybe a meat bun or pizza...)

His attention drawn by the appetizing smells wafting from the tavern, Rei headed for the counter.

Normally, he would speak to receptionists like Lenora or Kenny, but neither was present today.

Instead, there was a receptionist who had come to Marina's mansion to deliver a request, so he approached her.

The receptionist must have noticed Rei immediately. She gave him a hopeful look.

And it was Rei who spoke first.

"I've completed the request."

"I see. Please come this way. The Guildmaster wishes to speak with you directly."

At the receptionist's words, Rei nodded without saying anything further and stepped inside the counter.

He headed toward the back, climbed the stairs... Having passed through this place countless times before, he felt no particular hesitation.

Though, back then, the person he was with was Marina, who was now walking behind Rei.

"Excuse me, Guildmaster. Rei and the others have returned."

"I see. Please bring them in."

Walker's voice echoed from beyond the door, and the receptionist opened it.

(Come to think of it, this is my first time entering this office since Walker became Guildmaster.)

Thinking this as he entered the office, Rei felt a slight disappointment.

Because it hadn't changed much from when Marina used it before.

"Please have a seat. Regarding the report... Ah, could you fetch Runo and Slesha? It would be best for them to hear your report as well."

"Understood. Right away."

Bowing deeply, the receptionist exited the room.

After seeing her off, Walker, who had been organizing documents, smiled and spoke.

"Until the two of them arrive, why don't you take a seat on that sofa and rest? It was sudden, so you must be quite tired."

Rei had only ridden Set back and forth between the forest and Gilm, and Marina and the others had merely waited at the main gate.

Moreover, until the receptionist arrived earlier, they had been relaxing in Marina's mansion garden, so they couldn't possibly be tired.

Still, with nothing else to do, Rei and the others sat down on the sofa.

And the first to speak was Marina.

"Hey, Walker. Since this was urgent, I didn't ask about payment, but..."

"Ah, you're right. Sorry, I completely forgot about that."

Marina smiled slightly at Walker's words as he looked up from the documents.

If Marina hadn't brought it up... and if Rei hadn't mentioned it either, he probably would have put off discussing the reward.

Given that he managed the guild's branch office near the dungeon, Walker possessed a surprisingly strict sense about payment, despite his otherwise affable demeanor.

Still, given his position as Guildmaster, he wouldn't have refused payment outright.

"Would you like one of these, Rei?"

As he said this, Walker placed a wooden barrel on the desk.

A small one, about ten centimeters tall.

"Alcohol? Sorry, but I don't really drink..."

It wasn't that Rei couldn't drink it, but Rei, who simply couldn't bring himself to think alcohol tasted good, was about to refuse.

After all, a barrel only about ten centimeters tall meant the amount of liquor inside was easy to imagine.

It was probably just enough to drink in one gulp.

That's what he thought... but Walker shook his head at Rei's words.

"No, don't worry. This isn't alcohol."

Having finished organizing the documents, Walker carried the barrel over to the sofa.

Rei opened the barrel placed on the table, still wondering why it wasn't sake... and froze.

"H-hey. Could this possibly be..."

Inside the barrel was something Rei remembered seeing several times before.

However, compared to what Rei had seen before, it was much finer.

The ones he'd seen before were roughly the size of an embrace... there had been red ore too, but what was here now were several pieces about the size of a fingertip, and the rest was ground down to a fine powder like sand.

"Fire ore, huh?"

Walker nodded in response to Rei's tentative confirmation.

"Yes. I heard you were collecting fire ore before, Rei. I've been interested in fire ore myself for some time now. ...However, when fire ore is large, it becomes extremely expensive. But..."

Walker's gaze shifted toward the barrel Rei was holding.

"As you can see in that barrel, it's almost sand-like... no, powder-like would be more accurate? In that state, it becomes considerably cheaper. ...Though, even when cheaper, it's still fire ore, so naturally it commands a certain price."

At those words, Rei looked back at the barrel.

Rei wanted the fire ore to mix into his fire whirlwind, his secret weapon, to increase its power.

For that purpose, small ore pieces should be perfectly adequate, not requiring large ones.

"How about that barrel as payment this time?"

"...You're being quite generous."

Marina voiced her surprise at Walker's words.

In truth, even in its current state inside a barrel, the fire ore would command a considerable price if purchased.

At the very least, as payment for Rei's reconnaissance mission—which could barely even be called reconnaissance—it was nothing short of extraordinary.

All Rei had done was confirm whether the forest actually existed, gauge its size, and descend to the ground to observe its condition.

"Yes. It seems we'll need Rei to handle various other matters concerning this case as well... Ah, please come in."

A knock sounded on the door midway through Walker's speech.

Entering the office with permission were Rei, an adventurer he knew, and a female adventurer he hadn't seen before... Slesha.

"Thank you for coming. Please, have a seat."

At those words, Runo and Slesha sat down on the sofa—side by side, since there wasn't enough room for both to sit apart—and the receptionist, judging her duties done, bowed and left.

After confirming they were seated, Walker spoke again.

"Now then, perhaps I should introduce everyone. I am Walker, Guildmaster of Gilm. This is Slesha, who informed us of this matter. And this is Runo, who rescued Slesha."

Having said that much, Walker's gaze then turned to Rei and the others.

"They are... well, given the numbers, perhaps I should say 'they' are the rank B party, Crimson Wings."

Hearing the words "Rank B party," Slesha's eyes widened dramatically.

It was her first time actually seeing a high-rank party in person. Then there was Rei, hooded; Marina, dressed in a party gown that seemed out of place; Vihera, who looked like nothing more than a prostitute or dancer; and the child, Byune.

Seeing these four, she couldn't believe they were high-rank adventurers.

If Slesha's skill had been a bit higher, she might have sensed Rei and the others' strength, but unfortunately, even though she was considered a full-fledged adventurer, her abilities were still only at that level.

As for Runo, he already knew Rei and the others' abilities all too well, so naturally, he showed no surprise.

"Now, with the introductions out of the way, let's get down to business. First, regarding the forest mentioned in Slesha's report, I had Rei go scout it out."

"Huh?"

The dumbfounded sound that escaped Slesha's mouth was likely because he realized the contradiction between the distance to the forest and the time since he'd arrived here.

But Walker seemed to understand Slesha's reaction. He spoke up, trying to calm her.

"Rei has a griffin as his familiar. Have you heard the nickname Crimson?"

Hearing that alias, Slesha nodded in understanding... and stared intently at Rei, who was wearing the hood of the Dragon Robe.

Naturally, as an adventurer himself, Slesha knew the name Crimson, famous for his exploits in the war against the Bestir Empire.

But... he hadn't participated in the war himself, and none of his adventurer acquaintances had either.

Well, to be precise, there were those who did participate, but it would be more accurate to say they lost their lives in that war.

That was precisely why, even though she knew of Crimson... of Rei through rumors, she never imagined she'd suddenly meet him like this.

"This person... Crimson..."

"I'm Rei. Nice to meet you."

"Huh? Ah, yes! Nice to meet you!"

Her voice rose at the end, but considering a Rank D adventurer meeting an adventurer with a nickname, this reaction was only natural.

In fact, Rei found it refreshing that she didn't seem to look down on him because of his appearance.

"Well then, let's talk... Rei-san."

"Yeah."

Prompted by Walker, Rei stated his conclusion first.

"First, the forest was there. And it was in a location I hadn't seen it last time... probably last autumn. It was in a spot I hadn't seen it that autumn. And it was quite large. There's no way it could have grown that much in just a few months."

Recalling the scale of the forest he'd seen from above while riding Set, Rei continued his explanation.

"For a forest that size to appear out of nowhere... I'd say it would take decades... maybe even a hundred years."

"That long?"

Walker murmured thoughtfully at Rei's words.

Slesha also revealed a look of astonishment.

It was night when he was attacked in that forest, so he hadn't been able to fully grasp what kind of place it truly was... and above all, he'd been too overwhelmed, watching his comrades get killed one after another with his own eyes.

Given that, it was only natural to be shocked upon hearing it was truly that kind of forest.

"And..."

Muttering, Rei retrieved various items left behind at the campsite from the Misty Ring.

"You recognize this luggage, right?"

"Ah, yes. It's my... our belongings."

Those words must have reminded her of her companions. Slesha felt herself sinking into gloom, but she quickly pulled herself together.

She understood this wasn't a time for sorrow.

Still, a faint glimmer of tears lingered at the corners of her eyes.

"So... first, I need to confirm something. From what I heard, you camped outside the forest, right?"

"Ah, yes. ...But when I saw it while fleeing the forest, the trees were already..."

"I figured. I found this luggage a few minutes into the forest."

"...Huh?"

Slesha probably hadn't expected the forest to stretch that far either.

Forgetting the tears she'd been holding back just seconds ago, she let out a slightly dazed voice.
