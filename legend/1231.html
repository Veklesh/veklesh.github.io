"Ugh... ah..."

Rhodos awoke, letting out a sound barely worthy of being called a voice.

But his body wouldn't move as he wished, and he couldn't comprehend what was happening to him.

All he could see was his usually stoic mother with tears welling in her eyes, and his father—a man best described as bold and unrestrained—wearing an expression overcome with emotion.

"Wh-what—"

Rhodos wanted to ask Min and Elk what was going on, but his mouth wouldn't move as he wished.

Likewise, his body was completely unresponsive to his will.

It was a consequence of being unconscious for so long, but even so, Elk's magic item had minimized the deterioration of his body.

But... even so, it was only natural that his body wouldn't respond immediately upon waking.

What was even more unfortunate for Rodos was that he had gained almost none of the magic power and blood benefits that the Silver Lion's heart had held.

The bulk of that magic and blood had been poured into Vihera, and it was truly with the remnants used on Vihera that Rodos awoke.

But that wasn't because Grimm favored Vihera.

Even though both were unconscious, the amount of magic required to restore consciousness differed fundamentally between Vihera, whose body harbored the Ambris born from magical abnormalities, and Rhodes, who had lost consciousness while in a semi-brainwashed state.

Rather, it was precisely because it was Grimm that he could use the remaining Silver Lion's Heart after restoring Vihera's consciousness to awaken Rhodes's consciousness as well.

Had someone less skilled in magic than Grimm performed this ritual, they might have restored Vihera's consciousness, but reviving Rodos's would have been impossible.

"Rhodos... really, you overslept."

Min said, wiping the tears from the corners of his eyes, while Elk, beside him, smiled happily and spoke up.

"You're telling me. I never oversleep like you do."

"...Ah... Uh...?"

Though Rodos's mouth wouldn't move and he couldn't speak properly, Min and Elk could tell he had regained consciousness. They exchanged words, each looking pleased.

"We need to rebuild Rodos' weakened body."

"Yeah. Getting brainwashed? That's just pathetic for my son. This time, we need to thoroughly beat it out of him, right down to his very core, so it never happens again."

While they were happy that Rodos had regained consciousness, the conversation between the couple was far from something Rodos could hear and feel reassured by.

(Brainwashing?)

Rhodos tilted his head, his mind still not fully functioning.

He couldn't understand why he had lost consciousness. The circumstances surrounding it were completely beyond his comprehension.

The last thing he remembered was heading toward the Bestir Empire. After that, his memories were almost non-existent.

(What...?)

Naturally, he couldn't comprehend that roughly a year had passed since he lost consciousness.

His body wouldn't move as he wished; all he could do was stare blankly around him.

Then, with great effort, I moved my eyes... and the next instant, I saw Grimm's figure, and my body froze.

Grim's appearance was truly worthy of the name "undead monster."

As an adventurer, he was absolutely no one I could approach amicably.

Could it be that my parents were about to be attacked by an undead monster?

Misinterpreting the figure of Grimm behind his parents, Rodes struggled to speak, yet tried desperately to convey the situation.

"B...behind..."

"Huh? What is it?"

Elk tilted his head like Rhodes, but Min seemed to understand what Rhodes was trying to say.

Turning his gaze behind him, he nodded in understanding at the sight of Grimm and spoke.

"Don't worry. He's an undead monster called a Grimm, but he's a rational one. It was only with his help that we were able to restore Rhodes' consciousness."

"...Huh?"

Rhodos felt a bit uneasy about being saved by an undead monster, but talking it out like this gradually helped him sort things out in his head.

This quick judgment probably suited him well as an adventurer.

"You're awake?"

Amidst this, a sudden voice made Rodos turn his gaze.

There stood Rei, a familiar face even to Rodos.

The moment he saw Rei, Rodos felt like he needed to remember something, but the more he tried to recall it, the more it slipped away.

"Re...?"

"Hm? Oh."

Realizing his name had been called, Rei gave Rhodes a small nod in return.

Considering how they'd parted ways, Rodos had assumed Ray must hate him... Even if not, he'd expected some kind of reaction. Yet, standing face to face like this, Rodos showed no particular response.

While that was a relief, seeing Rodos weakened like this undoubtedly stirred a vague sense of regret within Rei's chest.

There were various issues between Rei and Rodos, and Rei certainly hadn't harbored affection for him. Still, he didn't dislike or hate Rodos either.

"It's been a long time since I last saw your face."

"Hii...ri...?"

"Huh? Well, I don't understand what you're saying, but you'll get back to normal eventually, right?"

"Long time no see? That's what he seems to be saying."

Rei looked satisfied at Min's translation.

"You wouldn't know since you were unconscious. It's been nearly a year since you lost consciousness."

"Wh...?"

Rei's words must have been quite a shock to Rodos as well.

He said only that and fell silent.

'Hmm... Rei, can I assume that's the end of your request for now?'

As if timing his words to Rodos' silence, Grimm addressed Rei.

"Ah, yeah. Um... Grimm. You really helped me out this time. Thanks."

Rei bowed deeply.

Several people watched Rei's actions with looks of surprise.

For most, seeing Rei bow his head in gratitude was an unprecedented sight, an almost unbelievable spectacle.

"Heh heh heh. Don't worry about it. If it's a request from Rei, something like this is easy for me. Besides, Rei is a special person to me too. If it's a request from you, something like this isn't even a bother."

"Well, if that's how you feel, I wish you'd helped me with something other than the Silver Lion's heart from the start."

"What do you expect? Relying solely on me? Do you truly understand what kind of being you are? Even if it's an S-rank monster, if you can't defeat a Silver Lion alone... well, you're still quite immature."

Of course, Rei knew exactly who Grimm was comparing him to.

Zepair, the genius among geniuses, whom Grimm had admired before becoming an undead monster.

Rei was the one who inherited Zepairu's... more accurately, the beast magic created by the Zepairu lineage. As such, Grimm's honest desire was for Rei to possess the strength to defeat a Silver Lion alone.

"...Against that Silver Lion?"

The muttered voice came from Elk.

But to those unaware of the relationship between Rei and Grimm, and between Rei and Zepail, demanding that one defeat a Silver Lion alone was nothing short of reckless.

That was precisely why it was only natural for words that could be described as foolish to come out of Elk's mouth.

Grim glanced at Elk and the others before speaking.

"Hmm. It may have been a special monster for you lot, but the Silver Lion isn't particularly strong."

"...Well, that might be true for a powerful monster like you. But honestly, after facing the Silver Lion, I know it's no match for a single person to handle alone, right?"

There was no trace of jest in Elk's words.

His voice conveyed genuine conviction.

"That's why I said it, didn't I? 'For you all.' Because Rei must become much, much stronger."

At those words, everyone around turned to look at Rei with pity.

It was only natural. Having experienced battle against the Silver Lion, hearing this now made them understand just how incredibly difficult it would be.

Even those who hadn't participated in the battle, like Johanna, felt the same.

No, precisely because they hadn't fought, their imagination of the Silver Lion monster ran wild, making their looks of pity toward Rei even stronger than those of Elk and the others.

"Rei... I mean, my condolences."

Hearing Johanna's words, Rei judged that continuing this conversation any further would be problematic in various ways—particularly regarding the topic of Zepair—and shifted his gaze to the Silver Lion's corpse.

Grim had already bled it dry, and that blood was now in the barrel that Rei carried.

But the Silver Lion's materials weren't limited to that.

The fur that had blocked most of Rei's attacks, the meat, the organs... Various parts were extremely rare materials. Selling them would undoubtedly fetch an enormous sum, and crafting equipment from them would undoubtedly be incredibly powerful.

"Well then, shall we discuss how to divide up this silver lion?"

"Yeah, we'll pass."

Elk was the first to respond to Rei's words.

Beside him, Min quietly nodded, seemingly in agreement with her husband's words.

"Are you sure?"

"Yeah. Just getting Rodos's consciousness back was more than enough for us. Hell, I even feel like we should be the ones paying you. ...What do you say? If you want, we could pay you something."

Rei shook his head at those words.

Elk's power had played an extremely significant role in this battle against the Silver Lion.

Furthermore, Min's magic, while unassuming, had contributed significantly to the effort by keeping the Silver Lion in check.

Above all, the primary reason Rei and the others decided to defeat the Silver Lion was to restore Vihera's consciousness. In that sense, Elk and Min were no different.

Given that, even if Elk and the others wanted the Silver Lion's materials, Rei had no intention of complaining. ...In fact, he would have accepted it as a matter of course.

"But... is it okay? I mean, this might sound weird, but we've been spending quite a bit of money lately, right?"

"Ah, yeah, that's true."

Elk nodded, scratching his head at Rei's words.

In truth, he'd bought various magic items for Rodos' treatment and even summoned mages who could cast healing spells.

The money spent on those things, even considering Elk was a Rank A adventurer, was far from something he could afford with ease.

When Ambris spawned countless hordes of monsters, Elk had accepted the extermination request to work because the guild lacked sufficient combat power, combined with the fact that Elk desperately needed money.

While Rei didn't know all the details of Elk's situation, he understood that Elk was struggling financially.

...Though, even if he was strapped for cash, the fact he could immediately arrange a carriage like the one he rode here in and the horses to pull it made Rei wonder, from his perspective, if he was truly that hard up?

But when it came to money sense, Rei wasn't exactly a paragon of virtue either.

It wasn't uncommon for him to impulsively buy magic items he liked on the spot. And above all, since Rei arrived in Gilm, numerous shops and stalls had seen their sales rise thanks to him.

"It's fine. Rodos has regained consciousness, and we are a Rank A party after all. Making money isn't that difficult. If push comes to shove, we could just dump Elk in some forest somewhere and be done with it."

"Hey, Min."

Elk wore a pitiful expression at Min's words, which casually suggested tossing her husband into the woods.

His demeanor was utterly unlike that of a renowned Rank A adventurer.

But several people, including Rei, didn't miss the faint curve that touched the corners of Min's mouth as she watched her husband.

This was the ordinary way of this couple... and indeed, as a family, including Rodos.

(I knew it, but he's completely under her thumb.)

Rei murmured inwardly at Elk and Min's interaction.

"Anyway, we don't need the materials. That's perfectly fine with us."

At Min's words, Rei and the others began discussing anew how to divide the Silver Lion's materials.
