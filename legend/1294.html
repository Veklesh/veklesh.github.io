"...Hey, you got my hopes up, and this is what you give me?"

Vihera swung her fist at the creature that had burst out of the bushes, crushing its skull cleanly and killing it. She muttered in disbelief.

Because the creature whose crushed head was now staining the fresh snow with blood, brains, bone, flesh, and bodily fluids was a goblin.

Having expected a powerful monster to leap out of the winter forest's thickets, Vihera's combat drive simply didn't kick in when it turned out to be nothing more than a goblin, scarcely worth fighting.

The goblins who had also burst out of the bushes... and by chance avoided Vihera's attack, surrounded Rei and the others, but were already backing away.

Goblins inherently possessed the courage—or rather, the reckless bravery—to attack even Set without fleeing.

But at the same time, they possessed the cowardice to flee the moment they realized their opponent was too strong.

The reason these goblins hadn't fled yet was likely because they believed Vihera's kill was a lucky strike... a mere coincidence.

To a trained eye... no, to anyone with a certain level of skill, it would be immediately obvious that he defeated them through sheer power. But these were goblins, creatures lacking any real judgment.

That's precisely why they still stand there, shrieking "Gyaa! Gyoh!" while wielding clubs fashioned from broken branches to keep Vihera at bay.

(If they pointed the broken end of the branch at him, wouldn't it still be a weapon, sharp as it is?)

As this thought flashed through his mind, Rei switched the Twilight Spear to his left hand and slipped his right hand inside his Dragon Robe.

Then, touching the Eye of Nebra at his waist, he channeled magic into it, creating a magic-infused arrowhead.

He confirmed the feel of the arrowhead in his hand...

"Hah!"

With the hand he'd withdrawn from the Dragon Robe, he swiftly flicked the arrowhead.

The released arrow sliced through the air in a straight line toward the goblin, striking its face.

The arrowhead sliced open its face, gouged out its eyes, and pierced its throat...

"Gyaaahhhhhhh!"

"Gyaaah!"

With such screams, it writhed on the ground.

The goblins likely didn't even understand what kind of attack had struck them.

Goblins, never blessed with high physical ability, couldn't even track the arrow's flight with their eyes.

And before they could even realize what had attacked them... they simply lost their lives.

"For someone who made such a big show of coming out, that was a bit anticlimactic, wasn't it?"

Watching the goblins lying on the ground, Vihera muttered with a look of exasperation... and boredom.

Vihera enjoyed combat, but she clearly didn't relish fighting opponents who were obviously weak.

For someone like Vihera, goblins—among the lowest of monsters—were clearly unworthy opponents.

"Well, even so. Goblins are the most numerous monsters, so you're bound to fight them often."

"...I know that, but it's still no fun, that's the truth."

Vihera grumbled in protest at Rei's conciliatory words.

Marina offered a calming smile to Vihera and spoke.

"But as a guild, we'd be happier if you killed as many goblins as possible, right? You understand how threatening their reproductive capacity is? Find one, and there are thirty... no, fifty more waiting."

Marina sighed softly.

She would soon relinquish her position as Guildmaster to Walker, but for now, she was still the Guildmaster.

And as Guildmaster, goblins were a terribly troublesome foe.

As seen in the recent battle, individual goblins were extremely weak. ...This time they faced strong opponents like Rei and his group, but even non-adventurers with decent skills could easily take down one or two goblins.

To compensate for that weakness, they possessed high reproductive rates, which only amplified their troublesome nature.

If they would at least assess their opponent's strength before attacking, things might be somewhat easier... but in reality, they have a nature that compels them to attack first, as seen when they assaulted Rei and the others, including Set.

The guild also bought magic stones and proof-of-kill parts cheaply, making goblins a surprisingly troublesome foe for the guild.

Especially in the case of Gilm, located in the frontier, where skilled adventurers abound. This has led many to avoid goblins altogether.

And with plenty of other monsters offering better rewards... it's only natural that few adventurers would choose to fight goblins.

While goblin extermination requests are always available as regular quests, most adventurers only take them on after completing other jobs, killing any goblins they happen to encounter along the way.

If the request involved slaying a higher-tier or rare goblin species, perhaps more adventurers would take it.

Those who actively seek out goblin extermination requests are mostly either oddballs like Rei, who just arrived at the guild and thinks adventuring means goblin hunting, or those who just became adventurers at the guild.

"Forget the magic stones for now, but shouldn't we pay a bit more for the proof-of-kill parts?"

It was an idea that had just popped into Rei's head, but the response he got was Marina's slightly exasperated gaze.

"You think any guild hasn't considered that?"

"...So it didn't work out?"

"Yes. The payment for proof of slaying parts is three copper coins. But even raising it to four or five copper coins wouldn't make much difference. If you absolutely insist, you could raise it to one silver coin... but you know what would happen then, right?"

"Hunt only goblins, huh?"

"Yes. If hunting stronger monsters only earns you less than goblins, it's only natural to target goblins exclusively, right? Then, of course, the payment for other monster slaying proof parts would need to be raised too, and once that happens, goblins would be neglected again..."

"Ah... I see."

Rei nodded in understanding, but Marina spoke up again.

"Also, I hate to say this, but the guild doesn't have an endless budget either."

"...Yeah, sorry for pushing it."

Sensing the conversation was turning a bit grim, Rei bowed his head obediently.

The guild had various sources of income.

Selling materials and magic stones purchased from adventurers, donations from major trading houses and nobles, revenue from the tavern attached to the guild... There were other sources too, but the main income undoubtedly came from the commission fees charged for accepting requests.

Increasing the purchase price for proof-of-kill parts meant finding that money from somewhere... and paying silver coins for goblin proof-of-kill parts was simply out of the question.

Raising the commission fees just to increase the value of goblin slaying proof parts... that was simply out of the question.

Considering that, Rei understood that as Guildmaster, the Guildmaster had to make all sorts of difficult decisions.

It would be different if it were like the previous situation in Ambris, where a horde of goblins had appeared and were running rampant.

"Yeah, yeah, let's just hurry up and move on. We might run into monsters unique to winter."

Vihera's words reminded Rei why they were here in the first place. He reached out to the goblin corpse and had the Misty Ring retrieve it.

"Rei, you're collecting that too?"

Vihera gave Rei a puzzled look as he retrieved the goblin corpse, which had almost no practical use.

Seeing Vihera's expression, Rei nodded without hesitation.

"Yeah. If we just leave the corpse here, it might turn into undead... Plus, it's better to have goblin meat."

"Come to think of it, you were looking for ways to cook goblin meat deliciously, weren't you?"

Recalling a previous conversation with Rei, Vihera asked. Rei nodded again.

"Yeah. If it works out, our food situation should improve significantly, right? ...Well, the ones who'll benefit the most are probably new adventurers or folks living in the slums."

"If you can pull that off, it'd be truly amazing... Is it going smoothly, though?"

Probably not.

Laying a small sigh before shaking his head at Vihera, who asked with that kind of atmosphere seeping through.

"Not at all. To be honest, the guy primarily running this research is swamped with other stuff right now and can't focus on it. So, yeah, the research hasn't really progressed."

"I figured. If goblin meat could be made tasty that easily, that would be an incredible invention. ...Well, never mind. Let's go."

Nodding at her words, Rei and the others began walking through the forest again.

They walked through the snow for about ten minutes. Despite encountering monsters one after another—a Snow Salamander and then a goblin—they didn't run into any other monsters this time.

Still, the tension wasn't particularly high, likely because several members of Rei's group possessed strong scouting abilities.

Set, whose senses were razor-sharp; Rei, whose senses were slightly less acute than Set's but still highly developed; Byune, a thief whose combat-loving nature had honed her battle instincts; and Marina, whose spirit magic granted her exceptional perception.

...Every single member of the party possesses a high level of perception. For an ordinary party, this would be enviable enough to make many jealous.

While not quite on par with mages, there aren't many thieves who excel at detecting enemies.

And yet, this party has several members whose perception surpasses even that of a thief.

Admittedly, it's true that ambushes like the goblin's earlier make them slightly harder to spot.

"Hmm... I'd rather fight stronger enemies. Cyclopes would have been nice."

Recalling a previous battle, Vihera's eyes glistened with lust as she licked her lips with her red tongue.

The sight would have been terribly provocative to any man, but fortunately or unfortunately, Rei hadn't seen it.

The sight of snow piled on the surrounding trees was anything but interesting to Rei.

Still, he couldn't completely ignore the possibility that other monsters might be lurking...

"Ah, Rei. Hey, look over here!"

Vihera, having completely erased the lewd atmosphere from mere seconds ago, called out to Rei while looking at a tree a short distance away.

For a moment, Rei wondered if it was a monster, but the voice calling his name carried absolutely no tension whatsoever.

After all, Vihera had a penchant for enjoying combat. If a worthy opponent appeared, it wouldn't be strange for her to feel a mix of excitement.

In fact, it made perfect sense.

Following Vihera's gaze with that thought, Rei spotted a squirrel on the snow, its fur patterned in green and red—a creature that clearly made a strong statement about itself.

"...An animal? No, a monster?"

"Juu!"

As if sensing danger from Rei's mutter, the squirrel clamped the piece of bark it held in its mouth and leapt from the branch, vanishing from sight.

"That's an animal. It might have looked like a monster at first glance, but—"

"That's one flashy squirrel."

Marina, who had been listening behind him, let out a small chuckle at Rei's stunned mutter.

"Mm."

Byune, leading the way, gave a short, cautionary sound at their exchange.

For Byune, currently exerting her full abilities as a thief, the commotion behind her was likely not very amusing.

Noticing this, Vihera lowered her voice, and Rei likewise fell silent.

They continued walking, their gaze fixed on the direction the flashy squirrel had just vanished.

(That was one flashy squirrel. ...I never would've guessed it wasn't a monster. Well, it didn't seem hostile towards us, so it probably wasn't a problem.)

Perhaps because it was so flashy, the image of that squirrel was strongly imprinted on Rei's mind.

Byune led Rei and the others deeper into the forest.

Occasionally, sounds would break the silence, but it was mostly animals that appeared.

And time passed...

"Rei, it's about time. We should head back."

At Marina's words, Rei looked up toward the sky.

There, the sun was already beginning to set.

It was winter, after all, and the sun set early this time of year.

As Marina said, if they pushed any further, it was certain night would fall before they could even think about defeating monsters.

If it were just Rei, Vihera, and Byune, they could have camped out using Rei's magic tent. But with Guildmaster Marina present, they couldn't push things that far.

Being here with Rei and the others now was already the result of pushing the schedule to its limits.

"...Alright, let's head back."

Having made that decision, Rei ultimately ended today's exploration there.
