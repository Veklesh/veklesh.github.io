Rei extracted information from one of Kathy's acquaintances and headed straight for Augusto's shop.

When he returned to the shop, he saw several figures lurking around it.

The figures showed a moment of alertness when they saw someone approaching the shop, but upon recognizing it was Rei, they exhaled in relief and spoke.

"Rei, are you all right?"

"...I didn't exactly go out to fight, so of course I'm fine."

A look of displeasure crossed Rei's face at the man's words, which seemed to imply that trouble inevitably followed his every move.

But facing such a Rei, the man opened his mouth with a wry smile.

"But that's what Zarust said—"

"Zalust..."

When he'd pursued the assailant who attacked Augusto, he'd been radiating murderous intent. But after Augusto, mortally wounded, recovered thanks to Rei's potion, he must have regained his senses considerably.

Otherwise, such flippant remarks wouldn't have come out of him.

"Well, fine. So are Zarust and Augusto inside the shop?"

"Yes. But I don't think it's a good idea to go in right now."

Rei tilted his head at the cautious tone in the words.

After all, it was Zarust who had originally asked him to gather information. To relay that information, he needed to explain it directly to Zarust and Augusto.

And this wasn't about building an alibi or anything like that—it was a report that Daldora had been attacked.

This information was also extremely important for Augusto, and the sooner the better.

So why? Realizing that question was on Rei's mind, the guard gave a wry smile and spoke.

"Actually, Ms. Kathy is here at the shop right now..."

Hearing only that much, Rei instantly grasped what was unfolding inside the shop.

He imagined the shop was filled with an unbearably tense atmosphere.

He didn't understand why Cathy was there when the attack had been kept secret.

For love? he wondered. Yet seeing how passionately Augusto and Cathy were in love, he couldn't dismiss the idea entirely.

"...What do you think I should do?"

"Well, asking me won't help."

"But you're the one who said it's better not to go inside the shop right now, right? So don't you think it's okay to at least discuss what to do?"

"...Um... Give up, maybe?"

"That's not an option. ...Ah, right. What about Set?"

Suddenly, he remembered his partner, who had split off to guard Augusto, and asked.

"Set? He's behind the shop. There aren't any stables he can get into, so I hear he's lying down on the ground."

"I see. ...Well, I'll go check on him for now."

In the end, the option Rei chose was to give it time.

Approaching Augusto and Kathy right now was dangerous in more ways than one.

But he thought that if he waited even a little while, they might become somewhat more reasonable... Yet, for some reason, the expression on the guard's face was closer to pity.

...Whether he knew these people, Augusto and Cathy, well or not. That difference seemed to have divided their judgments.

(Even if they kill some time, those two's heat won't cool down. ...In fact, the longer they wait, the hotter they'll get until they become untouchable.)

Going later was actually one of the worst possible choices...

Though the truly worst thing would be not reporting it at all.

Watching Rei's departing figure, the man returned to guarding the shop.

The tension that had built up before Rei's arrival had already dissipated, yet the man gripped his longsword tightly, determined not to let his employer, Augusto, suffer any further harm.

When Rei reached the back of the shop, he found Set there, just as he'd heard about out front.

Set must have noticed Rei approaching. Lying down, he purred happily.

A short distance away from Set, for some reason, Zarust was also present.

"Zalust? Why are you here?"

Approaching Set, Ray stroked his body while calling out to Zarust, who stood a little way off.

At Rei's words, Zarust shrugged his shoulders slightly before speaking.

"Didn't you hear before you came in? Ms. Kathy is here right now."

"Ah... yeah, I get why Zarust came here now."

Essentially, Zarust had escaped the sweet space for the same reason as Rei.

But the fact that Zarust was here now was also a relief to Rei.

"I gathered some intel."

"I heard Daldora was attacked?"

"Yeah, that's for sure. And it was attacked far more severely than Augusto."

"Really? Could it have been a cover-up?"

Zalust, like Rei, had likely believed the attack on Daldora was merely an alibi to divert suspicion from himself after attacking Augusto.

That's precisely why, upon hearing that Daldora had genuinely been attacked, he asked Rei in surprise.

"I don't think it was a sham. According to someone who saw the attack, three men attacked first. Men who felt no pain whatsoever. Then, just as they thought they'd defeated them, arrows flew from nowhere. If it weren't for Narsus, an arrow would've pierced Daldora's head."

Hearing that, Zarust couldn't help but growl, unable to believe the attack on Daldora was a sham either.

"But then... who's pulling the strings behind this? Augusto and Daldora... no, Augusto doesn't have that much influence, but still, it's hard to imagine someone making an enemy of Daldora..."

It was simply inconceivable. Hearing Zarust say this, Rei could only nod in agreement.

Having only recently arrived in Gosh, Rei didn't yet grasp the full scope of its rivalries.

What he did know was that Augusto and Daldora were at odds over the handling of the Tira Tree, and that Daldora had allied with the lord, Ryubrand... Thinking it through, one possibility occurred to him.

No, rather than a possibility, if Daldora wasn't the culprit behind this incident, then the only remaining influential figure left was Ryubrand.

"Could it be... Lord Ryubrand tried to assassinate Daldora?"

"No way. That's impossible."

Zalust immediately dismissed Rei's words.

"To be honest, the lord here manages things well precisely because Daldora is here. Not that the lord is malicious, but... how should I put it... a typical noble? Is that what you think a noble is like? Hmm, I'm not sure."

Zalust hedged his words, but Rei understood what he was getting at.

After actually meeting him once, Ray had gotten the impression that Zarust acted this way because he believed nobles should behave like this.

(For example...)

Rei shifted his gaze to Set, whom he was stroking.

Though he had voiced a desire to claim Set, who was unusual for an aristocrat, as his own, it seemed he believed that was simply what an aristocrat would say.

In fact, once Rei refused him once, he never pressed the matter further.

Compared to those whose eyes were clouded by desire, he was clearly different.

(Well, considering that, he didn't seem to take issue with my choice of words or attitude. ...Normally, a noble who acts like a noble would probably be a corrupt noble. Thinking about it that way, is he actually decent? ...That's dangerous.)

Rei muttered to himself.

If he remained the same Ryubrand, it wouldn't be a problem at all.

But since he was trying to mold himself to fit what might be called his own view of nobility, what if that view ended up being that of an arrogant noble?

It wasn't hard to imagine.

Gosh, who currently lives an ordinary life, would likely become a target for the lord's exploitation and plunder.

And Ryubrand, who would do this, would feel absolutely no guilt whatsoever.

Given that Gosh isn't exactly wealthy, if that were to happen, he'd likely be ruined before long.

"Rei? What's wrong?"

"No, uh... nothing."

He couldn't possibly admit he'd been imagining a future where Gosh was destroyed, so he brushed it off.

Zalust seemed to notice something off about Rei, but Rei, not wanting to be pressed on what he was thinking, forced the conversation forward.

"So, what makes you so sure Ryubrand isn't involved in this attack?"

"...Don't you dare call the lord by his first name."

"Well, what can I say? I don't know if he heard it from Augusto or not, but we did share a meal together."

At Rei's words, Zarust sighed in exasperation before speaking.

"The lord and Daldora are working together. One reason for that alliance is that Daldora is helping develop Gosh... specifically, lending his expertise for that purpose. Would you kill someone who's helping develop Gosh?"

"...Could it be that Daldora voiced some opinion unacceptable to Ryubrand, leading to a clash...?"

"That's about the only possibility I can think of, but honestly, I can't imagine Daldora doing anything to antagonize the lord in the first place."

Scratching his head roughly, Zarust muttered unhappily, wondering what on earth had led to the current situation.

"So, it must be a third party after all?"

"...That's about the only possibility, huh?"

Zalust looked even more annoyed.

Watching Zarust's expression, Rei stroked Set, who was purring contentedly, and turned his gaze toward the shop.

Just then, Augusto was coming out of the shop.

Fortunately, only Augusto emerged from the shop; there was no sign of Kathy, who had seemingly created that heated atmosphere just moments before.

"Oh, Rei. You're back. You could have come to report to me instead."

"Yeah, I figured I'd rest up here a bit before heading over."

Rei couldn't honestly say he didn't want to get involved in their lovey-dovey space, so he replied that way. However, Augusto tilted his head at Rei's attitude... and at Zarust's nod beside Rei, as if to say he understood Rei's feelings.

"Is that so? Well, that's fine then... Um, so you said you gathered information? What happened with that?"

Augusto asked, tilting his head as he did so. Rei answered bluntly.

"Daldora was attacked too. And it wasn't some cover-up to hide what happened to you, Augusto. They were genuinely trying to kill her."

"...Really?"

Like Zarust, Augusto asked without hiding his disbelief. Rei nodded in response.

"I heard the story from that woman, Kathy's friend. Compared to the attack on Augusto, hers involved a much larger group. If anything, I'd say yours was probably the diversion—the real target was hers."

"From what I've heard, that does seem to be the case... But attacking both me and Daldora almost simultaneously..."

Seeing her look of incomprehension, Zarust nodded.

"Probably a third faction. Any ideas, Augusto?"

"No, unfortunately..."

From those words, it seemed Augusto had half-concluded that the attack on him was Daldora's doing.

But since it was an open secret that he'd hired the Sand Pirates to attack Augusto's caravan, it wasn't unreasonable for him to think that.

"If even Augusto has no idea, then we have no leads."

"...My apologies."

Augusto bowed his head at Rei's words, but seeing this, Rei hurriedly spoke up.

"No, I'm not blaming you, so don't worry about it."

"But... you're not actually employed by me, Rei. You're just staying at my house. Yet I've caused you this much trouble..."

"Ah, that's fine. If anything happened to you, Kathy would be sad. And then all sorts of problems would arise, right? Cooking, cooking, cooking..."

"Just the cooking?"

Zalust muttered in exasperation, but Augusto smiled at their exchange.

"Well, yes. Cathy's cooking is worth that much."

Hearing Augusto's words, Zarust, who knew the taste of Cathy's cooking, found himself unable to say anything.

Watching this exchange, Rei suddenly spoke up.

"Since I'm already caught up in this, I want to know—why is Augusto so opposed to Daldora? Using Tira Wood would let us protect the Gosh more cheaply and safely, right?"

That's what he asked.
