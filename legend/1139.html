Watching the iron ball hurtling straight toward him, a small smile touched Rei's lips.

A massive iron mass, the iron ball nearly a meter in diameter, would possess tremendous destructive power.

The sheer strength required to swing it around with apparent ease belonged to someone who could only be described as a small giant, standing at over two and a half meters tall—a contradictory description that somehow fit.

But... even so.

Rei raised his Death Scythe and swung it down toward the iron ball charging at him.

(After all, it's just a plain iron ball, not some magic item. No need to use the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.)

Yes, the iron ball bearing down on him, threatening to crush him, was nothing more than a weapon, not a magic item or anything else.

If it were a magic item, Rei would have felt that familiar sense of incongruity he always did when encountering magic items.

...Though, to be fair, that sense of unease wasn't something he felt with every single magic item, either.

In any case, the Death Scythe Rei swung down should have been able to cleave the iron ball aimed at him in two...

That much was certain.

But... that was only if the blade of the Death Scythe struck the iron ball cleanly, and if Resonance hadn't deliberately altered its trajectory by violently jerking the chain he held.

Resonance possessed the ability to freely manipulate an iron ball likely over a meter in diameter. Her action caused the swung Death Scythe to slice through the air.

Of course, Resonance couldn't simply attack Rei with the iron ball either, so she pulled the chain back toward herself, retrieving the iron ball.

"Well, well. I never expected you to charge straight at this iron ball like that."

Resonance declared, pulling the chain back sharply once more and swinging the iron ball through the air.

Despite swinging a massive iron ball, there was no sign of strain in Resonance's tone.

Hearing those words spoken so naturally, Rei opened his mouth, slightly impressed.

"Seemed like you dodged it pretty easily, though?"

As he spoke, Rei's gaze swept briefly around the area.

In the direction of his gaze, Zarust and Narsus each wielded long swords, fighting other assassins.

Set, too, was rampaging freely, seemingly to assist them, blowing assassins away one after another without using any skills.

The reason they weren't using skills was likely because Narsus was present, though Zarust was another matter.

Set understood that Narサs's employer, Daldora, was only a temporary ally, and that Rei didn't want to reveal too much of their hand. That was precisely why he acted this way.

Though he only glanced away from the Resonance before him for an instant—judging it too dangerous to look away for long—even Rei could clearly see from his eyes that fighting them was proving difficult.

"...I see. So these guys don't feel pain either. Troublesome."

"That's our defining trait, you see. ...Though the effects are a bit strong."

"The effects are strong?"

"Oops, seems I've said too much. As I mentioned earlier, if you want the details, you'll have to defeat me. Then I'll tell you everything you want to know. ...Here we go!"

As he shouted, Resonance manipulated the chains once more, hurling the iron ball toward Rei.

Seeing the iron ball approaching, shattering the air, Rei charged toward it just as before... no, with a savage grin beneath his hood, he charged even more aggressively than before.

He raised his Death Scythe toward the iron ball closing in, threatening to crush him.

Up to this point, it was an exact replay of the earlier scene.

...The only difference, perhaps, is that Rei now knows Resonance possesses the skill and strength to freely manipulate the iron ball with his chains.

As he swung the Death Scythe down toward the iron ball, Resonance, seeing this, naturally manipulated the chains to move the iron ball through the air.

"Flying Slash!"

The only difference compared to the previous action.

was that Rei's strike wasn't a simple swing of the Death Scythe, but the use of its skill, Flying Slash.

Having reached Level 5, Flying Slash's power had increased dramatically. It sliced straight through the air, severing not the iron ball itself, but the chain controlling it.

Even if it had been a strike using the Death Scythe, it was unthinkable that Flying Slash could sever the iron ball, no matter how much its power had increased.

Rei's target wasn't the iron ball, but the chain.

His reasoning was that, in terms of preventing the opponent from using their weapon, severing the iron ball or severing the chain achieved the same result.

Of course, severing the iron ball would likely cause greater psychological disruption to the opponent.

But since the outcome would be the same, Rei judged there was no need to fixate on that.

The massive iron ball rolled across the desert, making a sound that could be called a thunderous roar.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, it rolled away in a direction completely unrelated to where Zarust, Narsus, and Set were fighting.

"Hmm, you aim for that one? ...But do you think there hasn't been someone who tried something similar before?"

Despite his weapon, the iron ball, being rendered useless, Resonance showed no sign of panic whatsoever.

On the contrary, he seemed to imply this was well within his expectations.

"You seem remarkably calm for someone who lost their weapon."

"Hmm, lost my weapon? Where exactly do you see that?"

"...It looks that way no matter how you look at it."

Rei replied, looking at Resonance holding the chain where the iron ball should have been.

Resonance's greatest weapon, the iron ball, had been cut from its chain midway and rolled away into the sand, rendering it utterly unusable as a weapon.

"Can't you see that?" Rei asked, but Resonance seemed to understand perfectly well, showing no change in expression.

"Well, here's your weapon, right here?"

Resonance declared, holding the chain that had lost its iron ball tip.

Resonance slammed the chain sharply into the ground.

It was a blow from Resonance, possessing the brute strength to swing an iron ball over a meter in diameter.

The sand beneath Resonance's feet was instantly gouged out and sent flying.

"...It's definitely a weapon."

Realizing Resonance's words weren't bravado, Rei readied Death Scythe once more.

This strike lacked the sheer power of the iron ball, but in terms of speed, it was incomparably faster.

And for an ordinary human, being struck by the chain swung with Resonance's sheer strength would be enough to reduce their body to bloody pulp.

Knowing Resonance's confidence wasn't bluffing or anything, Rei lowered his hood with his left hand while keeping the Death Scythe poised in his right.

Seeing Rei's face beneath the lowered hood, Resonance's eyes widened for just an instant.

Even though he knew of Rei's existence, he probably hadn't seen his face before since he usually wore a hood.

He clearly hadn't expected the face beneath to be so distinctly feminine.

In fact, seeing his slender build—slender even compared to other assassins, let alone himself—Resonance had briefly wondered if he was a woman, not a man.

Still, the way she immediately snapped back into focus suggested she was something special even among these assassins.

"Let's go!"

Resonance shouted as he closed the distance toward Rei.

Her movements were so light and agile, it was impossible to believe she was over two meters tall.

(No way is that his size!?)

Rei was momentarily startled by Resonance's movement, but quickly shifted his focus and advanced to intercept.

Both closed the distance, each aiming for the other... and the first to initiate an attack was Rei, who possessed long-range offensive capabilities.

"Flying Slash!"

The flying slash he unleashed streaked straight toward Resonance.

A straight-flying slash.

But Resonance already knew firsthand just how much power that attack possessed.

There was no need to take that attack head-on, the same technique that had effortlessly severed sturdy chains.

Fortunately, what was unleashed was a Flying Slash wielding the Death Scythe in a diagonal slash.

A slash delivered at an angle—if she knew it was coming, evading it wouldn't be difficult for Resonance.

Closing the distance with Rei, he tilted his body sideways, letting the slash pass harmlessly.

Then, having closed the distance to Rei, he swung the remnants of the chain, which could almost be called a chain whip.

Swinging with the sheer brute strength of someone wielding a flail, the chain would be impossible for a mere mortal to even react to in time.

The chain hurtled toward Rei at that incredible speed, but...

"Don't think an attack like that can touch me!"

He swung the Death Scythe, severing the tip of the chain whipping toward his face.

That strike shortened the chain whip, but it didn't mean the attack was over.

The chain whip was formed by severing the original iron ball at the tip, so cutting off a bit of the tip now was meaningless.

As Resonance pulled the chain whip back toward him, Rei, realizing this, made his next move.

"Magic Shield!"

The words to activate the skill were spoken, and a shield of light materialized. Resonance was momentarily startled by the sudden action, slowing his movements.

It was only a moment, but to Rei, it was a significant opening.

Seizing that opening, he lunged forward in one swift motion.

Seeing Rei move like that, Resonance snapped back to reality and swung the chain whip.

Leveraging his imposing stature, easily over two meters tall, he lashed the chain whip at Rei.

Of course, Resonance didn't expect Rei to simply take the blow.

She had clearly heard him shout "Magic Shield" and expected him to block it with the light shield before Rei.

But... even so, not only did he not dodge, he didn't even glance at the chain whip. Instead, he closed the distance between them head-on. This was a completely unexpected development.

The swung chain whip collided with the light shield Rei had deployed and was deflected.

Resonance tried to swing the deflected chain whip at Rei again... but seeing the light shield that had absorbed his strike dissolve into particles of light and vanish, he was momentarily distracted.

And even a momentary lapse in focus was something absolutely forbidden when facing an enemy like Rei.

Slash!

The slashing blow of the Death Scythe deeply gash Resonance's abdomen.

Even so, she managed to move her body instinctively, avoiding having her torso severed. She truly lived up to her reputation as the leader of the assassins.

(But even if this blow isn't fatal, it was deep enough. He's seriously injured, no doubt. This should slow him down...)

...slow down.

That's what Rei thought, but the next instant, the sound of air being sliced made him reflexively leap backward.

The next instant, a sharp sound and a dull thud echoed around him.

The sharp sound was the chain whip slicing through the air. The dull thud was the impact of the chain whip striking the sand.

"What!?"

Having temporarily distanced himself from Resonance, Rei was astonished that despite inflicting such a deep wound, it had immediately launched another attack... but he recalled the stories he'd heard from Daldora and his guards, and quickly understood.

"Right. Come to think of it, I heard you guys have something that numbs pain. Not just the others, but you too? ...I've fought against that sort of thing before, but usually those types rely on some external means... Well, you really are an assassin."

When Rei was in Japan, he'd seen a TV special about a disease that made people feel no pain.

It was congenital, and the number of people who developed it wasn't particularly high.

It's highly unlikely that everyone in this village has that disease...

(Come to think of it, that assassin I fought at the lab was from here too, right? Didn't seem like he was unaffected by pain though... Nah, he drank poison and died, so I guess I'll never know for sure.)

Even if someone feels no pain, drinking poison makes the issue of pain irrelevant.

As Rei pondered this, Resonance, despite spilling copious amounts of blood from his abdomen and even having his intestines protruding slightly, opened his mouth with a faint smile.

"Hmm, did you do something just now? Unfortunately, I have no idea what you did. ...And of course, regarding the secrets of this body, unless you defeat me, they'll remain a mystery, won't they?"

Smiling, straining his abdominal muscles to keep his organs from spilling out, Resonance showed no sign of suffering.

He began walking toward Rei again, step by step.

(Even if he doesn't feel pain, getting injured will definitely slow him down. ...Then, there are plenty of ways to handle this. And in the worst case, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour... No, using that would prevent me from extracting information.)

Death Scythe in hand, Rei also stepped forward toward Resonance, who was approaching him.
