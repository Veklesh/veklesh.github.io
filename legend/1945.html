Rei, Set, and the guards were walking toward the noble district.

Their destination was the base of the person believed to be behind the Kobold incident.

And this wasn’t just a disposable outpost like the one Grange had known about—it was a real, permanent base.

That said, given how cautious the enemy is, it wouldn’t be surprising if they had other bases besides the one they were heading to.

In any case, Rei and the others were heading toward that base, though they weren’t all walking together.

If Rei, Set, and a large number of guards were to move as a single group, they would naturally stand out.

That’s why Rei and Set were wandering around as if casually buying snacks, while the guards, dressed in plain clothes, split into several groups to move.

The reason for such high alertness was, after all, the belief that their current opponent possessed superior intelligence-gathering capabilities.

The biggest concern was the possibility of a traitor among the guards, but since they had acted swiftly after obtaining the information and were now monitoring each other, it was thought unlikely that information was leaking to the enemy.

(Well, if there are multiple people connected to them and they’re working together… then I can’t rule out the possibility that our information is leaking. But judging by how quickly they’re acting, I’d like to think there’s a good chance they haven’t given the enemy time to respond.)

While eating the skewered meat she bought from a street stall, Rei scanned her surroundings.

Perhaps because of their line of work, the guards didn’t stand out in any particular way; at first glance, they looked like ordinary passersby.

The way they blended in meant that the security guards were used to this kind of work.

(Come to think of it, I’ve seen unmarked patrol cars on special police TV programs—is this what that’s like? …No, if anything, is it more like plainclothes security guards or something like that?)

As she walked down the street, reminiscing about her time in Japan, the noble district must have been approaching, because the food stalls naturally began to thin out.

Some nobles simply cannot tolerate having anything resembling a food stall near where they live.

Of course, there are those who can tolerate them, but they are few and far between.

“Rei, we’re almost there.”

One of the approaching guards came up beside Rei and said briefly.

He was dressed in ordinary clothes that didn’t look like those of a guard, but his expression had shifted from that of a civilian to that of a guard.

This was true not only of the guard who had spoken to Rei, but of the other guards as well.

It was likely because they were approaching the noble district and the number of people around them had dwindled. Rather than staying scattered, they all gathered around Rei.

There were still a few passersby, but upon seeing Rei and Set, they simply assumed that was the way things were and walked past without giving it a second thought.

If Rei and Set were there, it wouldn’t be strange for anything to happen.

It was certain that was what they were thinking.

Rei had a few things he wanted to say about that, but since it worked out well for him at the moment, he didn’t voice his complaints.

They moved forward with the gathered guards, and eventually the building they were looking for came into view.

As expected of a location near the noble district, it was a rather grand mansion.

At the very least, it wasn’t the kind of building an ordinary person could easily afford—a mansion like that.

“Is that the mansion?”

“That’s it. …Though, that’s assuming what that man in the red cloth said wasn’t a load of nonsense.”

“Nonsense? It’s possible he resorted to those tactics to save himself… but he only sensed danger to himself after he’d already let that slip to me, didn’t he? Or perhaps he sensed the danger and pretended not to know anything… Nah.”

The guards around them nodded in agreement with Rei’s words.

If you spoke with that man even briefly, you’d immediately realize he wasn’t the type to think that far ahead.

It was obvious that he was just as simple-minded as he looked.

That’s probably why he obeyed a stupid order like breaking down the earthen wall.

“Anyway, let’s just sneak in already. …Just checking, but you’re okay with this, right?”

The guard Rei asked quietly nodded.

The question was meant to ask if it was really okay to proceed without something like a search warrant, but for a guard, this kind of recklessness is a daily occurrence.

After all, in a Gilm where so many adventurers gather, there are plenty of cases that simply can’t be resolved without taking such measures.

…Of course, if they went ahead with such a stunt as if it were perfectly normal and it turned out to be unrelated, they would be held accountable.

Especially in this case, even if it wasn’t in the Noble District itself, the incident took place at a mansion located near it.

If what the man in the red cloth had said turned out to be nonsense, the guards present here would undoubtedly be held accountable.

Even knowing that… even so, the guards had no intention of stopping this operation.

Even if they’re just Kobolds, the fact that someone in Gilm is capable of controlling such large numbers of them is clearly dangerous.

After all, if a massive swarm of Kobolds were to appear just as spring arrived, the streets filled with people, and expansion construction began, it would undoubtedly lead to a catastrophe.

As guards, given the high likelihood of such a dangerous situation occurring, they figured it would be no problem at all if it turned out to be nothing—they were willing to take the blame themselves.

Furthermore, it wasn’t as if the guards had no ulterior motives.

Naturally, the higher the risk, the greater the potential reward.

If they could catch the mastermind behind the Kobold incident here, it would bring significant benefits to the guards in their performance evaluations.

Still, from Rei’s perspective, it seems like the risks far outweigh the benefits.

(All things considered, he’s just a good guy, or maybe he has a strong sense of responsibility as a guard… that’s the kind of person he is.)

Watching the guards nod, Rei smiled and nodded in agreement.

“Well then, shall we go?”

“Yeah.”

The guards nodded at Rei’s words… and one of them spoke up.

“Just be careful, though. From what I can see, that mansion has plenty of places that would be convenient for hiding. In other words, if something happens, they might hide somewhere and wait for us to pass by, or depending on the situation, they might even launch a surprise attack on us.”

“Ah, you’re right. Outdoors, Set will probably spot places where enemies are hiding, but if they pull that kind of stunt inside the mansion, it’ll be a bit of a hassle. The good news is that there aren’t any Red Cloth guys here.”

The guards each reacted to Rei’s words… but perhaps because everyone naturally understood that they couldn’t stay like this forever, they stood in front of the mansion’s gate, ready to respond to a surprise attack at any moment.

If this were the noble district, or a mansion where people lived more openly, it wouldn’t be strange to have a gatekeeper… but unfortunately, there is currently no gatekeeper of any sort here.

Given that, they didn’t bother with the pretense of politely calling out from outside the gate; instead, they simply walked right through the gate and into the estate.

Normally, if someone were to trespass onto the estate’s grounds, it wouldn’t be unusual for someone from the household to come out immediately.

But at the moment, there was absolutely no sign of that.

“No one’s coming out. Do they not notice us at all, or are they waiting for us inside the mansion? …Which do you think it is?”

Rei asked, but the guards weren’t exactly familiar with this mansion either.

No, if anything, they looked at Rei as if to say, “Surely you should be able to predict this, given that you’ve known the man in the red cloth—who provided the information about this place—for at least a little while.”

But even though they’d known each other for a while, it had only been about an hour… no, just a few dozen minutes.

Rei’s honest thought was, “What could I possibly predict based on such a brief acquaintance?”

In any case, Set and a few of the guards remained outside the mansion, preparing to deal with anyone who might escape through a back door or window.

The rest of the group arrived at the front door, ready to enter the mansion.

“I didn’t expect there to be no reaction at all, even after coming this far. Did you think you could get away with it by pretending the place was empty?”

“I don’t know. I can’t say for sure they aren’t doing exactly as you said, Rei. But… I doubt they think they can pull a fast one on us like that. Once we find them, there’s no way to cover it up.”

“Like they didn’t notice someone was coming?”

Listening to the guards’ conversation, he knocked on the door as a matter of courtesy.

Rei didn’t expect anyone to come out, but still… or so he thought…

“Huh?”

The first to utter that sound was Rei, who had knocked.

He had been certain no one would come to the door, but he sensed someone approaching from the other side.

He was surprised precisely because he had assumed no one would dare to come out so brazenly, and seeing Rei’s reaction, the guards began to wonder.

But before the guards could ask Rei why he was so startled, the door opened. …It wasn’t Rei who opened it; it was someone inside the mansion.

“Hello, who might that be?”

The man who appeared, saying this, was a middle-aged man.

He was dressed impeccably, and looked exactly the part of a butler—anyone would agree.

“……”

Rei quietly turned his gaze toward the guard standing next to him.

He was asking with his eyes, “What do you think?” but the look in those eyes was one of deep confusion.

It was only natural. They had come here believing it to be a base linked to the mastermind behind the Kobold incident—the one pulling the strings behind the red cloth—only to be met by someone who looked nothing but a butler.

But the guard, caught in Rei’s gaze, was also at a loss as to how to respond to his confusion.

According to the security team’s records, this mansion is currently up for sale and shouldn’t have anyone living there.

That’s precisely why they’d judged it to be the perfect place to use as a base… but this outcome was completely unexpected.

“Ah, I’m sorry. I’m a security guard, but isn’t this mansion currently vacant?”

“A security guard, you say? For that, your attire is a bit…”

It’s not surprising that the middle-aged man would give the security guard who spoke to him a suspicious look, considering the guards were in plain clothes.

It was a sore spot for the guard to be called out on that, but another guard standing next to the one facing the old man managed to come up with an excuse.

“Actually, taking advantage of the fact that this mansion is vacant, we’ve recently received reports of suspicious individuals. However, as you know, this area is close to the aristocratic district, and if such a large number of guards were to move through here in a group, the suspects might notice and flee. That’s why we’ve come in plain clothes.”

“I see. But, well… as you can see, my husband has actually purchased this mansion and is currently living here. …Hasn’t the security team been informed of that?”

“No, they haven’t. That’s precisely why they assumed this was an unoccupied mansion. If I had to guess, perhaps the communication delay is due to the commotion surrounding the expansion work, the year-end and New Year’s chaos, or major events like the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.”

Rei shot the guard a look that seemed to ask, “Are you really counting the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle among those things?” but the guard simply brushed off Rei’s gaze.

“I see. Things have been busy at Gilm lately, too. Well, then, there’s no problem with this matter.”

“...No.”

It was Rei who spoke in response to the middle-aged man’s words.

There was something off about the words of the middle-aged man standing before him.

Rei couldn’t pinpoint exactly where the unease lay in what was otherwise a perfectly ordinary exchange.

He didn’t know… but there was no doubt that something felt off.

After all, it was an exchange that even the guards found unremarkable, so they, too, shot questioning glances at Rei, who had suddenly spoken up.

「Rei？」

At the guard’s words, Rei paused to think… and suddenly realized why he’d felt that sense of unease toward the middle-aged man in front of him.

“Why do I smell blood on you?”

It was such a faint scent of blood that an ordinary person probably wouldn’t have noticed it.

It was likely because Rei’s body had been created by the Zepairu clan and possessed senses sharper than those of ordinary humans that he was able to detect that scent of blood.

At Rei’s words, the middle-aged man’s body tensed.
