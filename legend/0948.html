Vicente's torso was severed as he spat curses, but even with the power of the Sanctuary, he hadn't become monstrous enough to survive in that state. He lay dead on the ground.

About thirty seconds after Vicente's life ended. The strange space surrounding them, called the Sanctuary and utterly incomprehensible to Rei, vanished as if it had never been there at all.

"Looks like he's really dead, huh?"

Vicente had harbored hatred toward Rei until the very end. In this world where magic existed, it wouldn't have been surprising if he'd somehow survived even after being cut clean in two.

That was precisely why Rei had remained wary of Vicente's condition, but now, finally, he could be certain he was dead. Exhaling in relief, Rei deactivated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and the search flames spread across the ground.

The surroundings plunged into darkness.

Of course. The brightness until just now had all been thanks to Rei's use of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and the flames spread across the ground.

"Grrr?"

Set approached Rei, who had just exhaled in relief, growling softly as if to ask, "Are you okay?"

The slight awkwardness in his gait was likely due to the impact of Vicente's strike.

During the fight, he'd been able to ignore the pain, but now that the battle was over and he was calmer, the pain had returned.

"Are you okay, Set? More so than me?"

Rei had taken several hits himself, but none were injuries that hindered his movement like Set's did.

Despite clearly being the one with the worse injuries, Set still worried about him. Rei patted his head in gratitude.

"Thanks, Set. Not just for worrying about my injuries, but if you hadn't come to back me up, I think I would've had a bit of trouble."

An old man wielding illusions and Vicente, a master of close combat.

Facing such a duo... and they'd even used that strange skill called Sanctuary. Had Rei fought alone, he would undoubtedly have struggled.

Though it seemed Rei had easily defeated the illusion-using old man, that was only possible because he fought in a way that prevented Vicente from coordinating with him.

"Grrrrrrr!"

It's okay! Set growled, rubbing his face against Rei's.

Seeing this, Rei smiled and pulled a potion from his Misty Ring.

It wasn't a superficial cut or wound, so the potion's effect wouldn't be immediately obvious, but it was still true that using it would heal the injury faster than not using it.

The potion, one of the more expensive ones Rei carried, immediately provided a noticeable effect when sprinkled onto Set.

There were potions in the Misty Ring with even stronger effects, but no matter how adorable Set was, Rei wouldn't use those on him.

After all, Set was a Rank A monster, and his self-healing abilities were incomparable to those of ordinary monsters.

That was the reason why even potions of this caliber were sufficient for treating a monster like Set.

"Guruu, grrrrrr..."

He must have felt the pain in his body fading. Set purred happily and rubbed his face against Rei.

"Honestly, Set's such a clingy little thing. But it's true, you saved me this time, so I'll indulge you as much as you want."

"Guruu!"

Set let out a delighted cry of "Yes!"...

"It's fine to pamper him, but I'd appreciate it if you could wait until after cleanup is done."

A voice suddenly echoed through the air.

"Huh!?"

Even though the battle had ended and their guard was down, they couldn't believe they hadn't noticed until spoken to. Rei gasped softly, and Set instantly went on high alert.

But Rei immediately recognized the voice. Stroking Set to calm his growling, he spoke.

"Calm down, Set. This one's an ally. ...Even if it doesn't look like it."

"...Grr?"

Set turned his gaze toward Rei, questioning.

Given they'd been fighting Vicente just minutes ago, it was only natural for him to be highly wary of a stranger.

Especially since both he and Rei had sustained injuries in this battle, his wariness was heightened.

Beyond that, he could sense that the person before him possessed immense power—far surpassing even Vicente, whom they'd just fought. As a high-rank monster, he recognized this immediately.

Seeing how Set looked ready to attack the figure before them—Sardato—at any moment if he weren't holding him back, Rei tilted his head.

"That's unusual. Set being this blatantly wary of someone."

"Well, that's understandable. I'd heard from various sources that you, Rei, have a griffin as your familiar, and I'd heard rumors too. But this is the first time we've actually met face-to-face, so it's only natural to be wary of a stranger, right? Especially to protect his master, Rei."

"...Huh? Oh, right. This is Set's first time meeting Sardat."

Sardat smiled slightly at Rei's words and nodded in response.

"That's right. Well, in my case, I was guarding Rei, so I've seen Rei with that griffin... Set, right? I've seen them together countless times, but this is the first time I've actually met him face-to-face. Oh, and I saw you flying through the sky earlier."

"Sorry, I totally forgot. Set, this is Sardat. He's the elf who was guarding me. And Sardat, this is Set—my partner, as you probably know."

"Grrr?"

At Rei's words, Set must have judged the person before him wasn't an enemy. He gazed at Sardat with his round eyes.

The wary gaze directed at him initially, and the innocent gaze directed at him now.

That both gazes came from the same creature was a bit surprising to Sardat as well.

Could one word from Rei really change things this much?

"So, what the hell are you here for? For someone who's supposed to be my escort, you showed up after the fight was over."

"Ah... yeah. Honestly, I feel bad about that part. Rei, you probably know since we met before, but there was another Stigma besides the one you defeated. I was held up by that one. So, I finally defeated it and came over here, but..."

The way he shrugged his shoulders slightly showed he understood he'd arrived too late.

But Rei didn't intend to blame Saldat for that.

Having experienced it firsthand, he knew better than anyone how troublesome the Stigmata were.

Still, Rei couldn't help but wonder about the skill used in the earlier battle. He glanced at Vicente's corpse, split clean through the torso, before speaking.

"By the way... just out of curiosity, but didn't that Stigma that held you back use some weird skill called Sanctuary or something?"

Hearing this, Saldat must have immediately understood that Sanctuary had been activated here. He looked at Rei with surprise in his eyes.

"Knowing that term means you were hit by Sanctuary too? ...Well, I'm surprised you survived that. Not that I should be the one saying it, but that Sanctuary is a terribly troublesome thing."

"Knowing that means you've been affected too?"

Sardat tilted his handsome face slightly, nodding in response to Rei's question as she stroked Set.

"Yes. I've fought Stigmata several times before. Some of them used similar skills. Though, deploying the Sanctuary... no, the effects of Stigmata vary greatly from person to person. It seems not everyone possesses uniform skills."

"Speaking of which..."

At Saldat's words, Rei's gaze shifted away from Vicente, whose torso had been split clean in two, and landed on the corpse of the old man lying some distance away.

If everyone possessed the ability to deploy a Sanctuary, then surely that old man could have used it too.

At first, I thought the old man used illusions because he couldn't deploy a double Sanctuary. But now I wonder if those illusions were actually the old man's Stigma ability.

"That other Stigma over there, the one split in half, was using incredibly elaborate illusions... so realistic you couldn't tell if they were real until you actually touched them. Could that also be a Stigma ability?"

"...I'm not sure. I don't know much about Stigmata either. The Holy Light Church is an organization with such deep darkness. Even though I've fought Stigmata before, it's not like I have extensive experience. ...So, to answer Rei's question, I can only say probably."

During the fight, I could tell it was an illusion almost purely by instinct, but before that, I even suspected he might be using teleportation magic.

Creating illusions that elaborate was astonishing even to Rei's eyes. Had Set not come to support him, even knowing it was an illusion, he would have been thrown off balance and struggled against Vicente.

Rei realized anew that the reason he was here, having defeated Stigma nearly unscathed, was because Set had come and taken on Vicente.

Though, that same Vicente had been focused on fighting Set... no, forced to focus by Set's strength, and it was that opening that allowed Rei to strike him from behind and end his life.

(Even if I did manage to defeat Noiz, it probably just came down to having the advantage this time. I didn't think I was getting cocky, but looking back at this fight, I can't help but feel that way. ...Sigh)

As Rei pondered the matter of battle compatibility once more, Sardat spoke up.

"...Well, whatever happened, it's best we're both safe. Honestly, I can't say I fulfilled my role as your escort at all."

"Well, there was that Sanctuary thing, after all. ...Honestly, it's been a while since I fought someone that difficult. I don't want to call it a matter of compatibility, but I guess it really is compatibility after all."

Sardat gave a wry smile in agreement as Rei sighed.

"Of course there are opponents you just don't mesh with. Though, even if you don't mesh, struggling is one thing—it doesn't mean you should lose. More importantly, cleaning up this mess is going to be a real pain, huh?"

Sardat's gaze fell on the battlefield where Rei had just been fighting Stigma.

Rei’s Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour had melted the snow into water, which had then evaporated into steam.

Just moments ago, it had been stiflingly hot, but now that the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour was deactivated, the cold night air of winter rushed in. The warmth they'd felt before made the cold feel all the more intense.

...Though, Rei was clad in his Dragon Robe, and Set was a Griffin.

In that sense, the one suffering the most from the cold was Sardat.

"Anyway, we'll handle cleaning up here. As for Rei and the others... I'd like to say you can go back to your rooms, but I suppose that's not possible."

"Besides, the room's pretty trashed since we got attacked by those Stigma guys while we were sleeping."

"...Let's head over to Erinde's place for now. She seems eager to hear what happened too."

Sardat's gaze fell upon a small bird figure familiar even to Rei.

"If she was watching us with her familiar's eyes, I don't think we need to explain the situation."

"Not really. Erinde wasn't watching our situation from the start either, right? ...Right?"

The little bird chirped softly in response to Sardat's question.

Its attitude confirmed Sardat was right.

"Besides, if the room was damaged, we'll need to tell Erinde to get us a new one anyway, right? Come on, it's cold in here. Let's get moving."

"Hold on. Let me at least salvage whatever I can while I still can."

Responding to Saldat's words, the Misty Ring retrieved the light ore armor, gauntlets, and greaves from Vicente's corpse, along with armor and a staff from the old man's body. Then they headed toward the school building housing the Headmaster's office.

Observing this, the little bird landed on Saldat's shoulder, indicating it would accompany them.

"Ah... If possible, I'd appreciate things being handled as smoothly as possible."

"Chirp chirp chirp!"

Listening to this exchange from behind, Rei finally realized that the Stigma that had attacked him had been dealt with.

(The third Stigma fought Saldato, and since he's here unharmed, it probably means the other one was either killed or captured.)

Watching the nearly unscathed Saldato, Rei prayed the conversation in the Headmaster's office wouldn't drag on, hoping he could get to sleep as soon as possible.
