How to distribute the Silver Lion materials the Elks said they didn't need.

Rei and the others were the first to react to Rei's announcement.

"Rei, we don't want the materials either. We weren't any help in the fight against the Silver Lion. We can't very well claim the Silver Lion's reward, can we?"

"...Is that okay?"

For Rei, it was precisely because Johanna and the others had taken care of the unconscious Vihera and Rodos that he could fight the Silver Lion with peace of mind.

Given that, he had intended to accept any request for the Silver Lion's materials... but before Rei could say anything, Johanna had already stated they didn't want the materials, so he couldn't force it on them.

He asked the others besides Johanna as well, but all he got back were nods of agreement supporting Johanna's words.

Amidst this, Johanna suddenly spoke up.

"Ah, right. We don't need the Silver Lion's materials, but please don't forget about the right to spend a day with Set-chan."

Her expression clearly conveyed that the right to spend a day with Set was far more important than the Silver Lion materials. She was, without a doubt, the ultimate Set enthusiast.

Rei could only nod with a wry smile at her words.

If Set disliked Johanna, it might be different. But for Set, who genuinely enjoyed people's attention, Johanna was someone who would play with him, just like Milein.

Even if Rei told him he could spend a day with Johanna, he'd be happy about it, never refuse.

"We already made that arrangement beforehand, right? ...So, you guys said you don't need the Silver Lion materials, but is there anything else you want?"

He nodded at Johanna's words and asked, but the other men looked around at each other in confusion.

"Other than that...?"

"Hmm, for a reward like this, is it still money?"

"But we're not exactly short on money either."

Each of them muttered to themselves.

Johanna and the others had come to this dungeon seeking a potion that could restore Vihera's consciousness.

And while repeatedly exploring the dungeon in search of that potion, even if they didn't find what they sought, their pockets naturally grew fuller with monster materials and various other items.

Long before worrying about winter provisions, they'd already earned enough to live off for the entire next year.

...That money had originally been saved in case someone else found the potion to restore Vihera's consciousness in this dungeon and offered to sell it to them. But now, the reason for saving it had vanished.

So naturally, that money would be divided among themselves.

They had already discussed and decided on those details the day before.

That's precisely why Johanna and the others didn't need the money... well, not exactly, but they didn't particularly want it that badly right now.

Of course, more money meant a more comfortable life, so it wasn't that they didn't want it.

"Hmm, let's see. If we're going to spend it, it should be on something money can't buy... Ah, I know!"

One of the men suddenly spoke up as if struck by an idea, turning his gaze toward Rei.

"Um, Rei-san. If it's not too much trouble, could we get a little silver lion meat? Not a huge amount is fine. Since we're here, we'd like to let the guys waiting in Gilm try some silver lion meat."

The others nodded in agreement.

The men hesitated, thinking it odd to ask for Silver Lion meat after saying they didn't need it, but Rei nodded immediately.

"Alright. Well, I didn't take down the Silver Lion alone, so I can't guarantee it, but I think it should be fine."

The truth was, Rei had been after the silver lion's meat too.

In fact, he valued the meat more than the fur, fangs, claws, tail, or internal organs. ...Except for the magic stones, of course.

Of course, materials from a rank S monster like the Silver Lion would make incredibly powerful magic items, weapons, and armor.

But for Rei, the most important material from the Silver Lion was, first and foremost, naturally the magic stone, followed by the Silver Lion's meat.

After all, the Silver Lion measured six meters in total length... and even excluding its tail, which was nearly two meters long, its body alone was close to four meters. The quantity of meat it yielded was substantial.

In terms of sheer quantity, bringing some back as souvenirs for the former rangers wouldn't make much of a dent.

Upon hearing Rei's assessment, the men nodded happily in agreement.

The higher the monster's rank, the greater the magical power it possesses, and the better the taste of its meat becomes.

So, just how delicious would the meat of an S-rank monster be...? There wasn't a single person who didn't harbor that expectation.

Even Elk, who had said he didn't want the Silver Lion's materials, imagined the taste of that meat and found his mouth watering.

But once he'd said he didn't want it, Elk's pride wouldn't let him go back and ask for the meat after all.

All Elk could do now was watch Johanna's companions with envy.

Naturally, Rei noticed Elk's state and braced himself for a smaller share of the meat.

"So that leaves me, Elena, Marina, and Ara—four of us."

The remaining members were practically family.

One of them was Vihera, but she hadn't done anything in this matter... In fact, she naturally declined, reasoning that it would be strange for her to receive materials when she had only been on the receiving end.

Because of that, Rei didn't need to agonize over the distribution of materials. He was thinking about how to divide the Silver Lion's materials...

"Um, Rei. I must also decline the Silver Lion's materials."

「Ara？」

Rei stared intently at Ara, surprised by her refusal to accept any materials.

Under his gaze, Ara wore a gloomy expression despite having accomplished the great feat of defeating the Silver Lion and seeing Vihera, Elena's friend and her master, regain consciousness.

With both Rei and Elena's eyes on her, Ara finally spoke reluctantly.

"I couldn't do anything at first during the battle with the Silver Lion. I was frozen in fear by its roar... I held everyone back. For someone like me to want the Silver Lion's materials..."

"But it's also true that we defeated the Silver Lion because of Ara's power. While it wasn't as devastating as Elk's Axe of the Thunder God, Ara's strike was undeniably effective against the Silver Lion."

It was Elena, not Rei, who said this.

It wasn't that she was covering for someone close to her... rather, purely considering the significant role Ara had played, the option of not receiving the Silver Lion's materials simply didn't exist.

Had it been someone like Elk, with the Rhodes incident, or Johanna and the others, who hadn't participated in the battle at all, that argument might have been accepted.

Ara had judged that if Elena was fighting, she naturally had to fight too, and joined the battle. And as Elena had said, she proved to be a fully capable combatant.

At the very start of the battle, the Silver Lion's roar might have rendered her useless.

But that wasn't unique to Ara. For ordinary adventurers, possessing a means to counter it was simply difficult.

Above all, while Ara was temporarily incapacitated by the Silver Lion's Roar, she did recover mid-battle.

There was no doubt she contributed significantly to the fight from that point onward.

"But, Elena-sama..."

"Besides, if Ara doesn't get materials, won't it make it harder for Marina and me to get any too?"

Not for Ara's sake, but so that she and Marina could receive Silver Lion materials too.

Implied by this, Ara finally nodded in agreement.

(It's not my favorite approach... but if I hadn't said it like this, Ara probably wouldn't have accepted the materials.)

Elena watched Ara as she thought.

She knew from their long association that Aara was loyal to her and had an extremely conscientious nature.

But precisely because of that, Elena wished Ara would think more about herself.

She understood this was partly her own well-meaning imposition, yet Elena still wished Ara could see things in a slightly more favorable light.

Besides, while it couldn't be said Ara's attack dealt a fatal blow to the Silver Lion, it had undoubtedly been an effective strike.

Thinking that, Elena's feelings weren't entirely misguided.

Seeing Elena like this, Marina spoke up in support.

"Well... I do need some materials myself, but... if Ara, who fought in the battle, can't get any, it makes me feel a bit hesitant."

"But..."

Hearing Elena and Marina's words, Ara looked troubledly toward Rei.

Since Rei was technically the leader of this party, her actions probably stemmed from wanting him to do something about it...

"Yeah. I think Ara definitely deserves her share of materials."

Rei promptly sided with Elena and the others.

No, it wasn't just Rei. Set and Yellow, standing beside him, purred and squeaked in agreement with Rei and Elena.

"Yeah. From my perspective, Ara was definitely useful. She even helped tend to my injuries."

Elk added, and Min nodded in agreement.

Under everyone's gaze, Ara opened her mouth, seeming resigned.

"Very well. If it's acceptable for me to receive something, I'd like the materials. ...Since I already have the Power Axe Rei gave me for a weapon, could I have the silver lion's fur for armor?"

Silver lion fur possessed strong resistance to both physical and magical attacks, so using it as armor material would undoubtedly create powerful gear.

Of course, to maximize that material's potential, you'd need an alchemist with the proper skill or a blacksmith who crafts armor... or other artisans for clothing or shoes made from the fur.

"Well, Ara's armor isn't that powerful anyway, so why not? Fortunately, we have a considerable amount of silver lion fur."

Considering the size of a Silver Lion, the amount of fur needed to make armor for one person probably isn't that much.

The specific part of the body used would likely affect the armor's performance, but considering it was the fur of a Rank S monster... one boasting high defense at that... it should be vastly different in quality from armor made from lower-ranked monsters.

With Ara safely agreeing to provide the Silver Lion's materials, Rei's gaze naturally turned to the remaining two.

"What about Elena and Marina?"

Elena was the first to speak in response.

"After saying that to Ara, I suppose I can't very well refuse to take materials myself. ...Hmm, well then. I'll take the Silver Lion's claws."

"Oh dear, I was aiming for the Silver Lion's claw to make arrowheads. Well then, I suppose I'll take the Silver Lion's tendon? It should be just the right material for making bowstrings."

Their desired materials didn't clash, and Elena and Marina's choices were settled easily.

But Rei, who had been listening, turned to the Silver Lion with a troubled expression.

It was only natural. Ara wanted the silver lion's fur, Elena its claws, Marina its tendons.

While these were undoubtedly extremely rare and valuable materials, the Silver Lion's materials still existed in large quantities.

What should be done with the vast amount of remaining materials?

As Rei pondered this, his gaze suddenly caught Grim staring intently at him.

His expression, as if watching some amusing spectacle, was slightly irritating to Rei... but considering this matter, although Grimm hadn't participated in the Silver Lion battle, he had played an even more crucial role.

To Rei, Grimm had every right to obtain his share of the Silver Lion's materials.

(After all, this whole affair had been nothing but Grim taking care of him. It was Grim who told him that the Silver Lion's heart could restore Vihera and Rodos' consciousness. It was Grim who transported him straight to the lowest level of the dungeon. And it was Grim who actually restored their consciousness.)

Despite all that help, Rei hadn't been able to repay Grimm in any way.

He was essentially taking advantage of Grimm's kindness solely because he was connected to Zepairu.

"Grimm"

"Hmm. Let me make this clear upfront: I have no use for Silver Lion materials. Unfortunately, there's nothing I particularly need right now."

But Grimm cut Rei off before he could speak.

And so, after discussion, it was ultimately decided that most of the Silver Lion would become Rei's.
