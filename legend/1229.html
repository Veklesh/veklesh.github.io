"Those scheduled to regain consciousness... Vihera and Rodos, you said? Lay them out on the floor."

Grim issued the order while keeping the silver lion's heart—still pulsing even after its magic stone had been extracted—floating near him.

Following his command, Johanna and her companions laid the two on the floor.

The sight of Vihera and Rodos laid out on the floor was starkly contrasting.

A woman and a man, one unconscious and the other emaciated.

Seeing them both, the group collectively exhaled softly once more.

These two would regain consciousness soon.

Even as they understood this, a lingering doubt remained in the back of their minds: Would they truly regain consciousness?

That doubt was likely tied to the fact that Grimm was an undead monster.

Grim was aware of the gazes directed at him from those around him, but showing no particular concern, he moved on to his next action.

He moved his heart above the unconscious pair, then lightly tapped the floor with the tip of his staff.

Instantly, a magic circle materialized on the floor, centered around Vihera and Rodos.

...Yes, rather than deliberately drawing it himself, the magic circle had been created instantly.

And it wasn't a small one either.

It was a large magic circle, centered on Vihera and Rodos, with a radius of about five meters.

"What!?"

The sudden sight before them drew gasps of astonishment from everyone.

That's how utterly absurd the scene unfolding before Rei and the others was.

But Grimm seemed oblivious to their cries, his left hand—the one without the staff, a hand now nothing but bones—moving in intricate patterns as he wove words incomprehensible to Rei and the others.

As the words... the incantation progressed, the pulsation of the heart hovering above Vihera and Rodos gradually weakened. In its place, light poured down from the heart, showering Vihera.

The fact that no light from the heart was reaching Rhodes at this point was likely simply an execution of prioritizing Vihera.

Elk and Min's expressions were stern and tense as they watched the light pour down upon Vihera.

It was only natural, for if the light faded before reaching Rhodes, it would mean Rhodes would not regain consciousness today.

Elk's fist, clenched with such force that his nails threatened to pierce his skin and draw blood, and Min, gripping her staff so tightly her knuckles turned white.

The others watched the light pouring down from the Silver Lion's heart, praying that somehow Vihera would regain consciousness.

How much time passed like that?

To Rei and the others, who watched intently, it felt like seconds, minutes... or perhaps even hours.

Eventually, the rain of light showering Vihera's body ceased.

The Silver Lion's heart, still suspended in the air, had shrunk considerably compared to when it first began showering light, yet it still retained a considerable size.

Seeing that heart, Elk and Min let out a slight sigh.

Judging by this, there was a chance they might be able to restore Rhodes' consciousness.

The foreign object within him.

Vihera had no idea how much time had passed since the battle against that foreign object began.

Outside, over two months had passed, but Vihera had no way of knowing that.

Her sense of time had completely vanished.

Amidst this numbness to time, Vihera continued battling the foreign entity within her.

Ambris. She knew it was called that, but even against such an opponent, she kept launching attack after attack.

Of course, these weren't attacks in the sense of direct punches or kicks like in the real world, let alone using skills.

It was a battle of recognition and rejection.

No, perhaps "exclusion" is more accurate than "rejection."

He fought this battle endlessly, yet perhaps due to the absence of any sense of time, Vihera felt no fatigue... not in the mental sense, at least.

On the contrary, as time passed, he was even able to gradually overwhelm Ambris.

But... while I can overwhelm him, I cannot touch what could be called the core of Ambris.

To be precise, I can attack it, but it's more accurate to say Vihera's attacks simply don't land.

Whether Ambris understood this or not, he retreated into his shell, adopting an impenetrable defensive posture, and began launching attacks as if seeking openings in Vihera's defenses.

Looking solely at the state of the battle, Vihera held an overwhelming advantage.

Yet, even so, he couldn't secure the win... It had become a stalemate, close to a perpetual check.

(This is frustrating. If this situation drags on, I'm probably worrying Rei...)

Even if spoken aloud, in this situation, the words wouldn't actually resonate as sound.

This was such a peculiar space.

Vihera had grown accustomed to it, but honestly, she couldn't think of a way to deal with the core of Ambris that lay before her gaze.

Other things besides that core... like the tentacle-like entities extending from it, trying to invade her—she could easily eliminate them by projecting a strong thought of exclusion.

But that only worked on the parts of Ambris extending from the core.

The core itself was untouchable.

Since he was eliminating parts of Ambris, he had sometimes thought that if he just kept at it, he might eventually defeat it.

But then I also wondered: if Ambris truly sensed danger to itself, wouldn't it stop even extending tentacles altogether?

As long as it remains within its core, Vihera's attacks are ineffective.

In other words, its core guarantees its survival at the very least.

That was extremely troublesome for Vihera, and it was also the reason why she couldn't carelessly destroy the tentacles extending from the core.

(What on earth should I do? This is incredibly troublesome... If my attacks don't work against Ambris, is it purely because my attack power is insufficient? But attack power in this world doesn't seem to have anything to do with the physical body itself...)

There's nothing I can do.

That was Vihera's conclusion.

Of course, she still tried to figure something out, thinking and experimenting... but in the end, it was all for naught.

As a result, even though over ninety percent of this world already belonged to Vihera, she found herself stuck in place.

(I think Rei will figure something out... Was it a while ago? Or just a moment ago? I've had that sensation several times where my body suddenly felt energized.)

That sensation was actually caused by Rei feeding Vihera the World Tree Potion by mouth, or by a healer casting recovery magic on Vihera... but the person himself was completely unaware of it.

Even if she felt that surge of strength, it was useless in this space.

...Well, it did help in the sense that it meant Rei was trying to do something for me from outside.

(No, I have to do something about this. If I stay like this, I'll never be able to see Rei again.)

The instant that thought crossed her mind... suddenly, truly suddenly, Vihera felt her consciousness growing stronger.

It was an increase in power so profound it made him want to ask, "What was I even doing before?"

If I had to compare it, if my previous self was a goblin, then my current self was more like a cyclops.

(Well, I do dislike comparing myself to a goblin...)

Even as he dismissed his own thoughts, Vihera couldn't help but smile at the surge of power filling his body, regardless of the reason.

Above all, Vihera understood perfectly well who had done this.

(It must be Rei, no doubt. Marina might be helping too. Or... come to think of it, has Byune returned yet?)

Images of various people flashed through Vihera's mind.

Even as she pondered, Vihera could feel her power steadily increasing with each passing moment.

Moreover... it seemed even Ambris within Vihera understood why her abilities were increasing, for Ambris's core wavered as if confused.

Until now, no matter what Vihera did, she couldn't damage its core. But now it was different.

Vihera, now visibly imbued with immense power, could undoubtedly destroy the current core. That was her thought.

Though, even saying he thought it, Ambris didn't possess clear, human-like reasoning abilities.

It acted almost entirely on instinct, so perhaps it was more accurate to say it sensed it.

But... precisely because of that, it feared the opponent before it.

Ambris had been stimulated by the fear of being annihilated by Rei, triggering its survival instinct. It chose to enter Vihera and take over its body as a means to survive. Now, Ambris's survival instinct was being stimulated once more.

If things continued like this, he would die.

He understood that, but having already entered Vihera's body, he could not leave of his own volition.

He would vanish if this continued, and there was no way to avoid it.

Ambris's greatest miscalculation was likely Vihera's mental fortitude.

Far from taking over Vihera's body, the current Ambrus merely clings to survival as a core within a mere fragment of Vihera's mental world.

Die, live, die, live, die, live.

Ambris lacked the capacity for complex thought, but precisely because of that, he understood simply that he would die if things continued this way, that he could not survive.

He understood from past experience that fighting meant defeat, and escape was impossible—he couldn't flee from within Vihera's mental world. Defending against attacks was another matter; while he might have managed a short while ago, there was no way his defenses could withstand Vihera's current offensive power.

It was truly a state that could be called utterly hopeless, beyond any doubt. That was Ambris's current situation.

Even if he wanted to fight back, there was no way to do it.

All Ambris could do was retreat into his core and fortify his defenses.

...In this case, it was fortunate that Ambris's self-awareness hadn't developed too much.

Had it developed a self-awareness, it likely wouldn't have been able to endure the terror of its own annihilation.

Even so, without making a sound, Ambris, awakened by its survival instinct, pondered whether there was any way it could survive.

Perhaps because it was originally a natural phenomenon, the idea of negotiating with Vihera never crossed its mind.

While Ambris hesitated, Vihera began to act.

It began to erode, targeting Ambris's core.

Until recently, no matter how hard it tried, Vihera could not erode Ambris' core.

But now, with Vihera's increased power, even Ambris's core could be eroded.

Of course, the entire core wouldn't be eroded all at once, but still, little by little... yet steadily, Ambris's core was being eroded by the very existence of Vihera.

Die. Lose. Vanish.

Ambris' instincts couldn't be clearly put into words, but instinctively, it was filled with an aversion to the idea of its own disappearance.

Vanish, vanish, vanish.

When it realized that its own annihilation was an absolutely unavoidable fate... a single thought was born within Ambris, alongside despair.

It could not accept simply fading away like this.

Therefore, by any means necessary... even if he could no longer recognize his own existence, he wanted to leave something of himself behind.

Even as this change of heart unfolded within Ambris, Vihera's erosion of the core continued.

The core, which had been absolutely inviolable just moments before, was already about forty percent eroded by Vihera.

If this continued, he would be consumed and cease to exist before long.

Action had to be taken before that happened.

Being an Ambris driven solely by instinct, once it decided it wanted to leave even the slightest trace of its existence, hesitation ceased to exist, and its actions followed swiftly.

It welcomed the Vihera with such utter simplicity that it was hard to believe it had been defending itself with its core just a moment before.

And as he watched himself being consumed, he spread himself out, allowing Vihera to absorb him completely.

(Huh?)

Though Vihera was puzzled by the sudden cessation of Ambris' resistance, it was precisely because they were connected mentally that he could understand the thoughts Ambris held.

If she was going to vanish anyway, she wanted Vihera to absorb her, hoping to leave behind even a trace of herself.

Why she didn't reject Ambris but accepted him, Vihera herself couldn't quite grasp.

Was it because they had shared this strange time together, both short and long? Or was it because she needed more power to stay by Rei's side?

The only thing he knew for certain was that accepting Ambris—or more accurately, absorbing him—would undoubtedly increase his own power.

And if her power increased, it also meant she could engage in more fulfilling battles against stronger foes.

For various reasons... as a result, Vihera decided to accept Ambris, absorb it, and make it her nourishment.

After completing the absorption of Ambris, light gradually filled the surroundings, and for the first time in a long while, Vihera performed the act of opening the eyelids of his own physical body.
