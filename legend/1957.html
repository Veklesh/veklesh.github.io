The report that Rei and his group had fought pink tentacles was, naturally, relayed to the upper echelons of the security force. The person who received the report felt unable to make a decision on his own and passed it on to his superiors.

In this manner, the report was passed up the chain of command, higher and higher... until, ultimately, it reached Daskar, the lord of Gilm.

However, the reason the person who received the report judged that they could not handle it themselves was not because they did not want to take responsibility... but rather, largely because it was an issue that could potentially endanger the entire realm of Gilm.

Those who received the report simply did not have the authority to handle it.

That is why all they could do was station guards around the mansion Rei and the others had searched to prevent people from coming and going freely.

...No, considering the location was near the Noble District, they should actually be commended for making that decision.

“So, no one can enter that mansion right now. Is that acceptable?”

“Yes. However, if what was written in the report is true, that means the underground space is connected to the surrounding mansions as well…”

The subordinate who brought the report to Daskar hesitated to say it.

But Daskar had no intention of reprimanding him for it.

Given its proximity to the Noble Quarter, it goes without saying that the owner of that mansion possesses considerable wealth—in other words, someone with influence.

Moreover, it’s highly unlikely that such a person would readily admit their estate is connected to a place like that if told so.

Furthermore, if the mansion is large, it’s quite possible that even the owner doesn’t fully understand its layout.

“In that case, I suppose I’ll have to actually climb those stairs leading from the underground space to see where they lead. …The problem is those pink tentacles mentioned in the report, isn’t it?”

Seeing that even Rei struggled with it makes choosing who to send extremely difficult.

Having commissioned Rei countless times before, Daskar naturally knows exactly how powerful Rei is.

Given that this is an opponent who gave even Rei a hard time, they certainly can’t just send someone off the street.

Daskar also doesn’t want to do something as reckless as sending his subordinates into a place where they’re likely to die without a proper plan.

“In that case, should I ask Rei…? No, since it mentioned there were a lot of stairs, I might need to hire other high-ranking adventurers besides Rei.”

“In that case, it’s fortunate that it’s winter right now. It’s rare for Gilm to be away on a job.”

“...Now that you mention it, that’s true. The problem is whether his body has gotten out of shape over the winter.”

If he’s not working as an adventurer and just spending his time partying at the tavern… well, naturally, his body will get out of shape.

That said, most adventurers do some training to keep their bodies from getting completely out of shape, but even so, they inevitably get a bit rusty compared to the spring and fall.

“I can’t really say for sure about that. But wouldn’t a high-ranking adventurer be on top of that sort of thing?”

“If Elk were here, I’d leave it to him.”

Daskar recalled the man with the nickname “Axe of the Thunder God,” who had a boisterous personality.

Elk and the others are currently away from Gilm, trying to heal the wounds—though “wounds” is a bit of an understatement—their son sustained during the incident in the Bestir Empire.

If they were here, they’d be quite reliable in a situation like this.

“There’s no point counting on someone who isn’t here. So, what do you want to do? Should we submit a request to the Guild for a high-ranking adventurer?”

“Yes, please do that. If possible, I’d like to explore that underground space tomorrow and secure the surrounding mansions as well.”

With a sigh and a gloomy expression, he poured water from the pitcher into a glass and drained it.

Daskar felt, just a little—really, just a little—as if the water had washed away his gloomy mood, and he returned his thoughts to the matter at hand.

“Whatever we do, that underground space is definitely the biggest headache. To begin with, does that so-called ‘crack’ in the space appear whenever someone enters, or is there some other reason? That part is particularly troublesome.”

“I suppose so. …If only we could get any information out of the man they captured…”

“He doesn’t seem to be spilling anything, does he?”

“Yes. Apparently, he’s received specialized training, so even torture was useless.”

Though known as a man of integrity, Daskar would not hesitate to resort to torture if necessary as a lord.

To be precise, he gives orders to have it done.

“This is troublesome. In that case, I’ll either have to go down to the underground chambers myself to investigate, or examine the evidence seized by Rei and the guards. …How is the code-breaking coming along?”

“We’ve currently summoned scholars and alchemists to look into it, but…”

Even without him finishing his sentence, Daskar could tell from his expression that things weren’t going well.

“In that case, I suppose I’ll have to go there myself. This is a hassle.”

“I suppose so.”

His subordinate agreed with Daskar’s words.

But just because it was a hassle didn’t mean he could simply leave it be; from Daskar’s perspective, all he could do was give it his all.

He prayed that if he had to go through a tough time today, it would make tomorrow just a little bit easier.

“……Huh?”

While Daskar was concentrating on his work, the man was in a foul mood.

And no wonder. After all, the crux of this whole affair—the underground chamber—had been discovered by the guards.

Moreover, given the timing, there was a very high probability that they had been seen sacrificing the Akabata.

Unless something like that had happened, it was inconceivable that the person who took the Akabuta would be found dead—and, like the Akabuta, reduced to nothing but skin and bones.

In other words, it was safe to say that the worst-case scenario had unfolded for the man.

“Um, so… what should we do?”

“What do you mean, ‘what should we do’? How the hell should I know, you idiot. Now that they’ve found that place, there’s nothing we can do about it. All we can do is quickly dispose of anything that might serve as evidence and get the hell out of Gilm. Fortunately, we don’t need to use the gate to get out right now.”

Realizing what the man’s words meant, his subordinate’s face twisted in horror.

Because that meant they would have to go outside through the earthen wall Rei had built.

“Wait, wait a minute! The Kobolds won’t just disappear right away, and if we try to escape, it’ll be night by then! And that means there’ll be hordes of monsters roaming outside Gilm…”

“True. …But even so, we need to escape from here. I thought you’d understand that much.”

At the man’s words, his subordinate was at a loss for words.

In fact, it was certain that staying in this mansion any longer would lead to disaster.

The discovery of the underground passage meant that a thorough search would inevitably reveal it was connected to this mansion.

If that were the case, there was more than enough reason to believe the mansion was already under surveillance.

He knew that, but including the man who had brought the report, there weren’t that many people here with actual combat ability.

Before winter fully set in and the number of visitors dwindled, there had been several people with combat prowess equal to—or in some cases, even greater than—that of the superior standing before him. But now, those people were nowhere to be found, and this superior was the only one with significant combat ability.

“If I hadn’t been summoned so suddenly, we might have managed… Ugh!”

The subordinate muttered in frustration... Hearing this, the man, his voice laced with irritation, hurled the glass of alcohol sitting on the desk at him.

The glass cup shattered the moment it struck the man’s head.

A strong smell of alcohol wafted through the room.

Was it a good thing or a bad thing for the man that the smell of blood from his cut, caused by the glass shards, was masked by the alcohol?

“Don’t talk nonsense. Are you trying to say that if those guys weren’t here, I’d get arrested? Huh?”

Facing a gaze that was less an intense stare and more a glare, the man didn’t even bother to wipe away the blood trickling down his face; his legs trembled.

He understood that if the person standing before him were so inclined, he could kill someone like him in an instant.

Moreover, that brutality was evident from watching several others being punished for failing their jobs.

In that sense, perhaps he should be grateful that it ended with just this much.

“I’m sorry. But the enemy has a large number of guards, including Rei. In fact, depending on the situation, they’ll likely bring in other adventurers as reinforcements. If that happens, we’ll inevitably be outnumbered.”

“...Tch.”

He picked up another glass that had been set out near the table to replace the one he’d thrown and shattered, then poured liquor into it.

It could be called an act of half-hearted desperation, but it also demonstrated that what his subordinate was saying wasn’t entirely wrong.

“Why not just unleash those tentacles on the town? …No, that’s impossible. As long as we’re bound by the contract, there’s nothing we can do about it.”

His subordinate, who knew just how vicious the tentacle was, felt a chill run down his spine at his boss’s words… but he was relieved when his boss immediately denied the idea.

If those tentacles were allowed to move freely within Gilm, Gilm itself would surely suffer massive damage.

No, in the worst-case scenario, it wouldn’t be surprising if Gilm were completely destroyed.

As a member of the Royal Faction, the city of Gilm is certainly a troublesome presence… but the materials and magical items obtained from Gilm undoubtedly benefit the Kingdom of Mireana.

No, even among the Royal Faction, there are those who are profiting handsomely from what Gilm has to offer.

Considering that, there’s no way I can agree to unleashing those tentacles all over the city.

“There was an organization that was trying to curry favor with us. Could we use that shadowy organization to create a diversion?”

He asks his bloodied subordinate while sipping his freshly poured drink.

But the subordinate shakes his head at his superior’s suggestion.

“If Rei gets involved, I don’t think it’s possible. If it’s Gilm’s organization, they’re aware of Rei’s power. They’d likely think, ‘Better not risk a clash with Rei and cause casualties…’”

“Tch.”

He clicked his tongue in irritation at his subordinate’s words.

But the fact that he didn’t fly into a rage like he did earlier was likely because he had anticipated this outcome.

After all, even he didn’t think he could beat Rei in a fight, so it wasn’t surprising that his subordinates—who were weaker than him—would make that judgment.

“So, what do we do? I’m pretty confident in my skills, so if I wanted to escape, I could make my way out from near the earthen wall… but there’s no point in me escaping alone.”

“That’s true. We also need to take the documents containing all the data and analysis from this experiment. Considering that, I suppose we’ll need a certain number of people after all.”

“I suppose so. Damn, that means we’ll have to figure something out. Isn’t there anyone around here who might be willing to help us?”

“...If this weren’t Gilm, there would still be various options available...”

It seems the fact that this is Gilm, Rei’s base of operations, is a major factor.

Given that, it’s going to be difficult to muster any fighting force.

“I know. But we still have to figure something out, don’t we? If you absolutely can’t come up with anything… you know what that means, right?”

His gaze toward his subordinate was filled with unmistakable murderous intent.

If no one here could provide the necessary personnel, that killing intent would undoubtedly be put into action.

Knowing this, the man hurriedly nodded.

“Y-yes. I’ll do my best to find some. However, if we’re going to push it this far, we’ll need some funds…”

“Ah, that’s not a problem. Use it however you like. Skimping on money here and ending up captured by the guards would be the worst possible outcome. Besides, if they’re the type who cares about money, the amount of the reward should directly motivate them. …Though, when dealing with Rei, we’ll need a sum that’s truly commensurate with the task. Anyway, I won’t say ‘tonight,’ but we’re getting out of Gilm as soon as possible.”

Fortunately, since a considerable amount of funds had been set aside for this operation, those conditions should be met.

With that thought in mind, the man took a swig of alcohol to suppress his frustration at how things had turned out.
