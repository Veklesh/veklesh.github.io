Time rewinds a little.

After seeing Rei and the others dash out of Azimov's house, Marina placed the unconscious Azimov onto water created by her spirit magic and headed straight for the clinic.

"The closest place... Hmm, Seraris' clinic should be fine."

Recalling a clinic run by someone she knew, Marina made her way down the road.

Right behind Marina, a mass of water that looked like a slime at first glance followed.

Unlike a slow-moving slime—though it had no legs—this water-formed slime-like substance moved by gliding across the ground.

The area around Azimov's house had little foot traffic, but it wasn't completely deserted.

Precisely because of that, the sight of a beauty like Marina leading a slime-like creature carrying an unconscious man on its back had been witnessed by a few people, however rare such sightings were.

......

But those who saw it all kept their mouths shut.

A beauty like Marina, the slime-like creature behind her, and the man being carried on it—seeing that made it obvious something was amiss.

Unfortunately, none of those who witnessed it knew that Marina was the former guildmaster.

Had they known, some might have approached her to offer thanks... or perhaps see it as a golden opportunity to get closer to Marina.

Regardless, Marina, accompanied by the slime-like creature, continued on her way, seemingly aware of the stares but not particularly bothered by them.

Marina was accustomed to attracting attention due to her beauty and the flashy party dresses she wore daily.

Even so, stepping out onto the main street in this situation meant she was certain to draw stares she couldn't ignore.

Considering that, it was fortunate that Seralis' clinic was located on a back street, sparing her from such intense scrutiny.

Arriving at her destination, Marina knocked on the door before her.

"Seraris, are you there?"

"Coming! Oh, is that Marina?"

With that voice, the door opened... and standing on the other side was a figure slightly shorter than Marina.

Not particularly handsome, but someone whose smile, when you looked at them, somehow felt reassuring.

"Sorry for dropping by so suddenly. Is this a good time?"

"Yeah, I wasn't busy right now, so it's fine... The problem is, that person over there? Or rather, isn't it Azimov?"

Seraris' gaze shifted to the slime-like creature created by the water spirit behind Marina... and then to Azimov sitting atop it.

Under normal circumstances, she might have been startled by the slime-like creature, but Seraris showed no particular surprise.

"That's right. He's an acquaintance of mine... or rather, an acquaintance of my party member. When I went to his house, he was injured and collapsed."

"Huh. Mr. Azimov, huh?"

Marina was slightly surprised that Seraris knew Azimov, but then again, Azimov was fairly well-known around here as a skilled alchemist... and as an eccentric.

Given that, it wasn't really surprising at all that Seralis knew Azimov.

Seraris quickly checked on Azimov, who was lying atop a slime-like creature. But when she pulled back the cloth covering his body, she frowned.

It was covered in a visible, copious amount of blood.

But as Seralis examined the wound, her frown deepened.

His clothes were slashed, and blood stained the fabric.

Despite that, there were absolutely no wounds on Azimov's body.

"Um, Marina. It looks like there aren't any wounds..."

"Yes. The wound itself was healed using a potion."

"...Then why did he come to our place? If the wound healed, there shouldn't be any problem, right?"

"That's the thing. ...Why do you think he got these wounds?"

At Marina's words, Seralis turned her gaze to Azimov.

Azimov was known for his skill, but that skill was strictly as an alchemist.

When it came to actual combat like an adventurer, he simply didn't possess the skill to be called top-tier.

Of course, being an alchemist, it wouldn't be strange for him to have various magical items for protection.

Even so, it was hard to imagine him willingly heading into a fight.

"Right. ...A fight? Well, your clothes are pretty badly slashed for that."

The wounds had already healed, so I couldn't say for certain, but even so, the slashes on my clothes made by some kind of blade didn't look like they came from a fight.

They clearly looked like wounds intended to take Azimov's life.

After seeing Seralis confirm this, Marina nodded in return.

"Right. I went to retrieve the magic item Rei had left with me, and this is what I found. Rei's potion managed to fix it, but still, since someone tried to kill him, I can't just leave it at that, can I?"

"...This is a place for treatment, not for hiding people."

Seraris replied with a hint of displeasure, but Marina, hearing this, offered a beautiful smile and spoke.

"Well now, the potion healed the wounds, but it can't bring back the blood that was spilled, can it? So until we figure out how to deal with that, I'd like to keep him here for now."

"Well..."

Faced with that, Seralis couldn't refuse.

She had chosen this work because she wanted to help the injured.

Told that even though the wound was healed, a lot of blood had been lost, she couldn't just leave them.

"...Alright. But I can't keep you here too long, you know? As you're aware, it's the busy season for adventurers now. Guildmaster Marina should understand that part, right?"

"Former. Former Guildmaster. I'm just a common adventurer now."

"Speaking of which, you did say you were quitting as Guildmaster. Honestly, you're such a peculiar one. How many people do you think actually want to be Guildmaster?"

"I live for love."

"...Yeah, yeah. I'll listen to your lovey-dovey talk another time. More importantly, what are you going to do now, Marina? Isn't that rumored guy with you?"

Though she said she'd hear about the lovey-dovey stuff later, Marina still smiled and opened her mouth when her friend asked about the rumored guy... Rei.

"As for Rei, he went after whoever attacked Azimov."

"Oh dear, dumped already?"

"Hehe, of course not."

Marina answered calmly, unfazed by the teasing.

She commanded Azimov into the building using spirit magic, then spoke again, her business done.

"I needed to move Azimov somewhere safe, and I also need to inform the guard squad about this incident. ...If I don't handle that part, it'll probably lead to all sorts of trouble."

Though Marina used the word "probably," considering Rei's personality, it was almost certain... no, absolutely certain that trouble would ensue.

Her acquaintance... her friend, Azimov, had been left mortally wounded, and the Sleipnir Boots had been stolen.

The possibility of Rei letting this slide was about as likely as spears falling from the sky.

...Though, Rei was skilled with javelins.

Nowadays, he had the Twilight Spear and wouldn't use other spears for throwing, but if he wanted to, creating a rain of spears wouldn't be difficult at all.

(a)

Marina must have realized this herself. She smiled, as if trying to cover something up.

"Anyway, it's fine if I handle Azimov, right?"

"Yeah, yeah. Got it. He doesn't need any special treatment, just rest, right? And once he wakes up, feed him something nutritious, that should be fine."

Expressing her gratitude that her friend had so readily agreed to take care of him, Marina left the scene.

For a moment, she considered that Azimov might need protection after the attack, but Seraris' clinic was full of adventurers.

If they were at the clinic, they were obviously injured.

But those injuries were rarely life-threatening.

Fractures, cuts, bruises... those were the most common injuries.

Some had been choked unconscious by tentacles, but such cases were extremely rare.

In any case, precisely because there were so many such individuals, their combat strength was by no means low.

Of course, they're somewhat inferior compared to being in peak condition, but with enough numbers, they can still fight effectively.

For that very reason, there was no particular need to worry about the clinic's defensive capabilities.

"Alright then, I'll leave it to you. I expect the guards will come for Azimov soon. When they arrive, hand him over."

"Yes, yes. ...Still, getting caught up in trouble the very day you resigned as Guildmaster? Talk about bad luck."

Though she said it in a half-teasing tone, Seraris' words still carried a hint of concern for Marina.

But Marina smiled and shook her head at her friend's words.

"No need to worry. I knew getting involved with Rei would mean trouble. It just happened a bit sooner than expected. Rei's the type to charge headfirst into trouble anyway, and he's got a knack for attracting it too."

"...Are you really sure you'll be okay? Well, I suppose you wouldn't have become a Rank B adventurer so quickly if you weren't."

Since Seralis also lived in Gilm, she naturally knew about Rei and had seen him before.

Her usual impression of Rei was just someone eating street food... but it was obvious that was only one facet of him.

"It's fine. I chose to form a party with Rei of my own volition. I anticipated this kind of thing from the start. ...Well, it was definitely a bit sooner than expected, though. Anyway, I should be going now."

With a beautiful smile, Marina exited the clinic.

She needed to head to the guard post next, so she couldn't afford to linger here too long.

"Right. Take care."

After exchanging a few brief words, Marina dissolved the water sphere she'd created with spirit magic and departed.

"...Really, be careful."

Seraris said briefly before returning to the clinic.

"Excuse me, may I have a moment?"

After leaving the clinic and stepping onto the main street, Marina called out to a guard who happened to be passing by.

The guard frowned momentarily at the voice... but upon realizing it was Marina who had spoken, he immediately broke into a surprised smile.

His initial frown was due to the annual spring influx of new adventurers arriving in Gilm, which invariably brought various problems.

Fights, or even duels, weren't uncommon... and he assumed this matter was related to that as well.

But looking at the situation now, the person standing there was Marina, a beauty rarely seen elsewhere.

Dealing with a rough-and-tumble adventurer was one thing, but when the opponent was Marina, the former guildmaster, it was a different story entirely.

"My, Marina. What's the matter?"

"I'm no longer Guild Master, so just call me normally. ...Anyway, there's been a bit of a problem regarding Rei."

"Ugh."

Hearing about Rei, the guard couldn't help but utter that from past experience.

...The incidents Rei had been involved in so far had, in many ways, become major affairs.

Considering that, it was only natural to wonder what kind of commotion was brewing next.

Seeing his partner honestly reveal his feelings, the other guard sighed.

Of course, he knew Rei's affairs would inevitably bring trouble, but he'd wanted to avoid acting like a partner.

"So, Marina. What did Rei get himself into this time?"

"Well... To put it simply, the alchemist who was holding Rei's magic items was attacked by someone. He's critically injured, and the magic items he was holding were stolen."

"Huh?"

The two guards gasped in unison at Marina's words.

It was only natural. Even hearing that Rei had caused trouble, they hadn't imagined it would be this serious.

To be precise, Marina had said trouble had arisen concerning Rei, not that Rei had caused trouble... but seeing these two, it was clear how Rei was generally perceived.

"So, would you mind giving me a moment?"

"...Yes. It seems we shouldn't discuss this here. We should go to the office and hear the full story properly."

"Yes, that would be helpful."

Marina smiled at the guard's words and nodded.
