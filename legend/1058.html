"...Well then. I suppose stripping the materials can wait."

Picking up the severed head of Del-Troy, cut off by the Death Scythe, and seeing his legs still attached to the branches, he swung the Death Scythe once more.

He severed the branch and stored it directly into the Misty Ring.

"If it were as large as an Aerowing, it might have been able to escape the Bubble Breath's effect."

Set's Bubble Breath possessed the property of transforming into a viscous liquid.

But even so, it was still only a Level 1 skill, lacking truly overwhelming adhesive power.

With a bit of flying strength, weight, or muscle, escaping the sticky liquid wouldn't have been difficult.

But... Del Troy, being an owl-type monster, specialized in stealth.

That's precisely why it couldn't escape from the branch it was stuck to.

"Bad matchup. ...No, facing Set, who possesses abilities to handle all sorts of enemies, meant defeat was inevitable from the start."

Glancing at the tree where Del Troy had been perched, Rei moved away from the spot.

It took longer than expected, but finding the Dark Elf children was the priority now.

(Going straight back would be faster, but...)

But Rei wasn't about to trudge through the sticky mess left by the Bubble Breath.

He could push through if he tried, but now that he'd already defeated Del Troy, there was no need to go that far.

His goal was to save the Dark Elf children, and that did require haste. But if he forced himself to return straight to Set's location now, dealing with the liquid covering his entire body would take time, ultimately only delaying the search for the children.

Retracing his steps, he pushed through the overgrown branches. Suddenly, the thick scent of blood wafted through the air.

(What is it?)

Though puzzled, I didn't suspect it was Set's blood and kept moving, eventually arriving at the spot where Set had been.

There, five orcs lay sprawled on the ground.

Some had their necks twisted unnaturally, others were missing parts of their torsos, and one had lost its head entirely.

Naturally, all five orcs were dead, and there was no need to wonder who had done it.

"Set, well done."

"GRRRRRRRR!"

At Rei's voice, Set growled happily.

Blood from the orcs stained his front paws and beak, and broken swords and spears lay scattered on the ground alongside the corpses. Yet, seeing Set's joyful expression now, no one would recall that gruesome scene.

"The spear... well, that's definitely unusable. Still, they attacked Set? ...Seems the abnormality is still going strong."

At first, Rei had thought Set might have spotted the orcs and launched a preemptive strike, but Set's position hadn't changed much from where he'd unleashed the bubble breath earlier.

If that was the case, it wasn't Set who attacked the orc, but the orc who attacked Set.

(Well, the outcome probably wouldn't have changed once the orc showed itself anyway.)

Like Rei, Set also had a taste for orc meat.

Despite their low rank, orcs are an exception—their meat is exceptionally tasty.

(I've had Iberian pork, black pork, Agu pork a few times, but even compared to those, the taste is superior. Well, maybe the brand pork I ate was just the kind sold in supermarkets, so truly delicious brand pork might be tastier than orc meat.)

Recalling the taste of pork he'd eaten back in Japan, Rei succumbed to the pressure of Set's round, pleading eyes and stored the five orc corpses in his Misty Ring.

"Set, if you're going to attack, try to avoid the torso. It'll reduce the amount of pork belly significantly... Plus, dealing with the innards is a pain."

"Grrr..."

Set purred apologetically, showing remorse.

Gently stroking Set's head, Rei continued encouragingly.

"Well, a little bit is unavoidable. Besides, the orcs were fighting back desperately too. Thinking about it that way, it might be hard to cleanly finish them all with one strike... huh!"

"Gya-gyu-gyo!"

Using his right hand instead of the one stroking Set, Rei activated the Nebula's Eye at his waist. He conjured an arrowhead in his palm and, using the snap of his wrist, hurled it toward the bushes a short distance away.

The goblin that had been waiting for an opening to attack Rei and Set let out a scream that echoed through the area.

"Dammit! Were you targeting me? Set? Or trying to steal the orc's corpse...? I don't know, but there are just too many of you!"

Shouting in irritation, he drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and charged into the thicket.

Following Rei's lead, Set also charged into the bushes... and it took less than a minute for the nearly ten goblins there to be reduced to corpses.

"...I heard there were a lot of goblins, but damn, there really are a lot. How many have we taken down now?"

"Grrr?"

Seeing Set tilt his head, Rei decided it was pointless to bother counting the goblins. He wiped the goblin blood from the blade of the Death Scythe and spoke.

"Anyway, we can't just stay here forever... Let's go back to searching for the child."

"Guru!"

Rei growled in agreement with Rei's words, and Rei climbed onto his back.

Leaving the goblin corpses behind as before, Set kicked off the ground and charged deeper into the forest.

They encountered several more goblins moving about afterward, but rather than bothering to stop and kill them, Set used Wind Arrows and Ice Arrows to strike first. As they passed by, he finished them off one after another with swipes from his front legs, pecks from his beak, and strikes from Rei's Death Scythe on his back.

They ran through the forest, slaying goblins, for several tens of minutes... Yet they still found no sign of the missing dark elf children, nor did they hear the whistle sound others were supposed to blow if they found them.

(This is strange. With so many of us searching the forest, it wouldn't be odd for the children to know they're being looked for. So why aren't they coming out on their own? ...Are they still alive?)

The worst possible scenario flashed through Rei's mind.

Being children, they probably wouldn't be used as breeding stock.

But... conversely, that meant they weren't even fit to be used as seedbeds. The natural destination for such beings was the stomachs of goblins, kobolds, orcs, and the like.

(They say lamb is more popular than mutton, after all...)

Having enjoyed lamb at Japanese yakiniku restaurants back home, Rei understood that younger meat was tastier.

It's not uncommon to see dishes using lamb or veal in cooking manga or on TV either.

(Though that's a bit tasteless, no matter how you look at it.)

Shaking his head to clear his thoughts, he carefully scanned his surroundings, searching for the dark elf children...

"Grrr!"

"Whoa!"

Set, who had been running, abruptly stopped in his tracks.

His limbs braced against the ground, leaving four distinct tracks in the dirt.

Since Set stopped abruptly without warning, Rei, who was riding on his back, naturally lost her balance too. Still, she managed to regain it.

"What's wrong?"

What came out of Rei's mouth wasn't a complaint directed at Set...

She knew Set wouldn't do something like this without meaning.

He had done this out of the blue, so there was undoubtedly some reason behind it.

With that thought, Rei asked. Set shifted his gaze from the path ahead to a patch of bushes slightly off to the side.

"Grrr..."

Set growled softly, his tone somehow gentle.

Watching him, Rei wondered, Could it be...?

"Is... someone there?"

The moment those words left Rei's mouth, the bushes a short distance away stirred faintly.

Turning his gaze toward the swaying bushes, Rei spoke slowly, careful not to startle whoever might be there.

His suspicion was strong.

But even so... if the children he was searching for were truly here, he couldn't afford to overlook them.

Facing the hesitantly swaying bushes, Rei opened his mouth again, trying to reassure them.

"I'm someone sent by the Dark Elf settlement to find the children who went missing. If you're from the Dark Elf settlement too, you know about the World Tree, right? I came here to treat it."

Talking like this, it would be utterly ridiculous if it turned out they were just unrelated wild animals or monsters.

Rei thought this in his mind, but still... he called out toward the bushes and waited for a response.

About a minute passed like that, then suddenly the bushes began shaking violently.

Several lights peered out at him from within the thicket.

Realizing those must be the eyes of Dark Elves, Rei spoke again.

"Hey, haven't you seen this guy before? It's Set. He stood out, so you must have seen him around the settlement, right?"

"Grrrrr."

Set growled softly, trying not to alarm the others. Soon, the bushes rustled again, and three children burst out.

"Please, save Lugdos... Save Lugdos!"

"Please, sis! Please save Lugdos!"

"Big sis! Big sis!"

Their hair colors varied—silver, black, blue—but their brown skin and pointed ears clearly marked them as dark elf children.

"Calm down. Just calm down. ...And just so you know, I'm not your big sister. I'm your big brother. Don't get that wrong, okay?"

He understood he had feminine features, but being called a girl like this was never pleasant for a man.

At Rei's words, the three dark elf children each nodded in response.

Seeing they had calmed down for the moment, Rei spoke again.

"So, you want me to help Lugdos? I came looking for you guys, including him... Did you get hurt or something?"

"Uh, yeah. He shielded us from the goblins..."

"Thanks to Lugdos, we were safe, but..."

"Big Sis? Big Bro?"

Seeing the children speaking one after another, Rei frowned slightly.

(What's with them? Especially this third one. Looks like he's not even five yet. Well, he is a Dark Elf, so his age probably isn't what he looks like.)

Watching the three desperately clinging to him, Rei sighed.

"Alright. Luckily, I have potions, so I can heal minor injuries. So? This guy named Lugdos... seems to be over there in the bushes."

Sniffing the faint scent of blood drifting over, he could roughly gauge how serious the wound might be.

Yet, despite detecting the blood scent, Rei wore a puzzled expression.

The drifting scent of blood was truly faint.

It was impossible to believe the bleeding was severe enough to immobilize someone.

(So, is he just unconscious? ...Well, that probably meant there was nothing these kids could do about it anyway.)

They were all children, appearing to be under ten years old. There was no way this small group could have managed against their leader, Lugdos... especially in the night forest teeming with monsters.

(No, was it Lugdos? Just the fact they didn't abandon that child and run away is impressive. Well, if they had, they'd probably all be dead by now.)

They survived because they all huddled together and hid in one spot.

Thinking this, Rei stroked each of their heads in turn.

"You're all right. ...Just to confirm, only the three of you and Lugdos entered the forest, right?"

The three children nodded at Rei's question.

Relieved by their gesture, Rei urged them on.

"Now then, take me to that Lugdos fellow. We need to get back to the settlement soon."

"Okay!"

Guided by the children who had just spoken, Rei started toward the thicket... but paused before stepping forward, turning to face Set.

"Set, you stay here and guard the perimeter. Keep any monsters away."

"Gururu!"

"Leave it to me!" Set growled, his throat rumbling. Leaving him behind, Rei headed into the thicket with the three children.

"Big bro, you okay?"

Running his fingers through the child's hair as he asked in a lisp, Rei shifted his gaze to the Rugdos hidden within the thicket.

Just as Rei had thought, the wound itself wasn't too severe.

Blood flowed from its forehead, but that was all.

But...

(The forehead... meaning he hit his head. That's a bit dangerous. If it were just a pure injury, a potion could heal it... but this area is better left to someone with more specialized knowledge. The amount of bleeding is also quite minimal.)

He stopped his hand as he reached for the potion and spoke to the three of them.

"If I treat this on my own now, it might cause problems later. For now, it's better to return to the settlement as is. ...Understood?"

At Rei's words, the three children nodded obediently. ...Though the youngest seemed to nod simply because the other two had nodded first.
