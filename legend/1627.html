"Hmm... Well, well. How did things escalate to such a commotion in such a short time?"

As the figure of Daskar vanished from the other side of the Paired Orb, Ronitas, who led the envoy, murmured thoughtfully.

Indeed, fatigue was deeply etched on Ronitas' face.

The other members of the delegation, too, had been told what Rei and his group had done, what they were aiming for, and what the Kingdom of Reburina was thinking—and how the delegation should act. Given all that background, it was far from something they could easily say, "Sure, no problem."

Even as this exchange took place, Daskar had already started another task, claiming he still had work to do. Ronitas couldn't help but think how incredibly demanding the duties of a lord must be.

There are those in the world driven by an intense ambition for advancement, many who yearn for higher status.

Especially the position of Lord of Gilm—to those who know nothing, to those watching from the sidelines, it must seem like a source of all manner of benefits, starting with rare materials.

But for Ronitas, who often witnessed firsthand just how busy Daskar was, the idea of becoming the lord of Gilm himself was utterly unappealing.

If one tried to exploit everything without doing any real work... well, in a frontier town like Gilm, that would quickly become unsustainable.

A town where the rewards are great, but so are the dangers. That was Gilm.

"Well, even so. It just sort of happened that way."

"Now that Gilm has expanded, this recent incident is actually something I'd welcome... but still, transporting nearly a thousand people sounds like a huge undertaking."

Ronitas wasn't involved in the women's relocation, but even imagining it made the difficulty obvious.

Moving nearly a thousand people? Just preparing meals for them would be a huge undertaking.

Though Rei received a look that seemed tinged with pity, he himself didn't feel it was that difficult anymore.

"It was tough at first, sure. But now that everyone's traveling by carriage, it's not that bad."

"Still, moving a thousand people sounds like a lot of work. Well, never mind that. So, the delegation... Is Gilm's intention to decline the proposal from the Kingdom of Reburina?"

The weariness in Ronitas's voice made it clear to anyone that he found it bothersome.

Still, if it's for Gilm's benefit... it was typical of Ronitas not to cut corners halfway through the job just because it was troublesome.

"Frankly, the fact that the upper echelons of the Kingdom of Reburina would try to pin their own misdeeds on Jaya makes them untrustworthy."

One of the envoys spoke, as if trying to placate Ronitas.

The others seemed to agree, nodding in unison.

However, this was only because it served Gilm's interests better.

Given their position as envoys, if making Jaya take responsibility for this incident served Gilm's interests, then Gilm's delegation would have had to choose that path, regardless of their true feelings.

Moreover, considering the impending relations with those soon to arrive from the Kingdom of Mireana, they couldn't simply act as they pleased.

"Anyway, we'll keep a close eye on Reburina over here, so don't worry about it."

"...I'm counting on you."

Ultimately, all Rei could do at this point was transport the women to Gilm.

Though, of course, that was a highly troublesome act for both Jaya and the Kingdom of Reburina.

"Also, was it Ryutas? Once that man joins you, it's fine for us to take him in here, right?"

"Do that. Ryutas himself is somewhat capable, and his guards should be useful enough not to be a hindrance. ...Still, they can't quite compare to Milein and the others."

"No, comparing them is the mistake here."

Elena muttered in exasperation at Rei's words.

It was completely unimaginable from the way she doted on Set, but Milein and the others were famous adventurers even among the younger generation.

Personality aside, Milein is well-proportioned and undeniably beautiful.

Perhaps because Ekril is slightly younger than Milein, describing her as cute rather than beautiful seems more fitting, though her features are undeniably well-proportioned.

In that sense, Sulunin is a middle-aged man, and while his appearance alone wouldn't cause a stir... his reputation as a skilled mage is certainly enough to make him noteworthy.

While Gilm hosts numerous adventurer parties, those with mages among them are few and far between.

"To be honest, I'm assigning Ryutas and the guards to the envoy group, so I'd like the envoy group to send a few guards over to us."

Ronitas frowned, deep in thought at Rei's words.

Thanks to the information exchange, Ronitas understood what Rei was worried about.

The adventurer guards arranged for the Zolgea Trading Company through Gimekara were chosen on the premise that they wouldn't cause any awkward problems, especially since most of the group were women.

In other words, they were chosen not for their skill but for their lack of personality issues.

Moreover, these adventurers were from the Kingdom of Reburina, whose adventurers were far inferior in quality to those of Gilm.

Considering that, it was undeniable that there was dissatisfaction with the defensive capabilities.

Especially since Rei and the others couldn't stay with that group forever, they urgently needed to secure combat strength for any eventuality.

Given that they managed to join forces with this envoy group, it would be unreasonable to expect Rei not to place hopes on them.

But even if such expectations were placed upon them, Ronitas's position made it impossible for him to simply say yes.

They were heading to the Kingdom of Reburina... a country whose upper echelons had made the mistake of allying with the criminal organization known as Jaya, and had even permitted the creation of beings like giants. Honestly, Ronitas probably wished, if possible, that Rei and his group would join their own escort.

But it's also undeniable that escorting nearly a thousand people all the way to Gilm requires skilled guards.

For now, in the Kingdom of Reburina, such extreme caution isn't necessary.

But the closer we get to Gilm, the greater the chance of encountering powerful monsters or larger bandit groups than those found around here.

In such a situation, what good would guards be whose skills were lacking, even if their personalities were fine...? It was only natural that the only image that came to mind was carnivorous beasts tearing apart a flock of sheep.

"One or two... I suppose I could manage..."

Ultimately, considering what lay ahead in Gilm, that was the extent of Ronitas' compromise.

No, rather, had he conceded that much? The rest of the envoy group, aside from Ronitas, were even impressed.

...The fact that they didn't express anxiety about reducing their own guards suggested they'd chosen suitable candidates.

"Is that okay?"

"You're the one who requested it, so asking if it's okay is a bit rich, honestly... Still, even if we do transfer guards to you, the Scorching Wind members are out of the question."

"Whaaat?!"

The first to react to Ronitas' words wasn't Rei and the others... but Milein, who had been playing with Set a little ways off.

Even while playing with Set, she must have been listening intently to Rei and the others.

That's precisely why she might have... just maybe, she might have hoped that they could actually act together with Rei—or more accurately, with Set.

But realistically, sending the skilled Scorching Wind guards—or even Milein alone—to accompany Rei and the others was an impossible choice for Ronitas.

"Milein aside..."

After glancing at Milein, who looked dissatisfied, Rei voiced his request to Ronitas.

"Instead of an adventurer, how about a knight?"

"That's impossible."

The one who immediately responded to Rei's words wasn't Ronitas... but the knight mentioned in the conversation.

In his thirties, the knight was in the prime of his working life. Rei had seen him several times before and even spoken with him.

The knight himself would have liked to help Rei if possible, but while he might have done so as an individual, he was here now in his capacity as a knight.

Given that, he had to prioritize his official duties.

"To begin with, there are few knights accompanying us this time. ...Because things are busy in Gilm. Given that, we must prioritize protecting Lord Ronitas above all else. Besides... it's also true that it's precisely because knights are present that we can demonstrate the envoy, including Lord Ronitas, are from Gilm."

That was precisely why he couldn't help.

Hearing that, Rei had no choice but to accept it.

If he tried to forcefully take a knight along now, he'd definitely get scolded by Daskar later, regardless of the immediate consequences.

Of course, with Marina around, there were plenty of ways to handle that level of anger... but even so, it was only natural to want to avoid unnecessary scolding whenever possible.

(So, I guess I'll need to arrange escorts in towns or villages along the way. Adventurers from the Kingdom of Reburina aside, once we enter the Kingdom of Mireana, even in the countryside, there should be some reasonably skilled adventurers around.)

After gathering his thoughts, Rei nodded back at the knight.

"Understood. I would have preferred you came with us, but I suppose I can't insist."

"Hmm. We too would have preferred to protect those without means of defense."

The knight stated, sounding slightly regretful.

In truth, this knight had become a knight precisely to protect those unable to fight for themselves. Given that, if asked who felt the most frustration in the current situation, it was undoubtedly this knight.

Perhaps sensing something in the knight's feelings, Rei didn't press further on the topic and instead brought up something else.

"Weren't there any villages or towns around here with some tasty food?"

"Well, there were some... but why bring that up all of a sudden?"

The knight looked at Rei with a slightly exasperated expression, but perhaps relieved that Rei had changed the subject, he proceeded to describe the dishes they had eaten in the villages and towns they had passed through on their way here.

The members of the envoy, starting with Ronitas, seemed to understand why Rei had changed the subject like this.

Surprised by Rei's unexpected consideration, they offered friendly smiles.

"Huh, a dish where you steam-roast meat on the ground? ...Sounds tasty, but it seems like it would take a while."

"Yeah. Several hours... apparently it's not uncommon for it to take over ten hours in some cases."

A cooking method where seasoned meat is wrapped in large leaves used for flavoring, buried in the ground, and steamed.

Rei knew about it.

He'd read about it in a manga back in Japan.

(Was it Chinese Beggar's Chicken? I think that was the name... Humans really do come up with similar ideas, huh?)

Since the seasonings and other ingredients besides the meat were different, it probably wasn't exactly the same dish Rei had imagined.

But since it was a similar dish, it was only natural he found it intriguing. However...

"If I had the time, I'd love to try it."

"Yeah. In our case, we happened to pass through the village just as the dish was being finished, so we could buy it right away... But if you're unlucky, you might arrive right after they've started cooking it, or something like that."

"That part is just pure luck."

For Rei and the others, rushing along the road to Gilm, stopping at a village was only for resupplying supplies.

Given that, staying in one village for ten hours just for a single meal was absolutely out of the question. An hour or two might be possible, but ten hours? No way.

Honestly, it would be far easier to have someone who knew how to cook that dish accompany Rei and prepare it for dinner... or rather, considering the cooking time, it might be breakfast.

After that, Rei continued gathering information from the knight about the villages and towns they passed through—particularly regarding safety and the atmosphere of each place.

Beside Rei and the knight, Marina similarly gathered information from Ronitas about what they'd need for the journey ahead.

Marina, having been a guildmaster, was well-versed in various matters, but information was constantly changing.

Elena and the other guards kept watch over their surroundings... while Set was being doted on by Milein.
