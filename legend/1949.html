“Bodies reduced to nothing but skin and bones… Are there really that many?”

At the guard’s words, Rei asked with a look of distaste.

It’s not that he has a strong aversion to corpses per se, but even so, hearing that there are large numbers of bodies that met such an unusual end isn’t exactly pleasant.

“That’s right. …Normally, corpses that have starved to death look like that…”

Even as he said this, the guard didn’t seem to believe for a moment that the corpses he’d seen were simply victims of starvation.

To begin with, it’s strange to hire such skilled guards just to protect corpses that died of starvation.

At the very least, the two men Rei had fought were clearly more skilled than the guards, and whoever had killed the man they’d captured in the study and pinned him to the wall was undoubtedly more skilled than the guards as well.

It felt distinctly off that such skilled fighters—relative to the guards, at least—had been hired to guard a corpse that was nothing but skin and bones.

“Besides, normally, if a corpse had starved to death, it wouldn’t be strange to find traces of it having tried to eat the person it was with. But that corpse… it was clean.”

He muttered with an indescribable, somewhat ambiguous expression, as if recalling the state of the corpse he had seen.

“Anyway, I found a place like that. I came here because I thought I should hurry and let everyone know.”

At those words, the faint trace of suspicion I still harbored toward the man in front of me melted away.

He had come here precisely because they’d found a corpse reduced to nothing but bones and skin and couldn’t figure out what to do on their own.

(That said, there’s a high chance there’s someone among the guards who’s in cahoots with them. If that’s the case, I can’t exactly trust everyone completely.)

Since there was a high probability that information from the guards was leaking to the other side, he couldn’t simply dismiss that possibility entirely.

“I get the reason, but what are we going to do now?”

As he said this, Rei turned his gaze toward the guard examining the switch on the wall.

Rei had originally brought the guards back to this study specifically to have them check the switch on the wall.

That’s exactly why he’d hesitated about whether it was okay to go over to the body right now…

“There probably aren’t any traps. …Of course, I can’t say for sure.”

Just then, the guard who had been examining the wall switch spoke up.

“So, should we press the switch or go check out the body… what do you say?”

After a moment’s thought, Rei responded to the guard’s question.

“Why don’t we try pressing the switch for now? It might lead to a secret passage, or it could be the key to unlock a storage area.”

Hearing Rei’s words, the guards—especially the one who had been checking the switch on the wall—wrenched a look of sheer distaste across their faces.

He had checked it himself and was fairly certain there weren’t any traps, but that was only based on his own inspection.

Since this wasn’t his main job and his skills were only on the side, he couldn’t say with absolute confidence that it was safe.

Thinking that... but he immediately dismissed the thought.

Even if he didn’t press the switch now, there was no doubt he’d end up pressing it eventually.

And since he’s the one most familiar with traps among everyone who’s come to this mansion, he reasons.

“Alright, let’s press it.”

Saying that, the male guard cautiously looks around at everyone present.

After confirming that no one objected to pressing the switch, he gently reached out for it.

Everyone, including Rei, prepared to respond immediately to whatever might happen... and in the next instant, with a click, the switch sank into the wall.

But nothing seemed to happen.

He had expected something to happen the moment he pressed the switch, but after a few seconds, he looked around in disappointment...

“Hey, nothing happened after all…”

Just as one of the guards said that, a section of the study’s wall—specifically, the spot where the painting of the sea had been hanging—collapsed backward.

“Oh!”

Coincidentally, everyone who witnessed the scene—including Rei—let out a surprised gasp, caught completely off guard.

There was a slight delay between pressing the switch and the mechanism activating, and the fact that Rei and the others were in a hurry was likely why they all gasped in surprise.

“I never would’ve guessed that painting was a trap. …So, what’s inside?”

Muttering to himself, Rei turned his gaze toward the space created by the falling painting.

It wasn’t a hidden door, passageway, staircase, or room… but rather a hidden shelf.

Inside the hidden shelf were several tools, including some documents.

“Do you think this is a lead?”

“I’m not sure. We won’t know until we examine it thoroughly, but I think it’ll be a clue of some sort. For now, Rei, let’s put this in your item box and head over to where the body was.”

Rei had no objection to that, so for now, he stored everything from the shelf into his Misty Ring.

There were a few items that looked like magic items, which piqued Rei’s interest, but for now, they had to prioritize examining the body.

(I’m not sure if it was lucky or unlucky that it wasn’t a secret door or something like that.)

If there had been a hidden door here, it would undoubtedly have taken a significant amount of time to investigate the area beyond it.

If that had been the case, the guard they’d left behind with the body would undoubtedly have been in grave danger.

After all, it was certain that at least one other person was still guarding the mansion.

“Well, let’s go. …Still, it’s a wonder you weren’t attacked. I can’t help but think you were really lucky.”

The guard who had checked the wall switch said admiringly to the guard who had come to report the skeleton-like corpse.

The guard who was spoken to merely offered a wry smile.

Since they hadn’t been attacked themselves, he’d probably thought they’d be fine, but he must have realized he’d been lacking a sense of danger.

Of course, seeing the large number of corpses reduced to nothing but skin and bones likely played a big part in his realization that this would be a major clue regarding the current incident.

“Ah… Rei. Sorry about this, but could you please put the bodies in the hallway in the item box too? I really didn’t want to do this, but leaving them here would be a bad idea for various reasons.”

The guard said this with a slightly sheepish look, but Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered. He stepped out of the room, stowed the corpse in his Misty Ring… and then, guided by the guard who had come to fetch them, headed toward the location of the other corpses.

“Whoa…”

The moment he saw the mountain of corpses, one of the guards muttered, seemingly unable to help himself.

He’d heard there were a large number of corpses reduced to nothing but skin and bones. He’d heard it… but even so, to be honest, he probably hadn’t expected it to be this bad.

Rei, too, could do nothing but stare in astonishment at the scene before him.

The way the corpses, reduced to skin and bones, were haphazardly piled up gave the impression that they were simply being bundled up like unwanted luggage to keep them out of the way...

No, for the person who actually created this scene, that was undoubtedly how it felt.

“This is really something…”

The guard who had come with Rei could only mutter in astonishment at the scene spread out before them.

In fact, with the number of corpses—dozens, perhaps even close to a hundred—both Rei and the guard could do nothing but stare in shock.

“You can see why we decided this was beyond our capabilities, right?”

The guard who had come to summon Rei and the others to the study spoke up.

When he first heard the report, he hadn’t been entirely convinced it wasn’t an exaggeration, but seeing this scene, he understood why they’d taken that approach.

“It’s a little strange that there’s no stench at all, even with this many bodies. …Though maybe that’s because they’re just skin and bones by now.”

“What do you think happened to their internal organs and such?”

“Well, I guess they’re probably gone.”

It’s not that every stench wafting from the corpses comes from rotting internal organs… but it’s true that they make up a large part of it.

However, the corpses piled haphazardly before us were nothing but skin and bones, and it was simply impossible to imagine that there were any internal organs left inside those torsos.

That’s probably why, even with this many corpses, there’s absolutely no stench or foul odor.

...Of course, the rest of the bodies are also completely dried out, reduced to nothing but skin and bones as if they’d starved to death, so that’s probably why there’s no smell.

“Still, these bodies are clearly odd. Logically speaking, it should take a considerable amount of time for them to become this completely desiccated. With this many people gone, it wouldn’t be strange if there were some kind of commotion. …If I had to guess…”

“The Red Cloth, I suppose.”

As if picking up on the guard’s words, Rei said this.

He had heard that Akabu had taken several people away, so it wasn’t hard to arrive at that conclusion.

But at the same time, a question remained. What could have driven them to turn Akabu into a corpse like this... especially since it hadn’t even been six months since Akabu began his activities.

And yet, to end up like this—a corpse that looked practically like a mummy—was clearly strange.

“Was some kind of magic item, spell, or skill used—something that accelerates the passage of time, or something like that?”

“...All of them? Why on earth would anyone need to do something like that?”

The guards are discussing it among themselves, but even Rei doesn’t know why they actually did something like this.

If you ask whether someone would do this just to kill time or for entertainment…

(Maybe someone who takes pleasure in killing people in a specific way…? I can’t say for sure that such a person doesn’t exist, but looking at the state of the bodies, something feels off.)

Deciding to examine the bodies more closely, Rei called out to the guards still present at the scene.

“I’d like to examine the body a bit. Would that be okay?”

“Uh… if possible, I’d prefer you didn’t touch the body. As you can see, it’s just skin and bones, and it’s completely dried out. If you touch it carelessly, the body might just crumble.”

The guard replied while looking at the pile of corpses, but Rei’s words left him with a question.

“Wait a minute. Then how did you manage to stack these bodies up?”

Indeed, if the bodies might crumble at the slightest touch, it was only natural to wonder how such fragile corpses had been transported here and stacked up like this.

Perhaps sensing Rei’s confusion, the guard turned his gaze toward the haphazardly piled corpses and spoke.

“I get what you’re saying, Rei, but I don’t know how these bodies ended up like this. After all, we can’t rule out the possibility that they were just ordinary corpses until they were piled up here, and only became like this after being stacked, right?”

“If anything, shouldn’t Rei be the one who knows more about those symptoms… or rather, in this case, phenomena? You’re the expert on that sort of thing, aren’t you?”

From the guard’s perspective, Rei was a renowned adventurer who traveled to various places and took on all sorts of requests.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange for the guards to assume that he might naturally know something they don’t.

But Rei shook his head at the guard’s words.

“Sorry, but I can’t really think of anything. …Well, I guess if you use magic skillfully, you might be able to make it work.”

For example, Rei is a mage who specializes in fire magic.

If he uses fire magic skillfully, he might be able to do things like rapidly dry out a corpse.

But that’s only because it’s Rei; if you were to ask whether just any mage could do it… it’s not impossible, but the question is whether they’d go to all that trouble to do something like that.

“I see. In that case, aside from magic… do you think there might be a magic item that could do something like that?”

“If you ask me if they exist, I don’t think it would be strange if they did. But why would anyone go to the trouble of doing something like that?”

“To prevent the stench of decay from spreading to the surrounding area. If the stench drifts out, people might find out there are a large number of corpses in this mansion.”

There was some merit to that argument, but considering the size of the mansion and the location of this room, it was hard to imagine any stench of decay escaping outside.

At the same time, Rei wondered if the reason wasn’t so much to prevent the smell from reaching the outside of the mansion, but rather to keep the stench from spreading within the mansion itself.
