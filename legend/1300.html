About ten days had passed since his date with Vihera... and Rei had come to Marina's house.

The only other person present was Set.

In that sense, it wasn't quite the same as being alone together like on her date with Vihera, but Marina was still perfectly happy.

Moreover, beyond the time alone with the man she loved, she could even witness the sight of Set absorbing magic stones.

As a woman, as an adventurer, and as an elemental mage, she sensed this would be incredibly fulfilling.

"Yes, Rei, tea is fine, right?"

Marina's garden, prepared by spirit magic just like during the previous party.

Once again, Marina placed the tea on the table that had been brought out just as before.

"Ah, I'm not good with alcohol. ...Yeah, it's delicious."

After taking a sip of tea, Rei murmured thoughtfully.

Purely in terms of taste, it probably couldn't match the tea brewed by Ara.

But the tea Marina brewed with all her heart tasted perfectly delicious to Rei.

"Really? Hehe, I'm glad to hear it. ...Set seems to be enjoying himself too, so inviting you and Set was definitely the right choice."

Rei murmured with a delighted, happy smile, her gaze shifting to Set running around the garden.

The garden, its environment adjusted by spirit magic, was already free of snow.

No, actually, it had been getting quite warm these past few days, and much of the snow had melted even in the city.

Moreover, today's weather felt just as promising for spring's arrival as when she had gone out with Vihera.

With such temperatures, most of the remaining snow had already disappeared... but in Marina's garden, the snow was gone, and green grass was already present.

Set was now happily running around on that green lawn.

Though Set had no problem lying down on the snow, it wasn't that he disliked green.

In fact, he preferred plants and trees over snow.

That was precisely why Set was running around on the lawn in Marina's garden like this.

Watching Set's innocent antics, Rei glanced at Marina sitting across from her while sipping her tea.

She was dressed in a party dress with a more modest cut than usual, perhaps to suit this tea party.

Even so, her shoulders were still exposed, making it easy to imagine just how flashy Marina's usual party dresses were.

To Marina, who sat there, Rei placed her teacup on the table and spoke.

"More importantly, is this really okay?"

"Oh? What do you mean?"

"Well, there's still quite a bit of snow outside, yet only this garden looks like this. Even if it's spirit magic, the effect doesn't last forever, does it?"

"Oh, that? Well... ordinary spirit magic can't do it, but... there's a trick to everything, you know?"

Though Rei knew almost nothing about spirit magic, seeing Marina's confident expression made him realize it would be rude to ask any more.

"I see. Well, I won't pry further then... Oh, speaking of which, what happened with Walker? I heard you'd almost finished the handover."

"Huh? Oh, that? As Guildmaster, I've pretty much done everything I needed to do. ...I even stopped by Daskar to introduce you."

Daskar Larks was the lord of Gilm and a central figure in the Neutral Faction, but for Marina, he was strongly associated with the man who had proposed to her when she was young.

While she maintains a proper demeanor in official settings, now it's just her and Rei... a completely private moment.

That's precisely why she could casually mention his name like this.

Though she didn't know the details of their relationship, she could tell they were close. Rei accepted it as natural, given her position as Guildmaster.

In a frontier town like Gilm, if two high-ranking figures like the lord and the guildmaster were to start a power struggle, the damage caused by it would be immense.

Precisely because it was a frontier, a misstep could prove fatal for Gilm.

Considering that, the good rapport between Marina and Daskar was something to be welcomed.

"Do you think Walker will get along well with Lord Daskar?"

"Hmm..."

Marina fell silent as if pondering Rei's question, sipping her tea before speaking.

"Probably fine, I suppose? In fact, Daskar might find Walker easier to deal with than me."

Compared to Marina, who knew all sorts of embarrassing childhood incidents about him, Walker, starting fresh from scratch, would be easier for Daskar to deal with.

Marina said this, but a smile she couldn't quite hide played at the corners of her mouth.

Walker didn't know Daskar's childhood like Marina did. But... even so, Walker was the person Marina had chosen as her successor.

If Daskar underestimated such a person... the damage he would suffer as a lord would be immense.

As long as he remained Guildmaster of Gilm, he wouldn't inflict fatal damage on the guild. Still, he had plenty of cards up his sleeve.

"Hmm. Well, I don't know Walker that well, but if Marina says so, I suppose I can trust him."

Rei had only met Walker a handful of times.

The incident with Elena, the Silver Lion incident.

Just those two times.

But even so, he believed Marina's judgment of people was spot on, so not trusting Walker wasn't an option.

After that, Rei and Marina spent time together, talking about all sorts of things. Sometimes they laughed, sometimes they blushed, sometimes they got angry... Happy times passed like that.

Set ran around the lawn-covered garden, spending his time being petted by Rei and Marina.

And then, some time passed...

"Hey, Rei. Could you show me how you absorb magic stones soon?"

Marina suddenly said to Rei.

Hearing this, Rei recalled that spending the day with Marina like this also meant showing her Set learning a new skill.

She had been unsure whether to have either Set or Death Scythe absorb the Snow Salamander's magic stone.

But now that Marina had asked, he reconsidered and decided it would be better to have Set absorb it.

Death Scythe's skills were certainly useful, but Rei had other combat methods like the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and the Twin Spear Style, not to mention various other combat magic items.

Therefore, it was certain that Set, who often acted alone, should definitely become stronger.

He scanned the surroundings, confirming no one was searching for them, before speaking.

"Alright then, if Marina says so, I'll do it. ...Hey, Set!"

"Grrr?"

Set, who had been running around the lawn, stopped at Rei's voice and turned around.

Realizing it was Rei calling him, he happily ran over.

"Guru?"

Tilting his head questioningly, as if asking "What's up?", Rei took the Snow Salamander magic stone from his Misty Ring and tossed it.

Seeing this, Set, almost without thinking, reflexively caught the magicite in his beak and swallowed it.

[Set has acquired the skill 'Ice Arrow Lv.2']

The familiar announcement message echoed in Rei's mind.

Set seemed equally pleased, letting out a cheerful cry of his own.

But unlike Rei, who was connected to Set, Marina couldn't understand this.

"What's wrong?"

"Nothing. Looks like he learned the skill safely. ...Set, will you use it?"

"GRRRRRR!"

Obeying Rei's command, Set unleashed Ice Arrow.

Ten ice arrows materialized around Set.

"Considering it was only five before absorbing the magic stone, it's simply doubled. ...Ten ice arrows is pretty strong."

Ice has its own inherent mass.

Even a single Ice Arrow could likely inflict a fatal wound on a goblin.

With ten appearing at once, considering they could pierce through ten... no, goblin bodies in a single strike, it could take down even more goblins.

At my current low level, there's a good chance Ice Arrows won't be effective against stronger monsters.

"Huh... That's pretty impressive."

Marina murmured in admiration at the ice arrows appearing around Set.

For Marina, who wielded spirit magic, performing a similar attack wasn't particularly difficult.

However, even though the incantation is shorter than for ordinary magic, it still requires some time to take effect.

Compared to that, Set could create ice arrows just by letting out a cry. There was no doubt that speed of activation gave him a significant advantage in battle.

"Hmph, I knew it, didn't I?"

Pleased that his partner had been praised, Rei flashed Marina a proud smile.

"Wait, shouldn't Rei be the one happy about that, not you?"

"Well, Set is my partner. ...Right?"

"GRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set growled happily.

At the same time, the Ice Arrow fell to the ground and vanished.

Lightly stroking Set's head, Rei gazed up at the sky.

"It's such a nice day. ...Times like this, it's good to just relax and enjoy the slow pace. Set seems happy too, running around freely."

"Yes. Compared to previous winters, this one has been quite mild, but it's nearly over now. ...Rei, you're ready, right?"

"Well, you know, I don't really have anything specific to do. Maybe I should get my body moving a bit more, though."

"GRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set growled, as if to say, I'm fine!

Considering how much he's been moving around like this, that statement certainly wasn't wrong.

"Hehe, Set is full of energy, isn't he?"

Marina smiled happily as she said this, her gaze fixed on Set.

Perhaps pleased by Marina's praise, Set purred and rubbed his face against hers.

"Hehe, there are many advantages to being in a party with Rei, but being able to interact with Set like this whenever I want is a significant perk."

"Ah... Hearing you say that makes me happy too."

Watching Marina play with Set, Rei smiled.

Honestly, considering how much of a mascot Set is in Gilm, just the chance to play with him would make many want to join the same party as Rei.

Especially the two whose affection for Set was unparalleled... the female party leader of a certain party and the female former member of a certain guerrilla squad were prime examples.

That said, the former is already in a party, and while the latter isn't in a party, she's one of the leaders among those living together.

If they were to abandon those ties and ask to join Rei's party, they would undoubtedly be met with utter bewilderment... or worse, they might be written off entirely.

To be thought of that way by Rei meant, naturally, to be thought of that way by Set, who adored Rei. Neither of them could bear that... and others who shared their mindset understood this perfectly well.

Besides, Rei and the others had no intention of adding more party members for now.

If they were to add anyone, it would be after Elena joined them... that was the plan.

However, if Byune left, there would be no thieves left in the party... They planned to manage that situation using Set's abilities, Rei's magic, and magic items.

"Hehe, I'm glad if it makes Rei happy. ...Oh, right. Could you wait a moment?"

"Huh? Sure, no problem."

Though Rei was puzzled by the sudden request, he'd already decided to spend the whole day with Marina, so he nodded without protest.

Seeing Rei's reaction, Marina flashed an amused smile and left him and Set in the garden, heading back inside the house.

After watching her go, a few minutes passed.

As he sat with Set eating tea and sandwiches... Marina soon appeared from inside the house.

"Well... what do you think?"

It was immediately clear to Rei what she was referring to.

Marina was wearing a dress with an unusually modest neckline, but a brooch was pinned to it.

"It looks good on you... but where did you get that brooch?"

"Hehe, Lenora and Kenny gave it to me since I'm stepping down as Guild Master."

"...I see. Well, I'll have to treasure it then."

"Yes. But Rei, just as I'll treasure this brooch, please treasure me too, okay?"

Marina spoke, her usual seductive smile replaced by a womanly smile brimming with happiness.
