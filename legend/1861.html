The first drink served to Rei and Elena was in a small barrel.

It was the kind of barrel typically sold to individuals, holding about two liters.

While not enough to drink to one’s heart’s content, it was a perfectly adequate amount for savoring the drink slowly.

“Since you mentioned it was a souvenir, how about this wine? It’s on the sweeter side, but it has a clean finish, and I’m confident anyone would enjoy it. In fact, many people buy this wine in bulk when they’re hosting a party.”

"I see. Well then, for now, give me that barrel... let's see, about ten barrels."

“Huh?”

The clerk was slightly taken aback by the words that came out of Rei’s mouth without a hint of hesitation.

He hadn’t expected him to buy it so readily.

The sake sold at this brewery isn’t exactly high-end.

Still, to the average person, many of them were priced like the kind of sake you’d drink during a slightly fancy meal.

While there were some fairly inexpensive options, the sake the clerk had recommended this time was certainly worthy of being called a bit of a luxury.

However, he quickly remembered who Elena and Rei were, and with a delighted smile, he spoke up.

“Understood, I’ll have it prepared right away. Now, we have many other types of sake as well—what would you like?”

At the clerk’s words, Elena turned her gaze toward Rei, as if to ask, “What should we do?”

Faced with that look, Rei thought to himself that having some sake wouldn’t hurt—even though he didn’t particularly enjoy drinking it himself—and nodded in agreement.

“Alright, bring it over. If it’s good, we’ll buy it.”

“Thank you! Please wait a moment!”

Hearing Rei’s words, the clerk must have decided they were a valuable customer. With a delighted smile, the clerk headed toward the back of the shop.

Since he knew Rei and Elena, there was no doubt he hadn’t thought they were bad customers from the start.

But even so… there was no doubt that they were far better customers than he had anticipated.

The sake sold at this brewery ranges from inexpensive to expensive.

Even the expensive ones aren’t exactly… well, not so expensive that you could build a house with the money, but there are still many that are considered somewhat high-end.

Specifically, they’re the kind of sake you’d drink at a small celebration or give as a gift for a joyous occasion.

If it were Rei or Elena, they might buy such sake in large quantities.

With that in mind, the clerk hurried to fetch the sake.

Of course, since this sake brewery is fairly well-known even in Anesis, there are other clerks besides the one assigned to Rei and Elena, and they are naturally attending to other customers as well.

Those other customers, too, can’t help but be drawn to Rei and Elena.

(People are watching us. …Well, it’s nothing new.)

Despite feeling the stares from other customers and staff, Rei tried not to let it bother him and spoke to Elena.

“How much alcohol do you think we should buy, specifically?”

“Hm? That’s a bit tricky. Well, alcohol isn’t just for drinking—if it’s high-proof, it can be used for disinfecting, and it makes a good gift too. Also, I’ve heard from Georgima that they use alcohol in cooking.”

“Ah… well, for cooking, I suppose.”

Rei found that explanation quite convincing.

That was because, back when he was in Japan, he’d seen an acquaintance who’d hunted a bear and shared the meat with him simmer the meat in a large amount of sake along with aromatic vegetables like ginger and green onions to remove the gamey smell.

In fact, Rei remembered that the bear meat cooked that way had absolutely no gamey smell, and thanks to the long simmering, it turned into a superb stew that fell apart easily in your mouth.

Besides that, although Rei had never actually eaten it herself, she had seen dishes like beef stewed in red wine featured on TV.

He had also seen Marina using alcohol when she cooked for him.

Considering all that, Ray could readily accept Elena’s use of the word “cooking.”

(Though, I think there was something called cooking sake too... If you use regular alcohol in cooking, what on earth is cooking sake used for?)

Rei was pondering this, but before he could voice his question, the waiter from earlier reappeared carrying a tray with several glasses of alcohol.

Normally, he would have brought a barrel, but in this case, he probably couldn’t do that.

Just how many varieties of sake were there? The glasses on the server’s tray provided the answer.

“Thank you for waiting. I’ve brought some slightly higher-end recommendations, as well as some cheaper options that are still more than delicious. These are all delicious sakes that I can confidently recommend.”

As someone who works at a sake brewery, the clerk must truly love sake.

Indeed, even now, standing before Rei and Elena, he wore a smile that couldn’t have been happier.

“Normally, we supply various shops, but the great thing about buying directly from the brewery is that you can get it a little cheaper than you would at those stores.”

“Well, I suppose that makes sense.”

Rei nodded in agreement at the clerk’s words.

If you bought sake at a shop—or a tavern, for that matter—the price would inevitably go up because the tavern acts as a middleman.

It only made sense that buying it directly from the place where it was made would be cheaper.

For Rei, who possessed enough money to live a life of luxury for several lifetimes even if he were reincarnated a few times, he honestly didn’t care about a price difference of that magnitude.

But that was strictly speaking only true for Rei; for others—specifically, those who came to this brewery to buy sake—there was no doubt that such a price difference was a major deal.

“Well then, here you go. Let’s start with this one. This sake is inexpensive, but in terms of taste, it’s undoubtedly top-notch.”

“...It’s top-notch in taste, yet it’s cheap? That seems like a contradiction to me.”

“No, the year this sake was made, the weather was bad and the harvest was poor. Because of that, this year’s batch was considered unlucky or unpalatable, so there were no buyers… but as I said earlier, the taste itself is absolutely fine.”

"Sigh," the clerk muttered regretfully.

From the clerk’s perspective, he probably couldn’t accept that such delicious sake was being undervalued.

Elena seemed to agree, taking the glass from the clerk and raising it to her lips.

The way Elena’s white throat moves with every sip is downright alluring.

Even a professional clerk like him was captivated by the sight.

That blissful moment of visual pleasure ended quickly, and Elena closed her eyes as if savoring the aftertaste of the drink she had finished.

Snapped back to reality by Elena’s gesture, the bartender opened his mouth in a fluster.

“Um, how was it?”

“Delicious. It’s a bit hard to believe something this flavorful is so cheap. …Though, if it was a poor harvest, I suppose there simply aren’t many bottles to begin with.”

“Y-yes, that’s true. But I’m sure the sake is happy to be enjoyed by someone with such a discerning palate as yours, Lady Elena.”

“Heh, that’s a clever thing to say. …All right, I’ll take this sake. Rei, since I need to give some out as souvenirs, is it okay if I buy this?”

Rei nodded without hesitation when Elena asked him.

Though the two of them were buying souvenirs together, there was no particular reason why Rei alone should have to pay for them.

If Elena wanted to buy them, Rei had no objection to her offer.

“Hmm. So, this wine… how much would a single-person barrel sell for? I heard the original stock was limited.”

“Well, let’s see. About ten… no, how about fifteen barrels? We still have some leeway, but considering the amount we need to set aside for other customers, we can’t exactly sell all of it.”

As a brewery, they naturally want their prized sake to be enjoyed by someone worthy of it.

But even so, there was no way they could just sell the entire stock to Elena.

After all, we don’t know what the future holds, and we might very well need that sake ourselves if something were to happen.

Elena, perhaps understanding these circumstances as a noblewoman, accepted the clerk’s offer without pressing the matter further.

“Very well, that’s fine. Now, as for the next sake…”

After wrapping up the discussion briefly, Elena reaches for the next bottle.

From then on, Elena continued to taste the wines and describe them with precision, delighting the clerk.

In fact, even other idle staff members gathered to hear Elena’s descriptions, and eventually, other customers, the staff attending to them, and even the brewery’s craftsmen began to gather around.

“Are you okay?”

After buying a full selection of sake—enough to last an average household a year, or perhaps even longer—Rei and Elena walked down the street after leaving the brewery.

To be honest, from Rei’s perspective, buying such a massive amount of sake—especially since he would likely never drink it himself—felt like nothing more than a waste of a precious resource.

He did have those thoughts, but he decided that since it was a souvenir, he could give some away, or Marina could use it in her cooking, so he decided to buy as much as he could for the time being.

Above all, he also had a thought that maybe—just maybe—there might come a time when Rei would actually drink the liquor and find it delicious.

As things stand, there’s no way Rei would find alcohol delicious.

But there are people who are this passionate about making sake, and at the same time, there are people who enjoy drinking it.

Wouldn’t it be a bit of a waste to drink it and say it’s bad?

The biggest reason Rei thought this was, of course, the presence of the clerk who had attended to them at the sake brewery earlier.

He simply loved sake—he radiated that kind of passion. Seeing that, Rei thought to himself, “If he feels that strongly about it…” and it was true.

That said, after trying a little, he realized it just wasn’t for him, and he didn’t feel like drinking any more.

After all, Rei only drinks beverages he finds delicious.

...If it were something like fruit juice, though, that’s exactly what Rei would gladly drink.

“Hmm. I guess I… had a bit too much. The cold wind feels nice.”

Even slightly drunk as she was, the winter wind felt pleasant to Elena.

Indeed, having inherited the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone, even in Anesis now that the snow had begun to fall, the situation was one that should be called cool rather than cold.

Not out of shyness, but slightly flushed from the faint intoxication, Elena looked incredibly alluring.

For Rei, the image of a seductive woman had always been strongly associated with Marina, but the Elena before him now possessed a seductiveness that rivaled even Marina’s.

Watching Elena’s profile, Rei handed her the fruit juice he had taken out of his Misty Ring.

Even if each type of alcohol was only a small amount, drinking them all would naturally add up to a considerable amount.

He did this because he thought the refreshing fruit juice would be a welcome change for the tipsy Elena.

……It was a response born from having seen his father, who would come home drunk from parties back when they were in Japan, ask for water time and time again.

Even though the fruit water was quite diluted, that was precisely why it tasted so delicious to Elena. She happily downs it.

“Phew. …That’s good.”

“I see. I’m glad you like it. Anyway, going to the next bar… doesn’t look like it’s going to happen.”

She wasn’t completely drunk, but Elena was definitely tipsy.

You could call it a slight buzz.

Normally, she’d be able to function perfectly fine with this level of intoxication.

However, Elena is the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel and is known by the nickname “Princess General.”

As such, if she were to let her drunkenness get the better of her and cause any trouble, it would be a disgrace to the Duke of Kerebel’s family.

Given Elena’s reputation, such a thing likely wouldn’t be a problem…

(After all, Elena isn’t exactly without enemies.)

I recall the woman who accosted Elena at the New Year’s party.

From what Rei could see, Elena wasn’t paying her the slightest bit of attention… but that doesn’t mean it’s okay to just ignore it.

“No, I don’t really have a problem with it.”

“Really? Well, in that case, maybe you should go somewhere else to get a little more drunk…”

...let’s wait until she’s sobered up a bit.

Just as Rei was about to say that, he stopped himself suddenly and turned his gaze in a certain direction.

Where his gaze fell... on the roof of the building, stood a black wolf.
