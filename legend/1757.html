Word that Sura and the others had arrived in Gilm spread throughout the town that very day, as was to be expected.

Since they had moved through the main street as if they were holding a parade, it would have been impossible for it not to become the talk of the town.

Meanwhile, Rei was enjoying dinner in Marina’s backyard as usual.

“Grrrrrrrrr!”

Set purrs happily at the Gamelion meat roasted in the magic item kiln.

Since it was this year’s Gamelion, you could technically call it the first of the season.

That said, since Rei and Set had already eaten it at a street stall, it wasn’t strictly speaking the first of the season.

"Hmm, Gamelion meat really is delicious. I heard the harvest this year is about average, but with so many people around, the price inevitably goes up,"

Marina murmurs thoughtfully.

In fact, there are more people in Gilm right now than usual.

And, naturally, it’s only natural that everyone wants to eat delicious food while they’re here, so the price of Gamelion meat goes up as a result.

Also, while Gamelions do appear in a few places outside of Gilm, defeating them requires considerable strength.

For these reasons, Gamelion meat is a major commodity in Gilm during this season, and many merchants seek to buy it.

Of course, even as the season shifts from autumn to winter, meat will still spoil.

Of course, it spoils more slowly than in summer, but since it’s not Rei’s Misty Ring, it can’t stay fresh forever.

To prevent this, merchants who buy Gamelion meat either hire specialized craftsmen to turn it into jerky, or, if they possess the skill themselves, make the jerky to increase its commercial value.

...However, while dried meat made by a professional is one thing, even those who possess the technique are, after all, amateurs.

As a result, the Gamelion meat they had gone to the trouble of purchasing was often ruined.

(Well, I suppose you could just say it’s because I got too greedy.)

As Rei sliced the oven-roasted Gamelion meat thinly, dipped it in a sauce made from a tart fruit resembling a lemon, and brought it to his mouth, he pondered these things.

“Come to think of it…”

Ara suddenly spoke up in the middle of the meal.

“I think Lady Elena should start getting ready to go home soon. If we have to rush the preparations, there might be some oversights.”

“Hmm. So I really have to go back after all?”

At Ara’s words, Elena muttered with a hint of reluctance.

For Elena, life in Gilm had been extremely comfortable.

Having the man she loved by her side was a major factor, but she was also glad that, with no aristocrats from the Noble Faction around, she didn’t have to be on her guard.

Of course, even when she was in the Duchy of Kerebel, Elena’s life was primarily that of a soldier, and she didn’t have much interaction with the nobility.

...Some nobles, in an attempt to establish a connection with Elena, would enlist their sons or relatives in the Duke of Kerebel’s army, but the Duke of Kerebel’s army was renowned as a formidable force, and naturally, its training was extremely rigorous.

As a result, those kinds of people usually end up running back home.

Despite this situation, as the only daughter of the Duke of Kerebel, she naturally had to participate in at least the minimum of aristocratic social engagements, such as parties.

However, while in Gilm, she didn’t have to do any of that.

Nobles from the Noble Faction in Gilm, as well as others who wish to get to know Elena, frequently request meetings, but most of these are turned down by Ara.

She only meets with those she absolutely must see, but even so, it’s overwhelmingly easier than when she was at her family home.

So, I had been thinking that, if possible, I’d like to spend this year in Gilm…

“It’s simply impossible to imagine the New Year’s party without Lady Elena, the very symbol of the Noble Faction.”

When told this by Ara, her trusted subordinate and close friend, Elena couldn’t argue any further.

In fact, a New Year’s party is held every year at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

Naturally, under normal circumstances, no one would travel through the snow, but these are the nobles of the Noble Faction.

They use their numbers and wealth to their advantage and gather at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

It is precisely at times like this that the wizards’ abilities are put to the test—whether it’s melting the snow or forcibly moving it.

The Kingdom of Mireana is vast.

That is precisely why, for a party attended by nobles from far and wide—except for those with estates at the very edge of the kingdom—the fact that Elena, who could be called the very symbol of the Noble Faction, did not attend was undoubtedly a source of great embarrassment for Kerebel Duke.

Of course, if there were a valid reason, such as illness, it would be understandable for her not to attend the party.

But Elena was, of course, not ill; she was in excellent health.

Given that, the option of not attending the party simply did not exist.

Elena understood what Ara was saying, but the fact that she still wanted to avoid it, if possible, was likely due to the comfort she felt being with Rei.

Of course, Ara understood Elena’s feelings.

And if it were for Elena’s sake, Ara would even be willing to skip the party herself—that’s what she wanted to say.

However, if she were to say that now, it would ultimately put Elena in a difficult position.

Given that, as much as it pains her, that is all Ara can do.

Elena, knowing this, nodded even as she harbored some resentment in her heart.

But… someone stepped in to put a stop to Elena’s words.

“Hey, Elena. The reason you don’t want to go back there is… because Rei… no, because you feel comfortable with Gilm, isn’t it?”

“It’s because Rei is there that you don’t want to go back, isn’t it?”

Marina was about to say that, but she judged that Elena wouldn’t readily admit it if she put it that way, so she changed her wording.

Of course, Elena… and the others… understood that as well, but no one pressed the issue.

“That’s true. I won’t deny it. As I’ve said many times, Gilm is a place where I can relax.”

“Right. …Well then, how about this? Rei should come to the New Year’s party that Elena has to attend.”

“Huh?”

At Marina’s utterly outrageous suggestion, Ara, Elena, Rei, and Vihera, who had been listening, all gasped in unison.

Byune didn’t let out a gasp of surprise, but seeing her usually expressionless face with eyes wide open showed just how shocked she was.

It was only natural. Rei and the nobility were a bad match.

And it wasn’t just a slight incompatibility—it was a fatal one.

Many nobles in the Noble Faction take pride in the fact that they are nobles.

……The problem is that for many of them, that pride is directly linked to meaningless vanity.

Starting with Duke Kerebel, many of the nobles in the upper echelons of the Noble Faction are well aware of this. …While “many” does imply that there are nobles with meaningless pride even among the upper echelons, they are still capable of adjusting their attitude depending on who they’re dealing with.

However, beyond that… simply because they are nobles of the Noble Faction, many nobles harbor the belief that they are a chosen few among the nobility, and that it is only natural for non-nobles to obey the nobles’ commands.

There are inevitably many nobles who harbor such thoughts.

Not all of them are like that, but what would happen if Rei were in a party with such nobles? The answer was obvious without even having to think about it.

Especially from Rei’s perspective, to the nobles trying to make a move on Elena, he would be nothing but a nuisance.

If Rei were to attend such a party, it was almost certain that trouble would ensue.

Marina, of course, must have understood this, and everyone present turned their gaze toward her, wondering why she would say such a thing.

……Well, since Set and Yellow were each savoring a piece of Gamelion meat, perhaps not everyone was looking.

In any case, Marina, who had drawn the attention of the others, smiled and spoke up.

“You know? Elena will have to get a grip on that eventually, right? After all, she can’t stay like this forever.”

“Well…”

At Marina’s words, Elena was at a loss for words.

In truth, Elena’s mind was already made up.

So much so that she couldn’t even imagine loving anyone other than Rei.

But that was merely Elena’s own belief; it wasn’t something everyone else assumed.

Well, those close to Rei probably knew how Elena felt… but within the Noble Faction to which Elena belonged, there were surely few who would acknowledge the relationship between her and Rei.

To those who believe they can do anything and get away with it, many view Elena as the woman who should be their wife.

For such nobles, the idea that Elena would settle down with someone like Rei—even if he is a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname—someone who is not of noble birth, was absolutely, under no circumstances, permissible.

That is precisely why Marina is telling her to bring Rei along to that party and clearly demonstrate who she loves… and under whose wing she wishes to remain.

She was implying that if Elena were to give up just because of the commotion, then her feelings for Rei were nothing more than superficial.

The look Marina gives Elena is neither a joke nor a tease—it is serious.

She’s telling her that if she loves Rei, she needs to make her stance clear.

There are many people who love Rei, even among those Marina knows right now.

Most of them are the kind of people who would not hesitate to give up their current positions if it were truly for Rei’s sake.

...In fact, to be with Rei, Marina had readily abandoned her position as Guild Master of Gilm—one of the most prestigious positions in the Kingdom of Mireana—and returned to being an adventurer.

As for Vihera, although she is a former princess of the Bestir Empire, she had already abandoned that status by the time she met Rei in the Labyrinth City of Exil, so there is no particular issue.

That said, abandoning the Labyrinth City—a place where Vihera could enjoy fighting powerful opponents—and coming to be under Rei’s command might have been proof of her resolve.

However, Rei is in the frontier town of Gilm.

It’s not uncommon for monsters there to be even more powerful than those found in dungeons.

Though she can’t hold a candle to those two, Kenny, the guild receptionist, also makes no secret of her feelings for Rei.

(Considering Rei’s personality, I bet he’s been getting into all sorts of trouble behind my back.)

Exchanging glances with Elena, Marina thinks to herself.

In fact, Marina’s guess was correct.

Take, for example, Rocha, the fisherman’s daughter from Emoshion.

Besides Rei, Rei had unwittingly captured the hearts of many women, though he was completely oblivious to it.

Rei’s personality isn’t exactly gentle, nor is he necessarily well-liked… but there’s no doubt he has a personality so intense it could be called extraordinary.

For that very reason, once people got to know Rei, many found it difficult to see the ordinary men around them as men—or even as members of the opposite sex.

Not everyone felt that way, though.

“So, with that said… what’s Rei going to do?”

Marina turned the conversation toward Rei.

Rei didn’t hesitate at her words… not even for a moment; he nodded without a second thought.

“If Elena’s okay with it, I’m fine with going. I don’t really have anything else to do during the winter anyway.”

To be precise, there are probably various tasks to handle regarding the expansion of Gilm.

However, since logging in the Treant Forest generally doesn’t take place during the winter, there’s almost no work that only Rei, with his Misty Ring, could do.

That said, there might be work involved in transporting materials—starting with lumber—from one storage yard to another, but that could easily be left to anyone looking for work.

Since there’s no danger in going outside Gilm, even if Rei wanted to help, the guild would likely ask him to hold off unless they were extremely busy.

“...Is that okay?”

Did she really not expect Rei to simply agree to come with her to the Duchy of Kerebel? Elena asked Rei, looking somewhat hesitant.

In response to Elena’s words, which seemed like a final confirmation, Rei nodded without showing any particular signs of nervousness.
