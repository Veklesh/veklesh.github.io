What was supposed to be a celebration for Vihera regaining consciousness ended up becoming a tasting event for dishes made with silver lion meat. Now, Rei and the others were walking outside.

It was already late autumn... and being nighttime, Johanna and the others shivered at the cold night air.

Still, whether it was the effect of eating silver lion meat or simply because they had just finished eating, they didn't seem genuinely cold.

They were merely putting on that act... or so it seemed.

"So, we plan to head back soon. What about you guys, Johanna?"

"Hmm, we'd like to get back to Gilm before the real snow starts falling too. We've accomplished our goal, after all."

Johanna murmured, turning her gaze toward Vihera.

Despite having just woken up today after nearly two months unconscious, Vihera appeared completely unharmed.

Johanna and the others had originally come to this dungeon to obtain potions or similar items to restore Vihera's consciousness.

However, in the end, Grimm had handled the task of restoring Vihera's consciousness. What Johanna and the others gained from this dungeon was the money earned by selling materials and proof-of-kill parts obtained from their descent... an amount that could generally be called a fortune.

In that sense, it wasn't as if their descent into the dungeon had been entirely meaningless.

For adventurers, money is never too much.

Replacing weapons and armor, purchasing consumables like potions, paying informants, and countless other reasons meant their gold flowed out faster than the average person's.

"When are you guys heading back, Rei?"

"Ah... well. I think we'll be heading back soon. I want to be in Gilm for winter, and more than anything, this dungeon is... huh?"

Johanna understood what Rei meant without him finishing.

Once the dungeon's core was destroyed, it would gradually lose its function as a dungeon over time.

Knowing that, the more impatient among them would likely consider retreating from here sooner rather than later... before winter set in.

If too many did that, it was certain things would get crowded and troublesome.

Johanna understood what Rei meant too. She shifted her gaze to the surrounding buildings.

This place, already approaching the scale of a small town beyond that of a village, housed a large number of residents.

Johanna didn't know exactly how many people might consider leaving early, but it was easy to imagine that even if just ten percent of the residents tried to leave, it would cause significant chaos.

Did she want to get caught up in that chaos? Of course not... Besides, since Gilm had the mansion where Johanna and the others lived, it was only natural to want to spend the winter there rather than in cramped lodgings.

"Then maybe we should head back soon too..."

"Hey, Johanna. We can't go back yet."

"Huh?"

Let's go back tomorrow, right away.

Johanna seemed to want to say that, but her companion put a stop to it.

Why? As Johanna pondered this, the man sighed before speaking.

"Hey, did you forget? We've got that alchemist escort job coming up in three days. Something about examining materials inside a dungeon."

"...Oh."

Vihera's... And then, with the whole Rodos business, she'd completely forgotten about it. Johanna finally remembered the request they'd received from the guild and voiced it.

That meant she couldn't return to Gilm with Rei and the others.

Johanna, wanting to stay with Set even a little longer, had hoped they could return to Gilm with Rei and the others.

But having accepted the guild's request, that was impossible.

If it were due to injury... or a broken weapon, they might have been able to cancel the request.

But refusing a request for personal reasons, not because of unavoidable circumstances... While not impossible, doing so would undoubtedly cause all sorts of trouble when accepting future requests.

And not just at this dungeon. This guild was a branch of the Guild of Gilm. If they refused a request here, the main guild in Gilm might even turn them down if they tried to take on a job there.

To avoid that, I'd have to go to another town or another country, but since guilds share information to some extent, even that wouldn't be absolutely guaranteed.

Moreover, the biggest problem of all was whether Johanna could live anywhere without Set.

Anyone who knew how much Johanna adored Set would say such a thing was absolutely impossible.

"Ah... I wanted to enjoy traveling with little Set..."

"Give it up. But in exchange, didn't you get the right to spend a whole day with Set when you get back to Gilm?"

"Yeah, but that means I have to wait so long before I can play with Set-chan!"

Unlike her usual self, Johanna whined like a spoiled child.

Normally, she was the dependable party leader who pulled everyone along, but when it came to Set, she turned into this.

But no matter how much of a brat she was, she understood how serious it was to accept a request and then just abandon it. In the end, Johanna gave up on returning to Gilm with Rei and the others.

"Set hates people who abandon their work midway too."

There was no doubt that this statement from Rei's mouth was the decisive blow.

"Ugh, Set-chan. Since it's impossible to meet here again... let's meet in Gilm, okay? I'll spend the whole day with you then, alright?"

"Grr."

At the fork in the road between the most expensive inn where Rei and the others were staying and the inn where Johanna and the others were staying, Johanna murmured sadly, as if parting forever from a lover.

Set, who had heard her, didn't seem all that serious about it, just saying "See you later"...

"Mm-hmm. It's okay, Set-chan. I'm always thinking about you."

In Johanna's mind, Set's current attitude seemed to translate into sadness over parting with her. She clung to Set, reluctant to say goodbye.

"Well then, you two be careful too."

"Ah, yes. Understood. But we're just escorting the alchemist, and we won't be going that deep, so I don't think there's any need to worry."

"Even if the enemies are easy to defeat, a dungeon is a dungeon. Anything could happen, so don't let your guard down."

Seeing a hint of underestimation toward the dungeon, Rei emphasized once more not to let their guard down.

Having operated in this dungeon before, he likely understood the nature of the monsters that appeared.

After all, purely in terms of time spent exploring dungeons, Johanna and her group undoubtedly had far more experience than Rei and his companions.

The men also knew just how strong Rei was.

After all, witnessing his power firsthand during the civil war in the Bestir Empire was precisely why they had come to the Kingdom of Mireana to escort Rei back.

When Rei himself reiterated the need for caution, there was no question of ignoring it.

"Understood. Once we finish escorting the alchemist this time, we'll be heading back to Gilm too."

Johanna and her companions had no intention of staying in a dungeon that had lost its core.

Above all, Johanna, the party leader, had the right to spend a day with Set once they reached Gilm.

Under those circumstances, it was utterly inconceivable that Johanna would agree to stay here any longer.

Moreover, the male members of the party strongly preferred staying at the mansion in Gilm rather than an inn if they were going to relax.

"Well then... we should be heading soon. Johanna, it's time to let go of Set."

"Aaaaaah, Set-chan... I'll get back to Gilm as soon as possible, okay? It'll be lonely until then, but wait for me, okay?"

"Gururu?"

Set growled curiously at Johanna's words.

To Johanna, that very expression on Set's face was undoubtedly adorable.

She clung to Set, unwilling to let go.

But she couldn't let this go on forever, so Rei pried Johanna away from Set.

"Come on, cut it out. If you fuss over Set too much, he'll get annoyed."

"Ugh!"

Rei's words had more effect than expected, and Johanna immediately let go of Set.

Though, to be fair, Set didn't seem particularly bothered by the hug.

"Se-Set-chan. You won't hate me, right? Right? Right?"

"Guruu!"

Of course! Set growled.

Seeing Set like that, Johanna seemed reassured. As if her panic just seconds ago had been a lie, she let out a sigh of relief.

"Well, since Johanna's gone now... See you later."

Besides Rei, Elena and the others exchanged farewells as well, parting ways with Johanna and heading back to the inn.

"Come to think of it, it's rare for us to walk at night instead of taking a carriage, isn't it?"

"Is that so?"

Rei voiced his confusion at Vihera's remark.

For Rei, who rarely rode in carriages or similar vehicles, walking wasn't particularly unusual.

...Though traveling by riding a Set instead of a carriage was another matter.

"It is to me. ...What about Elena and the others?"

"Hmm, that's true. Given my position, I often travel by carriage or horse as well."

"Oh? Then I suppose I'm the one who's had this kind of experience with Rei the most?"

At Elena and Marina's words, only Vihera declared with a slightly smug expression.

Seeing Vihera like that, Elena and Marina naturally wore displeased expressions and spoke up.

"Still, considering how little progress has been made with Rei, it doesn't seem very effective."

"Yeah. Isn't being together just becoming a habit?"

Hearing both of them say that, Vihera naturally wasn't amused either.

"Oh dear, oh dear. Getting jealous just because you lost a battle between women? I don't think that's very nice."

"Lose? Me? I'm afraid I've never lost to Vihera."

"Is that so? At the very least, I think you've lost to me when it comes to feminine charm?"

"Oh? Well, I'd say I've got my own confidence in that department, too."

Marina chimed in, echoing Vihera's words.

Elena, Marina, Vihera... All three possessed appearances worthy of being called rare beauties.

Yet, even as rare beauties, the impressions each conveyed were vastly different.

Elena had a dignified aura, Vihera a healthy sensuality, and Marina a womanly allure.

Though they radiated such different impressions, it was undeniable that Elena fell short of the other two in terms of sensuality and allure.

Of course, if she wore a party dress like Marina's, her appearance would become a captivating, alluring figure that drew the gaze of all who saw her.

But even so... having spent her life as a warrior rather than a woman, she inevitably fell short compared to Vihera... and certainly Marina, who radiated a rich, feminine allure.

Aware of this, Elena gently reached out her hand to her hair, which looked like pure gold in the softly cascading moonlight.

"It's... it's fine, Elena-sama. You are very beautiful! Right, Rei?"

Ara asked Rei, but the intensity in her gaze made even Rei flinch for a moment.

It was a light that made one genuinely believe that if he said otherwise, his throat might be torn out in that instant...

"Ah... yeah. That's right. Of course I think so too. Hey, Set?"

"Guru?"

Suddenly addressed, Set tilted his head slightly.

As a griffin, Set couldn't comprehend human notions of beauty or ugliness.

And when it came to talk about a woman's charm or allure, he couldn't understand it at all.

Rei tried to muddle through the conversation vaguely, but naturally, Elena and the others weren't fooled... and they all continued walking and chatting noisily until they reached the inn.

It was just a casual exchange... but precisely because it was so ordinary, Rei felt it deeply: this was why Vihera had returned.
