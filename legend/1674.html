"Hmm-hmm, hmm-hmm"

Rei, who had just stepped out of the pirate ship, was humming cheerfully to himself.

The pirates probably wouldn’t understand just how rare that was.

Elena and the others, who were always with him, would have understood that Rei was in an even better mood than usual.

Attached to the rope that Rei was pulling was a man with his wrists bound.

The pirates had a pretty good idea who that was.

But… even so, it’s probably true to say they still didn’t want to believe it.

After all, he is the captain of this pirate crew.

It was inevitable that the pirates’ morale would plummet now that their captain had been captured.

But… the reason Rei is in such a good mood isn’t because he captured the pirates’ captain, but because he managed to get his hands on the magic item the captain was carrying.

Unfortunately, it’s a one-time-use item, so he can’t use it repeatedly, but even so, he discovered nearly fifty similar magic items on the ship—in addition to the one the man had tried to use—and has already stowed them away in Misty Ring.

The fact that the amount of smoke produced varies depending on the force of the impact holds great significance for Rei… and for Rei riding on Set as well.

Since even an ordinary person produces a certain amount of smoke, it’s impossible to imagine how much smoke would be produced if Rei were to slam it down with all his might.

Furthermore, it was completely unknown how much smoke would be produced if a magic item were thrown to the ground from a height of 100 meters while riding Set… or from an even greater height.

Of course, there’s also the question of whether it’s even possible to drop a magic item onto a specific spot from that height.

“Boss… I’m sorry we weren’t strong enough…”

“We’re sorry, Captain. We failed to protect the young one.”

The pirates looked at the man being dragged by Rei and spoke with regret.

Seeing the pirates like that, he stopped humming and shot them a look of exasperation.

“If you’re going to be a pirate, it’s only natural that you’ll get caught eventually. If you didn’t want to get caught that badly, you shouldn’t have been a pirate… you should have been an adventurer or a fisherman instead.”

No matter how much the pirates lamented their own inadequacy, or how much they apologized to the man Rei had captured—the one to whom the former captain had ceded his position—that didn’t erase the fact that these men had been pirates.

Rei’s honest opinion was that if they were so afraid of getting caught, they simply shouldn’t have pretended to be pirates in the first place.

Of course, now that they’ve been captured, it’s too late to say anything.

Perhaps the pirates understood this too; many wore expressions of despair at Rei’s words—and, above all, at the fact that their own captain had been captured.

“Well, I suppose I can stow them away for now.”

Muttering to himself, Rei touched the ship… and in the next instant, it vanished.

“What!?”

The man who had been captured by Rei could only stare in utter astonishment at the sudden sight.

The other pirates who had been captured outside had already witnessed another ship being stored away in Rei’s Misty Ring, so while they were still surprised by this latest incident, their shock was less intense than when they first saw it.

In contrast, since this was the first time the man Rei had captured had seen a ship vanish—being stored in the Misty Ring—he could only stare wide-eyed in utter astonishment.

“Come on, stop being so surprised and come over here!”

Saying that, he pulled on the rope binding the man.

There was no way the man could resist Rei’s strength, and he was dragged along, his face still frozen in shock.

“Grrrr!”

Set, who had been keeping watch over the pirates, approached Rei with a delighted growl.

Naturally, the man being dragged by Rei also ended up looking at Set… and he was just as shocked—or perhaps even more so—than when the ship had vanished.

“Wh-wh-wh—”

“Come on, you can be surprised later. Come over here now.”

The man, half-dragged along, felt irritated by the situation.

From the man’s perspective, it was as if he’d been suddenly attacked while just hanging out on the ship.

On top of that, all his companions had already been captured.

On top of that, all his magic items—his trump cards for emergencies—had been taken, and when he was tied up with rope and dragged outside the ship, the ship he’d been on just moments ago had vanished, and there was a high-rank monster like a griffin.

Just as he was reeling from these unbelievable events, he was being forcibly dragged along by the rope.

It was simply impossible for him not to feel resentful about it.

Of course, no matter how much the man complained, Rei didn’t pay him any mind.

After taking the man to the other pirates and tying him to their ropes, Rei began to think about what to do next.

(First, I need to secure whatever treasure these guys are hiding... The problem is, how do I get them to that fishing village? Should I have Set carry them? Since they’re tied up with ropes, if I could just have Set hold onto those ropes...)

Even so, if he took the pirates along while they were tied up, the weight of those below would naturally be added to the burden of those already being carried by Set.

If the weight of nearly a dozen people were added, he would undoubtedly die under normal circumstances.

(But from here to that fishing village, at Set’s speed, it feels like it’s right next door. If it’s just that far, maybe he could manage to endure it… or could he? Ah, but maybe if we take them a few at a time, it might work?)

In reality, even if we transported this many people to that fishing village, how would they handle it...? Even if we tied them up somewhere, if we tried to take everyone here all at once, the fishing village would undoubtedly be thrown into chaos.

If that’s the case, there’s no doubt that taking them in small groups would cause less confusion for the fishing village.

“Alright, that’ll do. It’s a hassle to overthink things.”

At Rei’s muttered words, the pirates who heard him trembled for a moment.

They realized that Rei’s words had just decided their fate.

“I was wondering how to get you all to land, but I’ve decided to have Set carry you there in groups of a few, still tied up with rope. …You’re lucky. Flying through the sky is an experience you’d normally never get to have.”

Rei’s words were by no means a lie. They weren’t a lie, but… even if he told them to be happy about being carried away by a griffin in a situation like this, there was no way they could be happy.

“Wait! Wait a minute! You’re going to take us like this!? If you do that, we might end up dying!”

“Well then, do your best not to die. Humans can do just about anything if they try hard enough, you know?”

Rei had captured the man, and he shouted, but Rei replied casually.

Realizing that Rei wasn’t joking—that he was completely serious—the man’s face twisted in anguish as he desperately tried to think of a way to get Rei to relent.

Having lived as a pirate, he had long since given up on becoming a slave.

But even so... he wanted to do something to avoid dying for no reason at all, without even becoming a slave.

(Is there no way? Somehow... somehow... I can’t let the men who’ve served me since my father’s time die for no reason. Somehow...)

The man, who had been thinking desperately, suddenly remembered that Rei, who had taken the magic item he was holding, was in a very good mood, and shouted.

“A deal!”

“...A deal? What kind of deal do you think you can offer me at this late stage? You’ve already been sentenced to be sold off as slaves, you know?”

Judging by that line alone, Rei was clearly nothing but a villain.

But despite the situation, the man spoke to Rei with confidence.

“Since you defeated us, ownership of the treasures we’ve hoarded naturally transfers to you.”

“Well, I suppose so.”

The treasures possessed by the bandits become the property of whoever defeats them.

Of course, for items where the original owner is known, priority is given to negotiating a buyout, but such cases are rare.

And since pirates are also a type of bandit, it goes without saying that ownership of their treasure belongs to the person who defeated them… in this case, Rei.

“So, what kind of transactions can be made using that treasure?”

Rei asked this with a questioning look, but the man showed no sign of being fazed by her gaze.

(Is he the type who’s better at negotiation than combat? Well, even so, he was strong enough to be a pirate captain.)

While waiting for the man in front of him to speak, Rei observed his demeanor.

“I don’t know if you’ve checked, but we don’t hide any treasure in the house where we live.”

“That’s right. From what I saw, there was nothing there.”

To be precise, there were various household items, but it was true that there was no treasure.

“The treasure is hidden in various places. I think it would be difficult for even you, with a griffin by your side, to find it. But… if you take us away using peaceful means—not the violent methods you mentioned earlier—I’ll tell you where it’s hidden. How about it?”

“That’s easier said than done. …To be honest, transporting you guys by peaceful means is a huge hassle. If we waste time unnecessarily, there’s a chance you’ll find a way to escape, isn’t there?”

Even if the man were to say, “We won’t run away!” right now, if asked whether he could be trusted, Rei would immediately answer no.

“I get what you’re worried about, but if you let us use our ship—the one you stashed somewhere—we can all get to land without any problems. Of course, to make sure we don’t run away, you’re welcome to tie up parts of our bodies with rope.”

“……If you did that, wouldn’t we be unable to move the boat in the first place?”

The man says “a part of our bodies,” but if they’re going to tie us up with rope, the only options would be our legs or arms.

There’s no doubt that having his arms tied would hinder his ability to operate the ship, and tying his legs would be extremely dangerous as the boat would be tossed about by the waves.

But the man merely chuckled at Rei’s words, even flashing a smug smile.

“Do you really think we’d be stopped by something like that? Believe it or not, I’m confident in my ability to steer this ship.”

Even to Rei, the way he said it didn’t seem like a lie at all.

Of course, he wouldn’t go so far as to believe every word a pirate said, but if the man could even manage half of what he claimed, getting to the fishing village shouldn’t be too difficult.

(What should I do? I’d like to recover any treasure we can while we still can. But if I trust him… no, judging by the other pirates’ demeanor, it’s not that they’re absolutely loyal to this man… but they’ve definitely decided they have to protect him. So then…)

Quickly gathering his thoughts, Rei smiled and addressed the man.

“Alright. That’s fine. But you’ll have to stay within striking distance of me at all times. Naturally, if any of you try anything funny, you’re dead. Is that okay with you?”

As Rei spoke these words, fixing the man with a gaze laced with a hint of murderous intent, the man nodded immediately.

“I understand. That’s fine.”

Even as he said this, Rei could clearly see that the man’s knees were shaking so violently he couldn’t hide it.

And, naturally, the other pirates could clearly see the man’s state as well…

“Young master, you can’t do that!”

“That’s right! We can’t possibly entrust the kid’s life to a guy like that!”

Starting with those voices, the other pirates also shouted at the man, trying to talk him out of it.

(At a glance, doesn’t this make me look like the villain? …But I was only supposed to come here to hunt down pirates at the request of the fishing village folks.)

If I were to look at my current situation objectively, wouldn’t almost everyone who didn’t know the circumstances see me as the villain?

Even as he thought that, Rei spoke up.

“Well, if that’s what you want to believe, then so be it. …Just so you know, don’t even think about trying to escape by catching me off guard. I’m actually pretty skilled, you know.”

To call Rei “pretty skilled” is a massive understatement.

But since there’s no doubt he possesses a certain level of strength, what he’s saying isn’t wrong.

“...I understand. That’s fine. You lot, just stay put. I’m the captain of this pirate crew, and I’m the one leading you. As long as that’s the case, if there’s anything I can do for you, I have to do it. ...Please understand.”

At the man’s words, the others fell silent.

And so, the matter was settled, and Rei decided to accept the man’s proposal.
