"Nothing here, huh? I was sure it was a trap to lure me in."

Rei entered the room where the girl who had been fighting the guards was believed to have fled, but there was no one there.

It wasn’t a study like the one Rei had checked first, but more like a reception room for entertaining guests.

Given that it was a room like that, there were plenty of places to hide… but even when he scanned the area for signs of life, he couldn’t sense anyone there.

(Well, I can’t rule out the possibility that she’s someone skilled enough to mask her presence so well that I can’t detect it.)

Thinking this, Rei checked places where someone might be hiding, such as behind the sofa or behind the furniture, but he couldn’t find any sign of the girl from earlier anywhere.

“The window… if she escaped, it wouldn’t be strange for it to be open, but it doesn’t look like that either.”

Rei had hesitated slightly before entering this room, but even so, if she had escaped through the window, it wouldn’t be strange for some kind of trace to remain there.

But as far as he could tell, there were no signs of anything, and above all, the window was locked.

It was hard to believe she’d slipped out the window without leaving a trace and then gone back to lock it from the outside.

(Wait, could he do it if he had some kind of magic item?)

Normally, that wouldn’t be possible, but that’s based on Japanese common sense.

Since magic and magical items exist in this world of Elgin, it wouldn’t be strange if there were some way to unlock or lock a window from the outside.

Naturally, since such items are highly likely to be used for criminal purposes, if they actually existed, they would probably be strictly controlled.

(Right now, rather than dwelling on such a slim possibility, I need to find something more concrete.)

Realizing there’s no point dwelling on unlikely possibilities, Rei looks around the room once more.

He’d already searched most of the places where someone could hide, so that left only escape routes like secret passages or hidden staircases.

But even looking around the room like this, he couldn’t see any such places.

The room was about fifteen tatami mats in size and contained a fair amount of furniture, but even so, the number of places where someone could hide was limited.

“Where on earth did she go? I really should have brought Set along.”

If Set were here, he could probably track down where that girl went using his sense of smell.

Of course, that spot is probably hidden, but if I knew there was a path or staircase leading from there, breaking through the hiding place wouldn’t be that difficult.

But since Set isn’t here, there’s no point in thinking about that now.

Or, if I were to shout Set’s name loudly here, there’s a chance he might come… but doing something like that would likely lead to all sorts of trouble down the road.

“So… what should I do? Should I just search the place thoroughly, or should I call the guards and have them look into it?”

The guards who were fighting the girl who vanished from this room aren’t far from here.

Some of them were injured, but by now they should have received at least some treatment.

When it comes to searching, the guards are clearly more experienced than Rei.

There’s nothing strange about enlisting their help.

"Maybe I should have brought Byune along. If I had, Vihera would have come with us too."

Although Byune used to be a thief specialized in combat, this winter she had also been honing her skills purely as a thief.

Given that, it wouldn’t have been strange for Rei to want Byune to put her skills as a thief to use.

Furthermore, since the guards protecting this mansion were undoubtedly formidable, Rei predicted that Vihera—who craved battles against strong opponents—would have come here willingly.

(Well, there’s no point in thinking about it now.)

Realizing there was no point in worrying about someone who wasn’t here right now, he looked around the room again and again… when suddenly, he heard the sound of multiple footsteps approaching.

For a split second, he wondered if they might be enemies, but judging by the number of footsteps and the voices he heard, he realized they were the guards from earlier.

“Rei, where are you?!”

“I’m here, in this room!”

I called back to the guard.

Since the door was closed, the guard probably didn’t think Rei was inside this room.

Eventually, perhaps hearing Rei’s voice, the door opened and one of the guards I’d seen earlier poked his head in.

“Is it here!?”

Rei raised his hand to signal back to the guard.

“Yeah, this is it. …The guy I was fighting with you guys a moment ago ran into this room, but I can’t find him anywhere. He doesn’t seem to have gone out the window, so I’m guessing there’s a secret passage or something like that somewhere in this room… Unfortunately, I’m stuck because I don’t know where it is. Do you have any idea?”

At Rei’s words, the three guards who had entered the room looked around.

They seemed to be looking for some kind of clue, but simply looking around wasn’t going to turn anything up.

So, for the time being, the guards split up to search the room.

“What should I do?”

“Rei, keep watch on the surroundings. If we get ambushed suddenly like before, it’ll be hard for us to respond.”

“But as long as Rei’s here, they probably won’t attack, right? …Though maybe it’s just a precaution.”

“True, but it’s just a precaution. Since we don’t know what they’re thinking, we can’t rule out the possibility that they might actually attack even in a situation like this. …It’s a real pain, though.”

I’m not sure if “ordinary” is the right word to describe them, but if they were men or women—or at least of a certain age—I wouldn’t have been this worried.

But in this case, the opponent was still very small.

Naturally, despite that small stature, the fact that it wields a longsword meant for adults with ease makes it clear that this is not what it appears to be.

What’s more, upon seeing Rei, they must have judged that they couldn’t win, as they immediately retreated—a sign of remarkable judgment.

(The fact that something like that exists here makes it clear that something’s definitely off about this mansion. The real question is whether it was that same creature that pinned the guy who attacked me to the wall with a stake earlier. …I wonder how that fits into the picture.)

Despite his doubts, Rei stood in front of the door, ready to react at a moment’s notice.

Rei suspects that the person isn’t hiding somewhere, but has likely already left the vicinity of this room using some sort of secret passage.

Still, perhaps he was acting just in case—for the absolute worst-case scenario.

Another reason Rei is standing in front of the door is to prevent the intruder from escaping the room while completely erasing their presence by some means.

About five minutes passed…

“I found it! I found it! I don’t know if it’s a secret passage or not, but there’s definitely something on the other side!”

One of the guards suddenly shouted that.

Hearing that, the other guards and Rei’s group gathered at the spot.

Then, just to be sure, another guard checked the spot where they said something was... and eventually nodded.

“It’s definitely there. There’s definitely some kind of space on the other side of this wall. …That said, the question is how to open it…”

The guard paused there, then looked around again.

It was certain there was some kind of mechanism, but he had no idea how to activate it.

It was certain that finding the mechanism would take more time… and capturing the girl who had attacked them would become virtually impossible—though it was already nearly hopeless at this point.

In that case, he thought.

The guard made his decision.

“Rei, destroy this wall. Since we can’t afford to waste time looking for the mechanism, this is the quickest way.”

“...Are you sure?”

Those words came as a surprise even to Rei.

To begin with, the fact that they were forcibly searching this mansion due to this incident was already a highly dangerous situation.

On top of that, if they were to go so far as to destroy the mansion’s walls, there was a high probability they would face harsher punishment than anyone else.

But… in response to Rei’s words, the guard nodded as if it were no problem at all.

“Yeah, it’s fine. To begin with, I was prepared to face punishment from higher-ups the moment I set foot here. If I take the punishment and it means we can do something about the Kobold incident… no, about the people who’re summoning monsters to Gilm, then I don’t mind.”

“Just so you know, I’m the one who’s going to break this wall.”

“I’ll make it look like I forced Rei to do this at my request. …So, go ahead and break down the wall.”

Faced with that, Rei couldn’t very well refuse, so he nodded in agreement.

After confirming that the guards had all stepped back, he pulled the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring.

Showing no signs of nervousness, he swung the Death Scythe with lightning speed.

That single strike, which sliced through the wall without any resistance, would have been hard to make sense of if one didn’t know Rei.

But to the guards, who knew Rei’s capabilities, the fact that the wall didn’t shatter immediately upon being struck by the Death Scythe didn’t raise any particular questions… In fact, when Rei lightly tapped the floor with the pommel of the Death Scythe, the impact caused the wall to split into several pieces and fall to the floor.

All that remained after the wall was severed were the debris scattered across the floor... and the staircase leading down to the basement.

It was now an undeniable fact that the girl from earlier had escaped via these stairs.

“Stairs, huh. Well, I suppose that makes sense. …So, what do we do? Should we head down there?”

It’s a solid lead.

Although he didn’t say it out loud, it was clear that Rei wanted to say that.

At those words, the guard hesitated.

No, there’s no doubt that some kind of clue lies at the top of these stairs. There’s no doubt about that… but what awaits us there is undoubtedly that girl from earlier.

Despite being just a young girl, she’s a skilled fighter capable of holding her own against three guards.

It would be extremely dangerous to proceed without Rei.

However, there were surely still many other clues left in this mansion, and considering that, the thought of taking Rei beyond these stairs due to their own inexperience filled him with a sense of frustration.

Still, as long as there was a clear possibility that clues remained, there was something that took precedence over the guards’ pride…

“Please, Rei. Come with us.”

At the guard’s words, Rei nodded without hesitation.

“Alright. I’m fine with that. Anyway, it’s impossible to search the entire mansion right away. So, we should start by looking for places where we’re sure there are clues.”

“...Thanks.”

The guard expressed his gratitude for Rei’s words, but Rei shook his head, telling him not to worry, and sheathed his Death Scythe in its Misty Ring.

“Come on, let’s get moving. I don’t know what lies at the end of these stairs, but there’s a good chance they’re waiting for us there. Be careful.”

Saying that, Rei took the lead and began descending the stairs.

The only thing that surprised him a little after they started descending was how dark the stairs were.

Then again, since these were stairs prepared for a secret room, it would be impractical to keep them lit at all times.

Or, if the people using this mansion were nobles, they could have used their wealth to buy magical items for light or prepare lamps… but unfortunately, the people using this mansion aren’t nobles.

...No, to be precise, it would be more accurate to say that they are believed not to be nobles.

“Wait a second. It’s dark. Can you see anything, Rei?”

Since they’d just stepped from a brightly lit room onto a dark staircase, it was only natural for the guard to look bewildered.

But since Rei has good night vision to begin with, he can see down the stairs without any trouble in this level of darkness.

“Given the location, the staircase itself probably isn’t that long, so couldn’t we manage without a light?”

“Don’t be ridiculous. I’m not Rei.”

In response to the guard’s remark, Rei handed him a lamp from his Misty Ring—one he’d either bought or stolen from a bandit.
