"GRRRRRRRR!"

As Set's roar echoed through the area, his front paw came crashing down.

With the sound of shattering bone, the goblin's head literally scattered into pieces of flesh around the area.

Bone fragments, brain matter, eyeballs... all scattered together around him, yet Set looked slightly dissatisfied.

Last night, his Power Crush had leveled up using the Orc Leader's magic stone.

When goblins appeared on their way to the dungeon, Set seized the chance to use the skill... but since they were just goblins, he couldn't really tell the difference in power compared to a normal swipe of his front paw.

Watching this from the window, Rei sighed with a complicated expression.

(Power Crush reaching level five should definitely mean its power has skyrocketed. But against something like a goblin, it can barely show off that power at all.)

Considering that alone, Set should have been happy to have acquired such a powerful skill.

Yet, the reason for his complicated expression was that Set was effortlessly mastering the newly leveled-up Power Crush.

Rei, who used a similar skill called Power Slash, still couldn't be said to have fully... mastered it with complete freedom.

"Goblins, huh... They attack normally even with Set around. Just like last time."

Watching Set wreaking havoc from the carriage window, Elena murmured.

"Hey, Elena. Could you drop the goblin talk? It just reminds me of yesterday."

Marina frowned, likely recalling the taste of the goblin meat they'd eaten last night. She sipped her tea with a displeased expression.

Though Marina had once been an adventurer, she'd spent most of her time as a guildmaster since then. She probably never had the chance to eat goblin meat.

That's precisely why she suffered so much.

"Perhaps we should have Vihera try some goblin meat once she regains consciousness?"

Marina's gaze fell on Vihera, who lay on the sofa.

Her condition hadn't changed at all since yesterday; to an outsider, she simply looked like she was sleeping.

"Heh heh, you're right. It would be a bit unfair for only us to eat it. In that case, we should have Vihera eat some too."

Elena said with a smile, and Rei, who had been watching Set rampaging outside, joined in on Elena and Marina's conversation.

"Well then, we'll have to prepare some more stimulating goblin meat for that occasion."

Using "exciting" instead of "delicious" here was probably Rei's mean streak.

But both Elena and Marina responded to Rei's words with beautiful smiles of agreement.

To an outsider, it would likely appear as nothing more than two beautiful women sharing an elegant smile.

It was impossible to imagine that while the sleeping Vihera lay there, they were scheming mischief... mischief that was far from ordinary and hardly a joke.

The fact they could even discuss such things stemmed from everyone present believing Vihera would regain consciousness.

Despite facing a Rank S monster in the dungeon, no one seemed afraid.

They believed in their own strength and trusted their allies' power.

Rei, Elena, Marina, Ara, Elk, Min. And then there's Set and Yellow.

By any normal measure, assembling this much firepower is extremely rare.

...In this case, perhaps what's truly astonishing is the network of connections Rei possesses that allowed him to assemble such a force.

"A Silver Lion, huh. I heard slashes and magic barely affect it. The question is how to break through that and deal damage... Honestly, when facing enemies like this, Vihera is the most reliable."

Vihera's skill, the Infused Palm, delivers a direct shock through the opponent's body—whether it's armor, leather, fur... whatever it may be.

Against the Silver Lion we were scheduled to fight in a few days, it was undoubtedly an extremely reliable skill.

But right now, Vihera, the one who could use Immersion Palm, was unconscious.

That's precisely why we're heading into this dungeon—to save Vihera's consciousness.

"Elk's Axe of the Thunder God packs tremendous power in a single strike. As for me, while Mirage is only useful for holding enemies back, I have Dragon Language Magic. And if we're talking pure striking power, Ara is also quite formidable."

Rei nodded at Elena's words, which sounded like a confirmation, and continued.

"My Twilight Spear might not be very effective either, but its ability to pull enemies back to me should allow me to keep them at bay. Death Scythe has Power Slash, so I can probably count on that. And then there's Set too."

His griffin physical prowess, the magic item Power Bracelet, and the leveled-up Power Crush after absorbing the Orc Leader's magic stone.

Ideally, Set's strongest strike would be a straight plunge from high altitude, utilizing its speed for a devastating blow against a single target. But this is a dungeon, so that's out of the question.

Still, he should be able to deliver a sufficiently reliable strike.

"I suppose... I'll mainly focus on support. My bow seems almost useless here. Or maybe I could use spirit magic to create earth... no, the dungeon's lowest level wasn't earth. Could I make a stone spear attack work?"

"I wonder. It seems like it might work."

Rei replied to Marina's words.

Against the Silver Lion, which was largely immune to magic, would a strike using stone or earth conjured by elemental magic actually work? Even Rei couldn't predict that.

Still, he had a vague feeling it might have some effect... but that was purely based on intuition.

Listening to Rei and Marina's conversation, Elena spoke up while sipping her tea.

"Should we have brought more people?"

But she immediately shook her head, as if refuting her own suggestion.

"No. When facing an S-rank monster, a certain level of strength is essential. Besides, hiring someone completely unrelated would naturally require payment, wouldn't it?"

Those invited would naturally demand appropriate compensation for fighting an S-rank monster.

Among those demands, they might naturally ask for the most valuable magic stones among the materials, or they might want the heart that Rei and the others absolutely needed.

If someone would take the job for money alone, Rei would welcome them with open arms. But if they possessed the strength to be counted as combat power against an S-rank monster, they probably wouldn't be struggling for money anyway.

After all, they could easily earn money by completing the request alone.

For such individuals, what they truly covet would undoubtedly be the materials from an S-rank monster.

If they weren't careful, fighting over the materials after defeating the Silver Lion could spark another battle.

(Can't just let them get involved that easily, huh?)

Considering the kind of fight that could erupt between those capable of taking on an S-rank monster, it was clear it would be nothing short of a bloody mess.

"That's right. Plus, if too many people join, it'll be harder to maneuver during the fight."

Rei and Marina both nodded at Elena's words.

The members originally planning to fight the Silver Lion were mostly front-line fighters.

While more rear guards might be useful, Rei understood they didn't need any more front-line fighters.

(But then again, adding new rear guards would pose ability issues too.)

Since bow attacks were nearly useless against the Silver Lion, what was truly needed in the rear guard was a mage.

Of course, the Silver Lion's fur possessed high magical defense that blocked spells, but magic could still be used for support beyond just attacking.

Rei often encountered various mages, but among adventurers overall, mages were extremely rare.

It was precisely because Gilm attracted the most skilled adventurers that it had more mages than other towns or villages.

"If Lord Grimm would cooperate, defeating the Silver Lion would be a breeze, wouldn't it?"

Marina stated, as if it was a long shot anyway.

Grim is friendly towards humans... or more accurately, towards Rei and those around him. Still, he is an undead monster... and a powerful one at that, known as a Lich Lord.

Even if he was once human, his motives and sense of ethics differ greatly from those of humans.

Given that it's Grimm, the fact he's even helping restore Vihera's consciousness could be considered an extraordinary gesture.

And that generosity was likely only possible because Rei was involved.

"This must be a trial, I suppose."

Elena's muttered voice echoed inside the carriage.

"The Silver Lion... I've heard they're troublesome opponents, but I never thought I'd end up fighting them myself."

Elk murmured from the driver's seat of the carriage near Rei's.

Hearing the voice, Min replied from inside the carriage.

"But thanks to that Silver Lion, Rodos might awaken, right?"

"Yeah."

Ever since Rodos lost consciousness, they had tried everything to bring him back.

But despite exhausting every resource—magic items obtained through great effort and skilled healers—Rodos never regained consciousness.

After nearly a year of this relentless search, Elk and Min had often entertained the thought that perhaps...

It was during this time that Marina proposed the matter of the Silver Lion.

When Elk heard the plan to enlist the aid of a powerful monster like a Lich Lord—a creature potentially rivaling dragonkin—he honestly questioned Marina's sanity.

But the fact remained that they had no other options. Seeing it as their last resort, they decided to accept Marina's invitation.

"How's the situation in Rhodes?"

Even traveling by carriage, there probably wouldn't be any noticeable changes.

Even understanding that, Elk asked Min.

"Same as always."

Hearing Elk's voice from inside the carriage, Min turned his gaze toward Rhodes, who lay resting, and replied.

There lay Rodos, his muscles significantly more atrophied than before.

Still, his muscles weren't as atrophied as someone in a normal unconscious state.

This was due to the effect of a magic item Elk and Min had purchased.

It was an extremely expensive magic item, but one that Elk, a renowned Rank A adventurer, could afford.

Thanks to that magic item, while he was considerably thinner than before, his physical decline was still slower than normal.

Min gently stroked her son's cheek.

"I see."

Elk nodded in response to Min's words, then refocused his attention on driving the carriage.

That said, the horses pulling this carriage were exceptionally intelligent, as befitting their high price.

It didn't need Elk's instructions to simply follow Elena's carriage ahead.

Only goblins, incapable of comprehending Set's strength, dared to attack, and naturally, those goblins were killed by Set.

Until they reached the dungeon, there really was nothing else to do right now.

(Well, once we reach the location of the dungeon, there will still be things to do.)

Ideally, once they reached the dungeon, they wanted to challenge the Silver Lion immediately.

But the truth was, there were various things that needed to be done before that.

First, secure a room at an inn to use as a base.

Of course, once Elk and the others enter the dungeon, they'll have Grimm teleport them directly to the lowest level, so honestly, they don't need to explore the dungeon themselves.

Still, considering what might happen, securing lodging was essential.

Depending on what time they arrived, there was a possibility they might stay the night and challenge the dungeon the next morning.

Beyond that, there was the matter of storing the carriage and caring for the horses.

Elena and Elk's carriage, along with the horses pulling it, were extremely valuable. If they didn't stay at a high-class inn with strict security, there was a risk of theft.

At worst, the carriage could be stored in Rei's Misty Ring, but the horses couldn't be put inside the Misty Ring.

Furthermore, since Guildmaster Marina was attempting the dungeon, it was necessary to stop by the branch office.

Consumables had already been purchased in Gilm, so there was no particular need to buy anything.

With these thoughts in mind, Elk kept the carriage moving forward.
