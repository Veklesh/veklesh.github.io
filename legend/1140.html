Near where Rei and Resonance clashed, Zarust and Narsus... and Set too were locked in battle with the assassins.

The assassins fighting the two of them didn't possess the same extraordinary physical abilities as Resonance.

But as expected of those who lived as assassins, their strength was far from easily overcome.

That said, they were opponents strong enough to give Narsus—and even Zarust, who was slightly less skilled—some trouble.

(Considering they were said to be the Hundred-Faced Ones, existing secretly for so long, they weren't as strong as expected. No, one could even say they were weak. Their lack of pain perception was troublesome, though...)

Slashing the assassin diagonally with a single stroke of his longsword, Narsus watched the man fall dead to the ground before any pain could register. A split second later, he dodged the spear thrust launched by another assassin who had attacked in the ensuing chaos, and a question formed in his mind.

Their attacks were sharp, yet somehow predictable.

While pondering the assassins' behavior... he suddenly noticed someone aiming a bow at him and used a nearby man raising a battle axe as a shield.

"Guh!"

The sound of an arrow piercing flesh was followed by a gasp of surprise from the man used as a shield.

Despite the arrow piercing his back, it was shock, not pain, that made him cry out.

(Still, not feeling pain... that's troublesome.)

He severed both arms holding the battle axe with a single strike of his longsword, then met the next attacker head-on.

Near Narsus, Zarust was also swinging his longsword, fighting the assassins.

Though Zarust wasn't as effortless as Narsus, he still held his own against the assassins attacking him, fighting at least evenly.

He dodged a thrusting spear strike, sweeping his blade across the attacker's torso as he passed by.

Perhaps prioritizing agility, or perhaps caught off guard by Rei and the others' unexpected arrival, few assassins wore proper armor.

Most wore only slightly sturdier clothing, with a handful equipped with simple leather armor.

Considering the woman in leather armor has the highest defense, you can gauge just how inadequate their protection is.

Even so, an opponent who feels no pain is undeniably troublesome.

Even now, despite having been slashed horizontally across the abdomen, they immediately grabbed their weapons and charged at me again.

Even more troublesome than that, however, was the ground.

The blood spilled by the assassins was quickly absorbed by the sand and vanished, posing no problem. However, flesh fragments and bits of entrails weren't absorbed by the sand.

Worse still, I'd stumbled several times, losing my balance when I stepped on sand that had covered a piece of intestine, obscuring its presence.

And when I did step on them, bodily fluids and bits of flesh would stick to my foot, hindering my movement for a short while.

If it were daytime, I might have been able to see where the flesh and organs were, but unfortunately, it was night.

Moonlight and starlight were present, but they weren't nearly as bright as daylight.

That was precisely why Zarust was fighting while moving.

Narsus was doing the same. As the two fought their way forward, they eventually converged, standing back-to-back to face the assassins surrounding them.

"How's it going over there?"

At Zarust's question, Narsus gave a small smile before speaking.

"Opponents of this caliber pose no particular problem. ...Though it is a bit troublesome that they survive even after receiving fatal wounds... Still, if you strike them in the neck, they won't be able to move any further."

"Exactly. If they can move after their neck's severed, they're undead."

They exchanged a few brief words, their faces and bodies splattered with several spots of blood spurting from the assassins' bodies.

The way they even managed to smile made it hard to believe they were surrounded by assassins.

On the contrary, they seemed to believe they held the advantage...

The reason they could fight on without despair, despite being clearly outnumbered... that reason lay a short distance away.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set shed his usual adorable demeanor, letting out a ferocious growl as he swung his front paw.

It was a single strike, nothing more than a swing of his front paw, no skill involved.

Yet, combined with the magical item, the Bracelet of Might, and the physical prowess of a rank A monster like a Griffin, it became a fatal blow to a human.

If it struck the head, it would crush it and kill instantly. If one tried to block it with a weapon, the weapon would shatter or snap.

Worse still, it was not uncommon for the bones of the arm wielding the weapon to be shattered.

A body slam would shatter every bone in the body while sending the victim flying, often dragging allies along as they rolled across the ground.

Unlucky assassins were sometimes grabbed by the beak and swung around, their own bodies becoming weapons that attacked their comrades.

After being swung around to their heart's content as weapons, limbs would finally snap from the force of Set's flailing, flying off to strike allies and halt their movement.

This method of defeating enemies, relying purely on the luck of the draw, seemed to be Set at his best.

And Set did this without using any skills, purely through close combat.

Even those who felt no pain weren't immortal.

Destroy their heads and they die; break their limbs and their movements slow.

Moreover, the greatest advantage of the Hundred-Faced Ones is their ability to peel off their facial skin and take on anyone's appearance.

While this posed a significant threat if targeted in daily life, facing them head-on like this meant they were by no means unbeatable.

Even so, if they had known an attack was coming, they could have disguised themselves as someone the enemy knew.

But in this case, the attackers had invaded their village on the very same day they assaulted Rei and the others.

There was no time to prepare any countermeasures.

Even so, the assassins, seemingly unaffected by pain, desperately launched attacks against those who had come to assault them.

Their numbers gradually dwindled under the relentless blows of Zarust, Narsus, and Set.

If this continued, they could win.

Zalust held that thought... dodging a long sword strike that seemed to seize that opening, he countered with a flash of his own blade, severing the opponent's wrist.

The opponent's right wrist, along with the longsword, fell onto the sand. Yet the severed hand showed no sign of pain whatsoever as the remaining left hand picked up the fallen blade.

Then, he hurled what had been his right hand mere seconds ago at Zarust, using it as cover while swinging his longsword.

"Don't think that'll do anything!"

The right wrist struck him, and blood flowing from it splattered onto his face.

But Zarust, unconcerned, dodged the long sword swing aimed at him and, unlike before, severed the head.

He felt no pain, but blood poured from his severed neck. Without breath, he could do nothing, and death would come soon enough.

He kicked the man, who already faced only death, sending him flying into the dagger-wielder who had tried to catch him off guard.

At that very instant. A thunderous roar—not heard with the ears, but felt as a shockwave throughout the entire body—echoed throughout the surrounding area.

The sudden sound caused Zarust, Narsus... even the assassins engaged in combat to freeze for an instant.

Of course, the pause lasted only an instant before the fighting resumed.

Dodge the rapid succession of spear thrusts and evade the arrows flying in as support, Zarust turned his gaze toward the source of the sound.

What met his eyes was Resonance, somehow managing to catch the iron ball he had just been wielding.

While Zarust and Narsus fought the assassins who felt no pain, Rei was naturally engaged in battle with Resonance.

The chain whip in Resonance's hand was already nearly half its original length.

Though wielded with the brute strength needed to lift a one-meter iron ball, its tip was severed each time by Rei's Death Scythe.

"You're quite something, aren't you? ...I hear you have a nickname. That makes perfect sense now."

"Huh. You've done your homework on me, haven't you?"

"Of course. After all, you're the only adventurer out there with a griffin as a familiar. It wasn't hard to find out."

Forcing his muscles to stem the blood flowing from several wounds, Resonance even managed a smile.

Naturally, such actions made normal movement impossible, yet Resonance managed it through sheer physical strength... and by forcing his body to move despite the pain he felt.

"And yet, here you are, fighting me. If you surrendered quietly, I might consider treating you accordingly?"

Though he said this, ultimately, Rei was an outsider in this matter, merely an ally.

His strength, his reputation, and the presence of Set meant he commanded respect, but he was still an outsider.

Considering that, even if Rei asked Augusto or Daldora to help Resonance and the others, his opinion wouldn't necessarily be accepted outright.

It's true he does hold some influence, so it's not impossible they might consider that.

(If that doesn't work, maybe bringing them back to Gilm would be an option. Lord Daskar, who made the Grassland Wolves his subordinates, could easily use these guys as spies instead of assassins. If that fails, ask Marina to get them into the guild's intelligence division or something.)

Considering what's best for Gilm, that might actually be the better idea.

That's what Rei thought, but Resonance shook his head with a smile playing on his lips.

"Unfortunately, we won't serve under anyone. Besides, the only place we can call our base is this Ferris Desert. ...No, I've said too much."

Seeing Resonance's wry smile at having said too much, the numerous bits of information Rei had gathered since arriving at Gosh began to connect in his mind.

The Felis Desert, Augusto, Daldora, the Wall. ...And Resonance's words about only being able to operate in this desert.

"The Tira Tree...?"

The words that escaped Rei's lips were the very cause of the conflict between Daldora and Augusto.

If it could only operate in this desert, then it must need something found only here.

That conclusion, reached after pondering, was the word spoken. Resonance's expression confirmed it was correct.

"Looks like I was right after all."

Rei asked, reading Resonance's expression, but what came back was only Resonance's wry smile.

"I wonder. I've said it many times before: if you want to know, you'll have to defeat me. Then I'll tell you."

Hearing this, Rei concluded that no matter what he asked, he'd just be evaded. He immediately moved to attack.

However, he judged that merely inflicting minor wounds would be completely ineffective... well, effective, but it would take far too long for them to incapacitate him. Therefore, he opted for a slightly more extreme method.

Using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour would guarantee victory, but employing it carried a high risk of simply incinerating the enemy without inflicting any pain.

That's precisely why the method chosen was...

"I see. Then, I'll make you do it!"

Shouting briefly, Rei ran. ...However, Rei didn't head towards Resonance, but towards a spot a little further away where an iron ball lay.

Resonance, likely intrigued by what he intended to do, neither interfered with Rei's actions nor tried to stop him, merely watching.

Reaching the iron ball, Rei touched it and stored it in his Misty Ring.

"Hmm."

Resonance watched with apparent admiration.

He knew Rei possessed an item box, but while he might have expected it to hold weapons or similar items, he certainly hadn't imagined it could store even his own iron ball.

Then, Rei's next action was to close the distance between himself and Resonance once more.

Resonance, chain whip in hand, waited for Rei.

He stowed the Death Scythe in the Misty Ring and drew a spear next.

However, it was neither the Thorn Spear nor the Twilight Spear, but the spear Rei had been using until now—one that looked like it could break at any moment.

He charged toward Resonance, hurling the spear as he ran.

Naturally, Resonance wasn't about to just stand there and take such an attack.

But Rei kept throwing spear after spear... until several of them pierced through Resonance's legs, arms, and even her torso.

Under normal circumstances, it would have screamed in pain, but Resonance felt no pain.

Still, with multiple spears piercing its body, it becomes unable to move.

And just as it became immobilized... Rei arrived before Resonance and leapt.

Activating Sleipnir's Boots, he leapt through the air, one step, two steps, three steps.

Arriving about ten meters above Resonance... he drew a one-meter diameter iron ball from the Misty Ring, which he had just stored away moments before... and released it.

"Don't die,"

he murmurs.

The iron ball fell, naturally heading straight for the immobilized Resonance... Yet somehow, he managed to prevent it from hitting his head and caught it. That was likely only possible because he was the one leading the Hundred-Faced Ones.

Even so, he couldn't withstand the iron ball's weight... and being crushed was unavoidable.
