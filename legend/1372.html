That day, when the woodcutters returned from work and received their payment, it was an unbelievable amount.

Three gold coins.

Under normal circumstances, this was an amount that could never be imagined as a woodcutter's daily earnings.

No, even among woodcutters, those who were truly exceptional—those capable of felling special trees like the Mizuki or Kazuki—might consider this reward not just normal, but even a bit low.

But the woodcutters working in Treant Forest were all ordinary woodcutters.

Given that, receiving such a reward was completely unexpected.

Of course, there were several reasons for this.

First off, woodcutting work is paid by the job.

Furthermore, the most arduous task for a woodcutter—transporting felled trees—was handled by Rei, who carried an item box, resulting in almost no time lag.

Furthermore, the fact that the work was in Treant Forest meant a significant hazard pay component.

This is a forest where, as far as anyone knows, two adventurer parties have been wiped out. ...Though Slesha somehow managed to survive.

Those who have lived as woodcutters for a reasonably long time understand this.

Precisely because they understood, they focused solely on completing the tasks assigned to them, avoiding anything unnecessary.

But... for the young woodcutter who received a reward beyond his wildest dreams, Treant Forest was nothing short of a treasure trove.

Of course, they'd been warned beforehand that it was a dangerous place.

But the fact remained that while they were working, there was no danger whatsoever.

What's more, even the adventurers who were supposed to be their escorts had chopped wood alongside them and received their share of the reward.

Given their pride as woodcutters, they harbored some resentment toward those adventurers.

Among these young woodcutters, one suddenly realized something.

"Shouldn't we make as much money as we can while we can? The trees the adventurers cut down were ones we should have been cutting and getting paid for."

They knew the peculiarity of logging in Treant Forest—that the forest expanded almost daily.

Logically, since forests naturally expand, there shouldn't be much worry about running out of trees to cut... but among these woodcutters, there was resentment and jealousy toward the adventurers.

Originally, this woodcutter named Fekts was born and raised in Gilm.

And having grown up in Gilm, the very heartland of adventurers, it was only natural he aspired to be one himself.

But... around the time Fekts reached his mid-teens and was about to become an adventurer... his father, who worked as a woodcutter, died of illness.

This left his mother, sister, and younger brother struggling to make ends meet.

Adventurers, especially low-ranked ones, barely earn enough to support themselves.

Had Fekts insisted on becoming an adventurer then, his family would have struggled to survive.

It was to the point where, unless his mother had resorted to prostitution, they would likely have starved to death.

In such circumstances, Fekts couldn't insist on becoming an adventurer out of sheer stubbornness. His only choice was to become a woodcutter.

Even new woodcutters don't earn particularly high wages, but their pay is still significantly more than that of low-rank adventurers.

At least enough to keep his younger brother and sister, still practically infants, from starving.

And so... Fekts began his life as a woodcutter.

As it turned out, perhaps inheriting his father's talent for woodcutting, Fekts began to stand out even among the younger woodcutters.

By now, he was regarded as one of the influential figures among the young woodcutters.

Given that such words came from such a figure, it was only natural that the young woodcutters who idolized Fekts agreed with his opinion.

In truth, many woodcutters had reservations about the fact that he had come as an escort yet did no real work, instead mimicking them by cutting down trees.

Moreover, since he wasn't a professional woodcutter, the cuts on the felled trees were often sloppy. Many didn't pay particular attention to the direction the trees fell, resulting in logs tumbling haphazardly. To Fekts' eyes, it was utterly unacceptable.

Admittedly, the adventurers didn't think they could fell trees as skillfully as the woodcutters, so they worked at a distance to avoid causing trouble.

To Fekts, however, even that consideration seemed suspect.

To cut trees like that and still expect to be paid for guarding... Fekts simply couldn't make sense of the adventurers' actions.

And seeing one adventurer abandon his escort duties to go off alone with a woman deep into the forest only made his dissatisfaction grow stronger.

...In reality, Runo and Slesha were investigating various aspects of Treant Forest, but Fekts wasn't privy to those details... He didn't know Slesha was a survivor of a party wiped out in Treant Forest.

That's precisely why, to Fekts, Runo and the others seemed to be nothing but flirting, despite having accepted the escort request.

Considering Fekts' circumstances and personality, it wasn't strange that he didn't want to let such adventurers profit so easily.

"Alright, let's go."

Fekts muttered, and the others beside him nodded in response.

Those accompanying Fekts included those who admired him, those desperate for money, those who simply disliked adventurers... and others with various other motives.

Such people now headed toward the main gate with axes in hand, alongside Fekts.

The sun had already begun to set, its rays painting the surroundings red.

There was a hint of spring warmth, but even at this time of year, nights were still bitterly cold.

Though a chill had set in, Fekts and his group, fueled by the excitement of outmaneuvering the adventurers, walked without feeling the cold.

Upon reaching the main gate, they address the guards to complete the procedures for leaving the city.

This was the first gate.

The gates would close soon.

Given that, it was only natural the guards were concerned about the Fekts leaving the city at this hour.

"Huh? Fekts? What about the others?"

"Ah, I forgot something at the workshop. So I figured I'd come back and get it."

"Whoa... Can't you just put that off until tomorrow? There's not much time left before the main gate closes!"

"I know. But it's pretty important stuff. I don't want it left out overnight and messed with by monsters or animals."

"...Alright. But be careful, okay?"

The fact that he was merely being concerned about, rather than suspected, was likely because he'd become familiar with the guards through his work as a woodcutter.

Fekts gave a light wave to the familiar guard and proceeded with the formalities.

"Still, are you really taking over ten people with you?"

"Well, just in case something happens. I mean, I can't exactly hire adventurers just to go to my workplace and come back, right?"

"True."

Knowing that Fekts's family was far from wealthy, the guard's words were hard to argue with.

He had considered accompanying them himself, having received some combat training, but as the gatekeeper, he couldn't leave his post.

"Be really careful, okay?"

"Yeah, of course. Besides, with all of us together, we can handle goblins easily. Don't worry."

"...But I'm worried because other monsters besides goblins might appear too."

Waving lightly to the worried guard, Fekts completed the procedures to leave Gilm and led the other woodcutters out through the main gate.

The guard, still on duty, could only silently watch their retreating figures.

"Listen up. Tonight... well, until tomorrow morning, we'll be cutting down trees nonstop. We're going to make sure those adventurers don't set foot in our territory."

"Got it."

The other woodcutters nodded in response to Fekts' words.

They had already left the road and were heading toward Treant Forest.

It wasn't particularly unusual for woodcutters to be out at this hour.

After all, it wasn't uncommon for them to return to Gilm just before the gates closed after a day of logging.

Still, heading to their workplace at this hour was a first even for Fekts.

"Just in case, be ready to call out if anything happens. ...Well, considering how today's adventurers behaved, I doubt we'll need to worry about that."

"Yeah. Honestly, those adventurers were hired as guards, yet they didn't do a single guard's job. Might as well have just hired woodcutters from the start."

A woodcutter, who was deeply devoted to Fekts, voiced his dissatisfaction with the adventurers.

The others likely felt the same. They walked on, each muttering their own thoughts.

And so, just as the sun was about to set... Fekts and the others arrived at Treant Forest.

"Hmm..."

Fekts murmurs this as he casually looks around.

It wasn't a word with any particular meaning.

But still, those words slipped out of Fekts' mouth.

"What shall we do, Fekts?"

One of the woodcutters nearby asked Fekts.

Each of them had brought a full set of woodcutter's gear.

He had wondered if carrying an axe might arouse suspicion, but perhaps because they were acquaintances, the guards showed no particular sign of mistrust.

Then again, going outside Gilm without any guards, carrying weapons was probably only natural.

"There's no time to waste. Let's get started right away. Listen up, I don't expect anything to happen, but if anything does, shout to alert everyone immediately."

Normally, for a mere woodcutter who wasn't an adventurer to keep cutting trees all night long would be tantamount to suicide.

But during the day, while working in Treant Forest, he hadn't seen a single monster, let alone an animal.

Given that, he assumed monsters wouldn't appear at night either.

Under normal circumstances, Fekts wouldn't have fallen into such a mindset.

Still, the main reason he was acting this way now was undoubtedly jealousy over the adventurers he'd seen during the day.

An adventurer—something he himself could never become.

Even without that adventurer, they could cut down trees just fine.

He wanted to prove it. When his fellow woodcutters and the adventurers arrived tomorrow, he would say this:

"Adventurers? Makes no difference whether they're here or not."

he thought.

Just imagining the expressions on the adventurers' faces when he said that brought a delighted smile to Fekts' face.

...Fekts had heard the story of the party being wiped out in this Treant Forest.

But since it was an adventurer party that was wiped out, and the information came from the Adventurer's Guild, he couldn't bring himself to believe it outright.

He couldn't help but suspect they'd fabricated the story to boost adventurers' value, even if just a little.

He understood this was nothing more than petty suspicion, yet he couldn't help but think that way.

Watching his companions prepare to fell the trees of the Treant Forest one after another, following his orders, a fleeting thought crossed Fekts' mind: Is this really right?

But he shook his head immediately, telling himself this choice was the best one... and then he too began preparing to fell the trees.

It was like... like turning away from something unpleasant.

Even so, his skill was sharp, honed from years of life as a woodcutter.

As Fekts had said, his efficiency was incomparable to the logging work done by adventurers.

"Watch out, it's falling!"

He swung his axe, controlling the direction of the fall to bring the tree down outward.

Without needing to bother with things like removing branches, Fekts and the others felled tree after tree.

The speed... well, some might have reservations about it... but it was undeniably fast.

But working in the already fading light made the task inevitably more difficult.

"Lighting the fire! Watch out it doesn't spread to the forest!"

At Fekts' words, everyone responded in turn and returned to their work.

Making a bonfire in the forest was dangerous in many ways... but fortunately, Fekts had the confidence that his experience as a woodcutter would allow him to handle it.

Besides, as lumberjacks, they were all strong individuals.

Even if animals, monsters, or worse yet, bandits attacked upon seeing the light, they were confident they could handle it.

Drawing strength from that confidence... Fekts and the others continued their logging work deep in the forest.
