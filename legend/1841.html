The party begins with Liberte’s opening remarks, and everyone starts mingling.

For the nobility, this gathering is a party—and yet it is not a party.

To be precise, while it is a party, it is also a political arena.

They speak with people they rarely get to meet and gather information.

In some cases, it is not uncommon for business deals to be struck.

For that very reason, there weren’t that many people simply enjoying the party; rather, everyone was maneuvering to maximize their own interests.

However, it was almost exclusively the nobles who were maneuvering in this way, and in that sense, Rei—who was not a noble—didn’t need to worry about such things…

“Hey, Rei. The food here is as delicious as ever.”

As he was savoring the food with Elena, he was suddenly addressed by that voice.

As for whose voice it was, that was obvious to Rei without even having to think about it.

After all, it was someone he had been with just a few hours ago.

“That’s true. But considering Brewitt’s build, maybe you shouldn’t eat too much?”

Rei replied, glancing at Brewitt’s massive frame.

A short distance away, many nobles wore expressions that said, “He beat us to it!”

Naturally, there were many who had intended to speak to Elena—the daughter of Duke Kerebel and known as the “Princess General”—or to Crimson Rei.

Precisely because there were so many of them, they were all keeping an eye on each other and couldn’t bring themselves to speak… As a result, Rei and Elena were enjoying the taste of the food Georgima had prepared… but it was Brewitt who effortlessly broke that stalemate.

Normally, Brewitt would have had to play along with the nobles’ mutual war of nerves.

Things like titles, interests, and past favors.

But Brewitt, claiming such things didn’t matter, simply spoke to Rei as if it were nothing.

This is probably where Brewitt’s unconventionality as a noble comes through.

Of course, normally, ignoring conventions like this would lead to trouble later on.

Even so, Brewitt likely spoke to Rei, unconcerned by such things.

Several nobles shot Brewitt resentful glances, but he didn’t seem to mind and continued talking with Rei about the food.

“Still… looking at you two like this, I guess you’re a good match… or maybe not… it’s kind of hard to say.”

“Wait, which is it?”

Feeling slightly annoyed by Brewitt’s words, Rei retorted.

But Elena smiled at Rei and spoke up.

“Hehe, Rei. You don’t really need to worry so much about whether we’re a good match or not. After all, that’s just how others see us. What matters is how we feel about it ourselves.”

“...Now that you put it that way, I feel like I can sort of agree, but also sort of can’t—it’s a bit of a mixed feeling.”

Saying that, Rei brings a piece of roasted meat to his mouth.

As for meat, Rei usually eats high-rank monster meat, but even for him, the taste of the meat he put in his mouth was exceptional.

Since Rei isn’t particularly skilled at cooking, he didn’t know the specific steps used to prepare this dish. He didn’t know… but even so, it was clear to Rei, who was savoring the meat’s flavor, that this was definitely not just a simple roast.

It was obvious that the meat itself was of very high quality, but more than that, great care had clearly been taken with the cooking method.

“This meat is delicious.”

“Yeah.”

Brewitt nodded in agreement with Rei’s mutter, and Elena watched the two of them with an interested gaze.

As the three of them enjoyed themselves, one of the nobles who had been watching from the sidelines suddenly approached.

It was the noble who had emerged victorious from the silent standoff that had been taking place.

“Lady Elena, I look forward to another year of your company. I must say, seeing you all dressed up like this, I can’t help but be captivated.”

Elena gave a small smile in response to the nobleman’s words.

Seeing that she didn’t seem to mind, Rei, who was watching the scene from beside her, concluded that this nobleman must not be someone Elena disliked.

In fact, the nobleman even turned to Rei, who was standing beside Elena, and spoke to him with a smile.

“You must be Crimson Rei. My estate is quite far away, so unfortunately I didn’t arrive until after the mock battle, but my subordinates who watched it told me you’re quite formidable. And I hear you’ve even tamed that griffin as a familiar.”

“Hearing you say that makes all my hard work worthwhile.”

“Ah, feel free to speak more casually. Adventurers are quite active in my domain as well, so I’m used to dealing with them.”

At Rei’s words, the nobleman replied with a smile.

Normally, Rei wouldn’t use such polite language unless he were dealing with someone of clearly high status—and someone who wasn’t hostile—but he wouldn’t go out of his way to be that polite with anyone else.

But this time was different.

Since this was a party hosted by Duke Liberte of Kerebel, Elena’s father, causing a commotion here would be a direct blow to his reputation.

Rei wanted to avoid that at all costs.

He knew that Liberte was a man every bit as formidable as Daskar, and above all, he was Elena’s father.

It was only natural that he didn’t want to do anything that would bring shame upon such a man.

Moreover, Rei felt indebted to Liberte for tacitly accepting his relationship with Elena.

Of course, Rei also understood that Liberte hadn’t simply agreed to their relationship without any thought—that he hadn’t done so merely because Elena wished it.

Elena is known by the nickname “Princess General,” but she is also the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel. Naturally, it would not have been surprising if she had been arranged to marry for the sake of the Kerebel House’s prosperity, regardless of her own wishes.

Despite these circumstances, Liberte accepted the relationship between Elena and Rei.

Naturally, there were likely calculations involved—such as improving relations between the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction, or the potential benefit of being able to request Rei’s cooperation through Elena should the need arise.

The latter point in particular—the prospect of being able to request Rei’s assistance—was extremely reassuring to Liberte, who was well aware of Rei’s power.

Set and Rei. She is convinced that if this man and this beast were truly determined, they possess enough power to destroy a small country.

Considering that, acknowledging the relationship between Rei and Elena would be a major benefit for the Duke of Kerebel’s House and the Noble Faction.

...That said, this is strictly a matter of logic; many cannot accept it on an emotional level.

This is especially true among the young nobles who had their eyes on Elena; such men harbor intense jealousy toward Rei.

And naturally so. Elena is the daughter of a Duke, bears the nickname “Princess General,” and possesses a beauty one might see only once in a lifetime.

Many nobles are accustomed to women, but even such men find it difficult to maintain their usual composure in Elena’s presence.

And when she’s wearing a glamorous party dress like she is now, it’s no wonder those feelings are even stronger.

“In that case, I’ll let my guard down. Still, even though I’m an adventurer myself, isn’t it rare for a noble to interact with adventurers so often?”

“I won’t deny that. However, while our territory isn’t quite as remote as Gilm’s, we do have a deep, vast forest where a fair number of monsters dwell. Because of that, I inevitably have many opportunities to meet adventurers.”

“Huh.”

A deep, vast forest teeming with monsters.

Hearing those words, Rei—who was seeking magic stones from unknown monsters through his beast magic—felt a strong sense of interest.

The nobleman, perhaps sensing that Rei was intrigued by his words, spoke up with a hint of curiosity.

“Are you interested? If so, please come visit my domain sometime. It should be a town where adventurers can live comfortably.”

“Yeah, I’d like to visit if I get the chance… but I’m pretty busy right now, so I don’t know when I’d be able to go.”

There was a hint of disappointment in Rei’s tone as he said this.

But Rei really is very busy right now.

Although he is currently being treated as a guest of the Duke of Kerebel’s House, that is strictly a temporary arrangement.

Once this party is over, he plans to return to Gilm.

And once he returns to Gilm, the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle awaits him.

Normally, Rei wouldn’t need to be present for the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle, but given its mountain-like size, it’s best for the person in charge to be on-site in case any trouble arises.

After all, if a decision cannot be made immediately when something happens, that much time will be wasted.

Also, once spring arrives, the expansion work in Gilm will pick up again, and the members of Crimson Wings—including Rei, who possesses the Misty Ring—will become busy.

Still, knowing how tough those busy days would be for Rei and the others, they do take breaks, just like when they went to the beach in the summer.

I hadn’t explained anything specifically, but he must have understood the general situation from Rei’s expression. The nobleman looked a little disappointed, but he didn’t try to push the conversation any further.

“I see. It’s a shame, but there’s nothing to be done. Still, I’d be happy if you could visit sometime when you have the chance.”

“Yeah. I’ll be sure to do that.”

It wasn’t as if Rei was going to this noble’s estate out of his own free will.

After all, a magic stone from an unknown monster was something that piqued Rei’s curiosity as well.

Amidst all this, it wasn’t just Rei who showed interest in the nobleman’s story; Brewitt, who had been listening along, did as well.

“Hmm, that’s an interesting story. Just to be clear, am I allowed to visit Count Fanrail’s territory too?”

“Huh? Lord Brewitt, are you serious?”

The nobleman… Count Fanrail, cast a surprised glance at Brewitt.

Judging solely by his personality, one would never think so, but Brewitt is the heir apparent to the Count of Exoris.

Precisely because he knew this, Count Fanrail couldn’t exactly say he was delighted to hear that Brewitt was coming to his town.

However, Brewitt nods at Count Fanrail with a fierce grin.

“That’s right. I’ll eventually have to inherit the Exoris Count’s House, after all. Given that, I want to build up my strength as much as possible. And for that, there’s nothing better than gaining real-world combat experience.”

“That is certainly true… but, well, personally, I can’t really recommend it.”

“I see. Brewitt, you really ought to give some thought to your own position.”

The one who spoke was neither Rei nor Elena, but someone else.

Rei didn’t recognize the person, but Elena, Brewitt, and Count Fanrail surely did.

“Baron Muta, is that you?”

Count Fanrail’s tone carried a strong sense of familiarity.

Although there is a certain rank difference between a Count and a Baron, in this case, their relationship likely transcends such formalities.

“Um…”

“Ah, my apologies. I just happened to hear Brewitt saying something rather reckless, so I had to interject. It’s been a long time, Lady Elena. It’s an honor to meet you, Lord Rei. I am Baron Azuel Muta. I’ve known Count Fanrail since childhood, you see. And even now, we maintain a close, friendly relationship.”

“Rei. I’m an adventurer from Gilm… Well, after all sorts of things happened, here I am.”

“Heh, ‘all sorts of things,’ huh? That sounds like quite a challenge.”

Azuel, who laughed like that, looked to be about the same age as Fanrail—somewhere in his mid-thirties.

Of course, that’s just Rei’s impression based on appearance; in Elgin, where there are long-lived beings like the elves, one can’t really rely on a person’s looks to judge their age.

“So, Baron Muta. Why can’t I go to Count Fanrail’s territory? It’s not like it’s a big deal, is it? I’m not going to ask you to take responsibility if I get hurt or anything—I’m not going to make such unreasonable demands.”

Azuel let out a sigh of exasperation at Brewitt’s discontented words.
