Rei and Vihera had returned to the Dusk Wheat inn after hearing from Marina that magical energy anomalies were turning into monsters.

In Rei's room, the two faced each other.

That said, even though they were alone together in a man's room, they weren't discussing anything particularly suggestive.

Well, Vihera wouldn't have minded that at all—in fact, she might have preferred it... But what was happening in Rei's room now was a discussion about their actions for tomorrow and beyond.

"Magic anomalies becoming monsters, huh? You were exploring dungeons, right, Vihera? Haven't you heard anything like that before?"

Sitting on the sofa, Rei asked. Across from him, Vihera shook her head.

"No, unfortunately, I've never seen such a monster. I only learned the name 'Ambris' when Marina mentioned it. Honestly, I think it's an extremely rare case."

"I suppose so. There's no record of it in the Mireana Kingdom to begin with, and in the Bestir Empire... it seems to have only happened once, nearly three hundred years ago."

"Yes. ...And in the end, they couldn't defeat Ambris, right? That it faded away over time. ...If that's the case, even in the worst scenario, if we give it time, will it disappear too?"

That's right. The two had heard from Marina that they hadn't been able to defeat Ambris, the entity that had become a monster from the magical anomaly that appeared in the Valley of a Hundred Demons.

Even so, the situation eventually settled because, before anyone knew it, Ambris had vanished. But...

"If that's the case, the Leader Species will keep multiplying until Ambris vanishes."

At Rei's words, Vihera let out a small sigh.

Until Ambris vanished, numerous subhuman-type leader species were spawned around the Valley of a Hundred Demons.

Marina's theory was that Ambris turned monsters into leader species by offering its own body, distorted by magical energy abnormalities, to them.

After all, in the Valley of a Hundred Demons, Ambris's body had gradually shrunk over time, so it probably wasn't entirely wrong.

(Well, it's also possible that the magical anomaly composing his body simply faded over time... but it's better to prepare for the worst-case scenario, right?)

Rei didn't particularly enjoy dwelling on things too seriously either.

But there was a reason he had to.

Because Ambris differed significantly from ordinary monsters.

That single fact alone made it clear that conventional wisdom no longer applied.

"A monster without a magic stone... huh?"

That's right. Normal monsters, regardless of species, always possess magic stones.

But it was said that Ambris, specifically, did not possess a magic stone.

To begin with, its appearance was akin to black smoke, and it was unclear whether it even possessed its own will.

Back then, adventurers who first encountered Ambris desperately searched for its magic stone, but apparently, there was none to be found anywhere within its mist-like, gaseous body.

Because of that, they ultimately couldn't defeat Ambris. They just kept fighting the endless waves of leader types... After several months of this, Ambris suddenly vanished.

Given the magic stone incident, some speculated that Ambris might not be a monster at all, but rather some kind of phenomenon. However, the only confirmed sighting to date was that single specimen found in the Bestir Empire, and its detailed ecology remained completely unknown.

"It was my birthplace, and I knew about the Valley of a Hundred Demons... but I never heard that story before."

Vihera murmured thoughtfully at Rei's words.

It was an event from nearly three hundred years ago, and to date, that single sighting was the only confirmed instance of an Ambris.

Given how rare the case was, it was only natural that Vihera hadn't come across it despite her studies.

Still, the fact that enough information remained to be uncovered with thorough research was likely due to the guild's work.

And indeed, it was proving useful now. Keeping knowledge like this alive was truly remarkable... at least to Rei.

"Anyway. This case is similar to the Valley of a Hundred Demons three hundred years ago, but it hasn't been confirmed as Ambris. There might be another cause entirely. Considering that, we should be careful if we go searching."

"Right. Even if it's limited to high-rank adventurers, with so many differences like this..."

Sitting on the sofa, Rei and Vihera each revealed their resolve not to let their guard down.

After hearing Marina's explanation about Ambris, it was only natural that Rei and Vihera were commissioned to resolve this matter... likely the issue believed to be caused by Ambris.

That said, it wasn't a request specifically for Rei and Vihera; rather, it was a request only available to high-rank adventurers.

...To be precise, Vihera's adventurer rank wasn't yet B, so she couldn't strictly be called a high-rank adventurer... but in Vihera's case, her actual skill was deemed sufficient for rank B, so Marina had granted permission.

Of course, they weren't requesting every high-rank adventurer to search for and defeat Ambris.

Doing so would inevitably leave us scrambling to deal with the monster hordes.

For ordinary adventurers, dealing with packs that exploit their numerical advantage is inevitably difficult.

However, for a high-rank adventurer, dealing with a horde alone is not exactly easy... but it is not impossible either.

In other words, even if multiple swarms attacked Gilm like before, dealing with them wasn't difficult.

Still, if it was just individuals or a small group trying to infiltrate Gilm by exploiting their numbers, it would be harder to intervene.

Anyway, starting tomorrow, the search for Ambris would be Rei and Vihera's responsibility.

With Rei and Set, who possessed the mobility of Set, this kind of search request could cover a wide area.

In that sense, it could be said that Rei and Set were the greatest assets for this search for Ambrith.

Naturally, Vihera intended to accompany Rei and Set.

Rei had no particular reason to refuse, so he accepted it.

The reason he allowed Vihera to come along was that even with Vihera dangling from his front legs, his speed wouldn't decrease significantly.

"Still... monsters without magic stones? ...Hardly motivating."

"Heh heh, that might be true for you, Rei. ...Well, I feel the same way, though."

Rei lacked motivation because he couldn't obtain magic stones usable in beast magic, while Vihera's lack of motivation stemmed from having no means to attack the mist-like monster, Ambrith.

Not only was it immune to physical attacks, but it also possessed high magical defense, likely due to its creation through magical aberration, rendering ordinary offensive spells ineffective.

It was a troublesome monster in many ways.

And all Ambris could do was contact subhuman monsters and evolve them into leader species.

It was nothing short of a nuisance. Being a mist-like entity and lacking magic stones meant there was no way to obtain a proof-of-slaying body part.

...Though, to be fair, it was only confirmed once, three hundred years ago, and even then, it wasn't actually defeated, so it's not even clear which body part would count as proof of defeat.

"Thinking about it like this, maybe Ambris really is closer to a disaster or natural phenomenon than a monster, just like they say."

"I don't know if it has its own will... but it moves around, doesn't it?"

"Moving disasters or natural phenomena aren't exactly rare, are they? Tornadoes move around all the time."

My signature move, the Flame Tornado... the fire whirlwind, moves across the ground.

When I first used the fire whirl, it barely moved at all.

But the fire whirl Rei uses now can be moved freely, roughly speaking.

Thinking about it that way, natural phenomena moving around isn't really that strange.

Still, in this world of Elgin, where magic exists, there are inevitably differences from Earth's natural phenomena.

"Either way, what we have to do doesn't change. All we can do is search for Ambris. ...Whether we can find it and defeat it is another matter entirely."

"True. ...With Set here, the expectations on us are high, but are you okay with that?"

"I'm not sure. Considering Set's abilities, it wouldn't be surprising if we could find it... if Ambrith has a scent."

Set's Enhanced Sense of Smell skill had risen three levels in this short time.

If we could get close to Ambros even once, the enhanced scent should let us track him down anytime.

(Though finding Ambris in the first place is the hard part.)

The description of him being mist-like was based solely on eyewitness accounts from the Valley of a Hundred Demons. It wasn't certain that this Ambris was also a mist-like entity.

No, even before that, it wasn't clear if this incident was actually caused by Ambris.

Marina believed it was Ambris' doing based on the phenomenon of creating leader species from humanoid monsters, but she hadn't actually confirmed its form.

(Well, I can't imagine many other beings possessing the trait of evolving humanoid monsters into leader species. Thinking about it that way, Umbrus really is the most suspicious suspect.)

Given the extremely limited precedents, speculating about what kind of opponent it might be was largely pointless.

The best course was to be ready to respond immediately, no matter what happened.

"Anyway, starting tomorrow. Whatever we do, playing catch-up like this is no fun."

"Yeah. ...If only they'd evolved something more powerful than just demi-human monsters. That would've been a bit more fun. Or even a cyclops, if it had to be demi-human."

Vihera murmured, looking slightly disappointed, perhaps recalling the battle between the werewolf and the cyclops.

Cyclops, being a humanoid monster, does have a leader species.

However, perhaps because their individual strength is overwhelmingly greater than other monsters... like goblins, higher-tier variants are extremely rare.

Whether you call it a blessing or a curse, the absence of a leader species among Cyclopses might have been unfortunate for Vihera, but it was certainly fortunate for everyone else.

"Don't worry... though that sounds strange. If Ambris truly exists, it's only a matter of time before a higher-tier cyclops appears. Though Marina probably doesn't want that to happen."

For Marina, in her position as Guildmaster, the emergence of a true superior species of Cyclops would be a headache.

While many adventurers in Gilm are skilled, those unable to defeat a Cyclops are by no means few.

Given that, Marina naturally hoped a monster like a Cyclops Leader wouldn't appear.

That said, Vihera desired battles against formidable foes, and Rei wanted to use magic stones for beast magic, so they both secretly hoped a Cyclops Leader would appear.

"Well, there's no point in us arguing about that now. Hopefully, we can find Ambris ourselves."

"The chances are high. Having Set, who can fly, is a huge advantage. He can search an area dozens, maybe even hundreds of times larger than anyone else. ...But if this really is Ambris' doing, the problem becomes how to find that mist-like thing."

Thick fog is easier to see through. But conversely, thin fog is harder to see through.

Thinking about it that way, Ambris could be considered a monster with extremely high stealth capabilities.

Even for Rei, it was questionable whether an Ambris without a magic stone could even be considered a monster.

"If we can make the monster we touch the leader species, then if we manage to capture it skillfully, it'd be a big help for us."

"...You're being unreasonable again."

Vihera let out a sigh of exasperation at Rei's sudden remark.

Truthfully, what Rei was suggesting was that unreasonable.

And naturally, Rei understood it was unreasonable.

Even so, the ability to evolve monsters into higher species—albeit limited to humanoid types—was a huge advantage for Rei, who needed to gather magic stones.

(And while it's only Leader types now... what if it could work on Generals or Mages? Or even Kings and Queens...)

Rei seriously considered whether he could somehow capture Ambris.
