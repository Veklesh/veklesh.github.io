The meeting with Azimov ultimately decided nothing beyond reinforcing Srei Punil's boots.

To be precise, Azimov had various ideas for using the Silver Lion's materials, but the materials themselves were so incredible that he couldn't bring himself to use them.

He thought, "I want to do this, I want to do that, I want to do the other thing too..." and as a result, he ended up unable to do anything.

Even so, he took custody of several of the Silver Lion's internal organs and also took custody of Rei's Sleipnir boots, deciding to focus on those instead.

As a result, the current Rei was moving around wearing shoes from Azimov's house, not Sleipnir's boots.

Azimov's body was clearly larger than Rei's, and his feet were similarly bigger.

Therefore, the shoes Rei was currently wearing were slightly awkward to walk in.

"Maybe I should buy some shoes somewhere... Oh?"

...buy some shoes somewhere?"

Just as Rei was about to say that, white snow falling from the sky caught her eye.

It had snowed several times before, but fortunately—or perhaps unfortunately—the snow had always melted away without accumulating.

That had been a relief to Rei. But the snow falling now was clearly different in intensity.

Watching the snow, Rei wore a displeased expression.

Thanks to the Dragon Robe's makeshift air-conditioning ability, he didn't feel cold even with the snow falling like this.

Still, the frown was only natural for someone like Rei, who grew up in a snowy region.

(When I was little, I used to be happy when it snowed, and I have memories of playing outside... When did I start disliking snow?)

Having grown up in the mountains, snowfall meant opportunities for fun activities like skiing and sledding.

There were a few kids close to his age, so they'd have snowball fights, build snowmen, and make snow caves.

But that only lasted until elementary school. By the time Rei reached middle school, she didn't recall ever feeling happy about snow anymore.

A white Christmas wasn't such a rare sight for Rei either.

Even seeing scenes of people enjoying it in manga or anime, he couldn't quite relate.

Instead, only the drudgery of shoveling snow came to mind.

"Grrrrr!"

As he absentmindedly watched the snow, that growl suddenly reached his ears.

He turned his gaze toward the familiar sound and, as expected, found Set there.

And besides Set, Mireine was there too.

...The right to spend a day with Set, as promised before.

Milein was using it today, spending time together with Set.

"Ah, Set-chan! ...Huh, Rei? Just so you know, I'm the one spending time with Set-chan today, okay?"

"I know. I'm not going to demand you give Set back right now. Just give him back before the day ends, and that'll be fine."

"...Oh, really?"

Milein murmured, taken aback by Rei's words, having assumed he'd demand Set back immediately.

"GRRRR!"

Pay attention to me too! Set rubbed his face against Rei's.

Running her fingers through Set's fur, Rei smiled and spoke.

"Go ahead and be as clingy as you want with Milein."

"Guruu!"

"Of course!" Set meowed, and at the sound, Milein smiled happily.

For Set, Milein was definitely not someone he disliked. In fact, he was quite attached to her, as she always doted on him and gave him food.

In that sense, Milein and Johanna were probably at the top of Set's list of favorites.

(Though Johanna was pretty angry when she heard Milein would be spending the day with Set first.)

Johanna and the others couldn't return with Rei and the others because they had a dungeon escort request, but that was already finished, and they had returned to Gilm.

True to their promise, they immediately demanded a day with Set when they met Rei... but the timing was poor, to say the least. At that moment, figures of the Scorching Wind, including Milein, were also near Rei.

There was no way Mireine would quietly back down upon hearing this, and naturally, an argument ensued... resulting in Mireine, who had the prior arrangement, being granted priority.

Milein had been eagerly awaiting her day with Set for months.

The reason she hadn't mentioned it to Rei was likely because of the incident where Vihera had fallen unconscious.

Rei had been running around with Set trying to wake Vihera, so it simply wasn't a situation where Milein could bring up the promise.

But now, Vihera was awake. So, if that was the case... That must have been her reasoning.

In truth, Milein's proposal wasn't something Rei could refuse, and as a result, Milein had won the day with Set, just like today.

"Well then, I'm going to play in the snow with Set-chan... See you later."

"Yeah. Bring Set back to the inn by tonight. ...And whatever you do, don't take him back to your own place."

"Ugh! I-I know that!"

She must have been thinking something like that in the back of her mind. Milein flushed, looking caught out, then scrambled to cover it up.

(Things have calmed down quite a bit around Oita now. Maybe it's finally safe to absorb the Silver Lion's magic stone. Snow's starting to fall, so there should be fewer people venturing outside the city than before.)

Watching Milein stroke Set's head, Rei thought such things.

He wasn't particularly fond of going out in the snow, but this magic stone belonged to a rank S monster, the Silver Lion.

It was obvious he couldn't just carelessly absorb it.

"Guru?"

As Rei pondered the Silver Lion's magic stone, Set tilted his head slightly, as if asking, "What's wrong?" and let out a meow directed at Rei.

Laying a hand on his head to signal it was nothing, he then addressed Milein.

"Well, I'll be heading back soon. I'm counting on you for Set."

"Yes, leave him to me. I'll make sure to dote on little Set properly."

Milein said this with a beaming smile, clearly delighted to be with Set.

The snow falling around them had grown quite heavy, but it seemed to mean nothing to Milein.

After parting ways with Milein, who was going for a walk through town with Set, Rei started walking again.

"Welcome, welcome! Skewered Gamelion meat, fresh and in season! If you're eating this time of year, this is it! You absolutely must try it!"

As he walked down the street, he heard that call. Spotting a shop selling Gamelion skewers, Rei naturally headed toward it.

"Oh? Rei? How about it? Want to buy some skewers?"

"Give me twenty."

"Still buying as usual, huh? Well, it's good business for us, I suppose... Just give me a moment."

For the residents of Gilm... and especially for those selling food at stalls, it wasn't unusual for Rei to buy large quantities of food.

In fact, twenty skewers was actually on the low side.

Honestly, twenty skewers might seem a bit much for one person, but for adventurers, it's not that uncommon to be able to finish that much.

Of course, if it's meant as a snack or treat... then not many could manage that much.

"Still, there's a lot of Gamelion meat this year. Even setting aside last year's incident, isn't it more than the year before or the year before that?"

"Ah... Yeah. Adventurers have been putting in a lot of effort, but more than that, the number of Gamelions is just higher than usual this year."

"So the Gamelion population itself has grown?"

"Yeah, that seems to be the case."

"...I see."

Could it be related to the large number of Gamelions in the dungeon last year? Rei wondered, but decided it was pointless to dwell on it now.

"Here, twenty skewers."

After receiving the skewers and paying, Rei stored all but the one he intended to eat inside his Misty Ring.

Eating freshly grilled Gamelion skewers while snow fell.

It felt like a kind of extravagance... much like eating ice cream in a room so hot you sweat in winter, or eating hot pot in a room so cold you shiver in summer... He carried the skewer to his mouth, thinking such thoughts.

Just a little while ago, he'd been fed up with the snow, yet now he felt this way—a quick change of heart that was typical of Rei.

Walking along while eating the skewers, I encounter another familiar face—not Milein, but someone else.

They must have noticed Rei walking while eating skewers too. They looked slightly surprised, but the next moment, a smile spread across their face as they spoke.

"Rei, it feels like it's been ages since we last met like this, doesn't it?"

The one who said this to Rei was Min, the Axe of the Thunder God.

He had returned to Gilm from the dungeon a step ahead of Rei and the others, but they hadn't met since.

...Naturally, the Axe of the Thunder God's name was listed among the members publicly announced as having cleared the dungeon.

Whether he disliked that or was simply busy with Rodos's rehabilitation work, he rarely appeared in public.

Then again, the merchants approaching Rei were solely after Silver Lion materials, and since Elk hadn't received any of those materials, they probably wouldn't be swarmed by merchants.

"Are you alone today? Elk aside, it seems Rodos isn't here either."

"Yeah. Rodos isn't in a condition to go out yet. But it's been a while since then, and he can speak without any problems now."

"Glad to hear he's recovering."

Though treated as an afterthought to Vihera, Rodos also regained consciousness through the ritual performed by Grimm using the Silver Lion's heart.

However, perhaps due to the circumstances while unconscious, unlike Vihera who could stand and speak normally immediately upon regaining consciousness, Rhodes couldn't even stand up, let alone speak properly.

Hearing that even Rodos could now speak properly, Rei was genuinely impressed.

Not much time had passed since Rhodes regained consciousness.

Therefore, the effect of Min's nursing care must be significant.

(Elk... well, he doesn't seem cut out for nursing duties, does he?)

Elk had a character that was, for better or worse, rather rough around the edges.

Of course, he must worry about his son, but if Elk tried nursing him, Rhodes would likely scream bloody murder... That was Rei's prediction, and it wasn't entirely wrong.

"Ah. He says he wants to be able to walk outside by spring if possible, and he's working hard toward that."

"Is it really that simple?"

No one around Rei had ever needed rehabilitation before.

That was true even back in Japan, which was precisely why he had no idea how long rehabilitation took before someone could walk again.

Moreover, Rhodes had been unconscious for a long time. Even with some protection from magic items, his muscles had severely atrophied.

Considering those circumstances, it was only natural to wonder if he could really walk by spring.

(Wait, this is a world with magic. Maybe there are ways to speed up recovery?)

Since there were magic items that could mitigate the deterioration of Rodos's unconscious body, it wouldn't be strange if magic or magic items could somehow manage it.

Especially since the person standing before Rei now was a Rank A adventurer—a truly skilled mage.

"Of course, it won't be easy. But I think Rodos will see through what he's decided to do."

It was likely due to his expectations for his son. Min said this with a smile.

"I see. Well then, do your best. Elk and the others are fired up too, right?"

"Yeah. Especially since this whole thing happened partly because of Rodos' inexperience. He says he wants to retrain himself in those areas."

"...I see."

The words that came out of Rei's mouth were the same as those spoken seconds earlier.

But the emotion behind it was vastly different.

If Elk was going to train Rhodes more rigorously than ever before, it would likely be far harsher than Rei imagined.

"If you'd like, would Rei come see Rodos sometime?"

"Ah... Yeah. I'll try to go see him next time I get the chance."

Rei knew full well that Rodos didn't particularly like him.

That's precisely why he replied that way.

And so they talked for a while in the falling snow... eventually parting ways right there.
