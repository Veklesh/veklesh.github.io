Rei arrived at one of the buildings within the guild's grounds.

It was a building that should have been a warehouse.

Rei knew about this building because he had once transported a large number of Gamelions he had defeated in a dungeon into it.

But... what Rei saw inside was a sight that could not possibly be mistaken for a former warehouse.

Several tables had been brought in, and on them sat numerous glass bottles containing chemicals Rei had never seen before.

Magic circles were etched into the floor, and experiments of some sort were being conducted at each one.

...It was absolutely nothing like a former warehouse.

"Rei? What's wrong?"

Turning toward the voice, Rei saw a familiar figure standing there.

The creator of the Twilight Spear, an alchemist who could only be described as a madman.

"Azimov?"

With a hint of surprise, Rei called out the man's name.

A madman, greedy for anything that piqued his interest, yet utterly indifferent to anything that didn't.

He was the sort of person who, even if summoned by the guild, wouldn't bat an eye at ignoring the call because he had research to do or was still sleepy.

The very fact he was quietly attending the guild meeting felt utterly unnatural to Rei.

"Ha ha ha. What are you surprised about? It's not like it's strange for me to be here, right? Since I might be able to research this mysterious entity called Ambris, it's only natural I'm here."

Azimov laughed cheerfully, showing no sign of having been coerced.

If anything, it seemed he'd come here of his own accord, drawn by his interest in the entity known as Ambris.

"I see. You haven't changed a bit."

He glanced briefly at Marina beside him before speaking, but Azimov... didn't seem to notice Marina... or the guildmaster's presence at all, continuing to laugh cheerfully.

Though not quite as stunning as Marina or Vihera... Mireine and Ecuril were also above-average beauties.

In fact, quite a few of the alchemists inside the warehouse were captivated by them.

The looks Marina and Vihera gave Azimov were friendly.

Both were aware they possessed beauty capable of attracting men.

But even so, being subjected to such lustful stares was not a pleasant experience.

Some say women become more beautiful when admired, but Marina and Vihera, at least, were perfectly content with themselves as they were.

...Though if Rei desired it, that would be another matter entirely.

In any case, Marina's first impression of Azimov was surprisingly favorable, defying most expectations.

(His unusually high energy must be because he's still thinking about the Ambrith thing.)

Seeing Azimov's demeanor, Rei opened his mouth, thinking to himself that he was still marching to his own beat, putting his own feelings aside.

"So, about the Ambris incident..."

"You found it!?"

"Ah... well..."

Having been told by Marina that it was best not to spread the word about him defeating Ambros, Rei frowned, feeling the stares of the other alchemists around him and wondering what to do.

But Marina spoke up in Rei's place.

"Rei defeated Ambris."

A rustle.

The alchemists who heard Marina's words all wore expressions of shock.

But, being alchemists, many of them likely knew of Rei. ...Though, among alchemists, Rei's fame stemmed mostly from possessing powerful magic items like the Death Scythe, and above all, being Set's master.

Set, a Rank A monster... no, a rare species equivalent to Rank S, had materials—feathers, fur, claws—that were highly prized by alchemists.

That's precisely why, for the alchemists, Set would likely be seen as the main focus over Rei.

"...Then, does that mean the reason we were gathered here is no longer necessary?"

Asimov voiced the obvious question, and the other alchemists also turned their gaze toward Marina.

The alchemists present had been gathered here at the guild's request.

Some had been gathered somewhat forcefully—though not violently.

That said, alchemists are generally people with an extremely strong curiosity.

None of them refused when they arrived at this warehouse and were told the reason for their gathering.

In fact, some even set aside the work they had been commissioned to do when summoned, eager to research Ambris instead.

...The fact they were setting aside their work, rather than abandoning it, showed at least a modicum of conscientiousness.

That's precisely why, upon hearing that Ambris—whom they'd hoped to study—had already been defeated, they felt relief that Gilm's crisis was resolved, yet also disappointment that they wouldn't be able to research Ambris.

But Marina spoke up to reassure the alchemists.

"Rei definitely defeated Anubis. ...But almost at the same time, our Scorching Wind discovered another Anubis."

At Marina's words, the alchemists tilted their heads as if momentarily unsure what she had said.

But they quickly grasped the meaning. Speaking for the group, Azimov asked,

"So... that means there are multiple Ambriss?"

"Probably. That's why I need you alchemists to create magic items to find the Ambris."

"Hold on a moment."

Azimov muttered, his expression still troubled.

The other alchemists shared his look, clearly frustrated by their own lack of skill.

"So, even those with the ability to sense magic can't find an Ambris, right?"

"Yes. It doesn't emit any strong odor, and while it appears as a distinctive black mist, its shape makes it rather difficult to spot."

"...So how am I supposed to create a magic item for searching under those conditions? If it has no distinguishing features, there's literally no way to find it."

"Don't worry... well, maybe that's not quite right. But if we examine the weapon Rei used to defeat Ambris, we might find some residue or remnants. ...Rei?"

Following Marina's words, Rei drew the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring.

The alchemists reacted strongly the instant they saw them.

Surprise, jealousy, admiration... their reactions varied, but jealousy was undoubtedly the strongest.

However, it wasn't jealousy because Rei possessed the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear.

It was simply because, with their current skill, they couldn't create a magic item like the Death Scythe, and the same applied to the Twilight Spear.

Death Scythe, forged through Beast Magic, was one thing, but the Twilight Spear was an artifact created by Azimov alongside Pamidor.

While there might be those capable of creating it if they tried, gathering the materials used to make it was no ordinary feat.

Especially if the materials included items used by the world's only three Rank S adventurers, even the frontier town of Gilm, rich in rare things, held nothing comparable.

Well, perhaps the materials themselves exist in the frontier, but it would be more accurate to say acquiring them is far from easy.

In any case, Azimov, who had crafted the Twilight Spear—a masterpiece rarely seen in recent years—had become the center of attention.

Even among alchemists, who are often eccentric, Azimov was considered particularly peculiar.

Precisely because of this, the gathering of alchemists this time meant that quite a few people were seeing his face in person for the first time, rather than just hearing rumors.

"The Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear. We used these to attack and defeat Ambrys when he was trying to turn the werewolves into a leader species. If any remnants remain, it's probably one of these two."

"I see. Then, would it be alright if I borrowed one for a bit?"

Rei nodded in response to Azimov's question, asked as if to confirm.

"But as long as Ambris is still out there, I need to join the search too. That means I can't lend it for too long. Is that okay?"

"What!?"

The woman alchemist, who had been staring intently at the Death Scythe from a short distance away, let out a scream akin to a cry of despair at Rei's words.

Realizing her voice had drawn attention, the female alchemist flushed bright red and looked down.

"It's rare for Mulan to raise her voice like that."

"Well, rare... I've never seen it before."

"She's so shy, but she's really skilled..."

"Huh? Jealous? Well, Mulan's potions are about ten percent more effective than anyone else's."

"Just ten percent... but still ten percent..."

The voices of the alchemists chatting with their acquaintances reached Rei's ears.

(From what I hear, she's skilled but shy, huh?)

As he vaguely grasped the female alchemist—Mulan's—personality from their conversation, he opened his mouth.

"Sorry, but like I said before, I can't lend it out for too long. I hope you understand."

"Eh? Um, well... but... uuugh..."

Shocked at being addressed directly by Rei, Mulan looked around helplessly.

The fact that her pleading gaze ultimately landed on Azimov was likely because he was someone Rei knew. ...And there was undoubtedly respect for him having created a magic item like the Twilight Spear.

But Azimov shook his head at Mulan.

He understood that Rei's power was necessary to deal with the existence known as Ambris.

"Ugh..."

Even so, Mulan gazed at the Death Scythe with lingering reluctance, but in the end, she quietly backed down.

Others besides Mulan had also considered the short time allotted for examining the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, but seeing Azimov's attitude, they knew any protest would be futile.

"So, specifically, how long can we borrow it for?"

"Well... I'd like it back as soon as possible, but then we wouldn't be able to find Ambros. What do you think about that?"

Marina, meeting Rei's questioning gaze, paused to consider.

That gesture radiated a powerful feminine allure around her,

"It's true I'd prefer it as soon as possible. But if we can't find Ambris, there's no point in that... Well, how about until tomorrow morning for now?"

"Tomorrow morning... huh?"

Unfazed by Marina's intense feminine allure, Azimov muttered.

His words carried a hint of dissatisfaction, yet he refrained from saying it was too soon. He likely understood that Rei was the one who could most swiftly deal with the threat of Amblis.

Somehow, Azimov had come to speak as the representative of the alchemists gathered here. This was largely because he knew Rei and could ignore Marina's allure.

...Though beyond that, his skill in forging the Twilight Spear is undoubtedly the main reason he's respected.

"Can't we find another way? If possible, give us a few more days..."

"No. The Leader Species created by Ambros are numerous. Their collective abilities aren't that high, but there are many of them. Therefore, what's needed to deal with them is still Rei and Set's abilities."

"Then, how about borrowing them until the morning after tomorrow, once Rei returns to Gilm tomorrow?"

As long as Rei is in Gilm, borrowing the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear shouldn't be a problem.

After hearing Azimov's suggestion, Rei thought for a moment before speaking.

"That's fine, but we don't even know if there are any remnants of Ambrith left. And even if there are, wouldn't using the Death Scythe in battle make those remnants disappear?"

"...The Death Scythe was your magic-activated weapon, right? So lending you a staff or something..."

"Probably not. Not to brag, but my magical power is immense. Any magic-activating device other than the Death Scythe probably couldn't handle it."

Several alchemists frowned at Rei's words.

After all, it's the alchemists who craft the magic-activating devices, and saying they couldn't withstand it was tantamount to saying their skills were inadequate.

However, that dissatisfaction wasn't directed at Rei, but rather at their own lack of skill—likely stemming from their pride as alchemists.

Ultimately, it was settled that in combat using the Death Scythe, Rei would only use magic, while the Twilight Spear would be used for direct attacks against enemies.
