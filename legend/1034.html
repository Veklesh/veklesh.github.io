"I see. So the World Tree is a symbol of prayer for the Dark Elves... a kind of god, then?"

Sitting down on the sofa, Rei read the book the dark elf man had brought him—a book detailing the history of the dark elves—and words of understanding escaped his lips.

He understood why they felt something more than mere necessity for the World Tree in their daily lives.

The saving grace, though, was that even if it was considered the closest thing to a god, there weren't fanatics like those from a certain religion that had attacked Rei before.

If a World Tree cult had emerged, worshipping it, Rei might well have refused to heal the World Tree.

Given how much Rei despised religion, one could say the Dark Elves were fortunate in that sense.

Just as he was about to turn the next page... he suddenly stopped his movement and turned his gaze toward the door.

He sensed someone approaching.

Under normal circumstances, he wouldn't have paid it much mind.

But given that Rei was currently under house arrest, he couldn't help but be wary.

"Someone coming?"

Even so, considering his situation, he didn't immediately go on high alert.

He tucked a nearby string into his book as a bookmark and fixed his gaze steadily toward the door.

Within seconds, the guard in front of the door opened it.

"Rei, you have a visitor... um, should I let them in?"

The way he spoke to Rei was certainly not how one would address someone under house arrest.

This too, perhaps, was thanks to Marina.

Rei came to that conclusion and nodded without hesitation.

"Yeah. I was just bored out of my mind. If someone came to visit me, that'd be welcome. More importantly, I'd appreciate it if you brought something to snack on."

His gaze fell on the plate of stir-fried, salted nuts that had been brought along with the books.

The plate was already empty, and the guard looked surprised.

"You ate all of it already? ...Well, fine. Understood. I need to bring Lord Optis something to drink anyway. I'll bring some more while I'm at it."

"...Optis? Is that the name of the person who came asking?"

"That's right. He's an important figure in this settlement, so be sure not to be rude."

With that, the guard who had entered the room took the plate and left.

Replacing the guard, an elderly Dark Elf entered the room.

"So you're Rei?"

"And you? From what I hear, you're some important Dark Elf figure, right?"

"Ahem. I am Optis Ariansa, the elder of this settlement... To put it plainly for you, I hold a position akin to village chief."

"...Ariansa? That's..."

Hearing the familiar surname, Rei turned his gaze back to Optis.

He looked quite old, yet there was something about Optis's features that resembled Marina's.

Optis returned a nod at Rei's reaction.

"Marina, whom you know, is my granddaughter."

"Grandfather, huh? But why would that important person—Marina's grandfather and the elder of this settlement—be here with me?"

"Well, I wanted to see what kind of person you are. Until just a little while ago, we were discussing how to deal with you... If we didn't know who you were, any approach we took might end up being a waste of effort, wouldn't it? Besides..."

Optis paused there, fixing his gaze intently on Rei.

"I need to see for myself what kind of man my granddaughter has chosen... with these very arms of mine."

"...With your own hands?"

Rei wondered if the usual expression would be "with my own eyes," but the dark elf who called himself Optis smiled amusedly at Rei's reaction and spoke.

"Yes. My arm. If you are to be in such a relationship with my granddaughter, you must naturally possess the strength to match."

"Well, even if you say that..."

He understood that the figure before him was unexpectedly powerful.

From what Marina had told him on the way to this forest, it was clear the dark elf before him was formidable.

Rei had heard the story clearly: she had pursued the slave traders alone to rescue her kidnapped comrades, returning with her body stained in blood.

(Given that kind of personality, it's not surprising she wants to fight me directly like this... is it?)

While pondering this, Rei still recognized that going along with Optis's plan meant he could leave the room where he was being held captive, which was a significant advantage.

It was true he could live comfortably in this room, but a guard stood before the door, and though from a distance, he was constantly watched through the window.

He knew the guards were there not only to watch Rei but also to protect her from anyone who might harm her. Still, being watched was never a pleasant feeling.

Given that, it was only natural to think that since things were finally moving, he should go along with it.

"I see. So where should we do it?"

Rei's words seemed to surprise Optis as well. His eyes widened slightly.

"Well now, you're making up your mind rather quickly. I'm glad about that, but... are you sure?"

"Yeah, it's fine. Just hanging around here is boring anyway. I felt like moving my body a bit."

"...You've only been in this room for a few hours, haven't you? You're quite the impatient man. Though, perhaps it's precisely that personality that caught Marina's eye."

"Ah..."

Rei tried to say that the matter with Marina was a misunderstanding, prompted by Optis' words.

But since he didn't know Marina's true intentions, he hesitated to speak carelessly.

Besides, it would be a lie to say Rei himself didn't find Marina attractive.

If he hadn't felt any attraction to Marina to begin with, he might have said something.

But the truth was, Rei did harbor feelings for Marina.

In any case, Rei's words remained vague, and in the end, he said nothing more to Optis as he was led out of the room.

"...Where is this?"

Led out of the room, Rei found himself with Optis in a plaza-like area on the outskirts of the settlement.

"Where is this?" Rei asked aloud, but seeing the Dark Elves around them training their bodies and engaging in mock combat, it was obvious what kind of place this was.

At the edge of the square, Dark Elves could be seen drawing their bows toward targets.

"You can see for yourself. This is the place in the settlement for physical training."

As Optis spoke, the training Dark Elves seemed to notice him. Several turned their gaze their way.

Some, perhaps recognizing Rei, understood what was about to happen here and grimaced. Naturally, Optis paid them no mind, leading Rei toward the center of the square.

"Haaah! Ugh! ...Huh? Elder?"

"Whoa, Elder? What brings you here... I mean, it's not unusual for you to be here, but who's that human?"

Two dark elf men who had been sparring with rapiers near the center looked at Rei and Ray and asked.

Optis smiled at them... looking happier than ever as he spoke.

"Hmm. Marina's husband."

"...Huh?"

Not only the two men, but also those nearby who had been watching the situation reacted with similar exclamations.

Simultaneously, various gazes—filled with curiosity, jealousy, pity, and questions like "That guy?"—turned toward Rei.

The news that Rei had kissed Marina had naturally spread through the settlement at an incredible speed. However, the identity of his partner had become distorted along the way, mixed with various rumors, and ultimately transformed into a figure described as a huge, muscular man. So, it was only natural for them to be surprised by Rei's appearance, which was the complete opposite of those rumors.

"Anyway, would you mind if I borrowed this space for a moment? As Marina's grandfather... and above all, as the elder of this settlement, I wish to confirm just how much strength this one possesses."

"Uh, yes... that's fine, but... the Elder himself?"

It was precisely because he knew the nature of Optis' power. Optis nodded in agreement to the man who asked timidly.

"And everyone else, make sure you properly observe just how much power this one can wield."

At those words, everyone training in the square immediately distanced themselves from the two.

Even those practicing archery farther away stopped their training.

(Just how much attention are we drawing?)

That's what Rei thought, but battles in front of large crowds were nothing new to him.

After all, at last year's Bestir Empire Games, he had fought before the entire imperial capital... and before spectators who had come from neighboring countries.

Compared to that, fighting in front of this number of people was no problem at all.

(Well, the spectators at the tournament were mostly amateurs, but here, they're Dark Elves with considerable skill. That's different.)

Scanning his surroundings while thinking, Rei concluded that anything he said now would be futile. He drew the Death Scythe from within the Misty Ring.

True to his expectations, the Death Scythe—a blade larger than Rei himself—materialized suddenly from nowhere. The Dark Elves watching nearby reacted almost without exception with expressions of shock.

Optis, however, was the exception. He was surprised, but only to the extent of having seen something unusual.

Seeing this, Rei now wore a look of surprise.

"I thought you'd be a bit more surprised."

"Well, I've lived a long life, you see. This isn't the first time I've looked inside an item box."

"...Huh."

Rei voiced his admiration at Optis' words.

His attitude stemmed from knowing exactly how valuable such item boxes could be.

Though impressed, Rei remained vigilant, keeping the Death Scythe at the ready.

Optis, too, responded by readying his staff.

The staff Optis held served as both a magical catalyst and a melee weapon.

At first glance, the head he gripped in his hand appeared to have something crystal-like embedded within it.

The crystal-like object was at least twenty centimeters in diameter, making it clear that a blow from it would pack considerable power.

Furthermore, the tip of the staff was sharply pointed, reminiscent of thrusting weapons like an estoc or a rapier.

(That staff can't possibly be just a magic-casting wand, no matter how you look at it. Or rather, logically speaking, sharpening the tip of a wooden staff shouldn't make it that powerful... Surely, an elder Dark Elf wouldn't use such a weapon. First move!)

Gripping the Death Scythe, Rei kicked off the ground and charged toward Optis.

He hadn't channeled magic into the Death Scythe, as he had no intention of killing, but even in its raw state, it possessed overwhelming destructive power as a bludgeoning weapon weighing around 100kg.

Optis must have understood that power. He didn't try to parry or block the blow swung at him; instead, he leapt far backward.

With a thunderous roar, the Death Scythe streaked past where Optis's gaze was fixed.

The swing that struck wasn't the massive blade, but the handle that passed through the spot where Optis's body had been.

Recognizing the lack of intent to kill, Optis suppressed an urge to laugh inwardly.

(I never imagined I'd be held back like this at my age... But that's fine too!)

Turning amusement into fighting spirit, he kicked off the ground and closed the distance between himself and Rei.

Rei was momentarily startled by the speed of the lunge, which didn't match his apparent age, but he swiftly pulled the Death Scythe back to his side.

Seeing Rei like this, Optis, who was closing the distance, was also inwardly surprised.

The Death Scythe was nearly two meters long, and its weight seemed far from something one could handle casually.

His surprise stemmed from not knowing one of the Death Scythe's abilities: the user barely feels its weight.

Even so, Optis swung his staff, aiming to strike Rei's torso with the rounded crystal-like object embedded at its tip...

A high-pitched metallic clang echoed around them.

The source of that sound was the handle of Rei's Death Scythe.

Hearing the shrill sound it made upon catching Optis's swing, they exchanged smiles without either making any further move.
