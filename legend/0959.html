"Then we'll take these thieves into custody. What about the money they were sold for as slaves?"

"Hmm, I wonder how long that will take?"

The Sabursta guard, his eyes drawn to Vihera's appearance, paused briefly before speaking.

"We'll need to negotiate with the merchants from here on out... so I can't give you an exact timeframe. It will likely take several days, I suppose."

"...This is troublesome. We were planning to set out tomorrow."

Vihera wore a troubled expression, but Rei stepped forward.

Having survived numerous bandit raids and sold the surviving bandits as slaves, Rei understood quick ways to make money.

(Feels like I've become a slave trader hunting slaves myself.)

Despite that thought, he felt no remorse whatsoever.

He'd lived as he pleased up until now, forcing others to do his bidding through strength, so he felt it was only natural that he should be treated the same way.

"I suppose there's also the option of having the guard squad buy the slaves at a reasonable price, then selling them to the slave traders. Is that impossible?"

The guard nodded at Rei's words.

"True, that would let us pay you immediately, but it would be significantly cheaper under the regulations. It'd be based on the lowest price they could sell for. ...Well, since the difference goes to the guard squad, it's actually a relief for us."

"Yeah, but what should we do?"

"Right. ...I think faster is better, even if it's a bit cheaper. Can we decide this ourselves?"

At Rei's words, Vihera turned her gaze to the two merchants standing behind her.

Since Vihera and Byune were technically employed by these two, their opinion needed to be heard.

When merchants were attacked by bandits, how to deal with the bandits varied from merchant to merchant.

Some merchants who despise bandits might have them all killed outright, while others, like in this case, might sell them off as slaves.

In this case, selling them off as slaves could be considered a relatively lenient response.

That said, there have been merchants in the past who tried to reason with the attacking bandits, explaining the human way, and then released them. However, stories exist where the released bandits went on to cause significant harm to other merchants. As a result, the merchant who released them was blamed, ostracized as a merchant, and driven out of the town.

Furthermore, when the expelled merchant sought refuge in new villages, towns, or cities, word spread through the merchant network that this merchant had spared bandits, leading to increased harm. Consequently, he could no longer make a living as a merchant.

Ultimately, they ended up living as farmers. But for farmers too, bandits were creatures who stole their wealth, their food... and even young women and children too young to understand.

A person who let such bandits go couldn't possibly make a decent living as a farmer. Their crops were bought for next to nothing, and in the end, they met a gruesome fate, killed by survivors whose families had been slaughtered by the bandits they had let escape.

"Ah, we don't mind. No, it's not that we don't mind—it actually helps us. We still have time for the business deal in Gilm, but arriving sooner is always better."

"Yeah, it'd be a big help to us too."

After receiving permission from the two merchants, Vihera finally turned his gaze to Rei.

Although he had only fought briefly at the end of the battle against the bandits, Rei had still participated in the fight and deserved his share of the reward.

Especially since the hideout the bandits had mentioned was just getting spring supplies and didn't have much of value. The two merchants, aware of this, gave Rei slightly anxious looks, but he didn't voice any objections and simply nodded in return.

"Getting nearly ten spears is more than enough for me."

"Hmph!"

Vihera gave a small apology and ruffled Byune's hair, who was asserting himself, "Don't forget me!"

"Ah, sorry. Is that okay with you too, Byune?"

"Hmm... Mm!"

After a moment's hesitation, Byune nodded back.

Having originally ventured into the dungeon just to earn money, Byune was particular about it.

But she probably decided that heading to Gilm offered better earning potential than spending several days here for a little extra cash.

The frontier meant more danger, but also the potential for greater rewards.

"Well then, I'll leave it to you."

"...Ah, yes, please wait a moment. I'll bring the paperwork and payment right away."

The guard, who had been staring at Vihera without realizing it, snapped back to attention and hurried back to the guardhouse.

During that time, Rei and the others completed the entry procedures with another guard to enter the city.

As for Set, there was a bit of a commotion when he appeared at the gate. However, since he wasn't far from Gilm, it seemed plenty of information about Rei and Set had already reached them, so he wasn't treated with suspicion.

After completing the formalities and exchanging the rope-bound bandits for payment, the group entered Subursta.

"Hah... So that's Crimson. Didn't look all that strong to me, though."

"Yeah. But there are plenty of people you can't judge by looks alone, right? Especially those based in a frontier town like Gilm. There are a lot of high-rank adventurers around here."

The guards exchanged such words as they saw the merchant party off.

While Saburusta was somewhat famous for its proximity to the frontier, the existence of Avelo meant it was the closest town to the guild. Consequently, few famous adventurers used this town as their base.

It wasn't exactly nonexistent, but the vast majority established their bases in Gilm... or at the very least, in Abuero.

Even so, many travelers stopped at Subursta, which meant that serving as a guard here often brought encounters with figures considered major players.

But even these seasoned guards never imagined that one glance at Rei would reveal him to be such a figure.

Had it not been for Set, they might have even thought him a mere novice, fresh to adventuring.

Still, thanks to its proximity to Gilm, rumors about Rei were common. Fortunately, the guard post also included someone who had dealt with Rei during a previous stop here.

That's precisely why they recognized Rei as Rei.

...Though, to be fair, the griffin being his familiar made it a bit too obvious who he was.

"With spring here, more people are passing through this area. ...Hope it doesn't cause any trouble."

"You think anyone would mess with an adventurer who has a griffin as a familiar?"

"There are, right here. That troublemaker who caused a ruckus yesterday."

"...Ah."

Hearing his partner's words, the image of one man flashed through the guard's mind.

The figure of the man who'd been stirring up trouble frequently lately.

Thanks to that person, his already busy days had become even busier.

He should have remembered him first thing, but failing to do so was perhaps half an act of escapism...

"I just hope it doesn't get out of hand..."

"True. Subursta isn't as big as Gilm, but it's still vast. If the Crimson Brigade sets out for Gilm tomorrow, chances are nothing will happen."

The guard said it almost like a prayer, but if he knew Rei was favored by the god of chaos, he would have realized that prayer was pointless.

"Challenge me to a duel!"

Though Rei and his companions were accompanied by the griffin, Set, the innkeeper where the merchants always stayed in Subursta fortunately allowed them to lodge with Set, albeit somewhat apprehensively.

Of course, they paid a deposit as compensation if the griffin, Set, caused any trouble, and paid a generous extra amount specifically for Set's feed.

That deposit would be refunded, of course, if Set didn't cause any trouble.

Anyway, with lodging safely secured and evening approaching, Rei and the others headed down to the inn's dining hall to eat. Naturally, as evening fell, many patrons were drinking before dinner.

When drunkards spotted a beauty like Vihera, who seemed to be inviting men like him, they naturally started bothering her.

And when Vihera ignored that drunkard, they naturally started bothering Rei instead.

At first glance, he looked like a kid who'd just become an adventurer. Wearing a robe, he could only be mistaken for a mage. And specifically, a mage without a staff.

If this were Gilm, many would know the danger of messing with Rei. But this wasn't Gilm; it was Subursta.

Things might have been different if Set had been here, but Set was currently at the stables.

As a result, Rei gets picked on, and naturally, the drunks end up losing.

Normally, that would have been the end of it.

Even though the drunks had picked on Rei and Vihera, they weren't truly malicious.

Once sober, they'd likely feel ashamed of their actions and apologize to Rei and Vihera... perhaps with a hint of fear, but they'd apologize nonetheless and it would be over.

But it was unfortunate that someone else in that same dining hall had witnessed the entire exchange... well, not exactly the fight, but the whole sequence of events.

That person, realizing at a glance that Rei's skill was far from ordinary, approached the table where Rei and the others were sitting and demanded, without preamble, that they fight him.

"......"

For now, Rei said nothing, studying the man.

He was just under two meters tall, with a physique that was more lean than muscular. Not skinny, mind you, but the kind of build that looked that way because it was pure, defined muscle.

Judging by the ears protruding from his head, he was likely a wolf beastman.

The sharpness of his canine teeth, visible when he addressed Rei, further confirmed he was a wolf beastman. ...Though there was also the possibility he was a dog beastman.

He looked to be around twenty years old, his eyes burning with a fierce, almost savage fighting spirit.

"Hey, it's Magita again? He caused a duel commotion just before noon too..."

"That monk is pitiful too... Ah, but judging from their exchange earlier, he seems pretty strong. Maybe not? If Magita loses this time, he might finally learn his lesson."

"What the hell is he thinking, pulling stunts like this? It's such a pain. If this keeps up, it'll get so noisy we won't be able to eat. Could he please do this somewhere else?"

Hearing the voices at the surrounding tables, Rei realized the man in front of him wasn't just a companion of the guy who'd picked a fight with Vihera, but someone who'd caused trouble countless times before.

(In the sense of seeking strong opponents, he's similar to Vihera... But honestly, if he wants strong opponents, he should come to Gilm instead of this Saburusta. Gilm has plenty of powerful adventurers.)

Though Rei harbored such doubts, Magita seemed to lose patience with Rei's silence. She reached out, trying to grab the Dragon Robe and pull Rei closer.

Of course, Rei wasn't about to let her grab the Dragon Robe in that situation. He reached out and grabbed Magita's arm.

"What do you think you're doing?"

"Didn't I tell you? Fight me."

"What's in it for me to do something like that?"

"Who cares? I'm telling you to fight because I want to fight. You're pretty strong, aren't you? You must have some confidence in your own skills? Don't you want to show them off and bask in that feeling of superiority?"

"I don't feel that way. My strength is something only I need to know about, and I'll make sure my enemies know it when the time comes."

Rei declared this while reaching for a skewer of roasted orc meat, but Magita slammed the table as if he'd been waiting for this moment.

"That's exactly why I'm saying it should be me. Fight me. If you refuse, I'll have to take matters into my own hands."

"...Even if you say you'll take action..."

His gaze shifted to Vihera and Byune as he took a bite of the skewer.

There were two merchants present, but it was obvious they were merchants through and through, not the type whose livelihood depended on combat.

That's precisely why Rei couldn't completely rule out the possibility that Magita might target these two to provoke him.

Rei felt Vihera could easily defeat Magita in a fight, and while Byune might not win, he was confident she could hold her own.

"Right. Well then, why don't you try fighting them?"

The voice that suddenly reached his ears belonged to one of the merchants—the gruff-looking man.

"We've heard rumors about you too. But we've only seen you fight bandits. We'd like to see your true strength properly."

"I'm traveling with them, but I'm not officially hired as their bodyguard, you know?"

Even as he said this, he could picture Magita pestering him endlessly if he didn't act.

He decided it would ultimately save him trouble to clearly demonstrate the difference in their abilities now. Sighing, he finally rose from the chair he'd been sitting in.

"Fine. I recall there's a fairly large garden behind this inn. Let's go there."

"Oh? Finally decided to do it? Alright, let's go."

Magita flashed a delighted smile at Rei's words.
