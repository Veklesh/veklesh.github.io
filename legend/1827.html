The person standing there was a man named Rei, yet he didn’t look like Rei at all.

At the very least, anyone who only knew Rei from Gilm would never believe that Rei standing before them now was the same person.

“What do you think? I did my best to tailor it to suit you.”

The female merchant who had brought the custom-made suit Rei was wearing asked him that.

She was a woman in her forties, and it was clear from the fact that she had been summoned to the Duke of Kerebel’s residence that she was quite capable.

Although they had only taken his measurements a few days ago, the fact that the custom-made suit was already finished was surprising yet understandable to Rei.

If Rei had any real knowledge of custom-made clothing, he would have realized just how absurd it was to create a custom-made suit in such a short time—especially one suitable for a party hosted by the Duke of Kerebel, where he would be mingling with the aristocracy.

Naturally, he had used magic items to create it as quickly as possible—yet without cutting any corners—and looking at the suit Rei was currently wearing, no one would dare dismiss it as a hasty, makeshift creation.

“Yeah, it’s a little hard to move in… but well, it’s this kind of outfit, so I guess that’s to be expected.”

In reality, it’s precisely because it’s custom-made that it’s only that restrictive.

Rei usually wears the Dragon Robe, and the clothes he wears underneath are also quite loose-fitting.

That’s precisely why, no matter how high-quality it is, wearing a suit like this inevitably feels restrictive.

Rei understands this, so he doesn’t ask for the suit to be altered in this situation.

Even without knowing much about custom tailoring, it’s easy to understand just how difficult it was to make this.

Besides, it’s just a bit restrictive—it doesn’t actually hinder his movement to any significant degree.

Seeing Rei like this, the merchant woman who brought the suit feels relieved, though she doesn’t let it show on her face.

If he’d been asked to make alterations in this situation, it would almost certainly have become a situation she couldn’t handle.

(So this is the one with the nickname “Crimson,” huh. I’m glad she doesn’t seem like the type to act all high and mighty for no reason.)

Looking at Rei’s face again, she realized that despite his small stature, he had such well-defined features—so much so that one could even call them feminine—that he hardly looked like a man at all.

She was small in stature, but there was no doubt she possessed a certain radiance.

I suddenly find myself thinking that, if possible, I would have liked to take more time to prepare clothes truly befitting Rei.

(Though, of course, I don’t have time for that sort of thing.)

A shop run by a woman who employs skilled artisans naturally receives a wide variety of orders.

Especially at this time of year, with orders pouring in from nobles preparing for parties, she’s so busy she barely has time to sleep.

That said, the work isn’t so much about creating completely custom-made garments from scratch, like Rei’s, but rather about altering existing clothes.

They adjust clothes that used to fit—whether from a few months, half a year, or even last year—but no longer do because the wearer’s figure has changed.

Such work comes pouring in one after another.

Of course, not all of them are like that, just because they’re nobles.

In any case, with such a massive workload right now, there’s simply no time to spare for thinking about creating clothes worthy of Rei…

“If everything looks good, would it be all right if I hand this over to you now?”

“Yes. That’s fine.”

At Rei’s curt reply, the merchant smiled and simply said, “Please do come back if you have the chance,” before leaving the room.

And just as the merchant left, Miranda appeared in her place.

Normally, it wouldn’t have been strange for Miranda to be present during the conversation with the merchant, but she hadn’t been there because Rei had asked her to go check on Set.

“How was Set? Was he sulking?”

“Yes, he was playing with Yellow. But… he did seem a little lonely, didn’t he?”

“……I see.”

While Set unleashed his power without mercy against enemies—whether using his beak, forelimbs, or skills—he was fundamentally affectionate and had a clingy personality.

This was especially true when it came to Rei, whom he adored… So when Rei didn’t show up at the time he’d promised, it was quite lonely for Set.

“Now that I’ve finished handing over the clothes, maybe I’ll go see Set in a little while. I’ll pick up a souvenir from the kitchen or something.”

Although Set was being cared for in the stables at Kerebel Duke’s residence, the meals served there were, of course, even more delicious than what ordinary people ate.

Even though Set could eat raw monster meat without any trouble, he still preferred his meals to be delicious—that was likely his sentiment.

That said, the meals served to Set aren’t prepared by Georgima herself, but by others… yet even those others possess skills worthy of being called first-rate, thanks to Georgima’s teachings.

Of course, that doesn’t apply to everyone; there were some who, like when Rei went to observe before, were reprimanded for their control of the heat.

“I think that’s a good idea. But… before that, you might want to take off your clothes. You don’t want to get your nice clothes dirty, after all.”

Rei, hearing this, nodded in response.

If he got these clothes dirty, he’d have nothing left to wear to the party.

Well, considering the financial resources of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, there might be a way to handle it, but Rei didn’t intend to rely on them that much.

Besides, getting his clothes dirty was entirely Rei's own doing.

So, following Miranda’s instructions, Rei took off his clothes and handed them to her.

Normally, he could have stored the clothes in the Misty Ring, but Miranda forbade it.

Her reasoning was that there were various tasks involved in storing the clothes.

Given that explanation, Rei had no choice but to agree.

Having been a high school student in Japan, Rei basically had no knowledge of how to store suits or formal wear.

Rei had attended relatives’ weddings and funerals before, but since he was still a student, he had always attended in his high school uniform—a blazer.

That’s exactly why, when Miranda tells him he has to do it that way, he accepts it as just the way things are.

So, she changed into her usual Dragon Robe...

“Ah…”

Seeing Rei like that, Miranda let out a disappointed sigh.

A little taken aback by her obvious reaction, Rei asked,

“Um… Was something off?”

“Oh, no. It’s not that… It’s just that outfit really suited you, Rei. I thought it might have been nice to show it to Lady Elena and Lady Ara at least once.”

Hearing that she should have shown it to them, Rei felt as though she had somehow become a spectacle.

Still, since the people she wanted to show it to were Elena and Ara, he didn’t feel all that bad about it.

“I don’t think there’s any need to go out of your way to call them over just to see it. You’ll be able to see it in person at the party anyway.”

“You just don’t understand women. I mean, aside from Ara, Elena would definitely want to see it, wouldn’t she? Though, since I’ve already taken it off, I suppose it’s too late now.”

“...I suppose so. Well, let’s just consider it a treat for the party. Honestly, I don’t think it’s something to worry about that much.”

Rei said that, but Miranda, just like the merchant who brought this dress, felt that seeing Rei in a suit was definitely a treat for the eyes.

Of course, she understood that it wasn’t something worth making such a fuss over… that it certainly couldn’t compare to Elena in her dress. She understood that… but even so, it was an undeniable fact that it was worth seeing.

So much so that she even wondered if Rei might be upset that she was the first one to see him in a suit.

That said, Rei had already changed back into his usual clothes.

In that case, there was no point in saying anything more now.

Maybe—just maybe—if I asked Rei, he might put the suit back on, but as a maid, I couldn’t possibly say such a thing.

“Well then, I’ll leave the clothes to Miranda. I’m going to go see Set.”

“Yes, leave it to me.”

After exchanging a few brief words, Rei walked out of the room...

“Huh?”

The moment he stepped out of the room, several people passed by in the hallway a short distance away, and one of them noticed Rei and let out a gasp.

Rei turned his gaze toward the source of the voice. Though he didn’t let it show, he felt he’d run into a troublesome group.

Because the person who had spoken… and those around them were clearly wearing high-quality clothing.

People like that, present at the Kerebel Duke’s residence at this time of year.

It was obvious without even having to think about it who they were.

Yes, it was as if the nobles who had come to attend the New Year’s party had arrived in Anesis and were coming to greet Duke Kerebel’s Liberte… or something like that.

(Am I going to get hassled again?)

That’s what Rei thought… but his prediction turned out to be wrong—in a good way.

“Oh, could you be Rei of Crimson? My son has told me about you. Apparently, you wiped out the other adventurers and the mercenaries hired by the Noble Faction during the mock battle. It’s a pity that someone of your skill is with the Neutral Faction, but even so, if we can cooperate from now on, I’d be delighted.”

“Huh?”

It was no wonder Rei let out such a sound at the noble’s words.

She had been certain he was going to pick a fight again, but that wasn’t the case at all—on the contrary, he was approaching her in a friendly manner.

Still, Rei quickly came to his senses and spoke, sounding slightly flustered.

“Ah, I’m hoping for a good relationship as well.”

Saying that, Rei turned his gaze back to the man standing before him.

The man there was in his late twenties.

It wasn’t that she’d never interacted amicably with nobles of that age before, but they were certainly few and far between.

In fact, the nobles with whom Rei could build good relationships were more often those who didn’t seem like typical nobles, like Brewitt.

Elena and Ara were nobles, too, but if anything, they would likely be classified as “un-noble” nobles.

That said, in that sense, Miranda—who is officially assigned to Rei—also comes from a noble family, strictly speaking…

“Ha ha ha. I see. Well then, I’ll take my leave now. I have quite a few things to attend to. I look forward to speaking with you again… at the New Year’s party.”

“Viscount, it’s about time… there’s not much time left before your next engagement…”

“Understood. Well then, see you later.”

With that, the nobleman addressed as “Viscount” walked away from Rei.

His entourage followed behind him, but none of them cast a condescending glance in Rei’s direction.

“So there are nobles like that, too.”

That was Rei’s honest impression.

Then again, come to think of it, that was only natural.

If every noble in the Noble Faction were exactly like Gaiska, the faction itself would have vanished long ago.

Of course, many possess a high sense of pride, but surely there were more who possessed the ability to match that pride—or at least refrained from looking down on others for no reason.

With a slightly surprised look on his face, Rei left the scene.

Naturally, he headed for the stables.

Although Miranda had been keeping Set company, Rei felt he should go himself, even if it meant arriving a little late.

That said, from what Miranda had told him about Set’s condition, he could tell the boy was feeling lonely, so he hurried a little.

That said, he wouldn’t go so far as to run through the Kerebel Duke’s mansion.

After all, this was the residence of Duke Kerebel, the leader of the Noble Faction.

If someone were seen rushing about in such a place, it might be seen as rude and lead to contempt, or people might assume there was some serious problem.

Of course, if there really were a problem, running around wouldn’t be out of place… but at the very least, it’s definitely best to avoid doing something like that just to appease Set.

(For now, food is the way to win Set’s favor. …A dish made with Gamelion meat… no, maybe something even tastier would be better? I’ve been making him feel lonely lately.)

With those thoughts in mind, Rei hurried to the stable where Set was.
