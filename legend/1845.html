"Grr, grrrrrrr!"

Seeing Rei enter the stable, Set let out a cry as if it were only natural.

That cry showed just how happy he was that Rei had come to see him.

“Hmm… So this is Set, the griffin?”

Remuria asked this, her surprise evident as she watched Set rub his face against Rei.

Naturally, Lemuria was familiar with the griffin monster.

After all, since she needed to collect griffin feathers, it made sense that she would have gathered that information.

But… there was a huge difference between knowing about it as information or knowledge and actually seeing Set with her own eyes like this.

What surprised her most was his size.

With a body over three meters long, it wasn’t hard to imagine just how much power it must possess.

Presumably for Set’s sake, there was a magic item in the stable that emitted a light—not particularly bright, but sufficient nonetheless.

The sight of Set illuminated by that dim light even made Lemuria feel a desire to flee this place as quickly as possible.

Yet she didn’t make a move to flee—likely because she understood that Set’s feather was something of great importance to her. And then…

“Kyu? Kyu-kyu!”

The fact that Yellow was perched on Set’s head was also a major factor.

Even if you looked across the entire Kingdom of Mireana, there weren’t many nobles holding the title of Duke.

Amidst this, the Foolundal House, which belongs to the Noble Faction, naturally maintains close ties with the Kerebel House.

And since they were on such friendly terms, it naturally followed that they were acquainted with Elena, and thus knew of Yellow, her familiar.

That is precisely why, when Remuria saw Yellow perched on Set’s head, she felt the urge to run away but managed to stop herself from actually doing so.

“Ah. …Set, I don’t really need to introduce Brewitt, do I? So, this is Lemuria. Lemuria Furundal.”

“Growl? Grrr!”

Set tilted his head at Rei’s introduction, but he immediately looked over at Remuria and purred, “Nice to meet you!”

Seeing Set’s attitude, Lemuria finally seemed to relax and, looking composed, spoke up.

“Nice to meet you, Set.”

“Grrrrrr?”

Perhaps intrigued by Remuria’s “zoi” ending, Set purred.

Yellow, however, seemed used to Remuria’s speech patterns and showed no particular reaction; he flew off Set’s head and was picked up by Elena, who was petting him.

“Set, it seems Lemuria wants your feathers. Fresh ones, too… the kind that’ve just been plucked.”

“Grrr…”

The reason Set growled slightly in displeasure at Rei’s words was likely because he realized it wasn’t a feather lying on the ground, but one currently growing from his own body that was about to be plucked.

For Set, having a feather plucked isn’t that painful. It isn’t… but even so, there’s no doubt it doesn’t feel very good.

“Yikes!”

At the sound of Set’s slightly disgruntled cry, the wizard Remuria had hired let out a small scream.

While Lemuria couldn’t bring herself to act afraid of Set when it came to her own family, the mage had no such resolve.

Having suddenly found himself face-to-face with Set like this, it was only natural that he was startled.

Fortunately, Set merely glanced at the mage before turning his round eyes back to Lemuria.

Naturally, Set didn’t like pain either.

That’s why he hesitated for a moment… but then, staring intently at Lemuria, he eventually let out a short growl.

At that sound, the wizard employed by Lemuria let out another small scream, but Set didn’t even glance in that direction; instead, he turned his body toward Lemuria.

“...Rei?”

Confused by Set’s actions, Lemuria turned to Rei with a bewildered voice.

Since he’d turned his body toward her, wasn’t he signaling with his attitude that it was okay to pluck his feathers?

He wasn’t entirely without that thought, but if he plucked the feathers and was wrong, he might end up angering Set.

That was why she asked, just to be on the safe side… but in response to such a Remuria, Rei nodded and spoke.

“It seems it’s okay to pluck the feathers. Though, I do hope you won’t make it hurt too much.”

“Of course.”

Nodding at Rei’s words, Lemuria gently reached out to Set’s body… and eventually plucked out a few feathers.

Set seemed to show a slight sign of pain as the feathers were pulled out, but he didn’t struggle.

Then again, when it comes to pain, being a monster, Set does have some resistance.

After all, when fighting enemies—including monsters—it’s only natural to take the occasional hit.

“Grr…”

“Thank you, Set.”

As Set growled in response to having his feather plucked, Lemuria offered him her thanks.

As the head of the Duke's House, perhaps this current appearance was not befitting of her.

But when she considered that her family’s illness might be cured thanks to Set’s feathers, words of gratitude naturally came to her lips.

Hearing this, Set purred happily.

He probably wanted to say that enduring the pain to have his feathers plucked was worth it.

“Hehe, Set’s as friendly as ever,”

The one who said this was Elena, who was petting Yellow.

……That said, from Rei’s perspective, seeing Yellow so happy to be petted by Elena, he couldn’t quite bring himself to agree wholeheartedly when she called him friendly.

In any case, ignoring Rei and Elena’s attitudes, Lemuria placed Set’s feather into a container she’d taken from her bosom, then pulled the mage casting the summoning spell out of the stable.

Given her need for fresh griffin feathers, she likely wanted to send Set’s feathers to her territory as quickly as possible.

As he followed Remuria and Set, Rei suddenly found himself wondering.

(Come to think of it, Anesis should naturally have a barrier in place to ward off intruders, including monsters from the sky… so what are they going to do?)

As the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, Anesis naturally has strict defenses.

In the frontier town of Gilm, a barrier is erected at night to ward off monsters arriving from the sky—though the current construction phase is an exception—but in Anesis, there are those who fly through the air besides monsters, such as dragon knights.

Furthermore, even without flying, many attempt to illegally scale the walls and sneak into Anesis by other means.

That is why a barrier has been erected around Anesis.

Even if one were to summon flying animals or monsters, they should be unable to leave due to being blocked by that barrier.

It would be a different story if they had obtained prior permission from Liberte, the lord of Anesis, but he would hardly grant permission to temporarily lift the barrier so easily.

So, what would they do?

Rei’s suspicion was… confirmed the moment he saw Lemuria leaving the Duke of Kerebel’s residence in a carriage.

“Ah, I see. If she uses summoning magic outside of Anesis, the barrier doesn’t apply. …Even so, that’s quite the special treatment.”

Generally speaking, the gates leading outside the city, which are closed in the evening, are never opened unless there is a very exceptional circumstance.

But it seems that if Lemuria asks Liberte, she can receive that special treatment.

For Liberte, opening the gate wasn’t as big a deal as lifting a barrier, so it was only natural for him to grant permission in exchange for a certain fee.

“……Ah.”

Rei, Elena, Brewitt, Set, and Yellow—three people and two animals—had been watching the departing carriage, but suddenly, Brewitt spoke up.

“What’s wrong?”

“No, Rei. You said you were going to get a gem from that old woman in Lemuria, but you didn’t get it, did you?”

At those words, Rei thought, “Now that you mention it…” but Elena, who had been listening, immediately denied it.

“Someone of Duke Fuldar’s stature would never do such a thing. He left in such a hurry because he wants to deliver Set’s feather to his domain as soon as possible… but once that’s done—perhaps tonight, or by tomorrow at the latest—he’ll come to see us again to hand over the reward.”

Brewitt, too, didn’t seriously believe that Remuria wouldn’t pay the reward, so he didn’t make a move to contradict Elena’s words.

Rei, too, judged that given Lemuria’s character, she wouldn’t do such a thing, and so he didn’t interject.

Instead, he spoke while looking at the Kerebel Duke’s mansion.

“Well, this took longer than expected, but I think we should head back to the party soon.”

“I suppose so. I wouldn’t mind staying here and hanging out with Set and Yellow, though…”

Set and Yero both look delighted at Elena’s words, but… considering Elena’s position, she can’t very well act that way.

“I can’t do that. …Right?”

“Mm-hmm.”

At Rei’s words, Elena nodded regretfully.

Normally, Elena should still be at the party right now.

It was only because the request came from Remuria—a figure holding a title equivalent to that of the Duke of Kerebel—that she was able to step outside the party venue… or rather, outside the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion at all. But that was merely an exception.

It was precisely because Elena understood this that she reluctantly agreed with Rei’s words.

...In reality, Rei also wanted to play with Set and Yellow alongside Elena and Brewitt.

In any case, if they were to start playing around here, their party clothes would inevitably get dirty.

Considering that, they really didn’t have the luxury of time to linger here.

“Shall we head back?”

At Elena’s words, Rei and Brewitt nodded, while Set and Yellow let out disappointed cries.

“Oh my, Lady Elena. I thought you weren’t here… but it seems you were having a secret rendezvous with a gentleman, weren’t you?”

Upon returning to the party venue, Elena was suddenly addressed by that voice.

The tone was clearly provocative, and Rei, who was with her, honestly wondered at first, Is she serious?

And rightly so. Known as the “Princess General” and the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel, Elena was, in a sense, the very symbol of the Noble Faction. …Though she herself didn’t seem to care much for that fact.

To hurl such provocative words at a figure like her—words that seemed like a direct challenge—was undoubtedly an act of sheer impudence.

(Is she serious, this woman?)

Thinking this, Rei looked at the woman standing before Elena.

She was beautiful. Her features were so well-defined that one could easily call her a beauty, yet he wasn’t captivated by her at that moment.

Because the eyes she cast upon Elena were filled with intense jealousy.

At the very least, if asked whether he could have a good time with the woman in front of him, Rei would not be able to nod in agreement.

“Lirin, is it? It’s been a while.”

“Yes. That’s true. Since Elena doesn’t attend parties very often, it inevitably feels like it’s been a long time. …Though, for Elena, a barbaric battlefield probably suits her better than an elegant party.”

“That’s true. To be honest, I don’t care for parties like this. …That said, since this is a New Year’s party, I can’t very well not attend.”

Faced with such a bluntly sarcastic reply, the woman named Lirin flashed a look of frustration for just a moment.

But her gaze immediately shifted to Rei… and she addressed him with a beaming smile, as if to ask what had just happened to her expression.

“You must be Rei, right? I’ve heard all sorts of rumors… but I was sure you’d be a much more barbaric-looking sort of person.”

As she said this, Lirin’s gaze shifted to Brewitt, who was standing beside Rei, watching the exchange with an amused expression.

Normally, Brewitt might have been angry at Lirin’s attitude, but he seemed to find watching the exchange unfolding before him more entertaining than getting upset. He didn’t seem inclined to say anything in particular.

“I suppose I should say, ‘Thank you very much’?”

“……Well, I suppose so.”

Perhaps displeased by Rei’s tone, Leelin furrowed her brows with an expression that clearly said, “I’m not happy about this.”

When she was talking to Elena, that displeased look vanished in an instant, but she showed no sign of it toward Rei.

This is likely because, while Elena is the daughter of Duke Kerebel, Rei is nothing more than a mere adventurer.

“Just as I expected from a lowly adventurer. It’s beneath your station for someone like you to show up at a noble social gathering like this. And besides…”

Lirin was about to say something else, but...

“Lirin.”

Elena’s words echoed throughout the room.
