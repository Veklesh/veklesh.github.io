"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

A scream echoed through the summer sky.

Had this been near the highway, passersby might have heard it, but now the scream echoed from a place far from the highway... above.

The sky was clear blue, and the sun poured down its intense, summery light as if making a strong statement... And into that sky, Hester's scream echoed.

"Calm down already. You're the one who insisted on coming with me to take down the goblin leader, no matter what it took. So how about you just deal with this much?"

"Don't be ridiculous!"

Hester screamed.

Her voice came from below... below Rei ... and below Set, whom Rei was straddling.

Yes, from Hester, who was clinging to Set's front legs and dangling in midair.

Originally, Rei had intended to go defeat the Goblin Leader with Set, but Hester insisted she wanted to come along too.

Rei himself wanted to take Hester, whose comrades had been killed, but only Rei could ride Set.

And if he moved leisurely across the ground with Hester, there was a chance the goblin leader could escape somewhere.

As Rei hesitated, wondering what to do, a memory flashed through his mind: the sight of Set once catching a giant bear with just his front paws and flying through the sky.

That bear was clearly heavier than Hester... In fact, compared to Hester, who would likely be equipped with a longsword and leather armor, it was probably three times her weight... maybe even four times.

If he could fly through the air carrying that much weight with just his front legs, surely carrying one human wouldn't be a problem?

Acting on that thought, he tried it... and the result was this very scene of him flying through the air.

Set, flapping his wings as he flew through the sky, showed no sign of feeling the weight at all.

Riding on his back would be manageable only for a small child besides Rei, but in terms of carrying someone by grabbing their legs, he moves without any trouble at all.

The weight itself isn't an issue at all, but Hester struggling while being held is an absolute nuisance for Set.

"Grrr..."

Understanding Set's thoughts, Rei called out to Hester with an exasperated tone.

"If you struggle too much, Set won't be able to hold on and you'll fall."

The moment Hester heard the word "drop" from Rei's mouth, her movements froze completely, as if she were a statue or something.

They were currently flying at an altitude of about 100 meters, which was their usual height for Rei and Set.

But that was only the usual altitude for Rei and Set. For Hester, who had never flown since birth, this was the first altitude she had ever experienced.

In fact, this was her first experience of flying at all, and a hundred meters was probably beyond what she could handle.

(Huh? But if that's what you mean, then the first time I experienced flying was when I rode Set, right?)

Holding onto Set's front legs, completely still, Hester remained silent. Ray listened to her silence as he thought.

Even when Rei was in Japan, the act of flying wasn't particularly rare.

From commonplace things like airplanes to helicopters, parachutes, gliders, and so on.

But living in the countryside, Rei had no chance to ride any of those things.

Despite that, when Rei first flew through the air riding Set, he felt absolutely no fear whatsoever.

(Was it because he knew what to expect, having some prior knowledge even though it was his first time? Well, that would make sense.)

Footage taken by people flying is something you can see regularly on TV.

Thinking about it that way, in terms of prior knowledge, Rei had a significant advantage over Hester.

Beyond prior knowledge, trusting Set probably played a big part too.

Hester knew almost nothing about Set, and therefore couldn't trust him completely like Rei did.

"Just stay quiet like that. We're already over the forest. This is the area, right? Where you guys got attacked by goblins. Considering that, I think it's better not to make a sound."

Hester gasped at Rei's words.

She must have decided that avenging her comrades took priority over her own current situation.

This quick judgment... or rather, this ruthless pragmatism, was precisely why Rei had taken an interest in Hester.

Even at the guild, despite feeling awe toward Rei, she'd instantly decided to accompany him for the sake of avenging her comrades and bowed her head.

Quick decision-making was undoubtedly a necessary skill for adventurers, given the unpredictable situations they could get caught up in during a quest.

(Though whether goblins on the ground could hear voices from a hundred meters up was another matter entirely.)

But Rei's concern was far from unfounded.

It was highly likely that the goblin leader they were about to fight was a rare species.

In other words, it's impossible to completely rule out the possibility that its hearing might be abnormally acute.

Still, Rei doesn't believe its abilities are that limited.

"Gluu!"

While surveying the surroundings as he moved back and forth above the forest canopy, Set suddenly let out a sharp cry.

Understanding the meaning of that cry, Rei shifted his gaze to the ground... to where Set was looking.

The ground was a forest, naturally filled with various trees, their leaves thick and lush, especially in summer.

These trees served as a blind spot for monsters attacking from above, making it impossible to see down to the ground from above in many places.

However, that was only true in most places; it didn't completely block the view from above.

Especially for a griffin like Set, it was possible to see the ground through gaps in the dense foliage, and there were also areas not completely covered by leaves.

And while one or two goblins might be manageable, with around a hundred, it was impossible to completely conceal them all.

Rei also directed his gaze to the spot where Set was intently looking.

It was impossible to spot goblins through the gaps in the dense foliage covering the entire forest, but focusing intently on a single narrow area made it possible to scrutinize those gaps.

"...There it is."

There, at the end of Rei's gaze, was the goblin they were looking for.

Of course, this goblin wasn't necessarily the one that attacked Hester's party.

As Hester had said, he needed to confirm whether it was indeed a group of about a hundred... That's what Rei was thinking, but Hester, hanging from Set's front paws with both shoulders gripped, muttered resentfully.

"Rei, please. Hurry up and deal with those guys..."

"Calm down. We still don't know if that goblin is really one of the ones who attacked you. If that's the case, we should confirm it first."

"...No, I know. It's them."

Hester's certainty made Rei feel uneasy.

Was she just so consumed by hatred for goblins that she wanted to kill any goblin, no matter what?

If he could see her face, he might understand her feelings better.

But right now, Hester was hanging from Set's shoulders, so he couldn't see her face.

Should we go back? Rei considered it, but Hester spoke as if to stop him.

"I can't tell for sure from this distance, but I think that halberd the goblin in that gap is holding belongs to someone I used to party with."

"All halberds aren't exactly the same... but it's natural for them to share similar shapes, right?"

Before Rei could voice that thought, Hester continued.

"See, Rei? Can you tell the pommel part of the halberd is blue? That was Dargey's... the guy who used that halberd's personal touch. Even when everyone said it was tacky to have just that part blue, he never tried to fix it."

"...I see."

He muttered briefly, having noticed that the pommel of the halberd was indeed painted blue, just as Hester had said.

Hester had been able to confirm the blue-painted pommel because Set, who was flying, had been gradually descending.

And if Hester could see the pommel, it meant Rei could see it even more clearly than she could.

"Then it's settled. ...Alright, Set. Let's do this. He seems like a clever goblin leader, so we absolutely can't let him escape. We'll take him down. I'll use magic for the first time in a while."

"Guruu!"

At Rei's words, Set growled in understanding.

Despite Rei planning to use fire magic in this dense forest, Set showed no sign of concern for him.

That was how much he trusted Rei.

...Hester, hanging from Set's shoulder, heard those words and assumed Rei would use magic that wouldn't damage the forest... not his signature flame magic.

After all, Rei had always given the impression he could use magic other than fire, starting with his Flying Slash.

Considering that, it wasn't surprising Hester had made such a mistake.

"Hester, what are you going to do?"

Hester, who had been smiling at the certainty of the goblin leader's death, paused to think when Rei asked her that question from atop Set's back.

If possible, he wanted to kill the goblin leader with his own hands. But...

But... Before Hester could answer, Set's face turned toward the woods.

And Rei, too, turned a displeased expression toward that same forest.

Hester couldn't see Rei's movements, but she could clearly see Set's.

Hester, puzzled by Set's behavior, voiced her question.

"Hey, wait. Set's acting kinda weird. What's up? Rei?"

"...I heard the sound of someone's pain coming from the woods. I don't know if it's your party, but there's still someone alive in there."

"Huh!? Really!?"

Hester gasped sharply at Rei's words.

The thought flashed through her mind—maybe one of her party members was still alive.

That hope flashed through her mind.

But despite Hester's hopeful words, Rei's expression remained grim.

Even if they were alive in this situation, it wasn't hard to imagine what kind of state they might be in.

Why would goblins keep captured adventurers alive in the first place? Considering that, it was far from something to be happy about.

Understanding what it meant, Rei asked Hester, who hung from Set.

"Was there a woman in your party?"

"Huh!?"

That single question made Hester understand what Rei was getting at. She gasped sharply, though for a different reason than just seconds before. She nodded quickly, signaling there was no problem... but then realized that wouldn't show Rei her resolve, so she opened her mouth.

"Rest assured. Every member of my party was male."

Like orcs, goblins also rape humans, beastmen, elves, and others to leave their own offspring.

Having witnessed that very scene when they raided an orc settlement before, Rei let out a small sigh of relief at Hester's words.

"I see. Well, that's good. But... that still presents its own problem."

His relief was short-lived, and Rei's expression hardened once more.

While they might capture women to rape them, what purpose would capturing male members serve?

For food, or for abuse... There were likely other reasons too, but broadly speaking, it came down to those two.

And what reached Rei's ears was the sound of agony.

It meant there was no doubt that the situation Rei feared was unfolding in the woods.

"Rei!"

"Yeah, I know. ...Set, change of plans."

Originally, he had intended to use fire magic to incinerate the goblins all at once.

There was a risk of collateral damage, but he had judged that could be managed depending on the type of magic.

But... if Hester's comrades were being held captive, he couldn't possibly do such a thing.

If they were already dead, such a method might have been possible... but since cries of agony reached his ears, they were undoubtedly still alive.

"Hester, Set and I will take out the goblins. You secure and protect the one they've captured. Got it?"

"Got it!"

Rei nodded at the forceful shout... then leapt down from Set's back.

"Huh?"

Seeing Rei suddenly plunge straight down to the ground right beside her, Hester could only manage a startled gasp.
