"Hey, wait! Wait!"

Ilse shouted as he barely dodged Ilse's strike.

The shock from the Death Scythe's blow made moving his body difficult. Even if he could move, with Rei and his companions, plus the intelligence unit members all around, escaping would be nearly impossible.

Ajax understood this, but that didn't mean he wanted to take Ilse's blow head-on.

"Oh? Why are you running? Come on, take my blade properly!"

The blade swung again.

Ajax managed to dodge it, but the fact that Ilse showed no hesitation in swinging her blade at him was somewhat unexpected.

Of course, Ajax knew he was the enemy of Ilse's family.

But even so, Ajax hadn't expected that a young woman like Ilse, when finally taking revenge, would swing her blade down so decisively.

Had Ajax not dodged, Ilse's blade would undoubtedly have severed his ear... or worse, if it had missed its mark, dealt a fatal blow to his head.

Knowing this only made Ajax reflexively dodge Ilse's strike.

「Ajax！」

Seeing Ajax react, Levjef also shouted reflexively.

But with the tip of the Twilight Spear pressed against him by Rei, he couldn't take any action.

If he tried to do anything, the spearhead would surely pierce his own body.

If it were merely a matter of being pierced, Levegef wouldn't exactly welcome it... but he wouldn't necessarily object either.

But getting pierced would, of course, inflict damage.

And that damage would undoubtedly slow him down when trying to escape this place.

Given that, it was only natural to want to avoid unnecessary damage as much as possible.

"You talk like some righteous hero worried about his comrades, but you're Ajax's ally too. Considering all the evil deeds you've committed up to now, you can't possibly say that, can you?"

Rei, holding the Twilight Spear pointed at Ajax, muttered in exasperation at Rebejev's concern for Ajax.

To an uninformed observer, what was happening here looked like one man holding another hostage, while a woman attacked the helpless man one-sidedly.

If asked who was at fault, anyone lacking the background information would undoubtedly say Rei and her group were the villains.

"I know that. But telling me not to worry about my friend is asking the impossible!"

Rei frowned at Levegef's shouting.

"You'd say that if your own comrades were hurt, yet you've committed countless sins up until now. ...How selfish."

With those words, Rei stowed the Death Scythe in his right hand into the Misty Ring and then slipped that same hand inside his Dragon Robe.

The moment Levjef saw Rei's movement, a bad feeling washed over him... but it was too late to voice it.

In the hand Rei withdrew from his Dragon Robe, something was clenched.

Levjef didn't recognize it, but it was an arrowhead created by Nebra's Eye, one of Rei's magic items.

This arrowhead, created by magic, possessed the characteristic of disappearing after a short time.

But it was more than enough to use as a weapon in combat.

"Ajax, dodge!"

Realizing what Rei was aiming for, Rebegef shouted. But by the time the words left his mouth, Rei's right hand had already flicked the arrowhead.

The arrow, slicing through the air, left a thin cut on Ajax's left cheek.

It was a scratch, barely worth mentioning, but for Ajax, who was desperately trying to evade Ilse's attack, stopping his movement even for an instant was a huge mistake.

Under normal circumstances, sustaining such a minor scratch would have posed no problem in evading the attack.

But now, having suffered an attack from the Death Scythe, he was far from being in peak condition.

As a result, Ilse's dagger, swung down with force, easily severed Ajax's right ear.

"Gwaah!"

More than pain, it was heat.

That sensation assaulted Ajax's right ear.

Ajax instinctively clutched his right ear, but the familiar sensation of his ear was gone, replaced only by the slimy feel of blood.

Blood dripped from his clutching hand.

Even under the moonlight, Ajax could clearly see his right ear lying on the ground.

Even so, the scream that escaped Ajax's mouth was brief, likely because he was prepared for it.

"The left ear too..."

"That's enough, Ilse."

Ilse, who had finally succeeded in taking the right ear, was about to proceed to the left ear... but a voice stopped her.

Ilse knew whose voice it was. She knew... yet she shouted at the one who dared interfere with her striking the dagger at the enemy of her family she had pursued for so many years.

"Why, Rei-san!"

"Calm down. I should have told you beforehand. I'll hear the details from Ajax."

"But..."

Rei's words snapped Ilse out of her extreme agitation.

Yes, ultimately, she would take Ajax's life. Before that, it was only fair to hear his side of the story first.

"My apologies. I got a little carried away."

A little? The intelligence unit members watching her muttered inwardly.

Precisely because Ilse possessed a beauty worthy of the name, her earlier bloodthirsty frenzy had completely repulsed the men of the intelligence unit.

Killing people wasn't uncommon for men in intelligence work, given the nature of their jobs.

But... there was a difference in intensity between doing it themselves and seeing a beauty like Ilse do it.

Completely oblivious to the feelings of the intelligence unit members, Rei placed a hand over the spot where her right ear had been and spoke to Ajax, who was trying to stop the blood flowing from it.

"Now then, let me ask you again. Why did you go and kidnap three carrieloads of women? Where were you planning to take them?"

"W-Well..."

Under normal circumstances, Ajax wouldn't have listened to such words.

But now, Ilse, who was actually targeting his life, stood right before him, and his own body was barely functional from Rei's attack.

Moreover, with overwhelmingly stronger individuals surrounding him, he was utterly helpless.

(The only solace was that Haston had been healed and Levjeff hadn't been attacked.)

He knew it well: Ilse's target was Ajax.

Therefore, the fact that she hadn't laid a hand on the other two was a relief for Ajax. ...Hastun had his right knee severed and sustained several wounds in the ensuing battle, but fortunately, he's been treated now.

(But if I don't say anything here... knowing Rei's personality, he might just hand me over to Ilse without a second thought.)

I considered just talking my way out of it to confuse Rei, but even if it were just Rei, there were many others here besides him.

Given that, even if I could somehow deal with Rei, the others would be beyond my control.

Worse still, deceiving Rei could lead to the worst possible outcome.

What should I do to save myself and my comrades?

With that thought, Ajax opened his mouth, enduring the pain in his right ear.

"Alright! I'll explain the reason, and I'll tell you who gave the orders. But you have to guarantee the safety of me, Levjef, and Huston!"

At those words, nearly everyone present turned their gaze toward Rei.

To be precise, it was Ilse who wanted Ajax dead, and Rei was merely assisting her... Still, it seemed Rei's presence alone was enough to draw everyone's attention.

Under that gaze, Rei paused to consider his options... then, looking at Ajax, he spoke.

"Well... I might consider it, depending on the information you provide."

Hearing Rei's words, Ilse reflexively tried to say something to him, but was stopped by Byune standing right beside her.

"You mean it?"

"Yeah. ...So? Why did you pull this stunt? Those women you kidnapped are crammed into that carriage, right?"

"That's right. They're wearing slave collars, so they won't make any trouble. ...Gathering the women was an order from above. It wasn't our idea."

Rei explained the situation more readily than he had anticipated.

For Ajax, the question of which mattered more—the organization he currently belonged to, or his longtime comrades, Levjef and Huston—had its answer decided from the very beginning.

To save his two comrades, betraying the organization was nothing to him.

"From the top of the organization? Then what is that organization thinking? Surely they aren't just forcibly taking women this far just to run a brothel... that can't be it, right?"

"No, the women we took are working in the brothel. That's undeniable. This isn't a joke!"

The moment Ajax mentioned they were working in a brothel and saw Rei's gaze shift to Ilse, he asserted firmly.

But the intensity in Rei's gaze didn't waver.

"There should be plenty of women who want to be prostitutes anywhere, shouldn't there?"

Rei's words were not untrue.

Prostitution is a profession one can enter with nothing but their body; even women without any skills can work in it.

Of course, some women in the world refuse to accept prostitutes, but it's also true that the very existence of prostitutes helps suppress crimes like women being assaulted.

Moreover, for those living in the slums, prostitution is a quick way out.

But to say they came all the way to a place like Gilm to forcibly abduct a woman just to obtain such a prostitute... most wouldn't believe it.

At least, Rei couldn't believe it.

But seeing Rei's reaction, Ajax didn't change his words.

"It's no lie. I don't know what the higher-ups are thinking, but it's certain the women taken there are working as prostitutes."

Rei tilted his head, his gaze still intense as he listened to Ajax's definitive words.

(If he's so certain in this situation, Ajax is probably telling the truth. But... why go to the trouble of turning the woman they took into a prostitute?)

That part, I don't understand.

As Rei had said, anyone who wanted to be a prostitute could basically become one without issue.

Of course, not everyone can become one—some might not speak well for some reason, or be unattractive to men. Even after becoming a prostitute, many struggle to attract clients.

Even so, deliberately coming to a dangerous place like Gilm and making a deal with the underground organization... that clearly wasn't a normal situation.

By any normal reasoning, it was certain there was some reason beyond prostitution.

"You really don't know anything more than that?"

"Yeah, I don't know the reason."

Glancing at Ajax, who stated this flatly, Rei turned his gaze to the intelligence unit.

His gaze asked if they knew anything, but the intelligence unit members all shook their heads.

Concluding they truly knew nothing, Rei paused to think... then moved to his next question.

"Where is the headquarters of the organization that ordered you to handle this incident?"

Honestly, this information wasn't that crucial for Rei.

But even if it wasn't essential for Rei, it was crucial information the intelligence unit needed to know.

Since they had clearly antagonized Gilm to this extent, it was safe to recognize that organization as an enemy.

Ajax answered Rei's question without hesitation.

"The capital of Reburrina, Rossi."

"...Huh?"

The words that came out of Ajax's mouth were completely unexpected.

Since the word "capital" was used, this place called Rossi must undoubtedly be the capital of a nation.

But the capital of the Mireana Kingdom was called Kafiri, not Rossi.

"Leburrina!? Why would that country do such a thing!"

Rei didn't understand, but Elena, who had been listening to the conversation, seemed to recognize the name. Unusually, she looked at Ajax with an expression that could only be called astonishment.

Seeing Elena like this, Rei glanced at the others.

Marina, Vihera, and the members of the intelligence unit all wore expressions of utter shock. Byune remained impassive as ever, his thoughts impossible to read.

Ilse glared at Ajax with a murderous look in her eyes.

"So, this country called Reburlina... I've never heard of it. What kind of place is it?"

"It's one of the small nations bordering this one, and simultaneously a vassal state of the Mireana Kingdom."

Elena replied to Rei's words.
