Thud.

A man plops down on his butt.

With a thin layer of snow on the ground, his butt must be wet from the snow… but the man’s face is frozen in terror, and he certainly has no spare thought to notice the cold.

But that’s only natural.

Standing right in front of him is Rei, an adventurer with a notorious nickname even in Gilm.

“Now then, tell me about that red cloth wrapped around your arm. Your companions seem to be getting quite excited… Care to join in the fun?”

The man’s companions were getting excited, and Rei was saying they should get excited.

Even though the words themselves were the same, the man could tell the meaning was clearly different.

It was clear that the “getting excited” Rei was talking about was the kind that promised nothing but a miserable future for him.

The man was reasonably confident in his physical strength and had no intention of losing a fight to just anyone.

In fact, he’d even beaten a lumberjack who’d boasted about his strength, so it wasn’t exactly overconfidence.

But… that was only against ordinary opponents; he couldn’t possibly imagine winning a fight against someone like Rei.

It was his misfortune that Rei caught him trying to threaten a young man and steal his money.

No, being caught was bad luck, but what was even worse was that the man had a red cloth wrapped around his right arm.

A red cloth the man had received from a friend.

It was a “cloth of friendship”—a symbol of a promise that anyone wearing this red cloth was a comrade who would come to his aid in times of trouble.

Mind you, the man didn’t really believe in that sort of talk, but since all he had to do was wrap the cloth around his arm for the time being—and it didn’t cause him any particular inconvenience—he started wearing it.

As a result, there were a few times when he nearly got caught by security guards while extorting money, but since the person wearing the red cloth would warn him when a guard was approaching, he was able to get through those situations without any major problems… And so, today once again, he set out in high spirits, wearing the red cloth, to extort money and make a profit.

But then Rei caught me, and now I’m in this mess.

“H-h… I-I don’t know anything. I really don’t know anything!”

“You don’t know? I just wanted to ask you about that red cloth. You can’t possibly not know about that, can you?”

Rei continued speaking with a smile, putting pressure on his opponent.

Rei’s smile itself wasn’t particularly intimidating… but the fact that he was smiling in a situation like this put intense pressure on his opponent.

If it weren’t for the current situation, Rei’s smile would likely have looked like an ordinary one.

“O-okay. I’ll tell you anything I know! I’ll tell you, so—so—so… please help me!”

The man screamed desperately at Rei’s words.

Perhaps deciding that this was enough intimidation, Rei, still smiling, urged him to continue.

While the smile was the same as before, the one Rei was wearing now was clearly different from the one he’d had until just a moment ago.

Realizing this, the man exhaled as if relieved.

“Now then, you said you’d tell me everything honestly. So, who exactly gave you that red cloth you’re wearing? Is it safe to assume that everyone wearing that red cloth is a criminal?”

“From an acquaintance. A friend gave it to me. They said that if I wrap this cloth around my arm, my comrades will come to my aid if anything happens. I don’t know how the others use this red cloth, though.”

“I see. …So, where does your friend get these red cloths from? Everyone’s wearing the same one. They must be made by a specialist somewhere, right?”

“I don’t know that much! I just heard that if I tie this red cloth around my arm, they’ll come to my aid.”

“...Really? You really don’t know?”

A rage—not quite murderous intent—began to seep from Rei’s body.

But for a man who had never been in a life-or-death situation, even that level of anger was enough to strike fear into him.

He frantically nodded repeatedly.

"I’m telling the truth! I’m not lying! I asked about it once before, but they just brushed me off, so I haven’t really given it a second thought since!"

At the man’s words, Rei’s face twisted into an exasperated expression.

This wasn’t the first time Rei had spotted someone with a red cloth wrapped around their arm, stopped them in the middle of some criminal act, and pretended to ask for an explanation.

Several others had done the same thing before, and every single one of them had said exactly the same thing as this man.

(Are these guys just not paying attention? No, it feels more like they’re skillfully dodging the issue. Which means, probably…)

Although he had a vague feeling that what followed would be just like his previous experiences, he asked the man, clinging to a sliver of hope.

“So, who is this friend you’re talking about? Where do they live? Or where are they staying?”

“Well…”

At Rei’s words, the man tries to answer… but he can’t bring himself to say anything more.

Then, looking as if he were afraid, he managed to speak.

“I don’t know. I’ve never been to his house or his room. We only ever met in town. So, as for where he lives…”

Even hearing that, Rei didn’t direct any anger toward the man.

If anything, he felt a sense of confirmation.

That’s because, no matter how many people he’d encountered who’d been handing out red cloths, no one had known where the guy lived or stayed.

(He’s thorough. No matter how I look at it, this isn’t just a game. …Is he targeting me specifically? No, but… why?)

Rei felt puzzled, but of course he understood that there were many who were targeting him.

He understood that… but being targeted in Gilm right now was a bit unexpected.

If this were anywhere other than Gilm, it wouldn’t be strange at all.

But this was Gilm, Rei’s base of operations.

Most of the people living in Gilm are largely sympathetic to Rei, partly due to his relationship with Set.

That said, there are also those who have come for the expansion work—like the man now looking terrified in front of Rei.

“H-help me! Please, I beg you… p-please!”

Even seeing him desperately begging for mercy, Rei felt no particular sympathy.

After all, the man the man in front of him had just tried to blackmail was also bowing his head to this man, begging for forgiveness.

Despite that, this man had no intention of letting his opponent off the hook.

If Rei hadn’t happened to pass by, he would undoubtedly have taken the money from the man who was threatening him.

“Honestly, I think it’s a bit hypocritical to ask others to do something you wouldn’t do yourself. …But anyway, I guess I’ll stop scaring you any further. Come on, let’s go.”

“Huh? Um, where are we going…?”

“To the guard post, of course. I told the guy you were threatening earlier to go there. The guards are probably on their way over here, so I’m going to hand you over to them.”

“Wha—”

The man looked at Rei with an expression of disbelief.

Since he’d been threatened like this, he probably didn’t think he’d actually be handed over to the guards.

(I don’t know why he thought that… but judging by his attitude, he probably really did believe it.)

Shooting the man a look of exasperation, he grabbed him by the collar and tried to pull him out of the alley...

「Rei！」

Suddenly, that voice rang out.

But Rei, who had been suddenly called out to, didn’t seem particularly surprised. He turned his gaze toward the source of the voice and, seeing the person he had expected—a security guard—there, a smile spread across his face.

It was a smile that said she wouldn’t have to go through the trouble of dragging the man to the guardhouse herself.

“Over here. You heard about this from the victim of the extortion, didn’t you?”

“Yeah, that’s true, but…”

When the guard saw the red cloth wrapped around the right arm of the man Rei was holding by the collar, he gave him a look that said, “I knew it.”

It had been a few days since the incident involving the man with the red cloth had come to light... and the number of people with red cloths that Rei had already apprehended had already exceeded ten.

...Yet the number of people wearing red cloth hasn’t decreased. Is it because of the psychology that says, “Even if we know it’s a crime, we’re not afraid if we do it in a group”?

(It’s that “if everyone crosses on a red light, it’s not scary” mentality, isn’t it?)

Rei thought this, recalling his time in Japan, but in Japan today, even if pedestrians are crossing on a red light, there are plenty of drivers who plow right through, claiming they’re not at fault because it’s a red light.

If everyone tried to cross on a red light in front of people like that, it would surely lead to a major disaster.

And... even the people wrapped in red cloth were on the verge of a catastrophe in the face of Rei’s presence.

Nevertheless, fueled by a baseless confidence that “only we are safe,” the criminal acts of those wrapped in red cloth gradually increased.

No, in fact, it even seemed to Rei that these crimes had increased since he had started actively getting involved with the people wrapped in red cloth.

In reality, the current situation is the result of Carles manipulating things after determining that Rei had fallen for the red-cloaked ones’ tricks, but Rei hasn’t realized that.

Well, given the presence of those distributing the red cloth, she does suspect that someone is intentionally escalating the chaos.

“Here, I’ll hand this guy over for now. You guys handle the interrogation from here.”

“Ugh. Fine. It’s part of the job, after all.”

Perhaps deciding it was pointless to say anything more to Rei, the guard meekly took the man into custody.

Normally, the guard would have had plenty to say to Rei, but the fact is that over the past few days, there has been a sharp increase in what could be called petty crimes. Moreover, since the perpetrators are being hidden or let off the hook by those wrapped in red cloth, the arrest rate has been dropping.

Given the situation, it was only natural that the guard would want to enlist Rei’s help if he could.

(Petty crimes... huh. I suppose I should be glad that’s all it’s come down to so far.)

Even if they are minor offenses, acting as if I’m pleased that violent incidents—including extortion—have occurred is certainly not the right thing for a security guard to do.

But compared to a string of heinous crimes like murder, the guards honestly feel that the current situation is still better.

Of course, it’s precisely because these are minor offenses that the men wrapped in red cloth are willing to lend a hand so casually.

If they were asked to help someone escape or cover up evidence for murder, no one would cooperate as readily as they have until now... well, maybe not everyone, but the number would certainly drop.

“Alright, I’ll leave this one to you. I’m going to do a little more patrolling.”

“Got it. But wouldn’t it be better to take on a patrol assignment from the guild first?”

“I thought about that, too. But in situations like this, it’s better to be able to move around freely on my own.”

Those words were hard for a guard to understand.

After all, moving around in large groups is the fundamental principle of being a guard.

This is likely influenced by the fact that Rei has been operating solo for a long time, and also because each of his party members is skilled at working independently.

"I won’t say anything else. Just be careful. Something’s clearly going on behind the scenes."

“Yeah, I’ve got that covered. I’ll be careful. …Thanks for the warning. I appreciate it.”

With just those few words exchanged, Rei walked away from the guard.

The guard watches Rei’s retreating figure and thinks, “There are still idiots in Gilm who pick on Rei…” He imagines the fate of such a person and feels pity for them.

Countless people have tried to mess with Rei over the years.

After all, Rei has the griffin Set as his familiar, possesses one of the few existing Item Boxes, and owns various other magical items.

Magic items like the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear—anyone with even the slightest understanding of their value would undoubtedly want them, no matter the cost.

I suspect there’s someone like that pulling the strings behind the scenes this time as well.

Thinking that… I still feel that a tragic future awaits him in the end.

(Everyone in Gilm should know by now that it’s better to be friendly with Rei rather than antagonize him. If that’s the case… does that mean the ones pulling the strings this time are outsiders? I just hope the town doesn’t suffer any damage.)

The guard imagined the fate of the man whose face he didn’t even know, then dragged the captured man back to the guardhouse.
