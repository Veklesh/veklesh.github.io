Watching Gifnan being apprehended right before her eyes, Kena wore a complex expression.

After all, she had essentially sold Gifnan out to save herself. That was only natural.

Still, since she found herself thinking she'd rather live than die, she felt it was wrong but didn't regret it.

And the fact that this had secured her survival was a major factor.

"Well, this means we can hold Precious accountable for his crimes too."

"Don't be ridiculous! I made this decision myself! Precious has nothing to do with this!"

Gifnan shouted as security guards restrained him, but Rei merely smiled at him and spoke.

"When subordinates act, their superiors must take responsibility. That's what leaders are for—to shoulder the blame."

"Rei! You bastard!"

From the killing intent radiating from Rei, he knew Rei was overwhelmingly stronger than himself.

But even so, Gifnan couldn't stand by and watch his benefactor, Precious, be destroyed.

"What are you angry about? If Precious hadn't laid a hand on Tris in the first place, none of this would have happened! Normally, it'd be Tris who should be angry at you lot, wouldn't you say?"

Even if hired after arriving in Gilm, his subordinate had been killed.

And the carriage crashing into the shop was impossible to hide.

Tris being put in charge of the Gilm branch was the result of his past actions.

Of course, in reality, there was internal competition within the Spier Trading Company, and it was precisely because Tris had outmaneuvered Precious that she was entrusted with this matter...

In any case, Tris bears no fault in this matter.

And for Rei, Azimov, and the Azott Trading Company, who got dragged into this, it was pure collateral damage.

Given that, Rei had absolutely no intention of showing Precious any mercy.

"I told you that if I went with you, it would be for Precious's own good!"

"That's right. Since she got the chance to atone for her sins, I think that's for Precious's sake, don't you?"

"Don't... don't you dare joke about this!"

A scream like a soul's cry.

But even such a cry was useless to Gifnan now.

He even wanted to punch Rei, but he was completely pinned down by the guards.

"Well, this proves beyond doubt that Precious is involved in this series of incidents. So, we'll go pick him up."

"...Wait. The security squad is involved in this case too. We'll send someone from our side. Go with them."

Ideally, he would have preferred to send only the guards to apprehend Precious. But since the documents Marina brought explicitly stated that resolving this matter was to be left to Rei and his group, he couldn't refuse.

All the guards could do was accompany Rei and her group, keeping a close eye to make sure Rei didn't go too far.

...Given that every guard knew Rei showed no mercy to those who opposed him, this was probably the best compromise possible.

"Right. Then, we're heading straight to the inn where Precious is staying. Get some people ready over there. Ah... it doesn't have to be just one person."

He probably mentioned compromise because he remembered Precious had a fair number of bodyguards and subordinates.

If it were just Precious, Rei and his group could handle her easily.

But with more people, more would likely try to escape.

Therefore, the decision was simply that it would be better to bring a large number of guards from the start.

"Understood. We'll prepare immediately. ...Make sure this man doesn't pull any funny business."

This must be the highest-ranking guard present. At the guard's request to Rei for an escort, the other guards immediately sprang into action.

"Don't mess with me! Precious-sama didn't do anything wrong! It's all that bastard Tris's fault! Dammit, let go! Let go! Hey, you hear me, Rei!"

Gifnan shouted as he was dragged away, but Rei already seemed to have stopped listening.

"So, Rei. Can I come with you this time?"

"Yeah. Now that we've captured Gifnan, they probably won't try to harm Keena anymore. They've got bigger problems to worry about right now."

"That's true. With their bodyguard taken away, it's definitely not their priority right now."

Vihera interjected into Marina and Rei's conversation.

Hearing this, Keena's expression flickered with a hint of unease.

After all, while she was used to being the one doing the targeting as an assassin, being the target herself was a completely new experience.

For better or worse, Keena was fairly well-known as a skilled assassin.

She also had good instincts and, even when an assassination failed, never did anything that would reveal her true identity... Yet here she was, openly revealing herself.

Still, even exposed, being in a cell deep within the guard station's compound was safer than being out on the streets.

(Yeah... right. There aren't many places safer than this.)

Gazing at the iron bars before her, and squinting at the cell's temperature—which, while safe, had also become more bearable—Kena finally turned her gaze to the one she might call the symbol of her fear.

If safety was the measure, then being by Rei's side was undoubtedly the safest place of all.

Even if the destination was Precious, someone who likely wanted to kill her, being by Rei's side... and being protected by him, felt like there couldn't be a safer place.

(Though that assumes I'm not afraid of Rei.)

At that point, the option of Kena staying by Rei's side vanished.

The conclusion she ultimately reached was that staying in this cell was still the safest option.

While Kena contemplated her own safety, Rei and the others left the room containing the cell.

"Kena, hang in there a little longer. I'll adjust the temperature and such, so it should be fine for a while,"

Marina said that with a smile as she left.

All Kena could do was wave lightly as she watched them leave.

Leaving the guard post, Rei met up with Set, who had been lying outside, and they headed toward their destination.

Alongside Rei and the other members of Crimson Wings, about twenty guards followed them.

...Normally, when moving with security guards, the guards would be the ones drawing attention... but for Rei and the others, that was an unnecessary worry.

Set was there. Vihera was there. That alone made them stand out far more than the guards ever could.

In Rei's case, when wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, he was still less conspicuous than Set or Vihera.

Though if he removed the hood, his feminine features often drew others' gazes.

Drawing such attention from those around them, Rei and the others advanced... and eventually arrived at the inn where Precious was staying.

"Disperse, all of you. If anyone tries to escape the inn, capture them without question."

As the highest-ranking guard accompanying Rei and the others gave the order, the other guards dispersed one after another.

They positioned themselves to surround the inn, ensuring anyone trying to escape through a window would be spotted immediately.

Once the complete encirclement was established, the guard who had given the order nodded to Rei.

"Alright."

"Understood. Well then... let's go. Ah, Set. If anyone tries to escape from the inn, help capture them. Make sure not to kill them."

"Gururuu"

Set growled happily at Rei's words.

For Set, capturing escapees wasn't even work—it was practically play.

Considering the power of a griffin... no, of Set, it was only natural.

And so, Rei, Marina, Vihera, Byune, and the guard entered the inn.

The inn staff must have noticed the commotion outside.

The employee who had greeted Rei and the others when they arrived earlier asked Rei.

"What on earth is going on here?"

Given the current state of the inn, this employee wasn't entirely unaffected either.

But even so, as an employee of this inn, he needed to maintain a firm demeanor.

It was not Rei who spoke to this employee, but the man who had led the guards.

"Is a merchant named Precious staying at this inn? That individual has caused a bit of trouble. I'd like to speak with him briefly, so please let us through."

As an employee of an inn in Gilm, there was no choice but to obey when spoken to by a guard.

But... at those words from the guard, the male employee shook his head.

Refusing? That was Rei's fleeting thought, but the words that came from the employee's mouth exceeded the expectations of anyone familiar with the situation.

"If you mean Mr. Precious, he's already checked out."

"Huh?"

Not just Rei, but everyone listening let out a collective gasp.

A somewhat vacant... one might even say dazed, sound echoed around them.

Hearing this unexpected reply, Marina was the first to snap back to reality.

"I never imagined you'd abandon Gifnan so easily. ...But since Rei took you away, you probably decided there was nothing else to be done."

"But... the guards might remember Precious's face, right!?"

"...Maybe he has some other plan. Besides, even if he left the inn, he hasn't left Gilm. It's entirely possible he's hiding somewhere else."

Marina's words inevitably brought the slums to Rei's mind.

If Precious fled to an obvious location, finding her wouldn't be difficult.

But if she fled into the slums, tracking her down would be difficult.

Of course, the slums had their own order and information networks.

Using those might make finding Precious possible, but the problem was that Rei had no connections there.

The same applied to Vihera and Byune. Even Marina, as a former Guildmaster, didn't openly possess connections to the slums.

Conversely, she did have connections through unofficial channels, but those were only possible because of her position as Guildmaster. Now that she'd resigned, Marina couldn't use them.

"Huh!? Sorry, Rei. We're heading out first. We might find Precious before even entering the slums. And considering she could be somewhere else entirely, we can't just sit here."

The man commanding the guards shouted this, then instructed the other guards to search the room Precious had been using before leaving the inn.

The inn staff, having been requested by the guards, couldn't refuse to let them search Precious's room and obediently showed them the way.

"Rei, what should we do?"

At Vihera's words, Rei considered what they should do next... and quickly reached a conclusion.

"If Precious fled to the slums... or anywhere else, she wouldn't have taken a carriage, right?"

"Exactly. Traveling through the slums by carriage would be highly conspicuous. And above all, even if she were escaping somewhere, she couldn't flee down roads inaccessible to carriages."

Naturally, few roads in the slums are passable by carriage.

Given that, it was nearly impossible for Precious, pursued by guards, to escape using a carriage.

Therefore, it was certain that the carriage Precious had been using was still at this inn.

If this had been a planned escape, she could have disposed of the carriage to fund her flight.

But Precious's escape was sudden—triggered by Gifnan's arrest.

Knowing she was about to be captured, Precious judged that staying put meant certain arrest. What she needed most was time; she wouldn't waste it selling the carriage for cash.

And Rei had a companion named Set with an exceptional sense of smell.

(Ideally, I'd want to use Precious's personal belongings from the room to track the scent... but the guards will likely investigate everything, so taking them out is probably impossible.)

If there were no carriage, he might have gone to some trouble to borrow items from the room Precious used.

But with the carriage available, he didn't feel it necessary to go that far.

...Unless, of course, Set couldn't track the scent using the carriage.

Reaching that conclusion, Rei demanded the inn staff guide him to the carriage Precious had used.
