"Wait a minute... if that's the case, won't we have nowhere to stay when we get to Gilm!?"

As they passed by Sabrusta and walked toward Abuero... Sura shouted—almost yelling—at Rei, who was riding in the same carriage.

Under normal circumstances, the others would probably have scolded her for that.

But given what Rei had told her about the current situation in Gilm—namely, that there were almost no lodgings to speak of—it was only natural she’d feel like shouting.

“Don’t worry. Lord Daskar knew from the start that this group was coming. It’s not exactly a mansion… but there are houses built to last through the winter.”

For Gilm, the group led by Sura was nothing short of a godsend.

Of course, having such a large number of women suddenly arrive would likely cause various problems.

But since they bring benefits to Gilm that far outweigh those problems, Daskar had absolutely no intention of treating Sura and her group poorly.

“...Really? Well, if that’s the case, fine... But then... I know it’s not really our place to say, but couldn’t you have built more temporary lodging for us?”

“Listen, just so you know, it’s precisely because we knew you were coming in advance that we were able to make proper plans and build the houses. Trying to build houses for the people who suddenly showed up for Gilm’s expansion project on top of that is a bit difficult.”

Faced with Rei’s explanation, Sura had no choice but to accept it.

If a shack-like structure would do, they could probably build one right away.

But such a place might be better than being outside in the snow, but ultimately, that’s the extent of the difference.

Without heating and no way to block the wind coming through the cracks in the structure, there’s no denying the possibility of freezing to death.

“That said, since the chances of survival are still higher than freezing to death outside in the winter, we’d likely resort to that option if push came to shove… but at the very least, it wouldn’t be nearly as comfortable as the homes Sura and the others had originally planned to live in.”

It goes without saying which group Daskar, the lord of Gilm, would prioritize: those staying in Gilm only while the expansion work is underway, or those who have truly decided to settle there permanently.

Moreover, since Rei is fully cooperating with Sura and the others’ relocation, and Daskar holds Rei in high regard, it’s clear which group he’ll prioritize.

“Well, as long as we have a place to live secured, that’s fine with me.”

Sura spoke with a sense of relief, and the others remained silent, seemingly having no objections.

After all, Gimekara had originally come this far to relocate the Zolge Trading Company’s headquarters to Gilm, and while Rocks was serving as Sura’s escort, he was originally from Gilm and already had a place to live.

Sura, who leads the Resistance… and this group, and Sharia, who joined the Resistance in Mejogo, were concerned about the house built for them.

“Ah, and… maybe—just maybe—if we arrive in Gilm and run short on funds, we can still join the Gamelion hunt. The aid from Gimekara… no, from the Zolge Trading Company, only lasts until we reach Gilm, right? If so, we should figure something out while we still have time to save up.”

“...Gamelion? Are there still any left?”

The first to respond to Rei’s words was Rocks, who, being based in Gilm, knew a lot about Gamelion.

Given that this is usually the time of year when Gamelions start disappearing, Rocks’ remark was only natural.

“It looks like they’ve been sticking around longer this year. I’ve been too busy to join the Gamelion hunt myself, but that’s what I’ve heard from people I know. Once I finish up my urgent work, I’m planning to go hunt some Gamelions myself.”

“Hmm. Well, maybe I’ll give it a try too.”

For Gilm, the annual Gamelion hunt—a seasonal treat—was almost like a festive celebration.

For Rocks, an adventurer like Gilm, not participating was simply not an option.

He’d been busy with this and that this year, and with the issues in the Kingdom of Reburina, he hadn’t expected to be able to join the Gamelion hunt, so the information from Rei was a huge relief to Rocks.

“Um, well… what exactly is a Gamelion?”

Sharia asked, looking a bit confused.

As a wolf beastman, it was only natural for her to be interested in hunting.

Moreover, this group excels at fighting in packs, just like wolves.

For that very reason, the prospect of hunting Gamelions as a group was thrilling for Sharia as well. …Though, in a literal sense, her ample bosom was also swaying.

“Simply put, it’s a giant rabbit monster. However, it’s a seasonal monster that only appears during this time of year, as autumn turns to winter.”

“……A rabbit?”

At the word “rabbit,” Sharia muttered, seeming to lose interest slightly.

That said, since Rei and Rocks had anticipated such a reaction, they didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

Gamelion is undoubtedly a rabbit monster, but in reality, it’s merely a former rabbit; there’s absolutely no trace of its herbivorous past left.

But even if they explained that here, it was unlikely Sharia would listen properly.

Only by seeing it with her own eyes would she truly understand what a Gamelion is.

(Well, I guess she’ll just have to see for herself in the end. Still, the Gamelion hunt is already nearing its end and numbers are low—if Sura and the others show up… there’s absolutely no way there’ll be enough prey.)

Sura’s group has over a hundred carriages and numbers nearly a thousand people.

Under those circumstances, naturally, even if they defeat a Gamelion, the reward and amount of meat each person gets will be negligible.

If we’d arrived in Gilm a little sooner, we could have arrived during the peak of the Gamelion hunt… but there’s no point in dwelling on that now.

It’s not like Sura and the others deliberately chose to miss the peak season for Gamelion hunting, after all.

After all, even though everyone is traveling by carriage, it’s a group of nearly a thousand people.

Setting aside the speed of travel itself, it inevitably takes time to prepare for departure.

If these were female adventurers, they’d be used to getting ready quickly… but this group, while they understand how to fight as a unit using bows, aren’t really warriors at heart, and their personalities are closer to those of ordinary people.

...That said, given that they’ve become capable of killing people without a second thought and can casually strip the corpses of bandits, it might be more accurate to describe them as somewhere between adventurers and ordinary people rather than complete civilians.

(It’s a bit late to say this now, but going back to being ordinary civilians… I wouldn’t say it’s impossible, but it would be pretty difficult. Looking at how much they like Set, they look like nothing more than a bunch of women who love cute things, no matter how you look at it.)

That said, since there’s always the option of getting hired by the Daskar if push comes to shove, Rei isn’t all that worried about this group’s future.

Of course, it’s unlikely that everyone will actually get hired by the Daskar.

The reason Sura and the others possess such overwhelming strength is solely due to their numbers—over a thousand people—and the barrage of arrows fired collectively by that many, which could be called a “bullet curtain.”

But that is solely because Gimekara—or rather, the Zolge Trading Company—spares no expense in preparing arrows.

If they were to come under Daskar’s command, it would naturally be impossible for them to fight the way they do now.

“Well, if you’re interested in Gamelion, you can go after him once you arrive in Gilm… no, in this case, ‘hunt’ might be a more accurate term. You should do it that way. Though, the chariot charges Sura and the others were using… probably wouldn’t work.”

At Rei’s words, Sura wore a slightly dissatisfied expression.

After much consideration, they had concluded that this was the best option for them at the moment: a mobile battle using carriages—something that could be called “horse archery” rather than “mounted archery.”

It was a tactic they were confident in, and they had actually managed to take down several bandit groups using it.

So it was only natural that Sura felt dissatisfied, wondering, “Why…?”

But Rei directed a look of exasperation—not at Sura, but at Rocks.

Perhaps because he knew exactly why he was being looked at that way, the man simply looked away quietly.

Seeing Rocks like that, Rei let out a small sigh before speaking.

“You guys have basically been fighting bandits, right? As for monsters… just goblins and the like, I suppose?”

“Yeah. There aren’t that many monsters outside the frontier, and we’ve been traveling along the roads.”

“I suppose so. That’s probably why Rocks didn’t point that out either. …Listen. Your fighting style is effective against bandits… or rather, against slow-moving enemies. I can guarantee that. But… when you’re in a carriage and most of you only have bows, you’re utterly helpless against creatures like Gamelion—who are faster than the carriage itself, and whose fur deflects weapon strikes.”

A carriage, after all, moves faster than an ordinary person can run, so normally, you wouldn’t get caught up with.

That’s precisely why they can attack unilaterally from a distance… but when facing a creature like Gamelion, that advantage turns into a disadvantage.

Gamelion’s speed is incomparable to that of a human runner.

The fur covering its body cannot be scratched by any ordinary weapon.

Its massive body is a mass of muscle; even ten ordinary humans together would be knocked flying if they collided with a Gamelion at full speed.

Its tail is extremely flexible, functioning like a whip.

Its ears are blades, as sharp as any cutting tool—no, perhaps even sharper.

Its fangs are incredibly strong, capable of easily crushing a carriage.

...No matter how he looked at it, Rei couldn’t imagine Sura and the others fighting a Gamelion and actually defeating it.

“Well, for now… if we’re going to hunt Gamelion, we’d better assume we can’t fight the way we have before. We probably won’t be getting any aid from the Zolge Trading Company either.”

“That’s…”

He must have hit a sore spot. Sura was at a loss for words.

In reality, while a single arrow isn’t that expensive, that doesn’t mean they can use as many as they want.

Given that, if the aid from the Zolge Trading Company dried up, they would have to find a way to pay for the arrows themselves.

“If you’ve thought that through and believe you can manage on your own, why not give it a try?”

That’s what Rei said, though he probably thought it would be tough.

No, if it were just a matter of defeating Gamelion, they had Sharia, a wolf beastman with high combat prowess—one who would certainly hold her own even among the adventurers of Gilm—and those with backgrounds as former adventurers also possessed considerable strength.

But… even so, he didn’t think they could defeat Gamelion without suffering any casualties.

Rei’s prediction was that those lacking the necessary skill might end up getting injured… or even killed.

Sura, perhaps understanding what Rei meant, fell silent with a look of dissatisfaction.

She probably wants to say something back, but she understands that it would only come across as an excuse or sour grapes.

“Ah… Look. If you need it, I’ll help you out, so cheer up.”

Perhaps because Sura looked so downcast, Rocks spoke up encouragingly.

Gimekara looks at Rocks with a somewhat exasperated expression, while Sharia, having heard about Gamelion from Rei, is intrigued.

“Rocks is only supposed to guard these guys until we get to Gilm. I don’t think he needs to take care of them after that.”

“...No, well... you know?”

In response to Rei’s words, Rocks replied, sounding as if he were trying to gloss over something.

Seeing this, Rei tilted his head in confusion, and Gimekara’s expression shifted from exasperation to a smile—a rather malicious one at that.

Perhaps noticing Gimekara’s smile, Rocks tried to say something, but he was too slow to stop her from speaking.

“Since my lover might be in danger, I can’t just leave them be, can I?”

At those words from Gimekara, Rei froze in surprise for a moment... then looked back and forth between Rocks and Sura.

“...Lover?”

Thinking he might have misheard, Rei asked just in case, but the only response was Sura, her face flushed bright red as she looked away, too embarrassed to meet his gaze.

And then there was Rocks, who seemed unsure of what to say.
