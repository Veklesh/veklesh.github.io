Those present all wore expressions of wonder—genuine wonder—as they looked at the figure before them.

At the center of their gazes stood a small figure.

It was impossible to believe this person was leading nearly a thousand men.

Though their face was hidden beneath a hood, it was clear they were younger than the others.

Of course, there were those in the world who remained small even as adults, like dwarves. But looking at Rei's build, he certainly didn't look like a dwarf.

No, even from the jawline visible beneath the hood, there was no trace of the beard typical of a dwarf.

In other words, Rei was definitely not a dwarf.

Seeing Rei like this, yet showing no sign of contempt, was likely because Gime Kara had deliberately gathered these guards based on character.

If this had been some random adventurer, trouble would have erupted without a doubt.

(If only I could have brought Set...)

Having long since resigned himself to his appearance, Rei turned his gaze toward the outer walls, thinking of Set, who was likely hunting outside the city.

A rank A monster, the Griffin. ...To be precise, it was classified as a rare species, equivalent to rank S, but the higher-ups had refused to let Set bring it into the city.

What if such a monster was let into the city and went on a rampage?

Even when Gimekara brought up the name of the Zolgea Trading Company, it ultimately wasn't accepted.

Of course, from Rei's perspective, she could have forced Set into the city using somewhat aggressive means.

After all, Set was registered with the guild as Rei's familiar.

Using that as leverage, even the city's upper echelons would find it difficult to refuse.

If they had forcibly refused then, relations between Dolgana and the Guild would undoubtedly have soured.

Rei understood this, but he knew that forcing the issue now, when he needed the town's resources, could bring unnecessary trouble not just to himself, but to the others as well.

Considering these circumstances, Rei chose to temporarily part ways with Set.

That said, the separation was only temporary, limited to the time Rei spent within the city itself.

With nearly a thousand people in their group, it was impossible for all of them to stay at the inns within the city.

...Even before that, most had left Jaya with only the clothes on their backs, meaning very few possessed any money.

Some might have brought rings or jewels gifted by clients, but that was surely a tiny minority.

Moreover, given that anyone not present when the bell rang at nine tomorrow morning would be left behind, it was questionable how many would choose lodgings where they risked oversleeping.

Of course, if they've decided to stay here in Dorgana, that's fine too.

"I have a pretty good idea what you think of me. ...So then. How about we have a mock battle? If I prove I have enough strength to satisfy you, you won't have any complaints, right?"

Judgment in critical situations is also important for leading a group.

But really, the quickest way to put them at ease in a situation like this is to show them your strength.

"I-is that okay? I mean, we're actually pretty skilled, you know?"

A woman in Rei's line of sight spoke up.

Since most of Rei's group were women, there were quite a few female guards among them.

One of those women, concerned for Rei, spoke up.

From the woman's perspective, Rei's petite build probably made her think there was no way she could spar with them.

(Did Gimekara not mention me?)

Though he wondered this, he knew he needed to show his strength for what lay ahead.

"It's fine. Don't let my appearance fool you—I'm confident in my skills."

"...Really?"

Seeing Rei's confident expression, the woman relented.

Truthfully, with someone like Rei around, there was always the risk that someone might underestimate his abilities.

To prevent that, he needed to properly demonstrate his strength here.

Any concern that Rei might be just a child, as he appeared, had been dismissed by Rei himself.

If he was that confident, then... It wouldn't be strange for that woman... and the others to think the same.

"Alright, I'll go first... Are you sure you're okay with this?"

The woman who had initially shown concern for Rei said this as she took her stance.

Since it was a mock battle, weapons weren't used; it was strictly hand-to-hand combat.

Of course, if Rei used his usual weapons like the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear, there'd be no holding back whatsoever.

The others gathered around also watched with keen interest, eager to see just how much power Rei possessed after boasting so much.

The fact that no one objected to the woman fighting Rei was proof she held significant influence among the gathered bodyguards.

"Come on."

With that single word from Rei's mouth, the woman lunged forward in one swift motion.

This was likely just a test. The fist she unleashed showed neither the speed nor the power one would expect from her maximum effort... not by a long shot.

The woman herself had likely intended to stop short if Rei couldn't react to that strike.

But... before she knew it, the woman's form was already flying through the air.

"Huh!?"

She didn't understand why this was happening to her, but even so, she twisted her body mid-air and landed on the ground.

Seeing that she hadn't slammed her body into the ground like that, Rei slightly raised his estimation of the female adventurer within himself.

Still, judging someone's skill solely by appearance meant, from Rei's perspective, she was still lacking in real ability.

"What now? Still want to keep going?"

"Ah... no. I get it now. ...No, I get that I don't understand what happened."

"I see. ...So, what about the others?"

At Rei's question, the others all shook their heads.

That single sequence of movements had made it clear to Rei that they possessed overwhelming strength, far beyond their own.

"Then, no complaints about the escort, right? Not sure if you know, but the women with us have their own issues. Many of them dislike men, so keep that in mind."

The guards nodded obediently at Rei's words.

After confirming this, Rei held a brief discussion on various matters before heading to the next location.

Watching Rei's departing figure, the guards exchanged words.

"Who'd have thought a kid like that could possess such power?"

"Yeah. At first, I thought he was underestimating us... but after seeing that display of skill..."

"I understood she threw it, but I couldn't figure out how she did it."

They exchanged words, their gazes naturally turning to the woman who had been thrown.

"Do you remember how you got thrown?"

"No. I swung at him, and the next thing I knew, I was in midair. I have absolutely no idea how something like that could happen."

Hearing that, those remaining in the room truly grasped the extent of Rei's power.

How on earth could a child like that possess such immense power?

"Anyway, looks like the ones we're protecting have some serious baggage. Let's do this right so the client doesn't ditch us. If we pull it off, we'll get a hefty reward."

The others nodded in agreement with the man's muttered words.

"Gimekara, is the feed ready?"

When Rei arrived at the stable area, Gimekara, who had been waiting, nodded with a broad smile.

"Yes, of course. I've prepared enough to last us well until we reach Gilm, so rest assured. Though, to be honest, there isn't that much here."

Several people beside Gimekara looked at Rei with puzzled expressions.

They probably couldn't understand why Gimekara was being so deferential.

Having already received similar looks from the guards earlier, Rei paid no particular attention to the rude stares from those who seemed to work at the stables and spoke up.

"I saw your guards. Their skills aside, they seemed like decent fellows. That's a relief."

"No. I would have preferred to introduce individuals who excelled in both skill and character... but such people weren't so easy to gather."

"I figured."

Adventurers skilled and well-mannered are naturally in high demand.

Even for the Zolge Commerce Guild, which wields significant influence in the Kingdom of Reburina, gathering large numbers of such individuals was impossible.

Moreover, even adventurers considered skilled in the Kingdom of Reburina were ultimately only so within the confines of that small nation.

Compared to the adventurers of Gilm, where many skilled adventurers gather, it was undeniable that they were one, two, or even more levels below in strength.

Gimekara understood this precisely, which is why he chose based on character rather than skill.

"Now, about the fodder..."

"Yes, everything in that warehouse over there was purchased by us, so please feel free to take whatever you need."

"Thanks. ...But whether it's fodder, food, or water, I can't be the only one carrying it, right? We won't be together forever."

"Don't worry about that. We've prepared a cart specifically for transporting supplies."

"...Huh."

Rei let out a voice filled with surprise and admiration at the fact that they had also prepared wagons for transporting supplies.

Indeed, transporting that much cargo would require a significant number of wagons.

Even without the supplies, they needed numerous wagons to transport nearly a thousand people. Yet they had also prepared wagons for the supplies... and the horses to pull them.

Naturally, this meant significantly higher costs.

The fact that they had prepared supply wagons despite knowing this meant that Gimekara... no, the Zolgee Trading Company was dead serious about escaping from the Kingdom of Reburina.

(Well, messing with Gilm was bad enough, but Jaya has been doing all sorts of other shady things. Normally, no one would want to stay aboard a ship doomed to sink forever. In that sense, maybe we should commend them for acting swiftly, like true merchants.)

Of course, since it was a merchant house cooperating with Jaya, moving their headquarters to Gilm didn't mean they could immediately trade freely.

It was certain they'd be under surveillance for years.

Naturally, since Gimekara knew this going in, he wouldn't complain about that part. ...Or rather, couldn't complain, would be more accurate in this case.

"Having you go that far really helps us out."

"As merchants, it's only natural consideration. ...More importantly, once the fodder storage is finished, we'll move on to the food. Considering Rei-san, we prepared quite a bit of freshly cooked food instead of just preserved rations... Is that alright?"

"Yeah, that's fine. Though, it's fine while I'm here, but like I've said before, we can't stay together the whole time. Thinking about that, we'll still need preserved food, right?"

"I understand. But both the food and fodder have been divided into smaller portions and prepared at towns and villages along the road. So, I don't think we need to worry too much about the food going bad or anything."

"I see. That's a relief."

If you keep it inside the Misty Ring, it won't spoil.

But if we were moving with things like bread and cheese piled on the wagon, there was always the risk they'd spoil.

In winter, such worries wouldn't be necessary, but unfortunately, it was summer now.

Though the height of summer had passed, it was still quite hot.

"You're welcome to feel deeply indebted to us. As merchants, we're acting in our own best interest, after all."

Gimekara said this with a kind smile, looking every bit the merchant.

Facing such a Gime Kara, Rei was certain that at least the journey ahead to Gilm would be somewhat easier.

He understood that as merchants, they would sell their favors to the fullest extent possible whenever they could, aiming to improve Daskar's impression upon their arrival in Gilm.

Moreover, if Rei and his companions received various favors, it was certain they would naturally stop harboring ill feelings.

...Though he had no intention of lowering his guard.

With that thought, Rei continued discussing various arrangements with Gimekara.
