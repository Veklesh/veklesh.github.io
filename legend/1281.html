"Hmph!"

With that cry, Byune thrusts her dagger forward, but... Rei easily dodges the strike and sweeps Byune's legs out from under her with the stone tip of the Death Scythe.

With her foot swept out from under her, Byune fell face-first into the snow.

"Ugh! Ugh!"

Struggling to free herself from the snow by kicking her legs, Byune shot a look of utter disapproval at Rei, who had performed the maneuver, her expression remaining impassive.

"Calm down. It was your fault for neglecting your footing, Byune. Even if you prioritize speed... no, precisely because you prioritize speed, you need to pay attention to your footing, right?"

From a short distance away, Vihera, who had been leaning against Set's stomach as he lay sprawled out, watching the two train, called out to Byune with a hint of teasing.

Byune knew she'd been careless and deserved what she got, but that didn't make her any less annoyed.

"Hmph!"

Unlike Vihera, Rei couldn't understand what Byune was saying.

But he could tell she wanted a rematch.

As they formed their party starting this spring, increasing the combat strength of its members was something Rei welcomed.

Considering party roles, combat strength wasn't everything... but it was also true that the others' strengths were outstanding.

Rei, the one with the nickname; Vihera, who powered up after the Silver Lion incident; Marina, the former Rank A adventurer.

Amidst them, the thief Byune was clearly weak in combat strength.

Even among monsters, those with some intelligence wouldn't be unreasonable to target her weaknesses.

While mindless monsters like goblins might be an exception, for monsters like orcs, Byune would be an easy target.

...Furthermore, the fact that Byune was female also made her a target.

Though Byune could still be considered a child, would monsters like orcs take that into account? The answer was no.

For that very reason, boosting Byune's combat strength to a certain level could be considered an absolute necessity this winter.

Whether she understood this or not, Byune trained in combat with Vihera almost every day.

Rei was helping out with the combat training as well... and today, it had begun once more.

"Rei-san, that's so mean! Be nicer to Byune-chan!"

"Yeah, yeah! You should be more considerate of Byune-chan!"

Such voices could be heard from the inn's window facing the backyard.

Byune rarely changed her expression and basically only responded with 'Hmm'.

But even so, her features were undeniably well-proportioned, and she was undeniably adorable.

And at the Dusk Wheat inn, many adventurers and merchants, especially in winter, found themselves with time to spare.

So what happened? That was Byune's popularity.

"Hmm!"

Receiving that cheer—or something like it—Byune drew her dagger and faced Rei...

"Whoa! Hold on a second with that fight!"

"Kyaaah!"

A voice abruptly cut in, followed by a scream.

...It was a scream, not cheers, likely because the one calling out was Pamidor, whose ferocious face could easily be mistaken for that of a notorious bandit chieftain.

Pamidor's name was fairly well known even in Gilm.

Originally, he was fairly famous as a skilled blacksmith known only to those in the know, but after meeting Rei, his name spread like wildfire.

Of course, his notorious appearance, the fact he had an adorable wife despite it, and his surprisingly doting nature toward his children were also reasons for his fame.

Still, many knew his name but had never seen him in person. Those screaming now were likely among them.

Vihera and Byune, however, showed no particular sign of distress when the menacing-looking Pamidor suddenly appeared near them.

They had heard about him from Rei before, and more importantly, he showed absolutely no hostility or desire toward them.

...Though byune's case, it might just be that whatever he sensed didn't show on his face.

In any case, Vihera was confident she could subdue Pamidor immediately if it tried anything, and if it proved troublesome, she would welcome that challenge.

"Pamidor? What's up, all of a sudden?"

Rei, who had been holding his Death Scythe, lowered it and asked curiously.

Byune, too, seemed to decide the training was temporarily over. He lowered his guard and approached Set, who was lying down.

Ignoring the cheers that rose again from the window at the sight of Set, Vihera, and Byune, Rei looked at Pamidor and silently asked with his eyes what he had come to do.

"I didn't come here to bother you. Look, it's this."

Saying that, Pamidor showed Rei a dagger.

However, its length was clearly longer than a standard dagger, yet shorter than a longsword.

With a blade length approaching fifty centimeters, it was a weapon that fell somewhere between a longsword and a dagger... clearly leaning more towards the dagger end of that spectrum.

And the blade was white.

Even Rei could tell that blade, which didn't look like it was made of metal, was crafted from the claws of a silver lion.

"This... is quite a distinctive dagger, isn't it? Or can you even call this a dagger?"

As Rei muttered this, looking at the weapon that was neither a longsword nor a dagger, with a hint of bewilderment, Pamidor nodded, his expression suggesting he understood why Rei would say that.

"I understand what you mean, Rei. I do... but I judged this to be the optimal form for this weapon. Of course, if it proves difficult to use, I'll adjust it later. But as a blacksmith, I can declare this weapon complete as it stands."

Given the matter of the Twilight Spear, Rei trusted Pamidor's skill as a blacksmith.

If Pamidor says so, then... Rei held back any further complaints for now and accepted the dagger...

"Huh?"

Rei let out a surprised gasp at how light the dagger felt in his hand.

The blade was clearly longer than a standard dagger. Longer, yet... in terms of weight, it was noticeably lighter than a typical dagger.

"It's surprisingly light."

"Ah. It must be the properties of the Silver Lion's material. It was a bit heavier when it was just the material... Probably because some of the Silver Lion's magic still remains, affecting it."

"...I see."

With a blade longer than a standard dagger, its attack range was clearly superior to that of an ordinary dagger.

And since it's a dagger made from the Silver Lion's claw, its attack power is also clearly superior to a regular dagger.

"What do you think? This shouldn't be too awkward for that young lady to wield, right?"

"Yeah. ...Though, considering she's been using a dagger until now, she'll probably need to get used to the difference... Luckily, it's winter now."

Right? Pamidor nodded, looking a bit proud.

Watching Pamidor like this, Rei, holding the dagger that was probably good enough to have no complaints beyond weapon handling, called out to Byune, who was stroking Set.

"Byune, come here for a sec!"

Called, Byune tilted her head slightly, still expressionless, but approached since Rei had called her.

Intrigued by Byune's reaction, Vihera... and then Set also came over to Rei and the others.

"Try holding this. I had Pamidor make this dagger for you as your weapon."

"Hm!"

She muttered in response to Rei's words and took the dagger.

Her eyebrows twitched slightly at its unexpected weight, then she paused for a few seconds upon seeing the unusual white blade color. Eventually, she swung the dagger.

The blade length differed from the dagger Byune usually wielded, so the feel when swinging it was different.

He swung it once, twice, three times, still tilting his head as if not quite accustomed to the feel.

Cheers rose again from the inn at her adorable appearance, but Rei paid them no mind as she asked Byune.

"Well? Can you handle it?"

"Mm!"

As Byune hesitates slightly and shows him the dagger's hilt, Rei shifts his gaze to Vihera.

Though Rei generally understood what Byune meant, in moments like this, he couldn't quite grasp her words.

Meeting Rei's gaze, Vihera smiled and spoke.

"Seems the grip part's a bit thick."

"...Oh? Ah, uh... Sorry. That was my mistake. Right, it's not meant for an adult to use, so the grip should have been adjusted for that."

Pamidor looked momentarily confused by what Vihera was saying, but quickly realized that Byune was the one using the dagger he'd made—meaning it was for a child with small hands. He nodded in understanding.

Then again, this was hardly Pamidor's fault.

Most people who came to his shop asking Pamidor to make them weapons were established adventurers.

Considering the considerable sum required to commission work from the skilled Pamidor, this was only natural.

...Occasionally, someone who had misunderstood something—perhaps the son of a noble or merchant from a wealthy family—would request a weapon far beyond their skill level. Naturally, Pamidor would never accept such a commission.

Once, a man from some noble family, built like a barrel, came in full of confidence, declaring he'd have Pamidor craft his weapon.

Naturally, Pamidor wouldn't accept such a request. When he was glared at by Pamidor, who already had a fearsome face, the man immediately fled.

In any case, due to circumstances like these, Pamidor had hardly ever crafted weapons meant for children.

Moreover, this wasn't just any dagger—it was forged using the claws and bones of a Rank S monster... the Silver Lion.

He was delighted by that, so even if he made a few mistakes, it couldn't be helped.

"Right then. Let's see if it fits your hand properly. Give the hilt a grip."

"Hmm."

Following Pamidor's instructions, Byune gripped the dagger's hilt.

Watching her, Pamidor assessed how much the handle needed adjusting.

The sight of Pamidor—a figure whose appearance was truly worthy of being called the leader of thieves—holding the hand of a girl... or in some cases, a child who could easily be mistaken for a little girl, might have even led to a situation where guards were called.

(If that happened, that would be kind of interesting though.)

Rei observed the two, pondering this, but when he saw Pamidor step away from Byune, he asked about something that had been puzzling him.

"Come to think of it, you finished making that dagger pretty quickly. Azimov, who asked for Sleipnir Boots to be reinforced before you, is still struggling with his."

"Of course."

Rei answered with an air of utter certainty.

"Of course?"

"Yeah. What Azimov's doing is enhancing a magic item that's already a finished product. Compared to that, what I did was creating a dagger from scratch. And in my case, the process of making the dagger wasn't even that complicated."

So it was incomparably easier than what Azimov is doing.

Pamidor stated this, but before Rei could say anything, he continued.

"Just so you know, just because the process was easier doesn't mean it was simple. Using Silver Lion material requires intense concentration and precision work."

"Well, I get what you're saying."

In other words, even if the process itself wasn't difficult, each individual step required more care than usual. That's how Rei understood it.

"Hmph, good to know. Unlike Azimov, who has to consider things from all angles, my techniques are pretty well-established. Sure, some monster materials need extensive work, but the Silver Lion was almost an exception. Minimal intervention was enough. Actually, adding unnecessary touches would've lowered the quality."

Hearing Pamidor speak with such confidence in his work, Rei understood the craftsman's pride. He said nothing more, merely nodding in response.

"Well, I should head back soon. The dagger... even if it takes time, it should be ready by tomorrow."

"Understood. I'll come pick it up tomorrow morning."

After exchanging those brief words, Pamidor simply walked away from the courtyard.

"Hmm."

Seeing Pamidor leave, Byune murmured briefly to Rei.

Seeing this, Vihera explained to Rei what Byune meant.

"She's asking if it's okay for her to take it."

"Ah. That. Since he's joining our party, enhancing Byune's abilities is our top priority."

At those words, Byune remained expressionless... yet still gave a slight nod.
