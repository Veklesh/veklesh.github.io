Rei dodges the spear thrust straight at him by shifting his body ever so slightly.

The knight of the Duke of Kerebel’s Order who had launched that strike was so taken aback that Rei had dodged his thrust with almost no movement that he even wondered if the spear had simply passed right through him.

Still, he quickly regained his composure—a trait befitting a knight of the elite Duke of Kerebel’s Knights.

He quickly pulls the lance back to his hand and thrusts it toward Rei once more.

However, this thrust was aimed not at Rei’s face, but at her torso—a part of the body that is harder to move than the face.

Rei evaded the strike by stepping back with his right foot and turning his body sideways… then, using that back foot, he lunged forward in one swift motion, closing the distance between himself and the man.

Naturally, the man tried to react to Rei’s move, but the next moment, seeing the fist thrust right in front of his face, he conceded defeat.

“That’s enough!”

Philma’s voice echoed through the training ground, and Rei, who had been holding his fist just in front of his opponent’s face, stepped back.

The man with the spear breathed a sigh of relief as the fist that had been right in front of him vanished.

After stepping back, they exchanged a brief bow, and the mock battle ended.

“Rei, how the hell did you dodge like that!? The spear just passed right through your body!”

Leodanis approached Rei and exclaimed this, his astonishment plain to see.

It was one thing for his opponent to miss, but the fact that Leodanis—who had been watching from a distance—couldn’t even perceive Rei’s movements… was likely simply a matter of skill.

In fact, some people had clearly seen Rei’s movements, and even those who hadn’t could still predict what actions he had taken.

In that regard, it seems Leodanis is still quite low on the skill ladder even within the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights.

...Still, seeing that there were a few others who couldn’t see Rei’s movements just now, he probably isn’t quite at the very bottom in terms of skill.

“What I did wasn’t that difficult. I mean, Philma could probably do it without breaking a sweat.”

“Even if you call it ‘ordinary skill,’ it’s still something Captain Philma can do…”

As Rei and Leodanis were talking like that, the man who had been sparring with Rei approached them.

“Rei, that was impressive. To be honest, I didn’t expect my thrust to be dodged so effortlessly.”

There was a look of respect in the man’s eyes as he spoke.

As a member of Kerebel Duke’s Knights, this man naturally had confidence in his own skill with the lance.

That was likely why he couldn’t believe his thrust had been dodged so effortlessly.

That said, the fact that he could honestly acknowledge Rei’s strength led Rei to conclude that the man still had the potential to grow stronger.

The Duke of Kerebel’s Knights could be described as an elite group built on meritocracy.

It is precisely because they are such individuals that they have earned a reputation as one of the finest elite units in the Kingdom of Mireana.

Given that, it wouldn’t be surprising if some of them, being arrogant about their status, were unable to honestly admit their own mistakes or acknowledge their opponent’s strength.

But this man readily admitted his own mistake and acknowledged Rei’s strength.

(Part of it is that they select people with that kind of personality for the Order, but it’s probably also a result of Philma’s training.)

They are hungry for strength and can honestly use their defeats to their advantage in the future.

Seeing such individuals gathered in the Kerebel Duke’s Knights, Rei could understand that they were already strong enough as things stood, and that they would only grow stronger from here on out.

“Sorry to ask you to spar with me.”

“No… it’s fine. If I don’t keep moving, I’ll inevitably get rusty. Considering that, I actually welcome it. …That said, the purpose of my visit here today is…”

“I know.”

Philma, who had spoken up, nodded in response to Rei’s words.

Originally, the reason Rei had come to this training ground today was because he’d received a message from Philma.

That sense of déjà vu—or rather, that question—he’d felt before facing Philma: hadn’t he fought her somewhere before?

Rei had been summoned here today specifically to receive permission to answer that question.

To be honest, Rei had been curious about that, but now he didn’t feel a burning need to know.

However, since he had nothing in particular to do at Kerebel Duke’s residence, Rei chose to find out.

So he made his way to the training ground he’d visited before… and as it turned out, what awaited him was, for some reason, a mock battle.

That said, since there wasn’t much else to do—other than tasting ramen, having tea with Elena and Ara, or going out to Anesis to look around—Rei figured even a mock battle would pass the time, so he gladly accepted.

He fought mock battles with several knights, and his final opponent was the knight who had used the spear earlier.

“Wait here a moment. I’ll go give my subordinates some training instructions.”

Saying that, Philma gave instructions to a few of his subordinates on what kind of training they should do next, then returned.

“Shall we go? …There are a lot of people here, after all.”

“Ah… I can’t deny that.”

In fact, it was clear that several people in the vicinity were focusing their attention on the exchange between Rei and Philma, even while they continued their own training.

Their own Knight Commander and Rei, known by the epithet “Crimson.”

It would be a lie to say you weren’t curious about what kind of history those two shared.

But the fact that Philma fought Rei is, of course, classified. …Actually, since she fought him just to quickly find out what kind of person Rei—the one Elena is concerned about—really is, it might not be that much of a secret… No, from Liberte and Philma’s perspective, since she acted on personal feelings, it could be considered classified in the sense that they don’t want anyone to know about it.

In any case, led by Philma—who certainly couldn’t let anyone overhear such a conversation—Rei leaves the training grounds.

“May I ask where we’re going?”

“There’s no need to worry that much. It’s over there.”

Philma, walking ahead, pointed to a room a short distance away.

It was likely a place where those exhausted from training could rest.

Rei had thought it might turn into a hassle, but fortunately, since it was just a nearby room, he felt relieved that it didn’t seem as troublesome as he’d expected.

That said, just because they’d entered a room a short distance from the training grounds didn’t mean he could completely relax.

Once inside, Philma filled a glass from the water pitcher and placed it on the table.

Although it wasn’t snowing today, it was still cold enough that snow could fall at any moment.

For a moment, Rei wondered if water was really the best choice of drink under such conditions… but for Rei, who was wearing a Dragon Robe with basic air-conditioning capabilities, this was no problem at all.

After sitting down at the table and taking a sip of water to ease the fatigue from the mock battle, Rei turned his gaze back to Philma, who was sitting across from him, and spoke.

“Well then. I’d like to get right to the point. Judging by Philma’s demeanor, there’s no doubt you’ve fought me before. I assume I can ask you about that, right?”

“That’s right. …I have definitely fought Rei before.”

So, it was as I thought.

Convinced of this in his heart, Rei urged Philma to continue with a glance.

“So? Where did we fight? Someone as distinctive as you, Philma, is someone I’d never forget after just one look.”

Philma wasn’t particularly handsome, nor was he astonishingly massive.

As a knight—and as someone whose name is known even in other countries—he certainly has a sturdy build, but there are surely countless others who are larger than Philma.

Yet the reason I believe I wouldn’t forget Philma if I saw him once is that there was something uniquely distinctive about the aura—or whatever you want to call it—that he exuded.

(Philma said she’d fought me, and if that’s the case, we must have fought… but where, exactly?)

Based on my experience and intuition, I felt certain we had fought before.

Since Philma herself had confirmed it, there was no doubt about it.

But... even so, I can't quite remember where we fought.

“Azott Trading Company”

Standing before a pensive Rei, Philma simply said those two words.

Hearing that name, Rei recalled an incident from several years ago.

It was the story of a man, blinded by the allure of a being named Set and magical items, who had tried to meddle with Rei.

As a result, the man who had harassed Rei met his downfall, and the current Azoth Trading Company is headed by that man’s half-brother.

Recalling that battle... and then, in the next instant, the pieces in his mind began to fall into place one after another, and he remembered one of the people he had fought during that turmoil.

“That guy in the full plate mail!”

It was the opponent he fought when he stormed the Azott Trading Company.

He was clad in full armor, his face completely hidden... yet he was an incredibly powerful enemy.

Now that I think about it, Philma’s movements were strikingly similar to those of the full-plate mail-clad opponent I fought back then.

“Looks like you’ve remembered. …To be honest, I was worried that by playing it coy like this, you might actually have forgotten.”

Normally, one wouldn’t so easily forget the memory of fighting a formidable foe like Philma.

But in Rei’s case, in just a few years, he’s had experiences so intense that an ordinary adventurer wouldn’t be able to experience them even in a lifetime.

In fact, when it comes to formidable foes, he has even fought against Rank S adventurers and Rank S monsters.

Philma is undoubtedly one of the top-tier foes Rei has ever faced, but for someone like Rei, who has fought countless times against even more powerful opponents, it’s only natural that her impression of him has faded.

“There’s no way I’d forget him—he was a formidable foe. …Well, there’s no doubt there were plenty of other formidable foes, too. …So? What do you mean, Philma was cooperating with the Azott Trading Company? You’re not telling me the Duke of Kerebel’s family was in cahoots with the Azott Trading Company, are you?”

These days, the Kingdom of Mireana and the Bestir Empire enjoy friendly relations.

But that’s merely superficial—or at least, that’s how it appears on the surface.

For these two nations, which have been at war for so long, many likely view this not as a time of peace, but as a period of preparation for the next war.

Given this situation, if it turned out that the Azott Trading Company—which had been collaborating with the alchemists of the Bestir Empire—was actually connected to the Duke of Kerebel’s family as well, it would be a major crisis.

However, in response to Rei’s words, Philma silently shook her head.

“No, neither the Duke of Kerebel’s family nor I have any connection to the Azoth Trading Company. If Rei hadn’t clashed with the Azoth Trading Company, I probably wouldn’t have been there. …Though, in that case, it’s highly likely I would have fought Rei somewhere else.”

“……What?”

That was tantamount to saying that his goal was purely to fight Rei, regardless of the Azott Trading Company.

But why would he do such a thing… Rei couldn’t understand that.

“Why would you need to do something like that? You weren’t exactly at odds with the Noble Faction back then… The only thing I can think of is the matter of the Altar of Succession?”

“That’s right. I can’t explain the exact reason, but it’s definitely related to that incident.”

That’s what Philma said, but in reality, it was more about confirming what kind of man Elena had taken an interest in for the first time.

It wasn’t that she didn’t want to avoid having the Succession Altar discussed at length, but… the former reason was overwhelmingly more significant.

However, for Rei, who didn’t know Liberte or Philma’s personalities well, his strong impression was that the Altar of Succession was the reason Philma had challenged him to a fight.

In fact, Philma had told Rei that there was no doubt he was involved in that matter.

She’s trying to gloss over it, but Rei concludes that’s likely just part of her job.

“I see. Just so you know, though, I don’t plan on telling anyone about that incident. Besides, even if I don’t say anything, the people who need to know probably already do.”

Among those who ventured into the dungeon at the Altar of Succession with Rei was a man named Ver Sails.

That person eventually defected to the Bestir Empire, and it’s believed he passed on every piece of information he knew to them at that time.

In fact, it wouldn’t have been surprising if he knew even more about the Altar of Succession than Rei did.

Philma half-understood Rei’s line of thinking… but instead of deliberately clearing up the misunderstanding, she simply nodded in response.
