The tree roots that formed the root doll dissolved completely, sinking into the ground... and the moment it vanished, the ground shook as if timed to match.

"Eek! Wait, what the...!?"

A short distance away from Rei, Vihera, who had been fighting Treant, screamed at the sudden tremor.

That she didn't fall was likely thanks to Vihera's excellent reflexes.

Set, the four-legged one, showed no particular sign of being unsettled by the sudden earthquake. Instead, he seized the opportunity to strike Treant, who had been thrown off balance by the tremor, with a blow from his front paw, destroying Treant.

"Dammit! What's happening!?"

Rei, too, was startled by the sudden tremor, but he quickly readied himself, scanning the area as if expecting anything to happen at any moment.

Unaccustomed to earthquakes, Vihera took a step back from Treant and scanned the area.

Unlike Vihera, Rei was startled by the sudden tremor, but he didn't seem particularly surprised by the earthquake itself.

That was probably because Rei was born and raised in Japan.

Japan is one of the most earthquake-prone regions in the world.

Minor tremors of around seismic intensity 1 occur almost daily.

Even tremors of magnitude 2 or higher, strong enough to be felt, occur quite frequently.

Having grown up in such an environment, Rei had developed a certain degree of familiarity with earthquakes.

That's precisely why, even as the surroundings shook violently, Rei had the composure to simply assess the situation around her.

But as far as Rei could see, while the earthquake was shaking things, there didn't seem to be anything else unusual going on.

(What is it? It's definitely shaking like this, so something must be happening... What exactly is going on?)

She looked toward where the tree roots had sunk into the ground, but there was nothing unusual there either.

Seeing that Treant's movements had also stopped, she was certain something was about to happen.

But... when exactly would it happen?

(Could it be... that I'll just stay trapped in Treant's forest like this? Surely not?)

Rei briefly entertained that thought, then shook his head in disbelief.

The will conveyed through the image language was, above all else, an almost obsessive compulsion to expand himself... to expand Treant's forest.

If asked whether destroying a single root puppet could suppress that compulsion, Rei would answer no immediately.

That's how intense the will was.

He remained vigilant, staring intently at his surroundings.

Then... gradually, the intense earthquake shaking the surroundings began to subside.

If that were all, there would have been no particular problem.

No, it might even have been cause for celebration.

But the story changed entirely when, the moment the earthquake subsided, the ground beneath Rei and the others began to crack.

"Vihera, Set, we're evacuating this area immediately! If we fall into those fissures, it won't be a laughing matter!"

Set reacted first to Rei's shout.

He knocked Treant down with a single blow to his right leg and immediately ran toward Rei.

Vihera followed a split second later. Rei leapt onto Set's back, and Set flapped his wings, soaring skyward.

Without a moment's hesitation, Vihera grabbed onto Set's rising body.

From the moment Set delivered the final blow to Treant until they took flight, less than ten seconds had passed.

It was a remarkably swift sequence, yet it could still be considered pushing the limits of what was possible.

After all, mere seconds after Set took flight, the entire plaza was swallowed by a massive fissure and plunged into the depths.

"That was close..."

Judging from the ground below, the very open space where Rei and the others had been moments ago had been entirely swallowed by the fissure and no longer existed.

No, it was hardly even appropriate to call it a fissure anymore.

Considering the sheer amount of space it had swallowed, it was more accurate to say a hole had opened up in space itself... that description wouldn't be wrong.

"...But strangely, only that area of ground collapsed. Look, see the path we walked on?"

Pointing it out as she casually clung to Set's front paws, Vihera's words made Rei shift his gaze toward the path... and he nodded in understanding.

From above, the path hadn't collapsed into a fissure at all; in fact, there wasn't even a single small fissure to be seen.

If this had been far from the spot swallowed by the ground, Rei might have accepted it.

But this was right next to where Rei had been.

Despite that, the complete absence of even a single fissure was something he simply couldn't believe was normal.

"Well, it definitely wasn't a normal earthquake. So, magic or something must be involved. ...Still feels off, though."

Given that the enemy possessed the power to cause an earthquake of that magnitude, it wouldn't be surprising if they used any means necessary.

In fact, it was understandable... and Rei wasn't the only one who felt that way.

"So, what do we do now?"

"...Staying here won't accomplish anything. Let's head back to where Marina and the others are for now. I'm kinda curious how things are over there."

When Rei entered the forest, the battle was still raging outside its edges.

If that was the case, it was possible the fighting was still going on even now.

(Well, when the earthquake hit, both Treant and the tree root dolls stopped moving, so it wouldn't be surprising if Marina took advantage of that opening to act.)

Given Marina's command abilities, if the enemy suddenly stopped moving in the middle of battle, she'd undoubtedly launch a full-scale offensive immediately.

And Rei couldn't imagine the immobilized monsters being able to do anything against the attacks unleashed by the adventurers.

And indeed...

"Just as I thought."

Less than a minute after Set took flight, he arrived at the edge of the forest... the spot where Rei and the others had set up camp. There, the motionless monsters were being defeated one after another by the adventurers.

Witnessing their comrades wounded and killed... adventurers burning with hatred for the monsters, and those seizing this chance to defeat them—not for the magic stones, but to secure materials or proof-of-kill parts—were working relentlessly, as if driven by a relentless urge.

Time rewinds.

Not long after Rei had entered Treant Forest, Marina was simultaneously giving orders to the adventurers, using her bow to support her allies, and employing spirit magic to defeat monsters.

Managing three roles at once, yet still retaining some composure, Marina turned her gaze toward Treant Forest.

Even from here, illuminated by the moonlight, she could see several giant flowers growing.

(But should I call it good luck that fewer of those troublesome seeds are flying this way than toward the first flower?)

The very first giant flower to sprout had rained down seeds... well, calling it a rain might be a slight exaggeration, but from our vantage point on the ground, it certainly felt like it was showering us with countless seeds.

The seeds varied widely in size, from small to large, and some people even injured their arms despite shielding themselves from them.

It was a terribly troublesome attack, but the frequency of these seed assaults has decreased significantly.

Thanks to that, we could definitely focus on the enemies on the ground.

"Treants are gathering on the right! And watch out for plants suddenly sprouting from the ground! Besides fighting, keep your eyes on the soles of your feet too!"

Giving orders, Marina fired an arrow at the monster blooming with blue and red patterned flowers.

The arrow sliced through the air, flying straight and hitting the flower monster's stem, severing it cleanly.

Watching the stem fall to the ground, Marina further commanded the wind spirit to create countless wind arrows, which she fired toward the area where the Treants were concentrated.

The sight of countless wind arrows piercing and slicing through the mass of Treants was a display that perfectly showcased Marina's skill as an elemental mage.

While not quite on par with Rei's, Marina's spirit magic was exceptionally suited for wide-area attacks.

In fact, compared to Rei, who struggled with precise control, Marina's spirit magic held an advantage: the attacks executed by the spirits required almost no direct involvement from her.

...That said, depending on the spirits' whims, they could sometimes act in ways Marina hadn't even considered. Fortunately, right now, they were following her commands... achieving exactly the effect she'd desired.

Watching this, the other adventurers couldn't help but think that compared to Rei's wide-area annihilation, Marina's spirit magic, capable of focusing attacks within a certain range, might actually be more practical.

"Hey, wait, that!"

Amidst this, a single adventurer's startled cry suddenly echoed through the area.

It was probably just a coincidence that the voice carried through the battle, but for the adventurers, it could be considered fortunate.

Because the reason the adventurer cried out in shock was seeing the sight of giant flowers vanishing one after another.

Of course, "vanishing" didn't mean the giant flowers were literally disappearing completely.

Only the parts of the giant flowers were vanishing dramatically.

"That's... Rei."

Because she was part of the party and cherished that person, Marina understood how the scene unfolding before her eyes was happening.

She realized that the strike had come from the Twilight Spear.

And indeed, that was correct.

"That attack... Rei...?"

One of the nearby adventurers heard Marina's whisper and murmured softly.

But that quiet murmur somehow... strangely enough... reached the ears of those around them.

Even amidst the battle, that voice reaching their ears was enough to boost the adventurers' morale.

Even though their numbers were dwindling, they had defeated the monsters that had been raining down various creatures, large and small, from above.

There was no way their morale wouldn't soar.

"Aaaah, that giant flower was such valuable research material..."

"But judging by the way it looked, it doesn't seem to have been completely destroyed, so maybe that's a good thing..."

The researchers' murmurs reached Marina's ears, but she ignored them as if it were only natural.

She understood what they were saying, but her priority now was simply defeating the enemy.

"Now! Reduce the enemy numbers as much as possible before the seeds fall!"

She shouted as she drew her bow and shot an arrow through the Venus flytrap.

There was no way the adventurers wouldn't obey Marina's command.

She was a dark elf beauty fitting for the night, and her words came from the former guildmaster.

No one present could fail to be lifted by that sight.

Some of the female adventurers felt a bit uneasy about the situation, but given the current circumstances, they had no choice but to fight hard.

And so, with everyone attacking the enemy under high morale, the battle continued for a while.

Suddenly, the monsters stopped moving.

The adventurers battling the monsters were naturally the first to notice.

"What!?"

The adventurer who swung down his longsword had expected that blow to be blocked.

The strike he had just unleashed was deliberately intended to be parried.

The plan was to strike a fatal blow when the monster blocked.

That was the plan behind the attack... but Treant took the blow head-on.

He didn't even try to dodge or block it.

In the end, the swing succeeded in cleanly severing one of Treant's branches.

Since the goal was to make him block it, the strike itself wasn't that powerful.

But perhaps that was just what was needed for the adventurer to notice something was wrong with Treant.

Sensing something was wrong, the adventurer leapt back from the spot in an instant.

If he had a more aggressive nature, he might have followed up with another attack.

Indeed, glancing around in that instant, many were attacking the monster that had suddenly stopped moving for some reason.

"What!?"

The reason they cried out in shock again was because the ground suddenly shook.

The male adventurer, experiencing an earthquake for the first time, instinctively braced himself by thrusting his hands into the ground to steady himself.

Had the monsters been able to move freely at that moment, the adventurer's life would have been completely forfeit.

The shaking felt that intense.

But the shaking was only momentary.

Countless screams filled the air around them, but once the shaking subsided, all that remained were the monsters, which had inexplicably stopped moving... It was only natural that the adventurers launched their attacks on those monsters.
