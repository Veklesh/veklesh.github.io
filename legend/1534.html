Rei's reasoning and Elena's statement that, by common sense, it's hard to believe the upper echelons of a nation would aid in such an act.

At those words, silence filled the table where Rei and the others sat.

All that could be heard was the din of the cafeteria, the chatter of other patrons.

Some discussed what kind of people Rei and his companions were, others were mesmerized by Elena and her companions' beauty, and there were even voices betting on whether Nokor would succeed or fail in his flirtation... such was the murmur.

Thanks to Marina's spirit magic, Rei's conversation remained inaudible to those around them.

Instead, they should have been hearing another conversation, as if they were talking about something else.

Even knowing this, Rei and the others couldn't carelessly speak.

Originally, everyone present—except Nokor—had come to the Kingdom of Reburina as retaliation against Jaya, who had meddled with Gilm.

But upon actually arriving, what they found unfolding was not the work of a single organization, but a conspiracy involving the upper echelons of a small country, albeit a country nonetheless.

It was only natural that they found themselves at a loss for words.

It was Rei who broke the silence.

"Well then, what do we do now?"

Despite the situation threatening to shake a nation, even a small one, his calm demeanor as he spoke was astonishing to Nokor, who didn't know Rei well.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn't be strange for him to be somewhat intimidated by the magnitude of the situation.

Yet, while there was a hint of tension in Rei's tone, it was only that slight.

Then again, this wasn't the first time matters of national survival had involved Rei.

He hadn't just been involved in the turmoil of a small country like Reburina; he'd been involved in the civil war of the Bestir Empire, a power on par with the Mireana Kingdom... no, worse, he'd even served as a central military force in it.

Compared to that, this level of turmoil was surprising, but it wasn't enough to make him flinch.

Seeing Rei's attitude, the tension surrounding them eased.

"Yes. The real question is... whether it's right for us alone to resolve this."

Marina's words drew a look of unease from Nokor.

It was only natural. For Nokor, it was imperative that Rei and the others join forces with the Resistance to bolster their depleted ranks.

Unconcerned with Nokor's reaction, Rei and the others continued their conversation.

"Hmm, then I'll ask Ara. That way, word should reach Lord Daskar through her. We'll seek instructions on what to do next. Or perhaps it would be quicker to bring the Paired Orb before Lord Daskar and explain the situation directly?"

"Well, that sounds like the best plan. If we make a big move here and end up destroying the Kingdom of Reburina, that would be no laughing matter."

Marina said this half-jokingly, but her narrowed eyes showed no trace of humor.

She understood that with Rei's power... and their own strength, bringing down the Kingdom of Reburina wouldn't be particularly difficult.

"For now, we'll ask Lord Daskar how far we should go... Rest assured. I can't say if we'll go as far as confronting the nation itself, but retaliation against Jaya is absolutely certain."

Nokor visibly relaxed at Rei's words.

He judged that, for now, the current undermanned Resistance could undoubtedly catch their breath.

"Thank you. So, may I take that to mean we can establish a cooperative relationship with you, Lord Rei?"

"Yes. But we don't have much time. So, we want to strike at Jaya as soon as possible... Is that feasible?"

"That... I'm afraid I can't say for certain. I'll inform the Resistance, though."

Nokor's expression showed a hint of surprise at Rei's mention of time being short.

He hadn't expected that even skilled operatives like Rei and his team would have something so urgent they needed to rush.

Or rather, even if they did have something urgent, with Rei and the others' track record, he figured they could handle it without much trouble... that was probably more accurate.

...Though, of course, Nokor probably couldn't have understood that the real reason they were saying time was short was actually related to the expansion work on Gilm.

"Yeah, please. For now, what we're doing is retaliation against Jaya. And the target of that retaliation is Mejogo's underground facility."

A bitter tinge mixed into Rei's expression as he stated this.

If what Rei suspected was correct, that underground facility was a place truly deserving of being called hell.

But if the underground facility was such a place, it was undoubtedly extremely important to Jaya—and to the Kingdom of Reburina.

After all, precisely because it was so important, numerous buildings protected it, making it impossible to move around easily from the surface.

"The problem is, if that underground facility is where Rei suspects it is, we can't just use magic from above to burn the facility itself... or anything like that."

Everyone present nodded at Elena's words.

If pregnant women were gathered there, they were women forcibly brought using slave collars.

Burning down a place where such women were held was absolutely out of the question.

While Rei didn't particularly care if the villains lost their lives, it was a different matter entirely if the victims were innocent... or rather, if they were victims forcibly brought to Jaya.

He couldn't even begin to imagine killing those victims by accident.

"So then, I suppose we'll have to sneak into that facility ourselves and cause a ruckus..."

Vihera declared this with a combative smile playing at the corners of her mouth.

Even Vihera didn't know exactly how powerful these giants were.

But they were undoubtedly formidable foes, and the prospect of battling them filled Vihera with a certain anticipation.

Though, if the circumstances of that giant's birth were truly as Rei predicted, she couldn't help but feel a little uneasy.

"That's how it will be. And the more time passes, the more lives of the prostitutes will be lost. Well, we don't actually know if it was a prostitute who gave birth to that giant, and giving birth to a giant doesn't necessarily mean death either."

Nokor swallowed hard at Rei's words.

He hadn't imagined his actions would trigger such a massive shift in the situation.

"I apologize, but I will inform the Resistance of Rei's hypothesis and proposed course of action. If Rei's hypothesis is correct, it could spell fatal damage for this nation."

"I suppose so."

While nodding at Nokor's words, Rei already suspected that the situation might already be fatal for the Kingdom of Reburina at this very moment.

The fact that the nation's upper echelons had formed a cooperative relationship with Jaya in the first place meant significant damage to the country.

Moreover, Jaya was treating women forcibly brought from other nations almost like sacrificial offerings, using them to produce giants... no, it was highly probable she was actually creating them.

(Even if we're talking about the upper echelons of the country, the specific individuals involved in this collaboration... that too would become a problem in this case.)

If the king knew nothing about this, it might be possible to somehow preserve the nation's form.

But if even the king, the head of state, is involved in this scheme... even Rei couldn't predict how far the fallout from this turmoil would spread.

Indeed, if handled poorly, there was even the possibility that the Kingdom of Reburina itself could be crushed and absorbed by the Kingdom of Mireana.

Rei watched Nokor bow and depart, thinking that surely Nokor wouldn't want that either.

Once Nokor was out of sight, Vihera spoke up.

"So, Rei, what are your thoughts on this whole affair?"

"What do I think? Honestly... I never imagined it would become this serious. I'm waiting for word from Lord Daskar. Elena, please handle this."

"Right. After this meal, I'll contact Ara immediately. This matter is quite important, so we might even receive instructions from Lord Daskar tonight."

Daskar was already swamped with work due to the expansion project, and learning about this incident would undoubtedly make his schedule even busier.

Of course, that wasn't necessarily a bad thing, but in terms of Daskar's workload, it certainly wasn't all good news.

Rei understood that too, but the situation unfolding in the Kingdom of Reburina was undoubtedly beyond what they could judge on their own... Ultimately, they needed Daskar to make the call.

"I'm counting on you."

That was why he answered Elena's words with just that brief request.

Perhaps pleased that Rei had relied on her, Elena smiled as she spoke.

"Leave it to me. Though, to be honest, this plan is only possible because Rei lent the Paired Orb to Ara. In that sense, me getting the credit... well, I'm not sure that's quite right."

"Oh, well, since Rei went out of his way to praise you, you should just be happy about it."

Marina said with an amused smile.

Despite the nation facing an existential crisis, there wasn't a particularly grim atmosphere among Rei and the others.

This was likely partly because Rei and the others weren't originally citizens of this country, Reburina.

Also, they felt that even if they wore serious expressions here, it wouldn't change anything.

Therefore, they reasoned it was better to conserve their energy for tomorrow.

After the meal ended, Marina released the spirit magic.

This meant that while anything said inside had been fine until now, from this point on, they would have to think carefully about what they said.

"...Hm?"

Suddenly, Rei noticed something was off.

The other customers around them were staring at him with what seemed like admiring eyes.

But Rei was used to being the center of attention.

Ignoring the stares, he returned to his room.

Then, as if it were only natural, Marina, Vihera, and Byune entered Rei's room.

Elena, however, was already in her own room to inform Ara about the recent incident.

Rei did wonder if it wouldn't be fine to use the Paired Orb in his own room, but for some reason, when he mentioned it, Elena flushed bright red and glared at him, so he decided to keep quiet about it.

"Ah... Still, I never expected getting dragged into such a mess just for coming to retaliate."

Sitting on the floor with his back against the wall, Rei sighed and muttered.

If it was just about revenge, it shouldn't have been this complicated.

But retaliation risked dragging in a huge number of uninvolved... no, victims of Jaya's actions. It wasn't something he could just casually do.

"True. But for Rei, it looks like he might get his hands on a magic item. Isn't that a good thing?"

"Even if it is a magic item... What am I supposed to do with something like a slave collar?"

Marina smiled at Rei's words, agreeing.

"Rei doesn't want slaves. Normally, a lot of people want slaves, though."

Marina's words were undeniably true.

Rei might sell captured thieves as slaves, but he himself had no desire for slaves.

This wasn't because Rei had memories of living as a Japanese person... it was simply that he just couldn't bring himself to like the very idea of slaves.

Many adventurers used slaves as laborers, but for Rei, having slaves posed no problem whatsoever.

Before forming a party, he traveled by riding Set, so having slaves would only have been a hindrance.

And now, as part of the Crimson Wings party, everyone is capable of various things.

Marina's spirit magic, in particular, possesses power that Rei would consider almost omnipotent.

So if asked whether he wanted slaves in such a situation...

(Ah, a thief slave could improve Byune's skills. In that sense, I suppose I want one... but honestly, that doesn't require a slave specifically.)

Though a thief, Byune possesses combat-specialized abilities honed through the intensive training with Vihera during winter.

If possible, Rei hoped Byune would also properly develop her abilities as a thief.
