The first thing Rei felt upon entering the forest was a sense of unease.

Or rather, perhaps it was the absence of any sense of unease that felt strange.

The place where last night's battle had taken place was already swallowed up by the forest.

That alone probably wasn't the only reason, but still, the fact that there was absolutely no sense of something being off felt odd in itself.

"...What do you think?"

"Well, even if you ask me... The fact that there are no monsters or animals here is just plain strange to me."

The first to respond to Rei's words was Marina, a Dark Elf with deep knowledge of plants.

But even Marina had never seen or heard of anything like this forest of Treants before.

"So these ordinary trees turn into Treants? If that's true... it's strange, isn't it?"

Vihera gently stroked the tree trunk.

But, of course, the tree trunk didn't suddenly start moving... or anything like that.

The information that one of the woodcutters they rescued yesterday had inflicted the wounds on the attacked Treant as a mark on a tree he had planted as a marker had, of course, been reported to the Guild.

Of course, since Rei and the others had returned home immediately after the rescue yesterday, they were summoned again early this morning for a detailed interrogation about the circumstances.

"If that's really true, though..."

"Oh? Rei doesn't believe it?"

After a moment's hesitation, Rei nodded at Vihera's words.

"Yeah, I can't fully believe it, at least not this time. If it were an adventurer, I could believe it. But this was a woodcutter caught up in a fight, pushed to the edge. What he saw could easily be explained by coincidence—like the wound on Treant just happened to resemble the one he'd made."

"...I can't deny that."

It's hard to expect a woodcutter, unaccustomed to combat, to trust the wounds he saw in the midst of that fight.

Especially the idea that a mere tree could transform into a Treant without anyone noticing.

(Ah, but that Treant was probably defeated by us and handed over to the guild. If we were to see it again... I wonder.)

The words came from a woodcutter who could be called an expert on trees. And above all, this was the mysterious Treant Forest, full of enigmas in every sense. While doubting the woodcutter's information, it couldn't be completely dismissed.

Half-believing, half-doubting... no, maybe three parts belief, seven parts doubt.

"Anyway, there doesn't seem to be anything particularly unusual around here, so let's move a little further on."

Following Marina's words, Rei and the others headed deeper into the forest.

But, as expected, there were no animals, monsters, or even insects around them.

"Same as always. ...And yet the plants are growing just fine. I wonder how they manage pollination?"

"Look, Rei. Normal plants don't grow this much in a single night, you know."

"...You're right."

Perhaps because he'd been coming to Treant Forest almost every day lately, Rei's sense of common sense had drifted slightly from the norm.

Anyway, Rei looked around once more as he made his way deeper into the forest.

Soft, spring-like sunlight filtered through the gaps in the tree branches, creating dappled light on the forest floor.

To someone unaware, this scene might make them want to rest here.

But to those who knew the truth about Treant Forest, even such a scene might be seen as a camouflage meant to deceive those who entered.

Of course, that suspicion was certainly not wrong.

For Rei and his companions, who had experienced battle in Treant Forest, relaxing leisurely in a place like this was simply impossible.

Still, feeling a sense of relief here... in a way, it was probably inevitable.

"Hm!"

Amidst the sound of wind rustling branches and leaves, and the distant voices likely belonging to another survey team different from Rei's group, Byune's voice suddenly rang out.

Rei looked over and saw Byune standing there, holding a fruit in her hand.

"Oh, Byune. What's that?"

"Hm!"

At Vihera's words, Byune shifted her gaze to a tree a short distance away.

There, hanging from the branches, were several fruits similar to the one Byune held... fruits with yellow skin.

"Do you know what this fruit is?"

From Rei's perspective, the fruit resembled the color of a mandarin orange.

But could one truly trust fruits growing in Treant Forest? The answer was probably no.

In fact, when Byune tried to bring one to her mouth, Vihera stopped her.

"Don't do it. You never know what kind of fruit grows in this forest."

"Hmm..."

At Vihera's words, Byune reluctantly stopped trying to bite into the fruit.

Watching the two of them interact, Rei took another look around.

Except for the complete absence of living creatures, it looked like any ordinary forest you might find anywhere.

But still, this forest was abnormal.

After all, the very notion of trees sprouting overnight was undeniably bizarre.

"Come on, let's keep moving for now. Hopefully we'll find some kind of clue."

Rei announced as he listened to the rustling of branches and leaves.

The others seemed to have no particular objection to Rei's words and continued deeper into the forest.

"...I see."

After walking through the forest for about ten minutes, Marina suddenly murmured.

"What is it?"

Thinking Marina, who knew the forest well, must have noticed something, Rei asked. Marina pointed at the ground.

"Look. ...Doesn't it feel off?"

"...Something off?"

"Grrrrr?"

As if triggered by the word "off," Set began digging at the ground with his front paws.

Given that Set possessed enough power to blow away even large monsters with a single blow, extending his claws to dig the ground would be easy.

But there was only soil... and tree roots buried in the ground.

"What are you doing?"

Finding Set's behavior amusing, Vihera said this with a smile playing on her lips.

"Grr?"

Set stopped digging and tilted his head, asking what was wrong. But Vihera was just barely holding back laughter, though it wasn't clear exactly what had tickled her funny bone.

Watching the one person and one creature with a hint of exasperation, Marina spoke again.

"Look closely at the ground. See how there's a certain distance between each tree?"

"...Well, yeah, that's true."

That's precisely why walking here wasn't particularly difficult, and even the large-bodied Set could enter the forest normally.

If the trees were denser here... if it were more like a mountain path or a game trail, then vines and thickets would likely snag on Set's body, making walking difficult.

Then again, considering Set's immense strength, it probably wouldn't be impossible for him to simply tear through any snags as he walked.

Thinking that, he remembered how utterly unnatural this scene was.

Even when he was in Japan as a child, Rei used to run freely through the mountains, and since coming to this Elgin, he had entered mountains, forests, and woods countless times.

In such places, unless it was a well-trodden path, walking easily was impossible.

But this Treant Forest was just right... the space between the trees was wide enough that even Set could walk around without issue, and there wasn't much undergrowth either.

Of course, it wasn't completely free of them, but it was clearly less than in a typical forest.

"But this is Treant Forest, right? It's an absurdly expansive forest that grows every single day, so isn't that within acceptable limits?"

"When you put it that way, I can't really argue."

Since it was a special forest to begin with, it wasn't strange for it to have a few peculiar aspects.

Rei could only nod in agreement at Vihera's words.

"I understand what you're saying, Vihera, but hear me out a little longer. If this tree is going to become a Treant... well, obviously, if they're growing too densely, other Treants would get in the way, right?"

"...Well, that's true."

Trent walk using tree roots as legs, so if another Treant is nearby, their branches and roots will inevitably collide.

That would indeed make moving around difficult.

"Right? So, going back to what I was saying earlier, this tree has just the right amount of space for us and Set to walk through. ...Doesn't that make you want to believe the woodcutter's explanation about trees becoming Treants, at least a little?"

Hearing Marina's words, Rei pulled a demolition knife from his Misty Ring.

After glancing at the others to ensure they were ready to react to anything, he lightly thrust the blade into the tree trunk.

The tip sank a few centimeters into the trunk, but the tree showed no sign of moving.

Just to be sure, he thrust the knife into the trunk several more times, but still, the tree didn't start moving.

「……Marina」

"Oh, that's strange."

This scene was apparently unexpected for Marina as well.

She gazed at the tree with puzzlement.

But after a moment's thought... she finally spoke.

"Hey, Rei. Can I take this tree home with me?"

"Well, I guess it's fine... but are you serious?"

"Yeah. It shouldn't be too much trouble for you, Rei, right?"

"Well, that's true."

Whether you're a woodcutter or an adventurer, normally you'd have to swing your axe countless times to fell a tree.

But with Rei, it doesn't take that long to fell a tree.

After all, one swing of the Death Scythe is all it takes.

Marina gave him an expectant look. Laying his knife back into the Misty Ring sheath, he drew the Death Scythe in its place.

Seeing Rei draw the Death Scythe, Marina and the others instinctively backed away without a word.

The Death Scythe's strike was powerful, but its attack range was also wide.

Their actions were meant to avoid getting in Rei's way.

"Hmph!"

He swung the Death Scythe, channeling magic, in a flash to the side.

Even as the blade passed through, the tree didn't move at all.

But the moment it touched the tree... the next instant, the tree was stored away in the Misty Ring, leaving only a stump behind.

(It'll probably grow back tomorrow, though. ...Utterly troublesome.)

Stowing the Death Scythe back into the Misty Ring, Rei called out to Marina, hiding the unpleasant feeling he had about the endless, ever-expanding forest despite his unease.

"Is this good enough for now?"

"Yes, thank you. ...Rei's Misty Ring really comes in handy at times like this, doesn't it?"

Rei nodded at Marina, who told him this with a smile, and once again surveyed the surroundings.

But, naturally, there was nothing particularly noteworthy there; only the forest stretched endlessly before them.

"What should we do? Either direction is fine, I guess."

"Grrr..."

Set was the first to react to Rei's question about which way to go.

Glancing toward the right, Set growled.

Rei nodded, accepting Set's choice. Neither Marina nor Vihera voiced any objections either.

Byune too simply stroked Set's head, respecting his will.

And so, Rei and the others proceeded rightward through the forest.

After walking on for a little while...

"Guruu"

Suddenly, Set growled.

Rei, who had followed Set's gaze, soon noticed the approaching presence heading their way.

"Huh!?"

Rei swiftly drew the Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring.

He likely judged that fighting with the Death Scythe would be difficult in the forest.

Seeing Rei move, Marina, Vihera, and Byune also seemed to sense someone approaching and began preparing for battle.

"Hey, is there really something over here? Broken trees aside, the standing ones are radiating magic all over the place, so it's hard to tell, you know?"

"It's fine. My instincts whisper that something big is over here. Don't you think so too, Slesha?"

"No, I..."

"Wait!"

"Huh!? ...Wh-what was that all of a sudden?"

"Strong... strong magic... Huh? Wait, this is..."

"Powerful magic!? There really is something over here! Come on, let's go!"

"No, wait a minute. There's nothing special over there..."

With that voice, several figures forced their way through the branches where Rei and the others were looking.

The first to appear was a slightly overweight man in his twenties.

He was taller than Rei, though still considerably shorter than average for Elgin.

Next to emerge were... just as Rei had predicted, Runo and Slesha, along with several familiar adventurers.
