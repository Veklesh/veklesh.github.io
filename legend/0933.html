Rei chased after the man who had spilled his soup on the ground, arriving at a deserted back alley.

He arrived just as a man was cornered at the dead end, surrounded by four armed men who were treating him with contempt.

Well, time to properly thank him for the soup. That's what Rei thought, but he was startled to recognize one of the five people present... the man cornered in the alley.

It was the man Rei had spoken to on the road to Glacial when a carriage wheel had gotten stuck in a hole.

That man must have remembered Rei too.

The fact that he had his hood down while drinking soup earlier might have contributed to this.

To an outsider, he looked like nothing more than a novice wizard wearing a robe.

Yet, having seen Rei once before, it was clear to the man that the figure before him was Rei... the one who had commanded the griffin.

"You... Why are you here?"

Rei shrugged slightly and spoke to the man, who muttered with an air of disbelief.

"Why? I told you I was heading to Glacial, didn't I? I was chasing the guy who made me spill my soup, never expecting to run into an old acquaintance. More importantly, this is quite an interesting situation. What happened to your guards?"

Though Rei addressed the man, the four others weren't about to listen silently. Instead, they judged that an accomplice of the man they'd planned to torture to death had appeared, and exchanged glances.

Then, one of the men approached Rei.

He was about two meters tall, with a reasonably muscular build.

Holding a club in his hand, the man opened his mouth irritably, addressing Rei.

"Hey, hey, hey. You're in the way. Just when we were about to have some fun... So, die, die, DIE!"

His emotions must have flared as he spoke. He raised the club he held and swung it down toward Rei's head without hesitation.

If Rei had been an ordinary human, his skull would undoubtedly have been crushed.

But... Rei was far from what could be called an ordinary human.

Instead of stepping back to avoid the club aimed at his head, he stepped forward.

The man holding the club was startled by Rei suddenly appearing in his chest. Just before the club struck the ground, he felt a heavy blow to his stomach and his consciousness plunged into darkness.

"Guh!"

Glancing at the man who flew away, emitting a sickening scream, Rei turned his gaze to the remaining three men and opened his mouth.

"So, looks like you guys are the ones responsible for this mess... What the hell is going on?"

'......'

Rei asked quietly, but none of the remaining three men answered.

They simply couldn't comprehend what had just happened right before their eyes.

Before their eyes stood a figure who appeared to be a wizard.

Normally, such a presence would warrant caution, but a wizard without a wand or similar magical implement was hardly worth fearing. ...Thinking this, the strongest among them tried to kill him... but before they knew it, it was their own comrade who had been blown away.

If they had been blown away by some kind of magic, they might have understood.

But the small figure they saw, who they thought was a wizard, wasn't chanting any incantations. In fact, he didn't even have a magic-casting device.

Moreover, he had dodged the club's blow and closed the distance to the men's comrade.

A normal mage, even if they somehow managed to dodge an attack, would absolutely retreat to create distance in that situation.

And then, before anyone realized it, the man wielding the club was sent flying with a dull thud.

About five meters, in fact.

Clearly visible on the torso section of the leather armor covering the man's body were the distinct marks of a fist.

In other words, the club-wielding man had been punched... no, punched flying by the small wizard standing right in his line of sight.

After a dozen seconds passed, the men whose gaze remained fixed on Rei finally grasped what had happened. ...They had no choice but to.

It was an event they absolutely didn't want to believe, understand, or accept, but what had just happened before their eyes was an undeniable fact.

"Damn it! Who the hell are you?!"

The leader-like figure among the men shouted, pointing the dagger he held toward Rei.

His gaze no longer held any trace of the man he'd been planning to torture and kill just moments before.

He only perceived Rei as a threat before him, thinking solely of how to escape this situation.

The other two must have understood the leader's thoughts. Instantly, they too pointed their daggers at Rei.

"You're the kind who doesn't listen to others. ...I did ask you guys what caused this, though. Well, whatever. So, what's the deal?"

Avoiding the gaze of the men pointing daggers at him, Rei's eyes shifted to the nobleman.

The nobleman had seen Rei leading a griffin on the journey to Glacial.

In this kingdom of Mireana... and perhaps even within Elgin itself, as far as he knew, there was only one other being who could command a griffin as a familiar.

(Am I saved? Can I think that?)

He had heard rumors about the figure known as Crimson Rei, but he didn't think this person was someone who could do anything about his current situation.

Yet, having exchanged just a few words with him, he somehow possessed an aura that felt trustworthy.

"These men are assassins hired to kill me."

"...Again?"

Seeing this situation, I'd expected it, but getting tangled up with this sort of scum again was a hassle even for Rei.

They'd originally come after me because I spilled soup, but this situation was completely unexpected.

(Why am I even being targeted by some assassin... huh?)

He muttered his complaints inwardly, then suddenly realized something.

As the saying goes, "Let the professionals handle it." If they were in the same line of work, they might have information about whoever was after him.

"Thinking about it that way, this situation is actually convenient. Like a moth flying into a flame."

An inexplicable tension emanated from Rei.

The men, frozen stiff by that tension, must have sensed that the words just uttered by Rei were utterly inconvenient for them. Almost reflexively, they sprang into action.

"Dammit, let's do this! Kill this bastard!"

"Wooooooooooh!"

"S-stop it!"

Against the three men charging at her with daggers, Rei's action was brutally simple.

He simply charged straight at the trio advancing toward him.

However, the speed of his charge was incomparably faster than that of an ordinary human.

It wasn't quite as fast as when he wore the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, but it still seemed to vanish from the men's vision in an instant.

Furthermore, Rei wore a Dragon Robe made from dragon hide and scales, its defensive power capable of withstanding a blow from an ordinary weapon without a scratch.

So, what happens when Rei, clad in the Dragon Robe, charges head-on at a speed too fast for the men to even register?

The answer was revealed by the squelching sound of crushed flesh echoing through the alley.

And it wasn't just one sound of flesh crushing. It echoed twice, thrice, continuously around them. Simultaneously, the three men who had collided head-on with Rei were sent flying, crashing into the wall and collapsing to the ground.

They must have lost consciousness from the impact the moment they collided with Rei. The men lying on the ground showed no sign of movement whatsoever.

"Well, that's about what I expected,"

He muttered, picking up the dagger lying on the ground and stowing it away in his Misty Ring.

The nobleman hesitated, unsure how to react to the act of taking weapons from unconscious opponents without a second thought, but then remembered this wasn't the time for such thoughts.

"My apologies. You saved me. You did say we might meet in Graciall, but I never imagined we'd reunite so soon."

"I figured. ...So, what about these guys? They came for your life, right? Hand them over to the guards? Or just end their lives? Or maybe just leave them here?"

At Rei's words, the man shook his head without hesitation.

"There's no reason to waste time on these men. ...Rei. You are indeed the adventurer known as Crimson, correct?"

"Huh? Yeah, that's me. ...If you don't plan on doing anything with these guys, I'm sorry, but can I take them? I've got my own troubles to deal with. Someone from the underworld might know something about the situation."

At those words, the man's expression grew complex.

It wasn't just that Rei was also entangled in trouble, but more than anything, it made the words he was about to speak even harder to say.

But the fact remained that he couldn't afford to hesitate. He was safe now, but that was only for himself.

The men who came with me from my family home as bodyguards were fighting assassins at this very moment.

"I'm sorry, but I must be frank. My guards are currently engaged in combat with assassins. They used their wits to get me away from the scene, but..."

Hearing that far, Rei understood what the man meant.

In other words...

"You want my help?"

"That's right. It's sudden, and I have almost nothing to pay you with as a reward, but I'll give you everything I have on me right now. If that's not enough, I'll pay you back later. ...Can you do it?"

His words sounded condescending, yet strangely, Rei felt no aversion toward the man before him.

That was probably because, regardless of his tone, she could tell he genuinely cared about her safety.

He was judging her not just as a bodyguard, but as a comrade.

Observing the man and thinking for a moment, Rei decided it was better to secure as many sources of information as possible about whoever was targeting her. She accepted the bag he offered.

Without even checking how much money was inside, Rei nodded.

"I'll take it. So, where should I go? ...Ah, but first. You seem to know my name, but might I ask yours? It's probably best to know the client's name, right?"

"Right, I haven't introduced myself yet. I'm Eland Viden. I'm from the House of Viden, Viscounts."

"Eland. It'll be brief, but I'll be counting on you. So, the location? Lead the way."

At Rei's words, Eland immediately nodded and dashed out of the alleyway...

"Huh!? ...Rei!? And you're..."

He nearly collided with Glink, who was just about to enter the alley.

Rei, who should have been chasing after the person who spilled the soup, was somehow now moving alongside a man who looked like a noble.

From Glinck's perspective, it must have made absolutely no sense whatsoever.

But Rei didn't bother explaining to Glink. He simply stated his demand bluntly.

"Those four men lying over there—they're the ones who tried to kill this nobleman, Eland. I'm sorry, but I need you to secure them. They might have information about the guy who attacked me."

"Please, Instructor Glinck!"

Saying only that, Rei left the scene with Eland without waiting for a reply.

Rei could guess Eland's background—or rather, his position—from the fact that he'd mentioned Grink's name.

Glink hesitated for a moment, unsure whether to pursue the two. Still, he couldn't just leave the men who caused the disturbance in Glacial behind. And if they might hold information about the attack on Rei, he certainly couldn't ignore it.

After confirming the four were unconscious, Glink moved to summon the guards.

"It's around the corner over there!"

Rei ran down the main street, following Eland's instructions.

Though the crowd was dense, slipping through the gaps posed no real difficulty for Rei.

Turning the corner and continuing down the road with Eland, who was slightly behind, he soon began to hear a faint metallic clanging sound.

Yes, the kind of metallic clanging that sounded like weapons clashing.

For a moment, Rei considered leaving Eland behind here. But if he left him alone, another assassin could attack him, and then it would be hopeless.

He decided it was better to keep him close so he could protect him, and that Aeland would feel safer with his escort. Together, they plunged into the area that had become a battlefield.

It was an alleyway similar to the one where Rei had just defeated the four assassins.

However, it was several times larger, resembling a small plaza.

In that space, several men familiar to Rei were each wielding weapons, fighting against about twenty opponents.

Though outnumbered, Eland's guards held the advantage in pure individual skill.

And into the midst of that battle, Rei burst in, carrying Eland.
