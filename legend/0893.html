The day after the duel was settled, Rent was also present in Rei's room at Dusk Wheat inn as dusk fell.

He had brought cheese, part of the reward for hunting the Gamelion, but the expression on his unshaven face now was neither relief that the hunt had ended safely, nor the emptiness of his treasured homemade cheese disappearing from his hands...

"Huh? A duel with you? Who said that?"

"The Count of Eriell's household. Though, not their representative—or rather, not the highest-ranking noble here in Gilm, that Keep guy—but a proxy they hired. I'm the Baron of Sisne's proxy too."

"...How did that even happen?"

"This Keep guy from the Eriell family apparently took a fancy to one of Baron Sysne's maids and tried to drag her off by force. When Baron Sysne stopped him, it turned into a quarrel, and now they're settling it with a duel."

"What the hell? What was that Keep guy thinking?"

Rent muttered, looking utterly baffled.

It was only natural. To anyone listening, it was clearly Keep's fault—it seemed like he was just trying to use his power to forcibly take the maid away.

"I don't know. But he seems absolutely confident he'll win. He even drew up legal papers in front of Lord Daskar, stating he'd transfer half the Count of Eriell's estate to Baron Sisne if he lost."

"...Is that man sane? He's taking on Rei? He'll definitely lose half his fortune!"

Rent tilted his head in question.

Of course. He couldn't imagine anyone in Gilm wanting to fight Rei head-on.

Well, it is a guild where many adventurers gather. Perhaps someone might want to try fighting Rei, but even so, they couldn't believe they could win against him.

"Well, I don't know what he's thinking, but for me, it's just a fight. I'm ready anytime... but I'll be bored out of my mind until the duel day."

"When is the duel?"

"Huh? Afternoon, four days from now."

"...That noble opponent is completely finished. There's no one stronger than Rei in this Gilm right now..."

...probably doesn't exist. Rent started to say that, but immediately hesitated.

"No... it's winter now. Even Rank A adventurers who'd normally be out and about should have returned by now. Some of them have nicknames. If he hired one of those skilled fighters..."

Then they might be able to fight Rei on equal terms. Rent thought this, but quickly shook his head.

True, while the final outcome against a Rank A adventurer might be uncertain, they'd certainly possess the strength to fight Rei.

But in this case, fighting Rei would mean siding with the corrupt noble who tried to kidnap the maid.

Rent couldn't imagine anyone wanting to antagonize Rei—or more accurately, Rei, the owner of Set—and risk the ill will of Gilm's residents just to aid the enemy.

"Well, whoever they send, I'll just deal with them. Of course, I have absolutely no intention of losing."

Rei declared with a confident smile.

Watching him, Rent muttered inwardly, Yeah, right.

"Anyway, it's weird how this all happened so suddenly... You really are a magnet for trouble, aren't you? Honestly, it feels less like being favored and more like being cursed."

"Is that so?"

Rei recalled the string of incidents that had occurred since his arrival in Gilm.

Starting with the orc settlement incident, then heading to the dungeon with Elena, the Azoth Trading Company affair, the magic fever outbreak.

Beyond those, he'd been caught up in countless other incidents—or rather, he'd actively sought them out. So yes, he was definitely favored by trouble, loved by it... and even if someone called it a curse, he couldn't help but agree.

"Still, I'm pretty satisfied with it all. Thanks to those troubles, I've managed to get my hands on top-notch cheese like this."

He touched the cheese sitting on the table and stored it in his Misty Ring.

Watching this, Rent looked up at the ceiling with a slightly awkward expression.

For Rent, the thought that asking Rei for help might just be another one of those incidents was probably weighing heavily on him.

"Yeah, how about inviting Johanna to come watch the duel? Judging by that Keep guy's words and actions, he's probably aiming to decisively show everyone my defeat, snatch Asie away without anyone complaining, and likely gather a big crowd for it. Plus, from yesterday's exchange, it seems Lord Daskar is aiming for that too."

"Daskar-sama too? Huh, I see. That does sound interesting. Then, inviting Johanna... Hey, Rei. Do you think Johanna would be interested in a duel? After all, she was originally your subordinate, so she should know your strength better than anyone. Meaning, from Johanna's perspective, victory is completely assured."

There's nothing more boring than a fight where the outcome is already decided.

As Rent spoke like that, Rei opened his mouth while gazing out the window at the falling snow.

"In Johanna's case, wouldn't she be coming for Set rather than my fight?"

"Ah, I see!"

"That's unexpected," Rent exclaimed softly.

Though...

"Wait a minute. If Set's there, and probably a lot of people will gather... doesn't that mean Mireine might come too?"

As that possibility dawned on him, Rent's expression darkened with despair.

Johanna and Mireine. Rent, who had been present when they first met, knew all too well how badly the two women who both loved Set got along—as if they couldn't stand each other.

"Well, she'll probably come. But if you want to date Johanna, you can't leave Set out. And if you can't leave Set out, then naturally you can't leave out Mireine, who dotes on Set."

"That's... true, but..."

There was no room to argue with Rei's words.

Johanna, Set, Mireine. It was clear these two women and one creature would remain connected long into the future.

Whether that bond was good or bad was another matter.

"Besides, if it comes to a duel, there'll probably be bets and stalls setting up shop. It'll feel like a winter festival, right? Why not enjoy that festival with Johanna?"

"A festival, huh. Thinking about it that way, it does sound nice."

"Right? You could call it a snow festival, I suppose."

"A snow festival, huh. Using troublesome snow as a reason for a festival is a bit unusual."

"It might be fun to try making snow sculptures instead of stone ones and compete on how well they turn out."

Behind Rei's words lay memories from his time in Japan.

Though not broadcast on TV on a grand scale, snow festivals were held almost every year in regions with heavy snowfall.

He recalled participating in them and getting caught up in the excitement.

"Well, it's snowing pretty hard now... It's snowing so much you'd think, 'What was that about no snow just the other day?' In that sense, I can see what you mean, Rei. But instead of telling me, wouldn't it be better if you, who's close to Lord Daskar, suggested it?"

"...Yeah, you're right. I'll go talk to him right away. What about you?"

If I went with him, Lord Gilm might remember my face. That was the implication behind my words, but Rent shook his head.

"Nah, I've got something to do..."

Rei, sensing the reason behind his hesitation, grinned knowingly.

"I see. Well, getting Johanna to notice you will be tough, but good luck."

"What the hell is going on!? Why is there no one here to act as my proxy!?"

With a shout full of irritation, Keep swung his arm wildly.

In the process, a glass sitting on the desk was knocked off by his hand, hitting the wall and spilling its hot wine onto the carpet.

In stark contrast to the cozy atmosphere of the evening room at Dusk Wheat inn, this space was filled with a palpable tension.

Fultos, who had brought the report, was ashen-faced.

It wasn't that he feared becoming the target of Keep's temper...

That was part of it, but he was terrified because they still hadn't found a proxy for the duel scheduled in four days.

More accurately, he was terrified that if things continued like this, they would lose and be held responsible for the loss of half the Earl of Eriell's estate.

Initially, Fultos had felt some unease, but he had assumed finding a proxy for the duel would be straightforward.

Keep is the heir apparent of the Eliell family, part of the King's faction, the largest faction within the Kingdom of Mireana.

His influence in Gilm, the stronghold of the neutral faction, wasn't particularly high, but even so, for ordinary adventurers, he was a figure worth cultivating connections with. ...Or so Fultos, Keep, and all their knight subordinates had thought.

"That penniless noble will probably send some weakling bodyguard, right? ...If no one else shows up, I'll have to leave it to you lot. You don't mind, do you?"

Keep addressed the knights, his gaze fixed on them.

The knights were Keep's trusted confidants. He didn't want them injured in something as trivial as a duel, but if no one else came forward, it couldn't be helped.

"Leave it to us! Any fools who dare defy Lord Keep, I will—"

"No, I shall become Lord Keep's sword and cleave through any who stand in his way!"

"I shall pierce through anyone who dares harm Lord Keep with my spear!"

The knights declared this with confident smiles, seemingly convinced they could not be defeated.

Among those in the room, only Fultos felt a faint unease.

(As these men say, that guard was short and didn't seem particularly muscular. If that's the case, victory should be certain. But... then what is this unease in my heart? I hope the man I sent to gather intelligence manages to bring back useful information.)

Watching Keep and the knights thoroughly disparaging Rei, who would likely be his opponent in the duel, Fultos pondered.

"Excuse me!"

It was at that moment. A man, his face drained of color, burst into the room without knocking.

"Hii... Wh-what the hell, you fool! Barge into my room without knocking? What the hell do you think you're doing?!"

Keep let out a brief scream, but his face flushed crimson at his own embarrassment. He channeled his shame into anger and shouted at the man who had barged in.

That man was one of the servants brought along for odd jobs when Keep was sent to Gilm.

He was also one of the men Fultos had sent out into the city to gather information on Rei.

"My apologies, Keep-sama. I had sent this fellow out into the city to find out what kind of person your duel opponent is... Hey, you! Have some sense! What are you doing in Keep-sama's room... Huh? What's wrong?"

Fultos yelled, but noticed the man who had burst into the room looked terribly tense.

He wondered if it was because he'd been scolded by himself or Keep, but the level of tension seemed far too intense for that.

The man took a deep breath, as if to steady himself, before speaking.

"I investigated the man Lord Fultos ordered me to look into... I've identified his name."

"Huh? That's all you could find out, just his name? You're utterly incompetent, aren't you?"

Hearing that only the name was known, Keep showed his irritation.

But Fultos knew the man's capabilities.

He didn't believe this man, who handled everything flawlessly, would return after merely learning the target's name.

If that's the case, then that name must hold some significance...

"Who is it?"

"...Rei."

The moment he heard that name, Fultos felt the blood drain rapidly from his face.

As a resident of the Kingdom of Mireana, he naturally recognized that name.

As someone serving the nobility, gathering information about the kingdom was part of his duties.

And among the information gathered, naturally, were figures who had gained notoriety for some reason.

Yes, like someone who single-handedly decided the outcome of the war that occurred in spring and had a rank A monster, a griffin, as their familiar.

"Fultos, what's wrong? Just because you know that brat's name, is something bothering you?"

Keep, who only cared about himself, seemed to have no recollection of the name Rei at all. He stared suspiciously at Fultos, who had suddenly stopped moving.

The other knights also seemed unfamiliar with the name Rei. They directed worried glances toward Fultos.

Under Kip's gaze, Fultos opened his mouth, the flesh on his cheeks trembling.

"Crimson."
