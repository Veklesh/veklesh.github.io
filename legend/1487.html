As night deepened, those who had been feasting began wrapping things up, citing concerns about tomorrow's work.

Just as the date was about to change, a man appeared before Rei and the others... or more precisely, at the tavern where Rei and the others were currently waiting.

Judging by his appearance, one might think he was just another drunk who hadn't had enough and had wandered into the tavern.

The man approached the counter with a gait that could only be described as a drunken stagger.

However, his walking speed itself was faster than a normal person's.

At first glance, it looked like he was moving unsteadily precisely because he couldn't keep his balance with that staggered gait.

Then, clinging to the counter as if to steady himself, the man stopped moving and addressed the man before him.

"Ajax has moved."

That single sentence was not the voice of a drunkard, not by a long shot.

It was sharp and utterly composed.

Yet the bar attendant, hearing it, nodded without any particular surprise at the words spoken from across the counter.

Of course. The man pretending to be drunk right in front of him was his colleague... a member of the intelligence unit.

Technically, he was still in a trainee position, but his affiliation with the intelligence unit was undeniable.

The bartender placed a glass of water in front of the man and headed straight for the back.

Entering one of the rooms, he gave a small nod to Dahl, who was inside.

Seeing that nod, Dahl's expression tightened.

It wasn't the gentle expression he'd shown in front of Rei, but the stern look befitting an intelligence operative.

"I'll go inform Rei and the others. In the meantime, gather as much information as you can."

"Understood."

Watching the others in the room begin to move, Dahl headed for a room a little further away... the room where Rei and the others were staying.

"Excuse me, it's Dahl. Would you have a moment?"

Saying this, Dahl knocked on the door. Hearing an immediate reply from inside that he could enter, he opened the door.

The first thing Dahl saw upon entering the room was Byune asleep, using her hands placed on the table as a pillow.

Next, he saw Rei, Marina, and Vihera talking.

And Ilse, who remained silent.

Entering the room and drawing the attention of everyone except Byune, Dahl opened his mouth.

"It seems Ajax has made a move."

The moment those words were spoken, Ilse reacted violently.

Of course she would. Finally, she could avenge her family.

"Made a move? Does that mean they've left the inn?"

"Yes. However, we still don't know where they're headed. But judging from the situation, it's likely..."

"I see. Then, I need detailed intelligence. If they try to escape Gilm with the woman they took, they're bound to draw attention."

"Yes. We're gathering intelligence now. So, I'd like to ask you, Rei and the others, to be prepared to leave at any moment."

Rei and the others nodded in response to Dahl's words.

Vihera began waking the sleeping Byune.

"Well then, I'll compile the information immediately. Excuse me."

Seeing Rei and the others begin their preparations, Dahl promptly exited the room.

Watching Rei leave, Rei steeled himself once more. Today's climax was finally upon them...

"Damn it, I didn't say anything wrong... Why did I have to be blamed like that?"

A tavern.

In the tavern, where the number of patrons had dwindled, Melan voiced his dissatisfaction while drinking.

Melan sat alone at his table, no other patrons nearby.

Though there were still patrons a short distance away, no one approached the table where Melan sat.

It felt like he was completely isolated.

In fact, some had initially approached him after observing his demeanor, but Melan had gotten drunk and picked fights with them.

While he might say the right things, being an adventurer isn't just about doing what's right.

Quite a few of them engage in actions bordering on criminal.

That's precisely why, even when hearing Melan's righteous arguments, few could truly agree with him.

Those who found Melan troublesome gradually drifted away from the table, leaving him drinking alone.

Fortunately... or perhaps not, at present, Gilm had no shortage of work due to the expansion project.

He could always pay for his drinks.

But for the tavern, having no customers around Melan was not a good situation.

Then again, it was already close to midnight, and most patrons would soon be heading home. He looked half resigned, thinking today was just one of those nights.

"Ugh... Am I really that bad...?"

Despite his drunkenness, Melan stood up and settled his bill.

After leaving the tavern, Melan stopped in his tracks as he was about to head back to the inn.

If he went back to the inn now, he might run into Ilse.

Remembering what Ilse had said to him at the tavern, his feet refused to move forward.

He was right. Ilse was the one who was wrong.

Even understanding that, Melan, who had never been told so bluntly and directly that his words were shallow, felt Ilse's words pierce him deeply.

On Earth, this time might be midnight, but on Elgin, by now, the vast majority were already asleep.

Of course, some were still making noise in the entertainment districts and taverns, but at least on the street where Melan now stood, the only light came from the moon and stars.

Even so, several prostitutes could be seen waiting for customers.

Prostitutes generally worked in brothels, but those who disliked socializing or couldn't work in a brothel for some reason sometimes sought clients like this on the city streets.

Melan, standing still in the passageway, looked like the perfect customer to those prostitutes.

He was still young, with handsome features.

It was only natural that the prostitutes would want someone with a handsome face to embrace them if they were going to be embraced by anyone.

A prostitute in her twenties, having spotted Melan, called out to him.

"Hey, you. How about spending some time with me? I'll give you a good price."

The unfortunate thing for the prostitute at that moment was that she didn't know the nature of this man named Melan.

And while Melan usually didn't bother lecturing even prostitutes, today he was deeply wounded mentally by Ilse's attack... no, her verbal assault.

As if venting his pent-up frustration, Melan berated the prostitute, demanding to know what kind of person sells their body like that.

Naturally, the prostitute grew angry at such words.

Words were exchanged, and the prostitute walked away.

Melan, drunk and half-out of it, watched the prostitute disappear into the alley... but then he saw her, walking down the alley, suddenly have her mouth covered by someone and be forcibly dragged away.

The moment he saw this, Melan lunged toward the alley.

"Wait!"

Perhaps from the shock of the scene, the drunkenness had already vanished from Mélan's mind.

He shouted as he sprinted toward the alleyway... but even though the drunkenness had left his head, the drunkenness remaining in his body hadn't vanished.

He managed to run with momentum for the first few steps, but beyond that, his legs tangled and he stumbled.

Carried by the momentum he'd built up when he started running, Melan rolled across the ground.

When he came to, he found a man carrying the unconscious woman over his shoulder and another man staring at him with puzzled expressions.

"...Hey, what do we do with this guy?"

"What do you mean 'what do we do'? He's a guy, right? We're collecting women, so taking this guy along wouldn't make any sense."

"Ah... yeah. Then kill him?"

The word "kill" came out so easily.

That it wasn't a threat or anything else was clear from the look the man who said it gave Melan.

In his current drunken state, resisting someone who wanted to kill him was difficult.

Understanding this, Melan still tried to stand up somehow... but while he could usually handle his usual amount of drink, today he'd had more than usual, and his drunkenness made it impossible to even walk properly.

(If only I hadn't drunk so much!)

She regretted it while watching the man, but for now, she had to deal with him somehow.

Melan searched for a way out while thinking this...

"Stop it. Killing a guy like this won't get us anything. Besides, it's almost time for the deal. Ideally, we'd want to secure a few more women."

"What? Still gathering more? Isn't it about time? The higher-ups aren't expecting that much from us, are they?"

"Maybe. But it's best to follow orders from above as much as possible. It's in our best interest."

"...Damn it. Fine. At least let me take this guy's weapon. That much's okay, right?"

"Yeah. Knock him out too. That way, with all this booze on him, he'll just look like he's passed out drunk."

"Alright. ...Well then, I'll put your weapon to good use. See ya."

"Wait!"

Wait, he wanted to say.

That's what Melan must have wanted to say.

But before he could finish the words, Melan felt a shock to his solar plexus and lost consciousness.

The man who watched Melan lose consciousness then turned to the other man and asked again.

"Are you sure we don't need to kill him? From the looks of it, this guy seems like a real pain in the ass."

He muttered this because he had seen the prostitute who had been arguing with Melan being dragged away, and despite realizing he was drunk, he had come to help her.

A strong sense of justice could sometimes lead to unexpected consequences.

Understanding this, the man asked if it wouldn't be better to finish him off here... but the other man shook his head.

"We don't want to cause any trouble today. Other guys are moving too. If the guards find this guy's body and it causes a commotion, you know what'll happen, right?"

"Ah... I see. So we knock him out and make it look like he's passed out drunk?"

"Yeah. A man who's been killed, or a man who's passed out drunk. Which one would cause more trouble for the guards? You don't even need to think about it, right?"

"...Well, I guess so. But..."

The man seemed convinced by the explanation, but the other man tilted his head, noticing he still looked like he wanted to say something.

"What is it?"

"No, I agree with your plan... but I'm wondering about the others. Especially that hothead Jars. He's got blood on his hands, right? He might start trouble with the guards just to get girls."

At those words, the man's mind flashed back to Jars' usual demeanor.

Satisfied, the man nodded immediately.

"Yeah, you're right. Anyway, let's gather the women as quickly as possible before any unnecessary trouble starts. If there's a commotion, the guards will crack down harder."

"...At least there aren't any adventurer patrols, so that's something."

Gilm's night patrols, unlike daytime ones, were conducted solely by guards.

This was partly because the number of people out at night was vastly smaller than during the day. But it was also because if they were accosted by someone drunk and emboldened, guards, being accustomed to handling such situations, could usually defuse the situation. Adventurers, however, might well escalate things into a brawl.

Of course, it wasn't as if the guards could absolutely guarantee they could immediately calm a rowdy drunkard, but when it came to experience, the guards definitely had the edge.

For this reason, men with something to hide find it easier to operate at night.

That said, since not many people roamed the streets at night, moving carelessly also meant they were more likely to be spotted by guards.

In any case, the two men decided further discussion here was a waste of time. Taking the unconscious woman and the longsword they'd seized from Melan, they left the scene.

About thirty minutes passed after the men left... and eventually, Melan awoke.

"I... Am I... in the middle of town? Huh? Why...?"

She found herself not in an inn bed, but inexplicably in the middle of the city.

And it was closer to what you'd call an alleyway.

Wondering why she'd fallen asleep in such a place, she looked around...

"Guh!"

A sudden wave of physical pain made him groan.

Ironically, that very pain was what reminded Melan of what had happened here.

He remembered arguing with a prostitute while drunk, then the woman leading him into this alley... only to be attacked by two men and losing consciousness.

Seeing this, he had wanted to rush to help, but in his drunken state, he couldn't do anything and was knocked unconscious.

"Damn it."

He frantically searched for his weapon, but nothing was around him.

Realizing it had been stolen, Melan, still not fully sober, stepped forward to retrieve both the prostitute who had been taken and his own weapon.
