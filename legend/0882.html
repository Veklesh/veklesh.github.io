When Rei arrived in the forest with the rat beastman Glin, Gamelion was already in a critical condition.

Though Gamelion had dominated the battle against the Rank C party Beast's Fang, led by Chrono, once Johanna and Rent joined the fray, the sheer numbers overwhelmed him, and he was pushed back in an instant.

"GAAAAAAHHH!"

With a roar, Gamelion kicked off the ground and charged toward Johanna.

A strike born of the desire to take at least one of them down with him.

Though Gamelion possessed fur resistant to slashes, his right eye had already been taken by a spear strike. Inside his body, bones were broken and organs crushed by attacks from Shant's club—a bear beastman—and the hammer wielded by the former guerrilla fighter.

Despite this state, Gamelion charged toward Johanna for one final strike, but...

"If you think you can defeat me that easily, you're sorely mistaken!"

Johanna, holding her spear in a thrusting stance, awaited Gamelion.

"Johanna!"

Rent shouted at the sight of Johanna, but she paid him no mind. Fixing her gaze on the fangs closing in to crush her face... the instant those fangs closed, she narrowly evaded Gamelion's strike and thrust the spear's tip deep into the fangs... into the throat.

The monster, boasting a three-meter frame, and Johanna, of average height for a woman, crossed paths. Ultimately, it was Gamelion who fell where their paths parted.

The spearhead, piercing through the upper jaw and into the skull, shattered the skull and brain, delivering a fatal blow. Gamelion collapsed to the ground.

Those around them observed in silence for several seconds... until Chrono, acting as their representative, spoke.

"Did... she do it?"

"Yes. If it were still alive after that, it wouldn't be Gamelion anymore—it'd be some sort of undead creature."

Johanna approached Gamelion, who lay dead on the ground, limbs splayed.

She pried open Gamelion's jaw and pulled the spear from his mouth.

"Wow... that's incredible..."

Jonah, the fox beastman, probably felt a woman's curiosity. She directed a gaze toward Johanna that held a hint of respect.

"Even rotten, she's still a former commando,"

Rei murmurs softly under her breath.

Johanna's excessive affection for Set was noticeable, but as a former guerrilla fighter, her skill was undeniable.

That was only natural, given she'd undergone rigorous training with Rei.

"Good work. So, what about this Gamelion? Your share is..."

"Hey, hold on. What the hell is your problem?"

Shant cut Rei off, his expression sharp as he interjected.

Of course, Shant knew exactly who the person standing before him was.

Any adventurer in Gilm today who didn't know Rei would be either incredibly naive or a fake.

...Though there were alchemists who didn't know Rei, not adventurers.

Still, Shant addressed Rei in such a sharp tone because he probably thought Rei, who hadn't done anything, was about to take their prey, the Gamelion, away from them.

Feeling that gaze and vaguely realizing what they thought of him, Rei opened his mouth.

"Just so you know, while I don't mind the reward for fighting alongside you lot like Rent did, I'm not expecting any payment for this Gamelion. I've already made proper arrangements for a separate reward."

"...You're telling the truth? I can trust you? If that turns out to be a lie, I won't forgive you, you hear?"

Rei nodded again at Shant, who sought confirmation.

Only then did Shant seem to relax. He turned his gaze to Chrono...

"You... you super-duper idiot!"

He leapt forward, and Chrono's fist slammed into the back of Shant's head.

"Gah! Ow, that hurts!"

"Do you even know who you're messing with?! This is Rei, the Rank B adventurer known as Crimson! And yet, what the hell were you thinking, picking a fight like that?!"

"S-Sorry, Rei-san. Our idiot... um, we don't have anything against you personally."

Amidst the echoing shouts, Jonah bowed his head repeatedly.

Beside him, Gurin stood awkwardly, looking utterly out of place.

Since he was the one who brought Rei here, he probably didn't know what to do.

"Don't worry about it so much. I'm not going to do anything to you guys or anything."

"...I'm sorry, really."

Chrono apologized last, and for now, that settled the current dispute.

"Ah... So what about Gamelion's share, then?"

Rento addressed everyone present, knowing this was the one thing they had to decide for now.

Chrono, meeting Rent's gaze, thought for a moment before speaking.

"Well... without you guys, we definitely would've been killed by Gamelion. So, just getting out alive is a win. I think we should take Gamelion down..."

"Hey, Chrono. That's way too generous. We're not exactly rolling in cash either, you know? At least half... No, I get that saving our lives was a big deal, and it makes sense he'd get more, but we still need a decent share."

"...But how are we supposed to split it? Gamelion already has strong resistance to slashes!"

It was Johanna who spoke up to the arguing members of Beast's Fang.

"Well, I think Rei could easily slice a Gamelion right down the middle. What do you think?"

"Gamelion? ...Wait, is that even expected of someone with a nickname?"

Chrono muttered under his breath, his gaze shifting toward Rei.

Hoping that the rumors about Rei were true and that it wouldn't be too difficult, he opened his mouth.

"Um, Rei-san. Could you do it for us?"

"Hey, wait, Chrono!"

Having just spoken to Rei in such a confrontational manner, Shant couldn't bear the thought of Rei relying on Rei now.

He reached out to grab Chrono's shoulder, but Glin stopped him.

"Hey, Rei... no, don't be rude to Rei. We were saved by them, you know. If it weren't for Rei and the others, you wouldn't even be able to speak right now."

"Grin... Ugh, fine. You're right, it's pointless unless we cut up the Gamelion."

At Glin's words, Shant, though clearly displeased with his own massive frame, muttered in agreement.

(Not like it's been decided I'm the one to do it, though. Well, no point wasting time here.)

With a hint of reluctance, Rei drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

The members of the Beast's Fang revealed their surprise at the sight, but it was a reaction he was well accustomed to, so Rei paid it little mind.

He channeled magic into the Death Scythe he gripped tightly and swung the blade toward the fallen Gamelion.

That single strike easily severed Gamelion, who should have possessed strong resistance against slashing attacks.

Gamelion's corpse, its head and torso cleanly severed left and right, spilled its entrails, filling the air with a strong, pungent smell of blood.

"Impressive... so effortlessly..."

Chrono, witnessing that strike, realized the rumors were true... or perhaps the rumors had even understated the truth.

The others felt the same, especially Shant, whose eyes widened in shock.

"...Ah. Um, you guys decide how to split the organ materials. We got your help, so we won't complain."

Chrono stood stunned for a few seconds before snapping back to reality and addressing Johanna, Rent, and the others.

Johanna and the others, watching this, silently sympathized with Chrono and the others.

They had seen more than enough of Rei's unbelievable actions to be sick of it.

The fire tornado that nearly single-handedly annihilated an entire squadron, the vicious combat prowess displayed during training against the guerrilla squad.

Of course, the guerrilla squad members had helped somewhat when he annihilated the main force, but even so, Rei's abnormal power was seared into their eyes.

That's precisely why the look of astonishment on those seeing Rei's awesomeness for the first time was something Johanna and the others couldn't help but nod at inwardly.

"Right. Then let's begin dismantling it. ...Do you have any carts or wagons over there?"

"Yeah, they're parked outside the woods. Well, no one's watching now... Green, you handle it."

"Got it."

At Chrono's words, Grin answered and ran outside the woods... in the opposite direction from where Rei and the others had come.

The carts were parked over there, and he was probably going to fetch them.

Watching Grin disappear, Chrono turned his gaze toward Johanna and the others.

"What about that cart over there?"

"Huh? Oh. Well, we have Rei-san with us..."

"...I see."

Chrono shot them an envious glance for just a moment, but quickly shook his head and spoke up.

"Then, shall we take care of the organ matter?"

"Yes. Let's do it."

Everyone present except Rei moved toward Gamelion.

Watching them, Rei felt his presence was unnecessary and called out to Rent.

"Rent, I'm going to look for another Gamelion. You can handle this, right?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah, that'd be a big help. It'd suck to come all this way to hunt a Gamelion and only get half a body."

"...Make sure you handle that half properly. It'd be a big help if the guts were already dealt with by the time I find a new Gamelion and get back."

"Hah, depends on when you get back, doesn't it?"

"Ah, Rei-san. Are you going to see Set-chan?"

Johanna said enviously, but she couldn't very well leave him alone with the Gamelion business still unfinished. She reluctantly held back her desire to say she wanted to go with him.

"That's right. Wait here a bit. Knowing Set, he shouldn't take too long to find a new Gamelion. Though if they're all fighting adventurers, it might be tough to find one."

"Please do. Since it's Set-chan, he'll find the Gamelion quickly, so I'll get ready over here too."

"Do your best."

After exchanging only those words, Rei left the spot.

Rent, who watched him go, looked slightly pleased. Probably because he thought he could finally talk to Johanna now that Rei was gone.

When Rei was around, Johanna inevitably talked about Set with him a lot, and Rent, wanting to impress Johanna, found that a bit annoying.

Well, even so, he didn't pick a fight with Rei. Partly because he'd asked Rei to come this time, but mostly because he knew Johanna liked Set and had called Rei knowing that.

"Hmm... You guys are lucky, aren't you? Getting to party with Rei."

It was Shant who said that.

He'd maintained a prickly attitude whenever Rei was around, but the moment Rei was gone, his demeanor softened.

"Oh? You dislike Rei? Well... I wouldn't exactly call him a nice person, but he's not exactly a nasty, unpleasant person either, you know?"

"Is that so? Well, I suppose you're right. But personally, I'd rather not acknowledge him."

"Mind if I ask why?"

"Hmph. I just can't stand seeing him lose to a little punk like that."

Shant slung his club over his shoulder and declared with a snort.

Watching this, Chrono sighed and glanced at his party members.

One of Shant's flaws, as Chrono saw it, was his tendency to look down on those smaller than himself.

Shant was naturally large. He inevitably believed that those bigger than him were stronger, and this was a source of frustration for Chrono, leader of Beast's Fang.

Back in the town where Chrono and his group originally operated, there weren't any other notable adventurers around, so Shant's opinion had been valid. But now that they'd come to Gilm, Beast's Fang was just one of countless rank C parties scattered about.

(Well, that's probably why Shant was so stubborn about it.)

Chrono's honest wish was for his childhood friend—who boasted the highest attack power among them—to shed those strange prejudices if possible.

If only he could overcome that... he'd really step up his game.

"Hey, Chrono. You're handling Gamelion's innards, right? Don't just leave it to us—help out too!"

"Whoops, sorry. I'll be right there. With Gamelion, a lot of the innards are usable materials, so you gotta be careful."

Jona's slightly angry words snapped Chrono back to reality. Apologizing softly, he headed toward the Gamelion carcass where people had already gathered to start processing it.
