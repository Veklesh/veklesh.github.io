In the endless depths of the ocean... the fish swam as if to declare itself the ruler of the sea.

In fact, the fish’s fangs were incredibly powerful, making it a true natural enemy to the schools of fish swimming nearby.

Even fish as large as a human were nothing more than prey to it.

Convinced that there was no rival for it in these waters, the fish suddenly caught the scent of blood drifting through the water.

For an ordinary fish, it would be virtually impossible to detect the scent of blood dissolved in seawater with such acute sensitivity.

But for a fish that could truly be called the king of the sea, it was possible.

And so the fish swam toward the source of the blood’s scent… searching for its next meal.

Devouring several fish it encountered along the way, it eventually arrived at a spot quite close to the shore.

Still, it was fortunate that the water was deep enough for the fish to move about with some degree of freedom.

In any case, as it drew closer to the source of the blood’s scent… the next moment, the fish’s consciousness faded.

“Got it! Set!”

“Grrrrrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl, flapped his wings, and grabbed the massive body that had floated to the surface with his front paws.

It was a fish of considerable size, likely over five meters long.

With fangs more befitting a carnivorous beast than a fish, it was nothing like any fish Rei had ever seen.

(Is it like a shark or something? But looking at it like this, it doesn’t look like a shark. Then again, I don’t know that many kinds of sharks.)

When it comes to sharks, Rei knows them mostly as the great white sharks and hammerhead sharks that often appear in movies, or the shark sashimi sold at his local supermarket.

If someone were to tell me that there are actually sharks like this out there, even if Rei doesn’t know about them, it wouldn’t be strange for me to accept it.

(Actually, is this even really a shark?)

While Rei was pondering this, Set flapped his wings and headed toward their base.

That said, while it is a base, it’s not the one set up near the hot springs for their operations here; rather, it’s a spot by the sea where everyone can rest.

It’s a makeshift shelter constructed using cloth and poles.

Since it’s a rocky shore rather than a sandy beach, they can’t exactly lie down and relax… but even so, the fact that they don’t have to be directly exposed to the summer sun—which, even in late summer, is still quite hot—is a huge relief for Rei and the others.

Currently, near that base, Byune and Yellow were enjoying the crab fishing they’d heard about from Rei, while the rest of the group was simply enjoying regular fishing.

Bistol, who had come all the way here with Rei and the others specifically to catch fish, was fishing quite seriously; inside the makeshift fish tank he’d set up using nearby sea rocks, nearly ten fish were already swimming.

So it was only natural that people were surprised when Rei and Set returned to that spot carrying a gigantic fish over five meters long.

Rei had already caught fish and returned here several times before, but there was no doubt that the fish they had just caught was incomparably larger than any Rei had caught so far.

“Wow, that’s amazing. …How did you manage to catch such a huge fish…”

Even for Marina, the size of the fish Set was holding seemed quite unexpected, as she wore a look of surprise—a rare sight for her.

Since Marina was born a dark elf, she lives in the forest.

When she was an adventurer, she traveled to various places—she’d even been to the sea—and had seen fish even larger than this, but even so, it was only natural for her to be surprised by a fish of this size.

Moreover, after she stopped being an adventurer, she served as the guild master in Gilm, so she rarely ever came to the sea.

In that sense, it wouldn’t be strange for Rei, who went directly to Emoshion, to know more about the sea.

……That said, unlike Rei, who has only been to Emoshion, Marina visited numerous port towns and fishing villages during her days as an adventurer.

“Those are some sharp teeth… but it’s a fish, right?”

Vihera, who was fishing, asked Rei as she touched the fish with a puzzled look.

Her words sounded as if she were wondering whether what she was touching was really a fish.

In fact, a fish five meters long would be extremely rare.

If this were a monster, however, specimens nearly ten meters long—let alone five—wouldn’t be all that uncommon.

“I took it down with a single strike, so it’s good that it can’t struggle… but well, it’s a bit hard to tell if this is a fish or a monster just by looking at it. In that case, I guess I’ll just have to check if it has a magic stone.”

Even as he said that, Rei didn’t recognize the fish in front of him as a monster.

That was because he knew Set had made that judgment.

Rei knew better than anyone just how sharp his partner, Set, was.

If Set had judged it to be a fish, then it was undoubtedly a fish.

“Oh my. Are you going to gut this big fish? If it’s okay with you, could I help out too?”

“Nah, I’m not going to gut it just yet. I’m just checking to see if there’s a magic stone inside. It’d be better to gut them all at once later, right? Right now, we need to catch as many fish as possible.”

“Too bad. I was hoping to get a little taste.”

As she said this, Bistol’s gaze was fixed on the campfire by the side of the tent.

Seeing that, it was obvious without even having to think what kind of “taste test” she had in mind.

“Fresh fish would be delicious enough as sashimi, though. …The problem is, I don’t know how to fillet fish.”

Back in Japan, he had cooked fish during cooking classes at school.

But the fish provided back then had already been cut into fillets, so Rei had no experience filleting a whole fish.

He had caught river fish, including sweetfish, in the river many times, but since those were usually grilled as is, there was no need to fillet them.

If I had to name one thing, it would be that I’d occasionally remove the innards.

And since she couldn’t even manage to neatly slit the belly and remove the innards, fileting a fish this size was practically impossible for Rei.

Even if he tried to fillet it anyway, it would likely result in a significant reduction in the edible portions...

“Sashimi? What’s that? What kind of dish is it?”

Perhaps intrigued by the word “sashimi” that Rei had mentioned, Bistol turned his gaze toward Rei with a look of interest.

No, it wasn’t just Bistol. Even Byune, who was just as—or in some cases, even more—greedy than Rei when it came to eating, was looking at him.

Although she was expressionless as usual, her small frame radiated a kind of intensity that would make any low-rank adventurer in the vicinity instinctively step back.

To Byune, Rei was an unbelievable companion who created dishes she had never seen or heard of before.

And the dishes Rei had come up with—whether it was udon, meat buns, or pizza—were all incredibly delicious.

She’d even heard about something called “seafood okonomiyaki” by chance, and she was determined to try it someday.

That’s exactly why, whenever a new dish comes out of Rei’s mouth, there’s no way she’d pass it up.

“Hmm!”

Byune’s words, spoken with rare enthusiasm, Rei scratched his cheek as if troubled.

“I can tell you, but I don’t know if you guys will be able to handle it.”

Rei knew that even if a dish was commonly eaten in one region, it might not be accepted in other regions or countries.

Take octopus, for example—a favorite of Rei’s.

While octopus has become a common ingredient in the West in recent years, it used to be called “devil fish” and was considered absolutely unfit for consumption.

Similarly, whale meat is commonly eaten in Japan, and while dolphin meat is also eaten—albeit in limited regions—it is not accepted overseas.

Furthermore, even within Japan, while there are regions where people eat insects like bee larvae and grasshoppers, there are others who simply cannot bring themselves to eat them.

In that sense, Rei wondered if sashimi—raw fish—might also be unacceptable…

“You won’t know if it’s acceptable until you actually ask and try it, you know? At the very least, I don’t plan on saying anything until I’ve actually tried it myself.”

Beside Bistol, who said this with a cheerful smile, Byune was also nodding silently.

Elena, Marina, and Vihera were also looking at Rei with expectant eyes, though not quite as intently as the other two.

Given how extensive Rei’s culinary knowledge is—and the fact that no one had ever found a dish he’d created to be unpalatable—that reaction was only natural.

Seeing Elena and the others like that, Rei resigned himself to it and spoke up.

“Sashimi. If you ignore the finer techniques and such, it’s simply a dish where you cut a piece of fish into bite-sized pieces.”

“……Huh? That’s it? Does that alone make it a dish?”

At Rei’s words, Bistol looked at him with an expression of disbelief.

“Yeah. But, as I just said, it does require a lot of detailed technique. If you don’t use a sharp knife, the fish will turn to mush, and it takes considerable skill to cut sashimi. In that sense, it’s a dish that showcases a chef’s skill to the absolute fullest.”

“I’m a little skeptical about whether you can even call that a dish.”

“That’s what I said, right? I don’t know if people will accept it. In any case, there’s no doubt that it’s a dish that can only be made with fresh fish.”

Rei also recalled another dish called carpaccio, where fish is eaten raw, but since he didn’t know exactly how to make it, he kept that to himself.

(Marinate it in vinegar? No, that feels like it would just turn into sushi topping. I think they used onions or something… Nah, never mind for now.)

He thought about the carpaccio he’d seen sold at the supermarket, but in the end, no recipe came to mind.

Even when he tried to recall his knowledge from cooking manga, he didn’t think there was any mention of carpaccio, so that was the end of it.

“Sashimi… I wonder if it’s just eating slices of raw fish as is?”

“No, I think you eat it with soy sauce as a seasoning and wasabi as a spicy condiment…”

I tried to say it as if I’d actually eaten it before, but panicked and covered it up like that.

“Soy sauce? Wasabi? I don’t know either of those.”

“Soy sauce is apparently a seasoning made from beans. Wasabi grows in rivers with clear, flowing water. Oh, wait. I hear it also grows naturally in the soil around here, even without water.”

When it comes to wasabi, the general impression is that it grows in places with clear, flowing water.

However, in the mountains near Rei’s home, there was a spot where wild wasabi grew naturally, even though there were no rivers or streams nearby.

Wasabi that grows buried in the soil, not in a river.

Naturally, this was wild wasabi that was perfectly safe to eat, and they often gathered it as a mountain vegetable.

However, at Rei’s house, instead of grating the wasabi to eat, they would finely chop both the roots and stems, pour boiling water over them, and then rapidly cool the bowl containing the hot water and wasabi in cold water… that was their method of preparation.

The resulting wasabi “soup”—though calling it that might be a bit of a stretch—allowed one to enjoy both the spiciness and the flavor of the wasabi.

For Rei, the most familiar way to use wasabi was as a condiment, drizzled over simmered greens or similar dishes.

“Anyway, it’s a shame we don’t have soy sauce or wasabi… Ah, but if we go to the villages or towns around here, we might find fish sauce or something.”

Fish sauce, which can be made with just salt and fish, had a flavor that didn’t suit those unaccustomed to it, but for Rei, who had been eating shotturu nabe—a hot pot made with fish sauce derived from sand lance, along with sand lance, tofu, and green onions—since childhood, it was undoubtedly a very familiar taste.

Even if there isn’t any shotturu made from sandfish, surely there must be fish sauce made from other fish.

Seeing Rei looking so happy as he pondered this, Bistol and the others wore puzzled expressions.

This was because they simply couldn’t imagine that raw fish could be that delicious.

“Come to think of it, I heard before that there’s a custom of eating raw fish in the eastern regions.”

“Really?”

When Vihera asked this in response to Marina’s words, Marina nodded, confirming that it was indeed true.
