"Eek, eek!"

The owl, whose injury had been treated, let out a cheerful hoot.

There had been quite a commotion over treating its injuries, but the owl, which had been thrashing about at first, seemed to realize that Bistol was there to help it, and by the end, it was sitting still while being treated.

With the treatment finished, the question remained: what to do next.

“Considering this injury, wouldn’t it be dangerous to release it now?”

Saying this, Bistol stroked the owl.

The owl accepted Bistul’s touch so calmly that it made her want to ask, “What was all that struggling about earlier?”

“But I can’t exactly take it with me, can I?”

Hearing Rei’s words, Bistol was torn.

The owl that had taken a liking to her was incredibly cute.

But as a merchant, it was also true that taking the owl along as is would cause all sorts of problems.

Some of his business partners might dislike birds, and in some cases, the owl might even attack them.

Considering all that, no matter how adorable the owl is, taking it along isn’t an option.

For Bistol, who loves cute things, this is a real shame.

“I suppose so. …Well then, could I at least stay with you until we set off?”

Even though she’d only treated its wounds, Bistol seemed to have taken a liking to the owl.

The owl, too, seemed to have decided that Bistol wasn’t an enemy and remained calm.

Then again, perhaps it didn’t try to escape from Bistol’s grasp precisely because it knew Set was waiting outside the magic tent.

“Still… I wonder what kind of creature attacked that owl.”

Watching the owl in Bistol’s arms, Elena voiced her curiosity.

(Ah, so it really is an owl.)

Although Rei had been fairly certain that was the case based on its appearance, Elena’s words finally confirmed it for him.

“If you think about it logically, it’s probably a larger bird of prey… or maybe even a monster, depending on the situation?”

Vihera murmured this, gazing at the owl in Bistrol’s hands with a hint of curiosity.

Perhaps sensing something in Vihera’s gaze, the owl stiffened for a moment.

Since it was in his own arms, Bistol naturally understood what was going on. He immediately shot Vihera a reproachful look.

“Hey, Vihera. Could you please not scare this little guy so much?”

“……I didn’t mean to, though.”

Vihera was genuinely just a little curious about the owl; she hadn’t been plotting anything in particular.

And yet, being so blatantly feared like this was, naturally, not something she found amusing.

Then again, perhaps the owl had sensed the danger in Vihera almost instinctively.

“Eek… eek!”

Seeing it let out a small cry, Vihera couldn’t bring herself to say anything more.

When Vihera stepped back, the owl narrowed its eyes as if relieved.

“Aren’t owls nocturnal?”

“Oh, Rei. It’s not like nocturnal animals never do anything except at night, you know? For example, we basically sleep at night, right? So, we’re not nocturnal—we’re diurnal. But we do go out at night sometimes, don’t we? It’s the same thing.”

The reason Marina knew even a little about owls’ habits was probably because she had grown up in nature as a dark elf.

That was precisely why she said that.

Rei, who had always thought owls slept during the day and were active only at night, was genuinely surprised by Marina’s words.

As they waited out the rain while petting the owl, the rain gradually began to ease up.

“Looks like the rain’s letting up. What do you say? Should we head out now?”

“……If you think you can handle it, Rei, then why not? After all, you’re the one who has the hardest time when it rains.”

“I’m not saying I don’t understand what Vihera’s saying… but, yeah. Should I use spirit magic to create some kind of shelter from the rain? I think I could manage it if I use wind spirit magic.”

At those words, Rei thought for a moment… She glanced briefly at the owl Bistol was holding, then shook her head.

“No, I’ll pass.”

“Oh my, to think you’d go to such lengths for me… I’m so touched.”

There was no doubt that Rei’s words were out of concern for the owl, and that was precisely why Bistol was grateful for his consideration.

That said, it seemed Bistrel’s gratitude didn’t quite reach Rei.

“If that’s how you feel, then at least take good care of that owl until the rain stops.”

He said this, averting his gaze from Bistol’s face.

Part of it was probably that he felt embarrassed by her straightforward words of thanks... but at the same time, it was a little painful to see Bistol’s face up close as he charmed everyone.

In any case, for one reason or another, Rei and the others ended up spending the entire day taking shelter from the rain.

“It’s gotten late, hasn’t it? …Wouldn’t you have preferred to make a little more progress today, if possible?”

“I won’t deny that.”

Rei nodded honestly at those words, which carried a hint of Marina’s teasing tone.

In fact, just when the rain seemed to let up, it would pick up again, and just when it seemed to be getting heavier, it would ease off.

Even so, the rain had been falling steadily the whole time.

If Rei had truly intended to travel in this rain, he could have managed it, even if it was a bit reckless.

For both Rei and Set… that level of difficulty wouldn’t have been a problem at all.

Of course, getting wet and feeling uncomfortable was a given.

Still, the main reason they didn’t end up doing something like that was because they were worried about the owl.

If this were the usual Rei, he would have judged that the survival of the fittest is a law of nature and wouldn’t have gone to such lengths to worry about the owl.

“Can I ask why you took such an interest in that owl?”

“No, there wasn’t any particular reason, really.”

What came out of Rei’s mouth wasn’t wrong.

It wasn’t as if he’d done something like this because of some specific reason… yes, you could even call it a mere whim.

“Well, fine. I’ll just go along with that for now.”

Whether she believed Rei’s words or not…

Those were the words that came out of Marina’s mouth.

From Rei’s perspective, she could tell that anything else she said would be pointless, so she could only endure Marina’s teasing gaze.

“Rei, shouldn’t we have dinner soon? If you’re going to prepare it…”

“No, there’s no way I can cook in this situation. I’ll get it ready.”

Rather than let Marina keep teasing him like this, Rei began pulling dishes out of the Misty Ring one after another.

A soup packed with vegetables, stir-fries, simmered dishes, and freshly baked bread.

Every single dish seemed so extravagant that it felt out of place to be eating them in a place like this.

"Eek! Eek!"

An owl wrapped in a cloth and placed a short distance away seemed to have caught the scent of the food coming from the Misty Ring, and began hooting as if to assert itself.

Seeing the owl’s reaction, not just Rei but everyone inside the Magic Tent couldn’t help but smile.

To an outside observer, it must have looked like a rather opportunistic display.

Of course, from the owl’s perspective, it needed food to heal its injuries.

There was no way it was going to let such a golden opportunity to get that food slip by.

Moreover, the owl likely views Bistol—who had protected it—as a kind person.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange for it to conclude that if it made a fuss here, it would undoubtedly be given food.

“Ah, I get it. I’ll give you plenty of food, so calm down. …Should I give you some raw orc meat?”

“That should be fine, right? You usually eat mice and stuff like that anyway.”

“Grr!”

Perhaps hearing the words “orc meat,” Set’s cry echoed from outside the magic tent.

Actually, it wasn’t just his cry—he actually stuck his head right through the entrance.

Seeing Set like that, the owl, confused, let out a cry and tried to hide behind Bistol.

It seems he understands exactly who is there to protect him.

「Set」

Rei had only called his name briefly, but that was enough for Set to understand what Rei wanted, and he pulled his head back from the Magic Tent.

And once Set was out of sight, the owl seemed to calm down as well.

(It’s gotten quite attached to him.)

As far as Rei knew, wild animals didn’t usually warm up to people that easily.

But the owl had indeed taken a liking to Bistol.

...Though, to Rei, the main reason it seemed so tame was likely because it was afraid of Set, who was outside the Magic Tent.

(Could it be that Bistol has a natural talent for taming?)

While tamers are generally thought of as people who tame monsters, that doesn’t mean they can’t tame wild animals or birds.

In fact, non-monsters are actually easier to tame.

However, since wild animals and birds are naturally weaker than monsters, they see very little action in combat.

Conversely, this means they’re useful in all sorts of ways outside of combat.

For someone in Bistol’s position as a merchant, taming an owl would undoubtedly be extremely convenient.

Being able to handle night watch duties or deliver letters to distant recipients would be a significant asset for a merchant.

Of course, if the owl is attacked by other birds of prey or monsters while carrying a letter, there’s a high chance it will die.

In any case, after observing Bistoll, Rei voiced his thoughts.

“Hey, Bistol. Do you think you might have a talent for taming?”

“……Huh?”

At Rei’s question, asked as he served food from the Misty Ring, Bistol had no idea what he was talking about at first.

But after a few seconds of silence, his eyes widened in surprise.

“Huh? Wait a minute, Rei. Is that true? Can I really become a Tamer?”

Watching Bistrel, who seemed quite enthusiastic, Rei spoke as he served the skewers.

“Just so you know, it’s not a sure thing. I just looked at that owl and thought, ‘Maybe that’s the case.’ I don’t actually know for sure either.”

“Oh, why not? You’re a tamer too, aren’t you, Rei-chan?”

“……My case is a bit special. Besides, being a tamer isn’t like there’s a set formula where you just do this because of that. The way people tame things varies quite a bit from person to person.”

“Really? …Hey, you. Do you want me to tame you?”

Hearing Rei’s words, Bistol—perhaps sensing a slight chance—asked the owl in his arms.

But the owl, seemingly completely uninterested in Bistol’s words, had its eyes fixed on the skewered meat Rei had just taken out.

In fact, it was so eager that if Bistol weren’t holding it, it wouldn’t have been surprising if it had immediately moved to eat the skewered meat.

“Hey, Rei-chan. Do you really think I have what it takes to be a tamer?”

Perhaps shocked that her words had been so completely ignored, Bistol turned to Rei with a look seeking an explanation.

“Well, it’s hard to say. Like I just mentioned, every Tamer is different. I think you’ll just have to try out your own methods to figure it out.”

That implied that, under the right circumstances, anyone could become a Tamer—and it was, in fact, Rei’s honest belief.

If someone is truly motivated, he believed that most people could probably become tamers.

Of course, taming rare animals or monsters would require the appropriate aptitude.

“……Really? Right. …Is that so?”

Bistrol muttered “Yeah” over and over, staring intently at the owl in his hands.

Even though they’d only just met, it was clear—even to Rei’s eyes… and to anyone else’s eyes—that Bistol felt a strong connection to the owl.

It wasn’t hard for Rei to predict what kind of decision Bistol would make.
