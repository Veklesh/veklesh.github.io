The Treant Forest was expanding its reach toward the direction of Gilm.

No one knew exactly when the Treant Forest had first appeared, but it was certain that it had originated in the area between Gilm and Abuero. The place where the forest was now spreading toward Gilm had once been grassland.

And so, those who had resolved to fight the giant turtle—believed to be the embodiment... or perhaps the very essence—of Treant Forest, including Rei, had arrived at the grasslands a short distance from the forest.

Those who avoided the battle with the giant turtle and headed to Gilm to report on the matter were already gone.

Furthermore, while many researchers had stated they would remain, considering the possibility of being drawn into the battle with the giant turtle, Marina had requested the adventurers heading to Gilm to take them along, albeit somewhat forcefully.

Initially, some had held onto the hopeful observation... or wishful thinking, that perhaps the giant turtle wouldn't leave Treant Forest. That hope was swiftly dashed.

The giant turtle emerged from Treant Forest without hesitation, pursuing the adventurers... or rather, Rei, who was among them.

"Well, it's better it came out like this than staying in Treant Forest,"

Rei murmured, enveloped in the visualized, Crimson magic.

If Rei so desired, that magic possessed enough power to incinerate anyone merely by touching it. However, through Rei's control, its effect was limited to merely raising the surrounding temperature slightly.

Still, just seeing Rei's current form would be enough to crush the fighting spirit of any ordinary adventurer.

Of course, that only applied to those who opposed him.

Given his sheer presence, he was incredibly reassuring as an ally.

Indeed, many of those who remained to fight the giant turtle relied on Rei, clad in the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, as their emotional anchor.

With every step he takes, the ground shakes slightly.

There's a saying about looking up to someone, but in this case, it was quite literal. The opponent was so immense, seemingly the size of a small mountain, that even looking up, one couldn't fully take in its entire form from close range.

Facing such an entity, needing some kind of mental support was undeniably true.

"Rei, how are we going to fight this?"

Standing beside Rei, whose Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour remained active, preparing to fight the giant turtle monster, Vihera’s face bore an expression of delight... no, one might even call it pleasure or ecstasy, a look of pure lust.

For Vihera, who found pleasure in battle, this fight was not something to fear, but rather something she desired.

Battles against giant flowers, stronger-than-usual Treants, and various other plant-type monsters... and now, the fight against the giant turtle that emerged from the Trent Forest.

This moment tonight was, for Vihera, a time filled with more fulfillment than she could have ever imagined.

Watching Vihera like this, Rei found a smile forming on his own lips.

He had assumed most would be terrified of fighting the giant turtle, yet here was someone who actually craved the battle.

This eased the slight tension in Rei's heart.

(A giant turtle... that doesn't quite fit the image, does it?)

He thought that as he watched the giant turtle closing in on him.

"Marina, do you know the name of that monster or anything?"

He asked, holding Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, but Marina only shook her head in response.

"Sorry, I've never seen one before. Probably a new species."

"I see. ...Well then, for now, let's just call it Giganto Turtle for convenience. 'Giant Turtle' is a bit too straightforward, don't you think?"

As Rei asked this, some of the adventurers around them offered small smiles.

While the monster's name was ultimately trivial, facing the colossal presence before them—something that gave the impression of a small mountain moving—Rei's words undeniably made them feel a bit deflated.

"Hah, yeah. Fighting something called Giganto Turtle sounds way cooler than just a giant turtle, doesn't it?"

"You just wanna brag to the barmaid, don't you? I know what you're up to, trying to impress Hasyana-chan."

"Guh, you... you're saying that here?!"

Even that kind of joke echoed around them.

"...Well, for now, how do we attack Giganto Turtle... What kind of attack would be best is completely unknown. All we know is that shell has incredibly high defense."

After seeing the tension ease as laughter spread over the talk of hitting on women at the tavern, Rei stated this.

At his words, the adventurers still present nodded in unison, their attention shifting toward the Giganto Turtle drawing nearer.

Turtle-type monsters weren't exactly common, but they weren't nonexistent either.

Given that, some nodded with genuine understanding at Rei's words, likely having fought one before.

"So then... I'll take the first shot. Luckily, I have a spear that comes back to me."

Hearing Rei's words, those familiar with the Twilight Spear's capabilities shot him envious glances. Simultaneously, those who had witnessed the near-frenzy among merchants caused by the spear's fame directed faintly sympathetic looks his way.

Under such gazes, Rei switched his grip, taking the Death Scythe in his right hand instead of his usual left.

As a magic item usable while activating the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the Twilight Spear was likely the pinnacle.

Thinking this, he infused the spear with magic power... then, with a light running start and a twist of his body, he hurled it with all his might toward his target.

When he had used it above the Treant Forest, he had been riding Set, so he hadn't been able to use his full strength.

When fighting the Tree Root Doll, the embodiment of Treant Forest, I hadn't activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Now, there were no such constraints whatsoever.

The spear, propelled by physical abilities enhanced by the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, flew straight toward the Giganto Turtle.

The distance between Rei and the Giganto Turtle was still considerable, yet the launched spear sliced through the air as if that distance meant nothing, flying through the sky.

That spear, capable of piercing anything and everything in its path, flew straight ahead... only to halt its motion mere meters before the Giganto Turtle, as if colliding with something invisible.

"What?"

Who murmured that?

All the adventurers remaining at the scene watched the trajectory of the Twilight Spear Ray launched by Rei.

That very fact made it completely unexpected—if it had been blocked by the shell, that would have been one thing, but for the spear to be stopped in empty space was utterly unforeseen.

The answer to that perplexed question came, unsurprisingly, from Runo, who had been standing beside Slesha... who had also remained behind.

Rei's skill, Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, had temporarily blinded her, but that effect had worn off by the time she arrived here.

As for why Runo was here, it was obviously because Slesha was here.

Runo could have easily left her behind, but since they'd come this far together, they'd decided to stick together.

In one way or another, she was quite the caretaker.

In any case, Runo's eyes... her magic-seeing magic eyes clearly understood what the Giganto Turtle was doing.

"Magic! It's using magic to create a barrier!"

As the words left his lips, a sound like glass shattering on the ground echoed around them.

What happened? The gaze of everyone present naturally turned to the Twilight Spear.

But the Twilight Spear that had been there just an instant before was no longer in the same spot... It was heading straight for the Giganto Turtle.

"Impossible! That barrier, so easily!?"

It was precisely because Runo possessed the Magic Eye that she could see with her own eyes just how formidable the barrier the Giganto Turtle had erected truly was.

Given that, it was no wonder one might wonder just how much magic power Rei had infused into the Twilight Spear to destroy the barrier so effortlessly.

To be precise, precisely because she possessed the Demon Eye, Runo could see just how much magic power had been infused into the Twilight Spear... but her astonishment at the Giganto Turtle's magic barrier was simply that profound.

That the Twilight Spear could pierce through that magic barrier so effortlessly was what made its power truly astonishing.

Regardless, the Twilight Spear, having effortlessly pierced and destroyed the magic barrier, continued straight toward its target without losing any speed.

Originally, it was a strike aimed at the middle of the Giganto Turtle's three heads.

However, it collided with the magic barrier and destroyed it.

That in itself was no problem at all; in fact, those watching cheered. However... as a result of destroying the magic barrier, the Twilight Spear's trajectory deviated from what Rei had originally aimed for.

This was only natural, but considering the Giganto Turtle's immense size, it was also only natural that the spear flew toward its body rather than off in some random direction.

It shattered, pierced, and destroyed the trees growing from its shell.

Given they grew from the shell, these trees were likely no ordinary plants, yet none possessed the strength to withstand the Twilight Spear imbued with Rei's magic.

The trees were destroyed one after another, yet the Twilight Spear's speed did not waver.

And then, in the next instant... A jarring sound like metal clashing echoed throughout the area.

"...Tough."

Even when pierced by the Twilight Spear, the Giganto Turtle showed no sign of pain.

That was proof that while the spear could scratch its shell, it couldn't pierce it.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR

The Giganto Turtle's roar echoed throughout the area.

Though louder than its previous roar, it lacked the power to intimidate those around it.

It was merely the sudden pain and the irritation of having been wounded.

Of course, it wasn't a fatal wound.

It was akin to having flesh torn from the skin, as if it were human.

It was certainly no minor wound, but it was far from what could be called a fatal one.

And considering the Giganto Turtle's size... its form literally resembling a small mountain... the wound it received from this blow was hardly worth making a fuss over.

But... yes, but.

Even so, it was an undeniable fact that Rei's strike had damaged the Giganto Turtle, which had seemed invincible.

Witnessing that firsthand inevitably boosted the morale of the surrounding adventurers.

Even moments ago, they had believed they could never lose.

Yet, seeing the Giganto Turtle's form, it was undeniable that a sense of fear had crept in.

But seeing Rei's attack now, seeing the Giganto Turtle scream in pain, it was only natural they felt they could fight it too.

They knew they were forcing themselves to feel motivated, but right now, they had no choice but to ride the wave of Rei's strength.

"Let's go! Take it down! Those capable of ranged attacks, provide support! Those without that option, aim for the Gigant Turtle's legs! But beware its massive size. Stay well clear of its attack range!"

This was no joke. A single hit would kill them outright.

There was no doubt the Giganto Turtle, as big as a small mountain, possessed that kind of destructive power.

"What about Rei?!"

"Me? Me... It's obvious, isn't it?"

With those words, the Twilight Spear—which Rei had thrown earlier and should now be embedded in the Giganto Turtle's shell—returned to his hand.

The adventurer who had asked Rei probably knew of the Twilight Spear's existence, but not its full capabilities.

He stared blankly at the Twilight Spear that suddenly appeared before him.

"After immobilizing that turtle, I'll roast it to a crisp."

The magic barrier, the high defensive power of its shell, the trees growing on its back... and various other factors meant that even using magic Rei excelled at might not be effective in this situation.

Given that, the best approach was to first immobilize and weaken it... or perhaps try a less powerful spell first before launching the main attack.

Having made that judgment, Rei mounted Set... and Vihera, now clearly accustomed to the routine, grabbed onto Set's front legs as they soared into the night sky.
