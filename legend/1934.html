"Oh... really?"

Even as she said that, Vihera kept her gaze fixed on Rei, as if she wanted to say something.

That said, Rei understood why Vihera was looking at him that way, so he didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

Regarding the Snow Cyclops incident, it was certain that Vihera wished she could have fought it herself.

“Yeah. As for the Kobold situation, I think it’ll be resolved before too long. I’ve left Grange with Egg, so there’s no need to worry anymore.”

A base for communicating with the person behind the red cloth.

The guards, having found a spot to keep watch over it, had immediately begun their surveillance.

After confirming this, Rei, as promised, entrusted Grange to Egg—or, more accurately, since Egg wasn’t there, to his subordinate—explained the situation, and returned to the site where the Giganto Turtle was being dismantled.

Waiting for Rei was a visibly displeased Vihera.

“So, the Kobold incident. …But I wonder why it had to be Kobolds.”

“That’s what I wondered, too, and the guards were just as puzzled. In the end, we never figured out the exact reason. But some of the guards speculated that maybe they just wanted to overwhelm us with sheer numbers rather than relying on a few powerful monsters.”

At Rei’s words, Vihera grew sullen once more.

From Vihera’s perspective, if they were going to control monsters anyway, she wanted them to control more powerful ones… specifically, monsters like the Snow Cyclops that Rei had fought.

That said, if a swarm of Snow Cyclopses were to attack, the earthen walls Rei had built would likely be easily destroyed—or, depending on the situation, they might even be able to scale them without even needing to destroy them.

If that were to happen, unlike the Kobolds who are somehow managing to hold them back at the construction site, there’s a very real possibility that the Snow Cyclopses would break through and run amok in the town.

While Gilm has many skilled adventurers, that doesn’t mean they could immediately defeat monsters like Snow Cyclopses rampaging through the town.

Above all, it is winter now, so there are certainly quite a few people who spend the entire day in the tavern.

If such people were to fight the Snow Cyclops, they would likely only cause even more damage to the surrounding area.

“For now, let’s do it tomorrow. Vihera can come with us tomorrow morning. That way, there’s a chance some of the stronger monsters—drawn by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood or the scraps of its flesh—will be there.”

Normally, Rei would have considered using terrain manipulation to bury the soil currently exposed and bring up different soil from beneath the ground...

But considering Vihera’s condition, he couldn’t very well do something like that.

...That said, since there was a chance for both Rei and Set to obtain an unknown magic stone, it wasn’t as if they were doing it reluctantly.

“Oh, really?”

To her credit, Vihera’s mood improved instantly at Rei’s words.

(Though, there weren’t any powerful monsters yesterday, and considering that, it’s not necessarily guaranteed that there will be any today.)

Ultimately, what kind of monsters appear is a matter of luck.

Even if Vihera came here with Rei tomorrow morning, if they were unlucky, there might only be monsters like Kobolds or goblins.

Still, since you can’t get anything unless you take the challenge, Vihera had no choice but to face them here.

“……Growl?”

Set looks at Rei and growls.

It was as if he were asking, “Can I go play?”… and his gaze was fixed on the maze Rei had built out of earthen walls.

There, the escorting adventurers were still defeating opponents who had made their way through the maze.

It wasn’t a particularly complex maze, but for the Kobolds, it was probably enough.

“Ah, yeah. Just make sure you don’t get in the way of the guys taking down the Kobolds.”

“Grr!”

Set let out a happy growl at Rei’s words and kicked off the ground, heading toward the maze.

Watching this, Rei wonders whether Set wants to defeat the Kobolds, or if he wants to try his hand at the maze… or perhaps his goal is to experience both at the same time.

That said, now that he’d already given his permission, whatever Rei said at this point, Set probably wouldn’t listen.

...Well, if Rei really told him to stop, he probably would, but then he’d surely look disappointed after getting his hopes up.

So, since he wasn’t exactly getting in the way of those fighting the Kobolds, she decided to just let him do as he pleased.

“Phew. …Watching Set, I felt a little deflated.”

Vihera, who had been watching the exchange between Rei and Set, murmured with a smile, her earlier slight sulk completely gone.

(Could this have been Set’s plan all along?)

Rei thought this upon seeing Vihera’s reaction, but when he looked at Set, who seemed to be wandering around the maze right away, he couldn’t possibly believe that Set had acted the way he did earlier with such a plan in mind.

That’s how innocently Set was enjoying the maze.

“Anyway, when you storm the place where those guys behind the Kobold incident are hiding, make sure to take me with you.”

“Well, you’d be a help as a combatant, so sure… but what do you plan to do?”

“It’s obvious, isn’t it? I’m going to ask them why on earth they’d go and summon a Kobold. Ideally, I’d like to find out how they did it. That way, I might be able to summon even more powerful monsters.”

Vihera murmured this with a beaming smile, her eyes full of anticipation.

Rei smiled slightly at that typically Vihera-like display.

That’s Vihera through and through.

...Above all, fighting a powerful, unknown monster meant there was a chance Rei could obtain an unknown magic stone, which was a welcome prospect.

As he thought this, Rei saw Byune using the White Cloud to take down the Kobold that had emerged from the maze with a single strike.

Rei was certain that, when it came to making money, Byune wouldn’t object to fighting unknown monsters either.

“I agree with Vihera’s reasoning, but what if the magic item the people behind this incident are using is the kind that only summons Kobolds?”

“Magic items… Come to think of it, that was Rei’s argument. Though, I don’t know if magic items are really the cause of this incident.”

While responding to Rei, Vihera’s expression made it clear that, in her opinion, it was definitely not a magic item as Rei suggested.

Seeing Vihera’s attitude, Rei started to say something, but…

“Ooooooooooooh!”

Suddenly, that shout echoed through the area.

"What is it?" Rei turned her gaze toward the source of the sound, but there, for some reason, stood what appeared to be an adventurer, joyfully holding something that looked like scales.

“...Scales?”

“Why scales?” Rei voiced his confusion.

The Giganto Turtle, as its name suggests—despite being rather unconventional in many ways—is a turtle monster.

Naturally, its skin has no scales of any kind.

Or rather, to be precise, one should say it lacks the distinct scales Rei was imagining.

But what the male adventurer currently in Rei’s line of sight is holding is undoubtedly exactly the kind of scales Rei imagines… the very kind found on snakes or crocodiles—the kind generally associated with the word “scales.”

And the size of those scales is about the same as a regular shield, or even larger.

“Scales, huh?”

In response to Rei’s mutter, Vihera replied briefly.

But even Vihera’s face shows a hint of surprise.

“Since the guy we were butchering had them, those scales must be from a Giganto Turtle, right? …I didn’t notice they had scales at all when we took them out.”

Rei has touched the Giganto Turtle’s body countless times.

After all, he stores it in the Misty Ring in the evening and takes it out in the morning, so that’s only natural.

Despite that, Rei had never once noticed that the Giganto Turtle’s body had scales like that.

“Yeah. I’ve looked at the Giganto Turtle quite a bit too, but I don’t think there were any scales anywhere. …If that’s the case, I wonder where those scales came from.”

If the dissection had progressed to the torso, then maybe—just maybe—I could have thought they came from inside its stomach, but so far, the dissection has only reached the legs.

In that case, it would naturally follow that the scales came from the legs...

“Hey, where did that scale come from? I’ve handled that Giganto Turtle countless times before, and I don’t think it had a scale like that.”

Rei asked a nearby guild staff member—not the one in charge of the Giganto Turtle’s dissection, but someone else.

The guild staff member, perhaps not expecting to be addressed by Rei, looked slightly surprised but spoke up.

“Ah, yes. From what I’ve heard, apparently there were scales under the skin.”

“...Under the skin? Well, if that’s the case, I wouldn’t have been able to tell just by looking at it... Is that really true?”

“The person who removed them definitely said so.”

Hearing about scales beneath the skin, Rei felt a genuine sense of puzzlement welling up inside him.

And rightly so. If he were to believe those words, it would mean the Giganto Turtle had scales on top of its flesh, which were then hidden by its skin… that’s what it would come down to.

If the scales were on top of the skin, that would be one thing, but the idea of scales beneath the skin was something Rei simply couldn’t wrap his head around.

“Wait a minute. Was only one scale found? From the looks of it, no one else seems to have any scales.”

“That’s right. For now, I think it’s just that one. …But there’s a possibility we might find more scales in the future.”

“...I see. Rei, what do you think?”

Vihera asked Rei, but Rei was also confused by the current situation, which could almost be described as baffling.

Then again, the Giganto Turtle itself could be described as a somewhat mysterious entity.

Considering that, the fact that it has scales under its skin isn’t really all that surprising.

(Scales under the skin... Was it using them as a hidden shield or something like that? No, considering the Giganto Turtle’s size, those scales are way too small.)

It looked like a shield that would be just the right size for a human to carry—or perhaps a bit too large for some—but it seemed far too unsuitable for the Giganto Turtle to use for such a purpose.

So, even if asked why the scales were under its skin, Rei had no answer.

“For now, if scales start appearing from other places, I suppose I could accept that’s just how it is. I just hope that’s not the only scale. …What do you think?”

“Even if you say that, I don’t know anything about the Giganto Turtle’s biology either. Honestly, I don’t think there’s anything we can do. I guess we’ll just have to keep dissecting it, won’t we?”

Rei asked Vihera, but that was the only response he got.

Unless they actually proceeded with the dissection, it remained unclear whether the current scale was truly a coincidence, or if there were similar scales on the other legs for some reason...

Still, one thing was clear: those scales seemed like they could be used as some kind of material.

In fact, with just a little processing, they looked like they could easily be used as a shield, and it wasn’t uncommon for scales to be used as ingredients for potions and other medicines.

Exactly what kind of material it could be used as would require thorough investigation, but given the rarity of the Giganto Turtle, Rei felt certain it would serve as some kind of material.

“For now, I’ll just have to hope those scales turn out to be good material.”

In the end, that’s the conclusion they reached.

To begin with, we still don’t know how many unknown materials might turn up when we dissect the Giganto Turtle.

Given that, making a fuss over just one unknown material—considering what lies ahead—means we don’t know how much of a fuss we’ll end up making.

Although the scales had surprised him more than he’d expected, the Giganto Turtle’s skin was still an unknown material, after all.
