The Rei were running through the city streets, but as expected, Byune began to fall behind.

Though Byune possessed high abilities that surpassed Rei and the others in terms of explosive power, the difference in physical stamina due to age was significant.

Byune fell behind little by little, but steadily.

Unable to bear watching this, Vihera swiftly grabbed Byune's body and placed her atop Set's back.

While Set found it difficult to fly through the air carrying anyone besides Rei, if it meant running with someone aboard or flying while holding them with his front legs, then even considerable weight posed no problem.

For Set, the petite Byune's weight was practically nothing.

Though, to be fair, carrying a single child on his back while flying was certainly within his capabilities.

In any case, with Byune, who had been lagging behind, now on Set's back, Rei and the others' speed increased dramatically compared to just seconds ago.

As they raced through the city streets, the surrounding scenery gradually became familiar to Rei.

"This... Could it be...?"

The place at the end of this road was somewhere ordinary people living normal lives would never need to go.

But Rei, being rather special in many ways, had visited it many times.

Yes... to the Noble District, just ahead.

Running at a speed that paid no heed to their surroundings, Rei and the others naturally stood out.

With Set present, it was obvious that it was Rei running.

If they were simply walking, the adventurers or private guards patrolling the noble district would likely just give a casual nod upon seeing Rei.

But if they were running without regard for their surroundings, that was a different matter entirely.

As those responsible for the Noble District's security, they couldn't just ignore such individuals... even if they were familiar faces.

"Hey! Wait, Rei! What the hell are you rushing for?! Stop! I said stop!"

"You idiot! Look at him! Rei isn't paying us any attention at all. If this keeps up, he'll take Set's full-on charge head-on!"

"Dodge! Dodgeee!"

Adventurers patrolling the Noble District at just the right moment shouted desperately.

Just how much power Rei possesses is something every adventurer in Gilm knows.

Those who didn't know were practically considered fakes.

That's precisely why they never imagined they could stop Rei... or rather, Set charging forward themselves.

Especially Set, leading the charge. While he was treated like a pet at the Gilm now, he was actually a rank A monster—no, a rare species. Considering that even high-rank adventurers might only encounter one such creature once in a lifetime, he was essentially equivalent to a rank S monster.

And so, where the three adventurers had dodged, Set, Rei, and Vihera charged forward.

Rei, still boiling over about the Sleipnir Boots, paid them no mind whatsoever.

"Hmph."

"Sorry, I'm in a bit of a hurry."

Unlike Rei, Byune, riding on Set's back as they passed through, and Vihera, running behind Rei, both called out briefly.

They certainly didn't expect this to settle things, but they likely figured it was better than doing nothing.

Indeed, the three adventurers must have sensed something was amiss from Vihera's attitude.

The adventurers didn't panic immediately; instead, they exchanged glances with their companions.

"Hey, what do we do?"

"What do you mean, what do we do? ...I don't want to pick a fight with Rei, but we can't just stand by and let someone like him, who's lost his mind and barged into the Noble District, get away with it. Let's at least report it to the butler."

The other two nodded in agreement with their companion, who suggested informing the butler of the noble house that employed them—their direct superior.

"Yeah, you're right. If Rei causes trouble, it could seriously damage the surrounding area. In that case, we should probably let them know beforehand."

The three nodded in agreement and headed toward the noble's residence that employed them.

If possible, they hoped to prevent any unnecessary commotion... or at the very least, ensure any trouble remained confined within the grounds of the noble's residence who had angered Rei.

These three were veterans who had participated in the war against the Bestir Empire several years prior.

Because of that, they had seen the terror of Rei's fire whirlwind firsthand and fully understood its power and scale.

If such an attack were to occur in this noble district...

It was probably fortunate that these three didn't know Rei's fire whirl had evolved further, to the point where he could now control it with a degree of freedom.

Had they known, it wasn't just the mansion of whoever angered Rei that might have been destroyed, but the entire Noble District... or worse, Gilm itself could have been obliterated.

Unaware of this, the three hurriedly left the scene.

...The fact that the direction Rei headed and the direction they were heading were different undoubtedly succeeded in easing their agitation, at least to some extent.

"Guruu!"

"Here!? ...Is it here?"

Completely unaware that the three adventurers they had just passed were trembling with fear, Rei gazed at the mansion where Set had stopped and fixed his gaze.

That mansion was a place Rei had seen before.

The last time he'd seen it, it had been decorated in a way that could only be described as tasteless, but now it appeared subdued.

But the mansion itself hadn't changed much from before, so there was no mistaking it.

Even so, he couldn't believe the person in that mansion was involved in this matter. The one who had previously tried to harm him was no longer in Gilm, and the successor should understand it was dangerous to mess with Rei carelessly.

...Yes, the mansion before me was the one where Galahad, who had taken over as head of the Azoth Trading Company, was supposed to reside.

It all began when Borntar, Galahad's older brother—a figure wielding significant influence over the weapons dealers in this guild—had been blinded by greed for the various magic items and the Set that Rei possessed. He had demanded, forcefully... no, arrogantly, that Rei hand over the magic items and the Set.

As a result, Bornter, who had been the head of the Azoth Trading Company until then, was not merely deposed... but incurred Rei's wrath, had magic used against him, and ended up trembling in fear night after night.

Bornter's half-brother eventually took over the Azoth Trading Company... and since then, things had proceeded without any particular issues arising with Rei.

"Galahad's orders? ...No, I can't imagine him doing something like that."

Within the Azoth Trading Company, Galahad undoubtedly knew Rei's true capabilities best.

He'd seen Rei fight up close countless times during that incident with the Azoth Trading Company.

He couldn't imagine Galahad doing something to provoke him now.

Well, if it was unavoidable due to business matters, he might have done something to anger Rei.

But this time, it seemed like he'd attacked Azimov and forcibly seized Sleipnir Boots.

It clearly looks like he's picking a fight with Rei.

Knowing Galahad's character, Rei simply couldn't believe it.

(No, Azoth Trading Company may be smaller than before, but it's still one of the major trading houses within Gilm. Meaning, with that many people involved, perhaps someone plotted this behind Galahad's back?)

Rei was watching the mansion ahead, but if he kept doing that, he'd inevitably be spotted by the guards at the gate.

Well, merely observing the mansion wasn't particularly unusual.

The mansion was considerably quieter now compared to before, but it was still larger than most noble residences and undeniably worth a glance.

Indeed, there had been many such visitors before... but Rei and his companions were quite conspicuous.

Rei, not displaying his Death Scythe or Twilight Spear, and the small Byune were one thing, but Vihera possessed strikingly beautiful features and wore a sheer garment that was practically transparent.

And with Set, who had grown to nearly three meters over the winter, there was no way they could go unnoticed.

And above all... one of those gatekeepers had seen Rei before.

Yes, the man who had turned Rei away at the gate when she first came to this mansion after being summoned by the Azoth Trading Company.

He ended up getting severely scolded by his superior for that blunder.

"Rei? Huh? What are you doing here? And you're staring straight at the mansion... Hey, hey, I don't want any trouble."

"Senior, what's wrong? That person is Crimson Rei, right?"

"...Yeah. He and Azoth Trading Company had a falling out before. ...We settled things in the end and were supposed to have repaired our relationship..."

The man's words trailed off.

Normally, he could have confidently declared it no problem, but the Rei present now seemed to radiate a somewhat ominous aura.

Moreover, Set—who usually seemed gentle enough to be called a pet—was now glaring sharply toward the mansion.

It was impossible to mistake this for a friendly attitude.

But the gatekeeper made no move to act on his own here.

He wished they would just leave as if nothing had happened.

That was the man's hope... but unfortunately, his wish wasn't granted. Rei and the others continued toward the mansion... more precisely, they approached the gate to enter the mansion.

"...May I help you?"

His tone was polite, completely different from the first time.

But considering Rei's relationship with Azott Trading Company, this was only natural.

Galahad, who currently served as head of the Azoth Trading Company, felt a strong sense of gratitude toward Rei.

...While he had mixed feelings about the circumstances that led to his brother's downfall, he was glad his brother had been stopped from betraying the city... and the country. Yet, thinking about his brother's end—though he wasn't dead—left him with some lingering thoughts.

In any case, for that reason, Galahad had formed a personal friendship with Rei.

Since a friend of the Azoth Trading Company's chairman had come calling, he couldn't just turn him away like a gatekeeper would have done before.

Especially since it wasn't just Rei, but Set was there too. Using force was absolutely out of the question.

"Is Galahad here? I'd like to speak with him."

The voice asking the gatekeeper sounded like it was forcing back its own emotions.

Precisely because he understood this, the new guard who had first met Rei couldn't afford to say anything unnecessary.

In fact, just standing beside Rei made him feel a chill run down his spine.

Just standing before Rei, he felt a sense of rapid exhaustion.

"...Yes. Lord Galahad is at the mansion today."

"I see. Then contact him immediately and request an audience. Also, starting now, make sure no one... not a single person, leaves this mansion."

"Huh?"

At first, the man didn't understand what Rei was saying.

Normally, making such a demand to someone you've asked for something—especially someone in the position of head of the Azoth Trading Company—was unthinkable.

But Rei, standing right before him, was demanding it with complete seriousness.

Realizing this, the gatekeeper couldn't help but conclude that something major and troublesome had definitely happened, as he had suspected from Rei's demeanor.

"Understood. I can't guarantee we can meet your request, Rei, but we'll make every concession possible. ...Hey, go get the higher-ups on board."

"Yes, sir!"

A strange sound escaped the newcomer's mouth, but the gatekeeper had no intention of reprimanding him.

On the contrary, he thought the rookie showed remarkable nerve just by not fainting.

Meeting Rei for the first time, only to encounter her like this... a Rei raging with fury, was nothing short of a disaster for that man.

That was precisely why he sent the new guy.

Honestly, he would have been the first to want to vanish from this place himself.

But as a senior, he had to look after his junior.

With that in mind, he instructed the man to inform the mansion... and the rookie staggered slightly before disappearing through the gate.

"So, I'd like to ask... Did something happen? I haven't heard anything about Azoth Trading Company and Rei's group having a dispute."

The gatekeeper asked timidly.

His words were meant to coax out any information he could, but Rei shook his head.

"Sorry, but this matter goes straight to Galahad. It's not something I can casually tell just anyone."

"I-I see."

With that, the gatekeeper could say nothing more... Until the newcomer who had gone to the mansion returned, he endured Rei's imposing presence as best he could, diverting his attention by focusing on Vihera's beauty and figure.
