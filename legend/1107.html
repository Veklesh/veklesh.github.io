Rei looked ahead to see several Sand Lizardman corpses scattered across the desert.

Most lay with their backs turned because they had fled, judging they had no chance against Rei, only to be attacked from behind.

And Rei had done this without moving much from his spot...

Of course, such a feat isn't impossible.

Archery and magic—those two types alone come to mind immediately.

But what Rei did was nothing like that. Using a spear, he wiped out the Sand Lizardmen without moving much from his spot.

The four adventurers who had been attacked by the Sand Lizardmen first had witnessed this scene, of course. Precisely because they had seen it, they couldn't believe it.

(What? What happened? How did he pull that off...? Unless I'm seeing things, that spear he threw vanished and came right back!)

Among the four adventurers, the man who had spoken with Rei muttered to himself.

But there was no way what they had just seen was a mistake.

The best proof was the several corpses of Sand Lizardmen lying scattered around, killed by the spear.

Before the stunned adventurers, Rei sheathed the Twilight Spear into the Misty Ring.

Then, turning around, he began walking toward the adventurers.

...Under normal circumstances, approaching a complete stranger unarmed in such a situation would be nothing short of reckless.

Even if you had saved them from a monster attack.

But... witnessing the one-sided slaughter of the Sand Lizardmen, they instinctively understood that if the person before them felt like it, they couldn't win against him even if he were barehanded.

"Well, are you all right?"

The hood prevented a clear view of the face, but the voice alone suggested youth.

(Young... no, childish?)

With that question lingering, the man who seemed to be the leader of the four spoke up.

"Ah, yeah. Sorry, thanks for the help. None of us are hurt. When those guys showed up, we figured we had no chance, so we ran."

"Seems you've got good situational awareness. ...But if you knew they were intelligent monsters, you should've realized they weren't just chasing us blindly."

Following Rei's gaze as he muttered and looked up at the sky, the group saw something flying overhead.

As the figure became clearly visible, heading straight toward them, one of the four cried out in terror.

"A, a Griffin!?"

The monster, flapping its wings as it approached them, was a very famous creature, though not quite as formidable as a dragon.

The Griffin, feared as the Grim Reaper of the skies, possessed power that made it one of the worst possible encounters, especially in the desert where cover was scarce.

Realizing they would be killed if they stayed, the leader of the group hurriedly tried to call out to Rei to flee...

"Set! Over here!"

Before he could even speak, Rei waved his hand toward Set, who was flying through the sky, calling out to him.

"Huh?"

The leader couldn't tell who had let out that startled-sounding word...

He wondered if it might have slipped out of his own mouth, but even if it had, it wouldn't have been surprising.

No, rather, seeing the griffin... and seeing Rei, who seemed to command that griffin, and still managing to keep his surprise to this level was nothing short of a feat.

While that thought lingered in the back of his mind, Set flapped his wings and descended toward the ground.

Rei, who greeted him, showed absolutely no sign of fear, as was only natural.

"Hey, wait. What's going on here? A high-rank monster like a griffin shows up, and just when I thought it was over..."

The spear-wielding woman muttered in confusion, and the two adventurers besides the leader nodded in agreement.

But contrary to the four adventurers' common sense... or imagination, Set was rubbing his head against Rei.

It looked undeniably like he was seeking affection.

After finishing petting Set's head, Rei turned back to the four adventurers and spoke.

"Sorry to interrupt your conversation."

Despite having Set... the griffin, right there, Rei spoke to them as if nothing was amiss. The four adventurers could only feel astonishment.

Still, the man who seemed to be the leader managed to speak, likely because he felt he had to.

"Um, well... First, let me thank you. You saved our lives. ...But, it feels weird to ask this after you saved us, but why did you help us? As you can see, we're not high-rank adventurers like you. The reward..."

The reason they judged Rei to be a high-ranking adventurer was undoubtedly because he had Set as his familiar.

Moreover, the Twilight Spear was clearly a magic item of exceptional quality at a glance.

Even if the Dragon Robe's concealment effect prevented it from being recognized as a magic item, those two alone were enough to identify Rei as a high-ranking adventurer.

Above all, the series of actions he displayed during the battle with the Sand Lizardman clearly showed skill far surpassing their own.

He could also imagine that his ability to move through the air during the battle with the Sand Lizardman was the effect of some kind of magic item.

What could such a high-ranking adventurer possibly demand of them... Considering that, it was no wonder they were at a loss for words.

The man speaking with Rei briefly shifted his gaze toward his companion... the spear-wielding woman.

Though her face was currently covered by something like a mask to avoid the sun or perhaps a turban, there was no doubt she was female.

His actions stemmed from the fear that he might be told to lend the woman for one night as payment for saving his life, and the others likely shared that concern. His body stiffened with tension.

But... watching the four adventurers, Rei shook his head.

"I don't intend to demand anything. But since I defeated this Sand Lizardman, I suppose I can claim ownership, right?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. Well, I guess so."

Huh? That's it? The man nodded, his disappointment evident.

If they'd been asked to hand over the corpse of a Sand Lizardman they'd defeated, he might have had reservations.

But all the Sand Lizardman corpses scattered across the desert right now were created by Rei.

He couldn't bring himself to say such a shameless thing—asking for the corpses of Sand Lizardmen they hadn't even defeated themselves, after having their lives saved.

In fact, he almost asked if that was all they wanted. In the end, he didn't voice it, afraid that saying it would lead to a "So, you mean..." kind of response.

Whether he noticed the man's attitude or not, Rei didn't seem particularly bothered and continued speaking.

"I see. I have a few other requests as well, but can we handle those after we've disposed of the Sand Lizardman's corpse?"

Hearing that there were other requests, the four adventurers tensed up again. But based on the conversation so far, they couldn't imagine Rei saying something strange... something that would put them at an overwhelming disadvantage. Feeling a little relieved, albeit slightly, they waited for Rei's words.

"First, help gather the Sand Lizardman corpses in one place."

"...Well, that's fine enough. But what are we supposed to do with them? You're not seriously planning to skin them all here and now, are you? There are so many, I think you should just give up on that."

If we can't carry them back, we should just strip the proof-of-kill parts and magic stones and leave the rest here.

The man's implied suggestion would normally have been correct.

But... the word "normal" was something that absolutely did not apply to Rei.

"Don't worry. If you're helping, I'd appreciate it if you did that."

"...You mentioned wanting me to do a few other things too. You're not seriously asking me to carry Sand Lizardmen, are you? Just so you know, I don't have the stamina to haul this much."

Whether human or monster, corpses and unconscious bodies felt heavier than their actual weight.

Moving a group of Sand Lizardmen under those circumstances was absolutely impossible.

If ordered to do so, he would refuse at all costs. Seeing the man convey this resolve, Rei shook his head before speaking.

"I carry an item box. Now that I mention it, you should understand, right?"

"...Huh?"

The man muttered briefly, looking completely baffled by what Rei was saying.

Of course he didn't. An Item Box was a magic item of such rare value.

Being told he casually possessed one and expecting immediate belief was unreasonable.

Still, the men knew Rei was anything but an ordinary adventurer.

Besides, lying here would be pointless—it would be exposed immediately.

Such a stunt would only damage their own credibility and serve no purpose.

Above all, having witnessed the spear vanish just moments ago, they couldn't possibly claim it was a lie.

(So, he really does have an item box?)

Not just the men, but all three companions reached that conclusion. As they stared at him, Rei, preferring proof over words, reached out to the corpse of the Sand Lizardman he had defeated first.

The next instant, the corpse vanished from before the adventurers... Once that happened, the rest was easy.

If it was clear he possessed an item box, then granting the request of their savior was no trouble at all.

The Sand Lizardmen weren't exactly a massive horde, so gathering their corpses and the tridents each carried to one spot didn't take long.

After that, all that remained was for Rei to store them in the Misty Ring, and that would be the end of it.

"Now, Set... there are also the corpses of the Sand Lizardmen this griffin defeated, so I'll go stow those away. Luckily, there weren't many, so I should be back soon. Until then, you should get some rest."

Saying that, he took several fruits from the Misty Ring and tossed them over.

The men who caught them stared in surprise at Rei's casual gesture of handing out fruit.

Though the fruit had been purchased from Gilm's stall, unlike Gilm, this was the desert, so naturally, its value was higher here.

Fruit trees did exist around the oasis, and while the fruit was somewhat expensive, it was sold in the market.

However, the fruit Rei tossed was unlike anything the men had ever seen before. If they tried to buy it in Gosh, they had no idea how much it would cost.

So, when such a fruit was tossed aside as if it were nothing, it was only natural for the men to be astonished.

And while the men were stunned, Rei mounted Set and rode away.

Watching the lone figure and beast soar into the sky, the first to speak was the woman holding the spear.

"This fruit... Are we really supposed to eat it?"

"Well... he gave it to us to eat, right?"

The thin man muttered, and the last one, the shortest of them, spoke up timidly.

"But this fruit... it looks like something I've never seen before, right? If we sold it, wouldn't we get a fortune?"

"Sell it...? It's a gift, isn't it? Selling it would be a bit tacky, wouldn't it?"

"Well, even prostitutes sell gifts they get from men, right? ...Like the ring I sent..."

As he spoke, he seemed to recall something he'd rather forget. The small man looked dejected... and in the next instant, he bit into the fruit he held.

It was an act born of irritation, but the gesture brought an unfamiliar happiness... no, a mouthful of bliss to the man's mouth.

A flood of juice spread across his palate.

So much that it made him wonder if that much juice had been packed inside all along.

Having been chased by the Sand Lizardman and feeling his life in danger, he was extremely thirsty. This felt like nothing short of a blessing from heaven.

The juice was sweet, but never overly so, spreading a refreshing sweetness throughout his mouth.

One bite... he had taken only one bite, yet the flavor was deeply satisfying.

And yet, a desire to eat more surged up from within him.

Before he knew it, the small man had eaten every last piece of fruit he'd been holding.

"Phew... That was delicious..."

The man murmured deeply, sounding nothing like the person who had just moments ago suggested selling the fruit for money.

Seeing him like that... naturally, the other three also showed interest in the fruit they held... and soon, all of them were simply moved by the taste of the fruit they were eating for the first time.

That feeling of wonder would last until Rei returned with Set.
