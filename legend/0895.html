After leaving the lord's manor, Rei headed toward the House of Baron Sisne with Set.

Upon explaining the need for escorts, Muet immediately agreed to hire them, likely recalling his conversation with Keep.

It was likely to protect his son and the maid. Though the Sysne family was strapped for cash, they still prepared a reasonable reward.

However, the escort was urgent. If they were to place a request with the guild now, post it, and so on... it simply wouldn't be ready in time.

Therefore, it was decided that Rei would find members directly, bypassing the guild.

Entrusted with selecting the guards, Rei headed straight for the guild.

Since it was a request bypassing the guild, selecting guards through the guild itself would likely be problematic in many ways.

But it was also true that the place where the most adventurers gathered at this time of year was the guild... or more accurately, the guild tavern.

Arriving at the guild, Rei parted ways with Set in front of the building as usual and entered.

Inside the guild spread out before him was, unsurprisingly, the sparsely populated guild hall and, in stark contrast, the crowded tavern.

Rei glanced toward the guild hall just in case, but the people there didn't seem particularly skilled.

Well, being adventurers in the Gilm, they were probably above average, but not quite at the skill level Rei desired.

He glanced toward the counter, but unusually, neither Lenora nor Kenny were there.

Having rarely spoken to the other receptionists, Rei decided asking about the adventurers' skills here would be pointless. He turned his gaze toward the tavern... and it stopped at one spot.

At the end of Rei's gaze sat a dwarf drinking cheerfully and a woman watching the dwarf with an exasperated look while reaching for the food on her plate.

Those two were familiar faces to Rei.

The two who had acted together with him during the previous dispute with the Azoth Trading Company.

The dwarf man was Brass, the human woman was Fron. The two members of the Rank C party, the Shattered Warriors.

"Lucky me. ...Or maybe not."

Blasso was an extreme lover of alcohol to begin with, and now it was winter.

Most adventurers took extended breaks until spring, so naturally, a boozer like Brass would head to the tavern.

Finding Brass at the tavern was only natural.

He headed straight for the tavern and arrived at the table where Brass and Fron were seated.

"Huh? Who the hell is barging in... Oh, it's Rei. Long time no see."

Fron announced in her usual tomboyish tone.

Seeing Fron like that, Brass probably noticed Rei too. His face flushed, he opened his mouth cheerfully.

"Huh? Rei? ...Oh, it really is you. It's been ages."

"Long time no see. Actually, I was hoping to ask you two for a favor..."

As she spoke, she glanced over at Brassso, who immediately shook his head.

Rei instantly understood Brassos's unspoken demand: don't interrupt his free-flowing drinking spree.

"If Brass isn't available... what about Fron?"

"Me? Well, I am free right now... but if it's work from Rei, it's definitely going to be tough, right?"

"I don't think it's that difficult. I need you to do some escort work for the next four days, starting today."

At Rei's words, Fron's expression betrayed a hint of surprise.

He probably never expected the word "bodyguard" to come out of Rei's mouth.

Based on past experience, he'd assumed any request coming from Rei would be something like a monster hunt.

Admittedly, it was true that Rei preferred extermination requests due to the need to gather magic stones, so Fron's assumption wasn't entirely wrong.

"Hmm. Escort, huh? Tell me more about it."

Rei's words seemed to pique Fron's interest, albeit slightly. He placed the dried meat he'd been holding back onto the plate on the table and asked.

Relieved that he'd piqued her interest, Rei began recounting yesterday's events.

The moment she heard about the duel challenged to forcibly obtain Ashié, veins bulged on Fron's forehead.

Fron was a woman too. She instantly understood what the worst kind of noble kept in reserve intended to do with Asie once he got his hands on her.

...Meanwhile, several adventurers at a nearby table who had been boisterous mere minutes ago now cowered as if by magic. Fron, however, either didn't notice or chose to ignore it, and spoke up.

"I see. So that's why Rei became the proxy for the duel. ...Still, half the count's estate? What is that man thinking? Is he an idiot? Even if it's a fight he's absolutely certain to win, there are no absolutes in battle. Well, if he's the kind of fool who can't get a woman without power, I suppose it's only natural."

"Probably. Anyway, that aside. They'll soon realize what kind of person I am. Once they do, they'll judge it nearly impossible to beat me in a duel. And then their next target will be..."

"Make them call off the duel itself, huh?"

"Exactly. Like having everyone descended from the Baron Sisne family die out."

Hearing that far, Fron must have understood who Rei was referring to as his escort target. A savage grin spread across his lips as he nodded in return.

"Sounds good. Arrogant nobles make me want to punch them. Too bad I can't punch the noble himself, but getting to beat up his minions is more than I could ask for. ...Hey, Brass. You heard that. You're coming too."

"Hold on. Why am I being dragged along? This was supposed to be your job alone, wasn't it!?"

Blaso shouted, holding his beer mug, clearly offended.

But his words sounded somehow weaker than usual—probably because he was intimidated by Fron's imposing presence.

After all, Fron loved physical activity.

For someone like Fron, winter—a season where training is about the only thing to do—was incredibly stressful.

Even drinking in the tavern like this, the urge to move his body was strong.

In that situation, what came out of Rei's mouth was the very definition of a corrupt noble, using his power to try and take a woman.

There was no way Fron wouldn't be fired up by this.

"For the details on the reward, go ask the Baron Sysne household. I imagine it'll be paid from the property they'll have to forfeit as compensation for the duel, aside from any upfront payment. But just the two of you won't be enough people. I'll stop by a few other places I know and try to gather some more. ...Do you know where Baron Sysne's house is?"

"Yeah, no problem. We've actually been in the noble district quite a bit, so we know our way around. We can figure it out. If need be, we can ask the adventurers on guard duty. And about the numbers, I know a few people I can reach out to."

"I see. Count me in then."

Saying that, Rei stood up and headed outside.

Watching Rei go, Fron forcibly pried Blasso off the table—who was stubbornly clinging to it, determined to drink here no matter what—and headed back to the inn to prepare their equipment.

After leaving the guild, Rei and Set headed toward their destination along the route Johanna had previously described.

About a thirty-minute walk from the guild.

Amid the falling snow, the house finally came into view. It was large enough that calling it a mansion would be more accurate.

A stable stood in the garden, and nearby, several carriages were covered with cloth, snow piled atop them.

"...Is it okay to leave them out like this?"

Rei muttered while looking at the carriages, but quickly understood.

Even if there was a shed, it clearly wasn't large enough to store all the carriages.

Even a house large enough to be called a mansion clearly didn't have a shed spacious enough to store all the carriages belonging to everyone living here.

Still, the fact that all the horses were inside the stable meant it must have been quite spacious.

"Grrr?"

At Rei's mutter, Set scanned the estate. Spotting the stables, he turned his round eyes toward Rei.

The horses that had pulled the carriages used by the former guerrilla squad and their associates had grown quite accustomed to Set during the journey.

At first, they'd been frightened even by Set's approach, but by the time they reached Gilm, they showed no sign of unease even walking right beside him.

For that reason, the horses were probably good friends to Set.

Understanding what Set expected, Rei nodded and gave Set's back a light pat.

Watching Set head toward the stables, Rei knocked lightly on the mansion door.

A few seconds passed. Then the sound of someone running approached, and the door opened.

"Yes, yes, who is it? ...Wait, you're Rei, right?"

The face peeking through the door was one Rei recognized.

Not the former guerrilla fighter, but a member of his family.

"I came by on some business... How many of the former guerrilla squad members are here?"

"Huh? Well, almost everyone's here. Just give me a moment. Sergioooo! Rei-san is here!"

A woman's voice echoed through the mansion.

As he listened to the voice, he caught sight of the woman.

She was in her late teens, probably older than Rei.

Her attitude toward Rei made it clear she had a very bright personality.

Many former members of the guerrilla forces feel a sense of awe toward Rei.

Compared to those people, her attitude could be described as extremely friendly.

...Though, considering all the things Rei has done, that reaction is probably more normal.

"Well, it's cold out here, so please come inside."

"Ah, that'd be great. ...But man, you've rented quite a spacious mansion."

After brushing off the snow, he entered the mansion and exchanged words with the woman.

As Rei had said, it was visibly a very large mansion.

It was spacious enough to fit right into the noble district.

However, it was definitely not a new building; even to Rei's eyes, it seemed quite old.

"That's right. Apparently it was Johanna's negotiation skills and money that made it happen."

"...Is Johanna not here? Even when I... well, when Set came, she didn't show herself either."

"Yes. Apparently she went out fully equipped to battle some natural enemy or something?"

At those words, the image that came to Rei's mind was, naturally, Mireine's.

The Mireine in Rei's mind was just as fully armed as Johanna.

(I hope it doesn't turn into a fight to the death...)

Mireine's longsword and Johanna's spear. As he imagined the moment those two clashed and prayed for their safety, Sergio appeared from deep within the mansion.

"Rei, what are you doing here? Not that I'm complaining—I'm thrilled you came to visit."

"I have a favor to ask. How many men can you muster right now?"

"Right now? Well, as you can see, it's snowing and most people have nothing to do, so I can gather a decent number."

"I see. Then, how about trying your hand at a little escort work?"

"Escort... you say? You mean for Rei-san?"

Sergio couldn't hide his disbelief.

He couldn't imagine Rei needing bodyguards, and if Rei truly did need protection, their skills wouldn't be enough to handle it.

Seeing his expression, Rei seemed to understand what he was thinking and shook his head.

"It's not for me, exactly. It's for a noble acquaintance of mine."

"An acquaintance of yours, Rei? Does that mean you alone aren't enough?"

"I don't know. I could probably handle it if I wanted to, but I want to focus on the duel."

"...A duel? Rei?"

Sergio, hearing the details for the first time, could only stare blankly at the turn of events.

"Challenging Rei to a duel... That takes nothing short of a hero."

"Well, a hero it may be, but more of the reckless kind."

Interrupting the conversation was another familiar face to Rei: Dietz, the former guerrilla fighter.

"Long time no see, Rei-san. Seems like you've gotten yourself tangled up in trouble pretty quickly after returning to Gilm, huh?"

"Well, I have my own reasons. But as I said, the reason is clear. Could you ask the others to escort me? Don't worry about the reward."

"...Well, once Rei entered the duel, the outcome was practically decided anyway. Alright. I can't promise everyone will join, but I'll talk to the members still here. Would only those who want to participate be enough?"

Even if it was a request from Rei, his benefactor, he couldn't force his comrades.

Rei nodded back at Sergio as he conveyed this.

He'd hired the former guerrilla fighters for this escort precisely because he trusted them, but they weren't absolutely essential.

Since Fron, who had been informed earlier, said he'd talk to his contacts, it seemed unlikely they'd be severely short-handed.

"Alright, I'll go talk to them right away. ...What about Deets?"

"Me? Of course I'm joining. They said there'd be proper pay, after all."

Deets declared with a smile.
