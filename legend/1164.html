The blade swung down, slicing through the goblin's torso, and its entrails spilled out from the wound.

"Guhhhhh!"

The goblin clutched its slashed belly and screamed.

Stomping on the goblin's neck, crushing its bones, the adventurer shouts.

"Dammit, how many of them are there?! This is never-ending!"

"I know! But we have no choice but to fight now! If we let these things get near Gilm, the damage will be worse!"

The man wielding the hammer shouted to the adventurer holding the longsword.

The hammer was covered in goblin blood, flesh, and bone fragments, telling the story of the battle's ferocity.

The numerous goblin corpses strewn across the ground proved the battle's ferocity.

Over ten adventurers were fighting this goblin horde, but the goblins numbered over a hundred... no, over a hundred and fifty.

The adventurers fought individually, but even so, fighting at full strength for such a long time took its toll on their stamina.

If these were slightly more powerful monsters, the adventurers would be fighting with tension.

But their opponents this time were goblins.

It was impossible not to underestimate them, leading to sloppy fighting.

Some adventurers realize things are heading badly, but the truth is there's little they can do about it.

But... the guild certainly hadn't overlooked this possibility.

From Gilm, new adventurers arrived by carriage.

"Relief! Take a break for a while!"

Stepping down from the carriage, a male adventurer drew his bow, firing arrow after arrow as he shouted.

"Thank you!"

As the man wielding a longsword shouted, the man with the hammer retreated to the rear alongside the other adventurers.

The other adventurers also retreat to the rear, but... the goblins, unwilling to let such men escape, attempt to pursue. However, as if to thwart their advance, several arrows are shot, piercing the goblins one after another.

In that instant when the goblins faltered, the other adventurers who had dismounted from the carriage surged forward all at once.

Similar scenes unfolded elsewhere.

Most of the adventurers present were veterans, ranked C or D.

That very fact meant coordinating their actions on the fly posed no problem.

Finally catching their breath, the adventurers watched the bloody battle raging a short distance away and sighed.

"At least we can take comfort in the fact that the goblins are all idiots,"

The man with the hammer nodded in agreement to the man with the longsword who said this.

"Yeah. The fact they can't ignore adventurers right in front of them makes things easier for us too."

Normally, it wouldn't be strange for a few goblins to surround the adventurers in the front line while the rest attacked the resting adventurers or archers in the rear.

No, that would be the natural course of action, but goblins aren't monsters capable of such complex thinking.

They only knew two things: kill the adventurer in front of them and eat them... or, if it was a female adventurer, capture her for breeding. That was all.

That's precisely why, when adventurers were right in front of them like this, they focused their attention solely on them.

Even so, even goblins like these... or rather, precisely because they were goblins like this, if attacked with arrows, they would seek out the attacker and try to retaliate.

"Still, what the hell caused this sudden change? This goblin population is no joke, you know."

"The guild said the swarm wasn't that big. Could another swarm be joining them?"

The hammer-wielding man looked over, and just as he said, another goblin horde arriving from elsewhere was merging with the one the adventurers were fighting.

No matter how many goblins they killed, an equal number... or even more... were being supplied, so the battle showed no sign of ending.

"Damn it. I really wanted us to take them down ourselves."

The longsword man's muttered words held not a trace of unease.

He had no intention of losing to mere goblins, as long as the fight didn't drag on too long.

And even if the enemy was goblins, defeating this many should have earned them a decent reward.

Honestly, though, he'd rather take down the goblin leader commanding this horde.

According to what the guild had told them, the group wasn't that large.

Well, that part itself probably wasn't wrong.

After all, when the guild first got word of the situation, the group wasn't large.

The problem was that even as we fought, more and more goblin groups kept gathering.

Anyway, if we could take down the goblin leader commanding those packs, the reward would definitely be higher.

(Capturing him alive would be best, I suppose... but I don't want to go through all that trouble, and it's too dangerous anyway.)

He gulped down water from the flask inside the carriage and reached for the bread.

The guild must have anticipated this would be a long haul, because the wagon was loaded with adequate supplies.

"By evening, more capable parties will arrive. ...Ideally, we'd like to finish this before then."

Fortunately, it was still daytime. The request to deal with the goblin horde approaching Gilm had been taken by adventurers who were either looking for work at the guild or enjoying their day off.

Precisely because of that, there was a possibility that adventurers who had accepted the request in the morning, completed it, and returned to the guild might rush here as reinforcements.

Of course, being overwhelmed by goblins due to insufficient forces would be the worst outcome. Conversely, having highly skilled adventurers arrive and wipe out the goblin horde was something they'd rather avoid, considering their own earnings.

To the long-sword man's muttered words... the man wielding the hammer fell silent for several seconds before speaking.

"I wouldn't get your hopes up. This job is over."

"...Huh?"

The man with the longsword, looking as if he wanted to say "What the hell are you talking about?", noticed that the man holding the hammer wasn't looking at him, nor at the adventurer fighting the goblins, but somewhere else entirely.

Following that gaze, the man saw something flying through the sky.

At first, he couldn't tell what it was.

It was flying quite high, appearing only as a tiny speck from the ground.

But that small dot was gradually growing larger, likely because it was descending toward the ground, allowing the man with the long sword to clearly understand what it was. ...Allowing him to realize.

"Damn, why the hell is that thing coming here? And heading right towards us?"

As the man with the long sword muttered, in the direction he was looking, multiple fireballs materialized around the entity that had circled behind the goblins... The next instant, those fireballs struck the goblins from behind.

The goblin leader, positioned behind the group, was targeted first, turning into a living torch before collapsing to the ground.

Of course, more than one fireball was released. One after another, they struck the goblins, creating explosions and flames around them.

The goblins fighting the adventurers were likely distracted by the explosions behind them. Many frantically turned to look behind them... only to be struck down by the adventurers' fierce attacks the very next moment, their guard down.

Furthermore, just as the fireballs dealt heavy damage to the rear goblins, including the goblin leader, the creature... Set, lowering its altitude as if for a follow-up strike, unleashed a fire breath.

As it spewed fire that burned the goblins, Set moved its head.

Naturally, the flames followed its movement, and the number of goblins consumed by the fire breath increased.

Unlike the fireball initially launched by Rei riding on Set's back, it lacked the power to reduce goblins to charcoal in an instant.

But fire is still fire. The longer it took them to die, the more their suffering stretched out.

"Whoa... Rei's here?"

Another adventurer nearby muttered.

Even at this point, it was obvious who was attacking the goblins from behind.

If it was an adventurer from Gilm... no, even if not from Gilm, any adventurer would recognize who it was.

If any adventurer here didn't know who Rei was, they'd be considered a fake adventurer, and rightly so.

For that very reason, naturally, every adventurer present—including the man with the longsword and the man with the hammer—knew who Rei was.

And the moment Rei arrived, they all understood that this goblin horde was already doomed.

"Honestly, I wish some other high-rank adventurer besides Rei had shown up."

"...The guild probably didn't want any adventurers getting hurt either."

Given Rei's expertise in large-scale annihilation tactics, he was the perfect choice for dealing with monsters like goblins, which relied purely on sheer numbers.

The man with the longsword understood that too, but it also meant their role was already over the moment Rei showed up.

Since it was goblin hunting, they couldn't expect much in rewards, but still, taking down this many should have earned them a decent sum.

And above all, what disappointed the man with the longsword most was...

"Ah... Now when I tell Mie-chan about my exploits, she won't be nearly as impressed."

He could no longer brag to his favorite courtesan.

Of course, it was true he'd killed many goblins, and one could say he'd saved Gilm from danger—his contribution was undeniable.

But even so, there was an unbridgeable gap between his achievements and the overwhelming feats brought by Rei and Set.

"Comparing him to Rei is just wrong. He's got a nickname, and he has a griffin as his familiar, you know? And not just any griffin. It's Set."

The man with the longsword thought the word "rare species" should be added there, but considering how Set was treated in Gilm, he understood that "because it's Set" was the more fitting explanation.

That's how high Set's standing was in Gilm.

...Though, it wasn't the status of a combat asset like a normal tamed monster, but rather that of a pet.

Of course, even as a pet, it remained a griffin.

Its abilities were exceptionally high, and anyone who dared to treat Set as merely a pet and tried to mess with it would likely get a painful lesson.

"Well, I know that. I know it... but it still stings that I can't tell Mie-chan any more heroic tales."

The hammer-wielding man sighed at the voice of the longsword-wielding man, who sounded genuinely disappointed.

Many adventurers have a penchant for frequenting brothels.

But even so, wasn't his partner taking it too far?

He'd warned him several times before, but it had no effect, so he'd already given up.

Even knowing that, he still had mixed feelings about it.

"Then why not say you weren't just watching Set defeat them, but that you actively helped him slay the goblins?"

"That... That's right! That way, even those interested in Set would be pleased! Alright, let's go!"

"Yeah, yeah."

The fatigue he'd felt just moments ago seemed to vanish, and the hammer-wielding man let out a wry smile.

Still, there was no doubt his partner was someone he could rely on. Taking hold of his weapon, the hammer, he asked if he'd help craft a tale to share as a bedtime story at the brothel.

Being a metal hammer rather than a wooden one, its weight was considerable.

Only an adventurer in Gilm could wield such a weapon with such strength.

After drinking water and eating some light fruit to restore their strength and motivation with its sweetness—the longsword man was probably already picturing tonight's brothel—the two headed straight for the goblin horde.

"Hey, wait!"

The man shooting arrows a short distance from the carriage tried to call out to them, but... the current situation, with Rei and Set launching a surprise attack from behind the enemy, was truly the perfect moment to hunt goblins.

Given the situation, it was true that the more combat power they had now to reduce the goblin numbers, the better.

With the goblin leader dead, the remaining goblins would likely flee.

Considering that, having these two fight now was indeed the best option.

(Besides, since they're volunteering to be part of our forces like this, we really have no right to complain, do we?)

Rather, it's a blessing, the man thought as he drew back the bowstring.

Arrows released from the man's hands pierced one goblin after another.

Normally, the goblins would have charged the ones who attacked them first, but now they were confused by the multiple explosions behind them... and the flames being generated. They had no time for that.

Thus, the goblin horde was thrown into chaos by the surprise attack from the specialized forces of Rei and Set. All but those who lucked out and escaped were slaughtered on the spot.
