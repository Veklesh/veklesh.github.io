Unaware that his very presence was having a profound impact on the resistance in the Kingdom of Reburina, Rei wandered through Mejougo as he had told Sura.

Though several other prostitutes besides Sura had approached him, Rei's current appearance, concealed by the Dragon Robe's camouflage effect, made him look like he was wearing an ordinary robe found anywhere.

Given that, it was only natural for the prostitutes to approach men who looked like they had more money.

Watching these prostitutes, Rei recalled the information he had heard from Kotonara and began to understand.

Every single one of them wore a collar.

Of course, these weren't the crude slave collars like the one Ajax had worn.

They resembled a kind of choker, more like jewelry in shape, and their patterns and decorations indicated which establishment the prostitute belonged to. Given the current situation, it was easy for Rei to deduce that their essence was that of slave collars.

(But the fact that none of them seem to be doing this reluctantly... Does that mean only those who accept it are allowed out in public? Or does that slave collar somehow control that too?)

Rei didn't know the reason behind it.

Still, as evident from this Mejougo, it was undeniably a complicated and troublesome situation.

(Well, I did get some useful information... but I'd really like to have a proper conversation with someone wearing a slave collar. Though, that's probably impossible.)

Even Rei could easily imagine that when the slave collars were applied, orders were given not to discuss the organization's information or the personal details of the women working as prostitutes.

Given that, it was impossible to extract information so easily.

At the very least, it was impossible without somehow dealing with the slave collar.

But if you asked Rei if he had a way to deal with the slave collar...

(My method is far too dangerous.)

It's not that Rei doesn't have a way.

But, as Rei murmured to himself, it was also true that the risk was far too great.

The method Rei possessed involved channeling his own magic into the slave collar.

Since the slave collar is a magic item, it requires magic power or magic stones to function.

Of course, once activated, it could be used semi-permanently by drawing on the user's faint magical power. But what would happen if Rei channeled her own magic into it?

For an ordinary mage, nothing particularly significant would likely occur.

But Rei possessed magical power beyond comparison to ordinary mages—truly extraordinary.

If such immense magic were intentionally channeled into it, it would essentially overheat, making it highly likely the slave collar would break.

But since there was no way to know what would happen if that occurred, it wasn't something he could do lightly.

If it only broke the slave collar, that wouldn't be a problem. But in the worst case, the slave collar could malfunction, potentially endangering the wearer's life.

And if it were to explode from being unable to withstand Rei's magic power, the sheer magnitude of that explosion would be devastating, precisely because Rei's magic power is so immense.

Even an adventurer, unless exceptionally fortunate, would likely not survive if such an explosion occurred near their neck.

If even adventurers faced such a situation, there was no way Rei could resort to such drastic measures against the women working as prostitutes.

(The best option would be to remove the slave collars through proper means... but that's practically impossible.)

Lost in such worries as he walked, Rei suddenly noticed someone watching him.

Well, if you meant literally looking at him, then yes, many people, prostitutes included, were looking his way. But the moment they judged him to be unprofitable, they'd immediately look away from Rei and call out to someone who seemed to have more money.

But the person currently watching Rei, Rei could tell, was looking with some definite purpose.

Who is it? Rei turned his face toward the source of the gaze.

At the end of that gaze stood a man in his late thirties or early forties.

His cheeks were flushed red—whether from drunkenness or the lingering heat of the tropical night, even after dark, was unclear.

Regardless, the man was staring intently at Rei.

Remembering the earlier incident with Sura, Rei hesitated for a moment. Still, he concluded that if the man was watching him, he must want something, and began walking toward him.

"Um, do you need something from me?"

"...Hmph. For someone who noticed my gaze, you're acting awfully meek, aren't you?"

"I'm sorry, I don't quite understand what you're saying..."

I don't understand," Rei said, wearing the mask of his alias, Ren. But the man seemed completely unaware of any mask, smiling with cheeks flushed red.

"Well, I never expected to run into someone as strong as you here. What the hell are you doing in a weak country like this?"

"I don't quite understand what you're saying, but aren't you mistaken? I only came here because I wanted to see this Mejougo once."

"...Hmph. Well, if you insist, fine. But I'll give you one piece of advice. You'd better not cause any trouble here. If you do, it won't just affect this country. It'll spread to the surrounding areas... and to the Kingdom of Mireana too."

The instant the man spoke, Rei's gaze sharpened for a split second.

It was the gaze of Rei, the Rank B adventurer known as Crimson—not the mask of Ren, the naive rich kid from somewhere.

Seeing this side of Rei, the middle-aged man smiled with satisfaction.

Rei felt a twinge of suspicion at the man's smile, wondering if he'd been baited, but he quickly slipped back into Ren's mask.

He understood from the man's attitude that the man before him had definitely approached him knowing he was Rei.

To remove the mask just because he'd been seen through would be to acknowledge his words to some degree.

That didn't sit well with Rei.

"What are you talking about? From what you're saying, Uncle, does that mean this country is dangerous?"

"Uncle!?"

The man seemed slightly shocked by the word "uncle" coming from Rei's mouth.

From Rei's perspective, a man in his late thirties or forties was indeed an "uncle"... a middle-aged man. It was an undeniable fact, but he clearly didn't want to admit it.

But he quickly recovered from the shock, and with a slightly forced smile, he addressed Rei.

"Now then. I don't know why a skilled worker like you came to Mejougo, but could you please leave it alone?"

"Huh? What exactly do you want me to leave alone?"

Even as he said this, Rei heightened his guard against the man standing before him.

Because he judged that "leaving alone"... meant leaving Mejougo exactly as it was.

Rei wasn't exactly a saint or a doormat who felt compelled to save everyone.

His primary reason for coming to the Kingdom of Reburina was to settle the score with the organization that had messed with Gilm.

While there were various reasons for his actions, it was undeniable that Rei would cause harm to Mejougo... to the organization likely controlling it, and to the upper echelons of this country likely collaborating with that organization.

"Hmph. Well, if that's how you feel, I won't say anything more. But remember this: if a major disturbance erupts in the Kingdom of Reburina, it could end up hurting a lot of people."

"...I don't understand what you're trying to say. But I'll keep that in mind."

For now, Rei only said that much.

The man seemed to understand this, as he said nothing more, turned on his heel, and entered a nearby tavern.

Watching his back until he was completely inside, Rei let out a sigh.

(Things inside Mejougo seem pretty complicated. No major disturbances have happened yet, but I doubt that'll last. ...Besides, even if we don't meddle, the Mireana Kingdom will get involved anyway. It's just a matter of sooner or later.)

Rei didn't know who the man from earlier was.

At first, he'd thought the man might be an agent of the organization, but after talking to him, that didn't seem to be the case.

But then again, if he was someone opposed to the organization, there was no reason he would have said those words to Rei.

As he pondered this, he sensed Yellow, inside the Dragon Robe, stir slightly.

It felt like a prompt: Don't just stand still here forever. You should do something.

Feeling as if urged on, Rei lightly stroked the part of the Dragon Robe where Yellow lay and began walking again.

It wasn't as if they'd overheard his earlier conversation with the man, but the number of prostitutes approaching Rei decreased even further than before.

(I really want to find a way to ask the prostitutes what they think about the current situation... but that's probably impossible. So, the best place to gather information is still the tavern, right? I'm getting a bit hungry too.)

In Mejougo, taverns were likely provided in reasonable numbers for resting and recovering stamina and vitality after visiting brothels.

Also, Kotonara had mentioned that these taverns weren't like chain stores where every place was the same... each one had its own distinct features.

Of course, being right next to such brothels, it's no surprise that many of the dishes served are designed to boost one's stamina.

"For now... let's try that tavern over there."

Rei's gaze fell on a tavern with a fair amount of customers coming and going.

He'd normally prefer a quieter place to relax, but gathering information meant he needed to go where the crowds were.

"Kyuu"

Yellow seemed to have no objection to Rei's words, letting out a small chirp.

...Though Rei didn't know how Yellow, inside the Dragon Robe, could judge the tavern's atmosphere, he figured it was Yellow, so it must be fine. With that thought alone, he headed for the tavern.

As he walked toward the tavern, he suddenly noticed a man approaching him.

(What a day for getting hassled. ...Well, I guess it can't be helped since this is the entertainment district.)

Rei thought this while watching the man approach, but quickly dismissed the notion.

The word "pestered" applied to Sura and the man earlier, but their actions had mainly been probing Rei's background or trying to figure out why he was here.

But the man approaching Rei now, a visibly overweight guy in his early twenties, wore a vulgar grin on his face.

This was entirely different from the meaning of "picking a fight" with the two men who had approached Rei earlier... This looked like the kind of "picking a fight" that thugs do.

From the man's perspective, seeing someone like Rei—not exactly a child, but certainly not looking like a grown adult—in a pleasure district like Mejougo probably rubbed him the wrong way.

Furthermore, the fact that Rei carried no weapon whatsoever was a reason for the man to look down on him.

Of course, bringing blatant weapons like the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear wasn't possible, but even so, many carried daggers or similar items for self-defense.

Rei also possessed the magic item known as Nebula's Eye, which could create arrowheads, but it was hidden and invisible beneath his Dragon Robe.

That's precisely why the man had completely underestimated Rei.

...If the man had the ability to discern his opponent's strength, it would have been a different story. Unfortunately, he wasn't particularly strong to begin with, and on top of that, he was currently drunk.

For a moment, Rei thought it would be a hassle, but then reconsidered—a drunk might be more likely to spill information.

Of course, being drunk also meant he might just spout nonsense.

Still, gathering as much information as possible was the priority right now... Hiding his face under the hood of his Dragon Robe, Rei pretended not to notice the man and simply kept walking.
