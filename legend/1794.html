A murmur.

No, the spectators watching the mock battle were in an uproar—far beyond mere murmurs.

For those belonging to the Kerebel Duke’s Knights, the Knight Commander Philma Tejiru was the very symbol of invincibility.

While it was undeniable that he excelled more at leading an army than in individual combat, it was only natural that he was seen as the strongest, given that no one had ever seen him lose a one-on-one battle.

That said, considering she was nearly on par with Elena before she inherited the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone, she stood no chance against the current Elena; in fact, she had lost to her several times in one-on-one mock battles.

But Philma is not the type to let anyone see him lose so easily.

That’s precisely why he’d never before held a mock battle in front of an audience like this.

For that very reason, the knights watching from the sidelines could only stand there in stunned silence.

They fully understood that Rei was strong from the mock battles against the first two opponents.

But even so, they had believed that Philma could defeat Rei.

Watching the knights murmur in shock, Rei looked at Philma approaching him.

(Of course, this can’t possibly be his full strength. There’s no doubt he has several hidden tricks up his sleeve.)

That conviction stemmed from the fact that Rei himself hadn’t used his own hidden tricks either.

After all, this was merely a mock battle, and neither side had been fighting at full strength.

Well, in the sense of training, they were giving it their all, so it wasn’t as if they were holding back.

“Well… with that said, I’d like to ask you where exactly we fought before?”

Rei withdrew the Death Scythe he had been pointing at Philma and asked.

At Rei’s words, Philma realized, “Come to think of it…”

While engaged in the mock battle, she had been so completely focused on that alone that she had completely forgotten about her encounter with Rei during the Azott Trading Company incident.

“I’m sorry, but I’ll have to discuss that matter with Kerebel Duke first… Is that all right with you?”

After all, since the Azott Trading Company incident had been an order from his superior—Duke Kerebel—Philma couldn’t speak on the matter based solely on his own judgment.

Moreover, since he’d gone to such lengths to confirm what kind of person Rei—who’d made contact with Elena—really was, he’d probably prefer not to discuss it in front of his subordinates if possible.

Of course, since there are many fans of Elena among the knights, it’s quite possible that most of them would actually find it understandable.

Watching Philma’s reaction, Rei thought for a moment before nodding in agreement.

It’s not that he didn’t want to ask various questions right then and there, but when he thought about it, it wasn’t exactly information he absolutely had to know.

It was merely a curiosity—the sort of thing he’d like to know if possible.

Also, the fact that the mock battle with Philma had been sufficiently stimulating was likely one of the reasons Rei changed his mind.

(If possible, I’d like to ask for another mock battle… but considering his position as Knight Commander, he’s not likely to agree so easily. In that case, it’s probably better to build up some goodwill here while I can.)

Back in Gilm, there were plenty of sparring partners like Elena and Vihera who were more than capable of holding their own against Rei.

However, in the Duchy of Kerebel, only Rei, Elena, and Ara had come, and given Elena’s status within the Noble Faction, he couldn’t just casually ask anyone to spar with him.

Elena herself wouldn’t be the type to worry about such social niceties… In fact, to be honest, she’d probably welcome a mock battle with Rei.

However, the circumstances around her would not allow it.

As a result, Rei had to find someone other than Elena to spar with.

“I see. Then let me know later.”

“Mm-hmm. So… what are you going to do? We’re still planning to keep doing mock battles, but are you going to keep doing them too, Rei?”

At first, Philma had addressed him as “Rei,” but before she knew it, she was simply calling him “Ray.”

Rei couldn’t figure out why that had happened, but he preferred it this way to being addressed so formally.

(Logically speaking, is it because she lost to me in the sparring match?)

Even though they were both hiding several trump cards during the mock battle, acknowledging the loss had likely broken down a barrier between them... or so it seemed.

That was Rei’s conclusion, though he couldn’t help but wonder: wouldn’t someone normally treat him with more respect precisely because they’d beaten him?

“Nah, I’ll pass. I’ve already gotten a good workout today. Besides… you know what I mean, right?”

Rei didn’t finish his sentence, but Philma seemed to understand what he meant. She looked away, as if trying to brush it off.

In a way, Philma was the finest of delicacies.

If someone asked me to eat some cheap instant food right after eating that, I wouldn’t exactly be in the mood to say, “Sure, I understand.”

(Well, I guess instant meals have gotten pretty tasty lately, though.)

The instant meals I ate while in Japan… especially the frozen fried rice varieties… some of them were even better than the fried rice my mom makes… and in some cases, better than the fried rice at mediocre restaurants.

The okonomiyaki and takoyaki were also packed with ingredients, and they were definitely tastier than the okonomiyaki made with just cabbage sold at night market stalls, or takoyaki without octopus.

(Oh, takoyaki. Georgima can make seafood okonomiyaki, so maybe takoyaki too... Wait, how do you even make takoyaki? Can I use the same batter as for okonomiyaki? Actually, to make takoyaki, I’d need a special takoyaki griddle... and in Anesis, how would I even get the octopus for takoyaki?)

Rei’s mind was starting to be completely consumed by thoughts of takoyaki, but since it seemed like too much of a hassle, he gave up right away.

If he asked Georgima, he had a feeling she might be able to figure something out since it involved cooking, but while Rei did want to eat takoyaki, his desire to simply eat ramen was stronger.

If it didn’t involve much effort, that would be one thing, but explaining what takoyaki is and actually having someone make it would definitely be a hassle.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

“Ah, no. It’s nothing. I just realized I’m a little hungry.”

Philma accepted Rei’s explanation, but the maid who had been watching the mock battle looked slightly surprised.

That’s because the maid who had prepared it knew better than anyone just how much food Rei had eaten for breakfast that day.

He’d eaten enough breakfast for five people all by himself, so just playing around with Set and doing a little mock battle…

(Hmm? Come to think of it, maybe it’s not that surprising after all.)

He’d run around the stables quite a bit playing with Set and Yellow, and while the first two mock battles were one thing, the one against Philma was so intense that, from the maid’s perspective, she couldn’t even tell what was going on.

...Or rather, as a maid serving the Duke of Kerebel, I simply couldn’t believe that Philma had actually lost in a mock battle.

In reality, neither Rei nor Philma had used any secret techniques in the fight, but there’s no way a mere maid could understand something that even some of the knights couldn’t grasp.

That said, the reason I didn’t end up fearing Rei was probably because I understood his relationship with Elena.

“Feeling a little peckish? Then you should head to the kitchen. The cook should be able to whip up something for you to snack on.”

“Uh… the kitchen? I don’t think I’m quite ready for that yet…”

Rei hesitated when Philma suggested he go to the kitchen to eat something.

Since she’d taught him about ramen, he was probably working desperately on a prototype right about now.

Besides, what Rei had taught her wasn’t a proper recipe, but only a vague description of what the dish was supposed to be.

Because of that, there were so many things to experiment with—the soup, the noodles, the toppings—and given how strong Georgima’s motivation for cooking had been, Ray couldn’t imagine her agreeing to just whip up something on the spot.

But since Philma knew nothing about ramen, she tilted her head in puzzlement…

“Georgima might be busy with other things, but how about asking one of the other cooks to make it?”

For now, she suggests that.

Philma fully understood that Georgima was a bit of a difficult person to deal with.

That’s exactly why I thought, “Why not just have another chef make it?” But Rei looked convinced by those words.

Georgima had made such a strong impression that whenever someone mentioned a chef from the Duke of Kerebel’s household, Georgima was the only one who came to mind. But in reality, there were others who could be called Georgima’s apprentices.

Of course, there were other chefs present when the ramen was explained… but that just shows how strong Georgima’s impression was.

“Then, perhaps another chef…”

“Lady Elena!?”

I’ll ask one of the other chefs to whip up something light.

Just as Rei was about to say that, one of the knights shouted, seemingly without thinking, as if to cut him off.

Interrupting a guest like Rei was clearly rude.

However, the mere appearance of Elena had come as such a shock to the knights.

Rei wasn’t one to be overly concerned with etiquette, so he didn’t seem particularly bothered by the outburst and turned his gaze toward the entrance to the training grounds.

Sure enough, Elena was there, along with several maids, Ara, and what appeared to be the noblewoman’s daughter whom Rei had encountered before heading to the stables.

“Hmm, I came to check on things… but it seems the main attraction is already over.”

From the atmosphere lingering in the training ground and the expressions on Rei and Philma’s faces, Elena surmised that the mock battle she had been looking forward to must have already ended.

It was true that the mock battle had indeed already ended, but the knights and maids were looking at Elena as if to ask how she had known.

“Oh, really? That’s a shame. I was hoping to see the legendary Crimson Power with my own eyes, if possible.”

Teres, who had come to the training ground with Elena, wore a disappointed expression.

She knew, of course, that Rei was an adventurer with a nickname, but to Teres’s eyes, Rei didn’t seem nearly that strong.

If Teres possessed a certain level of strength herself, she would have been able to gauge just how powerful Rei was from his movements.

But while Teres is intelligent, his own strength isn’t quite up to that level.

As a result, even after watching Rei, she couldn’t clearly grasp his true strength.

……Then again, if strength can’t be judged by appearance alone, Elena herself looks like nothing more than a duke’s daughter.

However, it had been quite a while since Teres had last met Elena, and he had seen just how strong she was during training and other occasions.

That’s precisely why, to be honest, she probably wanted to see Rei’s strength with her own eyes.

But the fact that he didn’t ask her to stage a mock battle right in front of him is what makes Teres so admirable.

If this were any other noble—especially one who might be a bit delusional—there would likely be those who would order them to hold a mock battle right in front of them.

And in the end, they’d end up upsetting Rei… or worse, even upsetting Elena.

To avoid that, there’s no doubt that adopting an attitude like Teres’s is the best choice.

“Too bad, but we’ll have to save the mock battle for another time. I had a fulfilling time during the mock battle with Philma. Besides, I’m getting a little hungry.”

“Hmm, then how about joining us for some tea?”

“...Lady Elena, I believe you were drinking tea just a moment ago.”

Ara remarked with a slightly exasperated look, but Elena didn’t seem particularly bothered.

“That shouldn’t be a problem, right? After all, we were just talking.”

“I suppose you’re right.”

“Oh, I don’t mind at all. Just being able to talk with Rei makes me feel like I’ve gained something. Besides, since we’ve gone out in this cold, I’d like some warm tea too.”

With a smile on her face, Teres said this, and Ara didn’t object any further. They decided to return to the mansion with Rei and enjoy some tea.
