"Guh!"

With a single swing of Rei’s Death Scythe—though it was the hilt, not the blade—several soldiers were blown away all at once.

Since those soldiers served the nobility, they likely possessed considerable strength... but even so, from Rei’s perspective, they didn’t seem all that different from the others he’d fought so far.

(In that sense, the knights I fought second were the strongest so far.)

In fact, the battle with the knights took several minutes to resolve, and they were the strongest opponents he’d faced so far.

At first, Rei had assumed they would hold mock battles in order of weakest to strongest, but that wasn’t actually the case.

After all, no one stronger than those knights has appeared so far.

(Though there are individuals who are stronger than the knights.)

Even among the adventurers hired this time, there were a few who could truly be called skilled, and their strength was undoubtedly superior to that of the knights… the knights serving the Countess of Lelemera.

However, even from Rei’s perspective, their strength was only at a level that posed no particular problem, and they certainly hadn’t reached the point where quality outweighed quantity.

Furthermore, unlike the knights, they did not seem to be with the companions they usually formed a party with, so their teamwork could by no means be called good.

If those adventurers were to fight the knights, they might win in a one-on-one battle, but in a group fight, the knights would win decisively.

That was the extent of their strength.

“This is incredible! A literal one-hit KO! But where on Rei’s tiny body does she find the strength to blow five men away in a single strike!?”

The crowd cheered at the male announcer’s provocative words.

That said, mixed in with those cheers were the cries of those who had lost their bets, so not everyone was necessarily rooting for Rei.

At first, Rei had thought that since this was a mock battle, the opponents might be given a chance to shine. However, after repeating similar fights over and over, that feeling had faded considerably.

As Rei stood before them, wielding the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, his next sparring partner appeared... And with the male announcer’s voice echoing, Rei stepped forward to meet the men charging toward him.

As Rei defeated his mock battle opponents one after another—or rather, as he ran roughshod over them—the nobles watching reacted in various ways.

Some were convinced that the rumors they’d heard were true; others felt displeased that he had defeated the soldiers and knights who served them; some simply cheered for Rei’s strength; and still others wondered why someone as small as Rei possessed such immense power.

Amidst all this, the ones watching Rei’s overwhelming strength with satisfaction were likely Duke Kerebel, Liberte, and his daughter, Elena.

However, Arcadia, Liberte’s wife and Elena’s mother, was not present.

This is because, with the year coming to a close, she is hosting a tea party for the noble wives and daughters who are arriving one after another to attend the New Year’s party.

Of course, there are also those who have abandoned their tea parties to come here, drawn by the chance to witness the strength of Rei, who goes by the nickname “Crimson.”

“No way… What was that just now? I mean, not just that, but everything up to now… Are adventurers really this strong?”

“Oh, well, he’s the one proving that not all adventurers are strong. Among the opponents he’s defeated in mock battles so far, there were quite a few adventurers, weren’t there? Just because they’re adventurers doesn’t mean their strength is uniform.”

“Damn it, this is pathetic. Starting tomorrow, I’ll have to put them through even tougher training.”

“I agree. What do you think? If you’re up for it, maybe we could hold a joint training session sometime. It would be a great motivator for our soldiers and knights, too.”

“Rei of Crimson, is it? …It’s fine that Duke Kerebel maintains friendly relations with someone of such power… but between that and the matter of the Princess General, isn’t too much power being concentrated in one place? If this continues, the Royal Faction might start to view us with strong suspicion.”

“I understand that concern. But aren’t there many individuals with nicknames within the Royal Faction, starting with Rank S adventurers? If so, I don’t think there’s any need to worry that much.”

“Speaking of which, did you hear? I heard that Count Moltren of the Royal Faction has succeeded in recruiting an adventurer with a moniker.”

“Really? Is that true?”

“Yes, it came up in our intelligence network. …Given that the Noble Faction is strengthening its ties with the Neutral Faction, the Royal Faction must be feeling a sense of urgency.”

While everyone else was talking with those nearby, only Gaiska was glaring at Rei with a look of hatred in his eyes.

Since this mock battle was held in part to actually verify just how strong Rei was, the current situation posed no problem at all. No, for Gaiska, it was actually the best possible outcome.

Yet the fact that he still feels displeased is likely because he simply cannot stand Rei.

He knows this, but seeing Rei being praised by the other nobles is still no source of joy.

(Damn it, was it the Black Wolf? That guy really did come here to assess Rei’s true strength, didn’t he!)

Gaiska has already paid a sum that is by no means small—in fact, if his family found out, it would undoubtedly cause a problem.

Of course, he hasn’t paid the full amount, but even so, it’s a sum he’s scraped together by stretching himself beyond his limits.

The mansion the Marquis of Seisole owns in Anesis—including the very mansion Gaiska currently lives in—was acquired by borrowing money using it as collateral through a broker.

He’s also prepared the payment by using various other assets as collateral or selling them off.

If the assassination of Rei were to succeed, she would naturally have to pay the remaining amount as well… but Gaiska was somewhat optimistic about that.

He reasoned that if he sold the magic items Rei possessed, he would easily recoup the money he had paid to the Black Wolf.

(If I think of it that way, then the fact that he’s flaunting Rei’s magic items like this is meant to bring me a huge profit. Hmph, well then, I suppose I’ll just have to make him work hard for my benefit.)

Gaiska’s irritated gaze softened slightly.

As he watched Rei fight, Gaiska couldn’t help but feel a sense of disappointment seeing his men being defeated one after another.

(No, rather, even if the Black Wolf were to watch this battle, would he truly be able to grasp Rei’s true strength?)

From the sight of Rei fighting, Gaiska could clearly see that he wasn’t giving it his all.

On the contrary, he even suspected that Ray was blatantly holding back.

Given that, even if the Black Wolf were to target Rei, wouldn’t the assassination fail once he realized Rei’s true strength was only this much?

He even found himself harboring such thoughts.

“Gaiska, what’s wrong? You don’t seem very focused.”

Perhaps noticing something off about Gaiska, a man sitting a short distance away asked him that.

Although the words came from a man of a lower rank—a baron—Gaiska replied while hiding the scorn he felt inside.

“Ah, just a little. The Crimson I know was supposed to be much stronger. Considering that, the fight hasn’t been all that spectacular, so I was just wondering about that.”

“Ah… Well, that’s probably because her opponents are too weak. If she’s really going to show off Rei’s true strength, she’d need a worthy opponent… Oh, look at that. Speak of the devil. Here comes the Ice Blade.”

At those words, Gaiska turned her gaze back to the stage.

Standing there was a single man.

He looked to be in his late twenties or early thirties.

He wore leather armor made from monster hide, presumably for ease of movement, and held a rare longsword with a light blue blade.

But Gaiska knew that this longsword with the aqua-blue blade was a Magic Sword.

It was, as its nickname suggested, a Magic Sword capable of manipulating ice.

The nickname “Ice Blade” naturally comes from the Magic Sword he holds in his hand.

“This looks like it’s going to be interesting.”

Though Gaiska said this, he wasn’t looking forward to the battle in the literal sense of the words.

Up until now, Rei had clearly been holding back in battle, but Gaiska’s remark stemmed from his judgment that, when facing someone with a moniker, Rei would have no room to spare such obvious restraint.

(I doubt that Black Wolf would miss this fight, would he? ...I’ve heard he’s a skilled fighter, so that wouldn’t make sense.)

Watching Hyoujin and Rei conversing on the stage ahead of him, Gaiska focused his attention so as not to miss the moment when Rei finally showed his true strength.

“I never thought I’d get to fight that Crimson in a place like this. Well, it’s just a mock battle… but if we fought for real, we might cause damage not just to this stage, but to Anesis itself. In that sense, I’m glad it’s just a mock battle.”

“True. I never thought I’d end up fighting Hyouha’s Exolis either.”

Rei, of course, knew who the Exolys who had spoken to him was.

When he heard that many adventurers had been hired for this mock battle, he naturally concluded that it wouldn’t be strange for some of them to be nicknamed fighters.

Since Rei himself was a “Nicknamed One,” it only made sense to choose another “Nicknamed One” as an opponent to ensure a fair match against him.

Of course, hiring an “Alias-Bearer” who had just started their long winter break would naturally require a substantial reward.

At the very least, a reward that was incomparable to what Rei had received for fighting against the adventurers he’d faced in mock battles up until now.

(In that sense, hiring this Ice Blade… was a stroke of genius.)

According to the information Rei had gathered, this person named Ice Blade had a rather eccentric personality.

In fact, he’s the type who might accept a job even for a low fee if it interests him, but conversely, he’ll never accept a job no matter how high the reward if he doesn’t like it.

Facing such an opponent, Rei gripped his Death Scythe and Twilight Spear just as he always did.

“Up-and-coming… well, it’s been quite some time since Rei earned his nickname, so I don’t think I can really call him that anymore, but in any case, I’m more than happy to be fighting a new nickname-holder.”

“It’s not a bad feeling for me to fight someone with a moniker, either.”

“Is that so? Well, I’ll do my best not to disappoint you.”

Saying that, Exolis swings his Magic Sword lightly.

Following the path of the Magic Sword, an ice spear—no, perhaps it would be more accurate to call it an icicle—pierces the stage.

(So that’s the Magic Sword’s ability. …Though, it wouldn’t be strange if it had other abilities as well. Still, if he were to unleash the Magic Sword’s full potential, I doubt this would end as just a mock battle. …Well, whatever.)

Normally, it would be strange to so readily accept the possibility that he might die.

But Rei hadn’t expected to lose even in his mock battle with Exolis, which is precisely why he decided it would be fine to use the Magic Sword’s abilities freely.

Realizing their conversation had ended, the male emcee opened his mouth, stoking the audience’s anticipation.

“Now then, this mock battle will be a clash between two ‘Nickname Holders.’ It’s sure to be a different kind of battle from the ones we’ve seen so far. Everyone, don’t miss this battle between two ‘Nickname Holders.’ …Alright, let the mock battle begin!”

As the emcee’s words echoed, Rei and Exolis both lunged forward at once.

As they closed the distance between them, Rei was the first to launch an attack.

Unlike Exolis, who wields a Magic Sword, Rei’s weapons are a scythe and a spear—both long-handled weapons.

Given that, it was only natural for Rei to strike first.

The first strike was delivered by the Death Scythe.

Exsolis dodged the strike—which seemed as though it would reap his life—by lowering his stance, attempting to close the distance with Rei… but in the next instant, he leaped swiftly to the side.

The Twilight Spear, held in Rei’s left hand, pierced the spot where Exolis’s body had been just a moment before.

It was a strike that could easily have left an afterimage, but for Exolis, it was an attack he could handle with ease.

However... having put some distance between himself and Rei, Exolis frowned slightly as he held the Magic Sword.

“This is troublesome. Not only does he wield weapons with a long reach, but he’s using two of them at the same time.”

“Hearing you say that is a compliment to me. After all, I’ve hardly ever seen anyone else use the Two-Spear Style, and it’s practically my own unique style.”

Saying that, Rei flashed a smug smile.
