The stage where Rei was to hold a mock battle with the adventurers was quickly completed.

Rather than building a proper arena-like structure, it was just something simple enough to hold a mock battle for that one day, so it was quite crude in construction.

However, almost no one complained about it.

A few people did harbor concerns that it might affect the betting, and a handful of them were somewhat bothered by it… but that was the extent of it.

Most people were enjoying this break from the ordinary, buying food from the food stalls that were already set up—or rather, that would be set up soon.

Amidst all this, Rei was inside the small hut prepared as a waiting room.

This wasn’t just a makeshift shack, but a proper room, and a magical heating item had been set up inside, so it wasn’t cold.

That said, since Rei was already wearing the Dragon Robe, he didn’t really feel cold at all given the current outdoor temperature.

Besides, if she felt cold...

“Growl…”

If Rei touched Set, who was standing in front of her, it was warm enough.

That said, Set is looking outside the hut, clearly wanting to go out.

Because there are so many food stalls lined up, the aroma wafting over here is really whetting his appetite.

Especially for Set, whose senses are so keen, it’s impossible to completely block out the smells wafting in from outside, even while inside the hut.

“Hold on. If we go outside right now, people will definitely start gathering around us. To avoid that, we’ll check out the food stalls after the mock battle is over. …Well, I’m not saying I don’t understand how you feel, Set.”

While patting Set to calm him down, I take some Gamelion jerky out of my Misty Ring and give it to him.

Set probably wouldn’t have been satisfied with ordinary dried meat, but Gamelion’s dried meat is just delicious.

Set, who had been pleading with his eyes to go outside for something to eat because he was hungry, seemed satisfied with the taste of the jerky and didn’t make any further gestures suggesting he wanted to go outside.

(It’s fine that I brought him here, but in the end, I’m the only one doing the mock battles.)

Watching Set eat the jerky, Rei thought to himself.

Originally, Rei’s nickname, “Crimson,” wasn’t something he earned on his own; he gained it by acting alongside Set.

Given that, if they truly wanted to see “Crimson’s” true strength, it wouldn’t have been strange for Set to participate in the mock battle as well… but the adventurers scheduled to participate, as well as the knights and soldiers serving the nobles, had put a stop to it.

They claimed they couldn’t fight a Rank A monster like a griffin.

In reality, Set is recognized as a rare species and is considered a Rank S-equivalent monster, but whether it was good luck or bad luck that they didn’t know that is another matter.

Even if they had known, it wouldn’t have changed the fact that people still didn’t want to fight Set.

No, in fact, knowing he was Rank S-equivalent would likely have only made those objections even louder.

And yet, the reason Set is here is... because of a request from Liberte.

To show Set to everyone in town, so they wouldn’t be startled if he walked through the streets.

When put that way, Rei had no choice but to accept.

In fact, every time Rei and Set went out in Anesis, having knights from the Duke of Kerebel’s Order accompany them—as Leodanis did—was a huge hassle, and it meant the knights had to interrupt their training.

That is precisely why it was necessary to showcase Set’s presence here, where so many people gather, and demonstrate that he is not a dangerous threat.

Especially now, with the atmosphere resembling a festival, many people are more open-minded in various ways.

He likely judged that if he were to make Set’s presence known in such a situation, the residents of Anesis would be more receptive than they would be during ordinary times.

Since Rei understood Liberte’s reasoning, he had no choice but to agree.

After all, if this matter is resolved safely, it would mean he could walk around freely with Set.

“So, just bear with me a little longer today. I did tell Miranda to buy you something if she sees any delicious food at the food stalls.”

“Grr…”

At Rei’s words, Set, having finished the Gamelion jerky, growled reluctantly.

Set was happy to be able to eat food from the food stalls, but he probably understood that such food tastes best when eaten right there and then, rather than later.

Rei wasn’t entirely unfamiliar with that feeling.

He usually eats food from street stalls, but at festivals like this, it’s fun to savor what you might call the atmosphere of the moment.

That was exactly the case with the summer festivals held back in Japan—evenments where dishes like okonomiyaki, packed with nothing but cabbage and barely a trace of other ingredients, or takoyaki that sometimes didn’t even contain octopus, still tasted delicious enough as long as you were soaking up the festival atmosphere.

But if I took that kind of food home and reheated it in the microwave the next day, I couldn’t for the life of me think it tasted good.

That’s how important the festival atmosphere is, so it’s not surprising that Set would want to eat the food in that kind of setting if possible.

“Um… well, you know. After the mock battle is over, if we still have some time, maybe we should check out the food stalls?”

“Growl!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a happy yelp.

The guard standing outside the hut—no, the waiting room—was slightly taken aback by the exchange.

He had been standing in front of the waiting room to make sure none of the nobles did anything strange, so it was only natural that he was taken aback when he suddenly heard Set’s cry from behind him.

Still, the fact that he didn’t panic at Set’s cry was likely simply because he had grown somewhat accustomed to Set’s presence.

They had taken Set out for walks several times in places like the Duke of Kerebel’s gardens and the training grounds, so while there were still some who were afraid of him, fortunately, the guards stationed here weren’t among them.

No, that’s precisely why they’d been assigned to guard this waiting room.

The guards looked at each other’s faces and exchanged smiles.

It’s hardly surprising that those smiles were tinged with a hint of wryness.

Unaware of what was happening outside, Rei simply served up a few dishes from Misty Ring and enjoyed them with Set.

Rei was playing with Set like that, but a short while later, there was a knock on the waiting room door.

“What is it?”

“It seems preparations for the mock battle are ready. They’d like you and Set to come to the stage.”

“Got it. Alright, Set… shall we go?”

“Grr.”

Having just finished eating the stew—made with oak meat simmered until tender—Set purred contentedly at Rei’s words.

Seeing Set looking so happy, Rei decided that, regardless of his own mock battle about to take place, the debut for Set that Liberte had planned would surely go well.

Stepping outside through the door—which had been made larger than usual so Set could move in and out easily—they found a man standing there, likely sent by the guards to relay the message to come to the stage.

“This way, Rei.”

The man who had brought the message seemed slightly flustered, perhaps because he wasn’t used to Rei yet… and, feeling a sense of awe at the sight of Set, he said this.

“Rei, we’re going along as your escorts, just in case… though, judging by the way you look, you don’t seem nervous at all.”

“Of course not. How many battles do you think I’ve been through so far? Hey, Set.”

“Grr…”

Set, who had been distracted by the aroma that had whetted his appetite after leaving the hut, snapped back to reality at Rei’s words and growled.

“Look, we’ll put the food stalls on hold for now. I’ll let you eat as much as you want with the money I won from the bet.”

At Rei’s words, Set wagged his tail as if to say, “You promised.”

As he stroked Set, Rei recalled the details of the wager Miranda had told him about.

It seemed the stakes for this mock battle had been decided down to the finest detail.

How many opponents could Rei defeat...? To be precise, since the number of participants in the mock battle exceeded initial expectations, those fighting Rei had been divided into groups, and the bets centered on how many rounds Rei could win.

Normally, betting on Rei to win every match would have been a long shot, but the bookmakers had clearly gathered their information thoroughly. While the odds for Rei winning every match were high, they weren’t high enough to be considered a long shot.

Still, considering the amount of money Rei had entrusted to Miranda, he should have easily been able to afford street food with his winnings.

Of course, that’s all assuming Rei wins the mock battle… but Rei judged that he wouldn’t have any trouble with that.

He has confidence in his own strength, and above all, considering the opponents Rei has fought so far, he felt he simply couldn’t afford to lose to someone he’d face in a mock battle.

That said, letting his guard down would defeat the purpose, so he intended to take the mock battle seriously.

Stroking Set, Rei made his way toward the mock battle stage alongside the guide and the guards.

Perhaps hoping to catch a glimpse of Rei, a fair number of people were still lingering around even before they reached the arena.

(If this were a more formal arena, they’d probably make sure no one could see the fighters as they entered. Well, since this place was built just for this mock battle, I guess it’s understandable.)

That’s what Rei thought, but in reality, the area around the mock battle stage—and the surrounding area where food stalls were set up—was patrolled by knights, guards, and adventurers for safety.

Adventurers, in particular, were popular among those who didn’t want to fight Rei in the mock battle but were short on money, since they could earn money just by standing there—even though they had no intention of actually fighting.

In reality, given that this location was right next to Anesis, the likelihood of monsters or bandits appearing was quite low.

Of course, if such creatures did appear, they would have to fight, but the guards believed that such an event was unlikely—no, almost certainly impossible.

Unlike the adventurers who had voluntarily taken on this job, the knights and guards were stationed there under orders from above.

As a result, they couldn’t enjoy this unexpectedly held festival or watch the mock battles staged by famous adventurers.

In that sense, those knights and guards were probably unlucky.

While thinking of those unlucky souls, Rei was led to the mock battle stage alongside Set...

“Huh!?”

Suddenly, Rei stopped in her tracks and scanned her surroundings sharply.

“H-hey. What’s wrong?”

The guard who had been moving alongside Rei was taken aback by her sudden action.

Actually, it wasn’t just Rei who had acted suddenly—Set had done the same.

He crouched slightly, ready for anything, and kept a watchful eye on his surroundings.

About a minute passed, and finally, Rei let his guard down.

(Was I imagining things? ...No, that gaze directed at me was definitely there. I didn’t sense any killing intent or anything—it was more like a gaze observing something.)

But the gaze vanished the moment Rei reacted.

It was as if the gaze he’d felt just a moment ago had never existed.

If someone inexperienced had sensed that gaze just now, it wouldn’t be surprising for them to think it was just their imagination.

That’s how suddenly the gaze vanished.

“H-hey. Hey. What the hell just happened? All of a sudden…”

Judging by Rei’s current state, the guard must have decided it would be dangerous to provoke him carelessly, for he spoke to him with a timid air.

Watching the guard’s reaction, he concluded that the guard hadn’t felt the gaze.

“...Just a bit. Someone was giving me a weird look.”

“A strange gaze? Well, Rei and Set do stand out, so I don’t think it’s strange that someone would be looking at you like that.”

Relieved that Rei had finally spoken, the guard tilted his head, still seemingly confused by what Rei was saying.

“That’s not what I meant. …It’s just… I’m starting to look forward to the mock battle a little bit.”

Until now, the only thing he’d looked forward to in the mock battle was fighting a few skilled opponents, but the person who’d just given him that look was definitely someone who couldn’t be measured by such standards.

Assuming that such an opponent was likely participating in this mock battle as well, Rei began walking toward the stage alongside Set.

“More sensitive to gazes than I expected, huh? …I see. A formidable foe. Crimson, is it? He looks like an interesting opponent.”

Without looking directly at Rei and Set, but instead gazing slightly off to the side, the man muttered quietly… and then walked away.
