The explosion that occurred when I tried to touch the hut instantly destroyed the hut itself.

Nearly all the magic items that would have been inside the hut have vanished.

And what was most vicious was that it served as a means to attack anyone attempting to enter the hut while simultaneously destroying evidence.

The explosion of the hut itself turned the hut into a weapon.

To shield Azimov from the countless flying fragments, Rei used his Dragon Robe to become a shield. Protected by Rei, Azimov instantly activated the magic item bracelet on his right arm, deploying a defensive barrier around them that boosted Rei's defensive power.

"...Are you all right?"

Once the storm of hut debris had subsided, Rei asked Azimov behind him.

"Ah, yeah. ...Somehow, I'm okay."

As Azimov muttered this, the shattered bracelet fell from his right arm to the ground.

Seeing this, Rei realized the barrier from earlier had been caused by Azimov.

"That barrier just now... was Azimov?"

"Yep. It was a magic item made from some pretty rare materials, but still, it's a shame it got used up."

Sighing, Azimov's gaze naturally fell upon the bracelet lying on the ground.

As Azimov had said, the magic item crafted from rare materials was now useless.

Gently reaching out, Azimov picked up the bracelet and immediately began examining it.

He had crafted magic items for others countless times and tested them himself on numerous occasions.

But actually using one like this, in the heat of the moment, was an experience he hadn't had in a long time.

He had brought this magic item when Rei dragged him out of his house, judging there might be danger, and it had certainly proven its worth.

(Though she looks pretty dissatisfied about it.)

Rei looked at Azimov examining the bracelet and thought deeply.

The bracelet had protected both her and Rei, yet Azimov seemed dissatisfied with its performance somehow, his expression now that of an alchemist.

Seeing Azimov like this, Rei judged the danger had passed and turned his gaze back toward the hut... or rather, the spot where the hut had been.

The sight before him made it utterly impossible to believe a hut had stood there just moments ago.

If he had to find any trace of it, it would be the scorched ground.

"We've been had."

As Rei muttered this while looking at the hut's remains, Azimov lifted his gaze from the bracelet's wreckage and also turned his eyes toward the hut's former site.

The expression on Azimov's face was one of detached acceptance.

Being an alchemist himself, he likely understood what Zubo had aimed to achieve with this act.

Namely, to protect the secrets of his research, and above all, to exact revenge on those who tried to steal it.

"Ah. ...Given how things turned out, there's probably nothing left now."

Sighing, Azimov agreed with Rei's words.

As the two exchanged words with a sense of deep feeling, the sound of someone running suddenly reached their ears, and they turned their gaze in that direction.

"You're okay, Rei!"

"Hey, Rei. Are you okay!?"

Elena and Vihera rushed over to Rei, looking flustered.

The explosion they'd just witnessed was, of course, visible to Elena and Vihera as well.

In fact, the blast wave from the exploding hut, along with fragments carried by that blast wave, had flown toward them.

They weren't as close to the blast as Rei, so they avoided being hit by debris. Still, it was an explosion powerful enough to make them instinctively brace themselves. Naturally, they were concerned about Rei, who had been near the explosion, and that's why they rushed over.

But Rei, the one they were so worried about, seemed unharmed and nodded back.

"Ah, no worries. I'm fine over here, no injuries or anything. ...More importantly, Elena being here means..."

Wondering why Elena, who had been holding Adrienne down just moments ago, was here, Rei shifted his gaze toward Adrienne.

There she was, having had several magic items stolen by Byune, her limbs bound by what could barely be called ropes—more like bundles of thin threads.

Near Dias, Yellow remained alert, watching for any movement, while Paul was still bound head to toe in thorns.

Judging that Adria and the others seemed to be okay, Rei turned his gaze back to Elena and Vihera.

Both of them breathed sighs of relief upon seeing Rei was unharmed.

"...I was pretty close to that explosion too, you know."

Azimov remarked beside Rei, sounding slightly displeased.

While Azimov felt more like he was conducting an alchemy experiment than protecting a woman, he still found it a bit irritating to be ignored while everyone worried only about Rei.

"You were behind Rei, weren't you? I knew you couldn't possibly get hurt."

Azimov felt a twinge of irritation at Elena's words, but since it was true, he didn't bother to argue.

"Still, you pulled off quite a clever little trick there."

Vihera muttered resentfully as she looked at the remains of the hut.

Her unusually irritable mood likely stemmed from thinking Rei had taken the explosion head-on at close range.

The sight sent a chill down Vihera's spine.

Could it be that Rei was gone for good?

Could he be dead?

Elena, who had been dealing with Adria, gasped for just an instant before regaining her composure.

(Is this the difference in how long we've been together?)

Feeling the irritation within herself, Vihera turned her gaze toward Elena.

To be precise, it was precisely because she knew Rei's secret that she didn't panic to that extent... but that was something the current Vihera couldn't possibly understand.

"Right. For now, you should assume everything in the hut—the evidence, magic items, materials, all that—is ruined. ...What a troublesome thing to do."

Rei and Vihera shot Rei a somewhat exasperated look as he let out a sigh.

It was clear he wasn't worried about his own safety, but rather lamenting the loss of the magic items that had been inside the hut.

"Elena-sama!"

Amidst this, a voice suddenly called out.

Recognizing the voice, Elena and Vihera turned their gaze toward its source.

There, running towards them with two guards, was Ara.

"Ara!? Why are you here?"

Elena asked in astonishment at Ara's sudden appearance.

Approaching Elena, Ara quickly checked to see if she was injured, finally letting out a sigh of relief.

"I was following after you, Elena-sama, when suddenly this tremendous explosion sounded. I was beside myself with worry... But it seems you're safe and sound, which is the most important thing."

"Huh? Oh, that explosion? Luckily, I was far enough away. I could have handled it either way."

"I see. That's a relief. ...So it seems there was no damage from that explosion..."

Looking around and confirming that none of the people she'd met at the magic item shop had suffered any major injuries, Ara breathed a sigh of relief once more.

While Elena was undoubtedly Ara's top priority, that didn't mean she didn't care about the others.

"Rei was right near that explosion, yet he's unharmed... Well, I suppose that's just Rei for you."

"Kyuu-kyuu!"

Yellow let out a cry of agreement at Elena's words.

Vihera nodded beside Elena as well.

"You guys only get along at times like this, huh?"

"Hehe, I wonder..."

Vihera smiled at Rei's words and replied.

Judging that saying anything more would only put him at a disadvantage, he opened his mouth to get the important task done first.

"The one tied up over there is Adria, the one missing his right arm is Dias, and the one tangled in thorns and unable to move is..."

"Paul!? So it was you after all..."

Before Rei could finish, one of the guards shouted.

It was probably because Paul had been covered in thorns until now, and also because his attention had been taken by the explosion that happened shortly after they entered the slums, causing him to notice late.

Only now did he finally recognize the figure as Paul, and the realization made him cry out involuntarily.

(An acquaintance, huh. Well, he is a guard, so it wouldn't be strange for him to know a magic item dealer. ...Actually, it's strange that the alchemist Azimov doesn't know Paul's face. Where on earth was he selling magic items?)

The simplest and easiest way for an alchemist to make money is, of course, to sell magic items they've made themselves.

From simple potions to Magic Swords, or magic items that make daily life more convenient.

If you have the skills as an adventurer, you could also take on quests. Unfortunately, Azimov had neither combat experience nor talent.

Given that, he must have been selling magic items to make money. At least in this case, it seems Azimov wasn't selling them at Paul's shop.

That said, Paul's isn't the only magic item shop in Gilm.

Being a frontier town, Gilm has its fair share of alchemists, and naturally, the trade in magic items is fairly brisk.

"...Slon, huh?"

Paul, his face still free despite being wrapped in thorns, murmured the guard's name.

"Why are you doing this kind of thing..."

"I think you can pretty much figure it out by looking at the results?"

Paul's expression as he stated this matter-of-factly showed no sign of regret for his actions.

Hearing Paul's words, the guard who had spoken to him flashed a momentary look of sadness, but his face quickly returned to that of a guard.

"Understood. I'll have this thoroughly investigated once I return to headquarters. I'm sure you have your reasons, but as a Gilm guard, I absolutely cannot overlook this."

"Yeah."

Paul's tone held nothing but dispassion.

Seeing Paul like this, the guard they'd been talking to probably had a lot on his mind, but he said nothing more, simply continuing to calmly assess the situation around him.

Amidst this, another guard addressed Rei.

"Rei, I heard about this incident from Captain Ranga. That must have been tough."

This guard either didn't know Paul, or if he did, they weren't close, showing almost no expression toward him.

"Yeah, it was... really tough in every way. The worst part is that the guy who planned this whole thing ended up dead."

"Died? If that's the case... Wait a minute, could it be... that corpse!?"

The only corpse left at the scene was that of the rampaging Zuboz.

But that corpse was far too large to be human, which is precisely why the guard had assumed it was some sort of alchemically created chimera.

Or even a giant monster like a cyclops or an ogre – something so enormous it would make sense.

"That's right. He knew they were cornered. He took some kind of drug... and ultimately went berserk, ending up like that."

"Rampage..."

"Yeah. And that alchemist... Zuboz, or whatever he's called... his hideout was in that state."

Next, Rei's gaze shifted to the site where the hut had once stood, now completely gone.

"...No way..."

The guard stared at the ruins with a look of resignation growing stronger.

Seeing the guard, Rei suddenly remembered the magic item shop.

"If you came here with Ara, that means you came through the magic item shop's underground passage, right? Then you must have passed through the basement Zuzo was apparently using too? He must have been in a real panic when he fled from us, so there might be some clues left behind?"

"Oh!"

The guard's voice brightened at Rei's words.

While it probably wasn't exactly the kind of evidence they needed, it was still far better than nothing.

...Of course, most of the rare materials Zuboz brought to Gilm were already gone, and since the genius-type Zuboz planned and executed everything in his head, he left almost no paperwork behind... The guard's troubles would come a little later.
