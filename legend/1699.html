"You're being too soft. You're really being too soft."

Rei, who had mentioned going to the dungeon during his conversation with Walker, finished his day’s work—which included transporting felled trees from Treant Forest and hauling various other materials—and, as usual, began talking about the dungeon while having dinner at Marina’s mansion. Marina immediately shot down the idea.

“Even so. Since it’s a dungeon on a cliff, there’s no doubt that we—with Set—are the best suited for it, but that doesn’t mean there’s no one else. In fact, if there are high-ranking adventurers, there’s a good chance they could manage it even if it’s a bit of a stretch.”

“True. But that doesn’t change the fact that we’re the best choice, does it?”

Marina shook her head as she took a bite of the oak meat fresh from the pizza oven.

“In the end, Rei fell for Walker’s bluff.”

“Even so. It’s a dungeon, you know? And probably a brand-new one at that.”

The reason she couldn’t say for certain here was that there was actually a possibility the dungeon had grown inside the cliff, resulting in an entrance appearing on the cliff face.

Of course, the probability was low, but it wasn’t zero.

That said, even if it were such a dungeon, Rei had no intention of backing down from the challenge.

The terrain manipulation skill possessed by the Death Scythe is that powerful.

At his current Level 3, he could raise or lower the ground within a radius of about 50 meters by one meter.

That alone is incredibly powerful, but from his experience, Rei knows that once he reaches Level 5, the skill will evolve to a truly formidable degree.

Given that, there was no way he was going to miss this golden opportunity to level up his terrain manipulation.

Of course, since Byune—who knows nothing about Beast Magic—is here, he can’t mention it out loud.

However, even without Rei saying anything about it, those familiar with Beast Magic can understand what he’s getting at.

And precisely because they understand, they realize that no matter how much they oppose Rei’s proposal here, it’s pointless.

“If we’re going to the dungeon anyway, I wish it were winter or something.”

“Really? I think we should go as soon as possible, if we’re going to go at all.”

Once winter comes, the expansion construction work will at least slow down for the time being, so everyone here, myself included, will have some free time.

In response to Marina’s comment, Vihera, who harbors the hope that in a dungeon she might be able to fight powerful monsters, expresses her desire to go to the dungeon as soon as possible.

“So, what are we going to do?”

Elena asks Rei without seeming to feel anything in particular.

She was silently telling him that she would go with Rei... and Rei, too, was grateful for Elena’s combat prowess—no, for her very presence—so he had no complaints.

Ara, who has been stuck minding the shop in Gilm for quite some time now, shot Rei a look that was part envy, part resentment, but Rei pretended not to notice and answered Elena’s question.

“To be honest, I want to join the Gamelion hunt, so if possible, I’d agree with Marina’s suggestion to wait until winter. …But this dungeon seems to have started expanding rapidly recently. It feels wrong to just leave the residents of Goltz feeling anxious like that.”

Saying that, he took a bite of the seafood pizza made with fish he’d caught at sea—though the toppings consisted mostly of fish fillets rather than shrimp or crab—before speaking again.

“Besides, this isn’t exactly a request specifically for us. In other words, any other adventurers could take on the dungeon if they wanted to. …Well, there’s the question of whether they can actually get into the dungeon safely, and even if they do, how much equipment they’ll be able to bring with them.”

As he spoke, Rei could taste how the sauce—with its tomato-like tartness and sweetness—elevated the flavor of the white fish to a whole new level.

In fact, as Walker had mentioned, this job isn’t one that absolutely has to be done by Rei.

Of course, for the average adventurer… and while Rei doesn’t know the town of Goltz, Walker said that adventurers in the countryside probably wouldn’t even be able to enter the dungeon.

However, if an adventurer possessed a certain specialized skill—such as the ability to descend cliffs—entering that dungeon would be possible.

Moreover, many high-ranking adventurers and those with nicknames possess abilities that make them seem almost inhuman.

Given that, it’s likely that such individuals could enter a dungeon located on a cliff face.

After all, the likelihood that this is a dungeon is extremely high.

If it really is a dungeon, then naturally, whoever ventures inside will gain ownership of the various resources found within.

If word of this got around, there was no doubt that crowds would flock to Goltz in search of the potential for great wealth.

“So, I’d like to head to the Goltz dungeon as soon as possible… Does anyone know which town Goltz is in?”

“I know.”

“I know, too.”

Marina and Elena each replied to Rei’s words.

In contrast to the two of them, Vihera, who hailed from the Bestir Empire, and Byune, who struggled with social interaction, shook their heads at Rei’s words.

“How far is it from here?”

“Hmm… well. It’s definitely quite a distance. But I do have some interesting information regarding that, you know?”

As Marina tells him this with a smile, Rei urges her on with a glance. Then, with a smile different from the one just a few seconds ago—one that seems to hold something else—he opens his mouth.

“The noble who holds the territory surrounding Goltz is a member of the Neutral Faction. So I don’t think it’ll be too difficult to get Daskar to agree to this.”

“That’s a huge help.”

Rei was well aware that he—and they—were a significant asset in carrying out the expansion of Gilm.

Given that situation—especially since they had just returned from a vacation at the seaside—she had anticipated that Daskar would be put in a difficult position if they asked to go on another long-term assignment elsewhere.

Of course, Rei and the others were technically being hired on a day-to-day basis for the expansion project, so they weren’t bound to Gilm.

But even knowing that, Rei and his team’s abilities were something Gilm, who was in the midst of the expansion project, would very much like to have on hand.

However, the situation is different when it comes to Goltz, the destination Rei and the others are heading to—the territory of the Neutral Faction… in other words, the territory of one of Daskar’s allies.

Although the Neutral Faction is known as one of the “Three Great Factions,” their influence is still small compared to the other two factions: the Royal Faction and the Noble Faction.

Recently, thanks to Rei’s efforts, they have expanded their influence and established friendly relations with the Noble Faction… but even so, they remain a minor faction.

That said, this is strictly speaking only within the context of the Three Great Factions; to nobles who aren’t even part of the Three Great Factions, the Neutral Faction is a sufficiently powerful force.

In any case, precisely because the Neutral Faction is the weakest of the three major factions... or perhaps because of that very fact, the unity among the nobles belonging to the Neutral Faction is strong.

This is likely due in large part to the personality of Daskar, who leads the Neutral Faction.

For that reason, Rei didn’t think Daskar would object if she told him that a dungeon had been discovered on the territory of a noble belonging to the Neutral Faction, and that there were those concerned about it, so they were going to go and clear it out.

“Well then, I’ll go talk to Daskar first thing tomorrow. We’ll set out the day after tomorrow. …So, what do you think?”

No one voiced any objections to Marina’s proposal.

If they pushed themselves, it would be possible to leave as early as tomorrow, but when it came to clearing a dungeon, it was unclear exactly how much time would be required.

Given that, it was absolutely necessary to talk to Daskar and have the others take over the work Rei and the others were doing.

Especially in Rei’s case, since he’d have to transport the felled trees without the Misty Ring, we needed to find a replacement.

(Though, I have a feeling there’ll be quite a backlog by the time I get back.)

Whether it’s dozens at a time… or even over a hundred trees, Rei can transport them with the Misty Ring.

In comparison, transporting felled trees by wagon requires a considerable amount of effort.

And yet, the amount of wood that can be transported at once isn’t all that great.

Given those circumstances, there’s no doubt that the trees we can’t transport will end up piling up in Treant Forest.

And so, after deciding to set out for the dungeon in two days’ time, Rei and the others continued their pleasant dinner.

The next day, Rei was transporting trees from Treant Forest as usual, but...

“Aaaaaah, please, Rei! Stay in Gilm a little longer, just a little longer! At least until I finish my shift in the Treant Forest!”

One of the adventurers pleaded with Rei.

This adventurer, who had signed a long-term contract to work in the Forest of Treants, would naturally have to transport the felled trees if Rei were to leave.

Simply providing an escort was one thing, but loading the felled timber onto a wagon required considerable physical strength.

What’s more, after transporting them all the way to Gilm, there’s also the task of unloading the logs from the wagon and stacking them neatly in the designated spot.

When you think about it, there’s no doubt this is extremely physically demanding work.

It’s a job where the level of fatigue and difficulty changes drastically depending on whether Rei is there or not. That was the nature of the work in Treant Forest.

But in response to such pleas, Rei shook his head.

“Sorry, but I’ve got something else to do. Besides, this isn’t something I can decide on my own—it’s a party decision.”

“Ah… I see. Hmm, well, the contract isn’t that long, so I guess I’ll give it a shot.”

The adventurer who had been talking to Rei said this with a look of disappointment, and then a female adventurer who had been keeping watch from a distance smiled and spoke up.

“Since my work in the Forest of Treants ends today, I guess I’m lucky. …Hey, Rei. I heard that the season when those monsters called Gamelions start appearing is coming up soon. Is that true?”

At first, this female adventurer had spoken to Rei in a polite tone, but after working together in the same workplace for several days and exchanging conversations, her attitude had become more casual.

It was hard to tell whether this meant Rei was seen as approachable or if he was being taken lightly...

That said, if you were to ask whether Rei actually prefers being treated with deference, he wouldn’t exactly say he does.

It might be one thing if they only saw each other occasionally, but since he was in charge of the Treant Forest, they saw each other almost every day.

Being treated with excessive deference every time she sees someone like that feels out of place and, above all, is nothing but a hassle.

That’s exactly why Rei responds casually to the female adventurer.

“It’s true, but Gamelion is a pretty ferocious monster. Its combat power is high, as befits a Rank C. If you think you can handle it casually and try to take it on, you’re going to get hurt pretty badly. …Though there’s no doubt it’ll be a good payday.”

“Hmm… Are you saying you’re strong, Rei? That makes me hesitate.”

The female adventurer wasn’t strong enough to normally operate in Gilm.

That’s precisely why she was able to take Rei’s words to heart.

It was precisely because she could view her own abilities objectively that she was able to properly prioritize her safety.

If she valued making money over her own safety, she would have ignored Rei’s warning and gone hunting Gamelions.

Of course, she’s saying this now, but whether she’ll actually understand that and act accordingly depends entirely on her.

(There’s also the option of teaming up with someone stronger than me… but that’s a bit risky.)

She’d be teaming up with strangers to hunt monsters for profit.

Naturally, in that case, it wouldn’t be surprising if they ended up arguing over the split.

And if she’s a female adventurer who’s clearly outmatched, her reward would naturally be smaller.

No, that would actually be the better-case scenario; in the worst-case scenario, it could even turn into a fight to the death.

If that happens, the woman standing before Rei would have no way of handling it… and would almost certainly die.

Considering all that, Rei’s opinion is that it would be best to avoid hunting Gamelions.

To begin with, due to the expansion of Gilm, there is currently a surplus of work and a shortage of workers.

Since there’s no shortage of work, it was undoubtedly true that acting out of greed would likely be fatal.
