The meat buns sold by Golden Bread Shop quickly became the talk of the town among adventurers in Gilm.

Originally, this winter season offered little to do, so many adventurers were taking it easy and resting their bodies.

While some adventurers took on quests due to lack of funds, most who had saved enough for the winter spent their time eating, drinking, and visiting brothels.

Precisely because Gilm, being a frontier town, had a large number of adventurers, those adventurers had little to do during this season. Consequently, novel rumors spread quickly.

And especially when the rumor concerned a restaurant facing the main street... a restaurant known to every adventurer, no less.

The rumor went that the Golden Bread Inn was selling bread unlike any anyone had ever tasted before.

They said this bread was astonishingly soft, unlike any ordinary bread.

They said the filling and the outer crust complemented each other perfectly, making it impossible to stop eating.

They said the shop's girl, Sandrine, was surprisingly cute and friendly.

Rumors like these naturally piqued the adventurers' curiosity.

Adventurers are usually busy taking on quests, but now they're idle with nothing particularly pressing to do.

Given that, it was only natural that their curiosity would be piqued, making them want to verify the rumors about the Golden Bread Inn.

And so, drawn by curiosity, they head to the Golden Bread Shop... where they take a bite of the meat bun.

They'd eaten baked bread since birth, but steamed bread was completely unexpected.

As a result, many adventurers began frequenting the Golden Bread Shop day after day.

That alone might have been fine, but adventurers, while often quiet, also included many hot-tempered souls.

What happens when such hot-tempered individuals swarm over a limited supply of meat buns... well, that was obvious.

"GRRRRRRRRRR!"

Just as the adventurers were about to start a full-blown fight, that cry echoed around them.

Adventurers living in Gilm knew exactly who was making that sound... and they certainly knew the owner of the creature that made it.

The adventurers, who had been poised to fight in earnest in front of the Golden Bread Shop, froze, their bodies trembling. Like broken dolls, they turned their gaze toward the source of the sound.

There stood Set the Griffin and... Rei, clad in a Dragon Robe.

It was fortunate for the adventurers that neither carried weapons like the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear.

Though that was largely because, in this brawl, none of the adventurers had drawn weapons, intending to fight barehanded.

If they made a scene here, they would undoubtedly suffer a terrible fate.

The adventurers who came to that conclusion, and others caught up in the brawl, quickly lined up in front of the shop.

This was likely because it was widely known around here how Rei dealt with those who opposed him in Gilm.

While Rei generally didn't pick fights unless provoked, those who crossed him ended up suffering in various ways.

It was only natural the adventurers didn't want to suffer the same fate.

Seeing them line up like that, Rei seemed satisfied. He and Set began walking down the main street again.

(I'd heard it got pretty crowded after they started selling meat buns, but looks like it's true.)

Rodrigo, realizing it would be difficult to handle all these customers with just his own shop, had taught other eateries how to make meat buns. However, he had only taught them the basics.

He instructed them to develop their own refinements from there, keeping his own improvements to himself.

Some cooks voiced dissatisfaction, but most understood Rodrigo's reasoning well and were experimenting to create their own unique fillings.

...That's why not many shops sell pork buns yet, and even those that do can't match Golden Bread Shop's flavor.

It's only natural to want the tastier option when choosing between similar dishes... and that was one reason for this line.

Of course, thanks to that, they're making a hefty profit every day, and Sandrine is happily overwhelmed.

(Steamed buns are fine, but pizza will probably take a while longer. There's still a lot of research needed.)

Walking alongside the line, Rei passed the shop front and watched with satisfaction as the interior was packed with customers before continuing on his way.

"Grrr?"

"Aren't you eating any meat buns?" Set asked, looking at Rei and making a gurgling sound.

"It's crowded now. Later, maybe. ...Buying a bunch of freshly made meat buns and storing them in the Misty Ring would be nice too."

"Gurrr!"

Set hadn't really liked the first meat bun he tried, but the improved version clearly suited his taste perfectly.

"Later, later. It hasn't been long since the buns went on sale, so everyone's still focused on them. Things'll calm down a bit more soon."

"Grrr..."

Set let out a disappointed growl at Rei's words.

If either Milein or Johanna... or both of them had been here, they would probably have tried to get their hands on the meat buns somehow.

(The meat bun thing probably spread as gossip precisely because it's winter and people have free time. In that sense, Rodrigo sold the meat buns at the best possible timing.)

It was already nearly noon.

The snow that had fallen last night was packed down on the main street, so they couldn't enjoy the feeling of stepping on fresh snow.

Feeling a bit disappointed about that, Rei walked down the main street with Set...

"Ah, Rei-kun! Rei-kun!"

Suddenly, he turned his gaze toward the voice calling his name.

The speaker was bundled up so thickly that their face was hidden, making it impossible to tell who it was.

But the fact that they were bundled up so thickly their face couldn't be seen... and above all, the voice itself told Rei exactly who it was.

"Kenny... and that's..."

Beside the bundled-up Kenny stood another woman around Kenny's age.

She too was a beast-person, likely a dog or perhaps a wolf.

Despite that, she had a somewhat gentle expression and smiled at the sight of Rei and Set.

Rei and Set approached the two.

They had come out onto the main street this time with the specific purpose of taking a walk with Set, without any particular business to attend to.

If he had to name a reason, it was that he'd heard the meat buns were popular and, as the one who'd suggested the idea, he wanted to see for himself just how popular they really were.

"Nice to meet you! I'm Kenny's friend—my name's Kakou!"

His drawn-out speech pattern matched the gentle, warm-hearted vibe of Kako, the beastman.

"Ah, ah. Nice to meet you."

"Gurrrrrrr!"

Unlike Rei, who felt a vague sense of unease, Set happily growled and approached Kakou.

"Ah, Set-chan. Long time no see! How have you been?"

"Guruu!"

Watching that exchange between Set and Kako, Rei recalled seeing Kako play with Set several times before.

(Come to think of it, I have seen them together before. ...Yeah. Still, Kenny the cat beastman and Kakou the dog beastman. Normally, you'd expect them to be at odds with each other.)

Though his face was hidden, judging by Kenny's earlier words, it certainly didn't seem like he and Kakou were fighting. ...No, in this case, it's more accurate to say he couldn't hear it.

"So, it's rare to see Kenny, who hates the cold, out here like this... What's up?"

Watching Kako happily playing with Set, Rei asked Kenny.

Like Kako, Kenny also seemed happy—though you couldn't tell from his expression, so you had to judge by his voice—and began speaking to Rei.

"Kakou was asking my advice about something. ...Oh, right. Speaking of which, didn't you tell Golden Bread House and Full Belly Restaurant about new dishes, Rei? If you don't mind, could you hear me out for a bit?"

"Even if you say so, I can't just whip up a new dish on the spot. I don't have anything ready."

Having heard about Golden Bread House and Full Belly Restaurant, and having just seen the line for meat buns, Rei assumed Kako was either a chef or an employee at a restaurant. She replied to Kenny, but Kenny shook her head.

"No, that's not it. Kakou doesn't work at the cafeteria... See, he runs that shop over there."

Kenny pointed to a single shop.

However, Rei had never been inside that shop.

Because it was a shop selling cosmetics... specifically, perfume was their main product.

It might not be unusual for a high school boy these days to have some interest in cosmetics, but when Rei lived in Japan, it was in a place surrounded by mountains.

Sure, his high school had its share of students, but whether it was his innate personality or not, Rei never developed an interest in cosmetics.

He even thought that if he were going to spend money on any kind of cosmetics, he'd rather use that money to buy books or games.

And now, with his current body, whether cosmetics were even necessary was beyond consideration.

Well, maybe it would be better if he used them, but Rei had absolutely no interest in that sort of thing.

"A cosmetics store?"

"Yeah. It's called White Fragrance. It's actually pretty well-known in Gilm... You haven't heard of it?"

"I've heard the name, and I've seen it from the outside a few times. But I've never actually gone inside."

"Ah, well, that makes sense for you, Rei. I buy my cosmetics from White Fragrance too. ...Despite appearances, Kakou is actually a skilled artisan."

Kenny muttered this while watching the dog beastman playfully wrestling with Set, laughing "Ahaha~". Rei also wore a surprised expression.

He certainly didn't look like such a skilled craftsman.

But Kenny had no reason to lie to Rei here, and Rei couldn't help but realize that people really are not what they seem.

"Ah, yeah. I get what you're saying, Rei, but when it comes to blending perfumes, she's one of the absolute best in Gilm, right? Being a dog beastman probably plays a big part too."

Rei felt a slight doubt at those words, but quickly understood.

Perfume, as the name suggests, is scented water... water infused with fragrance.

Kako's ability as a dog beastman, with his heightened sense of smell beyond the ordinary, would be perfect for blending it.

Of course, beyond the dog beastman's sense of smell, the actual skill of blending perfumes would also be crucial.

But there was no doubt he had an advantage over ordinary people.

Hearing Kenny boast about his friend, Rei nodded, thinking that ability must be incredible... but at the same time, he had a question.

"Cooking, maybe, but what exactly do you want me to do at a cosmetics shop?"

"Hmm, well, Kako's shop is actually quite well-known as a cosmetics store... but apparently, their main perfume line isn't selling very well. Other cosmetics seem to be selling fine, so it's not like the shop is in crisis or anything."

Kako, who had been playing with Set, must have heard Kenny's words too, because she turned to look at Rei and Kenny with a dejected expression.

"Ah, come on, come on. Don't get down. Anyway, cosmetics aside, perfume isn't something most people use regularly, right?"

Kenny said this, but Rei knew he actually used perfume himself.

Working as the guild receptionist, it was probably necessary.

Even though she was bundled up now, Rei's superior sense of smell detected a scent from Kenny that could best be described as a refreshing sweetness.

"So, I was wondering if you had any thoughts on perfume, Rei-kun... What do you think?"

"No way, don't be ridiculous."

Rei shot back immediately.

Truthfully, he knew absolutely nothing about cosmetics or perfume.

When it came to cooking, thanks to manga and TV, he knew how to make a few things, but perfume was completely outside Rei's area of expertise.

Rei didn't even know the basic way to use perfume—applying it to the inside of your wrists and the nape of your neck.

"I don't know anything about perfume, okay?"

"It's fine, it's fine. Just give me any opinion, even if it's just off the top of your head."

Saying that, Kenny pulled Rei toward the direction of Kakou's shop.

For a moment, Rei hesitated, wondering if he should resist. But then again, he didn't really have anything else to do today, and he found himself somewhat intrigued by perfume, something he'd never had anything to do with before. So he let himself be pulled along.

If this had been a request like the one from the Golden Bread Shop, he probably wouldn't have taken it on.

It was purely a matter of circumstance, unrelated to any formal request... which was also why Rei had casually accepted Kenny's invitation.

...Though Kenny was certainly thinking about Kako, he was more delighted simply by the chance to be with Rei.
