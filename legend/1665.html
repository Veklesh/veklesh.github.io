"Ugh! I thought yesterday was safe, but now this out of nowhere!"

"Grr!"

As Rei let out a short cry from atop Set’s back, Set flapped his wings and twisted his body in sync, dodging the water ball that had flown out of the sea.

In terms of water balls, Set can use a similar skill.

However, the water ball flying out of the sea is slightly different from the one Set uses.

The biggest difference, after all, is that this water ball is made of seawater, not ordinary water.

Also, while the individual water balls aren’t that large, there are a lot of them.

If Set’s water balls were cannonballs, then the seawater balls would be like shotgun pellets… or something like that.

Of course, that’s just a comparison based on the current situation; in reality, there was a high probability that there were beings capable of using water spheres even larger than Set’s—like cannonballs.

“Set, get closer to the surface!”

“Grrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl and, flapping his wings, began to descend toward the surface of the sea.

But the monster that had been attacking must have noticed Set approaching. It made no further attempt to attack and simply dove back into the sea.

Rei threw the disposable spear he was holding at the monster, but since he was throwing it while riding Set, the throw was inevitably slower than usual.

He twisted his upper body to transfer the force to his arm and released the spear with that power... but the force wasn’t being transmitted from his lower body, which should have served as the most crucial foundation.

As a result, the spear’s impact was far from powerful.

Furthermore, the fact that the spear used was a disposable one—rather than the Twilight Spear, which is Rei’s finest—was undoubtedly another reason for the reduced power.

Although the strike was intended to avoid getting the Twilight Spear wet with seawater, the spear that was launched failed to hit the monster attacking Rei and the others as they flew through the air, and simply ended up piercing the seawater.

“This is a pain.”

Monsters that use seawater as a defensive measure only emerge from the water’s surface when they attack, and then immediately return to the depths once they’ve struck.

As an absolute defensive measure, the seawater was nothing but a nuisance to Rei as well.

(Did it attack Set because of the seawater? ...What a pain.)

No matter how high-ranking a monster Set might be, there was no doubt that he was a flying griffin.

Given that, it wasn’t unreasonable for an underwater monster to assume it would be safe as long as it stayed underwater.

Normally, monsters that recognize Set as Set and attack him are extremely rare.

That would be limited to monsters with low intelligence, like goblins, or those overly confident in their own strength.

However, the monster currently attacking Set—perhaps because it was in the water, a location that gave it an overwhelming advantage—showed absolutely no hesitation or fear as it launched its assault.

“Should I strike it when it surfaces near the surface, or should I just dive into the sea?”

If it were winter, diving into the sea wouldn’t even be an option.

But it’s summer now, the perfect season for swimming.

Given that, diving into the sea wouldn’t be a problem at all.

...To be precise, since Rei’s clothes and... all sorts of other magical items would end up getting soaked in seawater, it wasn’t exactly “no problem” in the strictest sense.

“Even though there’s a hot spring, I’d rather avoid it if possible… right?!”

Upon spotting a faint shadow on the water’s surface, Rei immediately pulled another disposable spear from her Misty Ring and hurled it.

However, perhaps realizing the spear had been thrown from above, the monster didn’t even bother to surface; it simply dove back into the water.

The spear plunged into the water in pursuit of the monster, but it seemed the monster, with the barrier of the sea on its side, held the advantage over Rei’s spear.

“Should I just turn the surrounding sea into boiling water?”

Rei considered this for a moment, but he realized that doing so would wipe out all the other creatures in the area as well.

Killing creatures for no reason at all—especially when they weren’t even enemies—went against Rei’s principles, even if it were just for his own consumption.

As a result, Rei abandoned the option of using fire magic and drew his spear from the Misty Ring once more.

“Well then, where will they come out next? …Set, you keep watch too.”

“Grr!”

Set growled, as if to say, “Leave it to me.”

As far as Rei was concerned, he would have preferred to use the King’s Intimidation to halt the enemy’s movements if possible, but its effectiveness was limited due to the fact that the enemy was underwater.

It wasn’t completely ineffective, but it still wasn’t as effective as it was against monsters on land or in the air.

……He’d already tried it once, so that much was clear.

“Grrrrr!”

Just as Set suddenly let out a growl, ten ice arrows appeared and flew toward the sea.

The ice arrows created by the Ice Arrow skill can deal damage even if the monster has the barrier of the sea, provided the timing is right.

That said, the same applies to the spears Rei throws.

However, while Rei throws a single spear, Ice Arrow fires ten ice arrows.

In that sense, there’s no doubt that Ice Arrow is more effective than Rei’s spear throw when it comes to attacking a wide area.

“Oh, did it hit?”

Seeing blood seep into the water’s surface, Rei muttered in admiration.

That said, judging by the amount of blood floating on the surface, it was clear that even if the attack had hit, it wasn’t a fatal blow.

Still, considering that the enemy had been using the sea as a barrier up until now, the fact that he managed to land a hit could certainly be called a stroke of luck for Rei.

“Whoa!”

Noticing that Set had suddenly leaned to one side, Rei shifted his balance to keep up with him.

Then, he saw a water ball flying out of the sea, heading toward the spot where Set’s body had been just a few seconds ago.

“Did they get hurt and get angry? Well, if that’s the case, it’ll make it easier for us to fight, so that’s fine by me.”

He muttered, raising his spear and glaring at the water’s surface.

As always, the sun’s intense glare made it difficult to make out the monster’s form through the light reflecting off the water’s surface.

Squinting against the glare reflecting off the water, Rei gripped his spear… and soon confirmed that the surface of the sea was rising slightly some distance away from Set.

Until just a moment ago, he hadn’t been able to detect the signs of a monster emerging from the water so easily, but the fact that he could now was likely due to the damage inflicted by the Ice Arrow Set that Set had fired earlier.

“Die!”

With that shout, the spear she hurled flew toward the surface of the sea, where the monster was about to emerge...

“GYOGYOGYUUUUU!”

The battle begins, and for the first time, the monster’s screams echo through the area.

Its appearance resembled that of a giant fish.

However, what sets them apart from ordinary fish is the single long horn protruding from their foreheads.

Also, though Rei couldn’t see clearly from the air, it looked as if its torso had something resembling crab claws attached in place of arms.

“Those claws look like they’re packed with meat—they look delicious. …Set!”

“Grrrr!”

Reacting to Rei’s words, Set dives straight downward.

Perhaps the monster noticed Set’s movements as well; even with its face pierced by the spear, it tried to dive into the sea in a hurry.

But... by the time it tried to dive into the sea, Set had already grabbed the monster’s body with his front paws.

“Gyo-ga-gyo-gya-ya!”

The monster wriggled desperately, trying to escape from Set’s grasp, but this monster’s strength lies only in the ocean.

Its strength stems from its ability to freely manipulate the ocean as a barrier.

Once caught by Set’s legs, there was nothing it could do in that situation.

Though it writhed frantically, that very action only made Set’s grip tighter, and eventually, the monster could no longer move at all.

(A water ball… no, should I call it a seawater ball? Why didn’t it use that in this situation? Since it can’t move its claws and biting is difficult, its only means of attack should be a seawater ball. Yet… no, is it because there’s no seawater? While Set can use a water ball even without water nearby, this monster can’t use a seawater ball unless there’s seawater around. Is that a possibility?)

While he was thinking about that, Set flapped his wings and headed toward the rocky coast that served as their base.

There, just like yesterday, Elena and the others were fishing.

The only difference was the presence of several corpses that appeared to be monsters.

(So it wasn’t just me—were Elena and the others attacked by monsters too?)

As he pondered this, they must have eventually noticed Rei and Set from over there. Waving their hands vigorously, Marina, Vihera, and Bistol were waiting for Rei and Set.

Since the monster Set was still holding onto was still alive, he didn’t move it right over to Marina and the others, but instead dropped it a short distance away.

Since it was a monster that lived in the ocean, once it was dragged onto land, it was helpless and could do nothing but thrash about.

Rei wondered if it could crawl using its crab claws, but the monster showed no sign of doing so.

Then, as Set landed on the ground and Rei climbed down as well... Rei pulled a spear from his Misty Ring—not a disposable spear, but the Twilight Spear—and approached the monster.

Set, too, moved closer to the monster while maintaining a defensive stance so he could react immediately if it made any strange movements.

Seeing this, Elena and the others, who had been fishing at a distance, naturally came over to Rei.

“Gyo-gya!”

Seeing Elena and the others, the monster must have assumed they were reinforcements for Rei.

The monster, realizing it would undoubtedly be killed if things continued as they were, mustered its last ounce of strength, slammed its body into the ground, and closed the distance to Rei in one swift motion... but in the next instant, its body was slammed into the ground with the same momentum it had used to lunge.

“Whoa, that was close. I didn’t think you still had the strength to do something like that. …Thanks, Set.”

“Grr.”

Still poised to pierce the charging monster, Rei spoke words of gratitude to Set—to Set, who had knocked the monster down with his front paws.

In response, Set purred happily.

“Hey, are you okay!? …I’m not actually asking, though. Knowing Rei and Set, I don’t think something like that would really hurt you.”

Vihera, who had been watching the exchange, said this to Rei and Set with a slightly exasperated expression.

In response, Rei raised her hand and smiled reassuringly at Vihera.

“So… what kind of monster is this? I’m just asking, but it’s not like it’s actually a fish or anything, is it?”

“No, isn’t it weird for a fish to have claws? Plus, it attacked using a skill, and Set also judged it to be a monster, so I’m pretty sure it’s definitely a monster.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set growled in praise.

It was confirmed to be a monster because it used a skill, but if it hadn’t used one, Rei probably wouldn’t have recognized it as a monster until she saw its appearance.

It’s obvious that having large claws is abnormal, but to recognize that, it would have to surface.

Since it lives in the ocean, whether one could clearly recognize those claws as claws just by seeing it underwater is…

(No, wait, I probably could. Ordinary fish definitely don’t have them, and they’d stand out even from above the water.)

Thinking that, Rei reconsidered what kind of monster this actually was.

A single long horn protruded from its forehead like a unicorn’s, and it possessed a pair of claws on its torso.

No matter how you look at them, those claws look exactly like a crab’s.

Rei tried to recall the contents of his Monster Encyclopedia, but he didn’t recognize this monster.

Of course, Rei understood that the Monster Encyclopedia didn’t list every single monster in the world.

If that’s the case, it wouldn’t be strange at all if this were a completely unknown monster.

Just as Rei was thinking that...

“Oh my. Isn’t this a Guztos? You’ve caught a rare monster, haven’t you?”

Bistol’s words revealed the monster’s name.
