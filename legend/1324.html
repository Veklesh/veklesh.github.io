"...What!? Is that... true!?"

Upon hearing that Azimov had been attacked, Pamidor fell silent for a moment, seemingly unable to comprehend what Rei was saying. But the next instant, he shouted loudly.

His voice was so loud it likely reached not only inside the forge but also the ears of those walking outside.

"Don't worry, Azimov is safe."

Watching Marina, Vihera, and Byune cover their ears from the noise, along with Kumito, who had been standing right next to Pamidor and had to hear it up close, Rei stated.

Pamidor also breathed a sigh of relief upon learning Azimov was safe, at least for now.

"Damn it, knowing him, did he use some magic item to chase them off?"

Hearing that, Rei briefly considered saying yes to cover it up.

But the reason he had come here in the first place was not only to have Byune tend to the White Cloud, but more importantly, because there was a very real possibility that the Pamidor family could be targeted by Precious's men.

If he said that even if Azimov had actually used a magic item here, it was someone he could easily handle... that could be fatal for Pamidor.

(Well, Pamidor might not need worrying about... but Kumito? That's a different story.)

His appearance was worthy of being called the leader of thieves, and his body and muscles, forged as a blacksmith, were incredibly sturdy.

A physique that makes you think he'd undoubtedly win even against some rookie adventurer just starting out.

No, not just new adventurers—even seasoned veterans would find it difficult to defeat Pamidor.

But that was only true for Pamidor himself.

His family... his children and wife were ordinary humans, unlike Pamidor.

Considering the possibility that they might be targeted, Rei shook his head at Pamidor's question, realizing he couldn't gloss over the matter with Azimov here.

"No, honestly, if we'd been just a few minutes later getting into Azimov's lab, he probably would've died. His injuries were that severe."

"...What?"

A gasp of shock escaped Pamidor's lips.

He surely never imagined Azimov had suffered such an injury.

But at the same time, he questioned the contradiction with Rei's earlier statement that Azimov's life was not in danger.

Kumito had visited Azimov's house several times with Pamidor, so they were reasonably close.

Seeing such a person attacked, left with life-threatening injuries, and yet somehow safe, left him deeply confused. For now, though, he remained silent, watching the exchange between his father and Rei.

"What's going on? Isn't what Rei's saying full of contradictions?"

"Not really. Normally, he should have died for sure. But I have various magic items. Among them was a potion that could heal Azimov, who should have died otherwise. ...It was pretty expensive, though."

Compared to potions made from World Tree materials, it was still something you could buy if you had the gold, but even so, he had used an extremely expensive potion to save Azimov's life.

...Potion prices range from mere copper coins for the cheapest to platinum coins for the most expensive... and some can't even be bought with light gold coins.

Potion made from World Tree materials was the very symbol of the latter.

Incidentally, a potion costing a few copper coins is the sort of thing that can't even completely heal a minor cut.

Considering that, you can imagine just how valuable the potion Rei used to heal Azimov's fatal wound must have been.

(I've gotten help with the Twilight Spear, the Kiln, Sleipnir Boots, and various other things, but should I still charge him for the potion? ...Well, in Azimov's case, he'd probably give me a potion with more potency than the one used on me anyway.)

Being a master alchemist, making potions probably isn't difficult for Azimov.

In fact, he probably already has a stockpile and might even hand one over immediately.

"Ah, so it's thanks to the potion."

Fortunately, Pamidor looked satisfied at Rei's explanation.

Kumito still seemed confused, but she probably decided it was fine since her father was relieved.

Convinced Azimov was safe, she didn't press the matter further.

"...Now then. Let's get to the main point."

"Huh? The main point?"

"Yeah. The reason Azimov was targeted this time was probably... no, almost certainly because of me."

"Backlash, you say?"

"Yeah. You know the Spier Trading Company, right? ...Well, no, Tris came here directly to hand over the White Cloud, so you must know them."

Hearing Rei's words, Pamidor nodded, likely recalling events from a little while ago... back when it was still winter.

"Ah, come to think of it, he was here. ...Wait. So, did this Tris guy attack Azimov?"

"No. To be precise, it was someone jealous of that Tris. I don't know if you're aware, Pamidor, but that Tris is the person in charge of the Spier Trading Company's Gilm branch."

Hearing that much, Pamidor finally understood the reason behind this incident.

And precisely because he understood, the muscles honed by daily forging tensed, making him appear to those around him as if his entire body had grown a size larger.

"So what? That bastard Azimov got dragged into some petty jealousy?"

"Unfortunately, yes. Incidentally, it seems Tris wanted it to look like he deliberately set me and the Azott Trading Company against each other..."

"...Huh."

A low voice.

Despite his menacing appearance, Pamidor was a man who cared deeply for his comrades, his friends, his family... that was his nature.

For a man like Pamidor, the idea that his friend had been dragged into a conflict born of petty jealousy was utterly unbearable.

"Calm down."

Though burning with rage, Pamidor snapped back to his senses at Rei's words.

That single phrase made it clear. Rei was just as angry as he was... perhaps even more so.

"I got dragged into this mess too. Naturally, I have my own thoughts about it, and I'll make sure whoever started this gets what they deserve. But... as you know, my acquaintance, Azimov, was targeted."

Rei paused there, his gaze directed not at Pamidor... but at Kumito.

As Pamidor's son, it was fair to say Kumi and Rei had a good relationship.

Above all, because Kumito was attached to Set, among the children in Gilm, the bond between Kumito and Rei was exceptionally strong.

...It might have something to do with the fact that when they first met, Rei had saved Kumito from being bullied.

Regardless, it was undeniably a serious problem if anyone targeted Kumito, even if Pamidor was involved.

Unlike his father, Kumito was just an ordinary child.

(Though saying "ordinary human" here would probably get me scolded by Pamidor...)

The thought crossed Rei's mind, but he kept it completely hidden as he continued speaking.

"I doubt whoever ordered the attack on Azimov would do something similar, but if they did, Pamidor and the others could easily become targets too. Of course, Pamidor wouldn't be the only one targeted."

"...Pamidor's fine, but what about our other acquaintances?"

Vihera's words made Rei pause.

After all, Rei had met quite a few people in Gilm.

Physically informing every single one of them about this incident and warning them to be cautious... that was simply impossible.

If you included people running stalls, the number of individuals who could be called Rei and Set's acquaintances would increase even further.

"So... that means we can't afford to drag this out for too long, right?"

"I suppose so. ...Besides, I don't think Rei could hold out that long anyway."

Marina nodded, as if to say she understood everything about Rei.

Honestly, given Rei's personality, it's not like he could just sit quietly and wait.

If anything, the fact he hasn't charged straight at the current Precious is the only sign he's holding back.

"...So it comes down to evidence. If we had any proof, we could turn him into rust for the Death Scythe right now."

"Yeah. It's a shame we don't have that proof. If we'd managed to capture whoever attacked Azimov, things would be different."

If we'd traced it back from the captured person, it should have led us to Precious in the end.

Of course, she knew it wasn't as simple as saying it was, but still... if they had managed to capture them back then... Rei thought... and suddenly realized.

"Evidence? Right. If we can't do anything to them because we lack evidence, doesn't that mean they can't do anything to us either if they lack evidence?"

It wasn't exactly a groundbreaking reversal of perspective.

But for Rei, whose nature leaned more toward offense than defense, taking action himself was undoubtedly the better approach.

He'd provoke Precious with actions that left no trace, unsettling them.

Vihera nodded happily at Rei's suggestion.

But Marina put the brakes on Rei's words.

"We still can't be certain if Precious really set this up. What if we do exactly as Rei suggests and confront Precious... only to find out we were wrong? That would be a disaster."

"So... you mean there's a possibility Tris is lying?"

"Yes. I don't think we should dismiss that possibility. That's precisely why we need concrete evidence. Besides... what if the Spier Trading Company isn't involved at all in this matter?"

Given how everything has fallen into place, that possibility is impossible.

That's what Rei thought, but when he realized Marina was voicing it even though she understood that too, he had no choice but to hold his tongue for the moment.

"But then what do we do? Just sit here and take it one-sidedly while waiting for them to make a move? We could get attacked like Azimov any time now!"

"Of course, I'm not saying all of Rei's ideas are bad. If there's no evidence, they'll hesitate to make a move against us. ...But the problem is the level of provocation we're applying to them."

Marina explained.

It's about applying pressure without inflicting decisive damage on them, keeping actual harm to a minimum.

In other words, Rei's plan involved inflicting direct damage on them, while Marina's plan involved inflicting psychological damage.

"Psychologically... like what?"

"Well, like when Precious is walking, deliberately passing right in front of them, then stopping and staring intently at Precious."

"...I hate that idea."

Pamidor, listening beside them, imagined the scene and muttered with disgust.

If Precious was behind this incident, it would cause significant psychological damage.

At the very least, if Pamidor were subjected to something like that, given his family situation, he'd have no choice but to take some kind of action.

"If that's the case, how about not directly harming Precious? Instead, attacking right beside where Precious is walking? Something like that?"

What crossed Rei's mind was using Flying Slash on the ground right beside Precious as she walked down the street.

It wasn't a direct attack, but rather striking the ground where Precious was walking...

"We'd better not do that. If by any chance this whole thing wasn't Precious' doing, there'd be no excuse for it."

"...Is it really necessary to think that far ahead? Either way, it's certain the Spier Trading Company is involved in this incident, right?"

"That's just Tris's opinion. No, I'm sure they're involved in some way, but since we can't be certain, it's best not to do anything that creates a debt with them."

After that, Rei, Vihera, and Marina tossed around various ideas, but ultimately concluded it was best to avoid any actions that could lead to injury, even indirectly.
