"Hmm... hmm, hmm... hmm..."

After hearing everything Rei had done in the Earl of Relduct's domain, Daskar let out a low groan while seated on the sofa.

Watching the pondering Daskar, Rei reached for the tea the maid had brought mid-explanation.

He had already drunk tea while waiting in the reception room before meeting Daskar, but Rei still had enough composure left to enjoy a delicious cup of tea.

As Rei sipped his tea, Daskar, finally ceasing his grumbling, spoke up.

"Hmm, I think you did well. Though cutting off Count Lerdact's arms and destroying the mine might seem a bit excessive, overall it's a thoroughly satisfactory result."

Daskar was usually quick to make decisions, but Rei's explanation this time presented some difficult points to judge.

However, Ray hadn't taken Lerdact's life as Daskar had demanded, and he had also been careful not to take any lives among the rebels.

Considering those circumstances, Daskar's demands had been met in full.

In fact, they had even fulfilled the demands made by members of the Neutral Faction other than Daskar.

Given that, Daskar had absolutely no grounds for complaint.

Admittedly, he certainly wished things could have been handled a bit more smoothly... but that was merely a wishful thought.

Rei possesses extremely high combat prowess, but precisely because of that, her battles tend to be flashy.

His proficiency with long-handled weapons like the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear likely stems from this tendency.

Moreover, this request was purely retaliation.

It was necessary to show those who were about to lay a hand on Gilm that the series of events in the Lerdact County were the work of Rei... or more accurately, the work done according to the will of his client, Daskar.

While this retaliation might seem overly elaborate and excessive for a simple revenge, it would certainly deter anyone from carelessly targeting Gilm in the future.

That said, even in such circumstances, one couldn't definitively say no one would dare make a move. Perhaps that was a testament to human greed.

That's how compelling the city of Gilm is.

As the sole frontier in the Kingdom of Mireana, it draws vast sums of gold, rare materials, and skilled adventurers.

While officially classified as a town on paper, its actual scale is that of a quasi-city.

And with this expansion project, it will undoubtedly reach full city status.

But even at that scale... or rather, precisely because of it, it must be made crystal clear that meddling with the Daskar is dangerous.

"Hearing you say that helps me too. ...So, I can keep the magic items we took from Lerdact, right?"

"Of course. ...Huh? Why the disappointed look? Collecting magic items is your hobby, isn't it?"

Daskar knew full well that collecting magic items was Rei's hobby.

That's precisely why he suggested letting Rei keep all the magic items seized from Lerdact... yet despite that, Rei didn't look particularly pleased.

"What's wrong? If something's bothering you, tell me. I owe you a lot, Rei. I can be flexible about this."

Daskar also knew just how capable Rei was... no, how excessively capable he was.

The item box, the high mobility provided by Set, the formidable combat power Rei and Set possess... Beyond that, if you started listing the finer points, there'd be no end to it.

That's precisely why it wouldn't be strange for Rei to want to help and find a solution if Rei had any worries.

Under Daskar's gaze, Rei opened his mouth, still wearing a troubled expression.

"Actually, it's about the magic items we took from Relduct..."

Saying this, Rei placed several magic items taken from the Misty Ring onto the table.

A jewel box with a music box function, a painting whose background scenery changed as magic power was infused into it, and so on.

"Well, for him, these are rather tasteful items. So, what's the deal? If you want me to buy them, I'd be happy to."

"No, that's not it."

Rei interrupted Daskar's words and continued.

"What I'm collecting are purely functional magic items. Like that magic tent I received as payment from you before, Daskar-sama. But these kinds of magic items that are more like art pieces... honestly..."

...I don't really want them, he thought.

Daskar nodded in understanding at Rei's explanation.

To adventurer Rei, these kinds of artistic items probably looked like nothing more than things to sell for cash.

But Rei already possessed enough money to live comfortably for the rest of his life, even if he included family, lovers, and friends.

Of course, having it is better than not having it, but if Rei wanted to, he could easily earn gold coins or platinum coins.

In fact, even light gold coins, which were worth more than platinum coins, weren't impossible to earn.

Compared to that, the kind of practical magic items Rei truly desires aren't things you can simply buy with gold.

Most magic items could probably be bought with gold, but the truly precious ones weren't something you could easily get just by paying.

"Ah... Sorry about that. I'll add a magic item you want to the reward for this job. Also, if you don't want this magic item, I can buy it from you. What do you say?"

Even if it was a magic item Rei had no particular use for and found uninteresting, it was different for Daskar.

Daskar wasn't particularly knowledgeable about art either, but as the Marquis of Larks, he needed to display this sort of artwork.

Not everyone who came to the lord's manor cared nothing for appearances like Rei.

He needed to put on a show for the merchants who visited almost daily lately, or for the nobles of his own faction. And when it came to nobles from other factions and their envoys, appearances became even more crucial.

That's precisely why Daskar's office is adorned with paintings by several renowned artists, and the doors themselves are practically works of art.

In that sense, Daskar had actually wanted to purchase Lerdact's art collection, which showed a certain taste, but Rei shook his head.

"No, I think I'll just hang this at Marina's place."

"...Oh, I see. I understand."

Daskar heard Rei's reply and brushed it off like that.

For Daskar, Marina knew about various incidents from his childhood... and his youth. She was someone he could never defy.

No, if Marina were to harbor ill will toward Gilm and the Larks Marquisate and act on it, Daskar certainly wouldn't follow her lead.

But Daskar believed Marina would never do such a thing.

(Though that probably depended on Rei's attitude too)

Daskar looked at Rei again.

Daskar knew full well that Marina harbored affection... no, deep love for Rei.

He couldn't help but marvel at how someone like Rei could make 'that' Marina fall so deeply for him. On that point alone, he even held deep respect for Rei, who was practically his son's age.

Marina, the very embodiment of feminine allure—or more accurately, dark elf allure—naturally had countless men pursuing her.

But as far as Daskar knew, she had never once reciprocated any of them.

Despite her appearance, she was a woman of strong moral principles. That was the impression held by many who knew Marina's character, including Daskar himself.

That was precisely why, upon learning Marina loved Rei, Daskar felt utterly incredulous.

At the same time, hearing that the woman of his first love had fallen for a man young enough to be his son delivered an indescribable shock.

That said, Daskar had already completely resolved his feelings for Marina. He harbors no particular thoughts toward Rei.

If anything, he respected him.

"Daskar-sama?"

"Hm? Ah, nothing."

He suddenly realized he was looking at Rei with respect in his eyes and immediately shook his head to dispel that thought.

Actually feeling respect for Rei and showing it on his face were two very different things.

As the one standing at the pinnacle of Gilm, such a thing was simply impossible.

Taking a small, deep breath to compose himself, Daskar spoke.

"I see. If you plan to display this at Marina's house, I won't say anything. But if you ever decide to dispose of it, remember me."

"Hmph, that's fine by me."

Rei couldn't understand why Daskar was so attached to this magic item.

To Rei, who knew little about art, it seemed like something only good for selling for cash or giving as a gift to someone.

As they chatted for a while longer, a knock sounded at the door.

Daskar gave permission for entry, and one of the butlers working in the mansion entered the room.

"Master Daskar, it's nearly time for your next meeting."

"Hm? Oh, is it that time already? ...Sorry, Rei. I really would have liked to talk with you longer, but I have work to do. Even if it's with a worthless person, I need to speak with them."

Daskar recalled the merchant who had been yelled at and kicked out of his office when Rei had visited earlier.

(Something about lowering the quality of materials for Gilm's expansion and pocketing the difference, I think.)

An irresponsible magistrate might have gone along with such a proposal.

But this is Daskar's stronghold, the guild he rules directly, Gilm.

Naturally, his attachment to the city runs deep. The fact that the Neutral Faction manages to function as one of the three major powers is largely because they possess the city of Gilm.

Given that, while he might consider a proposal to strengthen Gilm's defenses, he certainly wouldn't accept one that might weaken them.

(Even if you're going to propose something corrupt, you should pick your targets more carefully, I think.)

Thinking this way, Daskar felt a touch of pity for having to deal with someone who would make such a proposal.

If you're a noble... no, precisely because you're a noble, things rarely go your way.

(Thinking about it that way, I'd pass on becoming nobility myself. Well, I doubt anyone crazy enough to make me a noble would exist anyway.)

Rei mused casually while watching Daskar, though in reality, there were quite a few who coveted making him a noble.

If Rei were to say he wanted to be a noble, he could probably become one immediately.

...Though what awaited him then would be a mountain of incredibly tedious work.

"Well then, I'll take my leave here. I need to report back to the guild."

"Ah. ...Here, this is the sign for completing the request."

Taking the documents handed to him, Rei left the room.

Then, the merchant waiting by the door fixed a stern gaze on Rei.

He probably didn't like that Ray had met with Daskar instead of him.

Though strictly speaking, since Rei had squeezed in during Daskar's free time, it wasn't as if the merchant had lost any of his own meeting time with Daskar.

In the end, the merchant said nothing. Told by the maid to go inside, he entered the office.

(Well, I'm not carrying Set with me right now, and I don't have the Death Scythe, so he probably didn't think I was that famous adventurer. Still, spotting a business opportunity like that is what makes a good merchant, I suppose.)

Rei quickly forgot about the merchant who had entered the office and asked another maid to guide him to where Set was.

At the place they arrived at, just as usual, the maids, butlers, cooks, and various other staff working in the mansion were doting on Set while feeding him all sorts of food.

"Guruu!"

But when Set saw Rei, he let out a happy growl, stood up, and started running toward her.

Those who had been doting on him felt a pang of regret at his departure, yet they were soothed by the sight of him frolicking happily upon seeing Rei.
