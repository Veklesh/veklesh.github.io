The dishes prepared on the table were exceptionally delicious for Rei.

Byune ate with an appetite that matched Rei's, while Marina and Vihera both savored their food properly, though with good manners.

The dishes brought out weren't anything extravagant.

They were mostly simple, easy-to-eat items like skewers, stir-fries, stews, and sandwiches.

The only thing that surprised Rei a little was that there were steamed buns among the dishes brought out.

Of course, meat buns weren't exclusively a winter food.

Well, in Rei's mind, they were definitely a winter food, but the cook at Gilm, unaware of that, prepared them as something you could eat anytime.

Was it just Rei being a sourpuss that, while eating those meat buns, he found himself thinking he'd rather have pizza?

The pizza oven Rei had brought was already stored inside the Misty Ring.

This was an extremely rare item for Rei, and moreover, it was a magic item.

He'd left it behind when the Treant Forest attack happened so suddenly, but he'd made sure to retrieve it properly before defeating the Giganto Turtle and returning to Gilm.

(Grilled meat buns... I think I saw something like that somewhere before. Maybe I'll try making them sometime?)

Thinking such thoughts, Rei brought the meat bun to his mouth... and was surprised.

He had assumed the filling inside the meat bun would be a paste of minced meat and vegetables, but instead, it contained a substantial chunk of meat that offered a satisfying bite.

Rei couldn't tell how it was cooked, but the meat, cut like cubed steak, released rich juices into his mouth with each bite.

Combined with the bun's dough, it offered a taste quite different from any meat bun Rei had known before.

"Ha ha ha. Well? Surprised?"

Daskar, who had been watching Rei eat the bun, declared cheerfully.

"...Yeah. It's pretty different from the meat buns I know."

"Exactly, exactly. Actually, a few of our chefs tried meat buns during winter and were quite shocked by them. So they researched various things, and one of the dishes they created was that meat bun."

Rei had been asked by Golden Bread Inn to teach them how to make pork buns. ...Though, since Rei didn't actually know the precise method, all he could do was share his vague recollections of such a dish existing.

Anyway, it was essentially a meat bun co-developed by the Golden Bread Inn and Rei, but like the udon, the recipe was shared with the cooks at Gilm.

...One major reason for sharing the recipe was likely that the Golden Bread Inn alone couldn't meet the demand from customers seeking meat buns.

In any case, meat buns... and pizza too, are now widespread throughout Gilm.

Considering that spring had arrived and many merchants were coming to Gilm, it was certain that these dishes, like udon, would eventually spread throughout the Kingdom of Mireana... and the surrounding nations.

No, unlike udon, which requires bowls to eat, meat buns and pizza don't particularly need tableware.

Considering they can be eaten casually, like fast food, Rei believed they would spread more easily than udon.

In any case, the tense atmosphere from just moments ago seemed like a distant memory as a peaceful time flowed by.

Rei and the others savored the food, Daskar beamed happily as he listened to their reactions, and Walker shared bits of knowledge he possessed.

Such a pleasant time passed... until eventually, every dish brought to the table had found its way into the stomachs of those present.

Daskar called the maid again, having her clear away the now-empty dishes and prepare tea for after the meal.

With that, things finally settled down, and Walker spoke up.

"Well, now that we've finished eating, shall we get down to business?"

"Yes. While we're no exception, I imagine Daskar and the guild members don't have the luxury of relaxing either, do they?"

Marina's words, spoken over tea, drew a small sigh from Daskar.

It was awkward dealing with someone who had known him since childhood.

...Especially since, even if it was youthful folly, he had actually proposed to her.

(What on earth was I thinking back then?)

If my childhood self were standing before me now, I'd never let him do something like that.

Thinking this, Daskar opened his mouth, careful not to let his thoughts show on his face.

"Well, Rei and the others defeated Giganto Turtle, right? I do want to check how the Treant Forest is faring after that."

"...Yeah."

At Daskar's words, Marina's expression grew slightly sad.

No one died in the battle against the Giganto Turtle.

But... in the battle against the monsters that came from Treant Forest before that, it wasn't as if there had been absolutely no casualties.

Especially...

"Daskar, Walker, I'd like to ask you something... You're considering using wood from Treant Forest as building materials for Gilm's expansion, right?"

"Hm? Ah. That's correct... but is there a problem with that?"

"Well, yes. Not exactly a problem... but you do know we fought monsters that emerged from Treant Forest before battling the Giganto Turtle, right?"

"I heard about that from the ones who came here seeking reinforcements."

"...Then you know this too? Among the monsters that appeared in Treant Forest was one that was a giant flower monster."

"I know. I've heard that too. ...I won't touch those who were killed by that monster... no, turned into trees. Of course, if the relatives, lovers, friends, or comrades of those trees try to do something to them, I won't say anything either."

Turned into trees.

Put simply, it means a living human was forcibly turned into a tree.

Their family, lover, friend, comrade... such people wouldn't want to keep the one turned into a tree alive against their will. They'd want to put them out of their misery.

It wouldn't be strange if some felt that way.

Also, if a household had a somewhat large garden, some might choose to plant the tree in their yard, deciding to live alongside it.

The Daskar had no intention of interfering in such matters, leaving them to those involved.

"The fortunate thing is that adventurers hardly ever entered the Treant Forest. ...If adventurers had been able to enter the Treant Forest freely and become trees..."

Marina's words drew expressions of "Whoa..." from everyone.

In reality, even Rei and the others probably didn't know how many adventurers had actually entered Treant Forest. But at least as far as they were aware, the only party clearly known to have died in Treant Forest was Slesha's.

Rei had found other remains before, but in the end, they never knew whose party they belonged to... or even if they had truly been wiped out.

(Thinking about it that way, Slesha must feel complicated.)

Slesha watched her comrades get devoured by the Treant Forest right before her eyes.

Since she fled, she'll never know what ultimately became of them.

And precisely because she doesn't know, she can't help but wonder which of the trees growing in the Treant Forest is the remains of her comrades.

"I digress. ...Anyway, the timber from Treant Forest was the perfect material for expanding Gilm."

"But since we defeated the embodiment of the Treant Forest, does that mean the forest can no longer expand?"

"That's how it is."

Daskar nodded at Rei's words, but even seeing that, Rei didn't seem particularly apologetic.

After all, it was only natural to defeat the monster that attacked us.

Above all, that monster had targeted Rei specifically.

Thanks to that, no major damage occurred. But if the Giganto-Turtle had rampaged targeting anyone other than Rei... especially if it had rampaged inside Gilm... who knows how much damage it would have caused?

(Ah, but there is a barrier. ...Though whether that barrier is effective against the Giganto Turtle is another matter.)

To begin with, even though it's called a Giganto Turtle, it's not actually an ordinary monster.

Given that, whether Gilm's barrier against monsters would be effective is a bit of a gray area.

"Even with the current size of Treant Forest, is it insufficient for Gilm's expansion?"

"Well... it's a bit of a gray area. Probably, if you think about it normally, it should be enough."

"Then isn't that fine?"

Marina interjected at Rei and Daskar's words.

"I think it'll suffice for now, but that's only under the current circumstances. As you understand, Marina, when pursuing such a large-scale project, it's better to have as much leeway as possible."

"True. But originally, the Treant Forest didn't even exist, right? We were considering expanding Gilm even under those circumstances. Isn't it better to make the most of what we have now?"

"...I understand that. But that Treant Forest, dangerous though it is... no, precisely because it's dangerous, it's undeniably an incredibly appealing prospect."

Daskar said this with a small sigh, then exhaled again to shift his mood.

"For now, we'll need to look into Treant Forest later. If no monsters appear after the Giganto Turtle Rei defeated, it'll make things easier for us to move around."

"That would be a great help to us too."

"Now, about what happened in Treant Forest..."

Asked by Daskar, Rei began recounting his experience in Treant Forest.

What particularly caught Daskar and Walker's interest was the image language used by the tree root doll.

It was an unknown language, and Walker, as Guildmaster, was likely intrigued.

Daskar, on the other hand, wondered if this might increase the chances of taming monsters.

Of course, taming is a special skill in many ways.

Or rather, it's less a skill and more about innate talent and personality.

Even if the secret of the imagery language were revealed, whether that would mean one could actually tame... Rei would probably tilt his head in doubt.

It's possible. But that possibility is as thin as a spider's thread.

(Well, in my case, I'm not really a tamer anyway, at least not outwardly, so I can't say for sure.)

While pondering Taming, Rei answered questions from Daskar and Walker.

About an hour passed like this. Having heard the gist of the story, Daskar nodded with satisfaction.

"I see. I understand the general situation. ...For now, we'll just have to wait and see what happens with Treant Forest. Of course, we'll continue logging there in the meantime."

It was unclear whether the trees in Treant Forest would increase any further.

But even so... no, precisely because of that, it was necessary to harvest as many trees as possible now.

Otherwise, there was no telling when someone who noticed its peculiarity might appear in Treant Forest.

"I see. Good luck with that."

But such matters were of no concern to Rei.

He told Daskar this without seeming particularly concerned.

"Um, I'd like to ask Rei for various kinds of help too... Especially for transporting the felled trees, Rei's item box would be incredibly useful. Would it be possible to request your services like before?"

Rei's Misty Ring is a significant asset for transporting timber.

Without Rei, they'd have to hire people to transport it, and who knows how much that would cost.

Furthermore, while Treant Forest is relatively close to Gilm, there's still a possibility monsters might appear.

If that happens, we'd need to hire adventurers as escorts too, requiring even more funds.

Considering all that, having the Misty Ring, taming a flying monster like Set, and possessing personal strength worthy of a nickname makes it perfectly feasible.

Of course, paying Rei would be costly, but it would still be cheaper than assembling an entire transport crew, including guards.

Rei, meeting that hopeful gaze, thought for a moment before nodding.

"Well... I can't promise anything, but if I'm free, I'll take it. As for payment, flame ore would be appreciated if possible."

"Yes, of course we'll accommodate your preference. ...Ah, right. Speaking of which, I believe you can rank up to Rank A for the Giganto Turtle matter... What do you say?"

Walker shot Rei a meaningful look, but...

"Nah, all that etiquette and stuff is a pain, and besides, it seems like it'd just drag me into all sorts of other troubles. I'll pass. I'm not really struggling at Rank B anyway."

he declared.
