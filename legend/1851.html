The day after the party, Rei had gone to bed late, so he ended up waking up much later than usual.

Miranda woke him up, and after getting ready, Rei—having nothing in particular to do—decided to wander around the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion after breakfast.

Incidentally, Miranda had firmly assured him that she would return the clothes he had worn to the party—which she had picked up before he went to bed the night before—in perfect condition at a later date.

For Rei, wearing those clothes meant attending a party hosted by the nobility, so while he appreciated Miranda’s kindness, he felt conflicted.

That said, there was no doubt that he had ultimately expressed his gratitude.

And so, for now, he finds himself like this...

“Grr! Grrrrrrr!”

He was enjoying a walk with Set, who was purring happily.

As long as Set was with her, she naturally couldn’t just walk around inside the mansion. …That said, considering the size of the Kerebel Duke’s estate, there was plenty of room for Set to roam, but it was only natural that she wouldn’t want to go inside, just in case something happened.

Also, no matter how vast the Duke of Kerebel’s estate is, and even if Set could walk around freely, if something were to happen, it could easily turn into a repeat of what happened when we demolished the stables to repel the attackers who had previously raided us.

I suppose I think that way because I’m still dwelling on what’s happened so far.

(Besides, Set seems to be having more fun outside anyway.)

Given his large frame, Set probably prefers places where he doesn’t have to worry about those around him.

…That said, Set does enjoy playing with people, too.

“I wonder if Yellow is sleeping with Elena right about now?”

“Grr…”

In response to Rei’s words, Set growls in agreement.

Ever since he was welcomed as a guest at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, Yellow has spent quite a bit of time with Set.

Even when they were in Gilm, Yelo had spent a fair amount of time with Set, one way or another… but surely the fact that this is the Kerebel Duke’s mansion in Anesis—in other words, Yelo’s home—has something to do with it.

From Yellow’s perspective, it was as if Set had come over to his own home.

And since this was the first time, there was no way Yellow wouldn’t welcome Set.

That said, Yellow is still just a child.

It’s fun to be with Set, her favorite friend—or rather, her best friend—but it was only natural that she’d want to spend time with Elena, the woman who gave birth to her, her mother, so to speak.

“Set, did you have fun being with Yellow, too?”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a happy meow.

Although Set adored Rei, it was only natural that he had other people he liked besides Rei.

That’s exactly why he was so happy to have been able to play with Yellow.

As they walked, exchanging such banter, a sudden, spirited shout rang out.

Since Rei could hear it, it was only natural that Set could hear it too, but Set didn’t seem to be paying it any mind.

Rei could tell he was ignoring it, so he didn’t make a big deal of it and simply followed Set toward the source of the voice.

(That voice sounds unfamiliar. At the very least, I can be sure it’s not from the Kerebel Duke’s Knights. And above all, it’s high-pitched… or rather, childish?)

Rei didn’t remember every single voice in the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights perfectly, but he had sparred with nearly all of them.

Given that, if the voice belonged to someone speaking with such intensity, it wouldn’t have been strange for him to recognize who it was the moment he heard it.

“Well, I wonder who it is.”

It wasn’t as if he had any particular business with the person whose voice it was.

Not exactly… but there was no doubt that his curiosity had been piqued.

(A guard? No, well, guards do train, I suppose. But the voice I’m hearing is clearly just one person’s.)

If the guards were training, it would be strange if I didn’t hear the voices of several… or even dozens of them, depending on the situation.

But the only voice they could hear coming from the direction Rei and Set were heading was that of a single person.

As they walked through the gardens of Duke Kerebel’s mansion… eventually, the scene came into view.

As expected, what came into Rei’s view was a single man.

No, rather than a man, it would be better to describe him as a boy—or perhaps even a child.

From what Rei could see, he appeared to be around ten years old.

He certainly didn’t look like a guard, and considering that his voice hadn’t broken yet, it made sense why the high-pitched voice they’d heard belonged to him.

That said, even for a child whose voice hadn’t broken yet, the way he was practicing showed he was taking it seriously.

It was clear to an outside observer that this was no mere child’s play.

“Heh, yaa, tooh!”

The child shouted with determination as he thrust the spear in his hand forward sharply.

His movements were far from polished; in fact, “clumsy” would be a more fitting description.

But... even so, their expression is utterly serious, demonstrating just how committed they are to their spear training.

“Guruu?”

Watching the child, Set growls at Rei.

His attitude seemed to ask, “Are you still here?”

From Set’s perspective, it was only natural to think, “Since we’re here anyway, I’d like to take a longer walk with Rei.”

“Ah, yeah. Well then… hmm?”

“Well, let’s go somewhere else, then.”

Rei was about to say that when he suddenly noticed that the child’s voice he’d been hearing for a while had fallen silent.

Rei casually glanced in that direction and saw a child staring at him with eyes that were, for some reason, shining brightly.

That gaze was filled with intense admiration.

He couldn’t help but wonder, “Why is he looking at me like that?” but then he reconsidered: given that he was a person with a nickname, it wasn’t really that strange to be looked at that way.

“U-um, you’re Crimson Rei, aren’t you!”

“That’s right. People sometimes call me that.”

The fact that the child recognized the person in front of him as Rei was likely due to Set’s presence.

With the hood of his Dragon Robe pulled up, it was difficult for an outsider to recognize him as Rei.

But seeing Set standing right next to him, anyone who’d heard the rumors about “Crimson Rei” would recognize him instantly.

……Though, even if one had heard the rumors about Rei, whether or not they would simply believe them was another matter entirely.

Fortunately, though, the child looking at Rei with admiration seemed to have no ulterior motives.

“Um… I saw that mock battle the other day. It was amazing!”

He said he’d seen the mock battle, and here he was, practicing with a spear in the garden of Duke Kerebel’s mansion.

Given that, it goes without saying who this child is.

If it were just a matter of having seen the mock battle, it wouldn’t be strange even if he were a resident of Anesis.

But if he’s here, he must naturally be connected to the nobility.

(Perhaps a noble invited to yesterday’s party is staying at this mansion for some reason, and this is their child…?)

Convinced that his guess was likely correct, Rei was about to say something in response, but at that moment, Set looked behind him.

Startled by this sudden action, Rei stopped himself from speaking and turned his gaze in that direction.

Yet he didn’t seem on guard, likely because Set was acting as usual.

Seeing Set and Rei’s actions, the child looked puzzled and followed their gaze, but saw nothing there.

But… a few seconds later. Soon, the sound of someone running could be heard.

「Leodanis？」

Yes, it was undoubtedly Leodanis.

Among the Kerebel Duke’s Knights, he was the one closest to Rei… or at least, it wouldn’t be strange to say so.

Leodanis approached Rei and the others, breathing slightly heavily.

“Seriously, it’s fine to go for a walk, but you really need to tell someone which way you’re heading—otherwise it’s a problem.”

Speaking with a hint of annoyance, Leodanis approached Rei and the others.

Watching Leodanis like that, Rei suddenly turned his gaze toward the child.

There was no doubt that this child, who had been practicing with a spear all this time, was far more exhausted than Leodanis, who had just jogged over.

The child, caught in Rei’s gaze, didn’t understand why he was being looked at that way. He tilted his head, struggling to suppress his labored breathing.

The child probably didn’t want to show any weakness in front of Rei, but even if he could manage to hold back his heavy breathing, the steam rising from his body was impossible to hide.

(Huh? Is that what you call it in this situation, too?)

Rei was suddenly struck by that question, but Leodanis spoke up to him.

“Can I talk to you for a sec? There’s something I think you should know.”

“...I’ve got a bad feeling about this.”

Whether from past experience… or perhaps the influence of the manga, novels, and anime he’d read while in Japan, Rei felt a faint sense of foreboding at Leodanis’s words.

But Rei understood better than anyone that, in this situation, not listening to what he had to say was not an option.

In fact, if he didn’t listen to what Leo Danis had to say now, the worst possible future would surely await him.

Rei had a premonition strong enough to make him certain of that.

Not sure what to make of Rei’s attitude, Leodanis gave a faint smile and asked him.

“So? What are you going to do? I don’t mind just going home as is, you know?”

“...Alright. Tell me what’s going on. If you just leave like this, I’ll probably get dragged into all sorts of trouble later.”

In response to Rei’s surrender, Leodanis smiled contentedly and spoke.

“Hmph, fine then. I’ll tell you.”

Rei felt a slight irritation at Leodanis’s slightly condescending tone, but he decided that listening to Leodanis’s story came first for now, and urged him to continue.

“So? What exactly is this ‘something I should’ve told you’? Judging by your reaction just now, it must be something pretty serious. …If you’ve kept me waiting this long only to tell me something trivial…”

Rei didn’t say anything more, merely giving a wry smile.

But it was precisely that attitude that Leodanis found frightening.

If the information really wasn’t something that piqued Rei’s interest, what on earth would he do?

Was it a mistake to take out his frustration on Rei just because he’d been made to search high and low?

He thought that for a moment, but it was already too late.

No matter what he says now, there’s no turning back in this situation.

“It seems members of the Seisor Marquis family have been quite active since last night. And quite a large number of them have been dispatched.”

“...The Marquis of Seisor?”

Surprising.

That must be the expression currently on Rei’s face.

Come to think of it, it’s not really that surprising, but my first reaction was still one of surprise.

“Yeah. It started after last night’s party ended. To be precise, it seems to have begun after the head of the Seisor family returned to his own mansion… or so I’ve heard. Mind you, this is just hearsay, so I can’t say for certain.”

“Heard through the grapevine, huh. Still, information is information. …Now I understand why they were looking for me.”

The fact that Rei had a run-in with Gaiska of the Seisor Marquis family is fairly well known.

Given that, there was no way Rei wouldn’t be interested in the circumstances surrounding the Seisor Marquis family.

...That said, what exactly happened in the end?

Without knowing the details of that, it’s meaningless.

“So, is there nothing more specific on that front? I’d really like to hear more details, if possible.”

Rei asked, staring intently at Leodanis, but the only response he received was a shake of the head.

Under the current circumstances, no information had been gathered at all.

To begin with, the fact that the Marquis of Seisor’s family acted in secret makes this whole affair far too suspicious.

They must have understood that, which is likely why they didn’t want anyone else to find out about their actions.

That said, in the end, word has reached Rei’s ears anyway.

“Did things pick up after Zascal returned to his estate? If so, the only possible explanation is…”

Rei sorted through the various pieces of information in his mind.

Zascal himself seemed to want a relationship with me.

Given that, if they’re operating on the scale Leodanis described… there aren’t many possibilities.

And above all, the most troublesome possibility in the current situation is...

“Has Gaiska, who’s under house arrest, managed to escape?”

Half by intuition, Rei arrived at the correct answer.

...That said, it was merely a hunch, and Rei had no way of knowing whether it was actually true.
