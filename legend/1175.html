The first to lunge at Rei was the smallest individual among the werewolves.

Not as a sacrificial pawn to gauge Rei's strength... but simply because it possessed that level of power.

Its speed was completely different from when it had attacked the Cyclops.

Of course, it wasn't that he'd been holding back against the Cyclops; it was purely a matter of size—whether the target worth defeating was the Cyclops or Rei.

Rather than relying on the power of a single blow, it focused on speed for a hit-and-run tactic.

That was the tactic the small werewolf aimed at Rei. But...

"That kind of speed won't cut it."

The werewolf tried to slash through with its claws, but Rei wielded the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear.

Both were long-handled weapons, giving Rei an overwhelming advantage in range compared to the claw attack.

The werewolf likely intended to slip through Rei's attack and strike back.

But that move could only be described as underestimating Rei's physical abilities.

Rei thrust the Twilight Spear in his left hand forward sharply.

Normally, it would have evaded that strike with minimal effort, closing the distance as it pulled the spear back.

That was likely the plan... but only if the werewolf could react to the speed of Rei's thrusting spear.

"Gah!"

The thrust from the Twilight Spear released from Rei's hand exceeded the speed at which the werewolf could react.

The spear strike shattered its skull, and the impact drew a guttural scream from the werewolf's mouth.

Furthermore, the horizontal slash from the Death Scythe, released by Rei a split second after the Twilight Spear strike, severed the torso of the werewolf whose head had been crushed. It collapsed to the ground, still carrying the momentum of its charge.

The impact sent the upper and lower halves flying in opposite directions, spraying vast amounts of blood, entrails, and bodily fluids from the severed surfaces onto the surrounding area.

Watching the werewolf's corpse... or rather, what could be called its remains, Rei's brow furrowed slightly.

Originally, he had intended to unleash the Death Scythe's horizontal slash simultaneously with the Twilight Spear thrust. However, his body movement for the initial thrust was too large, causing the Death Scythe strike to be a split second late.

Against this particular werewolf, it hadn't been a problem. But against a more skilled opponent... someone with the skill level of Vihera, for instance, exploiting that split-second opening wouldn't be surprising.

(My two-spear style still has a long way to go. That's precisely why I'm doing this kind of practical training against werewolves.)

One real battle is worth ten days of practice.

While the exact ratio may vary, Rei doesn't fully buy into that oft-repeated saying.

Still, he knew there were things you couldn't grasp without actual combat experience. And while it might not be worth ten days of practice, it was undeniably solid training.

That's precisely why he was challenging the werewolf with the Two-Spear Style.

Moreover, the reason he was using the Two-Spear Style... or more accurately, the Death Scythe, was largely because a Cyclops had been fighting a werewolf here.

The surrounding trees had been snapped to some extent by the impact, creating the spatial freedom necessary to wield the Death Scythe.

Of course, it's not like there wasn't a single tree left standing, so I couldn't swing the Death Scythe completely freely.

But that too was something that could easily happen from now on when using the Two-Spear Style.

Thinking that way, this too could be considered good training for Rei in the Two-Spear Style.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAARRRRGH!"

The exchange just now must have made them realize Rei's strength was far from something to be underestimated.

The werewolf leader, holding back behind, let out a high, piercing howl.

Hearing the howl, the werewolves took it as their signal and charged at Rei all at once.

They displayed the agility characteristic of werewolves and coordinated with their comrades.

Knowing they couldn't defeat Rei alone, they likely decided to exploit the werewolves' strength in numbers to overwhelm him.

That strategy was not wrong.

In fact, using that tactic, the werewolf pack fought the Cyclops almost evenly, despite suffering casualties.

But... Rei and the Cyclops were in a completely different league.

The werewolf that attacked Rei first wasn't slow by any means.

In fact, being smaller, it had superior speed compared to the others.

Despite that, Rei's Twilight Spear struck without giving even such a swift werewolf time to dodge, taking its life in a single blow.

Considering that, it was undoubtedly difficult for a werewolf to outmaneuver Rei with speed alone.

"Whoa... Ha!"

Rei dodged the attacks from the werewolves assaulting him from all directions—literally all directions—and even from above, barely moving from his spot.

Then, in the instant their coordination faltered... seizing a fleeting opening that most would have missed, he swung the Death Scythe.

The wolf whose movement faltered paid for that mistake with its life.

Like the first one that attacked, it was severed at the torso and died instantly, scattering blood, flesh, and entrails around it.

The more precise the machinery, the more fragile it is.

It was somewhat similar to how, precisely because they possessed advanced coordination, the rhythm of their teamwork would falter if even one were killed.

Seeing the werewolf killed by the Death Scythe's strike, the others... weren't exactly shaken, but their coordination did falter.

It lasted less than a second, but for Rei, that split second was more than enough to launch his next move.

"Flying Slash!"

Seizing the initiative before the werewolf could attack him, he unleashed the Flying Slash.

The flying slash left wounds on the werewolf's body that could be called fatal.

Simultaneously, he threw the Twilight Spear held in his left hand, infusing it with magic.

Though thrown with only the strength of his arm, the tip of the Twilight Spear, infused with magic, pierced the torso of the werewolf Rei aimed for. It then pierced the torsos of the two other werewolves behind it in the same manner, embedding itself into a tree trunk.

Though the Twilight Spear had pierced through three bodies, the force of the throw had barely diminished, and the tip was completely embedded in the tree trunk.

Normally, pulling a spear completely embedded in a tree trunk would be quite difficult.

At the very least, pulling it out without showing an opening in the midst of battle like this would be difficult.

But... that was only true for an ordinary spear.

The spear stuck in the tree trunk was the Twilight Spear.

Rei believed it was likely one of the most powerful magic items in this world, and as such, this was nothing to him.

And indeed, the Twilight Spear, its tip completely embedded in the tree trunk, appeared in Rei's hand the very next moment.

One of the Twilight Spear's abilities was the power to return to its wielder whenever desired.

Seeing several werewolves killed instantly, or at least severely wounded, the pack leader emerged from the rear of the pack.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Enraged that his subordinates, or perhaps comrades, had been injured, the werewolf leader let out a deeply menacing growl directed at Rei.

Under normal circumstances, hearing such a growl would be enough to make anyone cower in fear.

But how many people would consider Rei ordinary?

In fact, holding both the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear in his two-spear style, Rei simply used the strength in his neck to pull off his hood. He flashed a savage grin at the Werewolf Leader.

"What's the matter? Come on then?"

Seeing Rei's face, the Werewolf Leader's feet halted for an instant, and he uttered those words in response.

But, perhaps sensing caution in Rei's demeanor, the Werewolf Leader merely kept his distance, watching Rei intently.

(Huh... Rare, I guess. I thought he'd just charge in without a shred of composure once the fighting started... This was completely unexpected.)

In terms of fighting, a simpler opponent would have been preferable.

But an enemy like this, one who actually faces him and gauges his strength, was actually convenient for Rei right now.

(An enemy who's reasonably strong yet doesn't underestimate me... In that sense, he's perfect as a practice partner for my Two-Spear Style.)

The surrounding werewolves showed no sign of interfering in the fight between their leader and Rei.

They believed the werewolf leader would win without fail.

But the very werewolf leader they believed in couldn't bring himself to move against Rei.

Rei held a large scythe and a spear in each hand—weapons that looked notoriously awkward to wield.

By all logic, he should be a favorable opponent for the agile werewolves.

But from the leader's perspective, Rei showed no signs of weakness.

The Wolf Leader had a strong feeling that if he attacked, he would be countered immediately.

"Grrrrrrr"

The werewolf leader growled, his throat vibrating with caution.

Several minutes passed with neither of them moving.

Yet those minutes held entirely different meanings for Rei and the Werewolf Leader.

While Rei was merely waiting for the opponent to make a move, the Wolf Leader strained to find an opening in Rei's defense, its mental fatigue weighing heavily upon it.

If the Warwolf Leader were a more powerful monster, exploiting the gaps in Rei's still-unmastered Two-Spear Style might not have been difficult. Unfortunately, the current Warwolf Leader couldn't find any openings.

If he wasted any more time like this, he would lose. In other words... die.

Thinking this, he felt himself being pushed further into a mental corner... and then, the moment the tension peaked, the Werewolf Leader kicked off the ground and closed the distance to Rei.

It wasn't a move born of any calculated advantage, but simply one born of an inability to withstand the tension any longer.

Leveraging his larger frame than a typical werewolf, he closed the distance, aiming to drive his claws—sharper and larger than a werewolf's—into Rei.

In response, Rei greeted the Werewolf Leader with a smile, as if a perfect practice opponent had arrived.

The first to attack Rei was a small werewolf.

In terms of speed alone, it was undoubtedly superior to the others. Yet the werewolf leader, seemingly a higher-ranking species, possessed a large build and was clearly faster than any werewolf encountered thus far.

Should it slash at Rei's neck, or sink its fangs—a more powerful weapon than claws—into him?

Hesitating over which to choose, it closed the distance with Rei...

"Gyaaah!"

Just as it closed in close enough to bring Rei within claw range, it was struck by some impact and let out a reflexive scream.

Even so, it regained its balance midair and landed on the ground—a testament to the werewolf leader's prowess.

...Though it also helped that Rei didn't pursue the attack.

"Gurrrr!?"

He clearly couldn't comprehend what had just happened. The Werewolf Leader looked around in confusion, his gaze landing on the Twilight Spear Rei had swung, now being pulled back to his side.

Only then did it seem to grasp that it had been struck by the Twilight Spear and blown away. Casting an even more wary gaze at Rei than before, it began walking again.

Earlier, it had attacked in a single, frantic rush, driven by impatience.

Given that, it decided to launch its next attack carefully.

Fortunately for the werewolf... and for Rei as well, the tension within the werewolf leader had already dissipated after taking the Twilight Spear's blow.

"What's the matter? Since you're accompanying me for practice, move a little more properly. That can't be your full strength, right?"

"GRRRR... WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Even if he couldn't understand Rei's words, he understood he was being mocked... looked down upon. A roar of self-encouragement erupted from the werewolf leader's mouth.

Simultaneously, it kicked off the ground and charged at Rei at an even higher speed than before.

He didn't care if he took the other's blow; he just had to land his own attack.

With the sharpness of his claws, he should be able to finish Rei off.

In an instant, the Werewolf Leader reached Rei's side and unleashed a strike with its right paw, aimed directly at him.

To deliver a sharper blow, it thrusts its claws forward rather than swinging them.

The thrust aimed straight for Rei's throat... but it collided with the outstretched Twilight Spear, sliding off the shaft.

The clawed strike slid off in an unintended direction... and in that instant when the attack was completely parried, the Death Scythe gripped in Rei's right hand delivered a horizontal slash, severing the leader's torso.
