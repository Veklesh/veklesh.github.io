After explaining the outcome concerning the Hundred-Faced One at Augusto's mansion, Rei immediately set off again on Gosh, heading for the Hundred-Faced One's village.

As expected, the guards gave him disapproving looks as he exited through the main gate, but since the order came from the lord himself, they couldn't refuse.

Thus, once outside Gosh, Rei mounted Set and flew through the night sky over the desert.

Just as when he had come from the Hundred-Faced Ones' village to Gosh, the moon poured its gentle light down upon the desert.

From the sky, the night desert seemed perfectly suited to the word "silence."

Of course, that was likely only because he was riding Set, soaring above it all.

Even in an environment that seemed impossible for life to survive if one actually descended into the desert, many creatures were active there.

Moreover, since the desert also harbors monsters that endure its harsh conditions, it is certainly not devoid of life.

Even Rei, who hadn't been in Gosh for very long, understood that well enough.

(Even in the desert within the dungeon, there were quite a few monsters. ...Or maybe that's precisely because it was inside the dungeon?)

Stroking the neck of Set before her, Rei scanned her surroundings.

Though only a few minutes had passed since they'd taken flight from Gosh, the village of the Hundred-Faced Ones was already visible on the ground below.

"Looks like we didn't get lost after all."

"Gururu!"

Set growled in agreement at Rei's words.

Even accounting for the time spent stopping to talk while walking, the village was less than two hours away.

For Set the griffin, such a distance could be covered in mere minutes, as it had been just now.

...Rei also realized this fact while traveling from the village to Gosh.

He understood that Set's flying speed was incomparably faster than moving on the ground, but walking through the desert... especially walking through the desert at night... was still vastly different.

That was precisely why, when Daldora asked him to come to Gosh again tomorrow morning, he agreed.

Of course, the thought of Resonance crossing his mind was also one reason he accepted the request.

(Sand ships can only be used in the desert, so it's better to use them when you can. Still, the fact that we didn't get lost over this distance... yeah, that's nice.)

For one reason or another, Rei and Set often get lost.

One big reason for that is probably that flying through the air is just too fast compared to walking on the ground.

As I pondered this, Set began descending toward the ground.

"We'll have to figure out something about the barrier too. But... can we even do that? I heard barriers like that basically require a city-sized area to deploy."

Even if we secured a village with an oasis, it'd be meaningless if we couldn't protect it.

No, if monsters or bandits—sand pirates around here—were to attack and destroy it just as we'd acquired the village and were settling people there, the money invested, and above all, human lives, would be lost.

In places like Gosh, money is an issue, but the sparse population is primarily due to the desert environment.

Therefore, we inevitably have to prioritize human lives to a certain extent.

Especially those capable of living in villages and farming using oases—they are extremely rare technicians.

You absolutely couldn't just stand by and let them die.

"Normally, building fences or something would provide some peace of mind... but this is the desert, after all."

Sand Snakes and other monsters that move underground are far from uncommon in the desert.

A village with no defenses whatsoever underground in such a place is practically inviting monsters to feast.

When the Hundred-Faced One lived here, the artifact's barrier somehow managed to handle it.

But... when Rei sliced through that barrier with the Death Scythe, he destroyed the artifact itself.

Rei didn't consider it his mistake, since he couldn't have found the Hundred-Faced One's village as long as that barrier remained.

Still, he couldn't help but feel something when using the village became inconvenient... especially when that inconvenience was nearly fatal.

"You're surprisingly early! I thought it would take you a bit longer."

Zalust, who had been keeping watch on the surroundings, immediately approached Rei as he descended into the village and spoke up.

"Huh? Just Zarust? Where's Narsus?"

"Yeah, Narsas is keeping watch a little ways off. ...It's a small village, but it's impossible for just the two of us to cover the entire perimeter. We still need to watch as wide an area as possible, so we split up."

"Considering powerful monsters might attack, isn't that a bit careless?"

"Well, yeah. But it's only for the short time until Rei and Set return. Even if monsters attack, we should be able to handle it."

Zalust muttered, showing a hint of confidence.

Truthfully, Zarust was confident he could survive whatever happened.

...Though it was certain that the village would suffer some damage as a result.

As they spoke like this, Narsus also appeared, likely noticing Rei and Set had arrived.

"Looks like you made it back safely."

Seeing Rei, he felt relieved. Even knowing Rei's abilities, anything could happen in the desert at night.

Rei understood Narsus wasn't saying that because he doubted his abilities, so he didn't dwell on it and spoke up.

"Now that we're all here, I suppose I should tell you. ...Ah, Set. Please handle perimeter security."

"GRRR!"

Before explaining, Rei called out to Set.

Set gave a short growl and headed toward the central plaza in the village.

Being in the village center meant he couldn't see the surroundings.

But instead, using his sense of smell, hearing, and his ability to detect auras and magic, he could easily keep watch over a village of this size.

Narsus probably came to Rei precisely because he understood this.

"For now, I've explained the matter of the Hundred-Faced One and this village to Augusto and Daldora. After contacting Ryubrand, it seems they'll send people tomorrow to investigate the village and deal with the bodies."

The speed of the action described by Rei seemed faster than expected.

Both Zarust and Narsus showed a hint of surprise.

But considering the village's current state, it was undeniably better to move as quickly as possible.

Especially regarding the cleanup of the bodies—if they took too long, there was a risk they might turn into undead... and above all, given the desert's daytime temperatures, they would rot and start stinking quickly.

"So, I'll stay here tonight too, but I need to head back to Gosh tomorrow. I have to bring someone to this village."

"Will you be okay without me tomorrow morning?" Rei asked, glancing at them. Zarust and Narsas nodded in agreement.

If they were told to guard the village all day long by themselves, it would be tough, but for just a few hours, it wouldn't be a problem at all.

Rei felt reassured by their confident words.

"Alright, then. Set and I will take tonight's watch. You two get some rest."

"Thanks, we'll leave it to you then."

"Count on it."

The two gave a brief reply and headed toward a nearby house.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, every house was empty now.

That meant they wouldn't have trouble finding a place to sleep.

...Instead, they had to find a place without corpses, or gather corpses themselves.

After seeing them off, Rei headed for the central square in the village.

In the moonlit square, Set was already lying prone, alert and ready to watch over the surroundings.

"Sorry I'm late?"

"Grr."

"It's fine," Set purred.

Suddenly, thinking about his exchange with Set just now, it somehow reminded him of the banter between lovers who had just started dating.

"...Come to think of it, I haven't been in touch for a few days. I wonder how Elena is doing."

For a moment, he considered using the Paired Orb to contact Elena, but it was already past midnight.

He took out his pocket watch from the Misty Ring and checked—it was past 1:00 AM.

He decided contacting Elena now would only bother her and gave up.

...If Elena knew about Rei's concern, she'd probably tell him not to worry about it and to contact her anytime.

He sat down beside Set, who was lying on the ground, leaning his weight against Set's warm body.

Thanks to the Dragon Robe, Rei felt neither heat nor cold, but even so, leaning his weight onto Set like this allowed him to feel the warmth of his body.

Perhaps this was the magic item's effect.

Following Set's example, I closed my eyes and scanned the surrounding atmosphere.

With Set here, she could tell immediately if anyone approached.

Normally, Rei wouldn't need to do this; she could have rested in some house like Zarust or Narsus.

But... even so, as the strongest member of this unit—three people and one beast, after all—she felt it necessary to stay awake like this.

He felt Set's heartbeat resonating through his body... and soon, his consciousness threatened to slip into sleep.

Today alone had brought so many events.

Augusto and Daldora were attacked in town, the lab was raided, he went to investigate and fought the Hundred-Faced One... After that, Set sniffed out the scent and found this village, the Hundred-Faced One's base, destroying the barrier set up by the artifact.

And just as the village revealed itself, we were met with battle against the waiting Resonance.

After winning and searching the village, they found everyone had drunk poison and died.

To report what happened, Rei returned all the way to Gosh to explain. Once that was done, he came back to this village and is keeping watch like this.

(This is clearly too eventful. It should at least unfold more slowly over several days.)

I know I'm just thinking about what's convenient for me, but when so many things pile up in a single day like this, I can't help but feel that way.

I just want to sleep soundly.

Feeling Set's warmth and heartbeat, Rei thought this, but immediately banished the weakness within himself.

(This won't do. I need something to wake me up... Wait, come to think of it...)

Suddenly, an idea struck him, and Rei, who had been leaning his weight on Seto, pulled away.

Set let out a slightly lonely whimper, probably disappointed that the warmth of his beloved Rei was gone.

"Sorry about that. But I've got something I need to take care of."

Stroking Set, she pulled down the hood she'd been wearing.

Instantly, the desert night's cold air brushed across Rei's face.

"Whoa, it really is cold."

Even as he said this, a smile appeared on Rei's face.

Cold... it was freezing air, but as a wake-up call, it was just right.

The temperature was around minus ten degrees Celsius, but Rei's body was now a creation born from Zepair and his faction's technology.

At this temperature, he felt the cold, but it was manageable.

Besides, the only part feeling cold was his face beneath the lowered hood.

His body was wrapped in the Dragon Robe, enveloped in a comfortable temperature—neither too hot nor too cold.

His face was a bit cold, but honestly, Rei couldn't care less about that.

Even so, feeling the chill, Rei glanced around just to be sure.

"Set, no one's looking this way, right?"

"Grr."

Set nodded at Rei's words.

The only ones currently in the village were Zarust and Narsus, sleeping in some house.

As long as those two were asleep, there was no way anyone was watching them.

Still, he asked Set just to be sure, probably because he had that nagging thought...

Rei was reassured by Set's answer that this wasn't the case, and he retrieved the desired items from the Misty Ring.

In Rei's hand were two magic stones. They were the magic stones of the Sand Lizardman.

The very stones he had tried to absorb at Augusto's mansion before, only to stop midway when he became certain someone was watching them.

Judging now to be the perfect moment... he tossed one magic stone into the air and slashed at it with the Death Scythe he'd also drawn.

"...No good."

After a moment, seeing nothing happen, he tossed the other magic stone to Set... but ultimately, neither managed to acquire any skills.

Despite his high hopes, this result left Rei feeling a little sulky.
