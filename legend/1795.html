"Huh... So Lady Elena was quite active in Gilm, too, then?"

“Yeah. When Gilm was undergoing expansion, there were all sorts of troublesome incidents, but thanks to Elena, many of them were resolved.”

Teres nodded in admiration at Rei’s words.

To Teres, the fact that Elena had distinguished herself wasn’t all that surprising.

After all, she had earned the nickname “Princess General” precisely because she had distinguished herself on the battlefield time and time again.

Still, the fact that she’s showing such admiration for Rei’s words now—while it might be partly flattery toward him—is largely because she’s genuinely surprised to see Elena making a name for herself outside the Noble Faction.

From Teres’s perspective… no, even from the perspective of other nobles in the Noble Faction, Elena is, after all, the “Princess General,” a symbol of the Noble Faction.

Given that, it’s no wonder her achievements are seen as unusual.

...Though, the person at the center of the conversation did seem a bit embarrassed.

Elena knows she’s the subject of all sorts of gossip, and it’s not uncommon for such talk to happen right in front of her.

But the fact remained that having it done in front of Rei was quite embarrassing.

Just as Elena was about to say something, a knock on the door interrupted her.

Elena gave her permission, and when the door opened, a maid stood there.

“Excuse me. Miss, Lord Ranigus Musey and Lord Gaiska Seisor have arrived. What would you like me to do?”

“Oh? Ranigus and Gaiska together? That’s quite an odd pairing.”

It was Teres who seemed slightly surprised by the maid’s words.

For Teres, who lived in Anesis, Ranigus and Gaiska were familiar faces.

That said, normally, since Gaiska was also part of the Noble Faction, his name would have been mentioned first. The fact that Ranigus’s name came first was likely a sign of whom she harbored feelings for.

In fact, since the maid had mentioned them in that order, it clearly reflected how Gaiska was viewed.

“Hmm… I see. What should I do?”

Elena looked slightly hesitant.

If it had been just Ranigus, Elena would likely have let him pass without giving it a second thought.

She knows that while Ranigus is a noble in the Royal Faction, he possesses common sense—not the kind of common sense expected of a noble, but the kind used in ordinary human interactions.

However, Gaiska, who had come with Ranigus, was from a family holding the high title of marquis, and as such, he had a great deal of pride.

It’s the kind of pride that stems not from his own merits, but from the belief that he should be given priority unconditionally.

What would happen if such a person were to encounter Rei?

As someone who knows Rei’s personality, Elena could understand this all too well.

Gaiska is a member of the Seisol Marquis family, which holds considerable influence even among the Noble Faction, and from Elena’s perspective, she did not want to see such a person clash with Rei.

“Well… he said he’d like to greet Rei, if possible, so he asked me to bring him in.”

“...What? Who said that?”

“It was Ranigus.”

At the maid’s words, Elena furrowed her brow slightly.

There are various inconsistencies here, she thought. With that in mind... she immediately began to guess what Ranigus was thinking.

(As a member of the Royal Faction, he probably wants to prevent Rei—no, the Neutral Faction and the Noble Faction—from getting any closer. And among the Noble Faction, there are people like Gaiska. In other words…)

They likely want to pit Rei and Gaiska against each other here, so that Rei comes to recognize the Noble Faction—not exactly with loathing, but at least as people he wouldn’t particularly want to be on good terms with.

Even as she made this guess, Elena thought for a few more seconds before nodding in agreement.

“I understand. Then, please bring the two of them here.”

“Lady Elena!?”

Ara had also understood Elena’s line of thinking.

That’s precisely why she’d expected the conversation to lead to either not meeting them at all, or if they did meet, to do so again in a place where Rei wasn’t present.

But what Elena said was to bring them here.

That clearly meant Rei and Gaiska would run into each other here… no, it meant Gaiska would pick a fight with Rei.

Why, when she knew that would happen?

Elena met Ara’s questioning gaze, but nodded back as if to say she understood everything.

(If I were to refuse here, Ranigus would likely try to contact him directly, bypassing me. It’s better to have them talk where I’m present than for them to meet somewhere I don’t know about. And Ranigus must have judged that I would think this way, which is why he came here with Gaiska.)

In other words, once Ranigus and Gaiska arrived together, the best choice for Elena was to summon them both here.

Elena realized she was a step behind, but she felt a slight sense of anticipation about what actions Ranigus might take in front of Rei.

“It’s fine. Ranigus and Gaiska specifically asked to come here. Since that’s the case, there’s certainly a reason for it. So, I can’t very well refuse without a reason.”

“As for a reason…”

“Rei is here, isn’t he?”

Ara wanted to say that, but seeing Elena’s expression, she judged that whatever she said here would likely be futile and fell silent.

Teres wore an expression full of anticipation, wondering what was about to begin, and showed no sign of trying to stop Elena.

“Then, Lord Ranigus, may I invite Lord Gaiska here?”

“Yes.”

At Elena’s words, the maid bowed her head and left the room.

“Ah…” Ara reached out toward the door the maid had just closed, looking as if she wanted to say something, but… in the end, the words never came out.

Instead, as if to make up for it, Ara turned to Elena and asked, seeking confirmation.

“Are you sure this is okay? It’s definitely going to cause a commotion, isn’t it?”

As Elena’s subordinate, Ara naturally knew about Ranigus and Gaiska.

That said, given her status as the third daughter of the Count of Scarley, she is considered inferior to Gaiska of the Marquis family.

...Since it’s highly unlikely that either the third daughter or the fourth son will inherit the family, the difference in status isn’t as great as Gaiska imagines.

No, in fact, given that she holds the position of Captain of the Guard as one of Elena’s subordinates, most people would likely view Ara as having a higher standing in public.

That said, for Gaiska, the bloodline flowing through his veins is still what matters most—it is everything to him.

Given that he is such a person, it would be a stretch to say he and Rei are a good match.

No, in fact, you could even say it’s the worst possible match.

(If it comes down to it… I’ll have to step in.)

In terms of combat ability, Ara was no match for Rei or Elena, but that was simply because the comparison was unfair.

Against Gaiska, who hadn’t trained specifically for combat, she possessed enough brute strength to easily overpower him.

Not wanting him to draw blood in front of Elena, Ara made up her mind.

...The fact that this resolve isn’t motivated by a desire to keep Gaiska from getting hurt clearly shows exactly how Ara feels about him.

“Hehe, this looks like it’s going to be fun,”

Teres murmurs happily, and before Ara can say anything in response, the sound of a knock on the door echoes through the room.

After Aara gave her permission for Elena to enter, the door opened and the same maid from earlier appeared.

The only difference was that this time, the maid had brought two men with her.

“Hey, it’s been a while, Elena. You’re as beautiful as ever.”

“Ranigus is right. Elena, you’re beautiful no matter when I see you. No, haven’t you become even more beautiful since I last saw you? It seems your beauty hasn’t faded even in a barbaric land like Gilm.”

The two men appeared to be in their late teens to early twenties.

Both had handsome features, but compared to the man who spoke first—Ranigus—the man who spoke second—Gaiska—had a certain nastiness written all over his face.

Given that he was undoubtedly handsome enough to be called a beauty, this made it all the more puzzling.

In particular, Gaiska’s words were more than enough to cause discomfort not only for Rei, who was based in Gilm, but also for Elena and Ara, who had been living in Gilm for the past while.

Was Gaiska doing this on purpose, or was that just his true nature?

Rei couldn’t tell, but at the very least, it was impossible for him to feel any goodwill toward the man.

For some reason… no, it must be because Rei was here. As Gaiska gleefully continued to disparage Gilm, Rei tried to say something… but someone acted before he could.

“Lord Gaiska, aren’t your words and actions a bit rude? To speak that way in the presence of someone from Gilm. Besides, I was in Gilm with Lady Elena as well, and it was a lively, pleasant place.”

Before Rei or Elena could speak, Ara spoke up.

However, upon hearing Ara’s words, Gaiska shot her a displeased look, annoyed that she had interrupted his gleeful denigration of Gilm.

“For the third daughter of the Scarley family to dare offer me advice… I’ll let it slide since we’re in front of Elena, but don’t get too big for your boots…”

“Calm down, Gaiska. Do you intend to cause a needless commotion in front of Elena? Besides, Gilm is the town where Elena was staying until just the other day. If you speak ill of it, of course Elena isn’t going to feel good about it, is she?”

Ranigus cut Gaiska off mid-sentence.

Gaiska’s behavior was exactly what Ranigus had expected.

...Or rather, perhaps I should say they were far more extreme than he had anticipated.

What was particularly unexpected was his tone toward Ara.

Ara is known as Elena’s confidante and best friend.

Naturally, criticizing someone like Ara was not going to put Elena in a good mood.

Or, if Ara were criticizing her because it was clear she was in the wrong, that wouldn’t be a problem, but this time it was clearly nothing more than a baseless accusation.

Looking at the situation objectively, it’s Gaiska who should be the one being criticized.

Ranigus had come here with Gaiska in order to improve Rei’s impression of him… but if there was a miscalculation, it was that Ranigus had underestimated Gaiska’s hostility toward Rei.

Since entering this room, Gaiska has glanced at Elena, Ara, and Teres, but has not looked at Rei even once.

Her attitude practically suggests she doesn’t even acknowledge his presence.

Of course, it wasn’t that he truly didn’t recognize Rei’s presence; rather, he was acting on the assumption that Rei wasn’t someone he would speak to.

Normally, Rei wouldn’t have found such an attitude amusing, but… since Gaiska was the very epitome of a noble—in the worst possible sense—Rei feared that if he said anything here, it might cause trouble for Elena… That’s why he’d been ignoring him…

“Gaiska, should I take that as an insult to Ara?”

To Elena, Ara is a childhood friend.

And at the same time, she is her most trusted subordinate.

There was no way Elena could stay silent while Ara was being disparaged like that.

Faced with Elena’s furious glare, Gaiska frantically shook his head.

“No, no, not at all. I was just trying to be mindful of my position…”

The shift from “I” to “I” likely stems from the difference in their social standing.

“Then, what do you think of a mere fourth son of a marquis’s family upsetting the eldest daughter of a duke’s family?”

At the sudden voice that cut in, Gaiska froze in his tracks.

It was as if the very words Gaiska had spoken had been thrown right back at him.

Gaiska had tried to silence her with the prestige of his title, but in terms of social standing, Elena was actually overwhelmingly superior.

“And who exactly are you, acting in a way that displeases Elena?”

It was Teres who delivered a fatal blow to Gaiska with a single, swift thrust.

Gaiska was about to shout at Teres, “Shut up, you mere count’s family!” but someone spoke up before he could.

“Well then, is this the end of the pathetic charade put on by someone who can boast of nothing but trivial things like bloodline, rather than their own abilities?”

It was Rei, who had been ignoring Gaiska until now, who muttered those words.
