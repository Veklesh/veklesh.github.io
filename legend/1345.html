"Welcome!"

Upon entering the inn, Rei and the others are greeted by that voice.

Of course, Set couldn't enter the inn, so he lay sprawled outside as usual.

However, he also has the duty of keeping watch to ensure Precious and the others don't run away upon learning Rei and the others have arrived.

"...They're not here."

Ignoring the clerk's voice, Rei scanned the inn's interior.

It was a typical layout: the first floor was the dining hall, the second and third floors were the lodgings. But from where they entered the shop, the person Rei was looking for was nowhere to be seen.

Not on the sofa set aside for meeting guests, not in the faintly visible dining room, not on the stairs leading upstairs... nowhere was the person Rei was looking for.

"Sir, if you're not staying the night, I'd appreciate it if you'd leave."

The clerk addressed Rei as he scanned the inn.

They surely knew exactly who they were addressing.

Being staff at a high-class inn, they surely hadn't neglected gathering information about Gilm.

Of course, few would recognize Rei just by seeing him wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe.

But when accompanied by a peerless beauty clad in a sheer garment like a dancer or courtesan, adorned with gauntlets and greaves, there was no way they couldn't identify Rei.

...Furthermore, while Byune is reasonably attractive, she inevitably pales in comparison to a beauty like Vihera in terms of striking presence.

Knowing she was Rei and yet maintaining this attitude was more than enough to make Rei feel favorably toward her.

"There's a merchant staying here named Precious. I need you to contact him."

Hearing Rei's request, the clerk hesitated for a moment, unsure how to respond.

Had Ray asked something like, "Is he staying here?", the clerk would have replied that as an inn employee, she couldn't answer such a question.

This inn prioritized security, and it often housed people with questionable backgrounds.

That's precisely why, if the clerk had asked out of genuine doubt, that would have been the proper response.

But Rei was different now.

She clearly knew Precious was here and asked precisely because of that.

In other words, she must have come asking because they had an appointment to begin with.

Given that this inn prides itself on security, it's standard procedure to inform the inn if someone is coming to inquire beforehand.

But of course, not everyone follows the rules, and it wasn't that unusual for guests to arrive without prior notice.

Especially if it's someone famous like Rei, it's entirely possible they'd skip telling the inn, figuring it'd be a hassle to explain everything knowing full well they'd be coming.

...Though, of course, Rei hadn't actually made an appointment with Precious.

Still, it was true that because Set had brought her here, she was certain Precious was here... or more precisely, that the person who had given Keena the platinum coin must be here.

And then... The inn clerk quickly gathered his thoughts in his head before bowing deeply.

"I'm terribly sorry, but I must go and see Lady Precious. Would you mind waiting here a little longer?"

"...I'd prefer it if you could send her out immediately."

Seeing Rei's slightly displeased expression, the clerk bowed again.

"I apologize, but it is the inn's rule."

"Guess there's nothing to be done. Ah, fine. Do that."

After muttering this, the clerk exchanged a few words with another clerk nearby and headed upstairs.

Watching them go, Rei sat down on a sofa a short distance from the counter with Vihera and Byune.

"Are you sure? They might run away if they find out we're here."

"I doubt it. They probably don't think we're involved in the attack on Tris... or rather, on Tris's escort. So I don't think we need to worry about that. Besides, even if they did run..."

Rei paused, his gaze shifting toward the door leading outside.

More precisely, to Set, who was likely waiting beyond it.

For Set, whose senses—including his sense of smell—were exceptionally sharp, detecting someone trying to sneak out of the inn wouldn't be difficult.

If that happened, it would mean Precious had fled because she felt guilty, and that could be fatal.

Rei didn't think Precious would foolishly attempt such a thing if she knew they were here.

"Because Set is there."

Rei stated, his words filled with complete trust.

Hearing this, Vihera and Byune felt anew just how much Rei trusted Set, and they couldn't voice any objections.

Silence enveloped the surroundings.

Of course, only Rei and his companions were silent; various voices could be heard from elsewhere.

Some were eating in the dining hall, their voices carrying a fair amount of noise, while outside the inn, the chatter of passersby could be heard.

Also, near the counter stood two bodyguards, each over two meters tall, their bodies covered in muscle.

(Just looks, huh?)

That was Rei's impression upon seeing the bodyguards.

Not the kind of muscle honed in battle, but muscle meant to intimidate opponents.

In Rei's estimation, they resembled the muscles of bodybuilders.

Compared to ordinary people living in the city, they probably had strength, but they weren't a threat to anyone with a certain level of power or higher.

Conversely, that also meant they were perfectly effective as a deterrent against opponents with only limited abilities.

Those two bodyguards probably understood that no matter how hard they struggled, they couldn't win against Rei and his group.

When Rei first said he wanted to meet Precious, he was terribly nervous.

As bodyguards, they couldn't just run away if their employer, the inn's staff, ordered them to eliminate Rei and the others.

That's precisely why they were immensely grateful that the inn staff had quietly listened to Rei and the others.

...The two bodyguards, caught in Rei's gaze, remained silent, though visibly uneasy, even if they didn't say so.

Being stared at so intently by someone they knew they couldn't possibly defeat, no matter how hard they tried, was not a pleasant feeling.

The tense standoff for the two bodyguards lasted until the inn employee who had gone upstairs earlier returned.

That's precisely why, upon hearing footsteps descending the stairs, the two bodyguards felt a deep sense of relief, though they didn't show it on their faces.

"Apologies for the wait. Precious-sama is ready to see you. Please this way."

Hearing the employee's words, Rei seemed satisfied that she hadn't fled after all.

He had wondered... just maybe, she might run away. But she must have decided that fleeing now would only be detrimental to her.

"Ah, well... let's go."

"Right."

"Mm."

Vihera and Byune nodded at Rei's words and followed the inn employee up the stairs.

True to its reputation as one of Gilm's finer inns, the interior was remarkably subdued... something truly worthy of being called elegant.

At first, Rei had wondered if the decorations were just pointlessly extravagant, but she had to admit her expectations had been pleasantly surpassed.

That said, the Dusk Wheat inn where Rei was staying was also known as a high-end lodging within Gilm.

Even that Dusk Wheat inn, while having a calming interior, wasn't exactly flashy.

Of course, not all high-end inns were like that; some were quite flashy.

That aspect likely depended on the preferences of the guests.

After ascending to the second floor, Rei and his companions were led to a room situated some distance from the staircase.

"Excuse me, Precious-sama. I have brought your guests."

"Ah, please come in."

At those words, the inn employee opened the door... and then waited in the hallway.

Their role ended here. That seemed to be the understanding.

Sensing this, Rei and the others entered the room without saying anything further.

Compared to the room Rei used at the Dusk Wheat inn, this one was twice... no, nearly three times as large.

(But in terms of magic item quality, it doesn't hold a candle to the Dusk Wheat inn.)

The room was spacious, but it didn't contain nearly as many magic items.

This difference likely stems from the Dusk Wheat inn's focus on ensuring guests have a comfortable stay.

"How did you find the inn where I'm staying?"

"I have various connections around here."

Rei answered Precious while looking around the room.

The room was spacious, but its contents—a bed, a dresser, a simple kitchenette—showed no particular difference from an ordinary inn.

The only noticeable differences from the Dusk Wheat inn where Rei stayed were the large desk, which could also serve as a work desk where Precious sat, and the several sofas scattered about.

And about five bodyguards stood near Precious.

One of them was the man he'd seen before in the slums... the one believed to be the person who hired Keena to attack Tris.

(I never thought he actually hadn't escaped.)

I'd been pondering various possibilities, but the fact he hadn't fled and was here was slightly unexpected.

"Well now, I'd certainly like to hear about these connections you mention. After all, I don't have many such connections in Gilm myself... and I'm struggling with various things. But anyway, that's beside the point. What brings you here today? I was a bit surprised you showed up without an appointment."

Rei nodded in response to Precious's prompt question, getting straight to the point.

"Right. I've got my hands full too. So let's get straight to it. ...Did you hear about the Spier Trading Company branch getting attacked today? And Tris being targeted by an assassin?"

"Yes. Believe it or not, I'm also part of the Spier Trading Company, so I've been worried about that incident. To be honest, I can't help but wonder if it's really wise to leave it all up to him."

"I see."

Rei found himself agreeing with the words coming from Precious's mouth.

The attack on Tris's guards was likely some kind of warning aimed at Tris himself.

But at the same time, the attack on the store itself would be a major blow to Tris... no, to any merchant.

That attack was likely aimed at achieving precisely that.

"What do you think, Rei? If you were to experience something like that—someone attacking a shop you patronize... would you continue using that shop?"

"I wonder. Wouldn't that depend on the customer's circumstances? If there's a product only available at that shop, then you'd have no choice but to keep using it."

Rei understood what Precious was getting at, but he didn't voice any further thoughts.

Vihera beside Rei and Byune behind her seemed to understand this was Rei's moment. Neither showed any sign of speaking up.

"I see. As expected of a high-rank adventurer, you're quite resolute, Rei. ...Well, we can't stand here forever. Please, have a seat on the sofa."

"No, I'm fine standing. There are various troublesome circumstances between you and me. Given that, sitting on the sofa and having a leisurely chat isn't really an option, is it?"

"No, no. I certainly don't think there are any troublesome circumstances between me and Rei, the maid."

"Oh? If you feel that way, I'm glad to hear it. Then, if you could grant my request in that spirit, it would be a great help to me."

"A favor? ...Honestly, I don't think I have any goods that would satisfy Rei right now."

Mentioning products when asked for a favor—I suppose that's because Precious is a merchant too.

A slightly sharper glint appeared in the eyes he directed at Rei.

Precious's current situation was far from good.

The people she had connections with in the underworld cut ties with Precious immediately upon hearing she was opposing Rei.

In other words, Precious currently had almost no forces she could freely command.

Even if she wanted to manipulate information, with so few people at her disposal, there was nothing she could do.

Given this situation, it was only natural for her to cling to a faint hope when Rei showed up asking for her help.

At the same time, she was certain he would oppose her due to the incident in the slums.

Precious was filled with complex emotions, but Rei, seemingly unconcerned, averted his gaze... looked at the man guarding Precious nearby, and spoke.

"I was wondering if I could borrow that bodyguard guy for a bit."

That's what he said.
