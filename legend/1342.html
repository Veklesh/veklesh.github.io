When Rei returned, the Spier Trading Company's Gilm branch had already resumed normal business operations.

The crashed carriage had been moved away, and while the damaged doors and walls weren't fully repaired, the temporary fixes were sufficient.

The curious passersby had also vanished, with only a few unaware individuals tilting their heads as they glanced at the shop.

Well, probably just to be safe. There were also a few guards present.

Still, the store operating as usual must be on Tris's orders.

Having officially begun operations in Gilm this spring, only for trouble to arise so soon afterward, would undoubtedly cause various problems regarding appearances.

Amidst this... Rei and Set strode boldly toward the shop.

The guards were initially surprised to see Rei, but both Rei and Set are well-known among the security personnel.

Because of that, seeing them now didn't particularly surprise them... rather, they jokingly called out to them.

"Hey, hey, Rei. You're not somehow involved in this latest business, are you?"

The guard who asked this wasn't particularly seeking a reaction. He'd simply used it as a conversation starter.

Considering he hadn't been here when Rei came to hand over Kena a little while ago, he must have been a replacement.

But... at the guard's question, Rei shrugged his shoulders slightly and opened his mouth.

"Yeah. I'm definitely involved. I came here because I needed to talk to Tris."

"...Are you serious?"

"Yeah. Didn't you know? I'm actually involved in this whole thing to some extent. The Azimov case and all."

"Come to think of it, there was that too. If Rei's involved, this could turn into quite a messy and major incident."

The guard let out a small sigh but said nothing more, letting Rei pass.

The guards were stationed here primarily to be ready for immediate action if anything happened, not to screen visitors coming to the Spier Trading Company.

After parting ways with the guard, Rei left Set there and entered the store.

(Huh... Despite all that commotion, there sure are a lot of customers.)

That was Rei's honest impression upon seeing inside the shop.

Inside, there were easily over ten customers, all engaged in various discussions with Spier Trading Company merchants.

Some were in the midst of negotiations, while others, seemingly having reached agreements, chatted amicably with smiles on their faces.

"Oh, is that Rei? What brings you here?"

One of the merchants inside the shop, who apparently knew Rei, asked with a puzzled look.

He asked with a puzzled look.

Rei, who had been unsure whom to approach, seized the opportunity and addressed the merchant who had spoken to him.

"I need to see Tris about something. ...Would you mind putting me through?"

"The manager? Well, but our shop is a bit..."

The man's gaze, which had trailed off, fell upon the hastily patched-up state of the shop.

Particularly the entrance area where the carriage had crashed through.

"Well, there was a bit of an incident earlier. I think the manager is a bit busy right now..."

"Tell him I'm here. That I need to talk about this whole situation."

"...Could it be that you're involved in this matter too, Rei?"

A hint of suspicion flashed across his face.

Then again, there were plenty of incidents where Rei got involved and caused trouble.

As a resident of Gilm who knew that, it was a natural reaction.

That wasn't wrong... but for Rei, this time he felt he was merely someone who'd been dragged into it.

Being looked at like that by the merchant man was unwelcome.

"It's not like I'm the only one involved, though. ...More importantly, talk to Tris. Tell him that maybe this whole thing could be resolved in one go."

"...Understood. Please wait a moment."

With that, the merchant man departed.

Watching him go, Rei noticed several people glancing his way. He ignored it, moving to the edge to avoid being in the way.

Soon, the other customers and merchants became engrossed in their own business discussions.

They were likely thinking that while Rei was intriguing, they needed to focus on the business deals right in front of them.

Watching them focus on their immediate tasks, Rei soon noticed the merchant who had left earlier returning with a serious expression.

He walked straight up to Rei and spoke, looking tense.

"The manager wishes to see you. This way, please."

"Ah, sorry about that."

Rei wondered why the man's face was so tense, but he also knew asking about it now would likely get him no answer.

As they moved, Ray noticed four male guards standing in front of the room.

(Ah, that's right. Kena killed one of his guards. So he felt threatened and hurriedly added more guards... that must be it.)

Rei felt the guards staring intently at his face, but he didn't react to it. He just watched silently as the merchant knocked on the door.

"Excuse me, manager. I've brought Rei."

"Let them in."

At those words, the merchant opened the door... and ushered Rei inside.

As if informed beforehand, the merchant did not enter the room but left immediately.

(An office, huh?)

A desk for official duties stood there, its surface covered with a pile of documents... not quite a mountain, but still numerous sheets were clearly visible.

At the desk, Tris was reviewing papers, signing them, and sorting them into different piles.

Besides Tris, two bodyguards were also present in the room.

Even to Rei's eyes, they were clearly more skilled than the four outside the room.

"Sorry, Rei. Things are a bit hectic right now."

"No, don't worry about it. I'm the one who showed up without an appointment. Besides... I heard about the carriage."

At those words, Tris lifted his face, the flesh on his cheeks jiggling.

"Is that so? ...And so, you say your coming here is related to this matter, but might it also be connected to the carriage incident?"

Her tone was polite, but Tris's eyes didn't smile.

Despite that gaze, Rei nodded back without seeming bothered at all.

"Yeah. ...Well, you've got your hands full over there too, so I'll cut to the chase. We caught the woman who orchestrated that carriage incident."

"Huh!?"

Tris's surprise was palpable at Rei's words.

It was only natural. They'd been told that someone they couldn't handle themselves had been captured so easily.

The guards, whose comrades had been killed, stiffened their expressions.

"So... who did you capture?"

"Unfortunately, after getting the full story, we handed them over to the guards. ...But when I heard the woman's account, I got quite an interesting tale."

"Interesting stories? If it's not too much trouble, could you share them?"

"Well, if you're going to leave this whole incident to me, I suppose I could tell you."

"What happens if I refuse?"

"Nothing much from me. I'll just proceed as I'd planned all along. Of course, it'd be easier in many ways if you agreed here."

Hearing Rei's words, Tris's usual gentle smile faded, and he furrowed his brow.

Shaking the characteristic flesh on his cheeks, he opened his mouth.

"I can't give a rash answer until you tell me exactly what you mean."

"Is that so? ...You might be right. While we don't have definitive proof yet, we might be able to prove that the series of incidents were Precious's doing. Though, it's still only a 'might' at this point."

Fundamentally, even if Precious is suspected in the carriage crash at the Spier Trading Company branch, if it's argued that's separate from the Azimov case, then that's the end of it.

Rei figured Marina would handle those details.

Still, if Precious's bodyguard is involved in this incident, it's certain Precious herself will face scrutiny.

It's quite possible they could connect that to the Azoth Trading Company affair.

(Besides... worst case, if they put up any resistance over this incident, and we can resort to force, that wouldn't be a problem either.)

Rei's involvement in this matter is almost entirely circumstantial.

Originally, it was about retaliation against those who had messed with him... those who attacked Azimov, stole Sleipnir Boots, and then tried to use them to pit Azoth Trading Company against him, with Tris allegedly behind it all. That was Rei's reason for getting involved.

Rei had been waiting for the other side to slip up, using his relationship with Tris—who'd been dragged into this mess just like him, or rather, who was the main target—as leverage. It seemed highly likely that slip-up had finally happened.

Given that, Rei had absolutely no intention of hesitating here.

Sensing something in Rei's demeanor, Tris... and even his guards, completely unaware, felt their throats go dry, their pores open, and a chill run down their spines.

"Understood. I'll leave this matter to Rei. So, would you mind telling me what happened?"

To antagonize Rei carelessly now would lead to significant damage.

Understanding this, Tris promptly changed course and stated as much.

Under normal circumstances, the guards would likely voice their dissatisfaction with Tris.

But today, at least, there was no need for such concern.

Or rather, there was no one foolish enough to make such a move in front of Rei right now.

It was fortunate for Tris that only those who understood the terror of making an enemy of Rei were present in this room.

Had anyone present been unable to sense the atmosphere Rei was radiating and voiced dissatisfaction to Tris... well, that person would have ended up bringing Tris immense regret.

In any case, once Tris agreed to her request, the atmosphere emanating from Rei shifted back to its usual self.

"I see. I'm glad you understand. ...Now, to the main point. The female assassin who carried out this job said the one who commissioned the work was a man who looked like an adventurer."

"...Is there something wrong with that?"

Whether Rei understood what Tris meant or not.

Regardless, prompted by Tris to continue, Rei went on with his explanation.

"At first, we thought Precious had gone through several intermediaries to hire the female assassin."

He avoided mentioning the female assassin's name, Keena, directly. It was likely to delay any action if they ultimately parted ways with Tris after the conversation ended.

Rei only referred to her as "the female assassin."

"And that turned out to be wrong?"

"Yeah. You already know we went to the slums before, right? And that Precious met with some kind of underworld crew there?"

"Yes."

"But it seems that female assassin didn't belong to any organization. Of course, it's possible that someone from the organization Precious was in contact with hired her to avoid any fallout reaching them if something happened..."

Tris, who had been listening to this, found it rather convincing.

At the very least, it was inconceivable that someone who knew they were enemies with Rei would use someone connected to him to stir up trouble.

But Rei didn't seem to think so. He continued his explanation.

"So, based on what that female assassin told me, the person who brought the request was probably the man who was guarding Precious."

"...Huh?"

That single word seemed to pique Tris's interest, eliciting a strange yet understanding sound from her mouth.

If that's true, it would indeed be fatal evidence.

The men guarding Precious are all alumni of the orphanage run by Precious's family for generations, and they hold deep trust in her.

If such a person was involved, it would be almost certain that this whole affair was Precious's doing.

"So, I plan to have that female assassin—the one who agreed to cooperate with us—find those guards now and have her observe them."

"I don't think the testimony of the person who plotted this can be trusted."

"True. But testimony is testimony. If it's actually suspicious, that's another matter, but it's almost certain Precious is involved in this incident, right? Then that's sufficient."

Tris eventually gave a small nod in response to Rei's words.
