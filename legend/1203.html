Seasons pass since Vihera lost consciousness.

Summer, when green leaves flourished, ended, and the trees' leaves turned red, beginning to scatter to the ground.

Even as autumn deepened, Vihera remained unconscious.

Ambris, the entity that had attacked Rei and fled into Vihera's body, seemed to be the original entity after all. Since the day Rei defeated Ambris, no other entity had been found.

Whether it was due to the effects of the near-fatal injuries, the impact of the main entity fleeing into Vihera's body... or some other reason, Rei couldn't tell. In any case, no trace of Ambris had been confirmed since the day Vihera lost consciousness.

Of course, Ambris is a black mist-like entity capable of moving underground, so it's possible he simply hasn't been found... but Rei judged that Ambris was likely no longer present.

But... even so, from the day Vihera fell until now, Gilm had never truly settled down.

Ambris may have vanished, but the hordes led by monsters evolved into leader species by Ambris remained intact.

Given that, the monsters forming these packs must be defeated, and most of Gilm's adventurers were busy with extermination requests.

Still, the fact that Ambris was destroyed meant the leader species wouldn't increase so easily anymore. As time passed and the packs disappeared, safety would increase accordingly.

And the adventurers gathered in Gilm were skilled; the annihilation of these packs was progressing smoothly.

The number of merchants, which had decreased from summer into autumn, had now returned to normal. ...No, one could even say it had increased further.

Merchants who couldn't come to Gilm during the Ambris incident were now arriving in large numbers.

Moreover, autumn would soon end, snow would begin to fall, and winter would arrive.

Those who venture to Gilm in winter are, fundamentally, only those with some specific reason.

The rest wait until spring to go to Gilm.

Many likely want to earn as much as possible beforehand, knowing they could end up trapped in Gilm for the entire winter if they're unlucky.

Rei walked through the bustling streets of Gilm, busier than usual, alongside Set.

He appeared unchanged. ...At least, that's how it seemed to the residents of Gilm.

But if anyone knew Rei better, they would have noticed that her unchanged appearance was only skin deep.

Ever since Vihera collapsed, he'd been searching high and low for any way to bring her consciousness back, but all his efforts had been in vain.

...Or rather, perhaps it would be more accurate to say that while some methods might have had an effect, none could restore Vihera's consciousness.

Of course, some had tried to trick Rei out of his money... but what became of those individuals goes without saying.

"Set, it's getting a bit chilly. Are you okay?"

"Grrr!"

At Rei's words, Set growled to show he was fine.

Set could sleep on snow in the dead of winter without any trouble.

Griffins possess abilities vastly superior to humans in that regard.

Still, the reason Rei worried about Set was surely because he cared so deeply for Set.

As Set rubbed his head against him and begged to be petted, Rei curved only the corner of his lips and stroked the griffin's head.

"I see. You're okay then... But autumn is deepening now. Snow will fall before long, so you need to be careful too, Set."

As he admonished Set, Rei and Set walked down the street... when suddenly, the tantalizing aroma of something delicious wafted over from a nearby food stall.

"Come to think of it, I am a bit hungry. Dinner won't be for a while yet. Shall we grab something to eat?"

"Growl!"

At Rei's words, Set happily growled and peered into the nearby food stall.

"Oh, Rei and Set! What'll you be having today? My recommendation is the lizardman skewers. ...Well, you might be sick of them by now."

The stall owner greeted them with a familiar smile.

His words were undeniably true.

The corpses of the monsters that had formed a pack during the Ambris incident naturally became food for the residents of Gilm.

Merchants had stopped coming, so everyone ate that meat... but even if it was only for a few days, eating monster meat every meal for over ten days was bound to get tiresome.

And if it went on for a month? Needless to say.

Other ingredients could be bought if one wanted them. But with supplies scarce, prices naturally rose above normal.

In the end, most people ate nothing but monster meat for every meal for quite some time.

Given that situation, once the matter in Ambris was resolved and merchants began arriving again, those who had grown tired of monster meat would naturally seek other dishes.

That said, even monster meat—as long as it wasn't from the humanoid monsters that hunted in packs—probably still had some demand.

Anyway, now that they were finally free from monster meat, quite a few people wanted to avoid it for a while.

Because of that, sales of the lizardman skewers at the food stalls were far from brisk.

"What? You're still selling lizardman skewers? You'd make more money selling something else!"

"Well, what can I say? My family's been selling lizardman skewers for generations. There's no way I can just change that now."

"...No, you should sell what the customers want. ...Ah, never mind. Just give me all the lizardman skewers you have."

The shopkeeper was momentarily stunned by the words coming out of Rei's mouth, but remembering who he was dealing with, he immediately broke into a broad smile.

"Alrighty! Just give me a sec. I'll grill up everything I've got ready too."

And so, what was handed over were the skewers that had already been grilled.

Any he couldn't finish could be stored in his Misty Ring, so Rei didn't have to worry about the skewers getting cold.

He took a bite right away... and nodded in approval.

(No wonder they've been selling lizardman skewers for generations. Honestly, these skewers are several levels tastier than the regular ones you find anywhere.)

The outside was crisped to perfection over high heat, yet the meat inside remained tender, never overcooked.

With each bite, the juices spread throughout his mouth, mingling with the sauce to break down the meat.

"Delicious."

"Gurrr!"

Set, eating skewers beside Rei, also let out a cry of delight.

Rei's words must have pleased the shopkeeper. He smiled happily as he grilled the next skewer.

After about twenty minutes, they received all the remaining skewers. Satisfied, they stored everything except what they'd eaten in the Misty Ring.

After paying the bill, they parted ways with the stall owner, who couldn't suppress a smile at what was likely his best sales day in a while. Rei and Set continued on their way.

Then, the guild came into view.

"...Probably not, though."

"Grrr..."

Rei murmured while petting Set, then entered the guild.

With winter approaching, many adventurers were busy preparing to survive the cold season.

No one wanted to deal with quests in the snow.

Though perhaps it was a blessing in disguise—many adventurers had earned a considerable sum by repeatedly taking on the Ambris extermination quest.

For those who had participated in the monster pack extermination quests, their winter funds were already nearly saved up.

Those working busily now were either adventurers who hadn't been at Gilm for some reason during the hordes, or hadn't fought.

Or, a certain number of adventurers arrived at Gilm after the Ambris incident was resolved.

Amidst this, Rei and Set had come looking for a request... or so it seemed.

"Ah, Rei. Sorry, but the requested item still hasn't arrived."

"...I see."

Rei, who had approached Kenny since Lenora wasn't at the counter, let out a small sigh at Kenny's words.

The request Rei had posted at the guild was, naturally, seeking someone or something to restore Vihera's consciousness.

The reward offered was five light gold coins. Even considering it a frontier request where rare materials and monsters gathered, it was an exceptionally high reward.

But for Rei, that amount wasn't exactly easy to come by... though it was a sum he could manage with a bit of a stretch.

He had originally thought ten light gold coins would be appropriate, but considering the difficulty of the reward, Lenora, Kenny, and even Marina scolded him, insisting five coins was the absolute limit. That was how this amount was decided.

Regarding the dismemberment of the numerous herd corpses obtained from the Ambris incident, that had already been submitted as a separate request and completed.

It yielded a considerable amount of meat, and since the reward offered for that job was also quite generous, it was completed promptly.

(Ah, come to think of it, I still haven't absorbed the Orc Leader's magic stone.)

Recalling the dismemberment request inevitably brings to mind the matter of the Orc Leader's magic stone.

I could have absorbed it much sooner, but I'd been so busy with the Vihera matter that the orc leader's magic stone had completely slipped Rei's mind.

Rei remembered it now, but more importantly... he called out to Kenny.

"Up until now, we've been recruiting based on things that definitely work, but do you think we should also gather rumors?"

"Better not."

Kenny immediately shot down Rei's suggestion.

"Why not?"

"Because we're currently focused on things that are proven to work, we're keeping scammers and the like away. Plus, you're a high-ranking adventurer, Rei. But if we start accepting rumors too, we'll just get flooded with people bringing us all kinds of nonsense."

"...That bad? Surely my name acts as a deterrent?"

"Yes. But like I said, that's because they're seeking certainty. If it becomes acceptable even as a rumor, then more people might think 'maybe...'. Even if they later realize it's nonsense, they can just say 'it was just a rumor' and be done with it."

"That... I don't like that idea."

The reason no one is accepting the current request is because they know what kind of person Rei is.

If this request weren't from Rei, but from some adventurer with no track record, people would definitely come looking to rip him off.

And if the word "certainty" were removed, there's no doubt some would try to cover things up as Kenny suggested, even if Vihera never woke up.

Rumor has it that this herb can restore anyone from any state.

They say, from what I've heard, this potion can even bring the dead back to life.

They say, I heard from the daughter-in-law of the boss of the father of a friend of my master's master, that using this fruit can overcome any illness.

They say, they say, they say, they say...

With so many people piling on like that, it was certain Rei would explode.

"Right? ...Ah, yes. This is just to be sure, but I have a request from the Marnino Viscount family in the Noble Quarter. I told them you're too busy to handle it right now, but they insisted I pass it along anyway."

But you're going to refuse, right?

Kenny's words implied as much, but Rei thought for a moment... then finally spoke.

"No, I think I'll take the request, partly for a change of pace. I've been on the move for a while now, and I'm feeling stuck on a few things."

Kenny's ears perked up at Rei's unexpected words, his surprise plain to see.

"Seriously? Are you sure? Just so you know, it's Rei, the noble you hate."

"It's not like I hate all nobles or anything."

There were nobles Rei actually liked to some extent—Elena, Daskar, and others.

Of course, there were also nobles he absolutely despised.

"So, can I ask what kind of noble this Marnino Viscount is?"

"Sure. He's a royalist noble from the Noble Faction. I haven't heard any major bad rumors about him. Which, conversely, means there are some minor bad rumors."

"What about good rumors?"

"Nothing major there either, but there are a fair number of minor ones. Overall, he's the kind of noble who's neither harmful nor particularly useful."

"...That's quite a bold thing to say."

Kenny's words caught Rei off guard for a moment.

"It's fine, really. More importantly, are we going to Viscount Marnino's mansion today?"

"No, I'll go tomorrow. Since it's such an opportunity, I want to make sure I'm fully prepared first."

"In Rei's case, you don't really need to prepare, do you?"

Kenny said this while looking at the Misty Ring on Rei's right hand—more precisely, on his right wrist. Rei shrugged his shoulders slightly.
