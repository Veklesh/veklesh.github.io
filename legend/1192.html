Mirei, Surnin, and Ekrill entered the office and noticed a familiar face sitting on the sofa.

"Rei!? Eh? What are you doing here? Vihera too..."

Rei was, unsurprisingly, the first to cry out in obvious surprise.

She had spotted Ambris and reported it to the guild, which led to her being summoned to the guildmaster's office... only to find Rei and Vihera there for some reason.

It was no wonder she was shocked.

"You found Ambris?"

Unconcerned by Mireine's shock, Rei spoke up.

Rei's voice snapped her back to reality. Mireine flashed a smug look and opened her mouth.

"Heh heh, you haven't forgotten our promise, have you? The promise that if I found Ambris, I'd get to spend a day with Set-chan."

"Ah... yeah. Well, that's fine, but..."

If Set disliked Mireine, that would be one thing, but that wasn't the case.

In fact, Set was quite attached to Mireine.

Set had interacted with many people over the years, and among them, Mireine definitely ranked high in his list of favorites.

The raid on the orc settlement happened not long after Rei and Set arrived at Gilm.

After seeing Set during that incident, Mireine had acted as if she was devoted solely to him.

Petting him, doting on him, feeding him... With Set showing him such affection, there was no way Mireine could be disliked.

If Mireine asked to spend a whole day with Set, Rei wouldn't refuse unless there was some special reason—like an urgent request.

But that wasn't the problem here.

(Well, for Mireine, spending a day with Set is probably a big deal.)

In terms of liking Set, there was also a rival: Johanna, the former guerrilla fighter.

In that sense, Mireine's goal of distinguishing herself from such a rival isn't necessarily a bad one.

Rei gently averted his gaze from Mireine, who was looking at him with eyes full of expectation.

Vihera, at the end of his gaze, quietly looked away, and Marina likewise averted her eyes.

Left behind by both of them, Rei hesitates over how to speak, but finally opens his mouth.

"Mireine... Why do you think we're here?"

"Huh? That... why?"

"Well, asking me won't get you anywhere."

Mireine turned her gaze to Surnin, the smartest member of the party, but naturally, Surnin also didn't know why Rei was here.

The fact that Rei and Guildmaster Marina were close was not widely known.

That was precisely why Surnin couldn't understand why Rei and the others were here.

Still, considering the recent circumstances at Gilm, it wasn't entirely unexpected.

"Um, could it be something related to the Ambrith matter?"

"Correct. ...Though the situation has gotten a bit complicated."

Marina sighed as she spoke, drawing the gazes of the three members of the Scorching Wind.

Meeting their pleading gazes, Marina turned her eyes toward Rei and began to speak.

"Rei and the others found Ambrith too. And not just found him—they managed to defeat him."

"Huh!?"

Voices of astonishment escaped Mireine and the others.

The three stared intently at Rei.

They couldn't believe he'd defeated Anubis, but a thought crossed their minds: Could it be possible for Rei?

They knew all too well just how extraordinary Rei was.

"Really?"

Mireine asked timidly, as was only natural.

Though the fear in her heart stemmed from the thought that finding Ambros might mean her day with Set was now erased.

But Rei, coldly, nodded back at Mireine.

Mireine's expression darkened with despair at the simple nod...

"Don't worry. Since finding Ambris is a fact, I won't pretend that bet never happened. In fact, I'd say it actually helped us."

"...Huh? Is that okay?"

Mireine clearly hadn't expected Rei to so readily grant her the day with Set. She looked at him with a stunned expression.

Faced with that gaze, Rei nodded, indicating it was fine.

In truth, the information Mireine and the others brought held immense significance.

Rei and the others had believed they had defeated Ambris, but in reality, Ambris was still out there.

This didn't necessarily mean Rei hadn't defeated Ambris... or more accurately, they'd believed they had defeated him, but it was possible he'd survived by mimicking death. However, another possibility existed. Namely...

(So there's more than one Ambris?)

Rei muttered bitterly, but even so, it was far better than letting time pass without knowing anything, only to be informed later that Ambris had reappeared.

Vihera and Marina shared Rei's thoughts, and Surnin, upon hearing Rei's claim of defeating Ambris, felt the same.

"Defeated Anubis... So what we saw was a different Anubis than the one that was defeated?"

Marina nodded at Surnin, who asked for clarification.

"Yes. It's highly likely the Umbris Rei defeated is a different individual from the one you found. Considering the time you discovered it, probably."

Of course, Mireine and the others didn't have a clock.

The estimate of about three hours ago when they found Ambris was based purely on their subjective sense of time.

Still, as a promising Rank C party, Mireine and the others should have had no error in the order of hours, even if there was some margin of error.

Even so, Sulnin, a veteran mage, possessed a keen sense of time precisely because of his experience.

"...This is troublesome."

Sulnin muttered. Milleine and Ecuril, who seemed to understand the situation, nodded in agreement.

Though Mireine was usually what you might call a Set-first advocate, she was, after all, the leader of the Scorching Wind party, and knew when to be firm.

"Yes. After Rei defeated Ambris, he mentioned feeling something off, so I knew this couldn't possibly end like this... It really is troublesome."

Surnin found himself momentarily captivated by Marina's eyes, filled with worry, despite his age. He quickly shook his head, realizing this wasn't the time for that.

"So, what do we do? I mean, I understand that since Ambrith's existence is nothing but a threat to Gilm, the only real option is to defeat him."

Yes, Surnin asked Marina.

Given her position as Guildmaster, Marina's answer was predetermined.

"We have no choice but to defeat him. I understand it's complicated in many ways."

Ambrith's ability to make humanoid monsters into leader species was extremely troublesome.

The danger level only increases if there are multiple such beings, not just one.

As Guildmaster of Gilm, and above all as a resident of Gilm, she absolutely could not leave Ambrith unchecked.

"But how do we defeat it? Even for Rei to defeat it, it took from the moment the Guildmaster anticipated Ambrith's existence until today, didn't it?"

Mireine's words held doubt, but no hint of resignation.

For her, as someone living in Gilm, leaving Ambrith unchecked was also non-negotiable.

...Though, a major part of that non-negotiable reason was that, as things stood, she couldn't spend a day with Set.

She had finally gotten permission from Rei to spend a day with Set, so she absolutely couldn't back down if she wanted to protect that blissful time.

(If Ambris keeps acting like this, there won't be any time to spend a leisurely day with little Set, right?)

In terms of finding Ambris, Set, who could fly, was an extremely important asset.

Given that, to experience the best day of Mireine's life, resolving the Ambris matter was absolutely necessary.

Ultimately, resolving the matter for Set's sake was something only Milleine would do.

"As for how to search... Well, unfortunately, for now, we have no choice but to keep using the same old manpower approach. If only there were some distinctive scent or magic signature to track, we could search for that, but..."

"Isn't there some kind of magic item for searching? The alchemists at Gilm have fairly advanced technology, right?"

"I've already asked them. ...But a magic item to search for Ambris with no clues whatsoever? That's not something you can just whip up easily. If only there were some kind of residue left behind... Rei?"

Marina cut herself off mid-sentence and turned her gaze toward Rei.

Yes, Rei had defeated Ambris once. If that were the case, it wouldn't be strange if some sort of remnant of Ambris remained from that encounter.

Numerous corpses transformed into leader types by Umbrus, like goblins and kobolds, had been brought in before.

It wasn't uncommon for dozens to be brought in daily.

It showed just how actively Ambris was moving, yet nothing resembling Ambris's remnants had been obtained from them.

Well, perhaps with more time, clues leading to Umbrus could have been found. But even if Umbrus was involved, too much time had passed since it had moved on, making it impossible to find what could be called pure Umbrus residue.

But... Rei had defeated the main body of Ambris directly. If that was the case, might there be remnants of Ambris that hadn't been altered?

Marina's gaze fixed on Rei, and Rei tilted his head, unsure what she wanted.

Realizing Rei hadn't grasped what she meant, Marina spoke, partly to confirm her own thoughts.

"Rei, how did you defeat Ambris?"

"How... I sliced through the Ambrosis possessing the werewolf along with its body using the Death Scythe, then pierced it with the Twilight Spear."

"...Could you show me the Death Scythe?"

"Is the Twilight Spear okay?"

"Yes. Just the Death Scythe for now."

Without hesitation, Rei nodded in response to Marina's request.

Had anyone else asked to see the Death Scythe, he might have hesitated somewhat.

But this request came from Marina herself.

There was no will within Rei to refuse it.

The Death Scythe, retrieved from Misty Ring, appeared within the office.

Mireine and the others of the Scorching Wind had seen this weapon, which could be called Rei's symbol, many times before.

But seeing it brought out again like this, right here in the room, they were still overwhelmed by its sheer size.

How could she, with her small frame, wield a Death Scythe larger than Rei himself?

Ignoring the members of Scorching Wind, Marina rose from her chair and approached Rei, who held the Death Scythe.

She stared intently at the massive blade and opened her mouth.

"Hey, Rei. Could I borrow the Death Scythe for a bit?"

"...Depends on how long. Even if we're searching for Ambris, without the Death Scythe my combat effectiveness drops significantly."

Since acquiring the Twilight Spear, Rei's fighting style had shifted to a dual-spear technique.

Even so, the core of his combat remains the Death Scythe—the catalyst for his skills and magic.

Without the Death Scythe, it could be said Rei's combat effectiveness would be halved.

Especially now that we're dealing with Ambros, the Death Scythe was absolutely essential.

"Right. ...I'd have to ask an alchemist about that, but if things go well, it might only take a few minutes."

"If we're attacking Ambris, wouldn't the Twilight Spear be useless?"

Vihera, who had been listening to Rei and Marina's conversation, asked.

"I wonder. Considering its capabilities as a magic item, the Twilight Spear is quite high-level, but the Death Scythe is incomparably superior. If Umbris's remnants remain, then the Death Scythe is probably the way to go."

"How about we ask the alchemist about that? I've got other things to do, so I can't agree right away."

And so, Rei set off to meet the alchemist.
