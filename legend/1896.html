The day after the party in Marina's backyard, Rei was standing in front of the guild.

Despite it being winter, a large crowd had gathered in front of the guild to show their affection for Set.

This is likely largely because Rei and Set had been away from Gilm for a while.

Many of those who had always doted on Set were eager to spend time with him now that they were seeing him again after so long.

...In reality, though, Set hadn’t been in Gilm for even a full month.

In any case, seeing Set being doted on by everyone, Rei decided he wouldn’t feel lonely here and entered the guild.

The first thing Rei noticed upon entering the guild was how crowded it was.

For Rei, who had spent several winters in Gilm, seeing so many people inside the guild during the winter season felt a bit odd.

If there had been a large crowd in the tavern area even inside the guild, Rei probably wouldn’t have minded it that much.

But in the current guild, most of the adventurers were gathered not at the tavern, but around the quest board and reception desk.

(I suppose this is due to the kobolds, too.)

The fact that kobold raids are occurring means that their materials, magic stones, and proof-of-defeat body parts can be sold for money.

These adventurers are probably here for that very reason.

That said, the adventurers here are mostly those who are short on money or those who want to have a little extra cash set aside to get through the winter—a fairly small minority even among the adventurers in Gilm.

Despite that, the reason there are so many adventurers inside the guild is likely because many came here for the expansion construction work and never left Gilm even after winter set in.

Ignoring the chatter of those forming permanent parties or temporary ones for kobold hunts, Rei heads toward the counter.

Unsurprisingly, a considerable number of adventurers were already lined up in front of the counter.

He had deliberately avoided the morning rush and arrived at the guild around nine o’clock, but even so, the sheer number of people present was unexpected, even to Rei.

(Well, judging by this, it must have been even more crowded this morning. Thinking about it that way, coming at this time was definitely the right choice… or so I’d like to believe.)

With those thoughts in mind, Rei joined the line, but unusually—or perhaps naturally—no one approached him to hassle him.

In the past, adventurers who didn’t know Rei would sometimes try to pick a fight with him, but this was Gilm—Rei’s home, so to speak.

Although the number of adventurers had surged due to the expansion project, even those newcomers had heard stories about Rei from other adventurers and recognized him as someone they absolutely must not mess with.

Above all, since Rei had been quite active during the expansion work, there were few who didn’t know his face.

If he’d been wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, there might have been some who didn’t recognize him and tried to pick a fight, but since Rei has his hood off now, anyone can see his face.

As a result, Rei stands before Lenora, the receptionist in charge, without causing any particular commotion.

“Is this regarding the kobold…?”

Having dealt with countless kobold-related matters before, Lenora assumed this was just another one of those cases, but the moment she realized who was standing before her, a look of utter astonishment spread across her face.

The fact that she didn’t scream or anything probably just shows how professional she is. Although…

“Ahh! Rei!? When did you get back!?”

Kenny, a cat-like beastman with a figure that attracts men, standing next to Lenora, shouts with a beaming smile the moment she sees Rei—a stark contrast to Lenora’s reaction.

To Kenny, Rei was someone special.

Lenora knew this, which is why she didn’t say anything in response to Kenny’s reaction… but instead, the other guild staff working behind the counter were shooting sharp glances at Kenny.

Due to the kobold incident, the guild has been extremely busy lately.

Precisely because of that, Kenny’s attitude was unwelcome to the others amidst such a hectic workload.

“Even though I’m working my hardest…”

“Yesterday. So, as we’d agreed before, I was planning to have you dissect the Giganto Turtle… but when I got back, I heard that kobolds have been attacking almost every day. I came to the guild to see what we should do about it.”

I also came to ask if there were any high-tier or rare species related to the kobold situation, but since that’s pretty much a long shot, I didn’t mention it.

“Ah, that matter. I’ve received instructions from the Guild Master regarding that. The reward will be lower than for a Kobold hunt. However, the proposal you made earlier, Rei, about transferring a small amount of Giganto Turtle meat—that’s not a problem.”

Rei wasn’t sure if that was a fair offer.

But he could guess that the price was set lower than usual.

After all, if they didn’t do that, everyone looking for a quick buck would rush to butcher the Giganto Turtles, so it wouldn’t be strange to set it that way.

(Though, that would create its own problems. I had intended to finish butchering the Giganto Turtle by spring, but if manpower gets tied up with the kobolds… there’s a high chance we won’t finish by this winter.)

To begin with, given the Giganto Turtle’s size, it was unclear whether the dismantling would even be finished this winter, even if every adventurer in Gilm pitched in.

As the name implies, it’s a massive—no, an enormous monster, so it’s a real pain that we can’t calculate in advance just how long it will take to dismantle it.

Still, once we actually start the dismantling, we’ll be able to make a rough estimate based on the pace of the work… but that’s only after we’ve actually started.

“Lenora, just to be sure, if the reward is low and few adventurers show up, does the Guild send people to help?”

“Yes. They definitely do. There are many former adventurers in the guild, and some are well-versed in monster disassembly. …When the kobold incident first happened, there were some who left the disassembly entirely to the guild, but there are hardly any of those left now.”

“Well, when you look at the price of kobolds versus the dismemberment costs, a lot of people figured they could at least break even. And if the kobold was in bad shape, the dismemberment costs would go up, so many ended up in the red. So, more people started giving up on the kobold materials and just bringing back the proof-of-kill parts and magic stones.”

Kenny interrupts Lenora’s conversation, but upon hearing this, Rei becomes worried for a different reason.

“Wait, is it okay to leave the kobold corpses there? They could turn into undead. It’s fine now since it’s winter, but once spring comes, the corpses might start rotting.”

“Oh, that’s not a problem. We’ve left that to the people in the slums. To be honest, the ones who’ve benefited the most from this whole kobold situation are probably the people in the slums. At the very least, they won’t have to worry about food.”

In the slums during winter, there would normally be many who starve or freeze to death.

……Even so, the number of people in the slums isn’t decreasing; if anything, it’s gradually increasing. That’s because more people are settling in the slums than are dying… but that’s beside the point.

With special permission from Daskar, the people of the slums gather the kobold corpses scattered across the battlefield and transport them back to the slums.

There, they butcher the bodies themselves, making effective use of the meat for their own food and the materials—especially the fur—for their own clothing.

In reality, the hides are far from usable in their raw state, but since the slum dwellers possess a variety of skills—including some who can tan hides—those with such abilities make a considerable profit.

Normally, tanning hides takes a certain amount of time, but some people—whether they obtained them from somewhere or brought them in themselves—use magic items to shorten the process.

Some people even left behind body parts and magic stones from low-rank monsters like kobolds, finding them too much trouble to collect, so those who had such items could use their connections to turn them into cash.

Overall, the residents of the slums were able to reap significant benefits from the kobold raids.

Some even managed to earn enough through luck and resourcefulness to leave the slums altogether.

After hearing this explanation from Lenora, Rei was amazed by the resilience of the slum dwellers.

“For ordinary residents, the kobold raids are nothing but a nuisance, but for the slum dwellers, they’re a golden opportunity to make money.”

“That’s right. Apparently, even among Gilm’s higher-ups, there were opinions questioning whether it was okay to let the slum dwellers handle the kobolds, but if we didn’t, the construction sites would end up as piles of corpses.”

“...I suppose so.”

From what Rei had seen from above when he returned to Gilm yesterday, there appeared to be about a hundred kobolds.

Considering that such a large number of kobolds were attacking every day—and not just once a day, but multiple times—it was obvious that disposing of all those bodies would be a huge hassle.

“Well, I get the situation now. So, what should I do? Should I come here tomorrow morning?”

“That’s right. However… please don’t come at this time of day; come earlier—around six o’clock, if possible.”

Um... Rei almost let out a slightly annoyed groan, but when he thought about it, Lenora’s request made perfect sense.

Since the Giganto Turtle to be butchered was stored in Rei’s Misty Ring, it was only natural that the job couldn’t be done without it.

That meant, in order to start the dismantling, Rei would inevitably have to coordinate with the other adventurers.

From the guild’s perspective, since the reward was coming out of the guild’s or Gilm’s coffers, they naturally wanted the dismantling done as quickly as possible.

That said, they certainly couldn’t just leave the Giganto Turtle outside Gilm in the middle of the night.

Doing so would attract not only the kobolds that attack Gilm, but other monsters as well.

Especially since they’d be butchering it outside Gilm, monsters drawn by the smell of blood would certainly show up, and hiring personnel to defeat such monsters—especially now in winter—would cost a considerable amount of money.

Moreover, since this was the frontier and it was winter, the monsters drawn by the scent of blood would naturally include high-rank monsters, and it was clear that the adventurers serving as guards would need considerable strength to defeat such foes.

“Ah… yeah. Got it. So, I should come to the guild at 6 a.m., right?”

“Yes. Well… it’ll probably be pretty crowded, though…”

“Rei, have you ever come to the guild at that time of day before? If it’s your first time, you’re definitely going to be surprised.”

Kenny added to Lenora’s words, giving a sly smile.

“I’d rather not, if I could help it… but I can’t keep putting off the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle forever.”

If Rei hadn’t gone off to Anesis, there’s no doubt the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle would have been done long ago.

Since that wasn’t possible, Rei couldn’t very well reject the guild’s request, especially after all the trouble he’d caused them.

...Though if Rei had flat-out refused, the Guild would likely have accommodated him by postponing the dismantling.

After finishing up some minor details with Lenora, Rei tried to leave the guild so as not to disturb the other adventurers…

“Hey, Rei! Want to grab lunch together today? Ouch!”

Kenny tried to invite Rei to lunch, but was immediately shut down by Lenora.

Holding the rolled-up documents she’d just brought down on Kenny’s head, Lenora spoke with an exasperated tone.

“Listen, you know I’ve been busy these past few days, right? It’s not like I can just take a break the moment it’s lunchtime. …Are you planning to be the only one to slip away in the middle of all this, Kenny?”

With Lenora’s piercing gaze fixed on him, Kenny tries to retort, but her intensity leaves him speechless.

Perhaps satisfied with Kenny’s reaction, Lenora shifted her gaze from him, broke into a smile, and addressed Rei.

“I’m sorry, Rei. Please don’t worry about Kenny.”

“……That seems like the best course of action.”

Kenny shot Rei a pleading look, but sensing that if he stayed there, Lenora’s anger might turn on him as well, he walked away.

All the while, he could hear Lenora lecturing Kenny from behind him.
