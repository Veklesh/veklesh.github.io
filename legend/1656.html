“I heard that bandits were gathering around here—could that be you guys?”

The one who spoke was a man standing in a position that seemed to be leading the approaching soldiers.

He looked to be in his mid-thirties.

There was a sly gleam in his eyes, and he was clearly looking down on Rei and his group.

Seeing such a man, the other adventurers besides Rei felt not irritation… but rather an emotion that could best be described as admiration.

At the very least, they knew they themselves couldn’t adopt such a haughty attitude in the presence of Rei and Set. That was why they understood.

“Now then, you are bandits who cause harm to Sublusta. Is that a fair assessment?”

“That’s right! These guys attacked us without warning while we were just hunting in the forest!”

When Rei and the others were asked this question, one of the captured bandits was the first to respond.

Then, as if in agreement with that bandit’s voice, the other bandits also claimed they had been captured even though they hadn’t done anything.

Hearing the bandits’ words, the man leading the soldiers feigned exaggerated surprise.

“Well now! I never imagined we’d come across a group of illegal slave traders who capture innocent people! We’ll have to arrest all of you and get to the bottom of this!”

“That’s right! Help us! If this keeps up, we’ll be sold into slavery even though we’ve done nothing wrong!”

As if echoing the man’s words, another bandit shouts.

The other captured bandits, perhaps harboring the hope that they might be saved, all protest in unison that they are innocent—that they were forcibly captured in the forest by adventurers… no, by illegal slave traders.

(So, this has turned into a real mess after all.)

Rei, whose prediction that seeing these soldiers would lead to trouble had come true, looked up at the endless blue sky, pleading for some kind of solution.

Even though summer was drawing to a close, the midday sun was still asserting itself with plenty of intensity.

A breeze flowing in from the forest calms the frustration simmering inside Rei just a little, and he opens his mouth.

“Shut up.”

It wasn’t said with particular force, nor was it a shout.

Yet, strangely, the words echoed through the air, silencing the bandits who had been clamoring that they’d been captured against their will.

“Hmm, judging by the situation just now, it seems you’re the one leading the slave traders.”

The man leading the guards addressed Rei with a sadistic grin.

He made no effort to hide his conviction that even an adventurer wouldn’t dare defy his words.

“Slave traders? What are you talking about? We’re adventurers. It feels a bit odd to say this myself, but I never thought there’d be anyone living around here who didn’t know who I am. Right, Set?”

“Grrrrrrrr.”

At Rei’s words, Set, who had been patted on the head, growled.

For Set, that gesture was nothing out of the ordinary.

However, he probably didn’t like the fact that there was someone who was clearly trying to antagonize Rei.

It wasn’t his usual affectionate purr, but a growl that sounded like a threat.

Hearing that growl, the man who had spoken provocatively to Rei instinctively took a few steps back, bumping into the guard behind him and finally snapping back to his senses.

……If there hadn’t been a guard behind him at that moment, he likely wouldn’t have been able to withstand Set’s pressure and would have fallen flat on his backside.

In that sense, the man should have been grateful to the guard.

But instead, the man glared at the guard as if to say, “Don’t get in my way,” and then, looking frightened—though he was actually frightened by instinct—he glared at Rei in an attempt to hide his embarrassment.

The reason he glared at Rei rather than Set, whom he was actually afraid of, was that Set—no matter how much of a familiar he might be—was ultimately a monster, and since there was no telling when he might attack him, he didn’t want to provoke him any further.

Compared to Set, Rei was, after all, human. …Although, strictly speaking, he was an android created using the pinnacle of the Zepairu clan’s technology, but there was no way the man could know that.

“A-are you trying to threaten me!?”

“Huh? Threaten? Why would you say that? Set just agreed with what I said, didn’t he? Besides, I didn’t actually attack you or anything. Do you feel threatened just because a dog or a cat barks?”

A coward who’s afraid of dogs and cats.

Implicitly mocked, the man’s face turned bright red with embarrassment as he spoke up.

“You!? You, you say! Who do you think I am?! Someone like you has no right to speak to me that way!”

“That’s all well and good. Unfortunately, I don’t know your name. Well, since you don’t seem to know mine, I’ll tell you: I’m Rei. I go by the alias ‘Crimson’.”

“Hmph, what’s a mere man with a nickname acting so high and mighty for? But I’ll give you credit for introducing yourself before asking my name. I am Talanian. I serve the magistrate of Sublusta.”

The man who introduced himself as Tarania seemed to think that simply stating his name and position would make Rei and the others back down, and he fixed his gaze on them with a look that said, “Well, what do you think?”

But Rei, who was on the receiving end of that gaze, looked back at Tarania without seeming particularly bothered.

Rei was the sort of person who, even when facing a noble, would never hold back if a conflict arose.

As for nobles, that’s one thing, but just because Tarania—a subordinate of a magistrate sent by a noble—gave his name, there was no reason for him to be surprised… let alone back down.

“So? What business does Tarania, a subordinate of the magistrate, have with us?”

Despite having identified herself, she was addressed simply as “Tarania”… and the fact that someone of Rei’s stature had treated her that way caused Tarania’s face to flush red once more.

“W-what do you think you’re doing! I’m a direct subordinate of the magistrate! And yet, some adventurer like you… Understand your place!”

“That’s easy for you to say. I’m an adventurer affiliated with Gilm to begin with. It’s not like I’ve never been to Sublusta, but that’s beside the point today. So, just because Tarania showed up here, how am I supposed to respond?”

Faced with Rei’s matter-of-fact words, Tarania was so enraged she couldn’t utter another word.

Up until now, within Sublusta, her words held absolute power as a direct subordinate of the magistrate.

Yet the man standing before her remained completely unperturbed in her presence, and that was what annoyed her most of all.

“Y-you! For a mere adventurer…”

“Huh? ‘Just’? Did you just say ‘just’? I see. So the Lord of Sublusta and his magistrate regard adventurers as ‘just’—that’s how they view us, is it? That’s quite an insight you’ve given me.”

A twitch.

Talanian’s cheeks twitched at Rei’s words.

No matter what she truly thought, if she went so far as to voice it directly, it could become the official stance of that person… and, depending on the situation, even of her own superior.

What’s more, Tarania had already stated that she was a direct subordinate of the magistrate.

It would have been fine if the person she’d spoken to was truly just an ordinary adventurer, but the person she’d spoken to this time was Rei, an adventurer with a nickname.

Realizing, objectively speaking, that she had said something quite unwise, Tarania cleared her throat softly.

“Well, I suppose I went a bit too far myself. But you’re at fault too. You’re supposed to show respect to your elders, aren’t you?”

“That’s easier said than done. I’d respect you if you were someone worth respecting…”

That was tantamount to implying—no, practically stating outright—that Tarania was not someone worthy of respect.

Tarania’s face flushed red again at those words, but this time she remained silent, careful not to let any more careless remarks slip out.

Watching Tarania like that, Rei spoke up again.

“Now, let’s get back to the matter at hand. I assume the people you’re bringing along are the ones who came to transport the bandits we captured, but may I ask why you’re with them?”

“Bandits? What are you talking about? Didn’t I just tell you? I came to capture an illegal slave trader.”

As she said this, Tarania suddenly seemed to realize something, and a sticky, almost leering grin spread across her face.

“Right. So, that means you’re adventurers… and not just any adventurers, but ones with nicknames—and yet you’ve captured innocent civilians and turned them into slaves, is that correct?”

At Tarania’s words, Rei turned his gaze toward the bandits the other adventurers had captured.

They claim they did nothing wrong—that they were simply hunting in the forest when they were forcibly captured.

“……Ordinary people? Those guys? I think that’s a bit of a stretch.”

“Huh!?”

At Rei’s words, Tarania was momentarily at a loss for words.

Tarania, too, must have understood that it was a stretch to call the captured bandits “ordinary people.”

But even so, from Tarania’s perspective, she couldn’t let the bandits be taken away by Rei… no, by Rei and his group.

(This is bad. No matter what flimsy excuse I have to come up with, I simply must get those bandits back from them. Of all people… is that what you’re saying?)

Tarania’s gaze fell upon a few of the captives held by the adventurers.

At the very least, she needed to have those people handed over to her; at worst, she’d have to silence them.

After all, they couldn’t let anyone know of their connection to us.

(If they were adventurers from Sublusta, I could have come up with any excuse. But of all people, from Gilm... They’re really giving me a hard time.)

The one who orchestrated this incident was Gilm… and more likely than not… no, almost certainly, it was Daskar, the central figure of the Neutral Faction.

If such a figure were to learn of what we’ve been doing and secure these thieves—who could be called witnesses—then nothing but certain ruin awaits us.

Given that, Tarania has absolutely no choice but to fight here.

Even if it means fighting the adventurers right here and now.

(We have no chance of defeating Gilm’s adventurers. I know that. But I don’t need to win. As long as I silence them, that’s all that matters.)

Among the guards Talanian has brought along this time are a few who serve as assassins.

The plan was that, if push came to shove, they would make it look like a scuffle with the adventurers while those agents carried out their mission.

To achieve that, I shouldn’t have meekly retreated here just because they told me to; instead, I should have provoked them and forced a fight.

Of course, the best option would have been to get the bandits handed over without a fight, but that’s not possible under the current circumstances.

Since fighting is the last resort—and one of the worst options—it should be avoided at all costs. Still, I took a few seconds to pat myself on the back for not cutting corners, but then I cleared my throat and spoke up.

“If you’re saying you didn’t capture these slaves illegally… well then. Why don’t you hand over a few of the people you captured to us?”

“Huh? Why?”

Before I knew it… truly, before I knew it, Rei was negotiating with Tarania as the representative of the adventurers present, yet none of the adventurers here seemed to mind.

Well, I don’t know what they’re thinking deep down, but as things stood, no one was showing any signs of dissatisfaction on their faces.

If anything, their honest feeling was probably that they wanted the negotiations to end quickly so they could get out of this sweltering place as soon as possible.

...That said, they absolutely could not tolerate having the bandits they’d captured after a confrontation taken away from them.

In front of these adventurers, the negotiations continue.

“If you insist that you haven’t done anything illegal, then we need to obtain information from you as well. Oh, and let me make this clear: we cannot accept you passing information obtained by you to us… because there’s a possibility you’d only pass on information that suits your interests.”

“If you put it that way, does that mean you can’t trust the information that came from the mouth of the guy you took away either?”

“I see. Are you suggesting that my position is the same as that of an adventurer or a magistrate’s direct subordinate?”

“If that’s the case, does that mean you can’t trust the information gathered by Gilm’s guards either? Or are you saying that no one from Gilm is trustworthy?”

“That’s…”

Such a sly move.

Yes, Tarania sneered inwardly.

It’s common knowledge that Gilm’s guards are elite.

If she were to say here that she couldn’t trust Gilm’s guards either, it would lead to all sorts of trouble later on.

But then again, she couldn’t very well let them take away the bandits who were actually connected to them. What should she do…? Just as she was pondering this, a voice suddenly rang out.

“What on earth is going on here?”

The one who spoke was a beautiful woman with golden hair, as if the sunlight itself had taken form... Elena.
