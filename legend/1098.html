The house that came into view was far too grand for a mere merchant to live in.

Rei had anticipated it would have stables large enough for Set, but it was considerably larger than he had imagined.

Of course, it wasn't quite a mansion, but to Rei, who usually lived in inns, it was more than impressive.

...Though it should be noted that the inn where Rei usually stayed was the Dusk Wheat inn, one of the higher-end establishments in Gilm, ranked near the top.

(It was only slightly smaller than the mansion where the former guerrilla squad members lived... or was it? Well, they were living communally with several people over there.)

What crossed Rei's mind were the members of the guerrilla squad who had fought alongside him during the civil war in the Bestir Empire.

Among them were those who came to Gilm for various reasons: those who never wanted to be enemies with the Mireana Kingdom where Rei resided, those who admired Rei's strength, those interested in the Mireana Kingdom, and those who thought Set was cute...

Of course, not all of them lived together, but still, many former members of the guerrilla squad lived in one mansion.

What lay before Rei's gaze now was a mansion slightly smaller than that one, yet clearly ample for a single family to reside in.

"...It's quite a large house."

"Is that so? I'm glad to hear you say that. ...Well, I didn't build this house myself; it was passed down through generations of my ancestors. The ancestor who built it was one of the foremost merchants in Gosh."

"Merchants for generations?"

"Yes. They were all far more talented than I. That's why they could build such a mansion. ...I can barely manage to maintain it myself."

Auguste said this, but even though he was being pushed around by the Elema Trading Company, he strove for honest dealings and was deeply trusted.

...Which is probably why he's managed to keep this mansion going, however precariously.

"Now, let's go. My wife should be waiting with a delicious meal prepared."

Urged by Auguste, I headed toward the mansion gates.

"Oh, Auguste. You're quite early today. ...And you?"

A middle-aged man who appeared to be the gatekeeper called out to Auguste.

It felt less like addressing an employer and more like greeting a friend.

"Günter, sorry to bother you again. Staying hydrated? Don't overdo it. This gentleman here is my guest."

Auguste addressed the man called Günter in a slightly more casual tone than he used with Rei or Zarust.

"This is Rei, who saved us from the Sand Serpent. And this is Rei's familiar, Set."

"Oh? You saved August? Then I must welcome you. Welcome, Rei, Set. I'm Günter, the gatekeeper of this house. Nice to meet you."

Gunter casually replied to Rei, then immediately turned his gaze toward Set.

Unusually for someone seeing a griffin, he showed no particular surprise.

Still, he didn't reach out to touch Set. Was it caution toward the creature... or simply because, regardless of his manner of speaking, he was doing his job as a gatekeeper properly?

Either way, the fact that he didn't look down on Rei and Set or make a fuss about them definitely made a good impression on Rei.

"Ah, nice to meet you."

"Grr."

Hearing the brief greetings from the man and the beast, Günter smiled and nodded in return.

After exchanging such brief words, Rei and Set finally entered the gate, guided by August.

"Auguste!"

As if waiting specifically for them... or more accurately, for August alone, a woman came running from the direction of the mansion.

She appeared to be in her thirties, like August.

Her features would normally suggest a gentle nature, but right now, her expression showed nothing but sheer agitation.

"Cathy, why are you running like that? Look, I'm fine, so you don't need to worry so much."

"But the messenger sent for Auguste was attacked by Sand Serpents and sand pirates! Of course I'm worried! Are you hurt? Are you really okay?"

As she spoke, the woman called Kathy proceeded to check directly for any injuries on August.

Auguste, being touched all over, suppressed a giggle, though he understood his wife was concerned for his well-being and that this was why she was doing this.

That's precisely why he gently stroked the hair neatly trimmed at the nape of Cathy's neck, weaving words to calm her.

"Rei and Set helped me out, so I'm fine."

"Really? Are you absolutely sure you're okay?"

"Yes."

Auguste nodded immediately.

But it was also an undeniable fact.

The Sand Serpent's head had been easily obliterated by the single strike of the Twilight Spear that Rei had unleashed. The Sand Pirates had attacked aboard their sand ships, but most of them had been rendered immobile by the roar unleashed through Set's skill, the King's Intimidation.

The only trouble for August and Zarust was binding the immobilized sand pirates and keeping watch to ensure they didn't act strangely while heading to Gosh.

Of course, that was difficult in its own way, but it was infinitely easier than fighting a hopeless battle.

Hearing this explanation, Kathy finally breathed a sigh of relief.

And then, naturally, her gaze shifted to Rei... or rather, to Set, whose overwhelming size dwarfed even Rei.

"Kyaaah!"

Cathy screamed and hid behind Auguste.

Seeing this, Set let out a slightly disappointed growl... but being feared by people he met for the first time was nothing new, so he left it at that.

It was actually rare for someone to not be afraid of Set upon first meeting, like the gatekeeper Günther had been earlier.

"It's okay. Set's gentle. He'd never hurt Kathy."

"...Really?"

"Yes. Have I ever lied to Cathy before?"

At August's words, Cathy shook her head slightly and fixed a passionate gaze on her husband.

Auguste met her gaze with equal intensity, and soon their faces drew closer...

"Um, ahem. I'd appreciate it if you could do that sort of thing somewhere private."

Rei's words snapped them back to reality. August and Cathy pulled apart abruptly, just before their lips touched.

Watching this, Rei felt a pang of sympathy for Günter, whom he'd only just met. Was he always subjected to this kind of exchange?

And he suddenly understood why Zarust hadn't come with them.

(He knew about this. ...Well, I did get a bad feeling when Auguste started bragging about his beloved wife... but I never imagined both spouses would be this type. Maybe we should find another inn... but that seems difficult. Should we just camp out?)

As Rei hesitated, seriously considering whether he should leave right now, watching the two scrambling to save face, Auguste—probably not sensing his thoughts—hurriedly spoke up.

"Cathy, you heard from the messenger, right? I'd like to have Rei stay with us for a while. Is that okay?"

"If you say so, I won't object. And that griffin seems calm and well-behaved enough, from what I can tell."

Rei couldn't help but smile wryly at how quickly she'd changed her tune, making her wonder where all that fear from when she first saw Set had gone.

"Come on in. Oh, wait. Before that, we need to show that griffin... um, Set-chan, right? We need to show Set-chan where the stables are. August, could you handle that?"

"Ah, leave it to me. If it's a request from you, I'd even bring back Dalidra 's head."

"...I appreciate it, but that's not quite a joke."

After a moment's silence, she heard his voice declare it, making Rei feel he was half-serious about wanting that to happen.

(Seems like a pretty deep-rooted problem. ...Well, hearing the story, I guess it's no wonder. Tira's tree... huh?)

Rei recalled the story he'd heard from Auguste.

The Tira Tree, which only exterminates monsters. Dalidra apparently plans to plant them surrounding Gosh.

The walls surrounding Gosh were also becoming quite dilapidated, and repairing them would require enormous costs.

Compared to that, planting Tira Trees would cost some money, but it would be considerably less than repairing the walls.

(Both opinions make sense, though.)

While Dalidra proposes planting Tira trees to keep costs down, Auguste opposes this and advocates for repairing the walls.

Of course, both were undoubtedly concerned for Gosh, but as merchants, that wasn't their only motivation.

Planting Tira trees would likely bring substantial profits to the Elema Trading Company, and that probably applied to Auguste as well.

But Rei had no intention of blaming him for that. As merchants, seeking profit was only natural.

"Right here. This stable isn't in use at the moment, so it's fine for Set to use it."

Auguste led them to stables located a short distance from the mansion.

The interior was spacious, but it seemed unused now. Aside from Set, Rei, and Auguste, there was no sign of life, giving it a somewhat desolate atmosphere.

There were newer stables than the one currently in front of Rei, but those seemed to be occupied by camels at the moment.

Set would have preferred not to be the only one in the stable, as it felt lonely, but no matter how much he did nothing, the camels wouldn't get used to the griffins right away.

And since Augustus's business would suffer without the camels, he couldn't possibly put Set and the camels in the same stable.

"Grrrr..."

Rei reached out and gently stroked Set, who was growling softly, expressing his loneliness.

"Don't worry. I'll be right there in the mansion, and if anything happens, I'll come visit."

As he stroked Set reassuringly, the loneliness seemed to subside a little. Set purred slightly, looking a bit happier, and rubbed his body against Rei.

For about a minute, Rei kept stroking Set.

Auguste simply watched the scene of one man and one beast.

(Rei and Set. Without this man and this dog, I would undoubtedly be dead. ...Meeting Rei, the adventurer with the alias, was unquestionably a stroke of luck. To protect Gosh, I must draw him into my group at any cost.)

To Auguste, Rei was an exceptionally attractive prospect.

Rei's personal combat prowess was worthy of his nickname, and his familiar was a rank A monster, a Griffin.

...Officially, it was classified as a rare species and treated as rank S, but Auguste had never seen Set use its skills.

And above all, what suited Auguste perfectly was that Rei was a Gilm... an adventurer from the Kingdom of Mireana.

This meant that even after this matter concluded, Rei would fundamentally have no reason to remain in Gosh.

It wasn't that Auguste disliked Rei. But having someone with overwhelming power around inevitably led to reliance on them.

As a resident of Gosh, that was something he wanted to avoid.

"Well then, shall we go? I'm sure your wife has prepared a delicious meal for you, Rei. I'll make sure to prepare Set's food properly too, so look forward to it."

At those words from Auguste, Set, who had seemed lonely just seconds before, purred happily.

Seeing his partner's overly straightforward reaction, a smile slipped onto Rei's lips.

But there was no doubt that a delicious meal was incredibly appealing to Rei as well.

"Set, I'll come back later."

"Gurrrrr!"

Rei let out a cry that said, I'll be waiting!

Seeing Set off, Rei and Auguste left the stables and headed toward the mansion.

"There's a famous dish in Gosh called Panita."

"Panita?"

The unfamiliar name piqued Rei's curiosity.

Meals were one of Rei's greatest pleasures. You could even call it a hobby.

But there was a vast difference between the gourmet cuisine often spoken of by nobles and Rei's meals.

For most nobles, gourmet cuisine meant dishes made from rare, expensive ingredients, painstakingly prepared by master chefs.

Compared to that, Rei was satisfied with food that was cheap but tasty.

Dishes like stewed offal and beans, the kind served in taverns, were among Rei's favorites, even though he didn't care for alcohol.

"Yes. You grill a chunk of meat, slice it thinly, then wrap it in a thin pancake made from wheat dissolved in water, along with vegetables and beans."

"...That sounds like a dish I've heard of before."

Suddenly, the name "doner kebab" flashed through Rei's mind—something he'd eaten at a festival food stall back in Japan.

While there were likely differences—thinly sliced meat, yogurt used for seasoning—based on the description, Rei's image matched doner kebab perfectly.
