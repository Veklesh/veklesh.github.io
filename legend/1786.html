The dinner with the Duke of Kerebel and his family went off without a hitch.

Feeling relieved about that, Rei lay down on his bed.

"Well, I guess I’ve managed to clear the first hurdle, at least."

He felt that his first meeting with Elena’s parents had gone reasonably well.

However, that was merely Rei’s own impression; he still didn’t know how it had actually gone.

That said, judging by the reactions of Duke Kerebel and his wife, Arcadia, it surely hadn’t been a complete disaster.

(I’m grateful to Georgima for that.)

After all, they served him a meal that made him want to nitpick about all sorts of things, and thanks to that, he didn’t have to get into a deep conversation with Kerebel Duke.

……Of course, since it concerns Elena, there’s no doubt we’ll have to have a serious talk eventually, so strictly speaking, it’s just that I’ve bought myself a little more time until then.

Rei understood that, but to be honest, he’d have preferred not to have things progress that far on their very first meeting.

(For now, since I don’t have anything specific to do for the next few days starting tomorrow, I’ll go check on Set…)

It was his first time in Anesis, and although Elena had told him that Yellow would be there with her, he still wanted to see her face for himself.

With those thoughts in mind... Rei eventually couldn’t resist the warmth of the bed, and, feeling a sense of relief at having successfully finished the dinner with Elena’s parents, drifted off to sleep.

“What did you think?”

Around the time Rei had fallen asleep, Duke Kerebel was enjoying some time with his wife in their bedroom, sipping wine together.

As her husband asked her this while eating dried meat—a special delicacy made by Georgima, no less—Arcadia, also savoring the aroma of the wine, set the glass she was holding down on the table.

“What do you mean, ‘what did you think’? Are you talking about Rei?”

“Yes. From what I saw, he seemed like a decent enough man. After all, he’s already achieved quite a lot.”

“...But that’s strictly as an adventurer, isn’t it? I’ve been gathering information on him myself, and I don’t think he’s suited to being a noble at all.”

Naturally, since her beloved daughter was involved, Arcadia had been gathering information on Rei as well.

She hadn’t let it show during her conversation with Rei, but she was well aware of the general outline of his background.

Indeed, Rei had become famous overnight in the past few years.

The story goes that before that, he was training under a wizard living deep in the mountains, but as Elena’s mother, Arcadia couldn’t simply take that at face value.

That said, based on their conversation today, he didn’t strike her as the type to pull off such a ruse.

Still, as long as there were suspicious elements, there was no doubt she couldn’t trust him completely.

“That’s right. …I’ve been gathering various pieces of information about Rei myself. Based on that information, I’ve concluded that it’s practically impossible for him to make it as a noble. He’s incredibly capable in combat, though.”

Judging by the war with the Bestir Empire, in terms of leading the charge on the battlefield as a noble, he’s unquestionably top-tier… no, he likely possesses even greater ability than that.

The fact that he possesses an item box is undoubtedly beneficial from a noble’s perspective.

Having a griffin as a familiar and being able to move extremely quickly is also a major advantage.

But... I simply couldn’t imagine him being capable of handling the essential duties of a noble.

Rei excels most at acting strictly as someone who is used by others.

That was the conviction Duke Kerebel had reached after speaking with Rei.

Above all, what makes Rei unsuitable as a noble is his fierce temperament. The fact that he would use force without mercy against anyone he dislikes—even if they are a noble—is practically fatal for someone trying to make it as a noble.

It might be different for an adventurer—though of course, that doesn’t mean an adventurer is allowed to use violence against a noble without condition—but for a noble to act that way is a fatal mistake.

While there are indeed some nobles who view commoners as little more than trash—believing they’ll multiply on their own even if left alone—it’s unthinkable that the leader of the Noble Faction would ever do such a thing.

“For nobles, there are many occasions to go to the battlefield. But managing and prospering one’s fiefdom is far more important than that.”

Though her tone was gentle, Arcadia spoke with strong conviction.

In fact, the main reason Arcadia is not usually in Anesis is to further develop the Duchy of Kerebel.

As the Duke of Kerebel, his true feelings were that he would prefer his wife to be by his side... but the fact remained that, thanks to Arcadia, public order in the Duchy of Kerebel had improved compared to before, and many villages and towns were flourishing as merchants began to gather there.

Given these results, the Duke of Kerebel could not stop his wife’s actions—nor did he intend to.

Though she is his beloved wife, the Duke of Kerebel—no, Liberté Kerebel—holds the position of Duke of Kerebel.

Since the fate of thousands, tens of thousands, or even more people hinges on a single decision by Liberté, he must prioritize his role as a public figure over that of a private individual.

If he were the sort of noble with misplaced pride, it wouldn’t be strange for him to say, “So what?” and act however he pleased.

But, unfortunately… and fortunately, Liberté possessed a sense of ethics rare among nobles, and his wife, Arcadia, was no different.

It is precisely because of Liberté’s character that the Noble Faction, led by Duke Kerebel, grew into a power base large enough to be counted among the three major factions of the Kingdom of Mireana.

If Liberté had been the sort of person with a sense of pride that was out of place for a noble, there is no doubt that the House of Kerebel, even if it had remained a ducal house, would never have attained the influence it holds today.

“That’s true. …In that sense, perhaps Elena is also only just passing grade as a noble.”

“Hehe, I suppose so.”

Elena, who showed no interest in high society and sought only to strengthen herself, was, from the perspective of the nobility, a figure who could be called a heretic.

If Elena had older siblings above her, her attitude would have been praised rather than frowned upon.

But right now, Elena is the only one in the Duke of Kerebel’s family with legitimate bloodlines.

Under normal circumstances, it would not have been at all unusual for her to be assisting Liberte in his duties as the next Duke of Kerebel, or accompanying Arcadia on tours of the Kerebel Duchy to help identify and implement improvements.

Or rather, it would be strange if she weren’t.

Or perhaps she should have taken a son-in-law.

After all, Elena is already in her twenties, an age at which it would be perfectly natural for a noblewoman to be married.

But… I don’t think Elena is interested in such things right now.

Both her parents understand their daughter’s competence, but when they consider her happiness, they don’t believe she could make it as a noble.

No, perhaps I should say that Elena’s stature is too great to be confined to the life of a noble.

That is precisely why Liberte ordered Elena to inherit the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone.

To be honest, it’s true that I had a feeling the inheritance ceremony would likely fail… but in the end, despite a few minor issues, the ceremony was largely successful.

(A successor, huh. Perhaps I should adopt a distant relative...)

In his mind, Liberte pictured capable-looking members of the family descended from Kerebel Duke.

But just as he was thinking this, Arcadia’s hand suddenly rested on his.

“What’s wrong?”

“No, didn’t Rei say so? That if we want to set Elena free, we should just have another child. …I think that’s better than taking in an adopted child. Don’t you agree?”

Liberte immediately understood what his wife was asking for.

So he stood up from the chair he was sitting in, took his wife by the hand... and headed for the bed.

“Grr!”

“Kyu? Kyu, kyu!”

The cries of Set and Yellow echoed through the stable at night.

Having been served a hearty meal and eaten their fill, Set and Yellow couldn’t quite bring themselves to sleep and were playing around in the stable.

Fortunately, since there were currently only Set and Yellow in this stable, playing like this at night wouldn’t bother any other animals.

...Actually, to be precise, because Set was brought into this stable, all the other animals—including the horse that originally occupied this stable—had been moved to different stables...

That said, while a strictly trained horse might be able to handle it, there’s no way an ordinary horse wouldn’t feel nervous being in the same place as Set.

I suppose the horses would get used to it if they spent some time together… but it was definitely better to keep them in separate stables from the start.

Even a trained horse wouldn’t cower in Set’s presence, but that doesn’t mean it wouldn’t feel any stress.

In that sense, it’s fair to say that those in charge of the horses in the Duchy of Kerebel made the best decision.

“Grr?”

“Kyu! Kyu-kyu-kyu!”

And precisely because there were no other horses—just Set and Yellow—they were able to use the stable freely.

The caretakers had even removed the partitions inside the stable so that Set could move about freely, and now the stable itself felt like Set’s own room.

It was, of course, their first time handling Set—or, more accurately, a griffin—but since Set possessed high intelligence and didn’t resort to mindless rampaging, the move was completed safely and without incident.

That said, it is undeniably true that many people still fear Set’s presence.

“Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

“Squeak! Squeak, squeak!”

Set and Yellow run around the spacious stable.

Naturally, there was no lighting in the stable.

But for Set and Yellow, the faint moonlight filtering in was enough to see their way around the stable, so it wasn’t a problem.

As befits the stables of the Duke of Kerebel, the building was suitably spacious.

It was spacious enough that Set and Yellow could run around without any trouble.

...That said, if Set were to start running around in earnest, the stable would definitely not be large enough, so it was really only spacious enough for him to run around while playing with Yellow.

Perhaps they enjoyed running around in the dim light, for Set and Yellow happily chased each other around the stables.

Sometimes Yellow chased Set, and other times Set chased Yellow.

The two of them were playing like that when... suddenly, Set stopped chasing Yellow and looked outside the stable.

A few seconds later, Yellow also turned his gaze in the direction Set was looking.

That said, there was no hostility or wariness in their gazes.

That’s because the presence outside the stable belonged to people both Set and Yellow knew.

“Grr?”

“Kyu!”

Shall we go? Of course!

Exchanging those brief cries, the two headed straight for the stable door.

Part of the reason they were in such a hurry was that they recognized the presence, but more than that, it was likely due to the scent wafting toward them.

They had eaten a proper dinner, but that was several hours ago.

Neither Set nor Yellow were starving, but they were feeling a little peckish.

Especially since they’d been running around inside the stable and working up a sweat until just now, so it was only natural.

Then, the door opened...

“Whoa!”

“Wait, Lady Elena!?”

Elena let out a cry of surprise as Set and Yellow suddenly charged toward her.

Ara, who was standing next to Elena, tried to shield her, but… before she could, Set and Yellow stopped in their tracks.

“Hehe, were you two that hungry, Set and Yellow? Ara…”

At Elena’s words, Ara put down the plate of food she was holding, though her expression betrayed a hint of displeasure.

The food was leftovers from tonight’s dinner party.

Normally, even if there were leftovers from Georgima’s cooking, the other chefs would have competed to eat them first—for their own training, and above all, to learn her techniques.

The fact that there was even a small amount left for Set and Yellow was because Elena had spoken to Georgima beforehand.

She had argued that since Set was Rei’s partner, he had the right to eat the same food as Rei.

She couldn’t prepare the meat that was the main course because it was too labor-intensive, but the other dishes had been set aside properly.

“There might not be quite enough, but please forgive me,”

Hearing Elena say this, Set and Yellow each let out a meow to tell her not to worry.
