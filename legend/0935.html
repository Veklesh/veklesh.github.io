"...Huh?"

Who had uttered that startled cry?

Even without knowing, it was clear that the arrow that had fallen from the sky had stopped the fatal collision between Rei and the suddenly appearing men.

An arrow... it was truly just an arrow.

An ordinary arrow, nothing special, not even a magic item.

But that arrow had undeniably halted this situation, which had been on the brink of explosion.

"This arrow..."

The first to move after the arrow fell was the man in his thirties among the trio with tattoos.

Seeing the arrow stuck between them and Rei, he murmured softly.

"Huh? You know about it?"

"Yes. ...If he sees the Heavenly Bow appear, we should avoid further combat here. A battlefield with so many hiding spots is his specialty. Of course, I'm not saying we'd lose if we fought now. But instead, this entire area could vanish."

The man in his thirties responded to the elder's words, and the man in his twenties nodded in agreement.

"Indeed. I too favor withdrawing from this location for now. If he has emerged, we should delay the execution of divine punishment upon Crimson for a while longer. Against those two, even with the Stigma unleashed, we cannot be certain of delivering divine punishment."

"Hmm, you're right. Even without releasing his Stigma, he's a man who gets back up immediately after taking your blow. Facing such a man and the Heavenly Bow simultaneously would be troublesome. Then... let us retreat from here."

The moment the old man spoke, an intense light erupted around them, blinding everyone present.

"You think we'll just let you escape like this?!"

Rei, judging the enemy was retreating, instantly closed his eyes the moment the light appeared. He drew a spear from his Misty Ring and hurled it swiftly.

It wasn't a perfect throw with his usual body twist, relying purely on brute strength. Yet, the spear released from Rei's hand vanished straight into the light.

The blinding light that had illuminated the surroundings vanished... and when he opened his eyes, he saw his spear embedded in the wall.

Still, it seemed he hadn't escaped unscathed. Several drops of blood had spilled onto the ground where the spear had likely passed through.

"...Damn it. Let him escape after all. If it had gone well, I could've pinned him to the wall."

He clicked his tongue as he approached the wall where the spear was embedded, reaching for the shaft... only to immediately frown.

Of course. The moment he gripped the shaft, it snapped.

Then again, the spears Rei used for throwing were usually ones with chipped tips or similar flaws.

Precisely because they could break at any moment, they were disposable spears.

Of course, he had plenty of normal spears too, but the one he'd thrown this time was one of those broken-down ones.

Still, it wasn't a one-sided defeat. He managed to make someone over there bleed. Calming himself down as best he could, Rei turned his gaze toward Eland.

However, Eland and his guards all seemed unable to recover from the overwhelming presence and shock they'd received from the three who had just revealed themselves. They stood completely still, as if time itself had frozen.

If even Eland and his guards, who possessed considerable skill, were like this, then some of the assassins who had been fighting those guards here must have been crushed mentally.

Glancing around, Rei's gaze finally settled on an arrow stuck in the ground.

That arrow stirred complex emotions within Rei—a mix of resentment and relief.

It was true that it had dampened the fight just as they were about to counterattack, but it was also true that the attack from the man in his twenties had inflicted some damage.

He had approached and attacked without Rei noticing at all.

Rei understood this man possessed extraordinary skill, but there were at least three others of that caliber. ...Or rather, given that they possessed the skill to approach Rei unnoticed, it wouldn't be surprising if there were more lurking nearby.

Facing opponents of that caliber alone, Rei... and on top of that, fighting while protecting a liability like Eland? Winning would be difficult.

(I don't want to say no... but a tough fight is inevitable. It was a short battle, but they were clearly A-rank adventor-class opponents. Three of them? ...Could we have managed if Set had been here?)

Thinking that with Set, he could have tracked down the three who retreated using his sense of smell, it felt like such a missed opportunity.

"So then. ...Who shot this arrow, after all?"

He muttered, examining the arrow once more.

As someone who'd been targeted by the poison arrow known as Heaven's Tear, Rei didn't have fond memories of arrows.

Then, he noticed something extremely strange.

The arrow stuck in the ground showed no tilt whatsoever.

It was as if it had been dropped straight down from above.

Rei looked up into the sky, but of course, there was no one there.

This meant whoever shot the arrow had aimed straight up, and the arrow, after flying in an arc, had fallen straight down without changing its angle.

Moreover, considering the person was nowhere within Rei's line of sight, it must have been fired from a considerable distance.

"They mentioned something about a Heavenly Bow... Who is it?"

The moment Rei muttered this, Eland shot her a look of utter disbelief.

Just a moment ago, he should have been completely immobilized... but that alone showed how unexpected the words coming from Rei's mouth were.

"You don't know!?"

"Huh? What?"

"I mean, the Heavenly Bow... Lord Sardat!"

"Sardart?"

At the familiar name, Rei closed his eyes as if recalling something... and immediately remembered the name.

"The S-rank adventurer from the Kingdom of Mireana?"

"That's right. Lord Sardart of the Heavenly Bow. They say his arrows pierce targets from any distance... I always thought it was an exaggerated rumor, but judging by this, it seems it wasn't entirely wrong after all."

Hearing Eland's words, Rei wore a subtle expression.

No, he was grateful to the owner of this arrow, but he still felt his battle had been interrupted.

Of course, he wouldn't go so far as to voice that thought aloud.

That's precisely why he deliberately changed the subject.

"From however far away... Can you really assess the surrounding situation that accurately based on your own senses, Eland?"

Rei knew the person before him was a second-year S-Class.

But the fact remained that, ultimately, that was the extent of it.

Judging by the abilities of the third and fourth-year S-Class students, he shouldn't be exceptionally strong.

After all, when Rei first caught up to Eland, he had shown signs of hesitation when facing the four assassins.

"That's..."

He probably understood his own lack of strength. Eland fell silent, looking somewhat dissatisfied.

"Sorry, I didn't mean to get you down like that. Besides, what you're saying isn't wrong at all."

Rei continued, his gaze shifting from Eland to the arrow embedded in the ground.

"Believe it or not, my senses are sharp. And facing those three earlier, I was pretty serious. Yet, I couldn't figure out where the arrows came from at all. Before I knew it, they were just falling from the sky like this. ...I see. So that's why it's called Heaven's Bow?"

An arrow falling from the sky. And one fired from such a distance... beyond even Rei's senses could grasp. That would be a tremendous threat.

Even Rei, when facing an opponent like the Heavenly Bow Saldat, could handle an arrow falling straight down from above.

But if an arrow were fired with pinpoint accuracy amidst the chaos of battle, he'd have to split his focus to deal with it, making it far more difficult.

"Of course, it's Lord Saldat, a rank S. Not only can he rain arrows from above, but he's also renowned for his advanced archery techniques. The story of him shooting through a thief attempting to infiltrate the Officer Academy through Gracielle's main gate is famous."

"...What kind of joke is this?"

Rei understood the distance from the main gate to the academy, having walked it himself.

Moreover, the path isn't even a straight line, with several turns along the way.

How could anyone pull off what Eland described in such terrain?

He dismissed it as an exaggerated tale for the moment, but Eland clearly held Sardat in high regard and wouldn't make such a claim lightly.

"More importantly, what are we going to do now..."

What should we do? Rei was about to ask, but sensing someone approaching, he readied his Death Scythe.

Could it be the trio from earlier returning? That's what he thought... but soon, emerging from the alleyway entrance was Glink.

Seeing his face, Rei let out a small sigh of relief and lowered the Death Scythe.

"Rei? What's wrong? Are you sure it's okay that these guys are assassins?"

"Ah... Well, the bigger fish got away."

At Rei's words, Glinck's face twisted into utter shock.

He understood more than well how powerful Rei was, having spent considerable time with him in class lately.

Of course, the students were no match for Glinck. Even so, he couldn't imagine himself fighting and defeating Rei... or even holding his own against him.

Hearing that Rei, possessing such strength, had let someone escape, the poisoned arrow once fired at Rei during class flashed through Glink's mind.

Thinking about it now, he could see a single arrow stuck in the ground.

Some of Eland's guards also used bows, but the arrows they shot lay on the ground, not stuck in it.

"Another arrow?"

"No, not that. This arrow belongs to the one who supported us. ...You know what a Heavenly Bow is?"

"Huh!?"

Shock struck me once more.

Of course. There wasn't a single person living in the Kingdom of Mireana who didn't know the name of their country's Rank S adventurer.

...Rei didn't know, but fortunately or unfortunately, Glink hadn't noticed that.

"Lord Sardat provided support? ...But, well, that's rather unusual. No, I mean, I don't doubt your story at all, Rei-san."

"Unusual? What about it?"

"So you think so too, Instructor Glink?"

For some reason, leaving Rei alone, Glink and Eland exchanged silent glances, their expressions suggesting they understood the situation.

It was a silent conversation born of sharing some unspoken premise.

Rei was puzzled by this, but when Glink met his gaze, he opened his mouth as if to deflect the question.

"So, these individuals... are they the same as before?"

"Yeah. ...Though I wonder how many of them are still alive. That part's a bit questionable."

"If we're trying to get information out of them, wouldn't it be better to avoid killing them if possible?"

Hearing Glink's words, Rei sighed, looked up at the sky, and showed his displeasure through his attitude.

"I didn't kill these guys. They were the ones fighting us before the Heavenly Bow appeared."

"...So the ones fighting Rei are the big shots you mentioned earlier?"

"Yeah. Even though it was a surprise attack, I never thought I'd take a direct hit like that."

Rei said, rubbing his side inside the Dragon Robe.

The pain lingered, but he'd instinctively leaped away the instant he felt the attack, likely avoiding a broken rib.

That said, it did mean he'd managed to inflict effective damage on Rei, who'd tried to jump to absorb the blow.

(Still... divine punishment... divine punishment, huh. Surely not...)

The incident that had occurred in the labyrinth city of Exil flashed briefly through Rei's mind, but before he could dwell on that possibility, Glink spoke up.

"It seems we've gotten ourselves into quite a mess. ...What shall we do about Eland for now? I intend to hand these individuals over to the guards."

"...Then we'll accompany you. Since we've dragged you into our affairs, an explanation is probably necessary."

His tone was quite different from how he spoke to Rei, but considering it was a distinction between how he addressed adventurers versus instructors, it made sense.

"Understood. Then, come with me. ...I'm sorry, Rei, but could you urgently relay this matter to the Headmaster? I believe he's in his office today. Having you here would complicate the follow-up procedures."

"Got it. I'll leave it to you. Also, about the matter of whoever targeted me..."

"Yes, I'll gather as much information as possible."

And so, Rei temporarily left the scene and headed for the Officer Academy.

"Well, well, you must be Rei? Rumor had it you were a man, but it seems that was a lie."

As Rei arrived at the principal's office, an elf man approached him, flashing a toothy grin as he said this.
