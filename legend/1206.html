Rei found his gaze fixed on a figure of striking beauty... Elena, known by the epithet Princess General.

"Kyu!"

From within Rei's arms, Yellow let out a short cry, flapping its small wings as it flew toward Elena.

Rei watched silently, but when he saw Elena take a step toward him, he too took a step toward her.

Many around them were startled, unaware of the situation, but neither Rei nor Elena seemed to notice.

Among them were a few who had participated in the previous war against the Bestir Empire, or who had seen Elena's face from afar for some other reason. Some of them, realizing it was Elena standing there, rubbed their eyes in disbelief.

Of course, Elena had been to Gilm before.

Some must have seen Elena's face then.

Regardless, despite all those eyes on them, Elena and Rei drew closer to each other... until Rei finally spoke.

"Elena, why are you here..."

At Rei's words, Elena smiled.

"After hearing about Vihera, I couldn't sit still. There was just so much work I needed to finish, so I couldn't come sooner."

Naturally, Elena knew about Vihera's current situation.

Rei and Elena could communicate using their Paired Orbs. While they didn't talk every single day, they spoke every few days nonetheless. So, it was only natural that she would inform him of Vihera's situation.

And after hearing about Vihera, Elena had been gathering information, using her influence to the fullest—both through the duke's household and through her own reputation as the Princess General—to find a way to restore Vihera's consciousness.

But the cause of Vihera's unconsciousness was Ambris.

An entity that had appeared only once three hundred years ago... and not in the Kingdom of Mireana, but in the Bestir Empire.

They had obtained almost no detailed information, and the fact they had managed to gather any at all likely demonstrated just how famous Elena's name was.

She hurriedly finished the minimum tasks she had to complete, waited until she had some breathing room, and then came to Gilm.

Since Elena had already stayed in Gilm for a considerable period this spring, those around her weren't pleased about her going back. But Elena had insisted on her own way.

"Last time..."

"In the carriage, right?"

Rei cut Rei off before he could finish, answering immediately.

When they'd spoken through the Paired Orb a few days ago, they'd already been en route to Gilm by carriage.

"You didn't need to hide it, did you?"

"Ara told me it's better to surprise people with things like this."

"...Speaking of which, where's Ara?"

Rei felt uneasy at not seeing the figure of Elena's loyal subject and close friend.

Ara's feelings for Elena were incredibly strong... no, more than strong—they sometimes bordered on reckless.

Considering the moment Rei first met Elena, when their gazes locked, it was only natural.

"Ara is currently preparing lodging. Naturally, it's the Dusk Wheat inn."

"How did they even have a room available?"

Currently, due to the matter with Ambris, many merchants who couldn't come during that time have gathered.

Among these merchants were naturally many who could be called great merchants, and the Dusk Wheat inn was one of the inns where such individuals stayed.

Given that the Dusk Wheat inn was likely packed with such merchants and adventurers, Rei was surprised to hear Elena had secured a room.

He had much he wished to discuss with Elena, but perhaps because their reunion had been so sudden, he couldn't bring himself to voice his true feelings.

"Hmm. Fortunately, many of the merchants here know me."

"...I suppose so."

Merchants for whom information is life... and these were merchants with enough financial means to stay at the Dusk Wheat inn.

Naturally, some recognized Elena's face. For those merchants, simply giving up their room meant forging a connection with Elena—nothing short of pure luck.

Seeing the jealous glances other merchants shot at the one who successfully negotiated with Elena made it clear just how significant it was to be acquainted with her.

"Anyway, there are people watching here. Why don't we go back to the inn?"

"Yeah, you're right. I've got a lot I want to talk about too."

As he murmured, he felt something heavy inside him lift just a little because he'd met Elena.

Of course, meeting Elena hadn't made it disappear completely, but even so, talking with her felt like the best possible distraction for him right now.

Whether she understood Rei's feelings or not...

Either way, Elena began walking with Rei toward the Dusk Wheat inn.

Following behind them, Set, having finished playing with the children, trailed along with Yellow perched on his head.

Two people and two creatures... That alone wasn't particularly unusual in Gilm, but seeing Rei and the others actually walking like this inevitably drew attention.

They walked together without speaking.

They walked on in silence, yet strangely, there was no awkwardness between Rei and Elena.

No, quite the opposite—they both enjoyed that silence... Rei enjoyed the silence with Elena present, and Elena enjoyed the silence with Rei present.

It wasn't ordinary silence, but a fulfilling silence born solely from their presence together.

Set and Yellow followed behind Rei and Elena, but no one dared speak to them, drawn in by the atmosphere the pair radiated.

Even those who would normally have definitely spoken to Set.

And so, maintaining their silence, they arrived at the Dusk Wheat inn.

"Grrr..."

Set let out a slightly lonely growl, but without waiting for Rei to say anything, he headed straight for the stable.

Whether Yellow felt sorry for him or simply wanted to play with Set more after so long, Rei couldn't tell, but Yellow followed them to the stable, still perched on Set's head.

Now truly alone together, Rei and Elena entered the Dusk Wheat inn.

"...Ah, Lady Elena! I thought you'd disappeared! You were outside!"

Seconds after they were alone, someone inside the inn spotted Elena and Rei and called out to them.

Of course, Rei knew exactly who that person was.

"Long time no see, Ara."

Before Elena could say anything, Rei spoke.

Seeing Rei like that, Ara found herself forced to swallow the warning she'd intended for Elena.

Reluctantly, she opened her mouth.

"Long time no see, Rei."

"Seems I've caused you quite a bit of trouble this time around... but honestly, I'm grateful. Elena must have gone to some trouble to meet me too, so please cut her some slack."

With Rei saying that much, Ara had no choice but to back down.

While Ara owed Rei a great deal, the biggest factor was seeing Elena smiling.

Ever since learning about Vihera, Elena had become lost in thought more often.

Of course, she would smile when necessary, but it was nothing more than a forced smile.

But now, Elena wore a genuine smile.

The reason Ray was momentarily speechless when Elena returned to the Dusk Wheat inn was because he saw her wearing a natural smile for the first time in a long while.

Granted, given the Vihera incident, it wasn't a full-blown smile.

Still, seeing Elena now, Ara felt a sense of relief.

(Elena... I'm so glad...)

Even though she thought that inwardly, she still disapproved of Elena going out into town alone.

Of course, it wasn't just because she'd left all the work to her... it was purely out of concern for Elena's safety.

Elena was a beauty who captivated everyone at first glance.

With someone like Elena walking alone in town, the chances of her getting caught up in trouble were extremely high.

Given Elena's strength, if someone tried something, there'd be nothing anyone could do about it.

I know that, but there's always the possibility of something going wrong.

Especially in this Gilm, where skilled adventurers gather, the worst-case scenario is possible.

"So, Ara. Is the room situation settled now?"

"Yes. I've properly thanked the merchant who gave us this room."

"I see."

Nodding at his words, Elena turned her gaze to Rei.

"I'd like to check on Vihera. Is that alright?"

"Sure. I'll take you there right away."

Vihera, still unconscious, lay asleep in the bed at the Dusk Wheat inn lodging.

Some had suggested taking her to the hospital, but given the circumstances of her unconsciousness, leaving her there would only occupy a bed without any prospect of treatment.

Considering the situation where many had been injured in the battle against the monster horde and there simply weren't enough beds to go around, it was an understandable decision.

Moreover, though the doctor hadn't said it outright, leaving a beauty like Vihera unconscious in a bed was likely to attract unscrupulous individuals.

Under normal circumstances, if someone tried to take advantage of an unconscious Vihera, it could have been handled to some extent.

But with the mass of wounded pouring in at that time, there simply wasn't enough attention to spare... Ultimately, the decision was made to let her sleep in the bed of the room reserved for Vihera at Dusk Wheat inn.

That said, the Dusk Wheat inn was known as a high-class inn, and naturally, its security awareness was high.

Even if someone tried to bother Vihera, the security was tighter than at a hospital.

Furthermore, the fact that she wasn't losing weight despite not eating or drinking anything, and wasn't sweating at all, was undoubtedly a factor in her being brought to the Dusk Wheat inn.

Currently, a woman has been hired to look after Vihera should anything happen, and she is stationed in Vihera's room.

The reason a woman was hired was, of course, to prevent any confusion.

Despite the job only requiring her to be present in the room and immediately notify us if anything happened to Vihera, the high pay meant competition was fierce when we advertised.

The woman who passed the interview conducted by Lana, the proprietress of the Dusk Wheat inn, is currently stationed inside the room as Vihera's attendant.

Rei and Elena now stand before Vihera's room.

At first, Ara had wanted to come too, but knowing the relationships between Rei, Elena, Vihera... and Marina, who wasn't present...

She decided forcing her way in now would only be a nuisance, so she stayed behind to organize the room's belongings.

"All right?"

"Mm."

Elena nodded at Rei's words. After confirming her agreement, he knocked on the door.

The sound echoed around them.

After a moment, a voice came from behind the door.

"Who is it?"

"It's me, Rei."

As Rei identified himself, the door opened.

Peering through the crack in the door to confirm, a middle-aged woman in her forties appeared.

She was of average height and build... though perhaps a bit wider than average, but her expression bore a gentle smile.

"Rei, you're quite early today."

"Ah. Someone came to visit Vihera."

He shifted his body to the side, revealing Elena standing behind him to the middle-aged woman.

The woman's eyes widened at the sudden appearance of Elena's beauty before her... but she quickly regained her composure and spoke.

"My, what a stunning beauty! To think there's another such beauty besides Vihera... Vihera will be furious when she wakes up, you know?"

"Heh heh, no need to worry. Like Vihera, I too have pledged my future to Rei."

"...Oh my. Rei is quite..."

The woman wore an amused smile, but Rei quietly averted her gaze.

He knew he'd be teased if he stayed like this.

He understood all too well from caring for Vihera these past few days that the woman before him was a meddler with a strong interest in others' love lives.

"Anyway, I'm sorry, but could you step away for a moment?"

"Oh, certainly. Just wait here a moment."

She went into the room, picked up the knitting she'd been working on until Rei arrived, and left the room.

"Then, call me when you're done. I'll be in the dining hall downstairs."

"Sorry about this. ...Here, have some tea."

Rei handed her a few copper coins, and the woman smiled as she left.

Watching her go, Rei and Elena entered the room.

The two beds in the room were because this room was rented by Vihera and Byune.

Though Byune wasn't in Gilm right now.

(When Byune returns, I'll have to tell her about Vihera, huh?)

Thinking such things, Rei looked toward the bed.

There, lying as if asleep, was Vihera.
