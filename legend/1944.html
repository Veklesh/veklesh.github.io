“Rei? What’s going on? Why did you come back so suddenly?”

It was a guard who asked Rei that in front of the guardhouse.

However, it wasn’t the guard with the stern expression Rei had seen earlier, but another guard with a much gentler expression.

The contrast with the previous guard was so stark that Rei almost laughed, but he managed to hold it in and spoke.

"Ah. I found a guy who has some leads. I brought him here. ...He's a survivor from the Red Cloth group who tried to break through the earthen wall."

At the mention of “Red Cloth,” the guard’s gentle expression vanished, and he fixed a sharp gaze on Red Cloth.

Faced with that gaze, the man instinctively took a step back.

(The guard has had quite a hard time with the Red Cloth. I guess this is inevitable.)

Rei understood the guard’s reaction, but that didn’t mean he could just leave the guard like this and let the man who held the clue cower in fear.

“Calm down. After all, we didn’t accomplish anything yesterday. Besides, right now, we need any clue we can get, no matter how small.”

“...I understand.”

Unable to argue with Rei’s words, the guard replied.

That said, just because he couldn’t argue didn’t mean his feelings toward the red cloth had vanished.

As proof of that, the gaze he directed at the man remained stern even now.

While they were talking, Set moved to the spot where he had been lying down earlier and lay down again.

“Let’s go inside for now. We need to get the details out of him, and it’s not entirely impossible that the mastermind behind this is sending people after him…”

At those words, the guard reluctantly granted permission to enter.

...Though it looked more like tacit approval, if anything.

Once Rei entered the guardhouse, the guards inside were naturally surprised.

It was only natural, since Rei had just left a short while ago.

And he wasn’t alone—he was accompanied by a man.

“Who’s that? Since Rei brought him here, does that mean he’s involved in this incident?”

One of the guards asked, looking at the man.

Rei gave the same explanation he’d given to the guard standing in front of the guardhouse, but… perhaps because the mention of the red cloth put many on edge, the guards’ eyes grew sharp as they looked at the man.

...Faced with those stares, the man was beginning to seriously regret having been talked into coming here by Rei.

(Ugh, is this really going to be okay? Maybe... I shouldn’t have come here? But Rei said pursuers were coming after us.)

As someone who wore the red sash, the man had some confidence in his skills.

But that was only compared to ordinary people; he didn’t think he stood a chance against an adventurer, and the same went for a guard who’d undergone combat training.

For someone like him to be protected in a place where guards were staring at him so intently was, no matter how he looked at it, mentally taxing.

That said, if you asked Rei if he’d recommend anywhere else, the answer would probably be no.

“So, all we have to do is get the information out of him.”

“Exactly. …But we have to do it without getting rough. For now, we’ve identified their base—not the disposable one Grange knew about, but their real base… What do you suggest?”

“What should we do?” Those words implied a choice: should they storm the base immediately, gather a little more intelligence first, post a lookout to see how the enemy reacts—as they did with Grange—or choose some other option?

That said, as long as there’s a possibility of a traitor among the guards, wasting time could easily put us on the defensive again.

(Oh, come to think of it, if there’s a traitor among the guards, isn’t it dangerous to leave this guy at the guardhouse? …Well, from the looks of it, the guards seem to be keeping an eye on each other, so it’ll probably be fine.)

Rei felt he’d made a slight misstep, but since he’d already come this far, there was nothing he could do about it now.

To begin with, the traitor among the guards was likely not one of the guards stationed here, but someone in a higher position. …That is, assuming there actually is a traitor.

Deciding it would likely be fine for now, Rei turned his gaze toward the guards.

At least, among the ones Rei could see, none of them seemed like a traitor.

…That said, since Rei didn’t exactly have a keen eye for judging people, he couldn’t guarantee that the guards here were truly trustworthy.

“It’s not like I have a choice. …Unfortunately, the guys guarding the base were ambushed, so there aren’t that many who can move right away. Maybe just a few people at most.”

“A few, huh? If we’re going now, Set and I plan to go too. In that case, wouldn’t a few people be enough?”

What Rei said was undoubtedly true.

In fact, with Rei and Set alone, that would be more than enough in terms of combat strength.

So bringing the guards along was likely just to have someone to blame in case of an emergency—someone to explain things to if necessary.

The guards probably understood that, but they nodded immediately.

It wasn’t that they didn’t feel like they were being forced into it, but rather because they believed that kind of work was simply what guards were meant to do.

Also, if they were going right away, having the formidable combat power of Rei and Set on their side was a major advantage.

Since they didn’t know exactly what kind of forces the other side had prepared, it was only natural to assemble as many forces as possible.

The guards present exchanged glances, and eventually one of them nodded.

“Understood. In that case, we’ll get ready right away. Even if we go immediately, this number of men won’t be enough.”

There were five guards in this room.

Given that, there’s no doubt that this force is insufficient for raiding the enemy’s base.

...To be precise, in terms of pure combat strength, having Rei and Set with us shouldn’t be a problem, but we need forces to secure the perimeter and ensure the people inside the base don’t escape.

No matter how strong Rei and Set are, they’re still just one person and one beast.

If the enemies were to scatter in all directions, there’s no way they could catch every single one of them.

If the goal were to kill everyone, Rei and Set could probably handle it without a problem, but our objective this time is strictly to gather intelligence.

Finding a clue leading to the Kobold mastermind was the top priority.

If this were truly one of their bases, there might be documents or other potential clues there, but the information obtained by interrogating the captives was undoubtedly invaluable.

That’s precisely why it’s only natural for the guards to want to assemble as many people as possible.

(If there’s someone among the guards leaking information, there’s a chance they might act suspiciously and be exposed.)

In that sense, raiding the base is significant because it might allow us to identify the person who leaked information in this incident.

“Well, with that settled…”

Watching the guards in the room rush off to gather reinforcements, Rei addressed the man in red.

“Where exactly is this outpost you know about?”

Rei glared at the man in the red cloth with a look that said he wouldn’t tolerate any lies or evasions, but the man in the red cloth, knowing his own life was in danger, wasn’t about to make the foolish mistake of lying here.

……In reality, the high likelihood that the man would be targeted was merely Rei’s prediction, but since an adventurer with a reputation like Rei’s had said so, he had come to believe it completely.

That said, from Rei’s perspective, it wasn’t an exaggeration at all; he genuinely believed it wouldn’t be strange at all if an assassin were sent after him.

“It’s a house not too far from the Noble District.”

“Near the Noble Quarter? …Even if it’s not in the Noble Quarter itself, I’m amazed they’d have a base in a place like that.”

Security in the Noble District is, naturally, tight.

This is because the soldiers, knights, and adventurers hired by the nobles themselves patrol the area.

Even if it’s not actually within the Noble Quarter, just being near it increases the level of safety to some extent.

But, naturally, renting or buying a building in such a location would be expensive.

“Well, I suppose that’s exactly why it was so hard for us to find them.”

Several of the guards nodded at the remark muttered by one of them, but then one of them voiced a doubt.

“Even if it’s not a building inside the Noble District, renting a place like that requires not just money but also credibility. When you think about it that way, there’s a possibility the person behind this is a bigger fish than we thought.”

“They’re manipulating the Red Cloth and gathering Kobolds. One thing’s for sure—they’re definitely not ordinary people.”

“Ah, well, that’s true. Since we only have circumstantial evidence to begin with, it’s not necessarily the case that the people who were hiding the Red Cloth are the masterminds behind the Kobold incident.”

If we’re talking purely about circumstantial evidence, it’s almost certain they’re the masterminds.

In fact, the moment they tried to destroy the earthen wall Rei built to keep the Kobolds out... and especially the moment they asked Grange to have the Kobolds kill the men in red once the wall was destroyed—it’s safe to say they’re clearly guilty as sin.

But there’s no concrete evidence.

If, as Rei claims, a physical magic item capable of gathering or controlling Kobolds were to turn up, that would be a different story.

(If it’s a real base and not just a front, is it possible that a magical item is stored there? After all, even though Gilm is large enough to be called a quasi-city, he can’t just set up as many bases as he pleases… well, not exactly, but it should be quite difficult.)

If that’s the case, there’s a possibility that the magical item is at the base located at the place he pried out of the man in the red cloth.

That said, Rei felt strongly that this was probably not the case.

“Anyway, once the guards are ready, we should head out as soon as possible. The less information that gets out to them, the better.”

At Rei’s words, a few of the guards wore slightly displeased expressions.

But given the high likelihood that information had indeed leaked, it was an undeniable fact that they couldn’t immediately rule it out.

“I’ve already had them get ready, so there’s no need to keep Rei waiting that long. …More importantly, as Rei said, we should just lock that man up in the dungeon, right?”

Rei nodded in response to the guard who asked this while looking at the man in the red cloth.

“Yeah, do that. Though he’s not exactly a criminal… well, I guess he is, technically.”

Given all the trouble he’d caused as a Red Cloth, it wouldn’t be wrong to call the man a criminal.

That said, the crimes the Red Cloth had committed—fights, extortion, assault… some of which were a bit too serious to be called mere misdemeanors—still didn’t include murder.

Of course, even minor offenses like those would undoubtedly become more serious if committed repeatedly.

“In any case, it’s an indisputable fact that you brought us information. With that in mind, make sure he doesn’t get cold and give him decent meals—something like that.”

“...Understood.”

Although he looked slightly dissatisfied, the guard still nodded in response to Rei’s words.

It was an undeniable fact that the man’s information was highly likely to play a major role in the Kobold incident.

Given that, the guard couldn’t simply ignore the man’s contribution.

“So, starting today, you’re officially going to jail.”

“……That’s not exactly something to celebrate.”

The man in the red cloth replied to Rei with a sour expression, but perhaps realizing that relying on the guard was undoubtedly his best option for saving his own life, he didn’t refuse the offer despite his complaints.

Being in a cell versus dying. If asked which he would choose, as a man, the former was the only option.

“Come on, let’s go. Don’t worry—like Rei said, we’ll treat you better than the other criminals.”

Urged by the guard, the man simply walks out of the room.

“Hmph.”

Once the man is out of sight, one of the guards snorts in displeasure.

As a guard, he probably wasn’t happy about having to shelter the man in this situation.

Still, since there was a possibility he might still have other information, they couldn’t voice their dissatisfaction directly.
