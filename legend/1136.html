What Set was looking at was clearly Gosh's main gate, which meant that Rei (the man) and the others were heading outside the city.

"Guru?"

Set tilted his head, wondering what was wrong, but Rei stroked him while turning his gaze to his companion.

"What do you think? Do you really believe the assassin's hideout is outside the city?"

Of the two asked, Zarust tilted his head, while Narsus shook his.

Narsus was the first to speak.

"If they're using the area outside Gosh as their hideout, they'd be vulnerable to monster attacks. That's certain. I can't imagine them setting up a hideout in such a place. Even if it were only temporary."

That explanation was perfectly understandable to anyone thinking logically.

No, in fact, everyone would agree it was undoubtedly the correct opinion.

"But we're talking about Set, right? A rank A monster, a griffin. Do you really think a monster like that would go outside the city for no reason?"

"...Well, that Rank A monster, the Griffin, started buying snacks at a food stall for some reason while searching for the enemy's hideout."

"Guh, w-well..."

When put that way, Zarust couldn't argue back.

Instead, it was Rei who spoke up.

"If he was confused, Set would have shown it from the start. But the fact he's trying to leave town like this means we can safely assume the assassin's hideout is definitely outside the city."

"Grr."

Set let out a growl in agreement with Rei's words.

Even after hearing that growl... or perhaps precisely because of it, Narsas shot Set a suspicious look.

The behavior of these three people and one beast was clearly suspicious.

It was already dark around them, and the main gate was closed.

Naturally, there was no one near the main gate...

"You three over there, what are you doing?"

It was only natural that the suspicious guard called out to them.

But Rei and the others faced the guard who had called out to them without showing any particular sign of panic.

"We're adventurers, Narsus. I believe the lord himself contacted you to provide us with assistance?"

Narsus stepped forward to speak with the guard.

As the leader of Daldora's escort, he was reasonably well-known, and the fact that Daldora had written a letter to Ryubrand was likely why Narsus had stepped forward.

And, as expected, the guard had received orders directly from Ryubrand.

"My apologies. I have been instructed to assist Lady Daldora and Lord Augusto. Is there anything I can do for you?"

Though inwardly displeased, a mere guard couldn't defy orders from the lord.

Narsus understood the soldier's feelings, but if the assassins' hideout truly lay outside the city, it was undeniable that something had to be done about it.

He considered heading to Augusto's residence first to report that the scent Set had picked up was leading outside the city, but time was precious now.

Among them, the one who could move the fastest was undoubtedly Set. Next would be Rei, followed by himself and Zarust, Narsus estimated.

But he couldn't send Set, who could detect the scent, to Augusto's mansion, nor could he leave behind Rei, who could communicate with Set.

Narsus himself was pushing himself to the limit just to be here; there was no turning back now. Zarust likely felt the same.

Both Narsus and Zarust carried the regret of failing to protect their charges from the attackers and wounding them.

Given that, it was absolutely impossible for them to turn back now.

(Then...)

The only guard left was the one within sight.

Since this was the main gate, there couldn't possibly be only one guard.

There must be several others nearby, and if so, asking one to relay a message would have been no problem.

"I'm sorry, but I have a few requests. Would you hear me out?"

With orders coming directly from the lord, the guard had no grounds to refuse.

"Understood. I'll accept them if they're within my power."

"That's a relief. First and foremost, I need you to get us out of Gosh."

"Are you out of your mind!?"

The guard shouted reflexively at Narsus's words.

Several guards peered out from the guardhouse near the main gate, their faces visible in the light of the bonfire. Narsas nodded reassuringly.

"It's fine. As you know, I'm confident in my skills, and Rei is a renowned adventurer who commands a griffin as a familiar. Zarust is also a well-known adventurer in this city of Gosh."

"But still! Going out into the city at night is... well, I won't say it's suicide, but it's dangerous!"

The guard's words stemmed not from genuine concern for Narsus and the others... but from fear that if they let Narsus go out on their own judgment and he ended up dying, they might face Daldora's wrath.

Knowing this, Narsus smiled confidently and spoke.

"There's no problem. Daldora understands that whatever happens this time, it's an incident unrelated to the guards."

Well, he probably never imagined the hideout they'd attack was outside the city. As Narsus conveyed this, hiding his true thoughts, the guards' attitude softened slightly.

However, the guard's gaze wasn't fixed on Narsus, but on Set.

With a griffin present, they likely assumed any trouble could be handled.

Even up close, the guards could sense the overwhelming presence emanating from Set.

They couldn't accurately gauge the extent of his power, but the mere presence of a griffin undeniably commanded an overwhelming sense of something formidable.

"...Understood."

"And now, the second thing I need you to do."

The moment Narsus uttered the word "second," the guard's face twitched slightly.

It was only natural. The first request was to open the main gate, which should have been closed already, allowing them to leave the city.

After receiving such a request, being told there was a second one... it was only natural to be wary.

But Narsus, as if reading the guard's mind, smiled and spoke.

"Don't worry. This one isn't asking for anything unreasonable. Do you know where Augusto's residence is?"

"Huh? Well, yeah..."

The guard, aware of the conflict between Augusto and Daldora, wondered if he was being asked to assist with something at Augusto's residence.

But Zarust, who was guarding Augusto, was present here, and so was Rei, who was said to be cooperating with Augusto.

In such a situation, would they really do something at Augusto's mansion? That question arose in the guard's mind.

Following the guard's gaze and understanding what he was thinking, Narsas smiled and continued speaking.

"Daldora is at Augusto's residence. I want you to tell those two that we headed outside the city. That our destination lies beyond the city walls."

"...Huh?"

The guard looked utterly baffled, but Narsas had no intention of saying more.

It wasn't solely about keeping secrets; it was also purely for the guard's own safety.

He didn't expect the guard to be targeted, but there was always the possibility.

"Well, I suppose that's fine."

"I see. Then I'll leave it to you."

After that brief exchange, Narsus turned his gaze toward Rei, Zarust, and Set before heading for the main gate.

The guard hurried to the guardhouse to open the main gate and explain the situation to his comrades.

Opening the main gate at this hour seemed nothing short of suicide to the guards.

But orders had come directly from the lord to comply with the request to escort Daldora, so they couldn't refuse.

One guard headed for Augusto's residence, while the remaining guards opened the main gate.

That said, opening the main gate didn't mean opening it fully.

Considering the possibility of monsters attacking, opening it wide was simply out of the question.

Therefore, opening the main gate meant opening it only minimally... just wide enough for Set, the broadest member of the group, to pass through.

"Um, when do you think he'll be back?"

The question came from one of the guards, who really didn't want to open the main gate again at night.

He hoped it could be postponed until tomorrow morning at the earliest.

That was the thought running through his mind, but Narサス cruelly denied that hope.

"I don't know. It might be tomorrow morning, or they might return in a matter of hours. Unfortunately, this time, it's not something we can decide here."

He hadn't expected the hideout to be outside the city, so Narsus couldn't say when they'd return either.

If the hideout were near the city, he might be able to return quickly... but then again, it was hard to imagine assassins having a hideout right next to the city.

(If they had a hideout right in town, I could say this would be over in no time.)

This situation was clearly beyond expectations. Honestly, Narsus even wondered if Set might be mistaken.

But that thought was quickly dismissed.

He couldn't doubt how much ability Rei and Set possessed.

...Though, to an outsider, Rei often seemed a bit scatterbrained. That was precisely why those unfamiliar with him assumed he was a top-tier adventurer.

"I see. ...Understood. Please be careful."

Though he said that, the guard's expression clearly conveyed a wish for them to return after the next guard had taken over.

Narsas understood the guard's thoughts, but aware that they were asking the impossible, he pressed no further.

He gave a brief nod to the guard's words and stepped through the open gate, heading out of the city.

Instantly, a cold wind typical of the desert at night blew in, causing Narsus and Zarust to grimace uncomfortably at the chill.

Meanwhile, as a griffon, Set was completely unaffected by this level of cold, and thanks to the Dragon Robe, Rei didn't feel the chill either.

Noticing this, Narsus and Zarust shot Rei a fleeting, envious glance.

Hearing the sound of Gosh's main gate closing behind them, Rei looked up at the sky.

It had been quite some time since they arrived in the Kingdom of Solrain... and in Gosh, which flourished around the desert oasis.

But... even so, gazing up at the desert sky, the dazzling light of countless stars filled his vision, captivating him.

(It's incredible, isn't it? This view alone was something I couldn't have seen back in Japan. ...Wait, maybe even if you couldn't see it in Japan, you could see it in a desert or something?)

As these thoughts crossed his mind, a sigh tinged with exasperation reached his ears nearby.

Turning my gaze that way, Zarust wore an expression of obvious exasperation, while Narsas looked as if he understood Rei's feelings.

Born and raised in Gosh, Zarust saw this view as ordinary and felt nothing special about it.

Even if it was a breathtaking sight for those who came from elsewhere, Zarust, who had grown up with it since birth, couldn't understand why Rei was so moved by something so ordinary.

In contrast, Narsas, who wasn't originally from Solrain, had been captivated by the sight of countless stars shining in the desert sky when he was recruited by Daldora and brought to Gosh.

...Though, having lived in Gosh for years now, he'd inevitably grown accustomed to the scenery.

Still, having had a similar experience, Narsas understood Rei's feelings.

But the fact remained that they couldn't let Rei linger in that awe forever.

They hadn't come out to Gosh to gaze at the stars in the night sky; they were here to raid the hideout of the assassins targeting Daldora and Augusto.

"Rei, sorry to interrupt your stargazing, but it's time to get moving. Staying still in the desert at night is practically asking monsters to attack us."

"Huh? Oh, right. Sorry."

"It's fine. I was captivated too when I first came to Gosh."

As Rei and Narsus carried on their conversation like this, Zarust sighed deeply, as if to say he couldn't possibly understand them.
