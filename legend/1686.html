[Set has learned the skill "Optical Camouflage Lv. 5"]

The familiar announcement echoed in his mind, and the next moment, Rei’s voice of joy rang out through the night forest.

On the night he defeated the jellyfish monster... just as when he defeated the black sea bream monster, since no one knew his true identity, the monster’s corpse was ultimately stored in the Misty Ring after the magic stone was extracted.

The nearly hundred tentacles, which were quite long, were a major hindrance when it came to dealing with the monster’s corpse, but since they could be stored in the Misty Ring simply by touching them, it wasn’t really a problem.

……Though dealing with a monster’s corpse the usual way would almost certainly have been a huge hassle.

As for Rei, since it was a jellyfish monster, she wondered if it might make for a good ingredient.

In fact, back in Japan, it was common for Chinese-style salads to appear on the dinner table, made by mixing Chinese jellyfish, bean sprouts, and ham sold at the supermarket with vegetables harvested from the garden or wild greens gathered in the mountains.

The problem was that while Rei knew jellyfish were edible, he had absolutely no idea how to actually prepare them.

He tried asking when he went to Galanka to buy seafood, but the locals there were actually surprised to hear that jellyfish were even eaten.

By the way, since we were by the sea, I’d asked the group that was gathering shellfish to also collect some seaweed. Since seaweed is commonly eaten in Garanka, I didn’t have to worry about them giving me inedible varieties.

That said, although I asked them to gather it as an ingredient, Rei doesn’t actually know how to cook seaweed.

The simplest way Rei knows to prepare it is to use it as an ingredient in miso soup or other soups.

Or maybe just boiling it and drizzling soy sauce on top.

But even if that’s all Rei could think of, to someone who actually specializes in cooking, it would undoubtedly be considered an unfamiliar ingredient.

……Still, it suddenly occurred to Rei that—whether it was from TV or a cooking manga back in Japan, or some other source he couldn’t quite recall—only Japanese people and a very small number of foreigners could actually digest and eat seaweed.

But since seaweed had apparently been eaten normally in Galanka for a long time, he decided it would probably be fine for now.

Besides, even if they couldn’t digest it, that didn’t necessarily mean they couldn’t eat it.

“Growl-growl-growl-growl-growl!”

Rei, who had been thinking about the seaweed, snapped back to reality at Set’s delighted meow.

“How about it? I did it! Isn’t that amazing?”

Seeing Set looking at him with those round eyes, full of pride, Rei smiled and ruffled his head.

The way Set narrowed his eyes in pleasure made him look, objectively speaking, nothing like a high-rank monster like a griffin.

If anything, he gave the impression of a kitten that had grown attached to its owner...

A three-meter-long giant kitten—a rather contradictory existence, though.

“Coming to the sea this time was lucky in so many ways. I never expected to get my hands on such a massive amount of magic stones. …Well, there are a lot of different kinds, but I guess you couldn’t exactly call it a massive amount.”

Every single magic stone they’d obtained from the monsters was different from anything Rei and the others had acquired before.

But since they only had one of each, the word “massive” didn’t really apply.

“Growl!”

Whether he was listening to Rei’s words or not, Set was purring happily.

But Rei didn’t seem to be paying any attention to Set.

Lately… for quite some time now, they’d been so busy with various things that they hadn’t had the time to absorb monster magic stones.

No, actually, since most of the enemies they’d been fighting were humans, they hadn’t even been able to obtain any magic stones to begin with.

When he thought about it, he’d now mastered several new skills, could eat as much seafood as he wanted, and was able to spend his time at a leisurely pace.

It would be impossible not to be happy about this.

(Though, of course, we can’t spend our time like this forever.)

Even as Rei and the others enjoy their vacation by the sea, expansion work is underway in Gilm.

In that work, Rei and the others were supposed to play a major role.

They had managed to wrap up most of the urgent work before coming to the beach, but when it comes to expanding a large town like Gilm, problems are bound to arise.

Even problems that could be easily resolved with Rei and the others present would require a significant amount of time and effort without them.

When you think about it that way, they couldn’t just keep enjoying their vacation indefinitely.

And while the expansion of Gilm is one thing, there’s also the matter of supplying provisions to those traveling from Mejogo to Gilm.

Thanks to the cooperation of the Zolge Trading Company, we generally don’t face any dire shortages, but that doesn’t mean we’re exactly well-supplied.

Even for the Zolge Trading Company, their investment in this matter is strictly limited to what is necessary.

In other words, they’re only providing the bare minimum of supplies.

So-called luxury items, such as sweet fruits or freshly prepared delicious meals, were not entirely absent—but they were extremely scarce.

That is precisely why, for those heading to Gilm, the supplies Rei brings are undoubtedly the most welcome things imaginable.

(Well, I guess I can enjoy those things a little later. If things really get desperate, Marina will surely let me know.)

Painting a picture of Marina—who was quite the natural leader even among their own group—in his mind, Rei spoke to Set while petting him.

“Alright, Set. Let’s try out the optical camouflage right away. At Level 4, I could stay invisible for eighty seconds… Now that I’m at Level 5, I wonder how much longer it lasts.”

“Grr!”

“Leave it to me!” Set growled, and Rei smiled as he pulled a watch out of his Misty Ring.

“Alright, when I give the signal, use your optical camouflage, okay? …Now!”

“Grrrrrrrrr!”

Following Rei’s instructions, Set lets out a growl.

At the same moment, the optical camouflage activates, and Set’s figure becomes invisible to the naked eye.

“You’re there, right?”

"Grr!"

Rei called out to Set as if to confirm, but Set immediately responded to his words.

Hearing this, Rei checks his watch with a sigh of relief… and eighty seconds pass.

But, as expected, Set doesn’t reappear.

It might be natural since he’d leveled up to five, but Ray kept timing just how long Set would stay like that.

“...You’ve got to be kidding me.”

One minute, two minutes, three minutes… Even after all that time had passed, Set remained transparent, still standing there.

Around the three-minute mark, I wondered if Set might have moved somewhere, so I called out to him—and, as expected, Set immediately responded with a cry.

In other words, it was an undeniable fact that Set was still there, transparent as ever.

Rei knew that reaching Level 5 would dramatically increase the skill’s effectiveness, but he was still surprised by just how long the optical camouflage lasted.

Then, four minutes passed… and the moment five minutes were up, Set became visible again.

“Growl?”

"Is it over?" Set tilted his head, turning his round eyes toward Rei.

Under that gaze, Rei could do nothing but marvel at the extraordinary performance of the Level 5 optical camouflage… no, at a performance so exceptional it almost seemed like a glitch.

Considering Set’s speed and strength, having such an opponent vanish for five whole minutes and wreak havoc at will could only be described as an unbelievable nightmare for any enemy.

“You did a great job. Yeah, optical camouflage… this is definitely going to be a trump card.”

“Grr!”

When Rei praised him, Set let out a happy purr upon hearing it.

For Set, it was a joy to realize he had grown stronger, and at the same time, being praised by Rei was more than enough to make him happy.

Seeing how happy Set looked, Rei smiled as well.

However, as much as Rei wanted to keep admiring Set, that wasn’t an option.

After all, optical camouflage has uses beyond simply making Set disappear.

Thirty minutes passed, and after doting on Set until the optical camouflage was ready to use again, he moved on to the next test.

“Alright, on to the next one. …Based on the patterns so far, the person touching Set can only stay invisible for half the time Set is using optical camouflage… I wonder how it’ll go this time.”

Now that he had reached Level 5, the amount of time Set could maintain optical camouflage on his own had increased dramatically.

Given that, perhaps the duration of the effect when using optical camouflage together with Set has also doubled... In other words, since Set can maintain it for five minutes on its own, it might not be just two and a half minutes, but even longer.

In fact, perhaps they could remain invisible for the same five minutes as when Set uses optical camouflage alone, or maybe even longer, depending on the situation.

With that in mind, Rei asked Set to use optical camouflage while she was touching him...

“Ah… so it’s the same as when I was at Level 4?”

The time I can stay invisible with Set has increased.

There’s no doubt about that.

But the fact that the time they could remain invisible together was two and a half minutes—150 seconds—half of five minutes, hadn’t changed from before.

“Grr…”

At Rei’s disappointed remark, Set hung his head apologetically.

“Hey, it’s not like it’s Set’s fault. Besides, if you think about it, having 150 seconds to disappear together is a huge advantage in battle.”

Rei said this to Set in an encouraging tone, but in reality, his words weren’t wrong at all.

If Set were to vanish and wreak havoc for five minutes, he would undoubtedly inflict fatal damage on the enemy.

But… if Set and Rei were to vanish together and wreak havoc for a full 150 seconds, it goes without saying just how much chaos that would cause.

In fact, if they were to attack a run-of-the-mill band of thieves while invisible, it would be easy enough to wipe them all out in less than a minute.

When you think about it that way, there’s no doubt that Set’s optical camouflage possesses terrifying power.

I’m not saying this to comfort Set, but simply to explain what I truly think.

Perhaps moved by Rei’s attitude, Set’s expression gradually shifted from one of dejection to one of joy.

“Grr, grrrrrrr!”

While petting Set, who was purring contentedly, Rei turned his gaze beyond the trees... toward the area where they were using a Magic Tent as their base camp.

Today, they had moved a fair distance away from the Magic Tent in order to absorb the magic stones that would allow them to master optical camouflage.

Consequently, Set was probably thinking he wanted to get back there soon to eat.

They had also left the magic item furnace there, and right now, Bistol was cooking a meal using plenty of seafood.

He was making the same incredibly delicious... Decadent Seafood Soup he’d made before.

That said, today’s main dish is udon served with that seafood soup.

A considerable amount of the udon purchased at the Full Belly Inn was stored inside Rei’s Misty Ring.

The udon dish made with that was today’s main course.

Recalling the taste of the “Decadent Seafood Soup” she’d had before, Rei climbs onto Set’s back.

Perhaps Set was eager to eat as well, because even without Rei saying a word, he immediately started running.

After a few steps of a running start, he flapped his wings and soared into the sky.

Although the place where Rei and the others were located was far from the base they used, that was only true if they were traveling on foot.

With Set’s wings, they reached the base in less than ten seconds.

When they touched down, the air was filled with a scent that really whetted their appetites.

“Wow, that smells delicious.”

“Welcome back, Rei-chan. I’m about to start preparing the udon, so please help out with the setup.”

At Bistol’s words, Rei turned her gaze toward the campfire.

There, water was boiling in a large pot.

All that was left was to boil the udon and pour the seafood broth over it, and it would be ready.

"I wonder how long I should boil the udon for."

Saying that, she goes ahead and drops the udon into the pot.

Then he stirred the udon with a wooden spatula.

A few minutes passed, and after tasting a strand of udon, Rei decided it was just the right texture... and soon, they were able to enjoy some exquisite udon.
