After wrapping up their discussion with the worker, Rei and the others left the office and met up with Ara.

It was an undeniable fact that things were about to get busy, but the fortunate thing for the workers and guild staff employed at this outpost was that autumn was now well advanced... and winter would arrive before long.

During winter, people generally ventured out less, and new arrivals to this dungeon would be significantly fewer.

Moreover, many adventurers would head back to Gilm once they had saved enough funds to get through the winter.

In other words, it was the perfect time to gradually scale back guild operations and prepare for the eventual return to Gilm.

Of course, even if Rei and the others conquered the dungeon and destroyed its core, it wouldn't become unusable immediately.

Exactly when that would happen was unknown, but it was a fact that there would be a certain amount of time.

During that time, they could arrange to scale back the guild's operations and, while people weren't gathering for winter, talk to the owners of the various shops that had sprung up around the dungeon.

Of course, for those who built their shops specifically targeting the dungeon's special demand... especially shopkeepers whose establishments had only recently been completed, this news would be nothing short of a bolt from the blue.

But the fact remained that various arrangements needed to be made.

If I didn't tell them now, it was certain that the shops would suffer even greater damage in the end.

(Thinking about it that way, I might end up being resented.)

Rei joined up with Ara and walked toward the Ogre Stew Tavern, the guild's chosen spot for the celebration, pondering these things.

"Grr?"

Seeing Rei like this, Set snorted worriedly, rubbing his face against him.

"Nothing, just a lot going on. About the strategy... the workers... no, the guild asked us not to say anything until they make the announcement."

"But that was probably for the best, right? If we'd gone public, we'd definitely have gotten caught up in the chaos."

"...That's undeniably true."

Clearing a dungeon was a significant event in itself.

Even if it wasn't as massive as the dungeon Rei and the others had cleared before, it would undoubtedly cause a stir.

And it wouldn't just be those wishing Rei and the others well. Jealous adventurers, shopkeepers here... countless others would inevitably harbor negative feelings toward them.

Of course, there would likely be many who genuinely wished them well from the bottom of their hearts.

Wanting nothing to do with such a commotion, Rei and the others decided to readily accept the worker's request.

However, this place was near Gilm, and a significant portion—perhaps even the majority—of those who had been active in this dungeon would likely become adventurers in Gilm.

The same applied to the shops built here; a fair number of people were bound to relocate to Gilm.

If that happened, it was certain that news of Rei and the others conquering the dungeon would inevitably cause a stir in Gilm too.

Still, it was true that things would settle down somewhat after a certain amount of time had passed.

Furthermore, if Rei and the others wished to boast about their achievements and announce them grandly, that would likely happen as well.

That is, if they actually desired it.

Many adventurers are confident in their abilities and enjoy flaunting their accomplishments.

However... whether it was fortunate or not, none of the members who cleared the dungeon this time were inclined to do such a thing.

It also helped significantly that most of the participating members already possessed a certain level of fame.

If one were to point out members who weren't famous, it would be Johanna and her group. Yet even they have recently made a name for themselves in this area by challenging dungeons, earning large sums of money, and acquiring many monster materials.

...Though if you go to the Bestir Empire, they're even more well-known for having served as the guerrilla unit led by Rei during the civil war.

"More importantly..."

Feeling reluctant to continue this troublesome conversation, Rei changed the subject to shift the mood.

"The Stewed Orc Inn, was it? Do you know where that place is?"

"Ah, I've heard about it, so that's no problem."

The first to speak up at Rei's words was Ara.

Ara probably jumped in immediately because she wasn't thinking about becoming famous or anything like that. ...Though, if she considered it in terms of spreading Elena's name, she might have been a little dissatisfied with the current situation.

Honestly, since Ara deeply admired Elena to begin with, having Elena's name become known wasn't something she resented at all—she'd even welcome it.

Still, she hadn't said anything because Elena, who was currently traveling with Rei, seemed so happy.

"The Orc Stew Inn, huh. It's gotten cold all of a sudden, so I hope they have some delicious stew."

For Vihera, who had been attacked by Ambris in summer and found herself suddenly in late autumn, the cold must feel even more intense.

For Vihera, who had skipped over the sense of the seasons, stew seemed to be something that really stimulated her appetite.

"Johanna said it was delicious, so maybe we can expect that? ...I can't imagine her feeding Set something that wasn't tasty."

Everyone who heard Rei's words to Vihera accepted them without question.

That's how strong Johanna's feelings for Set were.

It was an undeniable fact that almost no one else held feelings as strong as Johanna's.

And since Johanna had chosen this particular restaurant as the place to dine with Set, it was practically impossible for the food there to be bad.

...Though some might consider it not to their taste.

"Well then, let's relax and enjoy ourselves. Ara, please show us to our seats."

"Yes, Elena-sama."

At Elena's words, Ara responded with genuine delight, then proceeded to guide Rei and the others inside.

Naturally, Rei and his group stood out to those around them, drawing attention as a matter of course.

But Rei and the others, accustomed to being the center of attention, walked through the streets without seeming particularly bothered.

Thus, with a few of them frowning against the chilly autumn breeze, they made their way along the path... and eventually arrived at their destination.

Rei had expected some kind of trouble, but fortunately, they seemed to have reached the shop without encountering any such issues.

...Though it was abnormal for such trouble to occur in the first place.

But when it did happen, it happened to Rei, Elena, Marina, and Vihera.

"Ah, Set-chan! You're already here. I'm glad you made it sooner than I expected."

Johanna, who had been waiting outside the shop, called out to Set first. She walked straight over to him, petting him as she addressed Rei and the others.

The scene was exactly as Rei and the others had described on their way over, and they all couldn't help but smile.

"Huh? Wait, what? What's going on?"

Naturally, Johanna had no idea why they were laughing at her and looked puzzled.

"Nothing, really. Anyway, about letting Set in... how did it go?"

"Ah, yes. It's not possible when other guests are present, but apparently it's fine if we have the place to ourselves. ...Though, given the season, the fee will be correspondingly higher, I hear."

"Ah... yeah, I guess so."

Johanna seemed reluctant to say it, but Rei understood perfectly.

Winter was approaching, making it tough to do anything outdoors.

In such times, dining out was one of the few pleasures left.

And Oak's Stew House was reasonably famous for its delicious food; opening the shop meant guaranteed daily prosperity.

Given they were renting out the entire place, it was only natural the shop would charge a hefty fee.

"Well, I'll cover it this time. I got most of the Silver Lion's materials anyway, so thinking about it that way, paying for the celebration isn't a problem."

In fact, considering the price I got for the Silver Lion's materials, throwing celebrations like this dozens, hundreds, thousands, or even tens of thousands of times would still leave me with plenty to spare.

"Are you sure? That's great. Actually, I already told them we'd be renting the place out."

"...Well, I guess it's fine."

Rei said, wearing a slightly exasperated expression at Johanna's words.

Truthfully, this whole thing was possible because Johanna had made arrangements beforehand, and because she was on good terms with the shop owner.

"Of course, it's been arranged that Set-chan can come inside too, so let's have the celebration together, okay?"

"GRRRR!"

"Kyuu! Kyukyuu!"

Set chirped happily, and Yellow, perched on his back, chirped to remind her not to forget him.

"Of course, we've got Yellow-chan's portion all ready too, so no need to worry, okay?"

In Johanna's heart, Set was naturally number one.

But that didn't mean she didn't love the others.

For Johanna, who basically loved cute things, Yellow was also someone she found utterly adorable.

She even thought that if she had met Yellow before meeting Set, she might have chosen Yellow as her top priority instead.

"Kyu!"

Yellow chirped happily on Set's back, glad he hadn't been forgotten.

With that, the conversation came to a close, and they finally entered Johanna's recommended Oak Stew Tavern.

The shop itself didn't look particularly different from the surrounding buildings.

Perhaps it was because they were so confident in the flavors served within, or maybe they simply didn't care about appearances.

Only one thing... the size of the door was considerably larger than that of an ordinary shop.

So large, in fact, that even Set could easily pass through.

"Well, I'm going in. ...Don't be surprised, okay?"

"Hm?"

Rei felt Johanna's words were meaningful, but before he could ask anything, the door opened.

Instantly, the rich aroma of thick soup wafted out from inside the shop.

"Gurgh!?"

The first to react to that scent—which could only be described as violent—was, unsurprisingly, Set, with his keen senses.

That said, his reaction was anything but violent. If anything, he seemed almost intoxicated by the aroma wafting from inside.

"Wow, this smell is incredible. ...Why didn't any scent drift outside at all? I mean, it's one thing for us, but it wouldn't be surprising if Set had caught the scent before we even opened the door."

Vihera voiced her surprise at the soup's aroma.

It was nothing short of strange that Set, whose sense of smell was far sharper than a human's, hadn't detected the scent until the door was opened.

"Ah, it seems this building uses a magic item for deodorization. You can probably tell from the smell now, but it's quite a strong aroma... Well, apparently when this shop first opened, there were quite a few complaints from nearby businesses."

"I see. Using magic items for something like this is a bit surprising, but when you think about it, it's not really that strange, is it?"

"It's more like it was made for this purpose... I wouldn't be exaggerating to say that."

"No, that's an exaggeration."

Rei couldn't help but interject at Elena's words.

It's a magic item capable of perfect deodorization, so perfect that even Set couldn't detect the smell.

It's definitely not the kind of thing that should be used in a place like this.

(Though it's definitely better used in a shop like this than ending up in some strange place...)

Given how powerful this magic item is, if it were put where it should be, it would fetch a hefty price, and there would be no shortage of people wanting it.

It was certain enough that it would bring in enough money to live comfortably without having to run a cafeteria and work yourself to the bone.

That's what Rei said, but...

"Hmph, I cook because I want to. Now, get inside and close the door already. We'll get complaints from the neighboring shops again."

A voice called out from inside the shop.

A voice that sounded every bit the stubborn old man it was calling itself.

Guided by that voice, Rei and the others entered the shop.

Had they hesitated here, they would undoubtedly have been refused entry.

They instinctively knew it.

After smelling such an aroma, the choice to not eat this shop's food simply wasn't an option for Rei and the others.
