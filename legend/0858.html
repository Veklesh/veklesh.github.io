“Oh, it's Set-chan! Set-chan! You really did come back!”

“Well, it really is Set. I heard rumors yesterday that he'd returned... I wonder if this will liven up Gilum again?”

“No, no, Gilum wasn't gloomy just because Set wasn't here, was it?”

As Rei and Set walked through town, people who spotted them called out such remarks.

Among them, the stall owners seemed especially delighted.

It was already close to noon, and the stalls were bustling with preparations.

Despite this, each stall owner, steaming away in the cold while cooking, called out to Set.

“Hey, isn't that Set? Long time no see! Hey, wanna grab a bite? Our skewers are grilled to perfection!”

“Seto-chan, it's been a while. How about some of our soup, packed with veggies and meat, to warm you up?”

“Seto, got a sandwich? Wanna try it? The bread's fresh out of the oven—once you start, you won't be able to stop!”

“Over here, it's simmered udon. It's not chewy, but it's soft and delicious!”

Like that, Set went back and forth between the surrounding stalls, receiving food from each.

...Of course, it wasn't free. Each time, Rei paid the cost and bought his own portion too.

However, Rei always received more food than the amount paid.

The reason the stall owners generously upsized his portions wasn't just because Rei was a big spender who bought a lot at once, but also because he made their food look so delicious when he ate it.

Rei and Set. Anyone who saw that one person and one beast eating would be so tempted by how delicious it looked that they'd want to eat too.

It being lunchtime only added to the crowd of hungry people.

(If Mireine were here, she'd probably buy up most of the food at these stalls. And if that kept happening, they wouldn't have enough money to get through winter, forcing them to reluctantly take on winter jobs...)

Recalling the friend who went to tell their two companions that the Seeds of Restraint within them had vanished, Rei let out a small smile.

(Even Mireine chose the warning seed over Set... Well, I suppose that was only natural.)

Satisfied that Mireine hadn't forgotten her duty as leader of a Rank C party, even if she did like Set, Rei headed toward the guild, buying large quantities at Gilmu's stall for the first time in ages.

The closer he got to the guild, the more stalls catering to adventurers appeared, and naturally, the number of adventurers increased.

With that, more eyes turned toward Rei and Set...

“Hey, that's... Rei, right? When did he get back?”

“Ah... I heard he came back yesterday.”

“Still, just looking at him, you'd never guess he's a Rank B adventurer. You can tell because Set's there, but if Set wasn't around, you could easily mistake him for some random newbie, right?”

“Well, yeah. You could tell by his face if he took off his hood, but he usually keeps it on.” 　Adventurers who know Rei exchange words with their companions.

Since Rei appeared in this town, for better or worse, he's been involved in numerous incidents.

For that very reason, adventurers active in Gilum who've witnessed this recognize him as someone not to be carelessly provoked or antagonized.

Many feel he's not such a bad guy if you just talk normally, and seeing him with Set is actually quite soothing.

But... it's also true that Rei doesn't look particularly strong based on his pure appearance alone...

“Hey, hey, is that really Crimson? Aren't the rumors pretty exaggerated? No matter how you look at it, I can't imagine that short, scrawny guy being that strong.”

“Yeah, right. ...Probably just got famous because of that griffin, huh? Ha, riding high on his familiar's coattails. Pretty cushy life he's got.”

“But if you think about it, taming a griffin? That's gotta be some serious skill, right?”

“What? What are you talking about? Tamer's just some guy who hides behind monsters, right? A guy like that? We could take him out in one shot. One shot. ...Maybe we should just beat that kid Rei up and steal his griffin?”

“Idiot. Even if that Rei guy is weak, a griffin is a griffin. You think you'd get away with messing with him?”

A little ways off, adventurers who had just arrived in Gilm while Rei was on a mission to the Bestia Empire gathered to discuss him, likely seeing him for the first time.

In a way, it was a familiar sight in Gilm, and those who knew Rei watched these adventurers with somewhat lukewarm eyes.

...Though some did shoot sharp glances at those who mentioned targeting Set.

Such individuals appearing was, in Gilum, a situation that was more or less unavoidable.

Gilum was located on the frontier. While those who grew up in Gilum and became adventurers were one thing, others... those who came to Gilum from other regions were invariably skilled individuals, and consequently, brimming with confidence.

Since skilled adventurers gather in Gilum, newcomers are often treated as such. Naturally, they possess confidence in their abilities and a corresponding sense of pride.

Well, pride alone would be fine, but many among them are convinced their skills should naturally hold sway even in Gilum.

Having gotten by with their abilities up to this point, many simply cannot comprehend the existence of someone beyond their imagination.

To such people, Rei's reputation as a fighter—setting aside his ability to tame griffins—is likely just overblown. ...That's how they see it.

Of course, rumors of Rei's exceptional skill have spread too. But those rumors clash with his short stature and lack of obvious muscle.

Seeing Rei like that, it's no wonder they assume—if not that all the rumors are lies—then at least that the results of the griffin's battles have been misreported.

Well then, I'll be the one to strip away that facade.

With that thought, several adventurers started to call out to Rei, but...

“Hey, cut it out, cut it out. There's no way you lot could beat Rei with your measly strength. Besides, messing with Rei just leads to all sorts of trouble.”

Hearing that voice, several adventurers who had been about to call out to Rei froze in their tracks.

They reflexively wanted to shout back at the insulting words, but when they realized the figure they were staring at was a massive, muscular man whose sheer power was obvious just by looking at him, their words died in their throats, swallowed along with their breath.

“If you're scared stiff just looking at me, there's no way you'll ever beat Rei.”

With those words, the adventurers who had just arrived at the guild were spared Rei's baptism.

“...Well, whatever.”

Rei muttered, watching as several adventurers who had directed hostile glances at him were led away by an adventurer he recognized from the guild.

Had they kept bothering him, he would have fought them off in self-defense, knocked them out, and taken some weapons or armor... He'd particularly had his eye on the spear one of them carried. But since the trouble had been avoided, he decided that was fine too. He continued shopping at the stalls as he headed back to the guild.

By the time he reached the guild entrance, he was already carrying more food than he could hold. He set aside what Set would eat and stored the rest in his Mistling pouch, thinking it would be a waste if it got cold.

“Well, this might take a little while... though I doubt you'll get bored.”

“Gurrrr!”

In the usual familiar and carriage waiting area, Set lay sprawled out, purring contentedly.

Already, nearly twenty residents had gathered around him—from children around five years old to elderly folks in their sixties. Each stroked him, spoke to him, or tried to offer him food.

“Seto-chan! Set-chan! Set-chan!”

Rei thought he recognized a face among them from the stables at Komugi Inn just moments ago, but he told himself it was probably just his imagination and continued into the guild.

Inside the guild, it was already close to noon, so there weren't many people around.

Many adventurers had already finished preparing for the winter, and the absence of such figures likely explained why it was even quieter than usual for midday.

Not necessarily as a substitute, but the tavern attached to the guild was fairly busy with people eating and drinking.

“Ray-kun!”

As he scanned the guild, that voice rang out.

Ray turned his gaze in that direction and saw a cat beastman leaping over the counter.

It was Kenny, the cat beastman receptionist who harbored feelings for Rei.

Despite the stunt of jumping over the counter, her short skirt didn't even flutter.

If you asked Kenny, she'd probably say this was part of her etiquette as a receptionist.

“Hey, Kenny!?”

Lenora shouted, scolding Kenny for her sudden action.

But Kenny paid no heed to her friend's voice, rushing straight over to Rei.

“Rei-kun, I heard you went to the Bestia Empire. Are you okay? I was told you were with Lord Daskar, but when I came back and you weren't there, I was really worried!”

“Ah... yeah, sorry. I had something to take care of over there. Things just happened that way.”

“Things just happened that way... Well, whatever. You not hurt or anything?”

Saying that, Kenny reached out to check for injuries, running his hands over Rei's face and the body beneath the dragon robe.

If this had been someone trying to steal something from Rei, he would have stopped them immediately. But knowing this was an act of concern for him, Rei simply accepted Kenny's actions in silence.

After confirming Rei's body was unharmed, Kenny breathed a sigh of relief... but then, his gaze locked onto Rei's hand.

There, on one of Rei's fingers, sat a ring.

Rings are typically given to someone you have romantic feelings for.

Some wear them purely as fashion accessories, but Rei certainly wasn't the type to care much about fashion.

Which meant someone had made a move on Rei.

In an instant, Kenny processed this and turned his gaze toward Rei with a movement that seemed to make a grinding sound.

...Incidentally, though few in number, many adventurers within the guild had been watching Rei and Kenny's exchange with interest. Yet, the moment they saw Kenny's current movement, their bodies somehow froze.

This happened despite the presence of not only low-rank adventurers, but even C-rank adventurers.

“...Hey, Rei. Where'd you get this ring? Did someone give it to you?”

But Rei, seemingly oblivious to the tension Kenny was radiating, spoke without any sign of nervousness.

“Oh, I got it from Theorem.”

“...Huh?”

Kenny froze, as if time itself had stopped.

The reason for his pause was different from when he'd seen the ring earlier.

Of course. Theorem was unmistakably a man's name.

Which meant Rei was wearing a ring given to him by a man...

Several women who had been eavesdropping on Rei and Kenny's exchange somewhere in the guild let out shrill, uncontrollable screams.

In an instant, Kenny's eyes fixed on several adventurers and guild staff who had cried out, his sharp glare silencing them.

The aura emanating from Kenny, capable of silencing even adventurers, could only be described as demonic.

Still, by the time he turned his gaze back to Rei, that demonic aura had vanished completely. That was typical Kenny.

“Hey, Rei. Um... why would this person named Theorem give you a ring?”

For Kenny, this question was tinged with a sinister premonition: Could Rei, the one he harbored feelings for, possibly have no interest in women?

He asked as if it were nothing, but beneath his tone lay considerable tension.

But Rei, seemingly oblivious to Kenny's state, answered simply.

“I had a little request in the Bestia Empire. As payment for solving it, I got this magic item ring.”

“...Oh. I see.”

Kenny breathed a sigh of relief at those words.

Seeing Rei wearing the ring had thrown him into such a panic he'd forgotten—but now he finally recalled Rei's hobby of collecting magic items.

Rei wore bracelets and other jewelry besides the ring, yet Kenny's eyes had fixated solely on the ring. It was probably because Kenny was a girl at heart.

“It's fine... Ow! Hey, what the hell are you doing all of a sudden!?”

Just as Kenny was about to breathe a sigh of relief, Lenora's fist came crashing down on his head.

Kenny started to complain to Lenora, but seeing the smile playing on her lips, he swallowed his words.

“Kenny, jumping over the counter like that... what on earth were you thinking?”

Facing Lenora, who smiled but whose eyes showed no warmth, Kenny could only manage a forced smile in return.
