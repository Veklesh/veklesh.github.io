Having received an urgent request, Rei and his companions naturally headed straight for the cliff where the dungeon was located.

They had heard that a bird monster had flown out from there, but since they had no information on where it had gone, the dungeon was the only place that offered any clues—that was the reason.

Currently, several adventurers are searching for the bird monster.

Since this was also framed as an emergency request and was merely a search, the reward was meager. Nevertheless, many adventurers from Golz accepted the job to prevent harm to Golz… and the surrounding villages and towns.

...Of course, while there is an element of consideration for the other villages and towns, it cannot be denied that there was also the possibility that if they made a misstep here, the other villages and towns would use that as an excuse to try to muscle in on the dungeon’s interests.

In any case, the agreement was to raise a smoke signal immediately if anything happened, and so everyone scattered in all directions, searching for the bird monsters.

“...A cliff, huh?”

“A cliff, huh.”

Marina replied succinctly to Rei’s words.

In fact, what Rei was currently looking at was indeed a place that could rightly be called a cliff.

The cliff was quite high, and halfway up, there appeared to be a dungeon.

There was a gaping hole in the cliff, and Rei could easily see how a monster like a goblin, kobold, or orc would surely die if it fell from that height.

It was a sight that could only be described as a cliff.

“I’d heard stories about it, but I can’t believe a dungeon actually formed in a place like that.”

Relyu murmured thoughtfully.

“I’d love to show this view to Shumine.”

Of course, the fact that he immediately mentioned his wife’s name showed he was still the same devoted husband as ever.

Knowing that if she indulged Relieu, his lovey-dovey stories would go on forever, Elena spoke up to Rei.

“So, what are we going to do now? It’s good that we made it to the dungeon, but we don’t know if the bird monsters will come back here, do we?”

“True. But we don’t have any other leads… They say practice makes perfect, right?”

“……Do they?”

What Rei said was a line he’d often seen in manga and dramas back in Japan, but there was no way Elena and the others would get it.

“Anyway,”

Clearing his throat softly to hide his embarrassment, Rei spoke again.

“Anyway. There might be some kind of clue, and depending on the situation, that bird monster might come back. …So, Set, try not to give yourself away as much as possible.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set growled, “Leave it to me!”

Hiding his presence was something he always did when hunting, so he was used to it.

Rei stroked Set as if to praise him… and Relieu watched Rei with a look that seemed just a little envious.

That said, Relyu was also an adventurer with a nickname. He wasn’t careless enough to start doting on Set in the middle of a job.

“Then, shall we start by checking the nearby trees to see if there are any bird monsters?”

At Marina’s suggestion, Rei was about to say something, but before he could, the Golz adventurers who had been here since before Rei and the others arrived shook their heads.

“We checked ourselves, but we didn’t find any real clues.”

Their tone sounded somewhat submissive, but seeing that the griffin Set was with them, that was only natural.

It was precisely because Set wasn’t there at the guild that they hadn’t recognized Rei as Rei at first.

“Is that so? Well, I’ll look into it anyway. It’s not that much trouble.”

At Marina’s words, the adventurer, who had been tense, tilted his head in puzzlement.

There were several patches of trees growing at the base of the cliff.

If she were to thoroughly inspect every single one of those trees, she’d naturally have to go out of her way to climb them, which would be a huge hassle.

“But why?” the adventurer wondered, gently averting his gaze from Marina’s smile, which seemed designed to stir a man’s desires.

But the adventurer’s question was answered the very next moment.

Just as Marina chanted a short spell, a slightly strong wind blew.

“Huh!?”

Perhaps sensing something in the wind, Relieu scanned the surroundings with a sharp gaze… and, relieved to see Marina was unharmed, released his grip on the hilt of his longsword.

“Hey, could you please stop pulling stunts like that?”

“Oh, I’m sorry. But normally, you wouldn’t even notice, would you? …See?”

In response to Relieu’s complaint, Marina flashed a seductive smile and turned her gaze toward the adventurers from Golz.

In fact, the adventurers she was looking at seemed completely clueless about what was going on and were tilting their heads in confusion.

This, perhaps, was the difference between adventurers from the countryside and those with a reputation.

Relieu watched the adventurers, but they had no idea why they were being stared at like that and just tilted their heads in confusion.

“See?”

“……I get it. So, what happened in the end? Did you find any clues?”

“Unfortunately, just like those kids said, I didn’t find any clues. There was something that looked like a bird’s nest, but it was just a regular mountain bird.”

“In that case…”

“Grrr!”

Just as Marina spoke and Rei was about to say what they should do next, Set let out a cry, as if to draw everyone’s attention.

A sharp cry, audible only to those nearby.

The adventurers from Golz simply looked puzzled, unsure what Set’s cry meant, but Rei—who had known Set for a long time—and Elena and the others immediately drew their weapons.

“Shaaahhhhh!”

“Shh-aa, shh-aaaah!”

“Shhhhhhh!”

That cry seemed to come from nowhere.

From the crevices in the cliff and the gaps between the trees, Rei could make out what kind of creature was making those sounds.

It was covered in yellow feathers, and as if to show that it was no ordinary bird, it had two wings on each side—a total of four, or two pairs.

Though it was still too far away to see clearly, he could tell it was quite large.

“...So a bird monster is that yellow thing with four wings?”

“Y-yeah!”

The adventurer replied immediately to Rei’s words.

Hearing this, Rei realizes that the flock of birds heading toward them are monsters that have emerged from the dungeon… Rank D monsters known as Yellow Birds.

“I’m glad you came back, but why did you come back? Don’t tell me it’s some kind of homing instinct or something?”

Perhaps the Yellow Birds didn’t really care that they had left the dungeon.

He wondered if they had simply returned to the dungeon just as birds—crows, for instance—return to their nests at night.

And since there was no reason why the others couldn’t imagine what Rei was thinking, Elena and the others—including Relieu—all wore expressions of understanding. …Though only Byune showed no change in her usual expression.

“So, Rei. What are we going to do?”

“It’s not like I have a choice. I’m sure Relieu has already figured it out, hasn’t she?”

Since the monster they’d gone out of their way to find had revealed itself, it was only natural to assume they’d be allowed to defeat it.

At Rei’s words, Relue gave a fierce smile and nodded in response.

Seeing Relu’s smile, Rei addressed everyone present—including the adventurers from Golz—once more.

“So, as luck would have it, our target has come back here on its own. And since it’s our target, we’ll have to defeat it once it’s back… Now then, who here has the means to attack that Yellowbird that’s trying to enter the dungeon?”

In response to Rei’s question, Elena was the first to raise her hand. Following her, Marina and Byune raised their hands as well…

“Huh? Vihera too?”

“Yes. Though I’m not sure if you’d call it a means of attack, though.”

Saying that, Vihera picked up a stone lying on the ground.

“Ah… I see,” they said. Several people who saw this looked as if they understood.

In fact, simply throwing a stone is an easy attack method anyone can use.

That said, the real question is whether she can hit the target—in this case, the Yellowbird—with the stone, and whether she can throw it with enough force to deal effective damage if she does.

Especially since the Yellowbird was flying higher than the dungeon, and according to Rei’s estimate, it seemed to be at least fifty meters above the dungeon’s location.

If they were as strong as Vihera, it might be different, but for Golz’s adventurers, it was doubtful whether they could hit a Yellowbird with a stone powerful enough to do significant damage.

(That said, there’s a chance a lucky throw might hit the Yellowbird’s head, and since the goal is to distract them, it might be worth letting them try. …Though, if they just throw a bunch of stones, it might end up getting in Set’s way.)

Since Set, like the Yellowbird, is capable of flying freely through the air, if they were to throw a large number of stones, there was naturally a chance those stones could hit Set.

Or even if they didn’t hit Set, there was a very real chance they would get in the way when Set attacked.

Realizing this, Rei decided it would be best to limit the other adventurers’ stone-throwing to just the beginning, and turned his gaze toward Relieu.

Needless to say, there was Marina with her spirit magic; Mirage with her Sleipnir Boots that let her walk in the air and her linked swords; Elena, who possessed various magical attack methods; and Byune, who was naturally skilled at throwing objects, starting with her long needles.

Unlike them, Relieu—though an adventurer with a nickname—was a pure swordsman.

In fact, Rei had thought that, just like Vihera, her only means of attacking distant opponents might be throwing stones…

“Hm? Oh. At a distance like that, I’ll probably manage.”

Relieu, who had caught Rei’s gaze, declared this with full confidence.

Those words came as a surprise to Rei… but with the Yellowbird closing in, he didn’t have the luxury of time to ask for details.

His opponent was also an adventurer with a nickname, and although they hadn’t known each other for very long, Rei didn’t think he’d tell a lie that would be immediately obvious, so he simply nodded without saying anything else.

“Got it. In that case, I’ll leave the attack to Relue. What about Elena?”

Elena was suddenly addressed, but she seemed to understand immediately what Rei was getting at and nodded in agreement.

“I’ll join Rei in attacking from the air as well.”

“I see. Alright, then. For now, we’ll take down as many Yellowbirds as we can here to reduce their numbers, so keep that in mind. Marina will coordinate the ground attack. Set.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a short cry, then lifted Rei onto his back. With Elena holding onto his front legs, he soared into the sky.

The Yellowbirds hadn’t noticed Set yet. They were still moving in a flock of dozens toward what appeared to be a dungeon.

They moved to a spot even higher than the Yellowbirds… and Rei and Elena both leaped down from Set.

In Elena’s case, it wasn’t so much a jump as letting go of the front leg of Set she had been holding onto.

In any case, as they fell from the sky, Rei drew the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring.

Elena, too, transformed Mirage into a whip.

“Flying Slash!”

The strike unleashed by the Death Scythe cleanly severed Yellowbird’s neck.

The Yellowbird’s headless torso, naturally, fell toward the ground.

Elena’s Mirage strike similarly severed the Yellowbird’s head, and upon activating Sleipnir Boots, she kicked off the air to leap forward, swinging Mirage once more to take down the Yellowbird.

Attacks from the ground also dealt some damage, as stones thrown by the adventurers struck several Yellowbirds.

Furthermore, Marina’s spirit magic prevented the Yellowbirds from flying, causing them to crash to the ground.

Set’s strike caused the head to explode… and just as the number of Yellowbirds began to dwindle, Rei saw it.

A slash, identical to a flying slash, was unleashed from the longsword wielded by Relieu... slicing through the Yellow Bird’s wing and sending it crashing to the ground.
