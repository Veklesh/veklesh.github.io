Rei released magic power, and the exceptionally dazzling light faded.

Watching this, Rei turned his gaze toward Marina, who was touching the World Tree with her hand overlaid on his.

Marina met Rei's gaze and, as always, offered a radiant smile before speaking.

"The World Tree has definitely regained its strength. ...Look."

Marina's gaze shifted upward toward the upper branches and leaves of the World Tree.

There were the same branches and leaves as just moments before.

That much was undeniable, but the overwhelming power emanating from them was vastly different compared to when he first saw the World Tree.

Even when he first saw it, Rei had felt its abundant vitality and been overwhelmed by its size, but now it was different.

The difference was so stark it made her want to ask what she had been feeling just moments before.

It possessed an overwhelming presence truly worthy of being called the World Tree.

"This... is incredible."

Rei, who uttered words of astonishment, could be said to be one of the few in the group who hadn't completely lost their composure.

In fact, even Elena and Vihera were completely captivated by the presence of the World Tree now before them, utterly mesmerized.

"Right? ...But this level of recovery is definitely thanks to Rei's magic."

Even when every Dark Elf in the settlement poured their magic into it, they could barely keep the World Tree's condition stable. Yet now, just by Rei alone channeling his magic into it, it had become like this.

Without Rei's magic, the World Tree might have withered away.

Considering that, Marina's grateful gaze toward Rei was only natural.

"Ridiculous... He really healed the World Tree all by himself..."

The stunned voice came from Ragdo, who had been observing the World Tree's treatment from a short distance away.

He had heard about Rei's magical power the previous day in the plaza from his confidant, Rejuum. He had heard about it... but even so, Ragud, who lacked the ability to sense magical power, probably hadn't truly grasped its reality.

But seeing it directly before his eyes now, he couldn't help but accept Rejuum's words.

Rei's magic power was incomparable to their own... It was literally on a different level.

Ragdo stared blankly at Rei and Marina, but eventually, a smile touched his lips.

It wasn't exactly pleasant learning that the person Marina had brought was the one who healed the World Tree, but when asked whether he valued his own feelings or the World Tree's healing more, there was no need for hesitation.

Watching the World Tree, now visibly more vibrant than before the magic infusion, Ragdo sighed and turned his gaze toward Rei and Marina.

There, he noticed that Rei and Marina had finally placed their hands together. Both were blushing faintly—Marina still wore a radiant smile—and were in the process of pulling their hands away.

Naturally, Elena and Vihera found this amusing and moved closer.

Ara wore a pleased smile at Elena's reaction, while Byune glanced at Vihera before fixing her gaze steadily on the World Tree.

Set stood a little apart, Yellow still perched on his back, gazing at the World Tree just like Byune.

Ragdo had been observing Rei's group, but soon approached the four who were fussing over whether their hands had touched or not.

(Becoming involved with Lady Marina... I wonder how that would turn out. It seems like it would lead to all sorts of trouble. And with two other beauties as stunning as Lady Marina present, I don't feel envy—I feel pity.)

With two others possessing beauty on par with Marina's, he would undoubtedly be subjected to resentment, jealousy, envy, hatred... and become a target for all sorts of people.

Considering that, Ragdo envied Rei's position, but he certainly didn't want to be in his shoes.

Still, he felt some gratitude toward Rei for finishing the World Tree's treatment.

He knew she certainly didn't think kindly of him after yesterday's incident, but even so, helping her here and now seemed the least he could do.

"Shouldn't we head back to the settlement soon? The villagers must be quite alarmed by that light earlier. ...Well, the World Tree has clearly regained its power even from a distance now, so I doubt they'd be panicking."

Even if they're startled, it's a good kind of surprise.

After all, the World Tree, whose power had been steadily waning over time, had recovered in an instant.

No dark elf in this settlement could fail to understand that.

At Ragdo's words, Marina nodded hurriedly.

It wouldn't be fair to say that nod didn't carry any intention of glossing over the incident where she'd held hands with Rei, but it was also true that they needed to formally inform the settlement's people about the World Tree's recovery.

Rei also looked surprised at Ragdo, who had interjected at such a perfect moment.

He hadn't expected him to shield him.

Ragdo surely understood that Rei disliked him.

So why did he shield Rei? Was it partly to apologize for the Marina incident, or simply because he was grateful for the World Tree matter?

Regardless, it was true that Rei had been saved by Ragdo's words.

"Y-Yeah. Well then, I suppose we should head back to the settlement soon. The World Tree's treatment is finished, but we still need to monitor it for a while longer. And above all, we need to thoroughly check on the forest's abnormalities."

"Right. If we waste time here, it just means the situation back at the settlement won't be clarified sooner, and that'll only cause more confusion."

Rei agreed with Marina, who spoke hurriedly.

Elena and Vihera watched the two with questioning looks, but it was true as Rei said—they couldn't just leave the village unattended like this.

Reaching that conclusion, Elena and Vihera agreed to postpone further questioning until after this matter was resolved.

That they could communicate so deeply with just a glance meant they both shared the same thoughts... no, the same feelings.

"Alright. Then let's go for now."

At Vihera's words, the entire group left the World Tree and headed toward the settlement.

Naturally, Rei also started to leave the World Tree and head toward the settlement, but suddenly, feeling as if he sensed someone's gaze, he stopped in his tracks.

He looked around again, but no one seemed to be looking at him specifically.

(I've felt this gaze before... What is it?)

He was certain he felt the gaze, yet he couldn't pinpoint where it was coming from.

Worse still, if he let his guard down for even a moment, the sensation vanished.

And yet, it carried a sense of urgency.

It was a strange gaze in many ways, but after scanning his surroundings, Rei couldn't find its source.

"Rei, what are you doing? We're leaving you behind?"

Elena called out, probably noticing Rei lagging behind as he scanned his surroundings.

The moment his attention shifted to Elena's voice, the gaze directed at him vanished cleanly.

(What's going on?)

He wondered, but it was clear that he was the only one feeling that gaze... Even Set didn't seem to notice it. Set tilted his head, looking puzzled.

A gaze only he could feel. A gaze that seemed to evade even Set's keen senses, sixth sense, and ability to sense magic. It was nothing but a mystery to Rei.

Still, he didn't rush to resolve it, probably because he couldn't sense any malice in the gaze directed at him.

If it were a malicious gaze, Rei would have panicked, feeling compelled to do something about it. But the gaze directed at him now seemed more like one filled with goodwill.

「Rei！」

Despite having called out to Rei earlier, Rei had stopped again and was looking around. Elena called out to Rei once more, her voice louder than before.

"Oh, sorry. Just something caught my attention."

Running to catch up with Elena, who was a little ahead of him, Rei explained.

"Something on your mind? What is it?"

"Well, it's just that..."

I felt a strange gaze.

As if to cut off what Rei was about to say, cheers erupted from ahead.

The source of those voices, filled with unparalleled joy, were naturally the dark elves from the settlement.

They were likely celebrating seeing the World Tree, the most precious thing to them, restored to health.

And so, their faces beaming with joy, they were running toward the World Tree... which meant, toward where Rei and Elena were standing.

"This is getting dangerous. Let's move over a bit and clear the path."

Everyone nodded at Marina's words and moved to the edge of the path.

The Dark Elves who had been running over didn't seem to notice Marina or Rei at all, continuing to run straight toward the World Tree.

"Well, I suppose so. Youth is wonderful, isn't it?"

Learning that those who had run off were among the younger members of the settlement—though even they were mostly close to a hundred years old—Marina smiled as if looking at adorable children.

Seeing this, Rei wanted to ask how old Marina actually was, but the moment he opened his mouth, a chill ran down his spine and he fell silent.

"Honestly... I wish you'd act with a little more pride as a Dark Elf."

Ragdo muttered grumpily, though a smile he couldn't quite hide played on his face.

"Well, it can't be helped. Everyone probably has their own thoughts about the World Tree incident. More importantly, we should hurry to the settlement. We need to report to Grandfather. ...Since you're here, Ragdo, I expect you to speak up properly too."

"Understood."

Ragdo was also immensely pleased that the World Tree had been healed.

While he had mixed feelings about Rei, he had no intention of trying to conceal her achievement.

Once the dark elves heading toward the World Tree had settled down, the group set off for the settlement once more.

But... what awaited them in the settlement was an unexpected turn of events for everyone.

"Huh? Grandfather went outside the barrier? ...Why?"

Marina tried to explain the World Tree matter to Optis, but no one was home. After asking around about Optis' whereabouts, she learned he had headed outside the settlement.

"Well? I was too excited about the World Tree to notice. I only caught a glimpse when I was celebrating. ...But since the World Tree is thriving again, maybe he just wanted to take a look outside?"

Normally, it would be nothing short of suicide for an elder, someone called the village elder, to venture outside alone... especially outside the village where excited, increasingly violent monsters were multiplying.

But Optis was an exception.

Despite being a Dark Elf, she possessed the raw power to slaughter monsters with her bare hands. She likely had the confidence that she could handle anything, even alone outside the settlement.

And Rei, who had fought him directly, understood that Optis' confidence was never overconfidence.

"What do we do? If the person we were supposed to report to isn't here..."

"Isn't staying here just a waste of time?" Rei considered for a moment before turning her gaze toward the outside of the settlement... toward the barrier's protective circle.

"Right. Then let's go inform Grandfather. I also want to investigate how the World Tree regaining its power is affecting the forest."

"Are you sure? I mean, I'm fine with it."

Looking toward a spot a little further away, she saw people dancing with joy, overwhelmed by the World Tree regaining its power.

Watching the rare sight of Dark Elves dancing, he asked if it was truly okay to leave the current state of the settlement unattended, but Marina's gaze turned toward Ragdo.

"It'll be fine. Ragdo will handle this commotion."

"W-What, me!?"

She clearly hadn't expected the conversation to turn to her here.

After all, yesterday's exchange should have made it abundantly clear that the hostility between her and Marina was as stark as it could possibly be.

So why...

But Marina paid no mind to Ragdo's concerns. Entrusting the settlement to him, she led Rei and the others outside the village.

(Now that the World Tree has regained its power, whatever Ragdo says is pointless... right?)

Now that Rei, whom Marina had brought, had successfully healed the World Tree, it was obvious that it would be difficult for Ragdo to eliminate Marina.

And above all, Marina planned to return to Gilm once the World Tree matter was settled.

Even if there were political repercussions, at least for the time being... for a hundred years or more, there wouldn't be any particular trouble.

"Alright then, is that good?"

Since no one knew what would happen at the World Tree, it was convenient that everyone was already equipped with their weapons and armor, eliminating the need to return home.
