Leaving Set outside, Rei and Vihera entered the guardhouse and immediately addressed the guards.

“I heard you were looking for me. What’s up?”

“Ah, yes. Actually, we’re planning a large-scale search of the mansion and the surrounding estates with the guards. Captain Ranga will be participating this time.”

“...Ranga?”

Ranga is the head of the security force.

He has a stern appearance with a rugged face and a beard, but his personality is gentle.

That said, his gentle nature doesn’t mean Ranga is weak.

That said, since Rei hadn’t seen Ranga in a while, he hadn’t expected his name to come up here.

“Ah. He was incredibly busy with the expansion project until just a little while ago, but now that it’s winter, things have settled down somewhat. …Though apparently he’s been busy again lately because of the Kobold incident. Anyway, since yesterday’s mansion is apparently related to the Kobold incident, Captain Ranga decided to come out as well.”

“Hmm. …Well, it is that kind of place. It makes perfect sense for the captain of the security force to show up.”

If they’re going to investigate not just a single mansion near the Noble District, but the mansions in the surrounding area as well, it goes without saying that they’ll need a figurehead—someone who can convince the residents of those mansions and the people of the Noble District.

In that sense, it’s fair to say that Captain Ranga of the Security Force would make a suitable figurehead.

“Ah. I think Captain Ranga made his decision with that in mind. …He’s also asked a few other adventurers for their cooperation. Of course, if he were to publicly recruit them, there’s a chance the other side might find out, so he’s doing it discreetly with adventurers he trusts.”

“Hmm. Well, I suppose that’s fine. Though there are quite a few whose usefulness in the current situation is questionable.”

Winter is a time that many adventurers treat as a long vacation, so most of them are out of shape to some degree.

Still, they’re undoubtedly stronger than ordinary people, so I suppose they’ll be a useful force in a situation like this.

“So, does that mean we can count on Rei’s cooperation too?”

“Yeah, I’m fine with that. …And it looks like Vihera’s fully on board too.”

As I said this, glancing at Vihera, she turned her gaze toward me, flashed a beaming smile, and gave a slight nod.

“I mean, it’s fine because it’s me, but normally, we’d have to investigate… or in this case, conduct a search and seizure. Unless we’d mentioned a search and seizure beforehand, it’d be impossible for them to spring into action the moment they were told today, right?”

“Ah… I’m sorry. This whole situation came on so suddenly. We’ve been dealing with a lot of complications on our end, too.”

Saying that, the guard who had been speaking with Rei bowed his head.

Faced with such a sincere apology, Rei found it hard to complain.

Part of it was probably because he knew for a fact that yesterday’s incident had caused the situation to escalate rapidly.

After all, he never expected such vicious tentacles to keep appearing one after another.

“I suppose they’re using some method to control or summon the Kobolds, but the fact that those tentacles are the ones being used… Honestly, I feel like those tentacles are many times, dozens of times, and in some cases, even hundreds of times more dangerous.”

Rei’s Death Scythe could cut them off, but there was significant resistance.

It’s not that such a thing has never happened before… but it’s also true that it hasn’t happened that often.

If that’s what happens with the Death Scythe, just imagine if those tentacles were to appear in large numbers right in the middle of town.

Thinking about it that way, Rei can understand why they’re launching a forced search so soon after yesterday’s events.

“That’s exactly it. That’s why I’m counting on your strength too, Rei.”

“I know. …But if I hadn’t come here, what were you planning to do? Like, if I’d forgotten the message left at the guild because I was busy with something else?”

“I don’t think you’d forget, having actually experienced what happened yesterday.”

Was that trust, or was it a mix of exasperation toward Rei for getting himself into trouble?

In any case, the guard genuinely thought that, and the fact that Rei had come to the guardhouse was undeniable.

Thinking about it that way, the guard’s words—based on his reading of Rei’s actions—were undoubtedly correct.

“Was my behavior that simple?” Just as that thought crossed his mind, he averted his gaze from Vihera, who was smiling beside him, and spoke, partly to hide his embarrassment.

“So, when are we going to conduct the raid? Will it be at night, as expected, so they don’t get suspicious? If there are a lot of guards moving around, it’ll be easier for them to figure out what we’re up to.”

“No, this afternoon.”

“That’s pretty early, isn’t it? And in broad daylight, too?”

Winter-like clouds covered the sky, but the sun still peeked through the gaps, providing a fair amount of light.

Under these conditions, while adventurers with varied gear might go unnoticed, a group of guards moving together would undoubtedly attract attention.

……Though Rei, who’s traveling with Set and Vihera, undoubtedly draws attention in a different way than the guards.

“Ideally, we’d do this at night, but if Rei and the others found the underground space and saw the tentacles, they might decide it’s too dangerous and try to escape from Gilm. …Right now, we can slip out through the construction site without having to go through the main gate.”

“There is the earthen wall Rei built, but it wouldn’t be hard to climb over from the Gilm side.”

“Did you actually go and look at the earthen wall?” one of the guards asked.

In fact, that was correct; while the earthen wall Rei had built made it difficult to enter Gilm from the outside, getting out from the inside wasn’t particularly difficult.

After all, Rei originally built the earthen wall to prevent enemies—including Kobolds—from invading from outside Gilm, not to deal with anyone trying to leave Gilm, so that makes sense.

“In that case, the people on the Gilm side should have sunk the ground as well. Though there’s no point in saying that now.”

“Don’t worry about it. After all, no one could have predicted this would happen.”

One of the guards said, as if to comfort Rei.

“Hearing you say that really helps me out. …So, you mentioned we’re going on a forced search starting this afternoon—that includes other mansions besides the one from yesterday, right? If that’s the case, should Vihera and I go to different mansions?”

Setting aside the pink tentacles, considering the strength of the people guarding the other mansions—the ones who attacked Rei and the others—having combatants like Rei and Vihera combined into a single unit would likely be a waste of resources.

That’s what I was thinking when I asked, but the guards gave me complicated, or perhaps hesitant, looks.

“Um… well, you see. We can’t control Vihera, so if possible, we’d like Vihera to act together with Rei. As I mentioned earlier, we’re reaching out to reliable adventurers, so for now, we shouldn’t be short on manpower.”

Even after hearing the guard’s words, Vihera didn’t seem particularly dissatisfied.

She probably knew she was being treated like a nuisance.

Especially since the guards had been called out numerous times before due to the commotion Vihera had caused, she assumed they’d be even less eager to team up with her.

That said, it wouldn’t be surprising if some people thought it was dangerous to let Vihera run wild and insisted she travel with them, but for better or worse—or unfortunately—it seemed none of the guards currently present were that type.

It would be quicker and easier to leave it to Rei, who was in a party with Vihera and was used to dealing with her.

Rei and Vihera both nodded in response to those words, spoken with that calculation in mind.

“Got it. If that’s fine with you, I’m fine with it. …Vihera’s okay with it too, right?”

“Yes, that’s fine with me too. Still… hehe.”

Seeing Vihera suddenly break into a smile—a genuinely happy one at that—not only Rei but the guards as well shot her puzzled glances.

Given how Vihera had been treated, they probably never expected her to actually be in a good mood—at most, they’d anticipated her being upset.

That said, it wouldn’t be good if the guards asked her something here and she ended up saying she’d have to split up from Rei after all.

The guards thought this… but Rei, unaware of their thoughts, simply asked as if nothing were amiss.

“I was sure you’d be in a bad mood. What’s with the change of heart?”

“Stop it!” the guards screamed inwardly, though there was no way Rei could hear them.

...While screaming inside their heads, perhaps because they too wanted to know the reason behind Vihera’s smile, the guards watched her closely, even as they felt a sense of dissatisfaction deep down.

“Oh, well, Rei and I are going together, aren’t we? So it’s not a date.”

“……That’s quite a dangerous kind of date.”

Though Rei said this in response to her unexpected remark, it was undeniably true that he was actually a little pleased to hear Vihera say it.

The guards watched the two of them exchange words, their eyes filled with a mix of exasperation and jealousy.

Even though she had caused him trouble countless times, there was no denying that Vihera was a beauty so exquisite that even the term “exquisite” fell short of describing her.

As a man, it was impossible not to feel jealous of Rei, who was the object of Vihera’s affection—not just friendship, but romantic love.

That said, if asked whether he himself would want Vihera to harbor such feelings toward him, he would find it difficult to nod in agreement—no matter how peerlessly beautiful she was, she had quite a few personality issues.

Rei was the one being looked at like that, but since he was used to that kind of gaze, he didn’t seem particularly bothered and spoke up.

“So, if we’re conducting a forced search this afternoon, what should I, Vihera, and Set—who’s out front—do? We can’t exactly stay at the station all afternoon, can we?”

“Ah, yeah. It’s fine if you stay at the station. There’s no particular problem with that. Actually, isn’t there something you guys need to do before this afternoon?”

“……No. Well, there are various little things, but nothing that absolutely has to be done today. Though, if I had to pick something, I’d like to take a quick look at the earthen wall we built at the construction site. I think it’s probably fine, but just in case…”

He didn’t need to say more; the guards understood exactly what Rei was getting at.

“You’re right. We should probably check it out just in case. I’m sure it’s fine, but that’s definitely the easiest place to escape from. …Though, given that it’s the middle of winter out in the wilderness, no one in their right mind would want to go outside.”

“True. Under normal circumstances.”

Rei implied that whoever was behind this incident could by no means be called “ordinary.”

In reality, anyone capable of summoning tentacles like that and, as a result, luring Kobolds to Gilm could hardly be called “ordinary.”

It was precisely because he understood this that the guard had responded to Rei’s words with such a meaningful look.

“Well then, we’ll go check out that earthen wall for now. Is that okay?”

“Yeah. We don’t have anything else to do anyway, so we might as well check it out while grabbing a bite to eat.”

“You haven’t changed a bit.”

At the mention of “snacking,” a few of the guards smiled and replied.

Normally, if you were about to step into the stronghold of an unknown enemy, you wouldn’t have the slightest appetite.

Despite that, Rei seemed completely unfazed and simply said they’d grab some snacks.

From the guards’ perspective, it was only natural to wonder just how much guts he had.

That said, Rei had been through all sorts of things up to this point—countless experiences, in fact.

There was no way something like this would dampen his appetite.

“Is that so? You can’t fight on an empty stomach. …I forget which country it was, but there’s a saying like that. Appetite is important, you know.”

Of course, that’s a Japanese saying, but he couldn’t possibly mention that here, so he brushed it off with that.

“You can’t fight on an empty stomach, huh? Now that you mention it, that’s a pretty convincing saying.”

As a guard, those words must have struck a chord with him.

“Well, that’s the gist of it. Anyway, we’re going to take a look at the earthen wall.”

With that, Rei and Vihera left the guardhouse together.
