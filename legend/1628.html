After Rei and the others set off to meet the envoy from Gilm, the group's steps naturally grew heavy.

...Though traveling by carriage, their feet feeling heavy didn't truly slow their pace.

Still... it was only natural that those inside the carriage felt a certain gloom, unlike usual.

They knew capable individuals like Vihera and Byune remained behind, but still... it was only natural the atmosphere grew gloomy.

Would they really not be attacked by monsters or bandits? And even if they were, could the current number of guards handle it?

Given the current situation, it was only natural to feel such anxiety.

"Honestly, I've been underestimated quite a bit, haven't I?"

Vihera, riding in the lead carriage, muttered with a hint of dissatisfaction.

While most of those who had been forced into prostitution now wore ordinary clothes, only Vihera and a few other enthusiasts wore outfits that were distinctly prostitute-like... or even more provocative than that.

Though Vihera's attire was less suggestive and more accurately described as lewd, she now gazed out the carriage window at the sky, looking displeased despite her attire's suggestive nature.

A few clouds floated in the sky, but today, once again, a summer sun hung high, asserting its presence with fierce intensity.

Glancing up at the sun as if dazzled, Vihera then shifted her gaze to another carriage passing nearby.

Since everyone was traveling by carriage, it was naturally impossible for Vihera to know the specific expressions of the others.

Still, considering the atmosphere before departure, it was easy to imagine what the others were thinking inside their carriages now.

"Please don't say that. It's not that we don't trust your skill, Vihera. But still, with three people gone—Rei, Elena, and Marina—it's only natural to feel uneasy."

Gimekara, sharing the carriage, turned toward Vihera... yet averted her gaze as she spoke.

Besides Gimekara, others like Byune and Ryutas were also in this carriage. Even surrounded by so many people, if one stared intently at Vihera, one might easily become captivated by base desires.

"Well, I saw with my own eyes just how strong Rei and the others are when we left Mejogo, and I've seen them defeat cavalry and bandits since then too. It's only natural you're worried about Rei not being here."

Ryutas continued Gime Kara's words, stating this.

Truthfully, when it came to combat, Rei stood out terribly.

He's short, yet he rampages wielding Death Scythe and Twilight Spear in both hands—there's no way he could go unnoticed.

And precisely because he stands out, he inevitably seems like the most dependable member of the Crimson Wings. ...Being the party leader probably plays a part too.

That said, when it comes to standing out, Elena wielding the Mirage Chainblade and Marina, who freely manipulates spirit magic, are equally conspicuous.

And Set stands out immensely just by his appearance, even before considering his fighting style.

Moreover, Set is popular as a mascot character, and many rely on him not physically, but mentally.

Compared to such members, Vihera inevitably pales in comparison.

Punching, kicking, breaking joints... occasionally using the Magic Palm to destroy opponents from within.

His fighting style itself inevitably pales in comparison to the other members.

...Though in terms of appearance, Vihera is extremely conspicuous.

As a thief, Byune's combat style also doesn't stand out as much as the others'.

Throwing needles and striking with White Cloud are quick attacks, but they're hardly flashy.

"I know that. ...But still, when they're this blatant about it, it's no wonder I find it unpleasant, right?"

"I suppose so. But one thing. The reason everyone's feeling down isn't necessarily because Vihera-san isn't reliable... It's probably more about Set's problem."

"...I'm not sure I agree with that, though."

Whether she found Gimechara's explanation convincing or not, Vihera seemed slightly cheered as she looked up at the sky through the window again.

Earlier, even the seemingly endless, high blue sky had felt uninteresting to her, but now it didn't feel quite so bleak.

(Set's power... power? I'm not sure if "power" is the right word in this situation, but I certainly didn't expect him to become this popular.)

Vihera said no more, simply gazing up at the sky.

Byune remained still beside Vihera.

"...That young lady, was that Byune-chan? Children are terrible at keeping quiet, aren't they..."

Gimekara murmured quietly, making sure Byune couldn't hear, but Ryutas, who overheard, spoke calmly.

"She's a member of the Crimson Wings led by Rei. Obviously not just some ordinary kid you'd find anywhere."

"Now that you mention it, I suppose you're right."

Rank B Party, Crimson Wings.

The very fact she belongs to that party means Byune couldn't possibly be just some ordinary kid.

In some cases, they might even recruit a child under the pretense of protecting them.

But it was clear from watching their battles so far that Byune wasn't just some child being protected.

Gimekara hadn't seen the Byune fight, but as a member of the Zolge Trading Company, he gathered information thoroughly.

"Even a child possesses that much power... The world is vast, isn't it?"

"Yeah. Probably, there are some out there who wouldn't be recognized if they were just in Jaya. Though, if you asked me if I'd want to meet one of those guys... well, that's a bit of a gray area."

As they spoke, the carriage continued onward... and around the time the sun reached its zenith, they stopped for a break to have lunch.

Normally, they could enjoy the delicious food Rei pulled out from Misty Ring, but Rei was nowhere to be seen now.

As a result, most ended up eating a meal of dried meat and hardened bread washed down with water.

A few skilled hunters and those knowledgeable about edible wild plants managed to gather some game, but there wasn't enough to go around for everyone. It had to be shared among the ten members of the group traveling together.

After finishing their meal, they took a short break, including tending to the horses, before setting off again... and soon evening fell.

"We'll camp here tonight. Everyone, get to work preparing the meal together."

Following Vihera's command, nearly a thousand people began preparing simultaneously.

But, as expected, their movements were sluggish compared to usual.

(Honestly, I didn't expect morale to drop this much just because Rei and the others, and Set, aren't here. ...But we can't stay with this group forever either. They need to get used to this situation. Besides...)

Vihera's gaze fell upon the carriage, now unhitched from its horses.

Back when they first departed from Mejogo, they had repeatedly camped out, sleeping on the grass.

But now they had the Sand Ship, and even without it, they had the carriage.

Of course, sleeping inside the carriage wasn't exactly good for the body, but it was still gentler than sleeping directly on the ground, offering shelter from the rain and wind.

"Vihera-san, Byune-san, here's your meal."

"Yes, thank you."

"Mm."

Gimekara handed over dried meat and hardened bread, along with dried fruit as dessert, seemingly for supper.

While this was a familiar menu for ordinary adventurers, for Vihera, who was traveling with Rei, it was actually quite an exotic meal.

Byune, however, it was a menu he'd eaten to the point of disgust back in the Labyrinth City, long before he'd even met Vihera, so it held no novelty whatsoever.

"Well then, we'll eat a little ways off."

"Oh, really? I don't mind if we eat together."

"No. I have things to discuss with Ryutas-san."

With that, Gime Kara bowed and left the area.

In the end, only Vihera and Byune remained.

"Come to think of it, it feels like it's been quite a while since we've had a meal just the two of us."

"Hmm."

Vihera said while nibbling on dried meat, and Byune gave a short reply.

The way she drank water with the salty jerky reminded him somewhat of a small animal.

Though Byune's appetite was second only to Rei's, her eating manners weren't messy.

Even now, she didn't appear to be making a mess, and one might even feel a warm sense of affection watching her.

...If it weren't for Set's presence, she might well be treated like the group's mascot.

"I wonder what happened to Rei and the others. It's about time they came back."

"Hmm."

"Huh? You don't think they'll be back that soon? Well, exchanging information with the envoy group alone is bound to take quite a while, after all."

"Hmm."

"True. But if Rei had come back... no, even Elena would have been fine. If they'd been here, we could have enjoyed ourselves a little more."

"Hmm."

"It's fine. I wasn't planning on taking it that seriously anyway."

To Vihera's words, Byune only responded with her usual 'Hmm'.

Still, the fact that they understood each other perfectly was proof that Vihera and Byune had been together for a long time, no matter what.

If it were someone like Rei, Elena, or Marina here, they'd probably grasp the gist of what Byune meant. But to understand everything as precisely as Vihera did... well, that was simply beyond them.

After that, to an outside observer, it looked like Vihera was doing all the talking, with Byune only giving short replies... yet despite that, they continued their meal while maintaining solid communication.

The meal itself wasn't large, so it ended quickly.

...Though, even if you had a huge amount of preserved foods like dried meat or hardened bread, eating large quantities of it would be tough unless you were extremely hungry.

"It was a bit unusual, but still rather bland, wasn't it?"

Thinking this, Vihera glanced around.

Among the groups of ten eating together, some were already eating meat—perhaps they'd succeeded in some sort of hunt during this short time.

Seeing this, Vihera turned her gaze to Byune next.

Then she looked toward the woods a short distance away.

The thief Byune was skilled at moving through the woods.

Or perhaps he could even take down birds flying through the air with his long needle throws.

(Maybe I should have asked Byune from the start?)

Vihera thought this, but even if she had considered it after the meal was already over, there was nothing she could do about it now.

(Though Byune might still be able to eat more...)

Though small in stature, Byune ate more than Vihera.

There was no doubt that the preserved food they'd just eaten wouldn't be nearly enough.

"Byune, why don't you go hunt something? There's still plenty of time before bed..."

Vihera started to continue, but suddenly noticed an approaching presence and turned her gaze that way.

Though the look in her eyes wasn't one of caution, but of joy.

A moment later, Byune also turned her gaze in the same direction as Vihera.

Further behind them, those within the group who were sensitive to such things noticed the approaching presence.

That was largely because the approaching figure... no, figures, weren't hiding their presence.

"Better late than never... but it seems we ate a bit too early. If we'd waited just a little longer, we could have had some delicious food."

As Vihera murmured this, a sound like something heavy falling echoed around them.

That sound finally alerted even those who couldn't perceive subtle signs to the disturbance. They frantically scanned their surroundings.

But while it might have been possible during the day, the sun was already more than halfway down. It was now that time between evening and night... the hour known as twilight.

Because of this, few could discern Set's presence in the sky. Only when they saw him descend to the ground, crying out, did most finally realize it was Set... and that Rei and the others had returned.

And from those who noticed... especially those who found solace in Set, voices of joy overflowed.
