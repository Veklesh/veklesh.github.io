The flames extending from the Death Scythe gripped by Rei slowly flowed into the bodies of Elena and Ara standing before him.

In the next instant, the magic known as the 'Seed of Restraint' within Elena and Ara vanished completely. Only what could be called divine protection remained within them.

"...Somehow, it feels refreshing, yet like a hole has been punched in my chest. Such a complicated feeling."

Elena murmured, placing her hand upon her own ample bosom.

True to her words, Elena felt as if Rei, who had been with her just moments ago, had vanished.

Ara, standing beside Elena, tilted her head at her master's words.

"Is that so? I don't feel anything particularly different, though."

"Hmm. So then, is this something only I feel?"

Elena tilted her head slightly at Ara's words, yet the next moment, a satisfied smile spread across her face.

For Elena, being with Rei had never been a bad thing.

"Don't give me that pleased look for reasons other than what I think. You came to Gilm this time specifically to remove the 'Seed of Admonition,' right?"

The 'Seed of Restraint' was a magic that forbade anyone from speaking of Set's peculiarity.

A vicious spell that demanded payment with one's life if the vow made before using magic was broken.

However, during last year's civil war in the Bestir Empire, Rei had proven beyond any doubt that Set was a rare species.

Given that, there was no longer any need to use the 'Seed of Admonition' to force Elena and the others to keep his secret.

Instead, they used the Paired Orbs to communicate, and Elena and Ara came to Gilm to lift the 'Seed of Oath'.

However, shortly after arriving in Gilm, Elena got caught up in the Zoboz incident. Even after that concluded, there were tasks to handle, starting with dismantling the Cyclops, leaving no time to settle down.

Amidst all this, they ended up riding together in a carriage toward the Dark Elf Forest. Taking advantage of the time between setting up camp, they decided this was the perfect moment to lift the 'Seed of Admonition'.

"We should head back soon. We can't just leave all the work to Vihera and Marina, can we?"

Elena's carriage pushed its performance to the limit, covering significantly more distance than a regular carriage.

Even so, there was no way we could cover a journey that would take about ten days one way in a regular carriage in just a single day. Now that the sun was setting, we were preparing camp.

Since he'd slipped away for a bit, lingering too long here might earn him a scolding. Rei headed toward the spot where Elena and Ara were setting up camp.

"Oh? Already done? Didn't you have something important to do?"

Seeing Rei and the others return, Marina wore a slightly surprised expression.

She had said it was an important matter, so she had assumed it would take much longer.

But seeing them return so quickly must have made her question it.

"Yeah, that's all good now. More importantly, is the camp setup ready?"

"As you can see."

Marina's gaze fell on nothing but the campfire.

Normally, they would have to cut the grass growing on the ground for resting, prepare food, and set up sleeping bags and tents.

But this group didn't need to bother with such troublesome tasks.

Elena and the others slept inside the magic carriage, so no special preparations were needed. Rei also had his magic tent, eliminating the need to clear a spot.

As for meals, since Rei's Misty Ring contained an endless supply of food, they didn't need to go hunting for prey.

As for keeping watch, with Set the griffin present, there was absolutely no need for concern.

If anything needed doing, it would be caring for the horses pulling the carriage.

They needed to provide water and feed, wipe away sweat, and such.

"It's been a while since we left Gilm, but this is quite a comfortable journey, isn't it?"

Marina murmured, watching Ara go straight to tend to the horses upon their return.

In truth, not just in Gilm but across all of Ergin, the number of people traveling more comfortably than Rei and his companions was surprisingly small.

That alone speaks to the special nature of Rei's Misty Ring and the various magic items stored within it. And Elena's carriage is undoubtedly exceptional too.

"Still, we've covered quite a distance just today. Compared to a regular carriage, it was overwhelmingly fast. At this rate, we might arrive much sooner than seven days, don't you think?"

"I wonder. We're in the frontier now, so there aren't many bandits around, but if we keep going like this, bandits will definitely attack us. ...I hate to say it myself, but this group stands out far too much in every sense."

Vihera, who had been looking toward Rei sitting next to Marina, shrugged her shoulders slightly as she said this.

Her twin peaks swayed slightly at the movement, though she seemed utterly unconcerned.

"It's a bit pathetic that we have to be wary of bandits more than monsters, though."

"Heh heh, true. But since you're known as 'Bandit Eater,' Rei, wouldn't you welcome a bandit attack?"

Marina said with an amused smile, causing Rei to show a slight look of surprise.

He knew, of course, that thieves feared and despised him, calling him the Bandit Eater, but he had always assumed that reputation was confined solely to the thieves' underworld.

"How did you know that name?"

"Being a Guildmaster, I hear all sorts of things. How about it? Why not change your alias from Crimson to Bandit Eater? That might make the thieves behave themselves a bit better."

"Nah, nicknames aren't something you can just decide for yourself. They're something that gets given to you naturally."

Muttering, Rei's gaze turned toward the sky.

There was a sunset, a scene that somehow reminded him of his nickname, Rei Crimson.

Amidst the sunset dyeing the surroundings crimson, Rei's gaze caught sight of Set and Yellow flying through the sky.

Not a chase on the ground, but a three-dimensional chase through the air.

"...They're playing a rather interesting game."

At Rei's words, everyone except Ara, who was tending to the horses, looked up.

Among them was Audban, who harbored a sense of awe toward Rei, though fortunately, he wasn't showing it outwardly just yet.

"Yellow must be happy to have a companion who can fly too. Though for Set, it might be a bit unsatisfying..."

Elena's tender smile seemed far removed from her usual image as a goddess of war, appearing almost maternal.

Noticing Rei's steady gaze fixed on her, Elena suddenly turned her eyes toward him.

Neither spoke a word; they simply gazed at each other in silence.

A faintly bittersweet atmosphere hung in the air...

"Hm!"

Suddenly, Byune broke the silence.

Seeing her stroking her belly, it was immediately obvious what Byune wanted to say.

Elena and Rei both smiled slightly at each other before speaking.

"Shall we have dinner soon? Even if you're not as hungry as Byune, Rei, aren't you feeling peckish?"

"I won't deny that. ...Ara, are you almost done over there?"

When Rei called out to Ara, who was tending to the horses, a voice came back immediately.

"Ah, yes! I'll be done shortly, so please go ahead and eat!"

Even though Ara held the position of Captain of Elena's Guard Knights, she was originally a proper noble lady. However, looking at Ara now, not many would think she was a noble lady.

With Ara's words, Rei pulled various dishes out of the Misty Ring.

Freshly baked white bread, its fragrant aroma wafting around, crispy on the outside with a soft, fluffy interior that was still moist.

A hearty stew packed with vegetables and meat, simmered until the ingredients were fully tender, with additional toppings added for enjoyment.

Sautéed oak meat, where a sweet and sour fruit sauce perfectly complemented the rich flavor of the well-seared oak meat.

A salad mixing vegetables, cheese, and nuts with a special dressing.

Several other dishes emerged one after another from Rei's Misty Ring and were laid out.

The aroma of dishes that certainly wouldn't be eaten at a campsite spread throughout the area.

No, not just camping—even in town, this was a luxurious meal you wouldn't normally get.

"...My, this is quite extravagant. Lunch was just sandwiches and soup, you know."

Surprised by the dishes, Vihera asked with wide eyes.

"Well, it's the first day, so I splurged. Starting tomorrow, the quality might drop a bit."

"Gururururu!"

"Kyuu! Kyuuu!"

The scent of the food drew Set and Yellow, who had been flying around chasing each other, down to the ground.

Seeing them growl with excitement to eat, everyone present, including Rei, smiled.

Even Byune, usually expressionless, had a faint curve at the corners of her mouth. You can imagine what Set and Yellow looked like.

"Sorry to keep you waiting. ...This is quite a luxurious meal, isn't it?"

Once Ara, who had finished tending to the horses, arrived, dinner began with everyone present.

Every dish was incredibly delicious, and everyone ate with smiles on their faces.

Set and Yellow also enjoyed the food, their throats making satisfying sounds.

Once dinner time ended, all that remained was sleep.

However, having just finished eating, drowsiness hadn't yet set in, so they used the time until they felt sleepy as free time.

"Haahhhhh!"

"Too easy!"

Mirage transformed into a whip-like form, pointing its tip toward Vihera.

But Vihera, as if reading the attack, shifted her body to evade it and moved forward.

Seeing Vihera close in and clench her fist, Elena flashed a faint smile and flipped Mirage's handle with her wrist.

Then, the tip of the Mirage, which had been behind Vihera, changed direction and lunged at Vihera from behind.

He must have sensed it from the sound approaching from behind. Without even glancing back, Vihera merely shifted his forward trajectory slightly... At that very instant, the Mirage's blade sliced past the spot where Vihera had been standing just moments before.

"Well, you noticed. I thought that one would hit."

"Anyone could sense something like that."

"No, it's impossible."

Watching the two fight under the moonlight, Ara opened her mouth, as if unable to help herself.

If attacked from behind like Vihera just now, she was certain she would be struck.

An attack from behind... especially one from the Magic Sword Mirage. That strike would be fatal.

This was a mock battle, so neither was fighting at full strength, but even so, it was the kind of exchange that could easily result in injury under normal circumstances.

"Ara, aren't you participating in the mock battle?"

A voice suddenly addressed Ara, who had been watching Elena and Vihera's fight.

Turning her gaze toward the source of the voice, Ara saw Audban standing there.

"No. I have no plans to do so at this time."

"That's a shame. I would have liked to fight you myself."

A chill ran down Ara's spine for just an instant at the words that came from Audban's mouth.

She didn't think she would lose in battle, yet she felt that chill nonetheless.

It was a sensation born from instinctively sensing Audban's taste for cute or beautiful people. Fortunately, or perhaps not, before Ara could fully grasp the nature of that feeling, the battle between Elena and Vihera shifted.

"Let's go."

Vihera was the first to move.

She walked while constantly shifting her body's center of gravity, yet maintained a steady pace.

It was a gait that, even though she was walking slowly, made it feel as if Vihera's body was swaying.

It was a gait that could make an attack seem to pass right through her body if struck carelessly. Seeing this, Elena flashed a defiant smile and readied her Mirage.

"Come on. I'll take it head-on."

The moment those words left her lips, Vihera advanced, and Elena swung her Mirage... To an outside observer, it was a fierce battle, so intense it was hard to tell if they even understood this was supposed to be a mock fight.

"...Hmph."

Watching this battle from a short distance away, Byune stroked Set and Yellow while casting a somewhat exasperated glance their way.

Practice battles were fine, but this was getting way too serious.
