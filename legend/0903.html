In the darkness of night, a group walked through the noble district.

At the head of the group was Keep, spear in hand, clad in metal armor.

The spear was heavy, but the weight of the metal armor he wore for his own safety and for appearances was already leaving Keep breathless.

Behind him followed three knights, their expressions tinged with unease.

Is it truly right for this group to attack the Baron Sysne household and kill every living soul there? They felt a slight unease about this, yet the thought that their lord was with them was stronger. They believed the Earl of Eriell household would protect them no matter what.

Moreover, Keep insisted that if anything happened, the power of the Count of Eliell House would sort it out, so they couldn't deny feeling a certain anticipation for the looting, violence, and humiliation that lay ahead.

Following behind the knights were the men gathered by Fultos.

If one believed Fultos' words, they were supposed to be elite, but from an outsider's perspective, it was hard to see it that way.

In truth, most were brought from the slums or criminal organizations. While they likely possessed some skill, they were no match for Zeros, the Rank B adventurer who had dueled with Rei.

Yet, Keep was utterly convinced that these men possessed skills equal to Zeros'.

This was because of his trust in Fultos.

The knights, however, hadn't heard Fultos and Keep's conversation, so they simply regarded them as numbers to fill the ranks.

The three knights serving as Keep's bodyguards and aides, while despicable in character, were reasonably skilled.

...Though it was only reasonably so, meaning they could barely hold their own against Rank D adventurers.

Even from the perspective of such knights, the men following behind them seemed unlikely to include anyone skilled, save for a few exceptions.

"Heh heh heh. We get to raid a noble's mansion, huh? We're lucky bastards, getting to take part in a job like this."

"Hah, you're right. Those bastards are living it up off the money they've fleeced from us. Let's make them regret it big time."

"Hey, hey, what kind of women do you think are there? They're nobles, after all. They're probably nothing like the whores we usually buy, right?"

"You bet. They're nobility, after all. Top-notch skin and hair, probably smell amazing when you hold them."

"I care more about the money than the women."

"Screw the money. Show us their noble blood. Some of these guys brag about having blue blood running through their veins, right? This is the perfect chance to see with our own eyes if it's true."

"You idiot. There's no such thing as blue blood. The blood of that noble I killed on the battlefield was red, just like ours."

"Heh heh heh. See? Turns out nobles aren't so different from us after all."

"...But, but... If we raid a noble's mansion, won't we get the death penalty if we get caught?"

"You idiot. Yeah, if we get caught, we might get the death penalty. But that just means as long as we don't get caught, it's no problem, right?"

"Oh, oh. Right. You're smart, bro."

The men Fultos had prepared followed Keep, each chatting freely among themselves.

To these men, Keep himself was indeed a noble... though Keep himself was completely unaware of it.

And with such a group strutting boldly through the noble district, it was inevitable they'd be spotted by the adventurers hired as guards...

"Hey, you lot! Where do you think you're going, all gathered up like that!"

Unfortunately... or perhaps fortunately, they were stopped by a security adventurer approaching from ahead, not far from the Baroness Sisne's residence.

Those behind Keep briefly wondered if this was dangerous, but Keep himself, completely oblivious to the commotion behind him, glared at them sourly.

He already harbored hatred toward adventurers after the daytime duel, so being addressed in such a commanding tone was hardly amusing.

"What do you want? I'm Kip, heir to the Earl of Eriell. Adventurers like you shouldn't be addressing me so casually."

Answering with obvious displeasure, Keep turned his gaze toward the adventurers who had addressed him.

There stood three adventurers.

All young men, barely twenty if at all.

The one at the front, a man with a scabbard holding a longsword hanging at his waist, answered Keep's words.

"The next head of the Earl's household, were you? Our apologies. However, we too are hired as guards to protect the peace of this Noble Quarter, so please forgive us. ...So then, bringing this many people along, and from what we can see, even you, Kip, appear to be armed. Might you tell us what possible reason you have for such behavior?"

"Huh? Oh, you want to know? ...You want to see this spear in particular?"

The words that came out of Keep's mouth seemed like an answer, yet weren't quite one.

Seeing that he seemed to understand yet didn't, the three adventurers felt a faint sense of unease. They adjusted their stances, ready to react to anything, though discreetly so he wouldn't notice.

"Here, take a look."

They turned their gaze toward the spear thrust forward without hesitation... and in the next instant, the spearhead thrust further into the body of the man who had been speaking.

"Whoa!"

The man reacted only because he'd been prepared, but even so, he couldn't block the knight's attack, which closed in as if synchronized with the spear's thrust.

He was struck before he could draw his longsword from its sheath, knocked flying, and crashed into his comrades.

His comrades, now unable to move, suddenly found themselves surrounded by a large number of enemies.

And so began the beating.

The men's luck lay in the fact that the group led by Keep was more interested in plunder and women than violence.

They decided it was better to pursue the larger prize, the House of Baron Sysne, than to continue tormenting the three adventurers here. They left the unconscious adventurers behind and moved on.

Given the temperature where snow occasionally flurried, they might have frozen to death if left there... Fortunately, shortly after Keep and his group departed, another adventurer happened by and they received treatment.

"Ha! Look at that. In the end, no matter what they call themselves, adventurers are nothing special. We're strong. We can handle adventurers like that with ease."

Leading the group, Keep declared, swinging his spear.

It was a victory achieved through sheer numbers, but a victory nonetheless.

Not just Keep, but the knight and the men following behind him cheered in agreement.

It was the Noble Quarter, and though night had fallen, it wasn't yet the dead of night.

With so many still awake, making this much noise while marching down the street, it was only natural that some noticed Keep and his group.

Particularly the noble guards manning the gates, upon seeing such a group, immediately reported it to their superiors.

Of course, Keep harbored nothing but hatred for the Sysne Baronial House, who had inflicted the ultimate humiliation upon him, so he had no intention whatsoever of attacking any other noble house.

Those accompanying Keep also knew that since their intended target was already prepared, there was no point in causing unnecessary trouble here.

And so, as if to steel themselves, Keep and his group continued walking through the noble district at night...

"There it is."

Keep smiled at the familiar mansion.

It wasn't a particularly large mansion, though. Keep and the knights were fine with it, but the men behind them were somewhat disappointed.

Still, being an aristocratic residence, they were certain it would satisfy their desires...

"Well now, a large group arriving like this in the night... I hadn't heard any guests were coming."

The sudden voice reached their ears.

The first to halt his steps upon hearing it was Keep. Probably because he'd seen the owner of that voice speak many times before.

Simultaneously, the three knights near Keep also halted.

If the four leading the way stopped, those following behind them naturally had no choice but to halt as well.

Once everyone had stopped, the figure stepped forward in front of Keep.

Clouds had been obscuring the moon shining in the night sky, but the moment the figure stepped before Keep, the clouds were swept away by the wind, and moonlight illuminated the ground.

There stood a small figure cloaked in robes.

Not only from the voice, but also from the figure itself, Kip realized who stood before him. The excitement he had felt just minutes ago seemed to vanish completely. His voice raspy, he murmured the name.

「Rei」

"Who the hell are you?!"

As Keep spoke, one of the men behind him shouted.

The man's voice was loud, Keep's was soft.

As a result, Keep's voice was completely drowned out. The three knights and Keep were the only ones who understood who stood before them, glaring with hostile expressions.

It was only natural. They were about to raid a noble's mansion and indulge their desires to the fullest, yet these men stood in their way as if to obstruct them.

Furthermore, the fact that they had defeated the three adventurers guarding the noble district on their way here likely fueled the men's arrogance.

Above all, the fact that Rei wasn't carrying his massive scythe, Death Scythe, his symbol, nor his partner, Set, meant the men didn't recognize him and underestimated him based on appearance alone.

Had he lowered his hood, they might at least have seen his face. But now, with the hood pulled down, his features were almost entirely hidden.

In other words, the men perceived the figure blocking their path as merely a small boy, or perhaps a girl.

"Even if you ask 'Who?'... Well, perhaps it's easiest to say I'm a guard for the Baron Sysne household?"

"...What?"

The man's expression froze for a moment, caught off guard by the word "bodyguard" coming from Rei's mouth.

But the next moment, he burst out laughing, unable to hold it in.

And it wasn't just him. The laughter spread among the other men surrounding them.

It was only natural. To them, this person who looked like nothing more than a child was claiming to be their escort, sent to stop them.

They assumed Keep's trembling body was simply from trying to stifle his laughter.

In reality, he was trembling with fear, recalling the scene he'd witnessed that afternoon... when Rei had activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, enveloping himself in compressed red magic so intense it became visible, and the sheer heat it generated.

That sight, witnessed up close, had carved deep claw marks into Keep's heart.

And Keep wasn't the only one trembling. The three knights were shaking as well.

Ignoring the four of them, Rei and the man's conversation continued.

"Listen, kid. I'm not trying to be mean, but you should go home, crawl into bed, and sleep. What's about to happen is way too intense for you."

"Whoa, hold on a sec. If that guy's the Baron Sisne's bodyguard, he's obviously our enemy, right? There's no way we're just letting a guy like that get away. Seeing a cocky brat like this cry and scream is the best kind of fun, you know?"

"Ha! Nice taste you've got there. But what if the real target slips away while we're wasting time on this brat? Even if our boss somehow covers up this attack, if the target escapes and runs to the guard post, it won't be funny at all."

"...Hmph, fine. But I'm handling this brat myself! No complaints, got it?"

The man who had been about to torment Rei probably didn't want to miss out on the noble's mansion raid either. Saying that, he raised the club he held and approached Rei.

Rei, who had been silently watching this, gave the man standing before him a somewhat exasperated look.

But that look was unforgivable to the man. What he wanted to see was a look begging for mercy.

"Hey, you little brat! You think you're safe now?! Take this!"

The man raised his club menacingly, but of course, Rei showed no sign of fear in his eyes...

"Sigh. ...Why are fools always so..."

He muttered softly, and that voice only fueled the man's rage further, causing him to swing the raised club down toward Rei.

That he didn't aim for the head wasn't out of mercy... but purely because he wanted to see the pain-twisted expression on Rei's face.

That's precisely why he hadn't expected the descending club to be casually stopped with one hand.

"What?!"

"Hey, you're allowed to be surprised... right?!"

Holding the club steady with his left hand, he lightly kicked the man with his right foot.

He flew several meters, tumbling across the ground before finally coming to a stop. Gritting his teeth against the pain, he struggled to his feet and turned his gaze toward Rei, trying to comprehend what had just happened.

"So, what are you going to do with me? Care to repeat that?"

Dropping the club he'd been gripping in his left hand, he drew the massive scythe from the Misty Ring—the Death Scythe, also Rei's symbol—and asked.

Surrounding the men further, Fron, Brass, Sergio, Johanna, Deitz, and the other adventurers Fron had gathered, along with the former members of the mobile squad, appeared.
