The men who had sneaked into the royal castle in Rossi, stolen a carriage from there... and then successfully taken horses from the cavalry squad that had come in pursuit, continued onward.

Normally, it would have been quickest to simply hitch horses to the carriage right there and have the slower walkers ride it. However, the task of hitching horses to the carriage, selecting someone to drive it, and above all, deciding who would ride in the carriage would take time. So, Rei and the others left the spot as it was.

Unless something extraordinary happened, humans generally wanted to take the easy way out. Most people were the type who wouldn't pass up an opportunity for comfort if it presented itself.

Given that, selecting someone to ride in the carriage right then and there would undoubtedly cause a commotion.

The exchange with the woman who had first complained to Rei about being tired seemed to have spread somewhat, but even so, selecting someone to ride in the carriage now would likely result in many others demanding the same.

Of course, as far as Rei could tell, quite a few people were tired from walking for several hours straight, so it was only natural they'd want to ride the carriage.

Adventurers and merchants, for instance, often had experience walking for hours, half a day... sometimes even through the night.

But the vast majority of people here had likely never even left the place they lived.

Given that, it's only natural they aren't accustomed to the act of walking.

"Rei, we're reaching our limit. If we're walking again tomorrow, we should rest soon."

Rei nodded at the words of Vihera, who was walking near him.

"Yeah, you're right. I'd have preferred to get a bit further away from Rossi..."

Saying this, Rei called out to Ryutas walking nearby.

"Ryutas, what do you think about the pursuers from Jaya? Do you think they're heading this way?"

"Hard to say. That many people broke out from Mejogo, so they definitely haven't lost track of us. It wouldn't be surprising if survivors from that battle reported the Mejogo incident too. But considering whether Jaya has the resources for that right now..."

Ryutas paused there, shaking his head.

"Today... no, time-wise, yesterday? The damage Jaya suffered in yesterday's chaos is no laughing matter. All the prostitutes in Mejogo have regained their senses, and most escaped like this. The Giant's Nest was attacked. And above all..."

Ryutas paused again, aware that not only Rei and Vihera but also others listening to his words were now focusing their gaze on him. He continued.

"The fact that every giant in the cave was killed is a devastating blow to Jaya. Their entire main force has been wiped out."

Intelligence aside, purely in terms of physical strength—from an ordinary person's perspective, at least—they were incredibly powerful giants.

The loss of every single one of the over a thousand giants must feel like all the combat strength Jaya had painstakingly built up over time suddenly vanished.

(Is it like... the feeling of having saved tens of thousands of yen each month since becoming a working adult, only to have it suddenly stolen just before retirement?)

Of course, it's not exactly the same.

At the very least, the money earned through labor wasn't used for such atrocities as forcibly bringing women to make them prostitutes, or worse, devouring them as breeding stock for giants.

But for Rei, that was undoubtedly the sensation she felt.

"Given the scale of the damage, do you think Jaya considers it too dangerous to attack us?"

At Vihera's words, Ryutas nodded.

"I can't say for certain, but probably. ...The problem is that Jaya is a criminal organization. The damage they suffered this time is immense, but at the same time, it's as if Rei and the others thoroughly disgraced them, isn't it?"

"Meaning?"

"For criminal organizations... what you might call dark or underground organizations... face is far more important than ordinary people realize. After all, unlike regular organizations, violence is what backs them up."

"So if they've been humiliated... disrespected, the organization can't just let it go without retaliating?"

"Exactly. ...Though the question is whether Jaya still has that kind of fighting power left. Especially since you killed or injured nearly all the forces guarding the Giant's Nest, right? There's no doubt those gathered there were Jaya's elite. Of course, other executives might have personal forces too."

Hearing about forces held personally, Rei wore an annoyed expression.

Conversely, Vihera wore a faint look of anticipation, perhaps hoping they might finally face worthy opponents.

"I understand. Ideally, I'd prefer those guys not come after us until we're out of the Kingdom of Reburina. ...Alright, time to rest. We'll depart tomorrow morning, right?"

Hearing this, the women, who were nearing their physical limits, each breathed a sigh of relief.

They moved to a spot slightly off the main road, grouped the women into teams of ten, and distributed food and water.

Naturally, this still caused quite a commotion, but fortunately, perhaps due to their utter exhaustion, it settled more smoothly than Rei had anticipated.

Even when Rei announced they would be acting in these groups of ten from today onward, there were no complaints... Partly because making a fuss was too much trouble, but also because they understood that acting efficiently would give them more breathing room.

That said, since they were about to sleep, the rations were meager: one loaf of bread and one cup of water per person.

...But while the bread was one thing, those who took a sip of the water erupted in an uproar.

Not many knew the water came from the flowing dagger, or that its taste changed due to magic.

Of course, some hesitated to drink water from the dagger, but after taking a sip, no further complaints were heard.

It was truly heavenly nectar, the finest water imaginable.

It was undoubtedly just water. Undoubtedly so, yet the sensation of that water permeating their bodies was nothing short of pure pleasure, a delight worthy of such words.

It was water unlike any noble or royal had ever tasted.

Simply drinking it made bodies that had walked for hours feel instantly healed.

Exhausted as it should have been, it even felt like it could walk again.

(Did I jump the gun?)

On the very day they were freed from their slave collars, they undertook the act of walking for hours on end.

He gave the women and the Resistance fighters, who were naturally exhausted, some of the water from the flowing dagger, hoping it might restore their strength a little... but its effect was far more dramatic than Rei had anticipated.

Still, in terms of boosting morale, it could be said to have yielded the best possible result.

"Now, everyone sleep! We're leaving tomorrow morning!"

Even if he said sleep, there weren't enough tents for everyone, nor any cloth to use as blankets.

They would literally sleep on the grassland in whatever clothes they had on.

Fortunately, it was summer.

Even at night, the temperature never dropped below twenty degrees, making sleep possible.

If this had been a little later... closer to autumn, sleeping in clothing as thin as a prostitute's would have been impossible.

In that sense, it was undoubtedly fortunate that Rei and the others had come to the Kingdom of Reburina at this time of year.

"Sura, I think we'll be fine, but the Resistance members will take turns keeping watch. We'll send one person at a time from our side too."

"Understood. We're still not that far from Rossi, so we should keep a close watch. I'll leave it to Sharia. ...I don't think we'll need to worry about reinforcements from Rossi for a few more days."

Sura had been told by Rei that no more soldiers would pursue them, but since it wasn't absolute, she couldn't feel completely at ease.

"Do that. Soldiers aside, goblins or bandits showing up could be a real hassle."

What Rei found particularly troublesome were the bandits.

Unlike most monsters, they couldn't sense Set's presence... which meant they might just come right up to them.

To thieves, this group was like sitting ducks... no, it was like they'd brought the pot, the fire, and all the other ingredients along with them.

There were a large number of beautiful women.

Capturing even a few of them would be a huge profit.

"True. Soldiers aside, bandits will only become more troublesome the further we advance."

"...Come to think of it, Jaya was actually pretty useful, huh?"

Thanks to the organization known as Jaya, moving through the Kingdom of Reburina was relatively safe from bandit attacks.

Of course, instead of bandits attacking, there's the possibility of armies attacking, or even the far larger-scale Jaya itself attacking.

"Right. Anyway, that's the situation with the lookouts. Honestly, I'd like to hire adventurer escorts sooner rather than later..."

Rei understood what Sura was thinking as she hesitated.

Adventurers, by nature, tend to be rough and tumble types.

Of course, some are worthy of being called gentlemen, but looking at adventurers as a whole, it's only natural that the rowdy ones are in the majority.

Some adventurers even operate as thieves most of the time.

Adventurers gathering around places like Gilm tend to be fewer in number. ...Fewer, but not entirely absent – whether that's truly adventurer-like is debatable.

In any case, since the majority of this group were women—and specifically the type often called beautiful or cute—hiring a rough adventurer as a guard was guaranteed to cause trouble.

To prevent that, they had to either hire the few female adventurers available, somehow find and hire gentlemanly adventurers... or, if a rough adventurer tried to cause trouble, show them their strength and make them think twice about doing anything foolish.

The quickest solution was undoubtedly to demonstrate their strength.

That is, if it was possible.

(Sura and the others... Ah, Sharia is there.)

The beastman named Sharia that Rei met in Mejogo was now part of the Resistance.

And not just any member—she was the commander leading the beastman unit.

She herself seemed to possess considerable strength, and was now one of the resistance's most formidable combatants.

From what Rei observed, someone with Sharia's level of skill should be able to defeat most adventurers... or so he thought.

Of course, adventurers' abilities varied greatly depending on the person and their rank.

Among Rei's companions, the only one Sharia could likely defeat was probably Byune.

That said, that fight would be under the condition of a fair, head-on confrontation.

If the fight took place in a town or forest, it would be advantageous for Sharia, being a beastman, but Rei's assessment was that Byune, a thief specialized in combat, would still come out on top.

"Well, we can't stay with her forever. We'll probably head to Gilm ahead of her soon. Of course, that doesn't mean we'll split up and never come back—we plan to return every few days."

"In that sense, getting the carriage and the horses to pull it was probably a good thing, huh?"

"Yeah."

Even though Rei's Misty Ring contained a large amount of food, once it was taken out, long-term preservation was impossible given the summer season.

Fresh ingredients would last about three days. But since we can't come back often, we'll probably have to rely on preserved foods like dried meat and hardtack as our main meals.

(Returning about once a week would be ideal. There's also the expansion work at Gilm... The problem is how far the envoy sent from Gilm has progressed.)

Since he needed to leave Ryutas behind, Rei had to stay with this group until they met up with the envoy.

"Well, there's no point in talking forever. I suppose it's time to sleep."

"Oh, really? Well then, I'll take my leave around here too."

With that, Sura waved lightly and walked away... Rei, seemingly unfazed, pulled a magic tent from the Misty Ring.

The next instant, it suddenly expanded, illuminated by the moonlight.

Those farther away seemed unable to grasp what had happened, but those near Rei were another story.

In fact, far from it—Sura, who should have been leaving, was running straight toward Rei.
