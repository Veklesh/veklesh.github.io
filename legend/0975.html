It wasn't by chance that Vihera, Byune, and Dietz arrived at that location.

They had originally been riding their horses into the forest, and Dietz had anticipated the direction Mireine and Johanna might have taken, heading in that direction.

Whether it was fortunate or not, Dietz's guess wasn't particularly wrong, as proven by the sounds they heard while dodging the forest trees on horseback.

"Looks like they're already in a fight. I don't know if they were spotted by a Cyclops or if they saw an opportunity and attacked... No, wait, could it be Rei and Set?"

"Hmph!"

Vihera murmured, and Byune protested softly.

Unable to ride a horse herself, Byune sat forward, almost as if being held by Vihera.

Hearing Byune's adorable protest, Vihera smiled faintly and spoke.

"Don't worry. It's not like we're just going to leave it be. Besides... I've fought them a few times, and Cyclops make for quite the worthy opponents. After all, that massive body and high recovery rate are just perfect. We can attack them to our heart's content."

Vihera declared this with a smile that seemed to crave battle, utterly unlike that of a former princess.

Dietz, riding behind her, was overcome by a strange feeling—partly wanting to see the expression on Vihera's face, partly afraid to.

But before that feeling could show on his face, Vihera spoke.

"Well then, sorry, but I'm going ahead. Take care of the horse."

With that, she leapt off the galloping horse, still holding Byune in her arms.

Releasing the reins of the spare horse he'd brought along, Vihera landed on the ground without any sign of trouble.

Ignoring Dietz, who was scrambling to retrieve the horse Vihera had ridden, she stroked Byune's head and spoke.

"Alright, Byune. I'm heading to the battlefield, so you handle the support, okay?"

"Mm."

Seeing Byune nod, Vihera continued forward, weaving through the trees as if they were part of the landscape.

She emerged into what looked like a plaza.

Two Cyclopes were present there.

But upon seeing them, Vihera frowned slightly.

According to what Dietz had said, there should have been five Cyclopes, yet only two stood before them.

Moreover, one of them had its single eye slashed open—whether by a longsword or a spear was unclear—rendering it blind and causing it to rampage wildly, attacking anything within reach.

For a moment, he wondered if the remaining three had already been defeated, but no corpses lay scattered on the ground.

Only the corpses of horses lay scattered on the ground; not even a shred of flesh remained from the Cyclops' bodies.

If Rei was fighting, it was possible they had been burned to ashes by fire magic or the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour. But the figure in his line of sight didn't seem capable of such a feat.

(There is a mage here, but expecting them to have the same skill level as Rei would be a mistake.)

Seeing the wizard, who was considerably older than himself, he muttered inwardly.

To begin with, finding a mage capable of matching Rei's power was already... no, the conditions were extremely, no, absurdly strict.

(If that's the case, the logical conclusion is they dispersed the Cyclops. Cyclops typically operate alone anyway, so that makes sense. Then, deal with the two wounded ones quickly and focus on the remaining three...)

As Vihera pondered this, the situation unfolded before her.

A roar echoed from behind the mage.

Judging that the remaining Cyclops had arrived, Vihera kicked off the ground and closed in behind the Cyclops Mereine and Johanna were fighting. She swung her gauntleted fist, aiming for the back of its right knee.

That said, the blow itself wasn't particularly powerful.

It was only a blow with the force of being pushed from behind at the knee... but such a blow was enough to throw the Cyclops off balance.

As the Cyclops's right leg suddenly buckled against its will, forcing it to drop to its haunches, Vihera touched its back and unleashed a strike imbued with magic.

"Hmph!"

With a short battle cry, Vihera unleashed the Demon-Infused Palm from his hand. The shockwave generated by magic slammed directly into the Cyclops' body, destroying vital organs including its heart.

Even a Cyclops with its high regenerative abilities couldn't survive having its heart and internal organs utterly destroyed... It died instantly, blood pouring from its eyes, ears, nose, and mouth.

"Lady Vihera!?"

Startled by the Cyclops suddenly collapsing to the ground, blood pouring from its entire face, Johanna screamed upon seeing the face of the person standing behind it.

"You are Johanna, aren't you? It's been a while... though it seems we don't have time for pleasantries."

Vihera muttered, shifting her gaze toward Surnin... or more precisely, behind him.

Mireine and Surnin hurriedly turned their gaze that way too, just as five Cyclopes appeared.

Seeing the five Cyclopes, nearly unscathed despite minor wounds from Ekril's arrows that were already healing, Mireine and Surnin's faces stiffened.

Johanna, however, unlike the other two, showed little sign of such strong unease on her face.

She knew firsthand, from seeing it with her own eyes, just how powerful Vihera was.

Meeting Johanna's expectant gaze, Vihera flashed a smile so melting it could captivate any man... or even a woman, if she played her cards right. She opened her mouth.

"I heard there were five Cyclopes... but never mind. I'll handle the five coming this way. You lot take care of the wounded one over there."

With just that, she kicked off the ground and charged toward the five Cyclops that had finally emerged from the forest.

Watching her retreating figure, Mireine shot a questioning look at Johanna, who seemed to know this woman.

Could this woman who appeared out of nowhere truly possess the strength to handle five Cyclopes?

Johanna, meeting that gaze, nodded with absolute confidence. She dismissed the Cyclops already closing in from behind as insignificant, shifting her focus instead to the one whose single eye had been injured and was still rampaging indiscriminately.

The enemy they must defeat was that wounded cyclops.

Of course, wounded didn't mean it would be easy to defeat.

After all, they were facing a wounded Rank C monster.

If Ekiru were here, things might be different, but relying solely on Surnin's magic for long-range support fire was far from reassuring.

(Ekril, if you're safe, you should be coming back soon.)

Mireine gripped the hilt of her longsword tightly, her mind filled with worry for Ekril.

Since the enemy couldn't see, it was obvious that no matter how much strength it possessed, the accuracy of its attacks would be drastically reduced.

The only danger was a random, unprovoked strike.

Johanna must have reached the same conclusion. Their eyes met, and together they charged toward the rampaging Cyclops.

(Looks like we've got that one covered.)

Vihera watched their retreating figures.

It wasn't just a glance; it was a quick look while dodging the concentrated attacks of the five Cyclops.

...Though even with five attacking together, only two, maybe three at most, could strike simultaneously.

Occasionally, a cyclops left behind would fire a shockwave of magic from its single eye, but Vihera dodged every single one. Worse, she even used the cyclops trying to attack her as a shield.

Fortunately for Vihera, the cyclopes' massive size meant they required a certain amount of space to attack effectively. This worked to his advantage, but proved disastrous for the cyclopes themselves.

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHH!"

The club swung with a roar struck the arm of another nearby Cyclops before swinging toward Vihera.

"Gyaaah!"

The struck cyclops screamed, but naturally, no one heard it.

Vihera leapt backward with a soft flutter.

Her thin robe fluttered as she moved, further distracting the Cyclopes.

...This was the primary reason why the five Cyclopes ignored Mireine and the others, focusing their attacks solely on Vihera.

Normally, there was no need for all five to deliberately focus their attacks on Vihera.

Only about three could attack simultaneously, so the other two would naturally be unable to attack and could have idly shifted their attention toward Mireine and the others.

But Vihera's clothing, thin as a veil, fluttered provocatively in the air, taunting the Cyclopes.

Beyond the provocation, the faint outline of a supple body visible beneath that sheer fabric meant nothing to the starving Cyclopes. To them, Vihera was nothing but a feast dangled before their eyes.

They focused solely on devouring that feast themselves, completely forgetting their surroundings.

No, worse still, the Cyclops struck by the horizontal swing of a club moments ago now brought his own club down on the Cyclops who had dodged the blow and lost his balance.

"Gyaaah!"

The blow to the head didn't kill the Cyclops outright, but it still inflicted significant damage.

The scream from their comrade caused the Cyclops' gaze to focus on the source of the sound for just an instant...

"Fools. Taking your eyes off me."

That single phrase came from right beside the cyclops who had been struck on the head with a club and was now kneeling.

Vihera, who should have been far away just an instant before, now stood right beside the Cyclops, her hand touching its chest as it knelt in pain.

"Die!"

With a short cry, a strike from the Demon-Infused Palm was unleashed. The next instant, the Cyclops' life flame was extinguished without a trace, blood streaming from its face.

"Gah!?"

The other Cyclopes couldn't possibly comprehend what had just happened.

After all, their comrade had died from a mere touch by Vihera—a creature vastly smaller than themselves, one they had viewed as nothing more than prey, even if it was of the highest quality.

Had they possessed greater intelligence, they might have recognized Vihera's danger. Unfortunately, the Cyclops monster was never known for its sharp mind.

"Four left. ...Oh?"

The moment she murmured, the sound of wind cutting through the air reached her ears.

Simultaneously, one cyclops screamed, flung its club aside, and clutched its eye.

"Honestly, if you're going to help, you should be helping over there!"

The other Cyclopes stared blankly at the one who had suddenly screamed, unable to comprehend what had happened.

"Idiot."

"GAAAAAH!"

As one cyclops stood frozen, Vihera circled behind it, striking its knee to break its balance. From behind, she used her Immersion Palm to destroy its heart and internal organs.

A normal attack from Vihera wouldn't allow her to target the heart with the Demon-Infused Palm.

That's precisely why, just like the first cyclops she defeated, she needed to force it to the ground, even if only on one knee.

After defeating the second cyclops, Vihera immediately put distance between herself and the remaining three.

Having witnessed two of their comrades killed in an instant, the Cyclops now regarded Vihera with intense wariness.

Their gaze shifted from the predatory hunger for food to the hostile stare of an enemy, fixed firmly on Vihera.

"Byune, I don't need your backup. I'd rather fight them head-on anyway."

Vihera declared without breaking eye contact with the Cyclopes.

There was no response to her words, but she could imagine that attacks on the Cyclops would cease.

Those slender needles were also an ideal weapon for Byune, who was also a thief.

Especially in a forest battle like this, it was practically Byune's domain, given her small stature and ability to hide in various places.

Even against five Cyclopes, she might not be able to defeat them, but holding them back was certainly within her capabilities.

It proved, strangely enough, that the words spoken by Rei were true.

"Come on, then. You want to eat me, don't you? Well, show me what you've got!"

Vihera's words triggered the two remaining Cyclopes, whose single eyes hadn't been pierced by Byune's needles, to charge at her simultaneously.

It was also a coincidence that two Cyclopes died, one lost its sight, and even when the remaining two attacked Vihera together, they failed to hit their comrades.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

"Gyaaaah!"

The two let out war cries as they swung the clubs in their hands down toward Vihera.

The screams of the Cyclops clutching its single eye echoed, but no one heard them.

Diagonally right front, diagonally left front.

Facing enemies attacking simultaneously from both directions, Vihera's expression showed nothing but delight.

If anyone had seen those wet eyes now, man or woman, they would undoubtedly be mesmerized, as if their soul were being stolen.

Wearing that expression, Vihera kicked off the ground, closing the distance to the Cyclops.
