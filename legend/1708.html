As Rei and his party approached the dungeon, they passed several other adventurers along the way.

Some of the adventurers looked dissatisfied, while others wore happy smiles.

Considering the location of the dungeon, it was clear what that meant

(In other words, there were those who managed to secure the corpses of monsters that had fallen from the dungeon, and those who didn’t. Or rather, even among those who did, there’s quite a difference between goblins and orcs.)

As he walked along, pondering this, Vihera, who was walking beside Rei, gave him a look of exasperation.

For Vihera, who craves battles against powerful foes, obtaining materials and magic stones from the corpses of monsters she didn’t even defeat herself is, to be honest, not exactly a pleasant experience.

“So, we’re heading to the dungeon, then. I just hope the adventurers waiting down there don’t get in our way.”

Understanding Vihera’s mood, Rei changed the subject.

……Though, it’s not exactly fair to say he managed to change the subject.

“That’s true. From the perspective of the people waiting for monsters to fall, we’re probably not exactly the most entertaining opponents as we head into the dungeon.”

As she said this, Marina was wearing pants—a rare sight for her.

If the dungeon were on the ground, her usual party dress would have been fine.

But since the dungeon is on the wall, and to enter it we have to cling to Set’s legs to move,

If she wore her usual party dress in that situation, the people below would be able to see her underwear.

While she wouldn’t mind if Rei saw her, Marina had absolutely no intention of showing herself like that to any other man, so she reluctantly put on pants.

“Well, any adventurer knows who we are, and there probably aren’t any idiots who’d come bothering us…”

...probably won’t be around. Rei was about to say that, but when he saw several adventurers approaching them, he couldn’t bring himself to finish the sentence.

“Hey, you guys are the ones heading into the dungeon, right?”

“Yeah, that’s right. Is there a problem?”

It wasn’t Rei who replied to the men… it was Relieu.

Judging that Rei’s appearance alone would likely lead the men to underestimate him, Relue—who could exude an aura befitting his rank just by looking at them—stepped forward.

Seeing Relieu like that, the men stopped in their tracks, looking momentarily surprised... but the next instant, the man at the front spoke again.

“I see. Well then, good luck.”

“……Huh. I was sure you were going to tell us not to clear the dungeon.”

“Well, it’d be a lie to say I don’t feel that way.”

Though he said that, there was no trace of what a pity it was on his face.

The man turned his gaze toward the dungeon, which was visible even from where they stood, and continued speaking.

“But even I can see what will happen if the dungeon keeps growing like this. …Golz doesn’t have the resources to sustain itself as a labyrinth city.”

There are several labyrinth cities in the Kingdom of Mireana.

The fact that there are only a few is, of course, because it’s difficult to establish a labyrinth city.

At the very least, the man who spoke to Relieu understood that, given the abilities of the adventurers in Golz, it was impossible for the city to survive as a labyrinth city.

And since it’s impossible to sustain it as a labyrinth city, the dungeon must be cleared before it grows to that point.

For now, monsters like goblins are falling out of the dungeon and dying on their own, but there’s no guarantee that will continue.

In fact, yesterday a bird monster called a Yellowbird emerged from the dungeon.

Even after seeing that, the main reason some people still oppose clearing the dungeon is that, given the current situation, it’s an easy way to make money.

Naturally, there aren’t that many people who think that way, but there are certainly some.

Fortunately, the person who spoke to Relyu wasn’t one of those types.

“So, um… I just wanted to tell you to do your best.”

The man said this, looking a little embarrassed.

Seeing him like that, Relieu wondered if he’d said that just because she’d shown up, but judging by the man’s current demeanor, that didn’t seem to be the case.

“I see. I’ll gladly accept your words of encouragement. …So, we’re thinking of heading to the dungeon soon—is that okay with you?”

“Yeah, leave the dungeon to us.”

With that, the men who had spoken to him simply walked away.

“I was sure they were going to hassle us.”

Once the men were out of sight, Rei muttered.

Based on past experience, they usually got hassled in situations like this.

That’s why he’d assumed this time would be no different… but in the end, Rei’s prediction was way off the mark.

“Well, it’s only natural that some people think a dungeon is dangerous. There might be some who, like I mentioned earlier, are thinking of turning Golz into a labyrinth city… but considering Golz’s current state, I think they’re in the minority.”

Naturally, building a labyrinth city requires the combined efforts of many people.

Doing that in a backwater like Golz isn’t impossible… but it’s difficult.

(Well, since this is supposedly a territory ruled by a member of the Neutral Faction, if Gilm weren’t in the middle of an expansion project or something, maybe… just maybe, there might have been a chance.)

Even as I was thinking that, the cliff gradually drew nearer.

There were a few adventurers nearby, butchering monsters, and naturally, their gaze was drawn to Rei and the others the moment they saw them.

That said, with Set—who stands over three meters tall—in the picture, it’s impossible for them not to notice him, no matter how focused they are on butchering monsters.

If they were so focused on butchering that they neglected to stay alert to their surroundings, they’d have a hard time making it as adventurers.

That said, Rei and the others didn’t seem to mind the stares as they arrived right at the base of the cliff.

“Well then, it’s finally time to tackle the dungeon. You’re all ready, right?”

Everyone nodded at Rei’s words as he asked this while petting Set.

Among them, even if she didn’t show it on her face, the one who seemed particularly fired up was Byune.

For Byune, a thief, a dungeon is the place where she can truly show off her skills.

...That said, since Byune is a thief specialized in combat, she might not be well-suited for dungeon exploration.

That said, right now, Byune is the only one in Rei’s group who possesses the skills of a thief.

And even though she specializes in combat, that doesn’t mean her other abilities as a thief are any less impressive.

(In games, it’s common for other abilities to drop if you specialize in combat, but this is reality.)

Thinking that to himself, Rei watched as everyone made their preparations, then spoke to Set.

“Alright, first I’ll ride Set into the dungeon to check if it’s safe. Once I’ve confirmed that, grab onto Set and follow me in. …You’re okay with that, right?”

“If Rei, the party leader, says so, I don’t mind… but is that really okay? I always thought that in situations like this, it’s usually the hired hands like me who get stuck with that role first.”

“I can’t very well use an adventurer with a special title entrusted to me by Lord Daskar like that, can I? Besides…”

“And?”

Relyu, puzzled by Rei’s abrupt silence, asked, but Rei simply shook his head without offering an explanation.

“Nah, it’s nothing. It’s just that, even though I’ve been working with you, Relieu, and I know you’re someone I can trust, I still feel like I want to go in myself this time. It’s a dungeon no one’s ever been in before.”

Seeing the curiosity on Rei’s face, Relieu decided not to say anything more.

...No, it would be more accurate to say that Relieu simply couldn’t say anything.

After all, despite her protests, she couldn’t deny that she, too, was drawn to Rei’s words about a dungeon no one had ever entered.

“Alright, Set, I’m counting on you.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a cry, then ran off with Rei still on his back, flapping his wings.

It was the very definition of “soaring through the sky,” and in the blink of an eye, Set arrived at the dungeon’s entrance.

Then, once Set came to a stop, Rei climbed down from his back.

That said, he didn’t simply dive straight into the dungeon; instead, he used Sleipnir Boots to kick off the air midway… all while ensuring he’d be ready to deal with any monsters that might emerge from the dungeon at any moment.

In her hand, she held not her usual Death Scythe, but only the Twilight Spear—since the extent of the dungeon’s interior was still unknown.

With the Twilight Spear in hand, Rei entered the dungeon and quickly scanned her surroundings... though she didn’t even need to look far to notice a presence attacking her.

Almost without thinking, Rei thrusts the Twilight Spear into the attacker.

“Gyah!?”

The goblin clutched its pierced torso and let out a scream.

Pierced through the abdomen, the goblin collapsed onto the floor... and in the next instant, was trampled by another goblin closing in from behind.

Since the one doing the trampling was also a goblin, the one being trampled didn’t sustain a fatal wound.

However, since being pierced through the abdomen was already a fatal wound, it likely made little difference.

Rei, too, showed no particular concern for the goblin who had collapsed clutching his abdomen, and thrust his spear once more at the goblin attacking from behind.

The spear’s speed was far beyond what a mere goblin could handle… and it met the same fate as the goblin whose torso had been pierced first.

Having defeated the two attacking goblins, Rei scanned his surroundings once more without letting his guard down.

However, seeing no other monsters approaching, he finally lowered the spear he had been holding at the ready.

“If I’d been just a few seconds later, this goblin would have fallen to the ground too. …When I think about it that way, there’s no doubt we got lucky.”

If goblins had started falling from above just as they were about to enter the dungeon, it would undoubtedly have been a problem for Rei and his party.

It might even have dampened their motivation to tackle the dungeon in the first place.

(Though, from the dungeon’s perspective, was that actually the optimal choice? Well, it’s debatable whether the dungeon even has a will of its own.)

If the loss of two goblins managed to dampen Rei and the others’ motivation, that would undoubtedly be a major advantage.

That said, as Rei himself mentioned, it’s unclear whether the dungeon actually possesses a will of its own.

At least, in the dungeons Rei has explored so far, that hasn’t been the case, but there’s no doubt that cutting off the dungeon’s core allowed him to acquire terrain manipulation skills and become more powerful.

In other words, the dungeon’s core and the magic stone are similar in nature… and considering that monsters have their own will, the possibility that the dungeon has a will of its own cannot be ruled out.

“Anyway, it looks like there aren’t any problems other than that goblin.”

Judging that there was no danger in the vicinity, Rei looked at Set, who was waiting outside the dungeon.

Watching Rei, who had moved to the dungeon, Set didn’t seem particularly worried.

Set had always been close to Rei, but having spent so much time with him also meant he knew just how strong Rei was.

Given that, he didn’t think for a moment that anything would happen to Rei even if he ventured into the dungeon alone.

……Well, things might have been a little different if high-rank monsters had appeared, but so far, the only confirmed monsters in this dungeon are goblins, kobolds, orcs… and yesterday’s Yellowbird.

Given that, it’s clear that even if they attacked in a group, they wouldn’t be able to scratch Rei.

“Set, go get the others. I’ll stay here and keep watch inside the dungeon, just in case.”

“Grrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl to signal his understanding and descended to the ground.

Watching Set depart, Rei looked around once more... and was once again convinced that this was indeed a dungeon, not a cave.
