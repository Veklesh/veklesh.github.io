The moment he entered the room, Ajas angrily flung the scabbard of his longsword.

"Dammit!"

The sound echoed through the room, causing surprise to flash across the faces of his two companions inside—Levjef and Huston.

"Hey, what's up? Why are you so worked up?"

Huston, who had been drinking ale, asked Ajas, but it wasn't Ajas who answered him—it was Rebejev.

"Did something happen with Rei?"

After yesterday's incident, it was already clear that Ajas was under Rei's suspicion.

Given that, it was predictable that Rei would come looking for trouble with Ajas.

And, just as Levegef had predicted, Ajas nodded irritably.

"I thought I was safe today because Rei wasn't in Treant Forest at first. But then Rei showed up midway... No, never mind. I'd heard he comes to Treant Forest every day to haul the felled timber anyway."

"...Well, having someone with an Item Box would make transporting wood easier, I guess. It'd probably make hauling the women we prepared over here easier too. I want an Item Box myself. ...Too bad I wouldn't be able to smell the women's scent, though."

"You didn't know? Apparently, item boxes can't hold living creatures?"

"Ugh. ...But it does seem pretty versatile."

"Yeah. Apparently, they analyzed the item box and found similar magic items exist. But they're way out of our price range."

Though they should have been listening to Ajas, Levjef and Hastun had somehow drifted into discussing magic items.

Listening to them, Ajas felt the blood that had rushed to his head naturally drain away.

He doubted the two in front of him had intended this exchange to target that item... yet he realized he himself hadn't been calm.

Compared to when he first entered the room, he was somewhat calmer as he spoke.

"Forget about the magic item for now. ...Anyway, I don't know why, but it seems Rei has completely set his sights on me. I expected it by yesterday, but it's not exactly fun."

Ajas' words seemed to snap them out of it. Levjeff and Huston stopped talking about the magic item and turned their gaze toward Ajas.

"Well, not knowing the reason is a problem. Without clarity on that situation, we can't know when they might set their sights on us too."

At first glance, Levegef's words seemed like he was trying to cut Ajash loose, but that wasn't their intention.

The three had been working together for years, and this current mission was also under orders from above.

That very fact fostered a sense of camaraderie among them, and Ajash was the leader-type among the three.

Neither Levegev nor Haston had any intention of abandoning him.

"Yeah. Since I don't know why I got targeted, there's a chance Levegef and Haston could be next too. ...Luckily, it seems we don't have to worry about that just yet."

Given all the shady business they're involved in, it's only natural that Ajas and the others possess thieves' skills.

All three possessed these skills, which is precisely why they could detect when Gilm's intelligence unit was investigating Ajas.

But even those three... especially Ajas... couldn't understand why he had been targeted by Rei.

(Considering he mentioned something about a merchant, it's highly likely he was targeted from that angle... but which merchant?)

Given that they'd attacked many travelers, merchants, and adventurers before, they couldn't determine which merchant Rei was referring to.

To outsiders, it would seem like they had it coming, but for Ajas and the others, not knowing the details made things extremely troublesome.

"What should we do? We haven't gathered the planned number of people yet, but should we leave Gilm now? Fortunately, getting out isn't too difficult at this moment."

"But then we won't meet the numbers the higher-ups specified, right? They're already complaining about not having enough people over there."

At Huston's words, both Ajas and Levjef... no, even Huston himself, who had spoken, fell silent, looking displeased.

It wasn't that they disliked the idea of taking the women.

They'd done similar things countless times before, and had even committed far crueler acts.

What made them feel uneasy was knowing the fate that awaited the women they took.

Being raped by men... if that were all, it wasn't exactly rare in this world, though it sounded awful.

But considering the ultimate fate awaiting those women, even scum like the Ajash couldn't feel good about it.

At the very least, we absolutely refused to suffer the same fate ourselves.

(Well, us men probably won't have to endure that sort of thing... but if we're unlucky, we might meet an even more miserable end.)

Even thinking that, the Ajash had no intention of leaving their organization.

Precisely because it was such a dangerous organization, the rewards were beyond what they could have hoped for.

Besides... if they tried to run away, it was easy to predict they'd be hunted down relentlessly to eliminate the Ajash who knew their secrets.

After all, Ajas and the others knew at least some of the higher-ups' secrets.

"They're only after Ajas, right?"

At Levjef's words, Ajas nodded with obvious irritation.

"Yeah, for now."

...That's right. For now.

For now, only Ajas seemed under suspicion, but if things continued like this, Levjef and Huston too could potentially draw the attention of Rei, the intelligence unit, and others.

For now, they were acting separately, pretending not to know each other even when staying at the same inn. But he didn't believe this could last forever.

Given that, they had to consider the danger of the other two being found as well.

"Then, should Ajas leave Gilm for now? He could gather women in the nearest town... was it Abuero? And wait for us there."

"...I see. If I leave Gilm, it might spare you guys from trouble. That could work."

"Hmm, I'm not really in favor of that."

Hastun was the one who opposed Levjef and Ajas's suggestion.

"Why not? It's because I'm here that we're drawing suspicion! If I'm not here..."

"Yeah, I get that. I get it. But... Honestly, working in Gilm barely works with the three of us. If Ajas leaves, worst case, the work might grind to a halt. What do you think the slum underworld organizations we deal with will do then?"

Hearing that, the other two fell silent.

They were indeed sourcing women through deals with the slum's underground organizations, but those deals only ran smoothly because the three of them handled the work perfectly.

But the fact that three of us are barely managing the workload means that if even one of us drops out, all sorts of problems will arise.

If that happened, would the underground organization continue to cooperate quietly as before? It would be difficult.

Considering they originally came to Gilm to gather women, losing the deal with the organization would be a significant blow to Ajash and the others.

If they could no longer trade with the organization, Ajas and his crew would have to find women themselves.

Getting one or two out might be possible with just words.

But to meet the numbers demanded from above? There's no way they could manage that by doing things like that.

"That's... but if I stay here, targeted by Rei, there's a high chance you guys will get caught too, right? To prevent that, it's better for me to disappear from Gilm."

Since Ajas had a point, Haston couldn't help but hesitate.

Watching the two of them, Levjef thought for a moment before speaking.

"Hastun, how many women have gathered so far?"

"Huh? Ah... I'm not sure. I think there were about thirty."

"How many slave collars are left?"

"About twenty."

Escaping Gilm with fifty people wasn't something you could do normally.

Since we have to use illegal means to get out, we can't have the captured women causing a commotion.

And considering what kind of future awaited them if they stayed... they'd absolutely refuse to be taken by the Ajash.

To ensure no commotion when leaving Gilm, slave collars that enforced absolute obedience to orders were essential.

Of course, they were expensive items costing a hefty sum, but the fact that they could so easily procure fifty of them showed just how much power lay above Ajas and his group.

"So then, should we just leave Gilm altogether? With about twenty people, couldn't we manage in other towns and villages, starting with places like Abuero and Saburusta?"

"If word gets back to the guild that women are disappearing wherever we go, wouldn't that cause all sorts of trouble? If we get put on a wanted list, it'll make it hard to operate from now on."

Ajas and the others aren't operating as imposters.

They are officially registered with the guild and operate as adventurers.

And precisely because of that, Ajas and his group have been entrusted with the crucial role of gathering women.

Of course, all three of them have a fair amount of confidence in their own abilities.

Enough to believe they could survive in the underworld even if they weren't adventurers anymore.

That said, they have no desire to willingly abandon their current status as adventurers.

"Then, let's do it. ...How long will it take to prepare for departure?"

"Hmm... It depends on what to do with the deals I'm negotiating right now. If things go well, I might be able to get another five or so."

"Then let's make this the last negotiation we do. Forcing a break in talks could easily turn them into enemies."

Since I've already decided to leave Gilm, forcibly ending negotiations... well, I suppose I could just leave Gilm without ending them.

But if they harbor ill will and send pursuers or the like, it would be troublesome.

Besides, gathering more women in Gilm meant we wouldn't need to kidnap as many along the way.

This wasn't out of any particular sense of mercy; it was simply that the desire to leave no evidence that they had passed through there was stronger.

Plus, I didn't want to risk being tracked down by guards.

Ultimately, with everyone in agreement, this deal would be their last.

"In that case, we'll need a reason to leave Gilm."

"Ah... I see. People come to Gilm now, but not many leave, right?"

Haston replied to Ajas's words.

Gilm was booming thanks to the city's expansion projects.

Naturally, more people were joining than leaving.

There were plenty of jobs tied to the expansion work, and you didn't need to be an adventurer—just average physical strength would suffice to find work.

People had come all the way to the remote Gilm, living dangerously, to earn money. Few would think of leaving so easily.

But... even so, there are several ways to leave Gilm without it seeming unnatural.

The most common one is...

"Escorts, right?"

At the words from Ajas's mouth, Levjef nodded in agreement, while Hastun wore a look of surprise.

Indeed, while many people had gathered in Gilm for the expansion project, it was the merchants who were transporting the materials needed for that construction.

Of course, some materials could be sourced from the resources available in Treant Forest and the surrounding areas, but that alone couldn't provide all the materials needed for the entire expansion project.

And once the merchants delivered their materials to Gilm, they would leave again to obtain new supplies.

The Gilm expansion project was also a prime opportunity for merchants to make money—they could sell as much material as they brought.

Though, that was only true for merchants with a certain level of discernment.

...And so, Ajas decided to first complete the current transactions with his two companions before leaving Gilm as an escort for the goods.
