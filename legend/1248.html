The conversation at the station ended in less than thirty minutes.

The truth was, Rei and the others genuinely couldn't understand why they had been attacked in the first place.

Under such circumstances, it was only natural they couldn't provide a clear answer when asked why they'd been attacked.

As for the suspicion that Elena, the most suspicious person, might have hidden something in her carriage, even after searching it, they couldn't be certain.

Of course, it wasn't impossible that something had been hidden in the carriage without Rei and the others noticing, but even so, the likelihood remained low.

There was also the question of whether someone capable of such a trick behind Rei and the others' backs would send attackers of that caliber.

In the end, they concluded the only way to learn the truth was to question the unconscious attackers, so Rei and the others were released.

"I really would have liked to know the details, though."

"They'll tell us eventually. We've got a lot of important people here, and they'll get in trouble from their superiors if they don't report it, right?"

Sitting on the sofa inside the carriage, Marina responded to Rei's mutter.

The exchange that had just taken place had undoubtedly spread among the residents.

Now, as they headed toward the Dusk Wheat inn, many people outside the window saw the carriage carrying Rei and the others and were talking to those nearby.

"Hey, did you hear about that commotion earlier? They say the guy in that carriage got attacked out of nowhere on the main street."

"Huh? That guy's an idiot. Set was walking alongside that carriage, right? So that means Rei was in it. What the hell were they thinking, attacking a carriage like that?"

"I saw the attack, and they were shouting something completely nonsensical!"

"Whoa, seriously? That would mean he was planning to commit robbery within Gilm!"

"That's exactly what I'm saying. Plus, the attackers were masked men."

"...Seriously, what did they want to do?"

At those words, the passersby who had been listening could only tilt their heads in confusion.

Honestly, even they, who had watched from the sidelines, had no clue what the men who attacked the carriage were trying to accomplish.

In fact, even the guards and Rei and the others didn't know the reason, and the only lead they had now was the captured men.

"It was a pretty ridiculous attack, but there aren't many clues leading back to the mastermind pulling the strings behind the scenes."

Vihera murmured as she sipped the fruit water Rei had offered.

Normally, drinking cold fruit juice in late autumn would only make one feel colder, but this carriage was a custom-made piece commissioned by Duke Kerebel.

Naturally, it was equipped with heating and cooling functions.

(Similar to eating ice cream by a kotatsu in winter?)

Sipping her fruit water, Rei felt that way about the current situation.

"Yes. Still, I'd rather not think that someone who does such foolish things actually had something detailed planned out."

Marina replied to Vihera's words.

Elena seemed to agree with that opinion, nodding as she sipped her fruit water.

As they talked, the carriage continued onward, eventually arriving near the guild.

"Ah, I'll be getting off here. I have various things to attend to, and I need to spread the word about clearing that dungeon."

Marina had resolved to pass the guildmaster position to a successor, but for now, she was still the guildmaster.

Therefore, naturally, she had much work to do.

Especially now, since she had been away from the guild handling the Vihera matter.

Naturally, there were numerous documents requiring the guildmaster's verification.

She also needed to notify various parties about the succession.

For an ordinary guildmaster, this wouldn't cause such a big commotion, but Marina was a bit special.

She had served as guildmaster for many years in the remote land of Gilm.

Countless adventurers in Gilm had received Marina's care.

Even those who hadn't received her care would find it impossible to forget the sight of Marina, a stunning beauty radiating an almost overwhelming feminine allure.

And for men... and sometimes even women... many would be utterly captivated by her allure.

So when Marina announced she was stepping down as guildmaster, it was bound to cause a stir.

Beyond that, Marina also has deep ties to Daskar, the lord of Gilm.

The guild itself is already a crucial element within Gilm.

The fact that Gilm, situated on the frontier, manages to survive is largely due to the existence of adventurers.

When the guildmaster who oversees those adventurers announces they're stepping down, it doesn't mean they'll simply cease to be guildmaster overnight.

Making the rounds to inform everyone and, above all, the handover process are crucial.

...Though, to complete the handover, the Walkers would need to return here first, and that would likely be some time off.

When the Walkers finally do return, Marina intended to act proactively from now on—and through the approaching winter—to ensure the handover could happen immediately and the guildmaster's seat could be passed on.

"I see. ...I would have liked to walk you home, but I don't know where Marina lives."

"Heh heh, you're right about that. Until now, we've always met in the guild's office. My home is prepared in the noble district. For now, it doesn't matter, but once I step down as guildmaster, we'll likely meet there more often."

Hearing Marina's words, Rei felt a strange sense of understanding.

He understood the importance of adventurers within the Gilm, and since the Guildmaster was the one who oversaw them, it made sense she couldn't be careless about where she lived.

With Marina's abilities, she'd likely be perfectly safe even living in the slums.

But considering appearances, as Guildmaster... and even more so as the lord of Gilm, it was impossible to let Marina live in a place like the slums.

"Even after resigning as Guildmaster, is it okay for me to live in the Noble District?"

"Well, even if I resign as Guildmaster, I'm supposed to become an advisor. Besides, I bought the mansion with money I earned myself, didn't I? Though it is rather small for the Noble District."

Of course, if Marina had wanted to, she could have built one of the largest mansions in the noble district.

She possessed the wealth for it, and within the Gilm, she held sufficient influence.

But for Marina, a large mansion wasn't particularly appealing.

The larger the mansion, the more cleaning work it required.

Besides, she spent most of her time at the guild anyway, only returning to the house to sleep.

Building a large mansion for such a place would only mean more hassle.

She'd have to hire people to clean, and given her duties as Guildmaster, she couldn't carelessly hire just anyone.

As a result, the size of the residence Marina currently lived in settled at a scale that was more accurately described as a house rather than a mansion.

...As for cleaning that house, Marina handles it herself rather than hiring someone.

Though she doesn't use brooms or rags; she uses spirit magic to get it done quickly.

"Is that so? I'm looking forward to visiting Marina's house someday."

"Yes, I hope that happens as soon as possible."

Marina replied to Vihera, gave Elena and Rei a brief nod, and stepped down from the carriage.

Given the proximity to the guild, many adventurers naturally recognized Marina's face. Many had witnessed the earlier commotion or heard about it.

Because of that, when Marina stepped down from the carriage, she drew the attention of those around her.

Marina, however, seemed completely unfazed by the stares, treating it as nothing out of the ordinary. She bid farewell to Set and Yellow, who was perched on his head.

Once the carriage confirmed Marina had entered the guild, it began moving again.

"Lady Elena, the Dusk Wheat inn is fine, right?"

"Yes, please do. We should have headed straight for the Dusk Wheat inn, but it took us quite a while."

All of this was due to the masked attackers, something Elena couldn't possibly understand.

Originally, Marina was supposed to split off at the Dusk Wheat inn, but after what had happened, Elena couldn't possibly let Marina walk to the guild alone.

Of course, with Marina's skill, she could probably wipe out nearly a hundred men like that with spirit magic and her bow.

But even so, considering the worst-case scenario, it was undoubtedly better to escort her directly to the guild.

Precisely because we didn't know what the enemy was planning, we needed to take every precaution possible right now.

"Still, it's such a shame... coming all this way back to Gilm only to have things turn out so dull,"

Vihera murmured wistfully.

For Vihera, it had been two months since she last visited Gilm, so she must have had various things she was looking forward to. ...Though, admittedly, she had been unconscious for those two months.

"Yeah. I'd have loved to throw a big feast if possible, but I guess that's not going to happen."

To begin with, the other adventurers were busy preparing for the winter.

Those who've earned enough money are one thing, but there are adventurers who haven't saved up enough yet, or those who've saved a decent amount but want to upgrade their lifestyle.

"As long as Rei is happy, that's all that matters. ...So, Elena?"

"Hmm?"

Vihera, who had been talking with Rei, suddenly addressed Elena, who was drinking fruit juice.

"What is it?"

"How long can Elena stay in Gilm? Until spring?"

If only that were the case.

With that thought, Vihera asked Elena, but Elena shook her head.

"I'd like to stay that long too, but I pushed myself to come here this time. I need to return as soon as possible. Fortunately... though I'm not sure if that's the right word... it hasn't snowed yet."

That implied she'd leave Gilm before the real snowfall began.

"Is that so? ...But is it really okay to leave me behind like this?"

A smile touched Vihera's lips as his gaze shifted to Rei.

She said nothing, but even to Elena's eyes, what it meant was clear.

"...Well. That's an interesting thing to say. In that case, I have an idea too?"

"Oh? What idea? Do you really think Elena can do anything in this situation? Besides, Marina's also planning to resign as Guildmaster and join us. What are you going to do, Elena?"

Vihera asked with a mischievous look, but the light in her eyes was utterly serious.

Even as a former royal, she was now an adventurer.

Marina, who was abandoning her position as Guildmaster to join us.

Compared to that, Elena was still bound by the Noble Faction.

It was clear he wanted to say that.

Elena understood what Vihera meant. She frowned slightly and fell silent.

As things stood, it would be difficult for her to be accepted as Rei's partner.

For Elena, family... and the House of Kerebel... were not things she could simply abandon.

Of course, she understood that Rei was the most important thing to her, but the House of Kerebel was also important.

In that regard, one could say the depth of her feelings differed from Vihera, who had so readily fled the Bestir Empire.

Elena understood that, yet at the same time, she couldn't imagine herself capable of doing something similar to what Vihera had done.

Seeing Elena begin to agonize, the atmosphere inside the carriage grew faintly gloomy.

If Yellow were inside the carriage, it might have brightened things up a bit, but Yellow was currently outside with Set.

"Well, Elena, you have your own things to consider and do, but you should really think this through carefully. I won't say any more, so just think it over properly."

After saying that, Vihera fell silent.

Naturally, Elena also fell silent, and in that atmosphere, there was no way Rei could say anything.

The carriage continued on in silence... and eventually arrived at the Dusk Wheat inn.

And greeting Rei and the others as they stepped down was...

"Huh!"

It was Byune, whom they hadn't seen in quite some time.
