"Welcome. ...Huh? Rei?"

The owner of the meat bun stall is surprised to see Rei appear alongside Set.

There was something akin to admiration in the gaze directed at Rei.

...That said, the stall owner was a man in his twenties, and Rei wasn’t quite sure how to react to being looked at with such admiration by someone like that.

“Ah… so you’re selling meat buns?”

“Yes. This is the dish you came up with, isn’t it, Rei? Um, would you mind trying one? Oh, and of course, you don’t have to pay for it.”

From the stall owner’s perspective, Rei—who had come up with the idea for the meat buns he was selling—must be someone truly worthy of respect.

Precisely because of that, he was nervous about how his meat buns would taste… and whether Rei, who had conceived the recipe, would be satisfied with them. Yet, seemingly confident in his skills, he opened the steamer and took out two meat buns.

Naturally, one was for Rei and the other for Set to eat.

“Is that so? Well, let’s see.”

If they’re good, I’ll pay for more.

With that in mind, Rei took the steamed bun and, careful not to get his hands dirty or burn them on the heat, took a bite from the bun wrapped in paper.

The first thing that spread across his palate was the dough, with its subtle sweetness.

Then the filling—a mixture of meat and vegetables—delighted his palate.

(Bamboo shoots... no, maybe menma? I’d love it if there were menma in there for the texture.)

Around Gilm, bamboo shoots are naturally harvested in the spring.

They’re eaten as seasonal ingredients, and while they’re different from the menma Rei knows, they’re also preserved as salted or smoked foods.

Naturally, to harvest bamboo shoots, one must go out early in the morning. Since 6:00 a.m.—when the gates open and people can enter and exit the city—is too late, some particularly determined individuals even leave Gilm the day before to camp out.

Incidentally, the bamboo shoots harvested around Gilm include not only the so-called Moso bamboo but also varieties of the root-curving bamboo.

When Rei lived in Japan, only the root-curving bamboo grew in the surrounding mountains, so that variety is more familiar to him.

That said, since most of the bamboo shoots sold in supermarkets—including menma—are from Moso bamboo, she’s not entirely unfamiliar with those either.

In any case, while not quite as popular as Gamelion, bamboo shoots are reasonably well-loved as a seasonal ingredient.

That’s precisely why Rei expected that if this meat bun contained chopped bamboo shoots, it would take the flavor to the next level.

(Huh? The stuff in meat buns is menma, right?)

Rei had been convinced it was menma, but now he tilted his head slightly, wondering if maybe he was wrong.

That said, even if the meat bun really did contain chopped menma, Rei didn’t know how to prepare menma, so he couldn’t offer any advice anyway.

“W-well, what do you think? I’m pretty confident in it, though.”

Seeing the meat bun disappear into his mouth, the shop owner asked Rei with a look of anticipation.

“Hmm, it’s not bad. It’s not bad, but…”

If asked whether it was as delicious as the owner of the skewered meat shop—who had told him about this food stall—had praised it to be, Rei couldn’t bring himself to nod in agreement.

“Average… yeah. It’s got an average taste, so I don’t think anyone would say it’s bad. But since it’s really just average, if you asked me if I’d want to eat it again, I wouldn’t say I absolutely have to.”

“Is that so? …I guess that’s how it goes.”

Hearing Rei’s critique, the owner slumped his shoulders in disappointment.

But he quickly looks up and bows his head to Rei, his eyes full of hope.

“Please. If possible, could you help me make my food taste better? Actually, sales haven’t been good these past few days, so I just can’t seem to secure a good spot.”

“……Huh? Does your stall location depend on sales?”

The shopkeeper nodded at Rei, who said that was news to him.

“Yes. Though it’s not always like that. It’s a special rule that applies only during this season, from autumn through winter… when large numbers of merchants arrive.”

“So that’s how it works?”

Although Rei used the stalls fairly often, this was the first time he’d ever heard of such a system.

“It’s not surprising if you didn’t know. It’s not exactly common knowledge. You could say it’s a tradition among the people who run stalls on the main street.”

Hearing that, Rei had no choice but to accept it.

The fact that the shopkeeper Rei was talking to didn’t seem particularly upset about the food stalls being set up in this spot was probably one of the reasons Rei accepted it.

“I see. …So? You’re feeding me this meat bun—what do you want me to do with it?”

“Well, if you could tell me exactly what’s wrong with it… that would be helpful.”

Rei looked slightly troubled as the shopkeeper bowed his head and said this.

He does like eating good food, and it was Rei who pointed out what kind of dish this meat bun was.

That much is true, but Rei isn’t exactly a gourmet… he certainly isn’t the type to act like a food critic on TV or anything like that.

"Adjust the ratio of this ingredient, tweak that seasoning."

He simply couldn’t offer that kind of advice.

(Ah)

But even so, Rei could tell just by eating this meat bun that the texture wasn’t quite right.

“How about adding some variety to the filling? Like different textures?”

“Texture? Um, like what, for example?”

“Well, I’m not sure if you should be asking me this… but, let’s see. Off the top of my head, bamboo shoots or mushrooms. Or maybe crushing some nuts and adding them in would be good too.”

What came to Rei’s mind was the steamed bun she used to buy at a convenience store on her way home from school back in Japan.

It was a steamed bun with walnuts kneaded into the dough, and to Rei, it was exactly his kind of taste.

Since it was the same kind of steamed bun, he suggested that idea… but for the shopkeeper, Rei’s suggestion must have been a real eye-opener.

Of course, in this world, bread made by kneading dried fruits and nuts into the dough is quite common, so the fact that the shopkeeper hadn’t thought of that probably meant his perspective was a bit narrow.

“Well, the idea that if the texture is enjoyable, the bread tastes good—that’s just my opinion. There might be people out there who don’t like the variety of textures in the world.”

“Uh, well… then, what should I do?”

“Don’t ask me that. Those kinds of details are something you, the one running this stall, should be thinking about, not me.”

Faced with Rei’s sound reasoning, the shop owner realized he’d been called out and fell silent…

“Give me ten meat buns.”

Suddenly, that voice rang out around them.

When Rei looked in the direction of the voice, he saw a bald man with sweat beading on his face.

He was clearly taller than Rei, though not exactly muscular; his body was just well-toned.

He looked like he’d just finished exercising, and Rei wondered, “Would anyone really want to eat a meat bun in that state?” But the bald man didn’t seem to be straining himself in any way.

In other words, he wasn’t trying to boost the stall’s sales; he was simply buying them because he genuinely wanted to eat meat buns.

The shop owner, perhaps knowing the bald man, immediately began preparing a meat bun.

Watching this, Rei called out to the bald man.

“Hey, can I ask you something?”

“What is it, Rei?”

As expected, the man knew Rei and replied immediately.

Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered by this, and asked the man, who was already popping one of the steamed buns he’d bought into his mouth.

“What do you think of this shop’s meat buns?”

"What do you think? I don't think they taste that bad."

“Not bad, huh. So they’re not actually good, then.”

“I told you, didn’t I? I said they’re not bad. I just don’t think they’re that delicious.”

“For someone who says that, you seem to be buying them in bulk—like ten at a time?”

"I’m not really interested in food. As long as it fills my stomach, that’s enough. Right now, I’m more concerned with how to get stronger."

"Like Rei, for example."

With a savage grin, the man turned his gaze toward Rei.

(Ah, I see. So he’s that type.)

A specialized training ground near the meat bun stall.

It was only natural that anyone who frequented such a place would want to hone their strength.

That said, Rei didn’t feel the need to go out of his way to engage with him… He didn’t seem particularly bothered by it, merely nodding in acknowledgment.

The skinhead man, perhaps deciding it was pointless to say anything more to Rei, who wasn’t taking the bait at all, returned to the training ground while eating his meat bun.

“Um… the people who train here tend to buy quite a few on their way home…”

The stall owner, who had been watching Rei, said this and turned his gaze toward him.

“Any other customers?”

“Well, a few…”

Judging by his evasive manner, it was easy to imagine how many other customers would actually come.

“I see. For now, all I can say is—as I mentioned earlier—you should focus on making the texture enjoyable. Oh, or instead of mincing all the meat for the filling, try including some larger pieces. That’ll give it a satisfying chew and make people feel like they’re really eating meat.”

The man nodded in agreement at Rei’s off-the-cuff suggestion.

(Come on, even a cooking novice like me could come up with that. How can you, a supposed professional, not even think of something that basic?)

This food stall run by the man in front of him would likely go out of business before long.

Rei was half-convinced of it.

He suspected that this man wasn’t a professional chef, but had only recently started running the food stall on a whim for some reason.

That’s why he couldn’t immediately come up with something even someone like Rei could think of.

(The fact that he knew I made meat buns suggests he’s passionate about cooking… but it doesn’t seem like he trained at a restaurant or anything… Is this just an extension of his hobby?)

Rei recalled a TV show he’d seen while in Japan.

It was a show about someone whose love of cooking led them to quit their day job and open a restaurant, but because of the gap between a professional and an amateur, things didn’t go well and the restaurant wasn’t thriving.

He felt that the food stall selling meat buns right in front of Rei now gave off a somewhat similar impression.

Of course, that was merely an impression Rei had.

In reality, there might be all sorts of complicated reasons behind it… but that wasn’t any of Rei’s business.

(That said)

Yes, it’s none of his business, but Rei couldn’t help but add “that said.”

A food stall selling meat buns is a rare sight.

At least as far as Rei knows, she can’t think of any others besides the one right in front of her.

For that very reason, it’s also true that if this stall fails, meat bun stalls will likely be perceived as difficult to run, making it hard for others to follow in its footsteps.

Given that, as the one who introduced meat buns to this world… and above all, because it’s a food cart that can move anywhere, offering the option to enjoy meat buns casually, Rei really doesn’t want this cart to go out of business.

However, given the unspoken rule that stall locations are determined by sales during this season, and considering the current situation where this stall is placed in—if not the very worst—then certainly one of the worst spots, a turnaround seems unlikely.

What should I do?

Just as Rei was pondering this, she suddenly saw several people who appeared to be adventurers emerging from the training grounds.

If that were all, Rei wouldn’t have had any reason to be particularly surprised.

Since it’s a training ground, it’s only natural that they’d have no business there once training is over, and just as the skinhead man had come to buy a meat bun earlier, if their training was finished, there was no need to worry about it.

But… the people coming out of the training ground weren’t walking—they were running. And rather than having the calm demeanor of people who’d just finished training, they seemed almost menacing. It was impossible for Rei not to take notice.

“Guruu?”

Having finished his meat bun, Set, who had been waiting for Rei to speak, also turned his gaze in that direction.

As Rei and Set turned their gaze that way, the food stall owner naturally followed suit and looked over as well… and upon seeing the clearly well-trained men, his face twisted in a grimace.

But the men who had emerged from the training grounds didn’t even glance at the stall owner… Instead, they approached Rei, who was standing in front of the stall, and spoke.

“Please, Rei. Even if it’s just a little, could you train us?”

With that, he bowed deeply.
