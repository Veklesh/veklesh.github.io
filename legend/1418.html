Rei arrived at the guild after the busiest time of the day, the morning rush, had passed as usual.

That said, the current situation could be called a special demand for expansion work. Even for a guild that usually had few adventurers, quite a number were present now.

(There are a lot of people, but I'm used to it now.)

Looking around the guild, Rei sighed.

It had been quite some time since the increase in people had begun.

That was precisely why Rei was starting to get used to this situation.

"Rei, hey. They're calling you?"

Vihera, who was drawing the attention of all the adventurers around them—well, two people's attention, since Marina was there too—announced from beside Rei.

"Is it something troublesome again?"

"Well, I wonder. But considering they didn't come directly to the inn, it probably isn't that urgent, right?"

"...I hope so."

Feeling a vague sense of foreboding, Rei approached the person whose gaze was fixed on him... Kenny.

Normally, Rei would be handled by Lenora, but she was nowhere to be seen, presumably away from her post.

That absence only heightened the unpleasant feeling gnawing at Rei.

Anyway, since he'd been called, he couldn't very well refuse. Rei headed toward the counter with Marina and the other two.

Meanwhile, Byune was looking toward the tavern, where a pleasant, roasted aroma seemed to be wafting from somewhere, perhaps from nuts being roasted.

Though, to be fair, Vihera, Byune's guardian, was dragging Byune—who looked ready to head straight for the tavern—toward the counter.

"Rei, you've come at just the right time. ...Well, given the hour, I figured you'd be here about now."

As Rei and the other members of Crimson Wings approached, Kenny handed his work off to a nearby receptionist and addressed them.

"Well, it's the usual time."

Lately, Rei's job had been transporting timber cut down in Treant Forest.

Given that, heading to the lumberjacks' camp right at the start of the workday meant the trees hadn't been cut yet, of course.

Occasionally, there might be leftover logs from the previous day's cutting, but that was extremely rare.

So, even if Rei went to Treant Forest, all he could do was just wait around.

...Though, he could play with Set, take a leisurely nap in the shade, or help the woodcutters if he felt like it.

However, compared to the lumberjacks—who swung their axes repeatedly, carefully calculating the direction of the fall to fell trees... displaying a certain craftsman-like skill—Rei's method of cutting down trees was rather rough in many ways.

Using the Death Scythe's flash to fell trees was a reckless idea to begin with.

"So, what brings you here this time? Another private army... no, bandits attacking a merchant caravan?"

"No, it's not that. Actually..."

Rei's deliberately coy manner as she spoke only reinforced Rei's certainty that this was definitely going to be a hassle.

Still, as he'd discussed with Elena yesterday, expanding Gilm through construction was something Rei himself desired.

(Did some monster appear in Treant Forest? ...Well, that seems like it could happen.)

Since Rei defeated the Giganto Turtle, the Treant forest no longer attacked people at night.

Had the will of the Treant Forest vanished after the Giganto Turtle's defeat? Or had it weakened to the point where it lacked the strength to act?

The reason remained unclear, but regardless, the fact that the woodcutters were no longer attacked, even if they worked slightly later, was fortunate for them.

...Incidentally, Fekts, the woodcutter who had harbored resentment toward adventurers and had gone somewhat berserk, has now returned to work.

However, naturally, someone was always watching him, making him feel extremely uncomfortable.

Moreover, managing the Treant Forest solely with Gilm's woodcutters proved difficult. News of the incident had spread to woodcutters from other villages and towns, who now regarded him with cold, distrustful eyes.

Even so, Fekts worked diligently every day, immersing himself in his tasks to take responsibility for the trouble he had caused and to regain the trust of his comrades.

"Lord Daskar is calling for you, Rei."

"......"

Rei turned his gaze back to Kenny at the soft whisper in his ear.

His handsome features twisted into a mischievous smile as he tilted his head slightly, asking, "Huh?"

"Ah... Does that have something to do with that incident the other day?"

"Probably. Yeah, that's likely it."

If this current incident was connected to that group of private soldiers who seemed to have been dispatched from somewhere, it made him feel even more troubled than before.

Still, Rei was grateful for all the favors Daskar had done for him.

Especially the magic tent he received from Daskar as a reward for the war had been incredibly useful.

Furthermore, while Rei had encountered several nobles before, Daskar ranked among the most capable—if not quite the absolute elite.

This was evident from how he managed Gilm without excess or deficiency, and his role as a central figure in the Neutral Faction.

If the lord had been arrogant... someone who viewed the town he ruled as little more than a cash cow, Rei would undoubtedly have left Gilm long ago.

No, before that even, a noble with such a disposition would have tried to seize the magic items Rei possessed, and above all, the very existence of Set.

If that had happened, Gilm might well have been reduced to a sea of flames.

"What is it you wish to request?"

"No, I haven't asked anything. Just that I want Rei-kun sent to the lord's mansion as soon as possible. That's all."

"Me... huh?"

So I have no choice but to go, huh.

Thinking that, I glanced at the party members... There, Marina and Vihera wore amused smiles, while Byune, as always expressionless, stared intently at the two of them... at Rei and Kenny, standing at a distance that could truly be described as lovers' distance.

"Well, well, you two seem quite close."

"Huh!? N-no, it's nothing like that!"

Kenny scrambled to put distance between himself and Rei.

Though Kenny usually openly showed his affection for Rei and initiated physical contact to an excessive degree, he seemed weak in the face of such sudden situations.

"Um... well then, I have work to do, so I'll be off. Please take care of that matter from earlier."

"Yeah, yeah, I got it. Now get back to work already."

Though Kenny flashed Marina a resentful look, he flushed bright red and retreated behind the counter when he realized Rei was watching him.

"Heh heh, same as ever. ...Well then, shall we go?"

"Huh? Are Marina and the others coming today too?"

"Yes. If Daskar wants something, it's probably better if I'm there."

"Ah, yeah. That makes sense."

While Rei was never particularly skilled at negotiations, Marina, the former guildmaster, wasn't exactly inept at them either.

Above all else, Marina knew Daskar from when he was little.

And she hadn't forgotten the numerous mistakes he'd made back then.

...The biggest one of all was probably when the child Daskar proposed marriage to Marina.

For Daskar, it was a memory he absolutely wanted to forget.

Rei hadn't been told the full details, but even so, it was clear from watching their interactions that there was a semi-absolute power dynamic between Marina and Daskar.

Though Rei respected Daskar, he had no desire to be used by him.

For that reason, the entire Crimson Wings squadron headed together for the lord's manor.

"Wow... there sure are a lot of people here."

As they approached the lord's mansion, the sight of many people coming and going caught their eyes.

Seeing this, Vihera voiced her honest impression.

But that was only natural.

Rei and the others had had many opportunities to come to the lord's mansion before.

Compared to those times, the number of people near the manor now was incomparably greater.

Many of those around them were merchants, and there were also numerous figures who appeared to be their guards.

Why so many merchants? Rei wondered, but upon reflection, it made perfect sense. The chief overseer of the Gilmu expansion was, of course, Daskar, the lord of this Gilmu.

As merchants, it was only natural for them to pay their respects to Daskar beforehand and establish a rapport.

"It's been quite a while since I've been to the lord's mansion... but I didn't expect it to be like this."

"True. But well... I think the merchants here aren't that important anyway."

Marina murmured softly, just loud enough for Rei and the others to hear.

"Is that so?"

Rei, wondering if this meant all the merchants present, asked. Marina nodded in response.

"Yes. The truly important merchants needed for the expansion work should have been contacted in advance by Daskar. ...Though, even if they aren't crucial merchants, considering the scale of Gilm, it's probably a sufficiently lucrative deal for them."

Considering the scale of Gilm, Marina's point made sense.

If such a lucrative deal was possible, it wasn't surprising that merchants, greedy for business opportunities, were swarming the lord's manor like this.

"Hmm."

Given the incident with the Twilight Spear, Rei simply couldn't bring himself to feel kindly toward merchants.

Of course, he understood this didn't apply to all merchants, but it was probably inevitable that he flashed at least a hint of distaste.

"Ah, Rei! And Lady Marina, Ms. Vihera, and even Byune. Thank you for coming."

A voice called out to Rei, who had been wearing that faintly displeased expression.

The voice came from the gatekeeper of the lord's mansion.

With this many merchants arriving, it was only natural that many faces were unfamiliar.

Since it couldn't be ruled out that someone might be plotting something strange, the gatekeeper's duties required extra vigilance today.

Seeing the guards' vigilant stance, the merchants naturally tried to avoid causing any trouble.

Of course, this wasn't out of consideration for the guards' hardship... but rather an attempt to make a good impression.

Regardless, despite the crowd of merchants gathered before the gate, finding Rei was easy... simply because the members of Crimson Wings were extremely conspicuous.

Among the merchants, some even changed their expression upon seeing Set standing beside Rei.

Then again, getting to see a griffin up close like this was a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity... no, realistically, it was practically unheard of. So this sight wasn't strange at all.

Precisely because of that, as a merchant, Set could smell the scent of big money.

Yet he didn't act immediately, instead watching Rei and the others... which made sense, given he'd come here intending to get involved in this expansion project, however indirectly.

Information is a merchant's lifeblood.

Therefore, it stands to reason he knows about Rei, whose name is currently well-known in Gilm.

And with a little more digging, it wouldn't be hard to learn that many merchants—though none like the ones here—had flocked to acquire the magic item known as the Twilight Spear that Rei had once obtained.

Moreover, the sharp-eyed among them had seen the displeased look Rei flashed their way when he appeared here.

That's precisely why, as things stood, they couldn't carelessly approach him.

If you spoke first and made a bad impression, it wouldn't be irreparable... but it would undoubtedly put you at a significant disadvantage compared to the other merchants.

Given that, it was only natural to want to approach him under the most favorable circumstances possible.

The merchants exchanged wary glances... Whether aware of this or not, Rei ignored them and approached the gatekeeper who had called out to him.

"How'd you know we were coming?"

"Listen... with Set here, don't you think it's impossible to hide that you came?"

"Grr?"

At the gatekeeper's words, Set looked at Rei and growled softly.

Running a hand through Set's fur, Rei shook his head.

"Don't worry about it. It's not like Set did anything wrong. ...Anyway, I heard at the guild that Lord Daskar was calling for me."

At Rei's words, the merchants nearby who had been listening in were slightly startled.

They hadn't expected Daskar himself to summon someone directly.

The gatekeeper nodded to Rei and opened the gate to let her through.
