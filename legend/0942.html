After finishing his conversation with Elena using the Paired Orb, Rei had fallen asleep in bed.

But Rei, who had been sleeping in bed, suddenly woke up.

It wasn't because he needed to use the bathroom or was thirsty.

With a movement that could almost be called reflexive, Rei jumped up. Despite just waking, his gaze was sharp and focused on his surroundings the instant he was awake.

Though he was usually half-dazed upon waking, the current Rei showed no sign of such a state.

"...What? Just now, something..."

He pulled the Dragon Robe from his Misty Ring and wrapped it around himself while scanning his surroundings, but there was nothing there.

"What was that fleeting presence I just sensed?"

Had Rei possessed clairvoyance, he might have seen the scene unfolding on the rooftop of a building far from the staff dormitory: a Sardat and a Stigma man locked in combat.

He might even have understood that the presence he sensed was caused by the activation of Stigma.

But Rei possessed no such clairvoyant abilities; all he could do was scan his surroundings.

But... thanks to that very act, Rei noticed its presence.

The fist that suddenly flew toward him.

"Huh!?"

He crouched down in an instant, and the fist pierced the space where Rei had been just a moment before.

...Yes, it possessed precisely the kind of power that deserved to be described as piercing.

The one who had thrown the punch opened his mouth, a look of slight surprise on his face.

"Well. Unlike last time, I put quite a bit of real power into that. I never imagined you'd dodge this one too."

"You are..."

Rei recognized the face of the man who withdrew his fist, wearing an expression of genuine admiration.

His features were exceptionally well-defined, yet the tattoo-like mark on his right cheek made him impossible to forget.

He was the one who had attacked Ray when he saved Eland, someone Ray had initially thought might be part of the group sent to assassinate Eland.

But after what Saldat had told him in the Headmaster's office, he now knew his true identity with certainty.

"Stigma... right?"

"Correct. Though knowing that now won't change anything, of course."

Facing the man wearing an elegant smile, Rei let out a light click of her tongue.

The surprise attack while he was still half-asleep was one thing, but the fact that he wasn't fully prepared to fight back was painful.

Though he wore the Dragon Robe, the magic item he relied on most in battle—Sleipnir's Boots—remained in the Misty Ring. Likewise, Nebra's Eye, ideal for catching enemies off guard, was naturally still in the Misty Ring too.

The Death Scythe could be used immediately upon retrieval, but the Sleipnir Boots required wearing.

The Nebula Eye, however, only needed to be attached to his waist, so he could use it immediately upon retrieval.

Rei hesitated, wondering what to do about them... but he made his decision quickly.

After all, this was indoors, a battlefield ill-suited for someone like Rei who wielded a long-handled weapon.

In contrast, his opponent was a master of close combat, whose power didn't diminish in confined spaces... in fact, he thrived in such tight quarters.

There was no way Rei would be so polite as to fight on the enemy's advantageous terrain. The next instant, Rei chose to throw the dagger he'd taken from the Misty Ring at the man.

It was a throw using only the flick of his wrist, but Rei's physical abilities were beyond human.

The dagger sliced through the air, flying straight for the man's face.

A strike that would be impossible to dodge under normal circumstances.

But the man, a member of Stigma, caught the blade between his fingers without any apparent effort.

Though Rei showed a hint of surprise at this action, it was undeniable that the man's movement had halted, even for an instant.

He immediately kicked off the floor and sprinted toward the window, succeeding in breaking through it and leaping outside the next instant. But...

"Gah!?"

The moment he leapt outside, thinking he could finally fight freely, he suddenly took a blow through the Dragon Robe.

Being mid-air proved his undoing, and Rei was sent flying.

Had he been wearing his usual Sleipnir boots, he might have been able to kick off mid-air and regain his footing.

But the sleepy Rei was only clad in the Dragon Robe and couldn't manage such a feat.

(Dammit! What the... I didn't sense a thing coming!?)

Feeling a dull ache in his back, he shifted his gaze as he flew through the air.

What entered Rei's field of vision was an old man standing at a right angle against the wall, holding a staff.

He realized that the dull pain in his back when he'd flown out the window must have been caused by that staff.

(That guy...)

Just like the man who had suddenly appeared in his room, Rei recognized the old man.

He was the one who had been with the man who first attacked Rei. That meant the old man doing the absurd thing of standing on the wall was also a member of Stigma.

(Two Stigmas... There was definitely another one, right?)

Regaining her balance midair, she landed barefoot on the ground while scanning her surroundings.

Landing on the snow, she felt the cold, but now was no time for that. She drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and swung it wide.

"Flying Slash!"

The slash released with that command charged toward the old man still standing on the wall... but the instant it seemed to strike him, the old man vanished as if he'd never been there at all.

"What!?"

She couldn't comprehend what had happened.

I grasped he hadn't used a teleportation magic item, yet the old man vanished the instant the Flying Slash connected.

"Hehehe. Scary, scary. To attack even an old man like this without mercy. You should show a little more consideration for your elders."

The old man reappeared, smiling as he spoke.

But his expression showed no sign of being frightened at all.

Nevertheless, after confirming a certain distance had opened up, he fired several more Flying Slashes to keep me at bay before swiftly retreating.

He headed straight for a small wooded area not far from the staff quarters.

It was hardly a forest, with far too few trees, but for now, it was enough for Rei to hide his presence from the two Stigmas.

Leaning his back against a tree trunk, he pulled magic items like Sleipnir's boots and Nebula's Eye from his Misty Ring and began equipping them.

It took him only a dozen seconds to put everything on.

But that was more than enough time for the two Stigmata to find Rei.

"Huh!?"

Hearing the faint whistling of wind, he instinctively kicked off the ground and vanished from the spot.

The instant Rei vanished, the tree trunk he'd been leaning against shattered into pieces.

...Yes, not snapped or sliced, but shattered.

Though inwardly cursing such a blow, Rei, now fully prepared for battle unlike moments before, showed no panic.

Watching the tree trunk split cleanly in half, he stepped forward without hesitation.

"Oh? Have you given up on running?"

"Ah. Well, I was flustered when someone attacked me before I was ready for battle, but I'm fine now. ...Still, I'd think contacting someone before visiting is common sense. Doesn't the Holy Light Church teach even that much? Well, it's a trivial religion after all, so I suppose it's no surprise."

At Rei's words, the man in his twenties' cheek twitched.

But the old man standing beside him wore a kindly smile, deflecting Rei's provocation.

"Heh heh heh. Now, now, don't say that. Contacting before a visit is only proper, but we aren't exactly on those terms, are we?"

"True. What's needed to deliver divine punishment to a great enemy isn't courtesy, but a whip... No, my fist. Courtesy is useless against such an opponent."

"Hmph. So that Holy Light religion is just that shallow, huh? What's so holy about their goddess of light? To me, she looks like nothing more than a hideous, filthy, ugly self-proclaimed goddess."

To say there was no provocation in Rei's words would be a lie.

Facing two Rank A adventurers simultaneously was a first for Rei, and worse, these weren't mock battles—these were opponents harboring genuine murderous intent toward him.

Without Set present, he was at a disadvantage. So he provoked the Stigma-bearer—the one who looked like he'd lose his temper at the drop of a hat—to disrupt his calm judgment.

Rei's aim was correct. ...It was correct, but at the same time, there was a miscalculation.

That miscalculation was that the person standing before him was a devout believer... no, a fanatic of the Holy Light Church.

The man in his twenties, his intelligent beauty now twisted with fury, roared at Rei's words.

"Enemy! I declare this here! I will make you regret the day you were born, erasing you from this world in the name of my faith! That is the mercy Vicente, a member of Stigma, can grant you! Accept divine punishment, enemy Rei!"

As the man who called himself Vicente spoke, the tattoo on his right cheek began to emit a sharp, piercing light.

(This is bad!?)

He didn't know what the other was trying to do.

But even so, Rei judged it too dangerous to leave Vicente unchecked. Concentrating his magic power, he activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and kicked off the ground.

Closing the distance to Vicente, Rei's magic compressed, condensed, and transformed into a crimson hue, coiling around his body.

Seeing Rei like this, Vicente and the old man were the next to be shocked.

Neither possessed the ability to sense magic, so they didn't realize the force enveloping Rei was magical power.

Or perhaps, if not for the New Moon Ring on Rei's finger concealing his magic to the level of an ordinary mage, they might have sensed the overflowing power emanating from his body and realized what it was.

But that was all hypothetical.

Wrapped in magic compressed and concentrated to the point of being visible, Rei, having completed the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, closed the distance to Vicente in one swift motion.

Rei's movements, now clad in the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, were a world apart from before.

To ordinary eyes, the speed at which he moved would be impossible to comprehend. ...But suddenly, he noticed the figure of an old man running alongside him.

(He's keeping up with this speed!?)

It wasn't that he had let his guard down because his opponent was an old man.

In fact, when escaping through the window, he had taken a blow from above, and despite landing a flying slash, the old man had shrugged it off unscathed.

But even possessing that level of strength, the fact he could move at the same speed as him was completely unexpected.

"Dammit! Flying Slash!"

Closing the distance with Vicente, Rei swung the Death Scythe at the old man running alongside him.

The flying slash released from it headed straight for the old man... but in the next instant, the old man vanished again, soaring into the night sky.

But it was a sight he had already witnessed once.

Rei didn't react with surprise like before.

Taking advantage of the old man's disappearance, he focused solely on closing the distance to Vicente.

Only a few seconds had passed since Rei started running. Normally, that amount of time would have been enough for Rei to reach Vicente.

But due to the old man's interference, even that tiny fraction of time within those few seconds had been wasted... and that meant he wouldn't make it in time to close the distance to Vicente.

"Too bad for you."

"Guh!"

The moment those words left Vicente's mouth, Ray judged that continuing forward like this would be futile. He abruptly shifted his actions, abandoning the pursuit of closing the gap to instead create distance.

Maintaining the speed gained from closing the gap, he leapt, activating the Sleipnir Boots. Using the air as a foothold, he kicked off and made a wide jump sideways.

He kept kicking through the air, one step, two steps, three steps, each time using the momentum from his charge to gradually widen the gap between himself and Vicente.

But Stigma wasn't about to let Rei escape like this.

As Rei pulled away, taking four, five steps, the old man reappeared before him... thrusting the staff in his hand toward Rei.

Recalling the shock of the attack he had taken when leaping out the window earlier, Ray swung his Death Scythe to block it.

Just as the massive blade seemed poised to cleave the old man in two, he vanished once more, just as before.

(Is this really not teleportation!?)

Watching the old man vanish and reappear with such ease, Rei inwardly spat in disgust.

But now, before pondering the old man's abilities, there was something else he had to do.

Yet his movement was a step... no, two steps too slow.

Due to the old man's interference, the crucial moment for Rei to break free from that spot was lost.

"O great enemy, receive divine punishment!"

As Vicente shouted, something erupted from his body.

That something spread in an instant, engulfing the entire area that had become a battlefield.
