"Haa, haa, haa"

Ajax, sword in hand, scanned his surroundings, his breathing ragged.

Every goblin that had entered Treant Forest lay dead on the ground.

"Hey, hey. What's wrong, Ajas? They're just goblins, after all. Why are you taking it so seriously?"

One of the adventurers who had accepted the woodcutter's escort request alongside Ajax called out to him with concern.

But Ajas, addressed by the voice, glared at the speaker with eyes that seemed half-bloodshot.

"Huh!? Hey, Ajas?"

The man gasped for a moment before calling out to Ajas again.

Even as he did so, his grip tightened on the mace he wielded—perhaps instinctively sensing a threat from Ajas's demeanor.

Ajas seemed to sense the man's unease and calmed himself, looking at his workmate before him.

But his calm demeanor was merely a facade.

Could this man in front of him be one of Rei or Ilse's allies? He couldn't help but suspect it.

After all, his assignment to guard the woodcutters in Treant Forest had come at the strong request of the guild.

The original pay was good, and he could expect additional compensation for the timber he cut.

Refusing such a request presented to the guild would have been far too conspicuous.

(But judging by today's events... probably bringing me the request itself was the trap. If that's the case, it's safe to assume the guild has been investigating me thoroughly.)

Though unsettled, Ajas was fairly certain this was the case.

In reality, when he negotiated with Kenny to have Ajas assigned to him, he was undoubtedly deeply suspected. Even so, it hadn't truly been confirmed that Ajas was Ilse's killer.

The tattoo and the face of Ajas in Ilse's memory.

Those two things were the basis for it, but if it was just the tattoo, one could think it was a similar tattoo, and it wasn't certain that she still clearly remembered the face of the enemy she saw five years ago.

...Though, given those two pieces came together, Rei had thought it was practically confirmed...

In any case, it was only during their recent exchange that Ajas was truly confirmed as Ilse's killer.

Once he realized that, there was no way Ajas wouldn't suspect the adventurers who had received this request around the same time as him.

"Ah, sorry. This forest gave me more trouble with goblins than I expected. Got me a bit worked up."

"Hey, are you really okay? That's unusual—Ajax having trouble with goblins."

Seeing Ajas's state, the man gently relaxed his grip on his mace.

There had been several raids in Treant Forest before.

But the attackers were almost always goblins, and they hadn't been that difficult to handle.

The man had seen Ajax fight many times before, which was precisely why he was puzzled to see Ajax struggling with goblins like this.

"Ah, I'm not feeling too well. ...Sorry, but if I keep going like this, I might end up holding the others back. If things are fine without me, I'm afraid I'd have to head back for today. Is that okay?"

"Huh? Hey, hey, you're really feeling that bad? ...Well, it's not that busy here, so it should be fine. But shouldn't you tell Mr. Rugluno?"

"Yeah, I'll do that. I'm really glad I'm working here."

Ajas said this, genuinely feeling grateful for his job as a woodcutter's escort.

Honestly, if he'd been working at the construction site building the new wall, there's no way he could have pulled a stunt like leaving early.

At Gilm, where the expansion was underway, the busiest place was naturally the construction site.

If someone asked to leave early because they felt a little under the weather in a place like that, it wouldn't be easily granted.

...Though, if feeling unwell actually caused other tasks to fall behind, that would be a different story.

Anyway, after telling the man that, Ajas set off through Treant Forest to find Rugluno.

Given the recent goblin attack, finding Rugluno wouldn't be too difficult.

But... the moment Ajas saw Rugluno, he stopped in his tracks.

Because standing beside Rugluno were Rei and Ilse.

Could they be telling Rugluno something about me?

Once that thought entered his mind, he had no leisure to speak to Rugluno.

(It's better to disappear without saying anything.)

An ordinary adventurer couldn't pull off such a stunt.

But Ajas already understood that Rei knew his true identity.

Given that, he couldn't work as an adventurer anymore.

And it wasn't just Gilm.

Through the communication network linking guilds, Ajas's name would likely spread far and wide.

There was even a chance a sketch of him might be circulated.

Given that, he decided there was no need to deliberately refuse Rugluno here, and immediately left the scene.

I couldn't use a carriage or similar, but it wasn't an impossible distance to walk or run to Gilm.

(Should I head straight for Abuero? ...No, that won't do. My base is at Gilm. To prepare to disappear, I need to return to Gilm. Besides, I can't just leave those two behind.)

There was also the matter of the other women he had secured.

They were currently locked in cells borrowed from the organization, and he couldn't forget to retrieve them.

Ajas couldn't help but wonder if he should leave it to his comrades. But then there was the possibility that Rei, Ilse, or whoever was behind them might search the room where he was staying...

Of course, he had hidden anything that might serve as evidence.

But there was still a chance some kind of clue might remain. Considering that, it was probably better for Ajas to take the women with him when he escaped.

(No, taking the women with me would draw attention no matter what, even with the slum organization's help. So maybe I should be the decoy after all? Either way, I need to get back to the inn as soon as possible to discuss this.)

Muttering to himself, Ajas looked up several times... at the summer sun pouring down its intense rays, the blue sky, and the cumulonimbus clouds.

He was sweating profusely from his interactions with Rei and the others, the battle with the goblins, and most of all, from running like this. While he felt a certain resentment toward the sky, which was fair enough to be called fine weather, what weighed heavier on his mind was Set.

If a griffin could fly, couldn't it also pursue him from the sky?

That fear—though he himself would never admit it—was there.

...And it was justified.

After all, even now, Set was tracking Ajas from the sky.

But his purpose wasn't pursuit, as Ajas imagined. It was merely tracking.

If Ajas were to flee in this situation, it was highly likely he would head for his base.

Of course, there was also the possibility he wouldn't head straight for the base and would vanish instead.

If he tried that, Set had planned to apprehend him directly... Considering that, Ajash still had some luck left since he hadn't been caught just yet.

Incidentally, the reason Ajas couldn't spot Set despite him tracking her from above was simply the altitude Set was flying at.

If he'd been flying at an altitude of around ten meters, Ajas wouldn't have had much trouble spotting Set.

However, Set was flying at his usual altitude of one hundred meters.

Even though adventurers possess greater physical abilities than ordinary humans, spotting Set flying at a hundred meters was impossible.

Well, perhaps some might have been able to under certain circumstances, but it was certainly beyond Ajas's capabilities.

Unaware he was being followed, Ajas kept running relentlessly until he finally reached Gilm.

By the time the main gate came into view, he had slowed his pace and wore an expression of apparent discomfort.

Since he had originally returned claiming to be unwell, he probably thought this was the best course of action.

Of course, since he'd left without telling Rugluno anything, his unauthorized return would likely be discovered eventually.

But Ajas figured that as long as he could manage the present moment, it wouldn't be a problem.

After completing the entry procedures into Gilm, he headed straight for the guild... no, for the inn serving as his base.

Naturally, Set had been watching Ajas's movements closely from above.

Once he confirmed Ajas had entered the inn, Set continued flying overhead, watching to make sure Ajas didn't escape somewhere from the inn.

Of course, if Ajas escaped through the inn's basement or moved to a roofed area invisible from the sky, Set wouldn't be able to see him.

Regardless, Set continued his surveillance of the inn while flying through the sky, keeping a watchful eye on the surroundings.

"Haa..."

Finally back at the inn and able to relax, Ajas collapsed onto the bed, exhaling a sigh of relief.

Of course, he knew he couldn't afford to stay like this forever. Still, the sheer nerves it took to make his way back to this inn while constantly fearing an attack from Rei had worn him down considerably.

"I really didn't want to come back here..."

He muttered this because if the inn were searched, some evidence Ajash had forgotten about might surface.

But honestly, considering there was no time to spare, returning to this inn was his only option.

"Anyway, I need to figure something out as soon as possible... but honestly, there's not much I can do right now."

If he'd come to Gilm alone, it might be different, but he'd come with Levjef and Huston.

Of course, officially, we're supposed to be strangers, but in reality, we're working together on a job to gather women.

Given that, I couldn't just bolt from here without telling them anything.

Besides, he'd known Levjef and Huston for over five years.

Thinking it through... Ajash recalled the woman he'd encountered just now for the first time in five years.

(Back then... I was on my way to meet Levegef and Huston, right? I needed some cash on the side... Ah, yes. Yeah, I remember now.)

As he thought this and recalled Ilse's face, his fatigue eased considerably, and he rose from the bed.

"Anyway, whatever I need to prepare, I can't exactly go out shopping myself. That means I'll need someone to buy things for me."

There was a young errand boy at the inn who knew nothing about him.

Using such a child, it wouldn't be too difficult to buy the necessary food supplies after leaving Gilm.

"Food for three people... No, just the bare minimum here. It'd be better to buy the rest in Abuelo or Saburusta. Though I'd rather not stop there if possible."

The original plan was to wrap up the deal with the slum's underground organization as quickly as possible, then abduct the remaining women needed in Abuelo, Saburusta, or other villages, towns, or cities we passed through along the way.

But in the current situation, such leisurely actions were impossible.

At the very least, if they tried to abduct women in towns close to Gilm like Aburo or Saburusta, it wouldn't be surprising if Rei showed up while they were doing it.

"A griffin? That's cheating. And I hear they're a rare species too."

No matter how much Ajas and the others moved across the ground, it wouldn't be difficult for a flying griffin to spot them.

Even if they took the dangerous risk of straying from the road, being pursued by a griffin would mean they'd be found in no time.

Whatever they tried to do, their speed of movement worked decisively against Ajas.

Ajas couldn't hide his frustration over that fact.

Not that he was arrogant enough to think he could beat Rei if the griffin weren't there...

After all, he was only a Rank D adventurer.

With Levjef and Huston by his side, he might stand a chance against a Rank C... or even a Rank B adventurer. But Rei wasn't just any Rank B adventurer; he was a Rank B adventurer with a notorious reputation.

In any case, since defeat was already certain the moment they fought, the only thing Ajas could do was figure out how to avoid fighting altogether.

Ajas sat alone in the room, agonizing over how to find a way to do just that.
