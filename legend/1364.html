"Huh? Cut down more trees? Well, it's not like it's that hard for us, so it's fine... but why again?"

"I don't know. Asking me won't get you anywhere. I just got the order from higher up."

At the guild counter, Rei tilted his head at Kenny's words.

Usually, he'd receive requests from his assigned contact, Lenora, but unfortunately, Lenora seemed busy with various things today, so Kenny was handling Rei's request.

While secretly pleased by this, Kenny still performed her duties as a receptionist properly.

Then again, that was only natural for a guild employee.

"The trees in Treant Forest are huge, and bringing them back must be tough, right? That's why they asked Rei? Since you have an item box, you don't have to worry about weight or anything."

There was logic to Kenny's words.

Still, Rei couldn't help but feel doubt.

It was true that bringing back that many trees was difficult.

In Rei's case, since he traveled with Set, the travel time was minimal. But if moving by regular carriage on the ground, it would take a considerable amount of time.

And if you were walking, it would naturally take even longer.

Even understanding that, Rei still wonders.

Does that tree really hold that much value?

From Rei's perspective, that tree didn't look much different from any ordinary tree.

Of course, considering it grew so large overnight, there might actually be various differences... but even so, it wasn't that important to Rei.

"Well, if they're paying me, I don't really have any complaints."

"Right? We are adventurers, after all. ...Well then, I'll handle this end. ...Speaking of which, did you hear? Apparently, it's Gilm's policy to recruit more adventurers now?"

Kenny continued talking with Rei while filling out the request paperwork.

He had paperwork to complete, yet he also wanted to talk with Rei.

That was Kenny's thought, but he'd done this kind of thing countless times before.

Therefore, he managed both tasks without any particular difficulty.

"More than we have now? ...Why?"

Rei had been thinking about getting the request done quickly, but Kenny's words seemed to pique his interest. He asked.

If Marina, Vihera, or Lenora had been present, they would have either rushed off with Rei to handle the request or smacked Kenny over the head with paperwork.

But now, the busiest part of the morning had passed, and the number of adventurers in the guild was low. Only the adventurers who had decided to take the day off without taking any requests were making noise in the tavern since morning.

Because of that, the receptionist's work wasn't busy, and the other guild staff were largely overlooking Kenny's behavior.

...Truthfully, deepening ties with high-ranking adventurers was a significant matter for the guild itself.

"Well? The guildmaster only hinted at it vaguely, so nothing's actually decided yet... but I suppose something big is probably happening."

"Even so, gathering more adventurers? Honestly, even if something did happen, I feel like the current Gilm could handle most things just fine."

Rei's muttered words were true in one sense, but wrong in another.

Indeed, the number of adventurers in the Gilm guild was large, and their quality was generally high.

But they were, after all, adventurers. If they felt like it, they could easily relocate their base to another town or village.

Of course, few would foolishly abandon Gilm, the ultimate place for adventurers, to relocate so easily.

Still, if anything were forced upon them, it was certain that a significant number of adventurers would leave Gilm.

As the Guildmaster of Gilm, Walker couldn't possibly be unaware of this. Considering that, gathering adventurers must mean he has some reason for doing so.

"If this didn't just come up suddenly, could the reason be that he's been planning something for a while and it's finally being put into action?"

"Hmm, I wonder?"

"Surely it's not related to that Treant Forest, is it?"

"Rei!"

The moment the words "Treant Forest" left his mouth, a voice called out as if waiting for that exact moment.

Turning toward the voice, Rei saw the female adventurer he'd seen before... Slesha.

"Um, Slesha, right? What's up?"

"I heard Rei was heading to Treant Forest. Please take me with you."

"...Well, that's easier said than done."

When Rei heads to Treant Forest, he travels by riding Set.

Naturally, that meant only Rei could ride Set.

Marina, Vihera, and Byune all had their own business today, so only Rei was heading to Treant Forest... but clinging to Set's legs to travel seemed a bit difficult for Slesha.

Furthermore, Rei had only just met Slesha and didn't trust her that deeply yet.

Clinging to a flying Set naturally posed various problems unless the person was someone you could trust to a certain degree.

If Set were to do something strange mid-flight, Rei... and Set would have limited means to counter it.

That's precisely why the option of bringing along someone like Slesha, whom he barely knew, simply wasn't there for Rei.

"Sorry, but I'm traveling with Set. I don't want to bring along someone I'm not very close to."

"But... please, make an exception. I need to know the truth. Why did that happen in the forest... Treant Forest? And if possible, I want to avenge my comrades..."

Slesha said this in a strained voice, but Rei silently shook his head.

Though he didn't voice it, another major reason for leaving her behind was that Slesha's skill level was decidedly lacking.

So far, Rei hadn't been attacked in Treant Forest.

But since there were actual people, like Slesha's comrades, who had been attacked, there was no telling when it might happen.

If it were just Rei and Set, or even Marina and Vihera, it might be manageable. But having a Rank D adventurer like Slesha along would slow them down.

If they tried to protect a liability... they might fall behind.

And given that the Treant Forest itself was an unknown entity, the likelihood that such a delay could prove fatal was very high indeed.

"...Sorry."

Without voicing the full reason, Rei started to leave... but suddenly stopped.

"Come to think of it, what about Runo who saved you? Runo's a skilled solo adventurer. I think she could help you."

Though Runo was only known for his magic eye that could see magic power, he was indeed skilled, as evidenced by his preference for solo work.

In Gilm, solo adventurers were either overconfident fools or genuinely skilled individuals.

Before forming Crimson Wings with Marina and the others, Rei had also worked solo, but in that case, he had his partner, Set.

Considering that, even though she was technically solo under the guild system, it could be said she wasn't truly alone.

"Well... after Runo-san helped me and we talked in the Guildmaster's office, we parted ways there..."

Seeing Slesha looking downcast, Rei understood she probably wanted to work together originally.

"I see. ...But if you need help, you should ask Runo, not me. For better or worse, that guy Runo is pretty good at looking out for people."

"...But I've heard you're one of the top-tier fighters in the Gilm guild, Rei. And since you're actively taking requests related to Treant Forest now..."

...I'd like to help with that too," she concluded.

Rei understood what Slesha was getting at, but even so, he couldn't take it on in the current situation.

"Sorry. I'll do things my own way."

"If it's about the reward... I can prepare a little something."

Rei immediately understood where that reward would come from.

Because it was Rei who had found the traces of Slesha's party's campsite, and it was Rei who had brought back the belongings left behind there.

(Well, since the party was wiped out, ownership of that luggage belongs to Slesha. I could have taken it myself, but for a Rank D party, there probably wasn't anything particularly valuable anyway.)

So, even if Slesha decided to sell that luggage and give the money to Rei as a reward, he wouldn't blame her.

I can't help but think there might have been a better use for it...

At the same time, I'm impressed that the comrades Slesha lost meant that much to her.

"Sorry, but I'm not in a position to be that generous right now. That money would be better spent hiring someone like Runo."

Rei muttered, as if to emphasize his point one last time, then left the guild without another word to Slesha.

Slesha watched Rei's departure with a look of disappointment.

Yet, he showed no sign of giving up. Likely because he'd gathered information on Rei beforehand and anticipated it would be impossible to get him to come along.

While traveling with Rei would have been ideal, it was nothing more than wishful thinking.

(First, I need to find skilled adventurers... In that sense, it was lucky I ended up here at Gilm.)

In the area where Slesha's former party had operated, skilled adventurers were almost nonexistent.

...Though there were plenty who called themselves skilled.

That was one reason Slesha and the others had come to Gilm.

That's precisely why finding skilled adventurers in Gilm—meaning those more skilled than Slesha—wasn't that difficult.

Of course, some might try to deceive Slesha.

In any case, as an archer whose weapon is the bow, Slesha absolutely requires a front-line fighter for any operation.

A more skilled archer might be able to operate alone without frontline support... but unfortunately, Slesha hadn't reached that level yet.

(Treant Forest... I cannot die until I resolve matters concerning that forest. I will do it with my own hands, absolutely...)

With her resolve clear, Slesha set out to find companions.

"Grrrr!"

Rei and Set were heading toward Treant Forest as usual, but as the forest came into view, Set suddenly let out a sharp cry.

「Set？」

Though puzzled by his partner's behavior, Rei let Set do as he pleased.

He understood there was no reason for Set to act strangely in this situation.

As if sensing Rei's thoughts, Set began descending toward the ground.

However, the spot Set was heading for was quite far from the places Rei had visited several times before.

Given the forest's considerable size, the area where Rei had been operating naturally remained quite limited.

...Though perhaps that was only natural, given it also served to gauge just how vast the forest truly was.

"...That is..."

As they neared the ground, Rei also noticed what Set had spotted.

Something reflecting sunlight, visible between the trees.

It was a scene that felt strangely familiar.

Yes, like when Slesha's party found traces of a campsite.

"I suppose this was to be expected."

What had happened in the forest?

Understanding it, Rei murmured softly.

Coming to this forest isn't exactly forbidden by the guild.

But even so, it's publicly known that no monsters or animals of any kind exist here.

Even if you were to take on an extermination request, there's no prey.

For gathering requests, one might think... but since this forest suddenly appeared recently, it's unclear if the desired plants even exist there.

If you came to Treant Forest and couldn't find the plants you were commissioned to gather, it would just be wasted effort.

In that case, it was definitely better to go to another forest where the plants, mushrooms, or other things you wanted to gather actually grew.

(Naming it Treant's Forest might have been a mistake. People might come looking for Treant and end up here. ...Were those guys at what looked like a campsite too?)

While these thoughts crossed his mind, Set landed a short distance from where the campsite was believed to be... on the edge of the forest.
