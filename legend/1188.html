The existence of the Ambrith was promptly announced by the Guild.

Additionally, the characteristics of the Umbris that Rei and the others had observed—that it likely possessed no self-awareness akin to a living being, and that it could burrow into the ground and move underground—were likewise made public. Naturally, upon hearing this, the adventurers found themselves scratching their heads.

They understood it could fly, given its mist-like nature.

But the ability to move underground was completely unexpected. It made them wonder if they had overlooked it even when passing close by before.

...That said, there were none—well, perhaps not none, but very few—with abilities capable of detecting such an underground-moving entity.

If only they could sense magic, there might be a way to track it down... Even the few who could sense magic sighed.

Even then, it was uncertain if such individuals existed... Probably, it was better information to have confirmed Ambrith's existence rather than searching for it under the assumption it might exist.

Furthermore, some high-rank adventurers, including Rei, possessed the ability to break through Ambrith's high magical defense and inflict damage. Consequently, many were more proactive in searching for Ambrith than they had been the previous day.

But naturally, the adventurers' job wasn't just to search for Ambrus.

The primary reason Ambris was considered dangerous was its ability to create leader-type humanoid monsters.

As long as Ambrus remained undefeated, it was certain that hordes of monsters were being created in real time.

Dealing with those swarms was necessary, and beyond that, adventurers had numerous other requests to fulfill: subjugation, escort, gathering, and more.

With other requests piling up, they couldn't pour all their efforts into Ambris-related matters.

Amidst the increasingly hectic activity... Rei, Vihera, and Set searched the skies for Ambrus.

Of course, if it were to sink into the ground and move underground like Rei and the others had seen, they wouldn't be able to find it.

However, it was clear from the incident three hundred years ago that Ambrith's movement wasn't necessarily confined to the underground.

Given that, it was highly likely he was moving through the air.

"Well, I don't know if we'll actually find him,"

Set purred in agreement as Rei muttered this while gazing out over the grassland spreading below.

Vihera, too, hung from Set's front paws while surveying the surroundings.

Set flew through the blue sky, almost cloudless as summer should be.

"It's not that we don't know if we can find it—it's more accurate to say we can't find it. ...Honestly, we won't find it just searching aimlessly like this, right? And even if we did find it..."

"Unless there are adventurers nearby capable of attacking Ambris, including me, we'll just let it slip right past us, huh?"

"Yeah. Though even setting that aside, searching for Ambris is probably necessary regardless."

"I suppose so."

Searching for Ambrith naturally meant searching around Gilm, and if monster swarms were moving, finding them would be inevitable.

It's true that this also helps us discover monster groups.

...Though, to be fair, since Rei and the others fly, they often spot packs too.

In fact, today alone they spotted a group of goblins and werewolves moving together and wiped them out from the air using fire magic.

Rei had briefly considered wanting some werewolf materials, but gathering them proved incredibly troublesome. In the end, he simply handed the materials over to adventurers passing nearby.

Those adventurers cheered at their unexpected luck... but upon seeing how much effort it took to gather the corpses, and especially how much usable material had been burned away, they were stunned.

Still, regardless of the materials, many of the proof-of-kill parts and magic stones were intact, so in the end, they made a substantial profit.

"In the end, finding Ambrith requires something else... luck, I suppose."

"Luck? ...Unfortunately, my luck is anything but good, you know?"

"Same here. I get dragged into all sorts of trouble wherever I go, and then when I finally get back to Gilm, something like Ambrith shows up."

Rei, perched on Set's back, was fed up with his tendency to get caught up in trouble... no, it was more like he had a talent for attracting trouble.

(Huh? But if Ambrith had appeared when I wasn't there, Gilm would've suffered major damage, right?)

He pondered this while searching for the source of the current trouble, and it was indeed true.

After all, if Ambris had appeared while Rei wasn't in Gilm, that would naturally mean Set wasn't there either. Without Set, there was no way to conduct a wide-area search using his power.

The number of the swarm Rei crushed from the sky alongside Set was considerable.

Of course, considering the abilities of the adventurers in Gilm, there would be those who could defeat the swarm Rei annihilated in a direct confrontation.

But those who fought would undoubtedly be exhausted.

It was precisely because Rei possessed immense magical power that he could annihilate the monster hordes from above like a carpet bombing, without concern for the magical energy consumed.

Even when using large-scale magic capable of eliminating entire monster swarms, the mana consumed was only a fraction of the total within Rei.

And whatever magic Rei did expend would be quickly replenished once he rested or slept.

It wasn't arrogance or anything like that; Rei judged that, specifically for this incident, it was precisely because of his own power that Gilm still had some leeway.

He didn't think Gilm would be destroyed instantly without Rei... but even so, there wouldn't have been enough leeway for merchants to come from Abuero.

Not all merchants had made it to Gilm, and some who had arrived before the turmoil began were still unable to leave.

But for ambitious merchants, this was also an opportunity to profit.

While other merchants feared the danger and avoided Gilm, they alone ventured there. Naturally, the profits were substantial.

Of course, if they were discovered by a horde of monsters, annihilation was highly likely—it was akin to gambling with their lives.

But precisely because of that, the rewards for success were substantial. For merchants with ambition but not much capital, the current Gilm was also a place to make money.

"Rei, over there."

As Rei pondered the merchants, Vihera's voice reached his ears.

Vihera said "over there," but hanging from Set's front legs, Rei couldn't tell exactly what spot she meant.

Still, when he looked down, he saw a carriage overturned on the ground, its cargo spilled and scattered around it.

The driver, presumably, and the adventurer guard were desperately fighting goblins.

Just seeing that made it obvious what had happened, no need to wonder.

(Talk about speaking of the devil. ...Well, it wasn't exactly a rumor, but still.)

Thinking this, Rei leapt down from Set's back.

Normally, the quickest and most reliable way to wipe out a goblin horde would be to cast magic from above.

But this time, it was a merchant, a carriage, and their guards being attacked by goblins.

Using magic from above onto the ground in this situation would inevitably involve those people too.

Given that, what Rei had to do, though troublesome, was descend to the ground, protect the merchants, and defeat the goblins.

Using Sleipnir's boots as usual to slow his descent, he drew weapons from the Misty Ring.

Holding the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left, Rei landed a short distance away from the merchants.

"Whoa!"

Had he landed near the merchants, he might have been attacked instantly by them and their guards.

With one merchant and two guards, it was clearly a situation where they couldn't defeat the goblins. And then, something suddenly appeared right beside them. It was only natural.

Rei understood this perfectly well, which was precisely why he had landed a short distance away.

"Covering you."

He muttered, swinging his right hand's Death Scythe at the goblin before him while thrusting his left hand's Twilight Spear forward.

At this point, the merchants' safety was practically assured. But then, powerful reinforcements arrived from above—Set and Vihera—and it took only a few minutes for the goblin horde to be annihilated.

"Thank you! You really saved us!"

A merchant man, perhaps in his early twenties, bowed deeply to Rei and Vihera.

Beside him, the two guards bowed as well.

"Merchants willing to come to Gilm in the current situation are precious. ...Still, bringing only two guards shows you underestimate the frontier too much."

Laying aside the blood on Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, Rei addressed the merchant, who responded with a troubled smile.

"I know hiring more guards would be better, but we only have one carriage. There's a limit to how many guards we can fit. And of course, we need space for the goods too."

Hearing that, Rei couldn't press the matter further.

If this were a time without danger, he might have had the guards walk alongside the carriage, matching its slow pace.

But doing that now would be nothing short of suicide.

They needed to minimize time spent outside and hurry to reach Gilm as quickly as possible.

Naturally, this meant they might arrive at Gilm during the hours when the gates were normally closed. However, Daskar, as the lord of the domain, was also concerned about Gilm's current state.

Merchants, in particular, needed as much protection as possible. Normally, those arriving outside hours would be made to spend the night by the gate. But for some time now, even in the middle of the night, if a merchant arrived, the gate was opened immediately and permission to enter Gilm was granted.

This was a decision impossible in ordinary towns or villages due to fear of bandits... but in this frontier, those considering banditry were few, if not nonexistent.

Powerful monsters roamed the surroundings of Gilm, and on top of that, the Ambrith incident had the hordes stirring.

If someone could actually pull off banditry in such conditions, they'd likely have the skill to earn a living more easily as an adventurer.

Above all, even if bandits did consider attacking Gilm, the city itself was home to many skilled adventurers. Its guards and knights, despite being in the frontier, possessed considerable strength.

Furthermore, Gilm has its own underworld, and those involved knew that causing trouble in Gilm now would be suicide.

Such individuals also stand to lose if Gilm falls, so they cooperate voluntarily.

"I get that part. ...So, what do we do now? The carriage seems to be beyond repair."

The carriage where Rei's gaze fell lay overturned on its side.

If that were all, Rei and Set could manage it together, but whether from the impact of the fall or the aftermath of the battle, the carriage's axle was broken.

Fortunately, the carriage had only tipped over, and the horse pulling it was unharmed... Still, with the carriage flipped like that, they'd have to abandon most of the cargo.

"Yeah, it's a shame. ...But I guess we're lucky we're still alive."

Rei was slightly surprised by the merchant's positive words.

With damage this extensive, as a merchant, he'd suffered a huge loss... no, it was likely a deficit so severe he might have to shut down his business.

And yet, a smile played on the man's lips.

As if showing weakness before his benefactor went against his very pride.

"...Interesting."

"Yes?"

The merchant looked puzzled at Rei's unexpected words.

"No, nothing. ...Well, since I'm here anyway, I'll escort you to Gilm."

The merchant couldn't hide his surprise at the abrupt words coming from Rei's mouth.

This merchant, too, was heading to Gilm with ambition, so he knew of Rei and Set.

He didn't seem to know about Vihera, though... but since Rei had started traveling with Vihera, he'd barely appeared in public, so that wasn't surprising.

The merchant wondered why such skilled adventurers would take on work that didn't pay well... but for now, Rei had the two escorts help extract the goblin's magic stones to fund the merchant's journey.

Watching this, Set kept watch over their surroundings, while Vihera, thinking Rei's whims had started again, nonetheless helped extract the magic stones from the goblins.
