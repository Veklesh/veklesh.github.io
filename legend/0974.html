"What? They're still chasing us!?"

Mireine shouted as she ran through the forest, and Johanna turned around, snarling back in annoyance.

"They're still chasing us! Honestly, what a bunch of persistent jerks. Am I that irresistible or something?!"

Johanna shouted back, mixing in a joke, while the former guerrilla fighter smiled and spoke up.

"Charming is charming, but I'm not sure about being charming as bait. In the sense of tender meat, you know?"

"Well, we're all muscle and tough, so we probably aren't much to their liking."

"Silly! Do you really think Cyclopes care whether something tastes good or bad?! They'll eat anything as long as it fills their bellies!"

Though he couldn't have heard their conversation, a roar mixed with irritation erupted from behind.

(Ecuril, why aren't you coming back? Could you have been taken down? ...No, if that happened, all five Cyclopes would be chasing us... Could it be that red Cyclops? Please, stay safe.)

The faces of the party members she'd left behind to distract the cyclops flashed through Mireine's mind.

Right now, she had the breathing room to talk while running precisely because Ecuril had been shooting arrows at the cyclops, hindering its pursuit.

But because of that, Ekril had no choice but to stay behind and still hadn't caught up to them.

Running on while worrying about that party member, she soon saw the place where the horses and carriages were kept. ...But what she saw there was the sight of those who had stayed behind to guard the horses and carriages fighting two cyclopes.

Well, to be precise, it wasn't so much fighting as desperately holding their ground by focusing solely on defense.

"What!?"

There weren't just five Cyclopes.

As this realization hit her, Mireine was overcome by a strong sense of urgency.

Two Rank C Cyclops monsters.

Given the group present, defeating them wouldn't be easy, but they weren't opponents they expected to lose to if they fought.

But that was assuming a normal fight. If asked whether they could defeat those two before the five Cyclops behind them caught up, the answer was no.

"Mireine, what do we do!?"

Surnin, who had followed them, shouted while raising his staff.

Jolted back to her senses by Rei's voice, Mireine frantically scanned her surroundings.

The rampaging Cyclops must have done it. The carriage was already destroyed, and all the horses lay dead.

With the horses' mobility, escaping the Cyclops wouldn't have been difficult, but the horses weren't free.

They had been tied to nearby trees to prevent them from wandering off.

Under such circumstances, escaping the cyclops was impossible. A single blow from its club ended all the horses' lives.

(Fighting here would surely mean annihilation. So, should we all flee in separate directions? That would offer the highest survival rate, but...)

Normally, he would make a decision instantly, but this time he hesitated because of the red Cyclops that had caused them to flee.

It was likely a being capable of commanding Cyclopes, creatures that normally lived alone and never in groups.

The first sighting had been of a single one, and the guild quest Mireine and the others had accepted was also for a single cyclops.

But now that we'd come all the way to the forest, we'd found five Cyclops.

Plus the red cyclops, and then two more cyclopses where they fled to.

This meant there was a high probability of other Cyclopes lurking nearby.

It took only a few seconds to reach that conclusion. Ultimately, the choice Mireine made was...

"Everyone, scatter in different directions! And whoever can, get to Gilm... tell an adventurer with stronger combat power about this!"

Once Mireine made her decision, action followed swiftly.

Everyone present scattered into the forest at once.

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

With a roar that seemed to say "Don't run!" one of the two Cyclopes charged into the forest...

"Don't you dare show me your back! Don't underestimate me! Shockwave!"

With those words, Mireine's longsword swung down, striking the Cyclops that had shown its back.

Though the Cyclops possessed tough skin, the shockwave coursed through its body, unaffected.

This was Mireine's signature skill: channeling magic-generated shockwaves through her longsword and driving them into her opponent.

The effect resembled Vihera's Demon-Infused Palm, though its power was several levels weaker.

Perhaps it would be more accurate to say the depth of the impact inflicted was shallower.

Furthermore, the fact that the shockwave traveled through the body meant that the larger the body and the greater the surface area in contact with the ground, the easier it was for the shock to dissipate. In that sense, facing a Cyclops nearly four meters tall, it was impossible to achieve a one-hit kill.

Even so, the Cyclops couldn't possibly emerge unscathed from such a skill.

Not only the Cyclops hit by the shockwave, but the other Cyclops also judged Mireine to be dangerous and halted its pursuit.

If they left Mireine alone now, she might unleash a barrage of attacks from behind.

Even a Cyclops's head could grasp that much.

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

"GAAAAAAH!"

Both Cyclopes closed the distance toward Mireine, clubs in hand.

'Wind Arrow!'

A wind arrow was fired toward one of the cyclopes.

For Mireine, there was no need to wonder who had done it.

"Surunin!? Why are you still here...?"

She leapt sideways to dodge the club swung at her, shouting as she moved.

Landing her feet on the ground, she glared at the Cyclops but glanced away for just an instant. There, as Mireine had expected, stood Surnin, staff in hand.

No, that wasn't all. Johanna was also there, thrusting her spear toward the cyclops, which had momentarily staggered from the Wind Arrow.

The spearhead pierced the Cyclops' body, but... Johanna's strength wasn't enough to drive it in deeply. Only half the spearhead was embedded.

Frowning momentarily at the unexpectedly hard skin and muscles of the Cyclops, Johanna forcefully swept the spearhead sideways.

As she pulled the tip out, slicing shallowly through the Cyclops' torso, and brought it back to her hand, Johanna spoke.

"You're trying to play the hero, sacrificing yourself for everyone else. That's not like you."

"Shut up! What about your promise to Rent? You're going to be late if you keep this up!"

Johanna shouted back at Mireine, who was dodging another swing of the club, while keeping the Cyclops at bay with her spear.

"Promise? Oh, right, he said he'd treat me to something. But if I'm late, Rent'll just find someone else! Besides, if I abandon Mireine here, I'd never be able to face Set-chan!"

Using the shaft of her spear to parry the swinging club, she launched a counterattack.

That sharp thrust pierced the cyclops' body just as before, but just as before, only half the tip sank in.

"Surnin! You get out of here now! You're a magician to begin with—slow and clumsy!"

"Unfortunately, that's not possible. We need to wait for Ekril to return, and the Cyclops isn't particularly strong against magic to begin with. Besides... if I leave, what do you plan to do about the five coming up behind us? With magic, we might still stand a chance against them!"

Surnin declared, wand in hand, glancing nervously behind him.

Even in the midst of battle, the sound of five cyclopes approaching from that direction continued to echo.

Ideally, defeating the two cyclopes in front before they caught up would allow them to confront the five approaching from behind. But cyclopes were monsters ranked C—a level where even a full C-rank party fought them evenly.

Even with Johanna present, they lacked Ekril's rear support fire, and now faced two additional Cyclops.

No matter how you looked at it, fighting them head-on was impossible.

(Then we have no choice but to fight by unconventional means. To buy time until Ekril returns, and until everyone escapes! Besides, as the eldest, I can't just run away.)

Surnin, staff in hand, glanced back briefly to confirm the Cyclops still had some distance to cover. He concentrated his magic and began chanting.

'Wind, wind, thou art the sharp blade that severs all. Become the invisible blade that slashes the throats and limbs of the enemies blocking my path!'

As the spell was chanted, four wind blades formed around Surnin.

Wind Sword!

As the spell completed, the wind blades were unleashed, each pair heading for the Cyclops's eyes and throat.

"GAAAAAAHHH!"

"GAAAAAAHHH!"

The cyclops fighting Mireine parried the wind blades with the club in its hand.

The weapon the Cyclops wielded wasn't a properly crafted club, but rather a tree uprooted from the ground or a thick branch snapped off nearby.

Naturally, such makeshift weapons couldn't possibly block the wind blades, and the club was cleanly severed.

However, severing the club caused the wind blade to veer off course, flying off in an unexpected direction. It sliced through several trees and branches growing behind the Cyclops as it flew past.

The Cyclops itself only suffered a minor scratch on its cheek.

Meanwhile, the Cyclops fighting Johanna had been focusing on her spear, which had a longer reach, causing its reaction to be delayed.

Though Surnin's wind blade failed to slit the Cyclops' throat, it succeeded in carving a deep gash into its massive single eye.

"Haah, haah, haah..."

The magic just now drained a considerable amount of mana from Surnin as well, causing him to breathe heavily.

Still, thanks to his reckless gamble, he succeeded in depriving the Cyclops of vision in one eye.

An eyeball was a valuable material even among Cyclops, but it was something he could never trade for his own life.

Regardless, Surnin's magic had blinded one cyclops, rendering it powerless.

Of course, it had only lost its sight; it was still rampaging, swinging its club wildly, so it wasn't completely incapacitated.

But in the current situation, even partially incapacitating one cyclops was a huge achievement.

"Mireine, hurry and take care of that one over there! They're coming from behind too, so hurry!"

"I know!"

Shouting simultaneously, Mireine and Johanna launched a coordinated attack on the remaining, still-intact cyclops.

Their actions were perfectly synchronized, making it hard to believe these were the same two who usually clashed.

If an outsider saw them now, they might easily mistake them for close friends who were always in sync.

Of course, the two themselves would never admit it, loudly pointing out each other's flaws instead.

Mireine's longsword and Johanna's spear swung down, aiming to finish off the Cyclops.

But the Cyclops was a Rank C monster and incredibly tenacious.

The wounds inflicted by Surnin weren't healing instantly like the red cyclops', but the blood flowing from the wounds was visibly decreasing.

It was recovering, albeit slowly.

Desperate to finish it while it was still bleeding, Mireine and Johanna launched a fierce assault.

Mireine's longsword slashed the Cyclops' skin, cutting into its flesh.

Johanna's spearhead pierced the Cyclops' skin, gouging its flesh.

But... even so, they couldn't kill the Cyclops with a single blow.

They might have been able to defeat it eventually, but what was needed now was to kill the Cyclops as quickly as possible... and time flowed mercilessly onward.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAARGH!"

Hearing that roar from behind, Surnin turned with a stern expression.

With his breathing finally steady, he gripped his staff. The wind arrows he had finished chanting now hovered around him.

There were over ten of them.

While their attack power wasn't particularly high, the Wind Arrows compensated with blinding speed.

He aimed to pierce the Cyclops's massive single eye with them... but in the next instant, he saw a club hurtling straight toward him.

A direct hit was inevitable; evasion was impossible.

Making that judgment, he fired a Wind Arrow at the club to deflect its trajectory.

The club, deflected by nearly all the Wind Arrows, flew past Surnin's side and struck the Cyclops' head, which had been lashing out after its single eye was sliced open.

"GAAAH!?"

The cyclops screamed and collapsed to the ground.

Then, the cyclops that Mireine and Johanna had been fighting suddenly lost its balance and dropped to its knees. The next instant, it collapsed to the ground, blood spurting from its single eye, nose, and mouth.

Behind the fallen cyclops stood a figure with one hand extended forward.

A figure clad in a thin garment like those worn by dancers or prostitutes, with greaves on its legs and gauntlets on its hands.

The expression on their face, anticipating the struggle, could almost be described as lewd.

The only one present who knew who this person was... was Johanna.

"Lady Vihera!?"

Johanna's cry echoed throughout the area.
