The cry heard from beyond the thicket seemed to possess a certain charm that somehow drained one's strength.

Even so, Rei , who drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring, remained vigilant against the cry.

It wasn't just Rei. Elena, Marina, Vihera, Ara, and Byune all felt the same way.

Yet... among the group, only Set and Yellow seemed to gaze toward the bushes without any particular sign of tension.

As if they knew the source of the cry from beyond the bushes wasn't a dangerous monster.

Glancing sideways at the two of them, Rei wondered inwardly.

(It managed to stay this close without me or Set sensing it at all, so it must be a monster with high stealth abilities. That's fine... but why isn't Set on guard? It's an unknown monster! Normally, he'd definitely be wary.)

Yellow, riding on Set's back, was also surprisingly unconcerned.

In terms of pure combat power, Yellow wasn't particularly strong.

Yellow possessed excellent defensive abilities, like the power to vanish and scales that could deflect attacks from mediocre weapons. However, he was still just a child of the species called Black Dragons... a child so young it could be considered newborn by dragon standards.

Expecting offensive power from such a child—or rather, an entity closer to an infant—was probably misguided.

"Who's there? Show yourself!"

Rei called out from beyond the thicket, but the bushes only rustled; there was no sign of any monster emerging.

"Gurururuu, gururuu? Gururu"

"Kyuu! Kyuu! Kyuu-kyuu!"

Set, with Yellow on his back, cut in front of Rei, who was holding the Death Scythe.

「Set？」

Seeing how unconcerned Set seemed about any danger, Rei turned his gaze back to the bushes.

After a few seconds of thought, he finally lowered his guard, releasing the stance that had kept the Death Scythe poised to strike at any moment.

"Is that okay?"

Vihera, who had also generated magic claws on her gauntlets to respond instantly to anything, asked. Rei nodded, his gaze fixed on Set and Yellow.

"Yeah. Set and Yellow aren't that wary here. Probably means there's no need to be cautious. Right?"

"...Mm. Yellow's combat strength isn't as high as Set's, but his instincts are sharp."

Muttering, Elena also lowered her Mirage.

Normally, Ara would follow Elena's lead, but Ara also served as Elena's bodyguard.

Given that, she couldn't lower her weapon here, even if there was a slight possibility of danger to Elena.

Byune also hadn't lowered her weapon, but her actions stemmed from the intention to launch a preemptive strike if danger arose.

"They were making sounds like 'Por' or 'Pororo'... Do you recognize those cries?"

Watching Set and Yellow desperately calling out toward the bushes, Rei asked Marina, who knew this forest best.

But Marina shook her head at Rei's question and spoke.

"I'm afraid not. When I was in this settlement, there weren't any monsters or animals with cries like that. But even then, I didn't know every monster in the forest."

After saying that much, Marina paused before continuing.

"Considering how long it's been since I left the forest, it wouldn't be surprising if new monsters had arrived in the meantime."

Nearly a century had passed since Marina left the settlement. And in nearly a century, the forest's ecosystem could easily change.

If what lived there were monsters rather than animals, the likelihood of the ecosystem changing would be even higher.

That's precisely why Marina had absolutely no idea what kind of monster lurked beyond the thicket.

She could only guess, based on the cries, that it probably wasn't a particularly vicious monster.

It was precisely because she thought that Marina lowered the bow she had been holding.

"Guruu, gurururuu... Guruu"

"Kyuu! Kyu-kyu! Kyuuu!"

"Polo? Polur? Polu?"

This exchange continued for several minutes before Rei and the others.

When they finally noticed the bushes shaking more violently than before, Rei spoke up.

"Looks like it's finally making an appearance. ...Even with Set here, the fact it hasn't fled means it's definitely a monster with some kind of story behind it."

Though they'd half-expected it to be harmless, not knowing what kind of monster would emerge, all eyes remained fixed on the bushes.

And so, the first thing to emerge from the bushes was... blue.

A small one... smaller than even a Yellow, but this monster had a coat that was blue all over.

Another eye-catching feature was its forehead.

An oval-shaped red gem-like object was embedded in its forehead.

The monster peered out from the bushes, surveying its surroundings with just its head exposed. Eventually, deciding there was no danger, it emerged fully from the foliage.

Size-wise, it was about a size larger than a regular squirrel.

However, like its face, its entire body was covered in blue fur, and a soft-looking tail took up nearly half its body length.

But the most distinctive feature of all was undoubtedly its tail.

Not only was it about half its body length, but it also had multiple tails.

"What is this monster... I've never seen one like it before."

Rei muttered, and the others, except Marina, nodded in agreement.

Elena, who had lived through battlefields as a princess general; Vihera, who had lived as a princess of the Bestir Empire; and Byune, who had experience diving into dungeons alone as a thief—these three should have known more than the average person, yet none of them had ever seen a monster conversing with Set, Yellow, Guruguru, Kyuukyuu, and Poruporu.

Furthermore, Ara, of course, had never seen a blue-haired squirrel-like monster either.

"Huh? Wait... Could it be...?"

Rei noticed the oval-shaped red gem-like object on the forehead of the blue squirrel monster frolicking with Yellow, and something suddenly came to mind.

However, this wasn't knowledge he'd gained since arriving in Elgin. It was something he'd encountered back in Japan through games, manga, and novels.

"Carbuncle?"

"Poruru?"

At Rei's mutter, the blue squirrel monster turned its gaze toward him, as if asking, "Did you call?"

(Did it react to my voice? Meaning it understood it was being called... Does it understand human speech?)

Monsters capable of understanding human speech weren't common, but they certainly weren't nonexistent.

High-rank monsters like Set or Yellow might not be able to speak human words, but hearing and understanding them isn't unusual.

"Correct. You're quite observant for such a rare monster."

Hearing that voice, Rei turned toward it.

There stood Marina, smiling with satisfaction.

"Huh? So this monster really is a Carbuncle?"

"Yes. Though... it's quite different from the Carbuncle I've seen before. Especially the tail—the one I saw only had one."

Hearing Marina's words, the experienced adventurer thought it was different, but still directed his gaze toward the blue squirrel... the carbuncle.

As Marina said, it had multiple tails, making it vastly different from the Carbuncle Rei knew from games and manga back in Japan.

"Probably the same rare species as Set. The lack of ferocity—could it be because the World Tree was healed? Or is it because it's a high-rank monster called a Carbuncle?"

"A high-rank monster?"

"Yes. A Rank B monster. And since it's a rare species, it naturally gets treated as one rank higher, making it equivalent to a Rank A monster."

Listening to Marina's explanation, Rei gently reached out toward the Carbuncle.

A short distance away, the others watched Rei with envious eyes.

The Carbuncle, sniffing at Rei's fingers, looked incredibly adorable, and many of them wished they could pet it.

"Poru? Porororo..."

The Carbuncle, which had been sniffing Rei's finger, suddenly kicked off the ground and leaped onto Rei's shoulder.

"...It's quite friendly. Is it really a monster?"

Rei muttered in amazement at the Carbuncle's lack of shyness, but Vihera, looking envious, spoke up.

"Of course it is. An animal with a gem embedded in its forehead? That's not exactly something you'd see every day."

"Still, it's surprisingly friendly. It's a wild monster, so it wouldn't be strange if it attacked normally."

"Carbuncles are a naturally docile species. While they possess a special ability to reflect magic attacks, they can't use offensive skills themselves. ...Despite that, they're quite agile. They might attack if someone targets the gem on their forehead, but they basically never initiate an attack."

She added "basically" because they do occasionally attack animals for food.

However, while Carbuncles are omnivorous, they prefer eating nuts.

Unless extremely hungry, they won't attack living creatures.

Listening to Marina's explanation, Rei stroked the Carbuncle perched on his shoulder.

As he stroked its tail, he noticed it had nine tails.

"Not a nine-tailed fox, but a nine-tailed Carbuncle? ...You really are a rare species, aren't you? Is that why you're unaffected by this forest?"

"Polo?"

At Rei's words, the carbuncle tilted its head.

(Huh? Does it not fully understand my words? Still, that's impressive enough.)

Its fur felt as smooth as Set's, making him want to stroke it forever.

"Poro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-

But it seemed the Carbuncle had no intention of staying on Rei's shoulder forever; it leapt and landed on the ground.

Its next destination was Elena.

Whipping its nine tails, it gained speed running across the ground before leaping onto Elena's shoulder.

For a split second, Ara reflexively raised her power axe toward the Carbuncle, but she managed to hold back.

Elena's smile as she accepted the Carbuncle probably played a big part in that.

"Huh? You want to play with me now? You're full of energy. Here."

She stroked the Carbuncle's throat as it sat on her shoulder, and it purred happily.

That gesture made the Carbuncle, which looked somewhat like a squirrel, seem more like a feline creature.

"Polo! Hey, where'd you go? Polo!"

The sudden voice from somewhere made the Carbuncle on Elena's shoulder twitch in response.

Turning its gaze toward the source of the sound, the Carbuncle let out a high-pitched cry.

"Poro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro-ro!"

The Carbuncle's cry echoed around them.

(Could "Polo" be this guy? Maybe it's because he chirps "Polo-Polo"? ...That's too obvious.)

While watching the carbuncle that seemed to be named Polo, Rei suddenly realized something.

This place was within the Barrier of Confusion.

Meaning, whoever was calling this Carbuncle named Polo had somehow entered the Labyrinth Barrier.

Rei had heard that the World Tree's weakened state made it easier to get lost inside the Barrier of Confusion, and perhaps that was how they had entered.

But at the same time, since the nine-tailed carbuncle called Polo seemed to be friendly, perhaps it wasn't such a bad person after all.

With the sound of rustling bushes, he gradually sensed someone approaching him... and Rei quietly reached for his hidden weapon, the Nebula's Eye.

This magic item, capable of generating arrowheads when infused with magic power, wasn't a long-range weapon like the Death Scythe or the Thorn Spear, which were Rei's specialties.

But its ability to be used inconspicuously was a major advantage.

"Pororo?"

Polo turned his gaze toward Rei.

Though it had sensed the magic, it took no action, perhaps understanding that Rei wouldn't act recklessly.

In any case, after glancing at Rei for just an instant, Pororo turned back toward the bushes and let out a cry.

"Pororororororororororororororororororororororororororororororororororororororororororor

And as if responding to Polo's cry, an old man emerged from the bushes.
