After the battle with the Sand Serpent—if you could even call it that—Rei and his escort for Augusto's party were able to traverse the desert without encountering any monsters.

"If we keep going like this, the rocky desert will soon disappear completely, leaving only sand as far as the eye can see. Gosh is a city that developed around oases, with several oases forming its core. ...Well, there are various troublesome aspects, but fundamentally, it's a good city."

As Zarust spoke from the driver's seat, controlling the camel, Rei, perched on Set's back, scanned the surroundings.

Now that he mentioned it, compared to where Rei had defeated the Sand Serpent, the proportion of sand was significantly higher.

"If you're building a city, I'd think a rocky desert with solid ground beneath would be better than a sandy one."

"I understand that, but without an oasis, you can't build towns or villages in a desert."

It was an argument he couldn't help but agree with.

Water sources were essential for human habitation, which was precisely why towns and villages formed around oases.

Those oases were scattered throughout the desert, and it was only natural that some were large while others were small.

And the size of the oasis determined the scale of the town or village that could form around it.

Naturally, the area of an oasis is finite, and the number of people who could benefit from it was limited.

Furthermore, considering the number of merchants and adventurers who arrived there, it was impossible for a town to sustain the maximum possible population.

"...Considering those circumstances, Gosh possesses several large oases. It's one of the largest... no, the largest city in this Ferris Desert."

Zalust stated this, but his concerned gaze drifted backward... toward the carriage carrying Augusto.

"Is there some reason for that?"

He'd vaguely expected trouble when he'd initially butted in, and compared to the merchants who'd gathered around him in Gilm, Augusto was far more likable.

Precisely because of that, he hadn't intended to get involved in any disputes, but the flow of the conversation led him to ask.

...Even knowing Zarust had skillfully steered the conversation to that point, Rei didn't get angry.

"Ah. Actually, a certain merchant guild has been flexing its muscles in Gosh lately. Mr. Augusto is opposing them and getting all sorts of harassment in return."

"Ah... so it's related to that."

Though memories of the Azott Trading Company flashed through his mind, Rei concluded it couldn't be that bad. Zarust nodded at Rei's words.

"It's the Elema Trading Company. They were originally a mid-tier company in Gosh, but after the current head passed the reins to his son, they rapidly expanded their influence. Now, that son is in conflict with Mr. Augusto, and they're trying to bring him under their wing to contain him."

"Sounds like a familiar story."

"Yeah. It's a common story, but actually experiencing it is a whole different ballgame. Cargo that should have been delivered didn't arrive, orders were abruptly canceled, rumors were spread... Honestly, I think Mr. Augusto is doing remarkably well under those circumstances."

"Under those circumstances, it's surprising Zarust agreed to be August's escort. He's not even his exclusive bodyguard, right?"

Most adventurers wouldn't take a job from someone mired in such trouble.

Seeing Rei's expression, which seemed to say just that, Zarust snorted in displeasure.

"You're on that side too? ...Not that it's a bad thing, but I was taken under the wing by Mr. Augusto's father. I don't want to do anything that would betray that kindness. The same goes for the others serving as his guards now."

"The newbies too?"

Zalust sighed softly at Rei's implied disapproval of involving inexperienced adventurers whose skills weren't up to par.

"This was sudden. We were desperately short on manpower. I did pick the most capable ones among the new recruits. Besides... many of them had been asking to come along for quite some time."

Even as he said this, his slightly furrowed brow suggested Zarust understood it was reckless.

Still, selecting only those with the strength to defeat desert monsters—at least desert goblins—was Zarust's small gesture of sincerity.

"See that tree growing over there?"

As if changing the subject, Zarust pointed to a tree growing out of the sand.

The sight of a tree growing from sand surprised Rei a little.

In Rei's understanding, the only plants that grew in the desert were basically cacti.

"That's... a tree? Why in the desert?"

"It... looks like a tree, doesn't it? Well, it definitely is a tree, but it's a magical plant. A carnivorous one, at that."

Zalust explained about the tree in the distance.

It was said to be a carnivorous plant found only in the Ferris Desert. It emitted a scent monsters found appealing, luring nearby monsters closer, then put them to sleep with the scent and consumed them as nutrients.

"Monsters?"

"No, it's a magical plant. It doesn't have any magic stones either."

What defines a monster is, of course, the magic stone that resides within every monster.

Since no magic stone exists, the entity Zarust pointed out must be a magical plant, not a monster.

Understanding this, Rei still wondered if such a magical plant, even if not a monster, might be dangerous...

"Don't worry. That magical plant... the Tira Tree poses absolutely no threat to humans. In fact, even if a human approaches it, there's no problem at all."

"...What kind of tree is that? Doesn't it seem too convenient for humans?"

"I think so too. But it's also true that the Tira Tree keeps the number of monsters in the Felis Desert somewhat in check, so we can't carelessly interfere."

It attracts monsters and kills them. Yet it poses no threat whatsoever to humans.

It was truly a magical plant that worked perfectly for humans.

"Then, that tree... the Tira tree, right? If we could increase its numbers, wouldn't it make desert travel easier than it is now?"

"I'm not sure. As Rei mentioned, the Elema Trading Company is planning to do just that... but Mr. Augusto opposes it."

"Why?"

It reduces monster numbers and doesn't harm humans. Rei understood Erema Trading Company's reasoning for wanting to increase its numbers.

That's precisely why it was puzzling that Augusto was against it.

"The Tira trees are magical plants. If they increase naturally, that's fine. But if we try to do it intentionally, we can't predict what might happen."

Augusto, leaning out the carriage window, stated this to Rei.

"Is that so? ...I think it'd be beneficial for us to live normally, though."

"Yes. If only that were truly the case..."

Even as he said this, Augusto seemed to contradict his own words, as if certain of something.

(Is there something else?)

Watching Augusto's expression, I wondered as we continued on...

"Grrrr."

Suddenly, Set turned his gaze toward a sand dune slightly off the caravan's path and growled.

The sound carried a warning.

"Enemy?"

Rei, who had known Set for a long time, instinctively read the emotion behind the growl and quickly reported.

Zalust, who oversaw the caravan's guards, fixed a stern gaze on the direction Set was looking... toward the sand dunes.

Normally, he might hesitate to sound the alarm based solely on Set's cry. But Zarust, having witnessed Rei's abilities firsthand, saw no reason to doubt Rei's complete trust in the situation. Without hesitation, he ordered everyone to be on guard.

"Augusto. What do you say? Should we shake them off or engage them here?"

"As your escort, which do you recommend?"

"Even if we shake them off here, we'd want to avoid being pursued endlessly. Especially since we have to camp tonight, considering the risk of a night attack..."

"You think we should engage them?"

"Yes. If there were no new recruits among the guards, we could counter-ambush whoever launched the night attack... though it's doubtful they'd be careless enough to assume their attackers are monsters."

"They're probably human. Likely bandits."

Rei interjected, cutting into the conversation between Augusto and Zarust.

"...How do you know?"

"Monsters that approach on their own when Set's around are extremely rare. Well, not entirely nonexistent. Like goblins or other stupid ones. But it's more likely to be thieves who can't sense Set's presence. I can't say for sure, though."

Most monsters, sensing the presence of a Rank A monster like Set, would judge him to be an opponent they could never defeat and flee.

When hunting, Set naturally kept his presence hidden. But when acting as a bodyguard, like now, it was different.

He deliberately signaled his presence here to keep monsters at bay.

If something approached despite sensing Set's presence, it was more likely to be a person than a monster.

After hearing Rei's explanation about Set, Zarust made an immediate decision.

"We'll intercept them here. Bandits... Sand Bandits, that is. They'll definitely pursue us relentlessly. It'll be quicker to take them out here than to camp and wait for an ambush. Besides..."

Zalust's gaze naturally fell upon Rei.

If it were just them, facing sand pirates would almost certainly result in casualties... but with Rei and Set, individuals possessing extraordinary power, it was only natural to place hope in their combat strength.

Laying no particular feeling in Zaruust's gaze, Rei simply nodded.

He was hired as a bodyguard because he was favored by Augusto, so fleeing now was simply not an option.

(Still, sand pirates? Well, I suppose it's just the name for thieves operating in the desert. Like mountain bandits or sea pirates.)

Rei tilted his head inwardly at the unfamiliar term "sand thieves," but deciding they were likely just another type of bandit, he knew what needed to be done next.

"Then, should I go scout ahead? If you want, I could wipe them out from above."

"...That's pretty extreme..."

One of the nearby adventurers murmured at the word "annihilate."

He seemed to be a newcomer, clearly startled by Rei's casual mention of killing the sand thieves outright.

But Rei merely shrugged at the adventurer's words.

After all, in the Kingdom of Mireana, Rei had repeatedly destroyed numerous bandit gangs and seized the treasures they had hoarded, earning him the nickname "Bandit Eater."

Naturally, he showed no hesitation when facing thieves and fundamentally held no mercy for them.

"So, what do we do?"

Rei asked Zarust, leaving the newcomer behind. Zarust thought for a moment before shaking his head.

"No. I don't want them to know about Rei and Set. If they find out, they might try to escape."

"...Wait. Is that an insult? You think we couldn't catch up if the thieves fled?"

Having annihilated thieves countless times before, Rei couldn't accept the words coming from Zarust's mouth.

But Zarust opened his mouth, trying to persuade Rei.

"It's not exactly that. But Rei is fighting sand thieves for the first time, right? Don't you think it's better to observe them first, to see what tactics they use? Besides..."

Zalust paused, shifting his gaze toward the new adventurer who had reacted so sensitively to Rei's mention of annihilation earlier.

Drawn by this, Rei also turned his gaze that way, but the adventurer being looked at tilted his head, seemingly unable to understand why he was being stared at.

"We've got a fighter like Rei here. We couldn't let them experience combat during the Sand Serpent incident, so this seemed like the perfect chance... What do you say?"

"...Understood."

He really was too kind. Thinking that, Rei nodded.

"They're here! Sand Pirates!"

One of the adventurers shouted sharply.

Rei turned his gaze toward where the sand pirates were approaching...

"Huh?"

The sight that met his eyes drew a surprised gasp from Rei.

Zalust, Augusto, and the others reacted the same way.

Because... what appeared over the dunes was a ship of considerable size.
