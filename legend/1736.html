"What the hell?! You know who you're messing with, don't you?!"

“Well, even if you say that, we don’t have everything… There’s a limit to the magic items we can provide here.”

While asking passersby for information, Rei and Vihera arrived at the magic item shop... but there was no doubt that some kind of trouble was clearly brewing inside.

“What should we do?” Rei asked Vihera with a glance, but when he saw the look of amusement in her eyes, he immediately let out a resigned sigh.

There was no doubt she was planning to stick her nose into whatever trouble was unfolding inside the shop.

“Just to be sure, what do you want to do?”

“It’s obvious we’re going in, isn’t it?”

Vihera replied instantly to Rei’s words, and with a half-resigned “I guess so…” Ray reached for the door.

Since his goal was to find a magic item, turning back now was not an option for Rei.

And, naturally, Vihera—who was fully intent on getting involved in whatever trouble was brewing inside—had no intention of turning back either.

“Hey, we’re busy right now! Sorry, but come back another time!”

He must have heard the door open. The man said this without even turning around.

What surprised Rei was that he wasn’t told to leave in an imperative tone.

He was yelling, but he was also apologizing in a way that implied, “Sorry about this.”

Seeing that, both Rei and Vihera could somehow tell that the man wasn’t just picking a fight with the clerk for no reason.

That said, Vihera looked a little disappointed to learn that the tough-looking man wasn’t the sole villain here.

“We came to look at magic items, but it seems that’s not going to happen now.”

“That’s right. So, I’m sorry, but… Rei? And Vihera?”

The man, who had turned his back to them, muttered this as he looked at Rei and Vihera.

Then again, since the man was clearly an adventurer, it wasn’t surprising that he recognized Rei and Vihera.

After all, Rei and his party, having conquered the dungeon, were famous figures, especially among other adventurers.

“Normally, I’d rather not get involved in any trouble today…”

“Then just leave the shop right now. I’m sorry, but something troublesome is about to happen.”

“It’s a shame, but while I don’t want to get involved in any trouble, it seems Vihera is intent on getting herself into it. So, if possible, I’d appreciate it if you’d just do as we say before this trouble turns into an even bigger mess.”

Rei... no, to be precise, he must have realized that Vihera absolutely wouldn’t back down.

The man glanced at the clerk, who was glaring at him, and let out a sigh.

He turned his gaze to Rei and Vihera, scratched his head, and spoke.

“To put it simply, I ordered a potion from this shop that’s supposed to be a cure for a rare disease. But even though it’s been nearly half a year, there’s still no sign of it coming in. …And that guy is suffering from the disease even as we speak.”

“Ah, I see. So that’s the story. …In that case, I can understand both sides’ feelings. Just to be clear, you haven’t been slacking off and failed to get that special potion, have you?”

“Of course not.”

Even though he’d been cornered just a moment ago by an adventurer who was bigger and more imposing than himself, the shopkeeper answered without showing the slightest sign of fear.

It wasn’t a matter of putting on a brave face; it seemed to be his natural expression.

“However, since it’s a potion made with relatively rare ingredients and requires a certain level of skill to craft, the alchemists in Golz can’t make it, and there aren’t any alchemists in this area who can either. In other words, we’d have to find an alchemist who can make it and place an order.”

“Well, I suppose so. If we push an alchemist who isn’t skilled enough, it’s bound to end in failure.”

“That’s true. However, in this case, the issue is finding someone with a certain level of skill. I hate to say it, but Golz is the most prosperous town in this area. That means skilled alchemists from the surrounding region naturally tend to gather in Golz… though it’s not a guarantee.”

It’s common for alchemists to use alchemy to create magical items and sell them to make a living.

There are some alchemists who receive financial support from wealthy patrons and focus solely on research, but they are likely in the minority.

Given that lifestyle, it goes without saying that they need a variety of materials.

And the fact that Golz is the most prosperous town in this area meant that materials usable for alchemy were also easy to come by.

Just as there are alchemists who focus solely on research, I can’t say this is an absolute rule, though.

Some alchemists work in the surrounding rural villages or smaller towns because they want to live with their families, or because there are materials they can only obtain in those areas.

Still, when viewed as a whole, the majority of alchemists in this region do tend to gather in Golz.

And since the alchemists gathered in Golz cannot produce it, it is clear that making that specific potion in this area would be extremely difficult.

“So, that means we’d have to go somewhere farther away… to a town that’s just as developed as Golz, or even more so?”

“That’s correct. But of course, that would require a significant amount of funds, and above all, there’s no guarantee when it would arrive. There’s also the possibility of being attacked by monsters or bandits along the way. …Yes, I explained all that, but he just wouldn’t accept it.”

The clerk said this, turning his gaze toward the man, but the man, seemingly unable to contain his frustration, glared at the clerk once more.

“I get what you’re saying, but… my sister is still suffering from her illness right now!”

Watching this, Rei could understand the feelings of both the clerk and the adventurer.

From the clerk’s perspective, having done everything in his power to obtain that special potion, the honest truth was that no matter what was said, there was nothing more he could do.

And as for the man, he simply couldn’t bear to see his sister suffering from her illness.

“...What should we do?”

“It’s hard to say.”

Vihera replied to Rei’s words, sounding bewildered.

It was probably because this was completely different from the kind of commotion Vihera had been expecting.

(I remember seeing a news report about a time when I was in Japan—there was a dispute because a package didn’t arrive on schedule… and it eventually turned violent, but compared to this situation, that was nothing.)

Rei thought this, almost as if trying to escape reality.

Compared to Japan, where transportation networks were well-developed, moving between towns, villages, and cities in Elgin was quite dangerous.

There are problems with monsters and bandits, and the possibility of accidents occurring is by no means low.

Given that, it’s by no means rare for a package you’ve ordered to go missing.

The male adventurer probably understands that too… but still, seeing his sister in pain, he must feel the urge to take his frustration out on the shopkeeper who sold him the potion.

“She… Munes is only ten years old. And yet, even when she’s in pain, she’s always worried about me…”

If the man in front of him is in his mid-twenties, that means his sister is more than ten years younger.

Rei felt a little skeptical about that, but he accepted it as a possibility.

「……Rei」

It was Vihera who called out Rei’s name.

But the way she called Rei's name was different from before.

There was a hint of reliance in her voice.

The fact that the man’s sister was a ten-year-old girl must have had something to do with it.

Vihera had fled the Bestir Empire and eventually made her way to the labyrinth city of Exil, where, by chance, she met Byune.

When she learned that Byune—still just a girl herself—was venturing into dungeons alone, Vihera couldn’t bring herself to abandon her… and eventually, they began traveling together.

That’s precisely why, from Vihera’s perspective, seeing the girl suffering from an illness—and knowing there’s a potion that could cure her, yet being unable to get it to her—is something she simply cannot ignore.

Of course, if there were something he could do himself, Vihera would do it himself.

But in this particular case, it comes down to the distance to the distant town… and the strength required to bring that potion back safely.

“Ah… I guess that means our date is over for today. Is that okay with you?”

Even for Rei, hearing that a small child was suffering made it feel wrong to just look the other way.

If he hadn’t known, he probably wouldn’t have given it a second thought, but now that he knew… even knowing it was somewhat hypocritical, he couldn’t bring himself to abandon them.

“Yes. …Thank you.”

As a token of her gratitude, Vihera gently lifted Rei’s hood and kissed the cheek that was revealed.

...The fact that she kissed his cheek instead of his lips was probably because they were in public.

“……Um…”

“Ah…”

The shopkeeper and the male adventurer stammered at the act that had just taken place right before their eyes.

A kiss on the cheek isn’t exactly that unusual.

In fact, at taverns where you’re a regular, the female staff might even do something like that as a gesture of celebration.

But… this time, the fact that Vihera—a woman of rare beauty—had done such a thing was what had startled both the clerk and the male adventurer.

Of the two, Rei addressed the clerk specifically.

“So, how far away is this town with an alchemist who can make that special potion? And what about the ingredients?”

With those words, the shopkeeper—and the male adventurer—finally seemed to realize what Rei was up to.

They both turned their surprised gazes toward Rei.

(At first I thought he was expressionless like Byune, but I guess that’s not really the case.)

As Rei thought this, the shopkeeper’s expression shifted from surprise to exasperation as he spoke.

“Are you sure about this? I mean, I heard you two travel by griffin, but this isn’t a quest or anything, is it?”

“That’s true. But knowing there’s a child suffering from an illness, do you really think Vihera and I could just go around Golz having fun like this…? Don’t you think?”

Hearing Rei’s words, the clerk seemed to understand.

After a moment’s thought, he spoke.

“This isn’t a formal request. But since you’re going to the trouble of using the Crimson Wings, I don’t think it’s right for you to work for free. If you’d like, I’ll let you take any magic item from this shop—up to a certain price—as payment. What do you say?”

“What are you playing at?”

The adventurer muttered discontentedly at the clerk’s sudden change of heart.

He’d been begging him to do something about it all this time, and the clerk hadn’t paid him the slightest bit of attention—so why was he being so accommodating just because Rei asked? That’s what he was thinking.

But to the shopkeeper, that reaction was only natural.

To begin with, the reason he hadn’t been able to do anything until just now was simply because he lacked the means to do so.

If the clerk had actually been a skilled adventurer… then perhaps things might have been different, but the clerk wasn’t someone who worked out or anything like that—he was just an ordinary person.

So when Rei showed up just then and offered to go to the town with the alchemist in her place, there was no way the shopkeeper wouldn’t be grateful.

It wasn’t that he had any particular feelings for the man and was lying about not having any potions; he simply had no choice but to say that because he genuinely didn’t have any.

“As for the magic items, that’s fine by me since it’s good for us too. …So, where is that town? I’m planning to leave Golz tomorrow, so I’d like a detailed map or something.”

“You’ll be fine as long as you follow the main road. Unfortunately, I don’t have a map.”

Hearing that, Rei recalled that maps were extremely rare in this world.

Especially detailed maps were treated almost like strategic supplies.

In fact, when Rei had borrowed a map from Marina before, she had been thoroughly briefed on the situation and strictly warned not to lose or damage it, let alone make a copy.

After asking the clerk for the location of the town and the name of the shop, and receiving the necessary materials and a letter, Rei looked at Vihera.

“Sorry, Vihera. That’s all for today. I need to head out of Golz for a bit to call Set.”

“I understand. …Be careful.”

Saying that, Vihera flashed a beaming smile, as if to encourage Rei.
