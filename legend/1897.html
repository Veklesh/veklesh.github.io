Rei, having left the guild, was thinking of taking Set for a stroll through town and grabbing a bite to eat at a food stall.

But then...

“Set-chan, it’s been a while, but you’re still as cute as ever. Here, would you like this sandwich? It’s packed with your favorite cheese inside.”

Seeing the person fawning over Set like that, he couldn’t help but look up at the sky.

It wasn’t snowing, but the sky was so overcast that it could rightly be called a cloudy day, with the sun only occasionally peeking through the gaps in the clouds.

When I looked back at Set, sure enough, there was a familiar figure doting on him.

Deciding she couldn’t stay like this forever, Rei reluctantly called out to the person.

“Johanna, it’s been a while.”

“Hey, Set-chan. Want to go play together? Come on, I’ll take better care of you than Milein does. Okay?”

It seemed as though Johanna hadn’t heard Rei’s voice at all… no, not just seemed—she actually hadn’t heard it, and continued to dote on Set.

Rei was wondering what to do when a man who had come from a short distance away suddenly noticed him and called out.

“Rei, have you come back to Gilm!?”

Like Johanna, this man looked familiar to Rei as well.

He was a member of the Guerrilla Unit that Johanna had belonged to during the civil war in the Bestir Empire.

“Ah, you look well too. …Johanna’s the same as ever.”

“Ah, ahahaha. Ahahahahahaha.”

Seeing Johanna still oblivious to Rei’s presence and doting on Set, the man laughed as if to cover something up.

He knew Rei wasn’t the type to get angry over something like this, but seeing Johanna—their de facto leader—acting this way probably stirred up a lot of mixed feelings in the man.

Perhaps feeling a touch of pity for the man, he decided to leave Johanna to Set for the time being and changed the subject.

“Are you guys participating in the kobold hunt?”

“Huh? Oh, yeah. I’ve got enough money to spare, but I just wanted to keep my body in shape.”

“Ah… I bet there are plenty of guys like that.”

The thought of kobolds being killed for the sake of dieting—though strictly speaking, it’s a bit different—left Rei with a rather indescribable feeling.

“Yes. Also, quite a few people who are just killing time join in. There are quite a few who get bored even if they just take a break to get drunk at the tavern or visit a brothel.”

Kobolds killed just to kill time evoke the same indescribable feeling as those killed for weight loss.

Still, whatever the reason, the fact that they’re managing to defeat the kobolds is undeniable.

As long as it reduces the damage done to Gilm, the reason behind it didn’t matter.

“So, why are you at the guild, Rei? Are you planning to take on a kobold extermination quest or something?”

Even as he asked, it was clear he didn’t really believe that was the case.

From a man’s perspective, there was no benefit for Rei—a man with a nickname—to do such a thing… in fact, he probably couldn’t imagine Rei taking on a quest that would only cause trouble.

And since Rei had no intention of accepting a kobold extermination request, that assumption was certainly not wrong.

...That said, if it meant confirming the existence of rare or high-tier kobolds, there was a possibility he might have accepted the extermination request in search of unknown magic stones.

“We’re going to start dismantling the Giganto Turtle tomorrow, so let’s have a meeting about that.”

The dissection of the Giganto Turtle.

The moment they heard Rei’s words, the adventurers around him reacted instantly.

Even when I was talking with Lenora at the guild about the Giganto Turtle’s dissection, the adventurers nearby were trying their best to get their hands on that information.

For those accepting the Kobold extermination quest due to a lack of funds, the Giganto Turtle disassembly must have seemed like a lucrative job.

That said, since the kobold extermination request was of higher priority to Gilm, the reward ended up being quite modest.

As for Rei, although he wasn’t the one paying the reward, he did have some thoughts on the matter—since the reward was low, it meant fewer people would be interested in the job.

That said, considering the Giganto Turtle’s size, it wasn’t something that could be handled in just this winter, so he didn’t let it bother him enough to feel annoyed.

“Huh? Are we finally going to dismantle it?”

“Yeah. We were actually supposed to start before the new year, but I was in Anesis.”

At the mention of “Anesis,” the man turned his gaze toward Johanna, who was gazing fondly at Set with a complicated expression.

When Rei had gone to Anesis, Set was naturally absent from Gilm as well, and as a result, Johanna had developed what could only be called “Set withdrawal symptoms.”

There was no doubt that her current, almost excessive adoration of Set was a reaction to those withdrawal symptoms.

“Oh, that sounds like fun. I’ve actually been interested in the Giganto Turtle.”

“I bet. I know there are plenty of people like that. But we can’t just leave the kobolds alone, can we? Well, considering they don’t seem to understand why we keep attacking Gilm, there’s a chance they might stop attacking us entirely starting tomorrow.”

“Now that you mention it… you’re right.”

“So, the reward for dismantling the Giganto Turtle will be pretty low. If that’s okay with you, come join us. Oh, and even though the reward is small, I plan to let you take a few bits from the dismantled parts as a souvenir. …I don’t know if it’ll be tasty, though.”

As far as Rei knew, turtles weren’t typically eaten.

The exception was the soft-shelled turtle.

In fact, there are regions where sea turtles are commonly eaten.

Still, since the Giganto Turtle was undoubtedly a high-rank monster, there was no way its meat could be bad.

(I’ve seen soft-shelled turtle hot pot on TV, but I’ve never actually eaten it.)

Naturally, he didn’t know how to butcher a turtle, nor did he know how to season it.

Given that, the only thing Rei could do was leave it to a professional chef.

“Anyway, if those terms are acceptable, I’ll accept the request tomorrow morning.”

“I’ll take the job!”

The one who answered Rei’s words wasn’t the man she’d been talking to just a moment ago, but Johanna, who had been admiring Set until just a moment ago.

I don’t know where she’d been listening from, but since she said she’d accept the request, she must have heard that it involved butchering a Giganto Turtle.

What was Johanna thinking when she agreed to take on this job? To Rei, it was obvious without even having to think about it.

“Just so you know, we… well, Set, to be precise, won’t be watching the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle the whole time. After taking a quick look at the situation first, if there don’t seem to be any problems, we’ll probably head somewhere else.”

“That’s fine with me. If it means I can definitely meet Set, I have no intention of passing up that chance.”

Seeing Johanna state that so firmly, Rei couldn’t say anything more.

If Rei was accepting the request fully aware of everything, Rei might find it a bit of a hassle, but he certainly wouldn’t feel burdened by it.

After all, Johanna’s group—being former members of a mobile strike force—possessed considerable strength in terms of raw combat ability.

Even if monsters were drawn by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood, they wouldn’t have to worry about falling behind against most opponents.

“If you put it that way, I have nothing more to say. Are you really sure? For my part, it would be a huge help if Johanna and her group took on this job.”

It was precisely because he had led Johanna and her group as a Guerrilla Unit during the Bestir Empire’s civil war that he could trust them without a shred of doubt.

Johanna and the others, too, had seen Rei fight with their own eyes during the civil war, witnessed him obliterate enemy positions with a fiery tornado, and even encountered the being known as Set—which led them to conclude they absolutely did not want to fight Rei.

That was why they had moved to Gilm from the Bestir Empire, which was at odds with the Kingdom of Mireana.

...Even so, Johanna came to Gilm not because of any hostility toward Rei, but simply because she wanted to be with Set.

“Yes, leave it to me. I’m good at butchering monsters, and I heard we might get a little bit of Giganto Turtle meat, so I was actually kind of interested.”

“Ah… I’ll let the others know as well. There are some who aren’t taking on kobold extermination requests and are just hanging around.”

Johanna and the rest of the former Guerrilla Unit had rented a mansion and were living there in a large group.

Of course, not everyone lives in that mansion; some rent their own rooms, while others have found partners and are living together, or have gotten married and started families...

Still, since so many of them still live in that mansion, it was easy to reach out to them.

“Please do. The more people we have, the faster we can dismantle the Giganto Turtle. Plus, with you guys, I don’t have to worry about anyone stealing materials or anything like that.”

Although the guild had sent people to monitor the situation and ensure no one was stealing materials, the fact that there was absolutely no one who would steal was a relief to Rei.

Besides, the Guild is currently busy with the kobold situation.

That much was obvious just by looking at Lenora and Kenny, who were working at the guild.

That’s precisely why it was highly likely that the number of people dispatched from the guild would be reduced from the original plan.

No, judging by what Rei actually saw, the number would likely be reduced.

Rei has no particular complaints about that.

It was the original agreement, but given the current situation with the kobolds, it’s clear that dealing with that must take priority.

(Come to think of it, maybe we should proceed with dismantling the Giganto Turtle just enough to extract the magic stones, and leave the rest for next winter.)

The fact that he could even think that way was, after all, thanks to the Misty Ring.

As long as it’s stored inside the Misty Ring, there’s no need to worry about it rotting for the time being.

That’s evident from the fact that the Giganto Turtle is still fresh.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

“No, I was just thinking the kobolds are a pain.”

Rei replied to the man’s words.

In reality, that wasn’t exactly what Rei was thinking, but it wasn’t entirely wrong either.

For whatever reason, the kobolds have been attacking Gilm time and again, and as a result, adventurers who could otherwise be used for dismantling work are being diverted to deal with them.

“No one else seems to think the kobold situation is that serious, though. That said, I don’t know what’ll happen if this keeps up even into spring.”

Since it’s winter, the expansion work hasn’t progressed very far.

In truly critical areas where craftsmen are needed, they hire guards to proceed with the work… but such places are quite rare.

That’s precisely why we can assign guards to the craftsmen as well, and most of those craftsmen are brave enough to carry on with their work without batting an eye even if a kobold or two attacks.

But that applies only to a small, select group of craftsmen—and only because they have guards with them.

Once spring arrives, the expansion work will begin, and a large number of workers will be assigned to the project.

When that happens, there will naturally be a shortage of adventurers available to serve as guards.

Adventurers were hired as guards to begin with, but there’s the uncertainty of monsters that might or might not appear, and then there are the kobolds—low-rank monsters, yes, but they attack in droves.

Which of these is more troublesome... well, it depends on the situation, but purely in terms of manpower, the latter was undoubtedly more troublesome.

(Once spring arrives, the adventurers who left during the winter will return, so there’s no doubt the workforce will increase. It will increase, but… we can’t just focus on hunting down kobolds either.)

Once the expansion work gets underway in earnest, we’ll need every adventurer we can get.

“For now, we’d better deal with this kobold situation as soon as possible. We still have some leeway until spring, but we don’t have the luxury of taking our time.”

At Rei’s words, the man and Johanna both nodded with slightly serious expressions.
