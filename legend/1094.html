Gosh was, from Rei's perspective, the very picture of an oasis town! A desert town!

Even though it was a city in the desert... or rather, precisely because it was in the desert, walls encircled Gosh.

Visually, the walls aren't much different from those in Gilm, but the major difference is that they extend deep underground.

Given this is a desert, there are plenty of monsters that move underground, starting with sandworms and sand serpents.

To prevent these monsters from emerging into the city via the underground, it was absolutely necessary to build walls that blocked their intrusion deep underground.

...As a result, the finances of the already small nation of Solrain temporarily worsened. However, over a long period, it generated profits, recouping all the costs incurred in building Gosh, and is now in the black.

Anyway, when entering the city, there was the familiar aspect of Set being surprised, but thanks to Augusto, that was somehow smoothed over. The procedures for selling the sand pirates to the slave traders were finished, and now Rei was here with Set, looking at the Goush.

Beside Rei, Augusto was explaining about Gosh.

"Well, we've recovered all the construction costs for Gosh, but as time passes, various problems inevitably arise... Especially lately, the biggest issue is the deterioration of the walls."

"Ah... yeah, I get that."

Given that this was a desert, the walls naturally deteriorated much faster than those in locations like Gilm.

Sandstorms, intense sunlight, and the bitter cold of night caused the walls to deteriorate day by day.

Once that deterioration reaches its limit, the walls will inevitably break down and fail to fulfill their purpose.

"But lately, Gosh hasn't had much budget either... Look, see over there?"

Augusto pointed to a section of the wall.

Even to Rei's trained eye, it was obvious that repairs had been made.

"That's some shoddy work."

"Yes. As someone living in Gosh, I'd prefer more thorough repairs, but it's not just one or two spots. Proper repairs require adequate materials, of course..."

"But even so, isn't this a bit too bad?"

"...Back when the Elema Trading Company was in charge, they used to hire more skilled craftsmen."

Just hearing that, Rei somehow understood the situation. Understood it, but... what showed on Rei's face was utter disbelief.

"Are you out of your minds? You live here, don't you? Cutting corners on repairs like this isn't just about money—it's a matter of life and death!"

In the frontier... in Gilm, this was absolutely unthinkable.

Risking their own lives... and the lives of all the residents just to save money? Of course he'd be shocked.

(Wait, there was that Azoth Trading Company incident. But even they didn't go this far. Probably because Lord Daskar was there.)

The face of a person who had been popping into Rei's mind more often lately flashed through his thoughts.

The face of someone who showed unwarranted greed, angered him... and who must now be seeing hell every night when they sleep.

"I understand it's a matter of life and death. I understand that... but the Elema Trading Company has deep connections with those above..."

That was when Augusto murmured that far. Zarust, standing beside Augusto, fixed a sharp gaze on a single point.

"Augusto."

Following Zarust's words, Augusto traced the gaze. There stood a familiar figure.

This person wasn't here by chance... they had been waiting for Augusto to arrive.

Yet, they didn't approach Augusto, remaining frozen in place... likely because of Set standing beside Rei.

"Daldora... Why are you here? No, I suppose there's no need to wonder."

"Who is that?"

Seeing the lanky, nervous-looking face of a man in his forties, Rei turned to ask Zarust beside him.

He had the sun-darkened brown skin typical of desert dwellers, yet somehow didn't look rugged.

Combined with his nervous-looking face and eyes, Rei found him giving off a very sinister impression.

"He's the current head of the Elema Trading Company."

"...I see. That man..."

The Erema Trading Company was the one who provided the sand ships to the sand pirates... While it wasn't yet confirmed, it was a highly probable scenario.

Given that, it was only natural for the Elema Trading Company to be concerned: if they had indeed provided the sand ship, why had Augusto's caravan returned safely?

Zalust glared at Dalidra with irritation.

But Daldora... and the guards surrounding him... showed no sign of noticing Zarust's gaze, thick with murderous intent.

They were likely too stunned by the sight of Set, whom they were seeing for the first time.

"Let's go."

Augusto, wanting to avoid seeing Dalidra 's face for even a second longer, tried to leave the spot.

But it was probably August's action that finally snapped Dalidra back to his senses. He began walking toward August in a flustered manner.

Following Dalidra , the guards and entourage began moving as well.

"Well, well, Mr. Augusto. I hear you've been through quite a lot?"

Dalidra asked, her manner best described as serpentine.

However, she was carefully avoiding looking at Set, likely out of fear.

Still, the main reason she approached Augusto like this was the familiar's necklace hanging around Set's neck.

Monsters wearing a familiar's necklace cause trouble, and their masters are fundamentally responsible for the consequences.

In this case, that master was Rei.

Considering his position as head of the Elema Trading Company, Dalidra predicted he absolutely would not... could not do such a thing.

That wasn't necessarily wrong. But only as far as the residents of Gosh were concerned.

Even so, Rei said nothing in particular to Dalidra as she approached.

He simply watched the exchange between Augusto and Dalidra in silence.

"Well, well, you're quite quick to hear things. We've only just returned. It almost sounds like you knew we were going to be attacked?"

Though usually good-natured, Augusto delivered a sharp, sarcastic remark, as if to say he knew when to act.

(Getting attacked and brushing it off with just sarcasm is pretty impressive, I'll give him that.)

Rei glanced at Dalidra , her guards, and her entourage, thinking he wouldn't let it go at this level.

But the scale of the businesses they ran was different.

Dalidra , who owned multiple shops and was also the head of the Elema Trading Company, versus Augusto, who only had one shop.

The difference in their influence was immense, manifesting as a stark disparity in raw power.

After several minutes of trading accusations... Dalidra finally got to the point.

"Speaking of which, Mr. Augusto. I hear the sand pirates who attacked you had a sand ship. ...What happened to that sand ship?"

August realized that was the real issue.

Of course, he was also concerned about how August and his men had returned unscathed despite being ambushed by the sand pirates.

But it was clear he was even more concerned about the sand ship's whereabouts.

(It's understandable, though. A sand ship isn't something you can buy with just a little money. ...In fact, lending it to sand pirates with no guarantees at all would be utterly unthinkable under normal circumstances. ...Does that mean they despised me that much?)

He rushed to the main gate upon hearing their return report, thinking perhaps they'd brought the Sand Ship back. But in the end, it wasn't there.

Still, it was inconceivable that they'd abandon such an expensive magic item in the desert. I suppose they're wondering what became of it.

I predict Daldora's actions this way and feel it's only natural.

Though expecting someone to anticipate the existence of an item box is probably unreasonable to begin with.

"Well, well, you're quite quick to hear things, aren't you?"

Augusto uttered the exact same words as before.

He must have understood the mocking tone behind it. Dalidra twitched his eyebrows nervously.

Still, he showed no sign of anger, suppressing his irritation as he continued.

"Unlike some merchants, I have a wide network of information. ...So, what exactly happened to the Sand Ship?"

Considering the sand ship's value, Dalidra felt he absolutely couldn't back down here. August smiled and spoke.

"What happened to it, you ask? Why does that concern you, Dalidra ? Whatever the sand pirates possessed, the one who defeated them gains ownership. That's only natural, isn't it? It's not something you need to worry about."

Ownership.

The moment those words reached her ears, a flicker of agitation crossed Dalidra 's face.

And the gaze he directed was toward Rei.

His expectation was that someone who had a griffin-like Rank A monster as a familiar could easily defeat even sand pirates using a sand ship.

He looked at Rei, not at Zarust or his party members—the ones who seemed the strongest here and who had also accepted the escort request—because he recognized every single face among Zarust's group.

As the head of the Elema Trading Company, he naturally remembered the faces of those who opposed him, like Augusto, and those who had accepted the escort request.

That was precisely why he had deduced that the completely unfamiliar Rei was the tamer who had Set as his familiar.

(Still, I can't imagine someone so small could tame a Griffin... Oh, right, tamers can subjugate monsters using means other than brute strength, couldn't they?)

Even to Daldora, Rei didn't strike him as a particularly skilled adventurer.

But that was likely only because Dalidra was a merchant.

The men guarding him instinctively understood that Rei possessed strength incomparable to their own, precisely because he was a skilled adventurer. ...And they had.

The adventurers' impression of Rei was that of a wolf in sheep's clothing... no, a dragon.

That was precisely why they didn't want Dalidra , the person they were protecting, to pull any strange stunts with Rei.

"...That sand ship, you see, was actually stolen from us. We'd appreciate it if you could return it."

"Stolen, you say? ...The renowned Elema Trading Company, having a magic item stolen by sand pirates? Surely you didn't intentionally let them steal it?"

"Augusto, aren't your words a bit harsh? While it was our oversight that the sand ship was stolen, it's deeply offensive to imply we did so intentionally."

Dalidra declared this with particular displeasure, though he himself didn't believe August would take his words seriously.

But this was a charade Dalidra knew full well was being played.

No matter how much Augusto suspected Dalidra , he had no proof.

Well, evidence exists. But evidence that could be presented... since the sand pirates who witnessed it would be silent corpses by tomorrow, it might as well be said there was no evidence.

"Is that so? My apologies. But rest assured. We will handle matters concerning the sand ships properly."

At the word "disposal" coming from Augusto's mouth, Dalidra 's expression darkened with irritation.

Seeing Dalidra like this, the guards surrounding him were inwardly trembling with fear.

They desperately hoped this situation could be resolved without causing a scene.

Since his guard duty ended today, he didn't want to fight Rei... but he couldn't show that on his face.

"Handle it, you say? What exactly do you intend to do?"

"Hmm, even if you ask me that... I cannot answer, as I do not hold the ownership rights to that sand ship."

"...Then who does?"

"Rei-san."

With those words, Augusto's gaze shifted toward Rei.

Following Augusto's gaze, Dalidra 's eyes also turned to Rei.

"...And you?"

To Daldora, who asked with a reluctant tone, Rei opened his mouth while stroking Set's body.

"I'm Rei. I'm an adventurer in the Kingdom of Mireana, working for the Guild. I just stopped by Gilm for a bit."

To be precise, he had come here to take refuge until the commotion over the Twilight Spear died down, but he had no intention of telling that to the person before him.

He understood that doing so would likely lead to trouble.

"Gilm... Rei... Griffin!?"

Given his considerable influence within Gosh, he likely recognized the name Rei.

Daldora's eyes widened in shock... After a few seconds, she opened her mouth hurriedly.

"My apologies, but I've just remembered an urgent matter. I must take my leave now."

With that, he abruptly left his stunned entourage and guards behind, his sudden change in demeanor leaving them speechless, and hurried off alone.
