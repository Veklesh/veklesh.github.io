Rei and the others were spending a leisurely time in Marina's garden when a sudden voice caught their attention—it was impossible to miss.

"Who is it? From the sound of that voice, it seemed like they were in a pretty desperate situation."

As Rei murmured this, Marina stood up and spoke.

"It's the guild receptionist... If she came for me, something must have happened at the guild. I'll go hear what she has to say."

At her words, Rei, Vihera, Byune, and Set also stood up.

They decided it would be best to get any explanation over with quickly if there was indeed a problem.

Marina seemed to have no particular objection to Rei and the others' actions, and eventually, the entire group headed toward the mansion's gate.

Then, the woman standing before the gate saw Marina and the others... or rather, she saw Rei with them, and her expression relaxed as if she'd been saved.

"Thank goodness. You were here, Guildmaster... no, Marina-sama."

Though she usually didn't call Marina the Guildmaster anymore, the situation must have been that desperate.

In that situation, having finally spotted Marina, she had apparently slipped and called her Guildmaster without thinking.

As if understanding this, Marina flashed a reassuring smile at the receptionist and spoke.

"What's wrong? You were in such a hurry, so something must have happened, right?"

"Yes. Actually, there's an urgent request for Rei-san."

"...Me?"

From the tone of the conversation, Rei had assumed the request was for Marina, not him, and he showed a hint of surprise.

However, the receptionist showed no particular reaction to Rei's expression and continued speaking.

"Earlier, an adventurer was brought into the guild. ...That alone wouldn't have been a problem, but he said his companion was devoured by the forest on the way to Gilm."

"Eaten by the forest? Killed by monsters living in the woods? It's unfortunate, I suppose, but it's not exactly unheard of."

There was no particular surprise in Rei's words.

Adventurers, after all, are responsible for themselves.

If you challenge an opponent beyond your strength, death is inevitable.

Only those who can judge that properly can be called true adventurers.

Even among adventurers active in Gilm, it wasn't uncommon for someone to misjudge that line... or suddenly encounter a powerful monster during a quest, getting injured or, worst case, killed.

Of course, families, friends, or lovers of missing adventurers sometimes requested searches, but Rei couldn't understand why they'd come to him specifically this time.

The receptionist seemed to understand Rei's confusion. She took a small breath before speaking again.

"Well... from what we've heard about the person brought in, it doesn't seem like a simple matter... They say the forest itself attacked them, as if it were alive. And that the forest itself is eroding and expanding its surroundings... Or, hard to believe, but they say the forest itself is moving."

"...Huh?"

A hint of interest flickered across Rei's face.

This phenomenon, unlike anything he'd ever heard of, seemed to stimulate Rei.

If such phenomena existed, there might be unknown monsters lurking—that was another possibility.

Seeing Rei show interest in her story, the receptionist felt a slight sense of relief.

She hadn't mentioned the word "Trent" because she wasn't entirely sure if it really was one, and also because she didn't want to give him any preconceived notions.

After relaying the information she'd heard from Slesha to Rei, she spoke again.

"So, when I reported this matter to the Guildmaster, they said they couldn't tell what the situation actually entailed... whether it was something adventurers could handle, or if it was serious enough to warrant dispatching the Knights. That's why they'd like you, Rei, to go and scout it out. Of course, we'll make this a designated request."

The term "designated request" didn't particularly interest Rei, but the unknown forest and the monsters lurking within it did.

That's precisely why he nodded to the receptionist without any sign of hesitation.

"Understood. I'll go scout it out."

"...Is that okay?"

Marina asked this because, even though they were enjoying this leisurely time together, she wondered if it was okay to accept a designated request.

But to Marina's words, Rei nodded in agreement, signaling it was fine.

"Yeah. It's nice to spend days like this relaxing, but when it comes to unknown monsters, I can't help but be curious."

"Right. ...Thank you."

Though Marina had resigned as Guild Master, it was only natural she still felt affection for the place after working there for so many years.

That's precisely why she smiled at the thought of Rei accepting a request from the guild.

"The forest, huh. Ideally, it'd be great if there were some powerful monsters there."

Unlike Rei and Marina, Vihera voiced her own desire for a formidable foe to appear, speaking purely to satisfy her own craving.

Byune, seemingly having nothing in particular to say, silently stroked Set.

"Well then, let's get going. Where is it?"

"...I'm afraid the adventurer who was attacked fled the forest in such a panic that they ran desperately without remembering the exact location... However, it seems Runo encountered it on a road about an hour's walk from Gilm, so please focus your search around there."

"Runo? ...Ah, the one with the magic eye."

The image of a familiar adventurer flashed through Rei's mind.

A rare adventurer who possessed a magic eye and worked solo, just like he used to.

(Come to think of it, he's actually the oldest one among the adventurers I've met, one way or another.)

The party calling themselves Eagle Claw who had approached Rei when he first came to Gilm and tried to register as an adventurer at the guild.

...Though in Rei's mind, they were remembered as Goblin Drool.

Rei had been temporarily hired by Eagle Claw, who had just returned from the dungeon Rei conquered last year and was celebrating their victory.

It's absolutely true that they were the first adventurers Rei met in Gilm.

That said, they didn't interact much after that, barely speaking at all until the war with the Bestir Empire.

Anyway, Rei was slightly surprised to hear such an unusual name, but his interest quickly shifted to the unknown monster.

"Centered around a spot about an hour from the highway, huh? Normally, you'd say that's asking too much... Hey, Set?"

"GRRRRRR!"

In response to Rei's words, Set growled, signaling he had it covered.

Considering Set's flight speed, scouting around the highway wouldn't be difficult at all.

Besides, if there were any forests that were corrupted... or moved around, Rei and Set, who had flown around the Gilm area countless times, would have spotted it.

(Well, since we hadn't left Gilm lately, we wouldn't have noticed such a forest anyway.)

After the Spier Trading Company incident, Rei had been living peaceful days.

That's precisely why, upon hearing about this strange forest appearing, coupled with the unknown monster incident, he realized he was harboring a slight hope for this unknown entity.

"Well, I'm going... but what about Marina and the others?"

"I'm going too. It's a request from the guild, and it's intriguing. Though, all I can really do is wait near the gate."

"I could go with you like I did for the Ambris case, but I'd probably just slow you down. I'll wait here too."

"Hmm."

Marina, Vihera, and Byune each gave their answers.

Vihera briefly considered clinging to Set's leg to move, just like she did with Ambris... but ultimately chose to wait by the main gate.

"Well then, I'll leave it to you. I'll go inform the Guildmaster, so I'll take my leave here."

The receptionist found Rei here and murmured in relief.

"Ah, I'll head straight out of Gilm and go check out that forest right away. ...Is it just reconnaissance? Will I be going into the forest to fight monsters or something?"

"No, first, can you report whether that forest actually exists... and how large it is?"

"Really? ...Could it be that the guild thinks the adventurer who brought it in was lying?"

"I wouldn't say that. But there's always a chance of something unexpected. We can't rule out the possibility that they might be seeing illusions."

Hearing that, Rei had no choice but to agree.

Realistically, considering this whole incident, if asked which was more likely—a description of a living forest or an illusion—it was undoubtedly the latter.

(But... if that's the case, then thieves? No, even if a band of thieves possessed a mage capable of creating illusions, it's hard to imagine them deliberately operating around Gilm.)

Rei was left with doubts, but he decided that going to see the situation for himself would quickly clarify things. He nodded back at the receptionist.

"Alright, we're heading out."

"Thank you."

Bowing deeply, the receptionist departed to inform Walker that Rei had accepted the request.

Watching her go, Rei and the others also left Marina's mansion.

"The forest... huh. What do you think? Is it Trent after all?"

"Trent... Come to think of it, didn't Set fight him before?"

At Vihera's words, Rei asked while stroking Set.

Previously... when Rei had challenged the rank-up exam alongside Cullot, Arogan, and Scola, Set had been separated from Rei.

Entrusted to Milein's Scorching Wind crew, Set had fought Trent in that battle.

"Grrrr... Grrr!"

At Rei's words, Set growled reassuringly.

...But even seeing Set like this, Rei couldn't simply believe Trent was responsible.

Walking down the street, it was only natural that there were those who wanted to bother Set.

Children, adults, adventurers, merchants... without distinction.

Some might even see Marina or Vihera and want to pester them, but seeing Set made them hesitate.

And rightly so. Set, nearly three meters tall, might look adorable at first glance, but what would happen if you foolishly made an enemy of him... Well, no one wanted to think about that.

"Rei, Set, wanna grab a bite?"

"Ah, sorry. I have to head outside Gilm right now. Sorry, maybe another time."

Rei apologetically declined the sandwich vendor's offer.

The vendor, understanding Rei's situation, didn't press further.

"Hehe, Set's still popular as ever, but Rei's popular too, isn't he?"

Marina, who had watched the exchange, smiled and said cheerfully.

Vihera, who had been traveling with Rei even longer than Marina, nodded in agreement with Marina's words.

Indeed, many people approached Rei to speak with him, and the attitude adventurers had toward Rei was vastly different from the attitude the stall owner had toward him.

"Grrrr..."

"Look, I'll feed you once I've checked on the forest and come back."

Rei stroked Set, who was growling with hunger, as he spoke.

Being stroked like that seemed to shift Set's mood.

Soon, his meows changed from wanting food to wanting more petting.

"Set really is such a sweetheart. Even with that huge body of his."

Though his words complained, a gentle light shone in Vihera's eyes.

The battle-crazed side was also Vihera, but this scene was also very much Vihera.

Byune, who knew this best, gently held onto Vihera's thin cloak as they made their way through the city streets.

As they walked on, the main gate of Gilm soon came into view.

Perhaps because it was still before noon, there weren't many people going through the formalities at the main gate.

Still, several guards remained tense, trying not to let it show to those they were processing. It was undoubtedly related to the Slesha incident.

While the guild had taken responsibility for handling the situation, it remained uncertain whether that would truly resolve matters.

That's precisely why seeing Rei... no, seeing Crimson Wings arrive brought a slight sense of relief.

He intuitively understood that Rei would definitely take action regarding this matter.

...If it turned out that Rei was actually going to take on a completely unrelated request, the guards would have had a hard time resisting the urge to call him out on it.

"Handle the paperwork."

"Yeah. ...Do your best."

Ray seemed to understand what the guard meant.

Rei nodded and handed over his guild card.
