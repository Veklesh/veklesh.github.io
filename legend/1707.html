[Set has learned the skill "Wind Arrow Lv. 4"]

As the announcement played, Rei was slightly surprised.

The Yellowbird is a Rank D monster.

To Rei and Set, it’s by no means strong… rather, it’s nothing more than a weak, common monster.

So, the fact that Set could actually learn a skill using a Yellow Bird’s magic stone came as a surprise to Rei.

(Well, I guess since he’s learned skills from rare types of goblins before, it’s not really that strange that he’d learn a skill from a Rank D monster’s magic stone.)

Having acquired—or more accurately, enhanced—a skill, Set rubs his face against Rei’s while purring happily.

While petting Set, Rei looked around.

A few adventurers were training, but since it was still early morning, there weren’t many people in the courtyard of the Green Brook Inn.

“Haaah!”

“You’re being too soft on her, Elena!”

……Among those few were Elena and Vihera, though.

As befits a place in Golz that has stables capable of housing Set, the Green Brook Inn had a fairly large courtyard.

Normally, they would absorb magic stones in a secluded spot… but right now, Rei and the others are staying in Golz to tackle the dungeon.

And since they’re supposed to clear the dungeon alongside Relieu, who goes by the nickname “Gale,” they have almost no leeway to absorb magic stones without arousing suspicion.

That’s why they’d been busy absorbing magic stones since early morning… but Rei’s assumption that it wouldn’t work since they were low-rank monsters was quickly proven wrong, and Set successfully learned the skill.

Naturally, there were few people in the courtyard, so no one saw Set swallow the magic stone.

The few people who were there were too preoccupied with their own business to notice.

“Well, now that that’s happened… maybe, just maybe?”

Muttering to herself, she pulled the Death Scythe from her Misty Ring and, with the momentum, sliced through the magic stone she’d tossed into the air.

[Death Scythe has acquired the skill "Hand of the Wind Lv. 4"]

Just like before, an announcement message echoed in his mind.

“I accepted this extermination request to earn the trust of Golz’s adventurers and residents, but the results were unexpected. …Or perhaps the monsters in that dungeon on the cliff face are just that powerful?”

Even so, it was a dungeon best described as “ridiculous,” with monsters—starting with the goblins—plummeting to the ground one after another and dying.

(Well, in games, dungeons located in places you can’t normally reach are usually high-difficulty dungeons… so maybe my assumption wasn’t that far off?)

Rei pondered this while recalling the games he’d played back in Japan, but he knew those were just games—it wouldn’t be right to apply that logic to the common sense of this world.

Still, wondering if he should be on guard, Rei stroked Set, who was looking pleased after hearing the announcement that Death Scythe had learned a new skill.

“Well, anyway… The fact that the Wind Hand’s level went up is something I’m happy about, isn’t it?”

That was Rei’s honest impression.

"Wind Hand" was one of Rei’s secret weapons—a skill he used to create a fire whirlwind.

Given that, a higher skill level would undoubtedly be effective when creating a fire whirlwind.

(Still, Death Scythe is Wind Hand. Set is Wind Arrow. Considering that both are wind-attribute attacks, Yellowbird must have been good at that kind of attack. …Though yesterday was a surprise attack, so there was absolutely no room to show that off.)

Even though it was just support using stones thrown from a distance, it was probably just luck that Golz’s adventurers didn’t suffer any damage from Yellowbird’s wind-based attacks.

The main reason was probably that the sudden ambush left Yellowbird completely unable to react.

“As for Set’s Wind Arrow… it’s a bit difficult to verify that right here and now.”

“Grr…”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a slightly disappointed whine.

While petting Set, Rei took the Death Scythe in hand and tried out the Wind Hand.

“Wind Hand”

Tentacles formed by the wind extending from the Death Scythe.

Level 1: 100 meters, Level 2: 150 meters, Level 3: 200 meters.

Given that, it wasn’t hard for Rei to predict what would happen at Level 4…

“About 250 meters, I suppose.”

That prediction was by no means wrong.

Of course, since it wasn’t measured with absolute precision, there’s likely a margin of error of a few centimeters—or even a few meters, depending on the situation.

(But right now, it’s Level 4. And once it reaches Level 5, the performance of every skill will skyrocket. If that’s the case, will “Wind Hand” also extend all the way to about 500 meters once it reaches Level 5?)

While erasing the Wind Hand so others wouldn’t notice it, he pondered this as he watched the Death Scythe.

If Rei’s prediction was correct, it meant Rei’s trump card—the Fire Whirlwind—could become even more powerful and usable from a greater distance, so I wanted to get it to Level 5 as soon as possible.

“Grr?”

Set rubs his face against Rei’s, as if to ask, “What’s wrong?”

While petting Set, Rei shifts his focus away from the Wind Hand and starts thinking about today’s dungeon run.

I’d seen the dungeon itself yesterday.

Judging by the entrance, fortunately, it was spacious enough for even Set to enter easily.

This meant that when Rei and the others explored the dungeon, they wouldn’t have to leave Set outside—a relief for Rei… and for Set, who didn’t want to be separated from him.

Set is a reliable presence in many ways.

There’s no doubt that Set’s presence is incredibly reassuring when moving to low or high places inside the dungeon.

That said, since there are people like Rei and Elena who possess the Sleipnir Boots, it’s not as if they’d be completely helpless without Set…

As Rei thought this, the mock battle between Elena and Vihera came to an end.

He hadn’t been watching that intently, but from what Rei could see now, Mirage’s blade was pressed against Vihera’s throat, and it was clear to anyone watching who had won.

For Rei, it was a familiar sight—a mock battle. But even though they were staying at the luxurious Green Brook Inn, for the adventurers who ultimately centered their activities around Golz, it was a battle between individuals whose skills far exceeded his expectations.

Even if it was just a mock battle… no, perhaps I should say precisely because it was a mock battle, the sight of the two beautiful women fighting as if they were dancing was mesmerizing.

And while Elena, clad in armor, was one thing, Vihera was wearing nothing more than the sheer garments typically worn by dancers or courtesans. …Though she did have gauntlets and greaves on.

Seeing Vihera moving about so flamboyantly in that state, several men were even leaning forward in their seats.

Of course, since such a sight is nothing new to Vihera, she doesn’t seem particularly bothered by the lustful stares directed her way.

“Well, now that the mock battle is over, shall we head to the dining hall? Since we’re heading into the dungeon starting today, we need to eat well and build up our strength.”

“……Even if you say ‘recharge our energy,’ in Rei’s case, there’s hot, freshly made food even inside the dungeon, so I don’t think we need to worry about morale dropping when it comes to meals.”

“Eating in the dungeon and eating in the cafeteria just feel different, you know.”

Hearing Rei say that, Elena agreed.

Dining with Ara and Rei versus dining with someone steeped in the sense of privilege that comes with being nobility—even if the menu is the same… no, even if the latter is more lavish—Elena would instantly choose the former.

“You’re right. Well then, shall we eat? …I’m actually looking forward to seeing what kind of monsters we’ll encounter in the dungeon.”

Those were Vihera’s words—she’s the type whose spirits are lifted more by fighting strong monsters than by eating—but since Rei and Elena understand that’s just her personality, they don’t intend to say anything about it.

“You’re right. Marina, Byune, and Relieu might be waiting, so let’s head to the dining hall.”

“Oh, I wonder if Byune is still asleep. She’s usually not a morning person.”

As she said this, Vihera turned her gaze toward Rei.

Rei knew exactly what that look meant, even without being told.

After all, Rei himself wasn’t exactly a morning person either, at least not on a regular basis—though he was a different story when he was on a mission.

It’s not that he stays up late.

In fact, compared to when he was in Japan, he was clearly going to bed earlier now.

The reason why Rei, of all people, was up so early today... ...was, naturally, the dungeon.

(It’s probably like looking forward to a field trip so much that you can’t sleep at night or end up waking up early.)

Back in elementary school, during sports days, field trips, and school trips… I’ve been in similar situations a few times.

It’s not like this is my first dungeon, yet I still end up in this situation… I guess I can only describe it as “just something that happens.”

Or perhaps she finds the dungeon—located on the face of a cliff—simply intriguing.

Whatever the reason, there’s no doubt that Rei is looking forward to the dungeon she’s about to explore today.

Under a clear autumn sky, Rei headed to the cafeteria to fill her stomach before taking on the dungeon.

“Hey, Rei. You’re late. The young lady has already finished her meal…”

Seeing Rei and the others arrive at the cafeteria, Relieu started to say that... but when he saw Byune reaching for another piece of bread, he opened his mouth again.

“She’s in the middle of eating.”

“Kyuu!”

Byune, Yellow landed after taking flight from Elena’s shoulder.

“Hmm.”

Then, Byune tore off a piece of the bread she was eating and handed it to Yellow.

Yellow ate the bread with a happy chirp, drawing warm, gentle glances from the people eating breakfast at the surrounding tables.

……There were a few people who, knowing he was the Black Dragon’s child, had some unsavory thoughts, but even they quickly remembered who Rei and the others were and immediately dismissed their foolish notions.

“Where’s Marina?”

Rei asked, noticing Marina wasn’t at the table, but Relieu shook her head.

Watching Relue, Rei scanned the dining hall.

It was still early in the morning… in fact, it was just about the time the six o’clock bell was about to ring, so the dining hall was already fairly crowded with people getting ready to leave the inn.

That said, compared to Gilm—which has far more people than Golz—and especially compared to the Dusk Wheat inn, the number of people here inevitably feels smaller.

In any case, there were all sorts of people: those hurriedly shoveling bread and soup into their mouths; those who, perhaps having no work today, were leisurely enjoying a slightly lavish breakfast; and others who were eating skewered meat while discussing the day’s plans with their companions…

Even among them, if Marina were here, she would undoubtedly stand out.

That’s how strong Marina’s presence is; those around her simply can’t ignore it.

“I don’t know if she’s still asleep… but she’s not in the dining hall. I can’t exactly go up to her room, can I?”

As far as Relieu was concerned, if there were some urgent matter, that would be one thing, but he didn’t want to go to Marina’s room under normal circumstances.

After all, there was no guarantee that something wouldn’t go wrong and leave him unable to face his wife.

“Got it, then…”

“Oh, looks like there’s no need to worry after all? See?”

Vihera’s gaze fell upon Marina, who had just appeared in the dining hall.

Dressed in her usual party gown, she looked anything but appropriate for the dining hall in the morning.

Everyone in the dining hall—including those who had been looking at Rei… no, at Elena and Vihera—shifted their gaze to the newly arrived Marina, and all attention focused on her.

But she was probably used to such stares. She didn’t seem particularly bothered by them and walked over to the table where Rei and the others were sitting.

“Looks like I timed it just right.”

“...Spirit magic, huh? That’s convenient.”

Elena’s words, spoken after she realized from Marina’s remark how she had managed to arrive at the cafeteria with such perfect timing.

Her response was a smile.

“Well, since we’re finally starting our dungeon run today, let’s make sure to eat a good breakfast.”

Saying that, Marina sat down and placed her order.
