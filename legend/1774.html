The day before he was scheduled to leave Gilm with Elena and Ara... Since there was nothing in particular for him to prepare, Rei was once again conducting a patrol of his own accord.

Of course, his primary target was those wrapped in red cloth, but he also harbored a hope that he might find something else interesting.

Beside Rei stood Set.

Although Set had been acting separately from Rei for the past few days, he must have started to feel lonely. It seemed he had decided to stick with Rei today.

“Growl?”

Seeing the shop selling dried vegetables where Rei’s gaze was fixed, Set tilted his head slightly, as if to ask, “Are you going to buy some?”

“Ah… yeah. I’ve heard that dried vegetables are packed with flavor. …Or did I read that somewhere?”

Though slightly curious about where he’d acquired that knowledge, Rei muttered.

Perhaps having heard that umami is delicious, Set tugged at the Dragon Robe with his beak, signaling with his behavior that he wanted to buy some.

Being tugged along by Set, Rei strokes his head to try to calm him down.

“I get it. Look, I’ll go with you, so calm down a bit. The store isn’t going anywhere.”

Rei understood that Set liked meat, but he never would have thought he’d react this strongly to vegetables.

Of course, eating only meat would naturally lead to poor nutritional balance, so in that sense, Rei was actually glad that he was showing an interest in vegetables.

“Oh? Welcome, Rei-chan and Set-chan. Would you like to buy something?”

An elderly woman was minding the shop in that small store.

That was fine, but while Rei could handle being called “-chan” if it were just Set, she hadn’t expected to be called that herself, so she gave the old woman a slightly surprised look.

That said, the elderly shopkeeper didn’t seem to notice Rei’s reaction; wrapped in warm clothes and sitting in front of a magic heating device, she was smiling.

“Well, if there’s anything tasty, I’d like to get some. Could you tell me what you recommend?”

“All the vegetables we have here are delicious. Though I can’t exactly say they’re bursting with energy…”

The old woman burst out laughing, “Hahaha.”

In fact, the dried vegetables were as wrinkled as the old woman’s face, so it would be hard to call them “full of vitality.”

That said, the sunlight had concentrated their flavor and enhanced their umami, making them quite versatile in the kitchen.

That said, Rei mostly ate meals prepared at the cafeteria in the Misty Ring, rarely cooked for himself, and his cooking skills were limited at best.

……Back in Japan, there were ready-to-eat meals—like mapo tofu mixes where you just needed to add tofu, or twice-cooked pork mixes where you just needed to add cabbage—though Rei wasn’t sure if that description was accurate.

“For now, just give me a mix of whatever I can use for cooking for one silver coin.”

Saying that, Rei handed a single silver coin to the old woman.

If this were a restaurant, Rei would have spent more silver coins—or even gold coins in some cases—to buy a meal, but what he was buying this time were ingredients. And not just any ingredients, but dried vegetables he had hardly ever used before.

Naturally, that made him cautious.

(If I give this to Marina, I’m sure she’ll cook up all sorts of things with it, so I don’t think it’ll go to waste.)

Imagining Marina’s home-cooked meals, Rei took the assortment of dried vegetables from the old woman… and suddenly, he stopped.

Rei wasn’t the only one who stopped. Set, who had been standing beside him, eagerly anticipating what kind of dish the dried vegetables would become, did the same.

The old woman watched Rei and Set’s sudden reaction with a puzzled look.

But in the next instant, Rei and Set both turned to look in a certain direction… behind them.

To be precise, they looked at the building situated beyond the crowd of people behind them.

“Sorry, something urgent just came up!”

“Grr!”

Saying only that, they stowed the assortment of dried vegetables they’d received from the old woman into their Misty Rings and immediately dashed off in that direction.

Naturally, Set followed right behind Rei, and the old woman could only stare in astonishment at the person and the dog who had suddenly vanished from right in front of her.

But this old woman was also a resident of Gilm.

Judging that a commotion like this was, in fact, a common occurrence, she immediately returned to her work… back to minding the shop.

“Wait, how did she notice me from this distance!? You’ve got to be kidding me!”

A woman runs, weaving through the gaps between buildings.

She was one of Carles’ companions, the woman who had said she wanted to turn Set into a taxidermy specimen.

Normally, she should have been waiting in the room that had been prepared for her, but she simply couldn’t stand the boredom… so she said she was just going out for a bit and went outside to look around the town.

Along the way, she spotted Set and began chasing after Rei and Set.

If that were all, Rei and Set wouldn’t have noticed her… but what if she attacked? Couldn’t she take them down surprisingly easily? The moment she thought that, Rei and Set turned around and pinpointed her exact location.

And the moment Rei and Set saw her, she instinctively ran away.

Instinctively, she knew that if she stayed where she was, she would undoubtedly be caught.

That was the reasoning behind her action... but it was also a fatal mistake.

Because, having sensed that she had fled, Rei and Set concluded that the hostile intent directed at them had undoubtedly come from her, and they began to give chase.

That said, whether she would have been safe had she stayed put was also a bit of a gray area.

If asked whether she could have kept hiding as Rei and Set approached, the woman had no confidence she could have nodded in the affirmative.

If it had been just a few minutes earlier, she probably could have nodded without hesitation, but now that she had seen Rei and Set with her own eyes, she had absolutely no confidence she could do so.

Filled with deep regret over her actions, she moved away, determined to put as much distance between herself and Rei and Set as quickly as possible.

She was confident in her speed and her agility.

No matter how physically gifted Rei and Set might be, she was confident that she had the edge in that one area.

The fact that I still felt that way even after seeing Rei and Set with my own eyes… was it because I fully understood the harshness of the environment I’d lived in until now?

...That’s precisely why, when I noticed the footsteps approaching from behind gradually drawing closer, my mind went completely blank.

I never imagined there was anyone who could catch up to me in this situation.

Even if I was clearly outmatched in terms of combat power, fighting head-on was something only a fool would do; as a smart person, I would exploit my opponent’s weaknesses.

That was my line of thinking... but in this situation, with an opponent clearly stronger than me closing in from behind, there were no weaknesses to speak of.

To begin with, I didn’t think the man and the beast who had spotted me earlier had any real weaknesses.

The footsteps were gradually getting closer.

Even though she was running at full speed, the fact that she couldn’t pull away at all was nothing but terror for the woman.

If she turned around, she would know exactly how close they were.

But if she were to turn around while running, it would inevitably slow her down, even if only slightly.

And above all, she was certain that if she were to see Rei and Set chasing her from behind with her own eyes in her current mental state, her spirit would undoubtedly break.

That is precisely why, right now, all she can do is keep running, staring straight ahead.

Even so, the footsteps behind her never faltered...

“Well, don’t you think it’s about time we called it a day on this little game of tag?”

Startled by the voice that suddenly rang out right next to her ear, the woman slowed her pace.

And once she slowed down, she couldn’t pick up speed again… Gradually, her pace dropped, and eventually, she came to a complete stop.

Seeing the woman running ahead of them stop, Rei and Set also came to a halt.

It was clear from her speed that she was quite confident in her legs, and it made sense that the moment she realized Rei and the others had noticed her, she chose to flee without hesitation.

But… even so, her speed wasn’t exactly unexpected for Rei, and he had seen opponents faster than her many times before.

Besides, with not just Rei but Set there as well, it was obvious they would catch up the moment she chose to flee.

Still, her speed was certainly worthy of praise, so the fact that she gave up trying to escape was a relief to Rei, as it saved him the trouble.

Leaving Set behind the woman, Rei moved to her front.

This alley wasn’t exactly narrow—it was wide enough for Set to run—but with Rei and Set blocking her front and back, the woman had no chance of escaping.

“So, I suppose I can hear your story now, right? Of course, you know exactly what I’m talking about.”

“……W-what are you talking about? I just ran away because you suddenly started chasing me—I’ve got nothing to hide, you know.”

Even though she felt cornered, the woman decided to try and brush it off.

But as she spoke those evasive words to Rei, she also thought, Maybe things aren’t so bad after all?

There was no doubt that she harbored malicious intent toward Set—specifically, the desire to turn him into a taxidermy specimen.

However, that was merely a thought; she hadn’t actually taken any concrete action.

As long as that’s the case, even in this situation where Rei has cornered her, there’s absolutely no way he would ever lay a hand on her…

Slash!

As if to slice through the very thought the woman was using to try and evade the topic, a deep gash appeared at her feet.

At first, the woman didn’t understand what Rei had done.

Why had a gash suddenly appeared at her feet... a gash so sharp that the word “sharp” didn’t even begin to describe it?

But... the moment her gaze fell on Rei, she understood what had caused that gash.

Yes, just by looking at the Death Scythe Rei was holding, it was obvious what had done it.

“I don’t want to hear jokes like that. It’s clear you mean harm to me and Set. Given that, you’d better not expect me to go easy on you here.”

“B-but I haven’t done anything yet! If you harm me now, you must know what will happen…”

“That might be true. …But it might not be? For now, if I hand you over to the guards, I think they’ll be able to investigate what you’re up to, and worst-case scenario, I could always hand you over to the Knights.”

Rei said this, but it was practically a threat.

It was a fact that Rei was a very important figure to both Gilm and Daskar.

Still, since he’d only shown hostility and hadn’t actually taken any action, they couldn’t just capture him and interrogate him.

If she’d thought about it calmly, she probably would have realized that.

However, in this particular moment… having just been shown the full extent of Rei and Set’s power, her mind couldn’t grasp that much.

What particularly narrowed her perspective was the Death Scythe that had been drawn without her noticing, and the gash it had left in the ground.

It was an interrogation that blatantly flaunted their power, but the woman couldn’t bring herself to condemn it.

She’s convinced that if she says anything here, the blade of Rei’s Death Scythe will be turned against her.

“I… I… I…”

She had to say something. But if she said anything here that betrayed Carles, there was a very high chance she would lose her life.

So, what should she do…? Just as the woman was wavering, a voice suddenly rang out.

“There’s a disturbance right here! A man is using violence against a woman to force her to do his bidding! Security guards, please come quickly!”

The cry echoed around them, and Rei frowned slightly at the blatant nature of the accusation... without even looking back, she swung her Death Scythe.

In an instant, a sharp metallic clang echoed through the air.

Looking closely, a dagger, its blade sliced clean in two, lay on the ground.

“Huh!?”

Realizing that what had just happened was a teammate’s attempt to cover her escape, the woman reflexively tried to flee, but Rei, noticing this, slammed the hilt of the Death Scythe into her body. In the next instant, the woman crashed into the wall of a nearby building and lost consciousness.

“Tch!”

But upon seeing this, Rei clicked her tongue, swung the Death Scythe, and sliced the dagger that flew toward her in two, sending it crashing to the ground.

...Yes, not at Rei, but at the dagger that had been thrown at the unconscious woman.
