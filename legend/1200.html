It never possessed a will of its own.

It merely existed... Ever since it was brought into this world, it had done nothing but create and release its own duplicates.

Moreover, it didn't even know why it had been born. ...No, it didn't possess the intelligence to even contemplate such things.

Truly, its existence merely moved as its instincts commanded. Had Rei known its operating principle, he might have thought it was some sort of robot.

But... Amidst such actions, the entity suddenly sensed that one of its duplicates had vanished.

Even so, lacking any sense of self, it didn't think anything of it.

Besides, most of the duplicates it created burrowed into the ground and constantly acted in unison with it.

Occasionally, for some reason, an individual would start acting separately, but it didn't seem to care much about that. It moved around, burrowing into the ground or flying through the air, creating more duplicates. When encountering humanoid monsters, it instinctively evolved them into leader types.

It—Amblis—had been doing nothing but this monotonous routine, but that day was different.

A portion of its body had abruptly vanished.

Lacking self-awareness, Amblis could recognize humans and living things but could not understand them.

Even regarding humanoid monsters like goblins, it didn't evolve them because it specifically recognized them as goblins; it simply acted according to the instincts within itself.

It was like a spider knowing how to spin a web from the moment it was born.

Or like a newborn foal standing up on its own.

It was purely acting on instinct... and naturally, survival was part of that instinct.

Following that instinct, it avoids touching an opponent who seeks to destroy its body.

Here, if Ambrith had possessed a clearer sense of self, it would have distanced itself from the one seeking to destroy it... it would have fled.

But Amblis possessed no self-awareness; it acted only on impulse.

Even after distancing itself from the threat, that threat showed no sign of stopping, steadily erasing its body.

At this point, a strange sensation existed within Amblis, one he himself could not comprehend.

If Amblis had possessed a self, it would have described that sensation thus: ...Fear.

The terror of its body being erased piece by piece, of everything vanishing as if to say absorbing the separated entities was utterly meaningless.

And the being that carried out the act of dissolving him with utter dispassion.

It was nothing other than terror.

But... without a self, Amblis couldn't comprehend that it was fear.

Yet, driven by that something, he continued to eliminate himself... widening the distance from Rei.

Even with the distance, the unease within Amblis did not vanish, could not be erased, remained.

Tormented by the fear that Rei's magic might burn everything within him to ashes... only then did Amblis truly grasp the terror of his own disappearance.

One could say self-awareness had sprouted.

Something that should never have sprouted within Amblis, a natural phenomenon born from magical energy abnormalities.

That self emerged... and immediately understood.

That if things continued like this, it would simply vanish without accomplishing anything.

Of course, even with this budding self-awareness, it didn't possess the intelligence to think so clearly.

Still, driven by that self-awareness and the instinct for self-preservation, it searched desperately for a way to survive, even as its body burned from the powerful flames of the magic Rei had unleashed.

This too was not a clear thought about what to do or where to go, but rather an action driven half by instinct.

Amblis probes its surroundings, desperately seeking a way to survive.

The entity trying to destroy him... Rei was out of the question. He wanted to get as far away as possible.

Burrowing underground would have left Rei with no means to attack, but the fact that this thought didn't occur to him was likely due to the terror he felt toward Rei's very existence.

That left the entity flying in the air. But because Amblis was born from a magical anomaly, he could sense the magic present between Set and Rei.

He didn't want to touch a being connected to Rei, who could be called his natural enemy... Ultimately, the only one left was Vihera, hanging from Set's front paw.

Not through conscious thought, but driven by instinct, Ambris used nearly all the magic power remaining within his body.

The black mist forming Ambris's body, born from the magical anomaly... Using nearly all of that magic was tantamount to suicide.

But Ambris understood that if she didn't do this, Rei's magic would simply cause her to vanish. Driven by instinct, she used that magic... the transfer magic... on herself.

And when she came to, the densest part of Ambrise... the part where her self had begun to form... had vanished from within the inferno Rei had created. Before her eyes was Vihera's form.

Understanding that this presence before him was essential for his own survival, Ambris sank into that body without hesitation. ...No, in this case, perhaps "fused" is the correct term.

Yes, judging that death was unavoidable otherwise, what Ambrith chose was to flee into Vihera.

But... the one, fatal miscalculation for Ambris was Vihera herself.

Normally, she would have either taken over the other's body outright, or Vihera and Ambrith would have fused together unnoticed, becoming an entirely different entity... or perhaps some other outcome might have been possible.

Despite this, as Ambris began to seep into Vihera's body, he encountered fierce resistance from her consciousness.

Amblis, who should have fused with that body in an instant, encountered fierce resistance.

Within their consciousness, they devoured each other, eroded each other, attacked, defended, rejected, swallowed... attempting to eliminate each other through a multitude of sensations too complex for words.

Ultimately, their wills reached a stalemate... Vihera's consciousness began to fade... And with that, naturally, his grip on Set's front paws loosened, and he began to fall toward the ground.

"Dammit! Vihera!"

Hearing Vihera's scream amidst the battle with Ambris, Rei instinctively turned to see the sight of Vihera releasing her grip from Set's front paw and plummeting toward the ground.

Because he was wearing the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, even starting his movement the instant he saw Vihera falling from the sky to the ground was more than enough time.

He moved toward the spot where Vihera was falling and caught her body, cushioning the impact as much as possible.

A woman's body... and one taller than Rei's at that, had fallen from a height of about 100 meters.

The impact was something that, under normal circumstances, would be impossible to catch.

Still, the fact that Rei managed to catch her without sustaining any significant injury himself, and without causing her any, was largely due to the fact that he had activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

His ability to control the temperature of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour to some extent was equally crucial.

In any case, having safely caught the falling Vihera, Rei gently lowered her to the ground.

While Rei was in action, Set kept watch over the surroundings, ready for anything.

However, the lingering black mist in his line of sight—the remnant of the Ambris, if it could be called that—gradually faded and vanished, just like the Ambris Rei had defeated before.

"The main Ambrith died... no, vanished. So the others disappeared too?"

The reason he couldn't say it was dead was likely because the Ambris had vanished inside Vihera's body.

Given that Ambrith possessed the ability to evolve humanoid monsters into leader species, if it remained inside Vihera's body, wouldn't that have some adverse effect?

Thinking this, yet considering what Rei could do now... he retrieved a potion from his Misty Ring.

It was crafted using the World Tree's Dew, making it the highest quality potion possible.

Rei didn't know how it would react to Ambrith entering his body.

He tried to make her drink the potion... but hesitated.

If the potion's effects brought positive results for Vihera, there would be no problem.

But right now, within Vihera's body was Ambris... specifically, the core entity of Ambris, severely damaged by Rei's attack.

If that was the case, wouldn't giving the potion to Vihera also yield some positive result for it... he couldn't help but think.

After a few seconds of thought, he returned the potion to the Misty Ring.

Since he couldn't decide on his own, he judged it best to ask people like Marina.

"Set, I'm sorry, but I need you to hurry back to Gilm. We need to get Vihera seen by Marina and the doctors as soon as possible."

"Guruu!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a quick growl.

Having been with Vihera for a while now, Set was quite fond of her.

That's precisely why he felt regret that she'd been attacked by Ambris and lost consciousness while he was with her.

Of course, it was also a huge shock that Ambris, who had never once shown any sign of self-awareness until now, would suddenly transfer and flee into Vihera's body.

The actions Ambris had taken toward Rei weren't about counterattacking, but rather about keeping his distance.

In that sense, it wasn't surprising that Rei had gained the ability to transfer.

Anyway, I had to hurry and get Vihera to Gilm. I had to... but here, once again, Set's nature held me back.

Fundamentally, only Rei could ride on his back.

A child might somehow fit, but Vihera was far from being a child.

Could it work if she were unconscious? Rei tried mounting Set while cradling Vihera horizontally... but even that proved impossible.

Well, maybe they could have flown a short distance, but to get from here to Gilm? It was definitely impossible.

After pondering for a moment, the solution Rei came up with was...

"It feels a bit off since it's different from usual."

What was there was Set's belly.

...Yes, currently Rei was flying while Set held him by both shoulders with his front paws.

The unconscious Vihera lay horizontally in Rei's arms.

After much consideration, this was how they decided to carry her. Despite being held horizontally by Rei—in what's commonly called a princess carry—Vihera showed no reaction whatsoever due to her unconscious state.

(If I had Set grab Vihera directly, there was a risk of injury.)

Vihera is clad in a sheer garment so thin you could see through it. Even being held like this by Rei, she doesn't look like she possesses particularly high defense.

Of course, that was just appearances, but even so, Rei didn't want to carry the unconscious Vihera like that.

(Set... Right now it's just Vihera, so she was moving by holding onto Set's front paws, but that's only possible because of Vihera's physical abilities. Byune will probably come back here soon too, and then...)

Thinking that far, Ray remembered Byune was still a child.

Using Set's wings for a short time.

The idea was to place Vihera or Byune inside it and have Set carry them.

He was thinking about trivial things because, when he thought about Vihera, he could fall into endless negative thoughts.

Even though he knew this and was deliberately thinking about something else, he still couldn't help but worry about Vihera.

They had overtaken the Arogans a little while ago, but Rei hadn't even noticed.
