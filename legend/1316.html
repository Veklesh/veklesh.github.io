Spier Trading Company。

Of course, Rei knew that name too.

Well, to be precise, he had come into contact with one of the company's merchants—Tris—several times over the winter.

"Spier Trading Company, of all places..."

The others had also heard a bit about the Spier Trading Company from Rei.

Especially Byune didn't have a very good impression of them, having heard about how Tris had tried to buy white clouds from Pamidor.

"Then, your master is..."

"Yes, it's Tris-sama."

Having come this far, Terry likely decided there was no need to hide it anymore. He simply nodded in response to Rei's words.

Seeing Terry like this, Rei tilted his head inwardly.

For a moment... just a fleeting moment, he wondered if Tris might have been the one behind this whole affair.

But then, he thought, would the person who set this trap for Rei have gone out of their way to call him?

Reaching that conclusion, he immediately dismissed the thought from his mind.

Then, stroking Set, who had somehow ended up rubbing his head against him, Rei asked Terry.

"So, what should we do about Set? ...Getting him inside seems a bit impossible."

Whether it was the effects of all the things he'd eaten over the winter or simply being in a growth spurt... Set was now about three meters long.

At that size, he couldn't fit through just any door anymore.

(If he keeps growing like this, won't it become difficult to enter dungeons too? The passages inside are pretty wide, but the entrances are quite narrow...)

Rei suddenly felt that anxiety, but then remembered that Set had a skill allowing him to temporarily change his body size, and felt reassured.

...A thought flashed through his mind: what if Set's size-changing skill ran out while moving through a narrow passage? But he decided not to dwell on it.

"Set, there's a stable here, so please rest there. Of course, we've prepared proper meals for you too."

"Huh, that's thoughtful."

"Thank you."

Terry bowed deeply in front of the Spier Trading Company building.

The building itself was quite large, presenting a sufficiently impressive storefront.

Parked in front of such a shop was an obviously expensive carriage, and there was Terry—a man who embodied the very definition of ordinary—bowing.

There was no way this wouldn't draw attention.

As if noticing his own conspicuousness, Terry hurriedly opened his mouth.

"I'm sorry, I'll have everything ready immediately."

After saying this briefly, Terry called over two women nearby.

...The women he called out seemed delighted to be told they would be guiding Set.

What they thought of Set was obvious just by looking at them—no need to wonder.

That Set was well-liked was naturally a happy thing for Rei too, and he smiled.

Though only the corners of his mouth were visible beneath the hood of his Dragon Robe, even so...

"Ah, do that. ...Set, go with those two then. Don't eat too much, okay?"

"Grrrr."

At Rei's words, Set gave a short growl... likely pleased at the prospect of getting something to eat. He followed the two women toward the stables as if being led.

Watching the cheerful Set walk away, Rei turned his gaze back to Terry and spoke.

"So, you were going to show me around, right?"

"Yes. Lord Tris is waiting as well. This way."

Guided by Terry, Rei and the others entered the shop.

This building served as both a storefront and an office for the Spier Trading Company. Inside, numerous people could be seen negotiating with Spier Trading Company merchants.

(The lack of merchandise... Well, I suppose what's happening here is just negotiations, and the goods are stored elsewhere. I heard before that the Spier Trading Company had recently arrived in Gilm or something like that. It's quite bustling here.)

Rei pondered as he watched the people talking.

But naturally, such behavior drew the attention of those in the midst of negotiations.

And when they turned their gaze to the one watching them, there stood beauties like Marina and Vihera (the female).

Wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, Rei wasn't particularly conspicuous when Set wasn't around.

Of course, not everyone failed to recognize Rei as Rei; a few did notice his presence.

Still, they didn't cause a scene here. That was probably because the people present were, after all, fairly competent merchants.

They couldn't afford to cause a scene and embarrass the Spier Trading Company, and many knew from past incidents that Rei didn't hold merchants who made advances toward her in high regard.

That's precisely why, almost as an unspoken agreement, no one addressed Rei and her companions.

Terry led Rei and the others to the back of the shop, guiding them down the passageway.

"I heard Spier Trading Company only started operating in full swing in Gilm this spring, but they seem to be moving quite actively already,"

Marina murmured as they walked down the aisle.

Rei and Vihera both nodded in response to her words.

Terry, perhaps pleased to hear praise for the Spier Trading Company where he worked, let a slight smile touch his lips.

But the moment he saw the figures approaching from the direction they were heading, the smile vanished from Terry's lips.

"Hm? Ah, Terry-san. And over there... Oh my. Rei and the others? Well, well, you're moving rather quickly today."

The one who called out with a smile was a man in his thirties.

He was slightly overweight, but not to the point of being sluggish.

His most distinctive feature was a beard that resembled a catfish's whiskers.

Though smiling, his eyes didn't join in, giving Rei the same impression he'd gotten from Tris before.

"Precious. ...Since you're here, did you have some business with Lord Tris?"

"Yes. You know what it's about without me saying, don't you? It's about the future of the Spier Trading Company within Gilm."

"That matter should be entirely up to Lord Tris."

"Of course I know that. But I also know I have the power to assist Tris. Considering that, isn't it only natural I'm here?"

Facing Precious's nonchalant words, Terry's face—which seemed to embody the very essence of ordinariness—flashed anger for an instant, and he clenched his back teeth.

What was she aiming for? What had she done? And... why was she here?

That was precisely why he gritted his teeth.

Rei and the others could only watch the exchange between the two.

It was only natural, since this was their first meeting with Precious.

Seeing Terry's reaction, they could guess there was some history between them, but that made them wonder if it was wise to get involved.

Precious must have noticed Rei and the others standing behind Terry, who was glaring at him. He smiled and spoke.

"Well, well, well. Pleased to meet you. Mr. Rei, Ms. Marina, Ms. Vihera, and Ms. Byune, I presume? I am Precious of the Spier Trading Company. What a coincidence to meet in such a place. Did you have business with Tris?"

"Ah. We heard she might have some information regarding a matter we're currently involved in, so we came to hear it..."

"Hmm. ...Then, a word of caution. Things aren't always what they seem. What's visible, what comes to the surface, isn't necessarily the truth. Facts and truth may look alike, but they are different."

"...I see. So you're saying Tris's information might be wrong? Is that what you're implying?"

Rei asked, seeking confirmation, but Precious kept smiling and shook her head.

"No, no. That's not necessarily the case. That judgment isn't for me to make, but for Rei. All I can do is offer advice. How you choose to take it is up to you."

"I see. I'll gratefully accept the warning. But if that's all you needed to say, we can wrap this up, right? I'm a busy man."

"Yes, of course. I understand you're quite busy. I'm just glad your friend is safe."

"...What?"

Rei's steps halted abruptly.

The words about his friend being safe clearly implied knowledge of what had happened to Rei... knowledge of Azimov's attack.

Considering he hadn't mentioned anything about the Sleipnir Boots, he probably didn't know the full story.

(No, it's possible he knows and is simply not saying anything?)

Even in their brief exchange so far, it was clear Precious was a smooth talker.

Therefore, it couldn't be ruled out that he knew about the Sleipnir Boots but was keeping quiet about them.

Thinking this, I tried to say something... but before I could, Precious left in front of Rei and the others.

"...My apologies for the disturbance. Let's proceed to Lord Tris."

The passion she'd shown moments before seemed to have vanished already. Terry said only that and began walking down the corridor again.

After walking for several minutes, they arrived before a single room.

"Excuse me, Lord Tris. I've brought Rei and the others."

"Come in."

Hearing the voice from inside, Terry opened the door.

The room inside was likely an office.

Tris sat at a desk piled high with documents, repeatedly glancing over papers, signing them, and reaching for the next one.

Finally, it seemed he had reached a pause. Tris set down the documents he held, stood up, and offered a smile that didn't reach his eyes.

"Welcome, everyone. Sounds like you've been through quite a lot this time."

"Yeah, that's right. ...That guy Precious said something similar too."

The moment the name Precious was mentioned, Tris's expression faltered for just an instant.

But it was truly just a momentary flicker, and the next instant, he was smiling again as he spoke.

"I see, Precious. I was just speaking with him a moment ago... Did he cause you any trouble?"

"No, that part's fine. He did say some rather suggestive things, though."

"...I see."

Tris murmured thoughtfully at Rei's words.

But she quickly shook her head slightly and addressed Terry.

"Terry, bring some tea and a light... no, something to nibble on."

The brief glance toward Rei was likely because she knew exactly how much he usually ate.

Terry bowed in response to Tris's words and exited the office.

Thus, the office was left with only Tris and the members of Crimson Wings, starting with Rei.

"Please sit down for now. I'm sure you have many questions, and this will likely be a rather long conversation."

As he spoke, Tris rose from the desk where he had been seated and made his way over to the sofa placed in the room.

Rei and the others seemed to have no objection to the suggestion, quietly settling onto the sofa.

Of course, not everyone could fit on one sofa, so they sat on the sofas placed around a central table.

Rei and Marina sat opposite Tris, while Vihera and Byune took the sofas to his left and right.

And naturally, Tris was the first to speak.

"Now then, first of all... I apologize."

Once everyone was seated, the first thing Tris did was bow deeply toward Rei.

With his head bowed, Rei couldn't see Tris's face.

But if he could have seen it, he would have noticed that Tris, who usually smiled without his eyes joining in, looked genuinely and deeply apologetic.

Time passed in silence as they all remained still.

The only sound in the room was the murmur of voices drifting in from outside the office and beyond the windows.

In that silence... eventually, Rei spoke.

"Look up. I can't do anything if I don't know what you're apologizing for. It's definitely related to this matter, but please tell me more precisely what it's about."

At Rei's words, Tris lifted his face.

"Actually, the incident you were caught up in this time... it involves a power struggle within the Spier Trading Company."

That was what he had revealed.
