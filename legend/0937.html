Inside the principal's office, Rei turned her gaze back to the elf man before her and spoke.

"Things got pretty messy, but should I say thanks for helping me out earlier? Or should I be angry that you went and interfered with the fight?"

"Hahaha, you're full of energy, aren't you? ...Well, never mind that. Let's skip that talk for now and start with introductions. I think we both have a general understanding of each other, but we haven't properly introduced ourselves yet, have we?"

Sardat, smiling as if he'd already forgotten mistaking Rei for a woman and trying to flirt with her, addressed Rei.

Laying her head in her hands at the difference in his lighthearted reaction compared to Noiz's, Rei opened her mouth.

"Yeah, I guess I'll just say you saved me... but I am grateful. If we'd started a large-scale battle there, that whole area could've been wiped out. ...I'm Rei. A Rank B adventurer belonging to Gilm. I've been given the nickname 'Crimson'."

Sardat smiled at Rei's words, ran a hand through his hair, and spoke.

"I'm Saldart. Believe it or not, I'm an S-rank adventurer. My nickname is 'Skybow'. Nice to meet you."

The fact he said "despite how I look" himself probably meant he understood his personality seemed lighthearted to others.

"Still, I'm glad I was able to help you out there. ...Well, more accurately, the people around you. If you'd really started fighting there, those around you would've definitely suffered. Some might even have died."

"...So that's how strong the opponent was?"

Rei asked, seeking confirmation, but Saldat responded with a small smile.

"You should know that better than anyone, shouldn't you? Taking on three Stigmas at once is just reckless. Even I'd prefer to avoid that if possible."

"Huh? Even Saldat of the Heavenly Bow can't win against these Stigmas?"

Erinde, sipping her already cold tea, asked as she listened to Rei and Saldat's conversation.

"Of course not. We could fight them if we wanted to. It's just... a hassle, isn't it? Even if they're Stigma, I'd be more motivated if the opponents were women."

He said it casually.

The way he casually stated he could take on those three earlier without a moment's hesitation made Rei sense a fierce pride in his own strength.

(He's not just a lightweight... I suppose that's only natural for an S-rank adventurer.)

Even as he reaffirmed that the man before him was indeed an S-rank adventurer, Rei's body didn't freeze up.

This was likely because he had experience facing Noiz before.

Noiz was someone whose mere presence was enough to make one tense up.

Rei had managed to hold his own against Noiz, however unevenly.

That experience had solidified into unwavering confidence within Rei. That was precisely why, even now facing Saldat, his body didn't freeze up from nerves.

(Huh, impressive...)

Sardat was inwardly impressed by Rei's composure.

He knew his personality was lighthearted, and he was aware of it. But he liked that part of himself and had never tried to change it. Because of that, he'd been told many times that he lacked the weight expected of an S-rank adventurer.

Still, he understood that anyone possessing a certain level of power would show some kind of reaction when facing him.

But the girl—no, the boy—standing before Saldat now remained completely natural, despite understanding that.

To the point where, even if a fight broke out with Sardat right here, he could react instantly.

"So, that 'Stigma' thing... is that the trio from back then?"

Rei asked, both sizing each other up while keeping it hidden.

The word "Stigma" that had just come from Sardat's mouth was not something easily forgotten.

Someone capable of closing in unnoticed and delivering a single blow... and not just one, but two others likely possessing power comparable to, or even surpassing, that of the man in his twenties who had attacked him.

Given that this was information about such individuals, it was far from something he could simply brush off.

Rei urged an answer with a strong gaze, and Sardat nodded back without seeming particularly concerned.

"That's right. Their unit name... well, it's not quite a name, but the designation that signifies their existence is Stigma. The Holy Light Church's ultimate combat force."

"The Holy Light Church!?"

Rei was startled by the unexpected words coming from Saldat's mouth.

Had the thought never crossed his mind when he heard the word "divine punishment" from the men who attacked him... when he overheard it slip from one of the Stigma members? That would be a lie.

Given that Rei was the one who had antagonized Exil so thoroughly, thwarted their plans, and even caused them to cast doubt on the Holy Light Church itself, it wasn't strange that he was targeted by the Church's agents.

"...Wait."

He muttered, Rei's gaze shifting to Erinde, who was looking at him with interest.

Erinde must have immediately understood the meaning behind that gaze. She gave a small nod in return and spoke.

"Yes, it makes sense if we assume that incident with the Tears of Heaven was also their doing. For the Holy Light Church, the Exil affair was a fatal blow... well, perhaps not fatal, but it certainly dealt them significant damage. Given that, it's only natural they would harbor deep hatred toward Rei."

"You knew?"

The question wasn't about the Heavenly Tears incident, but about the events in Exil.

He hadn't expected news of the events in the labyrinth city of Exil to have reached Erinde's ears.

But she immediately shook her head slightly.

The incident that revealed the Holy Light Church as a dangerous entity was naturally met with great shock within the Kingdom of Mireana.

For those whose lands the Holy Light Church operated in, it was nothing short of a bolt from the blue.

Given that, it was only natural to investigate exactly what had happened in Exil. And it was an unavoidable fact that investigating that matter would inevitably bring up Rei's name.

"Was the Holy Light Church active in Graciall to begin with?"

"Not exactly active, but there's a fair number of them... that's about it."

Rei's brow furrowed slightly at Erinde's words.

That meant the commotion hadn't been caused by his arrival in a place where the Holy Light Church already existed. It meant the Holy Light Church had caused the commotion precisely because he was there.

"So I'm the cause?"

"Probably. But you don't need to worry about it that much, do you? Honestly, I'd like to thank you for stirring things up while you were in Gracia."

"...Why? You're actually happy I went out of my way to stir things up?"

Rei shot Erinde a look of utter confusion, despite the faint smile playing on her lips.

It wasn't Erinde who answered Rei's question... but Saldat.

"It's not exactly fun when trouble happens. But think about what the royalists are doing now. Among those they're purging, there are probably some who are suspected of having ties to the Holy Light Church, even if there's no clear evidence. Don't you think it's the perfect opportunity to deal with them?"

"Are there? People who'd deliberately choose to get involved with the Holy Light Church at this point?"

Rei's mutter wasn't entirely unreasonable.

It was true that the Holy Light Church had come under stricter scrutiny since the Exil incident, but banning it outright had never been explicitly declared.

Officially, Exil's case was deemed the rampage of a few individuals.

But even so, considering they caused a major disturbance, and that disturbance happened less than a year ago, it was hard to imagine any noble would dare approach the Holy Light Cult now.

Seeing Rei's expression, Sardat flicked back his hair and flashed a grin.

"Religion isn't something you can just walk away from so easily. Especially if that religion, like the Holy Light Cult, has something hidden behind it. And many nobles receive some benefit from the Holy Light Cult. Then there's..."

"You understand, right?" Sardat's words left Rei with no choice but to nod.

"Still, Stigma... That's quite a grand name. Is it strong?"

"Yes. At the very least, it possesses enough power to hold its own against me to some degree. To give you an example you'd understand, it's roughly on par with a Rank A-level powerhouse..."

"...I see. So it is that strong, after all."

Having taken a direct hit, he already had a good grasp of its power.

Weaker than Elk, perhaps, but still not an opponent to be underestimated.

That was Rei's impression.

"Three opponents like that... That's not just troublesome, it's downright dangerous."

"Right? So... how about I serve as your escort? Though, to be fair, I was already your escort to begin with."

At Sardat's words, Rei's gaze shifted toward Erinde.

But Erinde, meeting his gaze, nodded slightly without any sign of guilt and spoke.

"It's only natural, isn't it? You may be an instructor, but more accurately, you're a guest. Given that, we can't just sit idly by knowing you're being targeted. Of course, this isn't my own decision; it's an order from Duke Quent, so you can rest assured."

"No, what exactly am I supposed to be relieved about? ...Besides, I was targeted by those three, and you conveniently intervened. I expected as much. ...Still, assigning an S-rank adventurer as my escort? That's quite the lavish hospitality."

"As I just said, you're a guest of Duke Quent. It seems only natural to me."

"Yeah, yeah. I was actually looking forward to watching over the life of a lovely young maiden... but honestly, I never expected Rei to be a guy. It was a completely unexpected turn of events."

Sardat echoed Erinde's words, but the expression on his face didn't suggest he found it as dull as his words implied.

Rather, he seemed to be saying that this unexpected turn of events was delightful, most welcome indeed.

Aware of Saldat's expression, Rei deliberately ignored it and addressed Erinde.

"I never expected the Holy Light Church to show up. Oh, I asked Grink to send an assassin to the guards to try and get info on whoever's after me... What happens with that?"

"An assassin?"

"Oh, right, there was one."

Erinde couldn't follow the conversation, while the other two, having watched Rei closely, understood the gist.

Rei decided it would be best to explain it himself to them. He told them about how he had saved a second-year S-class student named Eland from an assassin's attack.

...Of course, he didn't mention that the trigger was having soup dumped on the ground.

"I see. So he had returned after all. Word had reached us that family troubles were delaying his return, you see."

It seemed they already understood the basics of the Eland situation.

(S Class gathers the top students of each grade, and they're promising for the future, so it wouldn't be strange if she remembered his face and name.)

With so many problems piling up at once, Erinde was racking her brain over how to solve them all.

"Well, do your best. I was only asked to be his escort, so you can count on that part being taken care of."

Seeing Sardat's smiling face, Erinde let out a small sigh.

She wanted to complain, but having an S-rank adventurer escort Rei was a huge benefit in itself.

If Rei were to get injured within the Duchy of Quent now... or worse, die... It wouldn't just be a matter of the Duke of Quent losing face.

"Personally, I'd rather not have someone hanging around me all the time..."

Rei's words carried the implication that he'd prefer not to have a bodyguard. Saldato smiled and replied.

"Is that so? Then perhaps I'll continue guarding you from a distance, as I have been."

"No, it's not that I want you to guard me from a distance... No, never mind. I feel like saying more is pointless."

He must have understood in this short time that Ray had a rather self-centered personality.
