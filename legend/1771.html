"Hmm, I see. ...So even someone like Crimson is just a softie, after all?"

"Yeah, I guess so. Considering that the guy who tried to steal the old lady’s luggage got caught, I’d say that’s about right."

"Hmm, if possible, I’d really like that griffin stuffed. Right? No good?"

“Of course not. Just follow orders from above. If you carelessly run into Rei, people like us would be crushed in an instant. We’d literally be reduced to mincemeat.”

The room where several men and women were conversing was furnished with fairly luxurious pieces of furniture.

Normally, furniture that was just functional enough would have been sufficient, but due to the woman’s whim—her desire to have the griffin stuffed—they’d ended up going out of their way to purchase luxurious furniture.

That said, the financial burden was negligible compared to the funds we’d received for this job, so there was really no need to worry about it.

“But it’s a stuffed griffin, you know? Even if it’s not as rare as a dragon, it’s not exactly something you can just get your hands on.”

“I get where you’re coming from, but if we do something like that, we’ll just make Rei even angrier than he already is. Our job is strictly to investigate Rei’s personality—to figure out what makes him angry and how far we can push him. I’m not about to get into a direct fight just to find that out.”

Hearing the man’s words, the woman—perhaps offended—pouted and looked away.

“Hahahahaha. Well, I guess that’s only natural. I’ve seen Rei from a distance too, and I could tell he’s got some insane power despite his appearance. At the very least, someone like me wouldn’t stand a chance no matter what I did.”

“Ah, I totally agree. On top of that, there’s the Princess General, the Imperial Princess of the Bestir Empire, and a former Guild Master—all skilled fighters—standing by her side. Honestly, the only opponent I could possibly beat is that bandit.”

Everyone in the room nodded in agreement at the words of the man who was tending to his favorite dagger.

The Rank B party led by Rei, Crimson Wings.

Their combat strength was exceptionally high, on par with—or even surpassing—that of a Rank A party.

On top of that, since Elena—who currently goes by the nickname “Princess General”—is traveling with them, everyone understood that making a reckless move would only lead to death.

Among those present, there were even some who wanted to challenge Rei, Set, Elena, Marina, and Vihera to battle just to experience the thrill of death.

But at the same time, given their professional commitment to the job they’ve accepted, they have no choice but to prioritize their work over their own enjoyment.

For better or worse, only those who understand this have gathered here. …No, perhaps it would be more accurate to say they were gathered precisely because they are such people.

“Still… ‘Crimson Rei,’ huh? To be honest, I wasn’t sure how much of the rumors were true… I’d almost assumed that, depending on the situation, they were just having minstrels spread exaggerated tales.”

At that single remark from the man who had remained silent until now, everyone in the room—who had been doing as they pleased—fell silent.

He hadn’t said anything particularly special, yet there was something in his words that was enough to bring this motley crew of eccentric individuals to attention.

“Carles, what are we going to do?”

“What do you mean, ‘what are we going to do’? Even now, aren’t we using these hotheads to check on Rei? If that’s the case, we should just wait patiently.”

“……No, but……what could a bunch like that possibly know about Rei?”

The only woman in the group—the one who had asked for a stuffed griffin—spoke up, sounding slightly dissatisfied with Carles’s words.

“……”

“Eek!”

But the moment Carles turned his gaze toward her, a scream escaped the woman’s lips.

For a split second—truly just a split second—a murderous intent undoubtedly swept through the room.

Yes, it flowed.

It wasn’t directed solely at the woman who had voiced her dissatisfaction; the killing intent was aimed at everyone in the room.

And in the very next instant, that killing intent vanished completely.

“Don’t worry. There’s no need to rush right now. The Red Cloth people are just here to observe the situation. Fortunately, winter is coming, so Crimson will likely stay holed up in Gilm for a while. Besides, the people who hired us said it’s okay if it takes time.”

“Y-yeah, that’s true. Plus, thanks to the expansion work, there are a lot of people around right now, so we won’t stand out.”

At Carles’ words, one of the men hurriedly agreed.

Everyone gathered here was highly individualistic, but they understood all too well that Carles was a figure of higher standing than themselves.

……That said, it was precisely because someone like Carles was leading them that the group hadn’t fallen apart, despite being made up of such strong-willed individuals.

“Um, so here’s the thing. According to the intel I gathered, it seems Rei really likes to eat. In fact, he’s apparently coming up with all sorts of new dishes and spreading them around Gilm.”

In reality, he’s even been promoting a dish called “seafood okonomiyaki” in the port town of Emoshion, but whether that specific detail hadn’t reached him or he’d decided it wasn’t worth mentioning, the topic didn’t come up.

“Oh, I know about that too. Udon, meat buns, pizza… stuff like that, right? They were all delicious, so they really stuck with me.”

The man who had been sharpening his dagger said this, and the others agreed.

In truth, part of it was an attempt to lighten the mood in the room, but more than that, they probably genuinely thought the food was delicious.

“And then there’s the story about helping out at the meat bun stall to improve the flavor, and the goblin meat incident related to that, right?”

Another man spoke up, and those listening nodded, as if suppressing their own surprise.

Carles was no exception.

That’s how much the technique for making goblin meat taste good was met with astonishment.

“Was it the Percy Trading Company? Maybe we should send someone there too.”

“No. Not the Percy Trading Company, but Percy’s Tool Shop. That guy Mayo over there. It seems he and Rei worked together to figure out how to make goblin meat taste good.”

“I hear all sorts of chefs are flocking to Percy’s Tool Shop. Though, since there isn’t much goblin meat yet, apparently not many have actually cooked and tasted it.”

From that topic, the conversation gradually broadened to what kind of meat goblin meat actually is.

Among them, there were quite a few who, before gaining the powers they have now—back when they were children—had nothing to eat and had to endure intense nausea to eat goblin meat just to survive.

That’s exactly why, upon learning there’s a way to make goblin meat taste good, they take an interest in it.

They wonder just how much better that incredibly unpalatable meat could possibly taste.

“Hmm, if you’re that curious, why don’t we go to a restaurant and try it?”

“Huh?”

At those words from Carles, the others who had been talking about goblin meat all let out cries of surprise.

Even though they wanted to try it, they never expected Carles to make such a suggestion.

“Um… are you sure it’s okay?”

The lone woman asked timidly, but Carles simply nodded at her, indicating there was no problem.

“I’m actually a bit curious about goblin meat myself. …Though, to be honest, I’m not sure if it really tastes as good as the rumors say.”

Saying that, Carles stood up and looked around at the people in the room.

“Well then, shall we go? Relying on others is fine, but seeing Gilm with our own eyes isn’t a bad idea either. Right now, Gilm is about to take a giant leap forward. Yes, from a town to a city. To witness that sight is to witness history.”

In response to Carles’s speech, those listening in the room reacted in various ways.

Some looked at him with eyes full of admiration, while others turned away, finding it all nonsense.

But… even so, there was no way for anyone in the room to avoid hearing what he said.

“Being able to witness a chapter of that history is a source of great joy for me. …Though, I am somewhat concerned about the various unnecessary conflicts that have arisen; but if I consider them merely a side dish, it’s not such a bad thing after all. …Well then, shall we go? To witness history.”

The words that came out of Carles’ mouth were grand… far too grand, perhaps, but they were by no means wrong.

Gilm, the only town in this frontier region, was about to transform from a town into a city thanks to population growth… and the vast building resources provided by the Treant Forest.

It was as if a butterfly were emerging from its chrysalis.

Although the group hadn’t originally been particularly interested in Gilm’s expansion, hearing Carles put it that way made them think, “Maybe it’s worth taking a look after all?”

Glancing at his companions’ reactions, Carles smiled contentedly and spoke.

“It seems everyone’s interested, which is great. Well then, shall we go? …So, about eating goblin meat—do you know exactly which restaurants serve it?”

“Ah. Oh, yeah. I’ve got that part figured out. Apparently, a few restaurants serve it. But I heard they’re all pretty crowded, so it might be a bit of a hassle.”

The idea that goblin meat can actually be delicious is that shocking.

Driven by a morbid curiosity—or in this case, a desire to taste it—some people want to try a dish made with goblin meat at least once. If those who’ve tried it spread the word that it was actually delicious, a few of those who hear it will go try the goblin meat… and then, based on those customers’ reviews, new customers will show up.

There’s truly no phenomenon for which the term “word of mouth” is more fitting.

On top of that, the chefs who were lucky enough to get their hands on goblin meat discuss among themselves what dishes to use it in, generally deciding on a direction to avoid overlapping with other restaurants’ customer bases.

...That said, since Mayo had thought this through from the start and selected chefs whose specialties didn’t overlap, there weren’t any major issues.

“Hmm, well… shall we visit a few places for now? I’m not particularly fond of crowds, but I suppose I can put up with that much.”

Following his suggestion, Carles and the others got ready to go out and left the room.

That said, the people gathered under Carles were a diverse bunch in many ways.

Some were wrapped in thick coats suitable for winter, while others were dressed lightly, making them stand out somewhat.

……The fact that they only stood out a little was likely because such a diverse group had gathered at Gilm’s.

Especially this year, with the expansion project underway, there were more people than usual, and among them, there were certainly quite a few who stood out even more than Carles and his group.

There’s a saying that “a tree is hidden in a forest,” and that perfectly describes the current state of Gilm.

After all, depending on the situation, there are people here with personalities far more striking than even Carles and his group.

“Well then, let’s start by checking out the shop selling sandwiches with stir-fried goblin meat. That’ll be a quick bite.”

“Eh… a sandwich? I’m not really in the mood for bread right now.”

“Then just don’t eat it. I’m not saying you have to force yourself to eat the whole thing.”

“Hmph. What’s that supposed to mean? Are you underestimating me?”

“Sigh, who said that? I didn’t say anything like that, did I? Look, I was just saying that some of the dishes using goblin meat actually take quite a bit of time and effort to prepare. You should just eat those, right? You can’t eat every single dish anyway.”

“Besides, even if you say you’ve gotten used to eating goblin meat, it’s still just goblin meat, right? If you’re comparing them purely on taste, orc meat is definitely better.”

One of Carles’ companions muttered, and in fact, that was true.

Just because goblin meat has become palatable doesn’t mean it’s superior to the meat of monsters like orcs, which have a flavor that exceeds their rank.

“Come on, everyone. Let’s go. We’re going to be busy with the Crimson matter this winter, so let’s take our time and recharge our energy.”

When Carles said that, everyone obediently followed him.

……But Carles and the others didn’t know.

That Rei would soon be leaving Gilm to head for the Duchy of Kerebel.

Whether it’s a good thing or a bad thing for Carles that they don’t know this yet…
