That day, Ilse had been wandering around Gilm since morning.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, Ilse could hold her liquor.

Even after drinking with men, she never got so drunk she passed out. In fact, she'd often ended up getting the men who tried to get her drunk drunk themselves instead.

That said, the three men Ilze had drunk with last night weren't the type to try to get her drunk and take advantage of her, so she didn't need to worry about that.

(Come to think of it, Mom was a heavy drinker, though Dad wasn't.)

Ilze's beauty was inherited from her mother.

Even after giving birth to two children, Ilze's mother showed no sign of fading beauty whatsoever.

No, if anything, she had only grown more attractive as a woman, radiating a sense of motherhood.

Precisely because she was Ilze's mother, many men, while separated from her husband for business reasons, considered getting her drunk and making a move.

But in the end, every single one of those men ended up getting drunk and passed out by Ilse's mother.

(Thanks to Mom, I can hold my liquor too... but honestly, I'd rather not follow her example in that department.)

Ilse walked through Gilm, her thoughts drifting to her modest... well, she'd describe it as modest, chest encased in leather armor.

She understood this wasn't the time to dwell on her physical insecurities.

What she needed first was detailed information about the enemy with the snake tattoo on his left arm.

And someone to help her defeat that enemy.

The latter, especially, should be a skilled adventurer if possible.

Fortunately, many skilled adventurers had gathered in the current Gilm.

Therefore, he felt it might not be impossible to find someone willing to assist him.

Among them, the most promising candidate is undoubtedly Crimson Rei.

A formidable adventurer who, in just a few short years, rose to become a renowned Rank B adventurer.

He had naturally asked the three from yesterday about this information, and this morning, he had also casually inquired about it with Melan, who still hadn't fully sobered up... but the response he got was something along the lines of, "You should be able to tell just by looking."

Ilze tilted her head at his expression, which seemed to say, "How could you not see it?"

But she couldn't push further with questions.

If she did, he might sense her interest in Rei.

Of course, as an adventurer famous for his alias, it probably wouldn't be a problem if he sensed a little interest.

Still, since it wasn't absolutely certain, she preferred not to let him know she was that interested in Rei.

For that reason, most of what she managed to learn about Rei was information she already knew.

Still, learning that finding Rei meant finding the griffin Set was one of the few useful pieces of information Ilze gained.

The griffin Set was treated like a pet in Gilm.

A Rank A monster, known as the Grim Reaper of the Skies.

Even Ilze, who had glimpsed a fragment of that spectacle, found it utterly unbelievable.

...Had she known that Set was actually S-rank equivalent, Ilze would have trembled with even greater shock.

When in Rome, do as the Romans do... Ilze didn't know that saying, but she understood that since she had come to Gilm, she had to follow Gilm's ways.

Anyway, as she walked along thinking about this, she suddenly stopped upon seeing a large crowd gathered around a food stall.

It wasn't unusual for food stalls to draw crowds at lunchtime.

Under normal circumstances, she would have simply thought it was a popular stall... but the presence of a griffon's massive form beside it changed everything.

It had probably been eating from the stall's food just a little while ago.

Now, it was rubbing its face against someone beside it, eyes half-closed as they stroked its head.

The weather was so hot that sweat poured out just from walking—not just fine weather, but scorching heat.

Indeed, even Ilze had beads of sweat forming on her face just from walking.

Amidst this, Ilze found herself slightly curious why only that one person and that one creature... no, why even the people around that food stall seemed somehow cool and composed. But for now, her priority was to make contact with her target, Rei... With that thought, she started to take a step forward, but the next moment, her foot stopped.

Because the person stroking Set looked vaguely familiar.

Given the heat, the number of people wearing robes wasn't large, but even so, Ilze knew immediately who it was.

It was the person who had stopped her yesterday at the guild, when she'd finally found the enemy she'd been searching for all these years and had instinctively reached for her dagger.

(Huh? That kid is Rei? No way!)

She thought that for a moment, but seeing him stroking Set... and seeing how Set was rubbing against Rei, acting affectionate, made it clear that it was true.

The Set Ilze had seen yesterday had also been happy with the person who gave him food, but it was still vastly different from how he was acting affectionately toward Rei now.

With Rei, it seemed like he was completely at ease and truly affectionate. With others, even if they were being petted, it didn't feel like they were this relaxed.

The sight of this one person and one creature drew not only Ilze's attention but also that of others eating nearby, creating a peaceful atmosphere around them.

Even though it was midsummer, being within that atmosphere somehow made the heat feel less oppressive... a strangely soothing feeling.

Rei probably hadn't noticed the atmosphere around them, but eventually, he and Set left the food stall.

Those around them, looking reluctant to see them go, said nothing more. They either resumed eating or returned their finished plates to the shopkeeper, each returning to their own business.

Amidst this, Ilze quietly began to follow Rei and Set.

It wasn't that she harbored any particular hostility or ill will toward Rei; she simply wanted an excuse to speak to him.

Given yesterday's incident where she'd fled the moment someone tried to speak to her, it was undeniably awkward.

(If I'd known that boy was Rei, when he spoke to me yesterday... No, it wouldn't have been possible. The moment I spotted that man, I had no room for such things. Thinking about it now, maybe I should actually be grateful he stopped me there.)

It's frustrating, she sighed.

She let out a small sigh...

"So, what did you want from me that you were following me?"

Before she knew it, Rei was standing right in front of Ilze, asking that question.

Rewinding a bit, while walking toward the site where Rei and Set were doing the extension work, Rei had naturally noticed someone tailing him.

This was partly because Rei was sharp... but more significantly, the stalker's skills were terribly unskilled.

(A trap?)

They were blatantly tracking him, drawing his attention that way, while the real target remained hidden from Rei.

Having read, seen, or heard about such traps somewhere before, Rei naturally suspected it might be one first.

But even assuming it was a trap and scanning the surroundings, the likely target remained unseen.

Of course, Rei... or more accurately, Set, who was with Rei, stood out.

Various people, whether original residents of Gilm or those who had recently arrived, directed their gaze toward Rei and Set.

But the person following him from behind was clearly looking at Rei with a different kind of gaze than those others.

Rei hesitated for a moment, wondering what to do. Eventually, deciding that staying like this could lead to all sorts of trouble, he spoke while stroking Set as he walked.

"Looks like someone wants something from me, so I'm going to go talk to them."

"Grr."

Set purred in understanding at Rei's words.

The worried look in his round eyes wasn't because he didn't trust Rei's abilities, but simply because he genuinely cared about him.

Understanding this, Rei stroked Set again before glancing behind him.

The moment he saw her sighing for some reason, he instantly pulled away from Set and closed the distance to the woman.

Reaching the woman, he spoke as if catching her off guard.

"So, what did you want from me that you were following me?"

"...Huh?"

At Rei's words, the woman looked up, seemingly startled.

Seeing her face, Rei realized he recognized it somehow.

The woman stood stunned in surprise, while Rei strained to recall the face before him.

A strange silence hung in the air for several seconds... until Rei finally remembered. The woman before him was the one he'd met at the guild yesterday.

She was someone who had suddenly drawn her weapon inside the guild, so Rei remembered her well.

Well, unfortunately, it wasn't all that uncommon for someone to draw a weapon inside the guild.

After all, it's a gathering place for hot-blooded individuals, so it's only natural they'd be quick to fight.

Especially now, with many adventurers gathered for the expansion work, beyond just the original adventurers of Gilm.

...Originally, they were adventurers who lacked the strength to even come to Gilm in the first place. Yet, precisely because of that, many of them were determined not to be looked down upon by the Gilm adventurers.

Rei had seen such individuals get into fights with other adventurers in the guild and draw their weapons several times.

But the reason Rei remembered the person in front of him was because he simply couldn't believe this person had the strength to even come to Gilm.

Of course, the fact she was beautiful played a part too.

Then again, Rei was used to interacting with women like Elena, Marina, and Vihera – women described as peerless beauties.

The woman before him... Ilze might be beautiful, but she wasn't someone who stirred his heart to that extent.

Finally, breaking the silence that had settled between them, Ilze spoke.

"Um... just to confirm, are you really Rei?"

She asked this even though she already knew the man before her was Rei.

This was likely more about preparing to converse with Rei.

Whether Ray understood Ilze's intentions or not.

In any case, Rei nodded in response to Ilze's words.

"Yeah, I'm Rei. Didn't you know that when you came looking for me?"

"Well... that's true. It's just... it wasn't quite what I expected."

Even when she tried to gloss over it, Rei didn't show any sign of anger over the matter.

After all, it had happened countless times before: people with preconceived notions about the adventurer known as Crimson would see Rei and react with surprise.

Rei found it silly to get angry at every such person, and unless they were someone who disrespected him or acted hostile, he'd learned to just let it slide.

"I hear that a lot. So? Did you follow me because I didn't look like someone nicknamed Crimson?"

"No, that's not it. It's just... I wanted to apologize about yesterday."

Ilze stated this as the opening to their conversation, but Rei seemed unconcerned as he spoke.

"Don't worry about it anymore. I stopped it yesterday, but even if I hadn't, someone else would have."

"But... oh, right. I'd like to treat you to something as a thank you... how about it?"

As she invited him, Ilze inwardly groaned.

She knew Rei had just eaten at a food stall a short while ago.

Offering to treat someone who had just finished eating could easily be taken as harassment, depending on the person.

Ilze tried to hastily correct her words, but Rei nodded without seeming particularly bothered by her slip.

"Alright then, that works perfectly... How about that place over there?"

Rei's gaze fell on a single diner.

It was lunchtime, so the place was crowded, but every customer leaving looked satisfied.

That meant the food at that diner was delicious.

"Huh? Wait, are you sure!?"

Ilze had just invited someone who'd just finished lunch to eat, but she probably hadn't expected such a straightforward nod instead of a refusal.

A slightly dazed voice escaped her lips.
