"Huh... You're pretty skilled for a thief. With skills like that, you really shouldn't be wasting your time as a thief—you should have just become a regular adventurer instead... don't you think?"

The thief unleashes a flurry of attacks with her dagger.

A thrust, a stab, and a kick following a feint.

While dodging the thief’s attacks, she launched a counterattack against his final kick. Unable to dodge it, the thief took the full brunt of the blow and collapsed to the ground, struck by her gauntlet.

From Vihera’s perspective, she couldn’t understand why a man with such decent skills was even working as a thief.

That said, seeing someone with this level of skill working as a bandit made it clear there must be some underlying reason.

In any case, with the last remaining bandit losing consciousness, the band’s annihilation was confirmed.

“Here, take this. You must be thirsty, right?”

“Thank you.”

The cup Marina held out was filled with cold water.

It was so cold—so much so that one might wonder how she managed to keep it that way all summer—but it wasn’t a magic item… it was simply the result of Marina’s spirit magic.

“Still, everyone’s alive, aren’t they?”

At Marina’s words, which carried a hint of teasing, Vihera wore a slightly displeased expression and spoke up.

“What I like is fighting, and nothing else. It’s not like I’m some kind of bloodthirsty killer.”

“I know. I’m just kidding, just kidding. …Though if this were Rei, it wouldn’t be such a joke.”

“Well, Rei doesn’t show mercy to thieves. But with this band of thieves, I don’t think even Rei would have been too eager to deal with them seriously.”

Saying that, Marina looked at the bandits lying around her.

This band of thieves did attack merchant caravans, but they tried as much as possible not to kill the guards or merchants.

Of course, doing something like that would get them reported to the guards, greatly increasing the likelihood of a punitive expedition being sent after them.

Since they were doing this knowing full well the risks, they must have been confident in their skills… and indeed, even from Vihera’s perspective, their combat prowess was impressive.

“Hmm!”

As Vihera sipped some cold water and relaxed, Byune eventually emerged from the bandits’ hideout, carrying several bags.

The contents of those bags were the treasures the bandits had hoarded at their hideout.

“Oh, you look so happy. Were there really items that expensive in there?”

“Hmm!”

Byune replied to Vihera’s words, but Marina, seeing this, simply tilted her head in confusion.

To Marina’s eyes, it simply didn’t look like Byune was that happy at all.

As usual, her expression didn’t change, and she spoke only a single word.

It was simply impossible to believe that she looked happy seeing Byune like that.

But Vihera saw through it and said she looked happy, and Byune nodded in response to Vihera’s words.

Seeing that, there was no doubt that the two of them were clearly communicating effectively.

“Huh… You haven’t done anything underhanded, but you’ve still managed to accumulate quite a bit, haven’t you?”

Vihera said admiringly as she looked at the jewels inside the bag Byune had brought.

“Of course. If they were the kind of people who slaughtered every member of a caravan they attacked, the caravan would fight back with everything they had. But if they know their lives will be spared even if their cargo is stolen, no one’s going to fight to the death.”

“I see. …So that means these bandits aren’t attacking that way because they don’t want to kill people; they’re just doing it as a job.”

“That’s right. Of course, there are some bandit groups who actually enjoy killing people. Naturally, there must be such bandit groups around Sublusta as well. …If possible, I’d like to prioritize taking down those kinds of bandit groups.”

“I suppose so. There’s no doubt it’s best to take out troublesome opponents like that as soon as possible. …Though, considering how unlucky those kinds of people tend to be, they might already be fighting Rei and the others.”

“...If that’s the case, I think things are going to get pretty bad. Rei, despite how he looks, shows no mercy to anyone he doesn’t like. He’ll even raise his hand against nobles without a second thought, you know? If they’re bandits, he’ll hesitate even less.”

At Marina’s words, Vihera let out a look that said, “Oh, no…”

After all, it was easy to picture that scene.

Specifically, the sight of the bandits being slaughtered.

...In fact, that prediction was spot on; Rei had, along with Set, slaughtered the band of outlaws—the members of the "Immaculate White"—who could only be called scoundrels. However, it would be a little while before these three found out about it.

“Haaah!”

Turning toward the fleeing figure’s back, Elena swung her Mirage.

They were separated by a distance far beyond the reach of a longsword, but thanks to Elena’s magic, the Mirage transformed from a sword into a whip, dramatically extending its range.

“Gyah!”

The bandit, who had been trying desperately to escape Elena, was slashed across the back—leather armor and all—and collapsed to the ground.

Though the blow was nearly fatal, perhaps because it lacked the power to kill him outright, the thief, lying on the ground, tried to crawl away... but then, as if reaching his limit, he stopped moving.

“Damn it! Don’t run! If you run now, you’ll die! Look at that woman! Don’t you want to have some fun with a woman that hot?”

One of the bandits shouted this as he fired an arrow at Elena with the bow he was holding.

Unlike swords or spears, the power of an arrow doesn’t change as long as you’re using the same bow.

Of course, skill comes into play when it comes to whether you can hit your target, and if you can, where you aim.

The arrow fired by the bandit flew straight toward Elena’s torso… no, toward her leg.

Just as he had shouted to those around him, he must have thought it a waste to simply kill a beauty like Elena.

Is it because he’s a bandit from the countryside that he doesn’t know about Elena, the Princess General, or is it simply that he hasn’t even bothered to gather any information?

In any case, the bandit’s arrow flew straight toward Elena…

“I won’t let you do that!”

As she shouted, a figure appeared and knocked the incoming arrow out of the air with the axe she was holding.

It was Ara, wielding a Power Axe.

Several bandits who had witnessed the scene—and the bandit who had fired the arrow—couldn’t believe their eyes.

If this had been done with a weapon like a longsword or a dagger, they might have understood what was happening.

There were no such skilled fighters in this band of thieves, but even so, there were a few among the thieves in the vicinity of Sublusta who could pull off a similar feat.

But... that is something only possible with a long sword or dagger—weapons that are easy to handle. Even if someone were to say that such a feat could be pulled off with a weapon like the power axe Ara was holding, under normal circumstances, they wouldn’t believe it.

Still, having seen it with my own eyes, I have no choice but to believe it.

“You’ve got to be kidding me!?”

I can’t help but believe it, but if asked whether I can accept it, even the bandits wouldn’t be able to nod in agreement.

To be honest, the very fact that they have to fight such a strong opponent is something they’d rather not believe.

“There are only two of them! No matter how skilled they are, there are still only two of them! So, in the end, we should be able to win!”

One of the most skilled members of the bandit group shouted this, but even if those words were true, it also meant that a significant number of bandits would likely be killed before they could ultimately win.

...It shouldn’t be hard to figure that out with a little thought, but few of the bandits think that far ahead.

No, that’s exactly why they’re bandits… or, to be precise, why they’re low-ranking bandits.

In any case, precisely because they’re treated as disposable, these low-ranking members are simple-minded; they follow the claim that if they all attack together, they can win, and their morale soars instantly.

“They’re quite an amusing bunch.”

“I suppose so. But if their morale rises this quickly, doesn’t that mean it’ll drop just as fast if something goes wrong?”

Ara, holding her power axe, replied to Elena.

Normally, hunting down bandits was not the sort of thing Ara would ever do, but even so, she was thoroughly enjoying the current situation.

It wasn’t that Ara enjoyed causing chaos or had a desire to trample on weaker opponents.

……She felt a deep loathing for the bandits who attacked innocent merchants and travelers, and she did feel a sense of satisfaction—almost like a thrill—in subduing them… but what outweighed that feeling was, above all, simply being with Elena.

Of course, when Elena had set out for the Kingdom of Reburina and was traveling separately from Ara, they had spoken almost every night using their Paired Orbs.

But even so, there was a huge difference between seeing her through the Paired Orb and having her right here by his side like this.

Being able to travel with Elena like this brought Ara immense joy.

If Rei were here, he would undoubtedly think, “She’s so hyped!”

“Anyway, it’s easier for us when the enemy comes toward us instead of running away… right?!”

With that sharp cry, a whip-like Mirage severed the head of a bandit who had closed the distance, sword in hand.

However, perhaps influenced by the thief’s earlier shout that they could win if they attacked in numbers, almost none of them flinched even after seeing a comrade killed in a single blow.

Another bandit standing near the slain one flinched, but seeing the others press forward undeterred, he decided that retreating now would make him look like a coward, so he forced out a war cry and charged ahead.

(What was that called again... the courage of a commoner? If Rei’s explanation is correct, that term would be fitting.)

Thinking this, Elena unleashes a series of Mirage attacks at the approaching bandits.

The reach of the whip-like Mirage is simply beyond the capabilities of ordinary weapons like long swords or spears.

In fact, depending on the situation, they can even be longer than a bow.

Facing Elena, armed with such a weapon, there was no way ordinary bandits could possibly stand a chance.

As the Mirage strikes landed one after another, the number of bandits dwindled rapidly.

Seeing Elena like this, even the bandit leaders—who had previously thought they would be willing to make sacrifices to get their hands on her—must have changed their minds.

What a pity to lose a beauty like Elena, but they didn’t think it was worth sacrificing the entire band—including themselves—to keep her.

After all, if they were to die, the woman would no longer be an issue.

As a result, instead of aiming for limbs like legs or arms, they launched a fatal strike aimed squarely at her head...

“I told you I wouldn’t let that happen!”

Every single arrow fired at Elena was knocked down by Ara, who was wielding her Power Axe.

Having demonstrated that her earlier strike was no fluke but a display of her true strength, Ara turned her gaze—filled with murderous intent—toward the bandits targeting Elena and glared at them.

She could not tolerate anyone who would harm her beloved Elena.

With that thought in mind...

“It’s fine. Go ahead.”

As Elena, having sensed Ara’s resolve, spoke those words, she kicked off the ground and charged headlong toward the bandits wielding their bows.

Naturally, this meant Elena would have to fend off the attacks that Ara had been blocking until now, but to begin with, there was no way these mere bandits could possibly harm Elena.

In that sense, it might be fair to say that Ara was overprotective...

That said, even though she wouldn’t get hurt, being constantly attacked was undoubtedly unpleasant, so she naturally dealt with the attacks.

“Haaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!”

The bandits were advancing toward Elena, but that group was easily wiped out by Ara’s swinging power axe.

If she had simply attacked the surrounding bandits, most of them would have died, but this time, Ara was targeting the bandit leaders.

After breaking through the horde of bandits, Ara charged straight toward the bandits who were drawing their bows.

Naturally, the bandits launched attacks to intercept Ara, but the arrows they fired were knocked down one after another by the Power Axe… It didn’t take long for the entire bandit group to be wiped out.
