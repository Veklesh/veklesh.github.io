Rei and Zuboz, along with Paul holding his ice-generating shield, watched as Adria was captured and Dias lost consciousness.

Additionally, Dias's subordinate had also lost consciousness from the pain of Byune's long needle attack.

Three battles unfolded here.

All three ended in victory for Rei's allies.

"...Well then, what now? All your allies are defeated, and the battle situation is overwhelmingly in our favor."

Zuboz's face flushed with rage at Rei's words as he held his Death Scythe, yet he suppressed his fury.

If he fought driven by this rage, it would certainly feel satisfying.

But he didn't believe he could defeat Rei fighting like that, driven by rage.

No, even fighting at full strength, victory would be difficult.

Zuboz understood there was simply that much of a power difference.

Even if he and Paul teamed up, fighting Rei head-on would leave them with no chance of victory.

(...Right. If we fought him head-on, that is.)

Zuboz muttered inwardly, shifting his focus to the trump card in his pocket.

Honestly, it was a trump card he'd rather not use.

But the truth was, he couldn't afford to be picky about methods if he was going to avenge Kabajido.

He hadn't considered winning against Rei without injuring himself from the start.

(If only I'd had a little more time, I could have made final adjustments... No, it's pointless to say that now. I've already caused several incidents using monsters for this experiment. Though, in terms of diversion, I wish they'd caused a bit more chaos.)

Reflecting on what I'd done so far, I felt a slight regret, wondering if I could have done better.

But still... what I must do remains unchanged.

I take a small breath in, then out, then in again.

He forcibly suppressed the lingering traces of fear and hesitation within him with sheer willpower and opened his mouth.

"Paul, please hold the line until I'm ready. And... once I'm ready, get as far away as you can."

"Are you going to use it?"

Paul asked, holding his ice shield and watching Rei's every move intently.

As someone who ran a magic item shop, Paul possessed a fairly deep knowledge of magic items.

No, as someone who had aspired to be an alchemist in his youth, Paul prided himself on having a deeper understanding of magic items than the average person.

Even to Paul's discerning eye, the trump card Zuboz had created was highly effective for breaking the current stalemate.

...If it were a finished product.

While he was aware of the risks, for Zuboz, that choice was by no means the worst possible option.

In fact, considering he could kill Rei with his own hands, it wasn't the best option, but it was undeniably the closest thing to it.

"You're stubborn. Fly Slash!"

The slash released from the swung Death Scythe flew straight toward Zuboz.

If he possessed a physique as sturdy as Paul's, it might be different, but for an alchemist like Zuboz, taking Rei's Flying Slash head-on would almost certainly result in life-threatening injuries.

But... before the slash could hit Zuboz, Paul, tasked with defending him, intervened. He created ice with the shield in his hand and deflected the slash.

"Not happening."

The words were brief, but the resolve behind them was anything but light.

Seeing Paul's stance, Rei switched Death Scythe to his left hand and drew a spear from the Misty Ring in his right.

Entirely deep green from tip to butt, this spear was the Thorn Spear—currently the most powerful spear in Rei's arsenal.

A magic item spear possessing special abilities fitting the current situation... a demon spear.

Though the Thorn Spear appeared suddenly, Paul showed no particular reaction upon seeing it. He silently positioned his shield before Zubo.

His actions were imbued with the resolve to protect her at all costs, no matter what happened to himself.

Seeing this, Rei took a few paces back with the Thorn Spear in hand, added ample body twist, channeled magic power, and poured every ounce of that force into the spear's throw.

"Wooooooh!"

With a cry bordering on a roar, the thorn spear left Rei's hand... Charged with magic, it flew toward Paul at a speed that could be called a streak of light rather than a spear.

Of course, Paul wasn't just standing there watching.

He infused his shield with magic, attempting to deflect the attack just as he had done with the flying slash earlier.

Even if he couldn't block it, deflection was possible. Thinking this, he channeled magic into his shield, creating ice to form a more powerful barrier.

...Under normal circumstances, this wouldn't have been the wrong choice. In fact, the ice shield he created now possessed a hardness that would be nearly impossible for the average adventurer to shatter. Moreover, due to the inherent nature of ice, any weapon striking it would slip and be deflected.

But... yes, but. Paul was facing Rei, the one known by the epithet Crimson, a being whose soul housed magic power beyond measure.

The thorn spear Rei launched, propelled by the infused magic and the inhuman speed granted by her own body... pierced the ice shield effortlessly in the next instant.

Not shattering it, not cracking it, but piercing it.

Creating a space just wide enough for the spear to pass through, the magic-infused thorn spear pierced the ice shield, then pierced the magic item shield itself that was generating the ice, and finally pierced Paul's right arm, pinning him to the ground.

"Guh!"

Despite his arm being pinned to the ground, no scream escaped Paul's mouth. Only a brief, agonized groan escaped him.

But... Paul's true trial was only just beginning.

The thorn spear, piercing Paul's arm and driving into the ground, activated its ability to generate thorns.

The thorns sprouting from the arm impaled by the spear first completely covered the arm in thorns, simultaneously extending those thorns from the arm to the shoulder, and from the shoulder to the torso.

"Guh, ugh, this!"

Being covered in thorns was an experience Paul had never known before.

Even though he wore leather armor, the thorns pierced through the gaps in the leather, tearing his skin and ripping his flesh.

The only small mercy for Paul was that the thorns stopped at the flesh and didn't reach the bone.

Even so, his entire arm was pinned to the ground, and the thorns spreading from there across his body were unstoppable.

The shield meant to protect his Zoboz was shattered by a thorn spear, leaving only the ice shield pierced by the spear still intact.

The ice shield, created by pouring every ounce of magic Paul could muster, possessed formidable defensive power.

Though pierced by the thorn spear, it could still hold out until Zuboz finished preparing... Paul, enduring the pain of the thorns spreading from his pierced arm throughout his body, directed his gaze towards Zuboz.

There, instead of his waist belt, Zuboz had finished the final step by channeling magic into a glass bottle he'd taken from his pocket. He was about to gulp down the glowing green liquid inside the bottle.

A moment's hesitation.

Zuboz himself knew the item he held was still incomplete.

It was something he hadn't even fully tested yet, little more than a theoretical construct.

But if that theoretical construct proved correct, the contents of the glass bottle should grant Zuboz immense power.

To avenge Kabajido with his own hands.

With that thought, Zuboz gulped down the contents of the glass bottle in one go.

The viscous liquid slid down his throat without any discernible taste.

Zuboz frowned at the sensation for a moment, but the next instant, he had no time to think about such things.

Thud. A sensation like something writhing inside his body overwhelmed Zuboz.

"Guh... GAAAAAAHHHH!"

What erupted from his mouth was either a roar or a scream.

Regardless, Zubo endured the sensation of something writhing within his own body.

Beneath the skin of Zubo's face, pulsating root-like things began to emerge.

No, not just his face. Root-like things were also protruding from the back of his hand, which was sticking out from his robe sleeve.

Given what was visible now, Paul watching him... and Rei who had hurled the thorn spear could easily guess that root-like things were probably emerging all over his body.

"Suicide... that can't be it. He did mention something about a trump card. ...But is it really okay to defeat that alchemist like this?"

Though he had his doubts, Rei still felt something troublesome would happen if left unchecked. He decided it would be better to knock him unconscious, even by force, and started moving toward the Zobuzo. But after taking a few steps, he kicked off the ground and shifted sideways.

Where Rei had been standing just an instant before, a sharp, pointed ice spear now stood embedded in the ground.

Rei turned his gaze toward where the ice spear had come from. There, an ice shield... or rather, what should be called a former ice shield, existed.

There was no doubt it had been a shield created from ice just moments ago.

But what now lay before Rei's eyes could hardly be called a shield.

The once thin ice shield had thickened, becoming something more akin to a block of ice than a shield.

That ice mass shifted... and once more, Rei kicked off the ground.

Moving sideways, Rei glanced back at where he had been a moment before. There, as if it were only natural, an ice spear was thrust into the ground.

Ice reflecting the light of dusk brought a somewhat fantastical atmosphere to those who saw it.

But Rei had no time to be captivated by such a fantastical sight.

Because one after another, ice spears were being launched from the ice block, aimed directly at Rei.

"Dammit! It had this function too!?"

Rei had assumed Paul was solely focused on defense with his shield. But the expression on Paul's face—his right arm pinned to the ground by a thorn spear, more than half his body covered in thorns—was nothing short of utter shock.

And beside the immobilized Paul lay his magic item shield, pierced through by the thorn spear.

Seeing this, Rei felt a bad premonition and turned his gaze back to the ice block.

Could it be... that ice block was running amok because the magic item that created the ice shield had been destroyed?

(But why is it only attacking me? Paul's right here too.)

The ice, which continued to attack only Rei with surprising precision for something supposedly out of control, was clearly abnormal.

But... Rei's question was answered almost immediately.

The shield lying by Paul's side, pierced by a thorn spear. A familiar pattern had emerged on its surface.

Yes, the same root-like pattern that had been on the skin of the red cyclops Rei had fought, and the same pattern now visible on the face of Zubo, who was still groaning.

"The shield!"

He didn't know why, but it was certain that the shield was somehow affecting the ice mass, causing it to manifest as this rampage.

Once he made that judgment, Rei's actions were swift.

Slashing away the ice spears hurled at him with Death Scythe, he kicked off the ground and charged toward the shield.

Whether the shield knew its end was near if he got close, or if it was mere coincidence, it fired ice spears one after another from the ice mass toward Rei...

"Too bad,"

As Rei murmured, immense magical power erupted from his body, condensing and compressing.

To whom were those words spoken? To the pole that created the ice shield? To the ice itself, now shattered and seemingly running wild? Or to the Zoboz, whose root-like tendrils were rising from its body?

Regardless, the magic power gathering around Rei became visible as a crimson glow... and in the next instant, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour activated.

"Fire is extinguished by water. That is a fact, isn't it?"

As he murmured, the heat radiating from the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour caused the surrounding temperature to rise by several degrees.

Even so, considering the temperature of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour enveloping Rei, the relatively small rise in temperature likely meant he could control the heat output to some degree.

Whether sensing a threat from Rei or simply reacting instinctively, the ice mass once again hurled several ice spears toward him.

Rei's response was simply to manipulate the magic power within the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour he wore.

With that alone, all the ice spears heading for Rei vanished... and as if in return, Rei lightly waved his hand.

In response to that movement, the crimson magic power of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour that had enveloped Rei detached and flew out... Upon touching the ice mass, the very next instant, that ice mass vanished.

"But fire can sometimes overcome water or ice... through evaporation."
