The cyclops that newly appeared before Mireine and the others clearly possessed a presence unlike any other cyclops.

While ordinary Cyclopes were barely over four meters tall, this one was nearly five meters.

Furthermore, while the skin of ordinary Cyclopses was green, its skin was red. The horns protruding from its head were also larger and sharper than those of other Cyclopses.

But the most significant difference was likely the weapon this cyclops carried.

The others wielded what could barely be called clubs—more like crude wooden clubs fashioned from nearby trees.

But the red cyclops wielded a hammer made of metal.

Moreover, this hammer was enormous, seemingly designed from the start for the red cyclops's nearly five-meter height. It was far too large for any human to wield.

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHH!"

The Cyclops roared in frustration, smashing the hammer into the ground.

Instantly, the ground where the hammer struck caved in, forming a large crater. Simultaneously, lightning bolts shot out from the center of the crater, streaking across the ground.

"Gyaaaaaaaah!"

One of the Cyclopes, struck by the lightning bolt, screamed in agony.

Finding the screams amusing, the red cyclops raised its hammer and slammed it into the ground once more.

Lightning streaked across the ground once more, causing the Cyclopes it touched to scream.

The lightning's power itself probably wasn't that strong, but that didn't mean it didn't hurt.

"Ugh, this sucks. What is that Cyclops holding...?"

Mireine, her face twisted in utter disgust, turned her gaze toward Surnin.

Surnin, receiving that gaze, nodded with a serious expression and spoke in a low voice.

"Yes. It's definitely a magic item. And the fact that even a Cyclops can use it means it requires very little magical energy to activate."

"...Why can a cyclops even use magic items in the first place?"

Johanna, who had been listening to their conversation, spoke with such bitterness that most present couldn't help but nod in agreement.

Cyclopes are fundamentally brute-force monsters, relying on their massive bodies and the strength derived from them for attacks. Yet, for some reason, the Cyclops currently in Johanna and the others' sights was using a magic item that required magical power.

"It's either a higher-tier species or a rare one. Unfortunately, I can't tell which."

The red cyclops enjoyed repeatedly smashing its hammer into the ground, making the other cyclops scream.

Watching this, Surunin murmured, unsure what to do.

Even against five ordinary cyclopes, they had no chance of winning if they fought alone.

That was precisely why he'd sent Deets to call for reinforcements. But those reinforcements were intended to handle five ordinary cyclopes.

And whether he could bring reinforcements back quickly was another matter entirely.

Should they retreat for now?

Such thoughts flashed through Surnin's mind.

(That red cyclops clearly possesses strength incomparable to the others. And above all, that magic item is troublesome. I don't know how it was obtained, but that hammer is amplifying the red cyclops' power several times over, maybe even dozens of times. If only it were wielding a club like the others, we might have a chance...)

Muttering to himself, Surnin refocused his gaze on the red cyclops.

In that instant, a chill ran down Surnin's spine.

Their eyes met.

The eyes of the red cyclops, who just seconds ago had been reveling in tormenting the other cyclops, and Surnin's eyes.

The red cyclops was about fifty meters away from where Surnin and his group were hiding, and they were concealed among the trees.

Moreover, the red cyclops had been using what appeared to be a magic item—a hammer—to generate lightning, clearly enjoying the sight of the other cyclops in pain.

And yet, for some reason, the red cyclops and Surnin's eyes were locked together now.

The ordinary cyclopes surrounding the red cyclops, perhaps puzzled that the lightning suddenly attacking them had stopped, looked confusedly in unison toward where the red cyclops was gazing.

A strange silence spread around them.

If they moved, their presence would undoubtedly be detected over there. Yet, if they stayed put without fleeing, they would inevitably be discovered.

(...No, judging by the red cyclops' demeanor, it's highly likely they've already been spotted.)

Yet they remained frozen, instinctively realizing that red cyclops was orders of magnitude stronger than the ordinary ones.

Normally, a rare species is treated as one rank higher than the base monster it's derived from.

A prime example is Set.

While ordinary griffins are treated as rank A monsters, Set is recognized by others as possessing power equivalent to a rank S monster due to being a rare species.

But the red cyclops in Surunin's line of sight seemed like an exception.

"We're getting out of here!"

In a situation where nothing but despair seemed possible, Mireine was the first to act.

At her cry, Surnin reflexively moved his body, instantly retreating backward.

There was no longer any room to consider whether they'd been spotted by the enemy.

Just run. With only that thought in her mind, she began sprinting toward where the carriage was parked.

Naturally, moving silently to avoid detection was utterly impossible under these circumstances, and the sound reached the red cyclops waiting at a distance.

"Gwaaaaah?"

But... only the red cyclops had noticed. The ordinary cyclops showed no sign of having heard the sound.

Cyclopes are known for their exceptionally high strength and stamina, but their senses are by no means sharp.

Indeed, if Cyclopes possessed keen senses alongside their immense strength and stamina, they wouldn't be classified as Rank C monsters, but as much higher-ranked creatures.

That's precisely why they didn't notice Mireine and the others moving through the forest, snapping branches as they went.

...Except for the red Cyclops, that is.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

A massive roar echoed throughout the area.

Hearing it, the Cyclopes frantically grabbed their clubs—their weapons—and charged toward where Mireine and the others were.

That said, the Cyclops' running speed was far from swift.

This is likely another reason why the Cyclops, capable of delivering powerful blows, is classified as a Rank C monster.

However, their massive size meant each stride covered a great distance. Though slow and heavy, escaping them was by no means easy.

While bushes and overgrown trees might hinder a human, obstacles like these were practically nonexistent for a colossal creature like a Cyclops.

With a dull thud, the five Cyclops pursued Mireine and the others.

At first, the Cyclopes didn't understand why they were running towards the thickets, but suddenly one caught sight of Surnin.

Mireine and Johanna were the oldest among them, and being mages—a profession not particularly adept at physical movement—they wore cumbersome equipment like cloaks and staffs.

Spotting a human figure, the Cyclopes naturally understood what they were chasing. Their speed increased as they ran to fill their stomachs, unsatisfied by the deer they had devoured earlier.

"GAAAAAAH!"

One cyclops let out a huge roar and hurled the club it held at Surunin.

The club, far too large for any ordinary human to lift, spun through the air toward Surunin... but mid-flight, it slammed into a nearby tree, sending a thunderous crash echoing through the area.

The tree snapped, but fortunately, this slowed the club's momentum, and it fell to the ground.

"We'll keep our distance and move on horseback!"

"Back to Gilm!?"

Johanna shouted at Mireine's command.

Yet, her cry carried a strong sense of acceptance.

They knew they couldn't defeat the red cyclops alone.

They hadn't actually fought it; they'd only seen it from afar.

Despite that, they instinctively understood that red Cyclops was stronger than them.

And besides the red cyclops, there were five regular cyclopses present.

If it came to a fight, there was no way they could win.

"Of course! If we fight and get wiped out, these things might go to Gilm! We sent Deets to Gilm, but who knows if that'll even work... Ouch!"

Running through the thicket, a low-hanging branch grazed her cheek, leaving a faint scratch.

Even so, there was no time to spare for such a minor pain now. They ran relentlessly toward where the horses were kept.

Behind her came the ferocious cries of five Cyclopes.

Determined not to let their prey escape, they pushed through the trees, chasing after Mireine and the others.

"Haa, haa, haa... I do train, you know. Guess it wasn't enough."

Surnin muttered while running, his staff in hand.

The wand, a magical catalyst, was particularly cumbersome for running, but as a mage, he couldn't simply discard it.

Wizards, often called ordinary people if they can't use magic, exist precisely because they can wield it.

And magic is something that holds the potential for a dramatic reversal when they find themselves in danger.

"Huh!? I'm moving away!"

Ekril, who had been watching Surnin, called out and broke away from the group.

Moving stealthily among the trees, she managed to slip away from the group unnoticed by the cyclops and swiftly climbed a nearby tree.

Naturally agile, her speed rivaled that of a thief.

Hooking her foot on a branch, she drew an arrow from the quiver on her back and drew the bow she held in her hand.

The arrow flew straight toward the cyclops leading the pursuit of Mireine and the others.

"GAAAAAAH!"

The sharp pain from the arrow piercing his right arm made the Cyclops roar in agony.

However, for a Cyclops with a massive body over four meters tall, an arrow was painful when it struck, but it didn't possess enough power to be considered fatal.

With the arrow still lodged in his right arm, he angrily swung his fist, easily snapping a nearby tree trunk about fifty centimeters thick. Then, as if he'd forgotten the arrow was still embedded, he resumed chasing Mireine and the others.

While breaking the tree in frustration, another Cyclops chasing from behind overtook it, leaving it at the rear of the group. It showed no sign of caring.

Still, the fact that the cyclops who had been in the lead had fallen to the rear meant they had bought at least some time against the cyclops pursuing Mireine and the others.

(If only I'd bought poison...)

Ecuril regretted this as he fired arrows at the cyclops now leading the pack once more.

Normally, the target for extermination was a single ordinary Cyclops, and against such an opponent, the Scorching Wind alone would have been more than enough to secure victory.

Furthermore, Johanna—who shared a rivalry-turned-friendship with Mireine—and her companions had somehow joined the hunt. Together, they formed a formidable force more than capable of handling a single Cyclops.

Seeing this, Ekril hesitated about buying poison but ultimately decided against it.

The Cyclops, a creature best described as an extreme brute with a brain the size of a pea, also possessed high healing abilities.

Any poison effective against such an opponent would be incredibly expensive.

(Honestly, I don't know if it's a rare or higher-tier cyclops, but why is there even such a thing? Cyclops usually act alone. ...It's definitely that red one's fault!)

With inner irritation boiling, he fired several more arrows.

Each time, an arrow pierced the body of the lead cyclops, eliciting a roar mingled with pain and frustration.

After several minutes, Ekril judged it was about time they'd spot her location. Refusing to be left behind, she leapt from the branch she'd been using as a foothold... The next instant, a fierce impact struck her body, sending her tumbling straight to the ground.

Simultaneously, the sound of destruction echoed around him.

If Ekril had been able to glance back at the branch he'd been standing on before losing consciousness, he would have seen the tree he'd been perched on snapped clean in half by the red cyclops's blow, charred from the lightning that erupted where the hammer struck.

...And then, he would have seen the griffin approaching him from above, and the familiar figure riding its back, wielding a large scythe.
