"Now then, to celebrate Lord Rei successfully completing his mission at the Officer Academy, and to pray for our reunion with Lord Rei... Cheers!"

"Cheers!"

At Erinde's words, those attending the farewell party raised their glasses high and let out a toast.

Of course, Rei, the star of this farewell party, also raised his cup high and joined the chorus.

...Since Rei couldn't handle alcohol well, the contents of his cup weren't wine or ale, but tea.

Taking a sip of that tea and glancing around, Rei noticed an unexpectedly large number of people had gathered for this farewell banquet.

The banquet was being held in the plaza right behind the staff dormitory. ...Yes, it was the very place where Rei trained almost every morning.

It seemed the place was originally treated as a multipurpose square, so when it was decided to hold the farewell party here, it was chosen without any particular objection.

Today, with classes at the academy, it was already past evening.

Even though spring had arrived, the sun still set early, of course.

Numerous magic items for light were prepared at the farewell party venue, and even in spring, the nights were still cold.

Therefore, several bonfires served as light sources, creating what looked to Rei's eyes like a somewhat fantastical atmosphere.

"Grr?"

Beside Rei, Set tilted his head, asking "What's up?" as he promptly stuck his beak into the food on the plate placed on the ground.

It had been about two months since they started living in this area. Set's presence was reasonably well-known now, and since it was understood that he posed no danger as long as they didn't provoke him, his presence beside Rei didn't cause a commotion.

Still, some people admittedly found Set frightening. But seeing him quietly eating beside Rei, they'd likely conclude he wasn't about to suddenly go wild.

"No, it's nothing. I'm just happy so many people gathered for you and me like this. ...Well, to be precise, the main reason was probably that they wanted to throw a banquet under the pretext of a farewell party."

Even so, the costs for this banquet—the food, alcohol, and such—were covered by the farewell party attendees.

All Rei did was provide some firewood for the bonfire, thinking it was the least he could do.

Of course, the Misty Ring contained plenty of firewood, so it wasn't like he'd gone out of his way for anything.

Still, that was probably a relief for the participants, to be honest.

Even in spring, mornings and nights were still quite cold.

You can never have too much firewood.

"Rei. Are you really going back to Gilm? If possible, let's study magic a little longer."

Not much time had passed since the farewell party began, but Samaloon, her cheeks flushed from drink, said this to Rei.

Though not as much as Rei, Samaloon probably wasn't very good with alcohol either.

"Well, what can I say? My original promise was to stay here only until spring. And it seems the royalist faction business has mostly been settled."

The very reason Rei had become an instructor at the officer academy in the first place: the royalist faction's disciplinary crackdown.

That had progressed considerably over the past two months or so.

Only those who had committed truly unforgivable acts had been punished. Many others had committed offenses, big or small, but they had been overlooked.

But having seen with their own eyes how those who'd acted recklessly until now were all punished, they'd surely think repeating such behavior would be suicide and would definitely behave themselves from now on.

(Though, there are plenty of fools among the nobility. ...It's written as 'noble clan' but read as 'nobility'. Probably, their quietness is only temporary; once time passes, some troublemakers will start causing a ruckus again.)

While dealing with Samaloon and reaching for the skewered meat on the plate, Rei pondered.

The royalist faction was undoubtedly the largest and most powerful within the Kingdom of Mireana.

But conversely, it's also a motley crew, meaning it's practically impossible to fully control them.

"Rei, you're really leaving? I thought it'd be fun to keep teaching at the academy with you."

The next voice to reach Rei belonged to a male instructor he often encountered in the mess hall.

They often chatted during meals, and Rei liked that he didn't judge him by appearances.

"Like I told Samaloon, my home base is still Gilm. I'm only here for work."

"Oh... Set's leaving too, right? That's kind of a shame."

"Yeah, yeah."

The two female instructors exchanged words with regretful expressions while feeding Set his sandwich.

Watching them, Rei found himself thinking that for some reason, it was mostly women who doted on Set.

Like the leader of that Burning Wind group or that former guerrilla fighter, for instance.

"That's how it goes. But if you really want to see Set, there's one way."

The moment those words slipped from Rei's mouth, not only the two women talking, but several others nearby also turned their attention toward him.

But what came out of Rei's mouth was a single, merciless sentence.

"Quit this academy and come to Gilm."

'......'

The unexpected words silenced those who had been hoping.

Those who had pinned their hopes on Rei's words probably expected her to say she'd quit being an academy instructor but continue adventuring here.

But what came out of Rei's mouth was completely different from any such expectation.

"Grrr?"

Perhaps noticing the atmosphere around him, Set lifted his face from his plate of stew and glanced at those nearby.

"Sorry, but I don't plan on moving our base from Gilm. ...At least not for now."

That final remark was likely due to the Holy Light Church issue.

Though it was unclear what methods they'd used, the Holy Light Church's roots were now spreading throughout the Kingdom of Mireana.

When that reached Gilm and became unavoidable... only then would Rei consider whether to move the headquarters from Gilm to another location.

Of course, he wasn't considering moving the headquarters the instant the Holy Light Church's hand reached Gilm.

If possible, the possibility of counterattacking the Holy Light Church also existed.

"Yes, that's unfortunate."

"Yes. I would have liked to play with Set a little longer..."

The instructors, who had been silent at Rei's words, didn't press further.

Set was certainly adorable, and they'd love to play with him occasionally if possible. Still, they hadn't considered moving just for that...

In that regard, she was different from a certain female adventurer who used to be in the guerrilla squad.

That person, famous even in Gilm as a Set enthusiast, would gladly follow along for a move... no, she did follow. Even crossing national borders.

(Well, not everyone could do the same thing, I suppose.)

For now, satisfied with the explanation about Set, Rei once again savored the food.

The food served at this farewell party was different from what was served in the staff dormitory cafeteria.

Ronald had prepared the ingredients himself.

Even though spring had arrived, there wasn't exactly an abundance of ingredients yet.

Still, gathering this much was only possible thanks to the power of the Quent Duke's household.

"These skewers are delicious, huh!?"

"And this bread... Hmm. It's just regular bread, but it's strangely delicious."

"Oh, speaking of bread, there was some bread over there with nuts kneaded into it. It was so good, freshly baked, I could have eaten as much as I wanted."

"Really!? Nut bread isn't that rare, though."

"The ingredients are different, and the cook's skill is different too. We better hurry and get some before it's gone!"

"Dammit, I won't let them beat me!"

Those who usually stood as instructors teaching their students were now letting loose and having a blast.

It was partly because it was a party, but it was also because they were sad that Rei would be leaving tomorrow.

"Here, Rei. Try this too. We made it ourselves."

"Well, 'made' is a bit of an exaggeration—it's just sandwiches."

"Hey, what are you talking about? You're the one who didn't even make the sandwich, you just sliced it!"

"Huh? Cut the crap. The way you slice a sandwich totally changes the taste, texture, and mouthfeel, you know? Honestly, you're the one slacking off, just stuffing ingredients between bread."

For the female instructors and staff, Set was cute, but Rei was in a league of her own in that regard.

He wasn't overly muscular to the point of being intimidating; in fact, his appearance could best be described as slender. Add to that facial features that could be called feminine, and it was only natural he was popular with the female residents in the dorm.

...Though to be precise, that popularity felt more like affection for a younger brother than for a man.

Even so, if there was someone he had feelings for, naturally, as a guy, he wasn't going to be happy about it...

"Hmph. Just because he's a little famous and strong..."

One of the men watching the women swarm around Rei, feeding him food as if he were some kind of pet, snorted in displeasure and took a long swig from his glass, filled to the brim with ale.

"A high-rank adventurer with a nickname. Plus, if his strength is real, it's only natural he'd be popular. Don't be so sulky. Even if he's popular with women, it's more like they see him as a pet or a cuddly animal, you know? They're not looking at him as a man, so don't worry about it."

"I know that, I know. But it still sucks, what can I do? Seriously, getting to rank B at that age? What's that even about? At first I thought it was thanks to the griffin's power, but..."

He stopped himself there.

He had once peeked in on Rei during a mock battle.

The fighting he witnessed there was far beyond what any man could handle.

"Excuse me!"

Suddenly, that voice echoed through the party venue.

What happened? Those attending the party turned their gaze toward the source of the voice. There stood a cadet in the academy uniform.

This man possessed an overwhelming confidence in himself. Most instructors could tell at a glance that he came from a noble family.

Moreover, being a highly renowned figure within the academy itself, many knew him personally.

"Inslah?"

It was unclear who murmured the name, but the sound still carried clearly through the room.

Insuura. A fourth-year S-class cadet, highly skilled but also extremely arrogant—a difficult student for instructors to handle.

...Yes. He was.

The reason he was spoken of in the past tense was undoubtedly because Insula's personality had changed drastically over the past two months.

Belonging to the Fourth Year S Class—arguably the most elite class in this officer academy—and boasting top-tier grades.

That arrogance, which had grown as a result, was swiftly and decisively crushed by Rei.

That alone didn't completely change her personality, but her arrogance had certainly been subdued compared to before.

"What is it? As you can see, we're holding a farewell party for Rei right now. If possible, I'd appreciate it if you could come back tomorrow."

Glink, who was near Insula, spoke up.

"Instructor... Actually, I came because I needed to speak with Rei."

Glink addressed him as Instructor, while addressing Rei by his first name alone was standard practice, so Glink continued speaking without concern.

"Rei-san is over there... What is it? As you can see, we're holding a farewell party for Rei-san right now. If it's not urgent, perhaps after the party, or tomorrow before Rei-san leaves Graciall..."

Spotting Rei looking puzzled at him from where he stood, Insula left Glink behind and walked straight toward Rei.

Amidst the murmurs of the surrounding party attendees, Insula stood directly before Rei.

Several instructors, noticing Insula's tense demeanor, readied themselves to act immediately if anything happened.

Considering Insula's behavior up until two months ago, it was only natural to worry she might do something strange to Rei.

While she had recently stopped looking down on people like she used to, it was only natural that many still doubted her personality could change so quickly.

Sensing the tension in the air, Set, who had been savoring steamed oak meat beside Rei, also turned his gaze toward Insula.

"What's up?"

As the surrounding silence fell, Rei asked Insula... The next instant, Insula reached into his coat, pulled something out, and thrust it swiftly toward Rei.
