"Phew... To be honest, this was a bit unexpected."

"Grr..."

In response to Rei’s words, Set let out a meow as if to agree.

Before the eyes of the man and the beast, nothing but the empty night stretched out before them.

Yet, just a few minutes ago, a fire whirl had been swirling through that very space—a fire whirl that had spawned blades of wind from every direction.

Those wind blades were created using the Fuma Ore that Rei had received as a reward from Liberte... specifically, the Fuma Ore he’d received as an additional reward after being asked to demonstrate the power of the Liberte—no, the Kerebel Duke—and to launch a sort of counterattack against the Seisor House by displaying the fire whirlwind.

That said, he hadn’t used the entire barrel—only about half of it.

(Looking back now, the fact that they so readily prepared an additional barrel of Fuma Ore like this means they probably knew from the start that I would accept this request.)

I can’t say I didn’t feel a little taken advantage of by that, but I still received more than enough compensation, and above all, I was able to test out a fire whirlwind using Fuma Ore, so Rei was thoroughly satisfied.

“I never expected a Wind Blade to appear. …Though there’s no doubt it’s become more vicious.”

Until he actually used the Fuma Ore, he hadn’t known exactly what kind of effect it would have.

For that reason, the outcome was unexpected, but at the same time, Rei’s honest feeling was that he was glad he hadn’t used it in actual combat right away.

Wind Blade… In reality, it wasn’t a blade in the physical sense, but an intangible blade composed of wind.

(Back in Japan, there were things like beam sabers in manga and anime... Is this Wind Blade similar in a way? ...Though wind and beams are completely different.)

Recalling his time in Japan, Rei stroked Set while gazing up at the sky.

It was beautiful... even though it was a winter night, not a single cloud marred the view, and the moon and stars shone brilliantly.

As Rei gazed at the sky, she eventually sensed a presence approaching her from the darkness of the night.

Perhaps because the other party wasn’t even trying to hide their presence or footsteps, she could clearly hear them approaching.

“What’s up, Elena? Do you need something from me?”

Rei asked without even looking up, but the person called by that name didn’t seem particularly taken aback and spoke up.

“I don’t really have any business with you. I just came to check on you, Rei. So, how was the Fuma ore?”

“It’s hard to say. All I can say is, it’s exactly as you see it.”

“The Wind Blade, huh. …If we send that toward the enemy’s base, it looks like we can expect quite a bit of destructive power.”

“I won’t deny that. …To be honest, I never imagined it would turn out to be such a vicious weapon.”

That said, what Rei had imagined was something like continuously firing Wind Blades—or something akin to Death Scythe’s Flying Slash—in all directions. In terms of destructive power, annihilation potential, and offensive capability, that would actually be even more vicious than the Wind Blade.

In that sense, Rei’s description of it as a bit of a letdown might be fitting.

“But still… at least it looked impressive, didn’t it?”

“Hmm. There’s no doubt it was flashy. Most of the spectators were delighted. …Though there were a few who were terrified.”

“Ah… well, I suppose so.”

At Elena’s words, Rei wore an expression of understanding.

From a purely Noble Faction perspective, Rei is, after all, a member of the Neutral Faction.

Currently, relations between the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction are good, but that doesn’t mean it will last forever.

If relations between the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction sour and lead to a conflict, the members of the Noble Faction would end up fighting Rei.

And if it came to that, would Elena—the very symbol of the Noble Faction—fight Rei? That’s a bit of a gray area.

The nature of the relationship between Rei and Elena is already widely known.

Given that, I couldn’t imagine Elena fighting Rei.

...Conversely, that implies Rei might not be able to fight Elena either, but for some reason, I seem to believe that Rei could do it.

Mind you, considering that Rei has shown no mercy in wielding his power even against nobles up until now, and gathering various rumors and information about him, it wouldn’t be strange to reach that conclusion.

“Well, there’s no point in standing here like this forever… Let’s head back.”

“Is that okay?”

“Yeah. It’s not like I was thinking about anything in particular or anything. …Right?”

“Growl?”

Set, addressed by Rei, purrs, “Is that so?”

He’d been keeping quiet so as not to interrupt Rei and Elena’s conversation, but now he rubbed his head against Rei’s, thinking, “You could’ve told me sooner.”

“Hey, Set. What’s with that all of a sudden? Oi, that tickles!”

“Hehe, you two are just the same as always. …Alright, let’s head back over there. Father and the others are probably waiting.”

Saying that, Elena stopped Set, who was playfully wrestling with Rei, and set off walking with the two of them.

“Come to think of it… it’s gotten quite warm.”

A winter night should normally be cold enough to make you shiver.

But, though it’s limited to this specific spot, thanks to the lingering heat from the firestorm, it feels quite warm.

Of course, that warm air would likely be blown away by the wind in a little while.

“It’ll only be for a little while, though. …More importantly, when are you planning to return to Gilm? I’ve already done what I needed to do in Anesis, so anytime is fine with me.”

As they walked, the topic of conversation suddenly shifted.

In response to Rei’s question, Elena paused to think for a moment before speaking.

“I’m sorry, but I’d like you to wait a few more days. Normally, I’d say we could leave tomorrow… but after what happened yesterday, I have a few things I need to take care of.”

“Ah… I suppose so.”

It went without saying that Rei understood exactly what Elena was getting at.

To begin with, the Black Wolf—who was famous throughout Anesis—had died, and Celestan, the leader of the organization connected to him, had also died.

On top of that, the house where Celestine lived had vanished—so much had happened in just a single day.

As someone deeply involved in most of it—and given her status as the daughter of Duke Kerebel—Elena had no choice but to rush about cleaning up the mess left by this incident.

If Elena were to leave Anesis without at least partially handling the aftermath, it would reflect poorly on her.

If there had been some kind of emergency—say, the urgent need to defeat a powerful monster—it would have been a different story.

“I’m sorry.”

Rei shook his head at Elena, who was apologizing again, telling her not to worry about it.

If this had required a few weeks or months, Rei would have had his own reservations, and he might have even gone back to Gilm on his own for a while, only to return to Anesis once some time had passed.

But if it was only a matter of a few days, Rei didn’t see it as a problem.

The Duke of Kerebel’s residence has a library stocked with a vast number of books Rei hasn’t read yet, and he also couldn’t pass up the chance to spar with the knight corps led by Philma.

Or he could head into town and look for some delicious food.

Despite all that, even though Rei had been eating his way around town, he hadn’t yet found anything that could truly be called a specialty of Anesis.

Given that, Rei was eager to find a local specialty dish.

From Rei’s perspective, it was almost like a treasure hunt.

He didn’t know exactly what kind of dish he might find, but there was no doubt that the prospect excited Rei.

……That said, some local specialties are simply inedible or just not something he’d want to eat, so it wasn’t necessarily guaranteed that the local specialty would be delicious.

“Don’t worry about it. I want to explore Anesis myself, too.”

Since arriving in Anesis, he had left the Duke of Kerebel’s residence many times to explore the city.

But as the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, Anesis was, as expected, quite vast.

In fact, a few strolls through the city aren’t nearly enough to see it all.

In that sense, Elena’s suggestion that they stay in Anesis a little longer wasn’t a bad idea at all for Rei.

She must have realized that Rei wasn’t just saying that out of consideration for her. Elena felt relieved… and just then, Rei and the others arrived at the spot where Liberte and the others were waiting.

“Oh, Rei. You really did a great job fulfilling my request. That was a magnificent fire tornado. It was so stunning, it almost took my breath away.”

The first to address Rei was, naturally, Liberte.

This was intended to demonstrate to the other nobles that she and Rei were on friendly terms, while also preventing Rei from feeling uncomfortable due to the other nobles swarming around him.

“I’m glad you enjoyed it. It was quite helpful for me, too, since I was able to conduct an experiment using Fuma Ore.”

Upon hearing the words “Fūma ore,” the nobles began to murmur among themselves.

And rightly so. Fuma Ore is a type of wind-elemental magical metal ore, and its output is by no means abundant, even when considering the entire Kingdom of Mireana.

A low yield means high value, so in that sense, this was a topic of great interest to the nobles present.

(Huh? Did I forget to explain that part?)

Rei, who had been observing the other nobles while conversing with Liberte, thought to himself.

“I see. I’m glad the reward I paid was of use to you, Rei. Well then, since we’ve seen such a magnificent… scene that exceeded our expectations, let’s head back into town. I’d love to linger in this afterglow forever, but with it being this cold, some of us might catch a chill.”

At those words, several nobles nodded in agreement.

As nobles, they were naturally dressed in warm clothing, but even so, being in a place like this on a winter night must still be cold.

If they were as physically conditioned as knights or soldiers, they might have been able to endure the cold.

Just as everyone began heading toward Anesis together, someone approached Rei and Elena.

“Rei, I’m honestly surprised. …I can’t believe you managed to unleash a fiery tornado like that. Now I understand why you’ve earned the nickname ‘Crimson.’”

The one who said this with deep feeling was Brewitt.

Beside him stood Ranigus and Teres.

Even Rei could see the excitement on the faces of those three.

...Perhaps because they were still young, they didn’t seem to mind the cold as much as the other nobles.

“You guys were here too? …Well, you were here yesterday, so I guess it’s not surprising you’re here.”

Rei said this upon seeing the three familiar faces, but a smile played on his lips.

It was also true that it was more relaxing to be with acquaintances like this than to be a spectacle for unfamiliar nobles… or at least those he’d only seen before.

Though, if there were a good reason, Rei wouldn’t mind being the center of attention himself.

“To be honest, it was a bit of a hassle getting out here, but I’m glad I came. That was a truly amazing sight.”

Uncharacteristically, Ranigus said this with a look of excitement on his face.

Ranigus was aware of Rei’s abilities, but there was still a huge difference between knowing about them and seeing them with his own eyes.

Especially for Ranigus, who was part of the Royal Faction, his opportunities to interact with Rei were inevitably few.

It wasn’t as if they never crossed paths at all, but the frequency was inevitably low.

“I’m glad you enjoyed it.”

“Ah… So, I have one question.”

Brewitt, who had been listening to Rei and Ranigus’s conversation, addressed Rei in this manner.

Even to Rei, Brewitt’s demeanor—so different from his usual self—felt slightly off.

“What’s up? That’s unusual for Brewitt.”

“Yeah. The Brewitt I know was supposed to be more… rough around the edges.”

At Rei’s words, Teres nodded in agreement.

Ara, who had somehow made her way over to Elena, also nodded in response to Rei’s words.

“Shut up. …Anyway, I’ve got a question. Do you think I could pull off that flame tornado thing too?”

“Well… I don’t know about that. You might be able to do something similar, but I don’t think you could do it exactly the same way.”

Since it was a combination of skills and magic, he told her that he didn’t think Brewitt could do exactly the same thing… but at those words, Brewitt wore a disappointed expression.
