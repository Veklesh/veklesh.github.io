The morning after the sand ship was launched, Rei was found beside the magic tent.

The magic tent had been pitched near the sand ship.

In the end, they decided to have Set stay in the captain's quarters, and Rei and the others slept in the magic tent as usual.

...Though, even though they slept together inside the magic tent, it wasn't like they were sharing a bed with Elena and the others.

The magic tent opened up into a spacious room, and that's all it meant—they slept there together.

Marina had half-teasingly, half-seriously suggested, "Want to sleep together?", but Rei ignored it and slept alone.

Rei's honest thought was that since they had the Misty Ring, they should have brought Elena's carriage, which had similar functions.

But without horses to pull it, it was useless as a carriage... though as a substitute for a magic tent, it would have served perfectly well.

As for horses to pull it, creatures couldn't be stored in the Misty Ring, so bringing them wasn't possible. But in the current situation, they had horses seized from Rossi's cavalry.

(Though, some of the horses were starting to look unwell too. Water could be managed with Marina's spirit magic, but food would need proper fodder, not just whatever grass grew around here. ...And even that grass—it was fine now while the grasslands stretched on, but these grasslands wouldn't go on forever.)

Surveying the grasslands spreading out around them, Rei contemplated what lay ahead.

Fortunately, the number of horses wasn't that large.

Even so, with about thirty horses, carrying enough fodder for that many would normally require tremendous effort. But for Rei, who possessed Misty Ring, such worries were unnecessary.

He could buy as much as he needed without any problem at all.

"Good morning, Rei-san."

As he pondered the horses, that voice reached his ears.

Rei turned his gaze toward the source of the voice and saw one of the Resistance members.

He was clearly older than Rei, but the look in his eyes as he regarded Rei held a spark of respect.

Since gaining his nickname, Rei had grown accustomed to that kind of look—though he was often underestimated based on his appearance—and now such gazes no longer bothered him.

"Ah, you're early."

As Rei said, it was still early morning.

There was still nearly an hour left until the breakfast time they had set for seven in the morning.

It was only natural for Rei to wonder why a Resistance man would emerge from the sand ship at such an hour.

That said, while Rei was generally not a morning person, that only applied when sleeping at an inn. During a mission like this one, he wasn't quite so weak in the mornings.

Of course, it wasn't absolute, so he did occasionally oversleep.

For now, he had managed to wake up normally today.

...Though it wasn't entirely accurate to say that being called by name in someone's sleep hadn't affected him, even if he hadn't shared a bed with Elena and the others.

"Ahaha. I never imagined I'd be able to sleep on such a splendid ship!"

The country of Reburina does have ships.

But only the country itself, or merchants connected to it, possess them.

The ships people like us in the Resistance have ever boarded were small enough that just a few people would fill them up.

There was absolutely no chance they'd ever board a ship as large as a Sand Ship.

As they talked like this, time passed. Soon, people began coming down from inside the Sand Ship... and preparations for morning routines and breakfast commenced.

Naturally, rumors about the Sand Ships were circulating, and as Rei and the others returned to the road, several individuals who appeared to be merchants attempted to approach them.

However, time was extremely precious to Rei and the others now. Only those merchants heading in the same direction as them managed to get any information.

Among the merchants, some were actually headed in the opposite direction from Rei and her companions. Yet, hoping to glean even a little information about the sand ship, several turned back toward the direction they had come from.

"So, what exactly was that ship?"

"I'm sorry, but I'm not allowed to disclose any information about that ship."

"Come on, just a little bit wouldn't hurt. From what I can tell, you all seem to have your reasons. If it's alright with you, I'd be happy to help out in any way I can."

The merchant driving the carriage addressed the quieter-looking woman among Rei and the others.

Having a carriage meant he was likely a merchant operating on a larger scale than those traveling on foot, but even so, the goods he carried were barely a drop in the bucket for Rei and the others.

The woman seemed to understand this too, and she spoke apologetically to the merchant sitting on the driver's seat of the carriage moving alongside them.

"Well, I'm afraid I can't really answer that myself. Could you ask Rei-san, who's leading the group?"

"Rei-san, you say..."

Hearing the woman's words, the merchant turned his gaze toward Rei at the front of the group.

Seeing him walking while stroking a griffin, it wasn't difficult to guess who that person was.

Merchants must be sharp when it comes to information.

Given that, it was impossible for him not to know of Rei, the one with the nickname Crimson.

And as a merchant, it was only natural to want to befriend a celebrity like Rei... but there was a reason he couldn't.

Because right now, numerous merchants were gathered around the group Rei led.

Those merchants naturally sought information about the sand ship Rei possessed, and they also desired to befriend Rei himself... and furthermore, Elena, nicknamed the Princess General, who was close to Rei.

They understood that becoming friendly with those two... and the members of the Crimson Wings led by Rei would likely open up significant business opportunities.

However, the number of merchants gathered around this group was over ten... More precisely, since several merchants often traveled together within a single caravan, there were about ten such groups present.

And among the gathered merchants, there were quite a few familiar faces.

All these merchants were wary of each other, keeping a watchful eye on one another.

If just one merchant were to make a move like speaking to Rei here, they would incur the resentment of the other merchants.

While it was natural for merchants to act to make a profit, doing anything to incur the resentment of other merchants would hinder future business.

Given that, avoiding unnecessary conflict was a common desire among the merchants.

(But I can't afford to think that way. There should be a village ahead... Emisma, was it? Ideally, I'd like to settle things before we reach Emisma.)

The merchant man frowned, glancing at Rei walking alongside Set.

If it meant guaranteed profit, he wouldn't mind cutting corners at the expense of other merchants.

Of course, that was only if the profit was substantial enough to justify the resentment of other merchants.

The other merchants also knew there was a village ahead.

Given the size of this group, it was unlikely they could fully resupply on food, water, or other goods at the village. Still, it would undoubtedly reduce his business opportunities.

He wanted to find out more about this group before that happened, and if possible, try to sell them something.

As the merchant pondered, his gaze met that of an acquaintance standing a short distance away.

They exchanged glances... and eventually, both nodded without prompting.

Going it alone to outmaneuver other merchants was risky in many ways, but together, they could manage.

Normally, he wouldn't want to get involved in such a risky endeavor.

But this was undoubtedly a prime business opportunity, and now that there were two of them, they sensed instinctively that they might just pull it off.

The other merchant seemed to feel the same way. After a small nod between them, they drove their carts forward to take the lead.

"Brr, brrrrr..."

"Brrr"

But just as they were about to reach the front of the procession, the two horses pulling the carriage suddenly began acting strangely.

The horses seemed to be expressing their unwillingness to go any further, leaving the merchant bewildered.

He glanced over at the other merchant, who also seemed to be facing the same situation—his horses were clearly whinnying as if they absolutely refused to go any further.

"What do we do?"

"What do you mean, what do we do? We beat the others to this spot. If we end up accomplishing nothing here, that'll be a complete loss of face for us."

"I understand that, but with the carriages in this state..."

"I'll watch your carriage. You go talk to them."

"Kuroga... are you sure?"

"Yeah. But in return, if you need anything, don't just deal with Shumra—run it by me too."

"...Understood."

After that exchange, the man called Shumura left his carriage in Kuroga's care...

"What's this? Feels like he's telling me to leave this to him and go ahead."

The sudden voice made him stop in his tracks.

Shumura and Kuroga both turned their gaze in that direction, where a man stood.

There was no need to wonder who it was.

After all, Shumura and Kuroga had come here specifically to meet this person.

"Rei-san...?"

"Yeah. Seemed like you might have business with me. But... well. I figured you couldn't get close in a carriage, so I came over myself."

Ordinary horses, naturally, were afraid of Set.

Even so, after spending days together, they might stop being afraid of him... But the horses pulling Shumura and the others' carriage had only encountered Set for the first time today, so they couldn't possibly be used to him.

Noticing the carriage approaching them, Rei had come over here himself before the horses could panic and bolt.

Shumura and Kuroga couldn't hide their surprise at Rei's unexpected attitude.

As merchants, showing such obvious emotion was a sign of immaturity, but both agreed it was unavoidable this time.

After all, an adventurer with a notorious reputation had gone out of his way to come to their side.

...From the other merchants standing a little way off, they received glances that were somewhere between resentful and envious.

It was only natural. If Shumura and Kuroga had been in the same position as those merchants, they were confident they would have acted the same way.

For Rei, too, this was undoubtedly a perfect opportunity.

Moving while keeping the merchants in tow would draw unwanted attention.

They were already standing out for all the wrong reasons as a group of nearly a thousand people—mostly women, many still dressed as prostitutes. But with the merchants tagging along, they'd draw even more attention.

He hoped to shake off those merchants at least a little... and perhaps acquire some of the supplies they were lacking.

"I see. Thank you. So... what exactly was that building we saw a little while ago?"

"The building? Ah, that's a Sand Ship. A magic item. You've probably heard of it, right?"

At Rei's words, Shumura and Kuroga seemed to understand, nodding in agreement.

"We've heard of it, but that was a Sand Ship?"

"Yeah, that's it. ...So, what are you guys selling? Depending on the goods, I might buy them all in bulk."

"...Everything?"

At Rei's words, Shumura muttered, momentarily confused by what he was being asked.

Kuroga looked equally perplexed, his expression saying, "Is he serious?"

"Yeah. Especially clothes and that sort of thing—I want as much as possible. Water bottles, food supplies... anything like that, I'll buy whatever you have."

"We have cloth, but actual clothing... As for food, we have a decent amount of preserved goods if that's acceptable. Kuroga, what about you?"

"We have cloth too, but no clothes. If a leather water bag is acceptable, we have those."

At those words, Rei smiled happily.

Fabric was what he needed most right now, while the water bottles and food were just nice-to-haves.

"Understood. Sell me all the cloth and sewing supplies you have. Food and water bottles too, of course. I'd like to buy other things as well, so it'd be helpful if you could gather other merchants here."

"No, that would actually be a favor to us. It wouldn't be right for us to profit while the other merchants miss out."

Saying this, Shumura smiled with relief.

It was a smile born of the fact that, while she had outmaneuvered the other merchants, by sharing the profits rather than monopolizing them, she reduced the chance of being resented unilaterally.
