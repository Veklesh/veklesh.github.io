Since no giant was born in Rossi, the secret to birthing giants must lie within this Mejogo.

Upon hearing this, Rei frowned slightly, realizing his own thinking had been mistaken.

(So the slave collar means nothing? Well, no, it's not that it means nothing in the sense that it forces obedience—it's more that it makes them prostitute themselves of their own free will.)

Slave collars themselves had existed before, but the one Jaya used was vastly different from any Rei had known.

Feeling uneasy about this, Rei spoke up.

"So, what else do you know? I understand that giants aren't born anywhere but Mejogo. Then, what's the reason for that?"

He won't tolerate lies; that's what matters. Overwhelmed by Rei's intensity, which carried that meaning, the man still insisted he was one of the elite and spoke.

"Unfortunately, I don't know. But I'm certain there's something in this underground facility."

"...You don't know? Really? You, who claim to be elite?"

"It's true! This underground facility is restricted to only a select few! You should understand that, since the gatekeeper assigned to it was stronger than us!"

"What do you think?"

"He doesn't seem to be lying. Besides, lying here would be like choosing death right then and there."

At Vihera's words, the man trembled for a moment.

After all, it was obvious that if he told any lies here, he'd instantly join his comrades scattered across the surface.

"I-I'm not lying, of course!"

That very outburst meant there was nothing more of value to be gleaned from him.

Sure, he probably knew other minor details, but Rei and the others were currently in enemy territory.

Given that, there was no time to linger here.

Having gathered the essential information, Rei judged it necessary to move forward... and turned his gaze back to the man.

"I won't kill you."

At those words, the man breathed a sigh of relief.

Even though he'd been told he wouldn't be killed if he had useful information, the possibility of being killed the moment he was no longer needed was still far from zero.

Especially since he was one of Jaya's elite, he'd enjoyed his fair share of perks.

For instance, having exclusive access to his favorite courtesan... and so on.

Precisely because of that, he understood he was far from being well-liked by the female-heavy party members like Rei.

Surviving in such circumstances was an unexpected stroke of luck for the man.

Considering the current situation, it wouldn't have been strange at all if he'd been killed after being forced to spill everything.

"Oh, right. Well then, I'll be going. Staying here would only get in your way!"

Knowing he wouldn't be killed, he needed to disappear from Rei and the others as quickly as possible.

Staying here too long meant there was no guarantee Rei wouldn't change his mind and kill him.

That was the man's reasoning for trying to leave... but before he knew it, Rei, who had somehow gotten hold of the Death Scythe, blocked his escape with its blade.

Faced with that enormous blade thrust before him, the man didn't even think about trying to run away in that situation.

"Um... wasn't the deal that you'd let me go?"

Just a few more steps, and he would have walked right into that blade.

Thinking that, the man cautiously asked Rei.

But when Rei met his gaze, a smile formed beneath his hood as he spoke.

"I said I wouldn't kill you, and neither would the others. But I never said I'd let you escape like this. I need you to show us around this underground facility."

The words that came from Rei's mouth made the man break out in a cold sweat, caused by something entirely different from the summer heat.

They were currently in an underground facility, considerably cooler than the surface.

Despite that, he couldn't stop the sweat pouring down his face.

He forced a voice out of his throat, parched dry by the sudden tension.

"What do you mean by that? I thought I'd be released as is."

"Don't joke around. We don't know anything about this underground facility to begin with! How are we supposed to get to our destination in this situation?"

"Well..."

Faced with Rei's retort, the man had no reply.

He wasn't entirely opposed to the idea of just moving forward randomly, but if he voiced that thought, Rei's scythe would undoubtedly come down on him.

But he also understood that simply sharing what little he knew about the underground facility would only waste time.

Rei demanding he guide them meant exactly that, he realized.

Jaya surely knew intruders had already entered this underground facility.

If that was the case, it was only natural that security measures would be in place, and Jaya would take action to eliminate whoever had broken in.

That's why he was being told to become a barrier... a human shield.

Naturally, the man resented those words. But voicing that resentment would likely get him killed by the man in front of him... or by the other women surrounding him.

Ultimately, the man had only two choices: either obey Rei's orders and become a human shield as they advanced through the underground facility, or gamble on the one in a million chance and try to escape from this place.

A third option—negotiating with Rei and the others—wasn't entirely out of the question... but since he couldn't think of any cards he could use in negotiations, that route was effectively closed.

"What's wrong? Move already. First, let's see... We're heading for something here, right? The giant... or maybe where the bigwigs are. I'll leave that part up to you. But if you make any strange moves, consider yourself dead on the spot."

Rei looked at him with eyes that said he had absolutely no intention of letting the man escape, then delivered that warning.

In terms of reconnaissance, there was indeed the presence of Byune, but this was an enemy stronghold.

Since we don't know what lies within, we still needed a decoy option for emergencies.

(Maybe this is why Byune's skills as a thief aren't improving...)

Even as he wondered if he was being a bit overprotective, Rei pressed the blade of the Death Scythe against the man.

The man, too, must have judged that further resistance would lead to a fatal outcome for himself.

Reluctantly, he began moving deeper into the passageway.

"Well then, let's go."

"Are you sure? You might betray me when it counts."

"If that happens, I'll deal with it accordingly."

Responding to Marina's words, Rei and the others followed the man.

They kept a distance of about five meters, just in case something happened and they got caught in the crossfire.

Suddenly, Rei remembered using canaries in mines.

It seemed somewhat similar to their current situation... but he quickly dismissed the thought, realizing the details would likely be quite different.

In any case, leaving the front guard to the man, Rei and the others advanced down the passageway, wary of someone attacking from behind or perhaps from a hidden passageway.

They didn't know how far word of their intrusion had spread, but at least there was no sign of anyone emerging from the rooms dotted along the passage they were traversing.

It was a very simple underground facility: a single wide passage wide enough for a carriage, with numerous doors lining both sides.

Still, Rei could easily imagine that constructing such extensive facilities underground must have required immense effort.

(Back in Japan, I'd seen TV specials about underground malls... This was like that. Of course, overall, those were more sophisticated than this.)

Thinking such thoughts, Rei and the others proceeded down the passageway.

The underground facility was enveloped in silence, but precisely because of that, a natural tension filled the air around them.

It was only natural, given they were on constant alert for a surprise attack from the other side.

The most tense person of all was undoubtedly the man forced to walk ahead of Rei and the others.

If Jaya made a move, he would undoubtedly be the first to suffer.

"Haa, haa, haa..."

The man possessed the skills befitting someone called an elite.

But that skill was strictly for direct combat; he wasn't particularly adept at reconnaissance like a thief.

That made the current situation particularly harsh for him.

With every step forward, he wondered if a trap might be waiting. If his comrades—or rather, his enemies—might appear from somewhere, intent on eliminating him.

Overcome by such anxieties, he pressed on through the passageway.

Still, after five or ten minutes passed, his tension gradually eased.

Even if he started out moving cautiously, his vigilance would gradually lessen as time passed.

That must have been why. The man grew increasingly bold... until that awareness suddenly... and forever... snapped.

The last thing the man felt was a single question: Huh?

"Damn it, a trap! Watch out!"

If this were, say, a Jaya person using a bow as a weapon, it would be a different story.

For someone like Rei, sensing the killing intent might not have been impossible.

But traps, naturally, involve no human presence, and one cannot sense anything like killing intent.

If Rei and the others had been targeted, dodging or slashing away arrows flying toward them would have been easy enough. ...Though Byune might be a different story.

However, the man before Rei and the others, while he might have been tense earlier, had now relaxed considerably, appearing almost careless.

That's precisely why he couldn't dodge the arrow fired from a trap that carried no killing intent whatsoever.

And the arrow that pierced the man's head wouldn't stop there, of course... One after another, they were fired at Rei and the others standing behind him.

But while the first strike might have startled them somewhat, once they understood what was happening, dealing with it wasn't particularly difficult.

Rei swung the Death Scythe in his hand, effortlessly slicing away the arrows flying toward him.

The others similarly dealt with the arrows aimed at them.

For several tens of seconds, arrows were fired continuously, but eventually, whether they ran out or not, the attack ceased.

"Killing his own men too? No... considering Jaya's personality, it's not that surprising after all."

Glancing at the man whose forehead had been pierced, dead before he could comprehend what had happened, Rei murmured.

He had expected something like this sooner or later, but he hadn't imagined being discarded this quickly.

He was grateful for the information he'd received, but... still, perhaps because he was one of Jaya's men, seeing the corpse brought surprise but no sorrow.

"This is just a normal passageway, right? Yet there's a trap set here... That was a bit unexpected."

Elena murmured, showing no particular disturbance at the sight of the corpse.

In truth, even from Elena's perspective, the man's existence hadn't been particularly interesting.

"Anyway, let's move forward. Since they've clearly shown hostility towards us, I can pretty much guess what methods Jaya will use."

Everyone nodded at Marina's words.

Thus, keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings, Rei and the others pressed onward.

Leading the way was Byune.

As a thief, this was only natural.

Although Vihera looked slightly concerned after the man was killed by an arrow, since they were acting as the party known as Crimson Wings, Byune, participating as a thief, was naturally expected to do her part.

Of course, the others were also keeping watch on the surrounding situation.

Outside the building, Set kept watch to prevent the Jaya people from approaching, but there was no guarantee this underground facility had only one entrance.

The man had said he didn't know, but it wouldn't be strange if there were several other exits leading outside the building.

Furthermore, the possibility of them circling around the underground facility and attacking from behind could not be ruled out.

Proceeding while maintaining vigilance over these points inevitably slowed their pace to some extent.

This was a surprise attack, so Rei thought they should achieve their objective and retreat as quickly as possible...
