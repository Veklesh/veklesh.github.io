The moment he stepped into the lab, the smell of rusty iron assaulted Rei's sense of smell.

Rei possessed senses far sharper than those of ordinary humans. And Set possessed senses even sharper than Rei's.

Set was lying down in front of Azimov's house, but normally, he would have noticed the smell of this much blood.

The reason Rei and Set didn't notice the blood's scent was due to the magic item Azimov was using in his lab.

When creating something through alchemy, a strong foul odor can sometimes waft out, regardless of success or failure.

That stench could easily spread outside the house... or even throughout the entire neighborhood.

In fact, he had once tried something like that before. Upon hearing about it, Pamidor had practically forced him to create a magic item for deodorization.

Its effectiveness was undoubtedly top-notch; in fact, it never caused Rei or Set to detect the scent of blood.

But this time, it had backfired completely.

Upon entering the lab, Rei's eyes widened for just an instant at the sight before him, but he immediately moved to his next action.

The amount of blood spread across the floor was considerable.

Calling it a sea of blood might be a slight exaggeration, but describing it as a river, a swamp, or even a lake of blood wouldn't be far off.

Judging by how little the blood had dried, it hadn't been more than a few minutes since the attack.

It was highly likely the attack had occurred around the same time Rei and the others had arrived at Azimov's house.

Approaching Azimov, Rei pulled a potion from his Misty Ring.

Not just any ordinary potion sold in shops, but a high-quality one.

Even adventurers from Gilm, who earned far more gold than those in other towns or villages precisely because they were in the frontier, couldn't easily afford such potions.

Only someone like Rei, who wasn't short on money, could afford it.

Holding that expensive potion, Rei turned Azimov, who was lying face down on the floor, onto his back.

"I'll do it."

A split second after Rei's action, Marina tore open Azimov's clothes, exposing the wound.

There, a deep gash, clearly made by a blade.

Blood continued to flow from Azimov's body beneath his clothes.

Watching the two, Vihera and Byune shifted their attention to securing the perimeter.

Judging by the wound and the flowing blood, Azimov had been slashed only moments ago.

If so, it wouldn't be strange for the perpetrator to still be nearby.

And seeing Rei and the others treating Azimov, it wasn't entirely impossible that the perpetrator might show themselves to interfere.

It was precisely because these two were present that Rei and Marina could focus entirely on treating Azimov.

Marina tore open Azimov's clothes as he lay on his back, exposing the wound.

The large scar stretching from his right shoulder to his left side was still bleeding.

Despite sustaining such a severe wound, it hadn't reached his internal organs. Was it sheer luck, or was there some other reason?

Rei didn't know the exact reason either, but even so, given the amount of blood loss, it was certain he wouldn't survive much longer in his current state.

"Be grateful,"

After uttering those brief words, Rei poured the potion he held into Azimov's wound.

True to its high potency, the wound closed before their eyes.

At a speed that might seem slightly unsettling to the unaccustomed eye...

If Azimov hadn't been unconscious, Ray would have liked to ask him how it felt.

That Rei could even think such thoughts was likely because the wound had healed.

Still, while the potion could heal the wound, it couldn't restore the blood he'd lost.

Thanks to the potion, death was no longer a threat, but he would still need to rest for a while to compensate for the blood he'd lost.

"The enemy?"

Relieved that Rei had finished treating Azimov, Marina asked Vihera, who was on guard against enemy attack.

But the only response was a shake of the head.

"...Right. What do we do?"

Rei, meeting her gaze, frowned and scanned the surroundings.

Though Rei was fairly close with Azimov, he still didn't have a complete grasp of what was where in this lab.

Even if some magic item had been stolen from the lab, without knowing what magic items were originally in this room, he couldn't possibly tell.

(Hm?)

Thinking it through, Rei suddenly realized something.

Sleipnir Boots were nowhere to be found in this room.

It was known that Rei was coming today specifically to receive the improved Sleipnir Boots. If that was the case, then Azimov would naturally have been doing the final check on the Sleipnir Boots.

Since Azimov was found collapsed in this lab, it was highly probable he had been conducting that final inspection of the Sleipnir Boots here.

Which meant it was strange that the Sleipnir Boots weren't here.

"Damn it! They're after the Sleipnir Boots!?"

Rei shouted, his voice dripping with impatience.

Had they come to Azimov's house targeting the Sleipnir Boots from the start, or had the Sleipnir Boots just happened to be among several other magic items they'd stolen?

He couldn't tell which it was, but one thing was certain: the Sleipnir Boots were nowhere to be found in the room.

It couldn't be ruled out that the final check had already been made, and the Sleipnir Boots were somewhere else.

But if he let himself indulge in such complacent thoughts, and it turned out the Sleipnir Boots had indeed been stolen... then regret would be useless.

(But what to do? How to track down whoever escaped from here? There were already clues... no, a scent!)

No one was here now, but since someone had been here, a scent was certain to remain.

The magic item prevents the scent from escaping the room, but conversely, that means the scent of the person responsible for this incident is still inside this lab.

He hesitated for a moment, but decided catching the culprit was the top priority now and shouted.

「Set！」

Set, having seen Rei and the others enter Azimov's house, lay sprawled out, basking in the warm, spring-like sunlight.

Normally, someone would come to play with him when he lay like this, but unfortunately, no one was passing by now, so he simply enjoyed basking in the sun.

Even in the depths of winter... even when the temperature dropped below freezing, Set could sleep without a care. Still, it was only natural he preferred the warm sunlight.

Set was drifting in a half-doze when... his keen hearing didn't miss the voice calling his name from inside Azimov's house.

It was Rei, whom he adored, calling him. And not just to play, but sounding genuinely desperate.

The moment he understood Rei's cry, his half-asleep consciousness snapped instantly awake, and his usually gentle eyes sharpened as he focused on the Azimov house.

The door to Azimov's house wasn't wide enough for Set to enter.

He possessed the skill to alter his size, but even that wasn't enough to move freely inside Azimov's house.

Set's actions were swift once he thought this.

If he couldn't get in through the door, he'd just go directly to where Rei was calling from.

He made that judgment, stood up, and headed not into Azimov's house, but toward the garden.

It seemed completely neglected, even after winter ended and spring arrived.

Set passed through the wildly overgrown garden in an instant and arrived directly outside the laboratory window where Rei's voice was coming from.

He smashed the window and thrust his face through.

Being a room dedicated to alchemy research, the walls and windows were naturally built sturdy and reinforced with alchemy.

But to Set's physical prowess, whether it was reinforced or not didn't matter at all.

The voice calling Rei's name didn't have its usual soft, gentle tone; instead, it carried a sense of desperation.

It made him fear that Rei was in imminent danger.

That was precisely why Set resorted to such forceful tactics, something he normally wouldn't do.

Shattering the window, Set forced his way into the laboratory.

...Considering not only the window but also the surrounding wall had been destroyed, the damage was likely quite extensive.

And with Set bursting in like that, it was only natural that papers, materials, and other things—things Rei couldn't even guess the purpose of—began tumbling onto the floor one after another.

But naturally, there was no time to worry about such trivial matters—trivial to Rei and the others, at least.

Facing Set, who had broken through the window and wall, Rei shouted sharply.

"Set, can you smell anyone else in this room besides us and Azimov?"

"Guruu... Grrrr!"

Set responded sharply to Rei's words.

What that meant was obvious without needing to think about it.

Especially since activating his heightened sense of smell skill made his already keen nose even sharper.

And immediately, Set succeeded in detecting the scent of someone else in the room besides Rei and Azimov.

"Grrr..."

Set's gaze fell on the spot just outside where he had burst in... meaning whoever attacked Azimov had escaped through this window.

(No, come to think of it, that makes sense. To begin with, there wasn't much time between Azimov being attacked and us arriving at this house. In fact, it's entirely possible someone was still in this lab when I called out.)

Despite trying to stay calm, the sudden turn of events had clearly unsettled him.

After a few seconds of reflection, I finally spoke.

"Me... and Vihera, Byune will follow after Set. Marina, take Azimov to a safe place, alert the guards... and yes. Contact Pamidor too."

Each nodded at his words. Marina used spirit magic to conjure water and placed Azimov upon it.

Strangely, Azimov didn't sink into the water.

Though Marina, who could use spirit magic, would have preferred her to come along for the pursuit, Ray also wanted to bring Byune, the thief skilled in tracking.

However, Rei couldn't understand Byune's words precisely.

Of course, he could understand her to a certain extent, but considering the urgency of the situation, he still wanted to take Vihera, who could communicate with Byune.

Furthermore, he knew that leaving only Byune behind would make transporting Azimov quite difficult.

As a result, Rei chose to have Marina stay behind.

Being the Guildmaster until just an hour ago meant her credibility with the guards was vastly superior to Rei's or Vihera's.

"Leave Azimov to me. You two be careful too. ...Vihera, please look after Rei."

"Yes, leave it to me."

Marina and Vihera exchanged a few brief words, and Rei and the others began moving.

Marina used water elemental magic to carry the unconscious Azimov out of the lab. Rei, Vihera, and Byune exited through the hole Set had opened.

"Set, I'm counting on you."

"GRRRR!"

Responding to Rei's call, Set let out a sharp growl and began running—not back the way he'd come... but toward the depths of the garden.

Though slightly surprised, Rei realized that if the assailant who attacked Azimov was still in the lab when they arrived, this was the obvious escape route.

No one would normally think to flee toward where Set was to escape from Rei and the others.

"Hope it's not a troublesome opponent."

"Saying that won't change anything. Honestly, someone who does something like this must have a lot of confidence in their own abilities, right? Or else they're just plain stupid."

"Hmm."

As Rei muttered while following Set, Vihera and Byune each replied.

It was a fairly well-known fact that Rei and Azimov were close.

There was the matter of the Twilight Spear, and the gathering of alchemists concerning the Ambris incident.

To Vihera, it seemed like suicide for someone who knew this to attack Azimov, who was close to Rei... and even more so to steal Rei's magic item.

But since they'd gone so far as to provoke Rei, the one with the nickname, they must have some kind of advantage, he thought.

(And the Sleipnir Boots... stealing one of the magic items that underpins Rei's combat ability... it really does seem like suicide. I can't imagine Rei would just give up halfway through, though.)

With these thoughts swirling, Vihera followed Set alongside Rei and Byune.
