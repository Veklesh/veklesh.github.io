The number of days in the previous chapter has been revised to twenty days to reach Glacial, and ten days by the carriage Maruka rode in.

"Hey, Set. Why do you think we're in a place like this?"

"Grrr?"

At Rei's words, Set turned his head while flapping his wings, growling curiously.

Rei and Set were currently high in the sky, about a hundred meters above ground, far from Gilm.

Set was flying through the falling snow, but the conditions were quite harsh for Rei as well.

Being a hundred meters up in the air while snow fell meant the temperature was naturally freezing cold.

The cold wasn't a problem thanks to the Dragon Robe, but there was a major issue separate from the cold.

"Dammit, this snow hurts!"

Yes, just as Rei said, flying at Set's speed through falling snow meant the snow hitting him hurt.

Temperature could be managed, but the robe didn't cover his body like a barrier.

Given that, the snow inevitably hit his body.

"Guess Lenora and Kenny were right after all. We should've waited for the snow to stop before leaving."

After the discussion about the designated request ended in the Guildmaster's office, Maruka immediately requested that Rei depart.

The reason given was that some factions loyal to the king had already begun to move.

Under normal circumstances, one would likely refuse, citing the need to prepare for the journey. However, for Rei, whether by luck or misfortune, all his belongings were stored within the Misty Ring.

Meaning, if he was willing, he could set out on the request immediately.

Adding to this was Maruka's request that he hurry to Glacial as much as possible. Given letters for the gatekeeper, the officer academy, and the House of Quent, he set off immediately.

Since there was nothing particularly urgent to do during the winter, Rei felt inclined to agree to the request. And so, he was flying through the falling snow...

"I should have waited for the snow to stop. ...Thanks to this, it's hard to even read the map."

The map was written on special paper created through alchemy, so a little snow or wind wouldn't affect it.

Still, knowing just how valuable the map was, carelessly unfolding it in the current conditions was impossible.

If it were carelessly blown away by the wind and lost, there would be no way to compensate for it.

Even if they demanded Rei's Death Scythe as compensation, it would be hard to refuse.

"Um... the road runs around that mountain, right?"

Recalling the map he'd seen before leaving, he scanned the snow-covered landscape.

"Grrr?"

It seemed Set spotted the road first.

"The road? Is that it?" Following Set's gaze as he growled, I saw the road clearly ahead.

"Yeah, thanks, Set. So, if we follow that road, it should eventually split into two paths. Let's head there."

"Grrr."

In response to Rei's words, Set growled in understanding and flapped his wings.

As they continued along the road, a carriage suddenly came into Rei's view, traveling along the highway.

"Whoa, they're running a carriage in this snow? If it were a special-made carriage like Maruka's, pulled by some kind of monster-like special-made beast, that'd be one thing, but it looks like a regular carriage... That's rough."

Rei's muttered words were simply an observation based on what he saw below.

But as if that had pulled some kind of trigger, the next moment the carriage stopped moving.

"Heeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeee

A heart-wrenching horse's cry echoed.

For a moment, Rei thought a monster might have appeared, but no monsters were visible around the carriage.

He remembered that this wasn't the frontier, so powerful monsters like that wouldn't just appear out of nowhere. He turned his gaze again, wondering what was causing the horse's screams.

"Ah... the wheel must have gotten stuck in a hole."

Even though it was called a highway, it wasn't paved with asphalt like in Japan.

No, even roads paved with asphalt develop potholes in winter – Rei knew this from experience.

Of course, asphalt sinking was due to the asphalt itself shrinking from the cold of the snow. If the road wasn't paved with asphalt like this one, it wouldn't sink from the snow.

(Instead, snow piles up in pre-existing depressions, hiding them and turning them into natural pitfalls, I suppose.)

What should we do? Set asked, his gaze fixed on Rei. After a moment's thought, Rei asked him to descend to the ground.

Partly because the snow was making the road difficult to follow in places, and partly because he wanted to ask for directions just in case.

"Gurururu!"

Growling in response to Rei's request, Set flapped his wings and descended to the ground amidst the falling snow.

Noticing Set's descent, the coachman who had been struggling with the carriage and the people who had gotten out of it stopped what they were doing, one by one, and looked up into the sky.

Normally, seeing a monster heading their way would be reason enough to flee.

But now, a person was riding on that monster's back.

They judged this wasn't just a monster attack, yet prepared to draw their weapons immediately, just in case.

"Huh."

On Set's back, Rei murmured with admiration.

It should have been possible to gradually understand that the approaching creature was a griffin, if not immediately, then at least by recognizing its silhouette...

And yet, not a single soul in the direction of Rei's gaze was abandoning the carriage to flee.

Even the coachman looked perfectly at ease, bow in hand.

Intrigued by their clearly battle-hardened demeanor, he waved lightly to signal they weren't enemies.

Seeing Rei's gesture, those on the ground must have judged he had no intention of fighting. The atmosphere visibly relaxed.

Then, Set descended near the carriage.

By then, those surrounding the carriage hadn't completely let their guard down, but they were still watching Rei with keen interest.

"What are you doing here at this time of year?"

As he dismounted from Set's back, he asked. One of the men, a man in his mid-thirties, smiled and spoke up.

He probably understood the person before him wasn't an enemy.

...Though the light in his eyes wasn't quite as optimistic as the smile on his face.

"As you can see, a wheel fell into a hole. I was wondering what to do about it."

"A hole, huh? Must've been hidden by the snow. Want some help?"

"Really? That'd be a huge help."

Just as he finished speaking, one of the men suddenly cried out.

"Griffin... Ah! Griffin!? Then... could you be Scarlet Rei!?"

At that single word, every man's face twisted in shock.

He lived in the Kingdom of Mireana and was responsible for guarding the person inside the carriage.

Naturally, they recognized the name of a famous figure in those circles.

"Ah, that's right. I'm riding a griffin, so there's no mistake."

"But Scarlet... you're neutral, right? This is the territory of Viscount Gizlus, loyal to the king! What the hell are you doing here?"

"Isn't it because he's an adventurer?"

"In this snowstorm, of all times?"

"Even so, do you think anyone besides Crimson could have a griffin as a familiar?"

Amidst these voices, the man talking to Rei opened his mouth as if to confirm.

"Um, you are Crimson, right?"

"Yeah. I won't deny being called that."

"...I see. So, why are you in a place like this?"

"I got lost... well, maybe not lost. It's my first time here, so I wanted to ask if I was going the right way. I was about to ask someone when I spotted a carriage stopped here."

"First time here?"

The man tilted his head at those words, but Rei ignored him and continued speaking.

"So, I'll take care of this carriage with its wheel stuck in that hole over here. After that, would you mind answering my question?"

"Well, I don't mind... but worst case, I'd have had to unload everything from the carriage before pulling it out..."

In this snow, unloading the luggage to lighten the carriage even a little seemed like a grueling task no matter how you looked at it.

And with the snow falling, they'd need to take precautions to keep the cargo dry. It was only natural to not want to do that in this cold.

Especially since the carriage contained the person the men were escorting.

If they made that person stand out in the snow and got them sick, they'd definitely get scolded by their superiors.

Worse still, they might even face a pay cut as punishment.

Moreover, the men genuinely didn't want to do such a thing out of goodwill toward the person they were protecting.

And then, the Rank B adventurer with the nickname appeared. It would be a lie to say they weren't hopeful.

"Alright, count on us. We'll help too..."

"Huh? Oh, no need for any help. It'll be over soon, just wait here."

Rei, responding to the man, drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

The sudden appearance of the great scythe before them froze the men for an instant, but they quickly reached for their own weapons again.

Seeing this, Rei thrust the pommel of the Death Scythe into the ground, as if to say he meant no harm.

"Don't worry. I'm not trying to harm you."

"...You expect me to trust someone who suddenly pulls out a weapon?"

"Well, I need this to deal with this carriage."

"What exactly are you going to do? I'd like some explanation."

"Well, you'll see. And if you're worried, I'll do it from a distance, so don't worry."

Saying this, he moved away from the carriage as if to prove his words.

Once he was about five meters away, the men likely stopped expecting Rei to do anything to the carriage. Their tension eased.

...Though Rei did possess the Flying Slash attack, fortunately the men seemed unaware of it.

As their tension eased, the men became curious about what Rei was planning to do.

"Let's go."

Warning the men not to be startled, Rei thrust the stone tip of the Death Scythe into the ground.

"Terrain Manipulation."

The moment those words left Rei's mouth, the carriage, which had lost a wheel and was tilted sideways, began to rise naturally.

"Wh-what?! Hey, wait!"

"Ah, ah... No way?"

"Earth magic, huh?"

"I see, earth magic... Earth magic? As far as I know, Scarlet was known for excelling at fire magic, just like her nickname suggests. Like, fire tornadoes and stuff."

"Just because she's good at fire magic doesn't mean she can't use earth magic. Probably just not her specialty."

"...That thing, huh?"

The man's gaze followed the carriage, which had already leveled out, no longer tilted.

That meant nothing less than the power to manipulate any targeted location at will.

To claim he wasn't good at it despite possessing such power was something I simply couldn't believe.

Though, in truth, what Rei was using wasn't earth magic but a skill, which explained the outcome.

Rei, who had drawn the attention of the men present, spoke up after confirming the carriage could move without issue.

"I got the carriage back on track, but since it fell into a hole, the axles or something might be damaged. You should get it checked out as soon as possible."

"...Ah, yeah. Yeah. That's a relief. Thanks."

The man Rei had been talking to replied, looking dazed.

However, Rei was used to people being surprised by his skills, so he didn't dwell on the man and stated his original purpose.

"So, does this road lead to the Duchy of Quent? Specifically, I want to know if it leads to Graciall."

"Graciall!?"

Rei gasped at the words he said. The man in front of him wasn't the one who gasped; it was the carriage.

"Did you just say Graciall?"

The carriage door, which had remained silent until now, opened, revealing a man.

Age-wise, he was probably the same as Rei or slightly older.

Despite being a man, he was striking for his shoulder-length blue hair.

Yet he didn't appear frivolous; if anything, he seemed rather serious.

That man watched Rei with keen interest.

...Meanwhile, the horses pulling the carriage, as usual, were startled and terrified by Set's presence, frozen in place.

That was precisely why the man inside the carriage could step down so easily.
