They continued flying on Set’s back for several days.

Eventually, he caught sight of a familiar town—Sabrusta—followed by Abuero, and finally Gilm, and only then did Rei feel that he had truly returned.

In reality, he hadn’t actually been away from Gilm for that long, but perhaps because he’d gotten caught up in all sorts of… yes, all sorts of trouble this time as well, Rei felt as though he’d been away from Gilm for quite a long time.

Rei gazed at Gilm with a sense of nostalgia, but he could see several people working at the site where an expansion was underway.

But… they weren’t actually working on the expansion…

“Monsters!?”

Just as Rei had said, a battle was raging at the construction site between monsters and what appeared to be adventurers.

Rei panicked for a moment, but upon closer inspection—no, without even needing to look closely—it was clear that the adventurers held a decisive advantage.

To begin with, the adventurers in Gilm tend to be stronger than those in other towns.

On top of that, when Rei took another look at the battlefield, he saw that the adventurers were fighting a group of kobolds.

While not quite as weak as goblins, kobolds are still a fairly weak type of monster, and to most of the adventurers in Gilm, they’re practically cannon fodder.

There was no way such kobolds, even as a horde, could win in a head-on battle against Gilm’s adventurers.

While relieved by this realization, Rei couldn’t help but wonder.

Why would a group of kobolds go out of their way to attack Gilm?

Cobolds are stronger and smarter than goblins.

Why would they do this to Gilm, a place they could never possibly defeat in a head-on battle?

That’s what bothered Rei.

“Well, there’s no point in dwelling on it here. Set, take me down to the main gate.”

“Grr?”

"We don't have to go help him?" Set growled, but Rei stroked him as he spoke.

“Even just watching from here, we’re clearly winning. So there’s no need for us to go out of our way and force our way in there.”

Rei had also been told to avoid descending directly into the city from the sky whenever possible.

If the adventurers fighting down there were at a disadvantage, it wouldn’t be a problem to drop down from the sky all at once… but doing something like that in a situation like this would undoubtedly cause all sorts of trouble.

Especially in such a lopsided situation, they’d likely be accused of stealing someone else’s prey.

Thinking about it that way, it was clear that we should just head back through the main gate as usual.

“Besides, we’ve got the Set Cage, right? …Well, if we were to drop the Set Cage right where the kobolds are, there’s no doubt we could deal significant damage to the kobold horde.”

After hearing that explanation, Set let out a purr as if convinced by Rei’s words and began descending toward the ground.

Naturally, while Rei was observing, thinking, and talking to Set, Set had been flying through the air, so he had already arrived near Gilm.

And so, Set dropped the Set Cage not far from the main gate.

Normally… well, from spring through fall, doing something like this would surely draw the attention of the guards.

But it was winter, and there were few people around.

In fact, there was no one in sight in front of the main gate even now.

That’s precisely why they were able to pull off this stunt.

After lowering the Set Cage to the ground, Set adjusted his position in the air and descended to the ground himself.

Just then, Elena, Ara, Yellow, and the dog emerged from the Set Cage and approached Rei and the others.

“You’ve finally arrived. …But don’t you sense something strange coming from Gilm?”

Being able to sense the atmosphere of battle even from this distance is likely a special skill of Elena’s, honed from her long time on the battlefield.

“It seems a group of kobolds has infiltrated the construction site. When I looked down from the sky, it had already turned into a battle.”

“I see.”

The fact that she didn’t ask if they should go send reinforcements right away was likely because Elena knew the capabilities of the adventurers in Gilm.

Ara knew that too, so the three people and two creatures headed toward the main gate without showing any signs of panic.

Naturally, they must have noticed Rei and the others when the Set Cage was lowered. The guards immediately came out to handle the situation.

“You’re awfully late. I thought you’d be back a little sooner.”

As Rei goes through the procedures to enter the city, the guard asks him that.

Having passed through this main gate countless times, and being a well-known figure in Gilm to boot, Rei had naturally become friendly with the guards.

……Though, to be honest, part of the reason was that he looked forward to feeding Set.

In fact, some of the people who hadn’t yet processed Elena and Ara’s paperwork were giving Set dried meat at the same time they were putting his familiar necklace on him.

Watching these interactions, Rei wasn’t sure what to say.

Being harassed by nobles and targeted by assassins, engaging in mock battles with the knights and soldiers serving the nobles, fighting against the boss of the most powerful organization in Anesis’s underworld as he tried to release a sealed monster… things like that—so many things had happened.

There wasn’t enough time to explain it all, and above all, it wasn’t the sort of thing one could just casually tell people. That’s exactly why…

“A lot has happened,”

He limits himself to that simple reply.

That said, perhaps even from that brief remark, the guard could easily imagine just how much trouble Rei had been caught up in, for he didn’t ask for any further details.

He simply nods, casting a somewhat compassionate gaze at Rei.

Rei had some thoughts about that guard, but right now, there was something more important he needed to ask.

“So, from the air, it looked like there was a fierce battle with a group of kobolds at the construction site. Do you know what happened?”

“Ah… yes. It started right around the turn of the year, I think. Since then, it seems a group of kobolds has been coming in from the construction site from time to time.”

Even as he said this, the guard didn’t look particularly worried—likely because he understood that, for the adventurers in Gilm, kobolds aren’t much of a threat.

Of course, if higher-tier or rare species of kobolds were to appear, they might face a bit of a struggle… but as far as the guard knew, no such reports had come in yet.

That was precisely why he wore that expression.

Whether people interpret this as complacency or as a sign of confidence in the strength of Gilm’s adventurers would likely vary from person to person, but for now, Rei decided it was the latter.

“Since the new year… well, if that’s the case, I suppose many of the adventurers are feeling dissatisfied.”

Generally speaking, adventurers take a long break during the snowy winter months.

Adventurers who haven’t saved enough money by winter do need to work through the season, but most of them take a long break.

And just when they’re relaxing and taking it easy, a horde of kobolds suddenly attacks.

Moreover, since they’re low-rank monsters, the materials, magic stones, and proof-of-defeat parts are cheap.

For adventurers who’ve come to Gilm and managed to save up enough money to get through the winter, it’s such a hassle that they’d rather not even bother killing them and skinning them.

...That said, for adventurers who failed to save enough money to get through the winter, even a kobold is better than having to go outside Gilm to fight monsters. Plus, since monsters have invaded the city, the guild issues emergency quests with slightly higher rewards, so some consider themselves lucky.

That said, even though the kobold incursions are being held back at the construction site, it’s a fact that monsters are invading the city.

So, of course, no one would dare say openly that they consider themselves lucky about that.

“That’s true. Still, the kobolds haven’t made it into the residential districts yet, so few of the residents of Gilm are complaining.”

The residents of Gilm understand that they live on the frontier.

Not everyone feels this way, of course; there are some who harbor grievances or anxieties about the kobold attacks… but most of those people are likely either newcomers who’ve arrived in Gilm in the last few years or those who came here specifically for the current expansion project.

Living on the frontier makes it easier to acquire rare materials, magic items made from them, and various other goods—and to earn money—compared to other villages, towns, and cities… but that’s only because it’s the frontier.

You could say it’s the epitome of high risk, high reward.

Since the vast majority of those living in Gilm are fully aware of this, they have no reason to complain under these circumstances.

Of course, that’s only true because all the kobolds near the expansion site have been defeated.

If we hadn’t managed to defeat all the kobolds, there would surely be people who would complain, even knowing the risks.

“I see. At first, I thought about going over there, but since that didn’t seem to be a concern, I just came straight here… Looks like I made the right choice.”

“It’s a big help to us, too. Thanks to that, we didn’t have to go through any of the hassle of paperwork. …Alright, the paperwork’s done. You can go inside now.”

At the guard’s words, Rei and the others enter Gilm.

As expected, there were few people near the main gate.

Even so, it wouldn’t be unusual to see a few people there on a normal day, but today—whether by chance or not—there wasn’t a soul in sight.

(Could this have something to do with the kobold incident?)

For long-time residents of Gilm, an incident like this might not be all that unsettling, but for those who’ve come to Gilm for some reason—such as the expansion project—it’s only natural they’d want to flee as quickly as possible from a place under monster attack.

That said, that feeling was limited to the area right by the main gate; once you walked a little further, the scenery that was so familiar to Rei spread out before him.

“Rei, I’m sorry, but I’m going to pay a visit to Lord Daskar. Though, I can’t guarantee I’ll be able to see him right away…”

As Elena, who had been dispatched to ensure the Noble Faction didn’t pull any shenanigans regarding the expansion of Gilm, she felt it necessary to report to Daskar, the ruler of this land, now that she had returned to Gilm.

Rei knew this too, so he nodded...

“What do you think? Should I come along too?”

“No, there’s no need for you to come all the way here, Rei. I’ll let Lord Daskar know about your situation myself.”

“Is that so? Sorry to put you to the trouble.”

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn’t be strange to worry that something suspicious might be mentioned when informing Daskar about Rei.

But with Elena, there’s no need to worry about that—I can trust her completely.

"Don't worry about it. Well... you know how Rei and I are."

Perhaps feeling slightly embarrassed at saying it herself, she blushed and looked away as she spoke.

Seeing Elena like that, Rei, who had been addressed, also looked away, perhaps feeling a bit embarrassed himself.

That’s when I noticed something: there were clearly fewer children gathered around than usual, even though Set was there.

(Just like at the main gate, is this also due to the kobolds’ influence? …Though, in a way, it’s impressive that so many are gathering to play with Set even in a situation like this.)

Wherever Rei looked, people of all ages—men, women, and children—were gathered around him.

Since Set is treated almost like a mascot at Gilm, there were probably many who had been disappointed that he hadn’t been around for a while.

“Grrrrr!”

Set ate the skewered meat offered to him and purred happily.

Even in this cold weather… no, perhaps precisely because of the cold, the freshly grilled skewers tasted absolutely exquisite.

Watching the skewers, Rei suddenly thought of Georgima.

In the end, the ramen Georgima made was different from what Rei remembered.

Still, it was a dish that could be called delicious enough, and she had said she would continue to refine it and improve its quality from here on out.

That was what she said… but what worried Rei was her claim that she would eventually come to Gilm, feed him the ramen, and make him admit it was delicious.

He seemed to have said it in a joking manner, but considering Georgima’s personality… it wouldn’t be surprising if he suddenly showed up in Gilm someday.

(If that happened, I can’t help but feel it might cause quite a commotion in the Duchy of Kerebel… but well, I’ll worry about that later.)

"Let’s put the troublesome stuff aside for now," he thought. With that in mind, Rei made his way over to Set, who was surrounded by a crowd.
