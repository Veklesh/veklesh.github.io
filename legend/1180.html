Rei, Set, and Vihera along with their one companion took flight from the location where the merchant caravan had been attacked by a horde of goblins and kobolds, soaring through the sky as usual.

Just moments ago, the bodies of the caravan, goblins, and kobolds were visible below, but at Set's speed, they vanished from sight in an instant.

As they flew through the sky, Rei suddenly had an idea and took a magic stone from the Misty Ring.

It was the magic stone of the kobold leader, one he hadn't absorbed yet.

To be precise, Death Scythe hadn't allowed him to learn any new skills with it, but Set hadn't tried it yet.

(I'd have preferred to try it after landing... but given the current situation, landing just to absorb this one magic stone is a hassle.)

If it were absorption via Death Scythe, that would be a different story.

Since the Death Scythe required cutting the magic stone, doing it while flying was difficult.

Well, it wasn't impossible, but it would definitely be unstable.

Compared to that, Set's method of absorbing magic stones involved taking them into his body... essentially eating them.

Given that, for Set, who often eats sandwiches and other bread while flying, absorbing a magic stone mid-flight wouldn't be difficult.

(I did get a sandwich too... but that's for later, I guess.)

The image of the basket holding the sandwich flashed through Rei's mind, but he quickly dismissed it.

"Set, can you absorb the kobold leader's magic stone while flying like this?"

"Gururu!"

"No problem!" Set growled reassuringly.

Normally, he'd land on the ground before absorbing magic stones.

Under normal circumstances, being told to absorb magic stones while flying through the air would be impossible to accept immediately.

But... for better or worse, both Rei and Set possessed personalities that were anything but ordinary.

The same was true for Vihera, clinging to Set's foreleg. Hearing the conversation above, she merely smiled, thinking it was pointless to argue.

Despite what Vihera felt inside, the smile on her lips was almost a full-fledged grin.

Because he was the man she had come to like... to love. He couldn't possibly be ordinary.

That thought rose up from the depths of her heart.

"Alright, Set. Here's the magic stone from the kobold leader. Don't drop it, and eat it all in one go."

At Rei's words, Set happily growled.

His delight at being able to absorb the magic stone likely stemmed from his very creation through beast magic.

But even more than that, the thought that becoming stronger would make his beloved Rei happy was huge.

He hoped he might learn some new skill. With that thought, Set swallowed the Kobold Leader's magic stone Rei offered him...

[Set has acquired the skill 'Enhanced Sense of Smell Lv.4']

The announcement message flashed through both Rei and Set's minds.

"Ah... yeah. Well, it is a Kobold Leader. Acquiring this kind of skill is only natural."

The Enhanced Sense of Smell skill wasn't useless by any means.

But for Rei, the fact that the Enhanced Sense of Smell skill kept rising like this felt somewhat off.

Then again, considering it's the magic stone of a Kobold Leader... a being that commands monsters with dog heads, it wasn't that unnatural when you thought about it.

No, it was almost inevitable that it would learn the Enhanced Sense of Smell skill.

"Grrr..."

Even so, Set probably wished he could have learned a more useful skill instead.

He growled softly, sounding a bit disappointed.

"Come on, cheer up. Enhanced Scent is a surprisingly useful skill. Better to have it than nothing, right?"

Rei tried to cheer Set up, but it took a little more time for Set to clearly regain his spirits.

"Huh? Goblin and kobold groups cooperating...?"

After hearing Rei's explanation, Lenora asked back, looking utterly incredulous.

Monsters cooperating with other monsters was rare, but not unheard of.

However, the fact that these monsters were goblins and kobolds—monsters of roughly equal strength—made it an almost unseen phenomenon.

And considering it happened now, when groups of multiple monster types had formed, it was only natural to think it was somehow connected to the current anomaly.

"So, did those groups have leader species?"

"Yeah. There was a goblin leader and a kobold leader. It's a relief that, throughout this whole commotion, there aren't any higher-tier types like archers or mages besides the leader types."

In Rei's understanding, leader species functioned like squad leaders. In other words, the Archer was a bowman, and the Mage was a wizard.

If such higher-tier types existed within the group besides the leader species, it wouldn't be surprising if ordinary adventurers, let alone Rei and his group, struggled.

Of course, for Rei, it meant more magic stones to gather, so it wasn't all bad.

"True. But regarding this pack situation, I've never heard of different species of monsters cooperating, only packs of the same species."

If it were about monsters of the same species cooperating, Rei had experience defeating several goblin groups gathered together.

But this was the first time since this whole pack business started that Rei had seen kobolds and goblins working together.

"The caravan that was attacked did mention reporting it to the guild, so we should be able to get more details from them."

"Well, selling that many proof-of-kill parts and magic stones, going to the guild would be the quickest way. Though, being merchants, they might have their own connections."

Vihera added to Rei's words.

Lenora, who had been listening, nodded in understanding.

"Merchants might have their own connections for magic stones. But the guild is the only place that can cash in slain monster parts, right? Well, I suppose they could sell the parts to adventurers..."

"If that's the case, not wanting to come to the guild... it's like saying they have something to hide, isn't it?"

"Yes. And if you sell to adventurers, you'd naturally have to sell them cheaper than the guild's buyout price. Thinking that way, bringing them directly to the guild is still more profitable."

Vihera and Rei nodded in agreement at Lenora's words.

...Incidentally, Kenny, who would normally butt in, was currently busy dealing with adventurers and couldn't spare a word for Rei.

As Lenora and Rei were talking... suddenly, one of the guild staff approached them.

Noticing this, Lenora tensed up, worried she might get scolded for talking too long... but the guild employee didn't look particularly stern.

To the guild staff, Lenora was a serious and capable employee.

Sure, she and Kenny caused a bit of a ruckus sometimes, but it was nothing beyond what was tolerable.

That's precisely why he had no intention of making a fuss even if she occasionally let loose.

If Kenny had caused the trouble, that would be a different story.

Besides, he hadn't come here to reprimand Lenora.

He was here purely to see Rei and Vihera.

"Mr. Rei, Ms. Vihera. The Guildmaster requests your presence."

"Marina? ...What could it be?"

Vihera's gaze shifted to Rei, but Rei also didn't know why they'd been summoned.

"Probably about this recent pack..."

He stopped there, suddenly recalling something Marina had mentioned before.

"Come to think of it, she did say she'd look into something about this swarm. Could it be about that?"

"I suppose so. But considering the Guildmaster summoned us, that seems the most likely reason."

"Oh, really? It's Marina, after all. Maybe she just wanted to see Rei?"

At Lenora's words, Vihera interjected teasingly... and Kenny, listening beside them, twitched.

But in the current situation, he couldn't interject. He moved his hands swiftly, determined to finish his own work as quickly as possible.

Lenora gave Kenny a faint smile at his reaction, but immediately turned her gaze back to Rei, Vihera, and the guild staff and spoke.

"Well then, please come inside. Since it's the Guildmaster speaking, we should probably hurry a bit."

"Yes, you're right. Please, go ahead."

The guild staff echoed Lenora's words. Rei and Vihera stepped inside the counter area and headed toward the stairs at the back.

After seeing them off, Lenora watched the guild clerk return to his seat before resuming her own work.

Kenny, who had finally finished his own tasks, shot Lenora a resentful look.

Inside Marina's office, the scene was exactly as expected.

Well, considering the number of documents on her desk had decreased compared to before, perhaps it was better than expected.

Even so, Marina's face, as she reviewed the papers on her desk, bore a certain weariness, showing she hadn't been relaxing while Rei and the others were fighting the monster horde.

"You look pretty tired. Are you okay?"

Vihera voiced her concern for Marina.

Hearing this, Marina flashed a slight look of surprise before immediately returning a smile.

"I'm fine. It was just a bit tough. More importantly... it's perfect timing that Rei and the others are back."

"Perfect timing?"

"Yes. About this pack... I think I figured out why."

"Really? Though I can't exactly say it was quick..."

Seeing the lingering fatigue on Marina's face, it was clear that "quick" meant she'd pushed her stamina to the limit.

Understanding Vihera's concern, Marina waved it off before speaking.

"Let's get straight to the point. Actually, there's a precedent for multiple monster swarms appearing like the one currently around Gilm. It just happened in the Bestir Empire, not Gilm."

"The Bestir Empire? Well, I suppose it's not surprising they'd have such information given their vast territory... but how did you manage to get hold of it?"

It hadn't been that long since the war.

Yet you managed to get that information. Marina responded to Rei's thought with a radiant smile.

...Exhaustion was evident, yet it revealed a different kind of allure than the feminine charm Marina usually displayed.

"I heard it when registering with the guild, and Rei, you've seen me communicate with other guildmasters before, right? So I don't think it's strange at all."

"Still, it's the Bestir Empire. Considering it's a country we fought against not long ago..."

"As long as we're adventurers, we naturally compartmentalize things like that. Of course, some can't compartmentalize, and fighting breaks out when we meet somewhere. ...Anyway, I asked several guildmasters in the Bestir Empire."

Realizing the conversation wasn't going anywhere, Marina cut to the chase, deciding to share the information she'd gathered with Rei and Vihera for now.

"There's a place in the Bestir Empire called the Valley of a Hundred Demons."

"Ah, I know that place. It's where a lot of humanoid monsters, like goblins, appear."

Though she had left, Vihera, originally from the Bestir Empire, supplemented Marina's information.

Even during her life as a princess, she had always possessed a thirst for battle.

Given that, it was only natural she sought places to satisfy it, and this was information she'd gathered back then.

"Vihera seems to know about it. Anyway, apparently around three hundred years ago, a large number of leader-type monsters appeared near the place called the Valley of a Hundred Demons. And they were monsters similar to what we have in Gilm now—goblins, orcs, lizardmen, werewolves..."

Vihera's gaze shifted to Marina with keen interest, as if she hadn't known this information.

Rei also turned his gaze toward Marina, careful not to miss a word.

Under the gaze of both men, Marina continued her explanation.

"To put it simply, some kind of magical anomaly occurred for unknown reasons, and that anomaly itself seems to be moving around in the form of monsters. They're commonly called Ambris. And when humanoid monsters come into contact with an Ambris's magic, they're forcibly evolved into leader species."

"...Is that even possible? No, it must be possible, which is precisely why we're facing this reality now..."

Magic anomalies becoming monsters.

That was all Rei could manage to say.
