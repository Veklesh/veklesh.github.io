A few days had passed since Rei had been wandering around Anesis with Brewitt, Ranigus, and Teres… That morning, a large crowd had gathered in front of one of Anesis’s gates.

Since it was winter, the number of people passing through there wasn’t usually that high.

But even so… today, more than a few dozen people had gathered.

It was only natural. Present there were Liberte, the lord of Anesis, and his wife, Arcadia; Philma, the commander of the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights, serving as their escort, along with his subordinate knights… and, in addition, nobles and their escorts who were connected to the person about to leave Anesis.

“Rei, come visit my domain sometime. It’s a pretty interesting place, and I think you’ll like it.”

“Hmm… Yeah, I guess so. I’ll need to focus on the expansion work in Gilm for a while, so it might be a bit difficult… but I’ll think about it once I have some free time.”

Rei replied to Brewitt, who had invited him to visit his estate.

“What’s that supposed to mean? So you’d rather I come visit Gilm instead?”

“Yeah, that’s true. I think that would be fun too. Right now… well, actually, once spring comes, a lot of people will gather in Gilm again for the expansion work, so I think it’ll be pretty lively and interesting.”

“Huh. …Well, given my skills, I’d probably need to hire an escort to make it all the way out to Gilm on the frontier.”

While Brewitt was fairly confident in his ability to handle street fights, he had absolutely no confidence that he could go alone to Gilm, a frontier town where high-rank monsters frequently appeared without a second thought.

Well, maybe he used to have that much confidence, but after being utterly crushed in a mock battle with Rei, he’d stopped overestimating his own abilities—or so he’d tell himself.

In any case, Ranigus interjected as Brewitt spoke.

“Huh, Gilm, huh? Sounds kind of interesting. Thanks to the expansion work going on right now, monsters aren’t showing up as much, right?”

“Well, around the main road, anyway.”

The amount of materials needed for the expansion work in Gilm is so vast that no amount would ever be enough.

Although they’re making good use of the timber harvested from Treant Forest, expanding Gilm—located on the frontier—naturally requires a massive amount of materials beyond just wood.

Transporting those materials requires a large number of wagons, and most of the monsters that appear on the highway are dealt with by their escorts.

Given its remote location, monsters that would normally be impossible to defeat even with a wagon escort do occasionally appear, but due to the construction boom, a large number of adventurers have been hired, and Gilm itself sometimes issues requests for their extermination to ensure the safety of the highway.

Considering all that, it was undeniably true that it had become easier to reach Gilm, at least compared to before the expansion work began.

“Is that so? Well, maybe I’ll give it a serious try then.”

“Well, I won’t stop you if you want to come, but there are a lot of rough characters in Gilm these days… though, I suppose you don’t need to worry about that, Brewitt. Still, there are some high-ranking adventurers with terrible personalities, so you’ll need to be careful about that.”

At Rei’s words, Brewitt paused for a moment.

Here in Anesis, Brewitt had been in the habit of getting into fights with anyone who provoked him, but if he tried something like that in Gilm... there was a chance the person he was dealing with could be a high-ranking adventurer.

Still, given Brewitt’s personality, if he saw someone being picked on, there’s no guarantee he wouldn’t step in to stop it.

“Ah… I’ll keep that in mind for now. So, what about Ranigus? Are we going to Gilm?”

“Me… Hmm, I don’t know. Hearing that just now… I’m kind of losing the urge to go. High-rank adventurers just walking around town like it’s nothing, and on top of that, they’ve got bad personalities? What kind of place is that?”

“No, it’s not like all high-rank adventurers have bad personalities. I mean, I’m a high-rank adventurer myself.”

Ranigus and Brewitt stared intently at Rei’s face as he said this… and eventually, they exchanged glances, nodded, and spoke up.

“So there really aren’t any decent people among the high-ranking adventurers, are there?”

“That’s right.”

“Hey!”

At such an outrageous assessment, Rei shouted in a reflexive outburst.

“I mean, come on… seriously?”

“Yeah.”

As the two of them seemed to understand each other in a strangely intimate way, Rei looked away with a dissatisfied expression.

Then, a short distance away, he caught sight of Set, who had somehow followed them, being petted by the maids from Duke Kerebel’s mansion and purring happily.

Perhaps sensing Rei’s gaze, Set tilted his head slightly, as if to ask, “What’s wrong?”

Ray shook his head, indicating it was nothing, and then turned his gaze toward the spot where the largest crowd had gathered… the place where Elena was standing.

There, in addition to Liberte and Arcadia, were several nobles, including Teres.

And, naturally, Ara was there as well.

The three setting out on their journey today are Rei, Elena, and Ara.

And though they aren’t human, there were also the two dogs, Set and Yellow.

Amidst all this, the person drawing the biggest crowd was… naturally, Elena.

“What’s wrong?”

Brewitt asked Rei, who was staring at Elena, with a puzzled look.

Although a faint trace of the playful atmosphere from earlier remained, Blueit must have found Rei’s demeanor puzzling.

“No, I just thought there’d be a lot of people around Elena.”

“That makes sense. She’s practically the symbol of the Noble Faction, after all. She’s bound to be more popular than Rei. …What’s the matter? Feeling lonely?”

“It’s not exactly that. It’s just that, even just watching her like this, I can see how much Elena is loved by the residents of Anesis… and by the Noble Faction.”

Even as he said that, Rei looked over at Elena once more.

Elena’s parents, Liberte and Arcadia.

And beyond them, the many members of the Noble Faction who admire Elena… the Princess General.

He wondered what those people thought of him, standing right there beside Elena.

Liberte and Arcadia had, in a way, tacitly accepted Rei’s relationship with Elena.

But Rei had no way of knowing what they truly thought deep down.

When he thought about it that way, it was only natural that a somewhat complicated feeling would well up inside him.

“Well, that’s true. Even from my perspective as a member of the Royal Faction, Elena’s presence is significant. In fact, when considering the Kingdom of Mireana as a whole, one could say her presence is crucial not only to the Noble Faction but to the nation itself.”

There was no hint of jest in Ranigus’s words; he was completely serious.

And then, in the next instant… Ranigus turned his gaze toward Rei and declared.

“But when it comes to important figures, that would be Rei. You’re the same. No, actually, since you’ve been making such a splash, Crimson has become more famous than the Princess General lately.”

“Is that so? Well, if you ask me whether I’m making a big splash, I can’t exactly say I’m not. But if anything, it’s not so much that I’m acting on my own initiative—it’s more accurate to say I’m getting dragged into these commotions.”

From an outsider’s perspective, it might look like Rei is running wild as he pleases, but from Rei’s point of view, he isn’t the one causing the commotion—he’s just getting dragged into it.

That said, it’s not as if there are absolutely no instances where a commotion arises because of Rei, like when Gaiska hired the Black Wolf.

“...No matter how I feel about it, if that’s how others see it, I guess there’s no stopping it.”

“Hmph.”

When put that way, even Rei couldn’t argue with it.

After all, it’s impossible to go around correcting every single rumor people spread.

If it were a ballad sung by a minstrel, it wouldn’t be impossible to correct it, but since minstrels have to earn a living through their songs, it’s not at all strange for them to embellish stories they’ve heard or researched, even if only slightly.

Of course, by the time the people who heard it from the minstrel pass it on to their friends and acquaintances, it’s not just a little embellishment—it’ll probably have grown into a whole series of exaggerations.

“Well, just give it up. I suppose your daily conduct is what counts in that regard.”

Brewitt said this with a smile.

In fact, Rei understood that his actions were often sensationalized because of his usual behavior, so he couldn’t say anything more.

From the minstrel’s perspective, there was no doubt that Rei’s flamboyant antics were a valuable source of income.

That said, Rei himself had hardly ever met a minstrel.

As they were talking, Liberte and Arcadia—having apparently finished their conversation with Elena—approached Rei.

Whenever the Duke and Duchess of Kerebel moved, it was only natural that many of the other nobles nearby would move with them.

Seeing this, Ranigus and Brewitt made room for them.

……However, perhaps because they knew about Rei and Elena’s relationship, they didn’t stop casting teasing glances his way as they left.

“Rei was in Anesis for only a short time. But it feels like enough commotion to fill several years happened during that brief period.”

“I can’t deny that.”

Rei smiled back at Liberte, who spoke with a smile on his face.

In fact, the major events that occurred since Rei arrived in Anesis… the mock battle in which many nobles participated, the battle against the Black Wolf, and the existence of the flesh cocoon—which appeared to be the monster Celestan was trying to unseal.

On top of that, defeating the flesh cocoon caused a spatial anomaly, and the site where Celestain’s house once stood vanished, leaving behind a scene that looked as if it had been scooped out with a spoon.

On top of that, with Celestain’s disappearance, the underworld of Anesis—which had remained relatively calm, for better or worse, thanks to Celestain—has become chaotic.

Internal conflicts within the organization are also intensifying as factions vie to succeed Celestine.

...Considering all that, Rei couldn’t deny what Liberte had said.

What’s more, these were merely the facts Rei was currently aware of.

In reality, there is a very real possibility that some problem—one that Rei isn’t even aware of… no, one that even Liberte hadn’t anticipated—could arise.

“That said, the Black Wolf matter was something we would have had to deal with eventually. Since he took care of it, I have no intention of complaining to Rei.”

“……I see.”

For Rei, the biggest regret from this whole incident was, after all, the Black Wolf affair.

There was a hint of bitterness in his reply to Liberte.

Incidentally, while Liberte has been informed through Elena that the Black Wolf was killed, the body is still inside Rei’s Misty Ring.

Rei, too, had no intention of handing over the Black Wolf’s body—which he believed had come from Japan just like himself—to Liberte.

He intends to burn the body in the mountains or somewhere along the way back to Gilm, then bury the ashes.

Just then, Arcadia interrupts the conversation between Rei and Liberte.

“Please take care of that child.”

“Yes.”

“……She seems like she can handle anything on her own, but in her personal life, she’s surprisingly absent-minded at times. That’s what makes her so cute, though.”

“Wait, Mother!”

Elena had been talking to other nobles a short distance away from Rei and the others, but she must have heard Arcadia’s voice. She cried out in alarm.

From Elena’s perspective, the idea of Arcadia revealing an embarrassing secret—at least in Elena’s opinion—to Rei was something she simply couldn’t tolerate.

Leaving the noble she had been speaking with behind, she ran over to Arcadia’s side.

Arcadia watched Elena’s reaction with a smile.

“Hehe, you’re such a mama’s girl.”

“That’s not true! Seriously, what were you trying to tell Rei?”

“Ah… I must admit, I was a little curious to hear it myself… but no, never mind. Yeah.”

The reason he couldn’t bring himself to finish what he was saying was likely because the gaze he directed at Elena was that intense.

“Did you say something?”

“No, nothing. Look, I thought it might be time to leave Anesis.”

Rei said this as if to brush it off, but in reality, more than thirty minutes had already passed since the send-off began.

Since it was a fact that they couldn’t just keep being seen off indefinitely, it was also a fact that they should leave.

“Hmm, you’re right. Well, let’s do that then.”

With Elena’s words, the decision to leave was made… Finally, Rei turned his gaze toward Philma, who had accompanied them as Liberte’s escort.

“I look forward to sparring with you again the next time we meet.”

“I’ll keep training and sharpen my skills as well.”

After exchanging a few brief words... Rei and the others set off toward Gilm.
