"So? What are we going to do starting tomorrow?"

That night, under a large, round moon so bright it could easily be called the Full Moon, Rei and the others were having dinner in Marina’s backyard as usual.

Inside the magical item kiln that Rei had produced, a chunk of meat was currently roasting.

Of course, there were plenty of other dishes as well, and the table was lined with food Rei had bought from various shops, all served fresh.

That said, these dishes were actually extras purchased to replenish the food they’d consumed during their recent dungeon run.

……Many shops were crying out in distress because Rei had bought such a massive amount of food from them, but you could say it was a happy cry.

After all, it was clear that because Rei had bought the food, today’s sales had doubled… or even if not quite that much, they had undoubtedly increased.

And in fact, when Rei asked to buy food, no shop turned him down; they were all happy to sell to him.

For those serving food, starting with the diners, Rei’s presence was a huge blessing.

That said, some shops offered dishes that took a long time to prepare, and unfortunately, there were those who had to turn him down, explaining that if they sold those to Rei, they wouldn’t be able to open for business tonight.

Rei felt sorry for them too, recalling the expressions of the staff members who looked genuinely disappointed and asked him to please let them know in advance next time.

It was a soup that had been simmered slowly for a long time, until the original ingredients had completely dissolved, and then additional ingredients were added and simmered further—a soup that was very much to Rei’s liking.

“Tomorrow, huh. To be honest, there are all sorts of things I need to do and want to do. Hunting Gamelions, helping with the expansion work, the people from the Kingdom of Reburina heading to Gilm… things like that.”

“Of those, hunting Gamelions is just something Rei wants to do, so that’ll probably be last.”

“...I know that.”

Rei nodded at Marina’s words, looking slightly dissatisfied.

As for the Gamelion hunt, many people were currently heading that way, and that was one reason the expansion work was starting to fall behind.

But what really makes Rei go “Ugh…” is that even those who came to Gilm for the expansion—people who, under normal circumstances, wouldn’t even have the ability to make it to Gilm—are likely participating in the Gamelion hunt.

And, as was to be expected, injuries and deaths were piling up, which was consequently starting to cause delays in the expansion work.

That said, Rei can’t say she doesn’t understand the mindset of those who joined the Gamelion hunt.

Gamelions are considered a delicacy, and even when sold to the guild, they fetch a very high price.

Those confident in their bargaining skills might even sell it directly to shops other than the guild and get a higher price.

In terms of pay, it was a world of difference compared to doing regular expansion work.

Of course, the reason the pay is so high is that it comes with the inherent danger of fighting the ferocious Gamelion monster.

But… for those who came to Gilm to work on construction projects, it goes without saying that they came to earn money.

So, if a method presents itself to earn more money in less time... setting aside the cautious types, it was only natural that those in financial straits for whatever reason, those wanting to make money faster, and the fearless would flock to Gamelion hunting.

Since the burden falls on others, it’s only natural that those overseeing the expansion projects aren’t too pleased about it.

However, even if those currently in Gilm are here for the expansion project work, that doesn’t mean it’s the only thing they can do.

Since adventurers have the freedom to choose their quests, if someone says they want to hunt Gamelions, the guild can’t very well refuse them.

Of course, when it comes to Gamelion hunting requests, there are restrictions on who can accept them based on rank... but if someone decides to go hunt Gamelions without accepting a formal request, there’s nothing the Guild can do about it.

“For now, why don’t you help with the expansion work tomorrow, and then check on Sura and the others the day after tomorrow? They should be getting pretty close by then, right?”

Rei thought for a moment and nodded at Vihera, who spoke to him while enjoying a soup made with Gamelion meat.

“Yeah, you’re right. I think that’s the best course of action. …If possible, I’d hope the Gamelions are still around even after I check on Sura and the others and head back to Gilm.”

“Honestly, there’s nothing we can do about that. We have no way of knowing when the Gamelions will disappear. Besides, they won’t all vanish at once. Some might even choose to stay here.”

“...I guess all we can do is pray that Gamelion’s departure is delayed even a little, or that as many of them as possible stay here even if it does happen.”

That said, there is a glimmer of hope in Rei’s words.

In fact, since Rei has a flying partner named Set, she has the advantage of being able to search for Gamelions from the sky, which puts her in a much better position than other adventurers.

Rei had absolute confidence that even if most of the Gamelions were gone, as long as there were still some left, he would be able to find them.

The problem, however, was whether he could finish his own tasks before that happened.

“Hmm. Rei seems to have his hands full. …Ara, what’s the status of our plans?”

As Elena asked this while enjoying her sautéed Gamelion, Ara swallowed the salad in her mouth before speaking.

“Well, there are a few people who wish to meet with you, Elena… but most of them can be ignored.”

“……‘Most’ means there are some people I have to meet, then?”

“That’s correct. Of course, if you absolutely refuse to see them, Lady Elena…”

“No, it’s fine. I’m here in Gilm solely to act as a deterrent, to ensure the Noble Faction doesn’t do anything foolish. If meeting with those people will allow the expansion of Gilm to proceed without issue, I have no intention of refusing.”

Hearing Elena’s words, Ara nodded in response.

“Understood. I’ll make the necessary arrangements.”

“Mm-hmm.”

It was a brief exchange, but it was enough to settle Elena’s schedule for the coming days.

Watching this exchange, Vihera spoke up while looking at Byune eating a sandwich made with Gamelion meat.

“In that case, I suppose we’ll be patrolling the town after all. Maybe it’s because merchants have been flocking in, but it feels like public safety has deteriorated compared to before.”

“Merchants… or rather, to be precise, the bodyguards accompanying them?”

“Yes. I understand that adventurers are hot-blooded… but with there being so many of them, I’ve seen a few altercations. The guards and adventurers hired to maintain order were handling them, though…”

The fact that she trailed off mid-sentence was likely because those people simply didn’t have enough manpower to handle everything.

For Vihera, the request to maintain public order felt like a return to her old stomping grounds… though calling it that might be an exaggeration, it’s true she felt a certain kinship with it.

That’s precisely why the current situation, where they can’t keep up with the workload, probably didn’t sit well with her.

In fact, back when Vihera used to handle that job, there were a few instances early on where adventurers who didn’t know her would pick on her appearance or style… but as word spread about what happened to those who did, the number of people willing to cross Vihera dropped rapidly.

Also, Vihera is quite good at looking out for others, including in the matter of Byune.

For that reason alone, there were many who looked up to Vihera, regardless of romantic feelings or physical desire.

For Vihera, it probably isn’t very amusing that such people aren’t doing their jobs properly.

“Ah… well, since the increase in merchants has likely led to negative side effects, like more adventurers showing up, why not cut them some slack?”

“...The shortage of inns is also becoming an issue, after all.”

At Marina’s words, everyone looked convinced.

In fact, even before the merchants arrived, the number of inns had already become insufficient due to the people who had gathered for the expansion construction.

With lodging inevitably running short, they had to build temporary structures near the construction sites where people could sleep on the floor... yet even that couldn’t completely resolve the shortage.

Given that situation, with new merchants arriving accompanied by their own escorts, it was only natural that lodging would become scarce.

The silver lining was that many of these merchants already had deep ties to Gilm, and quite a few had settled in Gilm hoping to make a profit from the expansion project.

...Among the more resourceful residents of Gilm, there were even some who would let acquaintances stay at their homes for a slightly lower price than the inns, but this has led to crimes occurring as a result.

“I’ve only just returned to Gilm, but it looks like things are going to get busy again starting tomorrow.”

“I suppose that’s something to be happy about… I guess.”

Marina muttered, looking a little melancholy.

Rei wasn’t the only one who found her demeanor puzzling. The others were also looking at Marina with puzzled expressions.

She must have noticed their stares. Marina shook her head, as if to say it was nothing.

Of course, given how obvious her reaction was, no one really believed she meant it.

“We’re friends, so there’s no need to hold back. …Well, if it’s something you can’t tell a guy, I won’t force you to say it.”

Perhaps sensing something in Rei’s words, Marina eventually let out a small sigh before speaking.

“Actually, it looks like there are going to be all sorts of problems this winter.”

“...Specifically?”

“Broadly speaking… well, I suppose you could say it’s related to the expansion project at Gilm.”

Hearing Marina’s words, Elena’s gaze sharpened slightly.

“Marina, could it be… that the Noble Faction is involved in some way?”

The first thing that came to Elena’s mind was likely because the Noble Faction had been obstructing the expansion of Gilm all along.

From what Marina said, Elena wondered if the Noble Faction might have been meddling in this matter as well, causing the problem.

That was why she asked.

If her suspicions were correct, it would mean that Elena’s reputation as a member of the Noble Faction in Gilm would be ruined.

Given that, she wouldn’t hesitate to pull out Mirage.

That was Elena’s line of thinking, but…

“Oh, it’s not really about the Noble Faction or anything.”

Relieved by Marina’s words, Elena softened her sharp gaze.

Beside Marina, Ara was equally relieved.

That was because, if the Noble Faction really did try to cause trouble, she had thought that Ara would try to handle it herself before Marina could do anything.

...Ara’s brute strength was more than enough to easily beat someone to death.

Given that, I suppose we should say that in this case, no unnecessary lives were lost...

“So? Then what’s the problem?”

“Simply put, the problem is that more people are staying in Gilm than Daskar and Walker had anticipated. …Well, when you think about it, people come to Gilm to make money. Since there’s work even when it’s snowing, and the danger isn’t that great, there’s no reason for those who want to earn money to go home. …Of course, not everyone stays; those who’ve made enough have already left Gilm.”

“You mean the number is lower than expected?”

“Right. …If this were spring or summer—or at least fall—it’d be one thing, but it’s winter and it’s snowing! If we run out of inns, people might end up freezing to death… The problem is figuring out how to deal with that.”

Vihera tilted her head at Marina, who sighed and took a bite of her salad.

“But you did set up temporary lodging, didn’t you? Isn’t that enough?”

“Temporary is just temporary. Drafts blow in, and depending on the situation, snow might too. Plus, there aren’t any heating appliances or anything like that.”

“I see. In that case… it really is tough. We can’t exactly start building inns from scratch now…”

“In the worst-case scenario, we’ll probably have to open up the guild’s warehouse. That would be more comfortable than the makeshift hut we built.”

“Even so, wouldn’t it still be hard to endure the cold?”

“We’ll probably need to take on a commission through the guild to buy some kind of heating equipment or something. If a few of us chip in, we should be able to afford that much. …By the way, I hear they’re considering charging a lodging fee for the warehouse, though it’ll be pretty cheap.”

"Really? I think it should be free, if we're going to do this."

“...If we did that, people would just camp out in the warehouse all the time without doing any work.”

At Marina’s words, everyone nodded in agreement.
