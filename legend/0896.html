"Guh!"

A man screamed and collapsed to the ground.

The woman watching muttered in disbelief.

"Hah, I never thought it'd come to this—just like Rei feared, they really did launch a night raid. Well, I suppose gathering enough men took longer than expected, so it happened today instead of yesterday. I get that they don't want to fight Rei, but honestly, that's a bit too obvious. And for an attack force, their skills are pretty weak too."

"Nah, nah, it's not as bad as you make it sound. They're reasonably skilled, aren't they? Well, even so, facing me and Fron would be tough. They're about Rank D level."

A dwarf wielding a massive hammer muttered as well.

Several figures lay fallen at the dwarf's feet.

"Hah! Rank D or whatever, if you get hit by Brass's Earthquake Hammer, you're done for. More importantly, I'm fine, but..."

Fron's gaze shifted mid-sentence, and just as if waiting for that moment, the sound of approaching footsteps could be heard.

The surroundings were already dark. It was a little early to call it midnight, but the only light came from the magic items illuminating the mansion and the moon in the sky.

For guard duty, the snow having stopped and the wind not being too strong made for ideal conditions.

Breaking through the night's darkness, a familiar face appeared.

One of the adventurers who claimed to be acquainted with Rei.

"Fron-san, there were three intruders on this side. We successfully captured all of them."

"Not bad. I expected that level of skill from someone who knows Rei, though."

The words that came out of Fron's mouth were unmistakably praise.

But the man receiving it wore a slightly displeased expression.

"Well, it's true we were assigned this escort job through Rei's introduction, but expecting us to possess power beyond common sense like Rei's is a bit much, don't you think?"

Being lumped together with someone like Rei was unbearable.

The man voiced this sentiment, his gaze shifting toward Fron and Brass.

Rei had requested their escort services, but before that, he had addressed the two standing before him.

That meant she trusted these two more than us.

I'd rather not be lumped in with Rei, but I still felt a twinge of envy toward those two who were trusted more than us.

Noticing the man's gaze, Fron tilted her head and spoke.

"What is it?"

"Ah, no, nothing. More importantly... I never thought they'd actually launch an attack."

"Yeah. Well, they couldn't back down either, could they? You heard from Rei, right? What they staked on the duel."

At Fron's words, Brass snorted unhappily and spoke up.

"Hmph. The enemy is a typical foolish noble. Even seeing Rei with their own eyes, they couldn't see through his true strength, could they? Only now, finally realizing what kind of man Rei is, they're making a rash move. There's no other word for it but incompetent."

Whether it was because his boozy lifestyle had been disrupted, Brass's words clearly carried a sharp edge.

But there was no denying the truth in his words.

"If they understand what kind of person Rei is, then why not just cancel the duel altogether? Even if it's been legally processed, if both parties agree, surely it can be handled any way they want?"

"True. But in that case, that noble... the Earl of Ariel, was it? That noble would lose face. They'd be admitting total defeat to someone of lower rank."

"The Earl of Ariel family. ...Prestige, you say. While prestige is certainly important for nobles, I'd think losing face is less damaging than facing off against Rei-san."

Fron and Blasso both nodded at that remark... but the truth was, it was precisely because they couldn't tolerate that loss of face that they'd launched this night raid.

"More importantly, what should we do with these unconscious people?"

"Right. Capture them for now. We don't know if these guys are actually from the Eliel family, but if they are, they could be useful in negotiations after the duel. Even if they aren't, we might be able to trace them back to their employer."

With that, Fron cast a cold gaze toward the unconscious assassins lying on the ground.

"Don't you dare!"

A fist swung in anger knocked a glass off the table.

It was a glass cup, far beyond the means of commoners, but in the next instant, it fell to the floor and shattered.

Even witnessing this, the man who shouted—Kip—could not quell his fury. He fixed a burning gaze of rage upon Fultos before him.

"I followed your suggestion and tried to assassinate that penniless baron... and the result is total annihilation!? And what's this about most of them being captured alive?! Some of those men, however tenuous, have connections to me!"

"Regarding that matter, please rest assured. I don't believe anything will come to harm Lord Keep. ...However, I never imagined they would assemble their guards so quickly. ...My apologies."

"I'm not asking for apologies! I'm demanding you fix this situation!"

Keep's expression, as he yelled at the bowing Fultos, was one of agitation.

Having learned the true extent of Rei's capabilities, he now fully grasped the dire situation they were in.

The desperate measure taken to counter this seemingly hopeless situation was the attack on the Baron Sysne household, disguised as a common robbery.

Since they couldn't defeat Rei himself, they targeted his ultimate source of power, his opponent in the duel.

But the result was exactly as Keep was saying now.

"What the hell am I supposed to do?! And during the festival?! What the hell is that Marquis thinking?!"

Another reason Keep was furious.

It was the proclamation issued this morning by Lord Daskar, the feudal lord.

Namely, a festival to be held in three days.

The main event of this festival, held under the pretext of the upcoming duel, was naturally the duel itself.

If the heir to the Eliell family were to lose to an opponent of lower rank in front of so many eyes, it would be a disgrace to his family's honor.

Furthermore, half of the Eriell family's fortune would be lost.

No matter how favorably one might view the situation, it was certain he would be discarded by his father, the current head of the house.

No, worse still, he might not just be discarded, but literally cut down.

For Kip, who possessed absolute confidence in his own bloodline, such an outcome was utterly unforgivable.

"What should I do... What can I do? Honestly, that guy named Rei being that strong... Isn't that some kind of mistake?"

He asked Fultos, clinging to a sliver of hope, but the response was a firm shake of the head.

"I thought maybe... and looked into it myself, but it seems the fact that Rei is strong is undeniable. We've even gotten testimony from those who participated in the war that happened in the spring."

"Hah! They're just adventurers. Aren't they just exaggerating rumors to make themselves look bigger?"

Keepp declared, as if insisting what he wanted to believe was true, but Fultos shook his head at that too.

"I even reached out to knights serving the Marquis of Larks to gather information. That rumor is an undeniable fact... no, it might even be an understatement."

"So what do you want me to do... Wait. I get that this guy named Rei is strong. But he's still just an adventurer, right?"

Suddenly, as if struck by an idea, a glimmer of hope lit up in Keep's eyes.

Noticing his master's expression, Fultos nodded slightly, though a vague sense of unease washed over him.

"Yes. It seems he might be hired by the Marquis of Larks, like during the Spring War, but he isn't actually one of the Marquis's subordinates."

"...Hmph, I see. Then he's just an adventurer after all. Give him a little money and he'll follow our orders. Hmm, yes. Why not make that Rei fellow my direct retainer? The one with the nickname 'Crimson'. He might be somewhat useful. And he has a griffin as his familiar, right? That looks impressive too."

Keep's lips curved into a satisfied smile, likely picturing himself with Set and Rei under his command.

Watching this, Fultos couldn't help but admire the idea. For Keep, this was actually a decent plan.

Turning enemies into allies was a well-worn tactic, but it was used so often precisely because it worked—and that's why it became cliché.

If Keep's idea worked, it could be a golden opportunity for a dramatic reversal.

However, a major pitfall lay in the fact that neither Keep nor Fultos harbored any doubt that Rei would serve the nobility.

Rei himself fundamentally harbored no goodwill toward nobles.

But it was different with Elena, Daskar, and a few other nobles—he actually liked them.

That's likely why no one could even guess through rumors what Rei truly thought of the nobility.

...Though it was also true that during the Spring War, nobles aligned with the aristocracy had been brought to the brink of death by Rei's hand.

"Yes, indeed. That is certainly a brilliant plan. If it goes well, we might even be able to make him lose in a duel."

"I see. Then perhaps I needn't hire anyone. I could enter the duel myself. That way, I gain the honor of defeating a renowned adventurer without lifting a finger."

It was pure wishful thinking, but Keep himself believed without a shred of doubt that his plan would succeed.

After all, serving the next head of the Eriell family would be more than enough reward for mere adventurers.

Fultos, while not quite as confident as Keep, also judged the chances of this plan succeeding were by no means slim.

"My lord, I must say, perhaps it would be best to refrain from the duel. Forgive my bluntness, but as you yourself said, they are merely adventurers. There's no guarantee they won't attack with deadly force by mistake."

"Is that so? ...I see. You're right. He is just an adventurer. He likely doesn't understand reason or logic."

Muttering words that would likely draw a "You're one to talk!" from any resident of Gilm, Keep turned his gaze to Fultos and nodded in response.

"Understood. If a little money settles it, that's probably best. We get that woman, Asie, and we get an adventurer with a nickname. ...Yeah, we should just hand over a few gold coins."

"Gold coins? Isn't that a bit too little? We're getting an adventurer who earned a nickname in their mid-teens. For someone like that, platinum coins would be..."

"You expect me to make that kind of concession for some adventurer?"

Fultos' words clearly didn't sit well with him. Keep's tone took on an unpleasant edge.

Seeing Keep like that, Fultos immediately bowed his head.

Having known him for years, he understood his temper was quick to flare, and this response was second nature.

"I'm not saying I don't understand why you're displeased, Keep-sama, but by showing great magnanimity here, wouldn't Rei also develop deep loyalty towards you?"

"Hm? Magnanimity... magnanimity, you say. Hmm, well. You're right. If I consider that far for an adventurer, he might understand my greatness. I see. Understood."

He seemed to like the phrase "magnanimity." Keep, as if his displeasure from mere seconds ago had been a lie, smiled contentedly and spoke.

"Alright, those terms are acceptable. Get things moving immediately."

"Yes, immediately."

Fultos quickly bowed his head, his cheeks and chin jiggling.

After finishing dinner, Rei gazed at the two magic stones in Dusk Wheat inn.

In his hands lay the ogre magic stone obtained during the dungeon conquest and a regular Gamelion magic stone.

Neither had been absorbed yet.

But...Rei's eyes didn't look particularly pleased as he looked at them.

"The Gamelion magic stone is one thing, but the ogre magic stone just gives me a bad feeling."

He muttered, recalling the opponent who had been noticeably smaller and weaker than a typical ogre.

Even if he absorbed the magic stone, if it came from a weak opponent, might he become weaker? That was why he felt this way.

He had absorbed magic stones from weak monsters like goblins before, but this time, it was the stone from a weak individual within the ogre species itself that troubled him.

Lying in bed, he stared at the magic stone in his hand, unsure what to do... when suddenly, a knock sounded at the door.

"Excuse me. Rei-san, would you have a moment? I need to speak with you."

Hearing that voice from outside the door, Rei tilted his head. He returned the magic stone to the Misty Ring, got up from the bed, put on the Dragon Robe he'd taken out of the Misty Ring, and headed toward the door.
