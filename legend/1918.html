"Huh..."

The adventurer wielding the greatsword could only utter a gasp of astonishment at seeing the kobold he had been struggling against killed in a single, anticlimactic blow.

Given that a greatsword is such a heavy weapon, what on earth had he been doing, struggling against a nimble kobold?

The outcome was so abrupt that he almost wanted to ask himself that very question.

What was unfortunate for the adventurer was that the kobold wasn’t wielding a weapon—he was unarmed… or, to be precise, he was using his claws as weapons.

Normally, kobolds would be wielding weapons like long swords, daggers, or spears.

But the kobold I fought this time had no weapon, and because of that, it was able to fully utilize its natural agility.

On top of that, while a kobold’s claws are usually sharp, they aren’t typically sharp enough to be used as weapons.

And yet, this kobold’s claws possessed both the sharpness and length necessary to be used as weapons.

I didn’t think I was outmatched in skill, but in this case, we were simply ill-matched.

That was likely why he was so stunned that the man had defeated the very opponent he’d been struggling against so effortlessly.

While he stood there in shock, Rei, accompanied by Set, approached the adventurer and spoke to him.

“Are you okay?”

“Huh? Ah, yeah. Um… sorry. You saved me.”

The stunned adventurer snapped out of it and expressed his gratitude to Rei.

It wasn’t that he didn’t feel anything about the fact that the opponent he’d been struggling with had been defeated so easily.

Jealousy over his own lack of strength and Rei’s overwhelming power.

But still, he had been saved… and considering that he had asked for help and been saved, there was no way he could choose not to say a single word of thanks.

“I see. I’m glad you’re safe. …So, about this kobold—is it a rare species or a high-tier species?”

“No, sorry, but I don’t know about that either. However, I don’t think any rare or high-tier species have appeared during this commotion. At least, that’s what I’ve heard.”

“Well, it’s true that kobolds can be hard to tell apart. …Anyway, can I take this kobold’s magic stone? You can do whatever you want with the rest.”

Since its head was blown to bits, I can’t secure any parts for the hunt certificate.

On top of that, if the magic stone—the most valuable material—is taken, the man stands to gain nothing.

(Well, maybe just having my life spared is a profit.)

The man tried to say something in response to Rei’s words, but changed his mind and nodded instead.

“Yeah, I get it. That’s fine. Since I wasn’t the one who killed this kobold, you can have the magic stone.”

“That’s a relief.”

With that brief remark, Rei immediately pulled out his knife, slit open the kobold’s chest, and extracted the magic stone from its heart.

“Well, sorry to rush you, but we’re in a hurry, so we’re going to head out now.”

“Where are you guys rushing off to?”

It wasn’t that he asked with any particular meaning in mind; it was more accurate to say he just felt like asking…

What could someone like Rei possibly be in such a hurry about?

Maybe he was in some kind of trouble?

That was what I was thinking when I asked… but Rei didn’t seem the least bit flustered; he opened his mouth as if it were nothing at all.

“Oh. I was just thinking of putting up a wall to keep the kobolds from getting into Gilm.”

“……Huh? A wall? By yourself?”

“That’s right. Though it’s not a solid wall—just a makeshift earthen wall I’ll create using magic. I don’t think it’ll stop every single kobold from getting in… but it should reduce their numbers.”

“Can you really pull that off? It’s going to cover quite a large area, you know?”

“Well, yeah. I guess it’s not impossible.”

“Grr!”

Rei said this matter-of-factly while petting Set’s head.

The male adventurer honestly couldn’t wrap his head around the man standing before him.

Just how much power would one need to pull off something like that?

“Ah… I see. I get it. Well, to be precise, I realize I can’t possibly understand you. …But what am I supposed to do now? I really needed to make a little more money hunting kobolds.”

“Ah. In that case, you should join the Giganto Turtle disassembly job the guild is recruiting for. If we build a wall here, the kobolds who can’t get into Gilm will naturally be redirected to the Giganto Turtle disassembly site, so you’ll be able to fight kobolds to your heart’s content over there. Plus, in addition to the reward, we’ll give you a little bit of Giganto Turtle meat.”

Saying only that, Rei left the area with Set.

The male adventurer still looked as if he wanted to say something else to Rei, but perhaps because Rei showed no sign of paying him any mind, he gave up and went off to look for another kobold.

“...Well, about this magic stone... what do you think?”

Holding the kobold’s magic stone—which he had just wiped with a cloth taken from his Misty Ring—Rei asked Set.

In response to Rei’s question, Set opened his beak and made a gurgling sound, as if to say, “You’ll know once you try it.”

“Right. …Well then, here.”

As if feeding him a piece of candy, Rei tossed the magic stone he was holding into Set’s beak.

Set swallowed it.

But... no announcement like the ones he hears when learning a skill or leveling up echoes in his mind.

“...Unfortunately, it looks like he didn’t acquire the skill. Does that mean that kobold wasn’t a rare or higher-tier species after all, but just an ordinary kobold? For one of those, he was strangely strong, though.”

That “strong” was, strictly speaking, only relative to an adventurer wielding a greatsword.

Still, there was no doubt it was stronger than a typical kobold.

“One possible explanation is that it came from long experience fighting as a kobold? Like a so-called veteran, perhaps.”

If we play it safe, that’s probably the case.

After all, in battle, experience is, in a sense, a more important factor than physical stamina or strength.

That said, even if that were the case, there were still things we didn’t understand.

Specifically, the claws extending from the kobold’s fingers were clearly longer and sharper than those of a typical kobold.

"Could it be that the monsters entering Gilm are gradually getting stronger... or something?"

“Guruu…?”

At Rei’s mutter, Set tilted his head, as if to ask, “Is that so?”

Rei felt a sense of relief at Set’s demeanor, which, despite his abilities, radiated nothing but cuteness, and realized that worrying about the kobolds was pointless.

To begin with, while he couldn’t say for certain that creating the earthen wall they were about to build would completely stop kobolds from entering Gilm, their numbers would undoubtedly decrease.

...Instead, all the kobolds would end up being diverted to the dismantling of the Giganto Turtles taking place outside Gilm.

“Anyway, once we build the earthen wall, even among those currently hunting kobolds, more will likely decide it’s not worth the effort and switch to dismantling the Giganto Turtles. So… anyway, let’s head to the spot that connects to the outside.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a sharp cry, then, with Rei on his back, he took off running.

As soon as Set started running, the surrounding scenery flew by at an astonishing speed.

Naturally, Set couldn’t possibly run at full speed in the middle of town.

If he tried to do something like that, people walking around would undoubtedly collide with Set and suffer serious injuries.

Alternatively, while avoiding injuries, there’s a high chance he’d end up destroying buildings in the city.

But here, without having to worry about such things, Set can run to his heart’s content.

That said, even at the site where the Giganto Turtle was being dismantled, Set had been running around freely, so he wasn’t exactly that frustrated.

And just like that, in the blink of an eye, Set arrived at the forefront of the expansion work—the spot connecting Gilm to the outside.

Along the way, he saw several adventurers fighting kobolds, but some of them were so oblivious to his presence—and Rei’s—that they didn’t even notice them moving so swiftly.

“Thanks, Set.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a short growl.

As he stroked Set, Rei looked around again, but... there was no sign of the kobolds.

“Well, I wonder what’s going on here. I was sure a horde of kobolds would attack the moment we arrived. …Though, it’s nice not to have any trouble.”

Was this simply a lull in the kobolds entering Gilm, or had they simply called it a day?

He didn’t know the details, but there was no doubt this was a stroke of luck for Rei.

Rare or high-tier species aside, defeating ordinary kobolds offered Rei almost no benefit.

Moreover, since even the slum dwellers couldn’t get very close to a place like this, there was even a chance that the corpses of the kobolds he killed here might turn into undead.

Considering that, it was certainly lucky for Rei that a fight didn’t break out here.

“Anyway, since we’ve come this far, let’s get this over with before anything else gets in the way. …Set, keep an eye on the surroundings while I’m concentrating.”

“Grr!”

Watching Set let out a cry that seemed to say, “Leave it to me!”, Rei pulled the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring.

Then, after confirming the effective range of the “Terrain Manipulation” skill he was about to use, he concentrated his mind on the Death Scythe in his hand and thrust the blade into the ground.

“Terrain Manipulation”

Those were the only words that came out of Rei’s mouth.

But that was all it took for the skill to activate, placing the ground within a 70-meter radius centered on Rei under her control.

First, a 150-centimeter-high earthen wall materialized.

Next, the ground outside the earthen wall sank 150 cm.

Normally, this terrain manipulation would only be capable of creating a 150-centimeter-high earthen wall.

However, by causing the ground on the other side of the earthen wall to sink 150 cm, a three-meter-high earthen wall—albeit a pseudo-one—had been created there.

“...Well, I guess that’s about it. Though, I’ll need to do this quite a few times.”

The area connected to the outside was fairly spacious.

Given that, in order to completely seal off the outside from the inside with the earthen wall, I would need to use terrain manipulation a fair number of times.

“Grrrrrr!”

Just as Rei was about to move to create the next earthen wall, Set’s roar rang out.

“What’s going on?” I turned my gaze toward Set, and there I saw him leaping over the earthen wall.

It wasn’t that an enemy had appeared; he was probably just curious about the earthen wall right in front of him and was jumping over it for fun.

“Ah… Take it easy.”

While it’s difficult for a kobold to jump over or climb a wall like this, for Set, a wall of this size is probably no trouble at all. To him, it’s nothing more than a toy.

Rei wasn’t quite sure what to say to Set, but since he wasn’t actually destroying the earthen wall and seemed to be keeping watch on the surroundings, he decided to just say that to him.

“Grrrr!”

In response to Rei’s call, Set let out a happy growl and leaped back onto the earthen wall to play.

Rei couldn’t figure out what was so fun about it, but there was no doubt that Set was deeply fascinated by leaping over the earthen wall.

Leaving Set there, Rei headed to a spot a short distance away—a new location where he would use terrain manipulation.

“Terrain Manipulation!”

Another earthen wall materialized.

He repeated the process over and over, and the earthen walls connected.

(As for the joints... I guess it’ll be fine if I use terrain manipulation to connect them. Though, to be on the safe side, I should probably call Marina and have her freeze them with spirit magic.)

She thought this as she gazed at the 150-centimeter-high earthen wall in front of her.

Considering the power of Marina’s spirit magic, having her fill in the gaps where his own abilities fell short was undoubtedly a huge help to Rei.

……Building a wall this size in just a few dozen minutes would normally be considered an impossible feat, yet the person who did it didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

Part of the reason was probably that there were few—if any—people around with similar powers.

In any case, satisfied that the earthen wall—150 cm from Gilm’s perspective, 3 meters from the outside—was complete, Rei shouted at Set, who was still flying atop it.

“Set! The earthen wall is done for now, so let’s head back inside Gilm! We need to find Marina and have her finish it up!”

“Grrrrrrrrrrr!”

Not sure what was so amusing, Set, who had been leaping frantically across the earthen wall, let out a high-pitched cry at Rei’s words and stopped playing.
