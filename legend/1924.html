The day after Suchi and the others from the slums had moved into the warehouse… Rei was standing in front of the main gate of Gilm early in the morning, before the sun had fully risen, just as he always did.

Surrounding him were adventurers, whose numbers had clearly increased since yesterday.

In addition, while not adventurers themselves, the number of people with demolition skills had also increased, and the number of slum residents led by Suchi had grown to about eighty.

If only the number of adventurers had increased, it would likely be because the number of Kobolds entering Gilm had decreased due to the earthen wall that Rei had built...

But as for the others… the fact that the number of people who aren’t specifically adventurers but simply possess butchering skills has increased is largely due to the prospect of receiving Giganto Turtle meat.

Furthermore, the increase in slum residents consists of those whom Suchi, upon returning to the slums after yesterday’s incident, told a trustworthy person about the situation and who, after observing things, judged that she could be trusted.

Naturally, most of these people have also moved their residences from the slums into the warehouse.

Amidst all this, Rei and the others, having finished their paperwork, stepped outside Gilm as usual, but...

“Just as I thought.”

“Grr…”

Even before they opened the main gate… no, from the moment they approached it, the smell of blood wafting from beyond the gate had grown even stronger, and Rei muttered this to himself. Beside him, Set growled in agreement.

Those who hadn’t noticed the smell of blood until the main gate was opened likely became aware of it once the gate swung open.

There was a lot of murmuring.

That said, they’d learned over the past few days that by morning, a fair number of monsters would gather at the site where the Giganto Turtle had been dismembered.

For that reason, not that many people panicked upon smelling the strong scent of blood.

That said, the proportion was higher among those doing this job for the first time today.

“Rei, please.”

“Got it.”

At the request of the usual guild staff, Rei stepped outside Gilm with Set...

“Huh.”

The moment he saw the figure before him, Rei let out a sound that was somewhere between admiration and surprise.

The figure before her was a monster Rei had fought before—and yet, at the same time, one she had never fought.

It stood over five meters tall, its body wrapped in fur that appeared to belong to some kind of monster or animal.

Its well-developed muscles exuded an overwhelming sense of power to anyone who looked at it.

It had a single eye, and sharp fangs protruded from its mouth.

A Rank C monster, the Cyclops… however, this individual was clearly different from an ordinary Cyclops.

This was because its skin wasn’t blue or green like a typical Cyclops’s—it was white.

And not white in the sense of a Caucasian person, but a pure, snow-white.

In other words, it was...

“A rare species, or perhaps a higher-tier species?”

At Rei’s muttered words, the pure-white Cyclops spat whatever it had been chewing onto the ground.

It was something Rei had grown accustomed to seeing over the past few days… the upper half of a Kobold.

The lower half had been torn off and was already gone, and the internal organs were visible through the upper half that had been spat onto the ground.

“Eek!”

Who was it behind Rei who let out that scream?

But that was only natural. Even a standard cyclops is ranked C, yet this cyclops was a rare species—or perhaps a higher-tier one.

In other words, it was of a higher rank than a standard cyclops… making it a Rank B monster.

Although Gilm is full of skilled adventurers, if asked whether there are a large number of people who can easily defeat high-rank monsters like a Rank B monster, the Guild staff would silently shake their heads.

After all, the number of Rank B adventurers—who are considered high-ranking—is extremely small.

Of course, even Rank C adventurers could win if they gathered enough people, set traps, or fought on a battlefield that favored them… but the likelihood of casualties would also be high.

On top of that, there are no Rank B or higher adventurers here. …That’s right, except for Rei.

If we’d waited a little longer, Vihera might have been here, but she usually doesn’t arrive until a bit later.

(There’s no doubt she’ll be jealous.)

Since Vihera was looking forward to fighting a powerful enemy, she’ll definitely be envious if Rei is the only one who fought this white cyclops.

Thinking that, I feel a sense of joy at the prospect of obtaining this white cyclops’s magic stone myself.

I took the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear out of my Misty Ring and spoke up.

“Set, circle around from behind!”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s command, Set let out a sharp cry and ran across the snow that had fallen overnight.

The white cyclops, as expected, tracks Set with its single eye.

To the white cyclops, Set was likely the most dangerous opponent in the group.

But… with Rei right in front of it, turning its gaze toward Set was nothing short of a fatal mistake.

Rei ran across the snow, trampled by battles with Kobolds, cyclopses, and other monsters.

Closing the distance with the white cyclops at a speed worthy of being called a sprint...

“Gaaaaaah!”

The cyclops, sensing Rei’s presence, opened its mouth… and in the next instant, a fierce blizzard erupted from it.

Ice Breath.

An attack method impossible for an ordinary Cyclops.

But it was clear from its appearance that this was no ordinary Cyclops, and Rei naturally understood that it possessed some kind of special attack method.

Using Sleipnir Boots, he flew through the air.

Watching the blizzard pass directly beneath him, realizing it was winter, and—though this might be a bit of a stretch—seeing its pure white body, reminiscent of fresh snow… Rei was almost certain.

(I see. This guy is probably one of those monsters that only appear in winter.)

Rei had fought monsters that only appeared in winter several times before.

Most of them were stronger than ordinary monsters, and a particularly notable characteristic was that, like this cyclops, they had a white appearance.

Rei was thinking about all this as he leapt through the air, but in the next instant, he swung his Death Scythe at the Cyclops closing in on him.

“Power Slash!”

This skill reduces the sharpness of the Death Scythe but increases the power of a single strike.

Facing the blade of the Death Scythe swinging diagonally toward him, the white cyclops must have sensed mortal danger. Half-instinctively, he thrust his right hand forward...

...and it was sliced clean off.

The Cyclops’s right arm was severed from the elbow down and flew through the air, spinning.

The fact that the cut surface wasn’t as smooth as usual was likely due to the use of Power Slash.

Then again, from the Cyclops’ perspective, since his right arm had been severed from the elbow down, it made no difference whether the cut was clean or messy.

“Gah, Gaaaaa, Gaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!”

The white Cyclops let out a bewildered cry, as if he hadn’t immediately grasped that his arm had been severed.

But the moment it realized its arm had been severed, the pain must have hit it.

It tried to staunch the blood gushing out violently... but the next moment, Set, who had moved behind the white cyclops, delivered a side-sweeping blow—a Power Crush—blasting it sideways.

A muscular physique over five meters tall.

Although his right arm had been severed from the elbow down by Rei’s Death Scythe, it was easy to imagine just how much weight that white cyclops carried.

In fact, those watching Rei’s battle from a distance couldn’t grasp what had happened at first, even though they’d seen the scene with their own eyes.

Set’s strike—delivered by a figure barely half the size of the white cyclops—sent that colossal body flying nearly ten meters.

The sight of it bouncing several times on the ground was shocking enough to captivate the onlookers.

“Set, follow up after my strike!”

Shouting, Rei slammed the Death Scythe into the ground, switched the Twilight Spear from his left hand to his right, and infused it with magic.

While Rei could easily handle a spear attack with his left hand, when it came to throwing, using his dominant right hand resulted in greater power and more accurate aim.

The spear flew through the air, piercing the torso of the white cyclops, which was struggling to get back up using only its left arm... and then flew off in an unexpected direction.

...But in the very next instant, the spear returned to Rei’s hand.

It was one of the Twilight Spear’s abilities: the power to recall it to his hand at will.

“Gah, gah…”

It was undoubtedly a fatal blow, yet the white cyclops still drew breath and struggled to rise...

“Grrrrrrrrrrrrr!”

Set’s strike came down once more.

Though not as powerful as the Power Crush from earlier, even with just Set’s raw ability, that strike was more than enough… While it didn’t blow the White Cyclops’ head to pieces, it had enough force to snap its neck.

"G... Gah..."

That blow proved fatal, and the White Cyclops collapsed to the ground.

That said, since they couldn’t be sure if that single blow had actually killed it, neither Rei nor Set let their guard down against the white cyclops.

Staying ready to respond immediately if anything happened... Rei, holding the Death Scythe on the ground, approached the white cyclops, which remained completely motionless in its two-spear stance.

Meanwhile, Set remained ready to attack at a moment’s notice, but… even as Rei approached right up to the white cyclops, it showed no sign of reacting.

Ray tries stabbing the white Cyclops’s thigh with the tip of the Death Scythe, but it still doesn’t move.

Confirming this, he swung the Death Scythe one last time, just to be sure.

The neck was cleanly severed, and since the white cyclops was lying on the ground, the blood gushing from its neck steamed as it stained the remaining snow red.

“Waaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!”

Seeing the white cyclops with its head severed, they must have finally realized that Rei and Set had won.

Cheers erupted from the crowd watching from a short distance away, as if to say, “This is it!”

Listening to the cheers, Rei stored the severed head of the white cyclops in his Misty Ring.

"The body too..." he thought, but given its five-meter height, the body was covered in blood, and the flow from the severed neck showed no signs of stopping.

(I guess I really need to bleed it out first... but how do I do that?)

If this were an animal like a boar or a deer—or even just a regular monster—he could use the method of hanging it upside down from a tree branch or something to let the blood drain out.

But since we’re near Gilm—and specifically at the site where we’re butchering the Giganto Turtle—there are hardly any trees growing around here.

No, even if there were trees, finding one strong enough to hang the White Cyclops from without breaking under its weight is practically impossible with the trees growing around here.

(In that case, would it be best to carry it to a place with a large tree and bleed it out there? Or… hang it from the Giganto Turtle’s shell… maybe I should just skip that.)

True to its name, the Giganto Turtle’s shell is enormous, and Rei was certain it would undoubtedly be of some use.

Given that, it was definitely best to avoid doing anything that would stain that shell with blood.

As he thought this, Rei was petting Set, who had worked so hard this time, when he saw a guild employee jogging toward him.

“Rei, that was amazing. I never thought you’d defeat a monster like this…”

“Ah, I’m glad to hear you say that.”

“So, about this cyclops… I believe it’s a Snow Cyclops, but what should we do about butchering it?”

“...Snow Cyclops? This is the first time I’ve seen this Cyclops—is that what it’s called?”

“Ah, yes. As the name suggests, it’s a higher-tier species of Cyclops that only appears during the winter. It’s a Rank B monster and quite powerful… but against you, Rei, it was over in the blink of an eye.”

Having put on a display that could almost be described as an instant kill against a Rank B monster, Rei naturally drew admiring glances from the guild staff… and from the butchers as well.

“Ah… yeah. Well, this is nothing. So, once we bring out the Giganto Turtle, I’m thinking of going with Set to drain the blood from this Snow Cyclops. Is that okay with you?”

“Yes, that’s fine. Um, if you’d like, we can handle the butchering here as well—what do you think?”

In response to the guild clerk’s question, Rei told him that if possible, he’d like to have it done here.
