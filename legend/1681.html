"Grrrrrrr!"

Set let out a loud roar, and a water ball was unleashed.

Having reached Level 5, its power was far greater than before.

Four water spheres, each about one meter in diameter, struck the surface of the sea. The very next moment, the impact sent a powerful shockwave rippling through the water.

The fish, stunned by the shock, floated to the surface.

It was the same principle as the fishing method he’d used in the river before: striking a rock to stun the fish with the resulting shockwave.

The fish that surfaced this way weren’t particularly large.

Of course, while they aren’t large, that’s only in comparison to big fish like tuna; they’re perfectly fine for a regular meal.

Rei scoops up the fish that have floated to the surface using a net while riding on Set’s back.

“Gurrr!”

Seeing the fish Rei scooped up with the net, Set growls, “Give me some!”

Seeing Set like this, Rei smiled, took a fish from the net, and handed it to Set, who was looking back at him.

Set quickly snatches the fish with his beak, savors the incredibly fresh fish, and lets out a happy chirp.

(Sashimi is one thing, but eating a whole fish right off the hook like that... Ah, but I think I’ve eaten things like shirasu that way before.)

Since Rei had never eaten a whole fish like that himself, he could only imagine.

Since it’s still moving in your mouth, it must be hard to eat.

While pondering the idea of eating a fish whole, Rei continues to scoop up the fish that keep floating to the surface with his net.

She immediately places the caught fish into the net she’d taken out of her Misty Ring—not a landing net, but a net specifically for holding the catch.

She hadn’t managed to catch every single fish, but she still succeeded in getting nearly 90% of the fish floating on the surface into the net.

(It would be great if I could store them in the Misty Ring… but since they’re still alive, that’s not possible.)

Since the Misty Ring cannot store living creatures, it naturally cannot store fish that are merely unconscious either.

Therefore, I had to keep the fish in the net until I killed them.

(Was it called "saba-ori"? The move I saw on TV when I was in Japan.)

In this context, “saba-ori” doesn’t refer to the sumo wrestling move, but rather to the action that gave the term its origin.

Since mackerel spoils easily, they used to break its neck and bleed it out immediately after catching it.

That was “saba-ori,” and it was the method Rei had just come up with.

That said, “saba-ori” was basically a practice used back when refrigeration and freezing technology weren’t developed. For Rei, who possesses a “Misty Ring”—a cheat-level magic item that stops the passage of time and preserves freshness as long as the item is stored—it isn’t exactly essential.

“Set, we’ve got quite a pile of fish here, so let’s head back for now.”

“Grr!”

Set let out a cry and flapped his wings, preparing to head back… but suddenly stopped in his tracks.

A moment later, Rei also pulled the Death Scythe out of the Misty Ring.

“Grrrrrr!”

The moment the surface of the sea exploded, Set flapped his wings and soared upward from where he had been just a moment before.

Then, a massive creature—one that appeared to be a fish, roughly the same size as Set, with a body length exceeding three meters—passed through the spot where Set’s body had been.

“A fish… no, a monster!?”

To be precise, Rei couldn’t tell in an instant whether it was a fish or a monster.

But since there was no doubt that this was the creature attacking him, he couldn’t just stand there and let it happen.

And above all...

“I don’t know if it’s a fish or a monster, but I’m not letting a creature that looks that tasty get away! Set, let’s take it down!”

“Grrrrrr!”

Rei’s words must have resonated with Set as well. He let out a sharp cry and fired a water ball toward the surface of the sea.

It was the same powerful water blast that had struck the school of fish moments ago.

The only difference was that earlier, the aim had been to simply deliver a shock to the school of fish, whereas this time, he was trying to hit the enemy’s body directly with the water ball.

Given the power of this water ball, it would undoubtedly shatter the attacker’s body, leaving little left to eat.

However, when considering what the most effective attack against an opponent in the water would be, a water ball was undoubtedly the right choice at this moment.

If the enemy had been visible, there would have been plenty of other ways to attack... but in the current situation, unfortunately, there aren’t many ways to deal effective damage to an enemy in the water.

If the goal were simply to attack, there were a few other methods, such as Ice Arrow, Wind Arrow, or the Magic Eye of Impact.

But those are merely ways to attack; if you ask whether they can deal significant damage, that’s a different story.

“Damn, it dodged the direct hit. Set, if it comes up… wait!”

Rei was about to say that if they leapt out of the water like before, he’d go for a direct attack, but the enemy fired something from the depths of the sea as if reading his mind.

Rei couldn’t clearly identify what it was, but he was still able to swing his Death Scythe to deflect the attack.

And by parrying the attack with the Death Scythe, Rei finally realized what the creature in the water had fired at him.

“Scales?”

Yes, the fragments sliced and severed by the Death Scythe reflected the sunlight, and only then did Rei realize that what the enemy had launched from the water was a scale.

(So, is it a monster? No, since it fired scales, I can’t say for sure it’s a monster. Anyway, for now, I need to see if the enemy will resort to another tactic…)

With that in mind, he continued to slice through the scales being launched one after another from the sea with the Death Scythe.

Naturally, Set, the beast Rei is riding, isn’t just taking a one-sided beating.

If attacks can reach them from the other side, it naturally follows that their attacks can reach the enemy as well.

Leaving the handling of the relentless barrage of scales to Rei, Set fired a continuous stream of water orbs into the sea.

“Set, is it impossible to use the King’s Intimidation?”

“Grrrr!”

In response to Rei’s words, Set, while unleashing water orbs and occasionally firing Ice Arrows, lets out a growl to signal that it’s impossible.

The King’s Intimidation could stop the movements of opponents weaker than himself.

Even if it couldn’t stop them completely, it could at least slow them down… but that was only true if one heard the roar Set let out while unleashing the King’s Intimidation.

Or perhaps, even with earplugs in, if he were directly exposed to the pressure Set emits.

But... this enemy is hiding within the ocean—a field that offers him an overwhelming advantage.

Although it did make a show of leaping out of the water for its first strike, it has shown no sign of emerging from the sea since then.

Since it couldn’t devour Set and Rei—or drag them into the water—with that first strike, it’s clear that the enemy is wary of them.

That’s precisely why it’s attacking one-sidedly from the ocean—a place it deems absolutely safe.

“It’s playing a troublesome game. But… as a big fish, it’s just right. Set, back off a bit! Just enough so it doesn’t give up on us!”

A difficult request came from Rei’s mouth.

But Set obeyed Rei’s words immediately, flapping his wings to rise to a higher position… yet high enough that the opponent targeting him from the sea wouldn’t give up.

“Hmm?”

“What are we going to do?” Set tilted his head in confusion. In response, Rei sheathed the Death Scythe and pulled out a harpoon instead.

A harpoon made by Emoshion… and one that Rei had completely forgotten about.

(Normally, monsters wouldn’t attack if they sensed Set’s presence… But the fact that it’s attacking like this means it’s not a monster after all, right? But would an ordinary fish really launch a long-range attack by flinging its scales?)

With these thoughts in mind, Rei readies the harpoon.

There’s a wire attached to the tip of the shaft—a clever design that ensures the harpoon won’t get lost even after it’s been thrown.

...For some reason, there was a similar harpoon in Garanka, too.

Though, perhaps because it was a rural village, the harpoon wasn’t attached to a wire but to a thin rope.

“When that thing gets close to the surface, I’ll throw this harpoon. After that, we just need to use Set’s power to pull it toward the shore.”

Even as Rei was explaining, scales continued to be fired one after another from the creature in the water.

But having gained some altitude, Set was now successfully dodging the scales flying up from the sea with ease.

“Grrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl, signaling to the enemy in the water to come out of the sea.

In response to Set’s actions, which could be interpreted as a provocation, the entity in the water eventually grew larger.

It didn’t make a move to leap out of the water like it had at first, but it did come up close to the surface in an attempt to close the distance with Set, who had moved higher into the air.

Of course, it remained ready to dive back into the water at a moment’s notice.

However... once Rei’s harpoon strikes, that’s all it takes.

Once the harpoon is embedded, all that’s left is for Set to use his strength to head toward the shore, and the harpoon, attached to a wire, will forcibly drag the creature along.

A moment of tension passed… and then, flapping his wings, Set flew straight toward the surface of the sea.

From the outside, it looked as though he might plunge straight into the sea—such was the speed of his flight.

But Rei, riding on Set’s back as he reached that speed, remained completely unflustered, raising his harpoon toward the massive shadow of a fish rapidly approaching on the surface of the sea.

The creature in the water must have realized that Set was rapidly closing in on it.

In a panic, it attempts to dive from near the surface to deeper waters.

However, with Set already in motion and the creature only just beginning to dive, the latter is inevitably at a disadvantage.

In this situation, the seawater that had previously protected it from Set’s attacks now became an enemy that hindered its movement.

And so, as Set descended straight down... just before hitting the surface, he flapped his wings widely, reversing his steep dive and soaring straight upward.

If someone unaware of the situation were to see Set now, it would have looked as if he had struck the surface of the sea and bounced back.

It was as if he had performed a triple jump using the sea.

Rei, who was riding on Set’s back, had thrown his harpoon into the sea the moment Set made that triple-jump-like movement—the very instant he was closest to the surface.

If this had been his usual spear throw, he would have transferred the twist of his body from his legs to his torso and then to his arms, delivering a strike of maximum power.

But this time, because he was riding on Set, he couldn’t generate that much power.

Instead, Set had approached the surface to near its limit, and that speed was added to the power of the harpoon that Rei had launched.

The sensation of the harpoon piercing the enemy’s body through the wire lingered in Rei’s hand, and at the same time, as Set shot upward, the enemy’s full weight was transmitted through the wire into his hand.

The enemy was about the same size as Set, who was over three meters long. Naturally, its weight was comparable to Set’s… no, considering the resistance involved in being pulled out of the sea, its weight was clearly greater than Set’s.

Rei was holding onto that wire, bearing such a heavy load, with just his bare hands.

If an ordinary fisherman were gripping that wire, it wouldn’t be surprising if he suffered severe injuries to his hands—the pressure was that intense.

But the one gripping that wire was Rei’s body—a physique forged by combining the techniques of Zepair, known as the Demon, and his lineage.

There was no way something like that could do him any harm.

“Ow! Damn, that’s a hell of a pull!”

...Though that’s not to say he wasn’t feeling any pain.

“Grr!?”

"Are you okay!?" Set looks at Rei, who is riding on his back.

In response, Set nods to indicate he’s fine before speaking.

“Set, move toward the shore!”

“Grrrr!”

At Rei’s request, Set immediately begins swimming toward the shore.

Naturally, this meant that Rei, riding on Set’s back, would also head toward the shore, along with the wire he was holding… and the target impaled by the harpoon connected to that wire would inevitably be dragged toward the shore as well.

Naturally, the target, with the harpoon lodged in them, resists and tries to fight back... but while they might have had a chance if they were completely separated from Set like before, now they are connected to Set via the harpoon, the wire, and Rei.

Given that, there was no way the target could easily resist Set’s strength… and gradually, Rei and Set drew closer to the shore.

As they neared the shore, the water grew shallower, yet Set showed no sign of slowing down... By the time Rei and Set finally reached the shore, the target impaled by the harpoon had been scraped against the rocks all over their body and was covered in wounds.
