Rei stored all the timber harvested from Treant Forest into the Misty Ring and returned to Gilm on Set as planned.

The flight itself lasted only a few minutes; it felt as though the moment he left Treant Forest, he was already before the main gates of Gilm... at least, that was Rei's subjective perception.

Upon landing before the gates, he found several knights waiting there, along with, for some reason, Marina, Vihera, and Byune.

(Knights?)

Why are knights here?

That question flashed through Rei's mind for an instant, but he quickly realized that the knights' order... or perhaps even higher-ups, or worse, the lord Daskar himself, must be involved in this Treant Forest incident.

For Rei, the bigger question than the knights' presence was why Marina and the others were here.

He had probably already noticed Set flying in the sky.

The moment Set landed on the ground, the knights approached.

Though Rei and Set had previously been told not to land directly by the main gate, it had now become tacitly permitted.

The reason was likely that Set's existence had become widely known.

Even Ranga, the captain of the guard, acknowledged this.

...Though lately, Ranga seemed busy with various matters and no longer handled the formalities at the main gate.

"Been waiting for you, Rei. So, what about the tree?"

The knight who called out to him was someone Rei recognized.

Originally, Rei had frequent dealings with the knights for various reasons.

Because of that, quite a few knights felt comfortable around Rei.

Moreover, this particular knight had fought alongside Rei during the war against the Bestir Empire, and he regarded Rei more as a comrade-in-arms.

His tone was light, the kind used with a comrade-in-arms.

Rei, too, found this approach far easier to deal with than being treated with excessive formality.

Smiling at this, he opened his mouth.

"Ah, no problem. I brought all the felled trees. So, where should I put them?"

"Over there, please. We'll handle the rest from here."

The knight who had spoken to Rei pointed to a spot right beside the main gate.

There, several carts were waiting... carts specifically used by woodcutters to transport timber.

People going through the entry procedures for Gilm were gazing at those carts with curiosity.

Though merchants, adventurers, and travelers were the majority, even to them, carts specifically for woodcutters seemed unusual.

The merchants, in particular, overheard Rei's exchange with the knights and wondered if it might lead to some profitable venture.

In truth, they weren't wrong. Leaving Rei aside, the fact that the Knights had come out directly like this meant something was definitely up.

...Though whether they could get involved was another matter entirely.

Within Daskar, it was already decided which merchant guilds would be approached regarding the expansion of Gilm.

Of course, the commissioned guild might pass work to others if they couldn't handle it alone or to strengthen their influence over rival guilds.

Whether the merchant observing Rei and the knight's exchange could join that guild was a delicate matter.

"Understood. ...But regardless, why are Marina and the others here?"

Marina, speaking on behalf of the three, opened her mouth in response to Rei's question as he looked toward the woodcutter's cart.

"Nothing in particular happened, so don't worry. We just had some free time, so we were walking around town..."

Marina cut herself off, her gaze shifting toward the knights.

"Ah... yeah, I kinda get why."

Watching Byune stroke Set with an expressionless yet somehow pleased air, Rei nodded in response to Marina's words.

It wasn't like Marina and the others coming here meant there was any problem.

If anything, he was glad to see Marina and the others.

"What were you guys up to today?"

"I was asked to do something... about the Treant Forest."

"...I see."

Hearing Marina's words, Rei somehow understood what she had been doing.

To be honest, when he'd taken Marina to Treant Forest before, she'd seemed quite flustered.

That confusion stemmed precisely from Marina's high abilities as an elemental mage. This time, however, it seemed Rei wanted Marina, in her capacity as an elemental mage, to examine the wood he had cut down.

While she was indeed a formidable spirit mage, the emphasis had been more on her knowledge as a dark elf who lived in nature.

Especially since Daskar had known Marina for a long time, and Walker was also fairly close to her as her successor.

"Ah... Rei. Anyway, I'll leave the trees to you. I'm pretty swamped with other things right now. Ideally, I'd like to get the wood transported as soon as possible. You'll still need to make several trips, right?"

Rei had been talking with Marina, but the knight's words brought him back to his current situation.

"Sorry. I'll load it onto the wagon right away... How many logs can you handle?"

"Well, we have plenty of horses, and the carts are specially made, so I heard about ten ordinary logs shouldn't be a problem..."

His uncertain murmur was likely because, naturally, the knight wasn't familiar with the woodcutter's work.

"Well, well, since we've prepared this many carriages, we should be able to manage even if the number is a bit higher."

Though Rei sensed a hint of unreliability in the knight's hurriedly added words, he decided it was best to retrieve the wood stored within the Misty Ring for now. He approached the carriage.

Normally, one to two horses would be used per carriage.

However, the cargo area was elongated and widened to accommodate the wood, making the carriage larger overall. Naturally, this required more horses to pull it.

"So this is a woodcutter's wagon... It's quite different from a regular carriage, isn't it?"

Muttering to himself, Rei promptly began selecting logs from the Misty Ring and loading them onto the cargo bed.

What Rei... and the knights hadn't anticipated was that the felled trees hadn't been trimmed of branches or shaped in any way.

Normally, after cutting a tree, a woodcutter would leave it untouched for a period—sometimes over half a year—to dry. During that time, the leaves would wither, and the excess branches would be trimmed. However, in this case, they had been instructed to transport the trees exactly as they were felled.

That's precisely why the trees remained exactly as they were felled, with all their leaves still attached.

As a result, those leaves and branches were getting in the way, making it difficult to load them onto the truck bed.

"Ah... Rei, sorry, but could you unload a few trees for now? And stack them alternately?"

"Got it. Hold on a sec."

He stored a few of the trees on the truck bed into his Misty Ring, then began stacking them alternately on the bed.

With the branches and leaves still intact, they couldn't stack them as neatly as a lumberjack would, but they managed to load considerably more than when they first started.

"Alright, this wagon is good for now. Go on then!"

"Understood."

The driver nodded at the knight's words and steered the carriage toward the main gate.

The formalities must have been taken care of beforehand.

The long-bed wagon slowly entered Gilm, careful not to drop the loaded timber.

After seeing it off, Rei moved to the next wagon's cargo bed and began loading the wood in the same staggered pattern.

Compared to when the woodcutters transported them, the number of logs was considerably fewer.

This was likely because they hadn't been dried after cutting; the wood still contained plenty of moisture.

Indeed, sap and moisture were leaking from the cut ends of some of the logs Rei placed on the platform.

The moisture content varies by tree species, but since they're stored in the Misty Ring almost immediately after cutting, a great deal of moisture seeps out of every log.

...To be precise, it had been about an hour or two, but considering they were meant to dry for nearly half a year, an hour or two was within the margin of error.

That moisture content was no joke, and naturally, they couldn't just haul them like the woodcutters usually did.

Even so, once we managed to load all the wood onto the wagon, the knight who had been talking with Rei earlier approached us again.

"Well, we've managed to get this sorted for now. ...But next time, don't come right away. Wait a bit longer. From what I can see, it doesn't look like the cart will be able to return immediately."

"...I suppose so."

The carriage was built with a larger cargo bed than a standard one, and with the massive load of wood, even horses pulling it moved at a painfully slow pace.

No, even this is a woodcutter's wagon... The fact that only this many horses are needed is because magic items are used to some extent. If this were an ordinary carriage, twice... or even three times as many horses would be required.

Even now, it was moving slowly. Considering Set's flight speed, even if it returned under these circumstances, we'd definitely be left waiting here.

"If you like, we could rest a bit in town. What do you say?"

"No, let's skip it. I want to explore the forest a bit more."

"...I see."

The knight muttered briefly, saying nothing more.

This knight, too, understood the dangers of Treant Forest, having been entrusted with this matter.

That's precisely why, if Rei could resolve it, he strongly wanted to leave it to him.

If even Rei couldn't figure it out, there would be almost no other options left.

But at the same time, he had also been told by Daskar that any wood brought from Treant Forest was welcome, no matter how much.

At least, as long as there was space in the storage area... no, he had been told to gather wood even if it meant stretching resources thin.

...Behind the scenes, Daskar's alchemist subordinates work day and night, but the knights know nothing of that.

And so, after exchanging words with the knight... and speaking briefly with Marina and the others, Rei took flight toward Treant Forest alongside Set.

Watching Rei depart, the knight and the others who had been waiting for him at the site each entered Gilm.

The procedure itself didn't take long, as there weren't many people waiting at this hour.

Still, the tediousness of the process remained unchanged: presenting the guild card, showing it to the guards, and so on.

Byune gave off a slightly annoyed vibe at the hassle, but she obediently completed the procedures, knowing she had to endure it to enter Gilm.

"I don't think we need to worry about Rei... but he'll be okay, right?"

At Marina's muttered words, Vihera nodded reassuringly.

"If someone really wanted to get to Rei, a forest wouldn't stop them. ...I've never been to that forest myself, but do you think they could get to Rei?"

Faced with that, Marina could only shake her head.

Truthfully, the forest Marina had seen was indeed unsettling in many ways.

But... even with that strangeness, she couldn't possibly believe it was enough to deal with Rei.

If anything, if the forest tried to harm Rei, wouldn't the forest itself cease to exist? ...That's what she even began to think.

This wasn't some convenient guess; it was a genuine feeling within Marina.

Ever since Marina had known Rei, she had been amazed by his abilities, but she had never been disappointed by any perceived lack of them.

"I suppose so. Am I overthinking this? ...No, perhaps I'm actually worried that Rei is trying too hard and might cause Treant Forest to vanish."

"That's actually a pretty common worry."

Even Vihera couldn't foresee Rei being affected by Treant Forest, but she could easily foresee Rei affecting Treant Forest.

Some might say it was precisely because she hadn't actually seen the Treant Forest... but in a case like this, even knowing the forest, she hadn't particularly worried about Rei.

It showed how well she understood Rei's capabilities, but a big part of it was simply that she couldn't imagine Rei dying no matter how she thought about it.
