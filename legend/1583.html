Soldiers appeared before Ryutas, who held a longsword, and his guards.

That said, their equipment varied from soldier to soldier; it wasn't a uniform set of gear.

Still, seeing men bearing weapons, one could only call them soldiers.

(If they were adventurers, we might have been able to negotiate...)

Adventurers generally accept quests for the money.

Moreover, the adventurers of this country of Reburina were by no means superior to those of the Kingdom of Mireana.

That's precisely why, barring a few exceptions, they should have agreed to a deal if it meant making easy money.

But standing before Ryutas now were soldiers.

(A noble's private guard... No, that's not right. A force this large must be the national army. Though their equipment varies considerably. So they must have been dispatched on an unofficial mission.)

The only saving grace was that the soldiers of the Kingdom of Reburina were weaker than those of the surrounding nations.

Of course, it was precisely because the soldiers were weak... and because it was a small country unable to muster numbers like the Kingdom of Mireana... that they resorted to using giants as a means.

Even if Reburina's soldiers were weak, Ryutas didn't possess the strength to do anything about it.

All Ryutas could do now was buy time.

He prayed the guards who had entered the cave would reunite with Rei and the others as soon as possible.

"Well, well, everyone. What brings you to such a place?"

Wearing his usual mask, Ryutas addressed the soldiers who had appeared.

"Soldiers, what brings you to a place like this?"

Though Ryutas spoke in a friendly tone, the soldiers weren't about to lower their guard so easily, especially with a longsword in his hand.

"And you?"

"I am..."

Ryutas started to utter the usual alias he used, but he was cut off mid-sentence.

Because the soldier next to the one he'd addressed raised his spear.

"Soldier, why on earth... Ah, it's because I'm armed, isn't it? Please rest assured... though that sounds strange. I assumed some bandit or something had appeared, so I armed myself like this. As a merchant, I need to be vigilant."

Basically, no bandits not under Jaya's control ever showed up around Mejogo. ...Though, there were probably exceptions, like when Ryutas had acted as bait before.

Given that Jaya's presence was a semi-open secret, the soldiers standing before Ryutas surely knew this too.

But if the soldiers thought Ryutas was just a merchant, perhaps they wouldn't know the circumstances and he could buy himself a little time.

Of course, Ryutas understood that words alone couldn't avoid a fight, so his goal was purely to buy time.

Fortunately, the man Ryutas first addressed seemed willing to play along with his scheme... but the soldier next to him, unfortunately, wasn't fooled by such tricks.

"Forgot? The order is to kill everyone in this forest. Even if they're not Jaya's men."

"No, but... look at them. They say they're merchants, right?"

"Listen, the fact they're merchants is just what that guy says. Besides, if they're merchants, where's their merchandise? Where do you find merchants without goods?"

True to his words, there was no cargo of any kind around Ryutas.

It was impossible to call Ryutas a merchant based on that sight alone.

Of course, there were several counterarguments to that.

For instance, the goods could be hidden inside the cave. ...As long as the soldiers right in front of him didn't know about the Jaya people or the giants in the cave, it wouldn't be impossible to cover that up.

For instance, he could have been pursued by bandits—in this case, Jaya soldiers—and, along with his companions, had to abandon the goods to escape.

For example, they entered this forest to search for new goods to trade.

For example, they were waiting in front of this cave for an important business meeting.

Such were the various possible reasons.

But while Ryutas hesitated over what to say, several soldiers emerged from the surrounding forest.

Since their goal was to kill the Jaya people likely inside this cave, it made perfect sense for the soldiers to surround the forest to prevent escape before heading for the cave.

(Which means the underground passage connecting to Mejogo is probably secured too.)

While Ryutas pondered this, the soldiers who had joined them here shot glances at the soldiers talking to Ryutas, as if to say, What the hell are you guys doing?

Their orders were clear: kill every single soul in this forest, children and the elderly included.

So why were they taking their sweet time talking?

"Hey!"

That dissatisfaction must have slipped out, as one of the newly arrived soldiers called out to his comrades in a displeased tone.

There were already nearly twenty soldiers present.

Faced with so many soldiers, Ryutasu tried to buy time somehow...

"Young Master, we can't hold them back any longer. All the soldiers except the one you spoke to are suspicious of us."

One of the guards warned him, barely moving his lips, using a peculiar way of speaking audible only to Ryutas.

If Rei had seen the guard now... and if the guard had been holding a puppet or something, he would have exclaimed in surprise, "Ventriloquism!?"

Regardless, hearing the guard's warning, Ryutas briefly considered trying to buy more time. But he quickly gave up on the idea, realizing that in their current situation, such a stunt would only result in more soldiers being sent.

Since they didn't have the strength to handle a large number of enemies, it was better to keep the enemy numbers as low as possible. He made that judgment...

He nodded slightly at the guard's words and stepped forward.

"No, no, no need to be so wary. ...Hm? Oh? Isn't that you, Mr. Ralkeno? You here on business too?"

Ryutas addressed the group casually.

Naturally, Ryutasu had no idea if someone named Larceno was among the soldiers.

But... when suddenly addressed like that, it was only natural for the soldiers to turn their gaze toward the person who seemed to have been spoken to.

Who? You? No, not you. Then you...

In this way, as if trying to determine who Ryutas' words were directed at, the crowd turned their gaze toward those near them.

If there had been a battle-hardened knight or a higher-ranking soldier present, they would have shouted angrily at the soldiers who averted their gaze from Ryutas.

How dare they avert their gaze in the presence of a suspicious figure, even if not necessarily an enemy.

But among the soldiers gathered here now, while some led small groups, there was no one who could be called battle-hardened.

To begin with, it was extremely rare for soldiers from a small country like Reburina to gain actual combat experience.

As long as they were connected to Jaya, if bandits appeared, Jaya would deal with them as an invasion of their territory. If neighboring countries attacked, they could simply call upon their suzerain, the Kingdom of Mireana, and that would be that.

If there were any sort of rebellion, soldiers would be dispatched, but nothing like that had happened recently.

The result was the creation of soldiers like these—numerous in quantity, but with practically nonexistent combat experience.

The soldiers all turned their gaze toward where Ryutas was looking... and in the next instant, Ryutas thrust forward the long sword he had been holding with the tip pointing downward.

For the soldiers, it was a completely unexpected strike.

The target was the soldier who had initially spoken with Ryutas.

He hadn't been exactly friendly toward Ryutas, but he had been genuinely trying to talk. Precisely because he was that kind of soldier, Ryutas struck him, knowing that if he felt betrayed, he would likely fight back fiercely.

The sensation of flesh being pierced traveled up to the hand gripping the longsword's hilt.

Ryutas's brow furrowed slightly at the sensation, but only for an instant.

He immediately moved to his next action.

Of course. Now was the enemy's moment of carelessness, the greatest opportunity.

Reducing the enemy's numbers by even a little during this chance was now the absolute priority.

This thought wasn't unique to Ryutas; his guards shared it.

The moment the soldiers' gaze wavered, caught by Ryutas' words, they launched their attack all at once.

Long swords, daggers, spears.

Using such weapons, they attacked the soldiers all at once.

In mere seconds, several soldiers lost their lives.

The screams of their comrades, blood splattering across their faces, and the sound of bodies crashing to the ground.

By then, it was easy for the soldiers to realize they had been deceived.

"What the hell are you doing?!"

The first to shout was the soldier who had raised his spear while Ryutas was talking to the soldier he killed first.

He probably never expected that soldier—the one trying to talk to Ryutas—would be attacked.

"Who cares!"

He swung his longsword at the shouting man while leaping backward.

The strike was aimed backward, not forward, so it couldn't wound the soldier's body.

The tip of the longsword barely scratched the surface of the leather armor, leaving only a superficial wound.

It couldn't break bones from the impact, let alone blow the soldier away.

But... the soldier clearly understood that the tip of the longsword had scraped away the surface of his leather armor.

That realization alone made his blood boil...

"You bastard, I'll kill you!"

He shouted with a face twisted by rage and murderous intent, chasing after Ryutas who was retreating backward, still holding his longsword... toward the cave.

The other soldiers, too, must have clearly recognized that the figure before them was their enemy.

They pursued Ryutas, who was retreating toward the cave, weapons drawn.

"Young master, hurry over here!"

One of the guards shouted sharply while hurling a nearby rock.

Ideally, they would have thrown daggers, but considering the number of soldiers, throwing daggers were overwhelmingly insufficient.

Given that, the best option was the cave, using the stones abundant all around.

...Though primitive, stone-throwing possessed a force that could never be underestimated.

Indeed, several soldiers lay bleeding from the head on the ground.

Even so, more soldiers arrived than fell, making it a drop in the bucket.

But it bought Ryutas and the others time to enter the cave.

Once inside the cave, they wouldn't have to face large numbers of enemies all at once.

They only need to deal with the soldiers who enter the cave.

They could see soldiers carrying bows, but this cave was a natural formation, untouched by human hands.

While the central area has been somewhat modified to allow giants to move through without too much difficulty, the walls are lined with ordinary rocks.

Rocks large enough to provide ample cover.

If you hide there, you needn't fear bows or similar weapons.

However, if the soldiers close the distance while you're hiding there, you'll undoubtedly face a tough fight.

(That's precisely why we need to make it look like we're struggling somewhat. To make them think they can defeat us without using bows. ...Though, even fighting normally, there's a high chance they'd think that anyway.)

Dodging the spear thrust from the soldier whose leather armor had been damaged by the longsword strike he'd fired while retreating earlier, Ryutas frowned slightly, thinking this was getting troublesome.

Then again, it was his own decision to stay here and wait for Rei and the others to arrive.

Given that, it wouldn't be wrong to say this situation was, in a sense, his own doing.

Dodging the spearhead, he closed the distance and thrust forward.

If asked which weapon was better suited for thrusting, most would say the spear.

And, in truth, that wouldn't be wrong.

But... that only holds true when facing opponents of equal strength, or at most a slight difference in skill.

Ryuutasu couldn't single-handedly annihilate an entire army like Rei, but he wasn't weak enough to lose to a mere soldier, someone who wasn't even a knight of the Kingdom of Reburina.

The tip of the released longsword pierced the soldier's throat in the next instant, severing his head with the momentum.

Blood spurted from the severed head like a fountain as the soldier's corpse collapsed to the ground.

Taking down soldiers like this also had the secondary effect of depriving the enemy of their foothold...

(Though it wouldn't last forever. Please, hurry up and bring Rei here.)

Thinking this, Ryutas seized the opening as the soldier, enraged by the death of his comrade, lunged forward, and thrust his blade once more.
