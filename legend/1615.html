As silence filled the room, Marina was the first to speak.

"So, in the end, your trading company... it was the Zolgea Trading Company, wasn't it? That Zolgea Trading Company wants to move its base out of the Kingdom of Reburina. Is that correct?"

Gimekara nodded, his expression one of relief at Marina breaking the silence.

"That's correct. As I mentioned earlier, this Kingdom of Reburina is already a sinking ship. Under those circumstances, we cannot afford to remain here."

"So? What exactly do you want?"

"Hey, Marina."

Rei spoke up with a hint of displeasure at Marina's words, which seemed to assume acceptance of Gime Kara's proposal.

It wasn't quite a confrontational tone, but still, for Rei, the idea of bringing in the Zolge Trading Company was an unwelcome development.

"It's fine. I'm not planning to do anything unconditionally either. That part, well, shouldn't Daskar decide? It's not something we can judge on our own."

Marina paused there, then glanced sideways at Gime Kara and spoke with a smile.

"Though, if it's somewhere other than Gilm, it'll have to go through us, and then through Daskar... so I imagine it'll get rather complicated?"

"Not at all!"

Gimekara declared immediately.

It wasn't a carefully considered response, but almost a reflexive reaction.

"If we could establish the guild headquarters in Gilm, it would be a tremendous help to us! But... are you really sure it's that simple to take on?"

"Oh dear, it seems you've misunderstood. All I can do is inform Daskar... the lord of Gilm, about you. From there, it's entirely up to your negotiations. ...Though, given how things have been until now, I imagine it will be quite a tough negotiation?"

"I don't mind!"

"Very well, then..."

Both Gimekara and Marina wore smiles.

But... to Rei, while Gimekara's smile was one thing, Marina's looked like the smile of someone watching prey caught in a trap.

"First, can you prepare enough horses and carriages for all of us, along with clothes and food? Oh, and we'll need horse feed too. Once that's done, we can arrange a meeting with the Daskar."

"...Huh?"

He didn't understand what she'd said.

Gimekara directed that look toward Marina.

But Marina, meeting his gaze, merely returned a gentle smile as if to say, What's wrong? Did something strange happen?

From Gimechara's perspective, he wanted to arrange a meeting with Daskar right here, if possible.

In reality, communication with Daskar had ended a little while ago. Even if they tried to use the Paired Orb to talk to Daskar again now, wouldn't that just be an inconvenience for them? That's what Rei thought.

If it were truly an emergency, that would be another matter. But considering their current situation, trusting the Zolge Trading Company or Gimekara was simply out of the question.

"What's wrong? Didn't you understand what Marina meant? Or... did you perhaps think that just because you asked to meet with Lord Daskar, he'd say 'Yes, understood'?"

"Well..."

Gimekara faltered at Rei's words.

He understood that Gilm had been elated at the possibility of moving the guild headquarters to Gilum.

But... even so, he couldn't simply accept Marina's demands without question.

"But I must say, your conditions are a bit steep. If you could guarantee Gilm would accept them, that might be one thing, but as payment merely for arranging a meeting with Lord Daskar... it seems a bit..."

"Is that so? Then why don't you arrange a meeting with Daskar on your own and negotiate?"

Marina stated it matter-of-factly.

She wouldn't budge on the terms. If they were unhappy with her conditions, they could try harder on their own.

That implication hung heavy... and Gimekara fell silent once more.

After all, wrapping up this negotiation on their own was nearly impossible.

Well, it might be possible if they took their time, but they didn't have that luxury now.

To shorten that time even slightly, they absolutely needed Rei and the others' cooperation.

"Could you perhaps adjust the terms a little more? As you know, our guild is one of the larger ones in the Kingdom of Reburina, but even so, it would be difficult to fully meet all your demands."

"Is that so? Then why don't you arrange a meeting with Daskar on your own and negotiate?"

Marina repeated the exact same words she had spoken seconds before.

Her gaze toward Gimekara held almost no friendly atmosphere.

Still, the fact that it wasn't entirely absent suggested she recognized Gimekara to some degree, likely because they were the first to make contact with them in an organizational sense.

"As I said before, the Zolge Trading Company was conducting business with Jaya... It's an indisputable fact that they were in a cooperative relationship. Given that, do you really think the people with us would be forgiven on simple terms?"

Faced with that, Gimekara couldn't argue.

No, several counterarguments came to mind, but he knew that even if he voiced them now, they probably wouldn't be heard.

Marina's gaze fixed on him was that stern.

Watching Gimekara like this, Vihera, who had been observing the exchange, spoke up.

"Honestly, I think Marina's instructions are too lenient. If it were me, I'd set much stricter conditions."

"Hmm."

Byune murmured in agreement with Vihera's words.

Elena nodded silently but firmly, agreeing with the sentiment.

Exposed to the women's gazes, Gimekara turned to Rei, the only other woman present, seeking salvation with her eyes.

But Rei, who had directly participated in the battle at Mejogo and knew exactly what Jaya was up to, returned Gimekara's gaze with equal intensity.

It was a gaze so intense that even Gimekara—known as a capable figure within the Zolgei Trading Company, entrusted with the role of liaising with Rei and the others, despite her gentle demeanor—could say nothing under its scrutiny.

"I'm afraid if you leave it to me, the terms will be far harsher than Marina's. If you're still willing to accept that, then we can proceed accordingly?"

"N-no... that's fine."

Realizing that this was no joke, Gimekara hastily shook his head.

He knew that even under the current harsh conditions, if even tougher terms were imposed, they would absolutely be unable to meet them.

"I see. ...So, what will you do? If you intend to accept Marina's proposal, I suggest you act quickly. We won't be here forever either. We're leaving tomorrow, and we're drifting further away from Rossi every day."

"I... understand."

Gimekara also understood that Rei and the others were only here today for the purpose of camping.

He also knew about their destination, Gilm, from information gathered in Emisma.

And he knew that fulfilling Marina's conditions sooner rather than later was best... but even so, preparing wagons for a thousand people, the horses to pull them, fodder, food, and clothing was far beyond what Gimekara could decide on his own.

Given that, whether accepting or declining, he couldn't give an answer here and now.

"Um, I'm sorry, but would it be acceptable if I took that matter back with me? Of course, we will do our utmost to respond with sincerity and dedication."

"Is that so? Then I'll leave it to you. But... don't forget that the longer you take, the worse our feelings toward you will become, understand?"

To put it bluntly, even if Gime Kara—no, to be precise, the Zolgee Trading Company—came forward later, after Rei and the others had already obtained everything they needed from the villages and towns they visited during their journey, claiming they had fulfilled Rei's requests, it would be meaningless.

Even if they brought more than necessary, if Rei and the others had no use for it, accepting it would be pointless.

In such a case, even if the Zolgea Trading Company met the conditions Marina set forth, it wouldn't be accepted.

Of course, Gimekara understood that much.

That's precisely why they needed to hurry and leave this place as quickly as possible, to force the guild master and executives to accept Marina's conditions sooner rather than later.

"I-I understand. Well... I need to hurry and relay those conditions, so I'll take my leave here. ...Late at night, I apologize."

After saying this to Marina... and the others, Gimekara bowed deeply and dashed away.

Waiting until Gimekara's figure disappeared from view, even in the moonlight, Elena spoke up.

"What do you think will happen?"

The meaning behind that straightforward question was obvious without needing to think about it.

How Gimekara... and the Zolge Trading Company would view the conditions Marina had presented.

"Their response will depend on how the higher-ups at the Zolge Trading Company view the current situation. If they're simply thinking about using this opportunity to expand into Gilm, they'll likely refuse, deeming it unprofitable. But if they truly understand the current state of the Republic of Reburina..."

You know what I mean, right?

Marina glanced at those around her, as if to say that.

In truth, for Rei and the others, it would be a blessing if the Zolge Trading Company accepted Marina's proposal.

Every time they visited a village, they had to negotiate with village and town representatives to purchase large quantities of wagons, horses, fodder, food, clothing... Accepting the proposal would eliminate that hassle.

For Daskar, who had requested their assistance this time, securing aid from the Zolge Trading Company would mean he wouldn't have to allocate a significant portion of his budget to Rei and the others' case.

Of course, having received such aid, they would have to make some concessions to the Zolge Trading Company... but could they pull off the same tricks they did back in the Kingdom of Reburina? The answer was no.

To begin with, while the Zolge Trading Company might have held considerable influence in the small nation of Reburina, that was only possible because it was a small country.

Here in Gilm, the sole frontier region of the Mireana Kingdom—a realm among the most developed in the suzerain states—there was no way we could pull off the same tricks.

"Well, it's clear there's nothing we can do about it here and now, no matter how much we think about it... Hm?"

Rei muttered, then suddenly turned his gaze toward the sand ship.

Then, from the belly of the sand ship... from the open area used for storing cargo, a woman appeared.

"Sura, what is it?"

Rei asked curiously at the appearance of Sura, who led the resistance... and who would effectively lead this group if Rei and the others left it.

"Huh? Wait... I thought I saw someone else besides Rei on the deck just now... Was I imagining things?"

Realizing that Gimechara's arrival had apparently been seen, Rei hesitated over how to explain.

(From Sura's perspective, she couldn't possibly have a good impression of Gime Kara... or rather, the Zolgea Trading Company. Explaining that incident could lead to all sorts of trouble... Ah, but considering what happened when we weren't there, maybe I should explain it properly after all.)

He hesitated... but considering what happened while they were gone, Rei decided it was best to explain. He told Sura about the earlier incident.

But, naturally... and just as Rei had expected, Sura, upon hearing about the Zolgea Trading Company, wore an expression of displeasure.

"So you still dislike the Zolgei Trading Company?"

"It's a bit difficult to answer your question, Vihera-san. Of course, if you ask whether I like or dislike them, I absolutely dislike them. But honestly, it's also true that without the Zolgei Trading Company, we would have faced various difficulties. ...For better or worse, the Zolgei Trading Company is a merchant, after all."

"Even doing business with Jaya?"

"Yes."

"Hmm. So then, maybe I can look forward to the escort they might provide?"

If strong guards come, it might be fun.

Vihera, who loved combat, smiled with anticipation.

Though, most adventurers from the Kingdom of Reburina are fundamentally weak.

At the very least, they're nowhere near as strong as the adventurers in Gilm.

(Vihera should know that, though.)

Thinking this, Rei found himself looking forward just a little to what tomorrow might bring.
