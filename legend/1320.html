The guard who saw Rei and the other men enter the guardhouse exchanged a few words with them before immediately leading them to where Azimov was.

The room they were led to was likely used by the guards as a duty room.

Rei, who had expected to be led to a treatment room or similar place, was slightly surprised.

But when told that actual injured people were currently resting there for treatment, he had to admit it made sense.

(Besides, Azimov was targeted. So keeping him in a room like this makes sense, in case someone comes after him again.)

Seeing Azimov sleeping on the bed, Rei thought to himself.

Though Azimov had sustained a wound severe enough to be considered life-threatening, it had already healed completely thanks to the high-quality potion Rei had used.

"He hasn't woken up yet, even though his wounds are healed."

Vihera murmured as she watched Azimov.

"Isn't it because the wound was deep? Even if the potion sealed the wound, lost stamina doesn't recover. He's probably recovering that stamina by sleeping."

"Is that so?"

"...Why wouldn't Rei, who used the potion, know that?"

Marina muttered in exasperation at Rei, who asked as if surprised.

"Well, even so. I've hardly ever used potions myself."

It wasn't that Rei had never used potions.

But he'd never healed a major wound like Azimov's with one, and above all, Rei's body possessed stronger-than-average healing abilities. He'd never experienced a situation where his injuries healed but his stamina remained depleted.

"Huh?"

Hearing Marina's explanation, Rei suddenly had an idea.

Setting aside the issue of major injuries for now, today was actually the day Rei was supposed to retrieve the Sleipnir Boots he had left in storage.

Which meant Azimov was doing the final check on the Sleipnir Boots... Given how intrigued Azimov had been by the Sleipnir Boots Rei had, it was entirely possible he'd been working on that check, skimping on sleep.

If that was the case... could it be that he wasn't waking up, even though his injuries had healed, simply because he was sleep-deprived?

That's what I started to think.

"Should we wake him?"

"Don't. I understand what Rei might be thinking, but even so, it's best not to do something like that in his current state."

"But still..."

"You came here to get information from Azimov anyway, didn't you?" Rei implied without saying it outright.

Marina understood that too, but considering the depth of Azimov's wounds, it was indeed true that rousing him would be unwise for various reasons.

The idea that he was simply sleeping due to lack of sleep was, after all, only Marina and Rei's guess.

It wasn't impossible that he was still asleep for some other reason.

...Though to Rei, it still looked like he was just sleeping because he was tired.

"So, we're just going to wait here until Azimov wakes up? That sounds a bit boring."

Vihera and Byune nodded in agreement at Rei's words.

This was, after all, just a guard duty room... a place for soldiers to sleep, with nothing to pass the time.

So, just waiting here felt like a waste of time, and Rei certainly wasn't the only one who thought so.

"Yeah, I guess. But getting info from Azimov is definitely the best option... I think we should wait for him to wake up now. If we push ourselves here and something happens to Azimov later, that'd be terrible, right? Besides..."

Marina paused mid-sentence, probably unaware herself, as she swept a seductive smile across her face while looking around at Rei and the others.

...At that moment, the security guard who had remained in the duty room, presumably because it was the station's command post, saw Marina like that and flushed bright red, lowering his gaze.

Rei understood the guard's feelings all too well, but unfortunately... or perhaps fortunately in this case, he'd been spending time with Marina lately. Seeing the smile she unconsciously wore like this no longer unsettled him much.

Of course, that didn't mean he was always perfectly calm.

"Today is the memorable day we formed our party, right? Well, getting caught up in this sudden commotion on the very day we formed the party was a bit unexpected."

The sidelong glance she cast at Rei as she spoke made it abundantly clear who she blamed for this situation.

Aware that this incident had been caused by his own actions, all Rei could do was quietly avert his gaze.

After all, if it weren't for Rei's reputation, this incident wouldn't have happened.

"But if Rei hadn't been involved, maybe someone else would have gotten dragged into this? Then it probably would have been an even bigger mess, right?"

"...Wait. Did you figure out the reason after all?"

The security guard who had been listening to Rei and the others interjected.

His face was still slightly flushed from the earlier incident with Marina's smile, but he clearly hadn't missed their current conversation.

"We've heard about the conversation at the Azoth Trading Company chairman's residence. But we still haven't been told who orchestrated this whole thing."

"Ah, that part. But it's still just circumstantial evidence. There's no concrete proof."

"That's fine. This incident is nothing less than a challenge to Gilm... no, to the security force protecting that order."

Hearing the guard's words, Rei turned his gaze to Marina, wondering what she would do.

Under normal circumstances, he wouldn't have thought to share this information, treating it as separate from other matters.

But Rei knew how hard the guards worked to maintain order in Gilm.

That's precisely why, when the guard pleaded with him from the bottom of his heart, Ray couldn't simply refuse.

...Of course, the fact that Rei had a reasonably close relationship with the security force probably played a part too.

"Well, yeah. I guess it's okay to tell them? The guards are reliable for getting info since it's their job. But this time, it's on the condition that we solve it ourselves, though."

What do you think? Marina's gaze fell on the guard, who wore a troubled expression.

For the guard, that proposal was far from acceptable.

But listening to Rei and the others now could mean important progress for the investigation going forward.

After a moment's hesitation... the guard finally nodded.

"I suppose that's fine... but..."

To be honest, if possible, they definitely wanted to handle it themselves.

But this season in Gilm saw multiple incidents occurring, and the guards needed to be running around resolving them.

After all, if it weren't for Rei and the others being involved, this case would just be an incident where a single alchemist was attacked.

Especially since Azimov had already healed his injuries with potions, and the stolen magic item had already been recovered.

Of course, there was the possibility that more than just the Sleipnir Boots had been stolen, but based on the flow of events so far, the guards... and Rei and the others felt it was almost certainly the case.

Objectively speaking, accepting Marina's proposal was the better choice.

That was the conclusion, yet as a guard, it was undeniably an unsatisfying situation.

Still, considering his duty to protect Gilm's peace as a guard, he believed accepting the proposal was best. He opened his mouth.

"I can't decide this matter alone. I need to consult my superiors. So, please wait a little longer for a formal response. However... even so, if there's anything we can do here, we'll lend a hand. ...What do you say?"

At those words, Rei paused to think... then turned his gaze to Marina, Vihera, and Byune, seeking their opinions.

Marina deemed it acceptable, and while Vihera seemed slightly displeased, she nodded in agreement.

Byune showed no particular change in expression, as if it didn't matter either way.

"Understood. Then..."

"Huh? What is it? Shut up."

I'll accept that proposal.

Just as Rei was about to say that, a voice cut in.

There was no need to wonder whose voice it was.

After all, there was only one person sleeping in this room.

"Finally awake? You slept in quite a bit."

"Huh? ...Rei? Huh? What's going on...!?"

After saying that much to Rei, he finally seemed to remember what kind of situation he was in.

Azimov frantically scanned his surroundings, then checked his own body.

But, as expected, there were no wounds.

That was only natural, since Rei had used a potion to heal him.

"How much do you remember?"

"Rei? And..."

He noticed Marina, Vihera, and Byune were also present, along with the guard, and his expression turned surprised.

"This is the guard post. ...You were badly injured."

"Seriously injured? No, but..."

"We used potions on you. You should be thankful, right?"

"...Ah, I remember now. Right, yeah. I was checking the Sleipnir Boots when suddenly something hit me... and then I lost consciousness."

At the word "suddenly," Rei frowned slightly.

Because if that was the case, it meant he didn't remember the face of whoever had attacked him.

"So you didn't see the perpetrator's face?"

"Yeah. ...More importantly, if Rei is wearing Sleipnir Boots... that means they weren't stolen?"

After confirming his own condition, his attention immediately shifted to the Sleipnir Boots Rei was wearing. That was undoubtedly why Azimov was Azimov.

"No, they were stolen. But it seems the one behind this didn't actually intend to steal the Sleipnir Boots themselves. They just wanted to use them as bait to lure me out."

"What!? Then... then... how did Rei end up wearing Sleipnir Boots now!?"

Azimov demanded of Rei with such force it was hard to believe this had just happened moments ago.

His energy was so intense, it was hard to believe he'd been asleep just minutes before.

You could call him the epitome of an alchemist, but at the same time, seeing him as a regular human being, you couldn't help but have some thoughts.

But Rei knew Azimov's personality well, so he opened his mouth without any particular surprise.

"Ah, don't worry. The goal of whoever plotted this was to pit me against the Azoth Trading Company. That's why they hid the Sleipnir Boots in the carriage of someone from the Azoth Trading Company. Thanks to Set, we managed to get them back."

He stated this, then showed Azimov the Sleipnir Boots he was wearing.

"These are the genuine article, the ones you modified for me. ...By the way, since we got them back, I'm wearing them normally like this. Is there any problem with that?"

"Hmm, yeah. When I checked them, there didn't seem to be any issues, but since they were stolen, I think it'd be best to have them thoroughly examined."

"Got it. It's probably impossible right now though..."

"It's fine."

Before Rei could finish, Azimov cut him off, holding out his hand impatiently. "Show me the Sleipnir Boots already."

"...Are you sure it's okay?"

"Yeah. No problem. So just show 'em already."

Pressed like that, Rei obediently handed over the shoes.

He'd worn them without issue until now, but since they'd passed through the hands of someone unknown, there was no guarantee they hadn't been tampered with in some way.

Of course, logically speaking, not much time had passed since Azimov was attacked before Rei and the others arrived. Then, relying on Set's sense of smell, they tracked him down almost immediately.

Like Sleipnir Boots... it was hard to imagine anyone having the time to tamper with the work of Esta Nord, said to be the greatest alchemist in history.

Still, if it had fallen into the hands of someone unknown, he wanted to be absolutely certain about that aspect.

"...Hmm, I see. ...If that's the case..."

For several minutes, Azimov remained still, fiddling with Sleipnir Boots while channeling magic to verify something.

When he finally looked up, a smile was on his face.

"No problem. The modifications went smoothly too."

"I see... Well, that's good. So, now that it's been modified, how does it work?"

"Ah. Before, it could only walk about two to three steps in the air, but now it can definitely manage five steps or more."

"...It sounds modest, but that's actually a tremendous improvement."

Recalling the battles he'd fought using Sleipnir Boots until now, Rei said this with deep feeling.
