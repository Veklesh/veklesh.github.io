After playing to his heart’s content with Set and Yellow, Rei was walking down the hallway with his maid, heading back to his room.

“Still, even if we go back to the room now, there’s nothing much to do, is there?”

For Rei, one of the biggest downsides of this world was the lack of entertainment.

While there were a fair number of books in the Misty Ring, most of them were practical guides, like monster encyclopedias.

The kind of manga and novels Rei used to read back in Japan were almost nonexistent in Elgin.

There were picture books, so it wasn’t as if there was absolutely nothing.

Other than that, entertainment like TV, DVDs, Blu-rays, and video games naturally didn’t exist here.

There are theater performances, but that wasn’t really Rei’s thing, and the formal attire and customs involved weren’t to her liking either.

“I’ve heard that Rei is quite a skilled adventurer, so why don’t you take a look at the knight’s training?”

Through her interactions with Set and Yellow, the maid’s tone toward Rei had softened, if only slightly.

Hearing the maid’s words, Rei pondered what to do.

(Knights, huh. According to Elena, the Duke of Kerebel’s army is said to be made up of quite a few elite soldiers. She mentioned that the Knight Commander, in particular, is incredibly strong, so maybe fighting him would be an option?)

She wasn’t entirely opposed to the idea, but she didn’t think someone in the position of Knight Commander would readily agree to a mock battle just because Rei asked.

(In that case, maybe I could just ask to join their regular training... specifically, their mock battles.)

Since he didn’t have anything specific to do, keeping his body active wasn’t such a bad idea for Rei either.

“Alright. So… um… do you think I should tell Elena about the situation?”

“No, Rei has been told he’s free to come and go as he pleases, so I don’t think it’s a problem.”

“Is that so? Well, if you say so, please show me where the training is taking place.”

And so, guided by the maid, Rei headed toward the place where the knight corps was training.

“Huh… They’re really putting their all into this.”

The place Rei and the others had arrived at was the training ground located within the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

It wasn’t snowing heavily, but it was snowing nonetheless, and naturally, training in such conditions would make one’s body grow cold.

Furthermore, the snow made the ground slippery, and in fact, Rei saw one of the figures who appeared to be a knight running on the training ground fall right before his eyes.

Rei had been watching the knights, but when he suddenly noticed a man running at the front of the group, he stopped in his tracks.

It was clear to him that this man alone was exceptional.

No, even to Rei’s eyes, it was clear that all the men who appeared to be knights possessed the requisite skill.

But… only the man in his late thirties leading the pack moved his body in a distinctly different way.

The way he swung his arms, moved his legs, shifted his center of gravity… though each movement was subtle, Rei could tell he was overwhelmingly superior to the others.

It was the kind of thing only Rei could discern.

That said, it was clear from the way the others were running that this man was respected and deeply trusted by those running alongside him.

As they watched the knights running alongside the maid, perhaps noticing the gaze between Rei and the maid, or simply because a maid in a maid’s uniform stood out in the falling snow, the knights eventually began to notice the two of them.

Naturally, in such a situation, it was only natural that the man leading the knights would notice Rei and the maid as well… and suddenly, his gaze met Rei’s.

In that instant, a sense of déjà vu washed over Rei.

(Have I met him somewhere... before?)

Rei thought this because he had fought that man during a previous conflict with the Azott Trading Company, but he didn’t seem to realize it.

He was a member of the Duke of Kerebel’s army, so perhaps they had met during the war with the Bestir Empire? That was the only conclusion Ray could draw.

……That said, it wouldn’t be strange if he couldn’t imagine that this man would go out of his way to help the Azott Trading Company just to verify the identity of a man named Rei, whom Elena had told him about.

No, actually, it would be strange if I could imagine such a thing.

“Um, who’s leading the way?”

Though he suspected it was likely the Knight Commander or someone of that rank, Rei couldn’t be certain, so he asked the maid.

The maid, too, must have immediately understood who Rei was referring to.

She smiled proudly and spoke.

“That is Lord Philma Deziere, the commander of the Kerebel Duke’s Knights. Alongside Lady Elena, he is said to be a key figure in the Kerebel Duke’s army.”

Hearing the maid’s words, Rei felt a strong sense of trust.

The fact that she was referred to as “Philma” must mean she is a figure of that much trust within the Duchy of Kerebel.

(So that’s Philma. Ah, come to think of it, I met him during the war with the Bestir Empire. …Elena had mentioned him briefly before… I see, he’s exactly as the rumors say.)

Rei had heard Elena, with whom he had been traveling constantly lately, mention this person several times.

The maid had described him as being on par with Elena, but Elena had told him that he was a figure of higher standing than herself.

That said, she had also firmly stated that while this was strictly in terms of command, if they were fighting purely as individuals, she herself was the superior one.

However, there was Philma, the commander of the Kerebel Duke’s Knights, and Elena, who effectively led the Elena Guard Knights.

Since both are commanders, leadership skills become just as important as individual strength—and in some cases, even more so.

In terms of command, even a natural genius like Elena stated outright that she had no confidence she could definitely defeat Philma.

...Of course, just because she couldn’t be certain of winning didn’t mean she was certain to lose.

As I recalled Elena’s words, the knights led by Philma eventually stopped running.

This run was probably just a light warm-up. Not a single knight among them was even breathing heavily.

“Well then, we’ll start with some sword swings! …Or so I’d like to say, but…”

Under normal circumstances, they would have simply begun the practice swings right then.

But the words that came out of Philma’s mouth were different from usual.

The knights exchanged surprised glances, but Philma, oblivious to their reactions, kept her gaze fixed on Rei.

“Lord Rei, known by the epithet ‘Crimson,’ is here with us today. Since he’s here, I’d like him to join our training… Is that all right? Ah, I am Philma Desir, Commander of the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights. It is an honor to meet Lord Rei, known as ‘Crimson,’ once again.”

In essence, it was a proposal suggesting that it would be acceptable for Rei to join the training, but in practice, it was tantamount to an order.

Under normal circumstances, with this many knights—nearly a hundred of them—it wouldn’t be surprising if a few of them voiced their dissatisfaction with Philma’s words.

However, that would only be the case with an ordinary knight corps. The group gathered here is the Duke of Kerebel’s Knight Corps, one of the most formidable military forces in the Kingdom of Mireana.

The members here place absolute trust in Philma, the commander of the order, and if Philma says so, no one would dare to refuse.

...That said, perhaps influenced by Philma—whom they respect—making remarks that seemed to favor Rei, there were some who shot him disapproving glances.

“Are you really a man worthy of our commander’s praise?”

Those glances likely stem from a feeling bordering on jealousy.

Still, since Philma had made the decision, no one actually voiced their dissatisfaction.

“Now, regarding the mock battle… that is, strictly speaking, only if Rei of Crimson so desires. Well, what do you say? We’re about to hold a mock battle—do you intend to participate?”

Challenging, fierce, and thoughtful.

Facing Philma’s gaze, which held such a strange intensity, Rei naturally had no intention of refusing from the start.

After all, he had gone out of his way to come to the Knights’ training grounds just to kill time, so there was no way he would refuse an invitation to join the training taking place there.

“We spoke briefly during the war with the Bestir Empire, so I suppose there’s no need for introductions. I didn’t have anything else to do anyway, and if I can join the training of the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights—who are rumored to be a group of elite warriors—I’d be more than happy to.”

Unlike at the dinner party he attended yesterday, Rei was wearing his usual Dragon Robe.

On top of that, he was wearing his usual gear, starting with the Sleipnir Boots… yes, he was dressed to respond immediately should a battle break out at any moment.

Given that, he had no shortage of equipment for the mock battle.

In response to Rei’s enthusiastic words, not only Philma but also the members of the knight corps turned their eyes toward him.

Those gazes were filled with a variety of emotions: belligerence, goodwill, jealousy, and mockery.

As the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights are considered one of the elite units even within the Kingdom of Mireana, it goes without saying that the knights belonging to that order surpass ordinary adventurers and mercenaries in ability.

Moreover, since it is Philma who trains them on a daily basis, their true strength is beyond question.

(Besides, considering that some of the guys in Gilm tend to slack off on training as soon as it starts snowing—taking that as a sign that winter has arrived—this knight corps must be training even harder than that.)

It’s not as if all adventurers in Gilm stop training entirely in winter.

No, in fact, those who do absolutely no training are probably in the minority.

Still, compared to spring through fall, the quality and duration of their training tended to decline.

Adventurers who haven’t saved enough money to get through the winter will take on quests like monster hunts even when it’s snowing, so those individuals naturally continue to see combat and their skills don’t get rusty.

“So… Rei, can we hold a mock battle right away?”

“Yeah, I’m good to go. But… what about weapons? Since you know my nickname, you must know my weapons too, right?”

Rei’s weapons were two long weapons: the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear.

He fights using a two-spear style, wielding each one with a single hand.

However, while spears are commonly used, scythes are rarely wielded, so there are no mock battle weapons designed for them.

In that case, should he just fight with spears? That’s what Rei was thinking, but…

“No, in our mock battles, we always use our own weapons.”

"Huh... So you guys take it pretty seriously, huh?"

“Of course. If we don’t train that seriously, we could end up making fatal mistakes in actual combat.”

Rei understood what Philma was getting at.

He understood, but... even though it was just training, using his own actual weapon—one with a sharp blade—meant that even the slightest mistake could actually injure his opponent.

It wouldn’t be a mere scratch; depending on the situation, it could easily be a fatal wound.

In a way, going that far just for training was a surprise to Rei.

(Well, I guess it’s not really my place to say this.)

Since Rei and the others also use their own weapons when training with Elena and Vihera, it’s only natural that he feels this way.

While Vihera, who uses her own limbs as weapons, is one thing, considering they use specialized weapons like scythes and linked swords, it was difficult to prepare weapons specifically for mock battles.

Especially Rei’s Death Scythe, which, as a magic item, possesses various effects; even if they were to hastily prepare a mock-battle weapon, its usability would be vastly different.

Anyway,

Since Philma said so, Rei took the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear out of her Misty Ring.

The moment they saw them, everyone except Philma gasped in unison.

The Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear. Either one of them is a first-rate—no, a magic item of even greater value.

With two such items suddenly appearing right before their eyes, it would be unreasonable to expect them not to be surprised.

“I do plan to hold back a bit, but I can’t guarantee anything. Are you really okay with that?”

“Yes.”

While the other knights were stunned, only Philma replied to Rei without showing any sign of being shaken.

It was as if he knew exactly what weapon Rei was holding.

(I don’t think this is the first time he’s seen the Twilight Spear, but oh well.)

With that thought in mind, Rei, assuming Philma’s nod meant he was in the clear, took his weapon in hand and began walking toward the knights.
