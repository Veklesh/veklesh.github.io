The spring sunshine poured down, making for pleasant weather.

In that weather, Rei and Vihera were being scolded by Marina in the courtyard.

"Listen. This is a mock battle. A mock battle meant to get your bodies moving, right? So why did you get so serious about it?"

"Well, you didn't use the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear, did you?"

"True. I didn't use the Immersion Palm or the gauntlet and greave abilities either."

"...But not using them doesn't mean it's okay."

Marina let out a sigh.

"Hmm..."

From a distance, Byune watched the three of them with her eyes slightly open. But she closed them again, leaned against Set, and began to doze off, bathing her whole body in the spring sunlight.

Seeing Byune's expressionless yet blissful aura, Rei and Vihera, who had been lectured, shot her envious glances...

"Hey, stop looking away."

Marina scolded them again, and the lecture resumed.

"Anyway, Rei's always so kind to Elena and Vihera, but to me..."

"Hold on. I have something to say about that too. Actually, isn't Marina the one Rei always relies on, not me?"

Rei noticed the conversation had somehow veered off topic from the mock battle, but he held his tongue. He sensed that if he said anything, the lecture would only drag on longer.

He figured as long as they kept talking without dragging him into it... Just then, a voice suddenly reached his ears.

Not Marina's, not Vihera's, certainly not the sleeping Byune or Set's—a third person's voice.

"Excuse me. Um, is this really the right place? Marina-san, are you here?"

Hearing that voice, Marina and Vihera—who had been engaged in what felt less like a lecture and more like a lovers' quarrel—froze.

The one living in this mansion was Marina, the former guildmaster, located in a corner of the noble district.

Considering that, it was unlikely someone had simply gotten lost and stumbled upon this mansion by accident.

Moreover, the voice clearly called out Marina's name.

There was no way it was a case of mistaken identity.

"Who do you think it is?"

"Hmm? ...Well, since I didn't just sneak in silently, I doubt they mean me any harm. I'll go see."

With that, Marina left the room.

Watching Marina's retreating figure, Rei breathed a sigh of relief.

The same was true for Vihera beside Rei; for now, they could only express gratitude to the visitor who had come.

"...So, what do you think it is, after all?"

"Who knows? Maybe Marina will tell us when she gets back? For now, let's just enjoy this brief moment of peace."

Rei nodded at Vihera's words... but in the next instant, his expression changed as he saw Marina returning to the courtyard.

「Rei」

"What's wrong? Did something happen?"

Sensing the urgency in Rei's tone as he addressed Marina, Set, who had been enjoying a nap under the warm sun, stood up.

Naturally, this meant Byune, who had been leaning against Set, would also wake up.

Though Byune had been completely at ease and fast asleep, knowing this was Marina's home courtyard and that she was leaning against Set, Set's sudden movement shifted her body and woke her.

"...Hm..."

Having just watched Rei and Vihera being scolded before enjoying her nap, she probably didn't understand what was happening at first.

Looking around dazedly... she eventually spotted Marina, who was clearly excited, and stood up, tilting her head.

"So, Marina. What was that all about? Or rather, who was it?"

"Remember that time we got hassled in the back alley? Well, we settled it by gathering info from that guy who seems to be the local big shot around there."

"Oh, right. So, was that info from that guy? But the voice earlier sounded like a woman."

His relief that the lecture was over lasted only a moment. When he learned the information brought in concerned Azimov's case... specifically regarding Precious, Rei's gaze sharpened.

"Of course it is. No matter what, someone from the underworld... especially someone fairly well-known around there... couldn't possibly come to the Noble Quarter."

Marina's point was undeniable, and Rei had no choice but to concede.

Still, that left him somewhat curious about how the person who had come to this mansion earlier had managed to get in.

If he came as that man's messenger, it was obvious that person wasn't exactly respectable either.

But Rei quickly shook his head, putting that question aside.

There were more pressing matters to consider right now.

(Besides, if he's from the underworld, it wouldn't be strange for him to have connections to someone who frequents the noble district. ...Well, someone with such connections would naturally be seen around the noble district often... Could he actually be a big shot?)

Turning his gaze back to Marina, Rei asked with a serious expression.

"So, what information did you get?"

"The target believed to be Precious is currently heading towards the slums."

"...Really? What the hell kind of stupid move is that?"

A hint of exasperation tinged Rei's muttered response.

It meant she was heading for the slums, but it wasn't out of concern for Precious's safety...

Even Rei didn't know Precious's exact standing within Spier Trading Company.

But if she had clashed with Tris over the Gilm branch incident, she was clearly more than just a mere merchant within the Spier Trading Company.

For someone of that stature, having bodyguards was perfectly normal, and there was absolutely no issue regarding personal safety.

The real issue was that Precious was currently suspected by Rei... no, he was practically convinced she was the culprit.

In that situation, heading straight for a slum that reeks of trouble is, by any normal logic, practically begging to be suspected.

And if you've heard about Rei's reputation, it's even more so.

"By any normal standard, it seems like a foolish thing to do. But..."

As if reaching the same conclusion as Rei, Vihera stated this much, then paused.

"Shouldn't we consider that he's doing it intentionally, knowing full well how it looks?"

"Right. If they're doing it knowing they'll be found... could it be to lure us out?"

"I see. But do you really think their forces can handle us?"

Rei knew that the party he led, the Crimson Wings, was extraordinary in many ways.

And when it came to being extraordinary, their combat prowess was particularly noteworthy.

Apart from Byune, each member possessed formidable strength individually. While Rei, who specialized in wide-area annihilation magic, couldn't fully utilize his abilities in the city, he was still exceptionally powerful in standard combat.

Marina's spirit magic could be used normally even in the city, and as for the fighter Vihera, she could fight without any problems even in confined spaces.

Furthermore, while Byune is practically useless in combat within the Crimson Wings, in an environment like the city streets, she could leverage her speed to become a significant combat asset.

As for Set, well, that goes without saying.

And yet, they're trying to lure us out in such a situation... When Rei said this, Vihera shook her head.

"I don't know about that. But they must realize that the longer this goes on, the more disadvantageous it becomes for them."

"But do you really think they'd do something like that knowing they'll lose? Even if they're restricted now, I can't imagine them picking a fight they're clearly going to lose. Unless, of course, they're completely helpless in their current state."

Merchants are, fundamentally, a race that's quick to seize opportunities.

Some lack that ability, but such merchants usually disappear quickly.

Rei couldn't imagine Precious, holding a fairly high position within the fairly large Spier Trading Company, lacking even that level of ability.

"...There's no point dwelling on it. Either way, they've come out to us. So, we'll proceed as planned and push them to the brink mentally."

"Right. If something happens in the slums, we can just crush whatever it is."

Everyone present nodded at Vihera's words, born from her confidence in her own... and their collective strength.

Even Byune, who lacked confidence in her own strength, could likely escape the battlefield before becoming a hindrance to Rei and the others by leveraging the agility inherent to a thief's existence.

Marina, who had decided she needed to be the brake in this party lacking one, had no objection to Vihera's opinion.

She was confident that if anything happened, the members of Crimson Wings could handle it with their strength.

"Right. Well then, shall we go? Staying here talking about this and that could end up being a waste of time after all."

And so, the party led by Rei, the B-rank group Crimson Wings, left Marina's mansion and headed for the slums.

Meanwhile, as Rei and the others headed for the slums... the very person being targeted walked along the street without any particular sign of being wary of their surroundings.

Surrounding them were many people wearing clothes that were clearly far shabbier than anything a Precious would wear.

No, many were barely wearing anything at all—just scraps of cloth wrapped around their bodies.

"Precious-sama, if you were coming to a place like this, you could have left it to us... Was it really necessary for you to come personally?"

One of the guards walking beside Precious murmured, keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

"Not necessarily. We need to show our sincerity to our guests. If we're to have a long-term relationship, this kind of action is essential, you might say."

"But you said you couldn't move right now..."

"Yes. But circumstances change. Especially in this case, our needs aligned with theirs."

"Aligned...?"

The guard, always ready to draw his longsword from its sheath, questioned Precious's words.

The man had been assigned as Precious's bodyguard this spring.

He was from an orphanage Precious personally funded and had previously worked as a Rank D adventurer.

The man's feelings toward Precious were pure goodwill.

Because many who grew up in the orphanage were directly employed by Precious, to him, she was a person worthy of being called a benefactor.

Due to various circumstances, he chose the path of an adventurer instead of working directly under Precious, but that didn't mean he wasn't grateful to her.

In fact, by working as Precious's bodyguard like this, he had been able to reunite with many familiar faces.

That's precisely why the man was concerned about what business Precious could possibly have in a slum like this...

Of course, he knew Precious was involved in all sorts of shady dealings.

But... for him, and for those who grew up in the orphanage, there was a shared sentiment: so what if Precious did a few shady things?

They hadn't died as orphans because Precious was there.

If asked whether to trust Precious or anyone else, trusting the former without hesitation was only natural.

That's how much they trusted, respected, and cared for Precious.

Honestly, the man wished such exchanges in places like this could be left to him.

"Gifnan, what's wrong?"

"Ah, no, nothing at all."

Realizing he'd been lost in thought while walking, the guard—Gifnan—shook his head, signaling it was nothing.

The slums were a familiar place even to Gifnan.

Though this was his first time in Gilm's slums, such places were all the same.

Unless you're incredibly lucky, the careless get devoured.

...He'd even heard stories where, if you're unlucky, it's not a metaphor at all—people get eaten in the literal sense.

In such a place, he admonished himself for letting his guard down, even for a few seconds, as he made his way through the slums...

"Huh!?"

When a man suddenly appeared, blocking their path ahead, Gifnan and the other guards drew their longswords from their sheaths...

"Well, well, Mr. Slein. It's been a while. Pleased to see you today."

Hearing Precious speak to the man in a familiar tone, they halted their movements.
