As a result, he got tangled up with Rei... and ended up suffering all sorts of terrible things.

"Well... Normally, you'd think it's because someone bought her freedom, or she got sick and couldn't work as a prostitute anymore, or something like that."

Despairing of the prostitute's life and running away... that choice would be normal too, but the women in Mejougo basically seemed to be working as prostitutes by their own choice.

Therefore, Rei thought that was unlikely.

Dozens arrive almost every single day."

"Yeah. My network confirmed where they're being taken. But... what happens to them once they get there? I don't know. Though, considering not a single woman has ever come back from there, you can probably guess."

Something unspeakable must be happening there.

If it were different, then it wouldn't matter if it were exposed to public view.

Being naturally clueless about relationships between men and women, she likely found what Jaya was doing utterly unforgivable.

If she were forced into prostitution, then taken away somewhere never to be seen again...

If that happened, she'd never find peace even in death.

But when Elena, who inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone, got truly angry, ordinary men were powerless against her.

"Huh!? ...Could you... tone it down a bit? Otherwise, this is mentally taxing for me too."

That said, only Unchu was scared. The others... well, even Byune included, weren't afraid.

Given that, she probably judged that even if Elena got angry, she wouldn't actually do anything to Byune.

"A passage leading underground in the center of Mejougo? Stairs? Anyway, we know something like that exists. Normally, destroying it without question would be the best course for retaliation against Jaya... but if the prostitute is there, we can't just destroy it so easily."

If only Jaya's people were there, Rei would have annihilated that underground facility without mercy.

Given its location beneath Mejougo, Rei couldn't use his signature area-of-effect annihilation magic. Still, he had several useful cards up his sleeve.

Using them, he could eliminate only those within the underground facility... Considering this, Rei changed his mind.

(Oh. An underground facility like this definitely has magic items. If that's the case, burning everything inside to the ground with fire is probably not a good idea.)

It was precisely because magic items, not people, were present that Rei abandoned burning the underground facility to the ground with his signature—or rather, only—fire magic. That was typical Rei.

Of course, it would be different if innocent people were present, but Rei doesn't recognize human rights for villains. ...Though, to begin with, this world doesn't have the concept of human rights.

In Rei's mind, it's nothing short of audacious for those who've caused so much harm to others to now beg for their own lives just because they might be harmed themselves.

"Please do. I don't know what's happening down there, but there's a chance they might still be alive."

Unchu said this, but the look on his face made it clear he didn't truly believe the prostitutes taken underground were safe.

But still... if there was even the slightest chance...

Marina replied to Rei's words, but Rei frowned for a few seconds at the unfamiliar term "Set cage?"

Of course, it wouldn't be completely defenseless, but there's a difference between defenses maintained routinely and defenses erected after recognizing an enemy attack.

Which is more stringent? That goes without saying.
