When Wock said he had already sent the potion Golz had asked for, Rei couldn’t help but think, “I knew it…”

(Probably got robbed by a bandit or something. Or maybe a monster—I hear goblins and other monsters are pretty common around here.)

Of course, there was also the possibility that Wock was lying.

But if he were to do something like that, his reputation would be ruined once it came to light.

He couldn’t imagine Wock doing something like that for no gain whatsoever.

Especially considering that the request was for a special potion that only Wock could make in this area, that suspicion only grows stronger.

“A thief?”

“...A thief? No, but...”

It wasn’t Wock who reacted to Rei’s words… but the guard, Welzas.

As a guard, he was naturally sensitive to talk of thieves.

“I haven’t heard of any bandits appearing around here recently. Of course, it’s possible that not a single survivor remained, so the very existence of the bandits might not be known.”

“It’s quite a distance from Somis to Golz. The possibility that they were attacked by bandits somewhere far from Somis isn’t exactly slim. …Still, bandits, huh? If only we knew where they were.”

For ordinary merchants, bandits are a terrifying foe.

There’s no doubt they’re exactly the kind of people you’d rather not encounter.

But for Rei, who is often called a “Bandit Eater” by the bandits themselves, the presence of bandits is actually something he welcomes.

If he defeats them, he saves travelers and merchants passing through the area, and he gets to keep the treasure the bandits had hoarded. If he captures them alive, he can even sell them into slavery.

For Rei, bandits are truly a blessing in every way.

……That said, since so many people have already fallen victim to them before Rei can defeat them, he can’t exactly openly rejoice at the bandits’ appearance.

(Besides, in this case, I don’t even know where the bandits are. So, hunting them down might be a bit difficult.)

Feeling a touch of disappointment, Rei takes out the ingredients for a special potion from his Misty Ring.

Upon seeing the ingredients, Wock—acting like a true expert, at least compared to the alchemists in Golz—carefully examined the materials Rei had taken out.

Though Wock had a somewhat lighthearted or carefree personality, his eye for ingredients was sharp.

However... perhaps the ingredients he’d received from the magic item shop in Golz were good enough to satisfy Wock, because after a while, he looked at Rei with a satisfied smile and spoke.

“Yeah, yeah. …All the materials are fine. …Alright, I’m going to go make a potion, so wait here.”

“Wait for me? How long exactly? Do I need to find a place to stay or something?”

They were scheduled to leave Golz tomorrow, but if making the potion took time, the worst-case scenario was that they might not leave until the day after tomorrow.

With that in mind, Rei asked...

“Ah, no. It won’t take that long. I’ll be done in a flash.”

“……Huh?”

Those words came as a surprise to Rei as well.

Since it was a special potion and they said only Wock could make it around here, he had assumed it would take several hours… or even a whole night, which wouldn’t have been surprising.

But since he said it could be done right away, it was impossible not to be surprised.

Seeing Rei’s reaction, Wock must have realized what was surprising him. He smiled and spoke.

“Just because it’s a special potion doesn’t mean it takes time to make. What’s most required is precise magical manipulation when performing alchemy. At least, for the potion I’m trying to make this time. Also, the uniqueness of the ingredients used in making the potion probably plays a role too.”

Saying this, Wock picked up one of the ingredients Rei had taken out of the Misty Ring—the dried root of some kind of plant.

“This is a bit of a rare ingredient. To use it in a potion, a special process is required, but…”

“Ah, never mind. Skip the explanation and just get on with making the potion.”

It was Wock who cut him off.

Precisely because they were acquaintances from the same town, Welzas knew that if Wock started explaining something, it would go on for quite a while.

That’s precisely why he took this action to avoid it.

Mind you, this wasn’t for Rei’s sake—he just wanted to get his hands on that special potion quickly—but purely because Welzas didn’t want to listen to a complicated lecture on alchemy.

“Is that so? Well, I guess… I have no choice. I’ll go make the potion for now, so wait here.”

Saying that, Wock grabbed his ingredients and headed for the back room.

Watching his back disappear, Welzas let out a sigh of relief.

“What’s wrong?” Rei was about to ask, but before he could, Welzas spoke up.

“Anyway, I’ll have some free time until the potion’s ready, so I’ll go buy some of the local food you said you wanted to try. As for the money…”

“For now, will this be enough?”

He handed over a single gold coin he’d taken from the Misty Ring.

“No, that’s too much. Way too much. I don’t need this much. Two silver coins should be enough for now.”

“Is that so? Well, if you like it, you can just buy more later.”

He took out a silver coin instead of the gold one and handed it to Welzas.

Welzas takes it and leaves the workshop.

“I don’t think he needed to rush off like that… Could something be wrong? He doesn’t look like it, though.”

Rei’s muttered voice echoes inside the workshop.

But Rei is the only one left here now.

Since that was the case, no one responded to Rei’s mutter.

(Still... is it okay to leave me here alone? Of course, I don't plan on doing anything.)

Glancing around, Rei is exasperated by Wock’s carelessness.

If Rei were a villain, an alchemist’s workshop with no one around would be nothing short of a treasure trove.

Perhaps it just shows how much he trusts Rei, but being trusted that much by someone he’s just met is a bit of a mystery to Rei as well.

(Is he just the type who, once he gets absorbed in one thing, doesn’t care about anything else? From what I heard earlier, that’s the impression I got.)

While thinking about Wock, Rei looks around the workshop—or rather, the office where he is currently sitting.

It was likely to explain to visitors what kind of magical items he was crafting. There were several shelves near the wall, and various magical items were placed on them.

That said, there were many items whose effects Rei couldn’t tell just by looking at them.

In particular, he even wondered if the wooden figure—or whatever it was—wasn’t a magic item at all, but just a regular doll.

“What… is this?”

He muttered to himself, but since Rei was the only one here, no one was going to answer him.

That said, he didn’t feel like reaching out to touch the magical item on his own, so Rei shifted his gaze to another one.

The next items that caught his eye were accessories like rings and necklaces, which also looked like magic items at first glance.

That said, I’d read in a book at the library before that among these kinds of magic items, there are necklaces designed to strangle the wearer in order to curse them.

That was exactly the kind of thing he didn’t want to touch before hearing an explanation—even more so than the wooden doll.

“Hm?”

As she was looking at the magical items on the shelf, Rei suddenly sensed someone approaching the workshop.

The workshop was located in an area with relatively little foot traffic.

However, since she didn’t sense any hostility, Rei decided he wasn’t an enemy for the time being and continued admiring the magical items.

“Wait, wait, wait, wait, wait! W-Wait, Master!? There’s a griffin out front—what on earth is that!?”

Shouting that, a woman—someone in her late teens—appeared.

She looked visibly flustered, or perhaps excited, and Rei could tell from her words that she was reacting to Set, who was near the workshop.

“Master, Master, Master! …Huh?”

The woman looked around the workshop in a fluster, and eventually noticed Rei inside.

“Um… are you a customer?”

“Something like that. As for your master, he’s currently having a special potion made, so I don’t think he’ll hear you making all this noise.”

At Rei’s words, the woman muttered, “Oh no…” and covered her face.

Seeing this, Rei concluded that Wock must indeed be the woman’s master.

But right before Rei’s eyes, the woman suddenly turned her gaze toward Rei as if she’d just realized something, and spoke up.

“Wait a moment. If my master is making a potion and you’re here… does that mean the griffin out front is…?”

“Yeah, that’s my familiar, Set.”

“I knew it! Um, would it be possible to get just a little bit of that griffin’s ingredients?”

Leaning her face close to Rei’s—so close that if she moved just a little closer, it wouldn’t be strange if she kissed him—the woman exclaimed.

Rei moved his body slightly away from her and shook his head.

“Sorry, but I have no intention of doing that. If I did something like that, all sorts of people would swarm all over Set.”

“Oh, no…”

The woman looked genuinely disappointed, but suddenly her eyes welled up as she looked at Rei.

“Please, is there any way you could let me have the griffin’s materials?”

“Even if you ask me that…”

“Fremena, that’s enough.”

Rei was wondering how to refuse her when, suddenly, a voice rang out in the workshop.

When Rei looked in the direction of the voice, he saw Wock standing there, holding a container filled with a deep green potion.

“Master! But it’s a griffin, you know!? I’ll do anything to get those materials!”

“Now, now, a young lady shouldn’t say things like that. …Huh? Wait, where’s Welzas?”

Noticing that the guard he’d left behind was nowhere to be seen, Wock turned his attention away from the woman named Rei and spoke.

“I’ve asked him to go buy some food. It’s an errand.”

“...To be honest, I’m not sure about sending a guard on an errand... but, whatever. Anyway, here you go. It’s the potion we agreed on. As for the leftover materials, I’ll take them, just like the letter said.”

Hearing this, Rei had no particular complaints.

As a result of being attacked by bandits, the special potion was gone.

But that wasn’t because Wock had done anything wrong.

Since Wock had faithfully carried out the work he was supposed to do, it was only natural that he would receive additional compensation if asked to take on extra tasks.

That said, from Wock’s perspective, it wasn’t exactly a difficult job, so he settled for keeping the leftover ingredients for himself.

“Got it. So, is that potion the one I’m supposed to take?”

“Yeah. …Fremena, you’d better take a good look at it.”

Fremena shot Wock a questioning look.

But Wock paid no mind to her gaze and handed the potion to Rei.

The next moment... the potion stored in the Misty Ring had completely vanished from Rei’s hand.

“Huh?”

Fremena’s slightly dazed voice echoed through the workshop.

“Um, is that some kind of magic bag or something?”

“Ugh… No.”

Wock shook his head in exasperation.

“Fremena, why don’t you stop obsessing over alchemy and try paying attention to other things for a change? Rei… it’s common knowledge that the guy with the nickname ‘Crimson’ is the one with the item box, isn’t it?”

“Eh… uh… WAH-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H-H

Had she really not known that Rei possessed an item box? A cry of astonishment rang out from Fremena’s mouth, echoing throughout the area.

Naturally, that cry carried outside the workshop as well… and Welzas, who had just bought various foods, entered the workshop, looking somewhat exasperated.
