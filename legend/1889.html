“So, Miss Elena, are you planning to go to Gilm within the next few days?”

A man in his fifties asked Elena that.

What he saw in her eyes was not trust in Elena... but rather a look of deep dissatisfaction.

Of course, whether they hide it or not, Elena fully understands that many men harbor such dissatisfaction.

For men like the one standing before her, it must be unpalatable that Elena—who bears the nickname “Princess General,” a figure who could be called the very symbol of the Noble Faction—would go out of her way to visit the stronghold of the Neutral Faction.

People like this do not take kindly to the current friendly relations between the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction; they believe it would be better to subjugate the Neutral Faction… and ultimately absorb them.

No, it’s not just that they think so—for people like this, it’s already a done deal.

That is precisely why they are displeased that Elena has gone out of her way to visit Gilm, the domain ruled by Daskar, who leads the Neutral Faction.

Though she understood the nobleman’s reasoning, Elena spoke without seeming to care.

“Hmm. Gilm is currently undergoing expansion, but some noble from the Noble Faction went and pulled a foolish stunt like sabotaging the work.”

“But even so… wouldn’t it be better to send someone else instead of Elena going there herself?”

“No. Even though my father ordered them not to interfere, they did so anyway. Given that, there’s no point in sending someone half-hearted. It’s important that I go myself.”

If she said anything that might raise the man’s expectations, it could easily be spread as fact without Elena’s knowledge.

Knowing he was the sort of man who would do such a thing without batting an eye, Elena stated it bluntly.

Hearing Elena’s words, the man looked as if he had much to say, but in the end, perhaps deciding that anything further would be pointless, he simply bowed and left the room.

“Phew.”

Watching the door through which the man had left, a sigh of relief escaped Elena’s lips.

A cup of tea was quietly placed in front of Elena.

“Thank you for your hard work, Miss Elena. For now, the person who requested a meeting today is the last one.”

At the words of Ara, who spoke with a smile, Elena gave a faint smile of her own and reached for the tea.

A rich aroma filled her mouth.

Elena had learned to brew tea herself, but she was still no match for Ara’s skill.

As she sipped the tea, she could feel the irritation within her fading away.

“Phew.”

Another sigh escapes Elena’s lips, but it is a completely different kind of sigh from the one before.

Watching Elena like this, Ara lets out a small smile.

“Hmm? What’s wrong? Did something funny happen?”

“No, nothing at all. By the way, there’s some dried fruit that goes really well with that tea. Would you like some?”

“I can’t help but feel like I’m being tricked… but oh well. I suppose I’ll just go along with what Ara says.”

Her tone was discontented, but a smile played on Elena’s face.

Ara placed a plate of purple dried fruit in front of Elena.

Although they had shrunk considerably from drying, they were still too big to eat in a single bite.

She tore off a piece of the dried fruit as easily as she would tear off a piece of bread and popped it into her mouth.

The next moment, an intense sweetness—concentrated by the sun—spread through her mouth. After savoring it for a moment, Elena took another sip of tea.

Strangely enough, the sweetness in her mouth is washed away by the tea… and she reaches for the fruit again.

She repeats this several times, then suddenly turns her gaze toward the window.

She realizes that outside the window, the sun has already set completely and it has grown dark.

(If possible, I would have liked to go out with Rei too... but no, I knew it was impossible.)

This incident, which occurred just as she was about to return to Gilm, naturally meant Elena had to take various actions.

That was precisely why Elena was meeting with nobles and influential figures in Anesis separately from Rei.

Even so, it was only natural that Elena would still want to spend time with Rei if possible.

“That’s too bad, Elena.”

“……What do you mean?”

That’s why, when Ara spoke to her as if reading her mind, Elena fell silent for a moment.

Seeing Elena like that, Ara smiled as if to say, “I understand.”

Feeling a bit embarrassed, Elena took a sip of the remaining tea, as if to hide her feelings.

“Still, thinking about going back to Gilm in a few days makes me feel a little lonely.”

“I suppose so. But I’ll still be going to Gilm. What about you, Ara? You don’t have to come with me if you don’t want to. If you’d rather stay here…”

“I’m going to Gilm.”

“It’s fine if I stay in Anesis.”

Before Elena could finish her sentence, Ara cut her off and declared it.

For Ara, Anesis was a place she could call her second home.

But even so… between Gilm, where Elena was, and Anesis, choosing the former was only natural.

“I see.”

Perhaps Elena understood how Ara felt, for she didn’t force her to stay in Anesis.

Some argue that, as the leader of Elena’s escort knights, Ara should remain in Anesis… but considering her role as Elena’s bodyguard, the prevailing opinion is that it’s better for Ara to stay with Elena.

That said, if Ara were particularly skilled at paperwork, perhaps there might have been a different opinion.

“Anyway, I’ve met with most of the key figures today. The ones left aren’t particularly important.”

“That’s true. …You really’ve worked hard.”

Ara said this in a consoling tone, and Elena responded with a smile.

Just as they were enjoying this leisurely moment, the sound of a knock on the door echoed through the room.

“Who is it?”

“Excuse me, Miss. Rei has returned. What shall we do?”

“I see, he’s back. …I hope no trouble happened?”

Given that Rei possessed a kind of celestial fortune that attracted—no, drew—commotion, Elena had assumed he must have caused some sort of disturbance while out in the city, but today he hadn’t caused any particular trouble.

That said, it wasn’t because Rei had been careful… but simply because the two nobles, Ranigus and Teres, were traveling together, and as a result, there were several knights serving as their escorts—that was the main reason.

However, Elena, who had assumed Rei was exploring Anesis alone, was unaware of this.

That’s precisely why she was worried that Rei might have gotten into some kind of trouble… but the maid who came to report that Rei had returned nodded with a smile.

“Yes, I haven’t heard of any particular problems, so please rest assured.”

Elena nodded at the maid’s words, but until she heard it directly from Rei, she couldn’t bring herself to believe them wholeheartedly.

Given Rei’s abilities, it wouldn’t be difficult for him to get caught up in some kind of trouble and still manage to handle it without letting it show.

“Maybe I’ll casually ask him about it during dinner.”

“That sounds like a good idea. I’m sure Rei is looking forward to telling Elena about today’s events as well.”

The maid had slightly misunderstood Elena’s words, but she didn’t correct her, and Elena told her she could return to her duties.

In fact, since there was no doubt she was looking forward to hearing Rei’s story, that was likely why she acted that way.

The maid bowed and left the room.

“Well, now that Rei’s back, I suppose I should ask him if he caused any trouble. Seriously.”

She seemed to have muttered this as if it were a bother, but Ara, who had been watching Elena, smiled slightly.

From an outsider’s perspective, it was obvious that Elena was looking forward to talking with Rei.

Elena noticed Ara’s reaction, but she figured that whatever she said in this situation would likely end up working against her, so she didn’t press the matter.

After finishing the remaining tea, she stood up.

“Ara, I’m going to see Rei…”

“I still need to organize some paperwork, so please go ahead without me. If I finish and still have time, I’ll come over to see you.”

“Is that so? I don’t think you really need to organize those papers right now…”

“I’d rather get it done while I can. After all, I’ll be heading to Gilm in a few days.”

Hearing that, Elena couldn’t say anything more.

After all, since she would be staying in Gilm for an extended period, it was obvious that she needed to take care of any work she could before leaving.

In Ara’s case, even though he was the commander of the knight corps, he was the type who fought on the front lines, so he left most of the paperwork to his subordinates.

But just because she delegated it didn’t mean she had no work at all.

When Ara wasn’t there, her subordinates would handle the paperwork, but when she was present, it was only natural that she should do it herself.

“I understand. Well then, take it easy.”

Saying that, Elena and Ara left the room, with Elena heading to Rei’s room and Ara heading to the room where the paperwork awaited.

(Seeing Elena happy is what makes me happiest of all.)

Recalling Elena’s smile, Ara walked down the hallway… and soon, she caught sight of several nobles talking in a corner of the hallway.

Among them was the nobleman who had earlier expressed his dissatisfaction to Elena about her going to Gilm, and Ara felt her previously calm mood rapidly fade.

Naturally, Ara didn’t let it show on her face.

She continued walking down the hallway, trying to ignore the nobles… but they wouldn’t let her.

“Lady Ara, may I have a word?”

The one who spoke was the nobleman who had met with Elena earlier.

Even as he asked if she had a moment, he approached her, seemingly confident that she would not refuse him.

Since he had spoken to her, Ara couldn’t very well ignore him, so she waited for the noble to approach.

“I still have duties as Knight Commander to attend to, so I don’t have much time, but I can spare a moment.”

“I see. That’s good to hear. Well then, I’ll get straight to the point: I’d like you to stop sending Elena to Gilm.”

“While I do lead Lady Elena’s escort knights, I have no authority to dictate her actions.”

“But aren’t you Lady Ara, Lady Elena’s childhood friend? If so, shouldn’t you advise her against her rash decision—not as a subordinate, but as a friend?”

At the nobleman’s words, the others nodded in agreement.

“Elena… no, the Princess General is a symbolic figure of the Noble Faction. For such a figure to go out of her way to visit someone who leads the Neutral Faction would be a blow to the Noble Faction’s prestige.”

“Exactly. If anything, it would be somewhat endearing if the Neutral Faction were to offer up a hostage or something.”

“Ho ho ho. It’s pointless to expect such a thing from that man Daskar. He’s the sort who doesn’t even understand what it means to be a noble.”

Hearing such presumptuous remarks, Ara felt a pang of displeasure... but she kept her expression neutral and spoke up.

“Unfortunately, Lady Elena would never go back on a decision she’s made herself. If you’re so determined to do that, you’d be better off speaking to her directly. At the very least, you’d have a better chance of success than if you asked me.”

“Oh? I hear you’re Elena’s closest friend and right-hand woman, aren’t you? If that’s the case, wouldn’t it be more effective for you to speak to her than for us to say anything?”

“I’m sorry, but I have no intention of doing that. Well then, I have work to attend to, so I’ll take my leave now.”

“……Hmph. So Elena is just a woman after all. She’s incapable of seeing the bigger picture.”

Ara was about to leave, but upon hearing one of the noblemen say that, she stopped in her tracks… and turned her gaze toward him.

That gaze didn’t say anything, but it clearly held power.

It was an intense power—so intense, one might even call it physical force.

“……”

Faced with Ara’s gaze, the nobles fell silent.

They felt as though they might be killed if they said a single word.

They even felt that kind of terror.

There is no doubt that the fact they had never actually been in battle had a major impact on them.

After fixing her gaze on them and confirming that the nobles said nothing, Ara walked away from the scene.
