It had been several hours since Byune discovered the traps inside the cave and had been making her way through while disarming them. Although she had remained completely focused throughout, by the time she reached the end of the cave, she was, unsurprisingly, quite exhausted.

If Rei and his party had been an ordinary group, it wouldn’t have been at all surprising if they had decided to call it a day, end their exploration right there, and set up camp.

Fortunately—or perhaps it should be said—there were no hostile monsters to be found in this hidden cave.

In other words, there were no safety concerns regarding setting up camp.

That said, whether they’d actually feel like camping in a cave littered with traps was another matter entirely.

In any case, Rei and the others didn’t seem to care about that at all and were exploring the area where they had exited the cave.

But that was only natural. Descending the stairs in the forest led to a space that looked as though it had been carved out of the rock. After defeating the rock plants there—though I’m not sure if “defeating” is the right term since they weren’t monsters—they found a hidden door in that space and proceeded through the cave that led from it.

I didn’t know what the intention was, but we kept moving forward while disarming the numerous traps there… and what spread out before us when we arrived was a lake that could only be called an underground lake.

“The water glows because of the moss growing in it, I suppose. It’s a type I’ve never seen before, though.”

Marina murmured as she gazed into the underground lake.

Just as she said, the depths of the subterranean lake were ablaze with light—red, blue, yellow, green… a myriad of colors, like a dazzling display.

It was vastly different from the image of an “underground lake” that usually comes to mind.

That said, it was fortunate for Rei and the others that the glowing moss in the lake and the moss clinging to the cave walls served as light sources.

If it weren’t for the moss, this place would likely be shrouded in darkness.

In that sense, the colorful moss in the water was undoubtedly a blessing.

“Even though this connects to that cave, the rocks here don’t glow, do they? …I haven’t explored many dungeons, so I’m not exactly an expert, but are all dungeons like this?”

Rei shook his head at Relieu, who asked this while gazing at the vast underground lake.

“Dungeons vary greatly from one to the next. Some places are like this, and others aren’t. As far as I know, I’ve never been to a dungeon without a light source, so you probably don’t need to worry about that.”

“……That’s pretty convenient, isn’t it?”

“I can’t say I don’t agree, but ask the dungeon’s core for a detailed explanation. I wouldn’t know if you asked me.”

Although Rei answered Relieu like that, in reality, he had destroyed the dungeon’s core several times before using the Death Scythe.

As a result, Death Scythe had learned the skill of terrain manipulation.

It’s unlikely, but if Relieu knew the details, she would undoubtedly have pressed Rei for more information.

“Anyway… so this underground lake is a dead end after all? If that’s the case, I think we need to figure out what to do next.”

Vihera muttered as she looked around the area surrounding the underground lake.

Indeed, as she said, they’ve come all this way, but if this is a dead end, they’ll have no choice but to turn back.

Of course, it was almost certainly true that this underground lake held some kind of significance.

Still, judging by the current situation, it didn’t seem like there was anything here.

“If we’re going to investigate, it’s got to be that underground lake, I suppose.”

In response to Vihera’s words, Elena murmurs as she gazes at the subterranean lake, which is bathed in a variety of lights.

In the direction of Elena’s gaze, Rei spotted a fish in the lake unlike any he had ever seen before.

“Ah, so there are fish even in an underground lake like this. …Are they edible?”

To Rei, fish were simply a source of food.

But the reason he didn’t state definitively that they were edible was likely because this was an underground lake.

If this were a river, the sea, a swamp, a pond... or even a lake, but an ordinary one on the surface, he would have declared it edible without hesitation.

(Well, I don’t think it’s inedible… but there’s no doubt it didn’t look very appetizing.)

It was only for a moment, but the fish Rei saw seemed to lack scales, like a catfish, eel, or conger eel, and had a somewhat curved body.

Of course, all of those fish are incredibly delicious.

But just because it looks similar, if you were to ask whether the fish in this underground lake is delicious… honestly, Rei couldn’t say for sure.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

“No, it’s this underground lake. It looks like there are fish in it. I was just wondering if they’d be tasty to eat.”

Marina shot Rei a slightly exasperated look.

As if to say, now is not the time to be thinking about such things.

All things considered, quite some time has passed since they descended the stairs in the forest.

In other words, if they had to go back from here, it would naturally take a fair amount of time.

That said, since Byune had already disarmed all the traps in the cave on the way here, it shouldn’t take as long as it did on the way in.

“Hmm. I’m not sure about Rei’s words, but since there’s something like an underground lake here, it’s almost certain there’s something here. That’s what I think… what do you say?”

Elena muttered as she scanned the surroundings for anything suspicious.

In fact, the cave leading to this underground lake had been concealed by a secret door.

Given that, there’s no doubt this underground lake holds some kind of secret.

The question is, what kind of secret is it…? That’s the point, isn’t it?

“Since there’s such an obvious underground lake right here, it’s definitely certain that there’s something in it, isn’t it?”

“I suppose so. Otherwise, I don’t see why they’d go to the trouble of hiding the path leading to this underground lake,”

Marina muttered, turning her gaze toward the underground lake.

Her gaze wasn’t the same one she’d had moments ago, enjoying the underwater illumination; it was a gaze focused on pondering what was here and how they might solve the mystery of how it came to be.

“Actually, this is my first time seeing an underground lake… Is this size normal?”

Rei muttered as he looked out at the underground lake spreading out before him.

The underground lake stretching out before him was quite vast.

(In Japan, would they say it’s about the size of one Tokyo Dome? …I don’t know why they’d use the Tokyo Dome as a standard for size, and since I’ve never even been to the Tokyo Dome, I have no idea how big it is, so I don’t even know if saying “one of those” is accurate.)

As Rei let his mind wander to the Tokyo Dome... suddenly, he noticed ripples forming on the surface of the underground lake, roughly in the center.

Naturally, everyone else saw it too, and all their gazes turned toward the lake.

And then… eventually, as if breaking through the water’s surface, an animal… or perhaps a monster, appeared.

But judging by the part sticking out—that slender part, specifically—it was impossible to tell how much of the rest was submerged.

“A snake?”

Who had muttered that?

In fact, judging solely by the part protruding from the water, it certainly looked like a snake.

...That said, considering that the part protruding from the water alone was likely five meters long, it would be difficult to equate that snake with an ordinary snake—the kind you commonly see out in the wild.

And underwater, I could see a long body that was repulsive even at a glance.

Was the snake’s throat moving because there was a fish or something inside it?

Rei couldn’t tell, but one thing was undeniably clear…

“It’s strong, isn’t it?”

Relieu murmured beside Rei.

Yes, Relieu had also sensed the power that Rei felt from that snake.

No, Relieu wasn’t the only one who felt it.

Everyone present… even Byune, who was a step or more behind in terms of pure combat skill, could sense the strength of the snake poking its head out of the water.

“What do you think?”

It was just a short question from Rei’s mouth, but it was enough to convey the message to everyone present.

“A boss monster… isn’t it? From what I can see, it seems to possess considerable strength.”

“I get why you’d say that, but then where is the dungeon’s core? …At the bottom of the lake or something?”

“Compared to the Silver Lion, I get the feeling we might still be able to handle this.”

Was she listening to Rei’s words, or not?

Elena muttered, recalling the strength of the Silver Lion they had fought before.

The Silver Lion they fought to save Vihera.

Its strength was so overwhelming that it was truly worthy of being called an S-rank monster.

Although Set, who masters a variety of skills, is treated as an S-rank monster due to his rarity, it’s easy to predict that if Set were to fight the Silver Lion one-on-one, the Silver Lion would almost certainly win.

That was the level of strength the Silver Lion possessed.

Compared to that Silver Lion, the giant snake currently emerging from the underground lake—while certainly a formidable foe—could by no means be considered stronger than the Silver Lion.

That is precisely why, even though Elena thought the snake was strong, she did not feel a sense of despair that she could never win.

(Of course, that snake is in the underground lake. Unlike the Silver Lion, it would need to be lured out.)

Just the other day, when they went to the sea, Elena had watched Rei struggle against the monsters there.

And this was despite the fact that, in a fair fight, the difference in strength was such that Rei would have won overwhelmingly.

……Of course, Rei was fighting the monster for food, so he couldn’t exactly blow it to smithereens; he had to defeat it in a way that would make it edible.

Also, Elena remembers having a pretty tough time when she fought a kraken before, so she understands that fighting an opponent underwater is incredibly difficult.

“What are we going to do? Are we going to fight? We’re going to fight, right?”

While everyone was speechless at the snake’s strength, the first to speak up was, as expected, Vihera.

For Vihera, who relished fighting strong opponents, it would have been impossible to suppress her desire to battle that massive snake, which was clearly a formidable foe at first glance.

Furthermore, since Vihera hadn’t participated in the battle against the Silver Lion, she was eager to face an even more powerful monster.

Her attitude made it clear she had no intention of letting a golden opportunity like this slip away.

But… in response to Vihera’s words, there were those who voiced their opposition.

“Is it really necessary to fight? As far as I can tell, they don’t seem to be making a move on us right now, so I don’t think we need to force a fight.”

The one who said that was Relieu.

From Relieu’s perspective—who had come along to support Rei and the others—facing a formidable foe like the serpent in this lake was something he absolutely wanted to avoid, even if it were just a common monster like a goblin.

To begin with, this dungeon had only just been created, and even the boss guarding its core was supposed to be a fairly weak monster... or so they’d been told.

However, once they actually entered the dungeon, the first floor was pretty much as expected—just a single Y-shaped intersection and the usual dungeon fare, though there was that baffling rock-plant thing—but when they went up to the second floor, what they found was a vast forest.

It certainly didn’t look like a newly created dungeon at all.

Descending the stairs within the forest led to a vast open space, where the same rock plants found on the first floor grew in abundance… and the final destination was this subterranean lake.

The giant snake currently standing before Relieu and the others is by no means the kind of opponent you’d expect to find in a newly formed dungeon.

In fact, it seemed to possess enough power that even if Relieu fought with everything he had, he might barely manage to win...

That’s precisely why I suggested they shouldn’t force a fight against such an opponent… but unfortunately, that reasoning didn’t fly with Rei and the others.

Vihera actually welcomed the chance to fight a powerful foe… and above all, Rei was desperate to get his hands on the magic stones of unknown monsters.

Since they’re enhanced by Beast Magic, you can never have too many magic stones from unknown monsters.

And above all...

“Look, Relieu.”

Saying that, Rei pointed to the bottom of the subterranean lake.

Under normal circumstances, it would be impossible to see the bottom like that, but this lake is covered in moss that glows like illumination.

Also, the water itself wasn’t ordinary either… even the bottom of this deep underground lake was clearly visible.

Yes, even what appeared to be the core of the dungeon at the bottom of the underground lake.
