Dusk Wheat inn… That’s the inn where Rei usually stays in Gilm.

...Lately, he’s been eating meals at Marina’s house quite often, and since she has a decent number of rooms, she’s even jokingly asked him if he’d like to move in and live with her. But Rei currently had no intention of moving out of Dusk Wheat inn.

Part of it was simply that Dusk Wheat inn was so comfortable, and part of it was that he’d been staying there since the very first time he came to Gilm, so he’d grown quite attached to it.

Also, the fact that there’s a stable where Set can stay without any issues is a major plus.

Marina’s house is fairly spacious, but there’s no stable specifically for Set.

That said, Marina’s garden is made quite livable by her spirit magic, so considering that, perhaps a stable isn’t really necessary after all.

“Oh, welcome back. So you’re back.”

Lana, the proprietress of Dusk Wheat inn, smiles upon seeing Rei and greets him.

There’s no doubt that Rei is a good customer for Lana.

……He has caused trouble a few times, but often the problem lay with others rather than Rei himself.

Also, the fact that he eats her food with such relish makes him a welcome customer to Lana.

“I heard you were in Anesis. What was it like there?”

Even as she spoke, Lana quickly finished the check-in at the front desk and handed Rei the room key.

Since he has a long-term contract and has paid his lodging fees in advance, there’s no need for any complicated procedures.

Besides, Rei had only been in Anesis for less than a month, after all.

“Yeah. It was huge. Gilm is doing some expansion work too, but getting it to that size… that’d be a bit tough. Then again, Anesis is known as the second-largest city in the Mireana Kingdom, so I guess that’s to be expected.”

“Huh, is it really that big? I’ve heard that Gilm is going to get quite a bit bigger with the expansion they’re working on right now, though.”

“Ah, that’s it. Hordes of kobolds have been appearing lately, haven’t they? Just today, when I was flying through the sky with Set a little while ago, I saw adventurers fighting kobolds over at the construction site.”

At those words, Lana’s face twisted into a slightly displeased expression.

That said, it wasn’t because she disliked Rei; it was an expression born of her distaste for the current situation—that kobolds were invading Gilm.

Since Lana lives in Gilm, she understands that it’s more dangerous here than in a normal town. She does understand that… but even so, hearing that monsters like kobolds are invading the town where she lives isn’t exactly something to be happy about.

“That’s right. For a little while now, kobolds have been coming in from out of nowhere, appearing from construction sites. For now, the adventurers are protecting us, so there’s no problem… but if it’s just kobolds, that’s one thing; if even more powerful monsters were to come after them…”

“That’s worrisome,” Lana said.

Rei understood exactly how she felt.

Currently, only kobolds are appearing, and the adventurers of Gilm are easily handling them, but if a high-rank monster were to show up here, the adventurers would likely suffer casualties as well.

This was the frontier; it wouldn’t be surprising at all if a Rank C, Rank B… or even a Rank A monster were to suddenly appear.

(Hmm, if that happens... maybe I should send Set over to the construction site? That way, unless they’re complete idiots, ordinary monsters wouldn’t dare approach if they sensed Set’s presence.)

That said, if you asked whether all the adventurers would want that, the answer would be no.

For those who didn’t have enough funds to get through the winter, being able to fight kobolds in the city—which is relatively safe (at least compared to outside Gilm)—and especially in a place where there are hardly any people because of the expansion work, is practically like a bonus stage.

Cobolt materials, magic stones, and bounty rewards are all quite cheap, but for those in dire financial straits, even if the reward per creature is small, they can simply make up for it by taking down more.

If I took Set to a place like that and kept the kobolds from coming out, he would naturally be dissatisfied.

(Ah, maybe I should have gone to see Lord Daskar with Elena after all? I wanted to ask him, as the lord, what he intended to do about the current situation.)

If I’d gone over there to talk to him, I wouldn’t have had to worry about this. With that thought in mind, Rei took the key from Lana and headed to his room.

(Come to think of it, how are Vihera and Byune doing?)

As he unlocked the door, Rei suddenly thought about the two party members.

Vihera and Byune should also be staying at the Dusk Wheat inn this evening.

Maybe I should go see them after I’ve rested a bit.

With that thought, he opened the door...

“...Why are you here?”

Seeing the scene spread out before him—the two faces he had been picturing just seconds ago—he asked that after a few seconds of silence.

Yes, for some reason, the two figures Rei had been thinking about just moments ago—Vihera and Byune—were right there in that room.

Vihera looked slightly surprised to see Rei there... but then she smiled and spoke.

“Oh, welcome back. I thought it might take a little longer.”

Seeing Vihera speak without the slightest hint of concern for Rei’s surprise, Rei decided there was no point in saying anything further.

(The door should have been locked... but then again, with Byune here, there’s no point in even thinking about it. No, the fact that I can now unlock the door to Dusk Wheat inn is probably proof that my skills in that area have improved.)

The Dusk Wheat inn is one of the more upscale inns in Gilm.

As such, security is naturally tight, and while the locks look ordinary at first glance, they utilize quite advanced technology… or so Rei recalled hearing from Lana before.

Since she was able to pick a lock like that, Byune’s skills as a thief have undoubtedly improved.

Until recently, she had been a thief specialized in combat, but it seems that while Rei was in Anesis, Byune hadn’t just been lazing around.

...Though, since he’d found out in this manner, Rei wasn’t sure if he should just be happy about it.

“Ah… well, yeah. I guess so. I’m home.”

Rei had a lot on his mind, but being greeted with “Welcome back” and replying “I’m home” still gave him a sense of relief.

“So, why did you go out of your way to come into my room while I was away? There’s nothing interesting in here, is there?”

For an ordinary adventurer, it wouldn’t be strange to find a room filled with personal belongings if they’d been staying there for a long time—even, years.

But in Rei’s case, thanks to the Misty Ring—a convenient, perhaps even too convenient item—his personal belongings were basically stored there.

That being the case, there were no personal belongings in the room at all… well, not exactly, but generally speaking, there were very few.

Being in a place like that was no different from being in an ordinary empty room, so Rei’s honest question was: what was so interesting about being in this room?

(If this were a manga, I’d probably be rummaging under the bed or something… but there’s nothing like that in this world. Well, maybe it exists, but it’s more accurate to say I’ve never seen it.)

In other words, the kind of “treasures” a teenage boy would typically have don’t exist in this world.

That said, while it’s not exactly a substitute, there are brothels, and many women work as prostitutes on their own, so I can’t say for sure which is better.

“I was a little bored, so I came over to Rei’s room to hang out. …I didn’t expect you to be back just then, though.”

“There’s nothing particularly interesting in my room, is there? I thought you knew that.”

This wasn’t the first time Vihera or Byune had come to Rei’s room.

In fact, since they’ve been coming here countless times before, it shouldn’t have been surprising that there wasn’t anything particularly interesting to see—it was something they should have known without even thinking about it.

"Then why?" Rei asked... but Vihera just smiled and spoke up.

“Well, it’s only natural to be curious about the room of someone you love, isn’t it?”

Faced with those brutally honest words, Rei hesitated for a moment, unsure of what to say...

“I see.”

In the end, he simply replied with that brief answer.

Perhaps finding Rei’s reaction amusing, Vihera smiled and changed the subject.

“So? What about Elena?”

“Hm? Oh… right. She said she was going to pop by Lord Daskar’s place for a bit.”

Rei was slightly taken aback by the sudden change of subject, but he managed to respond nonetheless.

“Ah, considering Elena’s position, I suppose she has to do that. …Besides, since Elena’s staying at Marina’s house, it’s not surprising she didn’t come over here, is it?”

“That’s true. …So, aren’t you going to help hunt down the kobolds, Vihera? It seems like you were fighting right where they’re doing the expansion work right now.”

“They’re back again?”

At Rei’s words, Vihera wiped the smile that had been on her face moments ago and adopted a displeased expression.

Byune, on the other hand, didn’t change her expression at Vihera’s attitude, but she was giving off an air of dissatisfaction.

(I see. So, while Vihera doesn’t like fighting kobolds, Byune wants to go fight… that’s the gist of it.)

Rei knew that Byune wanted to earn even a little money, so he didn’t find that surprising.

Rather, the fact that Vihera—who loves fighting—wouldn’t participate in a battle against kobolds… Thinking that, he reconsidered: if kobolds are low-rank monsters, then given Vihera’s personality, it’s only natural she’d take this attitude.

Although Vihera loves to fight, it’s not because she wants to trample on weak opponents, but because she enjoys battling strong foes.

That’s probably why she wasn’t interested in fighting low-rank monsters like kobolds.

“So, the kobolds really didn’t appeal to you? Well, I guess it can’t be helped since they’re low-rank monsters.”

“Yeah. I did show up a few times just to check it out… If there had been any higher-tier or rare species, I might have enjoyed it a little more, but all there were were ordinary kobolds.”

Rei nodded in understanding.

That said, hearing this raised a question in his mind.

“But the kobolds haven’t been attacking for just a day or two, have they? If they’re pulling a stunt like that, the kobold horde must be quite large, and it would be natural to assume that the kobold leading them is a high-rank or rare species.”

“That’s true. I think so too, and the other adventurers participating in the battles felt the same way. But… unfortunately, no matter how hard we look, we can’t find them. Byune tried her best, though… didn’t she?”

“Mm.”

In response to Vihera’s words, Byune replied with her usual brief one-word answer.

“I don’t know exactly how many kobolds are being killed each day, but it’s surely not just ten or twenty, right? When you think about it, there seem to be a lot of suspicious points.”

“I think so too. But the fact is, if they’re not there, there’s no way to find them. …A lot of the thieves participating in the battles are really frustrated about it.”

"You too, though," she added. Vihera poked Byune’s soft-looking cheek.

Showing no sign of disliking Vihera’s finger, Byune was trying hard to unlock the key she was holding.

“I see. …So, what about Marina? If she used spirit magic or something, couldn’t she track down the one leading the kobold horde?”

“Nope. Marina tried, but it didn’t work.”

“...Really?”

Hearing Vihera’s words, Ray couldn’t help but think, “You’re kidding, right?”

To Rei, Marina’s spirit magic gave him the strong impression that it was just as extraordinary as his own magic.

He had actually seen others using spirit magic a few times, but compared to Marina’s, theirs paled in comparison.

The phenomena she creates by manipulating spirits at will were so impressive that he even wondered if she had reached the very pinnacle of spirit magic.

If even Marina can’t find the one leading the kobold horde…

(Most likely, that kobold wasn’t physically within Marina’s search range. I don’t know how they’re leading the kobolds under those circumstances, though.)

With that thought, Rei, who had initially assumed it was just a simple kobold attack, smiled, finding the situation slightly intriguing.
