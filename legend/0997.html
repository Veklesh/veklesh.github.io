"Gurrrrrrr"

Set growled, his gaze shifting toward the food stall.

Following Set's gaze, Rei looked at the stall and wore a slightly surprised expression.

What the stall was selling was ice-cold fruit water.

As the temperature gradually rose and the weather grew hotter during this season, some people probably craved cold drinks during the day.

But what surprised Rei wasn't that they were selling fruit water.

It was because they were selling cold fruit water.

Naturally, with the warmer temperatures at this time of year, even if you made fruit water, it wouldn't be cold enough to call it cold; it would be more accurate to describe it as lukewarm.

Yet, at the stall where Rei's gaze was fixed, they were selling cold fruit water.

The reason? A magic item installed at the stall.

It was slightly different from the one Rei had found while exploring Gilm before, but it shared many similarities, allowing him to immediately recognize it as a magic item that emitted cold air.

"Could it be used instead of a refrigerator or air conditioner?" Rei thought, listening with interest to the explanation about the magic item. However, he decided against buying it due to its poor fuel efficiency—requiring both high-quality and large quantities of magic stones—and, more importantly, the fact that wearing a Dragon Robe made air conditioning unnecessary, and having a Misty Ring meant he didn't need a refrigerator either...

"How did you manage to buy this magic item?"

Rei, who had ordered fruit water, addressed the stall owner.

Responding to Rei's words with a smile was a massive, muscular man who, at first glance, didn't look like a street stall owner at all.

He towered over Rei, easily exceeding two meters in height.

The stall itself wasn't small, but standing next to the man made it look like a toy.

Yet his face wasn't intimidating; it even carried a certain gentle aura.

If he had a fierce face to match that size, he certainly wouldn't have been able to run a food stall.

Yes, like Pamidor, who, despite his caring nature, suffered from his intimidating appearance.

"Hahaha. This stall is more like a hobby for me."

"...So that's how you managed to buy all these magic items."

"That's right. Believe it or not, I'm a Rank B adventurer just like you, so I earn a decent income."

I wasn't surprised he immediately recognized me as a Rank B adventurer.

Seeing Set drinking fruit water from a specially prepared deep bowl beside Rei, anyone living in Gilm would instantly recognize Rei as Rei.

But unlike that person, Rei didn't recognize the man.

There were many adventurers in Gilm, so naturally, there were more faces Rei didn't recognize.

Besides, Rei himself wasn't particularly skilled at socializing, so he didn't have many acquaintances among his fellow adventurers.

"A Rank B adventurer can earn enough to buy magic items, huh?"

"I bet Rei has experienced that firsthand, huh?"

Rei shrugged slightly at the man's words.

While both Rei and the man were Rank B adventurers, Rei had been attacking thieves and stealing their treasures since he was lower ranked. He had also successfully completed several major quests, so he rarely struggled with money.

That's precisely why Rei couldn't fully agree with what the shopkeeper was saying. Still, he understood how much high-rank adventurers like Rank B adventurers earned.

He'd heard about it from folks like Lenora and Kenny, and even Elk, an A-rank adventurer, had mentioned it in casual conversation.

"Well, it's decent. Truth is, I'm no better myself—I'm pretty obsessed with magic items."

"Oh, so that's how it is. Honestly, not many people can tell just by looking at this that it's a magic item. By the way, what kind of magic items do you collect, Rei? I collect magic items that are handy for everyday life."

The most common magic item for daily use is probably lighting magic items.

But since even using those requires at least a fragment of a magic stone, you can't exactly use them freely.

There are also magic items that create fire sources, like those used to prepare cooking fires, but it was clear the shopkeeper wasn't referring to such commonplace items. He was clearly talking about the kind used in this stall.

"I'm talking about magic items you can actually use in combat. Spears, daggers... Well, the daggers aren't really usable in combat, but..."

The image of the Waterflow Dagger flashed through Rei's mind.

It was an incredibly powerful magic item that, if wielded well, could treat water itself as a weapon. However, with Rei's fire-specialized attributes, it was only good for drinking water.

That said, if you thought of it as drinking water, it tasted nothing short of heavenly, worthy of being called a divine delicacy.

"For combat, huh? You're quite serious. Well, even if our approaches differ, we both share the hobby of collecting magic items. I'd be happy if we could get along. ...It's not exactly a hobby people tend to approve of."

The shopkeeper's words overflowed with the joy of finally finding a kindred spirit.

Magic items are generally expensive. Looking at the high end, there were even items costing dozens of gold coins.

For instance, Rei's Misty Ring wasn't something you could buy no matter how much gold you offered.

Even items not quite that expensive were often bought with gold or platinum coins.

"I can say that. When I tell people collecting magic items is my hobby, they look at me like I'm some kind of weirdo. Same thing happened when I said collecting magic stones was my hobby."

"Huh? Magic stones? ...Well, I guess everyone has their own hobbies, so I can't really say anything..."

Even as he said this, the shopkeeper's gaze toward Rei suggested he considered him an eccentric.

Still, there was no malice in that gaze. Probably because the shopkeeper himself understood his own hobby was unusual.

"Anyway, I'm glad to meet a fellow enthusiast. Ah, come to think of it, I haven't introduced myself yet. I'm Seymour. Like I said earlier, I'm Seymour, a Rank B adventurer. Well, I run this stall partly for fun and partly for profit."

"...Practical use? Aren't you running at a loss? With magic items like these, wouldn't you need magic stones from some seriously high-rank monsters to power them?"

Many high-performance magic items naturally require magic stones from monsters of a correspondingly high rank.

In that sense, selling fruit juice at a stall like this probably doesn't cover the cost of the magic stones. That's what Rei was thinking when he asked, but Seymour just smiled and shook his head.

"No, no. The thing is, this magic item is highly effective, but it doesn't actually require magic stones from monsters that high-ranked. I bought it knowing that myself."

"...This cooling magic item?"

"Exactly. An E-rank monster's magic stone is more than enough to power it."

Rei's gaze grew somewhat skeptical.

Previously, magic items with similar effects required magic stones from rank C monsters.

Rank C monsters... meaning it needed the magic stone of a monster of the same rank as a Cyclops.

Why would a magic item with similar capabilities function using an E-rank monster's magic stone?

He wondered, but the fact remained that the magic item was indeed functioning right before Rei's eyes.

(Did Gilm's alchemy level skyrocket in such a short time? Well, I can't say it's absolutely impossible... but it's still unlikely to be that convenient. If that's the case, could the one I saw be an outdated model, made a long time ago?)

He pondered the question, but ultimately found no answer. In the end, he turned to Seymour, who was grinning proudly right in front of him, and asked.

"Why is it working with such low-rank magic stones?"

"I don't know. I'm not an expert on alchemy either... But I hear the Bestir Empire's alchemy level has risen dramatically overall in the last few years... or decades? Anyway, it's definitely different from what's made in Gilm, right?"

"Ah, so it's made by the Bestir Empire. That explains it... Huh?"

Suddenly, he felt a sense of unease about the words he'd spoken.

Watching Set happily drink the chilled fruit water, Rei thought for a few seconds... and realized what felt off.

"Wait. Hold on. Just wait a moment. Um, this magic item was bought in Gilm, right? Or did you actually go all the way to the Bestir Empire to buy it?"

At Rei's words, Seymour smiled as if he couldn't understand what Rei was talking about and opened his mouth.

"Well, that's obvious. Why would anyone go all the way to the Bestir Empire? Their magic items are tempting, but I'm not about to go to the Bestir Empire, which has been at war with the Mireana Kingdom for years. I know people who died in that war. Even knowing it's war, it still makes you think about things."

"Yet you use magic items made in the Bestir Empire. ...But seriously, why are magic items from the Bestir Empire sold in Gilm? If this were the royal capital or a city close to the Bestir Empire, fine, but this is Gilm, way out in the frontier!"

"It's not that strange. Merchants from the Bestir Empire do come to Gilm. It's rare, but it's not like it never happens."

Seymour's words were true.

While the Bestir Empire also had regions called the frontier, there were items only obtainable in Gilm.

Conversely, there were also items only obtainable in the Bestir Empire's frontiers, so naturally, a small number of merchants traveled from the Mireana Kingdom to the Bestir Empire's frontiers.

That much was true...

(Even so, merchants risking their lives to come all the way to Gilm couldn't possibly be numerous. We were enemies until last year. And above all, since the magic item incident, conveniently, magic items made in the Bestir Empire have started flowing into Gilm's market. Is this really just coincidence?)

One could choose to believe it was coincidence, but if it seemed connected to the alchemist Rei was searching for, that too was possible.

But if the alchemist I'm searching for really did create these magic items, why on earth would they need to do such a thing? Naturally, they wouldn't want to leave a trail for others to follow. If that's the case... a diversion? I see. That makes sense.

Making it look like they're in Gilm, while actually hiding somewhere else.

That possibility seemed entirely plausible to Rei.

At the same time, he also wondered if it wasn't a diversion at all, but simply a desire to make money.

If they were crafting magic items like golems and Magic Swords, they'd need a considerable amount of various materials. Could this be a way to fund that acquisition?

(Though it's also possible they wanted materials unique to Gilm. And with the golem incident, they might have run short on materials needed to craft magic items too.)

Even a brief consideration revealed multiple possibilities, leaving Rei unsure which one to pursue.

(Still, this is undoubtedly valuable information. If so, perhaps I should bring it up when I meet up with Vihera and Byune?)

Ultimately, he decided to discuss this matter with Vihera and the others. Rei headed toward Seymour to ask.

"So, where did you buy this magic item? If the magic stone issue is resolved, I'd like to buy one too."

"This one? It's a bit hard to find since it's down a side street off the main road... Do you know a shop called Lelouz?"

"Lerow's... Ah, the place on the main street that sells ingredients and stuff. I've stopped by a few times."

"Right, then this is easy. Head from Lelow's toward the guild, turn into the second alleyway, and just follow it all the way to the back. You'll see a shop displaying goblin skulls. That's the place."

"...A goblin skull? That's pretty tasteless."

"Hahaha. Fair point. But doesn't it seem just right? Honestly, it's a pretty hidden gem, and I go there occasionally. Though, they hardly ever had the kind of magic items Rei likes—the ones useful in actual combat—so in that sense, it might not be to his taste."

"Even though they have goblin skulls?"

At Rei's words, Seymour nodded seriously.

"Seems the owner isn't too fond of adventurers. In that sense, I'm not exactly popular there either... Well, if you mention I referred you, they probably won't turn you away. If you want to go, why not give it a try? Oh, sorry. Customers."

Seymour spotted a customer lining up behind Rei with keen eyes.

Not wanting to interfere with Seymour's business, Rei paid his bill and headed with Set to meet up with Vihera.

With an unexpected lead in hand.
