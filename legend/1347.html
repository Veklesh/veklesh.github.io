As Rei and the others approached the guard post, the sentry standing there broke into a delighted smile.

...While much of that joy likely stemmed from seeing Set relaxing, even knowing that, being greeted with a smile was still a welcome feeling for Rei.

"We'll be going inside, if that's alright?"

"Yeah, no problem. I've got the order too. ...But I'm afraid Set can't come inside the station."

"I know that already. Set."

"Grrr."

Technically, even Set as he was now could enter the guardhouse if he wanted to.

But the fact was, he'd barely be able to move inside. Considering that, he could only nod in agreement with the guard's words.

Incidentally, the entrance to the guard post was designed to be wide to accommodate bringing in various races and objects.

Watching Set walk away and lie down on the ground to enjoy the spring sunshine, Rei grabbed Gifnan's arm and entered the guard post accompanied by Vihera and Byune.

The guards inside seemed to have mixed feelings upon seeing Rei and the others, but they said nothing and simply made way.

It was Rei and his group who captured the assassin, Keena. It was also Rei and his group who brought the documents ordering Keena's release... or more accurately, her handover to the Grassland Wolves.

Thinking about it that way, the guards were practically useless.

It was only natural that some regretted their own inadequacy.

While a few harbored resentment toward Rei and her group for getting in their way, the overwhelming majority of guards felt a complex mix of emotions toward them, yet gratitude was the predominant sentiment.

"...So? Where am I going?"

"Almost there."

"Wait."

Before Rei could say anything to Gifnan, Vihera stopped him.

He smiled at Rei, who was looking at him curiously, and then addressed the nearby guards.

"Hey, can I have a word? I need a few of you to change into plain clothes and come with me."

"...Huh? What the hell are you talking about?"

Given Vihera's appearance, under normal circumstances, someone might have wondered if he was being invited out.

But, whether it was fortunate or unfortunate... none of the guards present could afford such thoughts in the current situation.

"Look, I'm taking them downstairs now, but to clearly prove they're Gifnan, it'd be better to have a few men line up with them, right?"

"...I see."

The guard seemed to find Vihera's words reasonable. The guard addressed immediately selected several men... of an age close to Gifnan's, ordered them to change into civilian clothes, and instructed them to accompany Rei and the others.

Watching this exchange from a short distance away, Rei recalled seeing something similar on police specials occasionally aired on TV back in Japan.

(So, is Vihera's approach like the Japanese police? ...It doesn't quite fit.)

Vihera, dressed in what could only be seen as a dancer's or prostitute's outfit, as police? He stifled a laugh...

"Oh, Rei. Did something amusing happen?"

As if reading his thoughts, Vihera asked Rei.

And with that beaming smile on her face, it only made her seem even more terrifying.

"No, nothing. Just lost in thought for a moment."

"Hmm... Well, we can leave it at that. But maybe you should have a proper talk with Marina and Elena later."

Beside the two engaged in this exchange, Byune quietly averted her gaze, trying not to focus on them.

While they engaged in what could only be called a lovers' quarrel, security guards dressed in civilian clothes eventually appeared.

"Well, shall we go?"

Rei nodded at Vihera's words and proceeded toward the cell.

Gifnan, his arm seized by Rei and unable to escape, felt as if he were heading toward some kind of final, decisive ruin.

Yet he didn't consider escaping because he'd been told it involved Precious.

Otherwise, he would have cut off his arm or done whatever it took to escape. ...Whether he could have actually gotten away was another matter entirely.

Leading Gifnan down to the basement... descending the stairs leading to the cells, they soon reached their destination.

The moment they entered the cell room, Rei sensed something and reacted, but quickly relaxed his guard upon determining it posed no threat.

Besides Rei, Vihera also sensed that something, but she too quickly relaxed her guard.

"Oh, welcome back. You're back rather quickly. I thought it would take a bit longer."

Approaching them with those words was Marina, who, as expected, had been assigned to guard this place.

Despite being in a subterranean cell—a place utterly unsuited to Marina, no matter how one looked at it... or perhaps precisely because of it—the smile she wore radiated an overwhelmingly feminine allure.

The sight of Marina made the guards flush, and even Gifnan, who was at the peak of tension, froze in place. That alone showed just how powerful Marina's allure was.

Many would likely agree that it was no longer just a smile, but one imbued with a spell of enchantment.

But for Rei, who had spent much of the winter with Marina, fortunately... though unfortunately for Marina herself, that power of enchantment had no effect.

"Ah. Negotiations with them went well. Thanks to that, I was able to come here like this."

Rei replied to Marina, then turned his gaze to Kena in the cell.

Kena had been captivated by Marina's smile, but upon realizing Rei was looking at her, she tensed internally, though she didn't show it.

Observing Keena's state, Rei glanced around the cell once more.

It was just the start of spring, so the cell should have been cool... or rather, chilly.

But now, the cell was as warm as the outside temperature, making it quite comfortable.

(Probably... no, definitely Marina did something with spirit magic. What I felt when I entered here was probably Marina's spirit magic too.)

Since Rei was the one who assigned Kena's guard duty, there was nothing strange about that.

I do envy the convenience of spirit magic, but that's about it.

(Spirits possess a degree of their own will, so they're highly adaptable. ...Unlike the kind of magic I use.)

That said, precisely because of that, it's known as an extremely difficult magic to master, and those with a natural aptitude for spirit magic are rare.

Furthermore, the fact that they possess a degree of will means that spells contrary to the spirit's intent might not even activate.

Having learned a little about spirit magic from Marina over the winter, Rei opened his mouth, thinking along those lines.

"Anyway, we've finally made it this far. Shall we get started right away?"

No one needed to say what they were starting.

After all, that was precisely why they had brought Gifnan here.

Naturally, to avoid singling Gifnan out, Rei's hand had already left Gifnan's before they entered the room containing this cell.

The moment Gifnan entered this cell... no, the moment he saw Keena inside the cell, he understood why he had been summoned here.

(Why... why are you still alive?!)

Gifnan screamed silently, overcome with intense agitation.

Gifnan knew exactly what kind of person Kena was.

After all, he was the one who had made the request.

He finally grasped completely why he had been brought here.

His gaze toward Rei turned harsh.

Gifnan had come here quietly in the first place because Precious had been taken hostage, at least in part.

And he had been told it wouldn't be a bad thing for Precious either.

Despite that, Keena was actually standing right in front of him now.

If he were captured, it would naturally cause trouble for Precious too.

(I can't do that... but what can I do!?)

Originally, he'd been asked by Precious to hire Keena, but Gifnan had no intention of blaming her for that.

Instead, he was thinking about how to get out of this situation.

(Escaping... is impossible.)

Rei, who had been talking with Marina just moments ago, now stood before the door of this solitary cell.

Gifnan had been working as an adventurer since leaving the orphanage, but even so... no, precisely because of that, he understood the gap in power between himself and Rei.

And considering the killing intent he'd felt at the inn, it was safe to say Rei's true strength had been etched deep into his soul.

(It's not like he's just exceptionally skilled at projecting that killing intent, while his actual strength is nothing special... that couldn't be the case.)

Considering the existence of Set, he briefly thought that might be a possibility. But Gifnan knew Rei's... Crimson Nickname, of course.

He wasn't arrogant enough to think he could defeat someone with such a nickname.

While he was still debating what to do, the situation progressed. Gifnan and the security guards, now in plain clothes, were lined up in front of the cell.

If he caused a disturbance here, it would be certain that his involvement in this incident would be discovered.

Given that, all Gifnan could do was pray that Keena would honor their bond and claim ignorance of him.

Gifnan fixed a pleading gaze on Keena, imploring him to pretend not to know him.

Kena, receiving that look, gave what seemed to Gifnan like a slight nod...

Perhaps it was merely a vision born of Gifnan's desperate wish.

But the truth was, Gifnan needed to cling to that wish right now.

Please do something. Please make it work. Marina spoke up, oblivious to Gifnan's thoughts.

...That said, the reason Rei remained silent here was likely due to his previous aggressive confrontation with Kena.

In truth, fear of Rei was deeply ingrained within Keena. Though she didn't show it on her face, she was under considerable pressure even in this situation.

Yet she was enduring it somehow because if she didn't get through this, she wouldn't be able to join Gilm's intelligence unit and would have to face punishment.

And for Keena, who had operated as an assassin, the punishment she would receive would likely be so severe that even death would be considered lenient.

Considering that, failure here was absolutely not an option.

As Kena swallowed nervously, Marina spoke up.

"Among those six people over there, there's the one who commissioned the assassination of Keena, right?"

"Yes, there is."

"Can you point out who it is?"

"...The second one from the right. That person."

As she said this, Kena pointed with a finger trembling slightly from tension. Naturally, it was Gifnan.

"What!? You bastard!"

The moment his only hope was shattered, Gifnan shouted, his anger fully exposed.

That faint glimmer of hope—it had vanished in an instant.

And it was certain that his mistake would now cause trouble for Precious too.

(Dammit! If only I'd asked someone else to handle that request!)

He thought, but since Gifnan wasn't originally from Gilm, he had no connections.

Naturally, he couldn't rely on the guild either. He could have approached adventurers directly, but if he made a clumsy request and was turned down, there was a risk information would leak from that side.

Slein, his last hope, had already withdrawn after learning Rei was directly involved... worse, he'd led his men out of Gilm.

...Furthermore, the slum district Slain had controlled became a power vacuum after his departure. Now, underground organizations eyeing that vacuum are waging turf wars... but that was none of Rei and the others' concern.

In any case, for these reasons, Gifnan had no one he could trust enough to be sure they wouldn't betray him.

That's precisely why he had no choice but to find Keena, whom Slain had informed him about, and make the request himself... but it backfired completely.

Gifnan, driven by rage, tried to charge toward the cell, but the guards, anticipating this turn of events, restrained him in the next instant.

After all, these were guards stationed in Gilm, a city teeming with adventurers. Subduing an adventurer—especially one with blood boiling in his head—wasn't particularly difficult for them.
