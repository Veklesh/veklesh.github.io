The claws extending from the Black Wolf’s fingers were, just a moment ago—specifically when Rei handed him the steamed bun—ordinary claws that didn’t seem out of place at all.

At the very least, they were certainly not the dagger-like claws he has now.

Since only a few days had passed since he handed the steamed bun to the Black Wolf, it was absolutely impossible for his nails to have grown this long in such a short time.

“Black Wolf, huh. I see!”

Given the situation here, Rei couldn’t very well say he didn’t want to attack the Black Wolf.

He pulled his usual weapons—the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear—from his Misty Ring and readied them.

Fortunately, the Black Wolf had chosen a wide-open space as the battlefield.

If this had been a place like an alleyway, he wouldn’t have been able to use his polearms as freely as he did.

That said, the Black Wolf didn’t choose such an open space as a battlefield out of any consideration for Rei.

It was simply because the Black Wolf’s fighting style made this area easier to maneuver in than a narrow space like an alley.

As always, the Black Wolf vanished from the spot without a word… and in the next instant, he was standing a short distance away from Rei.

(Teleportation? Is he teleporting on his own? No, but... it feels a bit off for teleportation. What on earth was that... was it really teleportation?)

Watching Rei and Black Wolf face off, Elena felt something was off about Black Wolf’s actions.

What would one need to do, and how, to be able to move like that?

Does he possess a magic item, or is it something else?

Rei knows that the Black Wolf’s ability is a skill, but he has never told anyone else about it.

That is likely why Elena is so perplexed by the Black Wolf’s actions.

Still, Rei has told Elena to leave this battle to him and not to interfere, so she doesn’t even reach for her beloved sword, Mirage, at her waist.

Above all, as far as Elena knows, Rei is the strongest man there is.

At least, that is what Elena believes.

Since Rei is the one fighting, all Elena can do is watch over him and believe in his victory.

“Your skills are as troublesome as ever. Teleportation… no, there’s something off about it for a simple teleport. But even so…”

Rei cut himself off and swung the Death Scythe in his right hand.

It was a strike that seemed to slice through not just the air, but space itself.

It possessed such force that even the Black Wolf—who should have the advantage in this situation—stopped moving for an instant, its eyes widening in shock.

“If you get hit by my strike, that’s the moment your defeat is sealed. If you’re willing to take that risk, I’ll accept your challenge.”

It was a remark bordering on a threat.

But, naturally, Black Wolf—renowned as a skilled assassin—wasn’t about to fall for Rei’s threat.

He vanished once more, only to reappear behind Rei the next instant, swinging his claws with the sharpness of daggers.

“I knew you’d do that!”

Shouting sharply, Rei dodged the attack from behind and swung the Death Scythe.

“Multi-Slash!”

It was one of the Death Scythe’s skills—simple yet extremely powerful.

“Huh!?”

The Black Wolf had dodged the Death Scythe’s initial strike, but Multi-Slash is a skill that allows two attacks in a single strike.

Yes, for example, in a situation like this where the Black Wolf’s evasion was by the skin of his teeth, the second strike would close that narrow gap and land a slash on his opponent.

...Even so, Black Wolf, who instinctively sensed the danger of Rei’s Death Scythe and the vicious nature of the Multi-Slash skill, did not sustain a fatal wound from that strike.

He sustained a faint scratch on his face, but that was all.

Of course, the fact that he managed to leave a mark on an assassin known as the Black Wolf means this was no half-hearted attack.

Perhaps even Black Wolf was surprised by the “Multi-Slash” skill, for he vanished from in front of Rei once more and reappeared in a distant location.

(That teleportation… was it teleportation? Anyway, I get that teleportation is a skill, but the fact that he can vanish and reappear without any preparatory movements or anything like that is a real pain.)

If it were a teleportation using a magic item, some sort of action would be required to activate that item.

But the skill the Black Wolf is using doesn’t seem to require any kind of preparatory movement.

In fact, before you know it, the Black Wolf is right behind Rei, so you can’t let your guard down for even a second.

If he were to charge at high speed... well, that wouldn’t be impossible to handle.

After all, Rei possesses senses sharper than those of an ordinary person; he can confirm a figure’s presence through sight or sense the flow of air through touch.

But if he doesn’t move at high speed or anything like that, and instead suddenly appears right behind me, it’s difficult to detect him that way.

(Well, thanks to my sense of touch, I can feel the Black Wolf moving behind me, so I’m able to handle it—it’s not like it’s completely useless.)

Above all, what makes Rei so terrifying to the Black Wolf is the way he wields his killing intent.

Just as the Black Wolf did when it appeared today, one moment it’ll strike Rei with its killing intent with pinpoint accuracy, and the next, as in this battle, it’ll target Rei without giving off any sense of killing intent at all.

That said, for the Black Wolf, Rei’s ability to respond instantly, as if he’s anticipated his every move, is nothing short of a nuisance.

What will Rei do next? The Black Wolf needs to assess that carefully.

If he makes a single misstep here, he’ll instantly take a fatal blow from Rei’s scythe or spear.

After all, he had already sustained a wound—albeit a shallow one—on his cheek from Rei’s attack.

“Well, what do you say? You were the one who got injured first… even if it was only a minor wound. Are you still going to keep this up?”

Rei, who had taken his usual two-spear stance with the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, said this with a provocative smile.

Normally, Rei wouldn’t have wanted to fight the Black Wolf, but since the other party had come to kill him like this, there was no reason for him not to accept the challenge.

No matter what the Black Wolf was thinking, the option of simply letting himself be killed did not exist for Rei.

But if he were asked, “Are you going to go through with this?” and immediately backed down, Black Wolf wouldn’t have gone so far as to attack him in broad daylight like this.

The Black Wolf’s response to Rei’s words was to extend its claws even further—claws that were already as sharp as daggers.

No matter how you looked at it, this didn’t signal that he was backing down; rather, it seemed to declare that he was ready to resume the fight right this very moment.

On top of that, several sharp fangs extended from the Black Wolf’s mouth, as if to flaunt them in Rei’s face.

“I see. So we’re fighting after all. …Well, considering we’re an assassin and the target, this outcome might be inevitable. In that case… from now on, I’ll be going all out, with the intention of killing you.”

If that earlier flurry of strikes had landed cleanly, the Black Wolf would have taken heavy damage—he might even have died.

But even so, Rei hadn’t put any intent to kill into that attack… it was an action taken with the thought that he didn’t have to kill him.

But from now on, it’s different.

He was about to challenge an opponent who had come to take his life—in the truest sense of the word.

Given that, it was only natural that he would strike with the intention of truly taking his opponent’s life.

“I can’t go all out here, but even so… there’s plenty I can do!”

Shouting, Rei pushed off the ground to close the distance with the Black Wolf.

The Black Wolf’s ordinary method of movement, unlike its ability to move in an instant.

But just because it was a normal way of moving didn’t mean his speed was slow—far from it.

In fact, it was difficult to even keep his eyes on Rei as he propelled himself forward by kicking the ground.

That said, there was no doubt that the Black Wolf was a top-tier fighter.

He had clearly seen Rei charging toward him with his own eyes, and had even considered striking back with a sharp claw as a counterattack.

“Do you really think I’d let you?”

Since Rei could see the Black Wolf’s attitude, he could almost intuitively understand what the other was aiming for.

Hand-to-hand combat, where one attacks with their own arms, is, above all, faster than combat with other weapons.

Especially for a top-tier fighter like Black Wolf, that single strike could be described as extremely powerful and swift.

It was an attack that an ordinary person would never be able to dodge.

But Rei was far from ordinary; above all, he had engaged in so many mock battles with Vihera — who also relied primarily on hand-to-hand combat — that it would be ridiculous to even try to count them.

While dodging the Death Scythe’s strike, he awaited the Black Wolf as it attempted to close in on him, ready with the Twilight Spear in his left hand.

Since the distance between them is different, he can’t unleash the weapon’s full power… but it’s still more than enough to blow his opponent away.

“Huh!?”

The Black Wolf deftly avoids the Twilight Spear’s sweeping horizontal strike by diving to the ground.

Of course, he wouldn’t stay on the ground for long; the moment the Twilight Spear passed over his head, the Black Wolf kicked off the ground and lunged forward.

His sharp claws target Rei’s legs.

The lower body is one of the hardest places to defend against an attack, and the Black Wolf was undoubtedly acting with that knowledge in mind.

That is precisely why the Black Wolf targeted that spot.

Though not quite as fast as the black wolf, Rei’s movements are extremely swift.

The wolf’s attack was likely a calculated attempt to disrupt those movements by striking his legs.

But... sensing the Black Wolf’s attack, Rei kicks off the ground almost reflexively and leaps into the air.

Being in the air limits your mobility, and under normal circumstances, it’s not a move you should make carelessly during a fight.

However, that’s only true under normal circumstances; for Rei, who wore the Sleipnir Boots that allowed him to move through the air by kicking, the air was no different from solid ground.

He kicked off the air to create distance from the Black Wolf, and the moment he landed, he drove the tip of his Death Scythe into the ground.

“Terrain Manipulation!”

The current level of terrain manipulation possessed by Death Scythe is four.

She could raise or lower the ground within a 70-meter radius centered on herself by up to 150 centimeters.

If she could manipulate the ground to that extent, it wouldn’t be difficult to instantly sink the ground beneath the black wolf and trap it in a hole.

Of course, since the hole was only 150 cm deep, he couldn’t completely submerge the Black Wolf’s body beneath the ground.

Still, it was enough to create a brief opening—no, a gap lasting several seconds—to halt the Black Wolf’s movement, and Rei, having pulled the tip of Death Scythe from the ground, ran toward the hole where the Black Wolf stood.

Sticking out of the ground was the Black Wolf’s neck.

Of course, since its body wasn’t buried, it could easily move if it wanted to.

But perhaps the sudden sinking of the ground was completely unexpected even for the Black Wolf; even upon seeing Rei approaching, it couldn’t react immediately. It couldn’t react… yet, in the very next instant, the Black Wolf had vanished from the hole.

Seeing this, Rei stopped in his tracks and fixed his gaze sharply to the right and slightly ahead.

There, the black wolf was staring intently at Rei with a wary gaze.

That attack must have been completely unexpected for the Black Wolf as well.

It hadn’t expected Rei to be capable of such a move, and now it fixed its gaze on him, seemingly waiting for an opening.

“What’s the matter? Even the famed assassin Black Wolf finds himself at a disadvantage when fighting head-on? To be honest, I don’t know what you were thinking when you challenged me to a head-on fight.”

Rei’s words were half provocation and half genuine curiosity.

Normally, assassins don’t boldly challenge their opponents to a fight like Black Wolf did.

Typically, assassins would poison someone’s food in their daily life… or strike at vital points as they pass by, or raid their sleeping quarters.

Of course, with Rei’s abilities, all of those tactics would be difficult.

But that just meant it was difficult—it wasn’t absolutely impossible.

At least from Rei’s perspective, he felt his chances of winning were higher than if the Black Wolf had challenged him head-on like this.

Yet, the Black Wolf didn’t resort to such tactics; he came boldly, head-on, to defeat Rei… no, to kill him.

If someone were unaware of Rei’s true strength and had excessive confidence in their own power, such behavior wouldn’t be surprising—but the one facing Rei is Black Wolf.

Even if he wasn’t going all out, they should have been well aware of each other’s abilities after their brief skirmish.

And yet, why had he chosen to challenge him head-on like this? Even Rei couldn’t figure that out.
