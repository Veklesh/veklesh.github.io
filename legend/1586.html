It took less than ten minutes from the moment Rei began fighting until every soldier inside the cave, and every soldier waiting outside who hadn't managed to enter, was wiped out.

Some had sensed the danger and fled, but Rei saw no particular need to pursue them and let them go.

Many of the soldiers were dead, but some had lost a limb and were no longer able to fight.

Others lay unconscious, knocked out by attacks not from Rei, but from Byune.

The kingdom's objective—to slaughter everyone in this cave and erase evidence of its own atrocities—had been reduced to ashes.

"...So, even if we deal with the Jaya people still in this cave now, the kingdom must have known about the giants, right? What did they plan to do about that many giants?"

Rei asked one of the soldiers.

This soldier was the one who had immediately recognized Rei's identity when he appeared on the battlefield.

Rei had thought he seemed to know a lot, and when struck by Death Scythe, he'd only been knocked unconscious by the butt of the blade, not the edge itself—a fortunate soldier.

Though calling it luck that Rei considered him a potential source of information was another matter entirely.

"W-Well..."

Trying desperately to put distance between himself and Rei, the soldier attempted to back away while still seated.

Whether from being brought out of the cave or the summer air and tension, sweat beaded on the soldier's face.

Desperately trying to distance himself from Rei, the only thing keeping the soldier conscious was the cold feel of earth on his palms.

If not for the existence of the earth beneath him, or the pain of the small stones, the soldier might well have gone mad from the sheer strangeness of Rei's presence.

Being well-informed, this soldier knew about Rei. ...Yes, he merely knew about him.

For instance, he knew the information that Rei had single-handedly annihilated an entire army.

But what that actually entailed... he only truly understood when he saw it with his own eyes.

Moreover, even facing hundreds of soldiers, Rei showed no sign of even being out of breath.

Of course, not every soldier who entered this forest was killed by Rei.

Before Rei and the others arrived, Ryutas and his guards had fought valiantly, taking down a significant number of soldiers. Byune had also defeated many using weapons like the White Cloud and the Long Needle.

Some soldiers, realizing they couldn't defeat Rei and his group, even fled.

Even so... it was an undeniable fact that many soldiers had fallen by Rei's hand.

This soldier, deliberately spared for information, had some confidence in his own abilities.

But that confidence was only slight, and when faced with a true power like Rei's, all he could do was cower like this.

He had believed himself a predator, one who hunted, but seeing Rei's strength up close made him realize that was nothing but arrogance.

As if to reassure the soldier, Rei spoke.

"You're an important source of information. I have no intention of killing you, so relax."

Rei tried to calm the soldier to extract information, but the soldier's expression twisted upon hearing this.

To the soldier, being an important source meant he wouldn't be killed... which meant they'd get the information, and once he was no longer useful, he'd be killed. That's how he interpreted it.

Under normal circumstances, he wouldn't have read that much into it.

But the soldier had seen Rei fight up close.

That made any action Rei took seem entirely plausible.

"H-help me... I'll tell you everything I know. Please, just spare my life!"

The soldier screamed, desperately clinging to hope for Rei's mercy.

The soldier's body was covered in his comrades' blood, many parts stained red.

Yet, there were no visible wounds on his face, hands, or limbs.

"Ah... it's fine. I won't kill you. I promise. So, why don't you just calmly spill the information?"

Rei thought deeply, glad he had stored the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear in the Misty Ring.

He could easily imagine that if he had weapons in his current state, the soldier before him would be far from able to speak.

...Though for the soldier, it wasn't about weapons at all. The mere presence of Rei was enough to strike terror into him.

"Alright, alright, Rei, step back for now. Look, he's scared out of his wits. We won't get any information out of him like this."

With those words, Rei was forcibly pushed back.

And stepping in front of the soldier in Rei's place was Ryutas.

Before Rei arrived, the situation had been nothing short of a life-or-death crisis, and he'd been gasping for breath from the unfamiliar combat... but now, his breathing had already steadied.

Though Ryutas was stronger than the soldiers, actual combat... especially facing hundreds with only a few men... must have placed an immense burden on him.

The battle had ended, and now, thinking he should try to extract information from the soldiers in Rei's place, he acted on that thought.

His actions stemmed from the awareness that he had been nearly useless in the fight.

"Rest assured. Despite his appearance, Rei won't draw his blade against those who are friendly towards him."

As usual... no, perhaps with an added intention to calm the soldiers, Ryutas offered a softer smile.

Putting on a mask, wearing a facade... For a man like Ryutas, it was something he could do with ease.

No, rather than the combat skills displayed earlier, this was likely Ryutas's true specialty.

Indeed, seeing that compassionate smile meant to calm his opponent, the soldier visibly relaxed considerably.

"Ah, ah. Yeah. Got it."

Seeing the soldier, who had been half-confused, now speaking clearly, Rei couldn't help but be genuinely impressed.

(Impressive.)

Watching Ryutas effortlessly accomplish something he himself could never do, Rei found himself looking at his own hands.

If asked whether he could do something like what Ryutas did right now, the answer was definitely no.

Of course, against an opponent who hadn't seen Rei's fighting up close, he could probably pull off something similar to Ryutas.

Rei's small frame and face that could easily be called feminine were highly effective at not raising suspicion.

...Though, if the opponent could sense his true nature beneath that appearance, based on his personality, that would be a different story.

"Now, calm down and tell me. Ah, you can relax. As Rei said earlier, once we're done with business, you'll be released."

"R-Really? You'll truly let me go? I can assume you won't kill me or do something like that just because I'm no longer useful!?"

The soldier's shout echoed through the forest.

Amidst the rustling of leaves in the wind, the cries of birds and animals... Rei stared at the soldier with an expression of utter disbelief, as if the admiration he'd felt mere seconds ago had vanished.

They had come to this cave to kill their target, yet now that they were being fought back and were the ones facing death, they were flailing around in a pathetic struggle.

To Rei's eyes, such actions could only be described as pathetic.

Then again, as soldiers, it was only natural they obeyed orders from above.

He told himself that, if he thought about it that way, he could almost accept it. He listened to the conversation between Ryutas and the soldier.

...As if to comfort Rei, Yellow, who had been in Byune's arms, flew up and landed on Rei's right shoulder.

Yellow's thoughtfulness? He considered it for a moment, but now, information was more important. He looked toward the soldier.

"Our orders were clear: everyone in this forest is a criminal. Kill them all without question. And make sure not a single one escapes."

"Hmm. They're being quite thorough, aren't they?"

Rei agreed with Ryutas's mutter.

(Kill everyone means they want all of Jaya's people dead too, right? But Jaya was an organization with ties to the upper echelons of the Reburina Kingdom, at least half of them. This order means... the higher-ups decided to cut Jaya loose after all?)

Since Ryutas's escort had requested reinforcements, and they'd come straight here only to immediately engage in combat, Rei had heard about the national army's attack and had expected it.

But that was merely an expectation... This time, he'd actually heard it from a soldier's own mouth.

"Inside this cave, there's something you might call Jaya's secret weapon. Did you hear anything about that?"

"Secret weapon? No, unfortunately, I haven't heard anything. Someone higher up might know about that sort of thing..."

His words trailing off, the soldier scanned the surroundings.

Spread out before him were corpses, corpses, corpses.

If they didn't clear the bodies quickly, with summer coming, decay would undoubtedly begin.

But... the biggest problem was who would clean up these bodies.

While a few had managed to escape, they were few and far between.

Moreover, there were quite a few who were seriously wounded but still alive.

Even so, the number of corpses was in the hundreds.

It was far beyond anything that could be handled casually.

Before the giant went berserk, we might have been able to make it clean up the mess.

But now that the Black Crystal has been destroyed, such a thing is impossible.

No, it was practically a certainty that the giants themselves would be wiped out entirely by Rei and the others.

That meant someone else would have to deal with the bodies.

(The best solution would be to have the country that caused this whole mess, Reburina, handle it somehow.)

Telling the severely damaged Kingdom of Reburina to clean up the mess would be nothing but humiliation for the victimized nation.

But there are circumstances that make it necessary.

This forest is not far from places like Mejogo and Rossi.

Leaving corpses in such places would make them highly likely to become undead.

A country where undead roam right beside its capital.

If neighboring nations were to learn of this, it would be a tremendous disgrace.

Given the already high-minded nature of the upper echelons of the Kingdom of Reburina, it was highly unlikely they would tolerate such a thing.

"So the sole purpose was to slaughter everyone in this forest?"

"That's what it seems like."

"...Have you heard anything about the cave?"

After a moment's thought, Ryutas asked the soldier.

If the plan was to kill everyone inside the cave, it would naturally require sending men into the cave itself, to the very place where the giants were waiting.

That would inevitably lead to an encounter with the giants.

Considering the soldiers' strength, the disparity in combat power against the giants was undoubtedly overwhelming.

Moreover, while the soldiers numbered in the hundreds, the giants exceeded a thousand.

What would happen if those two forces clashed? It was obvious.

"No, nothing. As I said before, only my superiors know the details..."

Once more, the soldier's gaze fell to the ground... to a corpse lying a short distance away.

That corpse, clad in slightly... just slightly better equipment than the other soldiers, was probably the superior officer the soldier mentioned.

Given that his head had been severed, it was impossible to imagine he was still alive.

"Ah... Sorry about that. Should've kept him alive a bit longer."

"H-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-h-

When Rei spoke, the soldier replied timidly, saying only that.

For the soldier, Rei's very existence had become a complete trauma.

...It was only natural. After all, among the soldiers of the Kingdom of Reburina, aside from training, those who had experienced actual combat were surprisingly few.

Such inexperienced soldiers had witnessed Rei fight—a man who knew real battlefields and had survived countless conflicts. And they'd seen it from the side being trampled.

To say this wouldn't cause trauma would be impossible.

"Rei, I'll hear your story."

For the sake of that soldier, Ryutas had Rei step back.

Partly to avoid disrupting intelligence gathering, but also to distance Rei and make the soldier more receptive to him.

Though Rei didn't sense anything specific in Ryutas' actions, he still kept his distance from the soldier, assuming Ryutas had some plan in mind.
