“...You’ve got to be kidding me...”

Rei looked at the steamed bun in his hand and let out a stunned gasp.

Its appearance hadn’t changed all that much.

However, thanks to advice from Edvard’s companions who were well-versed in cooking, the taste of the meat bun had improved significantly.

You could say it went from about a 50 to a 70.

But what really surprised Rei came after that.

About ten days after the mock battle, Rei happened to run into Edward in town. Edward told him the meat buns had been improved even further and asked if he’d like to try one, so Rei nodded. But the place he was led to wasn’t near the training ground like before… but rather a spot on the main street with a fairly good location.

The sight of so many customers buying meat buns was a surprise to Rei.

But what surprised Rei even more was that after he ate the meat bun he was given… Edward then told him that the filling contained goblin meat.

Goblin meat, as everyone knows, is so foul-smelling it’s practically inedible—the sort of thing people eat only to survive, enduring the taste so they don’t starve to death when they’re penniless.

How could a meat bun containing such goblin meat taste this good?

If the meat bun had been rated around 70 points based on advice from Eduardo’s companions, this one—made with goblin meat—had risen to about 80 points.

Of course, in terms of the magnitude of the improvement, going from 50 to 70 points covers a wider range.

But if asked which is harder—raising it from 50 to 70 points, or from 70 to 80 points—with a few exceptions, most would likely judge the latter to be more difficult.

And the main reason they managed to pull off this difficult feat, he said, was the goblin meat.

When Edwar told Rei this, it was impossible for him not to be surprised.

“Hahaha, I suppose you’re surprised. It is goblin meat, after all. But believe it or not, it’s actually surprisingly edible. …Though, apparently, it can’t be used in just any dish.”

“Still, it’s goblin meat! How on earth did you manage to pull that off?”

“Actually, I have an acquaintance among the people training there who’s been researching ways to make goblin meat edible.”

“……Hm?”

At Edvard’s words, Rei looked up from his meat bun as if he’d suddenly realized something and asked.

“Could that be Mayo from Percy’s Tool Shop?”

“Oh? You know him?”

Eduardo, wearing a look of considerable surprise, asked Rei.

At those words, Rei nodded.

There was no way he wouldn’t know.

After all, it was Rei who originally approached Mayo with the idea of researching whether goblin meat could be made edible.

That said, he’d left the research itself to Mayo; Rei’s role was limited to delivering any seasonings or ingredients that might be useful to her, and above all, he’d been extremely busy lately.

Between the expansion of Gilm, the matter with the Kingdom of Reburina, escorting the people moving to Gilm and restocking supplies, and clearing the newly opened dungeon, there was so much to do that he had almost no time to stop by Mayo’s place.

……Well, even so, he had been doing things like fishing at the sea just to clear his head.

“Ah, he’s an acquaintance of mine. I haven’t seen him around lately, but I guess he’s gotten to the point where he can actually enjoy eating goblin meat. …That’s impressive.”

Rei didn’t mention that he’d actually been the one to request that Mayo make goblin meat edible.

After all, Rei had merely made the request; it was Mayo who had actually conducted the research and made it possible to eat goblin meat.

Still, if it’s true that Mayo made goblin meat edible, there’s no doubt that’s a huge boon for Gilm’s current situation.

Some had come to Gilm seeking work on the expansion project, while others, like Sura, had arrived leading groups of nearly a thousand people.

Under these circumstances, it was certain that more food would be needed than in any previous year.

In that situation, if they could actually enjoy eating goblin meat… it wouldn’t completely solve the food problem, but it would certainly make things a lot easier.

(Yeah, that’s true. I don’t have anything in particular to do today, which is rare, so maybe I’ll stop by Percy’s Tool Shop. I’m also curious about how they managed to make goblin meat edible.)

As he ate the meat bun, satisfied that it had been prepared—as he’d requested—not just as ground meat but cut into larger pieces so he could enjoy the texture of the meat—which, after all, was goblin meat—Rei came to that conclusion.

“May I consider Rei’s request successfully completed?”

“Yeah, I have no complaints. Actually, it’s better than I expected.”

At Rei’s words of praise, Edwald smiled happily.

Edwald, too, probably never imagined that the food stall selling those meat buns—which were neither particularly good nor bad—would become such a hit.

In any case, since Rei had asked him to go out of his way to hold a mock battle, it was only natural that he felt relieved to have properly fulfilled his part of the bargain.

From Edvard’s perspective, he felt a deep sense of gratitude toward Rei for agreeing to the mock battle under such conditions.

Given that, he felt it was only natural to make the food stall this successful as a way of repaying that debt.

……In fact, if word got out that Rei would agree to a mock battle just for making the food stall somewhat successful, it wouldn’t be surprising if many who heard that came to challenge him.

“If those are the conditions, then I’ll do it too,” they might think.

In reality, it was a series of coincidences piling up… Rei happened to find a stall selling meat buns, it happened to be right in front of the training ground, Shuval happened to be training there when Rei was present and came to buy a meat bun, Shuval happened to have deep knowledge of cooking, and Shuval happened to be hungry for strength and came up with the idea of a sparring match with Rei.

It was only after all these coincidences piled up that this sparring match finally came to be.

Normally, such a series of coincidences wouldn’t happen.

That is precisely why Edvard was able to raise the bar when others, like himself, challenged Rei to a sparring match—not as a request, but as a plea… In other words, he was glad he managed to avoid causing Rei any trouble.

After talking with Edward for about ten minutes, Rei heads to Percy’s Tool Shop.

Incidentally, Set was nowhere to be seen by Rei’s side today either.

Well, at first, Set had been strolling through town with Rei, but along the way, he got caught up with some children, and since Johanna had even shown up, Set decided to play with them today, so he was currently alone.

Rei was on his way to Percy’s Tool Shop, snacking at a few food stalls along the way, when he spotted a familiar storefront… and at the same time, tilted his head in confusion upon seeing several people gathered in front of it.

Percy’s Equipment Shop is fairly well-known as a reputable store.

Since it sells high-quality equipment at reasonable prices, it is highly regarded by adventurers, and sometimes even traveling merchants come to this shop to stock up on supplies.

So, it wasn’t unusual for the peddlers still remaining in Gilm to visit the shop in search of goods, nor was it strange to see adventurers there.

But… it was a surprise even to Rei to see a group of people gathered there who clearly weren’t either of those—they looked like ordinary folk.

“Um… how did this happen? It doesn’t seem like the shop is just doing well…”

Though Rei had such doubts, he decided there was no point in staying there any longer and stepped inside the shop.

“Welcome! I’m sorry, but right now… Rei!?”

The clerk had initially thought the newcomer was a customer and was about to apologize for keeping them waiting, but upon realizing it was Rei, they was taken aback and spoke hurriedly.

"Rei, this way, please."

Saying that, she practically dragged Rei to the back of the shop.

The other customers watched this with envious glances, but even though he was wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, they recognized him as Rei and began to wonder, “Could it be…?”

Almost everyone here was involved in the culinary trade or the food business.

For that very reason, those who noticed Rei began to wonder if he was involved in the goblin meat incident as well... and, in fact, they were right.

While several customers were thinking along those lines, Rei was in a room at the back of the shop.

“Ah, Rei. You’re here quite early.”

The one who called out to Rei was a massive man, nearly 180 cm tall and clearly weighing well over 100 kg.

“Mayo, looks like you’ve gotten pretty busy.”

“...Honestly, I have absolutely no idea how word about that food stall got out.”

Mayo shook his head in exasperation, but since even Edvard knew about the goblin meat, it was certain that the information had leaked from somewhere.

“I guess it was that shocking to find out goblin meat could be so delicious. …So? How did you do it?”

Normally, he wouldn’t answer if asked about the secret to his money-making scheme.

But in Rei’s case, he was treated as Mayo’s research partner—albeit mostly in name only.

In fact, to make goblin meat edible, he’s even gone so far as to arrange for expensive spices from Gilm, so it’s not as if he hasn’t contributed to the research.

“Well, it’s not really that complicated. And to be honest, there are limits on when and how much of it can be used.”

“So, it’s not a fundamental solution?”

“Yes. It’s a method that uses a few of Gamelion’s internal organs to process the goblin meat. Since those parts aren’t particularly rare, they can be obtained at a reasonably low price. …At least, for now.”

“Ah… So, that means having Gamelion innards is a prerequisite.”

"That’s right."

Basically, internal organs spoil very easily.

Given that, even in Gilm as winter approaches, it wouldn’t be possible to store them for very long.

...Naturally, they’ll rot over time...

"So that’s why the clerk, upon learning I had arrived, immediately ushered me in here."

That’s right; time doesn’t pass inside Rei’s Misty Ring.

That’s why even the meat he defeated several years ago remains fresh and ready to eat.

“That’s right. To be honest, I was planning to call Rei once our research results were a bit more solid. Whoever summoned Rei here must have known the situation.”

“I see. …But, sorry, I’m pretty busy right now. I’ll be leaving Gilm in a little while… and I probably won’t be back until sometime after the new year. Is that okay with you?”

After hearing Rei’s words, Mayo paused to think for a moment before speaking.

“If you ask if it’s a problem, it is a bit of a problem… but if you can come back during the winter, I think I can manage most things. However, could you take about half of what I’m currently collecting? I’m planning to have the cooks experiment with the rest later.”

“Giving the goblin meat to the man running the steamed bun stall was also part of that research,” Mayo explained.

“So, you’re currently experimenting with different ways to cook goblin meat to make it taste good?”

“Yes. We’ve tried cooking it in various ways here too, but sometimes the results are absolutely terrible, while other times they turn out reasonably tasty. We don’t know exactly why that happens.”

“...So, the people inside the shop and gathered around it are those kinds of folks, then.”

“That seems to be the case. We didn’t call them over ourselves, but it looks like the information leaked out somehow. …Being chefs, they all seem to be really interested in goblin meat.”

To be precise, it’s not just ordinary goblin meat, but goblin meat that’s been properly prepared for eating.

And if Gamelion’s innards are required, the places where it can be eaten are inevitably limited.

That delicious goblin meat made such a strong impression that it was bound to become Gilm’s new specialty… so it was only natural that many chefs would want to try their hand at it.
