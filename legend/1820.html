Someone’s watching me.

At those words from Rei, Brewitt narrowed his eyes and scanned his surroundings.

……Brewitt rarely wore such a stern expression, but the look on his face now was the sharp glare he reserved for enemies.

In fact, looking at Brewitt right now, one might think a child would start crying.

But it’s no wonder Brewitt looks that way.

Although Brewitt had always been confident in his own abilities, he hadn’t realized he was being watched until Rei pointed it out.

If he had been attacked by the owner of that gaze in the current situation, there’s a high chance he wouldn’t have been able to defend himself.

That’s exactly why Brewitt’s gaze as he scans his surroundings has grown so sharp.

“Don’t worry. I felt this gaze before the mock battle, too. In other words, whoever’s watching isn’t interested in you—they’re interested in me.”

Even as he said this, Rei furrowed his brow slightly at the eerie gaze, which, as always, gave off absolutely no sense of hostility.

If this gaze had conveyed a sense of killing intent or something similar, Rei would have understood that this was an enemy of that sort.

But the gaze currently fixed on him contained none of those emotions.

It felt as though he was simply being looked at—nothing more.

“...Are you okay?”

Although Brewitt felt a slight sense of relief upon learning that this person was not an enemy, he couldn’t simply rejoice once he realized that gaze was directed at Rei.

He knows Rei is stronger than him, but if asked whether he could do anything against an opponent who hasn’t even noticed his gaze, he wouldn’t know.

(Since both Rei and Set noticed a gaze that I didn’t, I guess that means, at worst, we’re on equal footing…)

Brewitt also had considerable confidence in his own strength.

After all, as a noble who has fought on the front lines of battle, he has survived countless battles, and it’s not uncommon for him to get into fights in town, just like when he first met Rei.

He was certainly stronger than a man who had just become an adventurer, and he believed he had a chance of winning even against veteran adventurers—and that he wouldn’t lose so easily even if he fought an adventurer considered top-tier.

If there was a gaze he hadn’t noticed, then the person directing it was undoubtedly stronger than him.

“I’d like to think I’ll be fine for now. I don’t know exactly who’s behind that gaze, but I’m not planning on losing to just anyone either.”

Rei said this casually, but if he really did face a hopelessly overpowered opponent, he always had the option of hopping on Set and fleeing.

Or, there was always the option of riding Set and launching a one-sided attack from the sky.

(As long as Set and I are together, there’s practically no way we’d lose, no matter who’s behind that gaze.)

Thinking that, Rei savored the taste of the remaining soup.

“Gurrr!”

Unlike Rei, Set had already finished his soup and was relaxed enough to ask for seconds.

That said, even though Rei understood Set’s request, he didn’t let him have a second helping.

“We’re going to visit other food stalls later, so just leave it at that. You probably won’t get completely full, though.”

Rei was quite the eater himself, but since Set was physically much larger to begin with, he naturally ate even more than Rei.

At the very least, he almost never reached the point where he was so full he couldn’t eat any more.

Rei understood that, but if they stayed in front of the soup stall for much longer, they were sure to attract a lot of stares from the people around them.

“Growl…”

Set let out a disappointed growl at Rei’s words.

Just then, Brewitt finished his soup.

“So, what do you say? Which stall should we go to next?”

What came out of Brewitt’s mouth was something even Rei hadn’t expected.

Ray had been certain that Blueit would split off from him right here.

“Are you sure? There’s a chance that person watching us might set their sights on you too.”

“Ah, I don’t mind. They’ve probably already seen us together anyway. Given that, it would be more dangerous to split up from Rei here.”

“I can’t argue with that.”

While nodding at Brewitt’s words, Rei’s prediction was that the owner of that gaze probably wouldn’t go so far as to attack Brewitt.

(If they are targeting someone, is it to use me as a hostage to lure me out? No, but the one targeting me is probably Gaiska. If that’s the case, attacking a noble… seems like something they’d do.)

After all, he had spoken to Ara, the third daughter of the Scarley family, with a condescending gaze.

That said, the target this time isn’t Gaiska, but one of Gaiska’s subordinates… or perhaps someone Gaiska hired.

Given that, Rei concluded that the chances of an unrelated Brewitt being targeted were slim.

That’s precisely why he worried that if she continued to stay with him, she might get caught up in the actions of the person watching them.

“Come on, let’s go, Rei. And Set, too. I can smell something delicious coming from that food stall over there, so that’s where we’re headed next!”

Saying that, Brewitt tugged on Rei and started walking toward the food stall where the appetizing aroma was wafting from.

Rei tried to say something to Brewitt, but seeing the smile on his face, he somehow felt it would be silly to say anything.

“I get it. I get it, so just wait a second. We need to return the soup bowls first, right?”

Rei had more than enough money to cover the difference in the soup’s price—enough to simply ignore it.

Especially considering the money he’d received from Miranda, he had more than enough to buy the entire pot of soup being sold at the stall, contents and all.

However, since many people were looking forward to the soup, he had no intention of doing something like that.

Restraining Brewitt, who was eager to head to the next stall, he had the cost of the bowl refunded and headed to another stall.

Over by Brewitt, even Set was growling, eager to head to the next stall as soon as possible.

And so, as if being pulled along by one person and one dog, Rei headed toward the next food stall.

The fragrant, appetizing smell catches Rei’s attention.

(Fish? That’s unusual.)

The fish on display at the stall was one Rei had never seen before.

It looked somewhat like a carp, but the details were quite different.

The stall owner skillfully filleted the fish with a knife, wrapped it in a plant leaf along with some seasoning, and then grilled it directly over charcoal.

Although the cooking method was that simple, an appetizing aroma wafted through the air.

(I wonder what it is. I can’t really describe the exact scent, but there’s no doubt that just smelling it makes me hungry.)

However, despite that appetizing aroma, there were few customers lined up at the stall.

It’s not that there were none, but compared to the soup stall from earlier, the line was clearly much shorter.

“It looks so delicious, so why are there so few customers?”

“Hm? Oh, you don’t know, Rei? That fish is called Jorf, and it’s actually quite a high-end fish. The leaves wrapping the flesh are also from a herb that’s very difficult to harvest this time of year. Since they use those, the price per serving is bound to be high.”

“Jorf… I’ve never heard of that fish.”

"Well, I suppose so. They say they don't live in many places, and between spring and summer they hibernate... that's a weird way to put it, but anyway, they basically only move around in the fall and winter. It's a strange fish."

“...That’s just plain weird.”

As far as Rei knew, most fish were active in the spring and summer.

Well, there are plenty of fish that are in season in the fall and winter, so you can’t really generalize like that.

Especially fish like sand lance, which Rei loves, are truly at their best in winter.

But... even those kinds of fish don’t hibernate in spring or summer.

At least, as far as Rei knew, there shouldn’t be any fish like that.

And since they look so much like carp, it feels even more out of place.

“Right? That’s why the catch is so small. They only live in clean, fairly deep rivers. That’s why they’re more expensive than meat and such. …Though, as you can see from the line here, it’s not like they’re priced out of reach.”

Intrigued by those words, Rei joined Set at the back of the line.

Naturally, Rei and Set stood out there as well.

In particular, the man and woman standing in front of Rei were actually quite frightened by the fact that Set—a griffin—was behind them.

For a moment, Rei considered saying something to them, but whatever he said wouldn’t make them relax immediately, and above all, his only connection to the man and woman in front of him was that they were standing in line together.

Given that, he decided it would be better to keep quiet for now rather than say something that might make them even more nervous, so Rei simply continued his conversation with Brewitt.

“So, since you’re here like this, Rei, that means you’re going to attend the New Year’s party too, right?”

“Yeah. I even had my measurements taken and a suit made just for that. …Though I’m pretty sure there’ll be a commotion if I go to the party.”

There’s the matter with Gaiska, but that’s not the only problem.

Rei could easily imagine that there were many other nobles among the Noble Faction who didn’t take kindly to him.

“Just so you know, it’s not just the Noble Faction that finds you an eyesore.”

“What?”

“Think about it. Who do you think would be annoyed to see Rei, a member of the Neutral Faction, getting along with the Noble Faction?”

“……The Royal Faction, maybe?”

“That’s right. And there are nobles outside the three major factions, too.”

Hearing this, Rei looked surprised.

Every noble he had met so far had belonged to one of the three major factions.

There might have been others, but Rei hadn’t realized it.

“Now that you mention it, that’s true. So there are nobles besides those belonging to the three major factions?”

“Of course. …Oh, but look—it’s our turn.”

While they were talking about nobles, the line moved forward, and soon it was Rei and his friends’ turn.

Just as Brewitt had said, Rei was charged a slightly higher price than at the other stalls, but since he had a large amount of money he’d won from gambling, he bought about thirty of them in one go.

He didn’t try to buy them all up because there were still people in line behind Rei and his group.

He probably figured that if he tried to buy them all up right there, the people waiting in line would likely—no, almost certainly—get upset.

After buying a large quantity, they left the area.

Unlike at the soup stall, there was no need to return the dishes here.

So, Rei and the others decided to enjoy their meal a short distance away from the stall.

Of course, as long as there were people like Set and Brewitt—who stood out no matter what—they couldn’t really escape people’s gazes even if they moved a little further away.

Still, feeling relieved that the number of stares had decreased, even if only slightly, Rei gave some food to Set and then immediately brought the food she was holding to her own mouth.

First, a refreshing aroma reminiscent of green shiso fills his mouth.

Next, the flavor of Jorf’s tender, fatty white meat delighted Rei’s tongue.

This was likely done to fully let the flavor of the Jorf shine through. The seasoning mixed in with the Jorf and the herb—which smelled like green shiso—didn’t overpower the dish.

As he savored the dish designed to highlight the flavor of the Jorf, Rei handed Set a few more pieces as a second helping after he had finished his first bite… and continued to enjoy the dish.

“Jorf is a river fish, right? For it to be this fatty and not have any muddy taste… that’s amazing.”

“Yeah. They probably cleaned it really well to get rid of the mud. …Why are you so surprised?”

Perhaps taken aback by the surprised look Rei gave him the moment he mentioned “de-silting,” he returned Rei’s gaze with a slightly displeased expression.

“Well, I just didn’t expect a noble like Brewitt to know about things like de-silting.”

In Rei’s case, when he lived in Japan, he sometimes caught fish himself in rivers and marshes, and his father would catch some, or acquaintances would share what they’d caught with him.

That’s why he knew about the process of “mud-cleaning”… but he was probably surprised that a noble like Brewitt would know about such a thing.

As Rei said this, slightly evading the topic, Brewitt flashed a smug smile.

“I’m out and about in town all the time. Of course I know common sense like that.”

“...I don’t think that’s something the heir to a noble house should be doing.”

Even as he said this, Rei smiled and joined Set and Brewitt in savoring Jorf’s cooking.
