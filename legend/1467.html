Several days had passed since the day they decided to escort the woodcutters through Treant Forest.

The appointed day had finally arrived, and Rei had come to the guild.

...Though it was already past ten o'clock, and most were busy with their own tasks.

Naturally, the woodcutters and the adventurers escorting them had already set out for Treant Forest.

As for why Rei alone arrived at this hour, it was because he had various things to prepare beforehand.

Transporting building materials for a new wall, delivering the monster slain last night to the guild, and so on.

That was precisely why Rei alone had arrived here at this hour.

"Well then, Rei. I'm counting on you."

"...Please be careful, Rei-san."

Not only Kenny, but Lenora also wished Rei well.

While Lenora was talking with Walker, the decision regarding this matter was finalized.

Of course, if the adventurer truly called Ajas was the kind of person Ilse described, the guild couldn't just sit idly by and leave such an individual unchecked.

That was precisely why this particular case had been approved.

...The guild would have preferred to assign the job to someone other than Rei, if possible.

"Ah. I have no intention of losing to someone like that, so rest assured."

After exchanging a few brief words, Rei left the guild.

Set was already waiting there.

He had been waiting for Rei to arrive, entertaining a few adventurers and passersby.

"Gurrrr!"

Seeing Rei arrive, Set purred happily and stood up.

Seeing Set like this, the people who had been playing with him quietly stood up and returned to their own business.

...Only Johanna remained behind, gazing at Set with a look of reluctance.

"Rei... you're hogging Set all to yourself..."

"Well, what can I say? Set is my familiar, after all. More importantly, is your work okay?"

Johanna, a former member of the Roving Squad, was naturally involved in various tasks related to the expansion of Gilm's facilities alongside her former squadmates who were also part of the party.

Well, this time around, much like Rei and the Crimson Wings, even though they were part of a party, most members were operating independently.

It made sense. Parties were generally formed by gathering individuals with diverse skills.

Warriors, thieves, archers, mages... that sort of thing.

Of course, some parties have overlapping roles, but fundamentally, they're groups of individuals with several skills.

Given that, it only makes sense that everyone would take on the tasks best suited to them during Gilm's expansion.

"Huh? Me? I was planning to go patrol the town soon. There's still a bit of time before we meet up, right? So I was walking around window-shopping when I spotted you, Set-chan."

Johanna muttered resentfully, struggling to hold back the urge to reach out to Set.

If she'd known Set was in front of the guild, she wouldn't have wasted time window-shopping around the surrounding shops and would have come straight here.

(I could have outmaneuvered Milein and gotten to play with Set-chan...)

Filled with deep regret, Johanna's gaze shifted to Rei.

"Even if you look at me like that... I couldn't exactly tell you I was letting Set play, could I?"

"I suppose you're right... Never mind."

Looking disappointed, Johanna walked away from Set.

"Grrr."

Set growled at Johanna, as if saying, "Let's play again next time."

Johanna almost reached out to the round-eyed Set staring at her, but managed to hold back.

...Though, to be honest, it wasn't because she was thinking of Rei; she just didn't want to make Set feel awkward.

"If you get the chance, please play with Set again."

Saying this, Rei straddled Set's back.

Before the expansion work, Gilm couldn't simply take flight from the city like that.

But now, having destroyed the walls, flying through the sky posed no particular problem.

Moreover, it was precisely because Rei and Set could freely enter and exit Gilm without any special procedures that such antics were possible.

However, under normal circumstances, Rei would exit through the main gate.

This time, however, she needed to reach Treant Forest as quickly as possible rather than moving to the main gate.

They had obtained permission for that from Walker and Daskar.

Of course, while Walker was one thing, he hadn't gone to see the busy Daskar directly; Marina had relayed the message to him.

...Given that he held something that could be called a dark past, as long as it wasn't utterly terrible and wouldn't harm Gilm, he was usually accommodated.

"Huh... It's coming along nicely."

A fair amount of time had already passed since the expansion work began.

The old walls had been completely demolished, and not a single piece of rubble remained.

Instead, the ground was being dug up to build new walls, and in some places, walls had already been erected to some extent.

Of course, walls of that thickness couldn't stop monsters from entering, and above all, they couldn't hold a barrier.

But... even so, seeing walls taking shape was incredibly gratifying to Rei, as someone involved in the project.

"Grrr?"

Set asked, glancing back over his shoulder as if to say, "Is it okay to go?" Rei tore his gaze away from the wall and stroked his neck.

Interpreting this as permission to move, Set flapped his wings and headed for Treant Forest.

...Though, at Set's speed, it took only a few minutes after leaving Gilm... perhaps not even a full minute before they arrived at Treant Forest.

The Treant Forest had been heavily logged by woodcutters and adventurers.

That was obvious even from above.

Even from the sky, the sound of woodcutters swinging their axes below could be heard.

Even without Rei saying anything, Set continued his descent toward the ground.

Several adventurers on the ground, keeping watch over their surroundings, must have mistaken the descending Set for an attacking monster.

They frantically tried to raise their weapons... only to be stopped by the other adventurers nearby.

(The ones wary of us are the new guys assigned here today, while the ones stopping them are the veterans who've been in charge here for a while.)

It wasn't certain that every single one of them was like that, but still, if they were the ones who had been entrusted with guarding the woodcutters in this Treant Forest for a while, they should have known that Rei would come from the sky to transport the felled timber.

Some of them, perhaps misunderstanding something, had tried to raise their weapons.

Sending them a slightly exasperated glance, Rei leapt down the moment Set touched down.

Since this was a routine exchange they were already accustomed to, there was no chance of a botched landing.

...One particular woodcutter shot Rei a look tinged with irritation, but the target himself seemed unconcerned, glancing around instead.

The glances were naturally directed at those who still looked unaccustomed to the routine—the men assigned by the guild to guard the woodcutter starting today.

One of them—a man with a snake tattoo on his left hand—glanced at him too, but said nothing.

"Ah, Rei. I heard about you. You're late."

One of the veteran adventurers serving as the woodcutter's escort addressed Rei.

The man, in his thirties, used polite language even with Rei, who looked young enough to be his son.

It was likely an attitude born from knowing Rei's capabilities.

"The guys building the wall had their own business to attend to. ...So, how's it going over here? From what I can see, you haven't made much progress yet."

As expected of adventurers gathered in Gilm, all those assigned to guard Treant Forest possessed a certain level of strength.

Even so, from Rei's perspective, it felt slightly off that they weren't moving as smoothly as those who had been doing this escort duty from the start.

"Ah... Well, part of it is probably that they're still getting used to Treant Forest. Plus, it hasn't been that long since they arrived here."

"Ah."

Hearing that, Rei nodded in agreement.

Unlike Rei, who had arrived here in mere minutes riding Set, the woodcutters and adventurers had come by carriage.

While faster than walking, it was still no match for flying on Set.

And carriages don't even travel at full speed; they move at a leisurely pace.

"Hmm. Well, fine then. ...But, I'll be covering this area today too, though it's not my specialty. Can you assign me a few people?"

"I understand. Security here is different in many ways from the usual. We'll need someone to teach them the ropes."

At the man's words, several of those assigned to Treant Forest starting today wore dissatisfied expressions.

They probably wanted to say they weren't beginners who needed to be taught how to escort.

(Ajas... isn't showing any particular dissatisfaction. He's surprisingly quiet.)

From Ilse's words, he suspected... perhaps, just perhaps, that Ajas was indeed Ilse's enemy.

But even so, it still wasn't one hundred percent certain.

If he's the enemy, then he's the enemy. If not, then he's not. Either way was fine with Rei; his honest desire was to find confirmation through this incident.

(Well, I don't expect to understand everything today, but if I could at least get a sense of what kind of person he is... and if he is the enemy, what he's planning...)

If Ajax is the person Ilse described, Rei couldn't imagine him just quietly doing his job.

Therefore, he suspected they were probably plotting something.

(Leaving that to Egg and the others would probably be the quickest way, but they seem pretty busy themselves.)

With the expansion of Gilm underway, there are countless individuals scheming to either sabotage it or pull strings behind the scenes to reap the benefits.

Countless individuals—organizations, nobles, commoners—were scheming in the shadows.

The Eggs had crushed most of them.

Of course, they couldn't completely crush every single one, which is why exceptions like Count Lerdact emerged.

In any case, thanks to Elena, most of the Noble Faction had withdrawn, but many others were still scheming within Gilm.

The Eggs, dealing with this, were naturally busy.

In fact, it was fortunate that even one of them could be assigned to this particular incident.

...Though to be precise, since Marina requested Daskar, Daskar couldn't refuse.

"Rei, we can't just stand around like this. Shall we begin? I'll be with you."

The man who had been talking to Rei earlier addressed her.

Looking at him again, Rei noticed something odd: despite being an adventurer—a warrior, no less—he was uncharacteristically deferential.

Of course, just because he was an adventurer didn't mean he never encountered arrogant people.

But regardless, assuming he still held the same respect or even fear toward her as when they first met, she quickly spoke up.

"Alright. Then let's get started."

With Rei's words, they each dispersed.

Those who had originally been guarding this Treant Forest were instructed to keep an eye on the area around where the woodcutter was chopping trees.

After confirming there were no enemies, he too picked up his axe and began cutting.

Of course, unlike before, he kept his weapon close at hand while cutting, ready to act immediately if anything happened, so he wasn't solely focused on the logging.

They cut while remaining ready to respond to their surroundings at any moment, so the actual logging inevitably suffered.

Since they weren't professional woodcutters to begin with, their skill naturally lagged behind that of the actual woodcutters.

Given that, it was only natural that no matter how many trees he cut down, his bonus pay would decrease.

"Damn it, I wish a monster would show up."

"Don't say that. We're here as guards in the first place. The guard pay is decent enough, so let's just put up with it."

"Yeah. If we're just keeping watch normally, there aren't that many monsters anyway. If we give up on logging and focus purely on guard duty, the pay's decent and it's actually pretty easy work, right? Try changing your mindset."

"Don't be ridiculous. I need money... I need money to buy Irage-chan a hair ornament!"
