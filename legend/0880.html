Overcast skies covered the heavens, and in the bitter cold where snow could fall at any moment, Rei and his party had left Gilm and were traveling by carriage toward the Demon Forest to hunt Gamelions.

"Quite a few people, huh?"

One of the former rangers murmured, gazing out the window from inside the box wagon.

His gaze fell upon several adventurer parties.

Some parties were small, consisting of only about three people, while others were larger, with nearly ten members.

Then again, Rei's group also had six members: Rei, Rent, Johanna, and the three former rangers.

And with Set included, they were definitely on the larger side in terms of numbers.

...Not only in numbers, but also in combat strength, they were more than sufficient.

Rent, sitting in the back of the carriage, spoke up to the man.

"Well, the reason there were fewer Gamelions this year is because Rei figured out they're in the dungeon."

"Hey, Rent. Just so you know, it's not confirmed yet, okay?"

"Not really. In fact, parties hunting Gamelions over the last few days have reported encounters, and several groups have even defeated them. Given that, it's safe to assume Rei's explanation at the guild was probably correct."

"Grrr!"

Even outside the carriage, Set seemed pleased that Rei had been praised. Walking beside the carriage, he purred happily.

Johanna couldn't stay silent seeing Set like that, and she shot a look that said she wanted to pet him toward Set outside the window.

As usual, everyone inside the box carriage shot Johanna lukewarm glances.

Among them, Rent's gaze toward Johanna was particularly intense, something everyone except Johanna and Rent understood.

"Hey! Could you switch places with me soon!?"

The small sliding door, designed to allow conversation with the driver's seat, opened. One of the former guerrilla fighters, shivering, called out from inside.

Being at the driver's seat controlling the horses, this man was naturally exposed to the cold.

Even with layers of cold-weather gear over his armor, it was a different story when the rest of the crew were huddled inside the box carriage, sheltered from the chill.

The two other men flashed displeased expressions at his words.

Rei was, needless to say, the strongest fighter present, and they were in a position where they relied on his cooperation.

Johanna, who could keep an eye on Set in their current situation, seemed unlikely to take on the role of driver, requiring constant attention to the horses' movements.

Rent was the one who brought up this whole business, and he probably didn't want to be separated from Johanna.

That left only one of the remaining two men to take the driver's seat.

The two men exchanged glances, but eventually one sighed and stood up.

The fact that the man staying in the carriage seemed relieved suggested their exchange had reached some sort of resolution.

"Alright. I'll take over. You come back here."

"Thanks, that's a big help."

After exchanging those words, the man stepped outside through the door leading to the driver's seat.

Watching him go, Rei shot Rent a somewhat exasperated look.

(He didn't have to choose the path of a warrior...)

From what Rei could tell, it was clear Johanna didn't dislike Rent.

But that was merely that she didn't dislike him; it certainly didn't mean she harbored romantic feelings for him.

Even if she did feel affection, it would be the kind reserved for a friend.

Moreover, even if Johanna and Rent somehow ended up together, Johanna's personality—being so fond of Set—meant trouble was guaranteed.

(Ah, but Johanna's family runs a fairly large trading company. So if I got together with her, would that make me marrying up? ...Not that I'm aiming for that sort of thing.)

To say Rent was after Johanna for her money, he was far too intense in the way he looked at her.

How many people, seeing Rent like that, would actually think he was after her money?

Even as these thoughts crossed his mind, the carriage pressed straight on toward the meadow where Gamelion appeared every year.

"There really are a lot of people here, huh?"

"Didn't I mention it in the carriage on the way here? Word about Rei has spread quite a bit, and it seems a lot of people are hunting Gamelions now."

As Rent stepped down from the carriage, he replied to the former guerrilla fighter's mutter.

Even from a distance, within the grasslands where Gamelions were said to appear most frequently each year, several parties could be seen active.

Those heading here besides Rei and his group had been left behind because they were traveling by carriage.

Moreover, even though they had overtaken several parties on the way here, the grasslands were already teeming with groups searching for Gamelion.

To hunt Gamelion, one needed to bring carts or similar vehicles.

Otherwise, you couldn't transport the hunted Gamelions.

In that regard, Rei's party, with Rei able to use the item box, held a significant advantage.

"Alright, for now, I'll go with Set and search for Gamelions from the air. If we find any, we'll come back and let you know, right?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. That'd be a big help."

Rent, who had been looking for an opportunity to speak to Johanna, responded to Rei's words.

Even before arriving at this grassland, Rent had tried countless times to speak to Johanna, but he'd barely managed to find any opportunity.

Well, he had managed to talk to her a few times, so maybe he should count that as a decent effort.

(No matter how you look at it, she's the one at fault, right?)

Johanna's gaze wasn't fixed on Rent, but on Set.

Since Johanna was so engrossed in Set like that, even when Rent tried to speak to her, she often unintentionally tuned him out.

"Grrr?"

"What's up?" she purred, stroking Set's head before speaking.

"Nothing. Now, help me find Gamelion."

"Guru!"

"Leave it to me!" Rei hopped onto Set's back as he purred happily. Ignoring Johanna's envious gaze, Rei gave Set's back a light pat.

"Alright, count on you."

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Responding to Rei's words with a high-pitched bark, he took a few running steps, flapped his wings, and soared into the sky.

Johanna and the others... not just them, but other adventurers too, watched Set's departure.

However, the looks they gave him weren't filled with anticipation. They were looks of disappointment, sadness, envy, jealousy.

Then again, it was only natural.

Only Rei and Set could search for the Gamelion's location from above, giving them an overwhelming advantage.

Moreover, since they were all here to hunt Gamelions, many were naturally eager to gather information about them.

In other words, they knew that Rei had defeated a large number of Gamelions in the dungeon, obtained their corpses, and deposited them with the guild.

Why are you here again, after hunting so many Gamelions?

It's only natural to wonder that.

"Well, it all comes down to strength in the end. In that sense, Rent was smart to bring the offer to me. I wouldn't have agreed to cooperate if it weren't for that cheese."

Feeling the stares directed at him, Rei murmured from atop Set's back.

"Grrr?"

"Nothing. Come on, let's look for some Gamelions."

Petting Set, who tilted his head as if asking, "What's wrong?", Rei's gaze shifted downward.

Despite it already being winter, lush grass grew as if defying the season, forming a vast meadow.

Surveying the lush ground, Rei searched for signs of Gamelions.

Several Gamelions were already engaged in combat with adventurers in some areas, but he couldn't possibly interfere with Gamelions in the middle of a fight. He searched for Gamelions that hadn't yet come into contact with adventurers.

"Looking at it like this, I guess the dungeon really was the reason there were fewer Gamelions this year."

From above, he could see a decent number of Gamelions.

It certainly wasn't a scene where you'd come looking for Gamelions and find almost none.

"If that's the case, then the Gamelion season is about to begin. ...Well, it's certain snow will fall soon, so hunting Gamelion will become difficult due to the cold and such."

"Grr."

As Rei muttered, Set suddenly growled.

Following Set's gaze, Rei noticed a single Gamelion curled up in a grove within the grassland.

Was it sleeping? Was it hiding to avoid being found? Or did it have some other intention?

The reason was unclear, but what mattered was that no one had spotted that Gamelion yet.

(Though, even if it was quite far away, sleeping while so many Gamelions around it were fighting adventurers... Was it just thick-skinned, or did it have that much confidence in its own strength?)

If it was a Gamelion confident in its own strength, Rent and the others might be in danger.

For a moment, Rei hesitated about whether to tell them about that Gamelion, but ultimately, the decision to hunt it was Rent and the others'. Rei's role was to find the Gamelion.

Besides, Rent aside, he wasn't exactly strangers with the others.

If it was truly dangerous, he thought he might help them.

"Well, let's head back. Thanks for telling us where Gamelion is, Set."

"Grrr!"

As Rei stroked his back with words of thanks, Set purred happily and trotted back toward the carriage.

They found Gamelion after ascending and flying just a short distance, so they hadn't flown particularly far.

Once back on the ground, they'd reach the carriage immediately.

"Ah, Rei-san. How did it go? You came back so quickly—you found Gamelion, right?"

Rei nodded at the words of one of the former guerrilla fighters.

Seeing this, the others also let out happy voices.

...Though Johanna seemed desperate to pet Set.

"There's a forest a little way west from here. Inside that forest, there's a Gamelion curled up. I can't tell if it's asleep or not, but from what I saw from above, it didn't look like any other adventurers had found it."

"Then let's go for that one! If it's asleep, we can ambush it. Let's get there before other adventurers find it!"

Johanna, who was essentially the leader of this party, made the decision on which Gamelion to target.

Rei was purely a scout this time, existing outside the party, so he had no intention of questioning Johanna's judgment.

Unless it was clearly the wrong call, but in this case, he had no particular objections.

(Besides, it might just be sleeping... and if that's the case, we do have the advantage.)

Rei nodded and started to head toward the woods... but Rent's voice halted his movement.

"Hold on. If we all go, what about the carriage? Sure, there are other adventurers around, and with Rei here, I doubt any fool would try to steal it. Still, we need someone to watch it, just in case. We can't rule out monsters like goblins showing up."

At those words, everyone realized... and their gazes turned to the carriage they had arrived in.

The carriage the skirmishers had received as payment for suppressing the rebellion wasn't exactly high-performance.

Of course, it wasn't the kind that would break down immediately after riding it, but in terms of quality, it was slightly above average, maybe upper-middle range.

This applied not only to the carriages themselves but also to the horses pulling them.

A horse rigorously trained as a military mount could easily trample goblins underfoot without breaking a sweat.

But the horses pulling Rei and the others' carriage weren't military horses.

If attacked by goblins, it wouldn't be surprising if they bolted.

...Though they did possess enough nerve to grow accustomed to the griffin Set during the journey from the Bestir Empire to Gilm.

Even so, there was still a chance they might bolt off somewhere, carriage and all.

"Right. Well then... Set, can you handle this?"

"Grr!"

At Rei's words, Set growled in his throat, as if to say, Leave it to me!

Seeing this, those watching were convinced the carriage's safety was assured at that moment.

Anyone who dared mess with the carriage while a griffin was present could, in a sense, be called a hero.

...A hero boasting reckless courage, that is.

In any case, leaving the carriage to Set, the others followed Rei's lead as they made their way into the woods.

After walking for about twenty minutes, the destination forest finally came into view.

"That's... where it was."

Looking toward the woods, Rei heard the sounds of battle and shook her head slightly.

"Looks like we're a step too late...!"

Without finishing his sentence, he caught something flying out from within the woods.

He didn't punch it away or dodge it because it had the form of a person.

"...Doesn't seem like we're too late after all."

He muttered this as he set the small, unconscious man—who appeared to be a rat-like beastman—gently down on the ground.
