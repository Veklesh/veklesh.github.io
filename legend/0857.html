“Haaah... Hmm...”

Yawning, Rei sat up in bed.

Glancing around, he remembered he was in the twilight at Komugi-tei and turned his still-drowsy gaze toward the window.

Spread out before him was weather so fine it seemed impossible for autumn.

Not a single cloud marred the sky—a clear autumn day like summer.

It almost felt as if the weather itself was celebrating Rei's return to Gilum.

“...Well, probably just my imagination.”

He murmured softly, and only then did his body seem to fully wake up, his mind clearing.

The Twilight Wheat Inn was the lodging Rei always stayed at in Gilum.

When heading for the Bestia Empire, he'd vacated his room because he didn't know how long he'd be away due to the civil war. But now that he'd returned to Gilum, he'd rented the room again.

The day before his return to Gilum, it had taken longer than expected to separate Mireine from Set. By the time Rei arrived at the Twilight Wheat Inn, dinner service was nearly over.

Even so, the innkeeper, Lana, hadn't forgotten Rei—a valued customer and, in many ways, a memorable figure. She readily agreed when Rei said he wished to stay a while longer.

Fortunately, even in this late autumn season, there were still rooms available.

The Wheat Inn at dusk was one of the finest inns among the many in Gilum.

Naturally, it often hosted large merchant caravans and battle-hardened mercenary companies. Especially until recently, with winter approaching, it had been bustling with guests.

But now that had settled down. It wasn't exactly deserted, but there were still a fair number of rooms available.

That's precisely why Rei could get a room so easily, even arriving on short notice.

Of course, the fact that Rei had stayed at the Twilight Wheat Inn for an extended period before probably helped.

“Today... first, the guild. Not just for Gamellion's request, but I want to say hello to Renola and Kenny too. And if there's any dungeon info, that'd be great. ...Nah, the dungeon takes priority.”

While getting ready, Rei decided on today's schedule and pulled out clothes from Mistyling, starting with the Dragon Robe.

The room Rei was currently staying in was the same one he had used before.

It had only been one day since he arrived, but there was no sign of lived-in comfort within the room.

After all, all his essential belongings were stored in Mistyling. Even if days, weeks, months passed, his luggage might increase slightly, but fundamentally, the room would remain stark and bare.

After getting ready, he headed downstairs to the Wheat Inn for breakfast...

“Hm?”

Rei tilted his head at the sight before him.

Something felt off about the scene he saw upon entering the dining hall.

He stared at the dining hall for a moment... and finally realized what the odd feeling was.

“There are... few people?”

Under normal circumstances, given that this was an inn renowned for its cuisine, there should have been people coming in for breakfast besides the lodging guests.

It wouldn't be overflowing during breakfast time, but it should still be crowded enough that you'd have to look for a seat.

And yet, what lay before Rei's eyes now was a dining hall with about seventy percent of the seats empty.

A vague sense of foreboding washed over him. He pulled his pocket watch from his misty ring and checked the time. It read nearly ten in the morning.

“...Huh?”

He heard his own foolish gasp at how badly he'd overslept, but Rei still stared at the watch again.

Yet, mercilessly, it still showed the same fact: nearly ten in the morning.

“Mr. Rei? Breakfast time is over already... Is this an early lunch?”

The voice behind him sounded familiar.

Turning his gaze, he saw Lana, the innkeeper, holding laundry.

“Ah, no... Yeah. Right, just something light, whatever you have.”

Handing over a few copper coins, Rei took his seat and looked around the dining hall again.

The only people left in the dining hall now were either having an early lunch, as Lana had mentioned, or, like Rei, had overslept... Yet nearly a third of the seats were still occupied. It was likely those who had already finished preparing for winter gathering here to relax.

“I never imagined I'd oversleep by hours.”

Oversleeping by a few dozen minutes... or even an hour, Rei could still understand.

But now it was nearly ten o'clock, meaning he'd overslept by three to four hours.

Oversleeping this much was a bit unusual for Rei.

(Guess I was on edge the whole time I was in the Bestia Empire. Then, coming back to Gilum and ending up sleeping at the Wheat Inn at dusk, I finally relaxed and slept soundly.)

Rei hadn't felt like she was pushing herself that hard. She hadn't been consciously overworking her body; she thought she was just living normally.

...Though, if someone said the rampage he pulled in the Bestia Empire was normal, not many would agree.

Returning to Gilum, which he considered home, had probably made him let his guard down.

(Well, he'd planned to rest his body a bit anyway, and he had other things to do, so oversleeping a little was fine.)

Stretching slightly as he pondered this, Lana approached the table carrying a plate with a sandwich and another with soup.

“Take your time.”

Saying only that, Lana promptly left.

It wasn't that she disliked Rei; she was probably swamped with inn work.

“First things first... I guess it's the guild. Then there's the promise with the Scorching Wind, and I want to see if I can repair that magic sword I got from Noise.”

Muttering to himself, he brought a sandwich to his mouth.

True to their reputation for delicious food, the sandwich was unbelievably tasty for something made in such a short time.

The one Rei particularly liked was the one made with Gamellion meat.

(So Gamellion meat is already circulating after all. ...Maybe I should just take today off and head out tomorrow?)

While munching on a sandwich made with Gamellion meat marinated in sauce and grilled, Rei pondered this. Several others in the cafeteria noticed Rei's expression.

“Hey, wait...isn't that Crimson?”

“Huh? No, but that guy shouldn't be in Gilum right now, right? Then it must be someone else who looks like him? Besides, Crimson's robes are incredibly plain.”

“Huh? But do you think someone wearing such plain robes could afford to stay at this inn? You need serious strength to pay for a room here, you know?”

“Well, they don't necessarily have to be staying here. The food's good, so maybe they're just here for the meals.”

Listening to the voices around him, Rei decided this situation could get messy. He quickly finished his sandwich, savoring it as he went, then downed his vegetable soup. He left the dining hall and headed for the stables.

“...Yeah, I figured he'd be here. Just as I expected.”

The moment Rei entered the stables, he saw Mireine giving a sandwich to Set.

And the sandwich Mireine held looked somehow familiar.

...Of course it did. It was the same one Rei had just eaten in the dining hall.

Meaning Mireine had deliberately bought a sandwich at the dining hall of Yūgure no Komugi-tei and given it to Set.

“Ah, Rei. ...You're quite early. You could have slept a little longer.”

“It's already past ten.”

While responding to Mireine's disgruntled greeting, Rei shifted his gaze into the stable.

Naturally, many horses were present there, but about half of them seemed frightened.

The other half, the ones not scared, were either horses that had undergone rigorous training or horses that had been in the stable when Rei had used the Wheat Inn at dusk before heading to the Bestia Empire.

(Could it be... the scared horses aren't scared of Set, but of Mireine?)

As he scanned the stable, Rei suddenly had that thought, but he dismissed it as unlikely and opened his mouth.

“More importantly, did you hear about it from Surnin?”

“Huh? Oh, something about Rei needing something from us? About removing the seeds of restraint or something like that. Personally, I'm perfectly content just being with Set-chan.”

“...You're something else. Well, if you know about it, that's fine. So, what do you say? Since we've run into each other here, shall we remove them now?”

Though, to be honest, removing the Seeds of Restraint inside the stable feels a bit anticlimactic, Rei added. Mireine fixed her gaze on him.

It wasn't the soft, loving gaze she directed at Set. It was sharp.

“Hey, Rei. I'm happy you're removing the warning seed. Really, I am. But why suddenly? That magic was cast because we saw Set-chan's power, different from a normal griffin's, right? So why?”

For Mireine, having the magic binding her released was a relief. ...It was a pity that as a side effect, she would lose her strong resistance to flames, but still, the thought that she might not die if something happened was a relief.

But she knew how much Rei cherished Set, and at the same time, she knew how attached Set was to Rei.

It was frustrating... truly frustrating from the bottom of her heart, but Rei was the one who held the highest place in Set's heart.

Precisely because I understood the bond between that one person and that one creature, I wondered why he'd suddenly choose to remove the restraining spell.

“Is it strange?”

“Of course it is.”

“Well, to put it simply... I participated in the civil war that broke out in the Bestia Empire. During that time, I ended up using Set's skill in front of a large crowd. It became impossible to hide it.”

“...What on earth are you doing? Or rather, what were you thinking? Set already draws attention just being a griffin. And why get involved in a civil war in the first place?”

“Things happened. Lots of things.”

He couldn't possibly explain here why he'd become involved in the civil war, so he deflected.

This civil war was plotted by the neutral faction and the aristocratic faction within the Kingdom of Mireana working together.

Precisely because of that, he couldn't just blab about it freely outside.

“Even so, with Rei's power, you didn't need to go out of your way to reveal Set-chan's secret, did you?”

“...Maybe not, but if that had happened, I'd be dead.”

At the words coming from Rei's mouth, Mireine's eyes widened.

She was so shocked she nearly crushed the sandwich she held in her hand.

“Rei nearly dying... What kind of situation could lead to that?”

The Rei Mireine knew possessed power capable of overwhelming even an entire army single-handedly.

The idea of such a person struggling... no, nearly dying was utterly unimaginable.

“Well, he ended up fighting a pretty formidable opponent.”

“...Seriously? What kind of opponent makes Rei say that?”

Hearing Rei's words, Mireine seemed more intrigued than anything, urging him to continue.

Rei hesitated for a moment, unsure if he should tell her. But deciding there was no real need to keep this a secret, he spoke plainly.

“The Bestia Empire's Rank S Adventurer, Immovable Noise.”

“Huh!?”

That name was completely unexpected, even for Mireine. Her eyes widened dramatically. She fixed her gaze on Rei as if gauging the truth of his words. Several seconds passed before she cautiously asked.

“Really?”

“Ah. Well, the result was... I guess we fought alongside Set and managed to make them retreat, but looking at the actual battle, it was more like we lost—or rather, they let us off easy. Rank S, huh? I was pretty confident in my fighting skills, but I never imagined...”

“Well, of course. Rank S? Rei's rank was... B, right? Honestly, it's surprising you managed to hold your own at all given that rank difference.”

"Anyway, that's why I went all out with Set's skills during the final and biggest battle of the civil war. After that, I figured I couldn't cover it up anymore since I'd used the skills in front of so many people, so I just unleashed them freely on the enemy. ...In the current Bestia Empire, it's widely known that Set is a rare breed of Griffin. So, there's no need to keep you two bound by the Seed of Admonition anymore."

“Gururuu.”

Set growled at Rei's explanation. Mireine hesitated for a moment, but eventually spoke.

“But... it's a shame about the fire resistance. If you're not going to mention it about Set-chan, wouldn't the Seeds of Restraint be nothing but a blessing?”

“It'd be unnatural in various ways if you couldn't speak up when others talk about Set. ...Don't worry, I'll leave the fire resistance blessing as it is.”

“You can do that?”

“Yes. However, it requires the seed of restraint within your body, continuous exposure to magic for a certain period, and sufficient affinity.”

After hearing Rei's words, Mireine thought for a moment before nodding slightly.

“...Understood. Then, I request it.”

Hearing Mireine's words, Rei drew the Death Scythe, the magic-activating device, from Mistling and chanted the incantation.

'Flames, feed upon the seed of restraint embedded within this one and become this one's power.'

A small flame ignited at the pommel of the Deathscythe... Rei directed that flame toward Mireine.

‘Silent Flame’

Simultaneously with the spell's activation, the small flame was drawn into Mireine's body... Mireine distinctly felt the sensation of something burning within her own flesh.
