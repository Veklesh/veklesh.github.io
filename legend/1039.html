Early the next morning, after Elena and Vihera had cast suspicious glances at Marina. Rei woke up in the house provided for him, groggily got ready as usual for about thirty minutes, and stepped outside.

The house Rei was renting had a stable. He headed toward that stable...

"Huh?"

For some reason, Yellow was lying sprawled across Set's back.

"What are you doing here?"

Even as he asked, Rei understood why Yellow was here.

Yellow and Set are good friends.

So, he must have come to play.

There was a stable-like structure at the house where Elena and the others were staying, but it housed a carriage and the two horses that pulled it.

Yellow probably felt a stronger desire to be with Set, even if it meant being a little farther away, than to stay with those two horses.

(He could have slept inside the house with Elena and the others... but he probably just wanted to be with Set.)

Watching Yellow sprawled across Set's back, Rei gently reached out and lifted him up.

Touching the hard scales, he felt a faint chill.

Even as a child, being a dragon's offspring, its scales possessed a moist, smooth quality.

Even though they were scales, they were completely different from the scales of snakes or lizards Rei had touched back in Japan.

They were scales that could only be described as similar yet fundamentally different.

"Kyu? Kyu-kyu-kyu!"

Pleased by the caress on its scales, Yellow let out a happy chirp.

"Grrr..."

Seeing Yellow like that, Set, who had been lying down, sat up, wanting to be stroked too, and rubbed his head against her.

"Alright, alright. Set, you're still such a clingy little thing. ...Here."

After returning Yellow to Set's back, Rei reached out to stroke Set's head.

The completely relaxed look on Rei's face as he stroked Set now had an air about it that would make even someone who only knew him by his alias, Crimson, wonder for a moment who this person was.

After petting Set's head for a few minutes, Rei heard his stomach growl and pulled his hand away.

"It'd be nice to just stay here playing with Set, but then I'd miss breakfast. And since we're treating the World Tree today anyway, I really need to eat properly when I can."

"Gurrr? ...Gurrr!"

Set tilted his head slightly, looking disappointed for a moment, as if thinking, Already over? But being told he'd miss breakfast meant he couldn't keep fussing any longer.

Like Rei, Set was also hungry before breakfast.

With Yellow still perched on his back, Rei headed toward the house Elena and the others were renting.

"Huh? Yellow, I thought you were gone. You went to see Set? I was just about to go look for you."

In front of the house Elena and the others were renting, Rei was greeted by Elena.

She said she was going to look for him, but she probably expected him to be gone.

Then again, Yellow was surprisingly curious. There was also the thought that he might have been wandering around the Dark Elf settlement as he pleased.

"When I went to the stables, he was sleeping with his head on Set's back."

"...So you went to see Set last night, not this morning?"

"Who knows. But I wouldn't be surprised."

Anyone could tell from his attitude that Yellow adored Set.

Elena felt the same, of course. A small smile touched her lips as she caught Yellow, who leapt off Set's back and flew toward her.

"Honestly, he's hopeless. ...Anyway, dinner should be ready soon. Today's the day we finally treat the World Tree. We need to eat well and build up our strength."

"Eat well, huh? Well, I'm happy about it, but for Set..."

"That's no problem either. Marina had the villagers prepare it. Apparently it's a whole roasted oak. She seemed quite pleased while cooking it."

"...I suppose so."

Recalling Jusla's expression while Set fought the orcs on the way to the settlement, it was obvious what feelings the Dark Elf harbored toward them.

Rei understood Jusla's feelings weren't necessarily representative of all dark elves, but still, their hatred for orcs was likely shared to some degree.

Learning that the only orcs she seemed to like were the ones dead and turned into food, Rei suddenly recalled a line from a novel or something he'd seen back in Japan.

(Something like, "The only good something is a dead something..." was there, right?)

The blank could be filled with various words, but in this case, it would be orcs.

Truth be told, orc meat wasn't exactly from a high-rank monster, yet it was undeniably tasty. It wasn't surprising the Dark Elves enjoyed it.

"Gururu!"

Set seemed pleased to be eating orc meat first thing in the morning, growling contentedly.

"Well then, shall we eat?"

Seeing Rei announce this and start to head inside the house... Elena let a small smile touch her lips.

The reason for that smile became clear to Rei the moment he stepped inside.

Because among those gathered around the dining table sat one person who was utterly out of place.

Normally, this person would be hostile towards him... well, perhaps not exactly hostile, but considering yesterday's events, they definitely shouldn't be here.

Rei voiced that person's name.

"Ragdo, right? What are you doing here?"

As if displeased by the question, Ragdo opened his mouth with a sour expression.

"The elders told me to witness you healing the World Tree. I bet it was someone's doing."

Ragdo's gaze was fixed on Marina, seated directly across from him.

Even though it was morning, she still radiated a seductive aura. If it had been night, even Ragdo, her political adversary, might have lost his head.

But Marina, hearing Ragdo's words, turned her gaze toward him and spoke with a smile.

"Well, if someone sympathetic to me were the witness, who knows what you might say afterward? Then it would be quicker if you were present from the start, wouldn't it?"

That was undeniably true.

At the same time, it was precisely because she was confident that Rei could heal the World Tree that she acted this way.

Knowing Marina's true intentions, Ragdo averted his gaze from her and sighed bitterly.

That sigh contained his frustration at himself for being captivated by Marina despite their political enmity, as well as his struggle to resist being drawn away by the two women sitting beside her – Vihera and Elena, who had entered the room with Rei, both possessing beauty equal to Marina's.

Facing such a Ragdo, Rei wore a puzzled expression.

"Well, I get the whole witness thing, but why are you here? You don't need to eat breakfast with us, do you?"

"...Please ask Marina-sama."

Laying his dissatisfaction bare, Ragdo's words prompted Rei to turn his gaze toward Marina.

But Marina, seemingly unconcerned by Rei's reaction, spoke up.

"Well, might as well eat together, right?"

"Is that so? Well, I don't mind either way."

Even as he said this, Rei's gaze remained fixed on Ragdo.

Given yesterday's incident, there was no doubt he wasn't someone Ray particularly wanted to welcome.

Still, he didn't complain because Marina had decided it, and he judged that following Marina's instructions was the best course of action regarding this World Tree matter.

After that, the atmosphere remained awkward, yet he still stuffed himself full of the food Marina and Byune (the woman) had prepared.

...Naturally, Ragdo, witnessing Rei and Byune's appetites up close, grimaced in astonishment.

Once breakfast ended and the post-meal break concluded, they would head for the World Tree.

"Normally, this is the time you'd need to psych yourself up, right?"

"Guru?"

Outside the house, as Rei finished getting ready, Set looked over at him, purring and asking, "What's up?"

Laying a hand on Set's head, Rei shook his head, signaling it was nothing. But Elena, who had been petting Yellow nearby, smiled and spoke up.

"That might be true normally, but this time we're not exactly charging into enemy territory, are we? The World Tree is within this settlement... well, slightly outside it, and since a barrier has been set up to keep monsters out, expecting us to be tense seems unreasonable."

Rei nodded in agreement with Elena, who added, "Though I do think it's a bit optimistic."

"Still, based on past experience, there's usually something or someone there. If not monsters, then people."

Since arriving in Elgin, Rei had been caught up in numerous incidents. ...No, it would be more accurate to say he'd been continuously caught up in them.

Given that, it was only natural to wonder if maybe...

"I wish you wouldn't say such ominous things. If something really happened to the World Tree, it wouldn't be a laughing matter."

Marina emerged from the house, muttering with a slightly exasperated tone.

Behind her was Ragdo, his expression clearly displeased.

"Honestly, those who don't live in this settlement have it easy."

"Well, what can I say? It's the truth..."

Before Ragdo could raise his eyes and retort at Rei's words... Marina spoke again.

"Well, there's no point standing around like this forever... Shall we go?"

"The World Tree. I'd heard rumors about it before, but this is my first time seeing it with my own eyes. ...Well, actually, from this distance, I've been seeing it repeatedly since yesterday."

Vihera murmured, her gaze fixed on the enormous tree visible even from where she stood.

Following Vihera's gaze, everyone else nodded in understanding.

Ragdo, in particular, having been born and raised in this settlement, felt no sense of strangeness at the sight of the World Tree.

As they set off toward the World Tree, the group naturally stood out.

Three beauties like Elena, Vihera, and Marina were together, and there were also figures like Set and Yellow.

Moreover, many had witnessed Ragdo antagonize Marina over yesterday's incident, so it was impossible for the two of them to go unnoticed while traveling together.

"Lady Marina, where are you all headed with such a group?"

"To the World Tree."

"Oh my, could that possibly be..."

"Yes, you can assume it's as expected."

They continued on their way, exchanging such conversation with the dark elves who approached them, until eventually fewer dark elves came to speak.

And then... eventually, Rei and the others arrived beneath the World Tree.

"This is... the World Tree?"

It was Ara who murmured in stunned silence.

Faced with the sheer size of the World Tree stretching out before her, she could only stare in utter speechlessness.

She had heard it was weakened by illness, but to Ara's eyes, it didn't seem weak at all.

Its leaves were lush and green, appearing vibrant and healthy from every angle.

Beside Ara, Marina—who had only arrived yesterday—sensed that the vitality emanating from the World Tree had recovered slightly even in just one day.

"...It is indeed weakened,"

Ragdo murmured as he looked at the World Tree.

Rei noticed the unmistakable sadness mixed in with those words and wore a slightly surprised expression.

Despite not harboring warm feelings toward Ragdo after yesterday's incident, it seemed true that he genuinely cared about this settlement, just as Marina had said.

(But that doesn't mean I can forgive him for yesterday's actions, though.)

Looking away from Ragdo, who was transfixed by the World Tree, Rei turned his gaze toward it once more.

Looking up, he couldn't see the top of the World Tree, hidden by its branches and leaves.

"Well then, shall we begin? Rei, could you handle this?"

Rei, who had been captivated by the World Tree, snapped back to reality at Marina's voice.

"Got it. ...I'm not really used to this kind of work, though..."

Muttering, he touched the trunk of the World Tree... though up close, calling it a wall might be more accurate.

"Okay? Since it's your first time, don't pour too much magic into it all at once. Please do it little by little."

Nodding at Marina's warning, he gently... just like when using a magic item, channeled his magic power.

Thump.

Just like when Rei entered the barrier's protective sphere, the pulsation occurred again.

Startled by the pulse, Rei instinctively tried to pull his hand away, but Marina's hand gently covered his.

"Don't let go. It's okay... It's fine. This pulsation is proof the World Tree is gladly absorbing Rei's magic power. Slowly... slowly channel your magic power into the World Tree."

Following Marina's words, Rei began channeling her magic into the World Tree.

After several minutes, the entire World Tree began to emit a faint glow.

Several more minutes passed. Then, for just an instant, the World Tree shone intensely bright... and gradually, that light faded into a faint glow.

Watching the World Tree, Rei murmured.

"Did we do it?"
