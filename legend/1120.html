The day after requesting the disassembly of the Sand Lizardman at the guild, Rei was naturally present at the guild.

Before him stood fifteen adventurers.

Each of them had rushed to claim the job the moment the request was posted yesterday, after Rei had placed it at the guild.

The request was for a relatively safe dismantling job, offering higher rewards than usual. Plus, it offered the chance to meet adventurers with nicknames... Given all the perks, it was no wonder so many adventurers wanted to take on Rei's request.

There were simply too many applicants, so the guild selected those they deemed most suitable, resulting in this group.

...It was daytime, so it ended up being manageable, but the guild staff felt that if it had been posted during peak times like morning or evening when adventurers were more numerous, it would have caused an even bigger commotion.

Gosh didn't have as many adventurers as Gilm to begin with.

Consequently, almost no one had ever experienced a large number of adventurers rushing to the reception desk all at once... Given that even immediately after Rei returned, it caused such a commotion, some were terrified to think what would have happened if that situation had occurred during a peak time when more people were present.

"So, can we really leave it to these fifteen?"

"Yes. I believe they are all serious and capable adventurers."

Several of the adventurers praised by the receptionist in charge of Rei relaxed their expressions.

Their expressions made it immediately obvious to anyone what feelings they harbored for the receptionist.

"Is there a representative... or someone to supervise them?"

"The guild will provide one for that purpose."

"...Is that okay?"

The day before, they had mentioned the guild might send someone, but Rei had expected they'd actually leave it up to the adventurers.

However, the guild also wanted to build a friendly relationship with Rei, an adventurer with a reputation.

If they could gain favor with something this minor, dispatching someone was no trouble at all for the guild.

They probably figured that even if they didn't know how long Rei would stay in Gosh, if something major happened while he was there, they might be able to rely on him.

(Maybe they also want him to handle the Augusto and Daldora situation somehow...)

While vaguely understanding the guild's motives, Rei gladly accepted since it posed no disadvantage to him.

He reasoned that while he couldn't fully trust an adventurer acting as supervisor unless they were someone he knew reasonably well, like Zarust, if the guild intended to use him, they wouldn't pull any strange stunts here.

As Rei pondered this, a beastman approached her.

The soft-looking triangular ears perched atop its head made it immediately clear this was a fox beastman. But...

(Aren't fox beastmen usually female? A middle-aged male fox beastman... Honestly, what's up with that?)

Looking at the figure before him, Rei couldn't help but mutter inwardly.

Of course, even among fox beastmen, there were men.

Rei understood that, but even so, the reason he immediately pictured a female when told "fox beastman" was probably because he'd read games and manga featuring nine-tailed foxes back in Japan.

"Lord Rei? What's wrong?"

The fox beastman called out curiously to Rei, who had stopped moving and was staring at his face.

Snapping back to reality at the sound of her voice, Rei hastily shook her head, saying it was nothing.

"Nothing. I was just lost in thought. So, you're...?"

"Yes. I'm Fais, supervising the adventurers for this dismantling request."

Faith bowed her head as she introduced herself.

Her large triangular ears swayed slightly as she did so, catching Rei's attention.

"Ah, ah. Yeah. Nice to meet you."

"Leave it to me. I'll see this request through properly. ...Now, let's head to the warehouse right away. We need to retrieve the Sand Lizardman."

With that, Fais exited the guild.

Rei followed after him, and the adventurers who had accepted this dismantling request followed as well.

One of the adventurers watching the group asked a companion in puzzlement.

"Hey, you. Who's that little guy? You know why the guild staff are being so deferential to him?"

"Huh? ...Oh, you came back to Gosh yesterday for that escort job, huh? Just so you know, you'd be better off not messing with Rei. In terms of actual strength, too."

"Huh? Hey, are you saying I'd lose to that little one?"

"That's right."

Hearing his acquaintance, who should know his own strength, state it so flatly without hesitation, the man wore an expression of disbelief.

But the man who had made the statement nodded back at him, understanding his feelings.

"You wanna say that's not true, huh? But sadly, there's probably not a single adventurer in Gosh who could beat Rei."

"...That bad?"

He found it hard to believe, but seeing the other man's expression—devoid of any hint of joking—left him no choice but to ask again.

"Yeah. After all, he's a Rank B adventurer with a nickname."

"...Why is such a monster even here?"

The man who learned Rei's true identity muttered in stunned silence, but the acquaintance who heard it could only reply, "How should I know?"

Unaware that such rumors were circulating about him, Rei had arrived at the warehouse alongside the adventurers who had accepted the request and Fais.

Strictly speaking, it wasn't a warehouse, but its size made calling it one seem accurate.

It was the same warehouse where they had dismantled the Sand Serpent before, and this wasn't Rei's first time here either.

"Well then, I'll leave it to you."

Prompted by Fais, Rei nodded and began pulling the Sand Lizardman corpses out one after another from the Misty Ring.

The other adventurers watching let out loud gasps of surprise.

They understood Rei possessed the Misty Ring, but this was their first time witnessing it in action.

Ignoring the usual reactions around him, Rei finished removing all the Sand Lizardman corpses and turned to Face.

"Alright then, can I leave the rest to you?"

"Yes, leave it to me."

After exchanging a few brief words, Rei left the warehouse.

He headed straight out the front without going through the guild, where he met up with Set, who was waiting, and they wandered through the town together.

"Grrr?"

Set looked at him, growling as if to ask, "What now?" Rei hesitated slightly.

Since they couldn't leave the city, hunting monsters was out of the question.

That left him with options: continue eating his way through the town, absorb Sand Lizardman magic stones somewhere unseen, or return to Augusto's mansion to practice his Two-Spear Style. Or eat his way through the town, eat his way through the town... or eat his way through the town.

(Well, what to do... Ideally, I'd like to absorb the Sand Lizardman's magic stone, but there's that incident from before... Hmm, can't be helped. Eating my way through the city is the way to go here. I'd like to practice the Two-Spear Style too, but getting too absorbed in just that wouldn't make sense either.)

Above all, Gosh has many dishes spiced with fragrant spices, which piqued Rei's interest.

(Spices, spices... I'd love some curry, but I have no idea how to make it.)

For Rei, curry meant only the kind made with curry roux.

He'd heard things like mixing several kinds of roux makes it tastier, or adding ketchup, sauce, soy sauce, coffee, chocolate, or grated apples at the end makes it delicious... but that was about the extent of his knowledge.

That's why, even if he wanted curry, not being able to make it was a big problem.

(And I don't have any rice either.)

For Rei, who equated curry with curry rice, having just curry meant nothing... well, maybe not nothing, but it wouldn't satisfy him.

Even so, while searching for something similar, a scream suddenly echoes from afar.

"What was that?"

"Guruu?"

Rei turned his gaze toward the source of the scream, but it didn't stop at just one. It echoed repeatedly, and worse, it seemed to be getting closer.

"Something definitely happened... but what?"

Rei tilted his head in confusion, but the reason for the screams soon became clear.

"Don't come! Don't come! Don't you dare come, you bastard!"

A man wielding a spear was running down the road, shouting those words.

His eyes were bloodshot.

Blood stained the tip of the spear he was swinging wildly. It was obvious, without needing to think, what sort of act had brought him this close.

Even now, he swung the spear at a woman nearby, simply because she was in his way.

Fortunately, the man seemed half-mad, so the woman avoided being impaled or sliced by the tip. Still, she was struck sideways by the shaft and fell to the ground.

"Heh, heh. That's what you get for... getting in my way! Get out of my path if you don't want to end up like this!"

The man shouted, swinging his spear.

Those around him quietly stepped aside...

"Huh?"

Seeing someone standing in the way ahead, the man let out a suspicious sound.

He probably never imagined anyone would stand in his way now.

But a sneer quickly appeared on the man's lips.

Because the one blocking his path was someone smaller than him—not quite a head shorter, but at least half a head shorter.

He wondered if this was some powerful ally, but there was no one else around.

Wrapped in a robe with the hood up, the face was hidden, but the small stature made him wonder if it might even be a woman.

Fine then. What could a woman possibly do against him? With that thought, the man charged forward, spear in hand.

Completely unaware that the one standing there wasn't a woman... but an existence as deadly to him as the Grim Reaper himself.

"Get out of the way!"

As he closed the distance with the figure blocking his path—Rei—and swung his spear, the man's consciousness plunged effortlessly into darkness.

"...There was no need to have Set move."

Watching the man lie unconscious on the ground, Rei shifted his gaze toward the direction the man had come from.

There stood Set.

Seeing the man running toward them, he had positioned himself in front of the man and asked Set to block the escape route so the man couldn't flee.

And the most likely escape route for the man would be the unoccupied direction... meaning, the way he himself had just come from.

That's precisely why Set had moved swiftly to block the path the man had come from.

That was only possible because the man had caused a commotion, drawing the attention of those around them.

Under normal circumstances, seeing Set running would have definitely drawn everyone's attention.

...Though it might have been different if it had been Gilm.

"Gururuu"

At Rei's words, Set growled and approached.

While stroking Set's head, Ray found himself wondering what to do about the unconscious man.

He couldn't possibly kill him, but leaving him like this wasn't an option either.

Handing him over to the security guard who'd arrived seemed the safest option... but just then, he saw a man running towards him.

And he wasn't just approaching casually; his body radiated a murderous intent.

My eyes sharpened for a moment at the sight of the man radiating that killing intent, but they softened slightly when I recognized him.

"Zalust, you came chasing this guy too?"

Rei said, shifting his gaze toward the unconscious man.

But Zarust didn't answer Rei's question. He walked straight toward the unconscious man.

"Hey?"

Rei called out again, but Zarust showed no sign of responding. He raised the longsword he held...

"Hey!"

Instinctively, Ray extended the Death Scythe he'd drawn from Misty Ring, catching the blow of the longsword Zarust had swung.

Rei's strength combined with the hundred-kilogram weight of the Death Scythe. Zarust swung his longsword down with all his might, only to have the blow deflected as expected. The longsword slipped clean out of his hand.

(That strike just now... he was aiming for my arm. So he didn't intend to kill me? But why did he suddenly do something like that?)

Only then, after his sword was deflected, did Zarust seem to notice that Rei was there.

He tried to shout something, then stopped, his words catching in his throat in surprise.

「……Rei？」

"Yeah. So, why are you so full of murderous intent?"

"...Mr. Augusto..."

Zalust seemed unable to say more, but seeing his expression, Rei could easily guess why he was so murderous.

"Dammit!"

Without waiting for Zaruust to respond, he broke into a run.

...Toward the direction Zarust had just come from.
