"W-wait! Wait a minute! Please!"

Normally, he would be the kind of man accustomed to intimidating others with his intimidating demeanor, but with the blade of the Death Scythe pressed against the back of his neck, he couldn’t maintain his usual tough act.

The sight of the Death Scythe’s blade effortlessly slicing through the longsword’s blade was firmly etched into the man’s mind.

Normally, this would have been just another job as a bandit—attacking a passing caravan, just like always.

In fact, out of the three wagons, he had successfully stopped the lead wagon by shooting its driver with an arrow.

Once the lead wagon was in that state, the wagons behind it naturally had no choice but to stop as well.

There were a few adventurer bodyguards, but given the number of bandits the man led, they could easily handle such a small force.

……Things were going well up to that point, but just as they had killed all the guards and were about to make their move, despair struck.

From that point on, with no time even to think, the bandits led by the man lost their lives one after another.

Some were cut down by the scythe, others had their necks snapped with a twist, and still others were shot down by arrows… Many were killed in the blink of an eye, and before I knew it, the only one left was a single man on the verge of having his head taken by the Death Scythe.

Judging that there was absolutely no chance of victory, it was only natural for him to shout for surrender.

The moment he shouted, the blade of the scythe that had been pressed against his neck slipped away, and the man let out a sigh of relief, thinking he had been saved...

But whether the man was truly saved depended on the mood of the man standing before him... Rei.

As the man thought this... eventually, as if he knew exactly what the man was thinking, Rei spoke up.

“Tell me where your hideout is. And how many of you are there? If you answer those questions honestly, without lying, I might let you live for now.”

“R-really? You mean it? You’re not lying, are you?”

The sight of a man in his thirties desperately shouting at Rei, who is in his mid-teens, is almost comical in a way.

But with his life on the line, the man had absolutely no room to spare for such concerns.

“Yeah. Though, I suppose I’ll hand you over to the merchants over there, and you’ll end up being sold to a slave trader.”

“Huh!? …W-well, that’s still better than dying.”

The man gasped for a moment at Rei’s words, but in the end, he meekly confessed the location of the hideout.

Furthermore, upon hearing that all the bandits were here and no one was at the hideout, Rei left the negotiations regarding the bandits’ fate to Marina and headed for the hideout with Relieu.

True to his reputation as an adventurer with a nickname, Relieu showed no sign of distress even as he flew while hanging from Set’s leg.

“...It’s a bit late to ask, but why did you go out of your way to come along, Relieu? Since it’s been decided that all the earnings from this job are ours, you didn’t really need to come with us, did you? Wouldn’t it have been easier for you to stay with the merchants?”

Rei, having landed in front of the cave the bandits used as their hideout, asked Relieu, who had just jumped down from Set’s leg, which she had been clinging to.

As Rei said, it really was a belated question… but Relyu looked around and replied to Rei’s words.

“No, there’s no particular reason. To be honest, it was just a hunch.”

Ray had assumed there must be some important business involving only him.

That was what Rei had been thinking, but judging by Relu’s current demeanor, he must have concluded that there really wasn’t anything specific on his mind.

“I see,” he replied briefly, then headed straight into the cave.

Inside the cave, just as the man had said earlier, it was undoubtedly a bandit hideout.

However, it was just a bandit hideout in the countryside.

Naturally, there were no magic items of the sort Rei had secretly hoped to find.

In fact, there weren’t even any spears or similar weapons that Rei could use for throwing.

Then again, ever since he’d acquired the Twilight Spear, he’d been using ordinary spears for throwing much less frequently.

There were still plenty of spears slated for disposal inside the Misty Ring, but when it comes to ammunition, the more you have, the better.

(Well, they’re spears, not guns.)

He rummaged through the cave looking for something, but all he ended up finding were a few gold, silver, and copper coins, and a sack of wheat—probably looted from merchants and never even used for cooking.

Especially the sacks of wheat—there were quite a few of them piled up haphazardly, and there was enough to make me think, “If we use this, we won’t have to worry about flour-based dishes for a while, right?”

Among Rei and the others, Marina is the one who does the cooking.

Considering that, the wheat was probably the most valuable item among the things these bandits had.

Of course, if they had gold or silver coins, they could buy wheat themselves, so in the end, those might actually be more valuable.

“So, no hostages to speak of, then. And the weapons don’t seem to be of much use either.”

Even as he said this, he began stowing away daggers, long swords, and clubs one after another into the Misty Ring.

The clubs were reinforced in various ways, but since they were basically made of wood, he even found himself thinking they might make good firewood.

After stowing everything of potential monetary value from the hideout into the Misty Ring and stepping outside, he found Relieu there, smiling as she stroked Set.

Relieu had been wearing a smile that looked like pure happiness, but when she saw Rei emerge from the hideout, her expression changed immediately.

(Ah... I see.)

Seeing Relue like that, Rei finally understood why she had been acting alongside him.

While Set enthusiasts are generally mostly women and children, that doesn’t mean there aren’t any adult men.

This is particularly evident among people like street food stall owners.

That said, for stall owners selling skewers, bread, and the like, the sight of Set eating near their stall is a powerful draw for customers.

Since Set is eating there, those who wish to admire him gather... and seeing Set eating the food so heartily, passersby stop by the stall to buy various items.

Not a “crowd-drawing panda,” but a “crowd-drawing Set”… or perhaps a “crowd-drawing griffin.”

“...What is it?”

“Nah, nothing. It seems you’ve managed to keep Set from getting bored, so that’s good.”

“Huh!? …Ah. I was bored anyway, so I didn’t mind keeping him company.”

There was no way to hide the fact that someone had witnessed him playing with Set, but since Rei didn’t seem to be teasing him about it, Relieu answered with a sigh of relief.

Seeing that, Rei began to wonder: Could it be that Relieu accepted this request from Daskar—or, more accurately, this chance to repay a debt—because of the whole Set situation?

In fact, that assumption wasn’t entirely wrong; there was no doubt that Relieu had accepted this job immediately because of Set.

She is undoubtedly completely captivated by Set’s charm, but even while understanding that, she feels it would be shameful for a grown adult like herself—especially a Rank A adventurer known by the alias “Gale”—to be seen as someone who simply wants to admire Set.

Relyu genuinely believes this.

In reality, there are men who admire Set—men who are even older than Relieu or have a tougher appearance.

But Relyu simply cannot accept that.

“I see. Set, I’m glad you got to play with him.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set purrs happily.

Realizing that he wouldn’t be teased for playing with Set, Relyu felt a slight sense of relief.

As for Rei, he was actually relieved because he now understood why they had cooperated with them, even if it was for that reason.

“So, was there anything good inside the hideout?”

“Unfortunately, there wasn’t anything I was hoping for. But there was a huge amount of wheat—probably stolen from a merchant—so it was a big help in terms of food.”

“Hmm. Well, if it’s wheat or something like that, we’ll definitely be able to sell it if push comes to shove. Since Rei has an item box, he doesn’t have to worry about weight or anything, so that’s nice.”

“True. Though I don’t plan on selling any of it. …Anyway, since we’re done here, I was thinking of heading back soon. Is that okay with you?”

“Yeah, that’s fine with me.”

Relyu nodded casually, and together with Rei, they headed toward the spot in Set where Elena and the others were waiting.

“Oh, Rei. And Relu, too. You’re early.”

“Is that so?”

Marina called out to Rei as he dismounted from Set’s back.

Rei replied with a hint of puzzlement, likely because the caravan they had rescued was already gone.

From Rei’s perspective, it was a relief not to have to deal with tedious expressions of gratitude, requests for an escort to the nearby area, or the handover of the man who led the bandits… but even so, he couldn’t simply nod in agreement when told they were early, given that the caravan was already gone.

“Yes. Anyway, we should get moving soon. If we stay here much longer, animals and monsters might be drawn by the smell of blood.”

“You’re right. Let’s get moving as soon as possible.”

It was Vihera who said that—which was unusual.

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn’t be strange for her to suggest staying behind, thinking they might be able to fight a strong opponent… but I suppose the mock battle she had with Relyu earlier that day had a positive effect.

Since Vihera—who was the one most likely to say she wanted to stay here—had spoken up, Rei and the others left the spot.

They set off toward their destination, Golz... and eventually the sun set, and night fell.

Although it was still quite hot during the day, it was definitely getting to be autumn, so the sun was inevitably setting earlier.

As a result, Rei and the others end up spending the night outdoors...

“Whoa, what’s that?”

Relieu looked inside the magic tent Rei had pulled out and looked surprised.

A Magic Tent is a fairly rare magic item, but it’s not like an Item Box, of which there are only a handful in the world.

In fact, it’s the kind of thing an adventurer with a reputation would likely have seen before…

Unfortunately, it seemed this was the first time Relue had ever seen a Magic Tent.

“It’s a Magic Tent.”

“Well, I know that without you telling me. Still, seeing it in person like this is pretty amazing.”

“Is that so? Anyway, this is a bit late to ask, but what are you going to do, Relyu? We’re going to sleep inside the Magic Tent.”

If someone said they were going to sleep in the same tent, you’d normally imagine something rather suggestive.

However, the inside of the Magic Tent was actually quite spacious. While Elena and the others slept in beds, Rei often slept on the floor or the sofa.

……Since we’re definitely under the same roof, there’s no doubt that some people would get the wrong idea.

“Hm? Oh, me? I’m fine sleeping outside. I’m used to it.”

As a solo adventurer, sleeping outdoors is probably second nature to Relieu.

Since there’s no one like Set around, it was only natural for Relieu to sleep outdoors whenever he was on his own.

If he were sleeping in a tent or using a sleeping bag, he’d be completely helpless if monsters or bandits attacked.

(Besides, if he stays outside the magic tent, he’ll be able to play with Set to his heart’s content.)

From what Rei could tell, that was likely the main reason Relyu had decided to spend the night by the Magic Tent.

Of course, since Relieu was trying to hide it, Rei didn’t go out of his way to mention it.

Besides, many of the members of the Crimson Wings, led by Rei, have their own complicated backstories, and above all, there’s Elena, an outsider, with them this time.

Given that, it was certainly true that sleeping in the same room—albeit the Magic Tent—would be problematic in many ways.

Given those circumstances, there’s no doubt that Relieu’s offer was a welcome one.

“Sorry about that.”

“It’s fine. It’s no big deal. It’s not like I’m not getting anything out of it either.”

Smiling at Relieu’s unwavering demeanor, Rei began taking out various dishes for dinner from the Misty Ring.
