While Rei and Set were still searching for magic items at the construction site, undeterred by their previous experiences… just outside Gilm, the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle was proceeding steadily.

That said, given the Giganto Turtle’s size, the pace of the work could literally be described as slow as a turtle.

And beside the Giganto Turtle were the workers dismantling it, the guards slaying the monsters drawn by the scent of blood, and...

“Wow… I didn’t know monsters this big even existed.”

“To defeat a monster this huge… are all the adventurers in Gilm monsters themselves?”

“Hey! Just so you know, not every adventurer in Gilm can take down a beast like the Giganto Turtle! So, for heaven’s sake… got it? For-heaven’s-sake, don’t get the wrong idea!”

People who had come to catch a glimpse of the Giganto Turtle’s dissection were watching the scene unfold.

Some of the guild staff were even on duty to make sure those onlookers didn’t get in the way of the dissection.

Hearing the onlookers’ words, one of the guards—stationed there to defeat any monsters that might appear—shouted reflexively.

Since the person he was speaking to wasn’t an adventurer, he spoke in a tone that made it clear he couldn’t stand the thought of anyone thinking that any adventurer from Gilm could defeat a monster like the Giganto Turtle.

Even among the adventurers in Gilm, there’s no one besides Rei who could take down a monster of this caliber on their own… well, I wouldn’t go that far, but it’s truly only a select few at the very top.

“Huh? Is that so?”

“Of course! Seriously… I mean, do you really think there are people everywhere who are as strong as exceptions like Rei and Set?”

“Yeah, you’re right. In a way, it feels like rank fraud.”

Another adventurer standing next to the man who had shouted muttered thoughtfully.

Hearing those words, another adventurer nearby nodded in agreement.

“Technically, since I’m Rank B, I’m a high-rank adventurer… but from the perspective of other Rank B adventurers, they probably don’t want to be lumped in with me.”

“Do they think we’re in the same rank as that Crimson? Ugh, that’s no joke. At the very least, I don’t want anything to do with that.”

Being in the same rank means it wouldn’t be strange to be expected to perform similar tasks.

In that sense, what the man was saying was a fact that the other adventurers couldn’t deny.

To begin with, the very fact that there’s a Rank B adventurer capable of going toe-to-toe with a Rank S adventurer—of whom there are only three in the world—is strange in many ways.

Someone who knows nothing of the situation… someone like the guy who, after seeing the Giganto Turtle just now, spoke as if any adventurer from Gilm could do the same thing—if such a person were to get the wrong idea, there was a very real possibility that they might request a Rank B adventurer to fight a Rank S adventurer, reasoning that since Rei could fight a Rank S adventurer, a Rank B adventurer should be able to as well.

If he were forced to accept such a request, it would be tantamount to suicide.

Of course, since it’s ultimately up to the adventurer to accept the request, they could simply refuse such a job.

In the midst of that exchange, a voice suddenly rang out.

“Hey, wait a minute. Hey… isn’t that… the Princess General?”

It wasn’t exactly a loud voice, but the words “Princess General” definitely reached the ears of those around us.

And when I looked around almost reflexively, just as the words suggested, the Princess General—Elena—was standing there.

With luxurious, vertical-curl blonde hair that looked as if the sunlight itself had become her hair, and a beauty that drew the gaze of all who saw her.

Such a figure was walking toward the Giganto Turtle alongside Ara.

Elena’s presence captivated not only the adventurers who had been keeping watch, but also the onlookers who had come to watch the Giganto Turtle’s disassembly, those actually performing the disassembly, and the guild staff—many people were mesmerized by her.

She was accustomed to such admiring gazes—though some were tinged with lust—and walked on without seeming particularly bothered.

Soon, a guild official approached Elena and Ara, who were walking side by side.

“Welcome, Lady Elena. I’ve heard about you from Rei. I understand you’ve come today to observe the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle…”

“Mm-hmm. I heard about it from Rei last night. Still… seeing it like this, all over again, it’s truly impressive.”

Elena remarked as she looked at the Giganto Turtle.

Indeed, the size of the Giganto Turtle, the workers dismantling it, the adventurers guarding them, and the onlookers watching the process from the sidelines...

What was taking place there was no longer just a dismantling; it was more like a show.

That’s probably why so many onlookers have gathered here.

“Haha, you’re right. If we’d charged an admission fee, the Guild might have even made a profit.”

The fact that a guild employee could say that with a touch of humor showed just how many onlookers had gathered.

Of course, it’s only because the escorting adventurers are here that they’ve gathered like this… but even so, it can’t be said to be completely safe.

For now, most of the monsters drawn by the scent of blood are the kobolds attacking Gilm, and aside from them, it’s mostly Rank D… with the occasional big fish like a Rank C monster showing up.

However, the adventurers hired as guards were there to defeat them, and fighting such monsters had become a form of entertainment in itself.

Since there are so many adventurers hired as guards this time, there’s no risk of harm coming to the onlookers.

...That said, it’s rare for a monster’s dissection to become a spectacle in the first place, and even if it did, it wouldn’t draw this many onlookers.

Still, the fact that so many people have gathered is likely because it’s winter.

Unlike the spring through fall, there aren’t many forms of entertainment.

For these people, the dissection of this Giganto Turtle could certainly be called the perfect form of entertainment.

They get to see the carcass of a giant monster up close and witness a butchering scene with their own eyes—something that, aside from adventurers, they rarely get to see in their daily lives. On top of that, they can watch the guards defeat real monsters that attack right before their eyes, so it’s no wonder this event is so popular.

That said, the guards’ primary duty is to protect those actually butchering the Giganto Turtle, not the onlookers.

In a real emergency, the priority would naturally be the butchers… but since the crowd of onlookers casually includes Rank C and Rank B adventurers, even if something were to happen, the onlookers themselves probably wouldn’t suffer any significant harm.

“A spectacle, you say? If it’s the Guild of Gilm, surely they wouldn’t need to do something like this to avoid financial difficulties?”

Ara, who had been listening to the conversation beside Elena, asked.

At the Guild in the frontier town of Gilm, it was only natural that a large number of rare monster magic stones and materials were brought in.

The guild sold these to various shops and, in some cases, to people living in other towns and cities, reaping substantial profits.

In particular, the meat of Gamelions hunted in the fall sells for a decent price, aside from what is consumed in Gilm.

……Though the amount consumed in Gilm itself is already quite substantial.

“Given that the guild is making such a profit and also receives support from influential figures, surely there’s no need to charge an admission fee to those who have come all this way just to watch the dissection of a Giganto Turtle?”

“That’s right,” Ara said, and the guild official smiled awkwardly at her words, replying, “Well, that’s true, but…”

Even if they don’t intend to make more money than necessary, it wouldn’t be unreasonable to consider charging an admission fee now that there are so many spectators.

“But having extra funds never hurts. …So, what do you want to do? If you’d like to take your time looking around, I can get you a chair or something.”

“That’s fine, we’ll stay as we are.”

In response to the guild clerk’s words, Elena replied simply.

If this were a noble who hadn’t trained their body, they would likely have simply ordered a chair to be brought, since standing for so long would be painful.

But Elena is someone who has fought her way through countless battlefields.

Standing was no problem at all for her.

Hearing Elena’s words, the guild clerk was relieved that he wouldn’t have to prepare a chair or anything of the sort.

Since they still had to record the names of those who had done the dismantling work, ensure they were properly paid, and give them meat as a souvenir in addition to their wages, a simple chair and desk were provided.

However, these are truly simple items—one might even call them shabby.

They were certainly not the sort of furniture a noble would use.

In other words, if Elena had said she needed a chair, they would have had to return to Gilm and procure a suitable chair from the Guild or somewhere else.

As a guild employee, I suppose I can say I was relieved just to avoid having to go through that trouble.

“Well then, I have to give instructions for the dismantling work, so I’ll take my leave now. If you need anything, please call me. I’ll do my best to make time for you.”

“Right. Please do. I wouldn’t want to be the one to waste your time on a job Rei requested.”

“Thank you. Well then…”

The guild clerk bowed and stepped away from Elena, then called out to those whose attention had been drawn to her and were losing focus.

“Come on, focus on the dismantling. Even a rare monster like this loses its value if you fail to dismantle it properly. To prevent that, you need to concentrate properly.”

At her voice, many of them remembered their current situation—that they were dissecting a Giganto Turtle—and resumed their work.

“She’s quite the leader, isn’t she?”

“Well, of course. Even the guild wouldn’t want to send some incompetent person here and make things awkward with Rei. In that sense, it’s only natural to send someone with real ability, isn’t it?”

“It would be a disaster if they sent someone incompetent and word got back to Marina.”

Saying this, Vihera approached Elena.

Even seeing Vihera here, Elena doesn’t seem particularly surprised.

After all, she had heard those remarks last night.

“What about Byune?”

“Hm? Look, she’s over there.”

In response to Elena’s question, Vihera’s gaze falls on Byune, who is staring blankly at the scene of the butchering—perhaps because there are no monsters in sight at the moment.

A short while ago, they had defeated a kobold that seemed to be heading toward Gilm but had been drawn there by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood… but today, partly because there were more guards than yesterday, they had defeated it with ease.

The reason the number of guards had increased was that, after seeing more kobolds than expected yesterday, the guild staff had urgently hired many more to serve as guards.

For Byune, who was hoping to defeat the kobolds to obtain proof of the kill and magic stones, the guild’s actions had only served to increase the number of his rivals.

“She looks a little grumpy, doesn’t she?”

“……Is that so?”

Although Elena had known Byune for quite some time, she still couldn’t understand why he looked grumpy just by looking at him now.

From Elena’s perspective, the current Byune was just as expressionless as ever.

“Do you get it?” Elena turned her gaze toward Ara, but Ara, naturally, couldn’t tell the difference in Byune’s expression either.

Elena nodded in agreement as Ara shook her head, indicating she didn’t understand.

However, watching the two of them, Vihera tilted her head slightly.

“Is that so? I think it’s pretty obvious.”

“I suppose only Vihera would notice that, Vihra.”

“Just a little… just a tiny bit,” Elena murmurs, looking almost envious.

To Elena, no matter how skilled she might be, Byune was still just a child.

Given that, it was only natural for her to want to protect or dote on him, but to be honest, she found herself hesitant to get involved with Byune, whose expression rarely changed.

“Hmm… really? Well, if you stick with Byune, you’ll come to understand those things eventually.”

“I hope so. Anyway, putting Byune aside, is Vihera just here to observe?”

“That’s right. If a high-rank monster comes drawn by the scent of blood, I think that’ll be my cue.”

Saying that, Vihera flashed a seductive smile.
