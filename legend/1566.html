The soldiers of Jaya who attacked the fifth basement level.

Though their force numbered nearly fifty in total, every last one of them ended up being defeated by Rei and his companions.

...From the soldiers' perspective, the moment they opened the hidden door, Rei and his group were right in front of them.

They knew the underground facility they called the Giant's Nest was under attack, but they never imagined enemies would be right there the moment they opened the hidden door. They were completely caught off guard.

Of course, the unexpected turn of events was just as surprising for Rei and her group, but their ability to act immediately spoke to their level of training.

"Well, that was quite an unexpected turn of events, but at least we didn't have to search for the secret passage after all. That makes things easier."

Rei muttered as he looked at the hidden passageway.

Rei had anticipated the existence of a passageway for transporting the giants.

Still, even for Rei, a passage this size was surprising.

Then again, considering giants needed to move through it, this size wasn't really surprising.

"...Hmm."

Byune, who had been so eager to find the hidden passageway, was probably tired too, having fought relentlessly against the enemy soldiers.

Byune lacked her usual energy.

And yet, the look of satisfaction on her face wasn't a mistake on Rei's part.

Though she hadn't been able to demonstrate her full prowess as a thief, she had still been able to fully showcase her combat strength, her greatest asset.

Of course, in terms of pure combat strength, the others were overwhelmingly stronger than Byune.

That Byune could unleash her combat prowess this time was partly due to her strength, but more so because the conditions were favorable.

The Jaya soldiers never expected to suddenly encounter enemies in such a place. While it was wide enough for two giants to move side by side, it was clearly too narrow a battlefield for nearly fifty men to fight.

Furthermore, Byune had the powerful weapon, White Cloud, and the confined space allowed her to fully leverage her small stature.

(More than that, having their commander taken out so suddenly must have been a huge blow for them.)

Watching Byune, Rei thought such things.

Nearby, Vihera addressed Rei.

"So, Rei. What now? Still following this passage?"

"Yeah. Not exactly as planned... but we've got a path forward now. Might as well take it."

No one seemed to object to the suggestion, and everyone quickly got ready.

After all, they shouldn't be that exhausted after fighting opponents of that level.

Byune's stamina had recovered considerably after switching places with Vihera midway and resting in the rear.

If anything, they needed to replenish their Long Needles... but that wasn't a problem either, since they could pull them from the bodies of the dead or unconscious, or pick up any lying around.

"That's probably better. Even if many are unconscious or immobilized by pain now, you can't be sure they won't get any strange ideas."

Several of the figures sprawled on the floor reacted to Elena's words.

They weren't unconscious, just those left lying there with broken bones.

Of course, it was precisely because they knew they had absolutely no chance of winning that they remained completely still.

Rei, judging that there wasn't much useful information to be gleaned from the low-ranking members, also believed it was best to act quickly now.

"Stay put and keep quiet. If you do, we won't harm you any further."

Considering what Jaya had done, he wondered if letting them off this lightly was wise.

But fortunately, he'd just finished letting off steam, so he didn't feel the need to get his hands dirty himself.

(Though letting us pass unchallenged will undoubtedly cause us trouble later.)

The best outcome for the men would be for Jaya to collapse completely.

...Though if that happened, they might not be pursued over this incident, but they'd also lose their jobs.

Rei couldn't help but doubt that was truly the best outcome.

Anyway, seeing the men obediently fall silent at Rei's words, Rei and the others entered the hidden passageway.

"It's a bit late to mention this, but honestly, this doesn't look like a secret passage at all."

Rei muttered as they advanced through the passage.

Normally, Byune would lead the way, wary of traps, but since this was a hidden passage, she wasn't concerned about that.

After all, giants were the ones who normally used this passage. If there were traps here, it would be impossible for the giants to avoid them.

One or two might accidentally avoid it, but dozens, or even hundreds of giants all doing so... that was simply impossible.

"Since giants pass through here, it needs to be at least this wide, right? Though some might still think it's narrow."

Vihera replied to Rei's words.

As they spoke, they advanced through the hidden passageway...

"Oh, carriages. ...Well, come to think of it, that makes sense."

A considerable number of carriages came into view ahead, and Rei nodded in understanding.

As the shipping point for giants, this hidden passage must extend at least beyond Mejogo.

Given that, it was unlikely the fifty or so men Rei and his group had just defeated had walked all that distance.

Moreover, this attack by Rei and his group had come as a sudden shock to the people of Jaya.

Given the urgency to send reinforcements as quickly as possible, preparing carriages was only natural.

And... since they had prepared these carriages, it was certain there were several drivers as well.

"What?!"

One of the drivers must have heard Rei's voice. He looked up from talking with his companions and, noticing Rei and the others there, let out a cry of shock.

From the coachmen's perspective, their reaction was likely one of intense confusion.

They had sent nearly fifty men as reinforcements, and yet, not much time had passed before Rei and his group appeared. It was only natural for them to think that.

It was unreasonable for the coachmen to recognize Rei and his group as part of those soldiers.

After all, every soldier who had gone to the reinforcements was male; there wasn't a single woman among them.

While a few did have a tendency to use feminine speech patterns, their gender was undeniably male.

Given that, the coachmen could state with certainty that there were no women as stunningly beautiful as Elena and her companions.

Therefore, the figures in the coachmen's line of sight were undoubtedly not people from Jaya.

And knowing that Jaya had gone to such lengths to send soldiers this time, it wasn't difficult to figure out what kind of beings Rei and the others were.

But... the problem was that even if they understood, there was nothing the coachmen could do about it.

Had they turned the carriage back toward where they came from, or perhaps fled this spot altogether, escape might have been possible.

But the carriage had arrived here and remained stationary.

If they'd had a little more time, they might have moved the carriage... but the drivers were busy complaining about this sudden errand, putting it off.

...Of course, if it were just horses, it might have been different. But with horses pulling a carriage, even if they tried to escape, Rei and the others could have easily caught up.

"Well, it'd be a big help if you just played nice for now. What'll it be? Put up a fight?"

Faced with Rei's words, the only thing the coachmen could do was quietly surrender.

Even though they belonged to Jaya, they were still just coachmen.

Sure, compared to ordinary coachmen, they were somewhat accustomed to rough situations... but even so, they couldn't imagine getting the better of professionals.

Moreover, the fact that Rei and his group were here meant that the nearly fifty soldiers brought here just moments ago had all been defeated.

It wasn't impossible that they'd hidden somewhere, let the soldiers pass, and were now here... but there was no way to verify if that was true.

"We surrender."

One of the drivers declared first, and the others nodded in agreement one after another.

"I see. That's the best outcome. ...Though, I have no demands to make of you, so don't worry about that. Ah, but I'll be taking the carriages."

As he said this, Rei's gaze fell on the carriage at the very back.

The drivers looked at Rei, seemingly wondering what he was talking about.

It was only natural. There were ten carriages here.

And there were only five of them.

There was no way they could possibly manage all of them.

But even if they had complaints, the drivers said nothing, knowing their own safety came first.

Once the coachmen were moved and gathered together, the horses harnessed to all but one carriage were released, and their hindquarters were given a light slap.

Then, as if understanding exactly what Rei wanted and intended, all the horses ran toward the hidden passageway where the soldiers had come from.

Thus, the horses pulling the nine carriages vanished, leaving only the carriage bodies behind... Rei touched them and stored them away inside the Misty Ring.

The coachmen were startled by the sudden scene unfolding before them, but for Rei, who had seen the same reaction every time he used Misty Ring, their surprise held no particular meaning. He continued stowing the carriages one after another.

Finally, only one carriage and the two horses pulling it remained.

(The carriage bodies could be dropped from the sky as weapons, or sold if necessary. They probably had other uses too. Getting them here wouldn't hurt.)

Thinking this, Rei headed toward the lone remaining carriage.

Elena and the others felt a slight exasperation at Rei's actions, but since it was typical Rei behavior, they said nothing and boarded the carriage.

"...Wait, what about the driver? Oh well."

Marina muttered with a hint of exasperation before sitting down on the driver's seat.

"Brrrrrrr"

Seeing Marina like that, one of the two horses whinnied happily.

Marina, a dark elf, naturally possessed a personality that many animals found endearing.

Furthermore, her experience as an adventurer before becoming guildmaster had made her capable of easily handling the role of a carriage driver.

Indeed, even now, Marina succeeded in conveying her intent to the horses with just a light tug on the reins.

The two horses began moving back in the direction they had come from.

...Just a few minutes ago, with ten carriages still here, such a maneuver would have been troublesome. But the horses pulling the carriages had already been released and galloped away, and the carriages themselves were stored within Rei's Misty Ring.

In other words, turning the carriages around here wasn't particularly difficult.

The coachmen looked as if they wanted to say something, but Marina, who was steering the carriage, ignored them completely.

(Probably because if things stay like this, the carriages they were entrusted with will be gone, and they'll get blamed by their superiors or something like that.)

Marina's thoughts were half right, but also half wrong.

From the coachmen's perspective, it was clearly unacceptable that Marina, dressed in a party gown as if attending some aristocratic gathering, was driving the carriage.

Marina herself, however, showed no sign of caring about this at all, as the party dress was her usual attire.

In any case, Marina's carriage left the coachmen behind and drove off.

The coachmen could only watch in silence as it departed.

Then, just as the carriage disappeared from sight... one of the coachmen suddenly spoke up.

"So, what do we do now?"

"Well, what do you expect us to do? ...Guess we'll just have to walk back, huh?"

"...That distance?"

The other coachmen nodded in agreement with the one who spoke, his expression one of utter weariness.

Of course, it wasn't an impossible distance to walk, but it was long enough to feel like a hassle.

"I'm grateful you saved our lives, but I wish you'd at least left one carriage behind."

Another coachman muttered, and the others nodded in agreement once more.

The drivers resigned themselves to walking, though it was a pain, when one of them suddenly had an idea.

"How about we just go up to the giant's nest and travel by carriage from Mejogo...?"

At those words, all eyes lit up, and they began moving toward the giant's nest... It would take a little more time before they saw the soldiers they had brought, literally piled up like corpses, and let out cries of shock.
