"Yott!"

With that cry, the Sand Lizardman's scaly hide began to peel away.

Because it was scaled, Sand Lizardman skin could be sold for a decent price.

Though Rei wasn't exactly short on cash, he still didn't consider throwing away money he could get his hands on. He was skinning the Sand Lizardman partly to practice his skinning skills.

"Gurururuu"

The smell of flowing blood and the sight of the Sand Lizardman's flesh after its skin was removed made Set growl, signaling he was hungry.

Even though he'd just eaten a chunk of orc meat, watching the skinning process apparently didn't matter to Set.

(Normally, the sight of skinning makes people who aren't used to it feel like they're going to puke.)

As he slid the knife between skin and flesh, Rei recalled scenes he'd witnessed several times before.

Those raised in rural areas, where slaughtering livestock is commonplace, tend to be somewhat accustomed to facing monster carcasses.

But when those raised in so-called cities become adventurers, the sight of monster butchering comes as a huge shock.

(Probably because it lacks a sense of reality. ...Come to think of it, I remember reading or hearing somewhere that even in Japan, some people believe fish swim around as fillets.)

Rei suddenly recalled his time in Japan, but in that instant, he felt the Sand Lizardman's skin tear excessively and hurriedly refocused his attention there.

"It's tough, isn't it? Especially with humanoids."

Monsters often come in a wide variety of forms.

Some resemble animals like boars or horses, but even more commonly, they take forms you've never seen before.

The Sand Lizardman Rei was currently skinning was one such example. While roughly humanoid in shape, it possessed scales and a face that was distinctly reptilian, making it a strange creature in many ways.

That said, humanoid monsters weren't exactly new to Rei, starting with orcs and the like.

Therefore, despite struggling somewhat, he continues to dissect them one after another.

Water is essential for the act of dismembering monsters and stripping materials.

It's crucial for washing away blood and grease, and eliminating odors. ...Though some tough guys manage with desert sand.

In that sense, while most dismembering in Gosh happens in designated spots, Rei possesses the Water-Flowing Dagger, which generates water.

Because Rei never had to worry about water, he was able to perform the butchering within the grounds of Augusto's mansion.

Skinning, gutting, cutting the meat... Within two hours, he finished butchering two Sand Lizardmen.

"Skinning's still the hardest part, huh?"

"Grrr."

Set, who had been lying down a short distance away, nodded in agreement at Rei's words.

Set had initially watched Rei work, but after nearly two hours, perhaps growing bored of silent observation, he ended up lying down beside Rei like this.

Still, he seemed happy just to be near Rei. Set let out a rather cheerful growl.

"...Here, at least eat this."

Handing Sand Lizardman's right arm to Set, he stood up, purring happily, and began pecking at it with his beak.

Seeing that, Rei thought it was typical griffon behavior as he stored the other parts and hide into the Misty Ring.

Of course, the tip of the tail, which would serve as proof of the kill, had been cut off before dismemberment.

"Grrrrrrr"

Watching Set happily pecking at the Sand Lizardman's arm flesh, Rei performed the final step... or rather, in a sense, the main event: washing the magic stone clean with his water-element dagger.

"Well, that should be enough for the dissection for now."

There were still nearly ten Sand Lizardmen left, but Rei had no intention of dissecting more than the two before him right now.

If the goal was purely to obtain the magic stones, there was no need for the process of dissection and stripping.

After all, he could simply slit open the Sand Lizardman's chest and take the magic stone from there.

The reason he hadn't done that was likely to practice the very acts of disassembly and skinning.

Several years had passed since Rei first came to Elgin, yet he still struggled with these tasks.

"Would someone more dexterous be better at this? ...Ah, well. Keep at it and I'll get used to it eventually. ...Set."

"Guru?"

Though he was midway through eating the Sand Lizardman's flesh, Set obediently responded to Rei's voice.

What is it? His round eyes looking up at Rei were an incredibly adorable sight, but seeing the blood and bits of flesh around his beak left Rei feeling rather uneasy.

(I thought I'd bled it out properly...)

Wondering if he should have bled it more thoroughly, Rei wiped Set's beak clean.

Pleased by this, Set purred happily and began rubbing his face against Rei.

Petting Set as he rubbed his face against hers, begging to play and be noticed, Rei spoke.

"Set, is there anyone around spying on us?"

"Grr? ...GRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set sharply scanned the surroundings.

Using not just his senses—hearing, sight, smell—but also his ability to sense magic, he scanned the area... then suddenly froze.

"Grrr!"

Not his usual cheerful growl, but a sharp one.

Having spent much time with Set, Rei immediately understood what it meant.

Still, he didn't panic and frantically look around. Instead, he stroked Set while probing the surrounding atmosphere.

Within seconds, he detected a presence lurking a short distance away.

The mansion where Rei and Set were located was on the outskirts of Gosh, naturally less populated than the central areas.

Given that situation, someone outside the grounds of the Augusto estate wouldn't be noticed.

Gunter was the gatekeeper, but he wasn't particularly skilled.

Sure, he's perfectly capable as a gatekeeper, but expecting him to sense who's where across this vast estate... or even whether someone is outside the grounds at all? That would be impossible for most.

(Do they know that too and are probing us? Besides, if they're inside the grounds of the Augusto residence, that's one thing, but as long as they're outside the grounds, we can't really reprimand them.)

Even if we caught whoever was spying on us right now, it wouldn't be a crime.

After all, they're merely in a place that isn't private property. That's only natural.

(So, are they absorbing magic stones inside the stables? ...No, I can't rule out the possibility they're watching us using some method I don't know about.)

Rei didn't know about magic items that could see through walls or spy on distant locations.

But if we're talking about that, Rei also didn't know about magic items like the Sand Ship until he came to the Kingdom of Solrain.

And Daldora of the Elema Trading Company, who was at odds with Augusto, the man Rei was staying with, was spending lavishly to gather high-rank adventurers from surrounding towns and countries.

The Sand Ship Rei seized from the sand pirates originally belonged to Daldora.

Considering that, it wouldn't be strange for him to possess a magic item that allows him to spy on others.

(Well, if there really were a magic item that could spy on me from that far away, he wouldn't need to lurk near me like this. ...Or maybe if the magic item's effective range was small, it could work?)

Magic items often prove to be things beyond Rei's expectations in many ways.

Items like the Misty Ring, Dragon Robe, Death Scythe, and Sleipnir's Boots that Rei carried possessed abilities beyond common sense, so it wouldn't be strange if Rei had other magic items beyond his imagination.

(So maybe I should stop absorbing the magic stones. ...But then, why was there no guard when I first stayed at Augusto's mansion? Did Daldora change his mind about something?)

Wondering about this, he briefly debated whether to practice the Two-Lance Style since absorbing magic stones wasn't possible, or to play with Set... but he couldn't resist Set's round eyes pleading "play with me, play with me," and ended up playing with Set for a while.

"What the hell is going on here?!"

A shout echoed through the room.

If anyone who knew that person heard that shout, most would likely tremble in fear.

The one shouting was Daldora, head of the Elema Trading Company, a man whose influence was such that he could handle most opponents if he truly wanted to.

But... the person being yelled at by Daldora right now was one of the rare exceptions.

"That's your first word after walking in? Unusual for someone like you who's always nagging about manners and such."

The person in the room replied without any particular surprise.

While the outside temperature approached forty degrees Celsius, this room maintained a temperature just below thirty degrees.

This was only possible thanks to a magic item for cooling, but that was only achievable because the master of this room... the person who had just spoken, was the lord of Gosh.

A man in his forties, of average build and height. His features weren't so ugly as to be unrecognizable, nor were they so striking as to be captivating.

His aura lacked the imposing presence one might expect from a noble head of household; he was utterly ordinary... a man who left little impression.

Count Ryu Brand Salmas. He was the lord of Gosh and the head of the Salmas family.

Though calling it a count's house was generous; Solrain was a small country. Normally, they wouldn't have the wealth to casually purchase a magic item for air conditioning. Yet, it was a luxury made possible precisely because Ryuubrand had Daldora.

Seated at his desk, reviewing documents, Ryuubrand watched Daldora storm in and couldn't help but smile wryly.

Whether one saw it as a composed demeanor or an expression of utter blankness likely depended on the observer. To Daldora, however, with the report that had prompted her outburst fresh in mind, it undoubtedly appeared as if he were thinking of nothing at all.

"Didn't I explicitly forbid you from interfering with Rei?! Then why are you spying on her?!"

"Hmm, I heard about that. But I am the lord and noble of this Gosh. Even if you're an adventurer with a nickname, there's nothing you can do to me."

Ryubrand stated this matter-of-factly, showing no particular sign of agitation.

It wasn't bravado or anything like that; he genuinely seemed to believe that the adventurer couldn't do anything to him... no, that there was no way he would.

"W-well... that... you said it yourself. That man, Rei, would easily lay hands on anyone who opposed him, even nobles."

"Well now, I suspect those rumors are exaggerated. Nobles are special beings, after all. Rei should understand that."

Daldora frowned dramatically, his nervous-looking face turned skyward.

He couldn't think of anything else to do.

Ryubrand genuinely believed that, as a noble, he was untouchable.

If that were all, he'd just seem like an arrogant noble.

But he governed Gosh perfectly well, never engaging in outrageous tax collection or forcibly abducting women.

Objectively speaking, he was undoubtedly a nobleman who should be classified as virtuous.

That's precisely why Daldora paid him large sums and cooperated to develop Gosh.

Even so, he occasionally reveals this naive side, forcing Daldora to clean up the mess time and again. ...Yes, just like this time.

"Perhaps the rumors are exaggerated. But it's not certain they are. The possibility that he genuinely laid hands on a noble cannot be ruled out."

This was why Daldora had rushed over to Ryu Brand's place like she was barging in—because she'd received a report from her subordinate.

They said men loyal to Count Salmas seemed to be watching over the Augusto residence.

Having heard rumors of Rei's strength and been warned by his guards that he was someone you absolutely must not make an enemy of, Daldora naturally turned pale.

Realizing this was bad news, he first ordered his men to forcibly remove whoever was watching the Augusto residence before rushing to the lord's manor.

But showing no sign of understanding Daldora's troubles, Ryu Brand spoke up.

"I hear this Rei fellow has a griffin under his command. I'd like to see a griffin up close myself... Would that be possible?"

Hearing those words, Daldora could only look up to the sky once more.
