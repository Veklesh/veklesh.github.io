As desert nights grow colder, merchants and adventurer guards each sleep wrapped in blankets inside their tents.

But... Rei was enjoying a night of comfort entirely different from theirs.

Why? Because Rei was using a magic tent.

Naturally, it was designed for comfort inside. Whether in the desert or snowy mountains, being inside the magic tent allowed one to live completely detached from the external environment.

Even a magic tent could only withstand so much damage if attacked from the outside; it would break down quickly.

Set being near the magic tent also served to protect it.

"The Kingdom of Solrain, huh? I only know the name myself. I heard it's mostly desert."

Elena , reflected in the Paired Orb, stated while sipping her tea.

Having spent the spring in Gilm, Elena had accumulated quite a bit of work, though not as much as Guildmaster Marina.

Still, she used her natural abilities to steadily clear the backlog, and now she could make time to talk with Rei at night like this.

...Given Elena's position as a duke's daughter, there were parties and such she simply couldn't skip, so it wasn't every day.

"I see. I was hoping you might know something about Gosh."

'I'm sorry.'

Being relied upon by Rei yet unable to respond was a sad thing for Elena.

But Solrain was a country without any particular distinguishing features, even among the many vassal states of the Mireana Kingdom.

If one were to force a defining feature, it would be the abundance of desert, as Elena had mentioned.

This meant most of its land was covered in desert. Compared to its suzerain, the Kingdom of Mireana, and even its neighboring countries, its national power was extremely low... essentially, it was a small country among small countries.

That's precisely why Elena only knows the broad strokes about Solrain.

"Nah, I didn't know either, so don't worry about it. ...More importantly, what happened with that thing you mentioned before?"

"Hmm, that matter? It's a bit difficult... I suppose I should say. Father is considering it positively, but the target isn't me. As it happens, I'm the face of the aristocratic faction, so it can't be helped."

It couldn't be helped. Even as she said this, Elena sighed with a disappointed expression.

What Elena had proposed to her father, Duke Kerebel, was to send someone from the Noble faction to Gilm to maintain close contact with the neutral faction, with whom they currently had a cooperative relationship.

Of course, several nobles from the aristocratic faction already resided in Gilm. However, Elena's proposal to her father was to send a more important figure to solidify the alliance between the aristocratic faction and the neutral faction.

Her true motive, however, was not the Paired Orb, but the desire to meet Rei in person.

Duke Kerebel showed some understanding of Elena's proposal, yet he never even considered her as a candidate to send to Gilm.

Elena understood this well. She knew her position as a symbol of the aristocratic faction meant she would have to join the fight if hostilities broke out.

But... Elena anticipated that war was unlikely for the foreseeable future. The Bestir Empire, a longstanding adversary, had seen the rise of a pro-Mireana faction during last year's civil unrest.

It wasn't absolutely certain, since the emperor himself wasn't pro-Mireana.

"I see. I would've been happy if Elena had come too. Set was looking forward to playing with Yellow, and Vihera and Marina were probably looking forward to seeing Elena again, one way or another."

'Heh heh, those two would probably say they can relax more with Rei when I'm not around. ...Come to think of it, Vihera is currently back in Exil with Byune, isn't she?'

She must have recalled what Rei told her during their conversation at the Paired Orbs. Elena flashed a faint smile and murmured with amusement.

To Elena, Vihera was a friend, a rival, and a love rival all rolled into one.

The "friend" part was especially significant.

This applied not only to Elena but also to Vihera and Marina; all three had various unique circumstances.

For example, Elena was the daughter of a duke and bore the nickname Princess General. Vihera was the former Second Princess of the Bestir Empire. Marina was a Guildmaster and a dark elf with a deep connection to the World Tree.

For those with such unique circumstances, while they may be admired or feared, forming friendships on equal footing is extremely difficult.

In that sense, Elena is fortunate to have a friend like Ara .

Regardless, it was precisely because they were all individuals with unique circumstances that the three were able to become friends.

The greatest reason for this was likely the existence of Rei, the one they all came to care for.

The bond between the three, forged by both friendship and love, proved far stronger than Rei had imagined.

"Well, I'm glad we didn't drag Vihera into that mess. With Vihera, she might have ended up beating the merchants senseless or something."

It was practically a given to Rei that if he found something annoying, Vihera would take some kind of action.

Among the merchants swarming around Rei, there was no guarantee none would be dazzled by Vihera's beauty and get strange ideas.

Elena wasn't usually in Gilm, and Marina had her duties as the guildmaster of Gilm.

But Vihera was traveling with Rei, and it was undeniable that this increased their encounters with merchants.

In that sense, it was fortunate that Vihera had returned home to Exil with Byune. ...Fortunate for both the merchants who might have fallen victim to Vihera and for Rei, who might have been left scrambling to clean up the mess afterward.

After talking with Elena for a while longer, Rei eventually went to sleep as the night grew late.

Rei sat up in bed.

He looked around to assess his surroundings and murmured briefly.

"...Morning."

Though Rei was usually terrible at waking up, in situations like this, he could snap awake instantly.

This was probably largely instinctive.

Even though Set was keeping watch outside the magic tent, making it safer than the nearby villages or towns, a part of Rei close to instinct remained wary of the fact they were camping.

He quickly got ready and stepped outside the magic tent to find the morning sun already fully risen.

Thanks to the Dragon Robe, Rei didn't mind, but the temperature had already surpassed thirty degrees Celsius.

Around him, the others were already preparing breakfast, each calling out greetings to Rei as he emerged from the magic tent.

Strangely to Rei, the attitude of those around her had softened considerably compared to yesterday.

(Well, the reason is obvious enough...)

He glanced over at Set, who was lying beside the magic tent eating dried meat, probably given to him by an adventurer, and let a small smile touch his lips.

Several adventurers stood around Set.

Rei recognized those adventurers.

They were the ones Zarust had introduced yesterday, saying they would take turns keeping watch with Set.

Initially, it was supposed to be just Set and one adventurer on watch, but the fact that several were ultimately prepared was proof they didn't fully trust Set... or more accurately, they didn't fully trust Set and Rei.

That said, Rei didn't complain about it.

After all, he figured it was unreasonable to expect them to fully trust someone who suddenly appeared with a griffin as their familiar.

Even though everyone had only interacted with Set for a few hours, it was only natural that fearless souls would try to communicate with him when it was just them and Set alone.

Set was naturally friendly towards anyone who didn't harbor hostility towards him or Rei.

As a result, the adventurer and Set quickly became friends. Seeing this, the other adventurers cautiously began petting Set too... and when the watch shift changed, those who witnessed it... gradually became friends with the adventurer as well.

As for the watch, Set kept a firm eye on things, so there were no particular problems.

"So, in the end, none of the sand pirates came to help them, huh?"

With Set present, any attempt to aid the sand pirates would have been detected immediately. Yet, since no disturbance occurred during the night, even if there were traitors, they likely felt too threatened by Set to act.

Watching Set playing with the adventurers, Rei came to that conclusion and moved closer to Augusto and Zarust, who were talking a little ways off.

Zalust must have noticed Rei approaching. He gave him a wry smile and called out.

"You've become quite the popular figure overnight, haven't you?"

Rei didn't need to think to understand what he was referring to.

After all, right before his eyes, Set was playing with the adventurers.

"I expected it. Set's naturally friendly toward anyone who doesn't show hostility. Plus, back in Gilm, where I usually base myself, he's popular with the whole town."

"...Gilm, you say. It's famous within the Kingdom of Mireana as one of the towns where skilled adventurers gather."

Augusto, who had been listening to Rei and Zarust's conversation, murmured with a hint of envy.

While Augusto was reasonably well-known in Gosh, Gosh was ultimately just one town within the small nation of Solrain.

While it was one of the larger towns within Solrain, it was undeniably dwarfed by the Kingdom of Mireana.

This meant there were fewer rare goods worth dealing in as a merchant.

For a merchant like Augusto, it was only natural to dream of making his fortune someday in a great nation like the Mireana Kingdom.

"Ah, being on the frontier means there are plenty of ferocious monsters around. Camping at night basically guarantees an attack. Well, I don't have to worry about that myself."

"...Set, huh?"

The voice murmuring with deep feeling carried a hint of envy.

Their senses were sharper than humans', they possessed the ability to sense magic, and their individual combat prowess was exceptionally high.

They were monsters possessing abilities that were truly appealing for the role of sentinel.

"I've seen a few Tamers before, but this is the first time I've seen one with a griffin like Set as a familiar."

"Well, Set and I are a bit different from the norm."

The moment those words left Rei's mouth, a sharp gleam lit up in August's eyes, who had been listening to the conversation with Zarust.

Though often described as kind-hearted... or timid, August likely judged Rei's words as a potential business opportunity.

"Rei, what did you mean by that? Did you perhaps subjugate Set as a familiar using a method other than normal taming?"

"Yeah. But let me warn you, just because you heard it doesn't mean you can do it right away."

"Even so, I'd very much like you to teach me. I'll pay you a reward, of course."

Rei looked surprised at the unusual intensity coming from Augusto, who rarely showed such forcefulness. After a moment, he shook his head slightly.

"I don't need any reward. It's different from the usual method, but it's not like nobody knows about it."

"...Huh? What do you mean?"

"It's simple. I grew up with Set since we were kids. Since he was just a child, really. That's why he's so attached to me now. This method is similar to how a dragon knight hatches and raises a dragon from an egg, so anyone could do it if they tried."

"Though whether it'll work or not is another story," Rei concluded.

Augusto frowned upon hearing this.

Dragon Knights naturally existed in Solrain too, but maintaining them was extremely difficult for a small country like Solrain, as raising wyverns required vast amounts of food.

Still, for reasons of national defense and prestige, they maintained a few riders, so Augusto understood what Rei meant.

"But isn't that uncertain?"

"Probably. Quite a few actually die during the wyvern rearing process. But I'm absolutely certain that's how I made Set my familiar."

Technically, it was through beast magic, but since he couldn't say that, he had no choice but to stick to his usual cover story.

"If we could obtain a monster's young or an egg... or perhaps..."

"Augusto, shall we finish our meal soon? It'll be hot again today, and we should arrive in Gosh earlier."

Augusto nodded in agreement to Zarust's question as they gazed at the boundless blue sky stretching above them.
