"Hey, thirty skewers of Gamelion coming right up! Rei, Set, thanks as always!"

The stall owner smiled happily as he expressed his gratitude to Rei and Set.

Rei listened to these words while stowing most of the skewers into his Misty Ring.

Leaving one skewer for Set and keeping another for himself, Rei stood beside him. Set looked at Rei with round, hopeful eyes, urging him to eat already!

Under a cloudless sky, perfect for describing a clear winter day, Rei handed the skewers to Set and began walking down the main street.

"Come back again!"

he heard the stall owner's cheerful voice call after him.

Rei, who bought thirty skewers at once, was undoubtedly a valued customer to the stall owner.

With sales skyrocketing like that, it was only natural the vendor was in high spirits.

"Still... lately, I've been able to enjoy a pretty relaxed pace, haven't I?"

"Grrr..."

Walking down the main street while eating his skewers, Rei murmured thoughtfully.

He had hardly made any effort to conceal his power, so in a way, it was his own fault. Yet, since arriving in Elgin, Rei had been caught up in one trouble after another.

Trouble that most adventurers might only experience once in a lifetime, and he'd been caught up in it repeatedly.

But this winter was unusually... truly unusually free of any trouble whatsoever.

(Well, there was the time he restored Vihera's consciousness, and the battle with the Silver Lion in the dungeon... but that was before the snow fell, back in autumn.)

The only winter commotion Rei could recall was probably the party eating Silver Lion meat.

It hadn't even been a month since the snow barbecue party ended, yet it felt like ages ago.

...The memory of being so full from eating all that silver lion meat that he couldn't enjoy meals for days afterward was both good and bad for Rei.

"Ah, it's Set-chan! Set-chan, let's play together!"

Walking down the main street, a small child spotted Set and called out like that.

At the same time, the other children playing with that child naturally chimed in that they wanted to play with Set too, gathering around Rei and Set.

"Grrr?"

Set tilted his head slightly, asking if it was okay. Rei nodded.

They weren't walking down the main street today because they had any particular errand to run.

They were just taking a walk to kill some free time, so letting Set spend a little time playing with the kids was no big deal.

"Gururururu!"

With Rei's permission, Set happily growled as he started moving along with the children.

Their destination was a square.

A few years ago, a shop had stood here too. It belonged to someone who closed up shop and left Gilm, claiming their parent had fallen ill.

After various events, the shop was eventually demolished, and the area is now a slightly larger vacant lot.

Snow now covered the ground, and several snowdrifts sat at the edges of the square.

(It faces the main street, so you'd think someone would buy this land and open a shop or something.)

Seeing children already playing in the snow, I find myself thinking that.

But the fact that no new shop has been built suggests there must be some reason for it.

"Rei-nii-chan, Rei-nii-chan, come play with us too!"

One of the children playing with Set spotted Rei and called out.

Though Rei was feared among adventurers for various reasons, children didn't seem to care.

Then again, it probably helped that Rei didn't have the typical adventurer's bulky, muscular build. Instead, he was a slender, petite man with feminine features.

Anyway, since Rei didn't have anything particular to do, he figured it wouldn't hurt to play with the kids for once.

...Though Set's wide, round eyes pleading for him to join in probably played a big part too.

"So, what are we going to play?"

"Um, well... what do we play?"

The child who had invited Rei to play was now, for some reason, asking Rei what they should play.

"Well, you're the one who asked me to play. ...Hmm..."

There were four children in total.

All of them were looking at Rei expectantly, wondering how they would play.

But when asked what they should play, even Rei was stumped.

If there had been a slope here, they could have skied, sledded, or maybe even snowboarded.

(Come to think of it, we did skiing at school back in elementary school.)

Skiing suddenly reminded Rei of his elementary school days.

The elementary school Rei attended had a slope right near the school.

This slope, nearly 100 meters long, was where they skied during winter classes.

And naturally, on days we had lessons, we'd ski all the way to school...

(Wow, that brings back memories... But I can't exactly do that here.)

Though Rei felt nostalgic, there was no slope here suitable for skiing or sledding, so such antics were impossible.

Not that he owned any skis or such to begin with.

However, the association of skiing and slopes sparked an idea in Rei's mind.

It was something Rei had made countless times back in Japan.

He'd made it countless times since he was little.

"Do you know what a 'kamakura' is?"

"Kamakura? No idea."

"What's that?"

Of course, no one knew the word that came out of Rei's mouth.

"Alright. Well... first, we gather snow in the center of this plaza. We need a mountain of it."

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, it was clear now, but last night had brought a fair amount of snow.

There was a decent amount already piled up in the square, and shopkeepers along Gilm's main street had been gathering the snow in front of their own stores and bringing it over here.

(I see, so it also serves as a snow dumping ground.)

Just as a man who had loaded a cart full of snow was about to dump it, Rei spoke up.

"Could you wait a moment?"

"Huh? What is it?"

The man, who looked to be in his forties, responded, slightly surprised that Rei had spoken to him.

He probably never imagined that Rei, a celebrity, would speak to him.

"About that snow... if you're going to throw it away, I'd like you to bring it over here."

"Well, that's fine, but what are you going to do with it?"

"I thought I'd play with the kids a bit."

Hearing Rei's words, the man pulled his cart toward the center of the square where Rei and the others were gathered, pulling it from the entrance without seeming particularly interested in asking for further details.

The sound of the cart's wheels crunching through the snow echoed around them, revealing it was loaded with a considerable amount.

Reaching the center of the square, the man glanced around, asking with his eyes if this was okay. Seeing Rei nod, he tilted the cart and dumped the snow there.

It was unclear what exactly amused them, but the children cheered at the sight and ran around excitedly.

"Is this alright?"

"Yeah, thanks."

"No, no, it's nothing to be thankful for."

For the man, that was probably true.

All it meant was that the place to dump the snow was a little farther away, and the only extra effort needed was pushing the cart to the center of the square.

And it wasn't even a long distance—just a minute's walk at most—so it was only natural he answered so casually.

Watching the man walk away, Rei turned back to the children gathered around him and spoke again.

"Alright, listen up. Once you bring the snow here like this, you pile it up into a mountain like this. But make sure to pack it down firmly so it doesn't collapse right away."

"Yeah! Got it!"

One child said that, and everyone scattered off in all directions.

Hugging handfuls of snow, they brought it back to Rei and added it to the mound that had been started.

Of course, Rei and Set weren't just standing there watching silently.

They busily carried snow over and added it to the snow mountain.

The snow Rei and Set brought was, naturally, from places farther away.

They understood that just carrying the snow was tiring enough for the children.

...This is probably where their experience of shoveling snow endlessly back in Japan comes in handy.

Even though it was just the act of carrying snow, the children still carried it happily.

For them, this alone is probably fun enough.

Doing this naturally catches the attention of people walking down the main street, and a few more children join in.

Beyond the children, others also stopped, curiously watching as if to say, "Oh, Rei's up to something again."

After about twenty minutes, the pile of gathered snow was nearly a meter high.

(Well, it's a bit small, but it's not like I or Set are going in it, so it's fine. It should be no problem for kids.)

He could have made it bigger if he'd wanted to gather more, but considering the kids' stamina, he decided this size was sufficient.

"Alright, just to be safe, let's reinforce the sides a bit more like this. But don't make it too strong and collapse the snow mountain."

"Got it!"

As one of the kids said that, they slammed their fist into the snow pile... and the next moment, their wrist sank into the snow.

"...That's a bad example, so be careful."

"Yessir!"

The children answered in unison, patting the snow pile with their gloved hands to harden it.

With the number of children increasing, Rei and Set could only watch from the sidelines.

Once they confirmed the mound was sufficiently hardened, they next retrieved shovels from the Misty Ring.

Since civil engineering projects existed even in Elgin, shovels naturally existed too.

Rei recalled hearing somewhere before that some construction pros even carried shovels made of mithril...

Of course, the one Rei had was just an ordinary shovel.

(Should I have used it to compact the snow mountain? ...But we don't have that many shovels.)

Rei immediately dismissed the idea that had popped into his head and thrust the shovel into the snowdrift once more.

Probably because it was packed so tightly. Instead of the light feel of fresh snow, a heavy, crunchy sensation returned to his hands.

He dug swiftly, and once the hole was large enough for two children, he spoke.

"Now you two just get inside and carve it out with your hands."

"Huh? Can we borrow Rei-nii-chan's shovel?"

"...Physically, that's just not going to work. Ah, if you have a shovel, you could bring that. Or you can just chip away by hand. Figure it out yourselves. Once all of you can fit inside, the snow hut is complete."

"A snow hut?"

The one tilting her head in confusion was a girl around five years old.

"Yeah. Simply put, it's a house made of snow. Also called a snow house. I hear there are festivals in cold places where people spend the night inside them."

"No way! That's a lie! But it's snow! Snow!"

"Yeah, yeah, it's definitely cold!"

Some kids complained like that...

"Huh? What? It's warm!?"

Overcome by curiosity, the first child to enter the kamakura shouted in surprise.

Hearing this, the other kids looked utterly incredulous... but still, if their friend said so, one of them stepped inside the snow hut.

"Wow, it's true! Huh? How come!?"

"Because it blocks the cold wind from outside. And if it gets cold inside the snow hut..."

Rei stopped speaking mid-thought.

He realized there wasn't a charcoal brazier inside the Misty Ring, and a bonfire would be too hot, probably melting the snow.

"Hey, Rei, what's up?"

"Nothing. Anyway, since the wind can't get through, it's much warmer inside the snow hut than outside. Now that you know, start digging the hole. Just don't accidentally dig too deep and break through."

"Got it! I'll dig first!"

"Hold on, let me try too!"

"I wanna do it too!"

"Me too!"

The children dug away noisily, hollowing out the snow mountain.

It was just digging snow, but it seemed fun enough for them. Their shouts of joy echoed around them.

And after a while... a splendid snow hut had taken shape there.

"Wow, it's really warm in here!"

Hearing that voice from inside, Rei pulled out several skewers of roasted oak from the Misty Ring.

"Here, eat these too."

"Huh? Oh, skewers!"

"Hey, that's not fair! Give me some too, give me some skewers!"

Amidst the commotion, the children took the skewers and ate them with relish.

(If I'd grilled mochi over a charcoal grill, it would've felt more like a proper snow hut... but oh well)

Thinking that, Rei nodded contentedly at the kamakura she hadn't seen in so long.
