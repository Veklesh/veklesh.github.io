While Rei and the others were traveling through the hidden passage by carriage, the surface had grown considerably quieter than just moments before.

The reason for this quietness was undoubtedly the members of Jaya lying collapsed in front of the building leading to the underground facility.

Some were dead, others simply unconscious.

Some were still alive, but most were injured in some way and were hardly in a condition to move properly.

The faint moans rising from their lips echoed faintly in the now quiet surroundings.

"Grrrrr..."

Seeing no one else advancing toward the underground facility, Set, who had landed on the ground, growled contentedly.

But soon, Set's gaze shifted to a spot a short distance away.

There, Jaya soldiers who had gathered only now were watching for an opening in Set's guard, trying desperately to find a way to break through into the underground facility.

They probably understood that trying to force their way through was futile, given that soldiers stronger than themselves had been overwhelmed by Set.

"Grr?"

Amidst this, Set suddenly tilted his head in puzzlement.

He had spotted members of another faction present, besides the Jaya soldiers watching his movements.

Just because they were acting as a separate group didn't necessarily mean they were a different faction... Still, from Set's perspective, he instinctively understood they were different from the Jaya forces that had been attacking here just moments ago.

Moreover, considering there were only three of them moving, it was a stretch to call them a group.

Still, since he didn't know how they would act, Set kept a close watch, determined not to miss their movements.

"...Hey, I think I locked eyes with that griffin for a second. Was I imagining things?"

One of the trio dispatched by the Resistance muttered this, sounding almost afraid.

But being able to speak was actually the better outcome.

The other two had frozen completely the instant their eyes met Set's, unable to move.

Normally, making eye contact with Set wouldn't have caused such a reaction.

But seeing the dozens lying unconscious around Set... no, around the building leading to the underground facility, made it painfully clear just how formidable Set truly was.

Above all, there was not a speck of dirt on Set's fur or wings.

That meant he hadn't suffered a single injury in his fight with Jaya's soldiers.

For a high-rank monster like a griffin, that was probably to be expected.

After a dozen seconds passed, the others, except for the man who had spoken first, were finally able to speak.

"What now? We know what's happening over here. Should we head back already?"

"...Yeah. There's definitely a shortage of manpower over there too. If they'd known things were like this here, the three of us wouldn't have needed to come."

"That's hindsight. I never imagined the griffins would be this overwhelming. Anyway, if we're going back, let's get moving."

The men discussed this, but the situation shifted even as they spoke.

The Jaya soldiers, who had been waiting for an opening to storm the underground facility, suddenly moved as one.

They saw stronger fighters than themselves defeated by Set, lying on the ground. Yet they knew that if they didn't act now, they would undoubtedly face punishment from the organization.

Given that, it was only natural to cling to the faint hope offered by a choice that might allow survival, rather than certain death.

Indeed, a significant number of those defeated by Set were still alive.

In other words, they must have judged that attacking Set offered a higher probability of survival.

Moreover, if they succeeded in storming the underground facility against all odds, the reward from the organization would be enormous.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

"Let's do this! Let's do this! Let's do this!"

"Waaaaaaaaaaaah!"

Shouting like that, the Jaya soldiers charged forward, trying desperately to get inside the underground facility.

But of course, Set wasn't about to let that happen without a fight.

...Though, quite some time had already passed since Rei and the others had broken through inside.

So, even Set couldn't help but think that storming in now would be too late... Still, since it was a job Rei had asked him to do, he couldn't just let the Jaya soldiers pass through unopposed.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

As he let out a roar, fifteen wind arrows materialized around Set.

While the ice arrows, being made of ice, were difficult for the target to distinguish, the wind arrows were clearly more visible than the ice ones.

Being arrows made of wind, they were difficult to dodge once launched, though perhaps not so much if they were stationary.

At the very least, there was no way the soldiers gathered here now—hardly elite, merely numbers—could handle them.

The only saving grace was that it was a Wind Arrow, not an Ice Arrow.

Ice arrows are easier to distinguish than wind arrows.

However, in terms of mass, they are clearly superior to wind arrows.

Given that, in terms of the damage inflicted upon impact, the Ice Arrow surpasses the Wind Arrow.

Wind Arrows, too, are by no means lacking in offensive power when it comes to slicing through opponents and inflicting wounds.

In any case, whether Set chose not to use skills like Ice Arrow... or worse, Power Crash or Poison Claw, which would clearly be overkill against humans, was it because he saw through the low skill level of the attacking soldiers, or was it simply a whim?

Either way, Set's choice was fortunate for the men.

As he'd hoped, few sustained injuries that were fatal.

...Yes. Few, indeed.

Among the soldiers, some had their necks severed by Wind Arrows, collapsing to the ground in a spray of blood.

If they had used expensive potions, such men might have been saved... but unfortunately, none of the soldiers present possessed such potions.

If they were higher-ranking members of the organization, things might have been different.

In any case, seeing such sudden commotion, it was only natural that the Resistance members who came to check on the situation here were shaken.

"Hey, wait! Another fight just started out of nowhere!? What are we gonna do?! If this keeps up, we're gonna get dragged into it too, aren't we!?"

One of the three managed to shout out in a small voice, but the other two had no time to praise him.

All they could do now was hold their breath, watching the flow of the battle, determined not to miss what was happening.

"Shut up. Right now, we need to see how this fight plays out. ...Abelero, you go report this. We don't need all three of us here just to observe that fight."

"Well... damn it, fine. But you know this is dangerous, right? Are you sure this is okay?"

"I know it's dangerous. But that griffin is Rei's familiar, right? If so, it probably won't attack us. ...Probably."

The man wasn't speaking with absolute certainty either.

That's precisely why he kept repeating the word "probably."

Noticing this, Abellero ultimately said nothing more and left the scene.

After all, he needed to relay the information that fighting had broken out again in front of the underground facility.

Back when they were active as the Resistance, they couldn't even get this close to such a place... right next to the underground facility.

Even just the information obtained to reach this point was extremely valuable to the Resistance.

...However, given how extensively the underground facility had been attacked, it was unclear how valuable that information still was.

Still, he probably thought it might be useful for something.

After Abellero left, the remaining two watched the battle—one that could rightly be called a rout—while trying to calm themselves.

Fortunately, unlike the initial battle, it wasn't a fierce fight, so many of the soldiers had survived.

Still, most had at least one or two broken bones, making it difficult to escape under their own power.

Moreover, even if they managed to escape in that state, they would undoubtedly be sent back to the battlefield.

Knowing this, the wounded Jaya soldiers wouldn't be so careless as to return.

Some, despite being uninjured, even lay sprawled on the ground pretending to be hurt.

"Grrrrrrrrr!"

Set didn't want to kill his opponents needlessly either.

Setting aside those who attacked first, he knew the soldiers before him would lose their will to fight with even minor injuries, so he held back somewhat.

Using Wind Arrow was one example of this restraint.

Even the strike from his front paws was considerably restrained for Set.

"Dammit! Why is this monster here?! Hey, get more soldiers here! Not scum, but useful ones!"

The man in command shouted, but the soldier who heard the order inwardly spat out, "Don't be ridiculous."

(Doesn't he realize the competent ones are mostly already deployed? The best were guarding the underground facility in the first place! Since they broke through there, whoever was inside... whether killed or knocked out by Rei and the others, they're definitely incapacitated.)

More importantly, no matter how many lesser fighters they brought, he couldn't see how they could handle Set, who was right in their line of sight.

Since preventing Jaya's people from entering the underground facility was the top priority, they didn't stray far from the entrance they were guarding.

Some tried long-range attacks with bows instead... but attacks of ice, wind, and water spheres came back, and the bow user suffered a severe injury, his arm severed.

As a result, there was nothing Jaya's soldiers could do now.

If this were a normal army, retreating quietly might have been an option.

But unfortunately, Jaya is a dark organization... a gathering of criminals.

Given that, issues like pride and power struggles are far stronger than they would be in a regular army. ...Of course, even in a proper army, there are probably those who sacrifice subordinates as pawns for advancement.

In any case, the fact that the highest-ranking individual present possessed that type of personality was unfortunate for everyone here—except the Resistance and Set.

And misfortune tends to pile up. The man in command had earned his position by fawning over his superiors.

If things continued like this, he too would be used up and discarded.

Having made that judgment, the man confirmed that everyone around him was focused on Set... and then, with a dagger drawn from his coat, slit his superior's throat.

It wasn't sharp enough to be completely silent, but luckily, no one present was looking at him or his superior, so no one noticed his movement.

Hiding his boss's body in nearby rubble, the man shouted.

"Lord Rajada has been killed by a griffin attack! Therefore, I will take command from this point on!"

The soldiers' gazes turned toward the man who shouted those words.

Naturally, it was inconceivable that Rajada, the man who had been issuing reckless orders moments ago, had died from Set's attack.

But when asked how they could survive from here, it was clearly the unanimous opinion of everyone present that this man taking command was the better choice.

No one voiced any particular complaints.

Had Rajada possessed even a shred of respect among the men, their attitude might have been different. But Rajada was the sort who fawned over his superiors and lorded it over his subordinates – a man who could never command any real respect.

"Everyone, retreat! Retreat! That griffin is only guarding the underground facility! As long as we don't attack it, there's no problem!"

At those words, everyone began retreating as if they'd been waiting for that signal.

The two Resistance men naturally witnessed this scene. Even those who had been lying on the ground mere seconds ago quickly got up and retreated, leaving the Resistance men stunned.

"What is this feeling...?"

"I don't know. Anyway, Jaya's retreated. What should we do? Retreat too? Or stay here a little longer and see what happens?"

"Let's stay put for now. Something else might happen."

That's what they decided in the end.
