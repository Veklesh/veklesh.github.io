From the perspective of Rei riding on Set's back as they flew through the sky, the ground below appeared as a vast green carpet with a single line running through it.

Needless to say, that single line was a highway. Thinking that without it, the landscape would be entirely green, he felt just a little regret.

Looking up at the sky, a warm, spring-like sun shone, with white clouds floating by.

"Looking at it like this, it doesn't really feel like a forest at all... Well, I guess that's because we're flying."

"Guru?"

Set turned around, asking "What is it?" at Rei's muttered words, spoken as if he hadn't meant to say them.

"Nothing, really. More importantly... Ah, that must be the forest from the rumors."

They were flying at an altitude of about 100 meters, and it was Set doing the flying.

Within mere minutes of taking off from outside Gilm, the forest came into view.

Why did they identify it as the rumored forest? The reason was simple.

Having flown around Gilm countless times before, Rei and Set knew immediately when they saw a forest they didn't recognize.

Of course, they hadn't flown during the winter or even since spring arrived, having stayed in Gilm the whole time.

But even so, it was certain that a forest of the scale Rei was now seeing had never existed in this vicinity.

They certainly wouldn't have forgotten a forest this large.

It was located quite far off the main road, but if Rei's memory served him correctly, this spot should have been grassland.

"Could it be... that grassland somehow turned into a forest due to some anomaly...?"

Under normal circumstances, it would be highly improbable, but in this world where magic existed, it couldn't be completely ruled out.

"Even so... it's wider than I thought."

Rei was referring to the forest's scale.

It wasn't quite as vast as Gilm, but even at a glance, it easily covered over a square kilometer.

"Gururu?"

However, Set, observing the forest from above, let out a puzzled growl.

With his keen senses and abilities to perceive sixth senses and magic, Set had likely noticed the forest's unnaturalness—the complete absence of monsters, let alone any kind of animal.

Rei didn't possess senses as acute as Set's, but even he felt something off about it.

"Well, if both me and Set feel something's off, it's definitely not a normal forest. ...Though it's not normal for a forest like this to grow in just a few months either."

"Grr?"

What should we do? Should we go down? Set looked at Rei, but Rei shook his head.

"Nah, I'd like to get down and check it out too, but our main goal is to find out if this forest even exists. ...Still, I don't see any sign of it spreading out."

According to what Rei had heard, the forest was supposed to either grow or move, but looking down from above like this, it certainly didn't appear to be doing either.

As Rei scanned the ground, he suddenly spotted something glinting in the sunlight between the trees.

At first, he thought it might be water... a river or something, but there was no river flowing here to begin with.

Well, maybe a small stream existed somewhere... but regardless, that glimmer caught Rei's attention. It was near the edge of the forest, only about ten meters in from the entrance. Changing his original plan, Rei decided to descend to the ground.

"Set, sorry, but I need you to come down to the ground after all."

"GRRRRRR!"

Perhaps because Set was already intrigued by the sudden appearance of the forest, he flapped his wings toward the ground without hesitation upon hearing Rei's words.

Landing almost silently, he stared intently at the forest before him.

Descending to the ground would allow him to understand the forest's abnormality more clearly.

Despite its vast size, there was almost no sign of life.

The only faint signs of life were birds perched on branches.

And even those birds were far from numerous.

"Grrr..."

At Set's cry, Rei also focused his awareness, ready for anything that might happen.

He drew the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring, readying himself to counter any attack immediately, then stepped into the forest... toward the spot where something had glinted from above.

"Set, stay alert just in case something happens. ...A forest this size suddenly appearing out of nowhere. There's definitely something here."

"Gurrrr!"

In response to Rei's words, Set growled reassuringly.

The gaps between the forest trees were fairly wide, so even Set, who was about three meters long, could move through the woods without any particular trouble.

Though Rei and the others had advanced tensely, unexpectedly, they arrived at their destination—the spot where the sparkle had been—without encountering anything unusual.

It was only about ten meters from the edge of the forest, so it was quite close... but given how tense they'd been, Rei honestly felt a bit let down.

"This..."

What caught Rei's eye was the blade of a dagger.

It lay just below the gap between nearby tree branches, so the sunlight reflected off its blade unobstructed.

But what surprised Rei even more than the dagger was the traces of a campfire nearby.

That meant someone had camped here, and that someone was none other than Slesha and the others.

"So what does that mean? One night... no, since we don't know when the commotion started, at most one night? Anyway, does that mean the forest spread this far in that amount of time?"

This campfire site was located about ten meters into the forest.

And Rei had heard that they had camped at the edge of the forest, not deep within it.

Given that, this forest must have grown at least ten meters overnight.

(Well, I don't know if it's encroaching or if the forest itself is moving. Either way, it doesn't make much difference... It's dangerous either way.)

For now, the Misty Ring stowed away the various items scattered on the ground—sleeping bags, backpacks, and other belongings—belonging to Slesha and her party.

When Slesha and the others woke to their comrade's screams in the night, they must have rushed into the forest without time to prepare.

Most of the belongings left behind were just as they'd been when Slesha and the others fell asleep.

Of course, Rei couldn't know that much, but there was something else he could tell. Namely...

"No signs of the luggage being rummaged through, huh?"

Yes, the supplies should have included preserved foods like dried meat and hardened bread.

As active adventurers, having emergency rations prepared was only natural. They should have eaten something here before sleeping and had breakfast ready as well.

Despite that, the luggage showed absolutely no signs of being rummaged through.

Even if there was no food, monsters or animals would naturally have tampered with or investigated anything unfamiliar.

"If that's the case, then there really are no monsters or animals in this forest."

I'd heard about it from the receptionist, and entering the forest like this had given me a vague premonition.

But even so, being confronted with such clear evidence left me no choice but to accept it with undeniable conviction.

"...Grr."

Gazing at the abandoned campsite, Set let out a lonely growl.

He was probably thinking of Slesha's companions who had died in this forest.

In any case, they collected all the belongings left in the forest.

While ownership of such items naturally belongs to whoever finds them... for Rei, these were things he had no use for himself.

After all, he already possessed far more numerous and far more advanced tools than anything contained within these belongings.

"Well, this much is fine."

The luggage's destination was, naturally, Slesha.

Since it was unnecessary for Rei to begin with, he figured that much kindness was acceptable.

Having lost his entire party overnight, whether he continued as an adventurer or abandoned that path, this luggage should prove useful.

If he chose to continue adventuring, as tools for that journey.

And if he quit adventuring, he could sell it in Gilm for immediate living expenses.

Either way, it was something that wouldn't hurt to have.

(Is it because I've started forming parties myself that I think like this? ...Well, I have no intention of doing anything like destroying the Crimson Wings.)

Then again, if we're talking about that, Slesha and the others didn't exactly choose to wipe out their party either—though Slesha survived.

No, anyone who would deliberately try to wipe out their own party must be someone with some strange ideas.

"Anyway, business is done. Fortunately... well, 'fortunately' might not be quite the right word, but it's still good we confirmed there are no animals or monsters in the forest."

"Grrr?"

Is that so? Set tilted his head. While petting him, Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah. All that's left is to return to Gilm and report this. ...Come to think of it, we didn't set a reward in advance for this designated request... Well, I suppose we can leave that negotiation to Marina or someone."

Rei muttered, though in truth, there was probably no one better suited for negotiations than Marina, the former guildmaster.

For this type of request... especially a designated request, determining a reasonable reward amount was something the former guildmaster Marina could easily judge.

That said, monetary rewards held little appeal for Rei.

"...Still, he was saying something about being devoured by the forest, but nothing happened in the end. Was it because of Set?"

Regardless of who was behind this incident, facing a griffin like Set—especially a rare species equivalent to an S-rank monster—Rei couldn't imagine anyone making a foolish move.

"It is a forest, so burning it down with my magic would probably settle things neatly... but then, if it was the work of an unknown monster, there's a chance I'd burn the magic stone too. I want to figure out a way around that... but no, thinking about it now is pointless."

He shook his head, pushing the thoughts aside for now.

For now, informing them about this forest was the top priority.

"Well then, let's go. You don't want to stay in this forest forever either, right, Set?"

"Grr."

Set nodded at Rei's words.

Sleeping in the shade of the trees was one of Set's happiest moments.

But even that shade, in a forest like this, offered no peace of mind.

For Set, napping in Marina's garden under the direct spring sunlight was far more appealing.

Moving away from the spot where they had camped... he suddenly noticed a broken branch lying on the ground.

The branch, broken partway, must have snapped when Set moved.

(A tree branch... Well, might it be useful for something? If examining this reveals even a little something, that'd be a bonus.)

He decided, picked it up, and stored it directly in Misty Ring.

"Grrrrrrr"

Nodding to Set, who growled urgently to leave the forest quickly, Rei followed the dog as he led the way.

Since the spot where the campsite had been was right near the edge of the forest, they emerged from it quickly.

Once out, they looked back at the forest they'd just left.

From the outside, it looked like nothing more than an ordinary forest, showing no signs of anything unusual.

But in reality, this forest had swallowed up countless adventurers.

It was far from ordinary.

(Well, even in a normal forest, if you let your guard down, you could die in an instant from a monster attack... but this forest is nothing like that.)

Watching the forest, Rei murmured.

Having grown up near mountains in Japan, the complete absence of any living creatures' presence in this forest felt deeply unsettling to Rei.

Of course, back in Japan, he hadn't been able to truly sense such things either, but even then, he could hear animal cries and it wasn't impossible to see them.

"Grrr?"

What's wrong? Rei snapped back to reality at the sound of Set's growl and shook his head, signaling it was nothing.

"Well, anyway, the request is done. Shall we go?"

"Guru!"

Straddling Set, who purred happily, Rei soared into the sky with him.

The forest grew smaller before their eyes.

While both were green carpets in that sense, the grasslands and the forest trees were still different shades of green.

Considering Set's flight speed, they soon left the forest behind... and eventually caught sight of Gilm. At its main gate, Marina, Vihera, and Byune were waving toward Rei and Set.

...Unlike Marina and Vihera, who waved with smiles, Byune waved with her usual expressionless face.
