"Huh? That Gamelion is..."

After several minutes of searching for Gamelion's form from the air while riding on Set's back,

They spotted Gamelion charging at the goblin horde, fangs bared.

Goblins appeared everywhere, and this grassland was no exception.

To Gamelion, goblins were nothing more than weak, tasteless meat.

Still, the way it attacked the goblins right before Rei and Set's eyes—was it simply in the mood for it, or was it just hungry enough that goblins would do?

Whatever the reason, Gamelion attacked the goblin, tearing it apart with his fangs, slashing it with his ears, smashing it with his tail, and crushing it under his massive body.

There was no way an ordinary goblin could stand a chance against Gamelion.

Coordination might have changed things, but goblins were creatures that fled the moment they found themselves at a disadvantage, even when attacking in groups.

There was no way they could stand their ground against Gamelion, fight, and take on the role of rear guard.

"That Gamelion... I wonder if he's been attacked by other adventurers. ...Set, I'm counting on you."

"GRRR!"

Hearing Rei's plea, Set returned to the ground.

His destination wasn't the carriage... but the spot where Beast's Fang had fought Gamelion a short while ago.

Normally, it would be correct to descend to the carriage's location, wary of carriage thieves as before. But now, having witnessed Gamelion attacking the goblins, it was different.

If he took too long, Gamelion, having defeated the goblins, would likely leave the area.

He'd probably eat the goblin as bait, but it was also highly likely he wouldn't do something like that in such a conspicuous place.

Or, like how the Beast's Fang had beaten them to it earlier, another Gamelion-hunting party might get there first.

Considering all that, he judged it best to inform them of the new Gamelion's location as quickly as possible.

Of course, before descending into the woods, he glanced down at the carriage from above to confirm everything was in order.

Descending from above the forest, the scent of blood drifted up.

Rei immediately recognized it as the blood of the Gamelion Ray had cleaved in two, and without any particular sign of agitation, he floated down to the ground.

The ones who were startled by this sight were the ones who had been dismembering the Gamelion.

It was only natural. They had just finished processing the entrails when Set suddenly descended from above.

Indeed, the members of Beast's Fang swiftly assumed combat stances. Even Rent, who wasn't particularly familiar with Rei and Set, likewise drew his longsword.

The former guerrilla fighters who had some history with Rei tensed for just an instant, but relaxed immediately upon realizing it was Set carrying Rei who had arrived from the sky.

"Rei-san, coming back with Set means...?"

"Set-chan, you did your best!"

The former guerrilla fighter and Johanna each called out.

Though even in those greetings, certain differences were already apparent.

"Ah, I found the Gamelion. It's attacking goblins right now. Perfect timing to take it down. So, what about this Gamelion over here?"

"Ah, yes. As you can see, the internal organs have already been processed. Only the body remains..."

"Got it."

After exchanging a few brief words, Rei approached the Gamelion corpse that Rent and the others had decided to take. The instant he touched it, half of the corpse that had been there just moments before vanished as if it had never been there.

The fact that only half of the Gamelion remained only amplified the sense of unease it instilled in those around them.

"What is this..."

The members of Beast's Fang, especially Chrono, were particularly shocked.

They had seen Rei draw the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring, but the size difference between the Death Scythe and Gamelion's half-body was stark.

That's precisely why they stared wide-eyed, shifting their gaze between Rei and the half of Gamelion left on the ground.

While the Beast's Fang members stood frozen in shock, Rei spoke without seeming particularly concerned.

"So, I guess we can consider things settled here for now? Is there anything else I need to discuss with these guys?"

"Huh? Oh, no, nothing really."

"I see. Well then, we'll take our leave here. Come on, let's go. Set, you take care of escorting the carriage."

"GRRRR!"

Responding to Rei's call with a roar of "Leave it to me!", Set simply walked away without another word.

"Ah, Set-chan! You're leaving!?"

Glancing at Johanna's disappointed cry, Rei addressed the others present.

"Come on, you're going to look for Gamelion, right? If you keep wasting time like this, other parties will beat you to it, just like they did with Gamelion here. Is that okay with you?"

"No, it's not. Then, we'll need your guidance."

Rent snapped back to reality at Rei's words and declared, the others nodding in agreement.

Everyone except Johanna, that is.

"Well then, that's why we're leaving. See you around if we meet again."

Saying only that briefly, Rei pulled Rent and Johanna out of the woods.

The Beast's Fang members watching them depart finally snapped back to reality once Rei and the others had vanished.

"Well, for now... should we look for Gamelion too? Just this one feels a bit too few."

Chrono muttered, staring at the Gamelion corpse, slashed to pieces with half its body missing. Hearing this, the others agreed.

Though it had been a crisis for them, they were glad to have obtained at least some Gamelion. But honestly, they would have preferred a whole Gamelion if possible.

"I wish we could have gone with Rent and the others. Then we could have found Gamelions for sure, and bringing back the ones we killed would have been easier with an item box..."

"Jonah!"

Jonah said, sounding a bit envious, but Shant, who had been listening nearby, rudely cut him off.

"Asking for help from such a little kid? Don't you have any pride? We're the proud Rank C party, Beast's Fang!"

"...Look. Sure, we're a Rank C party, but Rei-san is a Rank B adventurer, right? And she's solo. Whether she's small or young, you have to admit a strong opponent is strong. Shant, you saw how powerful Rei-san is, didn't you? She split that Gamelion—which should have had strong resistance to slashes—in two with just one swing of her scythe?"

"Guhm... That's true, but..."

In terms of being shown true strength, Shant was probably more shocked than the others.

After all, he'd harbored the belief that his opponent was smaller than himself... yet here was someone who effortlessly cleaved in two the Gamelion they couldn't defeat alone, only managing to take it down with Rent and the others' help.

Then Chrono added, as if pressing the point.

"And Johanna and the others are mostly Rank C adventurers, right? I hear they were adventuring in the imperial capital of the Bestir Empire or something?"

When told they were adventurers of the same rank as themselves, Shant couldn't voice any further complaints.

"Besides, this year's Gamelion only appeared because of Rei, right? Considering that..."

"Enough, Jonah."

Jonah still had more to say, but Chrono cut him off, leaving him unable to continue.

He trusted her as their party leader, and personally, he had feelings for her.

"...Alright. Then let's go find the other Gamelions. We can't spot them from the air without Set, but with our beastfolk's keen sense of smell, we should be able to find them easier than human adventurers."

With that, Chrono accepted Jona's words and turned her gaze toward Glin, who was also a thief.

Grin, receiving that look, nodded slightly and began walking to search for a new Gamelion.

Shant hoisted the half-eaten Gamelion corpse onto his shoulders and carried it toward the cart.

And so, the members of the Beast's Fang set out once more in search of the next Gamelion.

"There it is."

With Rei's muttered words, Rent, Johanna, and the others behind him followed his gaze.

At the end of their gaze, the last goblin had just been sliced open by Gamelion's ear as it passed by, collapsing to the ground.

Having finished off the last goblin, Gamelion plunged its fangs into the goblin's corpse.

"Ugh, Gamelion really is a glutton. How can he eat goblin flesh?"

Rent muttered in a disgusted tone.

Johanna and the others nodded in agreement.

"If he's such a glutton that he eats goblin meat, why is Gamelion's meat so damn delicious?"

One of the former rangers muttered, and the others nodded in agreement.

Rei nodded in agreement, but took a step back.

"Well, as promised, we found the Gamelion. Unlike last time, no other party beat us to it. You could say everything's perfectly set up. I'll watch from here. Do your best and take down that Gamelion!"

"Got it, got it. Didn't exactly shine in that last fight, did I? I'm gonna go all out here!"

Rent said, equipping the shield he'd been carrying on his back to his left hand and the longsword to his right.

Though he left unsaid exactly who he was trying to impress.

"Well then, Rei. We're heading out too."

Johanna, one of the "someone" in question, seemed completely oblivious to Rent's appeal as she gripped her spear.

The three former guerrilla fighters each wielded a battle axe, halberd, and hammer respectively.

Normally, they would wield weapons more familiar to their hands, like longswords or spears, but this enemy was a Gamelion.

Their fur possessed strong resistance to slashing attacks.

Unless you had skills like Rei's and a powerful magic item like the Death Scythe, it was simply impossible for Johanna and the others to slash through a Gamelion.

That's precisely why Rent and Johanna, wielding longswords, would act as decoys, while the other three would defeat Gamelion with their attacks.

"Let's go! Wooooooooo!"

Catching it off guard would be ideal, but naturally, even while eating, the Gamelion remained completely alert.

Having evolved from a rabbit into a monster, its hearing was remarkably sharp.

That meant catching it off guard was impossible unless it was intensely focused on something else.

That's precisely why Rent, wielding the shield and possessing the highest defense, stepped forward, shouting loudly to draw Gamelion's attention.

"GAAAAAAH!"

Gamelion roared a warning at Rent.

But Rent took that cry as an opportunity and shouted even louder.

"Come on, you bastard!"

He swings the shield in his left hand dramatically, taunting Gamelion.

Johanna stood beside Rent, spear in hand.

Perhaps, for Rent, this was the peak of his happiness.

Of course, Gamelion had no intention of considering such things. He kicked off the ground, closing the distance to Rent.

A speed unimaginable for a body over three meters tall... but to Rent, it didn't seem that fast.

This was because the Gamelion he had fought alongside the Beast's Fang members in the forest had been superior in every way to the Gamelion standing before him now.

Especially in agility—the one Rent had faced before was undeniably faster than the one before him now.

Gamelion charged straight ahead, seemingly intent on sinking its fangs into Rent instead of the goblin... but just before those fangs touched Rent's body, they were blocked by a metal shield that appeared before him.

A high-pitched clang echoed as the fangs and metal shield collided.

Seeing the Gamelion pause momentarily after colliding with the shield, Johanna immediately thrust her spear forward.

The spear's speed was considerable, and Johanna thought the tip had pierced straight into Gamelion's right shoulder. Yet, in that instant, Gamelion had already leaped sideways, leaving only a faint resistance behind.

"Dammit! I knew it, but that burst of speed is a pain!"

Quickly pulling her spear back, Johanna spat out her frustration at Gamelion's annoyance, even if it was accidental that he'd used Rent as a shield.

As for Rent, once Johanna circled around to his left side where he held his shield, he couldn't swing his longsword.

"GAAAAAAHHHHHH!"

But Gamelion was far from having any room to breathe either.

He likely hadn't expected his fang strike to be blocked so easily.

He understood the two before him were troublesome opponents... The next instant, he felt a sudden impact to his side and leapt away.

Mid-air, he glanced back at where he had been standing. There stood a human man wielding a halberd.

Gamelion roared in disgust, but seeing another man charging toward his intended landing spot, battle axe raised, he shouted in frustration.

"GAAAAAH!"

He lunged, aiming to sink his fangs into the battle-axe-wielding man waiting to strike at his landing spot... But spotting the hammer-wielding man behind him, Gamelion sensed danger. Yet mid-air, he could only twist slightly before crashing straight into the men.
