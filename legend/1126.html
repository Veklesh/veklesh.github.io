Why does he oppose Ryubrand and Daldora? And why does he disapprove of using the Tira Tree—which preys on monsters and harms no humans—to protect Gosh?

Originally, Rei had no intention of actively getting involved in the conflict unfolding in Gosh.

His primary reason for being here was to wait for the troublesome merchants involved in the Twilight Spear affair to settle down.

Unless he was threatened like he was with the Sand Thieves, he intended to simply watch from the sidelines.

In fact, when asked which side he would take during the banquet with Ryubrand, he ultimately answered that he would side with neither.

Still, the reason Rei asked Augusto this question was because this recent attack was significant.

This incident wasn't the work of Daldora, nor was it Ryubrand's... and certainly not Augusto's doing.

Rei could foresee that leaving this incident, seemingly the work of a third party, unresolved would undoubtedly lead to troublesome consequences.

Given that, he felt he needed to get involved. And if he was going to get involved, he absolutely had to understand the root cause of the conflict between Augusto and Daldora.

That was the thought behind his question.

...If Augusto opposed using the Tira Tree for selfish reasons, Rei might even side with Daldora.

Augusto must have understood Rei's intentions behind that question. He fixed his gaze on Rei, then let out a small sigh before speaking.

"Using the Tira Tree as a barrier. If I think about it normally, I suppose it's a reasonable method. Well, the Tira Tree doesn't react to humans, so there is the problem that sand pirates or anyone with something to hide could freely come and go in Gosh."

"Surely you weren't opposing it just for that reason? It's not like there's no way to deal with that."

"True. But that's precisely why I can't simply agree to it. As you know, the Kingdom of Solrain is weak, and the adventurers in Gosh aren't exactly high-ranking either."

Zalust's brow furrowed slightly at Augusto's words.

For Zarust, a native of Gosh, Augusto's words must have been painful to hear.

But what Augusto was saying was undeniably true.

The guards stationed in Gosh weren't exactly elite.

Given that situation, if repairs to the wall covering Gosh—using the Tira trees as a barrier—were neglected, it was only natural that the adventurers would be relied upon.

"Well, I get that that's a pretty big deal. But that's not all, is it?"

Rei cut off Augusto, who was about to say more.

Receiving Rei's words, Augusto halted the stream of words pouring from his mouth and fixed his gaze intently on Rei.

Several minutes passed like this. Finally, Augusto took a small, deep breath before speaking again.

"Are you sure? If you listen to this story, you'll have to hear it through to the end."

Augusto stated, as if to emphasize his point.

Behind him, Zarust nodded silently.

"Now then, about why I oppose the use of the Tira Tree..."

In one of the rooms of Augusto's mansion. Only Rei, Augusto, and Zarust were present there now.

Realizing the conversation about to begin was work-related, Kathy sensed her presence would be disruptive and left the room of her own accord.

"Seems?"

"Yes. We couldn't possibly test it ourselves, so this part comes from documents left by our ancestors... When Tira trees grow denser than a certain point, they apparently start attacking people."

Tira trees, said to attack only monsters and not humans, would start attacking humans too.

Hearing this, Rei's eyes widened in shock.

"Is that even possible?"

"Ah... yeah, I see."

Rei nodded in vague understanding.

While Tira trees normally feed only on monsters, when they grow densely, the number of monsters available to them naturally decreases.

If that happened, they'd need to find some other means to obtain nutrients, and humans were probably chosen as that alternative source.

"...I heard they don't attack humans, but was that only true when there were sufficient monsters as food?"
