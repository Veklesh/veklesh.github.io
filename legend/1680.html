"Huh? Rei? What are you doing here at this hour? Didn't you say you'd come around evening?"

It was Pastra who called out to Rei as he arrived at Garanka.

He appeared to be heading toward the sandy beach with a harpoon in hand, as if he were about to go fishing.

Rei immediately realized—as the one who had made the request—that this was likely because he had promised to buy every fish Rei caught.

As Pastra asked curiously, Rei nodded, looking slightly annoyed.

“Yeah, that was the plan. But Lord Daskar of Gilm asked me to do him a small favor regarding that pirate I captured.”

“……You can talk to the lord directly? That’s amazing.”

Though surprised that he could speak directly with the lord, Pastra seemed uninterested in how exactly Rei had managed to talk to Daskar, and that’s how she responded.

Then again, living in a rural village like this, opportunities to see magic items aren’t exactly plentiful, so perhaps that’s only natural.

“Anyway, I need to see the slave trader. Do you know where he is?”

“Oh, the slave trader lives in the same house as the guards, so if you go there, he’ll probably be there. …Look, it’s that building over there.”

Rei looked in the direction Pastra was pointing, but there were several similar houses lined up there.

Of course, the exact locations were probably different… but it was the kind of difference you wouldn’t notice unless you got up close.

“Which one?” Rei was about to ask for a more precise location, but when he heard someone calling Pastra’s name from the beach, he realized he couldn’t keep her waiting any longer.

“Got it. So it’s one of the houses over there, then. I’ll go over and ask around. Pastra, you go fishing. Catch as many fish as you can.”

“Leave it to me. I’ll catch enough fish for you to never run out.”

Saying that, Pastra walked away with a confident smile.

(Does he actually have confidence in his fishing skills? Well, he lives in a fishing village, so it makes sense he’d be good at it.)

The more fish Rei catches, the happier Rei is.

In other words, Pastra’s hard work is a huge benefit for Rei.

After cheering Pastra on as he walked away, Rei headed toward the house used by the guards and slave traders.

It was a cluster of houses, and just as Rei had expected, he couldn’t tell which one the guards were in…

“Oh, Rei?”

Just then, a guard emerges from one of the houses and looks visibly surprised to see Rei.

Since he had just stepped outside intending to question the pirates, only to find the very person responsible for their capture standing right there, it would be unreasonable to expect him not to be surprised.

“What’s going on? I heard you weren’t supposed to come until this evening.”

“Ah, the Lord of Gilm asked me to do something. It seems he wants to buy up all the pirates I captured and turned into slaves. So, I came to ask the slave trader to handle that.”

“...The pirates, in Gilm?”

At Rei’s words, the guard wore a puzzled expression.

As a guard, the man knew exactly where Gilm was located.

There was no sea nearby, nor were there any major rivers capable of supporting large ships in the vicinity of Gilm.

Why would someone in Gilm—and in the position of a lord, no less—want pirate slaves?

Though he had serious doubts, he decided it was best not to dwell on such matters given his position, so he didn’t pry further and changed the subject.

“Lilrose is inside the house. Shall I go get her?”

“That would be a great help.”

Recalling that “Lirose” was the slave trader’s name, Rei asked the guard.

The guard immediately tried to go inside the house...

“There’s no need for that. I could hear the conversation from inside the house.”

The slave trader... Lilrose... appeared from inside the house, saying that.

“You were saying you wanted to buy that slave, weren’t you? We’re perfectly fine with that, but transporting the slave isn’t free, you know?”

Lirose’s words weren’t meant to rip off Rei—or Gilm—but were simply an undeniable fact.

Even back when Rei was in Japan, shipping fees were required to transport goods.

Naturally, here in Elgin as well, delivering a slave as cargo isn’t free.

Well, if someone were to come directly from Gilm to transport them, it wouldn’t cost Lilrose a thing.

But Gilm is already short-handed as it is, so he doesn’t have the luxury of sending someone all the way here.

Or they could ask Rei to make the trip… but considering the number of pirates, it would require multiple round trips. And above all, Rei knew it was selfish of him, but he didn’t want to do something like putting pirates in a Set Cage.

(Besides, Lord Daskar didn’t ask me to handle that part either.)

For Daskar, too, Rei—and Rei’s companions—are extremely reliable people.

Rei and his group play a very significant role in the expansion of Gilm.

He probably felt it would be a terrible waste to use such people for transporting slaves.

That said, to be honest, Daskar had hoped they would bring him back with them, even if just for a little while, when they returned from this vacation.

“I know. Just getting from here to Gilm involves all sorts of dangers.”

Even if we stick to the main road, it doesn’t mean monsters won’t appear.

What’s more, bandits and the like are everywhere, and whether you encounter them or not depends largely on luck.

That said, the bandits around Sabrusta have been nearly wiped out by Rei and his group, so it’s become somewhat safer to travel than it was just a short while ago.

However, since the area around Sabrusta remains an ideal hunting ground for bandits, there’s no doubt they’ll start gathering there again before long.

Cautious bandits will likely be on their guard, but those whose sole aim is to make easy money don’t have the sense to be so vigilant.

To prevent that, we’d have to keep defeating the bandits around Sabrusta on a regular basis… but it’s doubtful we’ll be able to pull that off.

In the worst-case scenario, we cannot rule out the possibility that adventurers, as before, will be dispatched by Gilm to take on requests to defeat the bandits.

Of course, if that were to happen, it would be an absolute blow to the pride of Sabrusta’s guild and guards.

After all, failing to even defeat the bandits near the town where they live is tantamount to being called out on it.

“What are you going to do about that?”

“Hire escorts to travel with you. Naturally, it seems Lord Daskar will cover all the expenses—both for hiring the escorts and for transporting the slaves.”

“...Are you serious?”

Lirose shot a stunned look at Rei.

She had thought she might be reimbursed for some of the travel expenses, but even so, at best, it would be about half. She had assumed she’d have to cover the rest out of her own pocket.

But when told that Daskar would cover all travel expenses, it was impossible not to be astonished by such unbelievably generous treatment.

If anything, Lilrose found herself questioning why Daskar would go to such lengths to acquire a slave from her.

If he simply wanted a slave, there were slave traders in Gilm as well.

So why go out of his way to come to me? Though she felt this doubt, there was only one conclusion she could draw.

(Does the slave have to be a pirate?)

Yes, that was it.

He couldn’t possibly imagine that the lord of Gilm himself would have any business with him.

That left only one possibility: he must have business with the pirates I’d enslaved.

Of course, Lirose also knew that the pirates he had enslaved were decent—at least compared to the truly ruthless pirates.

Even so, if “decent” was the only criterion, there were plenty of other slaves like that.

If we’re talking about debt slaves rather than the kind known as criminal slaves, there should be quite a few slaves with no character issues.

(If that’s the case, then… I suppose I should assume that Gilm needs sailors for some reason… No, is there any need for me to be thinking about unnecessary things here?)

It was true that I was curious about what I didn’t know, but I knew several people in Lilrose’s circle who had stuck their noses into things they shouldn’t have out of curiosity and ended up dead as a result.

To avoid such a fate, one must maintain a proper distance in one’s interactions.

(Besides, if I can get to Gilm on someone else’s dime, there’s no doubt this would be a huge business opportunity.)

To reach Gilm, located on the frontier, one would normally need to hire a skilled adventurer.

And, naturally, hiring a skilled adventurer comes with a hefty fee.

Since the lord of Gilm covers that fee, you should be able to arrive in Gilm safely as long as you don’t make a major blunder.

Once in Gilm, there are plenty of goods unique to the frontier.

If you bring those back and sell them, you could make a small fortune—no, you could probably earn even more than that.

After quickly running the numbers in his head, Rilose eventually nodded in response to Rei’s words.

“I understand. I’ll take that job. There are a few things I’d like to ask about, but I suppose it’s best not to bring them up.”

“I suppose so. I think that’s the wise thing to do.”

If it’s the Daskar Rei knows, he probably wouldn’t mind being probed about various things.

But that was merely an assumption; he had no way of knowing what moves the Daskar would actually make.

After all, there’s always the possibility—though not entirely impossible—that Rei’s prediction could be wrong, and that the Daskar might take drastic measures if they took offense at his probing.

“I understand about the travel expenses, but what about the price of the slaves?”

“...To be honest, I don’t know what to tell you about that. I’ve sold bandits and the like to slave traders before, but I’ve never actually bought any slaves myself.”

It wasn’t that he disliked slaves; in Rei’s case, he simply had enough ability to manage just fine without them.

Furthermore, aside from his strength, he possessed many magical items, which meant he simply didn’t need slaves.

For that very reason, he had absolutely no idea what specific prices slaves were actually selling for.

“Hmm. In that case, I’ll need to look into that once I actually get to Gilm. Though, considering how well I’m being treated, I suppose they’ll have to give me a decent discount.”

“I suppose so. After all, you bought those guys for a pretty low price, didn’t you? If that’s the case, it’d be a big help if they’d sell them to us cheaply.”

“No, no. We’re in business, after all. A small discount is one thing, but we can’t exactly go on a full-scale price-cutting spree.”

Lirose told him that, as a slave trader, he could never do such a thing.

Rei thought for a moment... He decided that anything he said beyond this point would be pointless, and that the price negotiations would be handled by Daskar—or, more precisely, by Daskar’s expert subordinates—so he stopped butting in.

“I won’t say anything more about the price either. So, when are you leaving for Gilm?”

“Well, after we return to Jubis and make various preparations… the twentieth. No, it might be a little later than that.”

Rei was about to say something about the twenty-day estimate, but when he considered that they had no means of transportation like Set or the Set Cage and would have to travel by carriage, he reconsidered and decided that was actually quite fast.

In fact, considering the time it would take to travel from this village to Jubis, plus the time needed for preparations, twenty days was actually quite fast.

Besides, the pirates won’t be needed until the facility for building land ships is completed.

Given that, even if they rushed to transport them to Gilm, there likely wouldn’t be any work for them to do.

With Gilm currently under expansion, there’s no doubt there are plenty of jobs available.

But even so… or rather, precisely because of that, it will be quite some time before the pirates can truly show what they’re capable of.

“I see. Well, since we weren’t in that much of a hurry to begin with, I think you should prioritize safety over speed when getting there.”

In the end, that was the only advice Rei gave to Lilrose.
