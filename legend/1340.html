When Rei returned with the guards to the place where Byune and Keena had fought, the scene that greeted them was completely unexpected.

Because, even though she was still bound, Keena was getting along with Marina and Vihera... even smiling as she talked with them.

"...Rei, I was called here because we captured someone connected to this incident. Why is this happening?"

"Well, I can guess roughly what happened... but this is still an unexpected sight."

Originally, Rei had taken a hard line with Keena because his role was to be the villain who cornered the enemy, while Marina and Vihera would then gently interact with him to lower his guard and extract information.

But Rei, who had been taking the hardline stance, was still present at the scene. Rei figured the other guy would remain tense if Rei stayed, so he'd gone to fetch the guards... but he never expected to come back and find this scene that could only be called warm and friendly.

"Ah."

Marina was the first to spot Rei and the guard.

Next, Vihera and Keena noticed them in turn.

And it was Keena who reacted most strongly upon seeing Rei.

Experiencing a life-threatening situation due to the Death Scythe must have been quite traumatic for her.

If it had been a pure life-threatening situation during combat, Kena had experienced that before, given her work as an assassin.

But the mortal danger he felt facing Rei was vastly different from anything he'd experienced before.

It made her wonder if all the killing intent she'd felt before had been fake.

"It's okay. If you just answer honestly what you're asked, Rei won't do anything."

"...Yes, I suppose so."

Kena relaxed her tension slightly at Marina's words.

Rei was surprised at how quickly she'd managed to get so close to the other person in the short time since he'd stepped away...

"Alright, I'll hand Kena over. She says she'll cooperate with the interrogation, so please don't get rough with her, okay?"

"Y-yes!"

The security guard, met with a seductive smile, flushed bright red as he answered.

Watching the guard, Rei found himself thinking about something trivial: Come to think of it, he hadn't really reacted like this to Marina much lately...

The guard, handed the end of the rope binding Keena by Marina, led her away.

Kena showed no sign of resistance, simply following along as the guard led her away.

As Marina had mentioned earlier, she probably intended to cooperate quietly with the questioning.

Once the two figures completely disappeared from view, Rei turned his gaze toward Marina with a look of mild exasperation.

"Well, I'll be damned. You tamed her in such a short time."

"Oh, 'tamed' is a bit of an exaggeration, don't you think? I'd prefer it if you said we became friends."

"Looks the same to me. She's an assassin who's killed countless people already. If that's the case, then probably..."

"Death penalty, I suppose."

Vihera spoke up, as if finishing Rei's sentence.

But neither Rei nor Marina disagreed with that statement.

After all, it was undeniably true that most assassins were sentenced to death when captured, so it was only natural.

Silence spread around them.

While Rei was one thing, Marina and Vihera, who had spoken a little, felt a slight pang of pity.

The one killed this time was one of Tris's subordinates... specifically, someone hired after arriving in Gilm, someone Marina and Vihera had absolutely no connection to.

...Well, Marina, the former guildmaster, might recall something if she heard the slain adventurer's name.

In any case, that very fact meant there was no grudge against Keena.

If anything, having spoken with him here, however briefly, they felt a certain familiarity.

As Marina pondered this, she suddenly recalled telling Daskar about resigning as guildmaster. At that time, Daskar had lamented that the Grassland Wolves, who handled Gilm's intelligence, were struggling due to a shortage of personnel.

The Prairie Wolves were a former band of thieves... no, righteous thieves.

Because of that, their original numbers were considerably smaller than other thieves' guilds.

Until now, they'd managed to make it work somehow, but with Gilm growing larger, that was no longer feasible.

Of course, the "scale" here referred not to Gilm's actual size, but to the scope of the Prairie Wolves' operations.

(Well, yes. From what I heard, they weren't all that bad, that's true... It might be worth talking to them. Whether they hire us or not is up to them.)

I'll talk to Daskar about it later.

With that thought, Marina opened her mouth again.

"Well, I learned a few things talking with Keena. ...First off. It seems the one who brought up this matter wasn't Precious... but a man who looked like an adventurer."

"...I figured."

Rei didn't think Precious would be so careless as to directly request an assassination from Keena.

If she were to give any instructions, he figured she'd have someone else relay them, or ask a subordinate to make the request instead.

(Ah, but using intermediaries... that probably wouldn't happen in this case? It depends on Precious's current situation... If she feels threatened by us after the slum incident, she wouldn't be so leisurely... Or is the time that's passed since then the reason?)

Some time has passed since the slum incident.

Then, maybe they hired Keena through some intermediaries during that time.

That thought flashed through Rei's mind for an instant, but simultaneously, he recalled the Precious he'd seen in the slums.

More precisely, not Precious herself, but the men who seemed to be her guards.

Among them was a man who looked like an adventurer... or more accurately, someone who appeared to be a former adventurer.

Of course, if you meant adventurer-like, they were everywhere in Gilm, where adventurers were plentiful.

After all, in terms of the number of adventurers, it was one of the top places in the Mireana Kingdom.

Still, the fact that one of Precious's guards looked like an adventurer bothered him.

(Why would they even bother putting an adventurer there if they were going to have someone in between? If they just used underworld types, there wouldn't be a problem.)

Thinking this, Marina explained her reasoning to Marina and Vihera.

...Meanwhile, Byune was leaning against Set, completely asleep in the spring warmth.

Considering the battle with Keena, Rei thought this much sleep was understandable. He finished his explanation without mentioning it, then spoke again.

"So, I'm a bit concerned about that adventurer-looking guy who was guarding Precious. What do you think?"

"Normally, you wouldn't expect someone directly connected to Precious to show up... What do you think, Marina?"

"Hmm. The most convenient possibility for us is that after the slum incident, no one would cooperate with Precious if she tried to do anything. She probably tried for a few days anyway, but it didn't work out... Ultimately, she might have ordered her own guard to assassinate her."

"In that case, if we show Precious's bodyguard to Keena, it would prove it was Precious's doing... right?"

"It'd be nice if they were that easy to pin down."

Vihera stated matter-of-factly in response to Rei's words.

After all, Precious hadn't left any clear evidence behind until now.

Thinking it would be different this time... would be a bit naive.

"But it's better than doing nothing, right?"

"...True. But then, shouldn't we have just handed Keena over to the guards?"

"That's beside the point now."

Originally, Rei took a hardline stance to extract information from Kena, and then disappearing himself was meant to put her at ease and make her talk.

And the convenient excuse used for Rei to disappear was that he was going to fetch the guards.

Moreover, since they never expected to establish such a friendly relationship with Keena, the current situation was truly unexpected for Rei and the others.

"Well, what can you do? I did have some reservations about Keena. If she cooperates with our investigation, I suppose we could offer her a reduced sentence... Though Tris might not be convinced."

Both Marina and Vihera had heard about today's work... her work as an assassin, from her conversation with Keena.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, the target wasn't Tris but one of his subordinates. Still, for Tris, it was no different from having a subordinate killed.

If asked whether he could forgive it emotionally, the answer was obviously no... Yet as a shrewd merchant, Marina was certain he'd prioritize the profit of cornering Precious if he saw it as beneficial.

"Then, can I leave that to you? To confirm whether that bodyguard is one of Precious's people, we'll need to find Precious first."

"But if it was Precious's guard who ordered the assassination... do you think they'd stay in Gilm forever? Couldn't they have already left Gilm?"

Vihera's words made Rei realize that possibility existed.

But at the same time, he wondered if someone trusted enough to be brought to Gilm—essentially enemy territory for Precious—would be sent back so easily.

If it were just a matter of simple manpower, one could hire mercenaries from the slums or back alleys, accepting that some details might become known.

But truly trustworthy allies like that were few and far between.

Considering that, it was entirely possible Precious's escort was still present.

(That is, assuming the person Kena had asked was truly that individual.)

Ultimately, that was where it all led.

But with no other leads, betting on that was the only option this time.

"As for Precious's whereabouts, judging by the fact they used Keena, she's probably in the back alleys, right?"

"That's highly likely. Still, Tris might have picked up some information, so I'll ask him about that area just in case."

"...But this Precious business is a race between us and Tris to get the evidence first, right? I don't think he'll just hand it over if we go ask him now."

"Maybe. But it's definitely better to do something. By blatantly showing we're gathering intel on Precious, we might... well, forget Tris for now, but we might make Precious panic."

That panic was probably what led to this branch attack.

But now that the perpetrator has been captured, even if it was a commission involving intermediaries, it could still lead back to me.

And if Precious's bodyguard had commissioned it directly, it would reach Precious even faster and more reliably.

"I'll make sure to thoroughly explain that situation to Daskar. ...But I can't guarantee anything, since I don't know how the person behind this will try to get out of Gilm."

Marina couldn't completely stop the man who had been Precious's guard from leaving Gilm either.

If he tried to leave without any special measures, they might be able to detect him. But if he tried to leave with someone unrelated, it would be difficult to find him.

And if they took on a personal escort job outside the guild and then simply left Gilm that way, it would become even more troublesome.

"Since he went to the trouble of bringing someone all the way to Gilm, he must be someone Precious trusts deeply. If that's the case, there's also the question of whether she'd let him go so easily... But there's no point dwelling on that now."

At Vihera's words, Rei and Marina each nodded... and immediately sprang into action.

"Then I'll go talk to Tris."

"I need to inform Daskar. What about you, Vihera?"

"Right. I'll head to the guard station. Now that Keena's been captured, someone might try to silence him."

Now that the assassin had been captured, they couldn't risk him revealing information detrimental to his employer.

It was a fact that there might be those who thought exactly that.

And so, the Crimson Wings each began their actions.

...Naturally, Byune would accompany Vihera, and Set would accompany Rei.
