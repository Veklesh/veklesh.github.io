“Rei, wake up. Rei!”

Hearing that voice, Rei’s consciousness rapidly snapped back to alertness.

He opened his eyes slightly, but Rei couldn’t make out who was standing before him.

No, the very thought of trying to figure it out wasn’t even in Rei’s mind right now.

“Ugh… Hmm… Um…”

Miranda, the maid who had come to wake Rei, couldn’t believe at first that the person mumbling “uh” right in front of her was actually Rei.

The Rei Miranda knew was someone who was always on guard, ready to handle any situation that might arise.

Moreover, her true ability was evident from the fact that she had won every single match in the mock battles against the Kerebel Duke’s Knights.

That was precisely why she found it hard to believe at first that the person standing right in front of her was showing such a defenseless side.

Above all, ever since Rei had arrived at the mansion, she had come to wake him up many times in the morning, and every single morning, as soon as she called out to him, he would give a clear response.

But today, Rei was completely groggy, and to Miranda, Rei’s appearance seemed adorable.

……If there were cameras in this world, she’d want to take a picture of Rei right now and keep it forever.

But Miranda’s role was, after all, simply to wake Rei up.

If possible, she would have liked to just keep watching Rei like this, but today is the day of the New Year’s party.

Naturally, Rei needed to get ready to attend that party.

What a pity, a real pity, but Miranda calls out to Rei.

“Rei, please wake up. Today is the day of the party, so we need to get ready. Rei, are you listening?”

She kept calling out to him like that for several minutes.

A glimmer of awareness gradually returned to Rei’s sleepy eyes.

“Miranda? Ah… morning, huh?”

“Yes. By the way, you woke up late today. Usually, you’re already up by the time I come to wake you.”

"I stayed up a little late last night."

With that single sentence, Miranda understood what Rei was talking about.

Yesterday, Miranda had been told to enjoy the party with Leodanis, and she hadn’t seen Rei at all since then.

But even so, anyone at the Kerebel Duke’s residence probably knew what had happened yesterday.

After all, Miranda was one of those who doted on Set.

Hearing that Count Caseres’ men had attacked the stables where Set was sleeping, she felt she could never forgive such an act.

“Did you stay up late last night because of Set-chan?”

“That’s part of it, but I think I also just happened to sleep very deeply today.”

Saying this, Rei sat up in bed and asked Miranda to help him get ready for the day.

That said, getting ready didn’t take all that long.

Especially in Rei’s case, being a man, he could usually wrap it up in just a few minutes. …Though, that was only true on ordinary days.

Today, a large gathering of nobles was taking place for the New Year’s party.

Since Rei was also attending such an event, he naturally couldn’t just go as usual.

He washed his face more carefully than usual, brushed his teeth, combed his hair...

“Hey, do I really need to be this thorough?”

It took him considerably longer than usual to finally get ready… about twenty minutes had passed.

Perhaps slightly annoyed by this, Miranda smiled at Rei’s question and nodded confidently.

“Yes. It’s necessary. I mean, if we don’t do everything we can right now, it’ll be a real hassle when the actual preparations begin, won’t it?”

I couldn’t help but feel a sense of contradiction at the phrase “the real preparations,” but I decided that saying anything now would likely be pointless, so I kept my mouth shut.

“Is it really that much?”

“Yes. Fortunately, Rei doesn’t have a beard or anything, so you won’t need to worry about that… but if you were someone with a thick beard, you’d have to shave it off properly before attending the party.”

“...I see.”

Nodding at Miranda’s words, Rei reached up to touch his chin.

The sensation he felt through his hand was that of smooth skin.

Not a single hair was growing there, and there was no prickly or rough texture.

Normally, at Rei’s age—mid-teens—it wouldn’t be unusual for him to have some facial hair.

In fact, depending on the person, some even have quite a thick beard.

However, Rei had never had any facial hair to speak of.

In fact, he hadn’t shaved once since arriving in Elgin.

In a sense, this is probably a manifestation of Zepair’s paternal instinct—though it’s slightly different.

Here in Elgin, a special knife is used for shaving.

But of course, it’s not the kind of sharp razor you’d find in Japan, and if the person shaving is clumsy, they can cut their chin, cheeks, or even their throat.

Aristocrats might be able to hire a specialist, but for most people, that’s not an option, so they have no choice but to do it themselves.

Naturally, since the blade is used near the neck, those entrusted with this task by the nobility are limited to those they trust.

It stands to reason that if such a task were entrusted to someone who had only just begun serving a noble, there was a risk that the person might be an assassin, and the noble could end up having their throat slit and being killed.

Perhaps Zepair prevented Rei from growing a beard so that Rei wouldn’t have to go through such a hassle.

(If that’s the case, I suppose I should thank him… right?)

With that vague thought in mind, Rei finished getting ready and stood up.

Given the current situation, it didn’t feel entirely right to go to all this trouble to get ready just to eat breakfast.

That said, there’s no point in complaining about anything right now.

With that thought in mind, Rei headed toward the dining hall.

Even along the way, Rei saw many servants bustling about, reminding him that today was indeed the day of the party.

“They really seem busy. …Is Miranda okay?”

“I’ll head over there too once I’m done showing you around, Rei.”

“...That must be tough.”

“Yes. Then again, this party happens every year. Besides, since we had so much fun at the party yesterday, we have to work that much harder today.”

“Is that just how it is?”

If it were Rei, after throwing such a massive party and having so much fun, I’d naturally want to take it easy the next day.

But judging by Miranda’s demeanor, it seems she’s actually full of energy today precisely because she threw the party yesterday.

“That’s right. Isn’t that how Rei feels?”

“If it were me, I’d want to take it a little easier.”

“...You’re still young, yet you’re quite the slacker, aren’t you?”

Miranda said this with a hint of exasperation, and Rei tilted his head, asking, “Is that so?”

As they exchanged these words, they eventually arrived at the cafeteria.

“Huh?”

But once inside, Rei noticed that the dining hall was completely empty.

Well, to be precise, the maids in charge of the dining hall were there—though fewer than usual—but Elena, Liberte, and Arcadia were nowhere to be seen.

“Huh? Where’s Elena and the others?”

“The young ladies have already finished their meal and are preparing for the party.”

“Ah… I see. So I was the last one.”

To be precise, it was simply that Rei had overslept today.

“In that case, wouldn’t it have been better to have breakfast brought to my room?”

“I’m not sure. That might have been better, but then again, doing that might have caused some other problem.”

“...Is that so?”

Even though I’d just exchanged a few words with Miranda, since I’d already made it to the dining hall, going back to my room now would be a hassle, so I decided to just quietly eat breakfast here.

What was served wasn’t leftovers from yesterday’s party… but rather a variety of dishes freshly prepared this morning.

Soft bread, soup with various ingredients, salad, bacon steaks, dried fruit… There were plenty of other dishes as well, and every single one had been carefully prepared.

“It’s the day of the party—is it really okay to put this much effort into breakfast?”

“It’s not that much trouble, and more than anything, I suppose it serves as a warm-up for the dishes we’ll be preparing later today.”

The dining room maid replied to Rei’s mutter.

Miranda, standing next to the dining hall maid, nodded with a smile.

“Warming up, huh. …Well, as long as I get to eat good food, I’ve got no complaints.”

Even as he said that, Rei thought to himself that Head Chef Georgima was probably disappointed he couldn’t research ramen.

For Georgima, ramen—no, to be precise, any unknown cuisine—should have been quite intriguing.

If she could make it, it wouldn’t be strange at all for her to focus all her attention on it.

But Georgima’s current position wouldn’t allow it.

Right now, Georgima is, after all, merely a chef employed by the Duke of Kerebel, which means she must fulfill her duties.

When she’s not working, or when things aren’t that busy, Georgima might have some freedom… but the New Year’s party is one of the busiest days of the year.

Given that, it’s only natural for a hired chef to prioritize the party’s cuisine.

(Well, even if today is busy, starting tomorrow I’ll have free time again and can work on my ramen research. If that’s the case, then there’s no problem… right?)

After finishing the last bite of the remaining bread, Rei finished his breakfast.

Technically, he could have kept eating if he wanted to, but if he did that, he’d end up wanting to keep eating indefinitely.

Since Rei also needed to start preparing for the party later today, he couldn’t afford to keep eating.

And once the meal was over, it was time to prepare for the party, just as Miranda had said.

“Is there really something for me to do this early in the morning?” was Rei’s honest thought.

If he were a woman, he’d understand that all sorts of preparations—like makeup and such—would be necessary, but Rei was, after all, a man.

The custom-made party suit had already been completed, and the final adjustments were finished.

Given that, he couldn’t help but think that he could probably get ready about an hour before the party started.

But if I mentioned that to Miranda, she would definitely call me out on it.

No, it wouldn’t just be a jab—she’d probably get really angry.

Knowing that, Rei meekly lets himself be led off to get ready for the party.

(Was it ‘Donna Donna’?)

Somehow—and I mean just vaguely—that’s what Rei was thinking as Miranda led him away.

They ended up in a certain room.

Once inside, she saw several people having their makeup done.

“This is…”

“This is the room where we do makeup for the men attending today’s party. …Excuse me, could you please do Rei’s makeup?”

Several men and women turned their gaze toward Rei and Miranda as they entered the room.

(A stylist... was it? So there were people who did that kind of work in Elgin, too.)

Rei was surprised, but the conversation continued regardless.

“Oh, isn’t that Miranda. So I just need to do that one’s makeup, right?”

A woman who seemed to know Miranda said this as she looked at Rei.

But when she saw Rei’s face—now that he’d taken off his hood—a look of surprise flashed across the woman’s face.

She naturally knew the details about Rei, so she wasn’t surprised by that fact. What surprised her was the sight of Rei’s face.

There were no blemishes, and he wasn’t even bearded. Yet his skin was smooth and dewy.

It was simply impossible not to get an impression that differed from the rumors about his nickname, “Crimson.”

And at the same time, the woman felt a desire to apply makeup to Rei.

Since makeup applied so well to Rei, she naturally wanted to demonstrate her skills—it was only natural for a professional to feel that way.

“Hey, Miranda. Will you let me do Rei’s makeup? I’ll put my best foot forward and make sure it turns out absolutely perfect!”

“Huh? Well, I did come here intending to have Rei do my makeup, so that’s fine… but don’t go overboard, okay?”

“Who are you talking to? Just leave it to me.”

With a smile brimming with professional confidence, the woman spoke those words, and before Rei knew it, she found herself having her makeup done without any say in the matter.
