Standing before me is Philma, a man wielding a halberd.

As befits a commander of some of the Kingdom of Mireana’s finest elite troops, his stance is flawless, and simply standing face-to-face with him exudes an overwhelming presence.

But… even as he felt the sheer force—almost like physical pressure—being directed at him, Rei couldn’t shake the feeling that something wasn’t quite right.

This was likely the first time he had met the man standing before him.

No, he had participated in the war against the Bestir Empire and had met him back then… but at the very least, this was the first time they were facing each other as adversaries—or, to be precise, in a mock battle.

And yet, Rei had the impression that he had fought Philma, standing right before him, somewhere before.

Could it be that he was feeling that way because he was overwhelmed by the intensity Philma exuded? Thinking this, he tightened his grip on the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, but there were no issues—there was no doubt that he was in his usual physical condition.

(So, it’s not that I’m nervous and imagining I’ve fought Philma before, then. If that’s the case, have I actually fought him somewhere before? No, but… if I’d fought someone as famous as Philma, I’d definitely remember it.)

Rei stares at Philma, trying to recall memories of those he has fought in the past.

But Philma, standing face-to-face with Rei, was certainly not going to miss the fact that he was thinking such things.

“What’s wrong? Do you have the luxury of thinking about such trivial things in front of me?”

“……No, sorry. I’d like to ask you something before we fight—have we ever fought before?”

Unable to recall anything immediately, Rei asks Philma.

When asked, Philma takes care not to let her surprise show.

He never imagined that Ray would remember him from a single encounter years ago.

“Well, I wonder. But… if you can beat me, that might make things clear.”

What came out of Philma’s mouth was a taunt.

In response to Philma’s words, Rei stepped forward, gripping his Death Scythe and Twilight Spear.

And he didn’t just step forward—he kicked off the ground and closed the distance in an instant.

A scythe, a spear, and a halberd.

While Rei and Philma wielded different types of weapons, there was no doubt that all three were generally classified as polearms.

Given that, Philma would naturally dislike having someone suddenly enter her personal space.

Rei’s actions were the result of that split-second calculation.

That said, since Rei’s weapon was also a polearm, there was no doubt he was at a disadvantage in close-quarters combat.

However, since Rei had taken the initiative, he had naturally anticipated how to attack after closing the distance.

The first weapon he swung was the Death Scythe.

Given that the Death Scythe originally boasted a weight of 100 kg, even a hit to the hilt—let alone the massive blade—would inflict significant damage.

The strike was aimed at the torso—the hardest part of the body to dodge—but Philma leaped backward to evade it… and immediately kicked off the ground, this time closing the distance with Rei on her own initiative.

Rei felt a slight sense of unease at Philma’s actions.

The first strike with the Death Scythe was a preemptive strike, but given that Philma is rated as being on par with—if not superior to—Elena, she should have been able to block it with her own halberd.

The fact that he went out of his way to dodge so dramatically was enough to make Rei question his actions, even if only for a moment.

That said, they were currently in the middle of a mock battle.

Rei had no time to dwell on the details; he blocked the horizontal swing of Philma’s halberd, as if to say it was payback for what had just happened.

Still, another fleeting doubt crossed his mind.

Philma is right-handed, so if she were swinging her halberd in a horizontal sweep, it wouldn’t be strange for her to aim it at Rei’s left side—the side where he holds the Twilight Spear.

But since Rei had attacked first with the Death Scythe, there was a high probability that attacking in that direction would have landed a more effective blow.

Of course, where to direct a halberd strike depends largely on personal preference, so if asked whether this particular strike was unnatural to the point of being fatal, I couldn’t exactly agree.

Still, to Rei’s eyes, it looked as though Philma was avoiding clashing with the Death Scythe.

“Guh!”

He blocked Philma’s halberd strike with the Twilight Spear.

But what escaped Rei’s lips was a cry of surprise at having taken an unexpectedly heavy blow.

Rei’s physical abilities were so extraordinary that calling them superhuman wouldn’t be an exaggeration; under normal circumstances, no one could possibly numb his hand with a single blow.

Given that the blow was powerful enough to numb Rei’s hand, it wasn’t hard to imagine just how much force had been packed into it.

(If I’d taken that blow head-on, wouldn’t I have been dead by now?)

While it wasn’t quite as severe as the blow Leo Danis delivered during their first mock battle—which had numbed his hand and knocked his weapon out of his grasp—Rei’s left hand was still left with a significant tingling sensation.

Of course, it’s not as if he can’t fight in that state… but this is, after all, just a mock battle, and he must ensure he doesn’t inflict fatal wounds on his opponent.

Philma’s strike, which clashed with Rei’s Twilight Spear, was also supposed to be stopped if necessary… or, depending on the situation, aimed elsewhere to ensure Rei wasn’t fatally wounded.

(Probably, anyway!)

Thinking this, he swings the Death Scythe in his right hand while turning his body halfway.

Naturally, that strike wasn’t with the blade but with the pommel of the Death Scythe… but Philma, realizing this, retreated to the rear without hesitation.

Twice.

...Yes, she retreated twice in a row to avoid the Death Scythe’s attack.

(He’s got a complete grasp of the Death Scythe’s special abilities.)

If he weren’t wary of the Death Scythe’s power—which far exceeds its appearance in terms of weight—he wouldn’t have gone to such lengths to avoid engaging in a direct exchange of blows with it.

That meant Philma knew the Death Scythe weighed over 100 kg.

Facing Philma, Rei shifts his gaze to the Death Scythe he holds in his right hand.

It’s not as if he’s been keeping the fact that the Death Scythe possesses such an ability a secret all this time.

After all, anyone who has fought Rei while he wields the Death Scythe would understand that it possesses far more weight than its appearance suggests.

Still, in the case of the Death Scythe, given how menacing it looks, it’s only natural that attention would focus on its massive blade rather than its weight.

His gaze lingered on the Death Scythe for only an instant, but a skilled fighter like Philma would never miss such an opening, even for a split second.

The moment Rei looked away, Philma stepped forward again.

“Hmph!”

The halberd swings with a sharp exhalation.

The speed had increased slightly compared to before; this was likely because Philma had judged, based on her fight with Rei, that this level of speed and power would be sufficient.

Rei tried to deflect that blow aimed at his torso with the Death Scythe… but Philma made no attempt to clash the halberd with the Death Scythe; instead, he used a flick of his wrist to forcefully alter the halberd’s trajectory.

Normally, forcibly moving something as heavy as a halberd using only a wrist movement would place a considerable strain on the wrist—it wouldn’t be surprising if he sprained it.

But that would be the case for an ordinary person; Philma is by no means someone who fits into the category of “ordinary.”

While appearing to avoid colliding with the Death Scythe at all costs, the halberd’s shaft—its trajectory now altered—surged forward, threatening to smash into Rei’s torso.

Rei intercepts that strike not with the Death Scythe, but with the Twilight Spear… attempting to entangle the halberd as he had done with the Sharisla, but what met his hand was a hardness akin to solid rock.

Philma must have anticipated that move—the one where Ray intended to entangle the halberd and fling it skyward—as if it were second nature.

It was precisely because he had watched the battle with Sharisla that he was able to respond instantly.

No, rather, he even used Rei’s movement to his advantage, attempting to entangle the Twilight Spear as a counterattack.

In response to Philma’s attack, Rei, just like his opponent, gripped the Twilight Spear firmly to prevent it from being taken away… and thus, a momentary stalemate between the Twilight Spear and the halberd ensued.

However, while Philma held the halberd with both hands, Rei held the Twilight Spear solely with his left hand.

In other words, the Death Scythe—the very weapon Philma was most wary of—was free to move...

“I won’t let you!”

Philma had been frozen in place due to the stalemate with the Twilight Spear, but when she saw the Death Scythe—which could literally mow down her opponent with ease—looming toward her, she chose to let her knees give way and sit down on the ground.

Had she simply retreated backward, it would have been an obvious move even to Rei, and he would have used that movement to entangle her with his halberd.

In fact, Rei had judged that Philma would act that way and was about to move the moment she retreated.

That’s precisely why Philma’s decision to drop to her knees came as a complete surprise to Rei.

And that unexpected action caused Rei’s thinking to stall, if only for an instant… yet his right hand, gripping the Death Scythe, followed Philma’s movement almost reflexively.

As soon as Philma grasped the Death Scythe’s movement, she leaped up from her crouched position.

Rei couldn’t fathom how she could make such a move from a crouched position. He couldn’t fathom it… but it was an undeniable fact that the Death Scythe had passed beneath Philma as she leaped.

On top of that, with that series of movements, he even managed to yank the halberd out of the Twilight Spear that had been entangled with it in one swift motion.

“……”

They kept their distance from one another, silently gauging each other’s condition.

The sharpness of the gaze directed at him was clearly searching for an opening.

Even the act of looking away from the opponent for just an instant—which had sparked the earlier exchange—was more than enough to trigger a fight in this mock battle.

And so they faced each other for a dozen or so seconds… or perhaps several dozen.

For that entire time, they observed each other in silence… then suddenly, as if carried by the winter wind, a plant leaf drifted out of nowhere, passing between Rei and Philma.

Why was there a plant leaf at this time of year, when snow was falling?

No one knew, but the moment that leaf passed exactly between them, Rei and Philma both lunged forward at once.

Both acted on the understanding that the other would not let this moment slip by, and for that very reason, they showed no hesitation even as the distance between them closed in an instant.

While dodging Philma’s halberd strike, Rei unleashes a strike with the Twilight Spear.

Since this was a mock battle, it was a horizontal slash rather than a thrust with the tip.

That strike, however, was blocked by the halberd… and as if seizing the brief opening created by his opponent’s momentary hesitation, Rei swung his Death Scythe.

Philma, too, tried to use the momentum from being knocked back by the Twilight Spear to spin around and strike Rei, but... her movement came to a sudden halt.

Naturally, there’s a huge advantage to striking while spinning versus striking normally.

Well, if the opponent were of a lower rank than oneself, it would be a different story… but Philma’s opponent this time was Rei, and he was by no means of a lower rank.

The Death Scythe was thrust right before her eyes. Staring intently at it, Philma briefly considered whether she could turn the tables from this position, but ultimately concluded there was nothing she could do in the current situation.

If this were a real battle rather than a mock fight, she’d still have a few tricks up her sleeve, but there’s no need to go out of her way to show them in a mock fight.

Besides, although she and Rei are on friendly terms, they aren’t exactly allies.

Depending on the circumstances, there was a possibility they might end up as enemies in the future.

Given that, what Philma should do here is not look for an opening to counterattack, but...

“I give up. I surrender.”

...it was to simply admit defeat.
