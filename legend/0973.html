Watching the red cyclops charging toward him, Rei pondered what to do.

Rei was extremely interested in the magic item hammer the red cyclops was wielding.

No, given the current situation, it would be more accurate to say the red cyclops was being used by the hammer.

Its abilities included striking lightning down wherever the hammer struck and granting the user an almost abnormal regenerative capacity.

However, right now, Rei could see something like roots extending from the hammer, spreading throughout the red cyclops' body.

Seeing that, Rei certainly wouldn't dream of using the hammer himself.

(Though, without some ability like Death Scythe's weight-negation, I'd probably struggle to wield that hammer too.)

It was a hammer large enough that even when held by the red cyclops, standing about five meters tall, it still looked small.

Even though Rei possesses physical abilities far superior to those of an ordinary human, if asked whether he could wield something that massive with complete ease, he could only answer no.

Even if the hammer possessed an ability like Death Scythe that made it feel weightless, its sheer size would be an issue.

He might be able to wield it while activating the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour like now, but his true specialty was the scythe, not the hammer.

Still, the reason Rei desires that magic item is the thought that even if he doesn't use it himself, bringing it to an alchemist or blacksmith might yield some benefit.

For various reasons, he hadn't gone to retrieve it yet, but before heading to Glacial, he had requested that the remains of the Magic Sword he'd taken from Noiz be reforged into a spear.

That's on Rei's mind too, but perhaps it could be used as some kind of material for it. That thought lingered within Rei.

"Well, all that... comes after I defeat you, you know!"

With those words, he leapt sideways, evading the hammer blow swung at him.

An electric shock shot from the hammer, which had smashed the ground into a crater, but Rei wasn't about to fall for an attack he'd already seen several times.

Using Sleipnir's boots just as before, he executed a triangular leap toward the red cyclops' right side... toward the half of its body holding the hammer, swinging Death Scythe.

True to the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour he wore, though the triangle jump itself was nearly identical to before, his speed was incomparably faster.

The blade of the Death Scythe swung toward the red cyclops's right arm severed it without resistance... yet in the next instant, the arm that should have been severed was already reattached.

Seeing this, Rei activated Sleipnir's Boots once more, kicking off into the air to create distance.

Boom! The hammer passed through the spot where Rei's body had been just an instant before.

"Your regeneration speed has increased too? Troublesome."

When he first attacked to save Ecuril, he severed the red Cyclops's right shoulder and arm.

That severed right shoulder had also reattached almost immediately, but it had remained severed for at least a brief moment.

At least it didn't perform the absurd feat of reattaching itself the instant it was severed.

Naturally, the burn scars he'd sustained when he closed to Death Scythe's range while activating the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour had vanished in less than a few seconds.

"So then, what do we do? Killing or destroying it seems manageable enough, but..."

In the worst case, there was the option of destroying the magic item, the hammer itself. But if possible, he wanted to avoid that and secure it without damage.

Rei was still pondering, but his opponent wasn't going to wait around for him.

It charged toward Rei, hammer in hand.

"Dammit! Give me a moment to think!"

With that, he launched a portion of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour toward the red cyclops charging at him.

Named Deep Flame, the skill sent the crimson magic flying straight ahead at a speed that seemed to slice through the air. It touched the red cyclops... and in the next instant, engulfed its entire body in flames.

Naturally, faced with such an attack, the red cyclops—or more accurately, the hammer controlling its body—thrashed about, trying to extinguish the flames covering its form.

But flames that would normally extinguish by rolling on the ground showed no sign of fading now.

Of course not. Deep Flame wasn't physical fire; it was fire created by Rei's magic.

It was a skill that produced flames exactly as Rei imagined them.

This time, Rei unleashed sticky flames.

Flames that cling to whatever they touch like gum or adhesive, continuing to burn.

Unless you dive into a large body of water or use some form of magic to extinguish it, the flames won't go out.

"While I'm at it, take this!"

Seeing the Deep Flame take effect, he unleashed another Deep Flame with the same properties.

Upon impact, the sticky flames covering the red cyclops' body grew even larger.

It was fortunate this happened in a clearing within the forest where no trees stood nearby. Had he used this Deep Flame where the battle with the red cyclops first began, the flames would undoubtedly have spread wildly.

(The fact it's lashing out after taking this flame means... even if unconscious, the red cyclops' body is still alive? When it dies, its movements slow down, so it's frantically trying to extinguish the flames... I think. Nah, either way, destroying the red cyclops' body itself should solve the problem.)

Watching the red cyclops struggle desperately to extinguish the viscous flames, Rei stepped forward without hesitation.

The explosive power of his step, amplified by the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, went completely unnoticed by the red cyclops, who was frantically trying to extinguish the flames still burning its own body.

Though its skin regenerated where scorched, the red cyclops remained a living creature nonetheless.

As a living being, it naturally needed to breathe. Yet it was suffering in a situation where even breathing was utterly impossible.

Though unconscious, its body was tormented by the agony of being unable to breathe... Worse still, attempting to inhale only drew viscous flames into its mouth. In this state, unconsciousness was arguably a blessing.

"Haaah!"

With that cry, the swing of the Death Scythe instantly cleaved the writhing red cyclops' body in two.

...No, it wasn't just split in two.

In that single instant, the blades swung more than ten times.

They hammered into the thrashing colossus in rapid succession, slicing through its right hand, right leg, left hand, left leg, head, chest, abdomen, and sides one after another.

"If you're going to regenerate, I'll just keep slicing you faster than you can heal!"

Even as he shouted, the relentless barrage of blows was no longer an attack, but rather a series of strikes that could more accurately be called processing.

The hammer was regenerating the red cyclops' body as soon as it was severed, but the speed was evenly matched.

The moment a severed limb seemed to heal, it was cut off again.

The blade of the Death Scythe swung down once more.

Beyond the Death Scythe's relentless assault, the sticky flames enveloping its body also demanded healing energy, further straining the hammer.

Cut, regenerate, regenerate, cut. ...This exchange continued for over ten minutes.

Gradually, the scales that had been wavering between regeneration and destruction began to tip toward destruction.

After all, the hammer's regeneration and lightning abilities weren't being used without cost.

They were forcibly converted from the red cyclops's formidable vitality into magical power.

This process was carried out by the roots extending from the hammer, spread throughout the Cyclops' body.

It was only possible because the user's consciousness had been stolen, turning them into a puppet.

But... no matter how high the Cyclops' vitality might be, it was not infinite.

In the sense of being finite, Rei's magic power was similar, but even though both were finite, their capacities differed vastly.

One was, after all, still just a single cyclops, even if not an ordinary one.

On the other hand, there was the one possessing the highest magical power in this world.

And with each injury, the red Cyclops was forced to consume its life force. While Rei was depleting his magic power due to the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, his remaining reserves were still ample.

Engaging in a war of attrition under such conditions, the red Cyclops... no, the hammer had no chance of winning.

Countless wounds were carved by the swinging Death Scythe.

Though those wounds were still regenerating now, their healing speed rapidly declined as time passed.

Time passed like this... until finally, Rei realized the Cyclops had been completely finished off by the blows of the Death Scythe.

Worse still, the viscous flames still clinging to its body burned on, reducing its hacked-up remains to charcoal as they burned away.

"...Damn."

Only the torso and right hand remained.

In the case of a typical Cyclops, the right ear—which serves as proof of slaying—along with the horn and single eye, highly valued as materials, had been completely incinerated by the flames, leaving them irreparably destroyed.

Since only the torso and right hand remained intact, the other limbs were also charred to ash. Naturally, there were no usable bones or tendons as materials, nor any tasty meat to be found.

"It all came at once at the end."

Sighing, Rei's gaze shifted to the red cyclops's right arm.

That right hand still clenched the hammer, emitting faint lightning around it as if to keep Rei at bay.

A war of attrition between Rei and the red cyclops, Rei being pushed back yet somehow maintaining the balance.

But once the scales tipped completely in Rei's favor, the collapse was swift.

So much so that he couldn't stop the viscous flames from consuming the very spot he'd sought as material.

"I never did figure out if it was a rare species or a higher-tier one, but whatever. ...I got what I came for, so I'll call it good."

Rei muttered, shifting his gaze toward the hammer. As if answering him, the hammer emitted purple lightning around itself.

Touch it carelessly, and Rei would undoubtedly get electrocuted. ...Normally, one would think that and hesitate to touch it.

"If you were going to do it, you should've just stayed silent and camouflaged yourself. Then, when I grabbed it, you could've used the root you used on the red cyclops."

Rei reached out toward the hammer emitting purple lightning... and in response, the hammer emitted even stronger purple lightning.

But while that might have worked against the ordinary Rei, the Rei of the present was currently deploying the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Against Rei in that state, the purple lightning the hammer could currently unleash had absolutely no effect.

A thought flashed through Rei's mind: Would it be safe to touch it like this? But then he remembered that if he didn't touch it, he couldn't store it in the Misty Ring... and recalled the Misty Ring's characteristic inability to store living things.

(This hammer controlled the red cyclops. Meaning, while it can't speak words, it definitely possesses some kind of will... I think. If that's the case, can't it be stored? But magic items are magic items. Even if it has a will, how does that factor in?)

Despite his doubts, he concluded there was no choice but to try.

If I were to store it in the Misty Ring, touching the hammer would only need to be momentary.

But if I were to carry it back like this, I'd have to keep touching it all the way until we reached Gilm.

When asked which option to choose, the former was the obvious choice.

"Phew... Alright."

Magic items that take over and control others were something even Rei had never seen before.

Precisely because of that, when touching it, he focused on the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour enveloping his body, ensuring he could react instantly to anything. Even so, he touched the hammer while suppressing the heat as much as possible.

Thud.

The instant he touched it, he felt something enter him from his right hand gripping the handle.

Yes, it was a sensation akin to permeation, starting from Rei's right hand and attempting to take control of his entire body.

And from the handle he gripped, roots attempted to invade Rei's body, just as they had with the red cyclops.

The hammer seemed to understand that Rei was not an opponent it could easily dominate.

But those roots couldn't invade the body of Rei, covered in the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour; they were incinerated the moment they made contact.

Moreover, the will emanating from Rei's grasping hand initially tried to take over her right hand. Yet, faced with the immense magical power dwelling within Rei's body, it was akin to trying to evaporate the sea with a candle flame.

It couldn't dominate Rei's body at all... rather, the will that flowed alongside Rei's magic trampled what might be called the hammer's will, and the candle flame was easily extinguished by the sea water.

All this unfolded in mere seconds.

To an outside observer, it was a decisive victory for Rei... no, an outright rout. Yet Rei, experiencing this battle of wits for the first time, exhaled deeply and gazed up at the clear blue sky.

...It would be several minutes later before Rei, attempting to salvage at least the magic stone from the body of the red cyclops—now reduced to only its right hand and torso—noticed that the heart itself had been completely and utterly obliterated.
