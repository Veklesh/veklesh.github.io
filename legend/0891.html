The voice came unexpectedly, but Rei and Asie couldn't grasp the meaning of those words at first.

That was only natural. While Rei was one thing, Asie was clearly a maid, and they were inside the Sysne Baron's residence.

In other words, it should be obvious at a glance that she served the Baron Sisne household, barring some extraordinary circumstance.

But the voice that had just spoken was definitely telling Ashié, not Rei, to accompany him tonight.

(Wait, does he think I'm a woman?)

Rei wondered, Could it be...?

Rei's body was smaller than average, and now that he was wearing the Dragon Robe, his build could be mistaken for either male or female.

...Though, to be fair, Rei's physique wasn't particularly muscular to begin with, so even without the robe, it wasn't impossible someone might mistake them for a woman.

And above all, the face hidden beneath Rei's hood was so well-proportioned it could be described as feminine.

Considering all that, it was possible someone might mistake Rei for a female adventurer—something Rei herself refused to admit.

But...the gaze of the man who had spoken was unmistakably directed at Asie, not Rei.

Judging by that gaze, it was certain his words were meant for Asie, not Rei.

"My apologies, noble sir. Are you speaking to me?"

When Ashié asked the noble riding in the carriage, the noble nodded arrogantly from the open window, as if to say it was only natural.

"Of course. From the looks of it, you're a maid in this household, aren't you? Then you'll obey my orders too."

Rei couldn't understand the man's bold declaration.

Why must a maid of the Baron Sisne household obey that nobleman's command?

Does Asie understand? Rei turned his gaze toward Asie, but Asie tilted her head, looking equally confused.

(He doesn't seem to be connected to the Baron Sisne household either. Why is he so confident?)

Rei couldn't make sense of how the conversation had unfolded.

Ashie, too, was unsure how to respond... or more accurately, how to refuse.

Observing Asie's hesitation, a knight who appeared to be the carriage's escort spoke up.

"Are you telling me a mere maid of the Baron's household can't obey the orders of His Lordship Keep, heir to the Count of Eriell? Just come over here already!"

Wearing metal armor in the falling snow, the cold must be unbearable.

The knight, his irritation plain to see, began advancing toward Ashié.

...Yes. That is, toward Rei, who stood beside Ashié.

And so, using a reason that could scarcely be called compelling, the knight reached out toward Asie... only for his hand to be stopped, as expected, by Rei.

"Hey, are you out of your mind?"

"You, you look like an adventurer? Think you can defy Lord Keep and get away with it? Get your filthy hands off her. Do it, and I'll let you off this time."

Hearing the knight's words as he approached Ashié, another knight standing near the carriage nodded and spoke up.

"Do you think a mere baron's house can defy a count's house? If you don't obediently follow Lord Keep's orders, this... the Baron House of Sisne, was it? I think it will cause trouble for the head of this house. It's meaningless for a baron's house like this to make a fuss. Ultimately, you will have to obey the orders of the royalist Count House of Eriel."

"That... but, well. I'm terribly sorry, but a woman like me is utterly unworthy of being the companion of someone like Lord Keep. Might you not grant me your forgiveness?"

Ashie tried to smooth things over, but her words only served to enrage the man named Keep inside the carriage.

"Don't be ridiculous! Are you saying a mere baron's maid can't obey my orders!?"

Rei couldn't fathom what part of Ashié's words had so deeply offended him, but Keep kicked the carriage door open and leapt out onto the ground.

Only now did Rei get a proper look at what Keep actually looked like.

After all, only his face had been visible through the carriage window.

He was of average height and build... that would be the correct description, but he gave the impression of being somewhat flabby, a man in his mid-twenties.

He wasn't actually fat, but he had that kind of build that gave others the impression he was.

He showed no sign of physical training whatsoever, and his gait couldn't be called refined, which was likely why he gave off that impression.

And as for his face, I'd seen it through the carriage window—it was essentially handsome.

But that condescending air he carried completely ruined his otherwise handsome features.

"You lot, just bring this guy over here!"

"Wait, wait a moment, Lord Keep! Even if he holds a lower title, he's still a proper noble. Forcibly dragging away his maid like this will cause problems later!"

A man scrambled out of the carriage in a panic.

He was visibly overweight.

His weight made him appear considerably older than Keep, but to Rei's eyes, he seemed to be about the same age.

"Enough, shut up, Fultos! Are you saying you can't follow my orders?!"

"That's not what I said! I'm saying that even if we want to get that maid, we need to properly explain it to them first!"

Fultos shouted, wiping sweat from his brow despite the falling snow.

Considering Keep's actions, he desperately pleaded for him to be stopped here at all costs.

Keep snorted unpleasantly, seemingly unmoved by Fultos' words, then turned to Asie and spoke.

"Bring me the head of this household. I'll declare you mine myself."

Ashie was being treated as an object rather than a person, but she didn't seem to mind.

In fact, judging by what Keep had just said, bringing Muet here would only make the situation worse.

She needed to find a way to get Keep to leave quietly, but she couldn't think of one.

And then... at the worst possible moment, Muet appeared outside.

"Ashie, Rei-kun is..."

Before she could finish, she noticed a carriage stopped in front of the house, along with several people, including one who appeared to be a noble.

What she couldn't overlook was that men who appeared to be knights were entering the grounds of Baron Sisne's residence.

"Excuse me, but who are you? At the very least, I don't know you. And I don't think it's very appropriate for people like you to be entering the grounds of this house, do you?"

Muet's bold declaration showed none of his usual timidity.

Muet's attitude clearly rubbed Keep the wrong way. He shouted irritably.

"Shut up! I'm the next head of the Count of Eriell family! Do you think you can give me orders?!"

"Ah, so you are from the Counts of Eriell. I am Muet, head of the Barons of Sisne."

Despite being clearly insulted, Muet maintained his smile.

Of course, he wasn't unaffected by the insult. The Earl of Eriell was a royalist noble, clearly of higher rank than himself.

Even if the man before him wasn't the current head but the next heir, causing trouble here would undoubtedly lead to complications later.

"Hmph. So you're this estate's penniless baron? Your maid has been quite rude to me. Well, I don't want to make trouble with a poor baron like you. Send that maid over to me. I'll let this incident slide."

The power of his own house versus the timid-looking middle-aged man before him.

Considering that power dynamic, the penniless baron before him should have obeyed his command without question.

Even if he was the head of the baron's household and Muet was the next heir.

That's precisely why Keep assumed Muet would hand over the maid, even though he wore a look of resentment.

(She's got a pretty good figure. She'll do just fine for tonight. Afterwards, I can just give her to the servants.)

He even harbored such thoughts as he turned his gaze toward Ashié...

"I refuse."

Muet declared with unexpectedly firm words.

"I see. Then you lot, take that woman immediately... What?"

Bring her here.

Keep's words stopped mid-sentence.

For several seconds, he contemplated what had just been said to him. When reality finally caught up, his face flushed crimson with irritation as he glared at Muet and shouted.

"What the hell do you think you're doing?! Do you intend to defy the Count of Eriell family!?"

"Defy? It's you who's trying to forcibly take my maid away for no reason at all! So, I can't just back down here."

"...Are you sure about this? If Father finds out about this, it will definitely cause problems!"

Keep warned threateningly, but Muet, shielding Asie behind him, declared resolutely.

"She serves in my household. If she were to willingly say she wants to go with you, that might be one thing, but I cannot entrust her to someone who resorts to such forceful abduction."

Keep's face flushed red with anger, clearly not expecting defiance from someone of lower rank—even if that someone was the next head of the house.

Seeing this, Keep's attendant, Fultos, judged the situation dangerous.

It was true they had been sent here to stir up trouble, providing the royalist faction with a reason to intervene.

But even so, if things continued like this, the problem could become far larger than anticipated.

A Keep consumed by rage might well order his knights to kill someone, even if that person held the rank of baron.

Moreover, that knight, fundamentally serving the count's house, possessed a sense of privilege.

Even if the man were a baron, they would likely kill him without hesitation if Keep ordered it.

If that happened, the Marquis of Larks, who governs this region, would undoubtedly intervene immediately.

If that happened, the royalist noble who ordered us here would abandon us without a second thought.

(Bad, bad, bad. I have to resolve this situation somehow. And without bloodshed... What should I do? What should I do?)

Seeing Keep about to give orders to the knights, Fultos' mind raced.

(Something... anything? At least something that would justify killing him... Huh? Killing him? Judging by the manor, he's clearly a poor baron, just as Lord Keep said. Even the head of the household doesn't seem particularly well-trained. Then!)

Having devised a way to break the deadlock, Fultos whispered into Keep's ear just as he opened his mouth, driven by anger.

Muet, watching this, felt a bad premonition but could only wait to see how the other would respond.

Meanwhile, Rei, who had been thinking he'd step forward if anything happened, simply watched silently as events unfolded.

When Fultos finished speaking, Keep, who moments ago had been red-faced with rage, now wore a cheerful smile as if it had never happened.

"Fine. Then let's do this. You and I will duel. Of course, you can hire a proxy if you wish. Though, that's assuming a penniless baron like you has the money for it. And if I win, I take the woman."

At those words, a bitter expression crossed Muet's face.

But then, he saw Rei nodding in the corner of his vision.

Take it on. The look that said, Leave this duel to me, brought absolute relief to Muet's heart.

The greatest misfortune for Keep and Fultos at that moment was likely that they hadn't realized it was Rei standing there.

Then again, it was hardly surprising, given he wore a Dragon Robe with concealment effects active, held neither his signature Death Scythe, nor was his griffin, Set, present.

"Just for the record, what will you do if I win?"

At Muet's words, Keep let a sneer touch his lips.

"Huh? What? You think a penniless noble like you could beat me? Ha ha ha. This is amusing. Well then... if you win, I suppose I could give you half of the property owned by the Count of Eriell family."

"...Are you serious? Lord Keep is the next head of the house, not the current one. I don't believe you have the authority to decide such a thing."

"No problem. There's absolutely no chance I'll lose. ...But, well. If you're that worried, let's tell the local lord and handle it properly through legal channels before the duel. No problem with that?"

"L-Lord Keep!? That's going too far..."

Fultos, who had been watching the conversation unfold from the side, cried out in alarm at the words coming from Keep's mouth.

It was only natural. If they fought a duel after having the lord handle it legally, there would be no way to cover it up if, by some remote chance, he lost.

But Fultos's panic only provoked Keep's anger.

"What is it, Fultos? You think I'll lose? Relax. I'm the next head of the Count of Eriell family. There's no way I'd lose to some penniless baron. ...Right. I can't have things covered up later. I'll go talk to the lord now. You come too.I'm going to inform the lord right now. ...Woman, it's not happening today, but once this duel is over, I'll spoil you rotten. Wait for it with anticipation."

Keep declared this, convinced that his bloodline meant he could never lose to someone of lower rank.

If anyone here knew the full details of the situation in Gilm, they would have definitely tried to stop him.

But since they couldn't recognize Rei as Rei, neither Keep, Fultos, nor the knights could recognize that they had made a fatal mistake.
