The mock battle organized by Rei and the nobles could be described as unconventional in many ways.

To begin with, the very idea of repeating a mock battle multiple times rather than just once is unusual for a mock battle.

Of course, there are mock battles fought against large numbers of opponents, but considering the number of people gathered this time, it was a bit excessive.

That said, there aren’t many who possess the skill to spar with Rei, known by the epithet “Crimson,” and if told that this was a mock battle designed to thoroughly assess Rei’s abilities, many would find it convincing.

Normally, Rei would have had every right to complain, but in this case, he was set to receive Fuma Ore as a reward from Liberte, so considering that, he didn’t really mind having to spar with so many people.

Also, since arriving in Anesis, she hadn’t had much to do and was feeling a bit bored… that was a big factor, too.

That said, even if he was bored, Rei’s honest feeling was that he’d rather skip having his body measured for clothes if at all possible.

“Alright, let’s begin the first mock battle! The ones fighting Crimson Rei are… first up, these ten!”

Saying this, the male emcee gestured toward the adventurers facing Rei.

It was a rather over-the-top gesture, but the audience—excited by the prospect of watching mock battles featuring adventurers with nicknames and caught up in the festive atmosphere—cheered at the host’s antics.

“These are the adventurers who want to try their hand at a mock battle against someone with a nickname! They know they’re still inexperienced, but they want to see just how far they can go against a nicknamed adventurer with their current strength! Let’s see how far these Rank C and D adventurers—who are considered fully-fledged in their own right—can hold their own!”

At the host’s words, several of those about to face Rei in a mock battle wore displeased expressions.

They didn’t think they could beat Rei even if they fought him, but they probably didn’t like being spoken to that way as if defeat were a foregone conclusion.

“Come on, calm down. If you’re upset, just show what you’re made of in the mock battle, right?”

A man standing nearby—perhaps an acquaintance of the disgruntled one—spoke up.

Hearing that voice, the man who had been annoyed snapped out of it, and the others were now ready to begin the mock battle at any moment.

Noticing this, the emcee turned his gaze toward Rei.

Rei, who was still empty-handed, responded to the host’s gaze by pulling the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring.

A massive scythe and a Crimson spear.

Anyone who had seen Rei fight before wouldn’t have been surprised by this sight, but for most of the people present, this was the first time they were seeing Rei’s weapons with their own eyes.

Because of this, they couldn’t believe that someone as petite as Rei could hold both a scythe and a spear in one hand, and a loud murmur arose.

For the adventurers in particular, having wielded weapons themselves, they could easily imagine just how heavy the ones Rei was holding must be.

They might be able to hold them with one hand themselves, but they certainly couldn’t handle them with such ease.

Because of this, they come to view Rei as someone beyond their comprehension.

In reality, setting aside the Twilight Spear, the Death Scythe possesses a special ability that makes it feel almost weightless to Rei and Set alone, so it isn’t heavy at all for Rei.

"Whoa! Crimson Rei has drawn a massive scythe and a red spear! The large scythe, famous as one of Crimson Rei’s weapons, and a spear that even a layman like me can tell is a masterpiece. Normally, weapons like these are typically held with both hands—what are known as ‘long weapons’—but… amazingly, Rei can wield both of these long weapons with just one hand each!　Just to confirm the rules: if you fall to the ground, you are considered defeated. Normally, the battle would continue even after that, but since there are so many participants this time, those are the rules! Also, stepping off the stage results in a loss!”

At the male announcer’s words, the audience let out cries of confusion and astonishment.

Setting the rules aside, they simply couldn’t imagine Rei being capable of wielding both a scythe and a spear at the same time.

But seeing him actually standing there effortlessly with them in hand, it seemed almost certain that Rei could handle both weapons at the same time.

Since he was wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, it was impossible to tell what expression he actually had on his face.

In any case, seeing Rei wielding weapons that even they themselves could barely handle, the adventurers flinched for a moment.

Still, they quickly regained their composure, likely because they understood this was just a mock battle.

“Let’s go! Our opponent is an adventurer with a nickname. We’re getting paid to spar with someone like that—it’s exactly what we’ve been hoping for!”

Shouting that, the adventurer wielding the longsword was the first to dash forward.

As if chasing after him, the others also sprinted off all at once.

Ten people—and ones who clearly excel at rough-and-tumble fighting—dashing off all at once created a powerful, overwhelming presence.

In fact, the spectators who had come to watch were left breathless by the sheer force of those adventurers.

But… Rei, who was on the receiving end of that charge, showed no sign of nervousness whatsoever as he waited for the adventurers rushing toward him.

Since Rei was someone who often engaged in activities like hunting bandits, he was used to facing large numbers of opponents at once.

Normally, the best strategy would be to charge forward and attack the adventurers coming at him, but since this was a mock battle, he judged that his opponents needed their moment to shine as well, so he remained where he was, waiting for them.

Some of the nobles, seeing Rei show no sign of moving even as the adventurers approached, cast contemptuous glances at him, assuming he must be cowering in fear… but Rei, as if it were the most natural thing in the world, paid absolutely no attention to those looks.

“Wooooooooooooooooooah!”

The first to attack Rei was, perhaps unsurprisingly, the man with the longsword who had started running first.

Right behind him was a woman wielding a spear, and behind and to the sides of her were the other adventurers.

Rei waited for his opponents to make their move... and parried the strike from the longsword of the man who had charged at the front with the Twilight Spear in his left hand, deflecting it cleanly.

It was a strike delivered with full force, but it was deflected along the shaft of the Twilight Spear, causing the man to lose his balance... and then, his foot was swept out from under him by the tip of the Death Scythe he held in his right hand, sending him tumbling to the ground.

Being an adventurer himself, the man managed to pull off the bare minimum of defense—avoiding a head injury as he fell—but the impact of his body hitting the ground meant he wouldn’t be able to move for at least several dozen seconds.

For now, Rei shifted her focus away from the man and turned her attention to the woman wielding a spear who had approached from directly behind him.

The thrust, launched straight at him, possessed considerable speed and sharpness.

But, conversely, that meant it was only “reasonable,” so it wasn’t difficult for Rei to dodge.

With just a slight shift of his body, he dodged the spear and, using the Twilight Spear that had parried the longsword strike moments earlier, entangled the spear’s shaft. In the next instant, the spear the woman held was flung into the air.

As the woman stood there dazed, having lost her spear in an instant, he swung the stone tip of the Death Scythe—just as he had done with the man earlier—at her legs, knocking her to the ground.

However, things were different from here on out.

Without pausing after sweeping the woman’s legs with the Death Scythe, he swept the legs of the two men who were attacking Rei from the side.

The two were tripped almost simultaneously and fell to the ground, and Rei swung the Twilight Spear at the next attacker.

And so, one after another, the adventurers are tripped and fall to the ground.

While some may have injured their legs, none of them are broken.

In the end, only one elf with a bow remained.

Normally, she would have taken advantage of the moment when her comrades—though they were merely on the same team by chance—were fighting Rei to shoot an arrow with a crushed arrowhead.

But once the mock battle actually began, it turned into a one-sided rout by Rei, leaving him absolutely no room to even think about shooting an arrow.

“Well… what do you say? You’re the only one left… do you intend to fight me?”

“...I do. This is a mock battle against someone with a legendary nickname. Since that’s the case, if I don’t fight here, I won’t know why I’m even here.”

With those words, an arrow flew from the elf’s hand.

The arrow, flying straight toward Rei, was effortlessly sliced off by the Death Scythe in the very next instant.

Glancing at the arrow, now split in two and rolling on the ground, Rei simply stepped forward... and began walking toward the elf.

He must have judged that if he couldn’t even block that arrow, he had no chance of winning. The elf discarded his bow and drew the dagger from his waist.

“Come on.”

The moment Rei spoke those words, the elf lunged forward.

However, since his primary weapon was a bow, he likely couldn’t put much power into the dagger. His lunge was a notch—no, two notches—below that of the man with the longsword who had initially charged at Rei.

Was Rei a bit of a letdown? Rei couldn’t help but think so, but as the distance between him and the elf closed… the next moment, he suddenly accelerated.

(I see.)

Acting slowly at first, then suddenly changing speed right in front of him.

The tactic was simple, but naturally, anyone would be taken aback if it were done right in front of them like that.

That said, that’s only true for ordinary adventurers. Even if someone suddenly changes their speed, it’s not something that would surprise Rei all that much.

In the end, the elf was tripped by the tip of the Death Scythe and fell... and the first mock battle ended in a decisive victory for Rei.

“That’s it! Rei wins the first round!”

As soon as the male announcer declared this, a significant number of spectators let out cries of despair.

These were likely the ones who, unaware of just how skilled Rei was, had bet on her losing the first mock battle.

That said, since it’s usually an extremely difficult feat for one person to take on ten opponents, it wasn’t exactly a major failure.

As for just how strong this man, Rei, really was... those who knew were the first to cheer, partly because they hadn’t lost their bets.

“Phew.”

Exhaling softly, Rei looked around.

There, he could see the adventurers getting to their feet and walking away.

Some had injured their legs so badly they couldn’t stand, but the guards stationed near the stage were helping them up and escorting them away.

(Did I go a little too far? But I heard there were people here who could use potions or healing magic, so it shouldn’t be a problem, right?)

As Rei watched the adventurers leave the stage, the male emcee spoke up.

“Wow, that was amazing! Lightning fast! Even though there were ten opponents, Rei overwhelmed them all single-handedly! He truly lives up to his nickname, ‘Crimson’!”

At those words, the audience’s excitement began to rise.

The male host must be good at hyping the crowd.

“But… b-u-t! The next opponents are a little different from the adventurers we just saw. That’s because they’re knights in the service of the Countess of Lelemera!”

Hearing that they were knights serving the nobility, the audience’s gaze turned toward the stage.

Five men stepped onto the stage.

Though their numbers are only half that of the adventurers from earlier, it likely means they’re a group of elite warriors.

(Logically speaking, there’s no need to field a team weaker than the one that came out before.)

They must have noticed that Rei was watching them. Several of the knights looked at Rei with eyes full of fighting spirit.

The five wore matching armor, clearly displaying their status as knights.

“Well, it seems everyone is ready… Let’s begin the second mock battle!”

At the emcee’s words, the knights charged straight toward Rei.

However, unlike the adventurers from earlier, every one of them understood their duties, and without a word, each was set on fulfilling their respective roles.

Two with longswords, two with lances, and one with a hammer.

The man wielding the hammer seemed to be a show-off, standing nearly two meters tall.

The blow from that massive hammer—if it hit me head-on, even in a mock battle, it would likely kill me easily.

(Could they be the noble’s henchmen sent to kill me? …But I don’t sense any killing intent.)

What I see in the knights’ eyes isn’t a killing intent, but a fighting spirit.

It seems they want to test just how far their own strength will take them against a formidable opponent like Rei.

(In that case... I’ll try landing a hit first.)

Having made that decision, Rei took a step forward, Death Scythe and Twilight Spear in hand.
