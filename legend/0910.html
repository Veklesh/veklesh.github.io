Having decided to place a commission request, Rei figured it was better to get the paperwork done than sit around waiting. So, accompanied by Maruka, Koan, and Set—three people and one creature—she headed for the guild.

"Well now, these skewers are delicious. Hmm, shopkeeper. I'll have another one."

"Ha ha ha. You want another? You're something else, young lady. Here you go. It's hot off the grill, so be careful."

"Mm. Freshly grilled food truly is delicious. And eating it like this, while snow falls, has a certain charm to it."

"Gurrrrr."

Beside Maruka, happily eating her skewered meat, Set also ate his skewered meat, happily growling.

...Yes, though Maruka held the position of Duke Quent's daughter, she wasn't riding in the carriage she'd arrived in at Dusk Wheat inn. Now, she was simply walking through Gilm.

And just like Rei and Set always did, she was snacking as she went.

Furthermore, her attire had changed from what she wore arriving at the Dusk Wheat inn. Though still of good quality, it was now so simple it hardly suggested nobility.

Seeing Maruka now, some might think she was the daughter of a wealthy merchant, but few would guess she was nobility... let alone a duke's daughter.

If anyone did, it would only be someone who had seen Maruka as a noble.

"Hey, young lady, you've got Set with you? Nice. ...Here, have some of this too."

"Huh? Is that okay?"

"Yeah, yeah. It's fine. Our sandwiches aren't your average street food, you know?"

"What? Really? Then I'll take one right away!"

Maruka energetically took a bite out of the sandwich she received from the stall owner.

In that instant, sauce squirted out from between the sandwich layers and splattered onto her cheek, but she didn't seem to notice at all and kept eating.

Just watching her made people feel happy, and those walking by couldn't help but smile.

"Is this okay? We're supposed to be incognito, yet you're drawing so much attention."

"Ahahaha. Well, if anything happens, I'll protect you. Besides, with Rei-san and Set here, no one would dare make a move."

Rei and Koan continued eating their skewers as they walked, stopping several times to buy snacks from stalls before the group arrived at the guild.

"Hmm hmm, Gilm is truly splendid. If I were to behave like this in Graciall, it would certainly cause a commotion."

"Well, it's only natural to stand out when the sole daughter of Duke Quento, Lady Marca, is out and about in his own stronghold."

"I understand that, but—"

"Grrr."

Upon arriving at the guild, Set purred without being told and headed straight for his usual spot.

Marca and Coan were once again amazed by Set's intelligence, and Rei entered the guild with the two of them in tow.

"Whoa! The guild! The guild! ...The guild, huh?"

Maruka cheered the moment they stepped inside the guild, but quickly noticed the sparse crowd within the guild itself. Simultaneously, he raised a questioning voice at the tavern attached to it, which was nearly full.

"Well now, Rei. Isn't this more of a tavern than a guild? Last year when we came, I don't recall the tavern being this popular while the guild itself was completely devoid of adventurers."

Maruka looked up at Rei and muttered.

For the short Rei, being looked up at from below was a very rare experience.

Feeling a hint of pleasure at this, he nodded without showing it on his face and spoke.

"Well, this place is both a guild and a tavern. I don't know much about guilds in other towns, but generally, guilds are often combined with taverns."

"I suppose it's designed so that after reporting completed quests and collecting rewards at the guild, adventurers can just head straight to the tavern to eat and drink. In fact, I've heard that taverns bring in a significant portion of income for guilds everywhere."

Koan murmured, as if continuing Rei's words.

After hearing their explanations, Maruka looked around at both the guild and the tavern again.

Only a handful of adventurers were in the guild area. In contrast, the tavern was nearly full.

"Hmm. But looking at it like this, there are more people at the tavern."

"It's winter now. Adventurers can still operate reasonably well in warmer places or areas with less snow, but unfortunately, Gilm apparently gets quite a lot of snow. Adventurers are on hiatus until it melts."

"Is that so? ...But just because it's winter doesn't mean there are no requests, right? As far as I know, there are monsters that only appear in winter."

"That's where they come in."

Koan's gaze shifted to the adventurers gathered at the guild.

They scanned the request board, searching for any decent jobs.

"Not every adventurer can save enough funds to get through the winter. Those who failed are looking for jobs they can do even in the snow, like that. ...Well then, young lady. Shall we take on a job?"

"Right! Let's go, Rei, Koan!"

Maruka, who had been looking around curiously, nodded at Koan's words and headed toward the guild counter.

There, as usual, were Lenora and Kenny, along with other receptionists.

But with the guild in this state, it was only natural they'd have downtime.

There was paperwork to sort through, but that wasn't something they could do all day.

That's precisely why Lenora and Kenny, who knew Rei well, noticed Rei enter the guild so quickly.

...Though they were also surprised to see him bringing Maruka and Koan along.

(Oh?)

Kenny exchanged a silent glance with Lenora, but suddenly realized he recognized the man with Rei.

(I'm sure... that man... he was talking with Rei-kun at the guild before, right?)

Kenny encountered dozens, hundreds, sometimes even over a thousand adventurers every day. Yet, despite only meeting this man once, she remembered his face—likely because she recognized him as someone connected to Rei.

But more than that, as the guild receptionist, she felt something about that man... Koan.

Rei, Maruka, and Coan headed toward Kenny—or more precisely, toward Lenora standing beside her.

"Well, well, so this is the receptionist. I see. They do have a point about being the face of the guild—they certainly keep the attractive ones around."

Maruka murmured with apparent admiration, looking at Lenora and Kenny.

That said, Lenora and Kenny felt a bit awkward being praised like that.

Because Maruka, who described them as "good-looking," was an incredibly adorable girl who hadn't even reached ten years old yet.

Right now, she was described as adorable, but it was certain that if she continued growing like this, she would become a beauty who attracted attention.

Still, they put on their professional faces and opened their mouths, careful not to show any such thoughts.

"Rei, who are these people? I think the one over there joined the guild last year, right?"

Kenny addressed Rei first.

Koan raised an eyebrow, slightly surprised by Kenny's reaction.

He probably hadn't expected her to remember him after just a brief encounter.

"Excuse me..."

"Enough, Rei. In this case, it's proper for the one making the request to speak. ...I am Maruka Quento. Well, wouldn't it be easier to understand if I said I'm from the Quento Duke's household?"

The moment she spoke, not only the receptionist but also most of the other guild staff behind the counter stared in astonishment.

It was only natural. The House of Quent was one of the most powerful noble houses in the Kingdom of Mireana, possessing some of the largest estates.

Few guild employees could maintain their composure upon hearing they had an associate connected to such a figure.

That said, some recalled him as the person who had come last year and assisted with the rank-up exam, leading them to speculate that might be the connection.

And since Rei, who had ranked up at that time, was present alongside him, it made even more sense.

"My apologies. Lord Marca Quent. What brings you here today?"

Though Kenny usually interacted with adventurers in a lighthearted manner, she had naturally received proper training as a receptionist.

Seeing Kenny bow formally and ask again, Rei flashed a small look of surprise.

Seeing that, it was only natural for Kenny to feel a little dissatisfied inside.

Lenora, standing beside Kenny, seemed to decide to leave this moment to him for now. She watched the exchange unfold without interjecting.

She was ready to step in immediately if Kenny did anything strange.

After all, she was the daughter of a duke. If any strange commotion broke out here, it could even become an issue between the guild and the duke's household.

Unaware of Lenora's thoughts, Maruka addressed Kenny.

"Hmm. I thought I'd request a specific job for Rei. That's why I stopped by the guild."

"Huh? Rei-kun...?

The words that came out of Maruka's mouth couldn't help but give Kenny a bad feeling. His gaze shifted towards Rei.

From Kenny's perspective, it hadn't been that long since Rei had returned from the Bestir Empire—at least, not in Kenny's view.

Yet now a request for him from the duke's household? It made Kenny worry if Ray would even have time to rest.

"Hmm. Actually, I was thinking of having Rei serve as an instructor at the officer academy in Gracielle, where my mistress's family resides."

"...Um, Rei as an instructor?"

His expression seemed to say, "What was I worrying about just seconds ago?"

Though Kenny usually had the air of a seductive older sister, no one would have thought that upon seeing Kenny now, after hearing Maruka's words.

And it wasn't just Kenny. Lenora wore the same expression.

To both of them, the words "Rei" and "instructor" were completely incompatible.

At the very least, neither of them could imagine Rei teaching anyone—especially cadets at the officer academy.

Of course, they knew Rei wasn't entirely unsuited to teaching.

After all, he had fulfilled the request to train Basler last year.

But that was Baslerro... a child barely ten years old.

At the officer academy, the students were in their mid-to-late teens, and some might even be in their twenties.

In other words, the students there would basically be Rei's age or older.

Considering Rei's personality, it was simply impossible to imagine him functioning properly as an instructor.

This wasn't just Kenny and Lenora's opinion. The other guild staff felt the same way.

Fundamentally, Rei was never particularly skilled at interacting with people.

So when they heard Rei was going to be an instructor, it was nothing short of a shock to the guild staff.

Some even worried the academy students might become useless.

Rei was the type to be friendly to those who were friendly and hostile to those who were hostile, and he showed no mercy to those who opposed him.

News had spread through the guard squad as gossip about what happened a few weeks ago: during the dispute between the Baron Sysne family and the next head of the Earl Eriell family, a duel took place. The loser, Keep, tried to cover it up by attacking the Baron Sysne family, only to have both his arms severed.

Yet Rei isn't feared in Gilm, likely because it's widely known he doesn't resort to senseless violence.

...Though it couldn't be denied that Set's presence greatly lessened the fear people felt toward Rei. His habit of spending lavishly at food stalls and other vendors probably played a part too.

"Hmm. Father consulted me about this before. That's when I remembered Rei."

"But, well... it seems to me Rei isn't really suited to teaching others."

Kenny tried to persuade Maruka to reconsider, fearing that if Rei accepted the request, he might leave Gilm again.

(I understand Kenny's feelings, but since we went all the way to Gilm to bring Rei back, I don't think he'll give up that easily. ...But Kenny's right, if Rei becomes an instructor, it could cause all sorts of problems.)

Lenora, who had been listening to Kenny and Maruka's conversation, cast a worried glance toward Rei.

Because Lenora thought of Rei like a younger brother, she worried he might cause trouble at the academy.

"No need to worry about that. Rei is scheduled to handle the mock battles. His strength should be more than enough motivation for the students."

"...I'm afraid it might do more than inspire them—it could crush their confidence."

Knowing Rei's strength all too well, Kenny's words carried a certain weight of conviction.

She tried to persuade him to assign someone other than Rei as an instructor afterward, but Maruka had no intention of listening.

It was only natural. After all, the purpose of this request for appointment was to temporarily protect Rei themselves.

And when Kenny finally stopped trying to persuade him...

"Kenny, that's enough."

The voice of Marina, the Guildmaster, echoed throughout the area.
