Although Relieu still looked a little dissatisfied, he was clearly concerned about the people who had chosen to travel through the woods rather than along the main road.

He arrives at the spot where the carriage overturned, deep in the woods, along with Rei.

But Relieu deeply regretted his decision.

He thought to himself, “I should have just waited outside the woods.”

She truly felt that way.

It wasn’t just Relieu. Rei and Set, who had returned here with her, felt the same way.

...Yellow, who had come here with Elena, was no longer there.

Seeing Marina looking sullen by the carriage, it was only natural.

And the reason Marina was so upset was obvious enough just by looking at the nearly ten women standing near the carriage.

Seeing them shackled at the hands and feet, unable to move freely, made it clear why they had been in the carriage.

No, if that were all, Marina would have been displeased, but she wouldn’t have been this furious.

The biggest reason for her anger was likely the Dark Elf woman who, like the others, was shackled but also bore clear signs of having been beaten on her face and limbs and had lost consciousness.

Seeing a fellow Dark Elf subjected to such treatment, the compassionate Marina could not possibly remain indifferent.

Naturally, Marina couldn’t just leave a fellow Dark Elf with such injuries, so she used water spirit magic to heal her.

Seeing Marina like that, Rei took out a fairly high-rank potion—meaning one with strong healing effects—from his Misty Ring.

This potion was a gift from the magic item shop as a token of appreciation for the special potion he’d brought back from Somis yesterday.

The deal was that, in exchange for bringing the special potion, the shopkeeper would give him something reasonably expensive from their stock of magic items... but there wasn’t anything at Golz’s magic item shop that Rei particularly wanted.

As a result, he ended up bringing back a few relatively high-rank potions that wouldn’t hurt to have on hand… and it seems they’ve already come in handy.

That said, Rei had already stocked up on a decent supply of potions just in case, so even if the matter with Somis’s potion hadn’t come up, he probably wouldn’t have been in any real trouble.

“Marina, use this.”

“...Thank you.”

With an expression unlike her usual self—as if she were suppressing her anger—she took the potion and used it on the dark elf.

“Still…”

Rei had been watching Marina, but when Relieu spoke, he turned his gaze toward her.

Whether Relieu noticed the gaze directed at her or not… in any case, she continued speaking while looking at the carriage.

“This makes it clear why this carriage was traveling through the woods instead of on the main road. Rei said it was either because there was a reason or because they had something to hide—and it turns out it was the latter, without a doubt.”

“Ah… yeah. There’s no doubt they’re illegal slave traders.”

Though he wasn’t necessarily following Relieu’s line of thought, Rei spoke up in agreement.

Slave traders, of course, are those who buy and sell slaves.

However, there are many different kinds of slaves.

There are slaves sold for money, slaves who sold themselves to pay off debts... and then there are criminal slaves, the type Rei is most familiar with.

By “most familiar,” he doesn’t mean that Rei buys criminal slaves, but rather that he sells the surviving bandits—those he has defeated… no, hunted down—as slaves, as he did this time.

In any case, while there are several other types of slaves… among them, forcing someone to become a slave by taking them away by force is, of course, illegal.

And the people crammed into the carriage that Marina and the others opened were those who had been taken away by such illegal means.

Naturally, if caught doing such a thing, the slave trader would face a very severe punishment.

That is likely why this slave trader was traveling through the woods rather than along a main road where they would be easily spotted.

As a result, they ended up catching the attention of bandits, so this outcome was their own doing.

“For now, I’ll leave these guys to you. I’m going to go question the bandits.”

Not wanting to stay by the grumpy Marina’s side, Rei heads toward the bandits tied up with rope.

Seeing Rei like that, the bandits tried to put on a brave front... but when they saw Set standing next to her, there was no way they could pull that off.

Especially since Rei had just beaten them to a pulp, it was only natural that they would look at him with fear in their eyes.

“Well then, where should I start… Hmm, I suppose I should introduce myself first. Since I’m with Set, some of you probably already know, but I’m Rei. I go by the nickname ‘Crimson’… but for you lot, ‘Bandit Eater’ might be easier to understand?”

“Huh!?”

At Rei’s words, the bandits—except for those who had already guessed his true identity from the fact that he was with Set—had their faces turn pale with shock.

And once he saw their shock turn to despair, Rei asked his usual question.

“So, where is your hideout? And how many of you are still there?”

“……If we don’t tell you, what are you going to do? Kill us?”

“Hmm. I suppose I could.”

The man who asked Rei that question was probably trying to negotiate.

If he killed them, he wouldn’t be able to sell them as slaves. Was that really okay?

In fact, there was no doubt that capturing bandits and selling them as slaves would fetch a decent sum.

That was precisely why he’d said those words… but neither the bandit he was speaking to nor the other bandits standing nearby ever imagined he’d so casually say he’d kill them.

The word “kill” is one that bandits use all the time.

But usually, it carries a strong connotation of a threat. Though, to be fair, there are quite a few who don’t hesitate to follow through.

In any case, the word “kill” that Rei spoke was neither a threat nor anything of the sort. It was truly meant in the most literal sense.

What’s more, Rei, who uttered those words, showed absolutely no hesitation about the act of killing. His attitude made it seem as though their lives would be mowed down just as easily as the grass around them.

“Whoa, I get it! I’ll tell you! I’ll tell you, so please spare us!”

The man who had tried to negotiate immediately shouted this at Rei.

He understood that if he hesitated even for a moment, Rei would kill him.

Many of the other bandits must have realized this from Rei’s demeanor as well. They shouted that they would obey Rei’s orders, looking panicked.

...Among them, there were some who weren’t quite that sharp, wondering why the others were obeying Rei so readily.

But they were smart enough to realize that speaking up alone wouldn’t accomplish anything here, and besides, with the seniors they owed a debt of gratitude all signaling with their eyes for him to obey as well, he couldn’t possibly refuse.

“I see. Well then, let’s hear what you have to say right away.”

Saying that, Rei began to extract information from the bandit about the location of their hideout and the people still there.

“Alright, I’m going to head over to the hideout for a bit. Can I leave this place in your hands?”

“Are you going alone, Rei? Well, I don’t think there’ll be any problem, though.”

Relieu’s words reflected his trust in Rei’s strength.

But there was no doubt that he also harbored a sense of uncertainty about what might happen at the bandits’ hideout.

Rei understood how Relieu felt, so he simply said there shouldn’t be any problems for now and left the scene with Set.

Fortunately, the location of the hideout they’d pried out of the bandits didn’t seem to be too far from here, so there was no doubt they’d arrive in no time if they rode on Set.

“They said there’s a mountain beyond the forest, and a cave right after you enter it… but where is it?”

“Growl? …Grrrr.”

At Rei’s words, Set also looked down at the ground, but much of the area was hidden by the autumn leaves and evergreen trees, and they couldn’t find the cave the bandit had mentioned.

That said, it was hard to believe the bandit had lied, so it was clear the trees were simply obscuring the ground.

(It’s hidden remarkably well. He said he’d only arrived in this area a few days ago, but for a coincidence, he seems to have found a pretty good hideout.)

I don’t think bandits operating in this area would be that concerned about surveillance from above.

In other words, either a tree happened to grow there and is covering the cave’s entrance, or it’s simply a cave that’s hard to spot from above.

“Oh well. In that case, I’ll give up searching from above and just walk through the mountains to look for it.”

“Growl?”

"Is that okay?" Set asked, clearing his throat.

He was worried that doing so would take too long to find the cave, delaying their return to the site of the overturned carriage.

But Rei gently stroked Set, who was staring at her with his round eyes, trying not to worry him.

“We can’t find it, so there’s nothing we can do. Besides, we need to heal the wounds of those captured women and ask them where they came from. And then there’s the matter of the Dark Elf.”

Rei didn’t know what had driven the man—presumed to be a slave trader—to inflict such cruelty on the Dark Elf.

She had wondered if it was to prevent them from using spirit magic, but being tortured doesn’t actually render one unable to use spirit magic.

If that’s the case, perhaps the slave trader simply hated the Dark Elves…

Whatever the reason, it’s certainly not good news for Marina, who is a Dark Elf herself.

To begin with, neither Elves nor Dark Elves are particularly numerous.

Precisely because of that, the sense of camaraderie among their kind is quite strong.

Thinking of Marina, Rei and Set descended to the ground and entered the mountains.

After searching for about ten minutes...

“Grr!”

Set, having caught the scent of a human, let out a short growl.

They finally find the place they’re looking for.

Of course, just because he’s picked up a human scent doesn’t necessarily mean this is the place they’re looking for.

After all, we can’t rule out the possibility that it’s just a hunter who happened to come into the mountains.

But... even so, given the current situation, it’s highly likely that what Set has picked up on is a group of bandits.

So, following Set... eventually, just as expected, they arrived at the bandits’ hideout.

“...Hey, is this... Damn, I expected it, but I didn’t think it would come to this.”

Most of the bandits had apparently gone out to raid the illegal slave trader that Rei and the others had found earlier, so only a few remained here… and even those weren’t particularly skilled.

He had told them that if they surrendered peacefully, he wouldn’t kill them, but the bandits chose to resist… and now, their corpses lay exposed outside the cave.

As far as Rei was concerned, honestly, it didn’t matter to him whether they surrendered or fought back.

Since the bandits ultimately chose the latter, it wasn’t such a bad thing for Rei to be able to test out the Multi-Slash and Corrosion effects she’d recently mastered in actual combat.

So, after killing a few bandits, he entered their hideout—the cave—to search for their treasure… and among it, he found a familiar potion.

A special potion that was supposed to be delivered to Golz’s magic item shop.

The fact that it was here meant this must be the band of thieves Rei had been searching for yesterday.

"Even though I couldn’t find them no matter how hard I tried yesterday, I never imagined I’d stumble upon them by such a coincidence today..." Rei felt a mix of emotions.

(Is this like how things never turn up when you’re actively looking for them, but you unexpectedly find them a few days after you’ve given up?)

Thinking that, he decides to pack up the treasures inside, figuring they’re special potions after all.

It might be a stretch to call it “lucky,” but there was no sign of anyone being held captive inside the hideout.

Perhaps this band of thieves simply didn’t engage in such practices, or maybe they had moved their operations elsewhere and didn’t bring the prisoners along because they’d be in the way.

Rei didn’t know the answer to that either, but one thing was certain: there was definitely no one inside the hideout.

That said, since there were hardly any items valuable enough to be called treasure, there weren’t any magic items that would make Rei happy.

Still, there was a fair amount of food, and while there were signs that the long swords and spears had been used to some extent, there were only a few of them.

There was also a box filled with a large amount of cloth that appeared to have been looted from merchants, various small items, and a fair amount of tableware and other things—perhaps intended for their own use—as well as gems, gold coins, and silver coins… In the end, it turned out to be a harvest that was reasonably satisfying.
