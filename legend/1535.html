After finishing her meal that night, Ara was relaxing in her room when she suddenly noticed the Paired Orbs activate.

"Elena? But it seems much earlier than usual..."

Even though it was night, it wasn't that late yet.

Usually, Elena's contact would come a bit later.

That very fact made her feel a twinge of doubt... Yet, unable to hide her smile at the prospect of speaking with Elena sooner than usual, Ara approached the Paired Orb.

And soon, as expected, the figure of Elena, whom Ara had been longing to see, appeared on the Paired Orb.

"My lady Elena, is something the matter? It seems much earlier than usual."

'Ah. I have a small request for you, Ara... no, rather, something I wish to inform Lord Daskar about.'

"Lord Daskar? Of course, I don't mind... but no, contacting me at this hour means it's urgent, right now?"

'I'm afraid so. It's become clear this matter is deeply connected to the current situation. I need you to take the Paired Orb and go see Lord Daskar. ...I considered sending a message, but given the importance of this matter, it's better to speak directly.'

"Understood. I'll head to Lord Daskar's immediately. Once I arrive, would it be acceptable for me to contact you again from there?"

"I'll count on you."

And with that, the brief communication ended.

Though Ara regretted that the conversation with Elena had ended so abruptly, she had no intention of refusing a request from Elena.

It was already night, and normally, heading to see someone of such high standing at this hour—especially Daskar, who ruled over Gilm—would be considered downright rude.

But, fully aware of this, Ara prepared to go out.

...Though when one says "preparing to go out," for most women it meant applying makeup and dressing up. For Ara, however, it meant preparing by donning weapons and armor.

Even so, while not quite as stunning as Elena and the others, Ara possessed features that could certainly be called beautiful, making her stand out more than women who wore only half-hearted makeup.

No, perhaps it's more accurate to say that in Ara's case, she arms herself precisely to avoid being accosted or harassed by men.

In any case, Ara left the inn as she was and headed for the stables.

Inside the stables were the military horses that pulled Elena's carriage.

Naturally, the military horses ran faster than she could run herself.

Of course, that "naturally" comes with various exceptions, but at the very least, it was undeniably faster for Ara to travel by military horse than to run herself.

"Well then, I'll leave it to you."

"Hee-hee-hee-hee!"

In response to Ara's request, one of the military horses let out a cheerful neigh and immediately began to run.

It was fortunate for Ara that, being nighttime, far fewer people were walking the streets compared to evening.

Even so, due to the current state of Gilm with its ongoing construction, a considerable number of people were still walking the streets. Naturally, upon seeing the military horse gallop by, they instinctively moved out of the way.

Arriving before the lord's mansion, Ara... was, unsurprisingly, met with suspicion by the gate guards, who thrust their spears at her.

After all, a horse charging toward the lord's residence at night, without any prior notice... and a military horse considerably larger than an ordinary one at that—their actions were entirely justified.

"Who are you?!"

"I am Ara Skarley, under the command of Lady Elena Kerebel! I have come to inform Lord Daskar of urgent messages from Lady Elena and Lord Rei!"

The mention of the name Rei caused the guards to relax their tension slightly.

Rei had visited this lord's residence many times before, and the gatekeepers were familiar with him.

Of course, just because Rei's name was mentioned didn't mean they could fully trust him.

However, the guards had seen Elena and Ara, who followed her. When the light produced by the magic item illuminated Ara's face, they finally lowered their spears.

"My apologies, Lady Ara. I'll notify them inside immediately, so please wait a moment."

"Yes, understood. But since this is an urgent message from Lady Elena and Rei, please hurry as much as possible."

As Ara dismounted her warhorse and spoke, one of the gatekeepers immediately entered the lord's residence.

Though it was night, many people remained within the lord's residence.

Those waiting outside the manor to see the lord were gone, but many still worked inside the manor day and night.

Amidst these workers, the steward, having received word from the gatekeeper, hurried onward.

...Though he hurried, he did not run recklessly.

For a butler to behave in such a manner would bring shame upon his master.

Moving with urgency yet without running, the butler arrived at the office door and knocked.

A voice immediately called out for him to enter. Opening the door... there sat Daskar, his desk piled high with stacks of documents.

"What's going on? It sounds rather noisy."

The butler bowed slightly and spoke as Daskar asked while glancing over the papers.

"It seems Lady Ara is here."

"...Ara? At this hour?"

Daskar turned his gaze to the window and saw a scene shrouded in the darkness of night.

Though the lights from the lord's mansion provided some illumination, they couldn't dispel all the darkness of the night.

Moreover, the moon, which normally cast its soft light upon the earth, was hidden behind clouds tonight, its form nowhere to be seen.

And since the moonlight did not reach the ground, naturally, not a single faint starlight reached it either.

Gazing at the night scene, Daskar turned his attention back to the butler.

"Yes. It seems there is urgent communication from Lady Elena and Rei."

It was only natural that Daskar flashed a bitter expression for a moment at the butler's words.

It was an urgent message from Rei and the others, who had gone to retaliate against the organization that had attacked Gilm.

If it was news that the retaliation had been successfully completed, then Rei and the others could have simply returned to Gilm and delivered the message themselves.

If Set flew, he could return to Gilm at a speed incomparable to traveling by land.

But since that wasn't the case, it was easy to guess that something unexpected had happened.

"Understood. Connect them immediately. We've just wrapped up a segment here anyway. We'll take a break and hear what they have to say. Bring drinks and something light to eat."

"Yes, I'll prepare it immediately."

Watching the butler exit the office after saying that... Daskar turned his gaze back to the window and sighed.

"Something troublesome has definitely happened, huh?"

Though he muttered this, Daskar had no intention of worrying about Rei.

He knew full well how capable Rei was and what she had accomplished.

Moreover, Rei now led the party known as Crimson Wings, and the figure known as Princess General Elena Kerebel was also acting alongside him.

No joke, they possess enough firepower to overwhelm even a full-strength army.

(And Marina's with them too.)

He recalled the dark elf who knew his own dark past.

Marina has a difficult personality in many ways, but her ability to wield spirit magic with complete freedom could, in certain situations, enable her to unleash area-of-effect attacks rivaling Rei's.

...Of course, its destructive power would undoubtedly be significantly less than Rei's.

Spirit magic, usable for far more than just attack, truly possessed power worthy of being called universal.

Honestly, Daskar felt there was nothing to worry about as long as such individuals possessed such immense power in so many ways.

He rose from his chair and stretched his body lightly.

The cracking of his bones attested to how long Daskar had been buried in paperwork.

In that sense, taking a short break probably wasn't such a bad idea after all.

"Hmm... I'm tired. Just how long is this busyness going to last? Well, the expansion project was my own decision, so I can't really complain to anyone."

Daskar stretched again, then closed his eyes, lost in thought for a moment. Soon, however, the sound of knocking on the door reached his ears.

He told her to come in. The maid placed a tray laden with tea and a large plate piled high with bite-sized sandwiches on the table before withdrawing.

Watching her leave, Daskar promptly began eating the sandwiches and drinking his tea. Soon, the sound of knocking echoed through the room once more.

"Come in."

"Excuse me. I have brought Ara."

The butler from earlier announced, and Ara appeared.

"My apologies, Lord Daskar. At this hour..."

"No, it's fine. You came all this way at such an hour. There must be a good reason, right? What message did you receive from Elena?"

"Yes, please take a look at this."

Saying this, Ara produced a Paired Orb.

Daskar knew of the Paired Orbs' existence, so he showed no particular surprise.

She moved the sandwich plate slightly and placed the Paired Orb upon it.

Then, as Ara infused them with magic, Elena's image soon appeared within the Paired Orb.

No, it wasn't just Elena. The figures of Rei, Marina, and Vihera were also reflected.

The absence of Byune and Yellow wasn't particularly strange, considering the subject of the upcoming discussion.

When Ara had spoken earlier, only Elena had been present, but since a fair amount of time had passed since then, Ara concluded they must have moved with the Paired Orb.

"My apologies for disturbing you at this hour, Lord Daskar."

Elena was the first to speak.

Daskar shook his head at Elena's words.

"No, it's fine. Fortunately, I was just about to take a break anyway. ...So, what's the matter?"

"Actually..."

Hesitating slightly, Elena explained what she had learned in Rossi, and above all, the incident Rei had predicted.

At first, Daskar nodded at Elena's words, but as he heard the details of what Rei had predicted, his expression gradually hardened.

Given Daskar's naturally intimidating appearance, his expression was enough to make a child start crying if they saw it.

The explanation coming from Elena's mouth was that shocking and, at the same time, that serious.

If it had been a prediction with no basis whatsoever, he might have dismissed it outright.

But Daskar's trust in Rei... and the fact that Marina, though she didn't want to admit it, hadn't contradicted Rei's prediction—that was why he couldn't dismiss it as mere conjecture.

"Foolish... What a foolish thing to do..."

Daskar understood why the Kingdom of Reburina resented being treated as a vassal state of the Kingdom of Mireana.

No, indeed, few would be satisfied with their own country being treated as a vassal state of another.

But... even so, to desire power and resort to such inhumane means... and worse, to forcibly abduct another nation's women and use them as sustenance... that was utterly unforgivable.

On top of that, Jaya has even extended its hand to Gilm... to the Kingdom of Mireana itself.

Of course, Daskar isn't entirely incapable of understanding that sentiment.

To create a single giant... no, to give birth to one, it seems the life of a single woman is required.

Moreover, for beings called giants, it's not guaranteed that all the infants conceived will be safely delivered by their mothers.

Given that such a process presupposes the death of the mothers, he did not wish to use his own country's women... And above all, if they must use women, let the Kingdom of Reburina use the women of the Kingdom of Mireana, whom they hate with a passion.

Using giants born from their own people to inflict damage upon the Kingdom of Mireana.

Considering that, it wouldn't be strange at all.

"Of course, this is merely my speculation; there's no definitive proof at this point."

Rei, standing beside Elena, stated this to Daskar.

But even hearing that, Daskar couldn't feel reassured.

While there was no definitive proof, the circumstantial evidence alone made the situation almost certain.

"For now, can you tell us exactly how urgent the situation is? Like, preparing that many giants and expecting the Kingdom of Reburina to revolt immediately or something?"

"No, unfortunately, we don't have that far ahead."

"...I see. Well, that's a bit problematic. Understood. I'll handle the groundwork for this matter. You two, do as you please. ...Got it? Do as you please, got it?"

Daskar declared this, wearing a smile as savage as a ferocious carnivore.
