Watching Ragdo's figure disappear into the distance, Rei soon noticed multiple footsteps approaching him.

He turned his gaze in that direction, and sure enough, it was exactly who he expected to see.

Elena wore a wry smile, Vihera a look of amusement, Ara a complex expression that suggested she didn't know whether to be pleased or angry, and Byune her usual blank face.

Set and Yellow were also heading toward Rei.

Amidst this...

"You idiot, Rei."

Marina, looking slightly exasperated, called out to Rei.

In response to Marina, Rei shrugged his shoulders slightly before speaking.

"It doesn't feel right that you're getting blamed for covering for me. Besides, even if I needed covering, it'd only be for something truly fatal to me. It's just the magic power thing."

Marina smiled faintly at Rei's matter-of-fact words.

"You're strong, Rei."

"Is that so?"

"Yes. To hear the way Audban looks at Rei and still say that with such conviction... it's pretty impressive."

"I don't know. Maybe I'm just naturally oblivious to that sort of thing."

"Oh? So does that mean this whole thing was unnecessary?"

Rei shook his head slightly at Marina, who wore a mischievous smile.

"Just because I say I don't mind doesn't mean I don't feel uncomfortable. It's just that, between me getting unpleasant looks and Marina getting blamed, I figured the former was still better."

Huh?

Marina seemed momentarily confused by what he'd said, but once she grasped the meaning of Rei's words, a delighted smile spread across her face.

"You really are... such an idiot."

Her words clearly scolded Rei, but her expression told a completely different story.

"Alright, alright. Sorry to interrupt your lovely moment, but let's leave it at that. Don't you think we should focus on what to do next instead?"

Seeing the somewhat romantic atmosphere between Rei and Marina, Vihera, who had been watching from a short distance away, wore a disappointed expression.

Optis had initially challenged Rei to a fight to see if he was worthy of Marina, but through that battle, he had come to understand what kind of person Rei was.

He had his dangerous sides, but he had still stood up to Ragdo for the sake of his beloved granddaughter.

For Optis, whose own son and daughter-in-law were already deceased, feeling gratitude toward Rei was only natural.

...Though, given his position as the village elder, he couldn't voice that sentiment here.

"Yes. Even if we're going to treat the World Tree, we can't just leave things as they are today. Ideally, we should treat the World Tree immediately, but with all this commotion... you see?"

Those Dark Elves who could sense magic likely understood what kind of person Rei was.

But it was also true that many recognized Rei as a dangerous opponent.

Given that, it was certain that if they proceeded with healing the World Tree alone, they would inevitably be viewed with suspicion.

To avoid unnecessary trouble, it was essential that someone with no connection to them... or at least a tenuous one... be present during the World Tree's treatment.

But even if they tried to pull something like that right now, there was no way anyone would be willing to come along.

(The World Tree is weakened, but it's not as if we need to rush it today or tomorrow. If that's the case, tomorrow should be fine. And those who fear Rei... by tomorrow...)

As this thought crossed her mind, Audban's face flashed before her eyes.

He hadn't come here because he had plans to meet a friend, but if he had been here, he would have looked at Rei the same way everyone else did.

Moreover, it took Audban a certain amount of time after their first meeting before his attitude toward Rei began to soften.

Thinking about it that way, even if I wanted to do it tomorrow... wouldn't it be difficult? I couldn't help but think.

Still, a day or two might be manageable, but leaving ten or twenty days between meetings would definitely be unwise.

(Then, should I ask Grandfather to come? That might be an option... but would the Ragdos accept it?)

Though politically opposed, Marina doesn't hate Ragdo.

She understood that, though their methods differed, Ragdo also acted with the settlement's best interests in mind.

(Ragdo... Hmm, then perhaps bringing Ragdo along is an option. Everyone here witnessed today's incident, so they know Ragdo and I are adversaries. If I bring Ragdo, that should be proof enough that Rei won't act strangely, right?)

Concluding this was the best course of action, she smiled and spoke.

"Well, I'm sure we'll manage. But since we have the chance, I'd like to make a dish using Aero Wing meat. What do you think?"

Though Marina's words seemed like an attempt to change the subject, the others—who did indeed want to eat Aero Wing meat—nodded in agreement.

"Grandfather, is it settled that Rei will be released?"

"Indeed. If we didn't release him here, the others would likely panic... no, they wouldn't accept it. As for lodging, he can use the house those young ladies are staying in."

Recalling the reactions of those with magic-sensing abilities, Optis smiled slightly and addressed his granddaughter.

In that smile lay both his pure affection for his granddaughter and his stance as the elder of this settlement.

"Thank you, Grandfather. However, Rei is still a man, so he can't stay under the same roof as a woman... especially a duchess's daughter of high standing. There was a house a little way from Elena-sama's place, wasn't there? It has stables, so Set can stay too. Wouldn't that be better?"

Marina suggested separating Elena and Rei as if it were the most natural thing, but from the Guildmaster's standpoint, it was only logical.

At Marina's words, Elena wore an expression that was both pleased and regretful.

Having been raised as a duchess's daughter, Elena naturally held strong notions of chastity.

Common sense told her that living under the same roof as a man was inappropriate.

Yet, conversely, Elena's true feelings naturally included a desire to live with Rei.

...Having originally fought on the battlefield, Elena felt no resistance to sleeping under the same roof as a man.

But that was precisely because she didn't see him as a man... or more accurately, as a romantic interest.

It might seem contradictory to her sense of chastity, but within Elena, it coexists without any conflict.

"Oh? That's a shame. I wouldn't have minded sharing a house with Rei... no, even sharing a room. Heck, I wouldn't have minded sharing a bed either?"

In stark contrast to Elena's inner conflict, Vihera murmured with genuine disappointment.

Since Vihera never hid her feelings for Rei to begin with, she could voice her emotions honestly.

"Grrrrr!"

In stark contrast to the two of them, Set purred happily.

As Marina had mentioned, the presence of stables meant he could be near Rei.

"Kyuu?"

Yellow, perched daintily atop Set's head, looked around completely bewildered, seemingly unaware of what was happening.

Watching this somehow soothing scene, Marina suddenly spoke up.

"Ah, come to think of it, there's something I need to do before we cook the Aerowings. I'm sorry, but could you get things ready first? I'll head over as soon as I finish my errand."

For a moment, most of those present turned their gaze toward Marina.

But the only response to those stares was a smile.

Seeing that, they must have decided it wasn't a problem. Without saying anything back to Marina, Rei and the others left the plaza.

"Where are we going to cut up the Aerowings? They're pretty big, so we'll need a fairly wide open space, right?"

"Well, the area behind the house we're renting is a bit spacious. How about there, Byune?"

"...Why ask Byune about that?"

"Because Byune is good at dismantling. Apparently she picked up the skill when she was soloing dungeons."

"Hmm."

Marina watched the group walk away, exchanging such conversation, with a hint of envy.

For just a few seconds, she imagined herself in their place, and a faint sense of regret welled up inside her... But she had returned to this settlement after nearly a century to heal the World Tree. She needed to focus her attention on that task.

"Are you going?"

She nodded in response to Optis's words.

For now, the greatest concern—that Rei would be harmed... or more accurately, that Rei would retaliate against any harm and cause major damage to the settlement—had been avoided.

Therefore, the next thing Marina needed to investigate was that pulsation Rei had felt when passing through the barrier's protective field.

She didn't know what caused it... no, she knew exactly what caused it. It happened when Rei passed through, so it must have reacted to Rei's immense magical power.

But whether that reaction was harmful... or beneficial... that remains to be confirmed.

Of course, sensing such a massive pulse, several Dark Elves undoubtedly went to check on the World Tree at that very moment.

In fact, I've already received reports confirming that no immediate ill effects have befallen the World Tree.

Even so, Marina wanted to see the World Tree's condition with her own eyes.

She felt it was her duty, having brought Rei here. And if Rei's presence truly did harm the World Tree, they would need to leave the settlement immediately.

"I see. ...Be careful. I don't think there's anything to worry about with Marina, but still, that pulse could have caused some change."

"Yes. I'll observe it with the utmost care."

With that, Marina turned and walked away.

Her destination was the World Tree, deep within the settlement.

What impact had the pulsation caused when Rei entered the settlement...? With great anxiety, and a faint hope that the World Tree, sensing Rei's magic, might be recovering somewhat, she made her way through the settlement.

Along the way, several Dark Elves called out to her. She waved back, exchanged words, and continued on her path.

One man, seemingly on a whim, tried to flirt with her. But Marina, accustomed to such advances, brushed him off effortlessly and kept walking.

Gradually, the number of Dark Elves decreased as they advanced.

The reason was likely that they were heading deeper into the settlement... towards the location of the World Tree.

The World Tree itself was visible from anywhere in the settlement.

It was only natural, given how enormous it was, towering above them.

Though its leaves were still lush and green, the vitality and sheer presence emanating from it seemed noticeably weaker compared to when she had left the settlement.

Gazing at the World Tree, Marina suddenly realized something.

"Hmm?"

It was a very slight difference.

But it was an undeniable difference.

Only those sensitive to magic could perceive it... and at the same time, only those of a special lineage like Marina's.

Understanding what it meant, Marina let a small smile touch her lips.

She had probably thought it would be fine, but seeing it confirmed with her own eyes brought a great sense of relief.

That difference was...

"I never imagined that just entering the barrier's protective sphere would allow Rei to recover, even a little... I knew it, but just how incredible is Rei's magic power?"

Marina murmured with a hint of amazement, yet her expression was thick with joy.

She understood now that bringing Rei along had absolutely not been a mistake.

Marina had been confident it would be fine, but obtaining this solid confirmation was still immensely important.

"If that's the case... if we have the World Tree channel magic to him tomorrow, he'll recover immediately. If so, perhaps we shouldn't linger here any longer."

She sighed, but a lingering doubt remained: could that truly resolve the root cause of the World Tree's weakness?

Considering Rei's magic, it seemed almost certain, but she couldn't say for absolute.

"Even after it recovers, we should probably monitor it for a little while... right?"

Regardless, the fact that Rei's presence had brought about even a slight recovery in the World Tree was undeniably good news.
