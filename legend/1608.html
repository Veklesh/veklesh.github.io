"Hey, don't just jump in like that!"

After her first encounter with Daskar—though it was through Paired Orbs—Sura scowled at Rei and snapped.

The conversation itself wasn't particularly long.

Daskar was extremely busy at the moment.

In fact, it was rare that Sura could speak with Daskar so quickly like this.

...Though the reason for that was mostly Rei's fault.

Not only had Gilm's expansion project increased his workload, but gathering researchers and engineers to make the Sand Ships usable on land, along with constructing the buildings and laboratories needed for that work, had further intensified Daskar's busyness.

Furthermore, dismantling the Giganto Turtle Ray defeated was no simple task due to its sheer size.

Because of all this, Daskar's workload, which had started to settle down somewhat, didn't decrease at all... no, it had actually increased a little.

Unbeknownst to Rei, Daskar was even taking potions to replenish his stamina.

And of course, not cheap, low-quality potions, but high-grade, premium potions.

"Even if it was sudden... we were bound to meet eventually, so why not now? It wasn't even face-to-face, just through the Paired Orb."

Watching Elena and Ara chatting happily after Daskar left, Rei replied to Sura.

Actually, that wasn't wrong.

Getting Daskar to meet Sura beforehand and remember her name would undoubtedly be useful later.

But even knowing that, suddenly meeting Daskar face-to-face was probably a significant burden for Sura in many ways.

...Sura, who led the resistance in the Kingdom of Reburina, and Daskar, the central figure of the Neutral Faction, one of the three major factions within the Kingdom of Mireana.

The gap between their respective positions was overwhelmingly vast, so it was no wonder Sura was so furious.

"Alright, I'll be more careful next time. But anyway, today's business is done. For tomorrow's sake, get some sleep as soon as possible and rest up."

"Listen... You suddenly make me meet such a high-ranking official, and then tell me to go to sleep right away... How am I supposed to sleep?"

Sura declared with an expression mingled with exasperation and indignation. Yet around them, many had already fallen asleep early, utterly exhausted from walking all day.

To Rei's ears, far more sensitive than a normal person's, she could hear voices crying. Whether from regret or sadness, it reminded her of her time as a prostitute.

Many had given their bodies to strangers, selling their purity for money.

For those with strong moral convictions, it was no wonder they wept bitterly.

Last night, freed from their slave collars, they had walked on without rest... and then the cavalry attack that night meant many still felt no real liberation from their lives as prostitutes.

But today, there hadn't been any particular commotion. Precisely because of that, they were reminded of their current situation... and of how they had been embraced by men as prostitutes all this time.

Even hearing those voices, Rei knew there was nothing he could do about the problem, so he could only leave them be.

"You should try to sleep as soon as possible. Last night was late because of the cavalry incident, and this morning was early again with deciding on the carriage and such. If you don't rest here now, you might not even notice it, but it'll interfere with tomorrow's journey."

"...I understand."

Rei's words were undoubtedly true, but for some reason, Sura didn't particularly like hearing it from him.

She knew it was sound advice, but...

"Hey, Sura! I need to talk to you about the lookout duty! Come here!"

While Rei and Sura were talking, a Resistance man called out to Sura.

Noticing Sura was talking to Rei, the man hurriedly bowed his head to Rei.

He was Sura's subordinate, but his reaction stemmed from knowing just how indispensable Rei was to this group.

...Though it was also simply out of awe after witnessing Rei's power.

The sight of Ray single-handedly defeating the Jaya soldiers who had been giving the Resistance such a hard time was seared into the man's mind.

"Ah, don't worry about it. We're done here."

"Ah, yes. Understood. Sura, let's go."

"That's fine... but isn't your attitude toward me and Rei a bit too different?"

Moving alongside the Resistance man, Sura voiced her dissatisfaction.

But at Sura's words, the man just smiled as if to say, "What can you do?"

"That strength, huh? You saw it too, right? And from what I heard, he killed all the giants too... Oh, no. Sorry."

The moment the topic turned to the giants, a dark shadow settled over Sura's expression.

Knowing how the giants were born... knowing that, it was only natural to have mixed feelings about their existence.

Especially since Sura was a woman.

To have her own child, unwanted yet forced upon her, transformed into a giant, only for that child to rip open her belly and devour her alive... expecting her to feel nothing about that would be impossible.

"It's fine. ...What the organization Jaya did in Mejogo, and the fact that the upper echelons of the Kingdom of Reburina were involved... it's utterly unforgivable. We can't do anything about it now... but the injustice we feel, Rei and the others will handle it."

Sura began discussing the watch with the man, as if trying to convince herself.

"Haaaaaah!"

With a battle cry, Vihera kicked off the ground, closing the distance between herself and Rei.

The morning sun revealed the elegant curves beneath Vihera's thin robe... Yet, faced with a sight that would captivate any ordinary man, Rei focused solely on Vihera's advancing movements.

A fist extending straight toward him.

Its speed was something a normal human couldn't possibly react to.

But Rei evaded the blow by leaping backward.

No matter how fast a fist is, a fist is still a fist.

It couldn't extend beyond the length of Vihera's arm.

(Well, I hear there are some folks out there who dislocate their wrist joints on purpose to extend their reach...)

Thinking this, Rei steps forward toward Vihera, who missed his strike.

Kicking off the ground, Rei closed the distance in an instant.

It was far too late to call it a counter, but if that strike connected, Vihera would certainly not escape unscathed.

"Whoa!"

...Yes, if it had connected.

Rei's swinging fist was caught by Vihera's left hand.

The impact left Vihera's hand slightly numb, but ultimately, that was all it amounted to.

Both knew this was training.

Vihera had removed his gauntlets and greaves, and Rei wasn't wielding weapons like the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear.

For them, it might be closer to a kind of play than actual training.

That said, it was only how they perceived it. If asked whether an observer would feel the same, the answer was no.

"Whoa, wait, what was that...?"

The one muttering in disbelief was the woman who had just woken up a few minutes ago.

She had been sleeping on the grassy plain, and because her sleep happened to be light, she woke up a little earlier than the others.

And what she saw was Rei and Vihera sparring.

It was a highly stimulating sight to see first thing in the morning.

She knew Rei and the others were strong, and she'd seen it with her own eyes.

Even so... no, precisely because of that, seeing them fight like this made her understand that the battle back then had not been a dream.

Besides the woman who first noticed the fight, others would naturally catch on too, given the intensity of their mock battle.

"Hey, what's all this commotion... huh?"

"Look at that. Can humans even move like that?"

"...If bandits thought we were easy prey and attacked, those bandits would probably end up looking pretty pitiful."

Those who woke up and witnessed the mock battle each voiced their own impressions.

If Rei had heard them, he would have recalled the word 'flag'. And then...

"Yee-haw! Check it out, bro! Women, women, women! A whole group of women! This is the best prey ever for us, right?! Damn it, what's with this Jaya nonsense? Does he really think he can do anything to us with that?!"

"Heh heh heh. You're right, bro. ...But seeing so many hot chicks together like this, we really are lucky, huh? After we've had our fill of fun, we can just sell them off as slaves..."

After noon, as Rei and the others grew irritated by the slightly weaker sunlight compared to yesterday, a group of about twenty bandits appeared before them.

Two men, presumably leading the bandits, shouted this while looking at Rei and his group, laughing from the bottom of their hearts.

To the bandits, Rei and his group must have seemed like nothing but prime prey.

"...Hey, Ryutas. It was like this when we first met you too, but did Jaya really not have that much control over the bandits?"

"That's a tough one to hear. If word of the Mejogo incident spreads, Jaya's reputation will take a hit, and more people like that will probably show up... but it's still too early for that. So, it must be coincidence."

Ryutas replied to Rei with an expression that was hard to read.

As someone who belonged to Jaya just days ago, he probably had mixed feelings about the current situation.

"Hey, you two! What the hell are you talking about?! Do you even realize your situation?!"

"That's right! You're about to meet your end as our prey!"

The two leaders of the bandits declared in unison.

But facing them, Rei opened his mouth in utter disbelief.

"First off, there are only about twenty of you, right? What exactly do you plan to do against this many people?"

"Hah, most of them are women, aren't they? Right, brother?"

"Yeah, you're right. With this..."

One of the men leading the bandits stopped mid-sentence.

The reason was Set, who had emerged from among the women.

At first, many had been intimidated by Set's size and the fact he was a griffin, a rank A monster. But after walking alongside him for so long, some of the women began to take an interest in him.

Set would purr happily and become affectionate toward anyone who paid him attention.

...Once that happened, the rest was quick.

More and more women grew fond of Set, and many found their fatigue from the long walk soothed by his adorable nature.

For that reason, Set never stayed in one place, wandering among the group... but when the group stopped moving, he must have been curious. That's why he came up front like this.

That it happened while one of the two leading the bandits was speaking was, for the bandits, the worst possible timing.

"Grrr?"

Glancing sideways at the frightened expressions of the bandits facing the suddenly appeared Set, Set purred, "What's up?"

Rei stroked Set's head, a smile on his face as he spoke.

"These guys are thieves. Looks like they were trying to attack us?"

"Guru?"

Set tilted his head at Rei's words, looking as if he were asking, "Seriously?"

But the thieves, seeing Set, couldn't react to his attitude.

Of course. Who could possibly imagine encountering a high-rank monster like a griffin in a place like this?

The bandits couldn't gauge the difference in strength just by looking at Rei and his group, but the appearance of a Griffin changed everything.

"Uwaaaaaah!"

The first to scream and flee the scene was one of the two leading the bandits.

Chasing after his partner, the other one immediately fled as well.

With their two leaders fleeing, it was only natural the rest of the bandits scattered too.

"What should we do?"

"Ah... Well, let's just leave them for now. We didn't suffer any losses, and we can't exactly let them kill someone in broad daylight like that, can we?"

Rei replied to Elena, who had asked him.

He understood well that, in terms of people dying, he had seen it up close during the battle to escape Mejogo. Still, he knew that killing people was something most people found hard to accept.

Adventurers might be one thing, but most people here were ordinary citizens forcibly brought by Jaya.

Therefore, it was only natural that many would feel repulsed by the act of killing.

...At the same time, given the earlier conversation that those bandits had only recently arrived here, he also considered that even if they captured them and made them reveal their hideout, there would likely be no treasure to be found there.
