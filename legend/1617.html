Many of the women who learned it would be a day off due to the rain showed expressions of relief.

After all, many had been walking all day for several days straight, so they were undoubtedly tired.

Of course, just because today was a day off didn't mean they could rest their bodies all day.

As Rei and the men had predicted the day before, an atmosphere emerged where they would use this opportunity to make as many clothes as possible.

Some of the women felt that since they rode in the carriage during travel and had it easy, sewing clothes should be left entirely to those who did so.

But they were the ones who would wear the finished garments.

Therefore, they needed to finish the clothes as quickly as possible.

Others were thinking about using leftover scraps of fabric from making the clothes to add a little bit of their own personality to their own garments.

In any case, for those skilled in sewing, it served as a welcome change of pace.

And even among those without such skills, many found joy in learning the method and sewing their own clothes.

But... naturally, not everyone was comfortable with such activities.

"Why should we have to help people who ride in carriages all the time?"

"Exactly. We walk and walk and walk every single day... yet those in carriages just sew clothes while moving around. And even that sewing work, they get others to help with... isn't that unfair?"

"It's nice, isn't it, being weak? I wish I could ride in a carriage and have it easy too."

Some gathered to grumble like this.

Naturally, such people were in the minority. Still, even without voicing it, Sura could easily guess that quite a few others felt the same way as she surveyed the scene aboard the Ship of Sand.

(This situation is getting a bit dicey. The discontent is still small now, but if it spreads, the atmosphere will definitely sour. Once that happens, all sorts of troubles are bound to arise... I should probably discuss this with someone.)

At that moment, the person Sura thought of wasn't Rei... but Marina.

While Rei was leading this group, he was still a man.

She couldn't help but feel he probably wasn't very attuned to the subtle dynamics between women.

Then again, he had Elena, Marina, and Vihera – unbelievably beautiful women – at his side. So, maybe... just maybe, he could be quite perceptive about those kinds of nuances.

Rei's judgment was probably influenced by their first meeting at Mejogo.

Even though he was pretending to be a prostitute, his attitude toward women was far from acceptable.

And the reason he chose Marina over Elena or Vihera.

It was because he judged Marina to be the most knowledgeable about that sort of thing.

It hadn't even been a month since they started traveling together.

Even so, it was enough time to grasp what kind of personalities Rei and the others possessed.

Given that, Sura concluded Marina was indeed the best choice to explain these matters.

(As for Byune-chan... well, we couldn't even communicate with her to begin with.)

Within this group, Set and Yellow enjoyed high popularity.

It's as if, when you're exhausted from walking, just seeing Set or Yellow appear makes your energy surge from somewhere deep inside, urging you onward: "Just a little further... just a little more."

Like those two, Byune also enjoys considerable popularity.

If it were just about having an adorable appearance, there are others like that too.

But despite that adorable appearance, she possesses high combat ability—at least compared to the girls—and her expressionless face gives her a doll-like quality.

Following Set and Yellow, she was becoming the third mascot-like presence. ...No, some people probably already saw her that way.

Anyway, until now, even if they felt dissatisfied, they had to keep walking. And at night, they needed to sleep as early as possible to recover their fatigue, so the dissatisfaction within the women never surfaced.

That dissatisfaction surfaced today, likely because they finally had a day off.

(They probably wouldn't go and cause unnecessary trouble here... but that's not entirely certain either.)

Early in this journey, when some women voiced their discontent, Rei had tried to expel them without mercy.

That incident was, of course, known to everyone participating in this journey. While minor quarrels might be tolerated, if anything became an obstacle before reaching Gilm, Rei would cut them off without mercy.

The fact that this didn't literally mean cutting them down... was at least some small comfort to Sura.

(Anyway, I need to handle this before any unnecessary trouble starts. ...The Resistance seems to be a bit off guard today, too.)

Regarding that, while there was a sense of camaraderie, Sura accepted it as unavoidable.

Unlike those who merely walked, they had to remain constantly vigilant against wild beasts, monsters, pursuers from Jaya and Rossi, bandits...

And they did it all with a relatively small number of people, protecting the entire group.

"Sura, you're here. Rei and the others are calling for you. What should we do?"

Just as she was pondering what to do about the women voicing their discontent, a Resistance man called out to her like that.

Surprised by the perfect timing, she nodded immediately, reasoning that he naturally wanted to know the situation inside the sand ship.

"Got it. Should I head over to the Magic Tent?"

"Yeah. ...Nice, that magic tent. I want one of those too, but I wonder how much it costs."

"Well, it's definitely not something we can easily afford, that's for sure."

After exchanging a few brief words and a smile, Sura headed toward Rei and the others.

A magic tent would be incredibly convenient.

It would make camping out almost bearable.

But Sura had once asked Marina how much a Magic Tent cost and understood it was far beyond what they could afford.

It was so expensive that if they had the money to buy one, they could buy many other things instead.

Unlike when they were sleeping, they were now seated on the floor, so they walked through the slightly more spacious area than at night before exiting the sand ship.

Out there, it was raining so hard it could only be described as a downpour.

"Whoa... I didn't realize how bad it was inside..."

The rain was stronger than she'd expected.

Thinking that, Sura dashed outside.

With no umbrella or rain gear to be found, Sura headed toward the location of the magic tent in the downpour.

The only saving grace was that the magic tent was right next to the sand ship.

As soon as she entered the magic tent, she wiped her hair with the cloth given her by Byune.

"Thanks."

"Mm."

In response to Sura's words, Byune murmured softly.

"So, you said you needed me... What's up?"

"I wanted to hear about the Sand Ship. Ideally, I'd go see it myself, but that would probably lead to all sorts of trouble."

That was an undeniable truth.

For the women, Rei—the one who saved them—was more than enough to inspire admiration.

...No, if Rei's features had been more unattractive, or even if they were well-defined but in a masculine way, he probably wouldn't be in this situation now.

But Rei had features that could rightly be called feminine, and his body was small.

Even women who felt repulsed by men didn't react to Rei the way they did to others.

Furthermore, the fact that Rei herself possessed incredible strength, and was also Set's master... that was a major factor too.

Of course, not every woman felt this way about Rei; some resented her for dictating their actions.

Still, as long as Rei leads this group, they cannot openly voice their dissatisfaction.

"Right. Basically, everyone's taking it easy on this holiday. Most are sewing clothes, but compared to walking nonstop, it's much easier work."

"I see. How's the cloth situation? I did have the Resistance folks take some this morning, but—"

"With that many people sewing, it'll probably run out soon."

Hearing this, Rei flashed a look of realization.

They had bought cloth on the way here, but it still wasn't enough to make clothes for everyone.

To be fair, he hadn't planned on resting like this in the first place, so he'd figured having enough cloth for those riding in the carriage would suffice.

"So, we still need to source more cloth somewhere, then?"

"Yes. It'll be cheaper than buying ready-made clothes... and it's important to show that the people traveling by carriage are working too."

Marina nodded at Rei's words.

They were buying clothes too, but cloth was cheaper. If you asked which could buy more for the same price, it was definitely cloth.

And for those walking all day long, the existence of people traveling leisurely by carriage... naturally wasn't to their liking.

But if they were sewing clothes all day inside the carriages, that discontent would at least be somewhat alleviated.

It wouldn't completely eliminate everyone's dissatisfaction, but it would quell the discontent of the majority.

"About that matter... I heard someone saw me sewing clothes during today's break and seems unhappy about it."

"Ah... so that sort of person has appeared. It's about the time they're getting used to walking and have the mental space to notice their surroundings. So, how many are there?"

"From what I've seen, just a few so far. But there might be others I haven't noticed, or people who aren't showing it openly."

At Sura's words, Rei frowned slightly and took a sip of fruit water.

Due to the rain, it was quite muggy inside the magic tent.

If they completely sealed off the outside, the magic tent's abilities would make the humidity unnoticeable, but given the current situation, that wasn't an option.

"What about Set?"

Changing the subject, Rei asked. Finally, Sura showed a pleased expression.

"Set-chan? He's playing with everyone in the captain's quarters. Seems like quite a few people are hanging out over there."

"I see. Well, if he's not bored, that's fine."

Rei felt a little disappointed he couldn't see Set, but given the current situation, bringing Set over from the Sand Ship was definitely risky, and Rei going to the Sand Ship was also fraught with danger.

"Well, once we have enough carriages for everyone, that sense of unfairness should disappear. ...But that probably won't be so easy."

"True. If the deal with the Zolgee Trading Company goes well, maybe something can be worked out?"

Sura's expression stiffened slightly when the name Zolgee Trading Company was mentioned, but she didn't let any further emotion show. That was proof she was keeping herself in check.

Of course, from Sura's position, she couldn't possibly want to borrow the power of the Zolgei Trading Company, which had been cooperating with Jaya.

But still... there was no doubt that their power was necessary to resolve the current situation.

"Yeah. The problem is how much they'll actually agree to our demands. ...For starters, we need as much cloth as possible. We've already used up all the cloth we got from the Giant's Nest."

Even so, it was still nowhere near enough to make clothes for nearly a thousand people.

Beyond that, carriages, horses, fodder, food... and reliable guards who wouldn't cause trouble were also essential for Rei.

"...I see."

The Resistance did have some stockpiled supplies.

But there was no time to go retrieve them, and even if they did, the stockpiled supplies wouldn't be enough for this many people.

"For now, let's rest our bodies today and recover our strength. Then tomorrow, we'll start moving again... if we can, that is."

Rei murmured, gazing outside the magic tent.

The rain falling down was growing heavier and heavier.

It was impossible to believe it would clear up by tomorrow... it was that intense.

The term "guerrilla downpour" crossed Rei's mind, but fundamentally, a guerrilla downpour meant intense rain falling for a short time; it didn't typically continue for hours, or even into the next day. So, strictly speaking, this rain couldn't be called a guerrilla downpour.
