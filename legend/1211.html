Upon hearing Grimm suggest using the Silver Lion's Heart, Rei and the others immediately moved to their next course of action once Grimm vanished from the Paired Orb.

They discussed how to restore Vihera's consciousness as quickly as possible.

"First, my carriage should be fine to get us to that dungeon. It's a magic item carriage, as you know, Rei, so I don't think it'll be a problem even with us and the Elks all together."

"That's true. Considering Elena's carriage, it might be a bit cramped with everyone, but it shouldn't be impossible."

Heading to the dungeon would be five people: Rei, Elena, Marina, Ara, and Vihera; plus two beasts: Set and Yellow. And if possible, three more: Elk, Min, and Rodos. Total: eight people and two beasts.

Set will be moving outside, so he doesn't need to be counted. Ara will be driving from the driver's seat.

That leaves seven people inside the carriage... or seven people and one animal, if Yellow doesn't accompany Set.

"But Vihera and Rodos are unconscious, so we need to transport them lying down, right? Won't that be a bit cramped?"

Marina, who had ridden in Elena's carriage when returning to her hometown, murmured worriedly.

Elena's carriage was somewhat larger than average, but it was still obvious that seven people would make it feel quite cramped.

...That said, it wasn't so cramped you couldn't move at all; the fact there was some leeway demonstrated the high performance of Elena's carriage.

"As for the food..."

"That's not a problem."

Rei answered Marina's words immediately.

Rei, who possessed a Misty Ring, stored a large quantity of food within it.

In fact, while inferior to Elena's carriage, he also possessed portable magic tents. In terms of preparing to delve into the dungeon, one could say he was already well-prepared.

"But the magic tent would be pretty tight if seven people tried to sleep in it, so we'll need a few regular tents too."

"I suppose so. Elena's carriage is available, but it's better to have some leeway in case something happens."

If we're just resting normally, sitting would suffice.

But if we're camping, the magic tent alone won't provide enough space.

Marina nodded at Rei's words, but immediately tilted her head and spoke up.

"But once we enter the dungeon, Lord Grimm will send us straight to the lowest level, right? So we'd only need tents for the trip there and back. Isn't that enough? No need to worry, is it?"

"Hmm, that's true. If it's fine with Rei, I suppose it's acceptable to me?"

"I... well, I think it'll probably be fine. Anyway, the hardest part will be carrying the two who are asleep. How are we going to handle that?"

If Grim sends us to the lowest level once we're inside the dungeon, then the effort of carrying the unconscious Vihera and Rodos is practically negligible.

But "hardly any trouble" still meant some trouble.

An unconscious human body is unexpectedly heavy.

Not having to carry them all the way through the dungeon to the lowest level was a huge relief, but the fact remained that we still needed to move them around quite a bit.

"Ideally, leaving them at the inn and having just us and Elk challenge the Silver Lion would be best..."

Marina's hesitation was likely influenced by Grimm's final words.

In other words, even if they extracted the heart from the Silver Lion, its potency would diminish over time.

If safety is the priority, as Marina suggested, they should leave the two sleeping at the inn, challenge the dungeon themselves, and bring back the Silver Lion's heart.

But if its potency diminishes over time, and given the overriding priority of saving Vihera, the Elks would likely find it unacceptable to leave the two at the inn, even if it slightly increased the chances of restoring Rodos's consciousness.

(Besides, Grimm said he'd be the one to revive him using the Silver Lion's Heart. ...There's no way Grimm could just show up at a regular inn, right?)

Of course, they'd take various precautions to conceal it, but if by some chance... even one in a million chance, someone else saw Grimm, it would undoubtedly cause a huge commotion.

And present there would be Rei, known as Crimson; Elena, known as the Princess General; Marina, Guildmaster of Gilm, the only town in the remote frontier of the Mireana Kingdom; and Elk and Min of the Rank A party, Axe of the Thunder God.

...And while they're likely outside the inn, there's also the presence of Set and Yellow.

The fact that the only one in the group without any distinctive features was the noble, Ara, proved just how conspicuous this gathering of individuals was.

Seeing such a group alongside Grim... or rather, a being who could be called a Lich Lord, was bound to cause ripples in more ways than one.

At worst, it could negatively impact the current relations between the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction, spill over into the power struggles within the Kingdom of Mireana, and ultimately even lead to civil war.

No, considering the Vihera incident, there was even the possibility of the Bestir Empire intervening.

Considering that, and the need to extract the Silver Lion's heart and have it ready for immediate use, taking Vihera and Rhodes into the dungeon was undoubtedly the best course of action.

"In that case, we'll also need to hire someone to guard those two while we're fighting the Silver Lion."

Elena knew there were no monsters on the dungeon's lowest level, but she understood it would be dangerous to simply leave the unconscious pair there unattended.

When they had descended into the dungeon, there were no monsters on the lowest level.

But that didn't mean it was still the same now, and unforeseen circumstances could arise.

"So where do we hire someone to look after them? Considering the size of the carriage and magic tent, it'd be easier to hire someone over there if possible."

"But we can't be sure we'll find someone who meets our requirements over there, right? In that case, wouldn't it be better to hire someone we know well and trust here?"

"It's a trade-off."

In this Gilm, the fame of Rei, Marina, and Elk is extremely high. Precisely because of that, hiring someone trustworthy shouldn't be difficult.

In contrast, in the area around the dungeon, Rei and the others aren't nearly as well-known as they are in the guild, Gilm.

Of course, that's only in comparison to Gilm.

Rei and Elena's fame in the Kingdom of Mireana is immense, and in dungeons near Gilm, the guildmaster's name is naturally well-known too.

(Though it's true that high recognition also attracts many who seek to exploit it.)

In that sense, Marina honestly preferred gathering people in Gilm, her home turf... but the fact remained that traveling by a single carriage was already crowded with so many people.

"Well, I suppose we have no choice. Once we get there, we should stop by the guild and ask them to help us find people... What do you think?"

Near the dungeon, a village-like settlement already existed when Rei and the others had visited before.

And because of the dungeon's presence, there was also something akin to a branch office—though not quite a full guild branch—operating there.

The guild staff working there were dispatched from Gilm.

That's precisely why, if Guildmaster Marina showed up in person, she could push for some special favors.

"...I know it's late to ask, but is it okay for Marina to come with us into the dungeon? I mean, what about her duties as Guild Master?"

"Well, I appreciate your concern, but I've been working as Guildmaster for quite some time now, you know? So, of course I can handle that much... In fact, you could even say it's just right."

"Perfect timing?"

Rei tilted his head, not understanding what Marina meant.

Elena similarly directed a questioning gaze at Marina, urging an answer.

"No, it's nothing. Just something on my end."

Though puzzled by Marina's evasive tone, Ray decided there were more pressing matters to discuss and continued.

"Elk... Do you know what he's up to now? I heard he went somewhere after defeating that orc horde during the Ambris incident."

Elk was busy running around for Rodos' treatment. Normally, he'd be in Gilm most of the time, but lately, he was often absent.

If he wasn't in Gilm, they'd need to contact Elk first, and then head to the dungeon.

If that happened, autumn could easily pass and winter arrive.

Venturing outside the city in winter isn't impossible, but it's extremely dangerous.

Considering all that, Rei hoped they could reach the dungeon before winter set in properly and restore both of their consciousnesses.

(But in Rodos's case, besides not regaining consciousness, he must have sustained quite a few injuries too... Is he really okay?)

Considering how much time had passed since Rhodes lost consciousness, perhaps his injuries had already healed.

Thinking this, he turned his gaze to Marina and asked about Elk's whereabouts. Fortunately... though "fortunately" might not be the right word here... she smiled and nodded.

"It's fine. Elk and Min have both returned to Gilm."

"...That's incredibly convenient."

It was convenient that Elk and Min were in Gilm whenever I needed Elk.

Marina smiled at Rei, who tilted his head slightly at this, and spoke.

"Think about it. This place is the frontier, after all. We have access to many materials you wouldn't find anywhere else. Which means..."

Hearing that, Rei understood why Elk was in Gilm.

"I see. So there's a possibility that some new material could restore Rodos's consciousness?"

That was it.

In truth, it wasn't uncommon for new materials found in the frontier to yield creations previously unimaginable.

Considering Rhodos, it made perfect sense that Elk was in Gilm.

"That makes sense. So, I should be able to contact Elk soon. ...What about you? Can you get ready to leave Gilm immediately?"

Rei nodded at Marina's gaze.

"Yeah. Most things are in the Misty Ring. If anything's missing, I'll just run to the store and get it."

"...Understood. Then I'll contact Elk immediately. Can you be ready to depart tomorrow... or as soon as possible if that's not feasible?"

Rei and Elena nodded at Marina's words, which sounded like a confirmation.

As Rei had mentioned, there wasn't really anything specific to prepare, so if they wanted to, they could leave Gilm right now.

If anything, contacting Elk was the more important task.

They needed to discuss the possibility of restoring Rhodes' consciousness, but they also needed to convince him that depending on the state of the Silver Lion's Heart, Rhodes might not regain consciousness.

Moreover, since they couldn't handle this alone, they'd also need to discuss Grim.

(Whether Elk can accept the existence of Grimm... that's likely to be the problem. Normally it would be difficult, but how about now?)

A considerable amount of time has passed since Rhodes lost consciousness.

Even a renowned adventurer like Elk spent all that time without finding any clues.

Or perhaps they found clues, but they were simply impossible to act upon.

Like what Rei and the others heard from the Grim.

But precisely because of that, Elk is highly likely to agree to this plan.

"Let's hurry, anyway. If possible, I'll contact Elk today too, so Rei and Elena, be prepared for that."

Marina's words met with nods from the two. Seeing this, Marina left the room.

Her pace was slightly faster than usual, a sign of how much of a hurry she was in.

Watching Marina go, Rei and Elena, left alone in the room, fell silent without either initiating it.

A deep, heavy silence filled the room. Eventually, without a word, both of them simultaneously turned their gaze toward Vihera on the bed.

Despite all the commotion, there was no sign of Vihera waking up.

Given the current situation, it might have been to be expected, but it still felt lonely.

"Elena... I'll save Vihera, no matter what."

"Of course."

Elena nodded at Rei's words and left the room to speak with Ara about preparations for tomorrow.
