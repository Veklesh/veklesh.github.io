The music filling the air wasn’t exactly lavish, but it was undoubtedly beyond the level of an amateur.

Amidst the music, Rei continued to dance with Elena... and eventually, the music came to an end.

It had only been a few days since Rei began learning to dance from Elena. Despite such a short time having passed, Rei had already mastered dance skills worthy of being called a proficient dancer, thanks to his natural athletic ability.

...That said, what Rei had learned was strictly limited to dancing; as for party etiquette and the like, he had mastered only the bare minimum.

If Rei were a noble, he would likely have been scolded for such behavior.

But Rei was, after all, merely an adventurer, so no one scrutinized his manners that closely.

That said, if she were too reckless, the nobles would certainly mock her.

“Hmm. As far as dancing goes, I have nothing more to say. It’s wonderful.”

Elena looked at Rei and smiled contentedly.

Rei, too, nodded in agreement at Elena’s words.

All things considered, practicing dancing with Elena had been fun for Rei.

“With the party coming up the day after tomorrow, I barely managed to get approved in the nick of time.”

“Hehe, since you’re dancing with me, you have to make sure everything is perfect.”

Elena smiled happily, but soon opened her mouth with a slightly apologetic look.

“In the end, I’ve dragged Rei into this mess.”

“You mean that guy?”

Elena’s words reminded Rei of the man who had appeared during the commotion at the mock battle.

In the end, that man hadn’t taken any action since that incident.

Did he have no intention of acting yet, or was there some other reason?

Rei didn’t know the details, but the fact that he wasn’t making a move was somewhat unsettling.

“Hmm. We’re working on it using the Kerebel Duke’s intelligence network, but…”

When Elena told him that no information had been gathered yet, Rei shook his head and told her not to worry.

“For those gathering intelligence, the current situation must be quite hectic. So, it’s only natural they can’t handle everything.”

With aristocrats from the Noble Faction gathering one after another in Anesis, it was naturally the perfect place for those looking to stir up trouble.

Given that, it was only natural to prioritize that situation.

If a major incident were to occur under these circumstances, it would be a severe blow to Kerebel Duke’s reputation.

That is precisely why the shadowy figures serving Duke Kerebel—those like the Prairie Wolves in Gilm’s terms—were running around busily.

It was precisely because he understood these circumstances that Rei did not blame Elena.

...Furthermore, considering how eerie the man Rei had seen was, if the spies were to make a reckless move and make contact with him, it could easily cost them their lives.

Given how eerie that man was, Rei believed that the current situation was by no means bad.

Elena’s spirits lifted slightly at Rei’s words.

“It’s a relief to hear you say that, Rei. …That said, even if we try to gather information, there’s absolutely nothing to go on—though there is some ‘probably…’ information.”

“Information that exists precisely because we can’t gather it?”

Not understanding what Elena meant, Rei looked at her questioningly.

But just as Elena was about to say something to Rei... she suddenly stopped herself.

Elena’s gaze fell upon the musicians who, until just a moment ago, had been playing the music for Elena and Rei’s dance.

They were musicians employed by the Duke of Kerebel’s household, but that didn’t mean they could carelessly discuss what they were about to talk about.

“Shall we move somewhere else? It’d be better than talking here.”

“If that’s what you say, Elena, I don’t mind.”

Rei, who had no particular objection to Elena’s suggestion, accepted her invitation and followed her to another room.

The musicians saw Rei and Elena off without saying a word… but once the two were out of sight, they exchanged a few quiet remarks.

“Hey, about those two… do you think they’re really in that kind of relationship? To be honest, from the way they were dancing, it definitely looked that way to me.”

“Yeah, I think so too. But… isn’t Lady Elena the daughter of a duke? Wouldn’t that make it a romance between people of different social classes? Eek!”

“Well, that’s true. But that guy over there is Rei, the one who won every single match in the mock battles at the festival the other day, right? I think they’ll end up breaking up anyway.”

One of the musicians said that.

The reason he harbors even a slight bit of hostility toward Rei is probably because he lost the bet he placed on that mock battle.

He hadn’t bet a huge sum, but it was only natural that he didn’t like losing.

Of course, the others knew this too, so the looks they gave the man were cold.

Especially the woman who had been imagining—or perhaps fantasizing about—a romantic relationship between Rei and Elena was shooting the man a glare colder than the temperature outside.

With everyone’s eyes on him like that, the man had plenty he wanted to say… but in the end, he said nothing more and simply looked away.

While the musicians were discussing Rei and Elena’s relationship, the two of them were in a room a short distance away from the one where they had been practicing their dance earlier.

This was because, under no circumstances—not even in a million-to-one chance—could the musicians… or anyone else… find out about what was to come.

As it was, without either of them saying much, a silence filled the room—a silence so thick it made the atmosphere from their dance just moments ago seem like a lie.

Elena seemed to hesitate for a few seconds, unsure of how to begin, but eventually, as if she had made up her mind, she spoke.

“Actually, an assassin has been active in Anesis for several years now. That man… or perhaps woman—in any case, we have absolutely no idea where that assassin came from. It’s even possible they aren’t from anywhere else at all, but are actually from Anesis.”

“...And that’s the one I saw? If so, it’s definitely not a woman. Well, I guess I can’t say for sure, since they might be altering their physique.”

Even as he said that, Rei was convinced that the person he had seen was definitely a man.

He didn’t have any concrete evidence, but that was his gut feeling.

“Ah. That is, assuming the person Rei saw was that assassin… Black Wolf.”

“Black Wolf. Is that his name?”

It wasn’t certain that the person Rei had seen was Black Wolf, but Rei stated it as fact.

Seeing Rei like that, Elena must have realized it was pointless to say anything more. She said nothing about the person Rei had seen and continued her explanation.

“All we know for sure right now is that he’s strong. At least, so far, there are only a few people who’ve survived a fight with Black Wolf…”

“Wait, if there are even a few people, wouldn’t that tell us what kind of creature the Black Wolf is? Like, for example, its appearance?”

“He’s an assassin, you know? Unfortunately, he doesn’t seem to show his face, and since he hasn’t spoken a word, we don’t even know if he’s a man or a woman.”

“Ah… I see.”

Hearing that, Rei nodded in agreement.

If an assassin were to show their face, that information would spread all the more easily.

Normally, they wouldn’t do something like that.

(Though the one I saw was definitely not what you’d call ordinary.)

That emotionless, observant gaze and that unidentifiable aura.

He was definitely not someone you could call ordinary.

“But even so, I’m convinced that the man I met is that ‘Black Wolf’ guy.”

He doesn’t have any proof, but Rei states it with certainty.

Elena, perhaps understanding that Rei’s instincts are usually spot-on, doesn’t object to his words.

That said, it wasn’t exactly because she fully trusted Rei’s words… but rather because it would make things easier to handle if she simply accepted that the person Rei had met was indeed the Black Wolf.

After all, it’s difficult to proceed with anything when the enemy’s true identity remains unknown.

So, even if he’s undoubtedly a formidable foe, it’s better to assume he is Black Wolf.

Above all, she knows that overestimating the enemy is better than underestimating it, as it prevents her from hesitating when the time comes.

“If Rei says so, then I suppose I should accept that. After all, since Rei—no, since only Rei and Set—actually saw that opponent, Rei’s instincts are the only ones I can rely on. But be careful. If what Rei saw really was the Black Wolf…”

“I know. Still, a Black Wolf is an assassin, right? If we fight head-on, I don’t plan on losing.”

Rei said that, but given that the opponent is an assassin, fighting head-on is basically out of the question.

After all, assassins are the kind of people who strike when their targets are going about their daily lives.

“You’d better abandon the idea of fighting an assassin head-on. Those guys are incredibly troublesome.”

“...You sound like you’ve been through this before.”

“Mm-hmm. Believe it or not, I’ve turned the tables on assassins many times before.”

Elena said this with a slightly confident expression, but normally, being targeted by an assassin is hardly something to boast about.

(Then again, this is Elena, known as the Princess General. To someone, somewhere, she must have been a nuisance.)

As if reading Rei’s thoughts, Elena continued.

“Anyway, I’ve been targeted by several assassins before, but none of them ever attacked me head-on.”

“What methods did they usually use, specifically?”

“Some tried to poison my food, others attacked while I was bathing, and still others struck while I was asleep. On a slightly different note, there was one who joined the war as a soldier and tried to catch me off guard by firing a poisoned dart.”

“……Wow, that’s all I can say. You’ve been attacked by assassins far more often than I ever imagined.”

Even though she’d been told she’d been attacked by assassins, Rei hadn’t realized it was to that extent, and he wore a look of genuine disgust.

However, in stark contrast to Rei’s reaction, Elena nodded back without seeming particularly bothered.

“Given my position, it can’t be helped. Besides, unlike before, I’m not in a position to do anything about some random assassin these days.”

Elena’s words carried a strong sense of conviction, leaving him with no choice but to accept them.

After all, having inherited the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone, Elena is no longer just a human—she has effectively become a being beyond human.

In that sense, even assassins considered top-tier might be said to lack the necessary strength.

Those who transcend the top tier—those who could be called “super-elite”—might be able to go toe-to-toe with Elena, but such assassins are, of course, few and far between.

...Amidst all this, the fact that the assassin known as the Black Wolf possessed the kind of skill that could be called “super-elite” struck Elena as a kind of irony.

“In my case, I’m not really the type to pay attention to those kinds of details… but if Set is with me, I get the feeling we’ll manage just fine in most situations.”

“With Set around, there’s no doubt we wouldn’t have to worry about that.”

Elena, too, had spent a fair amount of time with Set.

Precisely because of that, she understood from the bottom of her heart just how reliable Set was.

“Still, it’s one thing when we’re camping, but we can’t exactly be together in town, can we? After all, at an inn, Set’s in the stables.”

“With Set, though, I bet he’d sense if I was in danger even from the stables.”

It was a casual remark from Rei, but to Elena, who knew the bond between Rei and Set, it didn’t sound like a joke.

In fact, Rei and Set are connected through Beast Magic.

If either of them were in danger, it wouldn’t be strange for the other to sense it.

“Even so. If something happens to Rei, it won’t just be me. Marina and Vihera will be heartbroken too.”

“……I know. I’m not going to get killed that easily, either. Besides, it’s the Black Wolf who’s after me, right? If so, I’d expect him to attack head-on.”

Rei had heard all sorts of things about assassins, but considering the figure he’d encountered at the supply depot—who appeared to be the Black Wolf—he predicted that if it came to a fight, it would likely be a head-on battle.

It was a prediction with no basis in fact, but Rei believed it was likely correct.

And if it did come down to a head-on battle, he was confident he wouldn’t lose.

In fact, Rei had proven himself time and again, so Elena had no choice but to acknowledge that much.

That said, it was impossible for her not to worry about Rei, given the high likelihood that he was being targeted by the Black Wolf.

“Still, if that Black Wolf is targeting me, that means someone must have made contact with him, right? Yet, are the people of the Duke of Kerebel’s household unable to make contact with those kinds of people?”

“Unfortunately, no. Whether it’s the Black Wolf’s power or something else, no one who tried to make contact with the Black Wolf to probe the situation was ultimately able to do so. …The fact that they weren’t killed is perhaps the only consolation.”

Elena’s expression grew complex, torn between relief that those serving the Duke of Kerebel had survived and frustration at being completely ignored by the enemy.
