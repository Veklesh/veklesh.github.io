"Come on, dig in! Today's on me!"

After delivering the supplies, Bistol brought Rei and the others to a shop called Crystal Pavilion.

The shop had likely opened recently due to the increased crowd from the Gilm expansion project.

The establishment itself wasn't particularly elaborate... it was a hybrid of a regular shop and a food stall.

Tables were placed both inside and outside the shop, and if they ran low, they could add more—within the limits of the land the shop rented, of course.

Seeing this, Rei recalled the beer gardens he'd seen on TV back in Japan; it was probably similar.

Of course, beer gardens probably have a proper definition, but that was how Rei understood them.

Since it was a shop that set up tables and chairs outdoors, it meant Set wouldn't have to squeeze into the cramped space inside.

"Well then, I'll take you up on that. ...Though Marina and I aren't all that hungry, to be honest."

Saying this, Vihera, who had joined them before arriving at Suisho-tei, raised her glass of ale to her lips.

Beside Vihera, Byune was intently shoveling sweet-and-salty stewed meat into her mouth.

Having researched Rei... and the Crimson Wings to some extent while working in Gilm, Bistor naturally knew about Vihera and Byune as well.

Given that, he couldn't very well exclude those two from the group he was treating.

The fact that the two newcomers were women was likely why Bistor accepted Vihera and the others so readily.

He figured women wouldn't eat that much.

...But looking at Byune, it was clear that assumption was wrong.

Still, Bistor was a shrewd merchant, more than capable. ...Otherwise, someone with his appearance wouldn't be able to secure such a large supply of materials.

And certainly not earn the respect of those traveling with him, starting with his guards.

"Speaking of which, Bistor is here, but what about the others? I mean, they definitely suffered heavy losses in today's attack, right?"

"Ah, everyone..."

Bistol's expression darkened for just a moment at Rei's question, but he quickly shifted it into a smile and spoke.

"The others are resting up, you know. A lot of them got hurt, so they need treatment. I had stuff to take care of, so I waited at the guild."

"Then shouldn't you be too busy to be having a meal with us like this?"

"I may look like this, but I'm a merchant. Losing comrades is sad, but delivering the requested goods is my top priority. Besides... if those who died knew we were grieving because of them, or that they'd caused a hole in our business, they'd probably be angry with us."

That's why I'm thanking Rei and the others for retrieving the goods, she said.

Rei nodded in understanding at Bystle's concluding words.

Whether that was the common merchant's perspective or just Bistol's... he didn't know.

But still, if that's what satisfies Bistor, Rei decided to fully enjoy the hospitality.

"Hiccup! Hey, there's a monster here, a monster! And there's a beauty too!"

Suddenly, such a voice rang out near Rei and the others' table.

Though Rei was already used to being accosted, that familiarity didn't mean it didn't make him feel sick.

With the expansion, the current Gilm had more people, and naturally, it wasn't strange for some drunkards to pick fights.

...No, if that were all, it wouldn't be so strange.

After all, Gilm was a town where many adventurers gathered.

But... even so, those familiar with Gilm knew that even drunkards rarely bothered Rei.

Not many doesn't mean none, but that's precisely because it's Gilm.

In any case, it was clear that the person currently bothering Rei and the others knew nothing about Gilm's particular circumstances.

From Rei's perspective, the man had muscle, but it wasn't the kind built for fighting. If anything, it was the kind built for doing some kind of labor.

(A carpenter?)

Rei thought that looking at the men, and he wasn't wrong.

"Ahahaha! Look at that! A monster! A monster! There's a monster here! Hey, aren't there any adventurers around? We can make some money!"

He must have been quite drunk. Seeing Bystle, he shouted the question to those around him.

At that moment, if the men hadn't been drunk... no, even if they were drunk, if they'd been only slightly tipsy, they would have noticed the stares directed at them from some of the other patrons.

People who knew Rei, people who knew Vihera or Marina... and people who knew Set.

But the drunken men were oblivious to their surroundings. Worse, they hadn't even noticed Set.

Their work was done, they'd received today's pay, they'd drunk delicious sake and felt good... and their spirits had grown high.

Normally, it would be Rei, with his youthful appearance, who'd get hassled in situations like this. But today, there was Bistor.

Being with Marina and Vihera, Rei naturally stood out.

But Bistor was even more conspicuous than Rei.

That's precisely why the drunks picked on Bistor... but today, they just had bad luck.

"What'd you say?! Who the hell are you calling a rank S monster so creepy it makes your eyes rot just looking at it?!"

Bistol slammed the glass that had held his ale onto the table and shouted.

The men who had been picking on him flinched at the sheer force of his outburst, but fueled by their drunkenness, they quickly fired back.

"Nobody said it was that bad! If you're aware you look creepy, then at least dress properly! What the hell is up with your creepiness?!"

"Well now! How dare you say such a thing!"

Bistol wouldn't normally react this strongly.

But several of his close comrades had died, and the major job they'd taken on to transport materials had nearly failed when the crucial supplies were stolen. Through the guild, the Crimson Wings had cleaned up the mess, not only recovering the materials but also performing the funeral rites for his fallen comrades...

Honestly, today had been packed with all sorts of things.

He tried not to show it outwardly, but most of those events had been piling on intense stress to Bistor.

Then came the drunkenness from drinking ale, compounded by the man who had come to hassle him...

The next instant, Bistor—who should have been seated—instantly stood before the men who had approached him, spread his arms wide, and embraced them with all his might.

"Gyaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaa

Even though Bistol was a muscular man—a maiden, according to himself—the men who had approached him were also carpenters.

They were used to physical labor, so naturally, they were confident in their strength.

Yet, these three men were being hugged by a single man, and they couldn't break free.

The carpenters were muscular themselves, and normally, it should have been impossible for one man to keep hugging all three of them... Yet, for some reason, Bistol had made the impossible possible.

They were fellow carpenters, but being pressed up against him was anything but pleasant.

If it were a woman, they might be pleased... but the fact it was a man made it completely out of the question.

"Oh dear, what's the matter? When you cry out in such delight like that, it makes me feel rather awkward, you know."

Carpenters and merchants. ...Normally, you'd think merchants clearly had the edge in brains, while carpenters would win in brute strength.

But this time, it was Bistor who had the upper hand in brute strength.

"Whoa... I mean, whoa..."

"Pff, poor thing. ...Pff!"

"Hmph, serves them right. They've been noisy since earlier. Serves them right."

Hearing such voices from around him, Rei somehow found himself casting a pitying glance toward the men who had provoked them.

Of course, since they were the ones who had picked a fight with them, if asked if he wasn't annoyed, the answer would be no.

But this time, even though they'd picked on Rei and her group, they hadn't directly targeted Rei herself.

They'd targeted Bistor, who stood out more than Rei.

That's precisely why Rei didn't feel particularly displeased with the troublemakers.

...Beyond discomfort, seeing the men's current state actually made her feel more sympathy than anything else.

"Oh my, this bean dish is delicious. It's just simmered beans, yet it has such a surprisingly deep flavor... Perhaps the simmered broth was the key?"

Marina shifted her gaze away from the exchange and brought the dish before her to her mouth.

It was an extremely simple dish—just beans simmered in soup—yet the rich umami of the beans spread through her mouth along with the flavor of the broth.

It was a dish that, precisely because of its simplicity, possessed profound depth.

Seeing Marina eat, Vihera must have thought it looked delicious too. She also took a bite of the bean dish, looking somewhat intrigued.

"Yes. It's quite delicious. ...Do they make a profit at this price?"

This restaurant had been recommended by Lenora as a place that was both delicious and inexpensive.

That's precisely why it was bustling with so many customers.

Given that, Vihera worried whether they could actually turn a profit serving dishes like this... Yet right next to their table, three men lay unconscious on the floor, knocked out by Bistor.

...Had they lost consciousness from being hugged by Bistor, or had they been drunk and rowdy before passing out...?

He didn't know the reason, nor did he want to know.

In any case, Rei and the others didn't glance at their surroundings, focusing instead on savoring the food before them.

"Goodness gracious, how rude!"

Bistol returned to her seat, looking displeased.

After glancing at Bistol, Rei turned his gaze to the carpenters, who were likely unconscious, and saw three of them being dragged away by what appeared to be the establishment's security guards.

Still, it was already over. Rei turned back to Bistol and spoke.

"So, what are you planning to do starting tomorrow?"

"Hmm. Well, as long as we have opponents like that, we can't just take on jobs like this one so easily. At the very least, we'll need to increase our security detail."

"...I see."

Rei responded to Bistol's words with a hint of apology.

It was clear this attack wasn't the work of ordinary thieves.

But Rei hesitated about whether it was appropriate to say that to the person right in front of him. He figured the details would likely be explained tomorrow by the guards or the guild, if it was something they could share, so he kept quiet.

Seeing Rei's subdued expression, Bistor smiled and spoke up, perhaps sensing he'd dampened the mood.

"Well, anyway, there's no doubt we were saved today thanks to you, Rei-chan. So, please eat your fill. ...Ah, you too, Set-chan."

When she first saw Set, even Bistol froze for a moment.

But since she was gathering information on Rei, she must have known about Set too.

Moreover, seeing Set's friendly demeanor and round, gentle eyes, she fell for him instantly.

"Gurururu!"

"Aww, you're just so cute!"

Set, called to attention, tilted his head slightly at Bistor.

That gesture must have struck a chord with Bistol, because she broke into a broad smile and began stroking Set.

Bistol, now wearing a light layer of makeup, did indeed possess an appearance that, if called a monster, one might find hard to argue with.

But even seeing Bistor like this, Set showed no signs of surprise or intimidation.

That made Bistol very happy.

While petting Set, she placed an additional order with a passing shop attendant.

"Hey, is this okay? I mean, I'm happy you're treating me, but..."

The table was already completely covered with plates.

Despite ordering this much food, Bistor responded to Rei's question about ordering more with a beaming smile and a nod.

"It's fine. Besides, starting tomorrow, we'll need to regain our energy and work hard again. So today, we'll eat and drink to our hearts' content and recharge!"

Saying this, Bistol raised her ale glass and shouted.
