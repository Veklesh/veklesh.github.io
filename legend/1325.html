Several days passed since Rei and the others went to meet Pamidor.

As decided in the blacksmith's workshop, the members of Crimson Wings began their plan to exert psychological pressure on Precious. However, they stumbled right from the very first step.

Because they couldn't locate Precious.

Marina had gone to speak with the guards, who said she hadn't left Gilm yet. So it was certain she was still in the city... but they didn't know where exactly.

Even if they tried to track her using Set's sense of smell, Set had never met Precious and couldn't locate her by scent.

"They must know they're under suspicion, right?"

Vihera murmured while sipping tea in the living room of Marina's mansion in the Noble District.

"Yes. ...I suppose that means they've thought that part through carefully."

"Grrrr..."

Set, who had been lying in the courtyard, growled at Marina's words.

...Yes, while Rei and the others usually held their meetings in the dining hall of the Dusk Wheat inn, the main reason they had gone out of their way to come to Marina's mansion in the Noble District was precisely this.

Set lay sprawled in the courtyard adjoining the living room.

Set was normally required to stay in the stables at the Dusk Wheat inn during dusk, but that was strictly for the dusk hours at the inn... because it was a large establishment used by many guests.

Of course, compared to an ordinary inn, Set could relax considerably more here.

But even so, there were still inherent limitations to being in an inn.

As a business catering to guests, it was impossible to make special accommodations for Rei alone, including the proprietress, Lana.

That was only natural, even for Rei, who had been staying at the inn for years.

If it was just a minor favor, they might make an exception for a valued customer.

But allowing Set to move about freely was, as a guest business, simply impossible.

Incidentally, the Dusk Wheat inn was known as one of the higher-end lodgings in Gilm.

Its main clientele consists of merchants, but such individuals typically only stay during their visits to Gilm.

In contrast, those like Rei who made it their permanent lodging included individuals who kept rooms at the Dusk Wheat inn for years... sometimes over a decade, or even decades.

That's precisely why they were treated more leniently and received special services compared to ordinary guests... but even so, the matter with Set was impossible.

It was Marina who offered Rei a helping hand.

"If it's my house, there's no one else around, so you won't have to worry about that," she said.

And so... as a result, after forming Crimson Wings, whenever Rei and the others needed to meet, they ended up doing so at Marina's mansion.

Even now, as Set lay sprawled in the garden soaking up the spring sunshine, Byune was leaning against him, half-asleep.

Watching this scene of one person and one creature, the frustration over the lack of progress with Precious seemed to ease just a little.

(Well, it's not like anyone would stay irritated after seeing this scene anyway.)

Unless you were a real oddball, you couldn't help but feel a warm, fuzzy feeling looking at the scene before your eyes.

Though Byune was usually extremely unfriendly, seeing her leaning against Set like this made her usual self hard to imagine.

"Guess we'll just have to pin our hopes on the informant, huh?"

Rei murmurs as he reaches for a fresh fruit—about the size of a cherry, though purple with mottled patterns—rather than a dried one.

Popping one into his mouth, a sweetness tinged with refreshing tartness spread throughout his mouth.

Rei liked sweet fruits, but he also liked sour ones.

"Mm, delicious. I bought these at a shop on the way here. What a lucky find."

As he murmured this, Rei seemed to have forgotten the anger he felt over Azimov being injured and the theft of the Sleipnir Boots.

But that was only for now. Marina and Vihera, who knew Rei well, understood perfectly what would happen when he actually confronted the ones who had plotted this.

The flames of rage still swirled within Rei.

He was simply not showing it.

If an enemy stood before him now, that person would learn the hard way exactly what they had gotten themselves into.

Knowing this, Marina and Vihera paid no heed to Rei's state and spoke up.

"Crus fruit, huh. It's a springtime fruit, you know. ...Though sometimes bitter ones get mixed in... Judging by Rei's reaction, it seems fine. Probably because whoever picked it had a good eye."

Rei ate the fruit... Marina reached for the Cruz fruit, popped it into her mouth, and murmured.

A fruit with purple mottled patterns—at first glance, not exactly something you'd want to put in your mouth.

But it was a fruit from the forest near Gilm, a familiar taste to those who lived there.

For Rei, who possessed the Misty Ring, the concept of seasonal foods—those only available at certain times of the year—was less significant than for others.

After all, if you bought or hunted seasonal foods in bulk and stored them in the Misty Ring, you could enjoy that peak flavor anytime.

Gamelion meat was a prime example of this.

For Rei and Set, Gamelion meat held an important place in their meals, and they could eat it practically around the clock.

...Though, of course, the amount of Gamelion meat stored in the Misty Ring is limited, so they couldn't eat it unless it was for something celebratory.

And even rarer than Gamelion meat is silver lion meat.

This is genuinely available in extremely limited quantities, and one would normally have no chance to eat it unless something extraordinary happened.

Its extreme deliciousness comes with the luxurious drawback of numbing your taste buds for several days, which is also a problem.

"Still... I'm bored. It's painful that we can't make a move over here until the informant arrives."

"Yeah. If we take on a job here carelessly, we might miss something important happening in the meantime."

"I asked Elena about it when we spoke via the Paired Orb yesterday, but it seems she's only heard the name of the Spier Trading Company."

"Hmm... Well, the Spier Trading Company isn't exactly a major trading house, so the fact Elena even knew the name might be surprising in itself."

For a merchant house to be remembered by the Duchess of Kerebel's daughter is incredibly fortunate.

...Though, considering the impression Elena formed of the Spier Trading Company after hearing the details from Rei yesterday, that might be a different story.

For a while longer, Rei, Marina, and Vihera exchanged conversation, while Byune leaned against Set and dozed off under the spring sunshine.

Set lay down in the courtyard, careful not to wake Byune.

To an outsider, it was a scene that would make many envy Byune, but she herself didn't seem particularly bothered.

"...Hey, Rei. It's nice to relax like this, but how about we move our bodies a little?"

Just as he finished the last Cruz fruit, Vihera asked, as if she'd been waiting for that moment.

"Huh? Move my body? ...Well, okay. But keep it moderate, you hear?"

At Rei's words, Vihera flashed a slightly disappointed expression.

Still, seemingly unwilling to miss the chance for a mock battle with Rei, she headed toward the courtyard through the window without complaint.

The window connecting the living room to the courtyard was made of quite large glass panes.

It wouldn't be surprising if it were worth several platinum coins.

The fact that such window glass was used so casually suggested that Marina's mansion, though small, was indeed a residence in the noble district.

(I heard it was reinforced with spirit magic or something... Spirit magic is just too convenient, isn't it?)

Thinking such thoughts, Rei followed Vihera out into the courtyard.

He positioned himself far enough away from Set and Byune, who were enjoying the sun, before facing Vhela.

Of course, as Rei had mentioned earlier, neither was going all out. Rei held a training spear with its tip blunted, and Vihera hadn't conjured magical blades on her gauntlets or greaves.

"Don't push yourselves too hard. If I call it off, stop the mock battle immediately. ...Alright, begin!"

From the living room, Marina, holding a cup of tea, gave the signal to begin.

Her voice carried a hint of exasperation, but neither Rei nor Vihera seemed particularly bothered as they began their movements.

Vihera was the first to move.

Compared to Rei wielding a spear, Vihera's hand-to-hand combat inevitably gave her a narrower attack range.

To neutralize the range of Rei's spear, she needed to seize the initiative.

"Haah!"

He kicked off the garden ground, closing the distance to Rei in one swift motion.

As Vihera charged, Rei swung his spear to meet him.

...Yes. A swing.

Not a thrust, but a sweeping strike—a move with a wider attack range.

Vihera must have anticipated such an attack from Rei. He dodged the spear's horizontal swing aimed for his shoulder by slightly lowering his upper body, closing the distance between himself and Rei.

The moment they reached a distance worthy of being called close quarters, Vihera's fist shot forward.

His target: Rei's face, hidden beneath the hood of the Dragon Robe.

The Dragon Robe offered formidable defense, but it couldn't negate the sheer impact of a blow.

That he aimed for the face rather than the larger torso was likely driven by the desire to deliver the most potent blow possible.

Considering Vihera's skill, aiming for the smaller face rather than the torso wasn't particularly problematic.

Though one might call them a woman's slender arms under normal circumstances, Vihera's hands were encased in gauntlets.

Moreover, contrary to appearances, her arm strength was considerable.

And above all, she possessed the Demon-Infused Palm, a technique capable of inflicting massive damage on an opponent upon contact, almost entirely independent of brute strength.

...That said, this mock battle was meant to be nothing more than light physical exercise.

Naturally, Vihera hadn't considered using the Immersion Palm, and her strike aimed at Rei's face was proof of that.

Rei dodged Vihera's fist, which was closing in on his face, by slightly turning his head away and shifting his body back just a little.

Vihera's fist sliced through the space directly beside Rei's face.

Even in a mock battle, that strike possessed enough power to knock an ordinary person unconscious with a single hit.

Yet, while evading such a blow, Rei swept Vihera's feet with his spear.

Most people focus their attention on their attack when striking, neglecting their defense.

Especially since Rei's strike came from outside Vihera's field of vision.

Under normal circumstances, he would have been unable to dodge and would have been knocked to the ground... but Vihera lightly leapt, evading the spear strike.

Partly because he had anticipated Rei's movement, but also partly because he had heard the spear slice through the air and reacted almost instinctively.

"Hmph!"

After his light leap, Vihera immediately launched a kick toward Rei mid-air.

Though it was a light leap, he was undoubtedly airborne.

Meaning, even for just an instant, his body was suspended in the air.

Normally, no one could exploit such a fleeting opening, but Vihera was facing Rei.

In fact, had Vihera delayed her kick even a fraction of a second, Rei's spear would have swung through.

But Vihera's kick came a split second earlier, acting as a feint to halt Rei's movement.

Having kicked and created distance from Rei, Vihera stepped forward again.

Rei, spear in hand, awaited him.

As Vihera charged toward him, Ray unleashed a thrusting strike, utilizing the twist of his body.

A thrust worthy of being called a master's strike.

But Vihera deliberately loosened the power in his stepping right knee, intentionally throwing off his balance to evade Rei's thrust.

Though his posture was now compromised, he pressed forward, confident of victory as he sought to land a blow on Rei.

...But in the next instant, Rei's spear, which had shifted from thrust to sweep, closed in on Vihera...

"Enough!"

As Marina's voice echoed, Rei's spear halted just before striking Vihera's body. Conversely, Vihera's strike failed to reach Rei, and the mock battle ended.
