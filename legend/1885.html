Rei had just received the Fuma Ore, but immediately afterward, an unexpected remark slipped from Liberte’s lips.

“Rei, that fiery tornado—it’s practically your symbol. I’d really like to see it… would you mind showing it to me?”

“……Huh?”

Rei, who never expected such words to come from Liberte’s mouth, let out a voice that could be described as dazed.

In fact, many of the others in the room besides Rei also looked surprised by Liberte’s sudden suggestion, so it was clear this hadn’t been planned from the start.

However, even though the person making the request was Liberte—Elena’s father—Rei couldn’t simply agree to this proposal so easily.

The Fire Whirlwind is one of Rei’s trump cards.

Unlike the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, many people are aware of it, but just because they know about the Fire Whirlwind doesn’t mean they can do anything about it.

Since the fiery tornado moves freely, it’s not impossible for an individual to deal with it… though I wouldn’t go that far, it’s extremely difficult.

Perhaps only extraordinary individuals, such as adventurers with nicknames or Rank S adventurers, might be able to handle it.

That said, there’s no doubt it’s not something that should be casually exposed to public view.

In response to Liberte’s request, Rei opened his mouth to refuse… but before he could speak, Liberte spoke again.

“Of course, I’ll pay you a reward. The wooden barrel filled with Fuma ore that Rei just stowed away. I’ll throw in another one just like it. …What do you say?”

An unexpected... no, one might even call it an extraordinary reward.

Rei almost nodded reflexively at Liberte’s words, but the sheer size of the reward made him pause and think.

“Why are you suddenly offering this? Besides, I think the reward is a bit too high.”

“Not really. I think this is actually a fair reward. …No, perhaps someone like you wouldn’t understand that. If people could just catch a glimpse of the fire tornado Rei uses, there would be plenty willing to pay a far higher price than I could.”

“...Is that so?”

“Mm-hmm. Besides, I’m not the only one who wants to see Rei’s fire tornado. I’d like to show it to the knights in my service and a few nobles as well. Considering that, I think a barrel of Fuma ore is actually a bargain.”

“I see. Is that your plan?”

Normally, having one’s trump card turned into a spectacle wouldn’t be pleasant.

But having grasped the gist of what Liberte was thinking from his words, Rei actually understood and accepted his reasoning to some extent.

(I suppose the Seisor Marquis family is among the nobles as well.)

Because of yesterday’s incident, the Seisor Marquis family has undoubtedly incurred a significant debt to the Kerebel Duke family.

After all, they had deployed the Seisor Marquis family’s troops in a city ruled by the leader of the faction to which he belonged.

Depending on the circumstances, it wouldn’t be strange for people to think the Seisor Marquis family had declared war on the Kerebel Duke family.

On top of that, they went and pulled off such an intimidating stunt as displaying a fire whirlwind. It’s certain that the Seisor Marquis family will be at the mercy of the Kerebel Duke family for a while… perhaps even for decades.

At that moment, Rei’s feelings had shifted from what they had been just moments before, and he was leaning toward thinking it might be acceptable to use the fire whirlwind.

(Besides, if I can get another barrel of Fuma Ore, I’ll be able to see what happens when I use it together with the Fire Whirlwind…)

Even if he wanted to test the Fūma Ore’s effects, there was no need to use a whole barrel.

Since it’s just a test, half a barrel… or even a quarter, depending on the situation, would be fine.

With that in mind... he eventually made his decision.

“Understood. I’ll take that on.”

“Great! That’s a huge help. I’ve always wanted to see the Fire Whirlwind with my own eyes, too.”

“So? When are we doing this? I can’t stay in Anesis forever, you know.”

“Hmm. How about we do it starting today…?”

What came out of Liberte’s mouth was completely unexpected, even to Rei.

Setting aside the fact that he’d asked to see a fire whirlwind, Rei never in a million years expected him to say “right now.”

But if he was willing to do it right now, that was exactly what Rei wanted.

It wasn’t as if he was leaving Anesis tomorrow, but he’d decided it would be best to get the fire whirl business settled as soon as possible.

“Understood. So, let’s do it right now.”

“That would be a big help. …Though, if we’re going to summon it now, it’ll probably have to wait until after dark.”

“That’s fine with me. Actually, since it’s a fire tornado, it’ll probably look more spectacular after dark than if we try to do it in the light.”

It was well past noon when Rei woke up... around 3:00 PM.

And now it was just before 4:00 p.m.

Given the situation, he needed to contact the House of Marquis Seisor and the other nobles still remaining in Anesis, gather the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights, and inform the key figures that a fire tornado was about to appear outside Anesis.

If he called the knights and soldiers to assemble, they would likely gather immediately.

...Though many of them are probably thinking that their training or work will be ending soon, so some might be unhappy about it.

However, unlike those knights and soldiers, the nobles cannot simply gather immediately just because they are told to come.

This is true even if the order comes from Liberte, who leads the Noble Faction.

They’ll likely arrive faster than if summoned under normal circumstances, but the nobles have their own business to attend to.

In particular, many of the nobles still remaining in Anesis have stayed behind to negotiate with other nobles—or perhaps merchants—and some are likely in the middle of such negotiations right now.

You can’t just tell such nobles to come immediately and expect them to simply say, “Yes, sir.”

That said, the House of Seisor—which has incurred a significant debt to the House of Kerebel—is an exception.

“Hmm. Then, let’s get ready right away. …As for the nobles, I suppose it’s fine to say that only those who can come at a set time need to be present.”

“I’m not sure about the details, so I’ll leave that to you, Lady Liberte.”

And so, Rei ended up causing a firestorm in an unexpected place.

It was already past five in the afternoon, but as was natural for this season, the sun had set and the moon and stars were floating in the night sky.

Amidst this scene, Rei stood with Set a short distance away from Anesis.

Naturally, Rei and Set weren’t the only ones outside Anesis; a short distance away were Elena, Ara, Liberte… and even Arcadia.

Even Philma, leading the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights as an escort, was there… and there were other knights and soldiers as well.

Naturally, those gathered were not limited to members of the Duke of Kerebel’s household.

Starting with the Marquis of Seisor, there were also nobles who had managed to make time—or forced themselves to make time—to be there.

Given that they were told they could witness the “Flame Tornado”—known as Rei’s trump card, or even his very symbol—it was only natural that they would try to see it in person if at all possible.

“Well, I never imagined I’d actually get to see the Fire Tornado with my own eyes.”

“Absolutely. Even though I had business to attend to, I was lucky to have stayed in Anesis.”

“Come to think of it, I feel sorry for those who hurried back to their estates.”

“As for me, I had to wrap up negotiations with an acquaintance rather forcefully just to make it here. …Ideally, I would have liked to negotiate a bit more thoroughly.”

“Ho, ho, ho. That’s all part of the experience, isn’t it?”

And so, the nobles who had managed to make it to this place congratulated one another on their good fortune.

Even the noble who had “forced” the negotiations through spoke with a hint of bitterness, yet his expression betrayed an unmistakable curiosity about the fire whirlwind they were about to witness.

Amidst all this, a man stepped forward before everyone.

Thanks to the bonfires surrounding them, it was immediately clear who this person was.

The man who had gathered such a large crowd on this occasion… was Duke Liberte Kerebel.

“Everyone, thank you for gathering so quickly despite the short notice. The reason I asked you all to come today is that Crimson Rei… plans to use the reward I paid him to try out a fire tornado. Since that’s the case, I thought I’d watch it myself, and figured I might as well let others who are interested see it too.”

Though Liberte spoke as if it were obvious, the nobles remaining in Anesis were well aware of the commotion that had occurred the previous night and could easily surmise that the reward in question was likely related to that incident.

...Amidst all this, there was one person who wore a smile on their face while harboring deep bitterness within—and it was obvious who that person was.

The nobles around him also quietly distanced themselves from that person—Zascal Seisor.

Zascal watched the reactions of the nobles around him, but could say nothing.

In fact, there was no doubt that this incident had left him with a huge—too huge—debt to Duke Kerebel.

Given that, if he were to say the wrong thing in this situation, it could make matters even worse.

Liberte glanced at Zascal’s expression and spoke up again.

“Well then. It’s starting to get dark, so it’s the perfect time to see the fire tornado. …Alright, Rei, I’m counting on you!”

Though Liberte said this, Rei was positioned quite a distance away from her for safety’s sake.

Naturally, there was no way he could hear her call, so she waved the torch held by a soldier vigorously to signal to Rei.

A few seconds later... the first to sense it was someone with some combat experience.

“Is this… the wind picking up?”

It was unusual for this time of year; even though it was night, there was no snow falling, no clouds in the night sky, and no wind.

That was the situation... but they gradually realized that a wind had begun to pick up.

The wind grew stronger and stronger, eventually forming a tornado visible to the naked eye.

Even on a winter night with little light, anyone could recognize it as a tornado, so you can imagine just how massive it was.

Of course, there were some who couldn’t see the tornado even under those conditions… but the next moment, when the tornado was enveloped in flames like a suit of armor, even those with poor eyesight could confirm it.

“Ooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooo

Nobles, knights, soldiers.

Without distinction, they all let out cries of astonishment at the sight of the fiery tornado that had appeared.

...Even as they cried out in astonishment, there was no fear in their voices—likely because they understood that Rei would never attack this spot where Liberte was in this situation.

In fact, the fiery tornado did not head toward where Liberte and the others were; it remained stationary where it had appeared.

Those present were captivated by the fiery tornado... but eventually, it began to change shape.

“What is that sound…?”

It’s unclear who was the first to voice that question.

But shortly after that voice spoke, the others gradually began to notice a high-pitched, screeching sound coming from the fire tornado.

A normal fire tornado wouldn’t make a sound like that. It wouldn’t… but unfortunately, almost no one present had ever seen a fire tornado with their own eyes.

Furthermore, even those who had seen one during the war with the Bestir Empire couldn’t recall whether it had made such a sound before—the visual impact had been simply too overwhelming.

However… even those people could state with certainty that the change that occurred in the fire tornado the very next moment was clearly something they had never seen before.

Because, from every part of the fiery tornado, countless wind blades—countless, countless, countless—were sprouting forth.

It was likely only because the whirlwind was shrouded in flames that they were able to see those wind blades directly.

“That is likely the power of the Fuma Ore,”

Philma, who had been looked at by Liberte, replied briefly.

Liberte nodded in response, pleased that her prediction—that this was not an irregular occurrence—had been confirmed.

And so, in full view of everyone, the fiery tornado, sprouting countless wind blades, began to move slowly.

That said, it moved in the opposite direction from where Liberte and the others were standing—and away from the road—so those watching weren’t particularly alarmed.

However, given the considerable size of the fiery tornado, it was visible even from Anesis… and even though Liberte had already informed them, there was no doubt that quite a commotion had broken out.
