"Hmm... It should be around here, right?"

"Grr!"

In response to Rei’s words, Set growls in agreement.

Rei and the others were currently at a spot slightly off the main road, near a fairly large rock.

They had agreed to meet here after wiping out one bandit gang.

Ideally, they would have preferred to operate separately the whole time, but when collecting captured bandits or the treasures their gang had hoarded, there were times when Elena’s magic pouch or Marina’s spirit magic weren’t enough, so they had to store them in Rei’s Misty Ring.

Furthermore, the Magic Pouch—a scaled-down version of an item box—has a fixed capacity, unlike Rei’s Misty Ring.

Given that, entrusting the contents to Rei was the best choice in terms of making effective use of the Magic Pouch.

“Ah, there you are, Rei!”

Turning toward the voice, Vihera emerged from the forest a short distance away.

Then, as if chasing after Vihera, Byune and Marina appeared... and behind Marina, the bandits—their wrists bound with ropes created from water spirit magic—came into view.

“...That’s a lot.”

There were about twenty bandits bound by spirit magic.

Given that number, it’s not hard to guess that they’ve likely captured almost all—or possibly even every single one—of the bandits they defeated.

As for Rei, who muttered that, the number of bandits he had taken prisoner was zero.

All the bandits who had belonged to “Immaculate White” had been killed, and their bodies had been burned to prevent them from becoming undead.

Naturally, they had taken all the treasure found inside the cave, but even so, the anger toward the bandits—whose actions were the exact opposite of the name “Pure White”—would never completely fade.

“Oh? Just Rei and Set? What about the bandits?”

“They were scum not worth keeping alive, so we incinerated them.”

“Oh… I see.”

Looking at Rei’s expression, it’s easy to guess what kind of people those bandits were.

And why Rei was in such a foul mood.

Deciding that continuing the conversation any further wouldn’t be good for either Rei or herself, Vihera changed the subject.

“Come to think of it, Elena and the others are late. I think they should be back by now.”

Although her words expressed concern, there was no trace of worry in Vihera’s tone—likely because she understood that Elena would never lose to mere bandits.

……In fact, if someone were to say that bandits had defeated Elena, who had inherited the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone, it would certainly be a shock as if the heavens and earth had turned upside down.

“You’re right. Maybe they had more treasure stashed away than expected, or something like that? Or perhaps there are more bandits than anticipated, and it’s taking them longer to capture them.”

Setting aside the matter of defeating the bandits, there was a prediction that it would take quite some time to subdue the ones they’d captured.

“So, hasn’t the person we’re supposed to hand these bandits over to arrived yet?”

“……Now that you mention it, they are late. I’d think they’d be here by now.”

The sun was already directly overhead, and when he took out his watch from the Misty Ring to check, it was just past noon.

Since the appointment was set for noon, technically they were late.

That said, in this world, few people own watches, and they generally judge the time by the bells that ring every three hours.

Given that, a slight deviation from the agreed-upon time isn’t all that unusual.

That said, the people taking on the bandits this time were officially commissioned by Gilm, and they knew full well that Rei and the others were among those they were supposed to meet.

Given that, if asked whether they would want to cause Rei and the others any discomfort, the answer is undoubtedly no.

When it comes to working in Gilm, it goes without saying just how terrifying it would be to make an enemy of Rei—or, more precisely, his familiar, Set.

Just as they were talking, Marina, leading the bandits, arrived at Rei and the others’ location.

The bandits, unable to escape due to Marina’s spirit magic, saw Set standing beside Rei and fixed him with looks of shock and terror.

They must have realized, in the most stark way possible, exactly who they had made their enemy.

Fearing that even the slightest sound might get them killed right there on the spot, they remained silent and still.

All to avoid drawing Rei’s attention.

Beside the thieves, Rei tells Marina about the "Immaculate Purity."

The band of thieves captured by Marina understood exactly what the thieves calling themselves “Immaculate White” had been up to.

For these bandits, who had tried their best not to kill people, the honest truth was that they didn’t want to be lumped together with “Immaculate Purity,” even though they were all thieves.

Some of them, even while afraid of Rei and his group, even cast grateful glances their way.

“Hm? That’s… oh, a different group.”

Though puzzled by the bandits’ stares, Rei spotted the adventurers approaching, leading the bound bandits in their wake.

There was no doubt that these adventurers had also come from Gilm in response to a request from Daskar.

...In fact, there were some faces Rei had seen a few times at the guild mixed in with them, and perhaps having spotted Rei and his group as well, they raised their hands briefly in greeting.

“Looks like you had quite a haul.”

At Rei’s words, the male adventurer smiled happily.

It was only natural. The treasure the bandits had hoarded would become his own as a reward, and after the bandits were interrogated, they would be sold to slave traders, bringing in even more money.

It was only natural that hunting bandits would seem appealing, even to someone other than Rei. …Though, of course, only those with a certain level of strength could actually do it.

In particular, among the bandits around Sublusta, it’s not uncommon to find former adventurers.

Since such individuals attack in groups, hunting bandits is only possible if you possess the strength to handle them.

“You’re no exception.”

The male adventurer replied, looking at the bandits bound by Marina’s spirit magic, but Rei shook his head at those words.

“No, that’s not quite right. These are the bandits Marina, Vihera, and Byune captured. I was on a separate mission.”

Hearing this, the male adventurer was momentarily surprised by Rei’s ability to split up the party to deal with the bandits, but he quickly accepted it as only natural for Rei… and then a new question arose.

“Then, who did Rei capture?”

“None.”

“……Huh?”

As the adventurer looked at him with a completely bewildered expression, Rei, recalling the concept of “unblemished purity,” opened his mouth, his mood growing slightly sour.

“They were scum not worth keeping alive. I disposed of them all right there.”

“Ah… I see.”

Just from the word “scum,” he must have grasped what kind of bandit gang they were. The male adventurer vaguely brushed off Rei’s words.

Even if the male adventurer were to encounter such a band of thieves, he would probably kill a few of them, but he wouldn’t go so far as to wipe them all out.

After all, no matter how depraved the enemy might be, there’s no doubt they’d fetch a decent price if sold into slavery.

But that’s strictly speaking, in this adventurer’s case.

It wouldn’t be strange to assume that for Rei, who isn’t struggling financially, something like that wouldn’t be a problem, and the adventurer, who hadn’t suffered any particular disadvantage from it, had no intention of saying anything.

As I was talking with the adventurer like that, the others who had joined this mission began emerging one after another from the forest.

Most of them were holding the captured bandits in bonds, but among them were a few—or perhaps even some who hadn’t taken a single bandit prisoner.

When he considered why that was the case, Rei could only sigh at the frustration building in his chest.

Just as Rei had expected, some were leading not only captured bandits but also merchants and travelers who had been captured by the bandits.

As for why such people were with them, it was likely because they had been captured by the bandits themselves.

The men were there for ransom. And the women… besides ransom, they were there as objects of desire.

In fact, among the women accompanying the adventurers, some had unfocused gazes, while others were shooting murderous glances at the bound bandits.

They must have borrowed them from the adventurers. There were also those wearing clothes that clearly didn’t fit, layered over their torn garments.

I’m concerned about those women, but for now, I’ll prioritize exchanging information and talking with the other adventurers.

(Normally, I’d leave this sort of thing to Marina.)

As I think this, I realize that the reason Rei is talking to the others is likely because Marina was the guild master until just a short while ago.

Since this request was issued by Gilm, it was only natural that the adventurers of Gilm would take it on.

And, naturally, as adventurers from Gilm, they were bound to know about Marina.

Some had come for this expansion project, yet were still participating in this quest because their skills had been recognized for some reason.

Among them were some who still knew almost nothing about Marina, and when she spoke to them, they happily replied.

“Still, I’d heard there were bandits, but I didn’t think there’d be this many.”

“That’s true. And no matter how you look at it, this isn’t even all of them.”

The adventurer talking to Rei muttered this as he looked at the bandits captured by the other adventurers who had gathered around, and Rei agreed.

In fact, the number of bandits already captured by the adventurers had surpassed a hundred.

Considering that more adventurers would continue to arrive, that number could easily reach close to two hundred.

“But with this many bandits gathered here, why do you think Sublusta just left them alone?”

“I don’t know. Honestly, I wouldn’t know if you asked me. Well, I guess Saburusta isn’t exactly a Neutral Faction, so maybe they intended to interfere with the expansion of Gilm.”

“……Ugh, I definitely don’t want to get involved in any factional strife.”

Rei nodded in agreement with the adventurer’s words.

Rei had no idea what the ruler of Sublusta was thinking, but he couldn’t imagine that acting in a way that antagonized Gilm—or Daskar—would go unpunished.

To begin with, leaving bandits in the vicinity of the town one governs without subduing them is tantamount to demonstrating that the noble lacks the competence to rule that town.

Even if the noble isn’t governing the town personally but has sent a magistrate to do so.

No, in that case, it would mean the noble lacks the discernment to judge the magistrate’s competence… in other words, they would be ridiculed as a noble with no eye for talent.

If asked whether they would go to such lengths to take such a risk, at least from Rei’s perspective, it can only be seen as a foolish move.

……That said, the fact that Gilm’s adventurers are currently conducting a large-scale campaign to wipe out the bandits around Sublusta is already a massive blow to the reputation of Sublusta’s magistrate—and the noble who appointed him.

“Well, we shouldn’t get involved in that. We’ll just stick to the job we were hired to do.”

Another adventurer nearby approached, saying that.

“Even if we don’t want to get involved, they’ll come looking for us, won’t they? …Look.”

Another adventurer who had been listening to Rei and the others said this, pointing toward Sublusta.

And there, in the direction he pointed, were nearly thirty soldiers.

That said, while they were soldiers, they weren’t fully armed as if heading into battle; their equipment suggested they were merely guards.

Still, with nearly thirty armed soldiers, there was no doubt they commanded a certain presence.

In fact, the adventurers gathered here were all watching the soldiers as they approached.

And behind the soldiers was a carriage...

“Hey, that guy. Why is he with the soldiers?”

The adventurer who had been talking to Rei just moments ago glanced at the coachman behind the approaching soldiers and muttered, seemingly taken aback.

Hoping to offer the man even the slightest hint, Rei asks,

“Do you know him?”

“Know him? What are you talking about… They’re the adventurers we hired to transport the bandits we captured to Gilm.”

Those words echoed around them, and realizing they were definitely in for trouble, Rei looked up at the summer sky.
