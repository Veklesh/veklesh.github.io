"What the hell... What are you trying to do to me? Like I said before, I'm a researcher dispatched by the government. If you try anything funny with me, you'll be making the government your enemy. Do you understand that?"

Facing the men who stared at him with cold eyes—eyes that could be described as absolute zero—Rezel declared.

The confident expression he'd worn just moments before was gone.

...It was only natural, given that the giant who had been so supremely confident had been so easily defeated by Vihera.

And now, standing before Rezell were that same Vihera... and the Reis, who seemed to possess equal power.

"Turning the nation into an enemy, huh? Well... If it were a country like the Mireana Kingdom or the Bestir Empire, I might have feared making them enemies... But in the end, this Reburina Kingdom is just a small country among small countries."

Hearing Rei's words, Rezell trembled uncontrollably—something utterly unbelievable given his normally intimidating, muscular build that looked nothing like a researcher.

Even when told he was making an enemy of the nation, Rei showed no sign of flinching. To Resel, he looked like a monster beyond anything she knew, even the giants.

In truth, Rei had judged that even if he made the small nation of Reburina his enemy, it would pose no problem for him whatsoever.

In fact, he even believed it wouldn't be a problem to antagonize the Kingdom of Mireana or the Bestir Empire.

After all, with Set capable of flying, even if he antagonized a nation, he wouldn't be attacked so easily.

Even dragon knights couldn't match Set's flying speed.

Until recently, traveling with a party would have caused various mobility issues.

But now that he possesses the magic item known as the Set Cage, things are different.

The Set Cage doesn't just move the party; it also has a special ability to change color to blend into the surrounding scenery.

It takes about a minute to change color, so it's undeniably true that it's difficult to use anywhere but the sky... but conversely, that means it's no problem at all when flying.

And above all, Rei is a mage adept at area-of-effect annihilation, capable of easily slaughtering even an army.

Vihera specializes in individual combat, but her skill is exceptionally high.

Marina excels at versatile spirit magic and bow attacks.

Byune is about on par with a somewhat skilled thief in combat, inevitably falling one or two tiers below the others... Still, she possesses enough skill to easily defeat ordinary soldiers.

In any case, if they were to oppose Rei and his group, their high mobility would make them nearly impossible to locate in the first place. Furthermore, they use magic items that allow them to blend into the surrounding scenery, making them difficult to spot from the ground.

Even if a Dragon Knight somehow manages to spot Rei and her group, a Dragon Knight alone stands virtually no chance against Rei and Set.

In other words, they can move freely through the skies, unleashing Rei's immense offensive power—no, annihilation power—on armies, or at worst, towns, villages, or bases. And because they possess such high individual ability, they also excel greatly as assassins.

Possessing such power, Rei is confident he could handle anything, even if he made an entire nation his enemy.

Of course, becoming a wanted criminal would mean he couldn't shop in major cities... but he could still buy things in slower-paced rural villages. Some merchants would likely see him as just another customer, regardless of the bounty on his head.

After all, clear national borders are often non-existent. There's always the option of using Set's mobility to shop in other countries.

Attacking bandits was another option.

Moreover, nobles harboring grievances against the state might even secretly shelter Rei and his crew.

Thus, for Rei, who could manage even if he made the nation itself his enemy, making a small country like Reburina his enemy wasn't all that frightening.

...That said, Rei and his group had come solely to retaliate against Jaya for meddling with Gilm.

If they went too far, there was a chance their client, Daskar, might reprimand them...

(Besides, he told me to do as I pleased.)

With Daskar's explicit approval, there was no room for hesitation here.

"Well, we've got our own issues to deal with. Now then... right. You seem to know a lot about giants, so let's start by asking you about that. To give birth to a giant, must the prostitute who serves as the host absolutely die?"

Even as they spoke, a giant was being born from the belly of the pregnant woman bound to the bed.

If it were a normal birth, Rei wouldn't have been glaring at Rezell so fiercely.

But in reality, since the giant child tears through the pregnant woman's belly to emerge, the mother inevitably dies.

The only solace is that the pregnant woman, bound to the bed, is unconscious.

She could slip away as if falling asleep, never feeling the agony of her own child tearing through her insides.

That said, if asked whether one could accept that... the answer is no. Yet, Rezell shakes her head at the question forming on Rei's lips.

"Unfortunately, once she conceived a giant's child, there was nothing to be done. Even the most expensive potions couldn't have saved her."

"...Knowing that, you still made her work as a prostitute?"

Marina's sharp, icy gaze fixed on Rezell.

A gaze that seemed to carry a physical weight born of her long life.

Though Resel prided himself on his nerve, he couldn't withstand Marina's gaze and meekly nodded.

Had he not witnessed the giant he relied on being trampled before his eyes, shattering his spirit, he might have endured Marina's gaze.

"That's right. That was the reason Jaya and the kingdom decided to cooperate. With that giant herd, there's a chance we could break free from being a vassal state of the Mireana Kingdom and gain independence."

"...I wonder."

Resel murmured as if clinging to her own words, but from Rei's perspective, if asked whether having that giant meant the Kingdom of Mireana could gain independence from its vassal state status, he couldn't help but tilt his head.

Indeed, that giant possessed considerable strength.

While Vihera seemed to have crushed them one-sidedly, in reality, they appeared to possess strength comparable to the orc-like woman guarding the gate of this underground facility... roughly that of a Rank C adventurer.

Against ordinary soldiers, it wouldn't lose in a one-on-one fight.

There are undoubtedly those with the ability to defeat a giant, but it still comes down to numbers.

But... even so, Rei could easily predict that the Kingdom of Reburina would ultimately lose.

The giants were likely stronger than soldiers. Yet, even so, they were only at the level of Rank C adventurers, lacking the overwhelming power... the kind possessed by the likes of Rei, Elena, Marina, and Vihera.

In this world, quality undoubtedly trumps quantity, but achieving that naturally requires a corresponding level of strength in quality as well.

It simply cannot be done with the strength of a Rank C adventurer.

And considering the military strength the Mireana Kingdom possessed... Rei predicted that ultimately, the giants would be driven out and defeated.

(Besides, giants aren't exactly the brightest.)

Their colossal bodies, incomparable to ordinary humans, unleashed attacks of immense power, harnessing the brute strength derived from their sheer size.

However, from Rei's perspective, their attacks lacked any particular ingenuity and were straightforward.

They lacked even the slightest feint, making them relatively easy to evade for those possessing a certain level of ability.

...Though, the sheer intimidation factor of their massive bodies was considerable, so many would likely be cowed by that alone.

If these giants fought with more intelligence, they might actually stand a chance against higher-ranked adventurers.

(I wish they'd made smarter giants... but then again, maybe that's beyond their control? From what I've heard, they just seem to be using black crystals and such without much thought.)

In that sense, the man before me is likely just another pawn being used, much like the researcher encountered on the first basement floor.

That said, if you ask me whether I can forgive them for creating this kind of scene, the answer is an absolute no.

"Still, to think you'd launch an independence war against the Kingdom of Mireana... I'm surprised,"

Elena stated solemnly.

As the only daughter of Duke Kerebel, who leads one of the three major Noble Factions, she naturally knew the extent of the nobles' military strength.

Moreover, the Noble Faction, larger than the Royal Faction, naturally possessed even greater military strength. The Neutral Faction, though the smallest of the three major factions, still held considerable military power.

Even if a small country like Reburina attempted to gain independence by declaring war, the military strength possessed by such factions alone—no, worse still, the military strength of just a few nobles belonging to one faction—could potentially handle it.

Of course, if the Giants got involved, a few nobles might struggle to handle it.

"So then, setting aside the Chancellor who sent you, is the King also involved in this matter?"

"That's right. To be precise, it seems the king is leading this operation. Since someone in my position can't meet the king, I heard this from the Chancellor."

At Rezell's words, everyone, including Rei, nodded in understanding.

They understood this matter was one of the major events unfolding.

(I suppose I should inform Lord Daskar about this after all. I'm truly glad I entrusted the Paired Orb to Ara.)

If it were just about us, it might be different, but when it involves an entire nation, I can't decide alone.

Then again, today's events had naturally been relayed to Daskar by Ara, and they were scheduled to contact him later, so it worked out perfectly.

"So it's a state-wide affair. ...Jaya is both a criminal organization and a public institution, then."

"That's how it is. Of course, it's not something they advertise openly."

"Is the King of Reburina an idiot? Or does he not even realize how weak his own country is? Honestly, I don't see any chance Reburina could ever become independent. ...What do you think?"

Elena was the first to speak, meeting Rei's gaze.

"Yeah. Even if they tried purely military means, it'd be impossible. At the very least, even if there were a hundred or a thousand of those giants, the Mireana Kingdom would suffer casualties, but they'd be suppressed in the end. And what awaits them is..."

"An even more powerful domination than before, I suppose."

Marina stated, picking up where Elena left off.

Indeed, it was easy for Rei to imagine that if they dared attempt an independence war, the Kingdom would make an example of them with brutal suppression to deter others from thinking the same.

"Even if we did challenge them with an independence war, the enemy is simply too strong. Defeating the Mireana Kingdom with just this country alone is impossible unless something extraordinary happens. ...Well, those giants definitely aren't something ordinary, that's for sure."

Even after Rei said that, Rezell showed no particular reaction.

For Resel, the nation's independence wasn't that important to begin with.

That's precisely why she didn't seem bothered when told independence was impossible.

...Though, to be honest, his spirit was probably broken long before the talk of independence even began.

"Well, we'll inform Lord Daskar about the independence war later. It's too big a matter for us to handle alone."

"Right. It's better to leave troublesome matters like this to Daskar."

Perhaps because she'd known Daskar since childhood, Marina's words held no hesitation.

Smiling slightly at Marina's manner, Rei moved on to his next question.

"There should be other Giants in this underground facility, right? Yet you only summoned three... Where are the others?"

"...Unfortunately, the only giants left here are the ones that woman down there defeated. Yesterday was shipping day, you see."
