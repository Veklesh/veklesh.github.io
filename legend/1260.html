About an hour had passed since the snow barbecue party began.

It had only been about an hour, but the amount of food consumed during that hour was quite substantial.

Naturally, at parties like this, most people focus on eating right from the start.

Especially since what was prepared this time was Gamelion meat; even those who had initially voiced complaints found themselves inevitably drawn to it once they took a bite.

Gamelion meat, vegetables, and seafood were consumed one after another, grilled on skewers, on hot plates, or on grills.

Some were even grilling the fruit Rei had brought on the hot plate. Seeing them smile happily as they ate it, warm fruit probably wasn't so bad after all.

"...We're supposed to finish with Silver Lion meat, but haven't these guys forgotten?"

Watching the party members, Rei murmurs.

Yet, the lack of exasperation in his tone likely stemmed from his understanding that these party members genuinely rejoiced at Vihera's awakening.

Of course, some among them had never met Vihera directly.

Still, they were acquaintances of Rei, and they were openly expressing their joy.

Seeing them like this, Rei couldn't help but feel happy too.

"Heh heh, what's up? For someone complaining, you sure look pretty happy, don't you?"

"Is that so?"

Marina, holding a glass of chilled wine, voiced the very thought Rei had been having, prompting him to respond with that evasive remark.

...Yet, she couldn't hide the faint blush spreading across her cheeks. Probably because she'd taken off the hood of her Dragon Robe.

Seeing Rei like this, Marina smiled.

It wasn't her usual, effortlessly seductive smile, but one filled with the contentment and satisfaction of seeing her beloved man as a woman.

Several men who happened to see it found themselves captivated by Marina's smile.

They were likely completely taken aback by the smile Marina wore, precisely because they had always thought of her as a beauty with an almost excessive feminine allure.

While lightly nibbling on Gamelion skewers and chatting with Marina, Rei suddenly remembered something he'd been meaning to ask her.

"Hey, Marina. Do you know the book 'The Adventures of Replus'?"

"The Adventures of Replus? Oh, I know it. Why? ...But I never expected to hear that name come from Rei."

"I saw it at the library the other day. ...So, do you know who the author of that story is?"

"The author? Hmm... I'm not sure. Why do you ask?"

Marina asked curiously, wondering why Rei was asking about that.

"No, probably... the author seems to be in the same position as me, or something similar."

At those words, Marina seemed to grasp what Rei meant.

She shot Rei a surprised look.

"Are you sure about that?"

"Yeah."

"...What makes you so sure?"

"The developments mentioned in that story are similar to a book sold at the place I used to be."

"Isn't it a bit of a stretch to decide based on that alone? It could just be a coincidence, right?"

"No, it's not."

Rei immediately refuted Marina's words.

Marina gave him a look that said, Why are you so sure? Rei shrugged slightly before speaking.

"If it were just the general flow being similar, you could consider the possibility of coincidence. But it's the same kind of story development, and it packs in plot points from several other novels I know. At this point, it's hard to think of it as a coincidence."

"That's... probably right."

Marina seemed to agree with that opinion, nodding in consent after a slight hesitation.

"I understand Rei's point, but unfortunately, as I said earlier, we don't know who the author is."

"Right... I guess so."

"You read it at the library, right? Then maybe asking the librarian could get us some information?"

Thinking they must have had the same idea, Rei shook his head.

"I did ask, but they didn't know."

"I see. ...Then maybe we should post a request at the guild?"

"...Do you think they'd find it?"

It was winter, so there were quite a few adventurers using the library.

But even so, while they might read books, it seemed unlikely anyone would know who wrote them.

"Well, if you're gathering information, you need to get the word out to as many people as possible, don't you? That way, you might get information from somewhere completely unexpected, right?"

"Well... I suppose so."

"For example, look."

Saying this, Marina waved toward Min, who was tending to Elk at the end of her gaze.

Noticing Marina, Min muttered a word or two to Elk and approached Rei and the others.

"Guildmaster, did you need something from me?"

"Yes. Do you know a book called Replus's Adventures, Min?"

"Hmm... Well, I think I remember reading it before. Why do you ask?"

"Rei is searching for its author. I thought you might have some information... What do you think?"

"Well, even if you ask me..."

At Marina's words, Min shook his head with a troubled expression.

"First off, The Adventures of Replus is a book that existed before I was even born, right? Unless the author was an elf or some other long-lived species, I doubt they're still alive."

"True. But I'd still like to know what kind of person they were. ...Right?"

"Yeah. I'd want any information, no matter how small."

At Rei's words, Min shot her an intrigued look.

The Adventures of Replus was a fairly well-known work.

But why Rei was interested in such a book... or more precisely, in its author... he couldn't understand the reason.

"Why so much?"

"Ah... well. It's true I'm a bit interested in The Adventures of Replus."

"There must be another reason, right?"

"I won't deny that. ...But it's not something I can really tell people. So, sorry, I can't give you the details."

"You won't tell me why you're searching, but you expect me to tell you what I want to know?"

Though Min's words revealed his dissatisfaction, his eyes sparkled with curiosity.

"Well, that's how it is. You probably have your own grievances, but if you hear anything, I'd appreciate you letting me know."

"Yeah. I owe Rei a debt I can't repay with just a little something. If that's all it takes, I'll cooperate."

I'm also curious about Rei's secret.

Rei smiled and bowed his head to Min, who had just said that.

"My apologies."

"It's fine. But... that book is quite old. Finding its author will be incredibly difficult."

"Even so, please do your best."

After Rei asked again, Min eventually nodded in agreement.

Originally, Min owed Rei a huge... an unbelievably huge debt.

If repaying it meant doing whatever was possible, then that was exactly what he intended to do.

In that case, finding information on the book's author was no trouble at all.

"Hmm, well then. Rei, why not ask Elk too?"

The words that came out of Min's mouth were something even Rei found a little hard to believe.

Why bring up Elk's name now?

Of course, Ray knew Elk was a renowned Rank A adventurer. He knew that... but even so, it was clear Elk was completely unsuited for this particular matter.

"Does Elk even read books? He doesn't seem like the type..."

Elk's personality was exactly like a bully who'd grown up into an adult.

He really didn't strike you as the type to read books.

"I suppose so. But despite appearances, Elk is a renowned adventurer. You could even say he's one of the more respectable ones among those with nicknames."

"...You're asking another one of those awkward questions."

Rei gave a wry smile and hedged his words.

Nicknames are given to those who achieve feats beyond belief or perform actions so shocking they leave a mark.

Such individuals tend to be rather eccentric characters, more often than not.

(I suppose I'm a nicknamed adventurer too, though.)

Rei was also an adventurer known by the nickname Crimson.

He doesn't exactly consider himself one of the more reasonable ones among those with nicknames.

He's aware he has a quick temper, and after the incident with Set, he shows no mercy to those who oppose him.

However, she doesn't make unreasonable demands herself, so she's considered somewhat serious... or so she believes.

Marina, who had been listening beside Rei, silently agreed with Min's words, a smile playing on her lips.

Then, as if trying to deflect something from Rei's words, Min opened his mouth.

"What I mean is... not just this Gilm, but Elk is admired by many people. If word got out that Elk was gathering information, it's highly likely intelligence would start coming in from all sorts of places."

"...I see."

As if his thoughts from mere seconds ago had vanished, Rei's expression shifted to one of understanding.

Elk, still possessing that bully-in-chief personality, was incredibly considerate.

Even among adventurers with some renown, there were certainly many who owed Elk a debt of gratitude.

Knowing that Elk was gathering information, it was certain that those who felt indebted to him would lend a hand in various ways.

"What do you think? Elk wouldn't refuse a request from Rei, I'd wager."

"Yeah, I see. Can you ask him?"

"Of course!"

At Rei's words, Min smiled happily and nodded in return.

To be able to repay Rei, to whom he owed an enormous debt... Thinking that, Min couldn't help but feel happy.

It wouldn't cover everything he owed, but it would be something.

"Alright, I'll go tell Elk about it right away."

With that, Min happily headed off to Elk's place.

"Well, getting Elk's cooperation is pretty good, isn't it?"

Being the guildmaster, Marina knew Elk's influence far better than Rei did.

That's precisely why she thought that if Elk was willing to cooperate on this matter, they might be able to get some decent information.

"Ah! The guildmaster's hogging Rei-kun again!"

The one shouting was Kenny.

As always, she was bundled up in layers of clothing to fight the cold, looking appropriately puffed up.

Beside Kenny, Lenora savored a fruit chilled by the snow.

"Hey, Lenora. Don't you have something to say to the Guildmaster!?"

"Huh? Well, yeah. Guildmaster, Kenny's being a bit noisy, so could you maybe let her have Rei for a little while? You'll be able to be with Rei anytime from now on anyway, so please show some mercy to this poor cat."

Perhaps because she was focused on the fruit, the words that came out of Lenora's mouth could only be described as abusive.

Given it was directed at Kenny, whom she was always arguing with, she must have had a lot bottled up.

"Lenora... you, huh. Is that how you really feel about me?"

"...Ah, bliss. It's cold, but delicious. With the bonfire, I don't mind the chill so much."

Ignoring Kenny's words as they went in one ear and out the other, Lenora savored the fruit beside the bonfire.

It was a snow barbecue party, so naturally, some kind of heat source was needed.

Those near the charcoal grill might be warm, but the courtyard was quite spacious.

There was no way the heat from the cooking area alone could keep everyone warm.

Anticipating this, bonfires had been lit in several spots around the courtyard without anyone noticing. The one Lenora and Kenny were by was one of them.

...Even sitting right next to that bonfire, Kenny was still bundled up in layers.

"Hey, Lenora! Listen to me, will you!?"

"Come on, Kenny. Why don't you enjoy the party a little? You get to eat Gamelion meat, after all."

"Why does Lenora get fruit and I get meat? ...Well, it's delicious, so whatever."

Though feeling a bit resentful, Kenny sank his teeth into the meat Lenora handed him.

...Despite all the grumbling, it was undeniably true that everyone was enjoying the barbecue party after all.
