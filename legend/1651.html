“...Huh. Hunting bandits. That sounds kind of interesting. The reason there weren’t any strong opponents among the bandits we fought today is probably because they’d just arrived here, right? If that’s the case, we can’t rule out the possibility that there are stronger bandits out there, can we?”

While eating the pizza Rei had baked, Vihera murmured, her eyes sparkling with anticipation for the fight.

Seeing Vihera like that, Marina, who had just returned from her negotiations with Daskar, smiled as she sipped her cold fruit punch.

“That’s true. The bandits we fought today weren’t even bothering to hide themselves properly. Considering that, there’s a possibility that among the bandits we couldn’t find today, there might be an opponent like the one Vihera is hoping for… Well, I can’t rule it out.”

Marina said this, but if asked whether there were actually any thieves who would satisfy Vihera, she would likely tilt her head in doubt.

Of course, Vihera can enjoy fighting even against opponents weaker than herself.

But to truly enjoy a fight from the bottom of her heart, she needs a worthy opponent.

Someone like Rei or Elena, for instance.

...That said, Marina isn’t included in that list because she is an elemental mage who uses a bow, making her a pure rear-guard presence.

In terms of being a mage, Rei is similar, but in his case, he is essentially a “mage-warrior” who primarily fights with weapons, and in that sense, he is a suitable opponent for Vihera.

Of course, if Marina were to face some random adventurer, she has the skill to handle them with her bare hands.

However… that is not the kind of opponent Vihera is looking for.

“Hmm, even so… Lord Daskar is quite bold, isn’t he? After all, isn’t Saburusta a city ruled by the Neutral Faction? And yet, he’s going to meddle there?”

“Apparently, the damage caused by bandits has gotten a bit out of hand. …Well, come to think of it, it’s only natural. If you go to Gilm now, you’re sure to make a profit. Plus, since so many people are coming, the chances of being attacked by monsters on the road are low.”

“But…” Marina paused for a moment before continuing her explanation.

“For bandits, that makes them prime targets, doesn’t it? Especially since summer is almost over and autumn is approaching. Once that happens, winter will be here before we know it. Before that happens, merchants need to get to Gilm as much as possible, and the bandits want to make as much money as they can while they still can.”

That’s why it seems there’s been an increase in bandit attacks lately.

Marina concluded her explanation.

“I see. If that’s the case, I can’t say I’m entirely convinced, but I’m not opposed to it. I don’t mind hunting bandits anyway.”

As he ate a pizza topped with meat from the Gamelion he’d killed last year, Rei realized that since it was autumn, it must be Gamelion season.

(I wonder how this year’s Gamelion hunt will go. …No, even if a few more people take on the hunt than usual, it probably won’t be that different in the end, will it?)

Gamelions appear in areas not far from Gilm from autumn through winter.

They are believed to be rabbit-based monsters, but with their massive bodies, sharp fangs, blade-like ears, and tails that whip like a lash, they are quite vicious.

Although it is a Rank C monster, its meat is even more delicious than that of an orc, so many people in Gilm devote themselves to hunting Gamelions come autumn.

Its meat fetches a high price on the market and is delicious when eaten fresh, and given its massive size, it is a highly prized catch for adventurers worried about funding their winter survival. …That is, assuming they have the strength to defeat a Gamelion, of course.

At the very least, for the majority of adventurers currently in Gilm seeking work on the ongoing expansion project, it’s likely an opponent they can’t handle.

(If they’re unlucky, they might challenge them knowing full well they’re outmatched, or in some cases, they might even be used as cannon fodder. …Of course, the guild is probably already thinking of countermeasures for that, so I don’t think there’s any need for me to worry about it.)

With those thoughts in mind, I finish eating my Gamelion pizza.

“Anyway, I’ll clear out the backlog of work tomorrow, and the day after tomorrow, we’ll go hunt down those bandits. Is that all right?”

At Marina’s question, which seemed to seek confirmation, everyone present—including Rei—nodded.

“It’s been a while since I’ve been on the battlefield with Lady Elena… I’m looking forward to it.”

Among them, the one looking forward to the bandit hunt the most is Ara.

When Elena was traveling with Rei and the others, they’d talk through the Paired Orbs almost every night, but being in a position where she could actually touch her must feel different.

Given that she gets to act alongside Elena like this, it would be impossible for her not to be happy.

(Well, in Ara’s case, if Elena is nearby, she’ll be able to stop her immediately if she goes out of control, so it shouldn’t be a problem.)

Everyone here knows just how much Ara has a crush on Elena.

……Especially in Rei’s case, when he first met Elena, they locked eyes and froze in place—whether that triggered Ara’s instincts or sparked a sense of danger, he wasn’t sure—but he’d even experienced being attacked out of the blue.

Of course, they’ve already made peace over that incident, and both Ara and Rei consider each other friends.

In any case, glancing at Ara, who is delighted to be fighting alongside Elena, Rei murmurs his concern.

“If that’s the case, did we rush into today’s bandit hunt a bit too soon?”

Yes, the bandit hunt they’d conducted near Sublusta on their way to Gilm today.

As a result, they wiped out three bandit groups—though they left a few alive to gather and spread information.

Rei, known as the “Bandit Eater” and feared by bandits, had come to attack them.

By making them think that, we’d get the bandits to scale back their activities and, if possible, withdraw from the area around Sublusta.

Although the bandit hunt was carried out with that objective in mind, accepting a request from Daskar to hunt bandits meant that such bandits would end up escaping.

As Rei muttered, feeling a little disappointed about that, Vihera replied.

“You don’t need to worry that much, do you? If they have even a little confidence in their skills, I don’t think they’d flee the area around Sublusta—even if they don’t go so far as to actively attack the caravan.”

“In Vihera’s case, aren’t you just happy that there won’t be any weak enemies left?”

“...I won’t deny that.”

Vihera answers Marina’s words while gently averting her gaze.

From Vihera’s perspective, she has no desire to fight bandits she considers mere cannon fodder.

But if they’re right in front of her, she can’t just leave them be; she has to fight.

Thinking about it that way, her ideal scenario would be for the weak bandits to flee the area around Sublusta on their own, leaving only those confident in their own abilities behind.

“A bandit is a bandit. No matter how weak they are, if we let them escape here, there’s a high chance the surrounding area will suffer. To prevent that, we should probably take them all down. …Though, it might be too late to say that now.”

Since we hunted down three groups of thieves during the day today, the survivors from those groups must have already taken some kind of action by now.

And while we don’t know what decisions the surviving bandits will make or how they’ll act… even so, that information would undoubtedly have reached other bandit groups by now.

If Rei and the others were truly lucky, there was a slim chance the surviving bandits might have fled the scene immediately and managed to escape the Saburusta area without being spotted by other bandits... but it would be difficult to expect such a convenient turn of events.

“I think it’ll take the bandits some time to haul out all the treasure they’ve hoarded so far. Plus, bandits moving around with that kind of cargo are bound to stand out. There’s a high chance they’ll be spotted by guards from nearby villages and towns, merchant caravans, or adventurers.”

“Now that you mention it… I suppose so? Though, if that happens, it’ll probably cause quite a commotion.”

As bounty hunters, Rei and his group easily defeat the bandits.

But that’s only because they’re Rei and his group.

Without a certain level of strength, there’s a real possibility they’d be overwhelmed by the sheer number of bandits, or that the bandits might simply escape.

Thinking about it that way, today’s bandit hunt was really unnecessary… It’s hard to argue with that conclusion.

That said, the request to hunt down the bandits only came after they returned to Gilm.

Given that, even if Rei and the others’ actions were unnecessary, there’s no need to worry about being blamed.

After all, for the merchant caravans and adventurers passing through the Sabursta area, having fewer bandits is undoubtedly good news.

“Whatever we do, what’s already happened can’t be undone. Besides, we’re not going to hunt them down tomorrow, but the day after, right? By then, things will have already shifted to some extent anyway.”

“...Then, why not just make it tomorrow? I do have a bit of work piling up, but adding another day or so shouldn’t be a big problem.”

It was Marina who had decided to go on the bandit-hunting expedition the day after tomorrow.

For that very reason, even if they decided to go tomorrow, it should have been possible to adjust the schedule without any problems with just a little coordination with Daskar.

However, Rei shook his head at Marina’s suggestion.

“No, we’ve already decided on this, so let’s not change it just to suit our convenience.”

“Are you sure? I think I can adjust my schedule without any trouble.”

When Marina asked this to confirm, Rei nodded again.

Even though Marina said she was fine with it, forcing a change to a plan that had already been decided could lead to unforeseen complications.

Of course, Daskar wouldn’t do anything that would put Rei and the others at such a disadvantage, but Rei felt bad about making Daskar, who was already busy enough, take on extra work just to make adjustments.

“Considering the damage merchants will suffer just tomorrow, I think the sooner the better.”

It was, naturally, Vihera who said this.

While she was concerned for the merchants, her desire to fight a strong opponent was likely even stronger.

That said, if she truly wanted to fight a strong opponent, she’d likely be fighting Rei and Elena during the combat training that takes place almost every morning.

Her desire to fight others likely stems from the fact that if she keeps fighting people like Rei and Elena all the time, she’ll inevitably start wanting to fight other opponents as well… that’s probably it.

You could say it’s like eating gourmet meals every day and wanting to try other dishes once in a while.

Others might call it a luxury, but for Vihera, it’s a genuine concern.

Of course, she’s not getting tired of fighting Rei and Elena, but if she keeps it up, her body might start to get used to it as the norm.

Needless to say, Rei and Elena undoubtedly possess strength that ranks among the highest in this world.

Of course, it’s not as if they’re the strongest in the world with no one to match them… but even so, there should be overwhelmingly few who are stronger than Rei and Elena.

If she were to come to see fighting such opponents as the norm, then for Vihera, a combat maniac, the future would likely bring more suffering than enjoyment.

In that sense, Vihera needed to fight a variety of opponents to reset her sensory standards.

“Surely, even in Sublusta, adventurers take on requests to hunt down bandits, don’t they? Besides, merchant caravans usually have escorts, right?”

“I see what you’re getting at, Rei… but it’s the magistrate there. Apparently, the higher-ups are taking bribes from the bandits too.”

“In that case, wouldn’t it be better to deal with the higher-ups in Sublusta rather than the bandits themselves?”

“I suppose that would be the best course of action, if we could. But there are all sorts of obstacles in the way, so it’s not that simple.”

In response to Rei’s words, Marina replied with a complicated expression.

The fact that there are so many bandits around Sublusta means, naturally, that it must be a negative factor for Sublusta’s magistrate… and for the nobles who appointed him.

Yet, the fact that Subursta hasn’t taken significant action suggests there’s something at play that outweighs those negative factors.

“Things don’t always go as planned, do they?”

Vihera’s muttered words dissolved into the summer night air and faded away.
