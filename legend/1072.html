The ground is littered with countless monster corpses.

Many of them had limbs or torsos severed, and few corpses remained intact.

This was the result of the flying slash Rei had unleashed using his Death Scythe.

To say they stretched as far as the eye could see... was perhaps a slight exaggeration, but still, numerous corpses lay scattered before Rei.

Ragdo approached Rei, who had planted the stone tip of the Death Scythe into the ground and was surveying the surroundings.

His face bore visible signs of exhaustion.

It wasn't as if Ragged had never fought monsters before.

Still, facing this many monsters at once was a first for him, and naturally, commanding the fight against them was also a first.

"Well done. Thanks to you, Rei, we secured victory in this battle. ...Though, to be clear, victory is only certain here."

"True. Since we were nearly even with Ragdo in command, it might have been quite tough elsewhere."

"Well, I didn't expect you to speak so highly of me."

"Is that so? From what I saw, you fought quite well. Anyway, now that this place is settled, I'd like to head to the other locations soon. Is that alright?"

"...Yes. Please do. Honestly, I'm worried about how the other battlefields are faring."

For Ragdo, protecting the settlement outweighed everything else.

Relying on Rei, an outsider to the settlement, was a bit galling. Still, when weighing his pride against the settlement's safety, the latter clearly carried more weight.

(No, Rei and Marina must have a deep connection. In that case, he can't be considered a complete outsider. ...Though admitting that also stings.)

Considering Marina's... well, more accurately, her grandfather Optis's standing in the settlement, Rei, being closely connected to Marina, naturally became an important figure here too.

It wasn't that I didn't have my own thoughts about it, but securing the settlement's safety came first right now.

"Well, I'm heading out soon... Oh, right. Can I take the corpses of those two green wolf monsters lying over there?"

Rei's gaze fell on the green wolves lying there, each about a meter long. Their bodies had been severed into upper and lower halves by Rei's flying slash, killing them instantly.

Including its tail, it wasn't particularly large, but it was still a monster Rei had never seen before. He definitely wanted it for the magic stones it contained.

"In exchange, I'll leave the goblins, kobolds, and ogres to you. I'd prefer the orcs for myself, but only if possible."

"...Are you sure? That leaves almost nothing for you, Rei?"

Goblin corpses were the most numerous, but even though kobold meat wasn't as tasty, it wasn't as bad as goblin meat, and their fur had uses. As for ogres, their materials had far more uses than kobolds or goblins.

And the orcs that Rei wanted, even if only a certain amount, were top-tier as food.

Since he's saying it's fine to give up most of it, that's only natural.

But for Rei, the magic stones from unknown monsters were more important than the corpses of common monsters.

(Orc meat is a shame, but I'll get some anyway.)

He frowned briefly at the thick, lingering stench of blood in the air.

Killing this many monsters alongside the Dark Elf was one thing, but it wasn't as if Rei particularly enjoyed killing.

Still, he wouldn't hesitate when himself or his companions were in danger.

"Are those terms acceptable? If so, I intend to head elsewhere."

"Huh? Oh, yes. If it's alright with you, Rei, we have no objections whatsoever."

"Alright, then it's settled."

With that, Rei touched the corpse of the wolf with green fur and stored it in the Misty Ring.

Ragdo aside, several other Dark Elves nearby seemed to be seeing Rei use the Misty Ring for the first time. They fell silent, expressions of surprise on their faces.

Glancing at the dark elves, Rei turned his gaze skyward.

There, Set and the giant cicada continued their battle as before.

Flames, water, ice, and wind were unleashed from every possible direction.

They were unleashed upon the giant cicada, whose overwhelming bulk dwarfed even Set's...

"Set the Griffin, is it? I'd heard you were a rare breed, but you truly are something else. Mastering such a wide variety of skills—fire, wind, water, ice, earth..."

"Hm?"

Rei had been watching the battle overhead, but something about Ragdo's words caught his attention. Ragdo, standing beside him, was also looking up. Rei turned his gaze toward Ragdo.

Probably because Rei had directed his gaze at him, Ragdo looked back at Rei with a puzzled expression.

"What is it?"

"Nothing... Did you just say 'earth'?"

Muttering, Rei mentally listed the skills Set knew.

(Earth...? Did he mistake Poison Claw and Power Crush for earth skills? No, that can't be. While you could stretch it to think of either as earth-attribute, you wouldn't think that just from watching the fight without really focusing on it, right?)

As far as Rei knew, Set didn't possess any skills that were clearly earth-attribute just by looking at them, like firing stone spears or chunks of earth.

That's precisely why he felt doubt and spoke up.

"I thought Set didn't have any earth-type skills?"

"Huh? But... ah, look!"

Ragdo's gaze shifted back to the sky, where the unmistakable form of a spear made of earth was visible.

Though slower than the spears Rei threw, it was still a strike with ample lethal potential.

It flew through the air...

"Wait, it's targeting Set!?"

Yes, the target the earth spear was heading straight for wasn't the giant cicada, but Set.

Flap his wings to dodge the earth spear hurtling toward him, then launch a counterattack with a water sphere.

The water sphere flew straight toward the giant cicada, but perhaps because it wasn't a particularly high-level skill, it was easily intercepted by the cicada's ultrasonic attack.

"Why is that giant cicada using earth skills?"

Rei hadn't fought the giant cicada for very long. Even so, he wondered if it could have used an earth-attribute skill back then if it was capable of it.

As the ultrasonic waves indicated, the giant cicada's primary attack method was wind-based.

Why would such an opponent use an earth-attribute attack?

The moment that thought crossed Rei's mind, an image flashed through his mind: the giant cicada pupa clinging to the World Tree... or more accurately, the larva before it became a pupa.

"...I see. Since it lived beneath the roots of the World Tree, underground, it wouldn't be strange if it could still use earth-type skills even after emerging. But Set isn't so weak that he'd be taken down by such a skill."

Rei muttered, though his words contained a fair amount of bravado.

If the giant cicada's attack landed, it could deal significant damage to Set.

And Set's attacks were easily evaded by the giant cicada's ultrasonic waves and high mobility.

(If I'd been here... Nah, it's too late now.)

Had Rei been riding on Set's back when the giant cicada took flight from the World Tree, the situation wouldn't have reached such a stalemate.

But had he attempted such a stunt, the World Tree would have been utterly destroyed by the giant cicada.

And it would have been impossible to re-establish the barrier protecting the settlement now.

Thinking about it that way, the current situation wasn't the worst possible outcome.

BOOM! A massive flame erupted into the sky.

It was an attack from Set's fire breath, but a straight-line fire breath was by no means a difficult attack for the giant cicada to evade.

Watching the flames, Rei shook her head slightly.

(What I must do remains unchanged.)

Muttering to himself, he clarified his purpose. Then, his gaze shifted to Ragdo, whose attention was consumed by the fierce battle raging overhead.

"Ragdo, do you know what's happening on the other battlefields? If there are any dangerous areas, let me know."

"...No, unfortunately. I haven't received any information about the other battlefields. But if the situation is similar to here, I don't think they're being overwhelmed one-sidedly."

Ragdo snapped back to reality at Rei's words and replied. Rei thought for a moment and nodded.

"Understood. Then, as planned, I'll circle the settlement, tracing the barrier's perimeter. Set is holding that giant cicada down now, but who knows how long that will last. We need to quickly eliminate the monsters around the settlement and head to Set's aid."

"Yes, please do. This settlement is important to us... extremely important. Given that, we absolutely must defend it at all costs. So, if we can borrow your strength, Rei..."

Ragdo bowed deeply.

He had mixed feelings about Rei, but to protect his hometown settlement, he was willing to do anything... even bowing his head to Rei if it meant saving it.

Watching Ragdo, Rei nodded.

"Leave it to me. I won't hold back against monsters threatening Marina's homeland either. Besides, defeating monsters benefits me too."

"Benefit, you say?"

"Yeah. ...Well, if you're curious, I'll tell you later."

With that, he waved the hand not holding the Death Scythe lightly and ran off.

I would have liked to gather a bit more information about the other Dark Elves, but since he'd been fighting here the whole time, he couldn't possibly have intelligence from other battlefields.

"Be careful!"

Ragdo called out to Rei's rapidly receding back, unsure if he could hear it. The surrounding Dark Elves bowed their heads or shouted words of encouragement after Rei's departing figure.

"Alright, first, let's deal with the bodies. At this time of year, leaving them like this will cause a terrible stench, and that smell might attract beasts or other monsters. We can handle the detailed dismemberment later, but for now, let's roughly bundle them up. Once that's done, we'll leave enough forces to guard this place and head to reinforce the other battlefields."

Following Ragdo's orders, the dark elves began to move.

They felt compelled to push themselves here, knowing how hard Rei had fought to save them.

There was no doubt that Rei's overwhelming strength influenced their positive resolve.

They had witnessed and heard about that overwhelming power in the forest last night.

But seeing it here in the open light was clearly different.

With this, they could manage. They believed it from the bottom of their hearts, resisting the urge to rush to their comrades immediately as they gathered the monster corpses into one place.

A few monsters appeared along the way, but Dark Elves possessed high combat prowess to begin with.

A group attack would be another matter, but a handful of monsters were swiftly killed and added to the pile awaiting stripping.

(Rei... I'm counting on you...)

Ragdo cast one last glance in the direction Rei had departed, then began issuing instructions to the others once more.

Away from the battlefield where Ragdo and the others fought, Rei ran alone along the barrier's perimeter.

Well, technically Rei wasn't alone since the light sphere was by her side.

"Having this barrier means I won't get lost, which is nice."

For Rei, who was somewhat aware of his tendency to get lost, the fact that he only needed to follow the barrier's perimeter was a huge relief.

As if cheering Rei on, the orb flashed intensely.

After running like this for about five minutes...

While catching glimpses of the occasional firework-like exchanges of flames, ice, and wind in the sky out of the corner of his eye, he felt uneasy as a new earth attack joined the fray.

If the earth attacks were coming from Set, it might be manageable, but they were coming from the giant cicada.

"Set, don't get hit,"

As he murmured this, the orb flickered as if to say Set would be fine.

Seeing the light sphere keeping pace with his speed, Rei let a small smile touch his lips... then suddenly shot a sharp glance ahead.

A strong smell of rusty iron drifted from that direction... a smell he had already grown accustomed to.

While not as sharp as Set's sense of smell, Rei's senses, including his olfactory, were still far keener than those of the average human.

In other words, a bloody battle was unfolding where he was headed...

Rei's feet kicked the ground with even greater speed.

What came into view was the sight of Dark Elves forced into close combat with numerous monsters.

Perhaps lacking an excellent commander like Ragdo, they were fighting the monsters without effective coordination.

"Then I'll strike that monster horde's flank...!"

Tear through it. That's what Rei was about to say, but almost reflexively, he leapt backward.

Almost simultaneously, an ultrasonic wave slammed down from above... and most of the monsters Rei had been about to charge into were sliced apart by the magic-infused ultrasonic blade.
