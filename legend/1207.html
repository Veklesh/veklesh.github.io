Vihera lies in bed with her eyes closed.

Though Rei has seen this scene almost every day lately, she doesn't look like she's unconscious at all.

From this perspective, she just looks like she's sleeping... that's the only way it appears.

But Vihera never wakes up.

No matter how many potions Rei tried, or how hard those skilled in healing magic worked, she showed no sign of waking.

「Vihera……」

Elena, who was beside Rei, called out Vihera's name.

Still, there was no sign of her waking up.

"He just looks like he's sleeping, right? ...But Vihera won't wake up."

"...I see."

Elena also knew that Vihera would never regain consciousness.

But... seeing it with her own eyes like this, she couldn't help but accept Vihera's condition once more.

"Honestly. Sleeping all the time at a time like this? I have to say, it's a bit much, don't you think?"

Elena said this with a hint of sadness... yet also a touch of provocation. But whether the unconscious Vihera could hear her was unknown.

If she were merely asleep, it wouldn't be unusual for her eyelids to flutter occasionally, for her to speak in her sleep, or for her to shift position and move her body.

But right now, Vihera showed no such signs of movement. Not even the slightest twitch.

"You really won't wake up, will you?"

Elena murmured softly, gently stroking Vihera's face.

"Yeah. Right now, we're trying to find a way to bring Vihera back to consciousness. Not just me, Marina too."

Guildmaster Marina, using her position, the deep wisdom gained from her long life as a Dark Elf, and the connections she'd cultivated over the years, was searching for a way to bring Vihera's consciousness back.

It wasn't just Elena who worried about Vihera; she was also important to Marina.

As women who both loved Rei, and as those destined to be united with him.

"I've tried using various family connections to investigate..."

Elena murmured, gently withdrawing the hand that had been stroking Vihera's cheek. Her demeanor made it clear that the outcome was far from promising.

"I'm sorry."

"There's no need for you to apologize, Elena. I've been searching everywhere too, and I still can't find a way to bring Vihera back to consciousness."

Responding to Elena's apologetic expression, Rei turned his gaze out the window.

Outside, the scenery was still that of early afternoon.

Compared to when they defeated Ambris—or rather, when they failed to finish him off and he fled into Vihera's body—it was now autumn, and the temperature had dropped considerably.

Still, being inside the Dusk Wheat inn now, he felt no chill.

That very fact made the outside and inside of the inn feel like completely different worlds.

Still... seeing the clear blue autumn sky, he couldn't help but think that perhaps it was still summer, that perhaps not much time had passed since Vihera lost consciousness.

(No, is that just my weakness? ...Maybe my mind is really starting to break down.)

If I were being attacked by someone, I could fight back somehow.

But since it wasn't him being attacked, there was almost nothing Rei could do for Vihera right now.

"If this is just sleep, I'd like to think she's enjoying a deep, peaceful slumber."

"Heh heh, that's true. But Vihera is a woman too. I don't think she'd be too fond of having her sleeping face seen like this... by Rei."

Elena replied in a teasing tone to Rei's words.

Rei knew women didn't like showing their sleeping faces.

But that was merely knowledge; he didn't truly understand why she felt that way.

That's why, even hearing Elena say it, he could only think, "Is that so..."

And so, time passed with just Rei, Elena, and Vihera.

A silent time, without much conversation.

The sound of the autumn wind occasionally rattling the window faintly echoed in the room, a slow passage of time.

The three spent this quiet time together... until suddenly, Rei turned his gaze toward the door.

He sensed someone approaching this side of the room.

Of course, there were other rooms near this one.

Perhaps someone they needed to see was approaching.

That was his initial thought, but when he realized the approaching presence was someone he knew well, a slight question arose.

It wasn't unusual for this person to come to this room, but it was rare for them to come at this time of day.

Beside Rei, Elena also turned her gaze toward the door.

Rei must have sensed the presence a few seconds after Rei did.

As both pairs of eyes remained fixed on the doorway, the source of the presence finally stopped in front of the room and knocked.

A light sound echoed inside the room.

"Come in, Marina."

Rei spoke the name of the person whose presence they sensed.

Elena, too, must have understood it was Marina outside the room. She turned her gaze toward the door without any particular look of surprise.

And when the door opened, there stood Marina, just as Rei had said.

She was wearing her usual blue party dress, but her face showed a hint of fatigue.

But that was only natural.

She'd been gathering information relentlessly about the Ambris incident, and once that was done, she'd been scrambling around trying to find a way to restore Vihera's consciousness.

Even for Marina, who had been active as an adventurer, physical stamina had its limits.

The reason she didn't rest was likely because she desired Vihera's recovery so intensely.

Whenever she found a moment, she would come to the Dusk Wheat inn to check on Vihera like this.

Rest, Rei had warned her many times, but she insisted she was fine and refused to take a break.

Marina smiled at that tired face and spoke.

"I heard Elena was here, but I see she really is. ...It's been a while."

"Hmm, it has been a while. ...I'd like to say I'm glad to see you looking well, Marina, but you seem a bit tired yourself."

"Hehe, I'm fine. Seeing Vihera's face like this, and being with Rei, makes any tiredness disappear. ...Besides, Elena's here today too."

He could tell she meant it sincerely, but even to Rei's eyes, Marina still looked tired.

But he knew it was pointless to say anything. Hoping to at least ease her fatigue a little, he took several fruits from the Misty Ring.

Spring, summer, autumn... Normally, it would be impossible to taste fruits from different seasons side by side like this.

Dried fruit was a different story, but dried fruit inevitably tasted different from fresh fruit... especially in texture.

It was a luxury only possible by using the item box.

"Delicious. ...Especially this fruit is incredibly juicy. And yet, the sweetness isn't overly excessive either."

"This fruit is delicious too. It has a smooth texture and lets you savor a rich sweetness in your mouth."

Seeing Marina and Elena happily eating the fruits she'd offered, Rei felt a slight sense of relief.

If this could ease Marina's fatigue even a little...

As they ate the fruit, Marina, looking a little more at ease, turned her gaze toward Vihera on the bed with a soft expression.

"Honestly, I think it's about time Vihera woke up too."

"Yeah... Is there any clue or something?"

Marina shook her head at Elena's question as she brought another piece of fruit to her mouth.

"No luck. I looked into it all... Found a few ways to deal with someone who's unconscious, but whether any of them apply to this situation... I don't know."

"Yeah."

Rei, who was eating skewered meat instead of fruit a short distance away, nodded in agreement.

Over the past two months, they had tried several methods Marina had discovered.

But none of them had worked.

"If only we could ask someone with more detailed knowledge..."

"...What about the Dark Elf settlement?"

"I sent a letter there. It'll take a little longer for it to come back, though."

I sent a letter to the dark elf settlement first thing.

But since the Dark Elf settlement wasn't exactly nearby, receiving a reply immediately would be difficult.

Rei asked Marina if she could go to Set, but she declined, citing various complicated reasons.

He didn't know what those difficult reasons were, but since Rei also preferred to avoid leaving Vihera's side if possible, it was a relief, truth be told.

"If only there was something I could do to help..."

Elena murmured regretfully.

She was trying everything she could in her current position, but it was mostly useless.

"It would be nice if we could ask someone who's lived longer, wouldn't it?"

"Like a dark elf, perhaps?"

Marina nodded at Rei's question as he stored the finished skewers into the Misty Ring.

"Yes. But whether any of the elves or dark elves I know actually know about Ambris... Just being old doesn't mean anything. Unless they've actually had contact with Ambris..."

Marina sighed gloomily.

Someone who is both long-lived and knows of the existence of Ambris.

The former shouldn't be too difficult, given they're dark elves, but the latter would be extremely difficult, considering this is only the second confirmed sighting of Ambris in three hundred years.

"Then we need someone who's lived even longer than a dark elf..."

"Wait."

A thought suddenly occurred to Rei, and he opened his mouth.

The voice that emerged was not one suited to this moment... not one that belonged in a situation that felt more like a mutual venting session.

It was serious... yes, the voice of someone thinking deeply, from the bottom of their heart.

Seeing Rei deep in thought, neither Elena nor Marina could bring themselves to speak.

That's how serious Rei looked right now.

Some time passed like that.

Because they remained silent, it was impossible to tell exactly how much time had passed.

Was it a dozen seconds? Several dozen seconds? A few minutes... or perhaps even ten minutes?

That seemingly impossible state was likely due to just how unusual Rei's demeanor was.

Seeing Rei occasionally mutter to himself while deep in thought right in front of them felt slightly off, precisely because they knew the usual Rei.

But if this was an act to save Vihera, neither Elena nor Marina could bring themselves to say anything to Rei.

And then... eventually, Rei let out a small sigh.

Seeing that gesture, they must have decided it was finally okay to speak to Rei.

Marina turned to Rei and spoke.

「Rei？」

"Yeah, sorry. Maybe... just maybe, I might be able to find a way to help Vihera regain consciousness."

"Really!?"

Elena gasped in surprise at Rei's sudden words.

A clue to restore Vihera's consciousness—something even Guildmaster Marina and Elena, nicknamed the Princess General, had been unable to find.

The fact that Rei possessed it wasn't all that strange.

After all, he was Rei, a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname. It wouldn't be strange if he knew something they didn't.

But questions remained.

Nearly two months had passed since Vihera collapsed... During that time, Rei had desperately searched for a way to restore her consciousness.

If there was any kind of clue that might help restore her consciousness, it wouldn't have been strange for him to mention it much earlier.

"Ah. But like I said, I can't say for certain. I probably know something..."

Rei's hesitation only deepened Elena and Marina's doubts.

"Anyway, we have no leads right now. If there's even the slightest possibility, please try it."

At Marina's words, Rei nodded.

Taking a small, deep breath, he opened his mouth as if to confirm with the two before him.

"What we're about to do might be a bit problematic, to be honest. But at least for me, this is the only lead I have right now to bring Vihera back to consciousness."

Is that okay? Rei looked at them, and Elena and Marina both nodded.

"I intend to ask for help from... a Grimm."

With a serious expression, Rei announced this.
