This mansion where Galahad, head of Azoth Trading Company, resides has rooms available for the company's merchants to use freely.

Of course, paperwork and business transactions are handled at Azoth Trading Company's main office, or at their own shops and homes.

Nevertheless, it is true that having individual rooms within this mansion where Galahad resides is convenient in many ways.

When Bornter lived here, he never allowed such arrangements, considering the mansion solely his own.

After Galahad became chairman, they tried it out, thinking it might be a bit much to have unused rooms... but the feedback was overwhelmingly positive.

Now, being granted a room in this mansion is considered a great honor within Azoth Trading Company.

Amidst this, Rei and the others arrived at Coris's room and looked around inside.

"Well, there are some important documents here, so I'd appreciate it if you didn't look around too much over there."

"Even so, those documents might contain orders regarding this matter, right? We can't afford to overlook them."

When Vihera retorted to Coris's words, Coris, aware of his weak position, couldn't say anything more.

"Don't worry. Just because we see something here doesn't mean we'll go telling others about it."

"...Thank you."

All Coris could do was bow her head to Vihera, who offered this as a token of mercy.

While searching the room, Rei opened the window and called out.

"Set! Come here!"

The sudden shout from Rei drew surprised glances from everyone in the room.

But Vihera and Byune immediately understood it was just Rei and resumed scanning the room.

"Gurururuu"

Less than a minute after Rei called out from the window, that growl echoed, and Set's figure appeared outside the window.

Coris, who had begun to suspect he might be an enemy, grimaced at the sight of Set.

Ignoring Coris's reaction, Rei stroked Set's head and spoke.

"Hey, Set. Can you tell if there are any Sleipnir Boots in this room? Or maybe the scent of whoever attacked Azimov?"

"Grr... Grrrr..."

Sticking only his head through the window into the room, Set used his Enhanced Sense of Smell skill to sniff around. After a few seconds, he shook his head.

A faint scent did emanate from Coris's body, but it wasn't the direct scent of someone who had touched the culprit. It was merely an indirect trace.

Though he didn't fully grasp what Set meant, Rei clearly understood that there were no Sleipnir Boots in this room.

"I see. So there are no Sleipnir Boots in this room?"

"Grrr."

At Rei's words, Set growled softly, sounding slightly apologetic.

"No need for you to apologize, Set. Just knowing for sure they're not in this room is a big enough breakthrough."

The words spoken while stroking Set's head pleased Coris more than Set himself.

The absence of Sleipnir Boots in his own room meant the suspicion that he had attacked an alchemist close to Rei had diminished, even if only slightly.

"...I see. Well then, how about this? The scent definitely led all the way to the grounds of this mansion, right? So, what about checking the carriage?"

Rei nodded at Galahad's words.

Naturally, Set had no objection to Galahad's suggestion either, and he growled impatiently, urging them to hurry.

The others also had no objections to the idea. Coris, eager to quickly establish his own innocence, agreed.

And so, Galahad, Coris, Murt, Rei, Vihera, and Byune left Coris's room.

Naturally, Set, with his keen sense of smell, followed them back toward the main gate from outside Coris's room to keep pace with Rei and the others.

Rei and the others reunited near the main gate and were then guided by Galahad, Murt, and Coris toward the back of the garden... toward the warehouse where the carriage was stored.

As the warehouse came into view, Set, who had been advancing alongside Rei while surveying the surroundings, suddenly fixed his gaze on the warehouse as if noticing something and growled.

"Guruu!"

It wasn't a warning growl; the sound carried more joy than caution.

Rei wasn't the only one who sensed something in that sound.

"...Phew."

Looking at the warehouse, a sigh of relief escaped Rei's lips.

Considering Set's cry, it was certain that the thing they were searching for—Sleipnir Boots—was inside the warehouse.

Galahad and Murt must have noticed the softening in Rei's demeanor. They felt the tension drain from their bodies, more than they could consciously understand.

But... unlike the others, Coris grew tense upon seeing Rei's state.

If the stolen magic item was at their destination... that meant suspicion toward Rei had deepened.

Even as these thoughts raced through his mind, the group pressed onward, eventually arriving at the warehouse.

Inside the warehouse stood Coris's carriage.

There were no horses in the stables, presumably resting, but that wasn't the issue right now.

「Set」

"Grrrr"

At Rei's words, Set seemed to immediately understand what he wanted. He approached the carriage, his throat making a low rumbling sound.

Inside the empty warehouse... Outside, the spring sunlight poured down warmly, but inside the warehouse, it was slightly chilly.

Set approached the carriage in this environment... walking around it, using his heightened sense of smell skill to sharpen his already keen nose even further as he sniffed the carriage's scent.

Inside the warehouse, Rei and the others simply watched Set's actions in silence.

After several minutes of pacing around the carriage... Set finally stopped on its left side.

Is this it? With that thought, everyone, including Rei, watched. Yet, the action Set took next surprised even Rei and the others who were watching.

Because Set had expected him to bury his face in the cargo bed or somewhere similar, but instead, he thrust his face under the carriage... right around where the axle was.

Naturally, there wasn't much space under the carriage... and when Set stuck his head in, one side lifted up.

Just seconds ago, Coris had been worried about his own life, but seeing the carriage potentially tip over made him panic—perhaps because he was a merchant.

Everyone, including Coris, watched the scene with bated breath, wondering when the carriage would tip over.

But... fortunately, even after Set emerged from under the carriage, it didn't overturn.

It was shaking violently, but seeing the carriage like that, Coris breathed a sigh of relief. And then...

"Rei, look at that!"

"...Ah."

Seeing the direction Vihera pointed... that is, seeing what Set's beak was holding, Rei felt profound relief.

Because what Set was holding in his beak was something Rei recognized too—Sleipnir Boots.

Despite supposedly being reinforced with silver lion material, from a distance it didn't seem particularly different... Still, it was an undeniable fact that what Set was holding was Sleipnir Boots.

"Hmm, judging by the look on his face, it seems to be exactly what Set is holding."

"That's correct."

"But... hiding it in the carriage is one thing, but hiding it under the carriage? Isn't that a bit strange? It's fine now, but if the carriage had been moving, it wouldn't have been surprising if those shoes had fallen off."

Galahad's words were undeniably true.

Indeed, while Set had retrieved them easily enough this time—though the carriage nearly overturned due to his size—if they'd been moving normally through town and beyond, the Sleipnir Boots hidden beneath the carriage could have fallen to the ground at any moment.

Would you risk antagonizing Rei and then hide a magic item obtained that way in such a place?

Asked that question, most would answer no.

Of course, it's possible he didn't know the magic item stolen from Azimov belonged to Rei.

(Nah, probably not.)

Rei immediately dismissed the thought that had flashed through his mind.

Azimov's lab contained numerous magic items besides the Sleipnir Boots that Rei had left in storage.

Magic items that could easily fund years of leisurely living if sold were just casually lying around.

Given that, the fact that someone specifically targeted and stole only Rei's Sleipnir Boots meant it wasn't a random robbery. It was undoubtedly a targeted theft aimed at the specific Sleipnir Boots Rei had left there.

"If that's the case... then is Coris really just an ordinary person caught up in this incident?"

"Yes. ...But that raises another question. Why go to the trouble of stealing Sleipnir Boots and hiding them in Coris's carriage? And under the carriage, no less? They could easily have fallen off on the way here."

Vihera voiced her confusion at Rei's words.

Indeed, that question was valid.

The Sleipnir Boots that Set was holding in his mouth were an extremely valuable magic item.

Furthermore, Rei's particular pair of Sleipnir Boots was crafted by the legendary alchemist, Esta Nord. It was the sort of thing that could easily be traded for gold coins if offered in the right place.

Hiding such an item in a place where it could easily be lost, regardless of when or where, seemed utterly implausible, even without considering Coris's personality.

"Just asking, but there wasn't... like, a spot under the carriage where you'd hide something you wouldn't want anyone to see... was there?"

"No!"

Coris denied it immediately at Rei's words.

Even though this might finally clear her of suspicion, she absolutely wanted to avoid doing anything that would make her look suspicious again.

"If you insist, I don't mind if you search under the carriage."

Rei thought it was probably true since she stated it so firmly, but Galahad asked her to check under the carriage with her own eyes just to be sure.

Rei understood this action was meant to prevent any future accusations that there had actually been a hiding place...

Still, Rei complied with Galahad's suggestion because he judged it best to see for himself.

In the end, there was no such contraption under the carriage. It only confirmed that Sleipnir Boots could fall off at any moment.

"...Given this, it's highly likely Coris was being used by someone."

Watching Set hand over Sleipnir Boots to Rei, Galahad murmured with a troubled expression.

With the magic item back in Rei's hands, Galahad was fortunate that there was no longer any need for him to oppose Rei.

Yet the question of who had orchestrated such a scheme remained unanswered.

They tried to turn Rei and the Azoth Trading Company against each other.

Galahad understood that was likely the objective this time... and most present understood it too. Yet when it came to pinpointing who had done it, no one could immediately name a specific party.

Even if its power had waned somewhat, the Azoth Trading Company was still one of the major trading houses within Gilm.

Precisely because it was the Azoth Trading Company, it had countless opponents, enemies, and those who held grudges or resentment against it.

Especially now, with spring just beginning, many merchants besides adventurers had come to Gilm.

Many merchants visited the Azoth Trading Company seeking goods unique to the frontier, and some even attempted to deceive them.

Given that, if asked whether he had incurred the grudge of any specific individual, Galahad couldn't confidently shake his head.

"Someone sure is playing some interesting games."

With the Sleipnir Boots returned, Rei's initial anger and panic had faded, but he hadn't forgotten that Azimov had been injured.

What's more, they'd been dragged into the underhanded schemes of merchants who likely had nothing to do with them.

There was no way he could simply forgive this.

"Rei, calm down. Getting angry here and now won't help, will it? First, we need to find the person who plotted this. If you want to vent your anger, there's still time for that later, right?"

Seeing the anger rising from Rei's body, Vihera spoke soothingly.

Rei, whose aura of rage was so intense it made even Coris, let alone Murt or... even Galahad, the former Rank B adventurer, stop in their tracks, managed to quell that rage momentarily at those words.
