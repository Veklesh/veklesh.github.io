We managed to find the magic item shop in Anesis fairly quickly after we started looking.

That said, it was merely the closest magic item shop to where Rei and the others were, and there were actually several other magic item shops as well.

(Well, since there are multiple magic item shops in Gilm, it makes sense that there would be several in Anesis too, right?)

Rei wondered about this, but the fact that there are so many magic item shops in Gilm is largely due to its location on the frontier.

Monster materials that can only be obtained around Gilm, plants that grow only there, or ores found only in the frontier… Because there are so many of these items, alchemists studying them have gathered in Gilm in large numbers, and as a result, the number of shops selling magic items has increased.

Moreover, Gilm is currently undergoing expansion, and once that is complete, it is certain that the number of magic item shops will increase even further.

In any case, Rei and the others managed to safely reach the magic item shop.

“Welcome. Oh?”

The clerk was a woman in her mid-twenties.

The female clerk must have immediately realized who had entered her shop.

She flashed a look of surprise for a moment, but immediately broke into a beaming smile.

“I never expected the Princess General and Crimson to come to my shop. So, what kind of magic items are you looking for?”

Even after realizing that the customers who had entered her shop were the Princess General and Crimson, the clerk’s demeanor didn’t seem to change.

Realizing that this shop might have been a good choice, Rei voiced what she was looking for.

“I’m looking for a magical accessory. Do you have any recommendations?”

“Oh? That’s…”

At Rei’s words, the shopkeeper turned her gaze toward Elena.

It was only natural. Rei was with Elena, and given the situation, he was asking if there were any magical items that served as jewelry.

Even if he said he’d come to buy a magic item as a gift for Elena, she couldn’t help but believe him.

...Though, strictly speaking, buying a magic item for Elena isn’t exactly wrong either.

“What should we do? Should we pick something for Elena first? Or should we pick something for Marina and the others?”

That’s precisely why, the moment she heard Rei’s words, the female clerk froze for an instant.

As long as she lives in Anesis, she naturally knows what kind of relationship Rei and Elena have.

But even though she knew about Rei and Elena’s relationship, she naturally had no idea about the existence of people like Marina or Vihera.

Given that, the words Rei spoke seemed almost fatal when directed at Elena.

(Please, spare me the lovers’ quarrel right now!)

She knew both Rei and Elena, and both of them were people with aliases.

If those two were to start a lovers’ quarrel right here, the magic items in this shop would suffer damage.

...No, worse than that—wouldn’t even my own life be in danger?

With a chill running down her spine at the thought, the female shop clerk desperately hoped they wouldn’t start a lovers’ quarrel…

“You’re right. I can wait until later. Since we came here to buy souvenirs, we should probably pick out Marina’s and the others’ first.”

“Huh?”

The words that came out of Elena’s mouth weren’t accusatory toward Rei, nor were they spoken with jealousy; it was just a perfectly ordinary tone.

(Oh, maybe she’s not a love rival or anything like that—maybe she’s just a little kid?)

With that thought in mind, the shop clerk opened her mouth to clear up her confusion.

“Ornaments, you say? Who are you planning to give them to? Children and adults are quite different, of course.”

Assuming she would say something like “around ten years old,” the clerk began thinking about what kinds of magical items were available for children…

“There’s one child around ten years old, and two people about our age.”

“……Huh?”

While the presence of a child was as expected, the fact that there were two adults in addition to the child was completely unexpected, and a dumbfounded sound escaped the clerk’s lips.

In this world, having multiple wives or multiple husbands isn’t all that uncommon.

But despite having a beauty like Elena, to actually reach out to other women as well... The female clerk shot Rei an amused look.

(I wonder if he’s amazing in bed, too?)

It’s only natural for someone in the service industry to think such salacious thoughts while keeping them hidden from her expression.

And for someone selling magic items, it was only natural to think along those lines.

“I see. Alright, I’ll show you a few items… but which do you prioritize: appearance or effect?”

“Um… which do you think is better?”

Rei thought for a moment, but ultimately couldn’t come up with an answer, so he asked Elena.

If Rei were buying jewelry for himself, he would certainly prioritize the effect.

But this time, the items were gifts for Marina and Vihera—women of unparalleled beauty. …Though there was also Byune.

In any case, since such beauties would be wearing them, there’s no doubt that appearance is just as important as the item’s magical effects.

No matter how powerful a magic item might be, if it’s terribly ugly… or even if it isn’t ugly, but simply doesn’t suit Marina or Vihera, it would draw even more attention precisely because they’re so beautiful.

To prevent that from happening, appearance really did need to be prioritized.

“That’s right. It depends on the magic item’s effects, but generally speaking, it’s better to prioritize appearance. Unless the effects are truly extraordinary, that is…”

Elena paused there and turned her gaze toward the shopkeeper.

The meaning behind that gaze was obvious without even needing to think about it.

In other words, she was asking if there were any magic items with effects powerful enough to make one overlook appearance as a woman.

The female clerk, caught in Elena’s gaze, quickly shook her head.

That was because this shop didn’t carry any magic items with the kind of effect Elena had described.

Well, there were a few magic items with powerful effects for sale, but those were not decorative items.

There was no way they could possibly be sold as jewelry.

“I’m sorry, but we don’t have any magic items that powerful here. Of course, if you really want one, we could order it or have an alchemist make it for you… but it would take quite some time.”

“I figured as much.”

Rei understood that as well.

Considering the incident with the Twilight Spear, that much was obvious without even having to think about it.

Elena, who herself used multiple magic items, didn’t dispute the clerk’s words either.

Once he was sure they were both satisfied, the clerk smiled and spoke.

“Well then, I’ll go get a few magical trinkets for you, so please wait a moment.”

With that, the clerk headed toward the back of the store.

There were a few magic items displayed at the front of the shop, but those were likely cheap knockoffs meant to be handed over in an emergency—say, if robbers or the like showed up—and were probably items the shop wouldn’t mind losing.

That said, even if they were cheap, it was only in the sense that they were cheap as magic items; to the average person, they were undoubtedly expensive goods, and if they were stolen by a robber or the like, the damage to the shop would undoubtedly be significant.

“We need to choose something substantial for Marina and Vihera. As for Byune… she’d probably prefer something with a solid effect over jewels or the like.”

“Is that so? …Well, I suppose so.”

For a moment, Rei questioned Elena’s words, but while it might have made sense if Byune were short on money like when they first met, the current Byune wasn’t particularly strapped for cash.

Especially since she’d started teaming up with Rei in Gilm, she’d been earning so much reward money that it made one wonder what her life had even been like before.

Thinking about it that way, it was clear that if Byune received an ornament, she wouldn’t sell it off somewhere—she’d keep it for herself.

Given that, a magic item with a solid effect would be more appreciated than one that simply sells for a high price.

Rei nodded in agreement with Elena’s words, and just then, the shopkeeper returned carrying several boxes.

“As for magic items that seem to strike a good balance between appearance and effectiveness, how about these?”

Saying this, the clerk brought out a variety of jewelry items, including rings, bracelets, necklaces, and earrings.

As the clerk had said, setting aside their status as magic items, they were all quite acceptable as jewelry.

At the very least, even from Elena’s perspective—let alone Rei’s—there wasn’t a single piece that could be called a failure as an ornament.

“They’re all wonderful.”

Hearing Elena’s words of admiration, the clerk broke into a broad smile.

Although he had brought only items he was confident in, having them recognized by Elena—the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel—was undoubtedly a source of great joy for the clerk.

“Hearing you say that makes it all worth it. I’m proud of every single one of these pieces. …Of course, since they’re top-tier items, the prices are naturally a bit on the high side.”

Even as he said this, the clerk wasn’t worried about the price.

Rei, who would be paying for this, was an adventurer with a reputation.

Given that, it was simply inconceivable that he wouldn’t have enough money to buy magic items of this caliber.

...Many adventurers spend every penny they earn without saving any.

But for an adventurer with a nickname, the money they earn should be correspondingly high, so it’s hard to imagine they could spend it all just on food, drink, and women.

Besides, since Rei is with Elena, I couldn’t imagine him doing something like buying a woman.

“What do you recommend?”

At Rei’s words, the shopkeeper pointed to a necklace.

“Yes. This necklace is imbued with the magic of water, granting the wearer resistance to fire-based magic. However…”

The clerk paused there and turned his gaze toward Rei.

“It’s useless against magic as powerful as the spells cast by Rei—the one known as ‘Crimson’ and called the ‘Chosen One of Fire Magic.’”

“The Chosen One of Fire Magic… To be honest, that’s the first time I’ve heard that.”

He’d been called “Crimson” as a nickname before, and “Bandit Eater” because he liked to attack bandits, but “the Chosen One of Fire Magic” was a term even Rei had never heard before.

That said, he wasn’t entirely unaware of why he might be called that.

To anyone who’s seen the fire whirlwind he creates by combining skills and magic, it wouldn’t be strange at all to think of him as the very embodiment of fire magic.

“Is that so? I hear it quite often, though. …Anyway, I can reduce fire magic damage below a certain threshold through my resistance.”

“...So it doesn’t completely nullify it, then.”

The clerk nodded apologetically in response to Elena’s muttered remark, which sounded slightly surprised.

“Yes. This necklace doesn’t go that far… We do have items with stronger magical defense than this, but those aren’t accessories—they’re things you set up somewhere to use…”

“I feel like that might have its own uses, but I’ll put that on hold for now. …Don’t you think this necklace would look good on Vihera?”

At Rei’s words, Elena imagined Vihera wearing the necklace.

It was undoubtedly a sight that could be described as “suiting her perfectly.”

“It would suit her perfectly. To be honest, it makes me a little jealous.”

The words that came out of Elena’s mouth were words of praise tinged with a touch of jealousy.

The shop clerk, who had been listening, failed to hide the surprise on his face.

And no wonder. After all, Elena—a woman of unparalleled beauty—had just declared that the necklace would look so good on someone that it would make even her envious.

As a woman, how many people in this world possess beauty that surpasses Elena’s?

When she thought about it that way, the clerk could only feel a sense of awe toward Elena—and toward this person named Crimson, who had managed to charm not only Elena but also several others with beauty rivaling her own.

All the clerk could do was explain the magic items while trying not to catch Rei’s eye—a task of extremely high difficulty.
