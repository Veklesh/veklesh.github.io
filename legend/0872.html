The place Rei was led to regarding Gamelion was a warehouse a short distance from the guild.

Outside was dark, but inside the warehouse, magic items provided light and maintained a constant temperature.

Perhaps because the lack of wind made it feel warmer than outside, the guild employee who had brought Rei here let out a sigh of relief the moment they stepped inside.

Incidentally, the guild employee who had guided Rei to this warehouse was not someone familiar to Rei, like Lenora or Kenny, but a middle-aged man in his forties.

...Then again, Rei often found himself interacting with guild staff for one reason or another.

It wasn't as if he'd never seen the man's face before; they had exchanged words a few times at the guild.

"So, Rei. We'd like to perform the Gamelion's disassembly and material stripping here. What do you think? Is that okay?"

"Well, asking me is pointless. My role is just providing the Gamelion. The actual stripping of materials and cutting up the meat is the guild's job."

"Ah, no. That's not what I meant... I wanted to ask if all the Gamelions in your item box would fit here."

Realizing his own misunderstanding at those words, Rei flashed an embarrassed smile and nodded.

"That won't be a problem at all. ...By the way, the guild is handling the crew for breaking down the Gamelion and stripping the materials, right? Mind if I ask what kind of crew it is?"

Looking around the building, which was too spacious to simply call a warehouse, Rei asked. The guild employee nodded reassuringly and began to explain.

"We'll basically approach people skilled at stripping materials from the guild side. Primarily focusing on adventurers who haven't finished their winter preparations yet. Then there are butchers connected to the guild. We also have former adventurers within the guild itself, so I don't think we'll have a problem with numbers."

Could that many people be gathered so quickly? That was what Rei thought, but that opinion was half right and half wrong.

If Rei had brought back nearly fifty orcs, gathering that many people would have been impossible.

...No, even before that, the guild probably wouldn't have agreed to handle the butchering, which was part of Rei's request.

But this time, the prey was special.

Gamelions, rare creatures whose numbers were low this year alone.

The dungeons were blamed for the scarcity, but the fact remained that Gamelion meat wasn't widely available in Gilm right now.

That's precisely why the guild accepted this exception.

"That's a huge help to me too. So, is it okay if I bring out the Gamelion?"

"Yes, please. Oh, if possible, could you line them up in order? It'll make butchering easier."

Rei nodded at the guild employee's words, moved slightly, and selected the Gamelion from the Misty Ring's list.

The next instant, the Gamelion materialized on the floor.

The guild clerk watching it flashed a faint look of surprise.

Even though they knew Rei possessed an item box, seeing it manifest directly right before their eyes like this was still quite stimulating.

That said, Rei had used Misty Rings numerous times before at the guild—for purchasing materials and magic stones, retrieving proof-of-kill parts, and so on.

Noticing the guild employee's reaction, Rei showed no particular response and continued to summon Gamelion one after another.

The Gamelions were laid out one after another on the stone floor.

Most stood around three meters tall, with some individuals exceeding four meters.

With Gamelion corpses of that size being laid out, it was undeniably a sight to behold.

"This is... incredible. I knew you were a skilled adventurer, Rei, but seeing it like this really drives it home."

The guild employee's words were filled with admiration.

And rightly so. Gamelions are C-rank monsters individually, yet every single one laid out here is a Gamelion.

In other words, he had taken on a group of Rank C monsters and defeated them all single-handedly, without a scratch.

(Truly worthy of a Rank B adventurer with a nickname... I suppose?)

While Gilm boasts many skilled adventurers, if asked whether many could accomplish what Rei just did, the Guild staff could only shake their heads.

There are quite a few Rank C adventurers, and even among those one rank above, the same Rank B adventurers as Rei, there are a decent number. Rank A adventurers, including those with nicknames, also number a fair amount... but if asked who among them could do what Rei did, even the guild staff could probably only name twenty, if that.

...Then again, the fact that nearly twenty adventurers exist who can single-handedly handle a group of Rank C monsters highlights just how unique Gilm is.

Even in ordinary towns, villages, or cities, you won't find a place with such a concentration of skilled fighters.

The only exception might be the royal capital.

While powerful monsters don't exist around the royal capital, the diverse range of requests—including the unique escort duties only the royal capital requires—and the prestige of the royal capital itself mean it attracts adventurers who are every bit as skilled as those in Gilm.

(Though, let's be honest, we're definitely the stronger ones.)

The guild staff member murmurs silently to themselves.

Both the capital and the frontier boast capable adventurers, and precisely because of that, they harbor a strong sense of rivalry toward each other.

Still, the fact that it remains rivalry rather than outright hostility is probably proof that the guilds are true guilds.

(We were outmatched in numbers against the capital's guild, which boasts an S-rank adventurer. But... with Rei and Crimson here, we should be evenly matched.)

While the guild clerk inwardly laid bare his competitive spirit toward the royal capital's guild, Gamelion after Gamelion was pulled from the Misty Ring and laid out in a row.

The guild staff watched with satisfaction, but as the last few were laid out, their cheeks began to twitch.

Because the Gamelions being laid out were increasingly becoming what could only be described as slaughtered corpses.

Having limbs severed was still the lesser issue; among them were Gamelions whose limbs had been cut off, whose heads had been severed, and whose torsos had been sliced apart.

Their internal organs spilled out from their torsos, making it impossible to believe they had died in a normal battle.

Furthermore, a significant number of Gamelions had their fur singed, and it wasn't uncommon to find ones where the fire had even seared into the flesh.

(Come to think of it, Rei uses a large scythe... Well, I suppose that explains it. Besides, if they're already torn apart like this, dismembering them would be almost effortless. But those burns... Ah, considering how skilled he was with fire magic, I suppose it can't be helped.)

I was startled for a moment, but watching it long enough, I grew accustomed to it.

...Though toward the end, they became increasingly severed and charred.

"Well, it's like this... but could I ask specifically how long it takes to dismember them?"

"...Huh? Ah, yes. Well, from what I can see here, the ones laid out first will probably take a while to dismantle normally. But the ones toward the end, since their limbs and heads are already severed, won't take long to dismantle. However..."

Pausing mid-sentence, the guild clerk continued apologetically.

"The Gamelions at the end are badly damaged, so the fur parts will be significantly cheaper... Is that acceptable? Also, for Gamelions killed using fire magic, the purchase price will likely be lower than those killed by slashing."

"Yeah, that's fine. I expected that when I took down the Gamelions."

Rei had noticed this himself while stowing the Gamelions into the Misty Ring after the fight, so he had no intention of complaining.

He understood perfectly well that he had gone too far.

Hearing Rei's words, the guild clerk breathed a sigh of relief and continued.

"Then, we'll have the processing completed within a few days at the earliest, or ten days at the latest, and contact you, Rei. Is the inn you're staying at, the Dusk Wheat inn, acceptable?"

"Well, I don't know of any other inns that can accommodate Set anyway."

To be precise, several inns with stables capable of accommodating Set have opened recently.

They were targeting Set, but they were also counting on other familiars.

But Rei has absolutely no intention of changing lodgings just because he's used to this one.

After all, Dusk Wheat inn wasn't just about having stables large enough for Set; the inn itself was top-notch.

The cuisine was excellent, and the various magic items provided ensured a comfortable stay.

Of course, the lodging fees were correspondingly high, but for Rei, it was hardly an issue at all.

"Well then, that's settled. ...Good work today."

Having received the parts of Gamelion's meat he could take home and stored them in his Misty Ring, Rei was escorted out by the guild staff and stepped outside the warehouse.

"Gurrrrr!"

Set, who had been waiting, growled as he rose and ran toward Rei.

This was because he had come here with Rei from the guild when they arrived at the warehouse.

Though some still seemed eager to play with Set, it was clear that for Set, Rei was still number one.

"Guruu, grrrrrrr!"

As Set purred and rubbed his face against him, Rei smiled and reached out to stroke his head.

Pleased by the sensation, Set growled even more happily.

They spent several minutes like that... a scene that would likely drive a certain Rank C female adventurer who adored Set mad with jealousy. Eventually, that moment passed, and Rei and Set each started walking towards the Dusk Wheat inn.

The peak of the bustle seemed to have passed; the number of customers at the stalls had noticeably decreased.

They made their way along the street, casually buying up leftover goods from the stalls, until their destination—the Dusk Wheat inn where Rei and the others were staying—came into view.

"Grrrr..."

Set growled softly, sounding a little lonely.

He knew they were nearing the inn, meaning he would have to part ways with Rei.

Still, knowing he would definitely see Rei again tomorrow, Set didn't throw a tantrum.

After seeing Set off to the stables and giving him one last pat on the head, I headed back to the inn.

"Welcome back, Mr. Rei. What would you like for dinner?"

As Rei entered the inn, Lana, who had just come out of the dining hall, called out to him.

"I got my hands on some pretty good ingredients. Would you mind cooking them for me?"

"Sure, no problem. What did you bring?"

"One Gamelion leg."

Lana's expression flickered with surprise at those words, but she quickly broke into a smile and replied.

"Well, well... That fellow will be delighted to have something worthy of his skills. But a single Gamelion leg... Does that mean today's request is Gamelion hunting? I've heard Gamelions are scarce this year, and it's been tough. In fact, Gamelion meat is trading at quite a high price. We've secured a reasonable amount ourselves, but it's still not enough for everyone to eat their fill."

"I won't say tomorrow... but I expect a decent supply of Gamelion meat will be circulating soon."

"So, could it be that you, Rei, solved the problem of the Gamelion shortage this year?"

"I can't say for certain... but probably."

It was true that a herd of Gamelions existed within the dungeon, but whether that directly caused this year's Gamelion situation? He could only say probably.

That's precisely why Rei answered Lana with the word "probably."

"I see. I truly hope that's the case. Well then, I'll have this Gamelion served for Rei's dinner tonight."

"Yeah, please do. And if possible, I'd like you to make something with that Gamelion meat for Set too."

Perhaps taken aback by Rei's unexpected request, Lana looked surprised, but soon smiled faintly and nodded in agreement.

"Yes, understood. Please wait in the dining hall. I'll take the Gamelion to the kitchen. ...So, what about the Gamelion?"

Prompted by Lana, Rei pulled a Gamelion leg out of the Misty Ring.

Being a rabbit-based monster renowned for its explosive speed, the Gamelion's legs were visibly well-developed.

They were such prime ingredients that using them in a dish would undoubtedly be delicious.

Lana's eyes widened for a moment before she promptly took the Gamelion leg from Rei.

...It must weigh at least thirty kilograms, but she handled it effortlessly. Perhaps it was thanks to running business at Dusk Wheat inn, dealing with battle-hardened adventurers, mercenaries, and merchants.

"Well then, I'll deliver it right away. Look forward to tonight's dinner!"

After watching Lana's back disappear into the dining hall, Rei followed suit and headed there too.
