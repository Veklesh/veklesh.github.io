"Oh? What's the matter? For someone so full of confidence, is that all you've got?"

Vihera's voice echoed across the grasslands.

This spot was about thirty minutes along the road from Subursta toward Abuelo.

They had fought on the grassland slightly off the road, deciding that fighting on the highway would interfere with other travelers...

The outcome was exactly as Rei and Byune had predicted.

"Well, facing Vihera, that outcome was inevitable."

"Yeah. ...Still, she held out longer than expected, didn't she?"

The two merchants spoke while soothing the horses, now cowed and quiet from fear of Set.

Their gaze fell upon the scene before them: Vihera gazing down at Magita, who lay collapsed on the grassland.

Being fellow fighters, Magita had baselessly assumed she would win.

While they both enjoyed combat, the quantity and quality of experience accumulated between Vihera and Magita differed greatly.

At least at this moment, Magita stood no chance against Vihera. Every punch and kick Magita threw was dodged, parried, or deflected by Vihera.

Magita attacked relentlessly while Vihera kept evading for several minutes... Then, having fully assessed Magita's strength, Vihera's fist sank into Magita's torso with startling ease, separating the victor and the vanquished with unmistakable clarity.

"Guh, d-dammit... Me, losing to a woman like this..."

"Do you really think you can gauge someone's strength based on whether they're a man or a woman? Whether male or female, the strong are strong. Isn't that exactly what we're seeing here right now?"

The spring breeze fluttered Vihera's thin robe, but Magita had no leisure to notice or enjoy it.

...Instead, the two merchants found themselves captivated by this almost fantastical scene.

"Hm."

Byune, who had been watching the fight from a distance with Rei, began walking across the grassland toward Vihera.

Though hardly tired, Vihera felt soothed by Byune's presence and smiled, stroking his head.

"Good work."

"Grrrr!"

Rei and Set also approached Vihera.

Rei held a cup filled with fruit juice, likely retrieved from the Misty Ring.

"...I haven't forgotten you forced this on me, you know."

Despite saying that, unable to resist the temptation of the fruit juice Rei offered, Vihera took the cup and brought it to her lips.

The sight of her white throat moving up and down was alluring, causing the two merchants to involuntarily blush and avert their gaze.

"Even so. It's still easier for someone who uses combat to judge combat skill, right? ...So, what's it actually like?"

"...I'll make sure you repay this properly next time. Well, I'd say the natural talent isn't bad. It's just that the skill is still quite raw. Probably because they've gotten by relying solely on that talent up until now."

"I knew that, but he does have potential?"

"Yes. Being a wolf beastman, his physical abilities aren't low by any means. You can see that much just by looking."

"So it's not muscle for delivering a destructive blow, but muscle focused on explosive power?"

When you think of warriors or fighters, you generally imagine muscles with a substantial mass that intimidate opponents.

But Magita's muscles were built for explosive power, creating a lean, defined physique.

"If she has talent... well. Magita, like I said before, I have no intention of taking disciples."

"Huh!?"

Magita, who had been catching his breath, gasped at Rei's words.

But he couldn't argue back.

Losing to Rei and begging to be his disciple was one thing, but being rejected and then losing to Vihera, who he thought of as Rei's woman, was another.

Magita, who had boasted about his skills and held sway in Subursta until now, felt the vastness of the world after suffering two consecutive defeats, following last night's loss.

...Perhaps Magita's misfortune lay in facing opponents like Rei and Vihera, who were undoubtedly among the strongest in this world.

And his good fortune was that, though he didn't realize it, he had sparred with the skilled fighters Rei and Vihera.

"It's not exactly training, but I can show you a path to becoming stronger."

"...A guidepost?"

"Yeah. Well, it's not that complicated. Simply leave Subursta and come to Gilm. If you work as an adventurer there, your skills will improve whether you like it or not. Plus, you could request adventurers to spar with you as practice to get stronger."

"Huh!?"

The words that came out of Rei's mouth were completely unexpected, even for Magita.

He had been active as an adventurer up until now, but the idea of requesting combat practice from fellow adventurers had never even crossed his mind.

...Instead, he'd been fighting adventurers up until now in a manner that bordered on provoking them.

But that very approach had also been the reason his skills hadn't improved as much as he'd hoped.

Magita tended to pick fights in places like cafeterias or taverns, as he had done with Rei.

Or sometimes, he'd pick a fight with someone in the streets, creating a quasi-confrontation.

Given the circumstances of these fights, the adventurers couldn't exactly bring their full strength to bear.

Magita himself hadn't realized this, which is why he'd come to believe he was the strongest.

"Besides, if we're in Gilm, I might let you join my training sessions... maybe. If the timing works out, that is."

"I'm going! I'm going, Master!"

"I told you, I'm not your master. Well, whatever. So we're going to Gilm, then?"

"Yeah, of course! I was planning to head to Gilm with you anyway, so my plans haven't changed."

It wasn't the way Magita had hoped, but he was still glad he could head to Gilm with Rei.

After all, he'd seen how easily Vihera had been taken down by a single blow, and had assumed he'd never be accepted as a disciple.

...Though, naturally, as Rei himself said, he had no intention of taking Magita as a disciple.

He figured taking her to Gilm would be the end of their relationship.

Of course, having said it out loud, he did think they could spar a bit if the chance arose.

"Right now, we're in a protective role. If you want to go to Gilm with us, you'd better get their proper consent."

Rei's gaze fell upon the two merchants who were also the employers of Vihera and Byune.

Watching Magita immediately head toward the merchants, Rei and Vihera exchanged glances.

"You're surprisingly considerate, aren't you?"

"Is that so? ...Maybe my time teaching at the officer academy recently had an influence. Well, either way, if you were seeking strength in Subursta, you'd have ended up in Gilm eventually."

Watching Magita converse with the merchants, Vihera spoke up.

"But is that okay? He might get dragged into the Holy Light Church business, you know?"

"...If you're going to bring that up, didn't you head for Gilm as merchant escorts too? Even if they attack us, we'll get dragged in when we get dragged in, and we won't get dragged in when we don't have to."

Since they'd already exchanged information about the Holy Light Church at the inn last night, there was no need to delve deeper.

Like Rei, they'd been attacked by Holy Light assassins, but the problem was that Byune had been involved too.

(Well, from the Holy Light Church's perspective, Byune was involved in last year's incident, so it wouldn't be strange for her to be included among their so-called major enemies.)

As I thought about how things would be tense for a while, in more ways than one, the discussion seemed to conclude. Magita approached us with a pleased smile.

"Master! I got permission! Well, I'll have to escort you in return, but that's no problem at all."

"I see. Then... well, if any monsters appear before we reach Abuero, I'll leave it to you."

"Leave it to me. No matter what kind of monster shows up, I'll defeat it with my own power!"

Hearing that confident voice, Rei casually shifted his gaze toward Vihera.

Despite having been so overwhelmingly defeated by Vihera just moments ago, that unyielding strength of spirit was truly something.

(Though, considering what happened at the tavern, he probably couldn't completely let his guard down.)

Watching Magita's movements as he struck at the enemy in the air, a faint unease crept over him.

"Haah!"

Magita's right fist, unleashed with a sharp cry, struck the goblin's head as it swung its club, sending it flying.

Given the goblin's small frame, Magita's fist naturally came down hard on its face.

As a result, the goblin's head slammed hard into the ground. It bounced several times, its body scraping along the earth, before finally coming to a stop.

With the last attacking goblin now defeated, there were no enemies left.

...Despite this, Magita's dissatisfied expression didn't waver.

"Magita, what about the proof of extermination parts?"

"I'll take them, just in case."

She drew a knife from the pouch at her waist and swiftly removed the right ear as proof of slaying, along with the magic stone from its chest.

It was clear she was accustomed to this task, having repeated it countless times before.

(She's fast.)

Cutting off the right ear alone was something Rei could do fairly quickly.

But the magic stone was another matter entirely.

While it was always located near the heart, the exact spot varied from individual to individual.

Naturally, extracting the magic stone from each one would take time, but Magita was removing them almost instantly.

In terms of stripping materials, she was undoubtedly more skilled than Rei.

"...Master? What's wrong?"

"Nothing. More importantly, gather up the goblin corpses. If we leave them here like this, we can't be sure no other monsters will show up."

"Do I really need to bother with that?"

"You do. If this were a forest far from the road, it wouldn't be a problem, but we're near the highway here. What if adventurers pass by later? What if it's merchants or travelers?"

"Isn't that the weak ones' fault?"

Rei hesitated, unsure how to explain it to Magita, who looked utterly baffled.

Never particularly gifted at teaching others, Rei paused before speaking.

"Suppose we leave a goblin corpse here, monsters gather, and someone dies. Then the deceased's relatives might blame the adventurer who caused it. If you think about it, we'd probably end up losing out in the end, right?"

"Well, then, just beat up whoever's picking a fight."

"...Look. Never mind. Just gather the bodies. There's no point wasting time here. I'll explain the details on the way to Abuero."

Why me? he wondered. But considering he'd have to deal with the hassle of getting Magita to Gilm anyway, he couldn't help thinking bringing her out of Subursta might have been a mistake.

Magita felt a bit resentful at Rei's words, but she couldn't abandon her hope of becoming his disciple. She probably realized it would be unwise to upset him here. She obediently began gathering the goblin corpses.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, the goblins had been slaughtered one-sidedly, so the corpses weren't scattered too far apart.

Moreover, Magita's fighting style relied on hand-to-hand combat rather than weapons like longswords, so naturally, the goblin corpses showed almost no damage.

Thanks to that, gathering the corpses wasn't too difficult. Afterward, they left the goblin corpses, now charred to ashes by Rei's magic, behind and continued along the road.

After that, they arrived at Abuero without further monster attacks and stayed the night. An adventurer who knew Magita caused a bit of trouble, but they couldn't involve the two merchants they were escorting, so they managed to avoid the commotion... The next day, to prevent any further unnecessary trouble, they left Abuero early.

On the way to Gilm, goblins appeared several times, but every single one ended up as Magita's training partners, their corpses left exposed...

"It's finally coming into view,"

As one of the merchants on the driver's platform murmured, the silhouette of Gilm came into view before the group's eyes.

For Rei, it was his first time seeing Gilm in two months, while for Vihera and Byune, it was their very first glimpse of the city.
