"Wow... I don't even know what to say... Wow..."

Watching the scene of Relieu rampaging to his heart’s content, Rei muttered.

For the rock plant, its most powerful attack was firing off rock leaves in rapid succession.

And since a massive number of Rock Plants were doing this, the sheer volume of rock leaves was staggering.

However, such an attack was only possible because there was a certain minimum distance between the Rock Plants and Rei and his group.

In other words, if one entered the enemy’s range, as Relieu had done, the Rock Plants surrounding them would attack, but those at a distance could not.

For Relieu, dodging such a small number of attacks was easy.

And so she proceeded to cut down the rock plants one after another, but...

“Listen, Relieu. I told you I’d prefer to capture the rock plants while they’re still alive, didn’t I? Did you not hear me?”

“U… s, sorry.”

At Marina’s words, Relieu meekly bowed her head.

If this had been anyone other than Marina, he wouldn’t have bowed his head so meekly.

But to Relieu, Marina was someone who had looked after him when he was young.

Just because she had stepped down as Guild Master didn’t mean she could so easily change her attitude.

In contrast to Relieu, who was being scolded, the others were keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

Meanwhile, Rei, though surprised by the situation, continued to stow the remains of the rock plants into his Misty Ring one after another.

Since they didn’t know what the rock plants were, they decided to store them away for the time being.

After all, there was a chance they might come in handy someday.

It might not be of any use in the future, but since storing them in the Misty Ring doesn’t take up any space, there’s no real problem.

(Besides, since they’re rocks shaped like plants, they’d probably be devastatingly powerful if thrown into a firestorm.)

Thinking along those lines, I also realized that if I had needed to capture the rock plants alive, I would have had to return to Golz.

Even though it’s a strange entity like a rock plant, if it were alive, it would probably have been impossible to store it in the Misty Ring.

Plus, moving through the dungeon while carrying a rock plant would be difficult, and if I wasn’t careful, there was a chance I might destroy it from the impact of combat, even though it was still alive.

Thinking about it that way, the fact that they didn’t have to return to Golz was, in a sense, fortunate for Rei.

“Grr!”

Set let out a cry as he placed the remains of the rock plant, which had been lying a short distance away, next to Rei.

Set had brought the rock plant over on his own initiative, hoping to help Rei even a little.

Of course, the rock plant wouldn’t be a problem at all if he were just going to destroy it, but since he had to bring it to Rei without breaking it, he naturally had to carry it carefully.

While Set possessed the power to kill any monster in the area with a single blow, if he were to unleash that power fully, the rock plant would inevitably be destroyed.

To prevent that from happening, he had to carry it in his beak so it wouldn’t break.

“Sorry about that, Set.”

As Set placed the rock plant on the ground, Rei expressed her gratitude and immediately stored it in her Misty Ring.

It might be a stretch to call it “lucky,” but Relieu’s strike had sliced through the rock plant rather than shattering it.

In other words, instead of having to gather scattered rock fragments, she could store the rock plants—which remained in relatively intact chunks—directly into the Misty Ring.

By the time Marina finished lecturing Relieu, most of the rock plants in this space had been stowed away in the Misty Ring.

“Well, as it turns out, there aren’t any stairs leading up or down from here… What should we do? I suppose we have no choice but to go back.”

No one objected to Vihera’s words.

In fact, this place was nothing more than a vast, open space. It was a complete dead end.

Given that, the only option left was to return to the upper floor… back to the forest.

...Or so I thought...

"Hmm!"

The voice of Byune, who had been searching the room, echoed around them.

The Byune I usually know would never do something like that.

In other words, Byune had been forced to do something that required such an action.

Near the wall—specifically, the wall located directly opposite the staircase Rei and the others had come down—Byune beckons to them.

Byune was radiating an unusually cheerful aura.

...Even so, one could say that Byune’s expression hasn’t changed at all.

Once everyone had gathered where Byune was standing, Vihera spoke up on behalf of the group.

“So, what’s going on? Did you find anything?”

The fact that Vihera—rather than party leader Rei—asked the question was likely because she was the only one capable of accurately interpreting Byune’s intentions and words.

Rei and Elena have also known Byune for quite some time.

Despite that, for some reason, Rei and Elena couldn’t understand what Byune was trying to say as well as Vihera could.

I even felt that there might be something other than mere familiarity at play here.

That said, even if they couldn’t grasp every detail, they could understand the general meaning, so there was no problem with communication.

“Hmm!”

Byune, with Vihera and the others watching, gently touched the nearby wall.

As Byune gradually applied more pressure to the wall, it eventually gave way under her touch.

Yes, that was clear proof that some kind of mechanism had been installed in the wall.

And just as the wall was pushed in, a section of the wall in this space—which had seemed like a dead end—shifted sideways, forming a rectangular shape.

It moved as if the wall had turned into a side-opening door.

“...Well, I never.”

It was Relieu who muttered this, seemingly taken aback.

He probably never imagined there would be a mechanism like this in this space.

To begin with, Relieu is a warrior who wields a longsword.

He doesn’t possess the skills to spot traps like a thief or find hidden passages…

Normally, adventurers like that would form a party to compensate for their weaknesses, but in Relieu’s case, he rarely ventured into dungeons to begin with, and had always managed to get by relying on his exceptional physical abilities and sharp instincts.

Still, seeing Byune demonstrate her skills as a thief like this, he can’t help but honestly think she’s amazing.

Even though he thinks she’s impressive, the fact that he wouldn’t dream of teaming up with a thief is typical of Relieu.

He genuinely thinks she’s impressive, but for Relieu, that’s where it ends.

“So? What are you going to do, Party Leader?”

Facing Relieu’s gaze, Rei immediately speaks up.

“We’ll keep going. Even if we go back to the forest, we don’t have any other leads on stairs. So, we’re better off proceeding down this secret passage.”

Personally, Rei suspected there were still quite a few unknown monsters in the forest above, so it was undeniably true that he was drawn to that idea.

But considering the objective of clearing this dungeon, it was also true that they should move forward.

Moreover, since they were here under the pretext of clearing the dungeon, Rei couldn’t very well say he wanted to hunt unknown monsters in front of Relieu.

If they didn’t know about Beast Magic, the same would go for Byune… but in Byune’s case, if Vihera said she wanted to fight unknown monsters or wanted their meat, he’d just go along with it.

Relieu’s presence—a gesture of goodwill from Daskar—was, at least for now, nothing but a hindrance.

That said, it’s also an undeniable fact that Relieu has helped us out in many ways.

In particular, Vihera is experimenting with creating new skills through mock battles with Relieu.

Of course, that’s not solely thanks to Relieu; it’s likely a conclusion drawn from the accumulation of various battles Vihera has experienced so far.

“Anyone here want to go back to the forest?”

Rei asked everyone, but in reality, he was probably the one who wanted to return to the forest the most.

The others say nothing to Rei, and the group proceeds straight into the secret passage.

“Just in case there are traps, we’ll move in the same formation as we did on the first floor.”

Byune nodded at Rei’s words and led the way, and the others followed in the same formation as on the first floor through the secret passage.

Looking at the passageway, which resembled the space with the rock plants, Rei concluded that this was likely built in the same way as the passageway on the first floor.

(If that’s the case, does it split into a Y-shaped junction again? …No way, it can’t be that identical.)

Rei and the others proceeded while keeping an eye on their surroundings, but suddenly, Byune, who had been leading the way, stopped.

“Hmm!”

She signaled to those behind her to stop, crouched down, and carefully examined the ground, which looked exactly like the cave on the first floor.

A few seconds after performing some sort of action, at the end of the passage—about five meters away from where Byune stood—a sharp spear suddenly shot out from the side.

If they hadn’t noticed anything and stepped on that trap, they would have been impaled by the spear coming from directly to the side at just the right moment.

Of course, for Rei and his group, it would have been possible to deal with the trap the moment it activated, so they likely wouldn’t have suffered any harm.

Still, there’s no doubt that having the trap deactivated is a relief for anyone making their way through the dungeon.

After all, even if you can handle it, getting caught in a trap isn’t exactly fun.

“It looks like some pretty vicious traps have been set here.”

The others nodded in agreement as Elena muttered this, gazing at the spear.

If it had been Golz’s adventurers who had taken on this dungeon, it’s highly likely they wouldn’t have been able to discover this trap.

They would have fallen into the trap… and there’s no doubt that several adventurers would have been impaled on the spears.

Of course, there was also the question of whether the adventurers from Golz could safely make it through the forest on the dungeon’s second floor… and whether they could somehow deal with the massive number of rock plants in the great hall, and on top of that, find the secret passage that Byune had discovered.

Considering that they’d have to go through all that just to finally reach this spot, it’s fair to say it would be too much for Golz’s adventurers.

“Byune, be careful from here on out. If anything happens, come back here immediately. …Though, we’re not that far apart.”

At Elena’s words, Byune murmured “Mm” as usual and nodded.

Satisfied with that, Elena said nothing more and turned her gaze toward Rei.

Realizing the meaning behind the gaze directed at him, Rei spoke up again.

“Well then, let’s move on. As Elena said, let’s proceed with caution. I doubt that trap was the end of it…”

No one objected to Rei’s words, and they began moving forward through the hidden passageway in the cave once more.

There was no dampness, and while the temperature was a bit chilly, it wasn’t cold enough to make them shiver.

However, the ground wasn’t flat; there were raised sections, and several rocks were scattered about, making it very difficult to walk.

Given the trap they’d encountered earlier, it was only natural to suspect that there might be some kind of trap lurking beneath those rocks or raised areas.

In fact, since Byune had already discovered and disarmed several traps in such spots, it wasn’t exactly an overreaction.

“Hm? …Hmm!”

And now, having noticed a thin thread set up right next to the ground, Byune issued a warning before beginning to disarm the trap.

“There sure have been a lot more traps since we entered this secret passage.”

Relieu muttered, looking annoyed.

For Relieu, who lacks the skill to spot traps, a place like this—where so many traps are set—is extremely unpleasant.

After all, if he moves carelessly, there’s a high chance the trap will go off.

However, even if a trap is triggered, Relieu can usually force his way through most of them on his own.

Take, for example, the mechanism near the entrance to this cave where spears shoot out from the side—for Relieu, it’s no trouble at all to block or dodge the attack once the trap is triggered, or even to simply cut them down with his longsword.

Hearing Relieu’s words, Rei found himself wondering if it might be better to simply use his magic to disable all the traps at once.
