Yellow's attack, launched from midair, caught Adria completely off guard.

Under normal circumstances, even if she couldn't see Yellow, the sound of wind cutting through the air might have alerted her.

But the opponent Adria faced now was Elena, known by the epithet Princess General.

Using the magic item Wind Fang, she was barely maintaining the upper hand with an attack that could be called a hail of arrows fired from its handle. Even so, she couldn't afford to underestimate her opponent.

It was only natural. Elena had achieved enough to earn that title, the Princess General, through her accomplishments.

Adrienne was confident in her own abilities, but even so, she didn't believe she could win in a fair fight.

That was precisely why she was relying on the power of the magic item, the Wind Fang, to fight.

Amidst this, an attack from the sky... and not from something as blatantly imposing as Set, but from a small presence like Yellow? It was utterly unforeseeable.

...Considering that Yellow had followed her, revealing this location, Yellow was the worst possible match for Adria.

"What!?"

His gaze darted to the Wind Fang, flung from his hand and rolling across the ground, while simultaneously scanning his surroundings to see what had just collided with him.

What came into view was a small figure... one that could only be described as childlike, the very image of Yellow.

Even distracted by Yellow, she managed to react instantly—proof that Adria was a skilled adventurer.

But... though her eyes were drawn to Yellow for only an instant, considering Elena's power, that split second became a fatal opening.

The instant Adrià reached out toward the Wind Fang lying on the ground, attempting to grasp its hilt, Elena's Mirage blade was unleashed at a speed that seemed to slice through the air itself.

Maximizing its characteristics as a chained sword, the tip of the blade flew like a whip across a distance impossible for a normal longsword.

Infused with magic to increase its power and speed, the blade struck the hilt of the Wind Fang from directly above, shattering it instantly.

"Huh!?"

Adria, witnessing this, sucked in a sharp breath.

Had her hand reached for the Wind Fang's hilt even a fraction of a second sooner, it would have been severed—or shattered—along with her own hand.

"...Knowing Rei, I imagine he desires this sort of magic item."

Elena murmured as she returned Mirage's blade to its longsword form.

Knowing her beloved's tastes, Elena had wanted to secure the Wind Fang if possible. She had considered using the whip-like blade—a defining feature of the chain sword—to entangle the Wind Fang and pull it to her. But Adria's movements weren't slow enough to allow that.

If she had tried to forcibly retrieve the Wind Fang, it would have been snatched back before she could succeed.

Considering that, the best course had been to prevent Adria from possessing the magic item at all.

As a result, Elena ultimately chose to destroy the Wind Fang.

"...Impressive. You truly live up to your reputation."

Having the Wind Fang destroyed—the very thing that had given her the greatest advantage over Elena—must have been a blow to Adria.

A hint of impatience flickered across her face, which until moments ago had worn a carefree smile.

"Well then, your greatest weapon is now useless. I'd appreciate it if you'd kindly surrender peacefully."

Elena, holding Mirage, offered the surrender ultimatum.

As he listened to her words, Adria scanned his surroundings.

Dias, arguably their strongest fighter, was locked in a fierce battle with Vihera, using Wind Scales.

There was no way he could come to her aid right now.

Her gaze shifted to Zuboz and Paul, who were talking with Rei instead of fighting.

Under normal circumstances, one might question such leisurely behavior during combat, but with Rei drawing the enemy's attention, it could even be said Zuboz was holding his own.

Zuboz possessed high alchemical ability, but he was by no means suited for actual combat.

And accompanying him was Paul, whose body was sturdy, but who normally ran a magic item shop.

Against a normal opponent, Paul's physical prowess would certainly be sufficient.

But when the opponent was Rei, it was a different story.

Since fighting head-on offered almost no chance of victory, Zuboz's strategy of talking to prevent combat wasn't wrong at all.

(Though he probably doesn't mean it.)

Adria let out a wry smile inside as she watched Elena close the distance with her Mirage in hand.

Even in a cornered situation, she found herself smiling. That was probably because Adria was a born hedonist.

As long as it was fun, the outcome didn't matter. That mindset was the very source within her.

(Come to think of it, there was one more person...)

Suddenly, Adria realized there should have been another ally present here.

The man who came to inform Dias that Rei and the others had entered the slums.

But since the fighting started, he hadn't made a single move.

(Did he run away?)

Since he was technically one of Dias's subordinates, it made sense he wouldn't be able to join this fight.

Adria thought it wouldn't be strange if he had fled... but then—

"Gyaaah!"

A sudden scream echoed, and she turned her gaze toward the source of the sound.

Given the surrounding battlefield, hearing screams wasn't unusual.

But the direction the scream came from was strange.

Not from where they were fighting, but somewhere else.

Elena must have felt the same. She halted her advance, Mirage in hand, slowly closing the distance between herself and Adria, and turned her gaze toward the source of the sound.

What met her gaze was the sight of a man sprawled on the ground, his body covered in long needles.

Rolling on the ground from the pain caused some of the needles to come out, but conversely, they would pierce deeper into his body, causing even more excruciating pain... and that caused him to roll on the ground even more, trapped in a vicious cycle.

There was no need to wonder what had happened.

The man who had vanished since the start of the battle was now writhing in agony near the man and girl, who were likely low in combat strength. There was only one conclusion.

"Oh, what a pity. If we could have taken them hostage here, we might still have had a chance to turn the tables."

"...So you're saying you consider this fight already lost?"

"Well, maybe. It might have been a chance to win easily, after all? Anyway, what I'm trying to say is, if you give up on victory so easily, it's just boring."

At Adria's words, Elena tightened her grip on Mirage while channeling magic into it.

Infused with magic, the Mirage transformed its blade from a longsword into a linked sword.

"So, you absolutely refuse to surrender? You should know better than anyone that without that magic item, you have no chance of winning."

"Yeah. But... that doesn't mean surrendering is any fun for me either. So, I'll struggle as much as I can... got it!"

Simultaneously with those words, a dagger flew from Adria's hand.

Propelled by a flick of her wrist alone, the dagger streaked straight toward Elena's face, but...

"Too naive."

With a single flick of the Mirage's hilt, the whip-like blades of the linked sword effortlessly deflected the dagger.

"Who's naive here, I wonder!"

What Adria had been waiting for was the split-second opening when Elena swung Mirage.

Given its linked-blade design, Mirage was inherently less maneuverable than a standard longsword.

Had it remained a long sword, Adria would never have seen a path to victory.

But now, in its whip-like form, there was still a glimmer of hope.

Of course, she didn't believe she could defeat Elena in a fair fight.

If she had the Wind Fang, there might still be a chance, but that reliable Magic Sword had been reduced to debris by Mirage.

Even so... even if she couldn't win by sheer strength, that was only if she fought head-on.

If she used other methods, there was still a chance she could manage something.

Yes, like that man had done earlier—using a weaker opponent... or someone important as a shield.

Seizing a momentary opening, Adria kicked off the ground and rapidly closed the distance to her target, Yellow.

Yellow, noticing this, flapped his wings, attempting to escape upward... but his action was a step too late.

As long as Adria was human, escaping to a place beyond her reach would be an absolute safe zone for Yellow.

That much was certain, but comparing Adria's explosive speed to Yellow's ascent rate, the former held the advantage.

The outstretched hand gradually closed in, seeking to grasp Yellow's body.

Yellow desperately flapped his wings to escape that hand, but Adrienne's hand was about to touch Yellow's body...

"Huh!?"

But at the very instant of potential contact with Yellow, Adria instantly withdrew her outstretched hand and forcefully kicked off the ground to retreat from the spot.

The reason for this sudden action became clear the very next instant.

Elena's swung Mirage blade had just passed through the spot where Adria had been standing a moment before.

Had she not instantly abandoned her attempt to secure Yellow, Elena's Mirage Blade would have sliced through Adria's body.

The speed and sharpness of that strike were enough to make even the evader himself think so.

"Close call. You were almost there."

Adria muttered, taking a different dagger than the one she had just thrown while creating distance from Elena.

Was she saying it was close because she almost secured Yellow, or because Elena's Mirage Blade failed to catch her?

"Just so you know..."

As she reshaped the Mirage into a longsword, Elena fixed her gaze on Yellow, who was flying through the sky toward her.

"I can pretty much guess why you tried to secure Yellow, but it was probably a waste of effort."

"What do you mean? Don't tell me you think you couldn't attack that kid... Yellow, I hear that's her name... because she looks so cute?"

If that's what you think, you're underestimating me.

Adriana wanted to say that, but Elena merely smiled at her, shook her head, and denied what Adriana was likely thinking.

"I'm not saying I hesitate to attack Yellow. I'm saying attacking her is pointless."

"...Useless?"

"Yes."

Elena paused, then stroked Yellow, who had come close beside her.

The sensation was warm, yet firm.

True to being a dragon's child, its scales and the skin beneath them wouldn't yield to any ordinary blade.

Moreover, Yellow wasn't just any dragon's child—he was a being Elena had created by harnessing the full power of an Ancient Dragon through Dragon Language Magic.

The creature before Elena—Adriana—was not something that could be dealt with by a mere dagger.

(Well, areas not covered in scales... like the eyes, would be weak points, though.)

It was precisely because she understood this that Elena swung Mirage before Yellow could be captured by Adria.

"...Well then, the situation is completely settled. You wouldn't want to get injured in a pointless fight, would you? This time, I'll have you surrender quietly. Of course, I wouldn't want you to try escaping like you did earlier."

Holding the Mirage, Elena asked. Adrienne thought for a moment... then shifted her gaze back toward the Zobu.

The battle there hadn't even begun yet.

(Meaning, if I buy enough time, Zubo's trump card might just break this deadlock. Not impossible, right? I don't know how far Zubo can go against Rei, but he does have that ace up his sleeve.)

Though she only had a dagger as a weapon, Adria still pretended to negotiate, hoping to buy even a little more time while waiting for the situation to shift.
