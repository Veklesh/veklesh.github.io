When Rei went to look for the guards, luckily a pair of them was heading right toward his study, so he was able to catch up with them immediately.

“Oh, Rei. What’s up? Since you were heading this way, does that mean you didn’t find anything? By the way, we didn’t find any clues either.”

One of the guards muttered this as he looked at Rei.

The other guard, who had been with him, nodded in agreement with his partner’s words.

(If professional guards couldn’t find anything, does that mean there really was nothing… or was it set up so even guards couldn’t find it… Hmm, which is it?)

Maybe I should have brought a thief who’s better at searching than a guard.

Thinking that, Rei shook his head.

“I found one over here.”

“Oh, so that side too… Huh?”

The guard must have assumed Rei hadn’t found anything either.

But, unexpectedly, Rei simply stated that he had found something.

Watching the two guards look surprised, Rei continued.

“Also, while I was investigating, I was attacked by a guy who seems to have been guarding this mansion.”

“Really? I was going to say, ‘How did you manage to stay unharmed…’ but I guess it’s no problem for you, Rei.”

Since he had easily taken down the middle-aged man who had appeared when they tried to enter the mansion, the guards assumed that even if Rei were attacked by someone else, he’d be fine.

Rei, too, nodded without any hint of pride.

“Well, yeah. Actually, I think he was weaker than the guy who came out at the entrance. …Anyway, I’ve tied him up and left him in the study, so come with me.”

“The study? So the study was the place Rei was looking for?”

“Yeah. …Though, from a quick search, there wasn’t anything there. The only thing I found was a switch on the wall.”

Seeing something so obviously suspicious, the guards’ expressions naturally became more serious than before.

“So, did you try pressing that switch?”

“No, I was just trying to figure out what to do about that. If this were just some run-of-the-mill petty criminal, I would’ve pressed the switch right away… but the mastermind behind this incident is cautious. If I pressed something as obvious as a wall switch, the worst-case scenario could be that the mansion collapses to destroy the evidence…”

“Ah… I suppose so. Since they’re abnormally cautious, it wouldn’t be strange for them to have set up a trap like that.”

“Right? That’s why I was looking for someone who knows about this kind of thing—and that’s how I ended up meeting you guys.”

Hearing that, the guards finally understood what Rei was expecting.

In other words, he wanted them to check the switch and see if there was any kind of strange mechanism hidden inside.

“Got it. Let’s head over right away, then. We might even be able to get some information from the guy Rei took down.”

And so, Rei led the two guards back toward the study he’d been in earlier… but just as the study door came into view, Rei suddenly stopped in his tracks.

「Rei？」

“...I never thought he’d kill one of his own men so easily. Wait, maybe another guard came in after I left, and that’s the one who was attacked?”

One of the guards tried to say something to Rei, who had stopped abruptly, but he must have realized what had happened from those words. With a stern expression, he followed Rei’s gaze and looked at the door leading to the study.

“What should we do?”

“We’ve got to go in, at least. If the blood smell is from a guard being attacked, they might survive if we treat their wounds. …But since I tied up the guy I caught so he couldn’t move, if he’s the one who got attacked, he probably hasn’t had his bleeding stopped yet, so it’ll be tough to save him.”

“Even if there’s a captive there, he’s technically on our side, right? So why would they go so far as to kill him?”

“I don’t know. Maybe they didn’t know when we’d be back, so they silenced him because they thought it’d be hard to carry an unconscious man, or maybe they’re just not all on the same page over there. …Anyway, we’ll find out once we get inside.”

“Got it.”

With that, the three of them—including Rei—headed toward the study and entered the room.

Once inside, the guards must have clearly detected the smell of blood, for they glanced around the room with faintly displeased expressions.

“This is…”

“Look.”

One of the guards tried to say something, but another cut him off, pointing to the wall of the study—the wall where the painting of the sea that Rei had seen was hanging.

There, as if placed right next to the painting, was the man who had just attacked Rei, pinned to the wall.

Yes, in the literal sense—his arms and legs were spread wide in a cross shape, and the tips of his limbs were pinned to the wall by metal stakes.

However, the source of the strong, rusty iron smell wafting through the air was not the stakes piercing his limbs, but the gash in his slit throat.

Rei had been away from this study for about fifteen minutes.

To have done something like this so quickly in that time… and to think he’d even considered this man an ally—it was anything but ordinary.

“He’s quite the skilled bastard. …And he’s got a strong sense of cruelty and self-assertion.”

At Rei’s words, the two guards nodded, though their expressions were bitter.

If the goal was simply to silence him, they could have slit his throat, stabbed him in the heart, or impaled his head.

Despite that, going to the trouble of slitting the throat and then deliberately nailing the tips of the hands and feet to the wall with stakes—that must be an expression of his strong sense of self.

(Besides, I didn’t hear the sound of stakes being driven into the wall. If that’s the case, the person who did this didn’t use a hammer or anything like that—they must have driven the stakes into the wall by some other means. Like… pushing them in by hand…)

If there had been a sound—and since it involved both hands and feet, that would mean at least four strikes with a hammer—the sound would have been high-pitched, and it would be strange if Rei hadn’t heard it even from a distance.

The fact that this didn’t happen was proof that whoever did this used a method other than a hammer or a sledgehammer.

“Anyway, what should we do? Should we take the body down? If we leave it like this, it’s definitely going to get in the way of searching the room.”

“I suppose so.”

The guard nodded immediately at Rei’s words.

Lowering the body to the floor would indeed get in the way of searching the room.

But there was no doubt that it would be mentally easier to have the body on the floor—or even out in the hallway—rather than having to look at a corpse pinned to the wall over and over again while searching the room.

From the perspective of preserving the crime scene, it’s certainly not something to be commended.

In any case, after pulling the stakes out of the hands and feet of the corpse that had been pinned to the wall, Rei and the guard carried the body out into the hallway.

Rei offered to store it in the Misty Ring, but for some reason, the guard refused.

Was it because he disliked relying entirely on Rei, or was there some other reason?

Rei didn’t know which it was, but he decided it was best to follow the guard’s lead since he was the professional, and so he complied.

“So, what about the switch?”

“Come over here for a moment.”

He motioned for the guard to come over to the painting of the sea—the spot where the man had been pinned earlier.

Having witnessed the scene earlier, the guards didn’t find it particularly interesting, but since Rei had called them over, they assumed it must have something to do with the switch and approached the wall.

“Look, it’s right there. …Can you see it?”

At Rei’s words, the guards turn their gaze to the wall he’s pointing at.

At first, even when looking in the direction Rei indicated, they seemed completely unable to tell where the switch was, but after staring intently, they must have finally figured it out.

Eventually, one of the guards spoke up.

“I see it. It’s there.”

Next, the other guard also said he’d found it.

“I’m glad you both found it. …So, what do you think? Do you think that’s some kind of trap?”

“Hmm… I’m not sure. Considering it was hidden like this so we couldn’t see it, I don’t think they’d go out of their way to set a trap.”

“Yeah. If they were setting a trap, they’d have put that switch in a much more obvious, visible spot.”

The two guards agreed that, given how hard it was to find, it was probably not a trap.

Rei nodded, seemingly agreeing that there was some merit to their reasoning... but what he said differed from the guards’ conclusion.

“The person behind this incident is quite cautious. Precisely because of that, it wouldn’t be strange to think they’ve rigged something to a switch in a hard-to-find spot, would it?”

“Well…”

The guard must have thought there was some merit to Rei’s words as well. He couldn’t bring himself to say anything more.

After all, now that Rei had pointed it out, it was a very convincing argument.

“...Alright. I’m no professional thief, but I’ve got a bit of skill in that area, so I’ll take a look. Just don’t get your hopes up too high.”

Saying that, one of the guards headed toward the wall where the switch was located.

True to his word, he was thoroughly inspecting the area around the switch, but his movements were by no means those of a seasoned professional.

Still, given the current situation, there was no doubt that entrusting the task to a guard with even a modicum of skill was the best choice.

(Well, what on earth is going to come out? It would be a huge help if there were a secret room, and the mastermind behind this was waiting on the other side.)

Things wouldn’t go that smoothly.

Rei knew that, but honestly, he still hoped they would find some kind of clue.

Then, a few minutes passed… Rei, who had been examining the switch on the wall, suddenly turned his gaze toward the door.

The guard standing next to Rei naturally noticed the movement as well, his gaze hardening as he prepared to act at a moment’s notice.

He thought the person who created that corpse might have returned… but the figure that emerged from the doorway was one of the guards.

“Hey, what’s that body in the hallway?”

From the guard’s perspective, it was a natural question.

In response, Rei spoke without showing any particular surprise.

“He’s one of the guards for this mansion. Oh, and just so you know, I didn’t kill him. I suspect one of the other guards did it to silence him… But more importantly, are you alone? What happened to the guy you were working with?”

That’s right—only one guard had entered the study.

Normally, they would have been operating in groups of two or three, on guard against an attack by the middle-aged man or the man lying dead in the hallway.

Nevertheless, the fact that only one guard had shown up was enough to make Rei feel uneasy.

(A traitor among the guards... is that a possibility?)

Thinking this, Rei concluded that it wouldn’t be strange if this man were the one who created the corpse in the hallway. He prepared himself to respond immediately to whatever might happen, but the guard must have realized he was being suspected. Panicking, he raised both hands and insisted he wasn’t in cahoots with the enemy.

“I know what you’re thinking, but I’m not a traitor. It’s just that I found something—no, someone—unexpected over there. I came to ask for advice on what to do.”

“I get the reason, but you sure came alone.”

Saying that, Rei lowered his guard for the time being.

That said, given the situation, he didn’t fully trust the man, so he remained ready to react immediately if anything happened.

“It looks like there were attackers on Rei’s side, but there weren’t any over here. Though there weren’t any attackers, I did find someone unexpected instead. That’s why I came to ask for your advice on what I should do.”

Normally, one should avoid acting alone to guard against potential attackers.

But since there had never been an incident where someone entrusted with guarding this mansion had been attacked in this area, he had come alone.

“Don’t be reckless.”

The guard, who had been checking a switch on the wall, said this with a look of exasperation toward the man who had come alone to report the situation.

That said, this guard hadn’t found anything unusual in the areas they’d checked, nor had they been attacked.

That’s precisely why it wasn’t unreasonable to assume there wouldn’t be an attack.

“Well, just think of it as me being in a hurry.”

“...So, what exactly did you find?”

In response to Rei’s question, the guard who had come here wore a serious expression… and spoke.

“A large number of corpses—people reduced to nothing but skin and bones. More than ten or twenty.”

That was what he told them.
