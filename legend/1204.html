The day after being told there was something to discuss regarding the request for nomination, Rei arrived with Set at the front of Viscount Marnino's mansion in the noble district.

The mansion was fairly large, but naturally paled in comparison to the residences of nobles like dukes.

Rei didn't know what size mansion corresponded to a particular rank, but he understood it was incomparable to the homes of ordinary people lining the main streets of Gilm.

As expected, gatekeepers stood guard at the entrance to such a residence. There were two of them.

The gatekeepers tensed momentarily upon seeing someone approach, but quickly relaxed.

They recognized the approaching figures as Rei and Set.

...Under normal circumstances, they wouldn't immediately recognize who the approaching figures were.

But in Rei's case, with Set present, they could instantly understand who it was.

These guards were also residents of Gilm. Naturally, they knew Rei and Set's appearances and were aware they were high-ranking adventurers with aliases.

And since my superior had told me Rei and Set would be visiting today, there was no need for further tension.

"We were summoned by Viscount Marnino."

"Yes, we're aware. I'll fetch the person in charge immediately, so please wait just a moment."

The knight's tone was unusually polite for an adventurer.

This was likely because Rei was a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname.

...Though, I'd heard that once before, when Rei came calling, the gatekeeper had taken it upon himself to turn him away, and as a result, he'd been severely reprimanded by his superiors. So, this response was probably only natural.

Anyway, watching one of the gatekeepers enter the mansion, Rei decided to gather information about this Viscount Marunino fellow.

He had heard a general description of Viscount Marnino from Kenny yesterday, but hearing directly from someone who served him would undoubtedly be the most reliable and efficient way.

"Viscount Marnino isn't usually in Gilm, I hear... Why come all the way here? As you know, Gilm had quite a bit going on not too long ago."

"You mean about Ambris. I live in Gilm too, so I know about that. ...But unfortunately, I don't know why Viscount Marunino came to Gilm either. This mansion isn't even where Viscount Marunino lives; it's where his relatives reside."

"I see. So it's like a vacation home."

Building a villa in the remote Gilm is, in many ways, akin to an adventure.

To be precise, this mansion was built to maintain contact with Daskar, the central figure of the Neutral Faction.

So the head of the Marnino family suddenly showing up must have come as a complete surprise to the staff working here.

(From what I'd heard, they weren't exactly notorious, but they had their share of petty complaints—typical nobility, neither harmful nor helpful... But judging by this gatekeeper's attitude, maybe they're not such bad people after all?)

That's what Rei thought, but feeling a bit restless, he dismissed the idea while stroking Set.

From what he'd heard, this gatekeeper lived in Gilm.

If that's the case, he'd naturally know more about what kind of person Rei is than others, and wouldn't do something like picking a fight with her.

When Rei first came to Gilm years ago, there were quite a few who would pick a fight... but fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, Rei is now quite famous in Gilm.

Basically, no one would dare pick a fight with Rei like that.

...I say "basically" because there are always a few rogue elements.

After all, Azimov didn't know who Rei was when they first met.

Also, it's not uncommon for adventurers new to Gilm who judge others solely by appearance to pick a fight with Rei if they see him without Set.

Though such individuals usually end up regretting it.

"I've never met Viscount Marnino myself... but could I ask what kind of person he is?"

"Hmm, well... He's not a bad person. He even greets us gatekeepers."

"Hmm..."

Since he was employed by the Marnino family, it was only natural he couldn't criticize his employer.

Even thinking that, there was no trace of dislike in the gatekeeper's words.

He probably doesn't know the exact nature of his employer yet, since he's not the kind of person you'd normally find in Gilm.

That's precisely why Rei is sticking to safe, non-controversial words... or so Rei concluded.

He conversed with the gatekeeper like that for several minutes. Soon, the gatekeeper who had gone into the mansion earlier returned.

An older man accompanied the gatekeeper, and Rei guessed he was likely the butler or something similar.

"My apologies for the wait. I am Jonathan, head butler of the Marnino Viscount household. My master awaits you. Would you kindly come with us?"

The man who introduced himself as Jonathan was indeed, as Rei had expected, the butler.

Feeling a slight satisfaction that his guess had been correct, Ray nodded in response.

(A butler, yet no Sebastian... Though there was a butler with a similar name before.)

Thinking such thoughts, he passed through the gate and entered the mansion.

Naturally, Set couldn't enter the mansion, so one of the gatekeepers escorted him to the stables.

Set seemed disappointed he couldn't stay with Rei, but he obediently followed the gatekeeper anyway.

From past experience, he probably figured that going to the stables meant he'd get something tasty to eat.

This wasn't entirely baseless speculation; in fact, the aroma of stir-fried meat wafting from the direction Set was headed was stimulating his appetite.

This was because several of the maids working at the mansion were Set enthusiasts.

...One of the maids had asked a young cook, with a smile, to prepare a meal for Set... and the cook, who secretly harbored feelings for that maid, couldn't refuse.

It wasn't quite a romantic ploy, but the meal arranged by the maid was waiting in the stables. Naturally, the maids were waiting there too, ready to monopolize the sight of Set eating and admire him themselves.

"Well then, your husband awaits you here."

While Set dined in the stables, being doted on by the maids, Rei stood before the room Jonathan had led him to.

"Excuse me, Master. I have brought Lord Rei."

"Hmm, come in."

Jonathan opened the door at the voice from inside.

As Rei stepped through the open doorway, the one waiting for him was a man of medium build and height, appearing to be in his late twenties to early thirties.

(He's young.)

Putting his own age aside, Rei observed the man at the desk ahead of him, reviewing some documents.

In truth, the man before him was still young enough to be called a youth, quite different from what one might imagine as the head of an aristocratic house.

Though a viscountcy was not a particularly high rank among the nobility, it was still surprising to Rei that such a young person held the position of head of the household.

Then again, among the nobles Rei had encountered before, there had been even younger ones.

(Wait, were they not the head of their house?)

What flashed through Rei's mind was the daughter of a member of the Noble Faction... a fellow royalist like the man before him.

That child, with her peculiar speech patterns like "noja," was still a girl—no, a young girl—even compared to Rei, yet she fully embraced her duties as a noble.

"Well, so you're Rei. ...You're quite small."

Though Rei felt a slight irritation at those words, he was well aware of his own short stature.

Having heard it countless times before, he resigned himself to it, pulling down the hood of his Dragon Robe as he spoke.

"I'm Rei, a Rank B adventurer. I heard there was a request for me."

Twitch.

The man's eyebrows twitched at Rei's choice of words, but he didn't let any further displeasure show as he spoke.

"Linus Marnino. Sit down first. We'll discuss the details after that."

Viscount Marnino... Rei nodded at Linus's words and sank into the sofa in the room.

Then, as if timing it perfectly, a maid entered the room carrying tea.

Many maids adored Set, but some had their own duties and couldn't indulge in that adoration.

This maid was one of those unfortunate ones, inwardly feeling quite disappointed.

Still, seeing Rei up close—Rei who had Set as his familiar—was surely a stroke of luck for the maid as well.

While she enjoyed admiring someone like Set, she had also been able to see Rei's true face up close, a face often described as feminine.

Normally, Rei wore the hood of his Dragon Robe, rarely exposing his face in public.

It wasn't for any particular reason; it was simply because the Dragon Robe's built-in air conditioning function was incredibly convenient.

Anyway, the maid, struck by this unexpected stroke of luck, let out a silent squeal of delight in her heart as she left the room.

Left behind were Rei, Linus, a few knights who were likely Linus's guards, and Jonathan, the head butler.

Leaving the guards behind was only natural when dealing with Rei.

Despite his notorious reputation, Rei was still fundamentally an adventurer.

Therefore, having guards ready in case something happened was only natural.

"Now then, first, I must apologize for summoning you like this. We've had our own issues, and I couldn't just carelessly go to the guild."

"...That's fine. So, what is it you wish to request of me?"

"Hold on."

Linus raised his hand to stop Rei, who was eager to get straight to the point.

Since Rei wasn't particularly in a hurry, he nodded in agreement without objecting to Linus's words and fell silent.

"First, I've heard you're confident in your fighting abilities... though perhaps I needn't ask about that. Was it Ambris? I hear you distinguished yourself there too. And then there's the matter of the war with the Bestir Empire."

Within the Kingdom of Mireana, unless one was utterly disinterested in gossip or never ventured outside, they were bound to have heard Rei's name.

Nobles especially couldn't afford to be ignorant of such matters.

And for nobles loyal to the king, even if they kept quiet, they'd still get a fair amount of information.

There was no way Linus didn't know about Rei's combat prowess, one of his most notable abilities.

"The fact that he also has a griffin as a familiar is impressive too. ...Honestly, I'd like him to serve my house. What do you say? The reward is negotiable."

Though he made it sound like a joke, Linus's eyes held genuine intent.

If Rei even nodded half-jokingly here, he'd start the procedures immediately... it was that convincing.

That's precisely why Rei shook his head immediately.

"Sorry, but I have no intention of serving anyone right now. I've got plenty of things to do."

Even in ordinary times when he had nothing to do, Rei wouldn't have considered serving anyone.

And now, he was searching for a way to restore Vihera's consciousness.

He had absolutely no intention of serving nobility.

(So maybe that request was actually trying to scout me? Well, sorry to disappoint. ...For both me and them.)

Thinking this, Rei lost interest in the request itself and spoke up.

"So, I guess I'll be heading out around here..."

"Wait. Hold on. You haven't heard the whole story yet, have you?"

Sensing Rei was about to leave, Linus hurriedly tried to placate him.

Rei started to stand, but seeing Linus's expression, he sat back down on the sofa.

Seeing Rei like that, Linus let out a small sigh.

Rei felt a slight annoyance at Linus's expression, which seemed to say, "What a hopeless guy."

"Then, I'd like to hear about this request for a designated job."

"...Fine. But I'm warning you, once you hear the details of this request, you won't be able to refuse. Understand that before you ask."

"Wait."

Rei immediately retorted at Linus's words.

His usual tone, completely at odds with the polite manner he'd been consciously trying to maintain.

If he heard it, he couldn't refuse. He had absolutely no intention of accepting such a request.

"You mean I have to accept it without question, even before knowing what the request is?"

"Yes. You can assume it's that important."

"I see."

Hearing Rei's words, he seemed satisfied with his own reasoning.

Linus nodded with satisfaction, opening his mouth to speak...

"Unfortunately, I'd like to consider this conversation never happened. I have no intention of accepting the request."

His eyes widened dramatically at the words that came out of Rei's mouth.
