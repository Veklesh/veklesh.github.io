After finishing lunch at the house Elena and Vihera were renting, Rei used his break time to come to the house he was renting.

Besides Rei, only Set was present.

Even Yellow, who was often with Set, was nowhere to be seen.

When Rei said he was stepping out for a bit, Earth gave him a look that said, "Don't leave me behind..." but Rei pretended not to notice and came to the house he was renting.

Rei understood Earth's feelings—that he didn't want to be left alone in a place filled only with women, especially beautiful women and young girls. But right now, Rei had something he needed to do.

It was something he should have done much earlier... last night or this morning, even. That was...

"This spot should be fine. Set, check the perimeter for anyone watching us. I don't see anyone, but better safe than sorry."

"Grr!"

At Rei's words, Set gave a short growl and scanned the surroundings.

Many elves excel with bows, and consequently, many possess keen eyesight.

Some also possess the ability to enhance their vision using magic.

When Rei was under house arrest, it was someone with that ability who had been watching him from about a hundred meters away outside the window.

That very fact made him wonder if they might be under surveillance by some means right now too...

Their decision not to carry out their planned actions last night or this morning stemmed partly from this caution.

But now... with Earth and Polo, two outsiders present in this settlement, Rei judged it highly unlikely anyone was focusing their attention on them.

Of course, it wasn't impossible that someone was watching them by some means, but it would be impossible for them to remain completely undetected by Rei and Set's senses.

And indeed, as Rei and Set scanned the surrounding area for signs of presence, they detected no one focusing their awareness on them.

"...Alright, no one's here."

"Grrrrr!"

Set nodded at Rei's words, confirming no one was nearby.

Ideally, they should have done this outside the settlement, but seeing Earth's... no, the rare species known as the nine-tailed Carbuncle had undoubtedly stirred various thoughts within them.

That was precisely why Rei had decided to absorb the magic stones he'd been hoarding for a while now, right here with Set.

(I really wanted to gather at least ten more magic stones before absorbing them, though.)

The skills gained from absorbing magic stones weren't particularly powerful at first.

By absorbing magic stones from multiple monsters possessing similar skills, one gradually increases their pure power.

Of course, that refers to the raw power of the skill itself. Mastering its use depends on the user's skill and sense—like Set's or Rei's ability to wield Death Scythe.

Still, Rei believed that to purely increase power, it was better to absorb several types at once.

That said, it wasn't something he felt absolutely bound to follow; it was more of a "wouldn't it be better if possible?" kind of idea.

That's precisely why encountering Polo, a rare species of carbuncle, made him change his mind so easily and decide to absorb the magic stone like this.

Another factor was that the Aero Wing magic stone's attack method gave a clear hint of what skills it could absorb, and Rei really wanted that.

"...Well, I'll save the best part for last. Now then, the magic stones I have are..."

He muttered as he pulled out the magic stones.

Ogre, Gamelion, Cyclops, Aerowing.

They varied in size, but each was unmistakably a magic stone.

"There are multiple Gamelions too, but..."

muttering, Rei wore a slightly disappointed expression.

Because having multiple Gamelion magic stones was meaningless.

The Death Scythe had already absorbed the regular Gamelion magic stone, and only Set could absorb the regular Gamelion magic stone.

(Well, it could probably absorb the magic stones... but even if it could, it wouldn't matter.)

The magic stones currently absorbed by Set that hold meaning are Ogre, Gamelion, and Aerowing.

And the magic stones that Death Scythe can meaningfully absorb are Cyclops and Aerowing.

"...Well, let's try this first."

Rei reached for the Gamelion magicite.

Having obtained them in bulk from the dungeon, they held no particular value.

「Set！」

He called out and tossed the magic stone to Set, who deftly caught it in his beak and swallowed it.

[Set has learned the skill 'Power Crush Lv. 3']

Simultaneously, a message echoed in his mind.

"Huh... I never thought even a regular Gamelion could absorb them."

"Gurururu!"

Set purred happily in response to Rei's mutter.

Set was likely pleased that his skill had grown stronger and that it had made Rei happy. He purred contentedly.

"Power Crush is a skill Set uses quite a bit too, so in that sense, it's good. ...Considering Gamelion's combat style, it makes sense that he could acquire this kind of skill."

Gamelion, with its giant rabbit-like form, attacked using both the power derived from its massive body, contrary to its appearance, and the speed utilizing the agility characteristic of rabbit-type monsters.

Considering that, learning the Power Crush skill was a safe choice.

"Testing it... well, I'll try it out during the afternoon forest exploration. Next, let's try this one."

The next item Rei reached for was the ogre's magic stone.

However, his expression was slightly less than pleased.

Because the ogre that possessed this magic stone was a rather small specimen.

Is it really okay to absorb the magic stone from such a weak individual? That was her thought.

"Gamelion was absorbed by Set, and this ogre's magic stone... wait, come to think of it, I had Death Scythe absorb it before. So, I was thinking of having Set absorb this one too. Is that okay?"

Recalling the ogre they had defeated in the dungeon with the altar of succession they had visited with Elena and the others, Rei addressed Set.

"Gurrrrr!"

Set let out a happy growl at Rei's words. He must have sensed Rei's unease about the ogre's magic stone. Set stared intently at Rei with his round eyes.

Urged on by that gaze, as if saying "Hurry, hurry," Rei flung the magic stone he held toward Set.

Simultaneously, Set swiftly catches the ogre's magic stone in his beak and swallows it.

[Set has acquired the skill 'Power Crush Lv.4']

Hearing the voice echo in his mind, Rei wore a complex expression—part acceptance, part dissatisfaction.

Of course. Power Crush was a skill that had just leveled up a few minutes ago using Gamelion's magic stone.

(No, Set's attack just got stronger, so maybe there's no reason to be dissatisfied?)

Convinced inwardly, he stroked Set's head as the dog looked up at him, eyes asking, How about it? How about it?

"Well then, you remembered it well. I can't test just how powerful it is here... but show me when we get to the forest."

"GRRRR!"

Leave it to me! I stroked Set's head as he growled, then turned my gaze to the remaining magic stones.

Left were one Cyclops magic stone and one Aerowing magic stone.

He picks up one of them... the Cyclops magic stone. He tosses it into the air and strikes it again with the Death Scythe.

【Death Scythe has acquired the skill 'Pain Burst Lv.2'】

The message flashing through his mind made Rei tilt his head.

"Huh?"

What's wrong? Set looked at Rei with a puzzled expression.

He had expected Rei to be pleased at acquiring the skill... but seeing no sign of it, he must have been puzzled.

"No, Pain Burst is a skill that inflicts immense pain on the target when you wound them, regardless of the actual damage, right? So why did I learn this skill from a Cyclops's magic stone... I just don't get it."

"Grrr..."

Hearing Rei's words, Set growled in his throat, looking just as perplexed.

Watching Set like that, Rei probably decided it was pointless to question it now. He shifted his gaze to the hand gripping the Death Scythe.

Whatever the reason, the skill had been enhanced.

And if it meant increasing the pain inflicted on the opponent, even if the wound itself remained the same, using it in battle wouldn't be difficult to catch enemies off guard.

(Though it'd be useless against plant-type monsters or those without pain receptors. Still, better to have it enhanced than not learn any skills at all. ...I wish something like Power Slash had been enhanced instead.)

"Gururu!"

As Rei pondered Pain Burst, Set eventually cleared his throat. "What's up? Should we keep absorbing the magic stones?"

Rei smiled at Set and stroked his head.

"Yeah, I guess there's no point dwelling on every little detail. It didn't really put me at a disadvantage, so I should just accept it. Sorry, Set, did I make you worry?"

As Rei gently stroked Set's head while speaking, Set let out a pleased growl.

Returning the smile to such a Set, Rei then turned his gaze to the Aero Wing magic stone, arguably the star of this absorption session.

Even though he'd just been petting him, it felt a bit awkward to say this... but still, Rei spoke to Set.

"Hey, Set. I want to use this magic stone for the Death Scythe. Is that okay?"

"Grrr..."

At Rei's words, Set looked down, looking disappointed.

It was clear from his current expression that Set had also been eyeing the Aerowings' magic stone.

Seeing Set looking down made Rei feel a little sorry for him, but still, he wanted to handle this matter himself... more accurately, he wanted Death Scythe to absorb the magic stone.

"Hey? Set. Please. It's not like every single Aero Wing except this one got wiped out. Maybe we could find another Aero Wing this afternoon or something?"

"Guru?"

Set turned his round eyes toward Rei, asking, "Really?" Rei nodded.

He wasn't saying this without any basis.

He'd heard from Marina and Jusla that Aerowings originally lived in this forest too.

Therefore, as long as they were in the forest, there was no reason Set couldn't find an Aerowing.

And as proven when they defeated this Aero Wing, with a single strike from Set, ending an Aero Wing's life shouldn't be too difficult.

In other words, obtaining the Aerowings' magic stones shouldn't be that difficult.

"Grrrr... Grr, grrrrrr..."

Whether he finally understood what Rei meant and his mood improved, or he simply shifted his thoughts to the prospect of eating more Aerowing meat, Set turned his gaze toward Rei, urging him on.

Rei gently stroked Set's head with gratitude and reached for the Aero Wing's magic stone.

"...I'm counting on you..."

He murmured, flinging the magic stone into the air before striking it with a flash of the Death Scythe.

The sharp strike severed the magic stone without so much as a sound.

【Death Scythe has acquired the skill 'Flying Slash Lv.5'】

A message echoed in his mind simultaneously.

"Yes!"

Clenching his fist, a cry of joy escaped Rei's lips.

Yes, this was the skill he had been aiming for.

It was the skill Rei used most frequently, and it was also extremely useful for long-range attacks.

For long-range attacks, she had methods like Nebula's Eye and spear throwing, and if push came to shove, magic was also an option.

Still, the Flying Slash skill, which required no chanting or channeling of magic power and could be used casually, was incredibly valuable to Rei.

"I want to try it... but here might be a bit problematic. I can't exactly start slicing up this house's garden, can I?"

Of course, Rei knew the power of Flying Slash better than anyone.

That's precisely why he could clearly imagine what would happen if he unleashed Flying Slash at full power here.

In the end, testing the technique in the borrowed house's garden was out of the question. He decided to try Flying Slash later that afternoon while patrolling the forest perimeter.
