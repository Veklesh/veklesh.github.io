After rescuing the children being attacked by goblins in the settlement, Rei was running through the settlement again.

(Come to think of it, I should've asked that woman earlier where the danger spots were. Then I could've headed to the areas short on reinforcements.)

Realizing belatedly how panicked he'd been, Rei sighed inwardly.

...Even so, Rei's running speed didn't slow down at all.

Inside the settlement, there were almost no one moving about like the Lugdos earlier.

Those capable of fighting had headed outside the settlement, while the rest were either evacuating or tending to the wounded.

Sounds of battle echoed from beyond the barrier's protective sphere.

They came from multiple points, as if encircling the settlement.

And beyond the battle cries...

"BUGYI!"

Hearing a familiar voice from a short distance away, Rei changes direction.

He headed toward a house standing nearby.

Upon bursting inside, what Rei saw was an orc happily stuffing dried fruit into its mouth. The fruit was likely preserved by the home's inhabitants for storage.

Probably because it was so engrossed in the dried fruit. Though Rei had approached without particularly trying to hide his presence, the orc showed no sign of noticing, happily continuing to eat the dried fruit.

(I've heard that feeding cows or pigs fruit makes their meat taste better... Does that apply to orcs too? Well, if it were eating only fruit all the time, maybe, but just eating a little bit like this now probably doesn't make a difference.)

Holding the Death Scythe, Ray swung it at the orc, who still showed no sign of noticing him, though he was exasperated.

However, mindful that the orc was inside a house, he struck not with the blade of the Death Scythe, but with its handle.

Rather than just hitting it, he swung the Death Scythe in a half-turn, hooking it around the creature's torso.

"Gyup!"

Perhaps because he was struck while eating dried fruit, the orc let out a strange scream as he was blown out the door.

In the process, the flying orc's limbs knocked over furniture and damaged the walls somewhat, but Rei decided the damage was less than fighting inside the house would cause and pursued the orc.

"Whoa! Wh-what the hell!? An orc just appeared out of nowhere!"

Slightly startled by the screams coming from where the orc had been flung, Rei arrived at the scene.

There was an orc clutching its side and screaming, and a single dark elf wearing an expression of shock at the orc that had suddenly flown in.

Seeing blood on the elf's face, Rei briefly wondered if she'd been injured when the orc was blown away. But upon noticing the bloodstain was slightly dried, he felt relieved inside.

Then, standing before the dark elf with the Death Scythe in hand, Rei severed the head of the orc, which was already in a state close to critical injury from just that one blow.

The severed orc head flying through the air looked to Rei's eyes like nothing more than a soccer ball or something similar.

Blood gushed from the severed neck like a fountain.

The dark elf, witnessing this scene, was deeply impressed by the head flying through the blue sky and the fountain of blood.

The falling head made a sound like a ripe fruit hitting the ground and splattering.

"Ah... huh?"

"You okay? Looks like you almost got crushed by that orc... Sorry about that."

"Huh? Oh? Yeah."

The dark elf before Rei couldn't comprehend what had just happened before his eyes.

Had this dark elf entered the forest last night, he too would have witnessed Rei's strength with his own eyes.

Unfortunately, however, this dark elf possessed absolutely no talent for combat whatsoever.

That was why, even now, he stayed behind with the others, handling minor chores within the settlement instead of joining the fight.

While moving through the settlement to fetch boiling water and clean cloths—absolutely essential for treatment—he suffered the misfortune of encountering the orc blown away by Rei right before his eyes.

Still, it could be said the man was clearly fortunate. Had Rei not passed this way, a man with no combat skills might have encountered the orc himself.

"Um, that... was Rei, right? The one Lady Marina brought for the World Tree's treatment?"

"Yeah."

Nodding at the man's words, Rei stored the headless orc corpse into the Misty Ring.

He thought it was nicely bled out, which should make dissecting it later easier.

"The barrier disappeared earlier, but now it's deployed again. Does that mean we can assume the World Tree's treatment is finished?"

The dark elf man had intended to ask Rei, but it was the glowing orb floating beside Rei that answered.

The dark elf man stared at the rapidly flickering orb with puzzled eyes.

It was clearly an entity he had never seen before, but since Rei showed no sign of being bothered by it, he couldn't think of it as an enemy.

Even seeing it up close like this, he couldn't see it as an enemy. On the contrary, he even felt something warm in his chest.

It felt like someone who had watched over him since childhood, almost like a second parent.

(No, no, what am I thinking about this light orb?)

Rei, who had been watching the dark elf shake his head slightly in puzzlement, eventually nodded and spoke.

"Ah. I suppose we can say the treatment is complete. We've also eliminated the cause of the World Tree's weakness."

"What!? You figured out the cause of the World Tree's illness!? What was it?"

Rei lifted his gaze toward the dark elf man who asked with such intensity.

A spring sky blue enough to be called a clear May day.

Against that blue sky, a giant cicada and Set were currently engaged in fierce aerial combat, darting about the sky.

Flames, ice, water, wind... The dark elf man, captivated by the spectacle of various attacks coloring the sky, opened his mouth hesitantly.

"H-hold on a second. Could it be..."

Looking up as he asked, the dark elf man received only a silent nod from Rei in response.

That single gesture seemed to convey his understanding of the nature of the entity involved in this World Tree incident. His eyes were fixed on the giant cicada, over twenty meters long, soaring through the sky.

No, "staring" might be more accurate than "captivated."

As a Dark Elf from this settlement, the man naturally held deep feelings toward the World Tree.

Having weakened that World Tree to the point where it could no longer maintain its barrier, it was only natural he harbored resentment toward the giant cicada.

Rei watched the dark elf man for about thirty seconds before finally speaking up, realizing he couldn't waste any more time.

"Is it about time?"

"...Huh? Oh, yeah."

Realizing he'd been glaring at the giant cicada, the dark elf man hurriedly turned his gaze toward Rei at his words.

"So, do you know what the situation outside is like now?"

"Ah, yeah. From what the injured ones who came back told me—"

"Tell me."

"Well, but what I know is about when the barrier was down. The situation now that it's back up..."

"Even so, please. We have no information right now. We need any information we can get."

If Set were here, he could check the current situation from the sky. But right now, Set was locked in a fierce battle with the giant cicada and was far from being able to move freely.

That's precisely why Rei desperately wanted any information, no matter how small.

"Well, it seems the monster doesn't make any sounds like roars or screams, as if it has no will of its own. In that state, it was just relentlessly trying to destroy the barrier's protective field or something like that."

"The barrier's protective barrier?"

"Yeah. Apparently, it didn't even flinch when attacked."

"...That's his doing, right?"

Rei's gaze shifted back to the giant cicada battling Set.

They were fighting high above the ground, a little distance from the settlement, so there was no damage on the ground.

But... the forest had suffered considerable damage.

While Set's attacks were part of it, the giant cicada's assaults were undoubtedly the most devastating.

While the magic-infused ultrasonic waves seemed relatively easy for Set to evade, the trees in the forest below had no chance of avoiding the assault.

The trees were being sliced clean through by the ultrasonic blades, but Rei and the Dark Elf on the ground couldn't see that sight.

"That guy's...? That huge thing?"

"Yeah. There's no proof, but the timing's too perfect. The hordes of monsters attacking the settlement show up right when that giant cicada emerges. It's just too coincidental."

"That... well, yeah."

The Dark Elf man had no objection to Rei's words.

The timing of this incident coinciding with the World Tree's pulsation made it hard to call it mere coincidence.

"So that's how it is."

"But... how on earth could that be?! The number of monsters gathering could be hundreds... no, maybe even thousands!"

"We don't know the details yet..."

Even as he said this, Rei had a rough idea in mind.

It was probably done using ultrasound.

But he had no real certainty; it was simply Rei's guess.

"Well, I get the gist. So, where do you think I should go from here?"

"Where do you mean...?"

Seeing the dark elf look completely confused by the question, Rei let out a small sigh before speaking again.

"I'm asking where the most dangerous place is right now. Dangerous places need reinforcements, right?"

"Huh? Oh, right. Um... but I don't know much about that..."

"Don't make me repeat myself. I'm currently in a situation with zero information. In that case, even if it's somewhat outdated, some kind of guidance is better than nothing."

"According to the wounded who came back, it's the southeast direction... They said there are fewer of them over there, so it's dangerous."

Looking in the direction the dark elf man indicated, Rei nodded.

"Got it. That way, then. We're heading there. Needless to say, monsters could still be lurking in the settlement. Don't go off on your own too much."

"Ah, yeah."

Leaving the dark elf man nodding behind, Rei departed.

He continued moving, accompanied by the light orb... along the way, he dispatched several monsters like goblins and kobolds with his Death Scythe, as if they were mere roadkill, like a marauder.

When he finally reached the barrier's protective circle, just as the dark elf man had said, a battle was raging outside the settlement.

However, some details differed from the information he'd received.

He'd heard the Dark Elves were being pushed back and were in danger, but in reality, the Dark Elves fighting didn't appear to be suffering that much damage.

Of course, the Dark Elves present were fighting desperately, but even so, it looked almost like an even match. The reason for that...

"Looks like I didn't need to come here after all..."

Through the barrier's protective field, Rei's gaze fell upon Optis, reveling in the chaos as he rampaged through the swarm of monsters.

The other Dark Elves were positioned away from the monster horde, firing arrows at the monsters trying to reach the settlement or attacking them from a distance using spirit magic.

But Optis was different from them. He was engaging the monsters in close combat.

The monsters, realizing a Dark Elf was right near them, prioritized targeting her over the others.

Though most of the monsters present were merely numerous goblins, Optis relentlessly swung his staff—a blunt weapon—smashing goblin skulls one after another.

"Ho ho ho. Come on, try a little harder. There, there, over here! Oops, close. That was a close one."

Teasingly, Optis spoke as he took the lives of the monsters before him.

Despite his appearance as an old man, Optis fought on without the slightest hint of sluggishness.

Monsters that approached had their skulls crushed or hearts destroyed in the next instant.

(He's agile, isn't he? Well, maybe any Dark Elf could move like this... but no, that's not it.)

The image of countless Optis fighting flashed through Rei's mind, and he quickly shook his head to banish the vision.

He'd imagined a hellish scene where countless Optis, all together, challenged Rei to battle.

"Huh? You there, Rei? What's wrong?"

One of the dark elves shooting arrows near the barrier's perimeter noticed Rei and asked.

"I heard this place was dangerous. I came to reinforce you, but..."

"...Ah..."

The dark elf looked away, as if he had realized something.

Watching the dark elf, Rei asked him to at least get him outside the barrier's protective sphere.
