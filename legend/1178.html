After completing the absorption of the werewolf and werewolf leader's magic stones, Rei and Vihera each mounted a Set—Vihera hanging on, though—and headed toward Gilm.

The sun had already begun to dip from its zenith, signaling the arrival of afternoon.

"Took us a bit longer than expected,"

Rei's muttered words carried away on the wind.

Since he hadn't spoken the words for Vihera to hear, she, hanging onto Set's front legs, probably didn't catch them.

Without further words, they continued riding Set forward...

"GRRR!"

Set suddenly let out a cry.

Looking in the direction Set was facing, they saw a merchant caravan being attacked by a horde of monsters.

It was a horde of goblins.

Normally, goblins aren't particularly fast, so if you fled in a carriage, they couldn't catch up.

In fact, since monster swarms became frequent, some caravans arriving in Gilm had been attacked by goblins but managed to escape by driving their wagons at full speed.

Yes, normally, they should have been able to escape by running.

But... they couldn't do that because nearly half the horses pulling the caravans' wagons had already been killed.

A carriage is only a carriage because it has horses; if the horses pulling it die, it can no longer be pulled.

And the ones who killed most of the horses pulling the wagons were...

"Kobolds?"

Rei muttered.

From behind the wagons, a horde of goblins attacked the caravan, while from the front, a horde of kobolds attacked.

That was the scene unfolding before Rei's eyes.

Goblins and kobolds.

While they were similar in monster rank, that didn't mean they were cooperative with each other.

In fact, precisely because they were similar, it wasn't uncommon for them to fiercely oppose each other and kill one another.

Yet, in the scene before Rei's eyes, those two types of monsters were undoubtedly cooperating.

「Rei！」

The scene Rei and Set were witnessing must have been visible to Vihera, hanging from Set's forepaw as if it were natural. She shrieked sharply.

Though Rei's attention was momentarily stolen by the unexpected monster collaboration, Vihera's voice snapped him back to reality.

Rei and Vihera were on their way back to Gilm after completing a request to exterminate a pack of werewolves.

They wouldn't be blamed if they didn't help the caravan under attack in this situation.

But a caravan coming to the frontier town of Gilm... especially one that had heard reports of monster activity near Abelo, the closest town to Gilm, yet still came here... seeing them attacked and just letting it happen? That was no fun.

No, it's not just unsettling. Because of its position on the frontier, Gilm relies on supplies coming from outside.

Of course, most of these goods are considered luxuries and not strictly essential.

Still, the difference between having them and not having them is significant. There was no choice but to help the caravans bringing these luxury goods.

(Besides, with goblins and kobolds cooperating, it's important to get a good look at them.)

Given the various issues surrounding the hordes, there was a pressing need to understand as much detail as possible about this situation where two different hordes were cooperating.

Additionally, though it felt a bit like an afterthought given how significant it was, there was also the reason that he wanted the magic stone from the kobold leader.

For these reasons, letting the caravan under attack by both goblins and kobolds slip away was unthinkable. Rei addressed Set and Vihera.

"I intend to help that caravan. Is that okay with you?"

"Grr!"

Set nodded immediately.

Whether it was due to being created by beast magic or simply his naturally friendly nature, Set was always approachable.

While it made sense he'd want to help if his beloved Rei was in trouble, there was no particular reason now, so wanting to rescue the attacked caravan was only natural.

...Though if you asked whether he had ulterior motives, like hoping the caravan might give him something to eat, the answer would be no.

"Yeah, I don't mind either."

Vihera nodded in agreement, following Set's lead.

For Vihera, who enjoyed fighting strong opponents, goblins and kobolds weren't particularly fun foes to battle.

In fact, the power difference was so great that defeating them felt more like a chore.

Still, since the caravan was under attack, Vihera wasn't heartless enough to just leave them to die.

If it hadn't been an attack, but merely discovering a migrating horde, she would have ignored them.

"I see. Well then, let's get moving. ...Set, I'll check on that new skill too."

"GRRRRRR!"

Set growled in agreement.

These were perfect opponents to test the skills gained from the werewolf and werewolf leader's magic stones.

Enhanced Scent would likely be almost useless, but the exact effect of Poison Claws wouldn't be known until actually used.

It was also perfect for Rei to test skills like Penetrate, the newly learned Multi-Strike, and Pain Burst, which he hadn't been able to use during the fight against the werewolves and werewolf leader.

"Alright, I'll take care of the kobold pack fighting in front of the caravan. Set and Vihera, handle the goblins behind us."

Vihera, told to handle the goblins, hung from Set's front legs and looked down.

In terms of pure strength as enemies, kobolds were still stronger than goblins... but only marginally so.

Since neither fight offered a truly satisfying challenge, it made little difference to Vihera whether she faced goblins or kobolds.

"Yeah, no problem."

"Alright. Then, Set, carry me up to where the kobolds are."

"Gurrrr!"

At Rei's words, Set gave a short growl and flapped his wings, soaring above the merchant caravan.

But even while flying, the altitude remained at 100 meters.

Even at that height, there was no trace of fear on Rei's face.

This was likely due to his experience having jumped off Set countless times before.

(Probably the only one used to falling from this height is me, in this Elgin.)

To begin with, unlike Japan... Earth, where Rei once lived, flying in the sky is extremely difficult.

Therefore, it was only natural that few possessed experience descending from the sky.

Moreover, unlike Dragon Knights who glide down from the sky, this was a literal descent... no, a fall. How many could fly through the air with the effect of Sleipnir's boots?

(Ah, no. Elena also had Sleipnir's shoes, and if Yellow grows big enough, could he experience something like me? ...Well, if it's just flying, maybe I could grab onto Set's legs like Vihera did and fly?)

As he leapt off Set's back, an image flashed through Rei's mind: Elena, also possessing Sleipnir's shoes, leaping off Set alongside him.

The Crimson One and the Princess General—two figures with such nicknames—suddenly descending from the sky.

To the enemy, it must have been a nightmare-like sight.

"Set, Vihera, see you later."

Passing by Vihera, who was still clinging to Set's leg, Rei called out before his form began its descent toward the ground.

Using Sleipnir's boots several times mid-air to slow his descent, he drew the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring. Soon, Rei's form plummeted toward the swarm of kobolds attacking the adventurers guarding the merchant caravan.

"Multi-Slash!"

Just before hitting the ground, Rei swung the Death Scythe and used his newly acquired skill, Multi-Slash.

The blow caught the kobolds completely off guard. Before they could react, their heads were split cleanly in two.

Simultaneously, wounds not inflicted by the Death Scythe appeared on the split bodies.

Of course, since they were already dead from the first strike, the follow-up blow was completely meaningless.

"Waaah!?"

The kobold cried out in shock as its ally suddenly died from Rei's strike.

In response to the startled kobold, Rei activated a new skill.

"Pain Burst!"

As the skill that multiplies pain by eight activated, Rei swung his Death Scythe in a horizontal slash.

"Gyaaah!"

Several kobolds caught in the blade of the Death Scythe let out high-pitched screams.

Those with severed torsos died without even a scream, but the cobbolds with only partial wounds to their bodies suffered the full brunt of Pain Burst's effect.

"Penetrate!"

Swinging the Death Scythe horizontally, he immediately thrusts the stone-tipped end of the blade far back while activating the skill.

The wind-wrapped pommel smashed the head of a kobold that had been about to attack from behind Rei.

Within seconds... no, not even ten seconds, the sight of kobolds dying one after another must have struck fear into the kobold leader. It let out a sharp cry.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Following that cry, the kobolds temporarily halted their attack on the caravan and turned their assault toward Rei.

But even so, the disparity in power between the kobolds and Rei was vast, and corpses piled up one after another.

The moment the Death Scythe swung, the Twilight Spear thrust through the opening.

If a Twilight Spear pierced the instep and halted movement, the Death Scythe's blade would sever the neck.

Furthermore, newly acquired skills like Multi-Slash were used repeatedly, leaving many kobolds with double slash wounds from a single attack. ...Though, since most kobolds died from a single strike of the Death Scythe, few actually felt the full effect of Multi-Slash.

Even more vicious was the Pain Burst.

This vicious ability multiplied inflicted pain by eight, turning wounds that wouldn't normally warrant concern into crippling hindrances.

Other tactics included using Wind Hand to pull a tail, momentarily distracting the target before thrusting out Twilight Spear, or manipulating the terrain to shift the ground slightly, causing stumbles and slowed movement, then delivering a Death Scythe strike.

Such actions continued, and the number of kobolds rapidly dwindled.

And naturally, it wasn't just Rei fighting.

Even though they were being pushed back before Rei arrived, there were adventurers guarding the caravan. ...No, even the merchants seemed to have some skill, as they drew bows from the driver's seat and fired arrows.

Rei's arrival had instantly turned the tide, but he wasn't the type to let such an opportunity slip by.

Seeing the goblins turn their attention back to Rei, he seized the opportunity, launching attacks one after another at the goblins who showed openings.

Attacked like this, it was only natural the kobolds' numbers dwindled.

The adventurers who were originally his guards were also active near Gilm. Naturally, their skills weren't bad.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

The moment the last kobold fell, the kobold leader made its next move.

Rei's immediate judgment that it was coming was only natural, given the previous cobalt swarm's reckless, headlong charge.

But... the action the kobold leader took was to flee the scene.

He instantly turned his back on Rei and began running.

While it was true that kobolds, being a weak species among monsters, tended to act this way, given what had happened earlier, Rei was momentarily caught off guard.

And true to being a monster with a dog's face, the kobold's legs were surprisingly fast.

"Not so fast!"

Surprised by the unexpected turn of events for a moment, Rei immediately kicked off the ground.

"Hey, wait! If you chase it now..."

One of the escorting adventurers started to say, but Rei paid no heed, his gaze fixed on the back of the kobold leader ahead.

Rei's sprinting speed was certainly not inferior to the kobold leader's.

No, in fact, the distance between Rei and the kobold leader was closing rapidly before their eyes.

"Attacking me and then running away? That's way too selfish! Power Slash!"

The upgraded Power Slash struck, shattering most of the kobold leader's torso.

The cobalt leader's entrails and chunks of flesh were neatly scattered around the area.

Rei's slight frown was likely because he hadn't been able to completely absorb the recoil from that strike.

"...Phew."

Still, seeing the magic stone intact and rolling on the ground, he breathed a sigh of relief.
