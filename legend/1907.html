Early the next morning, after having savored the delicious meat of the Giganto Turtle, Rei was standing in the same spot as yesterday… outside Gilm.

But there were many differences from the day before.

Most notably, there were far more people who had come to butcher the Giganto Turtle.

It was still early morning; the sun hadn’t fully risen yet, and it was dim.

Despite these conditions, those participating in the Giganto Turtle’s butchering had gathered there looking full of enthusiasm.

That said, Rei could easily understand why this was the case.

The reason was, of course, the Giganto Turtle meat they had shared yesterday.

Since even Rei had been surprised by how delicious it was when he ate it yesterday, it wasn’t surprising that the others who had tasted it had become captivated by its incredible flavor.

Naturally, anyone who had eaten meat that delicious would tell those around them about it. Or perhaps some had even shared the meat with others.

Hearing such stories, or tasting the meat themselves… those who thought, “I definitely want to try that meat,” or “I want to eat it again”—that’s likely the reason why the number of people requesting butchering has increased.

“I’m happy that the numbers are growing, but… is everything okay with the kobolds? If we keep adding people to our side like this, I have a feeling we won’t be able to handle the kobolds anymore.”

Rei asks a nearby guild staff member.

The guild clerk smiled at Rei’s words and replied.

“That’s not a problem. Many adventurers think it’s easier to earn money by slaying kobolds themselves than by eating Giganto Turtle meat. …Though, if word gets out that we’re buying Giganto Turtle meat at high prices, who knows what might happen.”

“I don’t know if anyone would actually sell it, though.”

“Haha. That’s true. To be honest, I ate some Giganto Turtle meat with my wife and daughter yesterday, and I was amazed at how delicious it was. Once you’ve tasted that meat, selling it just doesn’t seem like an option. …That’s also why we’ve increased the number of guards today.”

Just as the guild official had said, there were definitely more guild officials surrounding the people butchering the Giganto Turtles than there had been yesterday.

I’d heard they were busy with the kobold situation and all, but the fact that they could still spare this many people really showed just how many staff the Guild has.

(Though, since they’ve diverted personnel here, things must be even busier than usual back at the Guild.)

For a moment, I imagined the resentful expressions on Lenora and Kenny’s faces, but I decided it was best not to dwell on that for now and deliberately pushed the thought aside.

“That’d be a big help. The only idiot caught yesterday was the one right in front of me, but I can’t rule out the possibility that someone among the bunch who ate that meat yesterday might start getting strange ideas.”

“Grr? Grrrr!”

Set, who had been listening to Rei and the Guild staff nearby, growled discontentedly.

It wasn’t just Rei who had eaten the Giganto Turtle meat yesterday—Set had as well.

Naturally, the taste of that meat had left a strong impression on Set as well, so it was only natural that he would get angry upon hearing that someone might try to steal it.

Set has a very strong appetite.

Given that, Set's attitude was only natural.

“Come on, calm down. Everyone knows that if anyone tries to steal the meat, Set will never forgive them.”

Rei calmed Set down by petting his head.

In fact, anyone who had heard the exchange between Rei and the guild staff earlier, followed by Set’s growl, had looked tense, so Set’s growl had likely served as the ultimate deterrent.

“Anyway, as long as you guys do your jobs properly over there, I’m grateful and have no complaints. I can leave it in your hands with peace of mind.”

If this were someone he barely knew, Rei probably wouldn’t be able to entrust him with such confidence.

However, Rei—who enjoys Daskar’s deep trust and is part of a party with Marina, a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname and the former Guild Master—is someone that Walker, the current Guild Master, cannot afford to cut ties with unless something extraordinary happens.

Given that the Giganto Turtle being dismantled is Rei’s property, it would be absolutely unacceptable for the person performing the task to steal it.

That is precisely why it can be determined that it would pose no problem for Rei to leave this area.

That said, Vihera, Byune, and…

“Come to think of it, I don’t know exactly when, but Elena and Ara said they’d come to watch the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle. If they show up, please keep them company.”

“……Huh?”

At Rei’s words, the guild staff member murmured just that one sentence after a few seconds of silence.

Elena and Ara. As a guild employee, he naturally knew exactly who Rei was referring to.

If it had been just one of them, it wouldn’t have been strange to assume it was someone else with the same name.

But when those two names were mentioned together, there was no doubt who they were… Elena, the Princess General, and her right-hand woman, Ara.

Elena wasn’t just a famous figure in Gilm; her name was known throughout the Kingdom of Mireana—no, not just the neighboring countries, but even in distant lands. The fact that she was coming was no small matter for the guild staff.

They knew Elena was staying in Gilm, and they’d heard the rumors—that she and Rei were on friendly terms, or even more than that—so they were aware of that as well.

Even knowing all that, the guild staff’s honest feeling was that they’d rather she give them a break if possible.

That said, there was no point in saying anything given the current situation, and it was a fact that they had been instructed from above to accommodate the client, Rei’s, requests as much as possible.

And above all, the reason the guild staff didn’t seem to be outright opposed to it was because they knew Elena wasn’t a tyrannical noble.

Although Gilm is ruled by Daskar, who leads the Neutral Faction, even in Gilm, nobles with arrogant attitudes are not uncommon.

There are those who refrain from such behavior when dealing with other nobles, but act arrogantly only when dealing with commoners.

Or those who behave arrogantly even toward those of a lower rank than their own family.

……Also, though it is extremely rare, there are indeed some who will pick a fight even with nobles of a higher rank than themselves.

The fact that Elena was not that kind of noble was a great relief to the guild staff who would be dealing with her.

“Understood. We will ensure proper arrangements are made for Lady Elena. Furthermore, the workers carrying out the demolition will surely be delighted to have Lady Elena visit the site in person.”

That was an undeniable fact.

The nickname “Princess General” is widely known even in the city of Gilm.

Starting with Rei, there are several others in Gilm who have nicknames, but even so, the nickname “Princess General” is known far more widely than any of them.

Furthermore, the fact that Elena possessed a beauty one might only see once in a lifetime would surely boost the morale of those working on the demolition here.

It wasn’t just men doing the dismantling; there were women there as well. Yet even those women couldn’t bring themselves to feel jealous of Elena’s beauty—they could only gaze at her in awe.

……If they hadn’t had to be careful not to offend Elena, the guild staff might have been able to simply rejoice at Rei’s words.

Still, there is no doubt that the fact it was Elena—and not some arrogant noble—who had arrived was a huge relief for the guild staff.

If an arrogant noble had shown up instead, they might have demanded, “Hand over the Giganto Turtle meat,” or “No, hand over the raw materials,” or “Hand over everything.”

After all, there was no guarantee they wouldn’t issue such unreasonable demands.

“I’ll leave it to you. Though, Elena and the others probably won’t actually arrive for a little while yet. …Oh, and I think Marina, Vihera, and Byune will be coming too, so please look after them when they arrive.”

“Huh?”

The guild staff froze because among the names Rei casually mentioned was that of Marina, the former guildmaster.

Since Vihera and Byune had been there yesterday, it wasn’t all that surprising to hear they’d be coming today.

But if Marina is among them, that’s a different story.

Although Walker is the current Guild Master, Marina, who served as Guild Master for many years, still wields significant influence.

This guild staff member had also been helped by Marina in various ways, so he was likely surprised to hear she was coming here.

Rei gave the guild staff member a light pat on the shoulder, saying, “I’ll leave the rest to you,” then headed back into the Gilm, accompanied by Set, who wanted to stay behind and keep watch for anyone trying to steal the Giganto Turtle meat.

The guild clerk watched Rei’s retreating back with a slightly resentful look… but deciding that, for now, he needed to focus on his work, he turned his attention to ensuring there were no issues with the Giganto Turtle’s butchering.

“Hmm… As expected, there are fewer people than yesterday. Though the adventurers seem happy about it.”

Rei muttered, his gaze fixed on the adventurers fighting the kobolds.

Just as he said, the increased number of kobolds being defeated meant things were looking very promising for the adventurers who were short on cash.

...In reality, those in the know had switched to butchering the Giganto Turtle because selling its meat was more profitable than defeating kobolds, so the ones here were either those who weren’t in the loop or simply those looking for a chance to get some exercise.

“Grrrr? Grr, grrr?”

“Why did you come here again today?” Set growled at Rei.

Here, where construction work is underway, they had already searched most of the areas they could access yesterday.

Given that, Set didn’t think trying to investigate anything here again today would yield any clues.

While petting Set’s head, Rei speaks up.

“It’s not exactly ‘practice makes perfect,’ but since the kobolds are actually attacking this place, we have no choice but to investigate here.”

What Rei is looking for is the magic item causing the kobolds to launch their attacks.

Whether it’s actually being done by a magic item or not, even Rei doesn’t know. No, in fact, since he searched here yesterday and found nothing, he thinks the possibility is quite low.

But since searching for magical items was partly just a way to kill time anyway, he decided to put those thoughts aside for now and just get to work.

As if sensing Rei’s thoughts, Set let out a purr as if to say, “Oh well,” and rubbed his face against Rei’s, begging for more strokes.

As he stroked Set’s head, Rei pondered where he should look for a place to search today.

(Given what happened yesterday, it’s almost certain that it’s not related to smells or sounds. But… if that’s the case, we really have no clues at all. Maybe I should just cover some ground and look around? Depending on the situation, I might find something else entirely.)

Having made that decision, Rei and Set set out to survey the construction site.

Seeing Rei and Set appear again, just like yesterday, the adventurers who had been hunting kobolds gave them complicated looks.

While they were fighting kobolds to earn money, Rei was just casually wandering around with Set in tow.

If Rei were a low-rank adventurer, it wouldn’t be surprising if they’d yelled at him to get out of the way.

But they certainly couldn’t pull a stunt like that against Rei, a man with a reputation, so they basically just went about their business without acknowledging his presence.

“Hmm… Do you think there’s anything around here?”

Rei asked Set while looking at the pile of building materials, but all he got in response was a shake of the head.

Even for someone like Set, he couldn’t very well pretend to find something if there was nothing there.

Seeing Set like that, Rei moved to another spot.

Rei was careful not to get in the way of those fighting the kobolds, so he moved around, sometimes taking a slight detour.

And so, while searching here and there…

“AAAAAAAAAAAAAAGH!”

Suddenly, a scream that sounded like a woman’s reached Rei’s ears.
