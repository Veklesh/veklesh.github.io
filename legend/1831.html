When Elena was led out of the party venue, she was dressed not as the Princess General she usually was, but as the daughter of a duke.

Though not quite as stunning as Marina, who always wears party dresses, Elena’s current appearance is certainly worthy of being called strikingly beautiful.

Though her white gown wasn’t particularly flashy… even so, anyone who saw Elena now—men and women alike—would surely find themselves captivated.

“Huh? What’s wrong? Why are you staring so much? …Um, does it not suit me?”

Seeing Rei staring intently at her, she must have felt a little uneasy. Rei shook his head at Elena’s question.

“No, I just think seeing Elena like this is a nice change.”

“I suppose so. To be honest, I’m not really comfortable in this kind of outfit. I’m actually impressed that Marina can not only wear clothes like this on a daily basis… but also move around so freely in them.”

Elena’s party dress, as she said, wasn’t particularly revealing.

It wasn’t as low-cut around the chest or back as Marina’s… yet, because Elena’s figure boasted curves every bit as feminine as Marina’s, to some observers, it was still sufficiently alluring, even if not quite as much as Marina’s party dress.

“In Marina’s case, that’s her everyday wear. It’s just a matter of getting used to it, right? …Anyway, shall we have a drink?”

Saying that, Rei took a bottle of fruit water out of the Misty Ring.

Elena took it without a word and moistened her throat with the cold fruit water.

For a brief moment, silence fell over the room.

All that could be heard were the cheerful voices of the guests enjoying the party in the grand hall.

Listening to those voices, Elena, her mouth moistened by the fruit water, spoke up.

“So? Why did you go out of your way to bring me to a place like this? I’m sure something has happened…”

It was clear from Rei’s demeanor that he hadn’t summoned her simply to talk.

That said, even if Ray had told her he’d called her out just to talk to her alone, Elena would have gladly agreed—far from getting angry.

“I’ll be blunt. …A little while ago, I encountered someone who appeared to be the Black Wolf.”

“...What?”

Elena, who just a moment ago had been looking at him with a hint of romantic expectation, instantly shifted her gaze to that of a Princess General at Rei’s words.

It was only natural. After all, the Black Wolf—who could arguably be called the strongest assassin in Anesis today—was, of all places, inside the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion.

A party was being held today to thank the servants, but that didn’t mean security at the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion had been relaxed.

No, if anything, security should have been even tighter precisely because the party was taking place.

Given that Black Wolf is said to have sneaked into such a place, there’s no way Elena could remain calm.

“Just to be clear, this isn’t some kind of joke… is it?”

“No. He was definitely there. It’s just… I’m certain of that, but the person I met might not have been the Black Wolf.”

“What do you mean?”

At Rei’s vague words, Elena furrowed her beautifully shaped brows ever so slightly.

Rei did so with the thought that if Rei said it was the Black Wolf, it was almost certainly true.

“I told you before that I met someone who looked like the Black Wolf, right? The guy I met today had the same face shape as the one I met back then, but his hair color had changed from black to silver, and his hair had gone from about back-length to shoulder-length.”

“That’s…”

Hearing the reason why Rei couldn’t be certain, Elena was also slightly puzzled.

After all, there are plenty of ways to change hair color—whether by dyeing it or using a magic item—and if the length is different, you can just cut it.

She could see that, but she wondered why someone would go to such lengths to do that.

“So, for that reason, I think it’s probably the same person, but I can’t be certain.”

“But… even if it is a disguise, why go to such lengths?”

“If I knew that, I wouldn’t be so worried. Though, I don’t think there are that many people capable of vanishing from right in front of me in an instant without leaving a trace.”

In other words, the fact that they possessed such skills—or perhaps some kind of magical item—was enough for Rei to be certain it was the same person.

“You should’ve said that first. …There can’t be that many people who could pull the wool over Rei and Set’s eyes. Though, Set wasn’t involved in tonight’s incident, was he? So, have you found out anything?”

In response to Elena’s questioning tone, Rei showed her his right hand, the numbness in which had completely vanished.

But when Elena was suddenly shown his right hand, she didn’t understand what Rei was trying to say and simply tilted her head in confusion.

“What’s wrong with your right hand?”

“I exchanged just one blow with the Black Wolf, and my right hand went numb when I took that hit.”

“……What?”

Elena had been wondering why he was holding out his right hand, but at Rei’s words, her expression turned serious.

That was only natural. There weren’t many opponents capable of landing a blow that would numb Rei’s right hand.

In other words, the Black Wolf was more skilled than she had initially anticipated.

Ever since she’d shared information about the Black Wolf with Rei, she hadn’t meant to underestimate his abilities.

But the fact remained that, as an assassin, she had always believed Rei would be overwhelmingly superior in a head-on fight.

That belief vanished instantly upon hearing that the Black Wolf had numbed Rei’s right hand.

With the kind of skill needed to numb Rei’s arm with a single strike, it wouldn’t be surprising if he were a high-ranking adventurer or someone with a nickname.

That was completely unexpected, even for Elena.

“You’re not joking, are you? Is that really true?”

“Yes. Though, considering I blocked the Black Wolf’s strike but he couldn’t block mine, I suppose I won.”

“No, I don’t think it’s a matter of winning or losing… but, I see. So, at the very least, he’s someone who can hold his own against Rei in a head-on fight.”

Even though she said it wasn’t a matter of winning or losing, the fact that Rei had the upper hand brought Elena a slight sense of relief.

This was likely less about military strength and more about the joy of seeing the man she loved come out on top.

“In any case, there’s no doubt he’s a troublesome opponent. …That’s why, from my perspective, the best choice would have been to take care of him today if possible. But due to various circumstances, I couldn’t do that. Specifically, it’s still unclear how he’s disappearing and such.”

“...That’s a problem. Is there really no way even Rei can figure out how he’s doing it?”

“Yeah. At the very least, I don’t think he had the leeway to use a magic item… but there’s a possibility it’s a magic item that doesn’t take time to activate—one that triggers the moment he decides to use it—and if it’s a skill, that makes it even more troublesome.”

In the case of magic items, there’s a possibility they’re using extremely high-performance artifacts—what you might call the legacy of an ancient magical civilization—that can be obtained in dungeons and the like. As for skills, you could say the conditions for activation vary wildly.

In Rei’s case, he generally needs to consciously focus on activating the skill and say the skill’s name out loud, but it wouldn’t be surprising if there were others who could activate skills without doing that.

“If it’s a magic item, I’d also like to seize it after defeating the Black Wolf, if possible.”

Taking magic items from defeated opponents is practically tantamount to robbery.

But for Rei, known as the “Bandit Eater,” taking from those who posed a threat to him carried absolutely no sense of guilt.

Vanishing without a trace… specifically, vanishing from the scene via teleportation or some other method so that neither Rei nor Set could sense any trace of them—that was a very appealing magical item to Rei as well.

“Knowing Rei, I figured you’d say that.”

Elena, too, had known Rei for a long time.

Precisely because of that, she knew exactly what kind of magic items Rei preferred.

“Anyway…”

Rei understood what Rei was getting at, but given the current situation, there was something more important she needed to ask.

“Since the Black Wolf has infiltrated, do you think we should beef up our security?”

“Hmm… I’m not sure. To be honest, as far as I know, the only ones who can take on the Black Wolves are Elena and Philma. Of course, I don’t know the full extent of the Duke of Kerebel’s forces, so there might be others capable of fighting them.”

Elena and Philma are exceptionally powerful assets even among the forces of the Duke of Kerebel’s household.

They possess the kind of power that, like Rei, can overcome sheer numbers with superior quality.

I can’t say for certain that there aren’t other forces like that within the Duke of Kerebel’s household.

That said, since I doubt Elena would answer honestly if I asked her such a thing, there’s probably no point in asking.

“Unfortunately, for now, it’s just me and Philma.”

Rei doesn’t know if what she’s saying is true. She doesn’t know… but even if it’s a lie, Rei doesn’t mind.

After all, Elena is not only Rei’s comrade but also the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel and a symbol of the Noble Faction as the “Princess General.”

In fact, if she were to blurt out things that should be kept secret, that would be cause for concern in itself.

“I see.”

That’s why Rei replied with just that brief remark.

“Hmm. …Still, for now, I suppose I’ll have to tell Father about the Black Wolf breaking into our estate. Whether he can handle it is another matter, though.”

“Ah… well, I suppose so. Since he’s the head of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, it’s only natural that he should be informed of such matters.”

But the fact that there’s little chance of dealing with a Black Wolf even if it invades his own mansion would be a blow to Liberte as well.

“I’d like to stay here a little longer and relax with Rei, but considering the Black Wolf situation, that’s not really an option. …Let’s head back.”

“Yeah.”

Rei had no particular objection to Elena’s announcement that they were returning to the party venue.

After all, their original purpose for coming here was solely to inform everyone that the Black Wolf had appeared at the mansion.

Now that they’d done that, it only made sense to return to the party as soon as possible.

That said, for Rei, it felt a bit of a shame to end his time with Elena, who was dressed in her party gown.

Still, considering the matter of the Black Wolf, he certainly couldn’t be selfish.

(Well, somehow… just a hunch, really, but I didn’t think that Black Wolf would go after anyone other than me.)

Rei didn’t know much about this “Black Wolf” either.

In fact, he’d only met him twice in person, and to make matters worse, the Black Wolf he’d seen today had different hair color and length compared to the first time, so he couldn’t be certain they were the same person.

……I didn’t think there were many people out there who possessed that much power—and who could vanish from in front of Rei without leaving a trace, whether through a skill, a magic item, or something else entirely.

“The moon is beautiful.”

On the way back to the party venue, Elena suddenly stopped and murmured as she looked out the window from the hallway.

Hearing her words, Rei also looked out the window.

It was partly because of the party, but even on ordinary days, the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion was never without the light provided by magical items.

While not every corner of the vast Duke of Kerebel’s mansion is illuminated, an unimaginable number of magical lighting items still light up the surroundings.

Such a feat is likely only possible thanks to the immense wealth of the Duke of Kerebel’s family.

However, even with such a vast number of magical lighting items, they cannot completely obscure the brilliant light of the moon floating in the night sky.

“That’s true.”

Rei, too, stopped beside Elena and agreed with those words as he gazed at the moon.

When one thinks of a winter night, the impression is usually of the moon hidden behind thick clouds, but the night sky Rei and Elena were looking up at now was completely cloudless, allowing them to clearly see the moon’s beautiful form.

Rei and Elena gazed at the moon in the night sky together for a few minutes, but realizing they needed to report the information regarding the Black Wolf, they set off once more toward the party venue.
