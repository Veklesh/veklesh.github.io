"...I see. So the goal was to avenge His Highness Kabajid."

Sitting on the sofa in the office of the lord's mansion, Daskar listened to Rei and Vihera's explanation and murmured thoughtfully.

His tone carried a bitter edge, as if it were only natural.

He knew all too well the reasons one might seek vengeance.

After all, he had intervened in last year's civil war in the Bestir Empire, siding with the aristocratic faction and joining forces with Vihera.

And he understood that Rei was the primary reason why Kabajid had been driven to the point of death, having been the most active figure in the civil war.

Even so, he hadn't expected such a massive uproar to be stirred up for revenge, and above all...

"His Highness Kabajido possessed sufficient qualities to be a leader."

"Indeed. Many admired my brother. His substantial achievements certainly played a significant role."

Daskar nodded thoughtfully as Vihera handed her sandwich over to Byune.

"So, in the end, most of the materials, documents, and magic items left behind by that alchemist were destroyed, right?"

Rei, who was asked while being looked at, nodded immediately.

"Yes. I think there are still some documents and such left in the room beneath Paul's magic item shop... but I suspect the truly important stuff was taken out by Zuboz."

And ultimately, they were destroyed in the explosion of the hut.

Whether it was a precaution in case they lost, or if there was some other intention behind it, even Rei couldn't say.

Still, the fact remained that the valuable documents and magic items Zuboz had possessed were gone, and it was a tremendous loss for Rei too.

(Well, I guess I was lucky to survive that explosion unscathed. Wait, does having bruises count as unscathed?)

Rei found himself thinking such things.

The fragments of the hut raining down from the explosion didn't slice or pierce Rei thanks to the Dragon Robe.

But even so, the shock transmitted through the Dragon Robe was unavoidable.

As a result, while Rei suffered no direct wounds, his entire body was covered in bruises.

Even so, the fact that the pain had largely subsided after just one night's sleep was likely due to Rei's specially engineered body.

An ordinary human would undoubtedly have been moaning in pain for several days.

"I've heard about that from Ranga. ...So, I'm afraid we'll need to examine the magic items and materials that Zoboz had on him ourselves before handing them over to you."

"I suppose that can't be helped."

Daskar showed a hint of surprise at Rei's straightforward nod.

"Are you sure? I thought you'd put up a bit more of a fight."

"Yes. That magic item Zuboz used seems quite dangerous. This time it was only Zuboz, but if other materials remain, there's no guarantee someone else won't create similar magic items. It's better to have countermeasures in place."

Hearing Rei's words, Daskar breathed a sigh of relief.

He had been fairly certain it would be fine, but a faint worry had lingered that Ray might still insist on the immediate return of the magic items, materials, and documents.

Daskar knew full well how strongly Rei clung to magic items.

And the magic items left behind by Zuboz would undoubtedly pique Rei's interest.

If that were the case, he would have had to negotiate persistently.

From Daskar's position as the Marquis of Larks, he could have resorted to forceful means.

But Daskar didn't want to resort to harsh measures against Rei, with whom he maintained a friendly relationship.

...Besides, even if he tried to take hardline measures, Rei was superior in pure military strength.

Furthermore, if such a move put Rei at a disadvantage, it was clear he would promptly mount Set and leave Gilm.

That would significantly reduce Gilm's military strength.

Considering Set's influence within Gilm, it could even spark a riot if handled poorly.

(...Gilm really leans on Rei and Set, doesn't it?)

He suddenly thought that.

In reality, Rei and Set's presence within Gilm was something that could never be underestimated.

Considering that, it was incredibly fortunate that Rei had willingly entrusted what could be called Zubo's legacy to them.

"Sorry about that. I appreciate it."

"No, don't mention it. Believe it or not, I'm quite attached to Gilm myself."

"Oh, you say 'as it appears' yourself, do you?"

Vihera, who had been listening to Rei and Daskar's conversation, spoke with a small smile.

Whether they found the remark amusing or not, Elena and even Ara had smiles playing on their lips.

But for Rei, who was being laughed at, the remark seemed to grate on him. He reached for the sandwich on his plate, looking a bit sulky.

"Heh heh."

Seeing Rei like this, Vihera smiled happily.

It was clear she enjoyed teasing someone she liked.

"Hmm, ahem. Anyway, we're saved this time. It's a shame we couldn't capture the alchemist..."

Daskar's gaze shifted toward Rei for an instant.

Rei, who had been sulking, reacted to that gaze and was about to say something when Daskar spoke.

"From what I've heard, it seems capturing him alive was simply impossible."

"Yes. He was already out of control, unconscious."

"I received the report. Apparently, he tried to devour his own comrades."

As Daskar murmured these words with deep feeling, images flashed through Rei's mind: Dias, whose right arm had been torn off, and Paul, who would have had his head torn off if Rei hadn't intervened.

It was true it had gone berserk, but in the end, Zuboz had likely gained a new intelligence while still rampaging.

Had they managed to capture it successfully back then, it could have been a source of many new discoveries.

But as he thought about that moment, Rei immediately dismissed the idea internally.

(Even if such a thing gained intelligence, it would undoubtedly lead to nothing good. In that case, I suppose I should be glad I managed to take it down...)

That intelligence might have been the result of Zuboz's hatred toward him running wild, but if that was the case, it made him even more certain it was better not to have captured it alive.

He had a vague, almost instinctive feeling that if he'd carelessly captured it alive, some tragedy would undoubtedly have followed later.

"Did you say Zuboz? It's a shame we couldn't capture that one, but we managed to take the other three properly instead. Especially Paul—he was surprisingly cooperative with us."

"That man?"

To Rei, Paul was someone whose thoughts were completely unfathomable.

He was an agent of the Bestir Empire, a man who had survived the previous spy hunt unscathed.

Considering Paul's position, it wasn't entirely surprising he'd cooperate with Zuboz.

But why, right here and now, was he being cooperative with the guards and knights who had captured him?

(It's definitely not just a simple case of calculating his gains and losses and switching sides...)

Still, while Ray couldn't fathom what Paul was thinking to act this way, it was undeniably beneficial for Gilm.

"What about the other two?"

At the question that came from Rei's mouth, Daskar's expression tightened for just an instant.

It was a truly fleeting change in expression, one that only someone who had dealt with Daskar to some extent, or someone exceptionally sharp, would notice.

Rei had some dealings with Daskar and knew his temperament. That's precisely why he noticed the shift. Elena and Vihera were sharp-witted to begin with.

Byune was a thief, and Ara, serving Elena, also possessed keen observational skills.

As a result, nearly everyone present noticed that Daskar's expression had shifted, however slightly. Yet no one pointed it out.

They assumed that since Daskar, the lord of Gilm, chose not to speak, he must have a good reason.

Daskar knew he'd been noticed, but he continued speaking without any particular reaction.

(Who would have thought a general who fled the Kingdom of Rejsea would be in the slums of Gilm? Rumor had it he was caught up in a power struggle, and it was less a voluntary departure and more like he was half-expelled.)

While conversing with Rei and the others, Daskar thought about Dias.

He was a man who had been practically driven out of his homeland, but having served as a general, he was undoubtedly capable.

Daskar wanted Dias as his subordinate, but given his involvement in this matter, it wasn't something he could easily arrange. More importantly, Dias himself didn't seem to desire that path.

(He couldn't be sent to the front lines after losing his right arm, but he could still command troops.)

Daskar pondered how he could bring Dias under his command.

"Anyway, you did well. Elena-sama only arrived yesterday, and to suddenly involve her in such a commotion... I'm terribly sorry."

"No, don't worry about it. I didn't expect things to turn out like this either, but getting to act alongside Rei wasn't a bad thing at all."

"Hearing you say that is a relief. ...So, how long will Lady Elena be staying in Gilm? Of course, as far as I'm concerned, she's welcome to stay as long as she likes."

Having Elena, a symbol of the noble faction, stay in Gilm long-term was certainly not a bad choice for Daskar, the central figure of the neutral faction.

But at Daskar's words, Elena opened her mouth with a slightly apologetic expression.

"I'm sorry, but I must return before summer. Unfortunately, it won't be for very long."

"...No, I think until summer is plenty."

Daskar had meant a long time as a few weeks... ideally around a month.

But the words that came out of Elena's mouth were "until summer."

This made Daskar completely forget about how he was going to persuade Dias, and he blurted out his true thoughts without thinking.

"Well, all of this is thanks to the fact that friendly forces toward the Mireana Kingdom have emerged within the Bestir Empire. While we remain wary of the Bestir Empire, things are still considerably easier than last year."

"Oh? Then perhaps you should thank me? How about this as a thank you—spending some passionate time alone with Rei?"

As Vihera said this, glancing sideways at Rei, it was Ara, not Elena, who twitched her cheeks.

Since Elena said nothing, she didn't act, but if anyone were to make a move, she wanted to be the first...

But... Ara was completely taken aback by the words that came next from Elena's mouth.

"Hmm, I see. Then you should enjoy your passionate time to your heart's content tomorrow or so."

"...Huh? Elena-sama!?"

She never expected Elena to accept it so readily. A startled gasp escaped Ara's lips.

"Hehe, what's wrong, Ara? Did something funny happen?"

"No, I mean... um, is that really okay?"

As they spoke, Ara seemed to calm down a bit, cautiously glancing at Elena.

What met her gaze was Elena's beautiful smile.

"Ara, aren't you misunderstanding something?"

"Misunderstanding...?"

"Yes. What kind of time do you think Vihera meant by 'passionate time'?"

"What kind... well..."

At Elena's words, Ara gently averted her gaze.

And where her gaze had fallen was upon Vihera, the one who had spoken those words about the 'hot time'.

Her radiant smile seemed to suggest that the 'hot time' was exactly what Ara had imagined.

"Oh? What's wrong? I don't know what you imagined, but I'm sure it wasn't too far off, right?"

"Huh!?"

Ara voiced her surprise again, but soon, perhaps feeling sorry for her,

Elena sighed softly before speaking.

"Vihera, cut it out. ...Ara, that Vihera over there is what you'd call a battle maniac. So, what that 'hot time' means... you get it, right?"

Muttering "battle maniac" under her breath, Ara finally understood she'd been teased.
