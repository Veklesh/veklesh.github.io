The fact that goblin meat could be eaten—and that it had become somewhat palatable—became quite a famous story in Gilm.

The cooks, having negotiated with Mayo and successfully obtained the goblin meat, began creating goblin dishes as their curiosity dictated and put them up for sale.

Regardless of whether one lived in Gilm or not, it was common knowledge in this world that goblin meat was extremely unpalatable.

For that very reason, those who first heard about goblin meat dishes bought them with some trepidation… but when they realized it was surprisingly tasty, they were astonished.

That said, not every cook succeeded.

Some, when cooking it with certain ingredients, ended up with a dish that was even worse than ordinary goblin meat...

It was also discovered that, generally speaking, the goblin meat sold by Mayo would turn out unpalatable if it underwent a long simmering process.

Of course, this was specifically due to the fact that it had been processed using Gamelion’s innards, and there was a possibility that if it could be made tasty through other methods, it might even be delicious in stews…

Unfortunately—or perhaps inevitably—Mayo has not made public any methods for preparing goblin meat to make it taste good.

After all, what Mayo sells is goblin meat that they have processed themselves, and the chefs who buy it have no idea what kind of processing it has undergone.

Naturally, some people would like to know, but from Mayo’s perspective, it took years of research to finally get to this point.

There are still various limitations—such as the fact that it won’t work without the innards of a Gamelion that appear in the fall, or that if it’s simmered for too long, the meat reverts to its original, unpalatable state—so it’s not possible to enjoy goblin meat deliciously at any time.

However, the fact that he managed to make goblin meat delicious is, without a doubt, a feat worthy of being remembered in history—and that’s no joke.

For that very reason, naturally, there are those who think, “If I’m lucky…”

“Guh!”

An adventurer hired by someone who had such thoughts took a blow from Rei and collapsed to the ground.

“I never thought there’d be an adventurer from Gilm who’d come up with such a foolish idea.”

“Don’t say that. Besides, these adventurers aren’t true Gilm adventurers. They’re probably just people who came to Gilm looking for work on the expansion project.”

Vihera, who had just knocked out another adventurer standing next to Rei, said in a slightly consoling tone.

“Well, with power like this… …But this is turning into a hassle.”

Various people are currently probing Percy’s Tool Shop, trying to uncover the secret to cooking goblin meat deliciously.

Rei had heard from Mayo that this included not only direct acts of violence like this incident, but also leaks from the shop’s business partners, and recently, there had been sightings of unfamiliar, suspicious figures lurking around the shop.

“It’s just goblin meat.”

Some might think that, but goblins aren’t just found in the frontier—they’re everywhere.

If one could figure out how to make that goblin meat taste good, it would bring in enormous profits.

……Given that, it’s not surprising that people would want to get their hands on it.

In reality, it requires the use of Gamelion’s innards—an ingredient available only in a few places, including Gilm—so it’s not as though one can enjoy delicious goblin meat anytime, anywhere.

But for now, Mayo has no intention of revealing that.

That’s only natural, considering the funds invested in this research, and Mayo, of course, has his own desire for glory.

For Rei, this wasn’t something he particularly cared about, so he hadn’t made an issue of it.

Furthermore, even though he has a desire for recognition, Mayo has no intention of keeping the research on how to make goblin meat taste delicious all to himself.

After all, it was Rei who first approached Mayo with the idea of researching how to make goblin meat taste good, and he’s even been given large quantities of goblin meat and spices that would normally be quite expensive.

Considering those circumstances, Mayo had no choice but to list Rei as a co-researcher.

Rei himself wasn’t particularly concerned about that, so honestly, it didn’t matter to him either way.

“Right. But while Rei is at Elena’s house, I’ll keep an eye on things here, so you don’t need to worry. If you want, you could even hire some guards from Percy’s Tool Shop.”

“Hire someone, huh? But is there anyone who’d take on a bodyguard job at this time of year?”

Winter is basically a long break for adventurers.

For Rei, it probably feels like summer or winter break.

Incidentally, the length of winter break varies greatly depending on where you live; in some places, it’s a week or more shorter than at the high school Rei attended, so some people might not even consider winter a long vacation… but for Rei, who lived in the Tohoku region, winter was definitely a long vacation.

Of course, since the winter break is long, the summer break is correspondingly short.

In any case, an adventurer accepting a bodyguard request during such a long break... while it might be understandable for an adventurer with insufficient funds to get through the winter, it was hard to imagine an adventurer who had properly prepared for that taking on such a job.

Of course, adventurers don’t spend the entire winter drinking, partying, and living a dissolute life.

It wasn’t uncommon for them to train their bodies to become stronger in preparation for activities starting in the spring.

However, many still tend to think of it as something to do after the new year—or rather, once spring is approaching—so doing it at this time of year… and right at the start of winter… is a bit difficult.

“In that case, would it be best to ask Vihera, Marina, or Byune?”

“Oh, I’m expensive, you know?”

Vihera said this jokingly, but it was only natural that hiring someone as skilled as her would be expensive.

“Ah… just put it on my tab for now. Knowing Vihera, she’s not just after the money, is she?”

If she could be hired with money, that would be a simple matter for Rei.

But knowing Vihera’s personality, it was only natural to Rei that this wasn’t the case.

(Since it’s what Vihera wants, it’s probably something like a mock battle or something like that.)

That’s what Rei was thinking, but the next words to come out of Vihera’s mouth made him freeze in his tracks.

“Then, once we get back from the Duchy of Kerebel, let’s go on a date. That’s fine, right?”

“……”

Caught off guard by Vihera’s unexpected words, Rei couldn’t bring himself to reply right away.

Seeing Rei like that, Vihera flashed a smug smile.

...As she smiled, her cheeks flushed faintly.

Even when she invited him to mock battles or paraded in front of crowds wearing skimpy outfits like a prostitute or a dancer, Vihera didn’t feel that embarrassed.

But even for someone like Vihera, asking Rei—the man she loves—out on a date must make her feel a bit shy after all.

Still, acting like a woman her age and trying to appear composed, Vihera asks Rei.

“What do you think? Don’t you think the fee is a bit low?”

“...In a way, it might end up costing me dearly.”

Despite responding that way to Vihera’s demeanor, Rei accepts her invitation to go on a date.

“Alright. So, once we get back, shall we go out somewhere? As for where… should I figure that out?”

“I’d be happy if you did.”

Vihera said this with a beaming smile, but while Rei smiled outwardly, he was inwardly confused.

Of course, it wasn’t as if he’d never been on a date before.

He hadn’t had such opportunities while in Japan, but since coming to Elgin, he’d found himself interacting more often with women who were—well, more beautiful than anything he’d ever seen on TV back in Japan… or rather, on Earth—and he’d become fairly accustomed to such situations.

That said, Rei was still at a loss as to where they should go on a date.

(A movie theater? Nah, probably not. A library… Would Vihera enjoy reading books at a library? A restaurant… Ah, maybe a diner with a good selection of sweet desserts? Or maybe just window shopping?)

He can think of a few places, but the sources of Rei’s ideas are, as you might expect, things like manga and anime.

Of course, most of that wouldn’t work here in Elgin.

“I’ll think of something for now.”

“Really?”

Vihera smiled and nodded.

As they were talking near the back entrance of Percy’s Tool Shop, the door suddenly opened.

“Good work, you two. I’ve prepared some hot soup, so please help yourselves.”

A clerk from Percy’s Tool Shop appeared, holding a bowl of soup that looked delicious.

Rei and the others were happy that he’d brought them soup, thinking they must be cold. They were happy, but…

“Um, about the ingredients in this soup—the vegetables aside, isn’t that meat… is that goblin meat by any chance?”

“Yes, that’s right.”

At Rei’s words, the female clerk in her mid-twenties smiled and nodded.

But upon seeing this, Rei wore a slightly disgusted expression and spoke up.

“I thought I heard that processed goblin meat isn’t suitable for dishes that require long simmering, like soup?”

At Rei’s words, the woman flashed a delighted smile.

“I’m so glad you asked! Actually, this soup is made a little differently than usual. Though, it’s not that difficult, really.”

Handing a bowl of soup and a spoon to Rei and Vihera, the woman continued her explanation.

“The goblin meat we processed here can be cooked into stir-fries and the like without any problems—it tastes delicious. But when used as an ingredient in soup, it just didn’t taste right. …So, all we had to do was change our approach!”

As she said this, she pointed at Rei, who, swept up by her enthusiasm, brought a piece of goblin meat from the soup to his mouth.

He intended to spit it out immediately if it tasted bad. But...

“Is it good?”

“That’s right. It used to taste bad when simmered in soup, but if you stir-fry it first and then add it as an ingredient to the soup, it’s perfectly edible! …Well, in that case, the soup won’t have the meat’s umami flavor, though…”

“Ah… I see. So you used the goblin meat purely as an ingredient.”

“Yes.”

“Columbus’s egg?” Rei thought for a moment, but he didn’t voice it.

To begin with, he didn’t even know the meaning of the phrase “Columbus’s egg” in this world, and from Rei’s perspective—someone who rarely cooked—it was a surprising idea. But he figured that to someone who actually cooked, it was probably just a perfectly ordinary cooking method.

In fact, though Rei didn’t know it, simmering ingredients for a long time just to extract the broth, then adding ingredients meant to be eaten once the broth has been extracted—this is a common cooking method in Japan, and it’s the same here in Elgin.

Of course, it was a method that required an extra step or two, but any cook would be familiar with it.

“So, does that mean you’ve overcome the drawback of not being able to use goblin meat in the soup?”

“Well… I’m not sure. After all, there’s a big difference between simply adding it as an ingredient and actually being able to simmer it thoroughly. I wouldn’t say we’ve truly overcome it. However, with our current processing method, there doesn’t seem to be any other way.”

It’s only recently that we’ve been able to make goblin meat reasonably tasty by using Gamelion innards.

If we’d known that from the start, it wouldn’t have been impossible to procure large quantities of Gamelion innards… but in any case, the supply of Gamelion innards is currently very limited.

Given that, there simply aren’t enough innards to spare for trial and error.

“Besides the method we found this time, are you going to look for other ways to make goblin meat taste good?”

“Of course. As things stand now, we can only eat goblin meat deliciously in this area… and only during this season. Well, it’s still better than before, though.”

Even so, many people try to break into Percy’s Tool Shop in search of the secret to eating goblin meat.

Rei couldn’t help but wonder: if the methods for eating goblin meat are so limited, wouldn’t it be better to make them public? But the fact that Mayo doesn’t do so suggests he must have some reason.

(I did hear that if you use spices, it’s not exactly inedible… but maybe that’s out of the question due to cost issues?)

With those thoughts in mind, Rei continued to guard Percy’s Tool Shop for the time being.
