“...For a treasure trove, there weren’t many magic items, were there?”

On the ship’s deck, Rei speaks to the man in front of him—the pirate crew’s captain.

The fact that all Rei can see is the back of the man’s head and his back is likely because the man has his back turned to her.

However, it’s not as if he’s simply standing there doing nothing, forcing the man to face him.

Rei is clutching the Death Scythe in his hand, its blade pressed against the man’s jaw from behind. It’s clear that if Rei were to move his hand even slightly, the man’s neck would be severed in an instant.

In such a situation, with Rei behind him voicing his complaints, the man naturally feels as if he’s on the brink of death.

In fact, his knees are trembling, his face is contorted, yet he is somehow managing to endure the terror.

He was absolutely certain that if he were to make any sudden, large movement for any reason, his head would be severed in an instant.

……It was only natural to think that way, having seen Rei effortlessly split a boulder larger than a man in two with the Death Scythe, through which he had channeled his magic as a demonstration before boarding the ship.

Since he couldn’t possibly believe his own neck was harder than a rock, the man had no choice but to rely on his subordinates to keep the ship steady.

“So, in the end, the biggest haul from this trip was that magic item that creates the smoke screen?”

Rei’s dissatisfaction boiled down to the fact that, in the end, they had barely managed to acquire any magic items.

If this had been an ordinary pirate, he wouldn’t have expected much.

But in this pirate’s case, the man Rei had a Death Scythe pointed at tried to use a magic item.

In other words, while they might not use them on a daily basis, they likely use magic items fairly frequently.

With that expectation in mind, they searched the place where the pirates had hidden their treasure, but all they found was ordinary loot... a massive pile of silver coins, a fair amount of gold coins, and a decent number of platinum coins. The rest was mostly gems.

There was one slightly unusual item that could be called a treasure sword, but it wasn’t a Magic Sword; it was merely a decorative longsword with gems studded into its scabbard and hilt.

It might have some value as a work of art, but it was certainly not something that could be used practically.

As for magic items, they were things like items that provided light or generated wind—though the wind was only strong enough to disperse odors—and were not the kind of magic items Rei was hoping for.

For that reason, Rei was unable to obtain the kind of magic item he had in mind and was feeling dissatisfied.

(Could it be that the usable magic items are hidden somewhere else?)

He wasn’t entirely without that thought, but considering the man’s interactions with the pirates, would he really risk his own life and those of his men just to protect a magic item? When he thought about it, that seemed like an unthinkable choice.

If this were an ordinary—or rather, a highly self-serving—bandit, that possibility might not have been entirely out of the question, but the man standing before Rei now was a pirate who cared deeply for his crew.

Given that, it was simply inconceivable that he would do such a thing in this situation.

Since it was certain they would end up as slaves, taking such a dangerous risk and endangering his own life and those of his men would be nothing but a bad move.

...Of course, it’s not entirely impossible that he’s actually acting convincingly to deceive Rei... but even so, from what Rei could see, that didn’t seem to be the case.

“Grrrrr!”

Hearing that sound, Set approaches the ship, growling.

However, Set wasn’t the only one approaching. Clinging to his forepaw was a human figure.

When he reached the deck, he let go of Set’s leg and landed on the deck.

The pirates on deck couldn’t help but fix their gaze on the figure who had just revealed himself.

Rei, with a Death Scythe blade pressed against her throat, was no exception.

The woman who appeared on the deck made such a strong impression on everyone around her.

“Rei, weren’t you supposed to go buy fishing gear or fish? I was wondering what was going on when Set suddenly came to pick you up.”

Though she complained with her words, her eyes were smiling, as if to say, “You’re up to something interesting, aren’t you?”

“Vihera is here? What about the others?”

“Bistol and Byune are still fishing, but Elena and Marina came over here with me. …Since there wasn’t a Set Cage, they had to hold onto Set’s leg like I did.”

“Oh, sorry. I should’ve had them bring a Set Cage.”

It’s not like the Set Cage can’t be used just because Rei isn’t here.

It’s usually stored in the Misty Ring, so it’s true that they can’t use it unless Rei takes it out.

In any case, since we sent Set off from the island with a letter, if we’d given him the Set Cage then, Elena and the others should have been able to come here without any trouble.

“That’s true. You should apologize to Rei later. …So? Who’s standing in front of Rei?”

“Ah. It’s the guy who was leading these pirates.”

“Hmm. …For Rei, that’s a pretty lenient response.”

At those words, the pirates—who had been gazing in awe at Vihera, who could easily be mistaken for a prostitute or a dancer—couldn’t help but scream inwardly, “What do you mean, ‘soft’!?”

But considering the acts Rei had committed as a bandit up until now, one could say his response was actually quite gentle.

If this pirate crew had been as brutal and ruthless as... well, the "Pure White" band of thieves that Rei had slaughtered in Sabrusta, he wouldn’t have bothered with the leisurely act of selling them off as slaves; they would all have been killed by his own hand.

...Though, perhaps because these pirates hadn’t witnessed Rei acting that way before, they all silently rolled their eyes at Vihera’s words.

“So? If you’ve already caught them, why did you call us? Could it be that there are still pirates out there who haven’t been caught yet?”

“No, that’s not it. Of course, there’s no doubt there are still pirates out there who haven’t been caught yet.”

In the end, Rei didn’t manage to capture every single pirate on that island.

To be precise, he could have caught them if he’d wanted to.

But he didn’t think it was worth the trouble, so he ended up leaving them on the island.

With no boats and monsters lurking in the surrounding waters, swimming to get around would be difficult.

It was essentially a form of exile, but he judged that as long as they lived within the confines of that island, there wouldn’t be any immediate problems.

The pirates captured by Rei also decided it would be better to stay on the island and live there, considering that they might end up as slaves like him—or, at worst, executed—so they didn’t say anything further.

(Well, there’s a chance they could build a boat on their own and escape the island… but if it’s just a raft, they’d probably get attacked by monsters and die right away.)

With that thought in mind, Rei turned his gaze toward the pirates working on various tasks on the deck.

Rei understood what the pirates were after, but since he wasn’t exactly desperate for money, the honest truth was that he just didn’t want to go through the trouble of dealing with them…

“Then why?”

“Even if we leave these pirates at that fishing village, we’ll need some fighting power in case of an emergency, right? I need to go to a nearby town, bring back the person in charge, and have them take these guys off our hands.”

At Rei’s words, Vihera glanced at the pirates.

Misunderstanding the situation, a few of the pirates who caught Vihera’s gaze even waved happily at her.

In reality, she was just checking to see if there would be anyone for Vihera to enjoy fighting against should the pirates cause trouble.

(There’s definitely someone who’s got the wrong idea.)

For some reason, feeling a sense of pity for the pirates at Vihera’s actions, Rei looked away from them and turned his gaze to the man he was holding the Death Scythe blade to.

Even during their exchange so far, the man hadn’t made any sudden movements, and his head remained firmly attached to his body.

Although the ship rocks with the waves, Rei adjusts his position to maintain a distance where the blade of the Death Scythe is just barely touching—or not quite touching—the man’s neck.

...The pirates had no way of understanding that what Rei was doing so casually was, in fact, only possible thanks to his extraordinary skill and physical prowess.

The man with the Death Scythe blade pressed against him also had absolutely no idea just how much skill was required to perform such a feat.

All he knew was that a woman named Vihera—a woman who truly belonged under the moonlight rather than the sun—had arrived.

And that his chances of survival had just become even slimmer.

Of course, he never seriously believed he could escape from Rei.

But… just maybe—and I mean just maybe—the chance of escaping wasn’t zero.

Yet with Vihera’s arrival, that possibility had become even slimmer.

(And besides...)

The man kept his face still, moving only his eyes to look toward the side of the ship.

There, the griffin Set was flying slowly, flapping its wings.

(So they can fly this slowly, too? ...Seriously, why on earth would an adventurer who has a griffin as a familiar be in such a backwater?)

Yes, under normal circumstances, it would be unthinkable for an adventurer with a nickname to come to such a backwater.

In fact, that was precisely why the men had decided to make that deserted island their base.

But… as a result, it was Rei who showed up.

For the men, it was nothing short of the worst possible outcome.

“What’s wrong? You seem to be staring at Set. Is something bothering you?”

“No, nothing. I just didn’t expect to be able to see a griffin this close up. I’m just a little surprised.”

“...Well, I suppose so.”

Rei didn’t believe the man’s words were entirely true.

However, based on his past experience, Rei understood that it wasn’t unusual for someone to be captivated by the sight of Set.

And Rei could tell that even if the man tried to do something in this situation, it would end up being pointless.

Given that, he decided there was no need to confront the man right here and now…

“Rei, I can see it now.”

At Vihera’s words, Rei turned her gaze toward the land.

Just as Vihera had said, the village was already visible in the distance.

And near the shore, he could see several villagers.

Perhaps they had gathered out of fear that the sudden approach of the ship meant another pirate attack.

Thinking this, Rei turned her gaze back to Set.

“Set, go tell Elena and the others that we’ve taken care of things over here. …Well, even if you don’t tell them, they’ll probably figure it out naturally once you head over from the ship.”

“Grr? Grrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl and flew toward the village.

Watching Set, Rei then turned to the man he was holding the Death Scythe against and spoke.

“Now then, I’m going to have you lot head to that village… You’re okay with traveling by small boat, right?”

This ship is equipped with small boats for navigating shallow waters.

Rei had already confirmed this, so he wasn’t worried.

What worries him is whether the pirates, with their hands and feet bound, will be able to safely maneuver the small boat and make it to the village without capsizing...

Under normal circumstances, it might be fine, but many of the pirates are currently bound hand and foot.

However, in response to Rei’s words, the man actually smiled with a touch of pride and spoke up.

“That won’t be a problem. We’re skilled sailors, after all.”

“...I see.”

If they’re that confident in their skills, wouldn’t it have been better for them to stick to trading or fishing instead of being pirates?

That’s what Rei thought, but now that they’d already been captured, there was no point in saying anything.

(If only they hadn’t gone and threatened that village, there might have been a way to work things out.)

Rei looked at the man and pitied his bad timing, but the fact remained that if the pirates hadn’t approached that village in the first place, Rei wouldn’t have had to go after them.

Given that, the pirates were truly unlucky… that was the only conclusion he could draw.

“Anyway, I see you’ll be able to make it to that village safely. The rest depends on what happens once you get there. …I hope you run into a decent slave trader.”

At Rei’s words, the man wore an expression as if he’d just swallowed a bitter pill.
