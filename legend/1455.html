While Rei and the Crimson Wings, along with Elena and Ara, were enjoying a pleasant evening at Marina's house... Ilse was feeling the exact opposite.

The inn where Ilse had taken a room was the type that didn't serve meals unless you ate in the dining hall.

Given that, she had to go somewhere—a restaurant, tavern, or food stall—to eat.

For that reason, and also to gather information about her enemy, Ilze had come to the tavern.

She ordered some ale and a few dishes, then listened intently, eavesdropping on the conversations of the noisy patrons around her to gather information.

That was the plan, but...

"So, there are some troublesome people around Ms. Vihera."

Thud.

Speaking with obvious frustration, Melan, who had somehow ended up sitting across from Ilze, slammed her ale glass down on the table.

Yes, for some reason, Ilze was currently having a meal with Melan.

At first, Ilze had tried to refuse, saying she was a low-rank adventurer and didn't have much money. But when Melan insisted on treating her, she couldn't refuse.

The patrols Melan was currently doing for the town required a certain level of combat ability due to the nature of the job, but the pay was correspondingly high.

...Though, compared to the mages working on the new wall, it was considerably cheap.

Still, treating a single woman to a meal was no problem at all.

"I see. It must be painful to have another man beside the one you love. ...And considering how beautiful Vihera is..."

At first, even hearing the name Vihera, Ilze didn't know who it was.

But after hearing Melan describe her—possessing beauty worthy of being called peerless, clad in the sheer garments worn by dancers and courtesans—

Hearing that, it didn't take Ilze long to realize this was the woman she'd saved today.

Now that he mentioned it, the men who had attacked her had called her Vihera.

"That's right! And that bastard Rei doesn't just have Vihera-san... he's got several other beauties like her serving him too..."

"...Several? Beauties as stunning as Miss Vihera?"

The words that came out of Melan's mouth were something Ilze simply couldn't believe.

A beauty you might see once in a lifetime... and there were others besides Vihera?

Ilze had a fair amount of confidence in her own looks, which was precisely why Vihera's beauty had overwhelmed her.

"That's right! Honestly, that guy Rei... just because he's got a little skill..."

"But, that Rei guy is that Crimson one, right? The one who has a Griffin as a familiar? Making an enemy of a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname... maybe you should reconsider..."

Ilze recalled the griffin she'd seen while walking through Gilm today.

Up until Abuelo, near Gilm, the towns had been fairly ordinary, with a few exceptions.

But Gilm alone could be called an extraordinary place in every sense.

"Just because she has a nickname, I won't forgive anyone who makes Ms. Vihera miserable!"

With irritation, she brought the glass she'd slammed on the table earlier back to her mouth.

She wasn't savoring the ale; she was forcing it down to quell her irritation.

"Phew! ...Another round, please!"

He called out to a passing waitress, then shoveled food from the table into his mouth.

He ate the stir-fried dish, cooked salty to pair well with alcohol.

Perhaps due to working under the blazing sun, the salty dish tasted remarkably good to Melan.

He drank the ale the waitress brought him again, took another bite of food... and then, probably because he wasn't that strong with alcohol to begin with,

Melan's face flushed red with drunkenness.

Seeing Melan completely drunk like that... Ilze suddenly felt like asking the man in front of her.

She wouldn't normally do such a thing, but Ilze was drunk too, no doubt.

Above all, finding the enemy she'd spent years searching for today was a huge factor.

"Hey, Mr. Melan. I have something I'd like to ask you. Is that okay?"

"Huh... What is it? Why so formal? We're friends, aren't we? Ask me anything."

A relationship with me? He couldn't help but think that, but if she was this drunk, it suited Ilze just fine.

Mainly because whatever she asked here would probably stay here—she wouldn't remember it tomorrow.

"What do you think... about revenge?"

"Revenge? Ah... I think it's a pointless act."

The moment he heard those words, Ilze felt the blood rush to his head, even though he was the one who'd asked.

She had asked several people before. What they thought of the act of revenge.

But most told her revenge was pointless, a foolish act.

Of course, not everyone said that, but still, most who answered told her revenge had no meaning.

She had expected such answers to come back.

Melan, whom she had spoken with until now, was fundamentally a man with a strong sense of justice, even if somewhat self-righteous.

Even understanding Melan's character, I still asked him what he thought about revenge.

And so, when I heard the words I'd expected... yet didn't want to hear, getting angry would be the mistake.

"Ah, look. Mr. Melan. This stew over here is delicious too, you know? How about it?"

"Hm? Ah, well, I suppose I'll try some."

Melan, drunk, was easily distracted by Ilze's words, which also served to change the subject.

His hands trembled with drunkenness as he ate the stew.

"Ah, delicious. Delicious. Just like Ilze said."

Ilze couldn't tell if he truly understood the taste in his drunken state.

But since Melan said it was delicious, Ilze decided it probably wasn't a problem.

(What would he do if someone served him poisoned food in this state? Well, maybe it just means he trusts me that much.)

Eating the stir-fried oak meat, seasoned slightly salty to pair well with alcohol, Ilze looked at Melan sitting before her.

Then, feigning nonchalance, Ilze spoke again.

"Speaking of which, I went to the guild today... and there was someone there with a snake tattoo on their left arm. It looked like the snake was climbing up the arm, as if treating it like a tree trunk. I'd never seen a tattoo like that before, so it surprised me a bit."

"Huh? I-re-z-u-mi?"

Eating, drinking... He was already completely drunk.

He had no energy left to respond to Ilze's words.

(Did I ask too late?)

Ilze, who had hoped to get some information about her enemy, wondered if she had made a mistake in the order she spoke while drinking her ale, glancing at Melan.

Melan was already practically sprawled face-down on the table, half-passed out from drunkenness.

"Ah... Uh...?! ...What about Rei?!"

Ilze glanced at Melen, who was uttering such sounds, and looked around the tavern, wondering what to do.

Quite a bit of time must have passed while she was talking with Melan.

Most of the patrons still in the tavern were drunk.

A few, like Melan, were completely passed out.

"Now, what should I do?"

Gathering information in this situation would be difficult.

Ideally, she would have gathered as much information as possible before things got this bad... With that thought, Ilze took a sip of her ale.

Of course, not everyone in the tavern was drunk.

There were naturally those who could hold their liquor, and a fair number of customers had arrived later.

If she poured drinks for those people, it wouldn't be hard to get them to talk.

She glanced over at Melan. He had been awake just moments ago, but now he was completely drunk and asleep.

Well then, she thought.

Ilze scanned the tavern once more.

Even when gathering information, you need to choose your targets carefully.

After all, it wasn't uncommon for people to demand physical contact in exchange for information, or even outright demand to be held instead of information.

Ideally, someone who would share information just for keeping them company over drinks... and someone who was somewhat loose-lipped.

Of course, someone like Melan, completely passed out drunk, was out of the question. But sober individuals who weren't that drunk yet weren't ideal either, as they tended to be tight-lipped.

While searching for someone who might have information... eventually, I spotted a trio enjoying themselves at a table a short distance away.

Perhaps because it was a tavern, they weren't wearing any armor.

Still, seeing them carrying longswords and daggers, Ilze could guess they were probably adventurers.

She cast one last glance at Melan, confirming he was sound asleep.

He wasn't as strong with alcohol as Ilze, and he was probably exhausted from continuing to train and keep his body moving even after work was done.

Additionally, drinking with a beauty like Ilze and jealousy towards Rei had made him drink faster than usual.

For various reasons, Melan had ended up in his current state.

(He won't be waking up anytime soon, I suppose.)

Confirming this, she headed toward the noisy trio drinking nearby.

"Excuse me?"

"Huh? What do you want, sis?"

The trio consisted of individuals in their thirties, twenties, and teens.

Of course, that was merely Ilze's guess based on appearances.

After all, starting with elves, there were certainly plenty of beings in the world whose ages couldn't be judged by appearance alone.

Ilze smiled at the man in his thirties and spoke.

"Actually, my companion passed out in no time,"

After saying this, Ilze's gaze shifted to Melan, who was passed out drunk.

The three men followed Ilze's gaze to the passed-out Melan and nodded in understanding.

"But I'd like to drink a little more. Seeing you all enjoying yourselves like that made me envious. If it's okay with you, could I join you?"

"Of course! We'd love to drink with a beauty like you!"

A man in his twenties welcomed Ilze with a delighted smile.

The teenage boy, however, gave Ilze a somewhat suspicious look.

A woman deliberately coming over to another table to drink with them.

It wasn't unheard of, but it certainly wasn't something that happened often.

Ilse noticed the stares directed at her, but she didn't seem particularly bothered. She sat down and spoke up.

"I just arrived in Gilm today. What about you guys?"

"We're in a similar boat. Only been in Gilm ten days or so?"

"Yeah, about that long."

The man in his thirties spoke, and the man in his twenties replied.

"Oh, so you've been working here for a while then? What's it like? I hear there's all sorts of jobs available."

Pretending to gather information... or perhaps just acting like a woman who wouldn't look out of place in a tavern, Ilze asked the men.

The man in his twenties smiled as he answered Ilze's seemingly casual inquiry.

"Well, yeah. The work's tough in many ways, but you get paid well for the effort you put in. I do stuff like hauling materials and odd jobs for the wall construction crew... but honestly, it's been a real learning experience."

"That much? You've worked as an adventurer before, right?"

"Ah... yeah. But I've never been involved in a construction project this big before."

"Yeah. But, you know... Where was it... Oh yeah, Ruta, right? We repaired the walls in that village, didn't we?"

"Walls are walls, but there's a huge difference between a single plank wall and a wall in Gilm, don't you think?"

The man in his thirties spoke, and the man in his twenties chimed in.

Ilze continued drinking happily with the three men for a while longer.

Then, when the conversation turned to how many adventurers in Gilm were rather peculiar... Ilze spoke up as if she'd been waiting for it.

"Speaking of which, when I went to the guild today, there was an adventurer with a snake tattoo on his left arm. It was a pretty big tattoo, so I was a bit surprised... Are there a lot of adventurers with tattoos like that in Gilm?"

"Tattoos? Hmm, I don't know about that. But if you mean someone with a lion tattoo instead of a snake... I think I saw one on the way to Gilm... in Abuero? Yeah, I saw one there."

The man in his thirties answered, and the other two nodded in agreement.

"Oh? So it really is rare, then?"

Though disappointed to find no information about her enemy, Ilze kept the conversation moving, hoping the topic wouldn't linger in the men's memories.
