Actually, he was holding a harpoon.

As he said this, the harpoon in Rei’s hand had a presence that clearly suggested it was far more advanced than the ones used in Galanka.

When Pastra saw it, she was transfixed by the harpoon for a few seconds, but then she spoke up, her voice tinged with exasperation.

“You have a harpoon like that, and you’ve forgotten what it’s for?”

“Well, what can I say? I haven’t used it at all since I had it made a few years ago.”

Looking back now, I should have used it when I was catching fish in the river.

Rei thought that for a moment, but when it came to catching a large number of fish at once, striking a rock to stun them with the shockwave was still far easier… and allowed him to catch a much larger quantity.

Also, even if he couldn’t catch all the fish, they’d merely be stunned.

Once they woke up, they could swim in the river just fine again.

“You had a harpoon like this and didn’t use it? …This wire part is for pulling the fish toward you after you’ve speared it, right?”

The reason I immediately understood the purpose of the wire attached to the harpoon was likely because the harpoons used in Galanka are operated in exactly the same way, using a rope.

That said, attaching a rope or wire to the handle of a harpoon is actually quite rare.

“Something like that. …I got the harpoon a few years ago. This is the first time I’ve been to the ocean since then. Well, I’ve fished in rivers a few times, but there was no need to use a harpoon there.”

“Hmm. …Can I borrow it for a bit?”

Nodding at Pastra’s words, Rei handed her the harpoon he was holding.

Pastrah took it, immediately gripped the harpoon to test how it felt in her hands… and soon wore a look of surprise.

“Man, this harpoon is amazing. At first glance, it doesn’t seem that different from the one I use, but when you actually try it, it’s quite different.”

“……Is it really that amazing?”

“Yeah! In fact, it’s so amazing that I can’t even forgive myself for not using a harpoon like this until now!”

Looking at Pastra as he said this with such a serious expression, it was easy to see that he wasn’t joking—he meant every word.

But from Rei’s perspective, while the harpoon’s “vibe”—if you could call it that—was clearly different from an ordinary one, in terms of usability, it didn’t seem all that different.

(Maybe the center of gravity is slightly different, or the thickness where you grip it—little details like that? Well, even with spears, things like that make a big difference, so I can’t say I don’t get it.)

Normally, it would be unthinkable to treat a spear and a harpoon as the same thing.

After all, harpoons and spears serve completely different purposes.

Moreover, in Rei’s case, she often uses it not to stab but to throw.

……That said, even with a harpoon, the way it’s used—whether thrusting directly or throwing it—can vary greatly depending on the user.

In any case, since this harpoon was crafted by a skilled artisan, Pastra, as a fisherman, surely understands just how impressive it is.

“Well, with this harpoon, I won’t need the one I was going to prepare.”

“Yeah, you’re right. Uh, sorry about that.”

In fact, Rei’s primary reason for coming to Galanka was to acquire fishing gear… specifically, a harpoon.

Of course, he also wanted a net—the kind used to scoop up small fish after stunning them—in addition to the harpoon.

That harpoon, his primary objective, was actually inside the Misty Ring, and it was clearly a much higher-performance model than the ones used in Galanka.

Under the circumstances, it would have been understandable if he’d gotten angry, thinking Rei was teasing him.

But Pastra imagined that Rei hadn’t been teasing her on purpose, and besides, Rei was her savior who had defeated the pirates threatening the village.

“Don’t worry about it. But if that’s the case… do you happen to have any nets or something?”

“No, I don’t have any of those. Besides, nets and the like have various uses besides fishing, so it wouldn’t hurt to have a few.”

Normally, since they’re bulky, you can’t carry a large quantity of them.

But in Rei’s case, he had the Misty Ring, and as long as he had that, he wouldn’t be troubled by the amount of luggage.

...Though, as with the harpoon incident this time, there are times when Rei forgets that he has a harpoon in the Misty Ring.

“Is that so? Well then… what size should it be? If Rei’s going to carry it, would a smaller one be better?”

“No, since you’ll be using it while riding Set, it’s fine if it’s somewhat large.”

“In that case, how about that one?”

Pastras pointed to a net that was also hanging on the wall.

The part you grip with your hand is made of wood, and that wood is fairly thick.

It was just thick enough for Rei to barely grip with his hand.

Proportional to the thickness of the wood, the net itself was also quite large.

“Of the nets we’re not currently using, that’s the largest one. However, the handle is quite thick so it can withstand the weight of the fish caught in the net. Are you sure you can handle it?”

After Pastra said that, Rei picked up the net.

Since it was made of wood, it was indeed as thick as he’d been told, but even so, when she held it, she didn’t find it particularly difficult to use.

“Yeah, I think it’ll be fine.”

“I see. If that’s fine, take it with you. Is there anything else you need?”

Hearing this, Rei thought for a moment.

As for the harpoon, he has a high-performance one with a wire, so using that for now should be fine.

As for the net, the one Rei currently has is more than enough.

That said, if he were asked if there was any other fishing gear he wanted…

(A cast net, maybe? No, but judging by what I see here, I don’t think they have cast nets. They probably don’t use them. It’s not something I’d go out of my way to ask for. …Well, if I had one, I suppose I could use it in battle or something.)

Especially when facing weak soldiers, adventurers, or thieves—what you might call “minions”—sweeping them all up with a cast net seemed like it would be quite effective.

Of course, even if you catch an opponent with a cast net, that doesn’t mean you can kill them.

At best, it would just slow them down until they managed to escape.

But… whether or not you can hold them back carries significant weight in this situation.

Being unable to move, even temporarily, on the battlefield essentially means certain death.

If you use magic, the immobilized minions are literally caught in the net, and even without magic, you have the option of leaving them to your allies.

(Well, if they knew the throw net was being used that way, they probably wouldn’t be too happy about it. In that case… a fishing rod? But honestly, rather than fishing, using a harpoon or smashing rocks would probably catch a lot more fish.)

In Rei’s mind, fishing has a strong image of being a hobby or a pastime.

Of course, there are exceptions like single-line bonito fishing, so that’s just Rei’s personal impression.

And Rei doesn’t particularly like fishing.

Even when he was in Japan, whenever Rei caught river fish like sweetfish, char, mountain trout, or sculpin in the summer, he used a harpoon instead of a fishing rod.

“Right. Well, for now, we don’t really need that much. This net is all the gear we need. By the way, what happened to the plan to buy some fish?”

“Oh, they’re out fishing for that.”

Rei was slightly surprised by Pastra’s matter-of-fact reply.

He says they’re out fishing casually, but there are monsters in the sea besides fish.

That’s precisely why, even though he knew there were still a few pirates on the island where their hideout was located, he’d left the sea monsters there to act as guards.

“How long will they be gone?”

“I think they should be back soon… but I haven’t exactly set a specific time for their return.”

In a rural village like Galanka, it goes without saying that there are no clocks like the ones found in towns… nor any mechanisms where a bell rings at specific times.

Given that, it was only natural that people were quite lax about time.

“I see. In this village… what kind of boats do you use for fishing?”

In response to Rei’s question, Pastra motioned for him to step out of the shed where the fishing gear was stored.

Once they stepped out, a boat-like vessel sitting on the sandy beach came into view.

“That kind of boat.”

“...Is that so? With something like that, wouldn’t you be unable to go very far?”

“If you go too far out, the sea gets deep. And the deeper the sea, the more monsters there are. The places where we can fish aren’t actually that far out, after all.”

Hearing that, Rei had no choice but to accept it.

(If we can’t go that far out to sea, it doesn’t look like we’ll be able to catch big fish like tuna. …In that sense, though, it’s just right for me since I’m looking for small fish.)

For now, the fact that he’d be able to safely get the small fish he wanted—relative to tuna and the like, at least—was nothing but a relief to Rei.

“Oh, by the way, besides fish, I’d also like shrimp, crab, and big clams—is that okay?”

“Large shellfish might be difficult, but I think we can manage the rest. Not many people go out to catch shellfish. As you can see, there’s just a sandy beach in front of the village, so you have to go all the way out to the rocky areas.”

“I see. Well, I guess there’s nothing to be done about it.”

Even as he said that, Rei felt a bit disappointed.

That’s because he had been hoping to eat shellfish like abalone, oysters, and turban shells if possible.

Seeing how disappointed Rei looked, Pastra thought for a moment before speaking.

“Well, um… It’s not possible today, but starting tomorrow, if you ask, they might be willing to go catch some for you. If you’re interested, I can ask them for you. What do you think?”

“Please do.”

Seeing Rei ask without even a moment’s hesitation, Pastra was careful not to let his surprise show.

He hadn’t expected him to be this eager for shellfish.

Living in Galanka, clams are something you can easily catch in the sea.

Since there’s only a sandy beach near the village, you have to go a bit further out to the rocky coastline… but even so, they aren’t that rare.

It’s not uncommon for clams to be used in meals prepared in Galanka.

Simply boiling them is enough to bring out their full flavor.

...That said, the clams found in shallow waters aren’t very big, so you’d have to catch a considerable amount to eat your fill.

“Got it. So, let’s leave it at that regarding the shellfish. …You’ll be coming back to Galanka, right?”

“If you’ll sell me clams and fish, I’d be happy to come. I came here from Gilm in the first place to catch seafood, starting with fish. It’s almost like I came here just for fun.”

“You come here to have fun, and while you’re at it, you go around hunting pirates? I guess that’s not really something for us—who were being threatened by those very pirates—to say.”

High-ranking adventurers are, in many ways, out of the ordinary.

Pastra looked as if she wanted to say just that, but Rei was used to being looked at that way, so he nodded back without seeming particularly bothered.

“In this case, the pirates weren’t that big of a threat. Besides, I make it a rule to take down bandits and pirates whenever I see them.”

“……I hear you’re called the ‘Bandit Eater’?”

It didn’t take much thought to figure out where that information had come from.

There was no doubt that Elena and the others had told her while Rei was in Jubis.

That said, it wasn’t exactly something he minded people knowing.

"Bandit Eater"… The reason he’d come to be called that was simply because he’d wiped out so many bandits.

Which means, unless you’re a bandit, you won’t be attacked.

“Well, yeah. I really didn’t even realize when that nickname stuck. I guess even bandits who attack others don’t want to be attacked themselves.”

“No, that’s only natural. Being attacked by someone who commands a monster like a griffin would be nothing short of a nightmare.”

Pastra muttered thoughtfully, but in all honesty, from a general perspective—and certainly from the bandits’ point of view—it was an opinion they would immediately agree with.

That said, Pastra had absolutely no intention of feeling any sympathy for the bandits.

“For now, if you can prepare some shellfish and fish, I’ll buy every last bit you have… Well, I plan to come back here tomorrow or the day after, so if you have any then, I’ll buy them. Of course, I’d also like to buy whatever you catch today, if possible.”

“Ah, that’s perfect. …Look.”

At Rei’s words, Pastra pointed toward the sea.

In the distance, a boat was just returning to the sandy shore… and as if to signal a bountiful catch, it waved vigorously at Rei and Pastra standing on the beach.
