The snow had completely melted, and adventurers who had spent the winter resting at the Gilm began to stir once more... Within the guild, Marina spoke to Walker as staff and adventurers looked on.

"Walker, starting today, you are Gilm's guildmaster. I've taught you many things over the winter as part of the handover, but you possess more than enough skill to handle the role here."

"...Though I am unworthy, I will do my utmost."

Walker responded solemnly to Marina's words.

Among the guild staff and adventurers watching this exchange, some wondered: Is Walker really up to the task?

It was only natural. Marina had run the guild smoothly as guildmaster until now.

If the top changed now, would things really run as smoothly as before...? That was the worry.

Some guild staff had worked alongside Walker during the winter, yet they still felt some unease. That was likely because Marina had been such a solid, commanding presence as guildmaster.

Walker does have experience running a guild branch near a dungeon.

Even so, it was inevitable that some would feel uneasy compared to Marina.

Walker probably understood that too.

Having worked alongside Marina during the winter, he saw firsthand how deeply she was admired by the guild staff.

Some likely admired her as an attractive woman, but more trusted her as their superior.

That kind of trust isn't something acquired overnight. Marina earned it through years of service as guildmaster, which is why everyone placed such trust in her.

Precisely because he understood this, Walker couldn't demand immediate trust.

"You've all worked under the previous guildmaster... Marina. I understand it's hard for you to trust me right away. But please, rest assured."

Hearing those words, those listening wondered what he would say next.

Though met with such gazes, Walker showed no sign of flustering and continued speaking.

"As you all know, Marina-san has passed the guild master position to me. However, that doesn't mean Marina-san will have no involvement with the guild. Since she will remain in an advisory role, if anything happens, you can rely on Marina-san."

Marina would remain as an advisor.

This had been known beforehand, but hearing it stated plainly before them now brought a palpable sense of relief.

Visibly, the expressions of those listening to Walker's words relaxed.

"Of course, we can't keep relying on Marina as an advisor forever. I... and all of us... have been entrusted with running this guild by Marina. Given that, please don't forget that each of us must eventually stand on our own."

At those words, everyone nodded.

Watching the exchange unfold before her, Marina exhaled in relief.

Marina, who had handed over the reins, knew full well that Walker was capable.

But whether the others would accept him was another matter entirely.

Fortunately, right now, Walker was being accepted before Marina's eyes.

Of course, Marina's presence as a backup in case of trouble probably played a part, but it was undeniable that Walker had been accepted here.

(Now it's just up to Walker to work hard from here and show his abilities and presence to those around him.)

Marina watched this exchange intently from a short distance away from Walker.

"Marina, thank you for all your hard work as Guildmaster until now. From now on, as an adventurer... and as an advisor to the guild, I look forward to working with you."

"Yes. If there's anything you don't understand, I'll lend a hand anytime. So you do your best too, okay?"

And so, the succession of the Guildmaster was formally concluded.

Technically, the formalities had already been completed this morning. What had just taken place was more of a ceremony—or perhaps a performance—for the adventurers, staff, and townspeople who had come to see the change in guildmaster.

Walker then stepped behind the counter and disappeared up the stairs leading to the inner office... the guildmaster's private chambers.

The guild hall had been reasonably quiet at first, but once Walker was gone, it returned to its usual bustle.

Marina glanced at the guild's atmosphere... and then began to walk.

Ahead of her, naturally, were the figures of Rei and the others with whom she would now share both hardship and joy, and destiny.

"Did I keep you waiting?"

With a satisfied smile, she addressed Rei and the men.

"Not really. ...But was it really okay to make it that simple?"

Given it was a guildmaster succession, Rei had expected a much grander ceremony.

But what actually took place within the guild was a much smaller ceremony than Rei had anticipated... no, something he hesitated to even call a ceremony.

"Oh, really? But what's the point of all that pomp and circumstance if there's no heart behind it? Besides, it's just a guildmaster change, after all. It's not exactly something that needs such a grand celebration."

"Hmm... Is that how it is?"

What flashed through Rei's mind as she muttered was a scene she'd seen on TV back in Japan.

When important figures like prefectural governors or mayors stepped down, scenes where all the staff gathered to see them off or present bouquets.

He'd assumed it would be similar, which made this feel... not exactly anticlimactic, but certainly unexpected.

But even if it was the only frontier guild in the Mireana Kingdom, it was still just one guild among many.

In Rei's terms, it felt more like a school principal resigning or transferring to another school.

"That's right. Anyway, I'm no longer the guildmaster. ...Well, I am still in an advisory role, though."

"But advisor isn't an official position, is it?"

Vihera asked while stroking the head of the dazed Byune.

"Yes. It's purely a private role where Walker... no, the guild master consults me when something comes up. Naturally, the guild doesn't pay me anything either."

In reality, if I'm consulted on something and resolve it, I do receive some money under the guise of a reward or token of appreciation.

But for Marina, who had been Guildmaster for so long, such rewards were merely a negligible sum, whether received or not.

Rather, since she'll be active as an adventurer from now on, the rewards gained from that would likely be greater.

Ordinary adventurers... especially those ranked D or C... couldn't possibly earn more than the guildmaster.

But Marina was forming a party with three people: Rei, Vihera, and Byune. And then there was also Set.

Considering Rei's actions during Marina's time as guildmaster, it was certain to be a substantial income.

After all, over the past few years, Rei has earned enough money to live off for a lifetime... no, enough to live off for lifetimes beyond that.

"Anyway, does this mean Marina can move freely now?"

"Yes. Of course, as an advisor, it's probably best to inform the guild if she plans to travel far away, but she doesn't have any such plans, does she?"

"True. Our coordination in battle isn't perfect yet either, so I'd prefer to keep things solid on that front."

At Rei's words, the others... even Byune nodded firmly in agreement.

They understood that a clumsy move during battle would only hinder their comrades.

Having soloed dungeons since childhood, Byune wasn't exactly skilled at coordination either.

In that sense, Vihera had also spent a long time soloing, and so had Rei.

Among them, the only one who had genuinely operated as a party for an extended period was Marina.

"So, what should we do? Head out to fight monsters starting today? Or practice at the guild's training grounds?"

Rei paused at Marina's question... then spoke.

"Ideally, the training grounds would be best... but it's crowded right now, isn't it?"

"Yeah. It's just spring, so lots of people are trying to get back in shape after the break, and others like us are probably checking their party coordination too."

Of course, the best way to check coordination is in actual combat.

But actual combat inevitably carried the risk of injury.

Even against goblins, getting careless and injured wasn't uncommon.

And if you get injured, you'll need potions and medicine to heal... which hits your wallet hard, especially this time of year when money is tight.

It would be different if someone could use healing magic, but even within Gilm, those capable of using magic are far from numerous.

And among them, those who could use healing magic were likely extremely rare.

"Anyway, shall we head to the training grounds?"

"...Hey, Rei. Isn't there something we should do first?"

Rei was about to leave the guild when Vihera stopped him.

"Something we should do?"

"Yes. Since Marina officially resigned as Guild Master, don't you think we need to declare the formation of our party, something we couldn't do before? If we don't handle this early on, it'll definitely become a hassle later, you know?"

Vihera's words made sense to Rei too.

With spirit magic, she could use all sorts of spells... including healing magic, and her archery skills were top-notch. Not to mention her beauty.

As the former guildmaster, she commanded great respect from those around her.

With someone like that operating solo without a party, it stands to reason that invitations to join a party would come pouring in.

Anyone who'd lived in Gilm for a while knew why Marina had stepped down as guildmaster, so they wouldn't commit such suicide.

But... spring is the season when the most adventurers flock to Gilm.

Those who were considered skilled in their previous places of activity... put kindly, they're brimming with confidence; put harshly, they're the kind you'd call 'mountain kings'.

Those who understand the importance of information would start gathering it immediately upon arriving in Gilm.

But those with only mediocre talent, who've never faced truly formidable foes... those who've never tasted defeat? They wouldn't grasp how vital information truly is.

It's not hard to imagine what such individuals would do upon learning about Marina.

Given Rei and the others' current situation, it was only natural to file their party formation notice with the guild here to avoid such trouble.

"Well then, let's go."

At Rei's words, the other three nodded and headed toward the reception counter.

Earlier... perhaps because it hadn't even been ten minutes since Marina stepped down as Guild Master, she herself had been wearing a smile that bordered on a shy grin.

"Ah, Rei. Gil... no, Marina-san, Vihera-san, and Byune-san too. What brings you here?"

Seeing Rei approach her, Lenora called out.

Marina wasn't the only one who seemed somewhat unaccustomed to the recent situation. Lenora, who had worked under her, felt the same way.

And beside Lenora, Kenny also wore a somewhat awkward expression.

Both Lenora and Kenny were aware that Marina doted on them.

That's precisely why they were at a loss for words in a situation like this.

"Ah—"

Amidst this, Lenora suddenly spoke up.

Her gaze was fixed on Marina's chest.

Normally, she might have had thoughts about those curves, vastly larger than her own, but not now.

Because there, pinned to her chest, was the brooch the receptionists—Kenny included—had pooled their money to buy.

...Yes. The brooch they had all given Marina as a farewell gift when she resigned as Guildmaster.

The moment she saw it, Lenora understood that the person before her wasn't the Guildmaster, but Marina, just another adventurer.

Seeing Lenora like this, Marina smiled... Unaware of Marina smiling behind him, Rei addressed Lenora directly.

"I'd like to request a party formation. The members are the four of us here. The party name is Crimson Wings."
