"Hmm... You mean people from the slums, not adventurers? That's a bit tricky."

Inside the guild, Lenora muttered, looking troubled.

Rei, who had parted ways with Suu-chi at the construction site, had continued searching for magic items, but eventually made his way to the guild.

The reason, as Lenora had mentioned, was to discuss the matter regarding Suu-chi in advance.

However, in response to Rei’s words, Lenora unexpectedly replied in a way that seemed to reject his request.

“Why not? We’re short-handed for dismantling the Giganto Turtle, aren’t we? If that’s the case, wouldn’t it be okay to ask for help from someone in the slums who’s good at dismantling things?”

“I understand what you’re saying, Rei. I do… but the Guild has its rules. Especially since we’re dealing with someone who isn’t an adventurer this time, right? That makes it even more…”

Having said that, Lenora shook her head.

Next to Lenora, Kenny looked as if she wanted to say something, but of course, she was also aware of the guild’s rules.

Given that, even Kenny couldn’t just casually tell Lenora to accept Rei’s proposal.

...The fact that they’d been busy dealing with adventurers was another reason she couldn’t speak up.

“For now, as for the reward, it doesn’t have to be as high as what we’d pay an adventurer who accepted the job normally… or even as high as what we’d pay non-adventurers. Can’t we work something out? I don’t think it’d be a bad thing for the Guild if the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle is finished sooner.”

The reason Rei had come up with this proposal to hire people from the slums was because a large number of non-adventurers—though many were former adventurers—had participated in the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle this time.

“Umm… Well, even if you say that…”

“Then, could you at least ask your boss? We’ve put up with the Guild’s unreasonable requests plenty of times before, so I think they could do us this much.”

“Well…”

At Rei’s words, Lenora was at a loss for words.

It was true that Rei had accommodated the Guild’s unreasonable requests many times before, and Lenora knew that.

Given that, she felt it was only fair for the guild to accommodate him a little in return.

After a moment’s hesitation, Lenora finally gave a small nod.

“All right. Please wait a moment. I’ll go check with my superiors.”

Saying that, Lenora stood up from her chair and walked over to the person working on paperwork at the far end of the counter.

The Kobold incident and the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.

They were handling two major requests simultaneously, and on top of that, there was plenty of regular work to do.

Given that, it was only natural that the guild staff had a heavy workload.

...And that was especially true now that they had dispatched several guild staff members to assist with the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.

In fact, even someone who had resigned from the guild a short while ago for some reason had been called back as a temporary hire for this project.

Although she felt bad about bringing up this matter with her boss, Lenora went ahead and spoke to him.

Lenora had been talking to her boss where Rei’s gaze was fixed, but it seemed she had been given some instructions and was heading further inside.

Rei knew what was there, having passed through that area several times himself.

What was there was a staircase leading to the upper floor… to the Guild Master’s office.

The Guild Master, Walker, should have been working there right now.

(Isn’t this getting a little too dramatic?)

Rei, who hadn’t expected the matter to reach the Guild Master, was slightly taken aback… but deciding that since he’d already asked Lenora, he had no choice but to wait, he stood still in front of the reception desk.

Kenny waited for a chance to speak to Rei while attending to the adventurers, but with Lenora gone and the adventurers who had been lined up behind Rei moving to other reception desks, he found himself having to attend to even more adventurers than before.

……Under normal circumstances, it wouldn’t be strange for the adventurers in line to confront an adventurer who caused the receptionist to leave while they were waiting.

But if that adventurer were Rei, no one would dare to confront him recklessly.

If Rei had been wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe, some might not have recognized him as Rei and might have confronted him—especially since they wouldn’t have heard Lenora call his name—but right now, Rei had taken off his hood to speak with Lenora.

Thanks to that, there was no need for unnecessary conflict, and he was able to wait for Lenora like this.

About ten minutes passed… and eventually, Lenora emerged from behind the counter.

The moment he saw Lenora looking at him with a smile, Rei was certain his proposal had been accepted.

“Rei, I consulted with the Guild Master, and he’s agreed to allow residents from the slums to participate, just this once. As for the reward, he said we’ll set it lower than what the adventurers receive, as you suggested, to ease any discontent.”

“...That’s surprising.”

At Lenora’s words, those words slipped from Rei’s mouth.

There was no doubt he had requested it himself, but he never expected that, even so, this particular matter would go exactly as he’d asked.

It was true that he would have been in trouble if things hadn’t turned out that way.

That said, naturally, not everything would go exactly as Rei had requested.

“It’s just… um… regarding the security personnel…”

Seeming reluctant to say it, Lenora explained that it would be difficult to dispatch any more personnel, and that one or two at most would be the limit.

Rei could see just how busy the guild was, so he knew that even just increasing the number of guards by one or two would be a huge help.

“Also, they said it would be difficult for the Guild to impose any kind of punishment if trouble were to arise in the slums.”

“So that’s out of the question too?”

“Yes. However, the Guild Master said it wouldn’t be a problem as long as Rei just says it like that.”

In other words, he’s telling me to bluff. That must be it.

But Rei seemed to have no particular objection to that either and nodded.

To begin with, there are actually quite a few adventurers who come from the slums.

For those born and raised in the slums, becoming an adventurer is the quickest way to escape them.

……Though, depending on their appearance, becoming a prostitute or a male prostitute is also an option.

In any case, partly due to the location of the frontier town of Gilm, there are many people in the slums with high potential.

If we were to block such people from becoming adventurers, they might end up joining underground organizations.

To prevent that from happening, it is necessary to accept the slum dwellers by providing the profession of adventurer as a safety net. …Though the number of slum dwellers who become adventurers does decrease somewhat after a year or so.

“I understand. If it’s just a bluff, I’ll go along with it.”

“Thank you. So, how many slum residents are you specifically expecting?”

Lenora asked Rei this, but Rei himself wasn’t sure how to answer.

After all, even Rei didn’t know exactly how many people Suu-chi would bring.

It might be just a few people besides Suu-chi, or depending on the situation, it could be dozens, or even close to a hundred.

“I’m not quite sure about that yet. Suu-chi did say she’d bring herself and some acquaintances… but I told them to be at the gate before the bell rings tomorrow morning, so could you check on that for me?”

“Huh? Hmm… Not knowing how many people are coming is a bit of a problem, though. But, well, I suppose so. In that case, I think we’ll count the number of people when they arrive, then have you notify the Guild so they can prepare the reward. As for the reward, would it be better to hand it over on-site rather than have them collect it at the Guild?”

Lenora’s words carried the implication that she’d prefer it that way.

That’s only natural. Even now, the guild is quite busy in the mornings and evenings.

If just one or two people showed up, that might be manageable, but if dozens of slum dwellers arrived, it would undoubtedly cause some kind of commotion.

It was precisely because she understood this that Lenora had requested they avoid coming to the guild and instead hand out the rewards on-site.

Rei had no particular objection to this, so he simply nodded in agreement.

After all, what Rei really wants is manpower to speed up the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle—not to bring slum dwellers to the guild.

In fact, many of the slum dwellers would likely prefer to avoid coming to the guild, where they might get dragged into trouble.

At least, the Suu-chi Rei knew would have made that judgment.

“Ah, that’s fine. …Sorry for asking so much of you. You’ve really helped me out.”

“No, the guild has made all sorts of unreasonable requests of Rei in the past, and Rei has sold us plenty of rare materials as well.”

With those words, Rei understood what Lenora—no, more accurately, Walker, the guild master Lenora had gone to speak with earlier—was trying to say.

In other words, they probably wanted her to sell them as many Giganto Turtle materials as possible.

(Giganto Turtle materials, huh... But to be honest, I have no idea right now how much each material is worth. It’s the first time I’ve encountered this monster. Or rather, even if someone has encountered it before, it’s a monster with no recorded data.)

As for the meat, I’ve tasted it myself and understand that it’s so delicious it’s hard to put into words.

But as for the other materials? Unless you’re an alchemist, apothecary, or blacksmith—someone who actually uses those materials to craft magic items, potions, weapons, or armor—you wouldn’t know.

And even then, it’s only after those people actually get their hands on the materials and experiment with them that they can determine whether they’re usable.

In other words, since it wasn’t clear whether the parts of a Giganto Turtle could be used as materials at all, it was impossible to know which parts could be sold as such.

……In that sense, Rei’s ability to store materials in the Misty Ring and prevent them from spoiling must have been a tremendous boon to the guild… and to Gilm.

“I understand about the materials. Right. For now, let’s sell a few materials in small quantities to see if they’re usable. If they are, we’ll sell more. Is that okay?”

“...Is that okay? If we do that, it’ll be a very favorable deal for the Guild.”

Rei’s words must have come as a surprise to Lenora as well. She said this with a look of astonishment… but Rei nodded, indicating he saw no particular problem with it.

In fact, Rei wasn’t short on money at all, so he wasn’t particularly concerned about the price of the materials.

The only thing he cares about is what kind of magic items can be made using Giganto Turtle materials… but from Rei’s perspective, if the Guild could distribute the materials to as many craftsmen as possible and have them create useful magic items, he wouldn’t have to investigate it himself, which would be a huge help.

“That’s fine. However, I’d like to know what kinds of items can be made from those materials. …That way, it won’t be a hassle for the Guild, right?”

“I can’t decide this on my own, but I’ll pass it on to the higher-ups.”

Lenora said this, and Rei, satisfied with that, left the guild.

(Since I don’t know which parts can be sold as materials, does that mean I need to save everything—including the Giganto Turtle’s internal organs—in the Misty Ring instead of throwing them away or burning them?)

Parts such as the Giganto Turtle’s skin and claws are currently stored in the Misty Ring.

Rei wasn’t particularly keen on storing not only those parts but also the internal organs in the Misty Ring, but since he certainly couldn’t just dispose of them, it was a fact that the Misty Ring was the only way to preserve them under optimal conditions.

“Grrrrr!”

Perhaps noticing Rei coming out of the guild, Set, who had been playing with a few passersby, growled happily.

Watching Set like that, Rei felt a little peckish and began to think about grabbing something to eat from a food stall.
