Shortly before Augusto was attacked, Daldora was listening to a report from his subordinate inside the shop.

"I see. So the Tira tree is not a problem. Are you certain about that?"

The question was directed at a man clad in a white coat.

...Though it seemed it hadn't been laundered in quite some time, looking rather dingy and hardly deserving of the name "white coat."

"Yes. That side is fine. However..."

"But?"

"No, it's the research funding that's a bit... I'd like to request a little more, if possible."

"Again? I distinctly recall paying you about a month ago after you asked for something similar."

Daldora's already nervous-looking face twisted in displeasure.

Even the influential Erema Trading Company in Gosh was, after all, dealing with the small nation of Solren... and not even its capital city.

Naturally, the amount of freely available funds wasn't limitless. ...Even so, they did possess the financial resources to gather skilled adventurers from neighboring countries.

Yet, seemingly oblivious to Daldora's state, the man in white continued speaking.

"Yes. As things stand now, I believe there are almost no problems. But 'almost' does not mean 'completely'. If we leave things as they are, it is possible... truly only a possibility, but we cannot rule out the chance that the Tira trees might take hostile action against people."

"I've never heard of the Tira Tree harming humans before,"

Daldora's words were uttered with a sigh, but the man in white shook his head vehemently.

"As you say, Daldora, there may have been no attacks until now. But that's only until now. The Tira trees have lived with almost no interaction with humans until now."

"...So, the lack of incidents until now is simply because human interaction with Tira trees has been minimal? But if we proceed as I suggest and plant Tira trees around the walls, there might be consequences? When you, Kuto, first brought this proposal, I didn't hear anything about this possibility."

"It's a possibility. Merely a possibility. But wouldn't it be better to eliminate potential sources of worry?"

At the words of the man called Kuto, Daldora exhaled softly and pondered.

He had already received reports confirming the Tira trees were safe with near certainty. So, was it really necessary to allocate additional funds just to verify their safety?

(But... to truly reassure Lord Ryubrand, certainty is required. Thinking that way, it is unavoidable.)

Still, Daldora hesitated, driven by the instincts of a seasoned merchant.

The money he was providing was being used by Ktoga for research on the Tira Tree.

That much was clear from the ledgers, and tangible results had been achieved, so it was certain.

But... even so, his merchant's instincts made him question whether he could continue trusting Ktoga like this.

(No, matters have progressed this far. Hesitating now would be pointless. That being the case...)

After gathering his thoughts, Daldora finally spoke.

"Very well, I'll approve the additional funds. Before that, however, I'd like to see inside the research facility. Is that acceptable?"

"Yes, of course. I'll gladly show you my precious Tira trees to your heart's content. Now, let's go. Let's go right away!"

If you were embezzling funds or using them for something unrelated to research, would you just nod obediently when asked to show the lab?

Normally, they'd refuse immediately.

Therefore, perhaps the funds were being used properly after all.

Thinking this, Daldora rose from the chair he was sitting in.

"Well then, let's go. Fortunately, I don't have any particularly urgent matters today."

muttering, Daldora exited the room with Kuto Ga.

The research facility entrusted to Kuto was located on the outskirts of Gosh, making it quite a distance to walk.

Besides, as the head of the Elema Trading Company, Daldora wasn't exactly idle all the time.

To shorten the travel time even slightly, he intended to board a carriage with his usual skilled adventurer companion as usual, but...

"Wait, Lord Daldora!"

One of the guards shouted, stepping forward to block Daldora's path as she moved toward the carriage, longsword in hand.

A moment later, the other guards formed a protective circle around Daldora.

At the center of that formation, beside Daldora, stood Kuto. Yet Kuto, confused by what was happening, frantically scanned his surroundings.

"My lady Daldora? What on earth..."

Kutoga asked Daldora beside him, but the response was Daldora's stern, tightened expression.

Though usually nervous, the current Daldora strongly reflected her role as the head of the Elema Trading Company, an entity wielding significant influence over Gosh.

"They're here!"

The man who had first warned Daldora shouted, and at the same moment, three men appeared.

"Who are you?!"

One of the guards shouted, but immediately realized the question was pointless.

Because the three who appeared were foaming at the mouth, their eyes drenched in madness.

"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

The three screamed, brandishing their daggers and charging straight toward the guards.

No, it was clear they weren't targeting the guards, but Daldora standing at their center.

It was possible someone among the guards was the target, but if asked who here was most likely the target, it would undoubtedly be Daldora.

"Block them! Three enemies, all practically amateurs!"

The guard who first spotted the trio shouted.

The other guards were also hired by Daldora for their skills.

They immediately realized the three men charging at them were definitely not skilled fighters.

"Hah, what the hell are these guys thinking, pulling a stunt like this?"

"Who knows. But don't let your guard down. Dying in a place like this would be no laughing matter."

"Yeah. But we shouldn't be troubled by enemies of this caliber... right?!"

With that, one of the guards swiftly drew his bow and released an arrow.

The arrow flew straight, piercing the torso of the man leading the trio attacking them without a single inch of deviation...

"Hey, what the hell is going on here?!"

The scream came not from the man hit by the arrow, but from the woman who shot it.

And no wonder. Despite the arrow piercing him, the front man showed no sign of pain whatsoever as he continued charging straight toward them.

Normally, an arrow piercing a limb... or worse, the torso, would slow a man's movement.

The pain itself could be managed by the excitement of battle, but the arrow itself became a hindrance.

The arrow embedded in the body would impede movement.

But... the three men now attacking Daldora showed no sign of such hindrance whatsoever.

The arrows should have hindered their movement, yet they closed the distance without a care.

No wonder the woman holding the bow let out a cry of astonishment.

"Calm down! Seeing how they move, it's no surprise they're insensitive to pain! If the pain doesn't slow them down, we'll just have to stop them from moving altogether!"

The man who seemed to be the leader of the guards shouted toward Daldora, who stood at their center.

"Daldora-sama, capturing them will be difficult, so we wish to eliminate them. Is that acceptable?"

He asked, "Is that alright?", but the man wasn't seeking permission from Daldora.

Knowing this, Daldora immediately nodded in return.

"Understood. If Narsus says so, capturing them must be impossible. Do as you wish."

Though Daldora usually showed no attempt to hide his nervous disposition, he was, after all, the head of the Elema Trading Company.

Even with his life targeted, he showed no sign of fear whatsoever.

No, Daldora is a merchant, not an adventurer who trains her body.

It wasn't that he felt no fear.

He simply didn't show it.

This was fitting for someone who led the Erema Trading Company, one of the largest trading companies in Gosh.

"We have Daldora's permission! Let's go—annihilate them!"

Following the voice of the man leading the guards called Narsus, three adventurers from the surrounding group moved toward the approaching men.

(They don't seem to be in pain, but judging by their movements, their actual skill is low. Then, our forces should be more than enough to handle them.)

Narsus made this assessment, confident in the fact that they had been gathered from surrounding nations as skilled fighters.

That judgment proved utterly correct, as the three who met the attackers swiftly demonstrated.

The attacks unleashed by the three guards ended the lives of the attackers without giving them a chance to do anything.

Though the three seemed to feel no pain, that alone couldn't possibly be enough to handle seasoned adventurers.

Their necks severed, their torsos cleaved, limbs torn away... Within a minute, the three attackers embarked on their journey to death.

Even after the three were dead, Narsas kept his guard up, scanning his surroundings.

They had launched an attack against him and his companions; he couldn't believe it would end so easily.

A second, third attack. He remained alert, expecting it... but no new assailants appeared.

Instead, passersby who had witnessed the exchange screamed, and others drawn by the cries began to gather.

"Lord Daldora, this place is dangerous. We should return to the building for now..."

"Yes, you're right."

Daldora thought for a moment, then nodded.

"Kutoga, I'm sorry, but we'll have to postpone the inspection of the Tira Tree for now. While this attacker likely targeted me, we can't rule out the possibility you were the target too. You should take refuge in my shop for a while."

"Ah... yes, you're right. ...I was hoping to get back to the lab sooner, though."

Kutoga sighed as he spoke, but he didn't dare defy Daldora's words. ...No, he couldn't.

Because Kuto understood that, above all else, he was a researcher.

Even if attacked, he didn't believe he could handle it like the battle just witnessed.

He knew full well he'd be killed without putting up a fight.

Given that, he obediently followed Daldora's advice to retreat to a safe place. Just as he was about to step back inside the shop...

"Danger!"

The cry echoed around them, and suddenly, both Daldora and Kuto were shoved forcefully from behind.

"Gah!"

Then came a scream.

No, it wasn't just one scream.

"Gyaaah!"

"Guh!"

"Gah!"

Screams of agony rose one after another.

Kutoga spun around in a flash. There, several humans were clutching their bodies and screaming.

Among those screaming was Daldora.

Shocked by the sight, Kuto-ga frantically checked his own body.

He wondered if perhaps he too had the reason the others were screaming... if an arrow was piercing his body.

In the current state of excitement, he wondered if he might not even notice an arrow if it were there.

Driven by that thought, he frantically checked his entire body, but fortunately, he found no injuries anywhere.

...Though, this was purely down to Kutga's good fortune.

After all, if Narsus hadn't pushed him aside just now, that arrow would have pierced Kuto's head.

"Kyaaah!"

"Hey! What's going on? What happened?!"

"They say Daldora was attacked!"

"Who would do such a thing!?"

"Who knows!"

Ignoring the screams rising again from the crowd, Daldora and Kutoaga hurried back inside the shop.

Watching them go, Narsas quickly scanned his surroundings, clutching the wound he'd taken shielding Daldora.

Over ten arrows had flown in.

Considering they were fired almost simultaneously, this wasn't the work of just one or two people.

Given that, he was determined to capture at least one or two of them.

With that resolve, he scanned the area... but all he saw were the residents of Gosh. Not a single person appeared to be holding a bow.

"...What's going on?"

Given the scale of the attack, he was certain someone must be hiding somewhere.

But contrary to that feeling, there was no sign of the attackers anywhere.

"Damn it, did they get away?"

All Narsus could do was spit out that curse before rushing back to the shop to check on his employer's safety.
