"Whoa! Wait, wait, wait!"

The cry echoed as a snowball flew through the air.

Dodging the snowball aimed at him, he threw one back as if to say, "That's payback!"

Rei and Set watched the scene of over ten children divided into two teams having a snowball fight.

The snow hut they had built a little while ago still stood in the center of the square.

The children were using the location of that snow hut as a dividing point, each team fighting their own snowball fight.

...Naturally, it was Rei who taught the children the rules of snowball fights.

While they had always played by throwing snowballs at each other, Rei had established clear rules for the game.

That said, Rei didn't actually know the precise rules of snowball fights either.

He'd only vaguely watched the scenes when an international snowball fight competition aired on TV, so it was only natural he didn't remember the finer details.

Still, having seen it on TV, he knew the broad... really, very rough outline of the rules.

Like, you plant a flag in each other's territory, and whoever attacks it with snowballs wins. That's about the extent of it.

Of course, getting hit by a snowball meant you were out—that was a given.

Rei had never actually played, but she imagined it was similar to survival games.

"Aaargh! I got hit, so why is Lucis still here?!"

"Well, I blocked it with my hand! That's obvious!"

"But you still got hit by the snowball! That's no good!"

"I blocked it, so it's fine!"

That exchange reached Rei's ears.

And naturally, the two arguing then asked Rei for his opinion on the matter.

"Rei-nii, if you block a snowball with your hand, it's okay, right?"

"Rei, even if you block it with your hand, it still hit you, so that's no good, right?"

Asked this by both of them, Rei couldn't answer since he didn't remember the exact rules.

"Ah, well... I think the rule was that if you got hit by a snowball, it counted as a strike, so I guess blocking it with your hands still counts as a strike."

"See? See?"

"Muuuuuuuu!"

Watching this exchange, Rei pulled a sandwich out of his Misty Ring, put it in his mouth, and handed another one to Set.

"Gurrr."

The sandwich, generously filled with stewed oak meat, was too substantial to be considered a mere snack in terms of satisfying hunger.

But for Rei and Set, it was just the right amount to eat as a snack.

"Come to think of it, I wonder what happened to those meat buns?"

"Grrr?"

Several days had passed since they'd shared the recipe for meat buns with Golden Bread Shop.

Unlike the udon noodles Rei had actually made before, meat buns... or rather, steamed buns, were something Rei had never made.

All he knew was the general method and the basic idea.

Still, Rodrigo, the cafeteria owner, was brimming with confidence that he could refine the meat bun-like thing they'd made together into a proper dish.

Considering that even timid-looking Rodrigo spoke with such conviction, it seemed reasonable to have high hopes.

"Want to stop by later?"

Since this square faced the main street, the Golden Bread Shop couldn't be too far away.

In fact, when he'd been dragged here by kids playing in the snow, he'd seen the Golden Bread Shop open for business.

"Grrr..."

But at Rei's suggestion, Set growled, sounding reluctant.

Probably because the meat bun substitute Rei had fed him hadn't been very tasty.

Rei gently stroked Set, who looked slightly dissatisfied.

"There, don't worry. If the cook's skills are solid, the meat bun won't be a fake anymore."

"Guru?"

Set tilted his head slightly, asking, "Really?" Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah. He's a skilled cook, so he must be doing well enough to run a place on the main street."

"Huh. So, Rei, did you feed Set-chan something that wasn't tasty?"

"No, it's not that it's bad, you know? It's just still in the trial phase... Huh?"

Noticing the person who had joined the conversation as if it were the most natural thing, Rei turned his gaze that way.

There stood Milein, her gaze fixed somewhat intently on Rei.

"Where did you come from?"

"Where from? I was just passing by and saw Set-chan, so I popped my head in. So, Rei. You said it was a test dish, but it's not... bad, is it?"

"It's not... bad. It's just a bit lacking, that's true."

"...Really?"

At those words, Milein shot Rei a skeptical look.

It was frustrating... really frustrating, but she knew Set adored Rei.

If she had to choose between me and Rei, she'd undoubtedly pick Rei.

That's precisely why she could predict that even if Rei served him something unpalatable, Set might protest a little, but he wouldn't genuinely dislike it.

...Though, knowing Rei liked Set just as much as Set liked him, she wasn't truly worried about that happening.

"Rei, is it true you bullied Set?"

"...And you, where did you even come from?"

Rei addressed Johanna with the same words he'd just spoken to Milein.

Being in the middle of town, Rei hadn't been paying close attention to detecting auras or the like.

Also, the fact that she didn't sense any hostility or wariness towards Rei was probably why she hadn't noticed the two of them appear.

(You'd think she'd be hostile after hearing I made Set eat that fake meat bun. Wait, does anything involving Set surpass my abilities? ...The fact I can't say that's impossible is pretty amazing.)

Considering the affection Milein and Johanna normally showed toward Set, it somehow made sense to Rei, which he found strange.

"So, I wasn't bullying him, okay?"

Even though Rei said that, the two of them weren't about to believe him right away.

Just as Rei was about to speak again...

"Ah! It's Milein-nee and Johanna-nee! Hey, hey, you two should join our snowball fight too!"

A child who had spotted them sharply called out.

Milein is the leader of the Rank C party, Scorching Wind, and could be called a rising star among the younger generation.

...While she's been overshadowed by Rei's arrival, she's still undeniably skilled among the younger generation.

Johanna, while not as famous as Milein, still held a certain level of recognition among those in the know as one of the leaders of a group of capable adventurers—the former members of the Roving Squad.

That said, the feud between Milein and Johanna over Set is widely known, so that's probably what makes them famous.

In fact, the reason the children having a snowball fight knew about Milein and Johanna was because of their rivalry over Set.

"Huh? Me?"

"...Hmm. Well, fine. If Milein says she doesn't have the confidence to beat me and runs away, I might let her off the hook."

"Mm. Fine. I'll do it. But in return, if I win, I get the right to play with Set-chan."

"Fine. But don't forget, if I win, the same goes for me."

Sparks were definitely flying between the two as they argued.

Don't just decide something like that without even asking my opinion.

Rei wanted to say that, but she fell silent, overwhelmed by their intensity.

"Guru?"

If Set had seemed displeased, Rei would have firmly put a stop to their arbitrary demands.

But Set showed no sign of disliking their behavior; he seemed to welcome the chance to play with them wholeheartedly.

It seemed strange for Rei to stop something Set clearly wanted... Thinking this, Rei ultimately remained silent, watching their exchange.

"Alright, we absolutely cannot lose to the other team! We have to win!"

Milein gave a pep talk to the children on her team.

Even if they couldn't grasp the full depth of her words, the kids naturally weren't thrilled about losing the snowball fight.

Thus, their team spirit surged, and their motivation soared.

"Everyone understands, right? If we lose to Milein's team, we'll be too embarrassed to show our faces outside! So we absolutely have to win. Got it?"

Naturally, Johanna also rallied her teammates, determined not to be outdone by Milein.

Johanna's team, determined not to lose to the other side, shouted out their own rallying cries.

With these two teams clashing, a referee was obviously needed...

"And that means I'm the referee."

Naturally, Rei, the only neutral person present, ends up being the referee.

"Grrrrrrr"

Beside Rei, for some reason, Set purred happily.

For Set, who had almost no experience playing in the snow with everyone like this, even if he was the referee alongside Rei, participating in the snowball fight was probably a joy.

"Alright, here are the rules. If you get hit by a snowball, you're considered dead and must leave the square. Even if you block it with your hands or feet, getting hit disqualifies you immediately. Never put anything inside the snowballs, and don't pack them too tightly."

The last part was directed not at the children, but at Milein and Johanna, their faces clearly visible.

...What was frightening was how disappointed both of them looked at Rei.

Of course, he probably didn't intend to throw snowballs made that way at the children...

But conversely, Rei felt that if it were Milein and Johanna as opponents, they'd likely use every means at their disposal without hesitation.

In fact, perhaps they both intended to do just that, because they nodded regretfully in response to Rei's words.

"Got it? Seriously, don't pull stunts like that, okay? If you try to break the rules, you won't get to play with Set. Right, Set?"

"GRRR!"

Set growled in agreement.

Once the fighting started, Set would mercilessly take down his enemies with his formidable physical strength.

But when not fighting, Set was fundamentally a pacifist.

That's precisely why Set warns the two not to do anything dangerous in this snowball fight.

Though the two often fought over Set, to him they were kind people who paid attention to him, played with him, and gave him food.

Naturally, he wanted to stop them from fighting.

"Ugh, fine."

"If Set-chan says so..."

Reluctantly, they both agreed... Seeing this, Rei spoke up again.

"Alright then, fight fairly and squarely. No dirty tricks. ...Begin!"

With those words, the Milein faction and the Johanna faction each threw snowballs at their opponents.

Since blocking with hands or feet upon impact meant instant disqualification, they focused solely on dodging immediately after throwing.

But not everyone could manage it perfectly, and naturally, some were hit by snowballs.

"Ow!"

"Freezing!"

"Whoa!"

"Whoa, what!?"

With cries that were neither screams nor cheers, the children hit by snowballs streamed out of the square one after another, heading straight for Rei.

People walking down the main street cast curious glances, wondering what was going on, but those in the snowball fight had no time to spare for such attention.

Show even the slightest opening, and snowballs would fly at them one after another.

Milein and Johanna were especially focused on targeting each other, but even when they weren't aiming at the children, some would get caught in the crossfire and hit by snowballs.

"Dammit, you're tough!"

"You're the stubborn one!"

Shouting like this, Milein and Johanna threw snowballs at each other, dodged them, and in the midst of their evasive movements, scooped up snow piled on the ground to form new snowballs.

They battle to hit each other with snowballs, using their adventurer skills to a remarkable degree... or perhaps pointlessly.

Children around them were also throwing snowballs, but as adventurers, their battle was truly in a league of its own.

"Take this!"

"Not a chance!"

The flashy snowball fight continued... until finally, each decided this would be their last shot and hurled their snowballs with all their might.

They both dodged the thrown snowballs... just as they were about to connect the next one, Rei's voice echoed around them.

"That's enough!"

Why!? Milein and Johanna shot displeased glances at Rei... but when Rei looked behind them, directly at the flags behind Milein and Johanna, snowballs thrown by both sides had hit both flags.

"This match is a draw."

That's how it ended up.
