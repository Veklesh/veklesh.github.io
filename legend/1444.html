Having mostly cleared away the large debris, Rei headed toward his next task, glancing at the wall section now passable for carriages.

Dozens of mages and spirit mages were already gathered there, preparing to build a new wall.

Even in a Gilm where adventurers often gathered, it was fundamentally rare for this many mages and elemental mages to assemble in one place.

Moreover, even among mages, those proficient in earth magic were not particularly numerous.

The reason so many were gathered here was simply because Gilm had assembled a large number of earth magic users specifically for this expansion project.

...Normally, such an expensive endeavor wouldn't be easily feasible, but this was Gilm.

They likely used the wealth gathered precisely because it's a frontier to assemble this many mages.

(Well, neither side is losing out, so it shouldn't be a problem.)

Rei pondered this as he made his way toward the gathering of mages.

The first to notice Rei was a mage in his twenties, chatting with companions nearby.

"Huh? Isn't that Crimson? Why's he coming over here?"

At those words, the others around the man also turned puzzled gazes toward Rei.

Still, while their gazes were puzzled, they held no trace of disgust or avoidance.

Given how few wizards there were, it wouldn't have been strange for them to harbor a certain elitist mindset, believing themselves chosen ones. But in Rei's case, as a wizard, he had achieved numerous... indeed, an unusually large number of accomplishments in recent years.

Moreover, it was common knowledge what kind of person Rei was... Rumors spread that he would casually eliminate anyone who opposed him, nobles included. Given that, no one present would even consider antagonizing Rei.

Fundamentally, Rei wasn't seen as a pure mage, but rather as a magic warrior.

Since casting spells fundamentally requires incantations, it was obvious that anyone opposing the mage warrior Rei would be attacked while they were chanting.

Amidst this, two figures gradually approached Rei.

Though one was a dwarf and the other a human, both had stern, distinctly artisan-like features, something Rei recognized immediately.

"Hey, you're Rei, huh?"

"Yeah, I am."

"...I hear you can use earth magic over a pretty wide area. Is that true?"

A rustle.

The dwarf's words to Rei caused a stir among those around them.

That Rei, reputed to specialize in fire magic, was actually quite skilled in earth magic too... they probably couldn't believe it.

Or perhaps they simply didn't want to believe it.

In reality, Rei was a mage specialized in fire magic, just as most other mages—those who hadn't gathered some information about him—assumed.

But Rei possessed a means to overturn that perception.

A means created through Beast Magic: the Death Scythe.

"Ah. Though it's not quite on par with fire magic, mind you. And the spells I can use are limited."

When Rei mentioned his magic was limited, those around him let out subtle sighs of relief.

The idea that a mage renowned enough to have an alias could be more skilled than them in magic other than that alias was a situation that caused a little... no, quite a lot of unease. It was only natural for them to feel envy and jealousy.

Though, to be fair, the mere fact he had a nickname meant plenty of people were jealous anyway.

"How limited, specifically?"

"Hmm... Can you see that pile of rubble over there?"

Rei pointed to the rubble about fifty meters away.

"Yeah."

"Taking me as the center, I can raise or lower the ground within a range from here to where that pile of rubble is, to about the height of my chest."

"Huh, that's quite something."

The dwarf man nodded, seeming impressed by Rei's words.

While the actual range of this magic wasn't as outstanding as Rei's fire spells, it still possessed more than average effectiveness.

And above all else...

"Also, I'm not bragging, but my mana reserves are quite substantial. I mean, I could keep the same spell going for hours without any problem at all."

At the words that came from Rei's mouth, everyone listening to the conversation wore expressions of utter astonishment.

It was only natural. Normally, using that much magic several times in succession would drain a large amount of mana, leading to mana depletion and loss of consciousness.

The two craftsmen who had asked Rei had assumed he could only use it a few times a day... well, maybe not just once a day, but certainly not more than that.

But if he could use it repeatedly, there was no doubt the pace of the expansion work would increase significantly.

"Is that true?"

"Yeah. But with my skill, I can only move it up to about chest level. I just can't get it any higher than that."

"...I see. We need to dig deeper to lay the wall foundations, but... well, even if it's only up to your chest, having that area already dug out would be a huge help. Could you show me how it works? Let's see..."

The dwarf craftsman paused, glanced around, then spoke.

"First, level out the ground around here. It's been quite a while since Gilm was built. The passage of time has inevitably left the ground rough. Even if we're going to build a structure now, it's better to have level ground."

"Understood. ...Then I'll get started on that."

Nodding at the dwarf's words, Rei thrust the stone tip of the Death Scythe he'd drawn from the Misty Ring into the ground and activated his skill.

"Terrain Manipulation."

With that word, the area within a fifty-meter radius centered on Rei was smoothed out exactly as he envisioned.

Those nearby who had been watching with interest to see what kind of magic Rei would use were first surprised by the spell being cast without any incantation, then startled as the ground beneath their feet shifted slightly.

Several seconds passed... and before they knew it, the ground within a fifty-meter radius centered on Rei was perfectly level.

(It's a bit pointless to think about it after the fact, but even if I level the ground like this now, with all the people passing through here for Gilm's expansion and moving heavy loads... doesn't that kind of defeat the purpose?)

He tilted his head, pondering this, but then reconsidered: At least it meant people wouldn't trip and fall while moving around, and they wouldn't waste stamina on pointless energy loss from subtle undulations.

"Whoa..."

The people around him let out gasps of astonishment at the speed with which Rei performed the action.

The people gathered here were experts in earth magic, earth elemental magic... a few even possessed skills to manipulate earth itself. In short, they were specialists in matters of the earth.

Even to their eyes, what Rei had just done was astonishing.

Of course, there were others who could do something similar, but even so, as Rei had said, they couldn't do it repeatedly like that... and if asked if they could smooth the ground this perfectly, it would be difficult to nod with confidence.

"Well, that's about it. Are you satisfied?"

"Yes, quite. ...Doesn't this mean we need to rethink the process somewhat?"

The dwarf man asked his colleague beside him.

Hearing this, the man returns a nod with a small smile playing at the corners of his mouth.

"Yeah. This is a happy accident. ...But if we move up the wall construction here, will the materials be ready in time?"

"...Hard to say. We've built in some margin, but... I'll tell them to hurry as much as possible."

"Do that. I'll have them report this up to the lord."

Is it really that important?

Rei wondered for a moment, but even a one-meter difference in initial subsidence held significant meaning in the ongoing wall extension project.

Of course, considering the subsequent work, it wasn't something Rei could resolve entirely on his own... Still, for those entrusted with this project, it was almost like a blessing.

In this project, the sooner it finished, the higher the wages would be, and those who settled in Gilm afterward would receive considerable preferential treatment.

...Naturally, they'd been warned upfront that sloppy workmanship wouldn't be tolerated no matter how fast they finished... In fact, they could even face criminal charges.

Considering those circumstances, the top priority is to build a solid, sturdy wall as quickly as possible.

"Anyway, I understand the ground situation now. ...Just to confirm again, this magic can be cast repeatedly, right? From what I saw, you didn't seem to be chanting either."

"That's not an issue."

Rei nodded firmly at the dwarf's words.

Seeing Rei like that, the dwarf must have judged it wasn't a lie.

After a moment's thought, the dwarf spoke up, loud enough for everyone present to hear.

"Listen up, everyone! To move this construction quickly and reliably, Rei will dig the hole first. Since he can only dig so far, the rest of you will finish it! Got it!?"

True to his dwarf artisan nature, his voice carried clearly throughout the area.

"However, it's pointless for you to be idle while Rei is using magic! Each team should follow the instructions of the craftsman present and proceed with the work within reasonable limits!"

The command reached the ears of every gathered mage and spirit mage.

The gazes those individuals directed at Rei held various hues.

Gratitude, jealousy, interest.

These three were the main ones, but there were also a few who directed looks of hatred.

And naturally, Rei noticed those who were directing such glances at him...

(Why am I hated this much?)

Unable to understand why he was so intensely disliked, Rei was filled with doubt.

Of course, Rei wasn't so naive as to think he wasn't resented by anyone.

Humans, no matter what they do, will always have those who dislike them or resort to ugly actions out of jealousy toward the successful.

A prime example was Bornter... the former head of the Azoth Trading Company, who had tried to take Set and the magic items from Rei before.

Still, even if people viewed Rei that way, Rei didn't seem inclined to take any particular action.

He believed that as long as they merely watched him, it posed no real problem.

...However, if they do take action, he intends to respond appropriately.

In any case, those capable of using earth magic, earth elemental magic, or earth-related skills—including Rei—head toward their assigned locations.

"Well then, Rei. Do your best."

Marina, assigned to this group, called out to Rei before moving off. Feilma didn't call out either, but gave a light wave in greeting before leaving.

"Alright, Rei. Count on you."

"Yeah, got it."

Given that the current area of Gilm had expanded by fifty percent, its size was vast.

Even with Rei's skill—or more accurately, Death Scythe's skill—it would take time to sink the ground around such a vast expanse of land.

For the craftsmen tasked with building the walls, their honest hope was that this unexpected ally, Rei, would push himself as hard as possible. Yet they also understood the danger of overtaxing him.

Though he didn't fully grasp the dwarf's feelings, Rei simply replied, "I'm fine," and immediately began moving.

Those desperately hauling rubble watched Rei intently, their eyes fixed on his figure even amidst their frantic work.

Even those who knew little about Rei had seen him store massive debris into his Misty Ring.

They had also witnessed the sight of him leveling the ground over a wide area with terrain manipulation.

Given that, they simply couldn't bring themselves to ignore Rei.

Among them were those who watched with anticipation, wondering what he would show them next.

(Not like I'm the entertainment guy or anything.)

Feeling those gazes, Rei sighed and muttered to himself.

He decided it was nothing compared to the hateful stares from the mages earlier, and headed straight for the location he'd been directed to first.

There, craftsmen and mages were looking at Rei with hopeful eyes.

"Alright, let's get started. How should we proceed?"

"...Look. See that line drawn over there?"

Rei nodded at the craftsman's words.

A fairly thick line was indeed drawn there.

"That's where the wall will go. I need you to lower the ground around it, leaving some space. ...Can you do it?"

Laying a confident smile upon his face, Rei responded to the craftsman's request.
