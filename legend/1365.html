Rei and Set landed outside the forest and cautiously entered the woods.

Their target was the object reflecting light through the gaps in the trees they had seen from above.

A place where something clearly left behind by humans existed.

Rei's hunch... soon became clear evidence, appearing before them.

"This... is a longsword? Anything else..."

Looking around, Rei found a stick stuck diagonally into the ground.

For a moment, Rei thought it might be one of the disposable spears he'd stuck into the ground to gauge how far the forest stretched, but this location was clearly different from where he'd been yesterday.

Moreover, the spear Rei had stuck in the ground was vertical, while this stick was angled.

Intrigued by the stick, he picked it up... and pulled.

It came out of the ground much more easily than Rei had expected. Seeing the spearhead at the tip, he realized for the first time that the stick he had been holding was actually a spear.

"A spear, huh... But considering it was stuck here, did a fight break out?"

"Gururuu"

As Rei murmured, Set suddenly let out a cry.

Rei turned his gaze in that direction and saw the remains of a campfire on the ground.

Clear traces suggesting someone had camped there.

But, naturally, there was no sign of anyone who had camped there.

Still, to Rei's eyes, it was an undeniable fact that whoever had camped here hadn't simply left; they had been attacked by someone.

The spear unnaturally stuck in the ground was one clue, but the biggest sign was the belongings left behind.

For a moment, just a fleeting moment, he wondered if they might have left temporarily for some reason. But he immediately shook his head, dismissing the thought.

The fact was, the fire pit clearly showed the fire had been out for quite some time.

Given that, there was no doubt that whoever... or whoever had camped here had been attacked and killed in the forest.

"But why on earth would anyone camp in this forest? ...Was it really for the merit points?"

The guild had publicly stated that Treant Forest contained no monsters or animals, yet many still questioned it.

Winter aside, this forest didn't exist at all in autumn, yet the moment spring arrived, a forest of this size appeared.

It's only natural that many people are suspicious.

Still, most people avoid Treant Forest since it doesn't pay well, and those who do come usually turn back quickly.

At the very least, no one was camping out like the group Rei was watching.

(Well, I don't know everything about this forest either, so I can't rule out the possibility that other adventurers might be inside.)

If clear traces of a campsite remain, like this time, finding them isn't difficult.

But those accustomed to camping are exceptionally skilled at concealing their traces.

At the very least, spotting camp traces while flying overhead would be difficult.

"...For now, I don't know who the adventurer was that camped here, but it's certain they suffered some kind of attack."

From Slesha's words, Rei could also guess that the attackers were likely Treant.

Still, as far as he could see around them, there was no sign of Treant anywhere.

"Set, can you tell if there are any Treants around?"

"Grrr? Grr, grrr... grrr..."

Set responded to Rei's words with an ambiguous attitude.

Normally, he would clearly indicate his presence or absence with his demeanor.

But right now, Set was looking around uncertainly.

Even Rei, whose senses were sharper than ordinary humans, and Set, whose senses were even more developed than Rei's, couldn't detect any scent even when using his Enhanced Sense of Smell skill.

Even Rei was surprised by this.

"You don't know? Set?"

"Grrr..."

At Rei's words, Set bowed his head slightly in apology.

Seeing the apologetic light in his round eyes, Rei gently stroked Set's head.

"Don't worry about it. I'm not blaming you for this. Besides, this whole thing has plenty of mysteries. In a way, the fact that even Set's keen sense of smell couldn't find the enemy is actually a valuable insight."

Rei had been saved countless times by Set's keen sense of smell.

But the fact that even that keen sense couldn't detect the enemy's presence was information rich in meaning.

Deciding this incident needed to be reported to the guild, Rei began stowing his belongings into the Misty Ring.

Since no one was left at the site anyway, taking the luggage wouldn't be a problem. But in this case, the luggage would likely be used to identify who had been camping here.

"If only there were a guild card... but that's unlikely."

Guild cards served as identification and were extremely important.

If lost, a fee would be charged for reissuance, however small.

Because of this, adventurers often keep their guild cards close at hand, tucked into their pockets or somewhere similar.

That said, it's just the majority—not everyone.

Some find carrying it a hassle and leave it with a companion, while others simply leave it packed away in their luggage.

Rei also doesn't carry his Guild Card directly on his person.

...Though in Rei's case, since it's stored inside the Misty Ring, it's far safer than carrying it around normally.

In any case, for that reason, even if you searched the luggage, it wouldn't be immediately clear whose it belonged to.

Still, if you examine the remaining tools in detail, it's not uncommon to figure out which adventurer they belong to.

Many adventurers coordinate matching gear within their party.

If such items are present, it would be immediately clear whose they belong to.

"...For now, this area should be fine."

He stowed the spear in the Misty Ring, thinking it might provide some clue, then scanned the surroundings again.

There were no particular bloodstains, and even if some kind of monster—probably a Treant—had attacked, there were no flesh fragments either.

Rei saw an ordinary forest.

But last night... or maybe not last night, but it was definitely here that an adventurer was attacked by a monster.

(Considering where this campsite was, it was probably last night.)

Even knowing that, there was nothing he could do now.

Since the situation was already over, all Rei could do was take the belongings back to the guild.

If someone like Slesha had survived, perhaps there might be someone to claim the belongings... but as an adventurer, Rei knew full well that possibility was slim.

Still, just in case...

After finishing all her errands, Rei mounted Set and rode out of the forest for now.

His destination was the spot where Rei had cut down the tree yesterday.

If he was just going to cut down trees, he could have done it right where he was.

But to see how much the forest had eroded since yesterday... returning to yesterday's spot was still the quickest way to check.

Set seemed to remember yesterday's location as well, heading there without any sign of hesitation.

They flew a short distance through the sky... and before they knew it, they had arrived near the spot where Rei had cut down the tree yesterday.

Of course, the traces had been completely erased by the forest's encroachment.

"Hmm, this is about fifteen meters, maybe? ...Considering it was only about ten meters yesterday, that's roughly a fifty percent increase. ...That's quite a difference. Could it be... those adventurers are the reason?"

The campsite remains he'd just discovered.

The adventurers who were attacked by the forest there... perhaps they became nourishment for the forest.

The thought crossed his mind, but it was undoubtedly baseless.

Even if it were true, there was no way to investigate it.

(Unless you used something like a sinner to investigate, but that's a different story entirely.)

Thinking like that, Rei confirmed how far the forest had spread and proceeded to the next task.

...Yes. Coming to this forest meant investigating how far it had spread, but equally important was the request to cut down its trees.

The more he cut down, the greater his reward would be.

Given that, this was essentially a bonus for Rei.

Normally, cutting down a tree would require swinging an axe repeatedly.

But in Rei's case...

"Haah!"

He channeled magic into the Death Scythe he drew from the Misty Ring and swung it through the air.

The first tree struck by the diagonal flash of the Death Scythe showed no sign of movement.

But... soon, a warm spring breeze blew. Perhaps triggered by this, the tree gradually shifted under its own weight and fell to the ground.

Having been cut diagonally, the sharp end pierced the ground... but the next instant, it toppled over, leaning against a nearby tree.

All that remained was the tree trunk, its sharply pointed end facing upward due to the diagonal cut.

"Could this be... kind of dangerous?"

he muttered, but then again, only Rei would come here in the first place.

This spot wasn't near the road, so the chances of anyone coming here were pretty slim.

Those coming from the road would end up reaching the area where Rei and the others had found the campsite earlier.

"Well, better safe than sorry."

He muttered, channeling magic into the Death Scythe once more before swinging it.

This time, unlike before, it wasn't a diagonal slash but a straight horizontal flash.

The severed section fell to the ground, forming a triangular shape.

He didn't know what this wood would be used for, but he judged that a flat cut surface would be better than a sharp edge.

He then stored the leaning tree and the triangular piece into the Misty Ring before searching for the next tree.

Of course, being deep in the forest, there were plenty of trees nearby.

"Ah, Set. I'll be cutting trees for a while, so you can do whatever you like."

"Grr? ...Grrrr."

Set growled in response to Rei's words and lay down a short distance away.

Normally, it wouldn't be strange for him to be running around playing nearby... but the fact that he was lying down where Rei could see him meant he sensed something unusual in the forest.

Especially since he hadn't been able to sense any monsters at the campsite earlier.

He would protect Rei, whom he loved dearly.

With that resolve, Set lay down, closed his eyes... and focused his senses on scent, sound, and the perception of magic.

Rei, too, seemed to understand Set's feelings. She smiled as she readied her Death Scythe.

"Haah!"

Once more, the Death Scythe's strike, imbued with magic power, was unleashed.

Unlike the first strike, this flash of steel was released straight sideways.

Without any resistance, the Death Scythe's blow cleaved through the tree.

With the Death Scythe still swung through the air, she gently reaches out to the tree... and in the next instant, it vanishes.

It had been stored within the Misty Ring.

All that remained was a stump, cleanly severed sideways.

"I should have cut it down like this from the start. That way, there wouldn't have been any collateral damage either,"

Muttering to himself, Rei's gaze swept over the surrounding trees.

More precisely, the trees that had snapped because the one he'd initially cut at an angle had fallen against them.

They'd recover from this much damage overnight anyway, so there was no real need to worry about it.

He thought that, but still, it was undeniable that various thoughts crossed his mind.

"Well, no use dwelling on it now. After all, this forest devours adventurers. ...Though for some reason, it seems to be stretching toward Gilm more than anywhere else."

Why was it heading toward Gilm? He didn't know, but the fact it wasn't heading toward Abuero meant it was fortunate for Abuero's residents, if not for Gilm.

It was precisely because it was heading toward Gilm that they could remain calm and deal with it for now. ...As Rei saw earlier, it seemed some had come to this forest of their own accord.

Had it been heading toward Abuero instead, the adventurers there likely wouldn't have been able to handle it calmly.

Abuero, situated at the border with the frontier, understood the horrors of the frontier better than anyone.

Considering this a silver lining, Rei channeled magic into the Death Scythe to fell the next tree.
