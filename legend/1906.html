After handing out various souvenirs—including Anesis’s cooking—in addition to the gem containing the healing magic, the conversation turned to what everyone had been doing that day.

Of course, since Rei had already mentioned that he’d watched the dissection of the Giganto Turtle and then searched for the magic item controlling the kobolds, the conversation mainly revolved around the others.

“I didn’t have much to do today. It seems Byune took down quite a few kobolds, though. …Still, the dissection of the Giganto Turtle was quite a sight. You don’t come across monsters that big very often, so I think you should check it out if you get the chance.”

Hearing Vihera’s words, the others who hadn’t seen the butchering site looked slightly intrigued.

Elena, Marina, and Ara weren’t exactly seeing a giant monster for the first time either.

Elena had been called upon to hunt a Kraken before, and Marina had encountered various monsters during her days as an adventurer, many of which were gigantic.

Still, it was true that they all wanted to see the dissection of the Giganto Turtle that Rei had defeated.

“Yeah, I guess so. I’m free tomorrow, so maybe I’ll go take a look. What about you two, Elena and Ara?”

“That’s true. We should probably keep an eye on things to make sure the Noble Faction doesn’t start meddling. …Besides, Father would probably be surprised if I told him about the Giganto Turtle incident.”

Hearing Elena’s words, Ara nodded in agreement.

“I’ll come along too. Fortunately, I don’t have anything specific planned for tomorrow.”

To be precise, there are plenty of people who wish to meet with Elena.

But none of those requests were urgent; most were simply from people who wanted to get to know Elena.

For nobles in the Noble Faction, simply being acquainted with Elena allows them to hold their heads high to some extent among the lower ranks.

Even among the Royal Faction and the Neutral Faction, simply being acquainted with Elena grants a certain degree of influence.

Elena, known by the nickname “Princess General,” possesses that much influence.

For that very reason, a veritable motley crew of people request an audience with her… and, naturally, the vast majority of them are promptly turned away by Ara.

If there were truly important reasons, Ara would have conveyed them to Elena, but since there were no such people, it was only natural to conclude that there would be no particular problem with going to see the Giganto Turtle tomorrow.

“I see. If there’s no problem, I’ll go see it tomorrow. …Rei, is that okay with you?”

“I don’t mind that, but I’ll just be keeping an eye on things at the beginning and end—I won’t be able to pay much attention to Elena, okay?”

“Hmm. If we’re looking for the magic item controlling the kobolds, I suppose that makes sense. That’s a bit of a shame, though… What about Vihera and Byune?”

“Me? I… well, it seems Byune wants to take on a quest to defeat monsters at the Giganto Turtle dissection site, so I suppose I’ll just tag along with her.”

Hearing that, Elena realized that Vihera and Byune were currently at the site where the Giganto Turtle was being dismantled, and she looked slightly pleased.

“So, what was Marina doing today? I was sure she’d come over to the Giganto Turtle.”

“Oh, me? Actually, she’s not from around here, but there’s a Dark Elf who came to Gilm. I was teaching her a little bit about how to use spirit magic.”

“A Dark Elf? That’s rare… or maybe not.”

Rei called it rare in response to Marina’s words, but immediately contradicted himself.

In fact, Rei knew one elf himself, and even if they weren’t acquaintances, he’d seen several elves and dark elves in Gilm.

Besides, with the expansion under construction, more people have been gathering here than before.

It’s winter now, so there are fewer people than in the fall, but even so, the number of people in Gilm is higher than usual.

So it wasn’t particularly strange if there were new elves or dark elves among them.

“That’s true. But that girl isn’t very good at spirit magic. She’s quite skilled with a bow, though.”

“...Just to be clear, you’re not using Marina as the benchmark for that ‘skill’ in spirit magic, are you?”

Rei knows a few people who use spirit magic, but even among them, Marina’s skill is head and shoulders above the rest—no, it’s far beyond that.

From Marina’s perspective, it’s likely that almost everyone else seems to be unskilled at elemental magic.

But as Rei thought this, Marina picked up a piece of cheese from the table and moved it back and forth.

It was a perfectly ordinary gesture, but the way Marina performed it made her intense feminine allure all the more apparent.

That said, having known Marina for quite some time, Rei had grown somewhat accustomed to—or perhaps even immune to—her allure.

“I’m not doing anything silly like that. I’m simply teaching her because she asked me to show her how to use spirit magic.”

Marina said this without seeming to have any sense of guilt whatsoever.

Relieved by this, Rei continued the conversation.

“Still, she’s from a different settlement than you, Marina? I’d like to visit that settlement someday, too.”

“Dark Elf settlements must be a rarity for you, Rei. I hear there are all sorts of interesting magical items there, too.”

The fact that she responded to Rei’s words alone likely meant Marina understood his intentions.

When you mention a Dark Elf settlement, it wouldn’t be strange to find magical items… or the materials used to make them.

Given Rei’s interests, it was only natural that he’d want to visit that Dark Elf settlement.

“That’s true. Plus, I’m curious about the food there, too.”

“...Orcs, huh?”

She must have recalled the time they visited Marina’s settlement. Vihera shot her a slightly exasperated look… while Byune, in a manner completely unlike her attitude when discussing gem magic, turned an expectant gaze toward Rei.

The last time they visited the settlement where Marina grew up, there were quite a few orcs in the vicinity.

For Rei, orcs were incredibly delicious monsters.

……Not “delicious” in the sense that their materials or magic stones fetch high prices, but “delicious” in the literal sense of eating them.

Rei’s Misty Ring still contained a large amount of orc meat, but since they were so plentiful, he ate them frequently, and naturally, his consumption was high.

Although he still has plenty of meat on hand, Rei’s true feeling was that if he could restock, he’d like to do so.

Orcs are stronger than goblins or kobolds even when alone, and above all, they possess the intelligence to coordinate with their comrades.

For men, they’re a troublesome foe to fight, and for women, they’re such a despicable presence that it wouldn’t be strange for them to kill an orc on sight.

Despite all that, to Rei, orcs were nothing more than a source of delicious meat.

“That’s true. Orcs are a nuisance to the dark elves, too, aren’t they?”

“I won’t deny that. But since orcs can be used as food once they’re killed, if we’re going to hunt them, we should probably get the settlement’s approval first. …Oh, I wonder if it’s about time.”

Mid-conversation, Marina’s gaze shifted to a kiln a short distance away.

It was the same kiln Rei had taken out of the Misty Ring as usual, but today, the ingredients being roasted inside were different.

……Because it was a piece of meat from the Giganto Turtle they had just butchered today.

Even for Marina, who’s been an adventurer for a long time, this was her first time eating Giganto Turtle meat.

Naturally, everyone was deeply curious about the taste… but with the gem magic incident, they’d completely forgotten about it.

However, an appetizing aroma wafted from the cauldron, as if to signal that the food was ready.

Even though they had already eaten a fair amount, everyone turned their gaze toward the oven.

“I didn’t know how best to cook it, so I just seasoned it with salt and grilled it as a start… but it smells really delicious, doesn’t it?”

Saying that, Marina used spirit magic to pull the Giganto Turtle meat out of the oven.

Placed on the table, the meat consists of several chunks about the size of a fist.

“I’ve never eaten turtle before, so I don’t know what it tastes like… but it looks really delicious again.”

Looking at the meat on the table, Rei spoke honestly.

Seasoned only with salt, and though a few herbs were used, it was a very simple dish.

Still, perhaps because the exquisite aroma of the meat itself mingles with the scent in the air, the dish looks incredibly appetizing.

“For now… please go ahead and eat, Rei. Since you brought the meat, it’s only natural that you should be the first to eat it.”

At Marina’s words, the others nodded in agreement, showing no objection.

(Could it be... she’s planning to make me taste-test it... or something? No, surely not...)

Rei thought that for a moment, but regardless, he couldn’t help but be happy that he was allowed to be the first to eat this meat.

She placed a piece of meat on her plate and, without cutting it with a knife, speared it with her fork and took a bite.

The first thing she tasted was the salt and the aroma of herbs.

But in the next instant, the flavor of the meat filled his mouth.

Since it was a turtle, Rei had expected the taste to be similar to chicken, like a reptile such as a snake, but the texture and flavor were, to put it mildly, completely different from chicken.

In fact, it was so different that Rei didn’t even know how to describe it in his own words.

In fact, Rei had eaten all kinds of meat, both while in Japan and since arriving in Elgin.

But even to Rei, the meat in his mouth was clearly different from any he had eaten before.

The texture was different, the way the meat melted in his mouth was different, the flavor of the juices flowing out was different, and the aftertaste lingering in his mouth after he swallowed was different.

(Is the base... chicken, after all? No, but somehow I feel like that’s not quite right either. At the very least, the flavor is incomparable to chicken breeds like Hinai-jidori or Silkie chicken.)

Because of his involvement in cockfighting, Rei’s father raised various breeds of chickens.

Among them were Hinai-dori, famous as one of Japan’s three great native chicken breeds, and Silky Fowl, renowned in China for being highly nutritious and delicious—and, above all, for having black meat.

Since these chickens were raised purely for eating—not for cockfighting—Rei naturally helped slaughter them and even ate them on occasion.

While all of them were undoubtedly delicious, the meat of the Giganto Turtle was on another level entirely—it was so delicious it could be said to be in a different dimension.

“Delicious.”

In the end, that was the only word that came out of Rei’s mouth.

But even with just that one word from Rei, everyone understood that it was truly delicious.

Marina, who had cooked the meal, and the others also served the Giganto Turtle meat onto their plates and began to eat.

...Though only Byune did what Rei did—biting right into it—while the others carefully cut the meat with their knives and brought it to their mouths.

And upon tasting the meat’s flavor and texture, everyone stopped in their tracks.

Even Elena, the daughter of Duke Kerebel, and Vihera, the former princess of the Bestir Empire, were unable to voice their impressions, perhaps because it was a flavor they had never tasted before.

Set and Yellow, who were eating the meat from plates placed on the ground, were also so captivated by the astonishing flavor that they didn’t utter a single sound, focusing entirely on the taste of the meat.

Rei, too, brought the meat to his mouth without saying another word.

Just like that, everyone remains silent, savoring the Giganto Turtle meat... and the silence continues until all the meat is gone.

“...It’s all gone, isn’t it?”

Marina murmurs regretfully as all the meat on the table is gone.

Still, the fact that she didn’t suggest cooking more was likely because she feared that if they did, they would end up eating the meat endlessly.

“That’s true. …At any rate, it’s now clear that Giganto Turtle meat is exceptionally delicious. Let’s just say that knowing that alone was enough for this time.”

Marina nodded at Rei’s words.

Vihera wore a disappointed expression, and surprisingly, even Byune—who rarely showed any emotion—looked disappointed as well.

Elena and Ara also looked a little disappointed, but in the end, they said nothing… and simply savored the freshly baked bread and soup that Rei had just taken out of the Misty Ring.
