Immediately after absorbing the giant cicada's magic stone, Rei had a sudden thought. What if he poured his own magic power into some nearby creature? Wouldn't it become a monster, just like the giant cicada?

While it was possible that the giant cicada had been a monster to begin with, it was equally possible that Rei's magic had transformed it into one.

Convinced he needed to verify this thoroughly, Rei, his thoughts tinged with a hint of hope, immediately took action.

Still, he knew it would be unwise to carelessly create a monster and unleash a being of similar strength and size to that giant cicada within this settlement. So, he took his Death Scythe with him, ready to kill the subject immediately if anything went wrong.

The test subject was an ant found right next to the stable where the magic stone had been absorbed.

(As for ant monsters... there were things like soldier ants, but... well, we'll see how this goes?)

Just like when using magic items, Rei channeled magic power into the ant resting on his palm... but the ant just kept walking around without any noticeable change.

"...Huh..."

Rei had assumed it would turn into a monster, but in reality, there was absolutely no change whatsoever.

He'd expected the ant's body to explode from the magic overload, or at the very least, for it to die—even if not in a spectacular fashion. Yet, the ant on Rei's palm walked around energetically, showing no signs of distress whatsoever.

"I wish it would at least react somehow. ...Maybe the magic wasn't strong enough?"

Muttering, Rei once again channeled magic into the ant crawling across his palm.

But in the end, there was no change in the ant whatsoever.

"Why? That giant cicada definitely turned like that because of my magic..."

The image of the giant cicada's corpse, now inside Misty Ring, flashed through Rei's mind.

Could it be different for the giant cicada and the ant? He wondered, but somehow it didn't feel like that was the case.

(Guess it's not that simple after all.)

If what Rei was attempting succeeded, it would mean he could create as many unknown monsters as he wanted.

That would make absorbing magic stones through beast magic even more efficient than before... That was the thought he'd been holding onto.

Still, if it meant he could attempt something akin to monster farming, Rei couldn't simply give up.

He pondered the difference between the giant cicada and the ant cases, and the first thing that came to mind was the amount of magic power used.

To heal the World Tree, a massive amount of magic—massive to everyone except Rei—was poured into it.

Compared to that magic, the amount channeled into the ants now was practically a drop in the bucket.

(But this settlement has quite a few people with magic-sensing abilities. If I unleashed my full magic power here now...)

That thought crossed Rei's mind, but suddenly his gaze shifted to the New Moon Ring.

The magic item that concealed the immense magical power within Rei.

Only now did Rei recall the New Moon Ring's existence. He looked around once more, confirmed no one was nearby, and then poured magic into the ants scurrying across his palm.

Set prepared himself to intervene immediately if anything went wrong, watching as the light orb flickered.

The magic power poured into the ant increased every few seconds... and when it reached about half the amount he had poured into the World Tree, the ant on Rei's palm stopped moving entirely.

"Hm?"

Puzzled by the ant's state, Rei stopped the flow of magic and poked it with his other hand... but the ant showed no reaction whatsoever.

"Is it... dead?"

"Grrrr..."

Set growled in agreement with Rei's words.

Rei dropped the ant from his palm onto the ground and poked it again.

But the ant showed no sign of movement whatsoever.

Without a doubt, the ant was dead.

"...Why?"

Rei had optimistically expected it to transform into a monster, but the result was the ant's death.

"What do you think?"

He asked Set and the light sphere, but since neither could speak, they couldn't answer.

Still, Rei could sense their confusion.

"Why? That giant cicada was definitely created by my magic... Huh? Cicada? Come to think of it..."

What flashed through Rei's mind as he muttered was the moment the giant cicada had been about to emerge from its shell.

Even as it drove its ten legs into the World Tree's trunk and struggled to emerge, it was still sucking the sap from the World Tree.

(So, could it be that the World Tree's sap and my magic need to combine to create a monster?)

That thought suggested a future that was extremely unappealing to Rei.

If the hypothesis Rei had just conceived was correct, it meant creating a single monster could potentially leave the World Tree near death.

No, if Rei hadn't been channeling his magic into the World Tree, it would have withered completely.

Considering that, no matter how much Rei wanted the magic stones from unknown monsters, he couldn't possibly risk carelessly creating monsters.

"Ugh... I'm glad I didn't fall for it. But then again, there's no such thing as a free lunch, right?"

I'll set aside that dream-like notion for now.

Still, he did want to conduct experiments little by little, just in case something happened.

Sighing, Rei absentmindedly stroked Set's body to pass the time.

As if to encourage him, Set purred softly, while the glowing orb flickered and fluttered around Rei.

About ten minutes passed like this when suddenly, he sensed someone running toward the house from inside.

Well, it wasn't exactly someone. After all, only Rei and Earth currently lived in this house.

Earth, running out of the house with Polo perched on his left shoulder as usual, saw Rei playing with Set by the stable and breathed a sigh of relief.

"Ah, thank goodness. Rei, just checking, but did you happen to have breakfast...?"

Realizing what Earth was worried about, Rei smiled and nodded reassuringly.

"Don't worry. I haven't eaten yet. I just woke up myself a little while ago."

"Oh, okay."

"Pororo?"

At Rei's words, the Pororo perched on Earth's left shoulder chirped in slight confusion, but fortunately, Earth didn't seem to notice.

Being a rare species of carbuncle, Polo had extremely sharp senses. Even though he was half-asleep, he understood Rei was leaving the house.

It was a bit too long ago to call it "just a little while ago," and Polo was probably bothered by that.

Only Rei and Set noticed Poloro's reaction, so the conversation continued.

"Earth seems hungry too. Shall we go eat? At this hour, Elena and the others should be waking up soon."

"Yeah. That'd be great. I've been starving since earlier."

"...After eating that much yesterday, how can you..."

Rei had seen Earth devouring large amounts of food alongside Polo at last night's feast.

Of course, there was plenty of meat to cook within the settlement, so they never lacked for ingredients.

Of course, those ingredients were the monsters that had attacked the settlement.

The carcasses of monsters slain outside the barrier's protective sphere were mostly brought inside the barrier by yesterday.

...Monsters like goblins, which tasted awful, had only their magic stones taken before being disposed of, so only about half of the total attacking monsters were actually brought into the settlement.

Even half was plenty, given the sheer number of monsters that had attacked.

Orc meat, in particular, was a hit with everyone. The cooking was consumed at such a pace that it seemed like every single orc that attacked might be devoured.

"Yesterday was yesterday, today is today. It's not like I can stockpile food or anything. ...Besides, if you're going to say that, what about you, Rei?"

Rei replied to Rei's words with an exasperated tone.

It was only natural Earth would feel that way. From Earth's perspective, Rei was clearly eating far more food than he was.

Knowing alongside Set that there was more than enough meat for this feast, Rei had seized the opportunity to eat as much as possible.

For Earth, who had watched this unfold, being told he hadn't eaten nearly as much as Rei was nothing short of insulting.

"Is that so? Well, I'm hungry too. Let's go eat."

"...You, you..."

Earth sighed and followed after Rei and Set as they left the stables.

Catching up beside Rei, he turned his gaze to the glowing orb floating near her.

"Hey, that light orb is the World Tree's will, right? Why is it staying by Rei's side?"

"I don't know. I was playing with Set in the stable when it just came over on its own. It hasn't done anything bad, so I'm just letting it be."

The orb flickered as if protesting Rei's words, but Rei didn't seem particularly bothered and continued speaking.

"Well, it was stuck like that for so long. Now that it can move again, it's only natural it'd want to roam around freely on its own."

"...Is that so? Is that really why?"

Earth tilted his head, but seeing the light sphere blink in agreement with Rei's words, he couldn't help but accept it.

Even though it was just the same act of flickering, somehow just by watching it, he could tell whether it meant affirmation or denial. He concluded that was probably the power of the World Tree.

"Well, fine..."

Walking beside Rei, Earth turned his gaze toward the World Tree, visible from anywhere in the settlement.

Its form seemed to pierce the heavens, and while it was said this orb represented the World Tree's will, he couldn't help but feel a bit unconvinced.

"More importantly, Earth. What are you doing today?"

"...Today?"

Earth tilted his head, unsure what Rei was talking about.

Rei opened his mouth in exasperation at such an Earth.

"Didn't you originally come to this forest to investigate the disturbance? You said you had business here, right? It's not good to stay in this village forever."

"Oh."

Those words finally reminded Earth why he was here.

Though, since arriving at this village, things had escalated rapidly, and his attention had been completely consumed by that.

"...Ah... Ugh..."

As if various things were suddenly hitting him, Earth let out a sigh from deep within, sounding like he'd truly messed up.

Leaving Earth frozen in thought, Rei headed toward the house Elena and the others were borrowing, accompanied by Set and the light orb.

"Polo-ro-ro-ro!"

Polo, who usually perched on Earth's left shoulder, must have decided he'd miss breakfast if he stayed put. He kicked off Earth's shoulder and landed on Set's back.

"...Earth seems pretty busy with everything. Are you going ahead too?"

"Polo-ro-ro!"

Polo rejoiced on Set's back... And so, Rei and the others left Earth behind, lost in thought.

"Rei! Why'd you leave me behind?!"

As Rei was eating breakfast in the garden with Elena and the others as usual these past few days, that voice suddenly echoed around him.

With a sausage in his mouth, Rei shot Earth a look of utter exasperation.

"Well, even so. You're the one who suddenly stopped in the middle of the road and started deep in thought, right?"

"Well... that's true. But even if you had brought me along..."

"More importantly, have you decided what to do next?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. I'll head back right after breakfast. I need to report on the forest's disturbance too. ...But the cause of the disturbance was that giant cicada, right? So explaining it now probably won't mean much."

Earth sighed, but Marina spoke up to him.

"You're leaving already? Then, as a token of my gratitude, I'd like to give you something... Would that be alright?"

"Uh... sure."

Earth felt slightly intimidated by Marina's sidelong glance.

Receiving Rei's magic during yesterday's battle had clearly been a major factor.

Or rather, perhaps it was because he'd seen how receiving Rei's magic power had made her hyperactive.

Marina continued speaking without seeming to notice Earth's unease.

"Looks like there's no carriage or anything, so something compact is definitely best. You've done so much for the settlement... I suppose magic stones would be good? And we need to give Rei the World Tree materials, as promised long ago."

Suddenly, the orb began to flicker violently, as if expressing some kind of will.

Since the orb represented the World Tree's will, it likely had strong feelings about handing over its own materials.

Yet the flickering didn't convey a negative impression to those around them. Perhaps the orb actually approved of giving away its own materials?

In any case, this marked the end of their days in the settlement with Earth and Rei.

And... the arrival of the final day at the settlement meant that the days Rei spent traveling with Elena were also drawing to a close.
