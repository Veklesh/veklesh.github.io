Having built an earthen wall three meters high—from the outside, at least—Rei and his companions realized they still had plenty of time before nightfall. So, in line with their original plan, they set out to find Marina in order to freeze the wall.

However, since Rei hadn’t asked where she was training in spirit magic, he was naturally at a loss as to where to look.

“Man, this is… what on earth am I supposed to do?”

Rei muttered this, but there was no trace of despair in his tone.

It was a bit of a problem that he couldn’t find her right now, but it was certain that Marina would return to her house in the noble district once night fell.

Given that, if it comes down to it, he can simply explain the situation to Marina tonight and have her freeze the earthen wall tomorrow—that would solve the problem.

...There was a possibility that a kobold could break through the earthen wall, but when Rei touched it, it felt quite sturdy, so it would be difficult to dig through it with his claws.

It’s more likely they’d try to climb the earthen wall using their claws, or jump over it by stepping on their companions or something like that.

(Though, I don’t know if their claws would even dig into the earthen wall.)

With these thoughts in mind, Rei searches for Marina.

Along the way, he buys bread at a food stall, picks up some skewered meat, and gets a hearty soup... You could say that’s just Rei being Rei.

That said, whenever he shops at a stall, he asks the owner if they’ve seen Marina to gather information.

Marina always wears a party dress, and thanks to her beauty and the fact that she’s a dark elf, she stands out quite a bit.

That’s exactly why, if she were nearby, there’s no way he could have missed her.

That was the reasoning behind his search, but unfortunately, he couldn’t spot Marina anywhere.

Instead, I did manage to find a skewered meat stall that used a somewhat unusual sweet-and-sour sauce.

“Gurru!”

“Next stop is there!” Set growled, turning his gaze toward the skewered meat shop a short distance away.

“Wait, didn’t we just eat skewers? Why don’t we try something different?”

“Gurrr…”

Set let out a disappointed growl at Rei’s words.

It wasn’t that Rei disliked skewers.

No, in fact, if you think of it as fast food, skewered meat was a very common dish in Gilm.

That said, while it’s a simple dish—just skewering meat and grilling it—that very simplicity means the quality can vary wildly.

The good places are really delicious, but the bad ones either overcook the meat until it’s tough or, conversely, leave it half-raw.

The chef also has to come up with the seasoning themselves, which means they either have to make the sauce from scratch or buy it from another shop.

Even with something as simple as salt, it can be too salty or, conversely, too bland, so a certain culinary sensibility is required.

...That said, so far, we’ve rarely had a bad experience at the food stalls Set has wanted to try.

Rei suspects that Set’s keen sense of smell—or perhaps his voracious appetite—has something to do with that.

“Anyway, look. There’s a sandwich being sold over there…”

“They look delicious.”

Rei was about to say that when he spotted a dark elf wearing a familiar party dress at the very stall he was looking at.

There was no need to even think about who it was.

Gilm is home to so many people it’s ridiculous to even try counting them, but among them, Rei knew of only Marina as someone who wore a party dress as everyday attire.

“So… the one with her must be that Dark Elf who teaches spirit magic.”

Unlike Marina’s long hair, which reached her back, the dark elf woman’s hair was only shoulder-length.

She seemed to be Marina’s apprentice, but unlike her master, she was wearing leather armor.

In that respect, she didn’t seem to take after her master.

In any case, since she had found the person she was looking for, there was no way she wasn’t going to speak to her now.

「Marina！」

Hearing the voice, Marina took a sandwich from the shopkeeper and turned her gaze toward Rei.

Following Marina’s lead, the other dark elf also looked at Rei.

Her features were more cute than beautiful.

In terms of age, while Marina looked to be in her twenties, the other girl appeared to be around the same age as Rei—mid-teens… or perhaps, depending on the person, even younger.

After exchanging a few words with the dark elf girl, the two of them eventually walked over toward Rei.

“What’s up? You were supposed to be helping with the Giganto Turtle’s dissection, right?”

“No, I had something to do and was looking for Marina.”

“Me?”

“Yeah. The number of kobolds coming in is getting out of hand. Because of that, we’re short-handed for the Giganto Turtle dismantling, too. So, I built an earthen wall in the area connected to the outside, and I’d like you to reinforce it with your spirit magic, if possible. Specifically, I want you to wet it with water and then freeze it.”

At those words, Marina’s face flashed a hint of surprise, but once she understood what Rei wanted to do, she looked convinced.

“I see. …That works out perfectly. Meru, you come with us too. It’s the perfect opportunity to see how your training has paid off. …Oh, come to think of it, I haven’t introduced you yet. I told you, didn’t I? She’s the Dark Elf girl I’m teaching spirit magic to.”

Marina gave a gentle nudge to the back of the dark elf standing next to her—the one called Rei—pushing her forward.

Meru, having been pushed forward, looked in turn at Rei, Set, and Marina standing next to her... and eventually bowed her head toward Rei.

“H-Hello! Nice to meet you! I’m Meru. I’m currently learning spirit magic from Marina! I’m absolutely thrilled to meet you, Rei—someone so important to Marina!”

Perhaps because she’s nervous, Meru’s voice comes out very loud.

In fact, not only Rei and Set, but even the passersby around them turned their gaze toward the group.

...That said, once they realized it was Rei and Marina, most people just shrugged it off as business as usual and kept walking.

A few curious onlookers, however, watch Rei and the others with keen interest, as if wondering if something interesting might happen.

“Ah, yeah. Calm down a bit. If you’ve been around Marina, you shouldn’t be that nervous just because I’m here, right?”

“Oh, what do you mean by that? Are you saying I’m scary or something?”

Marina’s words sounded dissatisfied, but her eyes clearly held an amused smile.

“No, I’d never dare think that Marina, the Priestess of the World Tree, is scary… I’ve never thought such a thing! Marina is the woman I look up to.”

“That’s so sweet of you to say. …Well, as Rei said, there’s really no need to be that nervous. It’s only a short time until spring, but since Meru will be my apprentice, you’ll eventually learn how to get along with Rei, Set… and others like Vihera, Byune, Elena, and Ara.”

Hearing Marina’s words, Rei, who had been listening, realized once again just what an impressive group of people he could call his own.

In the Kingdom of Mireana, there was Elena, the daughter of Duke Kerebel, who led one of the three major factions known as the Noble Faction, and who was known even in neighboring countries by the nickname “Princess General.”

Vihera, the imperial princess of the Bestir Empire, though currently in self-imposed exile.

Marina, who holds the important position of Priestess of the World Tree among the Dark Elves.

Though not quite on the same level as those three, there is Ara, who leads Elena’s knightly guard, and Byune, a descendant of the clan that founded the labyrinth city of Exil.

……That said, even Rei, who was thinking so carefree, is feared as a “Bandit Eater” for leading a rare S-rank griffin—known by the epithet “Crimson”—and raiding bandits for both pleasure and profit; just like the others… and in some cases, even more so.

“H-huh… oh my… oh my oh my oh my… w-wait, aren’t they all incredibly famous?!”

Those were the words that came out of Meru’s mouth upon hearing Marina’s remark.

But upon hearing this, Rei suddenly had a question.

He, Elena, and Ara had only just returned to Gilm a short while ago, and this was their first time meeting Meru.

But that was because Rei and the others had been in Anesis; wouldn’t it be natural for those who had remained in Gilm, like Vihera and Byune, to be acquainted with Meru?

Even so, judging by Meru’s current demeanor, it was certain that he had never met Vihera or Byune.

“Have you never met Vihera or Byune?”

“Huh? Oh, yes. Well… I haven’t had the chance.”

Hearing Meru’s words, Rei nodded in understanding and turned his gaze back to Marina and Meru.

“So, there’s no problem with freezing the earthen wall, right? If it’s okay with you, I’d like you to get started right away.”

“Yes, that’s fine. We’d just finished our practice for the day anyway. …Right?”

“W-wait, Marina-sama, are you serious? For me to do that right now… t-that’s a bit too much to ask.”

Perhaps lacking confidence, Meru shook her head in refusal.

Seeing this, Rei looks at Marina, wondering if she’s really okay with this. Marina, meeting his gaze, sighs and ruffles Meru’s hair.

“Meru, don’t you understand what I’ve been telling you all along? You’re already a fully-fledged spirit mage. So why are you so lacking in confidence? That’s your biggest flaw. You get it, right?”

“W-well…”

Meru couldn’t say a word in response to Marina’s words.

Meru, too, understands that her timidity is her weakness.

But even if he knows that, since his timidity is something he was born with, there’s no way he can change it overnight.

“Honestly, I wish Meru had even a tenth of Rei’s confidence.”

Hearing Marina’s muttered words, Rei called a halt.

“Ten percent? If that’s the case, wouldn’t I be a walking bundle of confidence?”

“Isn’t that right? …Though, in Rei’s case, it’s not just empty talk without any real ability—you actually have the skills to back it up, so it’s not a problem. …Anyway, come on, let’s go.”

Saying that, Marina handed Rei the sandwich she’d bought at the store.

“Is that okay?”

“It’s fine. I wasn’t that hungry anyway. Besides, you should focus on getting this job done with Meru and then enjoy a nice dinner.”

Hearing that, Rei understood what Marina was getting at.

A delicious dinner… she was undoubtedly referring to a dish made with Giganto Turtle meat.

Those participating in the butchering get to take home at least a little bit of the Giganto Turtle’s meat.

But it’s not enough to fill you up. …Unless you’re a light eater, that is.

In that sense, Rei, who gets to keep most of the meat from the Giganto Turtle’s carcass, was truly fortunate.

And Marina, who’s in a party with Rei and eats dinner in his backyard almost every day, is also quite fortunate.

“Got it. …Well, let’s get going then. It was Meru, right? Is that okay with you too?”

“Huh? Oh, yeah. …Wait, am I really going too? Ugh…”

Seeing Meru say that, Rei couldn’t help but wonder once again if she was really okay.

But since Marina, who had told Meru, said she was fine, he felt certain it would be all right.

“Come on, let’s go. Meru, steel yourself. With your abilities, you should be able to handle something like this without any trouble.”

“I understand. …I’ll do my best.”

In response to Marina’s words, Meru replied as if she had made up her mind.

(Is she really going to be okay? ...Is she really capable of pulling this off, being so timid?)

He couldn’t help but think that, but right now he didn’t have the luxury of arguing about such things; he needed to freeze and reinforce the earthen wall as quickly as possible.

Also, if Meru really proved useless, there was the plan that Marina, who was with them, would use spirit magic.

And so, the three people and one dog—Rei and Marina, Meru and Set—headed toward the construction site where Rei had built the earthen wall.
