"Wow, this is amazing..."

"Huh!"

After being led by Husta into the warehouse, Vihera murmured thoughtfully.

Byune voiced her agreement, her gaze drawn to the scene unfolding before them... numerous chunks of meat hanging from the ceiling.

Though usually expressionless, Byune's eyes now held an intense glow.

...Though that intense light wasn't curiosity, but purely the light of appetite.

"Ahaha. Well, late autumn to early winter is always a busy time for us. This is the culmination of all that work."

Hasta smiled as he explained the scene inside the warehouse.

Preparing this much meat was clearly a point of pride for Hasta too. Seeing the surprised expressions on Vihera and Byune's faces, he smiled happily, looking a little embarrassed.

This must be the meat he obtained by maximizing his abilities.

The meat lay ready—skinned, gutted, and needing only to be cut.

Some pieces, likely processed earlier, were salted for preservation or cured like ham.

"There's a lot of orc meat."

"Yes. I would have preferred more Gamelion meat, but... my skills aren't up to par."

Husta admitted this, yet several pieces of what appeared to be Gamelion meat still hung from the ceiling.

Still, the abundance of orc meat likely indicated which was easier to hunt and more plentiful.

Besides orcs and Gamelions, there was meat from various other types of monsters.

Nearly a quarter of the warehouse was filled with meat, so you can imagine the sheer volume of that curtain of meat.

"No way, this meat alone should easily cover us for the winter, right?"

Even a single orc weighs nearly a hundred kilograms.

Seeing so many different kinds of meat hanging there, including such a beast, it seemed to easily surpass the amount the Full Belly Inn would serve in winter... no, it seemed like there was far too much, enough to have leftovers.

But at Rei's words, Husta smiled and spoke.

"When spring comes around, if there's still meat left over, my dad holds a Meat Festival."

"A Meat Festival? What is that? That name really piques my curiosity."

It wasn't just Rei who thought that; Byune also stared intently at Husta, clearly expecting an explanation of the Meat Festival.

Perhaps not expecting such a look from Byune, Hasta seemed slightly surprised... yet, speaking of his respected father, he opened his mouth cheerfully.

"It's an all-you-can-eat meat feast. Well, obviously it ends when the meat runs out."

"All-you-can-eat? ...That sounds pretty lavish."

Rei had been to all-you-can-eat restaurants back in Japan.

It was a yakiniku all-you-can-eat, but the meat quality at that particular place was far from great, and the slices themselves were quite thin.

In other words, they probably couldn't turn a profit on an all-you-can-eat buffet without cutting corners like that.

Of course, that was just the all-you-can-eat place Rei knew. There were probably other places offering all-you-can-eat with proper dishes and decent meat... Unfortunately, the only all-you-can-eat place Rei had ever been to was that one.

That said, whether it was poor management or not, it went out of business shortly before Rei came to Elgin.

(Even though it was supposed to be all-you-can-eat yakiniku, in the end, you ended up eating more side dishes than actual grilled meat.)

Rei harbored this dissatisfaction, yet the place remained reasonably popular among middle and high school students—young enough to prioritize quantity over quality. ...Including Rei himself.

"Well, it's better to cook it properly and serve it than let it go bad. It doubles as advertising for the restaurant. ...If you'd like, why don't you and your friends join this year's Meat Festival?"

"Is that okay?"

Rei knew exactly how much he could eat.

And it wasn't just him—Byune also ate surprising amounts despite her appearance.

Rei asked if it was really okay for them to join the Meat Festival, but Husta nodded, saying it was fine.

"Though, Set... I'm afraid not."

"I suppose so."

Hasta said apologetically, and Rei nodded, understanding it couldn't be helped.

Considering Set's size and appetite, it wouldn't be surprising if he alone devoured most of the meat prepared for the festival.

While the meat festival was being held as a clearance sale and store promotion, disregarding profitability, having Set alone eat most of the meat would still cause various problems.

(Well, in that case, we could hold a 'Set Appreciation Society' or something... Huh? Maybe that would actually draw more people?)

True to its name, the Meat Festival drew almost exclusively men... young men in their prime, at that.

Of course, some young women and older men attend too, but proportionally, they weren't that numerous.

But if it were a gathering to admire Set, then yes, people of all ages and genders would flock in droves.

Set, who's practically become Gilm's idol and mascot character, has that kind of drawing power.

Thinking about it that way, Husta wondered, maybe that would be better?

Purely in terms of store sales, it might decrease, but if you think of it as advertising for the shop, having Set there would definitely be better.

"Hold on a moment. Maybe we could invite Set after all... But I need to check with my father about this. Could you wait a little while?"

"Huh? Well, if Set can join, that'd be a big help... Are you sure about this?"

"Yes, absolutely. It might even draw more customers than holding the Meat Festival."

Hasta's gaze shifted toward the warehouse wall... more precisely, toward Set waiting beyond it.

Seeing Hasta's actions, Rei must have understood what he meant. He didn't pursue the matter further and shifted the conversation to the reason he'd come to the warehouse this time.

"Anyway, about that. So, I'd like to get started right away and dissect the monster we hunted today. Is that okay?"

"Rei, just to confirm—we're only butchering the Snow Salamander today, right? The goblin can wait, correct?"

Though she didn't say it explicitly, Marina's interruption made her meaning crystal clear.

In other words, she was confirming: You won't be butchering the goblin, right?

But Rei never intended to dissect the goblin in the first place.

Originally, this goblin was prepared as a backup for research—to prevent it from becoming undead... and to ensure we could eat goblin meat deliciously when our meat supplies ran low.

"The goblin, you say?"

At Marina's words, Husta tensed slightly... yet her cheeks flushed a little as she asked Rei.

From Hasta's perspective, Marina was the guildmaster and, moreover, extremely attractive as a woman—hence his attitude.

Vihera was also a beauty who could rival Marina, but for Husta, Marina was probably more to his taste.

Still, he managed to speak up, likely because the mention of goblin meat had piqued his interest.

Goblin meat, something a novice adventurer who'd just started out might mention, thinking it better than starving to death.

Why would Rei, who Hasta assumed had deep knowledge about food, show interest in such a thing?

As the only son of the Full Belly Inn, it was only natural for him to question that.

But Rei wasn't secretly researching goblin meat.

Therefore, he answered simply.

"Right now, I'm doing a little research on how to make goblin meat taste good."

"...Are you serious?"

Not only the research itself, but also the fact that he was researching it at all—Hasta hadn't expected him to say it so casually.

Husta shot Rei a surprised look.

"I don't know which part you're referring to, but I'm dead serious about the research itself. Well, right now the guy I'm researching with is swamped with other stuff, so the research itself is on hold."

"...I've heard that my father did research on goblin meat when he was young, but apparently he gave up because it was hopeless."

"Dishot? ...I see."

For the owner of the Full Belly Inn, known for cheap and tasty food, if goblin meat could be made delicious, it would mean a whole new range of dishes on the menu.

But even someone like Dishot ended up giving up on researching goblin meat.

Rei looked slightly disappointed at that, but before he could say anything, Byune tugged on his Dragon Robe.

"Hm?"

Rei immediately understood what she wanted to say.

Stop wasting time with pointless chatter and get on with butchering the snow salamander.

"Yeah, you're right. There's no point in just standing here talking forever. ...Hasta, we're going to dissect it, but what about you?"

"Hmm, well... Since I've never encountered a Snow Salamander monster before either, would it be okay if I just watched for a bit?"

"Sure, go ahead. ...Right?"

Seeking confirmation, Rei glanced at the other three.

None of the three showed any sign of objection to that look.

Some people dislike showing others their monster dissection techniques, keeping them as their own skills, but fortunately, there was no one like that here.

"Thank you. This is very educational."

Hearing these words, Rei and the others headed toward a spot without the meat curtains... yet one with a proper workbench.

The meat curtains were likely hung with that area in mind.

In case meat became scarce during winter for some reason, they could bring downed monsters here and butcher them.

"Here it comes."

With those words, a Snow Salamander emerged from Rei's Misty Ring.

Standing one meter tall, its total length, including its tail, was nearly two meters.

It was a monster of considerable size for a lizard.

"This is... a Snow Salamander? I've heard about them, but this is my first time seeing one in person like this."

"Huh? So you've seen one that wasn't like this before?"

"Ah, yes. Years ago, I saw Father buying one from an adventurer. It was already butchered into meat cuts by then. It tasted like a richer, more refreshing version of mountain bird, incredibly delicious."

"...Rich yet refreshing? Well, whatever. We'll find out once we cut this up and try it. Alright, let's get started."

At Rei's words, Marina, Vihera, and even Byune pulled out their butcher knives and began cutting into the Snow Salamander on the table.

"Um, should I help?"

"No, it's fine. This is kind of like party practice anyway. I want us to be able to handle dissection with this many people. ...Besides, there's no room, right?"

"...Ahaha."

Hasta laughed awkwardly at Rei's words.

The Snow Salamander was a decent size, but even so, with four people butchering it, the space was extremely cramped.

Because Byune was small, this many people could dismember it without issue. But if there had been another adult here instead of Byune, it would have been quite difficult to move around.

In fact, it could be said that precisely because there were this many people, they were able to dissect it properly.

"First, should we cut off the head?"

"Then I'll start skinning it. Byune, please handle the innards."

"Be careful with the innards—there are a few parts that can be sold as ingredients."

"Mm."

Since Marina possessed the most detailed knowledge about snow salamanders, they proceeded with the dissection following her instructions.

Cut off the head, peel the skin...

"Huh? Wait, is this okay?"

The one who voiced the surprise was Vihera.

The reason was obvious upon seeing the snow salamander's flesh.

After all, the flesh was white... not just white, but the color of fresh snow.

Seeing such flesh, it was only natural for Vihera to be shocked.

...Though it did leave a few droplets of blood on her face.

"It's fine. This is snow salamander meat."

"That's right. The meat I saw was white too. ...Though, I think it seemed a bit cloudier."

"Ah, that's the difference in freshness. The longer it's been since it was butchered, the more that white turns into a cloudy white."

Amidst this conversation between those familiar with snow salamander meat, Rei and the others proceeded with the butchering.

After about an hour, the butchering was successfully completed, and they headed to the Manpukutei restaurant for a celebratory feast.
