A rat-beast man suddenly flew out from the woods.

Rei caught him and let out a small sigh.

It was obvious without needing to think about what had happened.

In other words, Rei and the others... or more accurately, Johanna and the others had come to hunt the Gamelion, but they were a step too late. Another party had found it first and attacked.

Rei had spotted from above while riding Set had appeared to be curled up asleep in the woods.

To the adventurers who found it, it must have seemed like an easy target to take down.

And so they attacked... The result was the rat beastman man who had been blown flying toward Rei.

"Hey, what do we do? Looks like they're already fighting."

"What do you mean, what should we do? Obviously, it's first come, first served with stuff like this, right? Even if we said we found it first now, it probably wouldn't mean anything."

"Then should we go somewhere else? I'd rather not pass up this spot since Set found it for us..."

Since we'd been beaten to it, there was nothing we could do. We should probably look for the next Gamelion... Just as we were about to settle on that, the beastman who had been blown away, caught by Rei, and thrown to the ground woke up.

"Hah!"

The fact that he instantly sprang up and scanned his surroundings for danger upon regaining consciousness showed he was an adventurer skilled enough to hunt Gamelions.

His swift glance around naturally fell upon Rei and the others.

"Did you guys save me?"

Rent nodded at the beastman's question as he scanned the area.

"Yeah. You were blown out of the woods, and Rei over there caught you."

"Rei? ...Rei!? Rank B adventurer Rei!?"

His eyes widened so dramatically it seemed impossible, and he scanned the others present one by one.

His gaze naturally came to rest on Rei, who was hiding his face under the hood of his robe.

"You're Rei, aren't you? ...Please! Help me!"

The beastman bowed his head toward Rei.

His eyes held the desperate urgency unique to those cornered.

"Gamelion was stronger than we thought... is that it?"

"That's right. We attacked Gamelion while he was asleep, but when we actually fought him, he was stronger than we'd anticipated. The battle should still be raging in the woods right now."

True to his words, everyone present could hear metallic clangs and angry shouts echoing from within the woods.

"Helping them is fine, but what about Gamelion? You know we're here for Gamelion too, right?"

It was Rent, not Rei, who answered the beastman.

He understood that Rei's presence was strictly for support, so he took the lead in negotiations.

...Though the biggest reason was probably his strong desire to impress Johanna.

"I don't think we can give you everything... but we can let you have more than half."

The beastman declared without hesitation.

His comrades were struggling hard in the woods.

Given that, if he didn't intervene now, they'd likely be wiped out.

If that happened, they'd gain nothing... worse, they risked losing their lives.

Therefore, he judged that even if he handed over more than half the rights to the Gamelion they had just defeated, they could still gain a significant advantage.

"I see. In that case, I'm fine with it. What about Johanna and the others?"

"I'm fine with it too. Since we're going to live in Gilm from now on, I don't want to do something like abandoning other adventurers."

"I agree. Abandoning them here would leave a bitter taste."

"Etc."

"Hey, at least give a proper opinion. Ah, I'm in too. I'm not going to oppose helping adventurers."

Thus, with everyone except Rei choosing to help, they left the beastman man there.

"Leave this guy to me. You guys do your thing."

As Rei declared he'd handle the beastman, Rent and the others each flashed confident smiles and charged into the forest.

Watching them go, Rei addressed the beastman, who stood there looking stunned.

"Come on, staying here won't do you any good. We should head into the woods to see the fight, right? We'll have to be careful not to get in the way, though."

"...Hah, no, that's not it! You're Rei, right? Then why are the others going to help instead of you?"

The beastman man shouted in panic.

From this man's perspective, it was likely frustrating that Rei, their strongest fighter, wasn't going to help his comrade in danger.

But Rei just shrugged slightly at the man.

"Relax. They're strong enough. They have the power to take down Gamelion. I guarantee it."

To be precise, while he knew Johanna and the other three's abilities, he hadn't actually confirmed just how powerful Rent truly was.

Still, one could generally gauge a certain level of skill just by watching.

Of course, when facing someone clearly superior like Noiz, it's hard to gauge their full strength just by looking.

But in Rent's case, he was clearly weaker than Rei in terms of raw strength, and that's precisely why I understood there wasn't much difference in ability between him and Johanna's group.

"But!"

"Calm down. Right. If they really couldn't handle it, I'd step in. Doesn't that make you feel better?"

"Well... I suppose you're right."

If Rei promised to come to their aid in a pinch, as a man, he couldn't really complain any further.

After all, adventurers operate on a principle of personal responsibility. Rei and the others weren't obligated to save men under any circumstances.

Still, helping was purely out of Rei's goodwill.

To demand more from that goodwill would touch on his pride as an adventurer.

Of course, if a comrade's life was in danger, he wouldn't think twice about his pride.

If abandoning that pride meant saving a comrade, I'd gladly do it.

But since they could be saved without that now, he had no choice but to accept their kindness.

"Alright, I'll... trust what you say, Rei."

With that, the man began walking toward the woods alongside Rei.

His gait was awkward, likely because he hadn't fully recovered from the damage inflicted by Gamelion's strike.

Gamelion twisted his massive body, and his tail lashed out with the sharpness of a whip, riding the momentum of the movement.

"Dammit, this tail is getting in the way!"

The dog-beastman dodged the tail strike aimed at him.

It was precisely because he was a beastman, with physical abilities and senses sharper than a normal human, that he could evade the tail's strike passing before his eyes.

He immediately thrust the spear he held toward Gamelion, but Gamelion, sensing this, used the momentum of his twist to spin around and deliver a slash with his razor-sharp ears.

The canine beastman blocked the strike with his spear, but the difference in size was too great, and he was sent flying.

Gamelion prepared to deliver a follow-up strike to the knocked-back man, but several arrows flew toward him.

"Hey, are you okay?! You're okay, right?! Tell me you're okay!?"

Calling out to the dog beastman who'd just been knocked away, the fox beastwoman fired arrows in rapid succession.

Despite her panicked tone, the speed at which she fired the arrows was remarkable.

"Gaaaah! Dammit, take this! Ha!"

As Gamelion instinctively froze under the hail of arrows, the bear beastman man swung his club with all his might.

"GAAAAAH!"

A sound similar to the bear beastman's roared from Gamelion's mouth as the club struck him...

"Guh!"

He launched a kick with his hind legs, clearly intending it as payback for the club strike.

Though the bear beastman stood over two meters tall, he couldn't block Gamelion's blow. He was sent flying, crashing into a tree that halted his momentum.

"Shant!? Hey, what are you doing, Chrono? You got Shant too!"

"What do you mean, what should we do... Grin... Hm?"

The dog beastman male called Chrono, who had started to say something, suddenly twitched his nose.

He realized unfamiliar scents were approaching their location.

"What? Someone coming?"

"Wait, you're not telling me it's a new Gamelion, are you!?"

Hearing Chrono's words, the fox beastman woman, bow in hand, shouted while watching the Gamelions who were carefully observing them as if waiting for an opening.

Even now, they were stretched thin. If reinforcements arrived for their opponents here... she suppressed her anxiety.

But...

"Relax, Jonah. Looks like they're allies."

Chrono, a dog beastman, could tell by scent that the approaching figures were humans, not Gamelions or monsters.

Furthermore, the scent of the rat beastman man—Grin, also a member of the Rank C party, Beast's Fang—who had been blown away by Gamelion's tail strike earlier, was still active. This confirmed his understanding.

"Shant, you're okay!"

"Yeah, I'm fine, you bastard!"

Shant, the bear beastman, shouted in irritation as he returned to stand before Gamelion, brandishing his club to intimidate the beast.

"Looks like reinforcements are coming our way. Hang in there a little longer."

"Reinforcements? Trying to steal our prey?"

"You fool! Do you think we could take that Gamelion on alone? That Gamelion is no ordinary Gamelion—he's incredibly strong. Right now, we just need to..."

"Shut up!"

Shant shouted in frustration, readying the club in his hand as he charged toward Gamelion.

"Damn it! Jonah, cover me!"

Chrono shouted, sword in hand, chasing after Shant.

Gamelion, waiting for Shant with his club, leapt off the ground with his powerful hind legs the next instant and charged at Shant.

Was he aiming to tear him apart with his fangs? Or to slice through his body with his ears, now sharpened like blades?

Whichever it was, Shant's life hung by a thread.

Jonah desperately shot arrows to try and distract Gamelion, but Gamelion dodged them with minimal movement.

And just as Gamelion and Shant were about to collide...

"GAAAH!"

Gamelion suddenly kicked off the ground and leapt backward, creating distance between himself and Shant.

"Whoa, what the!?"

Knowing he couldn't dodge Gamelion's strike, Shant had been preparing to swing his club, ready for a mutual kill. Gamelion's sudden action caught him off guard.

"Damn, hunting Gamelion is tougher than I thought."

"Yeah. I'd heard about it, but I didn't expect it to be this bad."

"Still, it's better than during the civil war, right?"

"Ah, true. Yeah, that's for sure. After all, there's only one enemy."

"Honestly, just having Rei-san behind us gives us such a huge sense of security."

As they spoke, the five adventurers came into view of the Beast's Fang members.

The group—one woman and four men—showed surprisingly little tension, even facing the glaring Gamelions.

(They mentioned Rei... So Rei is backing these guys up. In that case, a Gamelion or two really isn't worth worrying about.)

After all, this year's Gamelion hunt had only truly begun because Rei had conquered the dungeon.

Word had already spread that Rei alone had defeated over fifty Gamelions during that run.

With someone like Rei backing them up, they probably have no fear facing Gamelion at all.

"The rat beastmen asked me to help. Do you need any assistance?"

Before Shant could say anything unnecessary, Chrono spoke up in response to the unshaven male adventurer's words.

"I could use it. That Gamelion is stronger than the usual ones. I'd appreciate your help if possible."

"Chrono!"

Shant shouted, his voice dripping with disbelief. But when Chrono shot him a sharp glare, he had no choice but to swallow the rest of his words.

"You should know by now we can't beat that thing on our own. Even if we did, the damage to us would be too great."

"...Fine, damn it!"

With Chrono, the party leader, saying this much, Shant couldn't argue back any further and just spat out his response.

"So... does that mean we can cooperate?"

"Yeah, please."

"Got it. Alright then... let's go."

With those words, everyone present turned toward Gamelion, weapons drawn.
