Beneath the night desert, Rei camped with the four members of the Sand Claw.

"Hmm, so even with the sand ship, we couldn't make it to Goshu today after all."

"No, it still increased our marching speed considerably, didn't it? If we'd been traveling on foot, we'd probably only reach tomorrow evening, but with this, we'll likely arrive by tomorrow morning."

In response to Rei's grumbling words, Howl, the leader of the Sand Claw, muttered while tossing something into the campfire.

Rather than nodding at Howl's words, Rei fixed an intrigued gaze on the object tossed into the fire.

Noticing Rei's gaze, Haul held out the object in his hand toward Rei.

"What is it? Is this thing really that rare?"

"...Well, if you ask if it's rare, then yes, it is."

What Haul held was an object about the size of a palm, and it was being used as fuel for the bonfire.

But... this fuel wasn't dead wood, nor was it green wood that burned poorly.

Plants were generally scarce in the desert, making firewood for kindling overwhelmingly insufficient.

Yet, in a desert where nighttime temperatures drop below freezing, skipping campfires was unthinkable. Consequently, a unique fuel source developed in this region. It was...

"I never imagined they'd use animal or monster feces as fuel."

When he first heard this, Rei wore a slightly disgusted expression, but he finally relaxed when he realized burning it as fuel produced no foul odor whatsoever.

Even so, using feces as fuel still felt utterly bizarre to Rei.

"Oh? It's pretty common around here. Besides, if we used wood as fuel, the trees around the oasis would disappear, wouldn't they?"

The slender man among the Sand Claws... Odilon muttered, and the spear-wielding woman, Cardina, and the small man, Nicole, both nodded in agreement.

"Well, now that you mention it..."

Rei murmured, puzzled. Why hadn't he noticed this fuel being used when he camped with Zarust and Auguste before? But he quickly understood.

During that camp, he'd retreated promptly into his magic tent at nightfall, and hadn't lingered much by the fire when he woke in the morning either.

And since they knew Rei wasn't from Solrain, they probably avoided using the animal dung fuel in front of him, thinking he might be repulsed by it.

(So they were being considerate of me.)

Only now did it occur to Rei that he had made Zarust and Auguste feel uncomfortable, and he felt a little bad about it.

Compared to Zarust and the others, Haul and the others didn't seem particularly bothered and used the dung-based fuel right in front of Rei and Set... Was it just a difference in experience?

"...It hardly smells at all. I wouldn't have guessed it was made from dung until you mentioned it."

"Well, we don't use the dung as-is. We process it thoroughly and dry it. ...If we had more money, we could probably make a magic item for it, but it's expensive."

"...Hm?"

Hearing the words from Howl's mouth, Rei's expression turned slightly intrigued as he asked.

"You mean, like, magic items for campfires?"

"There are. But for just lighting fires, everyday magic items will do. For a bonfire... to keep it burning constantly, the price of the magic item skyrockets."

"Why's that?"

Kaldina smiled before answering Rei's straightforward question.

Since night had already fallen, she had removed the turban covering her face and the mask-like item that protected against dust. Now, nothing hid Cardina's face.

Her features were rather well-defined, and it was precisely because of this that she had been dragged into trouble on several occasions before. ...That was likely why she had been wary when he rescued her from the Sand Lizardmen.

"Of course. If it's just about creating the spark, that's one thing, but to keep it burning constantly, you need fuel to sustain it. In this case, that means magic stones or mana. Naturally, the cost becomes enormous. If you're using a magic item to keep a fire going..."

"You'd need either a rich person who can throw away magic stones like they're nothing, or a mage. Well, a mage probably wouldn't want to waste their magic power on something like that either."

Nicole continued, as if picking up where Caldina left off.

But for Rei, this was also extremely interesting.

Because Rei's magic power was so vast it could be described as inexhaustible.

"Tell me more about that."

"...Were you listening? I said only rich people or mages would find it useful, right?"

Compared to when they first rescued him, perhaps because they'd talked somewhat during the journey on the sand ship, the way the four of the Sand Claw spoke to Rei had become considerably more casual.

Feeling a sense of comfort in this change, Rei replied to Nicole.

"You saw me move the sand ship, right? I can use magic."

"Huh? But that sand ship was powered by magic stones, wasn't it!?"

Kaldina was the first to cry out in shock, but the other three were equally astonished.

"Yeah. Believe it or not, I'm a magic warrior who can use magic too. I never run low on mana."

'......'

The four listened to Rei's words, looking utterly dumbfounded.

Mages were extremely rare, and naturally, in the much smaller kingdom of Solrain compared to the Kingdom of Mireana, they were seldom seen.

Gosh had a few mages brought in by the Elema Trading Company for a hefty sum, but aside from that exception, there were only a handful.

Of course, they weren't seasoned wizards.

And yet, the one standing before them now possessed warrior skills vastly superior to their own, carried multiple magic items... and could even use magic.

"Guruu!"

Though he hadn't read the minds of the four, Set, who had been lying down a short distance away, let out a cry as if to say, "Don't forget about me."

Yes, beyond what they had just considered, he even had a griffin named Set as his familiar.

"Who... are you?"

Facing Rei, whose presence felt like he belonged to a different dimension compared to them, Howl murmured softly.

Within that gaze lay complex emotions—envy, jealousy, and a sense of awe.

"Just saying 'Rank B adventurer' probably won't satisfy you. Hmm... To put it in terms you'd understand... An adventurer with an alias? That might make it clearer?"

'A nicknamed adventurer!?'

The four voices overlapped once more.

Of course. A title-holder is akin to a title bestowed upon adventurers who have achieved extraordinary feats.

...In Rei's case, it was something he naturally acquired during the war with the Bestir Empire.

Some do come up with their own nicknames, but unless they possess truly formidable strength, they're basically just laughed at.

"Ah. ...But it seems it hasn't reached our side yet. Have you ever heard of Crimson?"

He asked, but seeing everyone shake their heads, he could only think, 'Well, I figured as much.'

"Well, rumors about me will reach here eventually... I think. Yeah, probably."

Looking at Rei with his hood down now, he certainly didn't look like someone with a nickname.

Still, having seen Rei's true strength with their own eyes, they couldn't doubt his power.

Despite confidently declaring his alias, Rei felt a bit embarrassed that it was completely unknown. He hastily changed the subject.

"Anyway, tell me about yourselves. Looking at you guys, you seem like a pretty solid party."

"Huh? Well, I guess you can tell that just by looking."

Even as he said this, he clearly didn't mind the compliment. A faint, pleased smile touched Howl's lips.

"Looks like there's no mage, but you've got a bow for ranged attacks."

The slender Odilon carried a bow, the petite Nicole was a thief. Then there was Howl with his scimitar and Cardina with her spear.

Two warriors, one archer, one thief. Close-range, mid-range, long-range attacks, plus a thief for exploration—it was undeniably a well-balanced party.

They all wore pleased expressions, likely delighted to be praised by Rei, the famed adventurer.

(Besides, their strong party unity was obvious from how they fled from the Sand Lizardman.)

If the party's relations were strained, the worst-case scenario could involve sacrificing one member as a pawn, leaving them behind as a sacrifice for the Sand Lizardman.

Seeing them all cooperate to escape without resorting to such tactics made their strong bond clear. Though...

(We got away this time, but escaping together isn't always the best choice, you know.)

He muttered to himself.

If Rei hadn't come, they would undoubtedly have been caught by the Sand Lizardmen.

With their stamina depleted from the desperate run, facing a pack of Sand Lizardmen would spell certain doom.

If someone had acted as a sacrificial pawn, the Sand Lizardmen pursuing from behind would have been forced to halt, and the other three fleeing members would likely have escaped.

That is, assuming they could handle the Sand Lizardmen lying in wait.

"Still, one or two Sand Lizardmen I could have sliced up with my scimitar. But I never expected to be ambushed by a horde like that."

As Howl spoke, he rose from the campfire and drew his scimitars from their sheaths. ...Yes, from both the sheath at his waist and the one on his back, which hadn't been visible from Rei's perspective.

"Dual wielding?"

Rei murmured at the sight of Howl holding a scimitar in each hand.

"Yeah, kinda unusual, huh? But these scimitars aren't as heavy as they look. Even holding one in one hand..."

Delighted by Rei's words, Howl began enthusiastically explaining dual wielding, but Rei wasn't listening to his explanation.

Instead, he stared at his own hands, deep in thought.

"Dual wielding... I see. That's the idea..."

Seeing Rei deep in thought, Howl, still holding the scimitars in both hands, gave him a puzzled look.

But Rei seemed oblivious to the gaze, continuing to ponder... until he suddenly stood up.

The four members of the Sand Claw, seeing Rei's serious expression, looked at him with puzzled expressions, wondering what had happened.

The reason they hadn't immediately shifted into combat stance, thinking monsters were approaching here, was probably because Set, lying down a short distance away, remained motionless.

Rei lifted his head when Rei moved, but after a glance at Rei, he closed his eyes again.

Under the gaze of the four members of the Sand Claw, Rei moved to a spot a little further away and stood still.

Not a single cloud existed in the sky. Only the large moon poured its soft moonlight onto the desert, while numerous stars faintly asserted their presence.

Only the sound of the wind filled the desert air.

In that silence, Rei drew the Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring.

Having seen Rei use it in today's battle against the Sand Lizardman, the four members of Sand Claw watched him as if wondering if he intended to train or something.

But the next moment, the four were stunned.

Switching the Twilight Spear from his right hand to his left, Rei immediately drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

Twilight Spear in his left hand, Death Scythe in his right.

Both were long weapons, the kind that normally required two hands to wield, even just one of them.

Moreover, though the Sand Claw Quartet didn't know it, the Death Scythe weighed nearly 100kg when wielded by anyone other than Rei or Set.

Even without that knowledge, it was a weapon with a two-meter-long shaft and a one-meter-long blade. It wasn't hard to imagine just how heavy such a weapon would be.

As the four watched, Rei first swung the Twilight Spear in his left hand, then attempted to swing the Death Scythe in his right... but the swing of the Twilight Spear had thrown off his balance, making the Death Scythe's swing wildly off-kilter.

"Whoa!"

The weight of the Death Scythe itself was lighter to Rei than a random dead branch lying around.

But even if it was light, the fact that the Death Scythe was a long, massive weapon remained unchanged.

Moreover, while wielding the Death Scythe with both hands might be manageable, Rei currently held the Twilight Spear, another long weapon similar to the Death Scythe, in his left hand.

Until now, he'd wielded one weapon with both hands, but this time he was holding two different weapons in each hand... and they were both long weapons: a spear and a scythe.

Naturally, mastering movements for both couldn't happen overnight. Before the eyes of everyone in the Sand Claw, Rei continued practicing his awkward dual-wielding... no, dual-spear style—though one was a scythe—for a while longer.
