Two days after the mine collapsed... Rei was in the town of Jawa, considered the heart of the Count of Relduct's domain.

To be precise, it was in a forest a short distance from Jawa.

Besides Rei and Set, members of the rebel army were also present there.

"So, we're infiltrating the city. We start moving once night falls, right?"

Rei nodded at the words of the man who spoke—the man leading the rebels.

"Yeah, do that. Normally, I'd go into town with you guys too... but, you know?"

"Grr."

At Rei's words, Set beside him growled apologetically.

A griffin about three meters tall was, of course, conspicuous.

If Rei, who commanded such a monster, tried to enter Jawa, it would inevitably lead to questions about who he was, and it was highly likely word would reach Lerdact.

And if someone had a griffin in tow, the members of the Noble Faction would understand without needing to think who it was.

...Yes, it was Rei the Crimson, the strongest blade of the Neutral Faction's central figure, Daskar.

What's more, Lerdact felt guilty about Daskar expanding Gilm and sending his private soldiers to interfere.

Given that situation, if Rei were to arrive in the city under his rule, the implications would be obvious... no need to think about it.

Engaging in combat with Lerdact's men in the streets of Jawa before Rei or the rebels could launch an attack—that was something Rei... and naturally, the rebels too, would not change.

As a result, Rei and Set decided to wait outside Jawa until nightfall.

After seeing the rebel man off, Rei leaned her weight against Set, who lay down in the woods.

"Grr."

Set purred happily at Rei and rubbed his face against hers.

"Come to think of it, it feels like it's been a while since I've spent quiet time like this with Set, no matter what else is going on."

"Grrrrr"

Set purred and rubbed his face against Rei's in response to Rei's words.

While they often spent time together outside the magic tent when camping, it had been rare lately for them to relax like this during the day.

That must have made Set happy.

The branches of the trees growing in the forest softened the intense sunlight somewhat, creating a scene worthy of being called dappled sunlight before their eyes.

It was summer, so it was hot, and even in the woods, they would likely sweat.

And leaning against Set, covered in soft fur, would make enduring that heat difficult indeed.

But Rei had his Dragon Robe, which could be called a makeshift air conditioner.

Summer or winter... no, even the height of summer or the depths of winter made no difference; he could spend time outside without a care.

"Here, Set. Want some of this?"

What Rei pulled out from his Misty Ring was a whole roasted mountain bird purchased from Gilm's cafeteria.

Slightly smaller than a chicken, this mountain bird was stuffed with various vegetables and roasted slowly.

Roasting it for a long time allowed the meat's savory flavor to infuse the vegetables inside, but that also meant the meat itself lost some of its own richness.

But the mountain bird meat now on the plate before Set was delicious enough.

(The texture isn't dry at all, if anything it's moist? And yet, the vegetables inside have absorbed the meat's flavor perfectly. ...How did they cook this?)

Serving some to Set and tasting it himself, Rei tilted his head.

Though Rei had introduced dishes like udon, okonomiyaki, meat buns, and pizza to Gilm and Emoshion, he was by no means an expert on cooking itself.

He'd only explained things based on knowledge gained while in Japan, and his explanations were often clumsy. It was more accurate to say the dishes Rei taught were properly completed only after the cooks themselves went through a lot of trial and error.

Given that background, Rei had no idea how the dishes before him were actually cooked.

"All I know is... it's delicious."

"Guruu!"

Set growled in agreement with Rei's words, nudging the plate toward Rei with his front paws, demanding more.

Since the mountain birds were only slightly smaller than chickens, Rei ended up taking out two more of the same dish from the Misty Ring.

The sound of insects echoed through the forest.

It was the kind of sound you could easily call noisy.

As darkness fell and the moon was half-hidden by clouds, Rei awoke.

"Guru?"

Noticing Rei had woken, Set—who had been serving as a sofa substitute... no, a bed substitute—moved his face from his half-asleep state of vigilance and looked at Rei.

"Huuwaaah... Hm, Set. Thanks. I slept well."

"Grrrr..."

As Rei thanked him and stroked Set, the cat purred contentedly under his touch.

Rei's mind usually took a while to kick in after waking up, but that was only when he could sleep soundly and safely, like in an inn.

In a place like this, truly deserving of being called enemy territory, he could move immediately upon waking.

"Set's body really is just right for sleeping, huh?"

"Grrr!"

In response to Rei's praise, Set purred proudly, as if to say, "I knew it!"

Indeed, Set's body was covered in soft fur, and considering his strength, it was unbelievably soft and supple.

If you could ignore the heat of being buried in Set's fur during summer, you'd get a very comfortable sleep.

...That heat, though, is usually unbearable.

(Ah, but Milein or Johanna might be delighted to hear they could sleep cuddled up to Set even in summer.)

Thinking such thoughts, he drew the Water-Flowing Dagger from the Misty Ring, lightly refreshed himself with water conjured by Rei's magic, and cleared his mind.

Though she'd only napped for a few hours, Rei's mind felt completely clear.

"...Alright, time to go. The others must be getting tired of waiting inside the J'aw."

"Gururuu"

Set growled in agreement.

They had discussed attacking at night beforehand, but no specific time had been set.

Well, even if they had wanted to set a specific time, aside from Rei who had a clock, the rebels had no way of knowing it.

During the day, bells would ring at six, nine, twelve, three, and six o'clock, but that was only during daylight hours.

Now that darkness had fallen, there was no chance of bells ringing.

So, in the end, they had to decide roughly what time to move beforehand and then just act accordingly.

"If only clocks were a bit more common..."

"Guru?"

Set tilted his head slightly, asking, "What's wrong?"

Shaking his head to indicate it was nothing, Rei immediately sprang into action.

Emerging from the woods, he mounted Set and headed for Jawa.

It was a town-sized place, and moreover, it was a Relduct base.

Naturally, that meant there would be some sort of barrier... While the frontier town of Gilm might be one thing, here, while not entirely devoid of vicious monsters, they were far fewer than in the wilds.

Barriers required massive funds to establish.

If it's meant to prevent monster invasions from the air, like in Gilm, it's difficult to fund with the assets of the Lerdact County.

It's not impossible, but it would consume a significant portion of the assets.

And if you ask whether such barriers would even be useful... well, that's another questionable point.

(Like building a nuclear shelter in a house in the Japanese countryside, perhaps?)

Even as he thought it, Rei felt his own reasoning was slightly off the mark, yet it remained the most fitting explanation.

In any case, since there weren't any monsters of that high rank, there was no need to make the barrier the same as Gilm's... or rather, that's what Lerdact's ancestors, who created Jawa, must have thought.

And every head of the Lerdact family since then shared that view. As a result, the barrier enveloping Jawa wasn't designed to block enemies, but rather to detect opponents approaching from the air.

In truth, that approach wasn't necessarily wrong.

A powerful barrier like the one used in Gilm requires significant energy just to maintain.

Considering the monsters present in the Lerdact estate, such barriers had been sufficient until now.

...Yes, until now.

「Set！」

"GRRRRRRRRRR!"

At Rei's command, Set let out a high-pitched cry and dove straight toward Jawa.

Without slowing down, he headed straight for the Jawa... for the manor house at its center.

Even though it was nearly midnight, numerous lights shone within the lord's mansion.

Normally, one couldn't freely use magic items that produced light like this.

The fact that they could be used this way meant that Lerdact possessed the wealth to afford it... meaning they were collecting exorbitant taxes.

Normally, it's taverns and brothels that have many lights burning, but at least from what Rei could see from above, Lerdact's residence surpassed them in the number of lights.

"Guruu"

As they descended toward the ground, Set suddenly growled.

What was that? Rei wondered for a moment, but the next instant, he sensed something and understood what had made Set growl.

Simultaneously, he realized that what they'd just encountered was probably a barrier.

(Right about now, someone... or something must be causing a commotion for invading from above Jawa.)

Thinking this, Rei's gaze remained fixed on the rapidly approaching ground... The next instant, Set flapped his wings wide, instantly halting their descent.

When Set landed in what appeared to be a garden, Rei, riding on his back, felt no particular impact.

In fact, despite the three-meter-long Set having fallen straight down while flapping its wings, the near-silent landing indicated just how skillfully it had managed to touch down.

But even landing silently, Set was still a colossal creature.

The overwhelming presence emanating from it was undeniable... the aura naturally radiating from a Rank S monster was impossible to conceal.

Though, as is often the case with Gilm, when he was playing around, strangely enough, he didn't give off that kind of aura.

In any case, within minutes of Set landing in the garden, his presence was discovered.

"The garden! There's something in the garden!"

"Something seems to have broken through Jawa's barrier. Probably that thing!"

"Huh."

It took less than a few seconds for Rei and Set to break through the barrier and land in the garden.

And from landing in the garden to being spotted by the soldiers, knights, and private guards... various individuals present in the lord's mansion... it took only a few minutes.

Despite that mere fraction of time, the news that the barrier had been breached had already spread. This meant the command structure here was solid, and many of those present were reasonably skilled.

(Well, command structure aside, 'skilled' might be a stretch?)

Glancing at those surrounding him, Rei thought this.

Truthfully, as far as he could tell from this survey, none of them seemed particularly formidable to Rei's eye.

Of course, it wasn't likely that all the forces from the lord's mansion were present here now.

If they were truly skilled, it would only make sense for them to be placed close by as Reldact's personal guards.

"Who the hell are you?!"

One of the men surrounding Rei and Set shouted.

Laying a smile on his face, Rei stepped forward toward the man who had shouted.

"Who am I? Can't you tell by looking at the situation? Do you really think someone who dared to attack the master of this place would just get beaten one-sidedly?"

"What? What do you mean by that?"

Was it because of the sudden nighttime attack that he couldn't connect seeing Set to Rei? Or was his mind still reeling from the abrupt assault?

Either way, the fact that even seeing Set didn't lead him to figure out his true identity was an unexpected miscalculation for Rei.

For a split second, the thought flashed through his mind to keep his identity hidden and just go on the rampage. But this time, he had come solely to exact revenge.

Given that, he needed to make his identity clear.

"Seeing this, do you know who I am?"

He declared as he drew the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear from Misty Ring.

The Twilight Spear was one thing, but not many wielded a scythe as a weapon. ...No, it would be fair to say only a handful did.

Some might use one out of admiration after hearing tales of Rei's exploits, but the scythe is incredibly difficult to wield effectively in actual combat.

For that very reason, those who saw the Death Scythe finally realized who stood before them.

"You are..."

"Seems you've finally figured it out. I'm Rei, an adventurer belonging to Gilm. ...Or would 'Crimson Rei' be easier to understand?"

With that declaration, he swung Death Scythe horizontally.

The blow, slicing through the air itself, made those watching gasp in awe.
