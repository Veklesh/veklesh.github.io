"Haaaaaah!"

With that cry, Arogan charged toward Rei wielding the Magic Sword.

Waiting for him, Rei held the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left, as usual.

"Hmph!"

With a sharp exhalation, Rei's left hand moved.

The tip of the Twilight Spear thrust out much slower than usual, heading straight for Alogan...

"Not a chance!"

He swung his Magic Sword, deflecting the Twilight Spear.

But... the next instant, the massive blade of Death Scythe, released a fraction of a second after the Twilight Spear's strike, was thrust against Arogan's neck...

"That's enough!"

Vihera's voice echoed through the Azimov family's courtyard.

"Damn it! It's not fair that I only have one weapon, but Rei has two!?"

Screaming, Arogan collapsed to the ground.

The grass growing from the ground softly cushioned his body.

When Rei had visited Azimov's house before, the garden grass had been left to grow wild... but they must have tidied it up when repairing the wall.

Now it was a proper lawn worthy of the name.

(Though, Azimov would never actually tend to the garden... It'll probably be overgrown again by summer.)

Watching Alogan lying on the grass, Rei stowed both weapons in his Misty Ring and spoke.

"Two-sword style isn't something you can just pick up easily, you know."

Dual wielding requires holding one weapon in each hand.

That alone demands considerable grip strength and arm power. Compared to that, Rei's dual spear style involved wielding the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear.

...Fortunately, Rei's innate physical abilities are exceptionally high, so wielding two long weapons in each hand causes him no trouble whatsoever.

Moreover, the Death Scythe possesses a unique ability that makes it feel weightless only to Rei and Set.

For that reason, weight was practically negligible.

However, the dual spear style presented other troublesome aspects.

Unlike dual swords, the weapons are long... meaning they're awkward to handle, and it's not uncommon for them to collide with each other.

In fact, after first conceiving the dual spear style, Rei struggled considerably for quite some time to master it, only reaching his current level through relentless practice.

That said, Rei herself wasn't satisfied with her current situation.

He didn't consider himself a master of the two-spear style; on the contrary, he understood his skills were still far from perfect.

That's precisely why he never skips his daily training.

"Hmm... So, does that mean I can't just easily switch to dual-wielding either?"

"Of course not. If it were that easy to master dual wielding, there'd be a lot more of us around."

Culotte, who had been watching the mock battle, muttered in exasperation.

(Two-sword style... two-sword style? I've been using the term 'two-sword style' normally until now, but I've never actually seen a sword. So why 'two-sword style'? If we're going to name it, shouldn't it be something like 'two-sword style' or something like that?)

That's what Rei thought, but he actually wondered why the term "dual-wielding" was the one that had spread in this world.

Though, to be fair, the one who actually popularized the term "dual-wielding" was Takumu from the Zepairu clan, who came from Earth just like Rei... but Rei had no way of knowing that.

"Anyway, this mock battle is over, right? I introduced you guys to Azimov as bodyguards, never expecting you'd end up being my sparring partners."

Yesterday, he introduced Arogan and Kyurot to Azimov, and today he came to Azimov's house again to wait for Precious's movements... but when Arogan saw Rei, he challenged him to a mock battle.

If he said he wanted to improve his skills even a little for the sake of the escort, Rei wouldn't refuse. ...Rather, she judged it would be perfect for her own training too, and that's why they held this mock battle.

"Hold on a second! You can't just forget about me. If you're having a mock battle for your guard training, then naturally I should be part of it too, right?"

The one who called out to Rei was, unsurprisingly, Arogan's partner, Culotte.

A dagger in hand, she was ready to spar with Rei at any moment.

(Ah... Well, I can't say I don't understand what she's saying. And maybe it would be good to know exactly how strong Culotte really is?)

Reaching that conclusion, Rei stowed the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear into the Misty Ring and faced Cullot barehanded.

"...Hey, are you sure you don't want to use a weapon?"

"Yeah."

"Huh... So you're underestimating me."

"Not exactly. I just thought this would be better for fighting Culotte."

Honestly, if Rei fought while wielding the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear, there was a chance he could seriously injure Culotte.

Of course, he intended to hold back, but even so, there was still a risk...

If this were Arogan, he'd have experience as a warrior, having trained his body and fought before, so he'd probably be somewhat accustomed to defense.

But the thief, Kyurot, wasn't exactly built for combat.

It's not that he avoids combat, but it's undeniable he's inferior to a combat specialist like Arogan.

Considering that, it was only natural for Rei to choose unarmed combat, where he could more easily control his strength.

It also served as training for unarmed combat.

"Make sure you don't regret it!"

Rei had his own reasons, but if asked whether Cullot could accept them, the answer would be no.

Shouting in displeasure, she drew her dagger and closed the distance to Rei in one swift motion.

Watching this, Rei didn't just stand by silently either, carefully reading Cullot's movements.

Since she usually fought primarily with the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear, this kind of bare-handed combat training was a rare occurrence for her.

...Though, when it came to actual fights, she often faced opponents barehanded.

True to her background as a thief, Cullot's movements to close the distance with Rei were agile.

His feints were probably better than Alogan's too.

Naturally, with Alogan wielding a Magic Sword... a longsword, and Cullot wielding a dagger, Cullot's attack speed was inevitably faster.

"Haah!"

A sharp cry accompanied the dagger thrust straight forward.

But Rei dodged the strike with a slight sway of his body, then reached out his hand.

Culotte didn't notice Rei's hand extending... so naturally, so utterly naturally, at first.

Even so, when Rei's hand was about to touch his right shoulder, he realized its presence and leapt backward on instinct.

Feeling the turf crunch beneath his boots, Cullot felt cold sweat run down his spine.

She understood then that she had truly been in mortal danger just now.

Had she noticed Rei's hand even a moment later, she would likely be sprawled on the grass right now.

She hadn't thought she could defeat Rei.

Still, she had thought... maybe she could land a single blow in return... She felt ashamed of that thought.

She had watched the mock battle with Arogan.

It was precisely because I had seen that mock battle that I thought I could fight Rei to some extent.

But in reality, just this one sequence of moves made me realize the overwhelming gap in skill between Rei and me.

(I can't believe Alogan even agreed to spar with someone like this. ...Well, it seems like he was mostly getting beaten one-sidedly anyway.)

Taking advantage of Rei not closing the distance, Culotte tried to calm her inner turmoil by forcing herself to think about unrelated things.

...The fact that his thoughts turned to Alogan rather than his childhood friend Scola, without him even realizing it, was surely proof that Alogan still held a significant place within him.

And as she unconsciously thought of Arogan, Kyurott felt her tense body in front of Rei regain some movement, however slight.

(This is doable!)

The moment that thought crossed her mind, Kyurott kicked off the ground again, closing the distance between herself and Rei.

Compared to before, her movements were noticeably lighter.

Aware of this change, she swung her dagger at Rei, who was closing in before her eyes.

Normally, using weapons without blades was standard practice for mock combat.

But...Culotte had no room to spare for such thoughts now.

She swung the dagger straight ahead, purely following her own will.

...Though deep down, she probably also held the conviction that even if her attack landed on Rei, it would mean almost nothing.

"Haah!"

"Too easy."

And indeed, the strike she confidently declared her best yet was easily dodged by Rei.

Worse still, she grabbed his wrist and used his own momentum to fling him into the air.

With the nimbleness of a true thief, she adjusted her posture mid-air and landed on the grass... yet the expression on Culotte's face was one of utter disbelief.

The fact that Rei hadn't even begun to fight seriously was obvious just from seeing him fighting barehanded.

What's more, though Kyurotto didn't know it, Rei still had a hidden card up his sleeve... the skill known as the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

"Damn! You're still as tough as ever."

Culotte knew Rei was strong, yet she refused to give up. That stubbornness showed her true grit.

Her own maximum attack had failed to land.

Culotte kicked off the grass again and charged toward Rei once more.

A direct attack wouldn't work on Rei.

With that now clear, Cullot's next choice was an attack heavy on feints.

The dagger, a weapon with short reach... precisely because of that, it was also a weapon well-suited for light, feint-based attacks.

Feinting a thrust, pulling the weapon back to his side, then thrusting toward a different spot.

But such attacks were, of course, easily read by Rei and effortlessly dodged.

Then again, that was probably exactly what Kyurott had expected from the start. Showing no sign of discouragement, she crouched low and launched a roundhouse kick aimed at Rei's legs.

"Whoa!"

Leaping backward, Rei confirmed it had passed right in front of him before stepping forward again.

Culotte, who had launched a sweeping kick that skimmed the ground, swung her dagger as a feint the instant she confirmed this.

It didn't need to hit; if it could buy even a little time... That was the thought behind the attack.

To Rei... no, to anyone of a certain skill level, Cullot's strike looked nothing but full of openings.

"Too easy. ...How many times now?"

As he said "naive," Rei kicked up the tip of the hilt of the dagger Kurutto was gripping. By the time he spoke again, Kurutto's arm was already seized by Rei, and she was being forced to the ground.

With her arms pinned behind her back and pressed into the ground, Cullot was utterly helpless at this point.

No matter how stubbornly competitive Kyurott was, there was no option to refuse to admit defeat in this situation.

"I give up."

"Then I win. ...Your speed and fighting spirit are good. They're good, but... your technique doesn't quite match up. Honestly, if we're just talking combat skill, Byune is better than you."

"Hmm."

Byune, who had been leaning against Set, raised her hand slightly at Rei's words.

"No way!? That kid!?"

Culotte shouted in disbelief at Rei's words.

But that reaction was understandable. Considering the age difference between Kyurot and Byune, Kyurot clearly had the advantage in raw physical ability.

Even so, Byune excelled at agile movements, leveraging her small stature, and she wielded a weapon crafted from materials of the rank S monster, White Cloud.

And above all... during the combat training held over the winter, Vihera had been Byune's primary sparring partner, with Rei and Marina occasionally joining in.

Every single one of them possessed combat strength ranking among the top in the Gilm guild.

Training with opponents like that meant the intensity itself was vastly different from ordinary combat drills.

Of course, from a thief's perspective, that was clearly unacceptable.

After all, as evident from Cullot, combat isn't a thief's primary profession.

While a certain level of combat skill is essential for those active as adventurers, the skills more often required are stealth, trap detection and disarming, lockpicking, and the like.

(Come to think of it, maybe Byune isn't really a thief anymore. A thief-like warrior might be a more fitting description.)

Thinking this, he offered a single word to comfort the shocked Kyurott.

"While Byune is better at combat, if we're talking pure thieving skill, I think Cullot is the better one, don't you?"

At the words of comfort, Cullot's expression softened just a little, as if relieved.
