From a distance, it looked like a small child.

But a normal child wouldn't be in a place like this at a time like this in the first place.

Moreover, the child sitting on the branch, illuminated by the moonlight streaming through the branches, revealed its monstrous form.

A life-sized doll... Perhaps that would be the right way to describe it.

But that was only if one insisted on saying so; ordinarily, calling it a doll would be a bit difficult.

A haphazard jumble of branches, leaves, and vines, it only resembled a child from a distance. Upon closer inspection, calling it a doll proved rather difficult.

"That thing..."

"What is it?"

Hearing the words directed at Rei, who had muttered, Vihera, clinging to Set's leg, voiced her confusion.

"Ah, over there... Huh?"

Rei snapped back to reality at Vihera's words. He glanced briefly at Vihera—though she was barely visible—then turned his gaze back toward where the doll-like thing had been. He froze, letting out a startled gasp.

Because the doll that had been there just a moment ago... no, that had been there, had already vanished.

Rei understood it wasn't a mistake on his part.

But as a matter of fact, the doll was no longer there.

"Wait a minute, Set!"

Rei hurriedly called out to Set, who was flapping his wings to leave.

"Grrr?"

Set growled, looking back at Rei perched on his back. Rei pointed at the ground... at the spot where the doll had been, and spoke.

"Set, land over there."

"Grrr."

Set didn't understand why Rei suddenly said that, but he descended to the ground anyway, trusting her words.

At that moment, Rei realized Set hadn't noticed the doll.

(Set's perception abilities are generally superior to mine. For even Set not to have noticed... Is that possible? No, then why was I able to notice it?)

Rei had found that doll purely by chance.

As Rei was about to go explore elsewhere, he called out to Vihera and glanced at the forest... and there it was. Or rather, it had been there.

Was it coincidence? A message from someone trying to reveal it only to Rei? Or something else entirely...? Rei wondered about it, but ultimately, dwelling on it wouldn't get her anywhere.

Honestly, Rei thought that if he could just see that doll again, things would probably become clear.

Of course, there wasn't any clear reason for it.

But he understood that the doll, which had revealed itself only to him, was undoubtedly connected to this Treant Forest.

"Going down? I thought I already gave it a quick look from above... Did you spot something else?"

Vihera, clinging to Set's front paws, voiced the question prompted by Rei's words.

It made sense Vihera hadn't seen the doll, since Rei had.

"Yeah. I just saw something that looked like a doll sitting on a tree branch."

"What do you mean 'doll-like'? ...And where is it?"

Vihera glanced toward the trees on the ground, slightly puzzled by Rei's words.

But there was nothing resembling a doll anywhere as Rei had described.

"Hey, Rei? You didn't just see things, did you? That can't be right, can it?"

"I definitely saw it... I think. But the moment I looked away, the doll was gone."

"Hmm... So it wasn't a mistake, then?"

Vihera knew full well the extent of Rei's abilities.

That's precisely why she didn't think Rei could have misperceived something...

Convinced the doll must have been there, Vihera felt a slight spark of interest and looked down at the ground again.

But even as Set descended to the spot Rei had indicated, Vihera kept scanning the surroundings. There was nothing there.

To be precise, there were trees, but nowhere was there any trace of the doll-like thing Rei had mentioned.

Timing it for when Set lowered his altitude close to the ground, Vihera released the hand he was holding onto.

With Vihera's physical abilities, he could land normally on the ground without rolling around.

Feeling Vihera release his foot, Set landed on the ground as usual.

He touched down, walked a short distance to slow his momentum.

Rei also dismounted from Set's back. Prepared for anything, he drew the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring and scanned the surroundings.

There was nothing around them. ...Indeed, not even the monsters that had relentlessly attacked them until they took flight atop Set, starting with Treant.

That fact alone was already abnormal.

Anything could happen.

Feeling that atmosphere, Rei once again scanned the surroundings.

What Rei felt, Set and Vihera likely felt as well.

While in the air, Vihera hadn't gone so far as to doubt Rei's words... but she hadn't fully believed them either. Seeing the current state of Treant's forest, however, she could clearly feel the stark difference from just moments ago.

As for Set, he had fully believed Rei's words, so he had no reason to doubt.

"...It's strange. Just now, so many monsters appeared, yet this time, absolutely nothing is coming out. Is this also related to the doll Rei saw?"

"I wonder. Probably, but I think it's related."

Rei answered Vihera's question while surveying the surroundings.

Even so, he remained vigilant, prepared for whatever might happen next.

"Grrrr..."

What should we do? Set's question made Rei pause to think.

There was a possibility that the doll he had seen earlier might have been a mistake or something.

But now, having descended into the forest and nothing happening... not even a single one of those monsters that appeared in such vast numbers showing up... it was certain something was definitely present in this vicinity.

"Let's explore a bit. ...There's definitely something here."

"Yeah."

Vihera immediately agreed, seemingly having no objection to Rei's suggestion.

Set gave a short growl and began surveying the surroundings.

Set used his Enhanced Scent skill to check for any abnormalities, but all that drifted to him was the scent of the forest; there was no particular unusual odor.

Then again, the only monsters that appeared in Treant Forest were plant-type monsters.

Thinking about it that way, it wasn't strange that only the scent of the forest lingered.

"Grrr..."

Unable to detect anything with his heightened sense of smell, Set growled apologetically at Rei.

Rei lightly tapped him on the back of the hand holding the Death Scythe, signaling not to worry about it.

That alone seemed to lift Set's spirits, and he purred happily.

"Now, now, make sure to scout the area properly. You never know what might be out there."

"Ah... yeah, sorry."

Prompted by Vihera, Rei took another look around.

The moonlight was blocked by the trees, making the surroundings dark, to say the least.

But the only ones here were Rei, Vihera, and Set—two people and one dog.

In this situation, it wasn't like the darkness made it impossible to see their surroundings.

...Though, if you asked whether that meant they could immediately spot anything suspicious, the answer would be no.

"Still, there's nothing here. ...That doll-like thing hasn't shown itself since then either."

Rei muttered while keeping watch over their surroundings... but as if that had been a signal, a branch on a nearby tree swayed.

Considering there was no wind blowing, and nothing else was moving, it was clearly something deliberate that made the branch rustle.

"Huh!?"

Rei instantly turned his gaze toward the rustling branch, but there was nothing there.

Vihera and Set also looked toward the branch, but they couldn't find anything either.

"It made a sound... right? And it was pretty unnatural."

"Yeah."

"Grr..."

Vihera and Set each responded to Rei's words.

Hearing their responses, Rei felt relieved that what he'd heard wasn't some kind of auditory hallucination... yet he still couldn't see anything around them.

Rei and Vihera possessed senses sharper than normal, and Set's senses surpassed even theirs.

The fact that even Rei couldn't detect anything was, in itself, abnormal.

Then, in the next instant, a branch in a direction other than where Rei and the others were looking swayed unnaturally once more.

"...Is it taunting us?"

It was just the act of making the branch rustle, but it looked like nothing less than a provocation.

Vihera must have felt the same. She channeled magic into her gauntlets, forming claws, ready to attack at a moment's notice.

"Well, I wonder. Calling us all the way out here means it's definitely intentional. At the very least, I doubt they're welcoming us."

Considering the damage they'd inflicted on Treant Forest, it was easy to predict they'd be resented, not welcomed.

They watched their surroundings intently for several minutes.

Once again, a tree branch swayed unnaturally.

But this time, it wasn't the branch where Rei and the others stood. It was a branch a little further away.

And as if that swaying branch were a signal, one after another, the branches began to move.

And it seemed as if they were gradually moving away from Rei and the others...

"Is it luring us? Does it want to lead us somewhere?"

"Us? We're their enemies, aren't we? ...But honestly, that's the only way it makes sense."

Vihera responded to Rei's words with a murmured question, and Set, who had been listening, growled in agreement with Rei's words.

As if to resolve the group's dilemma... or perhaps to deepen it, the same branch from before, slightly farther away, began swaying once more.

Seeing the swaying branch, Rei, Vihera, and Set—two humans and one beast—exchanged glances.

With something this blatant happening, it was certain something lay ahead.

The question was whether that something was a trap, or part of the very forest Rei was searching for...

Even so, Rei's hesitation lasted only a moment.

"Let's go check it out."

"...Are you sure? It might be a trap."

Though she knew it wasn't like her, Vihera voiced caution because Rei had spoken up with such proactive words.

That said, even as she spoke like this, Vihera couldn't quite hide her own feelings—that she agreed with Rei... no, that she wanted to accept the invitation even more eagerly.

Under normal circumstances, most adventurers would be wary of traps and proceed cautiously.

But... for better or worse, here were Rei, Vihera, and Set—all possessing top-tier combat prowess... superlative, even.

They possessed the firepower to simply brute-force their way through any ordinary traps lying about.

"Yeah, something might be there. But ultimately, unless we move forward, we won't know if there's something related to Treant Forest ahead, or if it's just a trap... right?"

"Well, that's..."

Either way, we won't get anywhere unless we move.

Vihera had no words to counter Rei's statement.

It was undeniably true.

...Though, in the worst case, there was always the option of simply putting distance between themselves and Treant Forest.

Before entering the forest, Vihera and Set were inside it, so that choice wasn't possible.

But now that they'd reunited, choosing that path was possible.

Even so, Rei chose to unravel the secrets of this place, the Treant Forest.

There was undoubtedly some clue right before his eyes.

It felt certain to Rei.

Though she couldn't fully grasp all of Rei's thoughts, Vihera still nodded, observing Rei's demeanor.

"Alright, let's go."

She murmured this partly because she didn't want to waste Rei's determination... but above all, because she felt her own desire to face a formidable foe.

Set judged that if Rei said it was fine, then it was fine... And with everyone in agreement, Rei breathed a sigh of relief.

"Alright. ...So, I'll lead the front, Set in the middle, and Vihera in the rear. Sound good?"

Set and Vihera silently nodded to Rei's formation instructions.

Thus, preparations complete... Rei and the others began walking ostentatiously toward the direction where the branches were swaying.
