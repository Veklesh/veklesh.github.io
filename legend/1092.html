After tying up the sand pirates on deck and subduing those inside the sand ship—whose resistance was weaker than those on deck, likely due to the king's presence—and repelling their desperate counterattack, a considerable amount of time had passed.

Even so, it was still not yet evening, so naturally, they decided to proceed toward Gosh... but here, a problem arose.

Namely, while it was fine that the sand ship now belonged to Rei , the question was how to transport it.

...Of course, those who raised concerns about this matter, like Augusto and Zarust, knew Rei possessed an Item Box. Common sense suggested the Sand Serpent might be possible, but the sand ship itself was impossible... Yet Rei, seemingly unconcerned by such doubts, touched the sand ship and stored it within the Misty Ring.

What exactly is an item box? Storing something as massive as the Sand Ship gave him a tangible understanding of it for the first time. Yet Rei himself, acting as if it were routine and showing no particular concern, spoke up.

"Well then, let's go."

"No, no, I never imagined you'd be so matter-of-fact about it... Normally, wouldn't you brag more? If you possess such a rare magic item as an item box?"

Augusto seemed to want to say, "Wait a moment," but Rei, seemingly unconcerned, turned his gaze toward the sand pirates who had been bound and were now being led away.

Thanks to the King's Intimidation effect, most had been captured without injury. Only those who happened to be aboard the sand ship had attacked, only to be repelled and wounded.

Of course, just because they were injured didn't mean they'd be treated.

No one here was the type to show such kindness to those who had attacked them.

Chained together in a line with ropes, their ends tied to the carriage and being dragged along as they walked through the sand.

The look on their faces was, naturally, despair... or so it seemed, until he noticed a defiant grin on the faces of several of the sand pirates.

(What? Do they actually think they can escape from this? No, they couldn't possibly escape in this immobilized state... Especially since they saw and felt what Set was like with their own eyes. They should know it's impossible.)

Then why? That question arises in Rei's mind.

"Zalust!"

Walking across the sand, Rei called out to Zaruust nearby.

Unlike before, the Zarust riding the camel, trying to calm the frightened animal, turned its gaze toward Set.

"What is it?"

"Don't you think the sand pirates are acting strange?"

"...The sand pirates?"

At Rei's words, Zarust turned his gaze toward the sand pirates bound together in a chain.

Since they were walking like this, the effect of the king's intimidation that Set had unleashed had already faded. While some sand pirates still showed expressions of fear toward Set, among them were several who wore smiles on their faces.

"Yeah, I see them. But why are they so relaxed? They're definitely going to be sold to slave traders if this keeps up."

"Something's up. ...Just to be sure, there aren't any of our guards in cahoots with the sand pirates, are there?"

No. Rei thought Zarust would state it definitively, but instead of answering immediately, he only wore a troubled expression.

Then, after a moment, he sighed and shook his head slightly.

"I... can't say for certain there isn't. This escort request was especially sudden. Because of that, we have new recruits. It's impossible to rule out the possibility one of them might be involved."

The faces of those accompanying them as guards flashed through Zarust's mind.

He excluded those who had been part of the party for a while and put those he recognized on hold. And indeed, the most suspicious were the new recruits.

Of course, he knew the new recruits too, and understood their personalities to some extent. But he didn't know them as deeply as his party members, nor had he interacted with them as much as the familiar faces.

Given that, the newcomers were indeed the most suspicious under the current circumstances.

As someone who was at least somewhat familiar with them, Zarust didn't want to suspect the newcomers...

(Still, the fact remains they were attacked by sand pirates.)

The desert's sheer vastness meant it wasn't easy for sand pirates to find targets.

Even if they possessed a sand ship.

But with the power of the Elema Trading Company, which could lend out sand ships, turning one or two rookie adventurers was hardly difficult.

(Working on assignments within Gosh, one naturally learns about the Elema Trading Company. Considering their recent influence...)

Even while engaging in the unpleasant act of suspecting his companions, he couldn't slack off on his job.

"So, what do you think we should do? Answer me from Rei's perspective."

From Rei's position. That is, asked for his opinion as a well-meaning collaborator, Rei opened his mouth while glancing at the other carriages.

"Well... it might be dangerous to assume only the guards are traitors. We can't rule out the possibility that some of the merchants are also betraying us."

"...I'd rather not think about it, though."

Having worked with these merchants for years alongside Augusto, Zarust probably didn't want to believe any of them could betray them.

But at the same time, he knew humans would betray without hesitation if given a weakness to exploit, so he couldn't dismiss Rei's words.

Watching Zarust's reaction, Rei continued.

"We arrive at Gosh tomorrow, right? Camping tonight?"

"That's right. ...So you're saying we move tonight?"

"It's possible. You're gathering intel from the sand pirates tonight, right?"

"That's the plan. Ideally, I'd gather intel now, but... moving is the priority."

Watching the sand pirates linked together in a chain, Zarust sighed.

He didn't voice it, but he suspected they wouldn't share information in front of their comrades.

If they did that, it would prove he'd betrayed his own people.

To overcome that psychological barrier, interrogating them one by one remained the best option.

(If I hear it from multiple people, I can usually tell who's lying.)

Several sand pirates, seemingly aware of Zarust's gaze upon them, nervously averted their eyes.

Some were likely pondering their own fates, while those smiling probably did so to hide their thoughts.

"Anyway, gathering information will have to wait until nightfall. For now, let's focus on moving forward. Rei might not understand, but the desert is something that takes human lives with ease."

"I know. It's not like I've never been in a desert before. ...Well, it's a dungeon desert, so I don't fully understand deserts either."

At the word "dungeon" coming from Rei's mouth, Zarust's expression shifted slightly, showing a hint of interest.

"A dungeon? I've always wanted to visit one myself... Unfortunately, there are none near Gosh, nor anywhere within the Kingdom of Solrain."

"A dungeon? A small country like ours couldn't turn one into a labyrinth city, could it? In fact, we'd probably struggle to conquer one on our own."

Whether the absence of a dungeon in one's own country was cause for joy or sorrow varied from person to person.

Reasons to rejoice: Creating a labyrinth city—a place of immense value—attracts merchants drawn to the magic stones and materials obtained from dungeon monsters. Simultaneously, adventurers flock to the dungeon itself.

This leads to economic growth, significantly boosting the nation's tax revenue.

The downside is the danger of the dungeon itself. Monsters could spill out, and the larger the crowd drawn to the dungeon, the greater the potential for conflict.

It's a case of extremes... or perhaps two sides of the same coin. To Rei's eyes, it was clear that Augusto leaned more toward the latter.

"Well, it's true that dungeons come with their share of problems."

"Dungeons aren't something that just appear whenever we want them, after all."

Augusto replied to Rei's words.

As they conversed like this while crossing the desert, evening eventually fell, and Zarust addressed Augusto.

"Augusto, I think we should start preparing camp soon. It's a bit early, but we also need to extract information from the sand pirates."

"...You're right. Then, let's do that."

Augusto considered it for a moment, but ultimately nodded in agreement with Zarust's suggestion, and preparations for camp began.

It was common knowledge that desert nights were cold, and temperatures dropping below freezing were not uncommon.

Compared to the scorching hell of daytime, it was hell in a different sense.

That's precisely why preparation is crucial when camping in the desert.

Things like preparing a campfire, meals, and setting up tents.

And, of course, caring for the camels pulling the wagons was non-negotiable.

Even so, this particular camp was undoubtedly going to be easier than usual. Because...

"Are you sure it's okay to leave the watch to Set?"

Zalust asked, pressing the point. Rei nodded back with a confident expression.

"Yes. No problem. As you know, Set is a griffin. He possesses senses far sharper than those of ordinary humans. If anyone comes to rescue the captured sand pirates, he absolutely won't let them slip by."

Rei's words were heard not only by Zarust but by the others as well.

This was intentional, meant as a warning in case anyone within the caravan or its guards had ties to the sand pirates... or to whoever was behind them.

With a Rank A monster keeping watch, few would dare make a move.

Even among the sand pirates, those who had been scheming to escape under cover of night heard Rei's voice and had no choice but to abandon their plans at that moment.

"So, Rei has his own tent, right?"

"Yeah. I'll handle that part, so don't worry about it. But Set doesn't seem to want to stray too far from me, so my tent will naturally end up near the center of the camp."

With Set on watch, it naturally had to be near the center of camp. Considering Set's abilities, he could fulfill his role as a sentry anywhere. But for August and Zarust, who had only just met Set today, they probably couldn't trust him that much.

The number of guards was fewer than usual, but Rei had been told that at least two would still be assigned to watch with Set.

Still, Rei accepted this without complaint.

If anything, he felt uneasy about August and Zarust's attitude, wondering if they truly trusted Set that much.

But as August and Zarust would say, they had witnessed firsthand a fraction of Rei and Set's power.

They believed that if Set had any intention of harming them, he could have done so at any time.

With camp preparations finished in this manner, it was time for dinner.

Normally, Rei would have taken his portion from the Misty Ring and eaten it, but since they had come all the way to the desert... to another country, he wanted to try the local cuisine. He accepted Augusto's invitation.

What was served was a porridge-like dish.

What made it a bit unusual was that instead of white rice, it contained finely shredded bread, and the soup base wasn't milk... but camel milk.

It was indeed what you might call a rough-and-ready man's dish, but considering it as the kind of meal adventurers in this world eat while active outside towns, it could be considered quite a luxurious offering.

In exchange for the meal, Rei gave Augusto and the others water from the Running Water dagger.

After drinking it, Augusto and the others even shed tears, praising the taste of the water.

For those who lived in the desert, water truly was something special... This incident made Rei keenly aware of that fact, and as a result, he received hospitality from Augusto and the others that left him feeling deeply honored.

Normally, captured sand pirates would receive only the bare minimum of food and water. However, thanks to the abundance of water provided by Rei's Water-Flowing Dagger, they were given a decent amount.

This was done out of concern that if they were too weak, the slave traders might haggle down their price.

And then... night deepened.
