The battle unfolding on that battlefield was supposed to be one where monsters, having regained their senses, fought each other while others seized the opportunity to escape—and those attempting to flee were to be prioritized for elimination.

But the Dark Elves' objective vanished almost immediately.

This was because several Dark Elves had charged headlong into the monster horde.

Whether driven by a rush for glory or unable to tolerate the sheer number of monsters... in any case, several Dark Elves charged into the swarm... and naturally, the monsters, having only just regained their senses, were disoriented for a few seconds and couldn't deal with these Dark Elves.

As a result, the Dark Elves who charged in first caught the monsters off guard and succeeded in defeating a number of them.

They succeeded... but when their comrades fell, the monsters naturally turned their hostility toward those responsible.

Consequently, the monsters near the dark elves stopped fighting each other and concentrated their attacks on the dark elves.

Of course, not every monster that regained its senses attacked the Dark Elves; at the edges of the horde, monsters naturally continued fighting each other.

Even so, the vast majority of monsters directed their attention toward the Dark Elf.

While they could handle monsters fighting each other, fending off a sudden, full-scale monster assault was another matter entirely.

"Dammit! What the hell were you thinking doing something like this?! Once this fight is over, I'm definitely going to get you!"

"Yeah. We'll need to vent our rage properly once this fight's over. So, you better not die in a place like this, okay?"

The dark elf man drew his bow, his voice filled with resentment... yet also mixed with concern for his fellow villagers. As he shouted, another dark elf man beside him, also firing arrows, yelled back.

Nodding at the man's words, the battle continued unabated... but ultimately, it was a case of many against few. The sheer numbers of the enemy held the advantage in combat.

There was no way the dark elves could hold off the overwhelming number of monsters as things stood.

To make matters worse, monsters that had initially been fighting each other stopped their battles upon seeing the surrounding monsters moving in one direction, and more and more joined them.

As a result, the number of monsters the Dark Elves had to fight increased, and they were gradually pushed back.

Even those who had initially charged into the monster horde now find themselves unable to resist the surrounding monsters and are retreating back toward the barrier's perimeter.

For now, those Dark Elves only received a mild reprimand from those around them, but that was solely because the battle was still ongoing.

Once this battle ended safely, they would undoubtedly be reprimanded.

No, getting scolded would be the mildest outcome. At worst, the possibility of intense combat training from Optis couldn't be ruled out.

If that happened... the worst possible outcome awaited those dark elf men who had lost control.

But perhaps it was a blessing in disguise that the current rampaging ones couldn't afford to think about such things.

They had to deal with the horde of monsters charging straight at them.

As the battle progressed in such a situation, the Dark Elves naturally began to be pushed back.

Yet, the dark elves didn't feel utterly doomed. That was likely because the barrier barrier was still behind them.

If they truly faced mortal danger, they could retreat into the barrier.

Then the monsters couldn't attack them.

The despair they felt when the barrier temporarily vanished was immense for the Dark Elves.

The fact that several Dark Elves charged into the swarm of monsters might have been driven by that very despair.

Compared to that despair, the Dark Elves didn't feel nearly as grim now that they had absolute safety behind them.

Of course, once the monsters entered the barrier's protective sphere, they would attack in an attempt to breach it.

If that happened, it would become difficult for the Dark Elves to leave the barrier's protective zone.

Regardless of having a safe zone behind them, the Dark Elves' desire was to avoid relying on that safe zone if possible.

Among them, naturally, was the desire to avoid burdening the World Tree by having the barrier attacked.

And then, while still somehow holding their ground despite being pushed back, one of the Dark Elves spotted a figure approaching them.

The figure wore a robe and a hood, but even so, the dark elf man who spotted it recognized who it was.

Because that figure held a scythe taller than themselves.

There was only one person in this settlement who wielded such a weapon.

Only Rei , who had come for treatment from the World Tree.

The moment he saw it, one of the dark elves shouted.

"It's Rei! Rei has arrived!"

In their current situation, being overwhelmed by the enemy, the best possible reinforcement had arrived.

The instant that thought crossed their minds, it happened.

Suddenly, some of the monsters swarming the Dark Elves were sliced to pieces all over their bodies.

The area of effect was one thing, but the power itself wasn't that strong.

Still, among the low-rank, small monsters like goblins, some had their necks severed by the ultrasonic waves, resulting in a number of monster corpses.

Monsters that were wounded but not killed, or those outside the ultrasonic wave's range, couldn't comprehend what had happened at all.

Suddenly... truly suddenly, without any warning, multiple monsters had their lives taken or were injured. It was only natural they wouldn't understand.

Had they paid a little more attention to their surroundings, they might have noticed that Set and the giant cicada, battling overhead, were slowly but surely drawing nearer.

Unfortunately, neither the monsters attacking the Dark Elves nor the Dark Elves struggling to fend off those monsters had any spare attention to spare for the battle unfolding overhead.

Set had wanted to fight away from the settlement, but in the struggle against the giant cicada, he had naturally drifted back toward the settlement... and thus, damage was inflicted upon the ground below.

The silver lining, if there was one, was that only the monsters suffered damage.

Had that giant cicada's earlier strike also harmed the Dark Elves, the battlefield would have instantly swung in the monsters' favor.

And the monsters' misfortune didn't end there.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAH!"

With that battle cry, Rei charged into the monster horde, swinging his Death Scythe—larger than himself—with all his might.

Spinning, he severed the torsos of several orcs, while the pommel crushed a goblin's skull. A single blow from the shaft sent several kobolds flying in a heap.

In terms of charging into the monster horde, it was the same tactic the dark elves had used at the start of the battle.

But... the decisive difference lay in his skill.

While the dark elves had some hunting experience, they had almost no experience in large-scale combat. Rei, on the other hand, had survived numerous major battles and, above all, was an adventurer known by the nickname Crimson.

Where several dark elves would eventually be overwhelmed by sheer numbers, Rei fought alone.

Monsters had their bodies severed one after another, collapsing to the ground. The surrounding area became impassable, littered with flesh, blood, entrails, and bones.

Had someone new to adventuring been present, they would have slipped on the blood and entrails and fallen flat on their face.

Amidst this chaos, Rei swung his Death Scythe while carefully choosing where to step, selecting ground that was still safe to tread. When there was simply no place to land after a jump, he used the Sleipnir Boots to kick off mid-air.

Such flashy maneuvers naturally drew the attention of Set, who was battling the giant cicada overhead.

Set, who was in a state close to stalemate, seized the opportunity and resolved to pick Rei up.

However, he couldn't simply toss the giant cicada aside like that, so he had to stop its movement, even if only temporarily.

Facing the giant cicada, now stronger from absorbing Rei's magic, such a feat was no easy task.

As a result, Set chose to sow doubt within the giant cicada.

"GRRRRRR!"

His first move was the Shock Magic Eye, the fastest strike among Set's skills.

Set understood its power was negligible, and it would likely be ineffective against an opponent like the giant cicada.

But... even so, it succeeded in momentarily removing himself from the giant cicada's focus.

"Giiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiii

Set's plan worked. Caught off guard by the sudden impact, the giant cicada, unable to comprehend what had happened, let out a cry of irritation.

Though the Shock Magic Eye's power is weak, even that becomes a source of irritation for the giant cicada.

It felt like being mocked, a profound humiliation.

Being a giant cicada monster, it had almost no facial expressions, yet its irritation still showed in its voice.

The giant cicada never considered that the shock might have come from anyone other than Set.

As it steadied itself with its four wings and turned back toward Set, the giant cicada was met by a Crystal Breath unleashed from Set's mouth, as if waiting for it.

Having repeatedly intercepted Set's fire breath with ultrasonic waves before, the giant cicada attempted the same defense this time... only to be startled the next instant, though it showed no expression.

Set thought it had intercepted its breath with ultrasonic waves, but it instantly transformed into tiny crystals and rained down upon the giant cicada.

The crystals themselves weren't particularly large, no bigger than fingertips.

Still, it possessed enough force to temporarily blind the giant cicada's eyes...

"Giiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiii

The giant cicada used ultrasonic waves again to completely obliterate the crystals blocking its vision, followed by a shockwave.

It was meant to catch the opponent off guard with the crystal breath blinding its eyes, but it caught them off guard even more.

The ultrasonic wave, unleashed with that intent, sliced through empty space, piercing it.

"Gigi?"

The giant cicada let out a puzzled chirp, noticing that Set, who had been relentlessly attacking it moments ago without moving away, was now nowhere to be seen.

Instinctively sensing that such persistence meant it couldn't have blinded its own eyes and fled, it scanned its surroundings.

But Set was nowhere to be seen.

This only deepened the giant cicada's suspicion.

It couldn't believe Set had fled; he must surely be waiting for an opening.

Therefore, he must be somewhere. Moving carelessly would only invite Set's attack... Such thoughts left the giant cicada frozen in place.

Set's previous attacks had posed no real threat, yet the giant cicada instinctively understood that Set was far more than that.

This might be due to the giant cicada possessing Rei's magical power, but regardless of the reason, to the giant cicada, Set was absolutely not someone who could be dismissed as a mere weakling.

And so, it waited for Set to make his move... which would bring Set a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

Watching the battle from the ground, Rei sensed something approaching her, even though she couldn't see it.

There was no way Rei could mistake what it was.

It was the partner born from his own magic.

And he understood why it had vanished, how it had achieved it.

(Optical camouflage skill!)

Anticipating Set's approaching skill, Rei swung the Death Scythe.

The orc who tried to block the Death Scythe's attack with his club was severed in the next instant, club and all, by the scythe's blade channeled with magic.

(Optical Camouflage is arguably Set's trump card skill right now. And it's the kind of skill you can only use once per battle.)

To be precise, it becomes usable again after thirty minutes have passed since its last use. But unless it's an exceptionally large-scale battle, fights rarely last more than thirty minutes.

...Though, the battle between Set and the giant cicada had already lasted over thirty minutes since it began.

Set judged that now was the moment he needed that skill.

Using optical camouflage to force the fight into close quarters, where Set excelled, might have worked.

But that would only work once, and if that strike failed, picking up Rei would become difficult.

Therefore, the best course of action at this moment was to pick up Rei.

Having made that judgment... Set, now transparent, charged into the swarm of monsters attacking Rei.

Descending and colliding while still invisible, the impact was immense, sending goblins, kobolds, and orcs flying one after another.

He continued toward Rei, still invisible.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set deactivated his optical camouflage, revealing his form.

The monsters were shaken by the sudden appearance of the griffin, but Rei approached Set, swinging his Death Scythe...

「Set！」

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Without nearly slowing down, he leapt onto Set's back. As if for good measure, he swung the Death Scythe, slicing through the monsters as the man and beast charged skyward.
