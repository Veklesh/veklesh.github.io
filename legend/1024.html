"Heh heh, I never expected Daskar to come see me off."

Marina flashed an amused smile and turned her gaze toward the figure standing before her.

There stood Daskar, just as Marina had said.

However, he came here not as the lord seeing them off, but as Daskar the individual, as evidenced by the fact he brought no subordinates with him.

Of course, being a lord, his guards were stationed at a slight distance, ready to rush to his aid if anything happened.

Having served as a knight in the royal capital in his youth, Daskar was confident in his own abilities.

He trained daily, albeit between his duties as lord, and his physique was truly rock-solid. ...It wasn't entirely wrong to say he used that training to blow off steam from the stress of his duties.

"...If it were just Marina, fine. But Lady Elena and Lady Vihera leaving Gilm together? Of course. Honestly, you go and make things complicated."

The slightly resentful look in his eyes likely stemmed from the fact that if Elena were to suffer any injury in this matter, Daskar would share the responsibility.

Daskar himself wasn't involved in this matter, but it was still a fact that the guildmaster of the guild under his jurisdiction had dragged Elena into it.

"Oh, oh, don't get so angry. Daskar would be in trouble if our forest disappeared, wouldn't he?"

"Well... that's true."

Though clearly displeased, Daskar grudgingly conceded Marina's point.

Marina was one of the most powerful dark elves, and her influence was correspondingly strong.

Thanks to her, Gilm could manage to conduct trade with the dark elves, however meagerly.

In that sense, the fact that the World Tree in the forest where Marina lived was sick wasn't entirely someone else's problem for Daskar either.

"Rest assured. With this much firepower assembled, there's no way Elena-sama could come to harm."

Those words were an undeniable truth.

The group comprised Rei , Elena, Vihera, and Marina—all possessing high combat prowess. Audban and Ara also boasted considerable fighting strength. In terms of combat power, Set was also present.

Furthermore, though Byune had the lowest combat strength among the group, she possessed a certain level of skill as a thief.

"...Well, that's true enough. But still, Marina's about to set out on a journey, so why on earth is she dressed like that?"

Daskar muttered in exasperation, his gaze fixed on Marina... or more precisely, on the clothes draped over her figure, which was undeniably appealing to men.

What she wore was less revealing than the party dresses she usually wore at the guild, but it was still essentially a party dress. It certainly didn't seem like suitable attire for travel.

"Oh, Daskar, you know what kind of clothes I wear when I'm adventuring, don't you?"

At those words, Daskar nodded resignedly.

After all, even the stories Daskar had heard from his father before were about her wearing party dresses, albeit slightly different ones than what she had on now.

"Well, we can't waste too much time here. I'd better be going."

"...Be careful."

With that, Marina opened the door of the nearby carriage and stepped inside.

The interior of the carriage was surprisingly spacious for a carriage, furnished with comfortable seating.

It was Elena's carriage, a magical item possessing abilities similar to the magic tent Rei carried.

No, it would be more accurate to say it possessed superior capabilities to the magic tent, since Elena's father, Duke Kerebel, had poured a fortune into having an alchemist create it.

The carriage itself was befitting of Elena, equipped with several special abilities.

The horses pulling the carriage are, of course, no ordinary steeds; they have undergone rigorous training since they were foals.

Seated on the driver's platform, controlling those horses, is Ara.

Confirming Marina had boarded the carriage, she urged the horses forward.

Normally, Elena would bring a servant to act as coachman, but since this was a meeting with Rei, it was preferable to keep the number of people involved as low as possible.

Beside the carriage stood Set.

While ordinary horses would be frightened by Set, the horses pulling this carriage showed absolutely no sign of fear toward him.

As Set was fundamentally a gentle creature, he was very pleased by the horse's demeanor.

...Though, having undergone rigorous training, Set completely ignored Seto's attempts to play.

On Set's back sat Yellow.

As usual, they were moving along the road, exchanging sounds like "Kyu-kyu, Glu-glu," a conversation that, to an outsider, made it impossible to tell if they were actually communicating.

Yet, to Ara watching from the driver's seat, it was a mystery how their intentions communicated so clearly.

A first-rate horse, obvious to any observer, pulled the carriage. Beside it walked the griffin, Set, with the dragon cub, Yellow, riding on his back.

Such a carriage couldn't possibly go unnoticed, drawing many glances from those leaving Gilm and those trying to enter it.

(Well, if we follow the directions I was given, we'll veer off the main road partway anyway, so I don't think we need to worry about attracting attention.)

Ara, seated at the driver's seat, understood that among the gazes directed at the carriage... and at them, there were also malicious ones.

She suspected they were probably connected to bandits or something similar, but since there were basically no bandits operating around Gilm anyway, there was no need to worry about it now.

Besides, even if bandits did appear, in terms of combat strength, they could easily match any local bandit group... no, they possessed enough firepower to annihilate an entire bandit group, so there was no problem at all.

In fact, against common bandits, Ara wielding the magic item Power Axe could handle them single-handedly.

"The Dark Elf Forest, huh. I hope it proves a valuable experience for Elena-sama."

He murmured, his thoughts drifting to his mistress inside the carriage.

Meanwhile, Elena, the subject of those thoughts, was handing Byune a loaf of bread baked with ground nuts found inside the carriage.

"Is it tasty?"

"Mmm!"

At Elena's words, Byune nodded, stuffing her cheeks full of bread and making a munching sound.

(She's like a squirrel, really.)

Sitting on the sofa inside the carriage, Rei watched Byune and pondered.

Truthfully, seeing Byune like this now, it was no wonder many imagined a squirrel.

"So, it took about ten days by regular carriage to get from here to the Dark Elf Forest, right? Then, how long would it take with Elena's carriage?"

Marina, who had been sitting on the sofa, glanced at Rei, thought for a moment, then spoke.

"I can't say exactly how fast this carriage can go, but it's clearly faster than a regular one, so it'll definitely shave off a few days."

Marina answered, her gaze drifting outside.

In fact, her words were not wrong at all.

This carriage was a magic item, vastly superior to ordinary carriages in performance, and naturally, that included increased speed.

Add to that the fact that it was drawn by top-class horses, and it was impossible for it not to be fast.

"Then let's aim for a week. To the Dark Elf Forest... Hmm?"

Muttering that far, Rei's gaze suddenly shifted out the carriage window as if just now noticing something.

There stood Set, with Yellow on his back.

Watching Set intently, Rei then turned to Marina and spoke again.

"If it's just about channeling magic into the World Tree, why not just have me ride Set alone? We wouldn't need days traveling by carriage."

Even if this carriage is a magic item and travels faster than a regular one, that speed is still limited to the ground.

Compared to Set, who could fly freely through the sky, it was undeniably slow.

Therefore, Rei's suggestion that he and Set could simply go quickly and infuse the World Tree with magic, without needing such a large group to head to the Dark Elf Forest, was certainly not wrong. However...

"Unfortunately, a barrier protects the forest. Without me or Audban, no one can enter. ...Well, perhaps Rei could force his way through, but that would destroy the barrier. I'd rather avoid that."

"Ah... Yeah, I see."

Marina's words made Rei imagine himself getting caught in the barrier, unleashing his full magic power to force his way through, inflicting fatal damage on the barrier.

This wasn't mere fantasy; it was entirely possible for Rei and Set.

Since the World Tree's magic was also used in that barrier, forcibly breaking through would damage the World Tree itself.

They were heading to the forest where Marina's tribe lived to heal the World Tree. Damaging it would defeat the entire purpose.

Worse still, considering Rei's magical power, the possibility that it could be a fatal blow could never be denied.

(No, it's not that it can't be denied—it would undoubtedly be a fatal blow.)

Listening to Marina and Rei's exchange, Audban turned his gaze toward Rei.

A look of awe lingered in that gaze, though he himself was completely unaware of it.

Audban recalled the magical power he had sensed from Rei yesterday.

Even though Audban's ability to sense magic power wasn't particularly high, the moment she removed the ring, she was overwhelmed by that magic power.

It was easy to imagine that if such magic power collided head-on with the World Tree's barrier, it would be a fatal blow, even to a World Tree not in its current weakened state.

(Scary... Yes, this is the feeling of fear. How can Lady Marina and the others keep their composure around Rei?)

Suddenly, he felt eyes on him. When Rei turned his gaze toward Audban, she immediately looked away.

Rei considered speaking to Audban, but decided that addressing him now would only push him further away. Instead, he reached for the table in front of the sofa and brought the tea to his lips.

It was tea Elena had brewed, and while not quite as good as Ara's, it still tasted quite delicious.

...Maybe because Elena made it.

"So, how is it? I've been learning a lot from Ara, but I still can't brew it quite as well as she does."

Elena asked timidly, her usual confident expression nowhere to be seen.

Seeing Elena like this, Rei smiled and spoke.

Since they were inside the carriage, her hood was down, so Elena could clearly see the smile on her face.

"I think it's perfectly delicious. For something brewed by an amateur, isn't it top-notch? I think it's even better than the last time you made it for me."

"Really?!"

Her timid expression vanished instantly, replaced by a beaming smile.

After all, Elena was a duchess's daughter. She had almost no experience brewing tea.

She had treated Rei to tea before, but back then, her brewing skills were even more clumsy than they were now.

Thinking about it now, being able to brew proper tea for Rei at this very moment was fortunate for Elena.

"A duchess brewing tea sounds like it might be rather... unpleasant, but... it's delicious."

"Yes, the tea you brewed, Elena, is delicious. So much so that I'd like our staff to learn from you."

"Hmm."

Vihera, Marina, and Byune each praised Elena's tea, and Audban agreed, albeit more reserved.

Normally, he would have seized this moment to lavish praise on Elena, but with Rei nearby, he couldn't quite muster his usual enthusiasm.

While Audban did have a fondness for beauties and young girls, if asked whether he'd stake his life to pursue that passion, the answer was no.

"Still, it seems we'll have some leisurely time like this for a while. Hey, Rei. Why don't we take this chance to talk properly?"

Marina, still seated on the sofa, cast a sidelong glance at Rei. Elena and Vihera, who had been smiling mere seconds ago, each reacted to her.

Elena flashed a slightly displeased expression before quickly smoothing it away, while Vihera, in stark contrast to Elena, wore an amused smile as she turned her gaze toward Marina.

Unaware of the two's reactions, Rei paused for a moment before speaking.

"You say 'talk slowly,' but what exactly are we supposed to talk about?"

When would Rei notice the secret battle unfolding between the women...? That, no one knew.
