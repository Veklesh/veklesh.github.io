"Um... I don't know how to put this. ...Yes, that's the only thing I can say. It's terrible."

At Vihera’s words, everyone—even Rei and Set, the very ones who had caused this disaster—couldn’t help but nod in agreement.

But that was only natural. A monster as large as Set had been impaled by a harpoon and couldn’t even flee; it had been forcibly dragged all the way here.

Naturally, a monster of that size couldn’t hide its body in the water once it reached the shallows, and its entire body had been lacerated by the rocks.

Its scales were peeling off, blood was flowing, and flesh was exposed.

With such wounds covering its entire body, Vihera’s reaction—that it looked terrible—was only natural.

“Even so. There aren’t many ways to take this thing down cleanly in that situation, so I guess it can’t be helped. …So, is this a monster? Well, I figured as much.”

Was it a fish, or a monster…? While Rei hadn’t been able to tell for sure while the creature was in the sea, he’d be able to tell for certain now that they’d managed to haul it ashore.

“I had a hunch the moment it flung its scales.”

Logically speaking, it’s hard to imagine a fish that shoots scales.

No, wait—on Earth, there’s a fish called the archerfish that shoots a jet of water from its mouth to kill insects for food, so it wouldn’t be strange if there were a fish in Elgin that used flying scales to catch its prey, he reconsidered.

“Still… naturally, I don’t know about this monster. Bistol. Do you know anything about it?”

Rei asked Bistol this because he knew information about a monster called Guztos.

But Bistol, upon being asked, shook her head regretfully.

“I’m sorry, but this is the first time I’ve seen this monster, too. …Given how big it is, I’d think it would be famous by now.”

After all, Bistol was a merchant who operated in Gilm and the surrounding frontier areas—in other words, places without a sea.

That’s precisely why she’d come along with Rei and the others to catch fish in the sea.

Her knowledge of Guztos was nothing more than a coincidence.

“Hmm, in that case… Marina?”

"I’ve never seen one either."

“Elena?”

“Same here, I don’t know.”

「Vihera？」

"I've never seen that one either."

“……I guess I’ll ask. Byune?”

"Mm."

In response to Rei’s words, Byune muttered a single word, as she always did.

But this time, Rei understood exactly what Byune was trying to say.

Because Byune had shaken her head.

Seeing that, it was crystal clear that Byune didn’t know anything about this monster either.

“Well, in that case… I guess we’ll just take the magic stone for now. We’ll deal with the proof of defeat parts and stuff like that later.”

No one objected to Rei’s suggestion.

If they knew what kind of monster this was, they could have butchered it right here... but since they didn’t, there was a chance that if they butchered it carelessly, the parts they discarded might actually be valuable materials or parts needed for the hunt certificate.

(Well, the edible parts are probably quite substantial. …From a distance, it looks like a black sea bream.)

He looked at the monster again and thought that to himself with a sense of realization.

Yes, the true identity of the monster that attacked Rei and Set was a black sea bream.

Of course, a black sea bream that’s three meters long and attacks by flinging its scales isn’t exactly your average black sea bream.

It merely resembled a black sea bream among the fish Rei knew; aside from its size, there were many subtle differences.

(Come to think of it, even though it flung so many scales, there were spots on its body where the scales were missing… well, I can’t rule out the possibility that the scales were chipped off by rocks.)

Watching the fish monster, he pulls out his knife to retrieve the magic stone.

Nearly half of the monster’s body was still submerged in seawater, and small fish and crabs were gathering, likely drawn by the scent of the blood flowing from it.

“Since I went to the trouble of defeating this monster, I really don’t want the fish and crabs to eat it…” thought Rei. But when he recalled how many times the monster’s massive body had slammed into rocks along the way as he dragged it here with Set, he found himself strangely convinced that it was too late to worry about that now.

(Well, if I think of it as them cleaning up the innards for me, having the fish and crabs around isn’t so bad.)

While many monster organs can be sold as materials, half of them had already scattered into the sea on the way here.

Given that, he didn’t think there was much point in removing the innards now, so he decided to use them as bait for the fish and crabs for the time being.

I gathered them up, thinking that maybe there wouldn’t be any meat left to eat.

“With a fish this big, it must be quite a substantial meal. …Though Rei and Set would probably polish it off in no time.”

Marina murmured this as she looked at the fish once more.

“Grr!”

As if he’d heard Marina’s words, Set let out a cheerful cry, as if to say, “Leave it to me!”

...Incidentally, Set was being fed the fish Marina and the others had caught, and he seemed to be in quite a good mood.

“Anyway, if you’re going to get the magic stones, you’d better hurry. Look, a lot of fish and crabs are gathering over there…”

At Bistol’s words, Rei looked back into the water… and there, compared to just a moment ago, an overwhelming number of fish and crabs had gathered.

No, that wasn’t all—there were even octopuses among them.

“They’ve really gathered in droves.”

Muttering in disbelief, Rei hurriedly extracted the magic stone from the monster’s body.

Since the monster’s abdomen had already split open on the way here, he managed to retrieve the magic stone without much trouble.

Next, he stored the monster’s corpse inside the Misty Ring… and turned his gaze to the net placed on a rock a short distance away.

The net there—or, more precisely, the fish inside it—were the ones Rei had caught before the monster attacked him.

Even while fighting the monster, Rei hadn’t let go of the net, holding it in the hand not occupied by the Death Scythe or harpoon.

Whenever he absolutely had to use both hands, he even went so far as to place it on Set’s back.

...As a result, the fish in the net were unharmed... but in exchange, Set’s back had taken on a faint fishy smell.

That said, for Yellow, perhaps the fishy smell on Set’s back was reassuring, or maybe it stimulated his appetite… he stayed still on Set’s back while Set happily ate the fish.

“Mmm! Mmm!”

Rei was feeling all warm and fuzzy looking at Set, but Byune tugged on his Dragon Robe, snapping him back to reality.

“What’s wrong?”

“Hmm!”

In response to Rei’s words, Byune gazed into the depths of the sea—toward the spot where the monster had been just moments ago—before holding out her hand toward Rei.

Not understanding what that meant, Rei could only tilt her head in confusion.

They’d been together long enough that he’d come to understand, more or less, what Byune was trying to say.

But that was only a general idea, not the whole picture.

As a result, Rei’s gaze turned to Vihera, the only person who fully understood what Byune was trying to say.

“Vihera, what is Byune trying to say?”

“She wants a net, right? Not the one with the fish in it, but the kind you use to scoop things up.”

Hearing this, Rei looks back into the water.

There, fairly large fish and octopuses were scrambling to eat the monster’s spilled innards.

Among them were fish resembling moray eels, creating a scene that could easily be described as a sort of fish paradise.

With such a massive gathering of fish and crabs, it wasn’t strange to think that instead of going to the trouble of fishing, they’d want to scoop them all up with a net.

“Got it. Here.”

Saying that, she took a net out of her Misty Ring and handed it to Byune.

Holding the net, Byune looked reasonably pleased, even to Rei’s eyes. …Though, since Byune’s expression hadn’t changed from usual, it would be more accurate to say that Rei sensed an air of amusement.

With the net in hand, Byune stood on the rock.

The gaze with which Byune looked down at the sea from the rock was sincere, coming from the bottom of his heart.

It was a level of focus that wouldn’t look out of place even compared to a professional fisherman.

The gazes of those around him were drawn to Byune’s serious demeanor… and soon, Byune’s hand holding the net moved swiftly.

And inside the net she scooped up from the water’s surface were several fish… and even a fish resembling a moray eel.

“Oh my, that’s amazing, Byune. That fish is quite aggressive, and I hear it’s pretty hard to catch… but I’ve heard it tastes quite good? Though, apparently, the texture is closer to meat than fish.”

Hearing the word “meat,” not only Byune, but even Set and Yellow turned their gaze toward her with interest.

Of course, it’s not that she dislikes fish, but she probably likes meat too.

Facing the gazes of Set and Yellow, Byune gave off a slightly disappointed vibe... and gently handed the moray eel-like fish to Set and Yellow.

Although the surface is slippery, Byune doesn’t seem to have any trouble holding it.

Is this a skill honed as a thief... or is it simply because Byune is used to handling things like this?

Rei couldn’t tell, but in any case, there was no doubt that Byune had decided to give the moray eel-like fish to Set and Yellow.

...Though, that decision was likely made precisely because there were still plenty of similar fish in the sea.

“Guruu?”

“Is that okay?” Set asks with wide, round eyes.

To Set, Byune silently nodded in response.

And so, the moray eel-like fish was handed over, and Set immediately picked it up in his beak and placed it on the ground—in a spot covered with countless small stones.

Yellow, who had been watching from Set’s back, fluttered down to the ground with his tiny wings and began nibbling at the fish Set had placed there.

The moray eel-like fish isn’t all that big.

In fact, if the two of them ate it, it would be gone in no time.

(Well, I wonder if Yellow can get his fill, even if Set can.)

Considering Yellow’s size, there’s bound to be a difference in stomach capacity.

Thinking this, Rei filleted the fish from the net and began storing them one after another in the Misty Ring.

……Perhaps because the innards of that giant black sea bream-like monster were still there, no other fish came swimming over, drawn by the smell of ordinary fish blood.

About ten minutes passed…

“Kyu! Kyu-kyu! Kyuu!”

Just as he finished processing all the fish and stowed them in the Misty Ring, he heard that sound.

Rei easily recognized it as Yellow’s cry… and turned his gaze toward the source of the sound.

There, he saw the same scene he’d witnessed before by the river: Yellow fighting a crab.

The only difference, however... was the size of the crab Yellow was facing.

Compared to the stream crab Yellow had fought in the river before, the crab Yello was fighting now was clearly much larger.

And of course, as the crab grew larger, its shell became harder.

"Kyu!"

Yellow launched an attack on the crab, but the crab moved swiftly... and, taking advantage of its surroundings, hid itself in a crevice between the rocks.

Once hidden like that, no matter how small Yellow is, he can’t attack the crab tucked away in the crevice.

"Kyu! Kyu-kyu! Kyuu!"

"Come out!" Yellow let out a threatening cry, but the crab showed no sign of emerging from the crevice.

(Unlike by the river, it seems the crab isn’t even bothering to engage Yellow? …Well, thinking about it logically, why would it fight Yellow when it has a place to hide?)

With the stream crab, it was partly because the crab was brave, but the fact that there was nowhere to hide was probably a big factor too.

Thinking that, Rei shifted his gaze to Set.

Having finished eating the moray eel-like fish, Set lay on his side, seemingly completely unfazed by the fact that the ground beneath him was made of small stones and rocks rather than soft soil.

With the gentle sunlight and refreshing sea breeze, it was the perfect setting for Set, now that his appetite was satisfied, to take a nap.

“At this rate, we won’t be able to head out to sea for a little while longer.”

“Hmm, well then, could you help me with my fishing for a bit? For some reason, the bait keeps getting snatched away.”

Hearing Elena say this, Rei nodded, figuring he had plenty of time to spare, and joined Elena—along with Marina and Vihera—in enjoying a spot of fishing.
