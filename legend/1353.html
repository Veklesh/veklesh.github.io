The adventurer who encountered Slesha, faced with her unconscious and pleading for help for her companions, immediately decided to return to Gilm.

They should have completed the request to gather specific magical herbs, but they couldn't abandon Slesha in this situation.

It was also undeniable that the fact Slesha was quite beautiful played a part in that decision.

"Sigh. Seriously, why do I always end up..."

Even as he muttered this, he scooped up the unconscious Slesha and turned on his heel.

Since he'd been the first to dash out after the gate opened today, he was the fastest man on foot among those not using carriages or horses.

(If only I'd appeared before the carriage folks, I wouldn't have had to carry her like this...)

He grumbled inwardly, yet still didn't abandon Slesha. Was it kindness... or ulterior motives?

In any case, the man hoisted Slesha... cradling her sideways as he made his way back to Gilm.

Naturally, this meant encountering other adventurers and merchants who had left Gilm after him was not uncommon.

Feeling the stares of the others, the man hurried back to Gilm.

...If this man's appearance had been suspicious, he might have looked like a kidnapper, but fortunately—or perhaps unfortunately—he was a fairly well-known figure as a solo adventurer.

"Hey, Runo. What's with that woman?"

Solo adventurer... Runo was called out by a familiar adventurer on the road.

"Nah, she just suddenly appeared and collapsed. And something about her companions being eaten in the forest or something..."

"The forest? Hey, is that true?"

"Well, I don't know. She just said that one thing and passed out."

"So, your magic eye couldn't do anything about it?"

The woman who was part of the party with the man talking to Runo asked him that.

Runo, who possessed the Magic Eye, was a fairly well-known figure even in Gilm.

That's precisely why the woman probably thought... maybe the magic eye could do something.

But when asked, Runo silently shook his head.

"Magic Eyes aren't some all-purpose, convenient tool that can do anything."

Though Runo possessed the Demon Eye, an ability to see magic power, it wasn't particularly useful in the current situation.

"Magic eyes aside, you really said this woman was devoured by the forest?"

The man interrupted Runo and the woman's conversation to ask.

The reason they were so concerned was that the men had accepted a request today to bring back the meat of two Fang Boars from that very forest.

Located not far from Gilm, it was a place adventurers often headed to on quests.

If such a place was rumored to devour people, it was only natural to be concerned.

But even when the man said that, Runo had no words to answer him.

"Well, what can I say? I only heard bits and pieces myself. Before I could get the full story, I ended up passed out like this."

Even in her unconscious state, deep fatigue lingered on Slesha's face.

That was why Runo had decided it was better to let Slesha sleep rather than wake him now.

But the men were heading into the forest now. Hearing such unsettling words about being devoured by the forest, they couldn't just stay silent.

"Grrr... What should we do? Go into the forest?"

"Well, even if you say that... I'm not going to get eaten by the forest, thank you very much! I don't know what happened, but something that dangerous..."

Leaving the two debating their course of action behind, Runo took the woman and departed.

He had explained the situation as best he could; deciding what to do next wasn't his job.

Passing several merchants, adventurers, and travelers along the way, Runo eventually arrived at the main gate of Gilm.

The peak of people leaving the city had ended, and things had calmed down for the moment. The guards were each enjoying a brief respite.

That's precisely why, when they saw Runo returning to Gilm shortly after leaving... and saw him carrying a woman in his arms, they stared in utter shock.

That said, they didn't immediately recognize Runo as a criminal who had brought the woman back by robbing her of consciousness. This was partly because Runo himself had a certain level of notoriety, and partly because they reasoned that if he had actually done such a thing, he wouldn't have dared to appear so openly before the guards like this.

He concluded that Runo must have found the woman collapsed on the road for some reason and brought her back like this.

"Runo, what's going on? Who's that woman?"

"Seems she was running off the road somewhere. Then, just as she came before me, she started saying something about being eaten by the forest and passed out."

"...Eaten by the forest?"

One of the guards tilted his head.

If she'd been eaten by monsters in the forest, that would make sense.

But what does it mean to be eaten by the forest?

"I don't know either. Like I said, that's all he said before passing out. We couldn't just leave him, so we brought him here to Gilm."

Hearing this, the guards exchanged glances.

How should they interpret this?

If it was simply an attack by a monster, it was unfortunate, but that fell under an adventurer's personal responsibility.

But if there was some kind of abnormality... and if it could cause significant damage to Gilm... then as guards, they couldn't just ignore it.

Determining that was the difficult part... but in the end, it was decided to leave this matter entirely to the guild.

The phrase "devoured by the forest" was unsettling, but ultimately, only one party had suffered.

I feel sorry for them in many ways, but as a guard, I can't show favoritism to just one party.

If we did that, parties would start lining up to demand our assistance one after another.

As it is, the number of guards in Gilm is far from ample.

Given that reality, the decision had to be made to cut ties where necessary.

Runo understood this, so she didn't harbor any resentment toward the guards.

The fact that the female guard called out Slesha's guild card from her pocket, revealing her rank was D, probably played a part too.

Rank D adventurers are technically considered veterans, but even so, there are countless adventurers of that rank in any Gilm.

To put it bluntly, monsters capable of nearly wiping out adventurers of that caliber are hardly rare in guilds located in Gilm.

You could probably find countless examples like that if you looked around a bit.

(Still... I wish at least one guard had come along.)

Runo thought this as they walked down the main street, carrying Slesha in a side-hug.

Naturally, such behavior drew a lot of attention, making them the focus of many passersby.

"Wow... Hey, look at that. Amazing, isn't it? Makes me envious. I wish someone would carry me like that too."

"Pfft, if I carried someone like you, my arms would break... Ow! Hey, stop it! That hurts!"

"That adventurer's brave, huh? Luna, was it? Huh? Runo? I'm pretty sure that's the adventurer's name."

Such conversations reached Runo's ears.

She had plenty she wanted to say, but she judged that speaking up now would only draw more attention and lead to no good. So she kept moving.

(At times like this, if I were in a party, someone else could... especially if a woman were nearby...)

The difference between him alone carrying a woman and a woman being near him, even if she was alone, was enormous.

It was only because Runo was carrying her openly that it seemed acceptable. Had he been moving stealthily to avoid notice, it wouldn't have been surprising if the guards had stopped him.

Overcome with shame, Runo finally arrived at the guild.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, the guild's busiest morning hours had already passed, and few adventurers remained inside.

...Though it wasn't exactly a completely quiet time like ten in the morning, so there were still a fair number of people.

Precisely because of that, Runo, carrying a woman into the guild, stood out considerably.

Adventurers scanning the remaining quest boards for something worthwhile, adventurers eating a late breakfast at the tavern... and the guild staff behind the counter.

Under the gaze of all these people, Runo approached the counter.

"Runo-san, I believe the request you accepted was for gathering magical herbs, not a kidnapping case?"

The familiar receptionist said this with a serious expression.

"It ain't kidnapping. ...I was walking the road when this woman suddenly appeared from off the path, saying her companions had been eaten in the forest. Her name's Slesha. Rank D adventurer."

Hearing it from Runo's mouth, she must have decided it wasn't a joke or anything like that.

The receptionist woman thought for a moment before glancing at the documents nearby.

She moved at a pace that made it seem impossible she could read everything, but Runo, who had known her for quite some time, understood that was sufficient to review the documents.

After scanning all the papers, the receptionist shook her head.

"It seems there's no record of any request being made to her at the Gilm. At least, there's no trace of an adventurer named Slesha receiving a request within the last five days or so. That leaves only one possibility... she might have accepted a request outside the Gilm..."

"Or encountered this situation while en route to Gilm?"

Runo finished his own sentence, and the receptionist nodded.

But immediately, another receptionist standing nearby asked curiously.

"But if he was heading to Gilm, why was he off the road? The road leads straight to Gilm, so there's no need to go out of his way to leave it, right?"

That was an undeniable fact, but at the same time, she couldn't say she fully understood the mindset of adventurers heading for Gilm.

"Probably because they wanted to bring some monster materials or something to Gilm first, to show off how skilled they are."

Having worked as an adventurer in Gilm for quite some time, Runo generally understood what adventurers arriving in Gilm during this season were thinking.

The receptionist felt the same way. ...No, actually, since she had frequent direct contact with such adventurers, she probably understood the situation even better than Runo.

"I see. ...Well, we need to hear the details. This way, please."

The receptionist led Runo past the counter.

She led him to the treatment room.

In a guild where hot-blooded adventurers gather, it's not uncommon for guild staff to get injured.

There were also those injured in conflicts between adventurers themselves.

For such individuals, the guild maintained a treatment room.

That said, it wasn't exactly a treatment room in the traditional sense. There were no healers present; merely a supply of potions and similar items.

Furthermore, since anyone other than guild staff had to pay to use the potions, adventurers rarely used them.

Runo, however, worked solo and possessed the Magic Eye that could see magic power, meaning he rarely struggled with money.

Today's request involved gathering magical herbs, not ordinary ones, and the reward was incomparably higher than for regular herbs.

Even accepting that request, Runo, with her magic-sensing eyes, could easily gather magic-infused herbs... and in large quantities at that.

Not being short on money, Runo had used this treatment room several times before.

"Please lay him on that bed."

"Sure thing."

Following the receptionist's instructions, Runo laid Slesha down on the bed.

"Now, I'd like to hear her side of the story... What should we do?"

"Well, asking me that won't help. ...Besides, do I even need to be involved in this?"

Runo had helped Slesha out of circumstance, but carrying her through the city was an utterly humiliating act.

Only now did he question why he'd felt compelled to go that far.

"Well, well, you're being rather cold-hearted. That's unusual for someone faced with such a beauty."

"...Hey, could you stop trying to force me into being a womanizer?"

"Hehe, I'll do that if I feel like it. ...Well then. Since I'm feeling a bit cheerful today, I'll give you a wake-up spray as a service."

The receptionist who had been conversing with Runo said this with a smile, then took a reviving potion from the shelf... and held it under the unconscious Slesha's nose.
