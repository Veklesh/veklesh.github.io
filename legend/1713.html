Day two of the dungeon run… Rei and his party had cleared the first floor without any trouble and were now in the forest on the second floor.

“No rock plants after all. I really wish they’d been in the same spot.”

It was Relieu who muttered this.

He wanted to bring his wife, Shumine, a flower made of rock as a souvenir.

That must have been what he was thinking.

However, when they stopped by the spot on the left side of the Y-shaped path—where the rock plant had been yesterday—there was nothing there.

In other words, once a rock plant is pulled up, that’s the end of it.

“Well, there aren’t any fruits around either… When you think about it, it’s not that strange that the rock plants are gone.”

Looking at the tree growing nearby—the one from yesterday where he’d relentlessly picked the fruit, leaving almost none left on the branches—Rei muttered.

The few fruits still hanging on—were they ones I’d missed yesterday, or had the small ones I’d left behind ripened overnight?

Rei didn’t know the reason, but since there were so few fruits left, he wasn’t going to bother picking any today.

…Though, Yellow had been flying through the sky to pick those fruits, bringing them down to Set so the two of them could eat together—but that was a different matter.

“For now… the problem is which direction to head.”

Since this was a forest formed within a dungeon, it stood to reason that Rei and the others were the first to come here.

Given that, there was no way there would be any convenient, well-trodden paths in the forest.

Instead, we did manage to find a few animal trails that monsters and animals likely used.

However, since there are so many of these animal trails, there’s no way to know what kind of monsters lie in which direction.

Based on the size of the trails, they could roughly guess what size of animals or monsters had passed through… but since they didn’t know what kinds of monsters or animals inhabited this dungeon, that was only a rough guide.

For that very reason, deciding which way to go was a dilemma for Rei and the others.

“For now, why don’t we just head toward the larger animal trails? It’s better to take out the big fish in this forest—meaning the strong enemies—as soon as possible.”

Being large doesn’t necessarily mean being strong, but as far as Vihera was concerned, if they were going to fight anyway, she’d rather face a strong opponent.

That was the reasoning behind her suggestion.

A few other suggestions were made, but in the end, none of them were particularly convincing… so ultimately, they decided to follow Vihera’s suggestion and head down the large animal trail.

“Hmm… We’re not encountering as many monsters as I expected.”

At Vihera’s feet, three orcs lay sprawled on the ground.

It was clear that they weren’t merely unconscious, as all three had their necks snapped and were staring off into the distance.

The orcs, who had attacked in hopes of taking advantage of Vihera and the others’ beauty, were subjected to a one-sided slaughter and ended up as corpses in less than a minute.

Without the slightest surprise, Rei stowed the orcs’ corpses away in the Misty Ring.

Given Vihera’s strength, he hadn’t expected three orcs to pose much of a threat from the start.

“It’s a little hard to tell if this dungeon is brand-new or not,”

It was Relieu who muttered this as he surveyed the surroundings.

While the second floor boasted a vast space, much like a forest, the variety of monsters appearing so far wasn’t particularly extensive.

If higher-ranked monsters—the very ones Banillas had worried about yesterday, like basilisks and cockatrices—were to appear here, they would likely be facing a much tougher battle.

“Grr!”

As if reacting to Relieu’s words, Set let out a sharp cry.

The group present noticed the warning in his cry and prepared themselves for immediate combat.

Byune, the weakest fighter among them, moved to the back of the line and readied her long spear so she could provide support at a moment’s notice.

A few seconds passed… Then, suddenly, a massive beak emerged from the nearby thicket.

Visibly smooth and glossy, yet looking ferocious... the creature, with a beak large enough to easily swallow a human head, eventually revealed its entire body from beyond the thicket.

It would be no exaggeration to call it a giant bird.

However, though it was a bird, it was one specialized not for flying but for running on the ground.

(An ostrich?)

That was Rei’s first impression upon seeing the bird-like monster.

In reality, while its body shape resembled that of an ostrich, its leg muscles were far more developed than any ostrich Rei had seen on TV or in books, and its body was covered in green feathers that gave it a far more voluminous appearance than an ostrich.

And above all, what sets it apart from an ostrich is the single horn protruding from its forehead.

To be precise, it would be more accurate to describe it as a blade—there was no doubt that it was a vicious-looking weapon.

“Grinbo!?”

Seeing that ostrich-like monster, a cry of astonishment escaped Marina’s lips.

Even before hearing Marina’s voice… or rather, even before that, it had likely already sensed Rei and the others’ presence.

The monster called Grimbo opened its beak wide as it looked at Rei and the others.

“Everyone, scatter! Wind Breath!”

Hearing Marina’s shout—she knew the abilities of the monster called Grimbo—Rei and the others reacted immediately.

If these had been adventurers from Golz, they wouldn’t have been able to react so quickly to Marina’s voice.

“Huh? Huh?” Startled by the sudden shout…

“Goooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooo

The next moment, they would have been hit head-on by the Wind Breath fired from Grinbo’s beak.

Although it’s called a Wind Breath, what was released from Grimbo’s mouth was not mere wind.

That wind contained a massive number of what could be called wind blades, and if I had taken the full brunt of the Wind Breath, my entire body would have been sliced to pieces.

The wind blades themselves possess sharp cutting power, but the wounds they inflict aren’t particularly large.

But… what would happen if a person were caught amidst countless tiny wind blades?

It would be as if their entire body were slashed by countless razors.

In Rei and the others’ case, they immediately followed Marina’s instructions and dodged the Wind Breath, but just how powerful it was… well, that’s clear enough from the countless small scratches left on the tree trunk growing right next to them.

“Cut off the head!”

After dodging the Wind Breath, he used the momentum from kicking the ground to activate the Sleipnir Boots and leap into the air.

Closing the distance to Grimbow with a series of triple jumps, Rei’s hand gripped the Death Scythe he’d drawn from his Misty Ring.

And so, the Reaper’s blade was swung.

The Death Scythe, whose sharpness had been greatly enhanced by magic, cut through Grimbo’s neck with no resistance whatsoever… as easily as if it were cutting through water.

Just like an ostrich, Grimbo’s long neck meant there was a large target area to aim for—a fatal misfortune for him.

As its massive head fell to the ground, the Wind Breath it had been spewing naturally ceased.

Perhaps because the blade was so sharp, there was a slight delay before blood began spurting from the severed neck.

“There are others!”

It seemed Grimbos weren’t solitary creatures, but monsters that moved in packs.

Rei, keeping his distance from the blood spurting from the Grinbo’s neck he had just defeated, shouted when he saw another Grinbo, identical in appearance to the one he had killed, emerge from the nearby bushes.

At Rei’s shout, the others immediately joined the fight.

A total of three Grimbos emerged from the bushes.

One of them appears to be a juvenile, only about half the size of the others.

“ROOOOOOOOOAR!”

Just as the others had joined the fight, the three Grimbos also engage in battle.

However, if they gave the Grimbos time, there was a risk they would unleash the same Wind Breath that Rei had just defeated.

Realizing this, the others launched a coordinated attack to prevent the Grimbos from striking first.

The first to move was Vihera.

She kicked off the ground to close the distance with the Grimbo, then attempted to slash its throat with claws created by magic.

But Grinbo, sensing Vihera’s approach, instinctively tries to intercept her with his beak…

“Gah!?”

The next instant, a long needle fired by Byune pierced his eyeball, causing him to scream... and, overcome by the excruciating pain in his eye, he let out a scream of shock and agony before his neck was sliced open by the magical claws.

However, unlike in Rei’s case, his neck wasn’t severed; it was merely slashed open.

Of course, it was a fatal wound, but it wasn’t enough to kill him instantly.

Blood spurted from his neck like a fountain, and Grinbo began running away from the spot.

“You can still move in this condition!?”

Vihera had expected him to be able to move a little after his neck was slashed, but she likely never imagined he would sprint away at full speed in that condition.

Stunned by the sight of Grimbo running while blood gushed from his neck... Mirage, transformed into a whip, swept past Vihera.

Catching up to the fleeing Grimbo, Mirage wrapped the whip-like blade around his legs... and severed them in one swift motion.

Even for Grimbo, who could move about freely despite having his neck slashed, legs were naturally necessary to run.

Having lost its legs, the Grimbo crashed into the ground at full speed and rolled around.

Of the remaining two Grimbos, the one that appeared to be a child had its movements sealed by Marina’s wind spirit magic and then took a blow to the front legs from Set, causing its head to explode.

……Unlike the Grimbo Vihera had defeated, this one had been immobilized by spirit magic, so it didn’t try to run off with its head missing.

Of course, there’s a world of difference between having one’s neck sliced open and having one’s head completely severed.

And as for the last one... Relieu used his sword skills to decapitate it, just as he had done with Rei.

It tried to attack Relieu, who had approached it, with its prized beak, but Relieu easily dodged the blow and severed its neck in the same fluid motion.

Grinbo, whose neck was severed while still in the motion of swinging his beak, fell face-first to the ground, carried by the momentum of the strike.

The blood gushing from its neck drenched the ground, but that was nothing Relieu particularly cared about.

The battle, which lasted less than a minute, ended, leaving a strong smell of blood lingering in the air.

“Rei, you’ve got the item box, right? Then why don’t we get out of here for a bit? There’s no guarantee other monsters won’t come attacking if they catch the scent of blood.”

No one objected to Relieu’s suggestion, so Rei quickly stowed Grimbo’s corpse in his Misty Ring and left the area.

“Come to think of it, following the Yellowbird, that Grimbo monster was also a bird monster. Could it be that in this dungeon… no, in this forest, bird monsters are the main force?”

“Oh, but there were three types: goblins, kobolds, and orcs. Since they were found dead from falling outside the dungeon or encountered on the first floor, I think they probably live somewhere in this forest.”

At Vihera’s words, Rei realized she was right.

Since they were falling to their deaths from the dungeon’s entrance almost every day, it stood to reason that there must be a nest for those monsters somewhere.

And since there’s only one Y-shaped intersection on the first floor and no other such places, it would be natural to assume that this forest is where the goblins have their base.

“Though I do wonder why they go down the stairs to the first floor and fall to their deaths almost every day.”

“Reconnaissance, maybe? …I don’t know the reason, but if we find something that looks like it, we should probably deal with it.”

In this case, “dealing with them” means slaughtering every last goblin, kobold, and orc in their lair.

If they did that, the residents of Golz would no longer be able to collect the magic stones, proof-of-defeat parts, and materials from the monsters that died at the bottom of the cliff.

Rei understood that, but when it came to prioritizing other people’s circumstances—specifically, those who profit not from fighting themselves but by scavenging the bodies of monsters that died in accidents, versus the hassle of fending off goblin attacks every time—if asked which he valued more… needless to say, it was the latter.
