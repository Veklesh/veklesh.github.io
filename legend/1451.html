I hate him, I hate him, I hate him.

Consumed by hatred, thinking of that person paints my heart black.

But... I understand that as someone who's neither an adventurer nor anything else, I simply can't defeat that person no matter what.

I'd heard rumors about him being a womanizer, so I considered flirting to lure him into a false sense of security. But he knows my face.

If that's the case, just getting close would make it obvious I'm up to something.

Failure is not an option.

Until I burn away this hatred consuming me... and until I avenge my family's grievances, I absolutely cannot allow myself to die.

After achieving my revenge, I wouldn't mind losing my life. But that's only after the revenge is complete.

To avenge the unfulfilled wishes of my kind father and mother, and my beloved older brother, I was prepared to take any means necessary without hesitation.

But... the enemy was an adventurer.

Compared to that, even if I possessed slightly better looks, I was ultimately just a woman.

She tried to train her body to carry out her revenge, but talent was cruel.

Even as I trained, I was overtaken one after another by adventurers who had started later than me.

It didn't take long to conclude she had no talent for combat.

Still, being an adventurer was a convenient profession for moving between villages, towns, and cities in search of her enemy.

And so she continued searching for her enemy for several years... Consumed by hatred, yet still unable to find her target, the woman suddenly heard a story in a certain village.

"Gilm, you say?"

"Yeah. I hear a lot of adventurers are gathering in Gilm now. Actually, not just adventurers—ordinary people are gathering there looking for work too."

"Gilm is in the frontier, right? Isn't it a place only skilled adventurers can go? And ordinary people, let alone..."

"Yeah, I was worried about that too. But apparently, the Knights of Gilm and adventurers provide escorts, or they travel in large groups... Seems there are various ways to manage it."

「Gilm……」

The woman's enemy might not be in Gilm.

But still, if many adventurers are gathered there, then maybe... I can't help but think that.

Since she couldn't find any clues, she had no choice but to head for a place where more adventurers gathered.

Having made that decision, the woman eventually left the village.

Her destination: Gilm.

She set out on her journey thinking that, precisely because it was a place where many adventurers gathered, her enemy might be there.

Under normal circumstances, it would have been extremely difficult for a Rank F adventurer like her to reach Gilm.

Fortunately, she managed to join a group heading to Gilm from Subursta. Though they encountered several bandit and monster attacks along the way, the woman arrived safely in Gilm.

"Alright, we'll disband here. You can head straight to the guild to look for work, find lodging for tonight, or go meet acquaintances if you have any... Each of you, do your best to make a life in Gilm."

The man who had led the group thus far announced this, and the group that had traveled together to Gilm dispersed.

Originally, this group had no particular connection between its members.

They had gathered for the same reason small creatures or weak creatures travel in groups.

Naturally, once they achieved their goal of reaching Gilm, they would scatter to pursue their own paths.

The woman was no different. She intended to head somewhere people gathered—a tavern, an eatery, a guild—anywhere to gather information on her enemy...

"Wait a moment, Ilze!"

Hearing the voice from behind, the woman... Ilze stopped moving.

"What is it, Jade? I don't know anyone in Gilm, and I want to move as quickly as possible."

Jade, the man called by that name, watched Ilze's green hair, cut neatly around her shoulders, sway as she turned to speak. He hesitated before opening his mouth.

"Well, um. What are you planning to do now, Ilze?"

"What do I plan to do... I already told you, didn't I? I don't know anyone in Gilm, so I want to move quickly. I heard the inns are pretty crowded, and I'd like to secure lodging before nightfall if possible."

Ilze was a Rank F adventurer, but she had a fair amount of money.

It wasn't money earned through anything shady; she'd saved it up doing jobs like gathering requests that didn't require combat.

Ilze didn't have much talent for combat, but fortunately, she was reasonably gifted when it came to gathering... specifically, identifying medicinal herbs, poisonous plants, and various other types of vegetation.

Furthermore, understanding her lack of combat talent, Ilze had also considered using poison as a means of revenge.

In any case, for these reasons, Ilze, who possessed a certain talent for gathering, had accumulated a fair amount of money for a Rank F adventurer.

...Though that was strictly speaking only as a Rank F adventurer.

Still, Ilze figured it would be enough to cover lodging.

In reality, most inns in Gilm were already fully booked.

"Well... if you don't mind, would you consider traveling with me?"

Jade mustered his courage to speak.

The meaning behind it was obvious enough from the flush spreading across Jade's cheeks.

But whether Ilze noticed Jade's attitude or not...

In any case, she shook her head without hesitation.

"I'll pass. I need to be moving around Gilm for various reasons, and I'd probably just be a bother to Jade."

"If it's trouble for Ilze, I don't mind!"

At Jade's words, several of the people who had come to Gilm with Ilse and Jade whistled in a teasing manner.

Many within the group knew that Jade harbored feelings for Ilze... not just friendly feelings, but romantic ones.

Jade himself thought he was hiding it, but given his impulsive nature, it was only natural that people saw through him.

Ilze, too, knew how Jade felt about her.

But Ilze, who had no room to think about her own happiness until she had exacted her revenge, naturally had no intention of reciprocating Jade's feelings.

She smiled and shook her head.

"I'm sorry. Unfortunately, that's not possible. ...Well then, I'll take my leave now. Let's meet again if the opportunity arises."

Her words were gentle, but the meaning behind them was a clear and unequivocal refusal.

Jade seemed to understand this too, falling silent without saying more.

"Well then, see you later."

Saying only that, Ilze left the scene.

Left behind were Jade, deeply shocked and dazed by Ilze's abrupt rejection, and the others who tried to console him by inviting him to the tavern.

"Sorry. Unfortunately, we're fully booked and have no vacancies."

"I see. ...Alright."

Told this refusal by the innkeeper, Ilze reluctantly backed down, though disappointed.

This was already the fifth inn where she'd been refused lodging.

At first, it had been disappointing, but after so many rejections in a row, she was starting to get used to it.

"Um, do you happen to know of any other inns with vacancies?"

"Hmm, let me see... If you'd come a little earlier, we might have had some availability."

The innkeeper said this, sweating in the midsummer heat.

Truthfully, this inn usually still had a decent number of rooms available at this time of year.

But due to the influx of people for the Gilm expansion project, there were no vacancies.

The proprietress thought for a moment, then spoke reluctantly.

"Well... some inns don't have private rooms, but rather large dormitory-style rooms for groups. There might be space there... but it's not really the kind of place for someone as beautiful as you to stay, you know? And, well, there might be some kind of mistake involved..."

Ilze considered the innkeeper's words for a moment before nodding.

"I suppose so. I'll try looking around other places, and if I can't find anything else, I'll stay at that inn. Could you tell me where it is, at least?"

"...Are you sure you want to do that?"

Normally, a woman... especially not just one woman, but a woman with such striking features, wouldn't stay at an inn like that.

There was the risk of someone attacking her in the dead of night, and sleeping surrounded by unfamiliar men would inevitably make her seem careless.

Wouldn't it be better to sleep rough than stay at such an inn...? Thinking this, the innkeeper suggested a few places to Ilze where people didn't tend to gather.

Of course, if someone unscrupulous found her sleeping in such a place, it could lead to all sorts of trouble. But if she wasn't discovered, it would be safe.

At the very least, it felt safer than sleeping in a crowded room with a pack of men who could be called beasts.

"Thank you. I don't know where I'll spend the night, but this is still better than having no information at all."

"I see. ...I'm sorry. I'd really like to let a girl like you stay, but..."

"No, it's fine. ...Well then, I'll try my luck at other inns."

With that, Ilze left the inn.

Stepping outside, she grimaced at the sunlight pouring down from a sun that seemed determined to assert itself with relentless intensity.

"Was coming to Gilm a mistake?"

After all, it wasn't certain her enemy was even in Gilm.

Adventurers gather here, so it wasn't unreasonable for him to be here... but perhaps he had already left the Kingdom of Mireana.

Thinking this, a sense of urgency stirred within Ilze's modest chest.

(Calm down)

But Ilze knew how to deal with that impatience.

The enemy she was targeting was someone who had taken up the adventurer's profession.

That's precisely why Ilze became an adventurer herself.

Anyway, being an adventurer is incredibly dangerous.

Perhaps her enemy was already dead.

That thought was always present within Ilze, and precisely because of that, she knew how to deal with the impatience within herself.

She recalled the final moments of her parents and brother, gathering the hatred within her to suppress the restlessness.

After a few seconds passed... once the restlessness within her had settled, Ilze looked around once more.

People, people, people.

The village where Ilze lived was filled with more people walking the streets than she had ever seen before.

It was so crowded, you might have thought it was a festival or something.

But Ilze already knew that wasn't the case.

This was Gilm's... at least, the everyday reality of Gilm now, with its expansion work underway.

"Anyway, we need to find lodging. We can check out the taverns and such after nightfall."

Naturally, taverns were liveliest after work ended... from evening into the night.

After work, they hold feasts with their companions.

Naturally, tongues loosen then.

Of course, a woman like Ilze alone in such a place might get hassled, but she had enough experience to brush it off lightly.

...In the beginning, she often caused various disturbances, even seriously angering people and having to flee the tavern.

Now, she could easily manage things like pouring someone a drink with a smile.

Being touched was something she didn't particularly like, though.

Anyway... Ilze tried several inns, but every single one was full.

It seemed I might really have to sleep rough or rent a room in a large dormitory... Just as I was thinking that...

"Oh, lodging? You're in luck. Someone just left saying they were heading to Abuelo."

The reply came from an inn near a back alley.

"Huh? Really?"

Having been turned away so many times before, Ilze felt doubt before joy.

But the innkeeper, perhaps understanding Gilm's current situation, didn't seem annoyed by Ilze's reaction and simply nodded.

"Yeah. So, what'll it be?"

"Please. Ten days for now. I don't know if I'll extend it after that."

Just as she was feeling relieved at having paid the deposit and secured a room at the inn, she noticed someone opening the inn door and stepping inside.

This caught not only Ilze's attention, but the innkeeper's as well.

But the innkeeper didn't seem particularly surprised to see the man and spoke to him.

"Melan, you're awfully early today... Finished work already?"

"Yeah, Ms. Vihera messed up a bit. ...Well, it was her fault, really. Huh? Who's that person over there?"

"A new guest."

Hearing the innkeeper's words, Melan flashed a friendly smile and approached Ilze.

"Nice to meet you. I'm Melan Canning. Since we're both staying at the same inn, let's get along."

"...I'm Ilze."

Slightly surprised to be addressing someone with a surname, Ilze bowed her head.
