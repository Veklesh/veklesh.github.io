Rei tilted his head at the scene unfolding before him.

Nearly a hundred pregnant prostitutes.

Yet not a single one was awake—the sheer abnormality of it all.

"Huh? ...Wait a minute. For being pregnant, aren't their bellies a bit small?"

The one who voiced this was Marina.

Dark elves were a race that found it extremely difficult to conceive children, but Marina had been away from her homeland for quite some time.

Because of that, she had seen pregnant women more than once or twice.

Most were happily married couples, though.

Compared to Marina, Elena was too busy with her training, Vihera sought battles against strong opponents, and Byune was struggling just to survive, so Marina had never seen any of them with a pregnant partner.

Well, to be precise, they probably had seen them, but it was more accurate to say the individuals themselves were too preoccupied with their own lives to remember.

That's precisely why Elena couldn't understand what Marina was talking about.

"Huh? What's strange? When you get pregnant, your belly gets big, right?"

"Yes. But from what I can see, none of these prostitutes look much different from ordinary pregnant women. Have you forgotten? They should be carrying giants!"

"Huh!?"

Vihera, who had asked the question, and the others... including Rei, were all taken aback by Marina's words.

Yes, they were supposed to be giving birth to beings that should be called giants, written as "giant people."

Therefore, the fetuses inside them should naturally be larger than average.

But the bellies of the many pregnant women before them now didn't look like they could possibly hold giants.

"What does this mean? Do you think there's some connection between all the pregnant women being unconscious and asleep, and the size of the fetuses?"

"That's a tough question. But... if this were normal, not an abnormal situation, couldn't we guess the reason?"

Marina's words made everyone present, including Rei, recall a single entity.

The entity responsible for creating these giants. That was...

The Black Crystal

Rei and the others recalled the name of that magic item.

Though they called it a magic item, Rei didn't even know if that Black Crystal was truly a magic item.

It could simply be a crystal, as the name suggests.

...Even so, it definitely possessed some kind of special ability.

"What should we do?"

"Even if you ask me that, there's nothing we can do. They're unconscious, so we can't just take them all with us."

With nearly a hundred pregnant women, it wasn't hard to imagine the immense effort required just to get this many people outside Mejogo.

Moreover, they hadn't come here to save the prostitutes, but for revenge against Jaya.

Of course, if they could save the prostitutes, Jaya's victims, that would be fine too. But forgetting their primary objective was absolutely something they shouldn't do.

"Besides, what's inside these women's bellies are giants, right? If we tried to force them out of here, they'd likely die before they even had a chance to give birth to the giants."

She probably didn't have any concrete proof for that, but even so, to Rei, Elena's words seemed truthful.

"Understood. So... we're leaving them here as is, then?"

"It's best to do so. Besides, just maybe... just maybe, if the black crystal's influence fades, they might not have to give birth to giants after all."

"...Understood."

Reluctantly, Rei nodded at Marina's words and left the room.

Upon checking the second basement level again, he saw several other massive doors identical to the one in the previous room lined up in a row.

Recalling what the researcher had told Rei on the first basement level, it was easy for Rei to imagine that this entire second basement level was a room for gathering pregnant prostitutes.

(So there are as many prostitutes pregnant with giants as there are doors... How many women have they abducted from the surrounding countries?)

He muttered inwardly, his voice thick with irritation toward Jaya.

'Grr.'

"Huh?"

Suddenly, Rei thought he heard his partner... Set's cry near his ear. He frantically looked around.

But Set was nowhere to be seen.

Of course not. Set should be fighting against Jaya's forces trying to enter this underground facility right now.

Why he heard Set's cry here, Rei couldn't understand.

But somehow, it felt like Set was telling him to calm down.

"What's wrong, all of a sudden?"

"Nothing."

Shaking his head at Marina, Rei realized he was more irritated than he'd thought.

He let out a small sigh and spoke again.

"Well, let's go. If we can do something about the black crystal, there might be a chance to save them."

Everyone nodded at Rei's words and continued down the passageway.

Strangely enough, this level seemed to lack any obvious traps.

Thanks to that, they discovered the stairs leading to the third basement level without any particular need for Byune's skills.

"What do you think? There were so many traps on the first basement level, but here there were practically none..."

"Logically speaking, it's possible all the traps on the first basement floor failed to detonate. Then, when the trap on the staircase meant to take us all out was easily bypassed, they concluded setting traps was pointless..."

"I get what you're saying, Elena, but even if the traps were bypassed, it wouldn't really harm Jaya, right? Well, maybe there's the effort and expense of setting the traps themselves..."

Even as she said this, Marina didn't truly believe they were sparing the effort and expense of traps just to avoid setting them for us.

Marina's guess was that they probably hadn't anticipated anyone making it this far.

It was precisely because they were exceptionally skilled individuals like Rei and the others that they were here now, unscathed. But in a small country like Reburina, such highly skilled individuals were usually unheard of.

That's precisely why they believed this layered defense system—the numerous defensive structures surrounding the underground facility, the elite guards protecting the entrance, the cream of the crop guarding the gate, and the many traps on the first underground level—made it safe.

...They never imagined anyone would attempt to move directly from the air to the very front of the underground facility.

And despite the attackers being individuals like Rei and the others, they possessed extremely powerful combat capabilities. That too must have been a major miscalculation for Jaya.

(No, Jaya must have definitely obtained the information that we entered Rossi. If so, I think he could have at least prepared some countermeasures... Did he simply not have time, or have those countermeasures not manifested yet? Or, unexpectedly, are the Giants the trump card?)

For Jaya, the Giant symbolized overwhelming military might. Not deploying it against Rei and the others was, by any normal logic, inconceivable.

Rei hasn't actually fought a giant yet, so he doesn't have a real sense of just how much power they possess.

They did get some information from the attacked Resistance members, but they couldn't even be sure how accurate that information was.

A surprise attack launched suddenly at a location where the main resistance forces had gathered.

Given that, it must have been a complete surprise for the resistance.

If they had been waiting for the enemy and lost in a head-on battle, that would be one thing. But for Rei, the strength of the giants gleaned from such a situation was difficult to use as a standard for assessing combat power.

...In truth, it was precisely because this was such an unknown combat power that Rei remained somewhat wary.

If it were an entity he'd fought before, Rei probably wouldn't be so concerned.

"Anyway, let's go down. The giants are being born on the third basement level, right? Then, we might get a chance to fight one."

Announcing this with a beaming smile was, of course, Vihera, who was clearly looking forward to the battle with the giants.

Honestly, I'm not sure about that... Despite harboring that thought, since descending deeper was necessary, everyone silently made their way down the stairs.

Just like the previous staircase, they couldn't rule out the possibility of traps, so they advanced while keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

But in the end, just like when they descended to the second basement level... no, even longer stairs led them down to the third basement level without encountering any traps.

(This kinda feels like tackling a dungeon. Well, I guess a dungeon with doors like this... isn't exactly rare either.)

The dungeon where Rei had first met Elena, the one with the Altar of Succession.

That place also had a floor with doors like this.

"Somehow, this reminds me of the first time I met Rei."

As if reading Rei's thoughts, Elena murmured.

"Oh, it was the talk of Gilm, you know."

Marina, who was Guildmaster at the time, naturally knew about Rei and Elena's first meeting.

That included the incident where Ara suddenly attacked Rei.

"Huh? Really? Did something happen that made it a rumor?"

"...Anyway, for now, we need to figure out what to do about this third basement level."

For Elena too, the events of that time must be quite complicated.

Not only was there Ara's rampage, but someone she thought was an ally turned out to be a traitor who went so far as to kill her comrades.

Technically, it was Rei who killed her, but given the circumstances Elena had been led into, that was how she perceived it.

Perhaps sensing something in Elena's expression, the others didn't press the topic further and instead turned their attention back to the third basement level.

Several massive doors similar to those on the second basement floor were present, but the difference was that the noise here was overwhelmingly louder than on the second basement floor.

What could be heard were moans, likely from someone enduring pain, and the cries of what sounded like a baby.

The reason they didn't state it was definitely a baby was because the sound was incredibly loud.

It was the volume of a grown man screaming, and even Rei couldn't possibly believe it was a baby's voice.

"What do you think?"

Implicitly suggesting that giants might have louder voices, Rei's words were met with silent nods from the others.

It was a response that seemed to silently agree with the unspoken truth in Rei's words.

(The smell of blood makes sense since she's giving birth... but somehow, opening the door feels a bit scary.)

Even thinking that, there was no choice but to open the door. Seeing inside the room was absolutely necessary.

Under the gaze of the others, including Elena, Rei nodded and reached for the door.

With a heavy, metallic creak, the door began to open... and soon, a strong, pungent smell of blood wafted through the small gap.

Not just one or two. More than that, a thick, heavy stench of blood.

Rei frowned slightly at this, but once there was enough space for them to enter, the sight that met his eyes was utterly unexpected.

No, perhaps he should have anticipated it the moment he caught that scent of blood.

"Wh-..."

Sprawled across the room was a giant child that had burst out of a pregnant woman's belly... no, devoured its way out.

Despite being newborn, its form was at least as large as Rei's.

Given its sheer size, it was impossible for it to be born normally.

Given that, it wasn't strange for it to burst out of the pregnant woman's belly.

However, the situation where it was devouring its own mother's body from the inside could only be described as abnormal.

Despite being newborn, it wasn't drinking breast milk but devouring its mother's internal organs, flesh, skin, and bones. Even for Rei, who had gained extensive experience killing people since arriving in this world, this sight was enough to freeze him in place.

"Th-this..."

Elena, standing right behind Rei, naturally saw the same scene Rei saw and instinctively froze.

Like Rei... no, her experience racing across battlefields alone surpassed his, yet even for Elena, the sight before her was utterly unbelievable.

"Such... such an act cannot be forgiven!"

Elena's cry, as if conjuring golden flames behind her, echoed throughout the area.
