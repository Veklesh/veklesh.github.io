With the New Year’s party over, Rei returned to his room and let out a deep sigh, as if he’d finally had a chance to breathe.

He had already taken off the clothes he’d worn to the party, and they were hanging on a chair in the room.

Rei had taken care not to wrinkle them by tossing them casually onto the floor, but… if he truly cared about the clothes, the best thing to do would have been to store them in the Misty Ring, even if he left them as he was.

After all, even though it was only for a short time, these clothes were made with great care by a skilled craftsman, so they shouldn’t be treated so carelessly.

“Ah… no, I’m really tired. Just so tired. Tired no matter what.”

Lying sprawled on the bed and muttering to himself, Rei must have looked, to an outside observer, truly and utterly exhausted.

In fact, as someone who rarely interacted with nobles in his daily life, Rei couldn’t help but find the party—with its many noble attendees—anything but entertaining.

Still, he managed to endure it to some extent because there were people like Elena and Brewitt, as well as a few others he could interact with casually.

If Rei had been told to attend today’s party alone, he probably… no, he definitely wouldn’t have gone, or if he had, he would have slipped away halfway through.

That said, it was an undeniable fact that he had already left a party once because of the Lemuria incident.

As I lay there on the bed, the sound of a knock suddenly echoed from the door.

“Excuse me, Rei. Do you have a moment?”

It was a voice Rei recognized.

With a slightly weary expression, Rei spoke the person’s name.

“Miranda?”

“Yes. The party’s over, so I came to check on Rei… May I come in?”

“Sure, go ahead.”

Since Miranda was in charge of Rei, it wasn’t strange for her to come check on him at this hour.

It wasn’t strange, but… Rei couldn’t help but think that being a maid was really tough.

The clock had already struck midnight; this was the time of day when the usual Rei would be fast asleep.

Naturally, the same goes for a maid… no, since a maid has to wake up earlier than the people living in the House, she should have been asleep by now.

Of course, there are situations where they might be called in during the night if a problem arises, and there are staff members assigned to the night shift.

“Excuse me. …Oh, just as I thought.”

Miranda murmured softly as she looked at the clothes casually draped over a chair.

If Rei were the type to be strict about manners, it wouldn’t have been surprising if he’d reprimanded Miranda for her behavior.

But Rei himself couldn’t care less about manners.

Well, of course, if he’d acted in a way that clearly violated etiquette, it wouldn’t have been strange for her to not just laugh it off but actually reprimand him. But Miranda was well aware of that, so she didn’t go that far.

Naturally, she kept her behavior strictly within the limits that Rei could tolerate.

“I’m not good at taking care of clothes, you know. …In that sense, I’m glad Miranda came.”

“That’s what Elena told me to say.”

Saying that, Miranda picked up the clothes hanging on the chair and looked at Rei, who looked sleepy.

“Well then, I’ll take my leave now. I’ll come to wake you up a little later tomorrow, so please get some rest.”

“Ah, okay.”

Miranda gave Rei a warm smile at his brief reply, then left the room.

To Miranda, Rei was firmly established in her mind as the love interest of Elena, the young lady of the Duke of Kerebel’s House whom she served.

For that very reason, seeing Rei looking so sleepy like this, she thinks that Elena made the right decision.

Of course, she wouldn’t dare say that out loud.

After seeing Miranda off, Rei finished getting ready for bed… and fell into a deep sleep.

Around the time Rei fell asleep... there was someone who was far from ready for sleep.

That person was Zascal Seisor, the head of the Seisor Marquis House.

The party itself had ended without any particular problems.

He had even been able to see Rei—the man his son had quarreled with—in person, and Rei had treated him in a friendly manner… at least, that’s how Zascal perceived it, and Rei hadn’t shown any hostility toward him either.

With this, perhaps the matter involving his son could be resolved… no, he would make sure of it—with that resolve, he returned to the mansion in high spirits… but what awaited Zascal was the discovery that Gaiska, who was supposed to be under house arrest, had vanished.

What made the situation even more serious was the information that Gaiska hadn’t fled alone, but had left with someone else.

Furthermore, the fact that several soldiers entrusted with guarding the mansion had been killed was a major concern.

As far as Zascal knew, Gaiska didn’t have the strength to kill soldiers.

Therefore, it was highly likely that the person who did this was the individual who had been with Gaiska.

That is precisely why Zascal’s frustration was mounting.

While he is angry that his subordinates were killed, there is something else as well… namely, a strong sense of frustration toward Gaiska.

Why did he go and do something so foolish?

He was aware that he was irritated with Gaiska over various aspects of his treatment of Rei.

But even so... yes, even so, there was no doubt that he was his own flesh and blood.

He’d always thought of him as a fool, but for him to actually pull a stunt like this… yes, it was only natural that he didn’t want to believe it.

No matter what, blood ties hold great significance for a noble.

That is precisely why the current situation only serves to add to Zascal’s frustration.

“Find him! Find him at any cost! I will never forgive the one who dared to do this to me! No matter what, you must find him!”

At Zascal’s shout, those standing by in the surrounding area shuddered involuntarily.

Zascal has a rather magnanimous personality.

So much so that he didn’t even take a particularly harsh stance against Rei, who had adopted a hostile attitude toward the Marquis of Seisor’s House.

But once a man like Zascal grew angry, it was certain that the one who had provoked him would face the worst possible outcome.

That said, Zascal’s anger was directed solely at the person who had provoked him.

Unlike Gaiska, he doesn’t go around attacking people with whom he has no connection out of sheer irritation.

For that reason alone, his subordinates could feel at ease. They could, but… the sheer presence he exuded was undeniably far greater than Gaiska’s.

Precisely because they served Zascal and understood this, their bodies trembled involuntarily.

Still, the fact that they only trembled for a brief moment was likely because they realized that if they didn’t act now, Zascal’s attention might turn to them.

“Yes!”

As one of them responded, the others followed the man who had spoken first and ran away from the spot.

That said, this was, after all, Anesis in the Duchy of Kerebel, not the territory of the Marquis of Seisor.

Even if they were to search for Gaiska, it was certain they wouldn’t find him so easily.

Especially since, at present, Anesis is home not only to nobles aligned with the Noble Faction… but also to a small number of nobles aligned with the Royal Faction and the Neutral Faction.

If they made a big scene in a place like this, they would undoubtedly attract the attention of a large crowd.

For Zascal, the fact that Gaiska had vanished with someone… and especially that this person had broken into the mansion and killed or knocked out several people… was nothing but a disgrace.

To keep this from becoming known to others as much as possible, he needed to act quickly… yet discreetly.

That said, given that these were men who had served as Zascal’s subordinates for a long time, he couldn’t imagine them failing at a task like this.

That is likely why Zascal sent his subordinates without any worry.

“Still… who on earth would do something like this? No, the only ones I can think of are the people Gaiska was in contact with. …Come to think of it, where did Deotres go?”

As he muttered to himself, Zascal suddenly realized that Deotres was nowhere to be seen.

Since Deotres had been acting alongside Gaiska, it wouldn’t be strange if he had some kind of clue.

That’s what he thought… but for some reason, Deotres had vanished from Zascal’s vicinity.

“So, does that mean this whole incident was something he orchestrated as well? No, but even so, this would be far too obvious.”

Although Deotres’ disappearance was sudden, it felt off precisely because it was so blatant.

Still, since he hasn’t shown his face, it would be unreasonable to expect me not to be suspicious.

“Anyone?”

“Yes?”

At Zascal’s brief command, one of the people still present quickly responded.

Many of those who had been here had already disappeared to search for Gaiska and the person who took him away, but some had remained in case Zascal suddenly issued an order, as he had just done.

One of them responded to Zascal’s words, but Zascal himself merely glanced at his subordinate before issuing a brief order.

“Find out where Deotres went. And also, the people he was in contact with while he was in Anesis. …Now that this has happened, it’s a real blow that I didn’t get the full story.”

“Understood. I’ll get on it right away.”

With that, the man walked away from Zascal.

Watching the man’s retreating figure, Zascal felt his irritation mounting at the current situation—he was giving orders like this even though it was well past the time when drowsiness should have set in—and he took a deep breath to suppress it.

What filled his mouth was not the cold winter air… but warm air.

This was only possible because the mansion was heated by magical items. Even if it couldn’t match the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, the fact that the entire mansion—which was quite spacious—could be kept warm, right down to the hallways, was purely due to the financial power of the Marquis of Seisor’s House.

Walking down that hallway, Zascal eventually arrives at a room.

“Wait here.”

“Yes.”

After briefly instructing the remaining person, Zascal entered the room.

However, it was not the man who had been told to wait, but the knight who entered the room with Zascal.

Normally, Zascal would show no mercy to anyone who disobeyed his orders, but that knight was a different story.

Entering the room—the study—Zascal sat down on the sofa and turned his gaze toward the knight.

“What do you think?”

A single, brief word.

But with just those few words, the man immediately understood what Zascal was getting at.

“I see. It’s highly likely that Lord Gaiska has fallen into some kind of trap… a sweet trap.”

“I suppose so. …That fool. What on earth was he thinking, pulling a stunt like this? If this gets out, I won’t be able to keep covering for Gaiska any longer.”

“Unfortunately.”

The knight who had served as his bodyguard for many years was someone he found easy to talk to, not just because of his combat skills, but because he understood his feelings so well.

“What do you think will happen?”

“To be honest, I’m afraid it might be a bit of a stretch to expect him to come out of this in one piece…”

At the knight’s words, Zascal let out a deep, melancholy sigh and rang the small bell on the table.

Immediately, there was a knock at the door, and a maid appeared.

“Excuse me, Lord Zascal. Did you call for me?”

“Ah. …Bring me something to drink. Something strong. As things stand, I don’t think I’ll be able to sleep.”

“As you wish.”

At Zascal’s words, the maid bowed and left the room.

The knight guarding Zascal watched him with a somewhat reproachful gaze.

Though Zascal was aware of the disapproving gaze, he had no intention of stopping himself from drinking.

There was no doubt he’d had his fair share of drinks at the party.

But after returning to the mansion in high spirits following the party, this was the commotion that awaited him.

He felt like he simply couldn’t cope without a drink, and above all, alcohol was essential for venting the anger he felt inside.

“We’ve known each other this long; surely he should understand,” Zascal thought to himself.
