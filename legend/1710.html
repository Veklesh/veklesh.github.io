"Whoa!"

Just as a leaf was flung from the rock-like plant, Rei immediately used the Twilight Spear to deflect it.

Although the Twilight Spear was wielded without any magic imbued in it, even viewed purely as an ordinary spear rather than a magic item, its performance was exceptionally high.

There was no way he couldn’t block a strike that merely sent a leaf made of rock flying.

The stone leaf, deflected by the spear’s shaft, fell straight to the ground and shattered.

“Rei, not yet!”

“I know!”

At Vihera’s words, Rei swung the Twilight Spear once more.

Following the leaf, the next thing hurled from the rock plant was a fruit.

Though the fruit is only about the size of a fingernail, given the circumstances under which it was hurled at her, it’s not hard to conclude that it clearly intends her harm.

As if it were second nature, Rei deflects the rock fruit and closes the distance between herself and the rock plant.

(Rock fruit? Doesn’t that sound more like a pebble?)

While such thoughts crossed his mind, Rei continued to close the distance, swiftly swinging the Twilight Spear between attacks.

The blade cut through the air with a sharp whistling sound, and in the next instant, the rock plant froze in its tracks... severed near the base, it collapsed to the ground.

“So… it’s not really a monster, then?”

Rei muttered, staring at the rock plant lying on the floor, completely motionless.

In fact, what was currently lying on the floor looked more like a stone statue of a plant.

If this were a monster, it would surely possess a magic stone, just like plant-type monsters such as the Treant.

But judging by the appearance of the rock plant, there was no sign of a magic stone anywhere.

“If there’s a magic stone, it’d be in the roots… but how do I dig it out? Well, I suppose I could dig if I wanted to, but honestly, I don’t want to waste my energy on that right now.”

At Relieu’s words, Rei turned his gaze to where the rock plant’s roots had been.

What was there was undoubtedly a stem that had been severed close to the rocky floor, but since it was a plant, its roots were buried in the dungeon floor. …Yes, in a floor made of rock.

If they were to dig up the roots, they’d have to break through the rocky ground.

And they’d have to do it while taking care not to damage the roots as much as possible.

It wasn’t impossible if they set their minds to it.

But there’s no doubt that digging through rock would take a considerable amount of physical strength… and if asked whether it was really necessary to go that far, Relieu was saying that he’d prefer to avoid it if possible.

Rei was a little troubled by Relu’s words, but for now, he stored the rock plant in his Misty Ring and, taking the Twilight Spear in his hand, spoke up.

“I’ve never seen this rock plant before, and it might be useful as a material, so I’d like to take it all back if possible. I’ll do the digging here, so Relue, you keep watch on the surroundings.”

“Got it.”

Since this was a dead end, if enemies appeared again from the path Rei and the others had come, it would be a major problem.

Knowing this, Relu volunteered to keep watch.

That said, the monsters appearing so far have been goblins, kobolds, and orcs.

Monsters like that can be easily defeated by adventurers of a certain rank.

……In a way, using a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname to keep watch against such monsters was an extreme luxury.

He channeled magic into the Twilight Spear and fired it at the rocky ground.

The ground burst open as if from an explosion.

Since it was a short distance from where the rock plants were growing, the explosion occurred at a distance that wouldn’t affect the plants’ roots.

While confirming that there were no plant roots in that area, she gradually approached the spot where the plants were growing.

Of course, the others weren’t just standing by watching; Marina was using earth spirit magic to dig up the ground.

About ten minutes passed, and they eventually succeeded in digging up the rock plant’s roots.

Since they had to dig them out without damaging the roots as much as possible, the detailed work fell to Marina, who could use spirit magic, rather than Rei, and in the end, they left most of it to her.

“It looks like a rock root… I’m surprised it doesn’t break.”

Judging by their shape alone, those rock-made roots didn’t seem all that different from ordinary plant roots.

However, they were, of course, made of rock, and they were so thin that it seemed they might snap at any moment if too much force were applied.

“Rei, put it away before you accidentally touch it and snap the root.”

At Vihera’s words, Rei agreed and put it away.

He realized that if they were to keep moving while carrying the rock root, they would undoubtedly break it.

Though they were fragile, given that they would have to fight monsters, that judgment was only natural.

“So, I wonder what this rock plant was, after all. Considering it was growing here at this dead end, I’m sure it must have some kind of significance.”

Rei muttered as they made their way back to the Y-shaped fork in the path.

Since even Marina had never seen it before, it was certainly a rare specimen.

“We’ll probably need to have the Guild look into it. Though, it’s also possible it’s a plant unique to this dungeon.”

“Ah, yeah, you’re right. It’s a dungeon carved into a cliff face, and the surroundings are clearly made of rock… Thinking about it that way, it wouldn’t be strange to find a rock plant. If there were a few more like it… or even a rock flower blooming, I’d take it back to Shumine as a souvenir.”

Muttering to himself thoughtfully, Relieu turned his gaze toward Rei.

Not just once, but over and over again.

Even Rei could immediately understand what Relieu was getting at.

“Ah, yeah. If I find a similar rock plant next time, I’ll give it to you, Relieu.”

“Oh, really? Sorry for asking you to go out of your way.”

“Just so you know, that’s only if there are other rock flowers like this one. If there aren’t, I can’t give this one away.”

“I know that. Judging by what I saw earlier, there are probably similar rock plants blooming in other places too.”

Was he so certain because of his years of experience as an adventurer, or was he just getting carried away because he was a devoted husband?

Rei couldn’t quite figure that out, but he could still make a rough guess.

In any case, Rei and the others continued on, chatting but never letting their guard down, and eventually arrived at a Y-shaped fork in the path.

Naturally, without any hesitation, Rei and the others turned right at the fork.

Given the abundance of monster tracks in the area, it was no surprise that monster attacks were frequent… but since that was the only path forward, it was inevitable.

And sure enough, less than ten minutes after they started down the right path, monsters—including orcs—attacked.

That said, since Set and Byune had already noticed the monsters approaching, they didn’t end up getting hit by a preemptive strike from an orc archer’s arrows…

Since everyone except Byune possessed high combat prowess, dealing with the arrows fired at us was no difficult task.

“The orcs are in the rear, and the goblins are the vanguard? What a nuisance!”

Relyu pierced the torso of a charging goblin with his longsword and lifted it up to use as a shield.

The next moment, several arrows fired by the orc archers pierced the goblin’s back.

“Gyah!”

Being pierced through the torso was a fatal wound for the goblin.

But even after sustaining that fatal wound, the goblin, struck again from behind, let out a hideous scream and breathed its last.

Relyu hurls the goblin’s corpse at another goblin—one that was heading toward Byune—to block its path. After tossing the goblin aside, he activates his skill with a return strike.

“Wind Slash!”

The flying slash released from the blade sliced through the orc archer, who had positioned himself behind the monster and was about to fire an arrow.

His right arm, which had been holding the arrow, was severed, and a scream escaped the orc archer’s mouth.

The other Orc Archer standing next to him, paying no heed to his comrade’s plight, tried to shoot an arrow…

“Piiiiii!”

A long needle pierced his eye, and he let out a scream.

With his eye pierced, he was naturally unable to aim his bow... in fact, the pain made it impossible for him to fight properly.

...Well, depending on the monster, there are some that turn the pain of having their eyeball pierced into rage and become even more vicious, but it seems the orc archer wasn’t that type.

Meanwhile, a single arrow pierced the head of the other Orc Archer—the one whose right arm had been severed by Relieu moments earlier—and took his life.

The one who did that was Marina.

Although she is a spirit mage, Marina, a dark elf, is naturally also a highly skilled archer.

The arrow fired by Marina took down one of the most dangerous Orc Archers in the area, and then pierced the head of another Orc Archer whose eye had been gouged out by Byune... and with that, the battle was over.

The goblins who had come as the vanguard posed no threat whatsoever to Rei and Set.

It was the kind of battle that perfectly exemplified the phrase “a mere brush of armor.”

“For now, let’s take the orc archers’ bodies with us, and just take the magic stones from the goblins. …But I wonder where the orc archers got their bows from? Probably before they even got here.”

“I suppose so. …What do you think? Should we take that bow too?”

Marina asked Rei, who had stowed the orc archer’s body in his Misty Ring.

At those words, Rei looked at the bow, hesitating over what to do... and eventually stowed it away.

The quality of the bow itself wasn’t exactly top-notch.

Still, since it could be used as a normal bow, he decided that keeping it for now might come in handy later.

Normally, he wouldn’t bother carrying a weapon that might only be useful “maybe.”

It would clearly be a hindrance, and there’s a high chance it could even lead to an attack by monsters.

But in Rei’s case, as long as he had the Misty Ring—a magic item that was practically cheating—he didn’t need to worry about that.

"If it might be useful later on," he thought. He stored the bow and quiver of arrows—the quality of the arrows wasn’t great either—that the orc archer had been carrying inside the Misty Ring. With that, his task was complete, and he continued deeper into the cave.

“Still, aside from that rock plant, there doesn’t seem to be anything particularly suspicious in here.”

“There must be traps set up somewhere, right?”

In response to Relieu’s words, Rei, who was walking at the rear of the group, replied.

Hearing Rei’s words, Relue nodded as if to say, “Now that you mention it,” though she didn’t seem entirely convinced.

If anything, his expression made it clear he was hoping there was something more.

“If possible, I’d really like to see something more… like a scene unique to a dungeon.”

“What kind of sights are unique to a dungeon?”

Perhaps piqued by Relieu’s remark, Vihera asked.

“Hmm. For example, a place like this with a huge waterfall.”

“...It’s not a waterfall, but there was a dungeon with a forest.”

Rei, who had been listening to their conversation, muttered this as he recalled the Altar of Succession he had visited with Elena.

“There were all sorts of things in Exil’s dungeon, weren’t there? As far as Rei and the others know, there were even deserts and places like that.”

“Ah… yeah, there was. A desert stretching as far as the eye could see. Then again, Exil’s dungeons keep growing, which is why they ended up like that, right? Compared to that, this dungeon is probably brand new, so I don’t think we’ll see anything like that here.”

“Really? Just because the entrance on that cliff face was the only one doesn’t mean it’s the only entrance to this dungeon. Maybe a dungeon that was somewhere else grew, and as a result, created a new entrance there. We can’t rule out that possibility, can we?”

“Normally, don’t dungeons have just one entrance? As far as I know, that’s the case.”

“That’s just as far as Elena knows. Elena doesn’t know every single dungeon, and it wouldn’t be strange if such a dungeon existed. Dungeons are entities that defy common sense, after all.”

Hearing Marina’s words, Elena nodded in agreement…

“Hmm!”

Byune, who was leading the way, let out a small cry. Rei and the others followed her gaze and discovered a staircase leading upward.
