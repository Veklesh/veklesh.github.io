"Phew, udon really is delicious."

After finishing his New Year's Eve udon, Rei murmured this, having drained every last drop of broth.

He glanced around to see the others... but most were still eating their udon.

Only Rei, Set... and Byune had finished their udon.

"Hm!"

As if it were only natural, Byune, seemingly not having had enough, asked Rei for seconds.

In response, Rei didn't reach for the Misty Ring and shook his head.

"No way. It's the middle of the night. Udon's easy to digest, but eating too much will still take a toll on your body. You're an adventurer too, Byune, so watch your health, okay?"

Of course, many adventurers this time of year drink until dawn.

If such people heard Rei's words, some might snort in derision.

(Let those adventurers be. Besides... it might sound odd coming from me, but Byune is still a kid. It's better to be careful with her health, right?)

Whether she understood Rei's feelings or not... Byune's expression gave nothing away. Still, seeing she didn't ask for more udon, Rei nodded... and pulled out several silver coins from his Misty Ring.

"Hm?"

Uncertain why silver coins were suddenly being handed to her, Byune tilted her head in puzzlement and looked at Rei.

The same was true for Vihera, Marina... and Elena, reflected in the Paired Orb.

Only Set lay sprawled on the snow, enjoying the strange sensation of his belly cold beneath him while everything else felt as warm as spring.

"What? There's this custom I know called 'otoshidama'. When the New Year comes, you give some money to the kids."

Rei didn't know the formal meaning of New Year's money—that it originally meant eating rice cakes to receive divine power—but he knew from his own experience that children received money during the New Year.

"Money? ...What a strange custom."

"Well, just think of it as a lucky charm. Byune, you can buy snacks at the stalls too... No, tomorrow. Tomorrow. Don't try to go now."

The moment the words "eating at the stalls" left Rei's mouth, he grabbed Byune's clothes, stopping her from bolting out the door, though her expression didn't change.

"Besides, you just ate udon. You're just going to sleep now, so why not wait until tomorrow if you want to eat something? And anyway..."

"Food stalls aren't open this late at night, you know? ...Right?"

She nodded in agreement with Vihera's words.

Yes, in Japan, it's normal to find food stalls when visiting shrines for New Year's prayers late at night.

But of course, the custom of New Year's visits didn't exist in Elgin.

Moreover, this was Marina's mansion in the Noble District. Leaving meant moving alone through the Noble District in the middle of the night.

A person running through the noble district at night... Logically speaking, no matter how you looked at it, the only word that came to mind was suspicious.

Still, even if caught temporarily, it was fairly well known that Byune was part of Rei's... and Guildmaster Marina's party forming this spring.

Rei was well aware of just how conspicuous they were.

That's precisely why forming a party wasn't something they were particularly hiding.

Naturally, when such famous figures form a party, many would want to join if possible. Many would... but the adventurers of Gilm who knew Rei's personality couldn't just casually say they wanted to join the party.

Well, some did try. But they were stopped by fellow adventurers they knew and gave up.

That could be considered a fortunate outcome. Those who didn't know their own limits, who only wanted a piece of the action... and who tried to get close to the other members instead of the dangerous Rei himself... were forcibly stopped by the members of the temporary party at the time.

They said, "We don't care what happens to you, but we absolutely refuse to be targeted by monsters like that because of you."

This incident offered a glimpse into how adventurers viewed Rei.

In any case, for that reason, Byune was also known to some extent as a member of Rei's party.

Even if someone saw her running through the Noble District and found it suspicious, she shouldn't have been subjected to unreasonable arrest.

"...Hmm."

"Well, it is year-end, so there might still be all sorts of people causing a ruckus around the taverns."

"Hey, Rei. We finally got Byune to give up on this, so don't say anything unnecessary. Look, Byune's completely set on heading to the tavern now."

"...Is that so?"

To Rei, Byune didn't seem any different from a few minutes ago.

But for Vihera, who'd known Byune the longest among them, it was different.

Keeping her eyes firmly on Byune, she cautioned Rei.

"Yes, it is. Can't you tell?"

Even after being told that, Rei still couldn't see anything particularly different about Byune's expression.

"...Can you tell?"

Rei tilted his head at Byune's expression and asked Marina.

But Marina just shook her head.

"Yeah, right."

"Yes. ...Unfortunately, I don't understand either. What about Elena?"

'If we meet directly, not through the Paired Orb, we might understand.'

That meant even sitting face-to-face like this, they still couldn't tell.

Seeing Rei and the others scratch their heads, Vihera seemed to realize he was serious about it. With a slightly exasperated yet proud look, she opened her mouth.

"Rei and the others have known Byune for quite a while now, so you'll figure it out eventually. ...Right?"

"Mm."

Byune, whose gaze was directed at her, muttered briefly, seemingly unconcerned.

"Right?"

"Well, even if you say so... Fine, I guess. At least Byune seems to have given up on going to the tavern."

Though Byune had been heading toward the tavern just moments ago, talking like this must have changed his mind.

She sat down in a chair and began to look sleepy.

But that was only natural. Byune possessed a certain level of skill—abnormal for her age—but she was still just a child.

And children naturally get sleepy early at night.

On top of that, she'd just eaten udon and her stomach was pleasantly full, so it was only natural that Byune would feel sleepy.

Thinking it was time for the child to sleep, Rei turned his gaze to Marina.

They were staying at Marina's mansion tonight anyway.

Going to sleep a little early shouldn't be a problem.

Marina, meeting his gaze, nodded back at Rei.

It was originally a mansion she lived in alone, but she had hired people specially for today to clean it.

...Of course, she usually cleaned it with spirit magic, but today was special.

Incidentally, when she advertised for cleaners, many odd individuals applied, seemingly motivated solely by the chance to enter Marina's mansion. Naturally, such candidates were rejected during screening.

For that reason, Marina's mansion today was cleaner than it ever was with spirit magic alone.

For that reason, showing guests to the guest room posed absolutely no problem.

"Right. So... what should we do? Talk inside the house from now on? Though, if we do that, Set won't be able to come in..."

Marina glanced at Set lying on the snow and murmured apologetically, but Set purred, signaling it was fine.

The current situation, where he could enjoy both the cold of the snow and the warmth of spring, was quite interesting for Set.

Moreover, knowing Rei was nearby meant Set didn't feel lonely.

In fact, with Set in this garden and Rei at Marina's mansion, he felt closer to Rei than he did at the Dusk Wheat inn.

"Set seems fine, so shall we go inside? ...Set, should we leave the kiln as it is?"

"Gururu!"

Although they weren't baking pizza or anything like that anymore, the oven still emitted a fair amount of heat.

It would stay that way until the magic Rei used to activate it faded, or until Rei deliberately turned it off.

Along with the heat, the oven also provided light, playing a part in creating the garden's fantastical scene alongside the light generated by Marina's spirit magic.

Rei asked if it was okay to extinguish one part of that scene, but Set shook his head and growled.

"I see. Well, I'll leave it as it is then... but don't pull any weird pranks, okay?"

"Guruu."

Hearing Set's growl, Rei turned his gaze to Marina.

"So, no problem there. Shall we head inside the mansion now?"

"Yes. Rei, please take care of the table and chairs. The kiln aside, it's better to bring these inside the mansion, right? I'll take the Paired Orb."

Without saying anything to Vihera, who was already half-asleep with Byune in her arms, Marina quickly gave her instructions.

(Seeing this, I really think Marina is better suited to be the party leader. ...Well, I get that she can't be the party leader since she's a former guild master.)

As he stowed the chairs and table into the Misty Ring, Rei thought such things.

Rei understood, too. He knew who among them would cause the least trouble if they took the role of party leader.

But even so, could he really lead this group? He couldn't help but tilt his own head in doubt.

He knew he'd have to sort this out in his own mind eventually.

At times like this, if someone were foolishly honest about their desires, they might demand physical favors as a party leader's privilege... But in this party, if anyone tried that, they'd probably be happy to be embraced by Rei instead.

Of course, Byune is the exception.

Rei hadn't thought that far ahead, but regardless, he couldn't help but question whether he was truly fit to be the party leader.

With such thoughts swirling, Rei entered the mansion alongside Marina and Vihera.

With Marina leading the way, Vihera, who had been holding Byune, disappeared, leaving only Rei and Elena, reflected in the Paired Orb, in the living room.

"Huh? What's wrong? You suddenly look like you've started worrying about something."

Elena asked Rei through the Paired Orb.

"Nothing."

"...You expect me to believe that?"

Faced with Elena's direct challenge, Rei hesitated for a moment... then smiled faintly, thinking he couldn't win this.

"You know Marina and Vihera both intend to push the party leader role onto me, right?"

Laying it out for Elena, Rei found himself... almost without realizing it, voicing words that could be called his own weakness.

Though Rei rarely showed weakness, now it was just the two of them... and the fact that they'd just finished the New Year's party and were feeling relaxed played a big part.

Hearing Rei say this, Elena smiled, looking a little pleased.

She was pleased that Rei had confided in her like this, but Rei frowned at Elena's smile and spoke up.

"What's with that? I get that you're fretting over something uncharacteristic for you. But honestly, giving people orders isn't exactly my forte to begin with, so what can you do?"

'I wasn't laughing at Rei's reaction, you know. ...But I get where Marina and Vihera are coming from. If someone has to be the party leader, I guess it should be Rei.'

"I think that's a bit too generous of you, don't you think?"

"Is that so? I don't think so. Besides... I'll probably join Rei's party in the future, right? So, if Rei is the party leader, I'd be happy about that."

"Even if you say so... No, logically, I know it'd be best for me to be the party leader. I know that, but..."

"The others all have their own issues. Rei has his too, but for those who don't know his circumstances, wouldn't it be safer for Rei to be the party leader?"

Rei's circumstances... If you didn't know Rei came from another world or about his beast magic, he'd just be an adventurer with a griffin as his familiar.

Of course, the griffin... From an outsider's perspective, the fact he has a rare, Rank S-equivalent monster as his familiar is nothing short of a complicated situation.

"Yeah. I still think Rei should be the party leader, don't you?"

"I agree with that too."

After Marina and Vihera returned from putting Byune to bed and said the same thing... Rei sighed and finally agreed to take on the role of party leader.
