Marina was telling Elena to calm down, but even for Marina, what Jaya was doing was utterly unforgivable.

In the city... and attacking an enemy stronghold, no less.

Of course, Rei and the others could always just force their way through and break the traps.

But if they suffered major damage in the process, it would defeat the purpose.

To address that concern, having a thief along was undoubtedly more convenient.

Still, Rei didn't think Byune would refuse the raid on Mejogo.

Given Byune's personality, she wouldn't refuse unless it directly harmed her interests.

Moreover, she'd likely calculate that destroying the organization known as Jaya could yield gold and magic items.

Normally, it would be best to get plenty of sleep before a large-scale battle, but this wasn't exactly that kind of fight.

Of course, Rei had no intention of deliberately holding back before the fight, but he was confident they could handle anything.

They were certain they possessed the strength to handle it.

As a warrior or a magic warrior, Rei was one thing, but as a mage, Rei primarily specialized in area-of-effect magic designed to handle large groups, not individuals.

Asking such a Rei to fight using magic inside the Mejogo... would be quite difficult.

If the underground facility had vast open spaces, that might be a different story.

If the location being fought over was somewhere that could be destroyed, that would be a different matter.

If it were purely a Mejogo stronghold, devoid of prostitutes or the like, then Rei could have done as she pleased with her magic.

Whether it would be useful or not was uncertain, but evidence was better to have on hand just in case.

Seeing everyone's resolve solidify, Rei spoke, and each nodded in agreement.

They were about to attack a key base of an organization—a shadowy organization that wielded significant influence over a nation, even if it was a small one.

Under normal circumstances, it would be impossible to sleep with such tension.

But... that was only for ordinary adventurers.

None of Rei's group were the type to lose sleep over something like that.

After all, which was more difficult: facing a single organization, or confronting the military of an entire nation head-on? ...The latter, of course.

The Resistance hideout in Mejogo.
