Transparent tentacles reach out from the sea, heading straight for Set.

Set dodges them by flapping his wings.

Just as Set shifts his body to the side, Rei swings the Death Scythe he’s holding, severing the tentacle…

"Tch!"

Feeling a killing intent from behind, he reflexively swings his Death Scythe.

But at first glance, there seemed to be nothing where Rei’s Death Scythe had struck.

...Even so, Rei’s hand, still gripping the Death Scythe, retained the distinct sensation of having severed something.

What it was would become clear as Set flapped his wings and flew upward to create some distance.

As if seeping out of the air, a tentacle identical to the one Rei had first slashed appeared.

Its tip, however, had been severed by the Death Scythe.

The day after he had fallen asleep leaning against Set… Rei was out at sea fishing, just as he always did.

Using a harpoon made from Emoshion, he had even succeeded in catching a rather massive tuna—one just like the giant ones he’d seen on TV back in Japan—but while he was searching for fish like that, he was suddenly attacked by a monster.

Although it only attacked by extending tentacles from the sea, the sheer number of them—and above all, the fact that it could make several of them transparent and manipulate them—was the problem.

Since the tentacles had a limited range, if he flew higher, he wouldn’t be attacked, and it was highly likely the monster would give up… but Rei had absolutely no intention of letting the monster currently attacking him escape.

Because the monster currently attacking them possessed the ability to make its tentacles transparent.

(Probably… no, definitely—if I have Set absorb this monster’s magic stone, his optical camouflage skill should level up. There is absolutely no way I’m going to let this opportunity slip by.)

The reason Rei held what could be called such a resolute determination was that monsters with the ability to become invisible were not that numerous, and above all, because Set’s current optical camouflage level was four.

Since reaching Level 5 would drastically enhance that skill, letting this golden opportunity slip by was simply not an option for Rei.

That is precisely why, even as he is endlessly attacked by tentacles rising from the sea, he refuses to retreat and instead chooses to engage the enemy.

That said, the number of tentacles reaching up from the sea completely exceeded Rei’s expectations—so many that it seemed pointless no matter how many he severed.

(I can’t just keep cutting these tentacles forever, can I? …It almost feels like there are an infinite number of them, and even if there are a finite number, if their numbers dwindle, there’s a chance the monster might try to escape.)

Of course, Rei had no intention of letting the monster escape, but if it were to dive deep into the ocean and flee, it would be difficult to pursue it.

That was precisely why he needed to do something to distract it and prevent it from escaping.

「Set」

“Grrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set immediately reacted and moved closer to the surface.

As if sensing this immediately, the monster in the water extended its tentacles.

The number of tentacles: one, two, three, four… no, they quickly became too numerous to count.

What’s more, the tentacles reaching out were only those within sight.

There was no doubt that some of them were the transparent tentacles characteristic of this monster.

(If there are so many visible tentacles, I naturally have to focus my attention on them. …Ugh, this is incredibly troublesome. Even if I cut through a massive number of tentacles… no, considering the sheer volume, will that even be enough?)

I thought that for a moment, but I certainly didn’t want to cut too many tentacles and alert the monster to the danger, causing it to flee…

Given that, even with a massive number of tentacles, I had to evade attacks as much as possible while somehow landing a fatal blow on the main body.

(But the problem is how to land a hit on an enemy that’s in the water… isn’t it?)

Since arriving in this sea, Rei had experienced battles with various fish and monsters, but there was one major difference between those fights and the current situation.

That is, the monster currently attacking Rei and her companions is deep underwater.

Until now, they had been able to clearly see their forms from the surface, and even when diving deep, they could still make out faint shadows.

But… the monster currently attacking Rei and Set is striking from a depth so great that its form cannot be seen from the surface.

That’s why, if they attacked recklessly and failed to deal a fatal blow in a single strike, there was a high chance the monster would escape, so they couldn’t afford to carelessly let it land a hit.

(First, I need to get a clear look at the enemy… It’ll take time, but I guess I have no choice. This is the only method I can think of right now.)

While dodging the tentacles lunging at him, Rei called out to Set, who was carrying him.

Generally, Set understands most things when Rei calls his name, but even so, in a situation like this, a clear explanation was necessary.

Since this was a somewhat complex action—at least compared to giving instructions just by calling his name—that was only natural.

“Guruu!?”

With dozens—in some cases, nearly ten—tentacles trying to grab Set and Rei… and pull them into the sea, Set flaps his wings to dodge them while responding to Rei’s call.

If Rei gave the order to attack, Set would immediately strike at the tentacles reaching out around them.

The tentacles themselves aren’t particularly hard, nor are they very flexible.

Naturally, a single strike from Rei’s Death Scythe would be enough to destroy them, but a single blow from Set’s foreleg would suffice as well.

The reason he doesn’t do so is because Rei has been instructed not to give the enemy a sense of danger.

If the situation becomes absolutely critical, Rei will use his Death Scythe to intercept, but that’s only when there’s truly no other option.

……If Set hadn’t grown to over three meters in length…… To put it in extreme terms, if he were only about the size of Yellow, we wouldn’t have had to fight this desperately.

That said, if he were about the same size as Yellow, he probably wouldn’t have been able to pull off a stunt like letting Rei ride on his back.

In any case, due to his current size, it’s incredibly difficult for Set to keep dodging the tentacles.

Still, the fact that he’s managing to dodge them somehow speaks to Set’s incredible prowess.

But this situation couldn’t possibly last forever.

If things continued like this, it was certain that Set’s body would eventually be caught by the tentacles.

If there were only a few tentacles, Set could probably tear them off easily given his physical abilities.

“While dodging the enemy’s attacks, head little by little toward the shallows. We can’t see the enemy in the water right now, but once we reach the shallows, we should be able to spot them!”

“Grr? …Grrrr!”

Whether he understood Rei’s words or not, Set let out a growl to signal he got it, dodging the tentacles’ attacks as he flew toward the shore.

However, since there was a high chance the enemy would give up if he managed to completely shake off the tentacle attacks, his movement was quite slow.

“Sorry about that, Set. Well then… I guess I’ll give it my best shot too.”

To make it easier for Set to move and to deal with the attacking tentacles, Rei sheathed the Death Scythe in the Misty Ring and drew a spear instead.

That spear wasn’t the Twilight Spear that Rei usually used… but rather a spear with a chipped tip, intended to be used as a disposable weapon for throwing.

It was a spear that would normally be discarded, chosen specifically for this purpose: not to slice the tentacles with the tip, but to strike them with the shaft to block the enemy’s attacks.

However, the goal was to sever the tentacles without alerting the monster targeting them, while still blocking the tentacle attacks.

For that purpose, the discarded spear Rei had pulled out was actually more suitable.

“Ah.”

That said, perhaps the tentacles were more fragile than Rei had anticipated, or perhaps the strike she delivered with the spear was sharper than expected… There were even times when, rather than cutting them, she managed to tear them apart with the sheer force of the spear.

Still, after swinging the spear once or twice, he managed to grasp the right amount of force needed to deflect the tentacles without destroying them.

Having become somewhat accustomed to handling the spear—or, more accurately, to wielding it without tearing the tentacles apart—Rei and Set made their way toward the shore, weaving through the tentacles.

Perhaps the monsters in the water were irritated by being toyed with by Rei and Set, for the number of tentacles reaching up from the sea soon increased even further.

Tentacles were everywhere… Set flapped his wings as he slipped through the tangle of visible and transparent tentacles stretching out all around them.

If someone who knew nothing of this saw Set right now, it wouldn’t be strange to describe it as him swimming through the sky.

And so, swimming through the sea of tentacles toward the shore, a few minutes passed.

If he had been flying at his normal speed, it would have taken Set no more than ten seconds to reach the shore.

But if he’d done that, the monster would undoubtedly have given up on attacking Set and fled.

To prevent that from happening, Set had to adjust his flying speed.

And that meant flying quite slowly.

Thanks to his careful speed control, the underwater monster didn’t give up on Set and continued to approach the shore.

(I’ve heard there are some sea creatures that can’t survive at certain depths… but fortunately, this monster doesn’t seem to be one of them.)

What Rei was recalling was a TV program he had seen back in Japan.

Fish that live in the deep sea can sometimes die from the difference in water pressure if they’re brought up to the surface too quickly.

However, fortunately, the monster currently in the water doesn’t seem to be affected by getting closer to shore.

(Is it because it’s a monster, or does it simply have that nature… or could it be because it’s an Elgin?)

Thinking this, he kept his gaze fixed on the water.

Of course, since it was moving while swaying in all directions—forward, backward, left, right, up, and down—under normal circumstances, I probably wouldn’t have been able to focus on it properly.

But that would only apply to an ordinary person, and Rei is by no means ordinary.

As a result, he was able to observe the underwater world clearly even atop the violently moving Set.

And just as the water began to get significantly shallower, Rei finally realized the enemy’s true nature.

“I could have guessed from the tentacles… but it really is a jellyfish! What a pain!”

Swimming through the water with swift movements uncharacteristic of a jellyfish, its translucent body made it extremely difficult to spot.

Still, thanks to the shallow water and Rei’s senses—far sharper than those of an ordinary person—she was able to spot it clearly.

“But once I’ve found it, I won’t let it get away. Set, get closer to the surface—just this once!”

Calling out to Set, Rei stowed the spear she was holding in her Misty Ring and pulled out a harpoon with a wire attached.

Following Rei’s instructions, Set approached the surface, letting out a cry.

Of course, getting closer to the surface meant being attacked by even more tentacles...

“Grrrrrrr!”

The flames of the Fire Breath spewed from Set’s mouth incinerated many of the tentacles protruding from the water.

Of course, since the flames of the Fire Breath are expelled from Set’s mouth… in other words, he can only breathe fire in the direction he is facing.

The tentacles approaching from below, to the side, or even from behind Set remain unscathed.

But that didn’t matter to Set at all.

That’s because he understood that the tentacles’ speed could never possibly catch up to his own speed when he flapped his wings.

And indeed, Set’s prediction was correct; the tentacles could not catch up to him

...Though, the monster probably never expected Set—who had been running around evading it until now—to suddenly charge straight at it.

Standing on Set’s back, Rei held her harpoon and smiled triumphantly.

He had devoted himself entirely to evasion up until now, and the result was that he had managed to lull his opponent into a false sense of security.

Then, as Rei saw the surface of the sea drawing near, he gripped the harpoon firmly in his hand...

「Set！」

“Grrr!”

At Rei’s call, Set flaps his wings wide and forcefully changes course.

It was a sudden, steep climb, almost as if they were tracing a V-shape.

And the harpoon Rei fired the moment they began climbing pierced the jellyfish’s body clean through.

“Alright, it’s still alive, but from here on out, we’re bringing it toward the shore! Just like we did with that fish monster!”

Smiling at the fact that he had taken down a monster carrying a magic stone with a high chance of yielding the Optical Camouflage skill—even though it was still alive—Rei called out to Set happily.
