Rei and the others successfully secured a room at the Silver Fruit Inn, but naturally, there was a bit of a commotion.

That said, the commotion wasn't about someone bothering Elena and the others... It was a commotion where Marina said she didn't mind sharing a room with Rei, and Elena and Vihera objected to that.

Ultimately, as Rei had initially suggested, it was settled that everyone except Vihera and Byune would have private rooms.

...There was also another scene where, when they tried to guide the familiar to the stables, the innkeeper was so shocked to find it was Set the griffin that they nearly fell over.

Anyway, they secured rooms without incident. Rei handed over the bags containing various belongings he'd been holding—mainly changes of clothes—and after everyone finished quickly unpacking in their rooms, they all gathered in Rei's room.

However, since Rei's room was a single room, it naturally wasn't designed to accommodate several people gathering.

It was considered a high-end inn even within Rossi, so there was a sofa for guests, but only one that could seat two people.

In the end, they sat wherever they liked on the sofa, chairs attached to the bed and desk, and the discussion began.

"So, what do we do first? Should we go to the guild?"

"Hmm... Well, even if we did, does Marina know the guildmaster here or something?"

Rei asked Marina, who had suggested going to the guild.

But Marina shook her head at Rei's words.

"No, I know quite a few people in the guilds of the Mireana Kingdom, and I have acquaintances in the Bestir Empire too... but I don't know the guildmaster here in Rossi."

"...I suppose so. This country is, after all, just one of the vassal states to the Kingdom of Mireana. Given that, the selection of the guildmaster probably isn't that strict."

Even though the Adventurers' Guild was nominally an independent organization, it was only natural for the guild itself to rank nations.

Since there aren't that many individuals with the capability to become a Guildmaster, it's only natural to want to place capable personnel in charge of the largest possible guilds.

Of course, there are exceptions to every rule, and Marina readily relinquishing her position as Guildmaster to return to adventuring was precisely such an exception.

In any case, for such reasons, it wasn't strange that Marina didn't know the Guildmaster of the capital of the small country of Reburina, one of the many vassal states within the Kingdom of Mireana.

If it were a capable person, Marina would likely have known or remembered them.

"But even if Marina doesn't know them, it's possible they know Marina, right?"

"I won't deny that."

Marina responded simply to Vihera's words.

In truth, Marina was a highly conspicuous figure among guildmasters.

Given that, it would be rather unnatural for this guildmaster Rossi not to know Marina's face or name.

"So, should we still try to contact the guildmaster through Marina's connections and gather information?"

"Yes. I wouldn't mind that. ...But, setting aside Rei and Vihera, doesn't Elena have any acquaintances in the Kingdom of Reburina?"

"Hey, why am I being lumped in with Rei?"

Vihera looked displeased at Marina's words.

But Marina looked at Vihera with a puzzled expression and spoke.

"Oh? Could it be that Vihera has acquaintances in this country?"

"...No, I don't."

"I thought so. Then it's fine, isn't it?"

Vihera couldn't argue with Marina's statement.

"Fine then. I'll go gather some intel at the tavern."

"Hmm, I can only see trouble brewing if Vihera goes to the tavern... Well, if you think of it as getting info from whoever bothers you, maybe it's not so bad?"

Vihera looked a little sulky, but Marina didn't seem particularly bothered by it. She turned to talk to Rei.

"Hey, Rei. Would you mind going to the tavern with Vihera? If I send Vihera alone, she might just wreck the place."

"...Marina, do you have some grudge against me?"

Marina shook her head with a broad smile at Vihera, who was somehow complaining about being treated badly.

"No, of course I don't hold a grudge! I'm not bothered at all about being dragged into that mock battle this morning."

Rei thought it was impossible to believe she didn't care, given how she was acting now. But speaking up would likely draw Marina's ire onto him too, so wisely, he said nothing more and simply nodded to her request.

Watching Marina, who was smiling faintly, Elena spoke up.

"Well then, I suppose I should head to the guild with Marina."

"Right. If word gets out about Elena at the tavern, gathering intel won't be possible."

After saying that, Marina turned her gaze back to Rei and Vihera.

The smile from seconds ago vanished, replaced by a serious expression as she spoke.

"Ideally, we should find an informant or something... but I think that might be a bit difficult."

"...Yeah."

Rei nodded in agreement at Marina's words.

Of course, if it were simply a matter of finding an informant, that wouldn't be difficult.

Though this was Rei's first time in Rossi, he could handle something that level without issue.

But if they carelessly approached an informant and asked about Ajax's organization, there was a real risk that the informant might leak information about Rei and his group to that very organization.

At the very least, there was no doubt that Ajax's organization wielded undeniable influence here in Rossi.

A few individuals gathering information on such an organization.

If asked which side they'd choose, anyone unaware of Rei's capabilities would undoubtedly side with the organization.

Of course, since Rei and his crew made such a flashy entrance into Rossi, any competent informant would naturally be investigating their backgrounds.

(In fact, knowing our information, they might be avoiding getting involved in the mess and refusing to cooperate... that's entirely possible.)

That was Rei's thought, and he himself had to admit it was probably the right choice.

Rei knew he possessed high combat prowess, and he knew the others acting with him this time, including Elena, also possessed correspondingly high combat abilities.

The weakest among them was Byune, but even Byune was undoubtedly a combat-specialized thief in terms of fighting style.

If such individuals were to clash with an organization, it would inevitably lead to a major disturbance.

And the number of people wanting to get caught up in that... would undoubtedly be small.

Marina understood this too, so she told Rei there was no need to forcefully search for an informant.

"For now, just gather whatever information you can find at the tavern. ...With you guys, Rei, there'll definitely be people who come looking for trouble, so I doubt you'll have trouble finding sources."

"Can't deny that."

To anyone unaware of Rei and the others' abilities, Vihera dressed like a prostitute or dancer, Rei the mage—one without a staff, no less—and the child Byune.

There was no way such a trio wouldn't get involved when they went to a tavern.

And if someone did hassle Rei and the others, they figured it wouldn't be a big deal to use slightly forceful means to get the information.

"Well, since we've settled that, shall we part ways for now? We'll return to the inn around evening, is that alright?"

At Marina's words, everyone present nodded.

Confirming no one had any objections, they each went their separate ways.

"One ale and two fruit waters. And some food, whatever you have."

A tavern a short distance from the Silver Fruit Inn.

Rei placed his order with the clerk there... but the clerk looked slightly surprised.

It was still daytime, so Rei had expected the number of customers to be low.

But he hadn't expected that his current order wouldn't draw any trouble.

Of course, that wasn't because the patrons in the tavern were particularly well-behaved...

They were simply overwhelmed by Vihera's beauty.

Faced with that beauty, everyone was so awestruck they hesitated to speak, each watching the others.

If the tavern had been more crowded, they would likely have been scrambling to get ahead of each other... but the daytime hours and sparse clientele created a situation where no one dared make a careless move.

(This was unexpected.)

That was Rei's genuine feeling.

Still, the tavern was filled with an oddly tense atmosphere, created by those watching each other warily.

Had it been evening or night, many would likely have focused on drinking with their own companions... but now it was daytime, and those present were the sort who frequented taverns during daylight hours.

Not everyone was planning to pick a fight with Vihera, but you could still sense the atmosphere around you.

"Here you go."

The waiter placed ale, fruit water, and dishes like stir-fries, stews, and skewered meats on the table.

However, the server's complexion wasn't good, and he kept shooting worried glances toward Rei and the others.

If one didn't know about Rei and his companions, that concern wouldn't be strange.

Despite their actual strength, Rei's appearance didn't exactly scream "tough."

Things might have been different if Set had been there, but unfortunately, he was left behind at the stables.

To the uninformed, the gauntlets and greaves Vihera wore probably just looked like a show of bravado.

"You'd better eat quickly and leave the shop,"

The shopkeeper murmured to Rei and the others in a voice low enough for others not to hear, then left the area.

The clerk had given Rei and the others a warning, but it wasn't entirely out of goodwill...

If a disturbance broke out inside the shop, chairs and tables might get broken, or even walls and floors. Worst case, the commotion could escalate to the point where guards showed up at the tavern.

That would certainly pose operational problems for the tavern's business.

But Rei and the others had come to this tavern specifically to gather information.

While his advice was well-intentioned, they couldn't just nod and agree.

...Besides, seeing Byune already digging into her stew, it felt a bit cruel to leave the tavern so quickly.

Therefore, Rei reached for the skewered meat on the plate, seemingly unconcerned by the earlier warning.

He took a bite... It wasn't exactly exquisite, nor was it bad; just ordinary skewered meat, he thought, taking a sip of fruit water.

(That dish called Panyan was delicious, but this tavern's food... is just average. I feel like it could be tastier with a little more effort. The meat prep is pretty sloppy too, leaving a slight bloodiness.)

If they'd just been a bit more careful with the prep, the meat on the skewers would definitely taste better.

As I lamented that fact... I soon sensed someone approaching us.

"Finally," he thought, but Rei didn't seem particularly bothered as he continued talking with Vihera.

"Hey, sis. Wanna join us for a drink?"

Amidst the war of words between customers, the man who broke free first was in his late twenties, his cheeks flushed red from drink.

"Oh? You need something from me?"

"Yeah. Leave those kids alone. Let's adults drink together."

Seeing him wearing a hood, he probably assumed Rei was a kid too, just like Byune.

While that wasn't particularly unusual, it certainly wasn't amusing to Vihera, who harbored romantic feelings for Rei.

She shot the man a sharp glance for just an instant, but the next moment, she opened her mouth with a smile.

"Well, I suppose I'd be delighted to hear what you have to say."

"Oh? What kind of story?"

"My boyfriend is quite intense at night. My body can't keep up, so I was wondering if you could recommend a good brothel?"

"......"

He probably never expected such words to come from Vihera.

The man was speechless.

Understandably so. He'd been about to make his move on her, only to receive this raw confession and request for advice.

The man looked thoroughly unimpressed by Vihera's words. Yet, the thought of someone else savoring this magnificent, beautifully voluptuous body made him involuntarily swallow hard.
