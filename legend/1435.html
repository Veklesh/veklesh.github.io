Rei flew through the sky aboard the Set, his gaze fixed on a distant sight... a familiar cityscape, though quite far away.

It was a place that could be called home to Rei in this world of Elgin... Gilm.

About ten days after delivering a letter to Dostrite in the Lerdact County, Rei had finally returned to a place where he could see Gilm.

He did end up buying tableware at Dostrite's shop, but only after negotiating a substantial discount.

Rei had insisted on paying properly, knowing how hard things must be for them, but they had insisted it was gratitude for bringing Jazz's letter, for helping his niece, Shira... and above all, for helping defeat Count Lerdact.

What would become of the Lerdact estate now that Lerdact had been captured by the rebels?

Even Rei didn't know, but Dostrite must have judged that it couldn't get any worse than it was now.

Regardless of the Lerdact noble house itself, within the framework of the Noble Faction, it was undoubtedly unwise to provoke discontent among the populace.

(Well, whether that "bad" leads to appeasing the people or to even harsher suppression for perceived disrespect toward the nobility... I couldn't say either way.)

Gazing at Gilm's distant figure, Rei still judged the likelihood of repression was not particularly high.

Because the Noble Faction had Elena.

If they were to pointlessly suppress the populace, what actions might Elena, nicknamed the Princess General, take...? Ray couldn't imagine anyone in the Noble Faction didn't understand that.

Still, Rei could foresee that various disturbances would likely occur regardless.

"Set, let's dismount then."

"Gurrrr!"

At Rei's words, Set growled and began descending toward the main gate of Gilm.

Though Rei had previously been asked not to dismount directly in front of the main gate, lately such matters had become increasingly ambiguous.

After all, unlike before, there was now no one in Gilm who didn't know about Rei and Set. The rumor had spread that there was an adventurer with a griffin as a familiar.

Among those coming to Gilm, there were even some who came solely to catch a glimpse of Rei and Set.

...Of course, beyond such individuals, there were also merchants hoping to get closer to Rei somehow.

Now, with the expansion work underway at Gilm, the number of people coming to Gilm was increasing day by day.

In fact, as Gilm drew nearer, Rei could see with his own eyes the long line of people gathered before the main gate, drawn by the sight of Set flying through the sky.

"Same as ever. ...Well, we weren't away from Gilm for that long, so I suppose it's only natural."

It hadn't been that long since Rei had departed for the Earl of Lerdact's domain.

...And Set's speed certainly played a major role in that journey.

Regardless, watching the queue unchanged since their departure, Rei landed on the ground.

"Whoa!"

Nearby guards and people going through Gilm's entry procedures cried out in surprise. The guards, however, immediately spotted Set and realized it was Rei, letting out sighs of relief.

"Rei? Don't scare us like that."

"Yeah, sorry about that. ...I've got my own issues to deal with. I'm going in."

Saying that, Rei entered Gilm, and the guard didn't say anything in particular.

"Hey! Why is that guy getting into Gilm without going through the procedures?!"

A man waiting in line to complete his own procedures shouted accusingly at Rei, but the guard calmly addressed him.

"Rei has been granted free access to the Gilm without formalities while the expansion work continues. Well, it just means he's been entrusted with a role that important."

Hearing the guard's words, the man fell silent, though his dissatisfaction wasn't entirely resolved.

Because he understood that such preferential treatment required commensurate power.

In this context, "power" didn't just mean combat strength. It encompassed wealth, connections, and various other forms of influence—not just direct power, but a multitude of abilities.

In other words, the man probably understood that Rei possessed that level of power.

...It wasn't unreasonable for him to grasp that the moment he saw Rei riding Set.

No, to be precise, he did understand it. But seeing Rei walk right past while he himself had spent hours queuing just to complete the entry procedures, he simply couldn't help but voice his thoughts.

"Look, you're done with the procedures too, so you can go inside now. There are more people gathered inside Gilm than usual right now. Try not to get caught up in any strange trouble."

Hearing that, the man nodded with a slightly unconvinced look and entered Gilm.

"Oh, it's Rei. Haven't seen you around lately. What's up?"

The gatekeeper standing before the lord's mansion called out upon seeing Rei.

As before, merchants seeking an audience with Daskar were gathered around.

Those merchants directed mixed glances at Rei.

Beyond jealousy, many gazed at him with desire, recognizing him as someone who could bring them money.

But Rei knew dealing with such merchants would only lead to trouble, so he paid them no mind and addressed the gatekeeper.

"I wish to report the completion of my request to Lord Daskar."

"...To Lord Daskar?"

The gatekeeper hadn't been briefed on this particular request. He tilted his head slightly at Rei's words, but another gatekeeper spoke up.

"Understood. I'll go inform him. Please wait inside the manor."

Normally, only those with business were allowed inside the manor, but Rei was exceptional in many ways.

He was highly valued by Daskar, and the benefits and achievements he had brought to Gilm thus far were incomparable to those of the merchants currently gathered before the lord's mansion.

They also understood that Rei disliked merchants... or more accurately, merchants who aggressively pressed their wares.

Ignoring the stares from the merchants, Rei entered the manor.

As usual, Set headed for the stables, while Rei was guided by a maid, who had been briefed by the gatekeeper, to one of the reception rooms.

The maid, who knew the situation, must have been considerate. There was no one else in the reception room Rei was shown to.

"I'll go inform Lord Daskar immediately, so please wait a moment."

After placing tea and baked goods on the table, the maid left the room.

Watching the maid leave, Rei looked around the room.

As befitted a reception room, the interior was designed for visitors to relax comfortably.

This applied to the paintings hanging on the walls and the vases placed on the shelves as well.

Normally, such... so-called art pieces wouldn't hold much interest for Rei.

But every single magic item Relduct possessed was an art piece of some sort.

Seeing them had probably sparked at least a little interest in that area for Rei too.

(Honestly, I wanted magic items like weapons... Could it be that Lerdact deliberately didn't send me those kinds of magic items as some kind of payback?)

He briefly considered that possibility, but immediately shook his head to dismiss the thought.

This was Lerdact, who had lost both arms, used the brutal method of cauterizing his wounds to stop the bleeding, and had tears, snot, and drool smeared all over his face.

In that state, it was utterly inconceivable he'd try to frame Rei.

It was conceivable that after some time had passed, his hatred for Rei would grow and drive him to act... but the idea that he would retaliate against Rei immediately after being attacked was simply unthinkable.

If he had that kind of resolve, he certainly wouldn't have ended up in such a pathetic state.

"Still... what to do with that magic item, huh?"

I took it from Lerdact as an act of revenge, but even if Rei possessed a magic item classified as a work of art, it held no particular significance.

No, in fact, having it locked away in the Misty Ring was practically harmful, keeping an art piece hidden from human eyes.

That's precisely why I want to do something about it, but I certainly can't just display it in Rei's room at the Dusk Wheat inn where he's staying.

Besides, it's an incredibly valuable item. Leaving it carelessly somewhere means it could easily get stolen.

Especially now, with so many people gathering here who wouldn't normally come to Gilm, the risk of theft is even higher.

...While Gilm's residents know that messing with Rei is practically suicide, many here are unaware of that.

Rei has no intention of dealing with such people, so there's no way he'd leave it lying around somewhere convenient.

"Marina's mansion, perhaps?"

Marina, an exceptionally skilled spirit mage, could certainly handle protecting a magic item in any way necessary.

Furthermore, entrusting the upkeep of the magic item to Marina would save Rei the trouble.

Rei had considered selling it for cash as a worst-case scenario, but displaying it at Marina's mansion now seemed the best option.

Once he finished reporting to Daskar and the guild, he decided to discuss the matter with Marina... Just then, as if waiting for his thoughts to settle, a knock sounded on the reception room door.

When he motioned for her to enter, the maid who had brought Rei here earlier stood there.

"Excuse me. Lord Daskar is ready."

"Understood."

Finishing the last of his tea, Rei rose from his chair and was guided by the maid toward the office.

"Oh, Rei. I'm glad you made it back. I never doubted you'd succeed, but seeing you safe and sound like this is a relief."

Rei gave a slight bow to Daskar, who was beaming with a broad smile.

"I believe I've completed the request thoroughly. ...Though I did end up getting involved in matters beyond the request itself."

What Rei was recalling was likely the matter with the mine.

Collapsing and destroying the mine was, in itself, a significant achievement.

...Though, as an ally of the Kingdom of Mireana, it wasn't exactly something he could openly boast about.

"I see. Then, tell me the report right away."

Daskar motioned for him to sit on the sofa, then moved from his desk to the sofa himself before saying this.

To Rei, it seemed like Daskar was wearing an expression that hinted at an expectation of an interesting story.

Lately, he'd been buried in paperwork, and perhaps he was hoping Rei's story would help relieve that pent-up frustration.

"Well, where to begin... First, on the way to the Earl of Lerdact's domain, I rescued a woman named Shira who had been taken by orcs. Since Shira's father was from the Earl of Lerdact's domain, I was able to gather quite a bit of information."

"...That's quite a convenient turn of events."

The person I rescued while heading to the Lerdact County was someone who had escaped from there.

Hearing only that, it was only natural anyone would think it convenient.

If someone told Rei that, he'd definitely think the same.

"Under normal circumstances, it might be understandable to think that... but Jazz, according to Shira's father, the Lerdact County was under oppressive rule, with heavy taxation so severe it could rightly be called tyranny."

"...The Noble Faction's general approach is that only nobles with noble blood can lead the people..."

Noblesse oblige. The noble obligation.

Rei had encountered various subcultures while in Japan.

It was a phrase that came up occasionally during that time.

Rei understood that the Noble Faction were likely those practicing this noblesse oblige.

Though, the result of twisting that concept to suit their own convenience was likely the kind of politics practiced in the Lerdact County.

"Anyway, it seems quite a few people have fled the Lerdact County. Thinking about it that way, maybe it's not so strange that I encountered Jazz."

"...Perhaps. Anything else?"

Prompted to continue, Rei went on with his report.

...It surprised Rei that Daskar showed even a little interest in a hot pot made with river fish.
