Silver Lion... It was the monster dwelling in the dungeon where Rei first encountered Elena.

This monster, famed as a Rank S monster guarding the dungeon's core, was left untouched when Ray and Elena first ventured into the dungeon. Their goal then wasn't to conquer the dungeon, but to perform the succession ritual for Elena at the Altar of Succession.

Even so, Rei was startled by the realization that such a vicious Rank S monster guarded the dungeon's core.

Of course, it's not necessarily true that every dungeon houses such a powerful monster as an S-rank.

...No, rather, it's probably extremely rare for such a powerful monster to be in a dungeon at all.

"A Silver Lion? Hmm, I see. Come to think of it, that dungeon did have a Silver Lion."

Grim must have thoroughly researched the dungeon where he first encountered Rei.

Recalling the monster Rei had predicted from the term "Silver Lion," he remarked with apparent admiration.

Was that admiration because Rei hadn't forgotten about the Silver Lion's existence... or because Rei hadn't forgotten the place where they first met?

Either way, the mere mention of "Silver Lion" from Rei's lips was enough to please Grimm.

"A Silver Lion? Come to think of it, such a creature did exist."

Elena, who had also ventured into the dungeon with Rei, murmured with a slightly bitter expression.

The dungeon they had explored in search of the Altar of Succession held many painful memories for Elena.

Her subordinates had been controlled and killed, and the one who controlled them was also one of her subordinates.

Even though both were now dead, the wound within Elena hadn't fully healed.

She didn't dwell on it much normally, but memories still surfaced unexpectedly at times.

"The Silver Lion, huh. I'd heard reports about it... but it's a troublesome opponent."

Marina murmured softly.

As Guildmaster of Gilm, Marina naturally understood the dungeon where the Altar of Succession had been.

Near that dungeon existed something akin to a branch office—though calling it a branch office might be a slight exaggeration—and information about the dungeon flowed from there to Gilm.

The dungeon itself wasn't particularly vast, so quite a few adventurers had reached its lowest level.

But... even so, almost no one dared to face the Silver Lion, and the few challengers who did were swiftly killed by it.

"Ah. The Silver Lion. If you need the heart of an S-rank monster, wouldn't the Silver Lion's heart suffice? Of course, defeating it would be quite a struggle."

'Hmm. As Rei says, possessing the Silver Lion's heart would undoubtedly be a significant force in restoring that woman's consciousness.'

Grim gave his full endorsement to Rei's words.

Seeing Grimm like this, Guildmaster Marina's beautiful face clouded with worry.

She understood just how powerful the Silver Lion monster was.

But if asked where other Rank S monsters might be found, she couldn't answer. And fundamentally, Rank S monsters were entities that couldn't normally be dealt with—that was precisely why they were Rank S monsters.

Even if other Rank S monsters existed besides the Silver Lion, she couldn't help but tilt her head when asked if they could be defeated.

But if the question was about methods other than defeating an S-rank monster, then the only option was what Grimm had mentioned first: the World Tree.

After all the commotion this past spring.

Naturally, the World Tree is in no condition to be pushed now.

To an outsider, it might seem fully restored thanks to Rei's magic, but even Marina couldn't say for certain if everything had truly recovered completely.

Moreover, like Grimm, the World Tree lives for an incredibly long time.

No, purely in terms of lifespan, it likely outlives Grimm.

For such a World Tree, this spring might feel like mere hours ago to humans... or even mere seconds ago.

Placing such a burden on the World Tree in such a short span of time was something Marina, in her position, could not allow.

If this were solely her own problem, it wouldn't matter at all. But the World Tree affects every Dark Elf living in the settlement.

She did briefly consider the World Trees of other settlements... but most elves, dark elves included, fundamentally dislike humans.

That meant, even if she sought the World Tree's aid, she'd first need the settlement's approval. Beyond that, she'd have to enlist the cooperation of another being who could be called a priestess of the World Tree, just like herself.

And who knows how long that would take... In that case, defeating the Silver Lion, a Rank S monster whose location was known, was still the more reliable option.

(To restore Vihera's consciousness, is it really necessary to go that far...?)

As she contemplated this, a familiar face suddenly flashed through Marina's mind.

A person, like Vihera, who remained unconscious for different reasons.

That person's parents were busy scrambling to find a way to bring them back from that state.

Elk, a Rank A adventurer known by the epithet "Axe of the Thunder God." He also led the party bearing the same name, Axe of the Thunder God.

Alongside his wife, Min, he should currently be searching for a way to restore the consciousness of Rodos, who fell unconscious during the civil war in the Bestir Empire.

If that's the case, perhaps they could cooperate here.

Since we're facing an S-rank monster, having high-rank adventurers like Elk and Min is definitely advantageous.

(Rei, Set, Elena, Ara, me, Elk, Min... Oh, and Yellow too. That makes six people and two beasts. It's definitely more than enough firepower to take on an S-rank monster. ...Well, it's not without its drawbacks, mind you.)

The number capable of providing rear support wasn't that high... To be precise, while some like Rei, Elena, and Set could handle both roles, most were more effective as frontline fighters.

The only true rear guards were Marina, who used spirit magic and a bow, and Min, a pure mage.

That said, having two mages in one party alone makes complaining about a lack of rear support a luxury most ordinary adventurers would scream about from the bottom of their hearts.

Spirit mages and mages are inherently very rare.

Typically, when people say "rear guard," they mean an archer wielding a bow.

However, that's only for a standard party. When facing Rank S monsters, it's undeniably true that their combat strength is far from sufficient.

(If possible...)

Marina directed her gaze toward the Paired Orb.

The Grim reflected there was an undead monster that lived for an eternity.

If it could contribute to their strength, it should be an immensely valuable ally.

"Hmm? Is something the matter?"

Grim, noticing Marina's steady gaze, asked. She hesitated slightly before speaking.

"If we were to challenge the Silver Lion... might we borrow your strength, Lord Grimm?"

"I'm afraid not. I'm free now, but I'm usually quite busy with various matters."

Marina was somewhat curious what exactly that "busy" entailed, but she couldn't bear the thought of carelessly asking and being told they were conducting experiments to flood villages and towns with undead.

Still, Grimm retained an ego unmatched among the undead, his consciousness nearly unchanged from his living days.

He wouldn't commit inhumane acts unless absolutely necessary.

Though it was the answer she expected, Marina still wore a disappointed expression.

Perhaps sensing something in Marina's reaction, Grim spoke from the other side of the Paired Orb.

"Hmm, well then. I won't assist in combat... but I suppose I could transfer you from near the entrance of that dungeon to its lowest level."

Hearing the word "teleport," Rei recalled how he'd been instantly transported back to the dungeon entrance when returning from it before.

"That'd be great. It'd be a huge help."

The last time he'd ventured into the dungeon, he'd fought countless monsters before finally reaching the lowest level.

He had even spent the night inside the dungeon.

The dungeon itself wasn't large, but its difficulty was far from low. It contained a forest, an area overgrown with giant mushrooms, and a place filled with undead and an intense stench...

"...That would be helpful, but if others are heading to that dungeon with us, will you teleport them too?"

"Others? Are you planning to bring someone else?"

For Rei, who had planned to challenge the Silver Lion with only those involved in this matter, Marina's words came as an unexpected turn.

Marina nodded at Rei before speaking.

"Forgot? There are others besides us trying to wake those who haven't regained consciousness."

For a moment, Rei didn't understand who she was referring to.

Right now, restoring Vihera's consciousness was his top priority, and precisely because of that, he had no mental bandwidth to consider any other unnecessary factors.

Then again, in terms of being unconscious, Rodos and Vihera were in the same boat.

Thinking about it, he could have gone to ask Elk or Min if they had any information on that front. But the circumstances surrounding their respective losses of consciousness were so different that he had completely forgotten about it.

"I see. Elk and the others."

"Yes. Elk and Min are skilled adventurers. Don't you think they'd be more than capable of fighting a rank S monster like the Silver Lion?"

Marina's words were true.

Rei knew well just how skilled Elk was, having once been targeted for assassination himself.

"Wait."

Enlisting Elk and Min's help.

Just as Rei was about to nod in agreement to that proposal, Elena called a halt.

"Borrowing Elk's strength. They'd be a reliable asset against an S-rank monster. But if we choose to use the Silver Lion's Heart to restore consciousness, would it work for both Vihera and Rodos?"

"That..."

At Elena's words, Rei and Marina exchanged glances.

For nearly a year, Elk and Min had fought relentlessly to restore consciousness to their only son.

Now, to tell them that using the Silver Lion's heart might restore consciousness, and that even if they borrowed its power to defeat the Silver Lion... only Vihera regained consciousness.

Rhodos remained in a coma... Given that, it wouldn't have been strange if Elk and Min snapped.

If that happened, they would inevitably have to fight Elk and Min.

And facing two enraged opponents... or perhaps just Elk, given Min's composure... but no one wanted such a pointless battle.

Moreover, Elk, as a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname, had a good personality.

Losing such a valuable adventurer would be nothing but a loss for Gilm.

"What about that part...?"

Grim, reflected in the Paired Orb, tilted his head at Rei's question.

'How should I put it... It depends entirely on the Silver Lion. When using the heart of an S-rank monster to restore consciousness, it depends on how much magical power that monster possessed.'

"So, if the monster's magic power is weak, there's a chance Vihera might not regain consciousness either?"

"I don't think you need to worry about that. Even if it's a Rank S, it undoubtedly possesses that level of magic power."

Considering his phrasing, "even if," it seemed the Silver Lion wasn't exactly a formidable foe to Grimm.

Though Rei had some confidence in his own abilities, the sheer scale of Grimm's power made the path ahead feel distant.

But now wasn't the time for that. Shifting his mindset, he spoke up.

"So, what do we do? I really want Elk's help, but I'd hate for it to end up with us fighting Elk and the others."

"Right... So, from your perspective, Lord Grimm, do you think using that Silver Lion's heart could restore consciousness for two people?"

'Hmm... As I said earlier, I can't say for certain. But I believe it will likely be fine.'

Probably.

It was an unsettling answer, but still better than no guarantee at all.

"Then, let's talk to Elk. But only on the condition that Vihera is our top priority."

"That's our only option, then."

Elena agreed with Marina's words, and Rei nodded as well.

"Hmm, seems it's settled then. Well, when you come to the dungeon, just call for me again."

Grim was about to end the communication between the Paired Orbs after saying this, but Rei called a halt.

"Hold on a moment. I need to confirm something. What's needed to restore consciousness is the heart, right? And that heart doesn't need to have the magic stone inside it, correct?"

"That is correct. That should be welcome news for you, Rei. Also, the longer it takes after extracting the heart, the less effective it becomes. If you intend to use it on both of them, it would be best to hand it over to me immediately after defeating the target."

With those words, Grim vanished from the Paired Orb.
