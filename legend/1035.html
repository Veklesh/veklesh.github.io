The mock battle with Rei ... though calling it that might be a bit of an understatement, ended, and Optis laughed cheerfully.

"Ha ha ha. I'd heard Marina brought a man, so I wasn't overly concerned... but he truly exceeded my expectations."

"...Should I be pleased about that?"

As Rei muttered while stowing the Death Scythe into the Misty Ring, Optis wore a slightly puzzled expression, but he likely judged now was not the time to ask.

He thrust his staff—a weapon worthy of the name—into the ground and turned his gaze toward the square's entrance.

A large crowd had gathered upon learning of Optis' actions, many directing admiring glances toward Rei.

Some wore bitter or resentful expressions, but Optis paid them no mind as he addressed Rei.

"Well, well, your companion has arrived, son-in-law."

"...Son-in-law?"

Stunned for a moment, he grasped the meaning of the words as Marina, at the end of his gaze, waved lightly.

Beside Marina stood familiar faces like Elena and Vihera , though the gaze directed at Rei was slightly sharp.

The reason was obvious: it lay in the word 'son-in-law' that Optis had just uttered.

Under the weight of two piercing gazes, Rei addressed Optis once more.

"So, what am I supposed to do now? Am I going to be confined again?"

"No, no, nothing like that..."

"Of course! That man harmed the World Tree! In fact, we should be grateful he's still alive!"

A voice cut through Optis' words, echoing throughout the area.

The loud voice echoed across the entire plaza, drawing the attention of everyone who had been watching the fight between Optis and Rei.

Seeing the man who had spoken, Optis's face briefly twisted in displeasure.

Meanwhile, Marina, observing the man from a slight distance, wore an expression of displeasure.

"Ragdo, this man is my grandson's husband. And he came all this way specifically to treat the World Tree! To place him under house arrest was already an act of disrespect. And now you dare demand more of him?"

"Of course. The moment this man entered the settlement, the World Tree began to show signs of distress. Nearly everyone present at the time saw it, felt it, experienced it, didn't they?"

The man called Ragdo stated this while surveying his surroundings.

His gaze met Marina's briefly, but having been half-defeated in the earlier meeting, he now wore a smug expression.

But... Marina showed no sign of flinching at Ragdo's words and spoke up.

"Well, I don't think we can say that pulsation is necessarily bad for the World Tree?"

"You feel that pulsation and still say that? The World Tree is already weakened. Adding such a pulsation... it can only be harmful. Don't you agree?"

"Then let me ask you, Ragdo, what do you intend to do about the World Tree's weakened state? Didn't you come back to me for help when you couldn't handle it yourselves?"

Marina's words were true.

If those who remained in this settlement could have handled the World Tree on their own, there would have been no need to call Marina back after she left.

It was precisely because they couldn't that they called her back, and the solution they brought with them was Rei. For Marina, trying to eliminate that very Rei was unbearable.

Moreover, since they were acting while completely ignoring Rei's danger, Marina honestly wouldn't be surprised if this settlement went up in flames at any moment.

(Fortunately, it seems a bond of trust has been built with Grandfather... but if someone like Ragdo appears, it can't be denied that Rei's attitude might change again. Besides, if we just leave things as they are, it seems likely to cause problems later...)

Under normal circumstances, that might be acceptable. Discussing things, getting her opinion across, listening to Ragdo's side...

But now was literally an emergency.

If she continued to waste time accommodating Ragdo, the World Tree would weaken... and worst case, both the Barrier of Confusion and the Barrier of Protection could vanish. Considering that possibility, it was better to force the situation forward, even if it meant being heavy-handed.

"But... if we take the proper measures, the World Tree should surely recover."

"It's precisely because those proper measures can't be taken that I was summoned, right? Am I wrong?"

Ragdo fell silent at Marina's obvious retort.

The truth was, including Ragdo, there was nothing anyone could do about the World Tree in its current state.

Of course, this settlement hadn't been idle either.

But even so, they hadn't been able to find a cure for the World Tree.

Well, they had succeeded in providing some measure of treatment by channeling magic, but the dark elves' magic was only capable of slightly improving the status quo.

When pressed on that point, Ragdo could only stumble for words...

Suddenly, one of the Dark Elves near Ragdo noticed something and looked at Rei.

She whispered something in Ragdo's ear, and at those words, Ragdo seized the opportunity to speak up.

"This is peculiar. This is my friend, Rezume, who possesses the ability to sense magical power... According to him, the magical power of that human man over there is definitely not high, you say? Well, it's probably higher than the average human, but compared to us Dark Elves, it's clearly low."

Hearing Ragdo's words, many in the square turned their stunned gazes toward Rei.

If that were true, it would mean Marina had brought Rei here for no reason.

Yet, many also harbored doubts.

Those with the ability to sense magic weren't particularly common, but among them, Marina should have possessed top-tier abilities.

How could Marina, with such abilities, fail to notice that Rei's magic power wasn't high?

Indeed, when several Dark Elves present attempted to sense Rei's magic, they found only a level slightly above that of an ordinary human.

And when those who sensed it shared this fact with others, the gaze directed at Rei shifted to one tinged with faint suspicion.

The reason it didn't turn into outright suspicion was likely because Marina was the one who had brought Rei here.

Marina possessed exceptionally high archery and magic skills within this settlement, along with great popularity.

Though she had only just returned after a century, a century felt like just yesterday to the Dark Elves.

Given that, there was also a sense that she was someone trustworthy.

(Honestly, she goes and does something unnecessary.)

Marina hesitates, unsure what to do.

Solving this particular matter alone wouldn't be too difficult.

That much had been proven beyond doubt during the Audban affair.

But doing so raises the suspicion that other Dark Elves might start looking at Rei with the same awe and fear they showed Audban.

Considering that, Marina's honest desire was to avoid it if possible.

But the atmosphere drifting through the square now was the opposite of Marina's wishes.

Sensing Marina's hesitation, Ragud likely saw this as his moment to strike.

Ragdo seized the moment and shouted.

"What's the matter? Surely someone of Marina's stature didn't bring us all here just because she wants to travel with her lover? If that's the case, I cannot help but feel disappointed in you, Marina!"

"Exactly! Coming here just because you want to travel with your lover during such a crisis? What were you thinking?! It's unforgivable!"

"Honestly. While we've been going through all this trouble, she's just relaxing like this... I just can't understand it."

"How much hardship do you think we've endured?!"

"Bringing a man along and indulging in romance as she pleases? That's despicable!"

As if drawn by Ragud's words, the surrounding dark elves each began to denounce Marina.

To an outsider, it was an undeniably blatant spectacle.

But for those actually present, it held a different meaning.

Since the others were condemning Marina, perhaps she really was at fault? Some began to think this way.

Of course, some held firm to their own convictions, but others inevitably swayed by the opinions around them.

Naturally, there were dark elves sympathetic to Marina, but considering the magic emanating from Rei, they simply couldn't defend her.

The way Ragdo and those around him were all piling on Marina to blame her looked like nothing less than a public shaming.

Yet Marina herself, the target of this public shaming, simply remained silent, accepting the words of blame directed at her.

She preferred that over Rei being met with fearful stares.

Those around Marina—Elena, Vihera, and others like Ara and Byune —seemed to understand this too. They showed signs of frustration but remained silent.

Jusla, standing a little apart, couldn't deny Marina's feelings either and remained silent.

"What is it, Marina-sama? If you say nothing, does that mean you accept my words? May I take that to be the case? If so, then naturally, the sin of uttering such falsehoods..."

Just as Ragdo finished speaking... he abruptly stopped.

Nothing had happened. He simply understood instinctively that he must do so.

It wasn't just Ragdo. The others who had been accusing Marina felt the same way.

"Silence!"

A voice echoed across the square.

It wasn't a particularly loud voice.

But it had undoubtedly reached the ears of everyone present in the square.

Whose voice was that? With that thought, the gazes of the dark elves in the square turned toward the source of the voice.

There stood the figure who, just moments before, had been locked in fierce combat with their own elder.

Yet, it was also the one who had caused Marina to be accused of being Ragdo's subordinate, possessing almost no magic power, and not being capable of healing the World Tree.

At Rei's words, everyone fell silent.

Only the elder, Optis, wore an amused smile on his lips, but he seemed to have no intention of doing anything further, silently watching the situation unfold.

"Wh-What do you mean, 'Shut up'?! It's your fault for coming here to heal the World Tree without any magic power in the first place!"

Ragdo, though leading only a small faction, was a leader nonetheless. Having lived a relatively long time as a Dark Elf, he possessed considerable experience.

Even so, having rarely ventured beyond the forest, standing before Rei's words felt like immense mental pressure. Still, he couldn't afford to look weak in front of his subordinates, so he shouted at Rei.

That stubbornness was, for better or worse, quintessentially Ragdo.

But... Ragdo's attitude only served to irritate Rei further.

That was precisely why Rei spoke again.

"Didn't I tell you to shut up?"

"Guh!"

Under the intensifying, inexplicable pressure, Ragdo instinctively groaned and took several steps back.

Realizing he had retreated, he grew irritated at the fear he had unknowingly felt toward Rei.

When he noticed the pleading looks from his subordinates surrounding him, Ragdo knew there was nothing more he could do.

Almost as if he had nothing left to lose, he summoned every ounce of will within him and stepped forward.

For a moment, surprise flashed across Rei's face at Ragdo's action.

But it quickly hardened back into a cold stare as he spoke.

"You said I was useless because I have no magic, and that Marina brought me here for no reason, didn't you?"

"Uh, yeah."

"Then... I'll show you that Marina wasn't wrong."

"Wait! No! Don't do it, Rei!"

Marina must have understood what Rei was about to do at those words. She shouted, trying to stop him.

The usual composed maturity Marina possessed was completely absent now. She was purely and simply worried for Rei's safety.

She feared that because of her, Rei would once again face the kind of gaze Audban had directed at him.

As Marina screamed with that fear, Rei glanced at her for just an instant... then, a small arc formed at the corner of his lips. He gently slipped off the crescent moon ring on his finger.
