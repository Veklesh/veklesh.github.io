"Oh, so that's all the trees we've cut down for today, right?"

"Yeah, I guess so. Man, having Rei around really makes the work a lot easier, doesn't it?"

Watching Rei stow the felled trees from Treant Forest into his Misty Ring, the adventurer hired as a guard here murmured thoughtfully.

In fact, not having to carry the felled trees was a huge relief for the adventurers.

...Furthermore, since Rei also does things like digging up stumps—including the roots—from the ground, he’s practically treated like a savior by the adventurers and woodcutters.

That said, when digging up stumps, all Rei does is use terrain manipulation to pile up the soil.

Actually pulling the stump out, roots and all, was the adventurers’ job.

Even so, the adventurers had no complaints.

The sight of countless stumps scattered about is quite a hindrance to movement.

That said, even if they wanted to clear the stumps, pulling them out is no easy task when the roots are firmly embedded.

High-ranking adventurers might be able to pull out stumps with ease, but the adventurers here aren’t quite that skilled.

If such adventurers were to try to deal with the stumps, they would have to dig into the ground to expose the roots to some extent, making them easier to pull out, then tie a rope or something to the stump and have everyone pull together... that sort of thing.

If Rei were to do it, however, thanks to her terrain manipulation, the roots would snap underground, allowing her to pull the stump out effortlessly.

There’s no way the other adventurers wouldn’t be grateful for that.

“I’m doing this because it benefits me too, though.”

Rei replied to the adventurer who expressed his gratitude.

In fact, Rei isn’t helping remove the stumps out of pure kindness.

During a discussion with the lumberjacks, Rei was told that he could do whatever he wanted with the removed stumps.

From the woodcutters’ perspective, the stumps left after felling trees are nothing but a nuisance.

Leaving them there gets in the way of walking, and even if they’re pulled out of the ground, they take up space.

If Rei takes them and stores them inside Misty Ring, they have no complaints—if anything, they’re grateful.

In fact, they’d be grateful.

...There were a few who didn’t like Rei and voiced their complaints, but if they were told, “Well, then you figure it out,” they wouldn’t know what to do.

At best, they could dump it somewhere out of the way… but even for a lumberjack, carrying all those stumps alone is no small feat… well, it’s not impossible, but it’s incredibly tiring.

Even if I tried asking adventurers for help, no one would agree to assist with something like carrying it to a spot out of the way—especially when Rei could just store it in the Misty Ring and be done with it.

It might be a different story if I offered a reward, though.

“Stumps for profit, huh? …What are you going to use them for? Dry them out for firewood or something?”

An adventurer listening from a short distance away asks, but Rei shakes his head.

“No. I’ve already got plenty of firewood, so that’s not a problem. This is… well. Just as an example, what do you think would happen if a stump fell from the sky while you were in the middle of a battle?”

Rei asked it in a lighthearted tone, but the adventurers who heard it felt a chill run down their spines.

A stump falling from the sky while they’re focused on fighting the enemy right in front of them.

The weight of a stump depends on its size, but it’s generally around 150 kg.

With a stump that heavy falling from above, if you were to take a direct hit without warning, it wouldn’t be surprising if your neck snapped in an instant.

And there were nearly a hundred stumps inside Rei’s Misty Ring.

In other words, he could drop that many stumps from the sky onto the ground, launching a one-sided attack.

Of course, this attack isn’t foolproof.

Depending on the shape of the stump, air resistance during the fall could cause its trajectory to veer off course, meaning it might not land where intended.

In that sense, it would be most effective to use it in a place where large numbers of people are gathered and fighting, just like in a war.

……Furthermore, although Rei didn’t mention it, wood naturally burns.

Although the stumps are green wood, they should burn without any problem when exposed to the flames of the fire whirlwind Rei creates.

Of course, the fact that it burns means it will eventually turn to charcoal, but conversely, that also means there’s a high probability that a burning stump will be blown toward you.

For those fighting on the ground, there was no doubt that the stumps were an unexpectedly troublesome weapon.

That said, while the adventurers were somewhat surprised and frightened by this... as long as they belonged to Gilm, it was highly unlikely they would ever find themselves at odds with Rei.

In that sense, the adventurers working alongside Rei like this could be said to be lucky.

Glancing at the adventurers who were half-consoling themselves in this way, Rei called out to Set, who was keeping watch from the sky.

“Set! It’s about time we head back to Gilm!”

“Grrrrrrrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set flapped his wings and descended toward the ground.

The adventurers nearby, seemingly already accustomed to the sight of Set, showed no signs of fear.

“Well, I’m heading back to Gilm, so I’ll leave the woodcutter’s escort to you.”

“Alright, leave it to me. …Well, I’ve never actually been in any danger in this Treant Forest before, though.”

The adventurer said this with a smile.

While those words weren’t exactly wrong, it didn’t mean they’d be safe from here on out.

Especially now that it’s getting colder; it’s the season when snow could start falling any day now.

And once the snow falls, monsters that only appear in winter will make their appearance.

For now, the Treant Forest was still safe, but once those monsters appeared, safety could no longer be guaranteed.

The adventurers serving as escorts probably understood that as well… but since they had entrusted Rei with transporting the felled timber, and since they themselves were tasked with guarding the lumberjacks in this forest, it was only natural for them to put on a brave face.

Rei, half-understanding the adventurers’ feelings, gave a light wave and flew away from the scene with Set.

Watching the figure of the man and his companion disappear, the adventurers resolved to at least do their jobs properly. Listening to the sound of the lumberjacks’ axes swinging against the trees, they each went on high alert.

“The numbers really have dwindled quite a bit, haven’t they?”

“Guruu?”

At Rei’s words, Set tilted his head slightly, as if to say, “Really?”

After quickly completing the formalities at the main gate of Gilm, Rei muttered to himself as he walked down the main street with Set.

Compared to a few days ago, the number of people going through the entry procedures at the main gate of Gilm had clearly decreased.

This was clear proof that the season was shifting toward winter.

It had been several years since he’d come to Elgin.

He had already grown accustomed to spending the winter in Gilm, which was why seeing this situation made him realize that winter was just around the corner.

That said, due to the expansion project, the number of people coming to Gilm this year is higher than usual.

“Well, all that’s left is to take the wood I brought to the usual spot… Hmm?”

That’s it for today’s work.

That’s what Rei was about to say, but before he could finish, he noticed a crowd gathering ahead of them.

What’s more, what’s coming from there are clearly angry shouts.

This was definitely not a peaceful situation.

That said, if that were all, Rei probably wouldn’t have given it much thought.

Many people, including adventurers, had gathered in Gilm, and naturally, there were some hotheads among them.

But… if Vihera’s voice was mixed in with the voices coming from that group, Rei couldn’t just ignore it.

“Let’s go.”

“Grrrrr…”

At Rei’s voice, Set growled and moved forward.

Perhaps they had noticed Rei and Set’s presence.

The crowd that had gathered around the commotion began to part.

And so, without encountering any particular trouble, Rei made his way to the front of the crowd—only to find… exactly what he had expected.

Lying on the ground were men who looked like they had a lot of blood in them.

However, what surprised Rei was that these men were unarmed.

As adventurers, they would naturally carry at least the bare minimum of equipment when heading out.

Even if they didn’t wear leather armor, they’d carry a longsword or a dagger… that sort of thing.

Of course, some choose not to carry long weapons like spears because they take up too much space.

In any case, the men currently lying unconscious on the ground—knocked out by Vihera’s punches, kicks, or throws—were completely unarmed.

If it were just one or two of them, I could have written it off as a few careless fools, but since every single one of them was like that, it was highly likely they weren’t adventurers at all, but people who had come to Gilm looking for work.

“Waaaaaah! Don’t you dare mess with me, you bastard!”

“Oh, ‘bastard’? I’m a woman, you know?”

The only man still standing, not yet knocked out, his face flushed red with anger—or perhaps panic—lunged at Vihera, swinging a punch.

(This is hopeless.)

Watching his movements, Rei made that judgment in an instant.

Perhaps because of his physically demanding job, the man’s body was visibly muscular.

Even though it was nearly winter and he was wearing long sleeves, his muscles were still clearly visible.

But… his footwork was far from what one would expect from an adventurer accustomed to combat.

Judging by the brawl he was having with Vihera, he was certainly used to fighting… but Rei couldn’t imagine him having any chance against Vihera.

Yet, contrary to Rei’s expectations, the fight—though calling it a “fight” might be a stretch—drags on.

It wasn’t that the man was unexpectedly strong; it was simply that Vihera wasn’t going to knock him out easily, as her goal was to torment him.

While dodging the man’s wild, formless punches, Vihera lightly taps his face and body with her gauntleted fist.

It wasn’t really a punch at all, but simply letting her fist make contact—an attack that couldn’t even be called an attack.

But the man has seen with his own eyes his comrades losing consciousness after being struck just once by a woman who looks to him like nothing more than a prostitute or a dancer.

If Vihera were to go all out, the man would surely join the ranks of those already lying on the ground.

The fact that this isn’t happening clearly means Vihera has no intention of defeating him.

And yet, by letting her fist brush against him, she’s making it clear with her attitude that she could knock him out at any moment.

“...That’s a pretty underhanded move for Vihera,”

Rei muttered, but Set tugged at the Dragon Robe with his beak, drawing his gaze to a spot slightly removed from the battlefield.

Following that gaze, Rei saw the sight of Byune standing beside a woman.

And the woman beside Byune had her clothes torn, exposing her white skin.

She was wearing a garment—likely prepared by someone—over her torn clothes, but it didn’t fully cover her pale skin.

Seeing such a scene, the reason for this brawl… and why Vihera is so furious—it was obvious without even having to think about it.

(Ah... Why on earth are these guys pulling such a stupid stunt right here on the main street... No, maybe Vihera and the others caught them trying to attack a woman in the alley between buildings?)

I never would have thought there were still people in Gilm who would brazenly pull stunts like that.

Thinking this, Rei watched the scene—which could hardly be called a fight, but rather an act meant to crush a man’s spirit.

“Haa, haa, haa, haa…”

No matter how much he attacked, he couldn’t land a single hit on Vihera.

And yet, Vihera’s fists were landing on the man’s body so frequently it was ridiculous to even try counting them.

To begin with, you can’t sustain full-out physical exertion for that long.

Moreover, this man had just come home from work, so naturally, he wasn’t in peak physical condition.

...Though, even if he had been in peak physical condition, there would have been nothing he could do against Vihera.

Another ten minutes or so passed… and eventually, drenched in sweat and gasping for breath, the man collapsed to the ground.

It wasn’t that Vihera had attacked him; rather, he had reached the limit of his endurance and his spirit had been completely broken.

She cast a contemptuous glance at the man lying on the ground, gasping for breath, and handed him over to the guards waiting nearby.
