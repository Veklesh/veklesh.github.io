A few days after Rei started teaching mock combat at the academy, the fourth-year S-class students were heading to the gymnasium again that day.

"Ugh... I hate this. Another mock battle with Rei today. They say if you land a single hit, you get top marks for the mock battle class, but there's no way I can land a hit on him."

"Yeah, right. I've attacked him countless times, and not a single one even grazed him. Is he even human? No matter how you look at it, he's gotta be something else."

"Exactly. I mean, he knocks arrows flying at him from behind out of the air with his spear without even looking? Attacking from blind spots doesn't work either. What the hell are we supposed to do?"

"I get it, I get it. But that's a ranged attack with arrows, so it's still manageable, right? When I attack with a dagger, I get countered instantly."

"If you're going to say that, what about me when I try to use magic? He charges right in the moment I start chanting. If the frontline could just protect me, I might actually be able to use magic and do something."

A student dressed in the classic wizard's robes and staff complained. Hearing this, the other students—mainly front-line types—grumbled in agreement.

"Don't be ridiculous. It's hard enough to hit them because they're so short, and then they move like crazy fast? Try to stop them, and they slip right through your fingers in an instant."

"Yeah, yeah. That speed is enviable. As fellow warriors, we should learn from it."

"Wait, isn't he actually a mage warrior? Not just a regular warrior? He didn't use magic in the mock battle though."

"Magic, huh. Yeah, that's right. What Deep Crimson is famous for isn't his warrior skills, it's his magic. And the griffin."

"A griffin, huh... What's a rank A monster like? I'd love to see one sometime."

"Ugh. A rank A monster? That just reeks of trouble no matter how you look at it."

"Yeah, sorry about that. Honestly, Rank A monsters are a pain even for professionals."

As the fourth-year S-class students bickered like this and entered the gymnasium, what awaited them was...

"GRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

It was the figure of Set, delighted to be able to move freely outside the stable for the first time in ages.

Over the past two years, Set had grown considerably. His body, which had been about two meters long before, now measured around two and a half meters.

Seeing a griffin that size suddenly appear before their eyes, it was no wonder the movements of the forty students froze.

"Grrr?"

Huh? What's wrong? Set tilted his head.

To those familiar with Set's personality and charm, it was an adorable gesture. But to the students seeing a griffin for the first time, it felt like he was glaring at them.

Frozen in shock, fear, and awe, the fourth-year S-class students stood motionless. Naturally, the first to speak was Rei, standing beside Set.

"Welcome. As you can see, Set will be assisting us in today's mock battle. You might feel some fear because he's a griffin, but don't worry about that. Set is basically a friendly guy. Unless you genuinely provoke him, he won't attack you in any meaningful way."

......

Though Rei was explaining, the students were far from listening.

They were so shaken they could have panicked right then and there.

The main reasons they hadn't fully panicked and started screaming were probably because Rei seemed completely unfazed, acting exactly as usual; because Set was rubbing his head against Rei, showing affection; and because Glinck, their actual instructor, didn't seem particularly disturbed either.

Granted, when Glink first saw Rei bring Set along, he did wear an unusually shocked expression.

But upon hearing that Ray had obtained proper permission from Erinde, he had no choice but to accept it.

(And then...)

One of the students directed their gaze toward the edge of the gymnasium.

There stood Samaloon.

He watched Set intently, his face excited.

While Samaloon was interested in magic, being Rei's familiar also meant he held a strong interest in Set.

Thanks to the instructors' presence, the students didn't panic.

Though, to be fair, many hadn't even noticed Samaloon was in the corner of the gym.

"Now then, normally I'd have you pair up in groups of five for a mock battle with Set, but..."

They flinched.

The students froze reflexively upon hearing Rei's words.

It was only natural. Fourth-year S-class students were generally equivalent to Rank E adventurers in strength.

In other words, what Rei had just said was essentially telling five Rank E adventurers to challenge a Rank A monster, a Griffin.

It was far beyond what the cadets at the academy were capable of.

That said, sparring with Rei, who possessed enough skill to hold his own against a Rank S adventurer, was no walk in the park either... Still, the fact that Rei looked human probably made a huge difference.

"Wait—"

One of the cadets nearly cried out in surprise, but Rei cut him off with another statement.

"However, Glinck raised the opinion that this was a bit too much."

Hearing Rei's words, the cadets shot grateful glances toward Glink.

Though the recipient of those looks wore a somewhat complicated expression.

But the students' relieved gazes froze once more at Rei's next words.

"Besides, the first lesson I taught this class was also one-on-one mock battles..."

Could it be... they're going to be told to fight a griffin alone? Every student's face paled, turning blue or white, as that thought crossed their minds.

This was especially true for the students of noble birth, whose pride ran deep. They refused to acknowledge Rei as superior to themselves, nor did they wish to admit it. Insula, who had fought Rei first, felt the same way.

"But Grink stopped that too. After discussing it, we ultimately decided on a battle involving all of Year 4's S Class and Set."

Hearing Rei's words, the students breathed a sigh of relief.

"E-everyone... Not just five, not even one, but everyone. If it's everyone, then maybe we can manage..."

"Y-Yeah. With this many people, we might just manage."

At the students' words, Rei gave a small smile, curling only the corners of his lips.

First, make an outrageously large demand. Then, when they're shocked, appear to compromise slightly while still getting your original request through.

It was a well-worn tactic, bordering on cliché, but the initial shock of bringing up a Griffin, a rank A monster, had left them reeling, allowing things to unfold exactly as Rei intended.

Before the students could fully recover, Rei spoke again.

"I don't know if anyone here knows this, but Set isn't just any Griffin—it's a rare breed. Normally, it could use several skills, but this time, I'll make an exception and let it skip using any skills."

......

At Rei's words, the students were left speechless.

Even a regular Griffin is a rank A monster, something you might see once in a lifetime.

An A-rank monster that's also a rare species? That effectively makes it an S-rank in terms of how it's treated.

Considering that, they couldn't possibly think they could fight it properly.

But ignoring the stunned students, Rei spoke up.

"Alright, let's do this. Ready?"

Rei looked at and asked was Insula, a student with whom Rei had connections in various ways and was easy to single out.

Ignoring Insula's stunned expression at being suddenly addressed, he continued.

"Hey, the rest of you, get ready for the mock battle immediately. ...What's wrong? Don't you need to prepare?"

Seeing Insula still staring blankly, Rei shifted his gaze to the basket of mock battle weapons placed in the corner of the gym.

Realizing this, the students judged that no amount of further protest could stop the mock battle and hurriedly went to retrieve their weapons.

Once everyone had their weapons in hand, Rei gave Set, who looked delighted to be moving his body again after so long, a light pat on the shoulder.

"Alright, let the mock battle begin!"

"GRRRRRR!"

At Rei's command, Set kicked off the floor and charged toward the students closest to him, who were already poised with their weapons.

But even though they had their weapons ready, a griffin over two and a half meters tall charging straight at them...

Its sheer force was undeniably formidable.

If it had been Rei charging at them, being humanoid like themselves, they might have been able to respond somewhat.

But facing a griffin charging at them? They couldn't help but panic.

"Ugh, waaaaaaaaaaaah!"

The first to lose his composure was one of the front-line fighters wielding a longsword.

Being in the vanguard meant he had to clash with Set first, and the sheer tension was too much to bear.

He swung his mock battle longsword down toward Set.

"Grrrrr!"

Set dodged the blow by kicking off the floor, immediately creating distance from the man.

Normally, landing a strike with his front leg would have knocked the man unconscious, but this time, he'd been told by Rei not to finish the opponent off too easily, so he was holding back.

"Can you do it? You can! He's not going all out. You can handle him now!"

Insula shouted, and everyone believed his words... or rather, forced themselves to believe them, launching their attacks toward Set.

Longswords, spears, halberds, bows, and various other weapons.

Each desperately launched their attack at Set.

It was an attack driven by an almost obsessive compulsion, as if giving the enemy even the slightest opening would mean they'd be wiped out in an instant.

(Though, to be fair, they weren't wrong.)

Watching Set effortlessly stop a halberd's horizontal strike by pressing it down onto the floor with his front paw, Rei murmured inwardly.

As the current exchange clearly showed, it was virtually impossible for the attacks of the students attending this officer academy to inflict any effective damage on Set.

Still, the reason they kept pitting Set against the cadets was partly due to the feeling that their time together had dwindled, and partly to alleviate Set's lack of exercise. But above all, it was to ensure the cadets wouldn't panic when they encountered an enemy clearly superior to themselves.

Understanding this, Glinck didn't object when Rei suggested using Set as a sparring partner; he accepted it quietly.

"...That's cruel,"

Glink murmured softly. Rei shook his head.

"Not really. Honestly, I was surprised they even had the guts to challenge Set to a fight. I thought some might just chicken out and run away."

"If you're going to say that, then Hazusuru deserves the praise. ...Ah, the student who attacked Set first."

"Ah, the one near Insula. ...It's true that man attacked Set with reckless abandon, and that actually helped ease the tension."

The image of that student charging at Set with reckless abandon flashed through Rei's mind.

...Though that same student was currently trying to swing his longsword, only to have his legs swept out from under him by Set's tail and fall.

Then he took a blow from the hind legs and was sent flying.

Even if he was holding back, it was still a blow from Set... one amplified by a magic item enhancing his physical strength.

While someone with a certain level of skill might manage, a mere student had no chance of taking that attack and parrying it.

In the end, the student holding the longsword was sent flying, bouncing several times across the floor before crashing into the students retreating to the edge, where he lost consciousness.

Some had already taken Set's blow, and the number still standing had dwindled to less than half.

"Grr?"

"Going?" Set growled.

However, the only one here who could understand what Set meant by that growl was Rei.

If this were Gilm, Set was so beloved by everyone that most could intuitively grasp his intent.

Especially the two women who adored Set above all others—needless to say.

"Come! A noble never turns their back on an enemy!"

Inslah declared, sword in hand, as he faced Set... but Set must have taken that as his cue. He kicked off the floor with one swift motion, charging not at Inslah, but at the woman behind him holding a bow. A strike with his front paw knocked her unconscious. Next, a strike with his hind leg knocked out the man holding the spear. Finally, he swished his tail, knocking the sword from Inslah's hand.

Though Insula, now weaponless, tried desperately to resist... he ultimately lost consciousness to Set's blow.

And then... this overwhelmingly one-sided outcome was something every other student would experience firsthand.
