"Hmm, I see. The military academy in the Duchy of Quent. I've heard of that too."

Though saying it happened every night would be an exaggeration, Elena and Rei's nightly conversations using the Paired Orbs still took place every two or three days.

Having been traveling toward Glacial for the past few days, Rei felt it had been quite a while since he last spoke with Elena.

"Is it really that famous?"

"Yes. It's said to produce many capable individuals for the Duke of Quent's knight order. The Duchy of Kelver has something similar, but it's not on the same scale as the Duchy of Quent."

Hearing Elena's words, Rei recalled the size of this military academy and wore a look of understanding.

Indeed, the sheer size of this academy was considerable, and it was obvious that a great many people existed as students here.

That said, Rei himself hadn't seen many students yet.

After leaving the headmaster's office, he had Samaloon show him around the academy's facilities, but perhaps out of consideration, Samaloon avoided crowded areas.

Even so, he had glimpsed a fair number of students from a distance.

'So that's why I hadn't heard from them in the last few days. ...Still, things have gotten quite interesting again.'

"As someone caught up in the commotion, I wouldn't exactly call it that interesting,"

Elena smiled softly at Rei, who shrugged his shoulders slightly and muttered.

Elena's features were naturally sharp and dignified, so the soft expression that appeared unexpectedly drew the eye.

Rei opened his mouth, trying not to show it.

"Anyway. Since I'll likely be at this academy until spring, regarding the 'Seed of Caution'..."

"It won't be a problem if it takes a little longer to lift. Besides, I hate to say it, but I've got some issues of my own right now."

"Problems?"

"Hmm. There's been a recent succession within the noble faction. That person is causing trouble for me."

"...Picking a fight?"

Hearing those words, two thoughts flashed through Rei's mind.

Namely, someone who finds Elena an eyesore and intends to eliminate her by force, and someone who desires Elena as a woman.

Given Elena's undeniably captivating presence, the latter possibility was highly probable.

"It seems he wants to form ties with the Duke of Kevel family and is trying to get my attention somehow."

And indeed, Rei's suspicions proved correct.

"...Huh."

The low murmur from Rei's voice carried a force that would undoubtedly send a chill down the spine of anyone who heard it.

Yet, upon hearing Rei's mutter, Elena's expression wasn't one of fear, but a smile.

And it wasn't a wry or forced smile, but rather what could be called a beaming smile, full of joy.

'Heh heh, don't worry. Of course I have no interest in a man like that. I turned him down right there and then.'

"Now if only he'd give up after that..."

The power of the Duke of Kelver house, Elena, known by the epithet Princess General, and a beauty that would captivate any man.

Furthermore, though only a select few know it, she had inherited the power of an Ancient Dragon at the Altar of Succession.

And yet she remains unmarried, with no fiancé in sight. For ambitious nobles, she is the ultimate prize.

In such matters, Rei's existence rarely becomes an issue.

To those eyeing Elena, Rei is merely a Rank B adventurer with an alias, and they consider marriages between nobles to be an entirely separate matter.

Even if told directly, they likely wouldn't believe the two genuinely love each other.

"No problem. The Duke of Kelver's household wasn't enthusiastic about it to begin with. Besides, if they try anything strange... then we won't be able to stay silent either."

The smile Elena flashed with those words was intensely combative.

It was precisely the kind of smile that would normally suit Vihera.

If that man were to lose his mind and attack Elena to create a fait accompli, he would regret it in more ways than one.

Understanding this, Rei opened his mouth to change the subject.

"Speaking of which, what about Yellow?"

"Hm? Seems he ate too much for dinner. He's sleeping now."

Elena, sensing Rei's desire to change the subject, didn't say anything in particular but went along with it.

For Elena too, there was a reluctance to discuss other men—especially regarding marriage—any further in front of Rei.

"I see. I suppose Set is sleeping in the stables by now too."

"I wonder what kind of meals they serve at the officer academy? Being a familiar... and specifically Rei's familiar, does that mean we can expect something special? Especially since Rei got caught up in the mess with the royalist faction this time."

"I wonder. The meals served in the staff dormitory cafeteria were pretty ordinary."

Then again, Rei's palate isn't exactly refined.

No, considering he dislikes alcohol, you could even call it a childish palate.

...Despite that, he does enjoy eating things kids usually dislike, like wild mountain vegetables.

"Knowing Rei, he probably ate a huge amount of food, didn't he?"

"I won't deny that."

Elena saw right through him, yet hearing her words, Rei strangely felt no resentment.

Had those words come from anyone else, things might have been different.

(I guess this is what they mean by someone you can be completely yourself around.)

Rei thought deeply about his time with Elena since they'd met.

His expression must have given him away. Elena, who had been reaching for her tea across the Paired Orb, opened her mouth in puzzlement.

"Rei, is something wrong?"

"No, just a little. It feels like a long time has passed since I met Elena, but I think it's only been a few years."

"...Hmm. You're right. It feels like a long time since I met Rei too. It's been... No, never mind."

"What's wrong? Cutting yourself off like that isn't like you, Elena."

"Shut up. Even I have off days sometimes."

Rei tilted his head slightly at Elena's expression, her cheeks faintly flushed.

He thought that the Elena he usually talked to wouldn't act like this...

Then again, for Rei, a girl's heart was one of the most incomprehensible things in this world.

He could easily imagine that pressing her further would only lead to a situation that would ultimately work against him.

That was precisely why Rei changed the subject again, just as he had before.

"I heard the royalists are pushing for a crackdown on discipline after this incident. What about the nobles? Personally, I feel the nobles need a crackdown just as much. Not that I think the neutral faction is completely safe either."

Though the topic shifted abruptly, Elena's expression grew faintly troubled upon hearing it.

She understood well what kind of nobles were in the Noble Faction, which her father was central to.

Perhaps it was the very name "noble faction" that was problematic, but those who behaved tyrannically toward the people they governed were endless.

Of course, many in the upper echelons understood this well, and those who behaved tyrannically were few.

...But few doesn't mean none. It meant that even among the upper echelons, there were still those stubbornly clinging to the nobility's sense of privilege.

'Now that the Royalist faction has resorted to such measures, it's certain we must take some counteraction. Otherwise, unlike the Royalists, the Nobility faction could be accused of lacking any self-cleansing mechanism.'

"I see. So that's the angle they're attacking from. ...Well, even so, the fact remains that the royalist faction made a complete spectacle of themselves. I doubt they'll be able to get away with such reckless behavior so easily."

'You're being naive. Nobles are skilled at exploiting their opponents' weaknesses. Do you really think they'd pass up such a golden opportunity?'

"If you're going to bring that up, doesn't the royalist faction have more weaknesses?"

The reason the royalist faction tried to enforce discipline this time was, needless to say, the incident with Rei.

More precisely, it started with him trying to forcibly take the maid.

And not because he'd fallen for her at first sight, but simply to make her his plaything.

On top of that, it caused such a huge uproar, bringing them utter disgrace.

If that point were pressed, the King's faction nobles would be helpless.

That was the sentiment Rei conveyed, but Elena shook her head.

"You understand the term 'royalist faction,' don't you? They could ultimately resort to bringing in His Majesty the King himself. ...Though, if they keep pulling stunts like that, everyone will lose patience with them, so it's not something they can do lightly."

"But does that apply to this situation, which isn't so simple? ...I suppose it does."

Though he voiced his doubt, Rei quickly convinced himself.

"It will. ...Anyway, it looks like we'll both be pretty busy starting this spring."

"Yeah. Vihera said she'd be coming to Gilm too... It's definitely going to be a big commotion."

Even just being the former princess of the empire is enough to generate buzz, but Vihera's appearance is incredibly flashy.

Her figure, which would inevitably draw the attention of people—or more accurately, men—was clad in thin garments that could easily be mistaken for those of a dancer or a courtesan.

Rei could vividly imagine the scene: adventurers, ignorant and unable to discern her strength, challenging Vihera only to be soundly defeated.

(In terms of sex appeal, Vihera certainly holds her own against Marina. Though Marina's is more of a quiet, mature allure... Well, that's just my uninformed opinion. Maybe a master of romance would see things differently.)

While Rei was pondering that Marina had a subdued allure and Vihera an aggressive one, he suddenly noticed Elena across the Paired Orb staring intently at him.

"What's up?"

"...Nothing. Just remembered something unpleasant. Anyway, you're supposed to run the mock battle as an instructor, right? Don't go overboard. If you fight seriously, you'll crush the students' confidence."

Unlike before, Elena changed the subject this time.

Understanding this, yet sensing that any clumsy remark in this situation could lead to a disastrous outcome, Rei went along with the topic.

"I don't plan on going that far either. The problem is, the cadets at the academy are basically older than me. And you know how it is—there's always someone who underestimates their opponent based on age."

'Heh, true. Especially since Rei is small. If you didn't lower your hood, you could easily be mistaken for a student. And when you take it off...'

"You'd be looked down on,"

Rei understood better than anyone that his face was far from intimidating.

(Well, it's true this was better than ending up with an ugly face. Besides, if I had a tough-looking face with this height, that would be unbalanced too. ...I wonder why Zepair made me this height and gave me this face. If he was going to call it his masterpiece, he could have at least made me look like I was in my twenties. Then I wouldn't have looked out of place standing next to Elena.)

He didn't know what they were thinking when they made him look like this, but still, it was the culmination of Zepair's technology, so Rei tucked his dissatisfaction away.

'Of course, once Rei shows his true strength, no one will dare defy him. Taking Set along is a good idea too. Even those on the front lines cower at the sight of Set, so cadets at the academy are a given, right?'

"True. To those who know nothing, Set is nothing but a symbol of terror. ...Even though his true nature is so affectionate."

"Heh heh, being a griffin, it can't be helped. People inevitably get influenced by preconceptions. You won't understand just how friendly Set is until you actually interact with him. ...Though his lack of mercy towards enemies does make him resemble his master."

"That's undeniably true. Though Set doesn't act that way out of choice—it's almost instinctual."

As the night deepened... Rei and Elena spent the next hour or so conversing through their Paired Orbs, enjoying their fleeting rendezvous.
