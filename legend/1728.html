Rei and Set, who had emerged from the cliff face where the dungeon was located, were first spotted by the adventurers waiting below for monsters to fall.

That said, once Rei and his party had entered the dungeon, hardly any monsters fell from above.

……This was a direct result of Rei and his party having significantly reduced the numbers of goblins, kobolds, and orcs in the forest.

While there were a few who were dissatisfied with this, most naturally knew that this was likely the dungeon.

That is precisely why few people voiced their complaints directly to Rei and the others, who were clearing the dungeon.

Though there were a few who grumbled about their earnings being a bit low.

“Hey, look! A griffin! Does that mean they’ve cleared the dungeon!?”

“Nah, no way… It’s way too soon for that. Maybe they just had to go back for some necessary gear or something?”

“They probably just couldn’t clear the dungeon and ran away.”

Even as they were talking, Set eventually began descending toward the ground.

The people clinging to Set’s legs climbed down one by one, followed by Set, who had Rei on his back, as he stepped onto the ground.

The people who had been chatting about Set’s appearance couldn’t say anything within earshot once they saw Set and Rei right in front of them.

Of course, that only applied to those who didn’t know Rei and the others… For those who were acquainted with them, it was a different story.

“Oh, you’re back!”

The one who called out to Rei was the adventurer they’d spoken with briefly on their way into the dungeon.

The one who had asked them to clear the dungeon.

“So, how was it? Inside the dungeon. Is it really that hard to clear!?”

“No, we cleared it.”

“……Huh?”

The man had approached them hoping to hear about the conditions inside the dungeon, but at first, he couldn’t make sense of the words that came out of Rei’s mouth.

What on earth was he talking about?

It was only natural. A dungeon isn’t the kind of thing you can usually tackle and clear in just a few days.

Especially for someone who’s never actually faced a dungeon before, it’s not uncommon to overestimate just how formidable they are.

The man who had spoken to Rei was probably that type of person.

“Um, is that a joke or something?”

“No, it’s the absolute truth. Look.”

Saying that, Rei showed him the core of the dungeon, severed by the Death Scythe.

But… for a man who had never challenged a dungeon, there was no way he could possibly know what it was.

“What the hell is that?”

“The dungeon’s core.”

“No way!? Is that for real!?”

At the words “dungeon core” coming from Rei’s mouth, those around them who had been listening to the man and Rei’s conversation also turned their attention to them.

They probably never imagined they’d be able to see the Core of the Dungeon in a place like this.

Even the others, who had left the conversation with Rei to that man, would naturally want to hear the details from Rei herself now that the Dungeon Core had come up.

“Hey, wait a minute. What are you going to do with that dungeon core? I mean, what can it be used for?”

“I have a friend of a friend of a friend who’s a famous merchant—would you be interested in selling it to him?”

“I’ve heard it can be used as alchemical ingredients—is that true?”

Besides that, all sorts of people showed interest in the dungeon core that Rei possessed.

Though, to be fair, there were a few who were clearly trying to pull off some kind of scam.

“Sorry, but I’m keeping this. I don’t know exactly what it’s for, but it looks like it could be used as alchemy materials or something.”

When Rei said that, most of them looked disappointed but gave up without saying anything further.

A few briefly considered trying to snatch the dungeon core from Rei, but they understood all too well that trying to force the issue with someone who had cleared a dungeon in just a few days would only shorten their own lives.

Rei understood that such people existed.

However, he judged that the likelihood of such a person actually attacking him was virtually nonexistent, so he simply opened his mouth without doing anything in particular.

“Well, sorry to say this, but we’re thinking of heading back to Golz soon. We just cleared the dungeon, so we’re pretty tired. We want to get some proper rest. Plus, we need to report back to the guild.”

“Huh? Oh, right. That makes sense. You just cleared the dungeon, after all. I bet you’re exhausted.”

Naturally, the most exhausted among Rei and the others was Marina, who had manipulated the water of the underground lake on such a grand scale.

But the others aren’t much different when it comes to mental exhaustion.

After all, clearing a dungeon isn’t something you can do easily.

What’s more, Rei and the others ended up facing the boss almost by accident.

And after destroying the dungeon’s core, they had to rush to escape while the dungeon could have collapsed or something at any moment.

So, of course they’re exhausted.

“Hey, so… what’s going to happen to that dungeon now?”

“Don’t ask me that. Honestly, I have no idea.”

If the inside of the dungeon were just an ordinary place—like a cave—nothing would look particularly out of the ordinary.

But in this case, the space inside the dungeon was clearly warped.

Otherwise, an underground lake—let alone a forest with something resembling the sun—would be far too massive to fit inside that cliff.

What’s more, Rei and the others went from the forest to the space with the rock plants, found a hidden door there, and proceeded straight to the underground lake… all the way to where the boss was.

When you think about it, there were likely several other staircases in the forest, and it wouldn’t be strange for them to lead to such places.

If that were the case, there was no way they could have managed all that just within the space inside that cliff.

“You don’t know… Could it be that the cliff might collapse… or something like that?”

“In the worst-case scenario, that’s certainly a possibility. That’s exactly why we need to keep a close eye on this dungeon from now on. Just in case the worst happens. Besides… since we don’t know what will happen to the space inside, we also don’t know what will happen to the monsters that were in the dungeon.”

There’s a very real possibility that the cliff could collapse and the monsters that survive it could come out.

Until now, the dungeon’s entrance was located halfway up the cliff—quite high up—so monsters that couldn’t fly, starting with goblins, would fall to the ground and die.

Because of that, the residents of Golz used to be able to obtain proof of defeat, magic stones, materials… and in some cases, even meat, without having to do anything special… but from now on, they won’t be able to get away with that.

It may well come to the point where they have to fight the dungeon’s survivors and defeat them to finally obtain those items.

Though, that’s how it’s supposed to be, really.

“...I understand.”

From Rei’s words, he must have understood the danger. The man nodded with a serious expression.

Once he was sure of that, Rei produced the Set Cage again and returned to Golz.

The moment they entered the guild in Golz, the eyes of the adventurers and guild staff inside turned toward Rei and his party.

Word that Rei and his party were tackling the dungeon had naturally spread among the residents of Golz, and since they had camped out in the dungeon the previous night, they hadn’t returned to Golz.

Some had even speculated that since they hadn’t returned, perhaps they had been wiped out… but with all of them now appearing at the guild, those rumors vanished instantly.

Rei was aware of the stares directed at them, but he didn’t seem particularly bothered by them as he headed toward the counter.

As Rei and the others approached, the receptionist, who had been startled by their presence, snapped back to her senses, smiled, and spoke.

“Welcome back, Rei. Since you’ve returned, have you found something rare again?”

“No, we’ve successfully cleared the dungeon, so I’m here to report that.”

“……Huh?”

A murmur ripples through the room.

Those who had been eavesdropping nearby were also stunned by the words that came out of Rei’s mouth, exchanging glances with their companions nearby and checking to see if they had misheard.

That surprise spread by word of mouth, reaching even those who were having a slightly early celebration at the guild’s tavern.

Because they were drunk, some of them did things they wouldn’t normally do.

“Hey, hey, hey, hey! You’ve already cleared the dungeon? You’ve got to be kidding me!?”

The man shouted this with a red face, and his companions nearby tried their best to calm him down.

“Hey, Dazura. Cut it out. That guy’s an adventurer with a reputation. He’s not someone you should be messing with!”

“Here, drink some water. Seriously, he’s usually so serious, but he turns into this when he drinks. Ah, I’m sorry, everyone. Please don’t mind us.”

A man and a woman each bowed their heads to Rei… and to the other adventurers.

The town of Golz is a typical rural town.

Because of that, many of the adventurers there know each other’s faces and names.

In a place like this, what would happen if someone went and picked a fight with a high-ranking adventurer with a notorious reputation...? Since Dazura’s drinking habits are fairly well-known, I don’t think it would become a huge problem, but it could still lead to trouble starting tomorrow.

To prevent that from happening, his two companions hurriedly bowed their heads.

But to Dazura, who was drunk and feeling bold, his companions’ concern meant nothing, and he called out to Rei.

“Hey, you. Did you really clear that dungeon? Then show me the proof, the proof… hiccup.”

Rei glanced at the drunk Dazura, then immediately lost interest and returned to his conversation with the receptionist.

“So, I’d like to see the Guild Master. Is that okay?”

“Huh? Oh, yes. Please, come this way. The Guild Master told me to let you through immediately if Rei arrived.”

While the receptionist’s words were true, she was supposed to have notified him that Rei and the others had arrived.

The reason she didn’t do so was likely because she judged that if she left Rei and the others here as they were, Dazura would undoubtedly get involved and cause a commotion.

The two members—a man and a woman—who were in a party with Dazura seemed to understand the receptionist’s intentions. Though they didn’t say anything, they gave a slight bow to show their gratitude.

And so, Rei and the others went inside the counter... Left behind and completely ignored, Dazura tried to say something, but... her two companions forcibly dragged her out of the guild.

Normally, since they hadn’t paid yet, it wouldn’t have been strange for people to make a fuss about them running the table, but since there were so many regulars there, many knew about Dazura’s terrible drinking habits.

Assuming she would come back to pay soon, no one called her out on it.

……Rather, everyone present agreed that it was more important to avoid a confrontation with Rei by not leaving Dazura behind.

And so, after Rei had disappeared, those who had witnessed the whole scene breathed a sigh of relief that it hadn’t turned into a major commotion.

“Oh my, you’re back early. So, what did you bring this time? Just so you know, we still don’t know anything about that rock plant yet.”

Upon entering the office, Guild Master Vanillas said this as she looked at Rei and the others.

She motioned for them to sit on the sofa, told the receptionist to bring some drinks and light snacks, and then turned her attention back to Rei and the others.

Rei smiled at her.

“If you’re asking what I brought, I suppose the quickest answer is that I brought this.”

Saying that, he took out the dungeon core from his Misty Ring.

True to his status as Guild Master, Vanillas immediately understood what Rei had produced.

He understood, but... he stared at Rei—and everyone else—with an expression of disbelief.

Finally, his gaze settled on Marina, who still looked a little tired.

Given that she was a former Guild Master, he figured Marina would understand what he was trying to say.

Marina, receiving that look, gave a small nod in return.

“Without a doubt, the core of this dungeon is the one we were facing—the one on the cliff face.”

She stated this with absolute certainty.
