About two weeks had passed since Vihera regained consciousness, and outside, snow had begun to fall, marking the complete transition from late autumn to early winter.

Fortunately, even though it had snowed, it hadn't yet accumulated significantly. Some adventurers were making one last push to save funds for the winter.

Gamelion hunting remained popular, but many also took on quests to slay monsters that only appeared during winter.

Amidst this... Rei had come to the Azimov residence.

Though calling it Azimov's house was perhaps less accurate than describing it as his research facility.

"Sorry about that. I've been a bit busy with various things."

Azimov, looking unusually tired for once, had been busy with various things lately.

Rei didn't know the exact reason for his busyness, but it was also true that Rei himself had been feeling the weight of prying eyes lately and hadn't had much breathing room.

...The news that Rei had defeated the Silver Lion and cleared the dungeon had been announced by the Guild.

The report, released a few days after Elena and Ara returned, naturally drew the attention of the adventurers in the guild to Rei.

The merchants, as expected, were particularly numerous.

The existence of the Silver Lion as the dungeon's boss monster was a fairly well-known fact.

That's precisely why, for the merchants, it was a golden opportunity to obtain materials from the Silver Lion... a Rank S monster.

Still, several coincidences worked in Rei's favor. It was late autumn, so few merchants remained in Gilm. Combined with the experience gained from the Twilight Spear incident, things didn't become as troublesome as they might have otherwise.

The fact that the townspeople and adventurers' gazes remained unchanged from before was another factor that spared Rei any stress.

For the adventurers, Rei was widely recognized as an exceptional individual possessing formidable combat prowess from the start. For the townspeople, Rei was seen as special in various ways because of Set's presence.

Still, some adventurers who had been earning their keep in dungeons occasionally came to Gilm harboring grievances, meaning Rei couldn't afford to let his guard down in many ways.

Fortunately, it was winter, and everyone was busy preparing for the cold season. As the attention directed at Rei began to wane, word came from Azimov, leading to this long-awaited reunion.

"I've had my own share of things going on too."

"Yeah, I heard. Never would've guessed you'd conquer a dungeon."

Naturally, Azimov had heard about Rei conquering the dungeon.

He turned his gaze toward Rei, as if looking at something interesting.

"...What's up?"

"Well, I heard you cleared the dungeon, but looking at you now, you seem exactly the same as before."

Rei nodded in agreement at Azimov's words.

Conquering a dungeon doesn't change someone's personality.

Of course, some people do change due to great fame, but in Rei's case, he already had a certain amount of fame even before clearing the dungeon.

Starting with his exploits in the war against the Bestir Empire, he had earned various other forms of renown as well.

For someone like Rei, the fame gained from conquering the dungeon now wasn't something he particularly valued.

...Of course, Rei understood that conquering the dungeon guarded by the Silver Lion, which he could only defeat with the help of others like Elena and Elk, was undoubtedly a great achievement for him.

"Honestly, it's not as bad as the Twilight Spear incident, but these merchants are a pain."

"Well, if it's material for an S-rank monster, I guess that can't be helped."

"It's not just merchants, though. I've been getting quite a few approaches from alchemists too."

"...I suppose so."

While merchants were desperate to sell Silver Lion materials, it was only natural that alchemists would start moving when it came to using the materials themselves.

Even materials from Rank A monsters often spark fierce competition among alchemists, but this was material from a Rank S monster.

It wouldn't be strange for someone to consider paying their entire fortune just to get their hands on that material.

Amidst this, Azimov, who was reasonably close to Rei, wasn't panicking quite so much.

Of course, Azimov was interested in the Silver Lion's materials too, but he believed Rei would bring them to him.

"So, did you bring that material?"

"Yeah. ...But I'm stuck on how to use it, just having the material alone."

Currently, Rei possessed weapons like the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear.

He also possessed several other combat magic items, so there was nothing he urgently needed.

Normally, one might worry about the materials deteriorating, but in Rei's case, he had Misty Ring, so that concern was unnecessary.

"Hmm... I see."

Rei nodded at his words, staring intently at his figure.

Rei was equipped with the Dragon Robe, Sleipnir's Boots, Misty Ring, the Soul-Sucking Bracelet, the New Moon Ring, and various other magic items.

And hidden within the Dragon Robe was another magic item: Nebula's Eye.

Considering how many other magic items were stored within the Misty Ring, it was fair to say Rei was practically a walking bundle of magic items.

"Looking at Rei, his entire body is reinforced with magic items. But you're the one who collects magic items as a hobby, right? So, you can't possibly not want more magic items?"

"Yeah. That's for sure. But I never imagined I'd suddenly get my hands on materials from a Silver Lion like this. I'm pretty stumped on how to go about getting them."

"...Specifically, what kind of materials does the Silver Lion have?"

He asked this because he'd heard rumors that several people, besides Rei, had joined forces to fight and defeat the Silver Lion.

If the materials were divided equally, the magic items you could craft would differ depending on which parts remained.

That was the reasoning behind the question...

"We got almost the entire thing. We handed over a few claws and such, though."

"...Huh?"

The words that came out of Rei's mouth were completely unexpected, even for Azimov.

"The whole thing?"

You're kidding, right? Or joking? That was the implication in Azimov's question, but Rei simply nodded quietly.

"Yeah. Pretty much intact."

"...Why?"

"I don't know?"

Azimov's question was one Rei had also asked herself.

But regardless of their individual reasons, the undeniable fact remained: they had given Rei the Silver Lion almost exactly as it was.

"That's weird, right?"

"Yeah, it is."

Normally, materials from an S-rank monster are something anyone would naturally covet.

At the very least, if Azimov ever got his hands on materials from an S-rank monster, he certainly wouldn't hand them over to someone else. ...He wouldn't want to.

"What's going on? ...Well, never mind. For now, just show me the Silver Lion's materials. They're in the item box, so they shouldn't have degraded or rotted, but I still want to check."

"Understood. ...But there's a considerable amount, so I can't just hand it all over here, you know?"

Materials from a Silver Lion measuring four meters in length... six meters including its tail.

Most of it is meat, of course, but the other materials are also quite large.

This room, now essentially Azimov's lab, was reasonably spacious, but it was cluttered with various tools for alchemy and research materials. There was absolutely no way he could bring out the Silver Lion's materials here.

Realizing this, Azimov gave a small nod and rose from his chair.

His unusually swift movements likely stemmed from his eagerness to see the Silver Lion's materials with his own eyes as soon as possible.

"Then come over here,"

Saying only that, he directed a look that said "Hurry up" toward Rei.

Seeing Azimov acting completely out of character, Rei gave a resigned smile and followed him.

And so, to Rei's surprise... it was a room that was actually tidy.

The room, of considerable size, was a space Azimov had tidied up specifically for his alchemy experiments scheduled for a few days later.

But for Azimov now, postponing that experiment posed no problem.

In fact, the Silver Lion's materials were his top priority.

"Alright, I'll get it out then?"

"Wait! It'd be a waste to just lay the Silver Lion's materials directly on the floor."

Saying this, Azimov spread a cloth on the floor.

And with the cloth laid down, he seemed reassured. Nodding contentedly, he urged Rei on.

"Alright, bring it out."

Nodding at his words, Rei began pulling the Silver Lion's ingredients out of the Misty Ring one after another.

The combined meat was too large to fit entirely on the cloth, but the meat was meant for consumption.

Therefore, most of the materials fit on the cloth.

The only exception was the Silver Lion's bones—there was no way around that.

"What about the bones? I don't think we can fit them on the cloth."

"...I suppose so."

While responding to Rei, Azimov's gaze remained fixed on the Silver Lion's materials.

Though Azimov was famously eccentric even among alchemists, he was still an alchemist at heart.

The materials of an S-rank monster were more than enough to capture his attention.

"Bones are quite versatile. You could repurpose them directly into weapons, or grind them into powder to imbue weapons with special effects. The internal organs... as alchemical materials, they're incredibly valuable. The eyeballs..."

Turning his gaze to the eyeball in the container, Azimov tilted his head and asked Rei.

"Why is there only one eyeball?"

A genuinely puzzled question.

Given that a Silver Lion has two eyes, the question was perfectly natural.

But to such a question from Azimov, Rei opened his mouth, looking slightly awkward.

"When we fought the Silver Lion, I thrust the Twilight Spear into its eye. It dealt massive damage, but that eye was destroyed as a result."

"...I see. So the Twilight Spear proved to be a powerful weapon."

Hearing that the Twilight Spear he had created had made a notable impact in the battle against the Silver Lion, he wore a slightly complicated expression.

While he was glad the Twilight Spear had performed well against the Silver Lion, the fact that the Silver Lion had lost an eyeball still gave him pause as an alchemist.

"Well, what's done is done. At least one eye is still intact."

"Hearing you say that helps me too. So, what kind of magic item do you think we can make?"

"Hmm..."

Azimov had been looking at Rei again, but suddenly his gaze shifted to Rei's feet... specifically, to the Sleipnir boots.

By channeling magic power, they allowed him to use the air as a foothold and kick off for a few steps.

While its effect seemed modest compared to Rei's other magic items, it had played a significant role in his adventures as an adventurer.

"Using a few internal organs, we could probably enhance the effect of the Sleipnir Boots somewhat."

"...Enhance?"

"Yeah. Specifically, it would increase the number of steps you can take using the air as a foothold by a few more steps than you can now."

"Really? If that's possible, it'd be a huge help for us too..."

「Rei？」

As Azimov turned his gaze toward Rei, who had abruptly stopped mid-conversation, Rei shook her head, signaling it was nothing.

He had thought about mentioning it when Elena was present, given that it concerned Sleipnir's boots.

Since Elena used Sleipnir's shoes just like Rei, she would undoubtedly be delighted to hear they were being enhanced.

(Well, she probably didn't have the time for that.)

To begin with, Elena had come to Gilm rather forcefully after hearing about Vihera.

Naturally, she must have left a lot of work behind, and she undoubtedly caused trouble for people everywhere.

Given that, once the Vihera matter was resolved, returning immediately was the obvious choice.

In fact, even last night when they communicated using the Paired Orbs, she seemed a bit subdued.

Still, the fact that she didn't bring it up made Rei think she was being cold.

...Then again, Elena, who always carried herself like a princess general, didn't have many people she could show even the slightest weakness to.

"Understood. Then enhance Sleipnir's boots."

Rei told Azimov that using the Silver Lion's materials was the priority right now.
