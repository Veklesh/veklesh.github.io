"So, what now? We've already made arrangements with the informant, so there's nothing left to do, right?"

Having concluded their deal with the security force's in-house informant, Rei and the others were walking toward the main street.

Beside Rei, Set happily ate a sandwich he'd bought as a souvenir from the tavern.

Despite being the kind of tavern you'd find on a backstreet, the food there was several times tastier than at your average, run-of-the-mill eatery.

Of course, it fell slightly short compared to renowned restaurants like the Full Belly Inn or the Golden Bread Inn, but it was undeniably a place that served delicious food.

Precisely because it was such a tavern, Rei and the others had bought sandwiches as souvenirs for Set, who was waiting outside.

...Under normal circumstances, they might not have offered such a service, but the shop knew who Rei and the others were and didn't refuse.

Though, to be honest, the biggest reason they let them take sandwiches home was probably because they were genuinely pleased to have their tavern food praised.

"Right. We've asked the informant, and we've also asked other people in the underworld to gather information. So, all we have to do now is wait for the information to come in, right?"

"...You're being unusually quiet for you, Rei."

"Is that so? ...Well, maybe you're right. But I made a promise to Tris. Besides, it's not like I attack just anyone. ...Haven't rumors about me been spreading in weird directions lately?"

"That can't be helped."

At Rei's words, Vihera shrugged her shoulders slightly.

Naturally, such a gesture caused Vihera's ample curves, barely covered by her thin robe, to sway. A man passing nearby found his gaze utterly drawn to the sight.

Whether she was his girlfriend or family... anyway, the woman with him slammed her elbow into his side.

The blow, delivered when he was completely off guard, carried enough force to send him sprawling.

Unconcerned by the exchange, Vihera continued speaking to Rei.

"It's normal for famous people, not just those with nicknames, to be subjected to all sorts of strange scrutiny, right? Even Elk gets talked about behind his back."

"...In Elk's case, he's got such a boisterous personality, so I guess it's only natural he gets talked about for that."

From Rei's perspective, there was no doubt Elk had a bold personality.

Though, even so, he wasn't arrogant about it – that was typical Elk.

It was also fairly well known that he was devoted to his family and couldn't stand up to his wife, Min.

...Though that devotion to family could sometimes run wild.

When the Bestir Empire took his wife Min and son Rodos hostage... while Rei was rushing to rescue them, Elk was extremely agitated.

So much so that he instilled fear in anyone who crossed him.

It was something the usual Elk would never do.

Seeing him like that probably spread the rumors.

Rei didn't know the full extent of it, but knowing Elk's usual personality, he felt it was only natural that strange rumors would circulate.

(Besides, he seemed pretty desperate about the whole Rodos situation anyway.)

Elk and Min were busy running around treating Rodos, who remained unconscious after the incident with the Bestir Empire.

Their desperate efforts inevitably became the subject of rumors.

Currently, in exchange for helping treat Vihera, who had been rendered unconscious by Ambris, Rhodes was also receiving treatment and was awake and normal.

Still, having been unconscious for so long, Rei had heard that he was now working hard at rehabilitation.

He had considered visiting him several times, but in the end, it hadn't happened.

It wasn't that Rei hadn't gone; Elk had subtly asked him not to see Rodos for now.

Rhodos, who harbored a competitive streak toward Rei, probably didn't want him to see him in his current state, barely able to move.

(I don't mind... but if he does, I guess it can't be helped. Besides, if he starts messing with me in some weird way and causes trouble, it'd be a real hassle.)

The sight of Rhodes during the civil war in the Bestir Empire had left quite a strong impression on Rei.

Now that Elk and Min were there, he figured it would be fine even if something happened, but it was still necessary to be extra cautious.

(In that case, is it fortunate that I've lost my memory...? Or not?)

He walked through the city streets, lost in such thoughts.

He had already moved from the back alleys to the main streets, and naturally, people began to appear who wanted to see and admire Set.

Rei watched as children and adults alike offered Set food and stroked him, but suddenly felt his Dragon Robe tugged.

For a moment, Rei wondered if it was a pickpocket or something. But when he looked over, it was Byune, still expressionless, tugging at his Dragon Robe.

"What's up? You don't seriously want to play with Set, do you? Well, since Byune's going to be with Set from now on, you could at least give him a little space, right?"

Ray thought to himself, wondering if Byune, who secretly enjoyed playing with Set, was sulking because she felt like he'd been taken away.

Thinking this, Rei spoke up, but Byune shook her head, denying his words.

"Hmm."

What she thrust forward was a dagger with a pure white blade.

Its blade was clearly longer than a standard dagger, yet it still fell under the classification of a dagger.

Seeing that weapon, named White Cloud and crafted from Silver Lion material, Rei sighed again at its beauty, yet wondered why Byune had brought out White Cloud here.

For a moment, he wondered if she wanted to forcefully deal with those admiring Set. But of course, Byune showed no such signs.

Then, was it just to show off? Considering Byune's personality, that seemed unlikely too.

In the end, to understand why this had happened, there was no choice but to ask someone who could grasp Byune's intentions.

"Vihera, what is Byune trying to say?"

Rei had come to understand Byune's will to a certain extent, but it still wasn't complete.

(Since we're adventurers in a party, I suppose I need to figure this out somehow. ...Well, somehow I just can't seem to manage it.)

While Rei was lost in such thoughts, Vihera immediately understood what Byune was trying to say and spoke up.

"If you don't have anything specific to do now, she says she wants to go to Pamidor's blacksmith workshop. We've been maintaining White Cloud, but it's still best to have a professional handle it, right?"

"...I see. Well, I figured we needed to inform Pamidor about Azimov anyway, and I thought we should visit him, so it's fine with me."

Besides, there was also the matter of informing him that they had formed a party.

After all, Rei and Pamidor had known each other for quite some time.

That's precisely why he felt it necessary to discuss these matters.

Pamidor and Azimov also had a long history together.

Well, strictly speaking, it was more accurate to say that Pamidor and Azimov had a deeper relationship as a blacksmith and an alchemist than Rei and Pamidor did as customers.

He had heard from the party before that Azimov was practically family to him, occasionally bringing him meals and such.

Given that, it was undoubtedly best to inform him that Azimov, who was practically family, had been injured.

Even if the injury was healed with a potion.

(Besides, Azimov was targeted precisely because he knew me. So, since Pamidor also knows me, she's definitely at risk of being targeted too.)

Of course, there was the question: Would Precious use the same method twice?

But at the same time, there's no doubt the Azimov incident had an effect on Rei.

Given that, there's no guarantee she won't repeat the same kind of action.

"Right. Then let's go see Pamidor."

"Mm!"

At Rei's words, Byune's lips curved into a smile—just a little... really just a little.

Seeing Byune like that made Rei feel a little happy too... He then informed the others gathered at Set that they were moving on and left the spot.

As Rei and the others approached Pamidor's blacksmith workshop, fortunately... or perhaps inevitably, unlike when they entered Azimov's lab, the smell of rusty iron did not waft toward them.

Unlike Azimov, he wasn't using any magic items for deodorization, so Pamidor must be fine.

Thinking this, Rei ordered Set to wait near the shop before entering the smithy.

"Pamidor, are you here?"

He called out, but no one was behind the counter.

But deep inside the shop... where the forge was, there was a sense of someone being present, so it wasn't completely empty.

"You fool, that's not it! When you swing that hammer, you swing it harder! Don't think about holding back—just go all out!"

Hearing the angry shout, Rei tilted his head.

Because that voice didn't sound like Pamidor's.

No, Pamidor had a face so vicious you wouldn't be surprised to call him the leader of a band of thieves, but even so, why was he shouting like this... More precisely, who was he shouting at? Rei wondered.

Originally, only Pamidor worked in this blacksmith's workshop, but the current angry voice sounded as if he were teaching someone how to swing a hammer.

That alone made it impossible not to wonder: Why?

The others felt the same way.

"What should we do?"

Vihera asked Rei, but since they'd come to see Pamidor in the first place, staying silent wasn't really an option.

"We have to go in. ...Pamidor, are you there? We're coming in."

He called out as he entered the forge... and the sight that spread before him made Rei's eyes widen in shock.

He suddenly remembered being just as shocked when he entered Azimov's lab, though this time it wasn't the worst possible sight like with Azimov.

Pamidor's son, Kumito, was swinging a golden hammer down again and again.

Given that Kumito was only a few years past ten, the hammer he held was obviously not the size Pamidor would use.

It was precisely the right size for young Kumito to wield.

Beside him, Pamidor pointed out Kumito's flaws in a loud voice.

"When you swing the hammer, you must value that fleeting spark of brilliance. Channel the flow within you directly into the hammer as you swing it. Understand?"

"I don't get it!? What the hell are you talking about!?"

Kumito, who had been swinging the hammer, couldn't help but yell at his father's incomprehensible advice.

His sweat-drenched hammering motion had stopped.

"Yeah..."

Rei, who had been listening to Pamidor's words, agreed with Kumito's statement.

Not only that, but Marina, Vihera, and even Byune—who usually didn't seem to care much about others—nodded at Rei's words.

By any normal standard, what Pamidor was saying could only be described as unreasonable.

Yet the person saying it showed no doubt whatsoever in his own words... and still, he twisted his vicious expression in puzzlement, as if wondering why they couldn't understand.

(I've heard that geniuses are terrible at teaching others... I guess Pamidor is one of those. Or rather, I always thought Pamidor was more of a hard worker than a genius type.)

Thinking this, Rei called out to Pamidor, who was clearly failing miserably in his attempt to pass on his skills to his son.

"Pamidor, got a sec?"

"Huh? ...What is it? Oh, it's Rei. And the others too. What's up?"

"Well, 'what's up' is exactly what I want to ask you."

"Kumito's going to work as a blacksmith, so I'm training him. ...Don't you get it?"

"If anyone understood that explanation, I'd be impressed."

Marina, Vihera, and Byune nodded again at Rei's words.

...No, even Kumito was nodding, not just Rei and the others.

Perhaps finding their attitude amusing, Pamidor spoke up, looking displeased.

"So, let me ask again—what are you here for? As you can see, I'm busy right now. Unless it's urgent..."

"Azimov was attacked by someone."
