"Hmm. So Ilze came to Gilm looking for work too. ...But coming all the way to Gilm at Rank F? That's reckless."

"I absolutely had to come to Gilm."

"Absolutely? Huh? Didn't you come for the expansion project?"

"No, that was the goal."

While responding to Melan at the counter, Ilze found the man talking right in front of her slightly annoying.

She'd secured lodging, and she wanted to get moving on gathering information as soon as possible.

Gathering intel at the tavern might be tough, but there were other places where people gathered.

Especially since the enemy Ilze was targeting was an adventurer, visiting the guild was essential.

If things went well... if they really went well, that is. It wasn't entirely impossible that her enemy, who knew nothing, might show up at the guild.

Besides, even setting that aside, she found it hard to stand Melan smiling and chatting up to her in front of Ilze.

Melan clearly possessed qualities of brightness, openness, light...

Compared to that, Ilze, who seeks to find and kill the murderers of her parents and brother, is clearly the polar opposite of Melan.

For Ilze, talking to Melan is mentally draining.

Though she never lets it show.

Melan, too, was one of the adventurers who had arrived at Gilm before her.

Therefore, there was a strong possibility she possessed information unknown to him.

Since she couldn't go gather information from the guild or elsewhere, she thought she should at least try to get something from Melan...

But then, a helping hand reached out.

"Melan, cut it out. That girl over there, Ilze, just arrived at Gilm today. She probably searched high and low for lodging before coming here... Let her rest."

"Whoops, sorry. Yeah, you're right. It goes without saying how hard it is to find lodging in Gilm right now."

Melan had walked around quite a few inns before finding this one. He could easily imagine how difficult it must have been for Ilze.

Though, when Melan first arrived, there were fewer people coming to Gilm seeking work than there were now.

Even then, it took a considerable amount of time to find this place. So Melan understood perfectly well how much harder it must be to find lodging in the current, more crowded Gilm.

"No, don't worry about it. I heard many useful things about Gilm from you."

What a good girl, he thought.

The innkeeper handed the key to Ilze, who had spoken to Melan in a deferential manner.

"Here, this is the key to your room. It's on the second floor, toward the back—the Fang Room."

"...The Fang Room?"

Normally, wouldn't they be distinguished by numbers or something?

Ilze wondered about this, but when told she'd understand once she went upstairs, she said no more.

"Ah, well then, I'll show you the way. This way."

Was he being considerate, or did he just want to talk to Ilze a little longer...? Either way, Melan led Ilze upstairs.

Ilze found Melan slightly bothersome, but since it meant she wouldn't have to search for the room herself, she followed along.

Finding the room with the fang plate hanging on it wasn't particularly difficult, though.

When they reached the spot not far from the stairs, Ilze hesitated about what to do with Melan.

She understood perfectly well that he wasn't looking at her with any kind of vulgar gaze, but was genuinely being kind.

But... still, it was an undeniable fact that Ilze disliked Melan's personality... found him difficult to deal with.

Then, as if reading Ilze's thoughts, Melan spoke up.

"Well, I'll be off now. I've got some training to do."

"Training?"

To Ilze, Melan seemed incredibly strong.

At the very least, he seemed far beyond what Ilze could hope to match.

"Yeah. ...There's someone I have to defeat. To save Ms. Vihera from that man's clutches."

When spoken with such intense resolve, Ilze couldn't dismiss it lightly.

She never wore her emotions on her sleeve to begin with, and in Melan's case, even if she did show them, he might not notice... Ilze couldn't help but think that, but it wasn't strange for her to consider it just in case.

"Is there someone you want to defeat?"

"Yeah. Ms. Vihera... The one deceiving the person I care about."

"Deceiving, you say?"

Ilze looked slightly puzzled, but since she had no intention of getting deeply involved with Melan, she said no more.

"Well then, I'd like to sort my things and rest a bit, so I'll take my leave here."

"Understood. If you ever have any questions, feel free to ask. If there's anything I can do to help, I'll assist you."

"...Thank you."

Saying only that, Ilze entered the room.

(If he really meant to help, knowing about Gilm and being stronger than me, he could be reliable... but it's probably just polite talk, right?)

Sighing softly, Ilze looked around the room.

Being close to the back alley, the room was quite cramped.

A bed, a desk, and a simple shelf occupied most of the space.

If Rei saw this room, he'd probably think it was about four tatami mats in size.

At the very least, compared to the room Rei stayed in at Dusk Wheat inn, it was considerably smaller, and the furnishings were worn and old.

"Guess I'm lucky it's not rotten,"

She muttered while looking at the wooden shelves, quickly unpacking her belongings.

Considering the lodging fee was relatively cheap, she figured it was what you'd expect for the price.

After all, if she'd been unlucky, she might have ended up in a large dormitory room sleeping on the floor with a bunch of men, or even had to sleep rough.

Considering that, she couldn't really complain about having a proper private room to sleep in, even if the facilities were a bit old.

Anyway... Ilze finished putting her things away quickly.

Regardless of her low rank, Ilze had wandered through various villages, towns, and cities searching for her enemy.

She was surprisingly seasoned for someone of her rank.

That's precisely why she had very little luggage when she came to Gilm, and tidying the room was done in no time.

As usual, she carried only her dagger and walked straight out of the room.

"Oh? Leaving already?"

"Yes. I need to find work as soon as possible."

He replied with a smile to the innkeeper.

Ilze's true purpose was to seek revenge or information leading to it, but her claim of looking for work was by no means a lie.

As a low-rank adventurer, Ilze had a hard time making ends meet.

...She had even considered using her reasonably attractive face to earn money as a prostitute on several occasions.

But thinking of her kind parents and brother, she couldn't just casually do something like that.

Perhaps... if she truly reached the end of her rope, she might have considered it. Fortunately, she hadn't reached that point yet.

"Well, yeah. If you don't pick and choose, there's plenty of work available in the current Gilm. If you're confident in your skills, guarding walls under construction could be a good option."

"No, unfortunately, I'm not very confident in my skills..."

In truth, Ilze only possessed the skills of a Rank F adventurer.

She simply wasn't cut out for combat.

Even so, Ilze hadn't given up on avenging her enemy.

"I see. Well then... At construction sites, adventurers are often given various odd jobs besides that, so maybe helping out with those could be a good option."

"Understood. I'll go to the guild and ask about that in detail."

"That's the right approach. It'd be pretty awkward if you claimed you could handle work you couldn't actually do, and then ended up accomplishing nothing."

After exchanging a few brief words, Ilze bowed to the innkeeper and left the inn.

"...She's got something on her mind, huh?"

Watching the door Ilze had just exited, the innkeeper muttered to himself.

Her appearance clearly didn't suit an adventurer.

Of course, many adventurers possessed strength that was impossible to gauge from their appearance alone.

But... judging by Ilze's demeanor, the innkeeper couldn't help but feel her skills as an adventurer probably weren't much to speak of either.

Still, she had come to Gilm, so there was undoubtedly some reason behind it.

In other words, she was undoubtedly troubled by something.

That said, the shopkeeper had no intention of getting involved.

He was merely providing her lodging.

He had absolutely no intention of prying into it.

(Well, if she caused trouble for my inn, that would be a different story.)

He glanced toward the door as he thought this, but quickly shook his head and returned to his work.

"It's crowded, isn't it?"

Though exasperated by the far greater number of people than she'd anticipated, Ilze headed toward the guild.

She knew roughly where it was from Melan, and besides, it was on the main street—impossible to miss.

Ilze walked along, lost in such thoughts, but then...

"Huh!?"

Mid-stride, a sudden sight caught her eye, making her gasp.

Through the gaps between the many people walking, she saw a creature with the upper body of an eagle and the lower body of a lion...

Ilze knew exactly what it was, of course.

A Rank A monster, a Griffin.

Yet despite the presence of such a creature, one worthy of being called legendary, the residents of Gilm showed no particular surprise.

In fact, some were even petting the griffin or offering it food.

(What's going on here?)

I thought only a few people accepted the griffin, but many walking down the main street were looking at it with expressions as if they were seeing something heartwarming.

(A dream? Or a hallucination?)

Was I losing consciousness from the midsummer heat and dreaming... or was I hallucinating from the heat?

For a moment, that thought crossed my mind, but I quickly dismissed it.

He pinched his arm lightly, and it hurt normally.

That meant the griffin at the end of those gazes was real... and then he remembered hearing that there was an adventurer in this Gilm with a reputation for having a griffin as a familiar.

It wasn't a story related to his enemy, so he hadn't paid it much attention.

But he remembered hearing similar tales in various places.

(Was it Guren? No, that's not right. Crimson? Yeah, I think that was the nickname.)

As if retrieving it from the depths of memory, I recalled the rumors about the adventurer who commanded a griffin.

It was said that no matter who they were—nobility or otherwise—they killed their enemies without mercy.

They say he possessed immense combat prowess, single-handedly ending the war with the Bestir Empire.

They say he's extremely fond of women and keeps beauties by his side.

(Huh?)

Recalling this far, Ilze suddenly realized.

Could this man be a powerful ally in her quest for revenge?

As an adventurer, Ilze possessed only low combat prowess.

Given that, she hadn't really considered taking down enemies with her own hands directly.

Of course, if she could take the lives of those who killed her parents and brother with her own hands, that would be best.

But Ilze understood her own limitations well enough to know that was impossible.

If she absolutely had to take revenge herself, she'd likely need to use poison or something similar.

Or... though the very thought sent a chill down her spine, she could lure them into bed and strike while they held her.

But ultimately, given her doubts about her own combat ability, the chances of failure were extremely high.

If that's the case, wouldn't it be better to drag someone with greater combat strength than herself into it?

(Fortunately, he's said to be a womanizer, so enduring being held is something I can endure).

Being embraced by my enemy fills me with revulsion so intense I'd rather die, but if it means avenging myself, I can accept it.

Ilze felt she was full of contradictions, but she could still accept her own reasoning.

(Besides, if the griffin is there, we can move quickly to other locations too.)

Since she didn't know if her enemy was in Gilm, Ilze reasoned that a griffin was the best means of transportation.

However, Ilze didn't know that Set fundamentally couldn't carry anyone besides Rei.

If she were to use Set for travel, she'd literally have to cling to his front legs.

With these thoughts, she left the spot and eventually arrived at the guild. Ilze glanced at the request boards as if just out of habit...

"Huh?"

She froze.

Because there, at the end of her gaze... on the arm of a man standing at the guild counter, was a tattoo she recognized.

The same tattoo as the man who killed her kind parents and brother.
