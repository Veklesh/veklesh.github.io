As Rei headed toward the guild with the tree he had cut down... he found five men lying unconscious on the guild floor.

If that were all, Rei probably wouldn’t have given it a second thought, considering it was just another day in Gilm.

But… seeing Marina, Vihera, and Byune standing beside those unconscious men, Rei couldn’t exactly say it had nothing to do with him.

Since this was clearly a commotion caused by Rei’s companions, he had no choice but to ask what had happened.

That said, perhaps the one stroke of luck for Rei was that many of the adventurers in the guild were looking at the unconscious adventurers with expressions that said, “They brought this on themselves.”

Seeing that reaction, Rei could vaguely understand how things had come to this.

For adventurers who had been at Gilm for some time, there weren’t many who didn’t know Vihera—who acted alongside Rei and enjoyed combat—not to mention the former guild master, Marina.

However, due to the expansion of Gilm, many adventurers who wouldn’t normally have the ability to operate in Gilm are currently working there.

Moreover, Rei and the others have been away from Gilm quite often lately, traveling to the Kingdom of Reburina, out to sea, or into dungeons.

So, for the adventurers who arrived in Gilm while Rei and the others were away, it wouldn’t have been surprising if some of them took the initiative to strike up a conversation with Marina and Vihera, two women of exceptional beauty.

...That said, if Marina and the others had merely been spoken to, they would have simply turned them down and that would have been the end of it.

The fact that this scene has unfolded suggests that the adventurers currently lying unconscious on the floor must have tried to make advances on them by force.

It was just Rei’s guess, but he was confident he wasn’t far off.

And indeed...

“Seriously, those guys really did something stupid.”

Judging by the attitude of the man who spoke to Rei, her guess was probably correct after all.

Rei turned his gaze toward the man and recognized him.

“Brasso?”

“Ah, it’s been a while.”

The man who said this with a smile was even shorter than Rei, who was already shorter than average.

That said, he was visibly much more muscular than Rei, and a long beard grew from his chin.

In his hand was a massive hammer known as the Earthshaker.

He was Brasso, a dwarf and member of the Rank C party, the Shattering Warriors.

He and Rei had worked together on quests before and had even resolved a dispute with a merchant who had been blinded by greed over the magic items possessed by Set and Rei, so they were fairly well acquainted.

Although he is a dwarf, he has no talent as a blacksmith… yet the fact that he has an extraordinary interest in blacksmithing has left a strong impression on Rei.

“What about Fron?”

When Rei mentioned the name of the companion he was partying with, Brasso shrugged slightly before speaking.

“He’s out hunting Gamelions.”

“...Didn’t you go, Brasso?”

Without even trying to hide his surprise, Rei muttered.

In Gilm during this season, Gamelions are a tasty monster to hunt—both for eating yourself and for selling the materials.

That’s precisely why, assuming his partner had gone Gamelion hunting, it wouldn’t have been strange for Brasso to go as well—that’s what he’d thought when he asked…

“I’ve hunted more than enough Gamelions and saved up plenty of money to get through the winter, so there’s no need to push myself. Instead, I’m currently learning all sorts of things from a blacksmith.”

“Hmm. …So, is the Gamelion hunt really coming to an end?”

“Hmm, yes. There’s no doubt the peak season has passed.”

“……I see.”

At the words “the peak season has passed,” Rei muttered regretfully.

Of course, just because the peak season is over doesn’t mean Gamelions have completely disappeared.

But it also meant that the period filled with the unique excitement of Gamelion hunting—an atmosphere that could almost be called a festival—had already come to an end.

Rei’s honest feeling was that he would have liked to experience that atmosphere together.

“So, even though the peak season is over, why is Fron still hunting Gamelions?”

“That idiot has been spending a bit too recklessly. If he doesn’t make as much money as he can from Gamelion right now, he might end up having to work through the winter.”

Seeing the exasperation in Brasso’s tone, Rei understood the situation.

That said, when Rei thought of an adventurer in financial trouble, the first person who came to mind was Milein, who had spent too much on Set, but he didn’t know what had led Fron to this situation.

It would be a lie to say he wasn’t at least somewhat curious, but he’d rather avoid getting dragged into some strange trouble by hearing the details here.

That said… although the peak season for Gamelion hunting has passed, it isn’t completely over yet, so Rei intends to go hunt Gamelions.

If that’s the case, there’s a decent chance he’ll run into Fron over there.

(Ah… but it seems the pace of the expansion work has started to fall behind while we’ve been away. It might not be that urgent right now, but I’d really like to do something about that too.)

For Rei, who considers Gilm his home in this world, the expansion of Gilm through the construction work was most welcome.

That said, it was also true that strange characters had started showing up because of the construction.

For that very reason, helping out with the expansion project wasn’t all that much of a burden for Rei.

He’d be paid well for it, and more importantly, once the expansion was complete, research to adapt the sand-sail ship for use on land would begin—a significant benefit for Rei.

Thinking that... the topic of sand ships reminded him of the incident involving the Kingdom of Reburina.

To be precise, the group of former slaves heading from the Kingdom of Reburina to Gilm.

(Come to think of it, autumn is getting well underway, and considering the travel time, it wouldn’t be surprising if they’ve already arrived in Gilm. I suppose I should drop by at least once, if only to resupply them.)

All things considered, Rei has plenty of things he needs to do… he hardly has any time to spare for leisure.

Well, if he really tried, he could probably find the time… but if he did, there’s no doubt the burden would fall on someone else to make up for it.

As he was thinking about this, Marina, Vihera, and Byune approached Rei.

“You know, usually in situations like this, isn’t it the man who worries about the woman?”

Marina said this with a slightly exasperated look on her face, but from Rei’s perspective, he didn’t feel there was any need for such behavior.

If the one who had come to pick a fight had been someone as strong as Relieu, things might have been a little different… but even from Rei’s perspective, this opponent didn’t look particularly strong.

In fact, they were the kind of opponents that even Byune alone could have defeated.

Given that, Rei’s honest impression was that if anyone should be worried, it would be the men who’d been attacked.

Of course, if he said that out loud, he’d undoubtedly get all sorts of flak, so he kept it to himself.

“Yeah. Looks like I should’ve been a little more worried.”

“It’s not just ‘a little’—shouldn’t you be worrying a lot more? …Well, never mind. Anyway, I’ve finished reporting to Walker, so I’m heading out. Do you still have something to do, Rei?”

Marina looked at Brasso and, after a moment’s thought, shook his head.

“No, if you’re done talking to Walker, then I’m done here. Brasso, see you later.”

“Hmm. Are you going Gamelion hunting too?”

“I don’t know. But I’d like to, sure.”

“I see. Well then, make sure you don’t end up like that idiot.”

Rei could easily guess who Brasso meant by “that idiot.”

However, he judged that if he mentioned it here, it would undoubtedly lead to all sorts of trouble later, so he simply nodded at Brasso.

“So, for now… shall we go to my place? Or should we stop by the Dusk Wheat inn?”

“Hmm… Dusk Wheat inn, I suppose. I’ve reserved a room, but I should probably let them know I’m coming back. After that, we’ll go to Marina’s place.”

With that brief exchange, their plans were set, and as Rei and the others were about to leave the guild...

“Rei, since you’ve gone to the trouble of coming to the guild, don’t tell me you’re going to leave without seeing me!”

A voice called out from behind them.

Rei, who had known the owner of that voice for quite some time, recognized it immediately.

“Sorry, Kenny. I’ve got a lot to take care of right now, so we’ll talk more another time.”

Saying that to the voice coming from the counter, Rei left the guild.

“Aaaaaah! I was supposed to have the day off today! If I had, I might have been able to go out to eat with you, Rei! You idiot, Lenora!”

“Calm down. It’s not like Lenora asked to be assigned work by the Guild Master. Besides, she’s busy right now with the Gilm’s expansion and the Gamelion matter, so there’s nothing we can do about it.”

Speaking to Kenny—who looked disappointed yet didn’t stop processing paperwork—wasn’t his usual partner, Lenora… but another receptionist.

Lenora isn’t at the guild today because she’s working on a job from Walker.

So, in her place, Kenny was filling in for Lenora.

If Lenora had been working as usual...

Thinking that, even though he understood it couldn’t be helped, it was only natural to feel a bit resentful toward Lenora.

That said, just as the receptionist had mentioned, Gilm was extremely busy right now.

In addition to the expansion project and Gamelion, as the receptionist mentioned, many merchants are rushing in because winter is just around the corner.

Gilm is already crowded enough as it is, and now even more people are coming.

It’s only natural that this would cause all sorts of problems.

Adventurers are sometimes called upon to handle these issues, and in some cases, the guild is asked to resolve them through formal requests.

The paperwork Kenny is currently handling involves a dispute between merchants, and the solution they’ve agreed upon is for each side to hire an adventurer from Gilm to settle the matter through a duel—though they’re not allowed to kill each other.

“Ugh… Even so, I really wanted to go out to eat with Rei… And I wanted to hear about the dungeon thing, too…”

Even as she grumbled, Kenny’s hands never stopped sorting through the paperwork.

This is probably proof of Kenny’s competence.

Naturally, the other receptionists are well aware of his competence. They know it, but… even so, the sight of him processing paperwork while grumbling has a somewhat peculiar air to it.

And it wasn’t just the receptionists watching Kenny like this.

The adventurers in the guild could naturally see Kenny like that too...

“Kenny-chan is such a great woman… Damn it, that Rei guy, even though he’s got nothing but beauties at his beck and call…”

“You’re absolutely right. I’m jealous. I’m envious. I’m bitter.”

“Hey, stop whining here. If you become as skilled as Rei, wouldn’t beauties like Kenny come flocking to you too?”

“...Hey, you. Do you even know how strong Rei is? Yeah, sure. If you were as strong as Rei, women would flock to you in all sorts of ways. But do you really think the kind of strength needed to earn a nickname is something you can just easily achieve?”

“You’re ridiculous. Are you giving up without even trying? So you’re just going to stand there, gazing at the powerful Rei from afar and complaining? If you’re going to say anything, at least say you’re going to beat her.”

“Guh…”

To be honest, even the girl who said it didn’t think she could actually beat Rei.

But even so, as a fellow party member, her honest feeling was that she wanted him to at least show that kind of spirit.

“Sigh… Being strong doesn’t necessarily mean women will like you, but an opponent who’s weak when it really counts is out of the question, right? Well, there are some women who want to protect a weak partner… but I’m definitely not one of them.”

“I’m not talking about you or anything. What I’m saying is…”

“Yeah, yeah, I know. You’re talking about Kenny. …But given Kenny’s personality, I don’t think he’d be into someone like you.”

At her companion’s words, the men all fell silent.
