Under the moonlit night sky, Rei flew through the air alongside Set.

The summery night sky lifted his spirits just a little, even as he pondered what he was about to do.

(People often say the summer sky is high, but seeing it like this, the summer night sky seems high too. It's a sea of stars.)

When Rei lived in Japan, it was in the countryside... right next to the mountains.

Given that, the air was naturally clearer than in the city, and looking up, the stars were indeed clearly visible.

...Though, having grown up in the countryside all his life, seeing stars was just a natural sight for Rei.

When did she realize how beautiful the night sky visible from her home was? Was it when she heard about it from relatives working in Tokyo... or when she saw the night view during her school trip?

Either way, the night sky Rei was seeing now was overwhelmingly more spectacular than anything he'd seen back in Japan.

"Grr?"

Set croaked, asking what was wrong as Rei gazed up at the night sky.

"Nothing, really. Just thinking about what's ahead."

In exchange for twenty spears, Rei had obtained various information from the rebels, including the location of the mine.

He had handed over more spears than originally planned, but they were spears stolen from bandits in the first place, not purchased with money.

Moreover, the quality of the spears wasn't bad, but it wasn't particularly good either.

They were just ordinary spears, really.

Still, they must have been incredibly grateful for them.

"Oh, I see it now."

Rei murmured, gazing at the mountain coming into view ahead.

Even to Rei's eyes, the mountain illuminated by moonlight was constructed in a way that made it difficult to recognize as a mine.

Lerdact knew this mine was extremely important within his territory, which was likely why it was concealed so well from the surrounding area.

Mines often featured well-built roads for transporting extracted ore, but as far as Rei could see from the air, no such roads were visible beneath the moonlight.

Of course, ordinary roads existed, but whether they were sufficient for transporting ore and such, even Rei couldn't help but scratch his head.

That said, for Rei riding the flying Set, this concealment was practically meaningless.

Lerdact certainly understood that there were those who could fly.

But those who fly were few and far between, like dragon knights or summoners.

Given that, it probably wasn't prioritized much from a cost-effectiveness standpoint.

"Well, thanks to that, we found you right away, so I can't really complain."

With a small smile, Rei gently stroked Set.

"Well then, shall we go, Set?"

"GRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a sharp growl and began descending toward the ground.

It was already past midnight.

Given that, it was only natural that no one was in the mine.

Rei, who had assumed there would be slaves or Lerdact's subordinates inside the mine, looked slightly taken aback.

"Well, there are guards, I suppose."

More accurately, they were less guards and more like lookouts or security personnel.

However, they weren't near the mine itself. Instead, there was a checkpoint—though calling it that might be a bit of an exaggeration—near the road leading to the mine.

"Even if they tried to stop us there, it's deep in the mountains. If someone wanted to get in, they could probably just walk right in. Oh, wait, maybe there are traps or something set up in the mountains?"

And if you set off a trap and cry out, the intruders would be discovered and captured.

That was probably how it worked, Rei concluded. Unfortunately, spotting Rei and Set flying through the sky—not the daytime sky, but the night sky—was beyond the guards' capabilities.

As a result, Rei arrived at the mine effortlessly, still riding on Set.

"Well, all that's left is to destroy this mine... but how should we go about it?"

Rei dismounted from Set's back and muttered as he looked at the mine entrance reinforced with timber.

"Grr?"

Seeing Rei like this, Set let out a small growl, as if asking, What's wrong?

"Nothing. Just thinking about how to destroy the mine. ...Ah, Set. Could you check if there's anyone inside the mine?"

"Gurrrrrrrrr!"

At Rei's words, Set happily growled and entered the mine.

It wasn't that he was happy to be going inside the mine; he was happy because Rei had asked him to do something.

Fortunately, the mine's entrances and passageways were built quite wide to transport large quantities of ore, allowing Set to enter without any particular trouble.

Watching Set happily disappear into the mine, Rei pondered how to render it unusable.

The quickest solution would undoubtedly be fire magic.

But with trees growing all around, there was a real risk of starting a wildfire if he wasn't careful.

Moreover, the mine entrance wasn't necessarily the only one in front of Rei.

If there were other passages connecting to the outside, a wildfire could spread the damage even further.

"In that case... I guess I'll have to find another way. And as for other ways to destroy the mine... well, the safest bet is probably this."

Muttering to himself, Rei looked at the Death Scythe he'd taken out of the Misty Ring.

There lay a large scythe capable of wielding various skills.

In a different sense from Set, it was a partner worthy of the name.

A weapon that allowed him to acquire skills by severing the magic stones of monsters.

One of the skills he had acquired this way was terrain manipulation.

Though difficult to use when first learned, now at Level 3, it allowed him to raise or lower the ground within a 50-meter radius by one meter.

Using it, Rei could handle a mine like the one currently before him with ease.

Rei was certain of it.

"Well, there are probably other ways too... but terrain manipulation is definitely the way to go."

As he muttered this, Set emerged from the mine.

"Set, was anyone inside the mine?"

"Grr."

Set shook his head slightly at Rei's question.

"I see. Then let's clear this mine out quickly. If we waste time here, they might notice us."

Though there were no people near the mine, making a commotion could still alert the guards patrolling nearby.

Set growled in agreement at Rei's words.

Lightly stroking Set, who had moved beside him, Rei focused his attention on the Death Scythe he held.

"Alright, let's go. Once we collapse this mine, we'll retreat to the airspace above. Then, after confirming nothing strange happens, we'll leave this place."

"Guruu!"

Set let out a spirited growl in response to Rei's words.

Fortunately for Rei, there were no villages or towns near the mountain where this mine was located.

The miners worked in a town located a fair distance from the mountain.

(From what the rebels said, they apparently use regular inns for lodging and don't seem to be short on food either. ...They've got their priorities straight on that front.)

Rei felt a slight change in his view of Lerdact, though he understood that the preferential treatment given to the miners wasn't out of any genuine concern for them.

Given that the mine's existence is already kept secret, bringing in new workers one after another would pose a significant risk to Reldact.

There was also the issue of wasted time until they learned the job.

At first glance, mining work seems simple: extracting ore and hauling it out.

But precisely because it's simple work, it's actually quite complex, and individual skill plays a major role.

If workers are treated as disposable, those possessing the necessary skills become expendable too, ultimately reducing Relduct's profits.

That's probably the reasoning behind it.

Honestly, Rei couldn't care less about that.

If destroying the mine caused no harm to the surrounding area, then that was fine by him.

(Well, the disappearance of the mine would mean the workers here lose their jobs, so it's not like there'd be absolutely no damage...)

Thinking this, he thrust the tip of the Death Scythe into the ground and activated his skill.

"Terrain Manipulation!"

With that word, the ground near the mine entrance rose about a meter.

Put into words, it really was just that simple phenomenon... but the mine entrance, reinforced with wood, couldn't withstand that intense shock.

The reinforced timber snapped, and the force caused the mine entrance to collapse.

The shock of the collapse spread throughout the entire mine, destroying the timber reinforcing the passageways and sending it crashing to the ground.

Once several trees snapped, a chain reaction triggered the mine's collapse.

Hearing the thunderous roar that would have woken anyone, even in sleep, Rei swiftly mounted Set's back and evacuated to the sky.

Even if a landslide occurred due to the mine collapse, being in the air guaranteed safety.

To be precise, at lower altitudes, there was still a chance of being struck by falling trees during a landslide or hit by rocks bouncing off the ground after rolling down from higher elevations.

But at an altitude of around thirty meters—not quite a hundred meters, but still—it was safe for this particular incident.

"...Well then."

Several minutes passed since the mine collapse, and as things began to settle, Rei once again turned his gaze toward the ground.

Fortunately, the mountain had barely collapsed, and there was no damage to the guards stationed at the base... keeping watch to prevent unauthorized entry into the mine.

Though, given the sudden collapse, they did appear to be running around in visible confusion, even from above.

For Rei, the only thought left was that it was good they hadn't died, at least for now.

The mine had collapsed suddenly while the guards were protecting it.

This would undoubtedly become a matter of responsibility...

"Soon enough, we won't have to worry about that sort of thing anymore."

"Guru?"

Set growled at Rei's mutter, as if asking, Still here?

The sight of the mine collapsing and the mountain crumbling in a chain reaction was rare even for Set.

But even so, staring at it forever would eventually get boring.

Stroking Set's fur, Rei spoke.

"Yeah. Before this reaches Lerdact, let's handle what we need to do next over here. They're probably waiting for us over there too."

"Gururu!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a happy growl and immediately flapped his wings, moving away from their current spot.

"For now, the request from Viscount Gale is settled. That means the real thing is finally next. ...Ideally, I'd like to get it done within a few days... Think you can handle it?"

"GRRRRRRRR!"

Set's attitude clearly said, I'm still full of energy!

Seeing Set like this, Rei smiled and stroked the back of his neck.

"Alright, let's go while you're still full of energy."

As Rei spoke, Set turned his back on the collapsed mine and took flight.

Shortly after departing the mine, they arrived at the place where Rei and the rebels had clashed... or more accurately, the place where the rebels had been crushed by Rei and Set.

Anyway, they arrived at that location.

"Whoa, you're fast. Huh? You're back already? Does that mean you've already destroyed the mine?"

The rebel members were startled to see Rei and Set. The leader-like man—the one Ray had blown away first during the rampage—asked this, his eyes wide.

Several rebel members had been training with the spears Rei provided, but they too halted their movements upon seeing Rei and Set return.

"Yeah. Well, it wasn't exactly a huge mine to begin with. ...Probably to keep the info from leaking to the neighboring Viscount Gale's territory."

"Just asking, but were any of the workers hurt..."

"Of course not. They weren't working at that hour. And there were no guards near the mine either... So no one should have died from the collapse itself. Though, if something was moving around in the mountains, who knows?"

At Rei's words confirming no casualties, everyone listening breathed a sigh of relief.

"So, what about attacking Count Lerdact's mansion?"

Rei asked the relieved rebels.
