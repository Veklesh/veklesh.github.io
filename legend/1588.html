"Hey, Rei! The plan was to capture the commander unharmed, right? Why'd you suddenly blow off his right hand?!"

"He can still talk, so it's fine, right?"

Emerging from the forest, Rei and Ryutas walked while arguing like that.

...They deliberately moved at a slow pace, choosing not to run.

Naturally, such behavior drew the attention of Rajalas and those nearby to their presence.

To be precise, including Yellow perched on Rei's left shoulder and Byune following behind them, it would be more accurate to call them three people and one creature.

The men normally assigned to guard Ryutas were nowhere to be seen.

They had been sent to the cave where Vihera was to explain the situation.

Naturally, the guards resisted leaving Ryutas's side. However, Ryutas's words, "There is no place safer than by Rei's side," contained absolutely no element they could deny.

Indeed, having witnessed Rei's power firsthand, the guards understood that being by Rei's side meant safety, even if it meant being in the thick of battle.

Upon hearing this, Rei tried to say something... but ultimately just sighed. Without another word, he decided to head with Ryutas to the location the soldiers had revealed.

That is, the command post outside the forest.

Though, he hadn't expected them to have such a beautiful, open tent set up so brazenly. At first, he even suspected it might be a trap.

Then, in a moment of madness, he spotted a pompous-looking guy wielding a longsword. Assuming the man was the big shot, he threw a disposable spear at him.

...The reason it was a disposable spear and not the Twilight Spear was largely because I didn't want that noble's blood staining the Twilight Spear.

Anyway, that's how Rei and Ryutasu ended up leaving the forest and heading towards the canopy.

Naturally, in such a situation, the escort soldiers would immediately prepare to intercept them...

(They're slow.)

Suspicious individuals... no, since someone who appeared to be a high-ranking official had been attacked, it wouldn't be strange for Rei and Ryutas to be recognized as enemies.

But the guards' response was clearly delayed.

At the very least, soldiers from the Kingdom of Mireana would likely face reprimands from their superiors for such sluggishness.

(In a small country like Reburina, it might be unavoidable, though.)

After all, they rarely even get the chance to fight.

No matter how much training they had, lacking real combat experience, or having only shallow experience, was fatal.

...Though, the soldiers had their own reasons.

They'd been made to stand outside their tents all day under the blazing summer sun, under the most intense direct sunlight imaginable.

To make matters worse, the target they were supposed to protect was Rajaras, a classic useless noble.

Their stamina was drained by the heat, and their motivation was severely sapped by Rajaras's words and actions.

Expecting them to move swiftly under such conditions was unreasonable.

But still, as soldiers assigned to guard him, protecting Rajalas was their duty.

Even if that meant protecting Rajalas—the very same Rajalas whose right shoulder had been pierced by something—more precisely, by a spear thrown by Rei—and whose right arm had been blown off somewhere by the impact.

"Who are you?! Approach any closer and you'll be considered an enemy!"

"...So if I don't get any closer, I'm not the enemy?"

Rei, suddenly struck by a question at the guard soldier's threatening words, asked that.

The speaker, seemingly caught off guard by such a response, halted his movements.

He'd assumed his words would be ignored and an attack would follow.

But when Rei actually stopped right there, he was left wondering what to do.

Honestly, with Rajalas having suffered the severe injury of losing his right hand, it was a real problem if he truly stopped here.

"W-well..."

He stumbled over his words.

He didn't know what to say next.

If he could somehow steer the conversation toward friendship here, perhaps it wouldn't come to a fight to the death.

The soldier who had been talking with Rei suddenly thought that.

That thought wasn't entirely wrong.

No, rather, considering the damage, it could even be called the best option.

At least, that was the best option for the soldier.

However, it was only the best option for the soldier.

For others... for the nobles present, it was commoners who had harmed the nobility.

No noble present could tolerate that.

"This commoner!"

The first to lunge at Rei was one of Rajalas's entourage.

In his hand was a rapier, likely drawn from its scabbard at his waist.

But the thrust from that nobleman, who was plump though not quite as much as Rajalas, possessed a sharpness easily avoidable not just by Rei... but even by lower-ranked adventurers in Gilm.

Indeed, Rei avoided the thrust with ease.

The attack came from someone who appeared to be a complete novice at combat, suddenly appearing out of nowhere.

For a moment, he hesitated, unsure how to react.

This was an opponent whose strength suggested that killing him would be easier than breathing.

That Rei hesitated even slightly before such an existence was likely largely due to the unexpectedness of the action.

Frankly, even after witnessing Rajalas's right shoulder pierced and his right arm blown off, Rei couldn't fathom why this noble remained so utterly confident.

But, misinterpreting Rei's confusion somehow, the nobleman, holding his rapier, flashed a confident... no, an overconfident smile and spoke.

"Ha! To dodge my rapier? For a commoner, you're quite skilled. But... don't think you can keep evading my thrust forever! The one called Lightning!"

With those words, the noble thrust his rapier toward Rei once more.

But Rei froze for an instant upon seeing the thrust.

It wasn't because the thrust was as lightning-fast as Rei claimed...

"Lightning?"

He muttered, swinging Death Scythe.

The next instant, the man's right arm flew through the air, still clutching the rapier.

At the tip of that spinning right arm, naturally, the man's form was absent.

This meant that the man's right shoulder and everything below it had been severed by the Death Scythe.

"You call this lightning at this speed? At least reach the speed of my strike before calling yourself lightning."

"Eh... What...?"

Swiftly, and ironically at the very speed the man had claimed was lightning, the Death Scythe's strike was so sharp that the man didn't even register the pain of his own arm being severed.

However, Rei's muttered words... and above all, seeing his own right arm fall to the ground, forced him to understand his situation.

And once he grasped his situation, the pain of his severed arm naturally... and violently surged up.

"Ah... ah... AAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

Screaming, the nobleman clutched his right shoulder with his left arm.

But what should have been there was nowhere to be found.

Of course not. His right arm, which should have been there, was lying on the ground.

"It hurts, it hurts, it hurts... AAAAAAAAH! My arm, my arm... my right arm... AAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

A wail that could only be called despair poured from the nobleman's mouth.

But that despair was only natural.

The Kingdom of Reburina was a small country among the vassal states of the Kingdom of Mireana.

Being such a minor state, it was difficult enough to procure a potion capable of reattaching a severed right arm, let alone a magic item prosthetic arm indistinguishable from the real thing.

Well, perhaps a high-ranking noble within Reburina might manage it somehow, but unfortunately, the only ones present were a viscount who ranked near the bottom among nobles and his entourage.

To ask if such people could obtain an intricate magic item prosthetic arm... it was practically impossible.

"Damn it! Do you think it's acceptable to do this to me, a noble?!"

The man screamed, clutching his right shoulder with his left hand.

Tears streamed from his eyes, snot dripped from his nose, and drool hung from his mouth... The sight of him screaming while expelling fluids from every orifice on his face was utterly unconvincing as that of a noble.

"Well, I wonder. Did you really think attacking me would go unpunished? ...That feeble jab of yours? It was more like a worm wriggling than a lightning bolt."

Taking a potion from the guard soldier and applying it to his right shoulder, the noble glared at Rei.

The potion was likely fairly potent, being one carried by the soldiers stationed here as his guards.

While it couldn't reattach a severed arm, it seemed to have the effect of stopping the bleeding and alleviating the pain somewhat.

The nobleman, described as wriggling like an earthworm, flushed crimson and tried to say something.

But Rei, finding it ridiculous to entertain such a noble, pointed the tip of the Twilight Spear held in his right hand at him. The nobleman, perhaps remembering the excruciating pain he had just felt for the first time in his life, said no more.

"Now then, I need you lot to spill some information."

Rei's gaze fell not on the two nobles who had lost their right arms, but on the other nobles, who were still whole.

Faced with Rei, who showed no regard for their noble status and had severed their right arms as casually as cutting weeds, they turned ashen-faced, unable to utter a word.

They were nobles.

They believed commoners, mere dust and dirt, could never harm them.

That was their belief, and indeed, until today, they had lived their lives convinced of it. To these men, the figure of Rei standing before them was nothing but an utterly unsettling presence.

The nobles and their guard soldiers, unable to utter a word.

Watching them, Rei spoke.

"The Kingdom of Reburina and Jaya should be working together. Then why did this happen?"

"Ha! Ha! Ha! Ha! Don't be ridiculous! Jaya is a criminal organization! Why would the Kingdom of Reburina need to ally with such a group!"

One of Rajaras's entourage shouted in response to Rei's words.

He felt intense fear toward Rei... and a sense of uncertainty about what Rei might do. Yet, as a noble of the Kingdom of Reburina, he couldn't simply accept Rei's words.

"...Hmm."

Rei murmured softly at the nobleman's words.

At that murmur, the member of Rajalas' entourage who had just hurled accusations at Rei flinched in sudden fear.

Was he afraid he might be attacked too? That was the fear, but Rei hadn't actually voiced any irritation.

(I understand some nobles don't know about the connection between the upper echelons of the country and Jaya. But... if you're going to attack Jaya, wouldn't it be normal to send someone who knows the situation? Or did you think that precisely because they don't know the situation, they could command a campaign of annihilation?)

If someone connected to Jaya were given orders to annihilate, they might show some restraint for whatever reason.

But someone completely unaware of the relationship between the Kingdom of Reburina and Jaya would have no reason to hold back.

Rei could understand the possibility that someone who thought they'd severed ties with Jaya made that judgment call.

Still, their mutual relationship was practically an open secret.

So someone unaware of it... well, perhaps only someone like Rajaras could have been prepared.

(In that case, it seems more likely the operation would fail due to the incompetence of the person commanding the unit.)

After all, they were currently cornering Rajalas and his men... Holding the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, Rei turned his gaze toward those before him.

When met with that gaze again, even the man who had told Rei not to mess around averted his eyes.

He might have been able to yell at Rei while riding that wave of momentum, but once that momentum faded, he found himself unable to say anything.

The guarding soldiers, too, judged that speaking up could bring about the worst possible outcome and remained silent.

Soldiers returning from the forest, who might have been expected to make a scene upon seeing Rei, were knocked unconscious by the guards to prevent any disturbance.

"Well... what should we do with these guys? Kill them all?"

Rei turned to Ryutas and asked him that.
