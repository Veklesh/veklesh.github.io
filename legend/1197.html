Under a clear blue sky, Rei was flying through the air on Set as usual.

As always, Vihera was hanging onto Set's legs, while a green carpet spread out below them on the ground.

Flying through this scene, Rei guided Set according to the magic item in his hand.

"Set, a little more to the right."

"Grr."

At Rei's command, Set flapped his wings and altered course.

"Hey, Rei. How many more Ambrith do you think are left?"

Hanging from Set's front legs, Vihera asked. Rei shrugged his shoulders slightly before answering.

"Hard to say. The first Ambris we took down, plus the ones Mireine and the others found. If you spot one, you can assume there are at least thirty more... though I hope it's not quite that bad."

Recalling the black bugs he'd seen several times back in Japan, Rei replied to Vihera.

(Come to think of it, why is it that I can touch beetles and stag beetles just fine, but cockroaches are the only ones that make me feel disgusted?)

Pondering such trivial things, he suppressed a yawn while gazing up at the blue, boundlessly high sky.

The detector to find Ambris was finished, so Rei and Vihera were woken up early in the morning... or rather, at a time that could still be called the middle of the night. But since it was too late to sleep properly after that, they ate breakfast and spent the time doing light combat training.

Vihera became so engrossed in his fight with Rei that it nearly turned into a serious battle... but fortunately, Vihera regained his composure just in time.

For Rei's two-spear style training, Vihera was a good opponent. Vihera had to hold back, looking a bit dissatisfied, but it was still a fulfilling training session.

As they concentrated on this training, the other lodgers began waking up. Some of them saw the training—or rather, the fight—taking place in the backyard and were startled awake by the commotion.

After finishing the morning training, they ate breakfast in the dining hall... and now, together with Set, they were using Ambris's detector to search for its form.

Naturally, they found several groups of goblin leaders today as well, incinerating them all at once with fire magic from above.

Even moving at Set's speed, we couldn't detect Ambrith's presence. It was likely due to Ambrith being a black mist.

If it were flying, it would be easier to spot, but if it's moving underground, it's impossible to find.

Even if it wasn't underground, if it was crawling along the surface, it would be difficult to spot, hidden by the grass.

While flying, I've reached locations where I've detected Ambrith's presence before, but I haven't been able to find him beyond that point.

Even now, I was scanning the ground and sky searching for Ambrith's form... but I ended up finding something else.

"Ah, Rei. That over there isn't an orc."

"What?"

At Vihera's words, Rei frantically scanned the surroundings.

Even if she said "there," Vihera was clinging to Set's front leg.

Naturally, Rei couldn't see what Vihera was looking at.

Still, looking around, he could spot the group of orcs Vihera had mentioned.

A group of about twenty orcs, seemingly led by an orc leader.

He couldn't tell if that number was large or small for a group led by an Orc Leader.

But for Rei, orcs were more than just a rank—they were delicious meat.

Regardless of what a normal pack size might be, he might complain if there were too few orcs, but he never complained if there were too many.

(Well, if he had to complain, he'd have preferred a higher-rank orc to show up.)

Orc meat is tastier than other monsters of the same rank, but it's not exactly top-tier.

If anything, it's the kind of meat you'd eat regularly, but it's considered top-tier among those.

Of course, it's still fairly rare for ordinary adventurers—due to preservation issues—but

For Rei, though, having the Misty Ring meant it wasn't an issue.

Anyway, the direction the group of orcs moving across the surface was heading towards was, naturally, Gilm.

The goblins were marching toward Gilm without a second thought, but the orcs knew the danger of antagonizing humans.

Yet they were heading for Gilm just like the goblins. Perhaps it was the personality of their orc leader or something.

"Well, since they're heading for Gilm, we can't just let them pass, can we?"

"...Doesn't sound like you're trying to attack very reluctantly, though?"

Rei murmured, recognizing the hint of joy in Rei's voice, but Rei said nothing in response.

After all, it was undeniable that he was delighted to encounter the orc horde.

For Rei and Set, orc meat was their everyday food.

Of course, they ate other monsters' meat too, and when in Gilm, they often ate dishes sold in shops or meals at cafeterias.

But even so, they still ate orc meat frequently in the wild... and the supply they'd obtained from the orc settlement was getting pretty low.

In that sense, finding orcs here was a stroke of luck for Rei.

"Vihera, sorry, but I'm going ahead. You and Set make sure none of the orcs escape."

"Even so, just the two of us... No, to be precise, since Rei charges in first, just me and Set alone can't possibly stop them all from escaping, you know?"

"Don't worry. Set, if it comes down to it, you can use the King's Intimidation. That should prevent them from escaping... or at least slow them down if they can move."

"...Then wouldn't it be quicker to just use that 'King's Intimidation' skill from the start?"

Rei nodded at Vihera's obvious question... then realized Vihera couldn't see Rei and spoke up.

"True. If we were just defeating them normally, using the King's Intimidation might be sufficient... But using the King's Intimidation makes the opponent freeze. That's similar to feeling tense. If that happens, the meat's flavor suffers a bit."

Back in Japan, Rei had seen something like that on a TV program.

What he saw then was the method used to kill animals.

Meat from an animal that died relaxed... or died without realizing it, versus an animal that knew it was being killed, struggled violently, and was then killed.

While both are killed, the state at death makes the meat from the same animal taste completely different.

Naturally, meat from animals killed while relaxed is overwhelmingly tastier, while meat from animals killed while struggling or tense becomes less palatable.

Of course, this is a general rule, and there are some species whose meat tastes better when killed while struggling.

In any case, orcs naturally fall into the former category.

Perhaps because it's monster meat, killing them while tense doesn't make it terribly bad, but the flavor does suffer nonetheless.

That's precisely why I wanted to take it down without resorting to using the King's Intimidation right off the bat.

"Well then, that's settled... I'm counting on you."

Having reached directly above the orc, Rei announced this and leapt down from Set's back.

As he passed right beside Vihera, he caught a somewhat exasperated glance... but to Rei, Vihera, who relished fighting strong opponents, was pretty eccentric himself.

Compared to someone like Vihera, prioritizing his appetite didn't seem so strange.

In midair, he drew Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring, then used Sleipnir's Boots to kick off and slow his descent.

Using the recoil from his final kick, he plummeted toward the Orc Leader—a higher-ranking species within the horde whose flesh tasted superior to other orcs.

He swung the Death Scythe, and the Orc Leader's body split apart, his entrails and blood spilling onto the ground.

The orc leader likely died without even knowing when it happened.

Rei landed on the ground, and the orc leader lay severed in two halves.

Perhaps taken aback by this unexpected sight, the orc horde froze momentarily.

"Flying Slash!"

Flying Slash struck the frozen orcs, severing the heads of several... Simultaneously, Twilight Spear shattered the skull of another.

Several more orcs fell to Rei's attacks, and only then did the orcs realize they were under attack.

"Buhihihihi!"

An orc near Rei let out such a sound as it swung down the club it held in its hand.

Had it continued, it would have struck Rei's head, but of course Rei wasn't about to take such an attack lying down.

"You're too naive!"

He swung the Death Scythe, channeling magic into it, toward the club swung at him.

He didn't deflect it with the Death Scythe's weight... instead, its blade sliced diagonally through the club.

"Buh!?"

Had the club's blow been blocked or dodged, the orc wouldn't have been so thrown.

But for the club to be... cut in two? Not some makeshift branch broken off a tree like the goblins used, but a proper club prepared as a weapon? That was completely unexpected, even for the orc.

The orc let out a cry of shock, but the next instant, Twilight Spear slammed into its head, severing most of it, and it collapsed to the ground.

Watching the fallen orc, Rei swung Death Scythe toward another orc.

"Multi-Slash!"

The one struck was the orc wielding the longsword.

It should have been able to block Death Scythe's strike.

But the blade was cleanly severed by the magic-infused strike of the Death Scythe. Simultaneously, parts of the orc's body that the blade hadn't even touched were sliced apart.

It was an attack using the skill Multi-Strike, allowing multiple attacks in a single strike. But for the orcs, unaware of the skill, they likely didn't even understand what had happened.

"Next!"

The orcs fell into chaos at Rei's shout.

If this had been an opponent they could still handle themselves, they might have tried to defeat Rei.

But seeing their leader killed before their eyes, and then watching their comrades fall one after another,

The orcs didn't have the courage to stand up to Rei in such a situation.

In this situation, there was only one thing they could do. Desperately put distance between themselves and Rei... and flee as far away as possible.

The orcs, deciding to make a run for it, charged forward—but Rei had already prepared for this.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

Set's roar echoed throughout the area.

Released while wielding the King's intimidation, that roar unquestionably halted the orcs' movements.

Some orcs managed to move, but their actions were extremely sluggish.

Set swiftly dispatched these orcs one after another with swings of his forelegs.

...He targeted only their heads, avoiding any damage to their bodies. Perhaps destroying the torso meant less edible meat.

While Vihera primarily relied on hand-to-hand combat, he couldn't match Set's ability to crush the very spot his fists struck.

However, Vihera possessed gauntlets that generated magic claws and greaves that produced magic blades.

And above all, it possessed the skill, Immersion Claw, which dealt internal damage completely unaffected by any defense built from muscle or fat.

Using these, there was no way mere orcs could stop Vihera.

One after another, the orcs breathed their last.

Of course, Rei wasn't just watching this battle unfold. He swung his Death Scythe and Twilight Spear at the orcs who still refused to flee, even in this situation.

In the end, the orc horde was wiped out in less than five minutes. Only three remained standing: Rei, Vihera, and Set.

"...Not very satisfying,"

Vihera muttered discontentedly, though her expression didn't quite match her words—perhaps because she'd judged the orcs weak from the start.

"Well, I get why you'd want stronger foes, Vihera, but that'll have to wait for another time."

While stowing away the orc corpses... especially smiling at having secured the orc leader's body and magic stone, Rei told Vihera.

"I know. Honestly, I wish a Cyclops leader would show up. Or maybe an Ogre?"

It probably wasn't triggered by those words, but in the next instant, Rei noticed adventurers running towards their location.

Warriors, thieves, mages.

The three figures in such attire were familiar to Rei.

"Arogan, Culotte, and Scola?"

"Rei! You're here just in time! Over there... there's an Ambris! And it's huge—nothing like the guild description at all!"

Arogan shouted that upon seeing Rei.
