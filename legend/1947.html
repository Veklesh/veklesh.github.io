"Hmm... I thought there might be something, but there's nothing in particular."

As he walked through the mansion, Rei muttered to himself.

He had been certain that people with strength comparable to that of the middle-aged man from earlier would attack him, so the fact that no one was attacking him at all was completely unexpected.

"Could it be that there’s no one else in the mansion? ...No, it’s a base, so that can’t be right. ...Oh."

Spotting a door ahead, Rei quickened his pace slightly.

Although the mansion was reasonably spacious, it wasn’t any larger than the mansions found in the aristocratic district.

…Though, of course, there are exceptions, like Marina’s house.

For that reason, there weren’t that many rooms, and Rei thought that might mean he could find some kind of clue without taking too much time.

When he reached the door, he gently reached out his hand... but stopped himself before touching the doorknob.

(There aren’t any traps, are there? …It’s a door inside our base, so I can’t imagine they’d set a trap there… It would be handy to have Byune around at times like this.)

Even as he thought that, he decided there probably wasn’t a trap set, and gently reached for the doorknob.

He did so while staying ready to react immediately if anything happened, but fortunately, no needles shot out of the doorknob, nor did an electric shock run through him the moment he touched it; the door opened normally.

Relieved by this, I enter the room… but not before checking for anything suspicious.

Just like with the doorknob, the reason I was being so cautious this time was simply because I believed the person behind the Kobold incident was undoubtedly extremely cautious by nature.

Otherwise, it would be impossible for there to be absolutely no clues at all.

To be precise, there had been a few clues, but they had run out before I could reach the mastermind.

Given how cautious this opponent is, if this is a real base—not a disposable one like the one Grange knew about—it wouldn’t be surprising if some kind of trap were set.

Fortunately, there were no traps this time, so Rei entered the room.

“A study…? Another hassle.”

There was a desk, and small bookshelves lined the walls.

Books are generally expensive items, so even just the ones here would be worth a small fortune… well, maybe not quite that much, but certainly a significant sum.

(At the very least, whoever is using this hideout isn’t short on cash. …Well, that much is obvious just from the fact that they’ve set up a hideout like this.)

Whether they’re renting this mansion, have purchased it… or are using it without permission is something even Rei doesn’t know.

But if they’re using a mansion in a location like this, there’s no doubt that various necessary expenses are involved.

If they were only staying for a very short time, that would be one thing, but if they’re planning to stay for a reasonably long period, those expenses are a given.

……That said, it’s not like I’m envious of them or anything.

(I suppose a place like this might be necessary if you want to display magic items and enjoy them… but for now, I don’t need a spacious mansion like this.)

Looking at the desk, Rei thought this to himself and began searching for anything that might serve as a clue.

However, the desk was neatly organized, and there were no obvious clues—such as documents—to be found anywhere.

Thinking there might be something in the desk drawers, he checked them and found a few documents, but none of them looked particularly important.

“Given the location, there’s no doubt this study is used by the master of the mansion, but the fact that there’s no evidence of that whatsoever… what’s up with that? Well, I suppose it might be because, even if this is the enemy’s base, it’s not their main headquarters.”

Rei hadn’t expected to find the magic items he was looking for so quickly.

Still, he had expected there to be at least some kind of clue leading to a magic item, so this felt like a complete letdown.

...Though the idea that there are magic items here is really just wishful thinking on Rei’s part.

“Even if it’s not a magic item, I’d think there’d be something else that looks the part. …What do you think?”

“Huh!?”

At Rei’s words, the figure outside the room realized someone was speaking to them and gasped.

But, as if he’d made up his mind immediately, he kicked the door open and charged inside, pinpointing Rei’s location in less than a few seconds and rushing toward her.

The fact that it took a few seconds to confirm Rei’s presence is likely why that person could still be considered inexperienced.

For someone of a certain caliber—like that middle-aged man who ambushed the guard at the entrance only to be effortlessly defeated by Rei—the time it takes to enter the room and spot Rei would be a mere instant.

A few seconds versus an instant.

Objectively speaking, there doesn’t seem to be much difference between the two, and that is a fact.

However, in the context of combat, that difference becomes infinitely significant.

And indeed, in this very battle, that tiny difference led to a fatal outcome.

Rei, who was fully prepared and waiting for the charging opponent, delivered a blow that easily sank into the man’s solar plexus, knocking him unconscious on the spot.

However, that single blow couldn’t possibly stop the momentum of his run, and the opponent crashed spectacularly into the desk with the same force he’d had when he hit the floor.

“If he attacked me, does that mean the others are under attack too? But for that to be the case…”

Rei focused his hearing, but he couldn’t hear any sounds of battle.

Just because Rei’s hearing was better than average didn’t mean it was as sharp as Set’s.

If they were fighting loudly, he would be able to hear it.

But if the fight began quietly and ended quietly—for example, if the guards were taken down in a surprise attack—he wouldn’t be able to pick up the sound of them hitting the floor.

As a countermeasure, the guards operate in teams of two or three, but even that doesn’t guarantee absolute safety.

If this were an ordinary criminal, it wouldn’t be a problem…

(For now, since I don’t hear any commotion, I’ll assume they weren’t attacked. If that’s the case, then why did this guy attack me…?)

I scan the study, looking for anything that might explain why.

Perhaps he attacked me precisely because there’s something in this room he doesn’t want me to see.

That’s what I thought, but this study is actually quite spacious.

It doesn’t feel that spacious, though, precisely because there are so many bookshelves.

“Is there something in the books?”

Rei muttered.

Even in the manga, anime, and novels he’d seen back in Japan, it was common for bookshelves to have some kind of hidden mechanism.

Rei remembers things like hidden doors opening when specific books were taken in a certain order.

But considering the number of bookshelves and books in this room, pulling off something like that wouldn’t be easy.

Besides, there wasn’t enough time to pull something like that off anyway, so if there really was a mechanism in the bookshelf, he’d have no choice but to leave it to the guards.

“In that case…”

He immediately gave up on searching the bookshelf and looked around the room again.

However, if the attacker notices him, there’s no denying the possibility that they’ll strike again immediately.

To prevent that, I used the rope I’d taken out of my Misty Ring to tie them up so they couldn’t move.

...Though, since I’m not a professional like the guards, there’s a chance a professional might be able to break free.

In any case, after tying up the attacker’s arms and legs, I looked around the room once more.

“Hmm… how about this?”

Something caught my eye: an ornament sitting on the desk.

A small wooden statue carved from some kind of wood.

Rei picked up the palm-sized wooden statue, but naturally, nothing happened just from holding it.

“Maybe I have to place it somewhere specific? No, but would someone really go out of their way to leave a wooden statue on the desk just to use it as a key?”

Coming to that conclusion, he placed the figurine back on the desk.

“I’m starting to think it would be faster to ask this guy directly. …Though whether he’d answer honestly is another matter.”

At the very least, the middle-aged man he’d just fought showed no sign of cooperating when Rei and the guards tried to extract information from him.

This man who attacked us is undoubtedly less skilled than that middle-aged man, but there’s more than enough reason to believe he’s tight-lipped.

(Well, I suppose I should leave the interrogation to the guards who specialize in that sort of thing.)

Thinking that, he looked around the study again.

Rei wasn’t a professional like the guards, nor was he a thief—though in a different sense, he could be considered a professional in his own right.

Precisely because of that, he didn’t know the standard procedure for how or where to start searching the study, which is why Rei decided to rely on his instincts rather than any set rules as he scanned the room.

Is there any spot that feels off?

With that in mind, he scanned the room… and suddenly, he stopped.

His gaze fell upon a painting.

Rei knew next to nothing about art, but even he could tell that this painting was well done.

It wasn’t a scene of Gilm, but a painting of a sandy beach and the sea.

At first glance, it looked like a well-executed painting, nothing more, but still, Rei felt something was off about it.

(What is it? What’s bothering me?)

Thinking this, he decided that he wouldn’t know the reason unless he examined the painting, so he quietly approached the wall.

Standing before the painting, Rei finally realized what had been bothering him.

“This… the sense of distance is off?”

Just as he muttered, the wall where the painting hung seemed a step or two farther away than it had from the center of the study where Rei had been standing moments before.

The distance wasn’t that great, but even so, a step or two meant a difference of nearly a meter.

If that’s the case, she couldn’t help but wonder why that was happening.

(Is the painting itself a magic item? Ah, but I remember seeing something on TV back in Japan about painting techniques that use optical illusions to create that kind of effect. Is that it?)

It’s what’s commonly known as an optical illusion or trick art.

It’s not a magic item, but a painting created purely through skill.

Impressed that it might be something like that, I looked around with that in mind...

"Hm?"

Suddenly, I noticed something in my line of sight.

It was a wall different from the one where the painting hung.

On that wall, there was something that looked like a switch—something I hadn’t seen until just now.

It wasn’t particularly conspicuous; in fact, it blended right into the color of the wall.

It was like camouflage—something that probably wouldn’t have been spotted if not for Rei’s eyes, which were focused on the painting.

“What on earth… what were they thinking, doing something like this? Well, since it was hidden so cleverly, I suppose they didn’t want it to be found.”

Muttering to himself, he approached the wall where the switch was located.

“Yeah, it’s definitely a switch.”

It’s the standard push-button type.

Since it was hidden using a form of camouflage, it makes perfect sense that they would choose a shape that’s easy to conceal.

For example, if it were a lever-type switch, even if you tried to hide it by matching the wall color like this switch, it would be easy to spot.

“...So, the biggest problem is this: whether or not it’s okay to press this switch... I wonder. I think it’ll probably be fine, though.”

It’s not a switch that’s conspicuously placed in a spot like this; it’s hidden.

It’s highly likely that pressing it will activate a mechanism to open a secret passage.

But at the same time, if you ask whether an opponent this cautious would go so far as to set up a switch leading to a secret passage—even if they’d hidden it with camouflage—Rei couldn’t bring himself to nod in agreement.

Someone who carelessly presses the switch might be attacked.

Or perhaps pressing it might reveal the mansion’s secret.

Since both scenarios seemed plausible, he hesitated over what to do… and in the end, Rei decided to play it safe and call a professional security guard.
