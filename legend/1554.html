"Shut up! You're being noisy! ...Who's there?"

Elena's angry voice echoed through the room, and just seconds later, that cry could be heard.

The thick stench of blood and, above all, the utterly unexpected sight meant he hadn't realized anyone else was in the room besides the pregnant woman, the giant's child, and the corpse of the pregnant woman devoured by the giant.

The scene unfolding before him was that shocking, but regardless, Rei turned his gaze toward the source of the voice.

There, at the end of his gaze, stood a man who appeared to be in his thirties.

However, he possessed a massive, muscular physique that was anything but that of a doctor or researcher.

The moment he saw the man, Rei finally managed to shift his focus from the abnormal scene before him to the man, calming his agitated mind.

"Who the hell are you?"

"Huh? You're with Jaya and you don't know me? ...Wait, hold on. You're not actually from Jaya, are you? What was it... I heard a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname, one who keeps numerous women in attendance, was coming..."

"Lord Rezell, it's Rei. Rei of Crimson."

A figure beside the man, presumably one of his entourage, whispered to the man called Rezell.

At those words, the man in front of him seemed to recall the name Rei.

"So, what's Rei doing in a place like this?"

"Why am I here? That's obvious, isn't it? Some idiot messed with Gilm, so this is payback."

"...Huh. Never expected you'd state your purpose so plainly. Is that really wise?"

Rei's words drew an amused smile from Rezel.

However, while he genuinely seemed amused, his short-cropped hair and stern face made that smile look like nothing less than that of a ferocious carnivore.

An ordinary person... someone who wasn't an adventurer or anything, just a regular person... seeing that smile alone would be enough to paralyze them.

But the one standing before Rezell now was Rei.

And behind Rei stood a group of individuals, each possessing strength worthy of being called a one-man army, a force to be reckoned with.

Given that, Rei shows no sign of being unsettled by the man's smile... On the contrary, he even smiles himself as he speaks.

"It's not a matter of whether it's good or not. This action was formally requested. Rather, to show everyone what happens to those who lay a hand on Gilm, we need to spread the rumor far and wide. Besides..."

The room where Rei and the others currently stood was remarkably spacious... so spacious that if Set were here, he could easily fly around and wreak havoc within it. Given that, even Death Scythe, Rei's partner who rivals Set, could be swung around with ease.

"...Speaking of which, I heard Crimson Rei carries an item box... I see, so it's true. So, what do you plan to do by pulling something like that out here?"

"Well, how about splitting you two cleanly in two, right around the torso? Or perhaps dividing your bodies into left and right halves? How does that sound?"

Sensing the killing intent emanating from Rei, Rezell's face twitched.

But perhaps it was impressive that Resel got off so lightly.

After all, every other man beside Rezell had lost consciousness and collapsed to the floor.

"You radiate some seriously intense killing intent, huh? So you're the real deal, just like the rumors say. ...Impressive."

"Getting scared by a killing intent this weak? Well, I suppose you've made up your mind now?"

"Sorry, but that's not going to happen."

The moment he said that, Rezell pursed his lips as if whistling.

But no sound came from his mouth...

"Wait, what? A sound?"

"It's a sound."

"I hear a sound."

"Whistling?"

"...Huh?"

No, the only one who hadn't heard the sound was Byune. Everyone else had heard the sound emitted from Rezell's mouth.

At that exchange, Rezell—who had maintained a composed expression until now—froze for an instant.

He probably never imagined that the whistle he produced using a special method would be audible.

...For Rezell, the biggest miscalculation was that Rei and the others were, in many ways, beings beyond human.

Rei's body, created by the Zepairu clan pooling their technology; Elena, who inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone; Marina, the Dark Elf known as the World Tree Priestess; and Vihera, who absorbed Ambris.

Only Byune, being an ordinary human, couldn't hear Resel's whistle. But that didn't mean Resel was saved.

The biggest problem was undoubtedly that someone had heard the whistle just now.

"Now then, what exactly was that whistle just now? I'd like to hear the details about that. Well, I can pretty much guess, but still."

Shouldering his Death Scythe, Rei asked Rezell.

As he said, it wasn't difficult to guess what the whistle had been.

Considering the very purpose of this underground facility's existence, it became clear almost immediately.

And considering how surprised they seemed earlier when they realized we could hear them... probably... no, definitely...

"You summoned the giant, huh?"

As he asked the question, Rei voiced the answer before waiting for Rezell's words, causing Rezell's eyebrow to twitch slightly.

The movement was barely perceptible, but for Rei, it was confirmation enough.

And upon hearing that giants were coming... the one who showed enthusiasm was, naturally, Vihera, who had been looking forward to fighting giants.

It was only natural, since he'd always looked forward to fighting giants.

On top of that, having witnessed the sight of the giant tearing into his mother's body, he also harbored anger toward the giant.

Though, to be honest, that anger was directed more at Jaya than at the giants themselves.

"Ah, I did call them. I don't know if you're aware, but giants are strong, you know? Even adventurers with nicknames wouldn't find them easy to defeat."

Rei hesitated slightly at Rezell's confident declaration.

...His hesitation wasn't about whether they could defeat the giants.

In that regard, victory was already certain in Rei's mind.

He placed that much trust in Vihera's combat prowess.

What troubled Rei was whether he should kill Rezell immediately here and now, or incapacitate him later to extract information.

Of course, doing either right now would be extremely easy.

Looking at Rezell's physique, it was clear he was reasonably well-trained, but ultimately, that was all it was.

For combat specialists—though he wasn't truly one—like Rei and his group, he was hardly an opponent worth mentioning.

But... even so, the reason he could remain so calm in front of Rei and the others was likely because he had strong confidence in the giants approaching them.

If the source of that confidence—the giant—were wiped out by Vihera... that would surely be a blow powerful enough to break Rezell's spirit.

And there's no easier target to extract information from than someone whose spirit has been broken.

(Honestly, I don't like what this man was doing. I don't like it... but I need the information, right?)

Even now, inside the room, several people were being used to produce giants by having their insides torn out and their stomachs ripped open.

Each time, one more life was lost... specifically, the life of a woman who likely never intended to become a prostitute, but had been forcibly brought to Mejogo by Jaya.

It's impossible not to feel something about that... Indeed, if it weren't necessary, Rezell would have been killed immediately.

Now then, what kind of information can be obtained?

As Rei pondered this, the giant finally revealed itself before his eyes.

The giant that entered through the door stood about three meters tall.

True to its name, its body was encased in what appeared to be incredibly sturdy muscles.

However, it appeared to carry no weapons in its hands.

Was this its natural fighting style, or had it deliberately come unarmed to avoid causing unnecessary destruction here?

The exact reason was unclear to Rei, but the giant loomed over Vihera, who stood before it.

Vihera, too, stared back at the giant with eyes brimming with the desire to fight...

(Come to think of it, do you count giants as 'pieces'? ...Well, they're probably closer to monsters anyway, so whatever.)

Three giants had entered the room in total.

Their attire differed slightly, but each possessed a massive frame worthy of the name "giant."

"So you're the rumored giants? I have my reservations, but I was actually looking forward to fighting you today."

"Wh-wh-what?"

Vihera addressed the giant, but the giant merely tilted its head in confusion.

Then, still tilting its head, it casually raised its right hand, clenched into a fist... and in the next instant, brought that fist down in silence.

It wasn't the kind of punch you'd normally imagine, aimed downward... but rather an attack method known as a hammer punch.

Even if a normal human used it, it wouldn't be that powerful of a strike.

At the very least, a regular punch... or more importantly, a strike with a weapon would be more powerful.

But when performed by a giant, it's a different story.

With strength incomparable to that of ordinary humans, the attack, while wide, was by no means slow.

Moreover, there were two other giants here, silently watching Vihera's movements.

If Vihera dodges the attack, they'll launch a follow-up strike.

If he dodged right, the giant on the right would strike.

If he dodges left, the giant on the left.

If he dodged backward, the timing would be slightly delayed, but the giants on both sides would strike simultaneously.

That was what the giants had been thinking, but what they saw was a completely unexpected sight.

Instead of dodging the giant's strike to the left, right, or back, Vihera stepped forward.

Of course, only Rezell was surprised by this.

The giants themselves didn't possess minds capable of such deep thought... precisely why they didn't freeze in shock.

Vihera plunged straight into the giant's guard. Instead of using his usual one-hit-kill Magic Palm, he aimed a kick directly at the giant's right leg... more precisely, at its right knee.

Normally, a kick aimed at an enemy's right knee would be called a low kick.

But, perhaps due to the giant's height, the kick launched was one that could be called a high kick.

Vihera's kick, protected by the magic item greaves, struck the giant's right knee squarely from the side.

Through the greaves came a sensation like something tearing—pikiri—and something shattering—gushari.

"Guh? Gah? Gah... Guhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh

The giant seemed momentarily confused about what had happened, but as his right knee shattered and he collapsed to the floor, he finally grasped the pain.

A cry of pain, confusion at not understanding what had happened, and the frustration of failing to take down the small woman before him with a single blow only to receive a counterattack—all these reasons combined into a sound that was neither a scream nor a roar.

Though the giant lay sprawled on the floor, whether from the pain or by design, he swung his arm wildly.

There, in front of him, stood Vihera.

Unlike the hammer punch earlier, his fist wasn't clenched... It was an attack that seemed like he was just wildly swinging his arm around. But coming from a giant nearly three meters tall, it still carried immense power.

Enough force to blow an ordinary person away.

But of course, Vihera couldn't be described as ordinary...

"Wh-..."

A gasp of astonishment escaped Rezell's lips at the sight unfolding before them.

That said, he'd already let out a cry of astonishment mere seconds earlier when Vihera shattered the giant's right knee with a single kick, so it wasn't unusual for Rei and the others.

After all, many were astonished by Vihera's attacks.

Still, the scene unfolding before Rei and the others now... catching the giant's arm swung with brute force, using that momentum to throw the giant who had been kneeling... even to Rei and her comrades, it was a truly astonishing sight.

The thrown giant slammed into the giant on its right and rolled across the floor.

Then, closing the distance toward the remaining giant on the left, Vihera advanced.

The giant likely never imagined that a small opponent like Vihera could hurl one of its own comrades.

It took several seconds too long for it to notice Vihera closing in.

"Hah!"

A fist strike was unleashed with a sharp exhalation.

Not a palm strike like the Magic Palm, but a punch delivered with a tightly clenched fist.

That single blow easily shattered the giant's knee joint.
