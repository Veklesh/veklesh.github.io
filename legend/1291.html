The fact that it was winter and work wasn't particularly heavy probably played a role too.

While the receptionist and others had various paperwork tasks to handle during this season, it was ultimately the work of the lower-ranking staff and didn't reach the higher-ups.

...Upon learning this, a certain feline beastman supposedly resolved to become Guildmaster someday! Or maybe not.

Anyway, with a bit more freedom now, Marina promptly invited Rei and the others to come outside Gilm.

"So, it's nice we're out here, but where are we going? We've got a carriage ready, so is it somewhere fairly far?"

Vihera asked Marina as she watched the white scenery flow by the window.

Marina, asked, smiled and opened her mouth.

"We're not going that far. You know, that place Rei often talks about where he dissects monsters? Near there, there should be a decent number of monsters, right?"

Marina smiled radiantly. Wearing her usual party dress with its plunging neckline, anyone unaware might easily assume she was heading to some gathering.

Though, seeing her bow and quiver, one probably wouldn't assume she was simply heading to a party.

Sitting in his seat, watching Set walk alongside the carriage while enjoying the feel of the snow beneath his feet, Rei turned his gaze toward Marina at her words.

Incidentally, this carriage was borrowed from the guild, and the driver had hired about three adventurers in need of money.

The reason they hadn't used a guild employee as the driver was likely because Marina's actions this time weren't as guildmaster, but purely as Marina... as an adventurer.

Of course, if there were no adventurers seeking work within the Guild, they might have used a Guild employee.

Beyond that, since the carriage needed to wait while Rei and the others fought monsters, using guild staff also posed a combat capability issue.

That's precisely why she hired three adventurers with some skill.

Admittedly, having three people on the driver's platform was a bit cramped, but considering safety, it couldn't be helped.

Besides, thanks to Marina's spirit magic, it wasn't too cold sitting on the driver's seat, so the working conditions weren't bad at all.

At the very least, the three hired adventurers were grateful they didn't have to wait for Rei and the others to return in the carriage, shivering from the cold at this time of year.

"Still... winter monsters, huh? I wonder what kind of monsters are out there. I'd be thrilled if there were some unknown monsters, if possible."

Rei's hope stemmed, naturally, from seeking magic stones... sustenance to grow Set and Death Scythe.

Still, he didn't say it outright. Partly because he couldn't let the adventurer driving the carriage overhear, but more importantly...

"Hm?"

"No, nothing."

Rei shook his head at Byune, who had turned her gaze toward him.

Yes. Among the members forming the party this spring, Byune was the only one who still knew nothing about Beast Magic.

Rei didn't think it was good at all that Byune was the only one in the party unaware of Beast Magic.

But when he considered whether it was right to tell Byune—who would likely leave the party in the near future, probably within a few years—about beast magic, one of his greatest secrets, he couldn't bring himself to nod immediately.

It wasn't that he didn't trust Byune...

Rei had known Byune for quite some time and didn't believe she would easily sell out his secrets.

But this world was full of magic items and spells.

Among them, there was a very real possibility of things existing that could extract information directly from one's mind or read one's thoughts, even if Byune had no intention of speaking.

If that were the case, then not knowing Rei's secrets, starting with his beast magic, was undoubtedly the safest option for Byune.

It was ordinary dried orc meat, but since it had been bought from a reasonably high-end shop, it wasn't exactly a cut above the dried meat sold haphazardly on the streets... but the taste was definitely superior.

"Hm?"

"Is it okay for me to eat this?" Byune asked. Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah. You're getting a little hungry, aren't you?"

"Mm!"

At Rei's words, Byune seemed pleased, at least verbally, as she took the jerky and put it in her mouth.

"Hey, Rei. Don't spoil Byune too much, okay?"

Marina smiled amusedly at Vihera, who spoke in a tone as if scolding her father, but Rei seemed oblivious, simply nodding back.

"Ah, sorry. But dried meat's fine, right?"

"Mm!"

That's right, that's right, Byune said, turning her gaze toward Vihera.

Vihera glanced at Byune... and the moment that gaze met hers, Byune quietly looked away.

"Heh heh, watching them like this, it's almost like..."

"Like what?"

At Marina's words, Vihera tilted her head, prompting her to continue.

But Marina stopped there, saying nothing more.

Vihera shot Marina a slightly displeased look... but the recipient of that gaze seemed completely unfazed, merely smiling.

"Marina, if you have something to say, you should say it clearly, don't you think? That way, things won't get awkward when we form a party, right?"

"Oh? What are you talking about? I don't have anything in particular on my mind... Does something bother you, Vihera?"

"Huh. That's interesting. Well then..."

Just as Vihera opened her mouth to speak, the carriage stopped, as if timed perfectly.

For a moment, Vihera wondered what was happening, but she quickly realized it wasn't a monster attack... or worse, bandits. She didn't show any sign of panic.

The carriage hadn't come to an abrupt halt, but rather stopped with a well-calculated precision.

Looking out the window, she saw nothing but trees.

With that, the reason for the carriage's halt was obvious without needing to think.

"Guildmaster, we've arrived."

The voice coming from the driver's seat confirmed the passengers' suspicions.

"See? Looks like we're here. Vihera, shall we get ready to step out?"

"...I'll do that for you."

Reluctantly, Vihera nodded at Marina's words and stepped out of the carriage.

Relieved that she hadn't been scolded despite her expression, Byune followed Vihera out of the carriage.

"Don't tease me too much."

Marina smiled and replied to Rei's words.

To be honest, Marina felt a pang of envy looking at Byune, Vihera, and Rei together.

Yes, husband and wife, and their daughter...

Of course, if she said that directly, Vihera would protest, "At my age, I don't have a child like Byune!"

(But I don't want to say that honestly, though.)

Realizing they had no retort, they quietly averted their gazes.

"Well, it's fine. Thanks to you, we get to take it easy."

"Hehehe. Um, go easy on us?"

"So, we should be back in Gilm before sunset. Until then, relax. But like I said before, the warmth only extends around the carriage. If you wander off, you'll get cold, okay?"
