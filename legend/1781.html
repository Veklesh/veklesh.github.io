"Hmm, that suits you. Your usual look is nice, but this style really suits you too, Rei."

Saying this, Elena looked at Rei with a delighted smile.

The outfit Rei was currently wearing wasn’t his usual Dragon Robe… but rather, attire befitting a party.

Normally, as an adventurer, it wouldn’t be strange for Rei to attend a party in his Dragon Robe.

After all, for an adventurer, that could be considered formal wear.

However, for the party taking place later today—or, to be precise, the dinner party—Elena had hoped he would attend in formal attire if possible.

Attending a dinner party without formal attire could invite unnecessary trouble.

No, normally that wouldn’t be a problem at all, but today it also serves as an introduction for Rei.

Even though it’s just a gathering of close friends… no, precisely because of that, she wanted to make sure no one would say anything when they saw Rei.

Even so, Elena wasn’t keen on making Rei change his clothes for that reason.

Still, the thought of the man she loved being criticized by her family was not something Elena found amusing.

...Besides, she couldn’t completely deny that she also wanted to see Rei in formal attire, rather than wearing his usual Dragon Robe.

Rei, who understood that his position in this mansion was unstable, changed his clothes obediently, even though he felt a slight resentment toward Elena’s words.

That said, the dissatisfaction Rei felt wasn’t because he disliked having to dress formally, but rather because the formal attire was stiff and made it difficult to move.

“Do I really have to wear this?”

It bore some resemblance to the suits Rei was familiar with, but when viewed as a whole, there were quite a few differences.

However, the way the neck was tied with a beautiful piece of fabric did look somewhat like a unique tie.

Precisely because of that, for Rei—who usually wore the Dragon Robe and was never used to having his neck constricted—that piece of fabric was not particularly to his liking.

“I did choose an outfit that’s not too stiff, even among formal wear… Is that not good enough?”

With Elena saying that, Rei had no choice but to agree.

Having spent a lot of time with Elena lately, Rei’s sense of values had become completely numb, but having Elena—the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel and known by the nickname “Princess General”—choose his clothes was something he simply couldn’t refuse under normal circumstances.

That said, in Rei’s case, it wasn’t about appearances; the sole and biggest reason he couldn’t refuse was simply that Elena was so enthusiastic about it.

“Ah… I guess so. For now, I think I can manage to endure this uncomfortable outfit for a short while. Besides, Georgima is going to serve some delicious food for dinner tonight, right? In that case, there’s no way I’m going to miss out on that.”

“I heard from Ara. I hear you provoked Georgima?”

Relieved that Rei didn’t seem to mind the formal attire, Elena cast a slightly amused glance his way.

Rei was met with a look that seemed to be scolding a mischievous boy, but he spoke up, feeling wronged by the gaze.

“I didn’t do anything like provoke her, you know? I just said that if she served a dish I genuinely thought was delicious, I’d teach her the recipe I know. And even with that dish, I don’t know every single detail of how to make it, so in reality, I’d probably just teach her the basics, and Georgima would have to figure out the rest on her own.”

Even as he said this, Rei had a hunch that he would probably end up teaching Georgima how to cook.

She had taken the seafood okonomiyaki that Rei had popularized at Emoshion—though strictly speaking, since Rei had only taught it to one person, “popularized” might not be the right word—and refined it to a whole new level of flavor.

I didn’t think a chef like that would cut corners in a showdown against Rei, who presumably knows countless—literally countless—completely unfamiliar dishes.

It’s almost certain that Georgima will serve up the dish she’s most skilled at and most confident in.

(If that’s the case, I guess I’d better come up with a plan… Hmm, I think it would turn out pretty well if I just remembered how to make ramen or something.)

Rei pondered this while looking down at his outfit.

Although ramen originated as a Chinese dish, in Japan it has been completely adapted to Japanese tastes and has become one of the nation’s staple foods, right up there with curry.

As for Rei’s personal preference, he liked rich miso ramen, but he knew there were many other varieties—shoyu ramen, shio ramen, tonkotsu ramen, tantanmen—and he’d actually eaten quite a few of them.

Ramen shops are popular, and even in the countryside where Rei used to live, there are several of them.

Also, the fact that you can easily enjoy cup ramen, instant ramen, fresh ramen, and so on is a major factor.

In that sense, if I taught people how to make ramen, it would definitely catch on. I think it would catch on… but Rei lacked the knowledge to actually make ramen.

Well, of course, Rei had a general understanding of it.

For example, he knew that ramen consists of three main components: soup, noodles, and toppings.

As for making the soup, if he thought back to when he made Kiritanpo hot pot, it wasn’t something he couldn’t manage.

As for the toppings, thanks to magic, there’s plenty of meat here that’s far more delicious than anything he had access to back in Japan.

Unfortunately, as for the menma that Rei loves… while there are bamboo shoots themselves, he doesn’t know how to turn them into menma, so he’ll probably have to go through a process of trial and error.

Incidentally, while Rei liked bamboo shoots in general, not just menma, the kind found in her hometown—or more precisely, around her house—were varieties called Nemagari-take or Hime-take, not the common Moso bamboo.

Thinking about that... Rei suddenly remembered a TV show she’d seen.

The program claimed that Nemagari-take didn’t need to be soaked to remove bitterness, but for Rei—who harvested and ate Nemagari-take practically every year—the honest truth was, “What are they talking about?”

At the very least, the Nemagari-take that grew in the region where Rei lived required de-bittering.

...As Rei was thinking about this, he realized his mind had wandered and brought his thoughts back to the ramen.

The soup and toppings could be managed to some extent, but the biggest problem was the noodles.

As far as Rei knew, ramen noodles were made from flour, water, salt… and sometimes eggs, but they should also require something called kansui.

And as far as Rei was concerned, he only knew the name “kansui.”

…Actually, the fact that Rei even knew what “kansui” was in the first place was surprising.

In any case, based on what Rei knew, ramen noodles required kansui, yet he had no idea what kansui actually was.

If he was going to teach her how to make ramen, he would have to explain that mysterious liquid as well.

Also, since Rei’s understanding of ramen is nothing more than an amateur’s opinion, it’s certain there are plenty of other things missing.

(Well, I guess I’ll just have Georgima figure that part out for himself. …Though, depending on the situation, I might be a bit stumped if he asks how it differs from udon.)

Udon and ramen.

If it were Rei... no, if it were any Japanese person, they’d naturally understand the difference, but if someone who only knows udon—or doesn’t know either—asked him specifically how they differ, I don’t think Rei could answer.

“Rei, what’s wrong? You’re suddenly deep in thought… Is there something wrong with your clothes or something?”

“No, it’s nothing. I was just thinking about what kind of dish to teach Georgima.”

Hearing Rei’s words, Elena gave a small smile and spoke up.

“What? So teaching her to cook was the plan from the start?”

“After eating that seafood okonomiyaki, I have to admit your cooking skills are definitely top-notch… no, even better than that. Besides, when I said I’d teach you to cook, it was partly out of gratitude for making that seafood okonomiyaki.”

“Hehe, so even Rei can’t resist good food, too?”

“Well, yeah. Plus, I’d like Georgima to make a dish from my world. I’m thinking of teaching her ramen—a noodle dish similar to udon—but it requires alkaline water, right? Do you think Georgima would be able to handle that?”

“Alkaline water, huh. I’m not sure… but considering Georgima’s passion for cooking, I think she’ll be able to handle that just fine. Still… food from the world Rei used to live in, huh. I’m looking forward to it.”

Since Elena is someone Rei has confided in, she knows that he came to this world from another one.

That’s probably why she’s curious about what kind of food exists in the world Rei originally came from.

In fact, the udon, meat buns, and pizza that Rei introduced in Gilm were all absolutely exquisite.

...Though, to be fair, Rei only taught the chefs the basic concepts of these dishes; they refined them based on his instructions, which is how they came to taste the way they do now.

In fact, the broth for the udon is different from what Rei used to eat, and the meat buns and pizza also differ in various details, including the ingredients.

Even so, they’ve turned out to be proper dishes, and the fact that they can be described as delicious is likely the result of the chefs’ ingenuity.

In that sense, I should have had high hopes for teaching Georgima how to make ramen.

(Since ramen has become so popular in Japan that it’s practically considered a national dish, if I taught Georgima how to make it, wouldn’t ramen shops start popping up everywhere the next time I visit Anesis? Well, actually, as someone who loves ramen, that would be a welcome development. I wonder if I should also explain the concept of “kaedama”...)

"Kaedama"—which could be called a "second helping of noodles"—is probably a bit of a surprising cultural concept to those unfamiliar with ramen.

Even Rei, who knows all about ramen, was surprised when she first learned about the practice of kaedama.

“Hehe.”

Seeing Rei looking troubled, a smile escaped Elena’s lips.

It was a truly joyful smile—not that of a Princess General, but of Elena Kerebel, a woman smiling at the man she loved.

“Is something wrong?”

“No, nothing. More importantly… it’s about time. Are you okay with that?”

“Yeah, as you can see, I’m fine, but does Elena not need to get ready?”

Rei had been made to change into a suit, but Elena, who was talking to him, didn’t seem to be dressed up in any particular way.

“Hm? Oh, I think I’ll go back and get changed soon, too.”

“...Will you make it in time?”

In Rei’s experience, women simply took longer to get ready than men.

Especially when it comes to something like a party dress, that’s likely even more true.

If she were someone like Marina, who wears party dresses on a daily basis, she’d be somewhat used to it and could change quickly… but Elena doesn’t usually wear that kind of clothing.

…No, actually, it’s Marina who’s the exception—she usually wears clothes like party dresses, which are very revealing and hard to move in.

“Ah, it’s no problem. Besides, today is just a gathering of family and friends. There’s no need to wear such a fancy party dress. We have maids here, so you can change quickly.”

“Then why did they make me wear this?”

Naturally, the maids are helping Rei change as well.

But if it’s just a gathering of family, wouldn’t it have been fine not to go to the trouble of changing at all?

Hearing Rei’s words, Elena shook her head with a serious expression.

“No, actually, since it’s just family, we should look our best. Especially since Mother is here today. If we show up looking strange, it could cause trouble.”

“Mom? Come to think of it, Elena has never mentioned her mother to me.”

Elena’s father is Duke Kerebel, a nobleman so prominent in the Kingdom of Mireana that there’s hardly anyone who doesn’t know him.

Even Rei knew that much.

……Well, actually, since he’s involved with Daskar, the leader of the Neutral Faction, and Elena, the Duke of Kerebel’s only daughter, it would be a problem if he didn’t know that much.

Feeling Rei’s gaze on her, Elena spoke with a serious expression.

“My mother… well, she has a rather unique personality. I’m sure Rei would take a liking to her, though…”

She didn’t say anything more, leaving her words hanging.

Rei was slightly concerned by Elena’s demeanor, but she quickly returned to her room, saying she needed to change.
