Rei and the others received that report just as they were about to leave Azimov's house.

After the mock battle, Alogan sighed at the persistent gap in skill between himself and Rei, while Culotte could only manage a dry laugh after Rei flatly declared he'd lose in combat even to a smaller opponent like Byune.

"Did I perhaps fail a little?" Rei found himself breaking out in a cold sweat, which was perhaps understandable in a way.

He had thoroughly crushed the fighting spirit of the two he intended to entrust with his escort... no, he had shattered it completely.

While it was supposed to be a mock battle adjusted to their strength, he wondered if he should have held back a bit more. With that thought, he decided to leave Azimov's house for now and gather some information... and that's when the woman who had previously brought news of Precious going to the slums made contact.

The information she shared was that a carriage had crashed into the Spier Trading Company's Gilm branch.

While it could have been dismissed as a mere accident, given the current situation, Rei and the others naturally didn't entertain such a thought.

It was almost certainly a spillover from the Precious incident.

Thinking this, Rei and the others started running toward the Spier Trading Company's Gilm branch.

"Probably... no, it's definitely Precious's doing. There's a chance it really was an accident, or maybe it was an attack by someone other than Precious."

"If that's the case, what do we do?"

Despite wearing a party dress that clearly hindered her movement, Marina's running speed matched Rei's even as she asked the question.

Of course, that was only because they were running on crowded streets; outside the city, the difference would naturally become apparent.

The same applied to Vihera and Byune.

And above all, Set was holding back considerably while running.

"Nothing. Of course, if it looks dangerous, I could lend a hand."

Rei owed Tris a favor, but he didn't owe her anything.

Given that, he had no intention of offering assistance on his own initiative.

It would be a different story if Precious handed over evidence or information linking her to the series of incidents.

Or if someone completely unrelated was on the verge of dying, he'd probably help then too.

"Well, it'd be pretty unnatural for a carriage to crash into Tris's shop under the current circumstances. It's more likely Precious set something up. ...But since it's already happened, I doubt there'd be any clues left, right?"

This is Vihera.

Running with a thin, flowing garment like those worn by dancers or prostitutes billowing in the wind, she stated this.

Running behind Vihera, Byune nodded silently.

"Yeah. I know that. But right now, I want any information or clues I can get. There might be a chance... right?"

After doing something like ramming a carriage into the shop, Rei couldn't believe it would just end there.

Of course, it was entirely possible it was nothing more than a threat.

That part needed to be confirmed by actually going to the shop.

As they continued down the road, the number of people gradually increased.

Once it got this crowded, Rei and the others couldn't run anymore, so they switched to walking.

"A carriage crashed into it?"

"Yeah. That's what it seems like... but apparently there was no driver? And aside from the injured, it's a miracle no one died."

"Huh? No driver... Then why was the carriage moving? If there was no driver..."

"Yeah. Probably some kind of incident. This shop is a branch of the Spier Trading Company, a guild that just arrived in Gilm."

"Whoa, really? Then maybe we shouldn't get too involved?"

As they walked down the street, voices reached Rei's ears.

They weren't speaking loudly, just exchanging quiet words with the person next to them.

But with Rei's hearing, he could easily make out the content of their conversation.

(A driverless carriage, huh. If it were a horse, it would probably just avoid buildings if they were right in front of it. Or stop. But the fact that it didn't do that... means it couldn't?)

Still pondering the question, he continued down the street until the shop the carriage had crashed into came into view.

Guards were already there, investigating the scene.

"Looks like we were a little late after all."

"It can't be helped. We only got the information that a carriage crashed here, then heard about it through others, and then came here like this."

Listening to Marina and Vihera, Rei spotted a familiar face... a guard who had been at the station when Azimov was attacked.

He must have spotted Rei too. ...Well, with prominent figures like Set, Marina, and Vihera present, it probably wasn't hard for him to notice them.

"Rei, what's up?"

"Nothing, just passing by. More importantly, I heard a carriage crashed?"

"Yeah. The damage was minimal, but..."

The guard hedged his words.

That was because there were curious onlookers gathered around.

Sharing important information here would be unwise for various reasons.

That was Rei's reasoning, but even so, Rei could tell there was some underlying reason.

"I see. Can I meet Tris?"

"No, I'm still gathering details about the situation. I'm afraid I can't let you see him."

"...I see."

With those words, Rei understood this wasn't just an accident.

Under normal circumstances, someone in Tris's position rarely appeared at the storefront.

Yet the fact he was being questioned like this meant something had happened.

Of course, even ordinary accidents sometimes required some questioning, so it wasn't necessarily conclusive.

"Grr."

Set gently tugged at the hem of Rei's Dragon Robe with his beak while Rei spoke with the guard.

For a moment, Rei wondered if he was hungry, but in the round eyes staring back at him, there was a sharp gleam.

It wasn't the gaze of Set the mascot, the one he showed in town... It was the gaze of Set the monster, the one he showed outside the city.

That alone made it clear Set had detected something unusual.

Marina and Vihera must have sensed the change in Rei's aura too. They approached Rei and Set cautiously.

A moment later, Byune also came over to Rei and the others.

"What's going on?"

"It's Set,"

Marina muttered briefly, following Rei's gaze until it settled on a woman.

She had hair cut straight across her shoulders and could be described as rather beautiful.

But the woman, perhaps noticing that Rei and the others were watching her, tilted her head as if unsure why she was being looked at, then eventually walked away.

(What should we do? Should we follow her?)

The hesitation lasted only a moment. Immediately, he called out to Byune, the most skilled in covert operations among them.

"Byune, follow that woman. But don't push yourself too hard. If you sense any danger, break off immediately. We'll track you using Set's nose."

"Got it!"

"Leave it to me!" muttered Byune, and immediately took off running.

The guard who had been watching Rei and the others tried to say something, but he probably understood that speaking up now would only make things worse.

In the end, he said nothing. He gave Rei a brief nod and returned to his post.

"Let's go."

Glancing at the guard, Rei said only that.

Then Set started walking, and Rei and the others followed behind.

"That was an unexpected turn of events. ...Well, criminals returning to the scene isn't exactly uncommon, though."

"True. But this time, there were probably a lot of unexpected things for them too, right? Otherwise, they wouldn't have done something reckless like ramming a carriage into the shop."

Vihera's words made sense to Rei too.

Indeed, under normal circumstances, such a large-scale... no, such a pointlessly flashy stunt would hardly ever be attempted.

While it wasn't impossible in a conflict between underground organizations, the Spier Trading Company was a legitimate, above-board merchant house. ...Though they might have some shady dealings on the side.

"So, something must have triggered it. ...Could it be... our business?"

Rei and the others had appeared in the slums, flaunting their power in plain sight, taking actions that could rightly be called threatening.

Originally, the goal was to panic Precious and make them slip up, but perhaps Precious's hand reached out not for Rei, but for Tris.

Vihera nodded at Rei's words.

"This happening at this timing... it probably means that. ...I didn't expect them to resort to this kind of tactic, though."

"Really? Attacking us would be reckless in many ways, wouldn't it? Then, as the next step they should take, I don't think this incident was a mistake at all."

Marina's explanation made sense.

Even if Precious didn't rate Rei that highly, the slum dwellers Precious had allied with were a different story.

Living in Gilm, they've gathered solid intelligence on just how strong Rei is.

Pulling any funny business here would make Rei their enemy.

But Ray wanted to accept Precious's request.

Thinking that way, Rei could understand why they'd come after Tris instead of him.

...In reality, there are far more complicated circumstances involved, but naturally, Rei knows nothing of them.

"GRRRR!?"

Set, who had been leading Rei and the others, suddenly let out a sharp cry.

There was no reason for Set to do such a thing without meaning, so it was clear something had happened.

And given the current situation, what could have happened... well, it was obvious.

「Byune！」

Realizing what it meant, Vihera shouted and increased her running speed.

"Hmph!"

With a rapier blade whizzing past right beside her face, Byune showed no particular sign of panic as she shifted into attack mode.

The woman who had been following her easily saw through this and counterattacked.

At first, he hadn't realized she was waiting for him, and the rapier's strike left him with a small wound.

Still, the fact that the woman's strike only resulted in a slash wound was likely the fruit of the combat training Byune had undergone during the winter.

Though she had been struck first, the fight had since become evenly matched... no, if anything, Byune was gaining the upper hand.

Compared to a rapier, the dagger had a shorter reach—though its blade was still longer than average.

Furthermore, in terms of physical size, Byune was still a child compared to the adult woman.

Though outmatched in both weapon and physique, the difference in reach was easily overcome by Byune's swift physical abilities and the experience gained from mock battles with Rei and Vihera.

"You're a troublesome little thing. Don't you think it's about time you took a break?"

The woman's words carried a hint of scolding, but Byune showed no sign of being bothered by them as she readied her weapon... White Cloud.

(The little one is troublesome, but what's more troublesome is that dagger. ...It is a dagger, right?)

For the woman... Kena, the rapier was a weapon she possessed complete confidence in.

Indeed, her ability to operate as a freelance assassin up until now stemmed from her confidence in her skill and stealth.

Naturally, as an assassin, using a weapon she was satisfied with was a given. The rapier, crafted from the materials of a Rank B monster, possessed a sharpness easily capable of piercing through leather armor lying around.

And yet, the moment she touched the weapon held by the child before her, she understood with absolute certainty from the feel of it: if this continued, her rapier would be destroyed.

(Why does a kid like this have a dagger like that?)

It was a strange dagger, clearly longer than a standard dagger yet distinctly shorter than a longsword.

A weapon clearly unsuitable for the child before him to wield.

It was precisely the kind of weapon he could put to better use himself.

Thinking this, Kena was momentarily captivated by the pure white dagger before her... the White Cloud...

"Ow!"

A sudden, intense pain made her cry out.

Glancing down, she saw a slender needle embedded in her left arm. There was no doubt it had been thrown in that split-second opening.

"You're making things troublesome for me, aren't you?"

"Hmph."

Kena took a step back, pulled the long needle from her left arm, and flung it to the ground. She glared at Byune with a look of utter disgust.
