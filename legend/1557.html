Shipment date.

That must mean shipping the giant, as expected.

To the people of Rezell... no, to the people of Jaya, the giant is their product.

And an extremely powerful one at that, with a buyer already decided.

Even anticipating this, Rei felt uneasy.

Of course, even to Rei, the giant possessed no semblance of knowledge.

They couldn't communicate with us at all, just like monsters.

Thinking this... suddenly, a question arose within Rei.

(Huh? Can't communicate? Then how are they moving the giant? Oh, right. The whistle earlier.)

He remembered Resel whistling when summoning the giant.

...It was actually a special whistle inaudible to ordinary humans, but Rei's body had been created using the techniques of the Zepairu clan.

Hearing it was no problem, and the others, aside from Byune, were more or less beings deserving of being called non-human. ...In Marina's case, being a Dark Elf, she was literally non-human.

"Shipment day, huh? Shipping giants means they're obviously being shipped to this country, right?"

"That's how it is. ...After all, this city was built for that very purpose."

"For that purpose? Yet it seems to be making quite a bit of money?"

Considering they're just impregnating women with giant children after turning them into prostitutes with slave collars, it's undeniable they're making a fortune from brothels and taverns.

"Even with the country's backing, Jaya is still essentially an underground organization. Given that, it's only natural they'd make money wherever they can."

"Yeah, that's probably about right."

It probably serves as a kind of camouflage too.

Leaving those thoughts unsaid, Rei turned his attention back to Rezell.

"So, about shipping the giants... where exactly are they gathering them? They're that big, so you can't just leave them lying around, can you?"

"If you think about it normally, probably the castle in Rossi?"

Vihera, her battle excitement subsiding, offered this suggestion, but Rezell shook his head.

"According to the Chancellor, that was the initial plan. But with well over a thousand giants, there's simply no room for them in the castle. Plus, some in the upper echelons are afraid to have giants so close."

"They're afraid of the giants they created themselves? Well, maybe it's hard to completely trust the existence of giants..."

Even as she spoke, a hint of contempt flickered in Elena's eyes.

She likely thought it was absurd that they feared these giants, despite having created them for their own convenience.

But Marina, who had been listening to Elena, spoke up, conceding it was understandable.

"Elena, it can't be helped. If they understood our language, it might be different, but giants can't comprehend words, right? So, you never know when one might suddenly go berserk."

"But..."

Still looking dissatisfied, Elena stroked the Yellow on her right shoulder.

For Elena, who had created Yellow through dragon language magic, it was hard to accept that despite creating the giants, they were being treated as nuisances.

Understanding Elena's feelings, Rei shook his head.

"Yellow and Set are different from the giants. ...I don't think you need to worry about it that much, Elena."

「Rei……」

Elena seemed to want to say more, but she must have realized it was pointless to continue.

Though she still harbored some dissatisfaction, she said nothing more about the giants' treatment.

Confirming this, Rei turned his gaze to Rezell.

"So, if not the castle, then where are the giants? I mean, they're that big. You can't just carelessly keep them waiting around in the city, right? They're living beings, after all. They'll need food and such."

"How much does that giant eat in a day... At the very least, it must need an amount incomparable to ours. Well, never mind Rei and Byune..."

Elena's voice reached Rei, but Rei paid it no mind whatsoever, focusing instead on Rezel's reaction.

Normally, this would undoubtedly be highly classified information that must never be disclosed to others... But with his spirit broken, Rezell had no choice but to answer Rei's question.

"You know there are several forests near the Jaya and Rossi roads, right? In one of those forests, there's an underground facility smaller than this one, and a giant is...?! Wha... what...?"

Just seconds ago, Rezell had been talking freely, but suddenly his words stopped.

Rei and the others looked toward Rezell, wondering what had happened, only to see him bleeding from every orifice—his eyes, nose, ears, mouth.

The sight was reminiscent of the giant from earlier, struck by the Magic Palm to the head.

But Rei and the others reacted instantly upon seeing Rezell like this.

They instantly grabbed their weapons and began surveying their surroundings.

But... after keeping watch for over ten seconds...

Rei and the others faced no attack whatsoever.

"It's... not an attack?"

"No arrows, poison darts, or long needles... I don't think there were any of those."

Rei denied Rei's question.

Then, Vihera, who had been listening, spoke up.

"Then magic?"

"That's the only thing left, I suppose... But if magic was used directly on us right here and now..."

Marina paused, then used spirit magic to scan the surroundings.

She frowned slightly, then shook her head.

"In a sealed-off place like this, relying on wind spirits is still quite difficult. ...At least, there's no one lurking around here or trying to escape in a hurry."

"So, did they attack remotely from somewhere else?"

"That... I'm not sure. There are various conditions, but it seems unlikely. Besides..."

At Rei's words, Marina lowered her gaze to the floor.

Following Rei's gaze, Rei discovered Resel's entourage... those who had lost consciousness from the killing intent Rei had unleashed. Like Resel, they lay dead, blood streaming from their faces.

"This..."

"You see? If it had only targeted Rezell, that would be one thing. But the others too... They killed everyone else except us, cleanly. So it probably wasn't an attack from afar. It likely had some condition attached, and once that condition was met, the magic activated."

"I see."

Marina's explanation made perfect sense to Rei too.

Because Rei himself possessed magic that activated when certain conditions were met.

For example, spells like 'Seed of Admonition' or 'Flame of Condemnation'.

But some others seemed confused, tilting their heads.

"Like, say, a spell that activates if you try to speak certain information, treating it as betrayal."

"...But then, why did Rezell tell us so much information?"

"I see what you mean, Elena, but in this case, the trigger was probably the location where the giants were being shipped. ...I had other things I wanted to ask him too."

Had he extracted the information, Rei would likely have killed Rezell too.

To Rei, what Rezell was doing was utterly unforgivable, and the same went for everyone else besides Rei.

But if he was taken out before we got the crucial information... it would be a huge hassle.

"Do you think the person who planted that magic would know that we were trying to get information, and that's why Rezell died?"

"They definitely would have. It wouldn't be surprising if they'd set something like that up."

"...This is troublesome. I didn't think the Kingdom of Reburina had mages that powerful."

Rei muttered with annoyance, but then heard voices coming from nearby and turned his gaze that way.

There, all the pregnant women had already been devoured from within their bellies, and giant children were being born.

The pregnant women, their bellies torn open, died, of course. But the giant children began devouring even their mothers' corpses.

The only sound now was the crunching of flesh as they devoured their own mothers' corpses.

Following Rei's gaze, Elena noticed and opened her mouth, suppressing the emotions raging within her.

"Rei, please."

Rei understood exactly what she was pleading for.

No, seeing the scene unfolding before them now, it would be strange not to imagine it.

Rei glanced at the others as if to ask for their support, but the only response he got was nods of agreement, as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

Confirming this, Rei took the Death Scythe from within the Misty Ring and focused his consciousness.

'Flame, manifest one of your true forms—destruction. You are that which consumes all, extinguishes all, annihilates all. Bring forth great destruction, and thereby become the sacrifice for new rebirth.'

As he chanted the spell, a single fireball materialized atop the Death Scythe.

However, this was no ordinary fireball—it was imbued with considerable magical power.

The fireball's range of destruction is set, and the spell is complete.

'Scorching Inferno!'

With those words, the fireball is released, heading toward the center of the room.

Confirming the fireball's movement, Rei led Elena and the others out of the room.

Seconds after exiting and closing the door... a thunderous boom echoed throughout the area.

Screams could be heard from beyond the door.

Well, maybe it wasn't screams, but to Rei's ears, it sounded like screams.

(Well, even if it was screams... they'd probably be the screams of a giant's child.)

As far as Rei could tell, every pregnant woman tied to the beds was already dead.

There wasn't a single one left alive, not even one with the sheer will to survive that might have been gnawed open from the inside.

"...This is making me feel sick."

The one who murmured softly was Marina.

Even for Marina, who had seen countless horrific scenes over her years as an adventurer and later as a guildmaster, what was happening here was utterly unforgivable.

The others felt the same.

What was burning inside the room right now was undoubtedly the worst possible sight imaginable.

"Losing Rezell... that hurt. If we had to kill him, I wish we could have gotten more information out of him first."

The others nodded at Rei's words, signaling a change of subject.

"If she tried to reveal any information, she would have been killed for sure. ...Ah, maybe it wasn't magic after all. Perhaps she had some kind of magic item."

Elena stated this, but since he was already dead, there was nothing more to be done about it now.

All Rei and the others could do now was deal with the giant situation.

It would cause significant damage to both Jaya and the Kingdom of Reburina.

"And then there's that black crystal supposedly in this facility. It's because of this black crystal that the giants are born. That means if we can deal with the black crystal, no more giants will be born. ...Though, what happens to the pregnant woman now, I don't know."

"Right. Without the black crystal... maybe, just maybe, there's a chance the child wouldn't become a giant."

Vihera murmured, glancing around at her surroundings.

Hearing those words, Rei turned his gaze toward Marina.

Marina understood what Rei was asking, yet all she could do was shake her head at his gaze.

"It might turn out as Vihera said, but it might not at all. We don't even know what kind of effect the black crystal magic item itself has in the first place."

After saying that much, she took a small breath before speaking again.

"If things go well, the children of the pregnant women might be born as ordinary babies. But if things go badly, the effects of the black crystal could go haywire, and they might be born as giant children immediately. Of course, in that case, they wouldn't be born fully grown."

In other words, they would die immediately after birth.

Yes, Marina continued... while Rei and the others listened in silence.
