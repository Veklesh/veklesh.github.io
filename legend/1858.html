"Hmm... The Black Wolf, is it?"

Rei sensed a hint of irritation in Elena’s voice, but it was only natural for her to be upset upon learning that an assassin was freely coming and going within his own mansion.

“Calm down, come on. Even the passersby are scared of you, Elena.”

At Rei’s words, Elena regained her composure slightly and composed her expression.

If this were the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion, Elena probably wouldn’t have had to worry so much about her appearance, but Rei and the others were currently in the streets of Anesis.

Moreover, since they weren’t wearing any sort of disguise, the passersby recognized Elena as Elena.

……At first, Rei had suggested they wear disguises, but that idea was rejected by Ara, Miranda, and all the other maids who were with them.

They told him that even if Elena were to disguise herself, she couldn’t hide her beauty.

They actually tried a light disguise on her, but Elena’s hair is a stunning golden color, as if sunlight itself had taken shape, and she has a lot of it, thanks to her long curls.

We tried preparing hats and the like, but while I wouldn’t go so far as to say it was impossible to tuck all her hair under them… it was certainly quite difficult.

We couldn’t hide Elena’s most distinctive feature—her golden hair—and forcing a disguise on other parts of her would only make her stand out more.

This was particularly evident with her clothes.

There was no way to hide her ample bust with clothing; if anything, it would only be emphasized further and draw attention.

If that’s the case in winter clothing, I can’t even imagine what it would have been like in the summer when people wear lighter clothes.

In any case, forcing her into a poor disguise would only make Elena stand out even more.

Given that, the reasoning was that if she was going to stand out anyway, not having her wear a disguise would likely deter people from coming up with strange ideas.

(Though it wouldn’t hurt to have some magic items for disguises or something like that.)

Thinking this, Rei spoke up to calm Elena, who was looking a bit grumpy.

“Come on, we’re going to buy souvenirs, right? If you buy souvenirs in a situation like this, you’ll just end up confusing the shopkeeper.”

“Hmm, that’s…”

Elena, perhaps realizing she had been in a bad mood, shifted her mood slightly at Rei’s words.

She also decided that since she and Rei had gone out into the city together, she should make the most of it.

“So, what kind of souvenirs should we get? Or rather, who specifically do you think we should get them for?”

“Hmm, let’s see. The must-haves are Marina and the others. And Lord Daskar, I suppose. As for the rest, shouldn’t we just get them for the people you feel you owe a debt of gratitude to?”

“People I’ve been indebted to?”

As he said this, Rei thought of the people at Dusk Wheat inn, and the two friendly faces at the guild reception desk. Beyond that, there were the folks at Full Belly Inn, as well as others he was reasonably close to.

“...All things considered, that’s going to be quite a few people.”

“I suppose so.”

At Rei’s words, Elena smiled and replied.

It was proof that Rei had formed connections with so many people in Gilm, and that made Elena happy.

As someone who knew Rei’s secret, Elena found nothing but joy in the words coming from his mouth.

……Though she’d have to be a bit careful around a certain receptionist at the guild.

“If that’s the case, I’ll probably end up buying quite a lot… but well, if there’s any left over, I can just store it in the Misty Ring, so it shouldn’t be a problem.”

Even with food that has an expiration date, it’s no problem as long as it’s stored in the Misty Ring.

In fact, she could even buy freshly made food, store it in the Misty Ring, and take it back to Gilm as a souvenir.

That’s why, even if you buy a large amount of food as souvenirs, as long as you store it in the Misty Ring, you can eat it without any problems even years later.

"Food? Hmm, I was thinking of something more like small trinkets."

“Ah… I see. Still, I think souvenirs are generally better when they’re consumable items like food or drink.”

Those words came from Rei’s own experience.

When he was in Japan, he had received souvenirs from friends and relatives on several occasions—from trips and such.

But when Rei received things like pennants or stuffed animals, Rei found himself struggling to find a place to put them.

In the end, those kinds of things would end up in a cardboard box, stored away in a closet or somewhere else, so honestly, he felt that consumable items—like lots of snacks, food, or juice—were better in that sense.

Of course, since they had gone out of their way to buy souvenirs for him, he couldn’t very well voice his dissatisfaction.

(At least they didn’t bring back a wooden sword as a souvenir.)

In reality, Rei had never received a wooden sword as a souvenir, but she’d seen that kind of situation in manga a few times.

If they had brought something like a wooden sword...

(Wait, wouldn’t that have been a problem?)

If you lived in Tokyo, getting a wooden sword might be a hassle, but in the countryside… especially in a place surrounded by mountains, you could use it to kill snakes or whatever, or just throw it at them, and it’s also effective for chasing away animals like monkeys, raccoon dogs, and foxes.

(Come to think of it, a wooden sword might actually have been more useful than a pennant.)

With that in mind, Rei walks through Anesis with Elena, looking for souvenirs.

“So, we’re buying souvenirs… but specifically, what kind of souvenirs do you think they’d like? Would food or something like that be okay?”

Rei asked Elena this based on his experiences in Japan, but the response he received was a look of slight exasperation.

Not understanding why she was looking at him that way, Rei asked bluntly.

“Did I do something weird?”

“Hmm. Well… I think that would be fine for anyone other than Marina and the others. But in Marina’s case, I think she’d prefer something substantial—something that actually lasts—rather than food.”

“Is that so? Well, if Elena says so, I’ll go with that.”

Rei would have preferred food, but since Elena said so, he figured it would be best to go along with it.

After all, Rei knew absolutely nothing about women’s hearts.

“In that case… jewelry? No, but something they can use in their daily lives would be better.”

Marina, Vihera, and Byune are the three who form a party with Rei as adventurers.

Given that, Rei had thought that even if he were to give them jewelry... but then he remembered two of his three companions and immediately took back what he’d said.

Marina usually wears a party dress, and she doesn’t change out of it even on the battlefield.

Vihera, likewise, usually wears clothes that look like they’re made of layers upon layers of sheer fabric, the sort typically worn by prostitutes or dancers.

...Given that both of them wear such restrictive clothing, I didn’t think wearing a piece or two of jewelry would really affect their movement.

Only Byune, dressed like a typical thief, probably wouldn’t wear any jewelry.

“Ah, yeah. I guess jewelry is the best option after all? What do you think?”

Rei asked this, but the only response he got was Elena’s beaming smile.

But even though she was smiling, her eyes weren’t.

Facing such an Elena, Rei unconsciously took a step back as he asked.

“Um… was there something wrong with jewelry?”

“No, there’s no problem. Oh, definitely no problem. …It’s just that I felt a little envious of Marina and Vihera.”

Rei immediately realized what Elena meant as she spoke with that smile still on her face.

Though Rei was clueless about women’s hearts, he wasn’t so oblivious as to not realize what Elena was envying in this situation.

“Ah… Well, it’s not exactly a souvenir, but to commemorate going out with Elena today, should I buy you something?”

“Is that okay? Just being able to go out with Rei like this makes me luckier than Marina or Vihera.”

The forced smile she’d been wearing until just a moment ago vanished, and Elena asked, “Are you sure?”

From Elena’s perspective, there was no doubt she felt a slight sense of guilt toward Marina and Vihera—women who loved the same man as she did.

Traveling alone with Rei—well, Ara, Yellow, and Set were there too—and introducing him to my family.

That’s what they’d been doing.

……That said, Rei had actually visited the Dark Elf settlement, Marina’s hometown, and had also gone to the Bestir Empire, Vihera’s hometown, where he’d met her family—her father, brother, and sister.

In that sense, Elena was actually the one who was the furthest behind.

Even so, just having this time to spend with Rei was enough for Elena; she had no complaints.

“It’s fine, isn’t it? …That said, I don’t know where they sell jewelry, so we’ll have to look for it from there.”

“Uh, yeah. According to what the maid told me, street vendors sell all sorts of things from spring through fall…”

Saying that, Elena looked up at the sky.

Above her was a canopy of clouds that looked as if snow could fall at any moment.

It certainly wasn’t sunny.

No, since it’s winter now, even if it were sunny, there probably wouldn’t be any street vendors around.

That said, whether the sort of trinkets street vendors sell would actually suit beauties like Elena, Marina, and Vihera—women so stunning the term “peerless” truly fits them—was another matter entirely.

“In that case… ah, how about magic items?”

Magic items used as jewelry aren’t exactly rare, and since they’re magic items, Rei has some expertise in identifying them.

So it was only natural for Rei to think that buying one would be the solution.

Especially since he was thinking of giving them to his partymates, it would be preferable if they had some sort of effect.

It was undoubtedly a rather bland gift for the women he was close to—essentially his girlfriends—but from the perspective of someone in the thick of battle, it wasn’t a bad idea at all.

Perhaps Elena had no objection to Rei’s opinion, for unlike before, she now wore a genuinely happy smile.

After all, receiving a gift—especially a piece of jewelry—from a beloved partner is bound to be a joyous occasion.

“I’m… well… happy,”

Elena said this quietly, but clearly.

Her cheeks flushed slightly with a mix of joy and embarrassment.

Though they couldn’t make out the details of her conversation with Rei, the passersby who had been quietly watching the two of them talk were captivated by Elena’s expression, and one after another, they bumped into other pedestrians or collided with buildings.

Even so, those who bumped into things didn’t consider themselves unlucky.

Because they had been able to witness with their own eyes the extremely rare sight of Elena’s cheeks turning red.

In fact, if they’d had a camera, there’s no doubt this moment would have become a once-in-a-lifetime treasure.

...Setting aside the buildings, for the pedestrians who got bumped into, it was nothing but a nuisance.

Whether she was aware of what was happening around her or not...

In any case, seeing Elena smiling happily, Rei smiled contentedly himself and spoke up.

“Well then, shall we head straight to the shop that sells magic items? …Do you know where it is?”

In response to Rei’s question, Elena shook her head again.

“I’ve been there a few times, but I was traveling by carriage back then. I don’t know the area that well.”

“Ah… I see. Makes sense.”

Elena is the daughter of Duke Kerebel and goes by the nickname “Princess General.”

It’s only natural that it’s rare for her to walk around Anesis on her own two feet, as she is doing today.

After all, normally, taking a carriage directly to her destination is the usual course of action.

“Well then, I guess I’ll ask around and look for a magic item shop. If I know one exists, I won’t have to worry about it so much.”

In places like villages and small towns, there might not even be a magic item shop to begin with.

It would be a different story if she were looking for magic items used for ignition, but what Rei is searching for undoubtedly falls into the category of expensive magic items.

Under those circumstances, even if a shop were open in a village or small town, it simply wouldn’t be able to survive.

But this is Anesis, the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana.

There was bound to be a magic item shop of the kind Rei was looking for.
