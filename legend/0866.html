"Whoa! That's so obvious!"

The only room with a door on the first floor of the dungeon.

Sensing movement inside the room, he poked it with his spear shaft. Nothing happened, leading him to conclude there was no trap. The instant he stepped inside, he sensed an attack descending from above.

Anticipating this move, Rei leapt forward, kicking off the stone pavement to evade the opponent descending from above.

As if waiting for Rei, two small, dog-faced monsters positioned ahead thrust the spears they held.

Twisting his body, Rei dodged the spears thrust toward him. Closing the distance, he swung the spear he held in his hand in a horizontal slash.

What came back through the spear was the sensation of striking flesh and shattering bone. Then, the sound of the wooden shaft snapping.

Cursing under his breath at the sensation, he shifted his gaze to the left.

Rei had struck the monster on the right with his spear.

Since there were two monsters ahead, the one on the left should still be there.

But perhaps because Rei's lunge was faster than expected, that monster had completely lost sight of him.

The broken spearhead spun through the air, flying off in a random direction, but Rei paid it no mind whatsoever. He thrust the spear shaft that still existed in his hand straight into the head of the monster that was still there.

The shaft Rei held was made of wood, and since it had broken off midway, the broken end naturally had a sharp point.

That sharp point pierced the monster's head... specifically, its eye. Propelled by Rei's force, the spear's remains easily reached the creature's brain and destroyed it.

"Gyaaah!"

The monster, with the spear shaft's remains still lodged in its eye, let out a short scream before being blown away. It rolled across the ground, twitching several times and emitting a faint whimper before dying.

Without pausing to watch it die, he spun his foot on the stone pavement and activated the magic item, Nebula's Eye, equipped at his waist inside his dragon robe with his now-free right hand.

He flicked the arrowhead materialized in his right hand with his finger, sending it flying.

"Kyaaah!"

A scream echoed from behind Rei.

Even so, the arrowhead created by Nebula's Eye couldn't finish it off. Clutching his face and screaming, the opponent still managed to raise the spear he held.

Only now, finally able to observe the opponent's form clearly, Rei frowned slightly.

Of course. Before him stood a small monster with the face of a dog. Its height was about the same as a goblin's, or perhaps slightly larger.

Of course, Rei knew the name of that monster.

Though this was only Rei's second encounter with it, it was a monster as famous as goblins.

Rank E monster, Kobold.

That was the name of the monster currently in Rei's line of sight.

In terms of pure physical ability, it was only slightly superior to a goblin, but it was smarter than a goblin and, like now, attacked in coordinated groups.

They were also dexterous, wielding weapons with greater skill than goblins.

"Well, it's still just a Rank E monster, after all."

He swung the Death Scythe he'd drawn from Mistyling in a horizontal slash.

The sheer sharpness of that strike was evident in the fact that it took several seconds after the horizontal swing for the monster's torso to finally be cleaved in two.

"Their materials and proof-of-kill parts fetch a higher price than goblins... but still, compared to the effort of butchering them. If they were at least higher-rank monsters, we could use the meat for Set's food."

In the end, he didn't store the kobold. He simply left the corpse's remains behind.

Like goblins, slimes would likely handle the cleanup if left alone.

He swung the Death Scythe once to clear the blood, stowed it in Mistling, and drew a spear similar to the one he'd broken earlier.

He hadn't used the kobold's spear because it was simply too crude.

It might have been acceptable for throwing and discarding, but it was clearly unsuitable for regular spear use.

In the end, he decided the kobold spear wasn't entirely unusable for throwing and stowed it away in Mistyling.

With that settled, Rei looked around the room.

"So, the stairs... Ah, there they are."

A staircase leading down to the basement stood alone in the middle of the room.

Other than that, there wasn't much else; just a large, open room.

Well, except for the corpses of three kobolds lying about.

"I wish the ogres had stayed in a place like this too. ...Or maybe it was precisely because this place was so spacious that they went outside?"

Feeling puzzled, Rei headed straight for the stairs...

"Gyaa!"

"Gyo gyo gyaa!"

Such sounds came from just below the stairs, making him frown in annoyance.

"Again? Seriously, there are way too many goblins."

Sighing at how many goblins he'd encountered today, he readied his spear and kicked off the ground, aiming to catch the goblin off guard before it spotted him.

Rei rapidly closed the distance to the goblins.

But the two goblins seemed completely unaware of Rei's presence, peering down the stairs.

(Probably more lurking around somewhere.)

Thinking it might get slightly troublesome, he thrust his spear sharply, shattering one goblin's skull. Before that goblin collapsed onto the stone pavement, he pulled his spear back and thrust it out again, shattering the second goblin's skull.

Both goblins were killed instantly, yet another goblin emerged from the stairs, oblivious to the carnage, just as Rei had anticipated... only to have its skull crushed by Rei's next strike, meeting the same fate as the previous two.

He waited just in case, but no fourth or fifth goblin appeared.

"Well, that's that."

Watching the headless goblin corpse slide down the stairs, he scanned the room just to be sure.

Finding only the corpses of goblins and kobolds, and confirming there was absolutely nothing else of value, he descended the stairs, following the path of the goblins he'd just encountered.

"I'm pretty sure the second level was cave-like, right?"

Muttering to himself, he advanced down the stairs. Stepping over the goblin corpse to exit the small room, he found the cave-like scene Rei had described spread out before him.

Unlike the stone pavement of the first level, the ground here was dirt, littered with small pebbles everywhere.

From the ceiling, faintly glowing stalactites resembling icicles hung down, illuminating the otherwise dark passageway just enough to avoid being a problem for light.

However, the passage's width was barely any different from the first level, meaning there was little space for combat, just as Rei had gathered from Gilm.

"All in all, it's a nasty passageway. If we were tackling this as a party, we'd have few people available for combat. Well, maybe we could bring more archers... but that would probably mean running at a loss."

The monsters that appear are basically only low-rank ones.

Even the ogre Rei fought was clearly weaker than the ones he'd faced before.

Given that, it's doubtful they'll recoup the cost of arrows spent in combat.

With luck, arrows might be recoverable, but they could break on rocks, have their tips chipped, or become unusable from monster fluids – reliability is low.

"Thinking about it, my Nebula's Eye is handy since it can mass-produce those expensive arrowheads. ...Well, they do vanish quickly though."

He walked along the path, pondering the extremely difficult-to-use magic item at his waist.

If he had been an archer, perhaps the Nebula's Eye before modification would have been an incredibly useful thing.

...It was odd that he was only realizing this now, but the truth was, Rei had never intended to use a bow as a weapon in the first place.

If he had to attack with a bow, he'd rather throw the spear stored inside Mistyling.

That was his reasoning.

"A bow, huh. In my case, I have the Mistling, so even if I used one, I wouldn't have run out of arrows. ...Whether they hit or not is another matter."

Given his physical abilities and the extreme sharpness of his senses—even his sixth sense—he probably had a natural aptitude for bows and such... But once the Death Scythe appeared through his Beast Magic, not using it wasn't an option.

"Whoops, got lost in my thoughts there."

Hearing the monster's cry from the end of the straight path, Rei understood the enemy was approaching. He readied himself to launch his spear at any moment... but in that instant, he realized there was no need to deliberately draw near.

(Well, I was just thinking about spear throwing, so it's only natural I'd figure it out.)

Muttering to himself, he leaned the spear he'd been holding against the wall and pulled out the crude spear he'd just acquired from Mistyling.

(Never thought the spear I took from that kobold would get its chance so soon.)

Since it wouldn't matter if it broke anyway—it was literally disposable—Rei took the spear and assumed a stance... his sharp gaze fixed on the end of the passageway.

Even though the stalactites on the ceiling provided some light, it wasn't nearly as bright as daylight.

In that case, discerning the enemy's position relied not on sight, but on their very presence.

The approaching sounds were unmistakably monster cries, confirming they were definitely not adventurers. In that instant, he took a few paces, twisted his body to put power behind it, and hurled the spear.

The spear sliced through the air, flying straight ahead...

"Bwooom!"

That scream reached Rei's ears.

"That aura and cry... not a goblin. This is... Huh."

Recognizing the aura, he twisted his mouth into a smile, grabbed the spear leaning against the wall, and kicked off the ground.

Unlike the stone pavement of the first level, the second level's ground was made of dirt, with rocks scattered here and there, preventing Rei from fully utilizing his leg strength.

Even so, his speed was overwhelming compared to the average adventurer's running pace. Before long, he spotted a pig-faced monster crouched on the ground ahead in the passageway.

It was a Rank D monster, certainly not a high-rank creature, but its meat was quite delicious for a Rank D monster.

The meat obtained from the previous raid on the orc settlement still remained in decent quantities within Rei's misty ring.

It was meat that both Rei and Set enjoyed, and for that reason, he had been thinking it would be good to replenish their supply... But even as he thought this, the orc in their path, its abdomen pierced by the spear Rei had thrown, was still trying to stand up.

Near that orc stood another, staring blankly at its fallen comrade, seemingly unable to comprehend what had happened.

Its dazed expression didn't clear even when it heard Rei's approaching footsteps.

A thrust aimed at the throat of the gutted orc shattered its neck, sending its head tumbling to the ground. Only then did the second orc realize they were under attack. It raised the club in its hand, swinging it toward Rei.

But Rei wasn't about to wait for it. Swiftly, before the club could come down, he launched his spear.

Three times, almost simultaneously.

A flash of light pierced the orc's abdomen three times.

Though the thick fat and muscle protected the orc's body, it seemed unable to completely nullify the power of the spear launched by Rei's strength, and it collapsed to the ground.

Its limbs still twitched faintly, proof that the orc was not yet completely dead.

Blood flowed from the spear wounds, staining the earthen ground.

Watching this, Rei raised the spear in his hand... and in the next instant, delivered a single blow to the head, ending the orc's life.

"These orcs seem weaker than the ones I fought before...?"

Rei muttered, gazing at the corpses of the two orcs.

Though that might simply be because the current Rei was stronger than when he had fought orcs before.

But what truly concerned Rei wasn't the orcs' strength per se, but rather the quality of their meat.

Having finally replenished his supply of orc meat, it was only natural he hoped the taste wouldn't change, regardless of their strength.

Stowing both the fallen orc corpse and its weapon into Mistyling, he set off again.

Rei advanced down the single-track path.

Continuing straight ahead, he soon reached a point where the path split left and right.

The difference from the previous level was that this wasn't a T-junction, but a Y-junction.

"Now then, which way should I go..."

In the first layer, the passageways had ultimately surrounded the room with the stairs leading to the second layer, but that wouldn't be the case at this Y-shaped junction.

Thinking this, Rei followed his instinct and stepped onto the path leading to the right.
