After spending the night in the forest on the second floor of the dungeon, Rei and his companions were, as expected, exploring the area the next day.

Normally, sleeping outdoors doesn’t allow you to fully recover from fatigue. …In some cases, it’s not uncommon to actually feel more tired than before you went to sleep.

However, Rei and his group had a magic tent, and if they slept soundly inside it, their fatigue was naturally and completely restored.

Admittedly, Rei and Relieu slept on the sofa or the floor rather than in a bed… but even so, there’s no doubt it was far more comfortable than sleeping out in the open.

And so, Rei and the others pressed on through the forest in near-perfect condition.

“Grr!”

As they made their way through the forest, Set suddenly let out a cry.

Judging from the sound that an enemy was nearby, Rei and the others all prepared for battle... but even after a dozen or so seconds had passed, there was no sign of an enemy attacking from anywhere.

“What’s this… Set?”

Rei, holding the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, turned his gaze toward Set, who had let out the cry.

Feeling his gaze, Set eventually stepped to the front of the group and...

“Grrrrrrr!”

He let out a loud roar, and at the same time, five arrows made of earth materialized around Set.

It was Set’s Earth Arrow skill.

...That said, he’s still only at Level 1, so the number of earth arrows isn’t that high, and their power isn’t exactly overwhelming.

Still, in a situation like this, it worked perfectly fine.

Because the goal wasn’t to defeat the enemy…

“Gishaaaaaaah!”

Just as the Earth Arrow pierced the tree trunk, that sound echoed throughout the area.

And right next to where the Earth Arrow had struck, a face had appeared out of nowhere.

“Is that a Treant? …But did a Treant make a sound like that?”

Rei muttered, recalling the Treant they’d fought in the Trent Forest, but the Treant, having been struck first by the Earth Arrow, swung its massive branches wildly, seemingly unconcerned with Rei and the others’ attitude.

Then, in the next instant, the leaves attached to the Treant’s branches were launched.

"Leaf projectiles"... just hearing the words, it doesn’t sound like a particularly powerful attack.

But in reality, the released leaves rained down on Rei and the others like throwing daggers.

(No, not daggers—more like shuriken or chakrams, something like that?)

Watching the countless leaves veer off in random directions thanks to the wind barrier instantly conjured by Marina’s spirit magic, Rei recalled the throwing weapons he’d seen in manga back in Japan.

(Come to think of it, there aren’t that many throwing weapons in this world. Maybe I just haven’t seen many.)

The most commonly used are throwing daggers.

There are a few others, but the one Rei sees most often is probably the long needle used by Byune.

Rei’s Nebula Eyes might also be classified as a throwing weapon.

(Maybe I should try making some shuriken next time? Though, the only shuriken I know about are the ones I’ve seen in manga and such. Whether they’d actually be useful or not—well, I’d have to try them out for myself to find out.)

The shuriken Rei knows are the cross-shaped ones that come to mind immediately when you hear the word “shuriken.”

Whether shuriken like that are actually usable is something I’ll never know unless I actually try them out.

(If they work, they’d probably catch on. …I wonder.)

Even as Rei was thinking about shuriken, Treant’s attacks—now that Set had seen through his true form—didn’t stop.

He throws not only leaves, but also fruits with purple mottled patterns.

But whether they were leaves or fruits, they were deflected by the wind barrier Marina had created and crashed into a nearby tree.

The impact of hitting the tree trunk crushed the fruit... and as liquid oozed out of the crushed fruit, the tree trunk began to melt slightly.

“So the fruit’s filled with a corrosive liquid? What a nasty plant.”

Relieu said with genuine disgust as he looked at the tree, then, still holding his longsword, called out to Marina.

“Guild Master, please remove the wind barrier. That Treant isn’t particularly strong, but it’s a nuisance. I’ll take it out in one go.”

“Yes, I understand. …But I’m not the Guild Master anymore.”

“Ah… Sorry, Marina…-san.”

When Relieu first began to make a name for himself in Gilm, Marina had helped him out countless times.

Naturally, he felt indebted to her, so he simply couldn’t bring himself to address her by her first name.

...Actually, deep down, Relieu thought Rei was pretty impressive for calling Marina by her first name, but she didn’t let that show.

“Hehe, so that’s okay, then?”

Marina asked with a smile that seemed to say she could see right through Relieu’s feelings, and Relieu nodded silently, partly to hide his embarrassment.

Seeing this, Marina made a request to the wind spirit… and in the next instant, a portion of the wind barrier was lifted for just a moment.

The wind barrier was lifted for only an instant—less than a second—but for Relieu, that brief moment was enough.

Instantly breaking free from the wind barrier, Relieu closed the distance to Treant, his trusty longsword in hand.

Treant also noticed Relieu’s movements. Perhaps recognizing him as a threat, he began hurling fruit filled with corrosive liquid and sharp, razor-edged leaves one after another.

But Relieu dodged every single one of those attacks flying toward him.

What was truly astonishing was that, even while dodging, he did not slow his forward momentum in the slightest.

Moving forward without slowing down, he evades every attack using only his body movements, without even swinging his longsword.

To Treant, such a Relieu was nothing but a threat.

He increases the intensity of his attacks, but... for Relieu, dodging attacks of that caliber is no difficult task.

In the end, he even unleashed a strike with a branch wielded like a whip, but even that was effortlessly dodged by Relieu.

“Hah!”

In the end, not a single attack landed… On the contrary, without even managing to leave a scratch, he allowed Relieu to close in, and with a sharp battle cry, she swung her longsword.

With that single strike, the Treant—as thick as an adult’s torso—is severed… and eventually collapses to the ground.

Rather than repeatedly swinging an axe like a lumberjack to fell a tree, he severed the tree trunk with a single flash of his longsword.

It goes without saying just how much skill that requires.

...Though, to be honest, most of the people here could pull off something similar.

“Nice swordwork. It seems you’ve improved your skills again—that’s the best part.”

As she released the wind barrier, Marina said this to Relieu.

At Marina’s candid praise, Relieu looked slightly embarrassed but turned her gaze toward the Treant’s corpse.

“It felt a bit stronger than a regular Treant. Was it a rare or higher-tier variety?”

An ordinary Treant wouldn’t do something like hurl a fruit filled with corrosive liquid.

At Relieu’s follow-up remark, everyone fell silent.

Amidst this, Rei voiced his doubt.

“For a rare species, it didn’t seem that different from a regular Treant to me. I was surprised by that fruit filled with corrosive liquid, but really, that was the only thing. In terms of raw strength alone, it wasn’t that different from a regular Treant.”

“...But don’t you think that cornel alone is reason enough to classify it as a rare species?”

“Let’s decide the details after we take this Treant back to the guild and report on it. …Fortunately, we won’t have any trouble carrying it back.”

A dead Treant is, in a way, not much different from an ordinary tree.

In other words, it’s just a matter of carrying back a tree that’s been cut down at the trunk.

And from a dungeon on the side of a cliff, no less.

Under normal circumstances, that would be nearly impossible, but with Rei and her Misty Ring, it was a different story.

“Alright, let’s do that. Even if it’s not a rare species, a Treant is still worth a pretty penny.”

As you can see from the expansion work on Gilm, Treants are highly sought-after as building materials.

Even branches that can’t be used for construction are perfectly suitable as materials for wizards’ wands and magic items.

……That said, the treants in the forest near Gilm are different from ordinary treants, so they can’t be used as materials for wands or alchemy.

Seeing Rei stow the Treant’s corpse in the Misty Ring, Relieu was astonished all over again.

"To think you managed to bring in such a massive tree..."

But when he saw Relieu staring at him in astonishment, Rei—perhaps mistaking her gaze for an accusation that he had stolen the prey she had defeated—hurriedly spoke up.

“Don’t worry. I’ll make sure to give you the money for this Treant to Relieu.”

“Huh? Nah, I don’t really need the reward for the Treant. The reason I’m here with you this time is strictly because of Lord Daskar’s request… wait, I haven’t actually received any money, so maybe it’s more of a favor? Something like that. Besides, you’ve been treating me to some delicious meals.”

He didn’t say it out loud, but for Relieu, simply being able to stay with Set was a huge deal.

In fact, if he could keep acting alongside Set like this, he even thought he’d be happy to come as reinforcements for free as many times as it took.

...You could call him the epitome of a Set fan.

Then again, if you were a Set enthusiast like Milein or Johanna, you’d probably feel the same way as Relieu.

“Is that so? If you say so, that’s a relief for me, too. …Well then, let’s head to the next spot. I wonder what kind of monsters will show up next.”

Saying that, Rei and the others pressed on through the forest once more.

But the monsters that attacked them along the way were mostly ones they were already used to—goblins, kobolds, orcs, and the like.

Other than that, as one would expect in a forest, there were quite a few Treants as well.

They continued walking like that for several hours… Just as noon was approaching, a river came into view before Rei and the others.

That said, it wasn’t a particularly wide river—more of a small stream, really.

“Shall we take a break here?”

No one objected to Rei’s suggestion, so they decided to take their lunch break near the river.

Even in autumn, it wasn’t that cold during the day.

Even with the river nearby, they felt a refreshing breeze rather than the cold.

…Though, maybe that’s just because it’s a small stream.

“So, what’s for lunch? Breakfast was delicious today, so I’m really looking forward to lunch too.”

In response to Relieu’s cheerful question, Rei brought out a spicy soup packed with vegetables and fish caught from the sea, along with a large number of sandwiches and skewers of oak meat.

Of course, he hadn’t forgotten to prepare a large chunk of meat for Set either.

Just as they were enjoying a meal so lavish it was hard to believe they were eating inside a dungeon, Yellow suddenly took flight.

Uncertain why he’d suddenly taken flight, Rei and the others, who had been chatting, found their attention drawn to Yellow’s actions.

“Kyu, kyu-kyu!”

Yellow lands on a nearby tree branch and, letting out short chirps, begins working on something.

From below, the branches and leaves blocked the view, so it was impossible to tell what Yellow was doing, but after a few minutes, Yellow came down from the branch.

However, even though Yellow had flown up to the branch alone, when he came down, he was holding a squirrel with an enormous tail—one that was more than half the animal’s body length.

For a moment, Rei wondered if it had captured the squirrel as prey, but if that were the case, Yellow would have killed it immediately.

While not sharp enough to inflict a fatal wound on a human, Yellow’s claws and fangs were sharp.

With prey as small as that squirrel, killing it would have been a breeze.

The fact that he didn’t kill it meant Yellow hadn’t caught the squirrel to kill it, but for some other purpose.

“Even though we’re in a dungeon, it’s still a forest, so it’s not unusual to see animals… Oh, look, it’s injured.”

Just as Vihera said, the squirrel had wounds in several places on its body.

“Hmm, so did Yellow bring that squirrel here because he wanted to help it?”

“Squeak!”

In response to Elena’s words, Yellow let out a squeak to confirm she was right.

"What should we do? I have a fair number of potions. Should we use one?"

“No, for wounds like these, my spirit magic will be enough.”

As Rei reached into his Misty Ring to retrieve a potion, Marina spoke briefly and covered the squirrel’s wound with water spirit magic.

Gradually, the wound began to fade away… and the squirrel gazed curiously at the water enveloping its body.
