The room prepared by the inn was, should one say as Rei had expected, or beyond his expectations... In any case, it was a room more than spacious enough for this number of people to stay in.

They split into men's and women's quarters. The group consisted of three men: Rei, Elk, and Rodos; and five women: Elena, Marina, Ara, Min, and Vihera.

Though, Rodos and Vihera were both unconscious.

In any case, since there were fewer men, the room Rei and the others were staying in was smaller.

But "smaller" was only relative to the room Elena and the others were staying in; compared to the room at the Dusk Wheat inn, Rei's regular lodging in Gilm, it was nearly three times larger.

The furnishings inside the room were so obviously expensive that even Rei, lacking any particular aesthetic sense, could tell at a glance.

(What's the point of building such a luxurious inn near a dungeon? Can it even turn a profit? Well, obviously they think it can, or they wouldn't be running it like this.)

Beyond the room where the furnishings stood out, a separate bedroom was provided.

Other magic items existed for lighting and heating, and there was even one with a refrigerator-like effect.

If monsters were to spill out from the dungeon... Beyond that, since this place isn't walled like a town, monsters slipping in from who knows where wasn't uncommon.

If large numbers of such monsters gathered, this inn would naturally suffer damage.

It's unclear whether monsters would take valuables, and they might not even show interest in magic items.

But that's just wishful thinking. In reality, they'd likely take everything of value, and might even destroy magic items or expensive furniture just for fun.

Considering those risks, it was beyond belief for Rei that such a luxurious inn could be built in a place like this.

"Rhodos is asleep. What now?"

This room had two bedrooms.

Each had two beds, meaning there were four beds in total within this room.

Thanks to that, Rei and Elk didn't have to worry about sleeping on the sofa or... worst case, on the floor.

Though, being one of the finest rooms in a high-end inn, the floor was covered in a rug that looked undeniably warm.

"Marina said she was going to the guild's branch office, so I plan to go with her."

Elk needed to take the luggage he'd brought from the carriage to the bedroom, but Rei didn't need to do that since he had the Misty Ring.

Laying that explanation to Elk, who was asking while shooting him an envious look, Rei replied.

Elk thought for a moment after hearing Rei's words before opening his mouth.

"The guild, huh... Should I go too, then?"

"Hmm... I'm not sure. I suppose it would be better if you did."

The party tackling the dungeon this time consisted of women aside from Elk and Rei, making them prone to being underestimated.

And while Rei's skill was another matter, his appearance certainly didn't seem particularly intimidating.

Of course, Rei's name had spread far and wide thanks to his past exploits.

But while many know his name, few know his face.

Having Set, who could be called Rei's alter ego, along would prevent them from being underestimated, but since they couldn't join the guild anyway, it wouldn't matter this time.

Considering the possibility of being hassled by half-baked adventurers who can't even gauge Rei and the others' true strength, it's better to have an imposing companion like Elk along from the start to avoid trouble altogether.

Besides, even if they don't know Rei's face, many recognize Elk, given his extensive experience as a rank A adventurer.

Though, there are those who don't even know Elk's face... In such cases, Rei wouldn't have bothered trying to do anything about it.

"I see. Then I'll take you up on that. ...Should I ask the innkeeper to look after Rodos?"

"Probably. I imagine Vihera is being looked after in the room over there too. If you're worried, before you go out..."

Why not check with the inn staff?

Rei was about to say this when the sound of knocking interrupted him.

"Who is it?"

"Excuse me, we've come to attend to Lord Rodos at Lord Min's request."

Elk's question was met with that voice from beyond the door.

"...That's awfully early."

Elk flashed a strangely proud smile at Rei's muttered remark, which seemed to slip out involuntarily.

He was probably pleased to show Rei how flawless Min was.

"Well, Min's good at handling these kinds of things. It's quicker and more reliable to leave it to him than for me to think about it."

"That approach seems to come with its own set of problems, though."

"Um... May I come in?"

Perhaps growing impatient with Rei and Elk's conversation, an inn employee asked from beyond the door.

"Oh, sorry. Come in."

Elk glanced at Rei, asking with his eyes, "Is it okay?" But the fact he asked Rei after already giving permission probably showed Elk's personality.

The man who entered the room was a middle-aged man in his forties.

Upon confirming Rei and Elk's presence, he bowed deeply.

"Pleased to meet you. My name is Terom."

"Ah, we're heading out soon, so please take care of Rodos for us."

"Yes, I've already been informed of that by Lord Min, so it's no problem."

Though he said he'd take care of him, Rodos was unconscious.

For now, it should be fine to think of it as general care.

Seeing them off, Rei and Elk stepped outside the room.

Just then, Elena and the others were coming out of a room a short distance away.

"Looks like everything's fine over there too."

Rei nodded at Elena's confirming words.

"I see. So Marina is heading to the guild's branch office. But what about us?"

"Huh? Aren't we going to the Guild?"

Elk looked confused, unsure what Elena was talking about, but when Min raised the staff she was holding, he immediately fell silent.

He knew from past experience that if he said anything odd in this situation, that staff would come crashing down on his head.

...Even so, Elk now wore a delighted smile.

Ever since Rhodes lost consciousness... no, more accurately, since they started searching for a way to restore his consciousness and hadn't found anything, Min had rarely smiled or engaged in lighthearted exchanges like this lately.

Of course, Elk wasn't exactly enjoying getting hit with Min's staff either.

"If all of us go to the guild together, we'll definitely stand out. ...Well, even if we go in a small group, with this lineup, we'd probably still stand out."

"If it's the same either way, wouldn't it be better to go with everyone?"

Elk replied to Min's words, but fell silent again upon sensing her raise the staff once more.

"True. Maybe someone forgot to buy something for tomorrow. Going separately could work, right? I'm heading to the guild. Anyone coming with me?"

"Ah, well, Rei asked me to come with him to make sure he doesn't get hassled by some weirdo."

What do you say? Elk turned his gaze to Marina, who smiled.

Marina's smile, radiating feminine charm, was so beautiful that even Elk, who had a wife named Min, found himself momentarily captivated.

...Seeing Min's gaze near Marina grow slightly fixed, she snapped back to reality in a panic.

Unlike before, the hope that Rodos might regain consciousness had lightened his mood.

Considering the possibility of battle with the Silver Lion tomorrow, she normally wouldn't be able to relax... but being able to do so was probably unique to an A-rank adventurer.

"Well, with Elk around, no one's likely to bother us. I think that's fine too."

With Min's permission, Elk headed for the guild.

Marina smiled at Min and thanked him, then turned her gaze to Rei.

"So, will you come along too, Rei?"

"Yeah, I don't really have anything else to do, so sure."

"Hmm, then I suppose I'll come too. Leaving Marina and Rei alone together seems rather dangerous."

"...No, wait, I'm coming too, you know."

Elk muttered, feeling a slight shock at being left out of the count for some reason.

"Of course I know Elk is here, and it's better than letting those strange people get involved. But in this matter, Elk's presence isn't that significant."

"Yeah, yeah."

Elk responded to Elena's words with a look that was less exasperated and more like he couldn't be bothered to engage. He glanced around again before speaking.

"Well then, as for Ara and Min..."

"Of course, if Lady Elena is going, I'll go too."

"If everyone leaves this inn, it might cause problems if something happens. I'll stay behind. I want to properly recover from the journey's fatigue in preparation for tomorrow."

Being a Rank A adventurer, Min had more stamina than the average man.

But even so, traveling by carriage was bound to be tiring to some degree.

Moreover, Min was a woman.

In terms of sheer physical stamina, she inevitably fell short of Elk.

...Had they been traveling in Elena's carriage, she wouldn't have been this tired.

But no matter how expensive the carriage was, it was still just a regular carriage.

Elena seemed to understand this. She nodded in agreement with Min's words.

And so, in the end, everyone except Min headed to the guild.

As the group entered the guild, they naturally drew attention.

Those who had seen Rei and the others come to the dungeon before wondered, Why again?

For those unfamiliar with Rei and the others, they were eye-catching in various ways.

Naturally, some recognized famous faces like Elk, Elena, and Marina, wondering why they were in such a place.

Amidst this... one person reacted completely differently from everyone else.

"Huh? Rei!? Why are you here?"

Captain Rei. The voice that uttered those words naturally echoed throughout the guild.

Rei and the others' presence had largely silenced the other adventurers.

"Huh? Hey, what are you guys doing here? And it's not 'Captain,' is it?"

"Ah, sorry, I just... But if Rei-san is here, that means Set-chan is here too, right? Set-chan is outside the guild!?"

That person... Johanna did respect Rei as a figure worthy of admiration.

But at the same time... no, more than that, Johanna's feelings for Set were stronger.

The others besides Johanna also recognized Rei.

They were all members of the guerrilla squad Rei had led during the civil war in the Bestir Empire, and had come to Gilm with Rei after the war ended.

"Then, I'll just admire Set-chan..."

"Set isn't here."

He told Johanna, who seemed ready to dash outside at any moment.

Johanna froze, her face contorted into an expression that could only be called despair as she turned her gaze toward Rei.

"Why...? A captain without Set-chan is like a meatless skewer, isn't it?"

"...That's a bit of an exaggeration, don't you think?"

A meatless skewer... that's not even food anymore.

Though, to be fair, Rei kept quite a few skewers stored in his Misty Ring, using them as handy weapons when killing someone wasn't necessary.

"Ah, ahahaha!"

Realizing she'd gone a bit too far herself, Johanna snapped back to her senses and let out a dry laugh.

"W-Well, more importantly! Why are you here, Rei? And... um, bringing all these people with you?"

The ones accompanying Rei were individuals each possessing an undeniable presence.

Why was he here with such people?

It wasn't just Johanna; her companions were also directing the same questioning looks at Rei.

"Actually..."

「Rei」

Elena cut Rei off sharply as he was about to mention Vihera's name.

Rei nodded in response to Elena's words, understanding she'd rather not mention Vihera's name in front of the guild members watching.

"I know. ...But more than that, having you guys here was a stroke of luck for me too."

"Huh?"

"...I think I can trust Marina and Johanna. What do you think?"

Marina thought for a moment before nodding at Rei's question.

"I suppose so. If you say so, Rei. I was planning to introduce you, but... Anyway, I'll go talk to the guildmaster here. Could you guys talk to her for me?"

Johanna merely tilted her head at Rei and Marina's exchange.
