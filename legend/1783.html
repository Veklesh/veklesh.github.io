If you were told that the woman named Arcadia Kerebel, whom Rei is looking at, is Elena’s mother, everyone would surely believe it.

She undoubtedly possessed that level of beauty.

That said, if Elena is a large rose blooming in the sunlight, Arcadia gives Rei the impression of a rose that, while of the same species, blooms in the shade.

Even though they are both roses, Elena and Arcadia are vastly different.

If the two were completely dissimilar, that difference would likely have seemed trivial.

But precisely because their features were so similar, the differences made a strong impression.

“Please have a seat. My people were a bit rude, weren’t they?”

At Arcadia’s words, Rei nodded and sat down in a chair… and finally let out a sigh.

Just sitting down in a chair—that alone had left him mentally exhausted.

But that was only natural. The people here were the Duke of Kerebel’s family.

They are, after all, the family with the highest title and the strongest influence in the entire Kingdom of Mireana.

…In fact, it was rather strange that Rei—a mere adventurer, even if he did have a nickname—was even present here.

In any case, once he sat down with the food in front of him, the aroma of the dishes wafted over, stimulating his appetite, and the presentation was a feast for the eyes as well.

(I seem to recall seeing or hearing somewhere that first-class cuisine is a feast for the eyes as well… Is this what they meant?)

In this world of Elgin, the culture of cooking has developed to a certain extent, but compared to Japan… no, compared to Earth, it’s overwhelmingly inferior in terms of technique.

That’s only natural. On Earth, it’s commonplace to transport freshly harvested vegetables long distances while preserving their freshness, and fish can be frozen immediately after being caught to maintain its freshness and shipped far away—even to other countries.

In comparison, ingredients themselves are simply incredibly hard to come by in Elgin.

Of course, using magic or magical items can solve the problem to some extent… but wizards are few and far between to begin with, and while common magical items are one thing, those that enable freezing or refrigeration aren’t exactly affordable.

Given those circumstances, it was only natural that Rei was surprised when such a dish—visually stunning and clearly appetizing—appeared before him.

(Was using a new dish as bait a mistake?)

The reason he thought that for a moment was probably because he wanted to eat the meal in a more relaxed state, rather than under such tense circumstances.

You often hear stories about people being so nervous they couldn’t taste their food.

That’s precisely why it was only natural for him to want to savor the flavor of this delicious meal to the fullest.

To be honest, it would be more accurate to say that Rei had misjudged Kerebel Duke.

Or perhaps, in this case, I should say I had underestimated him.

She knew he was Elena’s father, and she knew he led a large number of nobles.

But even so, he’d assumed that, in the end, he’d still be inferior to Daskar.

Yet the man standing before Rei was undoubtedly Daskar’s equal… no, as someone belonging to Gilm and maintaining friendly relations with Daskar, Rei was reluctant to admit it, but as a nobleman, he was likely superior to Daskar.

Of course, Rei understood that this was merely a view of one aspect of him as a noble.

Take noblesse oblige, for example—the so-called “noble duty.” If a noble house head were to take the battlefield in the name of that duty, and one were to ask whether Duke Kerebel or Daskar would be more useful, the answer would likely be Daskar, who, as a former knight, was accustomed to combat.

Rei understood that, but even so, he couldn’t help but conclude that the man standing before him was a force to be reckoned with.

“Well then, shall we begin our meal? Rei… would you like some wine?”

In response to Kerebel Duke’s question, Rei shook his head.

However, it was Elena who spoke up about Rei’s attitude.

“Rei doesn’t seem to handle alcohol well, so he always drinks tea, water, or fruit juice. I’m sure it’s a shame for you, Father.”

“Hmm, I see. Still… I think fine food goes best with fine wine. What a pity.”

“Haha. It’s not that I can’t drink it, but I just don’t find alcohol—not just wine—tasty. If alcohol is used in cooking, though, the alcohol evaporates, so it’s not a problem.”

Rei had said this rather casually, but perhaps his words had caught the Duke’s attention.

Duke Kerebel turned his gaze toward Rei as he enjoyed the wine poured by his maid, along with a salad of ham and vegetables.

“I see, so you use alcohol in your cooking? Unfortunately, I’ve always been more of a food-eater than a cook, so I can’t really say, but do you actually do that?”

Surprised that fresh vegetables were available this winter, Rei nodded in response while savoring the ham and vegetable salad.

“Yes. I don’t know the details myself, but I believe it’s a cooking method where you pour high-proof alcohol over meat sizzling in a frying pan, light it on fire, and let the alcohol burn. That imparts a flavor to the ingredients being cooked…”

Flambé is a cooking method that’s quite well-known in Japan, too.

That’s why even Rei, who isn’t very knowledgeable about cooking, was familiar with the technique.

That said, while he knew the method, he only had a vague idea of exactly what effect flambé had.

“Hmm. …That’s an interesting cooking method. I’m not familiar with it, but would it be all right if I taught this to my chef later?”

“That’s fine, but… if it’s Georgima, it wouldn’t be surprising if she already knew this cooking method.”

“Oh? If you already know Georgima’s name… have you met her?”

Rei, who was savoring a soup resembling a purée made from a potato-like vegetable, interrupted the conversation between Rei and Duke Kerebel to ask.

Normally, this would be considered rude, but when Arcadia did it, strangely enough, it didn’t seem that way at all.

Duke Kerebel, too, seemed to have no particular thoughts about his wife’s attitude, as he made no move to reprimand her.

“Ah, yes. She probably heard that I’d introduced udon and a few other dishes, and took an interest.”

“I see. …Come to think of it, I did hear a similar story from a merchant who had come to the town where I was staying.”

“Staying?” Rei wondered for a moment, but decided it would probably be best not to ask about it here, so he kept it to himself.

Finding out what the wife of Duke Kerebel, leader of the Noble Faction, had been up to… well, it wouldn’t exactly be all harm and no benefit, but the downsides clearly seemed to outweigh the upsides.

“Georgima’s cooking is delicious, after all. But… today’s meal seems to have even more effort put into it than usual.”

While enjoying the soup, Elena looked at the food with a puzzled expression.

In fact, today’s meal wasn’t originally supposed to be this elaborate.

...If only Rei hadn’t said something like, “If you make me think it’s delicious from the bottom of my heart, I’ll teach you a new dish.”

“Hehe, you look like you know something. I hear you met Georgima. Did something happen then? Like, if you made a dish that really impressed Rei, he’d teach you something you’d never even imagined before?”

Faced with Arcadia’s elegant smile as she asked this, Rei froze for a moment, wondering if she had seen right through his thoughts.

But he quickly shook off his surprise, reached for the soup with his spoon, and spoke.

“That’s right. I have a wealth of culinary knowledge in my head from what I saw while living with my master. That said, since I can’t really cook myself, I’d basically explain what kind of dish it is, and then the cook would properly finish it off… that’s how it would work.”

“Oh, really? From what I’ve heard, you explain things with a solid understanding of the flavor, regardless of the actual cooking method.”

At those casual words from Arcadia, Rei’s movements froze for a moment.

Rei reflexively tried to look toward Elena, but he managed to force himself to stop the movement through sheer willpower.

He realized that if he did that now, it would serve as proof to back up Arcadia’s words.

(Kind but scary, huh. That’s exactly how she is.)

Rei found himself strongly convinced, belatedly, by the description of Arcadia he’d heard from the maid.

In terms of her demeanor, she was undoubtedly kind.

That much was certain, given that—despite his daughter, Elena, the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel and known by the nickname “Princess General,” having brought along an adventurer of unknown origin (albeit one with a nickname)—he hadn’t outright rejected the situation but was instead engaging with him like this.

Once again, Rei turned his gaze toward Arcadia.

What he saw in her eyes was neither admiration for her beauty, nor desire for a woman more mature than Elena, nor joy at her kindness… but rather, a sense of wariness toward someone he could not quite fathom.

That was precisely the kind of gaze Rei was usually on the receiving end of.

Of course, it wasn’t as if Rei had never directed such a gaze at anyone before.

People like the Rank S adventurers of the Bestir Empire and the Kingdom of Mireana, or the Grimm—a being that has existed since Zepair’s time and is rightly called a “Rich Lord.” The Silver Lion, the boss monster that guarded the dungeon’s core at the Altar of Succession.

Beyond that, it wasn’t as though she had never seen beings she couldn’t comprehend.

But… even so, it was completely unexpected to sense such a presence in Arcadia, who, in terms of direct combat power, was clearly inferior to even an ordinary soldier.

“Oh, is something wrong? The delicious food will get cold if you don’t eat it.”

“...You’re right. Every dish is truly delicious, and it would be a shame if it got cold.”

Saying that, Rei brought the spoonful of soup to her mouth.

The only potage Rei had ever had before was basically what she’d had back in Japan.

That included the potage served in elementary and middle school cafeterias, the instant potage made from soup mix, and the kind he’d bought from vending machines in winter—the kind where the corn kernels were so thick they barely came out of the spout.

What on earth were those potages?

The potage that had just entered Rei’s mouth was so delicious that he almost wanted to ask that very question.

“This is delicious.”

Just a few seconds ago, he’d felt a sense of awe toward Arcadia, but that feeling had vanished completely from Rei’s mind.

The rich flavor and sweetness of the vegetables spread through his mouth.

And yet, the way it slid smoothly down his throat made him feel like he could drink it endlessly.

Of course, this dish merely resembles potage; it probably isn’t potage at all.

But in Rei’s mind, the dish he was currently drinking had become, and was now firmly etched as, potage.

“I’m glad you enjoyed it. This soup is called Georgima Soup, and as the name suggests, it was created by Georgima. It apparently takes quite a bit of effort to make, so it’s not something you get to eat very often… but I suppose I’m paying tribute to your culinary knowledge, Rei.”

“Oh, you. That’s a bit too much of a glorification of Georgima, don’t you think? Knowing him, I’m sure he’s planning to make Rei rave about the taste so he can get her to teach him a new recipe.”

Saying this, Arcadia picked up a piece of freshly baked bread from the table—which, naturally, had been carefully selected and baked by Georgima—tore it into bite-sized pieces, and popped one into her mouth.

The mere act of Arcadia tearing the bread filled the air with its fragrant aroma.

Arcadia ate the bread—which looked soft enough just by looking at it—with a look of pure bliss on her face.

Starting with the soup and salad, several other dishes—which could be called light appetizers—were spread out on the table.

These were the kind of dishes that, under normal circumstances, would make one want to eat until they were full.

But… what’s spread out here is, after all, nothing more than appetizers.

The full-fledged main courses featuring meat or fish had not yet been served.

Rei wondered if he would be able to fully savor those dishes when they were served… and above all, he felt that such delicious food should ideally be enjoyed without any tension, together with familiar friends.
