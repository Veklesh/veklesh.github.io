The snow falls steadily, steadily.

It took a while for the snow to start falling, but once it did, it piled up in an instant.

Amidst such snowfall, Rei walked down the main street with Set.

Several days had passed since Tris, a merchant who seemed quite difficult for Rei to understand, had arrived.

Rei had thought Tris might show his face again around the next day, but Tris... and the merchants from the Spell Trading Company he belonged to never appeared.

(Honestly, the Spell Trading Company was late showing up to me. All the other merchants and trading companies had already made their appearances ages ago.)

A fair amount of time had passed since the news of Rei conquering the dungeon had been made public.

For a merchant who should be quick to seize an opportunity, it was undeniably late for him to appear before Rei.

The snow that had been falling since last night had already been packed down by the residents of Gilm, robbing him of the pleasure of walking on fresh powder.

Still, watching the falling snow and overcast sky, Rei walked toward his destination.

"Guruu, gururuu, gururururuu!"

The snowfall itself seemed to amuse him. Set ran around Rei's side, purring contentedly.

...And yet, he never once bumped into any of the other people walking around. That was quite the griffin's pride and joy, wasn't it?

(He's like a dog. A puppy, even.)

Given his size, calling Set a puppy was a stretch, but the way he chased the falling snow as he ran around had an undeniable cuteness that made the comparison seem fitting.

It seemed Rei wasn't the only one thinking this. Other pedestrians walking down the main street couldn't help but smile when they saw Set running about.

"Ah! Isn't that Rei-kun?! What are you doing here?! Playing in the snow with Set-chan?"

Rei, who had been walking down the main street with Set, turned his gaze toward the familiar voice... and his eyes widened beneath the hood of his Dragon Robe.

There was no mistaking whose voice it was; he'd heard it for years.

But the figure he saw wasn't the face he expected... It was someone bundled in layers of clothing, their face obscured by a hat and mask, making their features impossible to discern.

For a moment, Rei thought she might be mistaken, but even so, she understood whose voice it was and opened her mouth.

"Kenny...?"

The fact that Kenny hesitated, ending the sentence with a question mark, was likely due to that figure.

"What? Did you mistake me for someone else?"

"...No, it's not that I mistook you..."

In this case, it was probably more accurate to say he hadn't recognized Kenny as Kenny, but saying that honestly would clearly get him scolded by her.

Understanding this, Rei hedged his words.

"Hmm... Well, whatever. More importantly, what are you doing out in this cold, Rei? ...Huh? Your shoes are different?"

Kenny, sharp as ever when it came to Rei, quickly pointed it out.

"Yeah. I had Azimov reinforce my Sleipnir boots. So I'm wearing these ones while they're being worked on."

"Oh? ...Wait, are those the Silver Lion's?"

As the guild receptionist, Kenny naturally knew Rei had cleared the dungeon.

In fact, among those at Gilm, she was probably one of the most knowledgeable about Rei.

"Grrr?"

Who? Who? Set paced around Kenny.

He probably recognized Kenny by scent and voice, but couldn't identify Kenny by appearance alone.

"What? What's wrong, Set-chan?"

Kenny gently strokes Set with his gloved hand.

The touch finally made Set realize it was Kenny.

He let out a happy meow and rubbed his face against Kenny's.

"Hehe, what's up, Set-chan? You're being quite the cuddle bug today."

"Well, Set's always been friendly. ...But more importantly, what's up with you, Kenny? What about the guild today?"

"I start in the afternoon. Paperwork and stuff..."

Kenny started to say something else, but stopped mid-sentence.

Marina had resigned as Guildmaster, becoming an advisor and adventurer... no, considering which role she herself prioritized, perhaps it should be adventurer and advisor.

In any case, not many people knew Marina had stepped down as guildmaster.

Not many within the Gilm itself, and even fewer among the Gilm members.

Kenny knew because he often interacted with Rei alongside Lenora.

If she were to return as an adventurer, Marina would naturally become deeply involved with Lenora and Kenny.

Knowing this, he informed her beforehand.

Rei wondered if Kenny, with his lighthearted nature, might accidentally let something slip—unlike the serious Lenora. But judging by his current demeanor, that didn't seem likely.

"I see."

Rei implied he didn't need to finish the sentence, then turned his gaze back to Kenny.

Normally, Kenny wore the guild uniform with its wide-open chest, as if flaunting his physique. But right now, he looked like the very definition of "bulky in layers" – a description that couldn't fit anyone better.

(Well, he is a cat beastman, so maybe it's accurate in the sense of curling up by the kotatsu...)

Seeing Kenny's demeanor, so different from Set's obvious delight at the snow, made it impossible not to nod in agreement.

Typical Kenny, he thought.

"Hey, Rei, what's up? ...Hey, more importantly, wanna grab a break somewhere? I know this place that serves some really good sweets."

"Ah... Sorry. I was planning to head to the library."

Yes, Rei's goal for today was the library.

The materials for the Silver Lion, currently gathering dust in large quantities within Rei's Misty Ring.

He was researching to see if he could do something with them.

He had asked Vihera and Byune before leaving the inn, but both had declined.

Rei thought, "Well, I don't really have anything else to do right now..." but for the past little while, Byune had been receiving training from Vihera.

Hearing about Ambris and the Silver Lion, she probably realized she was clearly underpowered to stay with Rei and the others from now on.

In truth, what Rei and Vihera expected from Byune while traveling together was solely her skills as a thief; combat ability was secondary.

But Byune herself couldn't accept that.

...While the difference in strength between Rei and the others was one reason for Byune's actions, there was no doubt that Vihera's transformation under Ambris was another factor.

For Byune, Vihera was a figure who stood apart from everyone else.

Like Set is to Rei, or Ara is to Elena... so too is Vihera to Byune.

That's the kind of person she is.

But now, Vihera's eye color had changed, and upon further inquiry, it seemed there were other changes as well.

The most significant change, however, was likely her lifespan.

Grimm, who performed the ritual to restore Vihera's consciousness, informed her that her lifespan had extended to a length incomparable to that of humans. That is what she has been told.

If things continue like this, I'll be left behind.

Driven by that fear, Byune is training with Vihera to become even a little stronger.

Of course, it wasn't just Vihera; occasionally, Rei would also spar with her.

From Rei's perspective, Byune seemed to possess a certain talent for combat.

With her small frame moving swiftly, she rarely got hit by attacks.

This was likely because Byune herself had kept diving into dungeons with that body all this time. In terms of evading attacks, her ability was unimaginable for someone around ten years old.

However... while her small frame was an advantage for evading enemy attacks, it could become a fatal disadvantage when attacking enemies herself.

Even when she struck an opponent, she could tear the skin, but she couldn't sever bone, let alone completely cut through flesh.

Given Byune's physical capabilities, this was only natural. She simply lacked the strength.

There were people in the world who possessed astonishing strength despite their small stature.

Rei was a prime example of that.

He possesses strength utterly unbelievable from his appearance.

While not quite on Rei's level, there are many others, starting with dwarves, who possess strength beyond what their size would suggest.

However... Byune's constitution only allows her to possess strength commensurate with her appearance.

That's precisely why she targets vital points or uses long-needle weapons like hers.

(But targeting weak points is easier for the target to defend against. It'd be different if you had an attack like Vihera's Demon-Infused Palm that bypasses defense entirely.)

After all, what Byune is truly suited for isn't defeating enemies, but keeping them at bay and using her innate speed to toy with them.

That's what Rei thought, but he knew it was pointless to say anything since Byune was determined to defeat her opponents herself.

"Rei? What's wrong?"

"Ah, no. Nothing."

"Hmm... More importantly, the library, right? I can't believe you actually want to go to a place like that. If you're going to the library, I'd rather walk outside in this snow."

Kenny, being the guild receptionist, possessed a certain level of refinement.

He had visited the library many times before, but only because it was necessary for his job as a receptionist.

He couldn't imagine ever wanting to go to the library on his own initiative.

"I've never disliked reading books, to begin with."

"...You're quite unusual, aren't you?"

"Is that so? I'd think most wizards wouldn't dislike reading books. ...Well, everyone's different, so maybe there are some wizards who do dislike reading."

"Come to think of it, there were a few adventurers who liked reading books too."

"Right?"

Adventurers' literacy rates have never been low to begin with.

It stands to reason. If you can't read, you can't read the quests posted on the board, and you wouldn't even know the reward amount.

Worse still, if they took a job from someone with malicious intent, there was even the risk of having their reward stolen.

Therefore, at the very least, adventurers can read and write to some extent... words like reward amounts, slaying, gathering, and materials. Those who can read and write are by no means few.

Moreover, given that Gilm is in the frontier and attracts many skilled adventurers, its literacy rate could easily surpass that of the royal capital.

And naturally, with so many literate individuals, some inevitably find pleasure in reading books.

...Though, perhaps it's just the adventurer's nature. It's also true that many still prefer moving their bodies over reading books.

"Hmm, we still have a bit of time before heading to the guild. I'd have preferred to relax with you, Rei-kun..."

"Maybe next time, if we get the chance."

"You promise? ...Alright then, I'm heading to the guild. I'll pass the time by teasing Lenora or something."

"You'll get yelled at again, you know?"

Kenny teasing Lenora was a familiar sight at the guild.

Some people even went to the guild just to see the two of them interact.

"It's fine. That girl takes everything way too seriously. I have to tease her a little to lighten her mood. ...See you later."

Saying that, Kenny waved lightly and left, looking a little disappointed that she hadn't had more time to talk with Rei.

Rei watched Kenny go, then stroked the head of Set, who had approached.

The library and the guild were in the same direction from where Rei was now, so he could have gone with Kenny... but Kenny hadn't said anything, so Rei figured Kenny probably had something to do over there.

"Well then, shall we go too?"

"Grr... Grrrr?"

"Going?" Set asked, turning his gaze toward Rei. Together, they started walking down the main street again.

Several passersby watched Set, who seemed delighted, with gentle expressions.

There was no way anyone looking at Set now could think he was a vicious monster.

(I guess that just shows how much the residents of Gilm have taken to Set.)

That thought made Rei happy, and he couldn't stop a smile from appearing on his lips.
