Rei returned to the fourth basement level to find Vihera laughing happily as she fought the giants.

However, since Rei had managed to temporarily repel the new giant attempting to ascend the stairs, the number of giants on the fourth basement level wasn't particularly large.

The one Vihera was fighting now was one of the few remaining survivors.

"Ahaha, ahahahahaha! More, more! Keep fighting harder!"

"GRAAAAAAAAH!"

Amidst the frenzied barrage of giant attacks—punches and kicks powerful enough to fracture bones, or even kill outright with a single blow—Vihera dodged the strikes, anticipated their trajectory, and gently placed her hand on the fist to redirect its force.

This alone caused the giant, whose attacks were already powerful, to be thrown with a speed proportional to his own brute strength.

The giant's sheer weight became a formidable weapon, inflicting massive damage on its comrades.

Watching this battle, Rei noticed that the giants killed on this fourth underground floor showed no signs of reviving.

(Just as I thought. Apparently, to revive a giant, it needs to come into direct contact with that black snow.)

To Rei, that black crystal seemed like an extremely troublesome thing.

Above all, it was its ability to resurrect the dead that made it so.

Thinking that black crystal couldn't be brought out into the open so easily, Rei headed toward Elena and the others.

Few giants remained alive on the fourth underground floor; only Vihera was still fighting.

The other Elena members were no longer engaged in combat, merely watching Vihera's fight.

That's probably why they immediately noticed Rei approaching their side.

Elena smiled, and Marina waved lightly.

Byune was focused on petting Yellow.

"You're back. ...How was it? From what I can see here, there are clearly more giants than just the survivors from the fifth basement level."

"Yeah. Correct. There were almost no corpses on the fifth basement floor now. That black crystal somehow gathered corpses around itself, then sprinkled something like black snow over them, and the corpses came back to life. Even lost heads and such grew back."

"...What the hell is that?"

Marina muttered in stunned silence at Rei's words.

Marina had seen the black crystal on the fifth basement floor with her own eyes.

She knew it looked ominous, but she never imagined it could be this utterly beyond comprehension.

Moreover, even for Marina, a Dark Elf who had lived for centuries, she had never seen or heard of a magic item that could so easily resurrect people.

"Well, I'd like to know that too. If it's just a simple magic ore, that's problematic in itself... And even if it's a magic item created by altering some kind of magic ore, that naturally raises questions. But..."

Rei paused his words there, shifting his gaze back to Vihera, who was still battling the giant.

"It seems bringing someone back to life requires black snow to fall around the black crystal. So, as long as we stay away like this... I think we should be fine."

He didn't state it definitively, likely because he was watching the black crystal use some power to pull the giant's corpse closer to itself.

That power's range probably wasn't very wide.

That was Rei's assumption, but it was merely a guess, not a certainty.

Maybe... just maybe, it could even pull the giant's corpse from the fourth basement level.

(Though, there's no sign of that happening right now.)

As far as Rei could tell, none of the numerous corpses on the fourth basement level showed any signs of movement.

This suggests the black crystal can only draw objects within a certain proximity.

Or perhaps there are other conditions, like needing to be in the same space as the black crystal. At the very least, Rei hadn't figured that part out yet.

"So, to deal with the black crystal, we first need to pull all the giants from the fifth basement level down to this fourth level?"

Rei nodded at Elena's question.

"That's how it would be. Though, even without us dragging them here, the giants would probably come on their own. ...See?"

Rei looked toward the stairs, where a new giant was just appearing.

The giant Rei had killed while descending the stairs earlier was just one. The remaining giants had collided with the lower half of the giant Rei had killed and then descended the stairs... no, they had simply fallen down.

If anything, it took longer than Rei had imagined for them to reappear like this.

And just as the new Titan fully materialized on the fourth basement level, the Titan Vihera had been fighting sank into the ground.

(Or rather, "sank into blood" might be a more fitting expression?)

It must have been from the Magic Palm to the head. Watching the giant lie sprawled on the ground, covered in blood, blood streaming from its eyes, nose, ears, and mouth, Rei had that thought.

"Oh my, you're still here? How delightful! Please entertain me some more!"

Seeing the newly revealed giant, Vihera shrieked gleefully and began running toward it.

At first glance, she resembled a child who'd found a new toy... but the expression currently on Vihera's face was so lewd that most men would swallow hard at the sight.

"Well, I suppose there's no stopping her now."

Seeing Vihera like this, Marina murmured with a hint of exasperation.

Recently, she'd only experienced mock battles or encounters even less intense than that, so this life-or-death fight was likely a perfect opportunity for Vihera.

She had fought three giants on the third basement level, but the presence of a bed meant she hadn't been able to fight them to her fullest potential in a truly optimal state.

In that sense, this was undoubtedly the perfect opportunity.

For Rei and the others, stopping Vihera now was out of the question. She was so excited she might even attack them.

Of course, defeating the giants posed no problem for Rei and the others either.

"Well, what's the matter? Try harder. Come on, get up, get up, get up! Ahahahaha!"

Vihera's cheerful voice echoed through the fourth basement floor, but hearing it, Rei and the others only felt glad that Vihera's stress had been relieved.

...To anyone unaware, it was a voice that could easily inspire terror.

The fact that Vihera was truly worthy of being called a peerless beauty only amplified the terror.

But for Rei and the others, their relationship with Vihera was long and deep, for better or worse.

They understood that even if Vihera seemed to have lost herself in excitement, she could calm down instantly if she truly wanted to.

As proof, the last giant to ascend the stairs met its end when the Magic Palm struck its heart. Within seconds, the frenzy that had consumed it moments before faded.

"Looks like it's over,"

Elena murmured, watching Vihera survey the scattered giant corpses around them.

Everyone nodded at her words, and Rei and the others headed towards Vihera.

Vihera, too, must have noticed Rei and the others approaching while she was still savoring the aftermath of the battle.

He greeted Rei and the others with a smile.

"Good work."

"Well, in this case, I think that's our line,"

Rei retorted to Vihera, who murmured contentedly.

After all, it was Vihera who had defeated most of the giants that had come up from the fifth basement level, while the rest of the group had basically only provided light support.

Then again, since Rei had gone down to the fifth basement level to check the situation, it wasn't unreasonable for Vihera, who had witnessed that, to offer words of appreciation and encouragement.

"Heh, maybe so. But I didn't exactly do anything that difficult."

"No, it was definitely tough."

Marina couldn't help but interject at Vihera's words.

After all, she had single-handedly defeated a giant of considerable strength, even if it was just one.

Under normal circumstances, it would have been utterly impossible.

"Oh? Well, if I had to be greedy... I wish there had been half as many giants, but each one had been even stronger."

Their massive size made attacking them fun, but she would have preferred more tactical depth in the fight.

As Vihera continued speaking, the others nodded in agreement.

Though their agreement wasn't because they wished the giants had been stronger, but rather because the giants' combat relied solely on brute, instinctive force.

Being giants, they undoubtedly possessed physical strength incomparable to ordinary humans.

But it relied entirely on that brute strength, and its swings lacked any semblance of technique.

If the giants had properly mastered martial arts like humans use, they would undoubtedly have been stronger than they were now.

Vihera regretted that and probably wished he could have fought such a Titan.

Of course, that was purely Vihera's opinion. For the rest of us, having weaker enemies wasn't necessarily a bad thing... or so we thought.

"An opponent stronger than this giant... Well, who knows? But many more giants must have been born by the time we arrived. So, maybe, just maybe, there could be giants with combat skills like Vihera said."

Even as he said this, Rei had no real proof that giants with combat skills actually existed.

There was the fundamental question of whether it was even possible to teach human combat techniques to giants. And even if they could learn them, could giants use them effectively?

Size aside, physically, humans and giants aren't that different.

But when asked if that meant they could use the same combat techniques as humans, Rei couldn't help but tilt his head in doubt.

(Well, for now, it's probably best to let Vihera believe that. Who knows? Maybe we really will face such an opponent someday.)

Of course, Rei had no idea how powerful the giants in this underground facility were compared to other giants.

Was it a Titan born after the one previously here was shipped out... or was it one of the elite Titans left behind for guard duty?

Either possibility would change everything significantly.

"So, what do we do now? We need to deal with that black crystal first, right?"

"That's the plan. But... seeing that scene of giants being revived, I don't feel like casually touching it."

Some kind of power... From Rei's perspective, it didn't seem like magic at all, but rather a psychokinetic-like superpower, moving the giant's corpse close to him, then reviving it with black snow.

There was no way Rei could see that black crystal as a normal magic item.

"I understand how you feel, Rei, but we definitely have to do something about that black crystal, right?"

"I know. I know... but seeing that scene, it's just..."

What awaits if you casually touch that black crystal?

Thinking about it gave Rei an uneasy feeling.

Of course, it might be perfectly harmless if he actually touched it, but that didn't mean he wanted to try it right away.

"But if we just stay here... Byune, don't do it."

Vihera, who had been about to say something to Rei, instead cautioned Byune, who was touching the giant's corpse a short distance away.

But Byune seemed to ignore Vihera's words, continuing to touch the giant's corpse.

A few seconds passed. Just as Vihera was about to speak again, Byune pointed at the giant's corpse and opened her mouth.

"Huh!"

Rei and the others exchanged glances at Byune, whose attitude seemed to ask, "Aren't you taking the giant's corpse?"

If this were a normal monster, they wouldn't hesitate to take the corpse, using it for materials or eating the meat.

Even with humanoid monsters like ogres or cyclopes, that wouldn't change... but facing this giant's corpse, Rei simply couldn't bring himself to feel that way.

The fact that giants were born from humans was probably a major factor.

Even if it had been born through unnatural means, like black crystal or a slave collar... still, Rei simply couldn't bring himself to eat it.
