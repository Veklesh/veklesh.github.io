About an hour had passed since Elena and Ara arrived at the construction site where the extension work was underway.

It was lunchtime, and I was about to speak up... but naturally, Elena drew the attention of everyone around her.

It was only natural, given she was known as a figure who could be called the symbol of the Noble Faction.

And since they'd even witnessed Rei and Elena embracing, it was impossible for anyone not to notice.

Even so, no one dared approach Elena directly. Partly, everyone was holding back, waiting for someone else to speak first. But more significantly, they sensed something about Elena—an aura, perhaps, that commanded respect.

Of course, some adventurers in Gilm also radiated a similar presence.

Those with nicknames or high-ranking adventurers possessed that kind of air or aura to a greater or lesser extent.

Daskar, who ruled Gilm, was a prime example.

Anyway, while everyone hesitated to speak for these reasons, Rei and Marina left the scene with Elena.

"...I kinda wanted to talk to him too,"

Several people overheard Feilma, who was somewhat close to Rei, mutter with a hint of regret.

After leaving the construction site, Rei and the others ate lunch in a private room at a nearby restaurant.

Though it was a cafeteria, it wasn't the kind that prided itself on being cheap and fast. It was more of a high-end establishment where you could take your time eating.

Normally, both Rei and Marina would just grab something quick nearby.

Partly because the food sold at stalls and the like was delicious enough, but also because they preferred the casual act of eating.

The reason Rei and Marina were dining at such a high-end place today, of course, was because of Elena and Ara.

...Incidentally, Set and Yellow never showed up at this gathering.

Then again, skipping a single meal probably wouldn't kill either Set or Yellow.

Besides, if hungry, Set could hunt nearby monsters or animals to fill his stomach. So Rei... and Elena, who knew how close Set and Yellow were, weren't worried.

"Well then... To our long-awaited reunion, cheers!"

"Cheers!"

At Rei's words, Elena, Marina, and Ara lightly clinked the cups they each held.

Though they were toasting, Rei's cup contained chilled fruit water, and the other three had wine diluted with cold water.

Leaving aside the fact that Rei didn't care for alcohol, the others couldn't exactly drink like they would at dinner, considering what lay ahead that afternoon.

"Well, the official welcome party will be tonight, I suppose. ...Vihera will surely be delighted to see Elena's face."

"She'd be delighted, no doubt... but isn't that because a worthy opponent has appeared?"

Hearing Elena's words, who knew Vihera's fondness for combat, everyone listening couldn't help but agree.

Marina was quite skilled in combat as well, but she primarily relied on ranged attacks using bows and spirit magic.

In that regard, Elena, wielding the linked swords Mirage, is a good match for Vihera, who excels in close combat.

...In terms of combat synergy, Ara would be the best match, but unfortunately, Ara's raw skill is simply nowhere near Vihera's level.

"I suppose so. Rei does spar with Rei during morning training, but..."

Marina muttered, glancing at Rei who was already savoring the stewed orc meat. The subject of her remark was enjoying a large chunk of meat... yet still relishing the sensation of the fibers cutting cleanly.

Swallowing the meat in his mouth, Rei spoke.

"What I'm doing is strictly training, you know. Vihera can't go all out either, so I bet she's feeling a bit out of shape or dissatisfied, one way or another."

"Hmm, in that case, it seems I'll be the one fighting Vihera after all."

There was no particular annoyance in Elena's tone.

No, there was even a hint of lighthearted joy.

Originally, there were almost no opponents who could match Elena's strength. But after inheriting the Ancient Dragon's magic stone, she had climbed another step... or perhaps two steps, up the ladder of power. Finding a suitable sparring partner had become difficult.

Of course, it wasn't that there were none at all.

But it was undeniable that the number had dwindled compared to before.

Ara was fine for basic training, but when it came to sparring, it inevitably felt unsatisfying.

Ara, who excelled in combat relying on her immense strength, and Elena, who favored speed and skill, were simply too well-matched. ...Or rather, in this case, perhaps it should be described as too poorly matched.

In any case, for Elena, sparring with Vihera was something she actually welcomed.

Rei and the others had been talking about Vihera, who wasn't present for a while, but eventually the topic shifted.

"So Elena came to Gilm because of the Lerdact incident after all?"

"That's part of it. ...But more than that..."

Listening to Rei's words, Elena flushed slightly, her cheeks turning faintly red in displeasure.

Seeing Elena's attitude, Rei couldn't help but realize his words had been a mistake.

"Ah... Speaking of which, I wonder where Yellow went?"

"You were the one who said he was playing with Set, Rei."

Marina told Rei, who was muttering evasively, with an expression that was mostly exasperated.

"Rei-sama, I thought you'd grown a bit while I wasn't looking..."

Ara too muttered this, exasperated by Rei and Elena's exchange.

"...My bad."

In the end, all Rei could do was apologize honestly like that.

"Don't worry about it. Well, it's true I came to see you, and the matter with Lerdact is related, but that's not the only reason. It's also to reduce the mistrust between the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction, even if just a little."

"...I see."

While nodding at Elena's words, Rei also thought, Isn't that a bit difficult?

No, perhaps it would be more accurate to say that thought was stronger.

The Noble Faction was made up of people with incredibly high pride.

From Rei's perspective, many of them seemed to confuse pride with arrogance.

In any case, many such nobles would likely find the idea of building friendly relations with the Neutral Faction unappealing.

And if they went so far as to send Elena, the symbol of the Noble Faction and the one with the nickname "Princess General," to Gilm for that purpose, it was only natural that it would increase dissatisfaction rather than reduce distrust.

(Or rather, was that the goal?)

For Duke Kerebel, who led the Noble Faction, having a large force was welcome, but someone like Lerdact, prone to running wild based on his own arbitrary judgments, was nothing but a liability.

Wasn't the purpose to flush out such individuals? For a moment, Rei thought that, but in the end, he didn't voice the question.

"So, how long can Elena stay in Gilm?"

"Nothing definite has been decided, but it's expected to be a fairly long period... That said, if any major developments occur, we'll need to intervene."

"I see!"

Hearing Elena would be in Gilm for an extended period, a delighted voice escaped Rei's lips.

They'd been communicating quite frequently using the Paired Orbs, but actually meeting like this must mean a lot.

"Hmm... You seem quite pleased."

Seeing Rei's joy, Marina remarked with a hint of envy.

Since Marina was usually with Rei, she probably envied the joy of finally seeing him again after so long.

Though, for Elena, it was Marina and Vihera who were always with Rei she envied.

"Yeah. If you ask me if I'm happy or not, I am happy."

Hearing Ray openly say he was happy to see her, Elena beamed. Seeing this, Marina let out a small sigh.

"W-well... even so, Crimson Wings? That party name is so typical of Rei."

Elena, flustered, hurriedly changed the subject.

Seeing Elena like that, Marina's mood seemed to lift. She picked up a piece of dried fish from the plate, broke it apart, placed it on a piece of bread, and brought it to her mouth.

"Yeah, I think it's a good party too. ...If only Elena could have been part of that party, though."

"Heh heh, don't ask the impossible."

Elena's cheeks flushed with embarrassment, but Rei's words seemed to snap her back to reality. She offered a smile... a regretful smile, and said so.

Elena, too, wished she could join Rei and the others as an adventurer.

But in her current position, such a thing was impossible.

After all, the Princess General was a symbol of the Noble Faction.

But... someday.

That thought naturally existed within Elena.

Normally, it would be utterly impossible for a noble from the Noble Faction to act as an adventurer.

But Elena had inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone, extending her lifespan.

And the three of them—Rei, Marina, and Vihera—also possessed lifespans longer than ordinary humans.

...Only Byune was an ordinary human, but her membership in Crimson Wings was temporary to begin with, and it was certain she would leave the group before long.

Considering these circumstances, even if it wasn't feasible now, there was a strong possibility she could eventually function as a member of Crimson Wings.

"Speaking of which, where are Elena and Ara staying? It's not easy to find a room at any inn right now, is it?"

Due to the expansion of Gilm, a large influx of people had arrived. Most of the cheaper inns had no vacancies left, and the mid-range inns were similarly full.

Those who would normally stay at decent inns were being pushed out, forcing them to seek lodging at more upscale establishments.

Some resourceful folks had befriended Gilm residents and crashed at their homes... but naturally, such people weren't numerous.

Alternatively, if one had existing acquaintances in Gilm, staying at their house might be an option.

But for Elena, carelessly staying at a residence of the Noble Faction would undoubtedly lead to trouble in more ways than one.

It was obvious that requests to meet Elena would flood in.

"I've been told to stay at the lord's mansion."

"Oh, at Daskar's? ...Then why don't you come to my place?"

"...Marina's house?"

"Yes. I live on the edge of the noble district, and the house is rather large when it's just me."

Marina's house was probably smaller compared to other noble residences in the district.

But the other noble houses... no, mansions. Besides the master of the house, they have maids, butlers, and all sorts of servants living there... sometimes even private soldiers.

Compared to that, Marina's house only has Marina living there.

Marina manages the house herself using spirit magic, so she doesn't really need any extra help.

As a result, Marina's house had quite a few spare rooms.

At the very least, it's no problem for Ara and Elena to stay over.

After Marina explained the situation, Elena seemed to warm to the idea. She placed the bread she was holding on a plate and turned her gaze to Ara.

「Ara」

Understanding what Elena meant with just that one word was likely due to their long association.

"Yes, I don't think it's a problem. ...Actually, in terms of not burdening Lord Daskar, it might even be a relief."

"Ah... yes. Daskar is busy with all sorts of things."

Marina murmured thoughtfully, likely recalling the merchants gathered at the lord's manor when she'd visited for a minor errand a few days prior.

"Hmm, then there's no problem at all. After we finish eating, I'll go tell them you'll be staying at Marina's house. Oh, speaking of which, I came to Gilm by carriage. Is that okay?"

"Yes, we do have stables. ...But they haven't been used in a while, so we'll need to clean them out first."

Normally, this would require manpower, but Marina handled it in an instant with spirit magic.

(Come to think of it, could she clean the stables from here using spirit magic too?)

While eating the skewered meat, Rei suddenly thought that... but for some reason, Marina smiled and looked at him, so he clamped his mouth shut like a clam.
