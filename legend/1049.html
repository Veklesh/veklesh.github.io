"This... No way... How did this happen?"

As Rei and Marina, led by Ragdo, made their way toward the World Tree, Marina suddenly cried out in surprise when they drew near it.

Despite wearing a party dress that restricted her movement, Marina kept pace with Ragdo without falling behind.

Her figure radiated an imposing presence worthy of being called a seasoned adventurer.

"I don't see much difference compared to yesterday... Is it really noticeable from here?"

Rei muttered, observing Marina. Ragdo, running beside him, shook his head.

"No, normally one might only grasp it when standing before the World Tree itself. But in Marina-sama's case, her lineage is different from the start. She can sense the World Tree's presence even from here."

"...Bloodline, huh."

Marina's surname, Ariansa, flashed through Rei's mind.

He understood that having a surname meant Marina had some kind of background, and he assumed it was related to her being the granddaughter of Optis, the elder of this settlement.

As Rei pondered this, watching Marina's stern profile, they eventually reached the base of the World Tree.

It seemed some Dark Elves had already sensed the World Tree's disturbance. Several Dark Elves stood at its base.

One of them noticed Marina and the others running up.

"Lady Marina!"

Her gaze held a desperate pleading.

Why was the World Tree, which should have been fully healed after yesterday's infusion of magic, in such a state?

His eyes held pure confusion and unease.

To that dark elf, Marina, who had reached the base of the World Tree, smiled reassuringly and spoke.

"It's all right. From what I can see, it may be weakened, but it's definitely much stronger than yesterday, the day before, or even earlier, right?"

As she spoke, Marina herself wasn't convinced by her own words.

It was true that the World Tree was in better shape now than before.

But that was only because it was just yesterday and today.

If tomorrow or the day after came, it seemed certain it would grow weaker still.

Given that, they absolutely had to resolve the cause of the World Tree's weakening.

(To prevent the World Tree from withering... no, dying... in the worst case, if Rei were to pour magic into it every day, it might work... but that would essentially bind Rei to this settlement.)

If it came to that, Rei would likely leave this settlement unless something extraordinary happened.

It was clear from their past interactions that Rei hated being tied down to one place.

And if the Dark Elves of this settlement were to interfere with Rei's departure, this settlement would undoubtedly be engulfed in flames.

Marina, the Guildmaster, knew full well the extent of Rei's power.

After all, she possessed power worthy of the nickname Crimson.

Considering that, I absolutely didn't want the villagers to do anything reckless.

(Well, after all the commotion caused by that magic detection incident last time, I doubt they'd be so careless again.)

Feeling the gazes of those around her, Marina closed her eyes and gently reached out toward the World Tree's trunk.

Thump.

That was the first sensation that reached her.

She could feel magic flowing in a regular pattern, but it was undeniably weaker than yesterday.

(At this rate, it'll take two months... no, under certain circumstances, just one month to return to yesterday's state. But why? Yesterday, the World Tree definitely regained its strength. If that's the case, something must have happened... Yet Ragdo said no suspicious figures approached.)

Opening my closed eyes, I scanned my surroundings.

Nothing seemed particularly different, and there was no sense that any monsters or anything were hiding.

But considering the World Tree had only just recovered yesterday and is already this weakened, there must undoubtedly be some underlying cause.

"Lady Marina... How are you?"

One of the dark elves nearby asked Marina.

They must have assumed she had already assessed the World Tree's condition by opening her eyes.

"...It's definitely weakened. I don't know what happened, but if this continues, it won't be long before it runs out of magic power."

"But... yesterday it was so..."

The dark elf covered her mouth as if unable to believe it.

The others around her wore equally shocked expressions.

Having witnessed the World Tree's recovery yesterday with their own eyes, the dark elves were struck by an even deeper sorrow.

Raise them up, then bring them down.

That was exactly what had happened.

Watching his comrades from a short distance away, Ragdo judged that continuing like this was dangerous.

The World Tree, their emotional anchor, had supposedly finished healing, yet it was weakening again. Having believed it had recovered, they were undoubtedly being struck by an even stronger despair.

Thinking it through, Ragdo wondered if this had been the enemy's plan all along.

(Enemy... Yes, I suppose we should call them that. If the World Tree were merely weakened, it should have recovered by yesterday thanks to Rei's magic. Yet, the fact that it has weakened again in this situation can only mean one thing: someone is deliberately doing this.)

Ragdo turned his gaze toward the World Tree's trunk and grimaced sadly.

Like other Dark Elves, Ragdo held deep feelings for the World Tree.

You could even say he cherished it.

That's precisely why he couldn't possibly forgive whoever was messing with it.

(No, now is not the time to dwell on my own thoughts. I must prevent everyone else from becoming unsettled.)

Scanning the area, he opened his mouth to address the many Dark Elves wearing sorrowful expressions.

"Everyone, there's no need to worry."

The sudden voice echoing through the air drew the dark elves' gazes.

There stood Ragdo.

Though Ragdo had greatly damaged his reputation with his exchange with Marina... or more accurately, with Rei, his words still reached the ears of everyone present.

"After all, you all saw... well, not exactly saw, but you know that Rei treated the World Tree yesterday, right? If that's the case, having Rei treat it again won't cause any harm to the World Tree."

Those words were the last thing Marina wanted to hear.

As if setting the stage for what could be called the worst possible outcome for Marina, the dark elves turned their hopeful gazes toward Rei.

Before one of them could speak, Marina opened her mouth.

"Wait a moment. I understand Ragdo's suggestion of having Rei treat the World Tree, but there's a slight problem with that."

The word "problem" from Marina's mouth made the other Dark Elves look at her questioningly.

Ragdo, who had steered the conversation to this point, also turned a questioning gaze toward Marina.

It was only natural. If there was a problem with Rei restoring the World Tree, it meant there was a high chance the World Tree would wither away completely if nothing changed.

"Understand? Rei did indeed treat the World Tree yesterday. That is an indisputable fact."

"Then..."

Before one of the dark elf men could speak, Marina cut him off and continued.

"Hear me out, okay? Rei used his magic to fully heal the World Tree in one go. But that act itself must have placed a significant burden on the tree. It was already weakened, and then it received enough magic to heal completely all at once. If it happened just once yesterday, that might be manageable, but what do you think would happen if it were done repeatedly?"

"That would mean... the World Tree would be healed..."

"Hold on."

Another man who had been listening to Marina and the dark elf man spoke up, as if he'd noticed something.

"So, repeatedly going from weakened to fully healed means it places an enormous burden on the World Tree?"

Marina's eyes lit up with delight at the man's words, and she flashed a radiant smile.

Despite the gravity of the moment, the man found himself momentarily captivated by Marina's smile before snapping back to reality and speaking up.

"But if it's a burden, that can't be good for the World Tree, right?"

"That's right. Remember how those who can sense magic reacted when they saw Rei? Pouring that much magic into the World Tree all at once? Once or twice might be manageable, but doing it repeatedly could ultimately lead to the worst possible outcome for the World Tree."

"Overwatering a tree can kill it too..."

Marina nodded in response to the muttered voice of another Dark Elf.

"Yes, that's another problem. Rei's magic power is abnormal in both quantity and quality compared to ordinary people... no, even compared to us Dark Elves. I've said it before, but once or twice is one thing, but pouring that much magic power into it over and over..."

Worst case, the World Tree will wither from magic overload before it even weakens.

Marina implied this, and the dark elves around her wore grim expressions.

Ragdo, observing this exchange, shot Marina a stern look without uttering a word.

What good would it do to demoralize everyone here?

Ragdo, too, was well aware of the problem with repeatedly having Rei heal the World Tree.

But... even so, giving everyone hope was necessary right now.

It was Marina who had interfered with that, and it was difficult to suppress his inner irritation.

(What are you trying to do?! If you get everyone down here, it'll spread throughout the entire settlement!)

Though she hadn't clearly sensed Ragdo's intent through his gaze, Marina still vaguely understood what he meant.

"I'm almost certain someone, somewhere, is doing something to the World Tree. That means we need to deal with that before treating the World Tree itself. That way, the World Tree won't weaken any further, right?"

"...But who's messing with the World Tree, and how? A lot of people were having parties around here yesterday. If someone did something, we would've noticed from the start."

"Yes, I heard about that from Ragdo. But... even so, we have to find the culprit messing with the World Tree, right? Otherwise, the World Tree will just keep getting weaker and weaker."

Marina's statement was an undeniable fact.

If we can't find the cause weakening the World Tree here, it will eventually succumb to its illness.

Once that happened, the barrier the World Tree maintained would naturally vanish too.

That must be avoided at all costs.

For the very survival of this settlement... and above all, to save the World Tree, which was also their symbolic presence.

"But..."

The man's words lacked confidence, likely because he had no clue who might be doing something to the World Tree.

"I don't think anyone in this settlement knows, but Rei is a famous adventurer. She's active as a Rank B adventurer at such a young age, and she even has a nickname."

The dark elves in this settlement knew about the nickname too.

They'd heard stories from the few merchants who traded with the settlement, brought along as escorts by adventurers. And within the settlement itself, there were a handful who, like Marina, had once been active as adventurers.

"A nickname...?"

Someone murmured, and the sound echoed around them.

And so, everyone turned their gaze toward Rei.

But there was no suspicion in those gazes.

Those with the ability to sense magic knew exactly how much power Rei possessed. Even those without such ability had seen with their own eyes how he had healed the World Tree in the blink of an eye—a tree whose disease progression they themselves could barely slow.

...To be precise, they hadn't witnessed the actual healing process; they had seen the World Tree after it had been healed.

For that very reason, when it was said that Rei was an adventurer with a nickname, almost everyone accepted it.

Part of it was probably because, at this moment, they desperately wanted something to hope for.

Rei, who had drawn the gazes of everyone around him, gave a small nod and opened his mouth.

"The World Tree's recovery was the job I was commissioned to do. Given that, I promise to cooperate to the utmost extent possible."

His confident words strangely resonated within the hearts of the dark elves present.
