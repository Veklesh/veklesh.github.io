“...Hey, give me a break, will you? You tried to break down the mud wall and failed. Not only that, but you couldn’t even get rid of that useless red cloth—it’s just trash. And on top of that, you say you lost track of that guy we hired, Grange?”

Hearing those words delivered in a matter-of-fact tone, the man who had come to report stiffened with tension and fear.

If he didn’t say something, he had no idea what his boss standing right in front of him might do.

But at the same time, if he said the wrong thing, he might end up facing the worst possible outcome.

“Hey, listen. If you’re that useless, there’s no point in keeping you around, is there? I could just replace you with someone else. Of course, you’ll have to face the appropriate punishment first.”

“W-wait a minute!”

I have to say something. But I can’t say the wrong thing.

That’s what the man was thinking, but realizing he would undoubtedly be punished if he stayed silent, he hurriedly opened his mouth.

Having previously witnessed a colleague have his fingernails—and toenails—ripped off in front of him in the name of “punishment,” he absolutely did not want to suffer such a fate himself.

“What is it? Do you have something to say?”

“Yes. Um, well…”

He interjected as if just to fill the silence, but he hadn’t spoken because he actually had something to say.

Thinking that things were looking bad, and feeling my boss’s gaze gradually grow sharper… I suddenly remembered there was one thing I should have mentioned right then and there.

“Regarding the building owned by the Viscount Cobus family, it is currently being monitored by Gilm’s guards.”

“...I see.”

His piercing gaze softened slightly.

That said, it was clear that this alone wasn’t enough to truly save me.

The problem was how to frame the situation to pique my boss’s interest… and make it seem beneficial to him.

“Given that this happened just a day after yesterday, I suppose it’s safe to assume the leak came from Grange. There were about ten people using that location to issue orders, but the most suspicious one is, of course…”

“Grange, huh. He’s a capable guy, particularly skilled at covert operations… wasn’t he? I recall receiving a report before that he was quite capable, but for someone like that, he betrayed us rather easily. Or did he focus solely on his skills and never consider the possibility of selling us out?”

“That… I’m afraid I don’t know. However, the guards haven’t realized that we’ve noticed we’re being watched. If we use that to our advantage, we can inflict some damage on them.”

He flinched slightly.

At the mention of inflicting casualties on the guards, the man in charge paused slightly.

Then, with a smile but without saying a word, he urged me to continue with just a glance.

“Gilm’s guards are famous for being elite. …Well, since they often have to deal with adventurers in Gilm, that’s only natural… but precisely because of that, it’s not easy to replace them.”

“Well, I suppose so. …To be honest, even among the Mireana Kingdom, the Gilm guards are among the very best.”

That is an indisputable fact.

The Gilm guards, who attract skilled adventurers, are undoubtedly a notch or two stronger than the guards of other towns, villages… or even cities.

Reducing the number of those guards was not part of the man’s job description.

However, if doing so would ultimately benefit him and those behind him, there was merit in listening to his subordinate’s proposal.

Feeling his earlier irritation subside just a little, he considered the forces currently at his disposal.

(There should still be some surplus of the Red Cloth left. I kept them because the quality of their souls is poor, but they should be sufficient as disposable soldiers.)

In his mind, he quickly calculated the pieces he could deploy as combat power.

There were others besides the Red Cloth who could be considered combatants, but he judged that now was not the time to use them.

That is precisely why his first thought was to use the Red Cloth men—forces that could be expendable without issue… no, rather, disposable forces that should be disposed of—as the perfect disposable assets.

Inflicting damage on Gilm’s guards aligned with the men’s objectives, but that didn’t mean they should expend their primary forces—the ones meant for a real emergency—just to do it.

Above all, given how strong Gilm’s guards are, there’s no doubt that attempting such a thing would deplete our own forces.

(That said, if the guards capture the Red Cloth men, our own intelligence might leak out. …Well, those scumbags know almost nothing about our side, so strictly speaking, it’s not a problem… Still, just to be on the safe side, I want to make sure we have a plan in place to dispose of them if they prove useless.)

Quickly gathering his thoughts, the man spoke to the subordinate standing before him, who was struggling not to let his anxiety show on his face.

“Understood. Do as you see fit. We should still have some of the Red Cloth left over, so use that.”

Not “there were some,” but “there was some.”

Those words alone made it clear how the man viewed the red cloth.

That said, the subordinate didn’t care at all about how the Red Cloth was being treated; on the contrary, he didn’t want to risk saying something that might draw his boss’s anger toward him.

In that case, it was safer for the subordinate to treat the Red Cloth as a disposable combat asset.

After all, the subordinate shared his superior’s view that the red cloth possessed nothing but an inferior soul unfit for ritual use.

Considering the hassle of arranging food and drink for such a being every day, it would be less trouble if he just disappeared.

“Yes, I’ll do that. …If I could use a slave collar or something, I wouldn’t have to worry about information leaks, though.”

“Don’t be ridiculous. Slave collars are actually quite secure. Of course, if you really wanted to get your hands on one, you could… but it’d be a waste to use them on mere Red Cloths.”

To the man in charge, slave collars held far greater value than the Red Cloth members.

It wasn’t that he didn’t have the leeway to make such a wasteful expenditure here… but losing the slave collars at the same time as the Red Cloth lot would be nothing but a major disadvantage.

Originally, he had accepted his subordinate’s proposal with the idea that, while disposing of the useless trash known as the Red Cloth, it would be a bonus if they could inflict even a little damage on the guards.

The option of throwing away the relatively expensive slave collars just to do that simply didn’t exist for him.

“I see. Then, let’s proceed that way.”

With that, the subordinate hurried out of the room.

Although his boss’s mood had improved somewhat, he understood that his superior was still dissatisfied with the current situation.

That was precisely why he wanted to escape as quickly as possible from a situation where, if he remained in the room with his superior, he might be harmed for some reason.

Above all, his boss possessed strength far beyond his own, and the subordinate understood that.

If his boss were to decide on a whim to kill him, there was no doubt he would die without even knowing why.

……That said, given my boss’s personality, it wouldn’t be wrong to say that being killed without my knowledge would actually be a merciful act.

“Hmph.”

The man snorted at the sight of his subordinate hurrying out of the room.

Of course, the man understood exactly what his subordinate was thinking.

He understood… and while it might make him feel slightly annoyed, he wouldn’t go so far as to do anything about it.

For them right now, the number of pawns they could use was by no means large.

To reduce those numbers through a petty tantrum for no particular reason was simply not an option for him.

...To put it another way, it simply meant that if there were a reason, he would certainly act that way.

“Damage to Gilm’s guards, huh? …I wonder just how much damage a bunch of red rags could actually do. Well, I suppose it’s better than doing nothing.”

Muttering to himself, feeling irritated by the situation not going his way… the man reached for the glass of liquor on the table.

“Everyone, line up properly! If anyone cuts in line, they’ll go without soup!”

The butchering of a Giganto Turtle was taking place right next to Gilm.

It was currently lunchtime at the site… and Rei was handing out soup he’d bought at the cafeteria.

That said, it wasn’t Rei who was handing out the soup, but a guild staff member.

Rei was serving the soup, and for those who hadn’t brought any tableware… while many had their soup poured into cups, for those who hadn’t even brought that, Rei was pouring the soup into cheap cups he’d bought on the spot at a nearby store.

(It would be really convenient if we had paper cups or something like that... Come to think of it, how are paper cups made? Is it just a matter of stacking several sheets of paper thickly on top of each other?)

Thinking such things, Rei also sat a short distance away and drank the soup he’d served himself.

Paper cups were commonly used back when I was in Japan.

But if you asked me if I knew how they were made, the answer would be no.

After all, even with everyday items like TVs, I could use them, but there’s no way I understood their inner workings.

“This is delicious, isn’t it?”

Vihera, who was drinking the soup with Rei, murmured thoughtfully.

Her cheerful expression was likely due to the battle with the Snow Ape that morning.

Although they were a lower-tier opponent than a Snow Cyclops, the Snow Monkeys, who coordinated well with their comrades, seemed to be a more than satisfactory opponent for Vihera.

“Mm.”

Byune also sipped her soup, murmuring softly in response to Vihera’s words.

The soup, packed with ingredients, probably tastes even better precisely because they’re working in the cold.

In reality, since they’ve been fighting Kobolds and butchering a Giganto Turtle—activities that require quite a bit of physical exertion—they aren’t actually shivering from the cold that much.

In fact, some of the workers were even breaking a sweat.

...Amidst all that, the ones actually shivering from the cold are the guild staff.

The guild staff were giving instructions—watching to make sure the butchers weren’t stealing ingredients or meat, or alerting the guards to approaching monsters—but they weren’t actually moving their bodies.

That’s exactly why their bodies don’t warm up—they just stay cold.

They’ve brought along a magical item for heating, but it only warms a palm-sized piece of cloth—it’s nowhere near enough to warm their entire bodies.

Yet, without complaining, he’s distributing soup to the workers first… I suppose it’s because he has the pride of a guild official.

(If the soup runs out, maybe I should give them a different kind.)

Watching the guild staff handing out soup, Rei considers which kind to give.

Rei had gone around looking at things and shopping with Set, and naturally, the soup he had wasn’t limited to what the guild staff were currently handing out; he’d bought various other kinds as well.

“Hey, Rei. Did anything come of it this morning?”

Vihera asks Rei as she eats her soup.

Even as she asked, she could easily tell from Rei’s expression that there hadn’t been any significant progress.

Still, she asked Rei anyway, driven by the thought that maybe, just maybe, something had actually happened.

……In the end, Rei shook his head, effectively denying that possibility.

“Nothing much happened. Besides, if there had been any progress, the guild would’ve contacted us by now. Since that didn’t happen, we can’t really expect anything on that front.”

“I figured as much. …But then, what are you going to do this afternoon? Go check the earthen wall again?”

“Ah… no. I’m thinking of going with Marina to look for the Meru. It’s a hassle to go check the earthen wall every time, so I figured I might as well use spirit magic to have it notify us if anything unusual happens.”

At Rei’s words, both Vihera and Byune nodded in agreement.

Considering that they ate at Marina’s house almost every day, they understood just how extraordinary her spirit magic was.

They thought to themselves that Marina would likely be able to do exactly what Rei was suggesting with ease.
