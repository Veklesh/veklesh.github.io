Remuria turned her gaze toward him, as if to make a request.

Rei had no idea what kind of favor it was, but since Remuria—Duke Fuldar—had gone so far as to speak to him, it was clear that it wasn’t a simple request.

“What is this request?”

Rei was about to ask, but before he could, Azuel spoke up.

“Lady Remuria, I’m sorry, but I think it would be best if we didn’t hear this, so we’ll take our leave now.”

“Oh, is that so? There’s no need to worry about it that much.”

“No, there are others we need to greet, so we must go see them as well.”

“Hmm, is that so? Well, I suppose I can’t force you to stay then.”

“Well then, we’ll be going now.”

“Goodbye.”

Though that parting remark lingered in his ears, Azuel didn’t seem to mind it; he exchanged greetings and, taking Count Fanrail—who had also bowed—by the arm, left the scene.

……Brewitt, however, remained behind, perhaps because he was concerned about Lemuria’s request.

“So? Is it okay for Brewitt to stay? If he stays here, there’s a chance he might get dragged into trouble, depending on the situation.”

“True. But it’s kind of interesting, you know?”

“It’s not something to worry about that much, you know?”

Remuria said this in response to Rei and Brewitt’s words, but Elena smiled at her and spoke up.

“Duke Fuldar, people like this won’t listen to us no matter what we say. Since staying here won’t do any good, I hope you’ll grant our request.”

“……But you’re still here, aren’t you?”

“I’m Rei’s partner. Since Duke Fuldar said he had a request for Rei, I can’t leave here—neither as Rei’s partner nor as a member of the Kerebel Household.”

Elena stated this matter-of-factly, a smile playing at the corners of her mouth.

She must have been pleased to be able to declare so boldly that she was Rei’s partner—rather than simply a member of the Kerebel Duke’s House.

“Ho ho ho. Young people are full of energy, aren’t they?”

Remuria watched Elena’s demeanor with a fond smile.

It was as if she were reminiscing about the past.

But for Brewitt, who was forced to listen to such lovey-dovey talk, there was no way he could take it seriously.

With an expression that seemed to say “This is a pain,” he ran a hand through his hair and turned his gaze toward Remuria.

“So, what exactly is this favor you’re asking for?”

“Huh? Now that you mention it, that’s right. Since that’s why I came, I can’t very well avoid talking about the favor.”

Remuria seemed delighted, but her gaze suddenly shifted to Rei.

In that gaze lay an unmistakably sincere... yet intense emotion, as if she were seeking something from the very depths of her soul.

It was an emotion so intense that it seemed entirely out of place coming from an old woman like Remuria.

“So? What exactly is your request?”

“Hmm. …Rei, what I wish to ask of you concerns your familiar, the griffin.”

“……Set’s?”

Perhaps he hadn’t expected Set’s name to come up here; a look of surprise at the unexpected remark flashed across Rei’s face.

The same went for Elena and Brewitt, who had been listening to the conversation.

In fact, even among the nobles who had been watching from a short distance away, some froze the moment they heard the word “Rei.”

Within the Noble Faction, the mention of “Set”—who was currently inside this mansion, or more precisely, the stables—was a taboo subject.

Whether he intended to seize that Set or harm him, Count Caseres, who had acted so rashly, had been subjected to the severe punishment of expulsion from the Noble Faction.

Given that, if anyone were to rashly try to lay a hand on Set under the current circumstances, it could lead to the worst possible outcome—not just for themselves, but for the entire House as well.

And yet, to bring it up so openly at a party like this? It’s no wonder those who overheard the conversation would think that.

(In that sense, Azuel and the others were probably wise. Even though they didn’t come here specifically to ask, judging by the reactions of those who overheard our conversation...)

The nobles surrounding Rei and the others were likely waiting for the right moment to speak to Rei, Elena, Lemuria, and the others.

Count Fanrail, who was the first among those waiting for the right moment to speak up, was no longer here, and that’s why many of them probably thought, “Next it’s my turn…”

However, this time, that would lead to a bad outcome for the nobles.

It seems their poor judgment—staying put when Remuria, head of the Foolundal Duke's House, said she had something to discuss with Rei—ended up backfiring.

In that sense, Azuel’s judgment was probably correct.

Thinking this, Rei turned his gaze toward Remuria.

The expression on her face was one of faint wariness.

If Remuria were to try to take Set by force, Rei intended to deal with her appropriately, even if she were an old woman.

However, Remuria, met with Rei’s gaze, showed no sign of being fazed.

Even though, if Rei were to get serious, Remuria’s life would vanish in an instant.

Facing Lemuria, whose gaze was quiet yet conveyed a strong will, Rei softened his own gaze slightly.

It was because he realized that, unlike Count Caseres’ men, she wasn’t trying to do something to Set without any discussion.

“So? What do you want with Set?”

Although Rei had softened his attitude, he was still not conversing using even the most basic polite language as he had at first.

It was an attitude born of his judgment that they were, in a sense, equals.

“Ah, actually, the griffin… Set, is it? I want Set’s feathers—freshly plucked ones, that is… I don’t know if I should say this, but I want some fresh feathers.”

“Feathers?”

Rei had been on guard, expecting to be told to hand over Set or something along those lines, but he never imagined the alchemist would want a feather.

No, he fully understood from his time in Gilm that alchemists wanted Set’s feathers.

But that a noble—and the head of the Duke’s Household, no less—would want them was completely unexpected.

At first, he was skeptical of Lemuria’s words, but the look in her eyes as she gazed at him was anything but a joke—it was utterly serious.

“Can I explain the situation here?”

“No… I’m afraid I can’t go into that much detail here. Lady Elena, could you please have a room prepared for us?”

“Understood. I’ll have one prepared immediately.”

At Lemuria’s request, Elena replied without hesitation.

But that wasn’t unusual.

At a large-scale party like this, it’s only natural that several private rooms—which also serve as rest areas—are prepared.

A party is also a venue for the nobility to conduct political affairs.

For that very reason, it serves as a place to discuss matters one wouldn’t want overheard, a place to rest if one feels unwell, and, in some cases, a place for lovers to spend intimate moments together.

As the host of the party, it was only natural to have private rooms prepared for various purposes.

Moreover, since this was the residence of Duke Kerebel, leader of the Noble Faction, there was certainly no shortage of rooms.

Consequently, Elena nodded readily at Remuria’s words and left the party venue.

……That said, the disappearance of Elena—who could be called the highlight of the party—from the venue did leave the party guests feeling somewhat disappointed.

A private room located a short distance from the party hall.

Sitting on the sofa in that room, Rei faced Remuria.

Incidentally, due to his large size, Brewitt was sitting on a different sofa rather than next to Rei.

Elena sat next to Rei and took a sip of tea.

……Incidentally, this tea was brewed by Elena.

When Elena first said she would brew the tea, both Lemuria and Brewitt were surprised, but Rei, who was fairly accustomed to drinking Elena’s tea, accepted her actions without much surprise.

After taking a sip of the tea, Rei speaks up.

“So, what’s the reason you want Set’s feather?”

“One of my relatives is ill, you see.”

Rei no longer seemed to mind Remuria’s speech patterns and tilted his head at her words.

“Illness?”

“Mm-hmm. It’s a rather rare illness. It’s not life-threatening right away, but it still makes daily life quite inconvenient.”

“That’s… a real hassle.”

If it were a life-threatening illness, that would undoubtedly be extremely troublesome.

But even if it wasn’t life-threatening, the fact that it made daily life difficult meant it was indeed quite a hassle, just as Rei had said.

(Hay fever, perhaps?)

That was the first thing that came to Rei’s mind.

Fortunately, no one in Rei’s family suffered from hay fever, but that was strictly limited to Rei’s family.

Among Rei’s acquaintances, there were a few people with hay fever.

Every spring, these people would wear masks to avoid inhaling even a single pollen grain, carry boxes of tissues with them, and some even wore special goggles.

And once you develop hay fever, it’s a yearly struggle.

Hay fever isn’t life-threatening, but there’s no doubt it interferes with daily life.

Seeing this, and knowing it all too well, Rei agreed with Lemuria’s words.

“Do you understand?”

“Yeah. …But I get the gist of it. So, if we use Set’s feather, we can either cure that illness or at least make it less severe. Am I right?”

“That’s right.”

Remuria nodded immediately at Rei’s words.

Seeing that, Rei could understand how she felt… but that didn’t mean he could just give her Set’s feather for free.

If he were to give it away for free right now, there was no doubt that others who found out would come asking for one too.

Given that, if he were to part with Set’s Feather, he would need to receive a suitable price in return.

(Well, it wouldn’t be strange if there were quite a few of Set’s feathers lying around in Gilm’s stables. …Ah, but since he mentioned they had to be fresh, I guess that’s out of the question. I don’t know exactly what counts as “fresh,” though. Maybe the feathers in the stables at Duke Kerebel’s residence?)

Feathers should naturally be lying around in the stables where Set currently lives.

So much so that if an alchemist were to find out about that area, it wouldn’t be strange for them to wonder if there was a way to get their hands on some.

“But I didn’t know Set’s feathers had that kind of effect, either.”

“It’s because it’s a special condition. Since they’re griffin feathers, I’m sure there are plenty of other ways to use them.”

“I get it. If Set—if the griffin—is willing to accept it, I don’t mind handing over the feather. But I can’t just give it away for free… What do you say?”

At Rei’s words, Lemuria nodded in agreement.

As far as Lemuria was concerned, she hadn’t expected Rei to agree so easily.

For that very reason, she didn’t want to do anything that would cause Rei any trouble.

“Hmm, that’s true. You could owe me a favor, but other than that… Oh, I know! How about a gemstone?”

“Gems? No, I don’t really…”

“Gems!?”

I don’t have a hobby of collecting gems.

Just as Rei was about to say that, Elena shouted, cutting him off.

It was rare for Elena, usually so calm and composed, to show such obvious surprise.

That’s precisely why Rei found himself wondering, “Why?”

As the daughter of a Duke, Elena must have seen more than her fair share of jewels by now, and if she wanted to get her hands on one—as long as it wasn’t something special like a national treasure—she could probably do so without much trouble.

But why was Elena, of all people, so surprised? Rei simply couldn’t understand it.

However, when Elena noticed Rei looking at her, she seemed surprised herself.

“Don’t you know? …No, I suppose it wouldn’t be strange if you didn’t. Rei, do you remember when we went to Exil before?”

Rei was puzzled by the sudden change of subject, but he immediately recalled something.

It was the magic item used by the opponent he fought during the commotion in Exil.

No, to be precise, it was an item far too unorthodox to be called a magic item.

“That’s right. The method the Priestess used—sealing magic within a gemstone and using it at will. The House of Fuldar acquired that technology and developed it further on their own. Naturally, they made sure it didn’t require any kind of sacrifice.”

That’s what Elena told me.
