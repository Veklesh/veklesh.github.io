It was noon on the day the Giganto Turtle was dismantled.

Everyone except those on watch—though even so, half the security detail was eating—was enjoying their lunch break.

It wasn’t snowing, but it was still winter.

While eating lunch and warming themselves by the fire fueled by firewood provided by the guild, Rei made his way toward the slum dwellers.

Incidentally, Rei had eaten lunch with Vihera and Byune and was now taking a break after the meal.

The others were chatting with those nearby, and some were even skillfully dozing off while seated.

Although everyone is spending their time with a certain degree of freedom, a sort of natural separation—or perhaps a division of space—has still formed.

In particular, no one is approaching the slum dwellers—perhaps because the others aren’t sure how to interact with them.

If we’d met in town, we might have chatted a bit, but I suppose many people feel uncomfortable seeing the slum dwellers participating in such a massive undertaking.

Or perhaps, if Rei and the Guild staff weren’t here, it wouldn’t have been surprising if someone had tried to pick a fight with the slum dwellers… but in a place where Rei and the Guild staff are present, there’s no way anyone could get away with such behavior.

...Amidst all this, Rei, seemingly unconcerned with the atmosphere around him, approaches the people from the slums.

The reason so many people are eating skewered kobold meat isn’t because they particularly like it, but simply because it’s the easiest food to come by in the current situation.

Almost every day, kobolds attack Gilm, and the adventurers defeat them; their corpses then become food for the slum dwellers.

Cobalt meat, while not as bad as goblin meat, is by no means delicious.

Still, if you’re willing to eat it, it’s perfectly edible.

Given that, it was only natural for the slum dwellers to make it their staple food.

(No seasoning or anything—just grilled, I suppose. …It doesn’t feel like they’re enjoying a meal; it feels more like refueling.)

To Rei, who enjoys her meals, the sight of the slum dwellers eating did not look appetizing in the slightest.

In fact, among the slum dwellers, those who can actually savor the taste of their food are a very small minority.

In that sense, the people here are probably typical slum dwellers.

“Um…”

As Rei approached them, those who noticed him shot him wary glances.

They know she’s the one who gave them the job, but since she’s a stranger, they can’t help but look at her warily.

Despite those suspicious glances, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered as he searched for the person he was looking for… and eventually found them.

“Su-chi, can I talk to you for a sec?”

“Huh? Oh, Rei. What’s up?”

“I’d like to talk to you guys about something…”

Saying that, Rei looked around.

The people around them were casting suspicious glances, wondering if Rei was going to harm Suu-chi or make some unreasonable demand.

In a place like this, he didn’t feel like talking about how people in the slums might envy Suu-chi and her group and attack them, or about how they could stay in the guild’s warehouse.

He felt that if he said any of that, the people around him would find some excuse to refuse him...

In reality, his words and actions were motivated by concern for Suu-chi, so if he explained properly, they would understand.

“I get it. …Look, don’t worry. Rei might look suspicious, but he’s actually not that suspicious.”

“Are you okay?” Suu-chi said soothingly, stroking the head of the girl—who looked to be about ten years old—as she turned her gaze toward her.

Perhaps understanding Suu-chi’s words, the girl reluctantly let go of the hand that had been clutching Suu-chi’s clothes.

“Alright, let’s go. It’s fine if we just walk a little ways away, right?”

“Yeah, that’s fine. …But do I really look that suspicious?”

“You look suspicious. Setting aside the robe, are you seriously telling me you don’t look suspicious with your face hidden by a hood? It’d be a different story if you were with that griffin, but right now, it’s just Rei.”

Hearing that, even Rei found it hard to argue.

In fact, with his hood up like this, it’s hard to tell that Rei is Rei.

Also, Set, who’s practically become Rei’s mascot, was running around the area as if making up for his usual lack of exercise.

In that sense, it wouldn’t be strange to describe Rei—who isn’t accompanied by Set right now—as suspicious. …Though he himself isn’t exactly thrilled about it.

“So? What did you want to talk to me about? I don’t think anyone’s caused any trouble.”

“Oh, it’s not about that. Actually, the guild staff were praising you for being quite efficient.”

“Really? …Hehe.”

Perhaps pleased that her friends had been praised, Su-chi smiled and scratched her cheek.

(Seeing her like this, you can tell she has a cheerful personality. …And, considering she’s supporting that many children, I suppose it’s no wonder she came all the way to the construction site to hunt kobolds.)

There are many children among those who came today.

Those children are solid assets to the demolition crew, and since they can act before being told, the others working on the demolition have no complaints.

Suu-chi blushed at the praise, but eventually seemed to snap out of it and spoke while looking at Rei.

“So, why did you call me here? If those kids are doing fine…”

“It concerns the safety of the children you brought here.”

At Rei’s words, Suu-chi’s expression turned serious.

After confirming that Suu-chi was fully prepared to listen, Rei explained what he had discussed earlier with the guild official.

He told her that there was a risk the slum dwellers might attack them, either for the reward obtained from dismantling the Giganto Turtle, its meat… or the money from selling that meat.

When he explained the matter regarding the underground organization, Suu-chi wore a troubled expression, as if she understood.

This was likely because she realized that what Rei was saying was by no means an overreaction or a joke, but a genuine possibility.

“……”

“I want to protect her, at least within my sight.”

That was what Soo-chi thought, but while she was used to a certain amount of rough and tumble, she was by no means an expert in that field.

That much was clear from the fact that Rei had saved her yesterday when she was nearly killed by three kobolds.

Even if people in the slums—who weren’t part of any underground organization—tried to attack Su-chi’s family, she would be utterly powerless to stop them.

It was precisely because she understood that she couldn’t argue with Rei’s words.

To be honest, Suu-chi hadn’t expected they’d be able to take on a job with such favorable terms—though the pay was clearly lower than what other butchers were getting.

On top of that, when she heard they’d be getting a share of the Giganto Turtle meat, she honestly worried that if the people in the slums—especially those who wouldn’t hesitate to attack others—found out about this job, it might be dangerous.

So when Rei brought up this very topic, she wasn’t sure how to react.

And while I’m hesitating… still, thinking about the safety of my comrades, I feel I can’t stay silent, so I speak up.

“So, what exactly are we supposed to do? Surely the Guild isn’t going to assign bodyguards to all of us… they wouldn’t go that far, would they?”

“I suppose not.”

To begin with, there are nearly fifty people… and if we’re going to assign bodyguards to all of them—including nearly twenty children—it goes without saying that a commensurate fee will be required.

...Given that Gilm is full of skilled adventurers, it’s only natural that hiring someone with a certain level of ability would come at a high price.

There might be some adventurers out there who’d take the job out of curiosity or kindness for a low fee, but even they couldn’t possibly protect all the children on their own.

Given that, Suu-chi and the others, who live in the slums, simply can’t afford to hire bodyguards by paying them.

“Wait a minute. Then what on earth are we supposed to do…?”

Seeing Suu-chi at a loss for words, Rei smiled and spoke up.

“Don’t worry. For now, I’ve brought a plan to deal with that.”

“……Really? Can you really do that?”

From Suu-chi’s perspective, after hearing what Rei had said, she couldn’t possibly believe it could be done.

But in response to Suu-chi’s skeptical question, Rei nodded, assuring her it would be fine.

“Yes. However, it will place a corresponding burden on you as well. …To be specific, I’m planning to have you stay in the guild’s warehouse instead of returning to the slums.”

It was a simple idea: since they’d be attacked if they returned to the slums, they just shouldn’t go back.

But perhaps that line of thinking was completely beyond Suu-chi’s imagination, for she glanced at Rei with a look that seemed to ask, “What on earth is this guy talking about?”

Rei had somehow anticipated that look, so he continued speaking without seeming to mind.

“It’s not that surprising, is it? After all, the only reason they might get attacked is if they go back to the slums, so if they just don’t go back, that’s all there is to it.”

“I… I suppose you’re right. But that’s impossible. Some of the people working with me here have left their families and friends behind in the slums. There are people waiting for me to bring home food with the money I earn here.”

“Oh… I see. That’s another side of it.”

Rei found himself convinced by Suu-chi’s words.

His suggestion had simply been based on the idea that they shouldn’t let the people who came here to work return to the slums, but after hearing Suu-chi’s explanation, he found himself convinced.

But… even so, if everyone here were to return to the slums as things stood, it was certain that some of Suu-chi’s friends would get caught up in trouble.

“But then, what are we supposed to do? I don’t think the slums know yet how much you guys made today, or what you got. …Unless someone brags or acts flashy, that is.”

“Well… hmm…”

She must have understood what Rei was getting at. Suu-chi was also unsure of what to do.

In fact, she probably hadn’t expected the job to pay this well, nor did she ever imagine she’d get even a little bit of Giganto Turtle meat.

When she accepted Rei’s offer, she’d figured it wouldn’t be strange if she got short-changed to some extent.

Even so, she’d hoped to make at least a little profit, but when she actually came today and discussed the terms with the guild staff, they were exactly the same as what Rei had told her… no, they were even slightly better.

Given that, there’s no doubt that what Rei mentioned becomes an issue.

None of Soo-chi’s acquaintances would do such a thing, but that’s because Soo-chi has a character so kind it’s unthinkable for a slum dweller—a rare exception.

Since Suu-chi understood that, she needed to take some kind of action to protect her family, friends, and comrades.

“Right. …I want to ask Rei something. How many people can actually fit in that warehouse?”

“People? Hmm, well, back when I took down a massive number of Gamelions, the place was spacious enough to line up their corpses by the hundreds.”

“Hundreds of Gamerions…”

Perhaps because the example Rei gave was so unexpected, Suu-chi repeated the words as if she didn’t understand what he meant.

Since Suu-chi lived in Gilm, she knew about Gamelion.

She also knew how delicious their meat was, having had the good fortune to eat it a few times in the past.

But at the same time, she’d heard plenty about Gamelions’ ferocity from the former adventurers she was close with in the slums—specifically, from some of the people who’d come with her today to help with the butchering.

For an amateur like Suu-chi, who couldn’t even defeat a kobold, challenging it to a fight was simply out of the question.

...Actually, since taking down even a single Gamelion would mean a hefty profit, I had considered trying to kill one if possible, but in the end, I decided to give up on the idea.

“Um… well… yeah, about that… can the families of the people working here stay at the warehouse the Guild provided?”

That’s what Suu-chi asked Rei.
