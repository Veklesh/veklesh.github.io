After selling the Sand Serpent materials, Rei had gone outside Gosh with Set.

Leaving the city like this without having accepted a request was bound to raise suspicion.

Anticipating this, Rei had told the receptionist when selling the Sand Serpent materials that he had a hobby of collecting magic stones.

Goush's Adventurers' Guild had obtained some information about Rei from Marina, but apparently hadn't been told about his hobby of collecting magic stones.

That said, their reason for leaving wasn't solely to collect magic stones from desert monsters; they had other business to attend to.

(Come to think of it, there was someone at the Bestir Empire's tournament who seemed desert-born too... but something felt slightly off. Even if they lived in the desert, were they fundamentally different? Or perhaps those desert folk were a special clan to begin with?)

Gazing down at the sea of sand stretching across the land from above, Rei pondered this sudden question as he flew through the desert sky atop Set.

The desert in daylight... especially at an altitude of about 100 meters above the ground, the direct sunlight was quite intense, and the wind was strong.

But for Rei, clad in his Dragon Robe, it felt no different than usual.

"Grrrrr!"

Set was equally unaffected by the conditions, seemingly enjoying flying through the sky with Rei immensely as he let out a satisfied growl.

"Set, there's no one around us... right?"

"Grrr!"

The landscape was nothing but desert, and there were few who could even attempt to chase after Rei and Seto as they flew through the sky.

At least, no one like Gosh would be here.

Still, just to be safe, Rei asked Set, who naturally purred reassuringly that it was fine.

"I see. Then land somewhere suitable. I have a few things to verify."

At Rei's words, Set purred as he descended toward the ground.

Though only about ten minutes had passed since leaving Gosh, if traveling on foot across the desert, they were already a day's journey away.

Unlike the Kingdom of Mireana with its roads, this area was desert, meaning there were no clear paths.

That meant they had to walk across the sand every time they moved, so it was only natural their speed would be slower compared to traveling on a road.

Landing in the middle of nowhere, truly the heart of the desert, Rei's first action was to retrieve the Sand Serpent magic stone from the Misty Ring.

"...Well, there's only one magic stone here for now... Is it okay if I take it?"

"Grr."

Set, whose many skills had leveled up due to the World Tree incident, nodded in response to Rei's words.

Gently stroking Set's head, Rei drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and flung the magicite into the air.

The blade of the Death Scythe flashed, and in the next instant, the magic stone was cleaved clean in two...

[Death Scythe has acquired the skill 'Corrosion Lv.4']

The announcement flashed through his mind.

"Corrosion, huh... Why? Wait, I heard Sand Serpents have poison, so maybe it's related to that? But if so, I'd think a skill like 'Poison Strike' or something similar to the poison claws Set possesses would make more sense."

Rei looked at Death Scythe with a hint of confusion, but it was definitely not dislike.

Corrosion wasn't a skill he'd have much use for against unarmed monsters, but it was extremely effective against foes wielding weapons.

(Well, using Corrosion obviously means the weapon rots... so you can't loot it after defeating the enemy.)

It was a skill with great utility, but precisely because of that, it also had its problems... It was a skill that was extremely difficult to handle.

"But since it's Level 4, it'll probably become as powerful as Flying Slash once it reaches Level 5. ...Ideally, I'd like it to develop an ability to restore weapons corroded by it... but that's probably impossible."

Rei muttered while staring at the blade of the Death Scythe.

Normally, he'd test what kind of effect the skill had here, but corrosion obviously required something to corrode... The only weapon he had that he wouldn't mind discarding was the spear he kept for throwing. After thinking about it for a moment, he dismissed the idea.

(It'd be a waste to throw away the spear. Might as well save it for when enemies show up.)

With Death Scythe's skill acquisition complete, the next task was the other one... investigating the Sand Ship.

"Set, stay back a bit, just in case."

"Grrrrr."

Set growled in response to Rei's words and moved back.

Once he confirmed a sufficient distance, Rei drew the Sand Ship from the Misty Ring.

The Sand Ship suddenly materialized in the empty space.

If anyone besides Rei and Set had witnessed this scene, they wouldn't have understood what had happened.

(Or would they think it appeared from beyond the dunes...?)

It was a bit of a stretch, but it was a more plausible explanation than it suddenly appearing out of nowhere.

"Now then, the question is whether I can actually operate this sand ship normally..."

Of course, Rei had no experience operating a sand ship. In fact, he'd never even operated a ship before.

He had been on a ship before, but whether that experience would help him pilot a sand ship? He couldn't help but tilt his head in doubt.

In other words, Rei had to teach himself how to operate the sand ship from scratch.

Of course, there were probably those among the Goush who could pilot a sand ship, but it was common knowledge that Rei was close to Auguste.

Given that, it seemed unlikely anyone would teach Rei how to pilot a sand ship now.

If Auguste could use a sand ship, that might have been a different story. But while his ancestors might have had the chance, there was no way the current Auguste had ever piloted one.

(And he certainly couldn't ask Daldora, the ship's owner.)

Rei pondered this silently while gazing up at the sand ship. However, since the current Dalidrawas trying to avoid antagonizing Rei, it was highly likely that if Rei had simply asked Dalidrahow to pilot the sand ship, he would have taught him.

...But Rei never got to know that.

"Set, help me out if anything happens, okay?"

Rei said it as a light joke, but Set must have taken his words seriously. "Leave it to me!" he growled energetically.

"...Yeah, I'll leave it to you."

Rei hadn't expected him to take it seriously, but he was naturally pleased that Set cared about him.

He let Set carry him up onto the sand ship and scanned the deck.

Normally, since this was a sand pirate ship, it wouldn't be strange to find bloodstains from battles on the deck.

But as far as Rei could see now, there were no bloodstains anywhere.

That was likely thanks to Set using the King's Intimidation when capturing the sand pirates.

The King's Intimidation, which had leveled up after the World Tree incident, possessed enough power to immobilize the sand pirates.

Of course, not all sand pirates were immobilized by the King's Intimidation. A small number who happened to be inside the sand ship managed to move... but even so, as a secondary effect of the King's Intimidation, their movements were slowed. Capturing such individuals was easy for the guards, including Zarust.

Because they had managed to capture the sand pirates with almost no bloodshed, the deck of the sand ship now spread out before Rei was remarkably clean.

...Though 'clean' meant free of bloodstains; the trash littering the deck remained untouched.

"It can't have been that long since the sand pirates started using this ship. Why is there so much trash?"

Scattered across the deck were broken weapons, and among them, half-eaten dried meat.

Various other forms of trash existed too, and even to Rei's eyes, it was a shocking sight – how could they have managed to make such a mess in such a short time?

(Whether I sell it or use it myself, I'll have to clean it up. ...What a pain. Ah, I could just post a job at the guild. Luckily, I doubt anyone would try to steal magic items from me right now.)

Walking across the deck, Rei pondered how to clean the sand ship.

As an adventurer known by the alias Crimson, it was widely understood that no one at Gosh's Guild would dare bother Rei now.

The adventurer who had picked a fight the day before hadn't been seen at the guild today either, likely scared off by Rei's true identity.

"Well, if you clean it properly, I might forgive you,"

He muttered, telling Set to leave the sand ship before heading inside.

True to its origins in the mage city of Ozos, the hull itself was exceptionally sturdy.

Normally, it would boast an overwhelming cargo capacity compared to a simple wagon. However, Dalidra used this sand ship not for practical purposes, but as a display of his wealth.

It had only been used a handful of times, and this assault on Auguste was one of those rare occasions... only to be so easily seized by Rei. It could only be called tragic.

Having carefully noted where the ship's controls were located during his previous incursion, Rei headed straight for the cockpit without hesitation.

It was located at the very heart of the ship's interior, a place where one could not possibly see the outside.

But even so, this was the correct place to pilot the sand ship. Because...

"...While the magic items all have distinctly fantasy-like effects, this one feels more sci-fi, doesn't it?"

Rei muttered, his gaze fixed on the four panels affixed to the front, back, left, and right sides.

In the center of the room, a crystal-like object sat atop a pedestal, making for an extremely simple cockpit with only these two elements.

Glancing around the room, he felt relieved to see it was completely untouched, unlike the rest of the ship.

So this is the control room... The sand pirates must have known this was the most crucial place aboard the sand ship.

"So this is how it's operated... Is this a magic stone? Ah, I see."

Inside the pedestal supporting the crystal was a hollow space, and within it lay a single magic stone.

At first, Rei didn't understand its purpose, but after a moment's thought, it became clear.

This sand ship was a magic item, and to move it, one had to either channel magical power or use a magic stone.

And channeling magic... well, freely wielding enough magic to move a ship of this size? That was beyond the capabilities of any ordinary sand pirate. Only a mage of considerable strength could manage it.

"Not a problem for me, though."

When it came to the sheer volume of magic power, there was probably no one in Elgin above him. With that confidence, he removed the magic stone from its pedestal and stored it in the Misty Ring.

The magic stone likely had almost no magic power left, but he acted on the thought that it might prove useful someday.

"...Right then."

He reached out to the crystal and channeled magic into it.

Instantly, the sand ship shuddered lightly and awoke.

The first thing to happen was the exterior scenery being projected onto the panels attached to the front, rear, left, and right sides of the cockpit.

"Isn't this more science than fantasy?" Rei thought, just as he had before. But it wasn't cameras projecting images; it was simply using multiple mirrors created through alchemy to reflect the outside view into the cockpit.

In other words, if something... or someone... blocked the path of those images, the feed would be interrupted.

In that sense, it was undoubtedly an unreliable external monitoring device.

"Well, if I'm the only one riding it, it doesn't matter. Even if I take someone else along, I'll just have to be careful about that sort of thing."

muttered Rei, though it was also true that his initial fascination with this sand ship had significantly diminished.

While it was good for moving large groups quickly, its usability beyond that was simply too poor.

If you need to carry a lot of cargo, there's the Misty Ring.

If you need to move quickly across the desert, you could ride on Set.

Since Rei could handle all those other advantages himself, the only real merit left was its ability to transport a large group at once.

"And that's only in the desert... specifically sand deserts."

If it could traverse rocky deserts, grasslands, or packed roads, that would be one thing, but since it can only move on sand, it's useless to Rei.

"Maybe I should put it up for auction or something before heading back to Gilm."

Rei muttered as the sand ship glided across the desert.
