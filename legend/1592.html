"...Well, you're quite early. Or should I say late in this case?"

Seeing Rei appear, Marina flashed a small smile.

Of course, the anger hidden beneath that smile was obvious to Rei, who was close to Marina.

To Marina, the people here belonged to Jaya... meaning they were the ones who forcibly abducted women from other countries, used slave collars on them, and forced them to work as prostitutes against their will.

No, if that were all, she might have been angry, but she wouldn't have felt such deep rage.

But they didn't just force them into prostitution. They impregnated them with the children of giants, then tore open their bellies to make them give birth.

Such acts were utterly unforgivable, impossible for any woman to tolerate.

Moreover, Marina's race, the Dark Elves, like Elves, possess the characteristic of having difficulty bearing children.

That made the people of Jaya involved in this incident all the more unforgivable.

"Ah... Sorry about that. There were more giants than expected, and the army from the Kingdom of Reburina was dispatched to slaughter every last one in this cave, so I was dealing with that too."

"...The Reburina Kingdom's army? But giants should be a valuable military asset for Reburina too, shouldn't they? So why?"

"It seems they want to make Jaya an enemy to confront alongside the Kingdom of Mireana. Though, this is purely Ryutas's speculation; there could be an entirely different reason in reality."

Marina nodded at Rei's words.

She must have immediately understood why they would do such a thing.

"I see. ...In that case, we should inform the envoy heading to Reburina about this. Otherwise, they might get the scoop on us."

"Would they? But wouldn't the envoys sent by Gilm already be aware of the situation? If so, they wouldn't do something so foolish..."

"They probably wouldn't. ...But you can never say for sure. Besides, more information is always better. Sure, some of it might be useless, but..."

"So, we should inform them about this incident after all. Well, we don't really have anything else pressing to do right now anyway."

The primary reason Rei and the others were dispatched this time was retaliation against Jaya.

But in terms of retaliation, it's already been sufficiently achieved.

No, what's happening to Jaya now is far beyond mere retaliation.

What was happening now wasn't something as simple as retaliation. It was damage so severe it felt like annihilating the organization itself.

Whether it was due to Rei and the others' actions or other reasons was unclear, but considering their current situation where they seemed about to be abandoned by the nation, the damage inflicted on Jaya would likely be substantial.

Given that, there's no need for us to do anything now. Leaking information to the envoy from Gilm wasn't a bad choice.

(Besides, I heard the Royal Faction's envoy isn't here with them.)

Rei came to this conclusion as he looked once more at the bodies of those who had worked here.

Many had died due to the giant's rampage. ...More accurately, they had been devoured.

Parts of bodies that could only be called remains, fragments of entrails, chunks of flesh and bone—all were piled together.

The soldiers moving about the area avoided that place, which could only be called a pile of corpses.

"Well, for now, let's leave that matter aside. What are you going to do, Marina? Are you staying here?"

"I'm going back. There's nothing left for me here, right?"

Marina stated it matter-of-factly.

For Rei, it was the expected response.

But... for the Jaya people still present, it was a completely unexpected statement.

"Wait! We can't have Marina leaving now! It's a problem!"

"That's right! Look at this situation! It's only because Marina is here that we're managing at all. And yet, to take away the key person... Have you no compassion!?"

"And anyway, what authority do you have to take Marina with you?!"

One of the soldiers who had been shouting that they wouldn't let Marina be taken was struck through the torso, collapsing to the ground.

The weapon that made the hole? A spear that pierced the soldier's torso, then tore through a nearby building wall before its tip shattered.

As the shattered tip made clear, it wasn't Rei's usual Twilight Spear, but one of the many broken spears still in stock.

To Rei, the people making a fuss right in front of him weren't opponents worthy of attacking with the Twilight Spear.

They were precisely the sort of opponents worthy of being attacked with a spear as worthless as trash.

The sudden act of violence unleashed by Rei silenced every single one of the noisy individuals.

They understood that if they made a sound now, they too would end up with holes in their bodies.

Facing the now silent crowd, Rei spoke.

"You lot, haven't you got it all wrong? We're not exactly heroes of justice. ...Well, if we were heroes of justice, slaughtering all of you who belong to Jaya wouldn't be out of the question. So, in that sense, you might consider yourselves lucky."

The gaze Rei cast upon the men was a freezing, icy color.

Seeing Rei like this, they instinctively sensed that saying anything more here would lead to the same fate as the man before them.

In truth, if such an opponent existed, Rei had no intention of holding back whatsoever.

But to the men, Marina was the one who had saved them.

For that very reason, they desperately wanted to avoid Marina being taken away from here.

Therefore, the men turned their gaze toward Marina.

If they opened their mouths to say anything, Rei would kill them.

So instead, they tried to convey their intentions with their eyes, without speaking.

But... Marina, meeting the gazes of everyone present, merely stared back at the men without changing her expression.

If the men could have known what emotions she harbored, they might well have fled the scene immediately.

But, for better or worse, the men lacked such sensitivity, and above all, Marina was exceptionally skilled at hiding her emotions.

As a result, the men fixed Marina with a gaze that was a mixture of pleading and expectation.

To those men, Marina opened her mouth, wearing a smile that could only be called a sneer.

"Hey, you guys. I've got a question... I am a woman, right?"

Confused by the sudden question, yet certain she was indeed a woman, the men nodded obediently.

Seeing Marina dressed in a revealing party dress that showed off her cleavage, there was hardly anyone who wouldn't think she was a woman.

That's precisely why they nodded... but it was a fatal mistake.

"Then let me ask you this. Why do you think I, a woman... could possibly be kind to members of an organization that forced a woman they kidnapped into wearing a slave collar, turned her into a prostitute, and then had a giant devour her alive once she got pregnant?"

In that instant, Marina unleashed the killing intent she had been holding back.

The men present had accumulated a fair amount of combat experience.

At the very least, they were clearly stronger than the soldiers of the Kingdom of Reburina.

...Though they were still inferior to the elite guards who protected the underground facility in Mejogo, the giant's nest.

"Wh-wh-what..."

"Aaaahhh..."

Faced with the sudden killing intent directed at them, the soldiers could only tremble.

Until now, they had listened to their requests, and given that they had been attacked by giants, the soldiers had been completely convinced they were allies.

But now, standing before them... there was no doubt this figure could not be called an ally.

Who could possibly think someone who fixed them with a gaze so icy it froze their very movement was an ally?

Especially someone who had committed an act that seemed to desecrate the very essence of womanhood...

Of course, there were women among the Jaya, so being female didn't necessarily mean they couldn't tolerate what the Jaya did.

But for Marina, it was absolutely unforgivable.

"Wh-what should I do... what must I do to earn your forgiveness?"

One of those subjected to that icy stare managed to utter only that.

"Forgive? Do you think I would forgive people like you? ...But, well. If you truly regret it, people from the Kingdom of Mireana will be arriving soon. If you honestly tell them what you've done and present any evidence... I might consider acknowledging it a little."

To Rei, it sounded exactly like telling them to turn themselves in.

But what made it different from a normal surrender was that the ones they'd be surrendering to weren't guards, but people from the Kingdom of Mireana.

(The Kingdom of Mireana isn't exactly a squeaky-clean nation either. No, being a major power, it naturally has its dark underbelly. But... regarding the giants, that's impossible now.)

To begin with, black crystal is necessary to create giants.

But that black crystal had already been destroyed by Rei's hand and no longer existed.

Since it once existed, one can't absolutely say there's no other source, but it was certainly not something found lying around.

Given that, even if research reports were found, using them to create new giants... that would be virtually impossible.

That's precisely why Marina told them to submit any research materials to the people of the Mireana Kingdom.

...Naturally, part of her reasoning was that Daskar, the central figure of the Neutral Faction currently heading here, would not commit such inhumane acts.

Having known Daskar since childhood, she trusted him not to resort to such tactics.

Naturally, if it meant protecting Gilm or the Neutral Faction, Daskar might resort to shady methods.

Even so, there should be clear standards for that.

"Understood. I'll do as you say!"

The first to speak up in response to Marina's words was a single soldier.

That soldier had been buried under the building's rubble when Marina had summoned an earth spirit to rescue him.

That's why he felt deep gratitude toward Marina. More importantly, as a soldier, losing research materials wouldn't cause him any particular hardship.

...Though it was also likely because he had never visited Mejogo, a place filled only with female prostitutes, due to his sexual orientation, which only aroused him to men.

"Hey, wait, are you serious?! If you do something like that, Jaya will..."

Another soldier standing beside the one who'd volunteered hesitated, fearing retribution from Jaya.

The other soldiers felt much the same.

They hesitated precisely because they understood just how much power the organization known as Jaya held within this country of Reburina.

"Calm down! Jaya's trump card, the giant, is in this state!? That means Jaya is already beyond saving, don't you get it!"

"Yeah, you're right."

Rei nodded at the words of the soldier who had first agreed with Marina's plan.

Originally, he had intended to watch this exchange silently, but seeing the current situation, he thought that if he pushed just a little here, he could undoubtedly steer the conversation in their favor.

When Rei suddenly spoke up, the man who had been about to retort after being told it was hopeless... no, not just him, but the entire crowd fell silent.

Having just witnessed their comrade killed so easily by Rei's hand moments ago, they absolutely couldn't risk doing anything to upset her now.

Seeing the surrounding silence, Rei spoke again.

"Just now. Just moments ago, soldiers from the Kingdom of Reburina arrived in this forest where the cave lies. Their orders were to kill everyone in this forest. You understand what that means?"

"...The kingdom... abandoned Jaya?"

"Correct."

Rei answered curtly to the words muttered by one person, timidly... as if they couldn't believe it.

"The kingdom's upper echelons judged it impossible to conceal Jaya's existence and decided to abandon her."

Having stated this, he relayed the prediction he'd heard from Ryutas.

Of course, he didn't reveal everything; he selected only what suited their purposes.

Given the current lack of detailed information, even such intelligence from Rei was likely sufficient to keep the soldiers in line.

The soldiers felt anger at the nation's betrayal and, following Marina's suggestion, began gathering research materials and the like.

Rei ended up taking custody of it because the surviving researchers opposed the soldiers' actions.

Fearing what might happen to the research materials otherwise, they entrusted them to Rei, who possessed the Misty Ring.
