Gaiska, too, as a member of the Marquis of Seisole’s household, could not tolerate seeing Rei—whom he hated—being fawned over.

(That guy Rei… well, I suppose it can’t be helped since he doesn’t like places like this.)

If these were ordinary children, Elena probably wouldn’t have been this concerned.

What is off-limits, and what is acceptable? Thanks to having learned these boundaries through their daily lives, almost no one does anything that would upset Set.

Even in those rare cases, a single warning was usually enough for them to comply.

But the children playing with Set right now are nobles’ children.

It’s not as if every single one of them has been raised to be completely spoiled, so perhaps there’s no need to worry that much, but still, something might happen.

That’s why I’m here…

(Sigh)

“What are you doing?!”

Normally, anyone who serves in her household obeys her every command.

And that’s only natural. Elena is a heroic woman who has fought on countless battlefields and is known by the nickname “Princess General.”

There was no way a mere child could do anything in the presence of such a woman.

...Though, perhaps Elena feels it’s childish to be so forceful with a child, as she isn’t projecting the same intensity she once displayed toward Gaiska.

“...Yeah.”

“That’s all I need to hear. Everyone, don’t do anything to Set that you wouldn’t want done to yourselves. Set is kind, so he won’t get angry over every little thing, but you should still know what’s acceptable and what isn’t.”

She gave the same warning not only to the child who had tried to pull Set’s tail, but to the other children as well.

Perhaps because they were children, their instincts honestly understood that Elena was not someone they could handle on their own.

Among the maids watching the scene from a short distance away, many were assigned the role of looking after the children.

The fact that such discipline was possible was, after all, because it was Elena—the daughter of Duke Kerebel, known by the nickname “Princess General.”

If the maids were to do what Elena did, they would immediately be reprimanded by their superiors.

...Of course, since it was Elena’s sheer presence that silenced the children, there’s no way an ordinary maid could pull off something like that.

(Elena, you really are something. You silenced that sassy-looking kid in one go. At this rate, it seems I won’t have to worry about Set getting into trouble.)

“Growl?”

Unlike Rei, who had been dealing with the nobles, Set had only been entertaining the children, so he wasn’t that tired.
