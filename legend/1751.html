"Great, they're still hunting Gamelions!"

The day after meeting Sura and the others... Rei called out happily at the guild.

Seeing Rei like that, Kenny—who had told him that Gamelion hunting was still going on—smiled happily.

“I’m glad you’re happy, Rei. But according to people who are used to hunting Gamelions, they’ll probably be gone in about five days… or ten at most, no matter how hard we try. So their numbers should be dwindling by now. …Well, I’m sure you could still hunt Gamelions just fine even now, Rei.”

While ordinary adventurers have to walk the ground searching for Gamelions, Rei has a partner named Set.

With Set, who can fly, finding Gamelions isn’t difficult.

...Though, it’s true that if they’re hiding in places like woods or forests, they do become harder to spot.

“Got it. Then I’ll go hunt Gamelions right away. I was actually worried I might not make it in time.”

Rei was delighted to be able to join the Gamelion hunt, and considering that Sura and the others would arrive in Gilm in just a few days, he’d likely manage to make it in time.

At first, Rei had thought it would be difficult for Sura and the others to hunt Gamelions on their own, but if Rocks were to join them in earnest, that would be a different story.

(Though, I never would have thought Sura and Rocks would end up together.)

The story he’d heard in the carriage yesterday had come as a huge surprise to Rei.

But when he thought about it, they had spent a considerable amount of time together and had even weathered various hardships side by side.

Given that, it probably wouldn’t be all that strange for them to develop that kind of relationship.

There is a noticeable age difference, but it’s not as if they’re shockingly far apart in age.

There were probably other candidates, like the former Resistance men, but perhaps chance—or perhaps what one might call fate—intervened.

In any case, it was a cause for celebration that two acquaintances had become a couple, so Rei had no particular complaints about the two of them being together.

Perhaps Sura had chosen Rocks as her boyfriend out of self-interest, for the sake of their life in Gilm… He hadn’t entirely ruled out that possibility, but at least judging by how they’d acted yesterday, they seemed to genuinely care for each other.

If that was the case, there was no need for Rei to say anything, so for the time being, he gave them a few Yellowbirds as a congratulatory gift.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

“Hm? Oh, just thinking about something. You know how they say the prospective immigrants from the Kingdom of Reburina are about to arrive in Gilm, right?”

“Yeah. It seems some people are pretty busy with that.”

During the journey to Gilm, many people had dropped off along the way—some to return to their hometowns, others to find work or a lover.

But even so, nearly a thousand people remained in the group led by Sura.

With that many people coming to Gilm, it’s only natural that things would get even busier during this already hectic season.

The guild was naturally swamped with work as well, but the guild staff were a group of highly capable individuals.

Kenny handled his work efficiently and quickly, making time to talk with Rei.

“I see. Thanks for taking the trouble.”

“It’s not like you’re the one in charge of this situation, Rei. Besides… once they know the circumstances, I don’t think there’s a single person in Gilm who would refuse to take them in.”

He hadn’t been informed of everything that happened in the Kingdom of Reburina—especially regarding the giants—but he had heard the official account: that women were forcibly abducted from various nations and turned into prostitutes using slave collars.

That’s precisely why Kenny had no choice but to help those people.

Kenny, in particular, was often viewed with sexual intent due to his attractive appearance, so such people’s plight was not something he could ignore.

Rei nodded in response to those words, spoken with a sense of indignation.

“It’s a relief to hear you say that. …Well, those women aren’t the type to just sit around lamenting their fate; they were thoroughly trained before they came to Gilm. Just yesterday, they wiped out a group of bandits in Sabrusta.”

“...They’re quite tough, aren’t they?”

“Yeah. That’s why I think they’ll do a decent job, whether as construction workers or as adventurers.”

“Well, then, I’m looking forward to it. Though, since Rei introduced them, I don’t think I need to worry about that.”

“I’ll count on that. Well then, I’m off to hunt some Gamelions.”

"Sure. Be careful. If you get back early, would you like to have some Gamelion stew with me?"

Saying that, Kenny invited Rei to dinner… but the very next moment, Lenora, who had been organizing papers next to him, gave him a light tap on the head.

“Ouch! Hey, what are you doing, Lenora?!”

“Look, it’s fine to chat with Rei, but you really should get back to work.”

As she said this, Lenora glanced behind her.

Kenny followed her gaze almost reflexively and saw his boss standing there with a beaming smile.

...Though his eyes weren’t smiling.

Having met his boss’s silent gaze—which clearly implied, “Now that you’ve finished your own work, go take care of someone else’s”—Kenny frantically took the documents Lenora had handed him.

Normally, he would tease Lenora about her chest… but he realized that if he did something like that now, lightning would undoubtedly strike him.

Seeing Kenny like that, Rei also decided it would be unwise for him to stay here any longer, and spoke up.

“Well, with that settled, I’m off to hunt some Gamelions.”

“Ah…”

Kenny called out wistfully to Rei’s back as he waved lightly and left the guild, but when Lenora gave a slight cough, he reluctantly returned to his work.

“Huh… I guess there are still quite a few people hunting Gamelions.”

“Grrrrr!”

Looking down at the ground from atop Set’s back, he could see several carts.

Given how big Gamelions are, you can’t exactly drag them away.

If this were an ordinary monster, you’d simply take the proof-of-kill parts, the magic stones, and the materials, then discard the rest.

But with a Gamelion, the fur, claws, fangs, ears... and various other parts could be used as materials, leaving almost nothing to discard.

Above all, since the meat is so delicious, there’s no way we’d leave the heaviest cuts behind.

That’s exactly why we need a cart to transport the Gamelion.

...If you were truly making the best choice, you’d butcher the Gamelion right there and carry it away, which would make cleanup easier later on. But if you tried doing that outside of Gilm, you’d risk getting attacked by some other monster.

That’s why, once a Gamelion is defeated, you immediately load it onto the cart and either head straight back to Gilm, or—depending on the size of the cart and the Gamelion—try to take down a few more.

“Grr!”

Rei was looking at the cart when Set suddenly growled.

Following Set’s gaze, Rei spotted an adventurer fighting a Gamelion… no, an adventurer being trampled by a Gamelion.

Their teamwork was poor, and their individual skills weren’t particularly high either.

In other words, an adventurer who had come to Gilm for the expansion project had joined forces with some people he met here to hunt Gamelions for the bigger pay… but he simply wasn’t strong enough to defeat a Gamelion.

“What should I do?” Rei hesitated for a moment, but he felt a sense of respect for the fact that they wouldn’t abandon their comrades, even as they were being crushed and lay on the ground.

Normally, if it were someone he’d just met, he wouldn’t have minded abandoning them.

Of course, the fact that they met in Gilm was merely Rei’s assumption; there was a possibility they had actually known each other before.

But… even so, not abandoning his comrades and facing an opponent clearly stronger than himself—even if he were in a fit of desperation—wasn’t something just anyone could do.

Moreover, whether by good luck or bad, a short distance away from the group struggling against a single Gamelion, another Gamelion was running toward the scene of the battle.

(No, it was hopping, wasn’t it? Just like a rabbit.)

Even as he thought that, Rei leaped down from Set’s back.

He jumped from a height of 100 meters, but for Rei, that was nothing out of the ordinary.

Showing no sign of anxiety, he called out to Set.

“Set, I’m going to help those adventurers who are under attack, so you take care of the Gamelion approaching from over there. Just so you know, don’t go blowing it to bits with a single powerful blow. That’ll just leave less for us to eat.”

“Grrrrrrr!”

Rei’s words served as the ultimate warning to Set.

Gamelion meat is one of Set’s favorite foods.

It was only natural that he’d want to avoid having less of that meat to eat, if possible.

Watching Set’s reaction as they fell, Rei activated Sleipnir Boots several times along the way to slow their descent.

"GRAAAAAAAAAAAAH!"

Gamelion let out a mighty roar and lunged toward the adventurer wielding a spear… The adventurer, unable to dodge due to his fallen comrades behind him, struggled to land a single spear thrust against Gamelion.

...Just at that moment, as if to preempt Gamelion, the ground between the spear-wielding adventurer and Gamelion was sliced open.

“Huh?”

“GAAAAA!?”

Both the spear-wielding adventurer and his fallen comrade let out startled cries in unison.

From Gamelion’s mouth came a voice filled with frustration at being interrupted just as he was about to strike.

Even if the meaning behind their cries differed, there was no doubt that both parties had halted their movements, if only for a few seconds.

And for Rei, who had fallen from above, confirming that they had stopped moving was enough to move on to his next action.

Thud.

Rei landed on the slash he had unleashed on the ground with his Flying Slash.

“Huh?”

The words escaped the adventurers’ lips once more.

They probably never expected someone to suddenly drop out of the sky.

“Well, I stepped in to help because you guys looked like you were in trouble, but are you going to keep fighting this battle yourselves? Or would you like to hand it over to me? Mind you, if you do, ownership of this Gamelion will become mine.”

“Please, help us!”

The adventurer wielding a spear shouted immediately at Rei’s words.

Weighing the benefits of defeating Gamelion against the lives of their comrades, they immediately chose the latter.

For those who’ve been in a party for years, this isn’t all that surprising.

After all, facing danger together is precisely what forges strong bonds.

But… while not everyone here was like that, Rei suspected that most of them had met for the first time in Gilm and had come to hunt Gamelion simply because they got along.

That’s precisely why those immediate words made a favorable impression on Rei.

“Leave it to me.”

Muttering that, Rei assumes his usual dual-spear stance, holding the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left.

“Ah, a scythe and a spear… Crimson!”

One of the adventurers, noticing the strikingly conspicuous scythe in Rei’s hand, realized its true nature and cried out.

And the moment they understood the meaning of the word “Crimson,” the adventurers felt a deep sense of relief.

They must have concluded that they were definitely going to be saved.

In fact, if even a tenth of the rumors about Rei were true, there was no way he could possibly lose to Gamelion.

Rei, who was locked in a standoff with Gamelion, naturally understood the atmosphere among the adventurers.

(I’d rather they didn’t let their guard down, though. …Well, I suppose it can’t be helped.)

From what Rei could tell, they were likely Rank D… or perhaps even E.

Given who they were, it was probably more a sense of relief than a desire to watch Rei fight in this situation.

“Well, for that reason, I’ve ended up being your opponent…”

Rei muttered, looking at Gamelion.

Under normal circumstances, Gamelion wouldn’t give Rei the luxury of time to chat leisurely with the adventurers.

In fact, given his ferocity, it wouldn’t be surprising if he attacked immediately.

The fact that he didn’t… suggests that precisely because Gamelion possesses such rare ferocity, he was able to sense the danger that Rei represents.

Of course, not all Gamelions are capable of understanding that; it’s likely because this particular Gamelion has an especially keen sense of such things.

“Let’s go.”

Muttering only those few words, Rei lunges forward in one swift motion.

Once he moved, the Gamelion had no choice but to follow... and the very next moment after Rei and the Gamelion crossed paths, the Gamelion’s neck was cleanly severed by the Death Scythe.
