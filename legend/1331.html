After the incident in the slums concluded, Rei and the others naturally left the slums.

They had gone to the slums in the first place to put pressure on Precious and the others, and since that had succeeded, they saw no need to remain there.

The slums were notoriously unsafe, and anything could happen.

...That said, word of Rei's arrival had spread quickly as information, so in reality, unless someone was extremely out of the loop or had just arrived in the slums, no one dared mess with Rei.

But unfortunately... or perhaps fortunately for the slum dwellers, Rei didn't know his information had spread so quickly through the slums.

Marina, as a former guildmaster, was aware of this aspect, but she kept quiet, judging that staying in the slums any longer would likely lead to various unpleasant incidents.

"So, you sort of threatened Precious... What are you going to do now? Honestly, I think they'll be pretty cautious after this incident."

"Hmm... Oh, wait a sec. I'm kinda thirsty. Wanna grab some fruit juice? Here."

When Rei turned to respond to Vihera's words, his gaze fell upon a street stall selling fruit water.

Fruit water made with spring fruits was a seasonal limited item.

It wasn't strange for Rei to want some.

After all, it was undeniably true that he was thirsty.

Vihera and Marina had no objection to Rei's suggestion, and Byune even eagerly tugged at the hem of Rei's Dragon Robe, eager to drink the fruit water herself.

As for Set, he was already walking toward the stall.

He bought the fruit water at the stall.

"Welcome! Our fruit water is delicious. We use fruit gathered by adventurers. Fruit from deep within the forest, quite a distance away. Plus, the containers we use are custom-made, so the flavor stays fresh longer."

"Huh... Well then, how about this? Give me all of it."

"Huh? Oh, all varieties? Alright then..."

At Rei's words, the shopkeeper immediately reached for the containers holding the fruit juice... but Rei shook his head at the shopkeeper's action.

"No, not all varieties. All of it. Everything here. ...Ah, well, if there's a limit on how much you can sell to one person, that's fine too."

"Huh? Eh? Wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh-wh

The man had probably arrived in Gilm this spring.

For someone who had been in Gilm from the start, it wouldn't be unusual at all for Rei to buy out an entire food stall.

That said, some stalls did limit the amount sold per person, wanting others besides Rei to taste their food.

That's why Rei had said, "If there's a limit on how much you can sell..."

Fortunately, this stall owner seemed to operate on the principle that selling was selling, happily selling all the fruit water to Rei.

He probably figured, since he had his own life to live and hadn't been in Gilm that long anyway, he might as well sell everything he could for now.

"Thanks for the business! Come back anytime!"

The vendor watched Rei and the others leave with a beaming smile.

Then, after Rei was gone, he looked up at the sky.

The sun was still high, and there was plenty of time left before the day ended.

There was still some fruit left in stock, so he could use that to make more fruit water.

Or perhaps he could indulge in the luxury of heading straight to the tavern and drinking in broad daylight.

Pondering these thoughts, he decided to act on the sudden stroke of luck.

Whether aware or not of the stall owner's delight, Rei walked down the street with Marina and the others.

The fruit water was delicious enough... yet, Rei still looked a little dissatisfied.

The fruit water he'd drunk before had been chilled by a magic item.

But the fruit water from the stall earlier was lukewarm.

Of course, he understood magic items were expensive, and that they weren't something you'd normally find at a street stall.

But having seen that before, it was only natural to think that way.

(If it's lukewarm, then cold... Ah, this is exactly when I should use Misty Ring.)

It suddenly occurred to me.

Misty Ring originally has the effect of stopping time for whatever is inside it.

That's why food taken out of the Misty Ring is always freshly made.

In other words, if a warm dish stays warm, then a cold dish stays cold.

If you chill fruit water with snow in winter and store it in the Misty Ring, you could enjoy chilled fruit water in summer... or even better, if it's frozen, you could have sorbet!

(Wait, fruit water doesn't have that much juice in it, so freezing it wouldn't make sorbet, would it? How do you make ice cream again? Chill milk and whip it?)

As Rei pondered the confusion between butter and ice cream making, he noticed a familiar figure in his line of sight.

And that acquaintance was heading straight toward Rei and the others. ...No, charging straight toward Set.

"Set-chan, are you okay!? I heard you got into some kind of dangerous situation while I was away on a bodyguard job!"

That person—Milein—seemed utterly oblivious to Rei and the others, and Rei shot her a slightly exasperated look.

Of course, Milein, receiving that look, didn't seem to care at all, devoting all her energy to doting on Set.

But that was hardly surprising.

When Azimov was attacked, Milein wasn't there.

The Scorching Wind, led by Milein, was considered the rising star among the young talents in Gilm.

...If we're talking about rising stars, Rei certainly fits that description too, but Rei is an exceptional case in many ways.

For the average adventurer, aiming for Milein rather than Rei was only natural.

Naturally, with Milein leading the Scorching Wind, various requests flood in during this season.

Among them are those hoping to establish connections with Scorching Wind now, and others who, for reasons beyond comprehension, consider making Milein their concubine.

(Well, maybe it wasn't exactly crazy...)

In front of Rei... or more accurately, in front of Set, Milein displays her sweet, affectionate side. But that's strictly in front of Set alone.

The usual Milein possesses a beauty undeniable to anyone, if not quite on par with Marina or Vihera.

And as befits the leader of the Scorching Winds, she's also a capable adventurer.

Though currently a Rank C party, rumors suggest they'll soon become a Rank B party.

A beautiful woman with skill. Her personality... well, setting aside the matter with Set, is completely unproblematic.

Considering all that, it wouldn't be surprising if someone wanted to make Milein their mistress.

But naturally, Milein wouldn't give such men the time of day.

Anyone who tried to force anything upon her would learn the true power of the Scorching Wind the hard way.

Milein has absolutely no intention of leaving the guild where Set is and becoming the mistress of some unknown man.

If Rei were to make a move on her, she might consider it—with Set in mind.

But she knew there was no such hint in Rei's gaze toward her.

"Ah... It really makes me feel alive when I'm cherishing Set-chan like this."

...Seeing Milein rubbing her cheek against Set's with that dreamy expression, expecting anyone to feel that way was impossible.

"So, Milein finished her escort job and came straight to us... to see Set, huh?"

"Of course! Johanna's on another escort job and not in Gilm right now... If I don't cherish little Set right at this moment, what am I supposed to do?!"

Her dazed expression vanished instantly. Clenching her fists, she raised them to the sky and shouted.

Many of those around who witnessed the scene applauded Milein's display.

For anyone living in Gilm, the love-hate drama between Milein and Johanna over Set was legendary.

It was only natural they found it entertaining to watch.

Set was fond of both Milein and Johanna, who played with him and gave him food, and no one was bothered by the sight.

Well, except for Rei, who was always with Set. Seeing this scene repeated so often, she couldn't help but have her own thoughts.

"I see. ...Well, I've finished what I needed to do today anyway. Meeting Milein relaxed me, so it's fine."

Today's plan was to apply psychological pressure to Precious... and hope something incriminating would surface.

But whether they'd take action the moment Rei and the others showed up...

(That'd be tough.)

Even when faced with him, and when he unleashed the Flying Slash onto the ground, Precious showed no particular sign of being shaken.

Given that, it seemed unlikely she'd suddenly make a move just because she suspected me... or because Rei and the others had acted today.

If they did move, it was entirely possible the informant... or the underworld man who tipped off Rei and the others about this matter, would be the one to act.

Normally, one might wonder if it's wise to trust underworld figures that much... but Rei knew his own capabilities. Given that, he judged it reasonable that underworld figures would want to improve their impression of him.

Especially in this case, for the underworld figures involved with Rei and her crew, it looked like their new recruit had picked a fight with Rei.

Given that, they needed to curry favor with Rei, even just a little.

If they had any information, they'd want to relay it to Rei immediately.

Rei himself had rarely clashed directly with underworld figures... but that very fact meant his power was feared in many ways.

Moreover, many within the underworld genuinely wanted to build a good relationship with Rei.

For these reasons, Rei decided it was safe to relax a little now. Still clinging to Set as he carried her, she led Milein toward the main street.

They weren't headed to the guild, nor did they intend to shop.

Since the place where they had their snowball fight in winter was a park, he figured it wouldn't hurt to play around there a bit.

"Milein, don't fall off, okay?"

"There's no way I'll fall. I'm riding on Set-chan, right? Then there's no safer, more secure place than this!"

Marina and Vihera smiled at Milein's confident declaration.

Not mocking smiles, but genuine, heartwarming ones.

Set had grown to about three meters in length, so Milein perched on his back looked like a doll clinging to a giant stuffed animal.

Thinking that... Rei felt a little down.

If Milein, who was taller than him, looked like that, what would he look like riding it?

(Do I always look like this to others?)

That thought was a bit of a shock.

But for everyone else besides Rei, it was probably nothing new.

After all, Rei riding Set was already a common sight in Gilm.

"Ah! It's Set-chan, Set-chan! Only Milein-nee-chan is riding! Take me too!"

As they made their way toward the vacant lot, their presence naturally stood out.

Envious of Milein riding Set, many children approached.

"No way. This is my reward."

"...Since when did it become a reward?"

Walking a little distance away, Rei replied to Milein's words.

But her voice was low... and more importantly, Milein seemed completely absorbed in admiring Set, so she didn't hear it.

Then again, even if she had heard, Milein would probably have boldly asserted her right to play with Set herself.

If that had happened, it would undoubtedly have led to all sorts of trouble.

"Rei, you decided to play today, right? Then maybe you shouldn't overthink it and just relax a little?"

Rei nodded at Marina's words as he walked beside her, then pulled back the hood of his Dragon Robe.

With the hood up, the glare hadn't been too bad until now, but now the sunlight hit Rei's face directly.

It was the soft spring sunlight, so it wasn't blindingly bright...

(Or was it just psychological?)

The summer sun and the spring sun.

Pondering that difference, Rei decided to take it easy for the day.
