Rei watched Fekts and his companions reeling from the shock before him, but he must have decided wasting time here was pointless.

He let out a small sigh before speaking.

"Anyway, this situation is the result of your actions, so save the moping for later. Once you get back to Gilm, you'll get an earful from the other woodcutters, your family, friends, and girlfriends."

He hadn't heard from Kenny who had requested the job from the guild.

Still, Rei could guess it was someone among those he'd just named.

"...Yeah."

Even Fekts, who disliked adventurers, was quiet now.

Before coming to this Treant Forest, he might have insisted they could handle things just fine without any adventurers.

But considering how helpless they'd been when attacked by monsters in a situation where no adventurers were present, he couldn't say they didn't need adventurers in the current circumstances.

For now, staying quiet was essential.

"Come on, let's get out of the forest. It seems there won't be any more attacks for now, but we don't know when they might strike again. ...Marina, how about you over there?"

"For now, it seems there won't be any more attacks."

Marina, bow in hand, answered Rei's words.

The wind spirit that had just swept away the vines had already vanished, and there was no one else visible around Marina.

Seeing Marina like this, the woodcutter who had watched the battle with the vines earlier started to say something... but ultimately said nothing more.

He understood there was no room for careless words in the current situation.

"I see. Then, let's get out of this forest. Whatever they throw at us, they have the advantage here inside."

Muttering to himself, he began stowing the corpses of the Treants they had defeated one after another into the Misty Ring.

Selling the materials was one thing, but what troubled him most was the absence of magic stones.

Monsters invariably possessed magic stones.

That was common knowledge for Rei, common knowledge for adventurers, and above all, common knowledge in this world of Elgin.

Yet, despite that, the Treant they had defeated carried no magic stone.

If this was simply because they couldn't find the magic stones inside its body, that would be one thing... But if it wasn't that, if magic stones truly didn't exist here, then this Treant Forest would be exceptionally peculiar in many ways.

...Though, to be fair, the fact it expands about ten meters every night already makes it peculiar.

"Wait a moment!"

Watching Rei stow the Treant into the Misty Ring, Fekts suddenly spoke up.

Rei didn't stop his work, only turning his gaze in that direction.

"What is it? If we keep going like this, there's a high chance of another attack. I want to get out of the Treant Forest as soon as possible."

"I know. I know, but... I'm asking you to swallow your pride. A little further ahead, there should be a tree we cut down. I want you to take that one too."

"...Are you serious? You want me to cooperate for your benefit at this point?"

Fekts was met with a look that was more than a little exasperated, but he shook his head.

"No! ...Well, broadly speaking, you're not wrong, but the money from selling this tree isn't for us. I want you to use it as payment for sending you here."

"Huh."

Rei let out a surprised sound, never expecting such words to come from Fekts' mouth.

He had assumed the money from selling this tree would be theirs to use... that's what he had thought.

But in reality, he stated he wanted it used as part of the payment for Rei and the others.

(Well, I plan to get my reward in fire ore anyway, so even if they sell this tree... Wait, are they going to buy fire ore with this money and use that as part of my reward?)

Or maybe the money would be used for something else entirely.

Even as these thoughts crossed my mind, seeing Fekts bowing his head to me like this made me feel like I could afford to be a little more accommodating.

Above all, if this felled tree could become part of this forest's power...

(Huh? Just now... something...)

Rei felt like he'd just thought of something, but he quickly reconsidered, deciding it was better to get out of this Treant Forest sooner rather than later.

If he needed to think about something, that could wait until after this matter was settled.

"Alright. Well, if we just leave it here, the monsters might find some use for it."

Nodding, Rei and the others began walking again, having just finished storing all the Treant.

Naturally, they kept their guard up, ready to counter any new monsters that might appear, but strangely, none did.

(Fearful of Set?)

Ordinary monsters wouldn't dare approach on their own if they sensed Set's presence.

Of course, there were exceptions—monsters like goblins, who were dim-witted, or those whose minds were consumed by a fighting spirit.

Considering that, it wasn't strange for a monster with some intelligence, like a Treant, to hold back simply because Set was present.

(But then again, this Treant Forest itself isn't exactly ordinary.)

Ordinary forests don't expand by tens of meters nearly every night.

Given that these monsters lurk in such a forest, it was hard to think of them as similar to monsters found elsewhere.

(Whatever the reason, it's true that if they don't attack us, it's a relief. Fighting while protecting the woodcutters is difficult enough as it is.)

One or two might be manageable, but there were over ten woodcutters.

Protecting that many people with just three men and one beast was harder than our abilities warranted.

Since they didn't have to do that, Rei had no complaints whatsoever.

Walking on while keeping watch, they soon saw a clearing in the forest.

Fekts and the others hadn't ventured that deep into the forest to begin with, so getting back here was quick.

...Though it was the Fekts who couldn't manage even that short distance.

Had Rei and the others not come, they would undoubtedly have been wiped out.

And when they finally emerged from the forest... cheers of joy erupted from the woodcutters.

The soft moonlight, previously blocked by the forest trees, now poured down as if rejoicing in the woodcutters' safe return.

For the woodcutters, who had truly faced mortal peril, this sight was the most precious of all.

Some of the woodcutters gazed at the moon in the sky, tears streaming down their faces.

"We made it through, didn't we?"

Marina spoke to Rei, who was watching the woodcutters fussing over Set.

Vihera stood beside Marina, her gaze also fixed on the excited woodcutters.

But while Marina seemed fine, Vihera's eyes held a look of dissatisfaction.

"What's wrong?"

"I thought the battle ended more easily than I expected."

The dissatisfaction in Vihera's eyes wasn't directed at the woodcutters, but rather at the battle that had taken place in the forest.

In fact, Vihera's single strike—the Demon-Infused Palm—had easily destroyed Treant from within, deciding the outcome instantly.

Having heard Rei's account of the Treant Forest, he had likely anticipated a full-fledged battle against formidable foes.

The greater his expectations, the greater his disappointment.

As if to encourage Vihera, Rei spoke up.

"You shouldn't be that dissatisfied. Today's fight ended up being this level, but we know something's off about this forest. We can't just let Treant's Forest keep expanding like this forever, right?"

"I get that... but do you really think that day will come? Looking at how the guild has handled things so far, they only see this forest as a resource, don't they?"

"Well, it is useful as a resource, that much is true. That's precisely why the woodcutters are here."

"Then they probably won't do anything to seriously eradicate this forest, right?"

"No, they will."

"...What makes you so sure?"

Vihera asked curiously at Rei's blunt assertion that they would simply clear it.

Rei opened his mouth, gazing at Treant Forest as he responded to Vihera's words.

"I get that you're frustrated we couldn't fight, but think it through. The point is, this forest isn't only spreading in this spot where we are. Even if we cut down all the trees in this one section, the forest will just spread from somewhere else."

"Well, I suppose so."

Even if lumberjacks and adventurers dedicated an entire day to cutting, the area they could clear would only be around ten meters.

Gathering more people would allow for more clearing, but even then, clearing ten meters all around the entire perimeter of the forest would be a pipe dream.

Moreover, the range the forest encroaches and spreads is only about ten meters at most, not necessarily exactly ten meters.

In fact, Rei had personally confirmed the forest had expanded by about fifteen meters.

Precisely because of this, it was undeniable that the forest continued to spread toward Gilm, bit by bit... truly bit by bit. If left unchecked, there was a very real risk the forest would eventually devour the road.

(As the lord of Gilm, Lord Daskar absolutely could not tolerate that either.)

Being on the frontier, Gilm was not entirely self-sufficient.

They rely heavily on merchants who come from other towns and villages to sell wheat and similar grains.

Beyond that, they naturally depend on merchants for various goods unavailable in the frontier.

Given that, if the road became impassable... or even if it didn't come to that, if word spread that the forest's encroachment threatened the road, the number of merchants visiting Gilm would surely decrease.

Goods would undoubtedly sell in Gilm, but whether one could even reach Gilm safely... Moreover, if the roads were swallowed by the forest, travel by carriage would become impossible.

(Well, worst case, I could go to other towns or villages as Set and buy things myself... but for everyone living in Gilm? That's a bit unrealistic.)

Of course, there are many supplies available to obtain in Gilm.

Especially meat—there are monsters aplenty for that.

(Ah, goblins.)

He briefly considered goblin meat, but immediately remembered he was talking to Vihera now.

"Anyway, we'll have to deal with this forest eventually, so once that happens, I think we can have the kind of battle Vihera wants. ...Well, I can't say for sure, though."

"...Right. Well, for now, I'll take your word for it."

Vihera reluctantly agreed with Rei's words.

Marina, who had been watching the two, saw the conversation was over and interrupted.

"Rei, let's head to Gilm anyway. We should get them back as soon as possible."

Marina's gaze fell, naturally, upon the woodcutters.

Now that they had exited Treant Forest, there was no immediate danger.

However, if the forest spread out and they were drawn back into it, another battle would likely ensue.

Of course, Rei was confident he wouldn't lose even then, but he couldn't predict how the woodcutters, who had just been relieved to be saved, might react.

Given that, Marina was right—it was best to leave this place as quickly as possible.

"Yeah, we'll do that. But I promised. I'll go stow that tree away first."

Rei's gaze fell upon the tree felled by Fekts and the others.

Having promised to take it, he had no intention of breaking that promise.

"Once that tree is stored, we leave immediately! Everyone, get ready!"

Rei called out to the woodcutters, who were still rejoicing at having left Treant Forest.

Rei's voice seemed to snap them back to reality. They immediately began preparing to leave.

Though, to be honest, there wasn't much to prepare.

Each held their own work axe in hand.

A few had lost theirs in the fight, but those men had nothing more to do.

For them, simply calming down completed their preparations.

Watching the woodcutters, Rei reached out to the felled trees and began storing them in the Misty Ring.

The felled trees themselves weren't particularly numerous.

As a result, all the wood was stored in the Misty Ring... and it didn't take much time at all before Rei and the others left Treant Forest.
