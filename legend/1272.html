The day after receiving the request from the guild, Rei stood in front of the diner Kenny had introduced him to.

It was a place Rei had visited several times before.

Unlike Rei's regular spot, the Mankyutei, tucked away on a back street, this one faced the main thoroughfare.

The food served was certainly not bad, befitting a place that could hold its own on the main street.

It wasn't usually packed, but he knew from eating there before that it wasn't the kind of place that ever had no customers at all.

"Well then... Set, sorry, but you'll have to wait here, okay?"

"Grrr!"

"No problem," Set growled, settling down right beside the shop.

Since Rei was busy thinking up new dishes, she couldn't pay much attention to Set today.

That's precisely why she'd planned for him to play in the snow in the backyard with the children who'd come to visit the inn today, near the stables, to keep him from getting bored... But for some reason, today of all days, Set made it clear with his attitude that he wanted to go with Rei.

The children... and Milein and Johanna, too, reluctantly sent him off, thinking, "Well, if that's what Set wants."

...Though in Milein and Johanna's case, it was probably also thanks to them competing to show how broad-minded they were, figuring if that's what Set wanted to do, then so be it.

As for Rei, he had anticipated why Set chose to come with him specifically today.

Hearing about the new dish, he probably remembered when they made udon before.

That he had tasted it.

Rei suspected that with this steamed bun... or rather, meat bun, Set was aiming for the tasting opportunity.

"Huh? ...Ah, Rei-san! Set-chan too!"

Hearing the voices talking to Set outside, the door to the shop—the Golden Bread Shop, which Rei had specifically requested for help creating this new dish—suddenly opened, and a woman appeared.

Rei recognized her immediately.

She didn't know her name, but she was the waitress who had served them when they ate at this shop.

She looked to be in her early twenties.

Her features weren't particularly striking, but the bright smile she directed at Rei and Set was undeniably charming—she was undoubtedly the Golden Bread Inn's poster girl.

"Ah, I'll be relying on you today. I'm Rei."

Rei gave a brief introduction to the waitress.

"Oh, I'm sorry. I haven't introduced myself yet, have I? I'm Sandrine. Come on in. We don't have much time."

Sandrine said with a smile, guiding Rei inside the shop.

Given the size of the shop's entrance, it was obvious Set couldn't fit through.

Rei hadn't intended to bring Set inside in the first place, but even so, Sandrine looked disappointed... She headed toward the back of the kitchen and brought back some kind of meat on the bone.

It was a bone, probably used for some kind of soup stock.

There wasn't much meat left on it, but it still seemed delicious to Set. His round eyes stared intently at the bone-in meat... or rather, it was more like a bone with some meat still clinging to it.

"Here you go, Set-chan. I can't let you inside the shop, but eat this while you wait, okay?"

"Gurrr!"

Purring happily, Set took the bone in his mouth and lay down beside the shop.

"So, I'd like to ask Rei-san to start working on a new dish with my father right away... is that okay?"

"Sure, I'm free anytime."

Saying that, the two headed into the kitchen.

"Daddy~, Rei-san is here~?"

"Ah, she's already here? Sorry about that, Rei-san. Coming in during such a busy time."

"...Ah, ah."

Seeing the cook who emerged from the kitchen—Sandrine's father—Rei was somewhat surprised.

Given that he was the chef of a restaurant facing the main street, she had fully expected a muscular man to appear... someone like Pamidore, for that matter, wouldn't have been out of place.

But the man who appeared was slender and looked a bit timid.

He was the complete opposite of the person Ray had expected upon hearing it was a restaurant facing the main street.

Seeing Rei's surprise, the man smiled and spoke.

"I suppose it is surprising. I hear that a lot. ...Um, I'm Rodrigo, the chef at this Golden Bread Inn."

Rodrigo bowed his head with a timid expression. Rei snapped back to reality and shook his head.

"No, sorry about the surprise. As you probably know, I'm Rei, the one who took this job."

"Not at all. Honestly, I never imagined someone would accept a named request at that price. Especially a famous adventurer like Rei. ...I figured I had nothing to lose, you see."

"I might not have taken it under normal circumstances, but fortunately—or unfortunately—it's winter now, and I didn't have anything else pressing to do."

Truthfully, the fee for this personal request wasn't particularly high.

But... even so, Rei would have accepted this job even if the fee had been lower.

As long as they operated with Gilm as their base, any improvement in Gilm's food situation was something Rei welcomed.

He didn't show it too much, but Rei promptly explained to Rodrigo what kind of dish he would make.

"There's a dish called 'meat bun'."

"Meat buns? From the name, I assume it's a dish using meat?"

"That's right. But what I know is only the basics. Like with the udon, the actual cooking will need to be handled by the chef who'll be making it directly from now on..."

You're okay with that, right?

Rodrigo met Rei's questioning gaze with a slightly uncertain smile, but still nodded in response.

"Yes. I'm not very confident, but I'll give it a try."

"Hey, Dad. You gotta look more confident here. You're already at a disadvantage with that appearance... Rei, Dad might look like this, but he's a great cook, so don't worry."

Rodrigo nodded at Sandrine's words, her guarantee of her father's skills, and Rei continued his explanation.

"By the way, is the bread served here bought from another shop? Or is it homemade?"

"Homemade."

"Huh."

Despite asking the question himself, Rei was genuinely impressed to learn the bread was homemade.

Most shops bought their bread from bakeries instead of making it themselves.

But in this case, that worked out well.

"Well, that's perfect. A meat bun is a type of steamed bread."

"...Bug bread? You mean with bugs?"

"We already did that yesterday."

Rei cut off Sandrine, who was making the same mistake as Kenny, and explained while looking toward the kitchen.

"Steamed dishes... like steaming meat. That's the meaning of 'steamed'. Absolutely not bugs... insects or anything like that."

"...You steam it? Bread?"

Rodrigo had experience steaming ingredients like meat, vegetables, and fish, but steaming bread was something he couldn't quite picture.

"That's right. Though it's not steaming baked bread, it's steaming the dough... Hmm, maybe it's easier to say we finish it by steaming instead of baking?"

"...Steam it. Hmm, I can't quite picture that."

"It gives it a moist texture, different from regular baked bread. And when you steam that bread, you stuff the dough with meat filling... meat and vegetables kneaded together... and steam it all together."

Having explained that far, Rei thought, Couldn't you also make shumai or gyoza from bread dough and meat filling?

But for now, the goal was steamed bread... specifically, making a perfect meat bun. He kept that thought to himself.

"Hmm. That's rather intriguing. A moist texture... I've never experienced that with bread."

Rodrigo asked with genuine interest, and Rei nodded.

"Yeah. But I'm not sure how the dough would be modified compared to baking regular bread. ...Maybe it doesn't need any modification, but I'd like you to figure that out through trial and error."

"Leave it to me. I enjoy that sort of thing."

He must genuinely love cooking.

Uncharacteristically for the usually reserved Rodrigo, an intriguing light shone in his eyes as he looked at Rei.

Seeing Rodrigo like this, Rei must have decided he could count on him. He moved on to explaining the meat filling.

"Now, about the filling for the steamed buns... from what I know, meat filling is the most common."

"Meat? Hold on a moment."

Rodrigo said this as he began preparing the ingredients, starting with the meat.

Watching Rei, Rei pondered other types of Chinese buns besides meat buns.

Besides meat buns, there were pizza buns, curry buns, shark fin buns, red bean paste buns... However, curry was problematic due to the spices, he didn't know how to prepare shark fin in the first place, and he also didn't know how to make red bean paste.

The only one he could possibly make was the pizza bun. But as a meat bun lover, Rei hadn't eaten pizza buns much before and couldn't picture what ingredients they used.

(Since it's called pizza, it probably has cheese and tomato... but wouldn't adding tomato make it watery?)

Thinking this, she decided to give up on explaining pizza buns for now.

He decided to have them hone their skills with meat buns first. Once they could develop their own unique style, he could tell them about such foods.

(...Ah, but if it's called pizza bun, maybe pizza itself would have been fine? Still, steaming probably makes a bigger impact than baking. Pizza can wait for later.)

While Rei pondered pizza buns and pizza, Rodrigo finished preparing the meat and various vegetables.

"Um, Rei-san. What should we do next?"

"Right. First, finely mince the meat... or maybe a bit larger? I'm not sure. Just cut it up. We should decide on the perfect size while actually tasting it as we go."

"Got it. Just meat for the filling?"

"No... ah, we'll need vegetables too. Some recipes just put stewed meat inside, but we should start with the basic version first."

He briefly wondered if simmered meat—like pork belly stew—might be easier than making meat filling. Still, in Rei's view, the fundamental dish was the meat bun.

Rodrigo seemed to agree that mastering the basics was important. He nodded without objection.

Then he cut the prepared oak meat into pieces slightly larger than mince.

"Next, the vegetables. ...Right, this one and this one. And this one too, finely chop them all."

Rodrigo nodded as Rei selected mushrooms, a vegetable resembling Chinese cabbage except for its green leaves, and another vegetable that looked exactly like a leek, then proceeded to mince them.

Watching this scene, Rei asked Sandrine, who stood nearby observing her father's cooking with interest, something that had been on his mind.

"It's a bit late to mention, but there aren't any customers today, are there?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. That's right. Yesterday, the guild told us that Rei had accepted a request, so we decided to have him teach us cooking during the day today and open the shop in the evening instead. ...Ideally, I'd like to serve the dishes he taught us and see how they go."

"That might be a bit much, don't you think? If I had properly memorized all the methods, it might be different, but he's only teaching the basics. We'd still need a chef to properly adjust the recipes."

"Yeah, I know. Too bad. ...But steaming bread? That's such an amazing idea! Where's this dish from?"

"It's from a country that no longer exists."

Unable to say it was Japanese cuisine, Rei deflected with that excuse.

(Wait, weren't Chinese buns dim sum, part of Chinese cuisine? ...Well, either way, it was undoubtedly a lost country to me, so I'm not exactly lying.)

Watching Rei mutter to himself, Sandrine tilted her head.

"Is that so?"

"Yeah. From what I know, they apparently liked eating it during cold seasons like right now."

"Well, since it's steamed, it's warm, right?"

"...Since there are steamed dishes, I think steamed buns should be okay too."

If they steamed meat, vegetables, and fish, why not steam bread too? When Rei asked this, Sandrine shook her head.

"It's a shift in thinking, I suppose. I doubt anyone ever considered it. Maybe steamed bread exists somewhere else... but at least in Gilm, I believe it's a first."

"Rei, I've chopped everything. What should we do next?"

While talking with Sandrine, Rodrigo had finished preparing the ingredients. Rei looked at the bowls nearby.

"Put it in that, season it a bit stronger, then knead it until it becomes sticky. Oh, do we have bread dough?"

"Oh, I'll get it right away. Just wait a moment."

Sandrine went to fetch the dough, while Rodrigo followed Rei's instructions, seasoning the meat filling with salt and other spices and kneading it.

Watching him, Rei racked his brain, trying to recall the next step in making the meat buns.
