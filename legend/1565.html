It was far too wide to be called a secret passage—a space where six people could walk side by side, or even more. Yet nearly fifty people were currently moving through it.

Seeing all fifty armed, it certainly didn't look like they were moving about for a picnic or anything like that.

But if you asked whether all fifty were tense, that wasn't quite the case either.

The sense of tension or crisis they carried varied greatly from person to person.

"But seriously, do we really need to go out of our way to send reinforcements? We've got a decent number of giants lined up, right? Then I don't see why we need to go ourselves. Especially not by using a carriage."

Muttering with genuine annoyance, the man glanced back.

Equipped with leather armor made from monster hides, he could turn around more easily than the men clad in metal armor.

But looking back, all he saw were his companions.

The carriage they had been riding in just moments ago was completely hidden from view, obscured by the figures of his companions.

He honestly doubted whether it was necessary to bring this many men, regardless of what kind of fool had attacked the underground facility in Mejogo... the place these men called the Giant's Nest.

"Well, yeah. Forget the giants shipped over here. They should've kept a decent number back there for guard duty. Chances are, by the time we get there, the fight over there will already be over."

The man beside him, the one with the bow, stated this.

True to his words, he seemed remarkably relaxed.

Seeing him act almost like it was a game, the others around him seemed to relax a bit. The tense atmosphere began to dissipate.

But it was only natural that those around him were tense.

After all, since Mejogo was completed, they probably never dreamed they'd end up walking through the very passage used to ship the giants, infiltrating the giant's nest like this.

"It's fine to relax, but don't let your guard down. We still don't know exactly what's happening over there."

The captain, leading the men, cautioned them all.

Being overly tense wasn't good, but being too careless was obviously no better either.

"We know that, Captain. But seriously, who'd be stupid enough to attack a giant's nest?"

The captain was asked this question, but he hadn't been briefed on the details either.

After all, the information that Mejogo was under attack had only reached them via messenger.

...If Jaya were a larger organization, they could have gathered swift and detailed intelligence using summoning magic or tamed monsters and birds.

Unfortunately, while Jaya was an organization unparalleled in the small nation of Reburina, that was only within the confines of that small country.

Moreover, it certainly wouldn't possess rare magic items like Paired Orbs.

Then again, even if such rare magic items existed, there were surely countless more effective ways to use them than giving them to this captain.

"Since we got the order to deploy, there must be someone like that out there. ...Though, to be precise, we never did find out exactly who the enemy was. ...But we'll find out soon enough, won't we?"

The captain's gaze fixed on the wall that formed a dead end.

Of course, it wasn't truly a dead-end wall; it was a sealed-off location serving as a hidden passage.

"Hey."

The captain muttered softly. One subordinate, understanding the order without further words, ran to the wall and began disengaging the mechanism.

Just as the captain and the others reached the wall, the seemingly solid barrier slid aside, revealing an entrance.

Being a passage used for shipping giants, the entrance was naturally large.

By this point, everyone except the captain was naturally on high alert...

"Huh?"

The tone of surprise came from the captain's mouth.

It was only natural. They had been preparing to defeat the intruders, but they hadn't expected anyone to be there the moment the hidden door opened.

Well, since this led to the fifth basement level, he had considered the possibility of a giant curled up asleep.

But finding a human the same size as themselves? That was completely unexpected, even for the captain.

Given the intelligence that the giant's nest was under attack, the possibility of intruders within the facility couldn't be ruled out... Still, the idea that intruders had made it all the way down to the fifth basement level was completely unexpected.

"...Tsk, ugh."

The captain, staring at the figure before him, finally snapped back to reality. Just as he was about to shout "Enemy! Attack!" in that instant, Rei's fist, striking almost reflexively, blew the captain away.

The captain was fortunate to be wearing his metal armor securely.

Thanks to Rei instinctively holding back, the armor wasn't destroyed.

Instead, the impact wasn't fully absorbed, resulting in several broken ribs.

......

The members of Jaya's unit couldn't comprehend what was happening.

It was completely unexpected that intruders would reach the lowest level of the giant's nest so quickly. They couldn't believe that their captain, whom they knew they could never defeat, had been taken down so easily with a single attack.

While the men stood frozen, Rei and the others moved on to their next action as if it were the most natural thing.

The first to charge in was, unsurprisingly, Vihera.

Her fists and kicks knocked several men unconscious in an instant. Next, Elena and Rei attacked almost simultaneously.

Only after being caught off guard like that did the men finally seem to realize the opponents before them were the enemies they needed to defeat.

Panicked, they each began their counterattacks.

However, only those at the front of the group were able to do so.

With a group of around fifty people moving together, those at the rear still had no idea what was happening at this point.

Taking advantage of this, Rei and the others launched attack after attack.

The one making the biggest impact in this battle was Byune.

Originally a thief specialized in combat, Byune had only been able to provide support against the high-rank monsters Rei and the others frequently fought, or during the battle with the giant earlier. But this fight was different.

The weapon Byune wielded, White Cloud, was crafted from the materials of a rank S monster, the Silver Lion.

It possessed no special abilities, but its sharpness was extraordinary.

At the very least, the shields and armor wielded by the soldiers present were utterly incapable of blocking its attacks.

Furthermore, it was unfortunate for the Jaya soldiers that their large numbers made it difficult for them to move freely.

Any careless combat maneuver risked injuring nearby comrades with their own weapons.

Some seemed utterly indifferent to the risk and charged forward regardless, but even so, the sheer number of people around them made maneuvering difficult.

As a result, the numbers of Jaya's soldiers dwindled in an instant.

Rei and the others advanced through the areas where the fallen Jaya soldiers lay.

With fallen comrades lying there—unconscious, injured and unable to stand, dead—engaging in combat there was dangerous.

It wasn't that they feared stepping on and killing those still alive... they simply worried about losing their balance by stepping on them.

With Rei and the others' strength, such concerns probably wouldn't be necessary.

But nothing is ever completely certain.

Given that, it was only natural to eliminate that risk beforehand.

"Hmph!"

Shaking off the White Clouds, Byune hurls the long needle with her other hand.

The long needle pierced the soldiers who had been trying to strike at Byune's opening.

It couldn't pierce their armor... but the soldiers weren't covered head-to-toe in protective gear, nor were their faces fully shielded.

If they'd been wearing full plate armor, it might have been a different story, but the soldiers of Jaya certainly didn't have the financial means to afford such gear. More importantly, wearing plate armor would have severely slowed their movements, given their physical capabilities as mere soldiers.

Even with full plate armor, gaps for vision and joint movement meant it wasn't truly impenetrable.

As a result, the soldiers clad in leather armor or light metal armor screamed in agony as long needles pierced their flesh.

"Ow!"

"Damn it!"

"Tsk!"

Amidst the echoing screams and the ensuing chaos among the soldiers, Byune darted into the fray and swiftly swung the White Cloud.

Soldiers were easily pierced through their armor, and even shields were sliced apart, leaving them barely able to defend themselves.

Some instinctively blocked the White Cloud's strike with their own weapons, but the arms the soldiers wielded were far from powerful.

In Gilm, they were second-rate... at best, third-rate goods at worst. Weapons of that caliber couldn't possibly withstand White Cloud's strike; they were sliced cleanly just like the armor and shields.

Some managed to block the blow, but even that was the best they could hope for.

The second and third strikes were impossible to fully defend against.

Unable to defend, the only option was to evade the enemy's attacks, but with allies nearby, such maneuvers proved difficult.

As a result, this battle truly became one worthy of being called Byune's personal stage.

Still, no matter how combat-specialized a thief she was, Byune was ultimately just a small child.

In a prolonged battle, physical limitations inevitably slowed her movements.

Several of the more skilled soldiers must have noticed this in Byune.

If they could drag this out into a war of attrition, they might have a chance.

That's what they thought...

"Oh? Did you forget we're here besides Byune? We wouldn't want you to forget that."

With that voice, Vihera charged into the enemy ranks, replacing Byune.

Feeling it was too dangerous to continue physically, Byune retreated straight back, returning to the space where the black crystal had been.

The soldiers who saw this clicked their tongues in frustration at letting her slip away, but the next instant, their attention was captured by the newly arrived figure—a stunning beauty clad in light garments like a courtesan or dancer.

...At this point, over half of Jaya's soldiers had already been defeated, and their remaining forces numbered less than twenty.

Under normal circumstances, such a depletion of forces would undoubtedly be judged as a crisis for them.

But the soldiers of Jaya were not soldiers or mercenaries at heart; they were members of an underground organization.

Moreover, those present here weren't exactly elite fighters.

Judging by their combat ethics, they were no different from common bandits.

For such men, encountering the peerless beauty Vihera suddenly appearing before them, they could not possibly comprehend the disparity in strength. It was only natural they would be swept away by their own desires rather than focus on the current situation.

Of course, not every single one of them was like that, but the majority inevitably fell into that category.

As a result, the few who couldn't move... naturally, there was no way Vihera and the others would let such prey escape.

Elena's whip-like Mirage attack and Marina's arrows, swiftly aimed and fired, took down those men one after another.

Naturally, Rei wouldn't just stand by and watch. He drew not the Twilight Spear, but the disposable spear he'd used before obtaining it, from the Misty Ring.

With a tip only half as thick as normal, this spear was utterly useless as a conventional weapon.

(The Twilight Spear would be a waste on these guys.)

If he channeled magic into it, he could easily deal with any blood or grease on the tip.

But even so, he felt it was far too wasteful to use the Twilight Spear against the worthless trash before him.

"Don't forget about me either... Yo!"

The spear hurled with those words... first shattered the torso of the first target it pierced, then drove deeper, delivering the same fate to several unlucky men.

Such a display snapped even the men who had been lost to Vihera's charms back to their senses.

They regained their senses... but unfortunately, it was too late. They were already beyond salvation.
