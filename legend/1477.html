The conversation between Ajash and Ilse proceeded with a surprising calmness, at least to Rei, who knew the circumstances.

Of course, Ilse was likely suppressing the vengeance burning within her as she spoke with her enemy, Ajas. Fortunately, through their conversation, she was gradually learning what might be called a method to control that vengeance.

Of course, controlling it didn't mean the desire for revenge disappeared.

But she was getting better at not letting it show in her expression, attitude, or mannerisms.

"Hmm. So Ilse is still low-ranked? Well, that makes it even less likely she'd find much use in observing Rei's work, I suppose."

"That's true. But precisely because my rank and skill are still low, I was told it might be good to observe how someone like Rei, who possesses the highest power among adventurers, handles requests..."

"Huh? Does Ilse have connections among the guild's upper echelons or something?"

As he asked this, Ajas raised his guard internally, though he didn't voice it.

As a low-ranked adventurer, Ilse was someone Ajas, who required a large number of women, would have liked to abduct if possible.

The fact she knew Rei... that was a problematic element in many ways. But to Ajas's mind, it seemed Rei had been forced to take on Ilse, a low-ranked adventurer.

Of course, if he tried to snatch Ilse away in front of Rei, Rei would surely intervene.

But he figured that even if he took Ilse away somewhere Rei couldn't see, Rei wouldn't go looking for her himself... or so he thought.

But if Ilse had acquaintances within the guild, that changed everything.

If someone took Ilse away, the guild might get involved.

(This is probably not a good idea.)

Ajas came to that conclusion without hesitation.

Ilse was truly beautiful. Naturally, she'd be popular with the men.

But considering the risk involved, Ajas decided it wasn't worth going that far.

If the connection were only to Rei, he might have considered trying to make it work.

This swiftness and caution in judgment were likely why Ajas's misdeeds had never been exposed until now.

And once he gave up on taking her away, Ajas's fervor gradually faded from his conversation with Ilse.

Whether it was a woman's intuition or a vengeful spirit's instinct that detected Ajas's change in attitude,

Whatever the reason, Ilse understood that Ajas's interest in her had sharply diminished.

That... she couldn't forgive the act of Ajas, the target of her vengeance, losing interest in her.

The vengeance she had been suppressing within herself until just moments ago surfaced.

"Speaking of which, Ajas. Actually, my parents were traveling merchants."

"Huh? What's this all of a sudden? Oh, peddlers, huh. Families running peddling businesses aren't that uncommon. So, are they still involved in Gilm's expansion work or something?"

"...No. They were killed five years ago while traveling as merchants."

"Ah... Bandits."

Merchants getting attacked by bandits wasn't exactly an uncommon story.

While the profit margin from attacking them isn't particularly high, it's because merchants don't travel in large caravans like merchant convoys, making them naturally inferior in terms of fighting strength.

For a band of dozens, attacking such a target holds little value, but for a smaller group, a merchant can be a worthwhile target.

"He was probably attacked by such a band..." Ajash said, but Ilse shook her head.

Watching the two of them, Rei hesitated, unsure whether to interrupt Ilse.

But Ilse was acting this way knowing full well who the target was. More importantly, it would be blatantly unnatural for Rei to stop her now.

Doing so would be like subtly hinting that there was some connection between Rei and Ilse.

If he did that, Ajas would undoubtedly suspect Ilse.

Though Rei didn't know what actions Ajas might take afterward, he decided it was better to let Ilse do as she pleased if it meant arousing suspicion. He chose not to stop her now.

"No, I'm not a thief."

"Huh? Not a thief? But attacking a merchant caravan..."

"Well, yes. Thieves are probably the most common. But if you can avoid revealing yourself as one... don't you think that's incredibly convenient? Especially if that person's status is an adventurer."

People living in villages, towns, or cities generally don't move away from where they live.

But adventurers are different.

It's not uncommon for them to travel elsewhere when a request comes in.

If such an adventurer were actually a bandit, it would be a huge advantage for the bandits and a huge disadvantage for the adventurer.

While obtaining information about requests to eliminate bandit groups is one thing, if they could travel together under the guise of providing protection, like Ilse's parents did, they wouldn't even need to search for prey.

Of course, failing to protect those they guard would lower their standing with the guild.

But even so, thieves are well-connected in the underworld, and restoring that damaged reputation wouldn't be particularly difficult.

Moreover, in Ajas's case, he had been carrying out such actions while coordinating well with his three companions, so his activities had never been exposed. ...That is, until now.

It was probably because he recognized what Ilse was saying. Ajas's expression stiffened, just for an instant, but unmistakably.

Still, perhaps judging that his true identity hadn't been revealed yet, he opened his mouth to cover up.

"Thieves, huh... Come to think of it, there aren't any thieves near this Treant Forest. Well, I guess that makes sense since it's close to Gilm."

In this case, it was skillful of him not to abruptly cut off the conversation.

Had he abruptly ended the conversation here, it would have been enough to make her suspect him.

...Of course, Ilse had recognized Ajas as a target for revenge from the very beginning.

Ilse nodded at the topic Ajas had diverted to, then spoke.

"Well, yes. There don't seem to be any bandits around here. But... there might be bandits disguised as adventurers."

"What are you getting at? It sounds like you've been implying I'm a thief since earlier."

At this point, he must have decided that any half-hearted evasion would only trip him up.

Ajas asked Ilse, who stood before him, wearing an especially displeased expression.

Fortunately for Ajas, none of the other members who had accepted the request together were present.

And... unfortunately, Rei was present.

The figure of a high-ranking adventurer with a notorious nickname.

This could be called misfortune that blows away any luck.

If Rei hadn't been here, it would have been possible to take care of Ilse right then and there.

Afterwards, they could have acted casually alongside the other adventurers, declined tomorrow's job, and simply vanished for a while...

But with Rei here, such a trick is impossible.

(I wish I knew how suspicious they are of me... If I didn't know that, I'd be completely... Huh? Ilse? Thief? Merchant...?)

As Ajas thought this far, the image of one particular family finally flashed through his mind.

Yes, it was no wonder he felt a sense of familiarity with Ilse standing before him. She was someone from the merchant he had attacked before.

Ilse back then was still a girl young enough to be called a child, so he hadn't noticed it immediately, but there was no doubt that the Ilse standing before Ajas now bore the resemblance of that girl from back then.

Even Ajas himself couldn't say exactly how many people he had killed. But with all this evidence piled up, he couldn't help but understand who stood before him.

Facing such an Ilse, it became difficult for Ajas to maintain his composure.

Because he now knew that the person before him was the one who had killed his family.

Once he understood that, there was no way he couldn't grasp why Ilse was here.

No, the presence of not just Ilse but Rei too meant they had targeted him from the start.

"No, I don't think you're a thief, Ajas. It's just small talk. ...Or is there something you're thinking of?"

Her words were gentle, but Ilse's gaze no longer hid the murderous intent reflected in her eyes.

Her attitude clearly stated: I am the survivor whose family you killed.

If Ilse had been alone, Ajas might have killed her and fled the scene.

But with Rei standing beside her, such a thing was impossible.

(What should I do? What can I do?)

It was already clear he had been completely targeted.

Right now, he needed to escape this place somehow and get away from Gilm as quickly as possible.

(Damn it, is the one investigating me connected to this case?)

He had to find a way to cover this up somehow.

Ajash made that decision, but as if timing it perfectly, Set's cry echoed from the sky... Then, a few seconds later, the angry shouts of the other adventurers reached his ears.

"Goblins! Goblins have entered the forest!"

"Huh!? I'm on it!"

Ajas, who had been caught in the gaze of Rei and Ilse, seized the opportunity. He shouted quickly and left the scene.

Watching Ajas's retreating figure, Ilse reflexively reached for the sheath of her dagger... but immediately stopped the motion.

Attacking Ajas here and now, with no evidence to back it up, could lead to her being captured and judged.

...That's right. Without evidence, no matter how much she wanted to target Ajas as her enemy, there was nothing she could do now.

"Well then, it's up to Ajas what he does next."

It was Rei's voice from behind that calmed Ilse.

If Ajas had made any move—specifically, if he had attacked Ilse—she had been prepared to stop him... but that preparation had become meaningless.

"Probably... he'll flee from Gilm. He probably thought he could handle me alone, but with Rei here, I don't think he stands a chance against that man."

Ilse murmured, taking a small, deep breath, trying her best not to show her excitement or anger.

Regardless of Ilse's current situation, Rei found himself agreeing with her words.

Even Rei couldn't see any way Ajas could win if he fought her head-on.

That left Ajax with few options.

He could ask someone who could fight Rei evenly to be his bodyguard, or find some way to frame Rei as a criminal and have the guards or knights deal with him... And, as Ilse had said, escape from Gilm before Rei could catch him.

(The sure thing is probably framing me as a criminal.)

Rei knew his own nature well.

He knows that whether his enemy is a noble or a wealthy merchant, he attacks without mercy, so he must have quite a few enemies.

If they could skillfully rally such people, they might have been able to make Rei a criminal.

But Ajas already had no time left.

Given that, making Rei a criminal was impossible.

(That leaves hiring bodyguards or escaping... though I doubt I could hire someone capable of fighting me evenly that quickly.)

In truth, it's not as if there are absolutely no fighters in Gilm who could hold their own against Rei.

But if you asked whether such a person could be hired immediately, the answer would still be no.

Moreover, hiring someone like that would surely require an enormous reward.

As for whether Ajas could afford such a reward... Rei's assessment was that it probably couldn't.

(Wait, maybe they could? If it was five years ago... no, if it was five years ago that they attacked Ilse's family, they might have been pulling similar stunts even longer. If that's the case, it wouldn't be strange for them to have amassed a considerable sum.)

He kept that possibility in mind... but ultimately, what he judged Ajas would choose in the end was...

"Escape, I suppose."

"Yes. But I don't think Ajas came to Gilm just to work normally. So, it wouldn't be strange if he was planning something..."

"We need to look into that as soon as possible."

Rei muttered this, but it was clear that the actual investigation wouldn't be done by Rei himself. It would go through Marina to Daskar, and then from Daskar to Egg.
