Rei , straddling the flying Set, spotted several lights on the ground ahead.

The moment he was certain they belonged to Gosh, he couldn't help but breathe a sigh of relief.

This was likely because, when he had previously traveled from Gosh, he had gotten lost despite the location being so close.

Still, Rei had his own reasons.

If there had been a proper road, he wouldn't have gotten lost.

But the place he had traveled through was a desert, devoid of any landmarks.

Moving at high speed through the sky under such conditions... at a pace incomparable to horses running on the ground... it was only natural things would feel different.

...Those traveling on the ground would probably call it a luxury problem.

"Grrr."

"Right. Then land in front of Gosh."

In any case, he needed to land now and inform them about the assassins' base village.

Ideally, I'd want to land directly at Augusto's mansion on the outskirts of Gosh, but there was no way I could pull that off.

If I tried something like that, I'd either take damage from the barrier protecting Gosh or destroy the barrier itself... Either way, it was certain I wouldn't end up in a good state.

(Besides, if I, coming from Gilm, did something like that, the blame might fall on Marina.)

Since Marina had suggested coming to Gosh in the first place, making a mistake here could cause trouble not only for Rei but also for Marina, who had sent him.

Of course, if this were an ordinary adventurer, it would be a different story. But for better or worse, Rei and his alias, and above all, the fact that he commands the griffin Set, made him stand out.

Naturally, if Rei made a mistake, people would start linking it to Marina, the guildmaster of the guild Rei belonged to.

Marina, in particular, was a dark elf, a former Rank A adventurer, and above all, famously possessed an excessive allure that stirred a man's instincts.

Given who Marina was, it was no surprise she had plenty of enemies.

Normally, if an adventurer made a mistake, it wouldn't concern the guildmaster of their affiliated guild... but there are exceptions to every rule.

"Oh, thanks to Narsus?"

As Set landed before Gosh's main gate, reactions immediately rippled through the city.

Argumentative voices echoed from within, lasting several minutes. Slowly, the main gate opened.

Of course, it wasn't wide open like during the day; it only opened just enough for Set to squeeze through.

Through the narrow opening, Rei and Set slipped inside Gosh.

Beyond the gate, the faces of the guards, tense and grim, were illuminated by the bonfire.

They didn't raise their weapons at Rei and Set, likely because Narsus had given strict orders before they left Gosh.

He had told them he might return even tonight, so upon seeing Rei and Set come back, they immediately opened the main gate.

...Normally, the guards would have hesitated to open the gate, seeing only Rei and Set without Narsus, who had promised to return. But... if they didn't open the gate here, it would mean disobeying Narsus's orders as Daldora's escort.

If word got out that they'd done such a thing out of fear of desert monsters at night, it could have meant making enemies of the Elema Trading Company.

That was precisely why they opened the main gate.

The guards looked somewhat frightened, but they must have decided it was still necessary to ask for an explanation. A middle-aged man who seemed to be in charge stepped forward.

"So, what about Narsus? I heard he was coming back too."

"Ah, Narsus and Zarust are still out there. I came back because I was asked to deliver a message. ...Because Set is there."

"Guru?"

He said this while lightly patting Set's back. Set tilted his head, as if asking, What's wrong?

To Rei, it was nothing but an adorable gesture... Unfortunately, to the guards right now, it didn't look adorable at all.

But it was hardly surprising.

These guards weren't close to Set to begin with, and they understood they weren't exactly strong themselves.

And now, even as a group, they were facing Rank A monster Set in a situation lit only by bonfires, moonlight, and starlight... Unlike when Rei and the others left Gosh, the renowned skilled fighters Narsus and Zarust weren't present either.

"I-I see. So, may I ask what you plan to do now?"

The guard asked, visibly tense, but Rei opened his mouth without seeming particularly bothered.

"Yeah. We're heading to Augusto's mansion. ...After that, who knows what happens. We might end up back outside again, so be prepared for that."

Hearing they might have to go outside again, the guards flashed faintly displeased expressions.

The desert at night was naturally home to powerful monsters, and they might have to face them.

It was definitely not something any of them would willingly choose to do.

Still, since the order came through the lord, they couldn't disobey. Reluctantly, they nodded at Rei's words.

They silently wished he'd at least wait until the next shift took over in a few hours before heading out.

"Well, I'll be heading out soon."

"Yes, be careful."

It was an exchange devoid of feeling... in fact, it was more like he was thinking, "Please don't bring me any more trouble." But Rei was aware he was causing inconvenience, so he said nothing more. He took the familiar's necklace and mounted Set's back.

"Alright, Set. Take me to Augusto's mansion."

"Guruu!"

Responding to Rei's words with a short growl, Set kicked off the ground and began running.

Though Set excelled at flying, being a rank A monster meant he was by no means slow on his feet.

He could run at speeds incomparable to ordinary horses, and his endurance was clearly superior.

(Ah, but maybe because this is Gosh, the standard isn't horses but camels? ...Though camels are apparently surprisingly fast too.)

Perched on Set's back, watching the scenery flow by under the moon and starlight, Rei pondered such things.

Even as he pondered, Set kept running.

Fortunately, it was night, and furthermore, Set was heading toward Augusto's mansion... meaning it was on the outskirts of Gosh.

It was already past midnight... well, maybe that was a slight exaggeration, but it was still a time when most people were asleep.

...Though even at such an hour, places like taverns and brothels were still bustling with people. In fact, many were working hard, seeing this as prime earning time.

If Set ran through such places, the townspeople, still unaccustomed to his presence, would likely be startled and frightened.

In that sense, it was fortunate for everyone that Augusto's mansion was located on the outskirts of Gosh.

Continuing to run along the road unseen, a familiar mansion eventually came into view.

Not only at the main gate, but numerous bonfires were lit encircling the entire estate. Was this also a precaution against assassins?

"Who goes there!"

Realizing Rei was approaching on his Set, several adventurers waiting before the main gate, and most notably the estate's actual gatekeeper, Günter, shouted nervously.

Had an assassin arrived? That fear was evident in their cries. But as the moonlight and torches illuminated their figures, several of them breathed sighs of relief.

Those who sighed in relief were those who knew Rei as Günter and Daldora's escort, and those who had seen Rei at the guild.

Those still tense were the adventurers hired by Daldora as temporary guards to protect the vast Augusto mansion, for whom this was their first encounter with Rei.

Still, seeing Günter and the others lower their guard, even those unfamiliar with Rei lowered their weapons.

...Though perhaps it would be more accurate to say they gave up resisting in the face of Set.

Glancing over at the group, all weapons lowered, Rei dismounted from Set's back.

"Sorry to startle you all."

"Absolutely. Coming straight here at that speed on a night like this? You wouldn't be wrong to expect an attack without warning."

Laying his head bowed once more to Günter, who sighed as he spoke,

"I'm sorry. But I had urgent news to deliver to Augusto and Daldora. That's why I was in such a hurry."

"...Speaking of which, what about Zarust and the others? They went to raid the assassins' hideout, right?"

"Yeah. And then things happened..."

Seeing the expression on Rei's face as he said this, Günter's face fell.

"I see... So Zarust is gone too... He was a good guy."

"No, he's alive."

'He's still alive?!'

Not just Günter, but everyone else who knew Zarust and Narsus had their expressions of sorrow instantly change. They all charged at Rei at once.

Surprised by this outburst and slightly confused as to why he was being yelled at, Rei frowned.

"Why am I getting yelled at?"

"Because you're being confusing. ...And why are you alone? Where are Zarust and Narsus?"

Günter's words carried a warning that he wouldn't tolerate any more strange remarks, but Rei explained without seeming to care.

"I can't go into details, but the book... no, the hideout was farther away than I thought. So I, who have Set with me, came ahead first."

"...Far? Even so, it's inside Gosh, right?"

"Huh? The guards didn't come?"

Remembering that Narsus had asked for a message to be delivered to Augusto's mansion, he asked. Günter nodded in response.

"Huh? The guards? They came, but they wouldn't bother telling us the message's contents. ...Well, I figured it out from what Rei said."

"I see. Then, is it okay if we go in? I have something I need to report to Augusto and Daldora first."

"Yeah, you should be fine. Set too, right?"

Rei nodded in agreement at Günter's question and slipped through the main gate alongside Set.

Several adventurers seeing Set for the first time froze completely at the sight of the man and the beast.

...Gosh's adventurers weren't exactly known for their prowess to begin with.

Given that, it was only natural that seeing Set up close would leave some unable to move.

If they'd seen him even once before, the shock might have been somewhat lessened... In that sense, the frozen adventurers could be said to be unlucky.

"Alright, Set. Wait for us at the stables. Just so you know, it seems quite a few adventurers hired by Daldora and Augusto are around, so don't attack anyone you don't recognize."

"Grr!"

Leave it to me! With that, Set warmed up his throat and parted ways. Rei entered Augusto's mansion.

Not having to be taken all the way to the stables, but being able to move there on his own, must be something those with horses—or camels, in Gosh's case—or familiar-like creatures would envy greatly.

Indeed, several adventurers shot envious glances at Rei.

"Rei... you're back?"

"Yeah. It's okay for me to go inside, right?"

"Sure."

One of the adventurers standing before the mansion was a man hired as Daldora's escort, the same man who had accompanied Rei to the research facility.

That's why he let Rei inside without needing to hear the details.

This man seemed to be the leader of the adventurers guarding the mansion's entrance, and since he gave the word, the others didn't object.

Once inside the mansion, Rei headed straight for the reception room.

Seeing guards stationed before the door, it was obvious an important person was inside.

(Isn't this basically telling a skilled assassin exactly where the target is?)

A fleeting thought crossed Rei's mind as he exchanged a few words with the guarding adventurer and entered the reception room.

Just as Rei had expected, inside the reception room were Augusto, Kathy, Daldora, and the guards Daldora had personally hired.

Everyone present turned their attention to Rei as she entered the reception room... Under that gaze, Rei spoke.

"I found the village."
