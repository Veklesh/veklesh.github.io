After spending about ten minutes with Set, Rei headed toward the principal's office as instructed the previous day.

Set clung to Rei, begging to play more, but Rei was, after all, employed as an instructor.

He couldn't very well slack off on his first day, so with a heavy heart, he parted ways with Set and walked through the academy grounds.

The sorrowful whinny Set had heard as they left the stables still lingered in his mind.

If possible, Rei would have liked to spend a little more time with Set...

(Well, even if I have classes, it's not like my whole day is packed. So, I could just go visit Set during my free time. ...Too bad for the horses, though.)

The stable where Set was kept was the staff stable.

There were a few horses kept there for instructors to use.

Some were privately owned, others borrowed from the school, but regardless of such distinctions, there were horses that made the stable their home.

But... living under the same roof as Set, who was practically an absolute overlord to the horses, clearly caused them stress.

(Getting used to Set before the stress gets to them... that's probably best.)

Like the stables at Dusk Wheat inn.

As Rei walked through the school grounds thinking about Set, he noticed many eyes focused on him.

(What is it?)

Wondering, he strained his ears.

"Hey, who's that guy? Not wearing a uniform means he's not a student here, right?"

"He's awfully short. Is he a girl? Or a boy? If he's a boy, that'd make him a kid, I guess."

"Hmph, another lowly creature invading this prestigious school."

"Hey, hey, is that okay? Not wearing a uniform probably means they're not a student, but someone invited as a teacher, right? Talking to them like that..."

"Well, plenty of people can't shake their sense of privilege even after getting in here."

"Hikaru, huh?"

"Huh? What's with you all of a sudden?"

"No, from what I've heard, that person is said to be a shining light among magicians. Well, I only heard Mr. Samaloon say it."

"So, you know who that guy is? Tell me."

"Nah, sorry, I don't. I only overheard what Professor Samaloon said. Besides, he's always wearing that hood, so you can barely see his face."

"Useless. Shouldn't it be easier to get info from Professor Samaloon? Then you should've at least asked that much clearly."

"No way. Talking to Professor Samaloon in that state would just be a waste of time."

"Anyway, he's not wearing a uniform, so he's not a student here, right? Then he must be an instructor, right?"

"An adventurer? Or a knight? Judging by his appearance, he's probably an adventurer... but he's smaller than us, right?"

"Well, it's not about actual combat, but theory about magic. Considering what Professor Samaloon said, it wouldn't be surprising, right?"

Those voices reached Rei's ears.

(I see. ...Well, I guess it's true. With so many students in uniform, me being the only one not wearing one would make me stand out like a sore thumb.)

The students whispering about Rei were all wearing uniforms resembling blazers.

The uniform, which shared some common design elements for both genders, seemed fitting to Rei as the uniform of the duke's family's officer academy.

(Though, of course, there are those who wear their sense of privilege like a badge.)

Rei felt like smiling at the contemptuous stares directed at him by those who seemed like potential candidates for the reserve squad, but he held it back and headed toward the school building.

Seeing Rei enter the building housing the principal's office, the students began to stir once more.

With those students behind him, Rei entered the building.

A few students wondered if he might be an intruder, but they quickly dismissed the thought, reasoning that no one would dare trespass so brazenly.

Their gazes turned toward the building Rei had entered, and the morning's talk was dominated by talk of a man who seemed to be an unknown adventurer.

"Rei, thank you for coming. Did you sleep well last night?"

Entering the principal's office, Rei was greeted by Erinde, who was checking some documents at her desk.

Erinde, an elf, had the same ambiguous appearance as yesterday, making it impossible to tell if she was male or female.

Of course, her appearance couldn't change in a single day, but if her clothing changed, then maybe...? With that thought in mind, Rei nodded, feeling a bit disappointed.

"Ah, I slept soundly. Though not as well as at the Dusk Wheat inn."

"Ahahaha. That's to be expected. When you mention the Dusk Wheat inn, it's one of the finest inns in Gilm. Especially the food there was delicious, wasn't it?"

Erinde's tone was that of someone speaking about a place she had actually visited.

(Well, she's an elf and lives longer than most. It makes sense she's been to more places than me.)

Ignoring the fact that she herself was in a state close to immortality, Rei opened his mouth.

"Now then, starting today, Rei will be serving as our mock battle instructor... but we can't possibly have you face off against every single student in the school."

"...I know it's a bit late to ask, but how many students are there at this school?"

Erinde's eyebrows twitched slightly at the question escaping Rei's lips, revealing a hint of surprise.

"Oh dear. Didn't Samaloon tell you?"

"I got a tour of the school, but I didn't hear about that part."

"Goodness, that man is hopeless. Listen, this is a four-year academy. Each class averages about two hundred students. That means there are nearly eight hundred students in total. Add in the instructors, staff, and support personnel, and close to a thousand people live here."

"How about that?" Erinde asked proudly, but Rei had doubts and spoke up.

"Nearly two hundred instructors and staff... The staff dorm I live in isn't that big, though?"

"Ah, that. There's another staff dormitory besides the one you live in, Rei. Plus, some staff don't live in dorms—they rent houses or commute from their family homes in Graciall."

"...I see."

Nodding, Rei thought to himself that if that was the case, he'd rather rent a place somewhere else or stay at an inn instead of the staff dormitory.

That way, he could play with Set whenever he wanted. But he quickly dismissed the thought.

(If it were an inn, wouldn't that mean he'd be farther away during class than if he stayed here at the stables?)

If that happened, Set might sneak out of the inn's stables to come see Rei.

No, Set could probably handle being separated from Rei for a few days, but considering the affection Set showed him, Ray couldn't dismiss that possibility.

"Well, given that, I doubt they'd actually ask Rei to spar with nearly eight hundred opponents, right?"

"...I suppose I could do it, but it'd be a waste of time. I'd probably get so focused on defeating them that they'd pass out before they even knew what hit them. That would defeat the whole purpose of a mock battle."

"Exactly. That's precisely why I want you to handle only the third and fourth-year S-Class students."

"S-Class?"

"...Samaroon didn't explain that either?"

Erinde asked, her cheeks twitching slightly. Rei nodded.

He hadn't actually heard anything about the classes.

"Honestly, his research on magic is excellent, and he's reasonably well-liked by the students... but he has these absent-minded moments, so I can't fully trust him."

Sighing at the hopeless man, Erinde continued her explanation to Rei.

"Class placement at this academy is fundamentally based on ability. Since graduates become knights or adventurers, it's necessary for everyone to understand their skill level, right?"

"...Well, I can't deny that."

"I'm glad you see it that way. Some staff members insist on class placement regardless of skill. ...Anyway. That class placement uses adventurer ranks as a reference. Meaning, S, A, B, C, D. Forty students per class, five classes total. That makes two hundred students per year. Of course, some drop out for various reasons, so it's not exactly forty per class every time."

"So, I'll be teaching the class made up of the top students scheduled to graduate this year and next?"

"That's right. ...What do you think?"

"I'll leave that up to you. I'm not familiar with this academy, and I don't know how best to teach the students. Still, I suppose I should assess their individual abilities before starting lessons."

"That would be a big help to us too. ...Well, it's almost time for the fourth-year S-class's mock battle..."

As if waiting for Erinde to say that, a knock sounded at the door.

"Seems the timing worked out perfectly. ...Come in."

"Excuse me."

The one who entered the room after announcing this was a muscular man completely bald on top.

He wasn't particularly tall, but he was still taller than Rei.

He looked to be in his thirties, with a stern face that would make perfect sense if he were a mock battle instructor.

(Shaved? Bald? ...Probably shaved, I guess.)

That was the thought that crossed Rei's mind upon seeing the man.

While most people would undoubtedly shrink back from such a face, Rei had seen plenty of stern-looking men before.

Daskar, the lord of Gilm, had a face that was incredibly stern too.

"You arrived right on time. Thank you, Glink."

"Not at all. ...So, you're the one we spoke about earlier?"

"...Huh?"

Rei, who had assumed the bald man would speak harshly, was startled by the unexpectedly polite tone coming from his mouth and let out a dumbfounded sound.

But the man seemed accustomed to reactions like Rei's.

The man called Glinck by Erinde bowed his head without seeming particularly surprised by Rei's words.

"Pleased to meet you. I'm Grink, an instructor for mock battles at this academy. It's an honor to meet you, Rei, renowned as Crimson."

"Ah, ah. Yeah. Rei. Um, it's only for a short time until spring, but nice to meet you."

Polite words came from that stern face. Yet, despite saying he was honored to meet Rei, he didn't seem particularly pleased. Rei couldn't quite figure out how to act around him, so he just said that for now.

"The honor is mine. I look forward to learning from your strength, Rei."

"Looks like we'll get along just fine. Well then, Glinck. Take good care of Rei."

"Yes. ...Well then, Rei. Shall we go?"

With that, Glinck urged Rei and they exited the principal's office.

Rei followed suit, exiting the principal's office after them.

The two walked side by side down the hallway, but neither spoke.

Rei wasn't particularly adept at smooth communication with people he met for the first time, and Glinck wasn't one to engage in idle chatter either.

Still, feeling uncomfortable just walking in silence, Rei asked Glink about the mock battle.

"Glink, right? I'm all for doing the mock battle, but could you tell me exactly how strong the third and fourth-year S-Class students I'll be in charge of are?"

"Strength, you say... Hmm."

Glink, who was probably also unsure how to interact with Rei, answered immediately when Rei brought up the topic.

Then again, that was only natural. Considering the difference in attitude between Rei toward Glink and Glink toward Rei, the latter simply had to be more cautious.

On one hand, a renowned adventurer; on the other, a mere instructor at the officer academy. The gap was vast.

"It's just a rough guideline, and it varies greatly by individual, but a third-year S-class student would be roughly equivalent to an adventurer ranked F, and a fourth-year S-class student would be around rank E."

"Lower than I expected,"

Glink shook his head at Rei's muttered remark.

"They rarely engage in actual combat, and training alone inevitably has its limits. While powerful monsters might appear in the frontier, they rarely show themselves around here. Of course, they aren't completely absent, but finding such monsters requires considerable effort."

"I suppose so."

Since arriving in this world, Rei had ventured beyond the frontier town of Gilm many times.

He understood that the monsters encountered there would naturally be weaker and fewer in number compared to the frontier.

"However... some students have actually defeated bandits, not monsters. I think those students are considerably stronger than the others."

Hearing that, Rei began to think he might be able to have a somewhat interesting fight after all.
