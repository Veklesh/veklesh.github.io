Outside the door, several Dark Elf warriors should be standing guard.

Their role was twofold: to prevent Rei from escaping the room and to protect her from unwanted interference.

Marina's kiss and her declaration that Rei was someone important to her. Because of that, Rei, who should have been locked away in some dungeon cell, was instead under house arrest in this fine room.

"I knew Marina was of special blood among the dark elves... but this was beyond my expectations."

When Rei first met Marina, she introduced herself as Marina Alianza.

Yes, Rei understood that bearing a surname meant she was born into some kind of special position, but Marina's standing among the Dark Elves was apparently greater than he had anticipated.

Rei's current situation proved it.

Lying on the bed, he turned his gaze toward the view outside the window.

The presence of a window meant escape was possible if he tried. But the Dark Elves, naturally aware of this, had placed surveillance outside the window as well.

Even under house arrest, there was no doubt the environment was more than comfortable.

"Still, I'm bored out of my mind."

As Rei scanned the room again, his eyes fell on the sofa and table where the Dragon Robe hung.

But there was nothing to pass the time with.

"Maybe I should just take a nap and wait for things to happen? ...That sounds like a good idea too."

Muttering this, Rei rose from the bed he'd been lying on, picked up the Dragon Robe from the sofa, put it on, and headed toward the room's door.

The dark elf watching from outside the window—about a hundred meters away—tensed for an instant, but Rei paid it no mind. He knocked lightly on the door and called out.

"Can I talk to you for a moment?"

Several tens of seconds passed after he called out. Eventually, the door opened quietly, revealing the dark elf.

He appeared to be a man in his twenties, but given the matter with Marina, it was impossible to tell the actual age of the dark elf standing before him.

"What do you want? I've been told to accommodate your requests as much as possible, but going outside is impossible."

There was no hint of disgust in the man's words.

Marina's important person meant that much to the man... no, to the dark elves of this settlement.

Marina's repeated instructions to treat Rei with utmost respect also played a significant role.

Even Marina must have been completely unprepared for things turning out like this, despite inviting him for the World Tree's treatment.

That's precisely why Marina was treating Rei as someone important to her.

As Guildmaster, she knew from the reports that Rei attacked without hesitation anyone who threatened her.

And with not just Rei, but also Set, a rare griffin species, present... the damage if even one person and one creature were to run amok was too horrific to contemplate.

With the barrier already weakened due to the World Tree incident, he absolutely did not want to risk such danger.

Of course, the kiss had been born of his affection for Rei.

He'd be sorry if it lasted too long, but spending a day or two like this wasn't so bad.

After all, they had come all this way for the World Tree's treatment, and they couldn't keep Rei, who was essentially its core, confined like this forever.

(Though, if it's about healing the World Tree, Elena's magic alone should be sufficient.)

"I'm not asking to be let out of here. It's just... being alone in this empty room is incredibly boring. Could you bring me something to pass the time... like a book, if possible?"

"...You can read?"

The dark elf man's guard shifted instantly, his expression shifting to one of genuine surprise.

This Elgin certainly couldn't be called highly literate.

But among adventurers, literacy rates were surprisingly high.

It made sense. If you couldn't read, you couldn't even read the request forms at the guild.

They could ask companions, acquaintances, or even guild staff to read requests for them if they had time, but that wasn't always possible. Worse still, there was the risk of being tricked by someone holding a grudge and sent to certain death.

With their lives on the line, adventurers naturally strive to learn to read... though only what's essential for their work. As a result, their literacy rate was quite high.

After Rei gave a brief explanation of the situation, the dark elf man nodded in understanding.

In Rei's case, his ability to read and write had nothing to do with being an adventurer, but he didn't bother explaining that now.

"Then, books necessary for adventuring should be fine. Wait here a moment."

With that, the door closed.

(Books are available inside the Misty Ring too, but wanting to read the books the dark elf has... that can't be wrong... surely)

Following the first one, those who now blame Marina are those who wish to politically eliminate her.

No, perhaps the real surprise was that Marina could charm even such seasoned opponents, if only for an instant.

Though it had been nearly a century since their last meeting, such a span of time was hardly worth fussing over for a Dark Elf.

"And...?"

Even using her magic-sensing ability, Rei, wearing the New Moon Ring now, felt like nothing more than an ordinary mage.

At most, he might have slightly more magic than the average human.

But she'd rather not show him without the New Moon Ring.
