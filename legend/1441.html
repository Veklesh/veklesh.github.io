Rei and the others were enjoying their conversation with the former guerrilla fighters, but since they were on patrol duty, they couldn't afford to linger too long.

Especially now, in the summer daytime heat, the blazing sun made them sweat just standing outside.

Regardless of Rei and his group, they decided it would be cruel to keep the former guerrilla fighters talking under the blazing sun. Rei shared several cold fruits he'd taken from the Misty Ring with them and parted ways on the spot.

...In the sweltering heat, cold fruit was like silver coins... no, something worth paying gold coins for.

The former guerrilla fighters were delighted by this, regained the energy to continue their patrol, and departed.

After seeing them off, Rei and the others headed for Marina's house.

Fortunately... or perhaps it was only natural, but Rei and the others arrived at Marina's house without encountering any further trouble.

(I kinda thought something might happen, though. Never imagined it'd be nothing...)

Given how often Rei got into trouble, when he heard someone was up to no good in the Noble District, he'd assumed they'd run into such people. But... in the end, they didn't encounter anyone like that.

"Come on in. ...Come to think of it, it's been a while since Rei came to my house."

"Huh? Isn't Vihera different?"

"Yes. Since there's been a troublesome person at the Dusk Wheat inn, we've gathered here several times since Rei left."

"...At Dusk Wheat inn?"

The slight surprise in Rei's tone was likely because he knew exactly what kind of inn Dusk Wheat inn was.

As one of Gilm's high-end inns, Dusk Wheat inn naturally charged steep lodging fees.

However, it was always bustling because the service provided was worth the price.

Particularly, it possessed a magic item with an effect akin to air conditioning, making it exceptionally comfortable during the height of summer or winter.

And now, with the expansion work underway, many people had gathered there.

Naturally, all the guest rooms at Dusk Wheat inn were completely booked.

...Though, to be fair, there were few vacancies to begin with, as many made Dusk Wheat inn their regular lodging.

Because of this, Vihera has quite a few acquaintances—not exactly friends or close contacts, but familiar faces nonetheless.

Despite that, Marina sighed at Rei, who tilted her head in confusion, wondering if it was really such a bother.

That even such an action could be seen as a gesture inviting men was proof that Marina was Marina.

"Look, the rooms are full, but the dining hall is basically open, right? And if you can eat delicious food there, people naturally gather... and then, Melan, was it? That person comes. And since yesterday..."

She trailed off, but Rei understood what she meant.

Melan was the man who had fallen for Vihera at first sight and was pursuing her. Marina had avoided mentioning Danz, who had arrived in Gilm yesterday.

Even such people could naturally enter the dining hall of the Dusk Wheat inn.

"Ah... I see. You've been through a lot."

"In that sense, I'm glad you came back today, Rei..."

Marina was being vague because there were probably others pursuing her too.

Those who were already in Gilm knew which party Marina and Vihera were with, and those who gathered information to some extent could probably find that out too.

But for those who couldn't... or wouldn't do that, finding beauties like Marina and Vihera—the kind you might only see once in a lifetime—it wouldn't be strange for them to try hitting on them immediately.

Did they enter the Dusk Wheat inn specifically targeting Marina and Vihera, or did they just happen to walk in and spot them?

Either way, it was clear the two women found it bothersome.

...No, not just them. Byune also found such a person bothersome.

There were people who approached them, seemingly intent on disrupting their meal, just as they were enjoying it.

Moreover, a few individuals with peculiar tastes existed—a small number who considered hitting on Byune instead of Marina or Vihera.

"It's fine if I act as bug repellent, but then I'll probably get guys hitting on me instead. ...Actually, that already happened."

Not much time had passed since Rei returned to Gilm.

And yet, he was already being hassled by two people.

There was no way he could feel safe here going forward.

"But if anyone tries to mess with Rei, anyone who knows even a little about the situation will probably stop them. And if he shows his strength to one or two people, I think he'll be safe unless something really bad happens."

Vihera said, and Marina and Byune nodded in agreement.

Many knew that Rei possessed strength that didn't match his appearance.

Considering that, it was unlikely many would foolishly pick a fight with Rei.

"Well, putting that aside for now... since we're all together for the first time in a while, let's take it easy."

Having just arrived at Marina's house, Marina said this with a smile.

She quickly used spirit magic to check if anything unusual had happened while she was gone, then entered the house.

Without a word, Set parted ways with Rei and the others and headed for the garden.

Inside the house, with all doors and windows shut tight, a stifling heat hung heavy. But Marina's spirit magic instantly cooled the interior.

At the Dusk Wheat inn, magic items maintained a comfortable temperature inside the lodging, but Marina could easily achieve the same effect with her spirit magic without using any magic items.

"Phew, sorry to keep you waiting. ...Want something to drink?"

"Nah, I'll cover that much."

In the living room they were shown to, Rei took a cold fruit drink from the Misty Ring while watching Set lying in the garden.

"...It's delicious."

Taking a sip, then another, Vihera murmured with deep appreciation.

The tartness and sweetness were perfectly balanced by the cold water, creating a flavor so delicious it felt like she could drink it endlessly.

In fact, Byune had already finished hers and was holding out her cup to Rei, asking for more.

Handing Byune another cup of fruit water, Rei spoke again.

"My part of the request is safely completed. ...It's just that the magic item we took from the Lerdact County isn't really to my taste."

Having broached the subject, Rei pulled several magic items from his Misty Ring.

Paintings, jewel boxes, sculptures... magic items worthy of being called works of art.

To the discerning eye, they were items worth paying a fortune for, but to Rei, they held no particular meaning.

"My, these are quite fine pieces."

Even Marina, with her keen eye for such things as Guildmaster, judged the items spread out on the table to be of high value.

She murmured with apparent admiration.

"This sort of thing isn't really my taste. If I kept it, it'd just gather dust in the Misty Ring. So I thought maybe you could display it in your house, Marina?"

"...Ah, so this is the present."

Watching the exchange between Rei and Marina, Vihera finally understood what Rei had meant when they reunited.

She had been a little concerned about him giving a present only to Marina, but knowing it was this kind of artwork made perfect sense.

"Even if I received this kind of gift, I wouldn't have anywhere to put it. I certainly couldn't leave it in my room at the inn."

The high-class Dusk Wheat inn naturally had far better security than the cheap lodgings found everywhere.

But even so, it was ultimately just an inn, not a nobleman's mansion or anything like that.

For a truly skilled thief, breaking in wouldn't be impossible.

Leaving the gift Rei gave me in a place like that would probably be bad for Vihera's heart.

"Right? Well, there were people around back then, so I couldn't exactly say what the present was."

"Isn't that more like forcing extra stuff on someone rather than giving a present?"

Marina said this with a slightly exasperated look, and she wasn't exactly wrong.

Rei quietly averted his gaze, likely having some thoughts on the matter.

But Marina, who had been watching Rei intently, eventually smiled.

"It's fine, really. It's a gift from Rei. Now that I know that, I'll have to display it properly. Usually, magic items like this have effects that prevent deterioration from the surrounding environment..."

Marina paused, then gently reached out and touched the painting on the table.

After a few seconds, she nodded slightly and spoke.

"Looks like it does have that kind of effect after all. Of course, I don't know the exact details, so we'll need an alchemist to examine it properly."

"Ah... I forgot to ask about that part. Things were pretty hectic."

Recalling Lerdact, Rei began explaining his experiences.

As expected, Byune reacted when she heard about the river fish hot pot, but he hadn't expected anything else, so he didn't mind.

Of course, it was certain that later, he'd have to take out the pot from the Misty Ring and feed it to Byune.

"Hmm... Still, destroying the mine was a reckless thing to do."

"Not really. It was night, no one was around, and there probably weren't any injuries or deaths."

To be precise, if someone had been hiding unseen near the mine, there was a real possibility they could have been caught in the collapse.

But since Rei had no information suggesting that, he decided it wasn't worth worrying about for now.

"Anyway, it's been a while... well, not that long, but still, since the Crimson Wings are gathered like this, I'd like to have a feast today. ...What do you say?"

"Mm!"

The first to cheerfully agree to Vihera's suggestion was Byune, who immediately thought of the delicious food she'd get to eat.

Marina, however, hesitated slightly before speaking.

"Having a banquet is fine, but as you know, there are a lot of people visiting Gilm right now. Naturally, every shop must be busy every day. Holding a banquet in that situation..."

Marina glanced at Set lying in the garden and murmured regretfully.

If it were just the four of them—Rei, Marina, Vihera, and Byune—they could probably eat and drink normally at any restaurant.

But with Set joining them, it suddenly became difficult.

If Set were to join Rei and the others making a racket in a shop, it would require a considerable amount of space.

Furthermore, it was entirely possible that someone new to Gilm might get drunk and start trouble, targeting griffin materials.

If that happened, it would mean extra work for the guards and patrolling adventurers, even though the place was already crowded and troubles were frequent.

To prevent that... Rei's gaze fell upon the garden where Set lay.

"There's no need to hold the banquet in the shop. In this garden, Set can join the banquet without any issues, and he won't be bothered by the others either."

"...Well, that's probably best."

Since it was her own home, Marina seemed to agree with Rei's idea.

She gave a small nod in return, then turned her gaze to Vihera and Marina.

"What do you say? If you two are okay with it, shall we have the banquet here? We could buy the food..."

Marina stopped speaking and looked at Rei.

Yes, since the Misty Ring stored a large quantity of food and ingredients, there was no need to go out of their way to buy anything.

Rei, understanding the meaning behind that look, nodded back.

"Alright, I'm fine with that. Besides... we've got an oven too."

The magic item oven made food... especially pizza... delicious. Byune knew this from having eaten it before.

"Hmm!"

That's precisely why Byune immediately voiced her agreement.

Vihera seemed equally excited about making and eating delicious food themselves, quickly nodding... and immediately getting started on preparing the meal.
