The man leading that caravan was a merchant named Augusto, who belonged to Gosh.

He was renowned for his honest dealings and held a good reputation among merchants in Gosh.

Precisely for that reason, he was treated like a thorn in the side by the major trading houses, and his business was often obstructed.

The reason he had suddenly decided to send out a caravan this time was that he could no longer procure goods he should have been able to obtain from other merchants due to interference from the major trading houses, forcing him to rush to another city to source them.

Even so, the adventurers Augusto always hired as guards readily accepted the sudden request.

Ultimately, the procurement was completed without incident, and they were rushing back to Gosh...

"Augusto! Monsters ahead! Just one! But... it's huge! A sandworm!"

Zedan, the thief among the hired adventurers, shouted with a grim expression.

Hearing this, Augusto turned his gaze to Zarust, who was leading the adventurers.

"What should we do?"

"Well, ideally we'd escape if possible... Zedan, can we outrun it!?"

"Impossible! It's faster than us! If we try to flee like this, it'll catch up to us midway!"

"...Can a sandworm really move that fast? No matter, if escape is impossible anyway, we should engage them head-on. We'll meet the enemy with our forces at full strength. ...Is that acceptable?"

Zalust's words met with a nod from August.

Though his skin was dark brown, befitting a desert dweller and rivaling that of a Dark Elf, Augusto himself was of average height and build, hardly cut out for rough work.

Still, having survived countless similar hellish situations before, he didn't panic at the news of an impending monster attack. He simply nodded.

"Understood. Leave everything to us. I'll stay out of the way, so please take care of it."

"Leave it to us. Sandworms? We can handle them. We've got plenty of Rank C adventurers here. ...Everyone, battle stations! We're going to meet those monsters head-on!"

Zalust shouted sharply, and the carriage halted its movement.

They must have noticed the approaching monsters. Some camels looked restless, but the caravan's drivers were calming them down.

"Honestly, first we had monsters flying through the sky, and now this? ...Could it be that monster summoned the others?"

One of the adventurers muttered resentfully.

They had spotted a monster flying in the sky a short while ago, but it had been a creature unlike any they had ever seen before.

Though the sunlight made it hard to see clearly, several types of flying monsters inhabited this desert, but this one had a completely different silhouette.

He'd been active in the desert for a long time, but he'd never seen a monster like that.

"We'll worry about that later! Right now, focus on dealing with the approaching monsters!"

Zalust shouted, and the man agreed, readying his weapon.

The weapon he held was a type of curved blade called a scimitar.

Unlike the longswords typically wielded by adventurers, its blade possessed a gentle curve resembling a crescent moon.

Its sharp edge provided exceptional cutting power, but compared to a standard longsword, its thinner blade made it more fragile.

The man held his scimitar, lightly touched the turban wrapped around his head, licked his lips, and waited for the enemy sandworm. But...

"It's here... Dammit, no! It's not a sandworm! It's a sand serpent!"

At the thief Zedan's cry, Zarust clicked his tongue and tightened his grip on the spear shaft he held.

"Damn it, a sand serpent!? What the hell is it doing here? They always stay in soft sand!"

While they both burrowed underground, unlike sandworms, sand serpents had weaker digging power.

That's precisely why it was unexpected for one to appear in a place like this, where rocky desert and sandy desert mixed together.

Still, as hired guards, fleeing was not an option.

And while the sand serpent was a surprise, if I fought calmly, it wasn't an opponent I couldn't defeat.

(Still... this might be a bit much for a rookie.)

Zalust watched the Sand Serpent advancing through the sand... glimpsing its scales faintly visible on the surface, inwardly regretting his decision.

Because it was a sudden request, the guards gathered, including his own party, weren't all seasoned veterans.

There were a few newbies mixed in, ranked E or F.

Asking such newbies to survive against a Rank C monster... especially one like the Sand Serpent, which requires a party to stand a chance against... well, it would be difficult.

While we had assembled a decent number of skilled adventurers, whether the newbies could survive in that situation was down to luck.

(But I'd like to keep them alive if possible. They're the young people who will carry Gosh's future.)

Running a hand over the turban covering his head, Zarust recognized his duty.

Defeat the Sand Serpent, protect the merchant caravan including their escort target, Augusto, and shield the newcomers as much as possible.

With that resolve set, he organized a defensive formation centered around the carriage. Soon, the Sand Serpent revealed itself.

Though its full length wasn't yet visible, it was already large enough to swallow a person whole.

"...Damn, it's huge."

Zalust had faced Sand Serpents in battle several times before.

But even among those experiences, he was certain the Sand Serpent before him now was the largest and strongest he had ever faced.

Though he felt bitter that protecting the new recruits would be difficult, Zarust called out to his comrades.

"Listen up, don't let your guard down! That thing... Huh?"

He didn't finish his sentence. The moment he thought he heard a sharp whizzing sound and felt something pass by, the Sand Serpent's head vanished.

Yes, literally vanished.

Most of its head was gone, leaving only faint traces of bone and brain matter on the ground... remnants that could only be called debris.

Before Zarust and the others could fully comprehend what had happened, the Sand Serpent's body collapsed into the sand.

Jalust, finally snapping back to his senses from the shock and noise, looked toward the red spear half-buried in the ground...

"What? It's gone?"

Right where Zarust was looking, the red spear vanished, as if to say what he had seen was nothing but an illusion.

But the hole left in the sand... and above all, the corpse of the Sand Serpent whose head had vanished, proved the spear was no figment of Zarust's imagination.

Many others besides Zarust had witnessed the spear vanish in that instant, and they could only stare in stunned silence.

Amidst this, Zarust, recalling the spear's trajectory when it struck, predicted its direction of origin and turned his gaze... upward.

What existed at the end of his gaze was a flying monster. A rank A monster, a griffin. And atop the griffin's back sat a figure that appeared to be human.

"Could that be..."

The image that flashed through Zarust's mind was the monster that had passed overhead just moments before.

It had been flying too high then to get a clear look at its form, but now that it was close, there was no mistaking it.

Wings sprouting from its back, the upper body of an eagle and the lower body of a lion. An overwhelming presence so palpable it was understandable even from a distance.

Compared to the monsters currently in Zarust's line of sight—sandworms and sand serpents—it was like comparing an infant to an adult... no, the difference in stature was even greater than that.

"A, a griffin...?"

In the dead silence, someone's muttered voice echoed around them.

Even in the desert, it wasn't usually shrouded in complete silence.

The sound of wind, the rustle of sand. In a rocky desert, one would normally hear the clatter of stones rolling or the faint rustling of sparse trees swaying in the breeze.

But now, none of those sounds could be heard. Only silence stretched out around them.

From Zarust's perspective, he almost wanted to praise the one who had just murmured for daring to speak in this situation.

Amidst this strange, pervasive silence... eventually, the griffin descended toward the ground, flapping its wings.

The moment they saw that sight, every single one of the guards, including Zarust, sensed their own impending death. ...It was over.

For the average adventurer, a rank A monster like a griffin was the very embodiment of an unavoidable, unreasonable force.

"Grrrr?"

But for Set, who was used to being petted, fed, and played with by people, it seemed strange that everyone else, including Zarust, had frozen.

He tilted his head slightly, purring as usual.

While most residents of Gilm would describe Set's mannerisms as cute, different strokes for different folks. ...Or perhaps in this case, it should be said that different people see things differently.

In any case, to Zarust and the others, Set's mannerisms looked like nothing more than the gaze of someone sizing up their prey.

On Set's back, Rei vaguely understood this.

(Well, I guess I'm partly to blame for taking down that Sand Serpent with a single Twilight Spear. Still, I saved them, so I don't want this awkward tension to build up and explode, leading to hostility or something like that.)

If time just kept passing like this, wouldn't they end up becoming enemies?

Wanting to avoid that outcome, Rei decided to speak up for now.

"Are you okay? I took care of this monster before it could hurt you."

He leapt down from Set's back and lightly touched the corpse of the Sand Serpent, now headless.

Blood continued to flow from where its head had been, staining the ground red and spreading a pungent scent of blood around them.

The first to snap back to reality at Rei's words was, unsurprisingly, Zarust.

"Ah, thanks to you, we're saved. We had a lot of newcomers here... Well, it feels weird to say this after you saved us, but who exactly are you? Having a griffin as a familiar? I wouldn't believe it if I hadn't seen it with my own eyes."

At Zarust's question, even the surrounding guards and merchants fixed their gaze intently on Rei.

The camels pulling the carriage, having never seen Set before, were understandably terrified and couldn't even move. Consequently, the drivers also fixed their gaze on Rei and Set.

Rei, bearing the gaze of the entire caravan, couldn't help but show a slightly surprised expression.

Until now, he had been active in the Kingdom of Mireana, and regarding the war with the Bestir Empire, there was scarcely anyone who did not know his name.

At the very least, among adventurers and mercenaries, there was no one who did not know the alias Crimson.

While the specifics of his appearance were less widely known compared to the fame of his alias, it was common knowledge that he traveled with a griffin. Seeing Set was usually enough for most people to recognize Rei as Rei.

The only other country Rei had ever visited was the Bestir Empire, and even there, though in a completely opposite sense to the Mireana Kingdom, his name was known.

...Well, considering the damage Rei inflicted upon the Bestir Empire, it was hardly surprising. And during the civil war, he certainly lived up to that nickname with his exploits.

Precisely because he thought that way, he never imagined that even upon seeing Set, he would be asked who he was.

Of course, some in Gosh knew the Crimson Nickname, and information on Rei naturally existed within the guild.

But even so, it was only natural that their interest would be drawn more to an adventurer from their own country than to one from a neighboring nation... especially one from a country they were forced to submit to.

"I'm Rei. I'm an adventurer in the Gilm of the Kingdom of Mireana."

The moment the name "Kingdom of Mireana" was uttered, Rei could see the expressions of those around him, including Zarust, stiffen slightly.

(I'd heard it was a vassal state... but apparently, it's not exactly well-liked, as expected.)

Rei grasped how the suzerain Kingdom of Mireana was perceived from their current attitude, but he couldn't simply part ways here.

Having defeated an unknown monster called a Sand Serpent, he definitely wanted to secure its magic stone.

However, he judged that they probably didn't want to deal with him, a native of the Mireana Kingdom, for too long either, so he stated his business bluntly.

"About this Sand Serpent... I can take it, right?"
