Rei passed right by the adventurers, his brow slightly furrowed.

It wasn't that he had any particular thoughts about the adventurers.

It was because his speed when kicking off the desert sand was overwhelmingly slower than usual.

Come to think of it, since arriving at Gosh... or more precisely, since arriving at the Ferris Desert, the first Sand Serpent Rei had fought was already decided the moment he fired the Twilight Spear from Set's back.

The sand pirates he fought after the Sand Serpent were also essentially immobilized by the roar using the King of Set's intimidation before the fight even began, and that battle took place on the deck of a sand ship.

And even the coconut crab they fought earlier, all Rei did was jump off Set at the start to draw its attention.

Fighting properly in the desert again after so long felt awkward.

(I knew it would be tough, but this is worse than I expected. My feet just won't move fast enough.)

Watching the Sand Lizardmen closing in before his eyes, Rei inwardly grumbled.

That said, Rei's speed was still fast enough to be considered swift by adventurers... and by the Sand Lizardmen rapidly closing the distance to him.

What felt slow to Rei was still more than fast enough for everyone else.

"Kishaaaaaah!"

The lead Sand Lizardman, seeing a creature entirely unlike the adventurers they'd been chasing charging straight toward them, let out a warning cry.

They couldn't recognize human faces, but they could tell by size and by numbers.

Above all, the Sand Lizardman had clearly seen something fall toward their direction just seconds ago.

They couldn't identify what it was, but now that it appeared before them, they understood exactly what it was.

"Kishaaah!"

The lead Sand Lizard Man thrust the trident he held toward Rei, who was advancing toward him.

Considering Rei's approach speed, the Sand Lizard Man was certain the trident's tip would pierce his body.

Small bodies meant fewer places to strike. That's what the Sand Lizard Man thought... but the next instant, his head shattered, and his consciousness sank into darkness.

"Huh."

The Twilight Spear strike, launched while evading the trident's blow, easily shattered the head of the Sand Lizardman leading the charge.

After delivering that strike, he used Sleipnir's boots to kick off the air, dampening his momentum, and landed on the sand.

He now stood directly between the Sand Lizardman and the four adventurers it had been fleeing with.

Seeing the Twilight Spear in his hand, Rei murmured with admiration.

He had experienced using it as a thrown weapon during trials at Gilm's guild and in the battle against the Sand Serpent.

But training aside, this was his first time actually wielding it as a spear to fight an enemy.

That's precisely why he wore a slightly surprised expression at having so easily defeated the Sand Lizardman with this single strike.

"Well? What's the matter? You want to attack them, don't you? Then you'll have to take me down first, won't you?"

They probably didn't fully understand Rei's words as he muttered while holding the Twilight Spear.

But the Sand Lizardmen recognized Rei as their enemy with crystal clarity.

"KISHAAAAAAAAAAAAA!"

"Kishaa, Gyaa Gyaa Gyaa!"

"Gyaaaaaaah!"

Each let out a cry, pointing the tips of their weapons at Rei.

"These guys have tridents too? What? Is this weapon standard for them?"

Facing the three-pronged spears aimed at him, Rei voiced his confusion. But since they couldn't understand his words, the Sand Lizardmen wouldn't answer anyway.

Strangely enough, both Rei and the Sand Lizardmen now faced each other, spear in hand.

The four adventurers watching silently consulted each other with their eyes, wondering if they could escape now, but ultimately settled on watching quietly.

They still didn't know what kind of person Rei was, but there was no doubt he had helped them.

If Rei hadn't been human, they might have fled.

But even though he wore a hood and his face was hidden, Rei spoke words they could understand clearly.

It was only natural they felt a sense of familiarity far greater than with the Sand Lizardmen, who were completely incomprehensible.

Besides, if they were concerned about not being able to see his face under the hood, the four adventurers were in the same boat.

They were traveling through the desert, after all. To block out as much sunlight as possible... and to prevent their bodies from losing moisture, they covered their heads with turbans, trying to keep their faces shielded from the outside air as much as possible.

Since they'd come to help them, they were undoubtedly skilled adventurers and masters of the spear.

That's how the four adventurers saw Rei. But precisely because of that, they froze when the words that came out of Rei's mouth next hit them.

"What's the matter? Come on. I'm a novice with spears. Throwing one is one thing, but using one like this, held directly in my hand? I'm definitely not good at it."

"Whoa, wait a minute!?"

They had entrusted their lives to Rei precisely because they believed him to be overwhelmingly stronger than themselves.

So when Rei claimed his spear was only at a beginner's level, it was only natural the men were overcome with unease.

"Don't worry. Just because I'm a beginner with spears doesn't mean I'm a beginner with long weapons."

Seeing him turn his back and speak to the four adventurers, the Sand Lizardmen likely saw it as an opening. They moved in all at once.

Without making a sound, they sprinted toward Rei, tridents raised, trying not to alert her from behind.

"Hey, wait! Behind you! Behind you!"

"I know, okay!"

Nodding at the words of the man who seemed to be the leader of the four, he spun around to face the Sand Lizardman charging at him.

Simultaneously, he used that movement to sweep his Twilight Spear.

A series of sharp metallic clangs echoed around them.

The source of those sounds was, naturally, the Twilight Spear swung by Rei.

But what was truly astonishing was how effortlessly he had blocked every single trident attack the Sand Lizardmen had launched in that sequence.

(Damn it, with the Death Scythe, I could've easily knocked them all away from my hands...)

Still, Rei glanced at the Sand Lizardmen who had caught him off guard, looking dissatisfied.

The Death Scythe felt incredibly light in Rei and Set's hands... as light as a withered branch, yet it actually weighed around a hundred kilograms.

That weight is wielded as easily as a dead branch, further amplified by the combination of Rei's physical strength and magical power.

Naturally, most weapons would shatter under such a blow.

But Twilight Spear is different.

While it is a top-tier magic item, its weight is not an issue.

It would be different if magic power were infused, but since he was currently using the Sand Lizardman as a spear practice target, he wasn't doing that.

"Well, then, I just need to get used to fighting with a spear... right?!"

He swiftly retrieved the thrusting spear and thrust it forward again.

The thrust, unleashed with a fluid twist of his body—from the foot striking the sand, through his hips, back, arms, and wrist—didn't look like the strike of a novice spear user, no matter how you looked at it.

At least, to the eyes of the four adventurers, it looked like nothing less than a strike from a seasoned warrior.

Especially since one of the four, the only one wielding a spear himself, widened his eyes dramatically at Rei's strike.

"Sharp... What an incredibly sharp strike!"

Though most of her face was hidden like the others, her voice made it clear she was a woman.

From the woman's perspective, Rei's strike—perhaps due to his unfamiliarity with spear handling—was one where she could clearly point out areas needing correction.

But... even so, Rei's strike was undeniably powerful, and that very fact made the astonishment in the woman's muttered words all the more pronounced.

What Rei was doing was unleashing a strike comparable to—no, surpassing—the one the woman had delivered by maximizing her years of training. Rei was achieving it solely through physical ability and instinctive movement.

For the woman, Rei's attack was something she couldn't possibly understand... nor did she want to.

Unaware she was being viewed this way, Rei thrust her Twilight Spear into the forked tip of a Sand Lizardman's trident. The moment she saw it, she forcibly flicked the spear upward.

The three-pronged tip meant a wider attack range than a normal spear.

But it also meant that someone with sufficient skill and physical ability could even thrust their own weapon into the fork and disarm it.

Indeed, Rei's strike with the Twilight Spear had just proven that.

"Kishaa!?"

Sand Lizardman cried out, seemingly confused about what had just happened... but that was a huge opening.

"Hmph!"

There was no way Rei would let that opening go to waste. After flicking the trident away, he swiftly brought it back to his hand and thrust again.

The Twilight Spear, launched straight ahead, easily struck Sand Lizardman's head... and pierced right through it.

The sensation was different from when using Death Scythe to cleave flesh and bone.

There was a similar sensation when using the stone tip of the Death Scythe like a spear, but this time it was different, likely because it was the Twilight Spear.

"KISHAAAAAAAH!"

Seeing their comrade suddenly killed, the other Sand Lizardmen, enraged or excited, all let out threatening cries while thrusting their tridents forward.

"Whoa!"

Understanding that movement inevitably slows on the desert sand, Rei leaps to evade the trident strike.

But despite having their attack dodged, the Sand Lizardmen showed no particular sign of panic, each retracting their trident back to their hands.

They understood. Once he was airborne, he would be nearly immobilized.

Though Rei evading their strike was unexpected, they still judged that this would ultimately allow them to defeat him...

The Sand Lizardmen's reasoning was not wrong.

In fact, if Rei had been an ordinary human, their aim couldn't have been more correct.

But... the Sand Lizardmen's one and only, greatest miscalculation was that Rei was a person to whom the word "ordinary" simply did not apply.

The fact that he could move through the air using the Sleipnir Boots was, in particular, a complete miscalculation.

He used the Sleipnir Boots mid-jump, kicking off the air.

The Sand Lizardmen's attacks, launched in anticipation of his descent, were all evaded by that single movement.

Then, what Rei did next was kick the space beside him, also using the Sleipnir Boots.

Timed to coincide with the Sand Lizardmen's attempt to pull back their tridents, this attack proved devastating precisely because it exploited the inherent vulnerability of long-handled weapons when penetrated at close range.

...In terms of proximity, Rei wielding the Twilight Spear was similarly close, but the mindset differed between the Sand Lizardmen suddenly closing in and Rei, who had aimed for that very moment.

The butt of the Twilight Spear swept the foot of the closest Sand Lizardman, sending it tumbling.

He then delivered a sweeping blow with the spearhead, scooping up the Sand Lizardman's jaw as it landed on its backside in the sand.

That single blow proved fatal for the Sand Lizardman, who had its tongue hanging out.

Its sharp teeth ended up biting through its own tongue.

"KISHAAAAAAAAAAAHHH! GYAH!"

Seeing the Sand Lizard Man writhing in agony after biting through his own tongue, Rei stomped hard on its neck with the foot clad in Sleipnir's boot.

He felt the sensation of crushing the neck bones through his foot, but unlike spears, this feeling was not something he was unfamiliar with.

Without even glancing at the Sand Lizardman whose life had been taken by the snapped neck bones, Rei scanned the surroundings.

Having finally realized that Rei was no ordinary foe, the Sand Lizardmen attempted to flee en masse...

"Fools."

Watching the Sand Lizardmen turn their backs and flee, Rei murmurs softly.

Changing his grip from the usual spear hold to a throwing stance, he infused it with magic and unleashed the Twilight Spear.

...Not a single Sand Lizardman escaped. Their corpses were left exposed in the desert.
