A large crowd had already gathered at the location where the duel was to take place.

The largest group, unsurprisingly, consisted of Gilm's residents... ordinary citizens.

Many adventurers were also eager to witness Rei's strength with their own eyes.

Though Rei had become notorious in Gilm as someone to avoid, very few had actually witnessed his true power firsthand.

The Spring War was undoubtedly when the most people witnessed Rei's strength firsthand.

However, many had been unable or unwilling to participate in that war, and the number of adventurers arriving in Gilm after the war ended was by no means small.

Of course, they understood from fellow adventurers that Rei's strength wasn't just talk, and that exaggerated rumors weren't the only thing spreading wildly.

Still, for those who wanted to see that strength with their own eyes, this festival of duels was a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

I'd absolutely refuse to fight Rei myself, but watching others do it...

For merchants and common folk who weren't adventurers, this battle was nothing more than a good spectacle.

For those who had suffered at the Keep's hands—like having their goods taken after being harassed in their shops—it was only natural their cheers for Rei carried extra weight.

Apart from these common folk, the nobles also paid close attention to this duel.

...Though for the nobles loyal to the king, their thoughts were likely more along the lines of "Why is this even happening?"

Unlike Keep, who had arrived in Gilm just before winter, most nobles had lived in Gilm for years... some for over a decade.

Given that, they naturally gathered information thoroughly.

The extent of power held by Rei, who was cooperative with the neutral Daskar, was arguably the top priority information to gather.

Consequently, many saw the path to the Earl of Eriell's ruin as inevitable.

"Hey, look. There are a lot of knights."

"Yeah... Probably preparations to prevent an outburst if Lord Keep loses the duel."

"Fight Rei, and if he loses, he hands over half his fortune... What was Lord Keep thinking, agreeing to such terms for a duel?"

"And apparently, the reason was trying to steal a maid serving the Baroness Sisne household?"

"The Eriell family must be in a panic right now, huh? It wouldn't be surprising if he were disinherited."

"True. They probably never expected legal proceedings to be conducted under the supervision of Marquis Larks. With everything lined up like this, there's nothing the Count of Eriell's household can say to change it."

"As someone aligned with the nobles, I welcome the weakening of the royalist faction's power."

"...Is that so? The collapse of a house like the Counts of Eriell means the cancer within the royalist faction is eradicated. Doesn't that mean the royalists become healthier?"

"Surely... you didn't bring Lord Keep to this Gilm for that purpose?"

"Driving a wedge into the neutral faction and eliminating your own internal threat? True, if that happens, whichever side collapses..."

"Surely not. It must be coincidence. I doubt anyone could foresee that far ahead."

"Whoever painted this picture, I doubt even they imagined it would lead to conflict with Crimson."

King's faction, neutral faction, noble faction. Members of each faction exchanged words.

Amidst this, as if guided by the sunlight pouring down from the heavens, the main character made their appearance.

The first to appear was Rei.

As always, he wore his Dragon Robe, wielding a Death Scythe larger than himself, with the griffin Set at his side. Following behind were members of the Baron Cysne household: Muet, Baslero, and Asie.

Emerging from the opposite side was a figure unfamiliar to Rei.

He wore metal armor and held a halberd in his hand.

Purely in terms of physical size, he was a head taller than Rei... no, even more than that.

His physique was also more muscular compared to Rei's, and standing before him, he literally looked like an adult and a child.

In the hand opposite the halberd, he gripped a metal helmet, revealing a face of imposing presence.

"Whoa... who is that guy? Was there an adventurer like that in Gilm?"

One of the adventurers who had come to watch the duel called out to an acquaintance nearby upon seeing the opponent.

He'd been active in Gilm for several years, yet he had absolutely no recollection of a man like the one standing before Rei.

Of course, he didn't know every adventurer in Gilm, and being a frontier town, adventurers came and went frequently. Still, if the opponent was someone notable, it wouldn't be strange to know his name or face.

Among the nobles, too, the Earl of Eriell's faction was discussing who the person they'd brought out was, but no one knew his name or face.

Those with a certain level of skill, regardless of whether they were adventurers or nobles, understood that the man was a capable fighter.

Following behind such a man were Keip, Fultos, and three knights in attendance.

In the days leading up to the duel, they tried to learn what kind of man this Rei was. They even attacked the Baroness Sysne's household to target Muet, hoping to prevent Rei from appearing at the duel, or attempted to bribe Rei to bring him over to their side.

But all of it failed... and in the end, he was dragged out to the dueling grounds like this.

"Hey, Fultos. Are you sure that guy's okay? Sure, he looks better than the other guy, but..."

Keep couldn't hide his unease as he addressed Fultos beside him.

"It'll be fine. That man is a fairly well-known Rank B adventurer in Abuero. Even if Rei has a nickname, they're both Rank B... Besides, I think I mentioned our trump card for when push comes to shove."

Fultos answered Keep with a confident expression, but inside, he felt nothing short of uneasy.

For now, he'd managed to keep it off his face, but he didn't know how long that would last.

(Fundamentally, even among Rank B adventurers, their accumulated achievements are worlds apart. The other guy is the sort who could wipe out an entire first-rate squad single-handedly, right? Sure, that Zeros fellow is strong, as befits a Rank B adventurer. But I don't think he can beat Rei. So, if the secret move lands perfectly, that's good enough. If it fails, though...)

He glanced at Keep, who wore a worried expression, and swallowed hard.

(If there's a clear person in charge, it'll take time for pursuers to be sent even after I'm gone. There's even a chance they might not send anyone at all. If that's the case, then before the Earl of Eriell sends pursuers, I could escape to another country... Fortunately, thanks to Lord Keep, I have the financial means.)

As Fultos contemplated sacrificing Keep if necessary to ensure his own escape, Zeros, who had been facing him, finally spoke to Rei.

"So you're Rei, huh? You certainly don't look particularly strong just by appearances. But that scythe of yours isn't just for show, is it?"

Though Zeros said this, there was not a trace of complacency in his gaze toward Rei.

His expression clearly recognized Ray as a formidable foe, stronger than himself.

And yet, what appeared on Zeros' face was a savage grin.

Given his intimidating expression, calling it vicious wouldn't be wrong.

"...I see. So this is the kind of opponent they prepared for me—a battle maniac like you. With an opponent like you, I suppose I don't mind hearing a little about it. You're not an adventurer from Gilm, are you?"

"Correct. I'm an adventurer from Abuero. Rank B adventurer, Zeros."

Rei's expression shifted to one of understanding at those words.

Yet, his gaze remained unwaveringly wary.

Out of the corner of his eye, he watched Daskar, who held the Death Scythe and was also the witness to this duel, approach as he continued speaking.

"By participating in this duel, you've obviously prepared yourself to die. I can assume that's correct, right?"

"Hah. Prepared to die... Prepared to die, huh? Just standing here facing you, I can tell you're strong. But who says you're the one who'll win? I've climbed my way up by defeating many stronger opponents than me. I'm not losing here!"

With that shout, Zeros swung his halberd wide.

The strike, which seemed to shatter the wind itself, possessed a skill level that even Rei could recognize as Rank B.

"When I first heard about that noble, I thought I'd smash his skull with this halberd. But then I listened closer—turns out I get to fight the one called Crimson? Never have I been more proud of my own patience than I was back then."

"...Nah, you weren't patient at all."

Rei couldn't help but mutter at Zeroth's words.

Hearing Rei's words, Daskar approached and spoke with a faint smile.

"Now then, let the duel commence. The outcome will be determined when one side surrenders or becomes incapacitated. Should Rei prevail in this duel, half of the Count Eliel family's fortune shall become the property of the Baron Sisne family. Should Zeroth prevail, the custody of Asie, a maid of the Baron Sisne family, shall become Lord Keep's. No objections from either side?"

Muet and Keep each nodded at Daskar's words.

"Ha! What a pathetic excuse—that you can't get a woman without brute force!"

The voice that echoed from nowhere at that instant filled the dueling grounds with laughter a moment later.

"W-Who was that?! Show yourself!"

Keep, now the laughingstock of those gathered, shouted loudly, his earlier unease still lingering.

But the voice that came from within the crowd was impossible to pinpoint.

"Hey, Fron. Cut the nonsense. You're ruining my drink."

Brasso said to his partner, Fron, as he sipped from his wooden cup.

Because they were lost in the crowd, almost no one except those nearby would have realized the earlier shout was Fron's doing. Still, Brasso grumbled that if this delayed the duel, he wouldn't be able to enjoy his precious sake.

"Shut up. Seriously, what's with that Keep guy? Trying to pick up a woman and causing this huge ruckus? If you're a man, use your own charm to win her over, not your parents' money or power. ...Guess he's just not confident in his own charm, that's why he's making such a scene."

At Fron's words, those around him, both men and women, couldn't help but nod in agreement, their expressions showing understanding.

The few who looked slightly displeased were probably those who had experience seducing women or men using something other than their own charm in the past.

Had these words been spoken in the presence of nobles, it might have caused quite a stir.

Fortunately, Fron and Blasso were in an area where commoners gathered to watch the duel, so it didn't become an issue.

...Though whether they hadn't heard it was another matter entirely.

"Quiet! Lord Keep, return to your seat!"

"Baron Larks! Do you intend to let the man who insulted me go unpunished!?"

"...Lord Keep, if you do not return, I shall consider it your decision to participate in the duel yourself?"

"Guh..."

At Daskar's words, Keep growled in frustration but returned to his original position.

The thought that if he ended up fighting Rei, he would be killed on the grounds of a duel was too strong.

Considering the coldness in Rei's gaze when he looked at him, this was far from mere groundless worry.

(Damn it, you Gilm scum... All of you mocking me. Remember this! Once this duel is over, I'll make you regret that attitude!)

Keeping his inner rage hidden, he returned to his original spot and fell silent.

Then again, Keep wasn't accustomed to suppressing his emotions to begin with.

To anyone watching, it was impossible to mistake his expression for anything but suppressed rage.

His knight escorts searched for words to try and appease their lord, but they knew full well that saying anything careless now would only bring blame upon themselves, so they remained silent.

Normally, Fultos would step in to mediate at a time like this, but the current Fultos showed no sign of speaking.

The knights exchanged glances, silently confirming their intentions, but before they could actually urge Fultos to act, the situation began to shift.

"Well then, are we both ready?"

At those words, Rei readied his Death Scythe and Zeros his halberd as they faced each other...

"The duel between the Sisne and Eriell families is now to commence. ...Begin!"

As Daskar swung his arm dramatically, the surrounding spectators erupted in cheers.

Simultaneously, Rei and Zeros stepped forward, weapons in hand, closing the distance between them.

A faint smile played on Rei's face, while a ferocious grin spread across Zeros's. It perfectly reflected the state of their minds.
