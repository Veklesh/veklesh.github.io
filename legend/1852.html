The members of the Seisor Marquis family are making their move.

Rei heard this, but that didn’t mean there was anything he could do about it.

At the very least, even if Rei were to go to the Seisor Marquis family and ask, “Is something wrong?” they wouldn’t normally tell him.

After all, the fact that they were operating in secret meant they didn’t want anyone to know.

Given that, all Rei could do right now was to be ready to take immediate action if anything happened...

“Hmm, how about this?”

“Hmm… the effect, though. If it’s just about producing water, I actually have something similar to my magic item.”

And so, regarding Lemuria and yesterday’s incident… all he could do was negotiate for the gem containing magic as a reward for handing over the fresh Set feather.

That said, Rei’s mind was already focused on selecting a gem containing usable magic, and the matter involving the Marquis of Seisor’s household was only in the back of his mind.

“You say it just produces water, but the amount of water this gem can produce is quite substantial, you know? Enough to fill this entire room, in fact.”

Hearing Remuria say this, Rei looked around the room he was currently in once more.

This room is about thirty tatami mats in size.

Of course, there aren’t actually tatami mats here, and since this is just a rough estimate based on Rei’s perception, the actual size might vary slightly.

“Enough to fill this whole room, huh? …I see. Now that I think about it, it does seem like it could be useful.”

As Rei said that, what came to Rei’s mind was a fire.

With enough water to fill a room of about thirty tatami mats, even if a full-scale fire is out of the question, it might not be impossible to extinguish a small fire in an instant... maybe.

(But if it’s just about putting out a fire, it’d be quicker to simply demolish the area around the burning building or whatever than to use water to extinguish it.)

In fact, the method of destroying the surroundings of a burning building to prevent the fire from spreading is widely known, and it’s frequently done here in Elgin as well.

If you do something like that, the people whose homes or other property are destroyed will be unhappy.

It’s only natural for them to think that if the fire had been successfully extinguished before it spread, their home wouldn’t have been destroyed.

Still, since it’s a quicker method than getting water, it’s only natural that it’s used so often.

“Hmm. So you don’t like this one. Well then, how about this one?”

Next, Lemuria produced a green gemstone... one that resembled an emerald.

That said, Rei wasn’t exactly an expert on gems, so he couldn’t tell if it was actually an emerald or not.

Rei studied the gem closely.

He would receive five gems with magic sealed inside as a reward.

Given that, he had to be extremely selective.

“What does this gem do?”

“When aimed in a specific direction… well, it can unleash a barrage of wind blades, each about the size of a dagger.”

“Huh, that’s handy.”

In terms of launching blades, one could say that Rei’s Death Scythe’s Flying Slash is similar.

However, while the slashes unleashed by the Flying Slash pack a massive punch and have a wide area of effect—which makes sense, since it’s a strike from a scythe rather than a longsword—it can only fire a single slash at a time.

In that sense, the green gem Remuria showed is by no means a bad thing.

It probably wouldn’t be all that useful against a powerful enemy, but against opponents that overwhelm you with numbers—like goblins, for instance—it’s undoubtedly an effective tool.

……Since Rei specializes in wide-area annihilation magic, she has several other similar attack methods at her disposal, but even so, it’s better to have more options.

“What’s the specific range? Since the wind blades are about the size of a dagger, I guess the power of a single strike isn’t that great, right?”

In response to Rei’s question, Lemuria shared everything she knew.

As Rei had predicted, the power of a single strike isn’t that great.

However, since it’s directed in a single direction, the effective range is fairly wide, and above all, the fact that there’s no need to chant a spell like when Rei uses magic is a huge advantage.

Since the magic itself is already sealed within the gem, all he has to do is channel his magical power with the intention of activating it, much like using a magic item.

Needless to say, chanting spells is a major hassle for mages.

While chanting a spell, one inevitably has to focus on that task, which means vigilance toward one’s surroundings is compromised.

For top-tier mages like Rei—or those even better—it’s almost impossible to get hit while casting a spell, but it’s not a guarantee.

……That said, in Rei’s case, he can pull off tricks like casting spells while riding Set high in the sky, where enemy attacks can’t reach him.

In any case, that merely highlights Rei as a rare exception; for ordinary mages, spellcasting is undoubtedly a hassle.

Given that, this gem—which allows magical effects to be unleashed without the need for spellcasting—was undoubtedly a most welcome item.

No, since it’s a magic item that even non-mages can use, it’s undoubtedly a very convenient item for non-mages as well.

That said, there’s no doubt that acquiring it would require a considerable sum of money.

This time, I received this gem as a reward for handing over some of Set’s fresh feathers.

But if I were to buy this gem, it certainly wouldn’t be something I could afford for just any old price.

Even considering the gem’s intrinsic value alone, it’s already a valuable item—and on top of that, magic is sealed within it.

There was absolutely no way such a magic item could be cheaper than a regular gem.

(I’d seen a priest use this gem in Exil before, and it seemed like a pretty handy item. No, actually, if you could buy that with money, I’d consider yourself incredibly lucky.)

Setting aside exceptions like Rei’s Death Scythe, Dragon Robe, and Misty Ring, items like Sleipnir Boots, the Twilight Spear, and the Dagger of Flowing Water… most of the other magic items I possess are also extremely rare.

Given that, even if you wanted to buy these magic items with money, it would normally be impossible.

So, if I could buy the gems Lemuria possesses with money, that would be nothing short of pure luck.

“Well, I’ll take one of these green gems for now. As for the rest… do you have any other recommendations?”

Several gems of various colors, shapes, and sizes are laid out on the table, but even looking at them, Rei has no idea what specific effects they might have.

Given that, it’s only natural to think it would be better to ask Lemuria directly.

“That’s right. How about this one?”

In response to Rei’s words, Lemuria pointed to a white gem.

It wasn’t exactly a transparent crystal… it was simply a pure white gem.

If it were cloudy, one might reasonably assume that the attempt to imbue it with magic had failed, but its whiteness was clearly not cloudy—it was pure white.

“What’s this?”

“This gem has a powerful healing spell sealed inside it.”

“Huh.”

Rei gazes at the pure white gem with interest.

Healing magic is a type of magic that even among the already scarce number of mages, very few are capable of using.

Even within the Crimson Wings led by Rei, the only one capable of using healing magic is Marina, who possesses exceptionally high abilities as a spirit mage.

To begin with, simply having someone in the party who can use healing magic is a stroke of incredible luck... which should give you a clear idea of just how rare healing mages really are.

“You mentioned ‘powerful healing magic,’ but specifically, what kind of injuries can it heal? What about life-threatening ones?”

“It can’t go that far. But if it’s just a broken bone, I can heal it instantly.”

“...A broken bone, huh?”

There are various types of fractures.

Depending on the body part, some can be quite difficult to heal, and even for the same body part, the difficulty of treatment varies depending on how the bone is broken.

But that’s only true for conventional treatment; when it comes to magic, the situation changes drastically.

I can’t completely ignore those factors, but there’s no doubt it’s much easier compared to treating them the conventional way.

“I’ll take it. Make sure the rest are all the kind where the healing magic has been sealed, too.”

“Oh my, you’re quite decisive, aren’t you?”

Though she wore a slightly surprised expression, Lemuria looked convinced by Rei’s decision.

After all, since there are so few practitioners of healing magic, one can never have too many means of recovery.

Besides healing magic, there are potions and other items that provide healing effects.

However, healing magic is more effective than potions, and it’s also faster in terms of treatment speed.

Given that, healing magic is clearly the superior option.

“Well, having healing options is just convenient in all sorts of ways.”

What Rei is thinking about as he says this is likely what might happen in an emergency.

He didn’t know exactly when or how such a situation might arise, but he absolutely didn’t want to die because he hadn’t prepared for that emergency and couldn’t heal his injuries.

Of course, this wasn’t just for Rei; it was also for the others in his party… and even if they weren’t in a party, having a way to immediately treat Elena if she were injured would surely be a huge advantage.

(Well, I can’t really imagine a situation where Elena would ever be in such a pinch, though.)

The Elena Rei knows is incredibly powerful, having inherited the magic stone of an Ancient Dragon.

So much so that Vihera, a battle maniac, has asked her to spar with her countless times.

“So… is this okay?”

Saying that, Lemuria handed Rei one green gem that fired multiple wind blades in a specific direction, and four white gems imbued with healing magic.

Rei took them, checked the gems, and nodded in response.

“Yeah, this is fine. …By the way, I just want to ask—does the color of this gem magic change depending on the attribute of the sealed spell? As far as I know, I don’t think that’s how it works.”

Rei said this, but he didn’t know all the gem magic that Pri had shown him in Exil.

Still, the gem magic he’d seen during his battle with Pri didn’t seem to change color based on its attribute.

...For Pri, who harbored an extraordinary affection for gems, it was simply inconceivable that she would tolerate the gem’s beauty being compromised as a result of sealing magic within it.

“Hm? Oh, you mean that? Well, in the original… the gem magic techniques I acquired, that was indeed the case, but I’d like you to think of it as a change brought about by not using sacrifices.”

“Ah… I see. Now that you put it that way, I guess I have no choice but to accept it.”

Rei didn’t understand all the details, but he grasped the general idea.

The drawback of being able to seal magic into gems without requiring a sacrifice was that the gem’s color changed depending on its attribute.

(Wait, maybe it’s not really a drawback? For someone like Puri, it wouldn’t be surprising if she knew which magic was sealed in which gem, but for someone unfamiliar with gems, being able to tell what kind of magic is sealed just by the color is actually a blessing.)

Of course, that also means that if the enemy has someone knowledgeable about gem magic, they’ll easily be able to figure out what kind of magic you’re trying to use.

“Well then, I’ll take this gem. If anything else comes up, feel free to hire me again. Though I’d be happy if you paid me with another magic-sealed gem next time.”

“Ho ho ho. If that’s the case, I can’t just casually hire Rei. Sealing magic into gems is quite a labor-intensive process, you see. Unless it’s an emergency like this time, I can’t just use them as payment so easily.”

In response to Rei’s words, Lemuria replied.

In fact, though Rei didn’t know it, the cost of sealing magic into a gemstone was enormous.

……The main reason for that is likely the fact that, unlike Puri, who used sacrificial offerings to create them, he was doing it without using any.

“Well then, if there’s ever a job where paying with that gem as a reward is acceptable, give me a call.”

Saying that, Rei stored the gem in his Misty Ring and left the room.
