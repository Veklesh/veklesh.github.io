As I fly through the sky on Set’s back, a town eventually comes into view.

It is Golz.

It’s the town Rei and his companions were heading for.

“It’s smaller than I expected. …Well, considering the towns around here, it might actually be on the larger side.”

Naturally, the town Rei was comparing Golz to was Gilm.

...However, even though they are both towns, it’s actually the wrong thing to compare them.

Originally, even though Gilm was called a town, it was actually large enough to be considered a city.

Now that it had become cramped, they were expanding it through construction to officially reach the scale of a city, but comparing such a place to a town in the countryside was, no matter how you looked at it, a mistake.

In any case, comparing the size of the towns didn’t really mean anything. If anything, it was just enough to let him bask in a slight sense of superiority because he was based in Gilm.

“I guess we made it before noon, somehow.”

Saying that, Rei recalled this morning.

They were supposed to arrive by morning.

The main reason it dragged on until nearly noon was the mock battle between Vihera and Relu.

Of course, there’s no doubt that Rei or Elena could have provided a fight that would satisfy Vihera.

Still, since she was able to enjoy fighting a different opponent for a change—one who was also appropriately strong—Vihera had become quite engrossed in the mock battle.

As a result, it took a long time to get ready to leave, and even though they arrived in Golz in the morning, it was almost noon by the time they got there.

“If we’d left early in the morning, the people heading for the cliff would probably have been gathering there, so maybe it was a good thing we left at this time.”

Because an entrance to what appeared to be a dungeon had formed on the cliff face, many monsters had fallen from there and died.

In other words, if you went to the cliff first thing in the morning, you could almost certainly find the corpses of monsters that had fallen to their deaths during the night, allowing you to obtain materials, magic stones, and proof-of-defeat parts without any risk.

Naturally, Rei could easily imagine adventurers—or even non-adventurers, in some cases—rushing to the cliff in search of these items.

(It’s not like there’s absolutely no risk, though.)

Even if a monster falls off the cliff and crashes into the ground, if it’s lucky, it might not die.

If that happens, anyone who carelessly approaches it will be caught off guard and suffer serious damage.

What’s more, depending on the monster, it might land on the ground smoothly and lie in wait nearby without a scratch.

If one focuses solely on not falling behind the others and tries to secure the monster as quickly as possible, that could become a fatal opening.

Of course, adventurers are ultimately responsible for themselves.

Given that, it was only natural that they would have to handle the situation themselves even if they were ambushed by a monster.

“Well then, Set. Let’s just go.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl and flew toward Golz.

Due to the effect of the Set Cage, it was difficult to spot Set from the ground.

For that reason, no one noticed even as Set drew near.

Besides, even if Set were flying through the sky, there aren’t many people around here who would be watching the sky.

Flying monsters rarely appear in this area, and there are no dragon knights to be found here either.

So, when the sound of the Set Cage being lowered to the ground echoed through the area, the few passersby looked around in astonishment, wondering what had happened, and only then did they finally notice the Set Cage.

And in the next instant, Set, who had lowered the Set Cage, touched down.

“What the—!?”

A merchant standing right next to Set let out a cry of surprise and stood there dumbfounded.

It was only natural. Before he knew it, the Set Cage was suddenly there, and Set—a Rank A monster, a griffin—was standing right there.

The only reason he didn’t run away was probably because Set was carrying Rei on his back.

After dismounting from Set Cage, Rei glanced at Elena and the others as they climbed down from the Set Cage, then addressed the merchant—a man in his forties who was staring at her in astonishment.

“Excuse me, but are you sure that town is Golz?”

“……Huh? Ah, yeah. It is Golz… but who on earth are you…?”

Who is he? That’s what he wanted to ask, but perhaps his mind wasn’t working properly due to the shock, and he couldn’t put it into words.

If his mind had been working properly, he might have remembered that the person standing before him was Rei, known by the nickname “Crimson.”

And while Rei was talking to the merchant, people passing by gathered to see what was going on.

If Rei and Set had started causing a commotion, those people would have fled immediately.

But seeing them talking like this, it was clear that neither Rei nor Set were dangerous.

Given that, it’s only natural that people would take an interest in Rei, Set, the Set Cage, and those who had emerged from it… At the same time, Rei noticed several guards approaching from Golz.

While those watching from a distance had their attention captured by the suddenly appearing griffin… and the three exquisite beauties, the guards made their way over to Rei and the others.

“Who… are you?”

Under normal circumstances, it wouldn’t be strange to ask this in a stern tone, even if only to intimidate a suspicious-looking group.

But in this particular case, there was simply no other way to describe it: they were up against the wrong crowd.

With Set and Elena and the others, it was obvious from any angle that they were definitely a group with a story.

...That’s probably why his tone sounded somewhat hesitant, as if he were sizing them up.

“I’m Rei. I’m an adventurer. I heard there’s a dungeon around here.”

Hearing that and seeing his guild card, the guard understood why Rei and the others had come.

He looked at the rank listed on the card and took another look at Rei, but eventually, his curiosity got the better of him, and he spoke up.

“So that really is a dungeon?”

Rei wasn’t sure if the guard realized he was Rei—the adventurer known by the alias “Crimson”—but he was slightly surprised that the guard had asked about the dungeon, and he gave a nod in response.

“Yeah. Based on the intel I’ve gathered, I’m pretty sure that’s right. Of course, I can’t say for certain, so I won’t know for sure until I actually go inside.”

“……I see.”

It was a brief exchange, but those who had been listening—especially the merchant Rei had spoken to first—widened their eyes in surprise.

They had a vague sense that what was on the cliff was a dungeon.

Still, since no one had actually confirmed it, they likely harbored doubts—wondering if it might just be a monster’s lair or something similar.

Of course, the merchant wasn’t the only one surprised by Rei’s words.

Those around them who had been listening to the exchange between Rei and the guard were also taken aback by his words.

However, as Rei looked at the people around him, he realized that amidst their surprise, there were two distinct reactions—joy and sadness—and he felt a little puzzled.

“So, is it okay for me to go inside Golz? I’d like to gather some information at the guild and find a place to stay.”

“Ah, yeah. That’s right. If we have an adventurer like Rei, we’d be happy to have you. But…”

As if finding it hard to say, the guard turned his gaze toward Set.

……There, a scene unfolded in which Relieu, who had moved over unnoticed, was petting Set.

“What’s wrong with Set?”

“There’s only one inn in Golz that allows familiars to stay. It’s one of the more upscale inns in Golz, but… um, is that okay with you?”

At the guard’s words, Rei immediately replied that it wasn’t a problem.

He understood, of course, that there weren’t many inns with facilities capable of accommodating familiars like Set, and that any inn possessing such facilities was, naturally, one that could afford to invest in them.

Such inns were the kind where large caravans, mercenary groups, and even nobles visiting Golz would naturally stay, and naturally, the room rates at such places were high.

When the guard asked if that was still acceptable, Rei nodded in agreement.

“I see. Well then, let’s get the paperwork done quickly. If we stay here any longer, people will just keep gathering.”

At the guard’s words, Rei—and the others—looked at the people gathering around them.

The number of people gathered there had undoubtedly increased since a moment ago.

As noon approached, the number of people passing through the highway was by no means as high as it was in the morning or evening.

Still, as Golz was the most prosperous town in the area, a fair number of people were heading there even in the middle of the day.

Although Rei and the others were used to attracting attention, being accustomed to it didn’t mean they didn’t feel annoyed.

Deciding that being a spectacle here wasn’t fun, they stowed the Set Cage in the Misty Ring and immediately headed for Golz.

……Many people were surprised by the way he stowed the Set Cage, but for Rei, that was a reaction he was already used to.

And so, accompanied by Elena and the others—who were drawing even more attention than he was—and Set, who was being doted on by Relieu, Rei headed toward Golz, which was visible in the distance.

(That Relu guy… he’s not even trying to hide how much he dotes on Set anymore.)

Out of the corner of his eye, Rei watched Relieu walking beside Set with a happy smile and thought to himself.

At first, he’d tried to hide how much he doted on Set.

That much was clear if you thought back to when Rei had gone to the bandits’ hideout.

But after spending some time traveling with Rei and the others, Relu no longer hides his affection for Set.

(I wonder how many people, seeing Relieu like this, would realize he’s the man known as “Gale”?)

Even as they watched Relieu stroking Set, Rei and the others kept moving forward, eventually arriving at Golz.

Since they had already shown their guild cards, the formalities for entering Golz didn’t take much time.

After asking the guard who processed their entry for directions to their intended inn, Rei and the others headed there.

Fortunately, the inn was not too far from the main gate.

High-end inns like this are often located near the main gate for easy access, or on streets lined with shops frequented by the wealthy.

Of course, if we’re talking about inns actually used by nobles—especially those with high titles—the latter is overwhelmingly more common.

The inn Rei and his companions had decided to stay at this time, while it might be classified as a high-class inn in that sense, was probably not a truly high-class inn in the strictest sense.

As Rei and the others walked down Golz’s main street, they naturally drew various glances from the locals—looks of curiosity, admiration, jealousy, and awe.

Despite those stares, they continued walking down the main street...

“Ah, isn’t that it over there?”

Vihera said, pulling on the hand of Byune, who was looking longingly at the bread being sold at a nearby food stall.

Rei followed Vihera’s gaze and saw a large building ahead, with a sign reading “Green Spring Pavilion.” He nodded.

“Looks like we’ve arrived.”

“Hmm… ‘Green Sawasui-tei,’ huh. It seems like a name that doesn’t quite fit the scale of the inn.”

At Marina’s words, the others—including Rei—nod in agreement.

When you hear “Sawamizu,” you probably imagine a small stream deep in the mountains—barely even worthy of being called a river.

It might not actually be the case, but at least that’s the image in Rei’s mind.

Since they agreed, it’s easy to guess that the others probably had a similar image in mind.

Yet this inn, named Sawamizu, is quite large.

It’s not surprising that this feels a bit off.

“Well, the name of the inn doesn’t really matter, does it? As long as we can stay there comfortably, that’s all that counts. As far as I know, there was even an inn called ‘Goblin’s Inn’?”

At the mention of that name from Relieu’s mouth, everyone… no, even a passerby who happened to be walking by, grimaced in disgust.

Goblins are the very epitome of bad meat. A name derived from their innards was, naturally, one that made people want to steer clear at all costs.

Though Relieu was met with disapproving glances, he didn’t seem to mind in the least as he walked into the inn.

Watching Relieu’s retreating figure with looks of exasperation, Rei and the others also entered the inn.

All the while, they resolved to ask Set whether they could at least use the stables.
