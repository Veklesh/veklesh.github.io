As the winter chill began to ease and the falling snow grew lighter... Rei walked down Gilm's main street with Vihera.

Usually, Byune and Set would be here with them, but today it was just Rei and Vihera.

Meanwhile, Marina seemed to be in the final stages of her handover as Guildmaster and was working with Walker today.

As for why Rei and Vihera were out together alone today of all days...

"Hey, Rei. Since we're out alone together, why not look a little happier?"

Vihera, holding hands with Rei, said this with a beaming smile.

...Yes, the reason they were out together like this was for a date.

Now that they were forming a party, opportunities to be alone together would be rare.

Therefore, today was for a date with Vihera... and once the handover was fully complete in a few days, it would be Rei's turn.

The idea had first come from Vihera during a conversation in Rei's room at the Dusk Wheat inn.

Naturally, Elena was present via the Paired Orb and voiced her opposition... but somehow, Rei and even Byune were kicked out of the room. After the three women discussed it, Elena conceded to Vihera's words, and thus the date was set.

"I'm trying to look happy, at least,"

He said, removing the hood of his Dragon Robe.

As if announcing the end of winter and the arrival of spring, the sky was a cloudless blue.

The sun floating in that blue sky shone so brightly it was hard to believe winter still lingered.

"Really? Well, if Rei's happy, that's fine... Oh, look. Maybe because the weather's nice today, there are a lot of food stalls. Shall we get something?"

"Yeah, I guess. Well, lots of stalls probably means lots of people with too much time on their hands."

Even adventurers, if they spend every day in idleness, inevitably start wanting to do something else.

Of course, it depends on the person, but those running stalls like this to kill time are probably that type.

Still, since they're running stalls just to kill time, it's only natural they can't compete with the professionals.

Still, they sell reasonably well—perhaps because the unseasonably warm winter weather has put passersby in a relaxed mood.

Still, some adventurers possess such innate talent that they can cook dishes rivaling professional chefs. That's why Rei scans the many stalls, searching for one like that.

"Hey, Rei. Not a food stall, but that one looks kinda interesting?"

Just as she was scanning the area for something delicious, Vihera suddenly tugged at Rei's hand.

True to her words about it looking interesting, Vihera's eyes sparkled with curiosity.

Following Vihera's gaze, Rei looked over and understood—yes, this was exactly the kind of thing Vihera would love.

What stood there wasn't the kind of food stall Rei had been looking at... but rather something closer to street performance.

Several adventurers stood there, surveying the crowd with confident smiles.

"Alright, alright! The challenge fee is two silver coins! Two coins! Then you pick one person from this group. You pull on a rope tied to each other, trying not to let the other step outside the circle on the ground. The winner is whoever makes their opponent let go of the rope or step outside the circle! The prize money changes depending on your opponent!"

To Rei, this contest... or game... seemed like a hybrid of tug-of-war and sumo.

Due to various restrictions, it required more than just brute strength—you also had to read your opponent's mind and outmaneuver them.

That aspect must have piqued Vihera's interest too.

"Shall we give it a try?"

"Sure. Since we're here, let's give it a try."

At Rei's words, Vihera smiled with amusement and approached someone looking for challengers.

Naturally, Rei followed after Vihera... and the recruiter noticed the two of them, his cheeks twitching.

Being an adventurer himself, this recruiter recognized Rei's face.

Moreover, he knew Vihera, who was traveling with Rei.

And above all, he understood perfectly well that these two were extraordinary in every sense of the word.

(Oh crap... what do I do?)

Since they were heading straight for him, it was certain they were challenging him.

The man, who didn't believe he could beat those two purely on physical ability, quietly glanced at his companions.

But every single one of those who caught his gaze nodded back at him.

If this were a pure display of strength... something like a mock battle, the men wouldn't have nodded so readily.

But this game was fairly well-known back in their hometown.

That's precisely why, in this game called En-Himo, they thought they might just stand a chance against Vihera and Rei.

After all, they had beaten higher-ranked adventurers at this very game before.

They didn't think they could easily beat Rei or Vihera, but perhaps... that thought lingered.

"Then, I'll take the strongest one."

And so, Vihera challenged the strongest opponent among the men.

He was the one who always placed at the top in the rope-tugging contests held in the village.

"Understood. Then I'll give you my all. Normally I'd ask if you need me to hold back, but... I suppose there's no need to ask you that."

"Yes. I'll give it my all."

Vihera declared this with a broad smile at the man's words.

She seemed to take a liking to his attitude.

After paying the challenge fee, Vihera and the man each gripped a rope and stood on the circle.

Then, the man who had been recruiting challengers shouted, stirring up the crowd's reaction.

"Now then, can the lovely warrior maiden defeat this villain?! You can't miss this great match!"

"Hey, wait a minute!"

His companion muttered discontentedly, muttering under his breath as he held the rope, annoyed that the man had so easily discarded him and instead cast Vihera as the righteous heroine.

But the man addressed didn't seem particularly bothered, declaring the game's start.

"Begin!"

At that word, the first to react wasn't the man accustomed to the game... but Vihera, whose reflexes were simply superior.

As if determined to settle the match in one fell swoop, she yanked the rope. But the man also knew that the round rope was the strongest among them.

He relaxed the grip on the rope, causing Vihera to suddenly stumble backward from the recoil of her opponent's sudden release of force.

But Vihera, as if anticipating the man's move, braced his feet and refused to budge from the circle.

Instead, seeing the man's weakened grip as his chance, he pulled the rope with all his might...

"Ah!"

The man realized the rope was vanishing from his grasp before his eyes. Desperately trying to stop it, he clenched his fist around the rope... but by the time he did, the rope had already completely disappeared from his hand.

"The winner is the warrior maiden, Vihera!"

The announcer's voice echoed around them.

The man who had been fighting Vihera probably hadn't expected to lose so easily.

He could only stare blankly at Vihera.

"Well, well, impressive. As expected of an adventurer who travels with Rei. I never imagined you'd win so easily..."

"Is that so? But this time, it was purely my physical ability that led to this. Next time, though, it probably won't be so easy to win."

The words from Vihera's mouth snapped the stunned man back to his senses.

He turned his gaze toward Vihera and nodded back with confidence.

"Of course! Next time we face Enchuu, I won't lose!"

"Heh heh, I suppose so. I'll be looking forward to it."

Vihera smiled at the man and accepted two gold coins as her prize.

Vihera was surprised by the unexpectedly high reward, but it was likely due to her absolute confidence in her rope skills.

Moreover, she had a proven track record of defeating every challenger who had ever faced the man.

The belief that no one could defeat Vihera in the rope game had completely backfired.

Still, today's earnings already exceeded two gold coins at this point, so it wasn't a loss.

Rei considered challenging him too, but when the emcee gave her a pleading look, begging her to stop, she couldn't refuse... and ultimately gave up.

"Hey, Rei. Don't look so disappointed. Since you're with me, you could at least try to look a little happier... But, well. Want something to eat? We got the prize money, so I'll treat you within that budget?"

"Hmm... Well then. How about we get a sandwich from that food stall over there? It smells pretty good and looks tasty."

Rei's gaze fell on a single food stall.

It looked like an ordinary stall, and there weren't many customers.

But the aroma wafting from it definitely stimulated Rei's appetite.

At Rei's words, Vihera also focused her attention on the food stall.

Sure enough, just as Rei had said, an appetite-whetting aroma wafted through the air.

It wasn't exactly stimulating, but rather nostalgic... a scent that evoked a sense of longing.

Though Rei and Vihera had grown up in different environments—indeed, different worlds—the impression they both felt from the drifting scent was the same.

"Right. Well then, shall we eat? ...If it weren't snowing, we could have taken our time more."

Vihera took Rei's hand and started walking toward the food stall.

Rei offered no resistance, speaking as they walked hand-in-hand with Vihera.

"Yeah. But spring's almost here. When it comes... maybe we could all go somewhere together? We could combine it with a gathering request and have a picnic or something."

"Oh, that sounds lovely. I'd love to relax by a lake sometime. ...Or maybe even the sea, though that would take too long."

"Ah... true. If we rode the Set, it wouldn't take that long to reach the sea, but that's where things get a bit tricky when you're traveling with a party."

That was the biggest downside to forming a party.

If it were just Rei... well, more accurately, just Rei and Set, then Rei could ride Set anytime.

Considering Set's speed, it literally meant a difference of orders of magnitude compared to walking normally.

But the only one who could ride on Set's back and fly was Rei... well, Byune was still a child and light, so only Rei and Byune together.

And even then, if both Rei and Byune rode on Set's back together, Set's flying distance would be limited.

Of course, clinging to Set's ankles for movement, like when searching for Ambris, posed no problem. Yet entrusting all aerial maneuvering to Set carried various dangers, and no one willingly preferred that method.

...Vihera, however, delighted in clinging to Set and flying through the air.

In any case, as long as they were traveling as a party, compromises like that were unavoidable.

"Speaking of which, what happened with the carriage?"

"Marina said she'd look into it for us?"

Vihera answered as she handed half of her sandwich to Rei after placing her order.

"Ah, right, Marina did say that. ...But what kind of carriage do you think it'll be?"

As Rei walked down the street, sandwich in hand, he asked. Vihera shook her head.

"I don't know what kind of carriage you're expecting, Rei, but I think it'll probably be a regular one?"

"...I guess that's how it'll be."

Rei secretly wished for a magic item carriage like the one Elena rode in, one with an expanded interior. Naturally, such a carriage was extremely expensive, and even if you had the money, it wasn't something you could simply buy.

It required connections, materials, luck... all sorts of things.

Even knowing that, having experienced Elena's carriage, it was only natural to crave a comfortable ride.

It was probably similar to how, once you'd grown accustomed to luxury, it was hard to go back to anything less.

"I really wanted that carriage Elena rode in, if possible."

"...If we, who just formed a party, had a carriage like that, wouldn't we get dragged into all sorts of troublesome incidents?"

"That problem... well, the fact that we formed a party in the first place means we're already surrounded by enough troublesome events, right?"

Rei, the Rank B adventurer with a notorious nickname; Set, the griffin; Marina, the rare dark elf and spirit magic user; and Vihera, the former princess of the Bestir Empire. ...And the latter two were stunning beauties who stirred men's desires to the extreme.

The least conspicuous is Byune, but even she is a survivor of a bloodline holding great significance within the Labyrinth City.

"...You're right about that. But more importantly, since today is our special date, let's not think about anything else and just enjoy our time together."

Rei nodded at Vihera's words, spoken with a smile... On this day, as winter drew to a close, they enjoyed their time alone together.
