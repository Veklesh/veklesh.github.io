Even the excited Daldora couldn't help but calm down when Rei thrust the Death Scythe at him.

If he made a real fuss here, what would become of him...? The Death Scythe possessed a sharpness that made him think just that.

The coldness of the blade touching his throat made him think it could cut his life down like mowing grass... no, hunt it down.

"...Hmm, ahem. It seems I got a bit too excited. My apologies."

At Daldora's words, his guards, Augusto, Zarust, and the others decided to calm down for the moment and resumed sharing information.

"So, about the attackers you captured, Augusto... they truly know nothing, you say?"

"Yes. Since they were treated as disposable pawns even by those who hired them, I doubt they'd share any information with such people."

"...Is that your conclusion after interrogating them?"

Augusto nodded in response to Daldora's question.

At the same time, Zarust, standing behind Augusto, also nodded in agreement.

Daldora, who had been watching the two intently, eventually let out a small sigh and nodded.

He probably wasn't hiding anything; the captured man truly knew nothing.

(I came here knowing the risks... but honestly, it's nothing but a disappointment. I never imagined I wouldn't get any information at all.)

Of course, he hadn't expected coming here would reveal everything.

But even so, she had assumed she would uncover at least some details.

This was the moment that hope vanished completely, and Daldora concluded there was nothing to be done about the current situation.

(So then, what should we do from here? ...No, since we've come this far, perhaps we should at least exchange information?)

He turned his gaze to the afternoon sunlight streaming through the window and pondered.

Having deliberately risked danger to reach this mansion on the edge of Gosh, Daldora concluded that leaving without any results would be a complete waste of time. He opened his mouth to address Augusto.

"About today's attack... What are your thoughts, Augusto?"

That question coming was probably just as expected for Augusto. He answered without any particular sign of haste.

"Well... the most reasonable explanation would be a third party attempting to fully escalate the conflict between myself and Daldora..."

"You think so too?"

Daldora agreed with Augusto's words, finding no room for doubt.

"Yes. I know it's awkward to say this in front of you, Daldora, but anyone somewhat familiar with the situation in Gosh knows about the conflict between us. ...Well, perhaps 'conflict' isn't quite right; it's more accurate to call it a situation where I'm at a one-sided disadvantage."

"No, no, that's not quite right. After all, Augusto has acquired a trump card in the form of Rei."

"That's true. Having Rei-san cooperate with me is a very welcome development. But even so, there are still many difficulties, so I can't say things have gotten easier."

They conversed with smiles on their faces, yet the substance of their talk encompassed even the possibility of their own deaths.

Precisely because they understood this, Zarust and Daldora's guards sensed the danger that one day, frustration might boil over and cause an explosion.

Daldora's guards were particularly tense, having witnessed Rei's true power firsthand.

If a fight broke out in this confined space, they knew they stood absolutely no chance.

Many had already sensed Rei's power to some extent, but actually seeing that strength with their own eyes was naturally enough to shake them deeply.

Completely oblivious to the tension around them, the two merchants continued their conversation.

"So, what do you think the motive was for whoever tried to escalate the conflict between me and Daldora?"

"Logically, it seems like they wanted to spark some kind of large-scale conflict in the city..."

"Yes. Logically, that's what it seems like. But despite that, they came after me with the intent to kill me for real. ...At first, I thought it might have been the killer's own initiative... but Daldora was also targeted with the same deadly intent."

"Yes."

The first attackers were three men whose pain receptors had been numbed.

Thanks to the strength of the guards, starting with Narsus, it took some effort, but we managed to defeat them.

But then came the countless arrows fired, using those three as decoys.

Those arrows were undoubtedly aimed at Daldora's life.

Had Narsus not shielded him in that instant, the arrows would have pierced Daldora's head without fail.

"So, their goal isn't to escalate the conflict, but to take my life and Augusto's... But if you ask who would do such a thing..."

Daldora shook his head with a sigh. Augusto nodded back, sighing just as deeply.

Even when they'd discussed it with Zarust and Rei before Daldora arrived, they'd ultimately been troubled, unable to figure out the identity of this third party.

"If there's any possibility, it would be members of other organizations eyeing the interests here in Gosh..."

Even as he said this, Daldora privately judged that line of possibility to be almost nonexistent.

He was confident that if any merchant guilds or other organizations were hostile to the Elema Trading Company, they would have been caught in his information network.

(If that were the case, it would be outside Gosh... but even the closest towns or villages would be quite far away.)

The question remained: would residents of such towns or villages really target their interests?

"My lord... it couldn't be the feudal lord, could it?"

"No."

Daldora cut off Augusto's words.

Daldora understood she was absolutely essential to Ryubrand's operation of Gosh.

He also took pride in the fact that he had gifted numerous magic items, enabling Ryubrand to live comfortably in Gosh.

If he were to be targeted by Ryubrand, it would only be if he himself harbored intentions of rebellion against Ryubrand.

But Daldora had absolutely no intention of defying Ryubrand.

While he was interested in pursuing commercial profit, he had no desire to govern the city of Gosh.

He preferred to focus on maximizing commercial profits rather than getting involved in such troublesome affairs.

"Then, any other candidates..."

"If someone's being targeted by assassins, doesn't that mean they've incurred someone's grudge? Well, this whole thing feels way too elaborate to really call it an assassination attempt, though."

Rei interjected as Daldora tried to look for other candidates.

It was easier to understand that whoever planned this attack wasn't seeking some kind of profit... but rather harbored hatred towards Daldora.

Whereas the attack on Augusto involved a single assailant, the attack on Daldora involved three pain-insensitive pawns. And there were multiple people who shot arrows.

What's more, no one could confirm the appearance of any of the archers.

Given that, if anyone held stronger resentment, it would naturally be toward Daldora.

After all, Daldora's Elema Trading Company wields significant influence in Gosh, and it's not uncommon for them to pull off forceful stunts using that power as a backdrop.

Someone resentful of that... or someone harmed by it... plotted Daldora's assassination.

"...What do you think? It seems entirely plausible to me. In fact, I'd say this is the more likely motive than any talk of vested interests or rivalries."

"That..."

Daldora was at a loss for words at Rei's statement.

He likely understood that he himself often incurred people's resentment.

Though Daldora remained silent for a moment, he must have decided that staying quiet wouldn't accomplish anything. Just as he opened his mouth to speak... a knock echoed through the room.

Currently, only one other person besides those gathered in this room was inside the mansion.

Augusto, aware of this, called out toward the door where the knocking came from.

"Kathy, what is it?"

At Augusto's words, the door opened and the person who had knocked showed their face.

It was, of course, Kathy.

However, she wasn't holding any drinks and wore a somewhat perplexed expression.

"Augusto, um... one of Daldora's subordinates is here. He says he has something urgent to tell you. May I let him in?"

"My subordinate?"

Daldora, who had been frowning unpleasantly, her face twisted in irritation at being interrupted while she was about to speak, now heard that her subordinate had come... and had even made the effort to come to Augusto's mansion, a place one could say was hostile to her. This raised questions.

He was in the middle of an important discussion about the future. For someone to interrupt like this...

(If it were just some trivial matter, he should understand he'd face my wrath. Yet he came anyway...)

Something had likely happened. Whether it was good or bad, he couldn't say, but something had definitely occurred.

"Augusto, if it's alright, could you please let my subordinate through here? Since he's gone to the trouble of coming all the way to this mansion, it's certain he has some urgent business."

"...I suppose so. But is it really okay to let them in here? If you'd prefer, I can prepare a separate room."

"No, here is fine. Both you and Augusto are targets right now. Given that, regardless of past disputes, don't you think we need to cooperate? For our mutual survival."

Augusto hesitated for a moment at Daldora's words.

If it were just him, he had Zarust, a reliable bodyguard, and above all, the famed adventurers known as Rei and Set.

Then, was it really necessary to team up with Daldora here?

That was his thought, but there was also the danger of potentially involving the people working in his shop.

In the end, Augusto made a painful choice: to temporarily join forces with Daldora.

"Understood. Then, Kathy, please bring those gentlemen in."

At Augusto's words, Kathy nodded, closed the door, and left.

She must have gone to fetch Daldora's subordinates.

"Now then... good news or bad news. Which do you think it is?"

Perhaps to pass the time, Daldora shook his head at Augusto's question.

"Considering today's events, it's hardly good news, is it? Though I'd certainly prefer it to be good news myself."

"...I suppose so."

Considering what had happened to him today, Augusto had no grounds to disagree with Daldora's words.

He let out a small sigh, and silence filled the room.

Augusto and Daldora pondered what would happen next, while the guards, including Zarust, resolved to protect them at all costs should another attack come... Amidst this, only Rei remained unfazed by the heavy atmosphere, quietly observing her surroundings.

A few minutes later... the door was knocked again.

And entering the room were Kathy and a man.

"Ah..."

Who had uttered that sound?

But it couldn't be helped. Seeing the expression on the man's face, it was impossible to think he had come bearing good news.

After watching Kathy bow slightly and leave, the man started to speak to Daldora. But with Augusto, who was hostile towards Daldora, present, he hesitated, unsure if he should speak.

(It's understandable. After all, until this morning, he was someone you wanted dead if you could get the chance.)

Daldora thought this, but since he was currently allied with Augusto, he decided there was no particular need to hide anything and spoke up.

"It's fine. I'm currently allied with Mr. Augusto. Whatever happened, please tell me. I don't mind."

The man's expression shifted to one of utter shock at the words "allied with Augusto," but he soon seemed to grasp that this was neither a lie nor a joke, but the truth.

After a moment's hesitation, he spoke.

"Just now... truly just moments ago, we received a report that Tira's Tree Research Institute was attacked. We've sent people to gather detailed information, but I felt it was imperative to inform Lord Daldora of this matter without delay, so I came to report first."

That was what he had said.
