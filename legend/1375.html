"...Looks like they made it out okay."

Seeing the woodcutters—some injured, but none with what appeared to be fatal wounds—Rei breathed a sigh of relief.

He swung the Death Scythe he held in his hand wide, standing between Treant and the woodcutters.

The Treants also paused for an instant upon seeing Rei... and in that very opening, the Treants flanking him on either side were each blown away.

One was struck by Vihera's fist, the other by Set's front paw.

Next, several spears made of wind flew out, piercing the torso of one of the Treants.

The woodcutter beside Fekts turned his gaze toward where the wind spears had come from. There, despite the situation, stood a dark elf whose beauty was captivating.

"You are..."

Seeing Rei, holding the Death Scythe in her right hand and the Twilight Spear in her left, Fekts murmurs.

Of course, Fekts knew exactly who it was.

After all, he had met Rei during the day, so it was only natural.

Moreover, as far as Fekts knew, Rei was the only adventurer who had a griffin as a familiar.

"...Step back a bit."

Glancing at Fekts, who seemed at a loss for words even though he wanted to speak, Rei murmured softly.

What lay there was frustration.

It was only natural. He'd done something reckless like coming to Treant Forest at night.

As a result, while Rei was eating dinner, thinking he could finally rest, he received an urgent request and ended up here.

He hadn't felt particularly annoyed while traveling, but seeing Fekts in person, it was only natural his frustration surfaced.

Normally, Fekts wasn't the type to stay silent when told off by the younger Rei. But the monster attack had left him physically and mentally drained.

Above all, he was acutely aware that without Rei and the others right now, they would undoubtedly be dead. So, while he did feel a bit of resentment, he didn't snap back at Rei.

...That he could still feel even a little dissatisfaction in such a situation was proof that Fekts still had some mental reserve left.

"There's something behind us too!"

Seeing Fekts fall silent, another woodcutter nearby shouted.

"What?"

Rei's voice echoed around them as he faced Treant, locked in a strange stalemate.

"Ah, yeah. It's plant vines, but they attack like snakes..."

"Marina, I'll handle that side."

Hearing only that, Rei gave Marina his instructions.

The snake-like vine monster and Treant standing right before him.

While the bow was another matter, the vine monster seemed easier to handle with spirit magic. That was the reasoning behind the order.

That's how much Rei trusted Marina's spirit magic. Having witnessed spirit magic countless times before... scenes that could almost be called omnipotent, it was only natural he felt that way.

Marina seemed to understand this too, silently moving behind the woodcutters.

Marina, wielding spirit magic and a bow, was purely a rear guard type.

Therefore, normally the front-line fighters would need to provide her escort...

"Heh heh, how passionate. But... do you really think attacks like that could capture me?"

With a smile that radiated confidence, she dodged the vines reaching out to entangle Marina's alluring form.

Dodging the attack at the last moment, she spoke to the spirits as if singing.

'Wind Spirit, let us sing together, chant, recite, intone, croon, and compose. Deliver our song to bring eternal peace.'

With those words, various beautiful women, seemingly clad only in cloth, appeared around Marina.

Though, judging by how most of them were semi-transparent, they weren't exactly physical entities.

And considering the chant... the invocation to the spirits, it was clear what had materialized.

"Wind... spirits..."

The woodcutter beside Marina murmured in a daze.

At those words, Marina flashed a seductive smile, glanced at the woodcutter, and in the next instant, waved her hand dramatically.

Simultaneously, numerous wind spirits... no, wind maidens each began to sing.

The released songs became blades of wind, severing the vines; became cannonballs of wind, shattering the vines; became spears of wind, piercing the vines; became songs of wind, confusing the vines.

Through the interplay of countless melodies, the vines that had attacked the woodcutters from behind were rendered powerless in an instant.

Within mere seconds of the wind maidens singing, every vine present was rendered harmless... and with that, the threat closing in from behind vanished completely.

The vines, which had endlessly surged forth without pause no matter how many times they were severed by axes, vanished cleanly and completely in the truest sense of the word.

Even a woodcutter harboring reservations about adventurers would be rendered speechless if shown this scene with their own eyes.

Their own arbitrary prejudice against adventurers suddenly felt utterly trivial.

That's what the woodcutter was thinking when Marina suddenly turned her gaze toward him.

Just that... despite it being only a glance, the woodcutter found himself completely captivated by Marina's appearance.

"It's dangerous. Stay back."

"...Yes."

Told off like that, he couldn't say anything else and had no choice but to step back.

Taking one step, then two, the woodcutter naturally bumped into his fellow woodcutter.

It was only natural. The Fekts had formed a circle.

But... despite the situation, the woodcutter who was bumped showed no reaction.

Several seconds passed before the woodcutter finally noticed. Marina had averted her gaze, so he looked forward.

And then... the sight spread out before him made him freeze, as if he'd forgotten to breathe.

"...No way... What the hell is this..."

What reached the woodcutter's ears as he froze was Fekts' voice, sounding utterly incredulous.

Those words echoed the same thoughts in the minds of every woodcutter present, including Fekts.

They didn't want to admit it. They didn't want to believe it, but they knew adventurers were stronger than them.

But... even so, the scene unfolding before them now was astonishing.

The treants they couldn't defeat, monsters they could only delay until death.

Facing such monsters, what Rei, Vihera, and Set—two humans and one beast—were doing was nothing short of a scene of utter devastation.

Rei's swing of the Death Scythe easily sliced through a Treant, while the Twilight Spear pierced its body.

Vihera's strike seemed ineffective at first glance, but in reality, the Treant's trunk burst from within the next instant.

As for Set, he merely swung his front leg widely, yet that alone sent the Treant flying away as if it were a joke.

It was only natural to wonder if the monsters standing before them were truly the terrifying threats they had feared.

And in the end, within mere minutes of what seemed like an utterly hopeless situation, the threat to their lives vanished.

Witnessing such an overwhelming difference in power, so absurd it felt like a joke, left Fekts and the others speechless.

A mix of emotions flooded them: relief at being saved from what had seemed certain death just moments before, jealousy toward the power that had instantly killed a monster they could never have defeated themselves, and the humiliation of realizing they were utterly helpless without adventurers.

A whirlwind of emotions left them speechless.

Yet, despite this turmoil within Fekts and the others, Rei merely glanced at them, his gaze shifting toward Treant as if to say he had no interest.

"Huh!? Hey! You! Didn't you come to save us?!"

Rei's attitude caused the resentment Fekts felt toward adventurers to surge far stronger than any gratitude for being saved, and he shouted almost reflexively.

It wasn't that they thought he wouldn't attack them since he'd come to help... it was purely words born of resentment.

Compared to himself, a lumberjack with built muscles, Rei's appearance simply didn't seem strong at all, which was another reason fueling his resentment.

Of course, he had seen Ray wielding weapons like the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear—weapons normally used with two hands—with just one hand each. Still, outward appearance seemed to be a major factor in judging others.

"...What? No magic stone?"

As if he hadn't heard Fekts' shout, Rei probed inside the Treant he'd sliced open with the Death Scythe and muttered.

True to his words, the magic stone that should have been present in any monster was nowhere to be found.

Since it was a monster, he had been certain it would have a magic stone, making his disappointment palpable.

One reason they'd accepted this rescue mission for Fekts and the others was, naturally, to obtain the monster's magic stone.

Especially since the Treant's magic stone was one Rei... or more accurately, the Death Scythe... had never absorbed before. He had hoped it might grant him some new skill.

That made the failure to actually obtain a magic stone the biggest miscalculation for Rei.

"No magic stone? ...You mean you just couldn't find it?"

"Even Treant knows the magic stones are usually in specific spots. ...What's going on?"

"Grrr..."

At Rei's words, Set growled.

Realizing this growl carried a hint of suspicion toward the monster, Rei turned his gaze back to Treant.

"Vihera, have you ever fought a Treant?"

"A few times in dungeons before. ...But why do you ask?"

Though Vihera had known Rei and Set for quite some time, unlike Rei, she couldn't fully grasp the meaning behind Set's growl.

It was probably similar to how Rei couldn't fully understand Byune's intentions.

"Judging by Set's cries, he seems to sense something off about this Treant."

"...Something off?"

Hearing the word "uneasiness," Vihera tilted her head slightly.

As he had mentioned earlier, Vihera had fought a Treant before.

But she hadn't felt anything particularly off during this battle with Treant.

"I don't really..."

"Hey!"

Apparently unable to tolerate being left out of the conversation any longer,

Fekts grabbed Rei's shoulder, about to shout something...

"Shut up, incompetent fool."

The single word that came out of Rei's mouth silenced him completely.

Had this been said by someone ordinary, his naturally strong-willed nature would have made him snap back immediately.

But the gaze Rei directed at him held only utter disbelief and contempt.

Delivered with an aura laced with anger, there was no way an ordinary person like Fekts could resist it.

"Huh!?"

In the end, he couldn't say anything more and simply took a few steps back.

Seeing Fekts like this, another woodcutter started to say something... but Rei cut him off, speaking again.

"To begin with, this whole incident wouldn't have happened if you lot hadn't gone out of town in the evening. Frankly, I can't fathom what possessed you to do such a thing. But because of it, the guild received an urgent request from the other woodcutters and their families. Do you understand the implications of that?"

Fekts and the others wore expressions mingled with dissatisfaction and confusion at Rei's words.

They genuinely didn't understand what Rei was talking about.

Realizing this, Rei opened his mouth, his expression growing even more exasperated.

"Look, despite how I might appear, I'm a Rank B adventurer... what you'd call a high-rank adventurer. And I even have a nickname. You're requesting my services? There's no way the fee would be the same as what you normally pay adventurers like those you usually hire, right?"

Setting aside this Treant Forest incident, the adventurers the woodcutter usually hired for protection were mostly Rank D types.

Of course, the payment for that escort work wasn't exactly cheap either, but it still couldn't compare to the fees paid to high-rank parties like Rei's... or the Crimson Wings.

Admittedly, this request was extremely urgent, and we haven't even discussed the exact reward amount yet.

Even so, it was certain the fee would be far beyond what low-ranked adventurers could command.

Finally grasping this, Fekts and the others turned pale.

It was only natural. They had come to Treant Forest at night because they harbored resentment toward adventurers, but also because they wanted to earn more.

This had brought about the exact opposite result they had anticipated.

It was more than enough to make them regret their foolish actions.
