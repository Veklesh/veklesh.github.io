Rei's expression as he descended to the fourth basement level was nothing short of displeased.

It was only natural. After all, in every other room besides the one Resel had been in, there were no surviving pregnant women left.

In the end, he had to burn the giant's child from another room and the mangled corpses of the pregnant women together.

He no longer hesitated about killing people, but burning the bodies of women who had merely been used by Jaya still left a bad taste in his mouth.

And while it was true that Rei, who had done the actual work, felt uneasy, the other women, starting with Elena, felt equally uncomfortable.

Being women themselves was undoubtedly a major reason for those feelings.

"The second basement level is where they keep pregnant women waiting, the third basement is where they actually give birth. If the black crystal is on the fifth basement level... what do you think is on this fourth basement level?"

"...Logically, wouldn't it be a room to hold the giants? Rezell mentioned shipping them, right? So, they'd need a room to keep the giants until they're shipped."

Marina answered Rei's question.

No one could hear that answer and think it was wrong.

But...

"From what we can see here, it doesn't look like there are any giants."

Indeed, compared to the third basement level, the fourth basement level was narrower, essentially a single room.

In other words, they had descended directly into the room via stairs, but the room was essentially empty, a scene that could be described as a vacant room.

Even if they had shipped them out, it wouldn't be strange for there to be giants left behind for guard duty, like the ones Resel had summoned earlier.

And Rei couldn't possibly believe that the three giants from earlier were the only guards.

If that were the case, it wouldn't be strange for a giant to be in this room...

That question of Rei's was likely shared by the others. They looked around the room with puzzled expressions... yet remained alert, ready to respond to anything that might happen.

The first thing that caught their eyes in the room was the staircase leading down to the fifth basement level.

Other than that, there was truly nothing else; just open space stretching out.

"A magic item warehouse or something... Personally, I was hoping for that kind of thing."

"But in the end, the magic items Jaya used weren't made by Jaya's alchemist, right? So even if there were magic items here, they probably wouldn't be that useful, huh?"

Marina's words made Rei nod weakly.

Of course, Rei had anticipated that possibility.

But he had clung to the possibility... just the slightest chance...

After all, the primary reason Rei had accepted the request for revenge against Jaya was to seize the magic items.

Given that he possessed a large number of slave collars, Ray had assumed there would be various other magic items as well, but that didn't seem to be the case.

"Well, black crystal is supposedly a pretty valuable magic item... but it kinda feels like a cursed one."

Rei muttered, sounding thoroughly fed up.

There was no doubt the black crystal exerted significant influence over the vast region of Mejogo.

But the effect it had was turning women into prostitutes—Rei suspected this part might be modifiable—and within that scope, if they became pregnant, they would bear giant children.

There was no way Rei wanted to use it.

(Well, maybe planting it in an enemy country... could work? No, but for the Black Crystal to take effect, you need the slave collar too. You'd have to use them together. Meaning, it'd be conspicuous.)

If using the black crystal in enemy territory, you'd absolutely need to find a way to use the slave collar.

That would mean resorting to methods like the ones used here in Mejogo, forcing the slave collar onto someone.

Alternatively, since a slave collar compatible with the Black Crystal doesn't look like a slave collar at first glance, you could try making it popular as some kind of fashion accessory.

...Though, to be honest, Rei isn't well-versed in those details, so whether that's actually feasible is unclear.

And if you asked Rei whether he'd want to go to such lengths just to use black crystal, he'd probably shake his head.

(Which leaves the possibility of utilizing the black crystal itself as some sort of material, then? ...Though using it as a material seems likely to conjure up all sorts of troublesome magic items, which is a bit scary.)

Thinking along these lines, Rei headed toward the stairs leading to the fifth basement level.

"Well, looks like there's nothing on this floor anyway. Let's head to the fifth basement level."

"Right. ...I never expected it to be this empty, so it was quite an unexpected sight."

Walking beside Rei, Vihera murmured.

From Vihera's perspective, she'd probably assumed there'd be a horde of giants here.

But upon actually arriving, the giants were nowhere to be seen, presumably already shipped out.

That was the main reason Vihera was dissatisfied.

(Though, the fact that only those three were prepared for security... is a bit questionable. If Jaya recognizes our existence, they could have tightened security more. Considering that, it feels unnatural.)

It seemed Rei wasn't the only one thinking this. Elena and Marina, while heading toward the stairs, were also keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

Naturally, Byune, sensing their presence, swiftly checked her surroundings like a true thief.

But in the end, nothing particularly happened, and Rei and the others arrived at the foot of the stairs.

"...I never thought nothing would actually happen,"

Elena murmured thoughtfully, and everyone agreed with her sentiment.

After all, if the information Rei and the others had obtained was correct, what lay on the fifth basement floor was the Black Crystal.

It wouldn't be strange at all to wonder if such an important item would be handed over to Rei and the others so easily.

"Yeah. It wouldn't have been strange if there had been some kind of trap. And with this much space... I think this is the perfect spot to launch an attack."

Rei muttered, scanning the surroundings once more.

To unleash a giant, its sheer size demanded a certain amount of space.

Even when Vihera fought the three giants earlier, the space probably wasn't quite large enough for them.

Of course, even if they had fought in a genuinely spacious area, Rei honestly believed there wasn't a single chance in a million that Vihera would lose.

"Anyway, it was definitely lucky for us that there wasn't a single trap here. That leaves only the fifth basement level. We don't know what awaits us underground, but let's stay alert."

"There's supposed to be some magic item called Black Crystal, right? Well, no one's going to let their guard down in a place like this."

Vihera looked as if she lived for battle, but for someone who relished combat like her, that mindset was only natural.

Seeing Vihera like that, Rei couldn't help but smile.

...But perhaps that smile wasn't amusing to Vihera after all. She shot Rei a slightly displeased look and spoke.

"What?"

"Nothing. I was just thinking that with Vihera around, I feel secure about combat. Right?"

When Rei turned his gaze to the others, they all nodded without hesitation.

Seeing the reactions around her, Vihera seemed like she wanted to say something, but she must have decided that even if she did, she wouldn't be satisfied with the outcome.

He kept his dissatisfied expression but closed his mouth.

As if taking that as a signal, Rei and the others began descending the stairs.

Unlike before, these were spiral stairs.

However, unlike the first staircase, there were no sudden traps like steps abruptly ending into a slope.

To those who had set these traps, having already failed with the same scheme once, activating a trap here would likely seem pointless.

In fact, they were correct.

Even if the stairs vanished abruptly here, Rei and the others would simply land on the fifth basement level without any injuries.

...Of course, if they'd been injured by the previous trap, things might have been different...

And so, as Rei and the others descended the stairs, what they eventually saw was... a massive... truly massive black crystal, easily three meters tall.

Its shape was jagged, its tips sharply pointed.

True to its name, the color was black.

It was a jet-black crystal, so dark it seemed capable of absorbing even the light of the magic items found on the fifth basement level.

Even knowing it possessed powers worthy of being called evil, Rei and the others were momentarily captivated by the black crystal's beauty.

But... the next instant, their faces were frozen in utter shock.

Because surrounding that black crystal were several giants.

They were curled up asleep, but there was no doubt they were giants and nothing else.

(So that's where they were—I thought they weren't on the fourth basement floor.)

Rei couldn't understand why this had happened.

But for some reason... seeing the giants surrounding the black crystal, he felt no sense of incongruity.

He didn't understand the meaning behind this situation, yet he felt no discomfort.

It was a scene that felt utterly natural.

Watching those giants, Rei finally... well, it was really too late now, but he found himself wondering.

(Being giants, they must eat a lot. So naturally, they must need a huge amount of food too... but how do they manage that?)

Though they had come all the way down to the fifth underground floor, Rei and the others had no memory of seeing any food for the giants along the way.

Sure, there had been something like dried meat in the basement warehouse on the first underground floor, but that was clearly meant for humans.

There was no way that would be enough for a giant to eat.

Rei had seen the scene where they tore through their mother's womb at birth and devoured her entire body, but surely they couldn't survive without eating anything after that.

Though Rei harbored this doubt, for now, his gaze turned to the black crystal and the giants curled up asleep around it.

Given the black crystal seemed to be growing out of the ground, Rei understood that what he could see now was probably only about half its actual size.

(Anyway, we need to deal with that black crystal... but there's no way those giants will just stay quiet. Should we just give up on taking it and destroy it instead? ...But that feels like it would lead to some kind of troublesome incident too.)

Having observed the fifth basement level from the upper part of the stairs, Rei and the others began discussing what to do next.

Rei was certain that if they descended the stairs and fully reached the fifth basement level, the sleeping giant would awaken.

It wasn't a conviction based on any particular reason, but rather an instinct honed from surviving countless battles.

Or perhaps Rei was picturing the standard manga trope he'd read back in Japan.

"What do we do?"

"What do you mean, what do we do... We have to deal with that black crystal somehow, or things will get messy, right? Besides, as revenge against Jaya, this is probably the best course of action we could take."

Vihera was the first to speak.

The battle with the giants on the third basement floor must have been satisfying enough for him.

His gaze drifted toward the giants, who were curled up asleep, as if hoping no fight would break out.

"If we fight here, won't the black crystal get damaged? If that's the case, I think we should just attack the giants right now. That way, we can reduce their numbers a bit, and if we draw the attention of the giants who notice the attack, the black crystal won't get damaged either."

"Hey, Elena. Since we finally get to fight giants to our hearts' content, could you just let us fight normally?"

"...I understand what you're saying, Vihera, but that could potentially damage the Black Crystal."

"We'll fight carefully to avoid that. ...Is that okay?"

Even now, Vihera's eyes glistened with excitement at the thought of fighting the giants.

Radiating overwhelming sensuality, Vihera turned her gaze toward Rei, but...

"No."

He was rejected outright.

If the black crystal were located farther away... Vihera might have nodded in agreement.

But considering the rarity of the black crystal... and contemplating what might happen if it were to break, she simply couldn't agree with Vihera.

Rei glanced at Marina to see if she had any objections, but she simply nodded back, indicating she had none.
