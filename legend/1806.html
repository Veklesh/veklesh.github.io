"Huh? Are you serious?"

The day after meeting Brewitt.

After finishing breakfast—consisting of sandwiches made by Georgima’s subordinate—or perhaps apprentice—and a hearty soup packed with meat, fish, and vegetables, Rei couldn’t help but respond that way to what Miranda had said.

But in response to Rei’s words, Miranda nodded as if to say it was only natural.

“Of course. There are only about ten days left this year. And the day after New Year’s, there’s already a party. Given that, we have to order the clothes we’ll wear to the party properly now.”

“But wait, don’t we have the clothes from when we first had dinner with Elena’s parents? Aren’t those good enough?”

“It’s not that it won’t work, but I think it would be better to have a dress that fits you properly.”

In this world, most clothes are basically made to order.

That’s why there are quite a few secondhand shops, and in some cases, it’s not uncommon for mothers to sew clothes for their children.

For that very reason, the outfit Rei wore to the dinner party was provided by Duke Kerebel; while it was very expensive, it inevitably fell short of a custom-made garment.

Furthermore, every noble attending the party was wearing custom-made attire, and as such, they had a keen eye for spotting when someone was wearing clothes that didn’t suit them.

Especially since Rei was attracting the attention of the Noble Faction—in every sense of the word—those gazes would likely be even more critical.

To avoid that, it was certainly best to have proper attire prepared in advance.

“Besides, considering your abilities, Rei, you’ll have plenty of opportunities to attend parties like this in the future. So why not take this chance to have some made? Your husband has instructed that the Kerebel Duke’s household cover the cost.”

At those words, Rei paused to think.

Considering his reputation—if one could even call it that—there was no doubt he would be expected to attend such parties in the future.

No, it was actually strange that she hadn’t been invited to such parties until now.

Part of the reason was that Rei had been based in the frontier town of Gilm, but a major factor was that Daskar had been subtly protecting him.

...Above all, the rumors that Rei is a person who shows absolutely no mercy, even to nobles, likely play a major role.

But now that he’s attending the New Year’s party at Kerebel’s residence, it’s almost certain that people will start trying to get Rei to attend parties from now on.

The unknown is frightening, but once you’ve seen it, it ceases to be unknown.

Considering these circumstances, I’ve decided that regardless of whether I attend other parties in the future, it wouldn’t hurt to have at least one outfit suitable for a party on hand, just in case.

(Fortunately, this body of mine doesn’t seem to be getting any bigger for the time being. …Wait, is that really a good thing?)

Since coming to this world—or, more precisely, since inhabiting this body—Rei hasn’t grown a single inch.

It’s nice that my build hasn’t changed, but to be honest, I would have preferred to wait until I got bigger, or for them to have created a larger body for me.

Here in Elgin, there aren’t that many people over two meters tall, but there are definitely more than in Japan.

Because of that, Rei is often looked down upon.

...Still, lately, Rei’s nickname, “Crimson,” has spread, and fewer people underestimate him based on his appearance.

In any case, since Rei’s height probably won’t increase from here on out, even if I made him a party outfit, it shouldn’t become too small for him in the future. …Though that would be a different story if he grew wider instead of taller.

“Alright, I’ll make the clothes. …But will I be able to finish in time?”

Since coming to Elgin, and even back in Japan, Rei had never once had clothes custom-made.

Perhaps that was why Rei had the impression that having a custom-made outfit would undoubtedly take about a month.

That wasn’t actually wrong.

But… this is Anesis, and Rei is currently at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

Most requests that would normally be impossible are easily granted by someone with the title of duke.

“It’s fine. My husband has assured me that won’t be a problem. …Well then, without further ado, I’d like to take Rei’s measurements right away. Is that okay?”

“Huh? Are you going to take them, Miranda?”

Though he had his doubts, he also thought that since she was a maid working at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, it wouldn’t be strange if she were capable of such a thing.

Miranda shook her head, denying Rei’s suspicion.

“No, the craftsman is already here, waiting.”

“……Is that so……”

If I had said I wouldn’t make the clothes, wouldn’t calling the tailor have been a waste of time?

She thought that for a moment, but they had likely anticipated from the start that Rei would make the clothes and judged that she wouldn’t refuse.

Whether that judgment came from Miranda, Elena, or Duke Liberte of Kerebel—

Rei didn’t know, but the fact remained that someone had anticipated his actions.

“May I proceed?”

“Yeah, do as you please. …But I’ll be paying for those clothes.”

“My master told me the Kerebel Duke’s household would be covering the cost…”

“I appreciate the kindness. But I’ll likely be wearing these clothes for a long time to come. That’s precisely why I want to pay for them myself. Fortunately, I’m not short on money.”

The fortune Rei had amassed was enough to live a life of leisure for several lifetimes.

It was more than enough to easily afford even custom-made clothes.

Seeing Rei’s expression, Miranda must have decided it was pointless to say anything more. She eventually nodded in response.

“I understand. I’ll let my husband know. Shall I go ahead and call the tailor now?”

At those words, Rei nodded in response.

While Rei was having his measurements taken at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence… Gaiska was listening to a report from Deotres.

But the only emotion visible on Gaiska’s face was displeasure.

“Why can’t we get enough people together?! It’s a simple job—just killing one person!”

Yes, that was the main reason Gaiska was so irritated.

Had it not been winter, the course of action chosen by Gaiska—who harbored a deep grudge against Rei—might well have been highly effective.

Since he was keeping the fact that the target was Rei a secret, there was no chance that the person Deotres approached would be intimidated by the nickname “Crimson.”

In fact, they likely judged it to be a good opportunity to make money and gladly accepted the request.

In fact, Deotres had anticipated that this would be the case... but unexpectedly, many of the skilled adventurers he approached had decided not to take on work during the winter.

Well, it’s unfortunate, but in a way, it was probably unavoidable.

For skilled adventurers, saving up enough money to get through the winter before the snow falls wasn’t particularly difficult unless something extraordinary happened, and there was no reason for such people to go out of their way to accept a suspicious request without going through the guild.

Of course, as the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, Anesis attracts a considerable number of adventurers.

It wouldn’t be surprising if some of those adventurers took on shady jobs… but this time, unfortunately—or perhaps fortunately—there were none.

In this case, the problem isn’t simply a matter of gathering enough people.

Since they would be fighting Rei, they needed not only a sufficient number of people but also as many skilled fighters as possible to wear him down.

But I was unable to gather such people.

“I did try reaching out to various people, you know. But it seems everyone I was counting on turned me down, one after another.”

“Why?! You mere adventurers dare to refuse my orders… no, my request?!”

The fact that she actually said “order” out loud was likely Gaiska’s true, unvarnished feelings.

That said, Gaiska isn’t the only one who looks down on adventurers, so this isn’t exactly unusual.

“Then what do you want me to do? Are you telling me to just let that lowly wretch named Rei do whatever he pleases? I have to take action to bring Elena back to her senses!”

“I’ll take care of it.”

Even as he said that, Gaiska had no real intention of taking action himself.

He planned to give orders, hire adventurers to follow those orders, and defeat Rei… even if that was impossible, he intended to make him regret humiliating him.

That was his plan, but it seemed he’d hit a snag right from the start with this incident.

As if to suppress the frustration within him, he raised the glass of wine to his lips.

He knew there were all sorts of problems with drinking this early in the morning, even though it was still before noon, but if he didn’t, his frustration would reach a boiling point again, and he’d take it out on his servants.

Ever since that day when Rei humiliated him, several servants had already become the targets of Gaiska’s venting.

Precisely because Gaesca was aware of this, he was willing to exercise some restraint, sipping the wine to quell the frustration within him.

It was precisely the kind of situation where, if provoked carelessly, there was no telling what Gaiska might do, yet Deotres spoke up without seeming to sense anything amiss.

“I suppose the anonymous request is what’s raising suspicions, isn’t it?”

“...Are you suggesting I mention the name of the Marquis of Seisol?”

“Yes. If we do that, there’s no doubt that those seeking a connection to the Seisole Marquis family will reach out.”

“But…”

At Deotres’ words, Gaiska gazed at the wine glass he was holding, hesitating.

Currently, he was trying to hire adventurers without mentioning the Seisole Marquis family.

That was also the reason why they were viewed with suspicion and shunned by many… If they mentioned the name of the Seisole Marquis family, those seeking connections would naturally accept the offer.

However, mentioning the Seisole Marquis family would inevitably reveal that he was the one pulling the strings behind the scenes.

If such adventurers were to attack Rei, it would be obvious who was behind it.

That said, right now, Gaiska is the only one in Anesis who harbors a deep grudge against Rei.

If he were to attempt an ambush under such circumstances, it would be obvious who was behind it.

If this were the Marquis of Seisole’s territory rather than Anesis, it wouldn’t be difficult to handle the situation simply by invoking the name of the Marquis of Seisole.

“Troublesome.”

“I suppose so. So, here’s a suggestion: why don’t we just forget about killing Rei altogether?”

“...You want me to forgive someone who humiliated me that badly?”

“No, no. I wouldn’t go that far. It’s just that, given the current situation, it’s impossible to gather the manpower needed to kill Rei. It seems there are a few people with aliases in Anesis, but even those kinds of people… no, perhaps that’s exactly why. They probably wouldn’t take on a suspicious job like that.”

Those with aliases are big names in their own right.

Since they can generally handle things on their own terms, there’s no need for them to go out of their way to take on suspicious jobs.

Of course, there are those in the world who enjoy the thrill or the challenge of fighting powerful foes.

Given that, it’s not as if they’d absolutely refuse a request… but even so, the likelihood is quite low.

“So, what should we do? Since you’re asking that, you must have some plan in mind, right? If you’re saying that without having anything in mind…”

“Of course I do.”

“I won’t let this slide.”

Just as Gaiska was about to say that, Deotres cut him off.

Gaiska looked slightly displeased at Deotres’ attitude, but since taking revenge on Rei was more important right now, he urged him to continue.

“So, what are we going to do?”

“First of all, retaliation is fine, but please forget about killing him. At least, I can’t go that far in this case.”

“What…?”

Gaiska was determined to kill Rei.

He had resolved to exact revenge on him—and on anyone who dared defy the House of Seisol.

“Why are you suddenly saying something like that?” An irritated gaze was directed at Deotres.

But the person on the receiving end of that glare didn’t seem to mind and spoke up.

“How about we hold the mock battle not to kill Rei, but to gauge her strength? That way, there’s a high chance many adventurers interested in Rei’s strength will gather, and we won’t need to hide our identities either.”

With a smile, Deotres made his proposal.
