The day after they delivered the special potion, Rei, Elena, Marina, Vihera, Byune, Relieu, Yellow, and Set—six people and two animals—were up in the sky.

As usual, Set was flying, with Rei riding on his back and the others inside the Set Cage.

Golz could still be seen a short distance away.

The sun was directly overhead, so it must have been around ten o’clock in the morning.

They had originally planned to depart when the six o’clock bell rang, but the reason it had been delayed until this hour was… because before leaving Golz, they had been seen off by a large crowd, including members of the guild.

Among those who came to see them off was the magistrate, so naturally, the send-off was quite lavish.

From the magistrate’s perspective, having learned that the dungeon he’d thought had just appeared near Golz was actually a rather vicious affair, he likely wanted to express his gratitude to Rei and the others in some small way.

This region was originally the domain of a noble belonging to the Neutral Faction.

Since Rei—who is regarded as Daskar’s right-hand man, the leader of the Neutral Faction—had cleared the dungeon for them, they likely couldn’t just abandon him.

……That said, while others view Rei as Daskar’s right-hand man, he himself does not see it that way.

Of course, there’s no doubt he respects and trusts Daskar, and he’d certainly be willing to prioritize any requests he might have.

But that, and the idea of serving Daskar, were—at least in Rei’s mind—entirely different matters.

In any case, while it wasn’t quite a grand ceremony, various events had certainly caused their departure to be delayed.

Still, the reason Rei didn’t voice any complaints was likely because many of those who came to see them off genuinely felt grateful toward Rei and the others.

“Still… all things considered, this whole affair turned out pretty well.”

“Hmm?”

In response to Rei’s mutter, Set let out a “Hmm?”-like growl.

In fact, there’s no doubt that both Set and Death Scythe were able to strengthen their skills in various ways through this incident.

Above all, for Rei, the fact that he was able to level up the Death Scythe’s terrain manipulation skill was a major boon.

The terrain manipulation skill is incredibly useful, provided you use it correctly.

As seen in the battle against Garangiga, it can be used in combat... and beyond that, for various types of construction work.

In fact, he could easily create something like a simple trench.

But… precisely because it’s such a useful skill, it naturally isn’t that easy to enhance.

For now, the only known way to level up the skill is to use Death Scythe to cut through the core of a dungeon.

In other words, to strengthen the terrain manipulation skill, you must clear the dungeon.

But, of course, dungeons aren’t places that can be cleared easily.

(Maybe I should ask the guild to help me find a location with a newly generated dungeon. The guild should be able to gather that kind of information easily. Just like in this case.)

That said, this particular incident could be described as the result of a series of coincidences.

Given that, it would be difficult to find a newly opened dungeon so easily.

That’s precisely why I’m taking the form of a request.

(With the expansion of Gilm, I’ve been taking on quite a few requests from the guild… In that sense, it might actually work out well.)

Thinking that, Rei looked up at the sky.

Before his eyes lay a blue sky with hardly a cloud in sight.

There had been some clouds this morning, but seeing the sky like this, he knew there was no need to worry about rain and let out a sigh of relief.

“Guruu?”

Set must have noticed Rei moving on his back. He let out a soft growl and turned his gaze toward Rei.

Rei shook her head at Set, as if to say it was nothing… then suddenly, she noticed something ahead.

Not a highway… but a carriage racing through a forest where no road exists.

And several men were shooting arrows at the carriage.

Normally, it would be difficult for people on foot to catch up to a carriage.

But that’s only true on a normal road.

A carriage requires horses to pull it and the carriage body itself for people to ride in.

To be precise, there are actually several other things required as well.

In any case, while a horse alone might be fast enough, a carriage inevitably slows things down. Moreover, since they were traveling through the woods, they had to carefully judge the gaps between the trees.

If they made a mistake in judging the gaps here, the carriage could get stuck between the trees and become immobilized.

That’s precisely why they couldn’t push the speed any further… and why they couldn’t shake off the pursuers coming up from behind.

(Bandits? ...Well, given that they’re traveling through the woods instead of the main road, the carriage must have some shady business of its own.)

While Rei was curious about the carriage, his interest was naturally drawn even more strongly to the bandits.

If these were ordinary bandits… no, in Rei’s case, he would have happily hunted them down even then, but now it was slightly different.

Could it be that those very bandits were the ones Rei had been searching for yesterday… the ones who had stolen the special potion that was supposed to be transported from Somis?

That was what he thought.

They had already traveled a considerable distance since leaving Golz.

Given that, it was a very real possibility.

Of course, bandits are the kind of creatures that, no matter how many you hunt, just keep popping up out of nowhere.

Given that, it wasn’t certain that the bandits Rei had his eye on were the ones in question.

No, in fact, the likelihood that they were the ones was probably quite low.

Still, for Rei, the mere fact that they are bandits is enough to make him resolve to take action.

He’s also interested in the carriage that seems to have a story behind it… but that can wait.

“I’ve spotted the bandits! We’re landing!”

Rei calls out briefly to the Set Cage and asks Set to descend.

Set let out a low growl as he descended toward the ground. Once he set the Set Cage down outside the woods, he charged straight into the forest, carrying his momentum with him.

Although he plunged into the woods at considerable speed, with Set’s agility, dodging the trees growing there was no difficult task.

“Brrrrrr!?”

The horse pulling the carriage that came into view ahead neighed violently upon seeing Set.

They had been running desperately when a griffin suddenly appeared right before their eyes.

It would be unreasonable to expect them not to be startled.

The panicked horse thrashed about, trying desperately to escape from Set, who was charging toward it.

For a horse, the griffin right in front of it is a far more terrifying sight than a bandit chasing from behind.

It’s like the proverbial “a tiger at the front door, a wolf at the back door”—or rather, a dragon at the front door (a griffin, that is) and a goblin at the back door.

But if the carriage tips over, the horse tied to it will naturally be unable to move.

“Neigh!”

A heart-wrenching cry escapes the horse’s mouth.

It must realize that if it’s targeted by an invincible creature like a griffin in this situation, there’s no way it can survive.

But Set, with Rei on his back, passes right by the despairing horse and advances toward the bandits behind the carriage.

For a moment, the horse looked as if it didn’t understand what was happening, but neither Rei nor Set paid it the slightest mind.

To the bandits chasing the carriage from behind, Rei and Set’s actions were… the absolute worst possible outcome.

“What the—!”

The man at the front of the bandits let out a cry of shock at the sight of Rei and Set suddenly appearing from the side of the carriage... but in the next instant, he was struck across the torso by the shaft of the Twilight Spear that Rei had drawn from the Misty Ring and sent flying.

From the bandits’ perspective, it was as if they had suddenly been struck by a counterattack from the very prey they were attacking.

That wasn’t actually the case, but at least that’s how it appeared to the bandits.

The fact that their leader had been knocked away likely only reinforced that belief.

“Don’t mess with us, you bastard! Do you really think you can defy us?!”

Some bandits shouted like that, but one after another, they were knocked down by Rei’s Twilight Spear.

……Some unlucky bandits had their throats slit by the spearhead itself, while others, even if struck in the torso, suffered ruptured internal organs due to Rei’s brute strength or had their necks snapped from the impact of crashing into a tree.

As the bandits were blown away one after another, the others naturally began to notice something was wrong.

“Watch out, there’s something there! It’s not prey—it’s something else!”

“You’re right!”

He sent the screaming bandit flying with a single strike of the Twilight Spear… and from there, it didn’t take long for the bandits to be wiped out.

“Well… now that that’s settled, the problem is that carriage… but he’s definitely dead, isn’t he?”

After finishing off the bandits, Rei’s gaze fell on a man lying on the ground.

If that were all, one might have thought he was merely unconscious.

But… with multiple arrows piercing his body and no sign of movement even in that state, he was almost certainly dead.

From the moment they’d moved through the woods away from the main road, out of sight of others, he’d understood that there was something suspicious about this carriage.

But now that the very person who could have explained why was dead, there was nothing he could do.

“Ugh… ugh…”

As I was wondering what to do, luckily—or perhaps unluckily, considering what was to come—I heard the voice of the bandit who had been unconscious.

Rei had been weighing the carriage against the bandit, but even though he’d beaten him, he couldn’t just leave him be, given the possibility that the bandit might try to escape.

“I’ll put checking this carriage on hold…” That’s what Rei was thinking, but just then, Elena and the others appeared.

“Seriously, you always move so suddenly.”

Elena looked slightly exasperated, likely because, even though he had called out to her before attacking the bandit, it had still come out of nowhere.

And before he knew it, the Set Cage had been tossed onto the ground.

Considering that, the fact that Elena looked exasperated rather than angry meant they were still lucky.

“Sorry about that. I was in a bit of a hurry.”

“……I suppose so.”

Marina, who appeared from behind Elena, muttered as she surveyed the scene around them—especially the overturned carriage.

Her tone naturally carried a hint of suspicion regarding the carriage’s unnatural behavior.

“For now… I’ll secure the bandits who are still alive, so why don’t you go retrieve the Set Cage, Rei? I left Relieu over there, so I don’t think there’ll be any problems.”

Rei nodded in agreement at Vihera’s words, which followed shortly after.

With Relieu there, no fool would even think of messing with the Set Cage.

However, that wasn’t a guarantee; there might be bandits—or even monsters, depending on the situation—who wouldn’t realize the difference in strength even when facing Relieu and would still try to meddle.

Given that, there was no doubt that Rei, who possessed the Misty Ring, should store the Set Cage away.

“I see. Well then, I’ll leave it to you. Use this to tie up any living bandits.”

Rei took a rope from the Misty Ring and handed it to Vihera, then mounted Set and rode out of the woods.

The place where he’d left the Set Cage wasn’t that far away, so there really was no need to go out of his way to ride Set there… but since Set was rubbing his face against Rei’s and purring to show he wanted to come along, Rei had no intention of refusing.

If there had been other monsters or bandits in the area, making things dangerous, it would have been a different story... but in the current situation, there was no need to worry about that.

And so, riding Set out of the woods, the scene that unfolded before him was exactly as Rei had expected.

In front of the Set Cage, Relieu looked rather displeased.

Relieu, of course, had noticed Rei and Set approaching him, and he opened his mouth with a look of dissatisfaction.

“Hey, you can’t just show up like this out of the blue.”

“Sorry. We were in a bit of a hurry, too. We found a carriage being attacked by bandits.”

“...Bandits? Not on the highway, but in the woods?”

Relieu looks at Rei with a skeptical expression.

Given that this was happening right next to the highway, it would be unreasonable to expect him not to be suspicious.

But Rei had actually seen the stagecoach under attack, and the driver had been killed in the bandits’ assault.

Given that, he had no choice but to report the facts as they were.

“That’s right. There’s clearly something fishy going on… or at least, it seems there’s something shady about it. Besides, I’m quite interested in these bandits as well.”

Saying that, Rei touched the Set Cage and stored it away in the Misty Ring.
