"Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrr!"

Set’s roar echoes through the Forest of Treant.

Yesterday, some people were so terrified by Set’s roar that their legs went weak, but now it’s different. They know that the voice belongs to Set, and to the people of Gilm, Set is a beloved figure.

Even those who arrived in Gilm while Rei was on his way to the Kingdom of Reburina were initially somewhat wary of the griffin, Set. However, having heard at least some rumors about him before actually meeting him, and seeing how friendly he was when they did meet, they quickly let down their guard.

“Set’s really giving it his all. Well then, let’s give it a little more of our best too! We need to cut down as many trees as possible before winter!”

“Hahahaha! That’s right! Since the wood we cut here will be the fuel for our fire, we’ve got to give it our all!”

Men who looked every bit the mountain folk exchanged such banter as they took turns swinging their axes at the tree trunks.

The sound of axe blades striking tree trunks echoed through the Treant Forest.

Watching the woodcutters, the adventurers stationed here as guards exchanged words while keeping watch on their surroundings.

“Man, I’m glad Rei came to help. To be honest, when I saw them haul that many trees just yesterday, I thought, ‘No way, right?’”

“Ah, well, that’s what most people would think. I thought the same thing when I saw it before.”

“But that’s an item box, right? Is it really safe to use such a valuable magic item so conspicuously? I’m sure there are people out there who’d get the wrong idea.”

There are fewer than ten confirmed Item Boxes in the world.

Seeing one, it’s only natural that some people would want one.

Of course, since it’s impossible to take it on by fighting Rei head-on, they’d likely resort to stealing it.

Even an adventurer with a reputation can’t keep a constant watch on their surroundings…

“In that case, maybe… just maybe, someone might try to steal the item box from Rei.”

To the male adventurer voicing this concern, the adventurer he addressed smiled reassuringly and spoke.

“How many people do you think have tried to get their hands on Rei’s item box so far? Despite that, the item box is still in Rei’s possession. That’s the whole answer. Besides…”

“And?”

Wondering why he’d stopped mid-sentence, the other adventurer urged him to continue.

The adventurer who had paused looked around, watching Rei stow the felled trees one after another, and spoke.

“Do you really want to make an enemy of Rei? You’ve heard the rumors about how much damage the Bestir Empire Army suffered in the last war, haven’t you? No matter how much money you want to make, making an enemy of Rei is nothing short of suicide. And while things might have been different before, Rei is now part of a party, so his combat strength has only increased.”

Even just Rei and Set alone were an incredibly formidable force to begin with, but now several others with great power have joined them.

I simply can’t imagine any fool daring to mess with Rei right now.

(Though, I suppose that’s exactly why fools are fools—they think they’re invincible.)

Anyone who has seen Rei fighting with the Death Scythe in hand would never choose to make an enemy of him.

Moreover, Rei isn’t just strong—he has a personality that shows no mercy to his enemies.

Even if the opponent is a noble, Rei would never hesitate to attack.

“Hey, you two! Stop just standing there talking and come help us out!”

Shouted at by a fellow adventurer standing near the woodcutter, the two adventurers who had been talking hurriedly made their way over.

“Well, if the rumors spread and stop those idiots from picking a fight with me, I’d welcome that any day.”

Rei muttered as he stowed the wood the woodcutter had felled into his Misty Ring.

The two adventurers, who had been talking some distance away, probably never imagined that Rei could hear their conversation.

However, Rei’s body was a special one created by Zepair.

His five senses, including his hearing, were overwhelmingly sharper than those of an ordinary person, so it was easy for him to hear a conversation taking place a short distance away.

Rei knew exactly what kind of rumors were circulating about him… but he figured it was better than having to deal with some idiotic troublemakers.

(I wonder why Zepail made my body like this. If he’d at least made me as tall as an adult man, things wouldn’t have gotten this complicated. …Was it a technical limitation?)

With such thoughts in mind, Rei went about finishing his work.

“Don’t mess around! Why are you joining the wood like that? You should just hammer a nail in there properly!”

“Don’t be ridiculous! Iron will rust and become useless over time! Ten or twenty years is one thing, but if you think about a hundred or two hundred years down the line, it’s obviously better to join the timbers without using nails!”

At the construction site for the addition, two dwarves were currently locked in a heated argument, each insisting on their own point of view.

These dwarves are renowned as some of the most skilled craftsmen around, and when figures of their caliber get into an argument like this, it’s only natural that work on the site comes to a standstill.

The exact same exchange that had taken place at Marina’s house the day before was happening right there.

“Hey, what are we going to do? If this keeps up, the boss is going to get mad at us again.”

“I know you’re saying that. But what am I supposed to do? If I say anything here, I don’t think they’ll listen to me.”

“Well… I guess so.”

The laborers exchanged words among themselves.

If the argument continued like this, there was no doubt the construction schedule would fall behind again.

If that happened, the people in charge here would naturally come under fire from the higher-ups, and if things went badly, they might even lose their jobs.

Of course, given the current situation in Gilm, it wasn’t as if there were absolutely no other jobs available if they were fired from this one…

Still, it would undoubtedly be less profitable than their current job, and they’d prefer to avoid such a situation if possible.

That said, sticking his nose into the dwarves’ argument could easily backfire.

Despite their short stature, dwarves possess incredibly strong muscles.

If a dwarf hit me, I could end up seriously injured.

“What should we do? You tell me.” “No, you tell me…” The laborers were having this back-and-forth when, suddenly, a mass of water appeared above the heads of the arguing dwarves. They immediately released the water downward.

Naturally, the dwarves below were drenched by the water falling from above.

“Whoa!”

Even though they were arguing, the fact that they shouted the same thing suggests they were, in their own way, in sync.

But, naturally, the ones who’d been drenched like that weren’t exactly amused.

It’s summer now, so even if they get a little wet, they’ll dry off eventually. Still, there’s no doubt that being soaked is unpleasant.

“Who was that?!”

That’s exactly why they both shouted in unison, demanding to know who had gone and splashed water on them…

“It’s me. So, do you have a problem with that?”

Showing no sign of being fazed by the angry shouts from the two dwarves, Marina appeared.

Dressed in her usual party dress, she stood out starkly in this setting of manual labor.

Naturally, many people’s eyes were drawn to Marina in that party dress, which exposed a large portion of her back.

However, the two dwarves who had been shouting stopped moving the moment they saw Marina.

It wasn’t because they were mesmerized by her beauty…

“N-no. Of course not. Right?”

“Yeah, of course. We don’t have any complaints at all, so don’t worry about it and just get back to your work.”

Backing away almost reflexively, the two dwarves said this.

Called by someone who had helped them in the past and an old acquaintance, the two dwarves had come while Rei and the others were away from Gilm, but perhaps because their personalities didn’t mesh, they were constantly butting heads.

Of course, the biggest reason for their clashes was that they were both so confident in their own skills.

In any case, these two proud individuals were, as expected, arguing again yesterday… and when Marina walked in on them, they adopted an attitude that was practically a challenge.

No, it would be more accurate to say they were completely picking a fight.

But… even if they were dwarves, they were ultimately just craftsmen, so there was no way they could win against a skilled adventurer like Marina.

Since Marina’s job involved using spirit magic to streamline various tasks, she wasn’t carrying a bow.

Even so, it was easy for her to handle the two dwarves using only her martial arts and spirit magic.

Especially since Marina had recently been learning hand-to-hand combat from Vihera, she was a completely unstoppable opponent for the dwarves.

Even a dwarf who prided himself on his strength stood no chance against Marina like that… and was thoroughly beaten down.

The fact that they weren’t completely broken-spirited was partly due to Marina’s mercy, but above all, it was because the dwarves were stubborn… or, to put it another way, mentally strong.

Even so, having been defeated so thoroughly that they couldn’t even put up a fight, the two developed a strong sense of unease toward Marina.

“Right. Then, stop fighting and discuss this quickly so you can proceed with the construction in the best possible way. I’m warning you, though—if you waste any more time pointlessly… you know what’ll happen, don’t you?”

Marina announced this—no, declared it—with a beaming smile.

One might say that Marina, at that moment, possessed a feminine allure while wearing a smile like that of a loving mother.

In fact, everyone except the two dwarves who saw Marina’s smile was completely captivated by it. While her feminine allure was a bit too strong to describe her as a motherly figure, considering it was Marina, it was within acceptable limits.

But… the two dwarves at the center of it all, the very ones she was smiling at, were far from being captivated.

Even though she was smiling, for some reason, they felt a chill run down their spines.

In that state, all the two of them could do was meekly nod in response to Marina’s request.

“I’m glad you seem to understand. Well then, I’ll be counting on you.”

Saying only that, Marina headed off to another location.

Marina, who wields earth, water, and wind elemental magic with ease, has become an invaluable asset on this mission.

She couldn’t afford to spend any more time dealing with these two.

As Marina disappeared, the two men working as her dwarf subordinates let out sighs of deep regret.

The two dwarves exhaled as well, but theirs was a sigh of relief, not regret.

“……”

They exchanged silent glances and eventually nodded to each other without a word.

Normally, they would want to push through their own demands and crush the other side’s.

They had those feelings, but if they tried anything like that right here and now, Marina would undoubtedly make them pay dearly.

Precisely because they knew this, they had to quietly listen to the other’s arguments and accept whatever they could.

As a result, later on… the two dwarves would successfully merge their technologies while arguing back and forth, and that technology would endure for generations to come… but that is a story for the future.

“What the hell!? Are you kidding me, you jerk! If you do something wrong, you apologize! That’s only natural!”

“N-no. That’s why I’m apologizing like this…”

“Don’t mess with me! Do you really think a verbal apology is enough?! Show some sincerity—I want to see your sincerity!”

A spot just off the main street of Gilm… What was taking place there was clearly some form of extortion.

A man with an obviously large build was intimidating what appeared to be an ordinary citizen.

And hearing the content of their conversation, it was impossible to believe they were resolving the dispute peacefully.

Of course, there was no doubt that the man was at fault for accidentally bumping into the intimidating man in front of him, but being blackmailed for money over it was something even he couldn’t tolerate.

As a result, the intimidating man was threatening him, while the man on the receiving end was trying his best to get through it without incident.

But perhaps unable to tolerate the exchange any longer, the intimidating man raised his fist...

“But…”

With his fist still raised, the man collapsed to the ground.

Emerging from behind the fallen man was Vihera.

“Alright, that’s enough. Honestly, you shouldn’t be committing crimes with skills this poor. Hand this man over to the guards.”

“Yes.”

The men under Vihera’s command nodded in unison.

“Byune, thank you for letting me know.”

“Mm.”

And so, Vihera and Byune set out to look for other trouble.
