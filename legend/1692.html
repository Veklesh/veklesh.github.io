"Wow... that's amazing."

A massive fish lay on the table... a colossal fish, about two meters long.

And since it had been stored in Rei’s Misty Ring, it still retained the freshness of having just been caught.

In fact, since it was a fish Rei caught back when he first started fishing in the ocean, it would be strange if it hadn’t gone bad by now.

It’s not that there are no refrigerators… but since this is a high-grade magic item, it’s not something the average person can just easily get their hands on.

A fish caught days ago—especially during this season, from late summer into fall—would normally be rotten without a doubt.

But that fish was as fresh as could be.

“Here you go. It’s a souvenir.”

“Are you sure? A fish like this would be incredibly expensive if you bought it normally.”

Daskar asked, looking at Rei, and he wasn’t wrong.

To begin with, since Gilm is located far from the sea, the only seafood they eat—barring rare exceptions—is salted fish.

Of course, salted fish can also be turned into a delicious dish if you take the time to thoroughly rinse off the salt and cook it with care, so it’s not that it tastes bad.

But compared to a dish made with fresh fish by a skilled chef, it inevitably falls short.

Even if he were handed such fish so casually as a souvenir, Daskar, for once, found himself at a loss as to how to handle it.

Still, he quickly came to his senses and called for a maid—a reaction likely born of the resilience he’d developed as the lord of a town situated in a place that was, in many ways, unique.

...Though, to be fair, that resilience has been put to the test more than ever since a certain someone named Rei arrived in Gilm.

“Did you call for me?”

The maid who knocked and entered looked slightly surprised at the enormous fish on the table, but that expression vanished just as quickly.

“Take this fish to the kitchen. It’s a gift from Rei.”

“Understood. So, shall we serve a dish made with this fish for dinner tonight…?”

“Yes, that’s fine.”

Hearing the word “fish,” Daskar’s face lit up with a hint of joy.

Watching Daskar like that, Rei recalled the taste of the dishes made by Bistol, who was no longer here.

Bistol’s cooking was better than what you’d find at any ordinary diner; his seafood soup, in particular, was so delicious it was dubbed “the decadent seafood soup.”

“Can he really make something even better than that soup…?” he even found himself wondering.

Then again, as a chef in the lord’s manor, his skills were surely far beyond anything a cook in town could offer.

(If that’s the case, I’d love to try it at least once. …Wait, come to think of it, I’ve actually had it a few times already.)

The light meal, like a sandwich, served when I came to see Daskar.

Naturally, those were made by a cook employed by Daskar.

Since sandwiches are easy to make, no matter how skilled the cook was, they didn’t really stand out from the sandwiches sold at other shops.

...To be precise, there were various touches of ingenuity involved, but Rei’s sense of taste wasn’t that refined.

Well, taste is one of the five senses, and since Rei’s body was created by Zepair, it’s undoubtedly sharp… but in that regard, it’s probably just a matter of Rei’s lack of experience.

“Judging by the look of that fish, it seems to have really enjoyed the sea.”

“That’s true. Though we did have a pirate attack or two.”

In this case, “pirate attack” doesn’t mean they were attacked by pirates; it’s more accurate to say they attacked the pirates.

Daskar knew this through the Paired Orb, so he didn’t make a point of pointing it out.

After all, it was Daskar who requested that the pirates they’d captured and enslaved be brought all the way to Gilm.

Considering the circumstances, it’s a bit like, “What’s the point of bringing that up now…?”

“The pirate situation was unexpected for me too, but in this particular case, the benefits far outweighed the costs.”

“That’s true. Even though it’s quite dilapidated, we did manage to get a ship.”

Even when building a land vessel based on a sand vessel, Rei isn’t so carefree as to optimistically assume everything would go smoothly from the start.

Given that, they definitely need something to practice with.

Also, when it comes to studying ship structures and such, the more ships you have, the better.

In that sense, the two ships Rei had seized from the pirates were indeed invaluable assets.

“As for the payment for those ships…”

“Ah, please negotiate that matter with Marina.”

“...I figured as much.”

At Rei’s words, Daskar wore a slightly disappointed expression.

After all, if they had to negotiate with Marina, it was certain they’d end up with a contract that was definitely disadvantageous to them… and advantageous to Rei and his crew.

(I guess I’ll just have to tell the negotiator to think of it as a learning experience. I’d better pay them a little something in advance.)

Those who had negotiated with Marina in the past had been so mentally exhausted that they’d needed several days off.

That’s just how mentally draining negotiating with Marina is.

That said, anyone who negotiates with Marina sees a noticeable improvement in their negotiation skills.

In that sense, perhaps we should be grateful to Marina, but that doesn’t mean we can simply accept terms that put us at a disadvantage.

Even if we understand that it will undoubtedly benefit us in the future.

Still, the fact that he ultimately decides to accept it speaks to Daskar’s exceptional ability as a lord.

“I understand about the ship. So, what about the slaves?”

“They said they’d head for Gilm as soon as possible, so they’ll likely arrive before winter.”

“Hmm, then we’ll be able to train crew members to operate the land ships over the winter.”

For Daskar, land ships are almost like a gamble.

Building the facilities needed to construct land ships costs a great deal of money.

And if the attempt to build a land ship fails, all the funds spent up to that point would be wasted—for Daskar… and for Gilm.

But even so, Daskar was confident that the land ship project would succeed.

His proof, of course, was the existence of the Crimson Wings led by Rei.

If they needed any magical items, they could simply ask Rei and his group to procure them.

Furthermore, the Crimson Wings include Set, a being with astonishing speed.

There is also Marina, who freely wields spirit magic and possesses deep knowledge.

Even Vihera, who lacks such skills, is undoubtedly a valuable asset in many ways, given that she is a former princess of the Bestir Empire.

Furthermore, the alchemists in Gilm are highly skilled, as befits those who have gathered in a frontier town.

Considering all this, developing a land vessel may be a gamble, but it’s one with a very high probability of success.

That is precisely why Daskar got involved... one might say.

If the odds were low, Daskar wouldn’t have gotten involved on such a grand scale. And even if he’d been forced to get involved for some reason, he surely wouldn’t have gone so far as to build a facility from scratch just to construct a land ship.

It is precisely Daskar’s attitude that indicates a high probability of success for the land ships.

“That’s right. In winter, many adventurers take a break. In that sense, it’s the perfect time to study how to operate a ship.”

“Hm? Marina, what do you mean by that? When Daskar mentioned training crew members, he wasn’t referring to adventurers, was he? If not, then doesn’t it matter when they study? Of course, it’s better to get a head start on that sort of thing.”

Perhaps puzzled by Marina’s words, Elena asked with a hint of doubt.

From the flow of the conversation so far, it was clear that the crew members Daskar had in mind for his land-based ship were not adventurers… but rather his own subordinates.

But considering Marina’s words, there was a question as to why adventurers were being brought into the discussion.

Marina, who had been looked at, shot a meaningful glance at Vihera.

Perhaps understanding the meaning behind Marina’s gaze, Vihera gently looked away and reached for the tea sitting on the table, which had become spacious now that the fish had been cleared away.

“Once winter comes and adventurers stay indoors to rest, the commotion will die down, won’t it? Well, there might be some who get drunk and cause a ruckus… or other adventurers who cause all sorts of trouble.”

Like Vihera.

She didn’t say it out loud, but it was clear from their exchange what Marina was getting at.

In Vihera’s case, who relishes fighting powerful foes, it’s not uncommon for trouble to arise in the end, one way or another.

Vihera knew this too, so he couldn’t carelessly argue back.

“Hmm, I see. …Lord Daskar, you’ve got your hands full, don’t you?”

“Haha, I’m glad to hear you say that. For some reason, there aren’t many people who seem to understand just how tough things are for me.”

Daskar says this with a laugh, glancing at Marina.

But unlike Vihera earlier, Marina didn’t look away; instead, she smiled and spoke up.

“Oh, I think Daskar’s doing a great job, too.”

“……If you think so, it’d be a big help if you’d go easy on me a bit.”

“If I go easy on you, the next generation won’t learn anything. Do you ask people to go easy on you even when it’s not me?”

“Well…”

There was no way he could do such a thing.

If he were to do something like that, Daskar himself… and the Neutral Faction led by Daskar would be looked down upon by those around them.

Given that they currently lack the influence of the Noble Faction or the Royal Faction, there’s no way they could get away with that.

“Right? That’s why this is also a stern lesson in love.”

“...Hmph.”

Having been thoroughly outargued, Daskar could only snort in dissatisfaction.

Still, his ability to shift his mindset so quickly likely stems from his experience as the lord of Gilm.

“For now, I’d like Rei and the others to get back to the expansion work starting tomorrow… Is that okay? If you have other business, you can finish that first.”

He said this likely because he understood he was placing quite a burden on Rei and the others.

Forcing an order like this and having Rei and the others end up harboring ill feelings toward Daskar as a result… that would be the absolute worst-case scenario.

(Besides, if I were to do something like that, I can only imagine what Marina would say.)

Having the details of his childhood antics—things that seem embarrassingly childish in hindsight—laid bare would be nothing short of a nightmare for Daskar.

And if she were to bring up the absolute worst of those embarrassing memories—his proposal to Marina—he might even die of embarrassment.

Of course, if it would benefit Gilm, he had no intention of hesitating to use his authority as lord.

But there’s no need to do such a thing in the current situation; it’s better for Rei and the others to work with a clear conscience.

That said, as far as working comfortably goes, they’ve been enjoying a vacation for over ten days now, so I suppose that’s enough.

“Got it. I don’t really have anything specific to do… oh, maybe just have the guild examine the monsters I caught at sea. Oh, and I’m holding onto the wood from Treant Forest that was being transported by carriage, so I’ll be taking that too.”

Starting with the jellyfish monster, there were several types not listed in Rei’s monster compendium—monsters even Marina, the former guild master, and Bistol, the merchant, didn’t recognize.

I’d managed to get my hands on a magic stone, but I needed to figure out which parts counted as proof of defeat and whether they could be sold as materials… I needed to look into things like that.

Also, if I’m lucky, I might even find out how to cook and eat them.

(Cooking, huh... Bistol must be pretty busy right about now.)

I parted ways with Bistol before arriving at the lord’s mansion.

Unlike Rei’s Misty Ring, the magic pouch Bistol carries is subject to the passage of time.

Given that, he’s probably eager to sell the dried fish he worked so hard to make while at sea as soon as possible.

From Bistol’s perspective—having tagged along with Rei and the others on this trip—he’d managed to acquire a massive amount of merchandise without spending much money—though he was the one in charge of the cooking.

While they might not be worth much in the coastal villages and towns, if he takes them to a place like Gilm, their value will skyrocket.

If he played his cards right, there was no doubt he could pull off a deal that was practically a windfall.

(Well, considering Bistrel’s personality, I don’t think he’d get involved in any shady business. …But then again, is there even anyone he could do business with?)

Rei and the others had already gotten used to it, but Bistol’s appearance was quite striking.

Just how many people would actually do business with someone like Bistol... With such thoughts in mind, Rei continued his conversation with Daskar.
