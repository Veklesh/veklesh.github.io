Elena looks at the letter placed on the table.

Then she turns her gaze to the man who had placed the letter on the table—Daskar—and speaks.

"Did Father send this letter?"

"Yes. I've been in contact with Kerebel Duke regarding this matter, and it was sent during that correspondence."

"...I see."

Daskar's words weren't particularly surprising.

After all, the current Noble Faction was moving to strengthen ties with the Neutral Faction.

Of course, some disapprove of this and are using the expansion of Gilm as an excuse to make their move.

Elena was dispatched to Gilm to keep such opponents in check.

"For now, I'd like you to read it. I don't think it will be unfavorable to you, Elena."

Though Elena didn't show it on her face, Daskar's words surprised her slightly.

Given the current situation, if a letter had come from her father—Duke Kerebel, who led the Noble Faction—she had assumed it would simply tell her to finish her work at Gilm and return to him.

But the fact that Daskar himself was saying it wasn't bad news meant he probably knew the contents of the letter.

(What kind of letter could it be in this situation?)

Thinking this, Elena checked the wax seal, confirming the letter was indeed from her father. She used the paper knife Daskar had placed on the table to open the envelope.

For several minutes, Elena read the letter while the others watched silently. ...Only Byune was thoroughly enjoying her baked goods.

"...Hmph."

Having finished reading the letter, Elena let out a small sigh.

Her expression was not gloomy at all, but rather cheerful—likely because, as Daskar had said, the letter's contents were not bad news.

"Lady Elena?"

At Ara's words, Elena flashed a radiant smile and spoke.

"It says I am to accompany Rei and the others to the Kingdom of Reburina and assist with this matter."

Hearing Elena's explanation, Ara was likely the most surprised.

She never imagined Duke Kerebel would order Elena to be involved this deeply in the current matter.

And Ara wasn't the only one wearing a look of astonishment.

Everyone except Daskar, who already knew the details, and Byune, who was engrossed in eating, was taken aback by her words.

"Why on earth? I mean, Elena is the symbol of the Noble Faction, right? And now you're sending her to the Kingdom of Reburina—a vassal state of the Kingdom of Mireana, but one that could effectively become an enemy nation?"

Vihera's question echoed the doubts felt by Rei and Marina as well.

Of course, they were delighted Elena had agreed to come with them.

Not just for her combat strength, but also for her morale-boosting presence.

But as Vihera said, Elena is the symbol of the Noble Faction.

Sending her off to another country so easily was bound to raise questions.

To Vihera's question, Elena answered, running her fingers through her golden hair.

"It seems Father intends to use this opportunity to formally establish a cooperative relationship with the Neutral Faction. Therefore, the Noble Faction has apparently decided to cooperate regarding the actions taken against the Neutral Faction this time... yes, actions that could be called an attack by the Kingdom of Reburina."

"More precisely, an organization within the Kingdom of Reburina... What is it?"

Elena nodded at Marina's words.

"That's right. But do you really think an organization capable of something this significant has no connection to the state? At the very least, it's certain someone connected to that organization exists near the heart of the state."

Marina seemed convinced by this reasoning and offered no particular objection.

Her earlier interjection had likely been more out of caution than anything else.

Seeing the others remained silent, Elena spoke again.

"For that reason, I've decided to cooperate with Rei and the others. ...But, Ara?"

"Yes? What is it?"

Since Elena said she was going with Rei and the others, Ara had naturally assumed she would be going too. Suddenly addressed, she tilted her head in confusion.

To that Ara, Elena opened her mouth, looking somewhat hesitant.

"I will go with Rei and the others, but I need you to stay behind in Gilm, Ara."

"What?!"

Before Elena could even finish her sentence, Ara shouted.

Her words carried an emotion that could almost be called heart-wrenching.

"I am the captain of Elena's guard knights. As such, it is only natural that I accompany Elena!"

"...I too would find it reassuring to have Ara with us."

There was no falsehood in Elena's tone as she said this.

Indeed, Ara, who had grown up alongside her since childhood and could truly be called her childhood friend, was undoubtedly a highly dependable ally to Elena.

Of course, in terms of pure combat strength, she was inferior to Rei and the others—excluding Byune—but even so, the power of a single swing from her mighty axe was enough to easily defeat any ordinary adventurer.

She was also invaluable as Elena's conversation partner. But...

"Ara, we came to Gilm solely to prevent the Noble Faction from making any strange moves. Given that, it's not good for both me and Ara to leave Gilm."

"That..."

Ara was silenced by Elena's words.

She understood Elena's point was correct and undeniable.

But even so, Ara couldn't bring herself to nod in agreement.

It wasn't that she didn't trust Elena's abilities; she was simply genuinely worried for Elena's safety.

Rei suddenly noticed the genuine look of concern on Ara's face.

"How about giving Elena's Paired Orb to Ara? That way, you could always contact Elena, right? If you want, I could lend Ara my Paired Orb instead."

At Rei's words, those around them nodded in agreement.

Normally, magic items like Paired Orbs were extremely valuable.

Selling them could fetch a substantial fortune, enough to live comfortably for years... or even a lifetime, depending on the circumstances.

Of course, Rei wouldn't lend his Paired Orb to someone whose character he didn't know well. Fortunately, Rei knew Ara's character.

He was confident Ara would understand that such a move wouldn't just invite Rei's revenge, but would also risk Elena abandoning her.

That's why Rei could confidently propose lending the Paired Orb to Ara.

"I'd be happy if you did that... but are you sure?"

Rei nodded in agreement at Elena's question.

"Thank you, Rei."

Though he still seemed a bit reluctant to part with Elena, using the Paired Orb meant he could talk to her anytime.

Having seen Elena use the Paired Orb to talk with Rei and having spoken with Rei herself, Ara understood this perfectly well.

Watching Rei and Elena interact while sipping his tea, Daskar spoke up.

"My apologies. I really do rely on Rei for so much, Rei."

"No, that's not a problem at all. I was also curious about what Ajax's organization was planning."

According to the information from Ajax, the abducted women were being used as prostitutes.

But given that there weren't exactly few women who wanted to be prostitutes, deliberately abducting them from another country—especially the sovereign nation—using an expensive magic item like a slave collar was just plain odd, no matter how you looked at it.

There was definitely a reason they'd go to such lengths, but the fact remained that he didn't know what that reason was.

Normally, it wouldn't be something worth worrying about... but Rei found himself inexplicably preoccupied with it.

An uneasy feeling was probably the most honest way to describe Rei's current state of mind.

If Daskar hadn't brought this matter up, it was possible that Rei would have headed for the Kingdom of Reburina on his own, without saying a word.

Of course, it wouldn't be right away; it would likely be around winter, once the expansion work on Gilm settled down.

For that reason, having Daskar directly request his help this time was actually quite convenient for Rei. And besides...

"That magic item, the cage Daskar-sama made. I suppose I could have it, right?"

Yes, the magic item Daskar had crafted this time was precisely the kind of thing Rei would be extremely grateful to receive.

Unlike when he acted solo before, Rei was now part of the party known as Crimson Wings.

For now, they didn't need to travel long distances together, managing by means like clinging to Set's legs.

But relying solely on that method for future travel was cause for concern.

On the other hand, moving the entire party would require a carriage.

While the Sand Ship they acquired earlier could be used for desert travel, unfortunately, the Kingdom of Mireana had only a handful of deserts.

That said, it's true that some of the dungeons they'd encountered contained desert layers, so it wasn't entirely useless.

For that reason, for Rei... and for Crimson Wings, the magic item basket Daskar had prepared was extremely welcome.

"Hm? Well, I suppose so. If that's what Rei wants, I'll add it to the rewards. Though, truth be told, that sort of thing doesn't have many practical uses. It was originally crafted for Set, after all."

"Thank you. I'd like to say that's sufficient for us... but once this matter is settled, there are several improvements I'd like requested. At the very least, I'd appreciate it if you could modify it to use my mana instead of magic stones when changing the color of the base."

"I understand. This current situation was rushed to begin with. Once the alchemists have more time, I'll have them prepare for various modifications. Also, if Rei and the others encountered any issues while using it this time, we'll incorporate those fixes too. Naturally, I'll cover all necessary costs and materials."

"...Are you sure? Going to such lengths for us?"

The words that came out of Daskar's mouth were unexpected even for Rei.

But it seemed only Rei felt that way. Marina offered her usual, bewitching smile as she spoke.

"Well, considering it's a request to go to another country... one that could even be called an enemy state given the current situation... and wipe out an underground organization, I'd say this is actually quite cheap."

Daskar hadn't used the word "annihilation."

But considering the need to settle this matter, it was only natural to arrive at that conclusion.

In truth, Rei undoubtedly intended it that way too.

"If you have any other desired compensation, I could consider it. Is there anything?"

"Well, first off, we get to split whatever assets that organization holds. That's fine, right?"

"Well, yeah. It's similar to when we hunt down bandits."

"Anything else...?"

Laying eyes on Marina, Rei paused briefly before speaking.

"I have a magic item called a Sand Ship. Could you modify it so it works even off sand?"

"Sand Ship? ...Ah, come to think of it, there was a report about heading to the desert before. Did you acquire it then?"

"Yes. It's incredibly convenient, but it only works on desert terrain. If it could be modified for regular ground use, I think it would be quite revolutionary."

"I understand. But... to make the Sand Ship usable on land would require deploying a considerable number of alchemists and mages, and the materials needed would be enormous. I'm afraid we can't go that far."

"...Then, does that include this reward, plus the payment for the job I'm working on with the Gilm expansion project?"

Originally, Rei had intended to be paid for the expansion work with materials like fire ore.

But if the Sand Ship became usable, it would undoubtedly be incredibly convenient both as a means of transportation and for camping out.

"Ah, in that case, you can use my share of the reward for that too, if you like?"

"In that case, I'll join you on that ship."

Hearing Rei's words, Vihera and Marina both nodded in agreement.

But even after hearing that, Daskar didn't nod. ...No, he couldn't nod.

If they were truly to undertake the sand ship's renovation, it would undoubtedly become a major undertaking that would raise the cost of the Gilm.

Even the Gilm expansion project was stretching us thin. Adding more on top of that... well, it would undoubtedly be extremely difficult.

There simply weren't enough resources to spare.

As Daskar agonized over this, Rei suddenly smiled as if struck by an idea and looked back at him.
