On the night he received the gemstone as a reward from Lemuria… Rei strangely couldn’t sleep, so he lay in bed reading a book.

This book was borrowed from the library at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, which Miranda had mentioned earlier.

Although the staff were not usually allowed to lend out books, Rei was treated as a guest of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, so he was granted special permission to borrow it.

Naturally, he had been made to sign a contract stating that he would have to pay for any damage or tears to the book.

In any case, although he had borrowed the book under those conditions, it was the perfect way for Rei to pass the time right now.

……That said, the book wasn’t the kind of manga or novel Rei used to read back in Japan; it was a practical guide.

Among them, the book Rei borrowed was a guide to dissecting monsters.

Rei had seen similar books before and even bought some, but the one in this library was different from any he had seen so far.

In other words, it described methods for dissecting monsters that Rei didn’t yet know.

That alone was a blessing for Rei.

It had been several years since she’d come to this world, and while she’d been absolutely terrible at monster dissection at first, she’d now reached a point where—though she wouldn’t go so far as to say she was skilled—she could at least handle it to a certain extent.

However, that was only to a certain extent; compared to Marina, who had been an adventurer before becoming a guild master, or Byune, who had been tackling dungeons alone since she was a child, he still fell short.

Rei knew this, so he studied whenever he had a spare moment, hoping to improve his butchering skills even slightly.

“I see. So you’re supposed to slice along the bone as if sliding your blade over it. But… will it really work as well as they say?”

He read the instructions for butchering a giant boar monster—a creature found only deep within the mountains—and while he understood the logic, he couldn’t help but harbor a slight doubt.

Still, since it’s written in a book, it surely can’t be wrong.

Having come to that conclusion... she suddenly stopped turning the pages.

But it was only for a moment.

He immediately turned the page again and continued reading.

“A scorpion monster, huh? …Its venom seems potent, and those pincers look like a pain to deal with. Still, I hear its meat is pretty tasty. Well, apparently, the higher the monster’s rank, the better it tastes. So, naturally, a Rank B monster would be delicious. …What do you think?”

Without stopping his reading, Rei speaks.

Even though Rei is the only one in the room.

Miranda, who was assigned to Rei, is currently out on another job.

Even though she’d been assigned to Rei, she wasn’t exactly his personal maid.

Given that, it was only natural that she would be called away if she had other urgent work to attend to.

In a room where, under normal circumstances, only Rei should be present, no one would have responded to her question. …Yes, that is, under normal circumstances.

“……”

The person who had appeared in the room without him noticing simply stared at Rei in silence.

Rei, too, closed the book she had been reading as if to meet that gaze and turned her eyes toward them.

Their hair was silver.

It was a different hair color than when she first saw that person, but that wasn’t surprising.

After all, the second time they met, the person’s hair was already silver.

“Still silver hair, huh. …Could it be that you’re dyeing your hair?”

The thought suddenly crossed Rei’s mind, but the silver hair of the black wolf before him didn’t look unnatural, as if it had been dyed.

...Even when Rei lived in Japan, there were a few high school students who dyed their hair.

He had seen them being repeatedly reprimanded by the guidance counselor, and there was always something slightly off about their hair colors.

Perhaps Rei felt that way precisely because he remembered what their hair looked like when it was still black.

Besides, Rei hated being lectured by the guidance counselor, and considering the hassle of having to re-dye his hair as it grew out, he found it too much trouble and never touched the subject at all.

In any case, although Rei wasn’t very knowledgeable about hair dyeing, even to his eyes, the Black Wolf’s silver hair didn’t look like it had been dyed.

Since Rei wasn’t an expert on the subject, it was possible that the dye job was simply so skillfully done that he couldn’t tell, or perhaps something like magic or magical items—things that didn’t exist in Japan, or at least that Rei wasn’t aware of—were being used.

“Why don’t you say something, at least a little? You didn’t say a word the other day either… It’s not like you can’t speak, is it?”

Rei asked, but the Black Wolf remained silent as usual, just staring intently at him.

(Is this guy really an assassin? He doesn’t look like one at all.)

To begin with, the assassins Rei knew would observe their targets to find a weakness, but they would never come this close to their target… and still not attack.

Well, if his true identity were unknown to the target, it wouldn’t be strange for him to act like this… but now, Rei already knows that the Black Wolf is a skilled assassin.

(Ah, but when we met on the balcony the other day, he did attack, didn’t he? …Though he vanished right after.)

Thinking that far, something suddenly occurred to Rei.

“Could it be that you’re not attacking me… because there’s some kind of problem with the Seisor Marquis family?”

The suspicious behavior of the Seisor Marquis family that he’d heard from Leodanis.

In the end, he never found out the exact reason, but according to the information Rei had gathered, it seemed they were searching for something.

Could that be related to the Black Wolf?

That assumption certainly wasn’t far off the mark.

To begin with, the only thing Rei could think of that would cause the Seisor Marquis family to be so desperate as to mobilize people like this was the matter involving Gaiska—who had likely hired the Black Wolf.

……However, given that the Seisor Marquis family holds significant influence and clout even among the Noble Factions, the possibility that some trouble Rei is unaware of has arisen cannot be entirely ruled out.

In any case, there is no doubt that the Black Wolf standing before him is the most suspicious figure at the moment.

“……”

However, the Black Wolf did not respond to Rei’s words. As always, he remained silent, simply staring intently at Rei as if observing him.

At first, I thought he might be looking for a weakness in my guard, but judging by his demeanor, that doesn’t seem to be the case.

They continued to stare at each other like that for several minutes.

Eventually, as if giving in, Rei spoke up.

“Hey, you. What do you want, anyway? Didn’t you come here to kill me? If that’s the case, you don’t seem to be doing anything about it.”

“……”

“Did Gaiska do something? Is that why you can’t mess with me anymore? …Or am I wrong?”

Rei asked the Black Wolf this, as if just testing the waters, but the Black Wolf still gave him no response.

Finally, as if he’d given up, Rei spoke to the Black Wolf.

“Listen, just so you know, I’m not idle enough to hang around with you forever. Do you intend to fight me or not? Could you just make that clear? To begin with, I’m leaving Anesis soon. Do you realize that once I’m gone, you won’t be able to attack me anymore?”

Rei didn’t understand why he was acting this way toward the black wolf—why he was giving it what could only be described as advice.

Normally, the person standing right in front of him should be someone out to kill him.

If that were the case, he wouldn’t need to go out of his way to give advice like this—he could just defeat him.

Rei understood from the incident on the balcony that Black Wolf was no pushover.

Even so, he was certain he could win if he fought with everything he had.

So, instead of going out of her way to act like she’s giving advice, she could just defeat him with her own hands… but for some reason, she didn’t feel like doing that.

Of course, Rei doesn’t think the Black Wolf standing before him is a harmless opponent.

To begin with, even though Black Wolf was standing still in front of Rei right now, he wasn’t exactly an innocent opponent.

On the day of the festival known as the mock battle… whether it was to test his strength or for some other reason, Rei didn’t know, but he had manipulated more than ten people with drugs and set them against Rei.

Rei had heard from Leodanis that many of those defeated by Rei, Set, and Brewitt at that time had died, and those who survived were now suffering from the aftereffects of the drugs.

As for how Leodanis—who was on a date with Miranda when that incident occurred—knew such details… well, since he belongs to the Knights, the information must have come from there.

“……”

No matter what Rei says, the Black Wolf remains silent, simply staring at him.

Standing before that Black Wolf, Rei feels his fighting spirit fading away even more.

The one standing before him is, without a doubt, an assassin.

He had come here to kill me.

And yet, why was he acting so recklessly?

It must be because of the Black Wolf’s demeanor.

Although he is undoubtedly attacking her, for some reason she cannot bring herself to feel hostility toward him.

In fact, it feels as though it would be easier to view that annoying noble as an enemy than to view the Black Wolf standing right in front of me.

(Could that be the Black Wolf’s ability? Something that prevents me from feeling hostility—no, animosity—toward it, or something like that?)

He wasn’t entirely without such thoughts, but Rei had never heard of any skills, magic items, or spells like that.

……That said, it’s not as if Rei knew everything about the magic, skills, and magical items in Elgin.

No, in fact, there were probably far more things he didn’t know.

To begin with, the Black Wolf could vanish from the spot without Rei even sensing its presence.

This time, too, there was no sound of the door opening, but before he knew it, the Black Wolf was inside Rei’s room.

The fact that he was able to sense the Black Wolf’s presence, even if only vaguely, might be because Rei had grown somewhat accustomed to it through his repeated encounters with the creature.

In any case, whether he fought or not, the Black Wolf’s ability to appear and vanish at will was something Rei wanted to get to the bottom of.

...Noticing that the Black Wolf was still staring intently at him, a sudden thought occurred to Rei, and he spoke up.

“Hey, Black Wolf. Is the ability to vanish and reappear without me noticing you the effect of a magic item or something?”

It was truly just a random thought.

Normally, no assassin would ever reveal their abilities to their opponent.

That’s why, even though it was probably pointless, Rei figured there was no harm in just giving it a try… or so he thought as he asked, but…

“……”

That’s exactly why he was surprised to see Black Wolf shake his head, even if he didn’t say a word.

She hadn’t expected that kind of response to her question at all.

“Um, just to be sure… You’re not appearing and disappearing because of some magic item’s effect, right? Is that the right understanding?”

In response to Rei’s question, the Black Wolf nodded.

Seeing that, it was clear the Black Wolf had answered Rei’s question.

Why is he answering my question here?

That was Rei’s genuine question.

He knew, of course, that the Black Wolf was targeting him.

So why would he go out of his way to reveal his abilities to someone like that?

He didn’t know the reason, but if the Black Wolf was offering information on his own, there was no reason not to take advantage of it.

“So, is it magic or something? Though, he didn’t seem to be chanting any spells.”

At Rei’s question, the Black Wolf shook his head again.

It’s not a magic item, and it’s not magic.

If that’s the case, there’s only one possibility left. In other words…

“Is it your skill?”

At Rei’s words, the Black Wolf nodded without the slightest hesitation.

“...I see. A skill, then.”

The hint of disappointment in Rei’s voice likely stemmed from the fact that he had been hoping it might be some kind of magic item—something he could take from the Black Wolf.

Looking at the Black Wolf now, however, he simply couldn’t bring himself to think that way.

Still, thinking “Maybe…” was only natural for Rei, given his hobby of collecting magic items.
