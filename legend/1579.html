Hearing the sound, Rei and the others hurried deeper into the cave.

Soon, they came upon a massive door identical to the one that had been in the place where Rei and the others had been just moments before.

However, there were clear differences.

For instance, the door was significantly warped.

And above all, the fact that it was shaking as if something were banging against it from the inside was clearly different.

"So, Rei's prediction was right after all?"

"No, it's a lot more dramatic than I anticipated."

Even if the giant was running amok, there was a glimmer of hope that, like what Rei and the others had seen earlier, it wouldn't cause such a huge commotion if the soldiers weren't there.

But judging from this, there was no doubt a giant was rampaging on the other side of that door.

If the reason for the rampage was giants fighting each other, that would be fine. But...

(If they're rampaging because there's no one to eat, and they come out looking for prey, that's a bit troublesome.)

That giants eat humans was clear from the scene they'd just witnessed.

But the question was: did giants eat other giants?

Honestly, Rei would prefer it if the giants just devoured each other.

If the door shaking was due to that devouring...

But if the giants weren't devouring each other, and instead had formed some kind of cooperative relationship to escape from there, and that was the reason the door was shaking... then that meant over a thousand giants were writhing on the other side of that door.

And judging by how they devoured the soldiers, they're hungry—if not starving.

"The problem is whether they'll ignore us the moment the door opens and try to leave the cave."

"...I see. In that case, maybe we should have asked Marina to come after all?"

Rei pondered for a moment at Vihera's words, spoken while her thoughts drifted to the prey likely waiting beyond the door.

Vihera's words weren't wrong.

If Marina were here, she could have used earth elemental magic to build a wall or something to stop the giant's movements.

Or she could have reinforced the door to keep it shut, preventing the giant from escaping.

That way, they might have even been able to wait until the giant starved to death.

(No, with the giant's strength, if the door wouldn't open, it would likely dig through the wall to escape. ...Worse still, it might collapse the ceiling. Thinking that way, trapping it is a bad strategy.)

A cave collapse was something Rei wanted to avoid at all costs.

If it could be limited to just the space containing the giants, that might be manageable, but that wasn't an option.

Rei couldn't help but wonder if Marina's spirit magic might work... but his guess was it probably wouldn't.

"It would've been nice to have Marina here, but then we'd have to leave Vihera behind to hold off those guys over there, right? Elena is acting as the Noble Faction's representative alongside me, so she needs to witness the proceedings here."

"Well then, let's start thinking about getting through that door."

Vihera's words, delivered without a moment's hesitation, made Rei smile wryly.

Thinking, That's so typical of Vihera... as he felt that familiar sense of recognition, Rei's gaze shifted back toward the door.

The door was warped from being struck from the inside.

And being warped meant it couldn't be opened, even if one tried.

(You'd think even a Titan would have enough intelligence to open a door. ...Ah, but if this was a place for confining Titans, it's possible it was designed to be unopenable from the inside, just in case.)

Muttering to himself while staring at the door, Rei contemplated what to do.

No, exterminating the giants was already a settled matter, but how exactly to do it?

"Judging by the door's condition, there's definitely a massive horde of giants right outside it."

"I suppose so. Then, I could handle it, if you like?"

It was Vihera, of course, who declared this with a confident smile.

"What exactly do you plan to do? I was thinking of using the Twilight Spear..."

Rei was certain that hurling the Twilight Spear would easily pierce a door of that caliber.

For the giants beyond the door, the Twilight Spear would suddenly pierce through the very door they were attacking and fly in.

They wouldn't be able to dodge such an attack, and while the door would get a hole, it would only be large enough for the Twilight Spear to pass through.

Given that, the door wouldn't break with a single strike, of course. And the Twilight Spear had the function of returning to Rei's hand whenever he willed it.

Until the door is destroyed, the giants would be subjected to relentless, one-sided attacks.

...Though, with the enemy numbering around a thousand, it was like pouring water on a burning stone.

Still, true to the saying that a journey of a thousand miles begins with a single step, Rei believed it was necessary to defeat them little by little. That's why he couldn't understand what Vihera was saying.

"Is there anything we can do?"

"Well, yes. I can take out a few more enemies before Rei uses the Twilight Spear."

Saying this, Vihera advanced toward the door without waiting for Rei's response.

Rei's actions seemed to say she would simply do what needed to be done, no matter what Rei said.

Watching Vihera like this, Rei silently pulled the Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring, saying nothing more, simply observing what Vihera would do.

Elena and Byune also silently watched Vihera's actions.

Though she possessed a penchant for combat, in terms of raw fighting prowess, Vihera was undoubtedly top-tier... no, her skill was so exceptional it could easily be called superlative.

(Practice makes perfect, they say. That phrase was practically made for Vihera.)

As Rei thought this, Vihera placed her hand gently on the door without any sign of tension.

Even as the door shook violently from the assault within, Vihera showed no sign of concern whatsoever.

The magic item providing light around the door had already fallen to the floor from the impact inside.

Still functional, they cast a steady glow over the surroundings.

Wearing a thin garment like a dancer or a courtesan, Vihera's figure, illuminated from below, presented a somewhat different appearance to those around her than usual.

Fantastical... that description would be fitting.

It was merely a change in the light's position, yet the impression it created was so different that it surprised both Elena and Byune.

...Only Rei had seen a scene in a movie or something where someone was pole dancing, and she had seen that kind of scene where the light shone up from below. So, while she was captivated by Vihera now, she wasn't quite rendered speechless...

Touching the swaying door, Vihera spoke without a trace of tension, her voice accompanied by a sharp exhale.

"Haah!"

Thud.

The sound echoed around them.

A dull thud, like meat being struck with a stick or something.

Then, from beyond the door came the giant's scream and the sound of something heavy collapsing.

"So the Magic Palm could do something like that?"

Elena murmured thoughtfully.

The Magic Palm was a skill that used magic power to drive shockwaves directly into an opponent's body.

Everyone present knew just how devastating its power could be.

But the fact it could exert that power through a door to the other side was likely unexpected.

Hearing that gasp of surprise, Vihera placed her hand on the door once more, turning to Elena and the others with a seductive smile.

Seeing Elena's astonished expression, she nodded with satisfaction and once again released the Magic Palm with a sharp exhale.

Each time, the sound of a giant struck by the Magic Palm crashing to the ground, or the thud of their body hitting the door upon falling, echoed around them.

Vihera smiled again and repeated the same action several times.

After several minutes, or perhaps ten, Elena suddenly spoke up.

"I wonder what those giants are thinking. It wouldn't be strange for them to understand that touching the door would harm them."

"I wonder. It's debatable whether they truly understand that simply touching the door will cause them to be attacked. Did the rampaging giants devouring the soldiers look like they were thinking about anything? Rather, I think it's precisely because they can no longer think about such things that it's called rampaging."

"...When they're rampaging, they don't care why their comrades fall, do they? Or rather, they can't care?"

"Probably. Elena, you've been on the battlefield countless times, right? Then, even if not to the extent of a Titan, you must have seen someone rampaging before?"

At Rei's words, Elena seemed to recall something and nodded.

But glancing back at the door, she spoke again.

"What Vihera's doing is impressive, but at this rate, won't the door hold out?"

"Probably."

The rampaging giants showed no sign of stopping their assault on the door, seemingly utterly indifferent even as their comrades fell.

No, perhaps the fall of their comrades only fueled their excitement, for their assault on the door grew even more intense.

The door was already completely warped, looking as if it could be blown away at any moment.

But Vihera, as if to say none of that mattered, kept on, kept on, kept on, using his Magic Palm to take down the giants beyond the door.

The Magic Pals were unleashed one after another, their number already nearing a hundred.

Even Vihera had never before unleashed such a continuous barrage of Magic Palms.

The Vihera who had been smiling seductively just moments ago was now breathing slightly harder.

Still, Vihera wasn't incapacitated. That was likely due to her monstrous stamina.

Though she'd probably take offense at being called monstrous.

"Phew... How about it?"

"Ah, that's enough. Come back. Staying out there any longer would be dangerous for all sorts of reasons."

The giant's attack had warped the door; it could come off at any moment.

While the door itself hadn't been broken, the cave wall it was embedded in was on the verge of collapsing instead.

It felt like it was only a matter of minutes... or even less, before the door would be torn out of its frame entirely.

That was precisely why Rei told Vihera to return.

Vihera, too, seemed to understand the door's condition perfectly well.

Eventually, he looked at the door and then at Rei in turn... and when he caught a faint glimpse of the giant's face, seemingly already beyond reason, through the gap created by the door's warping, he turned back to Rei with a hint of disappointment, though still obediently.

From Vihera's perspective, he had hoped to fight a giant even more out of control than the one they had just battled—one driven not merely by hunger, but into a frenzy bordering on madness.

Still, he obeyed Rei's words, instinctively realizing that would be more interesting.

Returning to Rei's side with a lightness of step that defied belief—a movement so nimble it could easily be mistaken for skipping—Vihera smiled and spoke.

"So? What now? The Twilight Spear, after all?"

"That's how it'll be. That door won't hold out much longer anyway... no, it'll probably just come off."

"...I suppose so. If you'd been a little more restrained, we could've reduced the number of giants."

"Do you know exactly how many giants we took down?"

Laying the Twilight Spear to his shoulder, Rei asked. Vihera considered for a moment, then shook her head.

"Since it was through the door, I can't say exactly how much damage my strike did. Still, I think a significant number of giants suffered fatal wounds."

Unlike a direct attack, this time there was the door as a buffer.

That was precisely why Vihera couldn't gauge the exact amount of damage she'd inflicted.

Vihera considered whether she should incorporate that aspect into combat training.

...If Rei had known about that, she would have definitely teased her about pursuing the path of an assassin instead of a warrior.

In any case, Rei nodded at Vihera's words... then readied the Twilight Spear. The next instant, he took a few paces of running start and hurled it toward the door.
