“……Huh?”

Those were the words that came out of Rei’s mouth as he looked around the room, and though Elena and Ara didn’t say it out loud, they were thinking the same thing.

Since they’d built up the suspense so much, they naturally assumed the mastermind behind this whole affair—specifically, the boss of the organization or someone like that—would be inside the room.

But when they actually opened the door, what they found was an empty room.

Given that, it was only natural for them to react that way.

“Shouldn’t we go inside and take a look, at least?”

Hearing Elena’s voice from behind him, Rei nodded in agreement and stepped inside.

He looked around the room again, but there was still no one there.

However, even Rei could tell that every piece of furniture in the room was extremely expensive.

In fact, they were in no way inferior to the furniture found in the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion.

No, in fact, depending on the piece, some of the furniture in this room was likely even more expensive.

Of course, that was merely from Rei’s perspective; the reality might be different.

“From what I can see… no, Rei.”

Elena, who had been looking around the room just like Rei, suddenly noticed the air in the room shifting… she realized that a faint breeze was coming in from somewhere.

Rei, who had been looking at the furniture with curiosity, also noticed the breeze coming from nowhere at Elena’s words.

“Where is this coming from?”

“Over here, Lady Elena, Rei.”

Ara, who had been looking in a different direction from the two of them, spoke up from in front of the wall where a painting of a massive mountain hung.

The wall Ara had found had a tiny gap, and the breeze stirring the air in the room was coming from there.

As Rei and Elena approached, they could feel the current of air more distinctly.

“Do you think this was… done on purpose?”

“They fled in a panic and didn’t close the wall properly. It’s not impossible that’s what happened…”

Saying that, Elena turned her gaze back to the room.

“All the furniture has excellent taste. I can’t imagine someone who collects such pieces would do something like that. Besides, this person leads one of the largest criminal organizations in Anesis. I simply can’t believe someone like that would flee in such a disgraceful manner.”

“Good taste and personal ability… or in this case, guts? I don’t think the two are necessarily proportional. Anyway, the fact that he ran off like this means he realized he couldn’t beat us, right? If so, isn’t this punishment enough? All that’s left is the paperwork and such.”

Glancing around the room, Rei’s gaze naturally fell first on the desk.

He had a hunch that if there were any documents related to the Black Wolf, they would likely be on the desk.

“What should we do?”

It was Elena, not Rei, who was in charge of this situation.

Given that, it was only natural for Rei to ask Elena what he should do.

“Hmm. …Ara, can you handle this area? Rei and I are heading toward that wall—beyond the secret passage.”

“Understood. Then I’ll stay here and see if I can find any clues regarding documents.”

Elena’s decision was to split their forces into two groups.

Normally, that would mean spreading our forces too thin.

But in this case, it wasn’t such a problem.

Rather, she sent Rei and Elena—her two strongest fighters—into the unknown area where enemies might be lurking, while Ara, who was clearly outmatched in combat ability, stayed behind to search for documents.

You could call this putting the right people in the right places.

Ara understood this, which is why she didn’t argue with Elena’s decision.

Ara probably wanted to go with Elena, but she wouldn’t make the foolish mistake of letting her own selfish feelings override the best course of action.

……The first time she met Rei, she saw Elena and Rei gazing at each other and immediately attacked Rei… but compared to that time, she had clearly grown.

Perhaps this was because she had taken on the role of leading Elena’s guard.

“Hmm, I’ll leave it to you. …Now, the question is how to proceed through what appears to be this secret passage…”

“Should we just force our way through and push forward?”

“What if that triggers some kind of trap? Even if we do it, that should be a last resort.”

Elena looked slightly exasperated at Rei’s suggestion.

Given that the enemy has gone to such lengths to lure them in, it wouldn’t be surprising if they’d set a trap that would destroy the house itself the moment the door was broken.

To begin with, the numerous traps set up in this mansion—how exactly were they set in the first place…? No, perhaps they’re being reset one after another; the method remains unknown.

It’s likely the effect of a magic item or something similar, but that isn’t confirmed either.

Given that, it was only natural to proceed with caution.

“So, what are we going to do? We don’t have time to leisurely search for a way to open this hidden door…”

We shouldn’t have any.

Just as Rei was about to say that, the door behind the painting slid open, revealing a secret passage.

“……”

Rei looked at Elena without saying a word.

He wondered if Elena had done something to open the door.

But Elena, whom Rei was looking at, was also staring at the door that had suddenly opened before them, her face frozen in surprise.

That left only one possible candidate for the person who had created this scene...

“Um…”

Rei, whom Elena—and, a moment later, Ray—turned to look at, reached into one of the drawers in the desk and froze.

Seeing that, it was clear what had caused this situation.

“Well done.”

The first to speak in the silence was Elena.

Praising Ara for a job well done, she looked visibly relieved upon hearing it.

(There’s a possibility that whoever’s waiting for us ahead intentionally opened this secret door… but no, maybe I shouldn’t mention that. It’ll become clear once we go through the secret passage.)

Rei thought this as he watched Elena and Ara, but he decided there was no point in saying it given the current situation, so he kept his mouth shut.

Besides, he felt that if it were something he had thought of, Elena would have noticed it too.

“Alright, Ara. Rei and I will go check out this secret passage. Ara, please continue searching for documents here.”

“Understood. …As long as Lady Elena and Rei are here, I don’t think there’s anything to worry about, but please be careful.”

Nodding at Ara’s words, Elena and Rei made their way down the secret passage.

As it had been built inside a room, the secret passage was barely wide enough for one person to pass through, and it would be difficult to pass anyone coming from the other end.

That said, if anyone were to come from the other end of this secret passage under the current circumstances, it would almost certainly be an enemy.

Given that, there was no need to worry about passing anyone.

In fact, given how narrow the passage was, it would be nearly impossible to swing the Death Scythe, and while it might be possible to thrust the Twilight Spear, it would be impossible to parry an attack.

For Rei, who excels at handling long weapons, this was a more troublesome location than a clumsy enemy.

“It’s dim, but there’s definitely a light here.”

“Mm-hmm. It seems a magic item that emits a faint glow has been embedded here.”

Magic items that provide light come at a considerable price.

But for someone leading the most influential underground organization in Anesis, the second-largest city of the Kingdom of Mireana, gathering magic items of that price range is likely a simple matter.

Besides, secret passages like this are usually only used in emergencies… and to ensure that the passage isn’t too dark to navigate during such times, it’s undoubtedly necessary to have some sort of lighting, even if it’s just dim.

(Though, the need for light is strictly limited to when I’m passing through this secret passage myself. Leaving the light on like this even after I’ve gone through… is that an invitation? Surely they wouldn’t leave the light on all the time when it’s not in use.)

It’s probably an invitation.

Convinced of this, Rei called out to Elena behind him as he made his way down the secret passage.

“Elena, I think this is an invitation… what do you think?”

“Hmm, I’m sure it’s an invitation too. But I do wonder why they’d go out of their way to lure us out.”

The two, known by the nicknames “Crimson” and “Princess General.”

Even if they went to the trouble of luring them out, it would be pointless if they ended up fighting and losing.

“Maybe they’re luring us to the end of this passage so they can escape from the house while we’re distracted?”

“That… no, it’s possible. It would be a blow to his pride, but if he figures he can make a comeback as long as he’s alive, there’s some merit to what Rei says. However, even if there’s a high chance we’re being lured out, there’s no way he’d leave such an obviously suspicious secret passageway alone.”

“I suppose so.”

Rei, too, understood what Elena was saying.

Just because there’s a high chance we’re being lured out doesn’t mean we can ignore this blatantly suspicious secret passage—it could lead to trouble down the line.

They might face criticism for not investigating such an obviously suspicious secret passage, or failing to do so could lead to unnecessary casualties.

“In that case, I guess we have no choice but to keep moving forward for now. …If possible, is the boss of the target organization ahead… Come to think of it, I didn’t catch his name. Would it be okay to ask about that?”

“Yeah. The name of the person we’re currently attacking is Celestan. He’s a man in his mid-thirties.”

“...That’s a bit surprising. He’s the boss of the most powerful organization in Anesis, right? For someone in that position, isn’t thirty a bit young?”

Rei said he was young, but Rei’s apparent age was mid-teens.

In other words, Celestan is a man more than twice Rei’s current age.

Of course, considering Rei’s actual age, it wouldn’t quite be more than double. …Still, there’s no doubt he’s nearly twice as old.

“That’s true. But the fact that he was able to lead an organization at that age means he’s exceptionally capable. Though, there’s no doubt that his capabilities were bolstered by the Black Wolf as a combat asset.”

Black Wolf is, after all, a freelance assassin, but the fact that Celestan could maintain contact with him means he could also commission Black Wolf to carry out assassinations.

If that’s the case, there’s no way Celestane would have chosen not to use Black Wolf—such a powerful weapon—to expand the organization’s influence.

“But Black Wolf is no longer here.”

For a fleeting moment, a hint of sadness flickered across Rei’s face.

But it vanished just as quickly, and he focused his attention on what lay beyond this secret passageway... when suddenly, he realized something.

“Isn’t this secret passage sloping downward? And it’s pretty curved, too… Was it this wide all the way here?”

At least five minutes have passed since they started down the secret passage.

Considering the size of the house Rei had entered, it shouldn’t have been big enough to walk through for five whole minutes.

Despite that, for some reason, they were still walking, and there was no sign of the secret passage ending.

It was clearly abnormal… but even so, for Rei and Elena, who had witnessed all those unreasonable traps, this was something they could easily imagine.

“This is probably some kind of magic item, too. I never expected Anesis to have a magic item like this.”

Elena muttered this with a slightly bitter expression, because whether it was the trap-inducing magic item or the one interfering with this space, none of them were things that could be easily obtained.

(Well, at this point… I can’t help but feel there’s a possibility it might not actually be a magic item.)

For now, since she couldn’t explain it, she was treating it as a magic item, but Rei felt a slight sense of unease at the idea that a single person could possess both a trap magic item and a magic item capable of manipulating space.

If they were magic items of that caliber, it wouldn’t be strange at all for rumors to spread just from the fact that someone possessed them.

For that not to happen, it would mean he’d acquired them but never used them at all.

Just as he was thinking that, as if on cue, a light appeared at the end of the secret passage.

“Looks like the exit.”

“Mm-hmm.”

Elena nodded at Rei’s words, and the two stepped out of the secret passage… and at that very moment, as if everything that had happened up to that point had been a lie, a strong, pungent smell of blood filled the air.
