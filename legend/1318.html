"...Huh? Um, why would that happen?"

After a few seconds of silence, Tris asked the man named Rei who was looking at her.

Though overwhelmed by Rei's presence, Tris managed to ask anyway—likely out of sheer pride as a merchant.

But even after asking again, the gaze Rei directed at Tris didn't change.

There's a term for it—"piercing gaze"—but what Tris was experiencing now felt like something almost physical, like pressure.

Tris was also known for her competence within the Spier Trading Company.

Even so, facing Rei now, she couldn't afford to move carelessly.

...No, the fact that he could even speak up like this meant he deserved some credit for that alone.

Facing Tris like this, Rei repeated the same question he'd asked seconds ago.

"Spier Trading Company is my enemy. Is that your understanding?"

"I have no such intention!"

Even after hearing it twice, the words coming from Rei's mouth were still shocking to Tris.

Still, she frantically shook her head, shouting that she had no intention of opposing Rei.

The flesh on her cheeks and jaw jiggled, giving her a somewhat comical appearance... Unfortunately, no one present could find it amusing.

"But you plan to deal with anyone who comes after me yourself, right? You want to stop me from getting involved. ...Then doesn't that mean our opinions fundamentally clash?"

"Rei, doesn't that make the word 'enemy' a bit inappropriate? I'd say 'competitor' is a more accurate description."

Marina said this, trying to calm Rei down.

Hearing Marina's words, Tris felt relieved, though he didn't show it on his face.

For Gilm specifically, antagonizing Rei would be problematic in many ways.

First, antagonizing Rei meant antagonizing his familiar, Set.

Given that Set is essentially Gilm's mascot character, it's only natural that those who adore him would likely hold a poor impression of the Spier Trading Company.

Beyond that, antagonizing Rei itself would undoubtedly cause significant damage to the Spier Trading Company.

After all, how many people could actually stop Rei if he went berserk?

"Competitors... huh. Yeah, that might be the more accurate description. But if you interfere with our actions, the Spier Trading Company will consider you enemies too. Are you sure you want that?"

Though Marina's words made Rei reconsider his stance slightly, the shift from outright hostility to merely a slight improvement was still minimal.

...That slight improvement could very well be a lifeline for the Spier Trading Company, but even so, Tris wished, if possible, that Rei would simply observe this matter quietly.

Wondering if there was anything he could do... eventually, Tris remembered Rei's hobby.

Yes, Rei's hobby of collecting magic items.

Clinging to a sliver of hope, Tris spoke up.

"I know you're angry about this incident, Rei. But could you please find a way to help us? Of course, we'll make amends for the trouble we've caused. I hear collecting magic items is your hobby? The Spier Trading Company handles magic items too."

"Out of the question."

Rei replied without a moment's hesitation.

It was true he had a hobby of collecting magic items.

That was a fact.

But... even so, the idea that this incident could be settled by bribing him with magic items was unsettling, even unpleasant.

"Don't underestimate me. ...It makes me want to burn the Spier Trading Company to the ground."

Rei pulled down the hood of his Dragon Robe and declared with a smile.

It was precisely the kind of smile Tris usually wore – one where the mouth curves upward, but the eyes remain cold and unyielding.

"Well... I suppose. If you're willing to hand over the Item Box to me, I might consider it. Of course, not the degraded version, but the genuine Item Box."

Item boxes are extremely rare.

Considering how many times Rei himself had been targeted for his item box, it was easy to imagine why.

Even the degraded version Elena possessed had significantly lower performance compared to the original Item Box it was based on.

There might be limits, but compared to Rei's Item Box, which he perceived as having almost unlimited storage capacity, the degraded version couldn't hold nearly as much.

Even so, it can hold about a room's worth of belongings, making it vastly more convenient for transport than a carriage.

The item box also possesses the ability to halt the passage of time for items stored within.

This is why Rei can eat freshly cooked meals anywhere.

In contrast, time passes normally for items stored in the degraded type.

Despite this vast difference in performance, degraded item boxes are extremely rare. It's not uncommon for them to be traded for light gold coins, let alone platinum coins.

If even the degraded type holds such value, it goes without saying how valuable a genuine item box must be.

Fundamentally, unlike degraded types, current technology cannot replicate them.

Only a finite number exist, and only a handful are known to exist.

Perhaps someone possesses one without anyone knowing, but even if that were the case, finding it would be difficult.

At the very least, it's not something the Spier Trading Company could acquire.

Spier Trading Company is a fairly large trading house, but it's only large to a certain extent.

If you look around Elgin... no, even limiting it to the Kingdom of Mireana alone, there are plenty of trading houses larger than Spier Trading Company.

Spier Trading Company is, at best, a mid-to-upper tier... or perhaps just mid-tier merchant house.

It was simply impossible to obtain an item box.

"Well... it's embarrassing for a merchant to say this, but it's impossible."

"Then give up, I suppose."

"But!"

Tris shouted, cutting off Rei's words.

For Tris too, retreating here was absolutely out of the question.

If someone else resolved this matter in the power struggle within Spier Trading Company, Tris would be looked down upon.

As a merchant, that was absolutely unforgivable.

If she did anything here that made others look down on her, it would be like smearing mud on the Spier Trading Company's reputation.

As the person entrusted with managing the branch, such a thing was absolutely impossible.

Information flows quickly among merchants, and it was only a matter of time before this matter became public.

The Azott Trading Company, which wields significant influence even in Gilm, has been completely drawn into the internal strife of the Spier Trading Company.

It's unlikely that the Azoth Trading Company would try to conceal this incident.

They would have taken some action, declaring a just cause.

"Please... please, is there anything you can do? I beg you."

Watching Tris bow deeply, Rei paused to consider.

In his mind, making Precious take responsibility for this incident was a settled matter.

But seeing Tris's state, he also felt it might be worth hearing their side of the story.

He had no grudge against Spier Trading Company or Tris himself... nor any particular attachment.

"What do you think?"

"Well... Neither Rei nor Tris intends to give up. So why not let each of them act according to their own will? And whoever reaches Precious first can do as they please. Though in that case, some kind of decisive evidence would be necessary."

Knowing Rei, if we don't make this perfectly clear now, he might just head straight for Precious without a second thought.

Marina thought this, and naturally, Rei made a displeased face... but he didn't voice any objection and nodded.

If he tried to attack Precious without any evidence, he'd be the one ending up chased by the guards.

Of course, if something happened, Rei wouldn't mind leaving Gilm.

But even so, Gilm was undeniably a comfortable place for him. Unless something forced him out, he wanted to avoid leaving if possible.

Tris watched Rei like this and breathed a sigh of relief.

In terms of pure strength, Tris simply couldn't hold a candle to Rei.

After all, Rei was said to be a force equivalent to an entire army on his own, so that was only natural.

But when gathering evidence became the top priority, he reasoned that his expertise as a merchant well-connected with information would give him an advantage.

"Any objections, either of you?"

"None."

"That's right, none."

Rei reluctantly... and Tris, smiling, responded to Marina's words.

Seeing this, Marina nodded again.

"Then, shall we start by gathering evidence to corner Precious? ...Ah, and just to be safe. There's a chance... just a very slim chance, but the culprit this time might not be Precious, so let's not jump to conclusions."

Marina said this because Precious had uttered various meaningful-sounding remarks when they encountered her in the hallway.

Marina could guess Precious had said those things to confuse and unsettle them, but even so, there was that one in a million chance.

Marina, with her experience as a guildmaster, had decided she couldn't fully trust Tris either.

Moreover, she had heard clearly about the various forceful—yet technically legal—actions the Spier Trading Company had taken over the winter.

"Oh? Are you implying my information isn't credible?"

"I wouldn't go that far. But there really is that one in a million chance, right? Just to be safe."

In truth, her words stemmed from her guildmaster experience—judging everything based solely on information from Tris was dangerous.

Perhaps Tris was trying to use Rei's power to frame Precious.

That possibility existed too.

"...Understood. Then, so be it."

Tris must have understood what Marina was thinking.

Though reluctantly, he accepted Marina's words without refusal.

"Good, I'm glad you understand. Well then, shall we end the discussion here? Or is there anything else you'd like to discuss?"

"Ideally, I'd like to discuss various business matters with you, Rei, but given the current situation, that's not possible."

As the person in charge of the Spier Trading Company branch, there were indeed countless things he wanted to trade with Rei.

Renowned as a skilled adventurer, Rei could gather plants difficult for ordinary adventurers to collect... or defeat powerful high-rank monsters to obtain materials and magic stones.

Furthermore, his ability to travel by riding a Set and possessing a Misty Ring allowed him to transport goods at speeds incomparable to moving large quantities by carriage.

Under normal circumstances, I'd absolutely want to discuss business with him... but in the current situation, that was simply impossible.

Tris muttered with genuine regret, and Rei nodded back.

"Yeah. It's a shame, but until this matter is resolved, I won't be lending a hand to the Spier Trading Company. Besides... no, I'd better stop there."

Rei stopped mid-sentence, but Tris understood what he was about to say.

It was about the trading house Rei had been personally close to, the one that had suffered significant damage at the hands of Spier Trading Company.

It had been a necessary move to gain a foothold in Gilm and secure a share of the market, but now it had come back to bite them.

(Should we have taken a different approach? But we didn't know about your connection to Rei... It's too late now.)

Their relationship with that merchant house wasn't based on any important contracts.

It was research into how to make goblin meat palatable—a pursuit that, to outsiders, seemed nothing short of reckless.

Precisely because of that, they hadn't realized Rei was connected to such a hobby... and as a result, it had become one factor in their current complicated situation.

Feeling a little regret... still, Tris racked her brains, determined to somehow turn the current situation around.
