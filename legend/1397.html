The adventurers naturally spotted Rei and his companions descending to the forest's edge.

At first, it was only natural for them to think these might be new enemies and raise their weapons.

After all, earthquakes and sudden halts in monster movements had occurred one after another. It would have been unreasonable for adventurers not to be on guard.

However, once they realized it was Rei riding Set and Vihera clinging to Set's front legs, they relaxed and resumed their attack on the monsters.

Though some, sensing something odd about the monsters' behavior, kept their distance.

Regardless, Rei, having descended to the ground, headed straight for Marina.

"Rei, you're safe. And so are Set and Vihera."

With the monsters frozen in place, Marina no longer needed to issue commands.

Yet, as if anticipating something else, Marina flashed a joyful expression toward Rei and Vihera. The very next moment, however, her gaze turned slightly suspicious as she spoke.

"Hey, Rei, Vihera. Just asking... but it's safe to assume this monster business is your doing, right?"

"Yeah. It's probably safe to say we're the cause."

"Wait, why am I included? In this case, Rei is the only one responsible, right?"

Vihera, who had been listening to Rei and Marina, declared with a look of dissatisfaction.

But that dissatisfaction was understandable.

After all, Rei was the only one who had actually fought the tree root puppet that seemed to have caused this incident.

Feeling resentful about that, Vihera turned to Marina and spoke up.

"The one who caused this mess is that thing, like the incarnation of Treant Forest. But only Rei actually interacted with it. Set and I were just fighting the Treant near the incarnation, so we're not involved."

"...Well, we'll hear all about that later. The embodiment of Treant Forest? Is that the one behind this whole thing?"

Marina asked, brushing off Vihera's words, while Rei hesitated over how to respond.

Meanwhile, naturally, the researchers standing a short distance away strained their ears, determined not to miss a single word Rei uttered.

This Treant Forest was a special place in many ways.

Precisely because of that, Rei's testimony about encountering the embodiment of Treant Forest was something they couldn't afford to miss.

The reason they didn't press Rei further was likely because they understood that upsetting her would be unwise in more ways than one.

The alchemists among the researchers, in particular, had experienced firsthand how a merchant's persistent harassment of Rei had caused him to temporarily leave Gilm, making the acquisition of griffon materials extremely difficult.

As for the others, they were either residents of Gilm... or individuals with deep connections to it.

Naturally, they knew what kind of personality Rei possessed, so they didn't press him themselves. For now, they were simply prepared to listen to what he had to say.

"A manifestation... that might be the right word. Ah, yes. I was able to communicate with the manifestation in Treant Forest. ...Was I? I wonder. Communicate? More accurately, I think it was them forcibly flooding me with their will through images."

"...Huh? What exactly do you mean?"

"Well, first, within this Treant Forest resides an entity that could be called the very will of the forest itself. And at the very heart of this Treant Forest, there was a terminal for that will... or, to put it in Vihera terms, an avatar—a doll made from the roots of the forest."

At the words "doll made of tree roots," Marina's expression shifted to one of understanding.

If it was the will of an entity like Treant Forest, then a doll made of plants wasn't so strange after all.

Marina urged him to continue, and Rei opened his mouth, recalling the events of that time.

"Considering it was seated on a throne also made of tree roots, it must have been trying to make contact with us somehow."

At those words, the researchers' eyes lit up with curiosity, and they fixed an intense gaze on Rei—so powerful it felt almost like a physical pressure.

"So that's how the image was implanted?"

"Yeah. For convenience, let's call it an image language. What came through in that image language was... above all else, expanding itself... expanding this Treant Forest... no, that was all it could think about."

"...I see."

So that was why the Treant Forest was expanding by ten meters almost every day.

Marina's expression showed understanding at Rei's words.

The researchers seemed eager to ask more questions, but they fell silent, deciding it best to listen to Rei's explanation first and then ask questions together.

"So, I told it that if it was going to spread, it should head toward where there were no people, like Gilm or Abuero. Well, actually, I didn't exactly say it... maybe it's more accurate to say I strongly wished it and conveyed it through the image language? After all, one reason we ended up here in the Treant Forest is because it was spreading toward where Gilm is."

"Wait a minute!"

Among those listening to Rei's explanation was, naturally, Slesha.

And beside Slesha stood Runo.

(Ah, they made it safely.)

Seeing the two familiar faces unharmed, Rei felt a slight sense of relief.

Runo aside, Slesha didn't have the rank or skill required to take this job normally.

He had only managed to join this mission by forming a party with Runo.

That's precisely why, if things had gone badly, Slesha could easily have been the first to die...

(It was only because Runo covered for her, I suppose.)

Shifting his gaze from Slesha to Runo, Runo met his eyes and gave a small nod.

"Rei! Are you listening?! Why would you even suggest leaving Treant Forest as it is?!"

As Rei and Runo exchanged glances, Slesha, seemingly unable to tolerate the situation any longer, shouted.

But Rei's response to Slesha was just a sigh.

"Look, listen. I get it. I think it's unfortunate and sad that your party member died in this forest. But you guys... you became adventurers knowing you might die, right? Don't you think dying here in Treant Forest is just as likely as getting killed by monsters or bandits while on a quest?"

"That's—"

Slesha, who had been furious at Rei's words just seconds ago, found herself unable to say anything more.

It was common knowledge that adventuring was a dangerous profession.

It was impossible to think only they wouldn't face danger.

Slesha probably understood that intellectually, but she hadn't truly grasped it until now.

"Rei, that's enough about Slesha for now. Let's talk about the incarnation of Treant Forest. ...Why did it spread toward Gilm?"

Rei, who had been talking with Slesha, nodded at Marina's words and began explaining again.

"The Treant Forest was moving toward Gilm because it understood there were many beings with strong magical power in Gilm... meaning, many potential sources of sustenance for itself."

"...I see. For Treant Forest, which prioritizes expanding itself above all else, the more targets with magical power suitable as sustenance, the better, right?"

Marina's face showed a hint of surprise, but nothing else.

She was, after all, dealing with the Treant Forest, an entity that defied common sense in every way.

Given that, even being told it sought out beings with high magical power as sustenance wasn't something that would unsettle Marina.

Besides, even if they did seek Gilm's inhabitants as prey, whether they themselves would tolerate it was an entirely different matter.

"So, what did Rei do when confronted by that guy demanding Gilm's inhabitants as food? He didn't actually agree to it, did he?"

"Yeah. I tried to stop them using the image language... but apparently, that touched a nerve over there, and it turned into a fight."

Hearing Rei's words, no one grew angry.

And rightly so. Given the situation Rei described, if it hadn't come to a fight, it would have meant sacrificing Gilm's inhabitants... specifically adventurers with high magical power... to the Treant Forest.

Considering that, it was only natural for those listening to Rei's account to feel he had done the right thing.

The researchers had some reservations about Rei's explanation, but even so, they wouldn't consider sending Gilm's inhabitants as sacrifices.

Some did wonder if criminals might be acceptable sacrifices, though.

Relieved by that, Rei continued describing the battle.

"The incarnations of Trent Forest weren't just one, of course. They were weaker than the one I encountered, but several incarnations... dolls made of tree roots appeared. And then, the Treant that was in the open area, much stronger than the ones here, also attacked. ...I left that one to Vihera and Set."

"A Treant stronger than the ones here?"

"Yeah. ...Well, it'd be easier to explain if I said it like regular soldiers versus elite guards, right?"

At Rei's words, Marina and the others listening nodded in understanding.

...Though a few researchers did wear slightly troubled expressions.

While major powers like the Mireana Kingdom and the Bestia Empire were one thing, some of the smaller nations surrounding them, protected by the larger powers and thus unlikely to see their royal families attacked, had taken in third or fourth sons of nobles as members of their royal guard.

Naturally, such individuals had no intention of seriously training themselves, becoming nothing more than nominal guards... They had probably heard about that.

Anyway, that wasn't relevant now, so the researchers didn't bring it up either.

"That Treant looks quite strong."

"Yes, it was considerably stronger than an ordinary Treant, wasn't it? Strong enough to make you wonder if it was a higher-tier or rare species."

Vihera replied to Marina's words, but Rei, having witnessed the scene where the Treant's insides were blown apart by the Demon-Infused Palm enhanced through fusion with Ambris, couldn't bring himself to nod in agreement.

If anything, Vihera seemed to have defeated the Treant with surprising ease.

"Anyway, when we ended up fighting that kind of monster, even using Death Scythe was useless—it's just tree roots, so even if you cut them, they just grow back right away. ...So, since the area around us was open, I figured there was no risk of the fire spreading, and I used magic to burn it down..."

Having said that much, Rei averted his gaze, looking slightly uncomfortable.

It was undeniable that the magic he had used was the cause of what had happened.

"...So? What happened?"

Marina urged Rei on, and Rei resignedly brought his gaze back.

"I burned the incarnation of Treant Forest, the Tree Root Doll, to ashes with my magic. But whether it was a tremendous shock to them, or sheer terror... I don't know the reason... but anyway, an earthquake happened."

"We sensed it here too. Right around the time that earthquake hit, the monsters suddenly stopped moving."

Marina stated this while observing the adventurers defeating the motionless monsters and gathering materials and proof of defeat parts.

"I figured. I only learned about it myself after arriving here. ...Probably every monster in Treant Forest is immobilized now. Anyway, that earthquake happened, and the plaza we were on was swallowed whole by a fissure. So, we evacuated like this, riding on Set."

"Well, that's quite... No, never mind."

In the first place, it wasn't a battle against monsters, but against the very embodiment of Treant Forest itself.

Rather, it was precisely because it was Rei that we were able to return alive.

If this had been an ordinary adventurer, they would have been swallowed by that very fissure that Rei mentioned and died for sure.

"Then..."

Just as Marina was about to say something else... another massive tremor engulfed the area.

This tremor was incomparably stronger than the one Rei had felt at the very heart of Treant Forest.

Rei still had enough presence of mind to wonder, "What magnitude could this be?" but for the others, this was an entirely new experience.

Those who had been attacking the motionless monsters now had no such luxury. They crouched or sat on the ground, desperately trying not to fall, simply enduring the shaking.
