After placing the monster captured by the guild into the warehouse, I exchanged brief words with Lenora and Kenny as I left the guild, glancing at guild staff, unemployed members, and even some ordinary residents dismantling the monster.

He hadn't felt anything in particular when storing the monster carcass, but even so, Rei found it extremely convenient that such people existed.

(The Giganto Turtle... wait, come to think of it, they said adventurers would handle the dissection. Besides, the Giganto Turtle won't fit inside the warehouse, so it has to be dissected outside. That means it'll definitely attract attention. In other words, it's good publicity for the guild too, huh?)

Of course, just being Gilm's guild gave it considerable name value, no doubt about it.

But in that situation, did doing something to draw even more attention really make sense...? At least, Rei couldn't figure it out.

(Well, I guess I don't need to worry about that. The guild's probably thinking about it too. For now, I just need to call Set and head to Treant Forest... Huh?)

Let's go.

That's what Rei was thinking when he suddenly thought he heard a familiar voice and turned his gaze in that direction.

"So listen up. Rei is definitely not your average guy. Just getting involved with him will bring you nothing but misfortune!"

"..."

Rei hadn't expected to overhear such blatant gossip about himself, but the one who spoke just now... Melan hadn't shouted it out to make sure Rei heard.

No, he probably hadn't even noticed Rei was here at all.

Normally, he would have avoided any involvement at all costs, but if the person Melan was talking to was Ilse, that changed things.

Yesterday, Rei had been asked to help with revenge, and while her response was still pending, she was still Rei's client... or rather, Rei's intended client.

If that person was being fed lies about him behind his back, he couldn't just ignore it.

Normally, he'd ignore it because Rei knew he was noticed in various ways and envied.

Becoming a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname in his mid-teens, and even more so, having the griffin Set as his familiar—it was inevitable he would be envied.

But Rei showed no mercy to those who opposed him.

Given that, he couldn't afford to confront him directly. And if he tried any kind of scheme, that person was far too dangerous to risk his own life as a gamble.

It wasn't that Rei was particularly clever at seeing through others' schemes.

But... his raw combat power was simply too overwhelming.

No matter how many traps you set, he'd just casually smash through them with sheer force. He was nothing short of a nuisance.

The last form of resistance such people could muster... was speaking ill of Rei behind his back.

While Rei possessed immense combat prowess, he had numerous flaws in other areas.

Those flaws were perfect ammunition... no, verbal ammunition for those who gossiped.

Of course, this was strictly something said among themselves, not something they'd say within earshot of Rei like this.

Regardless, Rei approached Melan, who was speaking ill of him audibly enough for others to hear, even if unintentionally.

Since she'd been speaking loudly enough for others to hear, several people nearby turned their gaze toward Rei.

That's precisely why those people could see Rei approaching Melan and Ilse.

Their eyes widened, and they instinctively moved away from Melan's location to avoid getting caught up in the commotion.

Ilse, who had been listening to Melan, was the first to notice the change in the atmosphere.

She noticed the people around them had somehow vanished and glanced around while listening to Melan.

What caught her eye was Rei approaching from behind Melan.

Recalling what Melan had said, Ilse sensed the impending commotion and wore a gloomy expression.

Melan hadn't noticed the changes in the surroundings, but she must have noticed the expression on Ilse's face right in front of her.

Wearing a puzzled expression, she opened her mouth.

"Hm? What's..."

"Frankly, I think it's a bit much to openly gossip about someone in a way that everyone around can hear."

Melan's movements froze for an instant at the voice coming from behind.

Of course. Melan understood all too well who that voice belonged to.

Melan himself had challenged Rei to a duel and been easily defeated.

There was no way Melan could mistake the voice of his romantic rival for Vihera – a high-ranking adventurer who stood before him like an insurmountable wall.

And hearing the words about backbiting only reinforced that certainty.

"Rei, why are you here..."

Melan turned at the voice behind him. The figure filling his vision was unmistakably Rei.

"I happened to pass by and overheard you... talking behind my back? Or was it badmouthing? Anyway, that sort of talk. I won't tell you not to gossip about people, but you should probably keep it quieter."

It wasn't meant to mock the other person; it was pure advice.

Given how often Rei drew envy from others, it wasn't surprising he'd be the subject of gossip.

Especially since Melan had a reason to oppose Rei: Vihera.

But... those words, spoken without any particular ulterior motive, sounded to Melan like nothing but contempt.

Still, she didn't explode with frustration here, probably because she felt guilty about having spoken ill of Rei within earshot.

Melan hadn't intended for her gossip to be overheard by others.

But as she talked with Ilse, she gradually became more excited, and without even realizing it herself, her voice grew louder and louder.

Because of these circumstances, Melan felt a complex mix of emotions—resentment, shame, acceptance, jealousy—and wore a complicated expression.

"Sorry. I got carried away."

Still, the fact she could offer a sincere apology to Rei was likely due to Melan's pride.

Rei, who had fully expected a hostile attitude, was slightly surprised.

So she wasn't just impulsive after all...

"Well, never mind that. But arguing like this on a busy street is rather embarrassing."

"...About that..."

At Rei's words, Melan turned his gaze toward Ilse.

Ilse, receiving that look, opened her mouth without seeming to mind Melan's gaze.

"I'm sorry, Rei-san. Apparently, they found out I was with you yesterday..."

"Ah... I see."

To Melan, Rei was someone he couldn't trust at all personality-wise, regardless of his status as an adventurer.

Part of that was the preconception that he was a romantic rival for Vihera, but objectively speaking, it wasn't entirely wrong either.

So it was only natural, in a way, that Melan—even though she'd only just met Ilse—would tell her she should avoid getting involved with Rei out of concern for her.

"...Rei. Why are you being friendly with Ilse?"

"I'm not exactly getting close to her, though."

There was no lie in Rei's words.

In truth, Rei had spoken with Ilse because she'd asked for his help with her revenge. It wasn't because he wanted to get close to her... or, to be specific, because he wanted to pursue her.

(But judging by Ilse's demeanor, she doesn't seem to be talking about revenge at all. ...Come to think of it, that makes sense.)

Even to Rei's eyes, Melan seemed to believe that everything right was absolutely right.

Of course, being an adventurer of a certain rank, it's hard to imagine she hasn't faced unreasonable treatment before.

In fact, challenging Rei to a duel unilaterally and then losing unilaterally could certainly be considered an unreasonable situation.

From Rei's perspective, being challenged to a duel unilaterally was unreasonable enough.

"From any angle, you're clearly close with Ilse, right?"

"Yeah... maybe because of a little trigger?"

"A little trigger?"

Melan looked utterly confused, but Rei, caught off guard, didn't know how to explain it properly and just blurted out something vague.

Ilse seemed to grasp this. Quick-witted, she spoke up.

"Well, Rei saved me when I was being harassed by some adventurers. So, I just treated him to a meal as a thank you."

Anyone who knew Rei wouldn't be surprised to think, 'That's suicide...' upon hearing Ilse's words.

Indeed, among the onlookers who kept their distance from Rei and his group while still watching to see what would happen, several cast Ilse pitying glances.

But Melan, who had gathered some information about Rei but didn't know about his bottomless appetite, found Ilse's explanation perfectly plausible.

"Oh, is that it? Well, you should've just said so."

"...I doubt I would have believed you anyway."

Rei's words were muttered softly, but Melan must have heard them. For a moment, a look of displeasure flashed across her face.

But she quickly regained her composure.

"What's the deal with Ilse and Melan? Are they actually lovers or something?"

"Huh?"

Ilse and Melan both voiced their surprise at Rei's question.

But while their voices were the same, the emotions behind them were not.

Melan was half-confused, while Ilse felt something along the lines of, "Why me?"

Of course, Ilse didn't dislike Melan.

She could see he was somewhat self-righteous, but she understood he was genuinely concerned for her.

Even so, to Ilse, Melan was still someone she had only just met. She simply couldn't see her in that light.

"Judging by how they act, it seems they're not exactly lovers."

"Of course not."

Ilse declared without a moment's hesitation at Rei's words.

Seeing Ilse like this, Melan felt a slight shock.

Of course, they had only just met, and Melan already had a lover named Vihera.

Still, being asked outright if they were dating and having it denied without hesitation wasn't exactly pleasant.

(Huh?)

Rei didn't understand why Melan was shocked, but since she'd been declared not his lover... he opened his mouth.

"If Ilse were Melan's lover, then maybe she'd have the right to interfere in that relationship. But if she's not, then I don't think it matters who Ilse is with or what kind of relationship she has, right?"

"Well... she's my friend, so it's only natural to worry, right?"

"That might be true, but at the end of the day, they're just friends, right? I don't think the relationship is that strong. At the very least, complaining just because they had a meal together seems like overstepping, even for friends, don't you think?"

"Well..."

In truth, Melan probably realized she'd feel annoyed if someone who wasn't her lover said something like that to her.

Rei's words left him with no further rebuttal.

"I get that Melan has a lot to say, but you've only just met, right? You should think about that before acting."

"Guh..."

Unable to retort, Melan was at a loss for words.

Still, as if she had something on her mind, Melan shot Rei a look dripping with dissatisfaction.

Despite that gaze, Rei continued speaking without seeming particularly bothered.

"Anyway, what about your work today, Melan? Vihera's already headed out for work."

"...I'm off today."

Melan stated this with obvious displeasure, but it was neither a lie nor anything else—it was the truth.

While she was patrolling as part of the guard duty, the workload was intense, to say the least.

With so many adventurers gathered, disturbances were inevitable.

Resolving these issues meant the patrolmen's physical exertion was naturally intense.

Therefore, to prevent them from getting injured carelessly, if an adventurer seemed tired, they were given time to rest.

Today was Melan's scheduled day off.

She insisted she was still fine, but the guild decided it was too much for her, so she was spending this unwanted day off.

While Vihera was moving around without any issues... she felt useless about herself.
