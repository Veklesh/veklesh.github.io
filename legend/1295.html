Rei and the others safely made their way out of the forest and returned to Gilm in a carriage driven by the waiting adventurers.

After completing the necessary procedures with the guards, they returned to the guild. Marina smiled at the adventurers who had successfully completed the request and spoke.

"Thank you for your hard work. You saved us today. I'll ask for your help again if the opportunity arises."

"N-no, thank you. Gyrudomashuta..."

The adventurer stammered, caught by Marina's smile—one radiating an intensely feminine allure.

Well, seeing that smile just now, it couldn't be helped. Another adventurer spoke up.

"We're truly glad the Guildmaster is satisfied. We're also very pleased to have completed the request safely."

Neither this man nor the one who stammered earlier were exactly inexperienced with women.

As adventurers, they'd naturally frequented brothels before. ...Though they couldn't afford it now.

They'd also had their fair share of experiences seducing women and forming such relationships.

But... even so, when a woman like Marina, who embodied sensuality itself, smiled at them, it wasn't surprising if they acted like they weren't used to women.

Marina was naturally aware of this, but she didn't let it show in her demeanor. She signed the documents handed to her by the man, completing the request procedure.

"Alright, that's all right. We'll take care of the carriage over here. Go collect your payment from the guild."

"Wh-what!?"

The man who had stumbled over his words at first shouted this, and Marina watched him enter the guild hall, receiving exasperated looks from his companions. For some reason, she returned the gaze of Vihera, who was smiling at her.

"Is there something you want to say?"

"Nothing in particular. Just... seeing this, I realize Marina's quite popular, huh?"

"If you're going to say that, I think Vihera isn't much different from me, you know? ...Just look around at the situation."

Exchanging words like this in front of the guild naturally drew attention.

Especially since both Marina and Vihera possessed looks worthy of being called stunning beauties.

While Marina isn't widely known outside adventurer circles, Vihera's name is fairly well-known as an acquaintance of Rei, thanks to her personality and looks.

No one would be foolish enough to mess with someone like that... at least not in the current Gilm, which was practically closed off.

Especially since Set was near the carriage, and Rei had stepped out of it.

Even so, a beauty like Vihera or Marina was enough to satisfy just by looking.

There's a saying about a feast for the eyes, and this was exactly that.

Especially Vihera, who, even in this season when most women wear warm coats, was clad only in a sheer garment so thin you could see right through it.

Her body line was a feast for the eyes, worthy of the term for men... and women alike.

Of course, some might prefer Byune (the female), and there were even exceptions who thought Rei was better.

"Ah, it's you, Set! How have you been? Were you out?"

A girl around ten years old spotted Set and happily ran towards him.

Her actions instantly dispelled the strange atmosphere that had been lingering around them.

"Grrrrr."

Whether Set sensed the surrounding mood or simply didn't understand anything at all... either way, he happily rubbed his face against the girl who was stroking him while purring.

Giggling, the girl delighted in it. Perhaps envious of the scene, several other children appeared from the surrounding area.

"Hey, it's not fair, Rei gets to play with Set all alone! I wanna play with Set too!"

"Kyaaah! Kyaaah! Ahahahaha!"

"Set-chan, it's been a while. Have you been well?"

And so, several children swarmed around Set...

"Hm?"

Suddenly, Rei noticed an adult mixed in among the children and turned his gaze back toward Set.

For some reason, among the voices he heard, there was one he recognized.

And Rei's senses were never wrong... The person standing there was exactly who he expected.

"Milein... Where did you spring from?"

"Hey, isn't 'popped out of nowhere' a bit harsh? Especially to a beauty like me."

That statement wasn't wrong at all.

Objectively speaking, Milein undoubtedly possessed the looks to be called beautiful.

Of course, she couldn't compare to the ultimate beauties like Marina or Vihera.

Understanding that, Rei opened his mouth in exasperation.

"Yeah. If she acted normally, you could probably call her beautiful."

Rei had no objection to Milein being beautiful.

But that's only when she's acting normal.

Seeing her all lovey-dovey with Set like this, if asked whether he could still think of Milein as beautiful... it would be hard to nod honestly.

(Though, there are certainly those with peculiar tastes... Guys who think, "Even this version of Milein is fine!" probably exist. Including Johanna.)

Recalling another famous representative of Set enthusiasts, Rei nodded back at Milein with a strangely understanding look.

Despite being acknowledged as beautiful, Milein wore a clearly dissatisfied expression at Rei's reaction.

She understood exactly how Rei viewed her. That was probably why.

"Rei, playing around is fine, but we should probably do something about this soon too."

"Yeah, you're right. We should combine the celebration with a debriefing..."

"Ah, Mr. Rei! Long time no see!"

"...Hm?"

Let's grab a bite somewhere.

The voice cut off what Rei was about to say.

It was a voice he hadn't heard in a while, but one he recognized.

Turning toward the source of the voice, Rei saw a figure he recognized... though compared to the last time he'd seen them, they had grown slightly taller and their physique had become more warrior-like.

"Hasta... right?"

It was Husta, the one who had accompanied Rei on his first Gamelion hunt.

Their connection began when Hasta, wanting to help out at the Manpukutei restaurant where his father worked as a cook, had come to ask Rei for help.

Though Rei visited the Full Belly Inn fairly often, it had been quite a while since he'd seen Hasta.

"Huh? Yeah. It's me, Hasta. What's up? Did you forget my face or something?"

"No, it's just that you seem to have gotten much bigger since I last saw you, so I didn't recognize you at first."

"Ah, ahaha. ...Come to think of it, I might have gotten a little bigger compared to before."

Noticing the subtle hint of jealousy in Rei's words about his size, a vague smile of evasion flitted across Hasta's lips.

Seeing Hasta like that, she felt a little envious... but still, she reminded herself that she was herself and others were others, and opened her mouth.

"Anyway, it's been ages. Is the shop okay today?"

It was winter, so most adventurers were spending their time eating and drinking.

For those living that kind of life, the Manpukutei—delicious, cheap, and generous portions—was the most wonderful shop imaginable.

In fact, when Rei had gone to the Manpukutei for udon before, he'd heard that winter was the most profitable season of the year.

Because of that, Hasta needed to gather as much meat as possible before winter—including Gamelion meat—without letting it spoil and go to waste.

Fortunately, the cold season falls between autumn and winter.

If they had to do this in summer, it would definitely rot.

Though, in summer, they could probably just hunt monsters daily and bring back meat as usual.

"Yes. We've hired a few temporary workers, and since things have been pretty busy lately, I took today off."

Hasta informed Rei with a smile, showing no signs of fatigue.

Having been an adventurer himself, he had plenty of stamina, and helping out with the shop work probably didn't tire him much.

(Though being an adventurer and a waiter are probably quite different in many ways.)

Rei had also done part-time jobs a few times back in Japan.

Things like working at a convenience store, or short-term gigs like grilling yakitori at night markets.

What wore him out during those times wasn't just the physical exertion, but also the significant mental fatigue.

Even if told to do waiter-type work, Rei didn't feel he could handle it.

Then, while they were talking... suddenly, Rei realized.

"Come to think of it, Hasta had a warehouse for monster dissection, right?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. We do have one?"

When Rei had gone Gamelion hunting with Hasta before, they'd used that warehouse to butcher the Gamelion. And...

"And... didn't you say I could use that warehouse too?"

"Yeah. It hasn't been used much since then, though."

"Even so. That warehouse is usually used by Hasta, so I can't really get in the way, right?"

It was a privately owned butchering warehouse, but it was fundamentally a place intended for Husta's exclusive use.

And Husta hunts the meat used at the Manpukutei, butchering it there before delivering it to the shop.

If Rei were to start butchering monsters in a place like that, it would be nothing but a disruption to Hasta.

Besides, the place Rei usually does his monster butchering is near the river in the forest he went to today.

There, he has access to abundant water whenever he needs it, making it a far more suitable environment for butchering than Husta's warehouse.

...Of course, butchering in the forest... if he spilled blood, animals or monsters drawn by the scent might attack.

The greatest advantage of using the town's dissection warehouse is that such worries are unnecessary.

But Rei has his partner, Set.

Ordinary monsters and animals rarely approach Set unless provoked.

For that reason, and because he often requested the guild to handle the butchering directly after defeating large numbers of monsters, he rarely needed to use Husta's warehouse.

But... things are different now.

Fortunately, Rei's current Misty Ring contained a Snow Salamander.

He hadn't processed it in the forest because time was precious, but now he had nothing else to do.

If anything, maybe a celebration.

That celebration could be held at the Dusk Wheat inn, or if we borrow a warehouse from Hasta, we could do it at the Full Belly Inn.

Having decided that, I turned my gaze back to Husta and spoke up.

"Hey, Hasta. Your warehouse isn't being used right now, is it?"

"Huh? Oh, yes. We're just storing meat for this winter, nothing else."

"...Storing?"

Unable to grasp what words had just come out of Hasta's mouth, Rei asked him again.

But Husta nodded back as if it were perfectly natural.

"Yes, that's right. Is something wrong?"

"No, not weird... but how can you just leave meat in the warehouse? Security there isn't exactly tight, is it?"

As far as Rei knew, the warehouse door was locked, but even so, it seemed like a place where breaking the lock wouldn't be too difficult if someone really wanted to.

Leaving processed meat in a place like that would make the warehouse seem like a prime feeding ground for those desperate enough to eat.

But to Rei's doubts, Husta spoke up with a confident expression, saying it was fine.

"The people living near that warehouse are regulars at the Full Belly Inn."

That should be enough to make you understand, right?

Hasta's attitude seemed to say just that, and while not entirely, Rei found himself convinced.

If a man who adores the taste of Manpukutei lives near the warehouse, the difficulty of breaking into it would be extremely high.

After all, if the meat were stolen, he wouldn't be able to eat the food from his beloved restaurant... Worse still, in the worst-case scenario, Manpukutei itself might go out of business.

Thinking about it that way, it was like having security personnel living right next to the warehouse.

Some might wonder, "Would someone risk their life just for a meal?" But considering Manpukutei's delicious food, low prices, and generous portions, it didn't seem so far-fetched.

"I see. Well, that might put my mind at ease. Anyway... I hunted some monsters outside Gilm today. Could I borrow your warehouse to butcher them?"

That's what he asked.
