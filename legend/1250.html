"...I see. It failed."

The man responded with those words alone, showing no particular surprise upon hearing the report.

"Um, Master. Even though we failed, is it really okay not to take the next step?"

"It matters not. Discovering his return early was a stroke of luck in itself. Therefore, this matter is settled. ...He must thoroughly regret refusing my request."

Muttering this, the man—Viscount Linus Marnino—set his wine glass down on the table.

Despite issuing a named request, having it refused had deeply wounded Linus's pride.

Particularly galling was that the target wasn't some nameless, ordinary adventurer, but Rei, a man with a notorious nickname.

It rankled him to think that other nobles loyal to the king would likely see him as the noble whose request had been rejected by Rei whenever they looked at him.

What's more, the very request he should have made had failed because Rei had refused it.

Considering all this, it was only natural that Linus now felt deep-seated irritation toward Rei. ...No, it was inevitable.

At least within Linus's own mind, his irritation toward Rei was justified.

"Then, next... what move should we make?"

"If we act rashly against Rei, we'll definitely suffer major damage. Given that, we need to ensure Rei doesn't know it was our doing."

Though unspoken, Marina, Vihera, and above all, Elena were naturally included in that "us."

Linus was a noble aligned with the king, but he wasn't a major figure.

If word got out that he'd laid a hand on the daughter of Duke Kerebel, leader of the Noble Faction, it would spell trouble on multiple fronts.

Of course, it wasn't just Duke Kerebel. Elena herself was a formidable figure, known by the nickname "Princess General," and he couldn't afford to make an enemy of her.

That's precisely why he orchestrated the carriage attack, taking every precaution to ensure no one discovered he was behind it.

Moreover, the attackers weren't adventurers, but mercenaries who knew little of the world... or rather, mercenaries who had fallen from grace.

Above all, the request had been passed through multiple intermediaries, making it impossible for the trail to reach Linus.

(But since he could only use such men, it ended up being nothing more than a petty nuisance.)

He brought the wine to his lips once more, savoring its taste.

It should have been a reasonably fine wine, yet strangely, it didn't taste particularly good now.

In fact, it even tasted somewhat unpleasant.

...This change in taste was due to Linus's anxiety, though he himself seemed unaware of it.

As for why he had come to Gilm in the first place, it had ended in failure.

For Linus, it was only natural that Rei became the target for his frustration.

But... now, deep down, he truly realized he had antagonized Rei.

Precisely because he was a noble of the Mireana Kingdom, and specifically a noble of the largest faction, the Royalist faction, Linus naturally had access to various pieces of information about Rei.

Among that information was the fact that he showed no mercy to those who opposed him.

Even as a noble, he would unleash violence without mercy on those who opposed him. ...And he wouldn't hesitate to carry out acts like severing arms or legs. That was truly terrifying to Linus.

For those raised in Elgin, the fundamental perception is that nobles are beings to be respected, beings of special status.

Of course, some didn't seem to care about that, but even those individuals acted that way while being aware of what might be called their own common sense.

But Rei is different.

As if nobility had nothing to do with him whatsoever, he extends his hand—or more accurately, his Death Scythe—without the slightest hesitation.

Naturally, this only happens when the noble is at fault, but for a typical noble like Linus, the fundamental belief is that the noble is always in the right.

"My lord?"

"...Ah, my apologies. I was contemplating what course of action to take next."

He murmured as if suppressing the fear within himself, then brought the wine to his lips once more.

"What move to make, you say? ...Well, what to do..."

To be honest, Linus felt almost no resentment toward Rei anymore.

Today's incident had provided some measure of catharsis, and now, fear toward Rei was welling up inside him.

If it became known that he was the mastermind behind this affair, Ray would undoubtedly come charging straight for this mansion.

Worse still, he had far too many skeletons in his closet.

Even if it hadn't come to light yet, if he got into a fight with Rei and the Gilm security squad started investigating, it was obvious they'd probe into his past... not painlessly, but definitely painfully.

(If only this were within the sphere of influence of the royalist faction, starting with the capital...)

He muttered quietly to himself while glancing at the wine.

But saying that now was pointless.

He'd already made his move.

"My lord, if you'd like, perhaps I could handle this?"

"...What do you intend to do?"

"Yes. Fortunately, we have the necessary connections. Using those..."

"Don't bother."

Linus cut his subordinate off before he could finish.

"Do you not trust me?"

The man asked, but there was no hint of reproach toward his master in his eyes.

Only a gaze of pure concern for his master.

But... precisely because of that, Linus had no choice but to reject the man's suggestion.

"No. But for now, I simply don't think it's necessary to push you that hard. ...Well, perhaps I was overthinking things myself. If we don't leave Gilm soon, it wouldn't be surprising if snow started falling any moment. If that happens, we'll be trapped in this remote area until spring."

"Then?"

"Hmm. Staying in a place like this might even start to affect me with its barbaric atmosphere. Perhaps that's why this whole incident happened in the first place."

"Understood. I'll arrange for escorts tomorrow."

"...Huh? Are my own soldiers not sufficient?"

"Yes. From what I've gathered, monsters unique to winter begin appearing around Gilm this time of year. They're said to be stronger than the monsters that show up from spring through autumn."

"So my household's soldiers alone won't suffice, huh?"

"Unfortunately."

Linus was confident in the strength of the soldiers he employed at his own house, but it was also true that he felt uneasy about sending them to face monsters appearing in the unknown frontier.

(In that case, they're just disposable adventurers anyway. It won't matter how many die.)

Linus had never been particularly fond of adventurers to begin with, but he hadn't considered them mere disposable pawns.

That he now felt even a slight aversion toward them was undoubtedly due to the incident with Rei.

...For the other adventurers who knew Rei's character, being lumped together with him was unacceptable in more ways than one.

His skill level was one thing, but his personality was another.

Linus had, of course, met other adventurers besides Rei.

But still, Rei's presence had left such a strong impression that adventurers and Rei became inextricably linked in his mind.

"Understood. Since time is short, I'll recruit escort adventurers now for the brief period until tomorrow's departure. However, due to the urgency, the quality will inevitably be inferior..."

"It doesn't matter. They're expendable anyway. As long as they serve as our shield until we leave the frontier, that's sufficient. ...If it's bandits rather than frontier monsters, our household soldiers should be able to handle them adequately, correct?"

"That should be the case. However, given this is the frontier, there's a possibility even among bandits there are strong ones..."

At those words, Linus's brow furrowed slightly.

He could tolerate his own men falling behind adventurers when facing monsters.

It was infuriating to Linus, but adventurers were, after all, specialists in dealing with monsters.

But to be told they were inferior to adventurers when dealing with thieves... dealing with people? That was no laughing matter.

Still, he didn't voice his dissatisfaction because he knew this frontier land was, in many ways, a place beyond common sense.

"I see. ...Anyway, we're leaving first thing tomorrow, so prepare..."

Don't forget.

Just as Linus was about to say that, he suddenly heard shouting from outside the room and frowned in displeasure.

"What on earth? For someone serving me to behave so rudely..."

Linus muttered his displeasure openly, but he assumed it would die down soon... The shouts coming from outside the room, however, showed no sign of stopping.

"Wait a moment! This is the residence of the Marnino Viscount family! Such rudeness is absolutely unforgivable..."

"I'll fetch my lord immediately, so please wait just a little longer!"

"Do you think such behavior is acceptable, even for the Knights?!"

Worse still, the voices seemed to be getting closer, heading straight toward Linus's room.

"What's happening? Knights?"

"Master, prepare to flee immediately."

The voice addressed Linus, who muttered in confusion at the sounds coming from outside.

At the words of this man, who could be called his closest confidant, Linus initially gave him a look that said, What are you talking about?

But...

"Hurry! If you're captured here now, your standing within the royalist faction will be ruined! But if you can escape this place now, the royalists' power can handle anything!"

Shouted in a stern voice, Linus finally snapped back to reality.

A truly capable man would have leaped out the window and fled immediately.

But while Linus wasn't incompetent, he certainly wasn't someone who could be called capable.

Hearing his subordinate's words, he shouted reflexively.

"You fool! How did you track me down!?"

It was a cry from the depths of his soul.

He understood why the Knights had come, but he couldn't fathom how they'd tracked him down in this particular incident.

"My lord!"

The man shouted, but Linus couldn't comprehend what was happening to him at all.

He had planned this whole affair with multiple people in between, ensuring they absolutely wouldn't reach him.

If they had waited a certain number of days before reaching him, he might have been able to accept it.

But Rei and the others had been attacked today.

So why, how... Such thoughts left him frozen... The next instant, the door to the room Linus was in swung open, revealing several men.

Linus recognized them instantly.

These were the knights of the Gilm Order.

"Damn it!"

Realizing they were in dire straits,

The man who had told Linus to flee from here drew a dagger, likely for protection, from his waist and lunged at the knights.

"Master, flee!"

Even the knights hadn't expected an attack at this late stage. Their next move was a split-second too slow against the man charging at them.

That split second was a complete opening, one that could be called fatal even for the knights.

But... before the dagger's deadly strike could reach the knight, something sliced through the air and pierced the man's right shoulder as he lunged at the knight.

"You guys got a bit too careless, didn't you?"

The voice belonged to a man whose physique could only be described as lean.

His hair, cut haphazardly, hid his face, giving him an eerie air.

The dagger he held was shorter and slimmer than the one wielded by the man who had attacked the knight to let Linus escape.

It had almost no hilt, typical of such blades, designed purely for throwing.

"Ah, ah. Saved. ...Seize him!"

The man who seemed to be the knights' commander thanked the dagger-thrower before issuing orders to his subordinates.

Though Linus had some training as a noble, he was no match for professional knights—especially those of the Gilm Knights from the frontier.

He was swiftly captured along with the men who had attacked with the daggers.

"You scoundrels! Do you think you'll get away with this?!"

Linus screamed in humiliation, but the knights paid him no mind as they seized him and dragged him out of the room.

After confirming this, the knights' commander bowed his head once more to the man who had thrown the dagger earlier.

"My apologies. You saved us. Thanks to you, we obtained this information, and then you went even further..."

"It's fine. The boss... no, Egg-san is hired by you lot too. He's practically one of us, right? As the Prairie Wolf handling the back end for Gilm, I just did what was expected."

With a faint, barely perceptible smile, the man declared.
