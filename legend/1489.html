"Damn, this is a pain."

In a certain spot within the slums, Ajax muttered with obvious displeasure.

Hearing Ajax's words, his two companions—Levjef and Hastun—nod silently in agreement.

All three were already equipped to leave Gilm at any moment.

The only slight difference was that Haston was carrying a large backpack made of leather.

The backpack was quite heavy, and he would have preferred to set it down on the ground.

But inside that backpack is several days' worth of food for dozens of people.

Packed as efficiently as possible to minimize waste, if left on the ground, the scent wafting from the bag would attract rats and insects.

Then again, it was summer.

Which means there are many insects flying about at night, and among them are many carnivorous ones.

For such insects and animals... and above all, for the residents of the slums, many of whom are constantly hungry, the food filling that backpack would be a mouthwatering prize.

Even if most of what's inside the backpack is preserved jerky and hardened bread, chosen for their shelf life and portability.

Ideally, they should have been able to secure a decent amount of food at the diner where Ajax and the others had stayed.

But they only managed to get halfway there, and in the end, most of their food consisted of things like dried meat and hardened bread.

Then again, considering the effort required to transport food, it wasn't entirely a loss.

Haston, his backpack strapped on, cast his gaze into the surrounding darkness.

With only moonlight and starlight as sources of illumination, it was impossible to see the surroundings clearly.

Of course, all of the Ajax had shady pasts, and since they often acted at night, they possessed some degree of night vision.

But it wasn't night vision that could see through all darkness; it was merely slightly better than that of ordinary people.

Ajax and the others knew many figures were hidden within this darkness. They knew it, but because those figures blended so well into the shadows, they couldn't make out what kind of people they were.

"Calm down, Ajax. Probably just Jestal's men."

Levjef said, using Jestal's name without honorifics.

If they were right in front of them, they'd probably use polite language too, not wanting to upset whoever it was.

But if they weren't... there was no need to treat Jestal with courtesy in this situation.

"I hope so."

"More importantly, is he really coming? Carrying this around forever is getting pretty tough, you know?"

Haston muttered discontentedly to the two talking like that.

"Well, even so. Levjef is the point of contact for advancing the deal with them. I can't exactly take that on myself, can I?"

"True enough."

Having Levegef, who was in direct contact with the other side, carry the food was like giving them an opening to exploit during conversations with Gestal.

And while Ajax was seen as the leader among the three, more than that, he was the most skilled fighter.

Of course, he wasn't strong enough to handle high-ranking adventurers or those with nicknames, but he could easily handle Jestal's subordinates.

Given those circumstances, it ultimately fell to Haston to carry the supplies.

"At least you don't have to carry water. That's easier, right?"

"...Of course. Carrying water would be no laughing matter."

Hastun answered Levjeff, who had interrupted their conversation, looking displeased.

As he said, just the food alone was this much of a burden. If he'd had to prepare water for dozens of people on his own, he definitely wouldn't have been able to move at all.

"You should've demanded food from them too, not just water."

"Doing that would just make our debt to them even bigger, wouldn't it? And then they'd charge us even more money?"

"...Damn, you're such an irritating guy. Wouldn't it have been better to deal with someone else?"

Hastun sighed as he addressed Ajax.

But Ajax shook his head at Haston's words.

"It wouldn't have worked. To begin with, Jestal's organization was the one within Gilm's underground network that suited us best. Any other group could have made things even more troublesome than they already are."

"Well..."

Hastun started to say something, but stopped himself.

Indeed, the dealings with Jestal's organization had undoubtedly been going smoothly until this incident.

Considering that, and knowing they were the ones who had pushed the other side, Huston could only remain silent.

"They're here."

Just then, Levjef announced.

Listening intently at his voice, the sound of carriages approaching could be heard.

And not just one, but many more carriages.

(Well, with dozens of women crammed inside, that many carriages makes sense.)

As Ajax thought this, he turned his gaze toward the darkness where the sound came from... Soon, the carriages appeared, cutting through the darkness.

The horses pulling the carriages were hardly what you'd call splendid.

Considering the amount paid to Gestal's organization, Ajax honestly wished they could have provided slightly better horses.

(Then again, we were the ones who requested they be ready immediately tonight. Thinking about it that way, it's not unreasonable.)

While he was thinking these thoughts, the carriage came to a stop.

Jestal stood on the driver's platform at the front carriage.

Normally, the head of the organization wouldn't appear for such a transaction, but this particular deal involved an enormous amount of money and effort.

Moreover, what should have taken much more time had its deadline suddenly moved up, and then again, pushed forward to tonight.

With a transaction of this magnitude, and multiple complications arising,

Given that, it was only natural for Gestal, the person in charge, to be present in case something went wrong.

"Ah, sorry to keep you waiting."

"Not at all. But it seems you're a bit later than scheduled... What happened? With the collars in place, I wouldn't have thought getting the women out of the cells would take so long."

"Tonight was the deadline, you see. I had the guys from the organization do one last job. Thanks to that, I got here late, but compared to when I spoke with you, there are five more."

"Hmm... That's helpful. But I still can't pay more than the amount we agreed on earlier, you know?"

"I understand. Think of this as a token of appreciation from us. We received a fair amount from you, after all. It's only natural we deliver work worthy of that."

Levjef couldn't tell how much truth lay in the words that came out of Gestal's mouth.

But he couldn't possibly believe it was all true. In fact, he even suspected Jestal might be scheming something.

After all, the contract was already firmly in place, the promised payment had been made, and there was no reason for them to go above and beyond now.

Even so, all Levjef could do now was express his gratitude to Jestal.

He absolutely had to avoid saying something foolish here that could cause the deal to fall through.

"Then, please proceed with the handover. And regarding the escape from Gilm..."

"Yeah, I know. But you guys better pay the rest of the money properly."

"Understood. Here's the rest of the payment."

Levjef handed over a leather pouch he'd taken from his pocket.

Seeing this from the driver's seat, Gestal turned his gaze to one of the guards standing nearby.

The guard, receiving the look, nodded slightly, approached Levjef, and took the leather pouch.

The man loosened the cord binding the pouch slightly, confirmed the platinum coins inside, and returned to Gestal.

"...I see. Undoubtedly the agreed-upon amount."

"Yes. I wouldn't betray you here. So, will you fulfill your part of the agreement as promised?"

"Yes. As I mentioned earlier, I managed to recruit a few more at the last minute. I put the slave collars you gave me on them, but they're still full of energy... Is that acceptable?"

"Well... Ideally, I'd have preferred to keep them locked up a few more days to wear them down mentally... But there's no point in asking for the impossible."

"Hearing you say that makes all my effort worthwhile. Also, the water is loaded separately into each wagon."

"Thank you."

At Gestal's unexpected words, Levjef bowed his head obediently.

However, Ajax and Huston, who were checking the surrounding situation near Levjef, didn't show it on their faces, but they wondered why he was being so kind.

If this were a normal business transaction, offering some service might be expected to secure future deals.

But for Ajax and the others, once this transaction was complete, they would likely never come to Gilm again.

After all, with the possibility of becoming bounty targets, they wouldn't willingly come to Gilm, a place teeming with adventurers possessing high combat prowess.

Despite that, the fact that they were going out of their way to provide such service meant there was definitely some reason behind it.

But asking why they were going out of their way like this felt hesitant.

If I did, it could easily backfire if they demanded something in return.

To avoid that, it was undoubtedly better to quietly accept their kindness.

(Ah, maybe they want to make a deal with someone higher up, not us? Thinking that way, maybe there's a possibility...)

Suddenly, Levjef had that thought.

He thinks it... but naturally, he doesn't voice it.

Right now, their top priority was to finish the deal quickly and escape from Gilm.

After confirming that the drivers of all three carriages, including Gestal, had stepped down, Levjef tried to turn his gaze toward Ajax and Hastun... but froze instantly.

Up until now, they had at least maintained a calm surface while negotiating with Gestal. But now, Levegef included, both Ajax and Hastun present sharpened their gazes and scanned their surroundings.

No, it wasn't just Levegef and his group who suddenly heightened their vigilance. Several of Gestal's subordinates, accompanying him as guards, also sharpened their gaze, alertly scanning the surroundings shrouded in the night's darkness.

"What's wrong? Did something happen?"

Asking one of the guards, Gestal tucked the leather pouch containing the platinum coins into his robe and drew his dagger.

Though Jestal now held the position of the organization's boss, he had naturally experienced his share of rough situations in his youth.

His skills had naturally declined since his youth, given his current position, but he was still confident he was more battle-hardened than the average adventurer.

"A voice... I heard what sounded like a groan. It was probably one of our guards stationed nearby. Or it could be another organization trying to make a move on us."

One of the guards, a man holding a dagger, reported this to Gestal.

"A groan? I doubt anyone would dare attack us in this situation. ...Wait, could it be someone targeting our loot or my life?"

"I can't say for sure. But we should definitely stay alert."

"Right. Fortunately, the transaction is already complete. We'll be heading back now. Is that acceptable?"

Though told this, Levjef and the others couldn't immediately nod in agreement to Gestal's words.

Because they understood, at least partially, who had attacked them.

Attacking with such precision in the darkness of night was no easy feat.

Especially here, not outside Gilm—the adventurers' main battlefield—but inside Gilm... specifically within the slum district filled with numerous ruins.

Operating in such a place required skills beyond those of ordinary adventurers.

There weren't many enemies capable of such a feat who also harbored a grudge against Levjef and his group.

"Dammit, they're already here!"

Behind Levjef, Ajax spat out the words with utter disgust.

Seeing Ajax like this, Gestal narrowed his eyes.

His words and actions suggested he understood who the attackers were.

"Hey, what the—"

Just as he was about to ask for details, a man was suddenly blown out of the darkness.

Realizing the man was one of his subordinates, assigned to his escort, Gestal turned his gaze toward where the man had been hurled... and saw the figure standing there, wielding a large scythe and a spear.

"Rei...?"
