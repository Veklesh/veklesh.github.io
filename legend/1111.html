"Augusto, it seems Rei still hasn't returned. I went to the main gate to check, so I'm certain."

"...I see. Given it's Rei, I wouldn't expect him to fall behind the monsters so easily..."

Augusto murmured his concern at Zarust's report, then glanced over the documents in his hand, signed them, and placed them on the desk.

Augusto's muttered words carried a heavy weight of concern for Rei.

In terms of pure strength, he didn't doubt Rei could handle himself.

Indeed, even facing most of the monsters in the Ferris Desert, they probably couldn't do anything to Rei and Set.

But... even so, there were countless concerns.

The biggest one, of course, was that this was a desert.

While fighting desert monsters might not be a worry, the desert environment itself could never be taken lightly.

Even if it's Rei... or rather, precisely because it's Rei.

"It'll be fine, Mr. Augusto. An adventurer of Rei's caliber could easily manage spending a night in the desert."

Thinking of the worried-looking Augusto, Zarust deliberately voiced an optimistic prediction.

It wasn't entirely untrue.

Given Rei's capabilities, spending a night in the desert shouldn't be a problem... He couldn't imagine her getting sick from the temperature difference between day and night.

"Could it be... that the Elema Trading Company had something to do with this?"

"Impossible."

Zalust immediately dismissed Augusto's words.

From Zarust's perspective, Daldora harbored an extraordinary fear toward Rei.

It felt certain that if they made a move, they would lose their lives...

For such a Daldora to attack Rei outside of Gosh was simply unthinkable.

If he were to make a move, it would be within Gosh, where it would be difficult for Rei to unleash his full power.

(Well, having seen Rei and Set up close, I still can't imagine him attacking in the city. Ah, thinking about it that way, is it entirely possible he'd attack Rei somewhere slightly more distant?)

Attacking Rei somewhere slightly removed from them is something I can understand emotionally.

In the desert, he could use vile tactics impossible within Gosh. ...Just like when he hired sand pirates, gave them a sand ship, and attacked Augusto.

Back then, Rei's presence nearby made things manageable, but if it had been just Zaruust and his crew, they could very well have been wiped out.

(Thinking about it that way, it's not impossible, is it?)

Zalust pondered this while watching Augusto, yet knowing Rei's true strength, he couldn't possibly believe Rei would be defeated.

If Daldora foolishly made a move against Rei, he genuinely believed it wouldn't be surprising if the Elema Trading Company ceased to exist today.

"If they knew how dangerous Rei is, they wouldn't dare lay a finger on him."

"...I hope so. Last night, while preparing dinner, Kathy was worried because Rei hadn't come home yet. ...When Rei returns, we really need to have a serious talk about that."

Augusto had been worried about Rei just seconds ago, but remembering that he'd caused concern for Cathy... his own wife... created an inexplicable weight.

Zalust couldn't help but sigh at the weight emanating from Augusto.

The man before him was, fundamentally, the best possible employer one could ask for. But among his few flaws, the greatest was his devotion to his wife... no, the fact that this devotion tended to run wild.

(Being able to love one woman that much is enviable, though.)

As someone whose hobby was frequenting brothels, Zarust had no desire to be tied down to one woman. At the same time, he sometimes felt lonely when he returned home to an empty house.

"...Hm, ahem."

Augusto, snapped back to reality by Zarust's gaze, cleared his throat softly and shifted his focus.

"Why do you think Rei and the others haven't returned?"

"Hmm. Logically, it could just be that they left Gosh..."

"That's unlikely, wouldn't you say? Rei is loyal to a fault, after all. If she had left Gosh, it wouldn't be strange for her to have left some kind of message."

Zalust nodded in agreement at those words.

"Yes. Rei wouldn't just leave silently. That leaves us with only one possibility: some unforeseen incident occurred..."

Considering Rei's strength, it's hard to imagine an unforeseen incident occurring.

And it wasn't just Rei; there was also the griffin, Set.

To deal with such opponents, you'd need to bring in something like a dragon.

Zalust's reasoning seemed clear to Augusto as well. He nodded back, his expression grave.

"I see. But what kind of unforeseen incident do you think could happen to Rei?"

"...Well, I'm not sure. We probably don't need to worry about attacks from monsters or sand pirates. That leaves getting caught in a sandstorm, then?"

At the mention of a sandstorm, Augusto's face twisted in displeasure.

The sandstorms that occurred in this region could sometimes be extremely vicious.

Sand whipped up by the wind acts like sandpaper, scraping away skin, slicing through flesh, and for the unlucky, even grinding down bone.

Of course, such storms were rare, but still, every year, a significant number of people lost their lives to them.

If caught in such a storm, even Rei might die.

Zalust's words made Augusto picture the corpse.

"I haven't heard of any sandstorms occurring around Gosh from yesterday to today?"

"That's true. But Rei has Set. Considering a griffin's flight speed, traveling far from Gosh shouldn't be too difficult. If that's the case..."

There was no need to hear more.

Augusto sighed, his face heavy with sorrow.

It would be a lie to say he hadn't counted on Rei as a combat asset.

But at the same time, it was also true that despite their brief acquaintance, he had grown somewhat fond of him.

Thinking about it that way, he simply couldn't bring himself to believe that Rei was dead.

"Huh!?"

Suddenly, in front of a stunned Augusto, Zarust tensed and reached for the scabbard at his waist.

Simultaneously, the door was knocked violently. When Augusto announced permission to enter, a beastman hurriedly rushed into the room.

The ape beastman, whose face was usually described as comical, had it set in a stern expression.

Recognizing the face, Zarust withdrew his hand from the scabbard.

Completely unaware that he had just narrowly avoided a life-threatening situation, the monkey beastman spoke.

"Augusto! A sand ship is approaching Gosh right now! And it's one we recognize!"

At those words alone, Augusto understood who was aboard that sand ship.

There weren't many sand ships he had seen, and more importantly, the last one he had seen recently was the one used by the sand pirates... meaning it belonged to Daldora, the head of the Elema Trading Company.

And the current owner of the sand ship seized from the sand pirates was...

"Rei has returned!"

Shouting in joy, Augusto rushed out of the room.

Zalust hurriedly followed after him.

An unmistakable smile spread across his face.

"Rei's back!?"

At the same moment Augusto was shouting in joy, another person received the same report and shouted just as loudly.

...However, the emotion contained within that cry was the exact opposite of Augusto's.

The man who had shouted with such loathing... Upon hearing Daldora's words, the man who had delivered the news of Rei's return nodded timidly.

Remembering that the man before him was someone he had hired only recently, Daldora took a deep breath, as if to suppress the emotions welling up inside him, before speaking again.

"So, is it true that Rei has returned?"

"Y-yes. Well... I saw the sand ship you had, Daldora, approaching, so I figured that adventurer Rei must have returned."

"...I see. So you haven't actually seen Rei's face, then?"

"No. But seeing that sand ship..."

Perhaps realizing he might be blamed for not having a definitive report that Rei had returned, the man hurriedly tried to continue.

Daldora, being naturally nervous, regretted bringing such incomplete information.

But Daldora shook her head at him, signaling he needn't say more.

This was the initial report; speed was prioritized over absolute accuracy.

"I understand. I'm not blaming you either."

Hearing Daldora's words, the man who brought the information breathed a sigh of relief, realizing he wouldn't be blamed.

Watching the man, Daldora tapped her fingertips repeatedly on the desk.

The expression on her face, which seemed almost neurotic, bordered on irritation.

He had hoped Rei would just disappear... and since Rei hadn't returned after leaving yesterday afternoon, he had begun to suspect something.

(After all, Rei had said he'd only stopped by this town by chance. I'd hoped he'd just stay gone somewhere. I never imagined he'd actually come back.)

He forced his churning heart to settle, then rose from his chair.

Regardless, he had to confirm whether it was truly Rei who had returned.

Maybe... just maybe, for some reason, he'd given the Sand Ship to someone else. Or, though it was extremely unlikely, Rei might have died, and by chance, another adventurer or merchant had acquired the Sand Ship.

I knew the odds were astronomically low, but the mere fact that I found myself hoping for it showed just how troublesome Rei was to Daldora.

His bodyguard had already made it clear that he was no match for Ray's strength, so he couldn't afford to make any careless moves.

But trying to persuade him with clever words was also impossible, since Daldora was at odds with Augusto.

Rei, who maintained a good relationship with Augusto, would naturally harbor negative feelings toward Daldora.

(After all, his first impression of her was terrible.)

Despite exhausting every avenue and even lending him a sand ship, the attack on Augusto failed.

Despite that attack, which should have succeeded without fail, when I heard Augusto had returned safely to Gosh and rushed to see him, it was Rei who was with Augusto.

Under such circumstances, his first meeting with Rei occurred. Furthermore, having even his own sand ship stolen by Rei, Daldora couldn't hold back, and their first encounter ended in what could only be described as the worst possible outcome.

"Wh-what should we do?"

Daldora, lost in thought, snapped back to reality at the words of the man before him.

Though slightly annoyed at being interrupted mid-thought, she opened her mouth, reasoning that the man before her had brought valuable information.

"What is it?"

"Well, about Rei... Shouldn't we properly confirm that the one who returned is really Rei?"

"...You're right. Then, I'm sorry, but could you go check?"

"Yes! Right away!"

Watching the man's retreating back as if he'd been waiting for those words, Daldora let out a small sigh.

He knew. There was no one else aboard the sand ship that had arrived but Rei.

After all, someone who commands a griffin as a familiar couldn't just be dealt with so easily.

Still, she had sent the man to check because she was gambling on that slim possibility, and more importantly, because his presence was distracting her from her thoughts.

"Now then, what should I do? Fortunately, they haven't made a move yet... No, this matter is already beyond my control. I suppose I should quietly bring this to Lord Ryu Brand's attention."

He uttered the name of the lord who ruled Gosh.

She couldn't directly deal with Rei herself, and even if she waited for him to leave, he'd simply return as easily as he had this time.

Daldora had wanted to handle this matter alone if possible, but since she had no means to resolve it herself, she had no choice but to turn to someone more powerful.

Even knowing that person might not be entirely reliable.
