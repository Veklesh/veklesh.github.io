The Twilight Spear thrusts straight ahead.

Exolis parries the strike with his Magic Sword as he advances.

Parrying while closing the distance isn’t that difficult a technique.

But that’s only true when facing ordinary enemies.

The strike of the Twilight Spear thrust by Rei possesses a sharpness that seems capable of piercing not just the air, but space itself.

To feel that so close at hand is, under normal circumstances, an impossible feat.

Yet Exolis, known as the Ice Blade, does just that.

And not with a death-defying resolve… but as if it were something he could do effortlessly.

As Exolis closed the distance along the path of the Twilight Spear, he heard the whistling sound of wind coming from his left the next instant and leaped away from Rei.

Rei’s Death Scythe swept past the spot where Exolis’s body had been just a moment ago.

But in the very next instant, the Death Scythe—which should have already passed by—attacked Exoris once more… Exoris blocked it with his Magic Sword almost reflexively, but was blown away the next moment… yet he twisted his body mid-air and landed on the stage.

“Damn it, how can he move that huge scythe so easily?”

From Exolis’s perspective, that scythe was clearly not something Rei could wield with ease.

He knew this precisely because he had just used the Magic Sword to block what looked like a blow from the handle.

It possessed an overwhelming weight that was almost unbelievable for a weapon.

Although Exolis had managed to block that strike, he had merely prevented a direct hit; he hadn’t completely nullified the power of the swing itself.

He was blown from the center of the stage to the edge… nearly ten meters away.

Of course, it was only because he was Exolis that he managed to survive that.

If this had been any of the others who had sparred with Rei up to this point, they would undoubtedly have been blown off the stage and defeated.

To handle a weapon of such immense weight with such ease—even from Exolis’s perspective, it could only be described as abnormal.

At the very least, if asked whether he could handle a weapon of that weight in the same way, Exolis would answer with a resounding “no.”

Rei was met with a gaze that seemed to say, “That’s just unreasonable,” but he didn’t seem particularly bothered and swung the Death Scythe with one hand.

“Even if you say that, I can only say that I can do it because I’m capable of it.”

To be precise, it’s the Death Scythe’s ability as a magic item—one that allows only Rei and Set to wield its hundred-kilogram weight with almost no strain—but Rei had no intention of going out of his way to explain that.

After all, knowing which magic items to acquire is part of what makes a good adventurer.

……That said, the Death Scythe is a magical item that came into existence through an unprecedented process—it was created alongside Set using Beast Magic.

Exolis probably wouldn’t have imagined that the Death Scythe was born in such a way either.

In any case, Exolis doesn’t expect Rei to reveal exactly how he acquired a magic item like the Death Scythe.

What matters now is how to attack next… and how to evade Rei’s attacks.

“I see. Well then, after this mock battle is over, I’ll have you tell me as a reward for my victory.”

“That’s not going to happen. I’m the one who’s going to win.”

“Hmm, I happen to think I’m the one who’s going to win, too.”

“I get why you’d say that… but judging by how things are going right now, I think I have the upper hand.”

“That might be true right now. But… in the end, the one who’s still standing at the very end is the winner…!”

As he spoke, Exolis hurled an icicle—created using the power of his Magic Sword—at Rei.

The icicles were faster than an arrow, but the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear wielded by Rei were even faster.

The ice shatters one after another, but for Exolis, that was all part of the plan.

Given that Rei was, at the very least—no, in terms of actual ability—more than worthy of the title “Legend,” Exolis couldn’t possibly believe an attack of this magnitude would be effective.

However, if the launched icicles could block Rei’s vision even for an instant, this attack would have served its purpose.

And so, using the icicles to conceal himself, he closes the distance to Rei and channels his magic once more to activate the Magic Sword.

However, this time, he wouldn’t resort to an obvious attack like firing multiple icicles.

(Take this blow. When you do, you’ll surely be surprised.)

Through the strike of the Magic Sword, he channeled cold air into his opponent’s weapon, making him feel the chill.

If I were to attack in earnest, I could follow up the slash with another ice attack—or rather, that was Exolis’s standard tactic—but this is, after all, just a mock battle.

So, as a second-best option, he decided to use the tactic of startling his opponent with the cold.

That said, this wasn’t a tactic Exolis was using for the first time; it was one he was well-accustomed to.

Anyone, if the weapon they’re holding suddenly turns cold, will freeze for a moment… or in some cases, for a few seconds.

Under normal circumstances, that wouldn’t be a big deal, but in the heat of battle, that split second is incredibly significant.

It’s the kind of moment where the outcome of the battle could easily be decided in an instant.

That was why Exolis believed he could create a sufficient opening to win without inflicting a fatal wound on his opponent...

“Guh!”

Just as he thought the swung Magic Sword had been parried by Rei’s Twilight Spear, he was struck by a violent impact, and before he knew it, Exolis was blown through the air.

He was sent flying... yet he managed to land at the edge of the stage—a feat only someone with the nickname “Ice Blade” could achieve.

If this had been an ordinary adventurer, they would undoubtedly have failed to land and simply collapsed onto the ground.

But... even so, landing at the edge of the stage was the limit for Exolis... The next moment, he hit the ground on his knees, and that movement caused his body to extend slightly beyond the stage.

“That’s it! The winner is Rei!”

“What!?”

Exolis had risen from the ground in an instant, but just as he regained his footing, the male announcer’s voice rang out, causing him to cry out in surprise.

Why? Exolis was about to shout that... but he realized that due to the movement when he knelt, his body—or more precisely, the leg he’d knelt on—had gone off the stage.

Being knocked to the ground or stepping off the stage means defeat.

Remembering that was the rule, he exhaled deeply, trying to calm the emotions surging within him.

“I lose… huh?”

“Yes. And I win.”

“...Is it okay for me to think it was a good fight?”

At Rei’s words, Exolis asked as he stood up.

Watching Exoris like that, Rei nodded in response.

“Yeah. There’s no doubt I enjoyed this as a mock battle, too.”

“I see. …Well then, I’ll take that as a win.”

Watching Exolis speak those words, seemingly satisfied with Rei’s response, Rei said nothing more.

(There’s no doubt he was strong. But… compared to Relieu, who goes by the nickname “Gale,” he’s definitely a cut below. Then again, this is just a mock battle. Since it’s not a real fight, it wouldn’t be strange if he was holding back in various ways.)

What Rei recalled was Relieu, the adventurer with the epithet “Gale,” whom he had cleared a dungeon with a short while ago.

Even among those with the same epithet, there’s inevitably a gap in their actual strength.

Given that, it was only natural that Rei felt a little disappointed about it.

That said, in the sense that he wasn’t showing his true strength either, Rei was in the same boat.

“A fierce battle! It was an incredibly fierce battle! The icicles unleashed by Exolis, the Ice Blade, were nothing short of mesmerizing! And in response to those icicles… no, Rei, who countered the Magic Sword’s abilities, has also clearly demonstrated the strength of an ‘Epic’!”

The male announcer’s voice echoed throughout the arena, and the audience nodded in agreement.

...Amidst all this, the ones wearing displeased expressions were those who were next in line to spar with Rei.

The mock battle between Rei and Exolis had been that spectacular.

Given that, it was only natural that they would hope for a similarly spectacular battle in the next mock fight.

But the previous battle was only possible because they were "Nickname Holders"; they couldn’t possibly imagine that they, as Rank C adventurers, could pull off a similar fight.

From the bottom of their hearts, they envied those who had fought before Exolis.

As the adventurers were thinking this, Exolis, having finished his mock battle and his conversation with Rei, walked past them.

“You guys are next? …Well, your opponent has a nickname. Just treat the fight as a chance to get some training.”

Hearing Egsoris say this, the adventurers felt a sense of resentment—they didn’t have to put on such a flashy show—but they didn’t voice it.

After all, their current predicament is entirely due to their own lack of skill.

“Let’s go.”

One of the adventurers murmured quietly, and the others, each gripping their weapons, advanced onto the stage.

And when they faced Rei on the stage… strangely enough, they felt the anxiety they’d been feeling inside them disappear.

(Maybe those words from the Ice Blade earlier were meant to prepare me for this.)

What the man—and his companions around him—felt was the opportunity to test their own strength against Rei, the man with the alias.

Just as Exolis had said earlier, it felt as though they were receiving training from a “Nicknamed One.”

In reality, being able to train with a “Name-Bearer” is an incredibly fortunate opportunity—it’s not something that usually happens so easily.

It would be a different story if they were to make a formal request, but even then, they’d need to pay a hefty sum to receive training.

Considering that, this request allows them to engage in a mock battle with a "Nicknamed One" while getting paid by a noble—it’s nothing but a win-win for them.

“Next up is… oh, the Rank C party with a reputation for skill, Moonlight Blade! This looks promising!”

Listening to the male emcee’s words, the male adventurer—the leader of Moonlight Blade—thought to himself that this was a bit of a stretch.

They certainly didn’t think their own strength was lacking.

That’s what their current status as a Rank C party demonstrates.

But… even so, if asked whether they could put on a fight on par with—or even surpassing—that of Exolis, he couldn’t possibly nod in agreement.

Even as he thought that, he realized he was still looking forward to the mock battle against the man with the nickname.

He is slightly surprised by the fact that his mood is completely different from what it was just a moment ago.

I don’t think we can beat Rei, but I’ve managed to get into the mindset of fighting with all my might and fully demonstrating our true strength.

“Alright, then… let the mock battle begin!”

With those words, the members of Moonlight Blade spring into action.

Judging by Rei’s behavior so far, he often lets his opponents take the first move.

I feel a bit disrespected, but considering the gap in our abilities, I quickly accept that it’s only natural.

In fact, they felt that if Rei were to launch an all-out attack right from the start of the mock battle, they would undoubtedly be trampled on without being able to do a thing.

Having been in the same party for several years, they each understood their roles without needing specific instructions.

The man with the dagger and the man with the halberd step forward, while the other two take up positions in the rear to draw their bows.

Naturally, the arrows were fired along trajectories that would not hit their allies.

But… Rei effortlessly sliced those arrows away with his Death Scythe and turned his attention to the enemy closing in on him.

(Bows aside, a spear and a dagger… I’ve seen the spear-and-longsword combo before, but is this the current trend?)

Thinking this, Rei parries the halberd’s strike with Twilight Spear and dodges the dagger’s thrust by shifting his body slightly.

That said, while the man whose halberd was parried is one thing, the man wielding the dagger had his attack simply evaded, so it’s not that difficult for him to follow up with his next move.

The man immediately followed up, attempting to attack Rei...

“Guh!”

The moment he tried to move, he was knocked flying by the butt of the Death Scythe.

He used the back of the blade—or, more precisely, the hilt—to knock the man holding the halberd away as well, and just as if seizing that opening, he knocked both of the two arrows that had been shot at him down with the Twilight Spear.

Although the arrows were fired almost simultaneously, Rei, holding the Twilight Spear in his left hand, managed to deal with them almost at the same time… He then closed the distance between himself and the two men wielding bows, thrusting the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear at them respectively, bringing the mock battle to an end.
