"Yummy!"

That cry echoes around them.

And it wasn't just one or two people.

Everyone who drank the water created by Rei with his flowing water dagger let out similar cries, their faces glowing with ecstasy.

Watching them, Rei smiled, satisfied that his plan had worked.

Walking endlessly under the summer sun was exhausting, even without monsters, soldiers, or Jaya's pursuers.

Those nearing their physical limits were transported by carriage, but even so, Rei and his group's pace was by no means fast.

Given that, some sort of incentive was necessary.

Of course, the mere fact that they didn't have to stay in Mejogo and become prostitutes was a significant carrot.

Furthermore, the promise of solid work waiting for them in Gilm was surely another substantial carrot.

But even so, those were ultimately just final incentives; they couldn't serve as the immediate incentive for that very day.

In that sense, Rei serving that water at dinner had a sufficiently immediate effect as a short-term incentive.

Still, producing enough water for nearly a thousand people to drink, even if just one cup each...

While it posed no problem for Rei in terms of magical energy expenditure, the time required to produce it was considerable.

"Still, no matter how many times I drink it, it really is delicious."

Vihera murmured thoughtfully as she drank the water created from the Waterfall Dagger.

Agreeing with her, those near Rei and Vihera—Elena, Marina, and Byune—nodded in assent.

"A cloudless night sky, lit only by the moon and stars... Just hearing that description makes one think of sweet moments spent together with Rei. But honestly, given our current situation, I can't think of it that way at all."

Marina said this with a smile as she drank some water.

Honestly, just hearing the words Marina used, it could almost sound like a sweet moment between lovers.

But with nearly a thousand people gathered like this, it was hardly a situation that could be described as sweet or romantic.

"Well, with this many people, it can't be helped. More importantly... I couldn't do it last night, but shouldn't we contact Ara soon? We need to let Lord Daskar know that persuading this group went well."

"Hmm, you're right. Besides, Ara tends to worry about things."

"But she never reaches out to us, does she?"

Vihera asked Elena curiously.

In truth, communication via the Paired Orb wasn't one-way.

Elena could contact her, and Ara could contact her—either way was possible.

But so far, Ara has never once reached out.

Everyone here knows how devoted Ara is to Elena.

So why? It's only natural to wonder.

Indeed, it's certain that everyone else besides Vihera is thinking the same thing.

"Well, since we don't know what might happen over here, we've basically decided that I'll be the one to make contact."

"Ah... I see."

Rei, who among them had the closest relationship with Elena and Ara, gave a somewhat understanding look.

...Though even those not as close could easily see how deeply Ara admired Elena, so the others besides Rei also wore expressions of understanding.

"Then, right away... no, if we're contacting Daskar-sama, wouldn't it be better to call Sura? When we're not here, Sura will be leading this group."

"Right. We should probably introduce Sura to Daskar too."

At Rei's words, Marina instructed the Resistance member eating a little ways off to fetch Sura.

At her words, the resistance man immediately ran off.

Part of his haste was to fetch Sura quickly, but it was also largely because he was half-dazed from having spoken directly to a beauty like Marina.

Marina smiled softly at his fresh-faced reaction.

Faced with such a Marina, Rei almost blurted out, "Are you some kind of villainess?" and he probably wouldn't have been wrong.

But sensing something in Rei's attitude, Marina spoke, still smiling.

"Oh? What's the matter?"

"...Nothing. More importantly, I thought we should contact Ara sooner rather than later."

Saying anything here would only bring trouble upon herself.

He judged that, and gently steered the conversation away.

Of course, what he said wasn't wrong.

Elena smiled faintly at Rei's attitude as she activated the Paired Orb he handed her.

It took only seconds after activation for Ara's image to appear on the Paired Orb.

This meant Ara was near the Paired Orb.

Given that she didn't know when Elena would contact her, this response was probably natural for Ara.

'Elena-sama? I'm relieved to hear you're safe.'

"Safe? Honestly, I didn't even get hurt."

In truth, even after the last communication ended, she had joined the fight against Jaya's soldiers at Mejogo and engaged cavalry units near Rossi.

But such battles probably didn't seem particularly dangerous to Elena.

(Of course. With a nickname like Princess General, she wasn't fighting renowned opponents, just soldiers from a criminal organization and a small country's cavalry.)

Indeed, compared to fighting such foes, battling giants would have been far more perilous.

"I wasn't worried about Lady Elena, but... I still can't help but be concerned. More importantly, is this about the matter discussed with Lord Daskar?"

"Hmm. That part went fairly smoothly. Some even volunteered to stay behind in Mejogo. I can't say I brought everyone back for certain, but..."

Elena's words were vague, but considering Mejogo's vastness, it wouldn't be surprising if some women remained unaware and still stayed behind.

Given how violently Set had rampaged on such a large scale, normally even those asleep would have woken up.

But given that being a prostitute is a job that demands more physical strength than one might imagine, if their sleep was deep, it couldn't be definitively stated that not a single one slept through the commotion Set caused.

Still, he had to believe the women when they said they'd searched the area while gathering their belongings, and conclude it was probably fine.

"I see. Then, should we inform Lord Daskar about this?"

"Yes. And I thought I'd introduce you to Sura, who leads the resistance."

'Understood. I'll inform Lord Daskar immediately. It's still early in the evening, so it should be fine.'

This incident was a major development for Gilm as well.

For that reason, Ara had been staying at the lord's mansion instead of an inn lately, so she could contact Daskar immediately if anything happened.

Ara herself possessed features that, if she remained silent, could be called beautiful, making her reasonably popular among the knights and soldiers... though Elena, the object of their devotion, seemed completely unaware of this.

"Come to think of it, there's not a single rumor about Ara... but staying at the lord's manor, maybe something's going on after all?"

Marina said this with a slightly amused tone, but Elena shook her head with a touch of melancholy.

"Probably not. Quite a few men have tried to court Ara before. But she's turned them all down."

In the past, Elena wouldn't have paid much attention to talk about Ara.

But by loving a man named Rei, Elena had come to understand how wonderful love could be... and how much strength it could give her.

So, as someone who had been with her since childhood, it was only natural to wish for Ara to find a lover too.

"Um... did I interrupt something?"

Elena's words were met with a somewhat hesitant voice echoing around them.

But everyone present had sensed the person approaching by their aura.

Showing no particular sign of displeasure, Elena shook her head.

Even in such a casual gesture, Elena's golden hair reflected the quiet moonlight, shimmering softly.

(Being beautiful sure has its perks.)

Sura thought this while gazing at Elena, though Sura herself was undoubtedly a woman who could be called beautiful.

In fact, when she first met Rei in Mejogo, Sura had approached him disguised as a prostitute.

And yet, she managed to do so without arousing suspicion. That alone was proof enough that Sura was indeed beautiful.

"No, you're not in the way. I'm about to introduce you to Lord Daskar, so keep that in mind."

"...Huh?"

The words that came out of Elena's mouth were incomprehensible.

That was Sura's honest reaction.

But even as Sura was overwhelmed by confusion, the situation progressed.

'Rei, it seems you've successfully brought the Mejogo people out safely.'

"Yes. So, I was thinking of introducing the leader of the Resistance to Lord Daskar today."

"Hmm, yes. As the lord of Gilm, I should see the face of the one leading the resistance."

Hearing those words, Rei turned his gaze toward Sura.

Though he didn't speak, Sura seemed to understand what Rei meant with that glance alone. Though not yet fully composed, Sura stepped forward before the Paired Orb.

"Ah, pleased to meet you, Lord Daskar. I am Sura, currently leading the resistance."

Though her first words came out stammering with nervousness, she quickly regained her usual composure. Perhaps it was the pride she felt from having led her fellow Resistance members.

Also, the fact that she wasn't meeting Daskar directly, but through the Paired Orb, likely played a significant role.

Even so, the overwhelming presence emanating from Daskar through the Paired Orbs was intensely powerful.

Sura honestly believed that if she were to meet him face-to-face, she would undoubtedly be overwhelmed by his presence.

"I see. Leading the Resistance at such a young age?"

"Yes."

Sura had feared being looked down upon for her youth, but Daskar's words gave no hint of such condescension.

Even so, it was obvious to anyone watching that Sura was tense.

"Hey, Daskar. Don't be too hard on the young ones. You were young once too, you know."

It was Marina who broke the awkward silence.

To begin with, not many people could be this bold with Daskar.

Marina, who had known Daskar since he was little, was one such person.

Daskar, knowing Marina was privy to several embarrassing stories from his childhood—what Rei would call his dark past—couldn't usually afford to be harsh with her.

Of course, it would be a different matter entirely if Marina were arbitrarily interfering in important decisions made by the Gilms or the Neutral Faction.

At the very least, now was not the time to make such judgments.

"Hey, now. Why bring that up out of the blue? ...Ah, Sura, was it? Just because you're young doesn't mean I'm going to go easy on you. Besides, if I were to go easy on you just because you're young, what about Rei?"

Sura is in her late teens.

Compared to that, Rei looks like she's in her mid-teens.

If Sura was being judged based on her youth, then it wouldn't be unreasonable to underestimate Rei too.

Though, to be fair, Daskar, who first learned of Rei, had only recently discovered Rei after registering with the guild and witnessed the reality of Rei defeating the Orc King. That might explain his reaction.

"Anyway, if Rei and the others recognize you as the Resistance leader, I won't say anything more. If you make it to Gilm, I'm sure Rei will fill you in, but I'll have work ready for you. We need all the hands we can get over here."

Because of someone, he thought.

Daskar gave Rei a complex look, a mix of joy at Gilm's growth and resentment at the massive increase in work.
