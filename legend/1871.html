That night, Rei was awakened by the sound of someone knocking on the door.

He had been planning to raid the home of the organization that had acted as an intermediary for the Black Wolf—or, more accurately, the home of the person leading that organization—so he had gone to sleep after eating an early dinner.

Normally, Rei is a groggy sleeper.

Even if he were woken by a knock, his mind wouldn’t usually start working right away, but this time was different.

Perhaps because it was right before a battle, Rei’s mind was already focused on combat, and by the time the knocking woke him, he was already in his usual state.

“Miranda?”

“Ah, yes. Um… you were already awake?”

Having seen Rei’s morning routine countless times, Miranda probably didn’t expect an immediate response after knocking.

She said this, looking slightly surprised.

In reality, he had woken up to the knock, but Rei decided that explaining that now would only be a waste of time, so he didn’t correct Miranda.

He motioned for Rei to come in and finished getting ready himself.

“Well, that’s about it. So, what’s this about?”

“Yes. Lady Elena said it’s almost time and asked you to come to her room.”

“Got it. Is Elena’s room okay?”

“Yes.”

After exchanging those few words, Rei immediately leaves the room.

Miranda seemed slightly taken aback by this—the difference between the Rei she knew and this version of him—but she said nothing more and simply led Rei to Elena’s room.

Rei knew where Elena’s room was, but that was likely part of her duties as a maid.

Since they’d finished their meal a little early and gone to sleep, it was naturally already dark outside.

Light from magical items and candles.

A hallway with multiple light sources would likely seem magical to anyone walking through it who didn’t know any better.

In fact, even Rei, who has walked these corridors at night countless times since arriving at the mansion, still feels that way.

Soon, Miranda, who had been guiding Rei, approached a room that was familiar to Rei as well...

“Oh, Rei?”

She was suddenly addressed by a figure standing in front of the room.

Rei immediately recognized who that person was.

That’s because they were people Rei had met a few times before.

Yes, those people.

Standing in front of Elena’s room were three people: Teres, Ranigus, and Brewitt.

Although they were all familiar faces to Rei, he couldn’t help but think it was an unusual combination.

“That’s quite an unusual group, isn’t it? I wonder what on earth led to this?”

Teres is Elena’s friend, and they’re close enough to enjoy tea together.

Ranigus was the one who had come along with Gaiska when Rei and Elena were having tea with Teres.

And as for Brewitt, having crossed paths with Rei numerous times before, he was someone who needed no introduction to Rei.

“Ah, I came across some information that piqued my interest. While I was looking into it, I ended up meeting these two.”

At Brewitt’s words, Teres and Ranigus nodded without changing their expressions.

“Yes. I’ve also managed to gather some information, so I came to speak with Lady Elena about it.”

“Same here.”

“...It’s fine that you got the information, but why on earth are you bringing it to Elena?”

If it were truly important information, they should have conveyed it directly to her father, Liberte—Duke Kerebel—rather than Elena.

The fact that they hadn’t done so led Rei to surmise that the information was likely not that important… or perhaps it was information without confirmation.

In any case, since standing here talking in front of Elena’s room forever wouldn’t get us anywhere, Rei knocked on her door.

If he had business with Elena, it would be best to get it over with as quickly as possible.

“Ah.”

At Rei’s action, Teres and Ranigus spoke in unison.

“What’s the matter? You had business with Elena, didn’t you? Besides… knowing Elena, I’m sure she’s already sensed your presence.”

Given that Elena is known as the “Princess General,” sensing someone’s presence is a piece of cake for her.

Hearing Rei say this, Teres and Ranigus wore expressions that were hard to read.

Brewitt, however, showed no sign of being particularly shaken by Rei’s words—perhaps because he already knew, or had anticipated, that Elena possessed the ability to sense their presence.

As if in response to Rei’s words, the door opened…

“You should all come in. I can’t keep them waiting for too long, though.”

Elena said this and invited everyone into the room.

Although Elena likely had been asleep just a short while ago, just like Rei, she didn’t look that way—at least not to Rei’s eyes.

Her demeanor itself simply didn’t look like that of someone who had just woken up.

Rei, too, was in a state that could be called “battle mode” today, so unlike usual, he wasn’t showing any signs of being groggy, but Elena’s demeanor was far superior to Rei’s… she seemed completely normal, as if she’d been awake the whole time.

(Ah, but Elena is the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel and a Princess General, so even if she hadn’t actually been sleeping and had been awake all along, it wouldn’t be strange, would it?)

As Rei is merely a guest of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, his and Elena’s positions are naturally different.

Moreover, when Rei returns to Gilm, Elena will be going to Gilm with Rei as well.

Their stay in Anesis wouldn’t be much longer… considering they likely had less than ten days left, Elena must have had a lot of work to do.

If Elena were an ordinary human, she wouldn’t be able to handle such a busy workload and then go on to raid the home of someone leading a secret organization… well, it’s not impossible, but if she tried to do something like that while exhausted from work, she naturally wouldn’t be able to perform at her full potential.

But Elena, who has inherited the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone, is obviously no ordinary human.

A little fatigue like that… no, she probably isn’t even tired to begin with.

The same was true for Rei, but he figured it would be better to rest if he could, so he took a nap—though it was too late in the day to really call it that.

Relieved that Elena showed no signs of fatigue, Rei entered the room with the others.

Inside the room, Ara was wearing her armor, ready to launch an attack at any moment.

(Ah, Elena too.)

Suddenly realizing the source of the unease he’d felt when he first saw Elena, Rei nodded to himself in understanding.

Whether it was Elena or Ara, while they were staying at this mansion, they hadn’t been wearing the armor Rei was used to seeing, but rather ordinary clothes.

Having seen them in those clothes constantly, even in this short time, Rei had somehow grown accustomed to it.

“Huh? That outfit…”

“Whoa, Ara’s armed.”

Teres and Ranigus also seemed to notice Ara’s appearance, letting out voices of surprise.

Since the two of them weren’t usually involved in battle, seeing Ara like that must have seemed unexpected to them.

...In reality, Ara was part of Elena’s guard, so it wasn’t all that unusual for her to be armed.

Compared to them, Brewitt—who roams the streets alone and even gets himself into fights—didn’t seem all that surprised.

“So? What exactly brings you here today? As I said before, we’re going to be a bit busy from here on out. Unless it’s an emergency, I’d prefer if you could come back tomorrow or later.”

It was currently around 9:00 p.m.

There’s no doubt that many commoners would already be asleep at this hour, and even for nobles, barring the possibility of a party, it could be considered somewhat inconsiderate to pay a visit at this time.

Given that three nobles were engaging in such an inconsiderate act, it was certain there must be a valid reason.

However, even understanding that, the fact remained that Elena couldn’t afford to have her time taken up by them, given what she had to do next.

However, Elena’s thoughts changed the moment she heard the words that came out of Teres’s mouth.

“Lady Elena, could this possibly be related to the matter involving the Marquis of Seisor?”

“……What?”

Those words were powerful enough to capture Elena’s full attention.

Since it was likely Gaiska who had hired the Black Wolf, mentioning the Seisor Marquis family here was bound to catch the attention of Elena… and of Rei and Ara, who were accompanying her today.

“What do you know?”

What came out of Elena’s mouth were sharp words and a piercing gaze.

Though Teres knew the usual Elena, seeing her like this—as the Princess General… and in a state of near-battle readiness—was a first for him, and he held his breath for a moment under that intense gaze.

Beside Teres, Ranigus—who, though not on such a grand scale, had participated in actual combat—spoke up.

“I’ve picked up a bit of intel myself. Do you know that the Marquis of Seisor has actually been making various moves?”

“Hmm. I’m aware of that. I hear it started the day after the New Year’s party ended.”

“Exactly. And apparently, even within the Seisor Marquis family, those skilled in combat—soldiers and knights—are making their moves.”

“……What?”

Unfamiliar with that information, a slight gasp of surprise escapes Elena’s lips.

In reality, however, the Duke of Kerebel’s household had already obtained that information.

After all, given the scale of the mobilization in Anesis, if they were to attempt such a move without the Duke of Kerebel’s permission, it wouldn’t be surprising if the army led by the Duke of Kerebel were to suppress them.

Given that this hadn’t happened, she should have concluded that the House of Kerebel must have been informed to some extent about this matter.

Elena was taken aback for a moment, but once she considered this, she spoke as if she had immediately come to terms with it.

“I’m sure Father is aware of that matter as well. But… if the Marquis of Seisor’s household is involved, it wouldn’t have been strange for me to have received information about it.”

Elena muttered, but immediately turned her gaze toward Rei.

“What do you think?”

“Well… one possibility is that Gaiska found out about the Black Wolf incident and ran away because of it? …Though, I don’t think Gaiska has the intelligence-gathering capabilities to pull that off.”

“No, that’s not it. Didn’t I tell you? The Seisor Marquis family started making moves on the day of the party.”

Hearing Elena’s words, Rei realized she was right.

Rei had defeated the Black Wolf today, and the Black Wolf’s corpse was still inside Rei’s Misty Ring.

No one should have witnessed the battle between Rei and the Black Wolf, and at the very least, neither Rei nor Elena had found anyone who had.

Given that, the only ones who knew the Black Wolf was dead were Rei and Elena themselves, along with the Intelligence Unit of the Duke of Kerebel’s household—to whom Elena had explained the situation—and perhaps Duke Liberte of Kerebel, who likely received a report from them.

Although the Seisor Marquis family wields significant influence even among the Noble Factions, it is unlikely they could have demonstrated such intelligence-gathering capabilities here in Anesis, the stronghold of the Kerebel Duke’s family.

“So, does that mean the Black Wolf has nothing to do with this incident?”

Elena tilted her head in response to Rei’s muttered words, which sounded as if he couldn’t quite believe it himself.

The first thing that came to Elena’s mind as a reason why the Seisor Marquis family would go so far as to incur a debt to the Duke of Kerebel to mobilize people was, of course, Gaiska.

And now that she was almost certain that Gaiska was the one who hired the Black Wolf, she found it impossible to believe that the Black Wolf wasn’t involved in the Seisor Marquis family’s sudden major move under these circumstances.

“Ultimately, we won’t know the details unless we go there ourselves. …I’m grateful to all three of you for bringing this information.”

At Elena’s words, Teres, Ranigus, and Brewitt each wore their own expressions.

Teres looked genuinely happy that Elena was pleased.

Ranigus wore a satisfied smile, pleased that he, a member of the Royal Faction, had earned a favor from Elena, the symbol of the Noble Faction.

...As for Brewitt, he wore a look of disappointment, knowing that a battle was coming but that he wouldn’t be able to participate.

Watching the three of them, Elena looked out the window and murmured softly, “It’s about time.”
