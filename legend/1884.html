"Hmm... Oh...?"

Rei woke up and only then realized he had been asleep.

Last night, after Celestan’s house had vanished and he’d returned to Duke Kerebel’s residence, he’d waited in his room, thinking he might be summoned for some reason…

(Is this what they call ‘falling asleep’?)

Gazing at the book lying on the floor, Rei pondered with his groggy, half-asleep mind.

The book on the floor was one he’d borrowed from the library at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

Needless to say, if he had dirtied them—specifically, if he had drooled on them or, in some cases, torn a page while half-asleep—he would have had to pay for them.

Fortunately, however, the book had simply fallen to the floor, and Rei hadn’t soiled or torn it.

Still, just to be sure they hadn’t gotten damaged when they fell, he began flipping through the pages while still half-asleep… when suddenly, there was a knock at the door.

“Excuse me, Rei. Are you awake yet?”

“Uh… I’m awake.”

It wasn’t a very loud voice, but it must have been heard on the other side of the door.

Soon the door opened, and Miranda appeared.

Miranda felt a slight sense of relief upon realizing that the person standing before her was the Rei she knew.

The Rei she’d come to wake up last night was clearly different from the Rei she knew, so she must have been worried that today would be the same.

“What about lunch?”

“……Lunch? Isn’t it supposed to be breakfast?”

“Yes. It’s already afternoon. In fact, it’s been quite a while since noon passed.”

“I see.”

Rei muttered, sounding a little disappointed.

That was probably because he’d missed Georgima’s breakfast.

No, since it’s already well past noon, that means he’s missed not only breakfast but lunch as well.

Every dish prepared by Georgima and her disciples is so delicious it could be called exquisite.

Missing out on two whole meals of that food was a major blow to Rei.

However, Miranda, unaware of Rei’s feelings, spoke up, puzzled by his shocked expression.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

“Hm? Oh, no. It’s nothing. So? Did you come to wake me up because I’m about to wake up?”

“That’s part of it, but Master is asking for you. He said if you’re awake, he’d like to see you. If you’re still asleep, he said there’s no need to force yourself… What would you like to do?”

In this case, the “Master” Miranda referred to was Duke Liberte of Kerebel.

Even though he’s being summoned by the master of this estate—and even though he’s awake—he can’t very well ignore it.

After all, Liberte isn’t just any noble—he’s Elena’s father.

“No, it’s fine. I’ll get ready right away, so please wait for me.”

Saying that, Rei quickly got dressed and left the room with Miranda.

……Incidentally, Rei checked the book that had been lying on the floor when he woke up again, but sure enough, there were no scratches or damage of any kind.

It was only natural that Rei felt relieved upon realizing this.

“Thank you for coming. Did you manage to get some sleep last night?”

In the room Miranda led him to, besides Liberte, there were Elena and Ara—the two people involved in yesterday’s incident—as well as Liberte’s wife, Arcadia.

Perhaps Yellow, Elena’s familiar, wasn’t there because he disliked formal conversations.

Rei sat down on the sofa Liberte had offered him, nodding with a smile.

Normally, most of that smile would be hidden by the hood of his Dragon Robe… but since he had taken the hood off, his smile was clearly visible.

“Yes. …Actually, I overslept a bit; I was actually asleep until just a moment ago.”

“Huh? In that case, did I wake you up too abruptly because I asked Miranda to do so?”

“No, I was awake a little before Miranda arrived.”

“I’m glad to hear that. …Now, the reason I called Rei here again is that I wanted to hear the details of yesterday’s incident directly from him. I’ve already heard from Elena and Ara, but with matters like this, it’s best to hear multiple perspectives to accurately understand what happened.”

“Though, Ara didn’t go into the space with the flesh cocoon,” Liberte added, sounding slightly disappointed.

Seeing Liberte like that, Ara looked apologetic, but… before she could say anything, Elena spoke up.

“Father, I’m sure we’ve already discussed that matter. Besides, wasn’t it precisely because Ara was looking through the documents that we were able to uncover various things?”

The slightly reproachful tone in her voice was likely meant to defend Ara.

……In fact, after returning to the mansion last night, Rei had handed over the documents that had been inside the Misty Ring, and those documents contained various important details.

There were no documents proving the connection between Celestan and the Black Wolf that Rei and the others had been searching for, nor could they find any documents indicating that Gaiska had hired the Black Wolf.

However, though not exactly a substitute, there were a few documents that would be of interest to Liberte as Duke Kerebel.

Specifically, documents containing information that would certainly put several aristocrats from the Noble Faction in Liberte’s crosshairs.

Although they hadn’t obtained the documents they were after, they had succeeded in acquiring documents unrelated to their original objective.

The mere fact that he was able to remove those documents from the vanished mansion was undoubtedly a significant gain.

At the very least, it was more than enough compared to the alternative of the house vanishing without him having done anything.

“Ah, yes. I don’t intend to blame Ara either. If anything, I’m grateful that she managed to bring out this many documents under those circumstances.”

“……Hearing you say that is a great relief.”

Ara, sitting next to Elena, bowed her head apologetically.

Seeing Ara like that, Liberte—who was being met with reproachful glances not only from Elena but also from Arcadia—opened his mouth, as if trying to hide his discomfort.

“So, then. That lump of meat Rei saw? I’d like to hear about that.”

At Liberte’s words, Rei nodded and began to recount his experience.

Unlike Elena’s explanation, his wasn’t particularly easy to follow, but Elena filled in the gaps where Rei’s account fell short.

“Hmm. The Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, is it? I knew Rei could use that skill, but hearing about it from someone who’s actually used it—I must say, it’s impressive.”

Liberte’s words surprised Rei, if only slightly.

However, given his position as Duke of Kerebel, it wasn’t surprising that he knew about the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, which could be considered Rei’s trump card.

Especially since he used it while fighting alongside Elena; it wouldn’t be strange for information to have leaked out from there.

Although it was originally treated as a trump card, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour wasn’t exactly a skill he kept hidden.

……In the first place, if it were a skill that needed to be kept secret, one should avoid using it in public as much as possible, but Rei didn’t seem to be too concerned about that.

“Should I just say, ‘Thank you’ for that?”

“I don’t know. …Anyway, I understand what you’re saying, Rei. I’ve dragged you into quite a mess with this whole situation.”

“No. In this case, I’d say I’m the one who dragged you into it. I never would have thought Gaiska would go so far as to hire the Black Wolf.”

After chatting for a few minutes, Liberte got down to business.

“Well then, let’s get to the main reason I called you here, Rei.”

A slightly serious expression appeared on Rei’s face.

This was because he was wondering what she was going to say.

Right where Rei was looking, Liberte rang a small bell.

Less than ten seconds later, there was a knock at the door.

“Master, I’ve brought what you asked for.”

“Hmm, come in.”

With those words, the door opened, and the butler appeared.

Rei thought for a moment that he’d seen the man somewhere before, then remembered that he was the man who had been invited to Liberte’s table at the end-of-year party.

He was considered one of the most capable servants in the household of the Duke of Kerebel.

Following closely behind him, two men entered the room carrying wooden barrels.

They were men who looked incredibly strong—one might even describe them as muscular—but their faces were quite red.

It was clear just by looking at them that they were giving it their all, even under the current circumstances.

Despite their condition, the two men placed the wooden barrels on the floor slowly and carefully, taking care not to scratch the floor.

Rei didn’t know exactly how far they had carried the wooden barrels, but judging by their appearance, there was no doubt they had come a considerable distance.

(A cart with wheels… the kind used to deliver tea? If they’d put it in one of those… Ah, no. Judging by the look of it, it seems pretty heavy, so if they tried to carry it that way, the cart would probably break. Or even if it didn’t break, the weight would likely leave tire marks on the floor.)

As Rei gazed at the wooden barrel the two men had carried in, Liberte smiled and spoke.

“Rei, this is your reward.”

“Reward?”

For a moment, Rei didn’t understand what she was talking about.

He felt strongly that storming into Celestan’s house had been done strictly for his own sake.

There was no reason for him to receive a reward...

Seeing Rei’s expression, Liberte realized he had misunderstood and spoke up before Rei could say anything.

“Just so you know, this Fuma Ore has absolutely nothing to do with yesterday’s incident. …Don’t you remember me saying I’d pay you a reward during the mock battle?”

“……Ah, now that you mention it…”

After pondering Liberte’s words for a moment, Rei finally remembered.

Come to think of it, they had promised to give him Fuma Ore as a reward.

Seeing Rei finally recall the promise thanks to her words, Liberte wore a slightly exasperated expression.

Two wooden barrels’ worth of Fuma Ore… that’s a sum that would amount to a considerable amount of money if converted to cash.

To Liberte, Duke of Kerebel, that amount of money wasn’t all that significant, but for an ordinary adventurer, it wasn’t quite a “strike it rich” kind of sum… but it wouldn’t be out of the question for it to be worth a “strike it hundred” kind of amount.

That said, to Rei, Liberte was not only his client but also Elena’s father.

On top of that, he’s the one who’s gone out of his way to help him in various ways, so to be honest, as far as the Fuma ore reward was concerned, he wouldn’t have minded if it hadn’t been there.

Still, the fact that he went to such lengths—and prepared even more Fuma Ore than Rei had expected—made him feel a certain fondness for Liberte.

That said, Liberte isn’t doing this out of pure goodwill.

Even setting aside the fact that he’s his daughter’s boyfriend, it’s always better to maintain friendly relations with an adventurer of renown.

That is precisely why, fully understanding the expense involved, she went ahead and prepared the Fuma Ore.

“It seems you’d forgotten, but… a promise is a promise. Please accept this properly.”

Hearing that, Rei couldn’t very well refuse.

It was true that he had forgotten about the Fuma Ore incident, but once he remembered it, it was also true that he naturally found this Fuma Ore appealing.

A secret weapon different from the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour… no, considering that the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour is virtually unknown, the Fire Whirlwind is generally regarded as Rei’s secret weapon or trump card.

What would happen if he used the Fuma Ore for that?

Rei was tempted to try it himself, but even though there were two in the wooden barrel, using them would naturally deplete the supply.

Given that, he couldn’t afford to waste them.

(Still, I’d need to actually try it at least once to see how it reacts. Even though I have time, I’d like to avoid using it in actual combat right off the bat.)

It was only natural that she wanted to confirm exactly what kind of... well, what might be called an “additional effect” the Fuma Ore would produce.

“Thank you. Well then, I’ll gladly accept these.”

Saying that, I stood up from the sofa I was sitting on and reached for the two wooden barrels on the floor.

The next moment, the wooden barrels vanished in an instant, so completely that it felt as though they had been nothing more than a dream or an illusion.

Seeing this, the men who had brought the wooden barrels looked dumbfounded.

The wooden barrels filled with Fuma ore, which they had gone to such great lengths to bring here, had vanished in the next instant.

It would have been impossible not to be surprised by that.
