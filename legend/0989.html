The longsword held by the kobold before me and the hammer wielded by the red cyclops.

They were completely different in form as weapons.

The longsword is a weapon for slicing through enemies, while the hammer is a weapon for smashing them.

Yet for some reason, Rei couldn't explain why, but seeing the kobold's longsword made him think of the hammer the red cyclops had wielded.

"WAAAAAARGH!"

The kobold with the longsword let out a high-pitched howl, and the other kobolds instantly turned to face Rei and his companions.

There were about twenty of them visible, but that was only the ones in plain sight. The sounds of movement coming from the surrounding thickets made it clear there were definitely more kobolds lurking out of sight.

Kobolds were pack-forming monsters to begin with, so while this group was large, it wasn't extremely large.

"What's this? Kobolds think they can take us on? Just because there are a few more of them, doesn't make this any less reckless, you know?"

Mireine spoke up, long sword in hand, and her words were far from exaggerated.

Almost everyone present, excluding the two who had fled earlier, possessed strength on par with Rank C adventurers.

Furthermore, there were those with even higher combat prowess like Rei and Vihera, and above all, there was the griffin, Set... But as she thought this, Mireine suddenly felt something was off.

(Normally, monsters wouldn't attack when Set-chan is here. It'd be one thing if they were goblins who couldn't gauge their opponent's strength, but kobolds have slightly higher combat power than goblins and far greater intelligence. ...Even if they were chasing adventurers, why would they charge right into a place where Set-chan is?)

Several others shared Rei's confusion, but now wasn't the time for that.

The reason was unclear, but the fact remained: they were now actually facing a group of kobolds.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAGH!"

The leader-like kobold wielding a longsword bellowed, and the others immediately launched their attack toward Rei and the others.

Armed with spears and longswords, they charged with the swiftness and sharp attacks characteristic of kobolds, far outpacing goblins.

"That doesn't mean you little kobolds can get cocky!"

Rei dodged the spear thrust at his chest by sidestepping, then thrust out his own thorn spear.

The act of thrusting a spear was the same as the kobold's, but its sharpness was so different it was hard to believe they were using the same weapon for the same action.

By the time the kobold tried to pull back the spear it had thrust, Rei's thorn spear had already shattered its skull.

...Yes, not pierced, but crushed.

The kobold's head scattered around the area.

The other kobolds behind the one killed instantly by Rei took the full brunt of their comrade's blood, flesh, bones, and brain matter right in the face.

This instantly robbed them of sight, while the stench of blood simultaneously overwhelmed their sense of smell.

The confused, frozen kobolds were met by Rei's thrust.

The thorn spear's magic item property involved entangling its victims with thorns generated from the spear itself, immobilizing them while inflicting pain with the thorns' barbs. Naturally, its power as a conventional spear was also formidable.

Rei had defeated the first kobold, and the three kobolds behind it had their heads crushed cleanly by the spear's strike, collapsing to the ground.

"Well, they were just kobolds,"

Rei muttered, watching the kobolds retreating from him after the exchange before reassessing his surroundings.

The area wasn't particularly large, but battles with kobolds were raging everywhere.

However, everyone present here was reasonably skilled, and in every fight, the kobolds were being overwhelmingly pushed back.

Archers stationed near the pile of Cyclops materials and Sulnin, wielding magic, protected the two pursued adventurers while firing arrows and spells to keep the kobolds at bay.

"This is definitely our victory... Hey!"

Victory seemed assured. But then, in the direction of Rei's gaze, an unmissable sight caught his eye.

Several kobolds were lunging their fangs toward the cyclops hanging from a branch, presumably to bleed it out.

Of course, the kobolds weren't trying to attack the cyclops.

After all, it was already dead, so there was no point in attacking it.

In other words, what the kobolds were trying to do now was probably just eat the meat, since the cyclops's corpse happened to be right where they'd attacked.

"How audacious!"

Several kobolds were fighting others near where the cyclops hung, creating an obstacle.

Judging that the cyclops would suffer damage if this continued, Rei kicked off the ground and leapt, activating the Sleipnir Boots.

He moved, leaping over the fighters battling the kobolds, one step, two steps, three steps.

From directly above the kobold about to sink its fangs into the cyclops's corpse, Rei descended, brandishing his thorn spear.

The sensation of tearing skin, ripping flesh, and shattering bone traveled through his hands. Landing, Rei immediately swung the thorn spear horizontally.

Despite the spear piercing clean through from the head, through the torso, and out the back near the waist, it posed no hindrance to Rei's swing.

The horizontal swing caused the corpse of the kobold impaled on the thorn spear to be dislodged from the tip and shaft, flying away.

The kobolds attempting to bite the cyclops were knocked off balance by their fallen comrade's body and couldn't move.

"Didn't anyone teach you not to snack on others...?"

Rei thrust his spear toward the kobolds who had been pinned to the ground by their fallen comrades.

Had he aimed for their torsos, they might have used the flung corpse as a shield of flesh.

But Rei aimed for the head.

The thrust, unleashed in an instant, was entirely deep green from tip to shaft, appearing as a flash of green light to those who witnessed it.

The kobolds whose heads were destroyed died instantly, of course, expiring with their comrades' corpses still piled atop them.

Glancing at the kobold corpses, Rei turned his gaze back to the battlefield.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

With a roar, a kobold was blown out of the bushes.

...Or more accurately, the corpse of a kobold missing its head and upper body.

Hearing that cry now, there was no need to wonder who had done this.

"Well then, it's about time..."

"WAAAAAAAH!"

Is the battle over?

The scream cut off Rei's words as he tried to continue.

If that scream had been a kobold's, it wouldn't have been particularly strange, but this scream was human... the scream of the former guerrilla fighter.

Instinctively turning toward the source of the scream, Rei could scarcely believe his eyes.

Flames were erupting from the blade the kobold swung.

And a man engulfed in flames was rolling on the ground.

"Haaah!"

Rei instinctively tried to rush toward the man, but Vihera moved first.

Approaching the man writhing in agony, engulfed in flames, Vihera kicked him away.

Of course, it wasn't to pursue him... but rather a strike to prevent him from endangering others while still engulfed in flames.

And where he was kicked landed was the river.

Thanks to Vihera's quick thinking, the man's body was doused by the river water, extinguishing the flames.

"A kobold wielding a Magic Sword? And using it effectively? No way!"

The one shouting was a former member of the Roving Squad.

He must have witnessed his comrade engulfed in flames just now.

"Th-that one! That one's the kobold leader! It's his fault we had to run!"

One of the adventurers who had initially been chased by the kobolds and ended up here shouted.

The expression on the man's face was one of terror. And rage against the injustice.

For these two adventurers, the kobold extermination request wasn't particularly difficult given their abilities.

But among the kobold horde they fought, one wielded a Magic Sword.

Obtaining a Magic Sword is difficult even for humans.

They wondered why a kobold possessed the Magic Sword, deciding instead it was a prime opportunity to seize it.

Initially, they hadn't perceived the kobold wielding the Magic Sword as particularly threatening.

After all, using any magic item, not just the Magic Sword, requires magical power.

But ordinary kobolds, unlike higher-tier beings like kobold mages, lack the ability to wield magic effectively.

...Or so it should have been.

Yet somehow, that kobold wielded the Magic Sword with skill. The two adventurers, realizing they couldn't overcome its power, fled... The kobolds pursued them, leading to this encounter with Rei and the others.

"A kobold wielding a Magic Sword, huh? ...This sounds rather interesting. Might I take this particular kobold for myself?"

While Rei thought, If there was a kobold with a Magic Sword, you should've said so from the start, it was Vihera who spoke up, wearing an amused smile.

The battle with the Cyclops had been reasonably entertaining, but she'd been somewhat disappointed by an opponent capable only of crude fighting tactics.

But this kobold before her, wielding a Magic Sword? That might offer a bit more of a challenge.

No one could stop Vihera, who harbored such thoughts.

If anyone could, it would be Rei, but even he didn't feel any particular need to stop her.

(A Rank C Cyclops versus a Rank E Kobold... No matter how you look at it, I can't see the Kobold being any more worthwhile to fight than the Cyclops.)

He glanced at Vihera facing the kobold and thought this, but he remained silent, knowing Vihera wouldn't be convinced until she actually fought it.

And so, silent, he swung his thorn spear, taking down one kobold after another.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAGH!"

A kobold wielding a Magic Sword let out a loud cry as it swung that blade.

Flames erupted from the blade of the Magic Sword.

A ball of flame about thirty centimeters in diameter hurtled straight toward Vihera.

But while such an attack might have been effective if it had caught him off guard like before, once he'd seen it coming, it stood no chance of working.

Advancing while dodging the fireball, Vihera had the kobold wielding the Magic Sword within her striking range the very next instant.

Not the Demon-Infused Palm... but a purely swung fist delivered a swift blow to the kobold's face.

Prioritizing speed, the attack looked to Rei's eyes like a jab in boxing.

He unleashed such rapid strikes in succession with both arms.

"Gyaaah! Waaah, Waaooon!"

The kobold that took the first hit swung its Magic Sword, desperately trying to counterattack.

But the fireball it unleashed flew off course, threatening to damage the surrounding trees, the river, or worse, its own kobold comrades.

Of course, some did come toward Rei and the others, but most present here were seasoned fighters.

They weren't slow enough to get hit by a fireball they'd seen before. ...Though the fireballs' lack of speed certainly helped.

"Gyaaah! Gyaaah! Gyaaah!"

The kobold tried desperately to counterattack, but the speed of the Magic Sword and Vihera's fists were overwhelmingly faster.

He kept swinging his fists relentlessly, and when the kobold's resistance ceased, he delivered a kick.

"Gyaaaaaaah!"

Screaming louder than ever before, the kobold was sent flying, its body slamming into the trunk of a nearby tree.

The one wielding the Magic Sword who led the goblins was finished too... or so everyone except Rei would have thought.

After all, unlike yesterday's cyclops, nearly all the kobolds that had attacked were already dead.

But among them, only Rei kept his gaze fixed on the kobold blown away by Vihera.

The reason was undoubtedly the Magic Sword the kobold had been holding.

While its size differed from the red cyclops' sword yesterday—small enough for a normal human to wield—the very fact that a creature like a kobold possessed a Magic Sword was absurd.

It wasn't impossible for a kobold to acquire a Magic Sword.

It's possible one attacked and killed a careless adventurer who happened to carry a Magic Sword, or found the corpse of an adventurer who possessed a Magic Sword.

But if you asked whether a kobold could actually wield the Magic Sword effectively, few would agree.

Especially if that kobold isn't some higher-tier or rare species, but just a regular one.

And then... Rei's concern proved accurate.

"Waooh, Waooon, Gyaaah! Kyauuuuun!"

The kobold, slammed against the tree trunk, struggled to rise before suddenly letting out a shriek.

This was not the confident kobold that had first appeared.

Screaming like that, the kobold desperately tried to release the Magic Sword from its grasp.

But no matter how many times it shook its hand, the Magic Sword wouldn't budge.

Because root-like tendrils had spread from the handle of the Magic Sword into the kobold's hand, up its arm, through its torso, and into its entire body.

"...I knew it."

Rei murmurs at the vaguely familiar sight, taking up the thorn spear and standing beside Vihera.
