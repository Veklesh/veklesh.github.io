On the night they dismantled the Garangiga, the Kelpie, and the Argos Bear… Rei had come to a forest some distance from Golz with Elena, Marina, and Vihera.

Yellow wasn’t there because he was asleep in bed with Byune.

It seemed that during the free time while they were dismantling the Galangiga, Vihera had put him through a rigorous combat training regimen, because the moment he finished eating and returned to his room, he collapsed onto the bed, hugging Yellow.

Actually, considering his eyes were already starting to close during the meal, that’s proof of just how rigorous Vihera’s training must have been.

That said, since Rei couldn’t very well let Byune find out about his Beast Magic, it worked out quite conveniently for him.

To be precise, since they’d been dissecting the creature today, he’d judged that the absorption of the magic stone would take place tonight, so his goal was to tire Byune out.

And since he was at it, he had Vihera train Byune… which led to the situation they were in now.

“Speaking of which, what happened to Relieu? I didn’t see her.”

Under the autumn moon—bathed in the brilliant light of the moon shining brightly in a cloudless night sky—Vihera asked.

“Relieu went out to a tavern. Apparently, someone he met told him about a place that serves great sake. He met them while he was buying souvenirs.”

“Hmm. I think Relieu’ll be fine, though.”

Going to a tavern recommended by a stranger and being charged exorbitant prices, or being drugged and sold off.

Under normal circumstances, one might have some reason to worry about such things… but given that the person in question was Relieu, no one present felt the need to worry.

If anyone tried pulling a stunt like that on a Rank A adventurer with a nickname, the only one who’d end up regretting it would be the fool who did it.

In the worst-case scenario, Golz might have been drenched in blood, but Rei’s honest thought was that if that happened, he’d want the fool who pulled such a stunt to regret it.

“Well, with that settled, there’s no need to worry about Relieu or Byune, so let’s get to the Beast Magic you’ve all been waiting for.”

“Grrrrrrrrrr!”

Set’s joyful roar echoed through the moonlit night.

……Some nocturnal animals, birds, and monsters, hearing that roar, were frightened and fled the scene… but for now, Rei and the others decided not to worry about it.

Then, Rei took out three magic stones.

Two for Kelpie and one for Garangiga.

As for the Argos Bear that Set had defeated, it no longer existed, having already been used by Set to learn Level 2 Earth Arrow.

“Since the Galangiga’s magic stone is the main prize today, we’ll start with the Kelpie. …Set.”

In front of Elena and the others, Rei tossed the magic stone to Set.

Set catches it in his beak and swallows it.

[Set has learned the skill "Ice Arrow Lv. 4"]

The announcement message echoes in Rei and Set’s minds.

"What a pity! Wait, why Ice Arrow on a Kelpie? I was totally expecting Water Ball to level up."

"Grr..."

Perhaps agreeing with Rei’s words, Set lets out a disappointed whine.

Of course, it’s not that he isn’t happy that Ice Arrow has reached Level 4.

After all, Ice Arrow is a skill that fires an ice arrow—though “icicle” would be a more accurate description.

For that reason alone, its power is undoubtedly high.

But… even so, it wouldn’t be unreasonable to wonder just how much stronger Water Ball would become if it reached Level 6, given that it had already been significantly strengthened when it reached Level 5.

Rei felt a twinge of disappointment at this, but he quickly shifted his mindset.

After all, with Ice Arrow currently at level 4, that means if it levels up just one more time, it will reach level 5 and become significantly stronger.

“Set, try out Ice Arrow.”

“Grrrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a high-pitched cry and activated Ice Arrow.

As a result, a total of twenty ice arrows appeared behind Set.

Considering there were fifteen at Level 3, it seems that for every level increase, five more ice arrows are added.

“In that case… Set, use Earth Arrow.”

"Grr? Grrrrrrrrr!"

As expected, ten Earth Arrows were created.

Since there were five at Level 1, there were ten at Level 2.

Given that, and considering the other arrow-type skills, it’ll probably be fifteen at Level 3, twenty at Level 4… something like that.

"Got it. Thanks. Just shoot them whenever you like."

"Grrr!"

At Rei’s words, the Earth Arrow flew off in the wrong direction, just like the Ice Arrow had.

Watching it fly off... Rei suddenly wondered, just as he had with the Ice Arrow earlier, "I hope that doesn't hit anyone, does it?"

“Both Ice Arrows and Earth Arrows have their uses, don’t they?”

Marina, who had been watching the exchange, spoke up, and Elena and Vihera seemed to agree. They nodded in response.

“That’s true. I won’t deny that. But setting Earth Arrow aside, doesn’t Kelpie give off more of a ‘water’ vibe than ‘ice’?”

“Hmm, yeah. Now that you mention it, that’s true… It doesn’t quite fit the image as well as, say, a Kelpie’s magic stone enhancing Fire Breath.”

“...If that were to happen, though, it might actually be kind of interesting.”

Generally speaking, the skills you can learn through Beast Magic are often related to the specific monster in question.

I say “often” rather than “always” because there have been times when I’ve learned skills that seemed completely unrelated.

Of course, in those cases, it’s likely that the connection was simply unknown, and the skill was actually well-suited to that monster… that’s the most probable explanation.

Even so, the idea that a Kelpie’s magic stone could grant Fire Breath was far more unexpected than just a little bit for Rei.

“Anyway, if that’s the case… that Kelpie must have been a rare species or something. …In any case, it’s my turn next. Now then, will I be able to learn a skill, or not? And if I do, what kind of skill will it be…”

Muttering that, he tossed the magic stone with anticipation and sliced it in half with the Death Scythe.

[Death Scythe has acquired the skill 'Corrosion Lv. 5']

The announcement message echoed in his mind.

It played, but...

"Um... Huh? Eh... Hmm? Why? Wait, isn't that actually the right attribute?"

“What’s wrong? From the look of it, it seems like you’ve acquired a pretty strange skill.”

Elena, puzzled by Rei’s reaction, asks curiously.

“Weird? Hmm, is it weird? The skill I learned is supposed to corrode the opponent’s metal equipment. …But why is it corroding with a Kelpie’s magic stone?”

“Corrosion, huh. Now that you mention the Water attribute, I suppose so?”

“Well, I suppose it’s more water-attribute than fire or wind. Though earth-attribute might be suitable for corrosion, too.”

At Marina’s words, everyone who had been listening—even Set himself—tilted their heads in confusion.

It’s not that strange to associate water or earth with corrosion.

If that’s the case, wouldn’t it make sense for the Kelpie’s magic stone to increase the corrosion level?

“Even if we try to check, it’s a bit hard to test. Since it’s reached level five, it’s definitely gotten a lot more powerful, though.”

“For now, why don’t you try attacking that rock or something? If its ability to corrode metal equipment has improved, there’s a chance it could corrode rocks too, right?”

“Rocks getting corroded? What kind of situation is that?”

Even though I thought that, there was no need to hesitate about giving it a try.

After all, with the Death Scythe, destroying a rock wouldn’t damage the blade.

Gripping the Death Scythe, I moved in front of the rock...

"Corrosion"

I activated the skill and swung it lightly at the rock.

...If I swung with full force, given the Death Scythe and Rei’s skill, it would be easy to cut through even a rock, so that’s only natural.

And just as the blade lightly touches the rock, I pull the Death Scythe back...

“Whoa!”

Seeing the rock melting, a cry of surprise escaped Rei’s lips.

“I can’t believe corrosion can actually melt rock… Actually, shouldn’t this skill be called ‘Melting’ or something like that, rather than ‘Corrosion’?”

Seeing Rei’s reaction, Marina—who had told him to try it on the rock—was the one who was actually surprised.

“I can’t believe it actually corrodes the rock… I guess it pays to ask. What about wood?”

Next, Marina set her sights on a fallen tree lying nearby.

Following Marina’s instructions, I approached the fallen tree.

While he had been somewhat skeptical with the rock, the corrosion effect had indeed worked on it.

Given that, it wasn’t unreasonable for Rei to think that it might work on wood as well.

It also seemed to Rei that the corrosion effect would have more practical uses on wood than on rock.

After all, there are quite a few people who use clubs as weapons.

So, he used the Corrosion skill again, and as the blade touched the fallen tree... just as Rei had predicted, the tree began to corrode before his very eyes.

“This… isn’t this actually turning out to be a pretty powerful skill?”

Rei muttered, gazing at the melted fallen tree.

Until now, the Corrosion skill had only been effective on metal.

On top of that, attacking with the Death Scythe was overwhelmingly faster and more powerful than using the Corrosion skill to destroy an enemy’s weapon.

Not to mention, naturally, there was the drawback that using Corrosion rendered the weapon useless.

That’s why, even after reaching Level 4, I’d hardly ever had a chance to use Corrosion… but if it works on things other than metal, that changes everything.

(It’s highly likely to be just as effective on an opponent’s body or other areas, not just their weapon. If that’s the case… the Corrosion skill has really come into its own.)

It’s likely that I won’t know how practical it is in actual combat until I try it out, but there’s no doubt it’s a skill worth looking forward to.

The others watching likely shared Rei’s expectations.

They gazed at the fallen tree corroded by Death Scythe, their expressions filled with admiration.

“The remaining question is just how useful it will actually be in combat…”

Rei nodded in agreement at those words from Elena.

Even if it seems quite usable in a test like this, there’s a good chance it won’t be very effective when actually used in combat.

That possibility certainly exists.

“Well, I’ll test that out next time I fight a monster or something. …Alright, that’s good enough for the corrosion part. On to the next one.”

Saying that, he sheathed the Death Scythe in the Misty Ring, and the next item he pulled out was the Garangiga magic stone.

Yes, that was the very reason he had gone out of his way to leave Golz at night today. It was, quite literally, the main course.

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set growled happily.

He probably understood that Rei had said this magic stone was meant for Set.

While his greatest hope lay in the Shock Magic Eye, Garangiga possessed several other powerful skills as well—such as venom, Water Cutter, and the ability to grow spikes from its body.

If that were the case, what would happen if the Death Scythe were to acquire those skills?

It would be a lie to say I wasn’t curious about that.

(A corrosive Water Cutter, for instance, sounds kind of interesting. Or maybe having Death Scythe sprout thorns from its blade or hilt whenever it wants would be cool, too.)

Even so, I understood from my battle with Garangiga that the Shock Demon Eye is an extremely powerful skill, far surpassing those others.

In Garangiga’s case, since there was an activation process involving the opening of the third eye on its forehead, it was possible to gauge the timing of its activation to some extent.

But Set’s “Shock Magic Eye” doesn’t require such a process. …At least, not at Level 1.

And since there’s no such process at Level 1, based on past experience, I can predict that the skill will continue to strengthen in that state all the way up to Level 5.

The only thing Rei is worried about is... whether a third eye might appear on his forehead like Garangiga’s, but Rei’s prediction—and perhaps wishful thinking—is that this likely won’t happen. And then...

[Set has acquired the skill "Shock Magic Eye Lv. 2"]

That announcement plays in his mind.

Afterward, he tried out the Shock Demon Eye… Fortunately, no third eye opened, and it turned out that, just as Rei had predicted, the skill had simply been enhanced. Rei quietly let out a sigh of relief.
