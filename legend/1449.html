"This... I see."

Following Daskar's suggestion, Elena arrived at the site of the expansion work. Stepping outside from where the wall once stood, she wore a look of understanding.

The reason Daskar had suggested she come see this place became clear.

The construction site was now filled with a large number of people.

As far as the eye could see, there were nearly hundreds... perhaps even over a thousand people working.

But... among them, one figure stood out distinctly.

And for Elena, that person was the most important person to her. So, in a way, it was only natural that her gaze was drawn to them first upon arriving here.

"Is that... Rei?"

Beside Elena, Ara also gasped in surprise upon seeing Rei.

No, it wasn't just seeing Rei that made her gasp.

After all, she had seen Rei countless times before.

But... seeing the moment he thrust the stone tip of the Death Scythe—one of his symbols—into the ground, causing the earth itself to writhe, it was only natural to let out a cry of surprise.

The Rei Ara knew was, of course, a supremely skilled... no, supremely skilled was an understatement—a wizard of unparalleled ability.

But even so, the only magic Ara associated with Rei was fire magic.

Of course, she had seen him use various other spells before, starting with wind magic—Flying Slash—but even those couldn't match the flashiness of his fire magic.

Yet the magic Rei had just used, even as earth magic, was incredibly spectacular.

The very earth itself writhed... Ara hadn't known he could wield magic like that.

Then again, it wasn't like Rei had shown Ara his entire hand, so it wasn't really surprising.

"Elena? It's Elena, right?"

Elena had been watching Rei cause the ground to sink, but upon hearing a familiar voice, she turned her gaze in that direction.

There stood Marina, dressed in a party dress that exposed her chest and back significantly more than the others, despite being at the construction site.

...Though, to be fair, the workers on the expansion site included professional staff, day laborers, adventurers... all sorts of people gathered together, so not everyone was wearing uniform attire.

"Hmm. It hasn't been that long, has it? We've spoken many times through the Paired Orbs."

Elena said this to Marina, who approached with a smile.

But Marina looked utterly shocked, as if wondering why Elena was here at all.

"You could have at least said you were coming. ...But if Elena is here, then what's been appearing in the Paired Orbs these past few days..."

"Inside the carriage, right?"

Normally, if the interior of a carriage was being projected in the Paired Orb, one would notice.

But Elena's carriage, thanks to spatial magic, had an interior more solidly built than your average inn room—a room fit for nobility... indeed, one that wouldn't look out of place for a member of the duke's household.

That's precisely why, even if the Paired Orb showed a bit of the room, it probably didn't seem particularly odd.

"Heh heh."

Seeing Marina's surprised expression, Elena wore a satisfied smile.

Beside Elena, Ara looked down, her face full of apology.

"...Well, never mind. More importantly, go show yourself to Rei. He'll be delighted."

"Hmm. I suppose I should... Huh?"

Elena nodded at Marina's words, but then suddenly turned her gaze toward Rei and tilted her head slightly.

As she did, her golden hair reflected the summer sunlight, causing those around her—including Elena and Marina—who were mesmerized by her to squint their eyes in dazzlement. Yet, the person causing it seemed completely oblivious.

They were captivated by the sight of the excited old man beside Rei.

"Marina, who exactly is that man?"

"Huh? ...Oh."

Marina followed Elena's gaze and immediately understood upon seeing Marz excitedly talking to Rei.

"Oh, Marz. He's sort of an authority on earth magic... well, maybe not quite, but he's a famous mage around here as an earth magic user. As you can see, he's incredibly curious."

"...He's quite unusual."

It was not Elena but Ara who murmured this in response to Marina's words.

Rei was fundamentally bad at socializing.

Because of that, he inevitably had many adversaries, and few he could truly call allies.

Of course, starting with Ara and the others, it's not like he has none.

Even so, it was quite rare for someone to become friendly with Rei in a situation close to a first meeting.

"Looks like she took a liking to Rei's magic at first sight,"

To be precise, the terrain manipulation Rei uses isn't magic, but a Death Scythe skill.

But only Elena and Marina know that here; it's a secret even Ara, Elena's best friend and confidante, doesn't know.

For that very reason, Marina pretends Rei is using magic.

Elena briefly returned her gaze to Marina, but finding no particular objection in Marina's attitude, she turned her attention back to Marz... or rather, to Rei, who was being addressed by Marz.

Elena stared intently at Rei for about ten seconds.

Suddenly, Rei, who had been talking with Marz, lifted his gaze... and met Elena's eyes.

At first, Rei stared back at Elena as if he were dreaming, but after several seconds passed, his eyes widened beneath the hood of his Dragon Robe.

He must have realized that the person standing there was the real Elena.

Unnoticing Marz's attempts to speak to him, Rei took one step, then two.

He continued walking for a few more steps, and it didn't take long before his pace quickened into a brisk walk, then a full-fledged run.

Seeing Rei rapidly approaching, Ara took a few steps back.

She decided she didn't want to interrupt Elena and Rei's reunion.

「Elena！」

Calling out, Rei stopped in front of Elena, pulled her body close, and embraced her.

Elena, too, embraced Rei's body back.

As the Duke's daughter... and as a key figure in the Noble Faction known by the nickname "Princess General," she knew this was clearly the wrong thing to do.

But as a woman who loved Rei, it was the only right thing to do.

「Rei……」

Seeing Rei after so long, Elena could only utter those words, holding him tightly as if to confirm he was truly there.

It was no wonder the sight drew attention—the radiant, peerless beauty that was Elena embracing a man.

Moreover, the nickname "Princess General" was quite famous.

Naturally, many had seen her on the battlefield and elsewhere.

Especially now, with more people than usual gathered in Gilm.

"Hey... wait. If I'm not seeing things, isn't that the Princess General hugging Rei over there?"

"...Yeah. I thought maybe the heat was giving me hallucinations, but looks like it's not just my imagination."

"Rei and the Princess General? What kind of relationship is that?"

"Well, what kind of relationship? You can tell just by seeing them like that, can't you?"

"Well... I guess so..."

"How lovely."

"It might be lovely, but is this something we should be seeing? Couldn't this be... a bit awkward?"

It's bad.

The people surrounding the woman who said that all nodded in agreement at the word "unseemly."

The Princess General was practically the symbol of the Noble Faction.

And yet, this Princess General was embracing a mere adventurer... and worse, Rei, rumored to be the right-hand man of the Neutral Faction Daskar.

It was absolutely not something that could be made public.

But... while it's not something that can be made public, the fact that they were embracing in public is undeniable.

It was certain this incident would soon spread as rumor.

Some would spread it for amusement, others out of malice toward Rei, twisting the story as they went. Still others would be here gathering information to report back to their employers... The reasons and the people doing it would vary, but the rumor spreading was inevitable.

Especially given Rei's personality... and above all, the fact that he had exclusive access to women like Marina and Vihera – women who, while different types, were equally peerless beauties on par with Elena. There were certainly many who felt jealousy.

Marina approached Rei and Elena, who were soon locked in a tight embrace, creating their own private world—though she probably wasn't reading the room.

Marina would have preferred to leave them be, but if things continued like this, they might get carried away—moving beyond just embracing to kissing.

They were already drawing attention just by hugging like that, so she decided it was best to stop them before things went further.

(Well, if they want time alone, my place would be fine too.)

Thinking this, she lightly clapped her hands to draw the attention of Rei and Elena, who were immersed in their own world.

The sound snapped them back to reality. Elena, realizing her situation, flushed and pulled away from Rei.

"Th-that... this is just because I got a little carried away, and..."

"Alright, alright, I get it. Let's leave it at that. I'll make sure you two get your time together later. ...Anyway, we're working right now, so please wait a moment."

Marina glanced up at the sky, checking the sun's position.

It was still morning work hours, but lunch break wasn't far off.

She told them they could finish their work, take a break, and talk properly over lunch.

Rei hadn't planned on stopping work mid-task either, so while he regretted not being able to spend time with Elena, he nodded at Marina's words.

...Incidentally, the one most delighted by Marina's words wasn't the craftsmen who'd been forcibly stopped from working... but rather Marz, who held a strong interest in Rei's magic and the Death Scythe.

"Rei-sama, next please!"

Hearing Marut's shout from afar, Rei gave a small, resigned smile and started to leave... but then, something caught his attention, and he paused.

"Hey, Elena. What happened to Yellow?"

Yes, what had caught Rei's attention was Yellow, Elena's familiar.

Rei asked, puzzled by Yellow's absence—Yellow who should always be with Elena. Elena looked up at the sky and smiled at Rei.

"On the way to Daskar's mansion, he flew off into the sky."

"Ah... I see."

With that alone, Rei somehow understood where Yellow was.

Currently, Set was flying above Gilm.

Due to the damage to the walls, the barrier's effectiveness had been greatly reduced, so they were on alert for flying monsters.

Whether Yellow spotted Set or sensed his magic... whatever the reason, Yellow must have sensed Set's presence overhead and headed that way.

"Yellow will be back soon enough, so there's no need to worry,"

Hearing Elena say this, Rei tilted his head. "Oh?"

"Since it's been so long since he saw Set, I'd actually guess they'll probably just keep playing together forever..."

"Well..."

Now that you mention it... Elena faltered at Rei's words, and even Ara and Marina, who knew how close Yellow and Set were, agreed.

"Ah... well, even so, Set is technically the older one, so let's hope he brings Yellow back properly. Besides, he'll probably come back when he gets hungry."

Everyone around them murmured in agreement with Rei's wishful words.

"Anyway, it'll be noon soon, so just wait until then. I've got a few things to take care of myself right now."

"Hmm. I suppose I'll watch Rei work here. Fortunately... though I hesitate to call it that, I don't have anything else to do today."

In reality, if word got out that Elena had come to Gilm, countless people would surely come seeking an audience.

But for Elena, watching Rei work was far more important—ten times, a hundred times, a thousand times more important... perhaps incomparably more important than dealing with those people.

Of course, if there had been any urgent business, Elena would have prioritized that.

(Terrain manipulation, huh. It could be useful for construction like this, and effective for quickly building fortifications too.)

...Watching Rei and thinking about it wasn't always just about thinking of the one she loved.
