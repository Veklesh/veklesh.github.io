While Set fought outside, Rei and the others advanced through the underground facility.

Occasionally, traps would activate as if suddenly remembering their purpose—arrows would fly, spears would rain down from the ceiling—but knowing the traps were there, such things were no big deal for Rei and the others.

Furthermore, Byune, leading the way, was spotting and disarming traps as she went.

It could truly be called smooth sailing.

But while things were going smoothly...

"The problem is where the hell we're supposed to go."

The man they'd brought along as a guide had already been killed by an arrow from one of the traps.

As a result, Rei and the others were wandering around the underground facility without a guide.

"For now, should we just go into one of the rooms around here and gather some information?"

"Guess we have no choice. ...If only someone were here, it'd be quicker."

Vihera sighed wearily.

In truth, they had no idea which room might hold information about this underground facility.

No, they didn't even know if any information existed at all.

"Can't Marina's spirit magic do something?"

Elena, seemingly finding the idea of searching each room one by one as tedious as Vihera, asked Marina that.

"Hmm, well... It is an underground facility, so there are wind spirits here to some extent... But still, it's underground, so the wind spirits don't really listen to me very well."

"Her spirit magic had quite high adaptability, but it seems to have an unexpected weakness. Well, maybe it's not really a weakness..."

Using fire spirits underwater or water spirits in the desert is indeed difficult.

...But the fact that she didn't say it was absolutely impossible, only that it was difficult, showed Marina's skill.

"Anyway, there might be someone in the room... Let's go check. Byune, is there any room where someone might be?"

"Mm!"

At Vihera's words, Byune immediately pointed to one room.

Whether she sensed a human presence there or determined someone was in that room for some other reason,

The exact circumstances weren't clear, but finding someone was welcome news for Rei and the others.

(Unlike that guy earlier, this one's inside the facility. Could be male or female, but they definitely know the ins and outs of this place. Using them as a decoy trap is out of the question.)

Honestly, Rei felt that anyone cooperating with Jaya was essentially expendable.

Still, if it meant gaining information about this underground facility, he couldn't afford to have that person die.

With that thought—that perhaps they should be a bit more cautious next time—Rei moved to the door Byune had indicated.

(The fact that no one is wandering around the underground facility... at least not right now... must mean the traps are active. ...Though I doubt the traps are actually active on a daily basis.)

If that were the case, everyone in this underground facility should possess at least a certain level of bandit skill.

Given that the organization Jaya is an illegal, shadowy group, it wouldn't be surprising if that were the case... but still, it felt off that every single one of them would be that skilled.

So, it's more likely that they hurriedly evacuated when Rei and the others entered, or perhaps there are escape routes like the door right in front of Rei, hidden in each individual room.

(Now then, which is it?)

Thinking this, Rei knocked on the door.

The wooden door echoed with the light sound of his knock... but no one came to answer.

For a moment, Rei wondered if they were holding their breath inside. But even so, there was no sense of tension in the presence of whoever was inside.

"What's going on?"

"I don't know. Asking me that won't help."

"...Well, I suppose you're right."

Rei muttered, puzzled by the lack of response from inside. Marina, standing beside him, seemed to think he was addressing her.

Rei nodded to Marina, who said she didn't know either, then tried to open the door.

It was fortunate for Rei that it wasn't a doorknob type, but a side-opening door.

But as soon as it opened a crack, the door stopped moving and wouldn't budge any further.

"Not a lock... they're blocking it with a bar or something? ...This is troublesome."

Of course, if Rei really wanted to, breaking down the door wouldn't be that difficult.

But with so many traps already set, forcing open the room door made him hesitate a little.

Rei hesitated for less than ten seconds, but Elena, who had been watching, spoke up.

"Yellow, please."

"Kyuu!"

Fortunately, the gap Rei opened wasn't wide enough for a person to pass through, but it was easily large enough for Yellow to slip through.

Elena's plan was simple: send Yellow inside to remove whatever was preventing the door from opening.

She was also confident that Yellow possessed extremely high defense, and the traps set since entering this underground facility wouldn't be able to harm him.

Yellow, bearing Elena's hopes, slipped through the door's gap into the room.

Even though she thought it would be fine, she still had various worries.

Holding her breath, she waited for the door to open... Then, the sound of wood hitting the floor echoed around them.

After hearing that sound, Rei opened the door, and the next instant, it swung open without any resistance.

Then, from the gap in the door, Yellow appeared, fluttering his small wings.

"How about it? I did it! Pretty amazing, huh?" Yellow showed off with that attitude.

If a human did that, some might judge it as a sly gesture and feel irritated.

But in Yellow's case, perhaps due to his inherent cuteness, his mannerisms were accepted by everyone without issue.

Yellow's owner, Elena, stroked the chirping Yellow, and the group entered the room.

The hallway was wide enough, offering ample space for Rei to swing his Death Scythe freely.

But such a stunt was impossible inside the room. Rei had already stowed his Death Scythe in the Misty Ring, leaving him bare-handed.

...Though, given Rei's superhuman physical abilities, being unarmed didn't mean he was fatally weakened.

In fact, his bare fists, launched with lightning speed, surpassed the attack speed of ordinary weapons precisely because they were unarmed.

With such a dangerous weapon still in his possession, Rei and the others entered the room.

The apartment itself wasn't particularly spacious, just the entrance and a few rooms.

(Is this an apartment or a condo?)

Rei thought this as he surveyed the interior.

Back in Japan, Rei's home had been a detached house, and all the houses surrounding his home were detached houses too.

But of course, apartments and condominiums existed in the city center, and some of his friends lived in such places.

Moreover, it wasn't unusual for apartments and condominiums to appear in manga and anime, and they were frequently shown on TV.

That's precisely why Rei got that impression looking around the room.

"Anyone there?"

Calling out after entering a room is clearly not what you'd call common sense.

But then again, Rei had forcibly broken into this underground facility in the first place.

Talking about manners at this point was pointless.

"...No answer. We should probably check inside. They must know it's nearly impossible to hide from us here, given the human presence."

Vihera muttered, then proceeded deeper into the room... until she stopped, turning to face Rei and the others behind her with an expression bordering on exasperation.

What happened? Wondering, but judging from Vihera's demeanor that there was no danger, they moved to where Vihera stood.

Then... seeing it, Rei understood why Vihera had looked so exasperated.

Because there, slumped over the desk, was a man fast asleep.

There was a bed placed a little ways away in the room, yet despite that, the man had fallen asleep on the desk.

(So he fell asleep at his desk?)

Seeing the man like that, she thought something like that, but she also realized that it was probably thanks to this falling asleep that he was the only one left in this area. She couldn't really blame him for that.

"The room's quite messy too. ...Is this what they call a model of a dedicated researcher?"

Marina, who had caught up, murmured as she surveyed the room.

Papers—or reports, perhaps—were scattered everywhere, along with numerous pieces of lab equipment that looked completely useless to Rei.

"Yeah. Well, considering what he's researching, I can't exactly praise him for it."

Among the research being conducted at Jaya, the first thing that came to mind was, naturally, giants.

Of course, they were probably researching magic items and such as well, but when it came to the most impactful presence, it inevitably came down to the giants.

That said, even if they appeared to be dedicated researchers from the outside, whether one could tolerate it... Rei shook his head.

"For now, I suppose I need to wake him to hear the details."

Carefully avoiding the experimental equipment, documents, and what appeared to be reports crammed onto the floor, Rei approached the man sleeping at the desk.

(With the floor like this, how does this guy even move around the room? Well, at least nothing's on the bed, so he probably usually sleeps there.)

With that question in mind, Rei reached the side of the desk.

But even with her this close, the sleeping researcher showed no sign of waking.

Then again, he hadn't woken up even when the doorstop he'd been using to keep the door open fell, so it seemed unlikely this researcher could be roused by a mere human presence.

(Come to think of it, I used to sleep like this during high school classes too. ...I got caught immediately, though.)

Since he was sleeping with his upper body completely collapsed onto the desk, it was only natural that the teacher spotted him immediately.

So, I could only sleep like this when I finished writing on a test and had nothing else to do.

Usually, when sleeping during class, the standard method was to prop your cheek on your hand and pretend to look at your textbook while actually sleeping.

Feeling nostalgic for those days as a high school student in Japan, Rei lightly tapped the sleeping man's head in front of her.

A soft thud, not a thud, echoed around them, but the man showed no sign of waking.

(Just how exhausted was he to sleep like that? Well, I guess that's why he didn't evacuate like the others in the other rooms.)

In Rei's mind, researchers lived lives with their days and nights reversed.

And Mejogo, being centered around brothels—well, almost entirely brothels, to be precise—also operated on a reversed schedule.

Given that backdrop, it wasn't surprising that a researcher who seemed to have fallen asleep on top of that would be sound asleep at this hour.

Under normal circumstances, it would be perfectly fine to let them sleep peacefully or even carry them to bed. Unfortunately, now is not the time for such indulgences.

To get information about this underground facility... especially about the giants, we absolutely needed a source.

"Hey, wake up!"

He hit the man's head harder than before.

Not with a fist, though, but with the palm of his hand.

Instead of a thud, a sharp "POW!" echoed through the room.

"Whoa! What, what!?"

The sleeping man, apparently roused more by this blow than by sleep itself, scrambled up in a panic.

He frantically looked around... and of course, doing so inevitably led him to discover the figures of Rei and the others in the room.

Not just Rei, but Elena, Marina, Vihera, and Byune.

Yellow was being held by Elena, so she wasn't taking up that much space, but even so, with six people in this room, it naturally felt cramped.

"Who?"

Seeing Rei, the man asked, looking utterly confused about what was happening. Rei smiled broadly and spoke.

"Just a little intruder. Care to tell us your story?"
