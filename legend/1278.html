Sandrine's remark that we couldn't use large quantities of tomato sauce wasn't so strange when you thought about it.

After all, there's no point in making a large quantity of ingredients only to let them go bad.

While having some leeway is necessary, it's only to a certain extent.

Thinking about it that way, there was no room to argue with Sandrine's words.

"Huh? Wait, Sandrine? Could we maybe... just use a little bit?"

Rei accepted Sandrine's reasoning, but Rodrigo, for some reason flustered, blurted out that he really wanted to use the tomato sauce.

For Rodrigo, cooking was both his job and his hobby.

When Rei suggested trying a new dish, Rodrigo was enthusiastic about it. Yet now, Sandrine's voice called a halt.

Rodrigo was usually shy, but seeing him desperately pleading with his daughter, wondering why it had to be Rodrigo and not him... made Rei think the same thing.

"Dad? Then let me ask you... if we serve this dish called pizza, are you okay with not being able to serve any other dishes that use tomato sauce?"

"Well..."

Faced with that, Rodrigo couldn't help but hedge his words.

He was interested in the new dish, but that didn't mean he could use tomato sauce freely.

"Well, then... couldn't we get some from a shop or someone who has extra?"

"Hmm... As a preserved food... or more accurately, as a seasoning that keeps well, I think some people have a decent amount... But if you're asking if they'd give it to us, that's a tough one."

Sandrine sighed.

"The magic herbs used in tomato sauce are expensive... well, not exactly, but they're certainly not cheap. So even if someone were to give them away, I doubt they'd do it for free."

"...I see. So what do we do? Put off the pizza until next year?"

Without tomato sauce, Rei couldn't make proper pizza.

He could probably make a pizza-like thing without tomato sauce... but that would only be something for his own consumption, certainly not something he could serve in the cafeteria.

"No, even if we can't serve it on the menu right away, I think it's a good idea to start researching it now for next year. Rei, I have a bit of tomato sauce to spare. Could you help me out?"

"Oh, come on... Dad."

Sandrine looked displeased at Rodrigo's words, yet the corners of her mouth still curved slightly.

For Sandrine, this side of her father wasn't something she disliked seeing at all.

"Okay? But only a little, you hear? If you use too much and we run out of tomato sauce for cooking, I won't forgive you."

"Yeah, got it. Well then, let's head to the kitchen right away."

Rodrigo decided and invited Rei to the kitchen.

Rei had no particular objection to making pizza, so he followed Rodrigo into the kitchen.

Following the two of them, Sandrine also headed for the kitchen.

She was probably worried about how much tomato sauce would be used.

Inside the kitchen, cooking utensils used after making meat buns were left out.

Though unrelated to the buns, the oven for baking bread was also thoroughly heated, presumably for dinner.

"Rei, pizza dough is rolled out into a circle, right?"

"Yeah. Apparently, there's this showy cooking technique where you spin the dough in the air to shape it into a circle... but well, you'll get to that part once you're more experienced."

"Huh... Is that really necessary?"

"I don't know. But if it gets people's attention, some might get interested in pizza and want to try it, so I don't think it's entirely pointless."

Rei knew what pizza was and had a general idea of how to make it, but when asked how to stretch the dough, she could only tilt her head.

Logically, you'd use something like a rolling pin, but he'd seen it done on TV countless times—tossing the dough in the air to stretch it.

"Hmm, you stretch pizza dough, right? How thick should it be, exactly?"

"For a crispy type, pretty thin, and for a soft type, somewhat thicker... I think."

"So, the thinner one bakes faster, right?"

"...Probably. But you start with a thicker dough, closer to regular bread dough, right?"

Rodrigo nodded at Rei's question.

"Considering the tomato sauce, I think it's better to start with the safer option."

Rei could only nod in agreement.

Since there wasn't much tomato sauce to begin with, they wanted to avoid failure as much as possible.

(Tomato sauce... Does pizza man even have tomato sauce? It is called 'pizza' man, so it probably does, right? Tomato sauce... Ketchup? Well, I guess I'll just have to leave that part to the pros.)

Thinking about such things while looking at the steamer for the meat buns, Rei searched for ingredients.

"For now, let's just make a basic pizza... Gather whatever leftover ingredients we have available. Oh, and for the meat, ham or sausage would be best if possible."

"We have both. Which would you prefer?"

Both ham and sausage were preserved foods.

Naturally, we've stocked up on them to last until spring, and if we run out of this sort of thing, we can always buy more.

"Hmm, right. Then sausage it is."

"Understood. As for vegetables... honestly, we don't have much."

Rodrigo bowed apologetically.

But since it was winter and vegetables were scarce to begin with, and since they were testing pizza recipes using leftovers from meals, Rei had no intention of blaming Rodrigo.

"No, whatever you can manage is fine. ...Ah, but mushrooms would be nice if you have any."

Relieved by Rei's words, Rodrigo gathered the usable vegetables.

He gathered green, blue, and purple leafy greens, white root vegetables—vegetables Rei had never seen before—along with something that, for some reason, looked like a fresh bell pepper despite it being winter.

"Like I said before, pizza is a simple dish: you just spread the dough thin and round, top it with ingredients, sprinkle cheese, and bake it in the oven. ...Easier said than done, huh?"

Getting the dough thickness right, choosing and balancing the toppings, and above all, mastering the oven temperature—these things don't happen overnight.

But, whether you call it fortunate or not, this Golden Bread Inn, true to its name, specialized in homemade bread.

Rodrigo baked that bread himself, so in that sense, you could say it had an advantage over other eateries that bought their bread from bakeries.

Of course, Rei simply thought having an oven meant you could bake pizza, but the temperature for baking regular bread is around 250°C, whereas pizza requires over 400°C.

That's nearly double the temperature difference.

"Right. Well then, shall we try making one first? Rei, what are the basic toppings for pizza?"

"You spread tomato sauce on the dough, then add the toppings. Sausage is the main topping, then mushrooms and vegetables, and finally you top it off with cheese."

Rei would have preferred to use mushrooms, but unfortunately, mushrooms weren't among the available ingredients.

Well, Rei didn't even know if mushrooms existed on Elgin.

(Ah, but hadn't he eaten mushrooms in some dish before?)

While Rei tried to remember where he'd eaten a dish with mushrooms, Rodrigo swiftly moved forward with the cooking.

He rolled out the dough into a circle—though he didn't spin it in the air—and, strictly for tasting purposes, made it slightly larger than his hand. He spread tomato sauce over it and began adding the toppings.

Sausage, bell peppers, mushrooms, a bright green onion-like vegetable... and so on.

Since this was strictly a trial run, he didn't use too many ingredients.

Finally, he sprinkled finely chopped cheese on top and slid it into the oven.

"How was it? Anything you noticed?"

"Well, what can I say? I only know the basics, and I've never actually made pizza myself, so it's hard to say... But yeah, your technique was pretty quick, like you run a cafeteria or something."

"Ahaha. Thank you. Now, I just hope it bakes well."

Rodrigo was usually timid, but when it came to his work—or rather, his hobby of cooking—he became more enthusiastic than usual.

He showed none of his usual timidity, his gaze fixed intently on the oven.

Seeing his father like this, he couldn't help but smile, even though he had a resigned look on his face.

(So he's a daddy's boy, huh?)

While he was thinking that, a minute, two minutes passed... Seeing the cheese on top start to melt, Rodrigo thrust a huge spatula-like tool into the oven to pull out the pizza.

But the moment he tried to pull it out, Rodrigo's expression turned puzzled.

"This..."

"What's wrong, Dad?"

"...No, this just won't be edible like this. The cheese is melted, but the dough isn't fully baked yet."

True to his words, the pizza dough still looked half-baked... maybe even more than that.

"Did we bake it for too short a time?"

If the dough was half-baked, it obviously meant the baking time was too short.

Rei asked, holding that question in mind, but Rodrigo nodded back with a complicated expression.

"Yes, that's right. Considering the bread dough itself, I think the baking time was too short. But if we'd baked it any longer, the toppings and cheese would have burned..."

Sandrine patted Rodrigo's shoulder encouragingly as he wore a troubled look.

"It's your first time making this dish, so it's okay to mess up a little. Dad, you can do it!"

"Do you think so?"

"Exactly! Besides, thinking about how to make a delicious pizza is more like you, Dad."

Rodrigo, seemingly regaining some energy from Sandrine's words, kept his gaze fixed on the undercooked pizza as he asked Rei.

"Rei, what do you think I should do?"

"Well, like I said before, I know about cooking and I know the basic methods, but I'm no expert. ...But, let's see. Simply put, the dough was undercooked because it was too thick, right?"

"Come to think of it, you mentioned there are pizzas with thin crusts, right?"

Rodrigo asked, recalling the explanation before cooking, and Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah. ...But then, I don't know how they make the thicker crust pizzas..."

"That probably comes down to the oven temperature. If you bake it for too long at a moderate heat, it'll burn, so I imagine they bake it at a much higher temperature for a shorter time."

Despite his timid nature, Rodrigo, who ran the Golden Bread Inn restaurant on the main street, answered Rei's question without hesitation.

This quick conclusion was possible precisely because Rodrigo regularly baked bread himself, possessing skills that could rival—or even surpass—those of the local bakeries.

"High heat? ...Ah, I think I remember something like that being written."

Hearing Rodrigo's idea, Rei recalled what he'd seen on TV.

He remembered the person making pizza back then also saying that pizza was all about the heat...

"Come on! Rei, if you knew, you could have just told me from the start!"

"Well, even if you say that, I forgot about it until now."

Rei protested to Sandrine, who was puffing out her cheeks.

"Hahaha. Rei doesn't remember everything either, so Sandrine shouldn't get so mad. Besides, I think this is pretty good for us, right? Until now, we only used the oven for baking bread, but now we can use it for pizza too."

"...We basically bake all the bread before the shop opens, so you mean we'll be making pizza in the oven while the shop is open? I get Dad's thinking, but that'll probably cost us even more for firewood than before."

"That might be true, but we can just buy firewood from somewhere else, right? If we make more money from pizza and meat buns, that kind of expense won't be a big deal."

"Well, that might be true, but..."

Sandrine had plenty she wanted to say to her father, but perhaps she found something convincing in his words, because she didn't voice any further complaints.

In the end, with the tomato sauce issue too, they couldn't do much testing that day. They just heard the pizza outline and called it a day.

First, Rodrigo seemed to prioritize researching the relationship between pizza dough and heat intensity.
