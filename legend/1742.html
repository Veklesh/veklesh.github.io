When Rei returned from the bandits’ hideout, he got a bit of a scolding, but that was all.

The reason was likely that it took time to treat the women who had been captured by the illegal slave traders, and above all, the dark elf, who had suffered the most severe injuries.

After all, treatment for everyone was only completed shortly before Rei returned.

“So, what are we going to do now? We need to hand the surviving bandits over to the guards, and besides that, we can’t just abandon those women, can we?”

The women in the direction of Rei’s gaze were looking at Relieu and the bandits with frightened eyes.

……The reason they weren’t looking at Rei that way was probably because, now that he’d taken off his hood, his face was anything but masculine.

His small stature also plays a role in this situation.

That’s why, as a result, even the women who are afraid of men don’t feel such a strong aversion toward Rei.

Given the current situation, the fact that he wasn’t being recognized as a man should probably be a cause for celebration, but for Rei, it was anything but a cause for joy.

That said, the fact that most of the women didn’t seem to fear Set all that much simply because they weren’t afraid of Rei was one of the few things Rei could take comfort in.

“Don’t we have to head to the town nearby? …Was there a town nearby?”

Vihera asked Rei this because he was the only one who knew the answer.

Since the others had been traveling inside the Set Cage, Rei, who was riding on Set’s back, was the most familiar with the surrounding area.

Also, considering that Rei had ridden Set when they went to search for the bandits’ hideout, she probably figured it wouldn’t be strange if Rei had found a town nearby.

However, when asked, Rei shook his head at Vihera’s words.

That’s because, from what he could see from above, he had confirmed there were no towns or villages in this area.

“Come to think of it, it wouldn’t make sense for bandits to set up a hideout near a town or village, would it?”

“Is that so? Among the bandits I’ve defeated so far, there were some who had hideouts near towns and villages. Actually, if a town or village is nearby, it’s easy to find prey coming out of there.”

In response to Elena’s mutter, Relieu replied.

Actually, you couldn’t say that was wrong.

After all, if a hideout is right next to a town or village, it’s not hard to spot the caravans, merchants, and—in some cases—adventurers and travelers coming out of there.

But… just as it’s easy for bandits to find such people, the reverse is also true: it’s not difficult for the people of the towns and villages to find those hideouts either.

It’s the very definition of high risk, high reward.

If they were to pull a stunt like that, it would likely only last a few days at most.

“Well, from the perspective of those hunting them down, it’d be easier if their hideout were near a town or village. …But these guys are pretty cautious, so I don’t think they’d do something like that. …Right?”

At Rei’s question, the bandits hurriedly nodded.

For the bandits, Rei is nothing short of a nightmare.

After all, he’s the kind of person who would try to kill them as casually as weeding a garden—it would be impossible not to fear him.

I finally understood the true meaning of why they were feared and called “the Bandit Eater.”

“Then, where is the nearest town or village around here?”

“About half a day’s ride by carriage from here is a town called Delitoste!”

Rei nodded in understanding at the immediate shout from one of the bandits.

“So, shall we head to this town called Delitoste for now? We’ll need to decide what to do about those women over there, too.”

No one objected to that suggestion, and they immediately began preparing to depart.

Relieu, who was eager to see his wife Shumine, didn’t dare bring it up in this situation and simply got ready.

“Yikes!”

One of the women let out a short scream, likely because Relieu had single-handedly and effortlessly righted the overturned carriage.

At first glance, it was hard to believe that Relieu was capable of such a feat, but the fact that he could pull it off so effortlessly was a testament to his reputation as a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname.

The horse that had been pulling the carriage showed no signs of injury and remained calm. …Though, of course, the horse’s calmness was likely due to Set’s presence.

“Hey, Rei. What should we do with this guy? He’s an illegal slave trader, right? Should we just leave him here?”

“If we do that, there’s a chance he might turn into an undead, and it’d be better to take the body with us so we can explain the situation to the guards or whoever. …Guess that’s my job.”

Since they were moving with the women in the carriage, they certainly couldn’t put a corpse inside, so in the end, they decided to store it in Rei’s Misty Ring.

To be honest, Rei didn’t feel too good about it either… but there was no doubt that it was the best option.

So, Rei and the others used magic to burn the bandit’s body to prevent it from becoming undead, disposed of it, and set off.

Since the horses were frightened, Rei and Set ended up riding ahead of the carriage for the time being.

The surviving bandits were chained together with rope and forced to walk behind the carriage.

“If it takes about half a day by horse… looks like we’ll be spending the night in a town called Delitoste today.”

“Hmm?”

At Rei’s words, Set looked back, throat-clicking in puzzlement as if to ask, “Is that so?”

He was probably thinking that if it was only half a day, they could cover more ground if they set out right away.

Rei agreed. …However, selling the bandits as slaves would require some paperwork, and there was also the matter of dealing with illegal slave traders.

If they had to explain the situation, hand over the body… and go through all that, it would take time no matter what, and they’d likely end up in the evening, or even at night.

Given that, it was only natural to think that instead of forcing a departure from Delitoste, they should just spend the night there.

“Well, the food in Somis wasn’t exactly great, but maybe I can find some good food in Delitoste.”

“Grrrrrrrrrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a happy growl.

...Despite Set’s meow being so soft, the horse pulling a carriage quite a distance away still flinched for a moment.

That said, it was only for an instant, and hardly anyone noticed.

Among those who did notice was Relieu, sitting in the driver’s seat.

It would have been rather awkward to be the only man in a carriage full of women, and more than anything, he didn’t want his wife, Shumine, to suspect him of cheating because of some strange behavior.

That said, walking to keep up with the carriage’s pace was exhausting… so Relieu decided to volunteer to drive the carriage himself.

(It’s fine when Rei and the others travel because we have the Set Cage. But wouldn’t it be nice to have a carriage that Set could pull? Though, if it’s just for carrying luggage, we do have Rei’s item box. I wonder what he thinks about that.)

If Set is going to pull it, naturally we’ll need to build a dedicated carriage. …But having something other than a horse pull a carriage isn’t all that unusual, so it probably wouldn’t be that much trouble.

Whether Rei actually wants one is another matter.

“Ah… I’m bored.”

As for the bandits being dragged along by ropes attached to the carriage, if they’d heard Relieu say that, they would undoubtedly have voiced their discontent.

“What does ‘bored’ even mean when we’re about to be sold as slaves?”

But the fact that the surviving bandits being dragged behind the carriage didn’t hear Relieu’s muttered words from the driver’s seat was probably a stroke of luck, in the sense that it spared them the trouble.

In any case, they continued on for several hours… drawing the attention of merchants, travelers, and adventurers walking along the road as they made their way.

They stopped for a midday break, ate a meal prepared by Rei from the Misty Ring as usual, and the women who had been forcibly captured by the slave traders were surprised but delighted by this.

After all, a hot, freshly prepared meal is surely a source of vitality.

...Though, of course, the bandits were left tied up and neglected, and were given absolutely no food.

If this were Earth, it wouldn’t be strange for people to call this abuse.

But this isn’t Earth.

In fact, given that they were being escorted, withholding food was perfectly reasonable—it ensured they wouldn’t have the strength to resist or escape.

The bandits were forced to watch the freshly prepared food being eaten right in front of them, and they did demand that something be sent their way as well… but as soon as Rei told them to shut up, they couldn’t say another word.

Still, since they could rest their bodies while Rei and the others ate, this break was probably not a bad thing for the bandits either.

And so, the break ended… and they began moving again.

The bandits walked along, as if being pulled by the carriage’s pace… and after a few hours,

The town they were heading for came into view.

It was clearly smaller than Golz or Somis.

But since it was still a town, there was no particular problem as long as Rei and the others could take care of their business there.

Of course, there was no doubt that moving to a larger town would fetch a higher price when selling the bandits as slaves… but considering the distance involved, the difference in price was negligible to Rei.

Given that, it was definitely better to wrap up all the formalities here, even if it meant getting a slightly lower price.

“Ah… well, I guess this is how it goes.”

It was only natural for a small-town town, but upon seeing the guards’ shock and fear at the sight of Set, Rei muttered under his breath.

Still, the fact that they hadn’t run away made it immediately clear that they were serious about their duties as guards.

“It’s okay. Calm down. As you can see from the fact that I’m riding him, he doesn’t attack people on his own.”

To be precise, it would attack anyone who approached with hostility, but Rei told him that calming the guard down was the priority for now.

Hearing Rei’s voice, the guard seemed to calm down a little. He looked intently at Rei, confirmed there was a person there, and lowered his spear, looking relieved.

Perhaps because the shock of seeing Set had been so great, he felt even more relieved upon seeing Rei.

“I’m Rei, an adventurer. This is my familiar, Set. We encountered a band of bandits about half a day’s journey from here and wiped them out, so I’d like to buy the survivors as slaves. Also, the bandits were attacking a carriage in the woods, and it seems that carriage belonged to an illegal slave trader. We’re protecting the women who were brought here from somewhere.”

At first, the guards couldn’t make sense of what Rei was saying.

Well, they did understand, but it came too suddenly.

But… they were still guards.

They were the kind of men who, even when they suddenly saw Set, were surprised and frightened, but didn’t run away.

They quickly regained their composure, and the scene became chaotic.

And after a few dozen seconds, several of them ran into the town.

They probably thought they couldn’t make a decision on their own and went to call their superiors.

As far as Rei was concerned, that was a faster way to get a decision than having them dither here, so he had no complaints about their actions.

As they waited, the carriage driven by Relieu eventually arrived.

Seeing that carriage—or, more precisely, the bandits tied up in the back of it—the guards’ expressions hardened.

He didn’t think an adventurer who had a griffin as a familiar would lie and claim to have defeated and captured the bandits.

They hadn’t expected it, but seeing the scene unfold right before their eyes like this, it was only natural for them to react that way.

...Then, one of the guards suddenly realized something.

(Huh? Wait a minute... An adventurer who has a griffin as a familiar... Could it be... Rei of Crimson?)

Until now, the suddenness of the situation had prevented him from noticing, but naturally, various rumors about Rei had reached this town as well.

Once he realized this, he finally felt truly relieved and quietly mentioned it to his companions.

As a result, they were able to handle the situation calmly without causing a major commotion until their superior arrived on the scene.

“Um, are these all the bandits?”

“Yes. These are all the survivors.”

Rei replied simply to the guard’s words.

Wondering what the guard had imagined upon hearing the phrase “survivors”… the guard let out a slightly dry laugh.
