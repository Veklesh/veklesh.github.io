(Now, what should I do?)

After confirming the orc-like woman had vanished, Rei contemplated his next move.

That woman was far too skilled to be from a small country like Reburina.

Did the organization called Jarya have other skilled fighters like her...? If so, they couldn't be underestimated.

That was the thought that crossed Rei's mind.

(Probably because it's profitable, they brought in skilled fighters like that... No, I can't rule out the possibility she was raised in the Kingdom of Reburina either.)

Had he underestimated the Jaya organization? Rei pondered this while looking at the headquarters.

But of course, Rei didn't think he would lose if he fought that woman from earlier.

Winning alone would be easy enough.

But doing so would inevitably cause a significant commotion, revealing that Rei had infiltrated this Mejougo under the guise of Ren.

To prevent that, Rei concluded it was best to avoid contact as much as possible this time.

(But I really want to infiltrate that guard post. If there's something there, I'd like to know about it if possible. ...Which means...)

Rei's gaze shifted toward the Dragon Robe... or more precisely, toward Yellow, who remained still within it.

The scene Yellow saw was, unfortunately, something Rei couldn't see.

But Yellow was a familiar.

If Elena were in Rossi, she could see the scene Yellow saw directly.

It was unfortunate that Rei couldn't confirm it directly here and now, but it was still a better choice than Rei forcibly sneaking into the guardhouse himself under the current circumstances.

It wasn't that Rei was incapable of stealthy action, but his focus was still primarily on combat.

If it were the dead of night with no one around, even Rei could probably infiltrate the headquarters... but this was a lively entertainment district, especially at night.

With so many people out and about now, sneaking into the headquarters was simply impossible.

...A direct assault might be possible, but it was still too early for that.

Besides, it was a headquarters; the possibility that it held no secrets couldn't be ruled out.

If they stormed in and found no secrets of Jaya's there, it would only serve to inform Jaya that an entity – one bearing the alias Crimson, no less – had clearly declared hostility.

That would undoubtedly be an unfavorable situation for Rei, who aims to uncover Jaya's secrets and inflict significant damage.

That's precisely why he considered having Yellow sneak in as the next best option.

Of course, Yellow couldn't hide if seen by human eyes.

While Yellow possessed the special ability to vanish, it wasn't necessary to use that power now—it was night.

For Yellow, a child of the Black Dragon, blending into the night's darkness wasn't difficult.

While Mejougo had magical items that provided light in various places, they couldn't completely erase the darkness of night.

Blending into that persistent darkness, and being as small as Yellow, meant there was little worry about being easily spotted.

"So, could you go take a look?"

"Kyuu!"

At Rei's words, Yellow chirped happily.

Yellow was still small to begin with and preferred moving around to staying still.

Yet, ever since arriving at Mejougo... no, even before boarding the carriage for that purpose, Yellow had stayed still inside Rei's Dragon Robe.

It was only natural he'd want to move.

Besides, considering Elena had also told him to assist Rei, there was no reason to refuse Rei's request this time.

"Alright then, I'm counting on you. Just so you know, make absolutely sure no one finds us. ...Well, if the enemy is lurking around a place like this, I doubt they'll be able to get to Yellow anyway."

"Kyuu!"

At Rei's words, Yellow let out a confident cry.

But this wasn't mere bravado; it stemmed from Yellow's full understanding of its own capabilities.

After all, few possess means to attack flying foes.

Magic, bows, or perhaps throwing stones were the only options.

But as far as Rei could tell, there didn't seem to be any mages in the guardhouse, and it was questionable whether anyone even had a bow.

The guard post existed primarily to deal with fights and drunken disturbances at the Mejougo.

It probably wouldn't do for the Reburina Kingdom to station its own guards in a Mejougo effectively run by a shadowy organization.

Even if there were connections between Jarya, who ran this Mejougo, and some of the upper echelons of the Reburina Kingdom, that was strictly a matter for the higher-ups.

Some guards likely resent criminal organizations running such entertainment districts with impunity, so it's only natural they couldn't be brought in.

In any case, due to these reasons, the guards stationed at the checkpoint were mostly low-ranking adventurers. They certainly didn't possess any means to attack a flying target.

Considering that, there was absolutely no need to worry about sending Yellow out.

(Well, if that orc-like woman who came earlier had been around, it might have been a bit dangerous.)

Recalling the figure of that woman, whose strength seemed unimaginable for a small country like Reburina, Rei gently stroked Yellow.

Rei narrowed his eyes at the cold feel of her scales, and Yellow, being stroked, also seemed to enjoy it, narrowing her eyes... Then, she flapped her wings and flew into the sky.

Rei watched Yellow disappear into the night sky as it headed toward the guard post.

"Kyu? Kyu-kyu?"

Chirping softly, Yellow flew through the air and entered the guard post through an open window.

Yellow looked slightly displeased at the smell of burning insect-repelling herbs, but the repellent was useless against him anyway.

Yellow continued into the guardhouse, flying close to the ceiling as he moved.

Humans generally have blind spots above them.

Of course, a skilled individual might notice something amiss overhead... but luckily for Yellow, there were no such skilled individuals in the guardhouse.

There were plenty who boasted of their skills, but none possessed sharp minds or keen senses.

Well, maybe there were some, but they were probably pulled into the commotion happening at Mejougo, or at least none were currently inside the guard post.

"Damn it... Why the hell do I have to work in a place like this? Normally, I should be..."

"Hey, don't drink too much. If you get drunk and can't do your job, who knows what kind of punishment you'll get."

"I know, I know. Seriously, why the hell am I stuck here... with all these prostitutes right nearby?"

"Don't say that. Once work's done, you can get any girl you want at a discount. That alone makes working at Mejougo worth it, I'd say."

"I want to get laid right now! Why the hell do I have to work while other guys are getting laid? Why not just hire guards or adventurers instead?"

"Well, adventurers aside, we're the guards, aren't we?"

Yellow watched the two men talking from the corner of the ceiling.

Naturally, the two men showed no sign of noticing Yellow's presence.

One drank and complained, while the other listened, trying to keep him from drinking too much.

Yellow watched the scene for a few minutes before eventually moving away.

He flapped his wings, but the sound was barely audible—perhaps due to Yellow's special ability.

Admittedly, if they hadn't been drinking and complaining, they might have noticed even the faintest flapping sound... but right now, they were far too preoccupied.

Leaving that room, Yellow flew near the ceiling again, surveying the interior of the guardhouse.

Fortunately for Yellow, the guard post was reasonably spacious.

Part of the reason was the need for a place to cool off those who caused disturbances in Mejougo, but among those belonging to Jaa were beastmen.

Some of these beastmen were tall, and the guardhouse had been designed so even such individuals could use it without feeling cramped.

That's precisely why there were many places for Yellow to hide, and even flying near the ceiling, he wasn't likely to be spotted.

After flying around the guardhouse to scout the area, Yellow next came upon a cell-like space.

Even though it was a prison cell, it was reasonably clean and properly furnished with beds.

Currently, several drunks were snoring away in some of the cells.

Several of their faces bore marks that looked like they'd been beaten, making it clear they'd been brought here after causing a fight.

Yellow glanced around the cell block before heading to another room.

The next room they entered appeared to be a storage area for weapons and armor. Long swords, spears, and numerous other weapons were stored there, along with leather armor likely made from monster hides.

That said, the monster hides used for the leather armor were clearly of much lower quality compared to those found in Gilm.

This difference stemmed from the fact that Gilm, being a frontier, had an endless supply of monsters, whereas the small country in the countryside rarely encountered them.

Yellow didn't understand the specifics of that situation, but he could still tell the leather armor stored here was of poor quality. That leather armor, he thought, could be easily torn apart even by his own claws or fangs.

Then again, this wasn't exactly the front lines of battle; it was merely a staging post.

Considering that, it wasn't surprising that only weapons and armor of this caliber were provided.

"Huh?"

Huh?

Yellow tilted his head like that.

I'd assumed this outpost must be hiding some big secret.

But in reality, there wasn't really any secret to speak of.

Of course, it was possible Yellow just hadn't noticed it... but that was something beyond Yellow's control right now.

Next, Yellow headed for a room where snoring could be heard, just like in the prison cells.

However, this room wasn't exactly like the cells; several beds were simply placed inside, where a few men were sleeping.

He must have judged that room held nothing particularly important. Yellow headed straight for another room.

The next room he entered was filled with the scent of cooking.

It wasn't exactly a proper dining hall, but it was a room with a few simple cooking utensils.

However, the aroma wafting from it didn't particularly stimulate Yellow's appetite.

If it was the same kind of food, the meals prepared inside the Misty Ring by Rei seemed far more delicious.

In fact, the food prepared here was more akin to feed than actual cooking, existing solely to fill stomachs.

There was no way Yellow would willingly eat anything prepared here.

After all, this was an entertainment district with taverns.

If he was truly hungry, he could go to one of those taverns and eat their food.

While many dishes were meant to boost stamina, they were perfectly fine for regular eating.

"Kyuu..."

Whether he realized that fact, or was simply disappointed there was nothing to eat... either way, Yellow let out a small whimper before heading to the next room.

The next place he went to seemed to be a storage room.

It was a place filled with various things: items no longer used, things people had decided to store for the time being...

If anything, it was more like a dumping ground than a storage room.

Yellow couldn't imagine finding anything useful here.

...Among the items was a large piece of cloth stained with blood.

Judging by the blood still fresh and unstained on the cloth, and the lingering, unmasked scent of blood, it had likely been used just tonight.

Yellow couldn't guess what they'd been used for, but it wasn't hard to imagine it wasn't for stopping blood...

"Kyuu"

Yellow searched the guardhouse for a while longer... but ultimately found nothing of significance and simply left the guardhouse.
