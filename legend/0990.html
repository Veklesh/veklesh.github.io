"Huh? What is this?"

Vihera murmured in stunned disbelief as roots sprouting from the hilt of the Magic Sword spread across the kobold's body, covering it like tattoos or some strange markings.

Since the kobold's body was covered in fur, normally even if roots were growing into its flesh, it would only be noticeable on parts of its face.

Yet it was clear the roots had spread throughout his entire body because the roots embedded within him emitted a dull glow.

It was a sight like a tattoo carved across the entire body, glowing.

And the kobold, screaming just seconds ago, now had no light of will in its eyes.

Vihera had heard about the cyclops incident the previous day from Rei, but seeing it for the first time must have been quite different.

He stared at the kobold, momentarily unable to comprehend what had just happened.

Standing beside Vihera, Rei held his thorn spear and took a stance facing the kobold.

"It's the same as the red cyclops we talked about yesterday. The kobold is probably being controlled by the Magic Sword. But... it's too late."

The kobold, seemingly controlled by the Magic Sword, continued to convulse uncontrollably.

The red cyclops hadn't taken this long when its body was taken over by the hammer; it had acted immediately.

(A cyclops... and not just any cyclops, but a rare or superior species, stronger than usual. And a regular kobold. No matter how you look at it, the kobold is weaker. So why is the Magic Sword taking this long to take over its body... The Magic Sword?)

What caught Rei's eye was the Magic Sword attempting to take over the kobold's body.

Seeing that Magic Sword, Rei understood immediately.

"The Magic Sword and the hammer—even though they're both magic items, their capabilities are different?"

Rei murmured, and it felt undeniably true to him.

Of course. Logically speaking, a Rank E monster like a kobold and a Rank C monster like a cyclops—especially one that seemed to be a rare or higher-tier variety, likely equivalent to a Rank B monster—had vastly different individual combat strengths.

Would you equip two monsters with such a disparity in combat power with magic items of exactly the same performance?

The answer was no.

At the very least, Rei would rather equip a powerful magic item to a formidable monster like a Cyclops than to a Kobold.

(Besides, I don't know how many alchemists from the Bestir Empire are involved in this incident, but since they're alchemists, they're probably remnants of Kabajido. If that's the case, are there really that many of them? ...Even if they defected from the Bestir Empire, I can't imagine them letting ten or twenty alchemists defect unnoticed.)

The kobold, its eyes now devoid of any glimmer of will, gradually shifted from a staggered gait to smoother movements with each step, eventually moving swiftly toward Vihera.

Being small and light, kobolds were a race naturally more adept at speed than strength. Add to that the Magic Sword forcibly enhancing its physical abilities by taking over the kobold's consciousness.

Its movements were incredibly swift and sharp, far beyond what one would expect from a Rank E monster.

Judging by the glowing roots embedded within the kobold's body, the Magic Sword was likely forcibly draining its stamina and vitality, much like it did with the cyclops.

It could literally be called power gained by sacrificing life itself.

Seeing the kobold charging toward him at such speed, Vihera noticed a smile flicker across Rei's face.

The face of a battle-crazed warrior craving a fight against a strong opponent.

Licking his lips with his red, lustrous tongue, he didn't wait for the kobold but stepped forward himself.

Judging that anything said to Vihera now would be futile, Rei stepped back to avoid getting in the way.

Vihera stepped forward, channeling magic into her gauntlets to form magical claws.

He caught the cobalt's downward strike with his claws, then wrapped them around the Magic Sword and flung it upward... Normally, he would have anticipated this, closing the distance further as the cobalt pulled the Magic Sword back.

But... the next instant, a deafening metallic clang echoed as the blade of the Magic Sword snapped clean in half. The kobold, still carrying the momentum of his charge, crashed to the ground, skidding several meters before coming to a halt.

"...Huh?"

The stunned voice belonged to Vihera.

Still unsure what she had done to break the Magic Sword, she unconsciously swung her right hand, flinging the blade—still hooked on the magic claws extending from her gauntlet—to the ground.

"Huh... Wait, what just happened? Is this really over? No way..."

She muttered in disbelief.

Of course she was. She'd been looking forward to a proper fight against someone worthy for the first time in ages, judging by their movements. But in reality, the Magic Sword had snapped cleanly under the magic claws.

"...Well, I kinda had a feeling it might end up like this."

Vihera shot Rei a questioning look.

"A Rank C monster and a Rank E monster. With such a huge rank difference, even if they're both magic items, their performance is bound to differ. High-performance magic items naturally require rare materials and a lot of magic power to create."

"...Yeah."

She found herself agreeing with Rei's words.

She realized that even if she were plotting something, she wouldn't give the same magic item to both the kobold and the cyclops.

"And considering the Cyclops' magic item didn't have regenerative abilities or anything like that, the Magic Sword is probably just a downgraded version of that hammer or something."

Laying her gaze on the blade of the Magic Sword lying on the ground, Rei murmured. She reached out, but stopped her movement.

He remembered how the cyclops' hammer had tried to steal Rei's consciousness when he touched it.

Compared to the hammer, the Magic Sword was several tiers lower, and now it was even broken in half.

Even so, considering the possibility of something going wrong, he had to admit it was still dangerous to touch it directly.

"Stay back a little."

At Rei's words, everyone present moved away from her, though puzzled.

Confirming this, he began to amplify his magic power... The next instant, the magic power emanating from Rei's body condensed, compressed, and became visible, coiling around him as crimson magic.

No one present could sense the magic itself, yet none could fail to notice the overwhelming presence emanating from Rei.

The former members of the Roving Squad had seen this form countless times before, and Mireine and the other members of Scorching Wind had witnessed Rei activate the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour during the duel held in winter.

Only the two adventurers who had fled to this place stood frozen, unable to move, utterly bewildered by what was happening.

Rei, enveloped in crimson magic, reached out toward the blade of the Magic Sword lying on the ground.

The moment Rei grips the blade... as if unable to withstand Rei's magic, the Magic Sword crumbles into sand.

"...Huh?"

Unable to comprehend what had happened, Rei stared at the blade of the Magic Sword, now turning to sand and slipping through his fingers.

Within seconds, the entire blade of the Magic Sword in Rei's hand had turned to sand, trickling away and disappearing, carried off by the spring breeze.

Staring blankly after it, Rei shifted his gaze to the thorn spear in his left hand.

(The Magic Sword broke just from being touched, but the thorn spear didn't do anything. Is this... no, is this also a difference in rank as magic items?)

The Thorn Spear was a high-quality magic item Marina had given him as a reward.

Comparing that thorn spear to a Magic Sword crafted for use by a kobold, even if it was an alchemist of the Bestir Empire, was wrong in many ways.

"...Um, Rei? Could you maybe, like, get going soon..."

Mireine, standing a short distance away, said this while sweating from the intense heat radiating from Rei.

While he had gained some ability to control his heat output, it still wasn't perfect.

"Ah, sorry."

He muttered, releasing the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Simultaneously, a breeze blew, carrying the air around them—warmer than spring temperatures... no, hotter—into the forest.

The breeze coming off the river felt just right for his overheated body.

As he felt that cool air, Set eventually approached Rei.

"Grr?"

Are you okay? Set tilted his head slightly and purred. Rei smiled and reached out his hand.

"Don't worry about it. It'd be a lie to say I don't feel disappointed... but it's not like I'm that disappointed either."

It was a Magic Sword that could be a clue for the alchemist, so he had wanted to get it if possible, but it wasn't absolutely necessary.

In terms of clues, there was a hammer far more intricately crafted than the Magic Sword that Rei had just destroyed.

If the goal was to gather information on the alchemist, examining that hammer would likely yield far more clues.

(Though honestly, the more magic items you can examine, the better.)

As he scattered the sand clinging to his hands into the wind, he caught sight of Vihera's suddenly stern expression.

Was it because the fight with the kobolds was boring? Rei wondered, but seeing Vihera's expression, he somehow understood she wasn't thinking about the kobolds.

"Vihera, what's wrong? Did something happen?"

"...Yes. But not here. I'll tell you later. More importantly, what about the kobolds? You probably don't feel like stripping materials from the cyclops anymore either."

Rei understood she was deliberately changing the subject, but judging that she must have some reason for it, he glanced around.

The kobold corpses scattered everywhere numbered nearly fifty, including those hiding in the bushes beyond the initial twenty that had appeared.

They had come here to strip the Cyclops, but everyone had completely lost the will to continue that task.

The fact that Mireine and the others had finished skinning their cyclops probably played a big part in that.

"I don't need any for now. It's too late anyway."

"We might want some. Dividing the reward from the Cyclops among this many people doesn't leave much."

At Mireine's words, the others nodded in agreement.

(Every little bit helps, they say.)

Over fifty kobold corpses.

If they stripped every last one, it could cover a week's worth of food... maybe even a month if things went well.

Not only did kobolds yield magic stones, but their pelts also fetched a decent price.

"Then why not just skin them? Vihera..."

Just as he spoke, Rei noticed Byune gathering several kobold corpses near her.

Noticing Rei's gaze, Byune puffed out her thin chest proudly.

These were the kobolds she had killed.

"Huh!"

"Yeah, I get that... but what are you gonna do with them? You're planning to skin them now or something?"

"Mm."

For Byune, who possessed a strong attachment to gold from birth, the option of simply leaving the kobold's corpse behind—something that would undoubtedly bring in gold, even if a little cheap—simply didn't exist.

Watching Byune like this, the two adventurers who had been chased by the kobold were astonished.

They were astonished to see a girl so much younger than themselves take down kobolds one after another, and even more so by her insistence on claiming the fallen kobolds for herself.

They exchanged glances, then nodded in unison before speaking together.

"Um... thank you for helping us."

"Honestly, even though we knew they formed packs, we didn't expect the kobolds to be in such a large group... and then there was someone wielding a Magic Sword..."

"Don't worry about it. These things happen. Besides, it wasn't all bad for us, somehow."

As the two adventurers bowed their heads, Rei replied while watching Mireine and Byune, who had already started skinning the kobolds.

Though they called it stripping, they seemed to have given up on time-consuming items like fur, except for the nicest pieces. They were efficiently gathering only the required proof-of-kill parts, magic stones, and the easily severed tails from the kobolds.

"With this many kobold corpses, there's no doubt today's earnings will be decent, one way or another."

"...You guys are strong, huh?"

One of the men murmured thoughtfully.

Hearing the man's voice, Rei let the comment pass without affirming or denying it.

(Given that kobolds were given the Magic Sword, these guys are probably just collateral damage from my fight. ...Still, the fact that kobolds were given Magic Swords means they can mass-produce them, regardless of quality... I have a bad feeling about this.)

Watching the scene of the kobold's materials being stripped away, he thought this to himself, deeply.
