"Whoa!"

Saying that, Vihera, who had been clinging to Set’s leg, stepped down onto the dungeon floor.

The cave was reasonably spacious, and there was enough room for Set to move around freely, but the ceiling was only about three meters high, so it wasn’t particularly high.

With a bit of effort, Set might be able to fly, but given that the height was so limited, he couldn’t pull off a move like a high-speed strike from a great height.

For that reason, the group clinging to Set had leaped in as if diving from outside the dungeon into the interior.

“Hmm, from what I can see, there doesn’t seem to be anything particularly unusual here. …Though, the fact that there’s a dungeon on the side of a cliff is clearly unusual in itself.”

Elena muttered this as she surveyed her surroundings.

Though she did so while slightly furrowing her brow at the sight of the two goblin corpses lying at the edge of the passageway.

To Elena, Vihera—filled with anticipation at the possibility of facing a formidable foe—smiled and spoke up.

“But this is still just the entrance, right? So it’s only natural that nothing’s changed yet, isn’t it?”

“The dungeon I challenged before was flooded the moment I stepped inside, though.”

Relieu replied to Elena and Vihera’s remarks, and those who imagined the scene grimaced in disgust.

Since they were taking on a dungeon, they weren’t about to shy away from getting a little wet.

Still, finding yourself in such a situation the moment you step inside a dungeon is quite effective at dampening one’s motivation.

“I’m glad we didn’t have to deal with that.”

“Yeah. A dungeon on the side of a cliff is a bit odd, but once you’re inside, it’s not so bad. …Though, I have no idea what’s further in. So, Rei. What formation are we going to use? Since you’re the party leader, I’ll follow your orders.”

“Right. For now, the thief, Byune, will lead the way.”

“Mm.”

At Rei’s words, Byune nodded expressionlessly.

She didn’t show it on her face, but even Rei could tell she was full of determination.

“Next is Vihera.”

Vihera, whose combat style is normally hand-to-hand combat, isn’t the kind of person who should be stationed in a place like this.

But it was also true that Vihera was the only one who could accurately understand Byune’s words.

Furthermore, Vihera’s burst speed is incredible; if an opponent is clumsy enough to be wielding a weapon, she can instantly slip inside… right into Vihera’s attack range.

“Well, I suppose there’s no helping it. Leave it to me.”

Vihera understands the situation regarding Byune, so she accepts the task without complaint.

In fact, if the thief Byune finds a trap that requires a detailed explanation, someone will need to explain it.

Whether or not she actually does it, in a dungeon, the ability to handle such a situation in a pinch can mean the difference between life and death.

“And, behind him, Relieu.”

“Me? Honestly, I don’t think I’d be much help even if I were in the middle.”

“Um, you know that skill called ‘Wind Slash,’ right?”

When I mentioned the skill similar to Rei’s Flying Slash, Relieu reluctantly nodded.

“If you put it that way, I guess that’s fine. …But Wind Slash can be used in rapid succession to a certain extent, but it’s not like I can use it whenever I want. Keep that in mind.”

“Got it. …So, Set and Marina will be behind Relieu. Marina has spirit magic, so she can provide support even from the back, and Set will keep an eye out for anything suspicious while acting as Marina’s escort.”

“Grr!”

“Got it.”

Marina specializes in elemental magic involving wind and water.

In that sense, using water elemental magic inside this dungeon is difficult.

Still, since they’re high up on the cliff face, the fact that some wind is coming in is a definite help for Marina.

Of course, she can use earth elemental magic too, though not as effectively as wind or water.

“So, Elena and I will bring up the rear, then.”

As for Rei, to be honest, he can handle most tasks no matter where he is.

Of course, he can’t do things like spot traps the way Byune can.

At long and medium ranges, he has magic, skills, and spear throws, and at close range, he can handle melee attacks just fine.

No one objected to Rei’s words…

“Alright, let’s get going then. I don’t think we’ll be able to clear the dungeon in just one day, but I’d like to make some headway.”

Nodding at Rei’s words, the group began to make their way through the dungeon.

That said, since they’d only just entered, there shouldn’t be any major traps set up yet.

Monsters like goblins and kobolds do appear, but after defeating them and extracting their magic stones, they simply leave the corpses lying around.

The only exception was orcs; whenever they defeated one, they stored the corpse inside a Misty Ring.

(Ah, but since we’re defeating them like this, does that mean the folks waiting on the surface for monsters to fall are just going to be left waiting? …Well, even so, not defeating monsters isn’t really an option.)

Even if we left them alone, the monsters would still attack Rei and the others.

The option of not defeating such monsters and simply letting them attack us doesn’t exist in Rei’s… no, in anyone here’s mind.

“Hm!”

Amidst all this, Byune, who was walking at the front, muttered softly.

Vihera immediately explains what that means.

“It’s a trap.”

If it were just Rei and the others, they would have been able to understand the meaning of Byune’s words to some extent.

Still, the fact that Vihera took the trouble to explain it like this was probably because, even though they had come to understand it to some extent, they didn’t fully grasp the details.

After all, you never know what might happen inside a dungeon.

Also, the fact that Relieu has only just met Byune plays a big role.

Unlike Rei and the others, Relue has almost no idea what Byune is trying to say, which is why Vihera’s interpretation is necessary.

“That floor over there. It sinks when you step on it, so be careful.”

“...What happens if I step on it?”

“I don’t know. If you want to try it, go ahead, Relue. I’d rather pass, though.”

Hearing Vihera say that, Relieu didn’t feel like trying it either.

She silently shook her head and gestured for them to move on.

They moved forward, careful not to step on the spot Byune had pointed out… and continued like that for a few minutes. Eventually, a Y-shaped fork in the road came into view.

“Which way should we go?”

“That’s a tough one. We don’t have any clues or anything…”

“Hmm!”

As Rei looked slightly troubled by Marina’s words, Byune pointed at the ground and muttered softly.

“Monster footprints, huh. From what I can see, there are more traces on the right path.”

“...Really?”

If the ground were made of soil or covered in dust, Rei would be able to tell just how many footprints remained.

But since this was a dungeon carved into the cliff face, the ground was, naturally, made of rock.

As far as Rei could tell, he couldn’t find any signs of how many footprints had been left behind.

“I can’t tell just from a quick look either. …He’s small, but his skills as a thief are reliable, I suppose.”

At Relue’s words, which sounded impressed, Byune seemed to give off a slightly pleased vibe.

In terms of combat ability, it’s fair to say that Byune is quite skilled as a thief.

But since she was a thief, there was no way she wouldn’t be pleased to have her skills as a thief praised.

“Well, never mind that. So, which way are we going? The side with more monsters? Or the side with fewer monsters?”

Rei hesitated at Relyu’s question, but made up his mind quickly.

“Let’s head toward the area with more monsters. If monsters are passing through there frequently, it’s likely connected to another area. Well, I don’t know if that’s up or down, though.”

This dungeon was located about halfway up the cliff face.

In other words, it could lead either up or down.

(If it goes up, it wouldn’t be strange to end up on top of the cliff… assuming there aren’t any spatial distortions or anything like that, of course.)

The reason Rei thinks this way is likely due to the unique nature of the dungeon—it exists on the face of a cliff rather than being a standard dungeon.

After all, many of the dungeons Rei had faced so far seemed to be places where space itself was warped.

Places where, even though they were inside a dungeon, there was a high sky with the sun and moon, or where there was a vast desert inside the dungeon with the sun pouring down intense rays... and so on.

Considering those circumstances, it was hard to imagine that a dungeon located in such a unique place would have no spatial anomalies whatsoever.

That said, since this dungeon was likely brand-new, there was also the possibility that it wasn’t all that huge.

“Well then, I’m going to head right. …Though I am a little curious if there’s anything on the left.”

Marina’s words made Rei’s heart flutter slightly.

In the RPGs he’d played back in Japan, clearing dungeons was a common part of the experience.

In those games, dead ends often contained treasure chests or similar items, so whenever Rei played an RPG, he made a point of exploring every possible passageway.

Of course, if he was using a strategy guide, the locations of treasure chests and the like were listed, so it wasn’t that much of a hassle.

"Hmm... Maybe there’s something important there. Should we head left?"

It was a somewhat indecisive decision on Rei’s part, but since no one expected to clear the dungeon in a single day anyway, moving left was accepted without issue.

So they headed left, where there didn’t seem to be many signs of monsters having passed through… but within ten minutes, they reached a dead end.

However, there were several rock-like, plant-like things blooming there.

Plants growing inside a cave are rare, but not unheard of,

but even for Rei, this was the first time he’d ever seen anything that looked like a plant made of rock.

“Does anyone here know what those are?”

Rei asked, hoping someone might know what those rock plants were, but unfortunately, no one could answer him.

Since even Marina—a former guild master who’s lived for a long time—didn’t know, there was no doubt that the rock-plant right in front of them was a rare find.

“In that case, we should probably take this with us. I don’t know what kind of value it has, though.”

“I suppose so. Still, a plant made of rock, huh… Since it’s in a place like this, it’s obviously not some kind of man-made statue or anything.”

Marina muttered as she looked once more at the rock plant before her, but Rei and his party were the first to enter this dungeon in the first place.

At least, that’s the official story.

...In reality, even if some high-ranking adventurer had entered before Rei and the others, it wouldn’t be surprising or illegal.

After all, there were no instructions or orders from the guild or the magistrate prohibiting entry into the dungeon.

It was precisely because of the cliff face—a treacherous one, with sharp edges and slippery surfaces—that no one had been able to enter the dungeon. But if someone had the means to deal with it, like Rei and his group, they could enter the dungeon without any trouble.

It wouldn’t be strange if such people were already there.

(Though, even if someone had entered before us, I don’t know why they didn’t show any interest in this rock plant. …Is it because it’s heavy?)

For Rei, who possesses a Misty Ring, weight is completely irrelevant—he can carry it with ease.

In that sense, finding this rock plant here was a stroke of luck.

“Byune, just checking, but are there any traps?”

“Hmm.”

Rei asked this because he didn’t want to get caught in a trap while trying to deal with that rock plant, but Byune shook her head.

After confirming that, he looked around again, checking for anything unusual… not necessarily a trap, but wondering if some kind of monster might be hiding somewhere. But there really was nothing there.

“Alright, I’m going to put this away, so keep an eye on the surroundings.”

Saying that, Rei approached the rock plant… but just as he got close enough, one of its leaves suddenly flew toward him.
