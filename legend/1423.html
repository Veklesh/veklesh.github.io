Zakunya's specialty dish was a hot pot made with river fish.

Rei had no particular aversion to river fish, as he used to catch ayu, yamame, iwana, and kajika in rivers during summer even when he was in Japan.

After all, in Gilm, far from the sea, fish meant river fish.

That's why Rei felt a little disappointed when he heard the dish used river fish. However, the dish that actually arrived was vastly different from what Rei had expected.

Surprised by the rich flavor, unimaginable for river fish, Rei asked for details and learned they used a type of river fish only caught in this area.

In fact, Rei was shown the fish before it was cooked. Even in Gilm, a frontier town attracting many merchants, it was a fish he had never seen before.

Its appearance actually resembled a long, slender fish like a Pacific saury.

Of course, it differed significantly from the Pacific saury Rei knew in many ways, so it probably wasn't exactly a Pacific saury.

In fact, its taste was vastly different from that of Pacific saury.

That hot pot dish, filled with that fish and seasonal vegetables... early summer vegetables, thoroughly satisfied Rei, even though it was summer.

...The villagers eating with him were sweating over having hot pot in summer... but with his Dragon Robe, Rei had no such worries.

(Ideally, I'd like to finish it off with rice porridge... but there's no rice.)

For someone originally Japanese, it wouldn't be strange to be fixated on white rice.

But Rei never had that kind of strong attachment to begin with.

If there was no white rice, she could just eat bread. That was her mindset... but even for someone like Rei, rice porridge after hot pot was never something she disliked.

In fact, back in Japan, when eating hot pot at home, she often saved ingredients specifically for the rice porridge. The next day, she'd use those leftover ingredients to make it.

However, as far as Rei knew, he had never seen rice in the Kingdom of Mireana.

Of course, it was true that as an adventurer, Rei had been to many places, but the places he hadn't been to were overwhelmingly more numerous.

It was impossible to completely rule out the possibility that rice was a staple food in such places.

(Well, it might not necessarily be called rice, though.)

While thinking along these lines, Rei listened to Shira and Regula recounting stories from their childhood as part of their post-meal conversation. Suddenly, Jazz addressed Rei.

"Rei-sama, I truly thank you for helping Shira this time."

"No need to be so formal. Like I said earlier, it just happened that way."

"Even so, if Rei hadn't been there, my daughter would be... I can't even face her mother, who asked me to do this."

Hearing this, Rei suddenly realized Jazz's wife—Shira's mother—was nowhere to be seen.

But he didn't ask where Jazz's wife was.

Frankly, Rei's main reason for stopping by was to savor the famous local cuisine.

Having achieved that goal, he couldn't bring himself to ask about something that didn't interest him that much.

He could guess she was probably already dead.

But Rei's attitude changed the moment he heard the words from the man standing near Jazz.

"Calm down, man. This isn't the Lerdact County anymore."

"...What?"

The words that came out of that man's mouth contained a word Rei absolutely couldn't afford to miss, given the current request he was undertaking.

"Count Lerdact?"

The man who spoke to Jazz probably didn't expect Rei to react to those words.

He opened his mouth, staring at Rei, who was fixedly looking at him, feeling a little surprised... no, perhaps even a touch of fear.

"Ah, ah. Jazz and the others originally lived in the Earl of Lerdact's territory. But things happened there, and they fled all the way here."

"Huh. Interesting. Can I ask for specifics?"

"...Why are you so interested in my story, Rei?"

Jazz asked, puzzled by Rei's words.

The Count of Lerdact held some power among the nobility, but ultimately, it was only to a certain extent.

And why would Rei, known as a member of the Neutral Faction, take an interest in the Noble Faction Count Relduct family?

Faced with that gaze, Rei hesitated over how to respond.

He couldn't very well say outright that he was going to retaliate for obstructing Gilm's expansion...

In the end, he cleared his throat slightly, as if to cover it up, before speaking.

And since I just happened to hear the name of the place I'm heading to next, it piqued my interest. You can never have too much information."

"...I see."

He nodded as if conceding, but Jazz didn't fully trust Rei's words.

Rei's words weren't lies, but they weren't entirely truth either.

That feeling probably stemmed from Jazz's rusty instincts as a former adventurer.

A former Rank C adventurer meant he was a skilled fighter, no doubt.

Setting aside exceptions like Gilm, that rank was high enough that in most villages or towns, he'd be treated as a top-tier adventurer.

Precisely because of that, Jazz sensed there was something behind Rei's words, but he had no intention of pursuing it.

Instead, he pretended not to care and spoke again.

"The Count of Lerdact's domain has pretty steep taxes. It's barely enough to scrape by... that sort of thing. That's exactly why I left the Count of Lerdact's domain myself."

"Yeah, I guess so."

Most wouldn't want a life where just surviving is all they can manage.

But even so, not many people actually move to find a new place to live.

After all, many people spend their entire lives in the village or town where they were born.

For such people, the very concept of leaving their home doesn't exist.

Becoming a merchant or adventurer overturns that common sense... but such professions were never very common.

Moreover, if taxes were truly so oppressive that survival became impossible, people might consider some means of escape. Yet the subjects of the Lerdact County, while barely scraping by, were not on the brink of starvation.

This, perhaps, was proof that Lerdact was by no means incompetent.

Though, using that talent solely to satisfy his own greed is very much like Lerdact.

"...Huh? Jazz moved here quite a while ago, didn't he? Shira said she was born here."

Considering Shira is around twenty, that means Jazz came to Zakunya even earlier than that.

If that's the case, it was hard to believe the Lerdact Jazz knew was the same person targeted by Rei.

According to the information Rei got from Daskar, the current Lerdact is in his thirties.

(Logically, the Earl Lerdact targeted for retaliation in this incident must be the son or something of the Earl Lerdact Jazz knows.)

Of course, it could be a much younger brother, a relative, an adopted child... but that didn't matter to Rei.

(No, the Noble Faction seems to pride itself on bloodlines, so brothers or relatives might be possible, but an adopted child? Unlikely.)

He couldn't possibly have been ruling the domain since he was ten or so.

Jazz nodded in agreement with Rei's speculation.

"Yeah, that seems to be the case. But according to rumors from merchants, minstrels, and adventurers, it was only the lord who changed—the policies of the domain remained the same."

Rei murmured in agreement at his words.

Even though the lord changed, the policies remained the same.

Hearing that, Jazz even felt a twinge of irritation.

But considering it was the result of education, he strangely found himself accepting it.

"So, if you hear anything about the Lerdact County, could you let me know?"

Rei, who never expected to get information about his next destination in a village he'd stumbled upon by chance, asked Jazz.

"The only information I have firsthand is over twenty years old. I doubt it would be of any use to you, Rei."

"That's not true. Information may be old or new, but it's never completely useless."

Having lived in Japan, Rei naturally understood the importance of information.

Whether you're doing anything, having information makes a huge difference.

"I see. Still, it shouldn't be that different from other noble territories. The tax rate is harsh, though. As far as I know, the Count of Lerdact household has a skilled knightly order. ...Though 'skilled' might be generous—they tended to do pretty much whatever they wanted."

"Ah... I see."

Rei nodded in agreement at those words.

With such heavy taxes, it was only natural that resentment toward the lord would arise.

It was only natural to maintain adequate military strength to deal with such people.

And if you asked whether those knights, who cooperated with Lerdact, were acting in a manner worthy of the title... well, by any normal standard, the answer would be no.

"So they're knights in name only, but essentially just private soldiers?"

"That's right. But apparently, many of them are confident in their skills."

Hearing that, the private army that caused this whole incident flashed through Rei's mind.

(Though the one I captured did show a surprising amount of loyalty to his employer... Well, since they're being sent to Gilm, it's only natural they'd consider that aspect.)

They probably chose the most discreet ones, but even so, they surely didn't expect all of them to be captured so easily.

Even without Rei, who actually took action this time, Gilm has plenty of skilled adventurers.

That they failed to consider the situation in detail probably stems from looking down on them as a small, Neutral Faction.

(If they'd properly researched Gilm, they wouldn't have made such a foolish mistake.)

The Neutral Faction is by no means a large power compared to the largest faction, the Royal Faction, or the Noble Faction that aligns with them.

But they are undeniably one of the three major factions.

"He's my brother."

A bitter expression flickered across Jazz's face as he said this.

He likely felt guilty about being the only one living peacefully here.

Though, considering what happened—his only daughter being taken by orcs—whether this could truly be called a peaceful life was debatable.

"I see."

That was why Rei only replied with that single word.

He knew that anything he said wouldn't offer Jazz any comfort.

"...My apologies. I got a bit sentimental there. Now, moving on. Within the Lerdact County, the place requiring particular caution is the town of Jawa, where Count Lerdact resides."

"Well, it is the stronghold, after all."

"Exactly. That's precisely why there are so many soldiers and knights there. If anything happens, they'll capture you immediately... but then again, you probably don't need to worry about that, Rei."

To capture Rei, it wouldn't be enough to send just one or two, ten, twenty, a hundred, or even two hundred men.

Of course, if they were individuals of exceptional skill, that might be a different story... but Lerdact, who was by no means an outstanding figure even among the Noble Faction, simply couldn't possess that kind of military strength.
