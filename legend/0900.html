In fact, Zeros, who had been knocked back but not blown away by the impact of the swung Death Scythe thanks to Rei's inhuman strength, could be said to possess the skill befitting a Rank B adventurer.

"Who'd have thought you could clash with the Death Scythe and still hold onto your weapon? Not bad at all."

"What I want isn't some mock battle. I want a real fight where even my own life could be on the line. I figured this was the best way to make that happen."

Zeros struggled to suppress the urge to attack at the opening Rei blatantly showed.

He understood that attacking that opening would undoubtedly invite a counter, leaving him open to a fatal blow.

...But even so, when he spotted such an obvious opening in Rei—a man who otherwise showed no weaknesses, who seemed to effortlessly counter any attack—the urge to strike there demanded tremendous patience to resist.

"If you'd asked me to fight seriously, even if it wasn't a duel, I might have accepted... but!"

Closing the distance with Zeros in a straight line, he flipped his wrist and thrust the pommel of the Death Scythe forward like a spear.

His movement speed combined with the speed of thrusting the spear meant that by the time Rei realized it, the pommel was already mere inches from striking his body.

Though Rei aimed for the torso rather than the head to avoid a fatal wound, that was also the hardest part of the body to dodge.

It was already too late to pull back his halberd to parry. All Zeros could do was twist his torso to barely evade the butt's blow.

The metallic clang of the metal armor covering Zeros' torso scraping against the stone tip of the Death Scythe.

"Guh!"

While he successfully dodged the butt strike itself without taking major damage, given the Death Scythe's weight, any attack from Rei would inflict some degree of damage unless completely avoided.

Zeros had never considered himself the strongest, but the realization that such a powerful opponent existed—a being that could truly be called a monster—was overwhelming.
