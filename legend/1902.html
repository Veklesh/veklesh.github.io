"Red Cloth." That was the nickname given to the group of men who had been causing all sorts of trouble in Gilm before Rei set off for Anesis.

It wasn’t exactly their official name, nor did they call themselves that, but they came to be known as such because they would wrap red cloth around some part of their bodies, as if to emphasize their presence.

To Rei, who knew at least a little about the Romance of the Three Kingdoms, it wasn’t entirely unfounded to wonder if they were a red version of the Yellow Turban Rebellion. But considering their actions, they were more accurately described as a color gang.

That said, most of Rei’s knowledge of color gangs came from TV dramas and manga.

The Red Cloths caused all sorts of trouble, from minor disturbances to major upheavals.

Because the events that took place in Anesis were so intense in so many ways, Rei had completely forgotten about the Red Cloth.

He hadn’t heard anything about it from Vihera, Byune, Lenora and Kenny whom he met at the guild, or from Johanna and the others.

……Then again, considering the current state of Gilm—with kobolds attacking it day and night, regardless of the hour—it might be only natural that no one told Rei about the Red Cloth incident.

“The red cloth? …Oh, come to think of it, shortly after Rei went to Anesis, I stopped hearing anything from her. Does the guild know anything about that?”

The guild clerk, met with Vihera’s gaze, immediately shook his head.

“The Kobold incident took up all our attention, so that’s a big part of it. However, if the Red Cloth had been making a big splash, we would’ve heard about it. The fact that we haven’t suggests they’ve stopped their activities, doesn’t it? …Though, I have no idea where they went after stopping.”

Some of the Red Cloth members hid their faces, but most left theirs uncovered.

Given that, it goes without saying that everyone remembers their faces.

…Though, even in Winter Gilm, there are plenty of people, so it might be difficult to definitively say that person was one of the Red Cloth.

As he thought that... a sudden thought occurred to Rei.

“Could it be that the Red Cloths stopped their activities because it started snowing and got cold?”

“Because it’s cold… well, I suppose that’s not entirely out of the question. Besides, if they were running around in the snow, they might get hurt.”

The Red Cloth’s activities have slowed down because of the cold.

Hearing just that, it’s only natural to react with a “Huh…?”

But what Akabura does is basically criminal.

For example, snatch theft, extortion, and all sorts of other things.

Given that, it goes without saying that they need to evade the guards.

Since Gilm’s guards sometimes face skilled adventurers, it wouldn’t be surprising if they were stronger than the average adventurer in some cases.

And since the Red Cloth isn’t generally that skilled… given that if the guards catch up to you, you’ll be captured, the fact that the snow makes the ground unstable is practically a fatal flaw.

“I see. …But if that’s the case, the Red Cloth’s activities will likely pick up again come spring. Also… just a thought, and maybe I’m overthinking this, but is it too far-fetched to think that the kobold incident in Gilm might be the Red Cloth’s doing?”

At Rei’s words, Vihera and the guild official looked surprised… and eventually, both shook their heads.

“No, that’s not it. As I’m sure you know, Rei, the Red Cloth are a bunch of people who became adventurers but couldn’t make it work, or who remained adventurers but are low-ranking… that sort of crowd. Do you really think people like that are capable of manipulating even low-ranking monsters like kobolds?”

“I agree with that assessment. If there’s any possibility at all, it would have to be a magic item—since we can’t expect them to possess such magic or abilities… but do you really think the Red Cloth lot could get their hands on a magic item that’s unknown to the world?”

With both of them rejecting his idea, Rei could no longer claim his opinion was correct.

After all, he hadn’t claimed that the Red Cloth was behind the kobold incident based on any concrete evidence, so there was no need for him to cling to that opinion.

Besides, the fact that what Vihera said made sense to Rei was a major factor.

“If that’s the case, then the kobold incident must have been the work of someone else…”

“Hey! You there! Hand over what you’ve got in your pocket!”

Could it be the work of someone else?

Just as Rei was about to say that, a loud voice rang out, cutting him off.

Shouting like that out of the blue naturally drew the attention of everyone nearby.

And the focus of those eyes was a guild official—a different one from the one Rei had been talking to.

With a stern look, the guild official strode roughly toward a young man—someone who looked to be in his early twenties.

The man tried to say something to the guild official approaching him, but—perhaps deciding it was pointless—he simply turned and ran away.

Because so many people had been walking around to dismantle the Giganto Turtle, there was no snow left on the ground nearby.

That’s probably why he thought he might be able to escape if he ran… but several people nearby tripped the running man, causing him to fall, and pinned him down.

“Damn it! Let me go! Let me go! It’s not like taking a little bit is such a big deal! It’s huge, I tell you!”

The man shouted, but for the men holding him down, the thought of losing their job dismantling the Giganto Turtle because of him was unbearable.

If the consequences of this man’s reckless act were limited to him alone, that might be one thing, but in this case, the blame could extend to them as well—the men who had been working alongside him.

Therefore, to prevent that from happening, they absolutely could not let the perpetrator get away.

“Shut up! What are you going to do if we get dragged into this because of you?!”

“That’s right! You know full well that we were supposed to get a share of the Giganto Turtle meat once today’s job was done! So why the hell did you pull this stunt?!”

Frustration, impatience, and irritation. As the man shouted, pouring all those emotions into his voice, the two men holding him down shouted back with even greater fury.

“Now, please calm down.”

The one who said this to the shouting men was the guild official who had spotted the man trying to steal a piece of the Giganto Turtle and had shouted at him.

Normally, the guild official would have been the one shouting at the man who tried to escape, but seeing that the other men had already caught him and were shouting at him first, he probably couldn’t say anything. Unlike when he shouted earlier, he spoke in a somewhat calmer voice.

Seeing the guild official like that, the two men who had caught the fleeing man, as well as those watching from the sidelines, breathed a sigh of relief.

This was because they understood that, for the time being, the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle would not be halted immediately.

“For now, we’re taking that man to the Guild… I’m sorry to ask this, but could you please hold him so he doesn’t escape and come with us? Of course, we won’t treat this as if you abandoned your post.”

“...Yeah, got it.”

“I don’t mind either.”

After confirming the two men had nodded, the Guild employee turned his gaze toward his superior—the Guild employee standing beside Rei and the others.

The staff member who caught his gaze said nothing further, merely nodding.

But that was enough. After confirming the nod, the guild clerk headed toward the guild, accompanied by the man being held by the two others.

After the guild staff members left, there was a bit of a commotion, but when a few of the staff told everyone to get back to work, everyone returned to their respective tasks.

“...So, um, what were we talking about?”

Glancing briefly at the man who had committed the reckless act being carried away, Rei muttered, seemingly paying him little mind.

Seeing Rei’s attitude, perhaps concluding there would be no penalty for them, the people near the man also returned to the task of butchering the Giganto Turtle.

The faces of a few of the men working to skin the Giganto Turtle had turned pale—perhaps because they were thinking the same thing as the man from earlier.

That said, having just witnessed firsthand what would happen if they did such a thing, unless they were extremely confident or foolish enough not to think of the consequences, they probably wouldn’t try anything strange.

In fact, the guild official supervising the dissection nearby had noticed those few men and was watching them intently, ready to intervene the moment they made any suspicious moves.

“It’s about the red cloth, isn’t it? Rei said something silly like the kobold incident might have been the red cloth’s doing, but you were just about to say that it might have been someone else’s doing… right?”

“Yes, I think so.”

The guild official, who had been looked at by Vihera as if for confirmation, nodded in agreement.

Seeing this, Rei recalled the conversation from just moments before and continued.

“Ah, that’s right. …If the Kobold incident isn’t the work of Red Cloth but of some other group, then we need to find a way to catch them. …Or rather, I don’t know how they’re controlling the Kobolds, but we need to deal with that too.”

“I suppose so. But the problem is how to find them. After all, we don’t even have definitive proof that this incident was caused by humans in the first place.”

“If we had any evidence or clues, I’d agree with Rei’s opinion.”

As Vihera and the guild staff had pointed out, the idea that this was the work of humans was, after all, nothing more than Rei’s speculation.

Since Rei was merely stating this without any evidence, there simply wasn’t enough basis to investigate it.

“I suppose so. I won’t force the issue. …But even if we can’t get someone else to look into it, there shouldn’t be a problem if I investigate it myself, right?”

“Huh? Well, I suppose… But are you really planning to look into it? Obviously, there won’t be any kind of reward for this, though.”

Rei suspects something himself and is investigating based on that intuition.

It went without saying, but investigating this… was, so to speak, nothing more than a hobby for Rei.

As such, there would be no reward for doing so.

Of course, if this incident really was caused by human intervention and he managed to stop it, that would be a different story.

That said, what Rei was really after wasn’t that kind of reward; he suspected this incident might have been caused by a magic item.

If it’s not just a trinket but an actual, usable magic item, then it’s something Rei would definitely want to get his hands on.

Even with something as simple as kobolds, he can control them endlessly—in such vast numbers that one might wonder where he even gathered them all from—so from Rei’s perspective, such a magic item seems to have a wide variety of potential uses.

For instance, when wiping out bandits, or in a war against the Bestir Empire—no, even beyond that, in a war with any other country, or when fighting hordes of monsters.

Given that it allows one to freely command a swarm of kobolds, it would be a very dangerous effect if misused, but if this incident was truly caused by a magic item, Rei’s impression was that he absolutely wanted to get his hands on one.

(Oh, magic items. Come to think of it, I forgot to hand out the souvenirs.)

He suddenly remembered that he’d completely forgotten to give the gems containing healing magic—which he’d obtained in Anesis—as souvenirs to Marina, Vihera, Byune, and a few others.

On the day he returned to Gilm, there had been a party at Marina’s house, and he had completely forgotten about it after that.

That said, since I had served a few dishes I’d bought in Anesis at that party, it wasn’t as if I hadn’t given them any souvenirs at all.

That said, I certainly couldn’t hand out those souvenirs—including the gem—right here and now, so I decided I’d just give them to Marina at her house during dinner tonight.

“Anyway, there’s not much to do in the winter. I just thought it might be fun to look for someone like that, partly just to kill time.”

Hearing that, the guild clerk nodded in understanding.

During the winter season, adventurers are generally very idle.

Given that, even if they thought it was a bit of a crazy idea to search for a person or item—whose existence they weren’t even sure of—just to kill time, they probably accepted it.

However, Vihera, who knew Rei well, looked at him and thought he must be serious about actually searching for it.
