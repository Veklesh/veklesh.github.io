What’s more, Rei was surrounded by Elena, Marina, and Vihera—all stunning beauties.

For Rei, who had come to this land to obtain fish, it was a stroke of luck that such skilled fishermen were gathered here.

And it wasn’t just fish.

Starting with abalone and turban shells—though they might not be exactly the same, they look similar—they also gather large quantities of edible seaweed for him.

Having them gather these items was, for Rei, almost a form of selfishness.

The fact that they listened carefully to his requests left a very favorable impression on Rei.

Of course, if he really wanted a massive amount of fish, he could just go to Emoshion.

(But since Emoshion is a port town where lots of people gather, trouble is bound to happen.)

While Rei never hesitated to use his power, that didn’t mean he enjoyed getting dragged into trouble.

If there were some kind of reward—like a magic item—it might be worth it, but most troubles don’t offer anything like that.

That said, if there were monsters with bounties on them, like Remrace, Rei would be all for going to Emoshion.

“Oh, Rei. Out to buy fish again today?”

Appearing before Rei and Pastra were the slave trader, Lilrose, and two guards who had come from Jubis.

“Yeah, I’m thinking of heading back to Gilm soon. I just came by to say hello.”

Lirose muttered thoughtfully.

From Lilrose’s perspective, Rei was the person who had arranged for him to do business in Gilm, but at the same time, he was the one who had saddled him with the difficult task of bringing pirates to Gilm.

That said, it was precisely because of that difficult task that she was able to do business in Gilm, so she certainly didn’t hold a grudge against him.

“As for the pirates, I’m counting on you.”

“What is it?”

Rei had thought about bringing up the land ships, but even in Gilm, only a select few knew about them.

He probably realized that bringing it up here would cause all sorts of problems.

He glossed over it.

Lirose, of course, realized he was being brushed off, but he didn’t press the matter further.

She probably understood that if she pressed him here—and ended up learning the details—it wouldn’t be surprising if she were silenced in the worst-case scenario.

If she asked Rei, he’d probably say there’s no way he’d do something like that… but still, for Lilrose, who had been active in the countryside until now, it was only natural that she couldn’t help but be suspicious.

“For now, just go about the transaction as usual and you’ll be fine, so don’t worry about it.”

“...I suppose so. Since it’s coming from Rei, who goes by the alias ‘Crimson,’ I’ll take your word for it.”

The fact that she went out of her way to mention Rei’s alias—especially in front of the guards—suggested she didn’t fully trust his words.

Rei vaguely understood Lilrose’s suspicions, but he didn’t say anything more about it.

She understood that the request she’d made to Lilrose at Daskar’s behest was, in many ways, beyond the bounds of common sense.

“I see… So Rei is leaving. Rei. I caused you a great deal of trouble with this pirate incident. And I’d like to thank you for defeating the pirates.”

One of the guards who had been listening to Rei and Lilrose—a man in his forties—said this and bowed his head to Rei.

From the guards’ perspective, Galanka wasn’t exactly their assigned post.

But even so, as a guard of Jubis—the most prosperous city in the entire region—he took pride in his duty.

That was precisely why, upon learning that Galanka—not far from Jubis, though still a few days’ journey away—was being threatened by pirates, he must have felt a deep sense of responsibility.

“I only ended up here by chance. Just make sure to guard this place properly from now on.”

“...That’s right. If you do that, it’ll be a great help to us.”

The one who spoke was the elderly village chief of Galanka.

Rei had said there was no need to bring her along, but Pastra must have thought it best to send someone to fetch her.

One way or another, everyone who had become deeply involved with Rei in this incident had gathered here.

“You really didn’t have to go out of your way to come.”

“What are you saying? If it weren’t for you, those pirates would have caused great damage to this village. Since the benefactor who saved us is returning, it’s only natural for me, as the village chief, to come and greet you.”

Rei laughed along with the village chief, who was grinning and chuckling, “Heh heh heh.”

In fact, the damage Galanka would have suffered from this incident would have been considerable.

It was only natural for the villagers to feel indebted to Rei for resolving the situation.

……Rei understood that, of course, but he wasn’t particularly fond of such a grand display, so he had gone out of his way to tell only Pastra that he was returning.

“Anyway. I’m sure you had your own reasons… but there’s no doubt that Galanka was saved thanks to you. I’m grateful.”

In fact, it seemed entirely possible that, in the future, the city might not even be able to sustain itself...

Rei was still young and hadn’t paid much attention to such matters, but he had heard his parents discuss them many times.

Even in Japan, it’s hard to revive a place that’s becoming depopulated, so Rei could easily predict that in this world of Elgin, it would be impossible unless something extraordinary happened.

That said, if a mineral vein were discovered near Galanka, or if some kind of research facility were built there, it wasn’t impossible that Galanka could develop… but if you asked whether that was actually feasible, it would be a long shot.

“Here you go. I decided to keep it simple and just grill it with salt. But since I cooked it in Rei-chan’s oven instead of over an open flame, it should be delicious.”

Since this was their last dinner in this place, Bistol had put his heart and soul into the meal.

The first dish to be served was a simple oven-roasted shrimp, about six meters long, that Rei had caught during today’s fishing trip.

That said, just because the cooking method was simple didn’t mean the dish itself hadn’t taken a lot of effort.

Slits were made in several places to prevent the meat from curling due to the heat, and the shrimp, flavored with wild herbs and mountain vegetables, allowed one to fully enjoy the shrimp’s deliciousness and texture precisely because of its simplicity.

What stood out most was that, despite its enormous size, the shrimp wasn’t at all bland.

The meat was incredibly delicious, possessing an umami flavor even more intense than the shrimp Rei had eaten back in Japan.

“Huff, huff… Mmm, delicious!”

At Rei’s words, everyone else happily agreed.

And just like that, the simple dish of grilled shrimp was devoured by Rei and the others in the blink of an eye.

(Grilling… Come to think of it, there was a dish called shio-gama-yaki… Maybe I’ll try making that next time. I think you just wrap it in salt and bake it, so it shouldn’t be too hard.)

While eating the shrimp, Rei was thinking about a new dish, but in reality, making shio-gama-yaki requires adding egg whites to the salt, so if he tried to make it based on his own idea, it wouldn’t turn out right.

That said, since Rei’s approach is to share the idea of a dish with a professional chef and have them prepare it, he hasn’t had any major failures so far.

(It won’t be long before Gamelion season. If I made shio-gama-yaki with Gamelion meat… hmm, that sounds delicious.)

Rei’s Misty Ring contains a large amount of fresh Gamelion meat he caught last year, but it’s only natural to want to eat ingredients at their peak when they’ve just been hunted.

As Rei imagines salt-crusted Gamelion, the next dish prepared by Bistol is served.

“Here you go, stir-fried seafood.”

There was a dish of stir-fried seafood—fish, shellfish, octopus, and more.

Just like the shrimp earlier, various wild herbs were used to ensure the flavor didn’t get monotonous.

Also, having learned from his cooking since arriving at the seaside that shellfish in particular become too tough if overcooked, Bistol had paid close attention to the cooking time.

(Seafood stir-fried noodles with thickened sauce... no, maybe seafood udon with thickened sauce? But I wonder how they make potato starch? It seems like it would be a pretty versatile ingredient if we had it.)

Thinking such things, Rei ate the stir-fry... and continued to savor the various dishes Bistol prepared afterward, thoroughly enjoying their final supper.
