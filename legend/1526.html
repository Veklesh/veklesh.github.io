Zelus urged Rei and the others to sit on the sofa, and cold fruit water was promptly served before them.

While Rei would normally have fruit water chilled in large quantities during winter in his Misty Ring, this was fruit water cooled by a magic item.

It was cold... so cold, in fact, that one might think it would start to freeze if chilled just a bit more.

Rei understood why one might crave something like this in summer, but he could easily imagine that it was precisely because Zelus drank this kind of thing... and because he didn't move his body... that he was overweight.

Then again, the guildmaster's job is to run the guild, not to fight.

In fact, Walker, who succeeded Marina as Guildmaster, possessed exceptional abilities as a leader. Yet, viewed purely as an adventurer... while he had some skill, he couldn't hold his own against veteran adventurers.

No, if someone talented came along, he might even lose to someone who just started adventuring.

In that sense, Zels probably does possess high ability as a guildmaster.

Watching Zels drink the chilled fruit water with relish, Rei thought this as he reached for his own glass.

The others also reached for the fruit water, just like Rei... Seeing this, Zels finally spoke up.

"So, what exactly is this business? You mentioned not wanting to draw attention..."

"Yes, that's right. ...Actually, I need the guild to introduce me to a trustworthy informant. One who absolutely isn't connected to anyone else... especially not Jarya."

Hearing Marina's words, Zels frowned, his face full of excess flesh.

When it came to informants, there were certainly those connected to the guild.

But finding one untouched by Jaya's influence... that was extremely difficult.

Jaya was the organization boasting the greatest power within the nation of Reburina.

"It's an organization with connections even to the upper echelons of the state. Even if its influence is confined within this country, Jaya's power is considerable."

"...Oh?"

Vihera, who had been listening to Zels' explanation, suddenly found herself questioning those words.

Zels paused his explanation and turned his gaze toward Vihera.

"Something?"

"No, wait—is Jarya only active in the Kingdom of Reburina? I mean, no offense, but Reburina is a small country, right? If they're looking to make money, wouldn't it be more efficient to expand into other nations?"

Indeed, if the goal was purely profit, that would undoubtedly be the best choice.

No matter how you looked at it, the Kingdom of Reburina was, after all, just a small country.

Even among the vassal states of the Mireana Kingdom, there were plenty of nations more developed and larger than Reburina.

In fact, most of the countries surrounding Reburina are larger than it.

So why limit activities solely within Reburina?

That puzzled Vihera.

"Well, Ajax did make a move on Gilm, and most of the prostitutes in Mejougo seem to be women forcibly taken from other countries."

"But that's... well, I don't like putting it this way, but it's like going to get the raw materials for your business, right? Besides, the prostitutes working in Mejougo were brought here by force, but now they're doing it willingly."

Several people grimaced at Vihera's description of the prostitutes as "business materials."

Still, they didn't blame Vihera, understanding he wasn't using those words out of choice.

And they couldn't help but agree with Vihera's point that Jaya wasn't active in other countries.

"So then, is there something about this country..."

"In this country? But... I hate to say it, but we're just a small country without any particular distinguishing features, you know?"

Hearing Zels speak, Rei thought, "Well, he does live here," but at the same time, she wondered, "Could it be that living here makes him unaware of certain things?"

"Anyway, I understand that Jaya holds significant influence over this country."

Marina, realizing the conversation was drifting off course, forcibly steered it back.

The fact that Jaya only operates within the Kingdom of Reburina, and in other countries merely forcibly brings women to work at Mejougo... was somewhat intriguing. But the current issue was about the informant.

"Ah, sorry. ...Anyway, for that reason, it might be a bit difficult in terms of the informant the guild uses."

Zels stated this, but upon hearing his words, Marina let out a small smile.

The others, including Rei, reacted similarly. Only Byune continued eating the dried fruit served with the fruit juice, expressionless.

"Saying 'the informant used by the guild'... does that mean you have another possibility in mind? Like, an informant Zelus uses independently of the guild... or something?"

"...You saw right through me."

At Marina's words, Zels smiled, his cheeks jiggling.

"Yes. The moment you used the phrase 'the informant the guild uses,' it sounded suspicious. So? Do you have other connections?"

"Yes. Actually, there's an informant I use personally, not through the guild. This informant harbors deep resentment toward Jaya."

"...You really use such an informant? If you pull that sort of stunt in the capital of this country, Reburina, won't that just draw Jaya's attention?"

"You're right. Honestly, under normal circumstances, I wouldn't risk such dangerous behavior either."

As if Marina's words had reminded him of something, a hint of sadness flickered in Zels' eyes.

It was an expression that suggested Zels and the informant had some past connection with Jaya.

But, deciding it wasn't the place to say more, Zels spoke before Rei or the others could say anything.

"A man named Unchu. He's been investigating various things about Jaya. I'm certain he'll be of use to Marina-sama... no, to all of you."

"Unchu, huh... But if there's an informant like that, couldn't you have told us about him yesterday?"

"He's also my friend. Given that, even someone like Marina-sama couldn't be introduced so easily... Besides, Jaya has eyes everywhere."

Hearing this, Rei raised his estimation of Zels.

He had seemed like the type to be swayed by Marina's charms, but apparently not.

(Well, he is the Guildmaster, so perhaps it's only natural he can handle such things.)

Even as he harbored the disrespectful thought that even a rotten sea bream is still a sea bream, the conversation between Marina and Zels continued.

"Not far from here, there's an inn. It has a sign with a sun painted on it, so you shouldn't have trouble finding it. Give this to the innkeeper and ask if they have Meel cow cheese. They should then guide you to where the Unchu are kept."

Rising from the sofa, Zels retrieved a dagger with a blue gem set at its pommel from a desk drawer and handed it to Marina.

What that dagger signified was obvious enough.

Rei thought it was quite a stringent measure, but he understood that opposing someone like Jaya in this country must be that serious.

He also reasoned that someone like Unchu was ultimately just an informant, and in terms of direct combat strength, he probably wasn't that formidable.

"Yes, thank you. Then I'll head straight to that tavern. ...It's a bit late now, but is it really okay for you to introduce us to this Unchu person? What if we end up targeted by Jaya..."

"That won't change, given we're already sheltering Unchu. In fact, we're the ones who'll be utilizing Marina-sama's forces."

Zelus's laughter, his cheeks jiggling, echoed through the office.

Rei and the others listened to Zels' laughter, then all stood up.

"Thank you, Zels."

Marina said with a smile. Zels returned it with a broad grin and nodded silently.

Then, Rei and the others left the office, went straight downstairs, and exited the guild.

Though they drew curious stares from guild staff and adventurers wondering what they had discussed, no one approached Rei or the others to ask.

It was likely because many knew they were acquaintances of the Guildmaster.

Several adventurers who hadn't been present when Rei and the others arrived were now there, but even they, perhaps having heard about Rei and his group from others, only watched from a distance.

As they exited the guild, Set, who had noticed Rei, approached happily.

Seeing Set like this, the other passersby nearby were simply startled and immediately tried to keep their distance.

"Grrrrr!"

"There, calm down. See? I came right back, didn't I?"

Rei said, but Set rubbed his face against Rei's body.

If this were Gilm, Set wouldn't be this lonely.

But here, in the Kingdom of Reburina, there was no one to dote on Set.

Given that, Set had plenty of time to just wait around... and above all, knowing he was feared probably made him lonely.

That's precisely why, seeing his beloved Rei arrive, he wanted to be affectionate.

Stroking Set like that, Rei turned his gaze toward Marina.

"Well then, shall we go?"

"Yeah."

And so, the entire group headed toward their next destination... the tavern with the sign bearing a painting of the sun.

Thanks to Set being there, they walked the streets without any trouble... and soon found their destination.

About a twenty-minute walk from the guild.

The fact they arrived without any particular commotion made them feel a little doubtful... probably because Rei understood he was the kind of trouble-maker type.

"Grr..."

"Sorry, Set. You'll have to wait again."

Having been petted by Rei and the others during the walk here, Set felt an extra pang of loneliness at having to wait alone.

(If Yellow were here, things might be different. But then again, that could just lead to more trouble.)

Set's friend... or rather, his best friend, Yellow, was currently at the Silver Fruit Inn.

Rei thought Yellow's presence might have eased Set's loneliness a little... but seeing a dragon child around meant there was no guarantee someone wouldn't get any bad ideas.

Here, not many knew what kind of being Yellow truly was.

Precisely because they knew nothing... it was highly likely some might do something to provoke Elena.

Given that organizations like Jaya already wielded significant influence in the Kingdom of Reburina, such an incident wouldn't be surprising.

With these thoughts in mind, Rei and the others left Set behind and entered the tavern.

Left behind, Set curled up a short distance from the tavern, perhaps deciding his presence would obstruct the path.

"Welcome..."

The tavern owner started to greet Rei and the others as they entered, but his voice trailed off mid-sentence.

It was daytime, and several patrons were eating inside. They too noticed the innkeeper's abruptly halted words, found it unnatural, followed his gaze, and similarly froze in place.

The impact Rei and her group had on their surroundings was that profound.

Ignoring the stares, Rei and the others walked straight toward the counter.

The tavern owner, perhaps coming to his senses, waited for Rei to approach.

When Rei finally reached him, he spoke again.

"Welcome. Food? Or drink?"

"...No. Neither."

As she spoke, Marina placed the dagger she'd received from Zels on the counter where the owner could see it.

Seeing the dagger, the owner's face stiffened in surprise, and he looked at Marina, who held it.

To the startled innkeeper, Marina offered a seductive smile and spoke.

"Do you have any Meel cow cheese?"

At Marina's question, an even greater look of astonishment than mere seconds before spread across the shopkeeper's face.
