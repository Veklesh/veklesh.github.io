At night, at Marina's house, Rei was recounting the events of the day while reaching for a sandwich on the table.

Listening were Marina, Vihera, Ara... and Elena.

Set was playing a little ways off, running around with Yellow under the moonlight.

Elena and Ara had originally planned to stay at the lord's mansion, but in the end, they had followed Marina's suggestion and were spending the night at this house.

Since it was Marina's suggestion, Daskar didn't particularly object.

No, rather, he probably thought it better for them to stay at Marina's house than under his own roof.

Indeed, upon hearing that Elena had arrived in Gilm, nobles from the Noble Faction had come to Daskar's residence that very day, demanding an audience.

For Daskar, dealing with such people was a hassle, and above all, he had plenty of work to do regarding the expansion.

Getting that work done was far more important to Daskar.

...Of course, some of the nobles from the Noble Faction had come knowing Elena was staying at Marina's house.

But Marina's household fundamentally employed no one.

Even the upkeep of the house was handled using spirit magic.

As a result, most people turned back at Marina's house, which had no gatekeeper.

Some attempted to enter Marina's house without permission, but such individuals were thoroughly punished by the spirits.

Most simply left a letter behind.

In any case, for these reasons, Elena and Ara were spending their time leisurely at Marina's house.

Fortunately, since the incident with Lerdact, Duke Kerebel had issued strict orders, so no problems with the Noble Faction had arisen.

For now, that is.

"Speaking of which, I saw a strange fellow at the guild today."

Rei, who had been recounting the story—about how several orcs had come to the site where the new wall was being built last night, and the adventurers who defeated them promptly butchered the orcs, grilled the meat, and ate it, only to have the smell attract a swarm of monsters that came and ate almost all of it—suddenly changed the subject.

"Weird? Well, with this many people gathering, not just adventurers, it wouldn't be surprising if there were one or two oddballs, right?"

"Hmm."

Byune, who was wrapping steamed orc meat in steamed vegetables—which looked like lettuce but had red and blue mottled patterns—dipping them in a strongly sour sauce made from fruit, and eating them, nodded in agreement with Vihera's words.

Indeed, Vihera's observation wasn't wrong at all.

With this many people gathered, it was only natural that a certain number of oddballs would be mixed in.

"Hmm, but he's weird in a different sense."

"You mean, like at the guild?"

Marina murmured, likely recalling what happened at the guild after hearing Rei's words.

"Yeah. Weird... no, he was peculiar, right?"

"I wouldn't go so far as to call him that... but yeah, he did seem a bit different from the norm."

Marina's meaningful mutter drew curious glances from the others present.

Though both Rei and Marina were the focus of those gazes, it was Rei who ended up speaking about the incident.

"There was a woman in the guild who tried to draw her weapon."

"...That's all?"

Shaking his head at Vihera, who silently conveyed disappointment, Rei continued.

"If it had been some hot-blooded adventurer or someone showing off their weapon, I wouldn't be saying anything. But what if the one trying to draw her weapon was just an ordinary woman?"

"Well, she was armed in the guild, so she wasn't just an ordinary woman, she was an adventurer, right?"

"Is that what you think, Marina? From where I stood, she didn't look like she had the skills to survive in Gilm, not by a long shot. ...Even if she was an adventurer, I'd guess she was just tagging along with some party or other."

"But would someone like that draw their weapon in the guild?"

Elena, who had been listening to Rei and Marina's conversation, murmured with doubt.

A novice... someone practically a complete amateur should have easily understood just how dangerous it was to draw a weapon in the Gilm Guild.

"That's true. But... given he was radiating killing intent, however crude, it couldn't have been a game or anything like that."

"Killing intent... huh. To pull something like that in the Gilm Guild takes some serious guts."

Vihera murmured thoughtfully.

"And the target of that killing intent hadn't even noticed the woman's presence. ...Well, with this many adventurers gathered, it might be unavoidable, but I hope it doesn't turn into something nasty."

"Well, with this many people gathered, problems are bound to happen."

Marina said, as if to comfort Rei, handing her a fruit drink chilled with spirit magic.

"Thanks."

"It's fine. ...So, what do you think will happen to that girl?"

For Marina, a dark elf, even if someone looked adult-like on the outside, she still treated them like a child.

And yet, she herself loved Rei, who appeared even younger.

"What do you mean, what happens? I don't think what happens at the guild concerns me."

"The guild doesn't get involved in conflicts between adventurers, so I hope it doesn't turn into a big mess. You're the former guildmaster, Marina. Can't you do something?"

"Listen, Vihera. If it were just Gilm's guild rules, maybe. But I can't possibly change rules that apply to every guild, can I?"

"...So you could do something about rules specific to Gilm?"

Ara, who had been listening to the three of them, directed a look at Marina that was both surprised... and yet somehow understanding.

It was a mixture of two emotions: the question of whether Marina, who had served as guildmaster for so many years, still held that much influence, and the strange sense of trust that perhaps she really could make it happen...

"Heh heh, I wonder..."

Marina responded to Ara with only a smile, cutting off any further inquiry.

Ara probably understood that saying more would lead to trouble. She said nothing further on the matter.

Instead, Elena spoke up.

Partly to shield Ara, but also because she genuinely wondered.

"Rei, come to think of it, it's been about ten days since I arrived at Gilm. How's the wall coming along?"

"Well, I've finished digging my part. Now we just wait for the other mages to do theirs."

Using terrain manipulation, he had caused every possible area to sink.

That left the rest up to the other mages.

Though, the other mages were thinking that Rei had been a huge help by sinking the first meter using terrain manipulation.

"I see. So what is Rei doing now?"

"He's got a lot of odd jobs. The biggest one is transporting trees from Treant Forest."

"...I suppose so. There's just too much difference in cost and manpower between transporting normally felled trees and having Rei transport them."

Marina murmured thoughtfully.

When Rei was heading to the Earl of Lerdact's domain, Marina had used spirit magic to assist the lumberjacks.

But even with Marina's extraordinary—or perhaps beyond common sense—spirit magic skills, transporting the trees Rei felled took incomparably less time.

...Of course. Rei could store felled trees in his Misty Ring with a single touch, and using Set to move them from Treant Forest to Gilm took mere minutes, if that.

No matter how skilled Marina was with spirit magic, it was impossible for her to compete with Rei like that.

Of course, compared to ordinary lumberjacks cutting down trees and hauling them to Gilm, Marina's spirit magic made the task vastly easier.

In this case, it was simply a matter of having the wrong comparison.

Still, since most woodcutters are men, if asked whether they'd rather work with Rei or Marina, the latter would be the obvious choice without a second thought.

In fact, many actually welcomed the longer transport times as an opportunity to spend more time with Marina.

The prime example of this is probably Danzā, the woodcutter who once picked a fight with Rei.

That said, having experienced firsthand just how capable Rei truly is, Danzā now focuses earnestly on his woodcutting work and rarely acts high and mighty toward the others anymore.

"Treant Forest... Come to think of it, there were all sorts of rumors about it."

It was Elena who reacted to the mention of Treant Forest.

Even for tales from the frontier, the ever-expanding Treant Forest possessed enough power to become the subject of rumors.

And then, ultimately, a gigantic monster called the Giganto-Turtle emerged from that very Treant Forest. There was no way it wouldn't become the talk of the town.

And if one gathered even a little information about Gilm, there was no way that story wouldn't reach their ears.

"That's right. I've heard stories of adventurers, starting with Rei, defeating a giant monster."

Ara continued Elena's words, stating this.

"Ah, the Giganto Turtle."

"Ah, that reminds me..."

Vihera, who had been eating a summer vegetable and cheese salad, interjected upon hearing the words "Giganto Turtle."

"Actually, after work today, I saw something a bit dangerous."

"A dangerous situation?"

"Yeah. There was this female adventurer who looked like she'd just arrived in Gilm, wandering down the road leading to the slums. And before she even got to the slums, she got hassled by some down-and-out adventurers."

"Ah... that kind of problem."

They came to Gilm seeking work, but the jobs turned out to be tougher than expected, or they couldn't get along with their coworkers, bosses, or subordinates...

Rei knew about others who dropped out for various reasons too—those who couldn't or wouldn't work.

Naturally, such people couldn't afford to pay for lodging.

Some even considered threatening Gilm's residents to steal money, but not only the guards, but adventurers like Vihera were patrolling those areas.

Moreover, the slum dwellers understood that causing unnecessary trouble in the current situation would only bring significant trouble upon themselves later.

As for the underworld figures, they were adventurers, rotten though they might be. They absolutely refused to allow those possessing any degree of power to gather and form a new faction.

For these reasons, those who threatened the city's residents faced attacks and were driven out by various factions.

The only saving grace was that these attacks didn't involve killing.

Regardless, since it was undeniable that such people existed, something had to be done.

...Actually, this was also related to what had been troubling Daskar lately.

"Hey, Rei. Speaking of which, what about dismantling the Giganto Turtle? Didn't the guild say they'd recruit people to do it once adventurers started running out of work?"

Needless to say, the Giganto Turtle was currently stored inside the Misty Ring possessed by Rei.

But at Vihera's words, Rei shook his head.

"Nah, it's too soon. And if you mean looking for work, there's plenty of other jobs out there besides what we've been doing. These guys won't touch that stuff. Even if Giganto Turtle dismantling shows up as a request, they probably won't come to us."

In truth, Gilm currently has no shortage of work.

Besides the expansion work, there's gathering materials, escorting merchants and travelers, and so on.

Worst case, there were plenty of odd jobs available within the Gilm itself.

You could hunt monsters and sell materials, magic stones, or proof-of-kill parts, but since Gilm is in the frontier, unexpectedly high-rank monsters are often just hanging around outside town. That'd be tough for anyone without a certain level of skill.

"Hmm, now that you mention it, you might be right. But then, what about those people? What do we do with them?"

"Honestly, there's nothing we can do about it."

If you're willing to work, there's plenty of it in Gilm right now.

You could even call it a special demand.

But if they have no intention of working in the first place, there's nothing we can do about that either.

Though the others looked disappointed at Rei's words, they could only nod in agreement.
