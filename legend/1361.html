The tree severed from its trunk by the Death Scythe came to rest leaning against another nearby tree.

Fortunately, if one could call it that, the fallen tree did not break any other trees.

This was likely because the surrounding trees were roughly the same thickness as the one Rei had cut down.

(Come to think of it, that's unnatural too. Most of the trees being roughly the same thickness...)

When Rei was in Japan, he had gone into the mountains countless times... literally too many times to count.

That's precisely why seeing all the trees within sight as being the same thickness felt utterly wrong.

Even if trees were planted specifically for timber harvesting from the start, various conditions inevitably cause each trunk to grow differently.

You could see the difference with your own eyes.

But looking out over Treant's forest like this, every single tree has a perfectly uniform thickness.

Of course, if you measured them precisely, there would be some variation.

But at least to the naked eye, they all look the same thickness.

(I probably should have noticed this yesterday... but well, it doesn't matter. Either way, what I need to do remains the same. Besides, I don't even know why they want this wood in the first place.)

To be precise, I do understand the reason for wanting this wood.

Since this forest produces such fine timber day after day, it must have many uses.

But if asked what it's specifically used for, even Rei couldn't say.

No, it's more accurate to say there are too many possibilities to narrow it down.

"Rei? What's wrong?"

Marina called out to Rei, who was staring intently at the tree he had just cut down.

Snapping back to reality at the sound of her voice, Rei shook his head, signaling it was nothing. He touched the tree he had cut down, which was leaning against a nearby tree, and stored it along with the Death Scythe in the Misty Ring.

"Well, today's task is done for now... I guess I'll head back to Gilm. I need to submit this tree to the guild too."

"Is that so? Right. Then let's do that."

Marina started to say something in response to Rei's words, but quickly gave up and agreed.

She probably just wanted to get out of this Treant Forest as quickly as possible...

Neither Rei nor Set had any objections.

The two humans and one beast continued walking through the forest.

(In the end, no Treant-like creatures appeared. I cut down a tree, so I was sure something would come out.)

This place was called Treant Forest, and above all, Slesha had been attacked here. There was no doubt something dangerous lurked within.

But even after cutting down the trees like this, nothing happened.

Rei and the others decided staying any longer was pointless and simply left the forest.

"...Nothing happened after all. I was sure something would happen the moment we left the forest and let our guard down."

"In this case, should I be glad about that... or disappointed?"

Marina was probably still concerned about the forest she'd just left.

She murmured while glancing back... but there was still no sign of anything unusual.

No, the fact that not a single animal was visible in a forest this size was the real anomaly.

Deciding that nothing more would happen while they waited like this, Rei pulled a spear from his Misty Ring.

It wasn't the Twilight Spear she'd been using lately, but rather a throwing spear she'd used before acquiring it.

It was a useless piece of junk, with parts like the shaft and tip missing or rusted away.

It was a spear meant to be literally disposable, and the one Rei pulled out this time had a broken tip.

A tip that wouldn't pierce with a normal thrust... but when Rei threw it, that was a different story.

"Rei? What are you doing? Could it be..."

Had she sensed a monster?

Marina asked with her eyes, but Rei shook his head.

"No, it's to establish a reference point for how far this forest extends tomorrow. Yesterday we could judge from Slesha's campsite ruins, but it'd be a hassle to move all the way back there every time, right?"

"True. Since Rei is the one coming back tomorrow, I think it's better to be thoroughly prepared for that sort of thing."

Marina agreed with Rei's words, and in the end, nothing else happened as Rei and the others left the spot.

After the two people and one creature vanished, what remained was a forest worthy of being called silent, where only the chirping of birds echoed around them.

"Ah, Rei! Long time no see! How have you been?"

As they entered the guild, that voice echoed.

Kenny, already a familiar face to Rei, waved cheerfully.

"Yeah, nothing much to report. ...But more importantly—"

Waving briefly to Kenny, Rei's gaze shifted to Lenora sitting next to her.

"I cut down a tree. Where should I take it? It's pretty big, so I can't just leave it here."

"Ah, for that, I'll show you to the warehouse. ...Well, Rei has used that warehouse several times before, so you probably don't need directions."

Lenora's words reminded Rei of the time he'd brought in a large quantity of Gamelion, and when the alchemists had gathered to study Ambris.

It was true they'd been busy back then, but looking back now, he found himself feeling strangely nostalgic about those times.

"Hey, Lenora. If you're going to show Rei the warehouse, I should..."

"Kenny has his own work to do, right? Once you're done with that, maybe you could help us out over here?"

"Well..."

Lenora's gaze was fixed on several documents in Kenny's hands.

Of course, since those documents were also crucial for the guild's important work, she couldn't just leave them.

But still... Kenny shot Lenora a slightly resentful look.

Lenora shot Kenny a look mixed with exasperation and opened her mouth.

"Look, if you'd been doing your job properly in the first place, I could have left it to you, Kenny! But because you weren't doing your work, you can't guide Rei... Honestly, telling me that just makes things difficult for me."

"Mph... Lenora's mean, stingy, flat-chested."

"...Oh? Did you say something, you incompetent fool who can't even handle your own work properly?"

At the word "flat-chested," Lenora's eyebrow twitched for just an instant, but she showed no further reaction. Instead, she asserted her superiority.

Lenora flashed a triumphant smile at Kenny, who was gritting his teeth in frustration.

Usually Kenny got the better of her, but today she was certain of her victory, hence the smile.

Seeing Kenny's frustrated look, Lenora seemed satisfied... but the fact that Rei was here meant Marina, who had been with Rei today, was also present...

Noticing Marina's gaze of disbelief directed at her, mirroring the one Lenora had given Kenny, Lenora hurriedly opened her mouth.

"Come on, let's go. There's no point wasting time here."

"Right."

Marina said nothing more, but that silence only fueled Lenora's fear even more.

The fundamental issue was that Marina no longer considered herself Guildmaster.

That's precisely why she didn't feel the need to say anything, even if she appeared a little lax.

Looking slightly flustered, Lenora led Marina and the others out of the guild.

Since it was still a little early in the afternoon, there weren't many adventurers inside the guild at the moment.

It was spring, after all, so most adventurers were probably busy with serious work at the moment.

"Speaking of which, did you inform the other adventurers about the Treant Forest?"

As they walked toward the warehouse, Rei suddenly asked.

As Rei's investigation clearly showed, Treant Forest was a place of considerable importance.

But if anyone found out about it, they'd naturally take an interest.

...Though, for now, only a handful know that Treant Forest timber is exceptionally excellent as building material.

Adventurers who know nothing about it would gain nothing by heading to Treant Forest out of mere curiosity.

Worse, they might get attacked like Slesha did.

(Well, considering Slesha's case, the Treant Forest is probably only active at night, so it should be fine. ...Oh, wait. Does that mean if you go to the Treant Forest at night, you're guaranteed to encounter monsters?)

Rei only just realized this now, but since he only learned about Treant Forest yesterday, it couldn't be helped.

"We've posted a notice about Treant Forest on the request board. But apparently, there's nothing particularly special about the forest itself, so we haven't banned anyone from going in. ...If we did ban it, people might actually find that intriguing and try to go anyway."

"Ah... I see."

Suddenly, the faces of several acquaintances flashed through Rei's mind.

Most of them seemed to be adventurers... many who followed his own style.

Precisely because of that, if it were forbidden, it wouldn't be entirely impossible for someone to approach it on their own.

But conversely, if it's not forbidden, and they're saying there are no monsters or anything there... then what happens?

Precisely because it wasn't forbidden, most would likely think there was no need to go out of their way.

Of course, some might still give in to their curiosity and head for Treant Forest, but it's impossible to regulate everyone's actions.

That part is entirely on their own responsibility.

"The location of Treant Forest is significantly off the main road. Under normal circumstances, one wouldn't even consider going there... but well, that varies from person to person."

Despite the guild warning people to stay away, if someone still heads to Treant Forest on their own, the guild bears no responsibility for whatever happens.

That's what they're trying to say.

While Rei couldn't say he didn't have some thoughts on the matter, if someone ignored warnings and threw themselves into danger, that was their own responsibility.

Lost in such thoughts, he arrived at the warehouse.

Rei unlocked the door and stepped inside the warehouse, but...

"Nothing here at all."

True to his words, there was nothing special inside the warehouse.

Only a vast empty space lay before him.

"Yes. Materials and such are stored in other warehouses. This one isn't being used yet. ...Though, in time, materials will likely be brought here too."

Lenora murmured, glancing around the room as she listened to Rei's explanation.

Marina, as Guildmaster, likely knew the ins and outs of such matters. She nodded silently.

Adventurers starting work in spring sell materials to the guild, which are then stored in the warehouse... As the seasons turn through summer and autumn, those materials accumulate.

Of course, the guild doesn't just hoard them; they sell them to earn funds, so it's not like they just keep piling up.

Vaguely understanding the situation, Rei quickly got to the point.

"So, where should I put the wood I cut down?"

"Ah, yes. Anywhere in this warehouse is fine... but, well, please put them somewhere not too far from the door so we can get to them quickly."

"Then this spot should be fine."

Following Lenora's instructions, Rei moved to a spot not far from the door.

And in the next instant, a felled tree lay sprawled inside the warehouse.

"Wow... It's amazing every time I see it."

Lenora's muttered voice held surprise.

She'd seen Rei use the Misty Ring countless times before, but seeing an entire giant tree like this inside still left her astonished.

"So, is this tree fine as is? No need to cut off branches or anything like that?"

"Um, yes, that's right. The client said they'd handle that part themselves."

"I see."

To be precise, it was Daskar's subordinate... the alchemist Zmos, who belongs to the Knights, who wanted the tree as-is to use as building material... or to investigate if it had other potential uses.

"In that case, I'll proceed with the payment arrangements. Please head back to the counter."

Though Rei couldn't help but think it was a hassle, verifying materials was an important part of the receptionist's job.

Considering that, this was likely the proper response for a receptionist.

Reaching that conclusion, he returned to the guild with Marina and reported that the forest was indeed expanding.
