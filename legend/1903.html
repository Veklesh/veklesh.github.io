The lunch break is over, and the afternoon’s work begins.

However, while those tasked with dismantling the Giganto Turtle could take a break, the guards tasked with fighting the monsters drawn by the smell of blood couldn’t all take a break at once, so they had to eat in shifts.

Rei, Set, Vihera, and Byune ate the food inside the Misty Ring… and they also distributed a bowl of warm, freshly made vegetable soup to each of the workers engaged in the dismantling.

Although it wasn’t snowing, they were working in temperatures near freezing, so naturally, those who received the soup were delighted.

Considering that the Giganto Turtle’s butchering was proceeding more smoothly than it had in the morning thanks to that, I suppose it was a necessary expense.

“So? What are you going to do now, Rei? Are you going to stick around here a little longer? Or are you heading back to Gilm? You said you’d look into that kobold business a bit.”

“Yeah. I’m heading back soon. I’m going to check out the construction site where the kobolds are attacking and see if I can find any clues.”

“Hmm… but there’s no guarantee you’ll find any clues, you know? After all, so many people have been running around trying to figure out why the kobolds are attacking, but no one’s found anything yet.”

Rei understood what Vihera was getting at, but this time, he had a trump card: Set.

There was a very real possibility that Set could find something that even humans—including Rei—or even beastmen and elves with senses sharper than humans’ couldn’t detect.

Following Rei’s gaze, Vihera saw Set playing with Byune—perhaps because there were no monsters left to fight—and from that alone, she understood that Rei was relying on Set to investigate the kobold incident.

“I see. Set, then.”

“...You figured it out, didn’t you?”

“Anyone could tell just by following Rei’s gaze.”

Now that she mentioned it… he recalled his own actions, felt a slight flush of embarrassment, and opened his mouth as if to cover it up.

“Anyway, I’m heading to the site where I’m working on the expansion with Set… What about you, Vihera? Want to come along?”

When asked, Vihera thought for a moment before shaking her head.

“I’ll pass. After all, the only monsters we’ll be fighting are kobolds.”

For Vihera, she’d probably want to fight strong monsters of her own accord, but when it comes to monsters like kobolds, she likely doesn’t even consider it worth fighting.

If there were a confirmed presence of high-tier or rare monsters, she might be somewhat interested... but since no such monsters had been found since the kobold attacks began, Vihera had no interest in that.

“Besides, it seems like Byune is actually looking forward to defeating the kobolds here.”

“Ah, I see.”

That reason is probably the stronger one.

For Rei, too, this whole affair was merely a hobby—or rather, something he approached with the mindset that it was a long shot—so he didn’t intend to force Vihera to come along.

That said, if there had actually been some kind of decisive evidence or proof, things might have been different.

“Hey, can I talk to you for a sec?”

After finishing his conversation with Vihera, Rei called out to a guild staff member standing a short distance away—the person in charge of coordinating the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.

Since they’d spoken with Rei many times before, the person didn’t seem particularly nervous at his call and approached him.

“What’s up? Is something wrong?”

“Ah. I’m going to head out of here for a bit, but should I come back around evening?”

Since the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle obviously wouldn’t be finished in a single day, it would need to be stored inside the Misty Ring once night fell.

If this were a smaller monster, it wouldn’t be a problem to store it in a warehouse in town—especially since it’s winter now—but with a monster this size, we couldn’t exactly dismantle it right in the middle of town.

“That makes sense. Please do that. But if possible, it would be a big help if you could come back a little early. Since today is the first day, a lot of people aren’t used to this yet, so I’d like to wrap things up a bit early.”

“Got it. I’ll come back a little early then.”

“Thank you. By the way… where are you headed next, Rei?”

“Just to the construction site for a bit.”

With those words, the guild clerk must have realized what Rei was planning to do.

With a smile, he spoke up.

“I hope you find a magic item.”

That was undoubtedly the guild clerk’s true feelings.

It hadn’t been confirmed that the kobold incident was caused by a magic item, and I had expected the guild clerk probably didn’t think so either.

Still, just maybe… and this is a long shot, but there was a possibility that some kind of magic item was the cause.

Especially since Rei was using Set’s power to investigate the kobold incident, there was a very good chance they’d find some kind of clue.

If this had been an ordinary adventurer—the kind you’d find anywhere—the guild staff probably wouldn’t have had such high expectations.

But the person standing before the guild staff was Rei.

He was, after all, someone who had accomplished unbelievable feats time and time again.

One of the things Rei had done was take down the Giganto Turtle, which a large group was currently dismantling.

There couldn’t be many people capable of defeating such a massive monster—not just in Gilm… no, not even in the Kingdom of Mireana, its neighboring nations, or the entire world of Elgin.

Precisely because Rei had accomplished that, perhaps… just maybe, he might be able to find some clue regarding the kobold incident.

Even if the guild staff thought that, it was, in a sense, only natural.

Unaware that such high expectations were placed on him, Rei nodded back at the guild staff member before calling out to Set.

“Set! We’re heading back to Gilm for now!”

“Grr!”

In response to Rei’s call, he let out a cry of “Got it!” and came over to Rei’s side.

Some of the people who had been dismantling the Giganto Turtle were captivated by the sight of Set.

It was as if they wanted to admire Set.

If this were anywhere other than Gilm, most people would likely be terrified of Set’s presence… but this is Gilm.

Since some of the workers carrying out the dismantling weren’t residents of Gilm but had come for the construction work, not all of them felt affection for Set.

Despite being the focus of such varied gazes, Rei and Set left the scene and headed toward Gilm.

“Hey, over there! He went that way!”

“Yeah, I know. Man, for someone who’s practically acting on instinct alone, he sure makes some strangely clever moves sometimes!”

With those words, the spear thrust out sharply pierced the kobold’s neck.

The adventurer’s face twisted into a bitter grimace for a moment—likely because, though he’d intended to aim for the torso, he’d actually struck the neck.

If it had been autumn, he would undoubtedly have been able to pierce the torso.

The fact that he missed his mark and ended up piercing the neck was partly because the kobold that had fallen to the ground was somewhat stronger and quicker than the others... but it was also largely because his body had grown sluggish from living through the winter.

He wore that bitter expression precisely because he knew this himself.

But he didn’t have the luxury of thinking about such things right now.

Besides, quite a few kobolds have invaded this area.

Under normal circumstances, the fight would have been a bit easier, but since there were fewer kobolds today, quite a few of them had given up on hunting them and turned their attention to dismantling the Giganto Turtle.

Thanks to that, the balance between kobolds and adventurers has been restored, but that also means the battle here is proceeding as usual.

“Ah… maybe I should have gone to help dismantle the Giganto Turtle too?”

Another adventurer nearby said this as he watched the other adventurers fighting the kobolds around him.

Considering their current situation, it’s hard not to think that fighting the kobolds endlessly is a hassle.

Of course, since their funds for surviving the winter had already dwindled considerably by this point, and they’d come here having judged that hunting kobolds was the best way to earn money, there was no point in complaining.

But… if Rei, riding Set, were to arrive right at that moment, that would change things.

“Is that so? If you’re coming to help with the dismantling, that’d be a huge help to me. The more hands, the better. Though, considering the size of the Giganto Turtle, I don’t think we’ll finish dismantling it this winter.”

“Huh?”

At the sudden sound of the voice, those who weren’t engaged in combat with the kobolds and were within earshot all looked up in unison.

“No way… Why on earth is this person here?”

...Even those who had never seen Rei’s face before could easily tell who it was just by seeing the adventurer riding a griffin. ...They just knew.

“Crimson Rei!? Wait, why is someone that famous here?! This is a place where only… well, only kobolds appear!”

The adventurer who had just said they should have gone to help butcher the Giganto Turtle shouted in a panic.

Perhaps because the other adventurers shared his confusion, several of them nodded repeatedly in agreement with his words.

Watching the other adventurers, Rei explained why he had come here, as if to say, “Don’t worry about it.”

“It’s clearly odd that kobold attacks keep happening endlessly, regardless of whether it’s morning, noon, or night. In other words, I think there must be some reason for it. So, I figured I’d take this opportunity to try and find out what that reason is.”

In reality, he had come here on the hunch that there might be a magic item related to this, but he didn’t say as much.

He didn’t say that because he suspected that if he did, everyone present might become interested in that magical item and start searching for it themselves.

Even Rei doesn’t necessarily believe there’s a magic item here; it’s true that part of the reason is just to kill time.

Given that, if he carelessly mentioned the existence of a magic item and drew everyone’s attention to it… and as a result, someone got injured or even died, it would leave a bad taste in his mouth.

“So, Set and I are going to look into why the kobolds are gathering here, but you guys don’t need to worry about that—just go ahead and take them down. We won’t get in your way.”

While petting Set, he watches the adventurers fighting the kobolds, unsure whether they’ve noticed him or not.

Even though Set—a griffin—is right here, the kobolds show no sign of fleeing the scene and continue to fight the adventurers.

(Is it the same as the kobolds that came to the Giganto Turtle’s lair? …Over there, the smell of the Giganto Turtle’s blood was strong, so I thought maybe they were just focused on that and hadn’t noticed Set’s presence.)

In fact, besides the kobolds, several other monsters—starting with orcs—had been drawn by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood, but many of them didn’t notice Set’s presence.

……To be precise, even among the monsters drawn by the scent of blood, those that did notice Set’s presence likely fled immediately and never made it to the dismemberment site.

However, there isn’t really anything in this area that would attract monsters.

If I had to name something, there was the possibility that the scent of blood had transferred to Rei and Set, who were watching the Giganto Turtle being butchered, but the kobold in their line of sight didn’t seem particularly drawn to the scent of blood or show any signs of heading toward Rei and the others.

Thinking about it that way, does the kobold really not notice Set’s presence at all, or is it simply incapable of noticing him?

(In the case of higher-tier or rare species, I don’t think they’d fail to notice Set’s presence… I suppose. If that’s the case, the most likely explanation is… a magic item.)

Could it be? Could it really be?

As Rei pondered this, Set purred and rubbed his face against Rei’s.

Stroking Set’s head, Rei pondered how to search for any magical items that might be hidden in this place.
