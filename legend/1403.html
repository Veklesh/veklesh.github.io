The knight who stepped down from the carriage had a friendly demeanor, so the other adventurers, including Rei, breathed a sigh of relief in his presence.

"So the reinforcements came from the ones who returned after all?"

"That's likely. Normally, arriving at Gilm in the middle of the night would find the main gate closed, but they were informed about the Treant Forest incident this time, so they handled it immediately. Like, right away."

"...So if they're here now, that means we've been fighting the Giganto Turtle for quite a while, huh?"

"Giganto Turtle?"

At Rei's words, the knight asked back, looking puzzled.

Seeing the knight's expression, Rei remembered something and opened his mouth.

"I assume you know what I mean—that giant turtle monster. Calling it just 'giant turtle' forever would be awkward, so I gave it a name on the spot. It's not an official designation, just a temporary one for now."

"Ah, I see. ...Well, that's fine. So, about this Giganto Turtle... what are we going to do with it?"

"Well, at this size, if we just leave the corpse lying around, it'll definitely rot. So I'll take care of disposing of it."

Hearing this, the knight nodded in agreement.

The knight naturally knew Rei had an item box, so he had no objections on that point.

In fact, leaving the Giganto Turtle's corpse here risked it rotting and, worst case, the stench reaching Gilm.

Even without that, the stench would definitely reach the highway.

Furthermore, monsters might gather seeking the Giganto Turtle's flesh.

Considering all that, the knight was grateful that Rei would handle this monster's corpse.

...Of course, with this much monster material, the knights are interested too. Selling it would undoubtedly cause quite a stir in Gilm.

"Understood. So, for now, we can consider this problem resolved, right?"

"Yeah. But I don't know what will happen to Treant Forest from here on out. I think we'll need to keep a close eye on it tomorrow and beyond."

"I'll send a few people back ahead to report on that."

After finishing his brief exchange with the knight, Rei turned his gaze back to the Giganto Turtle.

There, the reinforcements who had arrived by carriage were also gasping in astonishment at the sight of the Giganto Turtle's colossal form up close.

"Hm?"

Watching this exchange, Rei suddenly wondered.

"What is it?"

Vihera, who had been watching Rei and the knight's exchange nearby, turned her gaze and asked. Rei answered while keeping his eyes fixed on the Giganto Turtle.

"Hey, those monsters that first came out of Treant Forest... they turned into black dust and vanished when the Giganto Turtle appeared, right?"

"...That's what I've heard."

Realizing what Rei was getting at, Vihera also stared intently at the Giganto Turtle's corpse.

The monsters, starting with Treant, and the Giganto Turtle.

Their forms were vastly different, yet there was no doubt both were monsters born from Treant Forest.

And yet, the Giganto Turtle before Rei and the others didn't vanish. It remained there, a corpse.

"What do you think?"

"...I dunno? But anyway, shouldn't we stow it away as soon as possible? In the item box... Misty Ring, right? If it's in there, maybe it won't vanish, and the meat might stay fresh?"

"I'm a bit concerned about issues like bleeding it out, though. Oh well. I'll leave explaining the situation to you then."

Leaving the discussion with the knight to Vihera, Rei walked straight toward the Giganto Turtle with Set.

"Huh? Hey, wait a minute, Rei!"

The knight called out to Rei's retreating back.

Because, if possible, he wanted to talk to Rei.

It wasn't about the knight's personal preferences or anything like that. The main issue was that Vihera and Marina's attire was simply too stimulating for him.

For a knight unaccustomed to women, Marina in her low-cut party dress... and especially Vihera in her dancer's or courtesan's-like sheer attire, were nothing short of poison to his eyes.

Still, the fact it was nearly dawn was probably a saving grace.

If it had been midday and he'd seen Vihera's figure head-on, it could have been a fatal shock in more ways than one.

Ignoring the voices calling out from behind him, Rei approached the Giganto Turtle's corpse.

Naturally, Rei and his companions stood out, and those who had been watching the Giganto Turtle nearby made room for them.

"Sorry about that,"

With a brief apology, Rei touched the Giganto Turtle's corpse... specifically its leg. The next instant, the colossal body vanished.

It was only natural for them to be shocked, as a body the size of a small mountain vanished in an instant.

Leaving the surrounding adventurers gasping in utter astonishment, Rei next mounted Set and headed toward the severed head.

It was quite a distance, so he must have mounted Set.

Several adventurers had gathered around the severed head, curiously observing it... but as Set approached, they naturally made way.

After storing both the Giganto Turtle's body and its severed head in the Misty Ring, Rei headed back toward the knight.

...Of course, fragments of the Giganto Turtle's flesh had scattered around during the battle, but he didn't feel inclined to gather those as well.

"Everyone who participated in this battle, gather over here. I need to discuss the distribution of rewards."

Watching Marina call everyone together to discuss materials, just as she had told Rei earlier, Rei cast another glance over Treant Forest.

The Giganto Turtle, the embodiment of Treant Forest, had been defeated.

But judging by the tree root dolls, perhaps an incarnation is merely a terminal.

Of course, while weak opponents like the root dolls could be created endlessly, it stood to reason that a powerful entity like this Giganto Turtle couldn't be conjured so easily...

(Even so, this Treant Forest still holds many unknown elements in various ways. It's probably best to remain vigilant.)

I should probably discuss this with Guildmaster Walker tomorrow... no, today.

With that thought, I turned back toward the knight.

"Um, well... about that..."

Returning to find, as expected... the knight was stammering and fumbling his words while facing Vihera.

"What the hell are you doing?"

Rei, suddenly feeling foolish for having taken things so seriously just moments ago, sighed and said this with an exasperated tone.

Seeing Rei like this, the knight flashed a bright, relieved smile.

Of course, this knight wasn't misogynistic, nor was he attracted to men.

He was simply inexperienced with women, which probably made dealing with Vihera all the more difficult.

Watching the knight's expression, Vihera smiled and spoke.

"I've explained the basics for now. ...Though I don't know if you remember any of it."

The knight, meeting Vihera's gaze, felt the slight blush that had faded from his cheeks return.

"Ah, I understand. I'll handle the rest. Vihera... why don't you talk with Byune or something?"

Seeing Byune appear beside Vihera, who had returned unnoticed, Rei said this.

"Right. ...Well then, shall we talk a little?"

"Mm!"

Byune gave Vihera her usual brief, single-word reply before the two of them walked off together, moving slightly away.

Partly because they had things about women they didn't want Rei and the others to hear... but also, perhaps, out of consideration that certain topics might be difficult to discuss with them present.

(Well, it's not impossible that Vihera's presence would make it hard to speak calmly either.)

Watching the knight finally seem to settle down now that Vihera had left, Rei thought this, half-jokingly.

...Though, half-joking also meant it was half-serious.

Anyway, seeing the knight finally act normal now that Vihera was gone, Rei asked.

"So, did you hear what I said? ...You're not going to say something stupid like you were too distracted by Vihera's appearance to remember anything, are you?"

"Of course not. ...I remember something."

The knight declared, but given his inexperience with women, Vihera's beauty and physique must have been overwhelming in more ways than one.

If it had been daytime, he might have been able to half-jokingly brush it off.

"I see. Then... what should we do now? Honestly, I never expected to suddenly have to fight Treant Forest's true nature, or its core, or its true form... whatever you want to call it, today. It was completely unexpected."

He hesitated for a moment at his own use of the word "true form," but decided it was best to leave it at that for now.

"Right. First, I think we need to report this to Lord Daskar. And Rei, you should inform the guild, right?"

At the knight's words, Rei nodded while stroking Set's body.

As Rei had said, this incident wasn't supposed to escalate into such a full-scale battle.

But after the surprise attack and the way things unfolded, it ultimately led to the current situation.

"Yeah. It was all pretty unexpected. ...But even though it was unexpected, I think it was the best possible outcome."

"I agree. ...Still, facing that giant turtle monster, it's amazing no one died."

A fair number were injured, and many were receiving treatment from adventurers who had rushed in as reinforcements.

But no one had injuries that could be called fatal. The worst injuries were broken limbs.

"Fortunately... though I hesitate to call it that, the enemy only targeted Set and me while we were flying. Well, we did flaunt our strength to draw them in, and for them, letting me escape was absolutely out of the question."

At Rei's words, the knight's expression shifted slightly, a hint of puzzlement visible.

To that knight, Rei spoke of the tree root doll.

Not about the image language or the other things he understood, but solely about the existence of that incarnation: the tree root doll.

He suggested that burning that incarnation with magic must have ignited their anger and made them feel a sense of urgency.

"Ah... In Treant Forest, it's wood, after all. No wonder they have such a strong aversion to fire."

If this knight knew the purpose of the trees cut down in Treant Forest... or more precisely, if he knew about the expansion of Gilm, he might have wondered if it had strong magical defenses.

Unfortunately, this knight was merely a common knight, not part of the higher ranks of the order.

That said, given the level of magical defense required for Gilm's expansion and the sheer power of Rei's fire-based magic, any magical defense he had might as well have been nonexistent.

"That's why they targeted me from start to finish. Thanks to that, none of the other adventurers died. ...Well, that doesn't mean I can just be happy about it, though."

An adventurer with a broken bone won't be able to move immediately tomorrow just because they've been treated.

Recovery magic would change things, but there was no one here who could use it.

(Well, I suppose I could just go to the clinic back in Gilm and get some recovery magic cast on me.)

The exact amount Rei would pay hadn't been finalized yet, but it would definitely be several gold coins.

Even after paying for the fracture treatment, he still had a decent amount left over.

「Rei」

That's what Rei was thinking when a voice calling his name snapped him back to reality.

He turned his gaze toward the source of the voice and saw Marina standing there.

...The knight who had been talking to Rei just moments ago flushed slightly, though not as intensely as when he'd been near Vihera.

After glancing at the knight's expression, Rei replied to Marina without seeming particularly concerned.

"What is it?"

"We've settled things for now. Everyone's agreed to settle for some meat and four gold coins."

"I see."

The number of gold coins was slightly more than Rei had anticipated, but he wasn't short on money to begin with.

Considering they'd risked their lives fighting the Giganto Turtle, it was within acceptable limits.
