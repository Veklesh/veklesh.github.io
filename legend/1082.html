"Well then, farewell. You may return whenever you wish, my son-in-law."

Optis smiled at Rei standing before the carriage and said this, then bowed his head slightly the next instant.

Then, a shockwave generated by the wind spirit swept past the spot where Optis' head had been just a moment before.

Though called a wind shockwave, it wasn't a fatal blow. It was weak, barely stronger than a light punch.

"...Grandfather, please don't joke around too much, okay?"

"Ho ho ho. Is that so? I was quite serious, you know. Besides, there's the matter of the World Tree's materials... and above all, the World Tree's will."

Optis' gaze fell upon the light orb flickering beside Rei, more subdued than usual.

While its exact intentions weren't clear, everyone present could sense it was likely feeling lonely about Rei and the others leaving.

Especially since yesterday, when they went to collect a few leaves from the World Tree, it had seemed genuinely pleased, albeit a bit shyly. That only made the feeling stronger.

"We've been greatly indebted to you all, including Rei. Honestly, I was worried after our poor first impression..."

Ragdo bowed his head to Rei and the others, looking slightly embarrassed.

Though Ragdo had clashed with Marina over protecting this settlement, the settlement had been saved nonetheless.

If Marina hadn't brought Rei here, the World Tree would undoubtedly have withered.

And the fact they could repel the massive monster horde that attacked the settlement was largely thanks to the efforts of Rei, Elena , and Vihera .

...Though he did have some reservations about the giant cicada growing to such enormous proportions being related to Rei's magic.

"Yeah, we've been through a lot."

As Rei spoke with Ragdo, he glanced around. Jusla, Audban, and others were each exchanging words with Marina.

Audban, who had come all the way to Gilm to call for Marina, and Jusla, who had been rescued after being captured by bandits.

He recognized several other faces from the battle as well.

Rei noticed Elena, Vihera, and Ara (the female) talking with dark elves they had likely become acquainted with.

Though Rei felt a bit restless, he noticed four dark elves approaching him... and they were children.

He didn't need to see their faces to know who they were. Ian, Karuja, Myrth, and the ringleader, Rugdos.

"Big bro! Big bro! You're gone!"

Among the children approaching Rei, Myrth was the first to speak.

His usual lisping voice tugged at Rei's Dragon Robe.

Rei didn't know why they were so attached to him, but seeing someone look up to him didn't feel bad.

"Um... Are you really leaving? Couldn't you stay a little longer...?"

"Yeah, you can stay a little longer. If the elders say anything, I'll stand up for you!"

Karuja and Ian also told Rei the same thing.

"Hmph."

Lugdos snorted, glancing at Rei while trying to appear unconcerned.

As both a rival in love and the savior of his life, Lugdos harbored complex feelings toward Rei, making it impossible for him to be straightforward here.

It wasn't that he was unhappy about Rei leaving his presence, but at the same time, it also meant Marina—the one he'd fallen for at first sight—would be gone too.

These feelings mingled with gratitude, leaving him unsure how to act toward Rei.

Understanding Lugdos's feelings... well, perhaps not exactly, but Rei spoke while stroking Myrth's head.

"Lugdos, if you're looking after these guys, you better protect them if anything happens, just like you did when we fought the goblins."

"Hmph! I don't need you to tell me that!"

Lugdos snapped back, averting his gaze from Rei.

Whether Marina was where his gaze fell by chance... or if Lugdos had done it intentionally...

Regardless, Rei stroked Myrth again and repeated the same words.

"You're the one who has to protect these guys."

"...I know that!"

Lugdos shouted, as if he didn't need Rei to tell him.

Watching Lugdos like this, Rei suddenly felt a gaze and turned his head in that direction.

There stood Audban.

Their relationship was far from good, but Rei didn't really care.

After all, for Audban, it felt like he'd been the one to disrupt a reunion between friends meeting for the first time in nearly a century.

Moreover, Audban understood perfectly well that Marina was drawn to Rei, even unconsciously, and that only fueled his jealousy further.

"......"

Rei gave a small nod in response to Audban, who silently bowed his head... After that, he spent another twenty minutes or so saying farewell to the other Dark Elves, and finally, the time to depart arrived.

Finally, Rei gently reached out a hand toward the light spheres flitting around him.

He knew he couldn't actually touch them, but he still felt the urge to reach out.

These orbs were born from Rei's magic. In a sense, they were akin to Rei's children.

...And yet, the giant cicada had been born from Rei's magic combined with the sap and roots of the World Tree, so in a way, it could also be considered the child of Rei and the World Tree.

(Huh? So I created a giant cicada child between myself and my child... No, stop. That thought is far too depressing to be happy about.)

Shaking off the thought that had popped into his head, he opened his mouth toward the light sphere.

"I'm heading back now, but take care."

The moment he said that, the orb began to flicker violently.

It seemed to understand that if this continued, Rei would vanish.

It fluttered around Rei, blinking repeatedly, as if pleading for him not to leave.

Contrary to the orb's intentions, this spectacle was utterly mesmerizing, and naturally, it captivated the Dark Elves watching as well.

But while it might have been a mesmerizing sight to an outsider, for Rei, right in the center of it, it was unbearable.

"Calm down... Hey, it's blinding, calm down!"

After calling out to the light sphere circling him several times, it finally stopped moving and floated in front of Rei's face.

"I can't stay in this settlement forever. You understand that, right?"

"As for me, I wouldn't mind you staying in this village forever. In fact, as Marina's husband..."

"O-j-i-i-s-a-m-a?"

"Guh!"

Marina's fist slammed into Optis' torso for his unwanted interference.

She didn't normally possess such strength, but remnants of Rei's magic still lingered within Marina.

This heightened her physical abilities overall, allowing Marina's single blow to easily silence Optis.

Watching this grandfather-granddaughter exchange out of the corner of his eye, Rei continued persuading the orb of light.

"This isn't like we're parting forever, right? If I come back here, we'll meet again."

At Rei's words, the light sphere flickered again.

"Really?" Rei nodded back at the orb, as if it had spoken those words.

"Yeah, so stay strong until I come back. Then I'll play with you again."

With that, the light orb finally seemed to give up. It flickered slowly.

Seeing the light pulsing as if agreeing with Rei's words, he must have felt reassured. Rei turned his gaze back toward Optis.

Optis, who had been writhing in agony from Marina's earlier blow, now seemed to have somehow dealt with the pain. As usual, he was calm and composed, his gaze fixed on Rei.

(Wait, is "unflappable" even the right word for Optis? ...Probably not.)

What crossed Rei's mind was that, even during his short stay in this settlement, observing Optis's words and actions made him think it wouldn't be accurate to call him unperturbed.

Around Rei, light orbs flashed and flickered as they flew about.

His mood, sulky after his earlier exchange with Rei, seemed to have changed. Now, he energetically signaled his desire for Rei's attention.

"...Rei, we should go soon."

It was Marina who said this, even though she would normally have wanted to linger a little longer to bid farewell to the villagers.

Perhaps deciding they'd never depart if they lingered, she said it with a touch of forced firmness.

Marina, who usually embodied the very essence of allure, was now, unusually, giving off a faintly melancholic air.

"Yeah, well... see you."

His farewell was directed at the light orb, at Optis, and at the other Dark Elves.

At Rei's words, every single Dark Elf bowed their heads.

A few children looked around, seemingly unsure of what was happening, yet their parents or guardians still made them bow their heads.

"Rei... We cannot express our gratitude enough for saving our settlement this time. Our clan will never forget this kindness. If there is anything we can do to help, please come ask us anytime. There is no door we would close before you, Rei."

Optis's choice of words was completely unlike his usual manner.

It stemmed from his role as the settlement's elder... the de facto leader.

While they usually addressed him as "son-in-law" or "Rei," the shift to "Lord Rei" now was proof of that.

Facing these dark elves, Rei smiled and spoke.

"Don't worry about it. This was a request... No, that's not quite right. I only did it because Marina asked me to. For me, if Marina makes a request, something like this is nothing. Besides... I've already received my reward."

Marina's cheeks flushed faintly at Rei's words.

While Rei referred to the World Tree's branches, leaves, and sap as his reward, what flashed through Marina's mind was the kiss she shared with him.

Seeing his granddaughter like this, Optis seemed to vaguely understand what had happened. He wore a kindly old man's smile and bowed his head once more.

...He didn't say it explicitly, but the bow he just made contained not gratitude for saving the World Tree, but a plea to take good care of his granddaughter for the rest of her life.

Whether Rei's failure to notice this was fortunate or unfortunate remained unclear.

Regardless, with Optis still bowing, Rei and the others climbed into the carriage.

Only Ara headed for the driver's seat.

And so, with the Dark Elves seeing them off, the carriage eventually began to move.

Only the light sphere followed behind the carriage. As it approached the barrier's perimeter, a hole naturally opened within the barrier's protective field.

This was the work of the light sphere, the will of the World Tree.

Since the World Tree had deployed this barrier, creating an opening large enough for the carriage to pass through was, of course, no problem at all.

"I wasn't needed, was I?"

Marina murmured inside the carriage, likely sensing the barrier's opening through the spirit's presence.

Beside Marina on the sofa sat Rei.

Considering the events of the past few days, it wouldn't have been strange if Elena or Vihera had tried to stop Marina from sitting next to Rei. But now... just now, it was different.

Marina hadn't left the settlement because she'd been driven out. She'd simply been genuinely curious about the outside world.

In other words, she could have returned whenever she wanted to... but it was also true that various barriers had been erected while she was out working.

That made returning difficult, and this time marked her first return in roughly a hundred years.

Even for a dark elf who lived a long time... even for Marina, who possessed darker blood than other dark elves and had a connection to the World Tree, a hundred years was a considerable span of time.

That's precisely why, setting out from the settlement once more like this, she inevitably felt something akin to nostalgia.

To fill the emptiness in her heart, Marina leaned her weight onto Rei, who sat beside her.

Today, Marina wore a party dress with a deep neckline and open back.

Leaning her weight like that in such attire meant her massive twin peaks were inevitably crushed against Rei's arm, and the deep valley between them flattened, emphasizing their softness.

"Hey, Rei. You really saved me this time. ...As a thank you, how about I give you an experience unlike anything you've ever had?"

Marina, fully displaying her feminine allure, was blatantly provoking him. Elena and Vihera were on the verge of gasping at the moment.

(Right now, to fill this emptiness, please let us all make noise and forget it.)

Thinking this, Marina continued her seduction of Rei, half-convinced that if he desired it, she would offer herself to him.

A short distance away, Byune , wanting to avoid involvement, slowly savored the dried fruit she'd received as a gift from the Dark Elves... and then the cries of the two lovesick maidens, having reached their limit, echoed through the carriage.
