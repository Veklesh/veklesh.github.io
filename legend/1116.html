Amidst the desert's unique heat and a sun that could be called scorching, blazing down its intense rays upon the earth, Rei swung his Death Scythe and Twilight Spear once again today in the yard of Augusto's house... or rather, the vacant lot it was called.

He was practicing the Two-Lance Style, but even so, mastering both the scythe and the spear simultaneously wouldn't happen overnight.

The swing of the Death Scythe overlapped with the thrust of the Twilight Spear, colliding midair.

"Whoa!"

The shock transmitted through both hands forced him to halt his movement.

He let out a small sigh and looked up, where the sun was asserting itself with relentless intensity.

Confirming the sun's position, I pull out the clock inside the Misty Ring.

"Already past ten? I was pretty focused."

Since I'd started practicing here after breakfast and a short break, that meant around eight in the morning.

If so, that meant he'd been practicing the Two-Spear Style for nearly two hours straight.

Thanks to the Dragon Robe, he barely felt the desert's intense heat. Still, moving his body naturally made him thirsty.

He stowed the clock back into the Misty Ring, then replaced it with the Waterflow Dagger and a cup, quenching his thirst with cold water that could be called heavenly nectar.

"Gururu? Gurururu!"

Set, who had been lying down a short distance away, growled, demanding some for himself too!

Since Rei had started training near the stables, Set was naturally nearby.

Not that he intended to disturb Rei; he was just lying down, relaxing and sleeping as usual.

While doing so, he was also keeping an eye out for any suspicious movements around them.

Given that someone had been watching them the day before, Set probably suspected they might try something.

The desert heat was no bother at all to Set, a griffin.

Even temperatures that would make most Gosh residents involuntarily grimace were no hindrance to Set, a griffin, or to Rei, clad in the Dragon Robe.

"Alright, alright. ...There, this should be easier for you to drink, Set."

He channeled magic into the Water-Flowing Dagger to produce water, and Set drank it happily.

Of course, Set found it difficult to drink from a cup or even a plate filled with water.

It wasn't that he couldn't drink it, but having the water flowing freely like this was simply easier for Set.

After drinking his fill and catching his breath, Rei began preparing to go out.

Normally, he could have headed straight to the guild after breakfast, but he delayed his departure, thinking it was still early and the guild might be crowded.

At Gilm, things usually quieted down significantly after around eight in the morning, but he wasn't sure if that was the same at Gosh.

So, just to be safe, he shifted his schedule...

(Ah, maybe if I'd submitted the Sand Lizardman dismantling request this morning, someone would have taken it on today?)

He thought that, but since he already had the magic stones from two of them—though he hadn't absorbed them yet—he decided there was no particular rush.

"Well, that can be worked out somehow. ...Alright, Set. Shall we go?"

"Guruu!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a cheerful cry, as if asking if the water flowing from the short sword was okay now.

"Hmm, guess there really aren't many people around during the day. It's still morning, so you'd think there'd be a few more people about."

Rei muttered as they walked through the streets of Gosh.

It wasn't that the streets were completely devoid of people.

But compared to early morning or evening, it was undeniably quieter.

"Well, it is hot. I can't say I don't understand not wanting to be outside much."

The sun, where his gaze fell, continued to assert itself with relentless intensity.

A few clouds might have softened the glare somewhat, but unfortunately, the sky held not a single cloud—only a vast expanse of brilliant blue.

If you were in Gilm, you might frown at the heat but still call it a summery blue sky and be happy. But here in Gosh, right in the middle of the desert, basically no one rejoices at a cloudless sun.

Even though there weren't many people around, those running stalls and shops were diligently trying to attract customers, often from the shade.

Some stall owners selling food cooked over open flames were clearly suffering from both the sun's heat and the heat from their cooking. Rei quietly averted her eyes from such stall owners as she made her way down the street.

"Want to grab something to eat?"

The scent drifting toward them was tantalizing, stimulating the appetite.

As Rei muttered while gazing at the source of the scent, likely rich with spices, Set happily purred.

They had eaten a hearty breakfast of Sand Lizardman meat, but nearly three hours had passed since then.

Combined with the vigorous movement during his Two-Spear Style practice, Rei's stomach was now asserting itself, demanding food.

"Well then, first... let's go to that food stall over there."

Rei tried to say that, but a carriage stopped, blocking the way between him and the stall he intended to go to.

Rei thought the carriage was in the way, but since he was standing in the passageway, he understood it couldn't be helped. He tried to step around the carriage to reach the stall. However, a figure emerged from the carriage.

"Excuse me, are you Adventurer Rei?"

The voice belonged to a figure who gave off a peculiar impression—old enough to be called an elder, yet somehow not quite.

Outwardly, he appeared to be in his sixties or seventies, with hair streaked with gray.

That alone might have justified calling him an old man, but the light in his eyes was different.

The strong will and energetic aura radiating from his entire being made him anything but an old man by any measure.

(Huh, they really are out there, aren't they?)

Noticing the considerable strength radiating from the man before him, Rei gave a small smile and nodded in return.

"Yeah, I'm definitely Rei. And you?"

In response to Rei's question, the old man bowed respectfully.

"My apologies. I am Mebastian, serving the Count Salmas family who govern the Gosh."

Close, but no cigar. Hearing the name, Rei couldn't help but mutter inwardly.

But a butler named Sebastian was incredibly common, and having it be just one letter off was unexpected.

(Well, I mean, it's not like you have to be named Sebastian just because you're a butler...)

The man before him hadn't called himself a butler. He'd only said he served.

Still, his impeccable attire and courteous demeanor were enough to convince Rei he was indeed a butler.

"...So, what does this Mebastian fellow want with me? I'm on my way to the guild right now."

"Well, actually, my master, Lord Ryu Brand, wishes to meet with you, Lord Rei."

Rei knew the name Count Salmas from what Augusto had told him.

As Mebastian had mentioned earlier, he was the noble who ruled over Gosh, a man connected to Daldora.

Despite that, he didn't impose oppressive rule on Gosh's residents. ...Not that he governed benevolently either.

He was a rather mediocre figure, and Ray had heard that the people of Gosh sometimes even forgot he existed.

(Well, overall, he probably counts as a decent noble... but there are plenty of terrible nobles out there.)

Considering all the nobles I've actually seen, I can't help but think that. A noble like this would probably be considered decent.

"What do you think? I really want you to meet Lord Ryu Brand..."

As Mebaschan pressed for an answer, Rei pondered his response while shifting his gaze toward the food stalls.

He wanted to eat the meat being sold at that stall, whose aroma alone was stimulating his appetite. That was why.

Following Rei's gaze, Mebasthan quickly considered his options and spoke.

"Well then, if you would come to the lord's manor, we could prepare a sufficient meal for you, Rei... and your griffin as well. Though it is a bit early, we would be honored to invite you for lunch."

"Guru?"

At Mebastian's words, Set croaked and tilted his head.

Though it was likely his first time seeing a griffin, Mebastian showed no sign of being unsettled.

Was he thinking that a butler should never show his unease? Or was Mebasuchan simply thick-skinned?

Rei didn't know the reason, but he still felt favorably toward Mebaschan, who didn't seem afraid of Set.

"Alright, then I'll intrude. Is that okay, Set?"

"Grrr!"

Set purred happily at Rei's words.

If it meant getting a delicious meal, he was all for it!

"Then, Rei, could you please ride in the carriage? Your griffin cannot be placed inside, so together with the carriage..."

Mebastian started to say, but noticed the horse pulling the carriage was frightened.

(Come to think of it, it's horses, not camels. ...Must be because it belongs to the lord.)

He was slightly surprised that the carriage was pulled by horses, not camels, and at the same time understood why Mebastian had hesitated.

The horse at the end of his gaze showed absolutely no sign of moving.

It was as if time itself had frozen; the horse showed absolutely no movement whatsoever, as if it had decided that moving would mean certain death.

The object of its fear was, naturally, the griffin, Set.

(Same as always, I suppose.)

Rei concluded.

He'd seen animals cowering before Set countless times before.

Stroking Set's head as he looked disappointed at being feared, Rei spoke.

"As you can see, we can't exactly leave Set behind, so Set and I will be traveling separately from the carriage."

"No, but..."

For Mebastian, it was unacceptable to have the guest who came to greet him move on his own to the lord's mansion.

But forcing Set alone to leave the carriage and proceed on his own wasn't an option either.

Given the situation, if they were to go to the lord's mansion, Rei had no choice but to travel with Set.

"I'm sorry, but if you can't accompany Set, I'll have to prioritize my own business... Is that still acceptable?"

"...Understood. It can't be helped."

If the discussion dragged on any longer, Rei would likely never actually come to the lord's mansion.

Reaching that conclusion, Mebastian reluctantly agreed to Rei's terms.

"Then I'll proceed ahead in the carriage. Please follow behind."

"Understood. Then, I'll lead the way. ...Set, that's why we're going to the lord's mansion. Follow that carriage."

"Grr."

Set gave a short snort in response to Rei's words.

He seemed a little less spirited than usual, probably because the horse was still frightened.

"Very well, I shall serve as your guide."

Bowing deeply, Mebaschan exchanged a few words with the coachman before climbing into the carriage.

The carriage, which should have carried both Mebastian and Rei, ended up proceeding down the street with only Mebastian aboard.

The coat of arms on the carriage body clearly identified it as belonging to the Count Salmas family. People nearby noticed the carriage approaching and promptly made way.

The sun was out, and the sparse crowd on the road likely contributed to this. They proceeded without any particular trouble.

Along the way, shops selling weapons and armor, tool shops offering potions and such, and food stalls selling bread and skewered meats caught Rei and Set's attention.

(No shops selling magic items, huh?)

Rei murmurs, surveying the surroundings.

There were plenty of shops selling everyday magic items like fire starters, but shops selling the kind of magic items Rei was collecting were nowhere to be found.

(I figured magic items would be actively traded here since they have things like sand ships... Ah, but wait, we outsourced the sand ships to Ozos, didn't we?)

Rei had been looking forward to desert-specific magic items, but he pressed on, disappointed that at least the main street didn't have any magic item shops.

(Well, maybe I just can't find them.)

Magic items are expensive, so it wouldn't be strange for shops dealing in them to operate discreetly.

As he pondered this, he gradually noticed some grass and trees growing along both sides of the road.

It was likely a sign that he was nearing an oasis.

And then... a large mansion came into Rei's view.
