After leaving Galahad's mansion, Rei and the others naturally couldn't just walk straight out of the noble district...

Those who wished to know the circumstances insisted they must stop by the homes of their employers or masters.

Admittedly, they did want to know the details, but their desire to become acquainted with Rei and his companions was stronger.

Ignoring those requests, or rather, ignoring the guards who had come out with Rei and were explaining the situation, Rei and his companions pressed on through the noble district.

"People never used to crowd around us like this before,"

Of course, there had always been those who wanted to become acquainted with Rei.

He was a Rank B adventurer with a nickname, and he even had a griffin named Set as his familiar. That was only natural.

But considering Rei's personality, no one had ever pushed themselves on him that aggressively.

Well, there had been some initially, but it was more accurate to say they had dwindled as word of his temperament spread.

So why now?

Marina smiled at Rei, who was pondering this, and spoke.

"The fact we were forming a party was already fairly well known, right? And if someone sent people into town to gather even a little information about this incident... naturally, they'd find out today at the guild that we formed a party."

"Ah... I see. So it's precisely because they have power, starting with the nobles, that they gather information so thoroughly."

Rei murmured in understanding, but that didn't mean he accepted it readily.

"They probably want any information they can get on us... the Crimson Wings. But be careful about selling our info too easily, or they'll think less of us."

"...Probably. Especially with Byune – don't go following her just because she offers food, okay?"

"Mm!"

Vihera replied with a hint of displeasure at Byune's words.

Warning her not to follow strangers offering food was precisely the kind of caution one would give a child.

Though, in the sense of being a child, Byune was still very much a child.

If it weren't about food, Vihera wouldn't give Byune such warnings.

But when Byune was small... even smaller than she is now, before she even met Vihera, there was a time when she couldn't get proper meals.

That likely explains her petite frame now, but perhaps because of that reason too, Byune has a strong fixation on food.

...She always eats such enormous amounts, making you wonder where it all fits in that tiny body.

That's probably why Vihera warned Byune not to be lured by food...

"But if we feed Byune, won't it be hard to communicate with her?"

Given that Byune basically only utters the word "Hmm," trying to extract any information from her would undoubtedly be a struggle.

If you wanted to smoothly gather Byune's opinions, then Vihera would need to bring him along too.

And if you asked whether Vihera would follow such a companion... well, it shouldn't even be a question.

"Right. ...Look, here they come again."

Rei and the others were walking down the street when Marina's words drew their gaze to a figure ahead who appeared to be a butler.

The butler must have noticed Rei's gaze. He approached them and bowed deeply.

"Excuse me. I believe you are the members of Crimson Wings. My master wishes to speak with you briefly. If you would be so kind, might we have a moment of your time?"

What should we do? Marina looked at Rei, who shook her head.

Confirming this, Marina nodded slightly and spoke.

"I'm afraid we're busy right now. Sorry, but we can't accept your invitation. I don't know what kind of person your master is, but I'm sorry."

Marina refused flatly.

Hearing that, Rei thought maybe he'd try a little harder? But the butler said nothing more, bowed once more, and simply walked away.

"Huh... I thought he might say something else. Like, 'My master is so-and-so,' or something."

"But I doubt many people would try that with Rei at this point. Besides, I'm here too."

Even though she'd stepped down as Guildmaster, Marina still held significant influence within Gilm.

Furthermore, she was personally acquainted with the lord, Daskar, and her connections far surpassed those of ordinary nobles.

Few would want to make Marina an enemy by being rude to someone like her.

...Though, to be honest, the stronger feeling is probably just not wanting to get into a fight with Rei, who shows absolutely no mercy to anyone he's at odds with, even if they are nobility.

Moreover, since Vihera also relishes combat, trouble is more than welcome.

Given that everyone except Byune possessed formidable combat skills, antagonizing them carelessly could literally mean annihilation.

"Well, the real problem is this. How do we find the person Marina encountered? If we don't even know what they look like, it's going to be a bit tough to track them down."

"I suppose so. And since their face was so ordinary, the impression is already fading fast."

"Wait... does that mean Marina might not even recognize them if she saw them again?"

"...I won't deny it. But the fact remains we have no other clues, right?"

"Hmm... What about asking Azimov at the security guard station?"

Rei asked Marina with a glimmer of hope, but she shook her head as if to say, "You know the answer."

"First off, it's doubtful Azimov actually saw the attacker's face. ...And seriously, do you think someone planning to attack someone wouldn't even bother to hide their face?"

Hearing that, Rei had no choice but to shake his head too.

Fundamentally, this whole incident was orchestrated to pit Azoth Trading Company against Rei.

Given that, it was unlikely the perpetrator would leave clues to their identity.

Sure, they might have planned to kill Azimov, but even so, it was unlikely they'd be so careless as to attack without hiding their face.

"So, I guess we just have to search diligently like Marina said."

Thinking of the trouble ahead, Rei looked up at the sky.

There, the sun shone brightly, as if to say Rei's worries weren't such a big deal.

The warm spring sunlight might be perfect for a nap.

But knowing the task ahead would likely be futile, it just felt like a pain.

Ah, I wish I could just take a nap in some shady spot...

Thinking like that was surely because she was thinking about the task ahead.

"Ah, but... maybe it won't be such a bother after all?"

Marina murmurs suddenly.

Her words carried a certain conviction.

"What do you mean?"

Before Rei could speak, Vihera asked.

Diligently gathering information door-to-door was, of course, not Vihera's cup of tea.

That's precisely why Marina's words, suggesting they might not have to go through such trouble, gave her hope.

No, it wasn't just Vihera.

In fact, Byune, who struggled with talking to people, was staring at Marina even more intently.

"Simply put, the person who told me this orchestrated the whole thing to deliberately pit Rei against Azott Trading Company? Naturally, I don't think contact will end after just one meeting."

"So, if we wait, they'll contact us again?"

Rei nodded at Marina's question.

"Probably, but..."

"Then why do we need to search for them? Wouldn't it be better to just relax in the sun somewhere in town and wait?"

"...If they see us taking it easy, they might think twice about showing their faces, don't you think?"

Marina spoke while pondering, and Rei tilted his head, thinking, Oh?

The person who gave Marina the information probably acted that way because they naturally didn't want to antagonize Rei and the others.

If that's the case, wouldn't they make contact once they knew Rei and the others were looking for them?

Marina shook her head at Rei's words.

"If it's exactly as Rei thinks, then yes. But what if it's someone who wants to make sure we clash with the ones responsible for this incident? They've already given us information. I don't think they'd give us any more now..."

"So we have to find them after all. ...What a pain."

"Yeah. Ideally, whoever masterminded this would just come forward already."

If it meant fighting an enemy, Vihera wouldn't complain... but searching for someone, someone so ordinary they didn't even remember his face? It was nothing but a hassle.

Besides, while Vihera herself was acquainted with Azimov, she wasn't as close to him as Rei was.

"Good evening."

Though Vihera looked annoyed, when someone called out to him as he left the noble district, he had enough courtesy to wave back lightly.

The person who spoke to him wouldn't normally do such a thing.

But the fact that the person who had stormed into the Noble Quarter was Rei was already common knowledge. While the reason why Rei had done such a thing hadn't spread yet, no one wanted to antagonize Rei right now.

Fundamentally, the fact that Rei had safely exited the Noble Quarter like this was enough to suggest he bore no blame for this incident.

Of course, the question remains how many could actually succeed in defeating Rei.

Especially now, with not only Rei but also Set, Marina, and Vihera—all individuals of extremely high combat prowess—present.

Even Byune, the weakest fighter in the group, was no one to be underestimated when it came to using her long needle for distraction.

Then there was the dagger, Hakun, forged from the Silver Lion's materials.

Though called a dagger, its blade was longer than that of an ordinary dagger.

A single strike from Hakun could easily slice through an ordinary opponent... no, even one slightly above average.

In other words, while Byune may be the weakest in this group, she is by no means an opponent to be taken lightly.

With such individuals passing by, ordinary adventurers could only offer a brief greeting.

Perhaps understanding this, Rei and the others merely gave a slight nod to the adventurers who greeted them, doing nothing more before leaving the Noble District.

"Well then, first... should we head for the main street?"

"I wonder. If they're the ones making contact, they probably don't want too many others seeing us. ...Well, even if they do see us, it'll just fade from their memory anyway."

"In that case, somewhere less crowded... like the back alleys or that sort of place?"

"Probably."

Marina nodded at Rei's words, and the group headed not for the main street, but into the back alleys.

Byune rode on Set's back, surveying the surroundings with her usual expressionless gaze.

Marina and Vihera walked through the alleyways, observing their surroundings.

...Normally, if someone unaware of the area walked through the back alleys, they'd get hassled by all sorts of people.

Especially if there were individuals like Marina and Vihera, women who could rightly be called peerless beauties. Many would likely try to pick a fight.

But there was no one foolish enough to try such a thing here and now.

As residents of Gilm, they naturally knew of Rei's presence.

Gilm is a gathering place for many adventurers—skilled ones at that. Even those called rogues couldn't just attack anyone indiscriminately.

If they foolishly provoked someone far beyond their capabilities, it would spell their own ruin.

That's precisely why such individuals possess a keener eye for assessing an opponent's strength than the average adventurer. And above all, many of the people gathered here are famous in various ways, making it unnecessary to even test their strength.

"Excuse me, can I have a word?"

"Huh? Yes!? What is it?"

At first glance, he seemed like a rough customer, but since it was Rei who approached him, he couldn't respond like he usually would.

It was against Rei's nature to show even a hint of fear, but he judged gathering information was more important now and opened his mouth.

"Do you know anyone around here with an ordinary, unremarkable face?"

"Huh? No... I'm afraid not..."

The man answered thus...

"Are you looking for me?"

Suddenly, that voice echoed through the alleyway.
