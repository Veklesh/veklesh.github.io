Deciding that there was no point in staying in the slums any longer—and since the knights and soldiers of the Marquis of Seisor’s household had also left—Rei and his companions decided to return to the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

That said, although the location where Celestan’s house had stood had vanished, they decided to leave a few soldiers behind to ensure that the spatial anomaly and the sealed flesh cocoon wouldn’t cause any adverse effects… and also to confirm whether Gaiska had truly perished along with it.

“Grr! Grrrrrrrrrrr!”

On the way to Duke Kerebel’s mansion, Set purrs beside Rei, rubbing his face against Rei’s and acting affectionate.

Since Set possesses a high enough level of intelligence to understand human speech, he likely realizes that the events that occurred inside that house were quite dangerous for Rei as well.

That is precisely why he is acting so affectionate toward Rei now.

Rei knows this too, which is why he gently strokes Set as the dog rubs his face against him while they walk down the street.

“I worried you, didn’t I? But… there’s no way I’d lose to an enemy like that, right?”

“Heh, it seemed like you had a pretty hard time fighting because you couldn’t draw your weapon,”

Elena, who was walking beside Rei, says this with a hint of teasing.

Rei tries to retort to Elena, but… it’s pointless.

After all, it was an undeniable fact.

When moving through the house or the secret passageways, it was only natural that Rei hadn’t drawn long-handled weapons like the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear due to the narrow spaces.

But… once they emerged into that vast space where the Flesh Cocoon existed, if he had immediately drawn the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring, he wouldn’t have had any trouble with his weapons.

That said, it’s highly likely that the Misty Ring had been sealed by the power of the Flesh Cocoon the moment they entered that space.

“That incident certainly served as a valuable lesson. …Though whether I can fully put that lesson to use is another matter entirely.”

He drew his weapons from the Misty Ring right in front of the enemy.

What’s more, the weapons I drew were the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear—items so overwhelmingly powerful that their sheer presence alone was enough to intimidate.

Simply performing such an act serves as a strong deterrent against an adversary, and in some cases, it can even deal a fatal blow to the enemy’s will to fight by making them realize there’s no way they could possibly defeat Rei as he brandishes such weapons.

There’s no doubt that ending the fight before it even begins is extremely advantageous for Rei.

It would have been a terrible waste for Rei not to use such a viable offensive option.

“That’s not really something I can comment on. It’s up to Rei, who’s actually fighting. …Still…”

Elena paused there, exhaling deeply as she stroked Yellow, who was perched on her right shoulder.

“To be honest, I knew this situation would be troublesome, but it’s turned out to be more of a hassle than I expected. I never imagined it would cause such a commotion.”

“That’s true. When Elena and Rei burst out of the secret passage, I wondered what on earth had happened.”

Ara, who was standing beside Elena, nodded in deep understanding.

Even in such a situation, she didn’t doubt Rei and Elena’s command to flee for a moment; she didn’t show any confusion or hesitation, but took immediate action. It’s no wonder Ara’s judgment is worthy of praise.

“It’s a shame we couldn’t get our hands on things like the documents proving the connection between Celestan and the Black Wolf, or the evidence that Gaiska commissioned the job. I did bring some documents along just in case, though…”

Rei took the documents out of the Misty Ring and glanced through them in the moonlight.

“Well, the documents I brought aren’t exactly the important ones. I mean, looking at them like this, they seem to be some kind of ledger, so I guess they’re not completely useless.”

Rei had hardly ever seen any kind of ledger before, but he’d had a few opportunities to look at the ledgers kept by his parents, who were farmers.

The documents Rei had brought weren’t exactly identical to those ledgers… but at a glance, they looked quite similar.

Of course, the impression that it looked like an account book was purely Rei’s opinion; in reality, someone from the Duke of Kerebel’s household would have to examine the document thoroughly to determine exactly what kind of document it was.

“As for mine… Hmm, these are orders for several subordinates—specifically, the executives of the organization Celestan used to lead. These will be useful whether we choose to dismantle Celestan’s organization, turn it into a puppet, or take some other course of action.”

“...How can both Lady Elena and Rei read documents in moonlight like this?”

Ara’s words were tinged with exasperation.

It wasn’t that Ara couldn’t see in the dark at all, but even so, reading documents in moonlight this dim was a difficult feat.

…It’s only difficult, not impossible—probably thanks to training.

Still, since they could review the documents under proper lighting once they returned to Duke Kerebel’s residence, she didn’t feel the need to check them right there and then.

Seeing Ara’s reaction, Rei and Elena decided there was no need to read in a place like this, so they stopped reading the documents and hurried back to the Duke of Kerebel’s residence.

“Hurry, you say. …But haven’t you already sent word that this operation was a success?”

“Yes. But… it ended in an unexpected way. I never imagined the house itself would vanish. Father, upon receiving the report, is likely just as bewildered.”

At Elena’s words, Rei and Ara both looked convinced.

Originally, they had planned to strike a blow against Celestan, obtain documents proving the connection between the Black Wolf and Celestan, and gather evidence that Gaiska had commissioned the Black Wolf to assassinate Rei... but as a result, the house itself had been wiped out.

It would be unreasonable to expect anyone not to be surprised by that.

However, the fact that he couldn’t simply let it end there—simply because he was surprised—was due to his position as Duke of Kerebel.

Rei predicted that they would likely make some kind of move.

“What do you think will happen to the organization Celestan was leading? You didn’t actually intend to kill Celestan today, did you?”

That said, even if that had been the original plan, given the events of this incident, it’s highly likely that Rei or Elena would have killed him even if Gaiska hadn’t.

Regardless of the original plan, given that he was attempting to release what appeared to be a sealed monster, they couldn’t simply let him live.

Above all, if Celestan had been left alive, there was a possibility he could have used the ability of the Flesh Cocoon to keep Rei’s Misty Ring sealed.

(To be honest, I never expected the Misty Ring to be sealed. The very existence of such a method was unexpected. …That said, there’s nothing I can do about this.)

Even if there were a way to deal with the Misty Ring, Rei wouldn’t know how to handle it.

Magic items designed to counter specific actions—for example, items that increase resistance to poison or paralysis—Rei knew about and had even seen before.

But when it comes to resisting a state where space is manipulated to seal off Misty Ring… Rei has never seen or heard of anything like that.

(To begin with, the ability to manipulate space is undoubtedly extremely rare. …It would be impossible to find a magic item designed to counter that. In that case, rather than searching for one myself, wouldn’t it be better to have an alchemist create one?)

The person who came to Rei’s mind was the one who had crafted the Twilight Spear.

Given that person’s unique personality in so many ways, it was certain that asking them to create such a magic item would cause another huge commotion.

“Ah, Lady Elena. It seems a messenger has arrived from over there as well.”

Rei, who had been thinking about magic items, snapped back to reality at Ara’s words.

He turned his gaze in the direction Ara was looking and saw a soldier running toward them.

“Hmm, he seems to be in a hurry…”

“Considering what happened at that house, I suppose that’s only natural.”

Ara replied briefly to Elena’s words.

In fact, since it was still night, the commotion hadn’t escalated that much yet, but there was no doubt it would turn into a major uproar by tomorrow morning.

Still, the fact that Celestan’s house was deep in the slums was at least some consolation.

If this had happened on the main street of Anesis, it would undoubtedly have caused a huge uproar.

“Do you think this incident will cause that much of a commotion?”

“For starters, the underground organizations must be in a frenzy right about now. Word must have already spread that we were planning to storm Celestain’s house. …And in the midst of all that, the house suddenly vanished—literally—leaving not even a trace behind.”

Hearing Ara’s solemn words, both Rei and Elena nodded in agreement.

Just then, a messenger soldier arrived at Elena’s side.

“Excuse me, Lady Elena. I’ve come with a message from Lady Liberte.”

“Hmm. So, what does Father say?”

“First, he instructed me to ask if the initial report was true.”

“...It is true.”

Elena replied, her expression slightly displeased at the messenger’s words.

From Elena’s perspective, she had merely reported what had actually happened, but since the content was so unexpected, he had likely come to confirm it once again.

That said, even Elena would have done the same—checked to see if it was true—if she’d heard that report.

Thinking about it that way, it made sense that her father had instructed the messenger to ask—not as a joke or anything—whether the events had actually taken place.

“I see. Well, he asked me to tell you to return as quickly as possible. That is what Liberte instructed me to convey.”

“I see. ‘As quickly as possible,’ huh? I suppose this whole affair came as a surprise to Father as well.”

As Elena muttered this, seeming to accept it, Rei thought it was only natural but kept his thoughts to himself.

There was no doubt that it was Liberte who had ordered this operation.

Liberte had surely been gathering information on Celestan as well, but he likely hadn’t realized she was planning to break the seal on that flesh cocoon.

(Well, judging by his demeanor, he did seem to be thinking, “Since I can break it anyway, I might as well try…” Or perhaps the Black Wolf’s death has something to do with it…)

In the end, even Rei doesn’t know exactly what kind of relationship Celestine and the Black Wolf had.

Still, having spoken with Celestain, Rei could somehow sense that the bond between the Black Wolf and Celestain was more than just a business-like relationship.

“I understand Father’s request. Well then, Rei. I’m sorry, but I’m going to head back to the mansion ahead of you. Once you get back to the mansion, Rei, feel free to go to your room and rest. However, there’s a possibility Father might contact you…”

“Yeah, I’ll go too if that happens. Though, I doubt there’ll be much for me to explain after Elena’s already covered it.”

“Is that so? What did you say, Rei, when I was fighting that butler… yes, was it Deotres? You were fighting that man, weren’t you? If so, there’s a possibility Father might want to hear an explanation about Deotres from you. Especially since Deotres was a member of the Seisor Marquis family.”

“Ah… I see. That’s a possibility, too. Oh, I get it. In that case, wake me up if I’m asleep. Or… should I play with Set for a bit?”

Even while talking to Elena, Rei was petting Set’s head.

Hearing Rei’s words, Set’s eyes lit up with excitement—Really?!—but Elena shook her head.

“I’m sorry, Set, but I’d prefer if you stayed in your room. …Since we don’t know who might come looking for Rei, it’d be a big help if you stayed in your room instead of the stables.”

“Who do you mean? Isn’t that Miranda? She’s in charge of me, right?”

“Think about the time. It’s already late enough that it wouldn’t be strange if most of the maids were asleep.”

When Rei looked up at the sky after hearing that, he saw that the moon had moved noticeably from where it had been before they stormed into Celestan’s house.
