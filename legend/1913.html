“Hmm. So starting tomorrow, people from the slums will be coming to help with the demolition, too.”

Looking impressed… yet not entirely captivated, Vihera takes a bite of the dish—roast oak meat, tenderly cooked and topped with a tart fruit sauce… a dish that, in Rei’s eyes, could be called “roast oak” rather than “roast pork.”

Incidentally, this “roast orc” was something Marina had made using the magic item furnace that Rei possessed, and it was one of her signature dishes.

There are quite a few orcs living in the vicinity of the dark elf settlement where Marina is from.

That is precisely why the Dark Elves living in that settlement know so many recipes for dishes made with orc meat.

At first glance, this roast orc might seem like a very simple dish, but the marinade applied to the meat before cooking, the choice of herbs, and above all, getting the meat cooked just right are extremely difficult.

In that sense, one could say that the fruit-based sauce is actually the simpler part.

Rei, too, nodded in agreement with Vihera’s words as he ate the roast orc.

“Yeah. At this rate, the butchering isn’t going very fast. No, even with the slum dwellers helping out, I don’t think we’ll finish it this winter.”

Considering the size of the Giganto Turtle, adding just ten or twenty people wouldn’t really speed up the dismantling process that much.

Still, if it would speed up the dismantling even a little… that was the thought behind his actions.

Even if it’s impossible this winter, he wanted to finish the demolition by next winter if possible.

That was Rei’s prediction, though he suspected it would still take a while, one way or another.

“The slums, huh. …But are you sure it’s okay? Those people might be used to dismantling kobolds, but kobolds and Giganto Turtles are different, aren’t they?”

Marina, who was drinking her soup, said this, and her words were an undeniable fact.

The people in the slums dissect kobold corpses almost every day, so they’re used to it.

But that simply meant they were used to butchering kobolds.

And, naturally, kobolds and Giganto Turtles differ greatly in both shape and size.

“I suppose so. But considering how big a Giganto Turtle is, it’s definitely going to be a long haul. Since you know the basics of butchering, you’ll pick it up naturally as you go.”

Dismembering monsters, of course, requires a certain amount of knowledge.

No matter how many kobold corpses are brought into the slums, it makes no sense to ruin them by using them as practice.

Especially when it comes to furs—any damage means they’ll be bought for a fraction of their value. …Though, from the perspective of the adventurers who defeat those kobolds, they have no intention of stripping the kobolds for their materials, so they probably don’t give it a second thought when they’re fighting.

In any case, how can one properly butcher a kobold’s carcass without damaging it as much as possible?

Naturally, the best approach is to ask someone with experience.

And there are quite a few former adventurers in the slums.

You pay them some sort of fee and have them teach you how to butcher.

...Of course, there are differences in skill—some are better at butchering than others, and some are better teachers than others—so if you end up with a bad adventurer, it’s a waste of time.

In any case, it’s safe to assume that most of the slum dwellers currently butchering kobolds possess at least some degree of butchering skill.

In that case, Rei’s comment that one would naturally learn the process while butchering a Giganto Turtle wasn’t entirely off the mark.

“If Rei says so, then fine… but what did Elena think? You went to see the Giganto Turtle dissection site today, right?”

“Hm? Well, from what I saw, I’d say it’s the kind of thing where just adding a few more people wouldn’t make much of a difference. …That said, there’s no doubt the dismantling speed will increase at least a little, so I don’t think it’s a problem.”

When Elena said that, Marina, accepting her word for it, stopped trying to question Rei any further.

The next morning, in front of the main gate. …Uncharacteristically for Rei, he was taken aback by the scene before him.

That was because, while the number didn’t quite reach a hundred, there were definitely more than fifty people gathered there.

“This… is honestly unexpected.”

“Heh, well? You didn’t think I could gather this many people, did you?”

Sue-chi said proudly to Rei, who had voiced his surprise.

Rei nodded at her words, but immediately spoke up again.

“I’m definitely surprised. No doubt about it… but do you clearly remember the conditions I laid out yesterday? I’ll admit it’s impressive that you gathered this many people, but if even one of the people you brought here pulls any funny business, it’ll cause trouble not just for everyone here… but for the residents of the other slums as well.”

Technically, the Guild had told him they couldn’t handle that matter, but they had given him permission to use it as a bluff or a threat.

That’s exactly why Rei said that, but…

“It’s fine. I trust every single one of these people.”

Suu-chi replied without showing the slightest sign of fear at Rei’s words.

Rei could tell she wasn’t just putting on a brave face—she truly meant what she said.

(Could it be that Suu-chi has quite a reputation or a lot of influence in the slums? ...Though, there are quite a few children here, too.)

At first, Rei was surprised by the sheer number of people, but when he took a closer look at those standing beside Suu-chi, he saw that they were clearly younger than her… many of them were children around ten years old.

Of course, in this world, it’s not all that unusual for children that age to work as household help.

But… this job isn’t something just any kid off the street could handle; it requires a fair amount of specialized knowledge.

“It’s a big help that you gathered this many people, but there seem to be a lot of kids here. Can they really handle the dismantling and such?”

In response to Rei’s questioning tone, Suu-chi stroked the head of a child standing near her with an air of “Of course.”

...That said, the children seemed a little intimidated by the large number of adventurers standing before them.

Unlike the children, the adults from the slums didn’t seem frightened, but, unusually, none of them were making a move to pick a fight with the adventurers.

It’s not that every adult living in the slums harbors resentment or jealousy toward people outside the slums… but in that sense, you could say the adults from the slums standing before them are rational.

“Don’t worry about that. These kids have been butchering kobolds almost every day, so they’re used to the process.”

“Is that so? …Well, kobolds aren’t exactly huge monsters, so I suppose even kids could handle butchering them once they get used to it.”

It’s common sense that it’s dangerous for children to handle knives.

But children around ten years old sometimes help out with cooking as part of their family duties.

Since Rei had experience cooking during home economics class in elementary school, she tried to convince herself that it wasn’t all that strange… yes, she tried to convince herself of that.

“Yes, it’s fine. Actually, these kids are quite skilled at butchering. …They did get a few scrapes and cuts at first, though.”

“...I see. If you’re taking responsibility for this group, I won’t say anything more. ...Can I leave the rest to you?”

“Yes.”

The guild official, meeting Rei’s gaze, smiles and nods.

The guild official was secretly surprised—perhaps he hadn’t expected so many slum dwellers to be gathered—but he didn’t let it show on his face.

“Then, let me explain the arrangements regarding the slum dwellers.”

As he said this and began speaking with Suu-chi, the guards were already processing the paperwork to let them out of Gilm.

Fortunately, just as Rei had told Suu-chi beforehand—no, actually, the terms were even better than that—the discussion regarding compensation was settled without any issues. Furthermore, thanks to the Guild’s mediation, the slum dwellers would be allowed to enter and exit the city free of charge while the Giganto Turtle was being dismantled.

Normally, adventurers can enter the city for free by showing their Guild Card, but slum dwellers naturally do not possess such cards.

So, while this was granted as a special exception for the duration of the job, it wasn’t a difficult matter to arrange, as non-adventurers were also being recruited as labor for the dismantling.

(Still, the number of people has really grown.)

Rei had this thought not only upon seeing the slum dwellers, but also the adventurers and other volunteers.

The pay itself was low, but the fact that they were given Giganto Turtle meat as a souvenir was likely enough to attract a large workforce.

In this season, when there isn’t much in the way of entertainment, meals naturally become a major source of enjoyment.

In such circumstances, being able to eat even a small amount of meat so delicious that even royalty and nobility rarely get to taste it was a rare treat.

On top of receiving such meat, they also get a small reward.

...It was only natural that anyone with butchering skills—even if they weren’t currently an adventurer—would want to participate.

While Rei was thinking this, the discussion between the guild staff and Su-chi regarding the terms had concluded, and after the guards completed their procedures, they stepped outside.

Since it was still dim outside, there weren’t as many onlookers as there would be during the day. …Just because there weren’t many doesn’t mean there weren’t any at all—the fact that there were a few suggests there are plenty of people seeking entertainment.

As they stepped outside Gilm… though it was dark, Set sensed several monsters approaching, and a few seconds later, Rei sensed them as well.

“Monsters! They’re coming this way! Get ready to fight!”

At Rei’s shout, the adventurers hired as bodyguards within the group, as well as those who had accepted the butchering job but were adventurers by trade, reacted immediately.

However, in stark contrast to them, many of those who had joined this job solely because they possessed dismantling skills, as well as the slum dwellers who had joined just today, had never experienced combat before.

Those people were shaken, and some even tried to rush back into Gilm…

“Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

Set’s roar echoed throughout the surrounding area.

It was the king’s aura of intimidation.

That said, since Set had used it with reduced power this time, no one fainted upon hearing it.

Those with a certain level of strength were only slightly affected. ...It was unexpected even to Rei and Set that a portion of the monsters heading toward them had stopped in their tracks.

(Just as I thought, it was the goblins that stopped moving.)

Although it was still dim, Rei, with his keen night vision, could tell that the goblins had stopped moving even from a distance.

The others—such as the orcs and wolf-like monsters—slowed down slightly, but showed no signs of stopping.

(Even though Set is here? ...Could it be the result of yesterday’s dissection?)

Given the possibility that the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood hadn’t completely faded even after a night had passed, that assumption wasn’t by any means far-fetched.

Thinking this, Rei drew the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring.

The monsters closing in weren’t particularly powerful.

Still, since there were many people behind him who had never experienced combat, it was necessary to quickly defeat the approaching monsters.

“Those who aren’t confident in combat, please stay grouped together in the back and don’t get in the way! Just leave the fighting to the experts! As long as we have Rei, known by the nickname ‘Crimson,’ fighting monsters of that caliber is absolutely no problem!”

Listening to the guild staff member shouting from behind, Rei steps forward.

With a swing of her Death Scythe, she cleaves the orc’s torso in two.

Meanwhile, Set circled around behind the enemies to ensure none escaped, finishing off the goblins whose movements had been halted by the King’s aura of intimidation.

For Set, low-rank monsters like goblins aren’t meant to be fought—they’re meant to be trampled.

……If there had been any rare or high-rank monsters among them, they might have put up some resistance… but there were none among the goblins here, and they were effortlessly crushed by Set, with every remaining monster being defeated.
