"This is... quite spacious, isn't it? Why on earth is a place like this underground?"

What Rei saw as he descended the stairs was what could only be described as a vast basement.

Compared to the darkness of the staircase, the stonework of the basement glowed faintly; though it was somewhat dimmer than outside, it was still bright enough to see around.

In terms of size, this space was undoubtedly larger than the mansion built above it.

In fact, it was likely even larger than the grounds of the mansion above.

Naturally, if that were the case, this basement would have to extend onto the grounds of other estates—an act that would normally be forbidden.

(Do the people on the neighboring property know about this? No, if they did, there would naturally be an uproar… so does that mean they’re unaware of it?)

Gazing at the vast space, Rei was filled with doubt.

Beside Rei, three guards shivered as they scanned the surroundings.

This basement is noticeably colder than the rest of the mansion.

In fact, it felt just like being outside… no, depending on the situation, it wouldn’t have been strange to feel even colder than being outside.

However, since Rei was the only one wearing a Dragon Robe equipped with a basic air conditioning function, he didn’t seem particularly bothered by the cold.

“Hey, what do you think about this? …I think that guy from earlier must have fled here too.”

“Probably. But look.”

The guard nodded at Rei’s words and pointed to the wall of the basement visible in the distance.

There, just like the one Rei and the others had come up, was a staircase leading upward.

And there wasn’t just one visible staircase; there were several others as well.

“Where do you think those stairs lead? Given the location, I don’t think they lead to the mansion we entered.”

At Rei’s words, the three guards nodded in agreement.

(Come to think of it, the light illuminating this basement... is it the stone? It feels like the stone itself is glowing, but it does have a dungeon-like vibe to it.)

Rei has explored several dungeons before, and it’s not uncommon for the dungeon itself to glow, making artificial lighting unnecessary.

But of course, that’s specific to dungeons; Rei had no idea that such structures existed.

Even if you brought back glowing stone, rocks, or other materials from a dungeon, the light would eventually fade.

Despite that, the basement currently spread out before Rei seemed as though its very building materials were emitting light.

“...Well, at any rate, it’s clear that this basement is an abnormal place in many ways. But why do you think they prepared a place like this?”

“If you think about it logically, don’t you think it’s related to the recent Kobold incident?”

One of the guards answered Rei’s question.

Those words carried the utmost persuasiveness.

To begin with, the reason Rei and the others had come here was solely to find a clue leading to the mastermind behind the Kobold incident.

Given that such a place contained such a mysterious basement, it would be unreasonable not to look for a connection.

“So, something was going on here, then. …Rei, do you have any idea what it might be?”

Prompted by the guard, he focused his senses—especially his sense of smell—to see if he could find any clues, but there was no lingering scent of blood.

Of course, while Rei’s senses are sharper than usual, that’s only relative to an ordinary human.

Naturally, the sharpness of those senses doesn’t come close to Set’s.

“...There’s nothing. At least, there’s no smell of blood, so it shouldn’t be the case that someone died here or anything like that.”

At Rei’s words, one of the guards spoke up while surveying the surroundings.

“So something was going on here that summoned the Kobolds, huh? Logically speaking, it seems like some kind of ritual or something like that…”

“But even if it’s a ritual, it doesn’t necessarily have to involve bloodshed, does it?”

Rei could naturally hear this conversation, but as someone who supported the theory—though he knew it was partly wishful thinking—that a magic item was being used to summon the Kobolds, he couldn’t bring himself to say anything in response.

“Is that so? Since it’s a ritual to summon Kobolds, I don’t think it would be strange to use a sacrifice.”

“A sacrifice?”

At the word “sacrifice,” Rei recalled the scene he’d just witnessed… the many corpses reduced to nothing but skin and bones.

Rei didn’t know why there were so many corpses.

But if, as the guard said, they were performing some kind of ritual involving sacrifices here, he couldn’t say he didn’t understand the reasoning behind it.

(Besides, those corpses were nothing but skin and bones, but there were no other wounds on them. If they used those skeletal corpses—presumably the Red Cloth gang—for some kind of ritual here, then maybe that explains why there’s no smell of blood here, right?)

Rei was trying to gather his thoughts while surveying the surroundings when one of the guards called out to him.

“Rei, you reacted to the word ‘sacrifice.’ Is there something you’re thinking about?”

“Ah… right. I guess I didn’t mention it.”

Saying that, Rei began to describe the scene he had witnessed—the pile of corpses reduced to nothing but skin and bones.

Hearing his explanation, the guards grimaced at the gruesome details, which were far worse than they had anticipated.

“It seems this mansion really is quite a troublesome place. I wonder what on earth they were thinking, trying to pull a stunt like that here.”

“No… if you think about it normally, it’s not such a bad choice. Doing something like that here—normally, there’s no way anyone would find out so easily. …Well, even if it was just small talk or a passing mention, I think the fact that I told Akabu about this place was a big factor.”

“I suppose so. To begin with, most people wouldn’t even think there’s a place like this down here.”

Just as he muttered that, Rei suddenly realized something.

“Hey, do you think this basement was prepared specifically for this? Or do you think it’s been here all along?”

“……”

At Rei’s words, the three guards fell silent in unison.

It was because, having seen this underground space, they didn’t know what to say.

Normally, one would assume this place was built for the Kobold incident… but creating such a vast underground space would require a tremendous amount of effort.

There was no doubt that many people had gathered for Gilm’s expansion project, but even so, attempting to create an underground space of this scale would have been bound to attract attention.

Moreover, this place isn’t that far from the noble district.

Since the expansion work was being done at a location far from here, if a large number of workers had gathered here to dig a hole, it would inevitably have drawn attention.

(If that’s the case, the only explanation is… that this underground space wasn’t created recently, but much earlier. And not just a few months ago, but possibly even years ago…)

Rei made this assumption as he surveyed his surroundings, but if that were the case, it would raise a whole new set of questions.

The biggest question, of course, is that this underground facility extends beyond the grounds of the mansion Rei and the others entered, reaching into the grounds of other mansions as well.

From the perspective of the residents of the adjacent mansions, there’s no way they would tolerate this space being extended all the way into the basements of their own homes.

“Could it be that the owners of the mansions in this area are actually the same person…?”

“No way.”

The guard immediately dismissed Rei’s suggestion.

“How can you be so sure?”

“I’m a guard, after all. While this area falls under a different jurisdiction, it’s still the Noble District—one of the most important places in Gilm. Naturally, I’ve known that information from the start.”

“...I see.”

Hearing that, Rei had no choice but to agree.

At the same time, he was once again struck by just how impressive the guard was.

At the very least, Rei wouldn’t have bothered to memorize that area on his own.

“So, what does that mean? Could it be that the people in the surrounding mansions haven’t actually noticed this underground space?”

“Well… I suppose I can’t say it’s impossible.”

Even as he answered Rei, the guard couldn’t quite bring himself to fully accept that explanation.

It’s conceivable that this underground space lies at a considerable depth, and even if the surrounding mansions have basements, they likely wouldn’t extend this deep.

But still, given that such a vast underground space exists, it wouldn’t be strange if the residents of the mansions noticed any activity here.

As he thought this, Rei suddenly realized something.

“Hey, the residents… or rather, the owners of the mansions in this area are all different, right?”

“Yeah. That’s what I said earlier.”

“Then, listen. Isn’t it possible that there’s someone even higher up than those residents or owners?”

Rei said this, but the guard shook his head.

“The owners of the mansions surrounding this one are mostly connected to various factions—some are members of the Noble Faction, some are members of the Royal Faction, and some are members of the Neutral Faction. Given that…”

“I see. That doesn’t make sense.”

“Yeah. …Of course, it’s not like there’s no way to make that work…”

As for the possibility, the residents of the other mansions likely wouldn’t know, he thought.

Hearing this, Rei looked around once more.

Creating a space this large without anyone noticing… and considering they’ve likely maintained it for a long time, it feels quite off. It does… but given the current situation, it’s also true that we have no choice but to reach that conclusion.

“In that case, for now… what should we do? There’s no doubt this underground space holds some significance, but the guy who attacked us earlier is nowhere to be found, and there’s nothing else here to investigate.”

“...Now that you mention it, you’re right.”

At Rei’s words, the guard tried to say something… but the next moment, Rei suddenly covered his mouth with his palm.

The guard whose mouth was covered, along with his three comrades, tried to shoot a “What on earth?” look at Rei, but ignoring them, Rei dragged the guard over to the wall.

Seeing Rei like this, the guards must have assumed there was a reason Rei had to do this. The one with his mouth covered didn’t show any particular dissatisfaction and quietly moved to the wall.

They moved as quietly as possible… and then, a few dozen seconds after reaching the wall, something unexpected was heard.

I knew immediately what it was.

“A hum?”

One of the guards murmured softly, in a voice only those nearby could hear.

Yes, it was definitely a hum.

In the mansion where Rei and the others had been just moments ago, a search was underway by the guards and Rei, and furthermore, Set and the guards had surrounded the building to ensure no one could escape.

Despite that situation, a hum? Wondering about that, Rei was lightly tapped on the arm by the person whose mouth he was covering, so he decided it would be fine to let go and released his grip.

Another major reason he let go was that he believed it was essential to capture the person who appeared to be humming.

In any case, as Rei and the three guards held their breath and remained silent... eventually, the source of the humming appeared.

The person emerged from a location separate from where Rei and the others had come down.

In terms of distance, it was likely the mansion next to the one Rei and the others had gone to investigate.

When they saw that person, Rei—and the guards—felt as though the clue had come to them.

But when they saw that the person humming was holding a rope, and that at the end of that rope were several men linked together in a chain, they were left wondering what to do.

What’s more, the men linked together didn’t say a word; they simply let themselves be pulled along by the person holding the rope, without voicing any complaints.

On the contrary, they walked along, being pulled by the rope as if they had no will of their own.

「Rei」

Rei, who had been watching the man holding the rope and the men being pulled along by it, was suddenly tugged by the guard’s Dragon Robe and called out to in a low voice.

“What’s wrong?” Rei asks the guard.

The guard’s expression hardens as he looks at Rei… and speaks.

“I recognize some of the men tied to that rope.”

Hearing that, Rei understood what he meant.

In other words, that meant...

“The Red Cloth, huh?”

At Rei’s muttered words, the guard silently nodded in response.
