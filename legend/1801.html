As I walk through the streets of Anesis, the shop I’m looking for eventually comes into view.

It’s the shop Miranda mentioned, the one that sells baked goods packed with honey, nuts, and dried fruit.

To Rei, who had eaten similar bread before in the labyrinth city of Exil, it even seemed like it might be sweet bread rather than baked goods.

But he felt it would be wrong to say anything before actually trying it, so he kept those thoughts to himself.

……Meanwhile, Leodanis, who had initially suggested they visit the weapon shop, followed along quietly, though he seemed slightly dissatisfied with Rei’s actions.

Since Leodanis isn’t particularly fond of sweets, he probably wasn’t very interested in baked goods.

Even so, the main reason he came along was to prevent the town from falling into chaos due to Rei’s—or, more accurately, Set’s—presence, and to keep a close eye on Rei to make sure he didn’t do anything strange.

What the group saw was a line of about twenty people queuing in front of a single shop.

Fortunately, the snow wasn’t falling too heavily today; it was just fluttering lightly.

But even so, it was still cold enough.

Rei didn’t have to worry about that since he was wearing his Dragon Robe, but Miranda was bundled up over her maid’s uniform, and Leodanis was also wearing a winter coat.

Given that, it was only natural that Rei would be surprised to see such a long line.

“Oh, the line isn’t very long today. I wonder if it’s because it’s snowing.”

Next to a surprised Rei, Miranda murmured happily.

“Um…” Leodanis tried to say something in slight protest at Miranda’s remark, but he wisely decided it would be unwise to speak up in this situation and kept his mouth shut.

“Is the line usually this long?”

“Yes. This shop opened at the end of autumn, but it’s been doing great ever since. People love sweets, so I suppose that’s only natural.”

“...I get that, but isn’t it also natural for sweets to be expensive? The fact that you can get such large quantities of honey around here... that’s a bit strange.”

If this were Gilm, it wouldn’t be strange at all to be able to obtain large quantities of honey, given that there are all sorts of monsters in the area, including some based on bees.

But this is Anesis.

It’s not exactly a remote frontier, so naturally, you can’t just harvest massive amounts of honey nearby.

(The only plausible explanation is that, given its location as the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana, they’ve contracted with a specialized supplier to bring in the honey. …Does beekeeping even exist in this world?)

If one were a tamer or a summoner, they might be able to keep bee monsters as familiars and practice beekeeping.

But unfortunately, Rei didn’t know whether beekeeping as he knew it was practiced in this world.

Then again, none of Rei’s acquaintances were beekeepers, so his knowledge was mainly derived from TV and manga.

(Hornets try to prey on honeybees, but the honeybees swarm the hornets and suffocate them... or was it something like that?)

For some reason, that was the image that came to mind when he heard the word “beekeeping.”

If I had direct, practical knowledge of beekeeping, I probably would have been able to think more specifically about what the hives look like and how to harvest honey without destroying the nest.

In any case, Rei and the others joined the line as well.

Considering their status as guests of Duke Kerebel, they could probably buy the pastries without waiting in line... but if they pulled a stunt like that in a place like this, it was certain to lead to serious trouble eventually.

Especially considering women’s obsession with sweets, they absolutely couldn’t get away with such behavior.

So, they obediently lined up at the back of the queue… Naturally, no one in the line had ever seen a griffin up close, and, as expected, some began to fear its presence.

Still, what came as a surprise to Rei was that the moment Leodanis said Set was safe, the people who had been making a commotion immediately calmed down.

Of course, this wasn’t because Leodanis had that much influence; it was precisely because he had declared himself a member of the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights.

There’s no doubt that Miranda’s presence also played a role; she was smiling and petting Set, helping to calm those around them.

Perhaps because Set understood that he was feared in unfamiliar places, he was careful not to frighten those around him, being mindful not to make sudden movements or let out loud cries.

Thanks to the efforts of the two humans and the dog, neither the people lined up in front of Rei and her group nor those behind them showed any signs of being extremely afraid of Set.

On the contrary, a few of the more curious ones even reached out to pet Set, albeit tentatively.

Once one person petted him, the others stopped acting scared.

When the first person to pet Set flashed a happy smile, the others who had been watching began petting him as well.

While there are various exceptions, it is a woman’s nature to love sweets, but it is also a woman’s nature to love cute things.

As a result, Set was instantly accepted by the women lined up in front of the shop.

Leodanis looked on in stunned amazement at this scene, while Miranda nodded with a smile, as if to say, “Of course.”

Rei, too, felt no particular sense of incongruity at the sight.

He had seen this happen countless times before: Set, who was initially feared, gradually becoming accepted.

Considering how adorable Set is, his cuteness probably outweighs his fearfulness.

(Even though he’s now over three meters tall—much larger than before—if he bows his head in that situation and lets out a cry with those round, upward-looking eyes… normally, anyone would be charmed by that cuteness. Should I say I’m glad other griffins don’t make gestures like this? …They don’t, do they?)

Suddenly, Rei wondered what he would do if ordinary griffins were to act like Set, but then he reconsidered, thinking that a griffin known as the “Reaper of the Skies” surely wouldn’t do such a thing.

As they stood there, the line gradually moved forward... and eventually, it was Rei and the others’ turn.

“Welcome. The shop’s full right now, so takeout is the only option—is that okay with you?”

To Rei’s surprise, the one who spoke was a man in his thirties.

Moreover, he was a massive, muscular man who looked nothing like the typical pastry shop employee.

He was easily over two meters tall, and his body was covered in dense muscle.

It was the kind of musculature that could truly be described as rock-solid.

“Uh…” was Rei’s honest reaction upon seeing the man.

He was the complete opposite of the image one typically associates with a pastry chef.

But in reality, the customers in the shop didn’t seem the least bit afraid of him… on the contrary, they were chatting with him in a friendly manner.

“So? Takeout is fine, right? Three portions… Huh?”

The man was about to ask if three portions were really enough, but then he noticed Set standing next to Rei.

He’d been so busy handling orders from customers that he probably hadn’t had the time to notice Set’s presence until now.

Even so, the look of surprise on his face lasted only a moment; the next instant, he shook it off and spoke again.

“Would four portions be okay? I’m sorry, but since we’re busy, we’re limiting it to one per person.”

“Oh, yes. That’ll be fine.”

The man nodded at Miranda’s words and immediately began wrapping the bread.

After paying, Rei and the others left the shop.

With Rei and the others gone, Set—who had naturally been tagging along with them—was gone too, and the women who had been standing in line behind them, petting Set, looked slightly disappointed.

That said, the moment they realized they could now buy the pastries they’d been eyeing, their attention quickly shifted to that.

They walked a short distance away from the shop… and as they walked, Rei and the others opened the wrapper.

What appeared was a baked good that seemed to be somewhere between a cookie and a bread roll.

Just as Miranda had said, the pastry was generously drizzled with honey, filling the air with a sweet aroma.

“Growl! Grrrrr!”

The sweet scent must have been quite stimulating for Set as well.

“Let’s eat it! Let’s eat it!” he cried out.

Seeing Set like that, Rei handed him one of the packages.

At first, he poked at it gently with his beak, and once he decided the sweet pastry was delicious, he kept eating it over and over.

Holding Set’s portion in his right hand so Set could eat, Rei brought the portion he was holding in his left hand to his own mouth.

First, the crisp texture and the sweetness of honey spread through his mouth, and soon the nuts and dried fruits kneaded into the dough delighted his palate.

(Is this melon pan? No, it’s quite different… Unlike melon pan, where the biscuit dough and bread dough are separate, this is definitely a baked good baked all together. Well, melon pan is probably baked together too, but in a different sense.)

The bread with a biscuit layer on top is what is generally referred to as melon pan.

That is strictly the basic version of melon pan, but as far as Rei knows, there are variations ranging from melon pan with chocolate chips to luxurious versions made with actual melon juice.

...Incidentally, the reason it’s called “melon pan” isn’t because melon juice is actually used in the bread.

There is a theory that it’s because the lines drawn on the biscuit dough resemble the appearance of a melon.

Apparently, there are several other theories as to how it got the name “melon pan,” but at least Rei believed that theory to be the correct one.

In any case, while that was the origin of the name “melon pan,” there was no doubt that what Rei was currently eating was a food that was similar to, yet distinct from, a traditional melon pan.

It was a food that was hard to explain, but that was only because Rei knew what "melon pan" was.

Miranda, who didn’t know that, was happily savoring the sweetness, and even Leodanis, who had seemed reluctant at first, now wore a satisfied expression as he ate the pastry.

As for Set, perhaps because of his large size, the wrapper Rei had been holding was already empty.

“Gurrr…”

Seeing Set looking disappointed after finishing his portion, Rei gave him the baked good that resembled a melon pan, which he had eaten about halfway through.

Rei isn’t averse to sweets himself, but more than that, he simply didn’t want to see Set looking so sad.

(If possible, I’d like to buy up everything they have… but I can’t exactly do that in such a busy shop.)

Whether he was the owner or a clerk, the man who served Rei seemed like the type who would refuse even if she asked to buy the entire stock.

If this were a money-grubbing person, he might have been willing to sell the entire stock.

But there was no way that man seemed like the type to do such a thing.

Besides, there was still quite a long line behind Rei and the others.

If Rei and his group bought up all the remaining baked goods, naturally there would be none left for the people behind them.

What would happen if they were to buy up all the sweets right in front of the women who had been waiting in line, even in the snow, just to enjoy a sweet treat?

That was something even Rei didn’t want to think about.

(Yeah, I guess I’ll just go with the idea that I bought enough for everyone. Ideally, I would have liked to get two or three extra for Set. Besides, if I really want a large quantity of these, I could always order them separately later instead of buying them here.)

She also felt it wasn’t worth going out of her way to do something that would invite hostility from others.

“Yeah, it was delicious. It was delicious, but… standing in that line takes a bit of courage.”

Leodanis murmured thoughtfully after finishing his pastry.

Rei understood exactly how he felt.

Most of the people in the line earlier were women; men were an overwhelming minority.

Even the few men there—whether they were with girlfriends, friends, or family, it was unclear—weren’t standing in line alone; they were standing with women.

Given the nature of the place, no matter how much Leodanis wanted to have another one of those pastries, it would take a lot of courage for him to stand in line by himself.

Asking a woman from the Knights’ Order, or perhaps a personal female friend with no connection to the Order, to stand in line with him.

That was—or at least it should have been—the best option Leodanis could think of at the moment.
