After finishing his conversation with Vihera and Byune, Rei decided to head out right away.

When Rei told Lana that he was heading out again right after returning to the inn, she simply smiled and said, “You’re so busy.”

That said, that smile was the usual one… though compared to the smile Rei knew Lana usually gave, this one seemed a bit more cheerful than usual.

It was a smile that suggested she understood his feelings—that even though she must have some worries regarding the kobold situation, as long as she lived in Gilm, she wasn’t about to let such an opponent get the better of her.

Strangely, Lana’s smile gave him a boost of energy… He led Set out of the stables and, together with Vihera and Byune, headed for Marina’s house, which had become something of a hangout for Rei and his friends.

“Oh? …That’s unusual.”

Vihera said this with a look of surprise, likely because a carriage was parked in front of Marina’s house in the noble district.

And it wasn’t just any carriage—it was a high-quality one, the sort used by nobles and wealthy merchants.

In particular, seeing that the horse harnessed to the carriage, though it did flinch at the sight of Set, did not panic or even attempt to bolt, made it clear just how rigorously trained the animal was.

The person sitting in the driver’s seat must have noticed something unusual about the horse, for he looked around frantically and spotted Rei and the others.

That said, even after noticing Rei and the others, he didn’t react in any particular way; he simply stepped down from the box seat and began petting the frightened horse to calm it down.

“There, there. Calm down, calm down, calm down.”

“……Brrrr”

“Brrr”

Perhaps he’s good at petting them, because the two horses quickly seem to calm down.

(That’s some amazing skill. …I’d even like him to teach me how to pet Set.)

Watching him calm the horses in the blink of an eye, Rei thought to himself.

Of course, Set isn’t dissatisfied with Rei’s petting.

Still, even from Rei’s perspective, the coachman’s stroking was truly impressive.

“Rei? What’s wrong?”

Vihera asked Rei, who had been watching the coachman stroke the horse, with a puzzled look.

Startled back to reality by her voice, Rei shook his head to indicate it was nothing, then looked back at the carriage.

“Do you know where that carriage is from?”

“If Rei, who’ve lived in Gilm longer than I have, don’t know, why would you think I would? Well… judging by the looks of it, it seems like a pretty nice carriage.”

As Rei and Vihera exchanged words, they saw the door to Marina’s house open.

Emerging from the house were a woman in her fifties and Marina, the mistress of the house.

Marina, naturally, immediately noticed Rei and the others.

As usual, despite wearing a party dress that didn’t look like it would be very easy to move in, there was nothing unsteady about her movements.

That said, if we’re talking about attire unsuitable for the occasion or season, Vihera—wearing a sheer, see-through gown the sort of thing a prostitute or dancer might wear—is in much the same boat.

“Oh, look, Marina is beckoning us. …It feels a bit awkward to just stand here, so shall we go over? …Byune, let’s go.”

Vihera calls out to Byune, who is silently stroking Set—who was still a bit shaken from having spooked the horse.

“Mm.”

Responding briefly as usual, Byune heads toward Marina’s house with Rei and the others.

When they passed near the carriage, the horse naturally looked like it was about to bolt… but perhaps because the coachman managed to restrain it well, it didn’t turn into a major commotion.

When they finally arrived in front of Marina’s house, the woman who had been talking to Marina smiled at Rei and spoke to him.

“Are you Rei?”

“Huh? Oh, yeah.”

Perhaps because he hadn’t expected to be addressed so suddenly, Rei answered with a hint of confusion, but the woman who had spoken to him didn’t seem to mind at all and continued.

“Hmm. …Marina’s found herself a nice guy, hasn’t she?”

“Hehe, don’t you think so?”

At the woman’s words, Marina smiled contentedly, looking quite proud.

“Oh, Marina, you’re such a sweetie. …Anyway, I had a lot of fun today. Let’s have tea together again sometime when we have the chance? It’s wonderful to be able to enjoy tea comfortably in the garden this time of year.”

“You’re such a character, Karalia. You always say the same things. …Well, I’m glad you enjoyed it.”

With that, the woman called Karalia bowed slightly to Rei and the others, then left the spot and rode off in her carriage.

Watching the carriage carrying Karalia drive away, Rei found herself agreeing with her final remark—that it was wonderful to be able to enjoy tea comfortably in the garden during this season.

Whether it’s snowing, raining, or under the intense direct sunlight of summer… you can spend time here comfortably without worrying about any of it at all.

That was possible because Marina was an exceptional spirit mage.

If you asked just any spirit mage to do something like Marina does, they’d probably yell at you for being unreasonable.

That’s just how exceptional Marina’s skill with spirit magic is.

...Of course, magicians are already extremely rare, and spirit magicians are even rarer among them, so it’s not often that you’d meet another spirit magician.

“So? Who was this Karalia, anyway? You two seemed pretty close.”

“Karalia? She’s the wife of a merchant I met about ten years ago. She used to live in another town, but apparently she came here around the time winter was just about to set in, because of the expansion work on Gilm.”

They must be fairly close. Marina smiled happily as she told me this.

“Is that how it is? …Well, at least it’s good to know Marina has friends.”

“Listen. Just so you know, when it comes to friends, I definitely have way more than Rei, okay?”

Marina said this with a hint of exasperation, but in reality, her words were undeniably true.

Rei does have people he could call friends, but the number isn’t that high. …No, it would be fair to say there are simply very few.

Naturally, Rei’s personality played a role in that.

In comparison, even setting aside the fact that she was a former guild master, Marina—who was caring and easy to get along with—was naturally liked by many people.

(Set... no, saying that would just be the howling of a loser.)

If I included those who were fond of Set, I’d have even more friends.

Rei was about to say that, but in the end, he didn’t.

However, perhaps Marina and Vihera could see right through Rei’s thoughts, for the two exchanged glances with smiles on their lips.

As if to convince themselves that this tendency to put on such pointless airs—and the resulting awkwardness—was just so typical of Rei, and that was what made him so endearing.

The saying “every cloud has a silver lining” must have been made for moments like this.

Meanwhile, Byune paid no attention to the adults’ exchange and continued to stroke Set.

That said, Byune does have people she could call friends.

For instance, the orphans living in the Fraut family mansion in the labyrinth city of Exil, and the adventurers who train them.

That said, since the orphans had agreed to maintain the mansion in exchange for living there, they might actually be closer to servants than friends.

Furthermore, the adventurers training them might also be considered something like Byune’s servants, in that they live in the Fraut family’s mansion.

“Well, anyway… Welcome back, Rei and Set. When did you get back?”

“Hm? Oh, just a moment ago. I went back to the Dusk Wheat inn…”

She paused there, likely because she remembered that Vihera and Byune were in her room.

Vihera, who caught Rei’s gaze, flashed a meaningful smile.

Watching the exchange between the two, she must have figured out most of what was going on. Marina opened her mouth to change the subject.

“So? Since Rei’s back, that means Elena and Ara are here too, right? What about those two?”

“As for Elena and Ara, they said they’d stop by Lord Daskar’s place before coming here.”

Hearing this, Marina looked convinced.

Given Elena’s position, it only made sense that she would want to speak with Daskar first.

“In that case, it looks like it’ll be a little while before they get back. …I suppose there’s quite a bit to discuss.”

“Like the Kobold incident?”

For a moment, Marina was surprised, wondering if he already knew, but the kobold incident was already the talk of Gilm, and above all, if he’d been with Vihera, it wouldn’t be strange for him to have heard about it.

“Yes. To be honest, the kobolds’ behavior is clearly strange. It’s winter, so food is scarce, so it wouldn’t be odd if they were starving… but even so, the fact that they keep trying to infiltrate Gilm no matter how many times they’re wiped out makes me feel like someone is behind it.”

“Could it be the work of a rare species or a higher-tier species?”

“It’s true that’s a strong possibility, but no one has actually seen one.”

“Now that you mention it, you’re right. But isn’t that just because rare or high-tier species aren’t participating in the attacks?”

“If there’s no leader to rally them, don’t you think they’d just run away if things got tough? Especially the kobolds—they aren’t exactly strong monsters to begin with.”

“……Now that you mention it, you might be right.”

Even if they aren’t quite as cowardly as goblins, it wouldn’t be strange for them to run away the moment they realize they have no chance of winning.

But kobolds never show that kind of behavior; they keep fighting until the entire swarm that charged in is wiped out… or so Rei has heard.

If that’s the case, it’s true that I do feel a slight sense of doubt, just as Marina said.

Of course, it’s not so easy to know what monsters are thinking.

Given that, he couldn’t completely rule out the possibility that a rare or high-tier species might be involved in this incident.

“I’m sure Daskar has mentioned that to Elena, too. Though I doubt he’s expecting her to solve this for him.”

Rei simply nodded in agreement at those words.

Elena is in Gilm to keep an eye on the expansion project, making sure the nobles from the Noble Faction don’t interfere in any strange ways.

……Of course, that’s just the official reason; in reality, it’s Ara who’s keeping a close eye on things.

Since she’s in Gilm for that reason, she probably can’t just come out and ask her to resolve the kobold incident.

Though if Elena were to get involved in this matter on her own initiative, I wouldn’t be entirely surprised.

“Still… I never would have imagined the kobold incident would cause such a commotion until I actually returned to Gilm. To be honest, I was planning to have you start dismantling the Giganto Turtle as soon as I got back.”

“Come to think of it, you did mention that. …But won’t it be okay? Of course, we probably won’t gather as many people as we originally planned, but considering the number of adventurers in Gilm, I think we’ll still be able to gather a decent number, don’t you think?”

“Fighting the kobolds is a hassle, but I think the kind of people who are interested in the Giganto Turtle will probably show up. Though, I won’t know the official reward until I check with the guild, but depending on the situation, some might decide that dismantling it is a better deal than fighting off the kobolds, so there’s a chance people will flock to this job instead.”

Normally, it would be standard procedure for Rei to pay adventurers to dismantle the Giganto Turtle.

However, regarding the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle, the payment comes in the form of a reward from the Guild or Daskar, so Rei doesn’t need to pay the adventurers himself.

That’s precisely why Rei doesn’t know how much he’ll have to pay the adventurers. If the reward for dismantling the Giganto Turtle turns out to be better than what they’d get for defeating the kobolds, money-hungry adventurers might very well choose the dismantling job instead.

Alternatively, it’s not impossible that some might choose the dismantling job even if the pay is slightly lower, simply because it doesn’t involve combat.

“In the end, don’t we just have to go to the guild and check? …What do you say? Shall we go now?”

After hearing Vihera’s words, Rei thought for a moment before shaking his head.

“No, I’ll go to the guild tomorrow. I’ve finally made it back to Gilm today, so I want to take it easy.”

“Oh, so you came to my house because you wanted to relax. That makes me a little happy.”

Though she said “just a little,” Marina, beaming with a broad smile, entered the house with Rei and the others.
