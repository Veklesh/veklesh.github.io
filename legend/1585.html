"Damn it, I jumped the gun a bit."

Breathless, Ryutas spat out.

His body already bore several slash wounds.

Who inflicted them was obvious. The soldiers of the Kingdom of Reburina, currently fighting him inside the cave.

In terms of pure skill, Ryutas was undoubtedly superior to the soldiers.

But even with superior skill, there was no decisive gap.

It was merely a gap the soldiers could compensate for with their numbers.

That was precisely why, in the course of the battle, Ryutas... and his guards too, gradually began to sustain small wounds.

For now, they were only superficial scrapes, but if the battle continued like this... what would happen? That was obvious, requiring no thought.

Death.

Of course, since Ryutas and his men were superior in combat skill, the soldiers were also suffering heavy casualties.

Over ten had already fallen, and many more were wounded.

But even so, it was certain that if things continued like this, Ryutas and his men would eventually lose.

The battlefield had now moved entirely inside the cave.

Initially, the fighting had taken place near the cave entrance, but the soldiers' pressure had gradually pushed them further back.

Now, the area directly behind Ryutas and his men was a Y-shaped junction.

(I should have asked the one who went to call Rei and the others which way they went...)

Ryuutas regretted it, but it was only natural.

If they had retreated further back, toward the direction where Rei and the others were... and retreated down the path where reinforcements would likely come, there would be no problem at all.

But if they retreated toward the direction where Rei and the others weren't, they'd have to hold out on their own until Rei and the others broke through the enemy swarm and arrived.

Honestly, if asked whether they could hold out that long, it would be hard to nod in agreement.

That was precisely why Ryutas was torn about what to do now.

(The first place Rei and the others likely headed was where the Jaya people were gathered. But it's been quite some time since they entered the cave. Considering that, we can't rule out the possibility they moved toward the giants. Above all...)

The biggest problem is this: even if Ryutas retreats toward the passage where Rei and the others are likely to be, there's no guarantee the guards sent to call for reinforcements actually headed in the right direction toward Rei and the others...

They head toward the passage where Rei and the others are, and furthermore, the guard who went to call for reinforcements is also heading toward Rei and the others. Upon hearing this, Rei and the others immediately dispatch reinforcements.

Unless all these actions perfectly aligned, it would be difficult for Ryutas to survive.

(I want to believe I still have value to Rei... Otherwise, he'd abandon me immediately.)

He dodged the spear thrust from the attacking soldier, closed the distance, thrust his longsword forward, and pierced the man's neck.

Simultaneously, he leapt backward while pulling the longsword free from the enemy.

"Uwaah!"

"Damn it! How dare you, Dareu!"

Blood spurting from his comrade's neck splattered onto their faces, blinding several soldiers.

Blood is a highly viscous liquid.

If it gets in your eyes, it doesn't just wash out like water...

Especially in the kind of chaotic melee currently underway... and near the front lines, losing your sight would be fatal.

Indeed, Ryutas's guard seized the opening. He swiftly approached the blinded soldier and swung his dagger, inflicting a deep wound.

Meanwhile, a soldier who must have been an acquaintance of the man whose neck had been slashed shouted angrily.

"Young master, no more... We have to fall back! We can't handle this anymore!"

"I know! ...Dammit!"

Holding out here any longer was becoming impossible.

He needed to fall back and regroup.

But if he made the wrong choice here, it would mean certain death.

In other words, a choice he absolutely could not afford to get wrong.

...Though even if I made the right choice, it would require the escort to reach Rei's location without fail, and then for Rei to hear the escort's message and come to our aid... a choice with an extremely high difficulty level.

(What should I do? Which way should I retreat?)

Even as he pondered this, the battle between Ryutas and the soldiers raged on.

To the soldiers, he was the one who killed their comrades, their allies, their brothers in arms.

For Ryutas and his men, it was no longer about orders—they were seen as utterly unforgivable enemies.

"Dammit, you're persistent!"

A long sword swung with a roar of rage.

That strike, imbued with the frustration and impatience Ryutas felt... precisely because of that, it wasn't as sharp as his previous blows.

The hastily swung blow was easily parried when the soldier instinctively used his longsword as a shield.

If that had been all, he might have managed.

But... precisely because it was a strike born of haste, Ryutas's body lost its balance on a small bump in the ground—a bump so insignificant it would normally be unnoticed.

"Ah..."

An overwhelming blunder, obvious even to himself.

The soldier must have understood it too.

He didn't miss the golden opportunity Ryutas had given him in his haste. Raising the longsword in his hand...

(Damn it!)

A fatal mistake born of his own panic.

He thought about it... but instinctively realized it was already impossible to recover his stance now.

Out of the corner of his eye, he saw his guard trying to do something, but with the soldier's longsword already about to come down, it was too late for anything.

It's over...

Just as he thought that and was about to give up... Lutas felt as if a sudden gust of wind had blown.

What? Ryutas opened his eyes to see... a hole where his leather armor had been, a hole large enough to easily fit a fist through, made by one of the soldiers.

And on Ryutasu's face, something warm and sticky, something solid, was clinging.

"Huh? What the..."

There it was.

Just as he was about to mutter that, Ryutas felt another gust of wind howl right beside him.

"Leave the rest to me."

And at the same time, that voice reached his ear.

Moreover, the sensation of a robed figure passing right beside him.

Under normal circumstances, Ryutas would have easily understood what had happened.

But now, his life was in danger.

That was precisely why he couldn't comprehend what was happening to him.

But the situation wouldn't wait for Ryutas' actions or understanding.

A violent impact sent Ryutas flying.

At first, Ryutas didn't understand what had been done to him.

Still, with his innate drive, he pushed himself up with his hands on the ground and turned around... What entered his field of vision was red.

More precisely, blood.

And fragments of flesh, bone, and entrails were scattered all around.

The one responsible for this was the person Ryutas had wished would come... the one known by the alias Crimson...

「Rei！」

"Gotcha. ...Wait, usually in situations like this, the hero saves the heroine, right?!"

With those words, Rei swung the Death Scythe in his hand.

If this hadn't been inside a cave... some of the soldiers might have been able to dodge it in the nick of time.

But this was a cave.

While spacious enough for a giant to move about, its size was still finite.

And the weapon Rei wielded was the Death Scythe—a massive scythe with a two-meter-long handle and a blade nearly a meter in length.

Furthermore, besides the Death Scythe, he also wielded the Twilight Spear. Considering attack range alone, escaping from Rei's sights within this cave was nearly impossible.

Especially with soldiers packed behind him, the range for the soldiers in front to evade was truly limited.

Just a few seconds ago, the soldiers' victory seemed certain.

Though casualties had been suffered, they were ultimately within acceptable limits, and they believed victory was just moments away.

In truth, that belief wasn't entirely wrong... but only if the soldiers were fighting just Ryutas and his guards.

Then Rei arrived.

"For now, just fall back a bit."

After giving that brief order, Rei swung the Death Scythe at the soldiers once more.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

"What the hell? What the hell are you doing?!"

"Reglio! You son of a bitch!"

In a sudden flash, several soldiers were sliced clean in two at the torso.

Moreover, even before Rei attacked, the spears he had thrown earlier—not the Twilight Spear he currently held, but the broken spears stored in large numbers within the Misty Ring—had already claimed several lives.

To the soldiers, Rei was nothing but a grim reaper or a plague god.

But facing those soldiers, Rei stepped forward to shield Ryutasu and spoke.

"I don't know what a soldier like you came all this way for... but still, you came here as a soldier willing to risk your life. So, it's only natural we'd fight, right? ...Well, it's not like I enjoy killing people either. If you retreat now, I won't pursue you."

"Don't be ridiculous! You attack us and then expect us to just leave? Who the hell do you think you are?!"

"If you ask who I think I am... well. Don't you recognize who I am from this weapon? I thought my name was fairly well-known. A bit disappointing, really."

At those words, the soldier who had just shouted at Rei flushed crimson with rage and indignation, realizing he'd been utterly disrespected and mocked.

But even if this soldier didn't know Rei personally, with so many people here, it was only natural that someone would recognize her name...

"Rei? ...Impossible! Why would the famed adventurer of the Kingdom of Mireana be in a place like this!?"

"You're just making that up!"

"No, they say Crimson Rei wields a scythe far larger than himself."

"A scythe..."

At the mention of a scythe, the soldiers' gazes shifted to the Death Scythe in Rei's hands.

It was unmistakably a scythe, its appearance leaving no doubt it was anything else.

And a scythe is an extremely unwieldy weapon; normally, no one would use one like that.

Yet Rei, standing before the soldiers, wielded that scythe with just her right hand... and held a Crimson spear in her left hand as well.

It was absolutely not something just anyone... especially someone as small as Rei... could master.

Yet the soldiers witnessed with their own eyes that he was more than capable of wielding them. And they witnessed it through the deaths of their comrades.

Given that, it wouldn't be strange for them to realize that Rei... the petite figure before them... was the adventurer known by the Crimson epithet.

"This guy... Could he really be Crimson Rei?"

One soldier, somewhat knowledgeable about the affairs of neighboring nations, murmured under his breath.

Under normal circumstances, that voice would have been lost among the chatter of his comrades and gone unheard.

But... Rei's hearing did not miss that whisper.

"Correct. Seems you know quite a bit. Well then, I suppose I'll spare you for now. As for the rest of you... if you quietly retreat now, I might let you go?"

"Don't you dare joke around!"

One of the soldiers shouted.

They were soldiers of the glorious nation of Reburina.

They had been attacked without provocation, and now they were being offered mercy.

Such a thing was absolutely unforgivable.

...The Kingdom of Reburina was a superior nation, inhabited by a superior people.

Though they were currently subjugated by the Kingdom of Mireana due to numerical inferiority, that situation was bound to be overturned in the near future.

The soldiers, taught this by their superiors, had come to believe it wholeheartedly.

They were pure, perhaps.

But now, precisely because of that purity, they had no choice but to stand their ground before Rei.

Because they were superior beings.

They could not retreat before the inferior beings of another nation. But... such circumstances meant nothing to Rei.

"...I see."

With that single word, Rei had already moved right in front of the soldier who had been shouting, thrusting the Twilight Spear held in his left hand forward.

A thrust worthy of being called divine speed.

The soldier, who believed in his own superiority... or perhaps blindly trusted it, had his skull crushed before he even realized it, extinguishing the flame of his life.

Using the recoil from pulling the Twilight Spear back, he spun his body halfway and swung the blade of the Death Scythe. It sliced through the torsos of several soldiers, cleaving them cleanly in two along with their leather armor.

Watching Byune attack other soldiers out of the corner of his eye... Rei reaped lives with Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, dancing amidst the blood spurting from enemy soldiers.

Truly worthy of the Crimson Nickname.
