"Growl? Grrrrrrrrr!"

As he walks down Gilm’s main street, Set’s stomach growls at the aroma wafting through the air.

Intrigued by the appetite-whetting scent, Rei also heads toward the food stall where it’s coming from.

Since the scent of sizzling sauce was wafting through the air, it was only natural that the people around them began gathering at the stall where the aroma was coming from.

"Come on in! Grilled udon with Gamelion meat! It's a limited-time offer—you can only get it now!"

The stall owner, drenched in sweat despite the autumn evening that felt like winter was just around the corner, was calling out to attract customers.

Even as he did so, the man’s hands never stopped, continuously stir-frying the udon, meat, and vegetables on the griddle.

Gilm has become quite famous as the birthplace of udon.

There were also other dishes, such as meat buns and pizza, all made with Gamelion meat and sold at the stalls.

“Fried udon, huh. …It’s not exactly a mainstream dish, though.”

Rei muttered to himself, but in reality, most of the udon sold in Gilm was the kind served in soup.

Of course, just because it isn’t mainstream doesn’t mean there are no other dishes called “stir-fried udon.”

Although few in number, the dish known as yakisoba has been reasonably well-known for some time.

“Two silver coins, including the plate and fork! But if you return the dishes after you’re done, I’ll refund that part of the cost!”

“Yikes, isn’t that a little expensive!? Can’t you make it a little cheaper?”

A man passing by who heard the price—likely someone returning from a construction site—voiced his dissatisfaction at the two-silver-coin price.

In fact, based on Rei’s experience living in this world so far, one silver coin feels like about 1,000 Japanese yen.

Of course, since this is a different world called Elgin, you can’t simply compare prices to Japan, and besides, Rei is originally from a rural area in Tohoku.

Prices in places like Tokyo, which are known to be expensive, differ significantly from those in Rei’s hometown.

When you think about it that way, it’s purely a matter of perception… but even so, it was only natural for him to think that two thousand yen for a bowl of yakisoba at a street stall was expensive.

That said, this is the frontier town of Gilm.

Those who’ve come for the expansion work might hesitate to part with two silver coins…

“Oh, well then, I’ll have one.”

Next to the middle-aged man who had been complaining about the price, a woman in her mid-twenties placed an order with the stall owner.

Her body was clad in armor that looked like a combination of leather and metal, and she had a scabbard holding a longsword at her waist—she looked every bit the adventurer.

And even to Rei’s eyes, she was quite skilled.

At the very least, Rei estimated she was several levels above Marquise and the others, and perhaps on par with or slightly inferior to Fron.

The man who had been complaining to the stall owner turned a stunned gaze toward the female adventurer sitting next to him, who had just nonchalantly produced two silver coins.

If this had been a high-class restaurant, it might have been different, but he probably never expected someone to so casually hand over two silver coins at a street stall.

If this were some rural village, the man’s surprise would be understandable.

But this was Gilm.

For a skilled adventurer, earning that much money is entirely possible.

“Sure thing. Just give me a moment.”

With that, the owner began piling the fried udon onto a plate.

It’s packed with meat and vegetables, and the udon noodles have been perfectly seasoned to suit the stir-fry.

And above all, what defines this yakisoba is the sauce used for seasoning.

It’s made by simmering plenty of meat, vegetables, and herbs for a long time until the ingredients have dissolved.

It’s the seasoning that could be called the very heart of this yakisoba, offering a deep yet light flavor.

In fact, the smell of this sauce caramelizing was clearly what drew Rei, Set, and many others to the food stall in the first place.

And once the first person bought some, other customers followed one after another.

Naturally, Rei bought portions for himself and Set, and they began eating a short distance away from the crowd at the stall.

Since returning the plate and fork earned a small refund, many people ate near the stall so they could return their dishes immediately after finishing.

Naturally, if Rei and Set were eating in such a spot, the other customers would notice...

“Oh, Set’s eating yakisoba too!”

“Huh? Set-chan? Oh, he really is here.”

“Oh my, didn’t you two notice Set-chan was here? It seems your love for Set-chan is only that deep, then.”

“Guh!”

“W-well, speaking of which, when did you notice him?!”

“Me? I noticed him right from the start.”

"That's a lie! You just noticed Set because you came in right after him!"

While such a conversation was taking place among some Set enthusiasts, Rei and Set seemed completely unfazed as they ate their yakisoba.

(I really do think soy sauce-flavored yakisoba is the best. …Though, this yakisoba feels different from the one I had before—it’s neither sauce-flavored nor soy sauce-flavored; it feels like a third flavor.)

The yakisoba, worth two silver coins, was indeed enough to delight Rei’s palate.

The Gamelion meat, in particular, was delicious.

True to their reputation, the dish was prepared in a way that really brought out the flavor of the Gamelion meat.

For Rei... and for Set as well, the flavor of that yakisoba was more than satisfying.

The only downside was that, with so many customers crowding around the stall, getting a second helping seemed out of the question.

“Rei, would it be okay if I cleared away Set’s plate too?”

As Rei and Set finished their grilled udon, looking satisfied yet still a little unsatisfied, a voice called out to them.

Looking in the direction of the voice, they saw a woman in her mid-twenties standing there.

Come to think of it, there was a woman working near the owner, Rei recalled.

Naturally, it would be difficult for one person to handle everything alone during such a busy time, so it made sense that there were other employees.

“Hm? Oh, right. The yakisoba was delicious. It really brought out the flavor of the Gamelion meat.”

At Rei’s words, the woman looked surprised for a moment, then broke into a delighted smile.

“Is that so? I’m sure he’ll be happy to hear that.”

“Nah, considering how busy this place is, I think it’s obvious people think it’s delicious even without me saying anything.”

“No, no. It means a lot that you, Rei, said it was delicious.”

“……Me?”

Not understanding what the woman was saying, Rei tilted his head.

Rei didn’t really think his sense of taste was that sharp.

Of course, unlike when he was in Japan, his senses had become sharper in his current body.

But even though his sense of taste had become more acute, he wasn’t exactly putting it to good use.

Given that Rei was originally a high school student from the countryside, it was only natural that the food he ate wasn’t particularly luxurious.

That said, unlike the city, where sophisticated cuisine abounds, he was actually in a very privileged position in that he could fully enjoy freshly harvested, seasonal ingredients.

Also, even since coming to the world of Elgin, he mostly eats at places like cafeterias or food stalls.

……Well, he does stay at high-end inns from time to time, and the food served in those dining halls is often on par with—if not better than—that of fine dining restaurants.

In any case, I wonder why my praise—coming from someone who certainly shouldn’t be called a gourmet—is met with such delight?

Of course, as a cook, I could understand how it feels good when customers enjoy and praise your food.

“Ah… well, you see. For us street food vendors, people like Rei-san are… well, top-tier customers… no, you could even call them saviors. And besides, it was Rei-san who helped popularize udon, after all.”

Hearing the woman’s words, Rei vaguely understood what she meant.

In fact, for the people in Gilm—whether they run food stalls, diners, taverns, bakeries, or shops selling ingredients—Rei is a top-tier customer who buys in bulk all at once.

No, as the woman said, she was more than just a valued customer.

Normally, even when someone buys in bulk, it’s usually just enough for a family or a group of friends to eat together.

But in Rei’s case, it wasn’t uncommon for him to buy every single dish in the shop.

……Of course, buying that much food does place a considerable burden on the cooks.

It’s precisely because Rei has the Misty Ring—allowing him to eat freshly made food anytime—and because he’s a highly skilled adventurer who has no trouble paying for it, that he can afford to do this.

Furthermore, the dishes Rei has introduced in Gilm—starting with udon—are all delicious and have become local specialties.

It was only natural that, to those in the food service industry, Rei seemed like a savior.

And what’s sold at this food stall is yakisoba.

While the fact that Rei was the one who popularized udon hasn’t really spread to the general public, it was common knowledge among those working in the food service industry.

In other words, having Rei—such a figure—taste her yakisoba and praise it was a truly joyful event for the woman.

However, in response to the woman’s words of gratitude, Rei scratched his head, looking a little flustered.

“I did teach Dischot about udon, but all I did was tell him that this kind of dish existed and show him the basic method. It’s really Dischot’s achievement that it’s become so widespread.”

Although Rei said that, Dishot always mentions that this is a dish he learned from Rei whenever he promotes udon.

As a result, it was only natural that people often credited Rei with popularizing udon.

“Still, it’s thanks to udon that our business is thriving like this. …Though, to be honest, the profits aren’t all that great.”

At two silver coins, the price was clearly high for street food, but sourcing Gamelion meat—and clearly top-quality meat at that—required a corresponding expense. While they selected other ingredients that were as cheap and tasty as possible, naturally, those still came at a cost.

To be perfectly honest, the woman and the owner cooking at the stall both genuinely wanted to charge three silver coins.

However, since three silver coins would likely be considered a bit too expensive, they decided to keep the profit margin modest for now and set the price at two silver coins.

“Well, there are probably some folks who’d find even this price too high to afford, so raising it any further would be tough.”

Of course, even if they say they can’t afford it, two silver coins is a sum they could probably scrape together if they stretched themselves a bit.

But when it came to whether a single meal was worth that price, it was certain that a fair number of people simply couldn’t accept it.

To begin with, most of the people working on the Gilm expansion project had come to Gilm primarily to earn money.

Given that, spending two silver coins on a single meal is simply unthinkable.

Even among adventurers, many are working during this season to save money for the winter, so it’s a time of year when everyone’s purse strings are tight.

That said, thanks to the expansion project this year, even if they run short on money to get through the winter, they won’t have to head out of town to hunt monsters for work.

Even if the expansion project itself isn’t progressing, there are plenty of other jobs to be done.

In that sense, this year… and next year, or perhaps even the winter after that, there’s no need to venture outside Gilm at the risk of danger just because you’re short on money during the winter.

...Though, of course, there will likely be some who try to go outside Gilm to earn more money.

After all, while the working conditions are harsh—due to the snow and the ferocity of monsters unique to winter—there’s no doubt you can earn more money hunting monsters outside than working on the expansion project in a safe location.

“That’s right. …Oh, well then, I’ll take my leave now. I need to collect dishes from the other customers as well.”

Saying that, the woman collected the plate and fork that Rei and Set had been using—though Set hadn’t used a fork—handed four copper coins to Rei, and headed toward the other customers.

(Four copper coins… so, in reality, the price of this yakisoba is one silver coin and eight copper coins. I think it’s well worth the taste, but it might be a bit difficult to keep selling it at this price going forward.)

Watching the woman collect dishes from the customers and the line of people still waiting at the food stall, Rei found himself thinking just that.
