"Well then... let's raise a toast to the completion of the mission!"

“Cheers!”

The tavern attached to the Golz Guild.

The voices of adventurers echo through the tavern.

It was still only around 4:00 p.m., a bit early for the evening.

Even so, a large crowd of adventurers had already begun a cheerful feast at the guild’s tavern.

“Here you go, sorry to keep you waiting! First up, grilled orc meat skewers! I’ll bring out more orc dishes later, so don’t eat too much of this!”

Saying this, the tavern staff member placed skewers of orc meat on each table—not the kind with vegetables sandwiched between the meat, but skewers made of nothing but orc meat.

It had been about thirty minutes since the dismemberment of the Garangiga was finished, the materials and meat were handed over to Rei, and the quest was completed.

The adventurers currently feasting at the tavern had come here straight from the guild, still buzzing with excitement after finishing their work.

Naturally, the guild that had commissioned the butchering of the monster Rei had defeated had also asked the tavern to prepare a feast as a way to reward those who had finished their tasks early.

Perhaps what really helped the guild was that Rei donated one of the orcs to them.

Of course, the Golz Guild isn’t in such dire financial straits that a single banquet could make a difference.

Still, the entire guild staff felt deep gratitude toward Rei and the others for donating enough ingredients to feed a whole orc, not to mention their help in clearing the dungeon.

“Delicious! Yeah, a drink after work really is the best!”

“You’re right. You could almost say I live just for this drink.”

“The grilled orc skewers are… delicious. So good… But now that the dungeon’s gone, it’s going to be tough to get our hands on orc meat from now on.”

For the adventurers who found them, the orcs that had fallen to their deaths from the dungeon on the cliff face were, quite literally, a feast.

Orc magic stones sell for a decent price, and their materials are worth money too.

But for the adventurers, it was the orc meat that mattered most.

Even when traveling in a party of several people, it wasn’t uncommon for a single orc to weigh over 100 kg—and that was just the edible meat, after the internal organs and such had been removed.

Of course, there were some smaller orcs among them, but basically, whoever secured an orc’s corpse could eat as much of its meat as they pleased.

……That said, since it’s obviously impossible for a few people to eat 100 kg of meat all by themselves, they’d sell off whatever they couldn’t finish.

“Well, what can you do? It’s a dungeon that had a boss monster like that, after all. If a dungeon like that had just kept spreading out, who knows what would’ve happened.”

“Hahaha, there’s nothing we could’ve done about it. We’d have had to go out of our way to call in high-rank adventurers to clear it for us.”

“Isn’t that basically the same as our situation right now?”

“The big difference here is that this wasn’t a request—Rei and the others took on the dungeon on their own initiative. Since it wasn’t a specific request, they don’t need any kind of reward, right?”

“Those guys made a killing clearing that dungeon. They don’t need to go out of their way to collect a reward.”

As the man spoke while sipping his ale, the adventurers listening nearby nodded in agreement.

It makes sense. After all, they’d butchered monsters like the Garangiga, Kelpie, and Argos Bear that Rei and the others had defeated on a guild quest.

Just selling the materials they obtained from those would undoubtedly make a fortune… no, it wouldn’t be surprising if it amounted to even more than that.

The Galangiga, in particular, had grown to a size completely different from its original form—likely due to the influence of the dungeon’s core—and its strength had increased as well.

The adventurers who butchered it hadn’t actually fought the Galangiga themselves, and since the standard Galangiga doesn’t appear around Golz anyway, most of them aren’t even familiar with the creature.

For that very reason, there are a few adventurers—albeit a small number—who believe that all Garangigas are as large as the one they dismembered.

“Anyway, if you absolutely must get your hands on some orc meat, isn’t going to that dungeon your only option? Well, there’s always the possibility that an orc settlement might spring up around Golz.”

At those words, the adventurers listening all let out a collective gasp.

Among them, the one wearing the most grim expression was, naturally, a female adventurer.

For female adventurers who know what happens to women captured by orcs, the idea of an orc settlement forming near Golz is nothing short of a nightmare.

In fact, it wasn’t just the female adventurers—even the female shopkeeper was shooting the man a cold glare.

He must have noticed this. The man found himself hastily trying to explain himself.

“Well, I’m off then.”

“Sure, have fun. But since you’re going back to Gilm tomorrow, don’t stay out too late.”

The day after the dismantling of Garangiga was finished, Rei went out into town with Vihera.

Their final day in Golz was also the last day of their three-day date.

Elena and Marina were among those seeing the two of them off.

“I’m a little jealous.”

“What are you talking about?”

Marina muttered as she watched Vihera, who stood out even from behind, and Elena, curious, asked.

“Vihera gets to spend the whole day with Rei today, right? Elena and I didn’t have dates that long.”

“Ah, I see. …Now that you mention it, I suppose I do feel a bit envious of Vihera, but we got to go on dates with Rei first. When I think of it that way, I can understand why Vihera, being last, wants to enjoy her date with Rei to the fullest.”

While the two were having this conversation, Byune was sitting on Set’s back.

Yellow was also on Set’s back with Byune, and the one person and two animals were enjoying a leisurely time together.

Unlike yesterday, Byune didn’t have combat training today.

Since it was Relieu’s last day staying in Golz, she had already left to look for souvenirs.

“So, what should we do today? Why don’t we girls go out together for a bit?”

At Marina’s suggestion, Elena hesitated for a moment… then nodded.

“That sounds good. …What about Byune?”

“Hmm…”

In response to Elena’s call, Byune answered listlessly.

Was she still tired from yesterday, or had she just gotten sleepy after breakfast?

Elena wasn’t sure of the exact reason, but she understood that, for the time being, Byune didn’t want to go out and just wanted to take it easy.

“Alright. Then, you should rest in your room. If anything happens, Byune can handle it on his own anyway.”

She said this partly because Byune’s combat skills had improved, but also because she expected that even if something strange happened in Golz, it wouldn’t be a major issue.

“Mm.”

After giving Elena a brief reply, Byune climbed down from Set’s back and headed for the inn.

After seeing Byune off, Elena discussed with Marina what they would do today.

“Oh my…”

The moment they entered the shop, the clerk looked at Rei and flashed a knowing smile.

Then again, that was only natural. This was the shop where Rei had bought Marina a hairpin yesterday.

Naturally, the clerk remembered someone as striking as Marina, and he also remembered Rei, who had been with her.

And the person Rei was with today was radiating intense allure and leaving just as strong an impression, but she was clearly a different person from Marina.

In other words, from the clerk’s perspective, it looked like Rei was on a date alone with a stunning beauty for two days in a row.

It’s only natural that he would give such a person a meaningful look.

...On the contrary, he winks as if to say he knows exactly what’s going on, then opens his mouth.

“Welcome. This is your first time here, isn’t it? What are you looking for?”

The way she emphasized the word “first” was likely a subtle hint that Rei hadn’t been to this shop with Marina yesterday.

Instead, the clerk’s gaze silently pleaded with Rei: “You’ll buy something, won’t you?”

(Well, I don’t really need to hide it. But in the first place…)

From Rei’s perspective, the clerk’s behavior was completely off the mark. Because…

“Oh? Hey, Rei. That hairpin Marina was wearing yesterday—you bought it at this shop, didn’t you?”

That’s right—Vihera had come to this shop because she was curious about the hairpin Marina had gotten Rei to buy for her yesterday.

At Vihera’s attitude, the clerk’s expression twitched for a moment.

She probably never imagined anyone would come to the store for such a reason.

Still, she quickly hid her surprise—perhaps because she was a veteran who’d been in the service industry for years.

“Oh, is that so? Come to think of it, you do look a bit like the person who came in yesterday with that flashy Dark Elf girl… I’m sorry, she just made a stronger impression on me.”

The clerk tried to brush it off, but when you actually saw Rei, wearing a Dragon Robe and a hood, and Marina, dressed in a party dress, it was only natural that Marina would leave a stronger impression.

...Though if he weren’t wearing the hood, Rei would certainly leave a strong impression as well.

Whether she noticed the clerk’s reaction or not, Vihera turned her gaze toward Rei and spoke.

“Well, now that we’ve settled that, let’s take a look around. There might be something interesting.”

“I don’t think there’s much here that a pretty girl like you would like, though.”

Seeing him pay Vihera a compliment—though it wasn’t exactly flattery—Rei suddenly recalled that this clerk had also referred to Marina as “young lady” earlier.

(It’s obvious just by looking that Marina is a dark elf. If that’s the case, could he be saying it on purpose?)

It’s practically common knowledge that Dark Elves have lifespans far longer than humans.

Moreover, Marina didn’t look like a young girl; she had the appearance of a mature adult woman.

Of course, she should have realized that Marina was likely far older than the clerk.

“Look, Rei. Don’t you think this is kind of pretty?”

Vihera said, looking at a small box—presumably meant for trinkets—inside the shop.

The box, adorned with colored stones—not jewels—looked like a rather fine piece.

“Yeah, I guess so. …Should we buy it? We already got Marina a hairpin…”

“No, thanks. It’s fine. If I had something like this, I wouldn’t know where to put it. Just looking at it is enough to satisfy me.”

Saying that, she looked around the shop.

That said, the shop itself wasn’t all that big, so she finished looking around in no time.

In the end, she bought a small cloth pouch scented with flowers and left the shop.

"So, where should we go next?"

“Hmm, well… Even if you ask me where to go, I don’t really know what kind of places there are in this town. It would be interesting if there were an arena or something.”

“What do you expect from Golz?”

The idea of an arena was a request typical of Vihera.

…Though, to be clear, her request wasn’t to watch others fight in the arena—it was that she wanted to fight in the arena herself.

But, of course, a place like Golz wouldn’t have a colosseum.

If anything, if Golz had an arena, it would undoubtedly be even more prosperous than it is now.

That said, there’s no doubt that building an arena would require a staggering amount of money, and even if it were completed, the question of whether it could be run successfully would arise.

In particular, they’d have to gather a sufficient number of fighters—ones of a certain caliber—and if you asked whether Golz could pull something like that off… it would be difficult.

“Oh well. Then let’s just go look around somewhere. I’m sure there are places that are interesting in their own way.”

Rei couldn’t tell if the whole arena thing had been a joke to Vihera… or if it was just a light-hearted topic she could easily brush off, but regardless, it was definitely a relief for Rei that she wasn’t fixated on the arena.

“Even if you say ‘someplace interesting’… Should we go to the library or something?”

“N-No.”

It wasn’t just that she didn’t want to—the fact that she went out of her way to emphasize it meant she really didn’t want to.

As a former princess of the Bestir Empire, she should have had plenty of experience reading books… but what a waste, thought Rei, who loved reading.

“How about we go look for some magic items? Well, I doubt there’ll be any real gems, though.”

If this were a frontier town like Gilm or a place like the Labyrinth City, it might be different, but this is rural Golz.

There was a dungeon nearby, but since Rei and the others had already cleared it on their first visit, there was no reason to expect any magic items.

“Yeah, let’s take a look. There might be something interesting.”

With that, Rei and Vihera headed toward the shop selling magic items.
