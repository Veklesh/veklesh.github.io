News that Rei had participated in a mock battle reached the nobles by the following day.

Some of the nobles had even wondered if Rei might not actually participate in the mock battle... or if the rumors spreading throughout the world were simply because Rei was exaggerating his own achievements.

However, once Rei took the mock battle, those rumors naturally faded away, and the next issue that arose was who could gather a sufficient number of opponents for Rei’s mock battle.

For impoverished nobles, it might be just a few people… In some cases, there were even those who, confident in their own strength, declared they would fight themselves.

Others hired adventurers who hadn’t managed to save enough funds to get through the winter.

The latter, in particular, becomes a competition among the nobles—a race to see how many people they can gather… and whether they can recruit famous, skilled fighters.

As for the adventurers, once they learn they can spar with Rei—who goes by the nickname “Crimson”—many want to test their own skills against him, and surprisingly, many are eager to participate.

Amidst all this, Gaiska was meeting with a certain individual at his mansion, accompanied by Deotres.

That said, while Gaiska was seated in a chair, the man was made to stand, not even allowed to sit.

“So, you’re willing to spare no expense?”

“That’s right. For assassins like you, I’ll offer a reward so generous it’s almost a waste. So, gather as many skilled assassins as you can. However, I don’t want a bunch of nobodies just to make up the numbers. Only a few true experts.”

At Gaiska’s arrogant declaration, the man flashed a fawning smile.

“Aye. That goes without saying. After all, we’ve got a long track record of operating behind the scenes within Anesis. As long as we get paid, we can round up as many skilled men as you want. But… specifically, just how skilled do you want them to be?”

“I told you. I’m not skimping on money, so I want the most skilled people possible.”

“Well, but… um, hiring someone truly skilled like that would require a sum of money that even a noble like you would find difficult to pay…”

An assassin… no, not even an assassin—a man whose only skill is brokering such deals—had just looked down on him over money.

Interpreting the man’s words that way, Gaiska glared at him almost reflexively.

“Are you insulting me?!”

As Gaiska shouted in frustration, the man shook his head, saying it was out of the question.

“Not at all. There’s no way I’d do such a thing to a noble like you. But… well, if I may say so, assassins are highly skilled professionals. Naturally, the more skilled they are, the higher the fee. Especially if we’re talking about the very best available right now…”

Saying this, the man turned his gaze toward Gaiska with a hint of coyness.

Although his tone sounded somewhat fawning, the man remained faithful to his work.

He stated the price for the most expensive person available under the current circumstances.

“Eight gold coins. …Can you come up with that?”

“You’ve got to be kidding me!”

Without even thinking, Gaiska shouted at the man’s words.

But it was no wonder. Eight gold coins was far beyond the amount Gaiska could freely dispose of.

...No, it wasn’t just Gaiska; even her father, the current head of the Seisol Marquis family, couldn’t come up with that kind of money so easily.

Is he trying to trick me and rip me off?

With that thought in mind, Gaiska glared at him, but the man in front of her seemed completely unfazed by her glare.

It was a stark contrast to the fawning attitude he’d displayed just moments ago.

(This must be this man’s true nature. It’s a bit too much for the young master to handle.)

Deotres watched the two of them staring each other down in silence—though, to be precise, only Gaiska was glaring—and thought to himself.

This man, who had come for negotiations, appeared humble in his appearance and demeanor, but Gaiska suspected he had no intention of actually conceding ground when it really mattered.

Negotiating with such an opponent is simply too much for Gaesca to handle alone.

From Deotres’s perspective, it would be a major problem if Gaiska were to throw a tantrum here and take out his frustration on the servants the way he’s been doing.

“Young Master”

“……I know.”

Gaesca’s blood had boiled for a moment at the mention of the gold coin, but he still retained enough composure to snap back to reality at Deotres’ words.

“Paying in gold coins is simply impossible from my position.”

“I see. Well, eight gold coins is, after all, the amount required to hire the best assassin currently available, so I suppose there’s nothing to be done about it.”

“By the way…”

Hearing the words “top-tier,” Deotres’ interest was piqued, and he spoke up.

That said, it wasn’t as if he hadn’t intended to calm Gaiska down even a little bit by speaking up.

“Just how skilled is this ‘top-tier assassin’ we’re hiring for eight Light Gold Coins? Could you give me a rough idea, just for reference?”

“……Hmm, well. I can’t say exactly who they’ve killed, but royal family members of small nations, adventurers with nicknames, high-ranking adventurers… the leaders of knight orders whose names were known throughout the surrounding countries. That’s about it.”

True to his word, the man didn’t mention any specific names, but even so, if what he said was true, there was no doubt he was an incredibly skilled assassin.

Even in a small country, killing royalty isn’t something one can do easily, and it’s even less so when it comes to an adventurer with a nickname.

“If he has that kind of track record, then the price of eight gold coins isn’t exactly unreasonable, is it? …Anyway, young master. If hiring that assassin is financially out of the question…”

“Wait.”

“Let’s hire a different assassin.”

Gaiska cut off Deotres just as he was about to say that.

Gaiska’s gaze was intense… so intense, in fact, that if his gaze could exert pressure, it might just burn a hole through the face of the man acting as the assassin’s broker.

At first, he had thought that if he could just get revenge on Rei for the insult he’d suffered, that would be enough.

Though Gaiska felt irritated by Rei’s very existence, he was, of course, gathering information on him.

Even if not all of that information was true—even if only half of it was—the power that Rei possessed—not political or financial power, but simply raw physical strength—was not something Gaiska could easily handle.

Given that, he had concluded that the most he could do was engage in nothing more than a bit of harassment… but then, word came of an assassin who had even killed an adventurer with a reputation.

It would have been impossible not to take this seriously.

If there really is someone with that level of skill, they might actually be able to kill Rei.

If that were the case, there was a possibility of ordering the assassin to obtain the magic items Rei possessed… particularly the item box.

The Item Box... Misty Ring is set up so that no one but Rei can use it, and Rei has publicly stated that... but some people wonder if that might be a lie and that others could actually use it just fine.

Or, even if only Rei can use it now, perhaps others will be able to use it in the future. And above all...

(If Rei disappears, Elena will be mine. If that happens, I might even become the head of the Duke of Kerebel’s family in the future. Because I… I am the one who deserves that position.)

Once the thought crossed his mind, his consciousness was consumed by the future that would be best for him.

In reality, it wasn’t even a case of counting one’s chickens before they’re hatched, but unfortunately, Gaiska couldn’t bring himself to look away from a future that suited him so well.

He had genuinely come to believe that no matter what happened, he could handle it with his own strength.

“An assassin for eight gold coins… Is that truly the extent of your skill?”

“...Aye. That much is certainly true...”

The man acting as the intermediary, perhaps not expecting Gaiska to seriously consider hiring an assassin of that caliber, flashed a look of surprise for just a moment.

The fact that Gaiska’s words had caused such surprise in a man who could be called a veteran of a thousand battles showed just how shocking they were.

However, even if he were to hire an assassin of the highest caliber, the sum of eight gold coins would naturally become an issue.

Not even the head of the Seisole Marquis family, Gaiska’s father, could afford to come up with that much money on the spot.

And if you were to ask whether Gaiska—who wasn’t even the heir apparent, let alone the second son and a mere backup—could come up with that… everyone would unequivocally say it was impossible.

“Young master, how on earth are you going to come up with eight gold coins?”

“This is the gamble of a lifetime for me. And my chances of winning are incredibly high.”

Gaiska placed excessive faith in the fact that he had a track record of killing adventurers with nicknames. If he’d done it once, he figured he could do it again.

But… while many adventurers with nicknames are strong, there is a clear difference even among the strongest.

Gaiska wasn’t that knowledgeable about adventurers, so his understanding didn’t extend that far.

Deotres, of course, knew this, but since he believed it was better not to stop Gaiska for the sake of his own goals, he kept his mouth shut.

For the man acting as the middleman, if he could get Gaiska to hire an assassin for eight gold coins, the commission he’d receive would be a substantial profit.

As a result, neither Deotres nor the middleman made any move to stop Gaiska.

“But… um, is that really okay? From our perspective, summoning the Black Wolf isn’t a problem, but what if we can’t actually come up with that amount…”

“We can’t guarantee anything on our end,” the middleman says.

“The Black Wolf is the one we should hire for this job, but while his abilities are undeniable, he’s quite strict when it comes to payment. In fact, if you even try to haggle over the price, he’ll bare his fangs at you just like he would at his employer.”

Whether something similar had actually happened before or not, the intermediary’s words were laced with fear.

Even when speaking with Gaiska—or anyone of the marquis’s bloodline—the intermediary had never shown any sign of genuine fear, at least not outwardly. The fact that he now wore fear on his face made it clear just how much he dreaded this assassin known as the Black Wolf.

And naturally, the fear felt by the intermediary was conveyed to Gaiska as well.

Gulp. Gaiska involuntarily swallows hard.

He had blurted out the offer of eight gold coins almost reflexively, but of course, he had no concrete plan for how to actually pull it off.

Still, his desire to kill Rei, claim the magic item Rei possessed, make Elena his own, and inherit the Duke of Kerebel’s house gave Gaiska enough confidence to take the gamble of a lifetime.

...In reality, it wasn’t a sure thing, but rather wishful thinking. Deotres, who understood this, said nothing for his own convenience. The intermediary man reasoned that since Gaiska was a member of the marquis’s household, once he’d promised to provide the gold coins, there was no way he could possibly back out later.

“It’s fine. That Black Wolf, was it? Send him over. However, given the high fee, the amount I can pay upfront is… let’s see. Two gold coins. How about the remaining six as a success fee once the job is done?”

“I understand what you’re saying, but I’m afraid that might be a bit difficult. I’ll see if I can negotiate the fee… but up until now, we’ve always paid the full amount upfront.”

“...Even for someone like me, it’s not that easy to come up with eight Light Gold Coins. It takes time to prepare them, and it takes time to gather them. Given that, I simply can’t pay the full amount upfront.”

“If you could just call me once you’ve got the payment ready…”

“That won’t be in time!”

Gaiska shouted in frustration.

To come up with a reward of eight Light Coins under the current circumstances would require taking a dangerous gamble.

That would mean selling the mansion he currently lived in or the mines owned by the Marquis of Seisole.

He had considered taking out a loan using those assets as collateral, but Gaiska predicted that even with them as security, there was no way he could borrow eight gold coins.

The broker, who was being glared at by Gaiska, quickly ran the numbers in his head.

He could refuse outright if it really wasn’t feasible.

But if he did that, he would undoubtedly incur the man’s hatred, and above all, he would make no profit.

In that case, perhaps he should make a small concession.

Reaching that conclusion... he eventually relented, though he made a show of reluctance.
