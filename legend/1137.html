The desert at night—a time and place where one would normally shiver from the cold.

And yet, Rei and the others were walking across it.

Leading the way was Set, tracking the assassin's scent.

Rei followed beside him, with Zarust and Narsas bringing up the rear.

"...So, how far do you think they've moved?"

Zalust asked as they walked across the sand, but Rei's response was merely a slight shake of his head.

"We don't even know where their hideout is over there, so predicting that is tough. More importantly... why do you think they built the hideout outside Gosh when the targets, Daldora and Augusto, are inside Gosh?"

"Well, normally it'd be quicker to build the hideout inside Gosh. That way, if Mr. Augusto and the others showed any signs of movement, they could respond immediately."

As Zarust said, if they were going to take action, it would be normal to build the hideout near Gosh, where the targets, Augusto and Daldora, lived.

But this time, it was outside the city... and even then, it was located far from any sort of path, however rudimentary.

It was strange no matter how you looked at it.

(If there's a possible reason... maybe having a hideout inside Gosh is convenient, but also makes it easier to find?)

Though he found the feeling of his feet sinking slightly with each step in the sand unpleasant, Rei pondered.

(But does a temporary hideout really need to worry about being easy to find? If it looks like they're about to be discovered, they can just move the hideout to a different location. Setting up a hideout outside the city definitely has more disadvantages than advantages.)

Having a hideout outside the city meant they'd always have to enter the city to do anything.

That meant having to go through the main gate and have the guards process them every single time, making it impossible not to stand out.

For an assassin capable of changing their skin to disguise themselves as someone else, it might not be that big of a deal.

But nothing is absolutely foolproof. There's always the possibility that something could happen and your secret would be exposed.

(Reasons to go to such lengths to build a hideout outside the city... Huh? Is it really that inconvenient?)

Rei pondered this as he walked through the desert, but suddenly something in his own thoughts caught him off guard, and he stopped.

Naturally, Set, who had been walking beside Rei, also stopped, as did Narsus and Zarust, who had been walking behind.

"Why did you suddenly stop?"

Zalust's words carried a hint of irritation.

It was understandable he'd be annoyed, given they were trudging through the bitter cold with only starlight and moonlight for illumination.

But Rei ignored Zarust's voice coming from behind and focused on figuring out what exactly had caught his attention.

Zalust, his words ignored, started to speak again, but Narsus stopped him.

Knowing Rei's capabilities, he was likely intrigued by what Rei was thinking here.

At the same time, it was also true that Narsus sensed something in Rei's demeanor.

After pausing for about a minute... Rei finally spoke.

"Hey, doesn't this seem off? Even if they were assassinating Augusto or Daldora, why would they need to set up a hideout in a dangerous place like outside the city?"

"Well... like I said before, it's probably for secrecy, right? I've heard the assassin you defeated can change his facial skin to disguise himself as someone else. Isn't it to keep that kind of secret safe?"

"Probably. Even if they can disguise their appearance as someone else, they can't mimic mannerisms or speech patterns. If they carelessly ran into someone they knew, there'd be a high chance their true identity would be exposed."

Zalust agreed with Narsus's opinion.

But while Rei nodded in agreement, he shook his head.

"I considered that possibility too. But Gosh is a huge city. If he's hiding to carry out an assassination, there are countless places he could hide."

"But what about the 'what if' scenario? That's why we need to be extra cautious, right?"

At Zarust's words, Narsus silently nodded in agreement.

Facing the two, Rei opened his mouth, speaking softly enough that no one else could hear him, despite the desert night.

"What I'm about to say might sound incredibly unbelievable, utterly absurd. But if it turns out exactly as I suspect, things could get messy in more ways than one. I want you to hear this knowing that."

Rei's tone as he said this was anything but joking.

Seeing Rei's face, the two adventurers had no choice but to listen silently.

In terms of their activities in Gosh, Zarust and Narsus were seniors to Rei.

But purely in terms of adventurer rank... or considering their track record so far, Zarust and Narsus couldn't hold a candle to Rei.

That very fact made them feel they should listen to what came out of Rei's mouth... that they had to listen.

Facing the two who had resolved to listen in silence, Rei opened his mouth.

He shared what came to mind, including the sense of unease within himself.

...A story that, if told to someone else in this situation, they would find utterly unbelievable.

"The hideout we're heading to now. Based on what we've seen of Set, it's almost certain it's outside the city. So why is it located there? For the reason I mentioned earlier? ...I don't think so."

Hearing Rei's words, Zarust and Narsus both swallowed hard, unsure what to make of them.

Rei laying out his reasoning to the two, he continued his explanation.

"What if that location was prepared solely for the purpose of truly remaining undetected? What if it wasn't just a temporary hideout for killing the target we've assumed all along... but their actual headquarters?"

'No way.'

Rei's words caused the two men's voices to overlap.

Their expressions clearly showed they couldn't believe a word Rei was saying.

Even though Rei's opinion was immediately dismissed by the two, Rei didn't push back... in fact, he understood their feelings.

The idea that an assassin's headquarters could exist right next to a town like Gosh was simply too unbelievable.

After all, they had never known of its existence until now.

"No, but... Gosh is a major city in the Kingdom of Solrain. I've heard about and seen various incidents that seem like assassin activity before. But we've never had someone like that show up, someone who peels off people's facial skin like that?"

Zarust declared almost reflexively.

In fact, he wasn't wrong.

He was born and raised in Gosh, and even after becoming an adventurer, he'd spent a considerable amount of time there. It wouldn't be strange to call him a veteran.

Even so, if there were an assassin like that—one who peels off people's facial skin, the kind of thing you'd never forget once you heard about it—Zalust would never forget him.

Narsas hadn't been active in Gosh for as long as Zarust, but he was employed by the Erema Trading Company, the most influential organization in Gosh, and had served as the bodyguard for its head, Daldora.

But like Zarust, he had never heard of an assassin who peels off people's facial skin.

"Yeah, I've thought about that too. Honestly, when Daldora mentioned it, he seemed genuinely shocked. But..."

Rei paused, quietly scanning his surroundings.

As if to say there might be an assassin eavesdropping on their conversation.

...Of course, even while doing so, Rei absolutely trusted his partner, Set.

He knew Set hadn't been able to confirm the identities of those who shot arrows during the attack on Daldora, but he believed that if it were Set, he could find someone who had vanished using some method.

Therefore, this was more like a pose.

It showed how wary he was that there might be a place considered the assassins' base.

"If assassins had a base around here... do you think they'd cause a commotion right under their own noses? And not just ordinary assassins. Assassins who peel off their own facial skin? I don't think such peculiar assassins would risk exposing their identities."

Even as he said this, he suspected they probably weren't inactive in Gosh.

However, in that case, they'd likely make meticulous preparations to ensure their identities remained absolutely undiscovered, he added.

"...I see. So they refrain from activities in Gosh precisely because they understand their own peculiarity. Now that you mention it, it's not entirely implausible... What do you think?"

He seemed to find some convincing elements in Rei's explanation. Narsus nodded, then turned to Zarust, who still looked unconvinced, and asked.

Faced with Narsus's question, Zarust could only growl.

He knew he had to admit there was some logic to Rei's explanation.

But... even so, as someone born and raised in Gosh, the idea of an assassin's base being nearby was something he refused to accept.

"I can only say it's a possibility... Fortunately for them, and unfortunately for us, the lord ruling Gosh is far from someone with outstanding abilities."

"Huh? Wait. ...I understand that's how Ryubrand is now, but what about the previous lords? His father or grandfather..."

At Rei's words, Zarust's only reaction was to quietly avert his gaze.

Seeing that, Rei understood what he meant.

"So, it's been like that for generations?"

"...Yeah. Whether you call it fortunate or not, the lord's family isn't particularly outstanding, but they aren't particularly incompetent either. As far as I know, I've never heard of a lord being tyrannical and exploiting Gosh's residents..."

"I've heard that from Lord Daldora too. But it's not because they're particularly outstanding... I've been told it's simply because if anyone tried that sort of thing in Gosh, the town itself would likely be destroyed immediately."

"I see," Rei murmured, his expression showing understanding at their words.

This town is large within the Kingdom of Solrain, but being in the desert, it's certainly not wealthy.

Given that, it made sense that plundering or exploitation here would quickly deplete resources and lead to ruin.

"Considering that, I suppose the current lord's family became the lords of Gosh for that very reason."

"And while they didn't engage in exploitation, they also didn't notice the assassin group's base established nearby?"

"That... isn't necessarily confirmed yet, is it?"

Zalust responded to Rei's words.

But even Zarust couldn't definitively say there was absolutely no possibility of an assassin group being nearby, given everything Rei had said up to this point.

"That will become clear depending on what lies ahead in the direction Set takes from here. ...Set, can I count on you?"

"Guruu!"

Set, who had been silently listening to Rei while keeping watch, growled at Rei's words and led the group forward into the desert.

Set followed the scent, Rei walked beside him, while Zarust and Narsus pondered Rei's earlier words, keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

Nearly an hour had passed since they left Gosh.

Even accounting for the time spent talking along the way, it was by no means a short period.

(Well, even if they set up a base near Gosh, they couldn't exactly build it right next to Gosh... like right beside it.)

Rei suddenly wondered what if the assassins' hideout was right next to Gosh... But given that Gosh's guards weren't necessarily highly skilled, it wouldn't be surprising if they'd overlooked it.

(They probably anticipated that and set up their base nearby... but Ryubrand and his clan are being completely underestimated.)

Still, Rei concluded it was probably fortunate for Ryubrand that their activities in Gosh were minimal, as it helped conceal their true identities at least a little.

...Though, of course, that was merely Rei's own speculation, nothing more than conjecture.

Anyway, as the group moved through the desert night like that...

"GRRRRRR!"

Suddenly, Set growled sharply.

"Did you spot something!?"

For a moment, Rei thought so too, but there was nothing resembling a building in the direction Set was looking... just the night desert stretching out endlessly.
