"Well... I'm happy about this, but why are you going out of your way to do all this for us?"

At Marquis’s words, his companions—and even the woman who had been blown away by Gamelion and had lost consciousness—nodded in agreement.

However, when the woman regained consciousness, rather than showing joy, she groaned at the disgusting taste—which could only be described as the dregs of a potion—still lingering in her mouth. A sound escaped her lips, somewhere between a scream and a groan, that really shouldn’t have come from a woman’s mouth… but for the time being, everyone—including Fron and the others—chose to pretend it never happened.

“I told you I liked you guys, didn’t I? Right?”

“Growl? Grrrrrrr!”

In response to Rei’s words, Set growled in agreement.

Some of Marquis’s companions were frightened by Set, but others didn’t seem particularly bothered.

(Looking at these reactions, I can tell who’s new to Gilm and who isn’t, which is pretty handy. …Though it’s not always 100% accurate.)

Gilm is a big place, and there are a lot of people gathered here.

On top of that, there are more people than usual right now due to the expansion construction, and merchants are even flocking in.

Given that, it’s not surprising that there are people who have been in Gilm for quite some time but have never seen Rei or Set.

Even if they’ve heard about Set from Gilm residents, it wouldn’t be strange for them to be afraid of him if they’re seeing him for the first time.

So, the ones who look scared when they see Set are either those kinds of people, or people who’ve truly just arrived in Gilm.

“You liked him… Is that really all there is to it?”

“Yeah. …Just to be clear, you guys weren’t part of the same party before you came to Gilm… right?”

He asked that, but it was more of a confirmation than anything else.

The battle with Gamelion… Rei had barged in at what seemed to be the very end of the fight, but judging by their actions at that moment, they didn’t look like people who’d been in the same party for a very long time.

If anything, if they had been in a party together for years, they would have been so out of sync that it would be safe to say they didn’t work well together at all.

Was Rei’s hunch correct? Everyone, including Marquis, gave him a look that seemed to ask, “How did you know?”…

“Huh? Um, how did you figure that out!?”

Unable to stay silent, one of Marquis’s companions asked reflexively.

Seeing this, Rei was about to say something, but before he could, Fron spoke up.

“It’s obvious. Whether or not you’ve been working together for years—you can tell just by watching how you move in battle.”

Marquis and the others, who hadn’t expected Fron to chime in here, turned their gaze toward him and Fron’s group, looking puzzled.

“Listen up. Anyone with a decent amount of skill can usually tell just by looking whether a party functions properly as a party. Especially with guys like you—who are obviously brand-new adventurers. …Actually, why are you guys even here?”

“We’re here?”

Marquis muttered, looking as if he didn’t understand what was being said.

But Fron’s expression was serious, and it was immediately clear he wasn’t joking or being sarcastic.

“That’s right. I don’t know what your ranks are, but you certainly aren’t B or C. Even D would be a stretch. Why on earth would someone like you go out of your way to hunt Gamelions? This is Gilm. It’s the frontier, you know? It’s obvious without even thinking about it—just how few monsters there are that you lot could possibly handle with your skill level…”

“Well…”

Fron’s reasonable argument left Marquis at a loss for words.

In fact, there was no doubt that Marquis and his group had been overestimating themselves.

They’d defeated a goblin that had wandered into a construction site under expansion and convinced themselves they could handle anything.

Then, after seeing a party defeat a Gamelion and realizing just how much money that would bring in, they formed a party with like-minded friends and came here to hunt Gamelions.

As a result, because Marquis and his group had gone in so casually, they were saved by Rei just as they were on the brink of death.

(Well, considering that Fron arrived not long after I did, there’s a chance they might have made it even without me… but in that case, a few of them probably would have died. A Gamelion was coming up from behind, too.)

Considering they were already half-destroyed by a single Gamelion, being caught in a pincer attack by Gamelions would have been nothing short of fatal.

Rei patted himself on the back, thinking that his timing in coming to the rescue had indeed been perfect… while also acknowledging that Marquis and the others had simply been lucky.

Luck is an absolutely essential element when you’re an adventurer.

Even among those with equal skill, there are those who, for some reason, succeed in their quests, and even in battles against monsters, there are those who survive and those who die.

When you think about it, that ability—luck, which isn’t something you can see—was an essential ability for adventurers… for those adventurers who survive.

That being the case, even though they were in an extreme situation, the fact that Rei’s help arrived just in time to save Marquise and the others from the brink of death meant there was no doubt they possessed a fair share of that ability called luck.

(Well, they’ve got luck, but they still lack the actual skill.)

While Rei was thinking, the conversation between Marquis and Fron continued, with the two of them arguing back and forth.

...No, it wasn’t so much an argument as it was Fron lecturing Marquis one-sidedly.

But Marquis and the others probably understood that she was doing it for their own good.

Although he looks frustrated after being criticized on various points, there’s no trace of resentment in his expression.

That said, if Fron kept criticizing them like this, there was a chance Marquis and the others might lose heart.

Even though they’re companions who’ve only just met, seeing how desperately he’s trying to protect them, Rei—who’s taken a liking to Marquis and the others—felt it was time to put a stop to the conversation.

“Fron, let’s call it a day. We won’t find Gamelion by wasting time here.”

At Rei’s words, Fron finally sheathed his verbal spear.

“Hmph, fine. But listen up. If you’re going to stick with us today, you’ll have to follow my orders. I’m doing this my way, not yours. You’re still coming with us, right?”

At those words, spoken as if to drive the point home, Marquis nodded solemnly.

From their interactions so far, he must have realized that Fron and his group were clearly a cut above them.

If Marquis and his group were a bit more skilled, they would have realized just by facing them head-on that Fron and his group possessed power far beyond their own… but unfortunately, they hadn’t reached that level yet.

“Understood. We might cause you some trouble, but please take care of us.”

“Hmph.”

Though he snorted, a satisfied smile curved on Fron’s lips.

He must have liked the fact that I didn’t seem to be discouraged even after being told all that.

And if he’d managed to win over Fron, the leader of this makeshift party, then he could definitely breathe a sigh of relief.

Of course, just because she liked him didn’t mean she wouldn’t yell at him immediately if he did anything stupid while they were together.

“Well, it looks like we’ve settled that, so I’m going to go look for Gamelion. I’ll ride Set and search for Gamelion from the sky, and when I find him… let’s see, I’ll have Set make a big circle. That’ll be the signal. Then, Gamelion will be in the direction Set heads. …Is that okay?”

“If you do something that flashy, it might attract people who’ll try to chase after Rei, so I’d really prefer it if you could come down first.”

The fact that Rei rides the griffin Set is, of course, widely known in Gilm.

With Rei and Set up in the sky, hovering over the area where the Gamelion hunt is taking place… and then performing a maneuver like a wide turn, they’ll naturally stand out.

Given that the Gamelion hunt is already nearing its end, it’s not impossible that someone might try to pinpoint the Gamelions’ location based on Rei’s presence.

No, Fron was absolutely certain there were.

For that very reason, it was only natural to want to minimize the chance of being beaten to the punch as much as possible, but...

“Don’t be ridiculous. You know how fast Gamelions are, don’t you?”

As a rabbit-like monster, the Gamelion naturally prides itself on its leg strength.

Despite its massive size, it moves around at an unbelievable speed.

Precisely because of that, if they were to spot a Gamelion and then come down to the ground, there was a very real possibility that the Gamelion would vanish the moment they looked away for even a second.

But Fron spoke to Rei, fully aware of this.

“I know. But there’s a chance Gamelion might not be moving, right? Like when it’s eating something.”

“……Though I’d rather not think about what it might be eating.”

There are many other animals and monsters here besides Gamelion.

But for Gamelion, it’s easier to find adventurers who come looking for him than to search for those other creatures.

After all, they’re the ones coming toward him from his prey.

If a Gamelion like that was eating something, it was only natural that there was a high probability it was the body of an adventurer they had killed.

In other words, if what Fron said was true, they might end up finding the bodies of adventurers.

“That’s fine with me. As long as they’re adventurers, they must have come here knowing that risk.”

“Got it. But what if the Gamelion I find isn’t standing still? Like, if it’s moving around? Should I still come back to report that? Just so you know, I’m only going to find the Gamelion once.”

“Guh…”

Fron was at a loss for words at Rei’s remark.

Ideally, Fron probably wanted him to find Gamelion as soon as possible.

That alone was proof of how effective it was to search for enemies from the sky while riding Set.

No, it wasn’t simply because he could fly that it was so effective; it was precisely because Set possessed senses far sharper than those of ordinary humans, a sixth sense, and the ability to sense magic that he could perform such precise maneuvers.

Furthermore, Rei, riding on Set, also possessed senses sharper than those of ordinary people, though not quite as keen as Set’s.

Fron’s words had been based on his expectations for the abilities of that one person and one beast, but they were cut down to size by Rei.

“What do you say? If you’re okay with that, you can come back here once you find Gamelion.”

Fron, pressed for a decision by Rei’s words, felt the gazes of his comrades—and of Marquis—upon him, and eventually spoke.

“Alright. Just circle overhead. In return, I’m counting on you to find the biggest one possible.”

“That’s easier said than done. To be honest, what kind of Gamelion we find depends entirely on luck.”

A little while ago… during the peak of Gamelion hunting season, there were so many Gamelions that finding a large one would have been possible.

Of course, under those circumstances, it wouldn’t have been difficult to find a Gamelion without Rei going to the trouble of searching from the sky on Set.

Fron looked a little dissatisfied, but after Rei stowed the Gamelion’s carcass in the Misty Ring, he mounted Set and headed skyward.

“Now, as for where the Gamelions are…”

Rei murmured as they soared through the sky.

But given the time of year, there was hardly any sign of Gamelions left.

They managed to find a few, but those were already fighting adventurers, and there was no way they could tell Fron and the others to interfere.

Or, if the adventurers were in a pinch like Marquise and her group, that would be a different story, but there couldn’t possibly be that many inexperienced adventurers like them.

“Hmm, I guess there aren’t any after all. …Are there any others over in the woods or that way?”

While stroking Set’s body, Rei turned her gaze toward a small grove growing a short distance away…

“Ah.”

“Grr.”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a cry.

The moment he looked toward the grove, Gamelion emerged from between the trees, as if he’d been waiting for that very moment.

It had sunk its fangs into a deer of considerable size.

Assuming it was likely Gamelion’s meal, Rei had Set circle around, signaling to Fron and the others on the ground that he had spotted Gamelion. He then steered Set toward the deer, whose entrails Gamelion was devouring, to show Fron the direction Gamelion was in.
