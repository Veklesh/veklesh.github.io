Silver Lion meat.

The shock that word brought to the party venue was immense.

After all, the meat of the Silver Lion was the main attraction of this party to begin with, so it was only natural.

Of course, there were also matters concerning Vihera and dungeon conquests, but even so, the meat of the Silver Lion remained the party's biggest draw.

With all eyes on him, Rei spoke up.

"Those who've tasted it before know this, but silver lion meat is delicious. ...No, you could even say it's too delicious. Once you try this meat, its overwhelming flavor will leave you dissatisfied with other dishes for quite some time. Still, only those who want to eat it should do so."

Even after hearing Rei's explanation, no one considered skipping the silver lion meat.

If anything, most were even more intrigued.

(Well, it is the meat of an S-rank monster, after all.)

Rei's warning was merely a formality. He believed no one would abandon the chance to eat silver lion meat—a once-in-a-lifetime experience, if they ever got it—just because of his caution.

Though, for people like Elk and Min, there might have been a chance they could get their hands on Rank S monster meat.

Under the expectant gazes of those around him, Rei moved to the grill where several more barbecue skewers were roasting over the charcoal.

All eyes were fixed on his every move, but Rei seemed completely unfazed by the attention... In fact, clearly delighted at the prospect of the silver lion meat, he pulled it out from within the Misty Ring.

The first batch of silver lion meat he took out consisted of about ten chunks, each weighing around one kilogram.

A total of about ten kilograms.

It was an incomparably larger quantity than when they had sampled it before, but even so, it would undoubtedly be insufficient for this many people to eat their fill.

Despite having eaten various things, including Gamelion meat, before this silver lion meat, many still had room for it.

Of course, a big reason for that was probably because Rei had told them beforehand that he would serve silver lion meat before starting this party.

Even so, under normal circumstances, if delicious-looking food was right in front of them, most would reach for it.

But there were various types present: those who wanted to eat the silver lion meat so badly they could resist the temptation, and others who judged that this modest meal would never fill their stomachs...

"Anyone who can cook, give me a hand. Well, it's just cutting the silver lion meat into bite-sized pieces, so most people should be able to handle it."

When Rei said this, several people volunteered to help.

The stares fixed on the silver lion meat were intense, and it felt like things might get awkward if left unchecked.

It wasn't surprising that such anxiety lingered within Rei.

...Though the fact that the most intense gaze was coming from Azimov only made him smile wryly.

With the help of several others, Rei finished cutting the silver lion meat and placed a piece on the grill.

Sizzle. The sound echoed around them, and simultaneously, the indescribable aroma of cooking meat wafted through the air.

He wasn't using any seasoning, yet the aroma still stimulated the appetite.

Flipping the meat over, Rei brought it to his mouth once it was about seventy percent cooked.

The moment he chewed the meat in his mouth, an intense umami flavor exploded throughout his palate.

Despite having no seasoning, it was richly flavorful enough to make one think, "This is delicious enough as is."

"...Delicious..."

When you eat something truly delicious, that's the only word that comes out.

Rei recalled hearing that somewhere before, but now he knew it wasn't a lie or anything like that—it was the absolute truth.

Rei wanted to savor that meat forever, but snapping back to reality from that momentary bliss, he noticed the stares the others were directing at him.

The kind of stares that made him think, if he kept holding out like this, it would undoubtedly lead to a riot.

Of course, Rei had no intention of hoarding the Silver Lion's meat for himself. He opened his mouth wide, addressing those whose gazes were now fixed on him.

"It's Silver Lion meat! Eat up!"

'WOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOH!'

The moment Rei spoke those words, a roar erupted.

And then the crowd... young and old, men and women alike, all headed for the net below.

Well, not just the net—many headed straight for the griddle.

Watching them start to grill the meat, Rei slyly grilled the silver lion meat in the space he'd secured for himself.

"Heh heh, they're quite delighted, aren't they?"

Observing the scene around them, Vihera grilled the silver lion meat beside Rei.

Beside Vihera, Byune fixed an intense gaze on the grill—so serious, in fact, that Rei couldn't recall ever seeing Byune look this focused before.

Given how expressionless Byune usually was, this level of seriousness made her stand out remarkably.

"Mmm... mmm..."

After taking a bite, the next moment she broke into a beaming smile she would absolutely never normally show.

"Wow, I don't think I've ever seen Byune smile this much before... at least not that I can remember."

Vihera, who had been about to bring a piece of silver lion meat to her mouth, froze in surprise at Byune's reaction.

Given how she usually is, it was simply impossible to see Byune smile like this.

Vihera was utterly captivated by this extremely rare smile from Byune.

"Vihera, I get that you want to watch Byune, but the meat's gonna be gone."

"Huh? ...Oh!?"

At Rei's words, Vihera looked back at the meat on the plate.

Where a mountain of meat had been just moments ago, now only about seventy percent of it remained.

"Salt is fine on its own, but this sauce is delicious too. Where'd you get it?"

"Huh? Oh, yes. I got that sauce from a stall nearby."

A woman eating Silver Lion meat nearby answered Rei's question.

A stall? Rei flashed through his mind the countless stalls he'd eaten at before.

But the taste of the sauce Rei was eating now wasn't in his memory.

(So there was a food stall I hadn't been to yet... Well, I guess that's not surprising.)

Food stalls are much cheaper to start than actual restaurants or eateries.

This low barrier to entry meant all sorts of people got involved: those still young and short on cash, those wanting to test the waters before opening a proper shop, and those just looking to earn a little extra pocket money.

In other words, it was only natural that such a diverse array of food stalls would emerge.

Of course, with so many stalls popping up, many would inevitably struggle to keep going.

It was a mixed bag, and naturally, among them were many stalls Rei had never visited.

"Judging by the sauce flavor, is this a skewered meat stall?"

"Yes, it is. But the vegetables are quite tasty too, so I recommend them."

After saying that, the woman explained where the stall was located, and Rei decided he would definitely visit that stall another day.

(Well, probably after the aftertaste of the Silver Lion's meat fades.)

The dish made with Silver Lion meat, prepared as a trial by the cook at the stew house near the dungeon... even though there was only about a kilogram of it, the lingering taste remained long after the next day.

It was likely because the cook was skilled, but even so, given their current situation of cooking and eating it themselves, the lingering taste should still last quite a while.

The fact that we have plenty this time, compared to just a kilo before, is a big factor.

Right now, we're happily eating silver lion meat, but come tomorrow... if we're unlucky, we might start complaining about our meals tonight.

(The only saving grace is that it's not addictive.)

While savoring the Silver Lion meat and fully enjoying its flavor, Rei thought deeply.

It's the kind of taste that, once you've had it, leaves you dissatisfied with any other food until its aftertaste fades.

Of course, it doesn't mean he'd be dissatisfied with every dish.

After all, meat from other S-rank monsters should satisfy him just as well.

The problem, of course, was how to procure that meat.

"Delicious! What is this?! Delicious! Delicious!"

The one shouting in astonishment was Elk, eating Silver Lion meat away from Rei and the others.

Even for Elk, an A-rank adventurer, S-rank monster meat wasn't something he could get his hands on easily.

The overwhelming meat in his mouth! That was the feeling, causing him to cry out in surprise.

But those around him didn't say a word to Elk. They simply kept shoveling silver lion meat into their own mouths.

That's how delicious the silver lion's meat must be.

In fact, Elk is different from the others precisely because he managed to speak after tasting the silver lion's meat.

"It really is delicious... though it's a shame that after eating this meat, I won't be satisfied with any other dish for a while."

Marina, sitting happily near Rei yet wearing a complex expression that also seemed regretful, let out a small sigh.

"Yeah. We had all sorts of trouble with meals when we came back from the dungeon too. It got to the point where I thought eating goblin meat might be fine."

"No way."

At the mention of goblin meat, Marina's face twisted in displeasure.

Having eaten such a large amount of meat, she thought to herself that she absolutely didn't want to resort to eating goblin meat next.

"Goblin meat... I wish Mayo had come too."

Overhearing their conversation, Rei thought of Mayo.

She was his partner, someone he'd researched with to make goblin meat edible.

He had invited her to this party too, but she was currently busy with various things—not goblin meat, but regular work—and had declined, saying she couldn't make it.

Rei wondered if she was busy this time of year, even in winter? But when she was told it was shop work, he had no choice but to accept it.

Making goblin meat edible was closer to being Mayo's hobby than anything else.

(He's surprisingly reliable in that regard, huh?)

In Rei's mind, Mayo had always seemed like someone who lived for her hobbies.

"Hey, Rei. Looks like we're running low on meat?"

"...Already?"

At Vihera's words, he glanced around. The silver lion meat served on plates and various other dishes was already almost gone.

But that was only to be expected.

After all, there were over fifty participants at this party.

With that many people, ten kilograms of meat would vanish in no time.

Even if they'd eaten a fair amount of Gamelion meat beforehand.

"Sorry about that. I'll go get some more meat."

"Hmph!"

It wasn't Vihera who answered Rei's call, but Byune.

Stuffing piece after piece into her mouth, Rei's eyes lit up at the mention of more meat.

(You could almost hear the 'munch munch' sound with the way she's eating.)

Seeing Byune devour the Silver Lion meat so delightfully, Rei smiled happily and prepared to serve more.

The meat he pulled out again weighed thirty kilograms... not ten like last time.

"Is that a bit much?" Rei wondered, but then frowned, thinking, "With this many people, we won't just finish it—we might actually run out?"

And so, as the massive amount of silver lion meat appeared, the party reached its peak excitement for the day.

"Woooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooooo

Some were shouting, perhaps overcome with excitement.

Probably a wolf or dog beastman. Their howls echoed throughout the area.

"...Hey, Rei. Shouldn't we stop them? If they keep that up, it's going to be a real nuisance to the people around us."

"Ah, yeah. If they get any louder, we might have to stop them, but it's fine for now. It's a party, after all. People want to let loose a little. ...So, do you know who that guy is? I've never seen him before."

"Huh? Didn't you gather the participants for this party, Rei?"

"Nah, I don't know them. If that's the case, they're probably someone's friend or something."

Since he'd invited people to bring friends, it was only natural that there'd be faces Rei didn't recognize.

"Well, someone that flashy, you'd think it'd be obvious who brought them."

"I won't deny that."

Where Rei and Vihera's gazes fell, a dog-like beastman was happily running around in the snow.

Gilm had its share of distinctive individuals, but someone running around like that was bound to stand out.

...Anyway, the party that day continued with everyone happily eating the silver lion meat, and it ended in high spirits.
