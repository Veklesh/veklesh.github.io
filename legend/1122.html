Daldora was attacked. Hearing this news shocked not only Rei but naturally also Augusto and those around him.

Among them, the first to act was Zarust, hired as Augusto's bodyguard.

He signaled with his eyes for them to keep Augusto's location hidden from view, then hurried to catch up with the running adventurer, stopping him almost forcefully.

"Whoa! Hey, what the hell? ...Wait, Zarust?"

The adventurer whose movement was halted seemed to know Zarust's name.

After all, Zarust was a renowned skilled adventurer even in Gosh. His name was widely known, so it wasn't surprising this adventurer recognized it. However...

"Hey, wait. Is that okay?"

His fellow adventurer murmured softly.

Of course. As they'd been worrying about Daldora earlier, these two were adventurers hired by the Elema Trading Company.

Which meant they were effectively enemies of Zarust, who was hired by Augusto.

"No, but... do you think we can win?"

If they tried to force their way past Zarust now, he'd surely give chase.

If that happened, it could lead to a fight with Zarust at worst. Even if the two of them attacked the skilled Zarust together, they couldn't possibly win.

Therefore, the adventurer addressed by Zarust probably reasoned that it would be better to hear their business and get it over with quickly.

In truth, that reasoning wasn't entirely wrong.

Zalust only wanted the information; he saw no need to antagonize these two adventurers.

The adventurer who asked, "Is that okay?" seemed to understand this. He said nothing more, simply turning his gaze toward Zarust.

"I'm glad you understand. ...Now, I've got a question. You mentioned Daldora was attacked earlier. Is that true?"

At those words, the two adventurers exchanged glances.

Should they share this information with Zarust, who was hired by Augusto?

As employees of the Elema Trading Company, they couldn't act against their employer's interests.

But they quickly dismissed that thought.

If the commotion was significant enough to reach even lowly employees like them, working for the Elema Trading Company, then word of the attack on Daldora would surely spread quickly.

"Yes, it's true. At least the person who brought me the message didn't seem to be lying."

"I see."

He muttered under his breath.

What crossed Zarust's mind at that moment was the possibility that perhaps he had been attacked to create an alibi.

But based on what he'd just heard, it seemed unlikely that someone would have him attacked just to establish an alibi.

Of course, that was merely his impression based on observing the two adventurers before him, so it was possible that...

After a few seconds of hesitation, Zarust's gaze shifted toward Rei.

Rei, meeting his gaze, simply shook his head silently.

Rei wasn't smart enough to judge everything based on that alone.

After all, the primary reason Rei became famous as a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname was his combat prowess; he specialized in that.

Zalust hadn't been able to discern much from Rei's actions either, but realizing he couldn't get any more information from the two adventurers before him, he handed over a few copper coins.

"Sorry to interrupt your hurry. This is for the information."

"Ah, is that okay?"

"Huh? Hey, is it okay to take this?"

"It's fine. In this case, it's actually better for both of us if you take the information fee."

At those words, the adventurer who had been reluctant to take the copper coins from Zarust reluctantly nodded. The two bowed slightly and left the spot.

Watching them go, Zarust turned his gaze back to Augusto... and to his employer, who had been hidden from view by the two adventurers alongside Set.

Incidentally, since there weren't that many people, while Zarust was talking to the two adventurers, parts of Set—his tail, beak, and wings—had actually been sticking out from behind the people hiding him. Fortunately, the two adventurers seemed to be in a hurry and didn't notice.

Had they known the griffin was there, they would naturally have noticed Rei's presence as well, with Set as her familiar.

...Without Set, Rei probably wouldn't be seen as such a special presence by those around her. It would have been different if she hadn't been wearing her hood.

"So, it seems Daldora was attacked too... What do you think?"

Rei hesitated slightly before speaking to Zarust, who had asked the question.

"The most likely possibility is that he made it look like he was attacked too, to avoid suspicion that he was the only one targeted."

Most of those present nodded at Rei's words.

Normally, such a trick wouldn't seem plausible, but in this case, they thought it was entirely possible.

Especially with a combatant like Rei on Augusto's side, any overt act of hostility would spell certain ruin for Daldora.

"I suspect that too. To begin with, Daldora hired several skilled adventurers as bodyguards. For them to be bypassed and Daldora to suffer harm... it's a bit hard to believe."

The guards I saw protecting Daldora when I first came to Gosh seemed reasonably skilled.

Rei's assessment was that some possessed strength equivalent to Rank B, and Zarust, upon hearing this, nodded silently without denial.

Given such a guard force, it was hard to imagine Daldora being attacked so easily...

(Especially not by someone of this caliber.)

Rei's gaze fell upon the unconscious man.

The perpetrator who had attacked Augusto.

Asked if this man, who had been so easily defeated by Rei, could outmaneuver a Rank B adventurer, Rei would shake his head immediately.

(But then again, it's also true that this man outmaneuvered Zarust and dealt the fatal blow to Augusto.)

Despite Rei's doubts, Augusto's companions agreed with her.

"Yeah, no way he could've outmaneuvered Daldora's guards that easily. So then, it must be..."

"Calm down."

Zalust spoke to the men, whose heads were about to explode, urging them to calm down as he said.

As Augusto's guard, Zarust commanded deep trust from those around him.

That very fact meant his words took effect immediately.

"But Zarust! Augusto nearly got killed! We can't just stand by quietly!"

Admittedly, some were so devoted to Augusto that even Zarust's words couldn't calm them.

"Just calm down. Anyway, we can't just leave Augusto like this. No matter what they do, Augusto comes first."

Zalust's words, spoken as if lecturing, finally brought the shouting man back to his senses, and he nodded.

"Ah, yeah. You're right. ...Yeah, we need to get Augusto to a safe place first."

Augusto had recovered somewhat thanks to Rei's potion, but it was still true that it was better to be extra cautious and let him rest a little.

Furthermore, his clothes were torn and stained with blood from the spear strike.

If he remained in this state for too long, it was certain the guards would grow suspicious and question him.

If that happened, the man who injured Augusto would naturally be taken into custody, making it difficult for Augusto and Zarust to gather information.

Above all, considering the sand pirates, they couldn't possibly hand the assailant over to the guards.

"So, um... where should we take him? To Augusto's place?"

"No way!"

The moment one of them murmured this, Augusto immediately shot it down.

The sheer strength in his voice, unimaginable for someone who'd been near death moments before, silenced those around him for an instant.

Realizing this, Augusto softened his tone slightly before speaking.

"I'm sorry for raising my voice. But if I go home now, it'll only worry Kathy. I want to avoid that at all costs."

Hearing this, those aware of the deep affection between Augusto and Cathy immediately showed understanding.

Though he wasn't injured, his clothes bore large bloodstains. To the casual observer, it would be immediately obvious that Augusto had suffered serious wounds.

And if Cathy found out, she'd undoubtedly go half-mad.

"Well then... shall we head to the shop? They have clothes to change into there."

"Yes, that's right. That would be best. Then, let's do that."

Augusto considered the woman's suggestion for a moment before nodding in agreement.

"Yes, that's right. Let's go to the shop then. ...I'm sorry, but please make sure no one sees me."

"Oh, no problem. Here, take this."

The man held out his own clothes to Augusto.

Since Augusto's wounds had already healed, the only thing needing concealment was the large amount of blood on his clothes.

Understanding this, Augusto simply draped the offered clothes over his own.

Set also positioned himself as a barrier to shield Augusto from view.

"...Well then, shall we go, Zarust?"

"I understand."

Before Augusto could finish speaking, Zarust and another man nearby lifted the unconscious body of the fallen man.

To an outside observer, it would likely look like they were simply carrying a friend who had fallen asleep. ...Or perhaps a drunk friend.

Yet Zarust was prepared to respond immediately should the man regain consciousness, and the other man was equally ready.

As they walked, Zarust turned his gaze to Rei, who was walking with them, and spoke.

"Rei, I'm sorry, but I have a favor to ask."

"...Daldora, is it?"

Rei's words suggested he vaguely understood what Zarust meant. The man whose thoughts had been read nodded without breaking his stern expression.

"I ask this of you. If Daldora attacked Augusto to make it look like he attacked me, he should react when he sees Rei. Conversely, if he truly attacked Augusto..."

Zalust paused there.

Rei understood what words lay beyond.

That brief conversation he'd once had with Augusto. Namely...

"A third faction, huh?"

Yes. Attacking both Augusto and Daldora means there's an organization that has made enemies of both of them.

"That's right. ...Honestly, I find it hard to believe."

Zalust, born and raised in this town of Gosh, naturally knew the local situation well.

If such a third force existed, he should have at least heard rumors about it.

But Zarust had never heard any rumors about such a faction or individual.

(If that's the case, they must have come to Gosh recently...)

He thought that far, then immediately shook his head.

Given that Gosh was located in the middle of the desert, it was never a place frequented by many travelers.

Merchants did come to Gosh, but even then, their main purpose was to procure goods.

Adventurers coming here—while not nonexistent—were extremely rare.

Among those who had recently arrived, the name that immediately came to mind was... Just as he thought that, Zarust's gaze shifted toward Rei.

The person who appeared in Gosh at the most opportune moment.

But he immediately dismissed the absurd thought that had popped into his head.

Of course. Rei had helped them countless times before, and doubting that now seemed utterly foolish.

"What's wrong?"

"Ah, no. Nothing."

Rei asked Zarust, who had suddenly shaken his head while looking at his face, but was quickly told it was nothing.

"Is that so? ...Well, fine. Then, for now, I'll go find out what happened to Daldora. Should I head to the shop after that?"

"...Yeah, come to the shop first. But if I'm not there, come to Augusto's mansion."

Rei nodded at Zarust's words, gave a light pat to Set, who was guarding Augusto, and then left the spot.
