Upon hearing the report, Daskar sighed deeply.

His gaze drifted toward the window, where the moon hung, emitting a soft glow, against a cloudless night sky.

A beautiful night sky, the complete opposite of the troubles currently weighing on Daskar.

Daskar, who had been gazing at that night sky, sighed again and turned his gaze to the subordinate standing before his desk.

"Goblins and kobolds are one thing, but orcs and lizardmen are troublesome."

"Yes. Fortunately, only humanoid monsters are forming packs, so we should still be able to handle them."

"...I suppose so."

Monsters, of course, come in various types beyond humanoids—beast-like, undead, inanimate objects...

However, in this recent disturbance, all the monsters forming packs were humanoid... or perhaps we should call them subhuman-type monsters.

That's why we've been able to manage. If other types of monsters had formed swarms too, we might not have been able to handle it.

"Well, if our knights and adventurers cooperate, we should manage. ...More importantly, what's the cause? Even if we can handle it temporarily, if this drags on endlessly, our stamina won't hold out."

Daskar voiced the obvious, but suddenly, the image of one person and one beast flashed through his mind—those for whom such common sense didn't apply.

The individual who had achieved great feats even in today's battle against the goblin horde.

"Rei, huh. I'll have to properly thank him someday."

"Indeed. Losing a skilled adventurer like him from Gilm would be a significant loss in many ways."

Daskar's subordinate murmured thoughtfully in response.

Considering everything Rei had done since appearing in Gilm, many actions could be called problematic, but the benefits far outweighed them.

Rei's personal combat prowess, combined with the abilities of his familiar, Set.

Beyond that, the new udon dish Rei introduced has become a Gilm specialty.

Moreover, Set had become a symbol of Gilm—albeit in a mascot-like sense—and data showed that since Rei and Set arrived, minor crimes like fights had decreased.

(The idea that people were calmed by Set's cuteness, their irritability lessened, and as a result, fights and such decreased... I couldn't quite believe it when I first heard it.)

Daskar didn't believe that story at first, but when shown the actual numbers, he couldn't deny it.

Additionally, among the benefits Rei and Set have brought, he'd heard that shops—especially those dealing in foodstuffs—were seeing increased sales.

As the lord of Gilm, he certainly had no complaints about business becoming more active.

Beyond that, he'd heard that feathers and hair found around Set's stables were extremely useful as alchemical materials, and that alchemy throughout Gilm had improved compared to before.

Daskar understood perfectly well that it was absolutely impossible to treat such benefactors carelessly, given the myriad benefits, large and small, they received from Rei and Set.

(Not to mention Princess Vihera... no, the former princess, and even Guildmaster Marina are favorably disposed toward Rei. ...And then there's Princess General Elena. Was he born under a star that attracts beautiful women? Well, they're all quite unique individuals.)

For Daskar, the fact that even Marina held affection for Rei stirred up complex feelings.

For him, it was someone he wanted to forget, but Marina was his first love. ...In fact, he'd even proposed to her when they were children.

Of course, he no longer harbored such feelings for Marina now, but it still stirred something within him.

Remembering that time made him want to curl up in bed, clutching his head and screaming.

"Daskar-sama?"

Lost in thought, Daskar snapped back to reality at his subordinate's voice.

Now was not the time to dwell on memories he wished to forget, or on the colorful individuals gathering beneath Rei.

"So, why are only humanoid monsters rapidly producing higher-tier species and forming their own packs? What's the reason for that?"

"Unfortunately. We currently have scholars investigating that aspect, and we've also inquired with Guildmaster Marina."

"...I see."

Daskar gasped for a moment when the name of the dark elf he'd thought of just seconds ago came up.

Still, he managed to suppress that surprise—or perhaps agitation—immediately. That was likely a testament to his ability as Gilm's lord.

After all, this Gilm was in the frontier; surprises were bound to happen.

Showing visible agitation over mere surprise would make governing Gilm extremely difficult.

"Anyway, we can handle whatever comes at us right now. But there are more goblin hordes besides the ones heading for Gilm, and there are also packs of monsters far more powerful than goblins. Considering all that, we can't afford to linger too long."

Even if they aren't heading for Gilm now, if other monster hordes were to converge on Gilm together, Gilm might not lose, but the damage would be significant.

(Of course, the reason things escalated so dramatically today was because they attacked suddenly in broad daylight. Had we known such monster groups existed from the start, we could have prepared high-rank adventurers.)

It was precisely because he was thinking this way that Daskar wasn't taking it too seriously.

Of course, that didn't mean he was underestimating the current situation.

That's precisely why he was inquiring with various parties about whether the current situation could be resolved.

"Yes. Tonight, too, the knights and guards are ready to deploy at any moment."

Nodding at his subordinate's words, Daskar began considering ways to improve the situation, however slightly.

The next morning, after finishing breakfast in the dining hall as usual, Rei headed to the guild with Vihera and Set.

However, the atmosphere throughout the city was distinctly different from usual.

Everywhere in town, people gathered in groups, deep in conversation.

"Things have changed quite a bit in just one day, haven't they?"

"...Well, goblins aside, I hear other monsters are forming packs too. Given that, it's only natural to feel uneasy."

"Grrr..."

Set, who had been observing the surroundings, growled worriedly.

His concern wasn't for his own safety, but for the person who always paid attention to him... and the person who gave him food.

Given his naturally friendly disposition, it was only natural for him to worry whether his close companion was feeling uneasy.

(A normal monster probably wouldn't act like this. ...Maybe because it was created by that 'Beast Magic' thing, it inherited some of Rei's personality? But Rei isn't exactly as friendly as Set.)

Watching Set's behavior, Vihera murmured to herself, recalling the explanation Rei had given her yesterday.

Monsters created by Beast Magic don't necessarily inherit the personality of their magic's owner... but they must be influenced to some degree, right?

(If Rei were this friendly, that would probably cause its own set of problems.)

Rei's features were so refined they could almost be called feminine.

Yet the reason Rei wasn't widely liked by the general public likely stemmed from his own personality.

Even if the person is a noble, he firmly refuses when he needs to.

In fact, it's not uncommon for him to even strike back.

Few could truly fall deeply in love with such a Rei.

For Vihera, this wasn't a cause for sorrow; it was actually a blessing in disguise, meaning she had fewer rivals for his affections.

Though, to be fair, Elena and the other rivals were certainly no pushovers either.

(Perhaps I should have learned more feminine skills, like cooking or sewing?)

Vihera understood her own features were well-proportioned and that she possessed a figure men found attractive.

That's precisely why she believed that even if she found someone she loved someday, winning their heart wouldn't be difficult... She never imagined that others would appear, possessing beauty and figures that were her equal or even surpassed her own.

She had heard from Elena that it was already decided that Rei would be bound to multiple women, but even so, wanting to be the most important one was probably just her instinct as a woman.

"Vihera? What's wrong?"

Lost in thought as she walked, she snapped back to reality at Rei's voice beside her.

"Nothing. I was just lost in thought."

"Wondering if a powerful monster horde might appear, or something?"

"Oh? So if I'm lost in thought, it must be about battle? Wouldn't you think I might be worrying about delicious food or pretty clothes or something like that?"

"...Well, it is Vihera."

If it had been Rei, I would have accepted that explanation.

But this Vihera was different from before. Just seconds ago, she'd been agonizing over her femininity.

That's precisely why, hearing Rei say she wasn't feminine now, it couldn't be amusing.

"Yes, that's right. Unfortunately, I'm not feminine. I'm sorry about that."

「Vihera？」

Vihera reacted with unusual sensitivity to words she would normally have laughed off.

Rei didn't respond when Rei called out to her, leaving Rei behind and starting to walk away.

...Still, the fact that she was heading for the guild was typical Vihera, one might say.

"Grrr?"

Set looked worried, wondering if he'd upset her. Rei scratched his head, looking slightly troubled.

"Why'd she get mad? ... Seriously, they say women's hearts and autumn skies are unpredictable, and boy, is that true."

He looked up at the sky as he said it, but what met his gaze was a massive cumulonimbus cloud—hardly what you'd call an autumn sky.

No matter how you looked at it, it was a summer sky.

Rei stared at the sky for a few more seconds before suddenly feeling a gaze. He looked over to find Vihera had stopped moving and was staring intently at him.

"What are you doing? Aren't we going to the guild? Then stop staring at the sky here and let's hurry up and go."

"...Yeah."

He really couldn't understand women's hearts or autumn skies.

Thinking that, Rei set off after Vihera alongside Set.

"It's pretty quiet compared to yesterday, isn't it?"

"Yeah. Well, knowing there's a goblin horde around, I suppose we can figure out a way to deal with it."

Vihera nodded in agreement as Rei muttered while scanning their surroundings.

Having walked all the way to the guild, her mood seemed to have improved somewhat. Now, Vihera even wore a faint smile.

Though Rei suspected the main reason for her improved mood was likely her imagination conjuring up a powerful monster horde.

Being the combat-loving Vihera, he probably felt the exact opposite of the usual reluctance to fight powerful monsters.

(Well, powerful monsters usually yield rare materials, so thinking that way, it's not like everyone wants to avoid them.)

For adventurers confident in their own strength, powerful monsters meant gold coins, silver coins... platinum coins, and if the monster's materials were truly rare, even light gold coins.

Of course, that's only if you have the strength to defeat them.

"Ah, Rei-san. And Vihera-san too. You've come at just the right moment. We've received a named request regarding this pack. What shall we do?"

Lenora spotted Rei and Vihera and called out to them.

Though Lenora usually radiated boundless energy, the grueling work since yesterday had left her looking exhausted.

The same was true for Kenny, who was talking with other adventurers a short distance away. Normally, she'd notice Rei immediately, but she still hadn't seemed to spot him yet.

(She must be that exhausted from yesterday's events.)

Rei glanced at Kenny for a moment before quickly turning his gaze back to Lenora.

"So, where's the request from? What kind is it?"

"From the Knights. Apparently, a group of lizardmen is near Gilm. They want us to investigate... and if possible, wipe them out."

Hearing "lizardmen," Rei and Vihera both reacted simultaneously.

For Vihera, it meant the prospect of fighting powerful monsters, while for Rei, it meant the potential to obtain the magic stone from the lizardman leader.

"What do you say?"

"It's obvious, isn't it?"

With that brief exchange, they confirmed each other's resolve... and Rei and Vihera decided to accept the request.

Though Rei had various thoughts about Vihera, whose mood had completely lifted... but that was another story altogether.
