As I slurped the prepared udon, the customers inside the shop gradually left, one by one.

Seeing them off was Sandrine, smiling.

"Thank you for coming. Please come again."

Hearing this, the customers seemed to feel no ill will, returning a friendly smile as they left the shop.

Outside, Set, who had received the bones used for the broth, watched them go.

...The sight of him crushing bones as thick as an adult man's arm with his beak would normally be enough to inspire fear.

But the regulars at this shop are used to Set. Even seeing him chew bones doesn't particularly frighten them.

In fact, some even patted him on the head before leaving.

Though some were too anxious about leaving only Rei and Sandrine behind in the dining hall to have any room for fear.

To be precise, Rodrigo was there too, so it wasn't just Rei and Sandrine alone.

Still, the fact that people worried about them showed just how popular Sandrine was as the shop's star employee.

"Rei, how's our udon?"

"Ah, it's delicious. The noodles have a nice bite and aren't overcooked. The dipping sauce isn't bad either. ...Though, I feel like there could be a bit more toppings."

"Ah... sorry about that. That's leftover from lunch. Usually, there's a bit more topping."

Rei shook his head at Sandrine's words.

"I'm not blaming you or anything. It's my fault for showing up so suddenly at this hour. Besides, it's just that I feel like there's a bit less topping, but the flavor itself is satisfying."

If his palate were more refined, he could probably offer advice on flavor balance or the texture of the noodles. But in Rei's case, his taste buds were basically childish.

That was obvious enough, given how he couldn't handle alcohol of any kind.

If asked exactly how this udon compared to the udon at Mankyutei, where he'd first learned to eat it, Rei could only tilt his head in confusion... Fortunately, Sandrine didn't ask him that sort of question.

After confirming the shop was completely empty and finishing the last sip of his udon broth, Rei spoke up again.

"So, what's the deal with the meat buns? Have you served them yet?"

Even as he asked, Rei could easily guess they probably hadn't been served yet.

It felt odd for him to say this, having taught her the recipe himself, but steamed buns were a completely unknown dish in Gilm.

Given that, if they were on the menu, they would have been the talk of the town by now.

Sandrine seemed to understand this. She opened her mouth with a slightly sulky expression.

"Unfortunately, not yet. But Dad's working hard on it, so I think it'll be ready soon. I tried one yesterday, and it's gotten much tastier compared to the first batch he made. ...Daddy! How's it going?"

As she spoke, a hint of pride flashed across her face. It was likely because she was confident in the flavor of the steamed buns and, above all, trusted her father's skills.

"Okay, okay, just wait a moment. It'll be ready soon."

Hearing that confident voice from the kitchen, Rei nodded in understanding.

"Looks like Sandrine was right after all."

"Right? And I've been experimenting with the fillings too, you know? Like Rei said, not just meat paste, but stewed meat and stuff. ...Well, vegetables are a bit tricky this time of year, though."

"I suppose so."

Unlike Japan, this world of Elgin doesn't have greenhouse cultivation.

Of course, similar results could be achieved using magic items, but such items were expensive and not easily purchased.

(Plastic greenhouses... I know a fair bit about them. Should I teach her this method? But there's nothing to substitute for the plastic film...)

Since his family ran a farm, Rei had helped out there.

And since his family home had a plastic greenhouse, he possessed some knowledge.

But since vinyl doesn't exist in this world, building a vinyl greenhouse is basically impossible.

Moreover, even if one built a plastic greenhouse, growing vegetables might require some form of heating equipment.

There might be the option of a glass greenhouse, but Rei had no idea how much that would cost. More importantly, all she knew about was plastic greenhouses; her knowledge of glass greenhouses was limited to what she'd seen on TV.

(Besides, a greenhouse large enough to grow vegetables for eating? Only nobility could afford that, let alone a commoner.)

If he built a greenhouse based on his vague memories and it failed, it would be a literal eyesore.

As Rei pondered this, Rodrigo emerged from the kitchen.

On the plate in his hands sat three freshly steamed buns, still puffing steam.

Steamed buns were certainly a dish you could eat anytime.

But from his time in Japan, Rei still strongly associated them with cold weather.

In that sense, steamed buns at this time of year perfectly matched Rei's image.

"Here you go."

Rodrigo placed the steamed buns in front of Rei, his expression confident yet still tinged with nervousness.

Compared to when he and Rei had made and eaten them together before, he felt the flavor had improved considerably.

Still, having Rei—the person who taught him how to make them—taste his buns was more than enough to make Rodrigo, with his naturally timid nature, feel quite nervous.

He gently picked up the bun from the plate on the table.

At this point, he already knew this was different from the meat bun... no, the meat bun imitation he'd eaten before.

The texture of the bun in his hand was noticeably softer than before.

Bread baked in a regular oven versus steamed meat buns.

This difference must have been the result of trial and error with the bread dough recipe.

Splitting the bun in half... the moment the aroma of the meat filling hit Rei's nose.

That aroma, too, was different from before—it was now a more appetizing scent.

Previously, they had only seasoned it with salt, but this time several spices were added, making it distinctly different.

Amazed at Rodrigo's skill in making such improvements in such a short time, Rei brought the bun to her mouth.

The texture of the bun was distinctly different from when he had eaten it before.

Soft, yet possessing a subtle sweetness.

Next, the flavor of the meat filling spread throughout my mouth.

The oak meat, cut slightly larger than minced, allowed me to fully enjoy its texture.

The textures of several vegetables also complemented the oak meat perfectly.

The flavor of the meat filling was subtly different from the meat buns Rei knew, but since she didn't know what ingredients were used in the meat filling of regular meat buns to achieve that flavor, she couldn't point it out.

Even if someone said it tasted different and asked what kind of seasoning it was, Rei couldn't answer.

(Ah, but I think it had sesame oil in it... or maybe not?)

Thinking like that, she savored the flavor of the meat bun as she swallowed it.

"Mm, tasty. A bit different from what I expected, but this is definitely marketable."

Rei meant it as praise, but Rodrigo somehow looked disappointed and hung his head.

"Hey, Dad. What's wrong? Rei-san praised it, so why are you getting down?"

"Ah, no. I thought I did my best, you know. But to be told the taste is different from what I expected... well?"

Perhaps because he had been so confident, Rodrigo felt an even greater sense of helplessness at Rei's words.

"Calm down. First off, the only meat buns I know are the ones described in books. I was just guessing what they'd taste like based on that. It's no big deal if it's different."

Sandrine, who was half-convinced Rei had eaten meat buns before, felt a twinge of doubt at those words. Still, she decided cheering her father up was more important right now and offered words of encouragement.

"That's right, Dad. Besides, Rei said your own version was delicious enough. Have confidence!"

"...Really?"

"Yes."

"Yeah."

Rodrigo looked up at them questioningly, and both Sandrine and Rei nodded.

Still, seeing Rodrigo clearly lacking confidence in the taste of his own meat buns, Rei hesitated, unsure what to do... until she noticed Set peering inside from the shop entrance.

After a moment's thought, Rei picked up a meat bun and walked straight over to Set.

"Here, Set. This is delicious. Try it."

"Grrr..."

Though it was Rei who spoke, Set hesitated slightly.

The appearance was almost identical to what he had eaten before.

The aroma wafting up was quite different, yet Set still hesitated.

The meat bun he'd eaten before... or rather, the meat bun imitation, hadn't been particularly delicious, but it certainly hadn't been so bad he couldn't eat it.

But precisely because he'd eaten it with expectations, the taste hadn't satisfied Set.

What if the meat bun in Rei's hand was the same?

Set harbored that slight worry, yet he couldn't imagine the food Rei handed over with such confidence could be bad... Eventually, Set took the meat bun from Rei's hand with his beak and brought it straight to his mouth.

He tasted it cautiously, as if afraid...

"Gurrr!"

Soon, Set cried out with shining eyes, "Delicious!"

Not only that, he pleaded with Rei with his round eyes, wanting more.

Rei gave Set the rest of the meat bun in his hand while turning his gaze to Rodrigo.

Rodrigo's expression shifted from surprise to a smile that seemed to say, "I can't believe this."

(He wouldn't have trusted me or Sandrine if we'd said it, so why does he believe Set so readily? Well, I guess it's not my place to say since I'm the one who fed him...)

Watching Rodrigo's delighted expression, Rei wondered: Was it because Set was a monster, not a human, that he trusted him so completely?

Humans lie, but a monster like Set would honestly say something delicious is delicious.

"As you can see, Set is thoroughly satisfied. Doesn't that give you confidence? Since coming to Gilm, Set has sampled dishes from all sorts of restaurants, so his palate is reliable."

Those words were absolutely true.

Having roamed countless stalls and eateries, Rei and Set had probably sampled more diverse dishes than any other resident of Gilm.

...Though, in Rei's case, perhaps because his taste buds are childish, even if asked to describe flavors in detail, he could only come up with clichéd phrases.

However, since Rei and Set arrived, the sales of food-related shops in Gilm—stalls, cafeterias, bakeries—have been steadily increasing across the board.

Considering that, one could argue there was some persuasive power to what Rei was saying.

Rei's words, Set's cheerful prompting for "A little more, please?", and Sandrine's gaze—wondering why her own father couldn't have more confidence in his skills—finally made Rodrigo nod.

"Alright. If you say so, Rei... Well, due to prep work, it'll be tomorrow. I'll start serving it tomorrow."

"Ah, do your best. And as I think I mentioned before, this dish, the meat bun, can change its impression quite a bit just by altering the filling inside. So, you should keep experimenting, like trying different fillings instead of just the meat filling."

"Yes. ...Um, Rei. About these meat buns... would it be okay to teach the recipe to the other chefs?"

"Huh? Wait, Dad!? What are you suddenly talking about?!"

Sandrine gasped in surprise, clearly not expecting such words from her own father.

But seeing his daughter's reaction, Rodrigo smiled and spoke.

"Regarding these meat buns, that was always the plan. You know that, Sandrine, right? Thanks to the folks at Manpukutei spreading udon noodles, udon became so widespread in Gilm. So, don't you think we should do the same for these meat buns?"

"Well... but... Ugh, fine. But you have to explain how to make this dish yourself, Dad!"

"I know. I'll do my best. ...Besides, the fact that we were the first to learn about these meat buns hasn't changed. Don't you think that being the originals is a big enough selling point?"

At those words, Sandrine reluctantly nodded.

With that, the discussion settled. Rei, who had been silently watching the parent-child exchange, finally spoke up.

"Hey, do you know what pizza is?"
