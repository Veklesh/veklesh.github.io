A spot slightly removed from the dungeon's entrance.

The space there warped for just an instant, and the next moment, Rei and the others appeared there.

Yes... Rei, Elena, Marina, Vihera, Ara, Elk, Min, Rodos, Johanna, and Johanna's companions, and so on.

Naturally, Set and Yellow were among them as well.

Only Rodos couldn't stand on his own and was carried on Elk's back—the Axe of the Thunder God had been stored in Rei's Misty Ring—but everyone else was completely unharmed.

...Immediately after the Silver Lion battle ended, Rei, Elk, Ara, and even Set were covered in wounds all over their bodies, as expected. However, the external wounds had already been healed using potions.

Of course, that only applied to visible wounds; the internal pains and injuries remained untreated.

Rei, too, had taken multiple blows from the Silver Lion through his Dragon Robe, leaving him with numerous internal pains.

He didn't mention it partly because the pain wasn't unbearable, but the biggest reason was likely that he didn't want to be forced to drink potions to heal his internal injuries.

After all, Johanna would guide them to a shop where they would hold a grand banquet to celebrate Vihera and Rodos's return, their victory over the Silver Lion... and the completion of the dungeon's conquest.

Drinking a potion now would ruin his sense of taste, completely spoiling the delicious food at such a celebratory feast.

Of course, if there were any combat ahead, Rei wouldn't hesitate to drink the potion.

But since it was possible to teleport directly from the dungeon's lowest level to the entrance area, such worries were practically nonexistent.

Rei, having materialized, bowed his head toward Grimm, who stood a short distance away.

"Grim, you've been a great help this time. Thanks to you, we were able to restore Vihera and Rodos' consciousness."

As Rei spoke, the others also bowed deeply to Grimm. Even Set bowed his head. ...Though Yellow, perched atop Set's head, only bowed because everyone else was doing it.

It was only natural, of course. There was no doubt that everyone present felt gratitude toward Grimm.

Yet, despite their gratitude, Grim remained... a rather peculiar being.

There was awe, and there was fear.

To be honest, quite a few wished to be away from Grimm as soon as possible.

But they couldn't possibly act so discourteously toward someone they revered... or toward the one who had restored their son's consciousness.

Grimm saw the intense fear mixed with gratitude in the gazes others directed at him, but he never voiced it.

He was acutely aware that he was an undead monster, a being that inspired fear and revulsion.

If anything, he was slightly surprised that his actions in saving Vihera and Rodos had shifted their feelings from disgust to awe, however slightly.

"Heh heh heh. Don't worry about it. Rei is a special existence to me in many ways."

Rei felt relieved that Zepair's name hadn't been mentioned.

He understood that Grim treated him kindly solely because of the matter involving Zepail.

...Though, truth be told, by now it wasn't just through the Zepail filter; he genuinely cared about Rei and, at the same time, found her rather likable.

"Um, Lord Grimm. I'm terribly sorry for all the trouble you've gone through for me... No, thank you."

This was an unusual attitude for Vihera.

But he felt certain that it was only because of Grimm that he had regained consciousness... no, more than that, that he had absorbed the most potent essence of Ambris and the Silver Lion, increasing his power.

Without Grimm, she would still be trapped in the spiritual realm, holding the upper hand against Ambris but unable to deliver the decisive blow.

It became possible because Rei and the others defeated the Silver Lion, and Vihera gained power through Grimm using its heart.

Even if he was no longer human... no, even if he had become something beyond human, there was no trace of sorrow within Vihera.

Rather, there was even joy in the fact that she could live the same time as Rei.

...Yes, the current Vihera had ceased to be human, having absorbed both Ambris and the Silver Lion.

This was the conclusion reached after Grimm's investigation, and for Rei and the others, it was an indisputable fact.

While outwardly, the only difference was a change in eye color, internally, she was vastly different.

Naturally, her lifespan had become vastly longer than a human's. She possessed a lifespan that would surely rival Elena, who inherited the Ancient Dragon's magic stone; Marina, the Dark Elf; and above all, Rei—a homunculus or artificial human created by the pinnacle of the Zepairu clan's technology.

At the very least, she was not destined to live only a hundred years like ordinary humans.

Grim had already determined this with a simple check—literally within seconds of casting his magic circle.

"Do not fret. As one of Rei's companions, you shall spend an eternity together. Considering that, this is but a trifling matter."

Grimm replied to Vihera, but several present sensed a hint of loneliness... and sorrow in his words.

Because he had become undead, Grimm had no one left who had lived alongside him.

That said, if asked whether he regretted it, he would likely answer no.

While becoming undead brought loneliness, it also brought joys that outweighed it.

Especially meeting Rei, Zepair's successor—still immature, but nonetheless significant to Grimm.

That's precisely why, when Rei asks him for a favor like this, he feels compelled to respond.

"But..."

At Grimm's words, Vihera's cheeks flushed faintly.

Though Vihera usually made no secret of her feelings for Rei, being told this by someone else—someone likely close to Rei—made her feel embarrassed.

"Lord Grimm, I'd hate for you to forget about me."

"Yes, of course, don't forget me either."

Seeing Vihera blush as Rei's companion, Elena and Marina also began asserting themselves to Grimm.

(Somehow, it feels like the outer defenses around us have been filled in without us noticing...)

Watching Elena and Marina, Rei murmured to himself.

"Grr?"

What's wrong? Set purred, rubbing his face against Rei's.

Rei smiled at Set's concern and stroked his head.

It might be close to a form of escapism... but that didn't mean Rei disliked Elena and the others.

Rather, it was undeniable that he felt fondness toward them.

"Um, Mr. Grimm. Thank you for helping my son too."

While keeping an eye on Elena, Marina, and Vihera starting their own battle, Min bowed her head to Grimm.

She knew full well that her son regaining consciousness was largely thanks to Vihera's efforts, but still, without Grimm's help, who knew how long it would have taken for Rodos to regain his senses.

Considering that, Grim was indeed a benefactor to Min—not human, but a benefactor nonetheless.

"I see. If that is how you feel, then so be it."

For Grimm, Rodos was merely incidental to Vihera; his primary objective had always been Vihera.

Still, if he was going to express his gratitude like this, then it was acceptable to accept it.

That was the reasoning behind Grimm's reply.

"Ugh... ah..."

Rhodos tried to say something from Elk's back.

But it seemed Rhodes still couldn't form coherent speech properly, and it didn't come out as proper words.

Still, Grimm seemed to understand what Rhodes wanted to say, and the skeleton face gave a small nod in response.

"Well then, I suppose I should be off around here. If I stay here too long, it might cause unnecessary trouble."

"I see. ...Understood. Thanks to you, Grim, I was truly saved this time. If anything else comes up, I'll contact you."

"Heh heh heh. You needn't wait for something specific to get in touch. Talking with you is rather refreshing for me, you know."

Laughing, Grimm waved his staff-wielding hand broadly.

The next instant, Grimm vanished from before Rei and the others.

"Huh?"

Who had let out that sound?

But Rei thought it was understandable.

After all, Grimm had been right there in reality just a moment ago, and now he was gone.

Could it be that what they had been seeing just moments ago was some kind of illusion?

It was so sudden that even knowing it was impossible, he couldn't help but think that.

Of course, remembering the pressure Grim had been exerting made it clear that the figure before them hadn't been an illusion.

"...Should we go soon? There's no point in staying here like this forever."

Rei's voice echoed around them.

They were currently in a dead end within the dungeon... a place not far from where they had entered.

Someone might suddenly show up here at any moment.

(No, it's a dead end near the entrance. Unless there's some compelling reason, no one would deliberately come here.)

But that's precisely why dungeons exist—for those reasons to arise.

The others seemed to understand this well enough, as none objected to Rei's words.

...Though, to be honest, few would willingly choose to stay inside a dungeon like this.

"Right. Once we get out of the dungeon, there'll be plenty to do. Going to the guild branch office should be top priority. ...Rei, I'll be counting on you to come too, okay?"

"Huh? Me too?"

Marina's words caught Rei completely off guard, and he muttered in surprise.

Marina opened her mouth, looking exasperated at such a reaction from Rei.

"Of course. After all, Rei was the one who destroyed the dungeon's core."

Faced with that, Rei couldn't argue.

Marina's point was undeniably valid.

"Hmm... But even if Rei destroyed the dungeon's core, Marina saw it too, right? So maybe there's no need to force Rei to come along? There are things we need to keep hidden, after all."

Several of those listening to Vihera's words nodded in agreement.

Rei was never good at this sort of manipulation.

Whether he could cover up the truth... that was probably what worried them.

"Right. But that's why I'm coming along too, isn't it? So don't you think we can stop worrying about that?"

"...I think it'd be better to just say Marina destroyed the dungeon's core."

"Listen..."

Marina sighed at Vihera's words and shifted her gaze to Rei's right hand... or more precisely, to the Misty Ring embedded in her right arm.

"The dungeon's core is sliced clean through like that? And my weapon is a bow. How exactly am I supposed to explain that?"

"Hmm... Elemental magic or something?"

At that, those around them nodded again... but Marina shook her head.

"It's the dungeon's core, after all. Naturally, it'll be thoroughly examined. That should quickly prove it wasn't severed by spirit magic."

"Is that so? ...Well, it is a dungeon core. They'd definitely investigate it thoroughly, no doubt."

"Yes. And besides, as long as I'm Guildmaster, the guild might even try to seize the dungeon's core if things go wrong."

"I'd hate that."

Rei muttered.

Compared to the half-finished cores or those barely formed dungeon cores she'd destroyed before, this one remained intact, likely due to its long existence within the dungeon.

Considering its potential as an alchemy material, Rei's honest feeling was that if possible, she wanted to keep it for herself.

"So, Rei, you're going on a date with just me for now."

At the word "date," Elena and Vihera wore expressions that clearly showed they weren't amused.

But they knew full well it wasn't actually a date, so they didn't say anything more.

...Though they did look displeased when they saw the twin peaks shifting shape beneath the wide-open neckline of Marina's party dress—which she was wearing despite being in the dungeon—as she clung to Rei's arm.
