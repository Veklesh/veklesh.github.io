After Marina left, Elena promptly informed Ara that they would head to the dungeon as early as tomorrow.

Naturally, since they were going to the dungeon, she needed to explain the reason.

But... due to Elena's explanation to Ara, Rei watched and thought, "Is that really okay?" Yet Ara accepted the story about Grimm without question and immediately began preparing for tomorrow.

Ara hesitated a bit about what to do with the room at the Dusk Wheat inn, but since she'd heard that once inside the dungeon, they could reach the lowest level immediately—unlike before—she decided to leave it as it was.

While courtesy toward those who had kindly offered her the room was a factor, the bigger concern was whether she could even secure a room again upon her return given the current situation.

Anyway, Ara immediately began packing her belongings, visiting various shops to make numerous purchases.

Regarding luggage, since Rei had promised to use Misty Ring, there was no need to limit the amount.

...Though she had absolutely no intention of buying anything useless.

Once Ara returned, Rei stowed the newly purchased items into the Misty Ring one after another. Then, they enjoyed a pleasant meal in the dining hall for the first time in ages... And then, word came from Marina: they were to assemble at the main gate when the bell rang at six in the morning.

"Wow, it's pretty dark out."

"That's only natural. Winter is almost upon us. Ideally, I'd like to return to Gilm before the snow starts falling."

Rei stepped down from the carriage at Gilm's main gate and muttered discontentedly at the surrounding gloom.

Elena spoke to Rei, Set let out a cry, and Yellow followed suit.

Early in the morning... even if it wasn't fully light yet, such behavior naturally drew attention.

The gazes of merchants and adventurers, who were ready to leave Gilm the moment the main gate opened, gathered on them, but Rei and the others showed no sign of caring.

Both Rei and Elena were accustomed to drawing attention.

Precisely because of that, they showed no sign of being bothered by the stares directed at them. Though...

(Is that idiot still following us?)

For just an instant, Rei glanced toward a nearby building... or more precisely, toward the figure lurking in its shadows, the one directing hostility toward him. But it was only for an instant.

After all, they were about to leave Gilm. Even if someone was plotting something, there was nothing they could do about it now.

"Even if you say it's dim, it was already dark when we left the inn, wasn't it? It's not like it's that far from the inn to here. If it was dim then, it's only natural it's still dim now."

Ara, stepping down from the driver's seat, murmured with a hint of exasperation.

Since her words were undeniably true, Rei couldn't argue back any further.

"Do you think Elk and the others will come?"

Unable to argue back, he changed the subject instead.

The one who answered that question wasn't Elena, nor was it Ara... and of course, it wasn't Set or Yellow either.

"Of course they're coming."

Turning his gaze toward the source of the voice, he saw a carriage approaching.

The horses pulling it were inferior to those pulling Elena's carriage, but they still possessed the size and bearing worthy of being called first-rate steeds.

The carriage itself, while not quite on par with Elena's magic item carriage, was clearly a costly piece of craftsmanship. ...Though, for an A-rank adventurer, it made sense that the emphasis of that cost was on sturdiness.

The man seated on the coachman's seat wore his usual innocent smile, as if a mischievous child had simply grown up, and turned his gaze toward Rei and the others.

「Elk」

"Hey, we'll be relying on you this time."

Elk replied to Rei's words.

Elk's demeanor didn't seem to suggest he was troubled by anything concerning Rodos, not in the slightest.

That seemed to surprise Rei as well.

From beneath his hood, he shot Elk a slightly surprised glance.

But telling Elk not to be cheerful was impossible.

Despite their relentless efforts to restore Rhodes' consciousness since he lost it, they had achieved absolutely nothing.

Rhodos's body was gradually weakening from the effects of his prolonged sleep.

While a magic item obtained through connections provided some relief, Rhodes' body had grown so thin, so frail... no, so small, it was hard to believe he had once been a Rank C adventurer.

For Elk and Min, who had to watch this happen, Marina's proposal was something they couldn't pass up.

...Had this proposal come from someone untrustworthy, not Marina, Elk and Min would have been far more wary.

Elk was a Rank A adventurer, one with a nickname.

Knowing that such an Elk was searching for a magic item to restore consciousness, it wouldn't be surprising if someone tried to swindle him out of money.

Of course, they knew that messing with Elk could lead to a painful lesson if they failed. Still, success meant money that would make honest work seem ridiculous.

Moreover, Elk had a relatively mild-mannered disposition for a Rank A adventurer, leading some men to contact him, reasoning that even if they failed, they wouldn't be killed.

In the end, that man met his fate: his limbs were shattered as a warning to others.

Still, it was probably Elk's mercy that it ended with just his bones crushed, rather than something like having his limbs severed.

"Still, I didn't expect you'd even prepare a carriage. I thought we'd travel together in Elena's carriage."

"I intended that at first too, but Min said that even if your carriage is a magic item, having two unconscious people lying down in it would be a hindrance."

"Of course. Sitting would be one thing, but two people sleeping take up a lot of space. Besides..."

Min, who had stepped down from the carriage, paused mid-sentence and shifted his gaze toward Elena's carriage.

"As a woman, having an unfamiliar man sleeping next to her while she's unconscious... well, it's not exactly pleasant."

Though describing it as "masculine" might be slightly misleading, the words coming from Min's gender-neutral speech caused Rei and several others listening nearby to look slightly perplexed.

But as women, many of them likely understood Min's words.

The women waiting for the main gate to open nodded silently at Min's words.

"No, when you're on a job, you sleep together normally, right?"

"You idiot! You're such an idiot! That's exactly why you'll never get a boyfriend!"

"Ow! What the hell, hitting me out of nowhere!"

Hearing that exchange from a little distance away, Rei kept her mouth shut, thinking that if she said something careless, she'd probably get into all sorts of trouble too.

Elk, perhaps already cautioned by Min, quietly averted his gaze from the noisy group.

He'd probably been through something similar around here before.

As a result, a somewhat awkward atmosphere hung in the air... and then, as if to address it, a figure appeared.

"Oh, Elk and the others prepared a carriage too?"

"Guildmaster... I appreciate you inviting us along this time."

"Just call me Marina. We'll be traveling together for a while starting today. Calling me 'Guildmaster' every time would be a bit of a hassle, wouldn't it? Besides, it draws too much attention."

"I see. Then I'll take you up on that. ...Though, given Marina's appearance, I can't help but think she'll stand out no matter what."

At Min's words, everyone listening to the explanation nodded in agreement.

There was no doubt that Rei and his group stood out, no matter how you looked at it.

Both carriages were far from ordinary merchant vehicles, and the horses pulling them were incomparable to those of other carriages.

The horses pulling Elena's carriage were accustomed to Set, and even the horses pulling Elk's carriage, despite meeting Set for the first time, showed no signs of agitation.

...Despite the fact that most horses pulling other carriages were terrified and cowering in fear of Set.

"Well, there's no helping how conspicuous we are. I suppose we'll just have to resign ourselves to it."

Ara, seated on the driver's platform of Elena's carriage, murmured with a wry smile.

No one could argue with those words, and an uneasy atmosphere settled over them.

Amidst this... the timing was perfect, one might say, as the bell tolled and the main gate opened.

"Well, shall we go?"

At Marina's muttered words, everyone nodded and climbed into the carriage.

Those waiting before the main gate yielded their place in turn... and without much delay, Rei and the others stepped outside Gilm.

Several hours after leaving Gilm. Autumn had deepened, yet the sun still shone brightly in the sky, illuminating the earth below.

Though, considering the few rain clouds, it certainly couldn't be called a clear autumn day.

Still, traveling by carriage was considerably easier than walking.

Especially since Elena's carriage was a magic item; it didn't matter if it was a bit cold or hot outside.

Elk's carriage, being a regular non-magical one, wasn't quite as comfortable as Elena's, but it was still a high-end model and handled such conditions well.

While the carriage prioritized defense, eliminating elements that would make the ride uncomfortable was only natural.

Currently, two carriages were moving forward, with Set walking beside them. Above Set's head, Yellow chirped happily. This peculiar group—yet one that could be considered overwhelmingly powerful from an outsider's perspective—advanced toward the dungeon.

"Huh... Compared to before, this road's become quite solid, hasn't it?"

Rei murmured, gazing out the window.

Where the dungeon lay, there was naturally no proper highway, only a hard-packed path.

Though much time had passed since Rei last traveled this way, the path still carried the feel of that era.

"I suppose so. Since that dungeon over there hasn't been conquered yet, there are lots of merchants buying materials and magic stones, and others doing business with adventurers. People like that walk this path every day. ...See?"

Rei pointed as she peered out the window.

Looking in the direction she pointed, Rei spotted five adventurer-like figures walking toward Gilm.

They seemed reasonably skilled adventurers, chatting but keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

They also kept an eye on the two approaching carriages, checking for anything unusual.

...Unfortunately for these adventurers, Yellow and Set were walking behind the carriages, chattering away as usual, their voices sounding like "Guruguru, Kyuukyuu," and had somehow hidden their figures behind the carriages without being noticed.

It was a luxurious carriage, drawn by fine horses. As they were observing the carriage, Set suddenly emerged from behind it.

These adventurers, active in the dungeon, were confident in their skills.

Still, it was understandable that they froze, unable to comprehend the situation when Set suddenly materialized before them.

Normally, when walking along the roadside, you wouldn't expect a griffin to suddenly appear from behind a carriage moving straight ahead.

"Huh? Hey... what?"

"Yeah... that's right."

The adventurers chatted like this, but the carriage and griffin showed no sign of noticing them at all, continuing on their way until they passed each other.

Seeing the adventurers frozen in place, Elk and Ara on the driver's platform looked like they wanted to say something, but they must have decided it was pointless. In the end, they passed by in silence.

"Hey, do you think it's okay to just leave it like that?"

"Hard to say. But if they're from Gilm, I doubt they'd be surprised to see Set."

After passing the adventurers, the two carriages resumed their journey side by side.

On the driver's platform of one carriage, Elk and Ara exchanged those words.

In truth, most of those active in the dungeon they were heading to had some connection to Gilm, big or small.

To be precise, the usual routine is to stop by Gilm first, prepare there, and then head to the dungeon...

That's precisely why they couldn't understand why he seemed surprised to see them.

But they quickly decided it didn't matter and urged their carriage forward toward the dungeon once more.

Though not as fast as Elena's horse, they had prepared well-trained, expensive horses precisely to reach the dungeon as quickly as possible.

To restore Rodos's consciousness at any cost.

The reins gripped by Elk made a sharp, grinding sound... and with their resolve clear, the two carriages hurried along the road toward the dungeon, alongside Set and Yellow.
