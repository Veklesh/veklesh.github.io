Rei was making his way through the night forest, but naturally, a massive horde of monsters attacked him.

Tree branches, vines, thorns, grass, spines, fruit, pollen... Various attacks assailed Rei, but he ignored most of them and pressed forward.

He could defeat the attacking monsters, but without magic stones, there was little benefit to doing so.

...Though, to be fair, the materials could be used just like those from ordinary monsters, so it wasn't entirely pointless.

Anyway, unless he dealt with whatever was likely at the center of Treant Forest as quickly as possible, this assault wouldn't end.

(If, like that researcher said, it's not at the center but somewhere on the edge of the forest, finding it might be a bit tricky. But anyway, meeting up with Set is the top priority.)

Rather than running through the forest like this, riding Set and heading directly to the center from above would be vastly faster.

Plus, there'd be no worry about trees blocking our path.

"Out of the way!"

Swinging the Death Scythe, he sliced only the Treants blocking his path.

This was an action unimaginable from the usual Rei.

Under normal circumstances, she wouldn't hesitate to take down every monster in sight.

But there was no time now.

There was simply no time to deal with every monster appearing in this forest of Treants.

"Kuu, raaah!"

Using the twist of her body, she hurls the Twilight Spear, infused with magic.

The Twilight Spear flew straight ahead, piercing, shattering, and destroying the bodies of various monsters, starting with the Treant that tried to block its path.

It shattered the trunks of Treants, tore apart the writhing vines like snakes, and pierced the stems of Venus flytraps.

In this way, it killed dozens upon dozens of monsters at once. ...Well, perhaps "destroyed" is a more accurate term in this case.

Eventually, after destroying countless monsters and slowing its flight through the air, the Twilight Spear returned to Rei's hand in the next instant.

Confirming the path ahead was clear, it shot straight through the opening.

(These monsters really are... strange, aren't they?)

Running along the path forcibly created by the Twilight Spear, Rei murmured inwardly.

Under normal circumstances, seeing their comrades killed so easily should have allowed them to imagine what kind of beings their enemies were.

At the very least, some individuals would hesitate to attack.

But... the monsters of Treant Forest attacked Rei one after another, as if such things didn't matter at all.

Having fought a fair number of monsters before, Rei found something off about these creatures.

"Anyway, it's true they're getting in my way... but!"

Treant blocked the path created by the Twilight Spear, refusing to let Rei pass.

Among the monsters Rei had fought in Trent's Forest thus far, Treant possessed the greatest weight and the largest numbers.

In terms of sheer size, the giant flowers that still occasionally dropped seeds were larger, but in numbers, they couldn't match the Treants.

(Ah, and whether they can move is another matter too.)

Unlike the Treants, who could move freely using their roots like legs, the giant flowers showed no sign of movement as far as Rei could tell.

Then again, being shaped like a sunflower with an enormous bloom, it's probably true that moving would be difficult even if it wanted to.

Lightly leaping over vines reaching for his legs, he pushed relentlessly deeper into the forest.

Gradually, the number of monsters attacking Rei increased.

Rei didn't know who was commanding the monsters, but to whoever was in charge, his actions were undoubtedly nothing but a nuisance.

(No, the one commanding them is probably that entity in the center of the forest, right?)

Even while moving and swinging his weapon, Rei had the mental space to ponder such things precisely because he'd survived countless hellish battles before.

An absolute conviction that whatever came next, he could handle it with his abilities, and unwavering trust in the weapon in his hand.

And above all else...

"GRRRRRRRRRR!"

As that roar echoed around them, ice arrows flew forth, piercing the monsters.

「Set！」

"Grrrrrrrrr!"

At Rei's call, Set growled happily.

...Though, even as he growled, he shook his front legs, shattering the Treant trunk.

"Oh dear, haven't you noticed me?"

Vihera asked Rei with a smile from atop Set's back as they ran side by side.

"Of course not. ...More importantly, how did you even find me?"

"Thanks to Set. He was moving toward that flower, then suddenly changed direction. Though, right after that, I heard an incredible crash, so I had a pretty good idea."

Though Rei had some reservations about being linked to that sound of destruction, considering where he was running now, he couldn't really complain.

"Set, there are monsters around... Could you use the King's Intimidation?"

Rei said to Set beside her, as if to change the subject.

But it wasn't just to cover up the conversation.

In truth, using Set's Royal Intimidation would halt the movements of low-rank monsters.

In a situation like this, being attacked by countless monsters, it was undeniably an extremely effective tactic.

"Gururuu, gururururururururururuuu!"

Set gave a short acknowledgment to Rei's words, then the next instant, he roared loudly, as if demanding his voice echo throughout the entire area.

But... the very next moment, as if Set's roar hadn't been heard at all, a branch from the Treant's tree trunk swung down toward Rei.

"Dammit! What the hell is going on!?"

Of course, Rei didn't expect the King's Intimidation to work on every monster.

He understood it wouldn't work on powerful monsters... not quite up to the level of a Silver Lion, but still monsters of a fairly high rank.

But even so, seeing it fail against a monster like the Treant made Rei's eyes widen in surprise.

If only a few monsters, starting with the Treant, had been able to move, he might have accepted that they were simply powerful monsters among the Treant.

But the fact that every single monster within sight could move despite being hit by Set's Royal Intimidation?

This was simply impossible to attribute to coincidence or raw strength.

It had to be something else... something with a clear, definite reason behind it.

(Surely, these monsters aren't all on par with Silver Lions... No, that can't be.)

Only Rei, who had fought them all before, could instantly judge that they simply didn't possess that level of strength.

But then, the question arose: why was the King's Intimidation having no effect?

They weren't quite goblin-level... but judging by Gilm's standards, they were definitely low-rank monsters... or perhaps slightly above that.

Based on past experience, the King's Intimidation had always worked on such monsters.

Some monsters could still move under the King's Intimidation, but their speed was undeniably slowed considerably.

So why not now?

Even as he pondered this, it became clear that the King's Intimidation was indeed ineffective.

Given that, there was no point dwelling on a skill that was now useless.

(It'd be wise to keep in mind that there might be other skills besides the King's Intimidation that are ineffective.)

Was it only the King's Intimidation that was ineffective, or were there others as well?

As someone who primarily uses Death Scythe's skills in combat, it was something that concerned Rei.

Given that Ice Arrow worked, it couldn't be that all of Set's skills were useless.

(Even with the same skill, Vihera's Demon-Infused Palm was used normally in combat. Thinking about it that way... Damn, this part's going to be a trial by fire.)

As Rei pondered this, a giant Venus flytrap suddenly sprouted right before her eyes.

It didn't move like a Treant; it literally sprouted from the ground.

Within seconds, the Venus flytrap grew taller than Rei himself. It opened its fanged mouth, preparing to strike at Rei and the others...

"Flying Slash!"

Rei swung his Death Scythe, cleanly severing the stem. The plant fell to the ground.

(Does Flying Slash work? If so, then for now...)

Though it was only one, having confirmed the skill's effect, Rei called out to Set running beside him... and to Vihera riding on Set's back.

"Researchers said something... probably what makes this Treant Forest the Treant Forest... might be at the center of this forest. But. It seems it might be on the opposite side from where we entered."

"...That's pretty unreliable, isn't it!"

With claws extending from her gauntlets, she swiftly sliced through the thorns reaching out from a nearby tree.

"Anyway, that's why I want to ride Set and search the center of this forest from the air. What about you, Vihera? You can come along or stay here, I guess."

"Of course I'm coming too."

Without the slightest hesitation, Vihera declared she would go too.

Seeing Vihera like that, Rei also wore a look of understanding.

What lay at the center of this forest... or perhaps its edge, was unknown.

But even so, combat was almost certain. Given that, there was no way Vihera, who loved fighting, would choose not to act alongside Rei.

"Then, I'll have you hold onto my leg for the journey?"

"Yes."

Exchanging brief words, Vihera leaped down from Set's back.

Even as she leapt from Set's back, her feet didn't tangle or lag behind.

This was a physical maneuver typical of Vihera, who relished combat.

And yet, even while dismounting, she sliced through monsters targeting her with claws from her gauntlets—that too was distinctly Vihera.

"Don't worry about the surrounding enemies! Right now, the priority is to get on Set and survey the forest from above!"

"Yeah, I know!"

Vihera responded matter-of-factly to Rei, who shouted back at her while riding on the back of the Set she had dismounted.

After all, a battle against what seemed like a powerful enemy awaited them later. Wasting stamina here would be foolish.

She knew that, yet she couldn't suppress the surge of exhilaration and excitement welling up inside her.

Under normal circumstances, Vihera wouldn't be this worked up.

But this time, the anticipation of facing an opponent like Treant's Forest—something she'd never seen or heard of before—inevitably stirred Vihera's excitement.

"...Even so, how can you tell your surroundings in here?"

While swinging Death Scythe and Twilight Spear atop Set's back, defeating monsters that appeared one after another, Rei murmured thoughtfully.

The moonlight was almost entirely blocked by the branches, making the forest interior nearly indistinguishable.

Still, it wasn't completely indistinguishable. That was likely because the Twilight Spear had just fired earlier and cleared away some trees... or more accurately, some Treants.

But even so, while someone with special eyes like Rei's might manage, it puzzled Rei that Vihera could distinguish them with her own eyes.

Responding to the question that escaped Rei's lips, Vihera opened her mouth, her cheeks flushed from the excitement of battle.

"Ever since the Ambris incident, I've gained night vision!"

"...Come to think of it, that's right."

Rei recalled that through the fusion... no, the absorption of Ambris, Vihera could no longer be considered an ordinary human.

That said, the person who was no longer human didn't regret it at all... quite the opposite, she was delighted at the prospect of living alongside Rei for an eternity.

(Then again, she used to run through the night forest with Set, so night vision is hardly a new thing.)

Watching Set attack a monster—a rare or higher-tier species of Treant that fired walnut-like nuts with Wind Arrows—Rei spoke up.

"Anyway, let's head to the sky. ...Set!"

"GRRRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set flapped his wings, and as his body rose into the air, Vihera timed it perfectly and grabbed onto his leg.

And so, Set, Rei, and Vihera—two humans and one beast—dashed upward into the sky.
