When Rei used her scouting magic to spread flames across the surrounding area... an immediate reaction came back.

The reaction came from a position about twenty meters behind him.

Naturally, neither Vicente nor Set were there, and the old man vanished the instant he touched the flames.

At that point, it wasn't difficult to figure out who had been caught by his scouting flames.

He immediately swung his Death Scythe toward the source of the reaction behind him.

"Flying Slash!"

With that word, a flying slash was unleashed.

...But the power of that slash was clearly inferior to what Rei usually wielded.

(Damn it, is this also the doing of that so-called Sanctuary!)

Suppressing his bitter feelings, he activated his scouting magic and kicked off the ground, heading toward where the Flying Slash had gone.

Leaving Vicente behind, who hated him—no, the word "hate" didn't even begin to cover it—was somewhat worrisome. Yet, he also felt reassured, knowing he could leave it to Set.

Indeed, just before turning to head behind him, Rei caught sight of Vicente and Set. He saw Set struggling against the effects of the Sanctuary, yet holding his own against Vicente.

"Magic Shield!"

Even if the opponent was an old man, he was a member of Stigma.

And he possessed the troublesome ability to freely manipulate extremely intricate illusions.

Since it was impossible to know what other abilities he possessed, it was only natural to prepare for any eventuality.

As far as Rei knew, Magic Shield—capable of blocking any powerful attack—was an extremely useful skill when the enemy's hand was unknown.

But even such a useful skill had its problems.

Being a shield of light, it was highly conspicuous at night.

...Then again, since he was deploying the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the light from the Magic Shield probably didn't make much difference in terms of being conspicuous.

(My movements are still sluggish!)

Even though he was moving while using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the scenery flowing past Rei was clearly slower compared to before the Sanctuary was deployed.

Of course, even then, it was undoubtedly fast enough that ordinary adventurers would struggle to track a moving shadow.

But for the person moving, the difference felt immense.

The same was true for the old man who had noticed Rei heading straight towards him.

"Heh heh. I'm impressed by your skill in finding me... but do you intend to challenge me in that state?"

Thump.

He lightly kicked off the ground, and in the next instant, far from fleeing from Rei, the old man was closing the distance, moving toward Rei himself.

In his hand was the same old staff.

At first glance, it looked like nothing more than a cane an old man might use, but since it was wielded by a member of the Stigma, it couldn't possibly be just a cane.

True to expectation, the old man raised the staff, pointing its tip toward Rei.

The tip was sharply pointed, resembling an estoc in its emphasis on piercing power.

Furthermore, light gathered at the tip, glowing faintly white.

It was only because it was night that he could notice the presence of the white light; if this were under the sun, it was doubtful he would have noticed it at all.

(What to do? If it's another one of those mysterious abilities like that Sanctuary guy... No, never mind. Since I've left Set alone, I can't afford to waste time here. Finish him off in one go!)

It's not that I don't trust Set.

But having just entered this Sanctuary, Set would inevitably be confused at times.

If Vicente exploited that opening, even Set could be in danger.

Considering that, Rei needed to take down the old man in front of him as quickly as possible and head to Set's aid.

At the same time, he had to keep his exploration magic active to pinpoint the old man's location.

Activating the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour within this sanctuary while simultaneously using magic was consuming an amount of mana that would be unbearable for an ordinary mage.

Compared to using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour or magic normally, Rei's mana was draining at a rate that could be described as squared.

He couldn't deactivate the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour while facing Vicente, but once he defeated the old man before him, he could deactivate the magic. Therefore, he needed to defeat him as quickly as possible.

Moreover, the old man did not seem intent on settling the fight with Rei immediately; he appeared to harbor a desire to prolong the battle as much as possible.

He wasn't certain he could defeat Rei alone and preferred to wait for Vicente to arrive as reinforcements.

(Truthfully, it's unbelievable that we could even fight against a Stigma like you within this sanctuary... but you truly live up to your nickname, Crimson.)

It took less than three seconds for them to collide.

Even so, their minds had that much time to process. Soon, Rei's Death Scythe and the old man's staff collided.

"Power Slash!"

Rei struck first.

Rather than slicing through his opponent, it was a blow more suited to being described as smashing him to pieces, unleashed toward the old man.

He understood that the person facing him appeared to be an old man.

But as long as he was known as a Stigma, Ray couldn't possibly think of him as just an old man.

Even so, considering his physique was nearly the same size as Rei's, it was hard to believe his raw physical ability was on par with Vicente's.

Of course, even in that state, being a member of the Stigma meant he likely possessed something else that stood out beyond physical ability.

Therefore, the plan was to prevent him from using that special ability and overwhelm him outright with superior physical prowess.

That was the intent behind the Power Slash he unleashed.

"Whoa!"

A powerful strike, so fierce it felt like it might shatter his own body... yet delivered at blinding speed.

What appeared on the old man's face upon seeing it was a smile.

Though Rei questioned this, he needed to defeat the old man before him as quickly as possible, especially since he was currently maintaining the flames by casting a scouting spell.

With that thought, the Death Scythe swung straight toward the old man... yet for some reason, the old man neither blocked nor dodged the attack, accepting it directly onto his body.

Why? Rei wondered, knowing his location should be revealed by the flames.

Rei briefly wondered if the old man had resigned himself to his fate, seeing no escape, but immediately dismissed the thought.

No matter how approachable the old man seemed, he was a member of the Stigma... a member of the Holy Light Church.

Given that, there was no way he would meekly accept the attack of someone he called his mortal enemy.

Indeed, the blade of Rei's Power Slash sliced through the old man's body the instant it made contact, encountering no resistance whatsoever. The very next moment, the old man who should have been standing there vanished.

"Huh!?"

The instant he vanished, Rei realized he'd been tricked by the old man's illusion once more. Instinctively, he shifted his awareness to the search flames.

There. The old man's figure was undoubtedly right before his eyes.

His confusion at this phantom-like yet not-quite-phantom appearance lasted only an instant. Instinctively understanding that magic would never betray him, Rei manipulated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, whipping it forward to strike the figure before him.

"Gyah!"

The scream came from the exact spot where the old man had been moments before. ...Only, it was now much closer to the ground.

He created an illusion of himself standing, while the real him crouched, waiting for an opening to strike Rei.

That must have been the plan, but in many ways, Rei's existence was an extraordinary anomaly even for the old man.

It wasn't magic, nor the blade of the Death Scythe... and certainly not an attack like Rei's fists. The very red magical energy surrounding Rei as the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour itself launched the assault. This was completely unexpected, even for the old man.

Taking the full brunt of the attack, the old man, who was about the same size as Rei, was blown away.

The effect of the Sacred Ground had diminished the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour. Furthermore, the old man, being a Stigma, wore armor similar to Vicente's.

Even so, indirect heat might be manageable, but there was no way he could survive a direct hit from the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour itself unscathed.

The agonized scream that escaped the old man's mouth as he was blown away spoke volumes.

Normally, as a member of Stigma, the old man could have laughed off some pain.

But Rei's sudden strike with the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour exceeded the old man's limits.

Rei’s magic power, compressed and concentrated into the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, possessed that kind of destructive force.

"Guh... y-you... you're doing it. I never imagined even the two of us, me and Vicente, would struggle this much..."

The old man opened his mouth, but Rei ignored his words and swung the Death Scythe again.

It was obvious the old man was trying to buy time.

There was no time to indulge such tactics. He closed the distance, charging straight at the retreating old man.

"Flying Slash!"

He fired a warning strike to prevent the old man from moving carelessly.

It was a skill that had been nullified countless times by the old man before, but this time was different.

To prevent him from crouching and dodging like before, I launched a vertical slash.

If he moved left or right, the flames spreading across the ground would reveal his position to Rei. Yet, retreating backward or advancing forward offered no escape from the Flying Slash closing in straight ahead.

The slightest movement from his current position would be detected by Rei instantly.

Given the situation, there was only one option the old man could take to prevent Rei from detecting his movement.

"Up, right?"

With those words, Rei's speed increased as he advanced toward the old man's position.

A faint sound, like air being sliced, reached Rei's ears.

The moment the Flying Slash made contact, the old man vanished as before.

Even so, Rei believed the old man had leaped to evade the Flying Slash and pressed forward... The next instant, the Magic Shield Rei had deployed transformed into particles of light and vanished.

Why this happened was easy to imagine.

The wind-cutting sound heard just an instant before. The leaping old man must have launched some kind of attack against Rei, who was closing in on him.

But this was a Magic Shield capable of nullifying any attack, even if only once. A mere feint couldn't possibly affect it.

True, being an absolute defense limited to a single use, repeated attacks might have been another matter. Yet, despite being within the sanctuary, Rei's approach was incredibly swift, aided by the effects of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour. There was no time for the old man to launch a second attack.

"Flying Slash!"

The Flying Slash was unleashed once more.

The flying slash shot straight into the air...

"Guh!"

The moment it struck the empty space, a cry of agony echoed throughout the area.

Then, in the next instant, an old man appeared, blown through the air as if seeping out.

Despite taking the Flying Slash head-on, his torso remained intact—likely thanks to his armor.

Crafted using light ore, that armor possessed high defensive capabilities.

...Yet, as Rei pursued the flying old man, he could see the armor was dented.

The old man wasn't just waiting silently as Rei charged forward, channeling magic into his Death Scythe.

He managed to create an illusion, causing Rei to lose sight of him, and pierced Rei's body with the tip of his staff.

That was his plan... but at this very moment, Rei's running speed increased even further than before.

Catching up to the flying old man... no, closing in with such force it seemed he might overtake him, Rei sensed the old man's last desperate struggle and accelerated even further.

The old man's eyes widened in disbelief at a speed that seemed impossible within the sanctuary...

Slash!

With that sound, the Death Scythe, channeled with Rei's magic, was swung down with the enhanced physical prowess granted by the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour. It sliced the old man's body clean through, cutting him in two.

The single blow, entering through the shoulder and exiting through the side, easily took the old man's life. His body fell to the ground, scattering entrails, flesh, blood, and bone fragments as it rolled over ten meters.

Afterwards, the heightened temperature caused by Rei's Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour spread a strong stench of blood and entrails throughout the area.

But Rei ignored it, turning his gaze toward Set.

There, Vicente's movements were momentarily halted by Set's Crystal Breath.

Seeing Vicente's consciousness completely focused on Set, Rei, still clad in the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, kicked off the ground with all his might. He struck Vicente's torso, which had been halted once more by the Shock Magic Eye, with a single flash of his Death Scythe, infused with magic power, severing the torso cleanly.
