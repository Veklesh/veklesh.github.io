"Don't you think it's about time we headed home?"

To Rei’s surprise, it was Bistol who spoke up first.

There was no doubt that staying here as long as possible was the best way to get as many dried fish as possible.

And yet, for Bistol to be the first to suggest going home...

That said, there was no doubt that Rei was also a little concerned about Gilm’s condition.

Every night, Elena used a Paired Orb to talk with Ara, but she also had the chance to speak with Daskar from time to time.

That was because she had been asked, in a roundabout way, several times when he planned to return.

To be honest, Rei felt it was about time to head back, since they’d obtained a massive amount of seafood, and Set had been able to enhance several skills using the monsters’ magic stones.

……After defeating the jellyfish monster, he managed to take down a few more monsters, but unfortunately, Death Scythe wasn’t able to learn any new skills from them.

(If I stayed in the sea a little longer, I might be able to defeat other monsters… but if I did that, honestly, it doesn’t look like I’ll ever be able to return to Gilm.)

The ocean is vast, and it’s home to all kinds of monsters.

However, the ocean is a field that gives the monsters a significant advantage, making them extremely troublesome for Rei and Set to defeat.

To be precise, the biggest hassle is having to defeat them while leaving behind corpses that hold magic stones.

Also, if they sense even the slightest danger, they immediately dive deep into the ocean.

Once that happens, Rei and Set’s attacks basically can’t reach them.

Because of this, Rei and Set have let the enemy escape several times.

Being protected by the natural barrier of the ocean gives them an overwhelming advantage when attacking from the air, as we are doing now.

“So, what are we going to do? Should we head back soon?”

It was Vihera who asked Rei.

Her tone implied that if Rei wanted to stay, he could, and if he wanted to go home, that was fine too.

In fact, from Vihera’s perspective, it probably didn’t matter either way.

As for food, being by the sea meant they could eat as much seafood as they wanted.

Normally, one might lament not being able to eat meat, but in Rei’s case, the Misty Ring contains a massive supply of meat, so he has no trouble getting any.

In other words, as far as food goes, there’s no particular reason for him to have trouble staying here.

And as for Vihera’s fourth desire—the desire to fight, following hunger, sleep, and sex—here, she could engage in combat training with Rei and the others at any time.

Since these are merely mock battles and not real combat, they can’t fully satisfy that urge, but it’s still much better than not being able to fight at all.

“That’s true. Considering we’ve been here for quite a while, I suppose it’s about time we headed back. Ara’s been working really hard for us in Gilm, but she might be stretched too thin.”

Elena agreed that they should return to Gilm.

In reality, she had come to Gilm with the intention of spending time with Rei, but that didn’t mean she could neglect her official purpose—monitoring whether the Noble Faction would interfere with the expansion of Gilm.

The fact that Ara is Elena’s close aide and a very close confidante is something that—well, not just among the Noble Faction… no, even those outside the Faction who are somewhat well-informed would know.

With Ara around, if anyone were to blatantly try to interfere with the expansion work, it would lead to the worst possible outcome.

Anyone of a certain standing wouldn’t do something so foolish.

...Though, to be fair, there are some who, despite having no basis for it, believe that “it’ll be fine as long as I’m the one doing it” and go ahead and do something foolish.

And, much to Elena’s frustration, a significant number of nobles belonging to the Noble Faction are guilty of such behavior.

To keep such people in check, Elena’s presence is, after all, more effective than Ara’s.

(One vote in favor of returning, one indifferent. If that’s the case…)

Rei turns his gaze toward Marina.

Marina, of course, knows exactly what that look means, so she gives a small nod before speaking.

“I’m in favor of going back. It’s nice to enjoy ourselves here, but autumn is coming soon. …Once that happens, it’ll get pretty rough staying around here.”

“Now that you mention it, you might be right. Actually, it’s been getting pretty cool in the mornings.”

It wasn’t exactly cold, but as Vihera said, there was no doubt that it had gotten quite cool in the mornings.

Moreover, Rei and the others are right by the sea, so naturally, the sea breeze is strong.

Even so, there are several trees growing near the hot springs, which serve as a windbreak… but even so, it’s still cool.

It’s cool enough that it wouldn’t be surprising if someone suggested heading back sooner if they didn’t have the Magic Tent.

“Marina agrees we should head back, too. Well then, I guess it’s about time we went. How about we enjoy the ocean one last time today and set off tomorrow morning?”

“Oh, that’s unusual. Knowing Rei-chan, I thought once she decided to go home, she’d want to leave right this minute.”

Rei shot Bistol a disgruntled look as he said this with an expression that suggested he was genuinely surprised—not joking or anything.

In the past, she used to be startled just by catching a glimpse of Bistoll, but after living together like this for several days, she’d naturally grown accustomed to him.

...Though, even Rei wasn’t sure how she’d react if Bistoll suddenly appeared right in front of her in the middle of the night, with nothing but moonlight to guide them.

“We’ll need to let the people in Galanka know, too. They’re probably already out fishing.”

To the villagers of Galanka, Rei—who bought up their entire catch of seafood—was truly a person worthy of being called a valued customer.

The amount of seafood Rei had purchased, including seaweed, amounted to a considerable sum.

Naturally, all the seafood he’d bought was stored inside the Misty Ring.

That being the case, if Rei were to just leave without saying a word under the current circumstances, the village would have to deal with all the seafood he’d caught in such large quantities today.

When I say “dispose of them in the village,” I don’t mean throwing them away—rather, the village would eat them or turn them into preserved food… and so on.

“I see. That makes sense. Rei really does think about others, doesn’t he?”

Bistoll said this in a joking tone, but deep down, she was actually quite surprised.

He’d heard all sorts of things about Rei, but the rumor that she’d swing her scythe without hesitation even against nobles… that one was quite widely known.

That’s probably partly because the rumors themselves are so sensational.

There aren’t that many people capable of defying the nobility.

People might ignore unreasonable orders or try to talk their way out of a situation, but actually using force is another matter entirely.

The fact that he does this without batting an eye is proof that Rei is an extraordinary figure, for better or for worse.

Of course, Bistol, who has been interacting with Rei for some time now, understands that Rei is not simply someone who lashes out without reason.

Still, the fact that he went out of his way to tell the villagers he was leaving was probably unexpected.

But for Rei, it was only natural that he would feel more affection for the people who had hunted food for him than for the nobles and other people in power.

There’s also the matter of needing to have a word or two with the pirates, aside from the villagers.

“When I go to Galanka this evening, I’ll let them know I’ll be back in Gilm tomorrow.”

“Got it. Well then, I’ll make you a really, really delicious dinner tonight!”

With a beaming smile, Bistol announced this.

To an uninformed observer, that smile was the kind that might well have made them scream.

Still, Rei and the others, who had spent a fair amount of time with Bistol, weren’t the least bit scared… rather, they wore happy expressions.

Since they’d started camping here—though calling it “camping” felt like a luxury—they’d come to realize from the bottom of their hearts that Bistol’s cooking skills were superior to those of any professional chef in the area.

Dishes like “Decadent Seafood Soup” and “Ecstatic Seafood Stir-Fry” had undoubtedly captured Rei and the others’ hearts.

To be honest, Bistrol’s cooking was so delicious that Rei found himself wishing Bistrol would join the party as their official cook.

Of course, everyone—including Rei—knew that was impossible.

Although Bistoll is currently traveling with Rei and the others, his true profession is, after all, that of a merchant.

Given that, once this “vacation”—which was really just a trip to stock up on goods—was over, Bistol would naturally return to his business.

It was precisely because he knew this that Rei didn’t even try to invite Bistoll to join the party.

(Well, if Bistol were here, I suppose all sorts of new problems would pop up, though.)

Rei and the others have already gotten used to it, but when it comes to first impressions, it’s difficult for Bistrol to make a good impression on others.

There are all sorts of eccentric people in the world, so it’s not as if he’s guaranteed to leave a bad impression on everyone… but statistically speaking, there’s no doubt that more people would end up with a negative impression of him.

Because of that, there was a chance they might get hassled by other adventurers or nobles, but conversely, there was also a chance that having Bistol around would prevent them from being underestimated.

(Well, it’s not that we won’t be looked down on—it’s just that fewer people will bother us because they simply don’t want to get involved with a guy like Bistol.)

Is that a good thing or a bad thing?

Rei found it hard to judge, but there was no doubt he felt a slight sense of loneliness at the thought of Bistol leaving.

“Ah, I’m looking forward to the food. Well, this is our last day. Let’s make the most of it.”

At Rei’s words, everyone replied.

“Grrrrr!”

As they flew over the sea as usual, Set suddenly growled.

Set had spotted all sorts of things before, but this time was different.

It was a growl that clearly surprised even Set himself.

Naturally, Rei reacted to Set’s behavior… Since he didn’t know if the target was a monster or a fish, he grabbed the harpoon he’d taken out of his Misty Ring.

That harpoon, equipped with a wire, is devastatingly effective against fish when combined with Rei’s strength and Set’s agility.

This should be fine.

Thinking that, Rei looked out into the sea… and immediately understood what Set had spotted.

That’s because something so large it was visible even from the air was swimming in the water.

(Wait, what is it…? A shrimp, right?)

Since the creature was swimming in relatively shallow waters, its form was clearly visible even from the air.

And from what he could see, it looked like nothing other than a shrimp.

If it were an ordinary shrimp, it would be difficult to spot from the air even if it were swimming in the ocean.

And yet, the reason I was able to clearly identify it as a shrimp was... because that shrimp was enormous.

Yes, the shrimp currently swimming in the sea is clearly larger than Set.

In fact, it was so large that I couldn’t help but think it might be more than twice Set’s size.

(Since Set is about three meters long... six meters long!? What kind of shrimp is that?)

For a moment, I wondered if it might be some kind of new monster species, but judging by Set’s reaction, it was clearly just a reaction to a seafood creature, not a monster.

In other words, this shrimp currently swimming in the ocean is really just a shrimp.

...Though it’s debatable whether a shrimp about six meters long can really be described as “just a shrimp.”

(Come to think of it, can shrimp even swim straight like this? From what I’ve seen on TV, I thought they swam by kind of jumping backward.)

Of course, Rei knows very little about shrimp.

All he’s ever seen is on variety shows where celebrities dive into the ocean to catch shrimp.

But even so, a shrimp is a shrimp, and the fact that there’s a shrimp that swims straight like a fish was completely unexpected, even for Rei.

At the same time, though, this turn of events was incredibly lucky for Rei.

Because shrimp was Rei’s absolute favorite food.

In fact, he preferred shrimp over crab… and not raw like sashimi, but cooked—whether boiled or deep-fried—he loved the firm, springy texture of cooked shrimp.

He figured a shrimp this big would be plenty to eat… and, hoping it wouldn’t be bland but actually tasty, he hurled his harpoon.
