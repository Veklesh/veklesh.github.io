What on earth is Rei talking about?

That was the honest feeling of those who knew it wasn't just Augusto and Daldora... but also Rei, Zarust, and Narsus who had gone to raid the assassins' hideout.

How on earth could storming the assassins' hideout possibly lead to the absurd claim that they'd discovered the village?

They had been waiting impatiently for the report that the assassins' hideout targeting them had been destroyed, making that feeling all the stronger.

Yet, none of them—Augusto, Daldora, or the others—shouted angrily without warning. It was precisely because they understood Rei possessed strength incomparable to any ordinary adventurer.

They exchanged glances, and eventually, Augusto—who had the closest relationship with Rei among them—spoke up.

In terms of closeness to Rei, Augusto's wife, Kathy, also thought of Rei as her own child.

But in this case, she seemed to judge that Augusto was the more appropriate one to hear the story. Without saying anything in particular, she remained silent and left it to her husband.

"So, Rei. I believe you went outside Gosh to raid the assassin's hideout. How exactly did you end up discovering the village?"

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, the fact that he had gone outside the town had been relayed to the guard who had been entrusted with the message.

Seeing Augusto genuinely puzzled, Rei seemed to realize he'd jumped to conclusions and began to organize his thoughts.

"Right. Sorry about that. I rushed things a bit. ...First, I should probably say this. The assassin targeting Augusto and Daldora... that guy called the Hundred-Faced One. We destroyed his hideout—no, his base."

'The Hundred-Faced One!?'

At Rei's words, not only Augusto and Daldora, but even the guards cried out in astonishment.

Except for Kathy, who looked completely lost as to what Rei was talking about.

"Looks like you knew the name 'Hundred-Faced One'. ...Well, it's only natural since Zarust and Narsus knew it too."

"Of course we did!? The Hundred-Faced One is the name of an assassin group that could even be called legendary. ...Though, well, exactly what kind of foe they were remained completely unknown until now."

Daldora declared with what could almost be called reflexive speed.

But Cathy merely tilted her head at Daldora's words.

Even if they were famous, that fame was limited to those connected to the underworld. Augusto, who loved Kathy, probably never taught her about them.

"But... I see. So the 'Hundred-Faced One' comes from that skin thing. I was sure it came from somewhere else entirely."

Watching Daldora mutter in apparent understanding, Rei continued his explanation.

"Anyway. I think he knew we headed outside Gosh, but when Set finally arrived at that place, there was nothing there..."

From there, Rei began explaining what he had experienced.

The artifact barrier, the village hidden within it, the Hundred-Faced One's stronghold, the battle against the Resonances leading it, victory... And then, upon searching the village, discovering that the Hundred-Faced Ones, seemingly incapable of further combat, had chosen death by drinking poison.

The group listened to Rei's account, their moods shifting with each revelation. But upon hearing that every villager had taken their own lives, several faces grew somber.

"But... why would they choose to die like that? Of course, being an assassin clan, they would undoubtedly be held accountable for their crimes. Even so, there must have been a chance they could have survived."

"...Well now."

A drug made from the Tira tree that numbs pain.

Rei had no intention of saying anything about that.

Nor did he intend to stop Narsus from reporting on that matter.

After all, he was merely an outsider in this Gosh.

He judged that matters like that should be handled by those who lived in this land.

"Anyway, the village where the Hundred-Faced dwelled is small but has an oasis, so a manageable number of people could live there normally. ...I trust you understand what I mean by 'found the village'?"

"Yes, I see. Honestly, I never would have believed it—that such a village existed not far from Gosh, and that it was the base of the Hundred-Faced Ones... If it hadn't come from your mouth, Rei, I simply couldn't have believed it."

Augusto murmured thoughtfully, and Daldora and the others nodded in agreement.

Of course. It was like discovering a nest of snakes carrying deadly venom had settled right next to the house they lived in.

Under normal circumstances, no one would welcome such a thing.

The reason Augusto and the others were surprised but not panicking was likely because they'd heard the Hundred-Faced Ones had already been wiped out.

Thinking this, Augusto and Daldora felt relieved, but Rei offered a word of caution.

"What we defeated this time were only the ones currently in that village. Considering it's highly likely not all members of the Hundred-Faced Clan were in that village at the time, there's a possibility those who had left the village might return someday."

"What!? ...No, you're right. When it comes to the Hundred-Faced Ones, they'd hire anyone if they could. Thinking about it that way, it's impossible that the entire clan was in the village."

Daldora murmurs, his expression troubled.

What would happen if the Hundred-Faced One returned to the village and found no members of their own clan there?

It was obvious—they would have sought revenge.

Of course, that was merely Daldora's own perception.

But honestly, Daldora couldn't grasp what kind of thoughts an assassin might harbor.

(Personally, the scariest thing about the Hundred-Faced One is their ability to change their skin and become a completely different person. Without that, in terms of pure combat ability, they're not that scary, though.)

Rei muttered this to himself, but it was something only Rei could say.

Even if his individual combat skills weren't that high, he still possessed a certain level of power.

That was proven by the fact he'd managed to hold his own against Zarust and Narsus to some degree.

An opponent with that level of combat prowess was attacking without feeling any pain whatsoever.

For an ordinary adventurer... someone like those in Gosh, there'd be no way to deal with it.

...Though, when facing an anomaly like Set, not feeling pain and possessing some combat skill meant absolutely nothing.

"How you handle that is up to you for now. Unfortunately, I'm not settling down in this town."

The moment those words left Rei's mouth, the strongest reaction came not from Augusto or Daldora... but from Kathy.

Even so, she said nothing. Probably because even she hadn't expected Rei to move to Gosh.

But even so, Rei was like a child to her.

Hearing that Rei was leaving Gosh naturally filled her with sadness.

Seeing Kathy's expression, Rei could roughly guess what she was thinking, but he still said nothing.

He understood he had no right to say anything, being the one leaving this town.

"A village with an oasis? ...Honestly, it's too big. I don't know what to make of it."

Daldora murmured at Rei's words.

It was undeniable that he'd worked hard to develop Gosh up until now, but he never imagined he'd end up with an entire village here... an entire village complete with an oasis.

"If it's become such a significant matter, shouldn't we bring it to the lord?"

Augusto nodded at Daldora's words.

"You're right. But considering the time, Lord Ryubrand is probably already asleep. If we forcefully wake him now, he'll likely be in a bad mood."

"...Even for something this important?"

The Hundred-Faced Ones—an assassin clan renowned not only throughout Solrain but also in neighboring nations.

We've seized their stronghold and gained control of a village, albeit small, that holds an oasis.

Under normal circumstances, it would be perfectly reasonable for the lord to be the first to act.

Yet, the notion that it was better not to wake him because he was sleeping raised nothing but questions.

But it seemed only Rei harbored such doubts. Everyone else nodded in agreement with Daldora's words. ...Even Kathy, of all people.

"Well, like I said before, I don't plan to get involved in this matter. If that's fine with you, then I don't mind."

Saying this, Rei found himself agreeing wholeheartedly with Resonance's words... that Ryubrand was far from competent.

Rei had only met Ryubrand the other day, and at the time, he hadn't thought of him as someone who could be called incompetent.

In fact, you could even say they'd hit it off over their shared values regarding food.

(Is this town really okay?)

That's precisely why he couldn't help but worry about Gosh's future.

After all, there were many people in this town who were friendly toward Rei.

The prime examples would be the couple, Augusto and Kathy.

"Anyway, since Rei came here, what's happening with that village now? The other two are safe, right?"

"Yeah. I destroyed the barrier protecting the village. Now there's no way to keep monsters out, so I asked those two to stay behind and guard it."

"I see. But it's dangerous for just two people to face desert monsters at night. Rei, I'm sorry, but could you please return to the village for now? I'll arrange for people to be sent tomorrow."

"...Understood. Oh, and there are many corpses in the village, including the one who committed suicide. Send enough people to handle them. Given the desert heat, they'll rot if not dealt with quickly, and worst case, they could become undead."

At those words, Augusto, Kathy, and several guards flinched, but Daldora nodded back without seeming particularly bothered.

"Yes, understood. However, gathering and burning the bodies will take considerable time. Considering the village investigation as well, it seems we'll need to stay in that village for several days... Um, how far exactly is it to the village?"

How far? He tried to recall the exact time it took him to travel there, but he'd stopped to talk along the way and couldn't remember precisely.

Besides, the distance Rei, Zarust, or Narsus could cover on foot in the same amount of time was vastly different from what an ordinary person could manage.

"How long, you ask? Well, it... didn't take two hours, I think."

"Two hours at the pace of a skilled adventurer? If so, the distance is closer than I thought. ...No, I suppose I should be more surprised that someone with a hundred faces was this close."

Rei had expected to hear it was far, but what came out of Daldora's mouth was unexpectedly the word "close."

Huh? Rei wondered, but Daldora showed no sign of noticing his confusion as she proceeded to lay out tomorrow's schedule one item after another.

Right now, in Daldora's mind, there were probably figures of personnel ready to move out first thing tomorrow morning.

Then, Daldora's gaze returned to Rei once more.

"Rei, you're heading back to that village again later today, right?"

"Yeah, that's what Augusto told me too."

"Then... I'm terribly sorry, but could you please return to this town again early tomorrow morning? Normally, I'd ask Narsus, but when it comes to mobility, Rei is simply one or two steps ahead of him."

"I don't really owe you that much..."

After all, Rei wasn't employed by Augusto... and certainly not by Daldora either.

But... Just as he was about to refuse, the image of Resonance dying with that strangely satisfied look flashed through his mind.

"But, yeah. Maybe I could do that much for him once in a while. We've got the Sand Ship, after all."

Before he knew it, the words were out of his mouth.
