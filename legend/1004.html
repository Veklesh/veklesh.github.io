Rei was the first to climb the ladder extending from the underground passage.

Byune indicated she would go, but with an alchemist and a magic item shop owner who had escaped using the underground passage, they absolutely had to be wary of an ambush using magic items.

And Rei had confidence that no matter how many ambushers were waiting, he could handle them.

While Byune was a thief with considerable skill, thieves were fundamentally not suited for combat.

Of course, that didn't mean they were completely incapable of fighting.

Byune excelled at long-range attacks using her long needle and close-quarters combat with daggers, leveraging her small stature and light weight.

Even so, compared to the likes of Rei, Elena, and Vihera, her combat prowess inevitably fell short.

That's precisely why Rei, confident in his combat prowess, decided to climb the ladder first.

The Dragon Robe, a magic item with formidable defense, and the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour as a trump card in emergencies, were significant advantages.

"...No one here, huh?"

Peering out from the ladder to survey the surroundings, he saw only old buildings that looked like they could collapse at any moment.

There was no sign of the kind of enemy that might be lying in wait for him.

He climbed up the ladder and took another look around.

Judging that this place was more of a storage shed than a house, he gently reached for a nearby door.

Opening it revealed several dilapidated buildings bathed in the setting sun.

Several buildings stood in a row, looking far from clean.

Seeing this scene, Rei understood where he was.

"A slum, huh. Well, since they found the place I was hiding, there probably aren't many spots within Gilm where I could easily escape to. Thinking about it that way, fleeing to the slums isn't such a strange idea after all."

For now, there was no sign of people nearby... More accurately, there were signs of people, but he confirmed none harbored hostility, malice, or intent to harm him. He called out to the hole where the ladder extended.

"It's safe. There are no enemies around for now. Come up."

At his words, Azimov, Byune, Elena, and Vihera climbed up in that order...

"It's cramped with this many people."

The hut itself, being in the slums, wasn't particularly spacious to begin with.

Naturally, with several people gathered inside, it felt cramped.

Still, Rei and Byune were short, and Elena and Vihera, being women, didn't take up much space. ...Though their well-developed chests and hips did take up some room.

Azimov was an adult man, but he wasn't a muscular giant. He tended to be on the lean side, often skipping meals while focused on his research.

All five were smaller or slimmer than the average adult male, so they could somehow all fit inside the cabin. Still, the sheer number of people made it undeniably stuffy.

"So, what's it like outside?"

"Out here is a slum. Looks like they dug an escape route quite far. ...They couldn't have dug it by hand, so they probably used some kind of magic item."

When Rei answered Elena, everyone showed a mix of disbelief and admiration.

It was true that the magic item shop had been in a back alley, relatively close to the slums.

But digging an underground passage all the way from there to here was, in every sense, an extraordinary feat.

"No one's searching this way, so it should be fine to go outside for now. It'll be easier than staying cooped up in this cramped space."

No one objected to Rei's words, and they quietly exited the hut.

Spreading out before them was the slum district, bathed in the glow of the setting sun.

The scene, dyed crimson, looked so striking it made one wonder if a fire had broken out.

While Rei and the others stepped out of the hut and gazed toward the slum district, Adria, who had escaped from the guard's grasp, arrived at the slum district, surveying her surroundings as she went.

Naturally, she had stolen perfume along the way to cover the scent that had led to her capture, even if only temporarily.

Yet she couldn't feel completely secure. It had been some time since she'd stopped at the spot in the forest where she'd left the incense burner, and she'd bathed and wiped herself down since then. Still, Set had managed to smell the stench.

Given how keen Set's sense of smell was, even if she could mask the scent with perfume, it was impossible to feel completely safe.

"I made it through that crowd, so I suppose I should be safe... It seems the pursuit hasn't reached me yet. Well, they're probably busy dealing with the commotion at the place they let me escape from, so it can't be helped."

Adria couldn't help but smile at the thought of the guards floundering around in the thick smoke.

But that smile vanished instantly, her face shifting back to that of a battle-hardened adventurer.

The men who had been trying to hassle her for money—and likely would have demanded more than that once they realized she was a woman—instinctively stopped in their tracks under the sharp glare Adria shot their way for just an instant.

They must have sensed, with that unique instinct of slum dwellers, that if they continued to hassle her, they would undoubtedly suffer a painful lesson.

In truth, Adria's skill far surpassed that of the average adventurer.

Right now, while she found it amusing that she'd nearly been caught by the guards, there was also a hint of irritation at having been beaten to the punch.

Had the men not stopped and confronted her, they would have had a limb or two broken on the spot.

In the worst case, she might have even cost them their lives.

Considering that, the men who stopped here had narrowly escaped death.

"Too bad."

Feeling regret that there was no one to vent her rising emotions upon, Adria casually entered the slum district.

(If they'd attacked, I might have gotten some weapons I could use as backups.)

Adria had originally left the magic item shop where she'd been hiding partly because she'd grown bored underground, but also to buy food supplies.

Given Zuboz's recent activities as an alchemist, it was probably best for him to stay inside. Besides, he preferred conducting alchemical experiments to venturing into town anyway.

Furthermore, if Zuboz happened to see Rei in town, he might act impulsively.

Zuboz hadn't seen Rei's face directly and only knew general features like a feminine face and short stature. However, the fact that he was accompanied by a griffin was an unmistakable, unmistakable characteristic.

There was no way Zuboz wouldn't recognize Rei if he saw someone with a griffin. He really didn't want to cause a big commotion at this point.

(After all, it's a festival. We need to create a bigger stage.)

Adria walked, lost in thought about that moment, eventually arriving at a sparsely populated area within the slums.

Here, he encountered not just the half-hearted types like those at the entrance, but individuals possessing strength worthy of being called top-tier.

Those who had been banished from the surface world for some reason and fallen into this place.

Precisely because they were such people, when push came to shove, they would unleash desperate, frenzied power and attack their opponents.

(Yes, like this person, for example... right?)

As if waiting for Adria's arrival, a man appeared.

His face was completely hidden by a beard, making it impossible to tell his age.

Since Adria had only intended to go shopping, the only weapon she carried was a spare longsword.

If she had to fight him, she would probably... no, she would definitely struggle.

There was a very real possibility she could lose.

"This way."

...Right. If we fight, that is.

"Long time no see, Dias. So the Zoboz ran away too? Well, I got caught the moment I stepped out of the shop, so I guess it can't be helped."

Considering how I got caught, they'd inevitably search the shop.

Until now, it was just another common shop and hadn't drawn any attention. But once it did attract attention, various inconveniences were bound to arise. Above all, if they searched the shop thoroughly, the chances of them finding something they shouldn't were high.

Considering that risk, escaping while they still had freedom of movement, before the shop could be searched, wasn't a bad choice at all.

"Zubozu seemed pretty unhappy. Because we decided to flee in such a hurry, it seems he couldn't take most of the already scarce materials with him."

"Really? Well, I guess going shopping was also for Zubo's sake... ...So even an alchemist is just an ordinary person without materials, huh?"

As Adria walked and chatted with Dias, Dias opened his mouth, seemingly without any particular emotion.

"It's not like they can't do anything. Alchemists are magicians too. They can wield a certain level of magic, and in Zuboz's case, he carries several magic items with him at all times."

"Even so, alchemists aren't suited for combat. ...But was Zboz the only one angry? What about Paul?"

The image of a giant, quiet figure reminiscent of a mountain flashed through Adria's mind.

A truly enigmatic figure, so much so that it was hard to fathom why someone like him would be working for the Bestir Empire.

With Paul's shop now unusable, retrieving the numerous magic items stored there was practically impossible.

No matter how she looked at it, it was undeniably their fault he'd been caught in the crossfire, so Adria couldn't help but feel somewhat guilty.

But Dias spoke up, paying no mind to Adria's state.

"I don't know what you're thinking inside, but from the outside, you don't seem particularly angry. You're as quiet as ever."

"I see. I do want to figure something out for him as soon as possible, though."

They continued talking as they walked, eventually arriving at their destination.

From the outside, it looked like any other building standing isolated nearby – half-ruined, as if it might collapse at any moment.

But the hideout Adria had prepared for emergencies couldn't possibly be what it appeared to be.

It might look like a ruin, but that was merely the facade.

Adriana and Dias entered the building without showing any particular signs of tension.

Inside, they found furniture sufficient for ordinary living, with no apparent issues.

Unlike other buildings, it wasn't littered with trash, and it was undoubtedly far more comfortable than any cheap inn.

Inside that room were two people.

One was Zuboz, who could be called Adria's partner in this matter, and the other was Paul, the owner of the magic item shop who had been hiding Adria and Zuboz.

Just as Adria had heard from Dias, who had come here with her, Zuboz wore a distinctly displeased expression.

Adriya knew the reason was that he'd left a lot of materials at the magic item shop, but even so, Zuboz's bad mood wasn't exactly an amusing development for her.

Compared to Zuboz, Paul showed no particular signs of distress.

Zuboz had lost many materials, but he still had truly valuable items with him. Paul, on the other hand, had lost every single magic item in his shop.

By any normal measure, Paul should clearly be more devastated.

But from the outside looking in, Paul hardly seemed shocked at all.

In fact, he seemed almost unchanged from usual.

Finding Paul's demeanor amusing, Adria nevertheless remembered she couldn't just stay here.

"Do you know why I got caught?"

None of the three people present answered that question.

Of course not. When Adria was captured, Paul was inside the shop, and Zubozo was in the hidden room underground. As for Dias, he'd been in this slum all along, so he had no way of knowing how Adria had been captured.

Seeing no response, Adria sighed inwardly and spoke.

"It was the smell. That place where we put the incense burner. Apparently, the stench was still lingering, and the griffin caught wind of it."

The moment she said this, Zubo's gaze sharpened.

"So that means... Rei was there?"

"Yes, that's what it means."

"...I see. It was a mistake to run away from that shop when our enemy was right there."

"No, never mind that. For now, Zuboz, use this. It should at least cover up the stench enough."

As Adria showed him the perfume, Zuboz started to protest that this wasn't the time for such things, but before he could finish, a man entered the building.

Paying no heed to Adria discreetly reaching for her weapon, the man entered the hut, approached Dias, and whispered in his ear.

Hearing this, Dias clicked his tongue in irritation.

"Looks like we have a visitor."

There was no need to guess who that referred to.
