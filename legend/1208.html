"Grim?"

Only Marina tilted her head at the name that came from Rei's mouth.

Elena had heard stories about Grimm before... In fact, when she first met Grimm, unlike the other Ara, she hadn't lost consciousness and had listened to Rei and Grimm's conversation. So, of course.

"Ah, an acquaintance of mine... Simply put, a lich lord. An undead, anyway."

"What!?"

Marina gasped at the words coming from Rei's mouth.

But that was a perfectly natural reaction for an ordinary human—or dark elf, for that matter.

Still, the main reason Marina didn't immediately act hostile toward Rei was because she trusted him... and above all, loved him deeply.

"Rei, um... Could you explain what this means?"

"Yeah, right. I had intended to explain this to Marina much earlier. But she was busy with her duties as Guildmaster, so it kept getting pushed back."

"...Tell me."

Hearing Rei's words, Elena and Vihera must have realized they already knew the secret.

Looking slightly dissatisfied and even a bit resentful, she urged Rei to continue.

"Where should I start? ...Yeah, I guess I have to start here. I'm not from this world. Well, my soul isn't, to be precise."

After stating this, he began explaining the things he had been hiding, starting with how his soul had been retrieved by Zepair after he died in another world and sent to this one, and then moving on to the topic of Beast Magic.

Even Marina, who had lived a long life, found much of it difficult to grasp or understand. Still, she listened carefully as Rei explained, breaking things down for her, and gradually came to accept it.

About twenty minutes passed like that... and eventually, Marina nodded as if she understood.

"I was surprised to hear you're from a different world, Rei, but actually, it makes sense."

"...Really?"

"Yes. Considering the magic power you possess, it was impossible for me to think you were from this world."

"Well, human... no, I mean, he is human, but he's closer to being an android."

"So what? His lifespan increased, his physical abilities improved. There's nothing inconvenient about it. Besides, the magic power was something Rei had from the start, right?"

For Marina, the fact that his lifespan was longer than a human's was probably the biggest factor.

Dark Elves and humans naturally have different lifespans.

Marina loved Rei, but if he had been an ordinary human, they would have faced separation by death eventually.

...Well, even if Rei and Marina had been together, they might still have been separated by death eventually. Still, not having to part ways before a hundred years passed, like ordinary humans, lifted a tremendous mental burden from Marina.

While being together forever with the one she loved was probably impossible, she could at least spend a longer time with him than a human could.

"Anyway. Grimm was a necromancer from the era when Zepair was alive. He survived by becoming undead himself. ...Well, since he became undead, saying he 'survived' might not be quite right."

"...I see. From Zepair's time... If that's the case, then perhaps some Dark Elves or Elves might have actually met him directly."

"Ah, now that you mention it..."

Given how incredibly long-lived dark elves and elves are, that possibility couldn't be ruled out.

"There are actually a few who say they've spoken with Zepair."

"There are?"

Rei asked without thinking, but if they lived during the same era as Grimm, it wouldn't be strange for someone to have known Zepair personally.

"If possible, it'd be a huge help if you could look into that later. Especially if anyone knows about Takumu Suzunose, who was part of the Zepairu clan—I'd really appreciate it."

Rei's interest in Zepair was fairly deep.

But stronger than that was the existence of Takumu, who had come to this world just like him.

(Well, compared to me arriving in this world as just a soul, he came here in the flesh, so that's pretty rare. Transfer, reincarnation, possession... there are various ways to come to this world.)

Reincarnation was Kabajido, Vihera's brother and the First Prince of the Bestir Empire.

In Rei's case, it was a combination of reincarnation and possession.

In contrast, judging from the fact he was wearing a student uniform, Takumu must have transferred his entire body to this Elgin.

Where exactly does the difference lie? If only I could learn a bit more about that... Considering Rei's situation, it's only natural to think that way.

(Though, of course, all that will have to wait until Vihera regains consciousness.)

He glanced at Vihera lying in bed and shifted his focus.

"Takumu aside, Grimm remains undead and is still alive today. Meaning he's lived as a magician... or wizard since that era. He might know a way to restore Vihera's consciousness."

"That..."

It wasn't that Rei's words lacked persuasiveness.

Still, while seeking advice on defeating the undead might make sense, asking for help in restoring Vihera's consciousness was completely unexpected.

Marina felt momentarily confused, but if it meant finding a way out of their current situation... well, it was undeniably a glimmer of hope.

"Can you actually talk to that undead? I mean, does it have proper self-control?"

Generally speaking, the undead often harbor hatred toward the living.

Or they're so broken they can't even make that judgment, seeing the living only as prey.

Many also look down on humans, eager to flaunt their perceived superiority.

Of course, some undead retain their rationality from life, but they are extremely rare compared to the whole.

Even if they appear unchanged to an outsider, many have twisted personalities due to becoming undead.

That's precisely why, even if Rei and Elena say they know her, Marina had no choice but to confirm it.

"Ah. You needn't worry about that. Apparently, she was originally a mage who admired Zepair and his faction. And now, she's quite favorable towards me, having inherited Zepair's beast magic."

"...Who knew Set wasn't just an ordinary monster. Well, thinking about it, it's rare to find a monster that friendly, so it actually makes sense."

Images of Set, who was affectionate and clingy with many others besides Rei, flashed through Marina's mind.

Monsters tamed by a tamer often don't bond with anyone but their master.

Compared to them, the extent to which Set bonded with those around him was unbelievable in many ways.

But if one considered that Set wasn't tamed, but rather a monster created by Rei, it wasn't entirely incomprehensible.

(But if that's the case, does that mean Rei is actually quite friendly? From the outside, he certainly doesn't seem that way at all.)

From Marina's perspective, Rei was definitely not someone who excelled at socializing.

She preferred deep, close relationships over broad, shallow ones.

Indeed, she considered her relationships with herself, Elena, and Vihera to be quite deep, and she had also built good relationships with a small number of adventurers, starting with Elk.

(Well, I prefer it that way, personally.)

Rei's features are naturally well-proportioned.

Though it might be more accurate to describe them as feminine rather than masculine. If Rei had a more approachable personality, he'd be popular with many people... and likely have more romantic rivals than he does now.

"So, how do you contact that Grimm guy... no, not guy. That undead? Things are stable now, but you should handle it as soon as possible, okay?"

"Right. But don't worry about contacting him. ...I'll use this."

What he took out from the Misty Ring was a Paired Orb.

"You've got to be kidding me... You had those?"

"Yeah. I've used these to contact him before, so there's no reason we can't get through."

"Hmm. So, can we watch while you do it?"

"Yeah. I want to see it too."

Marina and Elena both said this together, turning their gaze toward Rei.

Rei hesitated for a moment, but reasoning that if they were acting together, they would likely encounter Grimm eventually, he nodded in agreement.

"Alright. Then I'd like to call out to Grimm right away... Is that okay?"

Seeing them nod in response, Rei channeled magic into the Paired Orb.

Elena, who was accustomed to using the Paired Orbs, and Marina, who possessed a similar magical communication device, showed no particular surprise at this alone. But...

'Hmm? Rei?'

When the image of a skull—a skull wearing a crown, no less—appeared in the Paired Orb, both Elena and Marina froze in shock.

This was Marina's first encounter with a Grimm, and Elena hadn't had the chance to properly meet one before either.

That very fact made them both stare wide-eyed at the image of the Grim reflected in the crystal before them.

"Ah, sorry about that. I contacted you because I had something I wanted to ask."

'No problem. Fortunately, my experiments have reached a pause anyway. ...But more importantly.'

Grim paused mid-sentence and leaned closer to the Paired Orb.

He likely did this to get a better look at Rei and the others, but it also meant they got a close-up view of Grim's face... or rather, his skeletal form.

In terms of being a skeleton, it was no different from a skeleton.

But seeing it with their own eyes, they couldn't possibly believe it was the same kind of skeleton.

Even as a skeleton, there was a distinct difference in stature, something clearly understood even through the Paired Orb.

"It's best if others don't learn of our affair. That's what I thought, but... is that truly acceptable?"

Seeing Elena and Marina approach Rei without complaint, as if confirming something, meant they had met Grimm's approval.

"Yeah. I've known these two for quite a while now. That part's fine. I've also explained everything else about me."

"Oh? Regarding Rei... Does that include the other world and Lord Zepair as well?"

Rei nodded in response to Grimm's question.

Seeing Rei like that, Grimm let out a cheerful laugh that echoed around them.

For a moment, Rei wondered where that laughter was coming from on Grim's skeletal form. But then he realized it was absurd to even question it—Grim had no vocal cords to begin with.

Deciding it was pointless to dwell on such details, Ray waited for Grimm's laughter to subside before speaking.

"Now that we've finally gotten in touch after so long, there's something I'd like to ask you."

"Hm? Long time? To me, it feels like just yesterday."

For Grimm, who lived through countless ages, a few years was likely no more than a few days in Rei's terms.

"For me, it's been a long time. ...Anyway, can we get to the point now?"

"Hmm, go ahead."

At Rei's words, Grimm nodded without seeming particularly bothered.

For Grimm, talking with Rei was a source of stimulation in his long life.

It would be different if it were an ordinary human, but Rei was a man acknowledged by Zepair.

That alone made Grimm's strong interest, curiosity, and favor toward Rei... exceptionally high.

"Do you know... Ambris?"

"No, I don't. What is it?"

Rei felt both surprise and resignation at the blunt response.

It was an entity known to have appeared only once, three hundred years ago.

Given that, it was only natural that Grimm, who lived avoiding human eyes, wouldn't know of its existence.

"It's a being born from abnormal magical energy. I'd call it a monster... but since it lacks a magic stone, strictly speaking, it can't be called a monster."

"...Huh? Could it be... something that makes specific monsters evolve?"

"Huh!? You know about it!?"

Before the words "I don't know" had even dried on his tongue, Grimm spoke, causing Rei to cry out in astonishment.

Seeing Rei's reaction, Grimm chuckled amusedly... then nodded.

"Hmm. To my knowledge, that entity wasn't called Ambris. ...Though it was an extremely rare existence even back then. The name I know it by... was Jarda."

Finally obtaining a clear lead on Ambris, Grimm spoke before Rei and the others, who held their breath.
