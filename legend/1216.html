Dungeons... or more precisely, areas that naturally formed around dungeons.

Inns, shops selling weapons and armor, shops selling tools, shops selling food, taverns, brothels... Beyond these, various other shops existed around the dungeon.

It was more vibrant and crowded than many villages.

This was likely also related to the approaching winter.

As if people were trying to earn as much as possible before winter set in, planning to spend the cold months in Gilm.

Of course, even in winter, snow never fell inside the dungeon.

In fact, depending on the level, some areas were warmer and more comfortable than the snowy, cold outside.

Even so, almost no one considers spending the entire winter inside the dungeon.

Because being inside a dungeon means monsters are inevitably present.

Living inside a dungeon means constantly facing the possibility of being attacked by monsters.

Of course, if you possess exceptional abilities like Set's to detect enemies, or strength so great that the enemies in this dungeon are no match for you... then that's a different story.

But naturally, those possessing such prowess are few and far between.

...Even so, a small number of people do consider spending the winter in the dungeon due to lack of money.

If you're confident in your own abilities, especially since this dungeon has a forest inside, you won't have much trouble finding vegetables and fruits.

And if monsters are present, securing more than enough meat should be possible.

Only such a very small number of people would actually attempt to spend the winter in the dungeon.

"Wow... It's changed quite a bit since the last time I came here."

Looking around from the carriage window, Rei murmured.

Two carriages, the kind you rarely see... and with griffins walking alongside them, this group certainly stood out.

But for Rei, who was used to drawing attention while traveling with Set, such things didn't seem to bother him at all. He had the leisure to look around.

The same was true for the others.

Princess General Elena, the beautiful Guildmaster Marina, Ara who constantly drew attention as Elena's attendant, Elk the Rank A adventurer with a notorious nickname, and his wife Min.

Most of them were individuals who would draw attention even if walking alone.

That's precisely why they were all accustomed to being the center of attention, showing no particular concern for the stares around them.

Though, being inside a carriage probably had something to do with it. ...Though Elk and Ara were in the driver's seat.

"Yes. Even though the dungeon hasn't been conquered, there are still adventurers who can reach near the lowest level. The number of merchants is increasing, seeking the materials that party brings back, I suppose."

Marina murmured while observing the surroundings, and Elena nodded.

"With winter approaching, merchants are probably busiest right now."

"Yes. Elena's right. If you stay here through winter, you're practically trapped. ...You could leave if you tried, but venturing out in the snow would likely cost you your life."

"...Well, there aren't any walls here to keep monsters out either."

Walls like the one in Gilm exist in many towns.

The existence of walls to prevent monster attacks provides a sense of security for those who live there.

In a place without such walls, especially if monsters attack during winter, defending against them would be difficult.

For merchants, who possessed almost no means to fight back themselves, spending the winter in this wall-less land was a situation they wanted to avoid at all costs.

As they walked along, talking like this, the inn where Rei and the others had stayed before soon came into view.

Among the inns surrounding this dungeon, it was the most luxurious.

Because of that, its security measures were solid, and they hardly needed to worry about things like horses being stolen.

The lodging fees were steep, but considering the financial situation of the group present, that aspect posed no problem either.

"Well then, I'll go handle the check-in."

"Yeah, I'll come too. This place is technically under my jurisdiction, so I think I can get them to bend the rules a bit. Besides... I don't know if they have any rooms available either."

With winter approaching, many merchants were rushing to procure materials from this dungeon, so naturally, the inn's rooms filled up quickly.

Naturally, even at a high-end inn like this, securing a room became difficult.

However, it was standard practice for such high-end inns to keep a few rooms available at all times, just in case something came up.

...Yes, for when unexpected guests arrived—like the Guildmaster of Gilm, renowned high-rank adventurers, or symbolic figures of the Noble Faction.

She understood that. Elena nodded in response to Marina's words.

Then, together with Ara, who had come from the driver's seat into the carriage, she exited the carriage.

After seeing Marina off, Rei and Elena also stepped down from the carriage.

"At this hour... it looks like we'll have to go to the dungeon tomorrow after all."

"That's right. I too wanted to enter the dungeon today if possible... but since we've traveled this far by carriage, we must be somewhat tired. In that case, it's best to be fully prepared. After all, we're facing an S-rank monster."

"Huh?"

The voice, interjecting into Rei and Elena's conversation, made them both turn their heads.

There, at the end of their gaze, stood a man in his twenties who looked earnest.

(Huh? Last time, a child took Set to the stables... Well, it is a high-class inn. They probably have several employees.)

He was a face they hadn't seen on their previous visit, but several years had passed since they'd last been here.

Naturally, the people working here would change over time.

Besides, this place was right next to a dungeon. Unlike walled cities like Gilm, it was extremely dangerous.

Normally, no one would want to keep working in such a dangerous place for long.

Anyway, Rei was curious about what the man in front of her had said, so she spoke up.

"Huh? You said 'huh'? Is something up?"

Asked by Rei, the man realized for the first time that he had overheard their conversation... or rather, that he had blatantly revealed he had overheard it.

For staff employed at such a high-class inn, overhearing guests' conversations was, of course, common.

But if they blatantly revealed they'd overheard, the guests would naturally feel uncomfortable. It was common sense to pretend not to have heard, even if they had.

"Ah, um, I'm sorry. I just... heard you talking about challenging a Rank S monster, the Silver Lion."

"Yeah. We plan to head into the dungeon tomorrow."

"I see. ...But, um, I heard the Silver Lion in this dungeon is incredibly powerful? Like, many adventurers have challenged it before and all were defeated..."

The man trailed off at the end, but the grim expression on his face made it clear what had become of those defeated adventurers.

"Of course. It's an S-rank monster. We know it has that kind of power. But there's a reason we absolutely have to defeat it."

Rei's expression, as he muttered while gazing toward Elena's carriage, held an unwavering resolve to defeat the Silver Lion.

Vihera, who appeared to be nothing more than asleep, had not opened her eyes once since the day of that battle with Ambris.

To save her, Rei was prepared to do whatever it took, even confronting an S-rank monster.

Having been inside the carriage until just now, the man saw Rei's face without his hood and likely realized that nothing he said could stop him.

But... even so, the male employee couldn't bear the thought of people dying needlessly and tried to speak up.

The unfortunate thing for this employee was, first and foremost, that he was a complete civilian, utterly unconnected to any violent affairs.

Had he possessed even a modicum of skill, he might have recognized the caliber of fighters he was dealing with when he saw Rei and Elena.

And above all, the greatest misfortune was that Set was hidden from view behind the carriage.

"Guru?"

That's precisely why, when Set, drawn by the sound of Rei and the man talking, peeked out from behind the carriage, the man stopped the movement he'd made to say something.

His first thought was: What kind of monster was that in his line of sight?

A monster that radiated an overwhelming presence. ...In reality, Set wasn't actually radiating that kind of presence.

It was just that the man saw Set and thought that.

...Then again, seeing a griffin, that reaction was hardly surprising.

In fact, this man's reaction was perfectly normal. What was abnormal was that griffins had become like pets in Gilm.

"Huh!?"

"Ah... relax. Set may look like this, but he's docile. He won't attack unless provoked."

So he wasn't here the last time I came.

Thinking that, Rei calmed the man down.

To show how docile Set was, he stroked Set's head.

Suddenly being scared by the man must have been a sad experience for Set too.

But seeing him purr happily as Rei stroked him, the man couldn't comprehend what was happening before his eyes. Then, hearing Set let out a pleased little cry while being stroked by Rei, his eyes widened in surprise.

To the man's eyes, what Rei was stroking could only be a griffin.

Yes, a rank A monster griffin.

A monster that, under normal circumstances, would never become this accustomed to humans.

"Huh? What? Um..."

Seeing that griffin purring like a kitten while being petted by Rei, the man was bewildered.

While petting Set, Rei spoke up, trying to calm the man.

"I told you, didn't I? Set won't lash out if we don't provoke him. ...Anyway, I want to take Set to the stables. Is it okay if I go on my own? There's also the matter of the horses."

Rei's words, both soothing and admonishing, seemed to finally snap the man back to his senses.

He hurriedly bowed his head and spoke.

"My apologies. I'll bring the person in charge right away. Please wait just a little longer."

As they watched the man's retreating back, Min emerged from another carriage.

"Rei, maybe you shouldn't tease people like that?"

"No, I wasn't trying to mock him or anything like that at all."

"...You were just being yourself? Or rather, since you're always with Set, maybe your sense of that sort of thing is numb."

To Min's somewhat exasperated mutter, Rei merely shrugged his shoulders slightly.

As Min said, he hadn't done what just happened on purpose.

That was precisely why he couldn't say anything back.

As if to rescue Rei, Marina and Ara returned from the inn.

"We managed to get rooms. But, well, it is peak season, so we could only get two..."

"Huh? Does that mean we split up—us and Rei's group? Or boys' room and girls' room?"

"Well... I'm not sure about that."

At Min's words, Marina turned her gaze toward Rei.

Honestly, Marina welcomed the idea of sharing a room with Rei.

But it was true that doing something like that while Vihera was still unconscious felt a bit wrong.

Elena felt the same way. Her cheeks flushed slightly at Min's words, but she shook her head.

"Yeah. I think separate rooms for men and women is better."

"I agree with Lady Elena."

Ara nodded in agreement with Elena's words, and with no objections from the others, it was ultimately decided to safely separate into men's and women's quarters.

"Then, do we need to carry Vihera and Rodos?"

"It might be uncomfortable for Vihera to be carried by men, so I'll take care of Vihera."

"Well, it's only right that I carry Rhodes."

It was settled: Ara would carry Vihera, and Elk, who had stepped down from the driver's seat, would carry Rhodes.

At an inn as high-class as this one, they could have easily had the inn's staff carry them.

But Rei and the others had no intention of leaving Vihera to others.

It wasn't that they didn't trust the inn's staff; it was more that they didn't want others touching her.

Watching as someone from the inn came to guide the carriage and horses, Rei steeled his resolve. It was finally time...
