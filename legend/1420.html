That afternoon, after receiving the request from Daskar, Rei stood with Set before the main gate of Gilm.

Normally, when setting out on a long journey, various preparations are necessary.

Transportation, preserved food, drinking water, escorts... While it was possible to resupply along the way, travel was unpredictable.

Given that, it was only natural to prepare for whatever might happen.

But... when it came to Rei, the story was entirely different.

For transportation, he had Set, who was far faster than any horse on the ground and could fly, making him immune to attacks from bandits and the like.

Food is stored in vast quantities within the Misty Ring, and drinking water is handled by the magic item, the Flowing Water Dagger.

And as for protection, having both Rei and Set was practically overkill.

Given those circumstances, there was nothing special Rei needed to prepare.

If he had to mention anything, it was buying every dish he could find at the stalls before leaving Gilm and stuffing them into the Misty Ring.

"Alright then? Make sure you don't get lost, okay?"

Marina said it as if lecturing a child on their first errand, but in Rei's case, while not extreme, he was somewhat directionally challenged.

That was probably why Marina was being so careful to make sure Rei didn't head off in some strange direction.

Originally, Marina and Vihera had intended to go with Rei.

However, that became impossible because Marina and Vihera had various tasks to attend to at Gilm.

Marina's spirit magic, in particular, possessed an overwhelming difference in power even when compared to other practitioners of spirit magic.

In terms of the spirit magic Marina wields, its versatility and universality are immeasurable.

Vihera has achieved remarkable results as a public safety officer.

...Though Rei couldn't help but wonder if Vihera's very appearance was what was disrupting the peace. Still, for adventurers, craftsmen, and those seeking work, it seemed a conspicuous figure like her was somehow necessary.

It had become something of a local legend that such individuals inevitably tried to pick a fight with Vihera and got thoroughly beaten.

Byune was excluded from the group from the start due to communication issues.

Rei had also reached a point where she could communicate with him generally, but it was only generally.

He couldn't understand the details of what Byune was trying to say the way Vihera could.

Given that, the option of bringing Byune along never existed for Rei from the start.

Taking her along would undoubtedly lead to confusion.

Worse still, the inability to communicate could easily lead to all sorts of unpleasant situations.

"I know. You take care of Gilm's side, too. ...Well, I guess I don't need to say it, but."

"Heh heh, it'll be nice to have a worthy opponent."

Vihera's reason for staying behind in Gilm was partly work, but also because the Noble Faction's interference might not be over yet.

Of course, more adventurers than usual had gathered in Gilm now.

With that much firepower, they probably won't attack as foolishly and straightforwardly as they did against Bistor.

But that also meant they might strike next time using methods beyond anyone's expectations.

Moreover, it was almost certain that some adventurers currently in Gilm were under the influence of the Noble Faction.

...No, it was better to assume that agents of those who found the expansion of Gilm troublesome, beyond just the Noble Faction, were also lurking.

The reason Vihera was chosen as the direct combat force was likely because, against such opponents, he would be a formidable foe, and he could enjoy a battle that satisfied him... that was probably the main reason.

"Well, I'm off then."

Saying that, Rei straddled Set's back...

"Set-chaaaan!"

As if timed perfectly, that voice suddenly rang out.

Rei turned his gaze toward the familiar voice and saw, unsurprisingly, Milein.

He saw Milein waving her hand and running straight toward Rei—or more accurately, toward Set in this case—but without waiting for her, he signaled to Set.

If they weren't in a hurry, he wouldn't have minded letting Milein and Set play together.

But now, they needed to depart as quickly as possible.

Before the Noble Faction made any major moves.

Wasting time here only increased the likelihood of the Noble Faction acting.

...Of course, wasting a few minutes here probably wouldn't make much difference, but when it came to Milein, that changed everything.

The affection Milein felt for Set was truly intense.

If we let Milein have her way here, it could easily cost us hours... or worse, a whole day.

Even Rei wanted to avoid wasting that much time.

"Hey, Rei! It's me, Milein! Wait a sec!"

Ignoring the voice calling out from behind... no, from below, Rei soared into the sky alongside Set.

Set tilted her head slightly, looking at Rei as if to ask, "Is it okay?" Rei nodded, indicating it was fine.

Set could have played with Milein a little longer, but since Rei said so, he simply flapped his wings and flew away from Gilm.

"Here, eat this first. It's not exactly a long journey, but our first stop is the territory of the Neutral Faction."

Rei could have headed straight for the noble's territory that had dispatched private soldiers, but to understand the situation there—especially what buildings existed where and how much security was in place—information was still needed.

And conveniently, the territory of the Neutral Faction lay right next to the one Rei was heading for.

Therefore, it stood to reason that Rei, having received this request from Daskar, could obtain information from that noble.

Daskar had likely considered these circumstances when assigning this matter to Rei.

(Well, the information about Gilm's expansion that the target noble... Count Lerdact, was it? ...got hold of was probably also from someone he'd infiltrated into that Neutral Faction noble's territory, so I suppose it's a case of tit for tat.)

Flying overhead, I directed my gaze downwards and spotted Treant Forest a short distance away.

The woodcutters seemed to be working hard to fell trees, but considering the sheer size of Treant Forest, most of it remained untouched.

Despite the forest's immense size, the path left by the Giganto Turtle's movement was clearly visible even from above.

(Well, in this case, it's precisely because it's from above, I suppose.)

A single line drawn through the forest.

It was a bit too thick to call it a line, but it was undeniably a very conspicuous presence.

"I wonder when they'll ever finish cutting down all the trees in this Treant Forest?"

"Guru?"

Hearing Rei's mutter, Set turned around, flapping his wings. "What is it?"

Facing Set's round, staring eyes, Rei shook his head as if to say it was nothing and stroked the back of the neck in front of him.

Set purred contentedly at Rei's gesture.

Seeing Set's adorable nature, which was hard to believe belonged to a Rank S monster, Rei understood why Milein felt such strong affection for him.

(Well, I already knew that part.)

And so Set continued flying, swiftly passing through towns like Abuero and Saburusta.

A speed orders of magnitude faster than ground travel.

This speed was likely one of the major reasons Daskar had entrusted this request to Rei.

This whole affair... the plan to raid the caravan and seize materials for the Gilm expansion. Daskar likely intended to finish the retaliation before the mastermind pulling the strings behind it received the information.

That said, Rei wasn't optimistic about that point.

He understood all too well, from personal experience, that magic items like the Paired Orb existed, enabling long-distance communication.

It was only natural, since this magic item was precisely why Rei could converse frequently with Elena.

However, the Paired Orb is an extremely valuable item. It's hard to imagine that Count Lerdact, who is said to hold a position not that important even among the Noble Faction, possesses it.

Considering how much effort Rei put into obtaining them, there's no need to worry about that point.

(Well, a noble could buy it with money... but do they have that kind of wealth? Probably not. Still, there are other ways to communicate besides the Paired Orb.)

For instance, by employing a Tamer or a Summoner to have a bird deliver a letter, one could learn the outcome of this matter far more swiftly than by sending a regular letter.

Summoning magic aside, tamers are extremely rare, so hiring one isn't exactly easy... Whether that's fortunate or unfortunate for Rei and the others is debatable.

In any case, many adventurers are currently gathering in Gilm.

Furthermore, to swiftly contact other villages and towns to issue instructions, Daskar had been favoring and hiring individuals with such skills.

Among them, naturally, there are those who take on jobs from others and complete requests.

For such individuals, they don't pry into who the client is.

Well, they might turn down blatantly suspicious requests, but there probably aren't many that gullible.

"Grrrr..."

As Rei contemplated the surrounding scenery, Set suddenly growled.

What's up? Rei turned his gaze in the direction Set was looking... and his expression turned to one of shock.

Because at the end of his gaze were several orcs.

Orcs themselves weren't particularly rare monsters.

While they could cause major trouble if led by higher-tier or rare species—like the settlement once ruled by the Orc King Rei had defeated—ordinary orcs usually traveled in small groups of a few, or at most around ten.

Of course, there were exceptions—groups of dozens without higher-tier or rare species, or even lone orcs.

In any case, the mere presence of orcs wasn't something that particularly surprised Rei.

But...if that orc was carrying an unconscious human, that changed things entirely.

Especially since the person being carried has long hair.

Although it's only a guess, the one being carried by the orc is likely a woman, not a man.

And a woman captured by monsters like orcs or goblins faces a tragic fate.

It meant having one's dignity—as a human, no, as a woman—utterly trampled.

"Now that I've seen this, I can't just... leave it like this, can I?"

It's not like Rei was some humanitarian.

He could accept that the adventurer's world was often described as survival of the fittest.

But... even so, he couldn't bring himself to abandon a woman who could be saved like this.

"...Guess I have no choice. Set!"

With that single word, Set seemed to understand exactly what Rei meant.

He flapped his wings and began descending toward the ground.

This time, he didn't let out his usual high-pitched cry. Perhaps even Set was concerned for the woman being carried by the orcs.

If Set had cried out here, there was no telling how the orcs who noticed his presence would react.

If they simply dropped the woman and fled, it would mean Rei could easily achieve his goal—the best possible outcome.

But there was no doubt that throwing the unconscious woman onto the ground was dangerous in many ways.

Considering the worst-case scenario where the impact of the fall could break her neck and kill her, there was no way such a thing could be tolerated.

(Besides, orc meat is pretty valuable.)

Though orcs were only Rank D monsters, not high-rank creatures, their meat was exceptionally tasty.

Generally, the higher a monster's rank, the better its meat tastes.

But there are exceptions to every rule, and orcs were one of them.

Back when... well, right after Rei first appeared in Gilm, Rei participated in an attack on an orc settlement led by an Orc King.

They had obtained a large amount of meat then, but Rei ate enough to be called a glutton, and Set ate even more than Rei.

Given that, it was only natural that the orc meat in the Misty Ring would dwindle... and if it could be replenished, there was no need to hesitate.
