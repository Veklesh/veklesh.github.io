"Ugh... Ah... Kill me... Please kill me... Please, kill meeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeee

In the darkness, a voice echoes around him.

But no one heeded the plea of the one making the sound; on the contrary, the more that person screamed, the faster the red blood flowed from the tubes embedded in their body.

Normally, they would have died long ago from the loss of so much blood, yet there was no sign of the person dying.

The reason was the gem embedded in the man’s forehead.

……If Lemuria had been present, he would undoubtedly have been astonished.

For it was a gem containing magic—a gem that, as far as anyone knew, only the Lemurian clan possessed at present.

And it wasn’t just any gem. That gem—white, so pure it could be called snow-white—indicated that the magic sealed within was healing magic.

Thanks to the effects of that healing magic, the man with tubes inserted all over his body was able to survive—even though a massive amount of blood had been drained from him, he did not die from blood loss—because the healing magic continued to activate, albeit weakly.

However, naturally, subjecting someone to such extreme treatment meant that the person on the receiving end of the healing magic wouldn’t get off scot-free.

In fact, because this person had a gem imbued with healing magic implanted in them, while they wouldn’t die, they were constantly suffering excruciating pain—pain so intense that it wouldn’t be surprising if they died at any moment.

To begin with, a gem imbued with magic loses its effect once it is used.

Depending on the quality of the gem, it might even shatter.

And yet, the gem embedded in this person’s forehead is not using all the healing magic sealed within it at once, but rather using it little by little—truly, bit by bit.

Naturally, this is not something the person with the gem embedded in their forehead did themselves; it was done by the person who bound the man’s limbs with chains and rendered him immobile.

One excruciating pain after another.

This person was screaming without ever growing accustomed to the pain... but suddenly, the screams escaping his mouth ceased.

“...Ah...”

Completely unaware of what had happened, the only sound that came from the man’s mouth was a dazed gasp.

Given that the excruciating pain had vanished in an instant, it was hardly surprising.

Still, after a few minutes had passed, he was able to recall why he had ended up in such a state...

Normally, it wouldn’t have been strange for him to feel intense anger toward the person who had deceived him and brought him here, but the excruciating pain he had endured up until that point had completely sapped him of any such energy.

For now, he just wanted to soak in this unexpected moment of peace.

It was only natural to feel that way.

If I had just a little more energy left, it wouldn’t be strange to try to find a way out of this situation… but for someone who has been subjected to endless suffering, there is simply no room to think about such things.

Going mad might bring relief, but the healing magic embedded in the gem on his forehead would not allow such a thing.

Even now, the healing magic imbued in the gem continued to take effect, albeit slowly, gradually trying to restore that person to a normal state.

“……”

Free from pain and all else, that person could think of nothing now and could only cling to this moment of peace.

But that moment of peace came to an abrupt end.

Suddenly, the only door in this room leading to the outside opened.

And it wasn’t as if someone had simply walked in slowly… no, it was violent, forceful—as if they didn’t care if the door broke.

“Young master, are you all right?!”

At the shout of the person who burst through the door, the person in the room slowly looks up.

If possible, I wish they wouldn’t disturb this peaceful moment that had finally arrived.

That was his thought.

But Gaiska’s hopes were shattered once again by a single word from the person who had entered the room.

“Young master, pull yourself together. Young master. It’s me, Deotres. Young master!”

“Deo…to…res?”

The name slipped from Gaiska’s lips.

It was the name of a man who was Gaiska’s most trusted subordinate.

The moment he uttered that name, Gaiska’s willpower surged back in a most mysterious way.

And this, despite the fact that just a few seconds ago, he had been in a state that could only be described as a living corpse.

“Ah, yes. Honestly, when I got back from the party, the young master was nowhere to be found, so I went looking all over for him.”

“You… found… him? How… long did it take?”

Ever since being locked in this room, Gaiska had been subjected to such excruciating pain that he had lost all sense of time.

Because of that, he had no idea what time it was now... nor exactly how much time had passed since he’d arrived at this house; his sense of time was completely numb.

“Anyway, let’s get out of here. It’s fine for now, but if we stay here, the people from this mansion might start gathering.”

Get out of here.

The moment he heard those words, Gaiska felt strength returning not only to his spirit but to his body as well.

The pain was still there. The pain was still there, but... he decided that getting out of this house quickly and seeking his father’s protection took priority over that pain.

It could be said that, right now, Gaiska’s spirit was truly overcoming his physical body.

She was supposed to die here, unnoticed by anyone, but her trusted subordinate, Deotres, had come to her rescue.

No matter what, he would escape this place and make sure the person who had done this to him received the punishment they deserved.

It’s not strange for Gaiska to feel this way. …No, considering Gaiska’s personality, it was only natural.

Fueling himself with a thirst for revenge, he somehow found the strength in his legs to stand up.

Watching Gaiska like this, Deotres used a key to unlock the chains binding his body and began pulling out the tubes protruding from his body.

Gaiska’s face flickered with a faint look of displeasure at the time, but he must have decided that removing the tubes embedded throughout his body took priority.

“So, to get out of here… what do I need to do?”

The tone of his question to Deotres was by no means strong.

Still, unlike when he had been surrendering himself to the momentary respite just moments ago, he was now able to take decisive action of his own volition.

“For now, let’s get out of here as quickly as possible. This house is full of skilled fighters, and if we’re attacked by them, I won’t be able to protect you all by myself.”

“...I get it. Don’t think you’ll get away with pulling a stunt like this on me. I’ll make you regret it—I swear I will.”

Muttering to himself, Gaiska leaned on Deotres’ shoulder as he left the room.

This room was located in the basement of the Celestan house.

That’s why there was a staircase right in front of them, and by climbing it, they arrived at the first-floor hallway.

“Deotres, from what I’ve heard, this house is supposedly rigged with a ton of traps… Are there any traps here?”

“Huh? Oh… I’ve already disarmed those, so there’s no problem. Otherwise, I wouldn’t have been able to make it down to you, young master.”

“I see.”

Gaiska accepted Deotres’ words without a second thought, but strictly speaking, Deotres wasn’t a thief or anything of the sort—he was a warrior.

Was it because Gaiska trusted Deotres that much, or was it because Gaiska was mentally exhausted from the near-torture he’d endured up to that point?

In any case, Gaiska showed no sign of finding this suspicious and headed toward the entrance.

Celestan’s house isn’t particularly spacious, like a mansion.

Apart from the fact that it’s located in the slums, it’s really just an ordinary single-family home. …Though it’s debatable whether it’s appropriate to call it an “ordinary single-family home” when there are traps set up inside.

Gaiska didn’t find this unusual; she focused solely on escaping the house… and eventually arrived at the door leading outside.

Since the house itself isn’t that large, the distance, naturally, is short.

For Gaiska, who was extremely drained both physically and mentally, this was a huge relief.

Then, Deotres opened the door leading outside...

“Oh? Where are you heading?”

Just beyond the open door stood the house’s butler, asking this with a smile.

Gaiska, of course, remembered the butler’s face.

In fact, he could be called one of the very symbols of terror.

Just a moment ago, he had been thinking that he would make everyone living in this house regret being alive… but the moment he saw the butler’s face, Gaesca’s thoughts were overwhelmed by terror.

Still, he thought.

The reason he didn’t make a scene the moment he saw the butler’s face was probably because Deotres was there with him.

With Deotres here, he should be able to defeat this butler.

And even if he couldn’t defeat him, it wouldn’t be a problem—Deotres could at least get him out of here.

Thinking that, Gaiska tried to turn his gaze toward Deotres, who was supporting him by the shoulder...

“Huh?”

Feeling a slight jolt, Gaiska let out a dazed cry and collapsed onto the floor.

The only consolation was that she was still inside the building and didn’t have to fall onto the cold winter ground.

Exhausted both physically and mentally, and caught off guard by the unexpected impact, Gaiska couldn’t even manage to put her hands out as she fell, and simply rolled over.

Lying there on the floor, he thought Deotres was reaching out to help him and turned his gaze in that direction, but...

“……”

What met her was not a hand reaching out to help her up, but a cold stare.

It was the kind of gaze one might reserve for trash lying on the side of the road.

“Wha… what do you think you’re doing?”

The words that barely escaped Gaiska’s lips were filled with rage.

It was as if he were asking, “Why on earth would you, my trusted subordinate, do something like this?”

Faced with that look, Deotres ignored Gaiska’s gaze and spoke to the butler.

“For now, this has at least restored some of this trash’s willpower, and as you requested, he should be harboring even greater despair and hatred.”

The words that came out of Deotres’ mouth were more than enough to make Gaiska realize the situation he currently found himself in.

“Why? Why… why would you do something like this! Do you hold a grudge against me?!”

“Of course I do. Did you think I didn’t?”

In response to Gaiska’s words, a short phrase escaped Deotres’ lips.

But there was no doubt that hatred was contained within those words.

Yet, unaware of that hatred, Gaiska spoke.

He likely understood that if he remained silent, he would be dragged back to that basement again.

“Why?! I’ve treated you with great favor! Yet why would you do something like this?!”

“...Relyed on me? That’s right. To be honest, you danced to my tune far more conveniently than I ever expected. So much so that I didn’t even have to go to the trouble of making you dance.”

There was not a trace of the ease or familiarity from just moments ago—when he had been calling Gaesca “young master”—in Deotres’s attitude or tone as he spoke.

He continued speaking without even trying to hide the hatred within him.

“The reason I put up with a worthless, incompetent fool like you was because I was thinking about how I could inflict the greatest despair upon you.”

“Why? Why would you do such a thing?!”

Gaiska’s words were almost identical to those he had shouted just a few seconds earlier.

But since Gaiska himself didn’t understand why he was being subjected to this, it was only natural for him to ask Deotres for an explanation.

At Gaiska’s words, Deotres exhaled as if momentarily dumbfounded… and in the next instant, he shouted, his anger laid bare.

“Who do you think would swear loyalty to you, the one who avenged Lucha, from the bottom of their heart!”

“Lusha,” he said. Even so, Gaiska couldn’t recall her.

However, since he was called an enemy, Gaiska could surmise that this was likely someone he had killed or harmed for some reason.

...To Gaiska, a child not even ten years old—someone he had cut down merely to test the blade of a famed sword he’d happened to acquire—was likely not worth remembering.

Nor did he care that this child was the only daughter, the sole surviving memento of Deotres’s beloved wife.

Deotres, who had been an adventurer, used his connections to fabricate a false background, infiltrated the House of Marquis Seisor, and waited for an opportunity for revenge.

All to bring about Gaiska’s definitive downfall.

And now, the long-awaited opportunity has finally arrived.

“When these guys approached me, I truly believed that heaven hadn’t abandoned me. …And so, Gaiska… no, young master. You’ll have to suffer right up until the very end before you die, but do your best.”

With a smile worthy of a demon of vengeance, Deotres reached out to lead Gaiska back to the basement.
