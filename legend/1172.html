Rei and Set, who had absorbed the Lizardman Leader's magic stone, along with Vihera who had been watching over them, stored all the Lizardman corpses in the Misty Ring and headed straight for Gilm.

Naturally, they didn't walk; they flew through the air riding Set.

Just as when they departed from Gilm, Vihera clung to Set's right leg.

(Set is holding on, and Vihera is holding on. The result of flying through the air is the same, but whether one's own will is present there is probably important. ...Well, not many can do that anyway.)

Gazing down at the green carpet spreading across the ground, Rei thought about Vihera beneath him... or more accurately, beneath Set.

She possesses the grip strength to hang onto Set with one hand for a considerable amount of time.

And yet, she possesses the delicacy to ensure Set doesn't feel any pain from her grip on his right front paw.

A strong sense of trust, believing Set would save her in an emergency.

There were other elements necessary for flying like this, but not many people could fulfill all of them like Vihera.

Though, when he thought that far, the faces of Mireine and Johanna flashed through Rei's mind.

Two female adventurers who would stake their lives on cherishing Set... If Set desired it, they'd likely find a way to obtain not just platinum coins, but even light gold coins.

The image of those two flashed through his mind. If they heard that Vihera had flown through the sky clinging to Set, they would undoubtedly demand to fly themselves.

Considering that, it was easy to imagine what would happen once they returned to Gilm.

(Though honestly, dealing with the problem of higher-tier creatures appearing one after another should come before riding Set and flying, I think.)

Goblin leaders, kobold leaders, lizardman leaders. And though Rei hadn't encountered them yet, orc leaders too—superior beings from numerous races.

If superior beings were appearing within each race alone, it wouldn't be that unusual.

But for superior beings from so many different races to appear all at once like this... it clearly felt like something deliberate was going on.

(For now, they're just leader-type higher-ranking species, so dealing with them isn't too difficult. But if generals, mages... or worse, kings start appearing, it won't be a laughing matter.)

Even a single pack led by an Orc King required the guild to form an emergency extermination squad.

If multiple other races simultaneously produced Kings... that would clearly be beyond what the adventurers in Gilm could handle alone.

(Well, the reason we hunted down the Orc King's pack was probably also because of the Orcs' breeding method involving attacking women. If that's the case, then the next dangerous ones would be the Goblins, right?)

Both orcs and goblins are species where only males exist, who reproduce using females from other races... though not necessarily humans.

Precisely because of this, humans—the most numerous species and, fundamentally, the weakest in combat (though there are exceptions like adventurers)—are the perfect prey.

That's precisely why killing goblins and orcs on sight is recommended.

...Though in the case of orcs, it's also purely because their meat is tastier than monsters of the same rank.

"Rei, I see it now!"

Rei, who had been deep in thought atop Set's back, snapped back to reality at Vihera's voice from below.

Looking down, he spotted Gilm a short distance away.

Confirming this, Rei gently tapped the back of Set, whom she was straddling.

Set, seemingly understanding Rei's intent, immediately descended to the ground.

Merchants, adventurers, and other travelers walked the road toward Gilm, and most of them were already aware of Set's presence.

That's why, even when they saw Set, almost no one was startled, made a fuss, or tried to flee.

Only a very few, those who seemed to be visiting Gilm for the first time, looked as if they might startle. However, they were quickly reassured by explanations from those around them.

The reason they couldn't feel completely at ease here was probably because they still couldn't believe a griffin existed as a familiar.

Amidst this familiar scene, Rei landed on the ground without paying it much mind.

Naturally, Vihera, who had been clinging to Set's leg, released her grip as the ground drew near. Simultaneously, the rope binding her to Set came loose, and she landed too.

The entire sequence unfolded with flawless smoothness, astonishing many of the onlookers.

"So, what now? The guild first?"

"Probably. We need to report defeating that pack of lizardman leaders. ...Oh, and we should probably report that adventurers were attacked too."

"Probably. Well, even if we don't say anything, those adventurers will report it anyway."

Exchanging brief words, Rei, Vihera, and the two men and one beast completed their formalities at Gilm's main gate and entered the city.

Returning to the guild, Rei and Vihera parted ways with Set at the entrance as usual... or rather, before they could part ways, for some reason—or perhaps naturally—they were intercepted by Mireine and Johanna waiting there, who promptly took Set away.

Seeing that Set wasn't displeased, but rather seemed pleased, Rei left him with the two of them.

Of course, he entrusted him precisely because he felt reassured that Mireine and Johanna wouldn't do anything Set wouldn't like.

The two entered the guild and headed straight for Lenora, as if it were only natural.

"Ah, Rei. You're back."

The first to notice Rei and Vihera approaching wasn't Lenora... but Kenny, who was sorting papers beside her.

This was where a lovesick maiden's true talents shone through.

Lenora, alerted by her friend's voice, spotted Rei and Vihera and smiled.

She understood Rei and Vihera were skilled adventurers, but seeing them return safely like this still brought her relief.

"I'm so glad you're both safe. So, what about the lizardmen horde?"

Lenora asked. Rei nodded back, indicating it was fine.

"We took care of the lizardmen and their leader. Want to see the bodies?"

"No, it wasn't an urgent matter, and I don't think you'd lie about defeating them if you hadn't."

"Right. Ah, but I haven't skinned them for proof of defeat yet. There were so many bodies, I just tossed them all into the item box... including the kobolds."

We also defeated the kobold leader's pack, but they're still inside the Misty Ring.

There was no doubt that dismantling and stripping everything would take a considerable amount of time.

(Yeah, I should probably put out a request for this.)

Having faced a similar situation before, Rei made this decision without hesitation.

However, he wouldn't place the request immediately.

The guild was already extremely busy dealing with the monster horde issue. There simply weren't adventurers available to handle the dismantling and skinning of dozens... or even a hundred monsters.

Well, if it were someone who had just become an adventurer after being born in the Gilm, they probably couldn't take on a request that involved going outside the city yet, so they could be asked.

But assigning such a task to adventurers who've never done dismemberment or skinning before... clearly, the likelihood of them failing at dismemberment or skinning was far higher.

Of course, considering the number of monsters inside the Misty Ring, a few failures wouldn't be a problem for Rei.

Failing to dismember or skin them would naturally mean those materials couldn't be sold... but then again, in Rei's case, who preferred hunting thieves, he wasn't short on money at all.

Even so, he had no intention of willingly reducing his available funds.

"Um, if possible, I'd prefer if you could handle that part later..."

Lenora, seemingly thinking the same thing as Rei, spoke up.

The combat strength of Rei, Set, and Vihera—two humans and one beast—was extremely important to Gilm.

With enemies... multiple groups of monsters present, he wanted Rei to be ready to move at any moment.

Rei understood this and nodded in response.

"That's the plan. But... if possible, I'd like to take the request to eliminate the Orc Leader's pack."

For Rei, orcs were literally delicious prey.

Some of the orc meat from their previous raid on an orc settlement still remained in the Misty Ring.

However, Rei and Set ate enormous amounts.

And Rei generously offered his cooking to other adventurers, naturally including plenty of orc meat in those dishes.

This meant the orc meat was consumed rapidly, and the remaining supply in the Misty Ring was never abundant.

That's precisely why, honestly, she wanted to restock now while orcs were appearing in large numbers like this.

"Orcs, you say? They're about as strong as Lizardmen... though, no, that goes without saying for someone like Rei or Vihera, doesn't it?"

In terms of raw ability, lizardmen and orcs are on par.

However, among adventurers who have fought orcs, it's by no means uncommon for some to find them tougher than lizardmen.

The reason lies purely in the orcs' lust for women.

That desire fuels their motivation, making them tougher than usual.

...Which also means they become weaker if no women are present.

And female adventurers are fewer in number compared to males.

Of course, it's not extremely scarce, but men still vastly outnumber women.

Anyway, Rei had a companion named Vihera, and to the orcs, she was undoubtedly the ultimate prey.

"Heh heh, if you're going to swarm me, I'll welcome it, you know? Well, an Orc General or something like that would be fine."

"No, I mean, the only high-ranking ones showing up in this mess are the leaders. The one leading the orcs is an Orc Leader, right?"

Rei replied to Vihera's words with an exasperated tone.

Hearing this, Vihera murmured regretfully, "Oh, right..."

"Anyway, about that request to exterminate the orc horde..."

"Unfortunately, there aren't any right now. There was one orc group a little while ago, but Elk and the others took care of it."

"...Elk?"

Rei was slightly surprised to hear the name of a familiar Rank A adventurer.

He hadn't seen Elk in a while, but that was because he hadn't been in Gilm—he'd been treating Elk's son, Rhodes.

During the civil war that erupted in the Bestir Empire, Rodos had ultimately fallen into a coma.

To treat him, Elk and Min had each done everything within their power, keeping their accepted quests to an absolute minimum.

They used the time they gained to travel to various towns seeking treatment.

"So you're back."

Vihera murmured beside Rei.

Vihera wasn't entirely unrelated to Elk either.

In fact, Rhodes held strong affection for Vihera, and the fact that Vihera harbored feelings for Rei... this situation had not been insignificant in influencing Rhodes' current circumstances.

"No, he's not in Gilm anymore. After Elk single-handedly wiped out the orc horde, he took his reward and left immediately."

"He sure is busy."

"I heard there might be someone in the capital who could restore Lord Rhodes' consciousness, so he probably headed there."

"I see. I hope Lord Rhodes regains consciousness soon."

Rei and Rhodes weren't exactly close.

In fact, quite the opposite—because of the Vihera incident, he was even jealous of him.

Still, Rei didn't dislike Rodos that much.

"That's right. Elk and the others are highly skilled adventurers."

Lenora murmured thoughtfully, as if agreeing with Rei's words.

Indeed, for the guild, the Rank A party Axe of the Thunder God was an extremely reliable presence.

Gilm had other Rank A adventurers and those with nicknames.

But most of those types had one quirk or two, making Elk exceptionally dependable in that regard.

Of course, possessing high skill was a given.

"Anyway, I'm praying for an orc extermination request to come in,"

With that, they reported on this quest and the assistance given to the adventurers, collected their reward, and Rei and Vihera left the guild.
