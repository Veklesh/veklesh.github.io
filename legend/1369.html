Amidst the soft, spring-like sunlight and a gentle breeze carrying warmth... a loud voice echoes through Treant Forest.

"It's gonna fall!"

With those words, the sound of creaking wood echoed around them... and soon, it began to fall.

The woodcutters and adventurers near the tree quickly moved back to avoid being caught in the fall.

The area where the woodcutters and adventurers stood, close to the roadside, was still uncovered by trees.

From the outer edges of the forest, a large number of woodcutters and adventurers were currently engaged in felling trees.

The outer forest was quite spacious; even with over fifty lumberjacks and adventurers gathered, it didn't feel cramped at all.

In fact, there was still plenty of room to spare.

"Even if we cut these down, new trees will grow back tomorrow, right? This forest is like heaven for woodcutters."

"I get it, I get it. Plus, there aren't any monsters in this forest... or even animals, for that matter."

"Plus, we don't even have to dry this wood, right? ...That's a huge help."

"Yeah, didn't Graeme have his cut wood stolen by goblins before?"

The woodcutter called Graeme nodded bitterly.

"Yeah, that's right. All that wood I cut... I went back the next day, and the goblins had chopped off the branches to make clubs and stuff, or were just cutting the tree for fun... Ugh, just remembering it makes me furious!"

As he explained, his anger flared, and Graeme gripped the handle of his axe tightly.

It was good wood, undoubtedly high-quality timber that would fetch a good price, and now it was ruined. His irritation was understandable.

"Calm down, now. Look, at least in this Treant Forest, there aren't any thieves stealing cut wood. Besides, the adventurers will haul the logs away for you, right?"

As the woodcutter tried to calm the irritated Graeme, another woodcutter who had been observing the situation nearby spoke up.

"Yeah, I know that guy. Rei, right? I've seen him around Gilm plenty of times."

"Heh heh, I've even petted Rei's familiar, Set, you know?"

"I've fed him jerky!"

Somehow, the conversation about Rei had devolved into a competition about who had fed Set the most food.

Realizing this was getting out of hand, one of the woodcutters shouted loudly.

"Hey, cut the chatter! We'll take a break, then move on to the next tree! We only work while the sun's up!"

Obeying the command, the chatting woodcutters headed for the next tree.

Though, to be precise, the "next tree" was the one right next to the fallen one.

And just as the sound of axes striking tree trunks echoed around them... Slesha and Runo were in the shallower part of Treant Forest.

"Looking around like this, there really aren't any monsters or animals in sight. But..."

Runo fixed her gaze intently on the tree growing right before her.

With her magic-seeing eyes, Runo could confirm that the trees growing in this Treant Forest contained a slight amount of magic.

Even to Runo's eyes, it was clear these trees were not ordinary.

Furthermore, the location where Runo and Slesha were situated was near the road... not too far from where the Third Eye had vanished.

However, even with Runo's magic eye, she couldn't sense the peculiarity of this Treant Forest.

To be precise, she could sense it, but it would be more accurate to say she couldn't pinpoint the source of that strangeness.

"Runo, is something wrong?"

Slesha, bow in hand and ready to react at a moment's notice, asked, but Runo shook his head.

"No, this forest has its suspicious aspects, but pinpointing exactly where... well, I find it a bit difficult to pinpoint."

"I see... Even you can't pinpoint it, Runo-san..."

Slesha murmurs, looking disappointed.

She had hoped that Runo, with his magic-seeing magic eyes, might uncover the secrets of this Treant Forest... and the secrets of the monster that killed their comrades.

That was the hope she had held.

Before accepting this request, the guild had explained that no monsters had been confirmed in this Treant Forest.

When she heard that, Slesha almost shouted, "That's ridiculous!"... but before she could utter a sound, Runo stopped her.

In truth, that explanation was probably correct.

Had she shouted such a thing upon hearing the explanation, she would undoubtedly have drawn unwanted attention.

That would have undoubtedly caused various problems for future investigations.

Thinking that the reason she and Runo were able to enter Treant Forest together now was because she'd held her tongue during that explanation helped calm some of the anger within Slesha.

Still, even after coming into the forest like this, it was unexpected that there truly wasn't a single monster or animal, just as the guild had explained.

Slesha had heard about Rei's previous investigation of Treant Forest, but she suspected it might have been mere coincidence.

If she could think calmly, she'd realize that while Rei might be an exception, griffins certainly wouldn't overlook monsters. But right now, Slesha had no room to acknowledge that.

"Well, we can't expect to find clues the very first day we arrive, right? Besides, now that we're here, it's clear there really are no monsters or animals. We were hired as guards, but we have plenty of time to investigate."

Of course, the people Runo and the others were guarding weren't just monsters and animals.

While rare around Gilm, the possibility of bandits appearing wasn't entirely zero.

Considering that, they couldn't completely abandon guarding the woodcutter.

(Well, most other adventurers are already with the woodcutter. Considering that, we probably don't need to worry too much about the escort job.)

The more wood they cut, the more income they earn.

I'm a bit worried they might focus too much on that and neglect the woodcutter's protection, but this request has a rank restriction to begin with.

Runo trusted that those eligible to join this quest—adventurers of Rank C or higher—wouldn't do anything foolish like that.

Among the adventurers who accepted this request, some were newcomers to Gilm, but many were also familiar faces.

That very fact gave him confidence that the woodcutters' safety was, at the very least, secured for now.

"Hm?"

Amidst this, Runo suddenly spoke up.

Slesha, who had been counting on Runo's magic eyes, reflexively looked up at the sound of her voice.

Perhaps she had found some kind of clue?

With that thought in mind, she opened her mouth.

"Runo, what is it? Did you perhaps find something..."

"Ah, no. Sorry, Slesha, but it's not what you're hoping for. It's just that Rei has arrived."

Rei's magic power was suppressed by the New Moon Ring, but it wasn't invisible.

And there was no way he could mistake Set's... the griffin's magic.

"I see..."

Disappointed that it wasn't what she'd hoped for, Slesha murmured.

But Runo spoke to Slesha encouragingly.

"Even if we don't notice something, wouldn't Rei or Set notice? They're... well, they're pretty special in all sorts of ways."

Normally, one would praise their skill here.

But...Rei was someone who should be described as peculiar rather than skilled.

Of course. He'd registered as an adventurer in Gilm and climbed to rank B in just a few years, and he even had a nickname.

Such an existence simply couldn't be described as merely skilled.

Not that I have any particular feelings about Rei, mind you.

Well, I do envy him in many ways, but that's about it.

In this regard, Runo was probably quite pragmatic, no matter what.

"I suppose so. Then... should we ask Rei?"

"I think you should. But I don't think you need to ask Rei right away."

"Huh?"

He says to ask Rei, but then says it's okay not to ask.

Slesha tilted her head, confused by what Runo was saying.

Seeing Slesha's expression, Runo spoke again.

"Rei's... well, calling it a 'trait' sounds a bit off, but think about that ability for a moment. Then you'll understand what I mean."

"His ability, you say?"

"Yeah, that's right. Didn't you bring tools belonging to Slesha's comrades? It's fairly well-known that Rei carries an item box. That Rei came here, where the woodcutters gather. That means..."

"Ah! Trees!"

"Looks like you get it. And not in the evening, but right now... It's only been a few hours since work started, yet Rei's already here. That means he plans to come back several more times today."

If Rei were here, he'd probably have let out a surprised gasp upon seeing Runo and Slesha's conversation.

Runo basically works solo, so he doesn't get too involved with others.

That's precisely why, from Rei's perspective, seeing him give even simple advice like this was undoubtedly surprising.

(Though, given how capricious Rei tends to be, even if he said something like "This is the last time today," I suppose I could accept that.)

Runo had known Rei for quite some time now, and they'd had their share of conversations.

That's precisely why she understood Rei's personality fairly well.

Rei would probably come here multiple times today.

(I'd heard from the guild that there was no need to worry about transporting wood, so I figured that was probably the case. Well, either way, if Rei was willing to help, it wasn't a problem for us.)

Even from Runo's perspective, if Rei came, he felt they could handle whatever came up.

"Well then, let's start by greeting Rei. He probably doesn't know we're here escorting the logging operation. If we're going to ask him for anything, we should explain the situation first."

"Ah, yes. Understood. ...But seriously, is Rei really that amazing?"

Runo was surprised by the words that came out of Slesha's mouth.

"Well, you know all about how amazing Rei is, don't you? Knowing that, and still saying something like that... Honestly, what's up with that?"

"Ah, no. I know Rei is amazing. After all, you couldn't earn a nickname like that without being amazing. But it's just... like this..."

Seeing her hesitation, Runo finally understood why Slesha felt that way.

It was a feeling someone living in Gilm... especially someone who had traveled with Rei... would never have.

But Slesha had never seen that side of Rei.

All she'd seen was Rei hanging out with Marina and the others.

If she'd seen him flying on a Set, things might have been a bit different... but this was probably just a series of unfortunate timing issues.

"Right. Well then, shall we go see Rei and say hello? He'll probably be surprised, huh?"

During the war with the Bestir Empire, Runo had seen Rei using the Misty Ring to transport massive amounts of supplies.

In terms of using the Misty Ring, Slesha also had her belongings brought from the Treant Forest, but those items weren't particularly large.

As Runo had seen, the sight of supply goods equivalent to several carriages—no, dozens of carriages—constantly being loaded into and unloaded from the Misty Ring would undoubtedly astonish anyone under normal circumstances.

Especially since Rei had come this time, it was undoubtedly to take away the felled timber... and it was easy to predict that Slesha would be astonished too.

"Is that so?"

Slesha still seemed unclear on the situation, tilting her head slightly as she followed Runo out of Treant Forest.

Then, the sight that met her eyes... the sight of trees vanishing the instant they were touched... left her gaping, eyes wide, utterly speechless.
