That's because, on the way to the guild, they were approached one after another by the owners and attendants of the stalls and food stands along the way.

Originally, Rei and Set were the best customers a shop could hope for in Gilm, meaning they bought a lot of food.

But since Rei had been absent almost the entire winter, those who had been counting on her were left disappointed.

Of course, no shop relied solely on Rei's business to stay afloat, so none were in immediate danger of closing. Still, the loss of expected income must have been significant.

"Whoa, this is crazy. Why are the masters so popular?"

Magita's eyes widened at the sight of Rei and Set being called out to after just a few steps.

Byune stroked Set as he ate bread, skewered meat, and fish, occasionally buying some for herself to eat too.

Meanwhile, Vihera was busy looking after Byune...

"Oh, sis. Are you partying with Rei? Or is it something more?"

"Who knows? Personally, I'd like to be that kind of relationship."

"Ha ha ha. Well, a woman as charming as you, I'm sure Rei would be moved."

The sandwich stall owner said this as he handed Vihera a sandwich.

"Oh, thank you. The cost is..."

"It's on the house. Having a beauty like you as a regular customer will make my shop thrive. Besides, it's wise to stay on good terms with skilled adventurers like you."

Speaking thus, the stall owner treated Vihera properly as an adventurer, not as a dancer or a prostitute.

Vihera smiled at the vendor's attitude.

It wasn't a forced smile, nor the kind used to provoke a man; it was a smile filled with genuine happiness.

Though she wore gauntlets and greaves, most who saw Vihera didn't notice such details. Their eyes were drawn instead to her alluring figure and dazzling beauty.

Of course, Vihera had no intention of changing that, as it served her well in seeking out fights. Still, it was a pleasant surprise to be seen as a proper adventurer.

It was likely the discerning eye of those who gathered at the guild, especially since she conducted her business not far from the guild itself.

"Is that so? Well then, I'll be sure to stop by again. These sandwiches are delicious too."

The sandwich, with its glazed meat and vegetables combining in Vihera's mouth, suited Vihera perfectly.

Pleased with the taste of the sandwich handed to her, Vihera told the shopkeeper so and followed after Rei and the others a little ahead.

Watching her retreating figure, the shopkeeper immediately turned to Rei with a complex expression... one that seemed to mix jealousy and pity.

He understood that if they headed straight to the guild now, it would undoubtedly cause a commotion.

It was practically common knowledge around here that Kenny, the receptionist at the guild, had her sights set on Rei.

Taking a beauty like Vihera there would naturally cause a commotion.

"Well, I guess that's the price of fame when you're out with a beauty like that. Now, I wonder who'll win?"

The shopkeeper muttered, reaching for the sandwich ordered by a passing adventurer.

"...It wasn't that far, yet it took us quite a while."

Vihera remarked with a wry smile. Rei, stroking Set, replied.

"It's been about two months since I came back. Set's popular, so everyone wanted to play with him, right? It was okay since there weren't many kids, but if there had been more, it would've taken a lot longer."

"Grrr..."

Hearing Rei's words, Set purred apologetically.

Hearing this, Rei hurriedly stroked Set's head and spoke.

"Don't worry, I'm not blaming you. I knew from the start you'd be doted on. Besides, I'm no one to talk."

Saying this, Rei shifted his gaze to the Misty Ring in his right hand.

He had purchased a considerable amount of food on the way here.

Since they couldn't carry everything they bought, most of it was stored inside Misty Ring.

He could have forced himself to eat it all, but he didn't want to waste such delicious food.

Many shops had given him extras, happy to see him return. He'd stored items like skewered meats, which cooled quickly, in the Misty Ring. That way, when he felt like eating them, he could take them out and enjoy them as if they were freshly grilled.

Thinking that way, there was absolutely no need to force myself to eat here.

"Set, we'll take care of some business at the guild. Wait here for a bit."

Even as they said this, it was obvious from the several figures lurking nearby that Set wouldn't be bored waiting.

Once Rei left and Set lay down in his usual spot, people would undoubtedly gather to pay him attention immediately.

It was spring, so even those unfamiliar with Set and Rei were gathering, but it was an unspoken rule that those around them would explain the situation.

Just as the senior adventurer had explained to the two adventurers who had tried to approach Vihera.

That's precisely why Rei could leave Set here without worry and head for the guild.

Though Magita's impatient glances were certainly another reason to hurry into the guild.

Upon entering the guild, Rei and the others became the center of attention.

It was daytime, so there weren't many adventurers in the guild, but that only made Rei and the others stand out more.

"Hey, wasn't Rei supposed to be out on a long-distance quest?"

"Didn't he come back because that job was finished?"

"Wait, what? Who's that with Rei? Damn, she's hot. And that kid and that cocky-looking guy?"

"...I don't recognize those faces in Gilm, do I?"

"Bringing a woman that attractive along means some idiot is bound to hit on her. And then, in the end, Rei causes a scene."

"Tch. Lucky bastard... If I could spend a night with a woman like that, I'd pay gold coins... no, platinum coins."

"Idiot, stop it. Rei will get her eye on you."

"It's rare to see Rei with someone."

"Women and kids are fine, but what's with that cocky-looking guy over there?"

"Aren't they in a party? Rei's basically a solo player, but sometimes he takes jobs with a party, right? Like, say, 'Scorching Wind' or something."

"Ah... but why with that guy? He looks pretty weak even among them. He could probably beat that little girl, but judging by her movements, she's probably a thief."

Such voices could be heard from various places.

Rei didn't seem particularly bothered, treating it as usual, and Vihera was the same.

These two tended to attract attention in various ways, so they were already used to this kind of scrutiny.

Byune didn't seem bothered at all by the attention directed at her.

But...

"Who the hell was mocking me?! Come out here!"

Magita, who had a half-baked confidence in his own strength, was a different story.

He understood he couldn't beat Rei or Vihera.

But that was because those two were too strong; he strongly believed he wouldn't lose to any ordinary adventurer.

That might have been acceptable in Subursta, but this was Gilm. The sole frontier of the Mireana Kingdom, a place where many adventurers gathered.

If he had the discernment to gauge others' strength, Magita would have realized most within the guild were stronger than him.

...Yet the adventurers didn't step forward against Magita because they assumed she was Rei's ally.

Rei lightly punched Magita's waist, a move that could be described as a fox borrowing a tiger's might.

Light, yes, but only by Rei's standards.

Magita, struck with an impact as if hit by a club, tried to glare at Rei for doing it... but the instant he met Rei's eyes, a chill ran down his spine.

"I told you, didn't I? There are plenty of people here stronger than you. Even among the adventurers here right now, there are countless who surpass you. Develop the eyes to see through your opponent's strength before you start showing off. ...Vihera, Byune, let's go. Magita, you can stay here and look at the request forms."

With that brief command, Rei left Magita frozen in place and led Vihera and Byune toward the counter.

Of course, waiting at their destination was Lenora, who had practically become Rei's assigned contact.

And beside Lenora, Kenny gazed at Rei with a complex expression—part joy, part sorrow.

It was only natural he'd be concerned, seeing Rei—the one he harbored feelings for—bringing along a woman who was clearly more beautiful than him and, on top of that, appeared to be an adventurer.

If Rei had come to the guild alone... or even if he'd brought Magita or Byune, Kenny probably wouldn't have worn such an expression.

Lenora, though not quite as tense as Kenny, offered a slightly forced smile as she addressed Rei.

To Lenora, who thought of Rei like a younger brother, Vihera's beauty made her impossible to fully trust.
