The decisive cry that escaped Daskar's lips.

"WOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOH!"

The roar-like cheers erupted from the spectators' mouths.

They instinctively understood. They grasped the sheer level of skill displayed by the being they had just witnessed.

"Hey, wait... When I saw Rei just now, I felt this insane pressure..."

"Ah, yeah. Me too. And... there was this red air? Or something like that, centered around Rei, right?"

"No doubt about it. Probably one of Rei's skills."

The spectators' murmurs echoed around them, and naturally, those voices reached Keep's ears too.

"I lost...? Me, the next head of the Count of Eriell family, lost to some penniless noble? That's... absurd..."

Keep muttered in a daze.

Unable to believe Zeros had lost, he looked around, wondering if it was all a dream or something. But Zeros remained slumped on the ground.

Looking around, it seemed the knights were keeping their distance from him.

No, worse than that, they seemed to be wearing expressions of mockery.

That mockery was entirely in Keep's imagination, but he showed no sign of realizing it now.

Then, suddenly, he noticed his pants felt cold.

"What?"

Startled by the coldness for the first time, he looked down. There, spread out, was a stain.

On the snow-cleared ground prepared for the duel, urine from Keep's crotch had spread out.

Keep's mind went blank for an instant upon noticing this... and the next moment, he was forcibly pulled to his feet by Fultos.

"Well then, everyone, I'm afraid my master is feeling a bit unwell, so we must take our leave for now. Baron Larks, regarding the duel, we will contact you again at a later date. Is that acceptable?"

"U-uhm. Yeah. That's probably best."

Even to Daskar's eyes, Keep was clearly so terrified he'd wet himself, and seemed dazed and confused, perhaps unable to admit it to himself.

Judging that they couldn't have a proper conversation in this state, Daskar nodded to Fultos' words. But...

"Regardless of what becomes of Lord Keep, the duel was properly settled. As such, the outcome of this duel is legally binding. Naturally, that includes the wagers placed on it. You understand that, correct?"

"Yes! I promise to inform them of this matter without fail."

"...Very well. Then that's settled. You may withdraw."

"Excuse me. ...Hey, let's go!"

Furutos, who had been urging the knights still staring in disbelief at the events that had just unfolded before them, left the scene with Keep in tow.

The knights snapped back to their senses and hurriedly gave chase.

"Honestly... Leaking information like that... It's nothing short of a disgrace to the royalist nobles."

A royalist noble dispatched to Gilm muttered bitterly.

Gilm was the domain ruled by Daskar, a central figure among the neutral faction, and at the same time, it was a frontier.

In other words, it was a place where anything could happen at any moment.

It was entirely possible that hordes of monsters could attack at any moment.

Given that they were being sent to such a place, it was only natural that those chosen were generally the boldest among the nobles, whether they were pro-King or pro-Nobility faction.

...Though, admittedly, many of those with guts tended to act even more arrogantly and recklessly.

Of course, some lacked that level of courage.

Even so, none of them resorted to the kind of disgraceful antics like that Keep fellow earlier.

"Compared to that... that man Zeros, who managed to stand up like that after seeing Rei up close... he's quite impressive."

At the muttered words of one noble, the others nearby turned admiring glances toward Zeros, who was attempting to rise.

Facing that Zeros, Rei slipped on the New Moon Ring and spoke.

"So, what are you going to do now?"

"Huh? What do you mean, what am I going to do?"

Zeros, muttering while holding his halberd, tilted his head, unsure what Rei was talking about.

"What do you mean... what? You lost to me, didn't you? That makes you the one who brought ruin upon the Count of Eriell's house. If things go badly, they might send assassins after you."

"Ha! Assassins sent by that guy? They're nothing but trash to me, trash. It'd be different if there was a monster like you around... But there isn't, right?"

As if his own words made him uneasy, Zeros asked again, seeking confirmation.

But Rei didn't understand the details of the situation either, so all he could do was shrug his shoulders slightly.

"Hmm. If you're worried, maybe you should head to another city. The capital's obviously under tight surveillance by the king's faction. Why not try somewhere out in the sticks?"

It was Daskar, who had been watching their exchange, who spoke up.

"Another town, you say?"

Zeros tilted his head in question.

Zeros had intended to return to Avelo as planned.

Staying in Gilm was an option, but he didn't want to leave Abuero, where his lover was.

That's precisely why Daskar's suggestion—to go to a different town, neither Gilm nor Abuero—was completely unexpected.

"That keep shouldn't be a problem, but sooner or later, someone connected to the Count of Eriell's household will come to Gilm. If you show your face then, whether it's in Abuero or Gilm, it won't be amusing for them."

"So they'll send assassins?"

"Well, that depends on luck. If the one coming to Gilm is a loyalist, they might send one. But wouldn't it be better for you to avoid danger?"

"If it were just my problem, I wouldn't mind."

Zeros pondered Daskar's words for a moment, then sighed and looked up.

Beneath a dazzling blue sky, unseasonably bright for winter, Zeros's heart remained anything but clear.

"Ugh, what a pain. How did things even get this messed up?"

"Well, that's because you take on jobs from guys like that without thinking,"

Rei muttered, as if to say it was his own fault.

"Ha ha ha. Well, don't say that. Anyway, I've arranged a room for you during this festival. Go rest up there."

Zeros breathed a sigh of relief at Daskar's words.

The time he'd spent facing off against Rei, who had deployed his Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, hadn't been long.

But even in that short time, Zeros's stamina had been severely depleted.

He might appear fine now, but Zeros honestly felt it would be impossible to continue fighting someone else if asked to do so right now.

Compared to Zeros, Rei shook his head at Daskar without showing any particular sign of fatigue.

"No, I'd rather enjoy the festival. Besides..."

He stopped speaking and turned his gaze to Muet, Baslero, and Asie, who were all delighted.

True, he had won today's duel, meaning Ashié wouldn't be taken by Keep.

Half of the Eriell family's fortune was staked on this duel. He couldn't possibly imagine them handing it over quietly.

He had attacked the Baron Sysne family several times before the duel, failing each time.

But knowing he truly had no way out, he wasn't the sort to simply give up.

In fact, if Muet and Basler were both dead, the wager would be null and void.

More accurately, without someone to receive it, the wager itself would cease to exist.

That was precisely why he deliberately removed the New Moon Ring as a warning, searing his presence into their minds.

Even if none of Keep's subordinates possessed the ability to sense magic, it was certain that among the nobility, someone with that kind of ability had been hired.

Though they had been adversaries, Set showed no sign of disliking Zeros.

At the very least, compared to his attitude toward Rodos, it was like night and day.
