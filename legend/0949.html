Sunlight streams through the window toward the bed.

That sunlight naturally fell upon Rei's face as she slept in bed, urging her awake whether she liked it or not.

"Hm... Huh?"

Rei woke up with an indistinct sound, but stopped moving as she sat up in bed.

After staring blankly for a few minutes, she stretched widely with a sleepy expression, then got out of bed and began getting ready.

It was still morning. Only a few hours had passed since the battle with Stigma ended and he finished explaining the situation to Erinde.

Or rather, it was more accurate to say he had managed to sleep for a few hours.

Normally, after surviving such a fierce battle, nerves would be frayed and sleep impossible. Yet Rei had slept soundly, as if none of that mattered.

"Um, this place... Ah, right."

He remembered that the room he'd been given had been rendered unusable during Stigma's attack, and that this room was one of the on-duty rooms located not far from the Headmaster's office.

"...So, what am I supposed to do now? Breakfast at the staff dorm? Greet Erinde when she wakes up? Surely I can't just go straight to class like this..."

Muttering to himself, he glanced out the window. It was clearly too early for classes to be starting.

It wasn't exactly early morning, but it was a bit earlier than his usual wake-up time.

He had fallen asleep immediately upon arriving in this room a few hours ago, and as he pondered what to do next, a knock suddenly sounded at the door.

For a moment, Rei thought it might be Stigma again, but he dismissed the idea—enemies wouldn't bother knocking before entering. He granted permission to enter.

"Excuse me, Rei. I am Sin, a maid ordered by Lady Erinde to attend to you. Though it will be a short time, I will do my utmost to ensure you lack for nothing."

The maid bowed deeply to Rei and brought a cart laden with breakfast into the room.

Lifting the metal lid, designed to keep out dust and debris, revealed a simple breakfast: bread, soup, salad, and sausage.

Even if it was a simple breakfast, it was by no means a poor one.

Despite it being winter, there was a salad with fresh greens.

The bread and soup were also dishes prepared by top chefs using carefully selected ingredients.

The aroma of the soup and the toasty scent wafting from the freshly baked bread were incredibly appetizing.

"Please. This is the academy director's treasured tea."

The tea offered to Rei as he sat down at the table carried a faint, fruity aroma.

Rei didn't know much about the finer points of tea, but even so, if it was Erinde's treasured tea, he couldn't help but think it might cost as much as gold or silver.

"Are you sure? Since it's a treasured blend, there probably isn't much of it, right?"

"Yes. Since this matter caused you so much trouble, Lord Rei, it's meant to appease you."

"...Is that okay?"

The same words came out of Rei's mouth again, but their meaning was vastly different.

The first "Is it okay?" meant "Is it okay to splurge on tea?" The second "Is it okay?" meant "Is it okay to say it's to appease him, even if it's true?"

But the maid smiled at Rei's words and nodded in response.

"Yes, it's fine. The Headmaster instructed me to say exactly that if asked."

"That's quite blatant..."

Even as he said this, Rei was grateful to Erinde.

After all, she had summoned S-rank adventurer Sardat to protect him.

...Though, to be honest, Rei felt Saldat was a questionable choice for a bodyguard.

Given that the royalist faction had effectively cornered the S-rank adventurers, it was undoubtedly cheaper for other factions to hire Saldat than to compete for them, both in terms of rewards and various conditions.

Still, being Rank S meant they weren't exactly easy to hire.

(Though, in Saldat's case, he seemed like the type who'd take on a request immediately if asked. Especially if asked by a woman, he'd probably accept right away. ...Not that it was absolutely guaranteed, though.)

After neatly finishing breakfast, the maid promptly cleared the table.

Rei watched her, but before the maid exited the room, he gave a slight bow and spoke.

"Lady Erinde requested that you come to the Headmaster's office once you've finished breakfast and settled down."

"Erinde? ...Understood. I'll head to the Headmaster's office shortly."

"Yes, I'll relay that message."

After seeing the maid off as she carried the cart out of the room, Rei sipped the last cup of tea she had poured and contemplated what lay ahead.

Originally, Rei had come to Graciall to avoid getting caught up in unnecessary trouble related to the royalist faction's disciplinary crackdown.

Many believed this latest crackdown was Rei's fault.

Even those who understood he wasn't the cause might still take out their frustration on Rei.

Such actions would only further sink the already declining prestige of the royalist faction.

Beyond the major blunders—the disgraceful performance in last spring's war against the Bestir Empire and the missed opportunity to fully capitalize on the Bestir Empire's civil war—there was also the arrogant behavior of the nobles belonging to the King's faction, the largest faction in the Mireana Kingdom.

That was precisely why the crackdown on discipline was necessary. To prevent Rei from being caught up in it, he had been invited to Graciall, the stronghold of the Duke Quent family—a key figure within the royalist faction—under the pretext of a request. Yet the result was the Holy Light Church running amok.

From the perspective of Duke Quent and his faction, it could only be called a major blunder.

The fortunate thing for Duke Quent and his faction was that Rei did not view this failure as Quent's failure.

Had Rei insisted the blame lay with Duke Quent, even someone with his considerable influence within the royalist faction would have suffered a significant loss of standing.

That said, from Rei's perspective, the Holy Light Church attacking him was the result of the events in the Labyrinth City of Exil. It was a case of reaping what they sowed... well, that might be a slight exaggeration, but he felt something close to that.

"Well, whatever happens, this whole thing will probably work itself out somehow. ...Right? The Holy Light Church aside."

He muttered, finishing the last sip of his tea before standing up.

After quickly tidying himself, he exited the room.

The room where Rei was staying wasn't particularly far from the Headmaster's office.

He made his way through the largely deserted school building until a familiar door came into view.

"Erinde, it's Rei. Is that okay?"

He knocked and called out, and was immediately told to come in.

Entering the room as usual, two scenes unfolded before him: one as expected, and one unexpected.

The expected sight was Erinde and Saldato inside the principal's office.

Both looked exhausted, likely busy cleaning up after last night's incident.

...Then again, it was only natural they'd struggle to contain such a commotion. After all, it had erupted in the middle of the night within the academy itself.

Since Rei hadn't used flashy magic, the commotion hadn't spread to the student dorms. But hiding everything within the staff quarters where Rei had been staying was impossible.

It was only natural. Many former adventurers lived in the staff quarters.

Amidst such people, he'd broken through a window to escape outside under the same roof, and even though it was some distance from the dormitory, he'd spread flames across the ground.

Quite a few noticed the Sanctuary Vicente had used.

Most understood it was some kind of unknown phenomenon, but few grasped that it was caused by a power beyond mere magic.

In any case, it was far too much commotion for a single night. Hiding everything and trying to calm things down was nearly impossible, yet Erinde and Saldat still had to... well, mainly Erinde had to run around trying to quell the disturbance.

Rei wondered if they might have had a bit more breathing room if they'd spent the time interrogating him and Saldat, or lecturing them, on actually resolving the situation. But he kept that thought to himself, knowing voicing it would likely earn him another lecture.

And then, an unexpected sight.

There, seated on the guest sofa in the Headmaster's office, were two figures.

One was a girl who looked barely ten years old. Rei recognized her face.

But someone like her being here this early in the morning was highly unusual—Marka Quent.

Standing behind the sofa as her guard was Coan, a former Rank A adventurer and Marka's bodyguard.

The other was Iskeld, the commander of the knight order under Duke Quent. He was the one who had come to greet Rei when he arrived at Graciall, but since Rei had spent most of his time at the officer academy afterward, they hadn't met again.

And finally, the last one. Sitting next to Marka and looking toward Rei was a man in his fifties.

He was clearly physically fit, but it was obvious he wasn't a top-tier powerhouse.

Yet, simply by being in the same room, he radiated an overwhelming presence.

That Rei showed no signs of tension in front of such an opponent was likely because he had seen others with similar presence—Daskar, who possessed high combat prowess as a knight, and above all, the Emperor of the Bestir Empire, Trajist, with his own eyes.

Compared to Trajist's overwhelming presence, the pressure this man sitting on the sofa was unconsciously radiating was nothing to speak of.

The atmosphere emanating from that middle-aged man... and above all, the fact that Marka sat beside him and Iskeld stood behind him as his guard, made it clear who this person was.

(This man is...)

The Royalist faction, whose influence far surpassed that of the Nobles and the Centrists.

A major figure within the largest faction of the Mireana Kingdom, the one effectively carrying out the Royalist faction's disciplinary crackdown this time.

After glancing at the figure for just an instant, Rei turned back toward Erinde and spoke.

"You called me out so early in the morning... is this about yesterday?"

Erinde let out a wry smile at the words spoken by Rei, who had lowered his hood to reveal his face.

Erinde and Sardat had been working tirelessly since last night to resolve the situation.

To someone like Erinde, who hadn't slept at all, calling him early in the morning... well, she could only say she envied Rei for having slept.

"Yes. Things were quite hectic on our end, but it seems you managed to get at least some sleep, Rei."

Even after pulling an all-nighter, Erinde remained as androgynous as ever.

Hearing Erinde's words, Rei couldn't help but smile.

"I'm a bit sleep-deprived, but I won't complain. ...So, you called for me..."

He paused, sat down on the sofa in the Headmaster's office, and stared intently at Rei, as if sizing him up.

It was immediately clear to Rei that this man had summoned him for a specific reason.

Still, he addressed Erinde first. Partly because she was the one who had summoned him here, but more importantly, because the middle-aged man hadn't been formally introduced to Erinde yet.

The fact that he'd heard beforehand that this man was very particular about etiquette probably played a big part too.

Had he ignored Erinde, who had summoned him here, and addressed the man directly, he would undoubtedly have caused offense.

"Ah, yes, just as you suspected. ...To be honest, I thought it would be a little longer before I could introduce you to Rei. Rei, the gentleman over there is His Grace Ronald Quent, Duke of Quent, the ruler of this Duchy of Quent."

Ronald finally rose from the sofa upon hearing his name spoken by Erinde and approached Rei, opening his mouth.

"So you are Rei. As just introduced, I am Ronald Quent. I've caused you quite a bit of trouble. I truly apologize."

With that, he extended his hand toward Rei, offering a handshake.
