The room Rei rents at the Wheat Inn at dusk.

A voice filled with suppressed laughter echoes there.

'Heh heh, I see. While I'm absolutely thrilled to see you, Rei... did you finally remove the seeds of caution from that party called Scorching Wind?'

“Yeah. Just today, actually. Of course, it went off without a hitch.”

‘So, you'll remove them for me and Ara too, right?’

Rei nodded in response to Elena's questioning look.

“Like I said before, Set's skills were seen by many during the battle against Noise. The Seeds of Warning are dangerous in many ways.”

‘True. But what should we do? It's a bit difficult for me to come to you right now. With the year drawing to a close, things are about to get busy in the Kevelar Duchy too. That leaves only one option: for you to come here... but that's also problematic.’

“I suppose so.”

Hearing Elena's words, Rei's expression showed understanding.

He understood that things would indeed get busy as the year drew to a close, and he knew that if he went there, it would inevitably cause a commotion.

‘Especially since it's the time of year when snow could fall at any moment. If things go badly, I might even get trapped in Gilum.’

This was another reason he couldn't help but nod in agreement.

After listening to Elena and thinking for a moment, Rei finally spoke.

“In that case, how about coming to Gilum next spring...? Or maybe I should go to Anesis, where Elena's house is? I could go there, but it'd probably cause a commotion too.”

Rei himself wasn't particularly conspicuous.

Well, to be precise, wearing his dragon robe and hood, he looked like any other novice mage you'd find anywhere.

But of course, Set was always by Rei's side. And heading to the territory ruled by Duke Kevel, a central figure among the nobles, meant getting tangled up with the nobility was practically guaranteed.

Considering all that, it was still best for Elena to come to Gilum with Ara.

And above all else...

“I hear Vihera will be coming to Gilum next spring too, so that makes three. If we bring Buune along, forming a party of four might be kind of interesting, don't you think? Wait, with Ara included, that makes five.”

'Hmm, I see. That might indeed be interesting. Meeting Vihera feels a bit awkward, but I'd like to see Buune again after so long.'

Seeing Elena still doting on Buune like always, Rei thought, Maybe that's actually a pretty good idea?

Vihera and Aara as frontline fighters, Buune as a thief, Rei and Elena who can attack regardless of distance. Set, a griffin who can fly through the air.

Though, Set isn't the only one who can fly. Rei and Elena both wear the Sleipnir's Shoes, magic items that let them walk through the air.

That means they can dash through the sky, albeit only for a few steps.

As a party, they possess abilities that rival not just Rank B, but even Rank A.

Rei's honest thought was that adventuring with such a party sounded rather fun.

“Heh heh.”

Seeing Rei imagining himself active as part of a party, Elena beyond the crystal let out a small smile.

“What is it?”

‘No, I just thought Rei might have some longing for a party too.’

Hearing Elena's words, Rei wore a displeased expression, feeling slighted.

“I didn't choose to go solo out of preference, you know? I only did it because of the whole Set thing. But now that I don't need to hide Set's skills anymore... In that sense, it's true joining a party has become easier.”

‘That's true. However... Unfortunately, I think becoming an adventurer is difficult for me.’

“Ah...”

Elena's words finally reminded Rei of the position of the person on the other side of the crystal ball.

She was the one known by the epithet “Princess General,” a figure who could be called the symbol of the noble faction.

No matter how you looked at it, it was impossible for her to actively work as an adventurer.

Rei, who had hoped she could act as part of a party, wore an even more disappointed expression. Elena gave a small smile and continued speaking.

“Well, yes. It wouldn't be a formal party, but if you mean accompanying me, I think I could spare some time. ...Though, naturally, my work will get quite demanding before and after heading your way.”

“Really? Then I'm looking forward to spring. ...Maybe we could even tackle Exigil's dungeon. Ah, or perhaps conquer the dungeon with the Altar of Succession. Back then, we never really got to the core of that place, after all.”

“Heh, true.”

Both of them had bitter memories of what happened back then... bitter memories indeed. Yet, here they were, able to talk like this now.

“Still, the floors with Water Monkeys are a pain because of their sheer numbers.”

“But this time, unlike then, we don't have to hide Set's skills, so it shouldn't be that bad...”

Just as Elena said that,

As if timed perfectly, a knock echoed through the door.

Before Rei could answer, the door swung open and a voice rang out.

“Ray-kun, I finally escaped from that flat-chested girl! Honestly, Renola, being so naggy like that, no wonder she can't even get a single boyfriend...”

The person barging into the room was Kenny.

Kenny smiled at Rei's presence in the room, but the next moment, he noticed the crystal resting on the table near Rei.

Well, if that were all, it wouldn't have been a problem.

But the fact that the crystal reflected the image of a dignified beauty changed everything.

The guild had orbs for communicating with other guilds, and Kenny had seen them used for business purposes several times.

That's precisely why he understood that crystal was a counterpart orb, similar in nature. He was also surprised that someone possessed a pair of orbs, something not typically held by individuals.

...But the biggest problem was undoubtedly the woman reflected in that crystal.

Kenny was confident in his own looks.

It's not that I'm conceited, but the truth is, guild receptionists are hired for their good looks.

Plus, I've had plenty of adventurers hit on me before.

But the one reflected in the crystal is undeniably more beautiful than me—even Kenny himself would admit it.

“...Rei, who is that?”

“Look, she barged into the room without permission... Fine, whatever. This one is...”

Ray was about to introduce Elena, but a voice from beyond the crystal cut him off.

'Rei, stop. Let me introduce myself.'

Elena's gaze through the crystal was sharp.

She had known of Kenny's existence long before.

When she sent Yello to Gilum during winter, Yello encountered Kenny and saw those memories.

That's precisely why she understood exactly who the Kenny standing near Rei now was. And his feelings.

“I am Elena Kever. It feels odd to say it myself, but if I mention the Princess General, would you know who I mean?”

“Huh!?”

At those words, Kenny gasped.

Given her position as the guild receptionist, she naturally obtained various information and had more opportunities to see things than ordinary people.

Precisely because of that, when the woman on the other side of the crystal... Elena... introduced herself, Kenny instantly recognized her face as matching the person in question.

“...My apologies, Lady Elena. I am Kenny, working at the Gilmu Guild.”

‘Ah, I know Kenny. And his relationship with Rei.’

“...”

With that single sentence, Kenny understood.

The figure reflected in the crystal was indeed the Duchess's daughter, the one with the nickname.

But that didn't matter now. It was irrelevant.

The person standing before him now wasn't that figure... but his enemy, his rival in love.

Elena must have judged that Kenny understood their relationship. She turned her gaze toward Rei.

“Ray, I'm sorry, but could you leave me alone with this Kenny fellow for a moment?”

“Are you serious?”

It was a question laden with several meanings.

For someone of the Duchess's standing, someone with a notorious reputation, to request a private conversation... especially in Rei's room.

If anything went wrong here, it would undoubtedly bring disaster upon Kenny.

Ray shot Elena a slightly sharp look, wondering if she understood that.

But it wasn't Elena who reacted to Rei's words—it was Kenny.

Of course, Kenny understood he couldn't act carelessly with the duchess's daughter... especially one considered a symbol of the aristocratic faction.

Even so, he couldn't back down now.

“Ray, please. I also have something I need to discuss with Elena-sama. ...Right. Once I'm done with my business, I'll come get you. Could you kill some time downstairs in the dining hall?”

At those words, Rei's gaze shifted toward Elena, hesitant.

In terms of status, Elena was overwhelmingly superior.

That's precisely why I thought I had to stop Elena if she insisted on pushing herself too hard. But if Kenny wants this too, I can't stop Rei.

In that case, all Rei could do now was ask Elena not to make things worse.

Under Rei's gaze, Elena gave a small nod and opened her mouth.

'Don't worry. There's no way I'd do anything to her over this. Just... well. Let's just say we have some girl talk.'

“Got it. ...Well, I'm getting a bit peckish. I'll head down to the cafeteria on the first floor.”

Saying that, Rei left the room.

It was careless to the extreme, considering it was his own room, but Rei trusted Kenny.

Besides, there was hardly any luggage in this room to begin with.

A few minutes passed after Rei left the room. Surprisingly, Kenny was the first to speak.

“Honestly, Rei-kun, shouldn't you be a little suspicious?”

‘Rei is ruthless towards enemies, but once he decides someone is an ally, he becomes endlessly lenient.’

“Is that based on personal experience?”

'Yes. But considering Rei's personality, wouldn't you know him better than I do?'

“Well, I can't deny that. When he first came to register with the guild, I heard he provoked a party that was getting picked on, dragged them into a fight, and then took their money and weapons.”

'Hmph, typical Rei. ...Well, enough preamble. You know why I wanted to talk to you, don't you?'

After a moment's silence, Kenny nodded at Elena's words.

“Of course. It's about Rei, isn't it?”

'That's right. ...Considering Rei's personality and abilities, his background, and the fact he has Set as his familiar, he's bound to get caught up in all sorts of trouble from now on. When that happens, I don't want anyone who might hold him back near him. ...No, that's an excuse. Let me be honest. I cannot accept you being by Rei's side. Emotionally speaking, too."

Elena could foresee Rei taking several wives in the future.

Even so, she held the belief that she would be his primary wife. While she might accept someone like Vihera, whom she had approved, the idea of someone she didn't know well entering that role was not something she welcomed.

Kenny sensed a momentary flicker of hesitation in Elena's words, but he spoke up immediately nonetheless.

“I'm sorry, but I cannot comply with your request, Elena-sama. My relationship with Rei is important to me as well.”

'Hmm... I see. I suppose so. But are you sure? It feels strange to say this myself, but I wield considerable influence. Doesn't that worry you?'

It wasn't so much a threat as a statement meant to confirm Kenny's resolve, but the addressee suppressed his inner flinch and nodded in response.

“Of course I feel uneasy. However, I've come to believe that someone who has been called a Princess General... someone who holds affection for Rei-kun... would never do such a thing.”

“...I think it's precisely because you care for Rei that you might take such actions?”

“Someone who intends to do such a thing wouldn't say something like that.”

“Hmm.”

Elena murmured in apparent admiration at Kenny's words.

Her eyes held a light that seemed to acknowledge Kenny's existence more than before.

“I see. Working for a guild in a remote place like Gilum, you've certainly got some guts. But... that alone won't be enough for me to accept you staying by Rei's side.”

“Elena-sama. I'm sorry, but who stays by Rei-kun's side is something Rei-kun himself decides. I don't believe it's something you should decide.”

“...Heh heh, you're quite right. Perhaps I was a bit too hasty.”

Elena's laughter echoed through the room... and the heavy atmosphere from before vanished.

In the end, it was about thirty minutes later that Kenny called for Rei, who was in the dining hall. What was discussed during that time remained unknown to Rei.
