"Hmm, I suppose this will do. …Once it’s turned to ash, at least it won’t get trampled on."

Elena murmurs as she looks at the stone in front of her.

Beneath that stone lies the black wolf’s corpse, buried in a state of ashes.

There is no snow in the vicinity; the ground is simply wet.

It wasn’t hard to figure out why things had turned out this way. It was simply due to the heat generated when Rei used his magic to burn the black wolf’s corpse.

"That’s true. In the depths of the mountains like this, no one usually comes around, so the black wolf should be able to rest in peace."

That said, it’s not impossible that adventurers or hunters might come this far in search of prey or to gather materials.

Even so, they probably wouldn’t go to the trouble of moving stones—or, in some cases, boulders—large enough to be called rocks, just to dig up the ground.

Rei and Elena looked at the boulder—one so massive they hesitated to call it a gravestone.

Ara watched the two of them from a short distance away, enduring the cold.

It was the middle of winter… and they were deep in the mountains. Even with winter gear, they couldn’t completely block out the cold, of course, and the chill was slowly seeping into their bodies.

Still, the sun was out, and they could feel the warmth of its rays, so for now, they were managing to endure it.

“Grrrrrrrrr!”

Set, who was supposed to be playing off in the distance, let out that sound as he flapped his wings and descended.

Hanging from his front leg was a deer.

How on earth did Set, who had been playing with Yellow, manage to catch a deer?

Rei had a slight doubt about that, but when he thought, “Well, it’s Set,” he found himself convinced.

“Grr!”

“Kyu!”

Set and Yellow, who was riding on his back, let out cries as if to boast, “Aren’t we amazing?!”

“Good job. But… it’d be a bit of a hassle to butcher the deer right here, so I’m going to store it in the Misty Ring for now. Is that okay?”

“Grr?”

A questioning meow escapes Set’s mouth, wondering why Rei isn’t butchering the deer here, but upon seeing Rei’s expression, he must have realized Rei had no intention of doing so here, and Set says nothing more.

That said, Yellow, who was riding on Set’s back, let out a slightly discontented growl.

……But even Yellow stopped voicing his discontent once Ara, who had been watching from a short distance away, came over and picked him up.

Rei touched the deer carcass lying on the ground and stored it in the Misty Ring.

(Come to think of it… though it’s really a bit late to mention this now… ever since the seal on the Misty Ring was lifted, I’ve been able to use it normally.)

I was surprised when the Misty Ring was sealed by that flesh cocoon, but after defeating it, I’ve been able to use the Misty Ring normally.

Since I’ve used the Misty Ring countless times since that battle ended, it feels a bit belated to be thinking about it now.

「Rei？」

Seeing Rei like that, Elena calls out to him in puzzlement.

Rei shook his head at Elena’s words to indicate it was nothing, then spoke.

“We can’t stay here forever, so I think it’s about time we headed down the mountain. It’s still quite a distance to Gilm, after all.”

“Hmm. …Still, it’s amazing they found a place to lower the Set Cage down into a mountain like this.”

As Elena murmured thoughtfully, there was indeed a spot deep within these mountains where, for some reason, no trees grew.

It was completely invisible from the ground, but to someone flying through the air, it was a spot so distinct that it was impossible to miss.

When Rei first spotted it, he was a bit suspicious, but since it seemed like a good spot, he decided to land there… and in the end, there wasn’t anything particularly special about it; he was able to use it just fine.

However, it was hard to believe that such a spot had formed naturally.

I suspect someone created it intentionally, but whether that someone was a human, a beastman, an elf, or perhaps one of the other rare races… or, depending on the circumstances, a monster or an animal.

The details remained unclear, but there was no doubt that Rei and the others were saved by being able to use it for the time being.

Upon arriving at the treeless clearing, Rei took the Set Cage out of his Misty Ring.

“Hmm, having this Set Cage… it goes without saying that it’s incredibly convenient. If we didn’t have it, who knows how long it would have taken to travel between Anesis and Gilm.”

“That’s true. Especially now that it’s winter—with snow falling and the roads frozen—and above all, the cold really takes a toll on the horses.”

Elena and Ara murmur thoughtfully as they look at the Set Cage.

For the people of this world, Elgin, flying through the sky is a privilege reserved for only a select few, such as dragon riders.

For that very reason, the fact that Rei and Set can fly through the sky—and even transport multiple people using the Set Cage—can only be described as beyond the realm of common sense to the people of this world.

Hearing their words, Rei stroked Set.

“But we can only do all this because Set is here. …Right, Set?”

“Grr… Grrrrrrrr…”

As Rei speaks these words while petting him, Set purrs happily.

As if to cool Rei and the others down, a cold wind blows around them.

...Though, among this group, the only one who feels the cold is Ara.

Rei is wearing a Dragon Robe that acts as a makeshift air conditioner, and for Elena, thanks to having inherited the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone, this level of cold is nothing.

As for Set and Yellow, they can sleep outside just fine even in much colder weather.

Perhaps noticing Ara shivering slightly from the cold, Elena tells Rei it’s time to get going.

“Rei, let’s head out soon. It gets chilly in the mountains, and I want to find a place to stay for the night early… plus, we’ve got to butcher the deer Set hunted, right?”

“Hm? Well, if Elena says so, I guess we should. There’s no point in staying here forever anyway.”

Rei hadn’t noticed Ara’s condition, but figuring there was no point in staying here forever, he nodded at Elena’s words.

Once they’re ready, Elena, Ara, and Yellow climb into the Set Cage, while Rei climbs onto Set’s back.

“Alright, I’ll be counting on you a little more today.”

"Grr!"

As he was stroked on the back of the neck, Set purred happily and began to run.

After a few steps of a running start, he flapped his wings and soared into the sky.

He circled widely in the air, then descended toward the Set Cage placed on the ground… and in the next instant, Set grabbed the Cage and flew back up into the sky.

Perched on Set’s back, Rei looked around once more.

It must be because there are no clouds. It even felt as though he could see all the way to the horizon.

(Is it because it’s winter and the air is clear? No, unlike Japan, I can’t say for sure that the air isn’t polluted… but it shouldn’t be that bad.)

That’s what Rei thought, but she had basically lived in the countryside of Tohoku, where the air is clearer compared to a big city like Tokyo.

For that very reason, she had never really experienced air pollution firsthand.

So, even looking at this scene, she couldn’t tell if it was beautiful because the air was clear, or if there was some other reason… she couldn’t quite figure it out.

(Well, there’s no point in thinking about that now. …I guess meeting Black Wolf is what’s making me feel this way.)

She felt deeply that the incident with the Black Wolf had made her think more strongly about her time in Japan.

Of course, if someone asked me if I was dissatisfied with my current life here in Elgin, I would immediately answer no.

However, when it came to entertainment, the lack of manga and anime was a real disappointment for Rei.

Technically, there are books with stories—or rather, novels—so it’s not as if there are absolutely no books for entertainment.

(Maybe I should just introduce manga or something myself? ...That’s impossible, isn’t it?)

He immediately dismissed the idea he’d just come up with.

To begin with, Rei liked reading manga, but he had never drawn any himself.

There were manga that depicted the struggles of becoming a manga artist, and he had read some of them.

But since I was just enjoying the story, I don’t actually know how to draw manga properly.

If I just drew something that looked somewhat like a manga, maybe I could pull it off… but unfortunately, Rei has no talent for drawing.

(I could just ask someone who’s good at drawing to sketch out the concepts… no, but since I don’t have a printing press or anything like that, it’d be hard to turn it into a book.)

If this were a novel or some kind of technical manual, text alone would suffice, so there’s no need to worry that much.

But with a manga, illustrations are obviously necessary, and if the person making the book lacks artistic talent, even if there’s a manga story, they won’t be able to draw it with their own illustrations.

That’s what Rei had been thinking… but then he remembered that dictionaries—starting with the Monster Dictionary, and including those on herbs, minerals, and animals—all had detailed illustrations.

(Huh? Books like that... they have proper illustrations, don’t they? How on earth are they mass-producing these books? ...Well, never mind.)

As for manga, Rei concluded that, for the time being, he couldn’t spread the concept on his own.

He concluded that since he might be able to teach the concept itself, he could pay a struggling artist to draw the manga and then show it to someone influential.

(Considering that there was Takumu from the Zepairu clan, and other people who came from Japan in the past, I think it might have been a good idea to at least spread the word about manga.)

Even as he pondered manga, Set continued to fly through the sky, flapping his wings.

Set wasn’t entirely without the urge to speak to Rei, but realizing she was deep in thought, he didn’t want to disturb her, so he flew silently and steadily toward Gilm.

After flying for a while, a town came into view ahead.

“Grrrrrr?”

Normally, Set wouldn’t have wanted to interrupt Rei’s thoughts, but now that the town was in sight, he couldn’t help but point it out.

Since they had a magic tent, camping wouldn’t be a problem even in winter, but staying in town would be the best option.

Besides, Set also harbored the hope that they might be able to try the town’s famous local dishes.

That said, depending on the place, there were towns that wouldn’t let him in because they were afraid of him.

“Huh? What is it? What’s wrong? …Oh, a town. I guess staying at an inn would be better than camping out. Set, could you land on the ground?”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set descends toward the ground.

He drops the Set Cage a short distance from the town… and explains the situation to Elena and Ara as they emerge.

“Hmm, I don’t mind either way. I’m sure the Magic Tent is comfortable, but I’m also curious about the town’s local cuisine. What about you, Ara?”

“I’m fine with either. However, since some of the townspeople might have ulterior motives, I think the Magic Tent is the safer option in terms of peace of mind. …Though, even if the townspeople do have bad intentions, I don’t think they’d be able to do anything to Elena or Rei.”

With two people bearing such formidable titles present, it was certain that any attempt to harm them would be nothing short of suicide.

That was likely why Ara spoke those words—precisely because she understood that.

Rei had been listening to Ara’s words, but when he saw several soldiers from the town approaching them, he spoke up.

“It seems they’ve sent someone to meet us.”

That was only to be expected.

Even though they were somewhat removed from the town, a Set Cage-like object had suddenly appeared in such a place.

If they were soldiers tasked with protecting the town, it was only natural that they would come to investigate.

And when the soldiers approached, Ara was the first to act.

Still carrying the Power Axe on her back, she approached the soldiers and, facing their wary expressions, revealed their identities.

What worked to their advantage in this situation were Elena’s beauty and the presence of the griffin, Set.

Anyone with even a little knowledge of the situation would recognize who they were.

She seemed somewhat hesitant to let Set into town, but since Elena took responsibility, Set was ultimately allowed to enter as well.

In the end, they had the cook at their inn butcher the deer; in exchange for the hide, all the meat was served to Rei and the others, and they feasted on it alongside Set, who had hunted it.

And so, they continued their journey... and a few days later, they finally arrived in Gilm.
