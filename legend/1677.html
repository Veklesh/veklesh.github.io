"Oh, I can see it now."

Perched on Set’s back, Rei murmurs as he spots the village of Garanka in the distance.

“Hey, we’re about to arrive in Galanka!”

Rei called out to the Set Cage, as if just to be sure.

There should have been several guards and a slave trader waiting there.

Normally, the captain of the guards who had negotiated with Rei would have come along, but someone in his position cannot leave the city for several days—or even longer.

If, like in Gilm, all the guards were elite—which was only natural given their location—it might be different, but even though Jubis is a large city by these standards, it’s ultimately just a small town.

That was precisely why the captain leading the guards could not carelessly leave the city.

As a result, the guards—including those escorting the carriage—headed for Galanka by land, while ahead of them, Rei was transported in a Set Cage along with the guards tasked with interrogating and guarding the pirates, as well as the slave traders who would purchase the captured pirates as slaves.

That said, Rei couldn’t tell whether the guards and slave traders—none of whom had ever experienced flying before—were delighted by it or terrified.

In any case, it was a fact that flying allowed Rei and the others to return to Galanka far more quickly than they could have by traveling on the ground.

“Hm? Oh, so the pirates are over there.”

Right next to the village stood something that hadn’t been there when Rei left… a structure made of earth.

That said, it wasn’t exactly grand enough to be called a building.

It was simply a massive lump of kneaded earth… that was the most fitting description.

There was no way Rei didn’t know what it was.

After all, it was Rei who had asked Marina to build a cage to hold the pirates.

Set descends toward the ground… and eventually lowers the Set Cage to the ground.

Naturally, Elena and the others noticed the Set Cage, and a little later, the residents of Galanka noticed it as well.

Watching the villagers emerge from the village, Rei and Set then descended to the ground themselves.

Elena and the others didn’t seem particularly surprised by the Set Cage, but the residents of Galanka were simply dumbfounded by it.

The fact that they didn’t draw their weapons was likely because Marina had told them about the Set Cage beforehand.

Or perhaps it was because Rei and Set came down to the ground immediately after lowering the Set Cage.

“Rei! You were back much sooner than I expected. Based on what you said before we left, I thought it would take a little longer.”

“The guy was reasonable. I was prepared for the worst—that I might have to spend the night over there.”

If the alias “Crimson” hadn’t been that well-known… or if there hadn’t been a way to confirm that Rei was indeed Crimson, they might have had to spend the night in Jubis, just as Rei had said.

Fortunately, however, the alias “Crimson” was known even in Jubis, and showing Rei’s guild card proved beyond a doubt that she was indeed Rei.

Thanks to that, things went smoothly, and we were able to return that very same day.

“...So? Why haven’t they come out yet?”

As Rei replied to Marina, he looked toward the Set Cage with a puzzled expression—but just then, as if timing it perfectly, the door to the Set Cage swung open.

“Flying through the sky… I’m done with that…”

The first guard to emerge looked completely exhausted, despite the short journey from Jubis.

His face was etched with exhaustion—enough to make anyone wonder how he could possibly be this worn out in such a short time.

Seeing the guard, Rei couldn’t help but wonder if he’d be any use at all. However, seeing that the next guard to emerge didn’t look nearly as exhausted, he felt a sense of relief for the time being.

As for the slave trader, he was even thrilled that they could fly through the air… As soon as he stepped out of the Set Cage, he rushed straight toward Rei.

“Rei! This… this is amazing! Being able to fly through the air at this speed is truly, truly, truly amazing!”

A burly man in his forties with a beard said this to Rei, his eyes sparkling with curiosity.

He must have really taken a liking to flying.

Rei, who had been watching the guards, was slightly surprised by the slave trader’s reaction, but nodded in response for the time being.

“I-I see. I’m glad you’re pleased.”

“Yes. Being able to travel so quickly… With this, I can go do business far away. So, what do you say? If you’re interested, why not come work for me…”

“Sorry, but I have no intention of doing that. Being an adventurer is more than enough for me right now.”

“Please, reconsider!”

Rei shook his head at the slave trader, who immediately pressed his case despite being turned down.

As an adventurer, he might consider being hired for a short period on a contract basis, but Rei had absolutely no intention of being hired on an exclusive basis.

Seeing Rei’s attitude, the slave trader wore a disappointed expression.

In fact, if there were a Rei who possessed the Misty Ring and had Set as a familiar, that would be a goldmine for any merchant.

For the slave trader, having someone who could make that happen turn him down so easily was nothing short of a disappointment.

That said, the slave trader didn’t press the matter any further with Rei.

He did so because he realized that if he tried anything like that, Rei would likely come to harbor ill feelings toward him.

He likely decided it was better to keep her holding even a little goodwill toward him than to risk alienating her.

“I see. That’s a shame. So… what shall we do?”

The slave trader addressed the guards, who were still reeling from the shock of being thrown through the air.

Perhaps snapped out of their daze by his voice, the guards eventually took a deep breath and composed themselves.

“Well then… is the village chief here?”

“Yes, yes, what is it? If there’s anything we can do to help, we’d be happy to.”

At the guard’s words, an old woman spoke up.

Realizing from her words that she was the village chief, the guard nodded in response.

“I see. It would be a great help if you could assist us. First, we need to question these men, and we’ll also need a place to stay until reinforcements arrive. Is there anywhere that’s vacant?”

“There is. The house where Rimoti lived until last year is vacant now. It’s been cleaned, so I don’t think there’ll be any problem using it.”

Hearing the village chief’s words, the guard briefly wanted to ask what had become of this person named Rimoti, but he decided to hold off on that for now.

If he’d simply left the village, there’d be no need to worry… but if they heard rumors that he’d actually died in that house, it would undoubtedly feel eerie.

If they were just going to use it to gather information—as a so-called interrogation room—that would be one thing, but they would have to stay in that house themselves until reinforcements arrived.

Given that, he didn’t want to ask anything rash.

“In that case, we’ll make use of that place. …Now then, should we ask about the situation or draw up a slave contract? I wonder which would be easier to do first…”

The guard’s words were directed at the slave trader.

The slave trader, looking as though the excited conversation he’d just had with Rei had never happened, was staring at the pirates locked in the earthen cage.

“Right. I’d like to negotiate the price first, so please start with the interrogation.”

“Got it. Then let’s get started right away. …Um, so how do I open this?”

Having finished his conversation with the slave trader, the guard immediately tried to question the pirates, but... The earthen cage holding the pirates had been created by Marina using spirit magic.

Created with the pirates’ inability to escape as the top priority, the cage naturally had no door that could be opened from the outside.

“Marina, please.”

If she tried hard enough, breaking the earthen cage wouldn’t be too difficult.

But then, it wouldn’t serve its purpose as a cage.

Given that, the best course of action was to ask Marina, who had actually created the earthen cage, for help.

“Also, I’d like to ask you to handle the price negotiations for buying the slaves.”

“Sure, that’s fine with me… but what about Rei?”

“I’ll handle buying fishing gear and fish.”

When it came to price negotiations, there was no doubt that it would be best to leave that to Marina as well.

However, since I was entrusting her with the negotiations to sell pirates as slaves, I intended to handle the negotiations for fishing gear and fish purchases myself.

“Chief, regarding the negotiations for buying fishing gear and fish, who should I talk to?”

“Hmm… To be honest, you’re the one who captured the pirates who were threatening this village. Given that, I think it’s only fair to give them to you for free.”

If the pirates had continued to steal food from the village, the village’s very survival would have been in jeopardy.

Since he had been saved, the village chief saw no problem in giving Rei the fishing gear and fish.

Of course, it would be a problem if he took so many fishing tools that it affected the village’s fishing operations, but all Rei wanted were items for his own personal use.

“If it’s just that much…” Rei shook his head at the village chief’s offer.

“No, fishing gear must be important to this village too. And I’m not in a bind for financial reasons. So there’s no need to go out of your way for me.”

“You’re being too honest. Well then… Pastra. You negotiate with Rei and decide how much fishing gear to sell.”

“……Huh?”

At the village chief’s words, Pastra—the person Rei had interacted with most when he first arrived in the village—let out a startled gasp.

She probably never expected to hear her name called here.

But Rei paid no heed to Pastra’s confusion. After entrusting the negotiations with the slave trader to Marina, he approached Pastra.

“Well then, let’s get right to the negotiations. First, show me what kind of fishing gear you have. I’d be happy if you have harpoons and nets.”

As Pastra was dragged by Rei toward the center of the village, he eventually seemed to give up.

He let out a deep sigh and began walking alongside Rei.

“Here, this way. I’ve got some fishing gear I’m not using right now, so take a look at that. I’ll let you have whatever you want from it. That’s fine, right?”

Rei had no objection to Pastra’s proposal.

After all, if the gear wasn’t being used for some reason, buying it wouldn’t affect their fishing.

He was led to a small hut built near the sea.

Since the village wasn’t very big, they reached the sea in no time.

“Um, you needed a harpoon and a net, right?”

“Yeah. We ride on Sets and catch big fish from the sky, but spears don’t have barbs, so they just slip right out.”

“Ah… well, I guess a spear is a weapon. It can’t really be used to catch fish the same way a harpoon made for that purpose can, right?”

Saying that, Pastra handed Rei the harpoon hanging on the wall.

Although it had been described as unused fishing gear, the harpoon wasn’t rusty at all and was reflecting the sunlight.

As Rei looked slightly surprised, Pastra flashed a smug smile.

“Just because it’s unused fishing gear doesn’t mean I don’t maintain it. It’s only natural to keep it ready as a spare, so it’s immediately usable if something happens, right?”

“Well, I suppose you’re right.”

As I found myself wondering, belatedly, if there was actually someone in this village who could work as a blacksmith, Pastra handed me the harpoon.

“Here, take this harpoon. Give it a hold. Does anything seem off about it?”

“Hm? Ah, the problem is… hmm?”

There’s nothing wrong with it.

Rei was about to say that, but then he noticed the back of the harpoon—the part that would be the tip of a spear—had a round hole in it.

“What’s this hole?”

“Hm? Oh, that. It’s not on right now, but you tie a string through that hole. And then…”

"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

Rei screamed, cutting Pastra off mid-sentence.

Faced with Rei’s sudden outburst, Pastra, who had been about to explain the harpoon, could only stare in astonishment.

But ignoring his reaction—no, showing absolutely no sign of noticing it at all—Rei handed the harpoon he was holding to Pastra, then pulled the harpoon he was looking for out of the Misty Ring.

That harpoon was clearly in a different league from the one Pastra had been holding.

And above all, a wire extended from the tip of the harpoon’s shaft.

“Come to think of it, I did have a harpoon.”

“...Hey.”

Pastra retorted, looking utterly exasperated.
