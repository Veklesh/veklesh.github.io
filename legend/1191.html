After the matter with Ambris was settled, Rei and the others arrived at Gilm without incident.

Considering that the most difficult part had been getting Warain's horse, which had become frightened of Set and refused to move forward, to proceed, one can imagine what their journey had been like.

In the end, Set went ahead under the pretext of reconnaissance, and they arrived in Gilm with nothing else of note happening.

They then guided Walein and the others to a shop that could repair the carriage. Once they unloaded the carriage and goods stored inside the Misty Ring, Walein bowed deeply.

"Thank you. Truly, if it weren't for you, Rei-san and the others... I don't know what would have become of us. We might have had to become adventurers ourselves, or get hired by another merchant just to survive."

"...I don't know why adventurers always show up first, but anyway, I'm glad you arrived safely. It must be tough with the carriage situation, but hang in there."

"Yes. We came to the frontier prepared for danger from the start."

After exchanging brief greetings, Rei turned his gaze to Giacomo and Mina.

"If you're heading to the guild, want to come along?"

The two had come to join the Gilm guild. Assuming they were heading there next, he asked if they wanted to go together... but surprisingly, they both shook their heads.

"We do need to go to the guild to collect our escort fees, but I think it's better to wait a little while."

Mina's gaze shifted to Giacomo.

This incident had shattered the confidence and pride that had once filled Giacomo.

He hid it around others besides Mina, but his childhood friend Mina saw right through him.

He understood that if he didn't talk about these things, whether he continued as an adventurer or quit, various problems could arise.

"Is that so? Well, I won't force you. Alright, we're heading out."

"Grr."

At Rei's words, Walein, Mina, and Giacomo bowed their heads.

Without Rei and the others, they would have certainly died.

It was precisely because they understood this that they bowed their heads to Rei with gratitude.

...Giacomo envied Rei for traveling with Vihera, a woman of exceptional beauty, but he wasn't so ungrateful as to forget the debt he owed for being saved.

With the three seeing Rei off, Rei and the others headed toward the guild.

"You defeated Ambris? Is that true? No, Rei wouldn't say something so ridiculous... But you don't seem very happy about it, though?"

Upon arriving at the guild, Rei and the others immediately requested an audience with Lenora to speak with Marina. Their request was promptly granted, and they were ushered into the guildmaster's office.

Today, Marina wore a white party dress.

The white dress complemented her tanned skin beautifully, and the deep neckline revealed her ample bosom, creating a pronounced cleavage.

It was Vihera, not Rei, who answered Marina as she sat on the sofa and asked curiously.

"It's true... well, that Rei defeated Ambris. But Rei said he felt something odd when he did it. ...Whether that was because Ambris wasn't a monster, or for some other reason, I don't know."

"What do you mean?"

Rei, whose eyes sought an answer, opened his mouth, looking slightly troubled.

"It's not like I have clear evidence or certainty about it. It's just... how should I put it? It's like... I feel a vague sense of unease... Does that make sense?"

"...Yes, I understand."

Marina also had a past as a skilled adventurer.

Because of that, she knew firsthand that a certain kind of intuition worked among adventurers.

In fact, during her own adventuring days, Marina had survived several times by trusting instincts over logic.

Given that Vihera enjoyed combat, she probably understood the existence of that kind of inexplicable intuition.

And Rei, perhaps due to the magic power he possessed, had a highly developed sense of that sort of thing.

Ultimately, the three of them each arrived at the same conclusion.

Namely, that the matter with Ambris was far from over...

There was no evidence whatsoever. In fact, given that Rei had defeated Ambris, it wouldn't have been strange for anyone to dismiss it as a figment of their imagination.

Still, considering the current situation, it was true that defeating Ambrosus sooner rather than later was preferable... Marina opened her mouth, looking slightly disappointed.

"Then, it's probably best not to announce that we defeated... maybe defeated, Ambris, right?"

"I suppose so. If we announce this matter is resolved, only for another horde of monsters to appear... you can imagine how unpleasant that would be, right?"

Marina nodded at Vihera's words.

Though not for an extended period, this recent swarm incident had significantly impacted Gilm.

It had certainly reduced the number of merchants coming to Gilm.

In the current climate, if they announced the Ambris incident was resolved, and then merchants and adventurers, filled with joy, were attacked by another monster horde...

Marina, who announced the resolution, would undoubtedly be blamed. Worse still, it wouldn't be surprising if even Rei's claim of defeating Ambris were dismissed as mere empty words.

After all, only Rei and her companions had seen Ambris, and only they had defeated it.

And the fact that Marina is close to Rei is fairly well known. It's not impossible that people might connect the dots and start making strange assumptions.

"I understand both of your points. But then, what do we do? We did defeat Ambris, the root cause of this whole mess... I know it's not my place to say, but I think we should report that as soon as possible."

His gut told him there was still something else, but he didn't have anything concrete to be certain.

To Rei, who murmured hesitantly, Marina nodded reassuringly before speaking.

"If Ambris really was defeated, then the Leader Species shouldn't appear anymore, right? Of course, it's possible that the Leader Species was created before Rei defeated Ambris, and that Leader Species is still alive..."

"Even so, since they can't produce any more Leader Species, the pack's numbers will definitely dwindle. ...That's what you mean, right?"

Marina nodded at Vihera's words.

"But if the damage continues without the pack shrinking even in this situation, it means this incident isn't over yet."

"I suppose so."

All three of them vaguely understood that this probably wouldn't end here.

After sharing their mutual understanding, Marina finally spoke.

"So no one else saw the scene where Ambris was defeated?"

"Yes. There was a merchant named Walein and two adventurer guards, but those three were waiting at a distance."

"So they came to secretly observe Rei and the others?"

Asking Marina, who inquired with thoughts like "just to be sure," "for confirmation," "just in case"... Rei responded with a confident smile.

"If someone could get close while me, Vihera, and especially Set were on guard, they'd have to be as strong as a high-rank adventurer."

"...I suppose so. I can't imagine those kids being capable of such a thing."

Images flashed through Vihera's mind: Mina, who adored her and looked up to her with admiration, and Giacomo, whose gaze was fixed on her.

Wearing provocatively thin clothing, Vihera was naturally sensitive to men's gazes.

Regardless of their potential, she couldn't possibly believe those two could outmaneuver them.

"So, only Rei and the others need to know about the Ambris matter, right?"

Rei and Vihera nodded again to Marina, who exhaled in relief.

"Then, I'm afraid we'll have to keep the matter of Ambris quiet for a while longer. In the meantime, we'll see if the pack is discovered... Oh?"

Marina cut herself off mid-sentence, her gaze shifting toward the door.

Rei and Vihera did the same, sensing the presence of someone approaching this office... no, more than that, they heard the sound of heavy footsteps.

Of course, this being the Guildmaster's office, it wouldn't be unusual for a Guild employee with some business to come by.

But in such cases, they would walk normally... At the very least, approaching with such heavy footsteps was extremely rare unless it was an emergency.

Which meant an emergency had occurred.

"What could it be? I really wish we could avoid any more problems right now."

Marina's mutter was almost simultaneous with the knocking on the door.

Not a normal knock, but a rough one that clearly signaled urgency.

It was clear something had happened. Marina, her bad feeling confirmed, told them to come in, her expression gloomy.

Incidentally, Marina's gloomy expression alone was filled with a melancholy and feminine allure that would send a chill down the spine of any ordinary man.

Fortunately or unfortunately, Rei was focused on the door and didn't notice Marina's state.

Vihera noticed Marina's expression and, even as a woman herself, found Marina's alluring feminine charm captivating, casting a slightly envious glance her way.

Such was the exchange... or rather, what could be called an exchange... surrounding Rei. Amidst this, the door opened.

And the man who burst into the office, his face drained of color, was a guild employee both Rei and Vihera had seen several times before.

Not the receptionist, but a guild clerk working behind the counter, his expression tightened with tension as he spoke.

"Guildmaster, urgent report! We've located Ambris!"

"What did you say?"

Marina's voice betrayed unexpected bewilderment.

Of course, she hadn't expected everything to be resolved just because Rei had defeated Anbrith, but she certainly hadn't imagined Anbrith had survived.

"When was he discovered?"

Perhaps the information about discovering Rei before Rei defeated him had only just reached them now.

That was the thought behind her question, but the guild clerk's eyes lit up as he answered.

"About three hours ago, they say."

Three hours... That coincides with the time Rei defeated Ambris.

Therefore, it's correct to assume a different Ambris was discovered, separate from the one Rei defeated.

(There was more than one Ambris? It's possible, but... the idea that multiple Ambris were created by the magic anomaly is a bit hard to swallow.)

Originally, Ambris were only known from the incident three hundred years ago. If that's the case, it wouldn't be surprising if they possessed a biology Marina wasn't aware of.

"Who discovered the Ambris? Is it someone trustworthy?"

"Yes. Rank C party, Scorching Wind."

"...Huh?"

The one who let out that dumbfounded voice was Rei.

It was only natural. Scorching Wind was a party deeply connected to Rei.

And above all, he had even made a wager with Mireine, the leader of Scorching Wind, regarding the discovery of Ambris.

The wager was that if Mireine found Ambris before Rei did, she would be granted the right to spend a day with Set.

The area Scorching Wind was searching was fairly large, thanks to having the archer Ekril with her keen eyesight and the mage Surunin.

But even so, compared to Rei and his companions who possessed the flying Set, their search area was overwhelmingly smaller.

Given that situation, there was absolutely no way Mireine and the others could find Ambris.

That's what I thought... which is precisely why it was only natural to gasp in surprise when told Mireine had found Ambris.

"You're sure it wasn't a mistake? That it really was Ambris?"

Marina asked, seeking confirmation.

Sensing something in her words, the guild employee nodded hesitantly.

Marina had expected the guild employee who brought this information to be delighted upon hearing the report of Ambris's discovery.

But the reaction she got back was slightly different from what she'd expected.

"Yes. I can't imagine there being another entity like that black mist. Well, maybe there could be... but I don't think a similar monster would appear in this situation with Ambris here. ...What's wrong?"

"Nothing. I just need to ask you something. Could you summon the Scorching Wind here?"

Nodding at her words, the guild official left the office to summon the Scorching Wind.

"What should we do?"

If our presence here is a hindrance, we can leave.

Marina shook her head at Rei's suggestion.

"No, please stay. I'd like to hear your opinions on various matters."

Though her words were vague, Rei understood she wanted him to interpret the Burning Wind's message since he had defeated Ambris. He nodded in agreement.

And so...

"Excuse us."

Mireine, acting as their representative, spoke up, and the three members of the Scorching Wind entered the office.
