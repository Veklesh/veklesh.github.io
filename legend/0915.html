After finishing their udon, Rei, Isceld, and Set—two humans and one beast—decided they'd eaten enough and walked down the main street.

Soon, a large building near the center of Gracia came into view.

Several other buildings stood around it, occupying a vast expanse of land.

Considering why Rei had come to Graciall, it was immediately clear what kind of building stood before them.

"So this is the Officer Academy?"

"Yes. ...Duke Quent... Ronald-sama was actually looking forward to meeting you, Rei, but he's busy right now. He's got other things to worry about."

From Iskeld's words, Rei understood that Ronald was the name of Duke Quent... meaning Ronald Quent was the name of Maruka's father. He vaguely grasped why Ronald might be busy.

"Ah... Is it about the disciplinary crackdown?"

"Yes. There are many troublemakers, and Lord Ronald is having a hard time dealing with them. Because of that, it will probably be tomorrow evening before he can meet with you."

The tone in which Iskeld spoke the name of Duke Quent was one of familiarity.

It was easy to imagine from his demeanor what this rugged man felt toward the person he served.

(Then again, he is Maruka's father. And seeing how much Gracielle has developed, there's no doubt Duke Quent is a capable man. Running this city, larger than Gilm, without any shortcomings is simply impressive.)

Typically, those in power are disliked by their subordinates to some degree.

But since entering Graciall until Rei arrived here, the number of people speaking ill of Duke Quent had been surprisingly small.

Of course, it would be premature to conclude that Duke Quent enjoyed universal support based solely on that.

Still, from what Rei observed, it was also true that more people felt gratitude toward Duke Quent.

(Though the royalist faction's reputation was poor...)

For Rei, the King's faction was still heavily associated with the recent issue of Keep.

Furthermore, the disgraceful conduct of the royalist faction during last spring's war with the Bestir Empire remained vividly etched in his memory.

Given these circumstances, it was undeniable that Rei's image of the King's faction was extremely negative.

Still, he understood it wasn't solely because of that, considering the existence of someone like Marca.

"Well, fine. I get that meeting Duke Quento is tomorrow, and I understand I'm teaching here. But why did you come here now?"

"Huh? Well, obviously, you'll be living here while you're teaching, right?"

"...I'll be living here? In this place?"

"Yes. There's a dormitory for staff, so I intend for you to stay there. I originally planned to prepare a mansion, but Lord Maruka informed me that Rei doesn't care for such things."

"By the way, mind if I ask what kind of mansion you were planning to arrange?"

"Well, the first proposal was for a rather large mansion. After all, you were dragged into the mess with the royalist faction. The person who suggested it probably meant well, wanting to ensure you had every comfort... but Maruka-sama said that would actually make Rei feel more constrained."

Hearing Iskeld's words, Rei silently thanked Maruka.

(Thank goodness she said that. If it had really been that kind of mansion, I would have... well, not exactly gone back, but I definitely would have needed to find lodgings elsewhere.)

Frankly, Rei himself didn't desire a life attended to by maids and butlers.

It would be a lie to say he wasn't interested, but having strangers constantly nearby would be mentally exhausting.

It would be different if it were someone Rei felt comfortable with, but being looked after by complete strangers was not something he particularly enjoyed.

But living in a dormitory came with its own set of anxieties.

The biggest one being...

"It's true that a dorm is better than being given a mansion like that. ...But what about Set then? You can't possibly make him live in the dorm, right?"

"Gururu?"

Realizing he was being discussed, Set growled softly, his anxious eyes fixed on Iskeld.

Iskeld, already accustomed to Set's presence, smiled at the dog looking his way, reached out, and stroked his head.

"Don't worry. Many cadets at this academy bring their familiars or personal horses. There are tamers and summoners too."

"That's nice to hear, but tamers and summoners? ...This is an officer academy, right? I just assumed it only trained knights."

"That's not entirely wrong. Many aspire to be knights, and indeed, many who graduate from this school join the Duke Quent's Knight Corps. But every year, a certain percentage of our graduates become adventurers."

"...Officer training school?"

Wasn't I supposed to be taking on the role of an instructor at the military academy?

Iskeld nodded at Rei's question, showing no particular reaction.

"It may be called an officer academy, but that's essentially what it is."

"I'm surprised the nobles don't complain. Any noble I know would probably say something like, 'How can I study alongside commoners?!' or 'I'm attending this school to become a knight, so why must I study with those lowly adventurers?!'"

"...What kind of nobles have you been associating with? I'm not denying such nobles exist, but—"

Rather, this crackdown is being carried out precisely to purge such nobles.

Of course, only those who went too far, like Keep, will be excluded this time.

The aim was that seeing such individuals purged might cause those who've been arrogant up until now to change their ways.

Rei, however, didn't believe nobles would change their ways so easily.

"I've encountered all sorts of nobles. Naturally, some I felt a certain fondness for. But among them were also scum like Keep, who caused this whole incident. Though..."

"However?"

"Ah, no. Never mind."

He stopped himself mid-sentence from adding that such nobles weren't exclusive to the royalist faction. In fact, they were more common among the aristocratic faction.

Iskeld surely understood that such nobles existed among the noble faction.

But even so, he didn't want to deliberately say anything here that would put the noble faction at a disadvantage.

Thanks to Marca, excessive hostility wasn't directed toward the royalist faction, but learning of Keep's existence still held significant meaning.

While he felt a sense of obligation toward the noble faction, which included Elena, within Rei's current mindset, the noble faction still held greater weight than the royal faction.

...Though, if the neutral faction had gotten involved, things might have been different.

"Well, anyway, let's get inside. It's quiet now since class is in session, but if I get spotted after class ends, it'll be a real hassle."

"Trouble?"

"Believe it or not, I'm quite famous in Graciall. And it's not just me. You're bringing a griffin, so you're plenty conspicuous too. If that happens, you'll definitely get dragged into trouble. Trouble in a different sense than mine, I imagine."

Rei vaguely understood what Iskeld meant.

If he were seen alone, it was unlikely anyone would recognize him as the adventurer known by the scarlet alias.

He didn't have the Death Scythe with him now, and simply wearing the Dragon Robe with its concealment effect would likely only lead people to recognize him as a newly minted mage adventurer, as usual.

But... if Set was beside Rei, his true identity would be immediately apparent.

After all, no one else but Rei had a griffin as a familiar.

(Well, it's not impossible that someone else out there has a griffin as a familiar, just not out in the open. Actually, that makes sense. This world seems to have all sorts of things lurking in the shadows.)

Suddenly, memories of the Holy Light Church encounter in Exil flashed through Rei's mind.

He hadn't encountered them since, but that was likely just because the areas where the Holy Light Church operated hadn't overlapped with Rei's own activities.

That said, word of the incident in Exil had reached the Holy Light Church within the Mireana Kingdom itself, and they were increasingly viewed with suspicion in many places.

The fact they weren't completely expelled probably meant there were still benefits to be gained from their existence.

(Well, my time in the Bestir Empire probably played a big part. After that ended, I went straight back to the frontier town of Gilm. ...So, does that mean the Holy Light Church only operates in the Mireana Kingdom? Though it seems likely they'd be active in smaller surrounding countries too.)

Rei felt a faint sense of unease, but suddenly felt warmth in his hand and turned his gaze there.

There, Set was gently rubbing his head against Rei's hand.

As if saying, "Cheer up," with his actions.

"Sorry, Set. Yeah, I guess worrying about it won't do any good."

"Huh? What is it?"

Iskeld, who had been watching Rei and Set interact, asked with a look of confusion, unable to grasp what had happened.

"Nothing. Never mind. Anyway, we were going inside, right? If standing here will cause a commotion, let's just get inside already."

Even now, people passing near the academy were turning their gaze toward Rei and the others.

Half of them first widened their eyes in shock at seeing Set, then relaxed upon seeing Iskeld with him, finally looking satisfied upon seeing Rei. The other half looked puzzled, wondering why a kid like this was with Lord Iskeld.

Those who looked at Rei and nodded in understanding were likely those who knew him by rumor.

Those with questions were either people who knew nothing about Rei, or those who knew him but assumed he was connected to Iskeld, not the Griffin.

Regardless, Rei, Iskeld, and Set entered the academy grounds while being subjected to various stares from those around them.

(If nobles attend here, you'd expect gatekeepers... yet there's not a soul in sight. This is the duke's territory—they can't possibly be too broke to hire guards. Is this intentional?)

Wondering about it, I followed Iskeld.

After walking for about ten minutes, a four-story building came into view.

It was clear the building had been standing for quite some time, yet it didn't feel old-fashioned; instead, it exuded a sense of history. That must mean it had been well-maintained.

"This is the staff quarters where Rei will be staying."

"...Hey, Iskeld. It's fine that this is the staff quarters, but what about Set's stables? I really want to see those first."

As if caught off guard by the question, Iskeld looked momentarily taken aback before speaking.

"Of course I'll show you that too. I just showed you this dorm first because it's closer."

"I see. Well then, I'll look forward to the stables too. ...Just to be sure, you did get the word out, right?"

Rei asked, looking at Set.

His reasoning was that bringing in a monster like a griffin meant the staff would definitely be shocked if they weren't prepared.

Though, as Rei argued, most people would get along with Set after spending a reasonable amount of time with him.

(Though Rei and Johanna definitely take it too far.)

He pushed the image of those two showering Set with excessive affection from his mind and continued on his way with Iskeld.

They arrived at the stables, located about ten minutes' walk beyond the staff dormitory.

"...It's surprisingly small."

That was the first thing Rei said upon seeing the stables.

Indeed, the stable before his eyes was by no means large.

It was smaller even compared to the one at the Dusk Wheat inn.

Considering it was the stables for the military academy where numerous nobles and commoners gathered, one couldn't help but question the size of this stable.

"Ah. These aren't for the cadets. This stable is for the staff, including the instructors. This size is perfectly adequate."

"...They separated them on purpose?"

"That's how it is. Anyway, let's go. I don't have all day either. After showing you around here and your room, I'll have the academy staff take over the tour."

With that, Iskeld entered the stables.

Rei and Set followed him... Naturally, a small commotion ensued as the stable staff looked surprised at Isceld, surprised at Set, and puzzled at Rei. Still, it never escalated into a major disturbance, likely because no other buildings existed around these stables.
