The day after Rei arrived at the academy, perhaps because his conversation with Elena the previous day had gone well, Rei woke up earlier than usual and made his way to the back of the staff dormitory.

It was an empty plaza, now cleared of accumulated snow to allow some movement.

But that was precisely why it was perfect for this occasion.

"Hmm... It's bright out here."

He squinted at the snow reflecting the sunlight, then proceeded to do some light stretching.

When he finished, he drew the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring.

Holding the Death Scythe, she swept the massive blade through the air as if confirming something.

Rei hadn't trained under any particular school; he was entirely self-taught.

That's precisely why those who had trained properly couldn't react to Rei's tricky movements, which maximized his physical abilities.

Though, even before getting into tricky movements, the sheer rarity of someone fighting with a large scythe probably plays a big part.

Unbeknownst to Rei, he had rapidly risen to become a Rank B adventurer using the Death Scythe, earned a nickname through his exploits in war, and even had a Rank A monster, a Griffin, as his familiar.

Such a figure was bound to be talked about, and among adventurers, there were quite a few who admired Rei—people Rei could only describe as oddballs.

Naturally, such people wanted to be like Rei.

They understood that taming a Rank A monster like Set was practically impossible.

That's precisely why the next step for such individuals was to emulate another of Rei's defining traits: wielding a great scythe.

But a scythe is an extremely difficult weapon for ordinary humans to wield.

No, even a regular sickle, not just a scythe, was no different.

In the end, a few enthusiasts even forced blacksmiths to craft scythes for them, but almost no one could master them.

After all, sickles are fundamentally ill-suited for use as weapons, so it was only natural.

Those who couldn't wield the scythe eventually realized they couldn't imitate Rei and quietly sought weapons better suited to them.

Even so, many still trained to master polearms like the scythe, leading to a proliferation of spear and halberd users.

Additionally, a few adventurers emerged who could use the greatscythe... well, not master it, but manage it reasonably well. This slightly increased the weapon's recognition compared to now, but that was still some time in the future.

"Hmph!"

A sharp exhale accompanied the flash of the Death Scythe.

The strike felt as if it sliced through the very fresh morning air itself. Had anyone witnessed it, they would have questioned just how heavy the Death Scythe truly was.

That's how effortlessly Rei swung it, as if the weight of the Death Scythe didn't exist at all.

He swung the Death Scythe for about ten minutes before finally stopping his movements.

"Phew... Now then."

With the Death Scythe still raised, he closed his eyes.

The image that came to mind was his strongest enemy.

The Rank S adventurer he fought in the Bestir Empire, the immovable Noiz.

He had only fought Noiz twice, but even so, the sheer intensity of combat—or whatever you'd call it—was incredibly dense.

A battle of that caliber was undoubtedly something he would never experience again.

As the image solidified in his mind, he opened his eyes, faced the vision suspended in the air... and kicked off the ground.

I didn't use the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, which could be called Rei's ultimate technique; this was a fight with my bare hands.

He closed the distance in one swift motion toward the imagined Noiz.

The moment Noiz's hand moved, a flash streaked across the sky.

He dodged the strike by slightly tilting his body, then swung his Death Scythe in a counter-like motion.

Had Rei possessed only ordinary physical abilities, that split-second evasion would have undoubtedly been met with a slash from the long sword.

But it was precisely his ability to seize that timing that earned Rei the epithet "Crimson."

A horizontal slash that would normally cleave an opponent's torso in two – a strike worthy of being called the scythe of death itself, in the truest sense.

But... the Noiz Rei created was already gone the instant that attack was swung... and before he knew it, the longsword's blade was pressed against Rei's neck.

"Game over, huh? Damn it, I couldn't win today either."

Before he knew it, the Noiz he had conjured from his imagination had vanished from before Rei's eyes.

"Brilliant!"

A voice spoke to Rei.

Stowing the Death Scythe back into the Misty Ring, he turned toward the voice. There stood Samaloon.

Seeing him looking at him with admiration in his eyes, Rei felt uneasy once again.

He understood that she genuinely respected him, but it felt like it was going too far.

"No, truly amazing. Rei's movements just now... Unfortunately, I couldn't follow everything, so I can't pinpoint exactly what was amazing, but it was undoubtedly amazing nonetheless."

"I see. I'm glad you enjoyed it. ...So, what brings you here?"

"Ah, yes. I woke up and went to Rei's room to pick you up, but you weren't there, so I was looking for you. I thought maybe you got lost while out for a walk."

Rei thought for a moment that this was an exaggeration... but then realized, considering the size of the academy grounds, it wasn't entirely wrong.

The grounds themselves were quite large, with numerous buildings scattered across them.

While someone familiar with the place might be fine, it wasn't surprising for someone who had just arrived at the academy to get lost.

After all, one reason Rei was training here was precisely because straying carelessly from the staff quarters could easily lead to getting lost.

(If Set were here, he'd probably figure something out in a pinch.)

My thoughts drifted to my partner, who wasn't here. Of course, Set couldn't just fly around freely within the city, but I seemed to be conveniently overlooking that detail.

Then again, given the nature of the academy, a little leniency wouldn't be out of place.

"I see. Sorry to trouble you."

"Not at all. More importantly, breakfast time is approaching. What shall we do? We still have a little leeway, though."

"Training's wrapped up for now. I'll head out soon."

"...Is that so?"

Samaroon replied, a faintly disappointed expression flickering across his face.

Though his expression clearly showed he wanted to talk about magic, Rei pretended not to notice and headed toward the staff dormitory.

Samaroon followed right behind him.

"First, we'll finish breakfast, then go to the Headmaster's office. We went there yesterday, so it should be fine, right? If you're worried, I can come with you. What do you think?"

Samaroon asked with an expression that clearly said he wanted to go along, but Rei shook his head at him.

"I'm fine. Besides, you have class preparations to do, right? If you keep fussing over me, won't you get scolded?"

"No, it's fine. I've been told to support you, Rei-san, so that part isn't a problem at all. In fact, I really want to stay with you and act together."

Rei understood this stemmed from Samaloon's interest in magic, yet he still felt something about Samaloon's words he couldn't quite accept.

(Is it that his goal is solely magic, and he doesn't see me as a person? Or something like that?)

Wondering about Samaloon's attitude, they walked on and soon arrived at the entrance of the staff dormitory.

"Come to think of it, I need to go see Set after breakfast."

"Set? I peeked in on him last night. I was surprised—it was my first time seeing a griffin."

"...You've got guts. I never imagined you'd go alone."

Knowing Set's friendly nature, hearing he went alone wouldn't be particularly surprising.

But for Samaloon, encountering a griffin for the first time, to go see Set alone without bringing Rei along—that was completely unexpected, even from Rei's perspective.

"Ah, ahahaha. The griffin... was that Set? I only decided to go see him last night. I figured Rei would probably be asleep already, so I went alone."

"...She's a tough one in more ways than one."

"Is that so? But you are Rei-san's familiar, right? If so, I don't think there's any particular danger."

Samaroon stated this matter-of-factly, leaving Rei unsure how to respond.

Should he scold her for trusting him too much? Or should he thank her for trusting him that much?

Still undecided, he arrived at the dining hall.

The breakfast served here was almost identical to what the cadets ate at the academy.

In other words, it was standard fare, a menu that left Rei's palate feeling a little unsatisfied.

(The portions weren't exactly filling either.)

Bread, soup with sparse toppings, stir-fried vegetables. A single slice of cheese was present, almost as an act of mercy.

The only saving grace was that the bread was white bread.

...Though for Rei, it was barely enough for a snack, let alone a meal.

"Isn't this an officer academy for training knights joining the duke's knight order and adventurers? Yet the meals are pretty modest."

"Hahaha. It'd be a bit more luxurious if it weren't winter right now."

Samaloon, sitting across from Rei, said apologetically.

"Ah, well, I suppose that can't be helped. Considering it's winter."

Muttering to himself as if to convince himself, he pondered what dishes might still be left in the Misty Ring... and suddenly realized.

"...Hey, Samaloon."

"Yes? What is it?"

Samaroon, who had been drinking soup, immediately answered Rei's words.

He seemed genuinely pleased to have been spoken to by Rei.

Feeling somewhat apologetic toward such a Samaloon, Ray continued.

"Since we can't eat much like this, how's things over at Set's place?"

"Hmm... I'm not sure. Probably, since he's bigger, he eats more than us, I guess."

"I see. Well, I guess I'll go check on him then."

"Huh?"

Leaving Samaloon with his slightly dazed expression, Rei shoved the remaining food into his mouth.

Neither particularly bad nor particularly good, he swallowed the meal before picking up his dishes and standing up.

He returned them to the cafeteria, leaving Samaloon standing there in stunned silence, and walked out.

"Hey, wait a minute, Rei-san!? I'm coming too, so wait up. Rei-san!?"

Samaroon shouted in panic, but Rei didn't wait for him and left the staff dormitory as well.

Samaroon tried to chase after him, but as a wizard with a slender build, he was by no means a fast eater.

That said, he couldn't very well leave his meal behind to chase after him.

Well, he probably could have if he tried, but doing something like that would earn him a severe punishment from the person who prepared the meal.

In other words, for Samaloon to chase after Rei, he had no choice but to finish every last bite of the meal in front of him.

"Oh dear. Samaloon got dumped."

"Hey, don't be such an idiot. That's the one, right? The mock battle instructor the headmaster specially summoned. The one with the nickname 'Crimson' or something."

"But she doesn't look that strong."

"You can't judge a book by its cover. Especially with someone who has a nickname. It's a mistake to assume they're ordinary. Look at this country's Rank S adventurers, for example. ...That's what they're like, right?"

"...Yeah. Sorry."

"Hey, hey! We don't have much time to chat here! We need to get ready for school soon!"

The other teachers in the cafeteria were fascinated by Rei and Samaloon's exchange, but when it came down to preparing for class, they couldn't linger forever. They finished their meals.

「Set？」

"Grrrrr?"

When Rei called out to Set in the staff stables, the familiar growl came back immediately.

The sound didn't indicate hunger or sadness.

It was simply the sound of a dog happy that Rei had come to see him.

If this were Gilm, at the Dusk Wheat inn where Rei stayed regularly, Set could roam freely outside to some extent—though still only within the grounds of the Dusk Wheat inn.

But here, having only arrived yesterday, there were still more people who feared Set than those who doted on him.

That was why Set had been told not to go outside on his own.

"Set, have you been okay? Well, it's only been one night, but..."

"Gurrrrrrr!"

Set purred happily at Rei's words.

As Rei stroked Set's head, he realized Set wasn't particularly hungry.

"Looks like you got plenty to eat."

"Gurrr"

Not hungry at all! he purred.

"You feed the familiar more than the instructor? ...Ah, because it's Set."

Was it fear of Set the griffin, or had his cuteness shot straight through her heart?

It was unclear which, but either way, it was decided that Set wouldn't go hungry until spring.
