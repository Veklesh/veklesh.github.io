Rei was momentarily stunned by the man who had suddenly appeared before his eyes.

Even though he had said aloud, “Come out,” he had expected the man to emerge from somewhere nearby—from a hiding place, he thought.

But in reality, the moment he called out, the man suddenly appeared right in front of Rei and Set.

It was as if he had been invisible until just now, but had suddenly stopped using that ability… or perhaps he had teleported there.

To be honest, Rei didn’t know what the man had done or how he’d done it. He didn’t know… but precisely because of that, he could easily guess that the man standing before him was the one whose emotionless gaze he’d been feeling all day.

“You’re the one who’s been giving me those strange looks all day, aren’t you?”

Rei asked, but the man didn’t react.

He just stared silently at Rei.

Given that, Rei naturally found himself observing the man.

He looked to be in his mid-twenties.

His black hair reached down to his back, and it wouldn’t have been strange to mistake him for a woman. …But even so, Rei didn’t think anyone would look at this man and think he was a woman.

His physique was by no means muscular, and compared to Brewitt, he seemed to weigh about half as much.

He is taller than Rei, but even so, he’s probably only around 175 cm.

In this world, where many people—starting with Brewitt—are over two meters tall, his height, while not exactly short, certainly couldn’t be called tall.

As for his features, while his eyes are slightly sharp, one could generally describe his face as well-proportioned.

He’s handsome enough that, if he were so inclined, he’d have no trouble attracting women.

“Grrrrr…”

Beside Rei, Set growls in warning.

This is likely because he understands that the person standing before him is by no means an opponent to be underestimated.

Even in the face of such a Set, the man shows no sign of being particularly shaken.

(Seriously, what is this guy? To show absolutely no reaction in front of Set... what on earth is going on?)

Rei dropped the club he was holding onto the ground... The moment the thud of the club hitting the ground echoed around them, the man vanished without warning.

It was as if the man had been there just a few seconds ago—as if it had all been an illusion.

“Set! Follow the scent!”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s command, Set immediately activated his Enhanced Scent skill.

But… after sniffing the air around them for about ten seconds, Set eventually turned his gaze toward Rei with an apologetic look.

Seeing Set like that, Rei understood exactly what he was trying to say.

"I thought it was some kind of optical camouflage... but I was wrong?"

One of Set’s skills is optical camouflage, which makes him invisible to others.

However, while that skill makes him invisible, it doesn’t change the fact that he’s actually there. In other words, it can’t eliminate his scent.

Despite that, Set couldn’t detect the scent of the man standing before him.

“Grr…”

“I’m sorry,” Set murmured softly.

While petting Set’s head, Rei began to question the man’s actions.

(With Set’s optical camouflage, the scent wouldn’t disappear. That much is certain. So… either a skill superior to optical camouflage, or magic or a magical item, or something completely unrelated to that. …Which is it? No, neither option is particularly welcome. The only other possibility is teleportation.)

Teleportation magic items aren’t entirely nonexistent.

In fact, the Bestir Empire mass-produces them, albeit in small numbers, and she’d had some trouble with them both before and during the war.

However, teleportation isn’t something you can use freely whenever you want.

At least, as far as Rei knew, there were various restrictions.

(In that case, it’s likely something else entirely…)

As I stroked Set and thought about the man from earlier, I eventually saw Brewitt return with several guards and knights.

The guards and knights seemed somewhat deferential, likely because they recognized Brewitt as a noble.

That said, since this is the Duchy of Kerebel, if a noble were to commit any kind of crime, they would be arrested without mercy.

“Whoa… what’s this…”

One of the guards let out an exclamation of shock upon seeing that several of the men lying on the ground were no longer breathing.

In fact, the sight of the bodies strewn across the ground was shocking enough even for those accustomed to violence.

The only consolation was that neither Rei nor Brewitt had used bladed weapons, so blood hadn’t splattered all over the place.

...Though, in their place, several teeth from the men who’d been struck with clubs were scattered on the ground.

“Rei, I’m glad you’re safe, at least. But you sure got targeted by some weirdo out of the blue.”

The one who spoke to Rei was a knight he’d fought against several times in mock battles.

Although he was a knight, he was someone Rei could talk to casually, and they were on reasonably good terms.

“Yeah. I never expected to get attacked by a bunch of guys like this right after the mock battle ended.”

“Ah… well, I suppose so. Considering Rei’s abilities, what these guys did was nothing short of suicide. In that sense, it’s practically their own fault. …But, you said they were on drugs?”

He must have heard about that from Brewitt. The knight asked Rei, looking distinctly displeased.

“Yeah. Even after his wrist joint was shattered, he didn’t seem to feel any pain at all and kept attacking us. If it were just one guy, I might think it was sheer willpower or some kind of natural constitution… but for all of them? That’s a bit much.”

Hearing Rei’s words, the knight nodded in agreement.

While Rei and the knight were talking, the guards, following the orders of the other knight, led the groaning men away.

The bodies remain here, but they must have decided that they should find out what happened first.

“Radinas, I’m taking these guys with me, so keep an eye on the bodies for a bit. I’ll go get some more guards right away!”

The knight named Radinas, who had been speaking with Rei, raised his hand slightly in response to the other knight’s words.

Brewitt, who seemed to have business with that knight as well, gave Rei a brief wave before leaving the scene.

“So, what are we going to do?”

“Well, even if you ask me what we’re going to do, you’re the one who was asked to stay here, right? So, I don’t really need to stay here, do I?”

“That’s true, but I’d still like to hear the details from Rei.”

“...Didn’t you hear it from Brewitt?”

Rei asked this, assuming that must be the case since Brewitt had come with him. The knight nodded but spoke up.

“That’s Rei. Rei is Rei. To be honest, it’s not uncommon for new facts to come to light when you hear from multiple people.”

Hearing that, Rei couldn’t very well refuse the knight’s request.

He took some freshly baked bread out of the Misty Ring and handed one to Set and one to Radinas.

“If we’re going to talk, it’s better to have something to eat, right?”

“Yeah, thanks.”

Watching Radinas accept the bread while saying that, Rei suddenly recalled a manga parody of a Japanese police drama he’d seen back in Japan.

When you think of detective dramas, the scene that comes to mind is probably someone eating katsu don in an interrogation room.

But in reality, when someone eats a katsu don in an interrogation room, it’s the suspect who pays for it—that’s the kind of comic book trope he was thinking of.

Of course, Rei wasn’t exactly a suspect this time… if anything, he was the victim.

...Though it’s unclear how many people would recognize that, given the scene currently unfolding before Rei and Radinas.

As she eats her bread, Radinas speaks up.

“So, it seems you have an idea… of why you were attacked.”

“Yeah, if you mean being attacked because the nobility hates me, I’ve got plenty of reasons.”

Just his recent visit to Anesis alone gives him a reason to suspect it, but there’s no doubt that many of the nobles Rei has dealt with in the past harbor a grudge against her.

That said, Rei was already half-convinced that the man from earlier was the one pulling the strings behind the attackers.

There was a possibility it wasn’t him, but after making such a meaningful appearance, for him to actually not be the mastermind behind this incident was something Rei found hard to believe.

(So what is he, anyway? It’s almost certainly true that he’s been hired by someone—probably a noble—to target me.)

Having thought that far, Rei suddenly realized once again that Radinas, standing before him, was a knight.

Although they’re a separate organization from the guards, they’re undoubtedly a force dedicated to maintaining order in Anesis.

If that’s the case, perhaps he might know what kind of person the man who had been staring at him earlier was.

With that thought, Rei spoke to Radinas.

“Actually, I know the person who… might have caused this incident.”

“...What?”

Radinas’s hand froze mid-bite as she looked up at Rei.

“Is that true?”

“Yeah. But I don’t have any hard evidence. It’s just circumstantial evidence, though.”

“Still, it’s better than having no leads at all. So? Who exactly was it?”

“Uh… Rather than that, maybe I should start by explaining how I recognized him in the first place?”

Rather than just describing the man’s appearance, wouldn’t it be easier to understand if I explained that gaze first?

Thinking this, Rei asked, and after a moment’s thought, Radinas nodded in response.

This incident wasn’t just a drug addict running amok.

To begin with, this commotion happened during the festival... in the midst of such a large crowd.

There was no way this was something that could be brushed off lightly.

Thanks to Rei and the others, the men lying on the ground were subdued—though it was quite rough to call it “subdued”—without causing any major commotion.

But if these men had gone on a rampage while the festival was in full swing… there would undoubtedly have been numerous injuries and deaths.

Just looking at the weapons the men were carrying made that clear.

Then, regarding the gaze Rei had felt... and how that gaze had continued to follow him even after the mock battle ended, leading him to realize that someone had been tailing him, Rei, Set, and Brewitt for some reason, bringing them here, where they were attacked. That’s how he explained it.

Listening to Rei’s explanation, Radinas nodded in understanding, though he shot him a slightly exasperated look.

“Listen. Why did you try to handle it yourself? You could have just gone and told the guards or something.”

“Well, even if you say that… At first, I didn’t even think they were after me. I actually thought Brewitt was the one being targeted.”

When Rei mentioned Brewitt’s name, Radinas fell silent.

That’s because he’d been told on the way here that Brewitt was a noble.

To be honest, Radinas couldn’t help but wonder why on earth a noble would be walking around dressed like that, without even a single bodyguard.

Still, the reason he didn’t voice that complaint directly was probably because, while Brewitt was a troublesome person, he was also unusually down-to-earth for a noble.

“Ah… I get the gist of the situation now. So? You’re saying the person who’s been staring at Rei the whole time is behind these guys?”

“I think so. Otherwise, they wouldn’t go out of their way to defeat this bunch and then come out on their own.”

“But you told Rei to come out, didn’t you? So, isn’t there a chance it’s someone else?”

“That’s a possibility, I suppose. But if you ask which scenario is more likely… well, I think the answer is obvious without even having to think about it.”

When put that way, Radinas couldn’t argue with Rei’s words.

With the situation lining up so perfectly, she had no choice but to admit that Rei’s theory was more likely than her own.

“I get the gist of it now. So, what kind of guy was he, anyway? Tell me what he looked like, or anything else that caught your attention.”

“Appearance aside, he’s definitely quite skilled… or at the very least, he possesses some kind of ability I can’t quite figure out. When he appeared, there were absolutely no signs beforehand, and when he vanished, he was gone before we knew it—even Set couldn’t pick up his scent.”

Having said that, Rei proceeded to describe the man’s appearance in more detail.
