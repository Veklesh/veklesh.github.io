"No, it wasn't me."

It was Augusto who answered Rei's question like that.

It was after another delicious meal prepared by Kathy, during the post-dinner break.

Rei, who had noticed the guard watching the mansion today suddenly disappear mid-duty, asked if it was one of Augusto's men. The answer was no.

"Not Augusto? He was observing the mansion, so I assumed you ordered him to Zarust."

"Hardly. He and I are on friendly terms, but he's not exactly my subordinate. ...Well, I'd like to make him one if possible, but I don't have the money for that right now."

Zalust is one of the adventurers in Gosh who could clearly be called part of the Augusto faction.

Compared to the Elema Trading Company led by Daldora, his faction is overwhelmingly outnumbered, making him a valuable asset in that sense.

Still, he remains an outsider, not truly a subordinate of Augusto.

His position is fundamentally different from the guards hired by Daldora—men gathered from neighboring towns and countries through the power of money.

The primary reason he couldn't do that was, as expected, largely a matter of money.

Zalust was a fairly well-known figure in this part of Gosh.

Precisely because of that, while hiring him for a short time, like when encountering Rei, was feasible, employing him permanently... well, even long-term was impossible.

"Then who took care of the guy spying on us? Well, it wasn't exactly a brutal takedown, but..."

Augusto is currently at odds with Daldora.

But even in this conflict, Daldora, who also serves as the head of the Elema Trading Company, vastly outstrips him in military strength, influence, economic power, and reach.

The only reason Augusto remains unharmed is because his personality is accepted by the residents of Gosh.

As evident from the mansion where Rei now resides, Augusto's ancestors once wielded significant influence in Gosh as great merchants.

But now that they have fallen into decline, that influence can no longer be relied upon.

In other words, the forces currently supporting Augusto are extremely small.

Many harbor affection for Augusto in their hearts, but most cannot openly act on it.

That's precisely why Rei was puzzled about why the person monitoring them today had disappeared partway through.

"It's not that anyone did anything. They probably just realized there was no point in watching Rei any longer and withdrew."

"...Do you really believe that?"

It's not good to say things you don't believe yourself. Hearing Rei's words, spoken with that very sentiment, Augusto offered a slightly timid smile and raised the glass in his hand to his lips.

Inside was not alcohol... but water.

However, it was water created by Rei from the flowing blade of his dagger. In Gosh... no, even within the Kingdom of Solrain, it was water that only a select few could taste, something that could be called heavenly nectar.

Enraptured by the taste of that water, Augusto continued.

"It's merely a possibility, mind you. But Daldora surely wouldn't want to anger you, Rei. I can't imagine them going so far as to monitor us."

"...Then the one watching us wasn't one of Daldora's men?"

Rei asked suspiciously, but in response, Augusto took his hand off the glass of water and nodded.

"Yes. To you, Rei-san, who lived in Gilm of the Mireana Kingdom, this town of Gosh might seem small. But it's still one of the five largest towns in the Solrain Kingdom. Naturally, various factions operate here..."

"You mean forces other than the Elema Trading Company got involved?"

"That's the likely scenario. And once Daldora learned of it, he probably ordered the faction monitoring it to back off."

Augusto's explanation wasn't entirely accurate, but it was close to the truth.

To be precise, Lord Ryu Brand of Gosh had heard rumors about Rei and taken an interest. As part of gathering information, he had ordered surveillance on Augusto's residence.

Upon learning this from his subordinates, Daldora had hastily ordered the surveillance withdrawn.

That he could predict the broad outline, if not every detail, was likely due to Augusto's abilities.

"So a third faction is involved? ...This is troublesome. And if things keep going like this, I might cause trouble for them too. Maybe I really should leave this house?"

"Don't be ridiculous."

The voice interrupting him belonged to Kathy, who was carrying a plate of simple food.

Though they had just finished eating, this was a dish meant as a snack with drinks—Rei had quickly stir-fried Sand Lizardman meat and seasoned it with spices.

...Though what Rei and Augusto were drinking wasn't alcohol, but water.

"Rei's still young, so he doesn't need to worry about others. Think of this place as your home and relax. Luckily, we have a spare room. Right?"

Cathy said this while turning her gaze toward Augusto.

Augusto, meeting her gaze, smiled and nodded as he reached for the Sand Lizardman meat.

"Yes. Of course. With Rei staying here, I can focus on my work with peace of mind."

For Augusto, having Rei stay at his mansion offered various benefits.

The greatest benefit, above all, was that as long as Rei and Set were in the house, Daldora couldn't pull any strange stunts. ...Yes. That was the case.

Now, however, the biggest benefit of Rei staying at his mansion was largely because Kathy liked him.

If Rei were in his thirties or forties, August might have felt uneasy leaving him alone with Kathy in the mansion. But Rei was still only in his mid-teens.

Cathy, Augusto's beloved wife, also sees Rei not as a man, but as a child-like presence.

(Though I suppose that's because she can't have children, isn't it?)

A momentary pang of sadness flashed through his mind.

Augusto and Cathy were, to anyone who saw them, a very loving couple.

Naturally, it wouldn't be strange for them to have children, but there was no sign of that happening.

Perhaps there was some reason within himself or his wife. He couldn't help but wonder, though he knew that even if he found out, he wouldn't have any solution.

In any case, for Cathy, Rei was like a child of her own, albeit a surrogate one.

...Both Cathy and Augusto were in their mid-thirties, while Rei looked like she was in her mid-teens. While the age gap might be a bit of a stretch, it wasn't unheard of for someone like Rei to have a child that age.

Moreover, Rei's face wasn't the rugged type one might imagine from the word "adventurer"; it was more delicate, almost feminine.

Given that, it wasn't strange that Kathy wanted to fuss over Rei.

"...So, um, what were we talking about?"

Sensing an unfamiliar atmosphere drifting around them, Rei opened her mouth, trying to break the tension.

Augusto would have liked to savor the current mood a little longer, but he couldn't ignore Rei's words and spoke up.

"It's about the third faction. ...Ah, naturally, there's no need for you to leave this mansion, Rei."

"Are you sure this is okay?"

Rei asked, seeking confirmation, and Augusto nodded in response.

"I hate to put it this way, but my current situation is far from secure. I think you know that well enough, Rei."

Those words reminded Rei of the sand pirates who had attacked aboard the sand ship.

It was the result of Daldora, head of the Elema Trading Company, maneuvering to eliminate Augusto, whom he found troublesome.

Of course, without any proof, there was no way to bring charges against Daldora.

But conversely, that meant he could be judged if evidence existed...

"Couldn't you extract any information from those sand pirates?"

Augusto opened his mouth, a slightly surprised expression on his face, as Rei asked curiously.

"You didn't know? The sand pirates from that time were all killed by someone the very next day. Thanks to that, we paid them a bit more than we would have if we'd sold them to slave traders."

The implication that it was hush money was clear, and indeed, Augusto had been explicitly told as much by the guards.

Augusto wasn't entirely convinced, knowing they could have fetched more money if sold properly. But saying anything more wouldn't change anything... In fact, it risked giving the guards a bad impression, so he had no choice but to quietly back down.

"Killed? Even though he was captured by the guards?"

"Yes. Considering such a threat exists, it would be a relief for me if you, Rei, could stay at this mansion. Cathy seems pleased about it too, you know."

"...Understood."

Rei also felt affection for Kathy, who doted on him.

Of course, that affection wasn't romantic; it was closer to what one feels for a mother. Perhaps it should be called affection.

Rei had never been particularly skilled at socializing, but Cathy had warmly welcomed him just as he was.

It would be unreasonable to expect him not to feel affection for someone like that.

"Then, let's leave this topic here. ...So, what are your plans for tomorrow, Rei?"

"Ideally, I'd like to take on a slaying request or something at the guild. Or, since there are quite a few Sand Lizardman corpses, I'd like to request their dismantling as a job."

Zalust had disassembled the Sand Serpent for free, but that was solely because he owed Rei a favor.

Now that debt was repaid, asking Zarust to do it for free again wouldn't be right.

(Ah, but Zarust is trustworthy. If he'd take it on in exchange for some Sand Lizardman materials as payment, that would really help us out.)

From their interactions so far, Ray understood Zarust had a conscientious nature.

Considering our relationship hadn't even lasted a full week yet, there was a possibility his conscientiousness was just a facade... but Rei felt it was probably fine.

It was nothing but a hunch, with no real basis whatsoever.

"Dismantling the Sand Lizardman? ...Well, that might be best. With a third faction emerging, venturing outside the city could lead to all sorts of trouble."

What crossed Augusto's mind as he said this was undoubtedly the sand pirates' attacks using sand ships.

It wasn't the kind of tactic anyone could pull off easily, which made it all the more striking.

(Considering that it resulted in Sand Reptile Man having his sand ship stolen by Rei, it was probably the worst possible outcome for them.)

On the flip side, it meant he'd deepened his connection with Rei. You never know how things will turn out in this world.

"Well, if they come after me about that, I'll deal with them. I need to practice my Two-Spear Style anyway."

"Two-Spear Style, you say?"

Rei gave Augusto a puzzled look at the words coming from his mouth.

He'd heard of the term "dual-sword style," but the phrase "dual-spear style" was unfamiliar.

"I don't know if that term actually exists, though."

"Judging by the name, does it involve using two spears?"

"Close enough. Well, one of them isn't a spear, it's a Death Scythe."

"Death Scythe... you mean that giant scythe? Isn't that a bit much to call a two-spear style?"

Rei must have recalled the Death Scythe he carried. He shrugged slightly at Augusto's puzzled question.

"Well, I just made up the term myself. I prioritized clarity when naming it. Anyway, mastering that Two-Spear Style is quite difficult. It requires not just training, but also getting used to it in actual combat to some extent."

"They say one day of real combat is worth ten days of practice," Rei continued.

Augusto wore a complex expression at Rei's words.

He knew Rei's skill. He knew it... but even so, could he really wield a spear and a scythe separately with one hand each? He couldn't shake that doubt.

Still, he didn't voice it. Probably because he'd seen Rei's strength with his own eyes.

With Rei, maybe he could pull off even such a reckless feat? It wasn't unreasonable to think that.
