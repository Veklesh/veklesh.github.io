On the night of the duel, despite it being winter, feasts were held throughout Gilm.

The festivities held during winter, and above all, the excitement of those who had witnessed the duel, kept the revelry alive.

...While most were joyfully drinking and eating, high on the mood, not everyone in Gilm was merrily celebrating, of course.

The nobles gathered at the royalist noble's mansion in the noble district drank bitterly, their expressions sour.

Though the offerings included expensive wine and dishes made with rare ingredients, the nobles gathered around them were utterly unable to savor them.

"Damn it! What is the Count of Eriell thinking!? To send such a fool!"

One noble slammed his cup down on the table, shouting as if he were cursing a mortal enemy.

The reactions of those who heard him split into two.

One group glared bitterly at the shouting noble, while the other shouted in agreement.

Though gathered at this mansion as supporters of the King, the nobles belonged to several factions within the King's camp, precisely because the Royalist faction was the largest of the three major factions in the Mireana Kingdom.

Those wearing bitter expressions belonged to the same faction as the one who had sent the keep.

The nobles agreeing with the shouting noble belonged to the other factions.

The royalist nobles were neatly divided into two groups, but one of the latter nobles spoke up.

"If this continues, the King's faction's reputation will be utterly ruined. It's bad enough that we tried to forcibly take a maid from a poor noble house like the Baron Sysne family, but then we escalated it to a duel and suffered a crushing defeat. What were they thinking, betting half the Count Eriell family's fortune, and having it legally processed?"

"Indeed. What on earth possessed that man to do such a thing..."

"Precisely because he wasn't thinking at all. In any case, what that fool Keep did was the height of foolishness. And the biggest problem is that he belongs to the royalist faction. ...I must ask, what were your factions thinking when you sent such a man to Gilm?"

The accusatory glances fell upon those belonging to the faction that had engineered Keep's dispatch to Gilm.

One of them, wearing a bitter expression, spoke up.

"To be honest, we never imagined he'd pull off something this idiotic. The original plan was to stir up a moderate amount of trouble, prompting the upper echelons of the royalist faction to intervene, driving a wedge between the neutralists and the aristocrats..."

"But for it to end like this... truly... who could have imagined it would cause such a foolish problem?"

"Indeed, indeed. Those in the capital who sent such a fool deserve blame. We here in Gilm must join hands as the royalist faction and cooperate."

The convenient words of the faction that sent Keep drew displeased expressions from the others.

Yet they held their complaints, knowing they had no choice but to cooperate.

Indeed, they were nobles belonging to the King's faction, the largest faction within the Mireana Kingdom.

But if that's the case, Gilm is also the stronghold of the neutral faction.

If they were to turn on each other here, there was a risk that neutral nobles, including Daskar, or even nobles from the noble faction, might interfere in some strange way.

To prevent that, it was indeed necessary for us to unite as the Royalist faction.

After all, today's duel had inflicted no small wound upon the King's faction.

"...Indeed, we must cooperate."

With that single statement, the immediate blame game was shelved.

With that settled, the next issue was Keip.

"So, what do you intend to do about that wet pants guy?"

At the muttered remark from one noble, everyone burst out laughing.

No one present could have imagined that the heir apparent to the Earl's house would actually wet himself in public.

"That's our Lord Wet-Pants for you. He'll probably resort to some rash action."

"Like, attacking the Baron Sysne household, slaughtering everyone, and pretending it never happened?"

"But he already tried that, didn't he? And in the end, he was repelled by the adventurers hired to guard the manor... Even Lord Wetpants wouldn't resort to a tactic that failed once, would he?"

"Precisely because it's His Dampness, I think he'd gamble on a Hail Mary if he had no other options."

"Hmm. Then what should we do? Inform Marquis Ralks about this matter? That would at least show the royalist faction isn't aware of Lord Wetpants' actions, wouldn't it?"

"...Hmm, that might be a good idea."

The royalist nobles all nodded in agreement and were about to send someone to the lord's manor immediately when—

The sound of a door being violently thrown open echoed through the room, drawing the gazes of everyone present toward the doorway.

That gaze turned accusatory, which was only natural considering the nobles present.

The lord of the manor shouted irritably at the rude intruder.

"What are you doing?!"

But the man being yelled at wore an expression that seemed more relieved than anything else, and he quickly approached the master.

The mansion's master must have found this behavior puzzling. He waited, his brow slightly furrowed, as the man drew nearer.

Then, the man whispered the information he had just received into the master's ear.

"You fool! That's far too soon!"

He shouted, as if unable to believe it.

Naturally, the other nobles who had been watching intently were now drawn in, curious to know what had happened.

But the nobleman, master of this mansion, opened his mouth without seeming to notice the curious stares of his peers.

To relay the information he had just received to his companions.

"I just received a report from my subordinate. ...It seems he's headed for the House of Baron Sysne with all the forces Keep can muster. Over fifty men."

"What!?"

At that report, every person present let out a gasp of shock.

They had certainly anticipated the possibility of Keep attacking the Baron Sysne household.

But they never imagined he'd suddenly make such a move tonight, of all nights.

"That's way too fast to act, no matter what!"

"He's an idiot, so he probably just acted without thinking!"

"Over fifty men? How did he gather that many?"

"What do we do!? If that wet-bed's actions drag us into this mess, it'll be a disaster!"

The utterly unexpected move made it difficult for the gathered royalist nobles to agree on a course of action.

Time rewinds.

After the duel ended and the winter sunset faded into night, Keep was tearing his room apart in a rage.

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

He swung a spear displayed nearby with great force, taking his frustration out on the furniture in the room.

Having never trained his body, Keep's spear swings were slow and lacked sharpness.

Still, he managed to break the furniture in the room.

Of course, sturdy pieces like the desk only served to send intense numbness through Keep's arms.

Finding even that irritating, he kept swinging the spear, smashing furniture...

"That's right... I'm not at fault. No matter who looks, no matter how you look at it, I'm not at fault. I'm not at fault. The fault lies with that poor noble who defied me. How dare that lowly pauper, with a lower title than mine, defy me... In the first place, if those bastards were gone, all my blunders would be erased. Yes, that means if I just eliminate that nuisance..."

The reason for this duel had already been conveniently rewritten within Keep as entirely the fault of the Baron Sysne family.

"If only that root of all evil weren't here, I could still... still start over. Yes, no matter who the opponent is, it's the fault of whoever interfered with me. I'm not at fault. It's their fault for attacking me. That's right, that must be it."

Keep muttered to himself while staring at the wall scarred by his spear strike, but his murmurs faded as the sound of knocking reached his ears.

"Who is it?"

"It's me, Lord Keep. As I said before the duel, preparations to attack the Baron Sysne household are already complete. All that remains is for you to give the order and lead the charge into the Baron Sysne household. Then everything will be settled."

Keep's eyes lit up for a moment at Fultos' words as he opened the door and appeared... but when told he would lead the charge, he hesitated.

"W-What? I'm supposed to lead the charge? Why must I do something so dangerous?"

He usually acted so high and mighty, yet the moment he might actually face danger, he immediately backed down.

Fultos knew Keep's character well, but he still couldn't stop himself from inwardly clicking his tongue.

(For me to escape this situation, someone else needs to cause a major disturbance elsewhere to draw attention away. Given the high profile this man gained from today's incident, having him cause a scene is the best option. I have to somehow get him to agree...)

Losing this duel meant Keep's disinheritance was inevitable.

And it was obvious that Fultos, who had come to Gilm as Keep's subordinate, wouldn't get off scot-free either.

Getting fired would be bad enough, but worse still, he could literally lose his head. I absolutely refused to suffer that fate caught in the crossfire of a man like Keep.

Given that, the only option was to draw Keep's attention and, in the meantime, take as much treasure as possible and vanish.

For Fultos, it was essential that Keep made a conspicuous attack on the Baroness Sysne's household.

"Rest assured, Keep-sama. We've gathered over fifty men. No matter how strong Rei is, he can't do anything against such overwhelming numbers. And if you lead everyone to attack the Baron Cysne household now, you'll get that maid you wanted. But if you're not there... well, without your command, those men might do whatever they want with her."

"...What?"

Keep, who had been trembling until now, froze at Fultos's words.

Even Fultos, who had thought it would probably be fine, showed a faint look of surprise.

(What? Why is he so fixated on that maid? She wasn't exactly a beauty worth going to such lengths for. Or is it that he ended up in this mess because he tried to get her, so now he wants at least her? Either way, it suits me just fine.)

Even if Fultos didn't understand the reason for such intense fixation on Asie, the mere fact that Keep showed willingness was enough.

"Therefore, the best course of action would be for Lord Keep to lead an assault on the Sisne household and make this entire affair disappear. That maid, too, would surely not refuse to come under Lord Keep's service once the Baron Sisne she serves is dead."

Such blatant words.

Under normal circumstances, even Keep would have sensed something off about Fultos's attitude.

But the Keep of the moment could hardly be called sane.

Anger, shame, desire—all manner of negative emotions existed within Keep, raging wildly.

When told all of that could be resolved, Keep couldn't resist.

"Alright, I'm going! I'm going! I'll make the bastard who put me through this pay! The others are usable, right!? You didn't just gather useless trash like in today's duel, did you?!"

Even then, his need to confirm revealed Keep's cowardice.

"Yes, we've gathered many skilled fighters. You should be able to rage as you please, Keep-sama. Please unleash that frustration to your heart's content. You are the next head of the Eriell Count's household; such a poor baron's family is a worthless opponent."

"Right. Of course it is. I am absolutely correct. It's wrong to be bothered by such scum in the first place. Yes, let them vent my pent-up frustration!"

Still gripping his spear, Keep exits the room.

Seeing the knights waiting in the hallway follow after him, a smile touched Fultos' lips.

Though it was a smile that could only be called scorn.

"Well then, the decoy is drawing attention spectacularly, keeping the others occupied. We should get out of this mansion as quickly as possible too. We'll gladly take any valuables we find, so do your best to stand out, Lord Keep."

Despite the honorific, his words held no respect whatsoever. Muttering this, Fultos left the room to prepare his escape from the mansion.
