The sun set, and darkness enveloped the surroundings.

Even so, outside the forest, light was maintained by bonfires.

If this were a routine request, they would chat for a while, then eventually leave a watch and sleep.

But the people gathered here were different.

The real investigation begins now... tonight, after dark.

"...I've heard about it, but are they really coming? We went into the forest during the day, and there wasn't a single monster in sight, right?"

One of the adventurers muttered, gazing at Treant Forest while sitting before the bonfire.

Clouds floated in the night sky, completely obscuring the moon.

During the day, the sky had been nearly cloudless, with warm sunlight streaming down from the sun.

Despite that spring-like weather, the night sky now held an eerie, ominous quality.

(Maybe it just feels that way because we're near Treant Forest.)

Another adventurer standing nearby murmurs to himself while scanning the surroundings.

A short distance from the forest, several campfires now burned.

Each group gathered around their own fire, either by party or by the teams that had acted together during the day.

With so many people, the number of fires inevitably grew large.

Initially, someone suggested lighting a single massive bonfire, but concerns about whether it could be extinguished quickly in an emergency led to the current arrangement.

"Hey, honestly, what do you think about this? I'm not really trusting that whole setup."

Apparently annoyed by the man's silence, the adventurer harboring resentment toward Treant Forest pressed the man he'd been paired with this time.

This team consists of one alchemist and five adventurers, totaling six members.

More precisely, it was two adventurer parties of three cooperating.

The alchemist wasn't present, having gone to exchange opinions with other researchers, and some of the adventurers were also absent, engaged in information sharing elsewhere.

As a result, one member from each of the two parties present had been left behind.

The adventurer addressed opened his mouth, looking slightly annoyed.

"Well, what do you expect me to say? Since some of us have actually been attacked, it's obvious there are enemies out there. ...Though, they might not be here right now."

Though he didn't truly believe it, he sensed the man who'd spoken up had soured the moment he mentioned enemies, and hastily added.

The sullen man seemed slightly appeased by this, and as he gazed back at Treant Forest, he spoke again.

"...You know nothing, yet you speak so freely..."

A short distance away from the two, Slesha, who had overheard their conversation, muttered, suppressing her anger.

If there was nothing wrong with Treant Forest, then what had attacked her... and her companions?

Having been attacked herself, Slesha felt nothing but disgust.

"Calm down. ...Here, drink this."

Runo, sitting beside her, handed her a cup.

Inside was tea.

Of course, given that it was tea meant to be drunk in a place like this, it wasn't properly brewed using tea leaves.

It was made by boiling tea leaves in a small pot placed over a campfire using a simple stove.

For those who truly appreciate proper tea or black tea, it was utterly unacceptable... yet for adventurers, it was a common method, considered sufficient for enjoying tea.

...Though it's also true that more people preferred alcohol over tea or black tea.

"Thank you."

Slesha drank the tea she received, yet she still couldn't completely quell the anger she felt toward the two standing a short distance away.

"Calm down. If you're so tense now, you won't be able to perform when it really counts."

One of the adventurers in their team called out to Slesha.

Among them, Slesha had the lowest rank.

It was only natural, since she didn't originally possess the rank required to participate in this investigation request.

That very fact meant the other adventurers kept a close eye on Slesha.

Of course, the fact that Slesha was a young woman undoubtedly played a part in that.

Had someone cocky and arrogant taken Slesha's place, the other adventurers would have taken notice in a different way.

...Though, to be fair, it's doubtful such a person could have gotten Runo to bow his head and agree to work together.

"I know. I know... but still, I just can't help it..."

Being spoken to seemed to calm her a little. Slesha murmured softly as she sipped her tea.

"Idiots like that are everywhere. People who can't see reality for what it is, who think their own common sense is absolute. ...You can't really say people like that are cut out to be adventurers."

"Aah! Hey, you there!"

The adventurer talking to Slesha must have heard them.

The adventurer who had just claimed there were no monsters in Treant Forest now yelled angrily at Slesha and the others.

Since they could hear the conversation over there, it was entirely possible they could hear Slesha and the others talking too.

"Calm down, man. Look, you're drawing attention. Don't you know what happens when you stand out in a place like this?"

The man who had been encouraging Slesha spoke with a hint of provocation.

But even if his tone was provocative, what he said wasn't wrong.

If they caused trouble while on a quest, the guild would certainly take issue with it.

Especially since this job came directly from the guild itself.

It could very well affect their evaluation for rank advancement.

The adventurer who yelled probably knew that too.

Still, for a man with a hot-headed temperament, dealing with the one who provoked him came first.

He stood up abruptly and started walking toward the campfire where Runo and Slesha were...

"Whoa!"

Suddenly, his neck tightened, halting his movement.

Could it be someone from his own team?

With that thought, he glared back in annoyance, only to find a man's face frozen in shock.

At least, he judged that this man hadn't done anything to him, and tried to remove whatever was choking his neck.

But it was wrapped so tightly around his neck that he couldn't even slip a finger between it.

A knife or something like that, he thought. In that instant, he was abruptly pulled backward.

With such tremendous force that he could barely keep his footing.

"Guh!"

Almost reflexively, he drew his longsword from its scabbard at his waist and slashed out, still unable to see his attacker.

The sensation returning through the blade made him realize he had struck something of considerable thickness.

From the moment he realized something had wrapped around his neck to this point had taken only seconds.

This sharp judgment was likely why the man had been able to participate in this rank-restricted quest.

"Enemy attack! Enemy attack! Monsters are emerging from Treant Forest!"

Runo, who had watched the sequence of events, shouted, but by then, most of the others nearby had already drawn their weapons and assumed combat stances.

The man whose neck was being squeezed turned toward the forest, his longsword raised.

And then, finally, the man realized the true nature of the entity that had been choking him.

It was a vine.

Despite being cut in half midway, it still writhed on the ground like a snake.

"Damn it!"

The man forcibly tore away the vine still wrapped around his neck, heedless of the claw-like scratches it left on his skin.

"Danger!"

The man slammed the vine remnants that had been wrapped around his neck onto the ground and, at the sound of the warning, instinctively leapt backward.

His ability to make such split-second judgments proved he had the skill to handle this job, even if he was hot-tempered and quick to fight.

In any case, something fell from the spot where the man had been standing just an instant before.

"What the hell!?"

The man shouted, looking up at the sky. What he saw... was a gigantic flower, existing at a height higher than the surrounding trees.

If Rei had seen that flower, he would probably have shouted, 'A sunflower!?'

Indeed, the flower bore a striking resemblance to a sunflower.

But, naturally, it was different in many ways.

For starters, sunflowers aren't this enormous.

What the adventurers now saw stood about ten meters tall, with a flower head exceeding three meters in diameter.

And inside the flower were seeds... those very seeds were what had fallen from the sky earlier.

"Whoa, whoa, whoa, whoa! When did that flower even grow?! There was no flower like that during the day, right?!"

A man who had narrowly avoided being hit by the seed a few moments earlier shouted in panic.

Considering the seed's power and what happens if it implants itself... it's easy to imagine just how dangerous these seeds are.

"More are coming!"

As one of the adventurers shouted, a Treant appeared, weaving through the gaps between the trees.

True to its name, the Forest of Treants, the number of treants was considerable.

Moreover, treants like the one that had just strangled the man's neck were now stretching out from the forest, weaving through the trunks.

"Look! Trees are growing rapidly out of the ground!"

Another adventurer, different from the first, shouted. Instinctively, the adventurers around him looked down at the ground. True to his words, trees were sprouting from the earth at a speed clearly visible to the naked eye.

A growth rate unimaginable under normal circumstances.

It was a sight that seemed to explain why the Treant Forest was expanding at such a tremendous pace.

But even as they were astonished by this, the adventurers present knew their priority wasn't observing the Treant Forest, but dealing with the various monsters emerging from it.

...That said, leaving the adventurers to their task, the researchers stood frozen in place, each intently observing the Treant Forest with keen interest.

"Look! Growing at this speed means it's definitely using the magic flowing through the earth."

"I get that, but what about those monsters over there?"

"You mean that giant flower scattering seeds? Isn't that the one using the magic flowing through the earth to grow that big?"

"Hmm... But enough magic to sustain a forest expanding at this scale? Well, it might manage at first, but wouldn't the magic eventually run dry?"

"...You mean it's getting nutrients or magic from some source other than magic?"

"Come to think of it, several adventurer parties have gone missing here, right?"

"Considering the numbers, I think that's a bit unlikely."

The adventurers grew increasingly irritated at the researchers, who were casually having such a conversation while they were fighting.

Especially among those left behind as the researchers' guards, not participating in the fight, some couldn't help but think, What the hell are they talking about so leisurely...

But considering the researchers' true natures, it was probably more accurate to say they each wanted to go to places that interested them and thoroughly observe or collect samples.

They were restraining themselves, gathering in one place to make it easier for the adventurers to protect them. You could say they were being quite cooperative.

...Whether the escorts acknowledge that is another matter entirely.

Amidst the growing frustration and irritation among the guarding adventurers, the tide of battle gradually turned in the adventurers' favor.

This is because adventurers who had been stationed slightly farther away began arriving one after another to provide escort.

This quest had a rank restriction to begin with, meaning the participating adventurers were reasonably skilled.

Given that, monsters emerging from the forest, including the Treant, weren't something to fear unless they launched a surprise attack.

Most could hold their own against a Treant, or even surpass it.

"Alright! If reinforcements keep coming like this, victory is ours for sure!"

One of the adventurers shouted as he smashed the Treant's torso with his hammer.

Hearing that, the other adventurers naturally roared in encouragement.

But... cutting them off, one adventurer shouted.

"Our forces are strong, but we don't know how many enemies are out there! This isn't a quick fight—we need to plan for a long battle!"

Shouting, the man chanted a spell and swung his staff.

What he unleashed were needles made of stone.

Over thirty stone needles pierced the Treants emerging from the forest and the vines slithering across the ground like snakes, riddling them with holes one after another.

He was a wizard renowned for his mastery of earth magic.

The way he slaughtered enemies using spells with low mana cost was that of a seasoned mage.

Hearing the wizard's shouts, the adventurer who had been shouting so boldly moments before yelled back, looking displeased.

"If you're scared stiff, then get lost!"

"...I'm not so sure about that."

With that shout, a shadow passed right beside the man who had yelled and swung a massive weapon.

That single swing severed Treant at the torso, and he collapsed to the ground under his own weight.

"Considering how unnatural this forest is, conserving strength over rushing into a quick fight isn't such a bad idea."

Shouldering the massive weapon... the Death Scythe, Rei murmured this.
