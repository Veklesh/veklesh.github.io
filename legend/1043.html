"So this is the Dark Elf settlement... Wait, what the hell is that huge tree?! You couldn't see it at all from outside!"

Earth, having entered the Dark Elf settlement, shouted as if unable to contain himself.

Rei , who had been watching him, thought that was a perfectly normal reaction.

But at the same time, he also thought, "Even though he didn't seem surprised when he saw Set..."

The World Tree had been completely invisible from outside, but once inside the settlement, there was no way to hide its presence.

Reaching a height of two hundred meters, its sheer size was bound to catch anyone's eye.

"Polo, Porurururu!"

Polo, perched on Earth's left shoulder, seemed excited by the World Tree's presence, wagging its tail like a dog or something.

Its nine soft-looking tails swung wildly, repeatedly hitting Earth's face... but Earth, seemingly used to being hit by those tails, showed no particular concern. After finishing his shout, he was utterly captivated by the World Tree.

"Surprised?"

"...Yeah, I guess I am. I've been to all sorts of places, but I've never seen a tree that huge before."

Stunned, Earth replied to Rei.

"And it's got this... weird presence, doesn't it? Kind of like that impression I got from that divine statue I saw in some dungeon before... though not quite this intense."

At those words, this time it was Rei who was startled.

The mention of a divine statue in a dungeon.

Of course, each dungeon possesses vastly different characteristics.

After all, there were even altars like the one where Elena performed her succession ritual, so it wouldn't be strange for there to be statues of deities.

He understood that, yet even so, Rei couldn't hide his surprise at Earth's words.

"You went into a dungeon?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah, quite a while back. I went down with a party led by this guy I know, Nicholas."

"Did you clear it?"

"No way. We couldn't even make it to the lowest level. We went down to the third basement floor, got the material we needed, and got the hell out of there. I've cleared some shallow dungeons elsewhere with friends, though."

"Poro-ro-ro-ro!"

For some reason, Poro let out a proud-sounding chirp at Earth's words.

Seeing Poro like that, Earth smiled softly and reached out to him.

Polo seemed to enjoy being stroked.

While stroking Polo, Earth opened his mouth with a pleased smile.

"There were powerful monsters where the materials were. And the reason we could defeat those monsters was because Poro was there. That's why he's acting all proud like this."

Though his words carried a hint of complaint, the expression on his face was gentle.

Despite all his grumbling, it was clear he cherished Polo.

"Still..."

Suddenly stopping his hand from stroking Polo, Earth's gaze shifted toward Rei.

The look in his eyes held pure astonishment.

"I'm a tamer too, but how did you manage to befriend a monster like a griffin?"

It was a natural question for any tamer.

A rank A monster like a griffin. And considering it was also a rare species, it was effectively an S-rank monster.

Even to Earth, taming such a monster was astonishing.

Being a tamer himself, Earth understood just how monumental an achievement it was.

That he didn't seem as surprised as he claimed to be was probably because he was a tamer himself.

He understood from personal experience that tamers' methods for befriending—taming—monsters varied greatly from person to person.

After all, tamers possess unique talents, and depending on their approach, even taming a dragon isn't that unusual.

Earth gazed at Yellow, who was sprawled across Set's back, and thought.

This man, known by the epithet Crimson, was different.

(Guess this is the kind of guy who becomes a hero. ...I can't let myself fall behind. I'll absolutely become a man called a hero.)

Ever since he was little, he'd loved tales of heroes. Over and over and over again, practically every night, he'd beg his parents to tell him stories of heroes.

Having grown up that way, it was only natural that he began aspiring to be a hero himself.

At first, he was just a child who understood nothing.

Looking back now, it makes me want to punch myself, to grab my head and roll around on the ground in frustration.

Even so, the adults around me, adventurers like Birsh, Sannison, and Shines, and above all, Liv—who I can confidently say was my closest adventurer companion—didn't mock me. They supported my dream of becoming a hero.

Since then, I've been caught up in countless incidents, surviving each one alongside my partner, Polo.

The result of all that is his current status as a Rank C adventurer.

Being a Rank C adventurer before even turning twenty was certainly impressive enough to be called skilled.

But... even so, compared to the achievements of the small boy standing before Earth, it was undeniably inferior by more than just a notch or two.

He couldn't afford to lose to a boy younger than himself.

He revealed his innate positivity and renewed his resolve.

Earth had faced countless crises before, and it was this very positivity that had allowed him to overcome them.

"Hey, um... Crimson?"

"Just call me Rei."

Responding to Earth's words, Rei stroked Set, who was rubbing his head against her.

Though Rei was used to being called Crimson, she still preferred being addressed by her actual name.

"I see. Well then, Rei. That griffin... Set, right? Can I ask how you tamed him?"

At Earth's question, Rei inwardly sighed, thinking, "Here it comes."

Since she was officially recognized as having tamed Set, she knew this question would come eventually from a fellow tamer.

That's precisely why he remained unruffled, recalling his own backstory as he spoke.

"I was raised by a wizard deep in the mountains since I was little. We've been together since then... since I was born, really. So if you ask how I tamed him, I can only say it just happened. ...Right?"

"Gururuu"

In response to Rei's call, Set growled in agreement.

Watching this scene from Earth's left shoulder, Polo tilted his head slightly, looking a bit puzzled.

"Pororo?"

Perhaps sensing something odd about Set, Pororo turned to look at Rei and Set.

As a rare species equivalent to a Rank A monster, perhaps he sensed something about Set.

Watching Polo's expression, Rei panicked, thinking it might be a bit awkward if the topic continued. He quickly changed the subject.

"Hey, you're the one with that rare monster, a Carbuncle... And a rare species at that? Where the heck did you find it?"

"Huh? Oh, Polo? Polo was near my village. Back then, I didn't think it was a Carbuncle, just some squirrel-like monster. But even so, thanks to this guy, I've gotten out of dangerous situations countless times. Right?"

"Pororu!"

At Earth's words, Polo proudly purred.

Seeing Polo like that, those watching nearby felt a warm, fuzzy feeling.

A Carbuncle alone was rare, but this one was a rare species with nine tails. In terms of rarity, it wasn't inferior to Set, the rare species of Griffin, or Yellow, the Black Dragon's child.

(Come to think of it, having three such rare monsters gathered together is pretty amazing. If there were anyone researching monsters, they'd definitely want to be here. They'd pay any amount of money for it.)

As Earth pondered this, Polo leapt from his left shoulder, landing right beside Yellow, who was sprawled across Set's back.

Considering its small size, it possessed remarkably powerful jumping ability.

Moreover, its nine tails swayed with every movement, making one want to reach out and touch them.

"...Hmm, ahem. So, what are we actually going to do now? For now, bringing Earth into this settlement seems fine with both Marina and Optis."

Rei, who had been captivated by the nine tails without realizing it, cleared his throat softly and asked Marina.

While Rei personally felt like exploring the forest a bit more, Marina was the one who had requested this mission.

Besides, Marina knew far more about this village than Rei did. ...Even though it was the place she'd returned to after nearly a century.

"Yes, well. I'd like to explore the forest if possible, but what should we do about Earth? Grandfather and I are one thing, but if Ragdo saw it, it might cause another commotion."

Recalling the previous day's incident, everyone unconsciously wore expressions of understanding.

Ragdo's attitude toward Rei had been blatantly hostile.

That had ultimately been resolved by the new moon ring Rei wore, but in Earth's case, there was little reason to convince Ragdo.

It might be rare as a Carbuncle subspecies, but whether that would satisfy Ragdo was questionable.

(Well, maybe it's fine as long as we don't introduce him to Ragdo. Besides, the World Tree issue has been resolved now that the treatment is complete. All that's left is to solve the forest's anomaly... I see. In that case, Earth, who has tamed a Carbuncle, could be a valuable asset.)

Pondering for a moment, she suddenly looked up at the sky.

The sun had reached its zenith, meaning it was nearly noon.

"...How about we eat first, then head back to the forest this afternoon? What about you, Earth? Looks like you didn't bring any food, though."

"Well, I did bring some, actually..."

Saying this, Earth pulled dried meat and hardened bread from the pouch at his waist.

While this was a standard meal for adventurers, it seemed rather meager to the group, who had grown accustomed to warm, delicious meals thanks to Rei's Misty Ring—far better than what they'd get at shoddy village or town eateries.

Still, Earth had his reasons.

Dried meat and bread were, after all, preserved food. They had originally intended to hunt animals or monsters in the forest for their meals.

Polo, in particular, excelled at hunting.

His keen senses allowed him to locate prey, and he could instantly finish it off with his signature lightning strike.

He could paralyze without killing, but since he ultimately killed anyway, finishing it off was quicker.

Especially the simultaneous discharge of the Nine-Tailed Lightning, made possible by the effects of becoming a Nine-Tailed Fox, possessed power truly worthy of a Rank A monster.

Because both Earth and Polo excelled at hunting, they only carried about three meals' worth of preserved food.

Earth was about to say that, but Marina spoke first.

"Then let's eat together. We wouldn't dream of not offering a meal to someone who came all this way from Cross out of concern for the forest."

"...Well, I did provide the ingredients..."

"Oh? So Rei expects Earth to make do with just that preserved food?"

Vihera asked in response to Rei's words, but Rei shook his head at that too.

"I didn't say that. I just thought you could have asked my opinion too."

"Heh heh, asking Rei wouldn't have changed anything, would it? If it were up to Rei, would he make Earth eat preserved food for lunch? While the rest of us eat warm meals?"

Faced with that, Rei couldn't say anything more and agreed to invite Earth to eat.

"So, how about Earth joining us for lunch? We're planning to serve oak meat today, after all."

Rei announced, his gaze fixed on Marina. She nodded without any particular change in expression.

As a Dark Elf, she fundamentally disliked orcs... and as Marina, possessing exceptional beauty even among Dark Elves, that was only natural. But when it came to eating them, it was a different matter.

In this regard, she shared the peculiar sensibilities common among dark elves, much like Jusla and Audban.

"Is that okay? Well, if you're treating us to dinner, we'd be delighted to join."

Ears turned his gaze to Rei and asked, his expression bright with delight at his good fortune.

Now that they were inside the settlement, they'd have to go outside to hunt for prey.

Buying cooked food from a shop was another option, but even so, Earth preferred not to spend too much money if possible.

Since he wasn't exactly rolling in cash to begin with, if someone offered to treat him to lunch, he was more than happy to accept.

(I never thought bow maintenance would cost that much.)

He recalled the amount he'd paid for maintaining his companion—a different kind of partner than Polo—on his back.

He had come to this forest in part to recoup some of the money spent on bow maintenance, so any opportunity to save was most welcome.

Meanwhile, Optis ignored Marina's request to hear Ragdo's report and promptly left to join the banquet celebrating the World Tree's recovery where his acquaintances were gathered. Marina watched him go with a broad smile—though her eyes didn't quite join in—and that was that.
