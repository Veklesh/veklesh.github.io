Rei takes a sip of the soup set before him.

The rich umami of animal extracts spreads through his mouth.

There aren’t any specific ingredients in it; the fact that it achieves such a rich flavor purely from the broth alone is, even to Rei, genuinely impressive.

Although it’s seasoned with nothing but salt, that simple seasoning somehow works surprisingly well with the rich umami.

Of course, producing a flavor of this caliber must require a high level of culinary skill… From Rei’s perspective, he could tell the skill was exceptional, but he couldn’t quite grasp just how advanced it actually was.

Still, there was no doubt that the soup itself was of a quality that could truly be called exquisite.

(I seem to recall reading somewhere that high-grade meat, like A5-grade, tastes better when simply seasoned with salt and pepper and grilled rather than overcomplicated with seasoning… Is this the same kind of thing?)

He thought that for a moment, but for now, Rei simply voiced his honest impression to Georgima, who was sitting across from him.

“Delicious.”

“Is that so?”

Georgima nodded at Rei’s words, but there was no joy in her expression.

It was as if she were thinking that her skills were still immature, that she wanted to master a higher level of technique.

“Judging by your expression, it seems you’re not quite satisfied yet.”

“Yeah. This soup was made using orc meat and bones, but aside from the umami, the gamey smell hasn’t been completely removed, and the flavor is a bit muddled. There’s still some bitterness and astringency, so it’ll probably take a while before it’s perfected.”

To Rei, the soup tasted perfectly delicious, but Georgima still had some complaints.

That said, considering she served this soup for lunch the very next day after Rei explained ramen to her, her development speed must far exceed that of an ordinary cook.

“Is that so? I think it’s delicious enough.”

“Is this really the right kind of soup for a dish called ramen?”

When asked that, Rei was about to answer that it was slightly different, but fell silent on the spot.

After all, his knowledge of ramen was limited to what he’d read in books.

Since he hadn’t actually eaten it himself, he couldn’t possibly say that the taste of the soup in front of him was different.

If Rei said something like that, people would surely wonder how he could possibly know the taste of a dish he’d never even eaten.

“Ah, well, for now, I think the soup is heading in the right direction.”

“Really? But with a rich broth like this, the salt might get a bit overpowered…”

“……Ah.”

Hearing the words “rich soup,” Rei recalls a certain soup.

For a moment, she hesitates, wondering if she should say it, but Georgima would probably figure it out even without Rei saying anything, and if Rei is going to eat ramen, she wants it to be the best possible ramen.

Reaching that conclusion, he spoke the name of the soup he’d remembered.

“I just remembered there’s a soup called ‘Double Soup’…”

“What kind of soup is that?”

The soup served to Rei had a decent flavor, but it was, after all, only decent.

From Georgima’s perspective, there were still more things to be dissatisfied with.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange for her to want to hear about it if there was even a possibility of improving the soup’s flavor.

As Georgima eagerly asked, Rei prefaced his answer with his usual disclaimer.

“Just so you know, just like with my ramen knowledge, all I know is what’s written in books. Keep that in mind when you listen.”

“I know. Right now, I just want some advice.”

“……This soup was made by simmering oak bones, right?”

“Yeah. Of course, besides the orc bones, I used plenty of aromatic vegetables to mask the smell.”

“I figured. I don’t think a bit of gaminess like this is a problem. But it bothers you, doesn’t it, Georgima?”

Although Rei possessed senses far sharper than those of an ordinary person, even his keen sense of taste wasn’t that highly developed.

No matter how sharp one’s sense of taste is, it’s useless unless it’s properly honed.

In that sense, Rei’s sense of taste was absolutely lacking in experience.

“Right. So, what exactly is this ‘double soup’?”

“Simply put, it’s just mixing the seafood broth with this oak broth.”

“……I see. I know that cooking method exists, but do you actually use it in ramen?”

He had assumed she would be surprised by his words, but Rei was actually more surprised to find that Georgima already knew about that cooking method.

Of course, Georgima was somewhat surprised as well, but Rei’s surprise was even stronger.

But then he reminded himself that Georgima had deep knowledge of cooking, so it wasn’t strange that she knew about it.

“Still, the soup affects the noodles too. …What about that part?”

When he asked that, Georgima shook her head as well.

“No good. To begin with, there’s nothing we can do unless we know what ‘kansui’ actually is. …Since it’s called ‘kansui,’ I suppose it’s water, but…”

“Even Georgima, with her extensive knowledge and skills in cooking, finds kansui difficult?”

Rei had assumed from the name “kansui” that it was a liquid, but it was actually possible that it was a different ingredient with that name.

“I’ll find it eventually. …But until then, I suppose we should use this broth for udon soup instead of ramen.”

“That’s probably the best choice.”

Rei wasn’t sure if udon would go well with ramen broth.

No, actually, since he wasn’t even sure if this broth could be called ramen broth in the first place, it was probably too early to worry about that.

(It’s not ramen, but udon, soba, or somen served in the broth of a kiritanpo hot pot are delicious. Thinking about that, I could just add udon directly to this soup, or I could make a double broth and add udon to that.)

While Rei was thinking that, Georgima was pondering something else nearby.

What kind of seafood broth would hold its own against this cloudy pork bone—or, to be precise, oak bone—broth?

(To create a strong flavor, I need dried ingredients… shellfish? Or perhaps dried octopus or squid… Come to think of it, I had some dried kraken. If I use that…)

Georgima quickly ran through the ingredients currently in the kitchen, thinking of ways to give the soup a strong flavor.

Rei’s idea of a “double soup” had come as a surprise to Georgima as well.

Considering the nature of soup, it wouldn’t have been strange if she’d thought of it herself.

Once she had a rough plan for the seafood soup, she stood up.

“I’ve got a general idea now. I’m going to use this soup made from the orc’s bones and try making a fish soup or something similar. Once that’s ready, I’ll bring it over, so please taste it then. I want to experiment with the noodles too.”

“I get the soup and noodles, but what about the toppings?”

“I’m making progress on that front too. It’s just that the toppings aren’t that difficult to make… or rather, I think I can recreate them pretty much using the cooking methods we have now.”

Hearing Georgima’s words, Rei wondered for a moment, “Is that so?”

But since he’d had similar dishes featuring char siu and had also eaten tender meat in stews, he figured it would probably be fine.

As for the boiled and smoked eggs, he should be able to make those without any particular problems either. …Though the idea of smoking a boiled egg was unexpected even for Georgima.

In fact, gaining the knowledge of how to smoke something like a boiled egg was a major takeaway from this whole ramen incident.

“Though, was it menma? The one with bamboo shoots is a bit of a problem. It won’t turn out the way Rei described.”

“Ah… I don’t know how to make it either.”

The menma that Rei knows requires fermenting bamboo shoots, but for Rei, who has absolutely no knowledge of that process, he can’t even imagine how to make it.

“I’ll figure that out through trial and error. Besides, I don’t even know how long it’ll take to make the noodles. Anyway, I’m going to start tweaking this soup right away, so let’s go.”

"Ah... and please take care of lunch, too."

Even though it was already lunchtime, all Rei had eaten—or rather, drunk—was the soup made from an orc’s bones.

No ingredients, just pure broth.

For Rei, who normally eats more than most people, a lunch consisting solely of this soup was far from satisfying.

Of course, the soup itself was tasty enough to satisfy even Rei.

"Huh? Oh, come to think of it, is it already that time? I’ll tell them to bring something when I get back to the kitchen, so just wait a moment."

Saying that, Georgima left the room.

Rei sat in his chair and watched Georgima’s retreating figure with a slightly exasperated expression.

“Well, as long as I get ramen, I’m fine with it.”

At this rate, there was no way ramen would be ready while Rei was in the Duchy of Kerebel.

Well, maybe they’d manage to whip up something half-baked… but whether Rei would actually be satisfied with that was another matter entirely.

A little time passed after Georgima left… After filling his stomach with the lunch the maid brought him, he began to think about what he should do next.

Naturally, since this was the Duchy of Kerebel, there weren’t many people here whom Rei could call acquaintances.

Even Elena and Ara, who were the closest to him, were busy catching up on work that had piled up while he was in Gilm, so at least for today, their schedules didn’t align with Rei’s.

How should he spend the afternoon?

As Rei pondered this, what came to mind was training with the knight corps led by Philma.

But immediately after that, he remembered that he was currently in Anesis—the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana.

(It’s so quiet in this mansion that I hadn’t really felt it, but… if that’s the case, maybe I should go explore Anesis after all? There might be dishes I haven’t tried yet, or ingredients, or magic items, or something like that.)

Once that thought crossed his mind, Rei realized he couldn’t just stay like this forever.

As if to say “strike while the iron is hot,” he gets ready to go out… and calls for a maid.

A bell had been placed in the room where Rei was staying; ringing it would summon the maid—who had become Rei’s personal attendant for the duration of his stay at the mansion—who was waiting in the next room.

Incidentally, the maid’s name is Miranda; she’s the one who was with Rei when he went to check on Set at the stables and when he had a mock battle with Philma.

Especially since the incident at the stables, Miranda has softened her attitude toward Rei.

That said, this is strictly her professional demeanor as a maid; she doesn’t personally wish to become friends with Rei.

“Rei, is there something you need?”

“I’d like to take a look around Anesis. Could you ask if it’s okay for me to bring Set along?”

At the mention of Set coming along, Miranda’s face takes on a slightly envious expression.

But she immediately hesitated before speaking.

“Well, I think some people might get scared if Set-chan comes along, so I think it might be a bit difficult.”

“I suppose so. This isn’t Gilm, after all.”

If this were Gilm, no one would be scared of going out with Set.

That said, since many people have gathered here looking for work due to the expansion project, it was only natural that some of them were frightened when they first saw Set.

“I’ll go ask my husband, so please wait a moment.”

“Alright. I’ll get ready to go out and wait here, so go ahead and ask him.”

With that, Rei bowed her head and left the room.

Watching her back as she left, Rei sat down on the sofa.

Although she said she would get ready to go out, Rei didn’t need to make any special preparations to leave.

Wearing the Dragon Robe, he could simply head out as he was.

During this snowy season, one would normally need to dress warmer, but with the Dragon Robe’s built-in air conditioning, there was no need to worry about that.

After waiting for about ten minutes... Miranda eventually returns.

“I asked my husband, and he said it’s fine for Set to come along. However, he mentioned that we should assign a single knight to accompany him so as not to cause confusion among the residents. Would that be acceptable?”

Rei had no particular objection to this, and simply nodded in agreement.
