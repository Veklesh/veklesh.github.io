Rei had requested the renovation of the Sand Ship as payment for this particular job. However, in Daskar's estimation, undertaking such a task would require a large number of personnel—not just ordinary workers, but alchemists and mages—and it was unclear how many materials would be needed.

Personally, Daskar felt he owed Rei for all the favors he'd granted him and wanted to accept the request. However, the reality was that most of Gilm's personnel were currently tied up in the expansion project, leaving them physically short on resources.

Even if Rei, Marina, and Vihera offered to redirect their compensation from the expansion work toward this, he couldn't readily agree.

In the current expansion work at Gilm, Rei was single-handedly handling the work of dozens... or potentially even hundreds of people by transporting supplies using Misty Rings.

Meanwhile, Rei's familiar, Set, was protecting Gilm—now without its protective barrier—from flying monsters like harpies.

Marina, too, was performing remarkably well using her highly adaptable spirit magic, though not quite to Rei's level.

While not as much as the other two, Vihera still patrols Gilm, suppressing troubles caused by hot-blooded adventurers, craftsmen, or those seeking day labor, acting as a deterrent.

The reward commensurate with these three's efforts would undoubtedly be substantial.

Daskar was told that they wanted him to use that reward money to renovate the Sand Ship... but even so, he couldn't simply agree.

"I understand the reward you desire, Rei. But... even so, accepting the sand ship's repairs as payment is still a bit difficult."

Facing Daskar as he stated this, Rei smiled and spoke.

"That might be true at first, but what if we could build a land-capable Sand Ship in Gilm? I think it could become one of Gilm's new industries."

"Well... that's probably true. If we could move ships normally on land, it would be a huge asset for transporting goods and such. But do you really think it'll be that easy?"

Daskar replied with a troubled expression at Rei's words.

Indeed, building a sand ship alone would require considerable technological prowess.

Since Rei already possesses a sand ship, analyzing it could provide the technical foundation.

But actually building a sand ship from scratch and then making it capable of moving on land... Needless to say, that's an extremely high level of difficulty.

That's precisely why Daskar couldn't tell Rei it would be easy.

"I can't say for sure about that. All I can do is make a proposal. But I do understand that if it succeeds, it could create a major industry for Gilm."

"True. Even if others tried to copy it, those sand ships... no, ground ships? Land ships? Whatever they are—if we leverage Gilm's location and use materials only found in the frontier to build them, that would make it difficult for others too."

Rei's words, adding to Rei's explanation, made those listening nod in agreement.

Indeed, if things progressed as Marina suggested, the town of Gilm, the sole frontier within the Kingdom of Mireana, stood to gain immense profit.

If someone tried to build the same thing elsewhere, they'd inevitably need to source materials from Gilm. That would undoubtedly add costs, starting with transportation fees.

Furthermore, selling those materials would directly increase Gilm's profits.

Under such circumstances, competing commercially with Gilm would be extremely difficult.

Of course, if we could find some substitute material, it would be a different story.

"Hmm. Could that work, then? But like I said earlier, there's nothing we can do about it right now anyway. We're swamped with the expansion work. Even this cage took a serious toll on the alchemists."

"Yes, I understand that part. So, I'm not asking you to do it immediately. It's fine to wait until things settle down a bit."

"Hmm... Alright. Well, I can't give you an answer right here and now, but I'll look into it. It's a project of this scale, and we're already spending a fortune on Gilm's expansion. I can't decide this alone. Personally, though, I'd like to give it a shot if possible."

There was no falsehood in Daskar's words.

It wasn't just empty words; his genuine desire to pursue it came through to Rei.

But that was only natural.

If Gilm could improve the Sand Ships, it would undoubtedly become a major industry for Gilm, just as Rei had said.

(Though sand ships themselves are incredibly expensive. Just building them doesn't guarantee they'll sell.)

That was Rei's thought, but for now, his primary goal was to have his own sand ship modified; anything else was merely a bonus.

Of course, he hadn't made the proposal without any hope of success.

Gilm's alchemists, while not quite matching the Bestir Empire or the mage city of Ozos, possessed considerable technical prowess, partly due to their easy access to rare materials from the frontier.

In fact, it was a Gilm alchemist who had crafted Rei's own Twilight Spear.

Given that, Rei believed it was by no means impossible for Gilm to build a Sand Ship.

"Well, let's leave the discussion about the sand ship for now. I'm motivated to do this, but if it truly proves impossible, I intend to compensate with something else, so rest assured."

"I don't think Daskar would ever deceive us, so I'm not worried about that part,"

Marina told Daskar with the easy familiarity of old acquaintances.

Daskar's expression turned slightly displeased at Marina's words.

He understood that if he tried to cheat on the payment, something definitely inconvenient for him would happen.

Marina knew something Daskar would consider his dark past.

Considering the possibility of her spreading it around, Daskar couldn't afford to be careless.

"Don't worry,"

He repeated the exact words he had spoken seconds before.

But the sincerity behind those words was clearly different this time.

Seeing Daskar like this, Marina flashed a small smile, deciding perhaps to stop teasing him for now. She spoke again.

"Let's leave this topic for now. First, could you show me the cage you plan to use this time? I need to prepare various things as well."

"...Hmm. Unfortunately, I have to get back to work. I'm not too familiar with magic items either. I've called the alchemist who made it, so you can get the details from them."

With that, Daskar rose from the sofa he'd been sitting on and returned to his desk.

A mountain of documents still awaited him there, and it was clear he intended to tackle them.

At that moment, a knock sounded at the door.

"Excuse me. I heard you were calling, Daskar-sama?"

"Ah. You know Rei, of course? The one using the magic item you made. Explain the magic item to him."

"Ah, understood."

The alchemist who said this looked visibly exhausted.

Seeing him, Rei and the others exchanged glances, wondering if he was truly okay.

"Rei, this is Amaros. Amaros, I'll leave it to you."

With that, Daskar returned to his work in earnest.

"Understood. Let's go then. The cage has been moved into the warehouse."

Following Amaros' lead, Rei and the others left the office.

Byune stuffed the last remaining baked goods into her mouth, but no one noticed.

They walked through the lord's mansion, stepped outside, and entered one of the buildings on the grounds.

Rei wondered if Set might be nearby, but neither Set nor Yellow showed any sign of arriving.

The buildings on the grounds were of considerable size.

Inside, however, as the name "warehouse" suggests, various items were stored.

It wasn't simply left untouched, though; many people came and went, taking various items out of the warehouse or bringing things inside.

"There sure are a lot of people coming and going."

"Yes, as you know, we're quite busy with the expansion work right now. This warehouse is also filled with various items related to that."

Rei had intended his words to be a muttered aside, but Amaros must have heard him. He replied.

"I see. ...So, where's the basket?"

"Over there."

Looking tired, Amaros pointed to a basket placed at the far end of the warehouse.

It was truly, literally, something best described as a basket.

However, it wasn't the kind of woven basket you might imagine. It was made of leather.

...And in this case, the fact that it was made from the skin of a Murder Chameleon was precisely what made it the most crucial item.

"So this is murder chameleon leather?"

Amaros nodded in response to Rei's muttered words.

"Yes. However, it was a bit of a rush job... so, performance-wise, it's not entirely satisfactory. It can't be activated directly using magic power, so we have to use magic stones, and changing its color to match the surroundings takes about a minute."

"I heard about the magic stones from Daskar-sama, but this is the first I've heard it takes that long to change colors."

Rei looked slightly dissatisfied, but not angry enough to get upset.

Looking at Amalos, it was clear he'd pushed himself to the absolute limit. More importantly, Lay understood that if they were flying through the sky, a minute for the color change wasn't a huge disadvantage.

If traveling through places like forests, taking a minute to change color would be a major drawback. It wouldn't be surprising if it were deemed unusable for that reason.

But unlike on the ground, the surrounding colors in the sky don't change much.

Sure, someone observing very closely might notice, but the likelihood of someone actually staring that intently at the sky was infinitesimally small, if not zero.

That's precisely why Rei didn't seem particularly bothered when told it took about a minute for the color to change. However...

"You know this cage modification is scheduled to happen after Gilm's expansion work is finished, right?"

"Yes, of course. To be honest, I was rushed for time on this job myself, so it's far from something I'm satisfied with."

Rei nodded at Amalos' words, spoken with a hint of regret... or rather, considering the parts not visible on his face, he likely felt far more regret than he let show.

"I see. Well, if you understand, that's fine. For now, using this cage is just a trial run. If you notice anything concerning later, let me know and we'll make the necessary modifications. Think of it that way, okay?"

"Yes, you're right. Thinking that way does make me feel a little better."

Rei's words seemed to lift his spirits a little.

A small smile touched Amaros's weary face.

In Amaros's position, being told to modify the cage right then and there wasn't something he could just do immediately.

But Rei's position held enough weight to make it possible.

He had consistently demonstrated that level of power and track record, and on top of that, Rei was currently part of a party.

Given that, his power could only increase, never decrease.

Amaros was likely pleased that Rei stepped aside more readily than he had anticipated.

His lack of physical stamina being a major reason, no doubt.

"The cage... um, does Set grab the top part?"

"Yes, that's correct. Also, the entrance is over here."

With that explanation, Amaros lightly pressed a section of the cage's side.

Then, the door opened inward.

"Huh... So that's the door."

"Yes. Well, it would be difficult to get in and out from the top, after all."

Following Amaros' lead, Rei and the others stepped inside the cage.

Inside, it was indeed shaped like a proper cage.

It was spacious enough for four or five people to sit, but not large enough to lie down.

There was a box where magic stones would likely be inserted, and several magic stones were stacked beside it.

(Two tatami mats... no, maybe a little smaller than three? They say it's made from Murder Chameleon leather, but how many of them did it take?)

Thinking this, Rei asked Amaros.

"So, what's this cage called?"

"Ah... Unfortunately, we didn't have the luxury of giving it an elaborate name, so for now, we simply call it 'the cage'. If possible, we'd appreciate it if Rei could give it a more fitting name."

Saying this, he smiled despite his tired face, scratching his head shyly.
