While Rei and the others were exchanging information inside the Earth’s Bounty Inn in Ursula… a tense atmosphere was beginning to build near the main gate.

“Here you go, Set. These are sandwiches from a bakery famous for being the best in Earthla. What do you think?”

When Milein held out the sandwich to Set, he happily picked it up with his beak and began to eat it, the aroma of toasted bread wafting from it.

“Growl!”

The sandwich, made with dried river fish, gave off a scent that was more than enough to stimulate the appetite, thanks to the toasty aroma of the grilled fish.

As Milein fed the sandwich to Set like that, there were women watching her who couldn’t hide their displeased expressions.

These are the ones who have made doting on Set their emotional anchor during their journey to Gilm.

As far as the women were concerned, they believed Set was theirs.

But as if to say that was nothing more than their own delusion, Milein began to dote on Set right in front of them.

“Hey… can I ask you something?”

“What is it?”

Amidst this, one of the women finally spoke up to Milein.

Normally, they wouldn’t want to speak kindly to someone who had taken their Set.

But... even from the women’s perspective, Milein had the bearing of a skilled adventurer.

Given that, they wanted to avoid making any careless hostile remarks or gestures here as much as possible. …That said, if you asked them if they wanted to be friends with Milein, the answer would be a resounding no.

“What’s your relationship with Set?”

What kind of relationship?

If you replaced “Set” with a man’s name, those words would sound as if she were confronting a lover caught cheating.

She must have sensed at least a hint of hostility in the woman’s words. Milein turned her gaze in that direction.

The fact that she didn’t show any signs of being flustered was likely because she had a rival named Johanna vying for Set’s attention.

While the women were tense, Milein spoke without showing the slightest sign of nervousness.

“What kind of relationship? Well… if I had to put it in one word… love!”

As she stroked Set and declared this, there was not a trace of hesitation in her demeanor.

If Rei were here, he would have vehemently objected to such a statement from Milein.

...Though whether Milein would have listened to him is another matter entirely.

And the women were just as dissatisfied with Milein’s words.

“Hey, what does that mean?! Set belongs to us!”

The moment she said that, the woman who shouted… and many of those around her… felt a chill run down their spines.

It was as if, despite it being the height of summer, winter snow had been dumped on their backs.

“Property…? Did you just call Set-chan ‘property’? You lot, that’s no laughing matter!”

Just because they participated in Rei and the others’ combat training didn’t mean they’d instantly become stronger.

Milein, in particular, was a Rank C adventurer, and her caliber was on a completely different level from the women present.

It was only natural that the women present would be paralyzed if even a hint of killing intent were directed at them by such an opponent.

“Guh…”

The woman who had been talking to Milein just moments ago groaned, but at least she was still able to make a sound.

Most of the others couldn’t even manage to groan.

If she had continued to radiate that killing intent for even another dozen seconds, some of them would have lost control of their bladders before they even had a chance to lose their will to fight.

It was thanks to the blow of the staff that came down on Milein’s head that they were able to avoid that worst possible outcome for a woman.

“Hey, what are you doing to an amateur?”

A dull thud echoed through the area.

“Ow!”

In an instant, Milein crouched on the ground, clutching her head.

Isn’t this the kind of behavior a woman like Milein—a woman truly worthy of being called a beauty—should never engage in?

Unable to hide her pain by simply crouching, the women watching Milein roll around on the ground thought this, as if they had completely forgotten the killing intent they had felt just a few seconds earlier.

After rolling around on the ground for several dozen seconds, Milein finally stood up—perhaps the pain in her head had subsided—and glared at the person who had brought the staff down on her head.

“Hey, Sulunin, what on earth are you doing?!”

“What am I doing? That’s what I should be asking you. Directing a killing intent at an ordinary person… how deplorable.”

Displaying an attitude that seemed to come from the depths of his soul, Sulunin raised his staff once more.

The moment she saw that, Milein stepped back almost reflexively.

That previous blow must have been incredibly powerful.

“B-but… you said Set-chan was ‘yours,’ didn’t you? ‘Yours,’ you know? Set-chan has his own free will, yet you tried to manipulate him for your own convenience! How could I not be angry about that?”

“Even so, I don’t approve of a high-ranking adventurer like Milein directing such killing intent toward… ordinary people.”

When she mentioned “ordinary people,” she glanced at some of the women present—those who had received at least some combat training—but ultimately judged them to be no different from ordinary people. She stated this without making any particular corrections.

The impression that mages are poor at close-quarters combat with weapons is widespread, and in fact, that isn’t necessarily wrong.

In fact, if told to fight Milein with weapons, Sulunin would have no chance of winning.

However, that was strictly when facing a Rank C adventurer like Milein; when facing the women right in front of him—who, despite having received some combat training, were ultimately still just ordinary people—he was confident he could win without a doubt.

That was the reason for Sulunin's attitude, but several of the women understood his thinking from his demeanor and took offense at it.

This wasn’t because Sulunin’s attitude was careless… but simply because the women’s intuition was sharp.

For these women, their experience working as prostitutes could be described as their worst memories.

But even so, if you were to ask whether those terrible memories left nothing behind, the answer would be no.

The ability to discern what someone is thinking from their mannerisms… that skill had naturally become second nature to them.

Although they had been in a semi-brainwashed state while working as prostitutes, the fact that they could still effectively use the skills they had acquired could be considered one of the few benefits for these women.

In any case, toward Sulunin... several of the women who felt they had been looked down upon by the middle-aged male wizard directed hostile glances not only at Milein but also at Sulunin...

“Grrrrrrr!”

Before the women could voice their discontent, Set’s cry echoed through the air.

It wasn’t the brave roar he made when encountering an enemy, nor was it a pleading cry… it was a scolding, reprimanding kind of roar.

Milein and the women, who loved Set, naturally couldn’t take any further action upon hearing that cry.

Sulunin hadn’t come here to start a fight in the first place; he’d come to stop one. Now that Set had brought them to a halt, he had no intention of doing anything further.

“Grr?”

“No more fighting?” Set asked, looking at Milein and the women with round, innocent eyes.

He hadn’t said anything directly, but his words still reached the hearts of everyone present.

Milein looked at the women, and the women looked at Milein... and while some of them had viewed Sulunin as an unwelcome presence, they decided there was no need to act in such a way that would make Set sad.

“It’s okay. Look, Set-chan. It’s not like we’re on bad terms with these girls. Right?”

“That’s right, we won’t do anything to make Set sad, so please don’t worry.”

Milein and the women linked arms and told Set this with smiles on their faces, but Milein was pinching the woman’s arm with the hand that was supposed to be around her shoulder, and the woman, for her part, was stomping on Milein’s foot.

And yet, neither of them showed any sign of pain on their faces; they were both smiling. Watching this from a short distance away, Sulunin found herself at a loss—should she be exasperated, smile, or get angry?

In the end, however, she becomes absolutely certain that they won’t make a scene like this in front of Set.

Sulunin doesn’t know the full details regarding the women traveling with Rei.

After all, even though they are adventurers serving as bodyguards, this particular incident is something that should remain confidential.

Even so, while he may not know the details about the women, he knows Milein—who is part of the party—as well as anyone could.

In fact, the fact that Milein would absolutely never do anything to make Set sad was as clear to Sulunin as the stone falling to the ground the moment he let go of it.

(Calling it a 'minor power struggle' feels a bit off, but I suppose I can overlook it if it’s just that.)

Thinking this, as if to convince himself, Sulunin spoke to Milein once more.

“Milein, it’s fine to play with Set, but we’re leaving tomorrow. Are you ready? Ekril has been looking for you.”

“Uh… well, that…”

Seeing Milein stumble over her words, it was obvious without even thinking whether she was ready or not.

Based on what she’d heard earlier about the distance between them and Rei’s group… and above all, on her woman’s intuition born of her love for Set, she was certain they would meet up in Asura, and that was why she’d been waiting here.

There was no way they could possibly be ready to leave.

“You can play with Set after this is over. …Come on, let’s go.”

Sulunin grabbed Milein’s hand and dragged her along forcefully.

Normally, Sulunin wouldn’t be able to drag Milein along with his strength… or more accurately, if Milein intended to resist, Sulunin wouldn’t be able to drag her along, but right now, Milein was being dragged along without a fight.

That was because she could imagine what would happen to her if she tried to resist Sulunin right now.

Milein is, of course, the party leader of the Scorching Wind.

However, while that might be true in a crisis, when it comes to who holds the real power in their peaceful daily lives, the presence of the older Sulunin is undeniably greater.

In particular, Sulunin had a personality that earned him the nickname “Milein’s external conscience,” and he was a more level-headed person than Milein.

“Ah… Seito-chaaan!”

Milein’s sorrow-filled voice echoed around them, but no one thought to help her.

The women found Milein a nuisance when it came to admiring Set, and since Set had let Sulunin lead him away like this, they assumed Milein must have something she needed to do.

“So, Set-chan… Is there anything you’d like to eat? If you’re hungry, I can go buy something in town.”

As she spoke, the woman’s gaze fell upon the women entering Asura through the main gate.

The only ones remaining here were the women who simply wanted to admire Set… the women who wanted to play with him; the others had simply gone inside Arsura.

This was proof that the Zolgue Trading Company held considerable influence, at least within the Kingdom of Reburina.

Though, of course, the women already knew full well just how formidable the Zolge Trading Company was from their journey so far.

Carriages large enough to carry nearly a thousand people, the horses to pull them, food, water, clothing, and guards.

On top of that, the Zolge Trading Company had provided all sorts of other supplies, so it would be unreasonable to doubt their power.

“Hmm, but… is there anyone here who still has some spare funds?”

“Well…”

No matter how much the Zolge Trading Company was helping out, there was no way they’d actually give them spending money.

Given that, the only way to get money is to earn it ourselves.

For those skilled in embroidery, there was the option of sewing something while traveling by carriage and selling it.

Also, some who knew how to hunt headed to a nearby forest to hunt in small groups. …Though I had no idea how the women managed to get their hands on bows and arrows.

Alternatively, those who had no qualms about working as prostitutes were selling their bodies to make money.

In any case, since we had to earn money by any means necessary, there was no way we could just sit here and do nothing.

“For now, I think I’ll try working in the cafeteria. I’m pretty good at cooking, after all.”

“...Hey, then why don’t you just cook for Set-chan?”

“That’s fine, but… what about the ingredients? We can’t just take supplies without permission, can we? Besides, it’s a bit difficult to cook with preserved food.”

Each wagon does have a certain amount of preserved food on hand, such as dried meat, water, and hardtack.

However, those were strictly for emergencies, and they couldn’t just use them without permission.

In the end, the women headed into Earthra in search of work that would take only a few hours.
