Inside the cave, the opponent Rei attacked wasn't particularly strong, as expected, and now lay unconscious on the ground.

That said, the fact that he wasn't strong was only because Rei was the one who attacked. Considering he had infiltrated enemy territory—a frontier teeming with all manner of monsters—he was probably quite strong by general standards.

Anyway, glancing over the unconscious bodies, Rei surveyed the cave interior once more.

There were piles of lumber, stone, and metal ingots, clearly in substantial quantities.

(Come to think of it, when we infiltrated, they mentioned the cave was already packed full. So, this isn't the first time these guys have attacked?)

Rei had heard that the merchant attacked today was carrying a considerable amount of supplies.

But even so, this amount of supplies felt a bit excessive.

Of course, it couldn't be completely ruled out that they were actually transporting this much.

"Well, anyway... confiscating all this stuff is a given, though."

He muttered, stowing the materials found inside the cave one after another into the Misty Ring.

Even though he could store them just by touching them, the sheer volume was considerable.

In the end, it took a good amount of time to store all the materials in the Misty Ring.

"Hm!"

Just as he placed the last stone into the Misty Ring, that voice reached him from the cave entrance.

There was no need for Rei, who was in the party with her, to even think about whose voice it was.

"Byune, you're here, so does that mean you're done outside?"

"Mm."

Seeing Byune nod, Rei nodded in agreement.

After all, Rei's party, Crimson Wings, could be described as a party specialized in combat power.

Considering even Byune, who was supposedly a thief, possessed combat prowess beyond her years, no one could deny that.

Vihera was a combat maniac, and with Rei and Set added to the mix, it was only natural they'd be seen as combat-focused.

In reality, they possessed numerous other outstanding abilities beyond combat prowess, such as Set's keen senses, Marina's knowledge, and spirit magic.

...Still, the fact remains that their combat prowess is exceptionally high, so that's probably how they'll be perceived.

"I see. Well then, let's go. ...No, wait. Before that, I should bind them so they can't escape even if they regain consciousness. ...But the problem is how to transport these guys."

"Hm?"

Using rope retrieved from the Misty Ring, he began binding the limbs of the men lying on the ground.

It was a special binding technique that wouldn't let them slip free easily, but that was only in the general sense.

If these men were special forces or something, they probably wouldn't be able to escape this binding so easily... but there was still a good chance they could get away.

(Should I ask for a magic item that restrains people? ...But the alchemist seems swamped right now, like he could use all the help he can get.)

The biggest reason for this was, needless to say, the wood they'd cut down from Treant Forest.

Even before defeating the Giganto Turtle, they'd cut down a considerable amount, and since defeating it, they've been logging massive quantities daily.

Considering that one of Rei's main jobs is transporting the wood cut in Treant Forest, you can imagine just how much he's hauling.

...Though, with Misty Ring around, asking Rei to do it is faster than transporting normally felled wood anyway, so even if it wasn't that much, it's highly likely Rei was asked to do it.

In any case, the wood itself isn't particularly special in its raw state.

However, by applying alchemy to it, it can be imbued with a certain degree of magical defense.

And since alchemy is involved, it requires as many alchemists as possible.

As a result, the alchemists of Gilm found themselves extremely busy.

Of course, they could have called in alchemists from other towns and villages, but the timber harvested from Treant Forest was crucial for expanding Gilm.

For that very reason, the upper echelons of Gilm preferred not to share the details with alchemists from other towns and villages if possible.

Indeed, among Gilm's alchemists, those whose actions seemed suspicious—those suspected of having deep connections to other towns, villages, or cities—were not involved in this matter.

This is precisely why the alchemists entrusted with the work have become even busier.

"Hmph!"

After binding all the men who had lost consciousness inside the cave to prevent their escape, they hurried out of the cave, urged on by Byune.

Looking around the cave's perimeter, he saw every man who had been in this hideout lying unconscious on the ground.

To neutralize this many men without killing a single one.

That alone should make clear just how much power Rei and the others possess.

"Ah, Rei. How was it inside the cave?"

Seeing Rei emerge from the cave, Vihera asked with a smile.

Seeing the man who had been called the captain lying unconscious a short distance away, Rei felt relieved that Vihera seemed in good spirits, meaning the fight with the captain must have been satisfying.

(And he's still alive.)

Vihera's skill, Immersion Palm, now possessed the effect of causing internal explosions within the body.

While it was an extremely reliable skill for killing an opponent, it was absolutely not suited for capturing someone alive.

That was precisely why Rei had felt a faint unease, wondering if the captain might be dead... Fortunately, the captain showed no signs of internal explosions and appeared merely unconscious.

Under Rei's gaze, Vihera seemed to understand what he was thinking. Her smile faded slightly, replaced by a hint of displeasure as she spoke.

"What? Did you think I might have killed him?"

"Ah... no. Not at all. I just thought it was good that Vihera got a fight she could be satisfied with."

"Right. As you were worried about, I didn't use the Immersion Palm or anything like that. I didn't use the claws on my gauntlets or the blades on my greaves either. It really was a fight in my natural state."

With those words, Rei understood why the captain was unharmed. Still, since Vihera seemed satisfied, there was no need to press further and risk upsetting her. He changed the subject.

"So, about what we do next... Don't you think it'll take a while to get back to Gilm with these guys?"

"...Yeah. Once they realize they've been captured, they'll naturally try to escape somehow."

Marina, who had been checking the surroundings for survivors—no one had died—interrupted Rei and Vihera's conversation.

"GRRRRRR!"

And beside Marina stood Set.

"Huh? Did you call Set?"

"Yes. Since none of the enemies seem to have escaped, there's no need to keep Set waiting overhead, right?"

"...I don't think we've defeated every last one of them yet."

Could there be someone hiding somewhere, waiting for an opening? To Rei, who voiced this concern, Marina opened her mouth with a confident smile—a smile that could melt anyone, man or woman.

"If anyone could escape spirit magic, then perhaps there are still hidden enemies lurking."

"Ah... yeah. I see. Got it. ...Spirit magic is still pretty ridiculous, huh?"

"Is that so? But very few humans have the aptitude for it, and getting spirits to move exactly as you wish is incredibly difficult, you know?"

"Even if Marina, who's been using it flawlessly right in front of us, says that, it lacks conviction..."

Marina's skill in wielding spirit magic was, as far as Rei knew, the absolute pinnacle.

He'd once encountered an elf spirit user in the Bestir Empire who fought by shaping water spirits into dragon forms, but in terms of freely commanding spirits, Marina clearly seemed superior.

(And Marina could probably create an even larger spirit than that water dragon if she wanted to.)

As Rei pondered this deeply, Marina tilted her head at his expression.

"What's wrong? Did something happen?"

"...No, nothing. Anyway, we've definitely defeated every last one of the ones who were here. Is that okay with you?"

"Yes."

"Right."

"Mm!"

"Grr!"

The three people and one creature each responded to Rei's words.

Hearing this, Rei thought, "Well then..." and pulled out a rope from her Misty Ring.

"In the end, tying them up with rope is the safest bet until we get them to Gilm. ...We should tie them up quickly while they're unconscious. Everyone spread out and take care of them all at once. Set, you check if anyone's regained consciousness."

"Guruu!"

Set growled happily at Rei's words.

Until just moments ago, he'd merely been flying above the forest.

He understood it was an important job to make sure the enemy didn't escape.

But even so, just flying around doing nothing was boring for Set.

Even when he spotted a few monsters in the forest, as a sentry, he couldn't possibly defeat them.

That made checking whether the unconscious individuals before him had regained consciousness a task Set found rewarding.

With his keen senses—hearing, smell, sight—Set was particularly skilled at discerning who was feigning unconsciousness and who was on the verge of regaining consciousness.

"Alright, let's move out."

With those words, the members of Crimson Wings scattered, each taking a rope Rei had retrieved from the Misty Ring.

Rei, too, approached a man lying unconscious a short distance away.

He proceeded to bind him just as they had bound the man in the cave earlier...

"Speaking of which, Rei. Did you retrieve the supplies from inside the cave?"

Marina, who was tying up the unconscious man's hands a short distance away, asked.

"Yeah, that's all taken care of. Everything's in my Misty Ring now, so the minimum requirements for the request are completely fulfilled. ...And bringing these guys back should get us the highest possible rating for the request."

"...Well, I suppose so. Though whether that's the highest possible rating isn't entirely clear yet."

"Huh? What do you mean by that?"

"Well, there's a lot about quests that adventurers don't know. Anyway, what I mean is, regardless of whether this one gets the highest rating, it's definitely going to be highly rated."

Being the former guildmaster, Marina likely knew many things Rei didn't.

But the fact that she deliberately didn't mention it to Rei meant it was something Rei didn't need to know.

Reaching that conclusion, Rei didn't press for further explanation and proceeded to bind the man's hands.

Incidentally, unlike the men bound inside the cave, he didn't tie their legs. This was because they would be returning to Gilm.

If his legs were bound, his walking speed would become extremely slow.

She absolutely wanted to avoid that, since she was trying to get back to Gilm as quickly as possible.

"Still... I wonder who these men really are? They certainly don't seem like mere bandits. Well, the moment they mentioned something about a mission, it was clear they weren't just common thieves."

"True. But we'll find out the details once we escort them back to Gilm. ...Daskar is fundamentally fair and just, but that doesn't mean he shies away from interrogation methods."

Daskar, a former knight whose character could rightly be called bold and open-hearted, held no hesitation about interrogation or torture, given his position as a lord.

He wouldn't hesitate to gather information to protect his domain.

Especially since these men were clearly acting to obstruct the expansion of Gilm.

Therefore, as the lord of Gilm, he could not be selective about his methods.

Daskar's position was not his alone.

It represented the people of Gilm, those serving the House of Larks, the Marquis of the Frontier... and as a central figure of the Neutral Faction, one of the three major factions, the livelihoods of many depended on him.

If there was something he could do for that cause, there was no need to hesitate.

"If only these fools hadn't gone and meddled with Gilm..."

Watching the men slowly regain consciousness, Rei murmured this with a hint of pity.
