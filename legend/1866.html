Facing the Black Wolf, Rei charges straight at it.

In his right hand was the Death Scythe, and in his left, the Twilight Spear—his usual dual-spear style.

As Rei approached head-on, the Black Wolf did not evade him with a teleportation as usual, but simply stared at him intently.

……However, what lay within the Black Wolf’s eyes was an intense hunger.

It was an intense hunger—the kind that suggested he might die of starvation if things continued this way.

His gaze recognized Rei, approaching him, not as an enemy who would take his life, but as prey he would devour.

(His hunger is stronger than I expected. I was sure he’d have a little more leeway.)

Exactly how much damage the Death Scythe’s strike inflicted is impossible to tell now that the wound has healed.

But judging by the fact that it didn’t quite sever his torso in a single blow… there’s no doubt it wasn’t a fatal wound.

Just how deep was the damage from that strike?

Since he couldn’t tell, Rei needed to land another blow just like it… or even a stronger one.

“Anyway, the best thing to do is just give it a try!”

As he spoke, Rei swung the Death Scythe the moment the distance closed.

It was a ferocious strike that seemed to slice through not just the air, but space itself.

The Black Wolf crouched to the ground with a beast-like movement and dodged the blow.

(What?)

Feeling a momentary flicker of doubt, Rei launched a strike with the Twilight Spear in his left hand at the Black Wolf crouched on the ground.

But the Black Wolf dodged that strike as well by leaping to the side.

(What?)

Seeing the Black Wolf’s behavior, Rei felt the same confusion as a moment ago.

Up until now, the black wolf would have used the skill “Transference” to dodge Rei’s attacks.

Whether it was the Death Scythe or the Twilight Spear, Rei’s attacks possessed enough power to be fatal with a single hit if they landed.

Considering the worst-case scenario, it wouldn’t have been strange for him to play it safe.

Of course, that didn’t mean he’d transfer every single attack—it wouldn’t have been strange for him to simply dodge when launching a counterattack, but at the very least, now was not that time.

Given that, there was definitely something off about the current situation.

“Gah!”

Following the movement to dodge Rei’s attack, the Black Wolf kicked off the ground and lunged toward Rei.

It was a strike with its fangs bared.

Unlike its previous silent actions, this was a strike driven by a fighting spirit rooted in hunger.

Compared to its previous attacks, this strike possessed a higher level of speed and momentum... yet Rei dodged it with ease.

(So... I see.)

After a moment’s thought, he understood why he had been able to dodge it so easily, even though it was a more powerful strike than before.

The speed was greater, but that was because the attacker hadn’t thought about his next move… it was a strike unleashed purely on instinct.

From Rei’s perspective, that undoubtedly made the fight easier.

No matter how much the speed of the attacks increased, if the opponent was merely driven by the instinct of hunger and only launched linear, even impulsive attacks, there were plenty of ways to deal with them.

Furthermore, the fact that the Black Wolf wasn’t using its greatest characteristic and advantage—teleportation—made it an even easier opponent for Rei.

(I never imagined that a single strike from the Death Scythe could change things this much. A major weakness for the Black Wolf… no, perhaps it’s precisely because it’s the Death Scythe.)

In a normal fight, it’s difficult to inflict wounds severe enough to be called serious on a Black Wolf that masters the skill of Teleportation.

The only reason things have gone so well so far is because Rei is using the Death Scythe.

“Well then, it’s finally the final stretch, isn’t it? …Just so you know, if you surrender now—”

Mid-sentence, the Black Wolf lunges at Rei once more.

It aims its fangs at his body, seeking to tear him to pieces and satisfy its appetite.

For a moment, Rei couldn’t help but wonder if Elena, who was watching the battle unfold from a slight distance, might actually be superior to him in terms of both the quantity and tenderness of the meat. But since the Black Wolf was coming straight at him, he figured there was no need to worry about that. Dodging the fang strike, he thrust out the Twilight Spear.

“Guaah!”

The Black Wolf instinctively twisted his body to dodge the strike... but having just launched an attack driven by hunger, he couldn’t evade it completely.

The tip of the Twilight Spear sliced deeply into the Black Wolf’s left arm.

“Gyah!”

Letting out a scream, the Black Wolf leaped far backward—perhaps because the pain and shock outweighed its hunger.

In the past, the Black Wolf would have healed the wound on his left arm the moment he put distance between himself and Rei. But...

“Gya, gyaaah! Gyaaah!”

Having put distance between itself and Rei, the Black Wolf let out a scream that sounded both bewildered... and as if it were enduring the pain.

(Did it work?)

Holding the Twilight Spear, Rei observed the black wolf.

The Black Wolf’s current state could be said to be exactly as Rei intended.

One of the Twilight Spear’s abilities is to hinder the opponent’s healing.

That ability itself isn’t particularly powerful; if high-level healing magic or potent potions are used, they can overcome the healing inhibition and heal the wounds.

And from what Rei could see, since the Black Wolf had recovered from the severe wounds inflicted by the Death Scythe to the point where they could move without hindrance, it was certain that the Black Wolf’s regenerative abilities surpassed the healing inhibition effect of the Twilight Spear.

But… yes, but.

The Black Wolf had merely healed the wounds inflicted by the Death Scythe, yet it was already displaying such intense hunger and was so exhausted that it could no longer fight as usual.

Given that, it could even be said that the wound inflicted by Rei’s Twilight Spear strike was nearly fatal to the Black Wolf, despite its strong regenerative abilities.

And indeed, the wound inflicted by the Twilight Spear’s strike has not healed, and the Black Wolf is groaning in pain and hunger.

“Gyah, gaaaaaaahhhhhhhhh!”

This figure seemed completely different from the taciturn Black Wolf Rei knew.

If Rei, who knew nothing of the Black Wolf’s current state, were to see him now, it’s doubtful he’d even recognize him as the same creature he’d encountered before.

Fangs protrude from its mouth, and saliva—perhaps due to hunger—continues to drip from them as it growls incessantly.

Its eyes were bloodshot, and it glared at Rei with the gaze of one fixated on food.

Seeing the black wolf like this, it was impossible to believe this was the same person who had been trying to feed him steamed buns and fruit juice.

“It’s about time we settled this. Unfortunately, I can’t keep dealing with you forever.”

Holding both the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear, Rei addressed the black wolf, which was moaning in hunger and pain.

Despite its condition, the black wolf must have sensed the killing intent emanating from Rei. It fixed its bloodshot gaze on him and let out a threatening growl.

“Gggaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaaah!”

That howl was not only a warning but also proof that the hunger within him had grown stronger than before, and he could no longer resist it.

As it let out that cry, the black wolf was overwhelmed by both the instinct of hunger and the killing intent emanating from Rei... and in the next instant, it bolted.

Rather than starve to death, it gambled on survival—however slim the chance—and set off to devour Rei’s flesh.

Already, there was no trace of sanity left in its eyes.

Overcome by hunger, he devours the one who had directed killing intent at him.

Thinking only of that, he ran straight toward Rei.

True to the name "Black Wolf," he did not run on two legs, but sprinted using all four limbs.

And as Rei watched the Black Wolf charging toward him, a fleeting glimpse of pity flashed in his eyes.

Already, due to starvation, the Black Wolf had no will of its own left; this was an attack driven purely by instinct.

It was simply impossible to believe this was the same person who had just been fighting him head-on.

It was undoubtedly Rei who had reduced him to this state.

But even so… for Rei, the Black Wolf was an opponent who gave him much to think about.

“I’ll set you free from that state. Sleep.”

He muttered, then swung the Death Scythe at the Black Wolf, which was charging straight at him, fangs bared.

“Power Slash”

A skill that enhances the power of a single strike rather than increasing the sharpness of the Death Scythe.

Its power was such that even Rei struggled to absorb the recoil.

And at the same time, he unleashed the Twilight Spear held in his left hand.

Infused with magical power, that strike unleashes its full potential, delivering an extremely powerful blow.

The Power Slash from the Death Scythe and the strike from the Twilight Spear.

The two strikes, unleashed simultaneously... struck the black wolf that had charged toward Rei, intent on devouring him.

As if countering the attack that came at him with its mouth wide open, the Twilight Spear pierced its mouth, passing through its throat and piercing its torso.

The Power Slash, swung at the same time, was delivered with one hand instead of the usual two, so while it couldn’t fully transfer its power to the black wolf, it still had enough force to sever its torso.

...Furthermore, since Rei had pulled out the Twilight Spear just before the body was severed, the spear that had pierced the body avoided the fate of being struck by the Death Scythe.

Wielding long weapons with both hands, unleashing them simultaneously, and yet ensuring they do not come into contact with one another.

It goes without saying just how much skill that requires.

But Rei is the one who pulls it off.

And… as a result of that action, the Black Wolf’s torso was split in two, each half blown away in a different direction.

Had it been a wound inflicted by the Death Scythe on his abdomen, he could have recovered.

But with his torso severed like this, there’s no way he can survive.

“Guh… ga…”

The fact that it didn’t die instantly reveals the Black Wolf’s extraordinary vitality, healing ability, and regenerative power.

Watching the Black Wolf’s condition, Rei swung the weapons in both hands to wipe away the bloodstains, then sheathed them in the Misty Ring and approached the Black Wolf… or, more precisely, its upper half.

Although they had ended up fighting each other with their lives on the line, Rei never truly hated the Black Wolf.

It was truly a mystery to Rei as well, but the fact remained that, since there was no hope of survival, he had resolved to see it through to the end.

He also wondered why it had suddenly attacked today, and why, even if it was going to attack, it had done so in broad daylight rather than at night—the time of day assassins typically prefer.

There was no doubt he also hoped to hear the circumstances behind all of this.

That said, the Black Wolf Rei knew never spoke, so he didn’t expect to hear any explanation.

……That was precisely why Rei’s feet faltered for a moment as he approached the Black Wolf.

“Here… is… I… what on earth…”

Those words, whose meaning was crystal clear.

After all, they had come from the mouth of the Black Wolf—who was clearly missing his lower body and had only his upper body remaining.

“Black Wolf, you… could you talk?”

“Black Wolf? Is that my… no… wait. I’m starting to remember… so, that’s it…”

Whether it was because he was called “Black Wolf,” or because he saw Rei’s face, the Black Wolf seemed to have realized who he was.

But the moment he tried to say something, blood spurted from the Black Wolf’s mouth.

It was only natural. Since his torso had been severed in two, the fact that he wasn’t dead at this point was a miracle in itself.

Even the powerful regenerative ability that had brought about that miracle couldn’t keep the dying Black Wolf alive.

All it could do was prolong his life a little, rather than causing instant death.

“I see. So… you’re… a meat bun, huh? …If I had my choice, I’d have preferred a braised pork bun… You know, the kind sold in Yokohama’s Chinatown… the ones I used to eat with Mom…”

With a sudden stop. Rei’s feet halted upon hearing the Black Wolf’s words.

If the words that came out of the Black Wolf’s mouth were simply “steamed buns,” there would be no problem.

But if the words “Yokohama Chinatown” came up, that was a different story.

Although Rei had grown up in the countryside of Tohoku, he still knew about Yokohama’s Chinatown from TV and various other media.

Naturally, the term “Yokohama Chinatown” shouldn’t exist in this world, so the fact that he’s using it… well, there’s no need to wonder where Black Wolf came from.

However, although the Black Wolf currently had black hair, when they met at night, it had been silver.

At least, as far as Rei knew, there was no one in Japan whose hair color changed depending on the time of day. …Unless they dyed their hair, that is.

“Hey, you… did you come from Japan…?”

“Did you come from Japan?”

Rei was about to ask that, but by then, the Black Wolf was no longer breathing; he had already fallen into a peaceful sleep.
