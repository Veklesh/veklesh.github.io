"Here are the monster materials we collected... or rather, the meat."

Inside the guild’s warehouse, Rei was told this by a guild employee and bowed his head.

All Rei could see before him was meat, meat, and more meat.

They were chunks of meat from various monsters.

A short while ago, Rei had entrusted a large number of monsters to the guild.

The massive amount of meat obtained from butchering those monsters was right there.

While Rei was set to take possession of a few rare materials, all other materials and magic stones were to be purchased by the guild.

For the guild, buying up such a massive amount of materials all at once would entail a significant expense.

However, since selling those materials would bring in more money than what they paid Rei, they certainly wouldn’t complain about it.

“But, um… what about the magic stones? I heard Rei collects them.”

“Ah, I already have the magic stones from the monsters I handed over. Don’t worry about that.”

Rei handed over to the guild were ones he had already tested his Beast Magic on.

Given that, the truth is, Rei would actually be at a loss if he were given magic stones.

That said, some of the magic items Rei possesses use magic stones as a power source—essentially acting as batteries—so it’s not as if he doesn’t need them at all.

Still, for magic stones of that caliber, they could simply dispose of the monster corpses piling up inside Misty Ring themselves, so it wasn’t as if they needed them right away.

……The only exception was the magic stone from the Giganto Turtle that came out of the Treant Forest.

“So, what’s the plan for dismantling the Giganto Turtle?”

“Well… I’m sorry, but without Rei here, there’s nothing we can do…”

The guild clerk hesitated in response to Rei’s question.

However, even after hearing that, Rei had no intention of blaming the guild staff.

The Giganto Turtle was massive enough to easily overrun Gilm itself.

Dismantling a monster that massive was, naturally, not something that could be done inside Gilm.

In other words, to butcher the Giganto Turtle, they would have to do it outside of Gilm.

And if they dismantle a monster outside of Gilm, monsters drawn by the scent of blood will inevitably show up, so they’ll also need to provide security for the dismantlers.

(The only saving grace is that we’ll be doing the disassembly in winter. There are far fewer animals, insects, and birds then than there are from spring through fall.)

That’s what Rei thought, but since many of the monsters that only appear in winter are vicious, there’s no doubt that guarding against them will require considerable skill.

Furthermore, since they’re butchering such a massive monster, there’s no way it will be finished in a single day.

If that were the case, leaving the Gigant Turtle’s carcass outside Gilm overnight would require hiring a large number of adventurers on a continuous basis. To avoid that, Rei would need to store the remaining carcasses in the Misty Ring.

In particular, the rewards for the adventurers hired to butcher monsters, including the Gigant Turtle, are not paid by Rei, but by the Guild or the Daskar.

Considering these circumstances, it was only natural to recognize that having Rei present was practically a prerequisite for dismantling a Gigant Turtle.

“In that case, it’ll have to wait until after the New Year, once I’ve returned from the Duchy of Kerebel.”

“That’s how it’ll be, isn’t it? I suppose so.”

The slight lack of confidence likely stems from the uncertainty of whether we’ll be able to finish dismantling the Gigant Turtle between the start of the year and spring.

Once spring arrives, many adventurers will naturally resume their activities, so the number of people willing to help with the dismantling of the Gigant Turtle will decrease.

Above all, spring means warmer weather, so there was even a risk that the Gigant Turtle’s carcass would rot before the dismantling was finished.

If that happened, the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle might have to be postponed until next winter at the very worst.

No, actually, since the expansion project is set to continue for several years, the guild and Lord Daskar probably even think that would be a relief.

In the sense of providing work for adventurers.

Rei understands these higher-level considerations, and he’s heard about them from Marina, a former guildmaster and a longtime acquaintance of Lord Daskar.

As long as he can get the Giganto Turtle’s magic stone this winter, Rei has no particular complaints about that.

……That said, with adventurers, you never know what might happen or what kind of trouble they might get caught up in.

Even if he plans for next winter now, there’s no guarantee things will go exactly as expected.

Especially since this is Gilm, the Kingdom of Mireana’s only frontier territory.

“As for dismantling the Giganto Turtle, as long as you can extract the magic stone this winter, don’t worry about the rest—you can take your time with it.”

“Really!? Ah, thank you so much!”

Perhaps Rei’s words pleased him greatly, for the guild clerk bowed deeply.

For Rei, as long as the bare minimum—extracting the magic stones—was taken care of, there was no particular problem, but for the guild clerk, that was likely a point of concern.

“What’s the matter? Did you think I was going to tell you to finish dismantling the Giganto Turtle by this winter?”

“Uh… well, some people have suggested that might be the best course of action…”

“If we start dismantling it now and keep at it until winter’s over, we might just manage it… but no, even that’s probably impossible, isn’t it?”

“I’m not sure. I think it depends on how many people we can mobilize.”

With that, the conversation ended, and Rei began stowing the monster meat—literally piled up like a mountain inside the warehouse—into the Misty Ring one after another.

It was enough meat to last an average household for several years, and it was being stored in the Misty Ring one piece after another.

The sight was truly breathtaking, and the guild staff watching couldn’t hide their astonishment.

Before their eyes, the mountain of meat vanished… After storing the other materials as well, the next thing Rei did was take the unbutchered monster carcasses out of the Misty Ring again.

Faced with a scene that completely defied their common sense, the guild staff could do nothing but stare in astonishment.

Aware of their stares, Rei continued to pull monster carcasses from the Misty Ring… and eventually, in place of the mountain of meat, even more monsters were lined up in the warehouse.

“Whoa…”

It was no wonder the guild staff member watching let out such a gasp.

But Rei paid no mind to the guild staff and extracted 50% more monsters than last time.

The reason for this increase was that it was about time for Rei to head to the Duchy of Kerebel with Elena.

While Rei was in the Duchy of Kerebel, he naturally wouldn’t be able to restock the monsters for butchering.

That’s precisely why he’d prepared more this time.

……Ideally, he could have prepared even more, but if he did, the corpses would rot even in winter.

The guild warehouse takes various factors into account—including magic items—and employs various methods to preserve them, but even so, time doesn’t stand still like it does in an item box.

“Alright, I’ll leave this to you.”

“Ah, yes. I’ll post the request on the bulletin board right now, so I think we can start dismembering them this afternoon.”

“I’ll leave that part up to you. Just… make sure no one steals the materials or magic stones.”

“Don’t worry about that. Last time, a few adventurers tried to steal some, but we caught them red-handed.”

“...I see.”

Rei couldn’t help but wonder, Isn’t it already a problem the moment they try to steal? Or, aren’t there some we haven’t caught yet? But he didn’t voice those thoughts.

After all, he was leaving all of that to the guild.

Of course, if Rei were to do the dismantling himself, or supervise other adventurers doing it, the risk of theft would be even lower.

But that would mean less free time for Rei… It’s a harsh way to put it, but in the end, I suppose that’s just a necessary cost.

After finishing his conversation with the guild staff, Rei stepped outside the warehouse.

……And the moment he stepped outside, he saw something white scattered about.

Yes, snow.

(The first snow, huh. I probably would’ve been happy about it when I was a kid.)

Watching the scene—where it wasn’t so much snowing as snowflakes were still dancing in the air—Rei found himself thinking that.

In Gilm, too, snow falls and accumulates almost every year.

Since there are even jobs for penniless adventurers like shoveling snow, you can probably imagine just how much it piles up.

“What’s wrong? …Whoa, is that snow…?”

The guild clerk who had followed Rei out of the warehouse looked at the snow and wore a genuinely disgusted expression.

That said, Rei had no intention of blaming the guild employee.

After all, having grown up in the countryside of Tohoku, Rei had been plagued by snow almost every year as well.

When he was in elementary school, he might have been happy to see the snow falling.

But once he reached middle school, he had to start helping with the snow shoveling in earnest, and since the middle school Rei attended was a fairly old wooden building, it wasn’t uncommon for snow that had blown in through the gaps in the windows to pile up in the hallways.

There was no air conditioning in the classrooms; instead, they had kerosene heaters.

But since it was inside the classroom—and right next to the teacher’s desk at that—it couldn’t warm the entire room. It was cold in the back near the hallway, and hot—no, scalding—near the stove.

Also, you had to be careful not to slip when walking on the streets, and you couldn’t ride a bicycle either.

Elementary school kids might enjoy playing in the snow, but for middle and high school students, snow is nothing but a nuisance.

Even so, the reason Rei doesn’t feel all that bad seeing the snow right now is probably because he’s staying at the Dusk Wheat inn, a high-end inn, and he doesn’t have to shovel snow himself.

The guild staff member is giving Rei a disapproving look—perhaps because shoveling the snow in front of the guild is their job.

“Do your best shoveling the snow.”

“...I just hope it doesn’t keep snowing.”

At Rei’s words, the guild employee looks up at the sky as if in prayer.

To an outside observer, that scene might look as if he were actually wishing for more snow to fall—which must be ironic for the guild employee.

Leaving the guild employee praying to the heavens behind, Rei enters the guild to collect the payment for the materials she sold from the warehouse.

“Be careful, Rei. I hear there have been thieves around here lately. …Well, I suppose I don’t need to worry about you, though.”

Lenora hands Rei a cloth bag filled with silver and gold coins and offers a word of caution, but before Rei can reply, Kenny, standing next to Lenora, speaks up.

“It’ll be fine. If there were a thief bold enough to attack Rei, they’d be nothing but a fool.”

“I know that, but it’s better to be safe than sorry, right?”

It’s true that it’s better not to run into a thief.

That said, if a thief were to run into Rei, they’d undoubtedly get caught, so for Gilm’s safety, it might actually be better to pray that Rei gets targeted by a thief.

“Well, there’s no doubt that running into a thief would cause all sorts of trouble, so I don’t want to run into one either.”

As far as Rei is concerned, even if he catches a thief, he’ll just hand them over to the guards—nothing much comes of it.

He might get a small reward, but even that would be nothing to write home about.

(Bandits are definitely more profitable than common thieves.)

For Rei, who had come to be known among bandits as the “Bandit Eater,” bandits were far more welcome targets than common thieves.

If captured alive, bandits could be sold off as criminal slaves.

Furthermore, whoever defeats the bandits gains ownership of all the treasure they’ve hoarded.

In many ways, hunting bandits was a lucrative endeavor for Rei.

Of course, that was only true as long as Rei had the strength to take them down alone… or with Set by his side.

“Well, with you, Rei, thieves are no big deal. …But since the person doing this is probably someone who came for the expansion work, just be careful, okay?”

Hearing Kenny say that... Rei nodded in response, thinking to himself, "That sounds like a plot flag."
