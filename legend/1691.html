"Oh, I can see Gilm now."

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set lets out a happy cry.

The vacation at the beach was fun, but for Rei and Set, Gilm is still the place they belong.

“Still… there sure are a lot of people here as always.”

Rei muttered this because the number of people heading toward Gilm showed no sign of decreasing compared to before.

It was clear that the expansion project in Gilm was a massive undertaking.

In fact, depending on the circumstances, it wouldn’t be surprising if it took several years to complete… that’s the kind of project it is.

Given that, it’s only natural that they’d want as many workers as possible to handle the various tasks.

Gilm was constantly accepting such workers, and as a result, it was undeniable that this many people were still heading there.

(But what about the inn situation?)

As far as Rei knew, the inns in Gilm should have been fully booked quite some time ago.

As the number of people sleeping rough increased, simple lodgings—though calling them “lodgings” would be a stretch—were built near the construction site to prevent that.

But with the numbers swelling like this, it was obvious that even that building, where people slept on the floor, was nothing more than a drop in the bucket. So where were all these people who had gathered here sleeping?

Just as Rei was thinking this, he spotted a wagon loaded with several logs moving along the road.

Naturally, ordinary wagons couldn’t possibly carry so many logs; these were wagons specially built for transporting timber.

Several wagons were coupled together, and naturally, there were many horses pulling them.

Rei had been wondering where to disembark, but he decided that since he was here anyway, he might as well stow those logs to save himself the trouble.

With that in mind, Rei told Set to descend to the ground.

Of course, the first thing to land—or rather, be dropped—was, naturally, the Set Cage.

It descended toward a spot slightly away from the wagons carrying the logs, and just as it reached its lowest point, the Set Cage was released.

The sound of the Set Cage hitting the ground echoed through the area, startling those who were unaware of what was happening.

Amidst this, only the very few who know of the Set Cage’s existence are startled by the sound for a moment, but quickly regain their composure.

The more considerate among them reassure the people around them, who are on the verge of panic, that it is safe.

In fact, the Set Cage’s ability dissipates immediately, and before long, only the Set Cage remains where the sound originated.

Soon after, the sound of flapping wings is heard, and eventually, Set descends.

(They’re surprised by the Set Cage, but not by Set himself… in fact, many of them seem delighted. I suppose that means Set’s presence is becoming more widely known… Hmm, to be honest, I’m not sure if I should be happy about that.)

While Rei was happy that Set had been accepted, he also worried that people might stop being afraid of monsters. Above all, he feared that there might be people who would try to mess with Set.

If teasing Set meant they were admiring him, she’d welcome it… but dealing with people who teased him just to get his fur or feathers was a huge hassle.

In any case, once Rei had descended to the ground, he immediately headed toward the carriage with Set.

The coachman and the guards, naturally, looked delighted when they saw Rei and Set.

...Of course, part of it was simply the joy of seeing them again after so long, but the biggest reason was likely that they understood their work would become much easier.

“Ooooooooooooh! It’s Rei! Rei’s back!”

“Our savior, Rei! No, Mr. Rei! Lord Rei! Master Rei!”

“Great, just great. This will make the job of hauling wood a lot easier.”

Everyone rejoices like this, while bystanders with no connection to the situation wonder why they’re so excited.

But Rei didn’t seem to care about their reactions and spoke up.

“So, is it okay if I take this?”

“Oh, of course! Please do! Without you, Rei-san, just getting this down would’ve been a real struggle, let alone carrying it.”

“I bet it was.”

If you had superhuman physical abilities like Rei’s, it might be different, but carrying this tree normally would require a tremendous amount of effort.

It would be like exhausting every last ounce of strength—almost to the point of physical collapse—for anyone doing the work.

(I thought the people in charge of that area would be the ones unloading it… but judging by this, the adventurers acting as guards will probably be called upon too.)

With that thought in mind, Rei began stowing the wood from the wagon into his Misty Ring.

Seeing Rei do this, the people around her who were unaware of the situation looked dumbfounded… but since this was par for the course, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered and continued speaking.

“So, how much of the felled timber has piled up over in the Treant Forest?”

“Uh… well… I can only describe it as ‘an enormous amount.’”

The escorting adventurer replied, sounding reluctant.

But Rei didn’t get angry; if anything, he seemed to understand.

After all, Rei and the others had been away from Gilm for over ten days.

It would have been one thing if they’d been away because of the Jaya incident, but this time, they’d known from the start that they’d be back soon.

That was likely why the lumberjacks had gone ahead with logging in the Treant Forest without hesitation.

Besides, it was already late summer… no, it was actually the time of year when one should call it early autumn.

Once autumn arrives, winter will be just around the corner, and once winter comes, the woodcutters won’t be able to work.

Well, it’s not that they couldn’t work if they tried hard enough.

Especially this time, since they’re logging in the Treant Forest rather than in the mountains.

But even so, the danger level is naturally high.

Especially in winter, there are monsters that only appear during that season, and those monsters are usually stronger than ordinary ones.

If we’re going to be cutting down trees in such conditions, we’ll need to hire adventurers who are stronger than the ones we have now… but for Gilm, who is in the middle of expansion work, that would be nothing but a waste of money.

If he were to do something like that, it would be far more efficient to simply send a large group of lumberjacks in the spring.

Besides, they were currently in the middle of desperately gathering enough lumber for the winter construction, so there was no reason to go out of their way to send lumberjacks into the winter.

“I see. Well, once things settle down here, I’ll head over to the Treant Forest. Are you guys heading back there now?”

“Ah… yeah. I think a few of us should head back to let them know Rei’s returned. Especially in this case, since we need to prepare for Rei to carry the load.”

“Preparations?” Rei felt a bit puzzled by the adventurer’s words, but decided not to worry about it for now, figuring he’d understand once he got to the Forest of Treants later.

“Alright, do that then.”

“Are you done with your business? Well, I’d like you to put the Set Cage away for now.”

Hearing Marina’s voice from behind him, Rei turned his gaze toward the Set Cage.

Sure enough, several people had gathered around the Set Cage, staring at it with curiosity.

Since he’d just appeared out of nowhere, it was only natural that many people were curious.

Moreover, there were many merchants currently traveling along the road.

From a merchant’s perspective, it wouldn’t be surprising if they saw the Set Cage as a major business opportunity.

No, in fact, any merchant who couldn’t recognize a business opportunity just by looking at the Set Cage would likely be unable to survive as a merchant.

...That said, there’s no doubt that Rei has no intention of getting involved in that.

“That’s right. If things keep going like this, people with strange ideas are bound to show up.”

In the current situation, laying a hand on the Set Cage—even if it’s just to get a single piece of material—would clearly be an act of making an enemy of Rei.

Making an enemy of Rei in Gilm naturally means making an enemy of his familiar, Set, as well. If that happens, living and doing business in Gilm would become—well, not impossible, but it would certainly become much more difficult.

But even knowing that, the Set Cage piques the merchants’ interest, and above all, merchants visiting Gilm for the first time like these don’t know the trouble of making enemies of Rei and Set.

Thinking it would be better to stow the Set Cage away in the Misty Ring before things got that complicated, the merchants headed straight for the Set Cage, leaving their carriages and the adventurers behind.

Furthermore, the adventurers were stiff with tension, partly because Marina, the former guild master, had suddenly appeared.

Still, their eyes were drawn to Marina’s attire—especially the deep cleavage—which was probably just a natural male instinct.

“Step aside for a moment. Sorry about this.”

Saying this, Rei pushed his way through the crowd of merchants and onlookers to approach the Set Cage.

Naturally, those being pushed aside weren’t happy about it, but knowing it was Rei—the very person who had been riding Set just moments ago—they couldn’t very well voice their complaints.

...Unless they were complete idiots, that is.

“Hey, that hurts! What the hell are you doing? Hand that over to me as compensation for the trouble!”

A man who looked less like a merchant and more like a full-fledged bandit yelled at Rei.

Several people around them looked at the man with obvious disgust.

It was obvious to anyone watching that he was picking a fight with Rei just to steal the Set Cage.

But more people than that were actually looking at the man with pity.

They knew exactly who this man—who was hardly worthy of being called a merchant, and was better described as a bandit—was picking a fight with.

Presumably, the man had been conducting his business up until now through his own strength—through violence.

In a small village, it wouldn’t be surprising if no one could stop him from pulling such stunts.

But… this was not the kind of place where the man had been doing business before; this was Gilm.

It was a place where many skilled adventurers gathered. …Though, admittedly, there were plenty of adventurers these days who weren’t all that skilled.

In any case, precisely because it was a place like this, it was virtually impossible for the man to succeed by doing business the way he always had.

And given that the person he’d crossed paths with was none other than Rei, the man’s future as a merchant was as good as over.

“Huh? Save your nonsense for when you’re asleep. Oh, wait—even your sleep-talking is annoying, so don’t say it even when you’re asleep.”

“What!?”

From the man’s perspective, he believed that if he threatened Rei as he always had, she would do as he said.

In fact, that was exactly how he’d made his living as a merchant—by selling the goods he’d acquired through such means—and above all, Rei was small and frail; he couldn’t imagine her ever defying his orders.

Rei, who was supposed to obey his orders without question, had argued back without showing any sign of fear.

That was likely why the man didn’t understand for a moment what had been said to him.

If the man had questioned why Rei showed no fear at all in response to his threat—or, as he would put it, his “business negotiation”—he might have been able to prevent the tragedy that followed.

But the man couldn’t accept that Rei had defied his orders… and unable to control himself, he clenched his fist as usual and suddenly tried to strike him.

To the man, that action was only natural.

Caught off guard by the sudden attack, Rei could do nothing but take the blow with his own fist.

Thinking that, he smiled smugly to himself...

"Gah!"

But in the next instant, he was hit by a violent impact and sent flying.

It’s quite a sight to see a human—especially a giant like the one who just attacked Rei—get blown several meters away.

Furthermore, despite what had just happened, none of the people gathered around the Set Cage were injured.

This is likely proof that Rei had carefully calculated his strike when he sent the man flying.

For Rei, it was merely a blow that sent him flying lightly—but that was strictly from Rei’s perspective.

For the man, it was a blow more than powerful enough; though he remained conscious, he was unable to speak.

Rei glanced at the man, lost interest immediately, and started walking toward the Set Cage again.

Faced with such a Rei, no one dared to block his path any further... let alone try to get in his way and steal the Set Cage.

“Don’t go overboard, okay?”

Hearing the words of Elena, who was standing near the Set Cage, Rei nodded in response and stored the Set Cage in his Misty Ring.

Since many people had seen him stow the tree away before, few were surprised by the sight.

Still, many were surprised that he actually had an item box.

“Oh my. It’s Jerisla. Long time no see.”

“Ugh, Bistol… What are you doing here…?”

“I’m good friends with Rei-chan and the others, you know. Thanks to that, I got to go to the sea with them.”

The merchant, who seemed to be an acquaintance of Bistol, cast an envious glance at the mention of the sea.
