"Hmm... I remember Marina once telling me that shopping is the ultimate form of entertainment for women... and it seems she was right."

Elena stepped out of the magic item shop and said this with a beaming smile.

The sky was overcast, and it wouldn’t have been surprising if it had started snowing at any moment.

Yet the sight of Elena smiling possessed such a radiance that it seemed as though the sky around her had turned blue.

Captivated by Elena, Rei nodded with a smile that betrayed a hint of weariness.

“I’m glad you’re happy. I managed to buy everything safely—not just for Vihera, but also for Marina, Byune, and… Elena too.”

For Rei, this shopping trip was a relatively large splurge.

However, for Rei, who earns substantial profits from bounty hunting, various commissions, and selling materials from slain monsters, an expense of this magnitude was no problem.

...Though he had paid enough to build a house.

Still, seeing Elena’s smile like this, it was well worth the money.

Actually, even though he said “house,” it wasn’t the kind of house nobles or wealthy merchants would buy, but rather the kind a middle-class family might purchase.

In any case, after finishing his shopping and leaving the store, Rei was unsure what to do next, so he turned to Elena beside him for advice.

“Elena, what should we do next? I’ve already bought souvenirs for Marina and the others, so we could get some food or something like that, right?”

Elena replied that for close friends, it’s best to give something that will serve as a lasting memento.

That implied a question: wouldn’t food or similar items be perfectly fine as souvenirs for people they weren’t close to—at least compared to Marina and the others?

Elena, who had been basking in the satisfaction of finishing her shopping thanks to Rei’s suggestion, thought for a moment before nodding.

“Hmm, you’re right. In Rei’s case, he can take the food back while it’s still fresh. Also, how about some liquor? Anesis has a fairly famous distillery. I hear the prices aren’t that high, so I’m sure he’d be happy to receive it.”

For an ordinary person, receiving a bottle of liquor that costs as much as a Platinum or Light Gold coin would likely make them scared before they could even feel happy.

From Rei’s perspective, it’s like being given a brand-new car—a luxury car, no less—as a souvenir.

For most people, receiving a gift like that would likely inspire fear rather than joy.

In that sense, if it’s a sake that can be bought at a reasonable price and comes from a brewery famous even here in Anesis, there’s a high chance anyone who drinks sake would be happy to receive it.

...Though, of course, I can’t rule out the possibility that some might find the taste doesn’t suit them.

“Well then, let’s go buy some sake first.”

That’s what Rei said, but to his palate, sake doesn’t really taste that good.

Of course, it’s not that he can’t drink it, nor does he get completely drunk after just one sip, but he simply cannot bring himself to think it tastes good.

That said, he knew it was simply that his palate didn’t suit sake, or perhaps his taste buds weren’t developed enough—what you might call a “childish palate.”

Also, it’s common to use alcohol as a secret ingredient in cooking, and in fact, there had been plenty of dishes Rei had eaten in the past where alcohol had been used that way.

With these thoughts in mind, Rei walked down the street, guided by Elena.

Whenever they passed a food stall selling something that looked delicious, they’d stop and buy some.

(Come to think of it, Elena didn’t know of any shops selling magic items, but she does know about the sake brewery. …Well, I’ve heard it’s a famous brewery even in Anesis, so maybe she’s been there before.)

As he ate the simple dish—roasted sweet potatoes smeared with sauce—Rei glanced over at Elena beside him.

Although she was eating the same roasted sweet potato dish as Rei, watching Elena eat it still seemed like a scene of quiet elegance.

Although Rei and Elena were eating the same dish, the types of sauce were different.

Rei’s sauce was slightly spicy, while Elena’s was sweet and spicy.

To Rei, it tasted similar to the salsa sauce his mother had bought from a supermarket deli or something similar when they were in Japan before.

That said, salsa isn’t necessarily always spicy… but at least the salsa Rei had eaten was spicy.

“Huh? What’s wrong, Rei? You’re staring at me… You must be embarrassed.”

Even for Elena, who loves Rei, it must be embarrassing to be stared at while she’s eating, as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

Just as she said, seeing Elena looking slightly embarrassed, Rei glanced at the food in her hand and said, as if to change the subject.

“No, my sauce is a little spicy but pretty good. I was just wondering how Elena’s tastes.”

“Huh? Mine? It’s sweet and salty—a bit of a strange flavor. No, it’s not bad, of course; in fact, it’s pretty good.”

“……I should’ve bought some of yours too.”

Since it was just a simple dish of roasted sweet potatoes, and because they were heading to the sake brewery and couldn’t spend too much time at the food stall, Rei had only bought enough for himself.

As Rei looked at the sweet potato he was eating with interest, Elena suddenly recalled something she had heard from Ara before.

Apparently, it’s something couples normally do…

In reality, since Ara didn’t actually have a boyfriend, it must have been something she’d heard from someone else or picked up from a book.

(My father tacitly approves of Rei and me dating. So… um, it’s okay if we do something like this… right?)

After asking herself this in her mind, Elena plucked up her courage and spoke.

“Um, Rei. If you’re curious about the taste of this dish I’m eating, why don’t you try a little? H-here. A-a-a-a-a-ah…”

Showing a level of fluster one would never expect from someone nicknamed the “Princess General,” Elena held out the dish she was holding to Rei.

Rei was taken aback by Elena’s sudden change in attitude, but he could still understand what she was asking for.

(Wait, before anything else, why does Elgin even have this “open wide” culture!?)

Rei had assumed that was a culture unique to Japan… no, to Earth, but upon reflection, he realized that several people had come from Japan to this world in the past.

Rei could guess that one of them must have left this behind.

(If they were going to leave something like this behind, they should have left something like soy sauce or miso instead.)

Looking at the potato Elena held out, Rei thought, half out of frustration.

But even though Rei thought that, while he vaguely remembered how to make fish sauce from seeing it on TV a few times, he didn’t remember how to make seasonings like soy sauce or miso.

For the record, Rei’s family didn’t buy miso at the supermarket; they had it made at a shop that specialized in making and selling it.

And they used soybeans grown right at Rei’s house.

Given that environment, if Rei had really wanted to know how to make miso, he probably could have learned.

However, since she never imagined things would turn out this way, she never thought to learn how to make miso.

As for soy sauce, Rei knows absolutely nothing about how it’s made.

“Um, aren’t you going to eat it? It’s a little embarrassing for me to just stand here like this.”

As Elena said this, looking bashful, Rei quietly scanned the surroundings.

Naturally, since a celebrity like Elena was doing something like this, they were attracting a lot of attention.

Several passersby nearby had stopped in their tracks, staring intently at Rei and Elena.

So intently, in fact, that he began to wonder if they were staring a little too hard.

It felt as if they’d become a spectacle, but Rei couldn’t very well ignore the sweet potato Elena was offering, so he gently took a bite.

(This is...)

The flavor that spread through his mouth was exactly as Elena had described: a simultaneous blend of sweetness and saltiness.

It was a rather rough approximation, but as he ate it, Rei was reminded of teriyaki sauce.

Teriaki sauce, invented by the Japanese, is—well, saying it’s loved all over the world might be a slight exaggeration, but it is a sauce favored in many countries.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange if a similar flavor existed in Elgin as well…

(Ah, maybe this is something Takumu or others who came to this world introduced—perhaps it’s survived, even if only in an imperfect form.)

If possible, I’d like to try some teriyaki chicken.

Thinking this, Rei savors Elena’s potatoes… or rather, the dish that could be called teriyaki potatoes.

Seeing Rei gnawing on the spot where his teeth had left a mark and savoring it to the fullest, Elena’s cheeks flushed faintly.

From Elena’s perspective, Rei’s actions must have been the ultimate in embarrassment.

...Rei himself, however, had no idea his actions were being perceived that way; he was simply enjoying the teriyaki potatoes with pure delight.

“Um… what do you think?”

Eventually, perhaps unable to bear the silence any longer, Elena asked timidly.

Seeing her ask with her cheeks and ears bright red, anyone familiar with Elena as the Princess General or the Duke’s daughter would find it hard to believe she was the same person.

In fact, the passersby who had been watching the whole scene were even wondering if this was really Elena.

“Yeah, it’s delicious. I made the right choice buying this dish.”

“Is that so! Hmm, I thought so too.”

Seeing Elena mutter this without seeming to notice her surroundings, Rei decided to leave the area before she noticed.

“Here it is.”

It was about a twenty-minute walk from where they’d shared the “ahh.”

They could have arrived a little sooner, but they’d stopped along the way to buy souvenirs from a stall that looked delicious and to purchase items like tableware and vases from shops, which is why it took them this long to get here.

That said, since both Rei and Elena were thoroughly enjoying their date, there really wasn’t any problem at all.

“So this is the place. It’s pretty big.”

As Rei muttered, the building now standing before him and Elena was quite large.

Of course, it was nowhere near as grand as the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion, but comparing the two would be a mistake.

The scent of alcohol wafting in from outside wasn’t exactly pleasant to Rei, who didn’t care for alcoholic beverages, but for those who did enjoy a drink, it was likely a scent they’d look forward to.

“Well, let’s just buy some and head home. But I have no idea what kind of alcohol to buy. What should I do? Do you know anything about that, Elena?”

“No, unfortunately, I’m not very knowledgeable about that either. But if you tell the shopkeeper your budget, they’ll probably pick something suitable for you.”

If you don’t know something, just ask the experts.

Rei agreed with Elena’s words.

After all, it makes perfect sense to let an expert make the decision based on their knowledge rather than having an amateur waste time trying to figure it out on their own.

“I know a little about alcohol myself, but I still can’t hold a candle to a professional. Besides, most of the drinks I know are high-end, and if you’re thinking of them as souvenirs for Rei to take home… well, that’s a bit much.”

“Got it. Let’s go inside the shop and ask them first.”

Saying that, Rei entered the shop with Elena.

The moment they stepped inside, the smell of alcohol hit them even more intensely than it had outside.

As Rei and Elena frowned slightly at this, a person who appeared to be a clerk from the distillery approached them.

“Welcome, Miss Elena, Rei. How may I assist you today?”

As an employee of this liquor store, the clerk naturally recognized Rei and Elena.

The fact that Elena’s name was mentioned first was likely because they were in Anesis, ruled by Duke Kerebel.

“Hmm. Actually, Rei and I are planning to go to Gilm soon. So, we came here because we wanted to buy some wine to take back as souvenirs.”

“That’s very kind of you. Well then, let me help you select some of our recommended wines right away.”

At Elena’s words, the clerk replied with a beaming smile.
