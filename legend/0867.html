"...Whoa, they went there. Well, I guess it wouldn't be weird if it happened like this under normal circumstances."

Rei muttered within the dungeon.

There was no path ahead where his gaze fell.

Following his intuition at the previous Y-junction, Rei had turned right. He'd continued along a single path, turning a few times, for about twenty minutes.

He had walked for what felt like an eternity within this dungeon, only to find a dead end right before his eyes.

Perhaps because it was a cave-like dungeon, the end of Rei's line of sight wasn't a clear wall blocking the way.

But the path itself narrowed, making it impossible to proceed any further.

"Do I have to spend all that time going back again? ...If the point is to sap an adventurer's motivation, this is pretty damn effective harassment."

Even though he'd been cautious of traps, the realization that all that time had been wasted was undoubtedly tough on Rei mentally.

Though Rei was thinking this, he immediately turned his gaze back toward the direction he had come.

The expression on his lips wasn't the wry smile from seconds ago, but a fierce grin reserved for facing an enemy.

"I see. A dead end does mean there's nowhere to run... Could it be that this wasn't about mental fatigue at all? Could they have been aiming for this from the start?"

Keeping his spear at the ready, he waited for the enemy approaching him to reveal itself.

If this were a straight corridor, he could launch a spear attack like he did against the orcs, but in such a winding space, that was a bit difficult.

"Well, not impossible, I suppose?"

He muttered, his gaze falling not on the spear in his hand, but on a stone lying on the ground.

If the goal was merely to deter the enemy rather than kill them outright, there was no need to be fixated on the spear.

Moreover, even a stone possesses considerable lethality; depending on where it hits, it can deliver sufficient offensive power.

In fact, considering it was a stone thrown by Rei, it could be said to possess lethal force from the moment it was hurled.

...However, since each stone had a slightly different shape, the difficulty of hitting the intended spot increased.

"Well, if they'll die from a rock like this, that's fine by me."

He picked up several stones lying on the ground and waited at the bend in the passageway for the monster to appear.

"Gyaa! Gyaa!"

"Gyo gyo gya!"

"Gagagya?"

The moment the sounds reached him, a look of annoyance flashed across Rei's face.

He'd heard these cries countless times since entering the dungeon, and even without seeing them, he knew exactly what kind of monsters were approaching.

(Goblins again? Seriously, aren't there too many of them? ...Well, I did hear there were a lot of weak monsters around here. Guess I get why this dungeon isn't popular. Nobody wants to go out of their way to fight goblins in a dungeon.)

Rei possessed an abnormal amount of magic power and physical strength that put ordinary adventurers to shame. Plus, with the Mistling in hand, he never had to worry about weapon supplies.

But for the average adventurer, they wouldn't want to go out of their way to fight goblins in a dungeon.

Even if goblins are weak, fighting them drains your stamina. Using magic drains your mana, and your weapons might break.

And what do you get in return? Materials and magic stones that barely fetch any money.

Honestly, it's hard to muster any motivation to challenge this dungeon.

(Well, unless you have another purpose like me... right!)

The moment the goblin appeared around the corner, I hurled the stone in my hand.

The stone flew straight, striking the goblin's head as it appeared, oblivious to danger, shattering its skull.

The headless goblin collapsed to the ground as expected...

"Gyaa!"

The goblin that appeared right behind it screamed, looking utterly confused about what had just happened.

But that cry was clearly a mistake.

As a result, the next stone Rei hurled shattered that goblin's skull too.

The last remaining goblin, screaming, turned tail and fled on the spot.

It perfectly demonstrated the typical goblin nature: aggressive against weak opponents, but quick to flee from stronger ones.

However, the goblin recognized as an enemy by Rei couldn't escape. Rei ran to where the two goblins lay decapitated, threw another stone, and killed the last one.

"So this was the kind of place where you cornered them at this dead end, cut off their escape, and then kept attacking without pause? It would've been effective against a normal opponent, sure."

The passage itself was narrow, barely wide enough for two people to stand side by side and fight.

If they'd been forced to fight goblins endlessly in such a situation, it would have become unbearable without a certain level of strength, not least due to the mental exhaustion.

"Well, on the flip side, if you're strong enough, you can probably handle it."

Like me, he thought. He glanced at the two goblin corpses lying on the ground, then lightly leaped over them.

Rei headed back along the path he'd just come from, but ended up encountering goblins several times before finally reaching the Y-shaped fork again.

"Right, this way then."

Perhaps irritated by the constant encounters with goblins, Rei's tone naturally grew harsher.

Still, aiming for the treasure at the dungeon's core, he headed left at the Y-junction.

Then, for some reason, no goblins appeared, and he walked straight down the single path.

"Why? Over there, there were so many monsters... no, so many goblins... but over here, why is there nothing? Well, it's easier, so it's fine."

It was certainly easier, but compared to the path they'd taken so far, the lack of enemies felt oddly unsettling.

With these thoughts in mind, I continued down the passageway. Soon, three stone doors embedded in the wall came into view.

The three doors, neatly aligned in the wall, looked suspiciously obvious.

"This has to be a trap, right? ...Considering the dungeon probably hasn't been around that long, I doubt there's anything fatal, but you can never be absolutely sure."

After observing them from a distance and confirming nothing unusual happened, Rei slowly approached the doors.

He approached prepared to use his spear immediately, ready for anything that might happen. In the end, however, nothing occurred as he reached the door.

"...There is a trap, right? No matter how you look at it."

It was absolutely inconceivable that a row of doors laid out this blatantly wouldn't have at least one trap.

That was the thought behind Rei's words, but unfortunately, Rei possessed none of the skills of a thief—like detecting traps or disarming them.

He did have the scouting magic called 'Thin Flame', but with the doors closed, it was impossible to investigate what lay beyond.

The only option was to simply smash through the door, trap and all, using brute force.

"They wouldn't actually explode, would they? Well, even if they did, staying clear should be fine."

She stowed the spear she'd been holding into Mistyling and instead drew her iconic scythe, the Deathscythe.

The space in front of the door was wide enough that swinging Deathscythe wouldn't be difficult.

But even with Deathscythe in hand, Rei didn't approach the door. Instead, he took a few steps back before swinging it.

"Flying Slash!"

With that word, a slash flew from the swung Deathscythe.

The slash flew straight, hitting the door on the far right exactly as Rei intended... and sliced right through it.

"Weaker than I expected."

While Flying Slash was a skill Rei often used, it wasn't powerful enough to be called a one-hit kill.

If anything, it's a skill that serves more as a deterrent strike.

That's precisely why Rei was surprised to see the door severed by that single strike.

But that surprise lasted only a moment.

Now, he needed to see what lay beyond the door, so he headed toward where it had been.

Rei had suspected some kind of trap might activate or already be active, but in the end, he arrived at the spot where the door had been without incident.

"...Nothing here. Inside either."

What lay beyond the door was a sight that made him hesitate to even call it a room.

A rectangular room about two meters deep. It was narrow, and honestly, calling it a room felt wrong—it looked more like a broom closet or storage room.

"What the hell is this?"

Tilting his head in confusion, Rei checked inside.

Lying on the floor was the door, split clean in two by the flying slash earlier.

Nothing else. ...A room so utterly devoid of purpose that it was impossible to fathom why it was built this way.

"This is completely baffling. ...Huh? Wait a minute. Then, maybe—"

Something suddenly crossed Rei's mind, and his gaze shifted to the leftmost door of the three lined up.

Given what was inside the right door, the left door was likely similar, and the middle one was probably the correct one.

Thinking this, Rei used Flying Slash again to investigate. His prediction proved spectacularly correct.

The right door, identical in structure to the left, only elicited a sigh from Rei.

"Maybe if the dungeon keeps growing, these room-like things will grow too... But is this just how a newly created dungeon is supposed to be?"

For Rei, who was only on his third dungeon, he didn't fully grasp how dungeons specifically evolved.

Of course, he had read what the books said, but in this world, it wasn't all that unusual for what was written to differ greatly from reality.

Since most texts were handwritten, information wasn't updated frequently, so such discrepancies were to be expected.

"Well, there don't seem to be any traps, and I found the correct path pretty easily, so I guess it's fine."

Holding the Death Scythe, I moved away from the central door.

(Since there haven't been any traps so far, this door should be safe too... but since this is the correct answer, there's definitely a chance a trap could be set here.)

Even as he considered the possibility that this might be another dead end, Rei swung the Death Scythe.

"Flying Slash!"

The slash he unleashed didn't go as smoothly as before...

The center door was definitely damaged, but it still hadn't been completely destroyed.

This clearly showed that this door was different from the ones on either side.

"...Well, at least I confirmed there's no trap. That's good enough."

Though slightly disappointed that even his upgraded Flying Slash couldn't destroy the door, Rei approached it.

Beyond the partially destroyed door lay a space similar in layout to the ones on either side.

The difference was that, where the spaces on either side ended in dead ends, the passage here continued further, leading into a large open area.

"So the middle was the right choice after all."

Judging there were no traps, he used the stone-tipped end of the Death Scythe to break the door.

He proceeded straight down the passageway and was immediately able to see what the large space looked like.

...However, what awaited there was something completely unexpected.

Within that space, countless monsters existed.

Massive in stature, with ears extending from their heads as sharp as blades, fangs glaring from their faces, and tails long and sturdy like whips.

"Gameriions!? What the hell are they doing here...?"

Seeing the Gamerians filling the surprisingly large space, Rei froze for several seconds.

Given the utterly unexpected sight, it was hardly surprising, but it was probably just luck that the group of Gamerians hadn't attacked him.

But once the shock passed, all that remained within Rei was understanding.

"I see. They said Gamerians were scarce this year... but if they're gathered like this here, it makes sense they couldn't be found on the surface."

Since returning to Gilm, he'd heard it countless times: the information that Gamellion was almost impossible to catch this year.

Realizing the reason for that was in the scene before his eyes, Rei couldn't help but let a smile touch his lips.

After all, if the Gamelians were inside the dungeon, searching for them on the surface was pointless.

Of course, he didn't believe all the Gamellion were here. Rei's guess was that the Gamellion on the surface had sensed their comrades had vanished and had likely moved away from the place they came to every year.

"GAAAAAH!"

A nearby Gamellion, seemingly noticing Rei entering the room, let out a threatening roar.

Even at a glance, there were at least fifty Gamerians in front of Rei.

All fifty reacted to the first Gamerion's cry, directing their hostility toward Rei.

"Didn't the one who went into the dungeon make it this far? ...Probably didn't."

Recalling the number of goblins he'd fought since entering the dungeon, he couldn't help but agree.

"Or maybe they kept quiet, thinking they could have these Gamerians all to themselves."

This many Gamerions. The price for them wouldn't just get him through the winter.

Especially now, with Gamellion meat scarce, the price had risen.

If I sold all this gamellion on the market, the price would naturally drop below current levels. But even then, it should still fetch more than a regular sale.

"Well, that plan went out the window the moment I found it!"

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Watching the Gamellion leap, fangs bared, ready to strike, Rei gripped his Deathscythe and shouted that.
