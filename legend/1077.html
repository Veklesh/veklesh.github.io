Watching the monsters retreat after hearing Set's roar from above, a glowing orb approached Rei and began to flicker.

I couldn't understand exactly what it was trying to say, but I could tell it was probably expressing gratitude.

"Kyukyuu!"

While glancing at the orb and petting Set, who had held down the giant cicada for so long, I suddenly heard that voice.

"Huh? Yellow?"

At the end of his gaze was, naturally, a black dragon cub flying through the sky. It was Yellow, Elena's familiar.

Flap-flap-flap. Flap-flap-flap. Flap-flap-flap. Flap-flap-flap. Flap-flap-flap. Flap-flap-flap. Flap-flap-flap. Flap-flap-flap. Flap

"You're here... Ah, I knew it."

When Rei looked down toward the ground, there was Elena, her golden hair glistening in the sunlight.

In her hand, she held Mirage, its blade still wet with monster blood, her gaze fixed on Rei flying in the sky.

And naturally, Ara stood beside Elena, also gazing up at the sky, her Power Axe in hand.

The scattered corpses of monsters around them numbered well over fifty. Most were goblins, but the scene below was nothing short of a sea of blood... no, a sea of blood, flesh, and entrails.

「Set」

"Grrrr..."

As Rei spoke while stroking Yellow, Set began descending toward the ground.

Upon landing, the thick stench of blood hung heavy in the air.

Given the number of monsters defeated, this sight was only to be expected.

"Rei, the fact that we're here means we defeated that giant monster, right?"

"Yeah. It took a bit of effort, but we managed. As for this side... doesn't seem like there's any major damage. Looks like the only corpses here are monsters."

A group of Dark Elves tended to their comrades' injuries a short distance away, but none of the wounds were life-threatening.

Considering the dark elf with the broken arm appeared to be the most seriously injured, it showed just how manageable the battle here had been.

(Well, having injured people means it wasn't exactly easy...)

Several dark elves must have noticed they were being watched. They turned their gaze toward Rei.

Though the gazes directed at Rei still held traces of awe and fear, they were overwhelmingly filled with gratitude.

Everyone understood. They understood who had healed the World Tree.

And who had faced that enormous monster flying in the sky... one they knew they had no chance of defeating.

That was precisely why, while awe and fear toward Rei remained, they no longer felt an overwhelming dread.

Feeling those gazes, Rei gently looked away.

"Hehe, shouldn't you just accept their gratitude honestly?"

Seeing Rei's flustered expression, Elena smiled and said this.

Ara, standing beside Elena, also smiled at Rei's expression.

"Kyuu!"

Yellow, nestled in Rei's arms, gave a short chirp as if agreeing with his master.

"That aside..."

Knowing full well he was dodging the topic, Rei, who disliked being teased endlessly, said this while looking up... at the sky.

This was the side of the barrier's protective field, and thanks to it, the sunlight wasn't blocked by the trees.

A spring-like blue sky and warm air. If monster corpses weren't scattered around... and if the smell of blood didn't linger in the air, he might have thought he'd come for a picnic.

"I think most of the monsters scattered after Set's roar earlier, but if any heard that roar and still remained, they'd have to be pretty powerful monsters."

"Yeah, probably."

If it stayed after hearing a griffin's roar, it was undoubtedly a monster with immense confidence in its own strength.

(Personally, I'd rather have monsters like that stick around. After all, the only magic stones we got from this fight were from the giant cicada and two green wolves.)

For Rei, who was a bit dissatisfied with this round's results, the presence of a powerful monster was actually something he welcomed.

Of course, the presence of such a formidable foe meant the Dark Elves were still fighting it.

And it was highly likely they were already dead.

"So, for now, I'll go scout around the settlement perimeter."

Elena nodded back at Rei without showing any particular dissatisfaction.

She did want to stay with Rei if possible, but Elena, known by the nickname Princess General, understood perfectly well that given the current situation in the Dark Elf settlement, she couldn't hold him back.

"Alright, do your best. ...Ah, right. I'm sorry, but could you take Yellow with you?"

"Yellow?"

"Kyuu?"

Yellow, who had been quietly being petted in Rei's arms, let out a cry, seemingly noticing his name had been mentioned.

Seeing Yellow's reaction, Elena smiled and nodded.

"Yeah. Yellow might not be a main force, but his support abilities are top-notch. Especially those scales—they'll stop most weapons."

"...Wait, are you telling me to use Yellow as a shield?"

"Kyuu!?"

At the word "shield," Yellow stiffened in Rei's arms.

Yellow's scales certainly possessed defensive strength capable of deflecting ordinary attacks.

Still, Yellow was just a child. While incredibly curious, that didn't mean he was unfazed by attacks.

"Huh? Oh, did I give you the wrong idea? I didn't say to use him as a shield. I just meant that even if something happened, Yellow would be fine staying by Rei's side."

"Kyu..."

Petting Yellow, who let out a relieved cry, Rei thought for a moment... then nodded.

"I understand. If Elena says so, then I'll let it be. ...Well then, Yellow. I'll be counting on you for a while."

"Kyuu!"

"GRRRR!"

Set, who had been listening to Rei and Yellow's exchange, immediately took off, flapping his wings.

Watching the one person and two creatures depart, Elena turned toward the Dark Elves once Rei was out of sight and spoke up.

"Alright, first things first—clean up the bodies! Left out in this season, they'll rot quickly. We need to bleed them out and skin them before that happens!"

At Elena's words, the dark elves rose to their feet.

Truthfully, they wanted to rest a little longer, but they sensed that if they didn't act now, they'd end up accomplishing nothing else today.

If they did, the smell of blood would attract beasts and monsters, the corpses would rot... it would be a mess.

Since they could still manage it now, they should push themselves to act.

"For the goblins, which are the most numerous, it's probably best to just take their magic stones and burn them all together."

Elena nodded at Ara's words.

"You're right. Goblin meat tastes awful, and the usable parts are cheap. If there were no other monsters, it might be different, but right now there are many monster corpses, starting with orcs and kobolds. Given that, time is limited."

"Yes."

Saying this, Ara grabbed the legs of two fallen orcs lying on the ground and dragged them away.

The surrounding dark elves could only stare in stunned silence.

Of course, the dark elves knew Ara was strong.

After all, she wielded a power axe with ease, a weapon most dark elves couldn't even lift.

Still... seeing her drag two orcs by their legs, one in each hand, was nothing short of astonishing.

If this had been a muscular giant, it might have been believable. But Ara's appearance was slender... well, perhaps "slender" was an exaggeration, but she certainly didn't look strong.

That made the sense of incongruity all the more staggering.

...Though, in terms of strength not matching appearance, Rei was much the same.

Under the stunned gazes of the dark elves, Ara carried the heavy, monster-like orcs.

While Ara drew the dark elves' admiring gazes, Rei moved along the barrier's perimeter with Set, Yellow, and the light orb.

They passed several locations showing signs of battle, but fortunately, the fighting had mostly ended there, and the dark elves were busy disposing of the monster corpses.

Perhaps because Set's presence had frozen their movements, some areas still had a few monsters like orcs and kobolds left behind by fleeing creatures. However, while a group might pose a threat, a handful were no match for the dark elves.

Other monsters finally came to their senses and fled, but while the dark elves fired pursuit arrows and spirit magic, they did not chase them into the forest.

...Right. So, for now, there was no sign of the monsters Rei had hoped to find anywhere.

"Well, it was strange to have so many monsters here in the first place, so I suppose it can't be helped."

Despite the sheer number of monsters, Rei sometimes forgot: he was currently in the space between the Barrier of Confusion and the Barrier of Protection.

If the World Tree were in perfect condition, this would be a place where only those who had broken through the Barrier of Confusion could exist.

"...Which means that giant cicada was probably the cause after all. The monsters in that berserker state I heard about also lack their own will, so it's similar."

"Gurrrrrrrr!"

"Kyuu!"

Responding to Rei's words, Set and Yellow each let out a cry.

The orb of light, too, flickered brightly, as if trying to convey some kind of message.

Moving along much more leisurely than initially expected, another group of Dark Elves came into view.

Among them were Marina and Earth.

Polo was perched on Earth's left shoulder, showing no signs of injury.

"So this is where you were fighting. ...Looks like you didn't sustain any major injuries either."

Relieved, Rei lightly patted Set's back, and Set began descending toward the ground.

"Huh? ...Aerowings!?"

Realizing the figure lying beside them was Aerowing, he couldn't help but exclaim.

Both Set and Death Scythe had already finished absorbing Aero Wing's magic stone.

In that sense, it wasn't exactly a tasty monster for Rei, but it was tasty in another way.

While there wasn't much meat to eat, what there was was exceptionally delicious.

The Aero Wing meat dish they ate at the house Elena and the others were renting left a strong impression on Rei as well.

"Oh? Are you more interested in the Aerowings than in me?"

Marina said with a hint of pout, and Rei smiled awkwardly, trying to hide something.

As he did so, he averted his gaze toward the forest... and as if timing it perfectly, Vihera and Byune appeared.

"Rei? Looks like you've taken care of that side too."

"Vihera? What are you doing here?"

"What do you mean 'what'? This is our jurisdiction. Of course we're here."

"Huh? No, then why..."

Rei's confused gaze shifted toward Marina.

Marina, meeting that gaze, smiled faintly and spoke.

"The reason we were here? Earth and I handled this side, so we came to reinforce the place where the fighting was still going on."

"...You defeated all the monsters that attacked?"

Rei declared in a tone of surprise, but upon hearing this, Earth quietly shifted his gaze toward Marina.

Reflected in his eyes was something bordering on awe.

Because Earth had witnessed Marina's power up close.

The sight of her using spirit magic to annihilate monsters one after another.

It was something one might call the work of the hero Earth admired.

But having witnessed that power firsthand, Earth felt awe alongside his admiration.

Precisely because Earth couldn't use magic himself, he was captivated by the exchange of spirit magic that could be called immense.

That it remained awe rather than fear was likely because Earth was someone who admired heroes.

Feeling Earth's gaze upon her, Marina took a step closer to Rei.

「Rei……」

Her eyes moist, she called Rei's name and approached him gently.

Then, glancing at the light sphere beside Rei, she smiled.

Being of the bloodline connected to the World Tree, she understood perfectly well what that orb signified.

In fact, she had understood it almost instinctively from the moment it appeared in the garden.

"Thank you for the World Tree. Without Rei, it would likely have withered completely. If that had happened... you would be the benefactor not just of me, but of every Dark Elf in this settlement. ...Thank you."

With those words, Marina gently pressed her lips to Rei's.

Imbuing the kiss with the tender feelings that truly existed within her.
