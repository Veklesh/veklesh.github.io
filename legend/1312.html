Galahad was about to let the guards into the mansion to explain, but just as the matter was being settled, a carriage arrived.

The carriage had approached at considerable speed, but the absence of any commotion in front of Galahad's mansion seemed unexpected. Looking slightly bewildered, the guards dismounted from the carriage.

Seeing Marina step out last, Rei and the others looked surprised but understood.

After all, it was Rei who had originally asked the guards to be notified about the Azimov matter.

"Looks like everything turned out all right."

Marina murmured with relief, looking at Rei's face... and then at the others.

He had assumed Rei would be fine, but it wouldn't have been surprising if Galahad's mansion and the entire surrounding area had been burned to the ground.

Seemingly unaware of Marina's concern, Rei calmly asked her.

"So, what about Azimov?"

"He's fine. His injuries were already healed by Rei's potion, right? I left him at the clinic, and after that, the guards took him in. He's sleeping at the guard post now."

"...I see."

Though he didn't show it, Rei breathed a sigh of relief.

Though, with his hood up, few could have seen Rei's expression.

Observing Rei's state, Marina suddenly realized something.

That Rei's shoes were different from the ones she'd seen this morning.

"Rei, did you get the Sleipnir Boots back?"

"Yeah. ...Some joker I don't even know decided to pull a prank on me."

"Let's discuss that inside the mansion for now. There are some things I'd rather not have people overhearing."

At Galahad's words, Rei and the others nodded in agreement.

The noble guards and adventurers who had come seeking details wanted to hear the story too... but since they weren't actually involved in this matter, it was impossible to let them in.

Still, when the guards promised to explain the general situation later, they finally quieted down and returned.

"This place is fine. You lot, head back to headquarters."

One of the guards said, and the guards who had arrived by carriage nodded obediently.

Normally, they might have voiced dissatisfaction at being summoned so urgently, but the fact that the Noble Quarter... and Gilm had been spared utter destruction allowed them to breathe a sigh of relief for now.

And so, Rei and his group, Galahad and his group, and one of the guards entered the mansion.

The others who had remained there also dispersed one by one.

"For now, let's talk inside the mansion. ...Coris, is that alright with you too?"

Coris nodded in response to the confirmation... and everyone except Set entered the mansion.

"First, take your time and have a drink. Rei, you'll drink now, won't you? Unlike earlier."

When Galahad offered him tea, Rei nodded back, unlike before.

"Yeah, I'll have some."

Saying that, he took a sip of the tea prepared by Galahad's subordinate.

When they first arrived at this mansion today, Rei had viewed Galahad... and the Azoth Trading Company... as enemies to be wary of.

But it had become clear that Coris had been used by someone else. Given that, Rei decided to see Coris not as an enemy, but as a victim caught up in events beyond his control.

That was precisely why he was drinking tea with him now.

"Still... I'd heard rumors that Marina was stepping down as Guildmaster. So it's true, then?"

Galahad was also originally a Rank B adventurer.

Though he had met Marina as a high-ranking adventurer, his relationship with her wasn't necessarily good, partly because he had been working as an exclusive adventurer for the Azoth Trading Company.

During Bornter's time as chairman, the Azoth Trading Company had often made unreasonable demands and behaved arrogantly toward the guild, so this was only natural.

That's precisely why Galahad, feeling indebted, couldn't help but adopt a deferential stance.

That said, this reserved attitude was strictly limited to private encounters.

In public, as the head of the Azoth Trading Company, he had to stand his ground against Marina with dignity.

"Heh heh, you're not Guildmaster anymore, so drop the honorifics. I'm just an adventurer now. I'm partying with these three, see?"

Hearing she'd joined Rei's party, Galahad, Coris, and Murt were all taken aback.

Of the three, the most shocked was undoubtedly Murt, the active adventurer.

"Whoa, Rei formed a party... Is that true!?"

"Well, is it really that surprising?"

"I mean, Rei's famous for never joining anyone, always going solo. ...And with a beauty like this, no less..."

Rei understood what Murt was implying, but she knew words would be pointless. She merely gave a wry smile in response.

He knew how others perceived him, and it wasn't entirely wrong.

He was well aware that Marina and Vihera both harbored affection... no, love for him.

But including Byune in that group was a bit troublesome.

To Rei, Byune wasn't someone he treated as a woman, but more like a little sister.

"...Galahad doesn't seem particularly surprised."

"Ah. Rei has been spending time with Vihera for a while now, hasn't he? As for Byune, she seemed to have been absent for a period, but she ended up joining last year. ...Though Marina joining the party was unexpected."

At Rei's words, Galahad turned his gaze to Marina, who was smiling.

"Hehe, the Guildmaster was planning to pass the torch to the next generation soon anyway. Thanks to Rei, it just happened a little sooner."

"Is that so?"

"Yes. I'm now a member of the Rank B party, Crimson Wings. If you have any requests, just tell Rei. He's the party leader now."

At those words, Murt was surprised once more.

It was true that Rei was the most famous, given his alias and the existence of Set.

But as far as Murt knew, Rei was far from having the personality suited for a party leader.

Noticing Murt's gaze, Rei sighed before speaking.

"I know what you're thinking. I don't think I'm fit to be party leader either. But for various reasons, I was the only one who could take the role."

Marina, the former guild master and current advisor; Vihera, the former princess of the Bestir Empire; and Byune, who had decided to leave the party in the near future.

Given that, it somehow ended up being Rei who had to take the role.

(Come to think of it, I should probably tell Elena too. ...It's a bit of a shame it's the Paired Orb, though.)

There was quite a bit of drama with Azimov's attack, but in the end, Azimov recovered from his wounds with potions without any major issues, and we managed to get back the stolen Sleipnir Boots like this.

Thanks to that, the anger he'd initially felt had faded considerably.

Still, that was only for now. If things had gone badly... if the potion had been used even a little later, Azimov could have died. And whoever stole the Sleipnir Boots, which were important to Rei too, deserved a proper visit to express our gratitude.

(Still, he's wearing the Sleipnir Boots just fine, and there don't seem to be any problems... But are the modifications really complete? Maybe I should ask him about that properly later, once Azimov regains consciousness.)

It's true that Rei was summoned by Azimov because the modifications were finished.

Considering that, it probably wasn't a problem. Still, it couldn't be ruled out that he was making final adjustments to the Sleipnir Boots when he was attacked.

Normally, one would consider the possibility that some strange tampering had been done to the Sleipnir Boots, but Rei wasn't worried about that.

Sleipnir Boots were originally crafted by Esta Nord; they weren't something an amateur could tamper with.

No, even a professional alchemist wouldn't have been able to do anything to them in such a short time.

Still, just to be safe, he decided to have Azimov take a proper look.

"...Well then, enough small talk. Shall we get down to business?"

Marina's words caused the expressions of those present to shift. ...Only Byune remained unchanged, seemingly unaffected, enjoying the taste of the fruit served with the tea on the table.

"Right. So, which one should we start with?"

At Rei's prompting, Marina paused briefly before speaking.

"Could you go first, Rei? I do have some things to share with you, but I'd like to hear yours first."

"We followed Set's scent and arrived at this mansion. Then, when we asked to speak with Galahad, we learned that Coris had arrived just before us, so we asked Murt to call him."

At those words, the gazes of those in the room turned toward Coris.

The person being stared at wore a slightly apologetic expression.

He probably felt rather incompetent as a merchant, having had stolen goods hidden in his own carriage.

Perhaps concerned for Coris, Rei immediately spoke again.

"Coris himself seemed completely unaware of anything, so I thought something was off and had Set track the scent... and that scent led to Coris's carriage. Specifically, hidden underneath the carriage, were Sleipnir Boots. I don't know what method was used, but the scent couldn't be followed any further."

"...I see."

With Rei's explanation, Marina understood the words of the person who had approached her.

It proved the claim that someone was trying to pit Rei against the Azoth Trading Company.

It was especially suspicious that they hadn't seemed concerned about the smell at all until hiding Sleipnir Boots in Coris's carriage, yet then the scent vanished completely afterward.

(Then again, considering the source who passed that information, it could just be another form of mind manipulation designed to steer our perceptions their way.)

Under normal circumstances, I would have trusted the other party's words more.

But I couldn't. It was because of the eeriness of the person who approached me.

A person who seemed to embody the very definition of "ordinary."

And yet, someone who gave off a distinctly unnatural vibe, unlike any ordinary human.

"What about Marina?"

At Rei's words, Galahad and the other Azoth Trading Company members also turned their interested gazes toward Marina.

What Rei had described was something the Azoth Trading Company members had actually experienced.

In contrast, the information Marina possessed was entirely unknown to them.

That was precisely why Marina was now the focus of such keen attention.

Under those gazes, Marina opened her mouth.

"First off, Azimov was healed by Rei's potion, and he's currently being held at the guard station. So unless something extraordinary happens, his safety should be guaranteed."

Though Rei and the others had heard this from Marina beforehand, hearing it stated plainly like this still brought them relief.

It was an operation designed to pit Azoth Trading Company against Rei and the others.

That much, Rei and Galahad naturally understood.

The problem, however, was who had orchestrated it.

"Unfortunately, I couldn't pursue them. Just as I was talking to the person, word came that Rei and the others had stormed the Noble Quarter. While I was receiving that report, they vanished into the crowd."

"Couldn't they have been caught?"
