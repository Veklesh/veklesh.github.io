The village known as Emisuma wasn't particularly large in population, and its size was naturally just what you'd expect of a village.

Nearly a thousand people had gathered before the village, home to only about a hundred residents.

The village chief of Emisma stood before the village, watching this crowd, unsure how to react.

Born into an ordinary village in the Kingdom of Reburina, he had lived there his entire life... and had been village chief for about twenty years.

The village chief, who was approaching fifty, could never have imagined a situation like this.

Even so, facing nearly a thousand people, he was wary... but it wasn't true wariness. That was likely because most of the thousand people before the village were women, and they looked like prostitutes.

"So, um... what business do you lot have in this village? I can tell you're not bandits or anything like that, from the looks of it."

The village chief asked this, looking at Rei, but he also harbored doubts: Was this really the right person to ask?

At first glance, Rei certainly didn't look like the type to lead such a group, so that doubt was understandable.

Still, seeing him with the griffin and how the others treated Rei as a superior figure, the village chief concluded he was undoubtedly the one to negotiate with.

From the village chief's perspective, Rei's appearance and the situation around him made him seem far from a powerful figure.

Considering that, he quickly realized the latter was probably correct.

Moreover, even if that assumption was wrong, since this person was representing the group before him and had come to speak, it was only natural for the village chief to treat Rei as the negotiating partner.

"It's business. We want you to sell us clothes. As you can see, the lot of us here aren't exactly dressed for walking around outside. If you don't have clothes, cloth and sewing tools will do. We'll have someone who can sew make them."

We had been able to purchase cloth and sewing supplies from the merchants who had accompanied us until recently.

Even now, the women in the carriage were using purchased cloth to make clothes.

Of course, they couldn't make clothes like those sold by professionals, but many were capable of sewing decent garments.

...Rei couldn't possibly manage such a thing, though.

Within the Crimson Wings, the two skilled in such work were Marina and Byune.

Vihera was raised a certain way and, personality-wise, wasn't cut out for such things. Elena, though not a member of the Crimson Wings, was much the same.

"Cloth? Well, that's fine... But what kind of group are you lot? You're walking around so openly like this, so you're not criminals, right?"

"That's right."

Rei replied simply to the village chief's question.

Technically speaking—or even without that—they were criminals, given they'd stormed Rossi Castle to steal a carriage and then seized horses from the pursuing cavalry.

However, the castle in Rossi was currently in complete disarray, lacking the forces to spare for pursuing Rei and his group.

Even if they did, considering the relationship with the Kingdom of Mireana, some believed it might be better to let this slide as a favor for now, as it could give them a slight advantage in negotiations later...

In any case, no soldiers were sent in pursuit from the castle, no wanted posters were circulated, and they weren't even put on the bounty list.

...Considering the visit from the upper echelons of the Kingdom of Reburina, registering them as bounty targets with the guild was simply out of the question.

"I see. ...Then, how much will you pay for it?"

The village chief didn't entirely trust Rei's claim that he wasn't a criminal. However, he reasoned that if Rei were a criminal, he wouldn't be walking around so openly with this many people. This made him more inclined to do business with Rei.

A significant part of this was likely wishful thinking—a desire to believe he wasn't a criminal.

Even though it was a village along the highway, the Kingdom of Reburina wasn't that large to begin with.

Naturally, the village itself wasn't very large, and travelers didn't leave much money behind.

While the relatively mild climate reduced the risk of crop failure, the village chief naturally wanted to be extra cautious.

"Marina, can I leave the negotiations to you? We need more than just cloth, right? You'd be better at it than me."

"Right. Understood. Then I'll take care of the negotiations."

Saying this, Marina stepped forward before the village chief.

For a brief moment, the village chief flashed a look of displeasure at this.

Marina was clearly a dark elf, and one who could be described as an adult.

In other words, it was highly likely she had lived longer than the human village chief.

Given that, it was only natural to assume she had extensive experience negotiating with humans.

Rei had entrusted the negotiations to Marina largely because, as a man, he wasn't familiar with the finer details women might need.

"Ah, and could you sell us some fodder?"

"Huh? Well, we've got plenty of fodder, so sure."

For the village chief, fodder was exactly what the word implied—something he had in huge quantities.

He could make as much as he wanted, and if push came to shove, he could buy it quite cheaply from other nearby villages.

That's precisely why he was a bit surprised to be asked to buy feed.

...In more developed towns or cities, fodder would likely be bought and sold normally, but around here, that probably wasn't the case.

Rei, judging from the village chief's reaction that buying fodder was indeed possible, relaxed visibly.

"Then, I'll leave that negotiation to you. Sell us as much food as you can manage without straining yourself. I'm prepared to pay well, so don't worry."

He took several gold coins from the leather pouch he'd pulled out of the Misty Ring.

Seeing this, the village chief... and the other villagers froze for a moment.

Naturally. Rei hadn't selected the gold coins from the leather pouch; he'd pulled them out carelessly.

That meant the entire pouch contained nothing but gold coins.

Moreover, Rei's hand holding the gold coins showed no tremor.

There was no doubt that this was the behavior of someone accustomed to handling gold coins.

If the village chief had taken that leather pouch, his hands would undoubtedly have trembled.

Of course, being the village chief, he hadn't never touched gold coins before.

Even so, he'd never casually handled such a large quantity of gold coins before.

It's money.

Looking at Rei before him and the group she led, the village chief became certain.

"Understood. I'll have them prepare whatever you require to the best of our ability. It seems you're not the type to switch to banditry just to save a few coins."

If it were merely a matter of possessing large sums of gold, the village chief would not have made such a decision so readily.

But the group Rei led was made up of women... mostly women dressed like prostitutes, unable to conceal their weapons.

It was precisely because they were such people that the village chief could trust them.

(If anything, I need to make sure the village youths don't get any strange ideas. What would happen if they did something to anger a group this size? Well, that goes without saying.)

Even thinking that, the village chief was still a man.

Recalling his own youth, he could instantly remember how deeply interested he had been in women.

To the young men currently living in the village, these women were nothing but a temptation for their eyes.

No, even older married men who still fancy women weren't exactly rare.

What if such a man, in a moment of madness, made a move on the women and caused a commotion?

As village chief, he certainly didn't want to make enemies of the griffins guarding Rei, let alone Rei herself.

Besides, though few in number, there are some men serving as guards.

These men had fought as resistance fighters... and above all, having survived the battle at Mejogo, they possessed a certain degree of confidence in their own strength.

...Had they overcome the battle against Jaya at Mejogo solely through the strength of the Resistance, that confidence would have been complete, not merely a degree of it.

But absolute confidence can easily turn into overconfidence.

Thinking about it that way, the Resistance members didn't cause any unnecessary trouble by overestimating their own abilities, so they could be grateful to Rei... but the feelings the Resistance men directed towards Rei included awe, worship... and even disgust in some cases.

This aspect differed greatly from what Rei had imagined.

"Well then, shall we begin with negotiations? And... there's a river nearby, right? A fairly large one?"

Just as they were about to begin negotiations, Marina suddenly uttered those words, causing the village chief to tilt his head in confusion.

"Well, there is definitely a river a little way from here, but what of it?"

"No, I just wanted to confirm that. Now, you lot must be bored waiting here. Why don't you go to the river and take a bath?"

'WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

The moment Marina suggested they go take a dip, shrieks erupted from the women's mouths.

No, it wasn't cheers anymore. It was more like angry shouts—the kind of sound you'd believe if someone called it that.

And it was only natural.

Ever since being forcibly awakened by the commotion at Mejogo, they hadn't had the luxury of cleansing themselves.

They had made do by wetting cloths with rationed water to wipe themselves down, but now, walking all day under the blazing summer sun, sweat poured from them naturally, and dirt—dust and grime—clung to their bodies.

Even in such conditions, they understood they were in the midst of an escape, and that was precisely why they didn't complain. ...Though some did complain and ask Rei to do something about it, considering the number of people in the group, wiping themselves down with a damp cloth was really the best they could manage.

For those women, being allowed to bathe in the river was the ultimate entertainment.

...And upon hearing Marina's words, several of the village men wore delighted smiles.

Seeing those faces, it was obvious what they were imagining as they smiled... no need to wonder.

The village chief looked at those men and wore a faintly troubled expression.

It was painfully obvious what the village men would do if things continued like this.

Then, as if reading the village chief's inner thoughts, Marina smiled and spoke.

"Village Chief, I should probably mention this... There aren't any people in this village who would do something as vulgar as peeping, right? I'll have someone keep watch, but... if anyone like that is found, I'll have them dealt with appropriately, you understand?"

"W-well..."

At this point, he should have been able to state definitively that no one in the village would do such a thing.

But truthfully, judging by the behavior of a few men, he couldn't honestly say peeping definitely didn't happen.

But saying something like, "They probably do it, but please handle it quietly if possible..." would make it seem like the villagers were only capable of such petty things.

Either way, the village chief didn't have many options at her disposal.

Thus, the village chief found himself negotiating with Marina from a position of weakness from the start.

...Marina wasn't actually planning to haggle the villagers down that cheaply, so it probably wouldn't get that bad.

"Well then, I have negotiations with the village chief... Vihera, and Sura, can you watch the river? If anyone tries anything inappropriate, make sure to let them know. And I mean, really let them know."

Marina announced this with a seductive smile unfit for daylight.

The village chief, despite his age, found himself utterly captivated by Marina's beauty.

But he wasn't the only one. The other men present were equally captivated, and even some of the women couldn't tear their eyes away.

Ignoring the village chief and the others, Vihera, having received her instructions, led the rest of the group away from the scene.

Since Marina's face had been turned toward the village chief and the others, the resistance fighters and women hadn't seen her face and hadn't been captivated... which was probably fortunate.

In the end, only the male members of the Resistance, starting with Rei, remained behind.

(Did Byune go bathing too? ...Well, she is a woman, after all.)

Thinking this, Rei waited to stow the items purchased in the village, starting with the fodder, into the Misty Ring.
