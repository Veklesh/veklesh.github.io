"Pleased to meet you, Rei. I am Erinde Exilia, the academy's headmistress."

The person who spoke, smiling at Rei and Samaloon seated on the sofa, had pointed ears.

Seeing those ears, Rei understood immediately that the being before him was an elf.

Her green hair, reaching nearly to her ears, further emphasized her elven nature.

But there was something he couldn't grasp. That was...

(Male? Female? ...Which is it?)

Yes, the gender of the elf smiling before Rei.

From Rei's perspective, describing that elf as androgynous seemed most fitting. It was impossible to determine whether they were male or female.

If told it was a man, she could believe it was a man; if told it was a woman, she could accept it as a woman. That's the kind of person it was.

"Um... I'm Rei. I'll be staying here for a short while until spring. I'm Principal Erinde Exilia."

Having said that much, Rei once again turned his gaze to the person before him.

Erinde Exilia. The fact that she had a surname indicated that the elf before him was of noble birth.

Though Rei had been startled by Erinde's androgynous appearance, it seemed she had only just realized the presence of a surname.

Understanding what the bewildered Rei was thinking as she looked back at her, Erinde smiled and spoke.

"Are you curious about my gender?"

"...Well, to put it simply..."

"Hehe, I'll tell you once we're a little closer. Until then, you can ponder it all you like."

The way she said it with that smile was truly the definition of androgynous.

(I see, the mischievous type. Well, she did send her familiar bird flying over here, so she's definitely curious. Still, normally it wouldn't be hard to tell just by looking at her appearance.)

Rei's gaze briefly drifted toward Erinde's chest.

If Erinde were female, that area should have been noticeably rounded.

But even to Rei's eyes, her chest didn't appear to be rounded.

Still, he couldn't deny she was male, because he'd read in a book before that elves often had thin builds.

(Dark elves, on the other hand, are well-built compared to regular elves.)

What flashed through Rei's mind was the guildmaster of Gilm, who seemed like the very embodiment of the word "alluring."

Marina, the dark elf, radiated an allure powerful enough to draw any man to her.

You couldn't possibly compare someone like that to an ordinary elf. That's what Rei thought, unaware that Erinde actually harbored similar thoughts deep inside.

(With her hood down, she certainly doesn't look like a man. Though, in his case, his gender is already clear, so it probably doesn't matter.)

Erinde wore a cheerful smile that gave nothing away as she spoke.

"Now then, the reason I asked you to come all the way to the Headmaster's office is partly because I wanted to see you. ...Well, I must say, I'm surprised. Magic items you can tell just by looking at them... How many do you have? It's been quite a while since I've seen someone with that many magic items."

She murmurs with apparent admiration, shifting her gaze to Rei's body.

To the places where numerous magic items were attached—his hands, feet, body.

"Not just the Item Box?"

Probably because he hadn't noticed it himself. Samaloon shifted his gaze toward Erinde, then looked back at Rei.

Without eyes that could see magic power, and not being an alchemist, he couldn't sense whether they were magic items or not.

To Samaloon, even looking at Rei, it was impossible to tell where he wore the magic items.

...If Samaloon had seen Set, he might have noticed the magic item on his front leg. Unfortunately, when Rei met Samaloon at the staff dormitory, he had already parted ways with Set.

"Ah. Well, I can't tell which is which specific magic item, but even so, just by looking at him like this, I can tell he's wearing several magic items. ...Still, that's quite a lot of magic items you have. It must have been quite a task to acquire them all?"

"I won't deny it. Actually, I got a lot of them as rewards for requests, and sometimes I found real bargains while wandering around town."

"How on earth did you manage to... Magic items are expensive everywhere, after all."

While everyday magic items might be manageable, the combat-ready magic items Rei collects are incredibly expensive.

Thinking about how much time it would take her to gather that many... Erinde couldn't help but utter a gasp of admiration.

Samaloon felt the same way.

No, Samaloon, who was even more devoted to magic, was directing an even more intense gaze at Rei than Erinde.

(I get that he's into magic and that's why he's friendly toward me, but I use magic intuitively, not theoretically. If he wants to discuss it, I'd be a bit stuck.)

Feeling a slightly unpleasant premonition at the gaze directed at him, Rei nonetheless opened his mouth without appearing to care on the surface.

"Hobbies are like that, right? Especially in my case, since I have a lot of magic items I got from my master."

He decided this was the perfect moment to leverage his backstory, launching into his usual explanation about his master.

(Thinking about it now, I'm amazed I came up with this backstory. Thanks, my imaginary master. Keep working hard for me.)

Unbeknownst to Rei, Erinde and Samaloon nodded deeply, seemingly profoundly moved by his words.

"I see. For a master to do that for their disciple... that's quite something."

"Yes. Normally, many would try to keep the magic item for themselves... or rather, I should say most would do so."

"That's true. To give such a gift to help your disciple stand on their own... Honestly, I'm not sure I could do that. Rei's master must be a truly remarkable mage."

"Huh? Ah... ah, yeah. That's right. I'm grateful to my master too."

Rei's cheeks twitched at how much higher his master was regarded than he'd imagined.

It was only natural, really. The master he'd conjured up in his imagination had somehow become regarded as a person of integrity.

"So, could you tell me why I was called here in the first place?"

As Rei tried to dodge the question, Erinde finally remembered and spoke up.

"There wasn't any particular reason, really. I simply wanted to meet you. After all, you're the adventurer known as Crimson. And since you'll be at my academy until spring, I wanted to know what kind of person you are as soon as possible."

"...And? What are your impressions after meeting me?"

"Well... it was a bit different from what I expected, I suppose? But then again, rumors tend to get exaggerated as they spread, so maybe that's only natural."

"True. Rumors about severing the limbs of hostile nobles? That's a bit much, don't you think?"

Samaloon nodded in agreement with Erinde's words... but for Rei, it was hard to admit that at least half of those rumors were true.

After all, the Keep, which was the cause of this whole incident, had both shoulders severed.

He was still alive, but after causing such a commotion, he would undoubtedly be discarded by Count Eriell. If that happened, obtaining prosthetic arms would be impossible.

Obtaining a prosthetic arm created through alchemy, in particular, was practically impossible.

"I don't really pay much attention to the rumors. Seems like a lot of truth gets mixed in anyway."

That was all Rei could say.

"Huh, you're quite humble. Normally, people would want to deny unpleasant rumors about themselves."

"That's exactly why you're Rei, Headmaster. You're probably one of the foremost figures among the magicians in this world. This magnanimity is truly remarkable. I should learn from it myself."

"...It's not like I deserve that much praise."

For some reason, he felt slightly uncomfortable with Samaloon's rapidly rising regard for him.

Normally, being able to respect someone younger than oneself so purely should be something to be praised.

But... Rei, the recipient of that gaze, sighed inwardly.

(I hardly ever get looked at like this normally. ...Ah, there was Baslero. But Baslero was quite a bit younger than me.)

Samaroon's demeanor reminded Rei of Baslero, but Baslero was only about ten years old.

To an outsider, Rei looked almost identical to mid-teens... which was how Rei appeared to the world. Yet Rei didn't seem bothered by it at all.

"Being looked at like that is a bit awkward. Please treat me normally."

"Even if you say that... From my perspective, Rei seems like someone who stands at the pinnacle of magic. As a fellow magician, having someone I can look up to like that is truly a blessing."

Faced with the gaze from Samaloon, Rei shot a pleading look toward Erinde.

Understanding the meaning behind that look, Erinde gave a wry smile and spoke up.

"Samaroon, leave it at that. Rei is clearly uncomfortable. How you feel about him is your own business, but some people simply don't like having those feelings displayed."

When the headmistress, Erinde, said that, Samalune couldn't press the matter further.

While Rei was someone Samaloon respected, he also held deep respect for Erinde, an elf and a mage, as someone above him in that regard.

"If the Headmistress says so... But, Rei. Would it be possible to hear you speak about magic sometime soon?"

"Hmm... I understand the sentiment, but it might be a letdown for you, Samaloon? In my case, I use magic based on feeling, not theory. I do tinker with the composition of spells, but even that isn't based on proper theory."

"...I-I see. Using magic without any theory at all... Geniuses really do exist, huh?"

Samaroon's expression clearly showed he found Rei's words utterly surprising. He looked stunned... no, more like he refused to believe it.

Seeing Samaloon like this, Erinde gave a wry smile.

She was an instructor beloved by the magic-using students at the academy, but sometimes she went too far, like now.

(If only she weren't like this, she'd be a great instructor. ...No, researchers are naturally curious. If Samaloon's actions lead to advances in magic, then it's something we should welcome.)

Erinde sighed with a look of frustration and relief, and even to Rei, it was still impossible to tell whether she was male or female.

Being an elf probably played a part, but it was incredibly hard to tell.

(If only she'd wear clothes that made it obvious she was female, like Marina does...)

The image of the guildmaster, also an elf, flashed through Rei's mind.

"Now then, I'd like to talk a bit longer, but unfortunately, my duties as Headmaster won't allow it. Let's leave this first meeting here. ...Rei, could you come back here tomorrow morning? I have several announcements to make."

Rei nodded at Erinde's words and rose from the sofa where he'd been sitting.

"Well then, though it's only a short time until spring, I'll be counting on you starting tomorrow. ...I might end up causing you some trouble, though."

"Hahaha. Everyone who comes here is young. While saying 'every day' might be a slight exaggeration, it's still not uncommon for trouble to happen. Keep that in mind and do your best. ...Well then, Samaloon. I'm counting on you for him."

"Yes. Leave it to me."

That seemed to be the signal. Erinde stood up and headed toward her desk.

Samaroon did the same, prompting Rei to leave the principal's office.

(Well, at any rate, I've arrived at the academy. That means I'll likely spend my days busy and noisy until around spring.)

Muttering to himself, Rei glanced back into the principal's office one last time before leaving.

Sensing his gaze, Erinde suddenly looked up and offered Rei a smile.
