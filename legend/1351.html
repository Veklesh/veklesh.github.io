About a month had passed since the commotion at Spier Trading Company, and the season had fully turned to spring.

Well, it was already spring when the Spier Trading Company incident happened, but mornings were still chilly back then.

But now, that was no longer the case.

It was springtime, a season where you could enjoy the warm, spring-like weather.

(If I were in Japan, I'd be going out to see the cherry blossoms...)

In Marina's house courtyard, using Set as a pillow as he lay sprawled out, Rei felt a light drowsiness wash over him as he scanned his surroundings with just his eyes.

Several trees did grow in the garden, but naturally, there were no cherry blossoms among them.

In fact, it had been several years since Rei had come to this Elgin, and he had never seen a cherry tree.

He'd seen various trees that resembled ones he knew from Japan. That's precisely why he thought cherry trees might be here too... but unfortunately, he hadn't found any yet.

Then again, the cherry trees in Japan are the result of extensive breeding.

Even if there were cherry trees here in Elgin, they would likely be vastly different from the ones Rei knew.

Back in Japan, around this time of year... around late April to early May, that's when the cherry blossoms would start blooming in the region where Rei lived.

That's probably why Rei wants to see cherry blossoms like this now.

For Rei, cherry blossoms aren't about graduation ceremonies or entrance ceremonies... if anything, they're more strongly associated with reaching their peak during the Golden Week holidays in May.

(Cherry blossom viewing... Even if that's impossible, maybe a picnic with the Crimson Wings crew, bringing bento boxes...)

Thinking like that, Rei drifted from a half-asleep state into full slumber.

"Well, well, does using Set as a pillow make you sleep that soundly?"

Marina murmured with a smile, watching Rei's state.

As usual, a table brought out into the garden using Rei's Misty Ring held a cup of tea.

"With this sunshine, it's no wonder Rei ended up like that."

Vihera murmured while sipping her herbal tea.

"Hmm."

Reaching for the sandwich on the table, Byune voiced her agreement.

After the Spier Trading Company incident concluded, Crimson Wings took on several requests, though none were particularly demanding.

Things like exterminating a goblin horde that appeared in spring, eliminating a harpy flock, and procuring fangboar meat for the cafeteria...

They weren't exactly the kind of jobs you'd expect from a Rank B party, but after getting caught up in that commotion right after forming the party, they'd decided to take it easy for a bit.

"Hehe, seeing things like that makes me want to enjoy a nap together,"

Marina murmured, her gaze fixed on the sky.

A few clouds drifted by, occasionally dimming the sunlight... yet the spring sun still cast a soft glow upon the earth.

If this were summer, the sunlight would be much more assertive.

You could say it was the sun unique to spring.

"Days like this, with nothing much happening... they're not so bad, are they?"

Hearing Marina's words, Vihera murmurs while looking at Rei.

Hearing Vihera's words, Marina gave her a look of genuine surprise.

It was only natural. Marina knew Vihera craved combat... beyond the three basic desires—appetite, sleep, and sex—she possessed what could be called a combat drive.

That's precisely why hearing such words come from Vihera's mouth was surprising.

It wasn't just Marina; Byune felt the same way.

Her hand, which had been reaching for a sandwich, froze at Vihera's words.

Seeing the two of them like that, Vihera opened her mouth, a slightly dissatisfied expression on her face.

"What? I'm perfectly capable of being interested in things besides fighting, you know?"

"Well, watching Vihera normally, I wouldn't think so."

"Hmm."

Marina's words prompted Byune to nod in agreement.

Seeing this, Vihera flashed another dissatisfied look, but she seemed to realize that anything else she said would be futile. She obediently took a sip from her cup of herbal tea.

Seeing Vihera like this, Marina smiled again.

And so, a peaceful time passed at Marina's house...

"Excuse me, is Marina-sama here?!"

That urgent voice echoed, bringing the elegant moment to an abrupt end.

Time rewound to several days prior, when that rude voice echoed through Marina's mansion... An adventurer party was walking far off the road leading to Gilm.

Though five members made them a fairly large party, they were well-balanced: two warriors, two archers, and one thief.

As a Rank D party, they were trying to break out of their newbie status and make a name for themselves in Gilm—a common ambition for parties during the spring season.

The party leader, a man who believed they could quickly make a name for themselves in Gilm, decided to veer slightly off the main road leading to the city instead of continuing straight.

Several members, aware they were in the frontier, tried to dissuade the leader... but in the villages where they'd operated until now, the leader's whims had often brought them great fortune.

Aware of this, the party leader shook his head at his companions' objections.

"Don't be stupid. Do you really think we can make it big in Gilm if we just keep doing the same things as everyone else?"

"I know that, but reconsider, Dagza. The road is safe precisely because it's the road, right? If we stray from it, we'll obviously get attacked by monsters."

"Soknan's right. If this were a normal place, I'd agree with you, Dagza—taking a little risk might be manageable. But this is the frontier. The monsters that appear here are different from the ones we've fought before."

The warrior man and the archer woman pleaded with party leader Dagza to reconsider.

But Dagza showed no sign of listening to his companions.

He believed everything had gone his way until now, so this time would be fine too.

People call that arrogance, not confidence, but those who fall into such a position often fail to recognize it.

Moreover, the other members, aside from the two cautious ones, agreed with Dagza's opinion, and they ended up proceeding off the beaten path.

...Of course, Dagza understood this was the frontier.

But even so, he had convinced himself that they could handle it.

The misfortune for this party was that they were the only ones traveling this road at that very moment.

If there had been merchants familiar with the frontier here, or even adventurers active in Gilm, they would undoubtedly have stopped Duzza and his group.

It wouldn't have been purely out of kindness.

If Dagza's party were attacked by monsters and fled back onto the road... it would inevitably endanger others.

Moreover, not everyone on the road possessed combat strength.

Most hire escorts, but even they don't seek out combat. Some, for various reasons, don't hire escorts at all.

What would happen if those being chased by monsters—and excited monsters at that—were to bring them to such a place?

It's obvious, isn't it?

Those without combat skills would become monster fodder without putting up much resistance, while those with combat skills would waste their stamina and weapons needlessly.

Magic items might fare better, but ordinary weapons would chip their blades or lose their sharpness from blood and grease.

This naturally requires maintenance, leading to unnecessary expenses.

And if adventurers, already drained of stamina and weapons after completing a quest, happened to be caught in such a situation, death could very well be their fate.

And naturally, whoever dragged everyone into such a disaster wouldn't get off scot-free. If they weren't careful, they could be arrested by the guard or the knights and punished.

...Dagza included, the optimistic ones hadn't even considered things going that far.

"Come on, let's just go! I'm the party leader, and I say it's fine, so it's fine!"

The others nodded half-heartedly at Dagza's declaration.

After all, following Dagza's lead had always worked out well for them so far, which was why they were heading to Gilm now.

Considering their past experiences, it wasn't entirely wrong.

So, Dazza and the others left the road. But it was still daytime... and they weren't far from the road, so the only encounters they had were with goblins.

For Duzza and his group, a group of several dozen might be another matter, but a few goblins were hardly worth calling enemies.

So, slaying goblins as they went, Dagza and his group pressed on.

"Hmph. They called this the frontier, so I was expecting some seriously powerful foes. But goblins? Couldn't we get something a bit higher rank... some monster that'll make us look good when we arrive in Gilm?"

Stripping magic stones and proof-of-kill parts from the goblins, Dagza muttered as he severed the corpses' limbs and heads, tossing them aside carelessly.

This wasn't him messing around with the corpses, mind you—it was a precaution against undead monsters.

Doing this made it harder for them to turn into skeletons or zombies, and even if they did, they'd be missing limbs.

Additionally, the limbs and head were just the right size for monsters or animals to carry them off and eat as their own prey.

...Though, to be fair, no creature particularly fancies goblin meat.

Anyway, Dagza and the others pressed on, slaying goblins as they went. Having strayed from the main road, they hadn't reached Gilm even as dusk fell.

"Damn it. Guess we have no choice. It's getting dark. Find a decent spot and we'll camp out tonight! We'll make it to Gilm tomorrow, so keep that in mind!"

Dagza shouted, and the others, who had been walking continuously despite taking short breaks, breathed sighs of relief at his words.

Less than twenty minutes after Dagza's declaration, the group came upon a deep forest.

"Camping inside the forest... I'm worried about monsters though..."

One of the party members murmured, watching Dagza head toward the trees.

Even in the areas they had previously operated in, nighttime forests were places where monsters, wild beasts, and bandits could attack.

And this place they were in now was the frontier.

They had made it clear they absolutely refused to camp in such a remote forest.

The others likely shared this view. They each voiced their opinions to Dagza.

Even as the party leader, he couldn't simply ignore the others' opinions.

In the end, Dagza agreed to everyone's opinion, and they camped a short distance away from the forest.

The reason they didn't completely leave the forest, despite their complaints, was probably because it was a treasure trove of deadwood and food.

While preparing camp together, they immediately ventured into the woods to search for food.

"Well, it's spring, so mountain vegetables and fruits should be common. And with a forest this size, there's no doubt animals are around too,"

Dagza said this, but... even after ten minutes deep in the forest, they hadn't spotted a single animal.

For some reason, there weren't any wild vegetables growing either; only fruits hanging from the trees.

They could hear birds chirping, but even those birds were small enough to be called finches.

"...What's going on here?"

The man called Soknan looked around in puzzlement.

"Yeah... Probably. I think a powerful monster was in this forest. And the other monsters and animals fled in fear of it... What do you think?"

Soknan frowned at the female archer's words and shook his head.

"I get what you're saying, Slesha, but I've never heard of anything like that before. Besides, if such a powerful monster was near Gilm, wouldn't adventurers come from Gilm to deal with it?"

"So, maybe adventurers came from Gilm and defeated the monster that was here?"

"...Hmm, I wonder. Right now, Gilm has Crimson, who has a griffin as a familiar, and there are other adventurers with nicknames and Rank A adventurers too. Thinking about it that way, it's not impossible..."

Listening to the two of them, Dagza scratched his head, seemingly fed up, and spoke up.

"Fine, whatever. Let's head back for now. We got some fruit and nuts, so we'll just have to make do with the jerky for the rest."

At Dagza's words, Soknan and Slesha said nothing more and left the forest.

...If only they had thought this matter through more deeply at that moment.

Suresha would later deeply regret it.
