While Rei and Set, along with Vihera, were each fighting a group of lizardmen... the adventurers who had been attacked by those lizardmen managed to escape the scene.

"Anyone injured!"

"Plenty of us are! But no one's hurt so badly they can't move, so don't worry!"

The man commanding the adventurers shouted, followed by a female adventurer's cry.

True to her words, all the adventurers had sustained injuries to varying degrees after being attacked by about forty lizardmen.

Still, as the female adventurer had shouted, most of the injuries were minor.

This likely proved that these adventurers possessed reasonably high skill levels.

They weren't a single party of a few people, but a group of several parties numbering over ten.

They had received a request from the guild to investigate the sudden appearance of several monster groups, but encountered the lizardmen swarm along the way.

Fortunately for these adventurers, the Gilm Knights had already discovered the lizardmen swarm and submitted a request to the guild for its extermination.

Thanks to that, Rei and the others arrived at the scene before suffering catastrophic damage, narrowly escaping disaster.

"So, what do we do now? That griffin was Set-chan, right?"

One of the adventurers shouted.

Over two meters tall, with a body of solid muscle, wielding a greatsword – a power fighter whose face could rightly be described as intimidating.

Despite that appearance, he actually had a soft spot for cute things and had given Set jerky and skewered meat many times before.

That was probably why he was the first to recognize Set.

"So, it's Rei. Then we don't need to get involved, right?"

"...But it looked like someone was hanging onto Set-chan's leg... Was I imagining things?"

"Nah, I saw that too."

The female adventurer muttered, and the other adventurer nodded in agreement.

They'd been too busy trying to get away from the lizardmen to get a good look, but still, someone hanging onto Set's leg had definitely been there.

"Hanging onto Set-chan and flying like that... that takes some guts, doesn't it?"

"Yeah. Set's friendly, but he's still a monster, right? Didn't they think about getting shaken off?"

"Hard to say. But Set's a Rank A monster, and apparently smarter than most humans around here. As long as you don't do anything to piss him off, you should be fine."

"So, you mean there's no way Set-chan would drop you?"

"Can't say for sure. If you get in Set's way while flying, he'll probably knock you right off."

As they talked like this, the man leading these adventurers finally spoke up.

"So, the question is what we do next."

"What do we do? Is there something?"

The man nodded in response to one of the adventurers' questions.

"Rei and Set... and one more. I can't say for sure who was clinging to Set, but it's probably that female adventurer who's been teaming up with Rei lately."

"Ah, that ridiculously beautiful one... I'd love to spend just one night with her."

"Disgusting."

Relieved they'd escaped the lizardmen, one of the men muttered. The female adventurer shot him a look of contempt and retorted.

"Shut up. We barely escaped with our lives. It's only natural to want to hold a woman after that. Ah, relax. I pick my partners too. I'd never touch a violent woman like you."

"Wh-what did you say!? How dare you... I won't forgive you!"

"Calm down, you two. Don't waste time sidetracking the conversation."

After warning the two adventurers who had started arguing, the man spoke again.

"So, what do we do now? Well, since Rei's here, the lizardmen don't stand a chance. Since they saved us, I think we should at least greet them."

"But... there might still be lizardmen left!"

Another adventurer pointed out, but the man, knowing Rei's strength, held no such concern.

"Relax. This is Rei we're talking about—the one who wiped out the entire Bestir Empire army. A few lizardmen? That many? Piece of cake."

He hadn't counted the lizardmen precisely, being too busy fleeing.

But even so, he could tell they were around forty, certainly less than a hundred.

There was no way Rei could lose to such opponents.

Most of the other adventurers shared this view, and in the end, the entire group decided to return to the location where the lizardmen were likely fighting.

And what the returning adventurers saw was...

"Damn, this is bad."

No one knew who had muttered that single word.

But the scene before them now was one that truly deserved that exclamation.

"Hey, what's wrong? Is that all you've got? You can land a sharper strike than that, right!?"

Vihera shouted, parrying the Lizardman leader's desperate thrusts with his spear.

A series of thrusts that would be difficult for an ordinary adventurer to parry.

Occasionally, the thrusts were mixed with attacks using his tail.

But Vihera dodged and deflected them all.

Worse still, between attacks, he landed light punches on the Lizardman Leader's body as counterattacks.

That single blow certainly didn't inflict any damage on the lizardman leader.

But... it served to remind the lizardman leader that if Vihera were to get serious, he himself would suffer significant damage in an instant.

The lizardman leader had seen it with his own eyes: lizardmen whose bones were broken by Vihera's single strike, whose skin and flesh were torn apart by his claws, and who lost consciousness from nothing more than a light touch.

His subordinates were crushed in an instant... utterly and completely overpowered before his very eyes. On top of that, his spear attacks were completely useless against him.

Then, as if to toy with them, fists struck their bodies.

...This continued for several minutes.

The returning adventurers knew full well that the one fighting Vihera was one of the lizardmen leaders who had led the attack against them. Yet, they couldn't help but feel pity.

To the adventurers, what Vihera was doing now could only be seen as a cruel act.

"Shhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh

The lizardman leader must have understood he was being toyed with. His frustration evident, he swung his spear again.

To humans, the expressions of lizardmen were utterly incomprehensible.

But even so, the adventurers could imagine that the lizardman leader's expression was tinged with frustration and impatience.

"Shhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh

With a sharp exhale, the lizardman leader thrust his spear forward.

But Vihera easily dodged the strike, and in the next instant, lightly touched the Lizardman leader's nose with a fist.

Not a punch, but a light touch.

That action, performed countless times before, utterly shattered the Lizardman Leader's spirit.

Clang. A metallic sound echoed throughout the area.

The source of the sound was the spear the lizardman leader had held just moments before, now clattering to the ground.

For the lizardman leader, that spear should have symbolized his strength... but realizing he could never defeat the opponent before him, he lost all will to fight.

But what truly shocked the adventurers and Rei watching this scene was the Lizardman Leader's next action.

He lay flat on his back on the ground, offering his belly toward Vihera.

"...Huh?"

That bewildered exclamation—one might even call it a dumbfounded gasp—escaped from someone's lips.

Though puzzled by it, Rei stared dumbstruck at the Lizardman leader displaying his belly.

Rei knew what it meant.

It was an act taken when acknowledging the other as a complete superior... a pose of submission.

But that was something dogs did. It certainly wasn't something a lizardman should do... no, it wasn't something a lizardman leader, a superior species of lizardman, should do.

(Or rather, why would a lizardman leader even know a dog's submission pose? Could it be... do lizardmen keep dogs as pets?)

Faced with this utterly unexpected sight, Rei found himself half-escaping reality with such thoughts.

And Vihera was just as flustered by the Lizardman Leader assuming the submissive pose.

No, seeing a Lizardman Leader larger than himself—over two meters tall—performing a submission pose right before his eyes, his confusion must be far greater than Rei's or the adventurers'.

(Could he be trying to lure us into a false sense of security?)

Vihera muttered inwardly while maintaining her guard, but the Lizardman Leader, lying completely flat on his back with nothing in his hands, couldn't possibly do anything in this situation.

In other words, the lizardman leader before her had completely surrendered to Vihera.

"...Rei, what do you think we should do?"

Confused by this unprecedented situation, Vihera cast a troubled glance toward Rei.

Rei, receiving that look, hadn't anticipated this situation either.

But at the same time, he couldn't help but feel that this scene was, in a way, somewhat akin to taming.

Originally, how a tamer subdues a monster differs from tamer to tamer.

Thinking that way, the current relationship between Vihera and the Lizardman Leader could also be seen as one form of taming.

"That lizardman leader surrendered to Vihera, so it's up to Vihera, right? If she's willing, she could make him her familiar."

"...A familiar, huh?"

Vihera murmured softly, her gaze shifting toward the Lizardman Leader lying on his back.

She seemed slightly hesitant, but eventually, Vihera gently averted her gaze from the Lizardman Leader.

"Go. I don't want to kill someone who's surrendered. ...But remember this well: if you ever attack humans again, you'll see what happens."

Vihera declared, though naturally, the lizardman leader couldn't understand human speech.

Even so, he somehow grasped that he was being told he didn't have to obey Vihera.

He understood this because his pride had been utterly crushed by Vihera, and he knew he could never defeat the person standing before him.

That was precisely why he vaguely grasped the meaning behind her words.

"Shhh..."

From the submissive posture of showing his belly, he let out a slightly... yes, a slightly regretful cry as the lizardman leader stood up.

He picked up the spear he'd dropped to the ground while assuming the submissive pose, glanced at Vihera, and walked away.

"Aren't you going to say anything?"

Watching the Lizardman Leader, who looked somewhat sad, Rei addressed Vihera.

But Vihera, having lost interest in the lizardman leader, averted her gaze, her attitude making her feelings clear.

Looking strangely lonely, the lizardman leader vanished from before Vihera, spear in hand.

...Years later, adventurers attacked by monsters in this area would be saved by the Lizardman Leader multiple times, eventually leading to the establishment of friendly relations with Gilm's adventurers... but that is a story for the future.

"Um..."

A voice called out to Rei and Vihera, who had watched the Lizardman Leader depart, and to Set, who was eating Lizardman meat a short distance away.

There was no need to guess whose voice it was. It was the adventurers who had been watching Vihera and the lizardman leader fight just moments ago.

"Huh? What is it?"

"You're Rei, right? Um, thank you for helping us."

As the leader-like man said this, the other adventurers bowed their heads in unison.

"No need to worry about it. We took the lizardman extermination request anyway."

Vihera, standing beside Rei, replied.

She seemed slightly displeased—was it because of the lizardman leader, or because being interrupted while with Rei bothered her?

Regardless, the man seemed slightly intimidated by Vihera's words, yet he managed to steady himself and speak up.

"Still, it's true you saved us. I never imagined we'd be attacked by a pack of lizardmen in a place like this."

"...Is that all? Well, either way, you should get out of here as quickly as possible. We're staying behind to recover the lizardmen corpses."

"Yes, understood. ...Then, please be careful."

With that, the men departed.
