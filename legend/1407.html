"Cheers!"

"Hey, sis. These meat skewers are delicious. Ten more, please!"

"Ahahahahahaha, ahah, ahahahahaha!"

"Hey, shut that guy up! He's being way too loud!"

"Cheers over here! Give us a cheer!"

Evening... In the tavern, many people were making noise to relieve the fatigue of work.

Due to the increased number of people for the expansion work, the tavern was considerably busier than usual.

It was getting quite warm... almost early summer, which only made the revelry more intense.

"Did you see that? My stonework is incredible! How many years do you think it took me to master that skill?"

"Hah! Well then, my carving isn't half bad either!"

Just when you thought the craftsmen were boasting about their skills...

"I carried that piece of lumber all by myself!"

"...Well, even if it was cut, lifting that log by yourself is honestly impressive. But I bet there are plenty stronger than you?"

"Ugh!... S-still, my pride lies in this strength!"

Those tasked with transporting lumber and other materials boasted about their strength...

"Hey, hey. Did you see that adventurer? He was so cool, right? I'd love to get closer to him if possible."

"Ah, you're still obsessed with good-looking guys. But you should be careful with people like that—they tend to have bad habits with women."

"Yeah, yeah. Didn't that Ragna guy end up having a girlfriend too?"

"Ugh, w-well..."

Adventurers, artisans, merchants... women gathered in groups, each chatting among themselves.

The tavern was bustling with noise, but in one corner... a few patrons sat out of the way, apart from the crowd.

"How did it go?"

"No good. Security's too tight."

"That's to be expected. City expansions don't happen that often. Plenty of people are thinking... 'for that purpose'."

"But if it's an order from above..."

"You idiot, who would say something like that in a place like this?"

A man tried to say something, but several others nearby hurriedly stopped him.

They absolutely couldn't let their secret be discovered.

That's why they acted...

"Relax. How many of us do you think are gathered here?"

The man who had slipped up said this to the others, trying to appear calm.

In truth, the tavern was currently filled with numerous patrons drinking, eating, and making noise.

There was no way the casual chatter from a corner of the tavern could be heard by those around them.

"Don't be stupid. Don't underestimate him. Gilm wasn't exactly weak in that area to begin with... but for the past few years, his intelligence capabilities have become strangely high."

The main reason Gilm's intelligence abilities became so outstanding was due to the machinations of Rei, which led to the bandits known as the Prairie Wolves falling under Daskar's control.

Naturally, such matters were never made public. Consequently, it became known that Gilm's intelligence capabilities had suddenly and inexplicably improved.

Particularly men like these, who had come to Gilm for some specific purpose, were by no means few in number.

Originally, Gilm was ruled by Daskar, who united the Neutral Factions, so it had already drawn attention from both the Noble Faction and the Royal Faction.

In recent years, an adventurer named Rei emerged seemingly from nowhere, distinguishing himself in the war against the Bestir Empire and quickly earning a notorious reputation.

Furthermore, it has been confirmed that the griffin accompanying Rei is a rare species, a monster equivalent to rank S.

While Rei is ultimately an individual, the mere presence of someone possessing such abilities within Gilm is already a threat to other factions and a situation worthy of attention.

Moreover, the fact that Rei possesses various magic items... particularly an Item Box, is significant.

It can store as much luggage as desired, and time stands still inside, preventing food from spoiling.

Such an item box could be considered a magic item of strategic importance.

With such a figure in Gilm, and one who is cooperative with the Daskar, it's only natural that attention would be drawn.

Moreover, with the current expansion project drawing many people to Gilm, it's easier for those with shady dealings to slip in.

"The expansion hasn't even started in earnest yet, right? So why is security this tight already?"

"Yeah. I've been wondering about that too. Of course, the actual construction is about to start. I get that they want to prevent materials from being stolen... but still, the security is way too tight."

He spat out the words with irritation, then brought the ale in his glass to his mouth.

Honestly, the men couldn't understand why security was so tight over mere construction materials.

"So it's that forest... Treant Forest, was it? Could that forest have something to do with it?"

"That's the only plausible explanation. If so, should we go to that forest and get some wood?"

"But the Knights are guarding it, right?"

"Even so, it's a forest that size. They can't possibly keep a complete watch over every inch of it. So, maybe we could somehow get our hands on it ourselves..."

"Don't be ridiculous. We've had combat training, but that doesn't make us professionals."

"I'm not saying we'd actually fight them head-on, right? Just somehow..."

Having said that, the man pondered how to obtain a tree from Treant Forest, but no good ideas came to mind.

Since they were in a tavern, not drinking would naturally arouse suspicion, so the men did drink a little.

But even a little, over time, added up to a significant amount.

Moreover, their work wasn't progressing well, and their frustration was mounting.

They drank and voiced their frustrations... and before they knew it, every man at the table except one was passed out drunk.

"Hic... Hey? What's up? Passing out from this little bit of booze... hic."

The man stared at his comrades, still drunk.

Seeing them all sprawled face-down on the table, he scowled and raised his ale-filled mug to his lips.

If he hadn't been drunk at that moment, he would have noticed something was off.

Under normal circumstances, drinking this much wouldn't have knocked them out cold.

But... for the man on the verge of passing out, it was already too late.

"Well, what's the matter? You seem to be drinking quite happily, don't you?"

"Huh?"

The man, his drunken mind still trying to figure out what to do with his companion, was suddenly addressed by that voice.

He looked toward the voice and saw a female bartender working at this tavern.

Working in a place like this, she wasn't exactly a stunning beauty... but she was the kind of woman who could still manage a charming smile.

She asked, wearing a smile that was both concerned and meant to ease his mood.

The man responded to her with an exaggerated spread of his arms and opened his mouth.

"Yeah, that's right! Honestly, I never thought I'd be this weak to alcohol... hiccup."

"Oh dear, oh dear. Well then, what shall we do? We do have a room here at the tavern for letting drunk patrons rest. Shall we carry you over there?"

"Ah... ah, ah, yeah, that's it. Yeah, do that for me."

The man must be pretty drunk himself.

The female attendant nodded at his drawn-out words.

"Understood. I'll go get someone, so please wait here."

"Aye-a-yeah, aye-a-yeah!"

The man shouted... and that shout seemed to be the final blow, as he collapsed onto the table just like the others.

"...Sir? Oh dear, this is bad. I'd better go get someone right away."

She gave the man who had collapsed face-first onto the table a gentle shake, glanced at him as he remained oblivious, then headed straight for the kitchen to call for help.

It was a lively tavern bustling with revelers, so it was hardly unusual to find people passed out drunk.

Given that, it wasn't all that surprising that everyone at one table had passed out drunk.

The woman headed to the kitchen and addressed the proprietor.

"A customer passed out. Could we borrow a room to sober him up?"

"Ah, it's ready. The Prairie Wolves should have it prepared too, so take them there immediately."

"Yes."

The shopkeeper replied while stir-frying meat.

The woman nodded at the shopkeeper's words and promptly left the kitchen to make preparations.

Then, as the shopkeeper transferred the finished dish onto a plate...

"You went to a lot of trouble."

A man nearby, preparing salad, remarked.

"Not at all. Thanks to everyone, the security around here isn't a problem anymore."

Not long ago... just a few years back, really, adventurers gone rogue had been running rampant around here.

They'd eat and drink without paying, and worse, they'd demand money, claiming they were protecting the place and that the shopkeepers owed them protection fees.

It wasn't just once or twice that shopkeepers were beaten for no reason.

The female clerk who came to report earlier had also suffered terrible treatment as a woman many times over.

If you tried to tell the guards, they'd find some way to escape immediately.

Once the guards were gone, they'd return and become even more violent.

The one who resolved such situations was the person making salads beside the owner... or more precisely, the group that person belonged to: the Prairie Wolves.

He was making salads, but this man wasn't actually employed at this tavern.

To be precise, in this case... it took a while for him to get drunk enough to help restrain the scheming individuals who had infiltrated Gilm, and he ended up helping out out of boredom.

They did use drugs to make the drunkenness accelerate, but even so, it took longer than expected for him to pass out.

"I see. If you get any more information, let me know. I'll handle it immediately. Of course, if anyone's causing trouble too. Especially now, with all sorts of people coming into Gilm... How about it?"

While answering the shopkeeper, the man showed him the finished salad.

"Thank you. I'm counting on you. ...But seriously, this is amazing. What do you say? Want to work for us?"

"Don't be ridiculous. I'll keep an eye on this area properly, no flattery needed."

"No, it's not flattery or anything like that."

The shopkeeper declared this with a serious expression, his eyes fixed on the plate holding the salad.

Plating a salad isn't that difficult.

It's the kind of job anyone can do.

...And precisely because it was a job anyone could do, the man known as a Prairie Wolf was helping out.

But precisely because it was a job anyone could do, it became clear that the man's salad plating was far superior to that of the average person.

He didn't just haphazardly mix vegetables and dump them on a plate; he arranged them carefully, considering how they would look.

Of course, this tavern wasn't some fancy restaurant; it was a place where people who weren't particularly well-off gathered.

But... precisely because of that, the beauty of that salad arrangement felt all the more striking.

The man must have realized the owner was serious.

After a moment, he opened his mouth, not entirely displeased.

"Yeah... if I ever have to quit this job... and you'd still hire me then, I'd gladly take it. ...Well, I guess I'd better go fulfill my duty first."

He was a man with plenty of shady pasts, yet he had no complaints about his current situation... this life of obeying Egg's orders and protecting Gilm.

Not that he was completely, utterly without complaints, but he still liked his current situation.

That's precisely why, after helping with the kitchen cleanup, the tavern attendant headed toward the room where the drunken men had been carried earlier.

The owner watched him go with a regretful look, then immediately returned to preparing new dishes.

It was extremely busy now—arguably the busiest time of the entire day.

Given that, he simply had to keep cooking.

No matter how many people came to Gilm, if the food was bad, the drinks were bad, or the service was poor... a tavern like that would inevitably have lower sales compared to others.

To prevent that, the owner kept cooking relentlessly.

...all the while quietly hoping the men he'd just gotten drunk would spill their information as soon as possible.
