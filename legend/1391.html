Set soaring directly above the forest.

Rei rode on its back, while Vihera clung to Set's front legs.

Even in this situation, Set seemed completely unfazed, flying through the sky as if already accustomed to it.

Normally, having Vihera clinging to one leg would likely throw off his balance mid-air... but for Set, this was probably nothing out of the ordinary.

"Grrrrrrrrr"

In fact, he purred contentedly as he flapped his wings through the night sky.

Part of his good mood was escaping Treant Forest.

In Treant Forest, monsters vastly weaker than him had relentlessly attacked without pause.

To make matters worse, every monster that attacked was a plant-type monster.

If they had at least been monsters with flesh, like orcs, Set might have felt a bit more motivated.

But the ones attacking were all plant-type monsters.

To be precise, aside from the Treant, there were quite a few monsters he could eat, starting with the Venus flytrap monster. Unfortunately, Set preferred meat over vegetables.

Of course, he'd happily eat vegetables if they were cooked and served, and if he was absolutely starving, that would be a different story.

Anyway, these assorted monsters were troublesome, and for some reason, using the King's Intimidation didn't work on them.

He wondered about this, but for now, he was flying through the sky.

Then again, Set possessed flight speed sufficient to reach Treant Forest from Gilm in no time.

Taking off from within Treant Forest and heading toward its center took what could almost be called an instant.

...If there were no obstacles, that is.

"Dammit, what a nuisance!"

The swung Death Scythe sliced away the seeds flying toward Rei and the others in the sky.

With a return slash, he also cut away the seeds flying from the opposite direction. Rei's gaze, filled with irritation, turned toward the source of the seeds... toward the giant flower growing deep within the forest.

These giant flowers had once been firing seeds toward the location adventurers had used as a base. However, after the first one was killed—or rendered useless—by Vihera, multiple new giant flowers had sprouted.

However, the number of seeds each giant flower fired at the adventurers was fewer than when there was only one... Yet, the moment Set took flight into the sky, a massive wave of seeds was unleashed as if to prove that claim false.

Set dodged the incoming seeds while intercepting them with his skills.

Rei intercepted them with Death Scythe, while Vihera intercepted them with the blades sprouting from her greaves.

"Damn it. At this rate, we won't even be able to search the center of the forest. And there might not even be anything there to begin with... Set, Vihera, I'm leaving the interception to you for a few seconds."

"GRRRRRR!"

"Leave it to us. ...Though I haven't been able to intercept much myself."

At Rei's request, Set growled in agreement.

In response, Vihera replied with a hint of disappointment.

After all, Vihera's specialty was close-quarters combat using hand-to-hand fighting.

Compared to Rei and Set, who possessed long-range attack methods like magic and skills, he inevitably fell short in that area.

Of course, it wasn't that he had no means of ranged attack at all.

He could hurl pebbles scattered around him, for instance. But... that wasn't something he could do while clinging to Set's front leg.

If the seed got really close, he could intercept it with the hand not holding onto Set's leg, or even kick it away... but there was no way Rei or Set would let a seed get that close so easily.

"I'm counting on you when push comes to shove. ...First things first, though."

Rei replied to Vihera, shifting his gaze to the giant flower that had been firing the most seeds at them since earlier.

He temporarily returned the Death Scythe in his right hand to the Misty Ring and switched the Twilight Spear from his left hand to his right.

He focused his magic, took aim... and hurled it.

Normally, this strike would be unleashed using the power and twist of his entire body. Unfortunately, Rei was currently riding on Set's back.

This meant he couldn't channel the power and torque from his lower body into this throw.

As a result, the blow is weaker than his usual strike, but the extra magical power infused into it compensates for that.

"Die!"

With that shout, he hurls the Twilight Spear, infused with magic, relying solely on the twist of his upper body and the strength of his arms.

Sensing the Twilight Spear slicing through the air and hurtling straight toward him, he redirected the seeds he'd been firing at Set toward the Twilight Spear instead, sending the thorns sprouting from the stems flying as well.

But... while a disposable spear like the ones Rei used before might have been manageable, this time it was the Twilight Spear.

There was no way something like that could handle a Twilight Spear infused with such a massive amount of Rei's magic.

The seeds and thorns it touched shattered instantly... and soon the Twilight Spear struck the enormous flower, piercing it cleanly in the next instant.

The petal count on the flower plummeted by more than half, likely due to the sheer magnitude of the magic infused within.

Even so, the Twilight Spear continued flying through the air unchecked, but in the next instant, it was back in Rei's hand.

"Did I put a little too much magic into it?"

Rei had hoped to preserve at least part of the flower to submit to the guild after tonight's business was done. But with more than half of the flower—arguably the giant bloom's most defining feature—vanished, submitting it would be pointless.

"Then, I have one I defeated. Why not take that one?"

Hearing Vihera's words from where she hung from Set's leg, Rei nodded in understanding.

The giant flower, its stem severed by the Immersion Palm, had fallen to the ground with its blossom intact.

Though Rei hadn't heard the details and didn't know, if Vihera said so, he figured it was probably fine.

"Alright. Then I'll take care of this flower. If Vihera's flower counts, even if half of these guys disappear, as long as we have enough numbers, we should be able to satisfy the guild to some extent."

At the very least, this giant flower was definitely an unknown monster, one Rei had never seen before or read about in books. So, even if the guild had some dissatisfaction that it wasn't in perfect condition, they should still be pleased.

(Though, come to think of it, everything except that Venus flytrap, the vines, the thorns, and all the other stuff was probably unknown monsters too.)

Of course, it was entirely possible that it was actually a known monster, just one Rei hadn't encountered before.

(Unknown monsters... Come to think of it, that pollen hasn't come since then. Well, me, Set, Vihera... Logically speaking, I doubt that kind of pollen would have any effect. Looks like not bringing Byune was the right call after all.)

The other members of the Crimson Wings besides Byune are all special beings in their own ways—dark elves, hybrids with Ambris, and so on.

Because of that, Rei had a certain conviction that even if they got hit by that pollen, they'd probably be fine.

"Well, I don't know what kind of being is releasing that pollen... but anyway, that flower is in the way, so I'll just get rid of it... right!"

Once more, magic surged through Rei's hands, and the Twilight Spear shot forth.

What followed was an exact repeat of the earlier scene.

Even though he was holding back somewhat, the giant flower had no chance against the Twilight Spear's strike. It was pierced repeatedly, causing most of the flower to vanish.

Having been told that Vihera had already defeated the flower portion without damaging it, Rei attacked the flower without hesitation.

To begin with, being a giant flower, its blossom was in a very easy-to-target position.

Attacking from the ground would be difficult due to the flower's height, but for Rei riding the flying Set, it was practically sitting ducks.

Before they knew it, the seeds flying toward Rei and the others had completely vanished.

"This should put the others at ease now,"

Considering how several had roots growing inside them from these seeds, Rei murmured that it was good they'd managed to defeat this flower themselves.

Whether those who had been completely transformed into trees by these seeds could ever return to normal... that remained unknown. Still, it was certain that no more victims would be added to the toll.

(If it were possible to reverse it, would it require healing magic or an alchemist or something like that? ...Well, if we had to rely on such people, it would undoubtedly cost a pretty penny.)

Thinking such thoughts, Rei looked down at the ground.

They weren't flying through the sky on Set in the first place to defeat the giant flower.

They were searching for something believed to be at the heart of Treant Forest.

Or perhaps not the center, but the edge of the forest on the opposite side from Gilm.

Still, Rei suspected the forest's center was the likely spot.

There wasn't any clear reason for it, just a hunch... or maybe it was because in the manga and novels he'd read back in Japan, that was the standard trope.

"Well? Anything?"

"Grrr..."

"Not sure about that."

As Rei asked while scanning the ground, Set growled and shook his head, while Vihera reported finding nothing.

Naturally, even to Rei's eyes, there wasn't anything particularly noticeable.

...If anything stood out, it was the giant flower stem Rei had defeated.

Half of the flower portion had vanished, and it was already in a state where it couldn't move as a monster anymore.

Even so, illuminated by the moonlight, its sheer size made it impossible to ignore.

"What should we do? I think this area is the center, but should we expand the search a bit more?"

"Yeah... We're running out of time, so maybe we should?"

"Grrrr..."

Even as Rei and the others flew through the sky searching, adventurers were battling monsters before Treant Forest.

The longer it took, the greater the risk that the adventurers fighting would lose their lives.

As adventurers, accepting quests is their own responsibility, and Rei didn't really need to worry about it that much... Still, the thought that his own delay was increasing the casualties among adventurers was an unpleasant one.

Of course, if the adventurers actually fighting heard Rei's thoughts directly, they'd probably tell him not to underestimate them.

"Alright, let's search a wider area. Maybe it's somewhere slightly away from the center of the forest... or something like that. It's possible."

"Do you think so? Well, even so, I suppose it's better to search somewhere where there might be something, rather than staying here where we know nothing."

Vihera felt a slight doubt at Rei's words, but she agreed that doing nothing would lead to finding nothing. It was better to take some action.

"Grrrr..."

And for Set, following Rei's lead was only natural unless there was a specific reason not to.

"Alright, let's expand our search area a bit more. ...In that case, I'm glad we took care of all those giant flowers at once."

"Yeah. ...Though it was a battle devoid of any sense of atmosphere."

For Vihera, who enjoyed fighting—no, who had a penchant for it—the truth was she would have liked to savor the battle against those fairly powerful giant flowers a bit longer.

...Still, since she had fought them thoroughly herself, she didn't harbor any major complaints.

"If we could take them down easily, wouldn't that be better? If it were any other time, fine, but right now we needed to deal with Treant Forest as quickly as possible."

"Yeah. ...Well, I'll let you off the hook this time."

Vihera looked slightly displeased, but she accepted Rei's words nonetheless.

While Vihera had a penchant for enjoying combat, she wasn't so ruthless as to care about others' lives just for her own amusement.

"Then... Huh?"

Rei tried to discuss which direction to head with Set and Vihera, but to see Vihera hanging from Set's leg, he naturally had to lean forward and look down.

Well, to be precise, since Vihera was hanging from Set's front leg, even leaning forward meant she could barely see Vihera... but she could see a little.

And what Rei saw as he leaned forward to consult them was... something that looked like a small child sitting on a tree branch... no, it was something else entirely.
