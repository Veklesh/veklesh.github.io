Since Grange had calmed down, Rei decided to head to a base of operations to make contact for the time being… but he realized there might be a slight problem with going there on their own.

If they were to storm in right then and there, there wouldn’t be any problem at all.

However, in this case, the primary objective was to capture whoever was behind the Kobold incident and resolve the current situation.

Given that, rather than charging in directly, they needed to keep watch for a while.

That said, there was no way Rei could take on the task of keeping watch himself.

Rei has his own tasks to handle, including dismantling the Giganto Turtle.

“So, to increase the number of people we can deploy here, I’m heading to the guard post.”

“...What about my safety? Being captured by the guards like this is something I’d prefer to avoid... no, I’d like to avoid it at all costs.”

Although Grange said this, he wasn’t trying to escape from Rei anymore.

Now that Set, who had been waiting nearby, had joined them, she likely understood that if she tried to run away in this situation, she would definitely be caught.

“Don’t worry. It’s been decided that you’ll be handed over to Egg… Gilm’s Intelligence Unit, so I won’t go so far as to hand you over to the guards. That said, if you start acting up from this point on, that’s a different story.”

Grange nodded at Rei’s words, replying, “Of course.”

He probably said that precisely because he knew his current situation was a real tightrope walk.

“Then there’s no need to worry. I’ll cover it up somehow. …Well, they’re professionals in a sense. They’ll probably think something’s fishy, but if I say I’ll take responsibility, they shouldn’t press the issue too far.”

The guards are the ones who maintain order in Gilm.

And this town of Gilm isn’t just any ordinary town; it’s located on the frontier and attracts large numbers of adventurers.

Given that they’re responsible for maintaining order in such a place, it’s only natural that they’d have a keen eye for spotting anyone acting suspiciously.

Given that, it’s only natural that they might find Grange, whom Rei is bringing along, suspicious.

……However, if Rei gets involved, that changes everything.

If Rei takes responsibility, there’s a good chance they’ll let it slide.

(If anyone still isn’t convinced, I’ll just say he’s someone I’m entrusting to Egg. …It’s not the case yet, but that’s actually the plan.)

With that in mind, I lead Grange—who seems rather reluctant—toward the guard post.

Perhaps because word of the earthen wall incident has spread among the adventurers, the atmosphere in town seems somewhat brighter compared to just yesterday.

Even though they knew Gilm had the firepower to handle something like a Kobold, there was still a lingering sense of unease.

But thanks to the earthen wall, that anxiety has vanished.

……To be precise, even with the earthen wall, there are Kobolds that could find a way to get over it, so we can’t be completely at ease.

Furthermore, since the barrier that usually protects Gilm isn’t currently active, they can’t claim to be fully prepared against flying monsters.

Still, when faced with the choice between the Kobolds that attack reliably almost every day and the aerial monsters whose arrival is unpredictable, the former is undoubtedly the more immediate threat.

With these thoughts in mind, I made my way through the town… and arrived in front of the guard post I was looking for.

Even without Rei saying a word, Set—who wasn’t allowed inside the guardhouse—was lying down in an open spot… but since that was par for the course, Rei didn’t seem particularly bothered and knocked on the guardhouse door.

“...I never expected you to come to the guardhouse for something like this.”

Saying that, Grange took a deep breath as if to calm himself.

Then the door opened...

“Huh? Hmm? Oh, Rei. And you over there… I haven’t seen you before. Is something wrong?”

One of the guards who came out of the door said this upon seeing Rei’s face.

From the guard’s perspective, he and Rei had been through a lot together, so it was fair to say they were familiar with each other.

Since the guard who emerged from the guardhouse was someone Rei knew, he spoke without seeming particularly nervous.

“It’s not so much that something happened, but more that I’m about to cause something…”

“Hey, hey, whenever you get involved, things always end up getting serious, so I’d really prefer it if you’d stay out of it.”

“Oh, really? …Like, say, if it’s about catching those guys who are somehow summoning Kobolds to Gilm?”

The moment Rei said that, the guard’s gaze sharpened.

It was no longer the relaxed demeanor he’d shown just seconds ago while chatting with Rei, but the face of a guard entrusted with Gilm’s peace.

“Is that really true? Just so you know, this isn’t something you can brush off as a joke.”

“Yeah, that’s not a problem. We only have circumstantial evidence so far, but I’d say it’s almost certain.”

“...Come inside. This isn’t the kind of conversation we can have out here.”

From his conversation with Rei, he must have concluded that this was no joke. Saying that, the guard motioned for Rei and Grange to enter the guardhouse.

Rei entered the guardhouse without seeming particularly bothered, but Grange—perhaps because of his shady past—wore a slightly displeased expression. Still, knowing he couldn’t escape Rei, he obediently followed them inside.

They were led to what could be called a living room—or rather, a waiting area.

There, five guards were on standby—excluding the one who had come out earlier—and currently, these six people were the personnel ready to spring into action at a moment’s notice.

“Hm? Rei… and who’s that over there?”

One of the guards in the waiting area said this, looking at Rei and Grange.

His tone was light, as if to suggest Rei had gotten into trouble again, but the guard who had been speaking with Rei moments ago spoke up with a serious expression.

“According to Rei, it’s about the Kobold incident that’s been causing trouble for a while now. Specifically, the culprit behind it.”

The moment he said that, even the guard who had been listening casually took on a serious expression.

Naturally, this wasn’t just the guard who had been speaking; the other guards reacted the same way.

“Is that true?”

Rei nodded in response to the guard’s question, which sounded like he was seeking confirmation.

“Yeah. Though, the information I’ve brought is just a lead. I don’t have any proof that it actually leads to the person behind the Kobold incident…”

“Tell me more.”

Prompted, Rei began by talking about the earthen wall.

Naturally, the guards had already received information about the earthen wall from Gilm, so they didn’t ask any specific questions about it.

Well, they did seem eager to ask about it, but they must have decided that hearing about the Kobold incident took priority for now.

Next, he explained about the Red Cloth members who came to destroy the earthen wall, and how their leader had intended to have the horde of Kobolds that emerged after the wall was breached finish off the Red Cloth members.

Upon hearing this explanation, the guards’ gaze toward Grange grew sharp and stern.

Since he was clearly a criminal, this was only natural for the guards.

“Wait a moment. This guy is supposed to be handed over to Daskar’s intelligence organization later. …Right?”

In response to Rei’s question, which was meant to confirm this, Grange nodded, maintaining the same unchanging smile even as the guards fixed him with sharp stares.

“Yes. I thought that was the best course of action. …And, just to be clear, I don’t recall having committed any crime in Gilm yet, do I?”

At the mention of “Gilm,” the guard’s gaze grew even sharper.

In other words, it was tantamount to confessing that he had committed some crime in other villages, towns, or cities.

But before one of the guards could say anything, Rei spoke up again.

“As I said earlier, this guy is supposed to be handed over to Egg… to Lord Daskar. Anyway, thanks to Grange, we were able to get important information about the Kobold incident. Don’t you think you should just be grateful for that?”

Even without the specific term “plea bargain,” such arrangements are common practice.

Given that, and since Grange hadn’t committed any crimes since arriving in Gilm, Rei suggested they let it slide.

At Rei’s words, several of the guards in the room tried to say something, but the guard who had brought Rei here stopped them… and addressed Rei.

“For now, we understand. But we’ll need to make sure to include all of that in the report. I don’t know how they’ll judge it after reading it… but is that okay with you?”

“Yeah, that’s fine. I’ll talk to Egg about it. That way, the word should naturally get back to you guys too.”

In fact, the intelligence organization Egg leads is always short-handed.

If he finds out there’s someone who can be an immediate asset, he’ll definitely pass the word to his superior… in this case, Daskar.

(Well, he said he didn’t do anything in Gilm, so there probably won’t be a problem… I hope. That said, if he’s committed any major crimes outside of Gilm, that’s a different story. Egg will judge that properly, though. He probably has a better eye for people than I do.)

Rei, thinking this, exchanged glances with the guard… and eventually, the guard let out a small sigh.

He must have decided to trust what Rei was saying, at least for now.

“Still, I never would’ve thought Akabu was involved in this incident. …I hadn’t seen him around for a while, so I just assumed he’d left Gilm.”

The others nodded in agreement as the guard muttered thoughtfully.

“From what I heard from the Akabu I made contact with, it seems she was being harbored by someone who harbors ill will toward Gilm. …Come to think of it, what happened to that woman who seemed connected to the Akabu we captured before the snow fell? …Wouldn’t it make sense for her to have some information on that?”

“Ah… I heard that woman was taken to the lord’s mansion and then sent to the capital. Apparently, she’d been causing trouble in other places too, so that’s probably why.”

The guard said this, but his expression suggested he didn’t fully believe the story.

They had likely experienced this before—people they’d captured disappearing due to some higher-up’s decision.

Daskar didn’t find such behavior amusing either, but ultimately, there was little he could do given his title of Marquess and his position as the leader of the Neutral Faction.

“I see. Since he’s connected to the Red Cloth, I figured there was a good chance he knew something.”

Saying that, Rei began exchanging the information he knew with the guards once again.

The exchange of information concluded after about thirty minutes, and by then, the guards who hadn’t been in the room earlier had gathered and were listening to Rei.

“...So, regarding that base Grange mentioned—it’s probably just a disposable outpost or something like that—does anyone here know anything about it?”

Rei asked this, but to his surprise, more than half of the guards in the room knew about it.

Well, since they’re Gilm guards who patrol the streets every day, it might be only natural that they knew.

“In that case… what should we do? If the outpost is disposable, I don’t think there’s much point in storming it by force. Personally, I think it’d be better to keep watch on that outpost and tail anyone connected to it.”

Rei had come to the guard station in the first place largely to ask for their help with exactly that.

And since the guards seemed to agree with Rei’s suggestion—they all nodded—the dog-like beastman with the highest rank among them spoke up.

“Alright. Then let’s go with Rei’s suggestion. It seems like that’s the best way to get a lead.”

“Ah, but since we’re at it, how about hiring a thief or someone to break in?”

One of the guards suggested this, but the dog-like beastman shook his head.

“If we were dealing with a regular criminal organization, that might be an option. But these people—whatever methods they use—are capable of controlling monsters… Kobolds… in such large numbers. Ordinary thieves would likely get caught. And if they’re caught, it would tip off the enemy that we’re onto them.”

Unable to refute that, the guard who had suggested sending the bandits fell silent.

It was likely because the dog-like beastman’s words carried such strong conviction.

“Alright, does anyone else have any objections? If not, let’s start assigning duties right away.”

Rei watched the guards as they discussed the matter, saying nothing further.
