An adventurer known by a nickname, who is visiting as a guest of the Duke of Kerebel, engages in a mock battle with adventurers from Anesis and the retainers of the nobility.

Word of this event spread from the adventurers who had been commissioned to the local residents, and many people expressed interest in the mock battle.

Naturally, some people began to think of turning this event into a grand spectacle... and requests from such individuals—primarily merchants—reached Duke Liberte of Kerebel.

“That’s why we’ve received requests to hold the mock battle on a grand scale.”

“What do you think?” Liberte’s gaze turned toward Rei.

Normally, discussions like this would take place over a meal, but today, Rei had been summoned to Liberte’s office to hear about it.

“Well, since I’m being paid to participate in a mock battle, I have no complaints if Duke Kerebel wants to do it… but is there even a place for that?”

If there were an arena of some sort, holding a mock battle wouldn’t be a problem.

But there are no arenas of that sort in Anesis.

So, perhaps it was the place where Rei had recently been training with Philma and the other knights of the Kerebel Duke’s Order?

Rei had thought so too, but then reconsidered: while that place was spacious enough for training, it was far too small to accommodate the residents of Anesis, the second-largest city in the Kingdom of Mireana.

As Rei was pondering what to do, Liberte spoke up, saying it wouldn’t be a problem.

“If you don’t mind becoming a spectacle, Rei, I’ll set up a mock battle arena outside Anesis. Though, since we don’t have that much time, we’ll just level the ground and build some simple spectator stands.”

“Is that okay? Doing something like that outside Anesis?”

“Of course. We’ll have security soldiers on standby just in case. Besides, this isn’t Gilm on the frontier. Monsters do appear, but not that often, and bandits and the like wouldn’t go out of their way to come near us.”

Hearing that, Rei was convinced.

If this were Gilm, it wouldn’t be surprising if a large number of monsters attacked, let alone bandits.

Besides, it’s winter now, and it’s already snowing.

At this time of year, the monsters that roam from spring to fall become sluggish, but those that only appear in winter tend to be quite active.

Rei had worried that such monsters were often powerful, and since the ordinary soldiers in Anesis weren’t used to dealing with them, it would be difficult to handle an attack—but it seems his concerns were unfounded.

(Though, I don’t think we’d get attacked by monsters if we brought Set along to the mock battle.)

Not all of them, but many monsters judge that they cannot defeat Set on their own, and once they realize he is there, they try to avoid contact with him.

“I see. That’s fine with me. …Besides, since it’s going to be a big celebration, there’ll naturally be all sorts of food stalls, right?”

Since they were going anyway, Rei’s honest hope was that if there were any stalls selling delicious food, he could buy a variety of things and store them in his Misty Ring.

“Alright, it’s settled then.”

“Yes. Well, I’ll be on my way then.”

“Ah…”

Rei was about to leave the room, but Liberte seemed to be trying to say something to him.

Rei must have noticed Liberte’s hesitation. He opened his mouth, looking puzzled.

“What’s wrong?”

“...No. Well, um. Are you getting along well with Elena?”

Rei hesitated slightly at Liberte’s question, which masked his complex inner feelings.

If asked whether they were getting along, he could answer that they were.

But the truth was that lately, Elena had been busy sorting through the paperwork that had piled up while he was in Gilm, so they hadn’t really had much time together.

Even so, when asked whether he and Elena were on good terms or not, he decided they were definitely on good terms and nodded in response.

“That’s right. Elena’s been pretty busy with various things these past few days, so we haven’t been able to spend as much time together as we’d like… but I don’t think we’re on bad terms.”

“I see.”

Nodding at Rei’s words, Liberte replied, though he felt a touch of disappointment that his response wasn’t quite what he’d expected.

That said, even if Rei had said something like, “Actually, we’re quite close, and we woke up in the same bed this morning…” Liberte would have been at a loss for how to react.

Unaware of Liberte’s subtle parental concern, Rei exchanged just those few words before leaving the study and returning to his own room.

Miranda, the maid assigned to Rei, was waiting in front of the study, so there was no need to worry about getting lost even in the vast Kerebel Duke’s mansion.

Miranda, who was guiding Rei, was a little curious about what Rei had been talking about with Liberte, but she led him on his way, regretting that, as a maid, she couldn’t possibly ask such a thing.

“Come to think of it…”

“Yes, what is it?”

Miranda replied to Rei’s sudden remark as they walked down the hallway.

“I think I mentioned before that I’m going to be participating in a mock battle soon…”

“I’d heard various rumors about that, but has it been officially decided?”

Even as she said this, Miranda already knew that the mock battle itself was going to take place.

After all, she had heard the story about Liberte requesting the mock battle long ago.

Given that, she had concluded that the mock battle wouldn’t be canceled unless something extraordinary happened—like a horde of monsters attacking or a violent blizzard striking.

“Yeah. Apparently, word has spread more than I expected, and it seems a lot of people want to use this opportunity to throw a big party. As a result, we’ve decided to set up a simple stage outside Anesis and hold the mock battle there. Honestly, I don’t understand why they want to throw such a party at this time of year, when the year is almost over.”

Once the New Year arrives, there will be festivities then anyway.

In that case, they could just wait a little longer instead of going out of their way to have a big celebration here.

While I thought that, Rei could also understand that a festive celebration is always a good thing, no matter how many times you experience it—the more, the merrier.

(Though, if you asked me whether people would actually gather just to watch a mock battle in the snow... well, maybe they would.)

People enjoy watching battles.

That was evident from the existence of arenas in this world, and back when Rei was in Japan, martial arts tournaments were held quite frequently as well.

That said, where Rei lived, there were hardly any shows of that sort on TV.

Still, come year-end, those kinds of programs would sometimes air even in Rei’s region, and it wasn’t uncommon for professional wrestling organizations to come and put on shows.

Given that, there’s no doubt that people like to watch fights.

If the fight were a matter of life and death, those who didn’t like such things probably wouldn’t watch it, but what was taking place this time was, after all, just a mock battle.

Since people can casually and safely watch battles between adventurers with nicknames, many spectators are sure to show up.

“With this kind of festive commotion, there’s usually betting involved… I wonder who’s running the book?”

“Betting?”

“Yes. No matter how strong Rei is, his opponents will outnumber him, won’t they? There’s a good chance some of them are well-known fighters, and above all, numbers are power. I think there’s a good chance it could work as a bet. Besides, even if people have heard rumors about Rei, not many have actually seen his true strength with their own eyes.”

“I see.”

Rei smiled, finding that an interesting point.

Rei wasn’t exactly short on money, but if there were people betting on his mock battles to make a profit, that didn’t sit well with him.

So, he’d bet on himself, reduce the bookie’s profits a little, and make some money himself.

That’s what he thought… and his gaze turned toward Miranda.

Perhaps sensing something in Rei’s gaze as he walked beside her, Miranda instinctively took a few steps back from him.

“W-what is it? I just got a slightly bad feeling…”

“No, I just have a favor to ask of you, Miranda. If it’s such a big event, you’re obviously going to participate, right? Well, I’ll give you my money, so bet it on me. I’ll give you thirty percent of the profits as a commission. And, if you want, you can bet on me too.”

“...You don’t think for a second that you’ll lose, do you?”

Perhaps she directed that slightly exasperated gaze at him because she understood just how formidable Rei was.

In response to Miranda’s gaze, Rei nodded without a second thought, as if it were only natural.

“I don’t know if any adventurers with nicknames will show up, but even if I face those kinds of people, I have no intention of losing. …Well, it’d be a different story if people like Elena or Philma barged in, but surely that won’t happen, right?”

Elena and Philma were among the most powerful combatants in the Duke of Kerebel’s faction.

Rei’s prediction was that they wouldn’t just casually send such figures into a mock battle.

(If it were a mock battle where no one was watching, or where only family members were present, it wouldn’t be a problem… but if it’s a mock battle with a crowd large enough to turn into a festival, losing would surely tarnish the name of the Duke of Kerebel’s family. Especially since this mock battle is being held at the request of the nobles.)

Under those circumstances, if the commander of the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights or Elena—who is considered the symbol of the Noble Faction—were to lose, the nobles would undoubtedly cause a commotion.

Given that Liberte understood this, Rei couldn’t imagine for the life of him that she would send out Philma or Elena.

Miranda thought about Rei’s explanation for a moment, then nodded as if she understood.

“That’s true. Even my husband wouldn’t pull a stunt like that in front of a crowd, though he might in private. If that’s the case, things might turn out exactly as you say, Rei.”

“Right? In that case, betting on me should yield a decent profit. …Though, if Kerebel is acting as the bookie, I won’t go that far.”

As a reward for participating in this mock battle, Rei was to receive Fuma Ore.

Given that, if Duke Kerebel were to act as the bookie, Rei had no intention of betting any more than that.

But, as if to reassure Rei, Miranda nodded and said it would be fine.

“That won’t be a problem. Usually, the bookies in these situations are people from that line of work.”

“...‘Those in the know,’ huh?”

In this context, “those in the know” undoubtedly refers to people from the underworld.

That said, Rei wasn’t the least bit surprised by this.

Perhaps it was because he had seen on the news back in Japan that the yakuza were the ones pulling the strings behind the scenes whenever bets like this took place.

“Then there’s no need to worry. …Though, if those kinds of people are involved, it goes without saying that a portion of the proceeds will be collected as taxes for Kerebel Duke.”

“I suppose so, but if you think about it that far, you’ll end up unable to do anything at all.”

“I won’t deny that. …Anyway, I’m planning to give Miranda the amount I want to bet later—how much do you think is appropriate? Maybe a little over one gold coin?”

“What!?”

Miranda let out a gasp of shock, clearly not expecting the words “gold coin” to come out of Rei’s mouth so suddenly.

“I-I think that’s a bit too much… I mean, even if we bet that much, there’s a chance they might not accept it…”

“Is that so?”

“Yes.”

Miranda nodded immediately at Rei’s words, without even pausing to think.

Seeing Miranda’s reaction, Ray took out a platinum coin instead of a gold coin, thinking, “If that’s how you feel.”

Miranda was still a little nervous, but five platinum coins wasn’t exactly a huge sum for Rei.

As a result, Miranda accepted the five platinum coins Rei offered her.

“These platinum coins might lower the odds a bit… is that okay with you?”

“I don’t mind. From my perspective, as long as I make a little profit, that’s all that matters.”

“...I understand. But please, Rei, keep this money for now. I’ll hold onto it so I don’t accidentally lose it.”

Rei couldn’t help but think she was a bit of a worrier, but he took a liking to Miranda’s words and stowed the platinum coins inside the Misty Ring.
