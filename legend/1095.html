Daldora, who had run away from Rei and Augusto as if fleeing, walked through the streets of Gosh with a nervous look on his face, his impatience evident.

No, describing his pace as running would be more accurate.

As the head of the renowned Erema Trading Company, one of Gosh's largest merchants, Dalidra was well-known in both good and bad ways.

That very fact made his unusual demeanor stand out like a sore thumb, though he himself seemed completely unaware.

Right now, only Rei occupied Dalidra 's mind.

(The Kingdom of Mireana, Gilm, Rei, the Griffin... No mistake. That man is the one who achieved great feats in the war between the Kingdom of Mireana and the Bestir Empire, the man known by the epithet Crimson. But why in a desert like Gosh? He said he just happened to stop by...?)

The image of Rei from earlier flashed through Dalidra 's mind, but he couldn't bring himself to trust it.

It was far too convenient.

He'd made plans to take down Augusto, even using a sand ship, only for this famed adventurer to show up and interfere.

...While Rei's name had spread due to events like the Bestir Empire's tournament and civil war, unfortunately, being in a minor nation... and not even its capital city, meant he hadn't been able to obtain that level of information.

(Anyway, Rei is dangerous. What's troubling is that he's cooperating with August. It would be ideal if we could recruit him... but that's unlikely.)

Though incomplete, Dalidra possessed information on Rei.

Considering that information, it was absolutely inconceivable that he would do something like lend a hand to someone like me now.

(Besides, losing the Sand Ship to him was a huge blow. Honestly, if only the Sand Pirates had acted before Rei joined them and taken down Augustus. Damn it.)

Losing the Sand Ship, a major asset for both the Elema Trading Company and myself, was a huge blow.

If Augusto had it, there were options like negotiating for it, or worst case, forcibly taking it back. But I couldn't imagine pulling such a stunt against Rei.

(Wait, come to think of it, Rei was supposed to have a hobby of collecting magic items. In that case, perhaps we could just consider the Sand Ship a necessary expense. It's harsh, but if we could lure him over with a magic item of the same rank as the Sand Ship...)

Even considering that, remembering the look Rei gave me, I couldn't possibly imagine him defecting to my side.

In fact, I could only envision a future of ruin.

(What to do... That matter is already in motion. Even if I tried to stop it now, since we've used a significant portion of the Elema Trading Company's assets, pulling out now would leave us with nothing but debt... If that's the case...)

Dalidra walked with a frown etched between her brows, looking visibly displeased to anyone who saw her.

Those in his path instinctively cleared the way, knowing better than to approach him—who knew what trouble they'd invite?

Raising his gaze to the sky as if tormented by the desert's unique heat, Dalidra suddenly recalled what Rei had said.

That he had come to this Gosh by chance.

It had been bad luck to be present during the sand pirate attack, but if Rei didn't intend to settle permanently in this land, there should still be opportunities ahead.

While Rei's words couldn't be fully trusted, for now, all they could do was hope they were true.

(Pause movement temporarily, then resume once Rei is gone. ...That's the only option, isn't it?)

As her thoughts coalesced, Dalidra finally noticed her guards and entourage were nowhere to be seen.

"Hmm, I suppose I'll have to make some adjustments."

He muttered, hearing footsteps approaching from behind him, and turned to look that way.

After Dalidra fled the scene, the gazes of those present naturally turned to Rei.

What exactly did you do? Asked outright by their stares, Rei thought for a moment before deciding there was no need to stay silent forever.

"Well then. I could tell you about myself, but how about recommending a good place to stay? Preferably somewhere with delicious food."

"Trying to dodge the question? ...Well, what's your budget?"

"I don't really care. Money's not an issue. Oh, but it'd be nice if Set could stay too."

Hearing Rei say he didn't care about the price, those around them looked surprised, but quickly understood.

They wondered why a child who looked like Rei had that kind of money, but the next moment, they saw Set beside him. Seeing that he had a griffin as his familiar and possessed such formidable strength as an adventurer, it made perfect sense.

Incidentally, Rei was naturally wearing the hood of his Dragon Robe.

Most residents of Gosh had brown skin, though not as dark as that of Dark Elves.

Among them, someone with pale skin like Rei's stood out remarkably.

Zalust had warned him about this, which is why he was currently hiding his face.

"Well, there are plenty of inns with delicious food... but finding one with stables where Set can stay is difficult. Especially given our current hostility with Daldra..."

Being at odds with both Daldra and the Elema Trading Company within Gosh inevitably brought repercussions.

And all the inns with stables capable of accommodating Set were high-end establishments, meaning deepening ties with the Elema Trading Company was inevitable.

"Worst case, we'll find some land to camp on and sleep in tents."

The words that came out of Rei's mouth drew expressions of pity from those around him.

But that look came from their ignorance of the fact that the tent Rei possessed was a magic tent.

Inside the magic tent, the amenities were incomparably better than any ordinary inn, making it extremely comfortable to live in.

As for water, a necessity for living, Rei didn't need to worry about it as long as he had his flowing water dagger.

Since they didn't know that, it was only natural they looked at him like that.

Amidst this, Augusto suddenly spoke up.

"Rei, if you'd like, would you come to my house? Fortunately, we have stables large enough for Set, and my wife would welcome a guest. The rooms aren't quite on par with a high-class inn... but I think the cooking rivals any inn, don't you think?"

As he spoke of his wife's cooking, a dreamy light shone in Augusto's eyes, as if he were savoring the memory of its taste.

"Ah, Rei. Augusto's a devoted husband, you see. I suppose you could call them a couple who've stayed happily married forever."

Zalust advised Rei, perhaps thinking it best to steer clear of staying at Augusto's house, given how passionate he was about his wife.

But Rei's expression grew troubled at Zarust's words.

Given Erema Trading Company's influence over the inns that would accept him with Set, Rei's only options were camping out using a magic tent or staying at Augusto's house.

Using a magic tent would let him use his time as he pleased, but he'd have to prepare his own meals.

Staying at Augusto's house would mean being subjected to displays of the couple's passionate love, but he wouldn't have to prepare meals. ...Of course, considering how much Rei and Set ate, he'd have to pay a hefty price.

He pondered which option was better, but he couldn't help leaning toward staying at Augusto's house, where he could try the local cuisine.

Speaking of local cuisine, there are street stalls too... but considering Elema Trading Company's influence, I also feel I can't risk eating at a shady place.

(Huh? Then how does Augusto's wife buy ingredients for meals? If the Elema Trading Company has such strong influence, I'd think shops wouldn't sell her ingredients.)

That question lingered in Rei's mind, but before he could find an answer, August pressed him, asking what he intended to do.

"Rei, what will you do? Personally, I'd really like you to come with us. I want to introduce you to my wife as the person who saved my life."

"...Ah, yeah. Got it. I'll take you up on that. I'll cover the food costs for both of us, so don't worry."

"You really don't need to worry about food expenses,"

Augusto insisted, given that Ray had saved his life. But Ray had acquired the magic item, the Sand Ship, and the sand pirates were to be sold as slaves, so he had some financial leeway.

Since it was a real desert, not the dungeon's simulated one, it was uncharted territory for Rei. He figured even if he asked about their base, finding it would be difficult, so he left that alone for now. Still, once he had a little more breathing room, he might try searching for it.

(Though chances are it'll be ransacked by someone else before then.)

Being the sand pirates' base, it naturally held all sorts of treasures.

And since Rei had captured most of the sand pirates, there should be none left at the base... or at least very few.

Faced with such prime prey, would other Sand Pirates... or adventurers just stand by and watch?

Considering his past actions, the answer was undoubtedly no.

Though perhaps that was precisely because it was Rei, feared as the Bandit Eater.

"Well, we'll discuss the food expenses later... but we can't stay here forever and be a bother to others."

His excitement seemed to have subsided, and Augusto muttered as he looked around.

Rei hadn't advanced very far into Gosh since entering.

In such a place, the gathering of Augusto's carriage, camels, and the adventurers guarding them was extremely conspicuous, and it was true they were obstructing other travelers.

"True. We also need to return to the guild now that our escort job is complete. ...Rei, what about you? As an adventurer active in Gilm, you're probably strong enough to be unmatched here in Gosh."

After witnessing his battles against the Sand Serpent and the sand pirates, no one could possibly underestimate Rei based on his appearance.

And considering he had his familiar, Set, there was no way anyone would claim Rei lacked the strength.

(The only cause for concern is...)

Zalust looked at Rei, hooded, and pondered the uncertainty.

Rei, visibly petite, would likely be underestimated based on his appearance alone. And if he lowered his hood, his pale skin—as if to declare he wasn't from the Kingdom of Solrain—and his features, which could easily be mistaken for a woman's, would be revealed.

In this country where manly men are respected, an adventurer with Rei's appearance would undoubtedly be looked down upon and harassed.

The fact that she had a griffin as a familiar would also likely be seen as Rei relying on Set's power to appear strong, leading to further contempt.

Had Zarust and the other adventurers not witnessed Rei's dispatch of the Sand Serpent with that first strike, they too might have looked down on her.

(Though skilled adventurers like those hired by the Elema Trading Company might be a different story...)

Skilled adventurers are rare, and it's only natural that the majority are inexperienced. Expecting such people to recognize Rei's strength would be unreasonable.

To prevent the adventurers at Gosh's guild from suffering unnecessary injuries, having Rei accompany them to the guild was the best choice.

Adventurers don't need to show their faces at the guild unless they're accepting a request, but...

"You'll be selling Sand Serpent materials too, right?"

Zalust asked, recalling the giant monster they'd encountered when they first met Rei. Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah, I blew the proof-of-kill parts away... but I heard the hide's sellable."

Laying out the details he'd heard from Zarust on their way to Gosh, Rei recalled the information about the Sand Serpent. The source of that information nodded in agreement.

"It should fetch a decent price as armor material. There are a few parts in the innards that sell too, and the meat is marketable..."

At the word "meat," Rei shook his head.

"Set seemed interested in trying it, so I'll give it to him."

Saying this, Rei had no intention of eating snake meat.

While he had no qualms about eating the meat of humanoid monsters like orcs, ogres, or cyclopes, he simply couldn't bring himself to eat snake.

He ate wyvern meat, and back in Japan, he'd enjoyed eel, conger eel, lamprey, and even more unusual fare like moray eel. To an outsider, his aversion to snake must seem utterly baffling.

"It's actually quite a luxury item, though. Anyway, even if we're meeting up with Mr. Augusto later, I guess I'll head to the guild for now."

With that, Rei parted ways with Augusto for the moment and headed toward the guild with his escort of adventurers, including Zarust.
