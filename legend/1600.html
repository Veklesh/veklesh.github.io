When Rei and Set joined the battle, the fight between Jaya and the Resistance rapidly drew to a close.

Though, considering that women like Elena, Vihera, and Byune had already been fighting before Rei and the others joined, the battle was likely already nearing its end at that point.

Originally, Elena and Vihera alone—with Rei lending a hand to some extent—possessed the strength to defeat nearly a thousand giants.

These soldiers were weaker and fewer in number than those giants.

There was absolutely no struggle whatsoever against such soldiers of Jaya.

Of course, there might have been moments where, like Rei, he struggled to distinguish between Jaya soldiers and Resistance soldiers.

Regardless, Rei, riding a griffin over three meters tall and wielding two long-handled weapons—the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear—was rampaging, killing only the Jaya soldiers.

Bodies severed vertically, skulls crushed, necks snapped... A scene worthy of being called a hellscape unfolded, and seeing it, the morale of the Jaya soldiers naturally plummeted.

Among them were soldiers who had survived the battle at the heart of Mejogo. For such soldiers, it was not uncommon to flee the moment they saw Set.

From the moment Rei joined the fight until it was completely over, it took about thirty minutes.

"Well, that's about it. Feels like it took a bit too long, though."

It was naturally Rei who muttered this while surveying the battlefield from atop Set's back.

The slight dissatisfaction in his words stemmed either from the battle taking too long, or perhaps from the summer sun turning toward dusk, its light now glaring.

Whatever the reason, it was clear Rei was dissatisfied...

"Wh-Why are you so dissatisfied!? We ended the fight so easily!"

A voice filled with surprise came from Ailen, seated behind Rei.

"Well, what do you expect? ...Honestly, if it hadn't turned into a melee, this fight would've been over in no time. And we spent this much time on it. It's only natural to feel dissatisfied."

The way he said it, as if it were obvious, made it clear to Ailen that this wasn't bravado or anything like that.

(So this is... the power of a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname...)

Though Rei was shorter than her, having witnessed his true strength, it no longer mattered to Ailen.

She fixed an admiring gaze on Rei...

"Oh, Rei. You've brought quite the cute girlfriend along, haven't you?"

Suddenly, that voice reached her ears.

"Wh-wh-wh-what? You mean... her...?"

Irene tried to deny the voice she'd heard, but the moment she saw its owner, she was speechless.

For the person standing before her was that beautiful.

The light of the sun, now turning into sunset, adorned the prostitute's dress she had been wearing just moments ago with a dazzling brilliance.

Vihera stood there with such serene grace that she seemed like a work of art... completely captivating Irene's gaze.

The shock of it blew away the admiration she had felt for Rei.

"Um, well..."

"What's wrong, Vihera? Rei is..."

Before the speechless Irene appeared Elena, possessing beauty rivaling Vihera's own.

The appearance of these two left Ailen utterly unable to think straight...

"I'll... I'll go report this right away!"

Saying that, she dashed away from the spot.

"What was that about?"

Rei, who hadn't quite grasped the situation, tilted his head in confusion. Set, standing behind him, mimicked Rei's gesture and tilted his own head.

Seeing Rei like that, Vihera sighed in exasperation, though she had no intention of telling Rei why.

"So, what happened in the end? Well, considering Rei was in the fight and was with that Resistance girl, I can pretty much guess why."

"Ah, we got approval from Sura, who leads the Resistance. Not everyone, of course, just those who wanted to."

"Is that so? Well, it stands to reason that some would want to return to their hometowns, while others in the Resistance would naturally want to stay in this country."

Home is something special to everyone.

Even Vihera, who had fled her own homeland, understood that completely.

"Yeah... Home is important."

Elena agreed with Vihera's words. ...Yet, those words held deep conflict.

As the sole daughter of the Duke of Kerebel, Elena was highly likely to inherit the Kerebel Duchy in the future.

If she weren't in that position, she would probably feel a strong desire to leave home like Vihera and act alongside Rei.

But Elena's position made that impossible.

Especially now, as she was able to spend fulfilling time with Rei, that conflict only grew stronger.

"For now, shall we go see Sura? She probably has various things she wants to discuss with us over there too."

Whether aware of their thoughts or not, Rei announced this.

Elena and Vihera exchanged glances at Rei's expression... and eventually wore resigned smiles.

"What's wrong?"

"Nothing. Anyway, we're going to see the one leading the Resistance, right? It would be rude to keep them waiting too long. Let's go too."

"...Well, I suppose so."

Though Elena's demeanor left him feeling slightly unsettled, given the current situation, it was undoubtedly the right course of action.

Even though it was already late afternoon and there was still some time before nightfall, it was best to wrap things up as quickly as possible.

Of course, if they were to walk from here toward Gilm, they'd inevitably arrive in the capital, Rossi, after night had fully fallen.

(Well, maybe that's actually better? Traveling near Rossi during the day would make us stand out too much.)

There were men from the Resistance, but a group of mostly women passing near Rossi would inevitably draw attention.

If the surroundings are bright, it would be especially noticeable.

If that happens, soldiers will definitely come to investigate what kind of group we are. If they find out we're the ones who were prostitutes in Mejogo, it's certain to cause a huge commotion.

For lower-ranking individuals like soldiers, it meant losing their familiar prostitutes.

And for those in higher positions, it meant the Mejogo district, which paid enormous taxes as an entertainment district, could no longer operate as such.

Naturally, they would interfere with Rei and the others.

Though whether that interference would involve force or simply a request to hear their story... that much was unclear.

That's precisely why moving past Rossi's place during the night wasn't a bad choice at all.

Fortunately, there were hardly any monsters around here, and thanks to Jaya, bandits were basically nonexistent.

...Though there's still a very real possibility Jaya's pursuers might show up.

"Still, that's probably better."

"What's wrong, all of a sudden?"

Vihera turned her gaze toward Rei and asked in response to his mutter.

Rei shook his head at Vihera, signaling it was nothing, then spoke.

"Well, we should wrap things up with Sura quickly. I'm going. What about you?"

"Oh? We'll go with you, Rei. We want to see for ourselves what kind of person leads the Resistance—not just hear about it from you."

"I see. Alright then, let's go. Set, I'm counting on you."

"Grr."

Without any particular objection to Vihera's words, Rei simply nodded and stroked Set.

Set purred contentedly at the sensation and began walking toward where Sura was.

Elena, Vihera, and Byune followed after Set.

Meanwhile, Elena's familiar, Yellow, was flying through the sky as dusk approached, seemingly lost in thought.

He must have something on his mind.

(Come to think of it, bats and such used to fly around when it got dim in Japan too.)

Being the countryside, there were various bat roosts, and seeing bats flying in the sky as dusk fell wasn't unusual.

Rei gazed at the sky, thinking of his time in Japan, as he made his way toward the Resistance headquarters.

The Resistance had returned to headquarters after the battle... and as he walked, he felt the gazes of the women with combat skills who had assisted the Resistance fixed upon him.

The gazes from those around him held a strong sense of awe.

Having shared the battlefield with them, they had seen firsthand just how much power Rei and the others possessed.

Still, what they felt was awe, not fear.

The main reason was likely that they had been saved.

Then, several men and women approached Rei and the others from the headquarters.

At the center of them is Sura, the leader of the Resistance.

Sura opened her mouth, her eyes glistening.

"Rei... um, thank you."

Her words were few, but all the more powerful for it.

It was only natural. Though she led the resistance, Sura was still young.

That very fact meant she was constantly on edge, and there was no doubt this battle had taken a heavy mental toll on her.

"Don't worry about it. Didn't I say from the start? It's because I'm on a mission. ...So, what do you say we do for now? Personally, I want to leave Mejogo and head for Gilm as soon as possible. Even by carriage, it'll take about a month. We should leave as soon as we can."

Hearing the words "heading for Gilm," most of those around them wore puzzled expressions.

Realizing Sura hadn't yet told the others about accepting volunteers in Gilm, Rei turned his gaze to her, asking silently, What about you?

His look implied: If Sura explains it, I'll leave it to her. If she doesn't, I'll explain it myself.

Sura nodded back at Rei, indicating she would explain herself.

Then, looking at the people around them... She knew not everyone had returned yet, but she spoke anyway.

"Listen up, everyone. You might have already noticed, but this is Rei. She's an adventurer from the Kingdom of Mireana, known by the epithet 'Crimson'."

A rustle.

Those who heard Sura's words reacted in their own ways.

Adventurers with nicknames were that rare.

At the very least, there wasn't a single one in the Kingdom of Reburina, and even among the women forced into prostitution, few had ever seen one.

Some, seeing Rei's small stature, doubted, "Is she really a nicknamed adventurer?"... But such doubts vanished the moment they saw Set.

Above all... seeing Rei and the others' power, there was no way anyone could choose to underestimate them.

"And then he made a proposal. Some of you might have heard of the town of Gilm in the Kingdom of Mireana? The lord there said he'd accept people with nowhere to go. Not just those taken by Jaya, but us Resistance members too."

At that moment, an even greater murmur arose than when Rei's true identity had been revealed.

It was only natural. After all, Rei's situation was, strictly speaking, someone else's problem.

Sure, we were involved too, but to put it bluntly, now that the fighting was over, one could argue Rei's presence was no longer necessary.

...In reality, it wasn't that simple, but most people thought it was that straightforward.

But Gilm accepting them meant it concerned their future... their very existence going forward.

It would be difficult to expect them not to care about that.

The murmurings stirred by Sura's words spread to others who hadn't heard them.

Watching the murmurs spread, Sura spoke again.

"Naturally, some of you will want to stay here, and others will want to return to your own homelands rather than the Kingdom of Mireana. It seems they won't forcibly take even those people to Gilm. It's strictly for those who wish to go. ...Right?"

Rei nodded at Sura, who was looking at him and asking.

"That's right. Also, those whose hometowns lie along the route from the Kingdom of Reburina to Gilm have been told they may travel there instead."

Silence.

A silence so profound it was hard to believe this was a battlefield engulfed the surroundings.

Then, suddenly, a woman broke that silence.

"But what are we supposed to do once we get to Gilm? Are they telling us to become prostitutes again? And a place big enough for all these people to live..."

"There is. I don't know if anyone knows about it, but Gilm is currently undergoing expansion. From a town to a city-sized settlement. Even after that's finished... I won't go into details here, but there will be work. At the very least, you shouldn't starve to death."

Yes, Rei replied.
