“……Huh?”

Rei stopped his hand mid-motion as he was about to take a bite of his sandwich, and looked at Walker with a puzzled expression, as if he didn’t understand what he was saying.

A dungeon has appeared in the mountains near the town of Goltz.

Rei could understand that; while it was unusual, it wasn’t exactly rare.

After all, there were two dungeons near Gilm.

But the idea that a dungeon had formed on the face of a cliff was something even Rei couldn’t quite wrap his head around.

“Um, did I hear that wrong?”

“A dungeon on the face of a cliff… If you’re saying something that looks like a dungeon has appeared, then it’s definitely not a mishearing.”

“...To begin with, how did you even know a dungeon had formed in a place like that? Couldn’t it just be a hole that looks like a dungeon for some reason?”

It was only natural for Rei to have his doubts.

After all, while it was unlikely, it wasn’t impossible for a cave-like hole to appear in a cliff.

However, in response to Rei’s words, Walker flashed a smile as if to say, “I’m glad you asked,” and spoke up.

“That’s true. If it’s just a hole in the cliff face, we can’t necessarily say it’s a dungeon. But… at the very least, that hole that appeared in the cliff face… no, let’s just call it a cave for now. It’s a fact that inside that cave, there are goblins, kobolds, orcs… and various other monsters.”

“How can you be so sure? Did you actually see monsters peeking out of that cave? Or, since it’s on the cliff face, did you go all the way down there?”

If it’s a cave on the cliff face, it would be extremely difficult to determine where the monsters are… or even if there are any animals besides monsters.

The best way to investigate the cave is, as Rei suggested, to climb down the cliff and check inside the cave directly.

However, Walker disagrees.

“No. Apparently, that cliff contains a special type of ore, so it’s quite slippery. On top of that, it’s sharply jagged, so there’s no way we could possibly try to climb down from the top.”

“I suppose we could lower an adventurer down using a safety rope. And if the rocks are sharp, armor or a shield might protect them. …Could the rope snap?”

“That’s a high possibility, apparently.”

“...So, in the end, how did you determine there were monsters inside that cave?”

“That’s simple. At the bottom of the cave… no, in this case, I suppose I should say at the bottom of the cliff. There are several corpses of the monsters I mentioned earlier—goblins, kobolds, orcs—and they all appear to have fallen to their deaths.”

“Huh?”

Once again, Rei looked at Walker as if he had no idea what he was talking about.

But Walker, while eating the sandwich made by Lenora, showed no sign of laughing or implying it was a joke.

In other words, what Walker had said was the unadulterated truth.

“Um… what does that mean? So, monsters are coming out one after another from what looks like a dungeon on the cliff face, and they’re just falling to their deaths from high up on their own. Is that what you’re saying?”

“Yes. Exactly as Rei said, it’s been that way. I’m not sure if it’s appropriate to say ‘thanks to that,’ but lately, it’s become a trend among Goltz’s adventurers—no, not just adventurers—to collect monster parts and magic stones at the bottom of that cliff. By the way, orcs are apparently the most popular.”

"Ah... well, I suppose that makes sense."

Orcs themselves are fairly strong, and you could even say they’re the worst kind of opponent for women, just like goblins.

But the meat of such orcs has a flavor far beyond its rank.

In fact, you could say it’s several levels above the meat you’d normally find for sale around here.

“Besides, considering how monster corpses have been piling up almost daily over the past few months, it’s hard to believe there was a monster in a cave that just happened to form by chance.”

“Isn’t it more likely that the cave connects to some other part of the mountain? Monsters don’t just suddenly appear out of nowhere, after all.”

“Of course, that’s a possibility. …Actually, I hear Goltz was worried about that very thing and conducted a fairly large-scale search of the mountain.”

Golts may be in the countryside, but it’s not on the frontier.

In other words, while it’s not impossible for monsters like orcs to appear, it’s extremely rare.

That’s precisely why finding the bodies of orcs that had fallen to their deaths almost every day—no, in some cases every few hours—must have felt so abnormal.

If there’s an orc… no, even a goblin or kobold settlement nearby, then Goltz and the surrounding villages and towns are in danger.

With that in mind, Goltz gathered adventurers and hunters from the surrounding villages and towns and conducted a large-scale survey of the mountain.

“...But in the end, we couldn’t find any place connected to that cave. Of course, since the mountain where the dungeon formed is quite large and expansive, it’s possible we missed it... but there have been almost no reports of monster attacks in that area.”

Walker’s words implied that there were no other monsters in the vicinity, or if there were, their numbers were small.

“In that case, the remaining possibilities are: is the cave’s continuation in Goltz? Or is it located further away, not in the immediate vicinity…”

“A dungeon, then?”

As if picking up on Rei’s words, Walker concluded.

Of course, there are various other suspicious possibilities, but as far as the most likely ones go, a dungeon is probably the most likely candidate at this point.

“I suppose so. But if the dungeon is on the cliff face, and that face is slippery or has sharp rocks, we can’t climb up or down. Besides, if the dungeon just formed, there’s no need to rush to clear it, is there?”

“That’s true. I agree. Especially considering Gilm’s current situation, it would be quite a challenge for you and the others to escape.”

“Despite that, the fact that you came to talk to me like this—does that mean something’s up?”

“Yes. Apparently, the number of monsters found dead at the base of the cliff has suddenly increased recently. Also, they say there are monsters there that we’ve never seen before.”

“So, you came to Walker for advice?”

“Yes. I had quite a history with the guildmaster over there back in my younger days.”

Although Walker referred to his “younger days,” he didn’t look like he’d aged much at all.

Rei couldn’t quite grasp exactly what age Walker meant by “in my younger days,” but he could understand that Walker and the Guild Master of Goltz had a friendly relationship.

“So, does that mean there’s a possibility the dungeon will expand?”

“Yes. How a dungeon expands depends entirely on its unique characteristics, and it seems Goltz’s dungeon does so rather quickly.”

So that must be why he hurriedly contacted Walker.

“So, you want me to go clear the dungeon?”

“That’s right. Of course, I’m not forcing you. But since it’s a dungeon with an entrance on a cliff face, I thought that if you’re a Crimson Wings with a means of transportation like Set…”

“It’s not the kind of place a dungeon would be in.”

A dungeon on a cliff face with slippery or jagged rocks.

In a dungeon like that, just getting in is a life-or-death situation.

Also, given its location, it would be difficult to bring porters along like you would for a normal dungeon.

In that case, the obvious necessity would be a means of flying, and in that sense, it was only natural that expectations for Rei and the others, who possessed Set, would rise.

“That’s right. So, what do you think?”

“Are we going to take on the dungeon?” Rei hesitates, unsure of what to do, as Walker asks this with a questioning look.

No, in terms of whether or not to challenge the dungeon, the decision to do so had already been made.

One of Death Scythe’s skills: Terrain Manipulation.

To raise the level of this skill—currently at Level 3—they need to destroy the dungeon’s core with Death Scythe.

(Well, there might be other ways to level up Terrain Manipulation, but since I know destroying the dungeon’s core with Death Scythe is the quickest way, I can’t pass up this opportunity.)

When it comes to destroying the dungeon’s core, Rei didn’t know if “newly born” was the right way to put it, but a dungeon like the one mentioned in this story—not a massive one like the ones in Exil—was the best choice.

Since it’s still in its infancy, the dungeon isn’t that large, and reaching the location of the dungeon’s core shouldn’t be too difficult.

That said, there are also concerns about that dungeon.

“I think it’s worth considering… but are the residents of Goltz—especially the adventurers—on board with this?”

Rei asked.

From the residents of Goltz’s perspective, monsters emerging from the dungeon would simply fall to their deaths on the surface without them having to do a thing.

Exactly how often does that happen... and how many die each day?

Rei didn’t know the details, but since they could make money without lifting a finger, it wouldn’t be surprising if some people, thinking only of the immediate benefits, came to resent the idea of exploring the dungeon.

Logically speaking, they probably understand that the dungeon will continue to grow, and that in the future, this could cause significant damage to the town where they live.

But as long as there’s money dangling right in front of them, there will always be those who pounce on it.

Adventuring, in particular, is a dangerous profession.

Depending on the job, it wouldn’t be wrong to say they’re risking their lives for their pay.

For such adventurers, clearing a dungeon means losing that source of extra income.

It was fine that they headed to Goltz to clear the dungeon, but if they were sabotaged behind the scenes, it was certain to become a huge hassle.

(Well, in the worst-case scenario, I could just set up a base somewhere else and stay there, like I did when I went to the sea.)

That line of thinking is only possible because of the Misty Ring, which allows them to store and carry large quantities of supplies—starting with food—necessary for daily life.

An ordinary adventurer would never be able to pull something like that off.

……Though, come to think of it, it’s precisely because Rei and the others aren’t ordinary that they were offered a job like this in the first place.

“I don’t think everyone is completely on board with this. However, we’ve been given the go-ahead to fight back if they resort to force, and we’ve been told that a little overreaction won’t be a problem. …But only a little, mind you?”

Judging by Rei’s past actions, she must have thought he might go too far.

At those carefully chosen words, Rei smiled and spoke up.

“I haven’t actually agreed to clear the dungeon yet, though. Besides, if we go to the dungeon, I won’t be able to participate in the Gamelion hunt either.”

For Rei, not being able to hunt Gamelions was a pretty big blow.

Since it was an annual tradition, he naturally wanted to participate if possible. …If asked which was more important—the dungeon’s core or the hunt—the answer would obviously be the latter.

Rei brought up the Gamelion hunt here purely as a bargaining chip.

If he’s going to the dungeon anyway, it’s only natural to think that the more rewards he can get, the better.

But... upon hearing Rei’s words, Walker shook his head regretfully.

“I see. Well, since this isn’t exactly a commissioned request, we can’t really offer any specific reward from our side. Besides, Rei has been a huge help with the expansion project. Given that, we can’t really ask you to do something unreasonable. I understand. So, unfortunately, we’ll have to ask another adventurer…”

“Wait.”

Rei reflexively called out to Walker, who was about to end the conversation so abruptly.

He hadn’t expected Walker, who had brought him the dungeon offer, to wrap things up so abruptly.

“What is it?”

Rei looked at Walker with a puzzled expression.

It didn’t seem like a joke or anything; it genuinely looked like he was trying to cut the conversation short right there.

Realizing this from Walker’s expression, Rei eventually nodded reluctantly.

“Alright, we’ll take on that dungeon. If Marina and the others can’t do it, I’ll go by myself.”

That was what he said.
