Rei couldn't comprehend what the alchemist before him—Zubozu—had just said for a moment.

The words that came out of his mouth were that unexpected.

But he quickly wore an expression of understanding.

If anyone was responsible for Kabajido's death, he himself was likely one of them.

Originally, Kabajido had kept the alchemists under his control.

Thanks to that, he had been able to use numerous magic items, so their relationship probably hadn't been all that bad.

In fact, given that there were now those targeting Rei as Kabajido's enemy, that assessment couldn't be wrong.

Considering that anyone who disliked Kabajido would have likely switched allegiances to another faction, Zubo's words weren't wrong either.

(Kabadji's death was almost like suicide... but I was definitely involved in pushing him to that point.)

Kabadiz's face flashed through Rei's mind.

But the feeling that he wished Kabajido had lived remains strong within Rei too.

Because it was from Kabajido's mouth that he spoke of Japan's cherry blossoms—something no inhabitant of this world could ever truly understand.

In other words, to Rei, Kabajido was a fellow countryman.

(Considering what happened with Takumu in the Zepairu clan, it wasn't an impossible occurrence... but still, the fact that he was right in front of me and I let him die is huge.)

The civil war that erupted in the Bestir Empire flashes through his mind for an instant, but he quickly redirects his thoughts to the figure before him, eyes brimming with hatred.

"Let me ask you this. Could this be some kind of mistake? Kabajido's death was practically suicide. Blaming me for that seems pretty off-base, don't you think?"

"Don't you dare joke about it!"

The rage echoing from Zubo's mouth was as thick with hatred as his eyes.

Even if one were to say the usual Zuboz was more on the quiet side, the level of hatred here was so intense that no one would believe it.

"First off. Why are you sticking up for Kabajido so much? The civil war in the Bestir Empire is over now. You should stop this nonsense and live as an alchemist in the Bestir Empire."

"...It was His Highness Kabajido who found me when my only future was death and raised me. You expect me to accept his murder silently?"

"Benefactor?"

Tilting his head, Rei briefly wondered if perhaps Vihera knew the man standing before him. But when he turned his gaze toward Vihera, he saw her dodging Dias's swinging claymore with wide evasions while trying to close the distance.

(Why is he dodging so wide?)

The usual Vihera would have anticipated the attack by a hair's breadth, then exploited that opening to land a counterattack... that's what he should be doing.

But right now, Vihera was only evading Dias's attacks with wide swings.

Rei couldn't fathom why things had come to this, but he judged asking about it now would be pointless.

(Besides, if Vihera knew this man, she would have said something when he appeared.)

Since Vihera said nothing upon seeing Zubozu's form, he likely didn't recognize him.

"I see. I understand why you hate me... well, not really, but I get it. But then, why did you give a magic item to a monster? You hate me, right? I don't think Gilm has anything to do with it."

Keeping a wary eye on Paul, who wielded a shield that generated ice, Rei asked.

Countering ice itself wasn't particularly difficult.

Fortunately, there weren't many buildings nearby, so if he really wanted to, he should be able to deal with the ice shield.

But dealing with the ice shield and capturing the person wielding it alive were two different matters.

From Rei's perspective, Paul possessed a physique as sturdy as a mountain, clearly well-trained.

Still, if asked whether he was accustomed to rough work, Ray couldn't help but tilt his head.

(If he were an agent of the Bestir Empire, some strength wouldn't be surprising... but judging by his movements, he's just average. But could he withstand an attack using the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour...? Then maybe I should try magic?)

As Rei pondered his options, Zubozu smiled and spoke.

"I already know this Gilm is a place dear to you. The feeling of having such a precious place desecrated... The best way for you to understand that is to experience it firsthand, wouldn't you agree?"

His earlier excitement seemed to have subsided, and Zubozu's tone had returned to his usual self.

Watching Zubo like this, Rei's brow furrowed in displeasure.

"So you're saying you caused all that commotion just to harass me?"

"Of course, that's not all. It was the culmination of all the experiments I've been conducting up until now."

"...I suppose so."

It was undeniably a feat of high alchemical skill, enabling even ordinary goblins and kobolds—creatures normally incapable of wielding magic items—to use them.

Rei, who had actual combat experience against kobolds, understood this well. And while it was unclear if the cyclops was a rare or higher-tier species, the already formidable creature had become even more powerful by wielding a magic item hammer.

Rei was able to defeat it easily precisely because he was Rei, but had a regular adventurer fought it, they would have suffered considerable casualties.

Understanding this, Rei had no choice but to concede to Zubo's words.

(Well, maybe it's useful for extracting information?)

Rei hesitated slightly, but he believed that buying time here would surely benefit Elena and Vihera.

Knowing their capabilities, he made that choice.

(Especially in Elena's case, the opponent didn't seem all that strong anyway.)

Elena had undergone the succession ritual and gained the power of the Ancient Dragon. She wasn't yet fully mastering that power, but even so, she possessed abilities that set her apart from ordinary opponents.

Right now, she was being overwhelmed by a magic item that caught her opponent off guard, but for Rei, it was practically a certainty that Elena would win before long.

Given that, gathering necessary information while the opponent was excited and talking freely wasn't a bad option at all.

(Besides, that magic item creating that ice shield couldn't keep working forever, right?)

Glancing at the shield magic item Paul held, Rei opened his mouth, speaking directly to him.

"How about we start with introductions? You seem to know my name, but unfortunately, I don't know yours."

Zuboz flashed a momentary look of surprise at Rei's words.

He probably hadn't expected to be prompted for an introduction at this point.

But he quickly regained his composure and spoke.

"Fine. It would be a pity to die without knowing who will take your life, wouldn't it?"

"Save your nonsense for when you're asleep. ...Actually, you shouldn't say that even when you're asleep. It's annoying."

Zuboz's cheek twitched at Rei's provocative retort, but he knew taking the bait here would play right into Rei's hands. He had no intention of falling for it.

His cheek still twitching slightly, Zuboz continued.

"First, the one using the Magic Sword I forged over there, Windscale, is Dias. As you can see, that Magic Sword is a claymore, so its single strike is powerful. But at the same time, it's difficult to fight against someone like Vihera who focuses on evasion."

"...Well, Vihera is a former imperial family member of the Bestir Empire who went rogue. Is it really okay not to use an honorific with her name?"

"You expect me to respect someone who contributed to His Highness Kabajido's death? If that woman hadn't meddled, His Highness wouldn't have died!"

Seeing how rigidly fixed his hatred over Kabajido's death had made him, Rei judged further words pointless and changed the subject.

"So, about that magic item that guy Dias uses—the Wind Scales or whatever it's called—aren't you going to tell me about it?"

"What are you talking about? I said I'd introduce you, but did I say I'd explain our weapons specifically?"

"...Earlier you mentioned something about it being effective against evasion-focused opponents like Vihera..."

Zuboz's face flushed with anger at Rei's nitpicking remark.

Zuboz had intended to tell them they were at a disadvantage, hoping to make Rei taste despair.

But it was only he who had been reacting sensitively to Rei's words since earlier; Rei showed no sign of being shaken at all.

That was precisely because Rei believed in Elena and Vihera's strength, and that only made Zuboz more irritated.

"Zuboz, calm down."

Perhaps unable to bear watching Zuboz like this, Paul, holding the magic item shield that created ice, spoke up.

Despite wielding a shield that was quite large, befitting his massive frame, he didn't flinch.

Paul's words seemed to calm Zuboz. He exhaled softly before speaking again.

"No, no. How could I have let myself get so worked up over your words? ...Now then, shall we continue? The one fighting the Princess General is my partner, Adria. As you can see, she's also using a Magic Sword I prepared: Wind's Fang."

Watching Adria continuously unleash countless arrows from its hilt, Zubo deliberately shrugged his shoulders before speaking.

"As for this one... well, since you've seen it in full use, I doubt I need to explain its abilities."

"I suppose not. Though I am curious how she manages to store that many arrows inside the hilt."

Not that it was a genuine question...

It was a magic item that stored something that should have been impossible to store.

Rei knew what it was.

No, he knew far more than that. After all, its symbolic counterpart was embedded in Rei's right arm.

(It's not like a Misty Ring-type item box. More like a degraded version, similar to what Elena had, built into the hilt of a longsword. But if that's all it was, then if she tried to draw the arrows, they'd appear in that woman's... was it Adria? That Adria's hand.)

Recalling his own Misty Ring, the simplified version Elena had used before, or what might be called a degraded magic item, Rei pondered.

(So, there's something else built into that longsword besides that?)

Rei returned a smile to the one on Zubo's face.

That seemed to displease him. Zuboz, who had been smiling mere seconds ago, opened his mouth with a sour expression.

"How can you afford to smile? Even a Princess General is, after all, just one person. Do you truly believe you can keep blocking an endless barrage of arrows forever?"

"I can."

Saying this, Rei glanced briefly toward Elena... no, toward a spot slightly off to the side of where Elena stood.

Seeing what he expected there, his smile deepened.

"This is Elena, the one with the nickname 'Princess General,' you know? Do you really think someone like her can be dealt with by a magic item that can only do nothing but keep firing arrows? That's a bit too much of an underestimation of Elena, don't you think?"

"...But she is, in fact, purely on the defensive like that, isn't she?"

"True. I'd even say that surprised me, honestly. She's just shooting arrows... or should I say launching them in this case? Yet despite that state, she's firing them to hinder Elena's evasion. Looks like a warrior type, but she can use a bow too, huh?"

"Yes, of course. That's precisely why I entrusted her with the Wind Fang."

Those words were half bravado.

When they escaped the magic item shop, they'd been forced to leave most of their goods behind. Among the magic items stored at the base Adria had prepared in this slum district and the few they'd managed to carry out, the only one Adria seemed capable of mastering was the Wind Fang.

Then, as if seeing right through that feeble bluff, Rei smiled and spoke.

"I see. That woman, Adria, seems to be handling that Wind Fang quite well. But... let me tell you something useful."

Waiting for the moment when the presence lurking at the edge of his vision made its move, Rei continued.

"Familiar and master are one and the same... you see."

"KYUUUUU!"

As if in sync with Rei's words, Yellow collided with Adria's right hand... and the Wind Fang she held in that hand was flung into the air.
