The Silver Fruit Inn, where Rei and the others have established their base in Rossi.

After buying a large quantity of Panyan, Rei and the others returned to that inn, but...

"I never thought it'd be empty,"

Rei murmured as he lay sprawled on his bed in his room.

He thought he should have played with Set a little longer, but somehow he wasn't in the mood for that now.

Besides, he could guess that Set was probably enjoying a nap in the stables right about now.

...Though, seeing the horses in the same stable tense up because Set was there was a familiar sight to Rei.

Of course, it wasn't good for the horses to stay tense forever, but after a few days, they usually understood Set wasn't an enemy, and the tension eased.

That said, it was merely that they weren't as tense, not that they weren't tense at all.

"If Yellow had been here, we could have played together."

When Rei and the others left, Yellow was still asleep due to yesterday's incident. But when Rei returned, Yellow was nowhere to be found.

Had Elena taken him? Or had he wandered off on his own? Rei didn't know for sure, but he wasn't worried that someone had forcibly taken him.

Of course, Yellow, a black dragon's child, would look like a treasure trove to anyone who knew what they were looking at.

But capturing Yellow—who could fly, possessed formidable defense thanks to his scales, and even had the ability to become invisible—was incredibly difficult.

It would be impossible for someone like those in Rossi, though perhaps not for skilled adventurers like those in Gilm.

With nothing else to do, Rei lay in bed, waiting for someone to return.

Lying there in bed, sleep-deprived from last night's events, Rei was no match for the encroaching drowsiness... his consciousness sinking into darkness before his eyes.

"...Hey, Rei. Wake up. How long do you plan on sleeping?"

"Huh? ...Hm... Huh?"

Rei, who had been sleeping in bed, was gently shaken and called out to, her consciousness slowly returning.

But unless it was an emergency, Rei was generally not a morning person.

Even now, though awake, she hadn't fully registered her surroundings yet, her sleepy eyes scanning the room.

No, he probably wasn't even aware he was looking.

"Oh, how cute."

A familiar voice reached his ears, followed by several others chatting as if agreeing with it.

About a minute passed like that before Rei shifted from his sleepy state to his usual self.

Usually, it took him about five minutes to shake off that groggy feeling after waking up in the morning, but this time it only took about a minute... probably because it was just a nap, not the deep sleep he got when he went to bed at night.

"Hm? ...You're back?"

Finally back to normal, Rei glanced at Elena, Marina, Vihera, Byune, and Yellow in his room before turning his gaze to the window.

When Rei returned to the Silver Fruit Inn, it was still too early to call it night.

He remembered seeing people walking the streets, sweating as they endured the summer sun.

But now, the view outside the window was already dyed in a crimson sunset.

It was a sunset so intense, the entire sky was dyed red, as if a fire were raging somewhere.

Realizing he hadn't slept for just an hour but several hours, he smiled slightly, looking a bit embarrassed.

Seeing Rei's smile, Elena and the others also smiled, as if to say, "Well, what can you do?"

A few minutes passed in that warm atmosphere... Then, as if gathering her thoughts, Rei spoke up.

"So, where were you guys?"

"Gathering intel. ...Though we didn't manage to get anything particularly important, I'm afraid."

"Ah... I see."

They already had a fair amount of information about Jaya, including what they'd gotten from Unchu.

But considering Rei and the others' position, it was only natural they'd want to gather as much information as possible.

Though, as Vihera mentioned, it seems they didn't actually uncover anything particularly important.

Rather, Elena and the others seemed to have spent more effort dealing with the bugs than anything else. Elena spoke up, looking thoroughly fed up.

"Honestly, neglecting their own duties and bothering us like that... They're slacking off."

Listening to Elena's discontented mutterings, Rei could roughly guess what had happened.

Frankly, with beauties like Elena and her companions around, it wasn't surprising that someone would want to hit on them.

Of course, with beauties as stunning as Elena and her companions, it wouldn't be surprising if most men felt intimidated.

Especially when three beauties of that caliber were together—plus Byune, who could easily be called a young beauty herself.

But even in such a situation, the fact that they could drive men mad suggested all three possessed a devilishly alluring beauty, didn't it?

Rei briefly considered saying this aloud, but realized it would likely lead to trouble, so he kept his thoughts to himself.

After chatting about various things, the conversation eventually turned to the Mejogo Rei had scouted.

"So? Did you find a place leading to the underground facility?"

"Yeah, I found a place that looked like it. But getting to where the stairs leading from the surface to the underground facility should be... that's going to be pretty tough. They had several defensive structures set up."

"Jaya must be keeping a close watch on that area too. ...But I guess we just had bad luck this time."

Rei nodded at those words.

Instead of advancing on the surface, they could descend directly from the sky in front of the building connected to the underground facility.

No matter how robust the surface defenses were, they were helpless against an aerial assault.

In that sense, Marina was right about it being bad luck.

"But you're going to cooperate with the Resistance, right? If so, don't we need to figure out something about the surface facilities too?"

"Ah... how exactly we cooperate is also an issue. We'd like to decide on that as soon as possible too."

For Rei and the others, there was a desire to settle things with Jaya as quickly as possible and return to Gilm.

Meanwhile, the Resistance felt resentment. They had fought Jaya all this time, and they didn't want some upstarts like Rei and his crew to swoop in and take her for themselves.

Especially since Rei and his group possessed the skill to take on dozens, hundreds... or even thousands of opponents single-handedly.

Of course, it depended on each individual's fighting style, but there was no doubt they were exceptionally skilled fighters.

Given who they are, what would happen if they truly became enemies with Jaya?

The Resistance understood this all too well, which is precisely why they couldn't simply leave things to Rei and his group.

In truth, the Resistance had recently suffered significant combat losses at the hands of Jaya... and the upper echelons of this nation.

Given that, they desperately needed Rei and the others' strength. Unfortunately, Rei and the others knew almost nothing about the situation.

"For now... shall we eat? Byune seems hungry too."

"Mm."

Byune gave a short reply to Vihera's words.

Byune speaking, the others seemed to remember their own hunger and raised no objections.

Rei had eaten some panya before his nap, but it seemed to have been completely digested while he slept, leaving him feeling a little peckish.

"Yeah, you're right. Let's do that. The food here is pretty good."

"Of course. This Silver Fruit Inn is one of the finest inns in Rossi. If the food served here were bad, there'd be no shortage of people complaining."

Agreeing with Elena's words, Rei and the others headed down to the dining hall on the first floor.

"Ah... Well, I kind of expected it. At least the silver lining is that this dining hall is only for guests staying here."

As Rei and the others entered the dining hall, the guests' gazes naturally turned toward them.

Well, to be precise, it was Elena, Marina, and Vihera.

Some gazed at Byune... but they were few and far between.

Hardly anyone looked at Yellow, and as for Rei, it was practically nonexistent.

Of course, that was because Rei had the hood of his Dragon Robe pulled up. If he took it off, he would undoubtedly draw some attention.

Regardless, seemingly unconcerned with the stares from those in the dining hall, Rei and the others sat down at an empty table and casually ordered dinner.

The Silver Fruit Inn was one of the more upscale lodgings in Rossi, and naturally, most of those staying there possessed considerable financial means.

That meant there were no men hitting on them for women... well, not exactly none, but still far fewer than in a tavern.

Thus, under the watchful eyes of others, they ate the meals that were brought to them.

While there were a few distinctive dishes, the cuisine was fundamentally not much different from what one would find in the Kingdom of Mireana.

That was only natural. Much of the culture in the Kingdom of Reburina had flowed in from the Kingdom of Mireana.

And naturally, that culture included things like cuisine, ingredients, and seasonings.

Of course, not everything was simply imitations of the Mireana Kingdom; some dishes had evolved uniquely within this country.

Panyan, which Rei particularly enjoyed, was a prime example of this.

Moreover, precisely because the food was familiar, Rei and the others had no trouble eating it; they could enjoy every dish with complete ease.

(When I was in Japan, I remember seeing or hearing something about how when people go to some foreign country, the local food might not agree with them at all, but they can still eat fast food hamburgers... or something like that? Is it like that?)

As he pondered this while eating the lamb skewers, Rei noticed someone approaching their table.

For a moment, Rei thought trouble was brewing again, but upon seeing the man's face—or rather, his expression—she looked puzzled.

He was in his mid-twenties, well-built. Definitely a man who'd undergone some kind of warrior training.

No, if that were all, Rei wouldn't have paid him much mind.

There were plenty of men who were bad-tempered just because they were trained as warriors.

But the man now approaching their table didn't have that kind of look... the kind of vulgar expression someone who thought of Elena and the others as his property might have.

Of course, some people could hide their true, despicable nature behind a carefully crafted facade.

But somehow, Rei could tell the man approaching their table wasn't one of those.

"Excuse me, if it's alright, would you mind if I joined you for dinner?"

That must be why. Under normal circumstances, Rei would have definitely refused such an offer, but he nodded.

Elena and the others also nodded in response to the man's words.

Only Byune, wary that her portion might shrink, wore a slightly displeased look—though she never let that dissatisfaction show.

Seeing the group's reaction, the man bowed slightly and took his seat.

Those around them who had witnessed the scene were stunned. They had assumed any advances would be rejected outright.

"For now, just bring whatever you have."

The man then handed several silver coins to the waiter and made his request.

Receiving a silver coin as a tip, the waiter departed for the kitchen with a pleased look.

Seeing this, those present, including Rei, thought he must be accustomed to such interactions in places like this.

Then again, since he was a guest staying at the Silver Fruit Inn, it was only natural he'd be accustomed to such interactions.

Seeing the man's attitude, Rei already had a rough idea of whose man he was.

That said, there were several candidates, so he hadn't narrowed it down completely.

The most likely possibility was still Jaya.

For Jaya, who considered this his home turf, it wouldn't be strange if he'd obtained information that Rei had gone to Mejogo yesterday.

In that case, the purpose would likely be to establish a connection with Rei.

Next was the messenger from Ryutas, whom Rei had saved today.

Rei, who sensed the presence of someone coming to help Ryutas, could understand that Ryutas was a person with some kind of story.

It wouldn't be strange for him to invite Ray to his house as a thank-you for today's help.

And then... the last one...

"I came on the recommendation of Mr. Unchu."

The man stated this, and Rei realized his final prediction had been correct.
