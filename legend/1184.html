Rei and Vihera fly through the summer sky.

To be precise, it was Set flying through the sky, with Rei straddling his back and Vihera clinging to his front legs... but the two humans and one beast seemed utterly unconcerned about such details as they soared through the air.

Three days had passed since Rei let slip about the race with Mireine—or rather, with the scorching wind—to see who could find Ambris first.

Even with that much time, they still hadn't spotted Ambris.

Of course, the creation of monster swarms hadn't stopped during that time either. Almost every day, new swarms were discovered and then exterminated by adventurers.

Goblins, already numerous to begin with, were particularly common. They were often discovered heading towards Gilm to attack people.

Given the situation, adventurers searching for Ambris were sometimes met with disapproving looks from others, as if to say they should be working harder. This often led to tense atmospheres.

No one gave Rei, famous in Gilm, such looks... Still, as someone assigned to exploration, he too felt it was time to make progress.

But if asked how to find a black mist-like entity, he couldn't offer an immediate answer.

Ultimately, all he could do was fly around Gilm's perimeter with Set, searching visually.

Even so, he couldn't find any sign of Ambris, and time slipped away uselessly.

Today, too, the sun floating in the sky had passed its zenith and was beginning to dip.

"It's almost noon. Shall we eat?"

Rei called out to Vihera, who was clinging to Set's front paws from atop his back.

Fortunately, the ground below was covered in green grass with several trees scattered about, providing ample shade to escape the direct sunlight.

It was a perfectly suitable spot for lunch.

Though, in terms of direct sunlight, it was hardly an issue for Rei and the others flying at an altitude of 100 meters.

"Yeah. I'm starting to get hungry... Let's eat."

Vihera, now completely accustomed to hanging from Set's front legs, announced.

Naturally, Set had no objection whatsoever to Rei and Vihera's suggestion to eat... In fact, he descended to the ground with obvious delight.

Set had always loved the act of eating.

He could enjoy everything from street food to raw monster meat.

And through experience and instinct, he understood that meals tasted even better when people like Rei and Vihera were nearby.

That's precisely why he flapped his wings toward a tree growing on the ground... and just before landing, he released his grip on Vihera's front paw. Both man and beast touched down on the ground without any mishap.

Just as seen from above, no other monsters or animals were visible near the tree.

A small bird rested on a branch, but upon seeing Set approach, it immediately flew away.

Set didn't chase after the little bird—though if it had been a meatier bird, that might have been a different story—and as soon as they entered the shade of the tree, Rei began preparing their meal.

Bread, soup, and skewered meats—ready-to-eat dishes pulled from the Misty Ring—were laid out one after another on the grassy plain.

It was a very satisfying menu for an adventurer's lunch.

That said, even those currently working on the Ambris matter carried decent meals with them, if not quite as elaborate as this lunch.

It wasn't as if they were on assignments that would keep them away from Gilm for days; everyone returned by evening.

That's precisely why they could carry simple meals like sandwiches.

At the very least, it wasn't the meager fare of hardened bread and dried meat they'd have on multi-day quests.

(I really should start showing up for the goblin meat soon too.)

As the two humans and one beast ate, Rei recalled the face of his comrade researching how to make goblin meat delicious.

Naturally, the research on making goblin meat tasty had made almost no progress.

Rei had given him spices, but those spices were only luxuriously usable because Rei had them; buying them normally would cost a fortune.

He recalled something he'd seen or read back in Japan—stories about pepper and gold holding equal value, or wars fought over spices...

Of course, spices aren't that expensive in Gilm, but they're not cheap enough for everyone to buy casually either.

Naturally, if you were to use large amounts of spices on goblin meat, it wouldn't be inedible, but it would become absurdly expensive.

(Not-too-expensive spices... I've heard mint is highly productive. Is it available here too?)

Mint is one of the most famous spices, with many uses.

In Rei's view, it's more of a herb than a spice, but he figured it was acceptable to consider it a spice for now.

Mint is incredibly vigorous and easy to grow.

In fact, it grows so vigorously that sometimes thinning it out becomes more work than actually cultivating it.

When Rei was in Japan, she saw a friend's sister fail to manage her mint plants, resulting in the garden being completely overrun by mint.

Considering there's even a term like "mint terror" out there, you can see just how vigorously mint spreads.

(Goblin meat and mint... I wonder what kind of dish that would make. Just grill it? Steam it? Boil it? Best to leave that to the cook, really. But then there's the problem of how to find the mint.)

Rei had seen mint proliferating all over his friend's garden, and had been given some mint by that friend... well, more like it was practically forced on him, so he knew what mint looked like.

If it were sold somewhere or growing in the wild, he was confident he could recognize it immediately.

Unfortunately, it had been several years since Rei came to Elgin. She hadn't seen mint, nor had she eaten it.

It's unclear whether this was just a coincidence that Rei couldn't find any mint, or if mint simply doesn't exist here, or if mint isn't recognized as a spice or herb at all.

(With Takumu here, you'd think Japanese culture would have spread to some extent. Well, chopsticks haven't caught on either, so maybe they just didn't bother promoting that stuff, or perhaps they tried but it didn't catch on.)

Especially chopsticks—they'd be incredibly awkward for someone used to knives and forks.

In that situation, trying to spread chopsticks might have been doomed to fail due to their inconvenience.

(But I'm talking about mint. ...Well, unless your family runs a farm, lives in the countryside with a big yard, enjoys herbal tea, or has someone who loves cooking, you probably wouldn't see mint normally.)

Incidentally, Rei's friend's older sister apparently got hooked on herbal tea and decided to grow mint in her garden.

Naturally, she couldn't possibly use all the mint that spread all over her garden for herbal tea or cooking. Like Rei, she ended up giving it away to anyone she could find, and finally resorted to making a herbal bath instead of tea to get rid of it.

At the time, Rei thought, "Why not just pull it up and throw it away instead of forcing yourself to use it?" But apparently, as someone who had grown mint herself, she couldn't quite accept that approach.

Anyway, if you could just find some mint, propagating it wasn't that hard. And if it worked out, maybe it could become a new specialty for Gilm? That thought crossed Rei's mind too.

Though, considering how vigorously it spreads, it probably wouldn't take long for other towns and villages to start growing it too.

"Rei, what's wrong? You suddenly got deep in thought."

"Nothing. I mean, I'm lucky to be able to carry delicious food like this around, but I was thinking... ordinary adventurers probably can't eat meals this fancy. Normally, it's just dried meat and hardened bread, stuff like that."

Even though he called it a luxurious meal, these were dishes commonly eaten within Gilm.

But outside the city, they suddenly become meals worthy of being called luxurious.

"...Yeah. When I used to explore dungeons with Byune, that's what we ate too."

"It's a bit late to say this, but how did you put up with that kind of diet?"

Lately, even Rei sometimes forgets, but Vihera is of imperial blood from the Bestir Empire.

Naturally, the cuisine served there was lavish, and Rei honestly wondered how Vihera, accustomed to such fare, could have endured preserved foods like dried meat and hardened bread.

At Rei's words, Vihera smiled as she tore off a piece of the remaining bread and popped it into her mouth.

"Not really. It was easier not having to taste-test everything. No matter how luxurious or delicious a dish is, once it's cold, it's just... well."

"Is that how it is?"

"Yes, that's how it is. ...Hey, Rei. Take a look at that."

Mid-sentence, Vihera's expression suddenly shifted from smiling to tense.

Rei turned to where Vihera was looking and saw a goblin.

That alone wouldn't have been particularly surprising.

Lately, they'd been appearing in large groups, and she saw them so often it was almost boring.

Whenever Rei spotted a goblin pack, he'd cast spells from above while flying over them in his Set, incinerating the entire group.

But the reason Vihera's expression changed upon seeing this particular goblin was immediately clear to Rei.

Because the goblin, stumbling uncertainly across the grassland, was shrouded in black mist.

No, "enveloped" wasn't quite right. It was more accurate to say the remnants of black mist lingered around them, making them appear that way.

Even setting aside that black mist, there was something distinctly off about the goblin.

"...What do you think?"

"Well, we certainly won't let it go. We've been searching high and low for Ambris, so we want any information we can get."

Rei nodded in agreement with Vihera's words.

They quickly stowed the remaining food into the Misty Ring and prepared to pursue the goblin.

Set looked slightly disappointed that the meal had been cut short, but he didn't protest to Rei and Vihera as they pursued clues about Ambris. He simply stood up.

And so, the two humans and one beast set off, not flying through the air, but following the goblin's trail.

They kept a safe distance, advancing so they could respond immediately if anything happened to the goblin.

The goblin continued walking across the grassland with its usual unsteady gait.

"Considering that goblin's condition, it's clearly strange..."

"Yes. The question is, when did that goblin make contact with Ambris? If it was within the last few hours, Ambris should still be somewhere nearby. ...But looking around like this, there's no sign of Ambris, is there?"

Set made a throat-clearing sound at Vihera's question.

Even using senses like sight, smell, and hearing, they couldn't detect any sign of Ambris nearby.

But given his inherent nature, it was only natural that Ambris was difficult to find in the first place. Even if he were nearby, it wouldn't be easy to locate him.

"I'd like to thoroughly search the area and find Ambris if possible... but that seems difficult too."

"Yeah."

"Guruu."

At Rei's words, Set let out a mew that sounded like an apology.

Rei and Vihera both stroked him, telling him not to worry.

But even though he was comforted, Set seemed to feel ashamed that he couldn't find Ambris on his own. Normally, he'd bounce back quickly, but he still hadn't fully recovered.

As they stroked Set's back and walked through the grassland, the goblin ahead of Rei and the others stopped moving. Its steps had been unsteady... you could even call them staggered, like it was drunk.

It wasn't a stop made by its own will, but rather a halt as if someone had pressed a pause button.

It was an unmistakably unnatural halt, and that's precisely why Rei, Vihera, and Set—the two humans and one beast—stared intently at the goblin's state.

In case something happened... specifically, so they could act immediately if an Ambris appeared.

An Ambris, born from magical anomalies, was impervious to physical attacks.

After all, it was only natural since it had no physical form.

Moreover, the phenomenon of the magic anomaly itself was a tangible entity, granting it high magical defense.

The best way to deal with such an Ambris was for Rei, possessing immense magical power, to forcefully break through its magical defense... or so it should have been.

Preparing to act at a moment's notice, ready for anything, he watched the goblin. Suddenly, the black mist surrounding the goblin began to dissipate.

Yes, as if being absorbed into the goblin's body.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!"

The moment all the black mist was absorbed, the goblin let out a roar... and its body was clearly larger than just seconds before.

The Goblin Leader was born.
