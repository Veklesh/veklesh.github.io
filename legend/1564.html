Within that room, multiple lights existed.

It was not the light of candles illuminating the room, but pure light floating in the air.

Suddenly, one of those many lights vanished.

The man inside the room had been writing something on the paper on the desk with single-minded focus, but the instant one of the lights vanished, he looked up as if he had sensed it.

"Hmm? That light... what was it? ...Ah, yes, the black crystal? But why would the black crystal be destroyed? There shouldn't have been anything suspicious about it."

Tilting his head, the man gazed in the direction of the vanished light.

The black crystal the man had created, while possessing various peculiarities, should have been an extremely powerful magic item.

"With that receiver, there should have been no shortage of forces to protect the black crystal. Above all, the crystal itself should have had defensive functions. Normally, anyone trying to destroy it would have their consciousness devoured and died... Was there some incredibly powerful adventurer?"

The man stared at the vanished light, thinking for a moment.

But he quickly shook his head and turned back to the papers on his desk.

"Well, the Black Crystal didn't perform as planned anyway, so breaking it isn't that much of a pity. ...The real problem is this formula. Why are the numbers so low? Theoretically, it should be three times... no, eight times higher."

The vanished light wasn't something the man felt particularly attached to, it seemed.

He refocused on the paper on his desk, muttering to himself, trying this and that.

"...Huh? This..."

Suddenly, Rei realized he had no idea what he was doing.

For some reason, he had activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and was reaching out into empty space.

On Rei's shoulder, Elena had somehow come close and was reaching out her hand—not exactly as a replacement, but still.

「Elena？」

"...Huh!? Rei!?"

"Yeah, as you can see... What's wrong? Didn't I tell you guys not to go down the stairs?"

"Too late. Way too late. ...And Rei, do you even understand what kind of situation you were in?"

Watching Rei's bewildered expression, Elena fixed him with an intense stare.

It was so intense, it felt like if her gaze had physical force, it might burn a hole right through him.

Seeing Elena like this, and noticing Marina, Vihera, and Byune standing behind her, Rei finally understood he'd caused Elena and the others worry.

"Ah... Sorry. I caused you worry."

"Honestly... really, Rei..."

Watching Elena's displeased expression, Rei recalled what he had experienced.

Darkness... Yes, he had been in a place that could only be called a space made entirely of darkness.

(No, my body was here, and Elena could touch me, so it must mean only my consciousness was in that dark space? And then the darkness began to consume me...)

Thinking it through, Rei finally recalled the situation he'd been in.

He had been subjected to some kind of mental attack by the black crystal.

"Sorry. I caused you worry. ...I'll explain."

Saying this, he deactivated the still-active Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and began explaining what he had experienced.

He had been in a space of nothing but darkness, with no light whatsoever.

In that darkness, he couldn't remember anything about himself. Worse, he couldn't even understand his own emotions.

The darkness had tried to consume his body.

Then, suddenly, he heard the voices of Set and Elena, and that was what finally snapped him back to his senses.

And finally, how he had activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour to burn the dark space itself to ashes.

"...And then, when I came to, I was in this situation."

Rei said this while shifting his gaze toward where the black crystal had been.

Normally, the black crystal should have been there... or at least some remnant of it. But there wasn't even a single speck of ash left.

Was it because the black crystal was always meant to be like that, or was it due to the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour?

Rei couldn't tell which it was, but it was undeniable that the black crystal itself had ceased to exist.

"The black crystal being gone... well, in hindsight, it probably worked out for the best."

Rei murmured thoughtfully.

Having experienced having his mind captured within that dark void, he couldn't possibly consider bringing the black crystal back himself.

He had considered using it as material for some magic item, but he couldn't imagine creating anything reliable from it.

"I suppose so. But... honestly, I never imagined something like this existed. I'm terribly curious who made it."

"I suppose so. The chances are slim, but if this black crystal were something that could be mass-produced, it could lead to all sorts of trouble."

Marina's words made Rei imagine scenes of entertainment districts similar to Mejogo springing up in various places.

Of course, Mejogo was ultimately a pleasure district created by the people of Jaya and Reburina to maximize the black crystal's potential.

Even if other factions obtained black crystal, they wouldn't necessarily use it in exactly the same way.

"Come to think of it, this black crystal was the cause of the giants, right? So, since Rei destroyed it, shouldn't we be safe now?"

"...Yeah. I think it'll be fine from now on, but honestly, I can't imagine what will happen to the pregnant women who were on the upper floors."

Rei hoped they could be saved, but he could easily imagine the chances were slim.

Elena gently placed her hand on Rei's shoulder.

"Even so, we've prevented any more casualties. You should be proud of that."

Elena spoke not to console Rei, but to state the truth.

Elena's words seemed to lift Rei's spirits somewhat. He gave a small nod before looking once more at the spot where the black crystal had been.

"Anyway, we can safely say the loss of the black crystal is a major blow to Jaya, right? And this underground facility took a serious hit too."

"True. I doubt Daskar would complain if he heard about this incident. ...But this black crystal business isn't just about the organization Jaya. There's also the matter of Rezell."

The name Resel slipped from Marina's lips.

She died the instant she tried to say something, so I never got the full details, but I did hear she was sent here on the Chancellor's orders.

In other words, it was highly likely that Jaya was part of a state-run organization.

No, more than just part of it—it couldn't be ruled out that it was actually a shadow organization run by the state itself.

Having attacked such a state organization, it was only natural that Rei and the others were enemies the upper echelons of the Reburina Kingdom couldn't let escape.

If they let Rei and the others escape now, their plans would be exposed.

...Though, in reality, they'd already informed Daskar of the general situation via the Paired Orb. So even if they somehow silenced Rei and the others here, the outcome wouldn't change.

"How Reburina will respond... Hmm. At the very least, the Chancellor is confirmed as an enemy, right?"

The hint of amusement in Vihera's tone as he said this likely stemmed from the prospect of finally facing a formidable foe.

(I don't think the Kingdom of Reburina has any powerful enemies, though. Giants? They probably have tons of those.)

Because they all look the same, fighting such giants inevitably felt somewhat unreal.

Still, that was merely Rei's perception. The fact that Vihera seemed pleased to Rei meant Vihera likely felt differently.

Considering what happened when they fought a giant horde on the fourth underground floor, that much was obvious.

"Well, whatever they do... I don't think we can do anything about it right away."

"Why?"

"The black crystal. The giants were only created because of the black crystal. Got that?"

Hearing Rei's words, not only Elena voiced her confusion—"Why?"—but the others nodded as well.

Feeling strangely like he'd become a teacher, Rei continued.

"Since black crystal was used to create the giants, don't you think it's also needed to command them? Especially since, when we first peeked into the fifth basement level, the giants were huddled around the black crystal, right?"

Seeing that, Rei somehow recalled the word "charging."

Giants could probably eat things, but just how much food would be needed to sustain those massive bodies... well, that was obvious.

One or two giants might be manageable, but there were probably hundreds... no, likely thousands, perhaps even over a thousand giants.

Filling the stomachs of that many giants couldn't be a simple task.

But... if what they were doing huddled around that black crystal was actually something like charging...

Of course, since the other giants weren't here, it was certain that not all of them could receive that recharge.

(Huh? If that's the case, am I overthinking the food situation? Maybe they actually have small appetites despite appearances... or something?)

Rei pondered these questions, but regardless of the food issue, he was certain the black crystal played a significant role in freely controlling the giants.

"Right. Since the black crystal created the Titans, it makes sense that you'd need to use it to control them. Of course, there could be another magic item specifically for that purpose."

Marina murmured in agreement after hearing Rei's words.

"Anyway, there's nothing left for us to do here. Shouldn't we get out of here soon?"

Vihera announced while surveying the fifth basement level.

Indeed, the fifth basement level seemed to contain nothing but the black crystal itself. Beyond that, it resembled a massive, unfinished underground chamber.

Given that, and considering the black crystal itself had been obliterated by the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour that Rei had used, it was clear that searching for anything here was pointless.

Furthermore, the fourth basement level was a single vast chamber, and the third basement level had been completely scorched by Rei's magic, leaving only the charred remains of the giant child and its mother... calling them remains was almost pitiful.

(That leaves only the pregnant woman on the second basement level and the room on the first basement level to investigate... No, wait.)

Thinking this, Rei recalled the word "shipment" once more.

"Since they mentioned shipping the giants, I think there's probably a hidden passage leading outside somewhere on either the fifth basement level or the fourth basement level... What do you think?"

"There definitely is one, I suppose. But finding it might be difficult, don't you think?"

They couldn't very well have a large number of giants moving openly through Mejogo.

And it's obvious that just getting the huge number of giants gathered on the fourth or fifth basement level out onto the surface would be a huge hassle.

So, there's definitely some kind of mechanism here.

"Should I look for it?"

Rei possesses a search spell called 'Thin Flame'.

Using that, finding hidden passages or the like shouldn't be too difficult unless something extraordinary is at play.

...Though, the risk remains that the spreading flames could cause panic among those unaware of the situation.

Fortunately, only Rei and his companions were present here.

If they knew beforehand that this was the nature of the spell, they should have been able to handle it however they wanted.

But it was Byune who put a stop to that.

"Hmph!"

Exploring hidden passages was, without a doubt, a thief's job.

For Byune, who felt she'd been of little use during the exploration of this underground facility, the honest truth was that if she didn't do it here, who else would?

If Rei and the others were truly in a hurry, they wouldn't have left it to Byune; Rei would have used magic to find the hidden passage immediately.

But there was no particular reason to rush now.
