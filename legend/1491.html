The moment he saw that man... and his companions, Gestal realized he was already in a hopeless situation.

If only Rei (the man) had been here, then maybe... just maybe, if everyone had scattered in different directions at once, they might have managed to escape.

But now that he understood Rei led the Crimson Wings and had several other reinforcements, Gestal knew the situation was extremely dire.

...At that moment, Gestal's only stroke of luck was that he hadn't recognized the reinforcements as Elena, nicknamed the Princess General, and her subordinates.

With only moonlight for illumination, and the overwhelming shock of encountering Rei, he likely failed to recognize Elena as Elena.

Including Ara, he could only perceive them as Rei's helpers, without knowing who they were.

"Rebejeff! What the hell is going on here?!"

That's precisely why Gestal shouted at his business partner.

Why was someone like Rei here?

Such an entity should not normally be found in a place like this.

Yet, since the reason Rei was here couldn't be them, it had to be the Rebejefs.

But... even when Jestal fixed his gaze on them, the Rebejevs showed no sign of concern whatsoever, merely exchanging glances amongst themselves.

"You, just shut up for a bit. The guards should be here soon. When they arrive, we'll have you spill everything you know."

"Guh—"

Rei, met with that gaze and those words, was rendered speechless.

Jestal was a man who had survived in Gilm's underworld and climbed to the top of the organization.

His nerve was certainly no ordinary thing, but even so, when Rei fixed him with that stare and told him to shut up, he couldn't bring himself to move his mouth any further.

Like a frog frozen by a snake's stare.

...Elgin did have frog monsters that fed on snakes, but unfortunately, Gestal couldn't become such a creature.

Then again, in Rei's case, it wasn't a cute little snake; it was closer to something you'd call a dragon.

In any case, having silenced Gestal with a single glare, Rei turned his gaze back to Ajax.

Two men stood near Ajax. From a distance, he'd recognized them as Ajax's companions, Levjef and Huston, the pair he'd heard about from Dahl.

"Long time no see... though maybe not that long. We just met earlier today. More like a few hours ago, to be precise."

"...Yeah. I'd rather not have seen you again."

Ajax's attitude toward Rei was different from when they'd met in Treant Forest.

That was likely because he no longer needed to hide his true identity... his true nature.

While conversing with Ajax, Rei watched as the intelligence unit members surrounded and captured Jestal's subordinates one after another.

Naturally, Gestal's men tried to resist, but when Set let out a growl that echoed around them, his subordinates became completely helpless.

In truth, the mere presence of the Crimson Wings had already crushed the will to resist in many of them.

They reasoned that it was better to avoid being beaten for foolish resistance here, only to face heavier charges later during interrogation for having resisted...

Naturally, the carriage carrying dozens of women wearing slave collars was also secured by the intelligence unit.

Seeing that... seeing their target carriage already secured, Ajax and the others wouldn't even think of trying to reclaim it.

For now, the absolute priority was to escape this place and flee from Gilm.

Originally, they had planned to rely on Jestal's help to escape Gilm, but with things as they were now, that was no longer possible.

Worse still, if they clung to Jestal here, they'd get captured too.

"Is that so? I was looking forward to seeing you. Still, I was surprised you vanished from Treant Forest without a word! If you had some reason to return, why didn't you tell us anything?"

"No, it was just something urgent that came up."

"Huh. So that business created this situation?"

Even while talking to Rei, Ajax kept darting glances around, trying desperately to find a way to escape.

But Rei and the others weren't about to let him get away with that. They just waited, watching for an opening.

Ready to pounce the instant Ajax or the others made any suspicious move.

"Nah, nah, that's beside the point. My business is mostly done, so I'd rather just head back now, if possible."

"...Is that so? Well, I'd prefer you stay a little longer and give me a proper explanation, though."

"I can't very well make Rei, the one with the alias, go through all that trouble, can I?"

"No, there are people who want to hear your story, so I really need you to stay here."

"Is that absolutely necessary?"

"Yes, absolutely. There's a lot I genuinely want to ask you, and besides me, there are others who have business with you. In fact, you could say that's the main reason."

Hearing Rei say that, Ajax must have immediately understood who he was referring to.

Ajax wore a look of disgust... pure, deep-seated disgust.

"Why would a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname get involved with someone like that? Normally, wouldn't you take on jobs with higher rewards? Like Gilm's expansion project?"

"Don't worry, I'm handling that properly too."

In truth, while helping Ilse with her revenge, Rei was also diligently working on the Gilm expansion project.

Though, since most of it involved transporting cargo using Misty Ring, it wasn't particularly time-consuming.

"...I see. You seem to be working quite hard, but wouldn't it be better to take things a bit easier?"

"Don't worry about that. I've got it under control without needing you to tell me. ...Well, we can't keep talking like this forever."

Seeing that the intelligence unit had finished binding Gestal and most of his subordinates, Rei announced.

"No, no, let's talk a bit longer. Considering the coincidence of meeting here, don't you think we should talk properly?"

"Coincidence, huh. Unfortunately, we don't have much time left... Huh?"

There's no time left, so we should wrap this up.

That's what Rei was about to say, but suddenly, a voice reached his ears.

Well, in terms of voices, many people around them were talking.

Gestal and his subordinates were all talking, but this wasn't that.

It was a roar, like someone about to charge into battle.

"What is it?"

Looking in the direction the voice came from, he saw a familiar figure running toward them, a longsword in hand.

It was Melan.

Seeing her running straight toward them for some reason, they were puzzled.

"Why is Melan...?"

"Huh?"

At Rei's words, Vihera let out a startled gasp.

Since Vihera had spent the most time with Melan while patrolling Gilm together, it was only natural she reacted to his name.

And it wasn't just Rei and Vihera who turned their gaze toward Melan's arrival. The members of the intelligence unit surrounding them did the same.

With their own lives on the line, there was no way Ajax and the others would overlook such a decisive opening.

Moreover, the fact that Ajax and his men didn't know Melan became the trigger for them to move.

If it had been Rei and the other members of Crimson Wings, or Elena and Ara, the Ajax would have been able to handle them even if they fled towards them.

But Ajax and his men knew Rei and his allies were formidable opponents, so they wouldn't simply head straight for them.

Instead, they headed toward the direction of the intelligence unit.

On top of that, the three of them had started moving simultaneously without any signal.

Hastun left the backpack full of supplies he'd been carrying behind, lightening his load for action.

What's more, all three moved without making a sound.

Rei was slightly curious how they communicated in that situation, but moving quickly was more important now.

"Flying Slash!"

He swung his Death Scythe wide.

The slash flew straight through the air... severing the right knee of Huston, who was running at the rear of the trio.

Hastun dropped his backpack and fled, but the extra movement still cost him time, leaving him slightly behind the other two.

That was the primary reason he ended up running last and became the target of Rei's Flying Slash.

"Whoa!"

Despite having his right knee severed, the sound that escaped Huston's mouth was oddly hollow.

The sharpness of the Flying Slash likely meant he hadn't yet fully registered the pain of his severed knee.

Furthermore, the intense tension gripping him in this situation was also numbing the pain.

"Terrain Manipulation!"

He immediately unleashed Terrain Manipulation.

As he thrust the stone tip of the Death Scythe into the ground to activate the skill, the movement of Ajax, who had been leading the charge, slowed.

It was only natural. A one-meter wall had suddenly materialized in the direction he was running.

...It was a spur-of-the-moment action, and several ruins collapsed as the wall appeared, but Rei didn't seem to care.

A one-meter wall? For an adventurer like Ajax, jumping over it wouldn't be difficult.

But that was only if they knew the wall was there and had prepared accordingly.

The wall that suddenly appeared before him—not exactly right in front, but close enough—made Ajax momentarily slow his running pace.

Normally, that slight hesitation wouldn't have been a problem, but Levjef was running right behind Ajax.

When Ajax's speed suddenly dropped right in front of him, Levjef collided with his back... lost his balance, and tumbled over, dragging Ajax down with him.

"Guh!"

"Hey!"

Ajax, too, had been focused solely on the dirt wall ahead, so he likely never expected Levegef to crash into him.

But obviously, the two couldn't just stay down like that.

They needed to escape this place immediately. That was their judgment... and suddenly, Ajax noticed something odd.

There were only two of them here: himself and Levjef, who had collided with his back.

This was good.

But... what about their other companion, Haston?

If he had been running with them, shouldn't he have already passed them by now? Thinking this... Ajax turned around.

What came into Ajax's view was Huston, somehow still on the ground, desperately swinging his longsword to hold off the intelligence unit members closing in on him.

For a moment, he wondered why he wasn't getting up, but blood flowed around Huston... and seeing his right leg, severed from the knee down, lying a short distance away, the reason became clear.

"Huston!?"

At Ajax's cry, Levjef also looked behind him.

And what he saw... was the same sight as Ajax.

Ajax, Levedev, and Huston knew they were the villains.

Precisely because they knew this, they cherished these three as comrades they would never betray.

And so, seeing one of those comrades fighting with their life on the line to let them escape, it was only natural their movements froze.

"What are you doing? Run!"

Noticing the two of them, Huston shouted while swinging his longsword.

But... it was already too late.

With just a few more seconds, it wouldn't have been difficult for Rei, Elena, Vihera, and Set to catch up to Ajax and the others.

Smoothly, the blade of Rei's Death Scythe touched the back of Ajax's neck.

It was midsummer, and today was hot enough to be called a tropical night, yet the blade touching Ajax's neck felt as cold as ice.

That coldness left Ajax speechless, unable to utter a word.

It wasn't just Ajax who couldn't move. Levjef was in the same predicament.

He had the tip of the Twilight Spear pressed against his face. It was clear that if he moved even slightly, that spear tip would easily pierce—no, shatter—his skull.

Holding both weapons in his hands, immobilizing the two, Rei opened his mouth with a smile that could only be called a sneer.

"There's no need to rush, is there? Take your time. ...Yes, take your time."
