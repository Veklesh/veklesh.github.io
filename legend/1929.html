"I came here to tear down the mud wall."

When told this, Rei didn’t seem particularly surprised.

To begin with, just by looking at the men in red robes standing before him, it wasn’t hard to guess that they had come to tear down the earthen wall.

The fact that most of them were wielding axes like those used by woodcutters, rather than weapons, made it easy to imagine they’d come to demolish something.

“I see, the earthen wall, huh? But let me tell you something… The earthen wall I built isn’t just an ordinary one—it’s something made with magic. Frankly, there’s no way you’ll be able to break it just by trying a little bit. In fact, even if a Kobold scratched at it, it wouldn’t even leave a scratch.”

“Is that so…”

Even though they were talking about destroying the earthen wall, the men in red cloth were surprised that Rei wasn’t angry.

They had been certain they’d be beaten to a pulp right there on the spot… or, depending on the situation, that losing a limb or two wouldn’t be out of the question.

“Hey! Why aren’t you mad?!”

Instead, it was a group of about ten men who got angry on Rei’s behalf—the same men who had complained when Rei went to inspect the earthen wall.

“Well, there’s no point in asking why. There’s no doubt that a bunch of nobodies like you couldn’t possibly destroy this earthen wall… and if it gets destroyed for some reason, I’ll just build another one.”

He stated this matter-of-factly.

It was as if he were saying that if a sand pile lying around got destroyed, you’d just gather more sand and build another one.

The ones left speechless by those words weren’t the men in red cloth… but the group who had actually seen the earthen wall with their own eyes.

That earthen wall, which completely sealed off the inside of Gilm from the outside, looked like something that would be quite a struggle to build, even to the untrained eye.

That’s precisely why they probably didn’t expect to hear such words come out of Rei’s mouth.

That said, actually building an earthen wall isn’t all that difficult if you use a Death Scythe terrain manipulation skill.

It does require some effort to soak it with water and freeze it to reinforce its sturdiness, but even that could be done immediately if they called on Marina.

……Normally, hiring a spirit mage of Marina’s caliber would cost a pretty penny… but since she’s a party member, she’d do it as a favor.

In any case, with just Rei and Marina, the earthen wall would be finished in less than an hour.

It was precisely because he knew that Rei could say it so casually… but to the men who had no idea how Rei actually built the earthen wall, their honest reaction was, “What the hell is this guy talking about?”

“Well, anyway…”

A few of them tried to say something, but Rei cut them off... slowly letting a murderous intent seep through his expression.

It wasn’t as if they had actually noticed that killing intent, but the men in red cloth fell silent, as if overwhelmed by Rei’s gaze.

“Even if it can be fixed right away, there’s no doubt that getting things destroyed isn’t exactly a pleasant feeling. That said, putting that aside for now, if you’re part of the Red Cloth, there’s actually something I’ve been wanting to ask you.”

Faced with Rei’s attitude, the men in red cloth instinctively took a few steps back.

They couldn’t sense the killing intent emanating from Rei, but they were likely still overwhelmed by an inexplicable sense of pressure.

“W-what is it?”

“Judging by the fact that you were trying to break through the earthen wall, you must have some connection to the Kobold incident. For example, perhaps you’re using some kind of magic item to summon Kobolds to Gilm?”

The idea that there was a magical item capable of summoning Kobolds was, strictly speaking, just Rei’s guess… or rather, it would be better to call it a hunch or wishful thinking.

Rei had thought it was unlikely, but now that the Red Cloth men had actually appeared, the situation was different.

Regardless of the magic item theory, the likelihood that the Red Cloth people were involved in the Kobold incident had become very high.

But... the words that came out of the Red Cloth man’s mouth when Rei asked him were unexpected.

“Huh? The Kobold incident… Why me?”

If he had been trying to cover something up, Rei would have seen right through it.

Even if he were a skilled liar and managed to deceive Rei’s gaze, it was impossible that every single person in red cloth present here was the same.

Yet, in response to the man’s words—which seemed to suggest he didn’t understand what was going on—every single Red Cloth member present wore the same expression.

It wasn’t a joke or anything of the sort; it was proof that they had absolutely no idea what Rei was talking about.

(Huh? Wait... Is that really not the case? No, but then why did these guys come here to break down the earthen wall?)

Where Rei’s gaze fell, the Red Cloth men exchanged glances with one another.

They didn’t understand what Rei was saying, but judging by the current situation, they decided it would be best not to say anything rash.

Faced with those glances, Rei pondered, filled with doubt.

(These guys aren’t involved in the Kobold incident? But then, if that’s the case… No, wait. Come to think of it, they said they didn’t come here of their own accord—they said they were told to come here to break down the earthen wall. In other words…)

The pressure emanating from Rei’s gaze intensified even further, and he spoke up.

“You guys came here to break down the earthen wall because someone told you to, right? Who told you to do it?”

Back to that.

It’s clear from their current demeanor that the men in red cloth aren’t involved in the Kobold incident.

So, what about the person who told them to break down the earthen wall?

Since they went out of their way to order the earthen wall to be destroyed, there must absolutely be some reason for it.

Naturally, it wouldn’t be strange to assume they’re involved in the Kobold incident.

(And if the people here haven’t been told anything, that means they’re just pawns. …Though it’s highly likely that the Red Cloth men were pawns to begin with.)

With that thought in mind, I wait for their response, but the men in red cloth show no sign of commenting on Rei’s words.

A few of them exchange glances, but that’s all.

Their demeanor seems to be asking, “What are we supposed to do?” and Rei shows a hint of irritation at their attitude.

“What’s the matter? Can’t you even tell me who sent you? …Let me make this clear: your future depends on your answer. Act with that in mind.”

Rei said this, but since he hadn’t actually witnessed them destroying the earthen wall, he had no right to do anything to the men in red cloth.

At best, he could hand them over to the guards as former Red Cloth men.

However, the men in red cloth knew Rei’s character well, and now they were feeling a strange sense of pressure from her.

In reality, it was a faint killing intent, but none of the Red Cloth men possessed the strength to understand that.

“W-wait a minute…”

“Hey, wait, is it okay to say that?!”

One of the men in red cloth, unable to withstand the tension in Rei’s presence, was about to speak up, but the man standing next to him said, looking flustered.

It was obvious that the person who had hired them was no ordinary person, so it was only natural to worry that speaking up now might lead to dire consequences later.

But for the man who had tried to speak up, if asked which was more terrifying—the person who might seek revenge in the future, or Rei standing right in front of him now—it was overwhelmingly the latter.

And above all...

“The ones who hired us and gave us orders were just errand boys or something like that. I’ve never seen the people who actually hired us! And… our comrades have disappeared without us even noticing…”

The man’s words—especially the part about his comrades vanishing—must have struck a chord with the others.

Even those who had been glaring at the man who spoke first fell silent.

“Our comrades disappearing? Don’t you mean they were simply captured by the guards, or decided they couldn’t stick around with you guys any longer and vanished from your sight?”

“Y-yeah. We were talking just fine as recently as yesterday, but before we knew it, they were gone. That’s happened a bunch of times.”

The expression on the man’s face as he desperately explained this didn’t seem like he was lying or making things up.

It was clear he was speaking from the bottom of his heart.

(People vanishing, huh. …To begin with, I don’t even know how this Red Cloth group has managed to survive until now, but from what I’ve heard, did they all live together? Well, I suppose that’s not entirely impossible.)

Even if each incident was a minor disturbance, they’ve been causing them almost every day… no, even more frequently than that.

Given that, even if they wore highly conspicuous red cloths, it wouldn’t be strange for people to look at something other than those red cloths.

In fact, the man who voiced his complaints to Rei in front of the earthen wall remembered the face of the person wearing the red cloth.

Given that, there’s no doubt the people in red cloth couldn’t just wander around as they pleased.

Especially in Gilm right now, since it’s winter, there aren’t people coming from other towns or going to other towns… well, not exactly, but still, the flow of people is inevitably lighter.

Even so, due to the expansion construction, there are more people than usual, but even so, there must have been someone who noticed the Red Cloth people.

“I see. So, that means you guys were living together in a group to some extent. In that case, how did you handle food and things like that? Did the guy who hired you bring that too?”

“That’s right.”

At this point, they must have realized that any half-hearted excuses would be useless.

Among the men in red cloth, the one who first tried to confess to Rei spoke up, and no one tried to stop him.

“So, is the guy who brought the food not here? He might understand the situation a little better, don’t you think?”

Rei asked, but the man in the red cloth shook his head to deny it.

“There is someone like that, but he’s not here. We didn’t all come together, after all.”

“Then who’s leading you guys?”

Among the Red Cloth, there are few who would go out of their way to accommodate others; most are strong-willed.

Naturally, when such people gather, fights tend to break out, but there was no sign of that now.

...Though, perhaps they’d already gotten into an argument with the group Rei encountered in front of the earthen wall before things could escalate to a full-blown fight.

In any case, in response to Rei’s question—that there must be someone acting as a leader—the Red Cloth members, including the man they’d been talking to, all turned their gaze toward a single man.

To Rei’s surprise, it was a man who didn’t particularly stand out among the Red Cloths.

But when he realized that the eyes of those around him—and Rei’s gaze—were fixed on him, he took a step forward.

Strangely enough, that single step seemed to amplify the man’s presence.

In fact, the others around him instinctively shifted aside to make way for him.

“Nice to meet you. I’m Granger, and I’m the one leading this group, for the time being.”

Rei could see that the way he bowed politely was clearly different from the others.

“Just to clarify, are you a member of the Red Cloth? Or are you one of those who employs the Red Cloth?”

“Well… honestly, that’s a difficult question. Specifically, I suppose I’m somewhere in between.”

“In between?” Rei wondered, but he looked at Granger, urging him to elaborate.

Granje, too, must have realized that if he made a misstep here, Rei would capture him… no, depending on the situation, he might even suffer a far worse fate.

He continued his explanation without any attempt to hide anything.

“Let me make this clear first: I am not a member of the Red Cloth. I was hired specifically for this incident to ensure these people do their jobs properly.”

Seeing Granger say this, there was something about his words that made it seem slightly plausible.

As far as Rei knew, everyone in the Red Cloth had a rough-and-tumble personality, and none of them spoke like this.

Of course, since he didn’t know every single member of the Red Cloth, it was possible that such a person might exist within their ranks.

However, Rei was the only one convinced by Granger’s words; the other Red Cloth men looked at him with disbelief.

(None of them had ever seen him, yet no one had ever mentioned it before. Does that mean, even though the Red Cloth men were being sheltered by their employer, they weren’t actually gathered in one place?)

As Rei pondered this, the man continued to smile, seemingly unconcerned with his surroundings.
