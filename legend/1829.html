The day after hearing the story of the Black Wolf… that is, on the last day of the year, Rei was spending the evening with the staff at the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion.

Since Rei had arrived in Anesis, he had usually eaten his meals with Elena and her family… but on this day at the end of the year, everyone from the Duke of Kerebel’s household who was available gathered in the great hall to enjoy the meal together.

“Honestly, I’m surprised. I was under the impression that nobles never ate with their servants.”

“Hehe, that’s true. That’s usually how it is. But just for today, at the end of the year, Master is hosting this party to thank us for getting through another year safely.”

Miranda, standing beside Rei, replied with a delighted smile.

(A reward, huh. Is it like a year-end party? Judging by the way it looks, I don’t think I’m too far off.)

Rei looked around and thought to himself, but the partygoers were all talking quite noisily, unlike their usual selves.

“I didn’t realize there were this many people in this mansion.”

“Of course. To properly maintain this mansion and keep it as a place befitting the Duke of Kerebel’s residence, we need this many people.”

Miranda said this, but even with over a hundred servants, the only reason the mansion is being maintained so well right now is because everyone working here at the Duke of Kerebel’s residence is a veteran.

If the staff’s skills were lacking, they’d need several times this many people just to keep things running smoothly.

Rei didn’t seem to have noticed any of these circumstances, but since it was a special party after all, he reached for some food nearby.

As befits a party akin to a year-end gathering, the atmosphere wasn’t overly formal.

On the numerous tables, a variety of dishes prepared by Georgima and the other chefs were laid out, and many guests were enjoying them.

Since it was a standing reception, everyone was chatting and laughing casually with their friends.

Since this was a party to thank the servants, members of the Duke of Kerebel’s family—such as Liberte, Arcadia, and Elena—were also in attendance. However, perhaps due to their status, they did not join in the revelry with the servants.

While the servants are at the standing reception, only Liberte and her group are seated at a table in a separate area, elegantly enjoying their meal.

What’s different from usual is that they’re dining at that table with what appear to be the higher-ranking members of the staff.

Rei watched them eating with a somewhat formal air, yet looking very happy, and Miranda smiled at him.

“Those whom Master deems to have worked the hardest this past year are allowed to dine together like that. …That’s why the competition is fierce, though.”

Hearing Miranda’s explanation, Rei found it convincing, yet he felt a bit uneasy knowing that, despite not having done anything special himself, he was dining at the same table with them almost every day.

Then again, from Liberte’s perspective, he was practically her daughter’s boyfriend, so it was only natural for her to want to dine with him.

...Though things might have been different if Rei were just an anonymous adventurer—one of the many you’d find anywhere—rather than an adventurer with a reputation.

For better or worse, Rei had made a name for himself over the past few years.

As a result, many now view him as Daskar’s right-hand man.

Rei himself has absolutely no such intention, but since he does, in fact, receive various requests from Daskar, he figures it can’t be helped if people see him that way. He also thinks that if it means fewer pointless hassles from the nobility, then so be it, so he doesn’t particularly deny it.

“Hmm. It’s a feeling I don’t quite get, but judging by the looks of it, there’s no doubt the person dining with Duke Kerebel is delighted.”

To be precise, “proud” might be a more accurate description than “happy.”

As for Rei, as he’d said, it was a feeling he couldn’t quite grasp, but he figured there was no need to say anything that might dampen the other person’s spirits, so he stopped himself from saying anything more on the matter.

Miranda, too, as a capable maid, generally understood what Rei was thinking, and deciding it would be best to avoid the topic any further, she steered the conversation toward Set.

With that, Rei saw no need to hold back, so he began telling amusing stories about Set.

“Hehe, is that really true?”

“Yeah. There are quite a few people who dote on… or admire Set, but Milein and Johanna are in a league of their own. Milein, in particular, was seen as a skilled, beautiful, and capable female adventurer before she got involved with Set… but now, ‘Set-lover’ has been added to that description.”

That said, it’s not all bad.

To begin with, if you were judging by looks alone, it wouldn’t be wrong to call Milein a “cool beauty.”

Because of that, many people used to think she was unapproachable... but as word of her interactions with Set spread, the way people around her viewed her began to change.

She came to be seen as someone easy to talk to.

Of course, there were still some who treated her with contempt, but even if she was a Set fan, that didn’t mean her skills had diminished.

As a result, those who acted foolishly ended up regretting it.

“She seems like someone I’d get along with, somehow.”

“I suppose so. Since we both like Set, I think Milein will be friendly toward you too. …Though that’s different when it comes to Johanna.”

As Rei and Miranda were talking, Rei noticed someone approaching them.

That said, it wasn’t exactly a stranger.

It was Leodanis, who was probably the closest friend among the Duke of Kerebel’s Knights.

However, Leodanis was currently glaring at Rei with a look of utter dissatisfaction.

Not understanding why he was being looked at that way, Rei decided to speak up just to see what was going on.

“What’s up, Leodanis? Do you need something from me?”

“……No, that’s not it. I just wanted to talk to Miranda for a bit. But then I saw you were with Rei.”

“Ah… I see.”

Seeing Leodanis’s attitude, Rei understood why he was in a bad mood.

To Leodanis, Miranda is someone he has feelings for.

Rei also understands that those feelings aren’t just those of friends, but romantic ones.

(Jealousy?)

Yes, seeing Miranda chatting happily with Rei must have made Leodanis feel jealous.

Even Rei, who isn’t very attuned to such emotions, can understand what Leodanis is thinking.

Then, Rei turns to Miranda and speaks.

“I’m good here, so you should go hang out with Leodanis at the party.”

“Is that okay?”

Miranda’s job is, after all, under Rei’s supervision.

If something strange were to happen while Rei wasn’t there, it could lead to all sorts of trouble.

As it is, from Miranda’s perspective, Rei doesn’t seem to possess the common sense expected of a noble.

That said, since Rei isn’t actually a noble, it wouldn’t be strange if he didn’t have those manners.

“Yeah. I doubt anyone at this party would try anything weird on me…”

Rei’s position is that of a guest of Liberte, the Duke of Kerebel.

If anyone were to pull a foolish stunt on someone like Rei, they might literally lose their head—not just figuratively.

Rei couldn’t imagine anyone serving the Duke of Kerebel’s household doing such a thing.

(Well, there’s a chance the Black Wolf might try something… No, why not take the initiative and invite him myself? I don’t know if the Black Wolf will take me up on it, but it’s better to try than not to.)

It was just a sudden whim, but he didn’t want to keep putting off dealing with the Black Wolf, that source of unease, any longer.

If he could settle this right here, that would be best.

Having made that decision... Rei turned to Miranda and spoke.

“Look, I’ll be fine here, so go check out the party with Leodanis. Georgima and the others have prepared all sorts of elaborate dishes, so there’s no way you can pass that up, right?”

“Huh? Oh, okay. …I understand. Well, I’ll take you up on that. Shall we go, Leodanis?”

“Yeah. …Are you really sure it’s okay?”

“No problem. Have fun.”

Prompted by Miranda, Leodanis checks with Rei, though he seems to be doing so just to be on the safe side.

Perhaps Leodanis had somehow sensed Rei’s thoughts or intentions, but it was merely a subconscious feeling, and in the end, he obediently followed Rei’s words.

“Got it. I’ll be off then.”

Though he harbored some doubts about Rei’s attitude, Leodanis eventually walked away from Rei alongside Miranda.

Leodanis glanced back several times, seemingly concerned about Rei, but eventually he couldn’t resist the temptation to enjoy the party with the person he had feelings for, and he focused his attention on her.

Rei watched the two of them… then suddenly felt a gaze and turned to look.

That said, it wasn’t the eerie gaze of a black wolf, but a look of concern… It was Elena’s, who was dining with Liberte and Arcadia.

Rei, of course, knew exactly what Elena was worried about.

After all, it was Elena who had told Rei about the Black Wolf in the first place, so it wasn’t surprising that she would anticipate his actions.

Nodding to Elena, who was looking at him with concern, to let her know he was fine, Rei quietly slipped away from the party.

“Well, will it be a demon or a snake? …Since it’s the Black Wolf, maybe ‘a wolf’ would be a more fitting expression?”

Saying this, Rei looked up at the night sky from a balcony a short distance away from the party venue.

From inside the building, he could faintly hear cheers coming from the party hall, and even what sounded like screams—though he wasn’t sure exactly what had happened.

That said, those screams weren’t genuine cries of distress; they sounded more like people were amused by the situation, so Rei didn’t feel any particular need to go to their aid.

Since he was out on the balcony on a winter night, it wouldn’t be strange if he felt a bone-chilling cold.

But since Rei was wearing a Dragon Robe equipped with a basic air conditioning function, he didn’t need to worry about that.

In the crisp winter air, he gazed intently at the starry sky, which looked even more beautiful than usual... While doing so, Rei waited for the Black Wolf to make an appearance.

If Rei’s description of the Black Wolf’s strength was true, it wouldn’t be surprising for it to appear here even in a situation like this.

That is precisely why he is waiting here for the Black Wolf to appear.

“Still, it’s the end of the year. The Black Wolf might be busy preparing for the New Year, too. …Heh.”

Even as he said it aloud, Rei couldn’t help but laugh.

It was because Rei couldn’t imagine at all the figure he had seen before—the one he believed to be the Black Wolf—going about his daily life.

And the sight of him doing things like preparing for the end of the year was even harder to imagine than his daily life.

(Making New Year’s Eve soba… no, wait, there isn’t even soba here, and I don’t think they have that kind of custom.)

As far as Rei knew, there were no dishes in this world of Elgin that were traditionally eaten on New Year’s Eve, like New Year’s Eve soba.

To be precise, there might be some, so perhaps I should say that, as far as Rei knows… in places like Gilm and Anesis, there are no such dishes.

Instead, it’s not uncommon for New Year’s parties like this to be held.

(There’s a New Year’s Eve party, and then a New Year’s party… That sounds like a lot of work. Especially for Georgima and the others who have to prepare the food—they probably don’t have the time to be researching ramen.)

It was just as Rei was thinking that. Rei felt the same gaze he’d felt before… an emotionless, observational gaze.

“...You’re here. Come out.”

Perhaps hearing Rei’s muttered voice, a presence suddenly appeared on the roof of the balcony.
