After leaving the guild, he made the rounds to see all his acquaintances... Once that was done, he wandered the streets with Vihera , snacking on various things from street stalls—though Vihera thought of it as a date—and eventually returned to the inn that day.

Naturally, since Rei had returned to Gilm, the lodging for the night was the Dusk Wheat inn.

It was a high-class inn, but it posed no problem for Rei's finances whatsoever. The same was true for Vihera, who had taken on monster-slaying requests while waiting for Rei's return.

The food was exceptionally delicious, and true to its reputation as a high-class inn, magic items kept the rooms cool, and the dining hall offered ice-cold drinks anytime.

Also, the fact that it had stables spacious enough for Set to relax comfortably was a significant plus.

...The only drawback was that, due to the comings and goings of other guests staying at Dusk Wheat inn, one of the horses in the stable had been replaced. Seeing the griffin suddenly enter the stable caused it to freeze in place.

Anyway, it was a day much like any other... The next day, Rei arrived at the dining hall on the first floor of the Dusk Wheat inn with Vihera.

"So, Rei. What are you doing today? It's the day after yesterday, so are you going to take on any requests?"

"Wouldn't it be normal to take it easy today, since it's the day after yesterday?"

Rei replied while savoring the soup. It had been made by simmering vegetables thoroughly until they dissolved, then adding more vegetables and bacon.

The bacon wasn't small either; it was hearty, substantial chunks.

Even though it was just a soup ingredient, each bite released a rich, savory bacon flavor that filled his mouth.

Of course, it wasn't just the bacon. The sweetness and umami from the vegetables that had dissolved into the soup were also fully present, and the vegetables, cooked through yet still retaining a satisfying crunch, were delicious.

This soup is so satisfying that you might think you don't need any other dishes, just this soup and bread.

The bread is freshly baked, still warm, fragrant, and soft.

As expected of a high-end inn that opens its first floor as a dining hall, the taste of the food was truly remarkable.

"Delicious!"

Only words of praise came from Rei's mouth.

And it wasn't just Rei. Across from her, Vihera sat savoring the soup as well, thoroughly enjoying every sip.

"This soup is delicious."

Though Vihera, as a princess, had tasted countless dishes, even she could only describe this vegetable soup as delicious.

For a while longer, Rei and Vihera ate their soup and bread in silence.

Around them, similar scenes unfolded at many other tables.

At every table, people were different from usual... no, the usual dishes were tasty, but many were sighing in admiration at the even greater deliciousness of this meal, or were deeply moved by its flavor.

Vegetable and bacon soup, with bread.

Asking for the menu would reveal nothing extravagant, but precisely because of that, the fact it had been elevated to such a level of flavor proved the skill of the person who made it.

"Hehe, looks like they're enjoying it."

A stout middle-aged woman approached Rei and Vihera's table with a smile.

It was Lana, the proprietress of the Dusk Wheat inn.

"Yes. This might be the most delicious vegetable soup I've ever had. ...Why such a soup today? No, of course I'm delighted to be able to drink this, and the bread is incredibly tasty too."

"I'd like to say it's to celebrate Rei and Set's return... but to be precise, it's because my husband felt like it."

Lana's husband was in charge of the kitchen at Dusk Wheat inn.

Every dish here is made by Lana's husband.

That said, Lana's husband occasionally whips up elaborate dishes on a whim.

Of course, it's a rare occurrence, but precisely because of that, the dishes he creates are often exceptionally delicious.

Anyone who tastes the vegetable soup on the table would understand that.

It's truly a dish of such supreme quality that even the nobility around here wouldn't be able to eat it.

"Anyway... without Rei here, Gilm somehow feels... calmer, or perhaps a bit lonely... It just shows how much Rei has become part of Gilm, doesn't it?"

"I'm flattered you'd say that, but I wasn't born and raised in Gilm, nor have I lived here for decades."

It's only been a few years since Rei settled in Gilm... or more accurately, since he came to this world called Elgin.

Considering he'd traveled to other places multiple times even during those few years, he found it a little questionable that he was supposedly so settled in this Gilm.

That said, if asked whether he disliked the compliment, the answer was no.

For Rei, Gilm was a familiar place, and above all, it was the town that accepted him without fear of Set. ...It was true that many had feared Set at first.

A second home... no, considering Rei's current body, it would be fair to say this is his true home.

"Are you staying in Gilm for a while?"

"That's the plan... but as they say, plans are subject to change, so I can't say for sure."

Especially in Rei's case, he's known as a skilled adventurer.

Considering Set's speed through the skies, he'd be incredibly advantageous for urgent requests requiring something delivered somewhere fast.

This was clearly demonstrated during the previous Magic Fever incident, and yesterday's goblin leader situation would also fit that description.

Without Set, they would have had to travel to the forest by horse or some other means, tie the horses to trees in the woods—and post a guard to prevent monster attacks—then venture into the forest themselves to search for the goblin group.

Goblins are a species that are numerous to begin with.

Even if we found goblins in the forest, we couldn't be certain they were the ones who attacked Hester and Pump... and if that were the case, Pump would certainly have been killed without a chance of survival.

The reason they could rescue Pump so quickly was because of Set.

Thinking about it that way, it was clear that Rei and Set were the ones to rely on when an emergency arose in Gilm.

"I see. Well, since Rei is an adventurer too, I suppose I can't really insist he stay here."

Lana said this, but her expression was tinged with regret.

To Lana, Rei was not only a valued customer but also someone she felt a genuine connection with.

Like a son... though unlike with Kathy, she didn't think of him quite that way since she had a real son.

Even so, Rei never caused any particular trouble for Lana... well, he had caused some issues, including the fight with Elk, but compared to other adventurers, he was within acceptable limits.

Above all, Rei was a valued customer because he stayed for extended periods.

Some guests demanded unreasonable discounts because they were staying long-term, but Rei quietly paid the asking price.

Plus, he eats enough for several people at meals. From the perspective of the Dusk Wheat inn's profits, he was the ideal customer.

Of course, that was only possible because Rei wasn't short on money.

"I plan to stay here for a while, so I'm looking forward to more delicious meals."

Exchanging such conversation, Rei resumed breakfast with Vihera once more.

Though simple in content, it was an extremely labor-intensive meal. After finishing, Rei invited Vihera out to the outskirts.

...What were they going to do? Dissect the goblin leader.

That said, goblin leader meat doesn't taste much different from regular goblin meat, and the ingredients themselves aren't particularly noteworthy.

Its skin could be processed into leather armor, but it wasn't particularly valuable, making the effort somewhat unworthy.

Amidst this... what Rei truly desired, naturally, was the goblin leader's magic stone. He brought Vihera to that location because he felt it was time to tell her about himself.

He'd resolved to do this before, but it had ended up getting swept under the rug.

(I probably should tell Marina too, not just Vihera... But Marina can wait. She seems busy with all sorts of things right now.)

Ideally, he wanted to tell them both together, but if he waited until they were both present, who knew when that would be.

And above all, given his tendency to attract trouble, it was obvious that any attempt to talk would get tangled up in some new mess, making it impossible to actually have the conversation.

If I'd hidden my power completely, I might not have gotten caught up in trouble, but the fact was, with Set in tow, standing out like a sore thumb was unavoidable.

(If I'd just retired somewhere deserted, it might have been a different story.)

That's the kind of life Rei imagined for himself... but for some reason, even if he did that, he felt like he'd still get caught up in other kinds of trouble, even if it wasn't human-related.

(That might have been surprisingly interesting too, though.)

Suddenly, he wondered what would have happened if he'd been living a survival life in the wild instead of being with Gilm. In reality, he felt like he would have managed somehow.

Of course, compared to his current life, it wouldn't be what you'd call comfortable.

"Rei, if you're just here to retrieve the magic stone, you didn't really need to come all this way, did you?"

Vihera muttered, surveying their surroundings.

Right now, Rei, Set, Vihera, and the beast were in a forest not too far from Gilm.

It was considerably closer to Gilm than the grove where the goblins had been yesterday, and the river where they now stood was a spot Rei was familiar with for dismantling monsters.

(I remember we once processed a bunch of monsters here before.)

Suddenly, he wondered what had become of the members from that time... Arogan, Kyurotto, and Skola.

Perhaps because they were active as adventurers and their schedules didn't align with Rei's, they had met a few times since then, but they still didn't see each other frequently.

"So, Rei. What's the plan now? You didn't bring me here just to dissect the goblin leader... right?"

"Hm? Oh, of course. ...But it's true we came to extract the magic stone from the goblin leader, so I'll be handling that part."

Vihera was puzzled by Rei's attitude, but she must have decided that saying anything now wouldn't get an answer.

Without saying anything in particular, he silently watched Rei do what he needed to do.

Set, who was nearby, rubbed his face against Vihera's as he purred.

"Oh? What's up? Feeling extra affectionate today?"

While watching Rei's actions, Vihera gently stroked Set's head.

Set, too, sensing what Rei might be trying to tell Vihera, was rubbing his head against her, hoping Rei wouldn't dislike him... and hoping Vihera wouldn't dislike him either.

"Gurrrr..."

While Set purred and was stroked by Vihera, Rei retrieved the goblin leader's corpse from the Misty Ring.

However, it wasn't the severed head he retrieved, but the torso.

That made sense, since the magic stone was embedded in the torso.

He then slit open the area where the heart should be with his knife and extracted the magic stone.

Even if he was clever, he was still just a goblin leader.

The magic stone extracted from it was by no means large... in fact, it seemed no different in size compared to those of ordinary goblins.

He washed the extracted magic stone in the river water.

It was summer, and dipping my hand into the flowing river water felt refreshing.

The blood and bits of flesh clinging to the goblin's magic stone washed away in the river's current.

(Does that flesh become fish food? ...Does it?)

Knowing how foul goblin meat tasted, if asked whether fish would eat those scraps, Rei could only tilt his head in confusion.

At the same time, he thought, maybe it's good research for figuring out how to make goblin meat taste good. He stored the goblin leader's corpse in Misty Ring.

(Come to think of it, I meant to bring spices, but I forgot about that. I also need to ask if there's been any progress with the goblin meat... Well, all of that depends on what Vihera thinks of me and Set.)

Lost in thought, I watched Vihera stroking Set.

Seeing how happy Vihera looked, Rei made up his mind and spoke up.

"Vihera, remember how I mentioned I have a hobby of collecting magic stones?"

"Huh? Oh, of course I remember! Though I do think it's a strange hobby."

"Actually, that was a lie. The magic stones I collect... are used like this. Set."

He called out to Set and tossed the Goblin Leader magic stone he held toward him.

Set deftly catches it with his beak... and swallows it the next instant.
