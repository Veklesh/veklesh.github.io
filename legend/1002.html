Adrienne, escorted down the main street by guards, appeared outwardly resigned and subdued.

Her arms were firmly held by guards on either side, and others stood in front and behind, keeping a close watch to ensure she couldn't escape.

Escaping under these circumstances would require intervention from outside forces, not anything Adria could do herself—such was the severity of the escort.

Indeed, the guards escorting her to headquarters kept a close watch on her movements while also maintaining vigilance over the surrounding area.

Maintaining vigilance both inside and outside could easily lead to divided attention. However, having worked as guards in the frontier town of Gilm, where many adventurers gathered, including high-ranking ones, these men had naturally experienced such situations multiple times before, having survived countless hellish battles. They escorted Adria to the station with practiced ease...

(Well, is it about time? Crimson and the Princess General seem to have moved away too.)

Without moving her face, Adrienne scanned her surroundings with only her eyes, murmuring silently to herself.

There was no trace of seriousness in her expression; if anything, she seemed to be enjoying the situation.

The movement of her eyes itself was skilled—she scanned the area without being noticed by the guards holding her arms.

At first glance, it seemed more like the skill a thief would need rather than a warrior, but preventing an opponent from reading one's gaze was a skill more than necessary for a warrior as well.

After confirming the surroundings with such movements, Adria judged she was sufficiently far from Rei and Elena and took action.

"Hey, can I talk to you for a sec?"

The guard holding her right arm spoke cautiously to Adria, who had addressed him in a light tone.

"What is it? Sorry, but I have no intention of listening to your demands. If you have something to say, do it once we get back to headquarters."

"No, no, that's not what I meant. I just have something I'd like to ask. Since I'm being led away quietly like this, surely it's okay to talk a little?"

The guard almost dismissed Adria's words outright, but experience had taught him that casual conversation could yield crucial clues.

That's precisely why, while remaining wary of Adria's movements, he opened his mouth.

"I'll listen only until we reach the station."

"Hey!"

The man gripping her left arm shouted sharply, reprimanding the guard holding her right arm.

But the man holding her right arm shot a warning glance at the guard holding her left arm, signaling he wouldn't let his guard down, then turned to address Adria.

"So, what is it you want to know?"

The guard asked, keeping a close eye on Adria to prevent any strange movements, determined never to let go of the arms he held, no matter what. In response, Adria even smiled slightly before speaking.

"You've got both my arms like this... How's the softness of a woman? Is it appealing? Like, enough to make you want to sneak into my bed at night?"

"...Huh?"

For a moment, the guard didn't understand what had been said, and a dumbfounded sound escaped his mouth.

It was only natural. He never expected to hear something like this in this situation.

Had these words come from a prostitute, a dancer, or any woman proud of her allure, he might have reacted with some surprise but remained on guard.

But Adria was the polar opposite of feminine allure.

Her leather armor made it hard to tell her exact physique, but even so, it was the kind of build that only upon seeing her up close did one finally realize she was a woman.

And her hair was cut extremely short, perhaps to keep it from getting in the way when she moved.

No one expected to hear a woman's opinion from someone like her. Not only the guard she was talking to, but even the surrounding guards froze in stunned silence for a moment.

That split-second opening was precisely what Adria had been waiting for.

A precious moment of time, more valuable than silver or gold coins.

The guards' stunned expressions lasted only an instant. That much was certain, but for Adria, that single moment was all she needed.

Her first move was to deal with the guard gripping her right arm.

Though "dealing with" didn't mean killing him.

She drained the strength from her body, moving her arm in sync with the guard's movements.

Feeling that movement, the guard reflexively tightened his grip on Adria's arm... only to have that very movement used against him in the next instant. Before he knew it, the guard who had been grabbing her right arm had thrown himself to the ground.

"What!?"

Seeing his comrade suddenly collapse, the guard holding her left arm tried to press her down by tightening his grip. But before he could react, a light shock ran through his jaw, and he too collapsed to the ground.

By the time the guards flanking Adria tried to move, she had already pulled a crystal sphere, barely the size of a fingertip, from her bosom. She channeled magic into it and slammed it into the ground.

The moment the crystal sphere shattered, smoke poured out.

The smoke enveloping the entire area was, of course, no ordinary smoke, being a magic item created by Zubo.

Being smoke created by magic, it naturally contained magic power within it.

It wasn't an enormous amount of magic—the kind any wizard could easily conjure—but magic was magic nonetheless.

Even if any of the guards possessed the ability to sense magic, it would have been impossible for them to locate Adria immediately.

Moreover, since the smoke had enveloped the entire surrounding area, the guards weren't the only ones affected.

It was evening now, and many people were walking through the streets. Naturally, they too suffered from the smoke.

Adventurers, mercenaries, or soldiers accustomed to rough situations might have handled this trouble to some extent, but among the people walking the streets were many Gilm residents completely uninvolved in such affairs.

Naturally, such people would start panicking, and the surrounding area was in complete chaos.

Adriana weaved through the crowd, running like a needle through fabric.

With the smoke obscuring visibility, those caught in it could only sense something had passed through.

(Now that I've been spotted here, I probably can't go back to Dad's shop. If that's the case, Dad and Zubo must have already escaped the shop too. We agreed on a meeting spot before, so getting there shouldn't be a problem... I just have to hope the Crimson ones didn't storm the shop before they escaped.)

Thinking about the rendezvous spot was a bit of a drag, but considering the fun that was probably waiting afterward, I could manage to put up with it.

(I worked hard to get Zubo motivated, so I'd better enjoy this a little longer or it won't be worth it. Besides, maybe they'll have fun over there too.)

Adria kept running, escaping the smoke-filled section while picturing the fun she'd have after meeting up with Zubo and the others.

Meanwhile, the area shrouded in smoke by Adria's hand cleared completely within a few minutes, finally returning to calm.

And though no one present noticed, the entity that had been following them since they handed Adria over to the guards continued to pursue her, never losing sight of her as she escaped the smoke.

Rei and Set, who had handed Adria over to the guards, along with Elena and the creature, arrived in front of a shop selling magic items.

There, Vihera, Byune, and Azimov stood watch, ensuring no one emerged from the shop.

Vihera's eyes widened at the sudden appearance of Elena. Even Byune, unusually for his usual expressionless self, showed a hint of surprise. Azimov simply stared, transfixed by Elena's beauty.

"Long time no see, Vihera. ...Glad to see you're still doing well, Byune."

"Well, we've spoken several times through the Paired Orb. Considering that, I wouldn't exactly call it a long time."

"Meeting face-to-face with a Paired Orb is different from meeting it directly."

The exchange was accompanied by smiles.

With Elena and Vihera—two women whose beauty was undeniably beyond compare—standing together, the alleyway felt illuminated as if a grand flower were in full bloom, radiating a dazzling brilliance to those around them.

The one who felt this most keenly was probably Azimov.

Normally, he had little interest in women, valuing his research above them and only occasionally visiting brothels. Yet now, his eyes were fixed on Elena and Vihera.

With Vihera alone, Asimov had felt something, but he rarely showed it.

But the moment Elena joined them, it was as if a synergy had occurred. His attention was completely captured by the two of them, and he couldn't look away.

Seeing him like this, Elena turned a questioning look toward Rei, demanding an explanation of who this man was.

Given he was the only person present she didn't know, her gesture was only natural.

Rei, meeting Elena's gaze, turned his eyes toward Asimov and spoke.

"This is Azimov. He's an alchemist, and he's helping us out a bit with this matter."

"I see. Well then, nice to meet you. I'm Elena."

"Ah, ah."

So captivated by Elena that Azimov could only manage a somewhat incoherent reply.

While Azimov was generally clueless about the world and didn't even know Rei's nickname, Crimson, he did know the title Princess General.

Under normal circumstances, hearing the name Elena and seeing her appearance would have made it clear to him that the person before him was the Princess General. But right now, Azimov was far from being able to comprehend such a thing.

After exchanging greetings with such an Azimov, Elena turned her gaze toward the shop Vihera and Byune were looking at.

Then, frowning unpleasantly at the neatly arranged goblin skull displayed near the door, she opened her mouth.

"A goblin skull? ...Tacky."

"Well, they sell magic items, so it's probably related. ...So, did anyone come out of the shop?"

At Rei's question, Vihera shook her head.

"I had Byune check if there might be a back entrance or something, but she said this is the only entrance."

"I see. ...Well then, what could be inside? Ideally, that alchemist or someone like that would be there."

Since there was a woman connected to the alchemist, he hoped there would be some kind of clue.

With that thought in mind, Rei opened the shop door.

As he did, the eyes of the goblin skull decorating the door glowed briefly, but the light was masked by the sunset glow, and no one noticed.

"...No one here, huh."

Rei peered inside the shop and announced.

His tone was thick with doubt and distrust.

Since that woman... Adria had come out of this shop, it stood to reason that the shopkeeper, whom Rei had met before, should have been inside.

But looking around the shop now, there was no one there, not even a trace of their presence.

"You were keeping a close watch on this door, right?"

Vihera and Byune nodded in response to Rei's questioning look.

Of course, Rei didn't seriously believe these two had missed the shopkeeper sneaking out through the door.

His question was purely for confirmation.

Knowing that, Vihera and Byune had nodded immediately.

"If that's the case, then I can only think there must be some kind of escape route somewhere, hidden from everyone..."

"But at the very least, there are no hidden doors in the walls of this building, right? Byune checked, so that's for sure."

"Well, it's hard to imagine a hidden door that even Byune couldn't find."

Rei trusted Byune's skills.

Before teaming up with Vihera, he had ventured into dungeons solo.

As a thief, he undoubtedly possessed considerable skill.

If there were any hidden doors or the like that even Byune's eyes couldn't find, then it would be something Rei couldn't find either.

(Though maybe Set's senses could find it...)

Thinking about his partner, Rei pointed at the ceiling and asked Byune.

"You checked the ceiling too, right? Not just the walls?"

"Mm."

Byune replied curtly, as if it were obvious.

Given it was a single-story building, climbing onto the roof wouldn't be difficult for someone with Byune's abilities.

"The walls are no good, the top is no good. If there's no magic circle or anything like that for using a transfer stone... then this is the only place left, right?"

Rei lightly kicked the floor, indicating to everyone that this was likely where the hidden door, or the entrance to the escape route, was located.
