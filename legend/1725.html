A poisonous water cutter fired by Garangiga.

It sliced through the spot where Rei and the others had been standing just moments before, and anything touched by the splattering toxic droplets melted away.

It was a sight that made one wonder if it was a corrosive solution rather than poison, but it was the same attack used once when Rei and Set lured Garangiga here from the shore.

Since it requires a brief charge-up, it wouldn’t be difficult for Rei and his group to dodge it once they saw it coming.

Just like they’re doing right now.

“Everyone, attack! Use the tactics we discussed earlier!”

Rei’s shout echoed around them, and those who heard it immediately launched their attacks.

Rei, Elena, Vihera, and Relieu ran across the ground to close the distance with the Garangiga, while Set flew into the sky once more to provide air support for the four.

Marina provided support from the rear using spirit magic and her bow, just as she had done earlier.

Meanwhile, Byune, the weakest fighter in the group, moved about in small circles from a distance alongside Yellow, aiming her long spear at Garangiga’s eyes.

Yellow’s scales are extremely sturdy, and—though it’s a crude way to put it—Elena has ordered him to act as a shield for Byune in case of emergency.

And the reason Byune targets Galangiga’s eyeballs with her long needles is, to be honest, because that’s pretty much the only place she can hit with them.

She’s the mid-range support, so to speak, compared to Marina’s rear guard.

Since the long spear inevitably has a shorter effective range than a bow, there was nothing to be done about it.

That said, that’s precisely why Elena ordered Yellow to act as a shield.

(The crossbow Byune used in Exil. He didn’t have it when he came to Gilm, but maybe I should get one ready again.)

As Rei closed the distance with Garangiga, he caught a brief glimpse of Byune in his peripheral vision and pondered this in the back of his mind.

While the crossbow is quite powerful for a bow, structural limitations—and above all, Byune’s physical strength—prevented her from firing rapidly.

Also, because it’s fairly large, it was quite a hindrance to Byune, who values agility above all else.

That was likely the reason Byune had let go of the crossbow… but the ability to deliver a single, powerful strike from a distance was a huge advantage.

Furthermore, even if it’s cumbersome to carry around, there’s Rei in the “Crimson Wings” party who possesses a Misty Ring.

In other words, there is the option of keeping the crossbow stored in the Misty Ring normally, and only using it when the time comes—specifically, in situations like this one.

While Byune’s combat prowess is somewhat lacking, the fact that there are so many frontline fighters in the Crimson Wings was also part of the reason Rei thought this way.

...To be precise, just like Rei’s magic and skills, or Elena’s magic, they all possess the ability to handle the rear guard if they’re willing to do so.

No, rather, given that everyone except Vihera—though, strictly speaking, Elena isn’t a party member—is capable of serving in the rear or mid-line, the Crimson Wings is a party with quite a fortunate lineup.

However, since direct attacks are more effective than providing support from the rear, those capabilities are rarely put to use.

Such thoughts quickly vanished from Rei’s mind; for now, defeating Garangiga was his top priority.

“SHAAAAAAAAAA!”

“Scatter!”

Seeing Rei and the others approaching, Garangiga opens the third eye on its forehead.

The moment he saw this, Rei shouted, and Elena, Vihera, and Relieu immediately moved away from where they had been running.

The ground where Vihera had been standing just a moment ago erupted as if it had exploded, sending small stones flying in all directions.

Like the poisonous Water Cutter, this was an attack Rei and Set had seen before.

It was the same attack method as one of Set’s skills, the Shock Magic Eye.

That said, in terms of power, it was incomparably stronger than the Level 1 “Shock Magic Eye” that Set possessed.

Since the shockwave is released the moment the skill is activated, it is difficult to dodge.

But… in the case of this Garangiga, since its third eye on its forehead opens when it uses the skill, they were able to detect its activation.

Despite the ground exploding, Rei and the others didn’t slow down at all as they closed the distance with Garangiga.

It was unclear whether Garangiga perceived Rei and the others as a threat or simply saw them as a nuisance, but it charged toward them, preparing to launch a direct attack using its massive body.

Garangigga’s first target was Rei, who was leading the charge.

Baring its fangs and opening its massive jaws as if to swallow him whole, it lunged at Rei.

“Do you really think I can’t dodge an attack that big!?”

He leaped to the side to dodge the bite attack, activated Sleipnir Boots to kick off into the air, and swung the Death Scythe in a triple jump.

“Multi-Slash!”

The Death Scythe was swung only once.

Yet, there were a total of three wounds slashed into Garangiga’s scales.

This skill, which produces multiple effects from a single slash, is still only at Level 2, so even counting the actual slash, there were only three in total.

That may be the case, but the fact that three slashes from the Death Scythe are unleashed at once means that single strike possesses considerable power.

“Shhhhhhh!?”

His pride and joy—that body encased in scales that would normally withstand most attacks—had been sliced open with ease.

For Garangiga, it was a completely unexpected, excruciating pain.

(Good. Compared to the Silver Lion, it really is weak!)

With the Death Scythe infused with magical power, it could easily slice through Garangiga’s scaled body.

Convinced of this, Rei thought, “Maybe I can defeat it more easily than I expected?” But… the next moment, he was stunned to see the wound he had sliced open enveloped in green foam and rapidly healing.

Of course, it wasn’t as if everything had healed completely.

The flesh beneath the scales was undoubtedly healing, but the scales sliced open by the Death Scythe looked noticeably softer than the surrounding ones.

For a moment, he thought the Galangiga’s scales might fetch a high price, but for now, defeating the Galangiga and surviving here took absolute priority over making money.

“This Galangiga possesses incredible healing… regenerative abilities!”

While dodging the Galangiga’s advancing body, Rei shouts.

“But even if its body heals, the scales protecting it can’t fully regenerate! Multi-Slash!”

As he shouts, he unleashes a Multi-Slash at the Garangiga’s body as it charges toward him once more.

Even though it’s a boss monster—and one equivalent to a Rank A monster at that—the Death Scythe’s strike manages to inflict a wound, but it doesn’t go far enough to sever the torso.

That just goes to show how tough the scales covering Garangiga’s body really are.

“Hah! No matter how sturdy your body is!”

The Garangiga that had attacked Rei writhed violently from the recoil.

Seeing it coming toward him, Relieu quickly swings his longsword.

It wasn’t quite as flashy a strike as Rei’s, but it still effortlessly sliced through the Garangiga’s scales, severing the spines beneath and cutting into the flesh.

“Defense is useless against me!”

Vihera gently touched the scales and unleashed the Magic Palm, driving a magical shockwave directly into the enemy’s body.

True to her words, the Magic Palm is useless even against thick armor, let alone the defensive scales of a Garangiga.

“Gaaaaaah!”

It was the dullest scream Rei had ever heard from a Garangiga.

That alone suggested the shock it had taken inside must have been pain beyond the Garangiga’s imagination.

“Good, it’s working! Keep it up…”

Press the attack all at once.

Just as Rei was about to shout, seeing Vihera’s Magic Palm, Garangiga’s scales suddenly stood on end.

Scales standing on end—a phenomenon that would normally be impossible.

The moment they saw it, everyone who had been engaging Garangiga in close combat immediately backed away.

It was a largely instinctive reaction, but the fact that they’d heard Marina’s information about Garangiga played a major role in this instance.

Yes, sharp spines were protruding from between the bristling scales.

Some of these spines were over a meter long, while others were only about half that length.

The fact that the spines weren’t all the same length made this a particularly troublesome attack for the target, as it threw off their sense of timing when trying to dodge.

Fortunately, everyone had managed to keep a safe distance from the Garangiga, so it didn’t lead to the worst possible outcome.

“SHAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!”

Letting out a particularly loud screech, the Garangiga thrashed about on the spot.

It wasn’t writhing in pain, but purely attacking the opponents around it.

If anyone had been there, they would have suffered severe damage from Garangiga’s massive body and the spikes protruding from it.

But everyone present, including Rei, had already evacuated...

"Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

Set fired a fire breath from the air at the Garangiga thrashing on the ground.

Water Ball, Ice Arrow, and, though somewhat unconventional, Bubble Breath.

Set actually possesses quite a few water- and ice-based attacks, but he didn’t think they would be effective against Garangiga, which makes its home in an underground lake, so he chose the Fire Breath.

The Fire Breath hits the Galangiga writhing on the ground squarely.

Although the Galangiga is writhing, it is still moving around, but Set, who is using the Fire Breath, is naturally able to move by flapping his wings.

As a result, the Galangiga sustained quite a bit of damage.

Extending its spines from within its scales is effective for attacking, but since it’s raising the very scales meant to protect its body, it’s clearly at a disadvantage in terms of defense.

Through the gaps in those scales, Galangiga’s body is scorched by Set’s Fire Breath.

Garangiga must have realized the pain as well. Instead of thrashing about to attack as he had been doing, he writhes on the ground in agony, retracting the spines he had extended from between his scales back into his body.

"SHAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA!"

Screaming in rage and agony, Garangiga unleashed a poisonous Water Cutter at the source of its pain—Set, who was hovering in the air.

Of course, if one considers only the fact that they caused pain, there were many on the ground, including Rei, and there was also Marina, who kept attacking from the rear with spirit magic and arrows.

But for now, the one who had caused Garangiga the most pain was Set’s fire breath in the sky.

It was precisely because his scales were bristling that he took the full brunt of that wide-area attack.

That is precisely why... he could not forgive Set, and attempted to counterattack with the strongest attack Garangiga possessed.

The Poison Water Cutter possesses enough power to easily slice through even rock upon contact.

When Rei saw it, his reaction was, “A laser weapon?”

Of course, it wasn’t something that existed in the real world—it was the kind of laser weapon seen in anime and manga.

Given that the attack gave off such an impression, even someone like Set couldn’t possibly remain unscathed if it hit him.

……Though, that’s only if it actually hits him.

"Grrrrrrrrrrrr!"

He interrupted his Fire Breath, dodged the poisonous Water Cutter attack, and let out a loud roar.

At the same time, Set’s body shrank visibly.

His body, which had been over three meters long, shrank down to just seventy centimeters.

“Huh?”

Relieu, seeing Set’s size-changing skill for the first time, let out a dumbfounded gasp.

It was only natural. Skills like Fire Breath or Wind Arrow were, after all, very easy to understand just by looking at them—they were quintessential skills.

But for a body’s size to change suddenly—even as a skill—was clearly abnormal.

Despite Relieu’s confusion, the battle continued.

Poison Water Cutters were fired one after another, aiming to take down the now-shrunken Set.

“Terrain Manipulation!”

Seeing Garangiga focusing on Set, Rei drove the tip of his Death Scythe into the ground to activate his skill.

The ground beneath Garangiga’s body rose by one meter, while the area next to it sank by one meter.

Under such circumstances, it was only natural that writhing would cause it to lose its balance.

The difference in height was two meters.

From Garangiga’s perspective, the fall was by no means fatal, but it was more than enough to make him look away from Set.

“Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

And in that split second when he looked away from Set... Set’s body returned to its original size, and a single strike—combining the power of his strength-enhancing bracelets, his falling speed, and the Power Crush skill—landed... shattering Garangiga’s head.
