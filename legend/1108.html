When that report was heard, no one could stop a smile from appearing on Daridora's face.

"Is that information true?"

Not wanting to get her hopes up... yet unable to suppress the faint glimmer of expectation flickering across her otherwise nervous face, Daldora asked. Her subordinate man immediately nodded in response.

"Yes. Our men confirmed that Rei left Gosh with the griffin."

"...Just to be clear, you weren't actually tailing him, right?"

Drawing his ire would mean becoming Rei's enemy.

Of course, it wouldn't be a problem if he didn't notice, but an adventurer with a nickname... one who has a griffin as a familiar? There was no way he wouldn't notice surveillance.

If they carelessly angered Rei here, it could easily become the worst possible situation for them.

That's precisely why handling Rei required extreme caution.

The man delivering the report shook his head at Daldora's words.

"It's fine. We didn't bother setting up surveillance. An Elema Trading Company member passing by the gate saw an adventurer with a griffin leaving."

"...I see. So that's it, then?"

The Elema merchant who had returned to Gosh saw the griffin in the town.

Naturally, they would be curious about what happened and try to gather information.

And when it came to gathering information, relying on their own Elema Trading Company, which held considerable influence within Gosh, was a given long before this incident.

Of course, even without that merchant, given the Erema Trading Company's influence in Gosh, the information that Rei had left Gosh accompanied by Set would likely have surfaced eventually.

"What shall we do, Daridora-sama? We must start moving on the matter of the Tira Tree soon... Since Rei and Set have already left Gosh..."

We should make our move. Though the man's gaze fell upon Daldora as he spoke, she remained silent, her eyes closed as if deep in thought.

The man seemed to understand that Daldora was deep in thought. He remained silent, saying nothing more.

Then... after several minutes had passed, Daldora opened her eyes and reached for the glass on the table.

"We can't say for certain that Rei truly left Gosh. He said he just happened to stop by Gosh, and that's probably correct..."

"But the longer this goes on, the greater the damage to Elema Trading Company becomes."

"True. But we can't afford to rush into this and make a mistake. If Rei is over there, we might suffer damage we can't afford to ignore. Considering certainty, I'd like to confirm he's truly gone before acting."

"Then?"

"Wait until tomorrow... no, the day after tomorrow. If Rei hasn't returned by then, we'll make our move."

If only it could be so. To Daldora's words, whispered from the depths of her heart, the man silently nodded in agreement.

While Daldora secretly wished Rei wouldn't return... as if oblivious to such thoughts, Rei stowed the corpse of the Sand Lizardman Set had defeated and the trident into the Misty Ring, then joined up with the group of four adventurers.

Though the four were still marveling at the taste of the fruit Rei had given them, seeing Set flying toward them brought them back to their senses.

They had journeyed to a place that could be called a paradise, but precisely because of that, returning to reality only made the surrounding desert seem all the more barren and monotonous.

"Did I keep you waiting?"

The man shook his head at Rei, who had dismounted from Set's back to ask.

"No, time just flew by while we were eating that fruit."

"That sounds exaggerated... or maybe not."

Rei had handed him the fruit while waiting, thinking he'd probably be bored and might as well have something to eat. He probably hadn't expected it to be so appreciated.

Uncharacteristically, a look of surprise flashed beneath his hood.

"I'm glad you enjoyed it. So, about that other favor I mentioned earlier..."

"Hm? Oh, what is it? You gave me such delicious fruit. I'll do anything you ask if I can."

Compared to before he'd handed over the fruit, the wariness in the four adventurers' gazes directed at Rei had diminished surprisingly.

(I knew the fruit was delicious, but I didn't expect it to lower their guard this much.)

Feeling somewhat puzzled, but thinking this was actually rather convenient, Rei opened his mouth without hesitation... but first, he asked for confirmation.

"Just to be sure, you guys are using Gosh as your base, right?"

"Huh? Oh, sure. It's the biggest town around here anyway. Besides, changing bases in the desert is a real pain."

"I see. That's good."

Rei breathed a sigh of relief.

After coming this far, it would have been incredibly embarrassing if it turned out they weren't actually adventurers based in Gosh...

Of course, just because they weren't from Gosh didn't mean he would have let them get attacked by Sand Lizardmen and done nothing.

"Relieved?"

At Rei's words, the man tilted his head in puzzlement.

Facing the man, Rei opened his mouth with a troubled look.

"Actually, I only arrived in Gosh yesterday... You saw earlier, but I usually travel by riding Set. And when Set flies, it's pretty fast..."

Hearing that far, the man understood what Rei was getting at. In other words...

"You got lost, is that it?"

"That's basically it. So, if you're heading to Gosh now, it'd be a huge help if you could take me and Set along. You saw how strong I am, so I won't be a burden."

"Well, if you're willing to come with us, that'd be more than welcome..."

The man glanced at his companions. The other three seemed to have no objections either. Nods came back immediately.

"So yeah, we're more than happy to have you. ...Ah, I guess I should introduce myself now. I'm Howl. I lead this Rank E party, Sand Claw."

The man who had been talking to Rei announced this, and then the person holding the spear spoke up.

"I'm Cardina."

"Odilon."

"Nicole."

After hearing the slender man and the short man introduce themselves in succession, Rei spoke next.

"Rei. Rank B adventurer from the Guild of Gilm in the Kingdom of Mireana. This is my familiar, Set."

At the mention of Rank B adventurers, the man who had introduced himself as Howl... and even the other three wore expressions of utter astonishment.

Gosh had no Rank A adventurers, and only a handful of Rank B adventurers.

Most of those few Rank B adventurers were lured away by Daldora's wealth.

For that very reason, Rank B adventurers were perceived by the people of Gosh as being at the pinnacle of adventurers, so it was only natural for them to think that the person standing before them...

Still, knowing full well how powerful Rei was, they didn't make the mistake of dismissing him as a fraud based solely on appearance.

"A Rank B adventurer... and from Gilm in the Kingdom of Mireana? That's the frontier where high-rank adventurers gather. Why on earth would someone active in a place like that come all the way to the Kingdom of Solrain?"

Howl asked, his surprise evident. In response, Rei merely shrugged his shoulders lightly.

Many adventurers had circumstances they couldn't share with others.

Realizing this, Howl stopped pressing for more details.

...Though to be precise, it was because he found the merchants hunting for the Twilight Spear rather bothersome...

"Anyway, back to the main point. If Gosh says he'll let me come with him, we should get moving right away. It'll be night before we know it."

"Huh?"

At Rei's words, every member of the Sand Claw let out a strange sound in unison.

It was as if they'd been waiting for that exact moment.

"Huh? What's wrong?"

Wondering why they'd reacted that way, Rei looked at Haul, who wore an expression bordering on pity as he spoke.

"Well, you see. We can't possibly reach Gosh from here today, can we? It'll probably take until tomorrow afternoon... no, evening at the earliest."

"...Really?"

It wasn't that Rei didn't believe Howl... but hoping he was joking, she turned her gaze to the other three.

But the three of them only nodded, as if confirming Howl's words were correct.

"Grrrr..."

Set growled, as if to encourage Rei.

At that sound, the four members of Sand Claw stiffened for an instant, but they didn't show any further fear, reminding themselves that Rei was there, the one who had saved them.

"Set, huh. Right. Considering Set's speed, it makes sense it'll take until tomorrow."

"Guru!"

Set seemed to be saying, "Exactly."

"...Is he that fast?"

Rei nodded in response to Howl's question as he watched the exchange between Rei and Set.

"Yeah. Set flies pretty fast. ...So, keeping distance from Goushu ended up like this."

"I see. I envy you. With that kind of speed, you could have reached Goush a bit sooner..."

But as he spoke, Rei suddenly remembered something inside his Misty Ring.

It was something he'd used just a little while ago, and it was perfectly suited for use in this sandy desert: a sand ship.

"I'd like to offer you my sand ship for your journey. Is that okay?"

'Sandcraft!?'

The four members of the Sand Claw, upon hearing the word "sand ship," spoke in unison once more.

"Yeah. On the way to Gosh earlier, I ran into some sand pirates riding one. I took it from them after defeating them."

"...Sand pirates who actually have sand ships should be extremely rare..."

"Guess that's what you get for being a Rank B adventurer."

Kaldina and Odilon muttered to themselves, casting admiring glances at Rei.

Sand pirates are a major nuisance for those who live in the desert.

Among them, sand pirates who possess sand ships are the absolute worst kind of nuisance.

To defeat such sand pirates and seize their sand ship—and to do so with only minor damage, allowing it to remain operational—left the members of the Sand Claw group utterly speechless with admiration.

"Alright, here we go."

He announced lightly from a short distance away from the four people and one beast. The next instant, the sand ship materialized on the sand.

They were startled by its sudden appearance from nowhere, but they already knew Rei carried an item box.

Still, the sheer impact of the sand ship's existence was immense. Their attention was focused entirely on it.

"Well then, I suppose it's time to depart. A sand ship should get us to Goushu faster than walking, right?"

At Rei's words, everyone nodded and boarded the sand ship.

"Whoa, there's not a single scratch on it... What's going on with this thing?"

At Cardina's words, everyone showed renewed surprise.

This clearly showed that Rei had won without letting the sand pirates do much of anything, so their reaction was understandable.

"...It's pretty dirty though."

Odilon muttered under his breath.

Hearing this, Howl immediately called out to Rei, who was already preparing to move the sand ship.

"Hey, Rei. Can I at least clean up this sand ship's deck? Having my life saved and then getting a sand ship thrown in on top of that... it feels way too much like I owe you."

"Is that so? You really don't need to worry about it that much... Well, if you insist, I'll ask you to clean it. We took it from the Sand Pirates, so it's pretty filthy."

"Besides, it'll fetch a better price if it's clean..." He trailed off, and all four of them, including Howl, froze.

"Um, well... Rei-san?"

Though Rei felt a twinge at Howl suddenly adding the honorific "-san" after having called him by his first name just moments ago, his gaze shifted toward Howl.

"If I heard correctly, you just said you're selling this Sand Ship..."

"Yeah. I plan to sell it. Sand ships are only usable on sand anyway. And my base is in Gilm, in the Kingdom of Mireana, where there's hardly any desert. So keeping it around would just be a nuisance, right?"

When Rei asked, "Is something wrong?", the four, including Howl, shouted loudly that it was strange, but they couldn't change Rei's mind.
