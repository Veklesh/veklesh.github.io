Rei and Set were led to a building about a twenty-minute walk from Pamidor's shop.

While it was also on a backstreet like Pamidor's shop, the surrounding buildings were quite old and showed no signs of anyone living there.

"...Hey, I'm starting to get a bad feeling about this."

Could it be that the reason no one lives around here is because of the alchemist we're here to see?

As Rei asked, still clinging to that uneasy feeling, Pamidor quietly averted his gaze.

Given that Pamidor looked like nothing but a vicious bandit, the gesture was almost comical.

"He's definitely skilled, so don't worry about it."

"...Are you really sure about that? If this turns out to be just talk, I'll be angry, you know?"

"Yeah, you can relax about that. His skills are no problem. His skills, I mean."

I said it twice because it's important. Pamidor's demeanor only made Rei's uneasy feeling grow stronger.

Wouldn't it be better to just leave without getting involved? That's what Rei thought, but he also knew that repairing the Noiz Demon Sword currently inside the Misty Ring absolutely required an alchemist's power.

That's precisely why backing out now wouldn't just be pointless—it would be completely detrimental. He told himself this as he opened his mouth.

"Alright. Let's go then."

"Yeah. Well, he's not as bad as people say, so I don't think you need to worry too much. Alright, let's go."

With that, Pamidor knocked on the door of the house where the alchemist lived.

...Yes, "knock" was hardly the right word for it.

His fist swung down as if he didn't care if the door broke, sending a thunderous sound reverberating through the surroundings.

At first, Rei thought the door might break, but he quickly realized there was no need for concern.

Because the door didn't creak at all, nor did any splinters fly.

(What is that door? Isn't it made of wood?)

Visually, it was undoubtedly a normal wooden door.

But considering Pamidor, a blacksmith by trade, possessed strength far surpassing that of ordinary adventurers, and yet the door showed no sign of damage whatsoever when struck by him, it was difficult to believe it was made of wood.

Rei pondered for a moment, but quickly understood whose house this was and nodded in realization.

(Alchemy, huh?)

Right. Since this was an alchemist's house, something like this probably wasn't strange at all.

As Rei watched with admiration, Pamidor continued pounding on the door.

"Hey, Azimov! You're in there, aren't you? Come out!"

To an uninformed observer, that call might even look like a debt collector demanding payment.

(If that's the case, then I guess I'm the henchman.)

An unpleasant thought flashed through Rei's mind, making him glance around.

But the house belonging to the man called Azimov by Pamidor showed no sign of anyone coming out, especially since both neighboring houses were vacant.

People passed by nearby, but none seemed interested in Rei or Pamidor.

Rei tilted his head in puzzlement at the surroundings.

Normally, the sight of someone like Pamidor banging loudly on another person's door wouldn't be surprising if it drew the attention of the guards.

But now, no one else seemed inclined to do anything like that.

On the contrary, if it was indeed Pamidor pounding on the door, some passersby even seemed to be sending him sympathetic glances.

Several minutes passed. Pamidor's knocking grew increasingly forceful, as if he might break down the door, which looked sturdier than average.

Just as Rei was thinking that, the door that had been locked finally opened.

"What the hell... Who is it? Huh? Oh, it's Pamidor. Damn it, I just fell asleep a few hours ago. Don't just barge in like that!"

Emerging from the doorway was a man in his thirties or forties, around Pamidor's age.

However, while Pamidor was a muscular giant, the man called Azimov was quite thin.

To Rei's eyes, he looked so thin he might weigh only half as much as Pamidor.

And yet, there was no trace of fear in his eyes as he looked at Pamidor.

If anything, his expression was downright sullen.

"Hey, you. What the hell are you still sleeping at this hour for? What time do you think it is?"

"Huh? Ah... I know it's before noon."

"So why are you still... Ha, forget it. I'm getting up anyway."

"Tch, do as you please."

Despite his grumbling, Azimov promptly tried to usher Pamidor into his quarters... only to finally notice Rei's presence.

And next to Rei, Set, who had been watching the exchange with puzzled curiosity.

As if petrified in an instant, Azimov froze. He turned his gaze toward Pamidor with movements that seemed to make a grinding sound.

"H-hey. Pamidor. That griffin..."

"Hm? Oh, Set?"

Pamidor replied casually to Azimov's words, but Azimov couldn't possibly let it slide.

"Ah, Set... No! What the hell is a griffin doing here?! It's a Rank A monster!?"

Azimov's words were beyond mere astonishment, but Pamidor just gave him a look of utter exasperation.

"Listen, Set's been in Gilm since last year. And you didn't notice at all? Even though he's often out and about with Rei?"

"Last year!? ...Damn it, damn it, damn it. I didn't notice at all!"

"Sigh. You really need to develop some interest outside your own alchemy."

"Guh, y-yeah, you're right..."

For Azimov, directing interest toward anything other than alchemy was an immense hassle.

Under normal circumstances, he would have dismissed Pamidor's words outright.

But seeing the griffin right before his eyes like this, he couldn't bring himself to complain.

Still, Azimov quickly regained his composure and began rubbing up against Rei.

"You... you're the master of this griffin, right? How about it? Could you spare me some of this griffin's materials?"

The moment those words reached Rei's ears, Rei's cheek twitched.

Noticing Rei's reaction, Pamidor tried desperately to restrain Azimov.

But Azimov himself seemed completely unfazed as he opened his mouth.

"What do you say? I'll pay a fair price, of course. If you prefer, I can even give you a magic item I made."

"Rejected."

Rei's implicit refusal didn't deter Azimov, who pressed on.

"Why? You look like an adventurer. Don't you want magic items?"

"I won't deny that. I do have a hobby of collecting magic items, after all. But that doesn't mean I want one so badly that I'd sacrifice my partner, Set."

"...Huh?"

Only then did he seem to snap back to reality. Azimov realized his understanding and Rei's understanding were somehow misaligned.

"Just so you know, I'm not asking for that griffin's... Set's? I mean, I'm not asking for Set's eyeballs or internal organs or anything like that. What I want is perfectly fine with just a griffin's shed feathers or fur. Or something a bit more unusual, like griffin saliva."

At those words, Rei's expression flickered with surprise.

He'd assumed, just like with the Azoth Trading Company before, that I was demanding they hand over Set.

(Overreacted, didn't I?)

He reflected inwardly, then stroked Set beside him.

"Grr?"

Set looked at Rei questioningly. Rei continued, glancing toward Azimov.

"Hey, Set. That guy says he wants a little bit of your feathers or fur. What do you say?"

"Grrrrrrr..."

Set growled softly, sounding a bit troubled.

He was probably remembering the alchemists who had come to the forest back in the Bestir Empire.

Time passed with Azimov tense... until Rei suddenly thought of something.

"Hey, does it have to be fresh feathers or fur from Set?"

"Huh? Fresh is better if possible, but older ones have their uses too."
