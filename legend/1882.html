One of the knights dispatched this time by the Marquis of Seisor… After speaking with Elena, the knight was relieved to hear that the soldiers had finally been able to enter the house.

The Marquis of Seisor was by no means cold-hearted or cruel. Still, it was only natural that he would favor those who were capable—those who carried out their assigned duties properly.

For that very reason, this assignment was a golden opportunity for the knight to advance his career, but at the same time, it was a mission he absolutely could not fail.

...It was a rather difficult mission: to capture the person who had abducted Gaiska and secure Gaiska herself.

Struggling alongside the fellow knight assigned to this mission with him, they somehow managed to get soldiers to storm the house.

It was by no means a situation where they could say the mission was a success just because of that, but the fact that they had managed to storm the house meant they had taken a step—no, more than that—toward completing the mission.

(The only—and biggest—problem was…)

Thinking this, the knight surveyed his surroundings.

Currently, the forces surrounding this house aren’t limited to those of the Seisor Marquis family, including himself.

There were also soldiers from the Duke of Kerebel’s household, who ruled over Anesis.

And the three of them—Elena, Rei, and Ara—had already stormed inside the house.

At that point, it wouldn’t be strange to consider this mission already half-failed. It wouldn’t be strange, but… even so, facing two individuals with aliases… let alone the Duke of Kerebel’s daughter, there was no way the knights could do anything.

I had been hoping—perhaps she was still lost inside the house, or hadn’t found Gaiska yet… but in the next instant, the soldiers surrounding the entrance were blown away spectacularly.

It looked as though they’d been blown out from inside the house… For a moment, I wondered if the traps had only stopped triggering outside, and the ones inside the house hadn’t been disarmed yet.

He had that thought… but when he saw the figure who had blown the soldiers away and emerged from the entrance, he was struck by an even worse premonition than before.

Because that person… the one who had blown the soldiers of the Marquis of Seisor’s household away head-on as he appeared was the same person the knight had seen earlier… Rei.

No, it wasn’t just Rei. Behind him, Elena and Ara also burst out.

And the expressions on their faces were ones of sheer desperation.

If it were just a minor monster appearing, skilled fighters like Rei and Elena would never have such expressions on their faces.

That meant something had happened inside the house—an event so dire that even Rei and Elena had to fight for their lives.

“Evacuate! Evacuate! Everyone, get out of the property!”

At the knight’s sudden shout, the soldiers inside the grounds immediately rushed outside.

Perhaps due to the rigor of their daily training, there was not a trace of hesitation in the soldiers’ movements.

Had these been soldiers with lax training, they would have been confused as to why they were suddenly ordered to leave the grounds, and in their confusion, they would have stopped moving.

Even among the Noble Factions, a major reason the House of Marquis Seisor held such significant influence was precisely because its soldiers were this elite.

“What’s going on!?”

Hearing the order to leave the premises, the knight’s comrades began to gather.

But before the knight could say anything in response… the reason became clear.

Because, just over ten seconds after Rei and the others dashed out the front door… cracks appeared all over the house.

In reality, it wasn’t the house that cracked, but the very space in which it stood—though the knights had no time to confirm this properly.

They didn’t know what had happened or how, but there was one thing they did know.

And that was that something had happened so dire that even those known by the nicknames “Crimson” and “Princess General” had to flee.

“We’re going to evacuate for now, too.”

When the knight shouted this as they made their way out of the house’s grounds, his fellow knights nodded in agreement without objection.

They didn’t know what had happened, but they understood one thing for certain: this was beyond their control.

“Everyone, get as far away as possible—not just from the property! It’s dangerous!”

The knight shouted, and the others not only left the grounds as instructed but also moved further away to put some distance between themselves and the danger.

Naturally, the soldiers of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, who had witnessed this exchange, retreated just as the soldiers of the Marquis of Seisor had.

It was likely largely because Rei, Elena, and Ara were shouting for everyone to get as far away from the house as possible.

Set, who had been watching the situation on the ground from above, and Yellow, who was riding on his back, also descended to the ground, assuming something must have happened.

And then... just as the crack that had appeared in the space seemed to spread across the entire estate grounds, the very next moment, without a sound... just like a sandcastle swallowed up by the sea and vanishing, it disappeared.

No one knew exactly what had happened or how it had come to pass.

Even Rei and Elena, who had defeated the flesh cocoon, had no idea what had caused this to happen.

The only thing that was clear was that they themselves had not been caught up in what could only be described as the shattering of space… and that the house where Celestan had lived had completely vanished.

This is no metaphor; there is truly nothing left where Celestan’s house once stood.

There is a large crater-like hole in the ground—perhaps a result of the house vanishing—but ultimately, that is all that remains.

“……”

Faced with a scene that could only be described as sudden—truly sudden—no one could utter a word.

This was especially true for those who had just burst into the house.

Fortunately, those who collided with Rei were flung violently out the front door by the force of the impact and, almost instinctively, fled the property.

Even those who had managed to dodge Rei’s charge inside the house were able to survive because they immediately fled the scene after witnessing the space crack open.

Having literally escaped death by a hair’s breadth, some felt relief at having survived, while others couldn’t even comprehend why they had been spared.

Still, as they realized in the darkness of the night that their lives had been spared, everyone gradually began to feel relieved… and the next thing on their minds was the question of what had actually happened to bring about this situation.

Naturally, many want to know exactly how things came to this, but the only ones who might know are the three who entered the house—Rei, Elena, and Ara—and they can’t very well ask them.

Still, from the perspective of the knights in charge of this incident, they need to gather that information to report to their superiors.

The knights kept pushing each other to go ask, and in the end, the knight who had spoken to Elena first—since they were acquainted—was forced to go.

That knight walked toward the three people who were talking, looking rather timid.

“Um, Lady Elena. Could you tell me what happened inside the house? I need to report this to my superiors…”

At the knight’s words, Elena seemed slightly hesitant.

This wasn’t because she was trying to hide what had happened… but simply because she was unsure how to explain it.

As for that flesh cocoon, there was much Elena didn’t understand.

No, it would be fair to say there is actually very little she does know.

“Unfortunately, I don’t know the details either. But… there was a monster or something sealed away in that house in the past, and it possessed the power to manipulate space. As a result of defeating it, that power probably went haywire or something.”

“...I see. So that’s what happened.”

Elena’s explanation didn’t fully satisfy him, but the knight nodded along, content to hear the essential details first.

And then… he asked about the most crucial person of all—the one who mattered most to him.

“So… was Lord Gaiska not inside the house?”

After all, the main reason the Seisor Marquis family had deployed their forces in the first place was to rescue Gaiska, who had been taken away.

In fact, the orders given to the knights had made Gaiska’s rescue the top priority.

That said, seeing as the house had been wiped out, the mission to rescue Gaiska appeared to have failed completely.

Given that, it was only natural to try to gather at least some information to report back to his superiors.

“He was there. But… when I saw him, Gaiska was already in a state where you couldn’t really call him alive. Well, considering he did manage to take revenge on Celestan, who put him through all that, maybe his sense of self was still there.”

“Um, could you tell me a little more about what exactly that situation was like?”

Hearing the knight’s words, Elena hesitated for a moment, wondering if she should mention that flesh cocoon. However, she decided it wouldn’t be possible to keep this matter hidden forever, and since all the evidence had been destroyed along with the house, she figured it wouldn’t matter if she spoke up.

“Hmm, I don’t know the reason, but Gaiska was embedded in something that could be called a flesh cocoon.”

Having said that, she proceeded to explain Gaiska’s situation to the knight.

That said, she didn’t tell him everything—only what pertained to Gaiska.

Upon hearing this, the knight’s expression turned… indescribable, a sort of subtle look.

That was only natural. For the Seisor Marquis family, Gaiska was nothing short of a thorn in their side.

Time and again, he had done things that tarnished the name of the Seisor Marquis family, and each time, they had scrambled to keep it from becoming public knowledge.

Despite all that, Gaiska was still a descendant of the Seisor Marquis family, and that was why the knight likely had mixed feelings about him.

In contrast to Gaiska, whenever the topic turned to Deotres, the knight’s expression grew stern.

Perhaps it was because, unlike Gaiska, he was clearly recognized as a traitor.

“I see. Both Lord Gaiska and Deotres… Thank you.”

Elena was taken aback by the words of gratitude that came from the knight’s mouth.

Was that gratitude for what he’d said about Gaiska’s final moments, for killing the traitor Deotres, or for something else entirely… or perhaps, for all of it?

While Elena was pondering this, the knight bowed his head and left.

From the knight’s perspective, having obtained the necessary information for the time being, he likely needed to report it to his superior as soon as possible.

Elena, who had been watching the knight’s retreating figure, snapped back to reality when Rei tapped her on the shoulder.

“So, what do we do now? …The outcome turned out quite different from what we originally expected.”

“Is that so? It’s certainly turned out to be a bigger deal than we expected, but I think we can still say we’ve achieved our minimum objective. …Though Celestan’s death was a bit unexpected.”

In terms of settling the score for targeting Rei, a guest of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, this incident was a huge success… well, maybe not quite that, but it was certainly a success.

Furthermore, in terms of undermining the influence of the Seisor Marquis family, causing this much of a commotion in Anesis—and above all, considering that Deotres had been collaborating with Celestine—this aspect is also satisfactory.

And as for deterring and intimidating the underground organizations other than the one led by Celestine—who is likely watching the situation from the sidelines—the fact that the house itself has been wiped out means this has succeeded in the best possible way.

It’s true that Celestine possessed what appeared to be some kind of sealed monster, and that Gaiska was used as a sacrifice for it, but looking solely at the results, it’s a fact that things didn’t turn out all that badly.

“Is that how it is? …Still, I wonder what the relationship between Celestine and the Black Wolf really was. It doesn’t seem like Celestine viewed the Black Wolf as nothing more than a disposable tool…”

“As for that information…”

Elena paused mid-sentence and turned her gaze toward the spot where the house had once stood.

Thanks to the moonlight, the bonfires lit by the knights of the Marquis of Seisor, and above all, Elena and Rei’s keen night vision, they could clearly see what was left of the site… but in the end, they couldn’t say anything more than that.

On that point alone, it remained a complete mystery.

The only thing that seemed to offer a clue was the document that Rei, Elena, and Ara had taken with them when they escaped.
