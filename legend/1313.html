Once the initial exchange of information was complete, the next topic naturally turned to who had masterminded this affair.

"Considering Rei's personality, it's entirely possible he's been resented. At the same time, Azoth Trading Company could also be resented due to its size and past incidents,"

Rei and Galahad both nodded at Vihera's words.

Both were well aware that they were often the targets of resentment for various reasons.

"Beyond grudges, we can't rule out the possibility someone wanted something Rei or Azoth Trading Company possessed... right? Honestly, the magic items Rei has in his possession are the kind people would stop at nothing to get their hands on."

Marina's words were undoubtedly true.

Especially Rei.

The Azoth Trading Company, despite its flaws, was known as a major trading house even within Gilm.

In other words, very few would dare take on such a massive organization.

But with Rei, it's different.

It's not that no one would consider targeting Rei because of his strength... but those who know the circumstances would never think of targeting him.

But that's because Rei possesses the strength of an adventurer with a notorious reputation, and also because his partner, Set, is always by his side.

If Rei possessed only the strength of a rank B adventurer and lacked Set's presence, yet still carried various magic items like he does now... he would undoubtedly have been attacked by someone.

Of course, such actions wouldn't come from legitimate adventurers, but from those with shady dealings... the so-called underworld.

And if Rei's strength had been insufficient, those kinds of people would have stripped him of every last magic item.

"I won't deny that."

Rei understood exactly how many magic items he possessed.

After all, the Misty Ring he used daily was said to be one of only a handful remaining of its kind—a rare item box.

Beyond the Misty Ring, anyone possessing a certain level of status, power, or skill would covet Rei's magic items, regardless of the Misty Ring itself.

"That's precisely why there are far too many possible targets for either of them. ...Honestly, even if we discussed it here now, we wouldn't know which one was targeted."

Everyone listening to Marina's words nodded in agreement.

"So, what do we do?"

"Well... The best option would be to make contact again with whoever reached out to me... but finding them seems difficult, doesn't it?"

No one could counter Marina's muttered words.

To begin with, no one even knew how many people were in this Gilm.

On top of that, finding someone who embodied words like "ordinary" or "average" within that group was nearly impossible.

"What about drawing a picture of their face and searching for it...?"

"...No. My memory of their face has already faded significantly in my mind."

Vihera suggested trying to make a sketch, but Marina shook her head as she replied.

"I'd say 'use scent'... but that's probably impossible too."

Rei considered relying on Set's enhanced sense of smell, but Marina was moving through the city streets. Naturally, she'd be covered in the scents of countless strangers.

If Set had been present when she met the person directly, he could have tracked the scent.

But, should he say unfortunately, or rather, unfortunately, Set was at Galahad's mansion at that time.

So naturally, there was no way he could identify the scent of the person Marina was talking about.

"So, I guess we'll just have to search diligently... Do you think they'll come out quietly?"

"Doubtful."

Everyone nodded in agreement at Rei's muttered words.

Unless the person who brought Marina the information this time made contact themselves, finding them would be difficult.

But the fact remained that they had no other leads.

If that was the case, they had no choice but to gamble on that infinitesimally small possibility.

"...I'll use Azoth Trading Company's resources to look into that area. They tried to pit Rei and the others against us. I don't know which target they were aiming for, but it's clear they harbor ill will toward us. We can't just leave it at that."

Galahad understood they'd been set up by someone, and he couldn't just let it go either.

That said, his insistence on not letting it go wasn't because he couldn't forgive being humiliated.

No, of course such a stunt is unforgivable, but the biggest reason they couldn't let it slide was a matter of pride for the Azoth Trading Company, one of the foremost guilds in Gilm.

If they took such an action and did nothing in response, simply letting it happen... it would give the impression to others that the Azoth Trading Company was easy prey.

If that happened, it wouldn't be surprising if other guilds started targeting Azoth.

To prevent that, it was necessary to give those who had laid a hand on us a fitting punishment.

Rei understood Galahad's intentions, so he nodded without hesitation.

"Understood. But the one who plotted this incident seriously injured an alchemist I know. Once you get their information, pass it on to us too."

"I will. But the same goes for you. When Rei and the others gather information on them, will you report it to us?"

Rei nodded at Galahad's words, and thus a cooperative relationship regarding this matter was established.

And with this alliance formed, things couldn't simply remain as they were.

The guard who had remained silent since entering the room, observing the conversation unfold, also promised to inform Rei and the Azoth Trading Company if he found such a person.

"...In that case, we should move quickly. Since this incident just happened, they probably don't expect us to act immediately... at least, that's a possibility."

"I would have preferred to talk with Rei about various things unrelated to this matter, though."

Galahad murmured, sounding slightly disappointed at Rei's words.

Beside him, Murt had been thinking about testing his skills against Rei, but such a stunt was impossible in the current situation.

"Well, we'll have plenty of time to talk once this business is settled. If possible, I'd appreciate it if you could serve up some delicious food."

"If I let Rei eat as much as he wants, I'll go bankrupt..."

Murt's quiet mutter reached Rei's ears, but Rei deliberately ignored it and rose from the sofa where he'd been sitting.

He needed to find whoever had orchestrated this incident and properly settle the score with them sooner rather than stay here any longer.

"Ah, speaking of which..."

Galahad called out to Rei's back as he was about to leave the office.

"Huh? What is it?"

"No, I was just wondering... those meat buns and pizza that are popular lately, did you make those dishes, Rei?"

"I suggested it, but I didn't make them myself. What I taught them was just the basic concept. It was the cooks who properly turned that into the dishes we have now."

Rei had only taught the basics of steamed buns and pizza at the Golden Bread Inn during winter.

The meat buns had already been sold during winter, and the pizza had recently started appearing for sale too.

...What surprised Rei most was that the meat buns were still being sold even after spring arrived.

To Rei, meat buns were a winter food, but that was just his own image.

For the people of Gilm, eating meat buns even in spring held no particular sense of incongruity.

Like Gamelion, they weren't seen as something eaten only during a specific season, but rather accepted as a common type of bread.

That said, meat buns are meant to be eaten piping hot, straight from the steamer; they become extremely unpalatable once cooled.

In that respect, they were significantly inferior to bread that remained edible even when cold and less flavorful.

Furthermore, the fact that they aren't tasty unless freshly steamed means sales inevitably drop in summer, which is another difference from regular bread.

"If possible, I'd like Azot Trading Company to teach us some new dishes too."

"...Huh?"

Hearing those words, Rei, who had been about to leave the office, stopped and turned around.

It was only natural. Azot Trading Company held considerable influence in Gilm, but it primarily dealt in goods like weapons and armor.

At least, that's what Rei thought, and in fact, he'd never heard of Azot Trading Company getting involved with restaurants before.

Of course, it was possible that someone affiliated with Azoth Trading Company might run a food stall as a hobby.

Just like adventurers setting up stalls.

But for the Azoth Trading Company to seriously get involved in the food business? That was a different story entirely.

That was precisely why Rei let out a somewhat dazed gasp.

Seeing Rei like that, Galahad smiled with a look of satisfaction.

"Well, it won't be right away. Sometime in the future... that's about it. Our guild is a major player, but that doesn't mean we can rest on our laurels, right?"

"I get that... but honestly, what's the point of reaching into a completely different field?"

Rei wasn't particularly knowledgeable about business, but even he felt it was questionable to venture into a completely different field.

(Surely they don't eat while looking at weapons and armor in a weapons or armor shop? ...It just seems like a surreal scene to me.)

Rei imagined a table set up inside a weapons shop, serving high-class cuisine, and couldn't help but shake his head in disbelief.

Seeing Rei like that, Galahad smiled and spoke.

"I can pretty much guess what you imagined, Rei, but let me just say... it won't necessarily turn out that way."

"Anyway, bring that topic back after we've settled this matter. If I have the time, I'll take it on. Though, to be honest, I can't think of any dishes off the top of my head right now."

Rei wasn't particularly knowledgeable about cooking to begin with.

Well, back in Japan, he did enjoy reading cooking manga to some extent.

That doesn't mean he remembered the details.

If he had, he could have taught them more detailed methods for making meat buns or pizza from the start.

That's precisely why, if Galahad... if Azoth Trading Company really did ask Rei to come up with a new dish, he'd have to think of something else.

(Udon... There's yakisoba, right? But what's the difference between Chinese noodles and udon? If you cut udon thinner, boil it, then stir-fry it in sauce, is that yakisoba? Ah, no, but I think I saw something labeled "steamed noodles" at the supermarket...)

While pondering the difference between udon and Chinese noodles, she shifted her focus. For now, dealing with whoever plotted this scheme took priority over cooking.

"If new dishes keep appearing like this, Gilm might become famous not just as a frontier town, but as a culinary destination too, don't you think?"

"Well, as the birthplace of udon, Gilm is already pretty famous, isn't it? Right?"

"Mm."

Marina, Vihera, and Byune were chatting among themselves, but Rei wasn't particularly paying attention.

Rei had always loved eating.

For someone like Rei, if Gilm flourished as a culinary hub, it could only be beneficial.

Being a frontier town, Gilm naturally boasts many ingredients unique to this land.

In that sense, it probably had at least some foundation for flourishing as a culinary hub.

Then Rei arrived, possessing a wealth of culinary knowledge, albeit superficial. And Rei is someone who thoroughly enjoys the act of eating. So it wasn't surprising that sooner or later, Gilm would become such a town.

(Though, of course, that would only be known locally around Gilm. It would take time for it to become known throughout the entire Mireana Kingdom. Ah, but considering information from merchants, perhaps it's not so far off?)

Marina wondered what kind of development Gilm would undergo from now on. While looking forward to it, she still spoke up as the stopper for the Crimson Wings.

"Now, Rei. We can discuss the cooking matter after this situation is resolved. For now, let's focus on capturing those who tried to frame us."

With that, Rei nodded... and this time, they truly left the office, and Galahad's residence.
