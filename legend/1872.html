In the darkness of the night, a large contingent of soldiers marched through the slums.

Among them were knights, their menacing presence hanging heavy in the air.

Had it been daylight, their mere presence here would have caused quite a commotion, but it was already nearly 10:00 p.m.

Since it was winter and the sun set early, most people were already asleep.

While some were still making noise in taverns or visiting brothels, the majority were likely asleep.

Or, if it had been just a little while ago—right after the New Year—there might have been some still celebrating, but it had already been a while since the New Year began.

New Year’s parties and such were hardly being held anymore.

...That said, many residents of the slums were still awake, and naturally, they were on guard if soldiers or knights showed up at their homes.

Still, they don’t directly question who these people are—likely because they instinctively sense that, given the current situation, saying the wrong thing could easily get them killed by the soldiers or knights.

Among those soldiers and knights were Rei, Elena, and Ara, with Set by their side.

“Sorry about this, Set. I’m dragging you into this.”

“Grr!”

In response to Rei’s words, Set growls to indicate it’s no problem.

As far as Set is concerned, he’s just happy to be out with Rei.

Even if it’s the middle of the night… and even if Rei is planning to raid somewhere.

“According to what Elena told me, the place we’re raiding is supposedly an ordinary house. Though, given that it’s built in the slums, I can’t really call it ordinary.”

If there’s an ordinary house in the slums, there’s almost certainly going to be someone trying to break in.

No, there are probably some who would consider not just stealing, but actually raiding it.

But what if that house belongs to the person with the most influence in the underground organization within Anesis?

Normally, no one would dare to raid the home of such a dangerous opponent.

...But in the slums, you can’t say there aren’t people who would attack without even considering such things.

In any case, while the location of the house is unusual, Rei has heard that the house itself is just a standard single-family home—slightly larger than average, but not spacious enough to be called a mansion.

Given that, it was undoubtedly impossible for Set, who stood over three meters tall, to fit inside.

“Grrrrr!”

“I don’t care,” Set growls, rumbling his throat.

Soothed by Set’s reaction, Rei gently strokes his head.

Set must have found Rei’s touch pleasant, for he purred contentedly.

“...Lord Rei, um... I’m sorry to say this, but... our interactions with Set make the tension disappear, so if possible, could you please stop doing that?”

Ara says this, looking slightly hesitant.

For those accustomed to battle, like Rei, Elena, and Ara, it was only natural to avoid getting too tense before a fight.

However, among the soldiers, there were many who, while they had experienced battle itself, did not have the same wealth of combat experience as Rei and the others.

For those soldiers, if a lighthearted atmosphere were created here, there was a possibility they wouldn’t be able to muster their strength when the time came for actual combat.

Rei must have realized this from Ara’s gaze and the expressions of the soldiers around them.

She stopped petting Set’s head.

“Sorry about that.”

“Grr…”

Rei apologized, but Set rubbed his head against Rei, begging for more strokes.

Seeing Set like that, Rei sighed in resignation and gave his head a light stroke.

“Sorry, but that’s all for now. Once this attack is over… well, it’s already nighttime, so it’ll be tomorrow, but I’ll play with you then.”

“Grrrrr? Grr!”

He looked a little disappointed, but perhaps satisfied enough by Rei’s promise to play tomorrow, Set stopped acting spoiled and asking for more strokes.

Watching Set like this, Elena turned her gaze to Yellow, who was sitting still on her right shoulder.

Normally, the moment he saw Set getting attention, it wouldn’t be strange for him to start meowing, “I want to play too!”

Her gaze was born of curiosity as to why he wasn’t doing that now… but upon seeing Yellow, she wore an expression of understanding.

That was because Yellow was skillfully sleeping on Elena’s right shoulder.

Since Yellow was still a child, he didn’t have much stamina at night.

He can push himself harder than a human child, but there’s no doubt he’s only been in this world for a few years.

Given that, it was only natural for Yellow to get sleepy once night fell.

Moreover—though Rei, Elena, and most of the people here didn’t know it—Set and Yellow had been chasing each other around the stables like a game of tag earlier that day, which had left Yellow quite exhausted.

(Should I just let him stay like this until we reach our destination?)

Deciding there was no need to force him awake under the current circumstances, Elena resolved to let him sleep until they reached the location of the ambush.

Another major factor was that Yellow wasn’t really expected to be much of a combat asset in this raid to begin with.

Yellow is still small and, as a pure combatant, is nowhere near Set’s level.

He does possess abilities such as scales that repel ordinary blades, the ability to blend into the surrounding scenery, and the ability to let Elena relive the scenes he has witnessed.

However, these abilities are not particularly useful in direct combat. ...In terms of defense, he could perhaps serve as a shield, but Elena, who dotes on Yellow, would never let him do such a thing.

The reason he came here with her today is that he has strong feelings—he wants to be with Set, he doesn’t want to be separated from Elena, and he wants Rei to pay attention to him…

“Lady Elena, we can see them now.”

Elena, who had been watching Yellow, snapped back to reality at the words of a soldier approaching from ahead.

The soldier she had sent ahead to scout the situation had come to report.

But seeing the strange look of panic on the soldier’s face, she grew suspicious.

“Hmm, what’s the situation? They must know we’re approaching, of course… but judging by that look of panic, could they have gathered their forces and been lying in wait for us?”

Even though it’s the largest of the underground organizations in Anesis, the forces gathered here belong to the Duke of Kerebel’s household.

Moreover, with Rei and Set now added to the mix, there’s no way a single organization could win in a head-on battle.

Still, if soldiers are marching toward their stronghold, it wouldn’t be strange for them to consider intercepting them.

Obediently following orders and disarming would be one option, but doing so would undoubtedly lead to them being looked down upon by other organizations.

If this were an ordinary merchant guild, that might not have been a problem.

But as an underground organization, they certainly couldn’t afford to do anything that would invite contempt from those around them.

Elena knew this, which is why she suspected that the other side might be preparing their forces and lying in wait for them... but the soldier shook his head, denying Elena’s assumption.

“No, that’s not it. Around the target’s house, there were soldiers and knights gathered who don’t belong to our group.”

“Doesn’t that count as their forces?”

“Yes. The soldiers and knights looked as if they were about to storm the house at any moment.”

“The House of Marquis Seisor?”

Based on the soldier’s explanation, Elena guessed which faction those forces belonged to.

She had originally heard from Teres and the others that the Marquis of Seisor’s forces were on the move.

She had heard it… but even so, the idea that they would come here specifically to attack the same place as them was unexpected, even for Elena.

(No, if it was Gaiska who hired the Black Wolf, then isn’t it actually not that strange for the Marquis of Seisor to come here? I don’t know if they’ve picked up on the news that the Black Wolf is dead, but I can’t rule out the possibility that they’ve taken action to cover up the circumstances surrounding that…)

I am already almost certain that Gaiska hired the Black Wolf.

However, there is still no clear evidence, such as a contract or testimony from those involved.

Given that, I had no reason to doubt that the Seisor Marquis family would take action to cover it up.

Admittedly, it was undoubtedly to prevent Gaiska from being held accountable... but this was not out of concern for Gaiska himself; it was strictly to protect the reputation of the Seisor Marquis family.

Whatever the reason, they were planning to attack the organization acting as an intermediary for the Black Wolves.

Given that, Elena couldn’t just leave the Seisor Marquis family to their own devices.

“What shall we do?”

“Let’s go. We can’t let the Seisor Marquis family do as they please. We need to take control of this situation.”

If we can manage to chip away at the Seisor Marquis family’s influence even slightly, that would be the best outcome.

However, what we must be careful of here is that we must not weaken the Seisor Marquis family’s power too much.

We need to find a balance there, and that makes it very difficult to strike the right balance.

Even so, we cannot simply leave the Seisor Marquis family’s military strength as it is.

We head toward our target house, hurrying along.

Led by Elena, the forces of the Duke of Kerebel’s household advance as well.

Normally, some might resent being led by a woman, but this is the Kerebel Duke’s army.

They were accustomed to being led by Elena, who bore the nickname “Princess General,” and under the current circumstances, there was no sense of discomfort whatsoever.

This is likely where the Kerebel Duke’s household truly excels.

As they continued on, Set suddenly twitched his head as if he’d heard something, and a moment later, Rei also realized what Set had reacted to.

(Ah, I see. Come to think of it, they did mention they’d already launched the assault. If that’s the case, then these sounds must be the sounds of battle.)

Yes, what they heard were the clanging of metal and screams. And wafting through the air was the rusty, metallic stench of blood.

Given that, it was obvious without even having to think about what was happening.

A moment after Rei, Elena must have sensed it too, as her pace quickened.

Naturally, the others followed suit, and the group’s pace gradually quickened.

Soon...

“Who’s there!”

Suddenly, that voice rang out in the darkness of the slums.

There was no need to even think about who had spoken; it was obvious.

After all, further ahead, the largest underground organization in Anesis—which acts as a mediator between the Marquis of Seisor and the Black Wolves—was engaged in battle.

The people here were likely serving as a barrier to prevent outsiders from joining the fight—specifically, to stop reinforcements from the opposing organization.

That was precisely why they reacted so sharply to Elena and her companions as they approached through the darkness.

“I am Elena Kerebel. I am of the Kerebel House. I have business ahead, so please let us pass.”

“Elena Kerebel… the Princess General!?”

The soldier didn’t seem to recognize who was standing before him at first, but he must have known the name Elena Kerebel and her nickname, the Princess General. He showed signs of being shaken, his surprise plain to see.

“That’s right. As a member of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, I have business at the house ahead. Would you kindly let me through?”

“What!? No, but…”

Perhaps because he had been strictly ordered not to let her pass through this area, he showed no sign of moving even after Elena spoke to him.

“What’s the matter? Anesis… Are you saying you can’t follow the orders of Elena Kerebel in the territory of the House of Kerebel?”

“Well… no, but… I can’t make that decision on my own, so please wait just a moment. I’ll go get a superior right away. Hey!”

At the soldier’s words, another soldier nearby hurriedly ran off.

It goes without saying what he was doing.

But Elena had no intention of waiting for him to return.

After all, even if he says a superior officer is coming, she couldn’t completely rule out the possibility that the officer might try to buy time for the Marquis of Seisor.

Given that, simply waiting here quietly would be a bad move.

“I’m sorry, but I don’t have time to wait around. I’m going to go through here.”

Saying this, Elena walked past the soldier.

The soldier instinctively reached out to stop Elena, but...

“Is there a problem with what Elena said?”

Ara, wielding her Power Axe, glared at the soldier with a look so intense it seemed she could kill him with her eyes alone.

Faced with Ara’s glare, the soldier was ultimately unable to do anything further.
