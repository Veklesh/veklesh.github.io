Several days after Rei and the others recovered the materials from Bystle... Rei was standing before the main gate.

Today, he was once again preparing to load timber harvested from Treant Forest into the Misty Ring for transport when he encountered Bistor, who was just leaving Gilm.

Or more accurately, a caravan led by Bistol.

Spotting Rei and Set, Bistol approached them with a delighted smile.

Just as when Rei had met her before, her physique—truly worthy of being called muscular—was covered in feminine clothing, making her stand out remarkably even here.

But whether she was used to such attention or not, she seemed unconcerned as she addressed Rei.

"Well, well, Rei-chan and Set-chan. Off to work today?"

"Yeah. What about you? Leaving Gilm?"

"Yep."

Even as she said this, Bistol's tone was brimming with energy.

Hearing that, Rei understood that Bistor wasn't exactly running away from Gilm.

"What are you going to do now? Last time you said you'd gather more forces or something."

"Yeah, I plan to figure that part out on my own. If we just back down like this, I'd feel bad for those kids too."

Rei immediately understood who Bistol meant by "those kids."

They were the ones who had fought to let Bistol and the others escape during the attack, and had fallen.

"So you're Rei?"

Rei was talking with Bistol like that when he was suddenly addressed.

Turning his gaze toward the voice, he saw a man in his forties standing there.

He looked like he was at an age where continuing work as an adventurer would be difficult—though there were many exceptions—but the man before Rei showed no such signs of decline.

"Um...?"

"Ah, my apologies. I'm Odolsta, assigned to guard Bistor-san's caravan. I meant to come see you sooner, but I've been rather busy."

"Ah... I suppose so."

The caravan had suffered considerable damage from the attack by the private militia.

To compensate, various actions were necessary. As an escort, Odolsta needed to replenish the depleted numbers and ensure proper coordination by thoroughly communicating with everyone.

"I heard Rei laid Buie and the others to rest. ...Thank you."

Odolsta bowed deeply.

When he heard the name "Bui," Rei didn't immediately recognize who it was, but the word "honored" made him understand it referred to those who had died in the private militia's attack.

"No, it's nothing to thank you for. It was just something I did in between requests."

"Still, I'm glad you did it."

Given that he was guarding someone as eccentric as Bistol, Rei thought he seemed like quite a decent person for an adventurer.

(Well, he probably couldn't guard Bistor otherwise.)

Watching Bistor, as intense as ever in every sense, stroke Set in the corner of his eye, Rei reflected deeply.

"Well then, Rei-chan. We should be heading soon. What are you and the others going to do?"

"Hm? Oh, I should be heading out soon too. I need to pick up some felled timber over in Treant Forest."

With that, Rei parted ways with Bistol and Odolsta, heading toward Treant Forest with Set.

Just as Rei and Set took flight into the sky, at the lord's mansion, Daskar tossed documents onto his desk with a sour expression.

"Hmph, finally spilled the beans. ...But the Noble Faction? I never expected that."

Daskar spat out the words, his expression radiating utter disgust.

"Indeed. To be honest, I believed this incident was the work of the Royal Faction... It was quite an unexpected turn of events."

The one murmuring this was one of Daskar's aides.

"Just to be clear, you're not lying in our master's name... are you?"

The thought that flashed through Daskar's mind was whether this might be a scheme to pit the Neutral Faction against the Noble Faction.

Until recently, the three factions—the Royal Faction, the Noble Faction, and the Neutral Faction—had been clearly hostile to each other.

But now, the Noble Faction and the Neutral Faction weren't enemies; if anything, they held a position of friendly neutrality.

Given that, it wouldn't be strange for the Royal Faction, finding this situation troublesome, to consider driving a wedge between the aristocrats and the Neutral Faction.

At Daskar's words, his aide shook his head.

"No, I doubt that's the case. ...Fortunately, if you will, the Crimson Wings have captured many prisoners. Thanks to that, verifying the consistency of the prisoners' information isn't difficult."

"...I see. To be honest, I'd rather not get into a fight with the Noble Faction."

Saying this, Daskar contemplated how to retaliate.

"Regarding this incident, it seems unlikely the Kerebel Duke's household was involved."

"I suppose so. I'm confident we've managed to get along reasonably well with Duke Kerebel. But... there are probably some within the Noble Faction who aren't pleased about that."

Those belonging to the Noble Faction are mostly individuals who take great pride in their noble status.

They also take equal pride in belonging to the Noble Faction.

For such nobles, Daskar's tolerant stance toward commoners is likely unacceptable.

No, if he were openly opposing them, they could dismiss him as a commoner-chasing fool.

But if they become friendly with the Noble Faction, such behavior—at least openly—becomes impossible.

Of course, Duke Kerebel possesses the discernment to keep his public and private affairs strictly separate.

Precisely because of that, the Noble Faction likely never imagined the two factions would so easily join forces... and then came the expansion of Gilm.

Gilm, which until now had been, at least nominally, a town smaller and lower in status than a city, had begun its expansion.

Many believe that by acquiring a city, the Neutral Faction—which previously held none—will become a worthy ally in Duke Kerebel's eyes.

Moreover, the fact that the Neutral Faction monopolizes the immense profits from the frontier is surely another reason for discontent.

Moreover, for those within the Noble Faction who have access to certain information, it wouldn't be difficult to learn that Elena Kerebel, the Duke of Kerebel's only daughter and the symbol of the Noble Faction, nicknamed the Princess General, is close to Rei.

In reality, most believe their closeness is merely that of friends, nothing romantic... Still, they are a man and a woman.

Moreover, Rei demonstrated his true strength during the war against the Bestir Empire, a feat that impressed Elena, who had publicly declared she would only entrust herself to someone stronger than herself.

Many believe his achievements were only possible because of Set... because of the Griffin. Yet some possess the discernment to see the truth.

Beyond that, many viewed Rei as dangerous for various other reasons.

And since Rei had made Gilm his base, it was only natural that hostility would be directed more toward the Neutral Faction.

(But let's be honest, there are situations where things only worked out because Rei was there. The most recent example? Treant Forest. Was it a Giganto-Turtle? That was a troublesome monster.)

Of course, there are other adventurers besides Rei who possess high combat skills.

But if you ask whether they could have defeated it without causing as much damage as Rei did, that's debatable.

Daskar confirmed in his report that Rei was able to defeat the Giganto Turtle largely due to his ability to fly and attack from above, as well as the power of the magic items he possessed.

"Well, fine. Anyway, we need to find out what made them attack us... though, come to think of it, that's probably obvious. So, we need to repay them for recklessly reaching out to us... What should we do? Is asking Rei for help still the best option in this case?"

The ability to fly grants high mobility, and on top of that, he possesses firepower that's off the charts for a single individual.

With Rei possessing the capability to take on an entire army single-handedly, he should be more than sufficient to retaliate... or rather, return the favor for the sabotage they carried out this time.

"But without Rei, the expansion work will fall behind in various ways."

Rei's item box had been a major asset in the current expansion work... or more precisely, in the preparatory stages.

With Rei gone, it was certain the upcoming work would face major delays.

Daskar considered this for a moment, then shook his head slightly.

"This time, thanks to Rei being here by chance, we avoided major damage. We even recovered the stolen materials safely. But if we leave things as they are, there's no guarantee the same thing won't happen again. Especially for someone who plotted something like this."

"Then... why not contact Duke Kerebel? The current situation can't be pleasing to him either. If we report this to him, wouldn't he be compelled to act?"

"...You're right. That's undeniable. But in return, I'd be creating a debt to Duke Kerebel. And he'd start seeing me as an easy target."

Exhaling softly, Daskar ran a hand through his hair with an exasperated look before speaking again.

"No, I don't mind being underestimated myself. The problem is that if I'm underestimated, it might prompt Gilm to take some strange action."

Someone like Daskar probably wouldn't fight back even if I pushed him a bit too far. I'd have to keep it within reason, just to avoid drawing the ire of those above me.

Thinking like that, more and more people started messing with Gilm.

As it is, we're already extremely busy with the expansion work.

Normally, I'd be confident enough to handle any minor provocation, but now it's bad.

To prevent that from happening, I need to fight back on my own, even if it means pushing myself beyond my limits.

"But haven't you been exploiting Rei a bit too much lately? What if he gets fed up and decides to move his base to another town?"

Normally, receiving a request from a noble—especially a lord—is an honor for an adventurer, not something to be resented.

But that's only for ordinary adventurers... those ranked C or D.

When it comes to rank B or A adventurers, the more skilled they are, the more distinctive their personalities tend to become.

Of course, some adventurers have pleasant personalities, but it's also true that many are more of the "go their own way" type.

And Rei, naturally, belonged to the latter category.

"I know that. ...But having Set is huge. He's got mobility and firepower, plus an item box and all sorts of magic items, right? Don't you wonder why one person is so well-equipped?"

If the person who commands a griffin, the person with high combat prowess, the person with the item box, and the person with numerous magic items... were all separate individuals, Daskar could ask for various things more easily.

Instead of burdening just one person, he could ask multiple individuals to handle different tasks.

But there's no point in saying that now.

Since there was only one person named Rei, it was only natural that requests would pile up on him.

(If the item box were usable by anyone, I could at least ask Rei to lend it to me, regardless of whether he actually would.)

But since only Rei can use it, that's impossible too.

(And as for Set, I hear he can only fly by riding on Rei's back. ...Well, there is the option of grabbing onto someone's legs like Vihera or Marina did. ...But that isn't something just anyone can do either.)

Since you fly while clinging to his front legs, the person holding on needs considerable grip strength.

On top of that, wearing armor or armor-like gear adds extra weight, and if you grip Set too tightly, it hurts him.

Above all, Set was friendly and attached to Rei.

Asking him to act apart from Rei would be unreasonable in many ways.

"...It's still no good. In terms of combat power, there are some who are... not quite on par with Rei, but still quite strong. But when it comes to movement speed, they inevitably fall short of Rei."

"Is it unavoidable? I really wanted to avoid delaying the expansion work..."

Daskar's subordinate let out a small sigh, mentally mapping out the sequence to advance the construction preparations as much as possible.
