I want you to go to the country of Reburina.

When Daskar told Rei this, Rei felt more like, "I knew it."

The organization that had been pulling the strings behind the Ajax incident—or more accurately, the one that had ordered it—had its headquarters in the Kingdom of Reburina.

More precisely, in Rossy, the capital of the Kingdom of Reburina.

A small country, merely a vassal state of the Mireana Kingdom, had deliberately stirred up trouble in Gilm—the very heart of its suzerain state, ruled by Daskar, a central figure of one of the three major factions, the Neutral Faction.

If Daskar did nothing, Gilm would be looked down upon.

And if it were disrespected, it would likely attract even more malicious individuals.

Given that, a proper reckoning was necessary.

Not as the Kingdom of Mireana, but as Daskar of the Neutral Faction.

Given that he had heard from others that this would likely happen, it was no surprise that Daskar brought it up now.

No, considering the Ajax incident, one could even say it took longer than expected for them to bring it up.

"I understand that, but..."

The first to speak in response to Daskar's request was Rei.

Among the members other than Elena and Ara... the leader of the Rank B party, Crimson Wings, was technically designated as Rei.

Given that, it was undoubtedly Rei's job to speak up here, not Marina's, not Vihera's, and certainly not Byune's.

"Traveling to the Kingdom of Reburina by carriage takes a considerable amount of time... I've heard it takes over a month. Given that, and the fact we obviously can't afford to take that long, I suppose it falls to me to go..."

If they wanted Rei to go to the Kingdom of Reburina specifically because he could travel by riding Set, then why did they call the others... and why did they even call Elena, who, while friendly with Rei and the others, has no connection to the Crimson Wings?

Though Rei voiced this doubt, Daskar had naturally anticipated it.

"I trust Rei's abilities completely."

The words that came from Daskar's mouth were, as they were spoken, filled with trust.

Of course. He had seen firsthand just how capable Rei truly was during the war against the Bestir Empire.

Moreover, as the lord of Gilm, it wasn't difficult for Daskar to learn of Rei's activities.

Having seen it with his own eyes and heard reports from below... it was only natural that he understood the true extent of Rei's abilities.

Probably... no, definitely, among those in Gilm who knew about Rei's abilities, he was top-tier.

Even so, Daskar harbored the expectation that sending only Rei to the Kingdom of Reburina would lead to various unmanageable situations.

Especially against an organization plotting crimes like this one. Naturally, there would be nobles backing them, nobles connected to them.

If Rei were to cause trouble dealing with such individuals, it could later become a liability for the Kingdom of Mireana.

Of course, if clear evidence and witnesses were secured, that wouldn't be an issue... From Daskar's perspective, while Rei was utterly trustworthy in combat, he didn't seem suited for covert investigations.

In that sense, there was likely a desire for someone else to accompany Rei besides Rei.

"But we don't know what lies ahead. Given that, wouldn't it be better to have more than just Rei?"

"Well, it's true that having Marina and the others would be helpful in many ways."

Marina is a powerful spirit mage who can be useful in many situations.

Vihera is extremely dependable in combat.

Byune possesses the specialized skill of a thief.

Those three would undoubtedly be useful if they acted together.

There's no doubt about that. Rei could state that with certainty...

"So, are we going by carriage?"

In the end, it always comes back to that.

For a short distance, clinging to Set's leg to move might be fine.

But clinging to Set's legs for hours, or even days, would pose various problems.

While air travel is vastly safer than moving on the ground, it's still not absolutely safe.

Bird monsters, harpies, wyverns... and countless other monsters besides.

If only Rei were riding Set, dealing with such monsters wouldn't be too difficult.

But with everyone clinging to different legs, even Set could be caught off guard.

To travel safely and avoid that, Elena's carriage was the best option.

(So that's why he called Elena?)

If borrowing Elena's carriage was the plan, Rei understood why she'd been summoned here.

That's what Rei thought... but... At Rei's words, Daskar shook his head.

"No, without Rei and Takeru, the expansion work will face various complications. I want you back as soon as possible."

"But... isn't that a bit unreasonable?"

"True. Traveling by Set is difficult. But traveling by carriage takes too long. ...So how exactly are we supposed to get there?"

Marina continued after Rei spoke.

It was true that Daskar's words contained several impracticalities, so everyone present fixed their gaze intently on him.

Daskar bore the full weight of those gazes, yet he opened his mouth without any sign of surprise.

"I'm aware of that issue and have considered countermeasures here. ...I had my subordinate alchemist create a small magic item. Using that should allow us to travel without any particular problems."

"Magic item?"

The first to react to those words was, unsurprisingly, Rei, who had a hobby of collecting magic items.

"Ah. The problem this time is that clinging to Set's leg for a long time is the issue, right?"

"Yes."

Rei answered honestly when asked.

Seeing this, Daskar smiled and spoke again.

"Just to confirm, Set can only be carried on the back by Rei, basically. But using his legs, he can easily lift even a giant bear. That's correct too, right?"

"Yes."

"There are no limitations... like, say, he can carry one bear, but if it's two, three, four, or five bears, he can't lift them? Nothing like that?"

"Well... weight-wise, I don't think it's a problem, but since Set only has four legs, five people might be a bit much, don't you think?"

"Ah, I suppose so. But that's not what I'm asking about. Basically, if you can tell me there are no restrictions, that's enough for me. ...The magic item I ordered you to make is, simply put, a cage."

"A cage?"

"Yeah. A basket made for Set to carry. Inside, it's spacious enough for four or five people to sit. Well, lying down wouldn't be possible, though."

What Rei imagined upon hearing those words was a shopping basket.

His understanding was that people would enter that basket to travel together...

"Isn't that a bit problematic in several ways? If Set is just flying, the chances of being spotted aren't that high, but flying with such a large basket makes it much easier to be seen. Of course, it depends on the size of the basket."

"True. But don't worry. I told you it's a magic item, didn't I? It's not just any basket."

"...What kind of magic item is it, exactly?"

Marina interrupted Rei and Daskar's conversation.

I'd intended to leave this solely to Rei, but unfortunately, when it comes to magic items, Rei tends to lose himself. Marina stepped in to prevent that.

"Sorry to disappoint, but since it was made during this busy time, it's not exactly that amazing. You know the monster called the Murder Chameleon, right?"

"Ah, that..."

Hearing the name, Marina grimaced, picturing a monster about three meters long... roughly the same size as Set.

It looked like a giant chameleon, but the "Murder" in its name came from its preference for attacking humans.

Its "preference" literally meant it favored human flesh, making it a Rank C monster.

It also possessed the ability to change its body color to blend into its surroundings, though it wasn't quite as sophisticated as optical camouflage... in fact, it was several steps below that, and if anything, even worse.

Even such a troublesome monster is only ranked C because it isn't particularly strong in a straightforward fight.

It possesses fangs capable of tearing flesh and crushing bones, and its long, extended tongue delivers a blow of considerable force.

But... that's all it has.

While the tongue strike might catch ordinary people off guard, any adventurer with a certain level of skill can easily dodge it if they see it coming.

The Murder Chameleon's movements are inherently sluggish, so it rarely gets close enough to bite or attack with its tail.

...Still, the primary reason it holds a C-rank despite such weaknesses is undoubtedly its ability to change its body color.

In any case, when told the cage was made from such a Murder Chameleon, Marina... and those listening nearby, understood what kind of magic item it was based on Daskar's previous explanation.

"Still, since I made it in a hurry, it's not exactly perfect. It can't be activated by the user's magic alone; it requires a magic stone to function. ...If I'd had more time, it might have been different."

Daskar muttered regretfully, but when Marina's gaze fell on him, he spoke again.

"Of course, for this incident, I'll provide the magic stones needed to use that magic item. ...It's quite inefficient, but fortunately, we've gathered a considerable amount of monster magic stones now."

"Ah, from the nightly defense battles..."

Daskar nodded in response to Rei's muttered remark.

The reason Rei noticed was simple.

Because it was Rei who carried the corpses of the monsters slain night after night back to the guild.

Since the expansion work began, how many monster corpses had he actually carried?

The amount of materials and magic stones obtained from them had become enormous.

(Even after defeating them night after night, there's no sign they're ever going to disappear. Honestly, why are there so many monsters?)

Monsters arrived at the guild every single night, so relentlessly it felt like they spawned infinitely, just like in the game Rei used to play back in Japan.

It's as if they're sharing information about Gilm's expansion work and the lack of walls, drawing them in.

Goblins were still the most common monsters, but naturally, many other types appeared too.

Sometimes even Rank B monsters would show up.

Fortunately, there were several Rank B and Rank C adventurers around at the time. By working together, they managed not to defeat the monsters, but to drive them off. Perhaps the damage they inflicted was significant, because those monsters never returned afterward.

In any case, for that reason, Gilm currently had no shortage of magic stones.

Furthermore, it wasn't uncommon for materials from monsters slain by adventurers en route to Gilm to be brought in.

"Understood. ...Honestly, it is a somewhat mediocre magic item, but it should be useful this time."

"Magic stones burn up pretty fast, you know. Something like a goblin magic stone only lasts about thirty minutes. It's only usable because of the item box that Rei has."

"Ah... I see."

If a goblin magic stone only lasted about thirty minutes, how many would be needed to travel from the Kingdom of Mireana to the Kingdom of Reburina?

Rei didn't know exactly how spacious the cage was, but carrying a lot of extra luggage would still be difficult.

Carrying around a large quantity of magic stones like that would normally be difficult.

But... with the Misty Ring, Rei could use that cage without any issues, no matter how many magic stones he carried.

Daskar nodded in apparent understanding at Rei and the others' situation, then turned to Elena and handed her a letter.

"Daskar, what is this?"

"A letter from Duke Kerebel to you, Elena."

Daskar replied to Elena's puzzled question.
