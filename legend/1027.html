"No, you really saved me. Thank you. I never imagined someone like me would get captured by thieves. Honestly, I thought I'd be sold as a slave right then and there... Ah, thank you."

The dark elf woman reached out for the sandwich Vihera offered, expressing her gratitude.

Then, stuffing the sandwich into her mouth, she looked around thoughtfully and spoke.

"Still, having a magic item carriage like this is pretty amazing. Well, I mean, Audban aside, it makes sense since Lady Marina is here."

Audban, dismissed with that "anyway," opened his mouth in exasperation.

"Listen, Jusla. How on earth did you get captured by bandits? You could have used spirit magic to get out of anything, couldn't you?"

"Well... you see. The spring sunlight in the forest felt so good, I ended up taking a nap. And then, when I woke up, I was already in that situation. I had a gag in my mouth, so I couldn't use spirit magic. And as for fighting unarmed... I'm fine with a bow, but close combat isn't my strong suit."

"Unbelievable. You know the forest's current state, right? Why would you deliberately leave the barrier?"

"Ahahaha. Just a little adventure. But I didn't leave the Lost Barrier, okay? I only left the barrier itself. ...Well, you know. Despite everything that happened, my virtue is still intact. I'd appreciate it if you didn't dwell on it."

Audban and Marina were stunned yet relieved by her matter-of-fact declaration.

The thieves hadn't laid a hand on Jusla, likely because she was a virgin—or even if she wasn't, they figured she'd fetch a higher price as a slave untouched. Still, thieves are thieves. It wouldn't have been surprising if they'd succumbed to their immediate desires.

Thinking about it that way, Jusla's safety was nothing short of a miracle.

"But what about mealtimes? They didn't just capture her yesterday or today, right? Even if they were going to sell her as a slave, she'd be useless if she was weak. They must have fed her at least?"

"Ahaha. Yeah, they did feed me. ...Well, like this. With a weapon pressed against my throat, though."

Everyone listening nodded in agreement at Jusla's words.

Spirit magic tends to have shorter incantations than regular magic, but it still requires time to chant the invocation to the spirit.

With a weapon pressed against your throat, slashing it open would be quicker than uttering the incantation to summon the spirit.

And if your throat was slit, further incantation would naturally be impossible. Death would be certain before you could even use spirit magic.

"If I were good at close-quarters combat, maybe I could have done something... But like I said before, I'm good with a bow, but close-range fighting? Not my thing."

Jusla scratched her head, embarrassed. She was a woman whose features could be described as well-proportioned, yet strangely, she didn't exude any sense of femininity.

"Honestly, I've been telling you this forever. I said you should train in close combat too, not just archery. Your skill with the bow is among the best of us, so you should be able to handle close combat if you put your mind to it."

"Ahahaha. It just doesn't suit me at all. ...Well, with incidents like this one happening, I suppose I should give it some thought. ...Anyway, changing the subject—"

Jusla stopped speaking and turned her gaze to each person inside the carriage in turn.

Rei , Elena , Vihera, Byune , Audban, Marina.

Ara would normally be here too, but she was currently driving the carriage.

Next, Jusla's gaze shifted to the carriage window.

Through it, she could see Set walking beside the carriage and Yellow sleeping soundly behind him.

"...What kind of group is this?"

"Well, you know. When I left the forest, the elder explained it to me, right? Jusla was there too, wasn't she?"

"Huh? Um... ah... yeah. I remember, I remember. Something about going to seek Lady Marina's help to heal the World Tree or something like that."

"Why can't you remember properly?"

"Well, the elder's speech was long. I just couldn't fight off the sleepiness."

Audvan looked utterly exasperated at Jusla's explanation that he'd fallen asleep.

The elder's story was, of course, important.

To hear that she slept through it because she was tired left him at a loss for words.

"Ahahaha. Well, you know. Don't worry about it too much. Everyone knows I'm not exactly serious anyway."

"You never change, Jusla."

Jusla flushed slightly at Marina's comment as she watched the exchange between Audban and Jusla.

"Ehehe. Ah, but I did improve my archery skills thanks to Lady Marina's lessons. I'm actually one of the top hunters in the forest when it comes to monster kills."

"Really? Well, I taught you archery when you were still little, so you don't need to be so modest about it."

Even as she said this, she seemed genuinely pleased that someone admired her. A rare, unadorned smile touched Marina's lips.

"Ehehe. What Lady Marina taught me is a precious memory for me."

"Hearing you say that makes me happy too. ...So, Jusla. Changing the subject, how are things in the forest?"

Marina, who had been wearing a gentle smile, asked with a slightly more serious expression.

At that question, Jusla dropped her playful demeanor, adopting a serious expression like Marina's and shaking her head.

"Compared to when Audban left, it's definitely worsened. It's not accelerating rapidly, but the World Tree's barrier is undeniably weakening. In fact, that's why I got captured by those thieves. Before, I would've been inside the barrier of confusion..."

"Right."

This meant the World Tree's condition was growing more serious by the minute.

Amidst this, Rei suddenly voiced a concern that had been on her mind.

"Why did the World Tree weaken so much? I mean, I heard it got sick or something, but it's called the World Tree for a reason, right? It's supposed to be special. I can't believe it weakened this easily."

"...I left the forest quite some time ago and haven't returned since, so I can't say for sure. What do you think? At least when I left the forest, the World Tree didn't seem like it was going to get sick."

Audban and Jusla, caught by Marina's gaze, exchanged glances and wore troubled expressions.

Marina had left over a hundred years ago, and she was probably recalling that time.

But Jusla quickly shook her head.

Audban aside, Jusla was still—with the caveat that she was a Dark Elf—young.

As mentioned earlier, when Marina taught her how to use a bow before leaving, Jusla was still quite small.

Compared to Jusla, Audban, while recognized as a Dark Elf like Marina, was somewhat younger at the time and thus remembered those days clearly.

"Yes, for a while after Lady Marina left, the World Tree shouldn't have been showing any signs of illness. It started weakening little by little... I think, just before Jusla began wandering the forest alone. Of course, it was so gradual back then that no one imagined it would become such a dramatic crisis like it is now."

"Little by little, huh. That's the insidious part. If the World Tree had started weakening rapidly over a short time, it might have been different, but when it happens gradually, it's hard to notice. Especially for us, living such long lifespans, it's even harder to perceive."

Coming from Marina, who had left the forest driven by a desire to see the outside world unlike most Dark Elves, those words carried an indescribable weight.

"Huh... Hey, Marina-sama. Is the outside world really that interesting?"

Marina smiled and nodded at Jusla's question, her eyes sparkling with curiosity.

"Yes. It's quite different from living in the forest, that's true. It was tough getting used to things at first, but I think Audban can understand that part, right?"

"That's right. When I first stepped outside the forest, it was quite difficult in many ways. Well, somehow I managed to get used to it though."

Recalling his own misadventures, Audban murmured with a complex expression, then turned his gaze back to Jusla.

"Anyway, back to the topic. Besides the barrier weakening, have you noticed any other abnormalities?"

"...Hmm, I heard there are monsters appearing now that we've never seen in the forest before."

"Huh? Wait a minute."

Rei, who had been listening to the three Dark Elves, suddenly spoke up as if he'd noticed something.

Audban's face stiffened for just an instant at Rei's words, but fortunately, only Marina noticed his reaction.

(I suppose I made a slight miscalculation. I didn't expect Rei's magic to make him shrink back like this. ...Well, I suppose this is normal, though.)

The magic emanating from Rei when he removed the New Moon Ring had completely intimidated Audban.

Marina hoped, if possible, that Audban would also become friends with Rei.

Even if it didn't go that far, she at least didn't want him acting hostile.

"There was a barrier around the forest, right? So monsters were actually coming out from the front?"

"Ah, that. Well, it's a pretty big forest, you know. Much bigger than the one Rei and the others fought the cyclops in recently. So the World Tree's barrier only covers the area around where the Dark Elves live. Besides, we have to eat to survive, don't we? If we're going to eat, high-rank monster meat is better, isn't it?"

Monster meat must be an important food source for the Dark Elves too.

And since they eat it, they prefer tasty meat... higher-rank monster meat. Rei understood that too.

"So if the barrier is strong and wide, the monsters that could be food won't show themselves either, huh?"

"Exactly. Plus, we need materials besides food."

With few exceptions, elves and dark elves generally possess higher magical power than humans.

Naturally, this doesn't mean they're poor at alchemy using that magic; they can craft a certain level of magic items themselves.

What they need then are materials, and among those materials, many come from monsters.

Hearing this explanation, Rei's eyes sparkled with curiosity.

(Magic items made by dark elves? Honestly, saying I'm not interested would be a lie. ...Since the reward for this job is said to be World Tree materials, maybe I should just have the alchemist in Marina's hometown make the magic items? They'd be used to handling World Tree materials, after all.)

Since she lives where the World Tree is, she'd naturally be skilled at crafting magic items using its various materials.

That was Rei's conclusion, but before he could voice it, Jusla spoke up.

"Anyway, that's the forest situation. I don't think it'll collapse immediately, but it's definitely getting worse. So, personally, I'm counting on you guys Marina brought here."

Several of those listening couldn't help but smile wryly at Jusla's words, especially when she added, "You did help me, after all."

Originally, they had thought it would be better to head straight for the Dark Elf Forest after defeating the attacking bandits, without stopping at the hideout.

If Rei had quietly accepted that suggestion, Jusla would still be trapped in the bandits' hideout.

Of course, had they known Jusla was being held captive, the others would have readily agreed to raid the hideout.

But there was no way to know that.

That's precisely why this whole incident was half circumstance... one might even call it sheer luck.

"...Marina. I have a question. Could there be more bandits ahead from here? If so, considering what happened this time, wouldn't it be best to eliminate as many as possible?"

At Elena's words, Marina gave a wry smile.

"Even if you ask me... You've probably heard me mention it before, but I left the forest quite some time ago. I doubt the bandits who were here back then are still around."

"I suppose so. ...Then what about you, Audban and Jusla?"

"How should I put it... When the barrier was up, bandits couldn't get in unless by some fluke. And I wasn't attacked when I left the forest, but..."

Hearing her uncertain murmur, Elena glanced at Rei with a slightly troubled look.

Receiving that look, Rei nodded back, indicating there was no particular problem.

"Well, if bandits attack, we'll just wipe them out one by one, right?"

At Rei's words, everyone nodded in agreement that this was the sure way to go, and thus the fate of the bandits in this area was sealed.
