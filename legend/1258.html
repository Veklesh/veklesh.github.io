After completing the procedures and entering the library, Rei was surprised to find far more people inside than he had anticipated.

There was a wizard man reading several books stacked on a desk, while a little further away, a wolf shapeshifter looked bored reading some book. A bit farther still, a woman who appeared to be a sheep shapeshifter slept with a table as her pillow, being shaken awake by an elf man.

Rei had first come to this library not long after arriving in Gilm.

He had visited several times since then, but he had rarely come during winter.

Perhaps that was why seeing so many people here surprised Rei a little.

(Is reading suddenly trendy or something?)

Reading wasn't something Rei disliked... in fact, he rather enjoyed it, so an increase in library users wasn't a bad thing at all.

But more library users meant there was a chance the books they were reading were ones Rei couldn't get his hands on.

(Or rather... at this point, there aren't any books left for me to read, are there?)

He had chatted a bit with Kenny before arriving at the library, but that wasn't why he was late...

The thing is, the library opens in the morning.

Rei, on the other hand, left the inn around ten in the morning.

Naturally, the library would have been crowded by then.

(I hope I find something about Silver Lion materials...)

Thinking about why I came to this library, I searched for books.

Along the way, several people spotted Rei and shot her surprised looks, but she paid them no mind.

He had always stood out in Gilm, and on top of that, it had been announced that he had conquered the dungeon.

He understood why eyes were drawn to him, and worrying about those stares would only lead to stress getting the better of him.

Ignoring the stares, Rei systematically searched the shelves. While he found books detailing monster materials, they only covered Rank C or D monsters... or at best, Rank A. There was no book describing materials from Rank S monsters.

But upon reflection, it made perfect sense.

After all, encountering an S-rank monster wasn't something that happened easily.

In fact, even encountering a rank A monster was a once-in-a-lifetime event.

...Though, the residents of Gilm did encounter Set, equivalent to an S-rank monster, on a daily basis.

Anyway, even setting aside the exception of Set, opportunities to encounter such high-rank monsters were rare indeed. It was only natural that the knowledge itself hadn't been passed down to the point of being written in books.

Of course, there have been those who fought and defeated Rank S monsters before, so it's not as if the knowledge is entirely non-existent.

But those are naturally extremely rare cases, far fewer than encounters with Rank B or lower monsters.

Naturally, this meant information about such encounters rarely circulated.

Moreover, in this world, disseminating information wasn't something that happened often.

"So there really isn't any? ...It's Gilm, so I thought there might be something."

If this were an ordinary village, town, or city, Rei wouldn't have been this disappointed.

But precisely because Gilm was a frontier, encounters with rare monsters were by no means uncommon.

That's precisely why... it wasn't unreasonable for Rei to hold out hope.

"Then, anything else..."

With the book he sought absent, Rei's reason for coming to the library had vanished.

Still, since he'd gone to the trouble of coming all the way to the library, he decided to browse for something interesting to read.

But naturally, interesting books are ones others want to read too, so such books are first come, first served.

Of course, what one person finds interesting differs, so not all interesting books were gone.

But the books Rei wanted to read were almost nonexistent.

Walking past the bookshelves, Rei wondered if there was anything, when suddenly one book caught his eye.

"The Adventures of Replus...?"

Even Rei didn't know why that book caught his attention.

Still, the fact that he reached out and picked it up meant there must have been some reason.

He looked for an empty desk and sat down.

When Rei opened it, what he saw was a story composed solely of text, without any illustrations.

It was a typical format for an Elgin book, and Rei began reading.

The story itself was a common heroic tale.

A boy finds a long sword stuck in a rock inside a cave, pulls it out, gains power, overcomes various trials, and ultimately becomes known as a hero.

Common... it was a truly common tale, but after finishing it in less than an hour, Rei tilted his head.

It wasn't that there was anything strange about this heroic tale.

But the story felt strangely familiar.

For instance, several of the trials he faced were similar to those in novels and manga Rei had read back in Japan.

Pulling a long sword from a rock, for instance, was a form of formal beauty that wasn't uncommon even in this world.

But beyond that, many other settings felt familiar.

(This... This was definitely written by someone with knowledge of Japan, specifically manga, right?)

Thinking this, Rei felt a question arise... He took the book and headed toward the librarian.

"Excuse me, do you know who wrote this book? If you happen to know, I'd appreciate it if you could tell me."

"Huh? Ah, um... The Adventures of Replus? This book is quite old, so who wrote it..."

The librarian shook her head apologetically.

"A long time ago? How long ago, specifically?"

"Hmm... At least since I was a child, I remember my grandfather reading it to me, so I think it's at least that old. There seem to be countless copies of the manuscript too."

The librarian was in her forties.

If the librarian's grandfather read it to him when he was a child, that would mean twenty or thirty years ago, or more.

"I see."

Rei had been certain there must be others besides himself who had come to this Elgin from Japan.

That's why she sighed disappointedly.

"I see. My apologies."

"No, I'm sorry I couldn't give you the exact year."

The librarian bowed slightly toward Rei.

But Rei shook his head at the librarian, signaling it was fine, and returned to his previous seat.

Behind Rei, the librarian who had finally lifted her head was now receiving envious glances from the other librarians.

They were probably envious that she had spoken with Rei.

Considering Rei's fame within Gilm, it wasn't surprising that some would react that way.

Unaware of the behavior unfolding behind him, Rei sat down and turned his gaze back to the book.

(Whoever wrote this... no, definitely someone from Japan. And judging by the source material referenced here, probably a few years before I came to this world. Or maybe not?)

The source material referenced here mostly consists of manga and such released several years before Rei's time in Japan, according to her understanding. But that doesn't mean Rei came to Elgin just a few years ago.

Perhaps it was just coincidence that the author's knowledge happened to draw heavily from that period for the content in this book, and the person who actually wrote it lived in a world even further ahead than that.

(Well, the chronological inconsistencies should have been obvious back when Takumu was around. ...Wait. Could this mean Takumu wrote it and it just survived until now? Surely it wasn't written by Kabajido?)

The idea that the First Prince of the Bestir Empire wrote such a novel doesn't quite fit the image.

Besides, if it was already a fairly famous work thirty or forty years ago, it's not something Kabajido could have imitated.

(But what if it was Takumu?)

He was from Japan, just like me... yet unlike me or Kabajido, who came to this world as mere souls, he arrived with his physical body intact.

And if Takumu arrived in this world long before either Kabajido or myself, it wouldn't be surprising if he wrote a book or two.

...Even so, Takumu's era wasn't decades ago, so it seems strange that even a manuscript from that time would still exist.

(So it must be someone other than Takumu? Well, it's certainly possible. Regardless of me, it's unclear why Takumu or Kabajido came to Elgin in the first place.)

Considering the era they lived in, it wouldn't be strange to think there were others besides Takum, Kabajido, and myself who came from Japan.

"So it's impossible to find out about this author right away after all?"

If they're still alive, I want to contact them by any means necessary.

That's what Rei thought, but if they weren't alive to begin with, there was nothing he could do.

He couldn't rule out the possibility of descendants, but what Rei wanted to meet was the Japanese author himself, not any descendants.

Of course, if he could meet them, he'd like to, but he didn't feel motivated enough to search intensely enough to think he might actually find the person himself.

"Well, I guess that's just how it is."

Feeling various regrets, Rei didn't dwell on it further and turned his attention back to the novel he believed was written by one of his kind.

Even though the plot was familiar to Rei, he found himself captivated by the story as he read.

He finished reading it in less than an hour and returned the book to the shelf.

Maybe there are other novels written by the same author?

With that thought, he scanned the shelves, but many books didn't have the author's name printed on them.

(Academic books, though, tend to have the author's name printed pretty clearly on them.)

Feeling disappointed, Rei tried to pick another book...

Suddenly, the loud rumbling of his stomach reminded him that it was almost noon.

After all, he'd arrived at this library just after ten in the morning. Considering how long he'd been reading, it made perfect sense that it was already noon.

Moreover, remembering that Set was probably waiting outside the library, leaving him hungry at noon was simply not an option.

It was disappointing not to find information on the Silver Lion's materials, but discovering the novel he'd read earlier meant his trip to the library had been worthwhile.

Feeling satisfied that it hadn't been a wasted trip, Rei left the library...

"Well, yeah. That makes sense with Set around."

The sight unfolding before his eyes left him both exasperated and strangely convinced.

There, several people had gathered around Set, each petting him or offering him food.

Beyond those directly petting or feeding Set, there were others simply watching the scene with satisfaction.

All told, there were easily more than ten people.

Set purred happily as he was petted by this crowd.

He was also enjoying easy-to-carry foods like sandwiches and skewered meats.

"Ah, Team Rei... Rei-san!"

Seeing Rei, one of the people around Set waved happily.

It was one of the party members who had gone to the dungeon with Johanna.

"Aren't you going to get used to how I address you yet?"

"Ahahaha. Sorry, I just can't help it..."

While Rei and the former guerrilla squad members had known each other for quite some time, Rei hadn't interacted with them as their captain for all that long.

In fact, their association since the end of the Bestir Empire's civil war was already longer.

Still, to the former guerrilla members, Rei was undoubtedly their captain.

That's how profoundly the shock they felt seeing Rei during the civil war had impacted them, and that's precisely why the image of Rei as their captain was so strongly etched into their minds.

Rei understood this, which is precisely why he couldn't bring himself to reprimand them too harshly.

Somehow, things always ended up being glossed over.

Of course, if Rei truly disliked being called captain, he would have insisted they correct it more firmly.

The reason things were the way they were was that Rei didn't seem to mind it that much.

"Speaking of which... about that party, when should we have it?"

"Ah, right, that too. ...Well, since we're at it, a barbecue party in the snow might actually be kind of fun."

The idea suddenly struck Rei, and Rei murmured it with a smile.
