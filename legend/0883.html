While Johanna and Rent were busy processing Gamelion's entrails alongside the beast's fangs, Rei was naturally riding on Set's back, soaring through the sky.

Since Set, left behind as the carriage guard, was now flying through the air like this, it was only natural that no one was anywhere near the carriage.

However, given that Set had been there just moments before, and considering the lingering scent of the griffin Set on the carriage, if asked how many monsters would actually attack it, the answer would likely be very few.

Of course, it wasn't absolutely guaranteed to be safe, but it was still highly secure.

"Besides, since we're up here, it's not hard to spot any suspicious movements below."

"Grr?"

At Rei's mutter, Set turned around, asking, "What is it?"

Rei stroked Set's head and shook his head, signaling it was nothing.

An altitude of nearly 100 meters above ground was the perfect vantage point for Rei and Set to survey the land below.

Even now, several adventurer parties could be seen battling Gamelion in the direction of Rei's gaze.

Some were struggling, but none were in genuine danger.

"...Ah, it got away."

Rei murmured as he watched the party in his line of sight get shaken off and lose the Gamelion they'd been fighting.

They had succeeded in inflicting some damage, but the Gamelion, instinctively judging that victory was impossible due to that damage, fled.

While Gamelions were generally ferocious and highly aggressive, it seemed some intelligent individuals understood what would happen to them if they continued fighting.

"Those Gamelions survive... while the ones like the one over there die, I suppose."

His gaze shifted to the Gamelion currently launching a one-sided attack on the adventurer party.

With fangs, blade-like ears, a tail that served as both whip and spear, and its sheer bulk, it seemed to be trampling everything in its path.

But if you watched the fight closely, you'd notice that while the adventurer party was taking hits, there were no signs of anyone suffering fatal damage, only minor injuries at most.

Though under relentless assault, the adventurers were solely focused on defense—dodging, evading, and blocking with shields—which is precisely why Gamelion appeared to hold such overwhelming dominance.

And then, two groups are moving around the Gamelion, which is relentlessly attacking the enemy before it.

Normally, Gamelion's keen hearing would have alerted him to any unusual activity immediately.

However, this Gamelion is desperately attacking the enemy before him. Each strike causes shields, armor, and weapons to clang loudly, drowning out his own hearing.

Furthermore, Gamelion's head was probably filled with rage.

"More likely, that flashy defense has another purpose besides drawing Gamelion's attention."

Rei's observation proved correct within mere minutes.

Two parties that had circled around from behind launched attacks on Gamelion, who had been focused solely on the one-sided assault.

Completely focused on the party directly before him, Gamelion failed to notice the threat closing in from behind... until a hammer struck his back, finally alerting him to their presence.

Even realizing the presence behind him after taking that fatal blow, a reversal was impossible.

Even if it tried to flee, the damage from the initial blow was too severe, slowing its movements.

Once that happened, there was nothing left to be done for that Gamelion.

Surrounded by three adventurer parties, it was attacked relentlessly and lost its life.

Watching this scene, Rei was impressed by the three parties' seamless coordination.

Three parties—just words, really—but the parties in his line of sight numbered at least four members each. The largest had seven.

The sight of parties of that size coordinating, avoiding interference with each other while dealing precise damage to their target, meant these three groups were accustomed to fighting together.

"Probably parties that hunt Gamelions every year. Or maybe they team up regularly to target big game."

"Gururu!"

Set growled at Rei's mutter, but unlike usual, it wasn't a sign of agreement.

Rei found Set's behavior puzzling, but the moment he met Set's gaze, he understood what the beast wanted to say.

Set was staring at the carriage they had used to get here.

That was fine. The problem was that several people who looked like adventurers were gathered around the carriage.

At first, Rei thought they were just adventurers who had spotted the carriage and come to look. But seeing them open the carriage doors to inspect inside, and touching the horses as if checking their condition, he quickly understood what they were up to.

"Carriage thieves, huh. Well, carriages do fetch a decent price. If they didn't know any better, targeting it wouldn't be so bad... but they're unlucky bastards. Set."

"Grrr!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a sharp cry and began descending toward the ground, flapping his wings.

Though the height was only about a hundred meters, that distance meant little to Set with his wings flapping.

In the blink of an eye, he closed in on the carriage. Before the men rummaging through it could even notice anything was wrong, Set touched down on the ground.

The fact he landed almost silently spoke volumes about Set's incredible prowess.

Rei dismounted from Set's back and stroked his back gratefully.

Then, pulling the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring, he addressed the adventurers inside the box carriage.

"You there, thief, come out."

His words clearly acknowledged their presence.

The adventurers rummaging through the box wagon for anything valuable likely realized they couldn't bluff their way out of this. They gave up and emerged from the wagon.

Though their surrender meant not apologizing to the wagon's owner, but threatening to seize the wagon by force.

In other words, they had decided not to settle things peacefully, but to seize the carriage by force.

Four men emerged from the carriage, their gazes fixed on the others with an intimidating intensity.

...But upon seeing the figure outside the carriage, those four men tasted despair.

The small figure clad in robes held a scythe in their hand, a blade larger than their own height.

Standing directly behind that figure was a griffin, gazing at them with piercing eyes.

At that moment, the men realized exactly who stood before them.

The very person renowned in Gilm as someone not to be trifled with.

"...Huh?"

"Hey, wait a minute..."

"Why is he here?"

"Well, it's not like it's impossible for him to be here, but the carriage..."

Each muttered under their breath, instantly dissipating any hostility.

They'd heard the rumors countless times: the person standing before them showed no mercy to those deemed enemies.

If they antagonized him now, they'd undoubtedly face consequences far beyond a joke.

They understood this, which was why they acted... but it was a little too late.

"Now then, what was the story with you rummaging through the carriage I rode in? Care to elaborate on that?"

"W-well... I saw the carriage just sitting here, so I figured someone must have left it behind."

"...Forgot a carriage, huh?"

The explanation—no, the excuse—from the man who seemed to be their leader drew a wry smile from Rei.

"Do you really think anyone here would believe that?"

Laying the blade of his Death Scythe across his chest, Rei asked. The men reflexively took several steps back.

Yet they didn't reach for their weapons. It was likely because they wanted to avoid outright hostility with Rei.

If they antagonized Rei here, it would mean fighting Rei.

Even facing Rei alone offered almost no chance of victory, but with Set present as well, it was hopeless.

That's why the men desperately racked their brains, trying to find a way to escape this situation... yet, in the end, they understood it was hopeless.

The most fatal mistake, above all else, was undoubtedly being caught rummaging through the carriage.

Faced with that situation, which could only be called a fatal mistake, all the men could do was...

"We're sorry!"

...was to simply admit their guilt and apologize.

Since fighting offered no chance of victory, antagonizing Rei here was absolutely to be avoided.

Considering how to escape this situation... making lame excuses would only anger Rei further, leaving them no choice but to apologize sincerely.

"Hmph..."

Rei sighed, watching the men bow their heads in unison.

Since standing here like this was practically meaningless, he needed to find some kind of compromise.

Truth be told, Rei wasn't nearly as angry as he appeared.

It was true that their carriage had nearly been stolen, so feeling annoyed was understandable.

But fundamentally, Rei's very presence here was an irregularity.

Had Rei not come this time, the carriage would very likely have been stolen.

(You could say Johanna and the others weren't vigilant enough... but well, since we asked Set to guard the carriage, it's only natural to assume we didn't need to worry about that. In that sense, it was also reckless of me to send Set up into the sky to search for Gamelion. If we were taking Set, we should have just brought someone else to guard the carriage from the start.)

Ultimately, I think my own mistake was significant, but that doesn't mean Rei is so lenient as to simply let the carriage thieves go.

I hesitated for a few seconds. Even knowing he was apologizing because he didn't want to antagonize me and didn't want to attack me in a rage, it was true that this made it harder for me to act against him at this point.

After weighing my options, the conclusion I reached was...

"Alright then. As an apology, each of you hand over one platinum coin. I'll let this matter drop."

A fine of one platinum coin each.

Hearing this, the men reflexively protested.

"No way! We don't have that kind of money!"

"Yeah, that's right! If we had that kind of money, we wouldn't be doing this!"

"This is outrageous! Outrageous!"

"Just because you've got a nickname doesn't mean you can go too far!"

The commotion began like that, but Rei spoke up before the men.

"So, do you want me to arrest you and hand you over to the guards? Or do you want to fight me as thieves? Either one is fine. You can choose whichever you prefer."

"Grrrrrrr"

Rei asked, Death Scythe in hand, while beside him, Set clawed at the grass with his feet and growled belligerently.

Whichever option they chose, it was certain they wouldn't fare well.

But handing over the platinum coin from their meager savings to Rei would make surviving this winter even harder.

He'd undoubtedly have to take on some job in the cold.

But if the guards turned him in, it would mean a fine or a flogging... and if other crimes came to light, the worst possible future was ending up as a slave.

And fighting Rei? Absolutely not.

That would be practically like being told to die.

In the end, what the men chose was...

"Four platinum coins, received."

Taking the platinum coins the men reluctantly handed over, Rei flashed a grin that could almost be described as smug.

It wasn't quite fatal for the men, but it was undoubtedly a significant expense.

It would undoubtedly leave them short of the funds needed to survive the winter.

Turning to the men, their faces twisted in bitterness, Rei stroked Set and spoke.

"Let me make this clear: don't even think about trying to earn back this loss through similar crimes. If I catch you next... well, what should I do? Steal a few more platinum coins? Hand you over to the guards without question? Or... let you taste the sharp edge of the Death Scythe for yourself?"

Hearing Rei's words, a chill ran down the men's spines.

Rei realized he was speaking his true thoughts without a doubt.

If they were caught trying to steal and sell off a carriage or cart in the same way, Rei would undoubtedly carry out what he had said.

Being handed over to the guards or losing platinum coins was one thing, but if they truly antagonized Rei, survival was impossible.

Knowing this, the men nodded quickly and repeatedly.

"...I see. If you understand, that's enough. Don't make any more foolish mistakes. Go."

At Rei's words, the men, wanting nothing more to do with this place, quickly departed.

Watching their backs disappear, Rei sighed before taking to the skies once more with Set, continuing the search for Gamelion.
