As far as Rei knows, there are several people who have come to this world of Elgin from Japan.

Whether they can transfer here in the same physical form they had in Japan, or whether they are reborn as humans in this world...

...In that sense, though, it’s hard to say which applies to Rei.

His mind remains exactly as it was when he was in Japan, while his spirit has possessed a body created by Zepair.

In any case, for whatever reason, the number of people coming to Elgin from Earth—or Japan—may be rare, but it’s certainly not zero.

There is no doubt that that many have come.

And the black wolf currently lying dead on the ground right in front of Rei was likely one of them.

No, considering he mentioned the words “Yokohama Chinatown,” it’s not just likely—it’s practically a certainty.

(Or, there’s a possibility that there was actually another Japanese person here, and he heard the story from them… but no, that’s unlikely.)

As Rei gazed at the Black Wolf’s corpse, he thought this, but immediately dismissed the idea.

The fact that he was entertaining such an unlikely thought was likely because this entire incident had been completely unexpected for Rei as well.

It wasn’t about the issue of not wanting to kill Black Wolf, or the whole “feeding him” business—it was something entirely separate… As he thought that far, Rei recalled how strongly Black Wolf had been fixated on the steamed buns.

And the last words the black wolf had left behind.

“I want to eat the braised pork buns from Yokohama Chinatown that I used to eat with my mother,” it had said.

Putting those two things together, it seemed certain that the Black Wolf was indeed someone who had come from Japan.

Of course, even if that were the case, there were still things he didn’t understand.

Why hadn’t he reacted when Rei had shown him the steamed buns before?

It almost seemed as if he only remembered his time in Japan when he was on the verge of death.

(What on earth is going on here?)

Rei was pondering this while looking at Black Wolf’s corpse, but there was another person present besides him.

Perhaps because the incident with Black Wolf had ended so unexpectedly, Rei had completely forgotten about it…

“Rei, what’s wrong?”

“Huh!? …Ah, no. Just a moment. The words Black Wolf spoke before he died… were about the world I was in before I came to this one.”

“……What?”

Elena, who had inherited the Ancient Dragon’s magic stone, naturally possessed keen senses.

Even so, Elena knew there was a connection between Rei and the Black Wolf that she wasn’t aware of, and that was precisely why she had covered her ears, thinking it might be inappropriate for her to hear the words the Black Wolf left behind at the moment of his death… but she never imagined such words would come from Rei’s mouth.

“Is that true?”

“Yeah. He spoke a phrase that points to a certain place in my country. Naturally, I don’t think I ever said those words in front of the Black Wolf…”

The reason he couldn’t say that for certain was probably because he had given the steamed bun to the Black Wolf.

It was possible that I might have mentioned Yokohama Chinatown during our casual conversation back then.

Speaking of Yokohama Chinatown, that’s a famous place even Rei knows about.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange for him to associate words related to that area with the steamed bun.

But... as he thought that, Rei immediately dismissed the idea.

“With his mother,” he thought. Black Wolf had definitely said that.

Given that, it was hard to believe that the words “Yokohama Chinatown” had slipped out of Rei’s mouth.

“No, that’s right. I definitely didn’t say those words in front of Black Wolf. I didn’t… but honestly, I’m stumped.”

The final moments of the battle with Black Wolf had pushed him to his limits in every sense.

Probably… no, without a doubt, if time had continued to pass like that, Black Wolf would have starved to death or met an even more tragic end.

In that case, there was no doubt that Rei taking his life was, in a way, a form of salvation.

Rei understood that, but it was also true that he felt she didn’t have to drop such a bombshell right before she died…

It was the same when he participated in the civil war of the Bestir Empire—why does the fact that he’s from Japan always come to light right before he dies?

He knows that, given the circumstances, there was nothing to be done about it. He knows that, but... Rei couldn’t help but feel it was unfair.

「Rei」

Elena called out to Rei again as he was lost in these thoughts.

However, the tone in her voice wasn’t exactly one of concern for Rei…

Perhaps noticing Elena’s voice, Rei turned his gaze toward her and asked, “What’s wrong?”

“Please listen to me calmly. The chances are very slim, but we might still be able to investigate the Black Wolf.”

“……What?”

The words that came out of Elena’s mouth were unexpected, even to Rei.

No, normally Rei might have realized the same thing, but since he had killed Black Wolf—a man who appeared to be from Japan, just like himself—Rei was still somewhat shaken and hadn’t been able to notice it.

“Black Wolf isn’t employed by any organization. Of course, he’s probably not completely unrelated to any organization, but on the surface, that’s how it appears. Given that, someone must be acting as an intermediary for Black Wolf’s assignments, and if that’s the case, wouldn’t that person know something about him?”

“That’s… I see.”

Rei found himself nodding in agreement at Elena’s words.

In reality, the Black Wolf that Rei knew definitely had, at the very least, difficulties with communication.

Given that, it was impossible to imagine Black Wolf receiving a commission directly from anyone.

That meant, just as Elena said, someone must have been acting as an intermediary for that request.

“But wouldn’t it be difficult to find out who acted as the intermediary for that request?”

The question that came out of Rei’s mouth was undoubtedly valid.

After all, if they had known who was acting as the intermediary, the Duke of Kerebel’s household would never have left an assassin like Black Wolf alone.

It wouldn’t have been strange at all for them to have taken action—whether that meant capturing the Black Wolf… or, if that was impossible, simply killing him.

“Hmm. It would be difficult. That’s true. But it wouldn’t be strange if there were some kind of clue. …The people who serve the Duke of Kerebel are highly capable, aren’t they?”

There was a note of pride in Elena’s tone.

That alone showed how much she trusted those who served her family.

In fact, since Rei had stayed with the Duke of Kerebel’s household as a guest, albeit for a short time, he understood that those serving the family were capable.

That was precisely why he had no objection to Elena’s words and could nod in agreement without hesitation.

“I see. Alright, then. Let’s go get that information right away. …But will they just hand it over to us that easily? Well, maybe if Elena’s there, it might be possible.”

“I’m not sure. To be honest, we won’t know until we actually ask. Even though I’m a member of the Kerebel Duke’s household, if it’s something truly important, I probably can’t reveal it without my father’s permission. But we can decide on that after we’ve actually asked.”

“If I can’t get it, I can always ask my father to grant permission.”

As Elena concluded her words, Rei expressed his gratitude.

“Sorry about that. …Well then, let’s head back right away. Honestly, I really just came here to buy some souvenirs. I never imagined things would turn out like this.”

“Now that you mention it, you’re right. Really… oh, really…”

Rei’s words reminded Elena that this outing had been a date between her and Rei.

The battle with the Black Wolf had completely ruined the mood, but that only made this whole situation all the more regrettable.

(The Black Wolf... no, whoever hired him—I absolutely cannot forgive them.)

There were two reasons behind Elena’s resolve.

One was, naturally, that they had interrupted her date with Rei.

The other was that she had ended up killing the Black Wolf, who appeared to have come from the same place as Rei.

That said, regarding the latter point, there’s no doubt that this only came to light because the Black Wolf had targeted Rei.

After all, if the Black Wolf hadn’t gone so far as to target Rei, Rei and the Black Wolf would never have met in the first place.

In that sense, the fact that Rei was able to encounter the black wolf was because the black wolf targeted him...

“Elena, what’s wrong?”

Unable to just leave the black wolf’s corpse behind, Rei, who had stored it in his Misty Ring, asked.

To Rei, the Black Wolf was someone who had come from Japan, just like himself.

Leaving the corpse of such a person behind wasn’t exactly ideal, and above all, if they left the body here, it was certain to cause a huge commotion if someone found it.

Fortunately, it was winter and the temperature was low, so even if the body wasn’t found, it wouldn’t start to stink. Still, there was a risk of spreading infectious diseases, or in some cases, the possibility of it turning into an undead.

Considering all that, he acted on the decision to bury or burn the Black Wolf’s body himself later.

“No, it’s nothing. More importantly, if we stay here, there’s a high chance of causing a commotion. To avoid that, we should leave this place now.”

“...Are you sure?”

Due to the battle between Rei and the Black Wolf, the surrounding area has suffered significant damage—though not quite catastrophic.

Considering that, wouldn’t it be better to explain the situation to the guards?

Hearing Rei’s words, Elena shook her head.

“We should handle that once we’re back at the mansion. Doing anything here and now could lead to trouble.”

“Is that so?” Rei wondered for a moment, but upon reflection, he realized that causing such a commotion in the middle of town would naturally lead to people asking for an explanation.

Given his current situation, Rei didn’t want to get into any trouble, and since his goal was to find an intermediary to commission the Black Wolf, he didn’t want to waste time on that here.

“Got it. Then let’s head back right away. So, who should I ask for information about Black Wolf?”

“Leave that to me.”

From Elena’s perspective, she’d decided it would be best not to introduce Rei to the person in charge of intelligence for the Duke of Kerebel’s household.

She acted alongside Rei and trusted him, but… that was a different matter altogether.

As the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel, Elena simply couldn’t bring herself to share that kind of information with Rei.

If it had been decided that Rei would marry Elena and become the next Duke of Kerebel—or the husband of the Duke of Kerebel—then it would have been a different story.

Rei probably understood, more or less, what Elena was thinking when she said that. He didn’t voice any complaints and simply nodded in agreement.

“I see. Well, that’s fine then. …Alright, let’s go.”

“Mm-hmm. …Still, this whole affair really has turned out in a way I never expected.”

Rei nodded in response to Elena’s wistful murmur.

Originally, the plan for today was just to buy some souvenirs.

Yet here they were, fighting the Black Wolf—there was no doubt this was a situation that could only be described as unexpected.

“I really wanted to buy souvenirs for a larger group, though. …But I’m definitely not in the mood for that right now.”

After fighting to the death against the Black Wolf—an opponent from his own hometown—he simply wasn’t in the mood to go shopping for souvenirs anymore.

If he forced himself, he might be able to go shopping like that, but Rei judged that there was no need to push himself in the current situation.

“Hmm. If I buy souvenirs in this state of mind, there’s no doubt the people receiving them won’t find it very enjoyable. In that case, how about we just buy the rest of the souvenirs later? Fortunately, we don’t have to leave Anesis right away.”

Although they had said they would return to Gilm once the New Year’s party was over, no specific date had been set.

Given that, Elena decided that postponing their departure by a day or two wouldn’t be a problem.

In the pitch-black darkness, one of the candle flames—the only light illuminating the darkness aside from the floating lights—suddenly went out.

The person who noticed this looked around to see which candle flame had gone out... and spotted one of them.

However, that person noticed that the candle flame in their line of sight had gone out and was slightly surprised.

That said, their surprise stemmed more from the fact that the flame had remained lit until that moment.

They quickly lost interest in the candle flame and returned to the task they had been working on.
