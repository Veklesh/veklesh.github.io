Resonance suddenly drew his last breath mid-conversation.

Naturally, Rei , Zarust, Narsus, and the others—who had been conversing normally just seconds before—couldn't hide their shock at the sudden turn of events.

"Huh? Hey, Rei. Is this some kind of joke?"

That's probably why Zarust asked Rei that question, looking as if he couldn't help himself.

But Zarust understood that Resonance was dead.

He asked anyway because the current situation was so utterly unexpected.

Of course, he had seen the blood pouring from his mouth, the numerous spears piercing his body, and the iron ball Rei had dropped from the sky crushing roughly half his body.

Given that state, he certainly didn't expect him to survive long... but even so, he probably never imagined the man who had been talking normally just moments ago would lose his life so abruptly.

"...No doubt about it. He's dead."

Unlike Zarust, who asked Rei in shock, Narsas swiftly approached Resonance and began checking whether he was breathing, if he had a pulse, if his heart was beating...

Even among adventurers, the difference in the hellish situations they'd each faced before was starkly evident.

"You're sure about this?"

Rei asked, seeking confirmation after Narsus declared the man dead.

But the only response from Narsus was a silent nod.

"GRRRR!"

Suddenly, Set let out a sharp cry.

Could it be a monster drawn by the scent of blood?

The three of them judged as much and prepared to draw their weapons, but there was no sign of a monster approaching.

「……Set？」

Set turned his gaze toward the figures lying fallen a short distance away, where Rei had directed his question.

Some were corpses, others had lost limbs but still lived, and a few incredibly lucky ones were merely unconscious.

Seeing them, Set growled. For a moment, Rei was confused, not understanding why he'd growled... but the next instant, he remembered the assassin they'd fought in the lab.

"Suicide poison!?"

He shouted quickly, rushing toward the bodies sprawled across the desert.

Zalust and Narsus must have understood why Set had let out that cry from Rei's words. They immediately followed after Rei.

Reaching the man closest to him, what Rei saw was a figure already dead, green liquid flowing from his mouth.

"No good, he's dead!"

"Same here. ...Suicide poison, huh?"

Zalust shouted. Narsus nodded in agreement, murmuring Rei's words as if repeating them.

He had heard about suicide poison from Daldora and the other guards.

Despite that, his muttered words were tinged with regret at having allowed it to be used right here.

"...Wait a moment."

The groans of the wounded had already faded away, leaving only the sound of the cold desert wind howling through the night.

Amidst this, Rei's words echoed softly but distinctly around them.

What is it? As Zarust and Narsus turned their gaze toward Rei, Rei directed his own gaze toward the village... the village protected by the artifact, according to Resonance.

Those who had come out to meet them chose death by drinking poison the moment they admitted defeat.

And what lay beyond Rei's gaze was the assassins' village. Naturally, it wasn't unusual for those not participating in the actual battle—women, children, and the elderly—to be present.

But if the assassins—the village's fighting force—were dead, what would become of those people?

As Rei gazed toward the village, Zarust and Narsus, seemingly realizing the same thing, grimaced visibly.

Even if it was small, it was still a village.

And considering the number of people who fought against them, it wouldn't be surprising if a considerable number of people were still in the village.

What had become of them... Realizing this, the three hurriedly ran toward the village.

"Set! Keep watch for monsters!"

"Guruu!"

Set let out a growl in response to Rei's shout as they ran toward the village.

Reasoning that with Set there, he needn't worry if any monsters did come, Rei followed after Zarust and Narsus, who were already ahead.

In an instant, Rei caught up to them, then passed them and entered the village.

Given the fierce battle that had just occurred outside the village, it was only natural that no one was outside their homes.

Still, if someone was there, it wouldn't be strange for them to be peeking through the cracks in the houses... thinking this, and feeling a bad premonition, he roughly opened the door of a nearby house.

There was no lock to speak of, and the house opened easily. Inside was...

"Damn it, just as I thought."

A bitter mutter escaped Rei's lips.

Inside the house, two elderly people lay collapsed, green liquid and blood flowing from their mouths.

The same cause of death as those he'd seen before.

...The only solace was the contented smiles on the faces of those two elderly people.

「Rei！」

Rei silently shook his head at Zarust and Narsus, who had burst into the house shouting.

Seeing Rei's gesture, and then the two elderly people collapsed inside the house, the village's dire situation was predictable.

Still, thinking there might be a chance, a slim possibility, the three split up to search the village.

For a moment, the thought flashed through Zarust and Narsus' minds that someone might still be fighting as an assassin, but they judged that confirming the villagers' lives and deaths took priority over that now. Accepting some danger, they began acting separately.

What Rei and the others saw as they split up... was that there was no one alive in any of the houses.

Elders and children.

Among them was a baby, seemingly just born, but every single one had been killed, green liquid and blood flowing from their mouths.

"...No."

After quickly scanning the village, Zarust and Narsas met up near the center. Hearing Rei's words, they both nodded solemnly.

This wasn't the first time they'd witnessed people dying; they'd experienced it countless times before.

But... even so, seeing helpless souls like the elderly and children dead was still no pleasant sight.

That was precisely why their expressions were grim.

"Why did you do this... Need I say more?"

As if to dispel the heavy atmosphere, Rei asked them both. Without hesitation, they nodded in response.

Using the blood of the Resonance clan and the Tira tree, it's possible to create a drug that eliminates pain.

While the exact method is unknown, if the premise is that it can be made, then actually creating it shouldn't be too difficult.

At the very least, it was undeniably far easier than starting from scratch with no knowledge whatsoever.

But... creating that medicine requires the blood of the Resonance clan. If they were to seriously pursue making it, the Resonance clan would become nothing more than livestock, milked dry for their blood.

Worried about that, and thinking that if it came to that, it would be better to end their lives while they still had dignity... that was Rei's prediction, and Zarust and Narsus agreed with him.

"It's not for me to say, but if Daldora learned of this matter, he'd surely try to make the medicine. ...He suffered significant losses this time."

Zalust nodded at Narsus's words, knowing his employer was lenient with subordinates but unhesitating when pursuing profit.

Having lived in Gosh, Zarust understood Daldora's character well.

After all, she had tried to have Zarust and Augusto attacked by sand pirates for her own gain.

(Well, it did result in the sand ship being stolen by Rei, though.)

But that was only because Rei was there.

Had Rei not been there, they would have been wiped out... or suffered heavy casualties at the very least.

Many of the camels pulling the carriage would have died, and the cargo loaded onto that carriage would undoubtedly have been lost.

And, most likely, even the life of Augusto—who the sand pirates were probably ordered to kill—might have been lost.

"But..."

Zalust, lost in thoughts of Daldora, snapped back to reality at Narsus's words.

"But?"

"No, now that we've found this place, it's definitely a huge benefit for Gosh, right?"

Hearing this, Zarust and Rei looked around, realizing he was right.

Everyone, including Rei, confirmed that there was a small oasis here.

While there were numerous corpses inside the houses now, once those were cleared away, the houses could be used normally.

In other words, Gosh had essentially gained another village for free.

Of course, it wasn't without problems.

For instance, until now, an artifact had maintained a barrier to keep them hidden from others, but that barrier had been sliced apart by a single strike from Rei's Death Scythe, and the artifact itself was now destroyed.

This means that to use this village, they must figure out how to deal with monsters and sand pirates.

Even a small village is still a village.

Managing it will likely require significant expense.

"Ah, why not conduct Tira's tree experiment here?"

Narsus frowned slightly at the words that suddenly slipped from Rei's mouth.

They already knew that when a certain number of Tira Trees gathered, they attacked people seeking sustenance.

Therefore, Narsus's true feelings were that he'd rather not live in a place where such dangerous experiments were conducted.

That said, Narsus was guarding Daldora, and he couldn't imagine the overly cautious Daldora ever coming to a place where experiments with Tira trees were being conducted.

(Besides, the danger of the Tira tree extends beyond that. The medicine... We'll need to figure something out about this.)

He recalled the conversation he'd just had with Rei and Zarust.

As someone employed by him, he had no choice but to report to Daldora about the medicine designed to prevent pain.

If that happened, Daldora would naturally begin research on the medicine from the Tira Tree.

It wasn't a matter of whether it could be done or not; the medicine definitely existed.

The problem, however, remains the ingredients.

If they were to look for something to replace the blood of the Resonance clan, the first thing they would naturally try would be human blood.

It would be strange if only the blood of a specific clan could be used as material, so at the very least, using the blood of ordinary people nearby would be a safe bet.

(If they were to conduct such experiments here... it wouldn't be surprising if undead started appearing.)

After all, this was a village originally settled by an assassin clan; it wouldn't be strange if undead appeared.

Considering that, I didn't want to conduct such experiments here.

"I'd rather not turn this place into that sort of thing, if possible. We've got an oasis here, after all. If it could become a sort of Gosh enclave, we'd welcome it wholeheartedly."

"...I understand what you're saying, Narsus, but the problem is whether Daldora will agree to it. Honestly, I think it'll be difficult."

Zalust's words carried weight, born of years spent opposing Daldora.

"But even if it's small, we've gained an entire village. Shouldn't we just leave that part to Ryubrand? ...Though Daldora will probably have something to say about it."

Rei's response implied he wouldn't leave it entirely to Daldora, but Zarust and Narsus both shook their heads in unison.

Rei looked at the two of them with a hint of sympathy.

It was sad when the lord of the town you lived in wasn't competent.

...Though objectively speaking, Ryubrand wasn't exactly competent, but he wasn't incompetent either.

In fact, thanks to listening to Daldora's opinions, Gosh had developed more than before.

"Well, anyway, there's no point staying here forever. Shouldn't we head back soon? ...It's not impossible for us alone to burn all the corpses... but it'd be a pain, right? Better to call for help and have someone else do it."

If we leave the bodies as they are, they'll likely turn into undead.

We need to dispose of them quickly to prevent that... but even if they're small, it's still a whole village's worth of bodies.

There was no doubt it would be extremely laborious for just the three of us.

No matter how capable these three were compared to ordinary humans, what this task required wasn't a few highly skilled individuals, but manpower.

Rei's words made sense—leaving the bodies here risked monster attacks. So Rei, who could reach Gosh faster, rode Set there while Zarust and Narsas stayed behind.
