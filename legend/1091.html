Emerging from beyond the sand dunes was clearly something that should be called a ship.

Sails were stretched across the ship's masts, riding the desert wind as it raced across the sand.

Nearly ten meters long, it looked like an ordinary ship to the casual observer.

There were no oars or paddles to be seen; it truly looked like an utterly ordinary vessel.

"I've heard people call the desert a sea of sand... but still, isn't this a bit strange? A ship on sand, for crying out loud..."

As Rei muttered while watching the ship rapidly approaching, Zarust, weapons at the ready, spoke up.

"A sand ship? Damn it, these sand pirates are bigger than I thought. I figured they'd just attack normally."

"Sand ship?"

"...Ah, right. This is your first time in the Ferris Desert, Rei."

Slightly surprised by Rei's words, Zarust continued his explanation while still giving detailed instructions to the adventurers.

"Sand Ships. As the name implies, they're ships that run on sand. Being magic items, they're obviously not something you can just easily get your hands on..."

"Huh. A magic item... huh?"

The surprise from seconds ago had already faded. What Rei saw now was the magic item called the Sand Ship.

...Yes, a magic item. And Rei's hobby was collecting magic items that were actually usable—especially in combat—not mere trinkets. For someone like Rei, a magic item like a Sand Ship was something he absolutely wanted to get his hands on.

"Just to be sure, in the Mireana Kingdom, if you defeat bandits, the treasure they carried basically becomes yours... Is it the same here?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah. ...Are you planning to take down the Sand Pirates alone?"

"Yeah. Normally, I could have helped you guys take down these Sand Pirates, but with that magic item called the Sand Ship involved, it's not that simple, is it?"

Zalust nodded at Rei's words.

Unfortunately, there were no mages among those guarding August.

Some carried bows, but their numbers were too few to offer much offensive capability.

In comparison, the enemy was clearly planning to attack from the start using the Sand Ship, so they undoubtedly had more ranged attack options.

Considering that, it was undeniably true that Zarust and his men were at a disadvantage.

Understanding this, Zarust turned his gaze toward Augusto.

Normally, decisions regarding the escort were left entirely to Zarust, but this time, facing the unique threat of a Sand Ship, he likely felt it was best for his employer, Augusto, to make the call.

"...Yes, that's right. Honestly, a sand ship is an extremely expensive magic item, so it's not something sand pirates could easily obtain."

Augusto stated as he watched the Sand Ship approach.

His tone carried a hint of gloom.

"Augusto?"

"...Rei. I suspect that sand ship was prepared by someone who wants me dead. Of course, it's not impossible that those sand pirates acquired it by chance, but..."

"The Elema Trading Company, was it?"

"Yes. ...I suppose I'm quite a nuisance to them."

"But you're not just going to die silently, are you? Besides, if that sand ship is a valuable magic item, imagine how the person who plotted this would feel if they lost it..."

There was no doubt they gave the sand pirates that expensive magic item, the Sand Ship, determined to eliminate the nuisance right here.

But considering the personality of the Erema Trading Company's heir that Rei had heard about, it was hard to believe they had truly given the Sand Ship to the sand pirates.

(Most likely, he just lent it to them if they were lucky, and once this incident is over, he'll take the Sand Ship back. Worst case, it's a way to silence them.)

Of course, the sand pirates probably see through that much too. But Rei guessed they figured if they killed Augusto and then simply fled aboard the Sand Ship, they could claim they'd fulfilled their obligation...

"As I mentioned earlier, the sand ship is extremely valuable. If it were to disappear, it would cause no small loss to the Elema Trading Company."

Augusto's words were exactly what Rei had hoped for.

Smiling, Rei watched the approaching sand ship and spoke.

"I heard it before, but the treasure the bandits had naturally belongs to whoever defeated them. And I was that sand pirate, right? If I defeat the guy on the Sand Ship, ownership of the Sand Ship naturally becomes mine too. That's correct, isn't it?"

"That... yes. There's no mistake about it... but judging from what you just said, do you intend to take on the sand pirates alone?"

"Yeah. That way, you guys won't have to deal with the sand ship, and you can inflict damage on that Elema Trading Company. I take down the sand pirates and get the sand ship, a valuable magic item. The people around here get rid of the sand pirates and gain safety. ...Not a bad deal for anyone, right?"

"No, I think it's the worst possible outcome for the Sand Pirates and the Elema Trading Company."

Zalust, who had been listening to Rei and August's conversation, couldn't help but interject.

It was true, but Rei didn't seem bothered by Zarust's words at all as he replied.

"Why should I worry about my enemies?"

"No, I guess not."

"Right? Anyway, since we're traveling together as escorts, I'll take care of those sand pirates myself. It's in my interest too. Is that okay with you?"

At Rei's declaration, Zarust, Augusto, and the others could only nod silently in agreement.

Beating the sand ship's crew with this many men wasn't impossible... but enduring their relentless attacks would surely result in heavy casualties.

It was highly likely that over half the caravan would die, and the guards would suffer similar losses.

If relying on Rei, who possessed the means to fly, could somehow turn things around, that was undoubtedly the best option.

...Within the decision to rely on Rei, there was, of course, a cold, calculated thought: since Rei had little connection to them, if he died, it would be better than a comrade dying... but Rei was acting with full knowledge of that.

Confident no one would object, Rei gave a small nod, then turned his gaze toward Set and spoke.

「Set」

"Grrrr."

At Rei's words, Set approached and crouched slightly to make it easier for Rei to mount him.

Leaping onto Set's back as usual, Rei gave his back a light pat.

Taking that as his cue, Set took a few running steps, flapped his wings, and soared up into the desert sky.

Of course, Set's figure could be seen from the sand ship as well, but it showed no signs of changing course or being on alert.

"So the sand ships got a magic item and now they're acting all cocky? Well, whatever works for us."

In the Kingdom of Mireana, bandits would flee the moment Set showed himself.

Considering that, the fact the sand pirates aboard the ship showed no sign of fleeing was refreshing, one might even say.

"Well, just because it's refreshing doesn't mean we're going to slack off. ...Set, I want to strike first. I want to secure that Sand Ship for us, so if possible, I'd like to avoid damaging it... Can you lend us the King's Intimidation?"

"GRRR!"

For Set too, this was the first time he'd used the King's Intimidation against enemies since leveling up.

For that reason alone, he flapped his wings, looking slightly pleased at Rei's words.

The sand ship rapidly closed in. The sand pirates on deck naturally spotted Rei and Set approaching.

"Hey, look at that! There's something coming from the sky!"

"Huh? The sky?"

One sand pirate, bow in hand, spotted Set approaching and alerted his companion beside him.

Then, as the sand pirate looked up... his movement froze.

"What the hell... That's... a griffin!?"

Hearing the sand pirate's cry, the others around him also turned their gaze toward the approaching figure...

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR

As if waiting for the sand pirates' reaction, Set let out a roar.

And it wasn't just any cry. It was the skill he'd leveled up after the World Tree incident: the King's Intimidation.

The moment they heard that roar, every sand pirate aboard the sand ship trembled and froze, unable to move.

"Ah... ah..."

A few managed to utter such sounds, but they were a mere handful. Nearly all were rendered unable to even cry out.

Rei, observing the sand ship from the sky, smiled in satisfaction at the expected outcome.

"Now we just need to stop the sand ship... What do you think we should do?"

"Guru?"

What should we do? Set growled in response to Rei's words.

If the sand ship continued straight ahead, it would inevitably plow into Augusto's caravan.

Since they were targeting Augustus and his men, that was an unchangeable fact.

"Then should we deal with whoever's controlling the sand ship?"

Just as Rei murmured this, the sand ship's movement began to slow, as if his words were a signal.

As if slamming on the brakes, the sand ship slowed to a halt right before the eyes of the Zarust who had been trying to retreat.

"...Well, it seems we managed to pull it off safely, after all."

Watching the stationary sand ship, Rei murmured with a sense of relief.

Had the ship not stopped, it would have been dangerous, but Rei had a feeling it would probably be okay.

It was somewhat optimistic, but even if it hadn't worked, this was the desert; there were no buildings or structures nearby.

Moreover, the Zarust and the others weren't about to be attacked by arrows from the approaching sand ship, so evading it shouldn't be too difficult.

The sand ship was a magic item he'd wanted if possible, but the fact that destroying it was an option in the worst case was significant.

"You're okay, right?"

Still, just to be sure, Rei descended to the ground while still mounted on Set and called out to Zarust and the others.

At Rei's call, Zarust and the others stiffened slightly but nodded in response.

That stiffness in their faces—was it because the Sand Ship had closed in right before them... or because of the roar emitted from Set using the King's Intimidation?

(The King's Intimidation shouldn't affect my allies, though. So then, it must be the sand ship?)

He pondered this, but the fact remained that they couldn't stay like this forever.

The sand pirates aboard the ship were currently immobilized by the King's Intimidation, but that effect wouldn't last forever.

"In the Kingdom of Mireana, we can sell thieves to slave traders. How about here?"

"Huh? Oh, that's the same here too."

Augusto snapped back to reality at Rei's question and replied. With that settled, Rei made his proposal.

"Would you help us bind up the Sand Pirates inside the Sand Ship? And escort us to Gosh? In return, I'd like us to split the money from selling the pirates as slaves."

"...Are you sure? That would mean you'd be taking a big loss, Rei."

"Yeah. I can't capture all the sand pirates alive on my own anyway. They'll probably regain their mobility while I'm tying them up one by one."

"They'll be able to move... So that loud cry from Set earlier was..."

"Yeah, just as I imagined. Anyway, I'll tie up the sand pirates and bring them along, so I'll leave that to you too. Since I'm asking you to handle all these chores, splitting the loot should be fair, right? Besides..."

Rei paused, shifting his gaze toward the sand ship.

"I've safely obtained the Sand Ship, my primary objective, completely intact. ...While I still don't know for certain who the true owner of this Sand Ship is, if they see all their sand pirates captured alive without putting up a fight, and then the Sand Ship itself taken... Well, I wonder what they'll think."

"They'll definitely be furious, no doubt about it."

"Right? Besides, capturing them alive means we can extract information. ...We can kill them anytime. But if we kill them, we can't get the information out of them."

Rei, wearing a wicked smile, immediately asked Set to head up to the sand ship.

Watching this, Augusto noticed there was no bravado or false bravado in the word "kill." It sent a chill down his spine, realizing just how many hellish battles the seemingly young Rei had survived.

Completely unaware of Augusto's thoughts, Rei lowered a rope ladder from the deck of the sand ship and called out to Zarust and the others.
