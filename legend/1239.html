"Hey, sit down, sit down. I'll bring the food right out, so just eat this while you wait."

The owner gruffly told Rei and the others, placing a plate piled high with ham and sausage on the table.

Technically, they had the place to themselves, but it wasn't because there was only one table left; it was simply that no other customers were present.

...Well, the space around the table where Rei and the others sat was deliberately left wide open, so even the large-framed Set could relax without any issues.

Who did that? Well, that was obvious.

It was obvious just by looking at Johanna, who had staked out a spot right next to the empty area, as if to say she'd never give up this spot to anyone.

Everyone at the table picked up sausages and ham from their plates and put them in their mouths, as if just to get started... and the next moment, several of them wore expressions of surprise.

Rei, Elena, Marina, Vihera, Ara... The five who had come to this shop for the first time wore expressions of surprise.

Set and Yellow, the two dogs, happily ate the ham and sausage on the plates prepared for them without making a sound.

Yes, the ham and sausage served looked perfectly ordinary from the outside—just the kind you'd find sold anywhere.

But the moment they tasted it, they knew it wasn't ordinary ham and sausage.

First, a subtle saltiness tickled the tongue, followed by the rich umami flavor of the meat itself spreading throughout the mouth.

The taste was a cut above the ham and sausage you'd find at an ordinary shop.

There was no way this was the kind of food you'd expect from a place like this.

Elena, Marina, Vihera, and Ara had all attended noble banquets on occasion. ...In Vihera's case, that was before she fled the Bestir Empire.

In any case, the ham and sausage on the table now were of such exceptional quality that they rarely appeared even at such noble banquets.

"Delicious! What is this...?"

Rei, having swallowed the sausage in his mouth, exclaimed involuntarily. Johanna and the men with her opened their mouths proudly.

"Even Rei, think it's delicious, huh?"

"See? I told you! I said the food here would satisfy even Rei."

"It's delicious, isn't it, Set-chan? Here, want some more?"

Though one of them was looking at Set instead of Rei.

"The stewed oak meat here is great, but honestly, everything is delicious. That's probably why Johanna chose this place for our celebration, right?"

"...Well, the food's good, but it's really because of you, Set, right?"

"Ah... yeah. Can't deny that."

Listening to the men's exchange, Rei and the others reached for their plates again.

Rei savored the flavors of the ham and sausages once more.

Before long, the owner emerged from the kitchen again, carrying several dishes.

"Hey, you guys, give me a hand. Cooking's easy enough, but bringing it out is a pain!"

"Ah, yes! We'll help right away!"

"Huh? Wait, Set!"

Johanna's companions, dragging Johanna away as she doted on Set, head toward the kitchen with the shopkeeper.

Seeing this, Rei and the others started to stand up to help too, but one of Johanna's companions smiled at them and said,

"Ah, it's fine for Rei and the others to stay right there. We're the ones hosting this celebration."

"...Is that okay? It looks pretty tough though."

"Yes, we're only using this table anyway. It's not that much trouble, so don't worry."

After seeing them off into the kitchen, Rei turned his gaze back to Elena and the others, reaching once more for the ham and sausages.

"Huh. Tasting it properly like this, I can tell they use quite a lot of spices. ...Given the price, it must be something special."

"Ah, no need to worry. The ingredients used in this shop's dishes are basically only ingredients harvested from the dungeon."

The shopkeeper, returning with an ale in hand, answered Marina's words.

Despite seeing Marina up close... and the stunning beauties like Elena and Vihera, the shopkeeper showed no particular interest, simply pouring ale into glasses and placing them on the tables.

Perhaps finding the shopkeeper's attitude rather likable, Marina smiled and spoke up.

"You make dishes this good using only ingredients from this dungeon?"

"Well, there are some tricky ingredients to work with, but in the end, it's all about skill. No matter how luxurious or rare the ingredients, if the chef's skill is lacking, it's meaningless. Conversely, even ingredients you can find anywhere can be transformed into a masterpiece with the right skill."

"...I see."

Marina nodded in agreement at the shopkeeper's words, yet felt a slight pang of guilt.

He might say ingredients are plentiful, but this ham, these sausages, and the meal they were about to eat were only possible because of materials obtained from the dungeon.

But the dungeon where those ingredients were obtained was certain to vanish soon.

Once that happened, she would never be able to eat these dishes again.

Well, maybe they could be eaten if the ingredients could be gathered, but even with the same ingredients, the taste differed depending on where they were obtained.

In other words, it was certain that we would never again be able to obtain ingredients exactly like those from this dungeon.

Seeing Marina like this, the shopkeeper's stern face showed just a hint of puzzlement.

But he said nothing more, returning to the kitchen to fetch the remaining dishes.

"Hey! Carrying it like that, you'll spill it on the way! Hold it tighter, dammit! You lot are supposed to be adventurers, aren't you?!"

Hearing the shout from the kitchen, Rei and the others exchanged glances and smiled.

Marina, who had been troubled just moments before, forgot her worries and smiled too.

And so, while being yelled at by the owner, Johanna and the others brought out dish after dish.

A few minutes later... before they knew it, the table was completely covered with plates of food.

And in the center of the table sat a large pot filled with stew.

It was Oak Stew House's signature dish, packed with plenty of oak meat.

And the source of that violent smell that drifted... no, assaulted them when they opened the door was that very pot.

An indescribably rich aroma that stimulated the appetite.

It was the kind of smell that made you realize the stew alone would be enough to satisfy you.

Not only Rei and the others seated at the table, but everyone else present had their attention drawn to that stew.

(Huh?)

Rei suddenly felt a twinge of doubt.

He understood why he and Elena, seeing the stew for the first time, were captivated.

But why were Johanna and the others, who had visited this shop many times before, also having their attention stolen by the stew?

As if sensing Rei's question, one of Johanna's companions spoke up.

"Well, we've eaten this restaurant's food many times before, but somehow this stew feels different from usual..."

"Well, that's only natural. You're paying a fortune to reserve the whole place. We use ingredients we can't normally afford, you know."

At the owner's words, everyone's expressions turned understanding.

It was a double expression of understanding.

One was the understanding that since they'd gone to the trouble of renting out the entire restaurant, it made sense to prepare dishes they couldn't normally make. The other was the understanding that they might get to eat food even tastier than usual.

...Though the latter was only true for Johanna and her group.

"Hey, it's a celebration, right? Cheers, cheers!"

The shopkeeper said this as he poured ale into the glasses that hadn't been filled yet.

He was about to pour into Rei's glass too, but Rei spoke up first.

"Sorry, but I'm not much of a drinker. Tea or something... if not, water would be fine. Do you have any?"

"Huh? You can't handle booze? You're not fit to be called an adventurer. Hold on a sec, I'll get you something to drink right away."

Despite the rough tone, the way he properly accommodated the customer's request was probably why he was the owner of a thriving shop.

(Is this what they call tsundere?)

Watching the owner retreat into the kitchen, Rei pondered.

Even then, the aroma wafting from the pot on the table never ceased, constantly whetting the appetites of those around them.

Soon, the owner returned from the kitchen and handed Rei a container filled with some kind of drink.

"What's this?"

Seeing the transparent container, Rei thought for a moment it might be glass, but upon touching it, he realized it was some kind of metal, not glass.

"No idea. Some alchemist made it from materials found in the dungeon. Anyway, it's got fruit water inside, so it should be just right for a kid like you."

Rei felt slightly annoyed by the "kid" remark, but considering his current appearance, it wasn't unreasonable. He also figured his inability to drink alcohol probably played a part, so he kept quiet.

Besides, he already knew from their previous exchanges that the shopkeeper here had a sharp tongue.

(Still, fruit water in winter... If it's stored inside Misty Ring, fine, but how?)

Needless to say, fruit water couldn't be made without fresh fruit.

That was Rei's common sense.

But since the fruit water was here now, it meant the owner had somehow obtained fresh fruit despite it being winter...

"Ah."

Immediately, he remembered the dungeon.

This time, Grimm's magic had effortlessly transported them all the way to the lowest level, but it was the forest level where they'd been attacked by a pack of water monkeys when descending with Elena.

In that forest, it shouldn't be impossible to gather fruit even in winter.

While Rei was processing this, drinks were passed around to everyone... and somehow, Rei ended up leading the toast.

After seeing the shopkeeper discreetly retreat to the kitchen, Rei spoke.

"To celebrate the safe resolution of all matters concerning the dungeon, Vihera, and Rodos... Cheers!"

"Cheers!"

Everyone raised their cups and spoke in unison.

Set and Yellow didn't have cups, unfortunately, but they seemed to understand they were celebrating too. They let out happy cries, and Yellow did the same on the table.

"Phew... Delicious! There's nothing like a drink after finishing a job like this!"

Johanna's male companion shouted happily after downing the ale in his cup.

The others seemed to agree, placing their ale-filled cups on the table, nodding, and opening their mouths.

"The best thing is that Vihera-san regained consciousness."

"Yeah, yeah. ...But I never imagined something could happen to make Lady Vihera lose consciousness."

"Set-chan, want some of this? It's delicious, you know?"

"Ah, I'll have the stew. Stew."

As they chatted like this, each reached for the food.

...But naturally, everyone's attention was focused on the pot in the center of the table... the restaurant's signature dish, oak meat stew.

Of course, the restaurant offered various other oak dishes as well.

Things simmered with vegetables besides the stew, or pure meat stews... and so on.

Still, the most popular dish was undoubtedly the stew sitting on the table right now.

Moreover, this stew was no ordinary stew. It was made with ingredients that wouldn't normally be used.

It was impossible not to focus on that stew.

Marina promptly began serving portions of the stew for everyone.

The stew served to Set and Yellow, transferred from the pot to plates and brought closer, released an aroma so potent it could be called an assault on the senses.

Using a spoon, Rei scooped up the first bite—naturally, it was the stewed oak meat.

Probably to make sure adventurers could eat properly. He tossed a generously cut piece of that meat into his mouth.

In that instant, the meat asserted itself in Rei's mouth with a vengeance.

Normally, stewed meat melts in your mouth, but this was different.

It retained a firm, meaty chew, yet an intense savory flavor spread throughout her mouth.

Despite being stewed, chewing the meat caused rich juices to overflow.

Was it grilled separately and added later?

That thought crossed Rei's mind, but she quickly dismissed it internally.

The stew flavor is thoroughly soaked into the meat. This couldn't be achieved without long hours of simmering.

"...Delicious..."

All Rei could do was murmur that, following the impulse from the depths of his heart.
