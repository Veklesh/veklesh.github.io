Guided by the man through the slums, Rei and the others walked for over ten minutes. Unlike when they had been moving on their own, they were able to proceed without getting lost.

Until just moments ago, it wouldn't have been surprising if slum dwellers attacked at the slightest opening, but now it was clear there were fewer people targeting Rei and the others than before.

"Is this because of that man?"

Elena murmured, scanning the surroundings.

Hearing her voice, the man guiding them turned with a forced smile and spoke.

"Well, it's because I'm here, you see. Since I'm guiding you, I think my acquaintances are holding back."

Perhaps trying to make a better impression on Rei and the others, his speech was somewhat more polite compared to when he'd been forcibly brought by Byune earlier and had spoken with Rei.

Polite, or rather, the language of a low-ranking subordinate, was more accurate.

"I see. But even so, it seems some are still targeting us?"

"The ones holding back are just my buddies, you know. Guys I don't know are still after us, I reckon."

"If they're going to make a move, I wish they'd just do it already. Honestly, this constant tailing isn't exactly pleasant."

"Well, if they attack, I'm the one in danger."

The man lived in the slums and had been waiting for an opening to attack Rei and the others, so he certainly wasn't lacking in confidence in his abilities.

But even so, fighting multiple residents from the same slum district wouldn't be an easy victory.

While camaraderie existed only among those connected to the man, they also didn't want to fight the slum dwellers if possible, knowing hostility could cause trouble later.

"If we keep going a little further, the number of guys trying to target us should decrease, so please bear with us just a little longer."

"A little further? You mean that place you mentioned earlier, where we're headed?"

"That's right. I'll excuse myself a little further down the road."

"Is that alright?" The man asked Rei with a glance. Rei nodded in response.

It was unusual for Rei to see someone clearly older than him being so deferential, but he shrugged it off as just another day.

For adventurers, ability was everything. That meant even someone as young as Rei could be respected if they had the skills.

...Though in Rei's case, perhaps due to his personality, he also had just as many enemies.

"My apologies. Shall we proceed?"

Satisfied that he wouldn't have to escort them all the way, the man moved ahead with a lighter step than before.

Though exasperated by his mercenary attitude, Rei and the others followed.

They walked on for over ten minutes. Eventually, the man stopped.

"I was told to guide you over there, you see."

At the man's words, everyone except Azimov looked convinced.

Several people were inside the building, and among them were those who gave off an aura suggesting they were reasonably strong.

(The residents of this slum may be accustomed to violence, but they're certainly not skilled fighters. Well, that's only considering the abilities of the ones who attacked us, so naturally, there are probably some skilled ones among them. In any case, if I were to take on the guy inside that shack using the skills I know...)

He didn't need to think to understand what came next.

And the man standing before me had witnessed that scene with his own eyes.

Even now, as we stood there, the man's legs were trembling ever so slightly.

Still, not letting that fear show on his face... that was pretty impressive.

(Huh.)

Impressed inwardly, Rei tossed a silver coin toward the man.

"Whoa! ...Heh heh, I'll take that. Well, I'll be off then. If you're going to do something to that building, be careful now."

He chuckled and started to walk away, but...

"Hey!"

Rei called out to his back.

Hearing the voice from behind, the man stopped dead in his tracks, unable to move further. He turned around with an awkward movement.

Then, the man's gaze met Rei's...

"Extra. Take this too."

With that command, a loaf of hardened black bread, baked for preservation, was tossed over.

For ordinary people, black bread... especially the kind adventurers carry as preserved rations, was hardly something anyone would willingly eat. Yet for the slum dwellers, it was practically a delicacy.

Catching the black bread as it flew through the air, the man opened his mouth, his expression one of utter confusion.

"Huh? Um, why?"

"Just felt like it. Anyway, I'm done with you now. You can go."

"Huh... um... well... I'll gladly take it then."

After muttering a quick thank you, the man didn't want to get caught up in whatever trouble might follow. He quickly ran off.

"What kind of whim is that?"

The words that came out of Elena's mouth clearly referred to Rei tossing the black bread.

At Elena's words, Rei smiled faintly and spoke.

"I just kinda liked it. The way you were clearly scared but didn't show it."

"...Is that so?"

Elena wore an expression that seemed slightly unconvinced by Rei's words, but she didn't press further, instead directing her gaze toward the building they were heading for.

"They're here."

"Yes. I wonder what kind of powerful opponent awaits us. I'm looking forward to it."

Vihera responded to Elena's words with a melting smile.

For Vihera, who naturally enjoyed combat, she hadn't had a satisfying fight in quite some time.

The fight with the Cyclops had been reasonably enjoyable, but it was still only reasonably so.

It was nowhere near the kind of battle that could truly ignite her soul.

Thinking that perhaps there was an opponent who could satisfy this hunger for battle within her, Vihera instinctively began to sense her surroundings.

It wasn't sinister enough to be called killing intent, but rather a pure aura of fighting spirit, born from the simple joy of combat.

Responding to Vihera's fighting spirit, movement stirred inside the hut.

Seconds later, the door opened, revealing five figures.

The five who stepped outside waited patiently, perhaps because they were still some distance from Rei and the others.

Rei recognized two of them.

The magic item shop owner, and the woman named Adria that Rei... or rather, Set had captured.

(I handed her over to the guards... but if she's here, she must have escaped. Or maybe they let her go intentionally to capture her when she reunited with her comrades? It's possible, but I can't imagine Ranga resorting to such an uncertain tactic. Unless there were no other options, that is.)

If Rei had known about the events that occurred while he was hiding in the magic item shop's basement, it might have been different. Unfortunately, Rei was unaware of Adria's escape, leading him to wonder if this might be part of Ranga's plan.

Still, considering Ranga's personality, he concluded it was probably not the case.

The others were a rugged-looking man with a beard covering his entire face, another man who seemed to be following him, and finally, a man who appeared to be of somewhat refined upbringing.

Several glass bottles hung from the latter's waist, evoking the image of an alchemist described in the information extracted from the thieves.

"An alchemist, huh?"

"Is that right?"

"Well, who knows? It's not certain that only one alchemist is involved in this matter... Even so, it's certain he has some kind of information."

"...Anyway, I'll take that bearded guy, if that's okay with you?"

Neither Rei nor Elena, nor even Byune, said anything to Vihera, who declared she would never let her prey slip away.

Only Azimov, who had just met Vihera, couldn't understand why she was so worked up.

Then again, Azimov wasn't used to rough business, so he had no idea the bearded man, Dias, was that strong.

(That guy earlier had a beard too. Is beards in style in the slums?)

That was the only thought Azimov could muster.

Incidentally, the reason many in the slums grow beards is that many can no longer afford to shave.

Like the man earlier, most slum dwellers are perpetually short on cash and rarely own sharp blades.

Even if they did have one, it was likely a rusty knife, so trying to shave with something like that wouldn't have done much good.

Occasionally, someone might have a sharp blade they stole from someone else, but they wouldn't risk dulling it by using it to shave their beard—it's their livelihood.

Others simply don't shave, aiming to conceal their faces even slightly.

It was obvious which category Dias, standing before Rei and the others, fell into.

"Hmm, then I suppose I'll take that woman. It would look far too bad if the one we just captured had already escaped."

"No, Elena didn't actually escape, did she? We handed her over to the guards properly back then. If something happened, it's their fault."

"Still. Honestly, it's not exactly pleasant to have just reunited with Rei only to have the person we captured at that time still on the loose."

"Ah... yeah."

Surprised by how directly Elena voiced her feelings, Rei flushed slightly and averted his gaze.

"Listen, let's save that for after this whole thing is settled. Seriously, if you're going to do that, you should have included me too."

"Huh?"

Byune, clearly confused by what Vihera was saying, tilted her head in puzzlement and turned her gaze toward Rei, seeking an explanation.

"Nah, don't worry about it. It's too soon for Byune."

"Hmph!"

Byune muttered discontentedly, annoyed at being treated like a child by Rei, who looked about her own age.

Rei turned his gaze toward Azimov instead.

"Sorry, but I need Byune to escort Azimov. I'd have preferred you take one of the other three, though."

"...Hmm."

Though somewhat displeased, Byune accepted Rei's request.

"Huh? Rei, something seems to be happening over there."

Rei turned his gaze forward at Elena's words and saw a man who appeared to be an alchemist handing one of the glass bottles he'd taken from his belt to Dias.

Dias took the glass bottle, raised it high... and the moment Rei saw that movement, he reflexively drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

He swung the massive blade horizontally.

"Flying Slash!"

The flying slash released with that command shot straight through the air, eventually striking and shattering the glass bottle thrown by Dias.

The shattered bottle, destroyed right between the alchemists and Rei's group, released a poisonous purple smoke as its contents spilled onto the ground, spreading outward.

As if designed to prevent harm to them, the purple smoke remained confined to an area only a few meters wide, refusing to spread further.

"...They've made something pretty nasty."

Azimov muttered bitterly upon seeing the purple smoke.

"What's that smoke? Looks dangerously colored, doesn't it?"

"It's called the breath of the Catoprepas. Touching that purple smoke corrodes you. ...Look."

Where Azimov pointed, the ground had corroded where it had touched the purple smoke.

"Well, that's just..."

Rei, who would have to fight the alchemist, wore a displeased expression.

While the Dragon Robe boasted strong defense against magic and physical attacks, it was ill-suited against something like the smoky breath of the Catoprepas.

Being smoke, it could seep through any gap in the Dragon Robe, and then corrode whatever it touched. It was unmanageable.

"Normally, you'd need Catoprepas material to make this, so it's not something you can just whip up easily. Technically speaking, it's not something just any alchemist could create either."

"...Any countermeasures?"

"Avoid contact with the smoke."

At this perfectly reasonable statement, a sigh escaped Rei's lips.

"That's obvious. Isn't there something else?"

"Well... since you can attack from a distance like you did earlier, keeping your distance while fighting would be a good option."

Hearing this, Rei turned his gaze toward the alchemist... Zuboz. There, Zuboz was holding a new glass vial, repeatedly preparing it as if ready to throw it at any moment.

Moreover, before he knew it, everyone—including the bearded man—had equipped magic item weapons.

"So they're fully prepared and waiting for us, huh... Well, no choice. If we waste any more time, it'll only give them the advantage. Let's take them down quickly."

As those words left Rei's mouth, the distance between him and Zuboz closed, and he plunged headlong into combat.
