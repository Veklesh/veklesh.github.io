"...Is this really all there is?"

Rei muttered as he gazed at the ogre's corpse lying on the cobblestones.

The tip of the spear he held was stained with the ogre's blood, dripping onto the stone pavement.

A spear had pierced its forehead, blood flowing freely from the wound, filling the air with a strong, pungent scent.

If it had been an ogre that Rei knew, it should have shrugged off minor wounds. He probably never imagined it would die from a single strike.

"Is it really an ogre? Or have I gotten stronger since then?"

He muttered, shaking his head.

He was aware he was stronger now compared to when he'd descended into the dungeon with Elena and the others before.

But even so, judging by the way this ogre died, it seemed like a problem beyond that.

"Is it even really an ogre, I wonder?"

The ogre he'd fought before was said to be about five meters tall, yet the corpse before him was only about three meters.

That two-meter difference made it impossible to believe its strength matched that of a Rank C monster.

Rei tilted his head in confusion, but then recalled the talk about the poor quality of monster materials appearing in this dungeon. If that was the case, maybe it couldn't be helped? He reconsidered.

"Actually, considering a goblin's head would have exploded, but this one's head is intact... it must be fairly strong for this dungeon..."

Still too weak, he sighed.

"I wish it had at least put up enough of a fight to let me use the Flame Emperor's Crimson Armor."

Despite his grumbling, he knew standing around here wouldn't solve anything. He touched the ogre's corpse and stored it in Mistyling.

"I did learn a skill from the previous ogre's magic stone... but is this really going to be okay?"

He tilted his head, scanning the surroundings.

A wide, empty space. Nothing special here.

No treasure chest, and of course, no stairs leading underground.

"...How did that ogre even get here?"

Compared to the ogres Rei knew, it was considerably smaller, but it was still about three meters tall.

If that was the case, passing through the passage Rei had come through would be impossible... well, not impossible, but certainly very difficult.

"If it didn't just appear here directly... Could it have forced its way through that narrow passage? Well, it's not impossible, I suppose."

An image flashed through his mind of the ogre squeezing through the passageway, twisting its body sideways or ducking its head. The sight of its awkward, clumsy form made Ray smile wryly.

But precisely because of that, he could understand why the ogre was here.

"They somehow squeezed through that narrow passageway and finally made it out here. And then, not wanting to go back through that narrow passageway, they just stayed here... that's the idea, right? Though, of course, it's also possible they appeared here directly."

Satisfied with that explanation, he examined his current surroundings once more.

But this reasonably spacious area contained nothing else besides the ogre's corpse, the bloodstain flowing from its forehead, and the club.

"I wouldn't call it a treasure chest, but I wish there had at least been stairs. ...The fact there are no stairs here means they must be in the other room. ...I'll take this much, at least."

He picked up the club the ogre had been holding and stored it directly into Mistyling.

It was merely a weapon fashioned from a broken branch, with no particular shaping or refinement.

At most, he'd trimmed off other branches that got in the way when swinging it. Calling it a club was a stretch; it was more accurately a branch.

Still, Rei decided it could at least be used as firewood.

"Nothing else seems to be... missing."

He gave the large space one last glance before leaving.

"For a newly created dungeon... it feels both narrow and wide at the same time."

Of course, compared to the dungeons Rei had explored before—like the one with the Altar of Succession, or certainly Exil's dungeon—this one was overwhelmingly small.

In that sense, it was small, yet it was also astonishing that a dungeon just created already possessed this much space.

While there's little threat now, if it keeps growing like this, it might not be too far off before it causes damage to Gilm.

Thinking like that, I kept walking straight ahead... only to hear goblin cries again.

"Again? There really are a lot of goblins here. They're weak, but their sheer numbers make them a real pain."

Sighing, I kicked off the stone pavement, closing the distance while aiming my spear at the goblin approaching from the end of the passage.

Thinking it was nothing more than bullying the weak.

"GRRRRRR!"

Set's cry was followed by a fire breath that erupted from his mouth, incinerating three goblins that had emerged from the rock leading into the dungeon.

He swung his forelegs at the goblins, their bodies engulfed in flames, thrashing and rolling on the ground.

A goblin couldn't possibly withstand a griffin's strike... especially one wielded with a bracelet of might. Their lives were extinguished without a fight.

Set, satisfied, let out a satisfied growl and headed back to where he had been lying moments before...

"Whoa, wait a minute!"

Ranga, who had witnessed the scene, shouted, his eyes wide.

In fact, it was impressive that Ranga could react and act immediately after witnessing that.

The other soldiers were frozen solid, seemingly unable to comprehend what they had just witnessed.

"Grr?"

What? Set growled.

The round gaze fixed on Ranga was so pure and innocent, it was hard to believe he had just effortlessly killed three goblins mere seconds ago.

Faced with Set's gaze, filled with expectation—maybe he wants to play? Or give me food like usual?—Ranga probably realized that even if he directed his current question at Set, he wouldn't get an answer.

Sighing, he scratched his head.

"Guess we just have to wait until Rei-kun gets here, huh?"

"Y-Yeah, I guess so. ...But still, I never thought Set would breathe fire..."

The soldier, who had finally started moving, muttered as if he'd seen something unbelievable.

When Rei returned to Gilm, Ranga was already present in this dungeon.

That was precisely why Rei's explanation to Daskar—that Set was a rare griffin subspecies—hadn't reached them yet.

"If you think about it normally, it's either a higher species or a rare species, right?"

"No, but it's a griffin, right? I mean, it's Set?"

"...Personally, I found it more convincing that it was a rare species precisely because it was Set. Even if an ordinary griffin became a familiar, I doubt it could understand human speech to that extent."

"So it's definitely a rare species? Not a higher species?"

"I've never heard of a higher-tier griffin."

"Well, then a rare species is pretty much the same thing. Honestly, thanks to Set, my senses are numb, but normally, griffins aren't exactly creatures you get to see every day."

Listening to the soldiers chatting behind him, Ranga gently reached out to Set, who was looking at him with hopeful eyes.

"Grrrr..."

Set stretched his head toward the hand reaching up to him.

Langa responded to the unspoken demand for petting and praise.

Whether he enjoyed being stroked or not, Set purred happily.

"Set is Set, huh?"

Ranga murmurs while watching Set, and the other soldiers nod in agreement.

At the very least, the Set being stroked by Ranga right now was undoubtedly the Set everyone present knew.

Ranga felt it deep in his heart: even if it was a rare breed, there was no need to be afraid.

The other soldiers watching Set being affectionate with Ranga felt the same way.

"He's cute, isn't he..."

"Yeah."

"I'm jealous of Rei..."

"Yeah."

"I wish I could be a tamer too..."

"Yeah."

Amidst such conversation, one of the soldiers suddenly spoke up as if noticing something.

"Hold on. Set is a Griffin, a Rank A monster. And rare species are basically treated as one rank higher than their species, right? So does that mean Set is a Rank S monster?"

"Wow, that's amazing. I've never seen an S-rank monster before."

"So, like dragons and stuff?"

"...Whoa... I mean, whoa... That's all I can say."

Though they expressed their shock in words, none of them feared Set.

They knew what kind of presence Set had been in Gilm until now, and the fact that they had just accepted him as Set also played a part.

Everyone wore surprised yet friendly smiles toward Set.

It was the best proof that daily conduct truly matters.

"S-S-Set. Can you do anything besides Fire Breath? If you can, show us!"

Asked by one of the soldiers, Set turned his face toward a spot devoid of any tent-like structures.

"GRRRR!"

With that growl, two water spheres about forty centimeters in diameter appeared around Set.

The two water spheres danced in the air, chasing each other, crossing paths, and darting about.

The soldiers watching, including Ranga, were utterly captivated by the sight... until the next moment, when the spheres collided, spilling water onto the ground.

"Holy crap!"

"...Yeah, that's incredible."

"Never thought I'd see something like this here."

Some voiced their amazement, while others let out cries of surprise for a different reason.

"Water balls alongside fire breath? Aren't those completely opposite elements?"

"And two water balls at once. ...Just how strong is he?"

"At the very least, goblins won't stand a chance."

Set, who wielded skills of fire and water—opposing elements—was a figure the soldiers found almost unbelievable.

And it didn't stop there.

"GRRRRRRRR!"

He aimed at the entrance rock of the dungeon and unleashed a Crystal Breath.

The soldiers, who had expected a fire breath, looked utterly bewildered as they stared toward the dungeon entrance... or more precisely, at the sight of a single goblin emerging from there, its body now covered in crystal.

However, Set's Crystal Breath was low-level, merely coating the goblin's body with a thin layer of crystal.

If that were all, even a goblin could eventually peel off the surface crystals over time...

"Gurrrr!"

As soon as Set finished unleashing the Crystal Breath, he leapt forward and attacked in one swift motion.

The blow from his swinging foreleg shattered the crystals clinging to the goblin's body, and simultaneously, the goblin's body itself shattered into pieces.

Though Set's strike would normally appear nothing but cruel, the shattering crystals reflected sunlight, transforming the scene into something almost fantastical.

Of course, that was merely an illusion.

After the goblin's body was destroyed and Set landed on the ground, the intense stench of blood and the foul odor of entrails filled the air.

"Grrrrrrr!"

The smell must have been unbearable. Fire breath erupted from Set's mouth once more, consuming every last remnant of the goblin's remains scattered across the ground.

Instead of the blood stench from mere seconds ago, the smell of burning flesh now spread through the air.

While still an unpleasant smell, the soldiers found it considerably more tolerable than the stench of goblin blood and entrails.

Several seconds passed, and one soldier, finally coming to his senses, approached to inspect the charred remains of the goblin.

"...Whoa... Wait, what? This..."

The soldier, staring at the goblin's charred remains and gasping in surprise, noticed a crystal lying nearby.

"Oh, right. This must be from earlier... Captain Ranga, what should we do with this? It seems a bit wasteful to just leave it here."

Ranga hesitated for a moment before answering the soldier, who had picked up a crystal fragment lying on the ground.

"Hmm, it might be a bit risky for us to handle this crystal fragment ourselves. Let's collect it for now and ask Rei what to do with it when he gets back from the dungeon."

"Eh... What a waste."

Another soldier, not the one who'd picked up the crystal, said regretfully.

"Hey, you... Seriously? How can you say that after seeing that? It's crystal covered in goblin flesh and blood, you know? Even if someone told me to do it, I'd pass."

"Huh? What are you talking about? A crystal is a crystal. Even if you say it's covered in goblin flesh and blood, the buyer wouldn't know that, right? Anyway, Set's attack just now was super useful. Is he some rare type who can use multiple skills?"

"Hey, hey, don't get any weird ideas. If you try to antagonize Rei, you'll end up like the Azoth Trading Company."

Just to be safe, one of the guards told the soldier who had been complaining about the crystal being a waste, and the soldier nodded in agreement.

After all, they had absolutely no intention of doing something as foolish as antagonizing Rei just to make a few extra coins.
