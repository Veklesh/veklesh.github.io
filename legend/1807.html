Gaiska reluctantly accepted Deotres’ proposal.

Although Gaiska hated Rei enough to want to kill him, the fact that he couldn’t even gather disposable adventurers to serve as combatants was a fatal flaw.

Well, if it were just a matter of gathering people, she could have rounded up low-rank adventurers, street thugs, or members of underground organizations.

But even if they were to wage a war of attrition against Rei, it would be pointless if they were too weak.

Unless they were opponents with enough strength to put up a decent fight, this whole endeavor would be pointless.

In the end, he convinced himself that he needed to first confirm exactly how powerful Rei was, but even that wasn’t a pleasant prospect for Gaiska.

That said, Gaiska forced himself to accept it by including a few assassins among those he gathered.

Once he decided what needed to be done, the next step was to take action to make it happen.

Currently, Rei is, after all, merely a guest of Duke Kerebel.

Since he was dragging such a person into a mock battle, it naturally wasn’t something Gaiska could do on his own.

First, he needed to discuss it with Kerebel Duke… but as Kerebel Duke, Liberte was in the midst of the busy year-end season, so an audience wouldn’t be easy to arrange.

The word “December” doesn’t exist in Elgin, but I suppose the end of the year is just as busy in any world.

Therefore, the first thing Gaiska needed to do was lay the groundwork.

That said, although Gaiska was a descendant of the Seisol Marquis family, he was neither the eldest son nor the second son, who would be his successor.

Consequently, he couldn’t reach out to influential figures like the heads of families he knew or their heirs apparent; the best he could do was approach those in a similar position to himself.

“Crimson? Will I get to see that Battle of Crimson?”

“Yeah. I happened to run into her when I went to greet Elena. So we chatted a bit.”

That wasn’t a lie, but it wasn’t exactly the truth either.

After all, it was an indisputable fact that when Gaiska went to greet Elena, he ran into Rei right there.

What he didn’t mention was that Rei and Gaiska had become bitter enemies.

A few people in the room cast suspicious glances at Gaiska.

If you know Gaiska’s personality, it’s only natural to conclude that he and Rei—who is rumored to show no mercy even to adventurers, let alone nobles—would be a terrible match, given that Gaiska believes bloodline is everything.

...Though there are some who fail to grasp that much and simply accept Gaiska’s explanation at face value... After all, since this gathering consists of people in the same position as Gaiska—those who can never inherit their family’s estate—many of them lack depth of thought, having been raised in a noble environment from birth.

That is likely why some accept Gaiska’s words so readily.

“So, I get that you know Rei of Crimson, but what are you going to do about it? If he’s a guest of Lady Elena, you can’t just casually summon him here, can you?”

“True. But… the Rei I saw didn’t look strong at all. No, if anything, just judging by his appearance, he even looked weak.”

Hearing Gaiska’s words, those listening wore expressions of disbelief.

That was only natural. Rei, known by the epithet “Crimson,” had made a name for himself through his formidable combat prowess.

It was impossible to immediately accept that such a person could be described as weak, even if only based on appearances.

“Ah… isn’t that just… the feeling that his appearance doesn’t match his actual ability…?”

“Probably. I think so too. So, don’t you want to see just how strong he is?”

“See for ourselves…? What are we supposed to do? You don’t mean we’re actually going to fight Crimson, do you? No way, I’m not doing that.”

One of the women gathered there declared with genuine distaste.

It wasn’t just this woman; most of the people here weren’t particularly focused on physical training.

They do move their bodies enough to avoid becoming completely out of shape, but if they were to face an experienced adventurer, they would almost certainly be unable to win.

No matter how many of them gathered, there was no way they could stand a chance against an adventurer with a reputation.

“Of course not. I’m not thinking of doing that anyway. Besides, at the end of the day, they’re just adventurers. We, who are of noble blood, don’t need to bother fighting them. Our goal here is simply to see just how strong Rei really is… to see if she’s actually strong, despite her appearance.”

“So, you’re saying we should just send our subordinates.”

At the words of the woman who had said she didn’t want to fight, Gaiska nodded in agreement.

“But I came here to make preparations for the New Year’s party. I did bring some guards, but there aren’t many of them, are there?”

The one who spoke was the third son of a baron’s family.

Although their positions were similar to Gaiska’s, in terms of rank, there was a significant difference between a baron’s family and a marquis’s family.

Naturally, Gaiska didn’t appreciate being spoken to on equal terms by such a person.

However, since he needed this man for the time being, he had no choice but to put up with it.

The man in front of him, and Rei.

If asked which of the two he felt more irritated by, it was absolutely the latter.

Given that, Gaiska even smiled at the man’s words before speaking.

……Though deep down, he was sneering at the man’s incompetence.

“In that case, just hire some adventurers. It’s winter now, and most adventurers are taking a break from work. If you approach those guys, there’s bound to be some who’d be happy to join.”

“Is that so? I wouldn’t take a job like that, though.”

“That’s because we’re nobles. Commoners like adventurers can’t think that far ahead. More than anything, they’d want to test whether their own strength stands a chance against Rei… an adventurer with a nickname. No, even without going that far, if they thought they could spar with an adventurer with a nickname, they’d volunteer to join.”

Gaiska said this with a smug air, but in reality, those weren’t his own thoughts—they were Deotres’s.

That said, since it was Gaiska who adopted his subordinate’s suggestion, perhaps it wasn’t entirely wrong for him to state it with such confidence.

“Adventurers, huh? I see. That’s a good idea.”

At Gaiska’s words, the third son of the baron’s family muttered as if in agreement.

There were a few others who hadn’t brought subordinates with them, and they were all smiling happily.

“In any case, we need to speak with Kerebel Duke about holding a mock battle.”

Once everyone had settled on the idea of holding a mock battle with Rei, Gaiska announced this.

Yes, that was the main reason he had brought this matter to the people standing before him.

Even though he was a descendant of the Seysol Marquis family, he was, after all, neither the head of the Seysol Marquis family nor, let alone, the heir apparent.

Given that, if he were to propose a mock battle to Duke Kerebel on his own, just to assess Rei’s abilities, there was a high chance it would be easily rejected.

That is precisely why it was necessary to present this proposal to such a large group.

Even if the proposal would have been flatly rejected had I made it alone, if a large group were to present it to Kerebel Duke, he wouldn’t be able to dismiss it outright without further discussion.

“How many of you here have family members—heads of households or those in charge—who have already arrived in Anesis?”

At Gaiska’s question, several men and women replied that their older brothers or parents had already arrived in Anesis.

It was slightly fewer than Gaiska had expected, but since there were still nearly ten days left until the New Year, it wasn’t surprising when you considered that.

In a few days, the number of nobles arriving in Anesis would likely increase rapidly.

But for now, those few days mattered most.

That time was more important than anything else right now.

It was best to verify Rei’s power as soon as possible. …To be precise, it was Deotres who would be verifying that power.

In any case, since the goal was to verify that power here and use it as grounds for retaliation, there was a need to hurry on various fronts.

“Understood. In that case, just tell Duke Kerebel that we want to see Rei’s power, so please arrange a mock battle. I think most of them will agree to it.”

Gaiska said this, but he wasn’t speaking without any basis.

Within the Noble Faction, the figure known as Crimson Rei was, in many ways… a person who attracted attention—both in a good way and a bad way.

Many had likely heard of her strength through others, but few had actually seen it with their own eyes.

Given that, Gaiska and Deotres predicted that no one would want to miss such a golden opportunity to witness her in battle.

“Rei the Crimson—he has a griffin as a familiar, right? I wonder if we’ll get to see that during the mock battle too.”

“I wonder. But I heard through the grapevine that they’re pretty friendly, right? If we can actually touch a griffin like that, I’m kind of looking forward to it.”

“Come to think of it, griffin materials are traded at pretty high prices, right? Maybe I could make a little pocket money off that.”

“Hey, hey, cut the nonsense. We’re dealing with someone who wouldn’t even consider that, even if they were facing a noble. I’d rather not mess with someone like that. Ah, but I do want to see what they’re capable of, so I’m all for the mock battle.”

Watching everyone spout whatever came to mind, Gaiska spoke up.

“Well then, sorry to interrupt your conversation, but everyone, get moving right away. I’ll go see Duke Kerebel and tell him that a lot of people want to watch Rei’s mock battle. As for you guys, if there’s anyone who can convey this request to Duke Kerebel, as I mentioned earlier, please do so through them.”

If possible, Gaiska would have preferred to stay and talk a little longer.

Why must someone in his position go out of his way to make these arrangements?

There was no doubt he resented that, but at the same time, he felt a sense of superiority, thinking that perhaps no one else could handle this task but him.

When he thought of them as his own hands and feet, his slight irritation quickly faded.

After exchanging a few brief words with those present, Gaiska left the room.

He then left the mansion where the others had gathered and climbed into a carriage.

“Good work, young master. How did it go?”

He snorted in response to Deotres’ words, who had been waiting inside the carriage.

“I handled it myself. Of course it went well. But… in the end, those people just want to see that lowly creature in action. I don’t know if they’ll actually gather the numbers.”

“We’ll just have to handle that part ourselves. After all, the reason we had your acquaintances lay the groundwork was to get others to act and lure Rei into a mock battle.”

Calling the people he’d met earlier “acquaintances” rather than “friends” was a masterstroke on Deotres’ part.

If he had called them “friends” here, Gaiska would undoubtedly have taken offense at Deotres.

After all, there were many associates of those with lower titles—such as barons and viscounts—gathered at that venue.

To Gaiska, who believed that bloodline was everything, such people were not friends.

At best, he would have considered them mere acquaintances.

If Deotres had called them “friends,” Gaiska would undoubtedly have harbored anger toward him.

“Hmph. Being able to handle even those kinds of people is a prerequisite for being a man. …Anyway, so the rest are adventurers, then?”

“That’s right. If they hear they can spar with Rei—not for assassination, mind you—there’s no doubt a large number of adventurers will show up.”

“I know. That’s exactly how I stirred them up. …But is that really okay?”

Although Gaiska had been humiliated by Rei, he was also acutely aware of the mysterious—and incomprehensible to him—power Rei possessed.

For that very reason, it was only natural for him to feel uneasy about whether simply hiring some random adventurers would be enough.

If possible, he wanted to finish Rei off during this mock battle.

Given Gaiska’s personality, it was only natural for him to think that.

He had intended to involve his acquaintances to first determine exactly how powerful Rei really was… yet, despite that, he simply couldn’t shake his irritation toward Rei.

“Is it possible to slip an assassin in among them?”

In the end, Gaiska asked Deotres that question.

Upon hearing the question, Deotres looked surprised for a moment... but the next instant, he broke into a broad smile and nodded in response.
