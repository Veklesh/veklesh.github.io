Rei, Vihera, and Set—two humans and one beast—returned to Gilm and came to the guild to report on the kobold leader.

As usual, they parted ways with Set at the entrance. The moment they stepped inside the guild, a murmur rose from within.

Several adventurers were clearly in an uproar, making it obvious something had happened.

Vihera, standing beside Rei, tilted her head as if it were only natural and spoke up.

"I wonder what's going on. Could it be that news about the kobold leader got twisted somehow?"

"I wonder. But then again, I don't see the three we met in the forest anywhere."

Normally, in a situation like this, one would ask an adventurer they knew for details.

Unfortunately, however, not a single adventurer Rei knew was currently in the guild.

Then again, it was still daytime, and most adventurers were out on quests.

That's precisely why, even looking around now, the number of adventurers wasn't as high as it would be in the morning or evening.

Not many, but still, the number of people was clearly higher than on a typical daytime.

...The buzz of adventurers chatting could be heard precisely because there were so many people making noise.

"Shouldn't we at least report the kobold leader situation?"

"Yeah, you're right."

Rei nodded at Vihera's words and headed straight for the counter.

However, inside the counter area, numerous guild staff were currently busy performing various tasks.

Normally, several receptionists would be waiting at the guild counter.

Especially in Gilm, where the number of adventurers was high due to its frontier location, the guild staff—including receptionists—was incomparably larger than in places like Gosh.

Yet, there was not a single receptionist at the counter now.

Even Kenny, who would usually greet Rei cheerfully the moment she saw him, was nowhere to be seen.

"I wonder what really happened. It's unusual for them to be this flustered,"

Vihera murmured, watching the guild staff bustling about behind the counter.

As if hearing Vihera's voice, several guild staff members suddenly turned their gaze toward her... or more specifically, toward both Vihera and Rei.

"Rei! Ms. Vihera!"

The one who called out was a male guild employee in his forties.

His words, spoken with a face beaming with joy, like finding a lifeline in hell, were naturally heard by the other guild staff as well.

"Rei? You're back?"

"You were fighting the Kobold Leader, weren't you? Well, for someone like Rei, a Kobold Leader isn't much of a threat, I suppose."

"Then, if we ask him..."

"That's fine, but which direction should we ask him to go?"

"Wouldn't it be the place farthest away, the one that takes the most time? Since Set-chan is here, there shouldn't be any problem with travel speed, right?"

Hearing voices chatting like that, Rei couldn't help but assume something else had happened.

The Goblin Leader, the Kobold Leader. Something similar must have happened again.

At the same time, he understood that news of the Kobold Leader incident had already spread.

Vihera glanced at Rei before addressing the guild staff.

"It seems word about the kobold leader has already reached you. So, could you tell me what happened? I get the feeling something rather troublesome is unfolding."

It wasn't a reproachful tone, but it was a voice that absolutely could not be ignored.

Several guild clerks addressed by that voice exchanged glances... and eventually, one of them spoke up.

"Well, actually... a group of various monsters, starting with goblins, appeared."

"Well, that's not exactly unusual, is it? Sure, it's a bit odd happening so soon after yesterday, but I still don't think it's something that should have the guild in such an uproar."

"No, the thing is... based on what we've confirmed so far, it seems several groups of monsters have formed, including goblins, kobolds, orcs, lizardmen, and various others."

"...What?"

Apparently that was unexpected. Rei, who had been listening beside Vihera, let out a gasp of surprise.

If, for example, there were several higher-tier goblins each forming their own packs, it would be somewhat surprising but still plausible.

Considering the kobold situation, he understood something abnormal was happening, but even so, the fact that so many different types of monster groups had formed was an unexpected development for Rei too.

In this context, "group" didn't mean a random gathering of monsters, but rather a group led by a higher-tier or rare species, like a Goblin Leader or Kobold Leader.

The existence of multiple such groups was clearly dangerous.

While many adventurers gathered in Gilm were skilled, they naturally had physical limits.

The example of Hester, whose party members were nearly wiped out by a goblin horde, made that danger abundantly clear.

Hester and his group possessed the strength to easily defeat a few... no, a dozen or so goblins.

But when facing nearly a hundred goblins, they simply couldn't sustain the fight due to physical exhaustion.

Moreover, goblins don't attack one by one in a fair fight.

It wouldn't be strange for them to attack from all directions.

"Is that really true? You didn't miscount or anything?"

Vihera asked for confirmation, but the guild clerk shook his head.

"These are reports from adventurers who saw each separate group and returned. It's hard to believe they'd all independently file false reports. If it were just one incident, you might consider the possibility of a mistake..."

But with so many people reporting it separately, it couldn't be a lie.

Vihera nodded at the guild clerk's words.

After all, Vihera had seen the kobold pack led by the kobold leader with her own eyes.

She had no desire to deny what she had seen with her own eyes.

"So, what about the group's movements?"

"Well... naturally, it varies by group. Some stay near where they were found, others move actively, and some are said to be moving away from or closer to Gilm."

"...They're moving about quite freely, aren't they?"

Vihera muttered with an exasperated expression, but Rei, listening beside her, tilted his head.

(So many high-ranking species appearing at once and forming their own separate swarms... no matter how you look at it, this can't be a coincidence. It's highly likely the result of some... or someone's will. And yet, each swarm is acting differently?)

Only questions remained.

If it were just goblins, or just kobolds, he could have understood.

But when multiple types of monsters suddenly produced high-ranking or rare species and formed groups like this, even Rei couldn't figure out why it happened.

"So... well. Actually, there's a request I'd like to ask of you, Rei and Vihera."

"...A request to eliminate the group approaching Gilm, I suppose?"

"Yes. Though several parties have already been dispatched. Fortunately... though 'fortunately' might be the wrong word... the ones approaching us are goblin groups, so they aren't particularly strong. However, multiple groups are arriving simultaneously at several locations."

"If other parties are handling it, doesn't that mean we don't need to go?"

"Normally, that would be the case..."

The guild clerk sighed, looking troubled.

As he said, under normal circumstances, it wouldn't be a problem.

But this time, it's a situation that could be called completely irregular.

Multiple types of monsters were spawning higher-tier and rare species, each leading their own packs.

Even the current situation was irregular, so there was no guarantee something else unexpected wouldn't happen.

Rei understood this too. After a moment's thought, he nodded.

"Alright, then I'll take that request."

"Are you sure? You just got back to Gilm, and you're taking on requests almost every day?"

Vihera asked, looking slightly concerned.

"Yeah, it's fine. Anyway, since the enemy is closing in on Gilm, we need to deal with it properly, right? Besides, goblins aren't exactly that much trouble."

"But this time, there's supposed to be a smart goblin leader, right? If that's the case, the goblins approaching Gilm might be monsters like that too."

"True. That's a possibility. But... with Set and me together, do you really think we couldn't handle goblins?"

"Well..."

Hearing Rei's confident words, Vihera couldn't deny it.

She understood that Rei to lose against a goblin horde was something that could never happen, even if heaven and earth were turned upside down.

"...Alright. But I'm worried about Rei alone, so I'm coming with you."

"Are you sure?"

In a reversal from just seconds ago, Rei asked Vihera.

While Vihera enjoyed combat, that preference was strictly limited to fighting stronger opponents.

Rei couldn't imagine fighting mere goblins would satisfy Vihera's thirst for combat.

"Well, it's fine once in a while. Besides, while Rei is in Gilm, I'll be here too. I want to avoid anything that might put this town in danger."

The guild staff nodded in agreement at Vihera's words.

"That's right. Basically, Gilm can manage on its own, but that's only the bare minimum. ...Well, considering many towns can't even manage that, it's actually quite impressive that we can handle at least the basics."

"Yes, I suppose so. If rumors spread among merchants that traveling to Gilm is dangerous, they'll naturally stop coming here. And then..."

"Logistics would stall, and we couldn't get all the various goods we source from other towns, right?"

Rei took over the conversation between the guild official and Vihera.

"Yes. Especially foodstuffs—we rely heavily on the merchants for those..."

"Alright, let's go."

Before the guild official could finish, Rei declared.

Given that Rei loved eating, he absolutely could not tolerate Gilm's food situation deteriorating.

If told to wipe out all the goblins to ensure proper food supplies reached Gilm, he'd likely do it without hesitation.

Purely in terms of food supply, there was no real danger of starvation.

Gilm was on the frontier, and considering the monster meat available there, things shouldn't have gotten that bad.

However, Gilm's population was far larger than that of the surrounding towns, rivaling that of a city.

Given that, no matter how many adventurers there were, feeding the entire population of Gilm solely on monster meat would normally have been impossible.

...That is, unless you had an extraordinary entity like Rei—someone with an item box who could swiftly travel to distant places.

(Wait, could I actually use the Misty Ring to bring food if it came down to it? ...At worst, moving to another town wouldn't leave us short of food, but that would mean abandoning Gilm, leaving a bad taste in my mouth. ...Huh? Food shortage?)

As Rei pondered this, a thought flashed through his mind: goblins.

More precisely, dishes made with goblin meat.

It was still too unpalatable to eat now, but if goblin meat became edible, the term "food shortage" would cease to exist.

That's how prevalent goblins were everywhere, making Rei think that way.

"...You've gotten motivated rather suddenly, haven't you? Well, knowing Rei, I can sort of understand why."

Vihera muttered, sounding slightly exasperated. Rei, who understood the reason behind her words, shrugged lightly.

With that shrug, he immediately started to leave the guild as if to leave right away.

"Ah, Rei. Please wait a moment. As I mentioned earlier, other adventurers are participating in this goblin extermination. I'd prefer it if you avoided attacks that might involve them."

"No, what the hell do you think I am?"

Rei muttered discontentedly at the guild clerk's words, which sounded like they were saying his actions could endanger his allies without warning.

But it wasn't the guild clerk who answered Rei's words. It was Vihera.

"Considering what Rei's done so far, it's pretty understandable, isn't it?"

"I don't recall ever attacking in a way that endangered my allies."

"True. But the problem is that people think you're capable of such actions, right?"

As Vihera stated this with a smile, Rei recalled his own past actions—how he'd casually antagonized even lords and nobles—and averted his gaze.

"Well then. We should be going soon. Even goblins can be troublesome when they're in numbers."

"Heh heh. Then let's do that."

With Vihera, who was inexplicably in a good mood, Rei left the guild without further delay.
