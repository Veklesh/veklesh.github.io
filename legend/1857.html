"Wooooooah!"

With a battle cry, the soldier thrusts his spear toward Rei.

Although the spear’s tip had been blunted for mock combat, a strike from a soldier trained like this, charging at full speed, would naturally pack a heavy punch.

In fact, for an ordinary person, even a spear with a blunted tip could easily be fatal.

But… that’s only true for an ordinary person; when the opponent is Rei, it’s a different story.

“You’re too naive. If you’re going to exploit an opening, you need to be much more stealthy.”

Saying that, Rei dodged the spear’s strike by shifting his right side back a step, grabbed the shaft right next to the tip… and swung the spear around.

Normally, even if an opponent managed to grab the spear and swing it around, they would have simply had the weapon taken from them.

But the soldier holding the spear tightened his grip on the shaft, desperate not to let Rei take it from him.

It was a split-second decision.

There was no doubt he was an excellent soldier, but at the same time, he had a strong overconfidence in his own strength.

He knew his opponent was Rei, but he likely still underestimated him based on his appearance.

That’s precisely why he didn’t let go of the spear… and as a result, his own body was used as a weapon, slamming into another opponent who had attacked Rei from behind with a spear.

“Hey, you idiot!”

The soldier whose body was used as a shield must have been completely caught off guard. Unable to simply abandon his comrade, he took the impact while grumbling... but facing Rei, such an action was nothing short of suicide.

In the next instant, Rei thrust the spear he’d taken from the first soldier he’d thrown at him, and that settled the matter.

“That’s enough!”

That voice rang out, and sounds of disappointment echoed around them.

About an hour had passed since the soldiers began sparring with Rei, but no one had managed to defeat him.

No, in fact, they hadn’t even managed to make him fight with any real intensity.

They knew Rei went by the nickname “Crimson.” They knew that… but still, as long as they were employed as soldiers by the Duke of Kerebel, the soldiers honestly believed they could at least put up a decent fight.

Quite a few people had watched the sparring matches, and they’d thought they could manage to hold their own… but seeing two soldiers known for their skill lose so easily made those thoughts vanish instantly.

“Listen, as you can see, when facing someone as extraordinary as Rei, don’t even think about trying to fight him head-on. It didn’t matter this time because Grain was a fool, but attacking from behind isn’t the wrong approach.”

At those words, Grain—the man who had been thrown without letting go of his spear—gently averted his gaze as he got back to his feet.

He must realize that he’d made a foolish mistake.

Even if he can understand it now that things have calmed down, when the time comes—when the battle actually begins—it’s not uncommon for him to act in ways that leave him wondering later why he did such a thing.

You could say it’s a half-reflexive action, but precisely because it’s reflexive, it’s a test of one’s combat instincts.

In that sense, Grain’s judgment in that split second was a failure.

After that, the man, who had explained the good and bad points of today’s mock battle, finally turned his gaze toward Rei.

“Rei, do you have anything else to add? If so, please do. It’s a rare opportunity to spar with someone who holds a title.”

Those words were true.

Rei’s mock battles in Anesis were almost exclusively with members of the Kerebel Duke’s Knight Corps; while he did engage in mock battles with soldiers, the frequency was inevitably low.

This wasn’t because Rei was showing favoritism or anything like that; it was simply because sparring with the Order was more beneficial for him.

Even the members of the Order couldn’t win if they fought Rei head-on.

However, the knights, whose individual abilities surpass those of the soldiers, move to try and counter Rei’s actions.

In that regard alone, the knights were clearly superior to the soldiers.

Moreover, the primary reason Rei engages in mock battles with the knights is the presence of Philma, the captain of the knight corps.

Even for Rei, this is an opponent he would lose to if he were to underestimate him.

Being able to spar with such a person was a huge benefit for Rei as well.

Normally, he could have sparred with Elena, but she is busy at the moment.

Now that the New Year’s party was over and many had exchanged greetings with Liberte, the leader of the Noble Faction, and were returning to their own estates, many of them hoped to meet with Elena.

……It was fortunate for Elena that, by displaying her relationship with Rei at the party, the number of suitors approaching her had decreased compared to before.

Of course, the fact that suitors still approach her even under these circumstances speaks to just how much Elena’s charm attracts men.

She is the daughter of a Duke who leads the Noble Faction, possesses a beauty that would not be out of place if she were called the goddess of beauty, and yet she also has a nickname.

For a nobleman, a catch like this is rare indeed.

It was only natural that there would be suitors for Elena, even if they were prepared to face the wrath of Rei.

Still, the fact that no one tries to woo Elena in front of Rei is probably because, deep down, they’re afraid of him.

“That’s true. I think they’re well-trained overall. As soldiers of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, they should be fine.”

At Rei’s words, the soldiers looked surprised.

No matter how they looked at it, they had been defeated one-sidedly without ever really putting up a fight.

Given that, they had expected to be told where they’d gone wrong, but certainly not to be praised.

But contrary to their expectations, what came out of Rei’s mouth was praise.

It was precisely because it was so different from what they expected that they were so surprised.

……That said. That was merely how the ordinary soldiers felt; the man who had been overseeing the mock battle—their superior officer—felt differently.

What came out of Rei’s mouth could undoubtedly be called praise. That was a fact.

However, I understood that he was implicitly telling me it was based on the premise of being an ordinary soldier, and that it fell short of the level of training expected of soldiers under the command of the Duke of Kerebel.

(Huh.)

Looking at the face of the soldiers’ superior, Rei showed a slight look of admiration.

Judging by the look of frustration on his face, Ray concluded that he had fully grasped the underlying meaning of his words.

(As expected of someone serving the Duke of Kerebel, he’s not exactly a fool. …Though, when it comes to training his subordinates, he’s average—nothing more.)

Since he’d trained them to an average level of strength, the man was by no means incompetent.

However, it was also a fact that, at this point, the soldiers’ strength was nothing to write home about, and it was equally true that, viewed as soldiers of the Duke of Kerebel’s household, they fell short of expectations.

“Rei, thank you for agreeing to conduct this mock battle. I will certainly put what I learned from this experience to good use in the future.”

To voice his gratitude so sincerely despite feeling frustrated—one could call that noble.

Rei must have realized that too. He smiled and spoke.

“Ah, if the mock battle with me was useful, then that’s fine. …Just so you know, normally, hiring me as an adventurer would cost a pretty penny, you know?”

Rei said it jokingly, but it was also true.

A Rank B adventurer with a nickname.

Although he was only Rank B, Rei—with his nickname—possessed enough skill to be considered a high-ranking adventurer… no, an adventurer worthy of being called top-class.

Naturally, hiring someone like Rei would mean a hefty fee.

Even so, the reason Rei was going along with the soldiers’ mock battle—even though it wouldn’t benefit him personally, much like the mock battles with the knight corps—was because he was acutely aware that he was a guest of the Duke of Kerebel’s household.

...Or perhaps it’s also because this is the home of a certain Elena—that’s undoubtedly a factor as well.

In that sense, these soldiers are undoubtedly fortunate.

“Hahaha. You’re right. Next time, I’ll make sure they’ve trained properly so the fight is more of a real challenge.”

“Do that. …Well, though I don’t know when that will be.”

In fact, although he had welcomed the New Year here at Elena’s family home—the Duke of Kerebel’s residence—he didn’t know when he would return next.

Given that, I can’t rule out the possibility that this might be the last conversation the officer ever has with Rei.

Perhaps realizing this, the commander’s face took on a slightly regretful expression.

Although he himself hadn’t been able to spar with Rei, simply watching his subordinates spar with her had been stimulating enough.

For that reason alone, it was what a pity that he would no longer be able to watch Rei fight.

“I see. …It’s a shame, but there’s nothing to be done about it.”

“If you absolutely need my help, you could come all the way to Gilm, and I might be able to take on the request. Or, if it’s truly urgent, there’s also the option of having the guild in Anesis contact the guild in Gilm directly.”

The guilds are connected through a network formed by magic items known as “Paired Orbs.”

The Guild in Gilm had received such messages several times before, and Rei had experience accepting requests through them.

(Well, since the guild master is Walker now instead of Marina, negotiations would probably be pretty tough, though.)

This doesn’t mean Walker is more capable than Marina…

Now that he’s been entrusted with the role of Guild Master, there’s no doubt that Walker is capable—but that’s simply a matter of his personal qualities.

In that sense, there’s no doubt that Marina was easier to approach for those seeking commissions.

In any case, after exchanging a few brief words with the soldiers, Rei headed straight for the library.

“Good work. …Is it okay for me to say that?”

Miranda walked alongside Rei.

From Miranda’s perspective—now that she’d been assigned to work with Rei—the mock battle he’d fought with the soldiers seemed like nothing more than a one-sided victory for Rei.

It might also have something to do with it being winter, but he didn’t seem to be sweating at all.

The Dragon Robe Rei is wearing functions like a portable air conditioner, so even if it were summer, he wouldn’t break a sweat that easily.

“Yeah, I guess so. I’m pretty tired, so I think it’s okay to say it.”

“Then, ‘Good work.’ …Since you’re walking this way, does that mean you’re heading to the library next?”

“Yeah. I’m going to return the book I borrowed. …And if I find another interesting book, I might borrow that too.”

Maybe if I read that book, the Black Wolf will show up again.

That was undoubtedly true, but even so, Rei still hadn’t decided what to do the next time he encountered the Black Wolf.

It was certain that the Black Wolf was an enemy and was targeting him.

But even so... perhaps because they’d interacted several times before, it was also true that he felt something akin to affection.

(What should I do?)

Rei looked up at the sky, looking troubled.

Spread out before him was a clear, blue winter sky.

For Rei, the word “winter” inevitably brings back memories of his time in Japan.

Days covered in clouds, with snow falling endlessly, and the chore of shoveling the snow in front of the house.

...Though, since her family runs a farm, they do get a break during the winter.

Depending on the crops, some farmers might even harvest produce from plastic greenhouses in the middle of winter, but Rei’s family didn’t grow anything in a greenhouse.

Well, to be precise, they did grow a small amount of vegetables in a tiny greenhouse—just enough to feed the family—but they didn’t grow anything meant for shipment.

“Rei?”

“Hm? Oh, no. It’s nothing. I was just thinking about what book to borrow next. Ideally, I’d like something with monsters I’ve never heard of, or a book about magic items—something like that.”

With that, Rei brushed it off and headed to the library with Miranda.
