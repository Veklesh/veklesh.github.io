That day, Kenny felt both joy and sadness.

His joy stemmed, of course, from Rei's return to Gilm after nearly two months. While he knew Rei's strength meant there was likely no cause for worry, Rei had been heading to the distant city of Glacial, and Kenny felt he would be truly relieved only when he saw Rei safe with his own eyes.

But the moment he saw the other people Rei had brought with him, Kenny froze in his tracks.

First, there was a man who looked older than Rei. This wasn't a problem in itself.

Kenny knew from personal observation that while Rei preferred solo adventures, he occasionally traveled with other adventurers.

To Kenny, who had seen many adventurers, that wolf beastman didn't seem particularly strong, but the way he was casting warning glances around was typical behavior for a boastful adventurer fresh to Gilm.

Next, a girl around ten years old.

She was undeniably adorable, but while girls her age usually had ever-changing expressions, this one stood out for her almost completely blank, expressionless face.

Judging by her equipment, which emphasized lightness and looked designed for easy movement, she was likely a thief.

Despite her youth, her movements carried a seasoned professionalism.

Kenny felt a pang of pity for her, but he didn't let it show.

Adventurers were responsible for themselves; she had chosen this path.

Still, she was on Kenny's mind enough that he'd want to offer help immediately if she ever seemed troubled.

...The other receptionists besides Lenora and Kenny also seemed curious about the girl, glancing her way discreetly while handling their own tasks.

And then... finally, the woman sitting beside Rei as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

Kenny was reasonably confident in her own beauty.

After all, becoming a guild receptionist required above-average looks, so it was only natural.

He was also confident in his physique, including his chest size, as he always teased Lenora about hers.

But the woman at the end of Kenny's gaze possessed a beauty that shattered that confidence into smithereens, wearing a sheer garment like a dancer or courtesan might, as if flaunting her body lines.

For Kenny, when thinking of the most stunning woman he'd ever met, Marina, the guildmaster, would undoubtedly come to mind first.

She possessed a face that could be called perfect for a woman, a beauty that could ensnare any man if she so desired.

She carried a slightly decadent aura, which might bother some, but such people were surely few and far between.

She was also a formidable former adventurer and highly intelligent, likely due in part to her Dark Elf heritage.

Yet she also possessed an approachable ease, readily striking up conversation with those below her.

To Kenny, she was the very picture of perfection.

Then there was Elena, whom he had never met in person, but whose image he had seen through the Paired Orb that Rei used at the Dusk Wheat inn, his regular lodging, and with whom he had conversed.

For anyone living in the Kingdom of Mireana, the nickname "Princess General" was one that everyone had surely heard at least once.

Her golden hair, seemingly solidified sunlight itself, formed a perfect counterpart to Marina's silver locks.

Her beauty, like a large rose, would certainly not be overshadowed by Marina's.

Though they hadn't met in person, only conversing through the Paired Orbs, Elena's dignified aura still came through.

If Marina represented the night, then Elena was the embodiment of the day.

Those two were the most exceptional women Kenny had ever encountered, yet the woman now beside Rei was their equal, if not superior.

Even to Kenny, who'd seen countless adventurers, it was hard to pinpoint her exact level, but she possessed considerable strength... so much so that he wouldn't be surprised if she were said to have power equivalent to a Rank A adventurer.

Given her caliber, it was only natural that Kenny recognized her as a new rival.

A sense of crisis similar to, or perhaps even greater than, when he encountered Elena last year—his greatest rival.

Driven by that sense of urgency, Kenny asked who the person beside Rei was.

"Hm? Oh, this is Vihera. Ah... just an acquaintance, I suppose."

"Oh? Just an acquaintance? I wouldn't mind becoming a deeper acquaintance."

"...Huh. Well, well. You're still popular with the ladies, aren't you, Rei?"

While Rei hesitated over how to explain—he certainly couldn't blurt out that she was the former Second Princess of the Bestir Empire, who had fled her country to live freely and independently—Vihera spoke up.

Hearing words that could easily be considered a confession of affection, Kenny found it anything but amusing.

Meanwhile, Lenora, listening beside Kenny, only reinforced her inner judgment that he was a bad influence.

"Hehe, did I tease you too much? Rest assured. For now, Rei and I aren't in that kind of relationship yet."

Vihera said, trying to calm the two in front of her, but her words hinted that she and Rei would eventually become that kind of relationship.

"Anyway, here. This is the sign for completing the request."

What she handed over was the signature proving the escort request was complete.

It was the item received from the merchants.

"...Please wait a moment."

Once that paper was presented, Lenora had no choice but to prioritize her work.

Putting personal matters before work and delaying her duties was something Lenora couldn't do, given her pride as a receptionist.

Though Kenny, who had finished organizing documents and had no urgent tasks, was a different story.

She wanted to ask him straight out what he thought of Rei, but she couldn't bring herself to say it in front of Rei. She hesitated, unsure how to begin.

Just as she finally tried to speak...

"Hey, sis. Let me take this request."

The wolf beastman who had come with Rei placed the request form on the counter.

"...Show me your guild card."

"Huh? Oh, here."

He said, presenting his card, which showed the man before him was a Rank D adventurer.

After confirming the man's rank, Kenny pushed the request back without further discussion.

"Sorry, but with your rank, you can't take on a B-rank request."

"What!?"

"Threats won't work. Besides, didn't they explain all this when you registered with the guild? Surely you didn't just ignore it?"

"Guh, w-well..."

In the past, his blustering often got him what he wanted.

But Kenny showed no sign of being intimidated by Magita at all, dismissing her words flatly.

After years of dealing with adventurers in Gilm, it wouldn't be strange if he wasn't easily shocked by ordinary things.

At the very least, he never flinched at someone trying to appear more powerful than they actually were.

"Huh!?"

Magita must have thought he'd been humiliated. Just as Magita was about to yell at Kenny...

"Calm down."

Rei placed a hand on Magita's shoulder, suppressing her anger.

"...Damn it. Fine. If the master says so, I guess I have no choice."

A murmur rippled through the crowd. The adventurers who heard Magita's words began to stir.

The mere mention of "Master" from Magita's mouth was clearly met with surprise.

Everyone present knew something about who Rei was, so the idea that he would take a disciple was nothing short of unexpected.

Rei had received requests for training before.

But those were always temporary arrangements.

To become a formal disciple meant being with Rei around the clock to receive training...

"Hey, Rei! You really took this guy as your disciple!?"

Kenny's voice cut through the surrounding murmurs.

To Kenny, the wolf beastman standing before them looked like nothing but a bad influence on Rei.

His current feeling was closer to "Don't hang out with bad friends."

"I told you, he's not my master. Stop giving everyone around us the wrong idea."

Unable to reach Magita's head, he lightly tapped her back instead.

However, that "lightly" was only light by Rei's standards; to Magita, it felt like a regular punch.

"Guh!"

Glancing at Magita, who let out a choking sound, Rei spoke again.

"Whether you call me master or whatever, I have no intention of training you. Besides, if you're taking jobs and working in Gilm, your skills will improve whether you like it or not. ...Well, maybe once you become a rank B adventurer like me now, I might train you occasionally."

Hearing those words, most adventurers concluded that Rei had no intention of training the wolf beastman called Magita.

Rank B adventurers were undoubtedly high-ranking, and even within Gilm, such highly ranked adventurers were far from common.

Well, to be precise, compared to other villages, towns, or cities, the number of Rank B adventurers was higher. Still, considering the proportion of adventurers in Gilm, it couldn't be called large.

It was only natural. Above Rank B, there were only two ranks: A and S. Rank S adventurers were a rarity, with only three such individuals existing in the entire world.

In essence, rank A is the highest adventurer rank, with B being one tier below it.

There was no way someone that strong would just be lying around anywhere.

But... upon hearing Rei's words, Magita opened his mouth with a serious expression.

"You mean it? If I really become a Rank B adventurer, you'll be my master?"

"Don't deliberately misunderstand. I might teach you for a short time, but I have no intention of becoming anyone's mentor. What I said was that if you become a Rank B adventurer, I'll spar with you occasionally. As you are now, fighting me isn't even the issue. Train at Gilm for a while."

"...Understood."

With that, Magita turned and walked back with the request form.

The adventurers watching them exchange glances, several casting envious looks at Magita.

Rei's treatment at Gilm was generally quite extreme in many ways.

Those with something to hide wanted nothing to do with him, while others came to consult Rei about cooking matters.

Surprisingly, his reputation among children wasn't bad either. ...Though, most of that was down to Set.

And among them, there were those who wanted to talk to Rei. Not many, but certainly not none.

The adventurers who had been casting envious glances at Magita were among that minority.

"...Well, I suppose we've strayed a bit from the topic, but I've confirmed the request was completed. This is your reward."

"Yes, thank you."

Watching Vihera take the bag containing the reward, Rei asked something that had suddenly occurred to him.

"By the way, what's your rank, Vihera?"

"C."

"Higher than I expected."

Considering Vihera's combat prowess, it wouldn't have been surprising if she were at least Rank B, as Rei had mentioned earlier. Vihera gave a small smile and shrugged her shoulders.

As he did, his ample breasts swayed slightly. A sharp gleam flashed in Lenora's eyes for just an instant, but Vihera seemed unconcerned as she spoke.

"Well, with Exil, I was always diving into dungeons. I didn't take on commissions or anything like that, except for a few exceptions, so my rank just couldn't climb."

"Exceptions, huh?"

While surmising that the exception likely referred to extermination requests, Rei nodded in understanding at Vihera's words.

Given Vihera's personality, it was something he understood without needing to think about it.

"Yes. ...Well, now that the escort request is safely finished... What should we do? I have somewhere to go shortly. Want to come, Byune?"

"Mm!"

Vihera smiled softly and ruffled Byune's hair as the child nodded in agreement.

"It might be a boring story, but having Byune there makes it easier for me."

"Well, boring business..."

Having vaguely guessed where Vihera was headed based on her conversation with Ranga, Rei implied that meeting a lord was no small matter. However, Vihera was, after all, a member of the Bestir Empire's royal family.

Given that, meeting a noble holding the position of Marquis of the Frontier shouldn't be particularly nerve-wracking, he concluded.

"Where will Rei be staying, Rei?"

"Dusk Wheat inn. It's a good place with delicious food."

"Then, since Byune and I plan to stay there too, could you reserve a room for us? ...My business might take a while."

"Ah, understood. It's a famous place, so if you ask anyone, they'll know right away... Just saying, try not to cause too much trouble. Should I come along too?"

Just looking at Vihera's attire, it was obvious he'd get caught up in trouble if Rei and Set weren't there.

But Vihera shook her head.

"Trouble's bound to happen sooner or later anyway, so it's better to get it over with quickly. Oh, if you want, I don't mind sharing a room with Rei?"

Saying that, Vihera shot Rei an inviting glance before leaving him behind and walking out of the guild with Byune.

"Ah."

Watching their retreating figures, Rei suddenly wondered if Vihera even knew where the lord's mansion was.

...It took a few seconds for Rei to notice the presence of Kenny and Lenora, both looking rather displeased, right beside him.
