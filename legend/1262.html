Rei usually wears clothes that are nothing particularly distinctive.

Well, judging by Japanese standards, the large opening at the stomach might seem a bit odd, but here in Elgin, it's perhaps somewhat unusual, though not so much that wearing it draws strange looks.

After all, some female adventurers in the vanguard prioritize lightness and wear what's called bikini armor, so clothes that show a bit of the stomach weren't out of the ordinary.

And unusually for Rei, who usually favored magic items, this outfit was ordinary clothing, not some sort of magic item.

The same applied to the pants; they too were not some sort of magic item.

By any normal standard, simply wearing a robe over regular clothes would be tantamount to suicide for an adventurer.

Especially since Rei wasn't a pure mage, but a mage-warrior who fought on the front lines using a Death Scythe while casting spells. ...And leaning more towards the warrior side, at that.

If he were a rear-guard mage, wearing heavy armor and only a robe wouldn't be strange.

Good examples were Sulunin, drinking ale while watching Milein with an exasperated look, and Min, talking with Elk.

These two are typical mages, not equipped with heavy armor.

But Rei, unlike them, fights on the front lines. It would be strange if she weren't wearing armor.

...What makes this possible is the Dragon Robe Rei wears.

True to its name, it's made lavishly from dragon materials.

Compared to ordinary armor, it was a magical item offering vastly superior defense... but it remained a robe nonetheless.

It could block slashes or thrusts from blades, but it couldn't completely absorb the impact.

That's precisely why weapons like axes, clubs, and hammers—weapons that the Silver Lion found troublesome, if not outright weak against—still inflicted a fair amount of impact damage on Rei's body.

In that sense, using Silver Lion fur to make a coat and pants was something Rei could understand as a way to compensate...

"But silver lions were weak against impact attacks too, right? So even if you make clothes from silver lion fur, wouldn't that still leave the Dragon Robe vulnerable?"

"Idiot."

That single, blunt word from Azimov's mouth made Rei frown unhappily as he opened his mouth.

"That's pretty blunt."

"Well, yeah. Think about it. Your Dragon Robe isn't exactly weak against impact-type weapons... but it's not that strong either. It doesn't completely let the impact pass through, right?"

"Well, obviously."

While the Dragon Robe is vulnerable to impact-type weapons, it naturally absorbs some of their power.

Otherwise, he would have suffered serious injuries several times by now.

...Though whether broken or cracked ribs count as serious injuries probably varies by person.

"So, the Silver Lion's coat and trousers naturally absorb impact too. If that's the case, wouldn't it be significantly different from taking an attack in the Dragon Robe?"

"...I see."

Now that you mention it, that's true.

He couldn't help but conclude he deserved to be called an idiot.

"So, now that you understand, what do you say? Shall we try making a coat and trousers using the Silver Lion's fur?"

As Azimov asked again, Rei considered what to do... and eventually nodded in agreement.

He didn't know what kind of request he'd be taking on next, but even so, having increased defense couldn't hurt.

Having received Rei's consent to make the coat and pants, Azimov's face lit up with joy, but it quickly turned to disappointment.

"What's wrong? Rei said it's okay to use the silver lion's materials."

Pamidor, who had been listening nearby, looked puzzled at Azimov's reaction.

"Ah... no, well. The thing is... I don't actually know anyone who can make clothes properly. Sure, I could cobble something together myself, but since we're using Silver Lion material, it needs someone with the right skill."

"Well... I suppose you're right."

Even if it's top-grade material... no, precisely because it's top-grade material, it demands corresponding skill from the person handling it.

(Like foie gras, caviar, shark fin, dried abalone... If someone who rarely cooks tries to prepare those high-end ingredients, it'll probably just turn out disastrous... or something?)

Rei nodded in agreement with Azimov's words, vaguely suspecting that was indeed the case.

Even the Twilight Spear, crafted from countless materials Rei had brought in, couldn't have been made by Azimov alone.

And the one who assisted him was Pamidor, the master blacksmith now staring at Azimov in disbelief. It was an undeniable fact that crafting garments and trousers from silver lion fur also required a skilled artisan.

"...So. Do you know any craftsmen who could make clothes like that, Rei? We can handle the leather side, but making the clothes is a bit..."

"Hey. You said you'd make the clothes, so isn't that part already taken care of?"

"I've been focused on the Sleipnir Boots! I haven't had time for that!"

His unhesitating declaration was almost refreshingly straightforward.

Seeing Azimov state it so boldly, Rei let out a small sigh before shifting his gaze sideways... toward Pamidor.

Pamidor, meeting Rei's gaze, frowned thoughtfully before finally speaking.

"Unfortunately, it's difficult to meet your expectations, Rei. I know a tailor or two myself. But they only make clothes for ordinary townspeople. Monster hides from around here might be manageable, but materials from S-rank monsters..."

Pamidor paused, then shifted his gaze toward Rei... or more precisely, toward the clothes visible through the gaps in Rei's Dragon Robe.

There, visible beneath the robe, was Rei's clothing—revealing his stomach despite the winter cold.

While this courtyard had bonfires burning in various spots, the undeniable fact remained that it was winter, and snow lay thick on the ground.

There was no way Rei's outfit, with his stomach exposed, could be considered appropriate.

The Dragon Robe, with its simple air-conditioning-like function, made it possible.

Anyway, what Pamidor was concerned about now wasn't whether he looked cold or not.

"The clothes underneath the Dragon Robe are pretty thin, right? If we end up making clothes from silver lion fur, it'll be quite restrictive. Considering that, we'd need to find a truly skilled craftsman... Unfortunately, I don't know of any craftsman with that level of skill."

Hearing Pamidor shake her head, Rei pondered what to do.

But while ordinary clothes sold anywhere would be one thing, he simply couldn't think of any craftsmen who could handle silver lion materials.

...If it were a cook who could handle silver lion meat, he could think of several people. This was purely related to Rei's personal tastes and preferences.

(Ah, come to think of it... Marina probably knows a lot about that sort of thing.)

Marina wore a party dress even in battle.

The artisan who made that party dress... could it be...?

That thought occurred to Rei.

"Can you figure something out?"

"Huh? Do you have an idea?"

"Probably... but it's just a maybe."

"Got it. Then I'll leave that to you. Let me know if you get in touch with the tailor."

Azimov seemed momentarily relieved by Rei's words and headed back toward the cooking area to enjoy the barbecue.

"He's as free-spirited as ever."

Pamidor murmured thoughtfully, watching Azimov's retreating figure.

Rei nodded in agreement at Pamidor's words.

"Anyway, since you're here, Pamidor, enjoy the party. There's plenty of delicious food, starting with Gamelion meat."

"Oh, I'll certainly enjoy myself."

With that, Pamidor excused himself, saying he'd be enjoying the food, and walked away.

After seeing Rei off, Rei headed next to where Set was.

Milein and Johanna were feeding Set, and he was eating it, completely captivated by the sight.

"Gururururu!"

Set, who had been eating skewered meat, noticed Rei approaching and let out a happy growl.

"Set, you seem to be eating... I'm glad to see you're enjoying it thoroughly."

"Guru!"

When Rei stroked him, Set purred contentedly.

Seeing Set like that, Milein and Johanna looked at Rei enviously.

"Rei gets to be with Set-chan all the time, doesn't she..."

"Yeah. I'm so jealous of you, Rei."

"Well, what can I say? Set is my partner, so it's only natural, right?"

Rei replied with a slightly exasperated tone while petting Set.

"Why don't you two try aiming to become Tamers yourselves? If you're a Tamer, no one will complain about you bringing monsters along."

"...But you need talent to become a Tamer, right? It's not like you can just decide to be one and become one."

Milein's words were true, but not necessarily correct.

"A tamer, simply put, is someone who can command monsters or be liked enough to stay with them. There are countless ways to do it. Besides, it can even vary depending on the individual monster, let alone the species."

Even as he said this, Rei couldn't help but think his own words were the very definition of "easier said than done."

If anyone with the drive could become a Tamer, there wouldn't be so few of them.

They should be as common as mages... or in some cases, even rarer than mages.

In fact, Rei himself had encountered fewer tamers than he had mages.

Though that might be partly because Rei primarily operates in Gilms.

(Honestly, summoning magic might be easier to learn than becoming a tamer... though, if you lack the aptitude for magic, that option wouldn't be available to begin with. And even if you could use magic, attribute compatibility issues could cause all sorts of problems.)

For instance, in Rei's case, he can only use fire-attribute magic. Everyone has their own attribute.

Of course, cases like Rei's, where someone is specialized solely in fire... no, overly specialized, are extremely rare.

Still, the possibility isn't entirely nonexistent.

Incidentally, only a very few people know that Rei can only use fire-attribute magic.

Thanks to the Death Scythe skill, he gives the impression he can use several types of magic, starting with wind.

"...Do you really think that?"

Milein murmured, her gaze fixed on Rei.

After encountering Set, Milein naturally researched tamers extensively.

She even went to the library, a place she normally avoided, and gathered information at the guild.

But in the end, the conclusion she reached was that it was definitely impossible for her...

Under Milein's gaze, Rei took a step back as if overwhelmed.

The fact that she made Rei, who hadn't retreated even during the battle with the Silver Lion, step back showed just how deep Milein's, and Johanna's, affection for Set truly was.

"Besides, even if you could tame a monster, it wouldn't be Set, so it wouldn't mean anything."

Johanna nodded in agreement at Milein's words.

"Grrr..."

Teasing Rei? Set purred, his round eyes fixed on Milein and Johanna.

Feeling somewhat uncomfortable with Set's reaction, the two women opened their mouths to change the subject.

"Rei-san. The party's been going on for quite a while now. Don't you think it's about time we ate some Silver Lion meat?"

"Ah, yeah, you're right."

Looking around, they could see many people chatting with those nearby rather than eating.

Some were still focused on eating, but they were few and far between.

Considering how much time had passed since the party began, and that it was about time to serve the silver lion meat, Rei couldn't really argue with that.

"Alright. Hey! It's about time to bring out the silver lion meat, the star of today's feast!"

Rei's voice echoed throughout the courtyard.
