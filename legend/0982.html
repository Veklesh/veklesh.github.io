After inspecting the hammer in the guild's warehouse, Rei walked through the town with Set.

Since they had the hammer used by the Cyclops, and he was also curious about the progress of converting Noiz's Magic Sword into a spear, they were heading towards Pamidor's blacksmith workshop.

Normally, going directly to Azimov's workshop—the center of this whole affair—would be best. But he figured it wouldn't hurt to drop by Pamidor's shop first, just to say hello.

"Grrrr..."

Set, walking beside Rei, purred contentedly.

Part of his good mood was likely due to the delicious dish of roasted nuts and meat bought earlier at the food stall, but meeting Pamidor's only son, Kumito, was also a factor.

While Set generally enjoyed playing with people, he seemed particularly delighted to play with a child like Kumito.

(Is it because their mental ages are similar?)

With that thought crossing his mind, Rei and Set continued on. After turning into a back alley and walking a short distance, their destination came into view.

"Ah, it's Set! Hey there, what's up? Long time no see! Haven't seen you around lately, but you been okay?"

"Guruu!"

Kumito, who had been playing with other kids in front of the shop, spotted Set first and dashed straight toward him.

The other children followed Kumi's lead, running toward Set.

Set watched them intently. Soon, the children leaped toward him. ...No, they hugged him.

"Set, how have you been?"

"I heard you came back yesterday, so you should've come to play sooner!"

"Um, Set... want some of this dried meat? It's delicious!"

"Ah, then I'll take this! Nuts!"

Watching everyone pet Set and feed him, Rei headed toward Pamidor's shop.

"Kumito, I need to talk to Pamidor for a bit. Keep Set company."

"Sure thing!"

Kumito replied happily at Rei's words.

Waving lightly to Kumi, Rei entered the shop.

But inside the shop, there was no one to be seen.

Well, to be precise, there was a presence in the blacksmith's workshop at the back, but it would be more accurate to say Pamidor wasn't visible in the shop area.

"...Wouldn't it be careless if thieves came?"

He murmured, but immediately dismissed the thought.

Anyone who knew what kind of person Pamidor was wouldn't even think of trying to rob this shop.

Pamidor himself was stubborn, but he wasn't the type to raise a hand to someone for no reason.

However, his face was extremely vicious. It was the kind of face that wouldn't look out of place on a bandit chieftain... not just some small-time gang, but a major leader commanding hundreds, even thousands of thieves.

Rei had his own thoughts about his current face, which sometimes led people to mistake him for a woman, but he was grateful to Zepail that it wasn't as savage-looking as Pamidor's.

If Rei had a face as vicious as Pamidor's, combined with his personality, he would undoubtedly have led a life embroiled in far more trouble than he does now.

...Though he was already involved in plenty of trouble as it was.

"Hmph, thieves? If any show up, I'll test-cut them with weapons I forged myself."

From the back of the shop, where the blacksmith's workshop was, a voice echoed as a figure emerged.

That vicious-looking face was definitely not something you could get used to easily.

If a child who knew nothing about Pamidor saw that face, it wouldn't be strange for them to cry out in terror.

(Come to think of it, it's surprising other kids played with Kumito right in front of this shop. Or maybe Pamidor actually appeals to children?)

Thinking rather rude thoughts, yet careful not to show it, Rei raised his hand to the Pamidor before him and spoke.

"Long time no see."

"Ah. I heard you were hired by some noble somewhere, but looks like that wasn't the case after all."

Word of Marka's arrival had spread quite a bit in Gilm.

It was already limited to those who came to Gilm in the snow, but then he arrived in a carriage so expensive that ordinary nobles couldn't afford it... well, that was bound to cause rumors.

With a carriage like that coming to pick up Rei, it was no wonder rumors spread that Rei had started serving a noble.

"Close enough. It's not like I joined the Duke of Quent's household or anything. I was just hired temporarily as an instructor for mock battles at the officer academy."

"...You, who caused such a huge commotion with the royalist nobles, joined the House of Quent, one of the most influential royalist families?"

Pamidor clearly understood the scale of the trouble Rei had caused and voiced his surprise.

"Well, there were... various things. Anyway, I came back yesterday."

"Ah, I know about that. It was the talk of the town, among those who knew. I heard you brought a woman back with you?"

"...I won't deny that, but somehow the meaning of the word 'woman' seems slightly different from what I had in mind. Anyway, that aside. I went out today as reinforcements for the guys hunting the Cyclops..."

The words that came out of Rei's mouth made Pamidor's vicious face show a look of utter disbelief.

"What the hell, man? Why are you so aggressive already, just a day after yesterday?"

"Well, I didn't exactly volunteer to fight the Cyclops myself, you know? An acquaintance of mine took a request to hunt one down in the forest, and instead of finding a lone Cyclops like it should be, there were five of them together. That's why he came asking for backup."

"Cyclops together? ...Well, that's quite a rare occurrence."

"Well, the result was... Ah, this is the floor, so it might be a bit dangerous. Sorry, but can we go to the workshop instead? The floor over there isn't like this, right?"

"Huh? Ah, sure, no problem..."

Pamidor didn't understand what Rei was suddenly talking about, but he still led him toward the workshop.

The workshop at the back of the shop had earthen flooring, and heat lingered inside, as if proving that forging had taken place there not long ago.

"Whoa, it's hot in here. ...This must be brutal in the summer, right?"

"Yeah. But if you're a blacksmith, that's just part of the job. So, what are you gonna show me?"

"This. This right here."

At Pamidor's words, Rei pulled a massive hammer from the Misty Ring.

Pamidor's eyes widened at the sight of the hammer appearing beside Rei.

No wonder. It was far beyond the size any ordinary human could wield.

"This is..."

"It's the hammer wielded by the rare species believed to have led the Cyclops. ...Do you understand?"

Without finishing his sentence, Rei murmured meaningfully. Pamidor touched the hammer.

Then, after a few seconds, Pamidor reflexively turned his gaze back to Rei.

What floated there was utter astonishment.

While today's story from Rei had contained many surprising elements, an even stronger shock now dominated Pamidor's expression.

"Could it be... a magic item!?"

"Correct. Impressive that you figured it out so quickly."

"Hold on. You defeated a Cyclops, right?"

"More accurately, a rare or higher-tier variant of a Cyclops."

"Why the vagueness? Didn't you bring the corpse?"

"The corpse itself vanished during the fight. This magic item's effect contributed to that."

Pamidor tilted her head, not understanding the meaning of the words coming from Rei's mouth.

(Can't blame him. Normally, no one would even consider being manipulated by a magic item.)

Pamidor wore an expression that, despite her vicious features, might be seen as somehow endearing... to some. Rei explained the hammer's ability to her.

Its ability to generate lightning where it strikes, grant its owner powerful regenerative abilities, and ultimately take root within the user's body, causing them to lose consciousness and allowing it to control them.

"...What can I say? You really had the nerve to bring such a powerful and vicious magic item here."

Pamidor, who kept her distance from the hammer as if repulsed by the very thought of touching it, heard Rei speak reassuringly.

"Don't worry, the abilities this magic item had—like controlling someone's body or growing roots inside them—are gone now. When it tried to take over my body, it got swept away by my magic instead and vanished."

"Overpowered by magic? What kind of magic do you even have?"

"Hmm, well... Decent?"

Of course, it wasn't a magical power level that could be described as merely decent, but he judged that mentioning it here would only cause unnecessary fuss, so he stated it that way.

I trust Pamidor, so even if my magic power were an abnormal amount, she probably wouldn't be that concerned... I thought to myself.

Pamidor seemed slightly puzzled by Rei's attitude, but since he hadn't said anything more, she assumed he had his reasons and didn't press further.

This kind of tactful restraint was probably why Rei found it easy to get along with Pamidor.

"Well, put my magic aside for now. How's that job I asked you about earlier? Forging the Noiz Magic Sword into a spear?"

"Azimov's basically handling that part. I'm just helping out."

"I figured. Anyway, I stopped by to say hello. Also... I was wondering if you could incorporate this hammer into the spear."

At the words coming from Rei's mouth, Pamidor flashed an expression that said, What the hell is this guy talking about?

He tried to say something, but no words came out. He opened and closed his mouth several times, and after a dozen seconds had passed, he finally spoke.

"Are you out of your mind? How the hell do you plan to use that hammer as a spear? What, are you gonna attach a spearhead to the hammer's tip or something? Well, just looking at the hammer part, it's got some serious attack power, and the sheer intimidation factor is no joke either."

"...Huh?"

This time, it was Rei who was momentarily confused, unsure what had just been said.

Pamidor caught sight of Rei's utterly stunned expression—a rare sight indeed. Unaware of Pamidor's reaction, Rei hurriedly shifted his gaze back to the hammer.

After studying the hammer intently, he looked back at Pamidor.

"If you melted part of this hammer and used it as material for the spear Azimov's making, could you make that spear generate lightning, or grant the user regenerative abilities, or something like that..."

"I don't think so. Well, to be precise, I don't know. At least, based on my knowledge, that kind of thing is impossible. Besides, the spear's material is a Magic Sword, right? Meaning it's a magic item. Mixing another magic item into that..."

"...You're kidding me..."

For Rei, since he couldn't obtain the red cyclops' magic stone or materials, the greatest reward he could gain from this incident was this magic item hammer.

However, a hammer this size was impractical for Rei to use normally. He had planned to use it as material when reforging Noiz's Magic Sword into a spear... a magic spear. But that plan had been completely derailed.

"Is it absolutely impossible?"

"I'm not that knowledgeable about alchemy, but a blacksmith's skill is essential when crafting weapons like Magic Swords or magic spears. I've actually made several such weapons before. Based on that experience..."

He stopped speaking there and shook his head.

That gesture spoke volumes about Pamidor's thoughts.

But he must have noticed how shocked Rei looked at his words. After a moment's thought, Pamidor sighed and spoke.

"I couldn't do it myself, but Azimov might be able to figure something out. He may look like that, and his personality is what it is, but he's a skilled guy."

"Really!?"

"Maybe. I can't say for sure. But you were planning to go check on the spear's condition later anyway, right? Why not ask him then?"

"Got it. I'll go right now. Come on, get ready, Pamidor."

Pamidor tried to say something to Rei, but seemed to decide it was pointless. Sighing resignedly, she shook her head.

"Alright, I'll get ready quickly. Wait here. I had something to take to Azimov's place anyway. ...I was actually planning to go after closing the shop."

With that, Pamidor finished preparing to temporarily close the shop and stepped outside.

He told Kumito, who was playing there, that he'd be out for a bit, then headed to Ajimov's place with Rei and Set.
