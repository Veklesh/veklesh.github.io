The matter with Tris was settled for the time being, and Rei decided they would act independently, though he remained somewhat dissatisfied.

Now Rei needed to find proof that Precious had orchestrated this incident. But since Spier Trading Company—no, Tris himself—had decided they wanted to resolve this matter themselves, they wouldn't just stand by and watch Rei's actions.

(How to outmaneuver them, huh?)

Pondering this, Rei exited the Spier Trading Company building with Marina and the others.

After getting directions to the stable where Set was kept, they headed there. There, women and children—and even some adult men—were gathered, all feeding Set, petting him, and doting on him.

(Here too?)

Seeing Set's unwavering popularity, Rei felt the intense emotions within him calm down a little.

Yes, getting worked up here and now wouldn't change anything.

After all, what needed to be done was already decided. All he had to do was do it.

"Ah, Rei-san!?"

One of the people who had been paying attention to Set noticed Rei and the others and called out.

At that word, the others immediately turned their gaze toward Rei and, looking disappointed, moved away from Set.

They understood that since Rei had arrived, their time playing with Set was over.

Grateful for their understanding, Rei called out to Set.

"Set, let's go."

"Grrrr..."

Responding to Rei's call, Set reluctantly rose from the food, still reluctant to leave it behind, and headed toward Rei.

Stroking him as he did so, Rei spoke.

"Thanks for keeping Set company. He seemed happy too."

"Grrr."

Just as Rei said, Set meowed happily.

Seeing Set like this, those who had been doting on him moments ago were enchanted... but the next instant, they snapped back to reality at what Rei said.

"For a while, I'll be your enemy... no, your rival, so I can't really get involved. But once this matter is settled, please be kind to him again."

"Huh?"

"Rivalry?"

"Competition?"

"Why?"

Several people murmured in response to Rei's words.

Their tone suggested utter confusion as to why things had come to this.

But Rei said no more, taking Set and leaving the scene.

(If this can slow down Spier Trading Company's movements even a little, that'd be nice.)

He thought to himself.

Although the Spier Trading Company had a branch in Gilm, naturally, not all of its staff had been brought over from the main office.

They had hired a considerable number of people after arriving in Gilm.

Those individuals naturally knew of Set's standing in Gilm.

In fact, even those who had just arrived in Gilm wouldn't find it difficult to grasp what kind of figure Set was to Gilm, provided they were reasonably well-informed.

That's precisely why those who heard the words coming from Rei's mouth were startled, their bodies stiffening.

...The only saving grace was that it had been rephrased as a competitive relationship, not an adversarial one.

Leaving the still-shaken individuals behind, Rei and the others exited the Spier Trading Company grounds.

Terry, who had come outside the shop, offered to send them by carriage. But Rei declined, not wanting to owe a favor to the Spier Trading Company, with whom they were now entering a competitive relationship, and began walking.

"...Isn't that a little mean?"

After moving a short distance away from the Spier Trading Company, Marina suddenly murmured this.

Rei tilted his head, deciding it wasn't something worth getting upset about.

"Is that so? But I think it's better they know sooner rather than later, rather than Tris suddenly telling them we're now competing with them."

"But that just means they'll have more time to panic, thinking we're now their enemies."

Vihera interjected into Rei and Marina's conversation.

"No, it's not an adversarial relationship, it's a competitive one, right?"

"Normally, it wouldn't make much difference. ...Well, I suppose it would be different if it were Rei."

Rei doesn't exactly go easy on those who become his adversaries.

Thinking about it that way, calling it a rivalry wasn't necessarily a bad thing.

Still, it had shocked quite a few people regardless.

"Well, that's that, and this is this. More importantly, what do we do now... Isn't Azimov waking up soon? His wounds were healed with potions, right?"

"Yeah, you're right. It wouldn't be strange if he regained consciousness soon. The problem is whether Azimov has any information about the person who attacked him."

Marina's words made Rei pause for a moment, but he quickly changed his mind.

To be honest, there weren't any real clues left behind from this incident.

They had Set track the scent, and that led them to Galahad's mansion.

Since the scent trail ended there, Azimov's memories were now their most significant lead.

...If brute force were an option, there was the method of asking Precious directly, but that sort of approach was forbidden this time.

(Ah, but what's forbidden is violent means, right? Then, instead of laying hands on her, I could talk to her to unsettle her... Impossible.)

No matter how much Rei tried to talk to Precious, she wouldn't be shaken by that alone.

And the chance that such agitation would lead them to make a careless move is practically zero.

If they did make a poor move, it would be fatal for them... That's what Rei thought, but he immediately shook his head.

Worrying about the impossible was pointless.

(Though, if truly cornered... if it came to that point of desperation, it wasn't entirely impossible they might take such a gamble for a last-ditch reversal.)

But then again, if Precious were truly cornered like that, wouldn't she just leave Gilm altogether?

The only reason Precious was still with Gilm was pure rivalry against Tris.

Rei couldn't imagine her staying at Gilm even if she thought it might lead to her own ruin.

(Well, if her resentment toward Tris is that deep, that's a different story... No, is that even possible? After all, the Gilm branch was entrusted to Tris. So, there's no reason Precious should be here, yet she came anyway. That alone proves her jealousy, resentment, and envy toward Tris are definitely strong.)

If this weren't a remote Gilm branch, moving to another town or village wouldn't be so difficult.

But this was the frontier.

A veritable hell on earth teeming with all manner of monsters.

For someone to come all the way to this Gilm of their own accord meant they definitely harbored strong feelings.

Of course, spring had just arrived, and many adventurers were coming to Gilm.

If you asked such people for escort, you could probably make it to Gilm without spending much money.

...But the adventurers coming to Gilm this season were truly a mixed bag.

The skilled ones possessed strength sufficient to survive in Gilm, but among them were also adventurers who mistakenly believed themselves to be skilled, when in reality they weren't nearly as capable.

Ironically, these are the ones who tend to be loud and brimming with confidence, so many merchants get half-convinced by their bluster.

(I hear Precious is quite the merchant even within the Spier Trading Company, so I doubt he'd fall for such tricks.)

Since she could stand up to Tris, that much was certain.

Still, despite being that kind of person, he also possessed the recklessness to pick a fight with Rei.

Regarding Gilm... and Rei... even a little research should have made it easy to imagine that they weren't opponents you could carelessly make enemies of.

And yet, he's actively antagonizing Rei right now.

"So, let's check if Azimov's consciousness is back... then start asking around? Informants or whatever—I've never met one myself."

Up until now, whenever he needed to gather information, luck had been on his side, and he hadn't faced any particular difficulties... well, he had struggled, but he'd still managed to gather information on his own.

But since informants existed, using one would undoubtedly be quicker.

Marina was the first to react to Rei's words about informants, having heard about them from Marina and Vihera.

Given her former position as guildmaster, she likely had some connections to informants.

Vihera had connections to informants back in the Labyrinth City she used to be in, but unfortunately, she had no such connections in Gilm.

She had thought she needed to build connections with informants, but somehow it kept getting put off.

However, Marina didn't have particularly detailed connections either.

Before becoming guildmaster, when she was active as an adventurer, she had known several informants.

But that was a long time ago, and almost none of them are still active now.

"How do you even make contact with an informant?"

"Information brokers themselves? You can find plenty if you look, right? It's just that truly trustworthy ones, capable ones, are few and far between."

Rei answered Rei's question.

Informants, like adventurers, are a mixed bag.

It's not uncommon to encounter those who bring completely irrelevant information, or worse, fabricate information outright, or charge exorbitant fees for their services...

In fact, compared to adventurers, the gems among them were even fewer.

That's precisely why connections to competent informants were extremely rare.

Moreover, competent informants themselves knew that making a careless mistake could cost them their lives, so few would willingly step forward.

That's precisely why gaining access to such a capable informant usually required a referral from someone influential.

Building that connection, however, was also difficult.

Because if you introduced someone careless and that person did something strange to the informant, the introducer would also be held responsible.

It stands to reason that a skilled informant wouldn't want to deal with such a risky individual.

If that happens, the connection between the adventurer and the informant is severed.

"Ah, I see it now."

They continued walking while snacking.

They walked while gathering various bits of information as they snacked, but nothing resembling real intelligence came from it.

...Even if Precious was openly active, she herself didn't have a particularly striking appearance.

At the very least, not like Marina or Vihera... nor like Rei without her Dragon Robe hood, and certainly not like Set, whose appearance made a strong impression at first glance.

Looking for information on someone like that wasn't going to yield results anytime soon.

"So you couldn't get any information after all?"

"But at least we got to buy all kinds of food. ...It was an amount we'd normally never be able to finish, but with Rei, Set, and Byune here, it was almost not enough."

"Well, with Set around, that part was pretty much a given, wasn't it?"

Rei said this while stroking Set, responding to Vihera's somewhat exasperated expression.

Set, being petted, tilted his head slightly, looking at Rei as if to ask, What's up?

"Nothing. We're going to chat for a bit at the guard post. You hang out around here, Set. It's just the right temperature, perfect for a nap."

"Grrr."

At Rei's words, Set looked up at the sky and purred happily at the soft light streaming down from the sun, then lay down near the guard post.

...Of course, guards stood in front of the guardhouse, but they didn't bother to warn Set.

They knew it would be pointless anyway... and more than anything, they could enjoy watching Set sleep right beside them.

As residents of Gilm, it was only natural that many of the guards favored Set.

No, since they handle procedures for those leaving Gilm, adventurers like Rei, who frequently go out, and the guards have many opportunities to interact.

Having frequent contact with Rei meant they also had frequent contact with Set.

Given that, it was only natural for the guards to develop affection for Set.

In fact, it had become something of a regular occurrence for idle guards to offer Set dried meat while Rei handled paperwork.

Of course, not every guard felt such affection for Set, but at the very least, the guard standing before the guardhouse now narrowed his eyes fondly at the sight of Set lying sprawled out.
