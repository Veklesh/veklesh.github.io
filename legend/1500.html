Several days after Ilse's revenge was completed... That morning, though a little earlier than usual, Rei and his group had arrived at the main gate of Gilm.

Even Elena and Ara, who usually stayed inside Marina's house to avoid the hassle of visitors requesting meetings, were present.

"Thank you all,"

Ilse said, bowing deeply.

Ara spoke to Ilse with concern.

"Ilse, are you really going? If you'd like, I could recommend you for Lady Elena's escort knights..."

"No, I'll pass. I'm nowhere near strong enough to join the knights right now... More than anything, I want to think carefully about what I truly want to do."

Ilse declared this with a smile.

Compared to just a few days ago, the day after she had slain Ajax with her own hands, she seemed much more energetic now.

Had Ilse spent these past days alone, she wouldn't have recovered this much.

The reason she had recovered so much was largely due to the presence of a friend she could truly open up to... Ara.

It wasn't just Ilse who felt friendship toward Ara; Ara also cherished the time she spent with Ilse.

That's precisely why she had even asked to join Elena's escort knight corps, wanting to spend time with her friend.

Given Ara's position as the commander of the escort knights, recommending a single new recruit to the order wasn't particularly difficult.

Of course, that would mean joining through connections, but it wasn't particularly unusual in Elgin.

But Ilse declined Ara's invitation.

She wanted to properly determine what she truly desired to do.

And so, Ilse decided to leave Gilm in search of her own path.

"Take care of yourself. You finally got your revenge. If you get killed by bandits or monsters along the way, it won't even be funny. Ara would be sorry to lose a friend she finally made."

After exchanging words with Ara, Elena also spoke to Ilse.

At first, Ilse hesitated to speak directly with Elena, known as the Princess General. But after living under the same roof for days, she gradually opened up.

Since Ilse was arguably the cause of this whole incident, she had already checked out of the inn where she had previously stayed, fearing possible retaliation from the underworld.

...But since it was the inn where she had stayed with Melan, Ilse showed no hesitation whatsoever.

It wasn't that the inn itself was bad, but the fact that Melan had been there was probably the biggest negative impression.

That said, the inn wasn't troubled by it.

The security guards had notified them that Melan was dead, and the belongings left in the room had already been sold off. New guests had moved in.

The room Ilse had stayed in also had another guest staying there the very day she checked out.

Many people still gathered at Gilm seeking work related to the expansion project.

For such people, being able to sleep at an inn is incredibly fortunate.

Due to the shortage of lodging, many people were sleeping rough even within Gilm, and that had been a problem until recently.

Now, several larger huts have been built near the construction site, and those without lodging are supposed to sleep there.

But naturally, such places offer no comfort like an inn, and most sleep huddled together directly on the floor.

They must bring their own blankets and, of course, provide their own meals and other necessities.

Above all, considering the risk of having personal belongings stolen, it's a highly risky place to sleep.

Still, many people were using these places now, reasoning that it was better than nowhere at all.

Compared to sleeping in such a place, the inn where Ilse had stayed was nothing short of heaven.

"Thank you. ...And thank you to everyone else who helped me seek revenge."

"Hey, Ilse! We're about to leave, so get ready!"

A merchant man, who had been discussing various matters with the adventurer escort a short distance away, waved toward Ilse.

If she was leaving Gilm, it was only natural she couldn't go alone.

Well, an adventurer with sufficient skill could probably manage alone outside Gilm now.

Due to the expansion work, many people were coming to and leaving Gilm, so most monsters near the roads had already been defeated.

...Yet, it couldn't be said they were completely wiped out. That's because, night after night, monsters gathered from who knows where, seemingly to replenish their numbers.

Of course, most of the arriving monsters are low-rank creatures... mostly goblins and kobolds, but occasionally high-rank monsters appear too.

That's precisely why, while she could move about alone, if a solo adventurer encountered a high-rank monster, death would be inevitable.

Even adventurers possessing a certain level of skill faced such danger. For Ilse, who had barely experienced any real combat since becoming an adventurer, moving through such a place alone was far too risky.

That's why Ara asked Elena for her friend's sake. Elena explained the situation to Daskar and, while she was at it, asked him to introduce a reputable merchant.

Of course, since Ilse had no combat experience, she couldn't be hired as a guard and was treated as a guest instead.

The merchant probably felt Daskar had foisted a troublesome client on him... but since Ilse's case had created a certain bond between him and Daskar, it wasn't all bad.

"Yes, understood! I'll go immediately."

Ilse replied to the merchant... then bowed deeply once more to Rei and the others before leaving.

As she turned, her gaze met her friend Ara's for just an instant, but she convinced herself that if she was alive, they would meet again.

Ara felt the same. She gave Ilse a light wave as she departed, looking a little lonely, but she ended her farewell with her friend.

The merchant who had put Ilse in the carriage bowed to Rei and the others once more before setting off.

The adventurers guarding the carriage were also slightly tense, knowing the person they were protecting was someone Rei knew.

They were likely thinking that they absolutely couldn't let anything happen to Rei's acquaintance.

If Ilse had been traveling with the merchant as an adventurer escort, it would have been different, but this time she was purely a guest.

That was precisely why they were nervous.

(If Ilse got hurt, I think her friend Ara would be angrier than me, though.)

Rei had helped Ilse with various things, but he didn't feel that close to her.

The one who feels strong affection for Ilse is definitely Ara.

Also, Elena, Ara's master who spends her days shut up in Marina's house, naturally ended up talking to Ilse quite a bit and developed a certain fondness for her too.

"She's gone,"

Elena murmured as she watched the carriage head beyond the main gate.

Hearing this, Ara nodded regretfully.

Though Ara had seen Ilse off with a smile, parting with a friend was undoubtedly sad for her.

Ara's personality wasn't one that made it easy for her to make friends.

After all, Elena was always Ara's top priority, so it was only natural.

That very fact made Ilse an irreplaceable friend to Ara.

It was only natural that Ara would feel lonely after parting with such a friend.

"I suppose so. But... if she's alive, we'll meet again someday. Besides, Ilse might change her mind and join the Knight Guard eventually."

Ara said this, but Elena's escort knights were essentially all elite.

With Ilse's current lack of combat skills, even if she did join the escort knights, she couldn't be considered a combatant.

At best, she'd be an administrative staff member.

Then again, the escort knights were short on that kind of personnel, so it might actually be a good fit.

"Yes. If she lives, I believe we'll meet again."

The one who said this to Ara was Marina, a dark elf who had lived for many years.

Having met and parted with countless friends over her long experience, Marina's words carried great weight.

"Well, we've seen Ilse off... I suppose it's time for me to get back to work. If Elena and Ara stay here much longer, it'll only cause trouble, you know? Wouldn't it be better to head back to Marina's place soon?"

Vihera, who didn't feel as strongly about Ilse as Ara and Elena did, urged them on with these words.

Indeed, a fair number of people were already passing through the gate.

Since the gate hadn't been open for very long, many people were entering or leaving Gilm.

This was true even under normal circumstances, and now, with the expansion work underway in Gilm, the numbers were even greater.

Amidst this, it was only natural that people would want to catch a glimpse of celebrities like Rei and his group. More than that, it was only natural that some would want to get close to them.

Especially now, with the radiantly beautiful Princess General Elena present.

The chance to meet someone who could be called the symbol of the Noble Faction was rare indeed.

Precisely because of that, many were waiting for a chance to speak with her.

...Still, it seemed they had enough sense to wait until the farewells were over before approaching them.

But now that Ilse's farewell was over, it wouldn't be strange for those waiting for a chance to speak to Elena and the others to act at any moment. And then...

"Oh my, isn't that Rei-chan over there? And Vihera-chan and the others too... It's been a while!"

Suddenly, that voice called out.

Those around them, who had been waiting for their chance and keeping an eye on the others, felt beaten to the punch by that voice. They tried to glare sharply at the person who had spoken to Rei and the others... but the moment they saw that person's figure, their eyes widened in astonishment.

Because that person was a huge, muscular man... yet, for some reason, he wore light makeup.

He was definitely not someone you'd willingly strike up a conversation with.

Those seeing him for the first time assumed Rei and the others would either immediately eliminate him or ignore him.

But those who knew who this person was wore bitter expressions.

Those around them, seeing these expressions, wondered why.

That question was answered the very next moment.

"Hey, Bistor. Long time no see."

"Yeah. Thanks for helping me out back then. Thanks to you, I was able to come back to Gilm like this."

As the man said this, closing one eye, those around him wore expressions of "Whoa..."

Men dressed as women and women dressed as men weren't unheard of, though they were rare.

But someone like this man was extremely rare.

"Rei, do you know him?"

Though Bystol's appearance would normally be enough to make anyone recoil, Elena showed no particular concern, calmly asking Rei.

Just how bizarre this was became clear to anyone seeing the look of utter shock on Ara's face beside Elena.

(Well, she just experienced parting with a friend and then saw someone like Bistor up close. Maybe it's not so strange she reacted this way.)

Observing Ara's reaction, Rei nodded back at Elena.

"Yeah. Just met him a little while ago."

"Oh my, this beauty over here... beauty... huh? Could it be...?"

Seeing Elena, she must have realized who that person was.

Even Bistor wore an expression of astonishment, wondering why this person was here.

"Yeah, just as Bistor expected."

"...Well, I never."

Even the battle-hardened Bistor hadn't anticipated encountering Elena.

His eyes widened in astonishment as he gazed at Elena's beauty.

"Bistol, shouldn't we be going soon!?"

The voice calling out to the stunned Bistol belonged to someone Rei recognized.

Someone he'd seen several times before in connection with Bistol's past affairs.

He noticed Rei and the others too, bowing deeply with gratitude.

"Oh, dear. You're right. I'm in a bit of a hurry. ...I'll make time another day. Let's have a meal together then."

"Ah... right."

Rei nodded to Bistol, then suddenly felt like speaking up.

"Do you know the country of Rebururina?"

"Huh? Oh, yeah, it's one of this country's vassal states, right?"

"...I see."

Hearing that, Rei resolved to ask Bistor about it when they met later.

What was Ajax... and the organization Ajax belonged to... thinking?

Because he simply couldn't stop wondering about it.
