After finishing lunch or breakfast, Rei and the others headed to the guild with Set in tow.

Bringing Set along serves as a sort of insect repellent.

Of course, the "bugs" in this case refer to men—and occasionally women—who harbor hopes of getting even slightly closer to Elena and the others.

If this were Gilm, they wouldn't need to resort to such measures; few would bother them. But this is the Kingdom of Reburina, where Rei and the others' names are still largely unknown.

Well, rumors of nicknames like Crimson or Princess General probably reach here, but few know their actual appearances.

In such a situation, Rei and the others acting alone would look like they were inviting trouble.

But with Set there... the idea was that few would dare pick a fight with such a group.

Indeed, even walking like this, no one came to bother Rei and the others, proving that aim was absolutely correct.

(If this were Gilm, it'd be the opposite—people would flock to them precisely because Set was there.)

Set, who had become something of a mascot for Gilm, drew children who wanted to play whenever he appeared, and adults frequently fawned over him, offering him snacks.

But here, the exact opposite was happening.

"Grrr..."

Set growled sadly, realizing he was being feared.

Stroking Set to comfort him, Rei continued toward the guild.

Many were startled to see Set, but seeing the familiar's necklace brought relief to most.

Continuing on through the crowd, they eventually arrived at their destination, the guild.

"I know it's too late to ask, but did we really need everyone to come?"

Rei murmurs as she looks at the guild building... a structure so small it couldn't compare to Gilm.

"Yesterday was quite a mess. ...You understand, right?"

Marina addressed Rei with a look of utter exasperation.

Even in such a casual manner, Marina's feminine allure was impossible to hide.

It wasn't intentional on her part, but Marina's voluptuous figure, her tanned skin, and the party dress revealing her cleavage... all of it drew men to her like bees to honey.

Just yesterday, before meeting the Guildmaster, she'd been hit on by several men. Some had even tried to drag Marina away by force.

Of course, Marina had no interest in going along with such men. She firmly rejected them all, and when one tried to force himself on her, she dealt with him appropriately using spirit magic.

For that reason, Marina had judged that going outside the Silver Fruit Inn to meet the guild's informant would only lead to more trouble. That's why she suggested taking Set with her.

...Also, the presence of others besides Marina—Elena, Vihera, and Byune—likely played a significant role in that decision.

If Marina alone caused that much commotion, having the others along would undoubtedly lead to a major uproar.

(Though, who knows what would happen if they joined the guild...)

Watching Set happily being stroked by Rei, Marina thought to herself.

Up until now, having Set as a deterrent meant there were no particular problems.

But Set obviously couldn't join the guild.

So, it was only natural to predict that trouble within the guild would be unavoidable.

(I wonder how much word has spread about the person who tried to lay a hand on me yesterday... Besides, it's daytime now, thankfully. Most adventurers are out on quests... No, that was the same yesterday too. Still, it caused quite a commotion then.)

Marina let out a small sigh of "Huh," which only added to her allure.

An adventurer who happened to pass near Rei and the others was startled by Set's appearance, but his gaze was immediately drawn to Marina's profile.

Unaware of this, Rei and the others headed into the guild together.

The adventurer, who had been captivated by Marina's troubled profile, watched them leave... Then, suddenly realizing something, he noticed the griffin, Set, staring at him curiously. He let out a small cry and ran off.

"Ma-Marina-sama!?"

The guild receptionist, upon seeing Marina, cried out at that instant and immediately stepped out from behind the counter to approach Marina and the others.

She was reasonably well-featured, more cute than beautiful, but she had a reputation for being dependable.

That's precisely why the adventurers watching her flustered state now wore expressions of surprise.

There were fewer than ten adventurers inside the guild itself, and about the same number in the attached tavern.

It being past noon, many were likely eating in the tavern.

Drawing the attention of those people, the receptionist made her way to where Marina stood.

Among the adventurers, some likely knew about yesterday's events. Seeing Marina, some were stunned.

Some had considered making a pass at the beauties—Elena, Marina, and Vihera—but the receptionist's immediate approach robbed them of that chance.

Then again, the receptionist had rushed over precisely for that purpose from the start, so it was all according to plan.

"Oh, um... Nanaris... was it?"

"Y-yes. It's an honor that Lady Marina remembers my name. So, what brings you here today? I mean... with such a large group?"

The receptionist called Nanaris turned her gaze toward Rei and the others standing behind Marina.

Two women possessing beauty equal to Marina's, a girl with an almost doll-like beauty, and Rei wearing the Dragon Robe.

...Within this group, it was somewhat unusual that Rei actually looked like the least conspicuous ordinary person.

Of course, that was only because Rei had the hood of his Dragon Robe up. Without it, Rei's feminine features would inevitably draw attention.

"Yes, I have some business at the guild. ...There are people around here, so could you lend us a room somewhere?"

"U-understood! I'll contact the Guildmaster immediately!"

With that, Nanaris turned on her heel and headed toward the back of the counter.

"Ah, wait... I didn't say I had business with the Guildmaster. I just needed a room..."

Marina muttered, but Nanaris was already gone.

Marina looked around, slightly troubled, but no one met her gaze.

The adventurers who had shown interest in Marina and her companions when they entered the guild now understood, from the current situation, that Marina and her group held some kind of special status. They quietly averted their eyes.

"Well, if you can talk to the Guildmaster for us, wouldn't that make things easier for us too?"

At Vihera's words, Marina wore a complex expression and turned her gaze toward Rei and Elena.

"Well, if the Guildmaster is willing to intervene, it's likely the person we're being introduced to isn't a problematic individual, right?"

"Hmm, I'm not that knowledgeable about adventurers, so I can't say for sure. But if adventurer Rei says so, maybe we can trust him?"

Marina tried to say something in response to their words, but... in the end, she said nothing more and looked away.

At that very moment, as if waiting for Marina to look away, the sound of footsteps came running from the counter.

And they were loud footsteps—the kind someone experienced in combat, like an adventurer, would normally avoid making.

"Here they come,"

Marina murmured, and soon the source of the footsteps appeared.

A middle-aged man easily weighing over 100 kg, his hair already thinning.

It being summer, he was running with sweat pouring down his face.

"Hii, hii, I-I'm sorry to keep you waiting, Marina-sama!"

The man announced, still panting heavily.

The current Guildmaster addressed Marina, the former Guildmaster and now an adventurer, with the honorific "-sama."

This seemed to starkly reveal the power dynamic between them.

(It's not like the guild master has to be a former adventurer... but still, isn't this a bit much?)

Rei watched the man, who was sweating and catching his breath, and thought to himself.

Though Rei didn't know the exact layout of this guild, he could imagine that, being a guild, its structure wouldn't differ greatly from Gilm's guild, despite the difference in size.

That's precisely why seeing someone sweat this much just from running from the guildmaster's office to here... even if it was summer, was a bit hard to understand.

Well, if he'd just been sweating a little, that might have been understandable... but the man's forehead was drenched in sweat, as if he'd run several kilometers under the blazing sun.

Of course, Rei knew a few guildmasters who weren't former adventurers.

Still, a man like this... I thought, and suddenly realized.

(So, even looking like this, he has the ability to function as guildmaster...? Well, this is the Kingdom of Reburina, so that probably plays a part too.)

If that's the case, then perhaps he's an opponent not to be underestimated.

As I observed the guildmaster like this, he finally caught his breath and spoke.

"My lady Marina, I'm afraid I've shown you a rather unsightly side of myself. Now then, what brings you here today?"

"...Well. It might be better to ask Zels than to bother the guild staff. I'd like to talk to you for a moment. Would that be alright?"

"Yes, yes. Of course! For Lady Marina, I'll make time anytime! Then, the conference room... no, would the office be better?"

Guildmaster Zels told Marina this, his eyes sparkling.

Watching Zels, Rei was surprised to see absolutely no desire for Marina in his eyes.

Well, to be precise, he was genuinely pleased to be speaking with Marina. But even seeing her in that party dress with its plunging neckline, he wasn't casting any lecherous glances her way.

If Zels had appeared as a small child, Rei probably wouldn't have felt anything particularly strange.

But Zels was extremely obese—the kind of man who sweats just walking from his office to the counter.

Based on Rei's experience, most men would stare, be captivated, enchanted, and drool at the sight of a beauty like Marina.

Not all of them, but at the very least, Ray couldn't possibly believe the Zelus standing before her was one of those rare exceptions.

(Wait, could he be some kind of sly fox? Is that it? Well, if so, I suppose I could understand...?)

While Rei was observing Zels, the conversation progressed, and soon Rei and the others were guided by Zels into the inner part of the counter.

The adventurers around them, the patrons eating and drinking in the tavern, and the guild staff—including Nanaris—all silently watched Rei go.

"Haa, haa, haa... Please, as I showed you yesterday, this is my office. Come in."

Even the exertion of climbing the stairs behind the guild counter seemed quite taxing for Zels. Wiping the sweat pouring from his face with a handkerchief, he announced this.

The current summer season might have contributed to his exertion beyond the physical climb, but even so, Rei couldn't help but think he was sweating far too much.

Thinking this, Rei followed Marina into the office.

The moment they stepped inside, cool air greeted Rei.

It was obvious magic items like those used in inns were at work here.

That said, since Rei was wearing his Dragon Robe, he didn't seem particularly bothered by the coolness.

(Well, judging by Zelus's state, working in the same temperature as outside would be impossible.)

Sweating that much, he might even get sweat on the documents. If that happened, the papers would be ruined too. Watching Zels, who seemed comfortable despite wiping sweat now that they were back in the cool room, Rei thought.

"Now then, everyone, please take a seat on that sofa. I'd like to hear the reason you came to see me."

Zelus said this with a smile on his face.
