"Grrrrrrrrrrr"

Hearing that sound from behind, she wiped her face with the cloth she'd taken from her Misty Ring.

She washed her face with the Running Water Dagger.

If anyone knew the circumstances, their eyes would widen in astonishment.

The water created by the Water-Flowing Dagger, which generates water through the magic it carries, and the water created using Rei's magic, though merely water, tasted so exquisite it could easily be called heavenly.

But even water that nobles would pay any price to buy was nothing more than ordinary water to Rei.

"Hey, Set. Come on, stop being so clingy."

Bathed in the morning sunlight, Rei stroked Set's head as the puppy rubbed his face against him, whining that he was hungry.

The grove they found on their way to Hazlue.

That was where Rei had decided to camp out yesterday.

The sight of the forest, where the morning sun created patches of shade, was captivating even for Rei, who was accustomed to the mountains.

"Well, it's quite different from the scenery I saw in Japan."

The mountain where Rei lived back in Japan and this grove seemed similar yet were quite different in many ways.

Many of the trees growing here were ones he had never seen in Japan.

Taking in the morning forest scene, Rei pulled various dishes out of the Misty Ring.

Skewers, stew, bread, stir-fries, simmered dishes...

A bit too luxurious to consider breakfast... or rather, some might say it's too heavy a meal.

But for Rei and Set, finishing off a meal like this posed no particular problem.

"Gururuu"

Set happily growled as he ate the stir-fry.

Watching Set, Rei ate his stew.

The stew, simmered to perfection and bursting with vegetable flavor, tasted absolutely divine when paired with freshly baked bread.

After finishing such a luxurious meal and taking a short break, Rei mounted Set and flew off toward Hazlue.

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

Rei and Set arrived in Hazlue just before noon, but the only welcome they received from the guards was a scream.

Rei found it a bit disappointing, as such things hadn't happened in the Viscount Gale's domain, but he reasoned that since Set was a griffin, such scenes were unavoidable. He presented his guild card.

"Relax, this is my familiar. More importantly, please process our entry into the city."

Upon seeing the guild card handed to him by Rei, the guard managed to stifle another gasp of astonishment.

(Rank B... And a griffin? So this guy... Crimson?! Why would a famous adventurer like Crimson...?)

The guard managed to suppress his shock—though it didn't look suppressed at all to Rei—and began processing them.

Watching the guard's reaction, Rei realized the news about the rebel army hadn't spread this far yet.

But that was only natural. Relduct had been attacked by Rei and captured by the rebels—this was his assumption—last night.

Rei had been able to reach this place so quickly precisely because she was riding Set, but it would likely take some time for the news to properly reach this area.

Considering Set's flight speed, it wouldn't be surprising if it took over a week.

In any case, after completing the formalities, Rei entered Hazlue with Set.

It was fortunate for Rei that they weren't told Set couldn't enter the city.

...Though it was probably largely because the guards harbored a fear of what might happen if they angered Rei.

"Now, the problem is... where Jazz's brother, Dostrite, lives, right?"

Rei had heard Dostrite lived in Hazlue, but naturally, he didn't know the exact location.

Deciding that asking someone was the best course of action, he told Set to wait a short distance away so he wouldn't be startled, then headed for a nearby food stall... but none of the dishes on offer looked particularly appetizing.

The heavy taxes imposed by Lerdact likely meant the quality of goods sold in shops had deteriorated considerably.

And yet, Lerdact himself was eating delicious food.

Even Rei could see why a rebellion might arise under such circumstances.

"Hey, customer, what's the matter?"

"Ah... Right. Give me a few of those skewers over there. And could you tell me where that Dostrite guy's house is?"

Saying this, he handed over a few copper coins, slightly more than the price of the skewers.

The shopkeeper counted the coins and, without hesitation, told Rei where Dostrite's house was.

His expression was desperate, as if he couldn't afford to let this money slip away.

Here too, the heavy taxes of Lerdact were having a strong impact.

(Well, if he holds out a little longer, the rebels will contact him. He just needs to make it that far. If I tell him, I'll only confuse things further.)

Knowing that his own careless words would only cause more confusion, Rei headed straight for the house of Dostrite he had been told about.

He passed several shops selling food along the way, but having grasped the general situation at the first shop, Rei refrained from his usual habit of buying in bulk.

In terms of charity, perhaps buying would have been better.

But if he did that at just one shop, the others would envy it.

To avoid that, he would have had to buy from every single shop.

Of course, with Rei's wealth, that much was easily within his means.

Especially now that he possessed magic items seized from Relduct.

But that would mean the items he acquired would be completely dead stock.

Thinking that wasn't quite right, Rei ultimately never shopped at any store after the first one.

If there were many people on the brink of starvation, Rei would have changed his approach considerably.

"Ah, here it is."

Moving to the location mentioned at the stall, he found a shop selling tableware.

(A tableware shop... Looking at the state of this town, it doesn't seem like they could survive on that alone.)

Finding that a bit odd, Rei told Set to wait a little ways off and entered the shop.

"Welcome."

Turning toward the voice, he saw a well-built man with a stern, almost grumpy expression—the very picture of a craftsman.

Who he was, Rei understood without needing to think.

After all, he had the same face as Jazz... and above all, the same build.

"What are you looking for, customer?"

Despite entering the shop, Rei wasn't looking around the interior but was staring straight at him. The man must have found this suspicious.

He asked with a slightly wary look.

In response to those words, Rei opened his mouth.

"You're Dostrite, right?"

"...That's me. But who are you? If you're going to ask someone their name, how about showing your own face?"

Rei nodded at Dostrite's words and pulled back the hood he wore.

Seeing Rei's face emerge, Dostrite froze in surprise for several seconds.

He'd probably imagined all sorts of faces, but he certainly hadn't expected to see one like Rei's—a face truly deserving of being called feminine.

"Sorry about that. I just didn't want any trouble."

"Ah, yeah... I guess so."

Dostrite seemed to have grasped the general nature of the trouble Rei might get into. He relaxed his guard just a little.

"So, what business does someone like you have with me?"

"A bit late, but I'm delivering a letter."

"A letter?"

"Yeah. From Jazz."

"Huh!?"

The unexpected name that came out of Rei's mouth made Dostrite's face contort in shock.

"Jazz?"

"Yeah. There was a Jazz in a village we stopped at on the way to this Lerdact County. So I picked up a letter for him."

There were actually quite a few details, but he kept quiet, figuring telling them would only worry Dostrite.

Rei then handed Dostrite the letter he'd taken out from the Misty Ring.

"It's from Jazz."

At Rei's words, Dostrite carefully took the letter and began reading it immediately.

(Jazz is an adventurer, so it's no surprise he can read and write, but Dostrite can too? ...Well, he runs a shop, so it makes sense he can read and write.)

Adventurers need at least basic literacy to read quest requests.

And merchants need it to keep ledgers and such.

Thinking about it that way, Rei understood why both Jazz and Dostrite could read and write.

"Huh."

While Dostrite read the letter, Rei had some free time. Looking around the shop, he let out a slightly impressed sound.

He could tell that every piece of tableware displayed for sale inside the shop was of considerable quality.

Rei had almost no artistic eye for aesthetics.

The displayed tableware had simple patterns carved into them, but they weren't extravagant enough to be called ornate.

The tableware sold in this shop was, after all, purely functional.

Metal tableware and wooden tableware were displayed in rows.

While Dostrite read the letter, Rei picked up several pieces of tableware.

"What are you doing?"

By the time Dostrite finished reading the letter, Rei's hands held several plates stacked together.

Deep plates, shallow plates, small plates, large plates, wooden plates, metal plates... many, many plates.

Seeing this, it was only natural that Dostrite asked, looking somewhat taken aback.

"Nothing. I just like eating."

"...So?"

"If I run out of plates while eating, that'd be a problem, wouldn't it?"

In reality, the Misty Ring did have various plates and bowls bought along with food from different cafeterias.

But even the same dish tastes different depending on the vessel it's served in.

To be precise, the taste of the food itself doesn't change, but it's more accurate to say the taste changes depending on your mood while eating.

In that sense, having more plates was always better.

Especially for Rei, who had quite a few opportunities to serve meals to large groups.

Considering what lay ahead, Rei's honest belief was that more tableware was indeed better.

"Well, fine. If you're willing to pay for it properly, I'm all for it. ...So, you wrote that you're going to fight the Earl of Relduct... What do you want me to do?"

"You don't have to do anything."

Dostrite's words were met with Rei's straightforward reply.

"Huh?"

At Rei's words, Dostrite initially didn't understand what he'd been told.

The letter had clearly stated he wanted Rei's help.

Jazz's daughter... Shira, who was like a niece to Dostrite, had been saved from the hands of the Oak.

That's why Dostrite had been thinking he'd do anything he could...

But the response he got back was that he didn't need to do anything.

"What's this supposed to mean? Didn't you come here wanting my help?"

"That was the plan at first. But before coming to this town, I ran into some people. Working with them, I've already taken care of my revenge against Lerdact."

"...Done? You mean you've already attacked Count Lerdact?"

"Yeah. ...Well, I suppose I should tell you. Last night, Lerdact should have been captured by the rebels. News of that should be circulating soon enough."

"Last night? How do you know that?"

"I told you. Because I attacked Lerdact. My assault weakened the defenses at his estate, and the rebels seized the opportunity to act."

"Wait, hold on. That doesn't add up. Jarwa, where Count Lerdact lives, is quite far from here. It's a distance that would take ten days even by carriage. So why do you know about yesterday's events?"

The person who brought the letter from Jaz wasn't lying.

Dostrite wanted to believe that, but he still sensed a clear contradiction in what Rei was saying.

But Rei, seemingly unconcerned by Dostrite's thoughts, opened his mouth.

"That's simple. My speed is beyond what's normally conceivable. ...I'll show you the secret. Come on."

He set down the dishes and led Dostrite to the front of the shop.

...Several dozen seconds later, Dostrite's stunned voice echoed throughout the area. Hearing it, the townspeople, intrigued by the unusual outburst from the normally quiet Dostrite, came to see what was happening.
