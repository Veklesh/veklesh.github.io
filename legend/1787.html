The day after Rei arrived in Anesis, located in the Duchy of Kerebel.

Having met the Duke and Duchess of Kerebel for the first time at dinner the night before and been thoroughly impressed by Georgima’s cooking, he was undoubtedly feeling mentally exhausted.

That said, even if he weren’t in the middle of a mission, Rei was generally not a morning person to begin with.

Furthermore, the bedding—the bed and futon where Rei was sleeping—was in a guest room of the Duke of Kerebel’s estate… and since Elena had brought him there, they had prepared the finest guest room available; as a result, all the furniture, including the bedding, was of the highest quality.

And since it was a winter morning, Rei was fast asleep, sleeping as soundly as could be.

Rei was enjoying this warm slumber when, eventually, the sound of a knock echoed through the room.

The sound came from the door.

Rei reacted slightly to the knock, but showed no sign of waking from the depths of his sleep and continued to enjoy his rest.

However, the person knocking couldn’t just leave Rei be just because she was still asleep, so the sound of knocking echoed once more.

After being knocked on for several dozen seconds, even a morning-hater like Rei would notice the sound.

……Actually, the quickest way to wake Rei instantly would be to emit a slight killing intent… but surely, the guest Elena brought along couldn’t possibly do such a thing.

In the end, the maid who came to wake him up kept knocking for several more minutes...

“Hmm?”

Finally awakened by the knocking, Rei let out a strange sound and looked around.

“Ah… Hmm…?”

At first, perhaps not knowing where he was, he looked around with a puzzled yet sleepy expression.

It was clear this wasn’t the Dusk Wheat inn, where Rei usually stayed, and it was equally clear this wasn’t inside the Magic Tent either.

After looking around for about ten seconds... the sound of knocking coming from the door again finally got his brain working a little.

“Right, I came to Elena’s house, didn’t I?”

Muttering that, he told the person knocking to come in.

The maid who entered was different from the one who had taken care of Rei yesterday.

She appeared to be in her late teens, and it would be no exaggeration to describe her as beautiful.

Upon entering the room, the maid bowed to Rei, who was sitting on the bed.

“Excuse me, Rei. Good morning. Breakfast is ready. May I bring it in here, or would you prefer to eat in the dining room?”

“Uh… What about Elena and the others?”

“As for the young lady, she has already finished breakfast.”

In other words, if she went to the dining hall, there would be no one there… or, if anyone were there, it would likely be Elena’s parents.

With that thought, Rei immediately voiced her decision.

“Alright, I’ll eat here. Please bring it in.”

“Yes. Also, Lady Georgima would like to speak with you, Rei.”

Rei, who wasn’t fully awake yet, didn’t realize at first who Georgima was when the maid mentioned her name.

Still, he figured out who it was within a few seconds—probably because the meal he’d eaten yesterday had been so memorable, and because Georgima herself had such a distinctive personality.

Especially the meat that was served as the main course.

At first glance, it looked like nothing more than simply grilled meat, yet it was an astonishing dish that allowed one to enjoy a variety of flavors with just a single bite.

Beyond that, the several dishes and desserts that followed the meat were flavors that even Rei—who knew the cuisine of Japan, a country that could be called a culinary powerhouse—found thoroughly satisfying.

And as I recalled the meal from last night, realizing that Georgima’s cooking had truly impressed me from the bottom of my heart, I remembered the promise I’d made to teach Rei the dishes I knew—the ones she didn’t know.

“Come to think of it, I did promise that. Yeah, I get it. I’ll get ready first, and after I finish breakfast, I’ll head over to Georgima’s place… Oh, wait a second. Did she ask if I have anything to do today?”

Rei didn’t mind meeting Georgima, but he wondered if perhaps Elena had some business to attend to? Or maybe the Duke and Duchess of Kerebel had something on their agenda? Rei asked, but the maid smiled—a smile tinged with a hint of exasperation—and shook her head.

“Since there was a matter regarding Georgima, they asked you to focus on that today. Elena has an acquaintance coming to visit, so she’ll be entertaining them, and as for the Duke and Duchess, they each have work to do.”

“So, that means I’m free today.”

“Yes, that’s correct.”

The maid replied to Rei’s words.

“Then, I’ll go see Georgima… Oh, come to think of it, do you know how Set is doing?”

“He’s your familiar, isn’t he, Rei? I heard he’s running around playing energetically with Yellow in the stables. He ate the breakfast we prepared here as well, so I don’t think there’s any problem.”

"I see. Well, that’s good."

Rei replied to the maid’s words, but of course he knew that Set was prone to feeling lonely.

I suppose I should go check on him.

With that thought in mind, Rei got himself ready and began to enjoy the breakfast Georgima had prepared.

“Ooh, ooh-ooh-ooh-ooh, ooh!”

When Georgima saw Rei appear in the dining hall, she approached him, letting out a strange cry that could easily be mistaken for a bird’s call.

If someone who didn’t know any better saw this, they would probably think a villain was bullying a child.

After all, Rei had to force himself to hold back the reflexive urge to throw a punch at Georgima as she closed in on him.

“Georgima, calm down! Come on, this is a rare opportunity—if he gets away now, it’ll all be for nothing!”

“Everyone, help me! Stop Georgima!”

There were over ten cooks in the kitchen, but Georgima dragged several of them along as she closed in on Rei.

“Run! You there, run away!”

“Ah, yeah, yeah. It’s fine, don’t worry.”

The chef desperately called out to Rei, but Rei, hearing him, reached out his hand as if to say it was fine… and the moment Georgima saw that, her movements froze instantly.

It wasn’t as if Rei had used any magic or skills, but even so, as a chef whose skills surpassed even the highest caliber, Georgima must have sensed something from him.

However, the other cooks who had been trying to stop Georgima had absolutely no idea how things had come to this.

They knew Georgima had a strong passion for cooking... which is precisely why they never expected him to stop just because Rei reached out.

“You’ve calmed down, haven’t you?”

“……Yeah. So, since you came here, does that mean you’ll teach me how to cook?”

At those words from Georgima, the other chefs turned to Rei with looks of astonishment.

They never expected to hear Georgima say she wanted to be taught how to cook.

Well, if the person in front of her had looked like a cook, things might have been different… but today, Rei was wearing the Dragon Robe, and she certainly didn’t look like someone who knew much about cooking.

Georgima must have noticed the reactions around him. With a hint of exasperation, he spoke up.

“This man is the guest Elena brought along—the guy who invented dishes like udon, meat buns, pizza, and seafood okonomiyaki.”

“What!?”

At Georgima’s words, the chefs who heard them were stunned and turned their gaze toward Rei in unison.

Since Rei was wearing the Dragon Robe as usual today, to some observers, he looked nothing more than a novice mage.

Given the size of the Duchy of Kerebel, there were a fair number of mages among its forces, and the cooks initially assumed Rei was one of them.

But once they realized the person standing before them was Rei, their reaction naturally changed.

The fact that Rei knew a dish even Georgima didn’t know was something that even the cooks found worthy of respect.

That is why the cooks present eventually shifted their gaze from astonishment to respect.

And Rei, now the object of their respectful gazes, found himself at a loss.

And naturally so. From Rei’s perspective, he hadn’t taught them the detailed recipes.

All Rei could do was share what he vaguely remembered.

That said, knowing what the finished dish looks like is undoubtedly quite beneficial in this situation.

It was precisely because Rei knew the finished product that dishes like udon and others became meals that could be properly enjoyed without taking too much time.

“So, since you came here like this, does that mean you’re going to teach me how to cook?”

“You’re the one who told me to come here, right? …Well, I guess I don’t mind teaching you how to cook.”

The moment Rei said that, Georgima’s gaze undoubtedly sharpened.

Given his insatiable appetite for food, he was clearly implying that he had no intention of letting this opportunity slip away.

“So, what kind of dish are you going to teach me?”

“It’s a dish called ramen. …You know what udon is, right?”

“Yeah, of course. That texture was a bit of a shock. It’s easy to eat, too.”

“It’s a dish similar to udon. You need three things: noodles made from wheat flour, soup, and toppings. To be precise, there are also condiments and such, but that part depends on personal preference.”

At the mention of it being similar to udon, Georgima looked slightly disappointed, but he must have understood that since the name of the dish is different, it’s a different dish from udon.

Georgima spoke with a serious expression.

“You said it’s different from udon, but how exactly is it different?”

“As far as I know, it seems to be a dish that originated in a different country altogether.”

Rei said this, but of course, he didn’t know where udon actually originated.

Since ramen is a dish that the Japanese adapted from Chinese cuisine, its roots can be traced back to China.

As for udon, however, while he knew of Inaniwa udon and Sanuki udon, that was the extent of his knowledge.

Then again, I didn’t think Georgima would be interested in that sort of thing.

“So, specifically, how should I cook it?”

“Unlike udon, ramen is a dish with several different types of broth that form the basis of its flavor. However… it seems you need seasonings like soy sauce and miso to make it. Do you know what those are?”

At Rei’s words, everyone—including Georgima—shook their heads.

(It seems there have been a few people in the past who were reincarnated or transferred from Japan—including Takumu Suzunose, who was part of Zepair’s group—but soy sauce and miso don’t seem to have caught on. …I was sure there might be some, and I just hadn’t found them yet. So, did they really not know how to make them?)

Maybe there were people who did try to introduce soy sauce and miso.

But perhaps the recipe was too difficult, or maybe it simply didn’t suit the palates of the people in this world—or the place where whoever made it came from.

In any case, the fact that soy sauce and miso haven’t caught on is undoubtedly a major setback when it comes to making ramen.

(Boiled eggs and chashu pork must have used soy sauce too... Well, I suppose I could substitute those with other seasonings to some extent?)

Thinking this, Rei spoke to Georgima, who was wearing an expression so grim it wouldn’t be an exaggeration to say he looked like he was on the verge of death.

“Don’t worry. There are soups you can make even without seasonings like soy sauce or miso. That said, the only kinds I know are salt-based and pork bone broth. …Was there a beef bone one too?”

He muttered the last part about beef bones under his breath so Georgima wouldn’t hear.

Rei had seen something called beef bone ramen on a TV show or something, but as expected, pork bone ramen was more famous and left a stronger impression.

“Also, while it’s a bit different from ramen, there’s a dish called cold Chinese noodles. …Well, the question is what to do about the sauce.”

When Rei heard “chilled Chinese noodles,” two types of sauce came to mind: one based on sour soy sauce and the other on sesame.

That said, one of them is difficult because it doesn’t use soy sauce, and I have absolutely no idea how to make the sesame-based one.

“Salt and pork bone… pork bones? Would oak bones work too? And this ‘chilled Chinese noodles’… what kind of dish is that? Judging by the name, it sounds like a cold dish.”

“I wonder about using oak bones…”

Since oak meat is several times more delicious than regular pork, perhaps using oak bones in tonkotsu ramen would taste better too... Unfortunately, Rei couldn’t figure out how that would work and ended up hedging his words.
