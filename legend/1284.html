"Hmph!"

With that familiar voice, Byune swung her pure white blade.

The dagger, forged by Pamidor from the silver lion's materials, had a blade clearly longer than the dagger Byune had used until now.

And to top it off, the speed at which she wielded the dagger was slightly faster than before.

The combat training she'd undergone recently had also contributed to Byune's increased fighting ability.

But more than that, the weight of the dagger itself was noticeably lighter than her previous one.

Despite this, the blade's sharpness was clearly enhanced compared to before. For Byune, who excelled in combat with daggers, this pure white dagger was precisely the weapon that would elevate her combat power by one... or even two levels.

Of course, she understood that her own skill hadn't yet caught up to the weapon.

That's precisely why she trains relentlessly like this—to master the weapon, not be mastered by it.

"Too easy,"

As Vihera's voice echoed, Byune's strike sliced through the air, sending her tumbling onto the snowy ground.

Realizing she'd been tripped, Byune scrambled back to her feet.

While she wielded a newly acquired dagger with a pure white blade, Vihera used no magic items like gauntlets or greaves.

Even so, Byune felt bitter frustration at being completely outmatched by Vihera.

As usual, her face showed no sign of it, but feeling that frustration was only natural for Byune, who was surprisingly competitive.

Cheers of encouragement for Byune echoed from the hallway facing the backyard and the courtyard, part of the now semi-regular combat training routine.

Whether spurred by those voices or simply refusing to lose at this level, Byune firmly planted his feet on the fallen snow.

Gripping the pure white blade once more, she faced Vihera.

"Hmph!"

Vihera smiled at Byune's declaration of "One more time"... a smile so captivatingly alluring it could ensnare anyone who saw it.

While Vihera believed she couldn't match Marina in feminine allure, the smile she wore when reveling in combat possessed a seductive intensity that could be called terrifying—one that captivated all who saw it, regardless of gender.

Indeed, those who had been cheering for Byune moments before were now mesmerized by Vihera's smile, their voices of support fading away.

Completely unfazed by the distracted crowd, Vihera beckoned Byune forward with a raised index finger.

Provoked by this attitude, Byune's fighting spirit flared, and she lunged forward.

Still, she knew charging straight in would be repeating her earlier mistake. Instead, Byune fired off several long needles as a warning shot.

Among the needles were transparent ones designed to be hard to see, but Vihera dodged them all.

Combined with the clothes he wore, his movements were a dance that wouldn't lose to any dancer around.

"Whoa!"

Rei, who had been leaning against Set lying on the snow, let out a startled cry upon seeing a flying long needle pierce the snow near him.

He was completely leaning on Set and couldn't move, so it couldn't be helped.

But Vihera and Byune paid no heed whatsoever to Rei's cry, continuing their combat training.

Closing the distance while firing long needles, Byune focused on the ground beneath her feet, determined not to repeat her earlier mistake, as she attacked Vihera.

But that very movement introduced awkwardness into Byune's natural motions, ultimately slowing her overall pace.

"Well? What's wrong? You were sharper just now. You're not committing fully."

With a smile, Vihera evaded Byune's strike and gently placed her extended hand against Byune's torso.

Had Vihera used her signature technique, the Demon-Infused Palm, that alone would have been a fatal strike.

Though Byune also wore leather armor, the Demon-Infused Palm was a vicious technique that delivered a direct shock to the opponent's body, even through metal armor.

This being a mock battle, Vihera refrained from using the Immersion Palm, merely making contact.

Thanks to that, Byune avoided taking the full force of the strike. Still, knowing the true power of the Immersion Palm, Byune froze for an instant.

"Come on, come on, try a little harder."

With a smile on her face, Vihera watched as Byune renewed her attack.

This time, however, she didn't charge head-on. Instead, she circled around to Vihera's side.

The long needle flew once more.

This time, instead of dancing away, she deflected the long needle flying toward her with her gauntlet.

It was an action only possible because she could clearly see the needle flying straight at her.

"Hmph!"

But Byune must have understood that too. Showing no particular surprise, he throws the long needles while moving in a circular pattern, keeping Vihera at the center.

Vihera deflected the needles using both gauntlets.

"What's the matter? An opponent this strong will see through such attacks right away, you know?"

Though she likely wasn't provoked by the taunt, Byune, who had been circling around Vihera, suddenly closed the distance.

The numerous footprints in the snow showed no sign of slipping.

That alone spoke volumes about the skill of Byune's footwork.

But... even so, her opponent was Vihera.

Even with superior footwork, no one could stand toe-to-toe with Byune and hope to prevail.

Even so... even so, he thought.

Though expressionless, Byune was fiercely competitive. Pouring every ounce of her being into it, she swung her dagger.

The pure white blade lunged toward Vihera, blending into the snow like a ghost.

Normally, the surrounding snow's whiteness would dazzle the eyes, causing even a momentary loss of focus on the blade's location.

But this was Vihera, who loved battle.

He wasn't confused by such a move. He saw the strike coming straight for him and dodged it.

Normally, she wouldn't dodge an attack of this caliber, instead parrying it with her gauntlets... but the dagger Byune wielded was crafted from materials of the Silver Lion, a rank S monster.

While Vihera had no intention of conceding to Byune in terms of skill, the dagger Byune wielded was an exceptionally high-performance weapon. If she wasn't careful, there was even a risk her gauntlets could be sliced apart.

(Though, if blocking is no good, I can always dodge.)

As she judged where the dagger's blade would strike, moving her body, Vihera thought and a smile touched her lips.

That smile wasn't one of relishing the fight... but rather one of joy at seeing her younger sister become this capable in battle.

Still, as her surrogate sister, she couldn't afford to lose to Byune.

She dodged the swing, watching the white blade slice through the air where Vihera had been a moment before, then reached out her hand.

Her touch was fleeting. She skillfully harnessed the force of Byune's downward strike, simultaneously redirecting it toward her intended target.

"Huh!?"

It was Byune who let out a cry of astonishment.

She had swung her dagger at Vihera, and the moment she thought her attack had been dodged, her own body was floating in the air. Her surprise was only natural.

Though Byune remained expressionless, the moment she realized she'd been thrown, shock flashed across her face.

Even so, she spun her body mid-air. When she landed on the snow, she managed to land on her feet and the hand not holding the dagger, rather than on her back...

"Yes, it's over."

Before he knew it, claws extending from Vihera's gauntlet were thrust right before his eyes. The battle was already over.

"...Hm?"

Byune looked disappointed, but Vihera pulled her up by the hand.

"Overall, I think you did pretty well, don't you? Your dagger work wasn't too bad either. But I suppose you're not fully accustomed to it yet. Your reactions were a bit slow."

"Hmm."

Byune seemed aware of it too. Hearing Vihera's words, she nodded regretfully.

"There were some close calls, what a pity. ...But, Byune. If you intend to keep using that weapon, make sure it doesn't get taken from you. During combat, and in your everyday life too."

At Vihera's admonishing words, Byune nodded understandingly, sheathing the pure white blade and clutching it protectively.

Anyone who had seen the weapon, even briefly, would undoubtedly covet that beautiful dagger with its pure white blade—a piece so exquisite it could easily be called a work of art.

Those who approached her through negotiation to buy it were one thing, but there were undoubtedly others who, seeing Byune's appearance, would simply decide to take it by force.

Once word spread that she'd been partying with Rei since spring, fewer fools would entertain such thoughts. But that only applied to those who knew Rei... and Marina, Vihera, and the others were part of Byune's party.

At this point, it's safe to say that no one currently living in Gilm would target Byune, who's traveling with Rei.

But spring... Yes, come spring, many adventurers arrive in Gilm every year.

Many adventurers are rough and tumble.

And seeing people like Rei, Marina, and Vihera, some inevitably think: We're stronger than them. The rumors are exaggerated. It's all thanks to the griffin's abilities.

It's only natural that some think that way.

What happens if such people set their sights on Byune?

Even though she was traveling with Rei and the others, Byune didn't possess the kind of isolated, exceptional abilities that the others did.

She'd been training desperately for the spring operation, but her limits were undeniable.

In other words, what if she, the weakest among Rei and the others, wielded a weapon crafted from the materials of a rank S monster, the Silver Lion... a weapon forged by the master craftsman Pamidor, a work of art in its own right?

The answer was obvious.

Most adventurers drawn to Gilm were confident in their own skills.

If such individuals attacked Byune, there was no way she could possibly handle them.

Therefore, as Vihera said, they needed to be careful not to lose their daggers.

"Hmph!"

Byune understood that too. She nodded in response to Vihera's words.

"Yes, it's good that you understand. ...Well then, shall we stop the combat training for now?"

"Mm."

Though she seemed a little disappointed by Vihera's words, Byune nodded.

Having spent a considerable amount of time training here, Byune's stamina was significantly depleted.

She likely understood that pushing herself to continue training now would only lead to injury.

From an age even younger than now, Byune had ventured into dungeons alone.

Because of that, she understood her own physical condition perfectly well.

Had she been unable to judge that, it was highly likely Byune wouldn't be alive today.

"You're exhausted. ...Here, drink this and ease your fatigue."

Rei, leaning against Set, handed Vihera and Byune a bottle of ice-cold fruit water.

Taken from the item box, it was a most welcome refreshment for the two who had just been exerting themselves.

...Though, if coldness was the issue, snow could provide as much as needed.

"Oh, thank you. You're quite thoughtful."

"Mm."

The two took the fruit water to their mouths and savored it thoroughly.

Even in winter, with snow piled all around, moving your body naturally makes you sweat and thirsty.

Their parched throats were quenched by the fruit water.

"...Speaking of which, it'll be New Year's soon."

"Oh? What's gotten into you all of a sudden?"

"Nothing, I just thought of it."

Vihera, who had been drinking the fruit water, tilted her head at Rei's sudden words.

"Since we're here, why not throw a party?"

"Well, even if you say that... We just had a party the other day. Having another one so soon..."

"It doesn't have to be anything big. Just me, Vihera, Byune, and Marina. Something to deepen the bonds among us who'll be forming a party starting this spring."

"...Oh, that sounds nice. Especially the part about deepening our bonds."

Whatever Rei's words made her imagine, Vihera readily changed her mind with a smile.

With Elena, their biggest rival, absent, they could monopolize Rei now... That must have been her thought.

"Mm."

"Grrrr."

Byune and Set, one person and one beast, agreed purely because a party meant delicious food.

And so, the three people and one beast decided to propose a year-end party to Marina.
