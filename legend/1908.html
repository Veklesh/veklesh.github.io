Hearing the scream, Rei and Set immediately headed toward the source of the sound.

To be honest, there weren’t any adventurers in this Gilm who would scream when facing a kobold… well, that wasn’t entirely true, but Rei understood that such cases were still rare. That was precisely why he started running toward the source of the scream.

In other words, it meant that something highly unusual—something beyond the ordinary—was happening, and that anomaly might be related to a magic item.

It was partly Rei’s wishful thinking—or perhaps even wishful speculation—but even so, it was better than searching the construction site with no clues at all.

And so Rei arrived...

“What is this?”

He muttered disappointedly at the scene spread out before him.

Even so, since he’d seen it, he felt it would leave a bad taste in his mouth to just walk away, so he drew his spear from the Misty Ring.

Not the Twilight Spear she always used, but the battered spear she’d often used before acquiring that one.

He acted on the belief that it would be a waste to use the Twilight Spear against a kobold.

After a few steps of a running start, he hurled the battered spear.

The spear flew straight through the air, shattering the head of the kobold that was about to bite the female adventurer. Perhaps slightly altered by the impact, it then pierced the chest of the kobold behind her and finally came to a stop after impaling the torso of the kobold behind that one.

“……Huh?”

The female adventurer, who had been in a life-or-death situation, let out a bewildered cry, clearly unsure of what had just happened.

And no wonder. The head of the kobold that had been right in front of her had just exploded, splattering her face with blood, bones, brain matter, and bodily fluids.

Unaware that a kobold’s eyeball had smashed against her cheek, the woman looked around.

What caught her eye was a male adventurer riding a griffin.

As a resident of Gilm, she naturally knew who he was.

「Crimson？」

He must have heard the whisper escape her lips. Rei—who had killed the three kobolds attacking her with a single blow (though, to be precise, the last one was still alive, but in a dying state, so it was certain it wouldn’t last much longer)—approached the woman alongside Set.

“...Sorry about that. I helped her on the spur of the moment, but I didn’t think it would turn out like this.”

Saying that, Rei handed the woman a cloth he’d taken from the Misty Ring.

She took it… and only then did she realize that the kobold’s blood and other substances were caked all over her face—no, her entire body.

She casually touched her cheek, found the soft, warm sensation suspicious, and brought her hand up to her eyes.

There, she found an eyeball that had been crushed after being flung against her cheek by the force of the explosion.

“Eeek… Eeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeeee

The moment she realized what it was, a shrill scream erupted from her mouth and echoed throughout the area.

That scream was undoubtedly louder than the one Rei had heard earlier.

Rei wondered if it was strange that the scream she let out upon seeing the kobold’s crushed eyeball was louder than the one she’d let out when she’d nearly been killed, but for now, he focused on checking the condition of the woman in front of him.

Age-wise, she was about the same as Rei. …In other words, she was probably in her mid-teens.

But… there was something else that caught Rei’s eye.

(Her equipment doesn’t fit? Plus, it’s quite old and hasn’t been properly maintained.)

Indeed, the woman’s armor was clearly too big for her body, and the longsword lying on the ground had rusty spots.

For a moment, he wondered if it was the weapon the kobold had been holding, but since he’d checked that area carefully when throwing the spear, that possibility was out of the question.

(I thought she might be an adventurer, but she isn’t? Or maybe she just became one? If that’s the case, I can understand why she’s struggling against a monster like a kobold.)

However, Rei also knew that, generally speaking, newly minted adventurers could only accept quests within the city.

While this construction site is undoubtedly within the city limits, Rei still couldn’t imagine the guild entrusting a brand-new adventurer with the task of exterminating kobolds—and not just one or two, but a massive horde of them.

“Are you… really an adventurer?”

Once the woman’s screams had subsided, Rei asked her that.

It wasn’t a subtle probe—it was a direct, straightforward question—but upon hearing it, the woman’s body tensed for a moment.

Just seeing that, Rei became firmly convinced that the woman was not an adventurer.

“Uh… well… um…”

The woman looked unsure of what to say.

Judging by her movements, she certainly didn’t look like someone who had trained her body or undergone combat training.

Even among adventurers, not everyone undergoes combat training.

Still, it seemed unnatural in many ways that she hadn’t undergone combat training, given that she had accepted a quest to exterminate kobolds—a quest that inherently involved combat.

“Who are you? Well, for starters, I’m pretty sure you’re not an adventurer…”

“I-I’m an adventurer! Otherwise, I wouldn’t be coming to a place like this dressed like this, would I?!”

“Then show me your guild card. You must have one if you’re an adventurer, right?”

Saying that, Rei pulled her guild card out of her Misty Ring—a card displaying “Rank B” with her name clearly written on it.

Even as she did so, she wondered if the woman in front of her might actually have a guild card.

Since guild cards don’t have photos attached like driver’s licenses or passports, even if someone were to hold up another person’s guild card and insist it was theirs, Rei wouldn’t be able to tell if it was genuine.

At a guild or guard post, there are magical items that can verify a person’s identity, though.

In any case, for that reason, Rei wondered if the woman in front of him might have obtained someone else’s guild card by some means and was about to produce it…

“Um, I kind of lost it…”

A blatant cover-up.

If she had actually shown her Guild Card, Rei would have been in a bind, so in that sense, he could say he was saved.

“Then, shall we go to the guild? It’ll cost a bit, but they can reissue your guild card.”

“I’m broke. Otherwise, there’s no way I’d go out of my way to take on a job like this, right?”

“That’s a lie.”

Rei flatly dismissed the woman’s words.

She must have realized herself that her behavior had been suspicious.

At Rei’s assertion, the woman was at a loss for words.

“So? What are you, exactly? I can tell from your gear and the way you move that you’re not an adventurer… so why are you pulling this stunt?”

“Well…”

The woman tries to think of a way to cover it up, but seeing the look in Rei’s eyes, she decides it’s pointless to say anything more and exhales in resignation.

“I’m Su-chi. …I’m from the slums.”

A person from the slums.

Hearing this, Rei didn’t look surprised—rather, he seemed to understand—and looked at the woman who had introduced herself as Suu-chi once more.

Perhaps to appear like an adventurer, her face had been thoroughly wiped clean of any dirt.

Her features weren’t quite as stunning as Elena’s (the woman’s), but they were reasonably well-defined.

If you asked ten people, it wouldn’t be strange if about half of them called her cute.

(She’d be perfectly capable of making a living as a prostitute with looks like these… but judging by her demeanor, I don’t think she has any interest in that.)

With that thought in mind, Rei spoke again.

“Normally, the corpses of kobolds slain by adventurers here are collected by the slum dwellers, who strip them for materials or eat the meat. In other words, she doesn’t need to pretend to be an adventurer and kill kobolds herself—so why is she doing this?”

It was a genuine question.

As Rei had said, the corpses of kobolds slain by adventurers are generally left here after the proof-of-kill parts and magic stones have been stripped from them.

It’s not that no one ever strips the materials, but spending time on that is less profitable than killing as many kobolds as possible to secure proof-of-extermination parts and magic stones—which ultimately yields more money.

For that reason, it was understood that the slum dwellers would collect the kobold corpses to strip them for materials or use the meat for food.

Naturally, the residents of the slums have to come and collect the corpses themselves, so when they do—even if it’s at night—there is a risk of being attacked by kobolds, but that’s really the extent of the danger.

At the very least, they should be able to make a decent profit without having to go to the trouble of defeating the kobolds themselves, as Suu-chi does, and they can even eat the meat—which, while not great, isn’t as bad as goblin meat.

So why go out of your way to do something so dangerous?

If Suu-chi is capable of fighting to some extent, I can’t say I don’t understand the reason for doing something like this.

“Why? …It’s obvious—because we need money, isn’t it?”

“Isn’t just collecting kobold corpses enough?”

“Exactly. We have a bunch of little ones at home, so we need as much food as we can get.”

Even a single kobold weighs around fifty kilograms… and some individuals are even bigger.

You have to remove the innards and bones, so not all 50 kg is edible, but it’s still a substantial amount… or so Rei estimated.

(And yet, it’s not enough? Just how many kids does she have? Well, I guess since we don’t know how long these kobold attacks will last, she might be trying to preserve as much food as possible by making jerky or something.)

Thinking this, Rei looked at the three kobold corpses lying on the ground.

The first one’s head had been blown to pieces, so there was no way to retrieve the proof-of-kill part.

The kobold standing a short distance away, pierced through the chest, must have had its magic stone destroyed along with it.

Only the last kobold, who had been impaled through the torso and was mortally wounded, still had both its proof-of-kill parts and magic stone intact.

Considering that he had been fighting the kobolds to make money, it’s true that this way of defeating them was problematic in many ways.

That said, considering Suu-chi was in mortal danger, she was lucky that Rei was nearby to save her.

“...Well, I get the gist of it. So, since the kobold corpses slain by adventurers don’t have proof-of-kill parts or magic stones, you wanted those, right?”

“To be precise, it’s the magic stones. The proof-of-extermination parts can only be exchanged for money at the guild, after all.”

“Ah… I see. Now that you mention it, that’s right.”

Basically, Rei sells materials and magic stones at the guild.

Since he cashes in the proof-of-kill parts at the same time, he’d just assumed that was the norm. But while proof-of-kill parts are one thing, magic stones and materials can be bought by places other than the guild.

In fact, depending on your bargaining skills, you can get a higher price than selling them to the guild.

...But conversely, if you’re a poor negotiator, you’ll end up getting paid less than if you sold them to the guild.

You have to carefully check the condition of the materials, negotiate over them, and handle various other negotiations… Since Rei found all that a hassle, she sold her materials and magic stones at the guild, even if she got a slightly lower price than she would have at those other shops.

Even if the price was a bit lower, the fact that Rei could defeat a large number of monsters meant he wasn’t dissatisfied with the purchase price.

Seeing Rei like this, Suu-chi seemed to understand how he usually handled the monsters he defeated, and she wore a look of dissatisfaction.

“I suppose someone with a nickname like you wouldn’t understand the hardships involved.”

“You’re right. I won’t deny that.”

In fact, Rei had skipped over the hardships that adventurers typically endure during their early days.

The fact that Set existed played a big role, and it also helped that an orc settlement had formed near Gilm not long after he started working there, and Rei defeated the Orc King there.

Furthermore, as a result of that, he began to be looked upon favorably by those in authority, which is how he was able to meet Elena.

When you think about it that way, it’s only natural that Suu-chi envies Rei.

Of course, it’s also true that becoming famous brings its own share of troubles.

Just like when they went to raid the orc settlement, there were adventurers who attacked them specifically to get their hands on Rei’s Misty Ring.

“Hmph… Anyway! I need to defeat kobolds and make some money! I don’t have the time to deal with you anymore! Bye!”

As Suu-chi shouted that and turned to leave, Rei called out to her.

“Don’t you want that kobold’s corpse?”

“I didn’t kill it. You helped me out, so I can’t just take it from you, can I?”

“...Then, here’s a trade. I’m gathering some intel right now. I want information on that.”

At Rei’s words, Su-chi stopped in her tracks, though a look of suspicion crossed her face.
