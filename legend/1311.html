The report reached the guardhouse while the guards were searching for Rei.

One of the guards—a man skilled enough to subdue most adventurers—ran into the station, gasping for breath.

"Haa, haa, haa, haa... G-get... get people together!"

"What's wrong!?"

True to his role as a guard in Gilm, he immediately asked upon seeing his colleague suddenly enter.

Had the intruder not been wearing a guard's uniform, they would have assumed an attack and drawn their weapons.

By the very nature of their job as guards, they often incurred grudges from those they apprehended.

And now, with Azimov under attack, the guards were naturally on high alert.

"It's Rei. Apparently Rei charged through the Noble District with Set and a few others."

The moment the name Rei was mentioned, every guard listening to the story grimaced.

They had heard the story from Marina, taken Azimov in... and were changing his clothes at the clinic. Even so, seeing the clothes Azimov had been wearing made it easy to understand just how severe his injuries were.

A friend—whether Rei would admit it or not—had sustained injuries that severe and was now unconscious.

The guards had been desperately searching for him too, but they couldn't find Rei, who was searching for the person who attacked his friend.

If it had just been Rei, that might have been one thing, but Set was missing too. If they were anywhere, they should have been found quickly, but they were proving difficult to locate.

Of course, while Gilm was classified as a town, its population had grown year after year, making its actual size indistinguishable from a city.

...So much so that Gilm's lord, Daskar, was wringing his hands over the fact that Gilm would need to be expanded soon if things continued like this.

Gilm was originally a city built by the Kingdom of Mireana, pouring immense national resources into it, gathering vast numbers of soldiers and slaves, and suffering significant losses throughout its construction.

While its initial construction relied on the nation's strength, expanding Gilm now falls solely to the lord's own efforts.

It was only natural. The lord of this land was Daskar, and this was Daskar's domain.

Just how much effort and funds would it require to undertake such a task in this remote frontier?

Of course, if he sought aid from other nobles, they would lend a hand without hesitation.

And naturally, they would demand various privileges within Gilm in return.

The fact that the Mireana Kingdom has only one frontier region demonstrates the value placed upon it.

That is precisely why the House of Larks, the Marquis of the Frontier, with only the city of Gilm, can remain the central figure of the Neutral Faction, following the Royal Faction and the Noble Faction.

But Daskar has no intention of allowing other nobles to gain influence in Gilm.

Not even to members of his own faction.

...Which is precisely why he's troubled about how to expand Gilm.

In any case, given Gilm's size, even if the guards searched for Rei, they wouldn't find him easily... And just as he was troubled by this, the guard who arrived brought the report he now held.

"No way! The Noble Quarter!?"

One guard exclaimed in disbelief, and the others shared his shock.

Of all places, the Noble District...

The Noble District, as the name implies, is naturally a district where many nobles reside.

Though technically, it might not be called the Noble District since people like Azoth Trading Company or Marina, the former guildmaster, who aren't actually nobles, also have residences there.

But... come to think of it, there was definitely a possibility nobles would cause trouble for Rei.

Just as new adventurers had joined the Guild this spring, a fair number of nobles had recently arrived at the Guild as well.

Such individuals naturally hadn't seen Rei with their own eyes, only heard about him through stories.

It wouldn't be strange to wonder if someone like that had caused trouble for Rei.

"What about the damage in the Noble District?"

One of the guards asked the person who brought the news.

The others listened to the information while preparing themselves to deploy at a moment's notice.

The enemy is Rei.

Even wearing armor with somewhat higher defense would be nearly meaningless against Rei's attack power.

Therefore, choosing equipment that allowed for easy movement was the best choice.

"No casualties have been reported so far. According to the adventurer who brought the report, it ignored the adventurers trying to stop it and just kept moving toward some single point."

"...It's Set."

One of the guards muttered while preparing for deployment.

We'd heard from Marina that Set was tracking a scent.

They didn't know exactly how sharp Set's sense of smell was, but given that he was a high-rank monster, a Griffin, it wasn't unreasonable to assume his sense of smell was vastly superior to that of humans.

"To contact Marina, all I had to do was crush this water, right?"

Hearing this, the guard who had been briefed by Marina nodded in response.

"Yeah. I don't want to deal with an enraged Rei, so call Marina immediately. She'll be able to handle Rei somehow, right?"

It was more wishful thinking than certainty, but it made perfect sense since the guards stood no chance against Rei in a fair fight.

That was precisely why, in this situation, it was undoubtedly better to act alongside Marina, who likely held strong influence over Rei, rather than trying to handle it themselves.

The water bottle was crushed in his fist. The guards prepared to face Rei while waiting for Marina to return to the guardhouse.

"The carriage is ready!"

The guard who had been preparing transportation to the Noble District shouted from inside the guardhouse.

The distance from here to the Noble District was considerable.

This time, they had to reach the other side as quickly as possible. To avoid running and wasting energy, they had prepared the carriage.

"Damn it, if only Captain Ranga were here. He'd handle Rei like it was nothing."

One of the guards muttered, but no one else voiced a complaint.

Because it was what every guard was thinking.

But Ranga wasn't in Gilm right now.

He was on a mission to Abuero, ordered by Daskar.

Honestly, most guards probably thought, "Of all times, why now...?"

Still, they couldn't just lament Ranga's absence and do nothing more.

That's precisely why they were doing what they could right now.

"Forget about the Noble District for now. The patrols covering other areas should stay as they are, right?"

"Yeah. Rei's case is important, but we can't rule out trouble breaking out in the city itself either."

This was one of the tough aspects of being a guard.

As long as they had the job of maintaining Gilm's security, they couldn't just take the guards patrolling other areas with them.

...Though, to be honest, the main reason was that it would take too long to contact and gather all the guards scattered throughout Gilm.

By the time the guards were ready...

"Am I a little late?"

Marina appeared at the guard post.

"No, it's fine. We were just about to leave."

"Right, the carriage out front?"

"Yes."

After exchanging a few brief words, the most capable guards currently available boarded the carriage.

Of course, not all the guards deployed could fit in the carriage.

Only the most skilled guards boarded the carriage; the rest would run toward the noble district.

Then, with several guards also taking their places on the coach box, the carriage set off.

"So, where was Rei in the end?"

Marina murmured inside the carriage.

Since the reason for using the carriage was to transport as many guards as possible, it was packed to near capacity... no, beyond capacity.

In such a situation, it was inevitable that some guards would end up pressed close to Marina. Those men, feeling the softness of her body and her scent up close, found themselves completely frozen stiff, even though they understood perfectly well what the current situation demanded.

The guard conversing with Marina, seeing his comrade like this, felt a complex mix of envy and pity before speaking.

"The Noble District."

"...I see. The Noble District, huh?"

Given that she owned a mansion here, Marina naturally knew the Noble District quite well.

And having been Guildmaster until today, she knew exactly what kind of commotion those arriving in Gilm come spring would cause.

There was also the information from the person she had been talking to just before being summoned by the guard's spirit magic-created water.

But... there were still things she couldn't quite reconcile.

"While I was searching for Rei, a strange person approached me."

"A strange person, you say?"

The guard tilted his head at Marina's abrupt words.

The frontier town of Gilm attracted all sorts of people.

Among them, it was hardly surprising that many could be called strange.

After all, even Azimov, the victim in this case, was seen as a strange person by those around him.

"Yes. How should I put it... He's the kind of person who looks like the very embodiment of the words 'ordinary' or 'average'. Average height, average features... that sort of person."

"Well now..."

The guard faltered slightly.

Being ordinary meant they wouldn't look out of place in most places.

In the city streets, they'd be the perfect candidate to blend into the crowd.

Precisely because he was a guard, he understood just how dangerous such a person could be.

The other guards listening nearby also tightened their expressions.

"Anyway, that person brought me a message. This time, they're trying to pit Rei against the Azoth Trading Company."

"...I see. That explains the Noble Quarter."

It was common knowledge that Galahad, head of the Azoth Trading Company, had his residence in the Noble Quarter.

The guards understood why Rei had headed for the Noble Quarter.

"But Marina, if they're trying to pit them against each other, does that mean Azoth Trading Company isn't behind this?"

"At least, that's what the person who contacted me said. Of course, I can't be certain if it's true. ...I can't imagine Galahad himself doing something to antagonize Rei, but that doesn't mean everyone in the Azoth Trading Company is like that."

"Yes, that's true. Honestly, if we start suspecting the entire Azoth Trading Company, it could cause quite a stir. Frankly, I hope that's not the case."

Recalling the incident between Rei and the Azoth Trading Company several years prior, the guard murmured.

In the end, that incident resulted in the capture of an alchemist dispatched from the Bestir Empire, which in turn led to a slight advancement in the alchemy of the Mireana Kingdom.

But in direct proportion to that, the guards found themselves busier than ever.

It was true that as guards, they had to handle disturbances when they arose, but that didn't mean they wanted to willingly live busy lives.

"Heh heh, that's true. Things got pretty hectic for Gilm back then too."

Marina smiled as she recalled those days.

Of course, it wasn't just the guards who were busy back then. The guild itself also went through a very busy period.

As she reminisced about those events from several years prior, the carriage soon entered the Noble District.

Being a guard's carriage, no one dared to question it.

Rather, upon learning that Rei had charged into the noble district, the adventurers were busy relaying the information to their employers and such, leaving them no time for such concerns.

"It'll take a little while longer to reach the mansion where the head of the Azoth Trading Company resides... but are you sure you'll be alright?"

The guard's concern was likely whether Galahad's mansion would still be standing by the time they arrived.

"Rei has sharp instincts, and he probably doesn't think the Azott Trading Company is hostile towards us, so I don't think it'll be a problem... At the very least, they won't attack without warning. They should investigate whether the Azott Trading Company is truly involved in this matter."

Marina's words proved correct... When they arrived at the intended mansion, what Marina and the guard saw was Rei standing before the main gate with Galahad, explaining the situation to the guards, other noblemen's private soldiers, and adventurers.
