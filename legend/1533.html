I was introduced by Unchu.

Hearing the man say this, Marina swiftly used spirit magic to ensure their conversation wouldn't be overheard.

...And yet, the fact that she could use the wind spirit's power to make Rei and the others' conversation sound like a different topic entirely was proof of Marina's overwhelmingly superior skill in spirit magic.

Seeing Marina nod, Rei spoke up.

"From Unchu... so it's about the Resistance?"

"Yes. My name is Nokor, and I'm assisting the Resistance in Rossi."

"Assisting? So you're not actually a member of the Resistance?"

Nokor nodded in response to Rei's question.

"Yes. To be precise, I am not a member of the Resistance. ...Though I am always ready to join them, you understand."

Nokoru seemed genuinely disappointed, a hint of sadness visible in his expression.

"Something happen? You seem to have a story."

"...Being a Resistance collaborator alone is reason enough to have a complicated past, I'd say."

Vihera murmured in response to Marina's words.

As if hearing Vihera's words, Nokor shifted his mood and continued speaking.

"Actually, not long ago, one of the Resistance's bases was attacked by Jaya's men. As a result, the Resistance's fighting strength was greatly reduced. That broke the spirit of the Resistance's upper echelons... Now, it's mainly the younger members who are active."

Hearing that, Nokor's mood seemed to shift.

Though it was a mood akin to having bitten into a bitter insect.

Indeed, the combat strength the Resistance had lost was immense.

The attack targeted a location where the core members of the Resistance had gathered, and they were nearly wiped out.

There were some survivors, but they were few.

And among that small number, those who escaped with only minor injuries or unscathed were even fewer still.

As a result, the Resistance's combat strength was drastically reduced, and this shattered the spirit of those who had been the upper echelons of the Resistance at the time.

"What hurt the most was losing Zuiil... He was the strongest among the Resistance... He had even defeated a Rank C adventurer in battle... And though it pains me to say it to you, Lord Rei, he might have even been able to defeat a Rank B adventurer..."

Seeing Nokor shake his head, everyone present could easily imagine what had happened to that man, Zuil.

But after hearing Nokoru's explanation, Rei and the others all shared a common question.

"Is there someone that strong in the Kingdom of Reburina? Well, seeing the adventurers at the guild, I already knew the quality was low. Ah, but I did see a woman who looked like an orc in Mejogo. She seemed pretty strong."

"Ah, that would be Rurina."

The fact that her name came up so easily after mentioning an orc-like woman meant she was undoubtedly famous.

"Rulaina is known as quite the skilled fighter even among the Jaya. But the one who attacked the Resistance... wasn't Rulaina. I only heard it secondhand, so I can't say for sure, but apparently the attacker was a massive figure."

"...A giant?"

While Rei was on the shorter side, people in this world were generally tall.

Whether it had to do with having to fight monsters, or if magic played a role... Rei didn't know the details, but regardless, most of the inhabitants of this Elgin world were tall enough that even Rei would call them tall.

In this world where people over two meters tall weren't that uncommon, when someone mentioned a giant, the only creatures that came to Rei's mind weren't humans, but monsters like the ogres and cyclopes he'd fought before.

"Yes. They say he's about two heads taller than me."

Hearing that, Rei recalled the height of Nokor when he approached earlier.

If he was two heads taller than Nokor, that meant he was around two and a half meters... no, closer to three meters.

Not quite as large as an ogre or cyclops, but clearly a giant by human standards.

Of course, that was assuming it was true.

If Nokor had seen it directly, it might be somewhat believable.

But if it was secondhand information, it was impossible not to doubt it.

Still, debating whether to believe that story in the current situation was pointless.

Regardless of the information, gathering it was the absolute priority.

"That giant... well, maybe it's a bit small to call a giant, but anyway, you were taken down by just one of them? The giant might have been strong, but still, to be taken down by just one..."

For a moment, Rei hesitated over whether to say "one person" or "one creature," but judging from Nokor's account that it was likely a person, not a monster, he settled on "one person."

That's what Rei said... but what came back from Nokor was a shake of the head.

"No, it wasn't just one. There were several... at least ten or more, apparently."

"...Huh?"

It was only natural that a dumbfounded sound escaped Rei's mouth at Nokor's words.

A person nearly three meters tall—if there was just one, it might be believable. Even two might be somehow acceptable.

But ten? That was nothing but absurd.

Even one person around three meters tall was incredibly rare. Why would there be over ten?

"Could it be... hallucinations? Or maybe they're not human, but smaller ogres or something?"

"No, their faces were human. ...But..."

Nokor seemed reluctant to speak, or perhaps worried he wouldn't be believed, but he gathered his resolve and continued.

"Well, this is just what I heard, but apparently all the giants had the same face."

"...Huh?"

A dumbfounded voice escaped Rei's mouth once more.

For a moment, Rei thought it might be a joke or something, but Nokor's gaze was utterly serious, and he didn't seem to be fooling around at all.

"Um, well... like twins... no, wait, in this case there were over ten of them, so what do you call that? Ten-twins? Anyway, it wasn't like that, right?"

"Look, Rei. Twins or triplets, maybe. But normally, it's impossible for a woman to conceive ten children at once. And they were all big enough to be called giants, right? If that's the case, it wouldn't be strange for them to be born with bodies that were already quite large."

Hearing Vihera's words, Rei nodded in understanding.

Pregnancy was an act permitted only to women, something completely beyond the comprehension of a man like Rei... Yes, it was an act worthy of being called a mystery.

If Vihera, a woman, said so, Rei had no choice but to accept it.

Indeed, glancing at the others, both Elena and Marina nodded in agreement.

Only Byune was still a child and seemed unsure about such matters, tilting her head.

"But... then why do so many giants with the same face appear?"

Nokor shook his head at Rei's question, which seemed to be a pure curiosity.

A massive body alone is a formidable weapon.

Of course, they might not be able to defeat those possessing strength above a certain level—like Rei and the others present here—but it meant they were an overwhelmingly powerful force against those below a certain level of ability.

Indeed, the fact that the Resistance's main force had been utterly crushed suggested Rei's prediction wasn't too far off.

"The same face... That seems like a clue. ...Huh? The same face... The same face? The same face!?"

Suddenly, Rei, who had been repeating "the same face," murmured with force, as if realizing something.

Those sharp words carried enough force that someone unaware might have fainted just from hearing them...

But, oblivious to the stares gathering around him, Rei was lost in thought.

(This body I'm using was created by Zepair and the others. If that's the case, even with the difference between science and magic, creating a physical body isn't impossible. So, even if there are homunculi or something like that...)

That was Rei's thought, yet doubts lingered.

After all, it took Zepair—a being of such power he was called a demon lord—and his entire faction... a gathering of geniuses among geniuses, unparalleled in history, pooling their strength together to finally create Rei's body.

Could something similar... well, not exactly the same, but something comparable... really be achieved by the small nation of Reburina? And even more improbably, by Jaya, which, no matter how large, was ultimately just a clandestine organization?

It was simply inconceivable that such a thing could be possible under normal circumstances.

(Then, something beyond pure magic or alchemy... Even if they possessed the technological capability to mass-produce slave collars, could they really pull off something like that...? Wait. Slave collars?)

Mid-thought, something suddenly struck a chord with Rei's intuition.

Slave collars, the ability to alter the consciousness of women forcibly brought from other countries, and the fact that women whose consciousness can be altered desire to become prostitutes. ...And then... the words spoken by that adventurer man who accosted him in Mejogo, about his favorite prostitute disappearing.

He'd already taken dozens, hundreds of women. Mejogo couldn't possibly hold them all, yet it operated without a hitch.

Putting all of this together... a thought arose within Rei, one that filled her with a strong, unpleasant... indeed, an extremely strong sense of revulsion.

Seeing Rei's face, Elena must have judged that thought had crystallized. Having quietly observed the situation, she spoke up, as if representing everyone.

"Rei, if you've thought of something, please tell us. We can't completely dismiss the possibility that it might help break this current situation."

"...Alright. But honestly, it's a pretty disgusting story. Still wanna hear it? ...I think it's best not to let Byune hear it, at least."

At those words, everyone turned their gaze toward Byune... but the person being looked at shook her head, still holding her skewered meat.

"Hmm."

As usual, she only murmured softly, but unusually, even Rei understood what Byune meant.

She was saying she wanted to hear it too.

Byune was still young... no, childish, but she was an adventurer.

She had experience adventuring solo in the Labyrinth City since she was even younger than she was now.

Furthermore, due to her birth, she had seen her fair share of ugly sights.

Being excluded from the group was not something Byune found amusing.

Her expression remained almost unchanged, but even so, Rei and the others, who had been traveling with Byune for some time, could tell she was slightly frowning.

Under that gaze, it was Rei who eventually gave in.

With a small sigh, Rei finally spoke.

"The giant that attacked the Resistance... the one that created it... no, literally gave birth to it... was a prostitute. And it's fair to say that prostitute sacrificed herself to give birth to it."

At Rei's words, everyone listening gasped.

Well, only Nokor showed a slight surprise, but that was all.

Seeing Nokor's reaction, Rei understood that he probably shared the same thoughts.

After all, the clues were laid out so blatantly.

As a member of the Resistance—or collaborator, as he called himself—operating in Rossi, reaching the same conclusion as Rei shouldn't be too difficult.

"So it is, then. ...Honestly, I'd be lying if I said I hadn't suspected it. But if you ask me whether such a thing is possible... Frankly, I don't know."

Nocor frowned and asked Rei.

Though an excellent intelligence gatherer, Nokor probably wasn't that knowledgeable about magic or alchemy.

That was precisely why, even though he suspected it was likely, he hadn't been able to obtain confirmation.

"If we're talking about feasibility... I can only say it's probably possible. Though, I don't know the specific method myself. But thinking that way, it all makes sense."

"...But would the upper echelons of the Kingdom of Reburina be involved in such an egregious act? If it became known they were doing something like that, wouldn't it cause a huge uproar? Even a king of a nation would be utterly helpless in such a situation."

"I suppose so."

No one disagreed with Elena's words.

Indeed, everyone nodded in agreement as if convinced by her words.

However... Amidst this, Rei had a sudden thought.

Reburina was a vassal state... and among the vassal states of the Mireana Kingdom, it was one of the smaller ones, lower down the list.

It seemed unlikely that the upper echelons of such a minor state would go to the trouble of collaborating with a clandestine organization just to achieve something...
