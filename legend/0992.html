Several days had passed since the incident at the gate of Kobold and Gilm.

During that time, Rei, Vihera, Byune, and Set had ventured outside Gilm, searching for the alchemist.

But finding a single alchemist outside Gilm proved far more difficult than locating one within its walls, and they still hadn't managed to catch sight of him.

"Grr..."

Set, flying through the sky with his wings flapping, made a sound of apology.

Rei, riding on his back, stroked Set's neck.

"Don't worry about it. It's not like it's Set's fault. You knew it would be hard to find him in the first place, right? Besides, we're not the only ones looking for him."

"Grrrr..."

Set perked up slightly at Rei's words.

While Rei and Set were searching the skies, Vihera and Byune were moving across the ground. Due to the gate incident, the guards were also patrolling inside Gilm... especially around the slums where people could easily hide.

That said, aside from Vihera and Byune, only Ranga and a few others within the guard force knew the details.

Given that, it was common for patrols within Gilm to be conducted by guards unaware of the situation, making their searches somewhat off-target.

...Not that it was a direct result, but the guards patrolling the city did serve as a deterrent against crime.

"Besides, Vihera seemed to be enjoying it, didn't he? He's doing his job with a smile, so don't worry too much about it."

"Guru?"

"Really?" Set tilted his head slightly.

In truth, Vihera did enjoy walking around outside Gilm with Byune.

Vihera had only just arrived in Gilm, and there were still quite a few people unaware of his relationship with Rei.

...Even though he'd been in Gilm for less than ten days, the number of people who knew Vihera was already growing. It was probably because he stood out so much.

In any case, even among those who didn't know Vihera was connected to Rei, the more aggressive types would naturally try to hit on her if they encountered her outside Gilm.

To this, Vihera would challenge them to a mock battle, saying she'd listen to their story if they could beat her... and as a result, she had won every single fight.

In fact, due to Vihera's actions over the past few days, she's been drawing attention for reasons quite different from Rei's.

"I doubt he's inside Gilm. If he were, he'd be pretty conspicuous."

"Grr."

Set growled in agreement with Rei's words.

If it were inside Gilm, small weapons like daggers might be manageable, but a massive magic item like the hammer the red cyclops used would inevitably draw attention when removed.

And it was hard to imagine Ranga's guard squad letting someone carrying such a load slip by.

(Could it be possible disguised as a merchant?)

Rei considered this, but the guards were professionals in their field.

Especially Gilm's guards, being in the frontier, checked such things meticulously.

(If that's the case, then we'd have to be hiding somewhere... but spending the night outdoors in the frontier, probably in small numbers, is just too much to consider.)

Rei pondered this while gazing down at the ground atop Set, who was flapping his wings. Suddenly, Set growled.

"Grr!"

"What is it!?"

Set, having growled, immediately flapped his wings and changed direction.

The way he descended toward the ground was the very behavior of a predatory bird that had spotted its prey.

From Set's back, Rei also turned his gaze toward the ground.

Landing on the ground, what caught Rei's eye was a single carriage overturned beside the road.

Rei thought it might be an accident where the wheels had caught on something, but all surrounding the carriage were the corpses of horses.

Moreover, the horses hadn't broken their necks from the impact of the overturned carriage; they had arrows stuck in them.

This was clearly the work of human hands, and if so, Rei could easily imagine what sort of people had done this.

"It's unusual for bandits to venture this far into the Gilm area. Is it because it's spring?"

Gilm was a frontier region, and its outskirts were home to numerous powerful monsters.

And monsters naturally don't consider thieves allies; if they encounter them, they attack without warning.

In other words, to operate as a bandit in the frontier, one needed the strength to defeat monsters.

It's not that many, but you need the kind of strength required to take on a Rank C monster like the cyclops Rei fought last time.

Of course, with that kind of strength, you could raid merchants and caravans coming to Gilm and seize large quantities of rare goods.

But doing so would make you a target not just for monsters, but for Gilm's adventurers as well.

If you possess the strength to handle that... then you could probably make a living through legitimate means, like becoming a mercenary or something, without resorting to banditry.

So, those who deliberately choose to be thieves near Gilm are either those confident in their skills who can't or won't choose any other way of life... or overconfident fools who know nothing of the circumstances.

"Well, I don't think we should jump to conclusions just because they're using bows. And with only one carriage, they might not even be merchants."

For humanoid monsters like orcs or kobolds, using a bow wouldn't be unusual.

Still, for Rei, it was preferable if they were bandits. Because...

"Birds of a feather flock together. If they really are bandits operating around here, it wouldn't be surprising if they know about the alchemist. Set, do you know where they are?"

"Grr!"

At Rei's words, Set turned his gaze toward a grove slightly off the road, as if to say, "Leave it to me."

Unlike the forest where they'd fought the Cyclops, this was a sparse grove. If a battle was happening deep inside, it wouldn't be hard to find.

「Set！」

At the call, Set growled and immediately sprang into action.

With Rei still on his back, he kicked off the ground and charged through the woods, dodging the trees.

After running for about a minute, a metallic clang finally reached Rei's ears.

(A minute on Set's legs means he covered quite a distance even dodging trees. If he managed to outrun the bandits that far, he must be incredibly fast?)

Though not as fast as when flying, Set's running speed was considerably swift.

He could easily outpace even a trained horse.

For Set to run for a full minute meant the distance covered was considerable.

Soon, figures battling could be seen through the gaps in the trees...

"Looks like we've found them. Set, take the guard of the one being attacked."

"GRRR!"

The number of suspected bandits was around twenty.

Against them stood five men who appeared to be the guards.

The numerical difference was overwhelming, yet the bandits still couldn't finish the fight. This was likely because the five guards, centered around two women in their twenties, were focused solely on defense.

(I don't know if they're merchants or not, but this must be why the bandits prioritized the passengers over the cargo in the carriage.)

The two women protected by the guards were, even to Rei's eyes, strikingly beautiful.

Even to Rei, who knew beauties like Elena, Vihera, and Marina, these three women, while not quite reaching their level, possessed beauty he could confidently declare as stunning.

It was no wonder the bandits' eyes lit up upon seeing them.

"Set, please. Use the King's authority first."

"GRRRR!"

As Rei called out while drawing the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring, he emerged from the trees into an open area resembling a square.

The first to notice the sudden intruder wasn't the thieves... but the five guards.

It was only natural, since Set had appeared from behind the thieves.

Seeing the guards' reaction, the thieves grew suspicious and turned to look behind them...

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

The roar echoing from behind made their legs tremble. They dropped the weapons they held in their hands, their minds filling with terror.

"Wh-what? What just happened?"

One of the guards gasped in astonishment at the sight of all the surrounding bandits suddenly frozen in place, staring blankly.

This was the effect of the skill "King's Intimidation," but the two women and five guards recognized by Set as those he must protect were exempt from its effect.

"...Didn't expect everyone to go down like that in one shot. Guess there was nothing left for me to do after all."

Muttering, Rei dismounted from Set's back and surveyed the area, Death Scythe in hand.

"Um, you are..."

One of the guards opened his mouth, staring at Rei's appearance.

At first, they hadn't known who Rei was... or what the situation was. But the guards had been coming to Gilm repeatedly as escorts for the past few years.

That's precisely why they immediately recognized the figure wielding a scythe far larger than himself, with a griffin as his familiar.

"You... you're Crimson, aren't you!?"

"Correct. ...Well, we'll talk about the details later. For now, we should tie up these thieves and make sure they can't move. Are you two over there okay?"

Rei's gaze fell on the two women surrounded by the guards.

They had been attacked by bandits, managed to escape with their guards, but were pursued into the woods. There, the bandits caught up, surrounded them, and were attacking them almost playfully, ready to finish them off at any moment.

If this continued, all the guards would die, and they themselves would be taken away by the bandits, violated, and ultimately sold into slavery.

Just as they were gripped by that terror, a sudden helping hand appeared.

Though hooded, their face was hidden, and they were certainly not tall.

It was impossible not to feel gratitude toward such a person.

"Thank you. Truly, I don't know how to thank you enough."

"Um, ah, thank you!"

Rei gave a light wave to the two bowing deeply.

"Don't worry about it. I had some business with thieves myself... Just checking, but you guys are thieves, right?"

Rei asked, knowing it would be no joke if they turned out not to be thieves. The five guards and the two women all nodded in response.

"They're definitely thieves."

"Yeah. If we said we weren't thieves but knights with faces like this, that would be surprising in its own way."

"Hey, hey, don't judge by appearances!"

Listening to the guards' exchange, a thought flashed through Rei's mind: If Pamidor were here, he'd probably be treated as the bandit leader without question.

While he found himself wanting to see that happen, he pulled a rope from his Misty Ring and tossed it to the guards.

"If we leave them like this, they could make a move at any moment. Better tie them up quickly."

Even while talking with the guards, the bandits remained unable to move.

No, worse than that—some had even lost consciousness.

They would likely flee the instant they could move, but the king's imposing presence still rendered them completely immobile. Even if they could move, Set's sharp gaze fixed on the bandits prevented them from doing so.

Set's eyes, usually round and emphasizing his lovable nature, now held a sharpness that made anyone seeing them doubt they could be the same person.

Rei and the guards swiftly bound the immobilized thieves.

Two of the guards, however, remained stationed near the women, ensuring the bandits didn't make any strange advances.

Though there were about twenty bandits, Rei and the others were experienced, and they were all bound within minutes.

"...So, um... now that it's over, what about these guys? You saved us, so we'll leave dealing with them to you."

One of the guards who had finished tying up the bandits asked Rei this, but Rei shook his head at the question.

"Nah, you guys handle these guys. The carriage was in that state, and you'll need supplies for all sorts of things, right?"

Normally, as a Bandit Eater, Rei would take the treasure the bandits had hoarded and sell the captured thieves as slaves for profit.

That alone would net him a fortune... or two fortunes, giving you an idea of how much Rei usually earned.

That's precisely why the guard's eyes widened in surprise at Rei's words.

"Are you sure?"

He was thinking, "If you just took these bandits to Gilm, you could make money off them."

"Yeah, but I'm a bit pressed for time right now. Before that, though, I'd like to ask these guys a few questions. I'm looking for something... no, someone. Might not be interesting to you lot, but that's why I got involved in this mess in the first place."

"No, we'd have been in serious trouble without you. It's doubtful we could have saved those two we were protecting either. We've got nothing but gratitude, no hard feelings. ...So, about what you wanted to ask. I'm fine with it, but you okay with it?"

The bodyguard who'd been talking to Rei asked the others, and all he got in response was a nod.
