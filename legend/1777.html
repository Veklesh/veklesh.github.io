Anesis is the most prosperous city within the territory ruled by Duke Kerebel, and it could be said to be Elena’s hometown.

And since it is a city, this means that Gilm—which is currently undergoing expansion—will reach that size in a few years.

Rei was currently gazing down at the city of Anesis from atop Set’s back.

“...It’s huge, isn’t it?”

Without realizing it, a murmur escaped Rei’s lips.

That murmur, however, was swallowed up by the winter air, which was so cold it stung the skin.

It was already snowing steadily, so the temperature was low.

Flying at an altitude of about 100 meters, the temperature was overwhelmingly lower than on the ground, and the wind was strong.

That said, thanks to his Dragon Robe, Rei was able to stay comfortable without any issues, and since Set was originally a griffin, this level of cold was no problem for him.

Elena and Ara were currently inside the Set Cage, so they weren’t affected either.

As a result, even though they were flying through the sky in the snow, they didn’t seem to be shivering from the cold at all.

Anyone who dislikes the cold would undoubtedly envy them.

And above all...

“From the perspective of those guys, I’m definitely the one they’d envy. …Still, how did they find out about us?”

“Grr.”

In response to Rei’s words, Set growled in agreement.

The gaze of that man and his companion was fixed on a wyvern flying toward them from the direction of Anesis.

Naturally, since it was flying from Anesis—the stronghold of Duke Kerebel—that wyvern couldn’t possibly be just any ordinary wyvern.

Soldiers rode upon its back—beings commonly known as dragon knights.

……However, the wyverns ridden by these dragon knights were the lowest-ranking of all dragons—including wyverns and flying dragons—and were extremely vulnerable to the cold.

If this were a higher-ranking dragon species, a little snowfall wouldn’t affect them at all.

Still, the fact that they’ve come out in the snow like this is likely because the one approaching Anesis is Set, a flying griffin.

(Because of the Set Cage, they shouldn’t be able to spot us from below. Really, I wonder how on earth they found us.)

Despite his doubts, Rei strokes Set and asks the approaching dragon knight not to attack.

If it were a higher-ranking dragon species, that might be one thing, but for a lowly wyvern, Set would likely see it as nothing more than a tasty meal.

Despite that, he gets along well with Yellow, but I suppose Set has his own reasons for that.

“Well, I wonder how he’ll react? If it’s Duke Kerebel, Elena’s father, he should know who I am, of course.”

As Rei muttered this, the dragon knight gradually approached Set.

That said, the dragon knight shows no sign of drawing his spear or bow, nor does he give any indication of ordering the wyvern to attack.

...Mind you, since wyverns are vulnerable to the cold, their flying speed is terribly slow compared to that of another dragon knight that Rei had seen before.

Even if the wyvern were in peak condition, the dragon knights wouldn’t stand a chance against Rei and Set—so to challenge them to a fight under these circumstances would be nothing short of suicide.

“Wait! I take it you are Rei of Crimson—is that correct!?”

“Huh,” he said.

Rei’s expression betrayed a hint of admiration at the dragon knight’s words.

A Dragon Knight is a “knight,” not a soldier.

Although they hold the lowest rank among the nobility, they are still full-fledged nobles.

That said, most Dragon Knights are of the so-called “one-generation nobility” status.

Despite that status, the fact that he wasn’t giving Rei arrogant orders certainly made a good impression on her.

“That’s right. I’m Rei, an adventurer affiliated with Gilm. Also, Elena and Ara are inside the Set Cage.”

Rei’s words must have been completely unexpected.

The dragon knights froze, their faces frozen in astonishment, and eventually turned their gaze toward the Set Cage Set was holding with movements so stiff it sounded as if a creaking noise might follow.

After a few seconds of silence, the man who appeared to be leading the dragon knights spoke up in a panic.

“O-okay! I’ll guide you to Anesis right away, so please follow me!”

Rei nodded in response to the man’s voice, which sounded almost like a scream.

The other dragon knights, aside from the one who shouted, also looked visibly tense.

This isn’t because they’re standing so close to an adventurer with a nickname who commands a griffin… but rather because of the presence of Elena Kerebel, known by the nickname “Princess General.”

(If she’s guiding us directly to Anesis, that means the barrier is… Ah, no. Come to think of it, the dragon knights took off, so maybe the barrier was already lifted at that point? Still, that seems to pose a slight problem… Or perhaps it just doesn’t take much time to redeploy the barrier?)

Since this isn’t a remote frontier like Gilm, the likelihood of flying monsters appearing is low.

Low, yes... but even so, while it might be acceptable for a rural village, it shouldn’t be the case that a major city like Anesis isn’t protected by a barrier.

In any case, deciding that there was no point in dwelling on it here, Rei asked Set to follow after the dragon knights.

The dragon knights flew toward Anesis, taking care not to get too close to Set as he soared through the sky.

Many of them had participated in the war against the Bestir Empire as part of Kerebel’s army, and they’d heard stories of how Rei and Set had single-handedly taken down large numbers of Bestir Empire dragon knights.

For that very reason, they have no intention of pointlessly picking a fight with Rei here.

Of course, if Rei and Set were to attack Anesis, they would resist without hesitation, risking their lives as dragon knights serving Duke Kerebel.

However, Rei isn’t taking a hostile stance toward Anesis… or the Duchy of Kerebel.

And above all, Rei—known by the epithet “Crimson”—was famous in the Duchy of Kerebel for being Elena’s match.

Of course, there are many who do not acknowledge this.

With these thoughts in mind, Rei and Set continued flying, eventually entering Anesis.

Since they were in the Set Cage, to the residents of Anesis, it must have looked as though a formation of dragon knights was flying through the sky.

“Grr…”

Set, flying alongside the dragon knights, felt a slight sense of dissatisfaction at the slow pace.

No, if it were just the slow pace, there wouldn’t be any problem. But since this was the first time he’d seen so many dragon knights flying around him in a peaceful situation, he probably felt a bit uneasy.

If these were hostile dragon knights, he would have experienced that during the war with the Bestir Empire.

“Calm down. The dragon knights around us aren’t our enemies. Just think of them as Yellow’s allies.”

As Rei says this while stroking Set’s neck right in front of him, Set lets out a purr, looking as if to say, “Oh, well.”

Perhaps sensing Set’s grumpy mood instinctively, the wyverns—the dragons flying around Set—became even more sluggish, despite their movements already being slowed by the cold.

Realizing that his wyvern was cowering, the dragon knight flying closest to Set—though still some distance away—called out to Rei.

“Lord Rei, the large mansion in the center is Duke Kerebel’s residence! Please land the one Elena is riding in the garden!”

“You say ‘the garden’… but which one?”

Duke Kerebel’s mansion was so vast that the word “mansion” hardly did it justice.

Naturally, from the air, it was clear that this massive estate had multiple gardens.

Being told simply to land in the garden, Rei had no idea which one he was supposed to choose.

The dragon knight who had ordered Rei to lower the Set Cage into the garden must have noticed Rei’s confusion. He hurriedly pointed with his lance at a garden that was noticeably larger than the others.

“Over there! Please land there!”

Nodding in response to the dragon knight’s words, Rei signaled to Set.

With that single signal, Set headed toward the spacious courtyard.

In the cold winter air, Set’s wings flapped violently.

And so, as Set descended toward the ground, he released the Set Cage he was holding only after he was close enough to the ground.

That sound must have certainly echoed through Kerebel Duke’s mansion.

The fact that several people emerged from the mansion within seconds demonstrated the high level of skill possessed by Kerebel’s guards.

“We’re landing in that garden too, so please follow us, Rei!”

The dragon knight shouted to Rei, who had lowered the Set Cage to the ground and returned to the sky.

Rei nodded in response and followed the dragon knights as they descended toward the ground.

The dragon knights formed a neat formation and descended to the ground with a level of discipline that, while not quite flawless, was close to it.

Considering it was winter and wyverns were sensitive to the cold, one could even say their discipline was exceptionally high.

Following the dragon knights, Set also flapped his wings and headed toward the ground.

Although his landing posture differed from that of the wyverns ridden by the dragon knights, Set still landed safely in the courtyard.

...In fact, the way Set landed in the courtyard after the wyverns made it look as if he were leading them.

“Grrrrrr!”

Having landed in the courtyard, Set lets out a cry, perhaps out of joy.

If this were Gilm, a crowd of people would have petted him or given him something to eat in response to that roar.

But… this isn’t Gilm; it’s Anesis.

Since it was his first time here, naturally, the people currently in the courtyard had never seen Set before.

Well, perhaps a few might have seen him during the war with the Bestir Empire, but even if so, they’d be a tiny minority.

For most of the people here now, this is their first time seeing Set… no, their first time seeing a griffin at all.

A Rank A monster.

While Set is treated as a pet in Gilm, he is actually a high-rank monster that adventurers might encounter only once in a lifetime.

When someone who knows nothing about them sees such a monster up close, it’s only natural that they feel fear rather than cuteness.

And if it’s a Rank A monster like a griffin, that’s only natural.

Among those who had come out into the garden, it would be fine if they were the guards assigned to protect this mansion.

But for everyone else… those who had simply come out into the garden for a sightseeing trip—upon seeing the griffin up close, and hearing its cry—let out screams of terror…

“Silence!”

Just as the first person was about to let out a scream of terror, a stern shout rang out across the garden.

Strangely enough, even those on the verge of panic found the voice naturally entering their ears.

And so, the courtyard fell completely silent.

A silence so profound it seemed as though the only sound to be heard was the soft fall of snow.

In that courtyard, Elena, who had spoken, opened her mouth again.

“This person is my guest. That griffin over there is my guest’s familiar, named Set. And… my guest is Rei, known by the epithet ‘Crimson’.”

She swept back her golden hair, styled in tight curls that shone even amidst the falling snow… and at that very moment, sunlight streamed through a gap in the snow clouds covering the sky, dyeing Elena’s hair a golden hue.

It was a sight so beautiful that it captivated everyone who saw it—even those who, just seconds ago, had been on the verge of screaming at the sight of Set.

It was a sight so breathtaking that one could easily imagine it becoming a masterpiece preserved for posterity if captured as a painting.

“Rei, over here.”

As silence enveloped the surroundings, Elena’s voice rang out once more.

As if guided by that voice, Rei moved forward with Set to stand before Elena.

Rei understood that Elena was a stunning beauty.

He also fully understood that she had a down-to-earth side, having seen her conversing with Marina and Vihera.

But… seeing Elena display her side as the Princess General so strongly was quite a rare sight, even for Rei.

For that very reason, he found himself drawn forward, not by the Elena he knew, but by the Princess General Elena.

Rei, who had moved closer to Elena alongside Set, sensed what was expected of him from her gaze and spoke up.

“I’m Rei. I’m a Rank B adventurer affiliated with Gilm. This is my familiar, Set. Set is generally docile, and as long as you don’t try to harm him, he won’t do anything to you, so don’t worry. At Gilm, he’s treated like a pet.”

The words that came out of Rei’s mouth were as true as could be… but those who heard them couldn’t bring themselves to believe him right away.

In any case, Rei had made a striking introduction to the servants of the Duke of Kerebel’s household.
