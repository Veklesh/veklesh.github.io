"Ah, I've been waiting for you. How did things go on your end? We wrapped up our end of the deal without a hitch."

Upon arriving at the meeting spot, Augusto waved cheerfully with a delighted smile.

Seeing him like that, it was clear the deal had gone well.

"Yes, our side wrapped up safely too. There was quite a bit of commotion, though."

Zalust and Set turned to the nearby man named Rei with complex expressions.

They knew Rei was a strong adventurer, but the fact that the guild receptionist had given him a nickname was even more shocking.

It wasn't just Zarust; Gorkas, who had gotten involved with Rei... and the other adventurers nearby who had overheard Rei and the receptionist talking felt the same way.

There was no way anyone could look at Rei's appearance and believe he was a nickname-holder. But the receptionist... It was absolutely unthinkable that a guild employee, who shared information between guilds, would mistake someone like that.

Above all, while the others didn't know, the guild staff had heard about Rei from Marina, so there was no way they could have been mistaken.

The fact that Rei didn't correct them when they called him "Crimson" only added to the credibility of the nickname... and eventually, the looks directed at Rei lost all trace of contempt.

The worst offender was undoubtedly Gorkas, who had been the first to pick a fight with Rei.

The moment Gorkas realized the person he'd picked a fight with was a nickname holder, his eyes met Rei's after Rei finished talking with the receptionist, and Gorkas immediately fled the scene.

The guild ended up filled with an awkward atmosphere. The escorts, including Zarust, quickly finished the request completion procedures and promptly left the guild.

Some had considered grabbing a drink at the tavern, but given the guild's current state, such a thing was out of the question.

In the end, everyone left the guild together, and those other than Zarust scattered throughout the city.

"A disturbance, you say? ...Well, I suppose I can understand why."

Augusto's gaze, accompanied by a smile, naturally turned toward Rei.

"With you bringing along the griffin, Set, it would be strange if there wasn't a commotion."

"...No, that's part of it, but there was another commotion too."

"Something else?"

"Yes. ...Actually, it turns out Rei is an adventurer with a nickname."

"Oh!? Well, I understood that Rei-san was strong, but... I see. A renowned adventurer? That's splendid."

Augusto murmured, looking at Rei with admiration.

His reaction differed from the other adventurers at the guild because, even if only for one night, they had camped together and he knew Rei well.

Moreover, becoming acquainted with Rei, a man of renown, was significant in itself.

An adventurer with a nickname was a special existence in many ways.

This held true not only for adventurers but also for merchants seeking commissions.

For Augusto, this could be called an unexpected stroke of luck.

(Considering he possesses such an outrageous magic item and even has a griffin as a familiar, it's not at all strange that he has a nickname, I suppose.)

Augusto turned his gaze back toward Rei and Set, a smile playing on his lips as he spoke.

"Well then, shall we head home soon? I've already sent a messenger ahead, so my wife should be preparing a delicious meal by now. How about you, Zarust? Since you always guard us, perhaps you'd like to join us for a meal sometime."

"No, I'd love to, but I promised Rei I'd skin the Sand Serpent. I need to prepare for that first."

Augusto nodded in understanding at Zarust's words.

The Sand Serpent was about five meters long. Though its head had been destroyed, leaving it around four meters, skinning a monster of that size meant it wasn't something you could just handle casually.

Moreover, this was the desert. They couldn't just work near a river like Rei had done in Gilm.

Zalust was probably saying that they needed to properly apply for permission to use the necessary facilities.

(Well, if he'll handle it, it'll be easier for me, so I'm all for it.)

As far as Rei was concerned, if someone else would handle all that paperwork for him, he had no complaints.

At worst, they could always strip it back at Gilm instead of doing it here.

"I see. That's unfortunate. ...Well then, Rei, and Set too. Shall we be going? I'm sure they'll be delighted."

Augusto looked slightly disappointed when Zarust informed him he couldn't join them for the meal due to business, but he quickly shifted his mood and addressed Rei and Set.

Rei had no objection either and nodded promptly.

"Well then, Zarust. Thank you for your escort this time. If anything else comes up, I'll count on you again."

"Ah, take care, Augusto. ...Well, with Rei and Set at home, if anyone tries anything foolish, they'll only regret it."

He directed the latter part of his words toward Rei.

Anyone who'd even consider sneaking into a house where a famed adventurer and a griffin are staying would undoubtedly be a hero.

(Though a brave fool, still a brave fool.)

Still, a hero is a hero, Zarust thought, flashing a wry smile as he bid farewell to Rei and Augusto and walked away.

"...Well then, shall we go? My wife's cooking is exquisite."

"Yeah. I've heard it many times, and I'm looking forward to it."

Augusto had boasted about his wife numerous times in this short while.

It was obvious without a doubt that he was what you'd call a devoted husband.

Augusto, smiling as he listened to Rei's words, led the way.

Gosh was one of the more prosperous cities within the Kingdom of Solrain, but it still couldn't compare to Gilm.

The fact that it revolves around an oasis probably plays a role too.

"I never imagined Rei were an adventurer with a nickname."

"Is that so? ...Well, I suppose that's true."

Rei knew how his appearance came across.

If someone could see him and immediately recognize him as a powerful figure, that would be one thing, but ordinarily, it was simply impossible to think of him that way.

"Since you have Set as your familiar, I assumed you were famous?"

Augusto's gaze swept around the room.

Most residents were startled by Set... by the griffin, and some were even visibly frightened.

The fact that it hadn't caused a major commotion was partly because Set wore a familiar's collar, but more significantly, it was likely due to Augusto being with Rei and Set.

Though targeted by the Elema Trading Company, Augusto wasn't hated by all of Gosh's residents.

In fact, one could say he was well-liked, thanks to his sincere personality. ...Though, of course, he couldn't openly show affection in front of Erema Trading Company associates.

(Absorbing the Sand Serpent's magic stone is one thing, but I really want to get the Sand Ship moving properly at least once. You never know if that guy Dalidra might come asking for it back.)

Generally, the possessions of thieves belong to those who defeated them. However, if the original owner demanded them back, negotiation would be necessary. ...That is, if they could agree on a price.

The problem here, as expected, was the price of the Sand Ship.

Rei didn't know the exact amount, but he could guess it wouldn't be something you could buy with gold or platinum coins.

(Or so I'm thinking, but realistically, it probably can't be bought with gold coins, right? ...Nah, he mentioned it was a significant expense even for the Elema Trading Company, so that's probably not the case.)

Though Rei worried about the Erema Trading Company's finances, Augustus's endless boasts about his wife soon grew tiresome.

He didn't know how long it would take to reach Augusto's house, and since this conversation was getting boring, he decided to change the subject. Stroking the head of Set walking beside him, he voiced something that had been on his mind.

"Hey, I've got a question. Is that sand ship I got only meant to move on sand?"

What came to Rei's mind as he asked Augusto this were the grasslands, rivers, and seas near Gilm—places other than the desert.

Rei typically rode Set when traveling outside the city.

Set's flying speed was overwhelming, and most monsters wouldn't even come close, making it highly advantageous.

But... nothing in life comes without its downsides.

One drawback of flying with Set was that he couldn't carry anyone besides Rei.

To be precise, small children can ride, but as they grow even slightly larger, it becomes difficult.

Adults are completely out of the question. Traveling with a large group was nothing but a pipe dream.

But if the sand ship could be used anywhere, that would change everything.

With that thought, Rei asked Augusto... but the response he got was simply a nod.

"A sand ship, as the name implies, is a magic item designed specifically for moving across sand. I'm no alchemist myself, so I don't know the exact theory behind it, but apparently, it can't move unless it's on sand."

"...True to its name, then."

"Yes. Unfortunately."

He had expected as much from the name, but hearing it was restricted to sand alone still left Rei feeling disappointed.

"So, if it's sand... does that mean it won't work in the rocky desert either?"

"That's right. Only on sand. Of course, that's just to the best of my knowledge. There might be others that can move elsewhere... But the Sand Ship you acquired, Rei, was made in Ozos, the so-called Magical City."

"...You sure know a lot. I didn't realize that when I first saw the Sand Ship."

Augusto shrugged slightly before speaking to Rei, who voiced his confusion.

"Dalidra bragged about it when I met him before. Back then, it was just that he'd acquired an Ozos-made sand ship. I actually saw one for the first time yesterday."

"The magical city of Ozos, huh. I've heard the name a few times before, but I've never actually been there myself."

"Really? I heard you have a hobby of collecting magic items, so I assumed you'd been there..."

"Unfortunately, I've never had the chance to go. I do want to visit someday, though."

He had heard the name Ozos many times before, but he had never been there.

That seemed odd to Rei as well.

Given that Rei had a hobby of collecting magic items, it wouldn't be strange at all for him to want to visit Ozos, arguably the cutting edge of magic. ...No, it would be perfectly natural.

Yet, for some reason, Rei didn't feel the urge to go to Ozos.

(Why? ...Ah well. I'll probably want to go when the chance comes.)

It was a completely different way of thinking than usual, but Rei himself seemed completely unaware of it and concluded as much.

"I see. Well, I've only heard about Ozos myself, never been there. I do hope to visit someday."

Rei nodded reflexively in response to Augusto's smiling remark.

"So... what were we talking about? Ah, yes. Sand Ships. If they can only move in sandy deserts, that makes them pretty impractical."

"True. But for the Elema Trading Company, that was probably sufficient. It was likely about appearances to begin with. Besides, even if they did use them, this is Gosh. It's a city built in an oasis within the desert, so using sand ships poses no problem at all."

"Well, I guess if you live here, you don't need to worry about that... But for me, it's true they're not very practical."

Maybe it would be better to just sell it here in Goshu, where there are plenty of people who want one.

That thought crossed Rei's mind, but selling it to someone careless would surely lead to Erema Trading Company getting involved.

(So, if I do sell it, I should do it in another city. Even if Elema Trading Company's influence is strong, it's probably limited to Gosh. Fortunately, I hear the Solrain Kingdom is mostly desert.)

Though it was a hard-won magic item, Rei had no intention of moving to Gosh, nor did he plan to let the Sand Ship gather dust inside the Misty Ring.

He believed magic items weren't meant to be displayed like art pieces for admiration, but only gained meaning when actually used.

Of course, some magic items are made solely for appreciation.

Things like moving paintings or dolls resembling pet robots.

For magic items like that, even if their purpose was to be admired, Rei could accept it.

As he walked along thinking this, Augusto suddenly spoke up.

"Ah, there it is, Rei-san! The love nest where my beloved wife Kathy waits for me!"

"...No, a love nest..."

Was staying at Augusto's house perhaps a mistake?

Rei hesitated briefly at the phrase "love nest."

Set's purring, as if to encourage Rei, was a scene that resonated deeply within him.
