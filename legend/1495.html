"Ah..."

Ajax's head accepted the blade of Ilse's dagger with uncanny ease.

Normally, a skull possesses considerable hardness.

Even someone unaccustomed to combat wielding a longsword couldn't easily break it.

Of course, with sufficient force... someone like Rei could easily crush a skull with his weapon.

But Ilse, who had scarcely engaged in combat at all, could never manage such a feat.

Despite this, Ilse's blade pierced through Ajax's head with surprising ease, penetrating deep into the brain beyond.

Ajax, accepting that blade, relinquished his life with surprising ease.

In his early to mid-thirties... the end of a man who had deceived, killed, and committed every conceivable vice was unexpectedly swift.

"Guhhhhhhh!"

Though Rebekehf, surrounded by intelligence unit men, had a cloth stuffed into his mouth to silence him, witnessing his comrade's death made him cry out.

Most of the sound was meaningless, yet Levjef's wailing echoed around him.

"If you could understand the sorrow of losing a comrade, you should have just acted like a normal adventurer from the start instead of pulling stunts like this."

The intelligence unit man near Rebegef muttered, watching Rebegef continue to scream even with his voice muffled.

"Well, the other one's just unconscious. Maybe it's easier for him."

Huston had fought like mad, but after receiving treatment for his severed right knee, he was now unconscious.

A few paces away, several intelligence unit men stood guard around Haston, who lay collapsed on the ground, ready for any emergency.

His hands were bound, his mouth gagged... Normally, his legs would need to be bound too, but with his right knee severed, that was impossible.

Then again, without his right leg, escaping from this situation was practically impossible anyway.

Unbeknownst to the exchanges happening around her, Ilse, who had finally avenged her enemy after five years of pursuit with her own hands, heard none of these words.

In fact, even though she had killed Ajax, she still felt no sense of reality, staring blankly at the dagger in her hand.

The voices around her barely registered; Ilse was simply in a daze.

The others, including Rei, watched Ilse's state but made no attempt to speak to her.

They knew that anything said to Ilse now would fall on deaf ears... or rather, even if it reached her ears, she wouldn't be able to comprehend it.

"Well then, Rei. We'll take the captured men with us."

Dahl glanced at Ilse before addressing Rei.

"Ah. Do that. I thought it was just some organization dealing in slave trade... but it looks like it's going to be a bigger deal than expected."

"...Yes. It was completely unexpected for us too."

It was an organization, but not one from this country—it was from another organization... not a powerful nation like the Bestir Empire, but from the vassal state of Reburina, subordinate to the Mireana Kingdom.

For the Kingdom of Mireana, it was undeniable that many felt disrespected by a vassal state.

(The foremost among them is undoubtedly His Highness Daskar.)

Allowing such blatant underhanded activity to occur within the city he rules has utterly destroyed Daskar's reputation.

It wasn't just having mud smeared on his face—it was like being covered in horse dung. That kind of humiliation.

Even in decline, he remains a central figure of one of the three major factions, the Neutral Faction. Under normal circumstances, this would be utterly unbelievable.

Of course, Gilm is currently in the midst of major expansion work, attracting many people seeking employment.

Considering that, it was probably the perfect place to scheme, plot, or commit some sort of misdeed.

In fact, precisely because there were so many such people, the intelligence unit was busy in various ways and hadn't been able to dispatch that many agents for this particular incident.

"Still, for now, it doesn't seem like the person ruling Reburina has directly interfered here. It's purely the work of that country's criminal organization... right?"

"Yes. But if you ask whether such a large-scale operation could be pulled off by just one organization..."

"It would be difficult."

"Yes. At the very least, it's certain someone connected to the organization is in the upper echelons of the Leburina Kingdom. Frankly, it's hard to imagine they could have procured this many slave collars so easily."

"That's true."

Slave collars are expensive magic items.

Preparing dozens of them isn't something just any organization could easily manage.

Moreover, it's not necessarily the case that only Ajax and his group were dispatched by that organization.

It was almost certain that members of the organization had been dispatched elsewhere besides this Gilm.

Considering they likely wore slave collars just like Ajax and the others, attributing this solely to one organization was unreasonable.

"Well then, I'll take my leave for now. I've sent word that this matter is resolved, but I suppose I'll need to report the details properly."

"Ah, understood. You've been a great help this time. Please convey my thanks to Ilse."

"No, thank you. It was a great help to have the support of the Crimson Wings."

Dahl bowed deeply to Rei, then to the Crimson Wings, Elena, and Ara.

Meanwhile, Elena's familiar, Yellow, was playing on Set's back, seemingly unaware of Dahl.

"Ah, and what about the captured women? What will happen to them?"

"Well, I suppose once we hear the circumstances of where they were captured, we'll give them enough money for a few days' living expenses and release them."

"...Huh."

Rei murmured, seeming slightly impressed by Dahl's words.

While hearing their stories before release was as expected, he hadn't anticipated they'd actually provide them with living expenses, even if only for a few days.

It wasn't just Rei who was surprised; the others were equally taken aback.

In truth, lords who went to such lengths were extremely rare.

That alone made it clear this particular incident was special in many ways.

"Well, this time it's connected to the large number of people gathered for the Gilm expansion project. So, it seems that situation is involved. Though, I heard this from a colleague in casual conversation, so it might not be entirely accurate."

"Hmm. Daskar probably doesn't want to be criticized for the expansion project either. Considering the reputational damage that could cause, a few days' worth of living expenses isn't that big of a deal."

Marina murmured in agreement at Dahl's words.

Then again, that's probably only because Daskar is a relatively decent lord.

(Of course, it probably wasn't purely out of goodwill. This whole incident could potentially bring significant benefits to the Kingdom of Mireana... no, to the Neutral Faction. Well, the risks taken for that benefit... that return were correspondingly large too.)

This time, since they managed to stop Ajax and the others, there were no particular problems.

But if Ajax's deal had gone through successfully, and they'd escaped safely beyond Gilm... Daskar's reputation would have been ruined even more than it is now.

It's certain Daskar would use this incident as leverage to make various demands on the Kingdom of Rebellina.

While not all the profits gained from it would go to Daskar, it would undoubtedly have been a significant windfall.

Furthermore, some sort of resolution or settlement for this incident would be necessary.

(Anyway...)

Rei shifted his gaze to Ilse, thinking that figuring out those details wasn't his job.

He had defeated the enemy he had pursued for so many years.

Whether he felt that reality or not, Rei couldn't tell.

But there was no mistaking that Ilse was staring at Ajax, who lay on the ground with a dagger still lodged in his forehead, looking somewhat dazed.

Perhaps it would be better to leave her alone for a little longer.

Rei wasn't entirely opposed to that idea, but it was getting late.

Rei and the others arrived in the slums before the date had already changed. While it was still technically night, the sun would rise in just a few hours.

Rei and the other members of Crimson Wings were considered the main force for the expansion work.

Given that, they couldn't very well say, "I'm sleepy, so I'm taking the day off..."

Though Elena and Ara had nothing special to do during the day, so they could just go to sleep normally.

But Rei and the others were adventurers.

Given that, they sometimes had to pull all-nighters for requests.

Fortunately, Rei had rarely taken on such requests, but he could consider this a good experience too.

(Though I'd rather not think of that kind of experience as a good one.)

Sleep was one of Rei's most blissful times.

Even though Rei's body possesses capabilities incomparable to ordinary humans, sleep isn't unnecessary—it's essential.

Considering that, pulling all-nighters really wasn't something to be desired.

"Ilse, it's almost morning. You should get some sleep while you can."

"...Yes."

As if Rei's words snapped her out of it, Ilse looked away from the Ajaxes on the ground and met Rei's gaze.

She still seemed dazed, but she had regained her senses to some extent. She gave a small nod at Rei's words and started to move from where she was... then suddenly stopped.

Her gaze returned to the lifeless body of Ajax lying on the ground.

To an outsider, it was impossible to tell what Ilse was thinking.

Was she basking in the joy of revenge? Was she overwhelmed by guilt for taking a life? Or was it something else entirely?

In any case, it was clear to Rei that Ilse was not in her normal state.

But it would look bad for a man like him to take care of Ilse.

Given that, it was only natural to think that someone female should be entrusted with Ilse's care.

Fortunately, everyone in Crimson Wings except Rei was female, and the two present here, Elena and Ara, were also women.

That meant there were plenty of people he could ask to look after Ilse.

Among them... Rei's gaze settled on Marina.

"Marina, can you handle it?"

"Sure, leave it to me."

She chuckled softly. Marina flashed a smile at Elena and Vihera, then approached Ilse and whispered something in her ear.

Seeing Marina like that, Elena and Vihera each twitched their cheeks.

They wore triumphant smiles, pleased that Rei had asked them specifically.

Marina herself had no such intention, but at least Elena and Vihera felt that way.

However, saying anything now would make them look jealous of Marina.

Also, considering Ilse had just achieved her revenge, they decided it would be better not to say anything here. Both of them settled for just giving Marina a slightly stronger look.

...Though, that slightly intense gaze was only intense from Elena and Vihera's perspective.

The others present... especially the members of the intelligence unit who normally had no contact with Elena and the others... found their knees trembling uncontrollably and their teeth chattering.

Though the intelligence unit members were a gathering of battle-hardened veterans, even for such individuals, the fighting spirit and killing intent emanating from Elena and Vihera must have been enough to send a chill down their spines.

"Huh? What's wrong?"

The moment Rei asked, the fighting spirit and killing intent vanished completely.

"Nothing. For now, we should let Ilse rest properly. Once some time passes, she'll be able to sort through her feelings a bit."

"I agree with Vihera. For Ilse, killing someone was probably a first-time experience, right? If so, it's only natural she'd be either excited or depressed."

Everyone present here had, in some form, experienced the act of taking a human life.

That's precisely why Elena understood Ilse's feelings.

...Though, in Rei's case, his sense of what's normal was messed with when he came to this world, so even if he killed someone, he felt nothing if they were an enemy.

In any case, the commotion that erupted in the middle of the night came to an end.

However, while this particular incident was resolved without incident, it sowed numerous seeds of future conflict and disaster... or rather, brought them to the surface.
