The aroma of something delicious wafted from the stove in Marina's garden.

The aroma of baking pizza was created by the toppings sizzling on the dough and the cheese melted on top, creating a truly mouthwatering scent.

Using the giant peel made by Pamidor, Rei began pulling the pizzas out of the oven.

One, two, three, four... Then, as he tried to grab Rei's pizza at the very back, he couldn't get the peel under it properly and ended up pushing it further back.

"Ah!"

A gasp escaped Rei's lips.

Marina and the others, who had been watching how his pizza turned out, instinctively turned their gaze toward Rei at the sound of his voice.

But luckily, or perhaps fortunately, Rei quickly thrusts the peel forward and manages to pull the pizza out.

It was a bit overdone, but not so burnt it was inedible.

Rei, relieved, placed the pizza on the table.

"So, how do we eat this? Just like this? That seems a bit awkward to eat."

Vihera asked, looking at the pizza. It was about 30cm in diameter, which was small for a pizza... yet still large enough for an average person to eat alone.

Note: While sufficient for the average person, for adventurers whose livelihood depends on physical exertion, a pizza this size is barely more than a snack.

It was literally a piece of cake. ...Even though it was already afternoon.

"You cut it into pieces with a knife or something like that. There's actually a thing called a pizza cutter, but..."

The image of that unique circular tool, meant to be rolled across the pizza to slice it, flashed through Rei's mind, but naturally, there was no such thing here.

Sure, if he asked someone like Pamidor, they'd probably make one, but if he could cut it with a regular knife, there was no need to force the issue.

"Well, at least we have a knife. I'll cut it with this."

He took a knife from his Misty Ring and tentatively lowered the blade onto his pizza... He could cut it, but the pizza stuck to the knife.

Well, if only the cheese stuck a little, that would be fine, but even the toppings seemed to be pulled by the cheese and stuck to the knife.

"...This is..."

Looking at the knife, Rei could only mutter discontentedly.

"Heh heh, that's no good. For something like this... here, let me try."

Marina smiled at Rei's scowling expression, took the knife, and cut her own pizza.

Even to Rei's eyes, it was clear Marina hadn't done anything special.

But unlike when Rei had cut it, cheese didn't stick to the knife, nor did the toppings get pulled onto it by the cheese.

The pizza was sliced normally.

"Huh? Why?"

Rei couldn't figure out how his technique differed from hers, but Marina seemed to find his confusion amusing. She smiled as she cut everyone else's pizza.

"There's a lot involved here—how you use the knife, how much pressure you apply, and so on. Anyway, everyone's slice is cut now, so let's all eat."

Though Rei remained slightly unconvinced, Marina's words forced him to shift his focus back to the pizza.

Vihera was eyeing the pizza with curiosity, and Byune, though expressionless, never took her eyes off it.

Set also stared intently at the pizza sitting on the plate placed on the snow.

"Yeah, let's eat for now. We're hungry anyway."

It was already afternoon, but since this party was a pizza party, they'd skipped lunch.

The two big eaters, Rei and Byune, along with Set and the dog, had already let their stomachs rumble several times for everyone to hear.

Especially since the pizza had come out of the oven, the aroma wafting around them was incredibly appetizing.

After Rei finished distributing the fruit water he'd taken from the Misty Ring to everyone, Vihera spoke up.

"Alright, Rei. Lead us in the chant, will ya?"

"...Me?"

"Yeah. Because Rei is the party leader, right?"

"I'm not exactly convinced about that yet, though."

The question of who would be this party's leader hadn't been settled yet.

Well, to be precise, Vihera, Marina, and Byune all say they want Rei, but Rei himself still isn't convinced.

If asked whether he was fit to be party leader, Rei couldn't honestly nod in agreement.

He knows he's short-tempered and quick to fight, so he worries something big might go wrong.

Besides, Byune is technically joining this party, but since she's supposed to leave eventually, she's out of the question for party leader.

That leaves only Vihera and Marina.

Rei pushed for Marina to be party leader, but she declined, citing her former position as guildmaster—and a highly capable one at that, having served long-term in the frontier guild of Gilm—as giving her too much influence.

That left Vihera, but she too had issues due to her background as a former princess of the Bestir Empire.

That's how it came down to Rei being the choice... but he still isn't convinced.

"Look, Rei's the one who prepared this oven and the pizza in the first place."

Convinced by those words for the moment, Rei sighed before speaking.

"To deepen the bonds of our new party... Cheers."

'Cheers'

"Mm."

"Gurrr"

Marina, Vihera, and Byune each raised their glasses of fruit water, and Set happily growled as the party began.

After drinking the fruit water, the next thing they naturally reached for was pizza.

The texture felt reasonably thick.

Not the crispy type, but regular pizza dough.

(Well, I knew that much when I received this dough from Rodrigo.)

Rei tried to cut it but failed, and eventually Marina sliced it into eight equal pieces. I took one slice.

Naturally, melted cheese drips from it, visually stimulating my appetite.

And since she lifted the pizza, its aroma naturally stimulated her appetite through her sense of smell.

As she brought the tip of the pizza to her mouth... that's when Rei noticed.

(Huh? The edge of the pizza is properly made too. Like bread, is that part called the crust?)

Grasping the raised edge of the pizza—a part that hadn't been there in previous prototypes—Rei tilted his head.

As Rodrigo continued experimenting with pizza, one of the final forms he arrived at—though still not quite perfected—was this crust edge.

Pizza is a dish where toppings are placed on a round dough base and baked, but sometimes the toppings fall off the dough during baking, or sometimes you want to put more toppings on.

That's where the raised edge of the pizza came in handy.

...Though, of course, it's completely useless if you pile on a mountain of toppings like Byune did at first.

(It's pretty handy when holding pizza too, huh?)

Thinking that, he brought the piping hot pizza to his mouth.

The pizza Rei made was a standard one with vegetables, mushrooms, and sausage.

But precisely because it was standard, it was also undeniably good.

The crispy texture of the outer crust, the soft interior of the dough (not the crispy type), and the flavors of the sausage, vegetables, and mushrooms all blended together, enveloped by the tomato sauce and cheese, creating a harmonious explosion of taste in his mouth.

"...Delicious."

He murmurs.

Looking around, he saw the others smiling too, clearly enjoying the pizza.

Of course, this pizza isn't perfect.

It's still a work in progress, and there's room for improvement in both the texture and flavor of the pizza crust.

Even Rei, who wasn't exactly a connoisseur, could tell that, so Rodrigo, who made this pizza crust, would surely keep refining it.

But unlike Rei, who knew pizza from before, the others were tasting this dish for the very first time.

If told that pizza is this kind of dish, they'd probably just accept it.

It's far more delicious than ordinary bread, so it's only natural they'd think that.

"Gururururuu"

Set, in particular, was happily eating the pizza with his beak.

Cheese clinging to his beak was so typical of Set.

"Oh, this is delicious. So this is pizza?"

"Mm."

Byune replied briefly to Marina's words.

...And yet, Byune's hand kept reaching for slice after slice of pizza, showing just how much she loved it.

"Honestly, look, you've got some on your mouth."

While tending to Byune like this, Vihera ate her own pizza.

It wasn't that it was the most delicious food she'd ever eaten... far from it. As a princess who'd tasted countless dishes, Vihera possessed a keen palate and could easily point out numerous flaws in the pizza if she wanted to.

Still, if she had to decide whether the pizza was good or bad, calling it delicious was the correct choice.

Soon, everyone finished their pizza... and naturally, voices arose wanting more.

Rei, too, felt unsatisfied after just one slice, so she retrieved more pizza dough from the Misty Ring and began making more.

It only took about a minute to bake once placed in the oven, so there was almost no cooking time involved.

Even the most time-consuming part of making pizza—preparing the dough—was handled by simply getting the pizza dough directly from Rodrigo. So, it was just a matter of spreading tomato sauce, arranging the toppings, putting it in the oven, and having it ready in about a minute.

A regular oven takes time to reach the right temperature for baking pizza and then to maintain that temperature, but since this was a magic item, there was no need to worry about that.

"Mmm! Mmm! Mmm!"

Byune happily announced as she ate the pizzas coming out one after another.

Set also ate pizza after pizza, happily making satisfying noises.

Rei, too, was thoroughly enjoying the pizza.

Marina and Vihera, having eaten a few slices, seemed satisfied enough and switched from eating to helping make the pizzas.

They made pizza after pizza for Rei, Byune, and Set, gradually improving their efficiency.

How much tomato sauce to spread, the compatibility of toppings, how to arrange them, how much cheese to sprinkle... that sort of thing.

"Oh, right. We're having udon around midnight, so don't fill up too much... Wait, never mind."

Rei said this while eating the vegetable-heavy pizza Marina had made.

They had already eaten nearly twenty pizzas in total.

Normally, one would think it was too late to say that now... but for Rei, Byune, and Set, this amount was literally only about eighty percent full.

Well, for Set specifically, maybe two-thirds or even three-quarters full.

Unlike Rei and the others, Vihera and Marina were feeling reasonably full.

...Even so, they were eating enough that an ordinary woman hearing it would be shocked, thinking, "How can you eat so much and be fine!?"

But Vihera regularly trains her body for combat, and Marina has a naturally slim build. Plus, since joining Rei's party in spring, she's been training daily to avoid holding them back.

"Right. It's getting pretty dark outside. Let's stop eating pizza and rest a bit. ...Just a moment, please."

It was winter, so naturally, it got dark early.

Marina looked around and used her spirit magic.

Suddenly, the area was lit up like... well, saying it was like daytime might be a slight exaggeration, but still, several balls of light flew around, brightly illuminating the garden.

"Wow... that's amazing."

Vihera murmured in awe at the scene, which truly deserved to be called fantastical.

Truthfully, it was undeniably a spectacular sight.

(Though, this kind of thing feels more like Christmas, doesn't it? ...Not that this world has Christmas, though.)

Marina, seemingly pleased by Vihera's words, used her spirit magic again. Instead of ordinary light, she created numerous orbs—blue, red, green—and sent them flitting about the garden.

The light orbs Marina created seemed to possess a degree of will of their own, darting about the garden as if delighted by the gasps of admiration.

For a while, everyone enjoyed the spectacle—a dance of light, perhaps—in silence... until they realized Marina's garden was the only place not shrouded in complete darkness.

"Wow, that took quite a while. ...Well, I guess it's not so strange if you think of it as a kind of hidden party trick."

Rei muttered, glancing at the surroundings now fully shrouded in night. The others seemed to finally notice as well.

Coming back to their senses, the party continued... though instead of eating pizza, it became a time for conversation.
