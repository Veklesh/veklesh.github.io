After finishing an early lunch in the inn's dining hall, Rei and the others immediately set off.

...Though "procedures" meant little more than checking out of the inn; there was no need for the hassle of gathering supplies like food, water, or camping gear that Elk and the others would have taken care of before heading to Gilm.

Those items were already stored inside Rei's Misty Ring.

If anything, Rei had to resist the urge to pull out a dish made with the silver lion meat stored inside the Misty Ring and eat it.

Even though they had just finished eating, he felt like he could easily eat more silver lion meat. ...And in truth, that wasn't just bravado or empty talk.

In any case, since Rei hadn't brought any personal belongings into the room, he was ready to leave almost immediately.

Compared to Rei, Elena and the others had brought a fair amount of personal belongings into their rooms—clothes, toiletries, and various other items—which they needed to pack into their bags, inevitably taking more time.

As Rei was about to leave the inn, thinking he should probably tell them to get the carriage ready while he went to check on Set...

"Oh, Rei. You're up early today."

Vihera was sitting on a sofa near the inn's counter, smiling and waving at Rei.

True to its reputation as a high-class inn, several sofas were placed near the entrance.

They served as places for meetings, business negotiations... and even for lovers to exchange sweet nothings.

"Vihera? You're up early."

Rei, who carried no luggage or belongings and certainly didn't look like he was about to leave the inn, responded to Vihera, who sat on the sofa drinking tea.

It was late autumn... or perhaps early winter. Yet Vihera, clad only in the thin garments worn by courtesans and dancers, naturally stood out.

Many who passed by the sofa wore expressions of surprise.

Yet, not everyone fully showed their surprise. Perhaps it was because the guests staying at this high-class inn were skilled at hiding their emotions.

Seeing Vihera's attire, which seemed to flaunt her supple form, yet still finding people who didn't show their feelings, Rei felt a slight admiration for that point.

"Are you ready, Vihera?"

"Oh? Do you think I need to be prepared?"

"Ah... I see. Yeah, I guess so."

Come to think of it, Vihera had been unconscious until yesterday.

She'd been brought to this inn in that state, so unlike Elena or Marina, she didn't have any particular belongings.

Of course, the inn might have provided necessary tools since she arrived last night, but she probably didn't intend to carry those back with her.

"So, Rei's at the stables?"

"Yeah. I figured I'd check on Set while I'm at it."

The horses pulling the carriage don't seem frightened by Set.

Partly because it had undergone rigorous training since it was young, but more significantly, it had met Set many times before.

While he didn't think there was much to worry about on that front, he still thought it best to be cautious...

"Set? Well, then, can I come along too? Yesterday, with everything going on, I didn't get to spend much time with him."

"Ah... I suppose so."

Yesterday had been a whirlwind: Vihera waking up, immediately followed by dismantling the Silver Lion, destroying the dungeon's core, reporting to the guild, and eating at the Stewed Orc Inn... it truly deserved to be called a torrent of events.

Of course, during the celebratory feast at the Oak Stew Tavern, Vihera chatted with everyone else, including Johanna and the others.

But... even that conversation paled in comparison to the dishes made with Silver Lion meat.

She'd been talking with Rei and the others in the dining hall until just now, but she wanted to talk with Set. That's what Vihera must have thought.

"If you wanted to talk to Set, you didn't need to wait for me. Set's pretty docile, even with just Vihera, right?"

It wasn't uncommon for monsters, animals, or even pets to bare their fangs without warning at anyone other than their owner.

But Set possessed an exceptionally high intelligence, even understanding human speech.

Given that, and the fact that Set was attached to Vihera, there really was no need for her to wait for him like this.

At Rei's words, Vihera opened her mouth, her expression slightly exasperated.

"Rei, you really ought to study women's hearts a bit more."

"...Did I do something wrong?"

Rei tilted his head at Vihera's words. Those around them, who had been quietly watching—or perhaps eavesdropping—on their exchange, felt a surge of frustration. They silently sympathized with Vihera, thinking of what lay ahead for her, all because of Rei's utter cluelessness.

"No, I know telling you this sort of thing won't do much good. More importantly, come on, let's hurry to where Set is."

Rising from the sofa, Vihera took Rei's hand and led him out of the inn.

...Among those watching them leave, quite a few men felt jealousy toward Rei, though naturally, Rei didn't notice.

"Grrrrrrrrr!"

The moment they entered the stable, Set must have known who had arrived. Or perhaps he had sensed them even before they entered. He purred happily.

The other horses in the stable whinnied slightly, as if confused by the sound, but Set seemed completely oblivious. He kept urging Rei and Vihera onward, as if to say, "Hurry up, hurry up!"

"He's pretty excited for just yesterday,"

"It must be the silver lion meat, right?"

Hearing that, Rei recalled the effect eating Silver Lion meat had on him and nodded in agreement... or rather, the moment he thought about it, he wanted to eat a dish made with Silver Lion meat again.

(Delicious... no, food that's too delicious probably comes with its own set of problems.)

His gaze shifted to the Misty Ring encircling his right wrist, reminding him of the large quantity of silver lion meat still stored there.

Especially for Rei, since he could eat dishes made with silver lion meat anytime he wanted, his mind was probably drawn to it more often than the others'.

"Gururu? Gururururu!"

Set growls, demanding to play.

Set smiled at him and gently reached out to stroke his body.

"It wasn't just Rei and the others... Set fought for me too. ...Thank you."

"Guru!"

Set shifted slightly in response to Vihera's words of thanks.

It was as if he wanted to say that fighting for Vihera was only natural...

Understanding what Set meant, Vihera once again felt gratitude toward him and gently stroked him.

Rei, too, smiled happily at the gesture Set had shown through his attitude and reached out his hand.

"We're leaving here today, so once we're outside, let's play to our hearts' content. ...If only we had a Frisbee."

"Frisbee? What's that? Some kind of magic item?"

Vihera tilted her head at the unfamiliar word.

"Ah... well. In the world I was in before coming here, it was common... yeah, probably a toy commonly used to play with pets."

If anything, Rei remembered using it more with friends than pets, though he'd seen shows on TV where people threw Frisbees for dogs to catch.

(Dogs and Set... No, Set has the lower body of a lion, so he's feline, right? But his personality is definitely closer to a dog's.)

Set's form was undoubtedly a griffin... his lower body was that of a lion, but to Rei, it was an indisputable fact that his personality was that of a dog.

In fact, when Rei strokes Set's neck right now, he purrs happily.

(Huh? Wasn't it cats that enjoyed having their throats stroked?)

While Rei pondered this, Vihera, likely intrigued by the name "Frisbee," spoke again.

"That Frisbee thing—can't you make one?"

"I wonder..."

Rei had played with the Frisbee back in Japan, and it was made of plastic.

But naturally, there was no such thing as plastic in this Elgin.

(Then, wooden? A wooden Frisbee... Since there are wooden boomerangs, a wooden Frisbee should be possible. The problem is how to shape it like that.)

A Frisbee is circular in shape.

In this world without machinery, how could one possibly shape something like a Frisbee?

Even Rei couldn't figure that out.

(Wait, there are dwarves here. If they have that kind of technology... maybe?)

The thought suddenly occurred to him, and he considered looking for a dwarf to make a Frisbee once he returned to Gilm.

He didn't know if boomerangs existed in Elgin.

But Rei had traveled to various towns and villages, starting with Gilm, and had never seen one.

Therefore, even if boomerangs did exist, he assumed they'd be limited to only a very small area...

(Well, since boomerangs are supposedly hunting tools, maybe I should suggest them to hunters?)

Rei was lost in thoughts about boomerangs, but when he actually considered whether hunting with one was feasible, he couldn't help but tilt his head in doubt.

If you're hunting prey with a boomerang, using a bow and arrow would be far more reliable.

At the very least, in terms of lethality, bows and arrows were overwhelmingly superior.

"Rei? What's wrong?"

"Nothing. I was just thinking frisbees seem hard to make."

"Oh?"

Nodding at Vihera's puzzled words, Rei began explaining what a Frisbee actually was.

You carve the wood into a perfectly round circle without any warping, then hollow out the inside.

Just hearing that description, even Vihera could easily imagine how difficult it would be.

"Looks hard to make, doesn't it?"

"Yeah. I don't think it'd be possible to just ask some random craftsman around here to make one."

But conversely, that also meant a craftsman with the proper skill could make one.

Rei, catching the hidden meaning in Vihera's words, nodded in understanding.

(But whether it's a Frisbee or a boomerang, they're basically cheap items. If a top-tier craftsman made one, it'd clearly be a loss. Maybe turn it into a work of art with jewels? No, even if not jewels, a Frisbee made of stone or metal would be too heavy.)

Though he thought the Frisbee would be perfect as a toy for Set, he decided it wasn't worth going to such lengths to obtain and gave up on the idea.

Of course, if he truly wanted it, he wouldn't hesitate to spend the money.

After all, I'd just acquired the Silver Lion's material.

"Lord Rei, Lord Vihera, you were here?"

As I stroked Set alongside Vihera, that voice suddenly echoed around us.

Rei and Vihera turned their gaze toward the source of the voice and, unsurprisingly, saw the figure of Ara.

"What's up?"

"We're ready to leave now. We've settled the inn's bill, and once we confirm Rei and the others are ready, we just need to prepare the carriage and we can depart."

Her slightly annoyed expression was likely because Rei had left Elena behind to be with Vihera.

It wasn't that Ara harbored romantic feelings for Rei.

Well, she did have feelings for him, but they weren't romantic feelings. They were feelings best described as friendship.

That's precisely why she could support her best friend and master, Elena's love, without hesitation.

"I see. Then I'll finish getting ready too. ...Set, let's go."

"Grr!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a short growl and exited the stable.

Leaving Ara behind to prepare the carriage, Rei, Vihera, and Set—two people and one horse—headed for the inn.

There, as expected, stood Elena and Marina. Seeing Vihera with Rei, both pairs of eyes immediately focused on Vihera.

Though Vihera was met with questioning looks, she herself seemed completely unconcerned, smiling as she spoke.

"Did I keep you waiting a bit?"

"Well, yes. I think we waited quite a bit. Honestly, I don't think it's right for you to just disappear without a trace like that."

Elena's words carried a slight edge, but Vihera merely shrugged her shoulders.

...Merchants who happened to be leaving the inn saw Vihera's expression and, naturally, stumbled and fell right there.

Worse still, the other merchants behind them tripped over the first one and fell down too.

Amidst this commotion, Elena and the others waited for the carriage... and headed toward Gilm.
