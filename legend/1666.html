At the word "Guztos," spoken by Bistol, Rei turns his gaze toward him.

“Do you know this monster?”

“I’ve only heard a little about it, though. I mean, a monster with such impressive horns and claws is incredibly rare, isn’t it?”

“I won’t deny that.”

For a crustacean monster, having claws isn’t particularly surprising.

But for a fish-like monster to have claws is quite rare.

On top of that, if it has a magnificent horn like a unicorn’s, it’s not hard to identify what kind of monster it is based on those characteristics alone.

……Of course, since we don’t have a complete catalog of every monster species, we can’t completely rule out the possibility that it’s an entirely new species.

“So? What kind of monster is this? Specifically, what parts can be sold for materials, or eaten? Any info like that?”

“Hmm… Like I said before, this is the first time I’ve seen this monster, so the info I have is secondhand. Is that okay?”

“Yeah. It’s better to have some information—evenhand or not—than to be completely in the dark.”

With Rei putting it that way, Bistol—who had been brought here thanks to her kindness—felt he had no choice but to tell her.

“In terms of raw combat power, it’s not that strong, but since it’s pretty smart, it’s classified as a Rank D monster.”

"Ah... I see. That makes sense."

Even when it attacked Rei and Set, it made full use of the ocean—its natural habitat.

Seeing that, you couldn’t possibly call him stupid.

“Though, for someone who’s supposedly smart, he did go and attack Set.”

"It’s a sea monster, after all. It probably figured that if push came to shove, it could escape to the depths and be absolutely safe."

“I can’t deny that.”

In fact, if Guztos escaped to the depths of the sea, it wouldn’t be impossible for Rei and Set to catch him… though it would certainly be a struggle.

They’d have to dive into the sea to chase it, or use Rei’s magic to turn the area around Guztos into boiling water.

Either way, they’d undoubtedly suffer significant damage—though perhaps not enough to call it “significant”—compared to what they’d gain.

“Anyway, this monster is actually pretty rare. From what I’ve heard, its horn fetches a pretty high price as an alchemical ingredient. …Of course, it’s nowhere near as valuable as a real unicorn’s horn.”

“Well, that’s true.”

It is said that a unicorn’s horn cures all ills… and in some cases, can even bring the dead back to life.

Of course, Rei wouldn’t go so far as to fully believe that legend, but there’s no doubt it can be used as a highly effective healing potion.

Even if Guztos’s horn doesn’t quite measure up to a unicorn’s, it still possesses sufficient healing properties.

“For now, I’ll keep the horn just in case, instead of selling it. Anything else?”

“The shell part is pretty hard, so I hear it can be used for armor? Though, it seems to be more popular as food than that.”

“So, we’ll eat the claws ourselves for now. We don’t have any use for the shell, so we’ll sell that to the guild.”

“Just so you know, make sure to wash it thoroughly after eating the meat inside, okay? Otherwise, they’ll try to lowball you.”

As a merchant, Bistol probably couldn’t stand the idea of such valuable ingredients being bought for a pittance just because of poor handling.

Still, the fact that he didn’t ask me to sell them to him… maybe it’s because Bistol doesn’t have a sales channel for Guztos’s claws.

"I see. ...So, where are the parts for the hunt certificate?"

“Hmm, from what I’ve heard, there’s a sharp spine at the tip of the tail, so that’s probably it. Oh, by the way, I hear that spine can also be bought as a material. Apparently, if you forge it using a special method, it makes for a sewing needle that lasts quite a long time.”

“...A sewing needle, is it?”

If it’s going to be a needle, couldn’t it be used as a weapon for Byune?

That was what Rei thought, but Bistol silently shook her head.

“Hmm…”

Seeing Bistol like that, Byune—who had been listening while petting Yellow nearby—muttered with a hint of disappointment.

If someone hadn’t known Byune for very long, they probably wouldn’t have noticed anything different about her even after seeing her like that.

All things considered, Rei had known Byune for quite some time.

That was precisely why, even if not to the same extent as Vihera, he could vaguely understand what Byune was trying to say.

Seeing Byune looking slightly disappointed, Rei suddenly recalled a magic item he hadn’t used much lately.

The Eye of Nebula still hanging at his waist.

Maybe he should let Byune try using it, he thought. That’s what he thought.

“Byune, can I ask you something?”

Putting Guztos’s explanation aside for the moment, Rei removed Nebra’s Eye from his belt and held it out to Byune.

“Why don’t you try using this Nebula’s Eye for a bit? Throwing long needles is your fighting style, Byune, but with long needles, there’s always the issue of remaining ammo… well, maybe that’s not quite the right way to put it, but you know what I mean, right? With this, as long as you have magic power, you don’t have to worry about running out of ammo.”

“……Hm?”

Looking slightly puzzled, Byune takes the Nebula Eye.

Bistol and the others watched Rei and Byune in silence.

It was probably because everyone present understood that speaking up in the current situation would be tactless.

“It’s a magic item called Nebula’s Eye… well, have you ever seen me use it?”

“Hmm.”

Byune nodded in response to Rei’s words.

Seeing this, Rei figured no further explanation was needed and urged Byune to try using Nebula’s Eye.

Seeing Rei’s reaction, Byune channeled her magic into Nebula’s Eye… but nothing happened.

Normally, when magical power is channeled into Nebula’s Eye, an arrowhead should be generated there.

Since it was a magic-made arrowhead, it would have disappeared after a certain amount of time, but there was absolutely no sign of it being created.

“...Why is that? Sorry, Byune. Let me take a look.”

Saying that, Byune readily returned Nebra’s eye to Rei.

When he channeled his magic into Nebra’s eye, an arrowhead materialized there as if it were the most natural thing in the world.

“It’s not broken. …Which means…”

“Rei, can I borrow it for a moment?”

Marina said this to Rei, who was holding Nebra’s eye and tilting his head in confusion, and took the eye from him.

She then channeled magic into Nebra’s eye… and upon seeing the arrowhead materialize, she wore an expression of understanding.

“You know, it takes a considerable amount of magic just to create a single arrowhead, right? Unfortunately, I don’t think you’ll be able to use it with just your magic, Byune.”

Hearing this, Rei recalled the time the Nebra’s Eye was crafted for her in the Bestir Empire.

Hadn’t the craftsman who made them said that a considerable amount of magical power was needed to activate Nebra’s Eye?

Even in its original state as a quiver, it required a considerable amount of magical power to produce arrows.

But by transforming it into a separate magic item—Nebula’s Eye—it had become impossible to generate arrowheads without consuming even more magical power.

In other words, this is a magic item that only someone with a great deal of magical power can master.

……That said, within the Crimson Wings, both Vihera and Marina possess high magical power, and in fact, Marina was able to activate Nebula’s Eye just like this.

In other words, within the Crimson Wings, only Byune is unable to use Nebula’s Eye.

“……Sorry about that.”

“Mm.”

In response to Rei’s words, Byune shook her head, indicating she didn’t mind.

In truth, for Byune, the magic item known as the Nebula’s Eye was convenient to have, but it wasn’t something he absolutely couldn’t do without.

……Of course, since it’s an expensive magic item, to be honest, I’d want it if I could get it.

“Come on, forget that. What about Guztos? Aren’t we going to take him apart?”

“No, we will. …Come to think of it, I heard the meat from the Scissors is delicious, but what about the rest of the body?”

“Of course it’s delicious. It’s a monster of a certain rank, so that goes without saying. …Though, to be honest, I haven’t actually eaten it myself—I just heard it secondhand—so there’s a chance it might not actually be that good.”

Basically, the higher a monster’s rank, the tastier its meat becomes.

That’s because magic power affects the flavor of the meat.

Of course, there are exceptions to every rule.

The most famous example of this is probably the orc.

They’re by no means high-rank monsters, but their meat tastes just as good as high-rank monsters’—and in some cases, even better.

(Guztos is also a Rank D monster, the same rank as an orc. I wonder how it compares to an orc... Though, an orc is meat and Guztos is fish. It’s probably hard to make a clear judgment about the difference in taste.)

Looking at the Guztos, Rei pulls out a butcher’s knife.

“Which parts of the innards can be used as materials?”

“Hmm… I’m not really sure about that. If you’re really worried, why not put them in a container and store them just in case? In Rei’s item box, things like innards won’t rot, right?”

“That’s true.”

Inner organs are generally prone to spoiling.

But with Rei’s Misty Ring, bringing them back isn’t a difficult task.

"Internal organs like these make great bait for attracting fish and such. Still, since I don’t know what kind of materials they’ll turn into, I’ll just save them all for now."

No one objected to Rei’s suggestion.

If the monster were listed in the Monster Compendium, they could check for themselves whether its innards could be used as materials, but since it wasn’t listed, they couldn’t make that judgment on their own.

If they carelessly threw away the innards here, only to find out later that they were actually quite rare materials... that would definitely be no laughing matter.

"Yeah. I think that’s the best course of action, too."

Rei shot Bistol a disapproving look at his remark, which sounded as if he were praising her for doing a good job.

It wasn’t that she disliked Bisturu, but for some reason, she felt like she was being treated like a child.

...Actually, considering Bistoll—who could be described as a burly, muscular giant—it wasn’t entirely unreasonable for him to view Rei as a child.

“Anyway, I’ll save the rest of the monster dissection for tonight. Right now, we need to catch as many fish as we can.”

He took this action precisely because he feared that if he stayed here any longer, Bistrol might get the wrong idea.

After stowing Guztos in the Misty Ring, I attached Nebra’s Eye—which I’d received from Marina—to my waist, then approached Set, who was playing with Yellow while being petted by Byune.

“Growl?”

"Are we going back to the sea?" Set let out a meow, and Rei nodded while petting him.

“Next time, I want to catch fish instead of monsters.”

“Grrrr!”

Since Set also likes fish, he purrs in agreement with Rei’s words.

“We’ve been catching a decent number of fish here, so instead of heading out to sea, why don’t you try fishing here?”

“It’s a nice offer from Elena, but when I think about what happens when we get back from the sea, I need to catch as many fish as possible. Not these small ones that come right up to the shore, but big ones like the ones I caught yesterday.”

As for Rei, he wanted to catch huge fish like bluefin tuna in large quantities, rather than fish like sharks.

Just like in Japan, where freezing technology hadn’t advanced yet, tuna was basically treated as a low-grade fish in this world as well.

That said, Rei only calls it tuna because it looks like the fish he’s seen on TV; he doesn’t actually know if it really is tuna.

However, when he ate it at Emoshion, he was certain it was tuna.

That’s exactly why he’s out here chasing tuna with Set.

Mind you, I don’t even know if bluefin tuna actually swim around here, so I was just acting on the assumption that if they’re here, that’s good enough.

“Set, count on you.”

“Grrrrr!”

At Rei’s words as she leaped onto his back, Set let out a growl, took a few steps to build up speed, flapped his wings, and soared into the sky.

Even on rocky ground, Set’s speed doesn’t waver.

With Set’s feet, he wouldn’t get hurt even if he stepped on a rock by accident.

“If we catch a big fish, Set can eat his fill too.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set purred happily and headed out to sea.
