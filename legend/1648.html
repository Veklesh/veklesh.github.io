"Yaaaaaaah!"

Early in the morning, a woman’s spirited shout echoes through the neighborhood.

In her hand is a spear.

It was a weapon prepared by Gimekara, but the spearhead was covered, so even if it pierced her, it wouldn’t be fatal.

Since the woman was charging forward with all her might, it was certain that even with the protective cover on the tip, she would sustain at least some injury, even if it wasn’t fatal.

……Though, of course, that’s only if she actually hits her target.

“You’re being too lenient.”

Elena muttered briefly, then gave Mirage a light flick. That alone was enough to entangle the spear’s tip, and in the next instant, the spear the woman had been gripping so firmly was snatched upward and hurled into the air.

And before the woman knew it, the tip of Mirage was thrust right in front of her eyes.

“Huh?”

Completely baffled by what had just happened, a small gasp escaped the woman’s lips.

Pulling the blade away from the woman’s face, Elena pointed out what she had realized during the sequence of events.

“Stabbing with a spear is fine, but if your speed is slow, your weapon will be taken from you this easily. To prevent that from happening, you need to move faster.”

“……Yes.”

Among those who had been forced into prostitution, this woman possessed a fair amount of combat talent.

Of course, that was only relative to the other women; if asked whether she could truly hold her own against an adventurer, the answer would be no.

However, the adventurers serving as this group’s escorts were hired more for their temperament—specifically, their ability to avoid causing trouble—than for their fighting skills, so if they engaged in a mock battle with an adventurer, they could hold their own to some extent.

Some of them even harbored a strange sense of confidence because of this… and in that sense, the woman fighting Elena was one of them.

Averting her gaze from the dejected woman, Elena looked at the other members fighting around her.

Elena, Vihera... and even the women who were being soundly beaten by Byune—every single one of them was someone who had grown arrogant from recent training.

I want that arrogance crushed.

That was the request Rei received from Rocks two days after they had joined up with Rocks’ group.

Thanks to Rei and the others joining them, the women—who had been suffering from what might be called “Set deficiency syndrome”—had recovered, and since they began traveling by carriage, the journey had been proceeding smoothly without any particular problems.

What the women were particularly delighted about was, of course, Rei’s sand ship.

Not having to sleep outdoors or in the cramped carriage was truly welcome news for the women.

Moreover, with Rei around, they could eat meals other than preserved food thanks to the Misty Ring, and this time, Gilm had brought quite a few supplies—such as fresh fruit and dried fruit with concentrated sweetness—that the women would love.

It would be no exaggeration to call this a blessing from heaven.

“Hmm, if we keep this up, by the time we reach Gilm, we won’t be amateurs anymore. Of course, I don’t think we should get too cocky.”

“Ah… thank you.”

Holding her Mirage, Elena muttered these words, and the woman holding the spear bowed her head.

Until yesterday… no, until she began training, she had believed that fighting wasn’t all that difficult. But now that she’s been shown so starkly just how limited her own abilities truly are, she likely has no desire to resist.

She bowed her head meekly at Elena’s words.

(Should I say she was lucky to be defeated by me rather than by Byune?)

Her gaze falls upon the woman being mercilessly attacked by Byune, who is holding not his usual White Cloud, but an ordinary dagger—still sheathed, no less.

The woman is trying her best to block Byune’s attacks with her longsword, but in terms of raw physical ability, she is no match for Byune at all. Furthermore, while Byune wields a dagger—which, though it has a short reach, is highly maneuverable—the woman’s weapon is a longsword.

If the woman had superior strength, she might have been able to wield the longsword as freely as a dagger, but unfortunately, her strength was nothing out of the ordinary.

Her skills have improved somewhat compared to before thanks to her continued combat training... but ultimately, that’s all it amounts to, and she isn’t skilled enough to wield the longsword with the same freedom as a dagger.

As a result, she managed to block Byune’s attack…

“Yikes!”

That was the limit of her ability to block the attack, and soon she let out a small scream and fell flat on her butt.

“Hmm.”

As usual, Byune addressed the woman with a single word, but unfortunately, since Vihera wasn’t there, the woman—who hadn’t known Byune for very long—had no idea what he was saying.

That said, she seems to realize she isn’t being praised, and reflects on how she’d been getting a bit too full of herself.

Unlike Elena and Byune, Vihera—who fully understands Byune’s words—is handling several women all by herself.

“That’s no good. Not good at all. You really should work out a bit more.”

The women’s attacks come one after another, but not a single one even grazes Vihera.

She’s wearing an outfit similar to what the women wore back in Mejogo… no, an even more revealing and provocative one, and though the fabric flutters in the wind, not even the fabric itself is hit by their attacks.

To top it off, Vihera, who is dodging the attacks, shows no sign of panic whatsoever and maintains a cool, composed expression.

Her movements were so graceful that it would be more fitting to describe them as dancing rather than simply dodging enemy attacks.

Vihera, dancing like that under the summer sun, possessed a kind of ethereal beauty.

“Haa, haa, haa…”

“Why, why won’t they hit me!”

“Just a little more, just a little more…”

The women who had been attacking Vihera eventually reached their physical limits, collapsing onto the ground and gasping for breath.

“If you want to touch me, you’d better get a little stronger first.”

Seeing Vihera, who hadn’t even broken a sweat, the women realized just how overconfident they’d been.

A short distance away from Vihera... a slightly different scene unfolded.

“That’s right. When you draw a bow, you pull it all the way back in one go. If you try to pull it little by little, that’s exactly when you’ll need even more strength.”

Beside Marina, who spoke those words, nearly twenty women held bows, aimed at the target tree in front of them, and released the arrows they had nocked.

However, while using a bow is one thing, hitting the target accurately is quite difficult.

For women who haven’t trained much, it must be quite a challenge.

Still, unlike elsewhere, this wasn’t a competitive match; Marina was thoroughly teaching them how to use the bow.

The reason for this difference was simply that the women learning archery here didn’t possess the combat talent of the women who had been soundly defeated by Elena and her group.

...To be precise, it would be more accurate to say they lacked talent for close-quarters combat using weapons like longswords or spears.

However, this group numbers nearly a thousand.

Figuring they might as well take advantage of the sheer numbers, Gimekara decided to equip them with bows.

Of course, it would be difficult to procure a large number of high-quality bows.

But since everyone here was using a bow for the first time, cheap beginner’s bows were more than sufficient.

And so, they were currently in the midst of training, using the large number of bows that had been gathered.

(With bows, you do need to get used to them, but unlike close combat, they don’t require any particular talent. Sniping is a different story, but when it comes to firing a barrage of arrows to create a wall of fire, as long as they can draw the bowstring, that’s good enough for now. And as for the arrows needed for training, as long as Rei is here, that’s not a problem either.)

While instructing on archery, Marina glanced at Rei, who was training Rocks.

The women, who would normally be the ones being guarded, launched a simultaneous attack with their bows—though not quite as skillfully as professionals.

And they do so while riding in carriages, firing from the windows and the driver’s seat.

If only one or two arrows were flying at them, someone with a certain level of skill could probably dodge or cut them down.

But when the number of arrows reaches the hundreds, it’s a different story.

While a few elite fighters like Rei might manage, the bandits in this area have no means of dealing with such a barrage.

(If we didn’t have to worry about arrow consumption, we might actually be able to make it work as a mercenary group. …Or maybe that’s still impossible.)

Marina immediately dismissed her own thought.

To operate as a mercenary group, various supplies are needed in addition to arrows.

The only ones who can currently provide those supplies are Gimekara and Rei… because they have the Zolgue Trading Company and Daskar, the lord of Gilm, backing them.

It was precisely because they weren’t concerned with profitability that they were able to do this.

(Though, I suppose any bandits who attack us before we reach Gilm will just get a rough time. Until then, we don’t need to worry about arrows.)

With that in mind, Marina resumed instructing the women practicing archery.

Meanwhile, some distance away from Marina and the others...

“Gaaaah!”

Shouting with all his might, Rocks swung his longsword at Rei.

Though the act of swinging a longsword was the same, the speed, the sharpness, the power behind it… all of it was different from the women training in the distance.

Anyone could tell just by seeing the sharpness of Rocks’s longsword that what the women were doing was still not much different from that of amateurs.

But at the same time... even with such skill, Rox’s longsword cannot break through the defense of Rei’s Twilight Spear.

The scene unfolding before them is the exact opposite of how Elena had completely shut down the woman wielding the spear by using a longsword-shaped Mirage.

That said, while Elena effortlessly disarmed her opponent by entangling her blade around the spearhead, Rei is merely deflecting the longsword wielded by Rocks with his Twilight Spear.

If he were told to do the same thing Elena did, he could probably pull off a similar feat even against Rocks.

However, it’s also true that wouldn’t be the kind of training Rocks was looking for.

That is why they were currently engaged in this back-and-forth exchange.

“More! Pick up the pace! You should be able to land a strike with even more speed!”

“Damn it, he’s just saying whatever he wants… Gaaaaah!”

As if guided by Rei’s words and actions, the speed of the longsword Rox wielded began to increase.

It may be only a slight increase, but in actual combat, that small difference can mean the difference between life and death.

A difference of just a few centimeters could mean an attack that should have missed actually hits the enemy.

That’s precisely why even the slightest increase in speed—even the slightest improvement in landing attacks on the enemy—becomes a powerful advantage.

...Though, of course, that doesn’t mean he could increase his attack speed that quickly.

In that regard, Rei was deflecting Rox’s attacks at just the right moments, calling out to him, and gradually—little by little—increasing his speed without him noticing.

Rox probably understands Rei’s strategy, but even so, his speed kept increasing as if he were being pulled along by Rei’s actions.

Even so, Rox’s attacks still fail to reach Rei.

No matter how skilled an adventurer Rox may be, he cannot sustain a full-power attack indefinitely.

He had been swinging at Rei nonstop, but eventually, his stamina—and above all, his breath—reached their limits.

The speed of the longsword he was swinging dropped sharply, then stopped...

“Gah, haa, haa, haa…”

Rocks’ face turned bright red from the struggle to breathe; he could no longer stand and collapsed to the ground, gasping for air.

That ragged breathing was proof that Rocks had pushed his body to its absolute limit.

“I’d say your swordplay is pretty sharp, actually. But if you’d just taken a step deeper…”

“Haa, haa, haa… Don’t compare me to a monster like you. That was all I could manage right now. …Haa, haa.”

He must have spoken too soon, before his breathing had fully settled. Rocks immediately began gasping for air again, trying to catch his breath.

“If you’re an adventurer from Gilm, you’d better be able to do at least that much.”

“S-since when… huff, huff… did ‘Gilm adventurer’ and ‘non-human’ become synonymous… phew.”

Perhaps having finally caught his breath while speaking, Rocks sat up, propping himself up on his elbows from where he’d been lying.

A chorus of cheers erupted for Rocks.

The voices came from the women who had been watching the mock battle from a short distance away.

While many harbored a sense of disgust toward men due to the events in Mejogo, not everyone felt that way.

Moreover, while Rocks’s words and actions are no different from those of an ordinary man, he rarely casts lustful glances at women.

...Though "almost" doesn't mean "never"—after all, Rocks is still a man.

“Look, there are people cheering you on, so give it a little more effort.”

“...You... you monster of stamina...”

Even as he said that, Rocks stood up.

No matter what, the chance to spar with an adventurer who has a nickname doesn’t come along very often.

So, Rock’s honest feeling was that he wasn’t about to let such a golden opportunity slip away.

And so, the combat training continued until Rocks could no longer move.
