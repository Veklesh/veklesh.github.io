That day, Daskar was processing documents in his office as usual when the butler entered and delivered a message, earning him a skeptical look.

"Is that true?"

"Yes. Lord Keep and Lord Muet are here."

"...That damn noble. Causing trouble again. How many times is this now?"

"And Rei is here too..."

"Huh? Rei? What do you mean Rei is coming? ...Damn it, that bloody noble dragged Rei into this? He never fails to make things complicated. Just to be sure, is Keep injured?"

"None to speak of. He's perfectly healthy."

"Hard to tell if I should be glad or not. If he'd lost an arm or a leg, maybe he'd finally settle down."

Daskar spat out the words irritably.

It hadn't been that long since Keep had arrived at Gilm.

But in that short time, Keep had caused trouble countless times.

Each time, guards, knights, and even Daskar himself had to deal with him.

If he were just some noble with no backing, they wouldn't need to be so careful.

But this noble had been sent here by the royalist faction as a check.

If they made a misstep, it was certain they would be called out on it.

And above all, the most critical factor was Rei's involvement.

While Daskar treated him amicably, that was partly because Daskar was exceptionally unconventional for a noble, and partly because he favored Rei.

But fundamentally, Rei's character could be described as harsh.

Even during the Spring War, when facing aristocrats loyal to the aristocratic faction, Rei wielded his power without hesitation.

What would happen if such a Rei met Keep, the epitome of a corrupt noble?

In that sense, it's sheer luck that Keep hasn't suffered a single injury yet.

"Damn it, even as a check, sending such an idiot over—what the hell are they thinking? Someone like Duke Quent would send a more suitable person. So, it's probably a renegade from the King's faction."

Even within the Kingdom of Mireana, the royalist faction is the largest.

Precisely because of that, the royalist faction itself is further divided into several distinct power groups.

Daskar had suspected that someone like Keep had been sent due to these conflicting interests, but since that wouldn't change the current situation, dwelling on it now was nothing but escapism.

"Who'd have thought they'd set their sights on Baron Sisne. Well, fine. Since he's come this far, I suppose I can't very well refuse to meet him. Especially since he looks down on me anyway."

Normally, a Marquis holds a higher rank than a Count.

Furthermore, Kip was merely the heir apparent to the Eliell Count's house, while Daskar was the current head of the Larks Marquis house.

Considering that, Kip shouldn't be able to take a strong stance against Daskar. Yet, he didn't seem to care at all, treating Daskar as if he were his superior.

(So, the royalists... or rather, whoever sent that fool wants me to make a move. What a troublesome thing to do.)

Sighing inwardly, he knew he couldn't stay here forever.

He stood up and, guided by his secretary, headed for the reception room.

He arrived at the reception room and, seeing who was there, let out a small sigh of relief.

It was the worst possible combination: Rei and Keep. Even though the butler had said they didn't appear to be injured, he couldn't truly relax until he saw it with his own eyes.

In this regard, Daskar could be said to understand Rei quite well.

Glancing back into the reception room, he saw a fat man and three knights beside Keep, while Rei and a maid stood beside Muet.

Perhaps the butler who prepared the room had been considerate; the sofas in the reception room were placed at a distance from each other.

Seeing Daskar enter the room, Keep stood up with an arrogant smile and spoke.

"Ah, Earl of Larks. You've finally arrived. I'm not impressed that you kept me waiting, but never mind that now. More importantly, please hear me out."

"Hold on, Lord Keep. Calm down a bit. ...Have tea brought."

Daskar deflected Keep's immediate demands and ordered the butler to bring tea.

After watching the butler leave the room, Daskar turned his attention back to those present and spoke once more.

"Now then, normally I'd prefer to hear this over tea, but it seems we don't have much time for that. Let's hear the story again."

"Hmm. To put it simply, that poor baron over there and I have agreed to a duel over that maid. I'd like to ask the Marquis of Larks to handle the legal formalities for that duel."

"...What?"

At Keep's words, Daskar turned his gaze toward Muet.

The Muet Daskar knew was a timid man, but now he stood with a resolute demeanor.

The maid stood somewhat uneasily by Muet's side, holding back...

(Rei, huh)

Finally, Daskar's gaze shifted to Rei standing behind Muet, as if to say he was Muet's escort.

The knights standing behind Keep occasionally shot hostile glances at Rei. Considering Rei's personality, Daskar could somewhat understand why.

"It's a legal formality. This penniless baron wants to make sure he can't make any ugly excuses if he loses."

"...I see."

While nodding, Daskar's honest inner thought was, 'Is this man sane?'

Rei standing beside Muet meant one thing: Rei was clearly signaling his allegiance to Muet.

If he intended to fight with Rei as an enemy, a Rank B adventurer of Rei's caliber wouldn't suffice.

An A-rank adventurer... and one on par with Elk, nicknamed the Axe of the Thunder God, would be necessary.

But whether such a person would side with Keep, a noble who was bad in every sense of the word, was something Daskar couldn't help but question.

(Does he have some strategy to win? Surely, being in Gilm, he must know about Rei's existence.)

Seeing Keep's overconfident demeanor, Daskar found it puzzling.

Admittedly, identifying Rei as the adventurer known as Crimson on first meeting would be difficult without Death Scythe or Set's presence.

And when Keep saw Rei, Set wasn't near him. When Rei came to the lord's manor, he'd been told to wait at Muet's residence before arriving, so Keep and the others genuinely hadn't realized what kind of person Rei truly was.

And there was no need for Death Scythe against the knights following Keep, so that great scythe wouldn't be needed either.

"What's the matter? This is a legal proceeding! Pro-ce-di-ng!"

Though Daskar felt irritation building inside at Keep's insistence, emphasizing each syllable, he managed to suppress the urge to shout and spoke.

"So, does Muet agree to this duel?"

"Yes. Honestly, I don't have confidence in my own fighting ability, but he said he'd fight as my proxy."

"...I see. And you, Keep, agree to the duel?"

"Didn't I say of course? More importantly, could you hurry up with the formalities?"

"Then next, the items each party demands from the other."

Daskar announced, suppressing his irritation, and Keep opened his mouth as if he'd been waiting for it.

"If I win, I take that maid over there. If I lose, I hand over half the Count Eliel family's fortune. Right?"

"Yes."

"...Huh?"

Muet nodded at Keep's words.

Up to that point, there was no issue. But it was Daskar who let out a dumbfounded gasp at those words.

Could he have misheard? Clinging to that sliver of hope, he spoke up.

"I thought I heard you say that if Lord Keep loses, he'll hand over half the assets of the Count of Eriell family. Did I mishear?"

"No, that's correct. Exactly. To lure a lowly, penniless baron into a duel, that much bait is necessary, isn't it? Besides, I'll win anyway, so I won't have to pay. Absolutely no problem."

The first to lose her composure at Keep's words wasn't Muet... but Asie, standing behind her.

"Even if his title is lower, isn't that a bit disrespectful? You may indeed be descended from the Earl's bloodline. But ultimately, you're still not the head of the house, are you? Would you kindly refrain from making mocking remarks toward my master, who is a Baron?"

"...Oh? A mere maid has the audacity to speak so arrogantly to me, the next head of the Eriell Count's family? But precisely because you're such a woman, you'll be worth training once I make you mine. You should be grateful for my generosity, shouldn't you?"

Keep stared at Asie with a gaze that seemed to embody the very definition of a vulgar look. Yet, he seemed satisfied when Asie frowned in displeasure. He turned his gaze back to Daskar.

"So, Marquis of Larks. How much longer do I have to stand here like this? Could you hurry up and prepare the documents?"

"Indeed, Lord Keep, as the registered heir to the Count of Eriell House, you can use that fortune with some freedom. But even so, betting half your fortune on a duel without informing the head of the house... isn't that a bit reckless?"

"Ha! What are you talking about? Like I said before, there's no way I'd lose to some shabby, penniless noble like him. Relax, that wager is purely to ensure the man doesn't run away."

Though Keep didn't doubt his victory for a moment, Fultos, standing beside him, hesitated before speaking up.

"Lord Kipp, even so, betting half your fortune surely requires the master's approval... Besides, the opponent is just a single maid. She's hardly worth risking half your fortune..."

"What is it, Fultos? Do you think I'd lose to a man like this?"

"No, of course not. But..."

"Enough! When I say I'm doing it, I'm doing it! Now then, Marquis Larks! The documents!"

"...Final confirmation: you truly intend to wager half the assets of the Count of Eriell household, correct?"

At Daskar's words, Keep nodded immediately.

After confirming this, Daskar began writing on the documents.

Normally, Daskar might have been obligated to reveal Rei's true identity to Keep.

Of course, since that was an act of goodwill, not an obligation, Keep couldn't blame Daskar for not saying anything.

Even with Rei standing right before him, Keep was utterly certain of his victory.

Moreover, from Daskar's personal perspective, he had no genuine intention of stopping this contest.

Having followed the formal duel procedures this far, losing would undoubtedly destroy the face of the Count of Eriell family.

Moreover, having formally documented the transfer of half his estate, he could offer no excuses should he lose.

"Now, each of you place your blood seal. This completes the legal formalities for this duel."

They wrote down what each was staking on the documents. Mue and Kipp each took the knives handed to them, made a small cut on their thumbs, and pressed their blood seals onto the papers.

The moment both applied their blood seals, the document emitted a faint green glow.

(With the blood seals on the document and the formalities properly completed, there was no turning back from the duel now. To do so would mean forfeiting without a fight, forcing one to unconditionally hand over their wagered item to the opponent.)

Though Daskar gazed solemnly at those present, inside he was cheering triumphantly.

He had finally devised a way to legally expel Keep from Gilm—the troublemaker who had repeatedly caused him headaches.

If Keep won the duel, it would be a different story, but with Rei present, that was highly unlikely.

In other words, once this duel ended, Keep would have to return to his own lands to prepare half of the Earl of Eriell's estate.

If it were something simpler—gold coins or jewels, for instance—a letter to his father, the Earl of Eriell, stating the matter would suffice.

But when it came to half the estate, the man who accepted the challenge had no choice but to return home himself.

(Well, even if this guy disappears, they'll just send the next one right away...)

Feeling the excitement from mere seconds ago fade, he opened his mouth without letting it show on his face.

"Well then, the conditions are set. We'll need time to prepare our respective seconds. As for the date and time of the duel... how about five days from now, at the Knights' training grounds? Would that be acceptable?"

At Daskar's words, Keep nodded with a smile confident in his victory.

Muet also nodded, his expression serious.

Seeing both men nod, Daskar declared.

"Then, the duel shall take place at one o'clock in the afternoon, five days from today, at the knightly order's training grounds within this manor. Each may bring only one proxy. The contest shall be settled when the opponent surrenders, becomes incapacitated... or loses their life. The victor shall gain the right to claim whatever the opponent wagered. Any objections?"

Confirming no one objected to Daskar's words...

"Then the terms of the duel are settled. Conduct yourselves without regret,"

he declared.
