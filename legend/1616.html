Several days passed since Gimekara appeared before Rei and the others... During that time, they stopped at one village, but there was no sign of Gimekara, let alone horses, carriages, fodder, guards, food, clothing... None of the things Rei and the others needed had been prepared.

After all, what Rei and the others were demanding was provisions for a thousand men.

Rei understood it wasn't something that could be prepared immediately, as one might expect.

Moreover, the fact that they could now sleep inside the sand ships each night instead of camping outdoors was a significant factor. They no longer had to worry about rain or wind, insects, monsters, or bandits, allowing the group's fatigue to be properly alleviated.

Moreover, as their fatigue subsided, they were able to walk every day. This allowed many to grow accustomed to the walking and build up their stamina, leading to a smooth journey.

And so, when night fell that day, Rei and the others were naturally camping out as usual.

...Though, to be clear, Rei and the others used a magic tent, while the rest slept in the sand ship—hardly what one would typically imagine when hearing "sleeping outdoors."

Despite being an open-air camp, the fact they didn't have to worry about rain or wind... combined with the food Rei pulled from the Misty Ring and the exquisite water he conjured with his Running Water dagger to soothe the day's fatigue... it was the kind of "camping" that would make any traveler or adventurer angry if they heard it was just camping.

"Come to think of it, Gimekara still hasn't arrived."

"Probably things are turbulent over there too. I can't say I don't understand that feeling."

Elena said with a smile, savoring the fruit water in her cup.

"I did tell them that if they're too late, helping us over here won't mean much anymore. I figured they'd make a move sooner or later."

"Oh? From what you just said, did you think something would happen sooner, Rei?"

Despite saying "sooner or later," Marina, who had been listening to Rei and Elena's conversation, addressed Rei with that remark.

Hearing this, Rei nodded while eating a bite-sized piece of pear... or whatever fruit it was.

In truth, while he hadn't expected it the very next day, Rei had been certain that within a few days, the supplies—starting with the carriage—would be ready.

It was undoubtedly an optimistic prediction, but he still thought it was entirely possible.

Since it had turned out completely wrong, it was only natural that Rei felt a little disappointed.

"So, the Zolgei Trading Company never seriously intended to escape from the Kingdom of Reburina?"

"Not escape. Relocating their headquarters... their main store? That's what it was, right?"

"That's escape. Isn't it?"

When put that way, Rei couldn't deny it.

After all, based on Gimechara's explanation, escaping the Kingdom of Reburina was undoubtedly the most significant point.

But... precisely because of that, they should have been eager to supply Rei and the others with goods as quickly as possible, to make them indebted.

"Well, the Zolgea Trading Company is a major trading house even within the Kingdom of Reburina. Getting everyone to agree on something immediately... that sort of thing is probably difficult, isn't it?"

"True. Besides, even those who'd never traveled long distances before are getting used to it now, right? You know that, Rei?"

"Yeah. They've gotten good at butchering deer, bears, boars, and such too."

Set's first kill was a deer, but he'd since successfully hunted other animals like bears and boars.

At first, they were hesitant about butchering the animals, and some of the more refined women even vomited.

But considering what lay ahead, it was essential that as many people as possible learn to handle the task.

Some of the women had lived as hunters, so they took on the role of teachers, instructing others in animal butchering.

Naturally, the butchered game ended up as food in their stomachs.

Fur items were set aside to be sold when they stopped at villages and were currently stored in Rei's Misty Ring.

There wasn't that much, so it could have been left in the wagon... but considering what might happen, it was probably judged safer for Rei to keep it.

Through such tasks, a sense of solidarity and camaraderie had also begun to grow among the women.

Being organized into teams of ten certainly played a part, but above all, the shared experience of having been brainwashed and forced into prostitution was undoubtedly one reason they became close.

"I wonder if it'll rain tomorrow?"

Marina murmured, gazing up at the night sky.

Usually, even if there were some clouds, moonlight would shine through the gaps, illuminating the ground, and the stars would be visible.

But tonight, the sky was completely covered in clouds, hiding both the moon and the stars.

Considering the warm, humid breeze blowing, it wasn't strange for Marina to think it would rain tomorrow.

"Rain, huh... Since leaving Mejogo, we've been blessed with good weather, one way or another."

Of course, they had encountered rain during their journey.

Even so, the rain had lasted only a few tens of minutes... at most an hour at most, before easing off, never becoming a downpour that made continuing the journey impossible.

But considering the current situation, it was certain that tomorrow... no, it was certain that rain would fall as early as tonight.

The current signs pointed to rain with such certainty.

"What do we do if it rains? Light rain is fine, but..."

What if it rains hard... what if it pours down in what you'd call a downpour?

Marina asked him that, but Rei couldn't answer right away.

As Marina said, a little rain wouldn't be a problem for continuing.

But if they encountered rain heavy enough to be called a downpour, and since not everyone was wearing proper clothing yet, there was a risk of catching a cold.

Also, it's true that the horses pulling the carriage would find it physically demanding to keep going in the rain.

With horses already scarce, it would be unwise to waste their strength needlessly.

These circumstances were what made Rei hesitate about what to do next.

(I want to reach Gilm as soon as possible, but I want to avoid pushing too hard here and wasting even more time as a result. And while it's good that we've gotten used to walking, we've still been walking all day for the past few days. In that case, maybe it's okay to have a day to rest our bodies properly.)

Pushing too hard now would only delay their arrival at Gilm further.

Reaching that conclusion, Rei spoke up.

"If the rain looks heavy, let's take tomorrow off. Not everyone here is as seasoned a traveler as us, after all."

Marina breathed a sigh of relief at Rei's words.

Marina knew some of the walkers were starting to feel the fatigue build up.

If Rei had insisted on pushing forward here, Marina would undoubtedly have voiced her opposition.

After all, being the oldest among them, Marina naturally took on the role of the group's stabilizing force for Crimson Wings.

"Right. Personally, I think a lot of people are tired, and if it rains even a little tomorrow, we could take the day off. But I'll leave that decision to Rei. ...Oh?"

Mid-sentence, Marina looked up at the night sky.

Of course, with the moon and stars completely hidden by clouds, looking up at the night sky now was pointless.

Even so, the others immediately understood why Marina had looked up at the night sky.

"Rain, huh?"

Vihera murmured, her words echoing around them.

True to her words, small raindrops began falling from the night sky.

Rei, who had turned her palm upward, felt the raindrops landing on it. At the same time, it felt like an even warmer breeze than before had passed through the area.

"Let's head inside the magic tent for now. The rain will put out the fire on its own."

At Rei's words, everyone entered the magic tent.

However, to prevent thieves or pursuers like Jaya from sneaking in under cover of the rain, they didn't close the magic tent completely, leaving it open enough to see outside.

"Oh, looks like someone on the Sand Ship noticed the rain too."

A faint voice drifted from inside the sand ship.

Whether it was cheers of joy or sighs of rain-weariness... even Rei couldn't tell. Still, with the rain starting, Rei had already half-decided they'd take the whole next day off.

(If it were just a light drizzle or mist, we probably could push through the journey. But catching a cold would be no laughing matter.)

In that sense, taking a day off to recover from fatigue was probably perfect timing.

"Though, whether the folks inside the sand ship will actually recover their fatigue in just one day... I can't say."

"...If it were a slightly bigger ship, we wouldn't have to worry about that sort of thing, I suppose."

"Probably."

The sand ship Rei possesses is simply too large for its sole purpose of being used by the Crimson Wings.

But if you asked whether nearly a thousand people could relax comfortably... the answer is no.

Even in private rooms, two people share a bed... sometimes three.

Some slept on the floor or in the hallways, and the largest number slept in the hull section of the sand ship... the cargo hold.

Given such conditions, if you asked whether one could truly relax and unwind, the answer would be no.

Still, it was more comfortable than sleeping directly on grass or dirt with no blankets, like roughing it outdoors.

Of course, with this many people, the toilets inevitably get crowded.

Thanks to magic items, they never overflowed, but the number of toilets was still limited.

As a result, many often ended up relieving themselves outdoors, away from the sand ship.

"For now, let's call tomorrow a rest day... We'll want to set out the day after tomorrow."

"Right. But we still need to tend to the horses, so it won't be a complete rest."

The horses used to pull the carriages were tethered near the sand ship.

Fortunately, wolves and the like didn't approach thanks to Set's presence... but still, there was always the possibility of something unexpected.

Plus, feeding them hay, giving them water, and the sheer number of horses meant they definitely needed a fair amount of manpower.

"Hmm. But in terms of making clothes, isn't this actually quite good? Normally, only those riding in the carriage would make clothes, but tomorrow, everyone can make clothes all at once."

"True. Though, it's not like everyone can actually do it, is it?"

At Marina's words, Elena tilted her head, watching the falling rain from inside the magic tent.

"Why not?"

"...Then let me ask you, Elena, can you sew clothes? What about Vihera? Byune... she might be able to sew. ...And Rei?"

When put that way, Elena could only agree.

In fact, even Vihera and Rei, who seemed to be caught in the crossfire, couldn't nod in agreement with Marina's words.

Only Byune, who had been mentioned as possibly being able to sew, gave off a slightly smug vibe, even without changing her expression.

"I see. I understand Marina's point. There are all sorts among the women, huh?"

"I suppose so. Of course, it's true that many possess the skill to sew clothes."

Among the women, there were several daughters of merchants with large trading houses, and daughters of nobles, though not of the highest rank.

Conversely, women who had been captured from places with no home or possessions likely couldn't manage something like sewing clothes.

Even if it wasn't that extreme, in reasonably wealthy households, there were probably those who didn't sew their own clothes, or who left that sort of thing entirely to their grandmother or mother...

"Well, even so, most of them can sew, right? Then it should be far better than sewing while moving in a carriage."

Rei said it somewhat evasively, but the words themselves weren't wrong.

The clothes themselves weren't elaborate; they were relatively simple to make. So it was certain that the number of garments would increase significantly by tomorrow.

(Though, of course, the possibility of progressing too quickly and running out of fabric couldn't be entirely ruled out.)

The amount of cloth purchased in the village so far was substantial, but it was only substantial.

If asked whether it was enough to make clothes for a thousand people... even Rei, who wasn't particularly knowledgeable about such matters, would shake his head without hesitation.

"Well, I was planning to take tomorrow off, but it looks like things are going to get hectic in a different way."

Watching the rain from inside the magic tent with the others, Rei murmured this to himself.
