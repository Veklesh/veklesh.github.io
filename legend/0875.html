"Whoa..."

Rei murmured involuntarily as she entered Asimov's house.

It was indeed filthy enough to warrant such a reaction.

Everywhere lay tools whose purpose wasn't immediately obvious, what appeared to be materials from some kind of monster, books on alchemy, and more. While it wasn't exactly impossible to find a place to step, it was still visibly cluttered.

Rei probably wasn't the only one feeling envious of Set, who was waiting outside as usual.

But Azimov paid no mind to Rei's thoughts and spoke up.

"Alright, just sit down anywhere. So, it was a demon sword, right?"

"...Trying to move the conversation forward in this situation is so typical of you."

Pamidor pushed aside the luggage on the sofa to make room for himself as he spoke.

Rei also secured his own spot before placing the magic sword on the table.

(Was it sheer luck that there was a sofa-like thing here?)

Given Azimov's personality, he probably wouldn't have minded if there hadn't been any sofa-like furniture, but that didn't mean there wasn't any.

But Azimov needed to eat, and he might invite guests into his home, as he had done this time.

Considering that, a sofa was probably essential after all.

Though, admittedly, the sofa was covered in various items and luggage that Rei couldn't quite make sense of.

"This cursed sword seems to have been crafted with quite advanced technology... but since it's broken, repairing it as-is might be a bit difficult."

"So, it can't be fixed?"

Rei asked sadly as he watched Azimov mutter while holding the hilt.

But after a moment's thought, Rei continued.

"Well... even if we did fix it, I doubt it could fully regain the abilities of the original demon sword. Some percentage of its power would inevitably be lost."

"...A certain percentage, huh. That's definitely how it feels."

Holding the blade broken in half, Pamidor murmured as well.

It was undoubtedly a demon sword of immense power, and even with some loss of ability, it would still be more than formidable.

But even so, repairing it while accepting diminished power held little appeal for skilled smiths and alchemists like Pamidor and Azimov.

That was precisely why Azimov shifted his gaze from the magic sword to Rei and continued speaking.

"If we repair it as a magic sword, its power will diminish. But if we use this magic sword as a base to create an entirely new weapon... then I think we could minimize the performance degradation more than if we just repaired the magic sword. What do you say?"

"Is that even possible?"

Rei, who had assumed repairing a demon sword meant keeping it as a demon sword, revealed a look of surprise at Azimov's words and asked back.

"Yes. Of course, it might be impossible for some incompetent alchemist you find lying around, but I'm a skilled alchemist, despite appearances."

He must be incredibly confident in his abilities. Azimov stated it with certainty.

As he listened to Azimov's words, Rei's gaze shifted toward Pamidor.

His gaze asked, Is that true? But Pamidor simply nodded back at Rei.

After all, if Azimov were just a talker, Pamidor wouldn't have gotten this deeply involved.

The fact that he kept bringing work to him without cutting ties along the way meant Azimov's skill was undoubtedly satisfying enough for Pamidor.

Even if his skill fluctuated depending on his mood, he possessed at least the minimum level of skill Pami-dor demanded.

Rei, having confirmed this, turned his gaze back toward Azimov.

"So, what exactly will this new weapon be?"

"Discuss that with Pamidor. We'll tailor the work accordingly."

Rei glanced at the magic sword before turning his gaze back to Pamidor.

"I don't really care what it is. Unless it's something completely unreasonable, I think I can handle most things."

His confidence stemmed from his exceptional skill as a blacksmith.

"If any weapon will do... Well, I mainly use the Death Scythe, so that leaves a spear... or perhaps a dagger?"

For Rei, the weapon he used most frequently after the Death Scythe was the spear.

Not wielded normally, but thrown for ranged attacks.

Or perhaps a dagger, for when an enemy closes within the Death Scythe's range.

Azimov's brow furrowed slightly at Rei's words.

"A dagger might be a bit tricky, considering its size and shape. A spear... Pamidor, what do you think?"

"I have no problem with either. What about you, Azimov?"

"I'm fine with a spear too. Not a magic sword, but a magic spear. That's interesting too. ...Hmm, in that case, I'd like to ask one thing. What were the original abilities of this magic sword? Even if we remake it into a magic spear, we can't do anything without knowing that."

Azimov asked, but all Rei could do was shake his head.

He knew this longsword was a demon sword, but he'd never actually seen it use its demon sword abilities clearly during the battle against Noiz.

It was possible that the sword had used its magic abilities without Rei noticing, but either way, the fact remained that Rei didn't know.

"Huh? You don't know its abilities?"

"Yeah, that's how it is. Probably because I broke the blade before it could unleash its demon sword abilities."

"...Well, that makes things a bit tricky. If I don't know its abilities, I'll have to analyze what kind of demon sword it is while working on it. That'll take some time, you know? Maybe with some cheap demon sword off the street, I could just wing it, but I can't waste time like that on a demon sword used by an S-rank adventurer."

"How long exactly?"

"Hard to say. We'll have to determine the full extent of this magic item's capabilities as we go. If we're unlucky, it could take months or even years. Even if it doesn't go that far, I'd estimate at least a month."

As Azimov replied while examining the hilt of the magic sword, Rei frowned.

He understood that repairing a magic item used by Noiz would take time.

But even so, months—let alone years—was completely unexpected.

"Can't we do something about it? It'd be a huge help if it could at least be usable by next spring."

"I hear you, but we're turning a magic sword into a magic spear, you know? It's going to take time no matter what. Sometimes specific materials are needed too. ...What do you say? Honestly, I won't push you into it."

Asked by Azimov, Rei hesitated.

Converting the magic sword into a magic spear was undoubtedly the best choice for Rei.

He didn't know how effective it would be, but since Rei wasn't used to wielding a longsword, there was a high chance the precious magic sword would go to waste otherwise.

"Alright. Then I'll go with the magic spear. ...What about the fee?"

"If you give me the griffin materials, I'm fine with that. Few people have materials like that, and even fewer can use them properly."

"...Is that really okay?"

"Of course. Rei, right? You don't seem to grasp the value of a Griffin, but its materials are incredibly rare. Well, if you still feel uneasy about it, just keep supplying me with Griffin materials regularly. That would put me in the black by a huge margin."

Is that all? Rei couldn't help but show his surprise.

But considering how alchemists and merchants in the Bestir Empire were trying to contact Set to obtain materials, Azimov's words probably weren't exaggerated at all.

Reaching that conclusion, he nodded... then suddenly remembered something and pulled a certain item from his Misty Ring.

It was a round, gem-like object.

However, it was cut diagonally, splitting it into two pieces.

It was the core of the dungeon Rei had conquered.

But dungeon cores aren't the kind of thing that just casually appear on the market.

Azimov probably didn't know what it was at first either. He examined the upper half of the dungeon core he'd received from Rei, but ultimately shook his head sideways, seeming to give up without figuring out what it was.

"What is this? I've never seen anything like it. Pamidor, do you know?"

"Let me take a look."

Pamidor took the upper half of the dungeon core from Azimov and examined it closely. After touching it for about a minute, he still couldn't figure it out and placed it back on the table.

"No clue. What the hell is this?"

Faced with questioning looks from both Pamidor and Azimov, Rei's lips curved into a faint smile as he declared proudly.

"It's the dungeon core."

"Huh?"

The words that flew out of Rei's mouth made both Pamidor and Azimov let out a dumbfounded gasp in unison.

They clearly hadn't expected such a thing to appear.

In that situation, Pamidor was the first to snap back to reality.

"Come to think of it, you said you were going to the dungeon after coming to see me yesterday... Did you actually clear the dungeon yesterday!?"

"Correct. Well, it was brand new and not very big yet, so that helped."

Even so, the room containing the dungeon's core housed over fifty C-rank monsters, Gamelions.

Any ordinary adventurer could have reached the core room, but they would have definitely ended there.

Only Rei could handle a group of Rank C monsters... and not just handle them, but actually manage to secure meat from them.

Unaware of the circumstances, Pamidor simply nodded in admiration.

"The dungeon? Wasn't it a few days' journey by carriage?"

"Ah... yeah, that's right. That'd be the case for you, I suppose."

Scratching his head, Pamidor addressed Azimov, who hadn't understood their conversation.

"There is indeed a dungeon where you mentioned. However, just recently, another dungeon was discovered near Gilm. They say the monsters that emerge are mostly weak trash mobs, and the materials are degraded, but a dungeon is a dungeon."

"...So, the core of this dungeon is real?"

"I haven't seen the core of the dungeon myself, so I can't say for sure. But if Rei's this confident about it, it's probably real. Right?"

You wouldn't bring a fake here, would you? Pamidor's eyes seemed to ask this, but Rei nodded back without a hint of unease.

"Yeah, it's the genuine article. Well, to prove it, we'd have to go into the dungeon and find the room where the core was... Honestly, though, since I took the dungeon core, it could collapse at any moment. I wouldn't recommend it. Luckily, it doesn't seem to be showing any signs of collapsing right now, so it might just stay there longer than expected."

"...I see. Then, would it be okay if I took custody of that dungeon core for now? If it seems usable for this magic sword, I'd like to try it."

"It can be used for that?"

Rei was slightly surprised by Azimov's words.

Although Rei was the one who retrieved the dungeon's core, he never imagined it could actually be a usable material.

Especially for the demon sword.

"I don't know if it'll work. I'm just saying I'll look into it. ...Pamidor, what about you?"

"Well, what can I say? It certainly is an intriguing material. If we used the dungeon core as a material when forging weapons, what kind of effect would it have? I can't say I'm not interested."

Following Azimov, Pamidor showed an intrigued expression. She likely understood the dungeon core was rare—not something easily obtained even with money. She seemed reluctant, yet still didn't say she wanted it.

"If you need it to turn that magic sword into a magic spear, using the dungeon core is fine. Otherwise... as a blacksmith, I could offer Set's materials—well, feathers and fur, but if that's acceptable, I'll use those as payment. Or how about the meat from one Gamelion? Or if you prefer gold, gold is fine too."

"...Hmm..."

Pamidor pondered Rei's words.

Gamelion meat, which had been scarce this year, was indeed very tempting, but he dismissed it immediately.

Sure, they could eat as much Gamelion meat as they wanted, but a whole one was more than a family of three could finish. It was winter, so it would spoil slower than in summer, but that wasn't a guarantee.

If that were the case, it would mean giving it to the people living nearby... and then, ultimately, the profit his own household would gain wouldn't be that great.

Of course, it's not that the Pamidor family neglects their neighbors, but that's beside the point.

So then, it would become material for Set or money...

After agonizing over it for a good few minutes, Pamidor finally spoke.

"I'll take the Set material."

As a blacksmith, he couldn't resist the urge to forge a weapon using griffin materials.

Even so, he tried to convince himself that a weapon made from griffin materials could fetch a considerable price depending on where he sold it... In Pamidor's mind, his beloved wife nodded in understanding, a wry smile playing on her lips.
