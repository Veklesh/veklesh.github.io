It didn't take long for the expedition team that left Gilm to reach the vicinity of Treant Forest.

Moreover, traveling by carriage to conserve stamina as much as possible was fortunate for the adventurers.

But... even so, as the Treant Forest drew nearer, the adventurers' expressions grew serious.

Some appeared to be joking around, but most were merely putting on a show.

...Though, admittedly, there were a few who genuinely seemed to be fooling around.

"Hey, you're that Crimson one, right? Is it true you can take on an entire army alone? I mean, yesterday we got to see the power of your item box, but that was basically it."

The young male adventurer riding in the carriage with Crimson Wings, including Rei, was one such person.

He was an adventurer who had just arrived in Gilm this spring, a Rank C adventurer with decent skill.

On top of that, being a solo adventurer made him, in a way, a very rare breed indeed.

"Was it Raznig? He seems pretty excited, but he knows we're heading to Treant Forest, right?"

"Huh? Yeah, of course. But I've got some confidence in my skills, you know. If anything happens... well, at the very least, I can protect myself."

Raznig's expression was brimming with confidence as he placed a hand on the scabbard at his waist.

Yet he didn't seem to think his own abilities were the absolute best... which was probably typical of a Rank C adventurer.

Though, admittedly, some adventurers—regardless of rank—harbor an excessive belief that they alone are the greatest.

Fortunately, none of the adventurers participating in this Treant Forest survey were like that.

At least, none who openly displayed it.

What they thought privately, however, was uncertain.

This Treant Forest holds significant meaning for the expansion of Gilm... that is, for Gilm's future development.

Walker knew this well, which is likely why he carefully selected the adventurers participating in this survey.

"I see. Then... show me your true abilities on this mission."

As the carriage came to a stop upon reaching Treant Forest, Rei addressed Razning.

"Heh heh, leave it to me. Whether there's magic stones or not, it's still a Treant, right? Then, opponents like that are no problem. Besides, I promised Mizuna-chan at the tavern I'd drink with her after this job. This is nothing."

"I see. Well then, do your best."

Saying that to Raznig, Rei stepped down from the carriage.

Walker was putting considerable effort into this investigation, it seemed.

The guild had provided five carriages.

Adventurers disembarked from each of those five carriages and turned their gaze once more toward the Treant Forest before them.

Most of the adventurers participating in this investigation were the same ones who had come here yesterday to guard the woodcutters' logging operations.

Yesterday, they had carried out what was essentially logging work in this forest, under the guise of being guards.

As a result, they earned more than they had expected.

Compared to the wood cut by the lumberjacks, their work was somewhat rougher, so the price was a bit lower. Still, considering it was a job without mortal danger, the pay was quite good.

But... there's always a catch to a good deal.

This forest they'd entered so casually was a place where monsters ran rampant at night... and above all, the fact that those monsters didn't carry magic stones was enough to make the adventurers view Treant Forest as an unsettling place.

"Looking at it like this, it just seems like a normal forest to me."

"Hey, you call a forest with no sign of monsters, let alone animals, 'normal'?"

"Well, there are nuts and berries, so I figured it could be a decent spot for gathering food."

"Well, forget this season. It should be a decent source for winter firewood, right? After all, no matter how much we cut, the trees never seem to decrease. ...The place we worked yesterday is almost completely covered now."

The adventurers gazed at Treant Forest as they spoke.

True to his words, the spot where the men had relentlessly cut down trees yesterday was already completely gone.

Still, they could recognize it as the spot they'd worked yesterday, albeit faintly, because the forest appeared indented compared to the surrounding area.

Even if the forest's expansion rate is constant, if the reference points differ, the forest's shape will naturally become distorted.

Of course, if only a few people had cut down a small number of trees... the forest's regenerative power would be stronger, and it would recover easily.

However, the recovery power of the Treant Forest wasn't strong enough to restore an area where numerous woodcutters and adventurers had concentrated their logging efforts.

A major factor was that the woodcutters and adventurers could focus purely on felling trees without having to transport the felled timber or trim branches.

The fact that they could focus so intensely, yet still only leave traces of logging visible to the discerning eye, demonstrated just how astonishing a presence Treant Forest truly was.

"Alright, we're heading out to survey the forest now. Everyone, please split into the groups you were told about beforehand!"

The voice of the person leading this expedition echoed around them.

In an ordinary forest, adventurers alone might suffice for such an investigation.

But the Treant Forest is different.

It was far from an ordinary forest.

Considering what lay ahead for Gilm, it was unthinkable to entrust the investigation solely to adventurers.

As a result, they had requested the assistance of alchemists, magic researchers, scholars... and such individuals, leading to this expedition.

Beyond those belonging to the guild, there were also those living in Gilm, those dispatched as Daskar's subordinates... such individuals were undoubtedly capable.

However, it was only natural that many of them lacked high combat prowess.

Given these circumstances, and because they possessed a different perspective from the researchers, it was decided that the researchers and adventurers would act together.

"What are we supposed to do? Nobody told us who to team up with."

Following Lenora's instructions, we joined the survey team preparing to depart and headed straight for Treant Forest.

Compared to the other adventurers who had prearranged partners, we were completely left behind.

The man giving orders to the other adventurers must have noticed Rei's situation.

He approached and spoke up.

"Rei and his party are ranked B, and their abilities are a step or two above the other adventurers."

Hearing this, the adventurers nearby who had been listening made slightly displeased faces.

They understood that Rei and his group were more skilled than them, but it was only natural to feel annoyed when it was stated directly.

But the man was thoroughly accustomed to such reactions.

As a guild employee dealing with adventurers, getting used to that sort of thing was only natural.

Ignoring the stares, the man continued.

"Also, I know you're conducting various activities in this Treant Forest. If you carelessly bring someone along, they'll undoubtedly become a hindrance. Given that, it would be wiser for just you, Rei, and your group to proceed. ...You have an item box, after all."

Even without Rei and the others accompanying them, having an item box meant they could bring back as many necessary items for investigation as they needed.

And without worrying about deterioration over time.

While merchants, adventurers, and soldiers would kill for such magic items, the ability to collect research subjects without degrading them made them incredibly valuable to researchers as well.

That was precisely why he wanted only the experienced members of Crimson Wings, who were accustomed to such operations, to proceed and investigate deeper into the forest.

...Of course, there was also the concern of whether they could reliably bring back rare items without overlooking them. But the fact remained that bringing along unnecessary companions was likely to cause more problems than it solved.

The presence of the dark elf Marina was also a significant factor.

For Marina, who possessed extensive knowledge of plants, discovering something suspicious in Treant Forest wouldn't be particularly difficult.

(And then...)

The man talking to Rei felt a strong sense of unease about Vihera in her provocative attire and Marina in her party dress.

Especially in nature, where people tend to be more open, or in an unfamiliar place where tension runs high, it wasn't impossible that someone might get strange ideas.

If that happened, it would undoubtedly become troublesome in many ways.

"What do you think? Is that okay with you?"

"Yeah, that'd be a big help to us too."

Rei nodded simply, accepting the man's proposal.

He judged it would be smoother to move with just the members of Crimson Wings than to act with someone untrustworthy.

Neither Marina, Vihera, nor Byune seemed to object to Rei's words, showing no particular inclination to speak up.

"Alright then. Please return by evening at the latest. The nighttime investigation will be the main event."

"Yeah."

His brief reply was likely due to his experience in Treant Forest at night.

The monsters themselves weren't particularly strong, but the fact that they possessed no magic stones made them extremely troublesome.

That's precisely why they were conducting this investigation with so many people, he understood.

(Fortunately, looking at the others, there weren't any extreme deadweights or selfish jerks.)

Of course, Rei didn't know every adventurer, and there was always the possibility someone was putting on a front.

Still, they were undoubtedly far better than someone who acted selfishly without a second thought.

(Wait, does that mean I'm the one acting selfishly here?)

A fleeting thought crossed my mind, but I quickly shook my head and focused my attention on Treant Forest.

"Alright, let's go then. ...It's okay for me to come, right?"

"Huh? Oh, yes. Of course. Well... I'm sure you know this already, Rei, but please be careful. You never know what might be lurking here."

"Yeah, I know. ...Actually, I might be the one who understands that best out of everyone here?"

"Ahaha. You might be right."

Rei's joking words drew a smile from the man.

But then, Rei caught sight of Slesha talking with Runo, a scholar-like figure, and several other adventurers. He wondered if his words had been inappropriate.

In the true sense of knowing the dangers of Treant Forest, it was Slesha—whose entire party had been wiped out—who was most qualified to understand that.

...Though he couldn't imagine Slesha being pleased to hear that.

「Rei？」

Marina called out to Rei, who had been looking at Slesha.

What's wrong? Marina asked with her eyes. Rei silently shook his head.

Set also came over, rubbing his face against Rei as if to comfort him.

As Rei stroked Set's face in return, the man who had been talking to Rei bowed his head slightly and walked away.

Perhaps he couldn't bear the tension between Rei and the others, or maybe he wasn't used to Marina—who had been their superior just moments ago—being present.

In any case, the only ones around now were Rei and his companions... the members of Crimson Wings.

Looking around for the others, some, like Runo and the others, were conferring, while others had already entered Treant Forest.

"Nah, nothing. Looks like most of the others are already in the forest too. Should we head in?"

"Yeah. ...I'd be happy if there were some strong monsters there, though."

It was Vihera, not Marina, who answered Rei's question.

Honestly, for Vihera, whether magic stones were present or not didn't matter—she just wanted to fight strong enemies.

In that sense, Treant Forest, likely home to completely unknown entities, was the perfect place.

...Though, to be fair, the only thing we actually fought yesterday was a Treant.

It wasn't particularly noteworthy in terms of strength either—just a perfectly ordinary Treant, except for the lack of magic stones.

It was undeniably a bit boring, precisely because of that.

That's precisely why he was hoping this expedition might finally bring a powerful foe to fight.

Drawn along by Vihera, Rei entered the Treant Forest alongside Marina, Byune, and Set.
