"I see, teaching the S-Class... That must have been quite a challenge. The S-Class students are exceptionally talented, of course, but they also have a lot of pride. I get a little nervous during my own classes too. If possible, I'd prefer to focus on my magic research."

"Unfortunately, that's not possible," he said, lifting the bread in his hand to his mouth.

The evening of Rei's first day as an instructor.

That evening, Rei was eating dinner in the dormitory cafeteria, conversing with Samaloon.

Well, Samaloon wasn't the only one sharing a meal with Rei at this table.

"Hmm, really? The S-class students listen to us pretty well, you know?"

A gentle-mannered female instructor in her twenties said this as she dipped her spoon into a stew packed with ingredients.

At first, the instructors and staff living in this dormitory had been unsure how to behave around Rei.

But when they were struggling with Samaloon, several offered help, and as a result, others had also become somewhat able to talk with Rei.

That said, being able to talk didn't mean they could have friendly conversations.

Rei, who wasn't particularly skilled at socializing to begin with, mainly talked about class-related matters and rarely mentioned anything personal.

Still, the instructors and staff seemed to understand that Rei was still a child—well, not exactly a child, but certainly not old enough to be called an adult.

They chose topics carefully to keep the conversation flowing without making Rei feel too stifled.

That most conversations ended up being about classes was perhaps unavoidable, given this was a military academy.

"That's because those guys are targeting you. ...Anyway. Teaching students, not just S-Class ones, means instructors learn a lot too. I'd be happy if you studied that aspect thoroughly as well, Rei."

One of the instructors eating near Rei murmured thoughtfully.

(A former Rank B adventurer... was it?)

Rei knew the man, as his name had come up several times during his conversations with Samaloon.

Even though he'd retired from adventuring, he'd originally been a Rank B adventurer like Rei.

Samaroon had mentioned that he didn't know much about adventurer-related matters, so if Ray ever needed advice in that area, he should feel free to consult him.

"Besides... from now on, quite a few adventurers will be showing up at the officer academy. Thinking about it that way, Rei being here shouldn't be that noticeable anymore, so I don't think you need to worry too much about it."

Somehow, the conversation had shifted to Rei's appearance. The former rank B adventurer was saying that Rei, being younger than the students, would stand out greatly as an instructor.

"Adventurers?"

Rei, who didn't understand why so many adventurers were coming, asked while reaching for a skewer.

It had only been one day since Rei started living in this dormitory, but in that single day, the dormitory cafeteria had changed significantly.

Specifically, both the quantity and quality of the food had improved.

The reason, apparently, was that having renowned adventurers involved in the royal faction's disputes come to Glacial and then not be satisfied with the food would be a disgrace to the Quent Duke's household.

When Rei mentioned the cafeteria meals to Samaloon, word reached the academy headmaster, and as a result, the meals had been greatly improved.

As long as one is alive, naturally, the tastier the food, the better.

This improvement was undoubtedly one reason the residents of this dormitory grew more favorably disposed toward Rei.

There are those who don't care much about food, thinking "as long as it's edible, it's fine," but they are definitely in the minority.

The biggest worry among those currently living in the staff dormitory is whether the quality of the meals will drop once Rei leaves the officer academy in the spring.

"Ah. It'll be the same at Gilm where Rei is, but adventurers generally don't take commissions during winter. That is, only those who managed to save up enough funds to get through the winter. Those who failed to save money for whatever reason naturally have to keep earning during the winter too."

The man's words made Rei think of two female adventurers who had squandered their money doting on Set.

One had genuinely run out of her savings and had to take on quests, while the other had tried to dip into the money she'd brought from the Bestir Empire only to be stopped by her companions, forcing her to take on quests too.

Of course, there were others who took on quests during winter, but those two left the strongest impression on Rei.

"Yeah, I bet. I know some folks like that too."

"...Even among your acquaintances with nicknames? Anyway, among the jobs such people take, the ones posted by the Officer Academy are pretty lucrative. You don't have to leave Glacial, and you get to move your body, so you don't get rusty."

"So it's quite a lucrative deal, huh?"

"Yeah. Though, that's only possible because the military academy is here in Gracielle, ruled by Duke Quent. Other towns... or villages, certainly couldn't pull off something like that."

The man's words were true.

This level of wealth existed precisely because this was the heartland of Duke Quent, proof of the significant funds poured into the military academy.

Hearing the man's words, Rei thought, If only something like this could happen in Gilm... but he realized it was impossible.

With that thought, he finished the skewered meat he'd been holding and went to return the skewer to the plate. Just then, Samaloon, sitting directly across from him, suddenly shouted loudly.

"Ah! That conversation just reminded me! Sorry, Rei-san. I forgot about that message from that muscle-head!"

At the word "brawn," several people in the cafeteria subtly frowned.

It was practically common knowledge among those who'd been at this academy long enough that Samaloon and Iskeld were friends—though the two of them would never admit it.

And most people also knew that when Samaloon said "brawn," he meant Iskeld.

No matter how close the two were, as residents of Glacial, Iskeld was still regarded as one of the heroes.

Precisely because of that, quite a few people find it unpleasant to hear that hero called a muscle-head.

Those people shoot reproachful glances at Samaloon, but he seems completely unfazed as he continues speaking to Rei.

"I have a message from that muscle-head. I believe he was originally scheduled to meet with Duke Quent tonight, but due to an urgent matter, that has become difficult. He asks that it be postponed for a while."

"Is that so? ...Well, I'm not too fond of formalities either, so I welcome the meeting being postponed. Actually, it'd be fine if it didn't happen at all."

"That might be a bit difficult. Since we're inconveniencing Rei due to our own circumstances, I think you should meet Duke Quent at least once."

"...Is that how it is?"

"Yes, absolutely."

Hearing Samaloon nod confidently, Rei glanced around.

Almost everyone who had been watching the conversation unfold nodded back in agreement with Samaloon.

(Honestly, I'm not really into all that formal stuff.)

Normally, an audience with the Duke is something you couldn't easily get even if you wanted it.

For those seeking to establish connections with Duke Quent, it could be called a golden opportunity.

If such people heard what Rei just said, they would undoubtedly fly into a rage.

Or perhaps they'd say they'd go meet Duke Quent themselves instead...

"Well, if your plans for tonight fell through, that's fine too. Though you'll probably need to find something to kill time."

"Something to pass the time? How about we talk about magic?"

"Ah... Sorry, but that's a bit difficult. I think I've mentioned it a few times before, but I use magic intuitively. So, talking about detailed magical theory would be a bit of a problem for me."

"But you do have a magic teacher, don't you, Rei? Didn't you learn all sorts of magic theory from them?"

"I don't know. Maybe I did, maybe I didn't... Anyway, the fact is, that stuff just isn't for me. Sorry, but I'd rather pass."

"...I see. That's unfortunate, but I suppose it can't be helped."

Samaloon probably realized that pushing Rei any further would only be a bother. He backed off quietly.

The other instructors and staff watching exchanged glances, their expressions saying, Here we go again...

Just seeing that made it clear how deeply Samaloon was devoted to magic and how much he wanted to talk about it.

(I'd rather someone else have that conversation, though.)

Rei finished his meal while pondering such thoughts.

After finishing the last bite of stewed meat, he stood up.

A man sitting nearby noticed and called out to Rei.

"Huh? Finished already?"

"Yeah. I'm satisfied enough. I should head back to my room soon. I need to think about how to teach mock battles properly, like a proper instructor should."

Whether temporary or not, as an instructor... he had been entrusted with the role of teaching others.

Rei had done similar things several times before, but teaching Baslero required a different approach, and conducting the kind of training he'd given the former guerrilla squad members would be difficult.

Especially if he tried training them using skills like the Overlord’s Armour or the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, like he did with the former guerrilla squad members, it wouldn't just fail to toughen them up—it could completely destroy their will to fight.

And not just temporary loss of confidence, but complete and utter loss... If handled poorly, it could inflict massive trauma upon them. If that happened, the worst-case scenario would be every single S-class student voluntarily withdrawing from the academy.

(Well, if the goal is to weaken the King's faction, maybe that's what should be done... I am cooperating with Lord Daskar, but I'm not exactly neutral either. Besides, Duke Quent's faction is supposed to be the ones enforcing discipline—the nobles who haven't rotted yet...)

I haven't met Duke Quent himself yet, so I can't say for certain, but I also suspect that if Duke Quent were a typical corrupt noble, a child like Marca wouldn't have been raised with such freedom.

(Then again, during today's mock battle, the entire class ganged up on me and ended up not even scratching me. Considering that, it's possible I gave them enough of a trauma to last a lifetime.)

What flashed through Rei's mind wasn't the fourth-year S-class students... but rather the third-year S-class students who had fought him in the mock battle afterward.

As usual, as expected, several of them couldn't tell at a glance that Rei was an instructor—which was understandable in a way—and challenged him while underestimating him, only to be sunk into the ground without being able to do anything.

After that, they realized Rei was no ordinary opponent and took the mock battles seriously, but they still ended up sinking to the ground.

Finally, the entire class challenged him together, only to suffer the same fate.

The fourth-years, being the senior class, seemed to have managed to pull themselves together somehow, but to Rei's eyes, some of the third-years looked like their will was about to break.

"That depends on tomorrow, I suppose."

"Huh? What is it?"

A man nearby turned his gaze toward Rei at the words that escaped his lips.

Shaking his head as if to say it was nothing, Rei left the cafeteria.

He went straight up to the second floor, returned to the room prepared for him, and lay down on the bed.

"Still... how exactly should I go about training them? Is pointing out their weak spots, like I did with Baslero, really the best approach? But pointing out everyone's weaknesses every single time? That'd be way too much hassle."

During the mock battle with Basler, he could use those methods precisely because Basler was alone.

But this time, it was the fourth-year S-Class and the third-year S-Class, both classes fully enrolled with forty students each.

Pointing out weaknesses for every single one of them would be incredibly tedious for Rei too.

"Plus, unlike Baslerro, they're half-baked and overly confident in their own abilities. Sure, I made them acknowledge me in today's mock battle. But whether they'll actually listen to what I say... I'm not so sure."

Feeling that tomorrow's classes were going to be quite a challenge... Rei spent the night.
