The warehouse overflowed with a massive amount of lumber.

...Well, considering they still had branches and bark attached, calling them lumber might be premature.

In any case, about thirty felled trees were lined up inside the warehouse.

The fact that all of them were felled and transported by a single person... and that the entire operation, including travel time, took only about two hours—even accounting for snack breaks—would be unbelievable under normal circumstances.

But the one doing it was Rei.

"This... isn't that a bit much?"

Lenora stared dumbfounded at the pile of wood in the warehouse and muttered.

She had left Rei with Kenny in the morning, but now that it was nearing noon and her hands were free, Lenora was tending to Rei as usual.

"Oh? Well, you asked for as much as possible, so I brought this much for now... If it's not enough, I can bring more?"

"I said it's too much..."

Lenora sighed and muttered in response to Rei's words.

Watching Lenora like that, Rei nodded in understanding.

After all, even though they'd received the request this morning and cut down all these trees to bring back, it was still before noon.

Thinking about it that way, he could easily cut the same amount again this afternoon... or even more.

But the guild's warehouse wasn't infinite.

No, rather, it was spring, and the adventurers of Gilm were actively taking on requests to replenish the money they'd spent over winter. Newcomers who had just arrived in Gilm were taking on requests to show off their presence... and above all, to gain the strength necessary to make it in Gilm.

Naturally, this meant the amount of materials obtained increased, and the warehouse was overflowing with materials waiting to be sold.

In other words, even if more wood were brought in, there was simply no place to put it.

For now, the warehouse still had some breathing room, but if they kept bringing in this many trees every single day, it wouldn't be long before the place was completely filled.

(Well, the guildmaster did say this wood gets hauled out every day, so maybe I shouldn't worry about it too much.)

Lenora was lost in thought, gazing at the trees before her, when Rei suddenly changed the subject.

"Come to think of it, are any parties missing since yesterday?"

"Huh? Hmm... Now that you mention it, there are a lot of adventurers, and some parties might be out on quests..."

"No, I don't think they took any requests."

They headed into a forest where no one knew what lurked, so it was unlikely they'd taken any specific request.

Moreover, with the number of adventurers in Gilm surging rapidly this spring, it was impossible for even the receptionist to know exactly how many adventurers were currently in Gilm.

"I'm afraid I can't really say. As you know, Rei, Gilm always had a lot of adventurers, but now there are even more. ...But why do you ask?"

Why did he suddenly ask that?

Considering Rei's request today, she was almost certain of the reason, yet she still asked, hoping it might be something else.

But... the next moment, the words that came out of Rei's mouth were exactly what Lenora had expected.

"When I went to Treant Forest today, I found what looked like a campsite. It felt exactly like when Slesha was there."

"...I see."

Lenora sighed regretfully, her own bad feeling having been confirmed.

Adventurers had a minimum level of discipline, but fundamentally, they were free spirits.

Even if they headed to Treant Forest, no one would blame them.

But... even so, as the guild receptionist, she really didn't want to see adventurers lose their lives.

Of course, for the Third Eye members who had gone missing this time, their own impatience was likely the reason.

They tried to overcome that impatience, only to end up losing their lives... but neither Rei nor Lenora could grasp the full circumstances behind it.

After all, at this point, it hadn't even been confirmed that those missing were the Third Eye.

"So, you mean the campsite remains..."

Realizing what Lenora meant, Rei retrieved the stored luggage and spear from the Misty Ring at the campsite.

"This was left behind."

"...Um, Rei. Would it be okay if I had the guild store this luggage for you? Technically, it belongs to you..."

"Yeah, go ahead."

Rei had never intended to keep the luggage for himself anyway, so he handed it over to Lenora without hesitation.

He did consider keeping the spear, but Rei's Misty Ring already held a large number of spears—most of them nearly broken.

He had plenty of spears for throwing anyway, so there was no need to insist on taking this one here.

(Well, if it were a magic item spear, it might have piqued my interest... but unfortunately, it's just an ordinary spear.)

The magic item spears Rei possessed.

The Thorn Spear, which entangles its pierced target in thorns, and the Twilight Spear, possessing various special abilities and serving as one of Rei's primary weapons alongside the Death Scythe.

If it were a magic item spear with effects like these two, Rei would have claimed ownership.

But since it wasn't, there was no need for such a hassle.

"Thank you. ...I'll immediately investigate who this luggage belonged to, to the best of my ability."

With that, Lenora bowed deeply.

Though she still didn't know whose belongings they were, she acted this way because, as the guild receptionist, she wanted to be sincere toward adventurers.

(She's a hard worker, isn't she? They say you should seek out hardship when you're young, but Lenora seems to be taking on others' share too. ...Well, that's just Lenora, I suppose.)

Watching Lenora, Rei spoke again.

"Alright, I'll leave the luggage matter to you. Also, about this felled timber... we can settle the commission fee for it later, right?"

"Yes, that's what I've been told."

"I see. Then I'll leave the wood matter to you. ...But this is just a warning: if we leave things as they are, the situation with Treant Forest could get messy. It hasn't blown up into a huge scandal yet, but..."

"...I see. I agree. However, regarding the Treant Forest matter, it seems things are already moving at the higher levels. I imagine they have their own plans in place."

The news about Gilm's expansion hasn't spread yet.

In fact, it hasn't even been definitively decided yet.

What's happening now is more accurately described as calculating whether it's actually feasible.

Of course, it's true that Gilm is already pushing its limits. If things continue like this, it's certain that Gilm—the only town in this remote area—will reach saturation point before long.

That's precisely why expanding Gilm soon was necessary, but even so, this particular matter came too suddenly.

It forced a major acceleration of preparations that were already underway, so it's only natural that things got chaotic at the top.

And those below, unaware of this, ended up in the current situation.

"Wouldn't it be better to clearly prevent people from approaching Treant Forest?"

"...But then, if something happens, adventurers who suspect something might head to Treant Forest even more than before."

That was something he'd been told before.

Still, Rei couldn't help but think it might be better than the current situation.

Of course, that was closer to Rei's own sentimentality.

If they actually banned people from going into Treant Forest, it was certain that more people would go missing than now, just as Lenora said.

And in this case, "missing" meant being devoured by the forest... synonymous with death.

"I see. ...Well, I'd be grateful if you'd at least consider it."

In the end, Rei couldn't push any harder, and that was that.

Lenora probably understood Rei's feelings perfectly well.

That's precisely why she decided to bring up what had just been discussed with the higher-ups.

"Hmm... Seeing it like this, it really is spring."

"Grrr..."

After completing the delivery of the felled trees, Rei walked through town with Set.

He had some lingering thoughts about what happened in the warehouse, but he had shaken off that gloomy mood for now.

Ultimately, he'd reminded himself that adventurers taking risks in dangerous places was their own responsibility.

"Speaking of spring, it's cherry blossom viewing season... huh?"

Back when Rei lived in Japan, relatives and friends often gathered under the pretext of hanami to hold parties.

Rei's family home was in the mountains, so cherry blossoms were plentiful around there.

...Though, whether those cherry trees were planted by the people where Rei lived or if they were naturally occurring, he couldn't say.

(It's called Treant Forest, after all, so you'd think there'd be cherry blossoms or something.)

As far as Rei could tell, Treant Forest was home to many different kinds of trees.

But there are no cherry trees there.

If they were as conspicuous as cherry trees, he would have spotted them immediately while flying through the sky on Set.

The fact that he couldn't find any led Rei to conclude there were no cherry trees in Treant Forest.

(Well, since the forest is being eroded day by day like that, it's possible cherry trees might sprout up eventually.)

If cherry trees grew automatically, he'd love to somehow control it and create a cherry blossom-lined path.

He found himself thinking that, though he knew it was impossible.

(Cherry wood would be great for making smoked foods, though... Well, I've never actually made any myself, so it's just knowledge from manga and such.)

Using cherry wood chips for smoking is such a common technique; it's often featured on TV shows and frequently appears in manga too.

I briefly considered smoking goblin meat with cherry wood chips... but then quickly dismissed the idea, realizing that goblin meat is so disgusting that cherry wood chips wouldn't make any difference.

"That sandwich with the stewed orc meat inside... Yeah, give me five servings."

"Got it, thanks as always."

Rei bought sandwiches at the shop where he'd previously bought delicious bread, then walked down the main street eating them with Set.

Several people looked like they wanted to play with Set, but he probably had to prioritize his own work. They walked away looking disappointed.

However...

"Ah, Set! Hey, Set! Let's play!"

"I wanna play with Set-chan too!"

For children without work—or more accurately, children still too young to have much work beyond helping around the house—it was only natural that they'd want to play whenever they spotted Set. It was also only natural that they'd act on it immediately.

If the person were someone scary... like an adventurer who lashed out violently at anyone and everyone, the children wouldn't dare approach such a person.

But in Rei's case, some of the children had known him for years, and many were well aware of his personality.

"Ah... well. What do you say, Set? Should we hang out with these guys? Or should we meet up with Marina and the others?"

"Grrr."

At Rei's words, Set growled while eating his sandwich... then swallowed it and rubbed his face against the kids.

"Ahahahahaha! Stop it, Set, you're tickling me!"

The child giggled, tickled by Set rubbing his face against them.

Watching Set like this, Rei let a small smile touch his lips.

Though he had much to ponder regarding the matter in Treant Forest, in this moment alone, he felt a deep joy at being able to spend such a peaceful time.

"Hey, Rei. What's up?"

"Hm? Oh, nothing. Come on, go play with Set too."

One of the children gathered around Set, perhaps noticing Rei's expression, asked this, but Rei shook his head, signaling it was nothing.

(Treant Forest, huh... I really think it's time to get serious about this. If we leave it alone, the forest will eventually encroach on Gilm too.)

Treant Forest spreads further almost every day.

Its expansion rate was roughly ten to fifteen meters per day.

But that was merely the situation up until now; it wouldn't necessarily stay the same forever.

Before it does, wouldn't it be better to deal with it all at once? That's what Rei thought.

Fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, Rei's specialty was fire magic. Using a fire whirlwind should be enough to wipe out the forest... or so it should be.

Watching the children playing with Set, Rei felt an uneasy foreboding.
