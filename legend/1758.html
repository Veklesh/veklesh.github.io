Once it was decided that Rei would accompany Elena to the Duchy of Kerebel, it naturally became necessary to make the appropriate preparations.

The most important task, of course, is to report this to Daskar and the Guild.

Normally, when an adventurer leaves that town for any reason, there is no need to go out of their way to report it.

However, in Rei’s case, he is deeply involved in the expansion of Gilm and the construction of the surface ships, and he is also connected to the matter involving Sura and the others.

For that very reason, while there would normally be no need to go out of his way to inform them, there was a risk that if something happened and they tried to rely on Rei, she might not actually be in Gilm, resulting in a waste of time… To prevent such a situation, it was necessary to let them know in advance.

And since they were heading to the Duchy of Kerebel, it was only natural that, when informing Daskar, Elena and Ara would be present alongside Rei.

Since Elena and Rei—two figures of great importance to Daskar—had come to visit, Daskar decided to meet with them immediately unless the matter was of extreme urgency.

And so, as usual, in the office, Rei, Elena, and Ara sat facing Daskar.

Meanwhile, Set and Yellow were currently playing together in the courtyard of the lord’s mansion.

“Hmm. Well, I understand that Lady Elena is returning to the Duchy of Kerebel, but is Rei coming along too?”

“Is there a problem? I don’t think there was anything particularly urgent at the moment, though.”

In response to Rei’s question, Daskar sipped his tea and casually glanced at the documents on his desk.

Following his gaze, Rei looked at the desk and noticed that the number of documents there had clearly decreased compared to before.

Of course, part of that was because Daskar had been frantically organizing the papers, but more than that, it was proof that the expansion construction was gradually beginning to slow down.

As winter approached, the cold was inevitably intensifying, and working outdoors was becoming dangerous.

Even so, they were continuing with the minimum necessary construction for the sake of those seeking work… but in any case, it was a fact that the number of documents requiring approval had decreased for that reason.

Perhaps noticing Rei’s gaze, Daskar shook his head, indicating it wasn’t a problem.

“It’s not really about the paperwork. It’s just that Rei stands out in many ways. I simply thought that with Rei around, there’d be fewer strange incidents. Mind you, this is just my personal feeling, so you don’t need to worry about it. Besides, you’ll be back in Gilm by spring at the latest, right?”

“That’s the plan. Well, to be precise, maybe after the New Year’s party is over.”

Daskar feels relieved at Rei’s words.

It wasn’t as if nothing would get done without Rei—the expansion project, for instance—but there was still a huge difference in efficiency whether he was there or not.

Above all, with Set as his mode of transportation, Rei could travel far more quickly than he could on horseback, no matter how far the distance.

……That said, he has a bit of a poor sense of direction, so he gets lost; he attracts trouble and ends up getting involved in disputes; and he spots bandits and hunts them down, so it’s not exactly a smooth, straight journey.

Still, even taking that into account, Rei and Set’s travel speed is high.

When there’s an emergency, it makes a huge difference whether Rei is there or not.

It was a shame that Rei wouldn’t be in Gilm until after the New Year, but it wasn’t a situation I couldn’t handle.

“I understand. I would have preferred Rei to stay, but you’re not my subordinate. If you’re going, I can’t stop you. …Lady Elena, Rei might cause you some trouble, but I’m counting on you.”

“Hmm. I’ll take care of it. …Though, I have a feeling it’s almost certain there’ll be a commotion the moment we bring Rei back.”

“No doubt about it.”

Heheh. Elena and Daskar exchanged glances and smiled at each other.

Rei wanted to voice his dissatisfaction, but he fully understood from his interactions since becoming an adventurer that he simply didn’t get along with ordinary… or rather, typical nobles.

That was precisely why, in the end, Rei could do nothing but remain silent as he listened to their conversation.

Seeing the three of them like this, Ara let out a silent chuckle.

“Well then, I suppose I’ll take my leave here. You’d better let Walker know, Rei. Besides, you’ve got a very eager receptionist waiting for you.”

It was obvious to anyone who heard Elena’s words exactly who she was referring to.

Rei, realizing exactly who she was referring to, nodded without objection.

“Hmm, I see. Even though I’ve got some breathing room now, I still have work to do.”

Daskar looked at the pile of papers on his desk with a slightly annoyed expression.

Although the pile had shrunk compared to before, Daskar had never been particularly fond of paperwork to begin with.

Still, disliking it and actually being suited for the job were two different matters. The fact that he possessed the ability to sufficiently handle his duties as the lord of Gilm… was that ultimately a good thing or a bad thing for Daskar?

As a former knight, Daskar essentially preferred physical activity, but he still had to handle the paperwork properly.

Looking as though it were a bother... yet, perhaps the conversations with Rei, Elena, and Ara had provided enough of a change of pace, for he stood up from the sofa and headed toward his desk... Suddenly, Daskar stopped in his tracks and turned around.

“Lady Elena, just to be sure, you’ll let me know before you leave Gilm, won’t you?”

“Mm-hmm. Of course, I wouldn’t dream of leaving without saying a word.”

“I see. Well, that’s good. If you and the others were to leave without a word, it would certainly come as a surprise to us.”

With those words, Rei and the others left the office and were escorted outside by a maid waiting outside the room.

Since Rei and the others had already visited the lord’s mansion numerous times, they didn’t really need a guide… but since they couldn’t be allowed to move about the mansion freely, it was likely a necessary precaution.

After all, as the lord’s manor, it’s only natural that various classified matters exist there.

Furthermore, there was a possibility of things that shouldn’t be seen by others, and if they were to accidentally catch a glimpse of such things, Daskar would have to take various measures.

And so, when they stepped out of the lord’s mansion… since Set and the others, who had been playing in the garden, had joined them, they naturally stood out.

Of course, Rei and the others were riding in the carriage provided by Daskar, but even so, it wasn’t difficult to identify who was inside just by looking through the window…

“Lady Elena, isn’t that you!? If you don’t mind, I’d like to speak with you for a moment…”

“Hold on! I’m the one doing business with Lady Elena! A small-time merchant like you should just stay out of the way!”

Such voices could be heard from outside the carriage, but Rei, Elena, and even the coachman, Ara, ignored them and continued on.

For the merchants, while trading with Daskar is profitable, it’s likely just as important to get to know Elena, who goes by the nickname “Princess General.”

“There are still merchants out there working hard. They really should be heading back to their hometowns soon; things are going to get tough from here on out.”

“Those merchants probably came to Gilm without a plan. They likely thought that if they just made it to Gilm, things would work out somehow. But…”

“It wasn’t that easy, was it?”

As Rei spoke, picking up where Elena left off, she stroked Yellow, who was sitting on her lap, and nodded in response.

“Hmm. Of course, those who arrived earlier probably made some profit… but the merchants who remain here are mostly the ones who got a late start. Though, there are probably some unlucky ones among them who simply came from places far away from Gilm.”

“Ah… I guess there’s nothing to be done about that.”

Rei says, with a hint of sympathy.

Unlike in Japan, where transportation like airplanes, trains, and cars is well-developed, in this world—with the exception of a few like Rei—people basically get around on foot or by horse-drawn carriage.

That inevitably makes traveling long distances time-consuming, resulting in late arrivals in Gilm and missed business opportunities.

(When I think about it that way, I’m not entirely opposed to helping them out… but if I were to leak monster materials or something to someone, I have a feeling that would just bring a whole lot of trouble my way.)

Recalling how merchants swarmed him over the Twilight Spear incident, Rei decides he has no interest in repeating that experience and rules out the option of helping them.

Some of the merchants were incredibly pushy, and Rei had no desire to get involved with people like that.

Deciding it was pointless to dwell on the matter any further, Rei glanced around the interior of the carriage he was riding in and changed the subject.

“This carriage… I heard we bought it from Gimekara—or, to be precise, from the Zolge Trading Company. Did you know that?”

“Oh? So, is this the carriage Sura and the others rode in? I heard that they were supposed to buy the carriages and horses they borrowed themselves.”

“Yeah. But since they’re buying them individually, naturally some choose not to buy them. Gimekara apparently sells the carriages they’ve bought from those people all over the place. At this time of year, carriages sell as fast as they come.”

With winter approaching, many merchants have gathered in Gilm.

Among these merchants, there are many who have bought too much stock in Gilm to transport themselves, as well as others who need carriages for various other reasons.

For Gimekara, who brought both the carriages and the horses to pull them as a set, these merchants are the perfect business partners.

Furthermore, aside from merchants, there are others like Daskar who simply want as many carriages as they can get.

Given this situation, Gimekara had no trouble finding customers.

And with the money earned from selling the carriages and horses, she is renovating the building that will serve as the main branch of the Zolge Trading Company.

“I bet Gimekara had this in mind from the very beginning. Otherwise, he wouldn’t be able to buy carriages and horses so easily.”

Elena nodded in agreement at Rei’s words.

“That’s quite the merchant-like move. Though, since they have to set aside the portion that Sura and the others plan to buy, the maintenance costs alone are likely to be a huge burden.”

“I won’t deny that. Considering there are hundreds of horses alone, I can’t even imagine how much we’re spending just on feed.”

“It’s surprising enough that they even prepared stables large enough to house that many horses in the first place.”

Of course, if Gilm were still the same Gilm as before, he wouldn’t have been able to pull something like that off.

It was only thanks to the expansion work that they had the capacity to do so.

Even so, it feels like it was only possible because the Zolge Trading Company pulled some strings to find several inns and stables willing to take in the horses, and only then...

As they were talking like that, the carriage continued on, and soon arrived in front of the guild.

“Lady Elena, Rei, we have arrived.”

Nodding at Ara’s voice, the two stepped down from the carriage… and suddenly, it struck Rei as a question.

“Hey, Ara. I think this carriage belongs to Lord Daskar, so how are we going to return it? You’re obviously going to be traveling with us, right?”

“That’s not a problem. It seems the guild has made arrangements to return it for us.”

“……Is that so?”

Given that the Guild and Daskar have established friendly relations, that wouldn’t be surprising.

Thinking that, Rei nodded.

“Grrrrr!”

When he looked over at the sound of Set’s delighted growl, he saw Set happily eating a skewer of Gamelion meat.

Rei thought to himself that Gamelion meat was fairly expensive, but when he saw who had given Set that skewer, it all made sense.

Because it was Johanna who had given him the skewer.

As Milein’s rival, Johanna was likely thinking that she wanted to monopolize Set as much as possible while Milein was away.

(Come to think of it, when are Milein and the others coming back? Since they’re negotiating with the Kingdom of Reburina, I doubt they’ll be back before winter… So, spring? No, but still…)

Logically speaking, no matter how quickly the negotiations wrap up, it seems impossible for them to return to Gilm before winter.

That said, if Set were here, it would be a different story… but if Set were here in the first place, Milein wouldn’t have come back to Gilm just so she could be the only one to admire him.

Could Milein—who is such a Set enthusiast… or perhaps even a Set addict—really accept not seeing Set for an entire winter?

When put that way, Rei couldn’t help but tilt her head in confusion… and she felt a slight pang of anxiety, wondering if Mireine might actually push herself to return to Gilm even in the middle of winter.
