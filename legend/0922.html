Only Rei and Glinch remained standing inside the gymnasium.

Everyone else, regardless of gender, is either sitting on the floor or collapsed.

The sweat glistening on their faces wasn't from physical exertion, but rather from the disbelief that they had been defeated so one-sidedly.

It was less about exhaustion and more about sitting on the floor, unable to believe the reality unfolding before their eyes.

Starting with Insla, who fought Rei first, Rei battled all forty students and defeated every single one.

Moreover, it was clear from the way he fought that he hadn't even been using his full strength.

Even when the students swung their weapons, their attacks rarely even grazed Rei, let alone hit the spear he held in his hand.

On the rare occasion a weapon did strike the spear, the student would find their weapon wrapped around it and vanishing from their grasp.

And it was only their own weapons that vanished like that. Rei still held his spear... and before they knew it, its tip was thrust right before their eyes.

That such feats were accomplished with such ease was nothing short of a nightmare for the students of Class S, Year 4—the highest-ranking class, gathered from the top performers of their year, who considered themselves the elite of this officer academy.

Many students were deeply shocked, having swung their weapons repeatedly to deflect the spearhead before them, only to find it thrust at them again the very next moment.

They couldn't comprehend how a spear could move in such a way to achieve that feat.

Many were confident in their own abilities.

But that confidence was strictly confined within the academy walls. Many learned the hard way that once they stepped outside, they were nowhere near as formidable as they had imagined.

"Impressive, Rei. To put up such a fight against this many opponents. Honestly, I didn't expect you to be this good."

To the polite words of praise coming from Glink's mouth, Rei responded with a shake of his head.

"It's nothing to get that worked up about. There are plenty of stronger guys out there. I'm still not strong enough to take on someone like that. ...Well, more importantly..."

Interrupting his conversation with Glink, Rei's gaze shifted to the students sitting on the floor, staring blankly ahead.

The gaze directed at them held a hint of exasperation.

"Did you guys seriously think you could beat me? Well, I guess it's not surprising considering Set isn't here and you don't have my signature weapon, the Death Scythe... But I'm a Rank B adventurer, you know? I can't exactly be struggling against some greenhorns."

Hearing Rei's words, every student present gritted their teeth.

It didn't matter if they were lying on the ground or sitting slumped on the floor.

His words carried an air of inevitability, as if his victory were some natural law, as certain as snow settling on the ground.

Resentment took root in their hearts, and some even harbored feelings bordering on hatred toward Rei.

Seeing these students, Rei was inwardly impressed.

(Huh. Not a single one of them broke spirit after getting beaten so one-sidedly. While the intensity of their feelings varied, the fact that none of them dropped out here proves they're truly the elite of this place.)

Some likely had a stubborn refusal to lose. Others clung to pride, or any number of other emotions. Driven by these feelings, all forty stood their ground, refusing to let their spirits be crushed.

(This might actually make them pretty strong. This request was mostly just a way to kill time, but doing it like this might actually be quite interesting.)

Facing the students whose eyes declared they would never lose their spirit even if defeated in battle, Rei smiled.

He himself thought it was an amused smile directed at them, but to the students receiving it, it was a smile that could only be described as ferocious.

Could it be... we've done something wrong? It was the kind of smile that made them think that for a moment.

That feeling proved correct the very next moment.

"Alright, I'm feeling inspired. What we just did was one-on-one battles with everyone, so next, let's change things up a bit..."

What words would come next from Rei's mouth?

As that thought crossed the students' minds, Rei, whose gaze was fixed on them, smiled and declared:

"Me against all of you. One against forty."

For a moment, not only the students, but even Grink couldn't comprehend what Rei had said.

Sure, Rei had hinted at it earlier, but no one thought he'd actually go through with it.

Amidst this confusion, it was naturally Instructor Glinck who snapped back to reality first.

"Rei, are you serious? Needless to say, the difficulty increases dramatically when facing forty opponents."

"No problem. As we proceed with these mock battles, we'll be doing more than just one-on-one. We'll cover many-on-many, one-on-many, and many-on-one scenarios too. Think of this as a sort of preview."

"...Understood. Well, you've been through many hellish situations before, Rei. I probably shouldn't even have to say this."

Rei shook his head dismissively at Glink's slight bow.

Then, shifting his gaze back from Glink to the students, he saw the fourth-year S-class students recovering from their momentary loss of confidence.

"You've got to be kidding me... You think you can take on all of us!?"

"True, we lost one-on-one... but do you seriously think you can handle all of us?"

"She may be younger than me, but she's already discarded any notion of mercy."

"Calm down, you guys. The thing is, Deep Crimson single-handedly took down a large force of the Bestir Empire army during last spring's war. Meaning, she's naturally skilled at fighting against large numbers."

"But that's—"

"Yeah. She could probably cast big spells in an open area outside. Plus, she doesn't have the griffin or the scythe from the rumors. Meaning, Shinku isn't in top condition right now."

"...But you saw that fight just now, right? You experienced it, didn't you? You felt it, didn't you? Even if she's not at her best, we're just plain outmatched!"

"We still fight! After being disrespected like that, how could we just let it go?! We... the elite of this military academy, us!"

"Right. Honestly, even in this state, Shinku's strength undoubtedly surpasses ours. But that doesn't mean we have no chance. Call her Shinku or whatever, she's still just one human being. Meaning, she only has one pair of eyes."

"Right! Then we can attack from blind spots like behind him!"

"Yeah. Our attacks should definitely be effective too."

Of course, Rei could hear their conversation.

For Rei, whose senses were sharper than those of ordinary humans, it wasn't difficult.

But while conversing with Glink, he let the students talk freely without saying anything to them.

About ten minutes passed like this...

"Well then, are you all ready?"

Holding a spear for the mock battle, Rei addressed the students.

His gaze held a distinctly provocative edge, further fueling the students' motivation.

All forty students held mock battle weapons in their hands.

Their faces showed a mix of emotions: hostility, rivalry, desperation, fighting spirit, self-mockery, smiles...

However, not a single trace of resignation existed among them.

They stand before Rei, determined to defeat him and earn his recognition.

With that single-minded determination in their hearts, they stood before Rei.

The students, their readiness evident in their posture, raised the spears they held.

"Well then, let's begin. Show me your struggles. When you encounter an overwhelmingly powerful enemy, what will you do? That should be essential knowledge for you, who will become knights and adventurers."

'......'

Rei's words were met with silence.

But the atmosphere emanating from the students was far from resignation.

Confirming this, Rei nodded with satisfaction before turning his gaze to Glink.

"Glink, I'm counting on you."

"Understood. Both sides, please refrain from inflicting fatal wounds on each other."

Though he said both sides, Grink's gaze was fixed on Rei.

Naturally, this warning was directed at Rei, not the students.

The students felt dissatisfied by this, but having been made keenly aware of the disparity in skill during the earlier mock battle, they didn't argue.

Instead, their priority now was to find even the slightest opening in Rei's defense and seize any chance to win.

"Begin."

The moment those words slipped from Grink's mouth, the students present all sprang into action simultaneously.

They didn't charge aimlessly toward Rei, but moved according to the plan they'd discussed beforehand.

Some charged straight at Rei to act as decoys, others drew their bows to support them, releasing arrows—though the tips were rounded for mock combat—while still others moved into positions to encircle Rei.

"Your tactics for fighting multiple opponents are quite polished,"

Rei remarked, though combat for him rarely involved cooperation with others.

He often worked with Set, but Set, created through beast magic, couldn't be compared to other adventurers.

Ordinary adventurers were vastly outmatched by Rei. They would become a hindrance, forcing Rei to divide his attention.

On the other hand, adventurers possessing the skill to truly fight alongside Rei were few and far between.

As a result, it was relatively rare for Rei to act alongside other adventurers.

"Still, they're just students. They're still pretty naive."

The decoy student parried the long sword strike aimed squarely at him with the spearhead, simultaneously deflecting it.

After redirecting the attack along the spear shaft, he immediately entangled the longsword's blade and hurled the ensnared weapon toward the man attempting to swing his sword from the right rear.

He then pulled back his spear, deflecting the strike from the woman attacking from behind with the butt of the spear. Using only a flick of his wrist, he forcibly changed the spear's trajectory, sweeping the woman's legs out from under her and sending her tumbling to the ground.

Not only did he knock her down, but her body rolled directly into the path of the man advancing toward Rei. Unable to step over the woman, the man was forced to change his landing spot... that split-second delay caused him to miss Rei's action.

The man was sent flying sideways by the spear shaft swung horizontally, crashing to the ground and taking another man caught in its path with him.

"What's the matter? Come at me harder!"

At those words, the students' attacks intensified even further, yet not a single blow landed on Rei.

In fact, it was rare for Rei even to need to block with the spear in his hand; most attacks were simply dodged.

The slightest lapse in their attacks resulted in being blown away by a single strike from Rei's spear, knocking them unconscious. Even if they didn't lose consciousness, the damage was often too severe to allow them to get back up.

And yet, the fact he hadn't landed a single fatal blow was proof that Rei still had plenty of room to spare.

"Dammit, everyone, attack him properly! He's completely toying with us!"

The one shouting is Insula, who first sparred with Rei.

Even though he possessed top-tier strength among S-Class, he was still within Rei's margin of error.

The blade of the longsword Insula swung while shouting naturally ended up slicing through the air without touching Rei.

Ducking beneath the longsword's strike to evade, he passed by Insula's side while swinging the spear he held.

The spearhead struck Insula from behind, hitting his right shoulder, and sent him flying forward.

"The rest... six, huh?"

Before he knew it, only six remained standing, still conscious.

Having witnessed Rei's fighting prowess throughout the battle, they must have finally grasped the overwhelming difference in skill. The gaze they now directed at Rei no longer held the fighting spirit that had filled their eyes when the mock battle began.

Still, unable to give up, they all coordinated a simultaneous charge, refusing to go down without a fight... only to be sent tumbling to the ground by Rei's spear, losing consciousness or unable to stand due to the pain of their injuries.

"Well, that's about it. ...Though, you put up a much better fight than I expected. You truly live up to being called this school's elite. ...But, you're still just the elite among students. I think you understood in this fight how vastly different it is compared to actual combat. Starting tomorrow, I'll be pushing you harder. Think carefully about what went wrong today and how you can fix it. Tell the unconscious ones that too."

With that, Rei's first day teaching the fourth-year S-Class came to an end.

...After this, Rei conducted a similar lesson in his other class, the third-year S Class, demonstrating the gap in their abilities.
