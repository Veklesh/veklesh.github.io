"Damn it. There's no doubt that flower is the reason the Treant Forest keeps spreading... Anyway, we gotta deal with that thing first, right?!"

As he spoke, he sliced through the torsos of two Treants charging at him, severing them cleanly.

One defining characteristic of plant-type monsters is their exceptionally high vitality.

For a Treant, severing a branch or two has no real effect.

...Still, severing the trunk means certain death.

And while they weren't particularly fast, their large size made them perfect targets for Rei, wielding the powerful weapon known as the Death Scythe.

A target, yes... but the endless waves of Treants emerging from the depths of the forest were still a major nuisance.

Hearing Rei's exasperated words, Vihera, who had been using Immersion Claw to destroy the Treants from within, shouted.

"Then I'll take care of it. Fighting the Treants is pointless anyway, and above all, we have few effective means to attack them."

Vihera, fighting with her thin robes fluttering in the moonlight, possessed a beauty that drew the eyes of those around her, even amidst the battle.

Among those fighting, many found themselves captivated by Vihera's presence.

Even in this situation, she fought Treants and the vines, deflecting or dodging the seeds falling from above. It was clear she possessed the skill required to participate in this rank-restricted request.

At first, the seeds falling from above were surprising, but they weren't particularly fast.

They simply fell at a normal rate.

Of course, for ordinary humans—say, researchers unaccustomed to combat—even that would make completely avoiding the seeds difficult. But for the adventurers participating in this quest, it wasn't hard.

"That's fine, but what about that giant flower? Doesn't it have any means of attack?"

Under normal circumstances, the thought of an unknown monster's magic stone might have flashed through Rei's mind for an instant. But the monsters in Treant Forest fundamentally didn't possess magic stones.

Given that, it didn't matter who defeated that monster.

However, the reason Rei hesitated was Vihera's means of attack.

The claws and blades formed from magic extending from its gauntlets and greaves had finite length.

They could slash at the Treant's trunk, but unlike Rei's Death Scythe, they couldn't cleave it cleanly in two.

Of course, repeatedly slashing the same spot could eventually sever the trunk, but even if slow, it was still faster than trying to cut through a Treant that was moving around, however slightly. It was quicker to just use the Immersion Palm to attack from within.

"Using the Immersion Palm should work. Worst case, we can attack repeatedly with the gauntlet claws."

"...Alright. Set, can you handle it?"

"GRRRRRRRR!"

After Set mercilessly shattered the Treant's trunk with a single blow from his forepaw, Rei instructed him.

"Carry Vihera and take us to that flower!"

"Guruu!"

At Rei's command, Set delivered one final blow with his forepaw, shattering the trunk of a nearby Treant. He then moved close to Vihera and crouched down low.

He positioned himself to make it easier for Vihera to mount.

"Sorry about this. I'll borrow Set for a bit."

Though Set couldn't fly with anyone but Rei on his back—except for children—carrying someone while running posed no problem at all.

With Vihera now on his back, Set weaved through the gaps between the Treants.

...And yet, as they passed by, Vihera slashed at the Treant's bodies with claws extended from her gauntlets. That was likely the very reason Vihera was Vihera.

Even without pain receptors, the Treants seemed to sense they were under attack. They tried to turn toward Set and Vihera as they passed by.

That was a fatal opening when facing Rei and the others.

Facing the frozen Treant, Rei swung his Death Scythe repeatedly.

The sight of Treant's torso being cleaved in two, one after another, made the other adventurers watching realize with stark clarity that the nickname "Crimson" was no empty title.

"Hmph!"

A short distance away from Rei, Byune hurled long needles one after another.

The needles, launched to blend into the night's darkness, pierced one after another into the areas on the Treant's trunk that resembled faces.

Though the pins didn't seem to cause enough pain to alter its movements, they were sufficient as a momentary distraction.

The momentary pause in the Treant's movement was followed instantly by a spear of rock rising from the ground, piercing it from below.

"I'm not exactly an expert in earth spirit magic... but this much is no problem,"

Marina murmured, a seductive smile playing on her lips.

Holding a bow in her hand, she fired arrow after arrow while issuing commands to those skilled in ranged attacks, including the archers who had gathered.

Under normal circumstances, it would be impossible to handle everything.

But that's precisely why Marina is Marina.

"Is it just me, or does it feel like everything will be resolved if we just leave it all to Marina?"

Watching Marina's lion-like, frenzied performance, Rei murmurs.

"Hmph!"

Byune shouts a short command at Rei, urging him to work harder.

It hadn't been that long since the battle began... or more accurately, since the Crimson Wings joined the fight.

Because of that, Byune still had plenty of stamina left.

Still, she complained to Rei because she knew her own body was small... meaning, while her burst speed was decent, her stamina wasn't particularly high.

Of course, she wasn't fighting at full power the entire time.

She varied her pace, throwing off the timing of monsters like Treants and vines while conserving her stamina.

Such a fighting style had become possible for Byune.

This was likely one result of the combat training she'd continued through the winter.

Urged on by Byune, Rei channeled magic into the Twilight Spear held in his left hand.

"Hmph!"

With a cry of determination, the Twilight Spear shot straight toward Treant, striking true... shattering his torso as it pierced through.

But the Twilight Spear, forged by Azimov's hands, wouldn't stop there.

It shattered and pierced through the torso of one Treant, its speed unabated, then crushed the torso of the Treant behind it, and the torso of the Treant behind that one as well.

One strike... The number of Treants killed by a single strike of the Twilight Spear imbued with magic power exceeded ten.

Considering the number of Treants that, while not killed, had parts of their bodies shattered, the sheer power of that single strike was evident.

And the Twilight Spear, which should have been heading deeper into the Trent forest, shattering Treants and tearing vines apart, had returned to Rei's hand before she realized it.

This was one of the Twilight Spear's abilities—it automatically returned to Rei's hand.

"Whoa... What the hell was that... No way!?"

"Damn... This... This is the one with the alias..."

"How splendid."

The adventurers fighting around Rei and the others let out gasps of astonishment at that single strike.

Even as they cried out, they didn't stop fighting Treants and the vines—that was truly impressive.

"Hmph...!"

As if sensing a moment of slackness when the Twilight Spear returned to his hand, Treant swung a branch—or what served as a hand for him—toward Rei.

Despite being a branch, the strike whipped like a whip. Yet the next instant, it was sliced away by the Death Scythe that Rei swung.

The counterstroke severed Treant's torso... Suddenly sensing something amiss at his feet, Ray leapt backward in an instant.

Then, as if piercing the spot where Rei's body had been just an instant before, a tree sprouted from the ground.

...No, "sprouted" was too gentle a word for it. It would be more accurate to say it burst forth.

Its sharply pointed tip, though made of rock rather than wood, reminded him of the stone spear Marina had used earlier.

(Wait, did it... copy her? Did it see Marina just now?)

Did it learn?

The thought flashed through Rei's mind, but... in truth, the Treant Forest had always employed similar attacks when hunting prey.

This could be called a complete coincidence.

However, the only opponents Rei had fought in Trent Forest were Treant and vine monsters.

This battle marked his first encounter with the giant sunflower-like flower, but he didn't think it had copied anything.

...The problem with imitating something that grows larger is figuring out what exactly to imitate.

"Gyaaaah!"

The tree sprouting from the ground wasn't just trying to impale Rei.

Screams echoed from multiple points across the battlefield.

What happened there became clear the moment one looked.

Though Rei dodged, reacting to such a sudden attack from underground was incredibly difficult.

The screams of those pierced through the soles of their feet echoed around them.

Some frantically tried to help their comrades, but with the trunks piercing through their bodies from the soles of their feet, they were effectively pinned to the ground.

Ignoring that and trying to cut the tree growing from the ground would likely require cutting off their own feet.

"Ah, a new one's appeared! Watch out!"

The warning cry echoed around them once more.

Rei looked toward the source of the voice. There, grass with sharp fangs lunged at an adventurer, only to be deflected by a longsword.

(That's... a Venus flytrap?)

Rei had seen them in books and on TV, but never with his own eyes.

Still, the plant looked remarkably similar to the one called a Venus flytrap.

Though real Venus flytraps weren't large enough to swallow a person whole, and above all, they had thorns but no teeth.

Frankly, Rei didn't even know if Venus flytraps existed in this world.

At least, Rei hadn't seen one in the several years he'd lived here.

(Gilm is a frontier region, and you'd think they'd be common in the forests, surprisingly.)

In this world of Elgin, most things could be dismissed as simply being the frontier.

Thinking such thoughts, Rei swung his Death Scythe.

"Flying Slash!"

The slash released from the Death Scythe cleanly severed the stem portion of the Venus flytrap, where the teeth hadn't yet grown.

It was surprisingly easy, considering it supported fangs large enough to swallow a person and the surrounding parts.

"That Venus flytrap's weak point is its stem! No need to panic—dodge its attacks and target the stem!"

"Venus flytrap? Is that the monster's name?"

The adventurer, who had been about to be attacked by the Venus flytrap, momentarily questioned Rei's words.

But he seemed to understand this wasn't the time for that, forcing himself to cast aside his doubts and shift to attack.

For now, he decided that if Rei said so, this monster must be called a Venus flytrap, and swung his blade at another of the same monsters a short distance away.

...This would be the reason this monster came to be called a Venus flytrap from then on, though Rei didn't know that yet.

Regardless, Rei felt a sense of crisis as new monsters kept appearing one after another.

If he were alone, they were opponents he could handle without much trouble.

But that didn't mean all the adventurers present could handle them.

Especially when facing unknown monsters like the Venus flytrap or trees suddenly sprouting from the ground, ordinary adventurers might be unable to handle them.

"This is troublesome."

Even more troublesome than that was the direction Rei's gaze fell... yes, the place where the researchers were gathered.

If it were ordinary monsters like the Treant, the adventurers assigned to guard them should be able to handle it.

As for the falling seeds, fortunately they had stopped earlier, so they weren't a concern now.

(Probably Vihera, though.)

For a moment, his thoughts drifted to Vihera battling the sunflower monster, but he quickly snapped back to reality.

Yes, the threats from the ground and the sky weren't a concern, but the one emerging from the earth was a different matter entirely.

If you didn't evade the moment they appeared, they'd pierce right through the soles of your feet.

Fortunately... or perhaps for some reason, the trees suddenly emerging from the ground haven't attacked the researchers yet.

But who knew how long that would last?

"Rei, look at that!"

While lost in thought, Marina's voice suddenly echoed around them.

Even amidst the chaos of multiple battles, Rei could clearly hear Marina's voice precisely because it was hers.

Rei stopped giving instructions to the archers and followed Marina's gaze. What he saw was a giant sunflower collapsing to the ground.

"Oh..."

His cry of delight lasted only a moment... The next instant, two, then three new giant sunflowers appeared.
