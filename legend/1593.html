A carriage races through the underground passage.

The driver was one of Ryutas' guards. And beside the driver's seat sat another guard.

Inside the carriage were Rei, Elena, Marina, Vihera, Byune, Ryutas, and two of Ryutas' guards.

Given that the carriage wasn't particularly large, fitting eight people inside was quite a squeeze.

That said, since not all of Ryutas' guards could fit, they had split up from the cave onwards.

About thirty minutes had passed since Rei had stored the research materials gathered at Marina's instruction into the Misty Ring.

Having escaped the cave, Rei and the others were heading to meet up with the Neutral Faction envoy delegation traveling from the Kingdom of Mireana... Meanwhile, anticipating assassins from Jaya if they stayed together, Ryutas and his group were traveling by carriage through the underground passage leading to the Giant's Nest in Mejogo.

"So, what do you guys plan to do now? Honestly, coming with us probably won't accomplish much."

Inside the carriage, Rei asked Ryutas while drinking fruit water he'd taken from the Misty Ring.

The others too... even Ryutas himself held a cup of fruit water in his hand.

Ryutas, who was somehow thirsty, moistened his throat with the fruit water before speaking.

"To be perfectly honest, I'd like to join forces with Rei and the others."

For Ryutas, it was undoubtedly the choice that offered him the best chance of survival.

But Rei shook his head.

"You'd just slow us down."

While Ryutas possessed some combat ability, he was still inferior not only to Rei and the others, but even compared to Byune.

As Rei had said, traveling with someone like Ryutas would only slow them down.

Moreover, Ryutas had his own escort.

Rei would have preferred to avoid traveling with that many people if possible.

"You tell the truth so easily... But is that really okay? My existence should be beneficial to the Kingdom of Mireana, shouldn't it?"

While it was true he was a hindrance, it was also undeniably true that he was the beneficial figure Ryutas had mentioned.

As one of the candidates to succeed Jaya, he undoubtedly possessed information inaccessible to outsiders.

In that sense, Rei couldn't deny that Ryutas was indeed a useful person.

"We don't necessarily have to stay together forever, right? We could just hand him over to an envoy from the Kingdom of Mireana or something."

"Ah... That might be the safest option."

Rei responded to Vihera's words and turned his gaze to Ryutas, asking if that was acceptable.

Ryutas thought for a moment, then nodded.

"Understood. Since we're the ones imposing on them, I can't really be selfish. Besides... the envoys will probably treat me with more courtesy than Rei and the others would."

Those words were calculated.

But as Ryutas said, it was undeniably true that the envoys from the Kingdom of Mireana currently valued him the most.

Of course, safety-wise, it was problematic compared to staying with Rei and the others.

But he had his own guards, and this was an envoy sent to a country where organizations hostile to Gilm were based.

Naturally, to protect themselves, they'd likely select skilled guards.

"Young Master!"

Suddenly, one of the guards on the carriage driver's platform called out to Ryutas.

For a moment, Ryutas wondered if it was an enemy attack, but there was no tension in the voice.

Feeling slightly relieved, he opened his mouth.

"What is it!?"

"Looks like a few guys are heading this way. What should we do?"

"Wait, what?! Aren't those guys enemies!?"

In this situation, anyone coming from the giant's nest they were heading towards would almost certainly be considered enemies.

Despite that, I wondered why they showed no signs of caution... But when we passed those people in the carriage, seeing they carried no weapons or anything else made it clear they weren't hostile toward us.

The people moving through the underground passage also looked puzzled when they saw the carriage approaching, as if wondering why it was coming now.

From their perspective, the carriage carrying Rei and the others moving through here must naturally belong to Jaya. That was surely the source of their confusion.

Currently, it was impossible to leave the giant's nest.

...Well, technically, they could leave, but attempting such a thing would undoubtedly draw attacks from Set, who was rampaging in the sky. More importantly, the prostitutes and resistance fighters who had been freed from their brainwashing were lying in wait.

Among the prostitutes, there were former adventurers, former soldiers... and in some cases, even former knights. Quite a few were attacking Jaya's soldiers.

What's more, fallen soldiers have their weapons taken. The longer this goes on, the more disadvantageous the situation becomes for Jaya.

Given the current situation, it's far from safe outside. Staying holed up in the giant's nest could eventually become untenable.

Given that, it wouldn't be strange to think about escaping Mejogo using the secret passage.

In reality, the cave itself was experiencing chaos from the giants' rampage, and the damage was extensive.

Still, it was a somewhat ironic outcome that it was safer than Mejogo, which was currently in the midst of major chaos.

"There are more people now."

"...This was the giant's nest, right? When we searched it, there were hardly any people. They must have been hiding, after all."

Rei's mutter drew a response from Vihera, who murmured with an expression that was half exasperated, half impressed.

Belonging to Jaya, she felt no particular affection for them.

But not liking someone didn't mean he couldn't acknowledge their judgment.

"Yeah. Well, we did have a pretty spectacular fight before we even got to the giant's nest. Considering that, they had plenty of time to hide. ...Though some of them probably overslept or something."

"Yeah... But couldn't we have avoided using this place to get back to Mejogo? If we'd moved from outside the forest..."

"If Mejogo is in chaos, entering it would be troublesome. Moving a carriage through a crowd would be especially difficult. If it were just the Jaya people, we could just charge right in with the carriage."

Elena's words carried no hint of jest; she seemed to genuinely believe it from the bottom of her heart.

If they belonged to Jaya, running them over wouldn't be a problem at all.

Hearing those words, Ryutas and the guards were once again reminded that Elena standing before them... no, that all of them, including Elena, harbored anger toward Jaya.

(Surrendering immediately was the right call. Because we approached them with a friendly attitude first, they judged they could get information about Jaya from us and decided to spare our lives. ...If we'd taken a hostile stance, we would've definitely been killed. In my case, given my position...)

Seeing Elena's state, he forgot to even make his usual expression as he thought such things.

Still, he breathed a sigh of relief that he hadn't been killed by Rei and the others... especially Elena, Marina, and Vihera, who couldn't tolerate what was happening to women in Mejogo.

(It was fortunate that I had met Rei regarding the bandit incident.)

It was an order bordering on harassment from someone who didn't think highly of Ryutas, but accepting that request meant he was still alive.

Ryutas found this strange connection both pleasing and amusing.

"Young master, we're approaching the giant's nest!"

The voice of the escort at the driver's seat echoed around them.

At those words, everyone's expressions tightened slightly.

The exact state of the giant's nest, and Mejogo, was unknown.

They could make guesses, but those were just guesses.

Therefore, it was certain that it was better to be prepared to respond immediately if any unforeseen circumstances arose.

That was the thought...

"No bodies?"

We reached the area near the hidden door... the very spot where reinforcements had been sent from the cave Rei and the others had been in just moments ago. Yet, not a single corpse lay scattered there.

Of course, as proof that the battle here wasn't a dream, bloodstains, fragments of weapons and armor... even several severed fingers lay scattered on the ground.

But the bodies themselves were nowhere to be found.

The number of soldiers who died here couldn't possibly be just ten or so.

That very fact made the complete absence of bodies feel deeply unsettling.

"We just passed those guys earlier. Maybe they moved them out of the way so they wouldn't be in the way when they came through?"

"I understand what you're saying, Ryutas... but do you really think people fleeing in such a hurry would bother doing something like that?"

Rei's words left Ryutas speechless.

Indeed, the people they had passed while traveling by carriage had been moving quite urgently.

It was as if they were trying to get as far away from Mejogo as possible.

If you asked whether people like that would have time to clear away all the bodies lying here... it would be a bit difficult.

"So, it really was those coachmen, then?"

"That's the most likely possibility."

Vihera's words met Elena's agreement.

Rei, too, understood that it was probably the most likely possibility, if only by process of elimination.

Still, he wondered what reason mere coachmen would have for going to such lengths.

(Could it be they actually had a sense of camaraderie? Though judging by how they acted, that seems a bit hard to believe.)

To Rei, the relationship between the coachmen and the soldiers didn't seem that close.

But he quickly shifted his thoughts.

Fundamentally, the absence of corpses on the ground during their movement posed no inconvenience whatsoever to Rei and her group.

Therefore, there was no need to dwell on the circumstances now.

(Though, if any giants had actually survived and gone berserk, devouring corpses to satisfy their hunger... then they'd need to deal with that, of course.)

But judging by the surroundings, that worry was unnecessary.

He dismounted from the halted carriage, keeping a watchful eye on his surroundings.

Even someone without senses as sharp as Rei's... even an ordinary human could probably still detect the lingering scent of blood here.

Though, to be honest, Rei didn't particularly want to be friends with anyone who found that smell pleasant.

"Let's go."

Everyone nodded at Rei's words, uttered with a slight frown beneath his hood.

And so, everyone who had disembarked from the carriage entered the giant's nest.

Though everyone remained alert to their surroundings, there was no sign of attack—not from the giants... nor from the Jaya people.

However, the moment they entered the room where the black crystal had originally been, an intense stench of blood wafted through the air.

Looking in the direction of the smell, they saw a massive pile of corpses stacked there.

"These must be the ones who attacked this place. ...If they were brought here and gathered over there, does that mean the coachman did it?"

Thinking this, Rei felt slightly unconvinced.

Corpses are incredibly heavy. Especially trained soldiers, and even more so when they're fully equipped.

The idea that the coachman... and even if he enlisted help from those who luckily survived the battle... could have moved them all still raised doubts.

From what Rei had seen, they didn't strike him as the type to be that loyal.

(Well, since they weren't eaten, it's safe to assume it wasn't the giants' doing. Then, maybe I shouldn't worry about it too much right now. For now, the priority is getting out of the giant's nest and meeting up with Set, who's probably rampaging around here somewhere.)

He came to that conclusion, averting his gaze from the mountain of corpses... For a moment, he glanced toward where the black crystal had been, but naturally, nothing remained there now.

Meanwhile, Ryutas also stared intently at the spot where the black crystal had been.

Unlike Rei, it wouldn't be strange for Ryutas to have various thoughts about the organization known as Jaya.

And within Jaya, the black crystal was an important artifact in many ways.

It wouldn't be strange if Ryutas felt somewhat melancholy.

But Rei, unable to waste time here forever, opened his mouth, feeling a little bad about it.

"Ryutas, we should get going. We can't stay here forever."

"Yeah, I know."

Rei's response was more straightforward than Ryutas had anticipated.

Finding this slightly unexpected, yet still grateful for the help in their current situation, Rei and the others began climbing upward through the giant's nest.
