It would be a lie to say Augusto wasn't tense when he heard that report.

But that was only natural.

After all, the person who had come to the mansion was an adversary of Augusto. He had even considered the possibility that the mansion might be attacked by that person's henchmen, which was why he had asked an acquaintance to serve as gatekeeper.

That was precisely why, when Augusto heard from Günther—the man he'd asked to guard the gate—that Daldora had come calling, his eyes widened in utter astonishment.

"...What should we do? We could refuse to meet him..."

Zalust murmured, but after a moment's thought, Augusto shook his head.

"No. If Daldora was attacked too, he must have information that I was attacked. If that's the case, he came all the way to my house because it's related. ...I'll meet him."

"Are you sure?"

The one asking for confirmation was Rei , seated across from Augusto.

Given that they'd gone out of their way to ask an adversary like this time, it wasn't unreasonable to think, as Augusto said, that it was related to the attack.

But conversely, the possibility that they feigned it to launch the attack couldn't be ignored either.

(One person attacked us, but Daldora had multiple attackers. ...From their perspective, it wouldn't be strange at all if they thought the attack on us was a bluff or an alibi, just like we suspected.)

Yes, it stands to reason that they're thinking exactly what Augusto and the others are thinking. It wouldn't be strange at all for them to try to strike first before we could make a serious move.

Still, since Daldora, an important figure in this town, had come to visit, refusing to meet him was not an option.

Well, maybe there was, but who knew what disadvantages might come our way if we pulled such a stunt?

"I'll meet him."

Despite the potential danger to his own life, Augusto's decision to meet Daldora was heavily influenced by Rei's presence.

Having witnessed that power firsthand, Augusto harbored the expectation that with Rei present, he could handle whatever Daldora might throw at him.

(To be honest, I find it rather pathetic to depend so much on a child like this... but still.)

Watching Rei lower his hood to reveal his face, Augusto inwardly felt ashamed of himself.

But appearance and true capability don't always match. Knowing several others like Rei, Augusto quickly shook off the thought.

"Alright, if you say so, I'll escort you too. ...Given the attack incident and all."

"Thank you. I was about to ask you myself."

Augusto was pleased that Rei had offered to escort him before he even had to ask, but Rei waved it off lightly.

For Rei, this mansion was his lodging while in Gosh.

Moreover, the meals prepared by Augusto's wife, Kathy, were exceptionally delicious, and Kathy herself doted on Rei like her own child.

If such a comfortable residence might be attacked by Daldora, naturally he couldn't just leave it be.

"Well then, let's go. If anything happens, I can at least serve as a shield."

Naturally, Zarust intended to join the meeting with Daldora.

If they tried anything, with just Rei and Augusto, Rei would have to protect Augusto, risking letting them do as they pleased.

To prevent that, it's better for me to protect Augusto and let Rei move freely.

(Besides, protecting Augusto was my job to begin with.)

Thus, the three of them would face the meeting with Daldora.

The mansion where Augusto lived was built by wealthy ancestors, so naturally it had many rooms.

Not every room was meticulously cleaned, but those used frequently were maintained diligently.

In one such well-maintained room, the guest room, sat Augusto, Daldora, and the others participating in this discussion.

Though, fundamentally, the conversation was supposed to be between just Augusto and Daldora... yet for some reason, Rei's figure was also present on the sofa.

This was despite Zarust and Daldora's guards standing behind the sofa.

This wasn't something Augusto had requested; it was Daldora's wish.

She wanted to hear the opinion of Rei, a high-ranking adventurer with a nickname, regarding this attack.

Given that, Augusto couldn't refuse.

No, having Rei participate in this discussion was actually a huge help to Augusto.

After all, it was Rei who had defeated the man who had actually attacked Augusto.

Moreover, having Rei present would significantly reduce the risk of being underestimated by the other side.

Of course, his mere presence as a bodyguard was already a powerful asset, but having him seated right beside him was incredibly reassuring for Augusto.

"Mr. Daldora. I hear you were attacked by someone earlier. I'm glad to see you're doing well."

"My, you have quick ears. But speaking of which, I hear you were attacked by a thug yourself, Augusto?"

"Yes. I'm ashamed to say I suffered a rather serious injury during that incident."

Augusto looked slightly surprised at his straightforward admission of injury.

For a bodyguard to be bypassed and injured was a shame.

He probably hadn't expected him to admit it so readily.

This effortlessly thwarted Daldora's plan to seize control of the conversation by exploiting the inadequacy of Augusto's guard.

But without showing any sign of this disappointment, Daldora turned a concerned gaze toward Augusto.

"Is that so? Well then, I can't keep taking up your time like this. How is the wound..."

"Ah, I'm fine. It just so happened that Rei-san was nearby. I recovered easily with the potion Rei-san had."

"...I see."

"No, really, if Rei hadn't been here, I might not be standing here right now."

"A potion capable of healing such a severe injury? It must have been a potion of remarkably high quality. To possess such a potion... I envy you."

"No, no, not at all. I heard you were the one who got injured, Daldora..."

"True. But my guards are excellent. It was barely more than a scratch."

While seemingly praising his own guards, Daldora subtly implied that Augusto's severe injuries were due to weak protection.

Zalust, standing behind Augusto, must have understood this. He flinched for just an instant, but he didn't voice it.

After all, it was undeniably his own incompetence that had left Augusto mortally wounded.

The only reason he survived was because Rei had high-quality potions.

Knowing this, he couldn't bring himself to argue back.

They continued trading barbs for a while longer... until about ten minutes passed, and they finally realized they couldn't keep up this charade. They got down to business.

"Now then, the truth is, I came to see Augusto today regarding this recent attack. ...I heard you captured the perpetrator who attacked Augusto without killing him. I'd very much like you to share that information with us."

"Well, well, your ears are sharp indeed."

"Indeed. To do business in this Gosh, the speed of information is absolutely essential. ...So, what do you say?"

Daldora fixed her gaze intently on Augusto.

Augusto, receiving that gaze, paused briefly before shaking his head.

"Unfortunately, while it's true we captured him, we were merely hired to do so. I know nothing of the details."

At Augusto's words, Daldora's gaze sharpened.

Of course. He was on the brink of obtaining information about those who had targeted his life, yet he seemed utterly unwilling to share any of it.

Naturally, Daldora assumed Augusto was using every means possible to extract information from the prisoners.

That was the fundamental premise, but therein lay the fatal difference between Augusto and Daldora.

Augusto, who could be called humane, versus Daldora, who would stop at nothing to achieve his goals.

That's precisely why Daldora believes Augusto is withholding information to put him, his adversary, at a disadvantage.

"You can't give me information, you say?"

"Huh? No, I mean, I don't have any information to begin with. So what exactly am I supposed to pass on?"

"...You'll regret this, you know? In fact, I'll have no choice but to assume you orchestrated this attack."

"But the information itself..."

There was no point in talking any further.

Just as Daldora decided this and started to stand up...

"Hii!"

A massive blade suddenly materialized before him, making Daldora cry out in shock.

The guards standing behind Daldora tried to move the instant they saw the blade... but before they could react, Rei manipulated the Death Scythe in his hand once more, thrusting the blade toward Daldora's neck to keep the guards at bay.

If Rei moved his hand even slightly, Daldora's neck would be cleanly severed, ending her life.

Faced with the Death Scythe, whose sharpness seemed capable of such a feat, the guards couldn't move.

They couldn't help but think that if Narsus, the most skilled among them, were here... but there was no point in dwelling on someone who wasn't present.

All the guards could do now was try to find an opening in Rei's guard and somehow dislodge the blade of the Death Scythe pressed against Daldora's neck.

Even with a decent amount of space, this was still an indoor location.

Given that, the Death Scythe, a weapon far larger than Rei's height, should be difficult to maneuver.

That fact didn't change, even for an adventurer with a nickname.

Daldora's guards locked eyes, signaling to attack the moment Rei's movements slowed as he used the Death Scythe against the room's furniture... but the next instant, the Death Scythe pressed against Daldora's neck had vanished.

『...』

Not only Daldora, the target of the sudden blade thrust by Rei, but even the guards froze, unable to comprehend what had just happened.

"Things have calmed down for now, right? Listen carefully. You seem to be misunderstanding something. The man we captured truly knows nothing. This isn't a lie or anything else—it's pure fact."

Well, I didn't actually see the interrogation, so maybe he did say something... he thought to himself, but he didn't dare voice it aloud.

In any case, thanks to Rei's sudden, almost impulsive action, the tension in the room, which had been on the verge of exploding, eased somewhat. ...Though to be honest, it was more that everyone was so stunned by Rei's behavior that they'd lost the will to be tense.

Seeing Rei like that, Zarust sighed, covering his face with his right hand.

He was startled by Rei's abrupt action, but even more than that surprise was the fact that he had absolutely no idea when Rei had pulled out the Death Scythe.

He understood the vast difference in their abilities, but even so... he hadn't even noticed the act of drawing the Death Scythe itself...

He had seen Rei take things out of his item box countless times since they met.

But what Rei did now was entirely different. It was, if you will, a combat-ready retrieval.

It wasn't that he drew the Death Scythe, then raised it to press the blade against Daldora's neck. The very act of pulling the Death Scythe from the item box was the act of pressing the blade against Daldora's neck.

He hadn't actually held the weapon until the very instant the attack was about to strike.

That was the terror Zarust had tasted.

...And naturally, Daldora's guards understood the meaning behind that action.

They were forced to recognize anew that even if they all attacked together, defeating him would be impossible.

Yet, seeing Zarust covering his face, enduring the mental strain, he found himself oddly convinced that just having Rei among his comrades was exhausting enough.

"Well, now that things have calmed down, let's talk things over again."

Calm down? Where?! That was probably the inner voice of everyone except Rei.

Unaware of these thoughts, Rei sat down on the sofa.
