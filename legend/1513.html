Mejo-go, a pleasure district in the truest sense, built on brothels and taverns.

In the end, it was Rei who was headed there... Currently, Rei stood alone in the gathering place where people were assembling to board the carriage bound for Mejougo.

"Huh?"

...Well, strictly speaking, he wasn't alone. Including Yellow, who had slipped into his coat, it was more accurate to say one person and one creature.

Normally, Yellow's role would be handled by Set... but Set was far too conspicuous.

Also, Yellow would be needed to fly if urgent communication was required in Mejougo.

If he had the Paired Orb, that wouldn't be necessary, but unfortunately, the Paired Orb Rei possessed was lent to Gilm's Ara.

That's why Rei was at the gathering spot to board the carriage with Yellow.

...Yellow traveling with Rei was surely partly for communication purposes, but Rei suspected it was also because Elena could see Yellow's memories too.

"Oh? Hey, you heading to Mejougo too? You look pretty young... You're quite the adventurer, aren't you?"

One of the men gathered there must have noticed Rei.

He approached Rei with a broad smile, calling out to him.

As usual, Rei was clad in his Dragon Robe, hood pulled tight.

Judging his age from his appearance was difficult, but even with the hood up, Rei's face was faintly visible... and above all, Rei's physique itself was small.

Still, Rei felt relieved that he wasn't told to go home like a child.

(Well, maybe my sense of that sort of thing is numb since I'm heading to Mejougo now.)

Feeling Yellow quietly settled within the Dragon Robe, Rei spoke.

"Yeah, my dad said I should go learn a little about society."

Even as he said it, Rei's tone made his own back tingle slightly as he replied to the man who'd spoken to him.

"Ha ha ha ha. I see, social studies. Mejougo might indeed be a good place for that. The kid's climbing the ladder to adulthood a bit early, huh?"

"Huh? Hey, Kotona, what's up with that kid?"

"Oh, this kid's going to Mejougo for social studies, apparently."

"Huh? Mejougo for social studies? Isn't that dangerous in more ways than one?"

The man who had spoken to Rei... the man who had addressed the one called Kotonara, muttered with concern while looking at Rei.

Both men were in their thirties, right in the prime of their working lives.

That very fact meant their desires were strong, and they were probably looking forward to Mejougo.

Still, the man who had spoken to Kotonara seemed to possess a sensible side.

He seemed slightly hesitant, wondering if it was really okay to take someone like Rei, who was his age, to Mejougo.

But Kotonara patted the man's shoulder several times, smiled, and spoke up.

"Don't worry about it. The first time I held a woman was when I was even younger than that kid over there, you know?"

"...In your case, you were just incredibly curious from a young age, or rather, you had a strong interest in women."

Watching the exchange between the two, who seemed to be childhood friends, Rei worried that if things continued like this, he might be sent back.

He made that judgment and hesitated.

(Was adopting this tone a mistake? No, but I might run into someone who knows my personality and stuff... Thinking about it that way, playing the naive rich kid definitely makes more sense.)

Still troubled, Rei addressed Kotona and the other man.

"Don't worry. I may look like this, but I'm actually pretty confident in my strength. If anything happens, I think I can at least run away."

"...Well, judging by your movements, you do seem like quite the skilled fighter."

The man who had spoken to Kotona remarked, looking at Rei.

If this were Gilm, and both Kotonara and the other man possessed skills above a certain level, they wouldn't have dismissed Rei's movements as merely those of a skilled fighter.

But this wasn't Gilm, where skilled adventurers gathered in large numbers. It was merely the capital of a vassal state of the Mireana Kingdom.

Naturally, there weren't many people skilled enough to be that good, and in fact, even Kotonara and the others couldn't discern his true skill level just by watching him.

"I'm glad you understand. So, you don't need to worry about me. Kotonara-san and... um..."

Seeing Rei stumble over his words, Kotonara spoke up.

"Ah. This is my old buddy, Jill. Nice to meet you."

"Hey, why are you introducing me? ...Well, anyway, this is Jill. Nice to meet you, kid."

"Yes, Kotonara-san, Jil-san, nice to meet you."

Saying this, Rei bowed his head slightly.

A smile played on his lowered face.

Rei knew little about the place they were heading to, Mejougo.

It was just what he'd heard from that guy who'd accosted him at the tavern.

Since even that man claimed he'd never been to Mejougo himself, the information he could gain from Kotonara and Jill—who, from the looks of it, had been there many times before—would be invaluable.

In that sense, putting on this act had paid off... that's what he thought.

When Rei lifted her head to look around, she saw that the crowd had grown slightly larger than before.

Thinking that Mejougo must be raking in the profits, a faint irritation surfaced within Rei.

If only those who came to Mejougo were ordinary people seeking prostitutes, Rei wouldn't have thought much of it.

While Rei himself wouldn't go, he might even admire it as the way things were done in this country of Reburina.

A town built solely around brothels and taverns... Logically speaking, it wasn't a bad approach at all.

Since customers came purely for drink and women, depending on the services offered, Mejougo would likely earn an absurd amount in a single night.

Moreover, if Mejougo's existence was known not only in the Kingdom of Reburina but also in neighboring countries, the number of customers would increase even further.

But... that was only true if it was done solely by those who chose to be prostitutes.

Even if they bought women who had become slaves through debt or crime and made them prostitutes, Rei could accept that.

But what Ajax did—abducting women from other villages and towns, even from other countries, and forcing them into prostitution—was utterly unacceptable.

(Besides, Ajax's actions were riddled with contradictions. Abducting women to make them prostitutes? Fine. But the number of prostitutes who could work in Mejougo was surely fixed. There was no way they could just increase the number of prostitutes indefinitely. Doing so would lead to a shortage of land.)

Even if this isn't the frontier, an outer wall to keep out intruders is probably necessary.

Especially since Mejougo is a place where women, alcohol, and money all converge, making it prime prey for bandit gangs.

Therefore, it's only natural to surround it with walls, which inevitably limits the available land.

Bringing women to such a place one after another to turn them into prostitutes would inevitably lead to a surplus of prostitutes and a shortage of brothels.

(And Ajax probably isn't the only one bringing women here. In fact, the Intelligence Unit hinted at that possibility too.)

Given that, while the number of prostitutes kept increasing, the number of prostitutes in Mejougo had to constantly decrease.

If it were just a few individuals, there might be a chance for them to be bought out...

In other words, it was certain that something was causing the number of prostitutes to decrease.

If we could confirm the circumstances surrounding that, this mission would be a complete success as reconnaissance.

"Oh, here they come. Hey, you too, get ready to board the carriage."

"Ah, yes. ...Come to think of it, I haven't introduced myself. I'm Ren."

"Ren, huh? I suppose I should say, 'Welcome to the adult world.'"

Kotona smiled at Rei, who had introduced himself under the alias Ren, just as the carriage came to a stop.

There were five carriages.

They were also fairly decent carriages. It would be difficult for an ordinary merchant to prepare this many.

(Looks like they're making quite a profit after all.)

As he marveled at this, the carriages finally came to a stop.

The doors opened, and they began boarding the carriages in order.

To gather information, Rei naturally lined up to board the same carriage as Kotona and Jill.

"Hey, wait a minute! Why?! I got to go to Mejougo before, didn't I?!"

That voice reached Rei's ears as he waited in line to board the carriage.

He turned his gaze toward the source of the voice. There, a man was being dragged out of the line by two others who had grabbed him by the shoulders.

"That's true. But you did assault a prostitute, didn't you? And not just once or twice. Countless times. You've been warned about this many times before, haven't you? Am I mistaken?"

"Well..."

When the coachman said that, the man couldn't say another word.

It was likely because what the coachman said was true.

(Come to think of it, they did say they screen out unsuitable customers.)

Thinking this, Rei turned his gaze to the man standing before the carriage door.

Judging from their exchange, it seemed those men were tasked with excluding anyone deemed unfit for Mejougo.

Kotona and Jill boarded the carriage without issue.

Following their lead, Rei also tried to board the carriage... At that instant, the two men waiting beside the carriage door shifted slightly, but they didn't say anything further to Rei and remained still.

Judging that he must have passed, he climbed into the carriage.

Inside the carriage, contrary to its flashy exterior, it was quite cramped.

Seats had been added to accommodate more passengers.

For a moment, Rei wondered if the nobility of this country might complain about this. But upon reflection, none of the people gathered earlier seemed to be nobles.

Therefore, he concluded nobles probably had their own dedicated carriages.

(Well, you can't see inside from the outside anyway.)

That this carriage was headed for Mejougo was something anyone with a little knowledge could figure out.

Which meant there were people who wanted to treat Mejougo as something special.

"Hey, Ren. Sit over here. Somewhere else, you might get crushed."

"Understood. ...But how can you get out of Rossi at night? Normally, the gates are closed after dark, aren't they?"

"Huh? Ah, that's no problem. Probably some stuff going on higher up... but it doesn't matter to us. As long as we can enjoy the taverns and brothels, that's enough."

"Huh... But isn't the gate closed at night because it's dangerous? What if a band of thieves attacks? A group moving around openly like this at night seems like prime prey for bandits to me."

If this were near Gilm, Rei would be more worried about monster attacks than bandits.

But this wasn't the frontier town of Gilm.

Of course, monsters like goblins could appear, but even so, the higher probability encounter would be with bandits.

Though Rei voiced this concern, Kotonara smiled and looked toward the front of the carriage... likely at the driver ahead.

"Didn't you see how easily they subdued that adventurer man earlier? Every single guard on this carriage is a skilled fighter."

"Skilled... So the one who subdued him was an adventurer too?"

At those words, Kotonara... and Jill, who had been listening beside him, froze.

No, not just those two. Even the others in the carriage who had heard Rei's words froze.

(Did I go too far?)

Rei glanced around, briefly wondering that, but there was no taking back the words spoken mere seconds ago.

A few seconds of silence.

Fortunately, however, Kotonara broke the silence the very next moment.

"Well, Ren, you're still young, so I get that you're curious about all sorts of things. But there are some things in this world you're absolutely better off not being curious about. Don't ever forget that, okay?"

"Yes. Honestly, I don't know what I shouldn't have asked, but I'll be more careful next time."

"Ha ha ha ha. Apologizing without even knowing what you did wrong? This kid's got guts."

Kotona's words dissolved the tension inside the carriage.

Rei smiled along with everyone else, though he couldn't help wondering what would happen if he actually said something like, "Actually, I'm a Rank B adventurer with a nickname..."
