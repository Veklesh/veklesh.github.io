A short while after Rei and Set finished their breakfast—or rather, since they were eating at Dusk Wheat inn, it was more of an after-breakfast snack—and just as the dim evening air outside had finally given way to full daylight, the dissection of the Giganto Turtle was well underway.

That said, they were still in the middle of the process, starting with skinning it to extract the meat from its legs.

As one would expect from the hide of a high-rank monster, the Giganto Turtle’s hide was undoubtedly a rare material.

Since we hadn’t tanned it yet, we didn’t know exactly what kind of material it was.

“Oh my, that’s quite… well, impressive.”

It must have been because it had been a while. Vihera and Byune, who had just finished breakfast at Dusk Wheat inn, came over to watch the Giganto Turtle being butchered and let out that exclamation.

Even to Vihera, the Giganto Turtle’s size must have been astonishing. …Though, due to the missing head, it was considerably smaller than its original size.

“Hmm.”

Beside Vihera, Byune, as usual, muttered a single word under her breath while gazing at the gigantic body of the Giganto Turtle before her.

“If you’d like, why not try your hand at the dissection? It might actually be pretty interesting.”

“I’ll pass on that. …I don’t like getting dirty.”

In response to Rei’s suggestion, Vihera immediately shook her head. Her gaze fell upon the figures in front of her, who were peeling the skin from the Giganto Turtle’s legs while staining their cold-weather robes with its blood and bodily fluids.

Getting dirty was inevitable when skinning an animal, but Vihera didn’t really want to join them. Above all…

“I suppose I prefer that sort of thing, if I had to choose. …No, maybe I should hold off.”

Adventurers fighting monsters drawn by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood.

But the monsters the adventurers were fighting were clearly mostly kobolds.

...Yes, the kobolds that should have been attacking Gilm.

(Huh? Could it be that the kobolds—who should have been attacking Gilm—are coming over here, drawn by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood?)

Rei had this thought, but when he thought about it, it made perfect sense.

As you can tell from their dog-like faces, kobolds have a keen sense of smell.

So, if such a kobold caught the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood, it would only make sense for it to come this way.

That said, since I didn’t know why the kobolds had been attacking Gilm day after day, it wasn’t a given that they’d come just because of the smell of blood…

(Since a kobold has actually shown up, my prediction was off. Though, expecting to completely predict a monster’s behavior was probably impossible to begin with.)

The reason Vihera is looking at the kobold with a displeased—no, a bored expression—is likely because fighting a kobold just isn’t interesting.

Although Vihera enjoyed combat, she preferred fighting opponents who could hold their own against her—she didn’t enjoy simply trampling over them one-sidedly.

If such a battle were necessary, Vihera wouldn’t hesitate, but she wouldn’t go out of her way to seek one out.

“Well, it doesn’t look like we need to get involved, anyway.”

To begin with, the monsters drawn by the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood—perhaps influenced by the fact that it was winter—were highly likely to be powerful.

Given that, the guards hired to protect those dismantling the carcass were, naturally, highly skilled individuals.

For people like that, dealing with something like a kobold was no big deal.

In fact, right where Rei was looking, a battle was unfolding that could rightly be called a rout.

But as Rei watched the battle, a sudden question crossed his mind.

Unlike goblins, kobolds have a nature that values their comrades.

Yet, even when their comrades were injured, they showed no sign of trying to help or shield them; on the contrary, they seemed completely unconcerned even when their comrades were killed.

“Those kobolds… they’re strange. Well, I suppose it’s strange that they’ve been attacking Gilm day after day. Besides, I don’t even know why there are so many of them.”

“Not caring about their comrades? People have been saying that ever since the kobolds started attacking Gilm, but in the end, we still don’t know the specific reason why that is.”

Vihera replied immediately to Rei’s words.

The fact that she could understand what Rei was wondering without him finishing his sentence was likely because she’d known him for quite some time.

Incidentally, where Rei’s gaze was fixed, Byune had somehow drawn the White Cloud—a blade with a pure white edge—and was now locked in battle with a kobold.

“Ugh… Is that okay?”

“Well, I guess it’s fine? It’s not like we’re not allowed to fight kobolds unless we’ve accepted a request or anything.”

Since she hadn’t accepted an extermination request, Byune wouldn’t receive any reward for defeating the kobolds.

However, since the guild will buy back the magic stones and proof-of-extermination parts, it’s not a complete waste.

It’s precisely because Byune understands this that she’s quickly cutting off the kobold’s right ear—the proof of the kill—after defeating them.

“In Byune’s case, I suppose she also just wants to get some exercise.”

“Her body? Doesn’t she do any combat training or anything?”

Hearing those words from Vihera, Rei asked in surprise.

After all, given Vihera’s personality, he thought there was no way she wouldn’t be doing combat training.

In response to Rei’s words, Vihera smiled and shook her head.

“Not at all. I do the bare minimum… just enough combat training to keep my body from getting rusty. Fortunately, the backyard at the Dusk Wheat inn is fairly spacious.”

Rei knew that, too.

Since it’s right next to the stables, he used to play around there with Set in the past.

“But is that really just enough to keep your body from getting rusty?”

“Yes, that’s right. My goal this winter is to maintain my current combat strength while improving my skills as a thief, like lockpicking. That’s why I was able to pick the lock on Rei’s room, wasn’t it?”

“Ah… I see.”

Hearing Vihera’s words, Rei recalled the time he returned from Anesis and entered the room he was renting at the Dusk Wheat inn.

Under normal circumstances, picking the lock of someone else’s room and entering without permission is absolutely unacceptable.

But when it comes to party members, things are a little different.

Besides, in Rei’s case, he didn’t keep any personal belongings in the room he was renting; it was purely a place for him to sleep. Even when someone went ahead and unlocked the door to enter without permission, he didn’t feel particularly angry about it.

“I feel like I was told something like that…”

In fact, as a thief, Byune currently possesses extremely high combat prowess.

Considering her age, it’s almost unbelievable.

But since Byune is, after all, a thief, it’s also true that her skills as a thief should take priority.

There are thieves out there who prioritize combat ability, but… to be honest, within the Crimson Wings, the group Byune belongs to, everyone except Byune—Rei, Vihera, and Marina—possessed extremely high-level combat prowess.

In that context, no matter how much Byune improved her combat prowess, it wouldn’t be meaningless… though the effect would likely be minimal.

Rather, there was no doubt that purely improving her skills as a thief would be more effective.

“Wouldn’t it be better for Byune to improve her skills as a thief?”

“I won’t deny that.”

After all, Rei possesses senses sharper than those of an ordinary person and can sense auras and the like.

As for Set, his senses in that regard are so sharp that they surpass even Rei’s.

Given that, the presence of a thief isn’t all that crucial when it comes to detecting enemies.

But… considering traps, lockpicking, and the various other specialized skills a thief possesses, it’s still better to have one along.

Of course, if push comes to shove, we could always temporarily recruit a rogue operating solo, but there’s a difference in trust between someone who’s just joined the party on a temporary basis and someone we’ve been working with for a long time.

“Right? Besides, Byune himself requested that we do it this way.”

“……I see.”

The brief silence was likely because Vihera was, once again, amazed by her own ability to grasp what Byune—who usually only says “Hmm”—was trying to say.

Back in the Labyrinth City of Exil, where Rei first met Byune, Byune had actually spoken quite a bit after they’d finally resolved the commotion.

But when he came to Gilm with Vihera, he had reverted to saying nothing but “Hmm.”

Rei knew from their time in Exil that it wasn’t that she couldn’t speak… but even so, he had no choice but to accept that Byune’s existence was simply defined by her saying nothing but “Hmm.”

Despite this, for some reason—truly, for some reason—Vihera alone could understand what Byune was trying to say.

Therefore, by having Vihera act as a sort of interpreter, Rei and the others are able to understand the details of what Byune is trying to say.

That said, since Rei and the others have known Byune for quite some time now, they can somehow sense the nuances even from a single “Hmm.”

Even as they carried on this conversation, Byune used the White Cloud to take down the kobolds one after another.

Rei watched the battle, which could almost be called a rampage, but then he glanced over at Set and realized the answer to his earlier question.

(Ah, come to think of it, it is strange that the kobolds are attacking even though Set is here. And the other monsters, too... Does the scent of the Giganto Turtle’s blood attract monsters that much? ...Well, I suppose it’s not entirely unreasonable.)

The Treant Forest, where the Giganto Turtle had appeared, was a place full of mysteries in many ways.

Considering that, it wasn’t surprising that the Giganto Turtle that had emerged from the Treant Forest was different from ordinary monsters; rather, it was something he should accept as a matter of course.

For a moment, I couldn’t help but wonder if the Giganto Turtle had something to do with the Kobolds attacking Gilm day after day. But when I thought about it, the attacks on Gilm had been happening even before Rei and the others returned to Gilm from Anesis.

If that was the case, it was clear that the kobold incident had nothing to do with the Giganto Turtle.

……Though I can’t completely rule out the possibility that some sort of synergistic effect occurred.

“Rei!”

Rei had been thinking about the Giganto Turtle and the Kobold incident, but then he heard someone calling his name.

When he turned his gaze toward the source of the voice, he saw a guild employee.

It was the person who had spoken with Rei at length about the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle.

Seeing that guild official approach him with a serious—or perhaps grave—expression, it was impossible not to get a bad feeling.

“What happened? Judging by that look on your face, I can tell something’s gone wrong.”

“Yes. Actually, I just received a report from the Guild. Apparently, the number of kobolds that have appeared at the construction site today is quite low.”

“...I see.”

Nodding at the guild official’s words, Rei turned his gaze toward the adventurers fighting the kobolds around the Giganto Turtle.

Since the kobolds were here, it was only natural that fewer of them would be attacking Gilm.

“Since there are fewer over there, are they coming over here instead?”

“Yes. Since they have extra manpower over there, a proposal has come in to reassign the idle personnel here to help with demolition and dealing with the kobolds… What do you think?”

“It’s not really up to me to say. Personally, I’m all for having more people for the dismantling, so as long as the guild has no objections, I don’t see a problem with it.”

If Rei were the one paying the rewards, he would have had to consider all sorts of complications.

But in this case, Rei doesn’t have to pay the reward for dismantling the Giganto Turtle.

Given that, as long as the guild has no objections, Rei has absolutely no problem with it… in fact, it wouldn’t be wrong to say he’d welcome it with open arms.

Hearing Rei’s words, the guild clerk smiled happily.
