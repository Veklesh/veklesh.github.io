Rei, Vihera, and Byune, guided by Murt, entered the mansion.

Set remained outside, but given the current situation, he couldn't just lie around as usual.

He stood near the gate, silently observing the surroundings, ready to detect anyone attempting to slip out not only through the gate but from any other location as well.

With Murt's permission, if anyone tried to leave the estate grounds, they could be stopped by force, as long as it didn't cause injury.

That was precisely why Rei decided to proceed with the meeting with Galahad for now, feeling somewhat reassured.

"This is it. ...Mr. Galahad, I've brought Rei and the others."

They were led not to a guest room, but to the study Galahad normally used for work.

It was also the very place Murt had barged into just moments ago.

Galahad had originally intended to hear them out leisurely in the reception room, but given the current situation—Rei being angry—he couldn't afford such a leisurely approach.

To be honest, Galahad would have preferred to have a leisurely conversation with Rei over tea.

"Ah, come in."

Hearing the voice from inside, Murt opened the office door.

And what Rei saw when he was ushered inside was Galahad, who appeared somewhat tense but otherwise composed.

He understood that if Rei lost his temper, he wouldn't get off scot-free either.

But as the head of the Azoth Trading Company, he couldn't afford to look foolish now.

"Good to see you, Rei. It's been a while since we met face-to-face like this... though it seems you didn't come here to talk."

"No, it's true I came to talk. ...To be precise, I came to ask what the hell you were thinking, acting like you're my enemy."

The tension Rei radiated was the kind that could knock a newbie adventurer unconscious in an instant.

But Galahad was a high-ranking adventurer himself, and as head of the Azoth Trading Company, he had experience dealing with seasoned merchants, however crooked they might be.

Even as Murt's cheek twitched slightly, Galahad kept any obvious tension hidden and spoke.

"I see. I don't recall doing such a thing, but allow me to explain. ...Anyway, take a seat on that sofa over there. I'll have tea brought immediately."

"No, thank you. Unfortunately, I can't trust you right now."

"...I see. Then, at least sit down. I want to hear the details."

He must have decided that while refusing tea was one thing, sitting on the sofa was acceptable. Rei sat down on the sofa, and Vihera and Byune also took seats.

After confirming this, Galahad sat down again on the sofa opposite Rei and spoke.

"Now then, about us... no, Azoth Trading Company opposing Rei. What exactly does that mean? As you know, we had a major falling out with Rei before and suffered significant losses. Do you really think we'd go and antagonize him again under these circumstances?"

"Normally, we wouldn't. But unfortunately, this time, something far from normal has happened."

He stated this, repeating the same explanation he'd given Murt in front of the mansion.

Azimov had been attacked by someone, the magic item he'd left in custody had vanished, and Set's scent had led them here to this mansion.

Hearing this, Galahad's expression grew stern and tense.

He had imagined from Rei's attitude that something serious had happened, something that couldn't be laughed off. But this was far beyond what he had imagined.

"...So, the scent really did lead to this mansion?"

"Yes. Well, if you don't trust Set's sense of smell..."

"No, I believe it."

Galahad knew full well, from the previous incident, just how exceptional Set's abilities were.

He also knew that Seto wasn't just any griffin, but a rare species capable of using various skills.

For Galahad, the option to not trust such a Set simply didn't exist. ...Though he did have a part of him that didn't want to believe.

"So, I want to meet the one who arrived here a little before us... Coris, was it? I want to meet that one."

"Murt, what's Coris doing now?"

"I've already sent someone to fetch him. He should be bringing samples of newly acquired goods and the sales report, so he ought to be arriving any moment now..."

As if responding to Murt's muttered words, a knock sounded on the office door.

"Excuse me, Chairman Galahad. I heard you requested my presence..."

"Come in."

Galahad's expression stiffened at the words spoken after the knock.

Samples of newly acquired merchandise.

Could it be the magic item taken from Rei? That was why he thought so.

Following Galahad's words, the door opened.

The man who entered was in his forties, of average build and height.

As befits a merchant from the major Azoth Trading Company, he had a somewhat unsettling look about him.

But that was only natural for a merchant; there was no particular sense of unease about him.

In fact, he showed no particular reaction upon seeing Rei and the others in the office.

Well, in terms of reaction, he did look somewhat surprised, but it was impossible to believe he could have ordered someone to attack Azimov and steal Rei's magic item before fleeing.

Of course, being a merchant, he was likely trained to hide any agitation from his expression.

Even so, to see him show absolutely no hint of guilt was something Rei found hard to believe.

"So, Chairman Galahad. Is there any connection between Rei being in the office and me being summoned here?"

When he asked that, a fleeting hint of expectation flashed across his face—perhaps he was about to place an order?—making it clear he definitely knew nothing about Rei.

(If this is an act, it's impressive... Well, I wonder.)

Rei, who had been holding back his anger until just now, felt a little of the tension ease as he watched Coris's current exchange.

Could that have been the goal? He couldn't help wondering, but from what he could see, it seemed unlikely.

Galahad probably felt the same way.

Did Coris really attack the alchemist and steal Rei's magic item? He asked Rei and Murt with his eyes.

But the only vehicle to enter the mansion before Rei was Coris's carriage, and before that, it would have been several hours earlier.

Given the circumstances, there was no doubt Coris was the culprit.

Yet, Coris showed no particular sign of agitation upon seeing Rei.

"Cut the nonsense. I'll ask straight out. Coris, did you order your men to attack the alchemist, and furthermore, did you have them seize the magic item entrusted to Rei?"

"...Huh?"

At Galahad's words, Coris wore a blank expression.

He genuinely didn't understand what was being said.

Despite this, true to his nature as a merchant of the Azoth Trading Company, he quickly regained his composure and spoke.

"Well, how did that happen? I mean, it's hard to believe anyone from the Azoth Trading Company would do something to antagonize Rei. Could this be some kind of mistake?"

Galahad breathed a sigh of relief, likely because Coris's words were exactly what he'd expected.

The man before him had been someone who'd cooperated with Galahad in various ways even after he became head of the Azoth Trading Company.

That was precisely why he hadn't imagined Rei doing something like picking a fight with Rei.

But... Coris's words alone weren't enough to put his mind at ease.

Hiding his relief from both Rei and Coris, Galahad continued with a stern expression.

"Why do you think Rei is here?"

"That... no, I don't know."

Coris must have truly understood he was being suspected.

The earlier excitement about possibly striking a deal with Rei had vanished. Realizing she'd been dragged into something terrible, she frantically shook her head.

At this point, Galahad was certain Coris wasn't involved in this matter... at least, he hadn't planned it himself.

After all, Coris, who usually carried himself like a calm merchant, was visibly flustered.

"You know about Rei's familiar, Set, right?"

"Huh? Oh, yes. Well, that's only natural for someone living in Gilm."

"And that Set possesses exceptional physical abilities and keen senses?"

"...Yes, of course."

Hearing this far, Coris understood why he was being suspected.

He understood...

(But I never ordered anything like that! Why!?)

Truly having no recollection whatsoever, Coris struggled to hide his agitation while screaming inside.

But the situation progressed as if completely oblivious to Coris's turmoil.

"Set detected scents other than Rei's group and the alchemist in the attacked alchemist's room. Following that scent led him to... this mansion."

"I have no recollection of this!"

If this continued, she would truly be made out to be the attacker.

Thinking this, Coris screamed, seemingly casting aside her usual composure.

It was only natural. If she were labeled the attacker now, she'd likely be arrested by the guards on false charges.

And once branded a criminal, there would be no hope.

Moreover, even before that, if it were to be claimed that he had instigated the attack on the alchemist and stolen Rei's magic item, he would completely turn Rei into an enemy.

What would happen if a merchant like himself made Rei an enemy?

That was obvious.

Not only in terms of pure strength, but Rei's familiar, Set, had established himself as a sort of mascot character in Gilm.

Considering that, it was certain that I... and the Azoth Trading Company itself would suffer significant damage.

In any case, continuing like this would mean ruin for me, and the Azoth Trading Company would suffer considerable damage.

Coris pondered how to prevent this, then finally spoke.

"Well, you might not believe me, but I truly have no recollection of this. Go ahead and investigate as much as you like."

"...Well, that's what you say. What do we do?"

Asked by Galahad, Rei removed the hood of his Dragon Robe while pondering what to do.

After speaking with Coris like this, he couldn't believe she was the one who attacked Azimov... or more accurately, the one who ordered it.

But the fact remained that Azimov had been attacked, the magic item stolen, and the scent of that person had followed right into this mansion.

While Rei pondered how to resolve this contradiction, Vihera, who had been silently listening, suddenly murmured.

"Could it be... that Coris isn't really the culprit?"

"Huh? What do you mean?"

"What I mean is, someone definitely attacked Azimov and stole the Sleipnir Boots. We saw it happen, so that's an undeniable fact."

Rei and Byune nodded in response to Vihera's words.

Indeed, had Rei and the others arrived at the lab even a little later, Azimov would likely have bled to death.

While physical training might have helped somewhat, Azimov, though a skilled alchemist, wasn't physically fit.

(Well, he might have managed with magic items... but the lack of any traces suggests they didn't give Azimov time to use them.)

In any case, it's clear this incident wasn't some kind of mistake.

"But... would someone considered that skilled really just head straight back to their own headquarters?"

In this case, the headquarters would undoubtedly be the mansion of the Azoth Trading Company's chairman, this very place.

Technically, the Azoth Trading Company's main branch could also be called headquarters, but at the very least, the scent in this incident led straight to this mansion.

"So, you're saying the Azoth Trading Company was used as a decoy?"

Vihera nodded at Rei's questioning words.

Despite the spring-like warmth in the room, Coris was sweating profusely.

It was certainly not the demeanor one would expect from someone capable of orchestrating this incident.

"...It's settled then. First, let's search Coris's room in this mansion and the carriage. Is that alright with you, Rei?"

At Galahad's words, Rei silently nodded in agreement.
