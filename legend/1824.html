On the evening after the mock battle ended, the Duke of Kerebel’s mansion served a meal that was more lavish than usual—though not quite a grand celebration—to mark Rei’s victory.

That said, the fact that it wasn’t a grand celebration was strictly from the perspective of the Duke of Kerebel’s household; to the average person, it was undoubtedly a sumptuous meal.

In any case, as they ate, Liberte spoke up.

“So, did you ever find out who that opponent really was?”

“No. I tried asking the knights and the guards, but in the end, I didn’t find out anything.”

Saying that, Rei shook his head.

That man—who appeared and vanished without even Set noticing.

In the end, they couldn’t gather any information about an opponent who was clearly strong enough to make one think that.

(Well, just because he’s strong doesn’t mean we can’t beat him. The real problem is how he managed to appear and vanish without us noticing him…)

As for that, he had absolutely no clue at the moment.

Even if he were to fight that man, Rei had no intention of losing.

But that’s only if they can fight normally. If he pulls a stunt like today—suddenly appearing out of nowhere to launch a surprise attack—even even Rei might struggle to handle it.

Even Set, whose senses are sharper than Rei’s, couldn’t spot that man.

In that sense, there’s no doubt he’s a very troublesome opponent.

“...I see. I suspect that the one behind him is...”

Liberte trails off mid-sentence, but everyone present understood exactly what she was about to say.

That’s precisely why Rei didn’t let her finish and changed the subject.

“Anyway, after that was settled, I went around to various food stalls with Set. I was surprised by how many delicious dishes there were.”

“……I see.”

The words that came out of Liberte’s mouth were the same as a moment ago.

But this time, there was no sign of worry—only a pleased smile.

For Liberte, the ruler of Anesis, it must be a pleasure to have the place she calls home praised.

Rei wasn’t just paying her a compliment either.

In fact, the food from the food stalls they’d stopped at after that commotion had all been quite delicious.

……Of course, there were some stalls that served bad food, so it’s not as if every single one was the best.

“Unfortunately, I rarely eat at those kinds of places… but specifically, what kinds of dishes are there?”

“I’d like to know more about that, too. If the food is good, I’d definitely like to try it.”

Arcadia asked, looking very curious.

Unlike Liberte, who primarily handles political affairs in Anesis, Arcadia often travels to various villages and towns—and sometimes even to small settlements that aren’t quite villages yet.

That’s probably why she was curious about what kind of food Rei had said was delicious.

“Well, for example… ah, there was this bread stuffed with plenty of meat and beans and baked—that was delicious.”

The bread contained chunks of meat and beans cut too large to be called ground meat, but when you took a bite, the meat juices spread throughout your mouth.

Because the meat wasn’t chopped fine enough to be called ground meat, it had a satisfying chew, and the juices that oozed out really brought out the flavor of the beans.

There are quite a few of these kinds of "filled breads," and Rei enjoys eating them, but among them, the one he ate today was definitely one of the best he’d ever tasted.

As Rei described how delicious it was, everyone instinctively swallowed hard, even though they were in the middle of eating.

That just goes to show how well Rei described it.

For a while after that, the Duke of Kerebel’s family and Rei discussed the food they’d eaten at the food stall.

The conversation about the food lasted about ten minutes, and once it had come to a natural pause, Liberte spoke up again.

“Well, let’s leave the food stall talk aside for now… I want to talk about the mock battle that took place today.”

As Liberte spoke, placing a bite of bread back on her plate, not only Rei but also Elena and Arcadia turned their gaze toward her.

Arcadia, in particular, had been busy entertaining the noblewomen currently gathered in Anesis and hadn’t watched the mock battle until the very end.

Still, she had managed to gather information from various people after the mock battle ended, and she knew that Rei had won every match with overwhelming strength.

Especially since he had defeated someone with a nickname—a rarity even in Anesis—she was surprised when she heard the news, even though she knew it was Rei who had fought.

……No, perhaps I was surprised precisely because it was Rei who fought him.

Arcadia, too, knows that Rei possesses the strength befitting someone with a title.

But that’s merely theoretical knowledge; I’ve never actually seen Rei fight.

The Rei Arcadia knows is the Rei she sees when they eat together like this, so when she hears that this Rei has achieved such feats, she is surprised even though she already knew it.

Feeling slightly uncomfortable under Arcadia’s admiring gaze, Rei turned his attention to Liberte.

“Were the nobles who proposed the mock battle satisfied?”

Even as he said this, Rei suspected they probably weren’t all that satisfied.

This whole affair had been arranged because the nobles originally didn’t trust Rei’s abilities.

It was a mock battle he didn’t really need to accept, but he agreed to it because the reward was Fuma Ore.

Rei didn’t know if everyone felt the same way. She didn’t know… but even so, as long as someone like Gaiska existed, Rei didn’t think everyone would be truly convinced after watching today’s mock battle.

(Well, if they’re satisfied with the mock battle, there’s a chance they’ll try to contact us next. …That would be quite a hassle in its own right, though.)

From the nobles’ perspective, it was only natural that they would want to get to know an adventurer with a reputation like Rei’s… and, if possible, make him one of their subordinates.

That said, Rei had absolutely no intention of serving a noble.

In fact, he even felt it was easier to be viewed with suspicion, since it meant such people wouldn’t approach him.

“That’s true. I think most of them were satisfied. If more people had actually seen Rei’s strength with their own eyes during the war with the Bestir Empire, they wouldn’t have had to resort to such tactics.”

“Ah… I suppose that can’t be helped. After all, there aren’t that many aristocrats who’d willingly go to war in the first place.”

That said, in reality, many third or fourth sons of noble families did take to the battlefield, hoping to distinguish themselves in war and join a knightly order. …And if things went well, there was even a chance they might be taken in as an adopted son by some noble house.

Even among the nobility, it’s not uncommon for those who fully understand their own standing to take to the battlefield.

...That said, this applies only to those who feel a sense of crisis regarding their current position; someone like Gaiska would never do such a thing.

After all, Gaiska is a descendant of the Marquis of Seisol.

For a marquis’s family, it would likely be easy to find work that ensures they won’t go hungry, simply by virtue of the family’s influence.

“A thrill-seeker, huh? …It seems you were traveling with one of them today.”

Hearing the word “eccentric,” Liberte said this.

As for who that referred to, it was obvious to Rei without even having to think about it.

“Brewitt, is it? …I’ve dragged him into this mess, but it looks like we’ll get along just fine.”

Although Rei generally disliked the aristocracy, there were still many among them, like Brewitt, with whom he got along well.

Even among non-aristocrats, there were certainly plenty of people he didn’t care for.

Ultimately, it seems there are people you don’t get along with, regardless of whether they’re nobles or not.

...Though it was undeniable that, perhaps due to his upbringing, there were many nobles whose personalities didn’t mesh with his.

“That man is a bit of an oddball for a noble… but perhaps that’s precisely why he got along so well with Rei.”

“I think so. I’ve met quite a few nobles over the years, but I felt Daskar had an aura quite similar to his.”

“Daskar, huh. …To be honest, I do feel it’s a waste that such a capable man ended up like that. No, perhaps it’s precisely because he’s Daskar that he was able to rally the Neutral Faction to that extent.”

From the expression on Liberte’s face as he muttered those words, Rei could tell that he truly felt what a pity it was that Daskar was not present in the heart of the matter.

In fact, as Liberte said, it was undeniably true that it was precisely because Daskar was there that the Neutral Faction had been united in that way.

Having interacted with Daskar himself, Rei understood that as well.

But even so, he couldn’t imagine Daskar doing what Liberte hoped—dissolving the Neutral Faction or simply submitting to the Noble Faction.

“I think Lord Daskar has his own ideas about things. He might be able to cooperate with the Noble Faction as he is now, but I doubt he’d ever submit to them.”

“……I suppose so. I’ve met Daskar a few times myself, and he struck me as someone with firm convictions. That’s precisely why I find it what a pity.”

“You should leave it at that. What a pity! No matter how much you regret it, I don’t think Lord Daskar will ever serve under you.”

Hearing his wife’s words, Liberte looked disappointed but said nothing more.

(Liberte addressed Lord Daskar by his first name, but when it comes to Arcadia, she adds “Lord” to his name. …Well, I guess that’s not so strange, is it?)

Thinking that, Rei brought the soup to his lips…

“Huh?”

He wore a slightly surprised expression at the flavor that spread through his mouth.

Seeing Rei like that, everyone at the table, including Elena, turned their gaze toward him... but Rei simply took another careful sip of the soup.

One spoonful, two, three… Seeing Rei savor the soup, which allowed the rich umami of the fish to spread through his mouth, Elena and the others looked on with puzzled expressions.

It was unthinkable, but if the soup had been laced with poison or something similar, and Rei had noticed something amiss after drinking it… then he certainly wouldn’t be savoring the soup like this.

However, even after Elena, Liberte, and Arcadia drank the soup, there was no particular reaction.

The flavor was slightly different from usual, but it was undoubtedly a delicious soup.

“Rei, what’s wrong? I don’t think there’s anything unusual about this soup. Well, I do think it’s delicious, though.”

Rei, who had been savoring the soup, opened his mouth as if snapped back to reality by Elena’s words.

“I just think this soup is incredibly delicious.”

Normally, such a simple statement wouldn’t be enough.

Because the soup Rei had just drunk had a taste that was familiar to him.

That said, even though it was a familiar taste, it wasn’t exactly the same as the one in Rei’s memory.

It was slightly—no, quite different from the flavor Rei knew, but the direction it was heading was undoubtedly the same.

It was a seasoning made from the extract of salted fish.

For Rei, it was the fish hot pot he always ate in winter... specifically, the fish sauce called “shotturu,” made from sandfish and used in what’s known as shotturu nabe.

Globally—though strictly speaking, this is just on Earth—fish sauce is a fairly common condiment.

In Japan, the shotturu that Rei knows is famous, and fish sauces like Thailand’s nam pla, Vietnam’s nuoc mam, and Laos’s nam pa are also well-known.

Of course, since these fish sauces are made in different places, their production methods, ingredients, and aging periods vary considerably.

In fact, the soup Rei was drinking right now tasted closer to nam pla than to shotturu… but unfortunately, Rei had never eaten any dishes made with nam pla while he was in Japan.

For Rei, fish sauce was synonymous with shotturu.

For that reason, despite a slight sense of unease, Rei enjoyed the taste of the soup.

At the same time, she could imagine that this soup was probably something Georgima had made specifically for ramen.

That assumption was likely based on the fact that Rei had eaten ramen made with shotturu at a ramen shop before.

In any case, since fish sauce is undoubtedly a type of soy sauce, in a sense, could this also be called soy sauce ramen?

Thinking this, Rei savored the soup and forgot about the unpleasant events of the day.
