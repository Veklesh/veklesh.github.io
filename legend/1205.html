As Rei stood up, intending to refuse the request, Linus spoke up in a flustered tone.

"Wait! Do you seriously think we'd accept you turning down the job after coming all this way!?"

"I don't know your situation. It's none of my business anyway."

Rei's tone was no longer as polite as when he first met Linus.

He had judged Linus to be hostile toward him, given what could be called an unreasonable demand.

"Don't mess with me! You came to this mansion, so you must have intended to accept my request, right? And yet..."

"It's true I was willing to take the job, but I'm not about to accept it without even hearing the details. And if you're saying that once I hear the details, I have no choice but to take it without question, then there's no way I'm taking that job."

If this had been someone like Daskar, who also had dealings with Rei, the situation might have been different.

Or if he'd been polite to Rei from the start, Rei might have accepted the request.

But Linus's attitude was brimming with the confidence of a noble... or, to put it another way, it was the kind of noble behavior that Rei found deeply unpleasant.

Of course, he didn't quite reach the level of the typical noble Rei despised most—the kind who assumed from the start that Rei would obey him and treated him condescendingly.

Still, it was undeniably true that Rei found him a thoroughly unpleasant person to deal with.

"Don't mess with me!"

Linus's angry shout rang out again.

Linus might appear composed, but it was far from genuine.

After all, if he truly had that kind of composure, he wouldn't have needed to come all the way out to the frontier town of Gilm.

The very fact that Linus, who wasn't even originally from Gilm, had come all this way to Gilm proved his lack of composure.

Even so, Linus held onto his pride as a noble—specifically, a noble from the King's Faction.

No matter how skilled he was, his pride wouldn't allow him to grovel before an adventurer.

His attitude stemmed largely from the fact that Rei didn't look like he possessed the formidable strength Rei had heard rumored.

Had Rei appeared as a muscular giant, Linus might have handled things differently.

"Is that all you have to say? Then I'll be taking my leave."

The knight guarding Linus's back shot a sharp glare at Rei, who had angered his master.

But Rei showed no sign of being bothered by the knight's presence. He stated this and rose from the sofa where he had been sitting.

The knights didn't move because their master, Linus, hadn't given them any orders yet.

If Linus gave the order, they would spring into action immediately.

...Though that wouldn't necessarily mean Rei could do anything about it.

"Wait!"

A voice called out, but Rei ignored it and walked out of the room.

"Damn it!"

Linus shouts in irritation.

"Shall we go instead?"

"No."

He immediately rejected the knight's offer.

He hadn't seen Rei's true strength with his own eyes.

But even so, if even half... no, even a quarter of what he'd heard from his allies in the royal faction was true, it was clear the knights' efforts would be futile.

The knights understood this too, but their words stemmed from the conviction that they couldn't simply abandon Rei, who had refused their master's request and brought shame upon himself.

Linus now understood he'd mishandled the conversation, but he couldn't turn back time.

His current position offered no room for complacency.

If asking Rei for help was now impossible, he had no choice but to take different action.

It was far from ideal, but it was all he could do in his current situation.

Rei, who had flatly refused Linus's request, went to the stables, left the mansion with Set, and exited the noble district.

Set, walking beside Rei, looked satisfied. This was largely because while Rei had been dealing with Linus, he'd been showered with food and affection by maids, soldiers, butlers, and others.

"Seems you had quite the elegant time while I was entertaining the nobles."

"Guru?"

"Is that so?" Set tilted his head. Rei gently reached out and stroked his body.

It must have felt good. Set narrowed his eyes as they walked, purring happily.

The irritation he'd felt talking to Linus faded away as he stroked Set.

Rei's frayed nerves gradually settled as well.

"Thinking about it like this, Set's like my personal tranquilizer."

Not understanding the meaning of Rei's words, Set tilted his head slightly.

Like that, Rei and Set walked down the main street, spending fulfilling time together.

Naturally, passersby on the main street noticed Rei and Set, and the children who had been playing came running over first.

"Set-chan, Set-chan, let's play together!"

"Ah—Set is playing with us, you know!"

"Yay! Set-chan is playing with us!"

"Shut up! Set said he's playing with us, didn't he!"

Children gathered, wanting to play with Set.

But the boys and girls each insist they're the ones who get to play with Set, arguing over it.

Usually, the boys would push their way through and insist, but... when it comes to playing with Set, the girls aren't backing down either.

"Play with us! We're the ones who get to play with him! Set-chan should play with us, right?!"

"Don't mess with us! Set is playing with us!"

The situation was tense, ready to explode at any moment, with one side likely to lash out at any second.

Adults watching nearby tried to step in to stop them... but someone stepped forward before they could.

"Gurrrrr!"

Set's warning growl.

Compared to the sound he made when fighting enemies, it was almost gentle... but even so, it was enough to draw the children's attention to him.

(Masterful technique...?)

Watching Set growl at the children, Rei suddenly thought that.

For an ordinary monster, hearing Set's cry would likely cause it to flee immediately.

But right now, Set was carefully adjusting his cry so it reached the excited children's ears clearly... while still directing their attention to him without frightening them.

It wouldn't be strange for Rei to think it was masterful skill in his heart.

The adults around them, who had been trying to stop the children, couldn't help but smile at the sight of Set.

Set's species... Normally, griffins, being Rank A monsters, are rarely seen even in the frontier town of Gilm.

No, Rank A monsters themselves were extremely rare, so seeing any other Rank A monster besides a Griffin would be equally uncommon.

...If Rank A monsters were as common as goblins, the town of Gilm would be destroyed.

Worse still, the very Kingdom of Mireana would likely be in serious trouble.

(Besides, Set is technically a rare griffin subspecies, equivalent to rank S.)

Rank S monsters everywhere... Just thinking about that makes me wonder if not just Gilm and the Mireana Kingdom, but the entire world itself would come to an end.

If that were to happen, would Rei abandon Gilm, taking Vihera with him...?

That's what Rei thought, but whether he'd actually do something like that was something he wouldn't know until the moment came.

(I can't imagine Marina abandoning the guild either. If she did, I'd probably end up helping her side anyway.)

Rei watched as Set began playing with both the boy and girl children. Suddenly sensing a gaze, he looked toward a nearby building.

There stood a familiar face.

One of the guards who had been standing behind Linus at the Marnino Viscount's residence earlier.

Their eyes met, and Rei immediately turned and walked away.

(What's his purpose? Could it be he came for revenge because he was displeased I didn't accept his request...? If that's the case, fine. I'll just respond with an appropriate attitude.)

However, while the knight's gaze toward him held irritation, it also contained a clear sense of reason.

If they made a move, he'd respond accordingly.

Thinking this, he looked around again.

Compared to when the Ambris incident happened, the number of people had increased considerably.

Of course, winter was fast approaching.

Once that happened, it was only natural that the population of Gilm, which had grown so much, would decrease again.

"Winter... winter, huh?"

"Huh? What's with that sudden question?"

Rei muttered without thinking, and a man who had been watching Set and the children playing nearby with a smile turned his puzzled gaze toward Rei.

"Nothing. Autumn will be over soon, and winter will come. ...Which means it'll be Gamelion season, I suppose."

"...You're right. With the Ambris incident this year, from summer into autumn, I'd completely forgotten about it."

Normally, as summer turns to autumn and the weather cools, people tend to be quite active.

Merchants also tend to head to Gilm early to stock up on goods before winter sets in, so while not as busy as spring, Gilm usually sees an increase in people.

But this year, with the Ambris incident, there were few people around from mid-summer until the start of autumn.

That's probably why, when Rei mentioned it was almost Gamelion season, he just thought, "Oh, right, that's true..."

That said, while Rei had gone Gamelion hunting almost every year until now, he wasn't in the mood for it this year.

The matter of Vihera, who still hadn't regained consciousness, was his top priority, demanding his full attention.

Today's request for a specific job was also largely a way to change his mood, as he felt stuck with no progress on Vihera's situation.

If Linus had properly explained the details of the request to Rei, he might have accepted it depending on the content.

Of course, if it was going to take a month or two, he would have declined.

"Ah, Gamelion. Winter's almost here, huh?"

Another man who had been listening to Rei and the other man muttered.

The others nodded in agreement at his words.

"Maybe it's still a bit early in the season?"

"Probably. Gamelion won't appear until it gets a bit colder."

Watching the group get excited talking about Gamelion, Rei shifted his gaze toward the Misty Ring.

That's because it contained the large quantity of Gamelion meat they'd obtained during last year's dungeon incident.

While a fair amount had found its way into Rei and Set's stomachs over the past year, much still remained stored in the Misty Ring.

Considering it was meat only obtainable from late autumn to early winter, they inevitably hesitated to use it.

As a result, vast quantities of orc meat—which was available anywhere, anytime—ended up being consumed instead.

(I hadn't planned on going Gamelion hunting this year anyway, but we've got plenty of meat to last us, right?)

Thinking such things, he watched the children playing with Set while chatting with those around him for about ten minutes.

Even just chatting like this was more than enough of a change of pace for Rei.

But he couldn't waste time like this forever.

It was time to head back... Just as that thought crossed his mind, Rei saw something impossible.

Well, not exactly impossible. Rei had seen that presence many times before.

Still, it was a presence that shouldn't be here. It was...

"Kyuu!"

Yellow happily chirped and flew straight into Rei's chest.

"Yellow!? What the hell are you doing here!?"

The utterly unexpected turn of events made a gasp of astonishment escape Rei's lips.

This black dragon cub was Elena's familiar.

In other words, it shouldn't be here.

And... Yellow being here meant...

The moment that realization dawned on him, the sound of those around him suddenly holding their breath reached Rei's ears.

And every pair of eyes was fixed in one direction.

Who could be there...? Given that Yellow was present, Rei understood.

Someone who, by all rights, should be even less present here than Yellow.

Laying eyes on the beautiful woman with golden hair that seemed to embody sunlight itself, Rei opened his mouth.

「Elena」
