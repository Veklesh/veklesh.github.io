The moment he entered the guild, those words caught Rei's attention.

Under normal circumstances, they wouldn't have caught his attention quite so much.

After all, monster encounters weren't all that uncommon in the frontier.

What caught Rei's attention was that the monster's name was Cyclops, and above all else...

"Deets?"

The name of the man frantically pleading at the guild counter slipped from Rei's lips.

Yes, standing there was Dietz, the former member of the Roving Squad.

Rei had heard that the day before, the Scorching Wind and the former rangers had all set out together on a request to slay a Cyclops.

That's precisely why the name Cyclops and the familiar face made the man's name spring immediately from his lips.

"Huh? That voice... Captain!? And Lord Vihera!?"

Two familiar faces stared back at Deets as he turned.

One of them, however, was someone he couldn't possibly approach casually.

Hurrying over from the counter toward Rei and the others, he opened his mouth without hiding his disbelief at Rei being here.

"Wh-why are you here...?"

"Well, what do you expect? Gilm is my base of operations. It's only natural I'd be here. And seriously, stop calling me Captain already."

"But the captain is the captain... No! Captain, you've come at just the right moment! I need your help!"

Faced with Deets' desperate expression, Rei hesitated.

His hesitation, however, wasn't about whether to help or not, but rather what to do about Vihera and Byune.

The members of the Scorching Winds, whom he'd known for a long time, and though he hadn't known them as long, they were still comrades-in-arms who'd fought alongside him on the same battlefield.

...Though, even when they fought on the same battlefield, Rei had mostly been operating separately from the unit with Set.

"Calm down and tell me what happened. I heard it all the way outside the guild. Five cyclopes?"

Rei asked, trying to calm Deets, who nodded immediately.

"That's right. They said only one cyclops had been sighted, but when we went to find it, there were five of them."

"The fact you're back at Gilm means it didn't come to a fight, right?"

Listening to Deets's familiar, almost brotherly way of speaking, Ray considered the words that came out of his mouth.

If it had come to a fight, facing five Cyclopses, the Scorching Wind and the former guerrilla squad alone wouldn't stand a chance. With that thought in mind, Rei asked, and Deets nodded again.

"Yeah, one... no, two Cyclopes? We could handle them easily. Even three, depending on how we fought. But five? No way we could do anything. So for now, we're just watching them from a distance. Mireine told me to get reinforcements right away..."

Rei was slightly surprised to hear Mireine's name instead of Johanna's, but remembering that the two of them actually got along surprisingly well, he decided it wasn't that strange after all and nodded.

"Five Cyclops? That's a significant force. ...Where is this?"

"You know there's a river not too far from here, right?"

"Yeah."

The river where they'd previously dismembered monsters flashed through Rei's mind.

Upon hearing the details, it seemed Rei's guess had been correct.

Deets explained that a group of Cyclopes resided in the forest upstream from that river. Rei nodded in response.

He then turned his gaze toward Vihera and Byune, but the nods that came back showed no hesitation whatsoever.

For Vihera, it was a perfect opportunity to fight enemies, and for Byune, they were just the right opponents to earn some money.

While Byune alone couldn't deal effective damage to the Cyclops, with Rei, Vihera, and Set present, the attack itself wouldn't be a problem.

"You're going, aren't you? I don't mind. It's not like we're strangers."

"Mm."

Rei's gaze fell on the two, who nodded enthusiastically, their motivation surging.

"Huh? Ah, well, Lord Vihera is appreciated and all... but this little one too?"

"Mm!"

Byune, called the little one, shot Deitz a displeased look.

However, since Byune was usually nearly expressionless, only Rei and Vihera could properly recognize that look of displeasure.

Normally, even if Vihera could, Rei wouldn't be able to grasp such subtle changes in Byune's expression.

But right now, Byune looked so displeased even Rei could tell.

"Don't worry. This child was with me when I was in Exil, diving into dungeons together. Her strength is no problem. ...Though her attack power might be a bit lacking to land an effective blow on a Cyclops."

"Hmph!"

Byune declared, almost as if protesting to Vihera, then drew out her signature long needle.

"Right. If we can stab this into the Cyclops' eye, it might be an effective strike. ...Though it'll probably make it go berserk in return."

"Even if we don't land a hit, distracting it should be enough. ...So, you two are coming along too, right?"

When Rei asked for confirmation, Vihera and Byune both nodded again without hesitation.

Given that this was the frontier town of Gilm, both Vihera and Byune had left fully armed when heading out for today's guide duty.

That meant they could head straight to the backup without needing to return to the Dusk Wheat inn, which was a significant advantage.

"Understood. Then I'll inform the guild that reinforcements are on the way. Vihera and Buune, start preparing to depart immediately. As for where we can borrow horses..."

"I know where. I'll guide you there right away. My horse ran at full speed to get here, so it needs to rest."

Deets cut off Rei's words to announce.

Though it hadn't even been half a year since Deets had come to Gilm, he still knew where the shops necessary for adventurers' activities were located.

Especially the horse rental shops—they were practically essential for adventurers.

The former guerrilla fighters had received carriages and horses as rewards for last year's civil war, but they still made sure to note the shop's location, knowing they might need to rent horses at any time.

"Got it. I'll take care of it. Meet me at the main gate."

With just that brief instruction, Rei headed straight for the guild counter.

It being daytime, few adventurers were inside the guild.

That's precisely why Rei could walk straight to the counter where Lenora was without needing to wait in line.

The mention of five Cyclopes by Deets had already drawn the attention of the adventurers in the guild and tavern to Rei and his group. However, since Vihera and the others had left the guild to prepare for departure as reinforcements, that attention naturally shifted to Rei.

"Rei, I heard what you said earlier. You're really going, right?"

Lenora asked, seeking confirmation. Rei nodded.

"It seems the Scorching Wind and the guys I brought to Gilm are going to fight the Cyclops together. One might be manageable, but hearing there are five, I can't just leave it be. As for the paperwork..."

"No, none are necessary. ...I have no worries about your abilities, Rei-san. I wish you luck in battle."

"Rei, be careful. This job... originally, only one Cyclops was confirmed, but now suddenly there are five... I think something must have happened."

Kenny, standing next to Lenora, said this to Rei.

"Ah, I'll take your advice. Well, even if there are five, they're Cyclopes. I'm sure I can handle it."

Rei remembered that he'd mentioned he'd fought a Cyclops before, though he'd only hinted at it.

Also, the fact that the Cyclops was Rank C and Rei was Rank B played a part.

Rank C and Rank D are both called veterans, but even among veterans, Rank C and Rank D are vastly different.

Put simply, rank C monsters are generally opponents a rank C party can fight evenly against, while rank D monsters are ones an individual can handle.

Rank C veterans are close to top-tier, while Rank D veterans are closer to beginners... that's the gist of it.

Of course, exceptions exist in everything. Take someone like Rei, for instance—a high-rank adventurer who operates solo (though he does have a partner named Set).

She possesses abilities that could be called extraordinary compared to the average adventurer. That's precisely why Kenny and Lenora, though somewhat concerned, didn't stop her from heading out as reinforcements.

"Please be careful."

"I don't think we need to worry about you, Rei, but don't let your guard down."

Rei gave a small nod to Lenora and Kenny's words, then turned on his heel and left the guild.

The adventurers inside the guild fixed their gazes on Rei's retreating back, their eyes reflecting complex emotions—envy, admiration, joy, sorrow, anger.

As an adventurer, someone like Rei—possessing strength that belied his appearance—was simply impossible to ignore.

"Grrrrrrr!"

The first to greet Rei outside the guild was, unsurprisingly, Set.

Having just seen Vihera and Byune rush out of the guild moments ago, he instantly understood something must have happened.

Then again, Set possessed senses sharper than any human's. It wouldn't have been surprising if he'd heard Deitz's cries inside the guild as he sought reinforcements for the Cyclops hunt.

Those who had gathered to admire Set must have also noticed his aura suddenly shift. Only children, oblivious to the situation, persistently tried to play with him.

Even that child would stop pestering him if a parent or nearby adult gently reminded them.

Rising without interference, Set greeted Rei as he emerged from the guild.

"Seems you heard it."

"Grrr."

Set growled in acknowledgment, and Rei smiled as she stroked his head.

Set's presence, understanding his intentions as if they shared a silent understanding, was deeply appreciated.

"Well then, shall we head to the main gate?"

"Gurrr!"

The people present didn't know where Rei and Set were headed next.

Still, they understood they shouldn't interfere here.

"Hang in there, Set."

"Set-chan, I'll treat you to some special meat next time, so come back safe and sound."

"I don't know what you're up to, but you'll be fine, right, Set?"

Set purred happily at the voices calling out to him from around him. ...Though the main reason for that was probably the mention of the special meat.

And the majority of the voices around him... more accurately, almost all of them were directed at Set, clearly showing what kind of position he held within Gilm.

The fact that there were hardly any words for Rei was probably the result of his usual behavior.

Regardless, Rei and Set continued toward the main gate while being called out to like that.

"Set, I assume you heard—our enemies this time are Cyclopes. Five of them. Five Rank C monsters would be a bit much for Mireine and Johanna's group. We need to hurry too."

"Guruu!"

Set and Mireine were close friends to Johanna too.

Knowing they were in danger, he naturally had no intention of abandoning them.

It was spring, so they ran through the streets, weaving through the crowds thicker than usual.

Naturally, many were startled to see such a large figure as Set running, but once they recognized it was him, their surprise turned to something almost endearing. They waved and even cheered him on.

Some, newly arrived in Gilm, didn't know of Set's existence. To them, others nearby explained, "He's a familiar. He's harmless."

Even so, seeing a griffin up close was a huge shock for those unaccustomed to it. Still, they had to accept that this was simply the nature of the frontier.

...If asked whether one could see griffins running through the streets in the frontier regions of other countries, no one could honestly nod in agreement.

Anyway, after running through town and arriving at the main gate, there they were: Dietz and Vihera on horseback, with Byune riding in front of Vihera, almost cradled in her arms.

Two horses carried the three of them, and two more were kept as spares in case of trouble.

They must have chosen well-trained horses, for they showed no surprise at the sight of Set.

(Well, they are heading to battle the Cyclops, so it makes sense.)

He muttered to himself, then swiftly completed the departure procedures to join the three who were already processing their exit.

There were others waiting to complete their own procedures, but when they explained they were heading as reinforcements to defeat the Cyclops, they immediately stepped aside.

It was partly kindness, but likely also a desire to reduce the risk of being attacked by Cyclops on the road themselves.

With the formalities concluded, Rei offered a brief word of thanks to Ranga and stepped outside the gates of Gilm.
