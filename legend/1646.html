Several days had passed since Rei and his companions returned to Gilm… Around the time when the summer air began to take on a hint of autumn—not just at night and in the morning, but even during the day… Rei and his companions were once again seen in the vast sky.

Rei was riding on Set’s back, and Set was carrying a Set Cage. Naturally, inside that Cage were Elena, Marina, Vihera, Byune, and Yellow—four people and one creature.

Furthermore, there were several other female adventurers as well.

Naturally, Rei and the others were heading toward Sura’s group.

Since their work in Gilm had come to a temporary halt, they decided to drop by to restock their supplies.

That said, since Gimekara’s employer, the Zolge Trading Company, handles the actual resupply, they shouldn’t have been in any real trouble.

They might be short on luxury items, but for a group that could easily be called refugees, that would be nothing short of a luxury.

And aside from Elena and her group, the others on board are adventurers who are scheduled to join Sura’s group as escorts.

Since they were tasked with guarding a group consisting almost entirely of women, they couldn’t very well bring along men who were starved for female company.

After considering this, they chose female adventurers.

Of course, they aren’t all part of a single party—some are solo adventurers, while others are women selected from existing parties.

“Hmm… I wonder how long it’ll take before we see them.”

“Guruu?”

Set turned to look at Rei, asking, “What’s wrong?”

Seeing Set’s reaction, Rei shook his head and said, “Nothing.”

What could be seen on the ground was a summer meadow.

However, since it was nearing the start of autumn, it wouldn’t have been strange for the grass in the meadow to be starting to wither.

After flying for several hours… a river that looked familiar to Rei came into view.

Yes, it was the river they had stopped at on their way back to Gilm before.

It was the spot where Set used to fish by striking the rocks in the river with his front paws to startle the fish into the shallows.

“It’s about noon, so let’s take a break there. Set, you’d probably like to eat some fish too, right?”

“Grrrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a happy growl, perhaps recalling the taste of the fish he’d eaten a short while ago.

For Set, that must mean the river fish he ate there was that delicious.

After Rei told him it was okay to land, Set flapped his wings and began descending toward the ground.

Eventually, they set down the Set Cage near the river, and Set landed safely.

However, since they had decided to take a lunch break based solely on Rei’s suggestion, they ended up getting a slight scolding from Elena and the others.

“Huh? Um… are we really allowed to eat that dish too?”

The female adventurer let out a cry of surprise at the sight of the food before her.

They had assumed they would have to prepare their own meals and had brought several preserved foods, including dried meat, but if asked which they would prefer—the preserved food they’d brought or the meal Rei had just taken out of the Misty Ring—the answer was obviously the latter.

Here, right in front of them, was a meal freshly prepared by a professional chef who had put in the time and effort.

Anyone who wouldn’t want to eat that must have some special reason, or simply prefer preserved foods over regular meals.

That’s exactly why the female adventurer asked Rei, who had offered the food to them as well.

“Yeah. I don’t know how long it’ll take to meet up with the others, but I’ll take care of meals for us in the meantime.”

“Um… are you really willing to go to all that trouble for us?”

“Oh, are you perhaps one of the adventurers who recently arrived in Gilm?”

It was Marina who stepped in between Rei and the female adventurer.

As a former guild master, Marina naturally remembered the faces of many adventurers.

Of course, she didn’t know every single adventurer, but since there were inevitably fewer female adventurers than male ones to begin with, it was only natural that they stuck in Marina’s memory more easily.

That said, it’s been quite some time since Marina transitioned from being a guild master to an adventurer.

During that time, many adventurers had come to Gilm, and above all, the expansion project had only increased their numbers further.

Given all that, it would have been impossible for her to remember every single adventurer who had come to Gilm during that time.

“Ah, yes. I arrived a little while ago.”

“I see. Don’t worry. I don’t know what kind of quests you’ve taken on so far, but at least here in Gilm… no, Lord Daskar wouldn’t do something so cheap as making adventurers pay for their own meals.”

The female adventurer seemed surprised that she had addressed the lord by his first name, as if she didn’t know who Marina was.

When a few people who knew Marina explained the situation to her, she finally seemed to relax. She happily reached for the food Rei had served.

“Once we’re done with lunch, we’ll take a short break and then set off again. Anyone who has something to do, please get it done by then. There’s a lot to handle. Also, if you catch some fish in the river, tonight’s dinner will be a bit more luxurious. …Set, you know what I mean, right?”

“Grrrrrrr!”

Hearing Rei’s words, Set understood what was being asked of him and let out a happy growl.

Actually, Set isn’t the only one looking pleased.

Some of the female adventurers had lived in the countryside before coming to Gilm, so they knew how to catch fish.

They enjoyed fishing partly as a recreational activity, and they taught those who had grown up in Gilm or had come from other towns and lacked such experience how to catch fish.

……Depending on where they came from, their methods varied greatly, and even among those who knew how to catch fish, they exchanged comments like, “Our way is better,” or “That method is worth trying.”

Since they were exchanging opinions near the cool river while eating freshly prepared food, the discussion naturally became quite heated.

“You’re good at this.”

Watching this, Vihera looked at Rei with a meaningful smile.

“What are you talking about?”

“……Until just a moment ago, you were all being reserved with each other, but now you’re speaking your minds without holding back. Well, I suppose that’s only possible because there aren’t any shy people here.”

“I’m not quite sure what you’re talking about. It’s just a coincidence, right? A coincidence.”

“Hmm. …Well, if that’s what Rei says, then so be it.”

Vihera said nothing more and went back to eating her meal.

Watching Vihera like that, Rei let out a small sigh.

He’d tried to cover it up because he felt a bit embarrassed, but he hadn’t expected his true intentions to be seen through so easily.

To hide that embarrassment, he looked around while eating his bread… and suddenly, he spotted Yellow by the river.

「Yellow？」

“Hehe, don’t mind him. Yellow’s in the middle of a battle with his sworn rival.”

Elena, who had heard Rei’s mutter, murmured in a voice tinged with a smile.

Even though it had cooled down considerably compared to a little while ago, there was no doubt it was still summer.

What’s more, today’s sky was almost cloudless, and the sun was shining intensely, as if refusing to let summer end just yet.

In the pouring sunlight, Elena’s golden hair sparkled.

Gazing at her hair with a slightly dazzled expression, Rei turned his gaze back toward Yellow.

He watched for a dozen or so seconds… and finally understood what Yellow was doing.

There, Yellow was fighting a crab—a creature that could be called his sworn rival.

...Of course, the crab’s attacks couldn’t possibly penetrate Yellow’s hard scales, so it was essentially a one-sided assault.

Even so, the crab focused on dodging while launching counters at Yellow, and the two were locked in a fierce, evenly matched battle.

“Kyu! Kyu-kyu! Kyuu!”

Perhaps unable to tolerate being disrespected by a mere crab as the child of a Black Dragon, Yellow repeatedly tries to strike the crab with its front legs.

However, his anger slows him down, and thanks to the crab’s clever use of the surrounding rocks, none of his attacks land.

“Um, Rei. What on earth is that kid doing?”

A female adventurer witnessing this scene for the first time asks Rei, but Rei can only offer a wry smile in response.

“It’s a battle between Yellow and his sworn rival. Just watch quietly.”

“U-uh…”

With Rei having said that, the female adventurer couldn’t bring herself to say anything more.

However, judging that the situation wasn’t serious, she decided to watch over Yellow.

This woman probably finds Yellow more endearing than Set.

A short distance away from her, Set had already launched a strike with his front paw at a rock, catching a fish.

For Set, it must be fun to see the fish get knocked out and float to the surface with a single blow. He looks in a very good mood.

“At this rate, we won’t have to worry about fish. …Well, river fish, anyway.”

Compared to river fish, which tend to have a mild flavor, I do find myself craving the rich taste of ocean fish.

But, of course, there’s no ocean near Gilm, so if I want ocean fish, I’d have to go all the way to a port town like Emoshion to buy some.

(Wait, if I think about catching them myself, I wouldn’t necessarily need to go to a port town—as long as I can get to the sea, that’s all that matters. …Actually, considering things like fishing rights, it’s definitely better to fish in a place where no one else is around.)

In Elgin, there is no such thing as a fishing license.

That said... if Rei and Set—who aren’t fishermen—were to catch fish in places like port towns or villages where there are many fishermen, it would naturally make them feel uneasy.

Especially since the fish Rei intends to catch aren’t just a side dish for dinner… but rather hundreds, or even more.

With so many hot-tempered fishermen around, if they see something like that happening right under their noses, it’s bound to lead to a fight.

Rei is willing to brush off any sparks that fly his way, but he has no intention of seeking out trouble.

That’s precisely why he decided to fish in a place where no one else was around.

Generally speaking, places with no people tend to have plenty of fish, but also plenty of underwater monsters preying on them… but with Rei and Set, there was absolutely no need to worry about that.

No, in fact, they’d probably defeat those monsters and use them as food instead.

(Hmm, now that I think about it, going to the sea might actually be a good idea. …I’d like to go while it’s still summer, if possible… but honestly, I’m not sure.)

There’s no doubt I want to go to the sea, but there’s the expansion work on Gilm and the matter with Sura and the others.

It’s not like I can just casually decide to go to the sea.

But even so, I still want some fish...

(That’s true. It’s not like it’s a long trip, and if it’s just a few days… I can think of it as a slightly late summer vacation.)

I’ve learned from this experience that a few days won’t be a problem for the expansion work.

So, I figured it would be fine if we were away for a little while… Once I started thinking that way, my mind kept drifting in that direction.

In reality, there’s no such thing as a summer vacation in Elgin.

When winter comes and the snow falls, many people take time off, so I suppose you could call that a long vacation.

Even so, considering that some people do piecework at home rather than taking time off, it’s not exactly a carefree situation.

...For adventurers in Gilm, if they save up enough money by winter, living a life of leisure during the winter isn’t just a dream.

On the other hand, those who haven’t managed to save enough money end up going out into the snow to slay monsters.

“Rei, let’s go catch some fish too.”

As Rei was daydreaming about a few days of summer vacation—or perhaps fall break—Vihera, having finished her meal, invited him along.

She seemed to have felt a little envious watching Set catch fish.

Beside Vihera, Byune was busily picking up small stones that had fallen to the ground.

Rei, who knew Byune’s fighting style, immediately understood what she intended to do with them.

Sure enough, Byune quickly threw the pebble she was holding, aiming at the fish swimming in the river.

That said, given Byune’s skill, he wasn’t exactly a bullseye every time; his hit rate was probably around fifty percent.

Still, since she throws so many stones, Byune is able to catch—or at least stun—quite a few fish.

The only problem was that dead or stunned fish couldn’t swim, so they were carried away by the current.

One of the female adventurers noticed this and ran downstream, but it wasn’t possible to recover every single one.

In any case, for a little while, Rei and the others thoroughly enjoyed their summer fun by the river and succeeded in catching a considerable amount of river fish.
