"Rei ... Well, it's goodbye for a little while. If possible, I'll come back to Gilm again sometime in the summer, so stay well until then."

A short distance from Gilm's main gate, several people had now gathered.

The number wasn't particularly large, but nearly all present were well-known figures within Gilm.

Rei, the young rising star known by the epithet Crimson, accompanied by his griffin, Set.

Vihera , who was part of Rei's party, clad in sheer garments that directly stirred a man's base desires.

Marina, the Guildmaster, who seemed to embody the very essence of feminine allure.

Byune , a thief who parties with Rei and Vihera, appearing to be only ten years old or so.

Daskar, the lord of Gilm, whose imposing visage could easily strike fear into first-time viewers, possessing a stature best described as imposing.

Being seen off by these five are Ara , seated on the driver's seat with a Power Axe strapped to her back... and Elena , who gazes at Rei with moist eyes.

Her golden hair, as if made from sunlight itself, and her noble beauty, radiating an unwavering strength of will that captivates all who see her.

(Honestly, how strange that I'm the most ordinary one here...)

Watching Elena, her dear friend and master, grieving their parting, Ara murmurs inwardly.

Even Ara wields a Power Axe, a weapon larger than ordinary, and possesses the strength to drag an orc corpse by one hand.

By ordinary standards, Ara would undoubtedly be considered abnormal... or special.

While her looks couldn't match those of Elena, Vihera, or Marina, her features were still well-proportioned enough to be called beautiful.

Yet, among this group, she clearly occupied a position that could only be described as ordinary.

Then again, Ara's top priority is staying by Elena's side. If she were to accidentally outshine Elena, it would inevitably lead to all sorts of unpleasant situations, so she has no complaints about the status quo.

...Ara's father, Count Scarley, worries that she cannot find a specific partner, but Ara herself doesn't care about that at all.

At the end of Ara's gaze, Daskar was addressing Elena.

"Lady Elena, please visit anytime. As the lord of this Gilm territory... and as a noble of the neutral faction, I am truly delighted that the Princess General, Lady Elena, has graced this city with her presence. I look forward to our cooperation with the noble faction as well."

"Very well. I shall ensure Lord Daskar's letter reaches my father."

"...I do feel rather sorry to have someone with the title of Princess General deliver a letter, but..."

Daskar wore an expression of genuine regret, just as his words suggested.

Truthfully, using the Princess General as a messenger could only be described as an extravagant luxury.

If others found out, they'd have every right to ask what on earth we were doing.

But Elena shook her head at Daskar.

"Please don't worry about it. I was also well taken care of in Gilm. I've just returned from the village that is also Marina's hometown, and though things are hectic, I must return soon."

True to her words, she had returned to Gilm from Marina's village just two days prior.

Normally, preparing for a journey for someone of Elena's standing as a duke's daughter would easily take over a week.

However, she was here now not as a duchess's daughter but as an individual. Drawing on her experience as a soldier, Elena found she could travel without hindrance even without gathering such a large amount of luggage.

...Though, that was largely thanks to the carriage, which was also a magic item.

The interior of the carriage was quite spacious, and the furnishings were luxurious.

For ordinary living, it contained everything one could possibly need.

"Are you really sure you want to leave? Haven't you forgotten that Marina and I are staying with Rei?"

"Hmm, you're right. That is a concern, to be honest... But I still believe Rei will choose me as his first wife."

"...You're awfully confident."

"Heh heh, well, that's just Elena for you, isn't it? I don't plan on losing out on that front either."

After Daskar, the next to speak to Elena were Vihera and Marina.

Some sort of discussion must have taken place between the three, for the fierce female exchanges of before were absent.

It seemed all three shared the understanding that one of them would become Rei's First Lady.

Elena had briefly encountered Kenny at the Paired Orb meeting, and the image of Kenny flashed through her mind for an instant, but she immediately dismissed the thought.

She felt it was wrong for her not to be here, given that they were all giving their all to win Rei's favor.

"Elena, what's wrong? Are you changing your mind about going back?"

At Vihera's words, Elena shook her head.

Seeing Elena like this, Vihera sighed, looking slightly disappointed.

To Vihera, Elena was someone who stirred rivalry in her in many ways.

They were nearly equal in combat strength, and she felt strong rivalry toward Elena as a love rival over the man named Rei.

Yet, Vihera felt no hatred toward Elena; if anything, she felt a sense of kinship.

She was both a rival in love and a companion who shared feelings for the same man. A complex relationship, both enemy and ally.

"No. It's bittersweet, but I can't stay here forever. It's true I'd like to stay if possible, but..."

Elena's gaze shifted toward a certain direction.

"Gururururuuuu!"

"Kyuu! Kyukyuu!"

"Grrr"

"Kyuu!"

Despite speaking completely different languages, the two somehow managed to communicate.

Set and Yellow. These two were still as close as ever.

You could even call them best friends.

It pained Elena to separate them, but since Yellow was her familiar, she had no choice but to take him along.

(Wait, Yellow can return on his own... Could I leave him with Gilm for a little longer?)

The thought flashed through Elena's mind.

「Yellow」

"Kyuu?"

For some reason, Yellow, who should have been talking with Set, was perched on his head... But upon hearing Elena's call, Yellow flapped his wings and headed toward her.

"Yellow, you can come back to me on your own, right? If so, you can stay with Set in Gilm a little longer, you know?"

"Kyuuu... Kyuu!"

At Elena's words, Yellow turned his gaze toward Set.

But Yellow quickly shook his head and landed beside Elena.

"Kyu!"

Yellow's cry made it clear to everyone: He wanted to stay with Elena!

Yellow also found it hard to part with Set. But still, being with Elena was more important.

Just as Set, even when getting along with others, still loved Rei the most, Yellow's number one was still Elena.

"Hehe, you're quite the favorite, aren't you?"

Marina said with a smile, and Vihera beside her nodded... then opened her mouth with a slightly mischievous laugh.

"Leave Rei to me. Elena should go home with Yellow."

Marina jumped on the topic.

"Oh? Why not leave it to me instead of Vihera? I may look like this, but I've lived a long time. Compared to Vihera or Elena, I should be quite useful in many ways."

"That's beside the point."

Without a moment's hesitation, Elena's words rejected the two before her.

All three were smiling and chatting, but for some reason, Rei, watching from a little distance away, felt a chill run down his spine.

Well, perhaps it was better for Rei. The one who was truly miserable in this situation was Daskar.

Having spoken with Elena just moments before, he was now directly exposed to the icy chill emanating from the three of them.

From Elena came a magical power that could be called dragon energy inherited from the Ancient Dragon, from Vihera came a magical power that could be called battle energy, and from Marina came pure magical power. Each emitted their own aura, steadily lowering the surrounding temperature.

No joke, Daskar felt like the temperature around him had dropped by two or three degrees.

(Honestly. Should I call it a curse of beautiful women? All three are stunningly beautiful, yet not a single one of them is normal.)

Daskar smiled as he turned his gaze to each of the three women speaking in turn.

The Princess General, the former Second Princess of the Bestir Empire and a battle maniac, the Dark Elf Guildmaster. ...From Daskar's perspective, all three were spectacularly far from being decent women.

"Oh, what is it, Daskar? Something for me?"

Marina, who had been smiling and chatting with Elena and Vihera, suddenly addressed Daskar.

Fearing she had read his thoughts, Daskar hastily shook his head.

"No, no, nothing at all. It's nothing, so don't mind me, just carry on."

"Oh? Really? It's such a shame, since Daskar gave me this present when he was a child..."

"WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA

It was a memory Daskar never wanted to recall... one that made him want to roll around on the spot if he did.

It was something only possible because he was a child; doing it now would be utterly impossible.

"...Daskar?"

Elena, puzzled by Daskar's reaction, turned a questioning gaze toward him.

No, it wasn't just Elena. Vihera, Ara, Rei, and even Byune had turned their gazes toward Daskar.

Perhaps snapped back to reality by the attention, Daskar cleared his throat softly and spoke.

"Well, I'm afraid I must get back to work. My apologies, Elena, but I must take my leave now. Come visit Gilm anytime."

With that, Daskar turned on his heel and walked away.

"...He ran away, didn't he?"

Daskar caught Marina's muttered words, as if she were considering pursuit. But any careless action here would render his escape meaningless.

He made that judgment and swiftly ran away from the spot.

He knew exactly how Marina would toy with him if he stayed.

As a lord, he wanted to avoid that... Shelving those memories he'd rather forget, he wondered why his childhood self had ever proposed marriage to such a woman.

(If my younger self were here now, I'd definitely warn him to stay away from that wicked woman.)

Daskar muttered inwardly, his back appearing oddly small to Ara's eyes from the driver's seat.

Feeling a hint of sympathy for that seemingly small figure, he shifted his gaze to the horses pulling the carriage.

He noticed the horses—horses that should have undergone rigorous training since childhood, the very kind that wouldn't flinch even if Set himself were nearby—shivering ever so slightly. He understood.

Even someone like her, who felt strong friendship and loyalty toward Elena, would find it difficult to step into the midst of those three right now.

...The fact that she didn't outright declare it impossible here showed Ara's greatness, but she herself still hadn't fully accepted that.

Unconcerned with Ara, the conversation among the three exceptionally beautiful women continued.

This spot was just outside Gilm, naturally bustling with passersby. Merchants and adventurers who would normally be captivated by the sight of these three women now averted their gazes unnaturally as they walked by, seemingly understanding that getting involved would undoubtedly lead to trouble for themselves.

Yet the three women, smiling and deep in conversation, show absolutely no interest in these passersby.

That said, their lack of interest was strictly limited to casual observation. Should any passerby harbor ill intent and attempt to attack Elena and her companions, they would face merciless retaliation.

"Rest assured. Even when Rei and I are apart, we meet every night... well, 'every night' might be a slight exaggeration, but we use our Paired Orbs to see each other. Nothing you need to worry about will happen."

"Oh, really? Paired Orbs are just Paired Orbs, after all. Meeting face-to-face like I do definitely has its advantages."

"I wish you'd say 'us' instead."

The three of them could have gone on forever if left to themselves, but sensing they had calmed down considerably compared to earlier, Ara spoke up.

"Lady Elena, I believe it's time for us to depart..."

"Hmm, I see. I would have liked to talk a little longer, but... Rei, it pains me to part..."

As expected, Elena chose Rei as her final companion.

"Ah. Whenever you have time again, feel free to come. You're always welcome."

"...Mm. I don't know when we'll be able to meet like this again... but still, I'll look forward to it."

Saying this, she gently pressed her lips to his.

Rei accepted it silently, then embraced Elena's body.

Had Vihera and Marina not discreetly shifted their bodies to shield them from the surrounding gazes, this action could easily have sparked a major scandal involving the Princess General.

The reason the two who cared for Rei didn't stop them was likely because they saw Elena as both a romantic rival and a comrade.

About a minute passed like this. Eventually, Rei and Elena separated and boarded the carriage, leaning their faces out the window.

Her white cheeks flushed bright red, Elena wore a happy... yet somehow lonely smile as she spoke.

"Well then, Rei. Until we meet again!"

With those words, the carriage driven by Ara set off... and Rei watched until her figure disappeared from sight.
