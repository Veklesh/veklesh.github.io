When Rei and the others gathered information and returned to the Silver Fruit Inn, Marina and the others had already returned to the inn. They immediately gathered in Rei's room to exchange information.

Rei had considered eating something in the dining hall... but unlike Gilm, he understood that they stood out like a sore thumb in this town of Rossi.

Furthermore, since they couldn't completely rule out the possibility that the organization hadn't caught wind of the Ajax incident, they decided it was best to avoid discussing things in a public place.

At the Dusk Wheat inn, where people were accustomed to Rei's presence, few would pay much attention even if they discussed things while eating in the dining hall.

Of course, for those always on the lookout for business opportunities, Rei's conversation could easily become a lucrative lead. So, while not for the reason Rei feared, it was highly likely someone would try to eavesdrop.

"Here, souvenirs. Rei liked these, right?"

Marina said as she handed over a pâté en croûte.

It was a dish made by hollowing out a large French-style loaf of bread and filling it with stir-fried meat and vegetables, a famous specialty of this country.

The first dish Rei had eaten upon arriving in Rossi, it was indeed a favorite, just as Marina had said.

Byune also looked delighted to receive the pâté de campagne Marina handed her.

But... Rei and the others had already eaten quite a bit at the tavern.

While those with large appetites like Rei and Byune might be an exception, their eating habits aside, their portions were only slightly larger than average—though still within the norm for adventurers.

For someone like Vihera, there was no way she could eat a filling panyan.

"Rei, please."

"Sure."

For the time being, they stored Vihera's portion of panyan in the Misty Ring and resumed their conversation.

"So, we found a place that was obviously suspicious."

At Rei's words, Elena looked slightly displeased, but Marina smiled.

Soon, Marina spoke up with interest.

"Mejougo... right?"

It seemed Marina and the others had also arrived at what appeared to be the correct location.

Rei was momentarily surprised, but then again, Mejougo wasn't exactly hidden, so it made sense.

Still, it was a pleasure district in the literal sense, built around brothels and taverns.

When most people hear the term "pleasure district," they picture a specific section within a town or city.

But Mejougo was exactly what its name implied.

Naturally, if beauties like Elena and Marina were searching for such a place, some might mistake them for aspiring prostitutes, or worse...

Still, it was a stroke of luck that Marina headed for the guild.

"The guild doesn't seem too fond of Mejougo either, huh?"

"...Huh? Why's that?"

Of course, brothel patrons come in all shapes and sizes, but adventurers are among the most valued customers.

Depending on the job, accepting something like a monster hunt means risking their lives.

Even other jobs... like escort missions, often involve life-or-death battles against bandits.

After completing such life-threatening jobs, many crave human touch and often head to the brothel.

Of course, if they have a lover, that's a different story.

In any case, brothels are as essential to adventurers as taverns.

If brothels didn't exist, sexual crimes would undoubtedly increase.

If that happened, naturally the reputation of adventurers... and the guilds themselves would suffer.

Yet, why the guild dislikes Mejougo... that was something Rei couldn't understand.

As if sensing Rei's confusion, Marina took a sip of the fruit wine Rei had prepared and began her explanation.

"Providing security for brothels in the pleasure district is basically one of the jobs adventurers do. Of course, if they have their own connections, like retired adventurers or acquaintances, they might hire them instead. But... that's only when you think of it as just one shop."

Hearing that much, Rei finally understood what Marina was getting at.

"So, in Mejougo, adventurers are only needed as customers? They don't actually hire adventurers for security work?"

"Correct. That Mejougo is apparently quite a famous entertainment district around here. So much so that people come in droves, not just from the Mireana Kingdom, but from surrounding countries too."

"With that many customers coming in, if adventurers had taken on security work, it would've been a huge income source, no doubt. And you weren't happy that got shut down... is that about it?"

Vihera interjected, and Marina nodded at his words.

"That's right. I met with the guildmaster, and he seemed to have quite a strong grievance about it."

"Hmm... By the way, that guildmaster wasn't someone Marina knew after all?"

Before heading to the guild, Rei had already heard that this guildmaster was someone Marina didn't know.

But maybe they had met before and he just forgot... That's what Rei had thought, but Marina shook her head.

"Unfortunately, it was someone I'd never met before. Though it seemed like he knew who I was."

"...I figured."

Marina was famous in many ways.

Indeed, anyone serving as a guildmaster in a vassal state of the Mireana Kingdom should know her name.

That's what Rei thought, but he noticed Elena suddenly looking displeased whenever the topic of guildmasters came up.

"What's wrong?"

"...Honestly, I can't stand the guildmaster here."

"Ah... I suppose that's understandable for Elena. Being fawned over so blatantly like that would be unpleasant for her."

"Is that so?"

"Yeah."

Elena gave a short reply to Rei's question.

Seeing Elena like that, Rei somehow sensed that the guildmaster Rossi's personality probably wouldn't suit him either.

"That sort of vulgar person is exhausting to deal with properly... well, maybe just a hassle. But if they think there's something in it for them, they won't betray you, so in terms of being useful, they're fine."

"I understand. I won't make a show of it either. It's only in front of people like Rei, Marina, Vihera, and Byune."

So, it meant she was showing her true feelings because she felt comfortable with the people present here.

Elena swept her golden hair back, her gaze drifting away, perhaps out of embarrassment.

"Anyway. It's safe to assume there's definitely some clue at that place called Mejougo, right?"

Elena said this as if to cover her embarrassment, but her words weren't wrong. However...

"If they can run a place like Mejougo without any issues, it's clear there's someone connected to the organization within the upper echelons of the Reburina Kingdom."

At Rei's muttered words, the others all nodded in agreement.

Under normal circumstances, running a pleasure district like Mejougo would be impossible.

The very fact it was being run suggested the upper echelons of this country were undoubtedly involved with the organization in some way.

"So, the problem becomes who goes to Mejougo."

Even as she said this, Marina's gaze fell on Rei.

It was only natural. After all, Rei was the only man in the group.

And naturally, the one going to Mejougo, where brothels are the main business, would be a man.

Consequently, the only conclusion from the start was that Rei would go to Mejougo.

But... naturally, there was someone who couldn't accept that.

"I object."

The first to voice opposition was Elena.

She couldn't accept sending the man she loved to a brothel.

Though, while going to the brothel was unacceptable, Rei couldn't quite understand why it was fine to take in Marina, Vihera, and the other two wives besides Elena.

"Hmm, I'm opposed deep down too... but who else is going to go besides Rei?"

Marina seemed dissatisfied, but not to the point of being unable to tolerate it.

"I suppose I'm more on Marina's side, too."

Vihera murmured, looking resigned to the situation.

In stark contrast to the three women, Byune watched the others' exchange indifferently, not caring either way.

"...I'd like to hear what Rei thinks."

"You're asking me now..."

Rei muttered with a look of dismay.

He understood that, being the only man present, he naturally had to go.

But at the same time, he could understand Elena's reasons for opposing it.

"So? What does Rei think... I'd like to hear that too."

Marina, who had been watching Rei, smiled as she looked at him.

Vihera, too, was looking at Rei just like Marina.

Under the gaze of these three, Rei pondered what to do.

He had many things he wanted to say, but fundamentally, Rei and his group had come to the Kingdom of Reburina on a mission.

Given that, it was also true they couldn't be picky about their methods when seeking revenge against the organization that had meddled with Gilm.

Moreover, all the members of Crimson Wings were playing significant roles in the expansion project.

Given that, he understood it was best to return to Gilm as soon as possible.

"Ah... Yeah. If there's no other lead, I guess I'll have to go. There's definitely got to be a clue about the organization at Mejougo."

"Well..."

Elena had no choice but to hold her tongue when Rei put it that way.

After all, the reason Elena was here in the Kingdom of Reburina with Rei and the others was because her father, Duke Kerebel, had instructed her to cooperate with them.

The conflict lay between her identity as the Duke's daughter and symbol of the Noble Faction, known by the epithet "Princess General," and her identity as a woman in love with one man.

The agony Elena felt, born of her own purity, was what caused her to waver between these two selves.

"Rei, are you really going?"

"I have no choice, do I? Of course, Rossi must have several bases for Ajax's organization. But where exactly they are and how many there are—naturally, we just arrived in Rossi, so we have no idea."

"And finding truly reliable informants won't be easy either."

Marina murmured thoughtfully.

That was something only someone who had once been an adventurer and then worked as a guildmaster could understand.

If they only sold lies or outdated information, that would be one thing, but in the worst case, they might sell Rei and the others' information to the organization.

Finding trustworthy informants who wouldn't do such things was difficult.

Especially here in Rossi, the capital of the Kingdom of Reburina.

With no one having any local knowledge here, the difficulty only increased.

Getting a referral from someone is an option, but then the question of whether that person is trustworthy becomes a major factor.

"Especially in our case, we can't afford to waste that much time,"

Everyone nodded at Rei's muttered words.

"Look, Elena. Just because Rei went to Mejougo doesn't mean we actually need to go to the brothel, right? In this case, all we need to do is confirm whether the prostitutes in Mejougo are the ones who were taken away by people like Ajax."

"But Marina... are you really okay with this?"

"If you ask if it's fun, of course it's not fun! But if we want to do something about our current situation with so little information, we have to be prepared for some danger. ...Well, it is dangerous, but not physically dangerous—more like danger to my virtue."

Marina murmured with apparent amusement, but Rei chose to ignore that last part.

"Where I grew up, we have a saying: 'You can't get the tiger's cub without entering the tiger's den.' Simply put, if you want something important, you have to face danger."

"...In Rei's case, I get the feeling that even if he encountered a tiger, he'd see it as a golden opportunity and just take it down..."

"Yeah. For Rei, a tiger would be no problem at all."

Vihera nodded in agreement with Elena's words, and smiles spread through the room.

Then, as the smiles faded, Elena reluctantly spoke up.

"Alright. I'll leave it to Rei."

Though clearly displeased, she conceded to Rei heading to Mejougo.
