It was just past noon the day after Rei and the others had parted ways with Sura, Gimekara, and the others… Eventually, a figure that was both familiar and yet unfamiliar came into view.

It was Gilm, a place that felt like a second home to Rei.

The reason it looked unfamiliar was likely because it was undergoing expansion work.

In reality, the time Rei and the others had been away from Gilm wasn’t that long.

Still, the power of human effort is incredible; even with this many people, they’ve managed to completely transform the town’s layout.

“They say they’re still short-handed even at this pace, so I wonder how far along the construction really is… I hope they’re not cutting corners, right?”

Rei muttered as he gazed at Gilm in the distance, but knowing Daskar’s character, there was absolutely no way he would allow such a thing.

If anyone tried anything like that on this project, anyone living in Gilm wouldn’t get off scot-free, and even those who’d come to Gilm as migrant workers might risk being put on the wanted list.

Given that working hard would undoubtedly pay off, would anyone really do something to throw that away? …It’s hard to imagine.

(Though, if someone harbored some kind of grudge against Gilm or Lord Daskar, might they actually do something like that?)

Given that Gilm is the sole frontier of the Kingdom of Mireana, it holds immense value.

There are those who covet it, those who have tried and failed… and various others besides, and among them, there must surely be no shortage of those who harbor grudges against Gilm or Daskar.

It’s highly unlikely that such people would let an opportunity like this slip by.

Of course, Daskar is well aware of this, and an Intelligence Unit led by the Prairie Wolves is guarding Gilm.

(With those guys around, I guess I can rest easy.)

Reaching that conclusion, Rei called out to Elena and the others riding in the Set Cage.

“Gilm is coming into view. We’ll be there soon.”

At his words, a sense of relief wafts out from inside the Set Cage.

Gilm is undoubtedly an important place not only for Marina and the others... but also for Elena, a member of the Noble Faction.

In front of Gilm’s main gate, some people are lining up to enter the city, while others—perhaps having received permission—are simply walking right through.

“Alright, I’ll let you out soon. The Set Cage isn’t visible from over there, so we’ll be a little ways away from Gilm—is that okay?”

“That’s fine. It’s not like I enjoy startling people, after all.”

In response to Elena’s words, Rei spoke to Set while petting him.

“Alright then, Set. Please lower the Set Cage a little way away from Gilm.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set purred happily and began descending toward the ground.

……Meanwhile, even among those near Gilm, those with keen hearing seemed to have caught a faint trace of Set’s voice, as they looked around in puzzlement.

Thanks to the Set Cage’s capabilities, they likely couldn’t tell where the sound was coming from.

Including those individuals, many turned their gaze toward the sound that had suddenly echoed through the air.

The source of the sound was, naturally, the Set Cage.

“Huh? Wait, where did that come from!?”

A few people let out cries of astonishment, but among them, a very small number wore expressions of delight.

Those with delighted expressions must know what the Set Cage is. They immediately looked up at the sky, saw Set there, and rejoiced.

“Grrrrr!”

Set’s roar echoed through the area, and only then did the others finally notice his presence.

Even if they don’t know about the Set Cage, anyone who comes to Gilm naturally knows about Set. …Though there are some who know nothing about Set at all, perhaps because they came here solely for work.

Especially now, with the expansion work underway and so many people around, there are quite a few like that.

“Set! You’re back!”

A guard spots Set and waves happily.

The men transporting lumber by cart also notice Set and wave happily.

In the guard’s case, he was simply happy that Set had returned, but the men transporting the lumber were wholeheartedly welcoming Set—or, more accurately, Rei, who was carrying the item box.

Amidst the expansion work, the ones who had undoubtedly been waiting most eagerly for Rei and the others to return were the lumberjacks and those transporting the cut timber.

They cut down trees, worked together to load them onto carts, and escorted those carts back to Gilm while protecting them from monster attacks.

Since the expansion work in Gilm has increased the flow of people, monsters have become more active in response, and attacks are not uncommon.

Previously, there were no monsters in the newly grown forest, but recently—perhaps because they’ve decided it’s a suitable place to live—their numbers have increased, so the escorts can’t let their guard down.

To these people, Rei was a true savior, someone who made their work dramatically easier.

Under the gaze of these people, Rei and Set first head toward the spot where the Set Cage was lowered.

“Hmm… It’s not that cramped inside, but it really feels nice to be out here like this.”

Vihera, having stepped out of the Set Cage, said this while stretching widely.

As she stretched, her massive breasts jiggled, drawing the attention of the men standing a short distance away... but she didn’t seem to mind in the least.

Then, following Vihera, Elena and Marina—beauties you might only see once in a lifetime—appeared, and the commotion grew even louder.

...Though far from being a once-in-a-lifetime sight, many residents of Gilm have seen Marina and Vihera countless times.

Many also turned their gaze toward figures like Yellow and Byune.

Despite attracting attention in various ways, Rei, treating it as business as usual, stowed the Set Cage into the Misty Ring without seeming to mind.

(The Set Cage had a few areas for improvement… but can I refine it before I need to use it again?)

Even if he were to improve the Set Cage, he has no idea exactly how to go about it.

Given that, he naturally couldn’t estimate when it would be ready, and even if he did improve it, it wouldn’t be a matter of tweaking one or two spots—it would undoubtedly require a major overhaul.

Given that, he decided it would be best to ask a professional.

“Well then, let’s go. We need to report to Lord Daskar that we’re back… and above all, we should help out with the expansion work as soon as possible.”

At Rei’s words, everyone nodded.

Only Yellow was lying on Set’s back, basking in the sun.

Summer is past its peak, and it’s starting to get a bit cooler as we head into fall.

However, that was only true in the early morning or at night; during the day, it was still hot enough to make one want to take a dip.

Even so, perhaps the intense sunlight pouring down from the sun didn’t bother Yellow, the black dragon’s child, as he was relaxing on Set’s back as comfortably as could be.

“Grr…”

“Kyu!”

As he chatted with Set.

With Set in tow, Rei and the others headed for the main gate of Gilm.

Normally, Rei and the others would have had to line up behind the people already waiting at the main gate... but perhaps because Daskar had given instructions in advance, they were able to complete the formalities immediately and enter Gilm.

That said, since Rei and the others were already registered as personnel for the expansion project, they could have entered without any special instructions from above anyway.

“Wow… It seems like there are a lot of people here.”

It was Vihera who said that.

Even though it was only for a short time, to Vihera, who had been active in the small country of Reburina, Gilm must have seemed incredibly developed and crowded.

Since they had been traveling with a group of nearly a thousand people, it wasn’t as if there were that few people to begin with.

No, in fact, with that many people traveling together—sleeping in carriages by day and on sandboats by night—quite a large group had gathered.

Even so, returning to Gilm like this, she couldn’t help but be amazed by the sheer number of people.

Originally, Gilm was the only frontier of the Kingdom of Mireana, so many people—including adventurers and merchants—gathered there.

But now, many others are coming here in search of work as well.

Even among adventurers and merchants, the number of people seeking work related to the expansion project has increased compared to before.

There are so many people now that makeshift lodgings have been set up near the construction sites... and even that isn’t enough to accommodate everyone who has gathered in Gilm.

(At Dusk Wheat inn, I’m glad I left my room as it was. If I had checked out, I definitely wouldn’t have been able to stay there anymore.)

Of course, not checking out means we’ll still be charged for the nights we’re not staying there.

But, conversely, as long as we can figure out a way to handle those fees, it’s not a problem at all.

And none of the group, including Rei, are short on money.

Only Byune has a frugal streak from his past life, but since Vihera, who shares his room, covers his lodging fees, it wasn’t an issue.

Of course, in the worst-case scenario, there was always the option of staying at Marina’s mansion in the Noble District.

“It’s nice that there are so many people, but it’s a bit of a hassle when it’s this hard to walk around.”

“It’s daytime, so that’s just how it is, isn’t it?”

Elena muttered as she looked around, and Marina replied.

In fact, it was currently midday, and many of the workers involved in the expansion project had come into town to eat.

“Hmm!”

Amidst the crowd, Byune glanced at a busy food stall and muttered.

“Let’s report to Daskar first, then have lunch. Maybe Daskar has already prepared something for us to eat.”

At Marina’s suggestion, which mentioned Daskar’s name, Byune reluctantly nods.

“Cold udon, huh? …Well, I guess it is that time of year.”

Seeing the udon stall that Byune had said she wanted to eat at, Rei muttered as if in agreement.

Amid the continuing hot days, it wouldn’t be quite accurate to say “as it should be,” but not many people were eager to eat the hot soup udon that had started being sold at Full Belly Inn.

It was only natural to want cold food precisely because it was hot.

That said, it hadn’t been all that long since Rei and the others had left Gilm.

Thinking about it that way, it wouldn’t have been strange if there had been stalls serving cold udon dishes all along.

(People come from all sorts of villages and towns to work, and they eat udon. The workers go back to their hometowns and spread the word about udon… In that sense, udon might become incredibly popular.)

With such thoughts in mind, Rei and the others made their way through Gilm.

Naturally, the group—with Set in tow and Elena and the others in their midst—stood out like a sore thumb…

“Oh, Set! Hey, Set! When did you get back?!”

“Set-chan, let’s play! Set-chan!”

The first people to spot Set came running over to Rei and the others.

“Ah, sorry. I’d love to let Set play, but right now I have to talk to Lord Daskar at the lord’s manor. So…”

“Oh, well then, wouldn’t it be okay to just leave Set-chan here? I’ll take care of him if anything happens. What do you say, Captain Rei?”

Just as Rei was about to refuse the group swarming around Set, someone he recognized interrupted him.

It was exactly the kind of person who made him think, “Thank goodness Milein isn’t here.”

“Johanna, stop calling me ‘Captain’ already. How many years ago do you think that was?”

“How many years? It’s not like it was ten or twenty years ago, so what’s the big deal?”

Johanna, who said this with a smile, is one of the most famous people in Gilm—though perhaps not quite on the same level as Milein.

Not only is she a skilled adventurer, but above all, she’s on par with Milein when it comes to her love for Set.

“Listen…”

“Anyway, what are we going to do? I don’t mind being left in charge at all, since I get to stay with Set-chan.”

At Johanna’s words, the children around them turned their hopeful gazes toward Rei.

Faced with those gazes, Rei hesitated for a moment, but eventually nodded.

“Alright. Then, I’m counting on you to look after Set.”

“Sure, leave it to me. Right, Set-chan? Let’s play together until Rei comes back, okay?”

“Grrrr…”

Hearing Johanna call him “Rei-san” instead of “Captain” as she had earlier, Set growled in agreement.

It had been a while since Set had returned to Gilm, so he was probably happy to have someone to play with.

In the end, Rei and the others left Set behind and headed for the lord’s manor.
