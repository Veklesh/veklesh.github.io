Rei was flying through the summer sky on Set.

Below him lay the forests surrounding Sublusta.

In a few spots, even from Rei’s vantage point, he could see people.

Whether they were bandits or adventurers… Rei couldn’t tell.

Two days ago, when Rei flew over this area, he had spotted a group of people who clearly looked like a crowd to him, and they turned out to be bandits.

Since he had wiped out three bandit groups, it wouldn’t be surprising if the bandits’ movements had slowed down a bit… that was Rei’s prediction, but as far as he could tell from the sky, the groups of people who appeared to be bandits were moving about as usual.

“Well, I suppose the number of bandits will be significantly reduced by the end of today.”

Normally, the task of subduing the bandits around Sublusta would naturally fall to the Sublusta Guild, the guards, and the knights.

But Sublusta isn’t doing it.

Well, technically, the guild has issued requests for bandit suppression, but it would be more accurate to say they simply haven’t been able to handle it.

As a result, the bandits are running rampant, and supplies meant to be transported to Gilm are being lost.

That is precisely why Daskar decided to take on the task of subduing the bandits.

Of course, this isn’t done through Sublusta… rather, since merchant caravans, people seeking work, and adventurers coming to Gilm are being attacked by bandits, Gilm is the one requesting their elimination.

Since they aren’t going through the Sublusta Guild, there’s no risk of complaints. …Of course, there’s no doubt the Sublusta Guild finds this unwelcome, but the power dynamic between the guilds in Gilm and Sublusta was overwhelmingly in Gilm’s favor.

In reality, the Sublusta Guild also possesses considerable power.

That’s only natural. After all, Subursta is a town situated at what could be called the gateway to the frontier.

There are a fair number of skilled and seasoned adventurers there.

But... compared to Abuero, which is closer to the frontier, or Gilm, which lies deep within the frontier, the quality of the adventurers here inevitably falls short.

Rei and the others’ current actions were a clear reflection of that reality.

“Guruu?”

“Isn’t it about time?” Rei looked around at the crowd as Set cleared his throat.

There weren’t that many caravans or adventurers on the road.

And there were no bandits lurking nearby to target them either.

(If there were bandits attacking a caravan or something, it would be obvious who our target is. …Well, we’re going to be hunting bandits all day today, so I guess it’s fine. Since there are other groups hunting bandits besides us, it’s not like we won’t find any.)

As for today’s bandit hunt, several skilled adventurers besides Rei and the others from the Crimson Wings are scheduled to participate.

……The fact that we’re waiting a day instead of setting out the very next day after Rei and the others return probably has to do with the travel plans of the other adventurers joining us.

“Yeah, let’s just lower the Set Cage somewhere out of the way for now. If we stow it in the Misty Ring, there shouldn’t be any problems.”

“Grrrr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl to signal he understood, then began descending toward the ground.

“I’m about to set it down, so watch out for the impact!”

He called out to the Set Cage as if to say, “Just in case…” and a few seconds later, the Set Cage was dropped onto the ground.

If anyone had been passing by nearby, they would have felt the impact.

Once Set landed on the ground, Rei caught sight of the figures emerging from the Set Cage.

Five people—Elena, Marina, Vihera, Byune, and Ara—and Yero, who was being held by Elena.

It was only natural that the group emerging from the Set Cage showed no signs of fatigue, since it had been less than ten minutes since they’d departed from Gilm.

That said, the Set Cage is designed so that one can relax comfortably inside.

It’s comfortable enough that a journey of about half a day poses no problem at all.

“So, Rei. What do we do now? You’ve found where the bandits are, haven’t you?”

Rei nodded at Elena, who wore a smile that was both fierce and radiant—a seemingly contradictory expression that, nonetheless, suited her perfectly.

“Yeah. I spotted a few suspicious spots from above. So, just like we talked about yesterday, we’ll split up from here, right?”

“That’s right. Considering the number of bandits lurking in the area, that’s undoubtedly the best course of action. Is that all right with you?”

“Yes, that’s fine.”

When Elena asked for confirmation, Marina nodded in agreement.

In fact, considering the adventurers likely coming from Sublusta, there’s no doubt they’ll need as many hands as possible.

In that situation, it’s true that having a skilled group like Rei’s act as a single unit is a waste of resources in terms of combat strength.

It only makes sense, then, for Rei and Set to operate separately from the rest of the group to avoid that situation.

Of course, there was also the issue of what to do with the treasure hoarded by the bandits they’d hunted down, but Elena possessed a magic pouch—something akin to a budget-friendly version of an item box.

Using that, it should be possible to store a certain amount of treasure.

If it absolutely couldn’t be stored in the magic pouch, then we’d have to call Rei… or perhaps load it onto the bandits’ wagon and haul it away.

Naturally, the bandits would need to convert the goods they’d obtained by raiding merchants into cash.

Or perhaps they trade them directly for food and alcohol, but either way, they need a means of transporting them.

They could ask their trading partners to come to their hideout, but from the bandits’ perspective, they’d want to keep their hideout a secret as much as possible.

After all, if they’re not careful, there’s a chance the trading partner might betray them and reveal their hideout.

For that reason, it was only natural for the bandits to prepare wagons and the like.

“Alright, then. …Oh, I saw a few groups of people gathered in the forest over there, so we might want to try attacking from that direction. We’d like to reduce the number of bandits as much as possible before reinforcements arrive—and for financial gain, too.”

In addition to the standard reward for this job, we’ll also get our hands on the bandits’ treasure, weapons, and supplies for the expansion project.

Naturally, these become the property of the adventurers who defeat the bandits and serve as additional rewards.

However, aside from gems, adventurers don’t really need that many weapons. And as for supplies for Gilm’s expansion project, they’d only be a burden to the adventurers.

For these reasons, Daskar has entered into a contract with the adventurers to purchase these supplies from them, albeit at a price slightly below market value.

This was a mutually beneficial arrangement for both the adventurers—who would be at a loss with such supplies—and Gilm, which wanted to acquire as many supplies as possible.

……Of course, for those whose supplies were stolen by bandits, it was nothing short of a massive loss……but not many merchants survive an attack by bandits.

That’s only natural. It’s common sense for bandits to minimize any evidence of their activities, and above all, given that so many bandits are currently active in this area, they can’t afford to let their information reach the guards or others.

If no one survives, the very fact that a bandit attack occurred disappears, and as a result, the damage caused by the bandits is recorded as zero.

However, things don’t always go that smoothly. Several people have managed to escape the bandits, and word of their experiences has spread to places like Sublusta, Abuero, and Gilm, leading to the situation we’re in now.

“If we make too much money, people might get jealous, you know?”

“I don’t think an adventurer from Gilm—especially one chosen for this bandit hunt—would do something like that.”

Even if there were someone who felt jealous of Rei and the others, they surely wouldn’t want to make enemies of them while operating in Gilm.

Rei, with his nickname; Marina, the former guild master; and, above all, Set, who has become Gilm’s mascot.

It’s easy to imagine what would happen if you made enemies of them.

At worst… no, even without things going wrong, it would become difficult to operate within Gilm.

I couldn’t imagine that any adventurer in Gilm who knew this would try to pull a stunt against Rei and the others.

“...I agree. But you never know what someone will do when they’re truly cornered, right? Considering that, I think it’s best to be cautious.”

“You’re right. In that case… if we happen to be hunting the same bandits, I’ll try to let you have them whenever possible. Is that okay?”

“Yes. I think that level of consideration is plenty.”

“I don’t really want to back down if there’s a strong opponent, though.”

The one voicing dissatisfaction at Marina’s words was Vihera, who had been looking forward to fighting the bandits.

It wasn’t fun to have a rare chance to fight ruined by petty jealousy.

Seeing Vihera’s displeased expression, Rei spoke up in a conciliatory tone.

“What the adventurers really want is the bandits’ treasure. So, in the worst-case scenario, Vihera could defeat the bandits and then hand over the treasure… wouldn’t that work?”

“Right?”

Vihera broke into a broad smile and immediately latched onto Rei’s suggestion.

That said, even Rei, who came up with the idea, didn’t really think things would go so smoothly.

With famous figures like Elena and Marina around, it would be difficult to determine whether they would accept a plan where the bandits are defeated and only the treasure is handed over.

Of course, for some people, that wouldn’t be a problem at all, and there would be those who would gratefully accept the treasure as a stroke of luck.

...In contrast to Vihera, who was delighted by the idea, Byune—whose goal was to make money—harbored some dissatisfaction, though he didn’t let it show.

“Anyway, you guys do your best over there. …Well then, Set and I are off. Set.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set let out a growl and turned his back.

Then, after confirming that Rei was on his back, Set took a few running steps and leaped into the sky.

Looking down at the ground from atop Set’s back, Rei saw Marina waving at him, Elena and Ara gazing in his direction, and Vihera and Byune already heading in the direction Rei had indicated to search for the bandits.

“Well then, I guess we’ll go look for the thieves too. Set, help me look. But I’d prefer a veteran thief—not some rookie who just arrived here and isn’t even bothering to disguise themselves.”

“Growl? Growl!”

Set muttered, seeming momentarily confused by Rei’s words, but then cleared his throat to indicate he understood.

(Veteran thieves are hard to find, after all. Since we can scout from above, we should take the lead in hunting them down. …Well, once we find them, Elena and the others should have no trouble taking them down.)

Just as Rei was thinking that, Set suddenly growled.

Rei followed his gaze and looked at a spot that seemed no different from the rest of the forest... but nothing seemed out of the ordinary.

But since Set had spotted it, there must be something there... It was almost certainly a bandit, so he signaled to Set to descend.

As they descended, they eventually spotted the glint of metal reflecting through the gaps in the tree branches.

Rei knew exactly what it was without even having to think about it.

A weapon or armor. They must have been hiding it behind the branches.

If they had simply been flying through the sky, they likely wouldn’t have noticed that metallic glint.

But with Set—who possessed senses far superior to those of a human—flying overhead, it was a different story.

“Grrrrrrr!”

The moment Set let out a roar, Rei leaped down from his back.

It was still a jump from a height higher than the treetops, but with the Sleipnir Boots that Rei possessed, that kind of height was no problem at all.

Managing to kick off mid-air to slow his descent, Rei landed on the ground with almost no sound.

What he saw as he fell was a naked woman, her wrists bound to the tree trunk and pierced by a dagger, rendered unable to move. …However, seeing that her body was pierced by numerous daggers yet she remained motionless, it was clear she was already dead.

“What!? Huh?”

“Too slow. You bastard.”

The moment he landed, Rei drew the Death Scythe from his Misty Ring and—no, even before that—swinging it at the thief’s torso, who was completely disoriented by Set’s cry and had suddenly appeared right before him.

“What?!”

With a somewhat dazed cry, the thief’s upper body shifted… and he collapsed, scattering his entrails all around.

And so, the worst possible moment for the bandit began.
