The next day after arriving at the dungeon, Rei and his party were heading toward the dungeon by carriage as if it were only natural.

...Normally, a carriage wouldn't be necessary for heading to the dungeon.

But since they needed to transport Vihera and the unconscious Rodos, a carriage was preferable.

Of course, they could have carried the two on their backs if pushed, but if they could travel comfortably, there was no reason not to use it.

Even so, perhaps out of some hesitation, they only used Elena's carriage; Elk's carriage remained at the inn.

When they left Gilm, Elk had prepared a carriage too, but he must have judged that Elena's carriage alone would suffice for the journey from the inn to the dungeon.

But naturally, that carriage was extremely conspicuous.

It wasn't adorned with unnecessary, ostentatious decorations, but the horse pulling it was clearly worthy of being called a thoroughbred, and anyone who knew what they were looking at would instantly recognize the carriage itself as an extremely valuable magic item.

"Well, I suppose this proves it was the right time after all,"

Ara murmured as she gazed out the window.

The reason Ara, who usually served as the coachman, was inside the carriage this time was simply because the innkeeper was driving it.

Given how valuable this carriage was, they naturally didn't want to leave it parked in front of the dungeon.

Besides, a carriage parked in front of the dungeon would only be in the way.

That's precisely why, after Rei and the others entered the dungeon, the carriage needed to be transported back to the inn.

The man currently driving the carriage was sent for that purpose.

The fact that the service around here is so thorough is probably largely because it's a high-class inn.

"I suppose so. The early birds are probably diving into the dungeon first thing in the morning."

Marina murmured as she gazed out the window at the surroundings.

True to her words, those motivated enough to venture into the dungeon would start early... this season, it wasn't uncommon for people to enter before dawn had fully broken.

Even if not that early, those seriously trying to earn money would be in the dungeon by seven in the morning... or at the latest, before eight.

Thinking about it that way, it was no wonder Rei and the others, heading for the dungeon around nine in the morning, were seen as clearly lacking motivation.

(Well, among those who entered the dungeon this morning, we'll probably be the first to reach the lowest level.)

Given the extreme shortcut provided by Grimm's teleportation magic, Marina couldn't imagine anyone reaching the lowest level before them.

Of course, staying overnight in the dungeon was perfectly possible, so it wasn't impossible that those who had entered earlier than yesterday hadn't reached the lowest level yet.

But the lowest level held nothing but the Silver Lion Marina's group intended to defeat this time. In terms of earning gold, reaching the lowest level wasn't necessary.

While these thoughts ran through her mind, the carriage continued along the road, eventually arriving before the dungeon entrance.

When the carriage stopped, Elk lifted Rodos and Ara lifted Vihera as they stepped down.

Several adventurers still lingering nearby cast curious glances their way. However, seeing the inn employee from where Rei and the others were staying seated in the driver's seat, and knowing guards were present for emergencies, no one seemed inclined to entertain any strange notions.

"Guards for the carriage?" Rei and the others had wondered briefly when first told, but considering the value of the carriage and horses, they quickly realized it was only natural.

"Well then, we shall take our leave here. We await your safe return."

With a polite bow, the carriage departed.

They probably wondered why two unconscious people were being taken into the dungeon... but not prying into guests' affairs was likely part of their training as employees of a high-class inn.

That said, Rei and the others didn't particularly mind the puzzled looks directed their way.

"Rei... Set-chan!"

Waiting for Rei and the others were Johanna and her companions.

She started to call out to Rei, but upon spotting Set playing with Yellow beside him, her attention immediately shifted.

Rei closed the distance at a speed that made her eyes widen, then began to dote on Set.

Set purred happily.

Yellow, perched on Set's head, was stroked as if it were an afterthought, letting out a contented purr.

"Ah... yeah. I figured it'd turn out like this."

Since he hadn't introduced Johanna and Set yesterday, he understood that when they met today, their joy would explode, making up for yesterday.

Seeing Johanna like this, the fellow adventurers bowed their heads to Rei, looking a little embarrassed.

"Sorry, Rei. ...Well, she kept saying since yesterday that she was going to brag to Milein about this."

"Why do those two get along so badly? Or maybe in this case, it's actually good?"

Birds of a feather flock together, or perhaps it's a case of birds of a feather hating each other. It felt like both explanations fit, and yet neither quite did.

Both harbored feelings for Set, and the intensity of those feelings was unmatched by anyone else.

Given that, couldn't they get along? Rei had once entertained such a thought, but he had long since abandoned such naive ideas.

Because he'd seen time and again that the mutual dislike was far stronger than the "birds of a feather" thing.

"Well, it's true that Set is adorable, so it's not surprising he ended up like that,"

Johanna's companion remarked thoughtfully.

Ray knew that neither Johanna nor her companions saw Set as merely an adorable presence.

During the training they underwent when assigned to Rei's guerrilla unit during the Bestir Empire's civil war, they had faced the real, full-powered Set.

Of course, if Set truly attacked, an ordinary opponent would die without being able to do anything, so they never actually laid a hand on him.

But simply facing a serious Set was no ordinary feat.

Considering what Rei and Set accomplished together during that civil war, that feeling could only grow stronger, never weaker.

Even beyond such experiences, the man before Rei could still find Set endearing... perhaps it was simply a matter of habit.

Then again, someone without that kind of personality probably wouldn't have gone out of their way to come to Gilm just to be near Rei.

"Hey, Johanna! Stop fussing over Set so much, we're leaving soon!"

A different companion of Johanna's, not the one who had been talking with Rei, called out.

That voice must have snapped her back to reality. Johanna, who had been petting Set and Yellow to her heart's content, scrambled to her feet and bowed again toward Rei and the others.

"Good morning. Sorry, I was just having some withdrawal symptoms..."

"Wait, what the hell are withdrawal symptoms?"

Rei couldn't help but say to Johanna, though he didn't need to ask what kind of withdrawal symptoms they were.

"Well, Rei. That's enough about that, right? We're diving into the dungeon together today, so let's get along."

Sighing softly at Marina's soothing words, Rei turned his gaze toward the dungeon.

Perhaps because it was already past nine in the morning, there were only a few people around.

The busiest morning hours had likely passed.

"Right. We won't have to wait in line now. Everyone ready?"

Everyone nodded in agreement at Rei's question.

Since they were about to challenge the Silver Lion, it was certain they were fully prepared.

"Then, let's go."

Nodding at Rei's words, the group began walking toward the dungeon.

A guild clerk checking guild cards there saw Rei and the others and wore a look of surprise.

It was understandable. While it wasn't uncommon to see people brought out of the dungeon after losing consciousness in battle, taking someone unconscious into the dungeon was simply unthinkable under normal circumstances.

Taking unconscious people into a dungeon could easily escalate into murder.

"Whoa, whoa, whoa, wait a minute. What the hell are you guys doing? What's up with those two unconscious people?"

The man declared, flustered.

He was a different person from the one who had checked their guild cards when Rei and the others had previously ventured into the dungeon.

Being a young man in his mid-twenties, he seemed slightly flustered by the unexpected turn of events as he spoke.

"Here. The guild is aware of this matter, so there's no problem."

Marina handed the documents to the confused man.

He took the documents with the professionalism expected of a guild employee, though he couldn't help but stare at Marina's smile with a lecherous look on his face. He quickly scanned them.

Perhaps it mentioned Marina's true identity, for the man suddenly straightened up, flustered, and spoke.

"My apologies. Please proceed."

"Whoa..."

Who was it who muttered that, seemingly unable to help themselves, upon seeing the guild employee's attitude?

His blatant change in demeanor stirred various emotions in those watching.

Though, considering Marina's position, that reaction was hardly surprising.

"Thanks. Well, shall we go?"

Following Marina, they entered the dungeon.

Behind Rei and the others, the guild clerk stood temporarily at the entrance.

This was because the documents mentioned earlier stated that for a short time after Marina's party entered, no other adventurers were to be allowed inside the dungeon.

The fact that Rei and the others had come at this time, slightly off-peak rather than during the busiest morning hours, was likely also due to this matter.

...It was a precaution to prevent Grim from encountering other adventurers, though neither the person who drafted the document nor the one who received it knew the exact reason.

It was because Guildmaster Marina had created it using her own authority.

It was an act that could easily be called an abuse of authority, but Grim's existence was something that warranted such secrecy.

Descending the dungeon stairs, they eventually opened a door.

The busy morning rush had already passed, and likely because several other adventurers had passed through here, there were no monsters to be found.

"We'll walk a bit. Near the entrance, someone might try to come out from inside."

At Rei's words, everyone except Johanna and the others nodded seriously.

Johanna and the others had been told of Grimm's existence, but they hadn't yet heard anything about how to reach the lowest level.

That's why they tilted their heads at Rei's words, yet remained silent—likely due to their strong trust in him.

...Incidentally, while Vihera and Rodos had been carried by hand up to the first basement level, they were now riding on Set's back.

Monsters appearing on this level shouldn't be a problem, but this was likely a precaution against any unforeseen circumstances.

If monsters attacked here, Elk would likely... no, definitely leap out immediately and annihilate the foe.

And it wouldn't just be Elk.

To prevent any unnecessary harm coming to Vihera, the others—Rei, Elena, Marina—would do the same.

"Vihera, I envy you. To be able to ride on Set-chan's back..."

Johanna murmured softly as she followed Rei and the others, who were walking ahead while keeping watch.

"Envious? Vihera is unconscious, you know? Even if she were riding on Set, I doubt she'd have any idea."

"Yeah, right. ...Honestly, I envy Set more for having Lady Vihera on his back."

"Huh? You have that kind of hobby? Like wanting to be trampled or crushed by a woman?"

Several people nearby took a step back at the man's words about envying Set.

The man tried to scramble for an excuse to his friends...

"Hey, keep it down. We've only just entered the dungeon, but this is definitely a dungeon."

Johanna and the others fell silent after being cautioned by Elk.

After advancing for several minutes, once they judged they were sufficiently far from the dungeon entrance, Rei scanned the surroundings.

No one was in sight, but he checked just in case.

What was about to happen absolutely must not be seen by anyone else.

After carefully scanning the area for any signs of presence, Rei turned his gaze back to Johanna and the others and spoke.

"I'm going to use magic to ensure you never speak of what happens next. Is that acceptable?"

"Magic?"

"Yes. It's magic that will cause my flames within them to take their life if they tell anyone. ...However, while this magic resides within them, it also grants the benefit of strong resistance to fire."

He had already revealed Grimm's existence yesterday, and he felt confident Grimm wouldn't betray him.

But considering the worst-case scenario, taking precautions was still essential.

Johanna and the others nodded without hesitation at Rei's question, spoken with that very thought in mind.

It showed just how deeply they trusted Rei.

Receiving Johanna and the others' smiles filled with trust... Rei drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring and began chanting the spell for the Seeds of Warning.
