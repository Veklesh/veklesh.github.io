Without the slightest hesitation, Set swallowed the goblin leader's magic stone. Naturally, Vihera was astonished.

His lack of hesitation clearly indicated he had swallowed magic stones countless times before.

"Huh? Just now... That wasn't my imagination, was it?"

Rei nodded in response to Vihera, who asked as if seeking confirmation.

As he did so, he stroked Set's head, who was happily purring with satisfaction after gaining a new skill... or more accurately, after his Enhanced Sense of Smell skill leveled up.

"Your Enhanced Sense of Smell leveled up? Well done, Set. That skill is quite useful."

Rei murmured thoughtfully, recalling several times before when Enhanced Sense of Smell had saved them.

Most recently, during the assassination attempt in Gosh.

Set's Enhanced Sense of Smell had been essential for locating the base of the assassins known as the Hundred-Faced Ones who carried it out.

Purely as an ability, it probably wasn't that powerful of a skill.

But in Set's case, he originally possessed exceptionally keen senses as a griffin.

His sense of smell, one of those senses, was naturally excellent, and enhancing that sense—Scent Enhancement—was a subtle yet useful skill.

...Though, when using Enhanced Sense of Smell, encountering foul odors inflicts even greater damage.

"Hold on a second. Don't just figure things out between Rei and Set—explain it properly to me too. From what I've heard so far, it sounds like swallowing the magic stone made you learn a skill... or enhance it? That's what I heard anyway."

Vihera asked, looking confused.

Of course she was. Swallowing a magic stone was completely beyond common sense, and the idea of acquiring a skill by swallowing one was utterly incomprehensible.

"Anyway, drink this and calm down."

Saying that, Misty Ring poured cold fruit juice from the flask she'd taken out into a cup and handed it to Vihera.

It was fruit juice bought in Gilm, but its effect was fully realized.

After taking it and sipping a mouthful or two, Vihera gradually regained her composure.

If this were an ordinary human, they wouldn't have calmed down this easily.

But unfortunately... or perhaps fortunately, Vihera was far from ordinary.

Like Elena and Marina, she possessed abilities and a character worthy of being called a heroine.

That was precisely why she could calm down with something as simple as drinking fruit water.

"...So, you're going to explain, right? That's why you showed me this scene, isn't it?"

The now-calm Vihera gently stroked the head of Set, who was nestled against Rei.

Set purred happily at Vihera.

No matter what Set was, she would never hate him.

She understood that sentiment from Vihera's attitude.

"Ah, of course. ...Now then, where should I begin? Hmm, let's start by asking this. Do you know the name of the magic called 'Beast Magic'?"

"Beast Magic? No, I don't know it. And magic? Not sorcery?"

"Ah, sorcery. At least when Beast Sorcery was created, magic didn't exist. Only sorcery existed."

The terms 'magic' and 'sorcery' refer to the same phenomenon.

But it's been quite a while since the term 'magic' fell out of use and 'sorcery' became the word used instead.

Of course, the term 'magic' itself still exists today, but it's more of a word you'd find in history books.

"So, does Set have some connection to this Beast Sorcery?"

"Yes. The Zepairu Clan, led by the demon Zepail. You've heard that name before, haven't you?"

"Of course. There's no one who doesn't know the name Zepair... Not anyone who's studied even a little history."

"Exactly. So, after I died in my original world, Zepail's soul saved me and sent me to this world."

"...Huh?"

That was all Vihera could manage in response to the words that suddenly came out of Rei's mouth.

It was that difficult to comprehend the words that had slipped so casually from Rei's mouth.

Seeing Vihera's reaction, Rei offered a bit more explanation.

"Zepair and the others were a gathering of geniuses. You know that, right?"

"Yes. They were a group of individuals who were considered geniuses among geniuses, weren't they?"

"Exactly. And what Zepair created was Beast Magic—a technique that uses the magic power of spellcasters to create monsters. The monsters born from this Beast Magic acquire new skills by absorbing the magic stones of other monsters."

"Monsters... magic stones... growth? ...Huh!? D-Does that mean...!"

As Rei explained, the legend of the Zepairu clan flashed through Vihera's mind.

It was said that the familiars they commanded could single-handedly take on an entire army... no, they possessed power sufficient to destroy an entire nation.

Vihera had always assumed the story had grown exaggerated over time, spreading like folklore or an old tale.

But... what if...?

Rei nodded back at Vihera, who had turned his gaze toward him.

"Probably... no, definitely true. Unfortunately, or perhaps fortunately, the current Set isn't quite that powerful of a monster yet."

As he gently stroked Set, a wry smile appeared on Rei's lips for some reason.

"I thought people would definitely point out that I'm not human like everyone else here, especially regarding the beast magic."

"That surprised me too... but if you say it's because it's Rei, I can understand it."

"No, it's not because it's me..."

Rei started to speak up in denial, but immediately, the countless things she had done flashed through her mind, leaving her speechless.

"Ah, yeah. Well, if Vihera doesn't mind that part, I won't say anything more either."

"Right. Honestly, I am curious about a different world... but hearing about it doesn't mean I can go there, does it? So naturally, I should focus my attention on things that interest me more right now."

Vihera spoke with a smile, her gaze fixed on Set.

"Guru?" Set, caught by Vihera's gaze, tilted his head slightly, purring softly, "What is it?"

That adorable expression could easily charm anyone who saw it, thought both Rei and Vihera.

"...So, is there anything else you haven't said?"

Surrendering to Set's charm, Vihera stroked his body with its soft, silky fur while asking. In response, Rei drew the Death Scythe from the Misty Ring.

Normally, suddenly seeing a large scythe appear right before one's eyes would be enough to startle anyone, but Vihera, trusting Rei, stared at the Death Scythe without any particular sign of surprise.

"What about the Death Scythe?"

"Beast Magic is a type of sorcery that uses the target's magic power to create monsters. Normally, creating the monster would exhaust their magic power... but if there's still magic power left over, it creates a magic item."

Rei figured it was probably Takumu, also from Japan like himself, who devised this mechanism, likely thinking it was a waste to let such vast magic power go unused.

He figured it was also a result of thinking about it from a game design perspective.

"So that Death Scythe too..."

"Yeah. It was created by Beast Magic. That's precisely why even the Death Scythe can learn skills from magic stones."

"Wait a moment. So, the spells Rei normally uses..."

Rei nodded and spoke to Vihera, who asked as if confirming.

"Well, this Death Scythe has a lot of skills too. My element is fire, so I can't use wind-based magic like Flying Slash."

"...Hmm. That's rather convenient. Hey, Rei. Can I use that magic called Beast Magic too?"

Vihera fixed a hopeful gaze on Rei, but he shook his head at her.

"Unfortunately, it's completely impossible with Vihera's magical power."

Rei didn't have an exact grasp of how much magical power Vihera possessed.

But even so, he didn't think she could possibly succeed with Beast Magic if her magic power was only enough to cast basic spells.

(Magic items are one thing, but in Vihera's case, she probably wants her own familiar or some kind of personal monster, huh?)

He recalled Vihera mentioning something like that when they reunited after returning to Gilm.

But no matter how much Rei wanted to let Vihera try Beast Magic, it was an undeniable fact that without the magical power required to create a monster in the first place, there was simply nothing he could do.

If she tried Beast Magic now, it would undoubtedly end in failure.

Strangely enough, Rei felt utterly certain of this.

But Vihera, unaware of Rei's certainty, couldn't accept being told it was impossible right away.

"How can you know without trying?"

"That's... exactly what I understand. But fundamentally, you do understand that Beast Magic is an extremely powerful form of magic, right?"

Though slightly annoyed by Rei suddenly changing the subject, Vihera nodded, enduring it since it was about Beast Magic.

"Yes. Legends aside, just looking at Set makes that perfectly clear."

Just being a rank A monster like a griffin makes it incredibly powerful, but it also masters numerous skills. And above all, it absolutely trusts Rei and can understand his words perfectly.

A griffin that could communicate, fully grasp Rei's intentions, and act accordingly.

Few would dare consider opposing such an ally.

Rei nodded in agreement with Vihera's explanation.

Then he continued, "But..."

"Why do you think such a convenient and powerful beast magic isn't used by anyone in this era? Well, I'm the exception, of course."

"Is that... the reason I can't use beast magic?"

Vihera asked in response to Rei's words.

Rei nodded back at Vihera.

"That's right. Using Beast Magic absolutely requires a massive amount of magic power. And if you want a magic item like the Death Scythe, it demands even more magic power than that... an enormous amount. Having said this much, you should understand without me finishing, right?"

It's impossible with my own magic power. Hearing this, Vihera's expression darkened slightly, a hint of dissatisfaction visible.

But increasing magical power is extremely difficult.

While it could increase through some trigger, what that trigger was varied from person to person, and the amount of magic power gained also differed between individuals.

Considering that, the possibility of Vihera being able to attempt Beast Magic in her current state was low, but it wasn't entirely hopeless.

"Besides, Beast Magic can only be attempted once in a lifetime. Whether it fails or succeeds... it's a one-time shot. You can't just try it at a time like this, can you?"

"...I suppose so."

Vihera reluctantly voiced her resignation.

Still, she couldn't help but think that if possible... someday...

She did want to befriend monsters like Set and Yellow, and it was her true desire to command creatures like the ones Zepail had supposedly led in legend.

But Rei's explanation made her understand that it was impossible for her right now.

"I see. Looks like we'll have to give up for today. ...Oh, right. So, wait—could it be that Yellow, the one Elena has with her, was actually created using that beast magic too?"

"No, Yellow is entirely a black dragon cub Elena created using dragon language magic. ...Well, if you ask if it's similar to beast magic, it definitely is."

Beast Magic, which uses magical power to create monsters, and Ancient Dragon magic... or perhaps it should be called dragon energy, which uses dragon language magic to create familiars.

These two are similar, yet fundamentally different in many ways.

(Well, they probably referenced this sort of thing when creating Beast Magic...)

If that's the case, then it's no wonder these two are so similar.

"Right. So what I should do is have an alchemist create things like golems and gargoyles, I suppose."

"It feels weird for me to say this since I suggested it, but I don't think you have to force yourself to make those kinds of things. ...More importantly, is that all you have to say after hearing my explanation?"

"That's all? Was there something else?"

"No, I mean... um, don't you find them... creepy or something?"

Though Rei asked as if it were nothing, deep down, he harbored anxiety about what would happen if Vihera refused him.

But when Vihera was asked that, she looked momentarily taken aback, as if caught off guard... and the next instant, she gently pressed her lips to Rei's.

Then, a few seconds later, she pulled away and spoke.

"Silly. I fell for Rei because she's Rei, Rei. Do you really think something like that could change how I feel now?"

She said this with a smile.
