Gently averting his gaze from the brown valley, Rei nodded at Marina's words, as if trying to cover something up.

"Yeah. There was a chance we could get the magic stone from an unknown monster."

Marina, who had been expecting Rei's answer, flashed a seductive smile and turned her gaze to Vihera beside him.

Though... seeing how it hung Pump and toyed with him half-heartedly, I suppose you could only call it a goblin.

"Right. Anyway, goblins aren't much trouble as individual monsters, but they become a real nuisance when they gather in numbers. Since he helped prevent that damage, we really should thank him properly."

"Marina, did you call Rei here just to say that?"

Marina implied he'd returned unexpectedly early.

Within Marina's question lay a hint of unease.

That unease wasn't about Rei having been harmed upon his return... but rather the fear that Rei might have caused some terrible incident in Gosh and could no longer stay there.

Knowing Rei as she did, that worry was far from overthinking.

"Um, Rei. You didn't... I mean, just maybe... cause any major trouble in Gosh, did you?"

Both things Rei mentioned were, by any normal standard, the kind of blunders you'd call fatal.

Marina, who usually carried herself with the composed air of a mature woman, now pressed her hand to her forehead as if enduring a headache and stared at the desk.

Concerned by Marina's reaction, Rei hurriedly spoke up.

"Just so you know, it was the Sand Thieves... a type of bandit. That guild leader lent them a magic item called a Sand Ship and had them attack other merchants! When I helped them, I took the Sand Ship from the Sand Thieves."

"...I see. Well, I suppose that can't be helped. You are Rei, the one called the Bandit Eater. Attacking thieves is practically one of your hobbies, right?"

It wasn't strange at all that she knew how Rei was referred to.

Especially since Marina was a girl in love.

"It's not like attacking thieves is a hobby or anything..."

If it really were a hobby, it'd be a pretty nasty one.

That's what Rei thought, but looking back on his own actions so far, he couldn't exactly deny it.

"So, what about threatening the lord?"

"That was to prove I don't treat nobles any differently."

"...I won't ask why things ended up that way, okay?"

Marina felt that hearing any more would likely give her a stomachache.

Watching the two of them, Vihera could barely hold back a smile.

Though Vihera and Marina were rivals in love, that didn't mean they disliked each other.

"Knowing Rei, he probably did something wildly unexpected again, didn't he?"

"The worst part is I can't even deny it outright,"

Marina sighed and replied to Vihera, who murmured with amusement.

She wanted to say it was normal for Rei... but considering the current situation, it was undeniably far from ordinary.

After all, if threatening a lord were considered normal, it would be utterly dangerous.

(Still, with talent like Rei's, I'd protect him no matter what Solrain says.)

A Rank B adventurer with a nickname, who had a griffin as a familiar. And in terms of offensive power, he possessed enough to take on an entire army single-handedly.

There was no way a vassal state could demand the disposal of such a talent.

Moreover, attempting to execute him over such matters and having him flee to another country would inflict devastating damage on the Kingdom of Mireana.

Even if they considered disposing of Rei before he fled to another country... he possesses the means to take on an entire army single-handedly, and commands a griffin with mobility incomparable to any carriage or horse. Confronting him would only lead to greater losses.

Furthermore, Rei was under the protection of the Neutral Faction of Daskar. Forcibly disposing of Rei risked turning the Neutral Faction into enemies as well.

No matter how one looked at it, eliminating Rei was simply not an option.

Considering that three extremely powerful individuals—Vihera, Marina... and Elena —admired Rei, the option of antagonizing him was simply off the table.

Naturally, Marina among those three would have used her authority as Guildmaster and her personal combat skills to aid Rei if he became a target for elimination.

(If that happened, perhaps we should just bring him to our settlement... Oh, that might actually be quite good. The settlement folks also have a favorable impression of Rei because of the World Tree incident.)

If she did something like that, it probably wouldn't just be their own affair.

Vihera might well intervene, potentially sparking another war between the Kingdom of Mireana and the Bestir Empire.

"...So, getting back to the goblin leader matter, is it safe to assume it was a rare species?"

"No, unfortunately, it wasn't a rare species. It was just like the ones in the monster encyclopedia. And when I showed Lenora the corpse, she didn't say anything special either."

Marina murmured drearily, her expression matching her somewhat subdued mood.

"Why are you so down? It was just a goblin leader, after all. I don't think there's any need to worry that much."

"...Yeah. I guess I hope it really was just that."

Rei and Vihera felt puzzled by Marina's vague, evasive response.

From Rei's perspective, having fought it, the goblin leader was nothing special.

He'd actually launched a Flying Slash right when they met and finished it off, so he hadn't really gotten a chance to gauge its intelligence.

"Anyway, Rei, you don't plan on leaving Gilm for a while, right?"

"...Oh my, how enviable. If possible, I'd like to join that conversation too."

"You have research to do, don't you?"

For some reason, Vihera agreed with Marina, making it hard for Rei to complain further.

"Fine. I'll be Marina's toy and behave myself. But you better take this investigation seriously, okay? This is related to the current situation, right?"
