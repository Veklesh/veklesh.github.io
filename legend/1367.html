"Please, Runo! Lend me your strength!"

Slesha bowed deeply, but Runo, seeing this, set the ale cup he held on the table and spoke.

"Look, I helped you out mostly by chance. So why do you think I'd cooperate now? Honestly, if you're going to ask me for help, you'd be better off asking another... party with more people."

This was one of the taverns in Gilm.

Being nighttime, it was packed with customers.

Amidst the bustle, Runo was drinking ale and eating dinner to unwind from today's work... but such a leisurely mood was ruined by having someone bow before him like this.

"Well... um... the reward is a bit... generous..."

Slesha's party was originally ranked D.

They were treated as full-fledged adventurers, but that didn't mean they were financially well-off.

Moreover, the money earned from selling a comrade's belongings wouldn't fetch much if they belonged to a Rank D adventurer.

Using that money to hire a party... especially for a request to explore Treant Forest, a place devoid of monsters or animals and utterly unprofitable, was simply unrealistic.

Of course, there were parties that could be hired for that amount.

But such parties usually had a reason to accept low pay... essentially, they were likely to have some kind of problem.

That was precisely why Slesha had set her sights on Runo, who had a connection to her and was operating solo—specifically, within Gilm.

In truth, Slesha's aim wasn't wrong at all.

In fact, one could even say she had good judgment in choosing Runo.

...However, hiring a high-ranking adventurer naturally required commensurate compensation.

"Right? Then that's the end of it. I'm enjoying my night out, so don't do anything to make my drink taste bad."

He lifted the stir-fried dish from the plate to his mouth—sweet and savory... seasoned richly to pair perfectly with ale.

Then he raised his glass to his lips, savoring the dish alongside the ale.

"Phew! ...Yeah, delicious. Now, you should probably get back to what you need to do. Staying here won't make your wish come true."

"...Well, is it absolutely impossible?"

"Yeah. I feel sorry for you too. I do... but I'm an adventurer. There's no way I can handle this for such a cheap fee, right?"

"Well... about that..."

At Runo's words, Slesha, sitting across from him, hesitated... then took a small, deep breath before speaking.

"If it's that difficult... instead of payment, you may do with me as you please. So please... lend me your strength."

Runo stopped mid-sip of his ale, clearly stunned that such words had come from Slesha's mouth.

He turned his gaze back to Slesha.

Her features could be described as rather well-proportioned.

He conceded that much.

But... to Runo, in his mid-twenties, Slesha still seemed very young.

Though Slesha was just entering her late teens, she was old enough to be seen as a woman.

In fact, he'd been approached countless times before.

Considering that, Slesha couldn't believe she lacked charm. ...She didn't want to believe it.

In this case, the unfortunate part was simply that Runo only saw women as women... more accurately, as objects of romance or desire, once he was in his twenties and beyond.

Furthermore, while Slesha had a pretty face, her figure was, at best, slender... or, to put it bluntly, flat-chested.

For that very reason, Runo simply couldn't see Slesha in that light.

"Take better care of yourself. If you keep doing things like this, you'll regret it when you finally find someone you like."

Runo told her this, but if Slesha had been a beautiful woman in her twenties, he would have reacted differently.

...Unaware of this, Slesha looked at Runo with a gaze of respect—or so it seemed—for worrying about her.

But that look of respect vanished almost immediately.

Slesha wanted to resolve the matter of Treant Forest herself.

Since Runo was practically her only hope for figuring out how to do that, no amount of gratitude could make her back down now.

"I'll do anything I can. So..."

"Even if you say that..."

Watching Slesha's expression, Runo took another sip of ale, partly to change the mood.

What to do... While pondering this, he suddenly recalled a rumor circulating at the guild earlier that evening.

(They said they'd be recruiting adventurers to guard the large-scale logging operation in Treant Forest. If I joined that... Ah, rank restrictions.)

The Treant Forest job comes with plenty of dangers.

As far as anyone knew, two parties—including Slesha's—had already been wiped out.

And that's just the known casualties. Setting Slesha's group aside, the other party... the Third Eye group... was only discovered because Rei was flying overhead on Set.

And the reason he saw them was purely by chance—light reflecting off their luggage through gaps in the trees. If luggage were hidden in densely packed trees, it would be literally concealed, making it hard even for Set to spot.

(Still, logging... Well, since Treant Forest expands daily, logging probably helps contain it. Plus, the pay is decent for the labor.)

The hardest part of a woodcutter's job is, of course, hauling the felled trees.

But for the job starting tomorrow, Rei, who has an item box, will handle that transporting task. So there's no need to worry about the hardest part.

In fact, the adventurer escort will earn more rewards for each tree they fell.

...Though, of course, the primary condition is that they don't neglect their escort duties.

That's precisely why this job has a rank restriction... and Slesha hadn't reached that rank yet.

(But what on earth are they going to do with all that wood? Sell it as lumber? The Treant Forest is pretty peculiar in many ways, so maybe that wood is special too?)

He probably never imagined Gilm's expansion was being considered.

Then again, Runo's lack of carpentry knowledge worked in their favor here.

Normally, felled trees need to be dried for a certain amount of time.

Depending on the wood, it could take as long as half a year.

...But, though only a few know it yet, the trees of Treant Forest were a different story.

Originally possessing trace amounts of... yet unique magical power, they didn't require natural drying when treated with a specific liquid used in alchemy.

"Um, Runo?"

"...Ah, right..."

Runo hesitated, unsure how to respond to Slesha staring at him... but considering Slesha had lost her entire party, abandoning her now just didn't sit right with him.

If it was a request for logging escort, a certain amount of reward was promised.

Of course, there were plenty of other lucrative requests out there.

Especially since Runo possessed the magic-seeing magic eye, and being a solo adventurer, it wasn't uncommon for other parties to ask him for backup.

The pay for those jobs would undoubtedly be significantly higher than guarding a logging operation.

Even so, Runo simply couldn't bring himself to completely abandon Slesha, who was right in front of him.

(I'm too soft-hearted.)

If she truly only cared about herself, she wouldn't have bothered helping Slesha when they first met. She could have just left her on the roadside.

But the fact that he couldn't do that made Runo realize he was soft.

He took another sip from the ale-filled cup... drained it, and set it back on the table.

"I understand."

A short reply.

But upon hearing those words, Slesha's face lit up.

"But let me make this clear: the only concession I can make is to temporarily form a party with you to escort the lumberjacks cutting down trees in Treant Forest. From what I've heard, that kind of request is supposed to come in starting tomorrow."

Slesha's face lit up at Runo's words.

But seeing Slesha's reaction, Runo spoke again, trying to calm her down.

"But this request has a rank restriction. ...Well, it's only natural for a place as eerie as Treant Forest. So, I'll form a party with you temporarily. ...But since I'm taking this request at your request, I'll get the bigger share."

This was Runo's greatest concession as an adventurer.

And Slesha, as if it were only natural, nodded in response to Runo's words.

"Understood! That's fine. My reward can be minimal, so please, I'd really appreciate it."

"...Well, I wasn't planning on fleecing you that badly."

Runo declared in exasperation to Slesha, who seemed to think that the bare minimum lodging and just enough food to keep her body functioning was sufficient.

Purely speaking in terms of adventurer status, even if they allocated things exactly as Slesha suggested, no one would complain.

But if others found out he was treating a woman like Slesha—no, in Runo's view, a girl was the appropriate term—like that, it would be a major blow to Runo's solo reputation.

Runo operated solo and was often called upon as a helper by other parties not only because of his skill, but also because of his character.

Whether it's a subjugation request, an escort request, or a gathering request... When hiring someone outside one's party or close acquaintances for a dangerous job, their character becomes a major concern.

It's only natural. If someone acted selfishly in a critical moment, or worse, betrayed the group, that would truly be the worst possible outcome.

That's precisely why Runo was careful to maintain his reputation.

That's precisely why rumors that she was unilaterally exploiting Slesha would be problematic in more ways than one.

"This request will be accepted as a party unit. Otherwise, Slesha would be restricted by rank limits. Is that acceptable?"

"...Yes, I understand."

For Slesha, the word "party" evoked memories of the party she had been part of before.

That's precisely why she was slightly taken aback when suddenly told to form a party with Runo. Still, considering their current situation, it was undeniably the only way to investigate Treant Forest.

After a moment's hesitation, she eventually agreed to the party arrangement.

Of course, it goes without saying that this party arrangement wasn't something Runo had sought out either.

After all, operating solo was one of Runo's selling points.

Especially in this remote part of Gilm, being a solo adventurer was a major selling point.

If Slesha had refused the party here, she would have been abandoned immediately.

"The reward... well, how about I take seventy percent and you take thirty?"

"Huh? Is it really okay for me to take that much?"

"Yeah. I can't exactly give you a reward so low you couldn't live on it... Got it? Then get back and prepare for tomorrow. And let me warn you—don't slack off on your guard duties just because you want to scout Treant Forest."

"Got it! I'll get ready right away. Excuse me."

"Wait, hold on. Were you even listening? The request is tomorrow. So why are you getting all fired up now... and coming here serves no purpose either."

Slesha bowed her head slightly to Runo, who looked exasperated.

"Ah, um... Sorry about that."

"Good. Anyway, meet me at the guild tomorrow morning, around six o'clock when the bell rings, okay?"

"Yes, of course."

"Alright, then go home and sleep. It's bedtime for kids."

"...Yes."

Slesha had mixed feelings about being treated like a child at the end.

She had always believed she was a woman in her own right.

But she couldn't risk saying anything here that might upset Runo. Without another word, she left.

Watching her go, Runo finally felt he could relax... and called out to the waiter to order another ale.
