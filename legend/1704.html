Although they caused a bit of a commotion at the Green Brook Inn—which was only to be expected, given the presence of Set, Elena, and other adventurers with nicknames—they managed to secure a room. Since it was still barely past noon, Rei and the others headed straight for the guild.

That said, they didn’t forget to grab a bite to eat at a restaurant they happened to find on the way to the guild.

As you’d expect from a place on the main street, the food was perfectly fine.

…That said, it wasn’t exactly delicious enough to make you think, “This is tasty!”—it was just decent.

Rei couldn’t help but wonder if it was a bit lacking for a place on the main street, but seeing that the restaurant was reasonably busy, he decided there was no need for him to say anything for the time being.

He did think he’d look for a better restaurant next time, though.

After all, since they’d gone to the trouble of coming all the way out here, far from Gilm, it was only natural to want to try some of Golz’s famous local dishes.

In any case, after finishing lunch and arriving at the guild, what Rei and the others saw was...

“There are more people here than I expected.”

Rei’s muttered remark, upon seeing the scene, said it all.

If this were Gilm, there would be almost no adventurers inside the guild around noon.

There might be plenty of people having lunch at the attached tavern, but it was usually the case that there weren’t many people around the area with the quest board.

But upon entering the guild and looking around, a considerable number of people who appeared to be adventurers had gathered there.

“Something must have happened.”

Relyu had looked somewhat displeased after parting ways with Set at the guild entrance, but upon seeing the scene inside, he seemed to have had a change of heart and muttered those words.

It might be a stretch to call it “fortunate,” but with so many adventurers gathered inside the guild, almost no one seemed to notice Rei and the others as they entered.

“Hardly anyone” means that a few people did notice, but even those who did didn’t seem inclined to say anything.

“Hey, did something happen?”

Probably to gather information. Relieu asked an adventurer standing nearby.

The adventurer looked slightly surprised and spoke up.

“What? You came here without knowing? I don’t know if that’s good luck or bad luck.”

“I just got here a little while ago, aiming for the dungeon.”

"Ah... I see. Well, I guess it’s no wonder you didn’t know. Actually, about that dungeon—apparently, dozens of bird monsters have been appearing there. Oh, do you know that this town’s dungeon is located on the side of a cliff?"

“I know. Thanks to that… though I’m not sure if that’s the right way to put it, the monsters just fall to their deaths on their own, right?”

“That’s right. But I never expected bird monsters to show up. That’s way beyond unexpected.”

The adventurer talking to Relieu wore a look of exasperation, as if to say this was the worst possible scenario, but Relieu... and Rei and the others, who had been listening nearby, also looked slightly dumbfounded.

Since it’s assumed that the dungeon is on the cliff face, it wouldn’t be strange for any kind of monster to come out of there.

Up until now, it had probably just been monsters like goblins, kobolds, and orcs, but if that really is a dungeon, then it wouldn’t be strange at all for a bird monster to appear.

(This is the countryside, and apparently there haven’t been any dungeons around here before, so I guess it makes sense.)

Thinking that, Relieu continued the conversation.

“So, does that mean all these adventurers gathered here right now are here to defeat that bird monster?”

“That’s right. It’s an emergency request from the guild… or, to be precise, from the magistrate.”

“I see. …But looking at this group, there don’t seem to be many mages or archers. Are we really going to be okay? Our opponent is a flying monster, right?”

Attacking a flying monster is no easy task.

Magic would be ideal, but mages are extremely rare; in a rural area like this, you’d be lucky to find even one or two.

That said, as Relieu mentioned, most of the people gathered here are equipped for close combat—with long swords, spears, clubs, battle axes, and the like.

Given that, I didn’t think they’d be able to mount an effective attack against a flying bird-like monster.

If the enemy were a monster that moved on the ground, gathering this many people would likely be enough to handle it.

But with dozens of flying monsters appearing, what were they supposed to do with just this many warriors? That was the question on Relyu’s mind… and on Rei and the others’ as well.

In response to Relyu’s words, the man she addressed wore a bitter expression as he spoke.

“I get what you’re saying, but we don’t have time to call in adventurers from other villages or towns, so what can we do? We don’t even know if this town’s barrier can stop them, and it’d be a real hassle if they managed to get to the nearby villages.”

“Is that… the reason? Well, what should we do?”

Only then did Relieu turn her gaze toward Rei and ask; the man who had been speaking with Relieu until then also turned his gaze toward Rei, Elena, and the others… and stopped moving.

Though, to be clear, they didn’t stop because of Rei—they stopped because they saw Elena and the others standing beside him.

“Bird monsters are interesting. Originally, I was planning to gather information today and head into the dungeon tomorrow.”

If they’d arrived in Golz a little earlier, they probably would have gone down into the dungeon for a bit today just to check out what it was like.

But due to the commotion with Vihera and Relu this morning, it was already past noon.

In that case, if he defeated the monster said to be plaguing Golz here and showed off Rei and the others’ strength, they might not get hassled by strange opponents in the future… or, even if it didn’t go that far, perhaps the number of such incidents would decrease.

That’s what Rei thought.

Also, while he doesn’t know what kind of monster the bird-like creature that emerged from what appears to be the dungeon is, if it’s an unknown monster, it would be the perfect prey for Rei and Set.

...Despite all his talk, that last reason was probably the biggest factor, but Rei nodded back at Relieu.

“You’re right. Since we’re going to be relying on Golz from now on, it can’t hurt to get in his good graces.”

“Hey, what on earth are you talking about?”

The man talking to Relieu is also an adventurer.

If Set were here, he would have recognized Rei as Rei… as Crimson.

But Set wasn’t here now, and all they could see was a petite figure wearing a hood and carrying no weapons—someone who appeared to be a mage.

Given that, it wouldn’t be strange for him to hear Rei’s words and try to stop her.

However, paying no heed to the man’s reaction, Rei speaks up.

“The Rank B party ‘Crimson Wings’ accepts the request to defeat the bird monster.”

The voice that came from Rei’s mouth strangely echoed throughout the guild.

This happened amidst the buzz of many adventurers exchanging information with their companions.

The guild fell silent... but the next moment, laughter rang out from those around them.

“Hah, a Rank B adventurer? A kid… no, a girl? I get that you admire adventurers, but sorry, I don’t have time to mess around with this right now. If this keeps up, Golz will…”

“What’s the big deal? Let her give it a try.”

“Hey! What are you suddenly talking about?”

“It’ll be fine. The bird monster’s already left the dungeon, right? So…”

“Crimson!?”

Amid the adventurers’ conversation, a sudden cry of shock rang out around them.

The war against the Bestir Empire that took place a few years ago.

Naturally, some of the adventurers who participated in that war were members of Golz.

And having participated in that war meant they had, naturally, witnessed the scene of Rei creating a fire tornado—a whirlwind of flames.

In particular, Golz is a town located within the territory ruled by a member of the Neutral Faction, and the man who just shouted upon seeing Rei had personally witnessed, right up close, just how devastating the fire whirlwind Rei had created truly was.

That is likely why, even in Set’s absence and with his face completely hidden by the hood of his Dragon Robe, he was able to recognize Rei as Rei.

“No way! Crimson? Crimson… you mean…?”

“No, but I’ve heard that too. They say Crimson looks like just any old apprentice mage at first glance or something like that.”

“Don’t mess with me! You’re telling me that’s an adventurer with a nickname? That’s impossible! Cut the crap already!”

Some looked at Rei with suspicion, others seemed convinced, and then… the man who had initially thought Rei was just a lost child when she first walked in spoke up in denial.

There’s no way the guild staff didn’t notice the adventurers’ reactions.

…Though part of it was that they’d heard Rei’s declaration earlier.

Still, at first, hearing that unfamiliar voice, they’d assumed it was just a newbie adventurer trying to show off… that’s what they’d thought.

The fact that they’d never heard the party name at the Golz Guild—nor anywhere in the surrounding area—was likely a major factor.

Even though they knew of the adventurer nicknamed “Crimson,” they hadn’t noticed any information about the party Crimson had formed.

In reality, Walker had told us that Rei and the others were heading out to clear a dungeon, and the information had been passed on clearly.

If this had been a normal time—if they’d arrived just a few hours ago—the guild would have recognized Rei as Rei without a doubt.

But right now, due to the appearance of the bird monsters, the guild was in a state of near-chaos.

And this was the result.

“I’ve heard that everyone from the Crimson Wings is here, and that Ms. Relieu of the Gale is here as well!”

At the voice coming from the guild counter, the other adventurers turned their gaze toward Rei once again.

The fact that those gazes were also directed at Relue, who stood beside Rei, was likely because they realized there was another adventurer with a nickname present.

In particular, the man who had been talking to Relyu just moments ago seemed utterly stunned, as if he hadn’t realized the person he’d been speaking to was an adventurer with a nickname.

Feeling those eyes on her, Relieu gave the man a light tap on the shoulder.

“Thanks for all the info, Rei!”

Rei immediately understood what Relu meant.

So, following Relieu, Rei—and Elena and the others as well—all headed over to the counter.

“Now then, I mentioned that Rei would be taking on this job, but I’ll be tagging along with Rei and the others on a temporary basis this time. We’ve got two adventurers with nicknames, after all. I think that should be more than enough to handle this emergency request, don’t you?”

“Yes. It’s a great help having you and the others here, Relieu-san. But… um… I’d like to ask just to be sure—the enemy this time is a bird-like monster that flies. As for its attack methods…”

“Are you sure you’ll be okay?” the receptionist, who looked to be in her mid-twenties, asked with concern.

It wasn’t that she doubted the skills of the “Nicknamed Ones” like Relyu and Rei; it’s just that there aren’t many ways to attack flying enemies to begin with.

Bows, magic… or perhaps slingshots, depending on the situation.

But in response to the receptionist’s words, Relieu nodded, indicating it wasn’t a problem.

“First off, you know that Rei has a familiar named Set, right? It’s pretty well-known.”

“Yes. Reports of his various exploits have reached our guild as well.”

The receptionist flashes a delighted smile and turns her gaze toward Rei.

That smile had the power to captivate many of the adventurers in the Golz Guild. …However, it didn’t have quite the same effect on Rei, who was with Elena and the others; he simply gave a small nod in response.

“There are plenty of others who use bows and magic, so we have plenty of ways to attack even flying enemies. I can’t use them as often as they do, but I can manage to some extent.”

“Huh?” Rei, who had been listening to the receptionist and Relieu’s conversation… no, Elena and the others, too, were puzzled.

As far as Rei and the others knew, Relieu’s only method of attack was with a longsword.

At least, during the mock battle between Vihera and Relieu, they hadn’t seen any signs of long-range attacks.

However, perhaps dissatisfied with Rei and the others’ reaction, Relieu, who had paused her conversation with the receptionist, shot Rei a displeased look.

“What’s the matter? Did you think I was helpless against aerial enemies?”

“Uh… that’s not exactly what I meant. You do have a nickname, after all.”

Although he still seemed somewhat dissatisfied with Rei’s words, in any case, Rei and his group ended up accepting this emergency request.
