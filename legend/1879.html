The dense magical energy emitted by Rei has been condensed to the absolute limit, allowing even those who normally cannot see magical energy to perceive it.

This semi-materialized magical power is then dyed red by the flames that could be described as Rei’s signature attribute.

Red magical power swirls around Rei.

It was a skill that could be called Rei’s trump card… the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

“Guh, ga…”

Upon seeing the activated Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, Deotres—likely unconsciously—let out a groan and took a few steps back.

Even after gaining a powerful physique and regenerative abilities strong enough to heal even if his torso were severed in two… even so, Deotres must have realized that Rei standing before him was an overwhelming presence, and that he truly had no chance of winning.

Naturally, Deotres wasn’t the only one who felt threatened by Rei, who had activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

No, if anything, it was Celestine—the very instigator of this incident—who, having retained a modicum of rational thought, likely felt the greatest threat from Rei, who had activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

……Though, given that he had abducted a noble’s child—even if not the heir—and was attempting to use that blood to revive something sealed away, it would be a stretch to call his thought process “sound.”

Mirales, too, had transformed into a monstrous form, but upon seeing Rei shrouded in red magic, she froze in her tracks.

In that situation, the first to act was Elena, who had known Rei the longest.

Naturally, Elena knew about the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, and given Rei’s current situation, she could easily imagine how he would attack with his limited means.

That’s precisely why Elena didn’t miss the opening created when Mirales froze. She kicked off the ground, activated the Sleipnir Boots to launch herself into the air, and swung Mirage—now transformed into a longsword—down toward Mirales’s head.

Mirales, who had been split cleanly in half vertically just moments before, was now split cleanly in half horizontally from the head down.

However, even though it would normally be a fatal blow, Elena didn’t stop moving. The moment she landed on the ground, she swiftly swung Mirage again, striking Mirales’ torso from the side several times, each blow landing in a different spot.

The Mirage, swung not just once, but twice, three times, four times, and repeatedly, blew off Mirales’s arms and neck, slicing the torso into several pieces as it fell to the ground.

Despite this, the pieces of Mirales’ body that had fallen to the ground began to foam as they tried to rejoin one another the very next moment…

「Rei！」

The moment Elena saw this, she let out a sharp cry.

Rei, of course, had noticed Elena’s movement and reacted immediately.

One of the most notable abilities of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour: Deep Flame.

It is a skill that allows Rei to project a portion of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour surrounding him, transforming it into flames with the exact properties he envisions.

Without so much as a flick of his wrist, Rei unleashed Deep Flame. It struck a piece of Mirales’ flesh—if one could even call it that—near Elena, instantly turning into searing flames that reduced the entire fragment to charcoal.

It had been only a few seconds since the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour activated.

In those few seconds, every fragment of Mirales’ flesh had vanished… no, been completely incinerated.

“Wh—”

Even for Celestine, who was aware of Rei and Elena’s existence, this turn of events must have been completely unexpected.

To begin with, while Rei is known for Set, the Great Scythe, and fire magic—and more recently, his dual-spear style has also gained fame—few people are aware of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Well, there are actually quite a few who have seen it in person, but compared to other rumors, fewer people are aware of it, so it’s accurate to say that, in the end, few knew about it.

That is precisely why Celestine didn’t know that Rei had the “Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour” as a secret weapon, even without the Death Scythe.

“Next!”

Showing no further interest in the enemy reduced to charcoal, Rei kicked off the ground.

The moment Deotres sensed a faint heat, Rei’s fist had already pierced through his torso.

Despite Deotres’s formidable muscles, he was still unable to block Rei’s strike, which had been enhanced by the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

Considering that when Rei first struck him, his ribs were broken but his skin and flesh weren’t pierced, this clearly demonstrates just how vicious Rei’s current strike was.

And, naturally, Rei had no intention of ending his attack just by piercing the torso.

He activates Deep Flame within Deotres’ torso, generating intense flames.

Flames burst forth from the eyes, nose, mouth, and ears of the monstrously transformed Deotres... and even from the part of his torso where Rei had pierced him.

Being burned from the inside must have been a shocking experience, even for Deotres, whose sense of pain was currently numb.

As Rei pulled his arm out of the torso and backed away, Deotres spent several dozen seconds moving forward a few steps, then retreating, or sometimes stepping sideways, as if confused… Eventually, he collapsed to the ground with a thunderous crash, and as if that were the signal, the flames inside him burned even more fiercely, engulfing Deotres’ entire body in fire.

Deotres’s body, lying on the ground, burned rapidly and turned to charcoal in a matter of seconds.

Rei, who knew of Mirales and Deotres’ regenerative abilities, did not immediately look away even as he watched Deotres turn to charcoal.

Perhaps he could revive even from this state.

Holding onto that thought, he kept his watchful gaze fixed... and without even glancing at the flesh tentacles flying toward him, he used Deep Flame to burn them to ashes.

From Celestine’s perspective, this seemed like the only and final chance to defeat Rei, whose entire body was now enveloped in red magic.

Even though she had joined forces with Elena, she had easily defeated the two who had been strengthened by a sealed entity whose name she didn’t even know.

Facing such an extraordinary being, and with that being currently focused on Deotres’s corpse, this was the perfect opportunity.

……In reality, even if she managed to defeat Rei here, Elena was still there. However, having seen the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, Celestine had completely forgotten about Elena, who was not making a move.

That said, from Elena’s perspective, she had judged that their victory was all but assured the moment Rei activated his trump card, the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, which was the main reason she remained still.

If Rei hadn’t used the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, Elena would have had to resort to her own trump card—Dragon Language Magic—but… since Rei had played his trump card first, she was fortunate to have avoided having to use hers.

Every single tentacle strike launched in an attempt to exploit Rei’s opening was completely incinerated by the Deep Flame.

On one occasion, the flames even spread from the scorched flesh of the tentacles all the way to the vicinity of the flesh cocoon. As a result, Celestan—or perhaps the flesh cocoon itself—realized the danger and ceased further attacks.

Several dozen seconds passed... With no sign of the charred Deotres reviving, Rei, having determined he was completely dead, turned his gaze toward Celestan while keeping the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour active.

“Huh!?”

It wasn’t as if Rei had said anything in particular.

But the moment their gazes met, what Celestane felt was overwhelming terror.

It was an intense fear—so intense that he could physically feel the pressure bearing down on him, making it a struggle just to remain standing.

An ordinary person would have been unable to even stand and would have dropped to their knees.

That he was still standing there, despite his legs trembling in such a situation… well, that was to be expected of the man who had led the most powerful organization in Anesis’s underworld.

However, even if Celestine tried to confront Rei, the disparity in strength was overwhelming.

It was like an elephant versus an ant… no, the gap was even wider than that.

Had the seal on the flesh cocoon been broken, things might have been different... but the time Mirales and Deotres were supposed to buy for him vanished completely the moment Rei wielded the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

“Well then,”

Muttering to himself, Rei took a step toward Celestine—or, more accurately, toward the cocoon of flesh.

Thanks to the use of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, the surrounding temperature had risen slightly.

Considering how scorching it used to be, his skill in wielding the ability must have improved considerably.

In any case, Rei closed the distance without giving Celestine any hint of his thoughts.

“Damn it, don’t come! Don’t you dare come!”

It must be because he’s cornered. The words coming from Celestain’s mouth no longer carry the sense of composure he had just moments ago.

Even in the face of such a Celestine, Rei shows no sign of changing his expression.

“What’s wrong? You look completely taken by surprise. …Don’t you have any other tricks up your sleeve? Well, I can’t spend all day on this either, so I’m going to put an end to this now.”

Even as he said this, Rei wasn’t exactly at ease either.

Well, in terms of whether he can win this battle, one could say he’s more than confident.

But the problem in this case is what happens after he wins the battle.

It must be due to the special ability of the flesh cocoon, but the effect of Rei’s Misty Ring is currently sealed.

For that reason, he was convinced that destroying that flesh cocoon would lift the seal… but that was merely a conviction within Rei; whether it would actually be lifted remained unknown.

Even though Rei believed it would likely be fine, he still couldn’t bring himself to truly relax.

Given the situation, there was no time to waste here, and it was only natural to think that he should defeat the flesh cocoon right in front of him in one fell swoop.

“I’ll give you one piece of advice. …Step back. If you stay there, you’ll get caught in my attack.”

At Rei’s words, Celestan briefly tried to pull his hand away from the flesh cocoon… but then, a part of the cocoon caught his eye. …The figure of Gaiska, embedded within the flesh cocoon.

Few would be able to recognize that as Gaiska at this point. Yet, there was no doubt that the figure was indeed Gaiska… and above all, considering Rei and Elena’s situation…

Coming to that conclusion, Celestan spoke up.

“Let’s stop this here, Crimson Rei. Gaiska is embedded within this seal you’re trying to destroy. You surely know what would happen if you destroyed this seal under such circumstances… don’t you?”

“That’s right. I’ll probably die.”

Rei states this matter-of-factly.

Even as he says this... Rei shows no sign of stopping his advance.

Even to Rei’s eyes, it was already clear that there was absolutely no way to save Gaiska.

And even if she could be saved, given that this is the very person who hired the Black Wolf to assassinate Rei, Rei had no intention of simply letting her off the hook.

In fact, he even thinks it would be better for both of them if he just incinerated him along with the seal in the Deep Flames.

Seeing that Rei shows no sign of stopping, Celestan is at a loss as to what to do.

As Rei draws closer step by step, it feels as though he can almost sense the heat of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

...In reality, due to Rei’s control, some heat does escape, but it’s not enough to make him break out in a sweat with every step Rei takes.

The fact that he feels that way is likely because Celestain is so frantic.

“Well, I’ve given you my warning… I won’t say anything more. If you don’t move from there, then you can just die right here along with that seal.”

“Wha—”

Wait.

Celestane tried to say that, but Rei showed no sign of listening to such words. He manipulated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour—which had been condensed to the absolute limit of his magical power and had already become a near-physical entity—and attempted to hurl the magical power currently surrounding him at the flesh cocoon…

Crack.

A faint… yet unmistakable sound of something about to shatter could be heard all around.

Hearing that sound, Celestane immediately turned his gaze toward the cocoon of flesh.

It was the moment Celestan had been waiting for… the moment when the time he had bought—even at the cost of sacrificing his trusted subordinate, Mirales—had finally paid off.

“This is it!”

He could win.

Just as he was about to say that, the Deep Flame fired by Rei engulfed the flesh cocoon in a roaring inferno that consumed most of the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and unleashed immense heat upon the surroundings.
