"Why don't you hire me?"

The villagers looked at Rei with a mix of skepticism and doubt—or rather, with eyes that were two parts belief and eight parts doubt.

To the villagers, even though Rei held a Guild Card as a Rank B adventurer, they had absolutely no idea just how impressive that actually was.

If there were a guild branch office here with guild staff, they would have judged him to be more than sufficient as a combatant—no, perhaps even an overkill—simply for being a Rank B adventurer with a nickname. But unfortunately, there was no guild branch office in this village.

That was precisely why, even when someone like Rei asked if they would hire him, they couldn’t make a decision right away.

Watching the villagers like that, Rei thought about how to convince them of his abilities… and eventually, he came up with the simplest solution.

“First off, let’s see. I’ll start by introducing my partner.”

“...Partner?”

The man who had spoken to Rei first was the one who replied to her words.

Nodding to the man, Rei looked back in the direction he had come from, took a deep breath… and shouted.

“Setoooooooooo!”

Startled by the sudden shout nearby, the man reflexively covered his ears with his hands.

The villagers standing at a distance did the same.

About ten seconds after Rei’s scream, the man removed his hands from his ears and tried to yell back at Rei…

“Grrrrrrrrr!”

Instantly, hearing that growl coming from the sky, he froze.

There was something about that roar—a strange force that compelled everyone to pay attention.

It wasn’t just the man who had been about to yell at Rei; the other villagers were the same.

Everyone turned their gaze toward the source of the sound... and eventually, even with the villagers’ eyesight, they noticed something flying through the sky and approaching them.

And once they noticed it, it wasn’t hard to figure out what it was.

But precisely because they understood, they were now frozen in place.

Even for an adventurer, encountering a Rank A monster is something that happens once in a lifetime, if at all.

Naturally, it was unthinkable that someone living in a village like this would ever encounter a high-rank monster like a griffin.

...And the fact that Set is actually an S-rank monster capable of wielding various skills is completely beyond their wildest imagination.

Though the villagers were staring at Set in stunned silence, he eventually flapped his wings and began descending toward the ground.

And when he touched down… the villagers, jolted back to their senses by the impact and the air from his flapping wings, were about to scream at the sight of Set thrusting his sharp-beaked face toward Rei…

“Grrr…”

“Good boy, good boy. Thanks for coming.”

Just as Set brought his beak close to Rei, he let out a happy purr, and seeing Rei happily stroke Set’s face as he approached, the villagers looked utterly baffled.

In fact, those watching were so taken aback by the unexpected turn of events that they had completely frozen in place.

Rei, who had been stroking Set, turned his gaze toward the villagers, then drew the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring.

Two-Spear Style… Lately, the Two-Spear Style has become synonymous with Rei, alongside Set’s presence and the Death Scythe.

(Well, even if I say "Two-Spear Style" here, it probably doesn't mean anything since they don't seem to know who I am... Still, the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear are incredibly imposing just to look at.)

The giant scythe and the Crimson spear possess such overwhelming presence that they would catch the eye of even someone who has never been involved in any kind of conflict.

Given that he wields such weapons, it should be easy to tell that Rei is not what he appears to be.

In fact, the villagers were staring at Rei—who stood with Set by her side, holding a large scythe in one hand and a spear in the other—with looks of disbelief and astonishment.

Facing the villagers, Rei gave them a confident look... and then spoke.

“Well, I guess you all understand now that I’m a skilled adventurer, right?”

“Ah, yeah. Rei, was it? You aside… just having a griffin with you means there’s no doubt you’re a formidable force.”

Even though he’d gone so far as to pull out the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear for a show, the man’s reaction was directed solely at the griffin.

Rei felt slightly dissatisfied with the man’s attitude, but he concluded that it was only natural for someone living in a place like this to be unaware of his background.

To begin with, Rei had come to this village simply because he wanted to get his hands on fishing nets and small fish—small, at least compared to tuna.

Since he’d merely happened to learn that pirates were attacking the village, he decided that if the man would just acknowledge Set’s strength for now, that would be enough to deal with the pirates.

“Anyway, that’s why I have a partner named Set. With this, you won’t have any complaints if we defeat the pirates, right?”

“Well, that’s… I guess… so?”

The man tilted his head, and eventually decided there was no problem. After a moment’s hesitation, he turned his gaze toward the villagers… and among them, toward an old woman.

She was leaning on a cane, in her sixties… or perhaps her seventies.

Even so, her eyes held a sharp, intelligent gleam as she looked at Rei.

“Young man, will you really… help us… help this village?”

“Hmm… well, yes. I came here to buy fishing gear and fish. If the village is being attacked by pirates, I can’t shop here with peace of mind, can I? Besides…”

Rei paused there, and the old woman fixed him with a piercing gaze, as if trying to see through his words, urging him to continue.

“And? What is it?”

“Since there doesn’t seem to be an Adventurers’ Guild or a branch office here, you might not know this, but when an adventurer defeats bandits or the like, all the property those bandits were carrying becomes the property of the adventurer who defeated them. Of course, if there’s someone who had it stolen from them, we’ll give priority to buying it back from them.”

“So, you’re after the pirates’ treasure?”

“That’s part of it, but that’s all. To be precise, besides the treasure the pirates have, I also want their ship, and selling the pirates themselves as slaves would bring in a decent sum. …Well, to sell them as slaves, I’d have to get to a town or city of a certain size, though.”

For now, Rei concludes that selling them as slaves in this village would be impossible.

Given how small this village is, there’s no way a slave trader would be here.

(In that case, even if I capture the pirates alive… I’d have to go to a town or city where there’s a slave trader. And to transport them, I’d obviously need a ship… Honestly, that’s quite a hassle. No, actually, wouldn’t it be quicker if I just went to the city myself and brought them back in a Set Cage or something?)

It was certain that things would go much more smoothly that way than if I were to lead the pirates along with me.

A slave trader would undoubtedly have slave collars on hand, and once those collars were fastened around their necks, the pirates wouldn’t be able to pull any funny business.

“I see. …So, you’re saying you’re not someone who poses a threat to this village?”

“Yes. In fact, I’ll exterminate the pirates for free, and if you’re willing to sell them to me, I’ll buy fishing gear, and I’ll buy every single piece of seafood you have. I think it’s fair to say I’ll bring a great deal of benefit to this village…”

“I’ll take your word for it.”

“Village Chief!?”

The moment the village chief said he believed Rei’s words, the man who had first spoken to Rei… and the villagers standing nearby all let out cries of astonishment.

This is a small village with fewer than fifty residents, but precisely because of that, the capabilities of the village chief who runs it are directly reflected in the community.

To put it bluntly, it’s a one-man show.

In a village like this, the village chief has been fulfilling that role for nearly several decades.

Even in a small village like this, over the course of decades, various troubles inevitably arise.

In fact, they’ve experienced several incidents similar to this one, where pirates demanded food and other supplies.

The fact that the village has managed to overcome such disturbances is entirely due to the village chief’s leadership.

For that reason alone, the elderly woman serving as village chief was respected and beloved by everyone in the village.

But even for a village chief like her, to suddenly say something like this was completely unexpected.

“Do you not trust my word? This man is by no means a malicious person.”

“Even so…”

Rei, having been called “not a malicious person,” felt the villagers’ gazes and stroked Set as if to hide his embarrassment.

Of course, Rei didn’t think of himself as a malicious person.

Still, when it was put so bluntly, it was impossible not to feel embarrassed.

“Anyway, for now. You’re leaving the pirate business to me, right?”

Seeing Rei look a little flustered, the villagers must have sensed something. The tension that had been hanging in the air vanished, and a somewhat peaceful atmosphere filled the surroundings.

“Hmm. I’ll leave it to you. …You should ask Pastra over there for information about the pirates.”

The one who caught the gaze of Pastra and the village chief was the young man—though, from Rei’s perspective, he was still quite a bit older—who had spoken to Rei.

“If the village chief has decided that, I’m fine with it. So, what do you want to know?”

“Well… To be frank, I want to know where the pirates’ hideout is.”

“That’s… over there.”

Saying that, Pastra pointed to an island faintly visible beyond the sea—one that could be seen even from where they stood.

“An island?”

“Yeah. It used to be uninhabited, but a little while ago, some pirates showed up and made it their hideout.”

“So, they demanded food from this nearby village, huh? When I think about it that way, maybe they aren’t such a terrible bunch after all?”

“Huh? What are you talking about? You mean they’re not the worst possible thing in this situation?”

Perhaps dissatisfied with Rei’s words, Pastra glared at him, her sun-tanned face and body betraying her frustration.

But such a glare was bound to have no effect on Rei, who had survived countless battles.

“I mean, think about it. If those pirates were truly the worst kind of people who didn’t think about the consequences, would they have just asked for food? They wouldn’t have just made that demand—they would have raided this village and taken everything, wouldn’t they?”

“Well…”

Pastra had been about to retort to Rei’s words, but now that he put it that way, she couldn’t say a thing.

After all, if they’d done something like that, there’s a high chance this village would have already been wiped out.

“But even so, I can’t just be told to forgive the pirates.”

“That’s only natural. I’m not saying you should forgive the pirates, anyway. I just meant you were lucky they weren’t that kind of pirate. …So, how many ships do the pirates have? Tell me their size, too.”

“As for size… there’s just one pretty big ship. But they did have a few smaller ones that could be loaded onto that ship. Otherwise, they wouldn’t have been able to make it all the way here.”

“Just one ship, huh. In that case, maybe we can assume there are fewer of them than I thought.”

That said, even operating a single ship requires a fair number of people.

It’s unlikely there are only about ten pirates…

(Besides, just because only one ship came to this village doesn’t mean they don’t actually have other ships.)

The small size of this village is obvious enough when viewed from offshore.

Given that, I can’t imagine they’d go to the trouble of bringing a large group of pirates here.

With these thoughts in mind, Rei considers what he should do next.

If possible, he’d like to handle this matter on his own.

But given the number of enemies… and above all, managing the captured pirates might be too much for Rei to handle alone.

(Ah, once they’re captured, I could ask the villagers for help.)

Since these are pirates who tried to harm them, the villagers probably wouldn’t just let them out of their sight so easily.

(Yeah, since Elena and the others are enjoying their time at the beach, it’s probably best not to do anything that might interrupt them.)

Rei turned his gaze toward the village chief and spoke up.

“If I capture the pirates, could you take charge of them for a little while?”

“Hmm… ‘Look after’ them, you say. Do you have any idea how long that might take? I’d certainly like to help if they’re the kind of people who’d harm this village. But I’ve got work to do myself.”

“...I see. In that case, I’ll pay you a reward.”

“If you do that, I’ll be able to take this job seriously.”

And so, even while on vacation, Rei found himself hunting pirates.
