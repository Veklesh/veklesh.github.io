That day, before leaving the place that had been his room all winter, Rei glanced around the room.

(Well, it wasn't exactly my room the whole time. I was attacked by the Stigma.)

His gaze returned to the room as he thought back to the battle fought on that winter night.

After that battle, Rei ended up moving to a different room.

Fortunately, there were several other vacant rooms in the staff dormitory, so it was lucky that Rei didn't have any trouble finding a place to stay.

"Thanks for everything."

He said only that briefly before leaving the room.

Principal Erinde had informed me ten days ago that today would be my last day as an instructor.

That meant today's class would be his last.

It had been about two months since Rei had been hired as an instructor at this military academy. The first half-month or so had been quite hectic, partly due to the Stigma incident, but after that, he had been able to spend his days in relative peace without any major disturbances.

...Though whether daily mock battles with the S-class students could truly be called peaceful was questionable.

(Well, for me, it's peaceful enough since no one's trying to kill me.)

Sighing at his own turbulent life, Rei headed to the stables in the staff quarters.

The stable had been destroyed twice by Set, but Rei had properly compensated for the repair costs.

The Quent Duke's household had offered to cover the costs themselves, especially for the second incident. They argued that it was their own failure that led to Rei being attacked by the Stigma, which in turn caused Set to flee the stables. Rei, however, shook his head at their offer and refused.

He wasn't short on money, and since he was the one who caused trouble with the Set incident, he insisted on covering the repair costs himself.

In the end, the Quent Duke's side... or more accurately, Erinde, acting as their representative, accepted this, and the repair costs fell to Rei.

"Grrrrrrr!"

Sensing Rei's arrival, Set let out a happy purr upon seeing him enter the stable.

If this had been when Set first entered the stable, the surrounding horses would undoubtedly have become agitated. However, after living together in the same stable for nearly two months, the horses seemed to have grown accustomed to Set.

It probably helped that Set never showed any aggressive behavior toward the horses.

Now, while it was hardly accurate to call Set an easygoing companion for the horses... they seemed to understand that he wouldn't do anything unless provoked. They had established a relationship based on the principle of "leave well enough alone."

...Set would have liked them to play together, but that was completely wishful thinking.

"Come on, Set. It's the last lesson, so shall we go together today?"

"Grrr!"

Set let out a happy snort at Rei's words.

Even before the Stigma matter was settled, Rei had occasionally taken Set to the mock battles.

There were days he didn't take him, but now it was about every other day... no, more than that, about two out of every three days.

Partly because Set begged Rei with those round eyes, wanting to be with Rei so badly, and partly because Rei didn't mind spending time with Set at all.

...Though, because of that, the third- and fourth-year S-Class students ended up fighting Set countless times.

Thanks to that, the students' fear of Set had significantly diminished, and some had even fallen for his adorable charm.

Even so, his popularity still didn't match Set's in Gilm. That was probably because fighting him was involved.

Because once they fought him, they inevitably realized he was overwhelmingly stronger than themselves, so they couldn't interact with him as casually as the residents of Gilm could.

"Guruu, gurururururuu!"

Despite that, Set must be happy that while he was feared at first, now he has a few companions willing to play with him.

Purring contentedly, he headed with Rei not to the gymnasium... but to the field.

The snow had mostly melted, allowing normal activities on the field.

...Normally, it would take a while longer for the snow to completely disappear and the field to become usable, but perhaps due to the good weather, it was already usable this year. ...Or so it seemed.

The truth was, Rei had used magic specifically for this day to forcibly melt the snow. Furthermore, she'd employed the terrain manipulation skill from the Death Scythe to make the field usable.

It was a bit reckless, but she'd convinced Erinde it was for the final lesson.

So, when Rei and Set arrived at the field, all the students Rei taught—both third and fourth year S-Class students—were present.

Also present were Grink, who handled support for Rei's classes, Samaloon, who handled daily life support, and of course, the headmistress, Erinde. Naturally, Marka and Coan were there too.

(Sardat is probably around somewhere too.)

Since the Stigma incident, Rei hadn't seen Sardat.

A Rank S adventurer... specifically one skilled in long-range attacks. Rei had hoped to spar with him, but Sardat seemed to have read his mind and stopped showing up.

(Then again, he is an S-rank adventurer, so it's entirely possible he's just busy as usual.)

Considering Sardat's personality, Rei couldn't help but treat him with a certain lightness, much like Sardat treated himself.

Anyway, since he wasn't here now, there was no point in saying anything more. Ray decided and opened his mouth.

"Now then, I've gathered the third and fourth-year S-Class students here because today is your final lesson."

The words slipped from Rei's mouth, and the entire room stirred.

Many had initially underestimated Rei, but the students present now fully understood – or rather, had been made to understand – that Rei was a formidable figure beyond their reach.

For over two months, they had engaged in mock battles with such an opponent nearly every day. Naturally, this had elevated their abilities several levels beyond where they were before winter. It was precisely because they could feel this growth that many wished, if possible, for Rei to remain here as their instructor.

...Though, admittedly, some lamented not being able to see Set's adorable antics anymore, while others, oddly enough, felt lonely at the thought of not seeing Rei.

Listening to the surrounding murmurs, Rei continued.

"The content of the final lesson is extremely simple. ...All of you, me, and Set."

The words that came from Rei's mouth caused the surrounding area to fall silent for a moment, followed by an even louder murmur than before.

Watching this commotion in silence were those who weren't students, starting with Erinde.

They knew exactly what kind of mock battle would take place during the final lesson.

When they first heard about it, they had voiced various objections, but in the end, Rei's opinion had prevailed.

"Calm down. Show me how much strength you've gained over these past two months. ...Show me. If all you can manage is pathetic displays, then an S-Class is a waste on you lot. You'd be better off as common soldiers, not knights or adventurers."

It was blatant provocation, but its effect couldn't have been more perfect.

Fighting spirit ignited in the eyes of most students.

...Though a few who knew Rei's true strength flinched in fear, the fact remained that most students were fired up.

"Alright, then. Grab your weapons. It's been since the first lesson since I faced all of you like this. ...Well, back then it was one class at a time, so facing two classes simultaneously is a first for me today."

As they listened to Rei's words, the students began picking up their mock battle weapons.

Normally, there weren't enough mock battle weapons for both classes to use simultaneously, but for this occasion, Rei had asked Erinde to prepare them.

Rei, too, took up his usual mock battle spear and shouted loudly.

"Come on! Show me what you've got!"

With that as the starting signal, the battle between Rei and Set against the third and fourth-year S-Class students began.

The first to charge at Rei was a man known for his hot-blooded temperament even among the third-year S-Class students.

He swung his halberd wide, aiming to land a solid blow on Rei.

"I tell you this every time. Your swing is too big. It's easy to see where you're aiming."

Before the halberd could come down, the shaft of Rei's spear reached the man with overwhelming speed.

The man would likely insist it wasn't about the swing being big.

Such overwhelming speed.

The man, blown away, took down the woman approaching from behind with a longsword, sending them both tumbling.

"Don't panic! Everyone, follow my orders! Launching sporadic attacks at Rei is pointless! Focus on coordination! Third-years, attack alongside the fourth-years! Even Rei can't keep track of everything happening around him!"

Insurra's voice echoed around them. Hearing it, the students obediently followed his instructions and began their assault on Rei.

"Hmm? I recall that student Insula was arrogant... in the worst sense, a truly aristocratic noble."

Erinde murmured with apparent admiration as she watched the mock battle.

The Insula Erinde knew would have exuded arrogance even when issuing orders like that.

That arrogance used to be so off-putting that even when the orders were correct, some refused to obey, and those who did obey had a slight delay... But in this command, there was no trace of that arrogance at all.

"He's been getting his ass kicked by Rei for the past two months... I'd say his nose was knocked right off its perch."

Glink's words made Samaloon nod in agreement.

Though Samaloon served as a magic instructor rather than a mock battle coach, he had clearly sensed a shift in the cadets' attitudes within days of Rei's arrival at the academy.

"Rei is one thing, but Set's progress is nothing short of remarkable."

At Marka's words, the gazes of those watching the mock battle turned toward Set.

There, a man had been blown away by a single strike from Set's tail, along with the spear he held.

Simultaneously, the long sword swung by a woman charging head-on at Set was easily deflected by his sharp beak... the blade snapping clean in half.

"Whoa, a griffin's beak... I mean, I suppose it's only natural for Set, but still..."

"...Since Set joined the mock battles, we've had to restock weapons more frequently, which is a headache for me too. Though, I know it's a small price to pay, considering you'd never normally get to fight a griffin like this."

Glink sighed and muttered in response to Samaloon's words.

As a mock battle instructor, he was extremely pleased that students were given the opportunity to fight griffins, but the budget was finite, not infinite.

Set did hold back against people, ensuring he wouldn't inflict fatal wounds or cause serious injury, but he couldn't do the same with weapons.

Not just the beak, but even a single strike from the claws would leave marks on the mock battle weapons.

Considering that aspect alone, Rei, who used the same mock battle weapons, handled them more skillfully than Set.

Even as they spoke like this, the students were being driven into incapacitated states one after another.

And twenty minutes after the mock battle began... standing on the ground were Rei and Set... and barely, though staggering, only Insula remained.

"Is this it?"

Glancing at the students sprawled on the ground, too exhausted and in pain to rise, Rei continued.

"Compared to two months ago, you've definitely grown stronger. ...However, as you can see from Set, it's also true that beings beyond your imagination exist in this world. ...As a final farewell gift, I'll show you one more interesting thing."

Saying this, Rei's gaze turned to Erinde.

Erinde, receiving that gaze, nodded back at Rei with a slightly reluctant expression, despite having been informed beforehand.

Confirming this, Rei began to amplify his magic, drawing the students' gazes—gaze of what he would do... no, what would be done to him.

The New Moon Ring concealed his magic power, yet the overwhelming presence emanating from Rei was impossible to hide. It instilled awe in the students... The next instant, Rei's body was enveloped in something red. Seeing this, nearly all the students collapsed in shock.

Insuara, the only one still standing, was no exception. Seeing Rei enveloped in the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour caused many others to lose consciousness.

"This is the power of a dimension unknown to you. You may one day face opponents possessing power of this caliber. I expect you to cultivate your own strength so you won't be overwhelmed when that time comes."

And so, Rei's final lesson at the officer academy came to an end.
