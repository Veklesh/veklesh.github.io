Upon hearing that Rei would continue as a mock battle instructor at the academy, the one who was most delighted was, naturally, Marka, who was present at the time.

Since Marka had gone all the way to Gilm to ask Rei for this favor, he must have felt quite responsible.

Rei had blown away that anxiety with a single word, so it was only natural Marka was delighted.

With his spirits lifted, Marka naturally voiced his desire to see Rei in action as an instructor...

"You're slow to retreat! Don't hesitate between attack and defense. Engrave the movements into your body so you can act instinctively!"

Rei's mock battle spear swept away Insula's foot as he failed to close the distance against Rei's advancing movement.

Though Insula was the last of the five to remain standing, his leg was swept out from under him, sending him tumbling toward the ground... just in time, he thrust out his hand and managed to prevent himself from falling.

Rei was impressed by Insula's reflexes, but the reason for his persistence became clear the next instant.

"How could I bear to show such a pathetic sight before Lord Marka!"

Shouting, he rose again, grasped his mock battle longsword, and faced Rei.

(Is it really true that having Marka watch brings out your true potential?)

Rei faced Insula, pondering this question.

But this academy was, after all, a place where those aspiring to become knights of the Duke Quent's Order... or adventurers gathered.

Therefore, it was only natural that many knew the face of Marka, the beloved daughter of Duke Quent.

Especially since Marka's magical talent was widely known, making her a very famous figure.

That's precisely why Insula, not wanting to look foolish in front of Marka, tried desperately to get one over on Rei.

Originally from a noble family and having known Marka personally, Insula likely felt this even more strongly.

"Come on. I'll show you just how much you're really worth."

"That arrogant mouth of yours won't last long!"

The condescending words directed at him by the younger Rei.

In response, Insula kicked off the floor, closing the distance to Rei.

The first strike he unleashed toward Rei was a sweeping horizontal blow aimed at the torso, the hardest part to dodge.

Normally, stepping back to evade would be the standard response.

But Rei effortlessly parried Insula's strike with a single-handed thrust of his spear.

Even so, Insula showed no sign of panic. Instead, he used the momentum from his parried strike to spin widely, aiming to slam his longsword into Rei.

"Too easy."

With that single word, the longsword was deflected once more by the spear.

But Insula shouted defiantly at Rei.

"You are!"

Insula never intended to settle the fight with this strike anyway.

The counterattack he launched using the recoil from Rei's spear carried almost no force, easily deflected and returned to his hand. Insula's follow-up strike was a thrust.

That thrust, heading straight for Rei, exploited an opening... or so Insula must have thought.

Indeed, it possessed a sharpness that could easily have taken down a low-rank adventurer.

However... the one Insula faced was Rei, an opponent who could by no means be called a low-rank adventurer.

In response to the thrust of the longsword aimed at his torso, Rei countered with a spear thrust.

Even though he clearly moved only after seeing Insula's thrust, the clash of the longsword's edge and the spear's tip occurred right beside Insula.

A thrust is both fast and powerful.

But if stopped before reaching peak speed, that high power couldn't be unleashed.

Furthermore, Rei and Insula possessed fundamentally different physical capabilities.

The result...

"Waaahhh!"

The clash of thrusts sent Insula flying backward.

Still, thanks to Rei holding back his power since it was a mock battle, Insula only flew a few meters.

It was also fortunate for Insula that they were inside the gymnasium, not outdoors.

...Though, for Insula, getting blown away right in front of Marka, it certainly couldn't be called fortunate.

"Guhh... Haa, haa, haa... Dammit!"

The figure shouting in frustration bore no resemblance to the one who had looked down on Rei when they first met.

It hadn't even been ten days since Rei started conducting mock battle lessons, yet he could already grasp the overwhelming difference in strength between himself and Rei.

No, failing to grasp that would make it impossible to remain in the fourth-year S-Class.

Even nobles end up in lower classes if their skills are lacking. That was the rule at this academy.

It was only possible because Ronald, holding the title of Duke of Quent, had personally arranged it.

"Hmm, splendid! To fight Rei to that extent is truly impressive. Your future looks bright. Coan, don't you agree?"

Marka, who had been watching the outcome of the match, nodded with satisfaction and addressed the guard standing beside him.

The guard addressed... Coan, softened his expression at Marka's words, nodded, and spoke.

"Indeed. I'd say he possesses top-tier skill within this class. However, he seems to struggle a bit with putting his weight behind his thrusts. Once he gets used to that, the power of his thrusts should increase by one... no, two levels."

Marka nodded in agreement at Coan's words.

While Marka possessed considerable knowledge of magic, she wasn't particularly well-versed in those who fought on the front lines, like warriors or knights.

Perhaps when she grew older, she might take the field like Elena, the princess general. Fortunately, at her current age, no one would order her to do such a thing.

...If anyone had, Ronald would have used all his influence to shut down the idea for his beloved daughter's sake.

Instead, she had studied magic in depth, and it was likely because of this knowledge, gained through rumors or something similar, that Samaloon was peeking cautiously through the gymnasium door, wanting to talk about magic with Marka, even just a little.

Samaroon had always possessed an intense curiosity about magic. Learning that Marka, a prodigy of magic, was within the officer academy walls, it was hardly surprising she would take such action.

However, Samaloon was completely unaware that the half-bloodshot look he directed at Marka was provoking Coan, Marka's bodyguard.

(Samaroon... I hope Coan doesn't mistake him for a suspicious person and take action.)

Given his role as a bodyguard, Coan might intervene if Samaloon made any suspicious moves.

Indeed, Samaloon peering through the gymnasium entrance was undeniably suspicious behavior.

Thinking he should stop him immediately if anything happened, since he was also his handler, Rei opened his mouth.

"Next five, come on!"

He shouted, and five new students stood up, full of spirit.

Even though they had seen Insula, one of the skilled fighters in this class, get defeated with their own eyes, the look in their eyes was less one of fear and more one of fierce determination to challenge Rei.

They had lost to Rei countless times in previous lessons.

Their attitude seemed to say that adding one or two more defeats now wouldn't change anything.

But that fighting spirit was precisely what was absolutely essential to survive as a knight or adventurer.

It was commonplace on the battlefield to face enemies stronger than oneself.

Especially for the students of this academy, upon graduation, they would face countless opponents stronger than themselves.

This held true even for the S-Class, the academy's top-tier students renowned as the elite.

That's precisely why being able to spar with Rei, who was normally an overwhelmingly powerful opponent they could never defeat, was practically a privilege for the S-Class students.

(I wonder how these guys will fare... Feels like a mix of unease and anticipation.)

Grasping her mock battle spear, Rei awaited the five advancing toward her.

"Hmm, I don't know much about those who fight on the front lines. I consider myself somewhat knowledgeable about mages, though... Even from my perspective, honestly, they're not quite up to par."

With the fourth-year S-Class lessons finished and the third-year S-Class lessons scheduled for the afternoon, Rei was currently having an early lunch in the cafeteria with Marka and Coan.

They would have preferred eating in the main school cafeteria, but with the famous Marka present, they stood out far too much.

Given that Marka was famous in more ways than one, they'd come to the staff dormitory cafeteria for a less crowded spot...

(Well, it's better than the student cafeteria...)

He scanned the cafeteria.

There were others like Rei, eating a bit early, and instructors and staff whose classes started in the afternoon, having a slightly late breakfast.

Naturally, these people also understood who Marka was and were drawing attention.

Unlike the students, though, these were sensible adults. It was easier on Rei's nerves that they didn't stare quite as blatantly as the students did.

"Being able to freely use magic in combat is difficult, you know. If I could cast while moving, that would solve a lot of those issues at once."

"...Rei, not many people can pull off something like that."

"But I can."

"As if there are many like you!"

Marka snapped reflexively at Rei's words, and Coan, seated beside him, nodded as if to say he felt the same.

If every cadet at the academy possessed skills like Rei's... Just imagining it made the academy's forces seem capable of destroying a nation on their own.

"Magic, you say? I'd love to hear more about that."

Samaloon, who had been waiting for an opportunity to speak with Marka, interrupted Rei and Marka's conversation.

...Though both Rei and Coan had sensed his approach.

Still, Coan hadn't decided to treat him as a suspicious individual, likely because Rei had informed Coan about Samaloon beforehand after the mock battle ended.

Samaroon was someone who had looked after Rei in this land too. He didn't want to see such a person treated as a suspicious individual and dealt with by Coan.

"Hmm, so you are Samaloon. I've heard about you from Rei. And from Iskeld too. I hear you know a great deal about magic."

When Marka looked at Samaloon and said this, Samaloon seemed pleased to be described as knowledgeable about magic by Marka. He smiled happily and opened his mouth.

"I'm a bit uneasy about coming from that muscle-bound brute, but it's an honor to have you know my name. Now, about magic... I hear Lord Marka can wield the three elements: wind, earth, and light?"

"Hmm. That's correct."

"How about the strengths of each? Like, is earth easier to use than wind, or something like that?"

"...Hmm. I don't have that kind of feeling myself. I can use magic of any element without any particular strengths or weaknesses."

"Huh!? According to the information I've gathered so far, most individuals who wield multiple elements have a dominant one. Take Rei over there, for instance. She seems to wield wind in addition to fire, but when you think of Rei, you think of fire magic—her dominant element is clearly defined."

Samaloon's gaze shifted toward Rei, who was sipping her after-dinner tea.

Rei, caught in that gaze, briefly considered saying something in response but ultimately remained silent.

Rei understood where the widely held belief that she possessed the wind attribute originated.

If it were only the Flying Slash he used to distract enemies or attack from a distance... it wouldn't be difficult to insist that was a skill, not magic.

But the fiery whirlwind that had become Rei's symbol—a disaster capable of incinerating an entire army—the fire tornado...

The ordinary wind tornadoes required to generate this were not something that could be explained as a skill.

He could claim it was a skill of Set, a rare species, and perhaps people would believe him. But if that happened, the attention on Set would only grow even more than it already was.

...If it were merely increased attention, Rei wouldn't particularly mind. No, rather, Set would likely be delighted that more people would pay him attention and he'd have more playmates.

But if it led people to try and claim Set for that reason, trouble was guaranteed.

(It'd be nothing but a pain if a bunch of jerks like those Azoth merchants showed up again. ...If anyone targets Set, I have absolutely no intention of holding back.)

As he half-listened to Marka and Samaloon discussing magical theory, a faint aura of killing intent seeped from Rei's body.

Coan reacted to it and glanced at Rei, but judging that Rei showed no sign of hostility toward Marka, he returned his full attention to guarding her.
