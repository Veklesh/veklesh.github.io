Lunchtime was over, and I had just started working for the afternoon.

It was hard to believe it was winter, as a bright sun hung in the sky.

There were clouds in the sky, but they weren’t the kind that bring snow—they were white clouds that reminded me of summer.

(They look like cumulonimbus clouds… but aren’t those supposed to form only in summer? Is it the weather conditions? Or something like that.)

It was knowledge based entirely on a vague memory, but even so, the sight of cumulus-like clouds floating in the sky wasn’t so bad for Rei.

It would be a problem if it started snowing, but there was absolutely no sign of that right now.

...Still, winter is winter.

Even though the weather was nice and the sun was shining with a warmth unusual for winter, the cold was still distinctly wintry, inevitably chilling the bodies of those outside.

(Maybe I should just use magic to make a huge bonfire? Nah, but then again... even if I did that, only the people nearby would stay warm. Plus, from Gilm’s perspective, it might look like a fire or something.)

I immediately dismissed the idea of a bonfire.

If this had been a somewhat enclosed space where the air could be circulated, I might have been able to light a fire to warm things up a bit… but no matter how big a fire I built outside, it was certain it wouldn’t get very warm.

So I just watched the Giganto Turtle being butchered.

By the way, the Snow Ape’s disassembly had already been completed that morning, and its materials had already been stored in the Misty Ring.

“Rei, what are we going to do now?”

As I watched the Giganto Turtle being butchered, Vihera, who was standing next to me, suddenly asked that.

That said, even though she was right next to me, Vihera wasn’t watching the Giganto Turtle being butchered—she was watching the monsters approaching from all around.

Vihera probably wishes a monster like a Snow Ape would show up. Unfortunately, the monsters appearing today are, as usual, mostly Kobolds.

Still, the fact that she isn’t all that grumpy is probably because of the battle with the Snow Ape this morning.

“What do you want me to do? Honestly, there’s nothing for me to do. It doesn’t look like there are any magic items hidden at the construction site.”

“Oh? Have you finally decided to drop the magic item theory?”

“No way.”

He retorted immediately to Vihera’s words, but the truth is, even Rei doesn’t know how to go about finding a magic item.

Still, the reason he clings to the magic item theory is probably because, deep down, he’d like to have one if possible.

(Since I can make them follow simple orders like gathering Kobolds and storming a place, they’d be pretty handy if used right. Like when hunting bandits.)

Hunting bandits was one of Rei’s hobbies.

There are many ways to use them for this: gathering Kobolds to storm a bandit hideout, or using them as meat shields or sacrificial pawns when encountering a horde of monsters.

(It’s not limited to the Bestir Empire, but the possibility of war hasn’t exactly disappeared either.)

While Rei generally prioritized quality over quantity, that didn’t mean he underestimated the value of numbers.

Even monsters as lowly as Kobolds can be quite useful depending on how you use them.

That’s precisely why Rei hoped the reason behind this incident was a magic item.

“Hmm. …Well, if that’s what you think, Rei, then fine. But just so you know, don’t forget there are other possibilities besides magic items, okay?”

“Ah, that… Hmm?”

Rei was about to reply to Vihera, but suddenly stopped himself.

That was because he noticed someone riding a horse toward them from the direction of Gilm.

If the rider had been an adventurer or something similar, he probably wouldn’t have paid it much mind.

After all, just like Vihera and the others had done until yesterday, there were always people joining the butchering later on.

Of course, compared to those who had been working since morning, such people would receive less pay and a smaller portion of Giganto Turtle meat.

In any case, if the approaching figure had been an adventurer, Rei wouldn’t have paid it much mind, but when it turned out to be someone wearing the guild uniform—and specifically, Lenora—the situation changed.

Lenora, who was in charge of Rei, was riding her horse toward them.

That was clearly an unusual situation.

(So Lenora can ride a horse, huh? And judging by the way she looks, she seems pretty used to it, too.)

Rei thought that for a moment, but immediately called out to Set, who was lying down a short distance away, basking in the sun—which was unusually warm for winter.

「Set！」

Set had been sunbathing just a moment ago, but as soon as Rei called out to him, he got up and started running.

As if he knew exactly what Rei wanted with just that one word, he didn’t come to a complete stop when he reached Rei, but merely slowed down slightly.

Rei seemed to understand Set’s behavior as well; as Set passed in front of him, he quickly leaped onto his back.

“Lenora is coming from Gilm. Go over there.”

“Grr!”

Set let out a quick yelp and started running toward Lenora.

Even though there was some distance to cover, this was Set.

In just over ten seconds, he reaches the horse Lenora is riding...

“Brrrrrr”

Naturally, the horse was frightened by Set’s approach and stopped moving.

For an ordinary horse, having an absolute powerhouse like Set charging straight at it is nothing short of terrifying.

Or, if it were a horse pulling a guild carriage, it might have been somewhat accustomed to Set since it had traveled with him before… but the horse Lenora was riding was not that kind of horse, and this was its first encounter with Set.

That said, for Rei, who had come to hear the details from Lenora, this was actually something to be welcomed.

“Rei!”

Lenora knew exactly who was riding Set, so she didn’t hesitate for a moment as she called out.

“What’s going on!? Since you came here in person, Lenora, you must have something you need from me, right?”

“Yes. Actually, I received a message from the guards…”

Hearing those words, Rei was certain his guess about why Lenora had gone out of her way to come here was correct.

After all, he’d been told that if anything happened regarding the Kobold incident, they’d notify him immediately through the guild.

Still, he never expected Lenora to come herself.

He had assumed they’d send someone from the guild staff—perhaps a low-ranking worker or someone more accustomed to rough business.

...Though Kenny—the one closest to Rei—would likely voice her displeasure—the fact that Lenora came might indicate that the Guild’s objective was to make it clear that something had happened.

“It’s about the Kobold incident, isn’t it?”

Since the message came from the guards, that was the only thing that came to mind.

Still, just in case it was something else, I asked to be sure… but the next moment, Lenora nodded quickly.

“Yes. Apparently, the guards watching over the outpost were attacked and injured. Fortunately, it seems no one died, but several people suffered broken bones.”

“...So that’s how it went.”

He never expected such a brazen attack… and for them to target the very guards who were maintaining order within Gilm was a bit of a surprise even to Rei.

He had assumed that if they realized the outpost was being watched, they wouldn’t use it anymore.

“Yes. So, a guard came to the guild asking that we inform Rei of this matter immediately… I’m glad you were here.”

Since she had commissioned the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle, Lenora had expected him to be here.

In reality, since Rei had been moving around to various places, his presence here was practically a coincidence.

In fact, he’d been wandering all over Gilm that morning.

“Got it. Then I’ll head straight to the guard post. The one from yesterday is fine, right?”

“Yes, that should be fine.”

Although Gilm is technically classified as a town, it was essentially large enough to be called a quasi-city.

For that reason alone, there were dozens—perhaps even more—guard posts scattered throughout Gilm.

After confirming with Lenora which post he should go to, Rei gave Set a signal.

Understanding the signal, Set left Lenora behind and headed toward Gilm.

“Alright, I’ll go ahead!”

“Yes!”

After exchanging those brief words... Set arrives at the main gate of Gilm almost immediately.

The guards at the main gate were aware of the incident where a guard had been attacked and that Lenora had gone out to call for Rei, so they didn’t waste time with small talk as usual and quickly completed the procedures for entering Gilm.

“Count on me!”

Nodding at the guard’s brief words, Rei dismounted Set and headed for the guardhouse.

If he had stayed on Set, he would have reached the guardhouse that much faster… but if he’d done that, the residents of Gilm might have caused a commotion wondering what was going on, and people might have been startled by Set’s running and fallen over.

Still, judging by the way Rei and Set looked, those around them must have understood that they were in a hurry.

Many people looked like they wanted to play with Set, but they all held back.

There were a few who tried to play with Set without considering Rei’s situation, but fortunately, those people were stopped by those around them.

……Truly, it was fortunate.

If they hadn’t been stopped and had tried to speak to Set, he would likely have ignored them… and in some cases, they would have been met with a grumble of displeasure.

Though Set was usually adorable, Rei was, after all, his top priority.

Anyone who got in Rei’s way was, naturally, deemed by Set to be someone he disliked.

In any case, with no one to interrupt them, Rei and Set eventually arrived at the guard post they had visited earlier that morning.

In front of the guardhouse, unlike usual, a person was standing.

His expression wasn’t exactly stern, but he couldn’t quite hide his tension.

The guard had been on the lookout for any suspicious figures approaching, but the moment he saw Rei and Set, he seemed to relax slightly.

“I heard the guy on watch got attacked?”

“Yeah. …Fortunately, the attackers were Red Cloth, and no one was seriously injured… but the buildings we were using sustained significant damage. Come inside for now and I’ll fill you in on the details.”

Rei nodded at the guard’s words and entered the guardhouse, leaving Set behind.

(Come to think of it, he mentioned that the building they were using for surveillance was borrowed out of goodwill. I wondered why he looked so grumpy, but I guess that’s the reason.)

The guards inside the guardhouse, unlike those outside, maintained stern expressions—perhaps because they didn’t have to worry about appearing in public.

“You say you were attacked?”

Rei already understood the general situation, but he asked the guards anyway to get the full details.

At Rei’s words, the guards looked even more displeased.

But since Rei was deeply involved in this incident, he couldn’t possibly try to hide the truth.

“Yeah. Man, I have no idea how they found out we were watching them, but we got hit with a full-on surprise attack.”

“So, it was the Red Cloth again? …Could it be that there were survivors from the Red Cloth led by Grange, or something like that?”

“Unfortunately, we don’t know the details. We did manage to capture most of the Red Cloth attackers, though…”

“Could you check with Rei about that later?”

At the guard’s words, Rei faltered… and eventually shook his head.

“Sorry, but I’d rather not do that. I don’t really remember most of the Red Cloth guys’ faces either. If I’ve had even a little conversation with someone, I might still have an impression of them, though.”

From Rei’s perspective, the Red Cloth members looked like nothing more than run-of-the-mill thugs, so their faces were only vaguely familiar to him.

Even if he saw the Red Cloth members who had been captured in that situation, he wouldn’t be able to tell for sure if they were the same ones he’d encountered yesterday.

Since that could potentially lead to a fatal mistake, Rei refused.

The guards seemed to understand Rei’s reasoning and accepted his decision immediately.

After all, even if the guards had been in Rei’s position, it was doubtful they would have been able to clearly remember Akabu’s face.

And so, a somewhat awkward atmosphere began to spread throughout the guardhouse.
