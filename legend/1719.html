Rei and the others kept walking upstream for about two hours.

To call it a “pathless path” might have been a slight exaggeration, but as Rei and the others walked along a spot that was slightly easier to traverse than a mere animal trail, they suddenly came upon that place.

A wide-open space.

Of course, there were trees growing all around, but in that specific area, there was nothing to hinder their progress.

And in the middle of that space stood...

“Stairs, huh?”

Rei muttered, and the look of puzzlement in his voice was likely due to those stairs.

Naturally, there was a staircase leading up from the first floor to the second.

So, he had assumed that in this dungeon, they would be moving upward rather than downward... but what now stood before Rei and the others was a flight of stairs leading down.

“A staircase, huh.”

Elena also muttered the same words as Rei.

She seemed unable to understand why there was a staircase leading down.

“Well, it wouldn’t be strange for something like this to happen. This is a dungeon, after all, so anything could happen.”

Marina, a former guild master—and a seasoned adventurer before she became one—muttered those words.

“That’s true. Since it’s a dungeon, it’s not particularly unusual.”

“Mm.”

Vihera and Byune, who had been active in the Labyrinth City, also agreed with Marina’s words.

“In Exil’s dungeons, it’s not uncommon to have sections where you head down to a lower floor, then up to a higher floor, and then back down again… I suppose you could call it three-dimensional? That kind of layout isn’t rare either.”

Hearing Vihera’s words, Rei and Elena could only respond, “Is that so…?”

Neither Rei nor Elena were exactly new to dungeons.

Still, they had never before encountered a dungeon like this—one that Vihera described as “three-dimensional.”

“It’s just… when a dungeon is structured like this, it’s rare for there to be only one set of stairs leading onward. Considering how vast this forest is, I suspect there are probably several other sets of stairs… What should we do?”

Should they look for more stairs, or take the ones right in front of them down to the first floor?

“Which should we choose?” Vihera asked, and Rei hesitated, unsure of what to do… but eventually made up her mind.

“Let’s go down. This forest is too vast; if we miss these stairs, there’s no guarantee we’ll find another set like them.”

Having ultimately chosen to head downstairs, Rei heads to the first floor with the others.

Since they had no idea what might happen the moment they reached the first floor, they naturally moved while keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

And so, as they descend the stairs...

“So you’re here.”

Rei’s muttered words escaped to those around him.

The area where they had descended to the first floor wasn’t a hallway, but a single large open space.

The reason Rei muttered upon seeing that space was because there were several rock plants growing there.

These rock plants were the same kind that grew at the dead end of the Y-shaped path branching off from the dungeon’s entrance on the first floor.

Such rock plants were densely clustered in this space.

“I wanted a rock flower, but seeing this sight, it doesn’t seem all that rare anymore.”

Relieu had hoped to bring back a rock flower as a souvenir for his wife, Shumine, but seeing the dozens, hundreds—perhaps even more—rock plants spread out before him, he muttered thoughtfully.

In fact, seeing so many of them growing there, there was neither novelty nor gratitude to be found.

A sense of novelty or gratitude arises precisely because something is rare.

Seeing them growing in such vast numbers, there is no trace of rarity to be found.

“Anyway, if we’re going to have the guild examine these rock plants, the more specimens we have, the better, right?”

“True. …But those rock plants—even though they aren’t exactly monsters, they do attack us. Their attacks aren’t that powerful, but with that many of them, it’s a bit of a hassle, isn’t it?”

Marina’s gaze shifts to the dense cluster of rock plants.

These rock plants attack anyone who approaches them, even though they aren’t monsters.

(Then again, even on Earth we have carnivorous plants like Venus flytraps, so it wouldn’t be surprising if plants in Elgin were even more aggressive toward their prey. …Though the fact that they’re “rock plants” does raise some questions.)

It is a contradiction in terms to have plants made of rock… but the fact that Rei feels it wouldn’t be particularly strange for something like that to exist in Elgin is probably a sign that he’s getting used to this world.

“Anyway, we’re going to collect as many of those rock plants as possible and bring them back. Fortunately, we don’t have to worry about luggage.”

No one objected to Rei’s declaration.

Everyone must be curious about what exactly these rock plants are.

Marina, in particular, is especially curious.

Being a dark elf, she seemed fascinated by the rock plants.

"I’d like to bring them back with the roots if possible… but that’s not going to happen, is it?"

“It’s impossible.”

Rei replied to Marina immediately.

It was clear from their previous encounter that, as long as they were alive, the rock plants would attack them endlessly. And above all… the rock plant right in front of them had clearly identified Rei and the others as prey and was already in an attack stance.

「Marina！」

At Rei’s words, Marina quickly cast a spirit spell.

Something that could be called a wind barrier was created, and the rocky leaves hurled at them by the swarm of rock plants were deflected one after another in random directions by that wind barrier.

If they were in the forest on the second floor, near the river, they could have created a much stronger water barrier for defense... but unfortunately, with the river currently dry, that wasn’t an option.

Well, it could be done if I took the time, but what I lacked in this situation was exactly that—time.

“The attacks themselves aren’t that troublesome… but with this many of them, it’s a bit of a hassle. Rei, I’d like to step forward for a moment—is that okay?”

With a longsword in hand, Relieu asked Rei.

With the rock leaves being fired one after another from the rock plants at a density that could truly be called a hailstorm, Rei leaves them to the wind barrier and turns his gaze toward Relieu.

“Then, can I leave it to you?”

Without asking, “Can you handle it?” or “Are you sure you’re okay?”, Rei simply states that much.

In terms of rank, Relieu is a higher-tier fighter than Rei.

To worry about such an opponent in the face of enemies of this caliber would be an insult.

That was the reasoning behind Rei’s words.

In fact, in response to Rei’s words, Relieu returned a confident smile, indicating there was no problem at all.

“Got it. Marina, I’m counting on you.”

“Yes. Relieu, just run straight toward the rock plant. I’ll remove the wind barrier to match your timing.”

At Marina’s words, Relieu nodded… and, gripping her longsword, dashed forward without a moment’s hesitation.

There was not a shred of doubt in her mind regarding Marina’s skill with spirit magic; she ran as if absolutely certain that the wind barrier would be lifted the moment she passed through it.

And… Relieu’s trust was not betrayed in the slightest; the very moment she was about to collide with the wind barrier, the barrier that had been blocking the rock leaves vanished in an instant.

It happened so abruptly that one might believe it if told there had never been anything there at all.

As a result, Relieu didn’t slow down at all and successfully burst through the wind barrier.

Once she burst out of the wind barrier, countless rock leaves—which had been blocked until then—naturally flew toward her.

However, Relieu did not slow down at all; she continued forward, dodging the countless rock leaves flying at her or slashing them away with her longsword.

Although the rock leaves were flying at him in countless numbers, to be honest, Relieu likely felt that the battles he had experienced up to that point had been far more grueling.

As a Rank A adventurer with a nickname—one of the top-tier adventurers in Gilm—Relieu had survived countless battles against enemies that ordinary adventurers would never have been able to survive.

For someone like Relieu, an obstacle of this magnitude was a nuisance, but by no means insurmountable.

“Huh… not bad.”

Even from within the wind barrier, Relieu’s sharp swordplay was, of course, clearly visible.

Seeing this, Vihera murmured in admiration.

They had sparred with Relieu several times before, but since those were merely practice matches, neither of them had fought with full force.

That was precisely why Vihera was captivated by Relieu’s current fighting style.

Even though she wasn’t wearing lipstick, the way she licked her red, sensuous lips could only be described as erotic.

……Though, what Vihera truly felt was a competitive spirit craving a battle against a formidable opponent.

In any case, as far as she could tell from watching through the wind barrier, Relieu’s fighting style was so steady that she felt there was absolutely no need to worry about him.

“Well, since we can leave Relieu alone without worry… shall we stay back and provide support instead of moving forward?”

As expected, it was Vihera who showed dissatisfaction at Rei’s words.

Without any means of long-range attack, there isn’t much she can do to provide support from the rear.

Still, since it was Rei’s order, she didn’t complain and, as a small contribution, picked up a stone lying on the ground.

It was debatable whether throwing stones at a rock plant would actually be effective, but she concluded that it was better than doing nothing at all.

"Grrrrrrrrrr!"

Set lets out a roar, and twenty wind arrows materialize around him.

Setting aside their speed, the attack’s power itself isn’t particularly high… but that’s only when the goal is to deal damage to the enemy.

For the purpose of supporting Relieu, who was charging in as he was now, all that mattered was diverting the enemy’s attention away from Relieu, so the attack’s power itself wasn’t a concern.

That said, it’s unclear whether the rock plant will actually be distracted from its objective by being hit by the wind arrows.

To begin with, we still don’t even know what the rock plants actually are.

In fact, we don’t even know if it possesses any kind of self-awareness.

Depending on the situation, it might easily divert its attention from Relieu, but conversely, there’s also the possibility that it will ignore any attacks directed at itself entirely and continue to focus solely on Relieu.

The others also prepare to attack...

“Marina, go!”

With those words, the wind barrier vanishes for a moment.

At the same time, a variety of attacks are unleashed: the Twilight Spear, wind magic, wind arrows, and long needles...

Most of them, as expected, collide with the countless rock leaves flying toward them, their power diminished… and many of them simply vanish.

However, conversely, there were some whose power was not diminished at all by mere rock leaves.

One such attack was the thrown Twilight Spear.

Infused with Rei’s magical power, the Twilight Spear destroys—no, shatters—the rock leaves the moment it strikes them.

The way it plowed forward, pulverizing the rock leaves one after another, could truly be described in a single word: vicious.

The Twilight Spear charged forward without losing any speed or power, eventually passing right by Relieu’s side.

For Relieu, that attack must have been completely unexpected.

But Relieu continues forward without flinching... and eventually arrives right next to the rock plant.

...Normally, the rock plant would have tried to counterattack Relieu now that he had come so close.

Just as when Rei first spotted the rock plant—not with its leaves, but with its fruit… or perhaps with the rock vines缠绕ing its branches and body.

But the Twilight Spear that Rei had thrown had pierced and shattered a significant portion of the rock plant.

From the perspective of capturing the rock plant, this was clearly a failure… but given that there are a considerable number of rock plants in this space, the damage inflicted by that single strike was hardly a problem.

As a result, after the Twilight Spear passed, the rock plant had almost no opportunity to attack Relieu.

Even so, they did mount some sort of counterattack, but the supporting attacks from the others—one after another—struck true, hindering the rock plants’ movements.

“Shh!”

Relieu, having reached the rock plant, let out a sharp cry as she swung her longsword in a flash... severing the rock plant.

That single strike did not stop Relieu’s momentum; she continued to slice through the rock plants growing all around her, one after another.
