When Egg heard that story from Daskar, he couldn't help but frown.

That said, Daskar, sitting at his desk at the end of Egg's gaze, pretending to review documents, jot something down, and sort papers into several boxes, wouldn't get angry at Egg for such an attitude.

Egg had become Daskar's subordinate through Rei's scheme... or rather, a method that could more accurately be called a dirty trick. Yet now, Egg even felt grateful for it.

Daskar was nothing like the nobles Egg had known.

He couldn't have imagined a noble who would casually strike up a conversation with Egg, a former thief—though Egg insisted he was a Robin Hood type.

What's more, the pay and issued equipment were more than satisfactory, and he chatted easily with Egg and the others.

In fact, Egg was now treated as the head of intelligence among Daskar's subordinates.

Naturally, his position prevented him from acting openly, but his standing was still equivalent to, or even higher than, that of a knight commander's deputy—though still below the commander himself.

The treatment they received went beyond mere generosity. Even though they knew it wasn't their style, Egg now felt a sense of loyalty toward Daskar.

It wasn't just Egg. Though his subordinates never voiced it directly, Egg, having known them for so long, understood perfectly well that they cherished their life in Gilm.

A peaceful daily life they would never have experienced in the past.

And yet, when it came to work, they could experience moments filled with tension.

It was the only frontier of the Mireana Kingdom, and precisely because of that, there were dishes you could only eat here, drinks you could only drink here.

They lived such fulfilling days... yet, even so, he couldn't bring himself to simply nod in agreement to the words that came out of Daskar's mouth.

"Um, Daskar-sama? I think you know this, but we're pretty busy with various things right now, you know?"

"I understand that."

Daskar answered Egg's words, yet his hands and eyes never stopped moving.

As a former knight, Daskar had never been particularly adept at this sort of paperwork. Still, after years of serving as a lord, he'd grown accustomed to it whether he liked it or not.

Now, he handled paperwork so smoothly it almost seemed like his true calling.

"Then why, in our current situation, do we have to investigate adventurers... adventurers who aren't even involved in anything, for that matter?"

"...Because Marina asked me to."

Only now did Daskar lift his gaze from the documents he'd been studying, muttering with a sigh.

If he were truly swamped and unable to do anything, Daskar could have refused outright, saying it was unreasonable.

But... fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, it was an undeniable fact that the intelligence unit, while busy, still had some breathing room.

Daskar didn't know how Marina had found out, but she was Marina.

It wasn't just Daskar who found it reasonable that she would know something like that.

And for Daskar, who now had secrets known to Marina that made him want to grab his head and roll around on the floor when he heard them, resisting her was impossible.

No, if Marina had demanded Gilm's secret, or the secret of the Larks Marquis family, Daskar would never have accepted it.

But, should one say unfortunately, or perhaps knowing everything all along, Marina never made such a demand.

"Ah... I see."

Even Egg, upon hearing from Daskar whose request this had been, couldn't protest any further. In fact, he even felt sympathy.

Egg knew Marina well enough to understand what kind of person she was.

It was Egg who had gone to report to Marina about a problem that had occurred within Gilm at one point.

(That was Rei's fault too, wasn't it?)

During the war with the Bestir Empire, Rei had distinguished himself tremendously... so much so that anyone could see he deserved the highest credit. Countless nobles' henchmen had been sent to Gilm, wanting Rei as their subordinate.

These weren't just those who approached Rei directly to negotiate becoming his subordinate. Many were those who sought to find some weakness to exploit... or, more bluntly, those who planned to threaten him with power or violence.

If Rei came into contact with such people, it could easily cause significant damage to Gilm as well.

For this reason, Rei was asked to leave Gilm for a short while, during which Egg and others would take action.

During that time, Egg met with Marina multiple times, naturally coming to understand what kind of person she was.

She was the very embodiment of feminine allure, possessing a charm that could be described as both bewitching and captivating.

For an ordinary man, a single sidelong glance from her would be enough to ensnare him.

Of course, preferences vary—some prefer women who are demure, others who are a certain age or older, or younger...

But Marina transcended such preferences entirely. She possessed a seductive allure powerful enough to arouse desire in most men.

Egg remembered how difficult it was to maintain self-control around Marina.

"So, I'm counting on you."

Egg sighed softly, resigned, before speaking to Daskar, who had returned his gaze to the documents.

"Alright. But if something comes up that I have to prioritize, I'll handle that first. Is that okay with you?"

To put it bluntly, there was someone stirring up trouble related to the Gilm expansion project. Egg couldn't just ignore that and focus on the investigation Marina requested.

Of course, if this investigation were to significantly impact Gilm, that would be a different story...

Investigating an adventurer with a snake tattoo on their left hand? It hardly seemed like something that would involve a major crisis for Gilm.

Daskar seemed to have no objection to Egg's words either, focusing on his own work without protest.

Seeing Daskar like this, Egg exchanged a few light words before leaving the office.

Daskar was currently swamped with merchants and nobles seeking audiences.

The reason he made time for Egg amidst this chaos was likely because he wanted to quickly resolve the matter Marina had asked him to handle.

As Egg exited the office, he watched Daskar's subordinate go to fetch the next visitor, pondering this.

To avoid drawing strange looks from the merchants and nobles, Egg exited the lord's mansion through the back door rather than the front.

Along the way, he encountered several maids, butlers, and knights and soldiers on guard duty, but since Egg's visits to the lord's mansion weren't unusual, he encountered no particular censure. He exchanged brief words with them as he left.

Though Egg had a face that could be called intimidating, aside from the early days, he no longer caused people to flinch or brace themselves at the sight of him.

Having left the lord's mansion, Egg headed for the building serving as the intelligence unit's base.

...It should be noted that there are buildings within the lord's manor grounds used by the intelligence unit, but those are merely branch offices.

Given the nature of intelligence work, which is best done out of sight, it was only natural that discretion was preferred.

Furthermore, while some members joined the intelligence unit after Egg began serving Daskar, quite a few were formerly part of the Prairie Wolves, the unit Egg once led.

It would be unnatural for such individuals to be stationed at the lord's residence.

At worst, they could easily be mistaken for captured bandits.

And there was the risk of being recognized as members of the intelligence unit.

That's precisely why establishing a base outside the lord's manor was only natural.

"Hey, I'm back."

Saying that, Egg entered a shop.

The shop, which sold sundries, appeared to be just that on the surface. In reality, however, it served as a base for the intelligence division.

However, only the employees hired for cover were inside; there was no sign of the intelligence division members.

"Hey, where's the rest of the crew?"

"Ah, a few are in the back. But most are out on assignments."

"I see."

Being employees of a shop that doubles as an intelligence agency base, the staff here showed no particular surprise at seeing Egg.

Well, they were surprised the first time they met him, but after years of dealing with him, it was only natural to get used to even the most intimidating-looking individuals.

Besides, Egg, despite his intimidating appearance, actually had a rational personality and didn't resort to violence without reason.

...Which meant he would use violence if it served a purpose. Fortunately, the staff working here had never made any major mistakes, so they'd never had the chance to find out.

Inside the general store, only a handful of customers were present.

Given the situation where many people gather in Gilm seeking work, it would be accurate to say the shop isn't exactly thriving.

But fundamentally, this shop was a base for the intelligence unit, and the general store itself was merely a cover. So this wasn't a problem.

In fact, fewer customers were preferable, as more patrons would only lead to various complications.

A few of those sparse customers, however, were startled by Egg suddenly entering the shop and addressing the clerk, and hurriedly left the store.

The clerk, seeing the customers leave, returned to his work without showing any particular disappointment.

Even if it was a front store... no, precisely because it was a front store, they couldn't afford to be half-hearted about it.

Glancing sideways at the staff's demeanor, Egg made his way deeper into the shop.

There, several subordinates were relaxing.

Even though Egg had entered the room, they showed no particular sign of standing up.

They merely raised their hands slightly in greeting.

Egg, observing this, showed no sign of anger either.

He wasn't strict about discipline to begin with; as long as they did their jobs properly when it mattered, he wouldn't complain.

...The former members of the Prairie Wolves aside, new recruits to the intelligence unit were initially surprised by such interactions.

But if everyone else behaves that way, trying to be the only disciplined one won't last long.

As a result, they inevitably end up relaxing their discipline just like the others.

"Hey, who's free right now?"

That tone was vastly different from when he spoke with Daskar.

It was only natural that his attitude differed between his employer—the lord of Gilm and a central figure among the Neutral Faction—and his subordinates.

"Um, I'm looking into something involving the Royal Faction."

"I heard there's a problem in the slums, so I'm heading that way."

"I'm looking into allegations of misconduct by several merchants."

"I'm keeping an eye on a noble from the Noble Faction to make sure they don't make any strange moves."

Each reported their respective tasks.

After listening, Egg thought for a moment, then addressed the man who'd mentioned monitoring the Noble Faction's movements.

"Alright, hand over what you're investigating to someone else for now. I've got another job for you."

"Ugh."

The man Egg singled out wore a look of genuine displeasure, exactly as his words suggested.

The case involving the Noble Faction's aristocrat had taken considerable time to investigate, partly due to the Lerdact matter.

That said, the arrival of Elena, the symbol of the Noble Faction, in Gilm had actually made the work itself easier now.

That's precisely why the man told to hand over this easier task to someone else looked so displeased.

Still, knowing Egg's personality, the man accepted the change in duties while outwardly showing his displeasure.

"So, what am I supposed to do now?"

"Investigate one adventurer."

"...Huh? An adventurer? Is this some dangerous target? Like Rei, perhaps?"

Having once been Egg's subordinate, the man knew full well what kind of opponent Rei was.

Perhaps that's why, upon hearing this, Egg sighed, clearly not amused.

"Listen, if adventurers like Rei were just hanging around, then... well, Gilm would be finished. No, the entire Kingdom of Mireana would be finished!"

The man nodded involuntarily at Egg's words, then asked again who they were investigating.
