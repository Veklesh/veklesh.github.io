"Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrrr

As the familiar town of Gilm came into view, Set’s joyful cry echoed around them.

Hearing Set’s cry, Rei also turned his gaze forward.

There was no doubt that Gilm was there, and it made Rei think, “They’ve come back…”

That said, the time Rei and the others had spent away from Gilm in Golz was less than a month.

Yet the fact that he feels they’ve returned like this is likely because, deep down, Rei understands that Gilm is the place he belongs.

“Ah, looks like I heard Set’s voice.”

Thinking this, he looks down at the ground and sees people startled by the sudden cry that rang out on the road.

……The fact that most people are merely startled by Set’s cry—is it because they recognize that the sound they just heard is indeed Set’s cry… or is it because, even though they hear the cry, they see no sign of its source and realize it’s coming from far away?

That said, while a few people were startled and on the verge of panic, they were being calmed down by those around them.

Amidst all this, there are also those who are delighted by the cry they hear.

Yes, for example... like the people transporting the large amount of timber cut down in Treant Forest by wagon.

Since they had spent so much time working alongside Rei and Set during the expansion project, they likely realized immediately that the cry was Set’s.

If it had been a case of mishearing, it wouldn’t have been surprising if a commotion had broken out, but I never imagined that everyone here would mishear Set’s cry.

It wasn’t impossible that a griffin other than Set had arrived, but unfortunately, a Rank A monster like a griffin doesn’t just show itself in public that easily.

However, this is the Gilm.

Given that, the possibility that a griffin other than Set might appear could not be entirely ruled out.

Come to think of it, given a griffin’s speed, it wouldn’t be strange at all for one to appear anywhere other than the frontier.

“Ah… It feels a bit pointless to just stay hidden like this, so I guess I’ll head down soon.”

There are quite a few people looking up at the sky for Set, but since he’s blending into the surrounding scenery like a chameleon thanks to the Set Cage’s ability, it’s hard to spot him.

To be precise, the Set Cage doesn’t instantly blend into the surrounding scenery; rather, it projects the scenery from about a minute ago… While that might be noticeable on the ground, it’s difficult to spot the difference when flying through the air.

Of course, it’s not absolutely impossible; if you’re paying close enough attention, it’s not impossible to spot him.

“Set, we’re getting close to Gilm, so let’s head down to the ground.”

“Grr!”

At Rei’s words, Set growled and began descending toward the ground.

That said, since there are many people moving toward Gilm in and around the town, they aim for a spot a little further away.

Once they were away from the main road, Set, having lowered his altitude as much as possible, lowered the Set Cage he was carrying.

A heavy thud echoed through the area, and those who had been searching for Set spotted the Set Cage.

And once the Set Cage was lowered, it wasn’t hard to spot Set himself.

Many people watch as Set soars back into the sky.

With all eyes on him, Set eventually began his descent toward the ground once more… and successfully landed safely.

By then, Elena and the others had also emerged from the Set Cage.

“Hmm… It’s reasonably comfortable inside the Set Cage, but still, being able to come out here and move freely like this…”

Leaving her sentence unfinished, Elena stretched her arms wide as she gazed up at the sky.

Elena’s hair glistened as it reflected the sunlight, and to those watching from nearby, it seemed as though she had turned sunlight itself into her hair—or perhaps gold itself had become her hair.

Following Elena, Marina, Vihera, and Byune appeared. And Yellow, whom Byune was holding.

Up to this point, since they were known in Gilm as members of Rei’s group, no one who wasn’t completely in the dark was particularly surprised.

...Some were surprised to see Elena, known by the nickname "Princess General," with them, but it was already a fairly well-known fact that Elena was traveling with Rei.

But... when Relieu appeared right after them, everyone watching from a distance was stunned.

Relieu, known by the epithet “Gale,” is, naturally, a familiar face and name to many in Gilm.

The fact that she operates solo, making her a somewhat rare type of adventurer, likely contributes to the spread of her name.

Of course, having a nickname means possessing some kind of outstanding ability.

Forming a party with someone possessing such abilities requires the party members to possess comparable skills as well.

And, naturally, there aren’t that many people who possess such abilities.

That being the case, many adventurers with nicknames usually operate solo, forming temporary parties only when necessary.

“H-hey. Isn’t that… ‘Relieu of the Gale’ over there?”

“Yeah, no doubt about it. I’ve spoken with Relieu before. I can say for sure—that’s definitely Relieu.”

“Wait a minute. So, does that mean Relieu belongs to the Crimson Wings? Even now, people say the Crimson Wings have too much firepower, and adding another ‘Nicknamed One’ to the mix… I wonder how that’ll work out.”

“With the Crimson Wings alone, they could probably take down a weak little country.”

“...No, if we’re dealing with a small country, we wouldn’t even need the Crimson Wings. Just having Deep Crimson Rei alone would be more than enough to handle it.”

“Ah… I can’t argue with that.”

With Relieu’s appearance, those watching from the sidelines began voicing their opinions freely.

Of course, Rei could hear those voices around her, but she had no intention of responding.

In fact, if she said anything here, there was no doubt she’d be swarmed by the crowd, who would seize the opportunity to ask her for an explanation.

So, Rei stowed the Set Cage in her Misty Ring and headed toward the group who were overjoyed by their arrival—yes, the adventurers she knew who were carrying the tree from the Treant Forest.

Under normal circumstances, those adventurers would have been delighted as well.

After all, with Rei’s Misty Ring, they could easily transport the logs they’d been struggling to carry themselves.

But today, at least, that wasn’t an option.

Because Elena and the others—especially Relieu—were right there, as if following Rei.

Since I’ve spoken with Rei quite a bit, I can talk to him normally without feeling nervous.

But that’s only because it’s Rei… I wouldn’t say it to his face—he’d definitely get angry—but it has to do with the fact that Rei has a cute, feminine face and doesn’t look like a skilled adventurer at all.

No adventurer assigned to the Treant Forest would ever underestimate someone based on Rei’s appearance.

Still, there was no doubt that Rei’s appearance—precisely because of that sweet, feminine face—made it easier to approach him.

Given that, when someone like Relieu—an adventurer renowned as a true first-rate expert, with a presence that matches her reputation—showed up, it was only natural that it would be difficult to interact with Rei as usual.

But Rei didn’t seem to care at all about the other adventurers’ reactions and spoke up.

“It hasn’t been that long… well, I guess it has been a while. Are you hauling this tree? It’s from the Forest of Treants, right?”

“Huh? Oh, yeah. That’s right… No, wait, that’s not it. Why are you with Relieu the Whirlwind, Rei?”

The male adventurer, taken aback by how casual Ray’s words were, responded normally for a split second, but quickly snapped back to his senses and shouted.

However, Rei, who had been yelled at, didn’t seem particularly troubled… rather, he opened his mouth as if wondering why he was being yelled at.

“Why, you ask? Well, because I asked you to come with me to clear the dungeon, that’s why.”

“A dungeon… Huh? A dungeon? I thought you hadn’t been around lately, but you’ve been going to dungeons!?”

“That’s right.”

“And since you’ve come back like this… could it be…”

Although the man’s words were vague, Rei understood what he was getting at.

There was no real need to go out of his way to mention that he’d cleared a dungeon, but it wasn’t exactly something he needed to hide either.

No, to be precise, since news of a dungeon being cleared would eventually spread through the guild anyway, there wasn’t much point in hiding it here.

“That’s right. I cleared the dungeon. Though, it was a brand-new one.”

“...For a newly opened dungeon, it was pretty out of the ordinary, though.”

At Marina’s muttered remark, the others nodded in agreement.

In fact, starting with Garangiga, there was no doubt that this was a place that was far from what one would expect of a newly opened dungeon.

At the very least, compared to the dungeon that had recently opened near Gilm—the one they’d cleared during the Gamelion season—there was no doubt that this one was overwhelmingly more difficult.

There was no doubt it was a brand-new dungeon, just as Rei had said. There was no doubt about that… but I couldn’t help wondering if it was really fair to lump it in with other brand-new dungeons.

“Um, is that right?”

The adventurer talking with Rei, of course, knew about Marina.

Among the adventurers who had come to Gilm since the expansion work began, there were some who didn’t know about Marina… but fortunately, this man was an adventurer who had been active in Gilm for some time.

That’s probably why he had doubts about what Marina said.

“Yes. …Though, if we talk about that here, it’ll probably draw a lot of attention… You should ask Rei about that later. Come on, Rei. It’s about time we headed to Gilm.”

No one objected to Marina’s words, and Rei, having stowed the felled wood in his Misty Ring, set off for Gilm with the others.

Naturally, the guards in Gilm had clearly seen Set descending, so they were able to complete the formalities without any issues.

And so, the first place Rei went wasn’t the guild… nor the lord’s mansion, but the place where the felled trees were to be delivered.

That said, bringing anyone other than Rei and the assigned adventurers to such a place would only cause unnecessary commotion, so Elena and the others were asked to head to the guild first and have Walker, who had brought this matter to them, explain the situation.

Relieu, too, went to report various details to Daskar, who had commissioned this job.

...Though just by watching him, it was clear that Relieu wanted to finish his report as quickly as possible so he could return home to his wife, Shumine.

“Oh, the tree… Huh? Rei and Set? You’re back? I heard you were out for a bit?”

The person in charge saw Rei and Set and called out to them in surprise.

That said, since he was in charge here, he had plenty of opportunities to interact with Rei and Set, so he wasn’t particularly surprised to see Set.

“Still, winter’s almost here, and yet you’re cutting down quite a lot of trees.”

“Hm? Oh, right. That’s exactly it—we want to process as much as possible during the winter for what we’ll use in the spring. We want to stockpile as much as we can while we still can. …And that means there’ll be more work to do during the winter, too.”

“Is that how it works? …So, where should we put the wood?”

“I’ll show you. Come this way. Hey, you guys, come along too.”

Saying that, he walked into the warehouse.

Compared to when Rei had seen it a little while ago, it seemed like quite a few more logs had been added.

“Wow… Looking at it again, it’s amazing we managed to haul this many logs.”

“No, most of these were probably carried by Rei.”

"Uh... y-yeah, now that you mention it..."

Hearing that, everyone’s gaze naturally turned to Rei.

“It’s not like I carried all of them myself, so isn’t that fine?”

"Huh? Really?"

“...But I’m not saying I won’t regret bragging about that later.”

“Ugh!”

The male adventurer, who had been thinking of bringing the waitress from the tavern he’d been frequenting lately here and trying to woo her by saying, “I brought this tree here!”—was left speechless by Rei’s words.
