Having finished collecting the goblin's magic stone and the proof-of-kill body parts, Rei and the others were heading toward Gilm.

"No, no, Rei really saved us. If Rei hadn't offered to escort us, who knows what would have happened..."

"Don't worry about it. There are fewer merchants coming to Gilm these days."

"Ahahaha. That's precisely why it's worth our while to come to Gilm, you see."

The merchant, who introduced himself as Walein, smiled from atop his horse and bowed his head once more to Rei, who was walking on foot.

The carriage lay on its side, its axle broken, but fortunately the horses seemed uninjured. Thus, Warain, the weakest among them, rode on one's back.

"Thanks to Rei, it looks like the damage will be minimal."

Walein announced cheerfully, though his joy was only natural.

Take the carriage, for instance.

It had been overturned by the goblin attack, its axle broken.

Under normal circumstances... especially in a situation like this, abandoning a carriage with a broken axle would be the obvious choice.

And if that happened, most of the cargo it carried would naturally have to be abandoned too.

But Rei stored the broken carriage inside the Misty Ring.

Axles are crucial components for carriages, and repairs are costly.

Even so, it was clearly cheaper than building a carriage from scratch.

Furthermore, most of the goods stored in the Misty Ring could be sold in Gilm.

Of course, not all goods were undamaged. Some items were rendered unsellable due to the impact of the carriage overturning.

Like the carriage itself, using these items as-is meant they wouldn't be discarded, so the loss wasn't significant.

Without Rei, the damage would have been so severe that continuing as a merchant would have been impossible. Thanks to Rei, the damage was kept to a minimum.

And since we got all the goblin magic stones and proof-of-kill parts too, we might even end up with a small profit in the end.

The fact that the goblin corpses were clean certainly helped.

Though "clean" was relative—most goblins had been sliced vertically through the torso by Rei's Death Scythe, then severed horizontally at the head, leaving only the head severed.

Some had their heads crushed by Set's blow, their bodies shattered, convulsing and foaming at the mouth from poisoned claws, then burned by fire breath... Others had their bodies sliced open by Vihera's strike, their entrails spilling onto the ground, or were killed by Immersion Claws, leaving no external wounds.

Despite the thick stench of blood and the feel of entrails, it was fortunate they could still strip the magic stones and proof-of-kill parts.

If Rei had done what he often did lately—burning the goblin horde to ashes from above while riding Set using magic—the corpses would have been reduced to nothing but charcoal.

The power of the flame magic that reduced not only the proof-of-kill parts but also the magic stones inside their bodies to charcoal was truly astonishing... but when it came to stripping materials, Rei's magic was simply too powerful.

That said, this particular incident was purely down to Walein's good fortune.

If Walein's carriage had been being chased by goblins, Rei wouldn't have had to consider friend or foe at all. He would have fired his fire magic down from the sky as usual.

In that case, naturally, neither the goblins nor the goblin leader would have been able to strip away the materials.

"Honestly, he always says he doesn't like dealing with people, but then he goes and does something like this sometimes."

Rei walked on the ground, while Walein rode beside her on horseback.

Watching the two of them, Vihera murmured.

Though her words were complaints, the gaze she directed at Rei was the tender look one gives a beloved person.

"...Is that so?"

The adventurer woman, Mina, who had been momentarily captivated by Vihera's appearance, asked Vihera as if trying to cover something up.

The other escort, a man named Giacomo, was so captivated by Vihera's beauty that he was unable to speak.

"Yes. Why do you think Rei, who usually travels on Set, is walking on the ground?"

"Huh?"

Mina tilted her head, clearly taken aback by Vihera's words. She looked at the two walking ahead—or rather, the two and one horse.

"When we unhitched the horses from the carriage, they were calm, right?"

"Now that you mention it, yes. He was incredibly agitated when the goblins attacked. Maybe he got tired from thrashing around in that weird position... or something?"

"No. Well, that might be part of it, but the bigger reason was because Set was there. As you know, Set is a griffin, a rank A monster. And he's omnivorous... though he prefers meat, you see. So then... you get it, right?"

"Ah."

Hearing that, even Mina understood.

For horses, griffins are practically natural enemies.

"Of course, Set is intelligent and wouldn't attack a horse kept by someone, not in the wild. But horses don't understand that. ...Well, maybe they would after spending some time with Set, but you've just met him, right?"

Vihera had seen it countless times before: horses initially afraid of Set, but as they spent time together and time passed, they stopped fearing him... they grew accustomed to him.

The most striking example was probably the Dusk Wheat inn in Gilm.

Compared to ordinary inns, the spacious stables housed many horses besides Set.

Those horses, too, were initially afraid of Set. Yet after living alongside him for a few days, they seemed to realize he posed no threat. Even with Set present in the stable, they relaxed, adopting easy postures.

Though Vihera had witnessed this scene many times, she knew that unless a horse had undergone special training, it wouldn't remain completely unfazed by Set's presence from the start.

After explaining about the horses, Vihera turned her gaze back to Rei, who was walking.

"That's precisely why Rei sends Set out on reconnaissance like that. So the horses Warain rides won't get scared."

"...Rei is kind, isn't she?"

Mina murmured thoughtfully after hearing Vihera's explanation.

Seeing Mina like this, Vihera's smile, which had been present until just now, shifted to something vaguely ambiguous.

"Well, yes. If we're talking about kindness, then yes, he is kind... But it's also an undeniable fact that, as Rei said, he's not good with people. In reality, Rei shows no mercy to those who oppose him."

Vihera had heard about various incidents Rei had caused.

For instance, during the war with the Bestir Empire, he severed the limbs of an allied noble who attacked him.

Though, after gathering accurate information later, it turned out to be both arms, not all four limbs.

Vihera was well aware that rumors tended to grow exaggerated as they spread.

But gathering that detailed information was quite a struggle, costing him not just gold coins, but even platinum coins.

It was only natural. Very few people were present where the nobleman's arms were severed.

The fact he managed to obtain the information at all meant someone had leaked it.

Apparently, the man who sold the information to Vihera had been asked by an acquaintance who was both a subordinate of Marquis Larks and had been requested by the noble himself to stage a little performance. The man laughed as he explained it.

...But if you ask who suffered the most in this affair, it was undoubtedly that nobleman.

Because when Vihera asked Rei about it, he genuinely had forgotten.

Vihera recounted everything she knew about the incident from back then, and only then did he finally remember.

"An adversary, you say? ...Is there really anyone who would oppose someone as strong as Rei?"

For Mina, having seen Rei's true strength up close, she couldn't imagine anyone wanting to antagonize him.

At the very least, Mina herself would never want to be his enemy.

"Yes. That's just the kind of personality he has, you see? That's why he inevitably ends up with so many enemies."

Some time had passed since Vihera had come to know Rei as a person.

That's precisely why she knew it wasn't unusual for Rei to be disliked by various people.

(Though it's also true that some people actually like that attitude of Rei.)

For example, herself. For example, Daskar.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

As he pondered such things, Set's cry suddenly echoed from ahead.

「Rei！」

"I know!"

Even before Vihera finished speaking, Rei had already kicked off the ground and dashed forward... toward the direction Set's cry had come from.

Seeing this, Vihera immediately followed after Rei.

"I'm going to check it out. You two stay here and guard Walrain! If any monsters appear, call us!"

He shouted to Mina and Giacomo.

He told them to call for Rei and the others if another monster appeared while Rei and the others were focused on whatever was happening ahead.

"H-hey. Hey. What should we do?"

Left in charge, Giacomo asked Mina, his partner, with concern.

Normally, he would have some confidence in his abilities, given that he was escorting the Walein to Gilm at this critical time.

But the shock of being attacked by a goblin horde and having the carriage overturned must have been too much. His voice sounded somewhat uneasy.

"Listen, it's not like we're supposed to protect Mr. Warain all by ourselves forever, right? So, until Mr. Vihera and the others get back, we should be able to manage somehow. Mr. Warain!"

"Yes. We'll leave the escort to you, then."

Walein moved her horse closer to Giacomo and Mina, smiling as she said this.

Even though they'd lost the combat strength of Rei and Vihera, Warain showed no signs of panic unlike Giacomo. Was it a matter of nerve, or did she figure she could escape quickly on horseback if push came to shove?

Whatever the reason, Walein was calmer than Giacomo, his escort.

Seeing her partner like this, Mina sighed.

"Look, never mind. We'll keep watch around here."

"But wait. If more goblins show up, we won't have a carriage this time, right? If that happens..."

...We wouldn't stand a chance. Giacomo's face twisted in despair.

Facing certain death against the goblins had broken Giacomo's spirit.

(He was all smiles when Vihera was around... No, by focusing on Vihera, he was just avoiding facing his own weakness. Besides, it's true we don't have a carriage now.)

Mina and the others had their carriage overturned before Rei and the others could help them, but standing in front of the fallen carriage prevented attacks from behind.

But that carriage was now inside Rei's Misty Ring.

If goblins attacked, they'd have to worry about being assaulted from all directions.

Mina wanted to avoid that.

(But...)

She turned her gaze toward the direction Rei and Vihera had gone.

This time, if she called, Rei and the others would likely come immediately.

That was far more reliable than fighting with the carriage behind them.

"There's no guarantee monsters will definitely attack! Look, for now, we just need to keep watch around us!"

In a pinch, having reliable combat power was essential.

Feeling relieved by that thought, Mina encouraged her partner.

Time rewound slightly.

To avoid frightening the horses, Set had gone ahead of Rei and the others to scout the surroundings. That's when it happened.

Instead of flying through the sky as usual, Set was walking along the road when he suddenly fixed his sharp gaze on a solitary tree standing nearby.

If it had been just a tree, Set wouldn't have fixed such a sharp gaze upon it.

But... Set's gaze had firmly locked onto the presence of something lurking behind that tree.

Sticking out from the tree was unmistakably a tail. Moreover, from the height of the tail and, above all, the familiar scent wafting from it, he understood without doubt that it was a werewolf.

It was unusual for a werewolf, creatures that often lived in packs, to be alone.

Still, since it was hiding behind the tree, Set thought it was undoubtedly trying to attack them...

"Grr?"

He tilted his head slightly, caught off guard.

The scent drifting from behind the tree seemed slightly different than it had a few seconds ago.

Alongside the beastly scent typical of a werewolf, there was something else—a smell like someone drenched in sweat.

Using his heightened sense of smell made it crystal clear.

Intrigued, Set used optical camouflage to approach the tree... and behind it, he discovered the figure of a werewolf, its body shrouded in black mist, Ambrus.

Having found the very being they sought, Set let out a high-pitched howl to alert Rei of this discovery.
