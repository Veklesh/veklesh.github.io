"Mmm... hmm... hmm?"

As the morning sun streamed through the window, Rei awoke in bed.

But he looked around completely unsure of where he was.

Rei tilted his head slightly at the unfamiliar surroundings, but his mind was still half-asleep, so he stopped moving.

If this were during a mission, on the battlefield... or if there were any signs of killing intent, he would have woken instantly.

But right now, Rei was completely alone in this room.

That's precisely why, even in his half-asleep state, there was no one to scold or warn Rei.

About ten minutes passed like that... until finally, Rei's mind cleared.

Then Rei remembered where he was.

"Ah, right. That's right. I came to the dungeon."

Remembering that he had come to the dungeon to restore the consciousnesses of Vihera and Rodos, and that he had defeated the boss monster, the Silver Lion, at the lowest level, he gently reached for his ribs.

It was the spot where he'd taken a blow through the Dragon Robe during the fight with the Silver Lion, causing a dull ache that hadn't quite reached the level of a fracture.

Fortunately, or perhaps inevitably, a night's sleep had already completely erased the pain.

It was likely only possible because of Rei's body, which possessed high natural healing abilities.

Feeling relieved by that fact, she quickly finished getting ready.

Glancing out the window, she saw the sun had already fully risen.

It was far too late to call this morning, he thought, pulling his pocket watch from the Misty Ring. It showed the time was already close to ten in the morning.

Back in Japan, this would still be considered the morning, but in this world, people generally went to bed earlier and rose earlier than they did in Japan.

...Of course. Magic items that provided light, while affordable enough for ordinary people to buy, weren't cheap enough to purchase as many as one desired.

That's precisely why a lifestyle of going to bed early and rising early was the norm.

Given that it was past ten in the morning in such a lifestyle, Rei might as well consider it close to noon.

"Well, there's no need to rush into anything anyway."

If Vihera were still unconscious, there might not have been the luxury of saying something like that.

But Vihera was awake now.

What's more, considering he'd been unconscious for nearly two months, he was unbelievably energetic.

"...Ah, I see. Despite everything, I guess I was still worried about Vihera after all."

Rei concluded that the reason he'd slept in this late for the first time in ages was probably because he felt relieved now that the matter concerning Vihera, who he'd been worried about, had been settled. He gave a wry smile at his own obviousness.

Stepping out of the bedroom, he suddenly felt something off.

Looking around to figure out what it was, he understood immediately.

There was no sign of anyone else.

This room had a living area where one could relax, and from there, two separate bedrooms led off.

But right now, there was no sign of anyone in this entire space, bedrooms included.

"Where are Elk and Rodos? ...Come to think of it, they said they were coming home today. Did they already leave?"

Rei thought they could have at least told him if they were leaving... but then he spotted a piece of paper on the table in the living room.

It was Elk's handwriting.

It simply stated that he had tried to wake Rei several times before leaving, but since he hadn't woken up at all, he was leaving as planned.

And at the end, there was a renewed expression of gratitude for helping Rodos regain consciousness.

"Ah... I really was out cold. I never imagined I'd be sleeping so soundly that even being woken up wouldn't wake me."

Realizing Elk had tried to wake him, Ray placed the note back on the table.

If Elk had truly tried to wake him by any means necessary, just radiating killing intent would have made Rei jump up.

But Elk had judged it unnecessary to go that far, choosing instead to leave this note.

Rei reflected a little on how out of it he'd been, but decided it was fine once in a while and left the room.

His destination was the cafeteria.

He decided to have breakfast for now.

Or perhaps an early lunch.

Either way, at this hour, the cafeteria should have been empty... or so he thought. Unexpectedly, it was surprisingly crowded with customers.

"Huh? ...Ah."

Why at this hour? Rei wondered, but looking around the cafeteria, the reason became immediately clear.

Probably to avoid getting in the way. Three strikingly beautiful women had caught his eye at the far end of the cafeteria.

Though not quite as stunning as those beauties, there were also women with faces that were still quite attractive by ordinary standards, and the form of a small black dragon.

This group, bound to draw attention no matter what, was so out of place that no one dared approach them.

Perhaps it was because someone knew the true identities of those individuals... the Duchess known as the Princess General, the Guildmaster of Gilm, the former Imperial Family of the Bestir Empire.

(No, the first two aside, it's unlikely anyone knows about Vihera.)

Originally, Vihera rarely appeared at official events as royalty and had fled the Bestir Empire several years ago.

Few people would possess that information in the first place, and even fewer who did would recognize Vihera's face in the Kingdom of Mireana... especially in the frontier, in a dungeon rather than Gilm.

Even so, regardless of Vihera's circumstances, her appearance alone was enough to draw people's attention.

(The fact that no one was hitting on her was probably thanks to Elena and Marina after all.)

Though nearly every seat in the cafeteria was occupied, no one actually seemed to be talking to Elena and the others.

Well, maybe there had been some at first, but at least now, there was no sign of them anywhere.

Amidst the somewhat peculiar atmosphere... Rei made no particular effort to avoid it as he headed toward the table where Elena and the others were seated.

Those seated around them shot Rei glances like he was a fool, but Rei didn't seem to care about those looks at all.

And naturally, Elena and the others noticed Rei approaching... The moment after, the surrounding customers, who had expected to be cut down with words, found themselves doubting their eyes.

"Rei, you're quite late today. I thought you might get up a little earlier."

Elena spoke to Rei with a smile, sweeping back her golden hair.

And Elena wasn't the only one smiling. Marina and Vihera were beaming with genuine happiness, clearly visible to everyone around them. As for Ara, while her smile wasn't quite as bright as the other three, she was smiling nonetheless.

"Kyu-kyu! Kyuu!"

Yellow, who had been eating a sandwich on the table, also let out a happy chirp upon seeing Rei.

No, not just that—it kicked the table, flapping its tiny wings desperately, and flew straight toward Rei.

"Whoa, what's up? You're being pretty clingy today. If you want to play with Set, you can go over to the stables, you know?"

Ray stroked Yellow, enjoying the cool, hard texture of its scales as he spoke.

Whether Yellow heard Rei's words or not, it didn't matter. Satisfied with being petted, Yellow kicked Rei and returned to the table, sinking its small fangs into the half-eaten sandwich.

Seeing Yellow like that, Rei smiled and sat down at the table where Elena and the others were seated.

"So, why did you oversleep?"

When Rei didn't answer Elena's question, Vihera asked again.

"Well, the reason I overslept... yesterday was pretty rough. I guess I was just tired."

He didn't say he was relieved about Vihera's situation; he dodged the question, probably to hide his embarrassment.

That said, it wasn't entirely wrong.

The battle against the Silver Lion had been one of the most intense fights Rei had ever experienced.

Even with the combined strength of many, including Elk, they barely managed to defeat it. The fact remained that he had pushed himself to the absolute limit.

That encounter had made Rei keenly aware just how troublesome formidable defensive power could be.

On top of that, according to what Grimm had said, there were apparently many more Silver Lions out there. That was more than enough to make Rei want to be able to defeat one alone someday.

(The materials... Fortunately, most were given to me. So, the next time I defeat a Silver Lion, I'll share those materials with Elena and the others.)

Since I'd received most of the materials—nearly all of them, really—I intended to repay that favor.

He considered it the utmost expression of gratitude he could offer.

After casually ordering breakfast from the waiter who came to take their order, Rei spoke up.

"So, Elk and Rodos weren't in my room anymore..."

"Ah, Elk and the others left early this morning. They wanted to get Rhodes to rest in Gilm as soon as possible, right?"

"I know that. Elk left a note in the room. ...I really wanted to see them off, though. Apparently, even when Elk tried to wake me gently, I didn't stir at all."

"...Not waking up even when Elk called out to him? Isn't that kind of bad for an adventurer?"

"I kinda think so too."

He replied to Vihera, then reached for his fork toward the breakfast—or rather, the surprisingly large portion of it—that had been brought over.

As expected of a high-class inn, it would probably be quite delicious if I just savored it normally.

He understood that, but still, remembering the taste of the Silver Lion meat dish he'd eaten the previous day, he couldn't help but feel something was lacking.

...Seeing Rei's attitude, they must have guessed what he was thinking. Elena and the others, including Ara, all wore similar smiles.

Elena spoke for them all.

"The food here isn't bad. No, I'd say it's delicious. But... even so, it feels lacking."

"...Do you understand?"

"Of course you do. The feeling of something missing that Rei is experiencing right now... everyone here feels it too..."

Elena paused her words there. On the table, Yellow had finished his sandwich and was now sinking his fangs into the remaining stir-fried meat.

The sight of her happily wagging her tail was hard to reconcile with the image of a dragonkin child, said to be the pinnacle of monsters.

Of course, Yellow was a being Elena had created using dragon language magic, not a genuine dragon.

"It's something everyone here shares, except for Yellow."

"Kyuu?"

Realizing his name had been called, Yellow tilted his head slightly and turned his gaze toward Elena.

Stroking Yellow's head, Elena looked at Rei, as if to say, "Right?"

Rei shrugged his shoulders slightly at Elena and the others, his posture clearly saying, "You're right."

"The food at this place should be plenty delicious too."

He had fully understood that when he stayed at this inn and ate here the day before yesterday and yesterday.

But now, he recalled the taste of the Silver Lion's cuisine from yesterday and couldn't help but compare it.

The downside of food being too delicious... one might call it.

No, since the owner of the Oak Stew Inn hadn't yet discovered a cooking method that truly brought out the Silver Lion's meat's qualities, perhaps in this case it should be called the drawback of ingredients that are too delicious.

"Yeah... Well, I'll forget that taste eventually. Though, with Rei having so much Silver Lion meat, that part worries me."

"Once we get back to Gilm and Johanna and the others return, I'll need to serve them some meat. So I can't really eat too much myself."

"Listen. That thing was that big, you know? Normally, wouldn't you say it's impossible to finish eating it all?"

Vihera, who had been listening to Rei and Elena's conversation, remarked with a hint of exasperation.

A Silver Lion six meters long... even without the tail, it's four meters long. The sheer amount of meat is more than one or two people could normally handle... or in this case, Set, one person and one beast.

In any case, it was far too much for just Rei and Set to finish. ...Under normal circumstances, that is.

Knowing Rei and Set's appetites, the silver lion's meat would vanish in a matter of days.

It wouldn't be surprising if someone thought that.

No, it was inevitable.

"...More importantly, what are we doing today? Should we just head back after all?"

Feeling the conversation was turning against him, Rei changed the subject... Everyone agreed there was no particular reason to stay any longer, and it was decided rather easily that they would return to Gilm.
