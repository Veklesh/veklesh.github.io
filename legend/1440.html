"Um... what do you call this feeling again? Déjà vu?"

Rei sighed and muttered as he looked at the man standing before him.

Behind him, Vihera gave a wry smile at Rei's words, Byune remained expressionless as ever, and Marina wore a somewhat troubled smile.

And before Rei stood a man with a physique and muscles worthy of being called a giant, his fists clenched as he glared at Rei.

"Hey, Danza. Stop it! That's Rei, you know!?"

The men behind the man standing before Rei—the one called Danza—shouted, trying desperately to stop him from doing something foolish.

But Danza paid no heed whatsoever to his comrades' words and opened his mouth.

"You're partying with Marina? Ha! Talk about not knowing your place."

Danza shouted to provoke him, but Rei completely ignored his words and asked Marina behind him.

"...Was there a lumberjack like that?"

"Seems he came to Gilm yesterday."

"Ah... I see. Well, you can never have too many woodcutters, I suppose."

Considering the vastness of Treant Forest, it was difficult for the woodcutters in Gilm alone to clear it all.

Even so, since there was no need to dry the felled timber, it was somewhat easier compared to cutting ordinary trees... Still, the Treant Forest was simply too vast.

To address that, it was only natural for Gilm to request lumberjacks from elsewhere.

And the man standing before Rei now, clenching his fists, Danza, was one such lumberjack.

(I haven't been gone from Gilm for that long, though. ...Well, I suppose it can't be helped with Gilm as it is now.)

Sighing, Rei took a fighting stance against the man.

"Heh, it's wrong for a runt like you to hog a hot chick like Marina all to yourself, huh? I'll teach you your place."

"Ah... Yeah. Got it. So just get on with it."

Unlike with Melan, Rei saw this man was radiating hostility toward him. Sighing, he stepped forward.

Danza's expression flickered with surprise at the slow, deliberate movement, but he quickly interpreted it as contempt.

A vein throbbed on his forehead as he swung his fist hard toward Rei, who was approaching him...

"You idiot!"

In the next instant, moving from his slow pace to appear before Danza in a flash, Rei swung his fist, grazing Danza's jaw with the blow.

Danza staggered back several steps from the blow... but smiled, realizing the attack had no effect on him. Yet the next instant, he collapsed unconscious to the ground.

"Well, that's the difference between an amateur and a pro, I suppose."

"I suppose so."

Vihera murmured in response to Rei's words, and Marina nodded in agreement.

Amateurs, when punching someone, instinctively pull their hand back to build momentum.

But someone accustomed to striking others understands that this creates a significant opening.

The moment between the wind-up and the actual strike creates an opening lasting a second, or less... And for Rei, that brief window was more than enough time to act.

No, that wasn't limited to Rei alone. Most adventurers with a certain level of skill could probably do it.

"Well, now that we've dealt with that hassle... since running into Marina here worked out perfectly, is it okay if we go to her place instead of the tavern?"

"Huh? Sure, why not. Why?"

"I've got a few presents for you."

"Oh?"

It wasn't Marina who reacted to Rei's words... but Vihera.

"Gifts? Just for Marina?"

"...Well, if Vihera wants one, I could give it to her, but I think she'd probably say she doesn't need it?"

From Rei's perspective, he couldn't imagine Vihera wanting any kind of magic item resembling an art piece.

"Let's discuss the details once we get home. Rei, go ahead. I'll report to the guild and then head over."

With that, Marina flashed Rei a smile that could melt any man and headed back the way they had come.

"That guy... he got left behind. Is that okay?"

Rei suddenly noticed Danz, unconscious and collapsed on the ground, and muttered this, but quickly lost interest.

Seeing several adventurers, or those who appeared to be, carrying him into the shade probably played a big part in that.

"Anyway, change of plans. Let's wait at Marina's house."

Nodding at Vihera's words, Rei headed toward Marina's mansion with Set and Byune.

"...It's surprisingly tense here."

Upon entering the noble district, Rei sensed that the adventurers and private guards patrolling the area were unexpectedly on edge.

The moment they spotted a figure, they tensed up. But when they recognized the familiar face—Rei—they visibly relaxed.

Slightly surprised that seeing him elicited such relief, Rei murmured to himself. Vihera then spoke up, as if noticing something.

"Oh, didn't you know, Rei? Among the people who've come to Gilm for this expansion project, it seems there are some with rather questionable conduct."

Rei felt a slight twinge of doubt at the mention of "bad behavior," but quickly understood.

With so many people arriving at Gilm, it was only natural that there would be many unfamiliar faces.

It wouldn't be surprising if some tried to take advantage of that to steal.

Of course, if they tried such tricks and were discovered by Gilm's undercover organization, they'd likely face punishment.

"So, are those kinds of people getting into the Noble District?"

"Yes. Fortunately, they're caught before actually breaking into noble houses... But then, how much of this is actually true?"

"Yeah."

Rei understood what Vihera was getting at.

Catching them before they infiltrated the mansion was perfect for showing off how capable his mansion's security was. But if someone snuck in, stole something, and got away... that would be a huge disgrace.

Few would willingly publicize such a disgrace.

Of course, if something like that happened, they'd probably be secretly tracking the intruders.

"That's why things are pretty tense in the Noble Quarter right now. The adventurers guarding it would get scolded by their employers if the Noble Quarter were breached, and apparently their standing with the guild would take a hit too."

"Hmm."

Vihera nodded at Byune's words.

It certainly wasn't just Rei's imagination that the usually expressionless face looked faintly displeased.

Rei could imagine something had happened while he was away.

"People gathering together isn't always a good thing, huh?"

"True. I wouldn't say everyone who gathers is perfectly polite... but if they don't actively cause trouble, things would be much easier."

But in reality, it wasn't that simple.

Many people came to Gilm specifically for crime, drawn by the increased population from the expansion.

Especially since Gilm was the sole frontier of the Mireana Kingdom. Its great wealth attracted many seeking a life-changing score.

Naturally, such individuals are almost always eliminated by Gilm's guards, adventurers like Vihera patrolling the streets, or members of the underworld who dislike their turf being invaded and unwanted trouble being stirred up.

...The fact that it was "most" and not "all" was precisely what troubled the current guards.

While most were caught, only those truly gifted with ability, talent, or luck succeeded in crimes like theft.

"Well, even so, sneaking into Marina's house would be impossible, though."

Recalling the security measures at Marina's house—what might be called spirit magic-based defense capabilities—Rei muttered.

Vihera and Byune nodded in agreement.

Set, being petted by Rei as they walked, seemed particularly unfazed, perhaps enjoying it.

"Anyone reckless enough to sneak into Marina's house would have Marina's terror... no, engraved into their heart."

"Oh my, that's quite a way of putting it. It makes it sound like I'm the one at fault."

Marina, who they had parted with earlier saying she was heading to the guild, was standing there.

She wore that quintessential Marina smile, a radiant expression distilling feminine charm, but fellow party members Rei and Vihera noticed a hint of mischief glinting in her eyes.

"...You're back surprisingly early. I thought it would take you a bit longer."

Vihera looked momentarily surprised, but quickly composed herself and said,

"Well, Rei came back. Of course I'd show up early, wouldn't I?"

Marina said it as if it were nothing. Vihera gave a resigned smile before speaking.

"So, how did things go in Treant Forest?"

"Business as usual. Nothing out of the ordinary happened."

Marina stated this, but considering the woodcutter who'd just been beaten up by Rei, even if she said there were no problems, it was hard to believe.

Then again, both Marina and Vihera were used to men hitting on them.

At the same time, their methods for dealing with it had already reached a level of expertise.

...Well, Marina aside, Vihera's approach was rather brutal: she told them she had no interest in weaker opponents and that if they wanted to court her, they'd have to fight and win.

"So, how did it go with Rei?"

"Ah, well... Actually..."

Rei started to explain the incident in the Lerdact County, but stopped mid-sentence when a familiar face approached from the direction they were heading.

At the same time, they must have noticed Rei too.

Seeing the figure made him tense for a moment... but upon realizing it was Rei with Set, he broke into a delighted smile, called out, and approached Rei.

"Captain Rei! Long time no see!"

"...So, I'm not the captain anymore, right? It's been ages since I was with the Roving Squad. You should get used to it by now."

Rei sighed as he said this.

The people before him were those who had fought alongside Rei as part of the guerrilla force during the civil war in the Bestir Empire.

After that civil war ended, they came to Gilm with Rei and now work as adventurers.

...Their reasons for coming to Gilm varied, but one thing they all shared was an absolute desire to avoid antagonizing Rei.

Having witnessed Rei's fighting up close during the civil war, they likely felt it was dangerous to remain in the Bestir Empire, which had long been at odds with the Mireana Kingdom Rei belonged to.

Of course, there were other reasons too, like their admiration for Rei.

"It's wonderful to see you well, Lord Vihera."

The adventurers greeted Vihera at the same time as Rei.

Being from the Bestir Empire, they were likely delighted to speak with Vihera, who enjoyed great popularity among the people.

Their cheeks flushed with excitement at the chance to speak with Vihera, they offered their greetings.

"Speaking of which, is it just you lot today? I don't see Johanna anywhere."

Johanna, who possessed affection for Set that rivaled even Milein of the Scorching Wind, was nowhere to be seen.

Noticing that no one had rushed over to Set first, Rei asked the others.

"Huh? Oh, yes. Johanna's handling work over by the wall."

The former guerrilla squad wasn't just a handful of people.

They numbered in the dozens.

Naturally, it was impossible for all of them to operate as a single party.

As a result, the former guerrilla members split into several separate parties.

Of course, since quite some time had passed since arriving in Gilm, many had joined parties outside the former guerrilla group.

Yet, the group currently standing before Rei was comprised entirely of former members of the guerrilla squad.

"I see. So, are you guys patrolling the Noble District?"

"Yes. ...Well, we might look like this, but we're actually pretty competent, you know?"

"I'm sure you are. Not just anyone gets assigned to patrol this area."

Being entrusted with patrolling the Noble District naturally requires individuals worthy of trust.

The guild absolutely must avoid hiring someone harboring questionable motives who might then steal, assault nobles... or commit similar acts.

"It means a lot to hear you say that, Rei-san."

He said it with genuine delight, and for a while, Rei and the former rangers enjoyed lively conversation.
