"...That was unexpected, huh. I was sure we'd be stuck there for a few more days."

Walking along the highway, Rei lifted his gaze toward the sky.

Spreading out above him was a summery, clear blue sky.

Yesterday, the rain had fallen without stopping even once, but today's weather felt like a lie.

No one could have possibly predicted this current situation.

"Yeah. They say summer weather is fickle, and that's exactly what happened. ...Though, for us, it was an unexpectedly pleasant surprise, so we really can't complain."

Elena, walking beside Rei on either side of Set, said this while stroking Yellow, who was sleeping in the sun on Set's back.

In truth, Rei had no complaints about yesterday's weather turning into a crisp, clear day as if yesterday's weather had been a lie.

Still... the road wasn't made of solid concrete like in Japan.

There were potholes in various places, and many spots had puddles from yesterday's rain.

Furthermore, with a group of nearly a thousand people, not everyone was walking on the main road. Some walked slightly off the beaten path.

Those people had to walk through areas with even more puddles than the main road.

Many wore the clothes they'd made yesterday, and the number of those dressed as prostitutes had decreased significantly compared to the day before yesterday.

"Maybe we don't need to ask the Zolge Trading Company for cloth and clothes after all."

"Shouldn't we take them? Clothes and cloth aren't things we'd have trouble carrying, right?"

Marina, walking a short distance away, responded to Rei's mutter.

Normally, even receiving a thousand sets of clothes and cloth would be a problem finding space to store them.

But in Rei's case, there was the Misty Ring.

Whether it was enough for a thousand people, two thousand people... even ten thousand people's worth of cloth and clothes, he could easily store it all.

Moreover, time doesn't flow within the Misty Ring.

That meant deterioration from the passage of years simply didn't occur.

Given that, Marina's words rang true: since it might be useful someday, it's better to take it if offered. Rei glanced back.

"Honestly, having this happen over and over again isn't exactly fun."

Moving with a few dozen people was one thing, but leading nearly a thousand—most of them untrained—was a huge burden for Rei.

(We moved with even larger groups during the war with the Bestir Empire. ...But back then, everyone was reasonably skilled and had stamina, so it was easier.)

Our movement speed was faster back then too.

Given that, he couldn't help but think their current pace was slow.

"Well, it probably won't happen dozens or hundreds of times, but there's a chance it might happen a few more times, right?"

"Not exactly thrilled about that."

This time, Rei had ended up leading this group somewhat by circumstance, but if he were told to handle such troublesome matters repeatedly, he certainly couldn't just nod and agree.

Honestly, he wanted this to be the last time.

"I agree with Rei. The giant was reasonably strong, but not satisfying enough. ...If possible, I'd rather fight stronger enemies."

Next to speak up was Vihera.

Regarding this particular request, since the enemy was a shadowy organization from the small country of Reburina, she hadn't expected they'd get to fight such a powerful foe...

Still, she must have held onto a sliver of hope.

For Vihera, the battle against the giants was the sole highlight of this mission... but even then, it was only barely acceptable, compensating for quality with quantity.

It was nowhere near the kind of battle that could truly satisfy Vihera.

"Gurrrrr!"

As they spoke and moved forward, Set suddenly let out a cry.

His gaze fell upon a wild boar running across the grassland for some reason.

Of course, there was no rule against boars running near the road. There wasn't... but even so, boars typically moved through woods or forests.

Because, for those with sufficient skill, a boar was literally nothing more than prey.

Their meat is vastly more delicious than preserved foods like jerky or hardened bread, which travelers prioritize for their shelf life. ...Though some travelers are disastrously bad cooks.

Still, generally speaking, wild boar are the ultimate game.

This held true for Rei and the others now as well...

"Go, Set!"

"GRRRR!"

At Rei's command, Set immediately charged toward the prey.

The boar must have sensed Set's presence as it gave chase.

It began running even faster than just seconds before.

...Of course. It was only natural. The boar, nothing more than a mere wild animal, no monster at all, stood no chance against Set.

Facing Set, it must have instinctively realized there was absolutely no way it could survive.

But... the moment it was spotted by Set, the boar's fate was sealed.

A boar's running speed is roughly fifty kilometers per hour.

Considering its weight, it could never sustain that speed over long distances, only short sprints.

As a result, Set easily caught up to the boar. With a single blow from his front paw, he shattered the boar's neck bones, extinguishing its life.

The boar's body, its neck snapped at full running speed, plowed into the ground with that momentum.

But even that movement stopped when Set caught up to the body again and pinned it down with his front paws.

"GRRRRRR!"

Set growled happily at having taken down the boar with a single strike...

'Huh?'

From the woods a short distance away... the very woods the boar had burst from, several men emerged, exclaiming in surprise.

Watching them from the road, Rei, who was carrying a bow, momentarily wondered if they were pursuers sent by Jaya or Rossi? He grew wary... but judging by the men's clothing, he quickly realized they weren't.

(Hunters, huh? Of course, the possibility that they're pursuers disguised as hunters can't be completely ruled out yet.)

Just in case, just in case, Rei didn't completely let down his guard against the men.

But seeing them stare dumbstruck at the boar Set was carrying—easily over a hundred kilograms—made him strongly suspect they were just hunters.

"Hey, go ask those guys who they are."

"Got it!"

The resistance man immediately responded to Rei's words and left.

Watching him go, Rei wondered if he should have said something to Sura... but the resistance fighter was completely oblivious to Rei's expression.

While Rei technically led this group, he didn't command the Resistance itself.

Given that, the proper way to issue orders to the Resistance should be through Sura.

But the resistance man followed Rei's instructions without a hint of unease.

As Rei pondered this, his gaze followed the resistance man as he approached what appeared to be hunters.

While they conversed, Set returned to Rei, dragging a boar behind him.

"Grr!"

He set the boar he'd been carrying down on the ground and looked up at Ray, as if to say, "Well?"

Rei stowed the boar in his Misty Ring and gave Set a firm pat on the head.

Pleased by the praise, Set rubbed his face against Rei.

Of course, they kept walking even while doing this, so properly praising Set... well, that wasn't really possible.

Still, having been praised by Rei, Set seemed perfectly satisfied.

He walked beside Rei, purring contentedly.

Of course, they wouldn't butcher the boar Set had caught right now.

That would be done later at camp by those with free hands.

...While those raised in villages might be different, among women raised in towns or cities—places with many people—few had naturally done things like butchering animals. Some even vomited at the sight.

But even so, Rei... and those in positions of authority didn't exempt the women who disliked it from the task.

This wasn't out of spite or anything like that. Considering the journey ahead until they reached Gilm, it was simply a matter of ensuring everyone was capable of handling such tasks.

In Rei's case, she was accustomed to butchering monsters, so she felt no particular aversion to handling wild game.

That's precisely why he assumed others would be fine too... His actions stemmed from that kind of optimistic feeling. Still, he knew some would faint at the sight of blood, and he was already dreading the hassle ahead.

Originally, Rei had helped butcher chickens his father raised back in Japan, so he didn't have much resistance to that sort of thing...

(Come to think of it, even in manga, there are scenes where people are scared to dissect frogs for experiments and can't do it...)

As he pondered this, the Resistance members soon arrived at Rei's side, bringing the hunter men with them.

"Rei, it seems these men are hunters from a village a little ways from here."

"I see. I'd like to stop by if possible... but how far away exactly is this place?"

The hunters snapped back to attention at Rei's question.

Not that it was an unusual reaction.

After yesterday's incident, many were now wearing ordinary clothes, albeit rough ones, but there were still quite a few who remained in their prostitute attire.

The fact that this group was leading such women made them extremely conspicuous.

What's more, the one leading them was someone as short as Rei. Even the Resistance men, who were clearly more robust than Rei at first glance, showed no sign of underestimating him.

And beside Rei stood the griffin that had easily dispatched the boar they'd been chasing—or rather, let escape—plus three stunningly beautiful women they'd never seen before.

Where to even begin? It was utterly bewildering. Faced with such a group, it was only natural the hunters were flustered.

"Ahem."

The Resistance man, understanding the hunters' feelings, cleared his throat softly to get their attention.

One of the hunters, finally snapping back to reality at the sound, opened his mouth, sounding confused by Rei's words.

"From here, it should be a few hours' journey."

"Along the road? That would be helpful."

"No, it's not. Our village is off the road."

Normally, when building a village, people choose a location that's easy for others to pass through.

That's because if a village is near the road, travelers passing by will stop and spend money there.

Merchants also have no qualms about such locations, unlike villages deep in the mountains.

(So why build a village out of the way? There must be a reason. Maybe I shouldn't go there after all. But if I can buy supplies, I'd like to get at least a little something.)

Since it's still morning, and yesterday's rain forced us to rest, going out of our way to spend several hours traveling to a village off the main road would just be a waste of time.

But I do want to restock supplies.

After considering this, Rei came to a conclusion...

"I'd like to go to that village alone to ask for supplies. Is that okay? ...Ideally, I'd want a horse too, but since it's just me, that's probably impossible. Cloth, clothes, food, and if there's any fodder or a cart I could sell, that would be helpful."

"Ah, ah. I suppose that's fine... But just you? That's..."

One of the hunters glanced at Set, who was being stroked by Byune beside Rei.

Seeing that made it clear how Rei intended to get to the hunters' village.

Rei nodded back at the hunters.

"Yeah, I'll ride Set. ...If I say I heard about it from you guys over there, will they believe me?"

Rei understood that if he tried to go to the village alone on Set, it would obviously arouse suspicion.

"Thanks for the consideration. I'm Albad. If you say you heard about it from me, they shouldn't treat you coldly over there."

The hunters, perhaps noticing Rei's consideration, bowed their heads in response.

Rei was slightly surprised by the hunters' reaction.

After all, they hadn't witnessed his power firsthand.

Given that, it wouldn't have been strange if they looked down on him...

...Though, seeing him command a griffin and still daring to show contempt would make them either exceptionally powerful or utterly foolish, unable to grasp the situation...

After that exchange, Rei took flight with Set toward the direction Alvad had indicated.
