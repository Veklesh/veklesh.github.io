Several days after the snow barbecue party... As expected, everyone who attended that party and ate the silver lion meat left satisfied.

But, as was to be expected, that very night, the next day... and for some, even longer periods afterward... they found themselves unable to feel satisfied by their meals.

They lived through such days, but eventually, as time passed, the lingering effects of the Silver Lion's meat faded away.

And just as everyone was returning to their daily lives... Rei found himself summoned by Mayo for some reason.

"Sorry about this, Rei. Making you come out specially."

"No, it's fine. I was planning to drop by sometime soon anyway. ...But you look a bit thinner than before, don't you?"

"Hahaha. ...Well, things happened."

At Rei's words, Mayo offered a somewhat dry laugh as he brushed it off.

Mayo, a giant standing about 180 cm tall and weighing around 100 kg, seemed to have narrowed slightly in width from Rei's perspective, and his face also appeared a bit thinner than before.

"Things... You didn't even show up to the party the other day. Are you really okay?"

"Well... To be honest, things haven't been going too well."

"...I thought your trading company was pretty big. Is it really a situation you can't handle?"

"Well, yeah. It's true that being big brings its own set of problems."

Mayo looked uncomfortable as she wiped sweat from her brow and sighed, despite the snow falling outside.

He was probably sweating, but more than that, Mayo was clearly quite stressed out about the guild's current situation.

"So, Mayo, with all these issues, what brings you here to see me?"

"...It's embarrassing to say, but the only one who can save our merchant guild from the crisis it's currently in is you, Rei-san."

"Me? Why does my name come up here?"

It was true that Rei was working hard to be able to enjoy eating goblin meat alongside Mayo, but he wasn't specifically helping Mayo's merchant guild... or rather, the guild run by Mayo's father.

Rei couldn't understand why his name was being brought up here.

"Spier Trading Company. Does that name ring a bell?"

「……Spier Trading Company？」

Rei tilted his head at the name that came out of Mayo's mouth.

It was a name he felt he should recognize.

"And also the name Viscount Linus Marnino."

"Huh? That's..."

Recalling the name mentioned earlier at the guard post, Rei remembered the man's face.

The man who had made an unreasonable request, held a grudge when Rei refused, and attacked Rei and Elena while they were in her carriage.

And he also recognized the name Spier Trading Company.

A merchant named Tris had approached Rei, wanting to purchase materials from the Silver Lion.

While the number of merchants who had approached Rei was considerable, the reason he remembered Tris... and the name Spier Trading Company, was likely because Tris was different from the other merchants.

The other merchants had approached Rei immediately upon learning he had conquered the dungeon and returned to Gilm.

But Tris had arrived after the commotion of numerous merchants contacting him had subsided.

Considering the goal of buying Silver Lion materials, it was clearly a poor strategy.

But... in terms of making an impression on Rei, it had clearly worked.

In fact, while Rei barely remembered the faces of the numerous merchants who had approached him, he remembered Tris clearly.

Of course, that was likely influenced by Tris being quite different from the other merchants.

A merchant whose eyes didn't smile even when his mouth did was someone Rei couldn't help but notice.

That was precisely why Rei left such a strong impression on Rei.

But why were both of them appearing here now? That was the question Rei found himself asking.

"How on earth are those two connected? And to your trading house, no less?"

"Well, the Marnino Viscount family and the Spier Trading Company are, simply put, in a relationship of borrower and lender."

"...Debt?"

He recalled the Marnino Viscount's residence he'd been invited to before—not the main mansion, but a villa or secondary residence—and it certainly hadn't seemed like a place burdened by debt.

"It seems the Marquis Marnino undertook large-scale flood control works on his estate. He borrowed a substantial sum from the Spier Trading Company for that."

"I see. That sort of work is said to be expensive, after all."

"Yes. But if it goes well, it will enable farming in areas previously unsuitable for agriculture, increasing income."

"Oh, that's the angle?"

Hearing "flood control project," Rei had assumed it was about fixing areas prone to river flooding, but that guess was apparently off.

"No, I believe preventing flooding was one of the objectives too? It seems various factors were involved, which is why the project became so large-scale. But... naturally, including both aspects meant the costs increased significantly."

"...I see."

Just that much made Rei vaguely understand what Mayo was getting at.

The ballooning costs must have meant the money borrowed from Spier Trading Company increased even further.

(But then, what exactly was that request back then? He said it was the kind of request you absolutely couldn't refuse once asked. If they were short on money, did he mean something like raid a bandit hideout and hand over the treasure there?)

Thinking that, he found himself accepting it.

Rei had become known among thieves as the Bandit Eater.

Naturally, he held a significant advantage when fighting thieves.

...Of course, defeating thieves was almost like a hobby for Rei. Though it certainly served a practical purpose too.

Many frown upon hunting thieves, but for Rei, it meant his pockets grew warmer, and the surrounding area saw less damage from thieves. Also, depending on the situation, selling the thieves to slave traders meant the traders got more slaves to sell... not quite killing two birds with one stone, but closer to three.

So asking someone like me to take down bandits? Doesn't that seem a bit... off? That's what I can't help thinking.

(Besides, that Marunino Viscount, was it? He was said to be loyal to the king. So why would he go out of his way... especially during that time of year, when winter was approaching, to come all the way to the frontier town of Gilm?)

Just because Rei is called the Bandit Eater doesn't mean no one else can defeat bandits.

In fact, bandits who are a threat to ordinary people aren't exactly tough opponents for adventurers.

Of course, there are exceptions like the Grassland Wolf, but exceptions are called exceptions precisely because they are few in number.

It was hard to believe a royalist noble would go out of their way to request Rei specifically for bandit hunting... especially the head of the house himself, coming to the stronghold of the opposing Neutral Faction.

(If there's another possibility...)

Is there something? Rei wondered, but then recalled his own ability... specifically, the Death Scythe skill: terrain manipulation.

That ability to freely manipulate the ground could be incredibly useful for flood control work.

The area Rei could manipulate was only about one meter.

But even one meter of freely manipulable ground would undoubtedly reduce the labor required for flood control work significantly.

That would undoubtedly speed up the flood control project considerably.

(But he did say he couldn't refuse once he heard the request... If it's flood control work, he wouldn't say that, right?)

Even if he'd heard the request was for flood control work and then refused, it shouldn't have caused any particular trouble.

Of course, if asked whether he accepted the job after hearing the details, Rei would answer no.

At the time, he was desperately searching for a way to restore Vihera's consciousness.

If it were just a day or two of commitment, that might have been manageable. But accepting a request for flood control work could take a month, two months... or worse, it might even take half a year, a year, two years.

There was no way Rei had the leeway to take on such a request.

(Besides, his ability to manipulate terrain by one meter stemmed from conquering a dungeon and destroying its core. Even if he had accepted a flood control request back then, it wouldn't have made any difference.)

It was unclear how much the other party understood about Rei's terrain manipulation, but it wasn't impossible they judged it could be somewhat useful for flood control work.

"...Anyway. I get the connection between the Marnino Viscount family and the Spier Trading Company, but how does Mayo's company factor into this?"

"Actually, it seems the Spier Trading Company is currently... well, 'invasion' might be a bit strong, but they're making moves to absorb our company or bring us under their umbrella."

"Why on earth would they do that?"

From Rei's perspective, it was puzzling why they'd do such a thing.

"There are several reasons. The biggest one is probably that our guild has firmly established roots in Gilm. Having used Gilm as our base for so many years, we hold advantages in various aspects when it comes to operating here."

"...But since Tris came to see me, doesn't that mean the Spier Trading Company is also active in Gilm? If so, I don't see why they'd need to force such a move."

Rei shook her head at the muttered words.

Rei noticed the flesh on her cheeks tremble slightly. She must be exhausted from this whole affair. Compared to when they'd researched goblin meat together before, the trembling seemed less pronounced.

(Well, considering her health, losing weight would definitely be better.)

Watching Mayo, who was a bit too plump, Rei worried about her health.

Unaware of how she was perceived, Mayo continued speaking.

"There's bound to be a difference between us, based in Gilm, and the Spier Trading Company, who come to Gilm for supplies."

"Is that just how it is?"

Living as an adventurer, Rei naturally knew little about the merchant world.

He had clashed with the Azoth Trading Company before, but that was a conflict between a merchant and an adventurer, not between merchants themselves.

He used shops regularly, but that was strictly it.

(Wait, there was that one time before the war with the Bestir Empire when I was hired as an escort. But even then, it was purely an escort relationship.)

Thinking about it that way, he remembered the female adventurers he'd escorted back then.

They invited me to join their party, but I declined... and I haven't seen those female adventurers since.

Completely unaware, Rei had no idea that one of those female adventurers harbored feelings for him.

"I understand, Rei, that as an adventurer, you're not very familiar with merchants. Anyway, my merchant guild... well, my father's guild, to be precise... is currently locked in fierce competition with the Spier Trading Company. Not violent competition, mind you, but business rivalry."

"I see. If it were about violence, or physical force, I could have helped out."

"If that were the case, we could have used your help."

Mayo gave a wry smile at Rei's words.

In truth, the Spier Trading Company likely knew full well about Mayo's connection to Rei when they took this action.

As Mayo had said, when it came to disputes between merchant guilds, the use of physical force was extremely rare.

But "rarely" doesn't mean "never."

In fact, it wouldn't be unusual for them to make arrangements without the other party knowing, then hire thugs to take action...

But... in this particular incident, nothing of the sort had been done.

Mayo surmised this likely meant the Spier Trading Company knew of Rei and Mayo's connection, didn't want Rei involved... and didn't want to antagonize him.

That said, by letting Rei know their guild is under attack by Spier Trading Company, they're trying to sour his impression of Spier Trading Company. In that sense, Mayo is quite the shrewd merchant, as one would expect.

"Anyway, due to those circumstances, research on goblin meat has barely progressed. ...My apologies."

"No, don't worry about it. If that's the situation on your end, it can't be helped. We've been pretty busy ourselves lately, too."

Whether he understood Mayo's intentions or not.

Either way, Rei stated this.
