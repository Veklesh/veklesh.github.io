Rei used a sand ship to transport people, carriages, and camels when moving to the village of the Hundred-Faced Ones.

Nena, a leader among Daldora's subordinates, was astonished by the sudden appearance of the sand ship before her. She was astonished that it belonged to Daldora, astonished that it so readily took everyone aboard, and astonished by its speed of movement.

The sand ship could be called the best means of transportation for moving across the desert.

Of course, there were beings like Rei's partner, Set, who could travel from the Hundred-Faced Ones' village to Gosh in mere minutes.

But that was an exceptional case, not the norm.

Moreover, unlike the sand ship, Set cannot carry large amounts of cargo. ...Though, in Rei's case, there is the method of Misty Ring.

In contrast, sand ships can transport large quantities of cargo, people, and animals all at once.

Even so, the reason sand ships haven't been used for cargo transport until now is likely because they are extremely expensive.

"I never imagined we'd arrive this quickly. It's wonderful. If only sand ships were more affordable, travel and trade in the desert would be so much smoother..."

After disembarking from the sand ship upon arriving at the Hundred-Faced One's village, Nena expressed her gratitude for its use before turning to Rei and saying this.

"Yeah. If we stripped down the features and kept only the bare essentials, it might be a bit cheaper, but that'd be tough."

A sand ship isn't just a vehicle; it's also a status symbol, a sign of wealth simply by owning one.

Would anyone deliberately create a cheaper version of that status symbol, thereby diminishing the value of their own sand ship? The answer was no.

Within the Kingdom of Solrain, there are many others besides Daldora who possess—or possessed—Sand Ships.

But would such people do something that would diminish the value of their own Sand Ships?

Nena let out a small sigh, agreeing silently to Rei's implied question.

Her sad expression—was it because she'd missed out on a great business opportunity?

Anyway, as Nena looked sad, a somewhat exasperated voice echoed around them.

"You're still good at putting on that cat mask, aren't you?"

Rei turned her gaze toward the voice and found Narsus standing there.

His expression matched his tone, a look of mild exasperation as he turned his gaze toward Nena.

(Both of them are Daldora's subordinates, so it makes sense they know each other.)

Rei accepted this, but Nena, who should have been dejected, as if to say "What does that matter?" abruptly lifted her face and glared at Narsus.

"Well, well, Mr. Narsus. I thought you weren't at Gosh, but here you are. I assumed you'd been beheaded."

"Hmm. You didn't know I was here? I suppose Daldora-sama deemed it unnecessary to inform you."

They exchanged words, but the air between them was icy.

Nena's lips curved into a smile, but her eyes remained utterly unamused.

Narsus, for his part, made it clear through his demeanor that he found conversing with the woman before him thoroughly unpleasant.

And yet, neither seemed the least bit inclined to back down. Rei could almost see sparks flying between them.

"Ah... Is it about time? We can't just stand here like this forever, right? The others seem to be getting worried."

This sudden exchange came even though not everyone had disembarked from the sand ship yet.

Naturally, the others were stuck on the sand ship, unable to disembark, and some looked confused.

...Though, to be precise, only the personnel sent by Augusto seemed confused. Those sent by Daldora wore expressions that seemed to say, "Here we go again."

Even so, wasting time here when they wanted to investigate the village as quickly as possible was nothing but a nuisance.

Neena and Narsus, being their superiors, didn't voice it, but honestly, they probably wanted them off as soon as possible.

"Oh, my apologies. I'll step aside immediately. ...Really, thanks to someone somewhere, we've wasted precious time."

"Oh? And who might this 'somebody' be? I'd like to hear the details."

"You don't know? Really, this is why..."

"Personally, I'd hate for some female fox to meddle with Rei and cause unnecessary trouble."

"...Oh? A fox? But rest assured. If such a creature exists, I'll chase her away for you."

"No, no, that's impossible. After all..."

As the exchange began again, Rei opened his mouth in exasperation.

"Cut it out already. We need to get moving on investigating the village and... disposing of the bodies. You know things will get messy if we don't, right?"

Hearing Rei's words, both Nena and Narsus seemed to snap back to reality. They cleared their throats, looking slightly embarrassed.

"Ahem. ...So, Rei. Is this everyone? Well, since we came by sand ship, I can't imagine there being anyone else."

Laying a cover-up, Narsus's words drew a nod from Rei.

"Yeah, that's everyone. So, anything unusual happen while I was gone... Nah, probably not worth asking."

If anything had been wrong, they wouldn't have had the luxury of making such foolish small talk.

And sure enough, Narsas nodded in response to Rei's words.

"No problems. Zarust went to check on the oasis, but he should be back soon."

"I see. ...Then we should start investigating the village and cleaning up soon."

Following that order, everyone present disembarked from the sand ship and entered the village.

The adventurers dispatched as guards, in particular, hurried ahead of the others. They needed to understand the village's situation as quickly as possible; otherwise, they wouldn't be able to handle a monster attack.

In fact, it was clear the adventurers would be the busiest during this village investigation.

They had to provide security, conduct the investigation, and also deal with the bodies found in the village.

Following the adventurers, the camel-drawn carriage also disembarked, and in an instant, the sand ship was empty of people.

The only one seemingly unconcerned by the increasingly intense sunlight on the deck was Set, who lay sprawled out.

Of course, this lying down didn't mean he was actually asleep.

As usual, he was scanning for any signs of monster attacks.

"Set, it's fine now! Come down here!"

"Grr!"

That's why, when Rei called out, Set rose from the deck and came down from the sand ship.

"Grrrrrrr"

Rei smiled and stroked Set as he purred affectionately.

For some reason, Nena watched that scene with an envious look.

"Well then, I'll go take a look around the village. After that... well, I'll think about it when the time comes."

"Oh, is that so? I was hoping to hear more about your plans, Rei-san."

Nena shot Rei an inviting look, but beside her, Narsus was giving her a suspicious stare.

Watching the two of them, Rei shook his head slightly.

"What you should be doing right now is investigating the village. Listening to me here won't get the village investigation done."

"That's right. Rei's absolutely correct."

"...Hey, whose side are you on?"

Nena, who had been instructed to build a good relationship with Rei, declared to Narsus with obvious displeasure.

But even Narna couldn't see how she could possibly get anywhere with Rei, so she just shrugged her shoulders slightly.

As someone with a certain confidence in her beauty, Nena disliked Narsus's attitude.

...Though, Narsus also didn't believe Rei would fall for Nena's seductive charms, so he was simply showing through his attitude that it was better not to waste effort.

To an outsider, their exchange looked like nothing more than lovers' quarrels.

Though the two themselves seemed completely unaware.

"Well then, sorry, but I'm heading out first. You two stay here and bicker to your hearts' content."

"Hey, Rei!? Who's having a lovers' quarrel with who!?"

Nena shouted indignantly, but Narsus's response only poured fuel on the fire.

"Exactly. Why would I be with a fox like this?"

"...Hold on, who's a fox and what exactly? We need to talk about this in more detail."

"Huh? Are you sure? Your mask is slipping in front of Rei, you know?"

Unable to bear watching the two bicker any longer, Rei stored the Sand Ship in the Misty Ring and headed into the village with Set.

Despite the nearby sand ship being stored away in the Misty Ring, the voices bickering behind them showed no sign of stopping. Honestly, Rei felt he couldn't be bothered with them any longer.

"Grr?"

Is it okay? Set asked, purring softly. Rei gently stroked Set to deflect the question.

Set wasn't entirely unaffected by the voices behind them, but savoring the comfort of Rei's touch took precedence.

As they walked like that, the first thing they saw, unsurprisingly, was several people carrying a corpse out of a house.

Living in the desert meant death was closer at hand than in ordinary villages or towns.

Venturing into the desert itself carried greater risks than simply stepping outside a normal village or town.

Given that, it wasn't surprising that some people were accustomed to handling corpses.

But... even so, the men's handling of the bodies could only be described as careless.

Was it because the bodies belonged to assassins?

Rei could understand the men's actions.

First, Daldora's research facility had been attacked by the Hundred-Faced Ones, leaving not a single survivor.

As fellow subordinates of Daldora, it wouldn't be surprising if some among those carrying the bodies had known the researchers personally.

And for those who had lost acquaintances, friends... or even family or lovers, the people lying dead in this village were members of their enemies' faction.

Thinking that way, perhaps it was inevitable that the bodies were being treated so carelessly... but Rei felt a slight unease at how they were being handled.

"Hey, don't handle the bodies so carelessly. I'm not saying treat them with reverence, but don't treat them like trash."

That must be why. Those words slipped out of Rei's mouth without him meaning them to.

"Ahh!? ...Rei-san? My apologies. I'll be more careful from now on."

The man had initially started to shout angrily, but upon realizing it was Rei who had spoken, he immediately bowed his head and carefully adjusted his grip on the corpse he'd been holding roughly.

After watching that, Rei and Set headed off to another location.

Their destination was the oasis, the lifeline of this village.

Though considerably smaller than the oasis in Gosh, it was still likely sufficient to sustain the village's inhabitants.

As Rei surveyed the surroundings, he noticed several people examining the oasis.

Each carried tools Rei had never seen before, and they were all discussing their own theories.

Watching them, the unpleasant scene he'd witnessed just moments ago seemed to fade from his mind.

Feeling a little better, Rei approached the person closest among those examining the oasis.

"How's it going? Found anything?"

The man hadn't expected Rei to address him out of the blue.

The man she addressed looked slightly startled and hurriedly bowed his head toward Rei.

"Ah, Rei-san. Well... yes. We've confirmed this water is safe to drink for now. But raising fish like Gosh probably won't work."

"...Probably not."

Compared to Gosh's enormous oasis—one you'd almost call a lake—the one right in front of them was clearly small.

Considering that, it was only natural to assume fish couldn't be raised here.

After talking with the man for a few more minutes, Rei once again walked around the area with Set.

The desert sun poured its intense rays down upon the earth once more today.

(Maybe it's time to head back to Gilm.)

Watching this scene, Rei found herself thinking that.
