With the blade of the Death Scythe pressed against him, the unconscious man lay there. Rei gave him a cold glance.

Then, he scans his surroundings.

Not a single guard who had appeared to defeat Rei and her companions remained standing.

Some lay dead, others merely unconscious... but there was no sign of anyone still intending to do anything about Rei and his companions.

"Rei, we should go soon. If we stay here much longer, we'll end up fighting again."

Rei gave a small nod at Marina's words.

"Yeah. Once we're inside the underground facility, they probably won't be able to pull off such a flashy fight... or maybe they could?"

If Rei and his group were just petty thieves, sending a few skilled operatives would suffice.

But since it was Rei and his group who infiltrated the underground facility, even Jaya probably didn't think sending a few people would be enough to handle them.

In that case, they would naturally deploy their maximum force. And Jaya's maximum force would inevitably be the Giants.

And if the giants were unleashed, the damage would be commensurate.

"True. ...But still, why keep that man alive?"

As Elena asked, flicking blood from Mirage's blade, Rei responded, similarly wiping blood from the blade of Death Scythe.

"We'll need some information on the underground facility, right? Of course, we don't have time to leisurely interrogate him, so we'll have to take him with us."

"...Can we trust him?"

Elena's gaze was fixed on the unconscious man lying before Rei.

Given his placement here, he was undoubtedly one of Jaya's elite.

But being elite didn't guarantee he had information on the underground facility.

Above all, there was the question of whether he'd willingly give up information to the very people who'd attacked them.

"Probably fine. If he doesn't talk, he knows better than anyone what happens. ...Besides, if we stay here too long, reinforcements will likely arrive. That means more fighting, so let's get inside quickly. ...Set, I'm leaving this place to you!"

"GRRRR!"

At Rei's words, Set let out a high-pitched cry.

Jaya lacked aerial combat capabilities, and Mejogo didn't possess any kind of barrier either.

Given that, Rei judged that leaving Set, who could fly, behind posed absolutely no problem.

The underground facility had ample space for giants to maneuver, so Set should be able to operate freely.

However, Rei desperately wanted to avoid the scenario where, after they stormed the underground facility, additional forces from Jaya were sent here and also entered the facility, trapping them in a pincer attack from both front and rear.

That was precisely why he decided to entrust the rear guard to Set, whom he trusted absolutely.

"Alright, let's go then."

Glancing at Set, who was growling his refusal to let anyone pass, Rei grabbed the collar of the unconscious man and dragged him toward the building leading to the underground facility.

Since Rei wasn't particularly tall, the man was dragged along by his waist and legs.

The fact he was still unconscious probably meant Rei's blow had been terrifying.

...Then again, had he opened his mouth just a moment later, he would have seen his own head severed up close. So, it couldn't be helped.

"...Hey, this. There's gotta be another entrance somewhere, right?"

The building itself, leading to the underground facility, was quite large.

The entrance was large enough for a carriage to pass through normally, and the passageway was similarly sized.

Still, the others nodded in agreement with Marina's suggestion.

If there were giants in the underground facility, they would obviously need a way out.

Even Rei couldn't imagine that the giants who had launched their attack when the main force of the Resistance was defeated had emerged from this building at the very center of Mejogo.

Doing something like that would undoubtedly spark rumors about the giants.

Yet, currently, the existence of the Titans was scarcely known.

Only the Resistance and those connected to them had heard of them.

Of course, it wasn't truly unknown.

"Probably. Likely somewhere in Mejogo with fewer people... no, even that might be a bit conspicuous. So then, it wouldn't be strange if there were an exit to an underground facility outside Mejogo. Finding it would be difficult, though."

If there was a path leading to the underground facility from outside Mejogo, there would have been no need for such a direct assault.

But the fact that we don't know that is precisely why we're in this situation now.

"Right. That was a silly thing to say. ...Anyway, shall we move on?"

Marina nodded at her words, and Rei and the others moved forward.

The building itself wasn't particularly large.

That made sense, as a slope leading underground was immediately visible upon entering.

In other words, the building they'd entered was likely constructed solely to conceal the slope leading underground.

"...A slope. It's fine for now, but going back is going to be a real pain."

Thinking about climbing this slope later, Rei wore a weary expression.

His feelings clearly visible, he descended the slope...

"Ow! Whoa, what the hell is this!?"

Suddenly, that cry reached his ears.

Rei knew instantly whose voice it was, without needing to think.

"You're awake?"

"Huh? Whoa! ...!?"

Rei stopped in his tracks and roughly tossed aside the man he'd been dragging.

Tossed onto the slope, the man seemed momentarily lost, unsure of where he was or what situation he found himself in.

Still, being one of Jaya's elite, he quickly assessed his current situation.

Once he did, the man opened his mouth, trembling with fear.

"So, this is on the way to the underground facility, right?"

"Yeah. It's quite a grand slope, though."

If we're going up a slope like this, won't the horses pulling the carriage get injured?

The thought crossed Rei's mind, but he judged he had no time to voice such concerns now. He said nothing more and fixed his gaze on the man.

It was a silent gaze, but he understood what it urged him to do. He wasn't a man who couldn't grasp that.

No, he understood perfectly well that if he dared say he didn't understand here, the man would undoubtedly kill him.

"If you keep going down this slope, you'll find a massive gate. Beyond that gate lies what you might call the core of this Mejogo. Of course, entering requires various procedures, and there are several guarding soldiers."

"But when we caused trouble on the surface, it seemed like no reinforcements came from this building?"

Confirming the man had opened his mouth, Rei called out to him again as they descended the slope.

Elena and the others nearby simply watched the man's actions intently.

Ready to kill him instantly if he made any careless moves.

The man often bought prostitutes in Mejogo during his off-duty hours, but even with all these beauties staring at him... he felt no excitement.

Fundamentally, his own life was in danger in the current situation.

He certainly didn't have the luxury of focusing his mind on thoughts of embracing a woman.

"The ones guarding the door are basically the elite among us. Of course, I'm one of the elite too, but I'd say those guys are the cream of the crop."

The man implied he was useful because he was elite, but Rei didn't seem to care at all and continued speaking.

"So, how many of these elite of the elite are there in total?"

"Well, that's..."

The man faltered at Rei's question.

Seeing the man's reaction, Rei took a step forward.

That was all... truly, that was all he did. Yet, for the man, it was more than enough of a threat.

He opened his mouth in a fluster.

"I-I don't know! That kind of information is kept secret from us too! They're higher up than us!"

"...Huh. So then, does that mean the information you have isn't that important either?"

Saying the wrong thing here would get him killed. He had to avoid lying at all costs, yet still say something that would pique Rei and the others' interest.

Overcome by such fear, he somehow managed to speak.

"I don't know the details about the guys guarding the gate, but I know quite a bit about the passageway beyond that door. This is true! I definitely know quite a bit!"

Seeing the desperate look on the man's face, Elena and the others turned their gaze toward him, asking silently, What should we do?

"...For now, this is fine. If there's a trap, this guy'll be the one to fall for it."

"Hey! You plan to send me in first!?"

"Of course. If you don't like it, that's fine too."

The man, told he'd be killed if he couldn't use his powers, immediately nodded in agreement.

In terms of combat strength, he posed no particular threat to Rei and the others... yet his ability to sense danger was astonishing.

(As a trap detector, he might actually be a good choice?)

That's what Rei thought, but this passageway was one used by carriages transporting prostitutes.

The same would likely hold true beyond the gate.

Considering whether someone would deliberately set traps in such a place... the answer was probably no.

(Well, maybe things are different now.)

Since they were the ones attacking, it wouldn't be strange if they'd set traps to respond accordingly.

"Still, just like before, I wonder if this country is particularly skilled at building underground facilities?"

Marina murmured, keeping a watchful eye on their surroundings.

She avoided specifying "before," but Rei could guess it probably referred to the Unchu incident.

She avoided saying the name "Unchu" because she was wary of the man.

"Yeah. That place was a bit of a surprise too. ...I see it. They're here."

Responding to Marina, they descended the slope until a gate came into view.

Seeing the gate's considerable size, Rei naturally imagined giants.

(They don't seem to deploy giants from here, but that doesn't mean they can't. ...Which means if we break through that gate, giants could be waiting for us there.)

As he pondered this, he noticed about ten people near the gate.

The group, composed of humans and beastmen, seemed to be waiting for Rei and the others walking down the slope.

No, not as if—they were definitely waiting for Rei.

"Those guys. They're the gatekeepers. They're strong, those guys."

The man stated while watching the gatekeepers, and the first to react upon hearing this was, naturally, Vihera.

"Strong?"

"Yeah. Like I said before, they're the cream of the crop."

"Huh. ...Oh, Rei."

At the man's words, Vihera called out to Rei as if she'd noticed something.

Rei looked at Vihera at the sound of her voice, but followed that gaze... and soon understood why Vihera had called out to her.

Because at the end of that gaze... among the gatekeepers, stood a female figure resembling an orc.

It was undoubtedly the same person Rei had encountered when infiltrating Mejogo before.

(Well, I can't tell orcs apart anyway... Ah, right, she wasn't a pig beastman or a smart orc, she was human.)

As these thoughts crossed Rei's mind, Rei grasped what Vihera was trying to say.

Given Vihera's inherent love of combat, hearing about the orc-like woman from Rei had likely piqued his interest.

And now, that very person had appeared right here.

So, it was only natural he'd want to fight here.

Besides the orc-like woman, there were many others present.

"Alright, I'll handle her. ...What about the others?"

"Right. I'd prefer to take her down myself, but it might be a bit troublesome."

Vihera said this with a smile, but in this case, "trouble" didn't mean she'd have trouble fighting the orc-like woman.

It was simply that Vihera's combat style was specialized for one-on-one combat.

"Then I'll go. Is that alright?"

"...If Elena steps in, it feels like that alone would be the end of it. Oh well."

From Vihera's perspective, the orc-like woman was among the top-tier fighters among the gatekeepers.

Of course, each guard might have something hidden—power, skills, magic items, weapons—but overall, the orc-like woman still seemed the strongest.

"Well then, I'll be off."

Vihera charged down the slope, a savage, carnivorous smile on her face.

Elena, holding Mirage, followed in pursuit.

Rei and the others watched the two depart without a single worry.
