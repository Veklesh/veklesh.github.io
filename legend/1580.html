Piercing the air, a spear flies through the sky.

To those who witnessed it, the Twilight Spear's strike—a crimson streak of light that could easily be mistaken for such a vision—flew straight toward the door.

The magic imbued by Rei, possessing extraordinary magical power, unleashed the Twilight Spear's full might.

A door made of metal so sturdy... a metal so strong that even a giant possessing strength incomparable to any human, attacking with all its might, could only manage to bend it.

Yet, the crimson beam of light released from Rei's hand pierced through that metal door with ease.

As if tearing through wet paper, with utter ease.

Precisely as Rei aimed, the hole opened in the door was only as large as the spear itself.

The Twilight Spear, having pierced the door, then pierced the body of the giant that had been relentlessly pounding the door.

...And, as was to be expected, the giant pierced through the body was not the only one.

The spear, having exited the giant's back, pierced the body of another giant standing directly behind it, then another behind that one, and another behind that one... and another...

Ultimately, it pierced the bodies of nearly twenty giants before finally losing momentum and coming to a halt.

The giants pierced through their bodies initially had no idea what had happened. Only when the pain of being pierced through their bodies set in did they finally understand they had been attacked.

...Though many giants never grasped it, their life flames extinguished as they collapsed to the ground.

For the giants, it was a truly sudden attack.

But the giants' time of despair was far from over.

Because, from the body of the giant the Twilight Spear had pierced last, the spear itself vanished, still lodged within.

Regardless of how much intelligence the giants possessed, they surely understood that the spear before their eyes had vanished.

"Gah?"

One of the giants tilted its head, as if forgetting the irritation and hunger it had felt just moments before.

Why did the spear vanish? It wondered...

"Guh?"

The next instant, the Twilight Spear flew through the door once more, piercing the giant's torso.

Completely unable to comprehend what had happened... eventually, that single strike proved fatal, and it collapsed to the ground.

The Twilight Spear that pierced that giant's body then stabbed into another giant's body, stopping its movement.

Then, the spear vanished once more... and the giant's screams echoed around them.

The giants had no idea what was happening.

Suddenly, their comrades screamed and collapsed on the spot, so they recognized they were under attack.

But with nearly a thousand of them present, how could they comprehend the nature of the attack...? Unless they saw the weapon, the Twilight Spear, with their own eyes, understanding was impossible.

Or even if they did see the Twilight Spear, it was doubtful they would have understood.

The giants' confusion was nothing but a perfect opportunity for Rei, who was carrying out the attack.

She hurls the Twilight Spear, piercing through the door and then through the bodies of the giants beyond, one after another.

She repeats this action endlessly.

She stopped throwing the spear when she had hurled it at the door a little over fifty times.

The door was already riddled with holes from the Twilight Spear's assault, to the point where one might think it could be easily destroyed if the giants launched a serious attack.

The reason Rei stopped moving in that situation was probably because he saw no giant on the other side through the holes in the door.

The giants weren't exactly the brightest, but they understood that staying near the door meant certain death.

"...Well, the door's broken just right. Might as well use some magic."

"Huh? Weren't we supposed to avoid magic because of the cave collapse risk?"

Elena voiced her confusion at Rei's words as he sheathed the Twilight Spear back into the Misty Ring and took hold of the Death Scythe.

Rei gave Elena a small nod before speaking.

"True. Magic affecting a wide area might trigger the cave collapse. But don't you think a narrow-range attack spell should be fine?"

"If you can control it properly, I suppose it wouldn't be a problem?"

Though implicitly asked if he could handle such adjustments, Rei flashed a confident smile, certain that aside from the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, magic itself posed no issue.

In truth, controlling Rei's fire-specialized magic wasn't particularly difficult.

While some aspects like constructing incantations or magic circles followed systematic rules, a significant part relied on imagery and intuition.

And for Rei, who came from Japan, imagery was something he excelled at beyond measure.

As for his sense, he possessed a massive amount of magical power from the start, and being specialized in fire magic meant he had an exceptionally high sense specifically for fire magic.

Put nicely, she was a genius; put unkindly, she was a specialized fool who couldn't use any magic besides fire.

"First, I'll break that door and cast a spell that engulfs the giants away from it. Elena and Vihera, attack them from the other side after I cast my spell. Oh, and Byune, you'll stay by my side in case something happens."

No one objected to Rei's instructions.

Elena, who harbored a deep aversion to giants, and also because it was Rei's order, had no objection to charging through the door.

Vihera, who had recovered her stamina while Rei continuously hurled the Twilight Spear, felt nothing but joy at being the first to cross the threshold—no complaints whatsoever.

Byune knew she couldn't defeat the giant alone, so standing by Rei's side to be ready for any emergency was exactly what she wanted.

"Kyuu!"

"What about me?" cried Yellow, Elena's familiar.

Hugged by Elena, Yellow probably wanted some work for himself too.

He chirped repeatedly, trying to get Rei's attention. ...Though, to anyone watching, Yellow just looked utterly adorable.

"Ah, right. Yellow, you'll stand by just like Byune. If anything happens, I'll have you go get Elena."

"Kyuu!"

At Rei's words, Yellow chirped contentedly.

In reality, it would be quicker for Rei to go get Elena himself than to have Yellow fetch her.

That way, Rei could immediately convey the words he wanted to say directly, avoiding the time loss of Elena having to come back here.

In the end, asking Yellow to be a messenger was probably just lip service.

...Still, Yellow's scales were incredibly sturdy, and he possessed the ability to blend into his surroundings.

Considering that, it could still be a viable option in a pinch.

"Alright, now that everyone's roles are set... I'm going to use magic. Elena and Vihera, prepare for the assault."

With that, Rei took hold of the Death Scythe and began chanting the spell.

'Flames, gather at my fingertips. Gather upon the great scythe that reaps life, gather before my foes, gather, gather, gather. Small power, great power. Rage as I command.'

As the incantation ended, nearly fifty flames appeared at the tip of the Death Scythe.

However, each flame was only about the size of a fingertip.

That said, the magical power contained within them was enough to exhaust an ordinary mage even if they gave it their all.

'Breath of Flames'

As the spell activated, the fifty tiny flames shot forth at a speed faster than a person could run.

The first flame struck the door, already riddled with holes... and in the next instant, the door was engulfed in flames and melted.

The remaining flames pass through the gaping hole... and where the giants stand, a scorching hell appears within a very narrow area.

A searing hell for two or three giants.

Nearly fifty such hells appeared.

And yet, outside the immediate area where the flames landed and burned, there was almost no heat.

...Almost none, meaning it wasn't completely heat-blocking. Perhaps because Rei couldn't fully control the flames?

Either way, for the giants, it was nothing short of hell.

The only saving grace was that the magic was cast from outside the door.

Rei had confirmed no giants were near the door, so the swarm of flames, released from an unseen position within a suitable range and carefully aimed to avoid hitting walls or ceilings, was an opponent the giants could do nothing against.

Giants, being specialized in physical attack and defense, are terribly weak against magic.

In a small country like Reburina, where there were likely few mages, that might not have been a problem.

But when facing an enemy like Rei who could use magic, the giants became terribly vulnerable.

"Good. Go on. There should be heavy casualties among the giants beyond that door."

"...But the door's glowing red where the flames touched it. Are you sure it's safe?"

"Ah... well, if you slip through the gap in the door..."

Vihera said, and Rei brushed it off with that excuse.

Vihera stared at Rei in disbelief for a few seconds, but fortunately, the door itself was riddled with holes from the giants' attacks and, above all, from Rei's Twilight Spear. It seemed easy enough for a person to squeeze through.

Had the Twilight Spear not struck, it would likely have been impossible to proceed beyond the door until the flames cooled.

Vihera subtly considered this, but told himself they needed to get to the other side now. He turned his gaze to Elena.

"Well then, shall we go?"

"Yeah, this side's fine."

They exchanged glances and started running toward the door.

"I think it should be okay, but be careful!"

Whether they heard Rei's voice or not, Elena and Vihera waved lightly while running, or perhaps nodded in Rei's direction.

With the supple movements of a predator, the two vanished through the door.

(Elena aside, I was worried Vihera might snag that thin dress on the door... but it seems she was fine.)

Given that her outfit resembled that of a dancer or courtesan, Vihera's clothing was extremely thin, fluttering in the wind as she ran.

As they slipped through the door's gap, Rei wondered if it might catch, but apparently, it wasn't an issue.

"Well then, what should we do?"

"Hm?"

"Kyuu?"

Rei's words drew responses from both Byune and Yellow.

Yellow was being held by Byune, occasionally getting stroked and looking quite pleased.

"Guess we just have to wait here."

Perhaps those belonging to Jaya might come here.

Since Marina had been instructed to hold down the location where the Jaya members were, there was no need for worry.

But among the soldiers we defeated along the way, there's still a chance some of those who only fainted might come looking for Rei and the others.

"Nah, probably not."

"Hm?"

Byune turned her gaze toward Rei's muttered words.

Shaking his head dismissively, Rei turned his gaze back to the door... though calling it that was already difficult; it was more accurately described as a mass of metal.

From the other side came sounds of something crashing to the ground, the sound of something being sliced apart, screams... and laughter that sounded genuinely delighted.

It was obvious enough who each belonged to, without even needing to think about it.

"...I said I'd wait here, but there's nothing for me to do."

"Mm."

Byune gave a small reply to Rei's mutter.

In truth, Rei had chosen to wait here largely to ensure Byune remained out of harm's way.

In other words, the fact that Rei was bored meant nothing more than that things were going smoothly.

Still, there was no doubt he was bored.

"Want some?"

Rei pulled out a skewer from the Misty Ring and offered it to Byune.

Byune says nothing in particular and simply takes the skewer.

"Kyuu!"

Seeing Yellow chirp as if wanting something too, Rei pulls out a sandwich next.

The sandwich, packed with meat and vegetables, was large enough that eating just one could easily fill you up.

But for Yellow, even such a sandwich seemed like something he could easily finish.

He happily tore into the sandwich, devouring it piece by piece.

Watching him eat like that, Rei was reminded once more that Yellow was the child of a black dragon.

Watching the meal scene between the one person and one creature, Rei thought to himself that maybe, after a little while longer, he'd take a look beyond the door.
