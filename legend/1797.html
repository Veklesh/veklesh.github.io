"Damn it! Don't mess with me! You... you... you..."

"Guh, G-Guy... s-stop... please forgive me..."

Having been humiliated beyond belief, Gaiska returned to the mansion and proceeded to punch and kick a maid who happened to be passing by.

The maid hadn’t done anything in particular to upset Gaiska.

If anything, it was just bad luck… she’d happened to be passing by when Gaiska was in a foul mood.

Her face, kicked with absolutely no restraint, was swollen—a face that, as a maid of the Marquis of Seisol, would normally be considered above average in beauty—and blood was flowing from her lips, perhaps from a cut.

Hearing her words—spoken with a voice already too hoarse to be coherent—seemed to further fuel Gaiska’s sadistic urges, and his assault on the maid grew even more violent.

Just as he was about to deliver another blow to the maid, who was already unable to move... his body suddenly froze.

It wasn’t that Gaiska had stopped assaulting the maid of his own volition; rather, he had been suddenly grabbed from behind and yanked away.

Surprised by his own unintended action, Gaiska shouted in frustration at being stopped just as he was reaching the peak of his excitement.

“Wagunora! Let go!”

“No, young master. If you go any further, the maid will die.”

“My orders are more important than the life of a single maid!”

Gaiska shouted at the man holding him—Wagunora, the middle-aged knight in charge of the mansion’s security. But there was a decisive difference in physical strength between a man who had survived on the battlefield and never neglected his daily training even in his middle age, and Gaiska, who only moved his body enough to avoid becoming sedentary.

No matter how much Gaiska shouted, as long as Wagnorola didn’t let go of his hand, he couldn’t break free from the restraints.

Thus, separated from the maid, Gaiska finally calmed down a little as he watched the maid, who had been assaulted until then, being led away on Wagunora’s orders.

Well, to be precise, it’s not so much that he calms down as that he suddenly loses all motivation.

“Wagunora, that’s enough. Let go. That maid is gone now. You can let go, can’t you?”

At Gaiska’s words, Wagunora released her grip.

In that instant, Gaiska spun around and landed a punch squarely on Wagunora’s face, but for Wagunora, who had been training for years, a blow of this magnitude was nothing to worry about.

If it had been a maid like the one Gaiska had just struck, that single blow might have sent her flying.

“Are you satisfied now?”

“Hmph. You tried to stop me. Consider yourself lucky that I’m letting you off with just this.”

Gaiska hadn’t expected Wagunora to be knocked flying just from a single punch, but seeing him look so unfazed was hardly amusing.

His fist ached from striking with all his might despite being unaccustomed to fighting… but if he mentioned that, he’d likely feel pathetic all over again, so he said nothing and simply walked down the hallway.

And so, when Gaiska—whose presence had been like a gale—vanished, the maids and butlers who had been watching the situation, along with everyone else present, all breathed a collective sigh of relief and turned grateful glances toward Wagunora.

In this mansion in Anesis, there aren’t many who could say anything to Gaiska.

And when it comes to actually being able to stop his actions, that number is even smaller.

Wagunora is one of those few.

One of the butlers, having witnessed the maid being beaten and kicked by Gaiska, hurried to call for Waguruna... and as a result, the worst possible outcome—the maid’s death—was averted.

That said, as a result of being punched and kicked, the maid’s face was bleeding and swollen.

Beaten so badly that one could no longer recall her original lovely appearance, her condition naturally aroused revulsion in anyone who saw her.

In this case, however, it is not the maid who is the object of that revulsion, but Gaiska, who committed the act.

Still, no one says it outright because Gaiska possesses the power of the Seisol Marquis family.

If one could stand up to the Marquis family on one’s own merits, like Rei, then there would be no need to worry about noble lineage at all.

But only a very few are capable of such a thing.

Ordinary people cannot defy those of the Marquis’s bloodline, and above all, the people working here are employed by the Seysol Marquis family.

...Strictly speaking, they are employed by the Seisole Marquis family, not by Gaiska... but still, as long as they are here in Anesis representing the Seisole Marquis family, there is no way they can defy them.

“Everyone, please return to your duties. I’ll go check on Lord Gaiska.”

At Wagunora’s words, many of the maids, butlers, and others present returned to their duties.

That said, having witnessed a defenseless maid being assaulted, it was unlikely they could get back to work in good spirits right away.

In fact, given the possibility that if Gaiska were to catch them, they might suffer the same fate as the maid from earlier, it was only natural that they would work while holding their breath.

The only consolation was that there were wizards in this mansion capable of casting healing spells, and that they also possessed various expensive potions.

The fact that the assaulted maid’s face was left without scars was the only consolation.

……Though, of course, the emotional scars left on the maid who was assaulted for no reason cannot be healed by recovery magic or potions.

“Why was Lord Gaiska so furious?”

“I don’t know. But… he’s always been a hot-tempered person. I’m sure something happened.”

“I hope this doesn’t turn into another hassle.”

“What the hell was that… Why was that maid suddenly getting punched? You’re kidding, right?”

“Ah… so you just got here. I guess you’ve been lucky enough not to witness scenes like that until now.”

"Whoa. I guess it’s a toss-up whether that’s good luck or bad luck. It’s a bit of a mixed bag."

“True. Then again, it’s rare for things to get this bad, so in that sense, maybe it’s a good thing this was the first thing you saw. If you can handle this, you should be able to manage working here from now on.”

“That’s true. The Marquis of Seisole’s household pays better than other houses…”

They exchanged such remarks as they returned to their respective tasks.

Some of them—perhaps those seeing Gaiska’s atrocities for the first time—were visibly shaken.

That said, compared to the others, who didn’t seem particularly shaken, the difference was stark.

The main reason the salaries for those working for the Seisole Marquis’s household—or, more precisely, in this mansion—were high in the first place was because of the incident involving Gaiska.

Since many of them were working here fully aware of that fact, perhaps this reaction was only to be expected.

In any case, the brief rampage came to an end… and the servants eventually disappeared from the scene.

“A commoner like him dares to defy a noble like me? Damn it, people who lack common sense are such a nuisance. Just because he has a nickname, does he really think he’s better than a noble?”

Sitting on the sofa, Gaiska muttered, his irritation plain to see.

The fact that he didn’t destroy anything in the room was likely because he had already vented some of his frustration on the maid.

That said, although it was originally Ara who had first cautioned Gaiska, the fact that the object of his resentment had somehow shifted to Rei... likely meant that Gaiska was simply unable to stomach being openly defied by a commoner like Rei.

“To pull a stunt like that on me… to act in such a way as to insult the House of Marquis Seisol—I can never forgive that.”

Even as he thought this, just picturing Rei for a moment sent a chill down his spine.

If he hadn’t backed down right then… or if he’d let his anger get the better of him and challenged Rei to a duel, he would undoubtedly have been soundly defeated.

No, depending on the circumstances, he might even have been killed.

Because Gaiska is of the Seisole Marquis family lineage, he has never set foot on a battlefield.

...In reality, as one can see from Elena, it is not uncommon for nobles to fight on the battlefield, but in Gaiska’s case, he regards combat as something base, so he has never undergone combat training.

The only “fighting” Gaiska engages in is the one-sided use of violence against those of a lower standing—like the maid from earlier—and those who absolutely cannot defy him.

Since Gaiska has no combat training, the fact that he didn’t inflict a fatal wound is a blessing in disguise.

If Gaiska were the sort to undergo combat training, then not only the maid who was taken out on today, but everyone else Gaiska has ever punched or kicked might have lost their lives rather than merely sustaining injuries.

We were lucky it didn’t come to that.

“I have no need to deal with such a barbaric creature myself. Lowly people should be dealt with by other lowly people… Hmm, fortunately, I hear there are many adventurers in need of money these days. …Hey, call Deotres!”

At Gaiska’s command, the man waiting in the adjacent room moved. About ten minutes later, there was a knock at the door, and a man appeared.

The man, who appeared to be in his thirties, entered the room, stood before Gaiska, and spoke.

“Young master. I heard you called for me. Is there something you need?”

“Ah. Deotres, you once mentioned that when winter comes, some adventurers run out of money and find themselves in a bind, didn’t you?”

“That’s right. There’s inevitably less work during the winter, and no one wants to go out of their way to work in the snow.”

“So, specifically, how many are there?”

“How many, you ask… Do you mean in terms of numbers?”

Gaiska frowned slightly at Deotres’ tone, but the man before him, though he spoke with a touch of insolence, was still someone who worked as his right-hand man.

Given that, he told himself it was important to show some leniency. With that in mind, he spoke up.

“That’s right. I want to gather as many people as possible.”

“Well, that depends… on what kind of work we’re talking about, doesn’t it? We’ll need to explain the details when we post the recruitment notice with the guild.”

“I need to teach a lesson to the one who… brought shame upon the name of the Marquis of Seisole.”

“You’re not talking about Crimson, are you?”

Hearing the nickname “Crimson,” Gaiska’s brows furrowed in displeasure.

But he quickly reminded himself that he was gathering a group to take revenge on Rei, and managed to quell his irritation.

Gaiska, of course, knew that Rei specialized in wide-area annihilation magic.

But this is Anesis.

There’s no way there’s a place where he could use such magic.

“That’s true. But don’t worry. The magic that man specializes in affects a wide area, right? If so, he shouldn’t be able to use that kind of magic in the middle of town.”

“No, from what I’ve heard, his skills as a warrior are also quite impressive, aren’t they?”

“I know that too. But if we wear him down using the adventurers without letting him cast spells, we’ll have the upper hand in the end.”

It was practically the same as saying he was treating the adventurers as disposable pawns, but he felt absolutely no guilt whatsoever.

To Gaiska, adventurers were nothing more than expendable tools—the kind of people he felt they should be grateful just to be used.

Given that, he felt absolutely no guilt whatsoever about how many people might die fighting Rei.

“No, but… we’re talking about Crimson, right? If we try to gather enough forces to fight him, no one will even accept the job. Even if we hire them outside the guild, once they find out the opponent is Crimson, plenty of them will run away without even fighting.”

If the request went through the guild, there would be penalties for deserting mid-mission.

In some cases, they might even be declared a bounty target.

But for a request made outside the guild, naturally, no such penalties exist.

Naturally, their reputation would suffer, but if they were to hire someone without disclosing that they’d be fighting an adventurer with a notorious nickname, it was almost certain that nearly everyone would run away.

That said, if you mention from the start that they’ll have to fight an adventurer with a notorious reputation—even even if it’s a job not arranged through the guild… and even if you promise a high reward—few would accept the job.

No, it’s not just that there would be few—it’s unclear if anyone would accept the job at all.

Naturally, there were adventurers in Anesis who had participated in the war against the Bestir Empire, and many of them had seen with their own eyes just how inhuman Rei truly was.

Having heard stories from such people, even Deotres wondered how many would actually accept a mission to fight Rei.

However… facing a pensive Deotres, Gaiska smiled.

“A mock battle. We’ll gather them under that pretext… and then reveal our true purpose only to a select few skilled fighters. …What do you think?”

He said this with a dark smile.
