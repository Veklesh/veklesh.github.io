While an unprecedented commotion was unfolding in Mejogo...

A vast space spread out underground.

Countless giants existed there, so many it seemed foolish to even attempt counting them.

Yet, not a single sound of commotion came from them—likely because the giants were asleep.

Until now, this had posed no particular problem.

Thanks to the fragments of black crystal surgically implanted within the giants' bodies.

Of course, this wasn't high-level surgery like Japanese doctors perform.

The procedure was simple: they would slit open the giant's body with a dagger and implant a black crystal fragment the size of a fingertip.

A wound of just a few centimeters made by a dagger would heal completely in no time at all, thanks to the giants' regenerative abilities.

It was the kind of procedure where calling it surgery might be considered an exaggeration.

But... the shoulder of one of the giants, who had been sleeping with its knees drawn up, twitched slightly.

If the black crystal fragment was working, this movement should be absolutely impossible.

If any of the Jaya people in this cave saw that sight, it would easily cause a huge commotion.

But only giants were present here, and there was no one to be surprised by it.

The giant's movement didn't stop... and it wasn't just one; several other giants also stirred slightly.

And the moving giants shared a common trait.

Namely, the black crystal shards that should have been embedded within their bodies had vanished.

The black crystal was essentially the most crucial key for maintaining firm control over the giants.

It was precisely because of them that the Titans lay quietly here, requiring no sustenance.

But... at this very moment, the black crystal fragments vanished.

And the giants whose black crystal shards vanished gradually began to regain their movement.

The number of giants making small movements steadily increased, and soon those movements could no longer be called small.

It was as if their previous state of slumber was giving way to awakening.

It wasn't just one or two giants; it spread to every single giant gathered here.

The awakening of a thousand giants. What would that bring... No one knew the tragedy it would unleash.

But that tragedy was drawing near.

"Hmm, there are quite a few of them after all, lookout."

Rei muttered, looking at the unconscious soldier.

Fortunately for the soldier, while his consciousness had been severed, there were no signs of broken limbs.

He probably got off with only bruises because he hadn't mentioned anything about prostitutes or the like.

The cave was a single path, but they encountered soldiers several times.

It seemed the soldiers weren't patrolling, but rather had fixed positions where they stood guard.

Thanks to this, they avoided having to fight large numbers of enemies in such a confined space.

(In manga, there's that setup where enemies lie in wait on each floor of a tower or enemy base, and you take them down one by one... Is this somewhat similar?)

That thought crossed Rei's mind, but given that this cave certainly wasn't easy to navigate, the person in charge here probably figured it was more efficient to station soldiers within a fixed area rather than have them patrol.

In truth, that wasn't necessarily wrong.

Under normal circumstances, it should have been possible for someone to call for reinforcements while allies were under attack, or to alert others to the enemy assault by some means.

But... the one and only, and greatest, miscalculation was that the attackers were individuals like Rei and his group, possessing overwhelming strength.

Thanks to that, before they could take any action, the soldiers had their consciousnesses reaped one by one.

Many of the unconscious soldiers were severely wounded.

This was because, in the area where many soldiers were on standby, there was nothing specific they needed to do, so they ended up chatting idly with their comrades.

No, idle chatter alone wouldn't have been such a concern.

But the fact that the topic of that chatter was about the prostitutes of Mejogo deeply provoked Elena and the others.

"Even after sending a considerable number of reinforcements to Mejogo's underground facility, they still have this much leeway, huh?"

"More accurately, they probably sent everyone except the soldiers needed to guard this place,"

Vihera spoke, and Marina replied.

Indeed, if there were still spare soldiers in Jaya... or more precisely, in this cave, they should have sent even more reinforcements.

Given that, the number of soldiers left here must be small.

That was Marina's reasoning... but the problem was that the number of soldiers she perceived as few actually exceeded her expectations.

(Then again, this place is a crucial facility for holding... preserving? Storing? Giants, so having a large number of soldiers here is only natural, right?)

Thinking this, she advanced deeper into the cave.

The passageway, strangely devoid of any branching paths, naturally made Rei question why.

Why was there not a single fork in the path, to such an unnatural degree?

Ever since entering the cave and passing the fork leading toward the giants, there had been absolutely no branching paths.

It seemed nothing short of unnatural.

"Hey, this cave... it's been tampered with, right?"

"I think so too. ...Though it does bother me that the ground remains so difficult to walk on. If they really had tampered with it, you'd think they'd have made it easier to walk on."

Elena, wearing the same Sleipnir Boots as Rei, lightly stepped on the cave floor.

What lay beneath was a rough path that would normally make running difficult.

If the Jaya people here were transporting supplies like materials, daily necessities, food, and drink, this rough ground should be a major drawback.

While it was wide enough for a cart to pass, if one were here, its wheels would undoubtedly get stuck and it would be unable to move.

Thinking this, Rei suddenly realized.

"Are they using giants to transport supplies?"

Given the attack on the Resistance, it was clear that Jaya could freely command giants.

And given the size of the giants, they could carry a considerable amount of cargo.

Moreover, since all the giants are the same size, they could even load carts with supplies and carry them barehanded.

"It's possible, I suppose. Though, using giants for something like that seems a bit difficult for an organization like Jaya."

"Since they're giants prepared as combat forces, wouldn't they avoid such actions?"

Rei couldn't deny Marina's point.

(Anyway, if we move forward... Huh?)

Rei, who had been walking down the passageway, gently reached out a hand, signaling for them to stop behind him.

Seeing the signal, Elena and the others halt.

"What's wrong?" Elena and the others turned their gaze toward Rei. Rei pointed ahead down the passageway.

There stood a massive door.

It was a door large enough for even a giant to pass through with ease.

...Honestly, though, having seen similar doors countless times in Mejogo's underground facilities, his reaction was more like, "So what?"

Still, the mere existence of such a door meant that beyond it were undoubtedly people from Jaya... and if Ryutas' words were correct, those deeply involved in the giant affair.

And naturally, soldiers stood guard before the door.

But unlike the soldiers Rei and the others had encountered before, these weren't wasting time chatting idly with their comrades.

Being entrusted with guarding this door, they were undoubtedly elite... but at the same time, beyond that door were people holding positions of significant status within Jaya.

For the soldiers, showing any lack of seriousness in front of such individuals would be tantamount to suicide.

After all, this wasn't a regular military unit, but the secret organization, Jaya.

Things that would be unthinkable in a regular military force could be easily carried out here because it was an underground organization.

It's precisely because you weren't taking your guard duty seriously that it's unacceptable. You're not needed.

So, to verify the giants' combat strength, fight them with your life on the line... that sort of thing.

Thinking about it that way, it's only natural the soldiers would take their jobs seriously.

"This is troublesome."

Rei muttered under his breath, and Elena and the others nodded in agreement.

If they were guarding seriously, it meant they couldn't just exploit an opening and rush in to take them down like they had before.

Of course, they had the confidence they could do whatever they wanted if they really tried, but making a big move here risked alerting whoever was on the other side of the door to their presence.

"Even if I use magic, mine is about as flashy as it gets."

The only magic Rei could use was fire magic.

Naturally, it often ended up being flashy magic.

"What about Elena and Marina?"

"I suppose it's not impossible, but if those soldiers were to fall, wouldn't it still alert those on the other side of the door?"

"Right. If we take them down, it has to be with one strike... and silently. Or maybe make them vanish instantly so there's no sound."

Marina, who casually suggested annihilation, clearly had her own thoughts about the organization known as Jaya.

"That's a bit much. ...Well, what can we do. We'll just have to take them all down as quickly as possible, even if it makes some noise."

In the end, she decided it would be better to just take them down normally rather than use magic.

"Are you sure? They'll find us right away."

"True. If we just walk over there normally, we'll be spotted. But... what about approaching from above?"

This cave had ample height for giants to move around freely.

The height to the ceiling was about five to six meters.

However, there were places where stone icicles—stalactites—hung down from above.

Given that, the method Rei was considering carried various potential dangers.

"Elena and I have Sleipnir Boots that let us walk through the air. If we used them to launch a surprise attack from above... I won't say we wouldn't be spotted, but don't you think it'd be harder to be seen?"

Fortunately, Rei and Elena were currently positioned at a bend in the path leading to the massive gate.

It was a gentle curve, but it posed no problem for hiding their figures.

The only drawback was the considerable distance from the bend to the gate itself.

If there were no ceiling, they could move higher up and avoid being seen by the soldiers, but with the ceiling at its current height, there was a real possibility they could be spotted.

Still, deciding that was ultimately the best option, Rei turned his gaze to Elena.

Understanding the meaning behind that look, Elena nodded immediately.

"Once we launch our attack, Vihera and the others should start moving too. I think it should be fine, but you never know if they might pull something strange."

"Yes, understood. But we need to be as quiet as possible, right?"

Rei nodded in response to Marina's questioning words.

"Right. ...Elena, are you ready?"

"Yeah."

Nodding at Rei's words, Elena retreated backward alongside him.

Even when using Sleipnir Boots, it was better to have some distance to cover to gain even a little advantage.

Their movement reflected this understanding.

After retreating about ten paces, they both began running together.

Reaching maximum speed in just a few strides, they leapt, trying to make as little noise as possible.

In mid-air, they activated Sleipnir Boots, taking their first step in the air.

That single step propelled them nearly to the ceiling. With the next step, they kicked off mid-air like a triangle jump, materializing in the hallway with the door.

The soldiers, who had been seriously monitoring the surroundings for any abnormalities, suddenly noticed something off.

"What is it?"

But even with that sense of unease, they couldn't tell what was there.

He glanced at a nearby colleague, who seemed to have felt the same unease and was also surveying the surroundings.

Then, almost without thinking... truly without thinking, the moment he glanced upward, two figures moving near the ceiling came into view.

Because the figures were near the ceiling, they could only stare in stunned silence... The next instant, a figure descended from above onto the soldier, knocking him unconscious.

I thought I needed to shout something...

...Rei gazed at the unconscious soldier and then turned his eyes to Elena, who stood before another soldier who had lost consciousness just as he had.
