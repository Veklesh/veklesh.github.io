"Sorry, Rei. Did I keep you waiting?"

"Nah, I haven't been waiting that long. I just got here myself."

As Rei exchanged these words with Kenny, he suddenly wondered if this exchange might resemble the typical small talk people have when meeting up before a date.

……That said, seeing Kenny arrive sitting in the driver’s seat of a horse-drawn carriage, it certainly didn’t match the scene Rei had imagined, not by a long shot.

"So you went with the carriage after all."

"Yeah. …I mean, Rei, you can’t ride a horse, can you?"

“No, I can ride if you tell me to.”

Rei replied to Kenny’s remark, and it wasn’t exactly a complete lie.

However, the only horses Rei could ride were those he’d trained rigorously since childhood as a military mount.

It wasn’t because there was anything suspicious about Rei’s riding skills… but rather because of the influence of Rei always being with Set.

In other words, Set’s presence and scent have become ingrained in Rei’s body.

Even so, if he’s at a distance—like in the driver’s seat of a carriage—it doesn’t affect the horse that much… but when Rei tries to mount a horse, the horse inevitably senses Set’s presence.

As a result, some horses may even panic.

Even if it doesn’t go that far, there’s a high chance the horse will strain itself unnecessarily and get injured.

To prevent that from happening, it was safer for the horses’ well-being to travel by carriage rather than have Rei ride them.

“Hmm. Well, as your older sister, I’ll let it slide this time.”

Kenny smiled from the driver’s seat, and Rei decided there was no point in saying anything more… so she quietly got into the carriage.

“Huh… So this is where Rei-kun dismembers monsters?”

Kenny muttered, looking around with curiosity.

To Rei, since he’d been here countless times before, it didn’t seem like there was anything particularly unusual about it.

But for Kenny, who normally never leaves Gilm, simply being outside Gilm was a novelty in itself.

On top of that, coming all the way out to the forest like this—especially considering the risk of monster attacks—was something he could never normally do.

The only reason they could feel safe outside Gilm like this was because they were accompanied by Rei, one of Gilm’s most capable adventurers.

As a cat-like beastman, Kenny’s senses were sharper than those of an ordinary human, but they still couldn’t hold a candle to Rei’s, who was a professional.

……Though, to be precise, Kenny viewed Rei not as a bodyguard, but as a date.

“That’s true. Though, it’s not exactly a place reserved just for me.”

This spot is fairly well-known among the Gilm’s adventurers.

It’s not too far from Gilm, and since there’s a river flowing nearby, any blood, bodily fluids, or pieces of meat left over from the butchering can be washed away immediately.

However, naturally, this spot isn’t open to just anyone.

Well, technically, you don’t need anyone’s permission just to use it, so you can use it normally. But if you’re butchering outside the borders of Gilm and letting the smell of blood linger, it’s not uncommon for monsters or wild beasts to be drawn to the scent.

If you’re unable to properly handle an attack from such creatures, you’ll likely lose the monster you’re butchering, and in the worst-case scenario, you might even lose your own life.

Unless you have the strength to handle such situations, using this place could lead to the worst possible outcome.

“Huh, I see. And it’s so beautiful, too.”

Kenny murmured thoughtfully as he looked around.

The sunlight is filtered by the leaves of the surrounding trees, casting dappled light all around, and the sound of the flowing river creates a refreshing atmosphere. To someone unaware of the dangers, this place might seem like the perfect spot for a date.

To be honest, Kenny would have preferred to come here alone with Rei, bringing a sandwich or something, rather than engaging in an activity like skinning animals...

That said, there was no doubt he was more than satisfied just to be here with Rei like this.

Seemingly oblivious to Kenny’s mood, Rei pulls the black sea bream monster, Baeros, out of his Misty Ring.

“Yikes!”

A scream escaped Kenny’s lips at the sight of Baeros.

He had seen Baeros when Ray took it out yesterday.

But even so, seeing a monster nearly three meters long suddenly appear was bound to startle him.

Moreover, since it wasn’t the kind of monster he was used to seeing in Gilm, but one that lived in the sea, it was no wonder Kenny didn’t recognize it.

“So, anyway… you brought the tools for scaling it, right?”

“……Huh? Oh, yeah. One of the senior members of the guild used to work as an adventurer in a town near the sea, so I asked them to lend me some. …That’s why I was a little late getting to the meeting spot.”

Saying that, Kenny walked around to the back of the carriage and pulled out a strangely shaped blade.

Looking at the blade, which curved sharply, Rei felt as though he’d seen something like it before… and eventually remembered.

It was a weapon that appeared in a manga he’d read back in Japan—a blade called a “chotelle.”

Because the blade curves so sharply, it allows the user to attack in a way that dodges the opponent’s shield, even if they try to block the attack with it.

Of course, the details differed from the chotel Rei had seen, but its general shape was unmistakably similar to that weapon.

It was also quite large, and to Rei, who recognized it as a chotel, it was no longer a tool but unmistakably a weapon.

“That’s quite a vicious-looking thing.”

“Really? To me, it looks a bit hard to use. …Besides, this is supposedly a magic item, so maybe you’ll be interested in it too, Rei?”

There were various other tools for scaling fish, but the main reason Kenny chose this one—aside from the fact that the person he consulted told him it was the easiest to use—was that it was a magic item.

It’s fairly well-known that Rei collects magic items.

Naturally, Kenny knew this too, and he’d chosen it thinking that if he showed Rei a magical item he hadn’t seen before, he’d be delighted.

Sure enough, when Rei heard that the blade resembling a scythe was a magic item, he looked at it with keen interest.

“Here, take this.”

Kenny simply handed the magic item he had been holding to Rei.

Rei took it and, examining the blade, asked Kenny.

“So, what kind of effect does this magic item have?”

“Apparently, the blade’s shape changes to conform to whatever it’s touching.”

“...So, if I press this blade against the fish’s body and then activate the magic item, the blade’s shape changes, and that’s how I peel off the scales?”

“That’s what the person who lent it to me said, but Rei, can you do it?”

“I don’t know. I’ll give it a try, anyway.”

Saying that, he touched the blade of the short sword to the scaled part of the body near the gills of the Baeros lying on the ground and channeled his magic into it.

Then, the blade slowly… yes, very gradually began to change shape.

(Ah... I thought it might be interesting if I could use it in actual combat, but at this speed, it’s a bit hard to use. If it could change shape instantly, I suppose there would be a use for it in battle. I guess I don’t need to negotiate a purchase after all.)

It’s undoubtedly a handy tool for stripping scales, but its uses are too limited, so it probably wasn’t something Rei really wanted.

In any case, she carefully slid the Shotel—which had taken nearly thirty seconds to take the shape of Baeros’s torso—along the surface, taking care not to injure herself.

“Oh, I’ll hold this end. It’s so big, I don’t think the recoil will make it move.”

Saying that, Kenny pressed down on the tail section of the Baeros.

Deep down, she was thinking of it as “working together with Rei,” but she didn’t let it show on her face and simply held the tail down earnestly.

At the same time, she can’t help but wonder just how delicious Baeros’s flesh must be—probably because Kenny is a beastman.

“...This is tough.”

Rei used his Shotel to peel away Baeros’s scales, but it was harder than he’d expected.

Since he has almost no experience scaling fish, he simply can’t seem to get the hang of it.

Besides, today is the first time he’s ever used a chisel.

He’s managing to remove the scales, but in many places, he’s peeling off the fish’s skin along with them.

To begin with, Rei had never been particularly skilled at tasks involving skinning.

She was aware of this herself, so she actively worked on honing her skills, and by now she had reached a level slightly above average—but that was strictly limited to monsters on the ground… or those that fly.

Sea monsters are inevitably different from land monsters in various ways, and as a result, this was causing Rei to struggle.

Still, if she peeled the scales carefully, it wouldn’t end up being too bad in the end, and she managed to successfully remove all the scales.

Since these scales could be sold as materials, it was only natural to store them securely in the Misty Ring.

“Now that I’ve finished peeling the hardest scales, the rest shouldn’t be too difficult. …There aren’t any internal organs or anything, either.”

After peeling the scales from both the front and back, Kenny spoke these words, and Rei couldn’t deny it.

Normally, handling a fish’s innards presents a different kind of difficulty from peeling the scales.

You have to slit open the belly, remove the innards without damaging them, and cut them out neatly.

But... during the commotion of capturing the Baelos, its belly skin had been torn, and most of its innards had been washed out into the sea to feed the fish, shellfish, and crabs.

In that case, what remains isn’t raw material but edible parts… only ways to use it as food.

“Filleting it into three pieces… that might be a bit difficult with this chisel, don’t you think? But then again, a knife wouldn’t work either.”

The Baeros is nearly three meters long.

Naturally, the flesh is thick, and trying to fillet it into three pieces with just a knife was quite a challenge.

(A knife like a Japanese sword—the kind used in tuna dissection shows—would be handy in a situation like this.)

Staring at the knife, Rei hesitated, unsure what to do.

Of course, filleting it into three pieces with just a knife was a stretch, but that didn’t mean it was impossible.

However, if he tried to do that, the flesh would undoubtedly end up in tatters, and a large amount of meat would likely remain on the bones.

That said, with a knife, it might be possible to scrape the meat off the bones.

(I seem to recall that the "naka-ochi" cut was that kind of meat.)

Rei recalled his time in Japan, but that didn’t mean he wanted to go to the trouble of cutting the fish in a way that left the flesh attached to the bones.

“I might as well use the Death Scythe…”

“Wait, wait, Rei! Isn’t that going a bit too far, no matter how you look at it?! If you’re going to use a Death Scythe, I think you’d be better off using that chisel.”

Hearing Rei suddenly mention the Death Scythe, Kenny must have realized what he was planning to do.

He said this in a flustered tone.

Even to Kenny, the flesh of the monster known as Baelos looked incredibly delicious.

In fact, the field guide he’d found in the library stated that its flesh was exceptionally delicious, so there was no doubt it would be tasty.

That’s exactly why seeing Rei casually carve up a monster… no, a fish, was something a cat-beastman simply couldn’t tolerate.

“Then, with this chotel… Tsk, even though there are no innards and hardly any blood left, it must have been drawn here by the smell. Kenny, wait here near this fish for a moment.”

Sensing something approaching, Rei said with an air of annoyance and handed the chotel to Kenny.

Seeing Rei’s expression, Kenny must have realized that something—whether a monster or a wild animal, he couldn’t tell—was approaching.

Kenny took the shovel handed to him and turned his gaze toward the carriage.

If the horses were attacked, it would be a real mess, and Kenny’s honest thought was that he needed someone to help defend them.

As a receptionist at the guild, Kenny had learned basic self-defense, and as a cat-like beastman, she possessed exceptional physical abilities.

Even so, if a high-rank monster were to appear, there would be nothing she could do.

Kenny trembled slightly, but Rei, as if to reassure her, pulled the Death Scythe and the Twilight Spear from his Misty Ring and spoke.

“Leave it to me. I’ll definitely protect you.”

Saying that and flashing a smile at Kenny, Rei immediately ran toward the source of the approaching presence.

As Kenny watched Rei’s back disappear into the distance, there was no trace of fear left within him… only Rei’s smile, which gave him such a sense of security just by looking at it, was etched deeply into his mind.
