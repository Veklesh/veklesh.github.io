Under the summer sun, Rei flew through the sky astride Set.

Gazing at the cumulonimbus clouds, Rei spoke up.

"You okay back there!?"

The wind was fairly strong, so he shouted a bit louder than usual.

From beneath Set, a voice answered Rei.

"I'm fine, no problems so far!"

Yes, it was the voice of Vihera, who had accepted the lizardman extermination request alongside Rei.

Set couldn't fly with anyone but Rei on his back, but he could carry things like the huge bear carcass or Hester when she was attacked by a goblin horde.

Hearing this, Vihera suggested this method.

Though, it wasn't anything particularly special.

Hester was being carried with her shoulders gripped by Set's front paws, while Vihera was simply holding onto Set's right front paw with her own hand.

Of course, a rope tied between Set's paw and Vihera's hand ensured safety in case of a fall, but even so, most wouldn't willingly attempt such a stunt.

But doing it with glee was just Vihera.

This was a request to exterminate a lizardman horde that she and Rei had accepted. Whether traveling by carriage or on horseback, they simply couldn't keep up with Set's speed.

This was the method Vihera proposed.

This way, it wouldn't look ridiculous to onlookers, and most importantly, if push came to shove, he wouldn't have to ask Set to let go; he could detach himself from Set by his own will.

Of course, she couldn't pull off something like Rei, who had Sleipnir's shoes, and jump down from high in the sky.

But even so, the fact that she could leave of her own volition when necessary was a great relief to Vihera.

(I never imagined the view from this height would be so breathtaking.)

Hanging from Set's front legs, Vihera gazed down at the earth once more.

Spread out below was a vast green carpet.

It was a view she could see for half the year, during spring and summer, yet it remained a sight worth seeing for Vihera.

(Do dragon riders riding flying dragons always see a view like this? I must admit, I'm a little envious.)

So the two and one creature flew through the air. Finally, about ten minutes' distance from Gilm—which was quite a distance, given Set's wingspan—they spotted their target: a group of lizardmen.

They spotted them...

"Why is the battle already underway?"

Rei muttered, gazing down at the ground.

...Yes, currently on the ground, the lizardmen were engaged in combat with a group that appeared to be adventurers.

Originally, the request to exterminate the lizardmen had been accepted by Rei and Vihera.

After accepting the request, they had a meal under the guise of a meeting—a snack, as far as Rei was concerned—but even so, considering Set's speed, it was faster than simply galloping a horse at full speed.

Despite that, when they arrived, the battle had already begun.

"What should we do?"

Hanging onto Set's leg, Vihera asked. Rei paused for only a few seconds before deciding and speaking up.

"We took on this fight in the first place. That means the ones fighting now are doing it on their own. So, if we join the fight, they can't complain, right?"

"That's true, but if we're not careful, we might end up clashing with those adventurers too."

"We've got the justification of having accepted the request. Even if they..." He cut himself off mid-sentence.

Cutting himself off mid-sentence, Rei shifted his gaze toward the adventurers battling the lizardmen.

They were trying desperately to break away from this spot, but the lizardmen wouldn't let them.

In other words, it wasn't that they'd willingly challenged this horde to battle; it was an unexpected encounter.

(And there are two... no, three lizardman leaders? They're trying to surround those adventurers.)

The pack led by the lizardman leader numbered just over ten, bringing the total to about forty.

While considerably fewer than a hundred goblins, the individual capabilities of lizardmen were incomparable to goblins.

Of course, not all packs are the same; they vary greatly depending on the monster leading them.

If there are Goblin Leaders commanding dozens of goblins, it wouldn't be surprising to find a Lizardman Leader commanding nearly a hundred lizardmen.

Among them, the lizardman leader commanding this group could be considered standard.

"Then I'll take down the lizardman leader of the group attacking those adventurers head-on. What about you, Vihera?"

"Right. Then I'll take the lizardman leader of the group on the adventurers' left side."

From where Rei sat astride Set's back, she couldn't see Vihera's face.

But even so, he could easily imagine Vihera smiling.

"Then, Set, you take the right-side group."

"Gururu!"

After this brief... truly brief discussion, Rei had him move directly above the targeted group and then casually leapt off Set's back.

"Alright, I'm going ahead."

He muttered this as he passed by Vihera, who flashed a look of surprise for just an instant.

He knew she had Sleipnir's boots, but even so, he probably hadn't expected her to jump from such a height... a hundred meters above ground.

(Come to think of it, was this the first time I showed him jumping from this height? No, I think I've shown him before. I've jumped from this altitude so many times, I don't even remember anymore.)

Recalling Vihera's startled expression, Rei retrieved the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear from the Misty Ring as he fell through the air.

Fortunately, the enemy, though strong, was merely a Lizardman.

To Rei, they might be somewhat tough, but he didn't consider them formidable foes.

That was precisely why he had prepared his dual-spear technique.

There was also the simple fact that using both spears meant more attacks than relying solely on the Death Scythe.

As he continued his descent toward the ground, he used Sleipnir's Boots several times to adjust his position.

His target landing spot was, naturally, directly above the Lizardman Leader, whose body was larger than the others.

Just as he had done with the Goblin Leader, he intended to take down the Lizardman Leader with a single strike.

He swung the Death Scythe in his right hand... but at that instant, for reasons unknown to Rei, the Lizardman Leader sensed the attack and swiftly raised the longsword in his hand overhead.

A normal strike might have been blocked.

But unfortunately, the one attacking this time was far from ordinary.

The blow from the Death Scythe, swung with just his right hand and without channeling any magic, effortlessly sliced through the blade of the longsword held by the Lizardman Leader. Simultaneously, it cleaved the leader's head clean off his body.

His body split left and right, entrails spilling from the severed stumps as the bisected form collapsed to the ground.

The surrounding lizardmen couldn't comprehend such an attack from Rei.

It was only natural, given it had come crashing down from directly above.

It was a perfect surprise attack... a strike worthy of being called lightning fast.

Witnessing this scene unfold before their eyes, the lizardmen's movements froze for an instant.

And that single instant was all Rei needed to make his next move.

"Flying Slash!"

The only ones around were enemy lizardmen.

Given that, there was no need to worry about harming his allies.

A slash flew from the swung Death Scythe, severing the neck of one lizardman.

Simultaneously, the Twilight Spear gripped in his left hand crushed the skull of the lizardman to his left.

He then attempted to deliver a sweeping horizontal strike with the Twilight Spear, which he had swiftly pulled back to his side... A metallic clang echoed around him, causing Rei, who had produced it, to frown slightly.

The force of violently pulling the Twilight Spear back caused its shaft to collide with the shaft of the Death Scythe.

But his panic lasted only an instant.

The next instant, he severed one nearby lizardman at the torso with the Death Scythe and crushed another lizardman's skull with the Twilight Spear.

Only now, after being dealt such blows, did the lizardmen finally come to their senses. They charged toward Rei all at once.

Among them were undoubtedly those seeking vengeance for their fallen leader.

In response to the lizardmen's attack, Rei deliberately swung his Death Scythe to deliver a warning strike.

...Under normal circumstances, merely swinging the Death Scythe wouldn't be perceived as such a threat.

But with Rei, it was different.

After all, the flying slash released from the blade of the Death Scythe had just taken down one lizardman.

That alone forced the lizardmen to be wary of Rei's strike.

(Huh. Unlike goblins and kobolds, they don't immediately attack anyone they see as an enemy. ...Well, at least not at first.)

Recalling how goblins aside, kobolds had attacked him even when they knew they stood no chance against him, Rei wore a slightly surprised expression.

He glanced at the lizardmen surrounding him, then suddenly shifted his gaze to another group of lizardmen.

Set had already defeated the lizardman leader and was now trampling the remaining lizardmen.

With a single swing of Set's foreleg, lizardmen's limbs were blown off; some had their torsos torn in half, others their skulls crushed.

A single strike from his beak easily pierced the lizardmen's scales. While his body slams didn't instantly kill, they sent victims flying several meters.

Though he wasn't using any skills, the Lizardmen were being utterly crushed by Set.

Rei, judging that Set was fine, turned his gaze toward Vihera.

There, Vihera was swinging fists and feet at the lizardmen.

She wasn't attacking the lizardman leader who was giving orders to the others. It wasn't because she wanted to take him down with a surprise strike like Rei; she simply preferred to challenge him head-on.

Though, this wasn't about fighting fairly... it was simply because Vihera genuinely enjoyed the fight.

Regardless of her motives, the lizardman leader's attention was undeniably fixed on Vihera.

Two of the lizardman leaders present were already dead, and the remaining one couldn't take its eyes off Vihera.

As a result, the adventurers under attack by the lizardmen swarm managed to escape without taking any further hits.

(Well, at least this should be enough to save those adventurers over there. That leaves just these lizardmen to deal with.)

Swinging his Death Scythe wide to hold them off, Rei charged into the lizardmen, whose movements froze momentarily at the sight.

Closing the distance while wielding long-handled weapons... one in each hand, no less... was nothing short of suicide under normal circumstances.

The most powerful part of a polearm is, naturally, its tip—the spearhead for a spear, the axe head for a poleaxe, and so on.

But closing the distance makes attacking with that most powerful point impossible.

But... Rei's physical prowess easily defied such common sense.

The swinging Death Scythe and the handle of the Twilight Spear blew away surrounding Lizardmen, snapping their arms and torso bones.

"Shhhhh!"

Screams erupted from the lizardmen's mouths as they were blown away.

Sensing the threat as Rei closed in, the lizardmen each tried to retreat swiftly backward... but naturally, Rei gave them no such opening.

The relentless blows of the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear claimed the lizardmen's lives without mercy.

Within mere minutes, every lizardman remaining in the area lay fallen to the ground.

Few remained intact—limbs, tails, heads severed. The air thickened with the pungent, metallic stench of rust... of blood.

"...Well, that's about it."

He swung the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear, flicking the lizardmen's blood from their blades, and muttered.

He carefully scanned the area, wary that any lizardmen might still be alive and feigning death, but fortunately, there were no living lizardmen left.

"Now then, what's happening over there?"

He turned his gaze toward where the battle between Set and Vihera was taking place.
