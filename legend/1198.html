A giant Umbris? Hearing Arogan's words, Rei took out his detector and directed its beam.

Sure enough, the detector indicated an Ambris existed in the direction Alogan and the others had come from.

The intensity of the reaction felt somehow different from the detector he'd been watching until just now.

(This... Could it be...? Could it really be?)

A stronger reaction than before. And above all, considering the words "giant Ambris" spoken by Arogan, could it be that there was something akin to the master of the Ambris?

It wasn't unreasonable for Rei to think that.

"Vihera, Set."

With that brief call, the one person and one creature seemed to understand exactly what Rei wanted.

Vihera nodded swiftly, and Set gave a short bark.

"Sorry, but we're going to meet this giant Ambris thing. I wanted to catch up and talk about everything after so long, but we'll have to save the details for later."

Culotte shouted at Rei's words.

Scola, Culotte's childhood friend and the group's voice of reason, opened her mouth, slightly out of breath.

"Hey, Calm down a bit, Cullot. We need to hurry right now. ...Rei, that Ambris was huge. Be careful, okay?"

"Yeah."

Though Scola usually spoke with a gentle tone, she quickly relayed the message now, seemingly without that luxury.

"I know. What are you guys going to do now?"

"We were planning to go to Gilm and report this, but..."

She paused there, likely because Rei was standing right in front of her.

They were peers who took the rank-up exam together, but Rei had grown rapidly since then.

At the very least, they understood there was a huge gap in power between him and themselves, still languishing here.

He was now among the top combatants even within Gilm.

Precisely because of that, meeting Rei here now gave Ambrith hope that things might work out.

But at the same time, remembering the enormous Umbris they had seen, he knew he couldn't feel completely at ease.

Ultimately, all Scola and the others could do was inform Gilm about the giant Ambris they had seen, just in case something happened to Rei.

"I see. Understood. Then I'll leave that to you. Once you arrive at Gilm, tell them to send reinforcements. We intend to handle this ourselves, but since the enemy is an Ambris, we can't predict what might happen."

If this were an ordinary monster, Rei wouldn't have been this cautious.

But this was an Umbris—a being without magic stones or self-awareness.

It couldn't be called a monster... it was more fitting to call it a natural phenomenon.

Precisely because it was such an opponent, a sense of unease stirred within Rei.

The Umbrus Rei had encountered before had been defeated with surprising ease.

But precisely because it was an existence seemingly devoid of any semblance of self-awareness, if asked whether he could defeat it just as easily this time, he couldn't help but tilt his head in doubt.

Whether Scola understood Rei's thoughts or simply recognized that the Ambrith they faced was utterly hopeless, she nodded immediately at Rei's words.

"Got it. Well, we'll handle this end, so you guys do the same, okay?"

Exchanging only a few words, without warming up old friendships after so long apart, Rei and his group parted ways with Skola and hers.

Rei mounted Set's back and, after a few strides, leapt into the sky.

He turned mid-air and descended close to the ground, where Vihera leapt up and grabbed Set's front legs.

This familiar method of travel had become routine lately. Rei and the others flew toward the direction indicated by the detector's readings.

Considering Set's flight speed, they should find Ambrith in just a few minutes.

As Rei flew through the sky thinking this, he spotted Ambrus ahead in his line of sight before even a few minutes had passed.

This was because it possessed a size incomparable to the Ambrus Rei had defeated before.

The one Rei had defeated before was an Ambrith attached to a werewolf, so its size was naturally smaller than or comparable to that of a werewolf.

But the one now in Rei's line of sight was larger than a ten-meter-tall tree, wider in breadth, and denser in its mist-like quality. It was an entity that would be more appropriately described as a rain cloud than mere mist.

"...It's huge, isn't it?"

Vihera's voice reached Rei's ears as she clung to Set's front paws.

There was no fear in that whisper.

Only pure astonishment existed.

"Yeah, you're right. It's a bit bigger than I expected."

Of course, there was no trace of fear in Rei's own mutter either.

While he felt something unsettling about the existence of an Ambris, the fact that he had defeated one before likely played a significant role.

Also, the fact that Rei wasn't acting with self-awareness like the Ambrus they had defeated was a factor.

"What do you say, Rei? Strike first and finish it off in one go?"

"Yeah, that's probably the better approach."

Despite the considerable distance, Ray could still clearly perceive Ambrus.

To Vihera, who asks if he'll attack such an entity head-on, Rei gives a short reply.

If this were a monster, it might be necessary to wait and see how it moves.

But in this case, the opponent was closer to a natural phenomenon, devoid of self-awareness.

Moreover, it was an utterly unreasonable natural phenomenon, the sort that could evolve humanoid monsters like goblins into leader species.

Given that, if we carelessly try to wait and see, we have no idea what kind of reaction we'll get. It's quicker and easier to take it down with a preemptive strike.

"...Ah."

A small sound escaped Vihera's lips as she watched Ambrith.

It wasn't a reaction to Rei's words, but the Ambrith at the end of her gaze trembled slightly before its edge separated... and in the next instant, the separated part flew through the air as if carried by the wind.

Judging by the separated part alone, it was roughly the same size as the Umbris Rei had defeated.

"I see. So what I defeated was one created like that... created? Anyway, it was a fragment of the real Ambris."

"Yes. And by the way, what Mireine and the others saw was probably the same kind of thing. ...But what do we do? Even if we defeat that main body, what if the other small Ambriss remain?"

We didn't know how many had been born from that main body so far, but it was undeniable that numerous Ambrus had been spawned.

"What do you expect me to do? Anyway, if we don't deal with that main body, it'll just keep spawning more Umbris, right? Besides... we don't have time to talk about it anymore."

Set, realizing Rei and Vihera were talking, had slowed his approach toward the giant Umbris. Even so, Set's... the griffin's flight speed was incredibly fast.

The giant Ambris had already closed to a position not far from Rei and the others.

"Alright, I'll take care of that giant thing for now. Vihera, keep an eye on things and let me know if anything seems off."

"...Be careful, okay?"

Vihera murmured, sounding slightly worried.

If the opponent were a monster, she wouldn't have been this worried.

But this time, the enemy wasn't a monster—it was a natural phenomenon.

Maybe something was wrong with Rei too.

It was only natural to think that way.

"Leave it to me. I've defeated it once before. ...Well, it is that big, so I can't say for sure if it'll go down as easily as last time. ...Alright, I'm off."

As he closed in on the Giant Amblis, he gave that brief announcement. Directly above the Giant Amblis, Rei leapt off Set's back.

As he descended toward the ground, Rei compressed the magic power within his body.

Watching the giant Ambrus's form rapidly approach directly below him... as he fell through the air, the magic power within Rei's body turned crimson.

The concentrated magic, now visible, turned red, crimson, vermilion... and thus activated the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

The Death Scythe, retrieved from the Misty Ring as he fell from Set's back.

Holding that great scythe in his hand, he falls toward the giant Ambros.

"Die!"

The Death Scythe swung with a battle cry sliced through the black mist.

...However, the black mist formed Ambros's body.

Naturally, there was no satisfying impact when he swung the Death Scythe at Rei, yet it was undoubtedly slicing through the black mist.

(How about this...!?)

Landing on the ground, Rei lashed out at the remaining core of Ambrith, whipping the crimson magical energy enveloping his own body—the flame magic—into a whip-like form.

The Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, born from Rei’s magical power—unmatched by ordinary mages—effortlessly incinerated the giant Ambrith’s body.

Though the black mist vanished upon contact, Rei remained vigilant, unleashing the skill Deep Flame to project the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour magic.

Deep Flame, severed from the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour and striking the ground, instantly ignited a hellfire.

The black mist that touched those flames was instantly consumed and vanished.

Despite having parts of its body—parts it could be said were its own flesh—burned away one after another, the giant Ambris showed no sign of caring.

Just like the smaller Ambris Rei had fought before, it seemed destined to simply vanish without a fight.

Manipulating the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, swinging the Death Scythe, and launching Deep Flames, Rei contemplated this.

Within seconds, most of the black mist that had formed the giant Umbrus vanished... but the next instant, the remaining portion of the black mist before Rei suddenly began gathering into a single spot.

"Guess it's not going to be that easy after all."

Rei clicked his tongue in frustration, though of course the mindless Ambris couldn't possibly respond.

Still, even without words, the black mist forming Umbris's body gathering in one spot was clearly a response to Rei.

(They say it's like a natural phenomenon... but even so, how would it respond if I were harmed?)

The thought flashed through Rei's mind, but he possessed no such propriety as to simply wait passively for the other side to act.

"Flying Slash!"

The Flying Slash, unleashed while wearing the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour, shot straight toward the gathering black mist.

Immediately after releasing the Flying Slash, Rei charged straight toward the gathering point of the black mist.

With the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour enhancing his physical abilities beyond normal, Rei caught up to the Flying Slash in an instant.

Simultaneously, the Death Scythe shifted to Rei's left hand, while the Twilight Spear gripped his right.

"Haah!"

The Twilight Spear, forged by Azimov using vast quantities of rare materials, easily absorbed the magic power of Rei, who had deployed the Flame Emperor’s Crimson Armour.

He channeled magic into the Twilight Spear, piercing the thickest part of the black mist.

Then, instantly withdrawing the Twilight Spear, he swung the Death Scythe in his left hand.

"Multi-Slash!"

As the swung Death Scythe sliced through the black mist, another slash tore through the space directly beside it.

Then the launched Flying Slash arrived, slicing through the black mist once more.

"Finish it! Take this!"

The final attack unleashed was Deep Flame, releasing the fiery magic that had surrounded Rei.

Launched while maintaining distance from the momentum of the Multi-Slash, the Deep Flame blazed into a colossal inferno, large enough to swallow the black mist itself.

"...How was that?"

The flames that flared up instantly gradually faded away.

When the flames finally extinguished completely before Rei, who watched intently... all that remained was the black mist, almost entirely consumed.

But seeing that Ambrus's black mist still lingered, Rei concluded it wasn't dead yet. He switched the Death Scythe and Twilight Spear he held, placing the Death Scythe in his right hand and the Twilight Spear in his left as usual, then stared intently at Ambrus.

(The first Ambros I defeated vanished without much of a fight... So, will this one just disappear too? Or should I land a finishing blow here?)

Against a normal monster, a follow-up strike here would finish it off easily.

But what stood before Rei now wasn't a monster... or even a living creature. It was a natural phenomenon.

Not knowing what would happen if he attacked now, he watched its movements... Suddenly, several black mists rose from the ground.

Of course, Rei knew exactly what they were.

Because it was the very thing Rei had been attacking just moments ago.

The black mists gathered together... and before he knew it, the colossal form of Ambrus stood before Rei once more.
