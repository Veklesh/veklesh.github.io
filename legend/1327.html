Rei and his companions arrived in the slums, naturally drawing attention as they made their way through the area.

Normally, a group consisting of one man—a small, teenage-looking guy—alongside two stunningly beautiful women and a petite girl would be seen as easy prey, and it wouldn't be surprising if someone tried to hassle them... but in the slums today, no one dared lay a finger on Rei and his companions.

Word about Rei and his group had naturally reached the slums, and there were no fools reckless enough to mess with such opponents.

...Especially since Set was with them.

Who in their right mind would want to make an enemy of a griffin... especially a rare griffin? Only someone suicidal would consider it.

"They're quieter than I expected,"

Vihera murmured, her words tinged with a hint of disappointment.

She'd just finished sparring with Rei moments ago, and her combat urge probably wasn't fully satisfied yet.

Vihera scanned the area, hoping someone strong might show up.

There were clearly some who fit the description of thugs, but when Vihera's gaze fell on them, they quietly looked away.

Beyond them were children, but they hadn't registered in Vihera's eyes from the start.

(They avoid getting involved with strong opponents, huh. Of course, not everyone is like that... Somehow, this feels less like a slum district and more like a slime district.)

Watching Vihera, Rei suddenly had that thought.

Of course, he didn't voice it.

"Vihera, there's something more important to do right now than looking for a strong opponent."

Without letting Vihera know he'd been thinking about the slum district, Rei addressed him.

Normally, Vihera would often tune out others' words if there was a chance of fighting a strong opponent, but he must have judged there was no particularly strong opponent around right now.

He showed a hint of dissatisfaction, but quickly stopped his search for enemies.

"Still, I heard Precious went to the slums, but we don't actually know where she is, right? So how are we supposed to find her?"

Vihera's gaze shifted toward Marina, the source of the information.

But the source herself, dressed as always in her party gown, merely shrugged her shoulders slightly.

"Looks like they didn't have that detailed of information either. From here on out, we'll just have to find her ourselves."

"...The slums?"

Though referred to collectively as the slums, the area was quite vast.

Because every year, countless adventurers flock to Gilm.

Just like right now, in this season.

Well, spring sees the most, but even in seasons other than winter, the number of adventurers coming to Gilm is by no means small.

Naturally, adventurers who come to Gilm are confident in their abilities. Yet, many of these adventurers experience their first real setback only after beginning their activities here.

A typical example would be someone who, in their previous hunting grounds, had faced little more than goblins.

Some die outright when encountering powerful monsters mid-quest... yet many survive.

They come to realize they cannot make it in Gilm, their overinflated pride crushed along with their spirit... and the destination for such individuals is the slums.

If they couldn't make it in Gilm, they could have chosen to leave and head for a safer place. But those whose pride had been shattered had no such luxury, and they ended up in the slums.

Of course, the slums are harsh in their own way, claiming many lives... yet, being adventurers who still possess—or rather, once possessed—a certain degree of confidence, those who adapt and survive in the slums are by no means few.

And since there are former adventurers... and others who make the slums their home for various reasons, as long as more people flow into the slums than die there, the slums will naturally expand.

The existence of these slums was also a thorn in the side of the Daskar who ruled Gilm.

Being a slum meant significantly less tax revenue compared to other areas. Moreover, its very nature made it a haven for underworld figures and criminals, a breeding ground for all manner of crime.

While investigations were conducted using the Grassland Wolves, a group of former thieves, it was precisely because the Grassland Wolves held significant power within Daskar's intelligence network that they had countless other tasks demanding their attention.

As a result, the slum district was effectively left to fend for itself.

...While the slums were indeed home to many failed adventurers, one major reason for this neglect was that some residents possessed skills capable of holding their own against even high-ranking adventurers.

In any case, expecting them to search such a complex and vast slum district on their own was clearly unreasonable.

Marina seemed to understand this too. She looked around hesitantly.

But... there was no way anyone would willingly get involved with a group like Rei and his companions, who clearly had something to hide.

Anyone Marina looked at immediately averted their gaze.

The reason they didn't immediately leave the area was likely because they feared doing so would cause Rei and the others discomfort, and they might retaliate... or perhaps they were simply frozen in place, unable to move in the presence of the griffin, Set.

Or perhaps they were simply frozen in place, unable to move with the griffin, Set, standing before them.

Please don't come over here.

Among those silently praying this... Marina's gaze settled on a boy in his early teens.

Of course, in this Elgin, being in your early teens meant you were considered just another pair of working hands.

And this was the slums. Naturally, they started working at a much younger age, and the boy Marina had her eye on was no exception.

"Hey, kid. Got a sec?"

"Wh-what the hell?! You want something from me!?"

If others saw him scared, they'd look down on him.

The boy shouted defiantly, driven by that fear, but it was obvious he was forcing himself.

But Marina smiled at him, as if to calm him down.

...And the boy found himself captivated by Marina's smile.

It might be a bit early to call it adolescence, but he was still at that age where he'd just started taking an interest in the opposite sex.

For someone like that, Marina's smile... not the usual, unconscious, alluring smile she wore, but a gentle one... was devastatingly powerful.

"I've got something I want to ask you. Oh, and of course, I won't ask for it for free. ...Rei, could I have some bread?"

"Bread? Wouldn't copper coins be better?"

"No way. Anyway, I'll explain the reason later."

"...Well, if you insist."

The Misty Ring contained plenty of both food and copper coins.

That's why a little expense wasn't really a problem.

(After all, they'd been dragged into the Spier Trading Company's internal strife, and they were sure to earn more than they spent.)

For Rei, who intended to squeeze every last penny out of Precious under the guise of an apology if she was indeed responsible for this incident, this level of expense was utterly insignificant.

"Answer my questions, and I'll give you this bread. ...How about it?"

"What do you want to know?"

The boy asked, his gaze fixed on the bread Marina held—not a sandwich or anything fancy, just plain bread—though it was freshly baked.

Rei showed no particular reaction to Rei's Misty Ring.

Was it because he didn't understand the value of the magic item Misty Ring itself, or because he knew what would happen if he foolishly tried to take it from Rei?

"Did someone named Precious come to the slums today... no, a little while ago?"

"Precious? You tell me a name, but I don't know who that is."

The boy's words were only natural.

Rei and the others knew both the name and face of this person named Precious, but there was no way someone in the slums would know it.

Unless they were close to Precious, that is.

"Right, sorry. He's a bit chubby, and he has this... long, thin beard."

"Chubby with a beard? ...Ah, yeah. I passed him by a little while ago. Mr. Slein took him away with a few others."

"Slein? Where can I find him?"

"...You're going? I think you should reconsider. Mr. Slein was a skilled adventurer originally, and I hear he's quite strong."

Marina smiled at the worried boy and handed him the bread she was holding.

"It'll be fine. We're strong too, you know, despite how we look."

Even so, the boy couldn't quite believe it.

After all, he had no way to gauge the strength of others, and to him, Rei and the others just looked dangerous.

"That bread is payment for the info. Eat it quickly."

At Marina's words, the boy quickly ate the bread she'd given him.

A hint of sadness flickered in Marina's eyes as she watched him.

She wished she could do something about children like this living in the slums... But even so, there was nothing Marina could do right now.

All she could do was hand him bread like this.

Leaving the boy behind, Marina urged Rei and the others to continue through the slums.

"Hey, why bread instead of copper coins?"

"...Because coins are likely to be stolen by someone else. Bread, on the other hand, can't be stolen once it's eaten."

"I see."

The slums had more adults than children.

If such people found copper coins, many would likely snatch them with glee.

To prevent that, she gave them bread they could eat right away. Hearing Marina's explanation, Rei could only agree.

As the atmosphere grew slightly somber, Vihera spoke up.

"More importantly, this Slain guy is a former adventurer and a skilled fighter, right? What kind of opponent is he?"

"Just so you know, we're here to inflict psychological damage on Precious, not to actually fight him."

Vihera's words dispelled the gloom, but while grateful, Rei gently admonished her.

At his words, Vihera flashed a slightly displeased expression.

Still, he quickly remembered their current situation and fell silent.

"Well then, let's go. We just follow this path, right? ...I don't know how Precious will react, but if she slips up, I'd be delighted."

"Heh heh, right. But I'll be the one dealing with that Slain guy, okay?"

"Right. If it comes to a fight, count on me. ...Well, I doubt it'll actually come to a fight, though."

If he's connected to Precious, it wouldn't be strange for him to know my name.

If that's the case, would he attack knowing that? ...That's a bit of a gray area, Rei concluded.

Vihera must have sensed Rei's mood. She wore a slightly bored expression... yet still clung to a sliver of hope that a fight might break out as they made their way through the slums.

And, as expected, someone was observing Rei and the others.

"Hey, isn't that the Crimson Wings formed recently? The Rank B party. What business do they have in the slums?"

A short distance from where Rei and the others walked.

From a room in a two-story building—a bit unusual for the slums—one of the people watching them outside muttered.

"Huh? That kid's Rank B? No way! I'm stronger than that!"

The one muttering this was a newcomer who had just joined the men's organization after arriving in the slums.

He hadn't even been in the slums long, let alone in Gilm, but his confident words drew a sigh of exasperation from his partner—or rather, his mentor.

"Are you stupid? No, you were stupid. Otherwise, you wouldn't have ended up in the slums this fast."

"Hey, senpai, that's a bit harsh, don't you think?"

"...Just listen. Look closely. That's a griffin Rei's got with him, right? Do you really think some random kid could make a monster like that his familiar...? Even you should know better than that."

"Luck?"

"Sigh. ...Well, I won't deny that."

Honestly, without luck, you wouldn't even encounter a griffin in the first place.

Though, normally, encountering a griffin would mean certain death, so the man couldn't fathom how much luck it would take to defy that and actually tame one.

"You're a former adventurer too. You've heard the nickname 'Crimson,' right?"

"...Huh? That kid is Crimson!?"

"You, man. If you're coming to Gilm, you should at least do your homework."

The man sighed and sent the newcomer running to report this matter to the higher-ups.
