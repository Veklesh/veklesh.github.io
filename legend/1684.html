"Wow... you sure brought a lot of these."

Marina said this after seeing the several nets Rei was holding.

In fact, the nets Rei was holding were packed to the brim with abalone, turban shells, oysters—or, to be precise, shellfish that closely resembled each of those—as well as oddly shaped shellfish that even Rei had never seen before.

In terms of weight, it was likely dozens of kilograms… no, it wouldn’t be surprising if it exceeded a hundred kilograms.

Rei carrying all that without batting an eye is one thing, but Set, who flew over with Rei on his back, is another story entirely.

“Even so,” Ray said. “There are various reasons for this.”

“Reasons? Is that why there are no fish, and you only brought shellfish in the nets?”

“Yeah. We can’t store them in the Misty Ring if they’re alive. We managed to store the fish because we killed them all, but I didn’t know how to kill shellfish. So, I figured I’d rely on Marina’s spirit magic.”

“……Huh. I see. I’ve never tried it myself, but… I think it’ll probably work just fine. For now, could you put those shellfish down on the ground? If Rei keeps holding them, who knows what might happen.”

Prompted by Marina, and under the curious stares of the others, Rei set the net down on the ground.

The sound of the shells clattering against each other as he did so made it easy to imagine just how heavy the contents of that net were.

“I knew you could do it, Marina, but I’m really glad you actually did.”

Seeing Rei’s relieved expression, Marina opened her mouth with an exasperated look.

“Listen, if I hadn’t been able to do anything about it, what were you planning to do?”

“In that case, I would’ve cooked it so it was ready to eat right away, or cut it into fillets so it could be used in a dish immediately, and then stored it in the Misty Ring.”

That was a method only possible because the item box was a storage space where the passage of time didn’t apply.

Hearing Rei’s words, Bistol wore a slightly envious expression.

Bistol has a Magic Pouch, a simplified mass-produced version of an item box, but its storage capacity is limited, and time still passes normally inside it.

That’s exactly why Bistol dries fish overnight almost every night.

……Of course, fish dried overnight like that naturally have a strong fishy smell, and insects are drawn to that scent.

He tries to keep them out with a net or something, but depending on the size of the bugs, they can still squeeze through the mesh.

Also, as long as Set is around, other monsters and animals don’t come near, but that’s only true as long as Set is there.

During the day, when Set is out with Rei, most animals stay away because Set’s scent and presence linger, but that’s not a guarantee.

Animals that are poor at sensing a presence… or those so hungry they’re willing to take a bit of a risk… sometimes wandered in, especially when Set wasn’t around.

As a result, a few of the dried fish were snatched away by animals and birds... For Bistol, this was annoying, but it wasn’t really a significant loss.

To begin with, Bistol hadn’t spent a single penny on travel expenses to get here, and while Rei and the others provided the ingredients, Bistol was the one doing the cooking. …The sight of a burly, muscular man diligently cooking was, at first glance, nothing short of jarring.

Even so, the taste of Bistol’s cooking surpasses even that of Marina, who is the best cook among Rei and the others.

I have no intention of letting that sense of incongruity affect how delicious the food is.

And as for the all-important dried fish, he borrows his fishing gear from Rei and sources his bait locally.

All Bistol really spends is the time fishing and the effort to make the dried fish.

“I’m jealous that Rei-chan can manage to pull it off even under those circumstances,”

Bistol, who had been watching the exchange between Rei and Marina, muttered this.

“Is that so?”

“Yes. Honestly, you have no idea how lucky you are.”

I’m a little taken aback by Rei’s words.

For Rei, the Misty Ring was something she’d been using ever since she came to this world.

Using it had simply become second nature to him.

“So, for now, it’s okay to squeeze all the shells, right?”

Marina interrupts the conversation between Rei and Bistol, and Rei nods in agreement.

The moment Rei nodded, Marina used her spirit magic to create a mass of water.

In this place, with the sea nearby and hot springs even closer, using water spirit magic must be incredibly easy.

The entire net, filled with a large number of clams, is enveloped in water and begins to float upward.

“It’ll take a little while, so you can go do something else, Rei.”

“……Is that okay? Alright, then, I’ll leave it to you. Though, watching this does look kind of interesting.”

In fact, the water covering the nets filled with shells had become quite dirty in this short time.

It’s incredible to see how quickly she’s removing the dirt from the shells and the impurities inside the clams—fast enough that you can actually see it happening.

(Clams… or is it river clams? Were cockles included too? I heard you usually have to soak them in water overnight to get rid of the sand, but with Marina here, it’s done in no time.)

Rei watched the clams submerged in the water, thinking to himself... then gave Marina a quick wave and walked away.

Marina watched Rei walk away, then set to work thoroughly cleaning the dirt from the water-covered clams.

“My, my, you’re quite diligent.”

Bistol smiled wryly as he watched Marina.

Marina, acting on Rei’s request, looked somehow innocent… yes, she reminded him of a newlywed.

Seeing Bistol like that, Marina looked happy too.

Normally, she exuded an almost overwhelming feminine allure, but right now, Marina radiated not only that allure but also a sense of happiness.

“That’s true. I’m happy to be an adventurer with Rei, but enjoying a leisurely vacation like this isn’t bad either. …After all, I’ve been working nonstop ever since the incident in the Forest of Treant.”

Marina was absolutely right.

After resolving the incident in the Forest of Treant, there was the expansion of the Gilm Hall; then dealing with the members of the Noble Faction who wanted to obstruct it; then dealing with the criminal organization that tried to exploit the crowds at the Gilm Hall; and even traveling to other countries to ensure they never tried anything like that again… There really had been a lot going on since then.

Thinking about it that way, it wasn’t strange to take it easy here for a while, and surely no one should blame her for it.

That said, if I linger here too long, there’s no doubt things will get chaotic back at Gilm.

(I guess I have about ten days left... to relax here.)

Thinking that, Marina let a smile spread across her face.

“Phew… Yeah, there’s nothing like chilled fruit water in the summer.”

“Growl.”

In response to Rei’s words, Set growled in agreement.

Rei, who had pulled down the hood of the Dragon Robe, and Set, whose fur was wet, had just come out of the hot springs.

Since there was a hot spring here, and since they had made this their base, it was only natural to enjoy it to the fullest.

They hadn’t been swimming in the ocean, but even so, after venturing out to sea and spending several hours exposed to the ocean breeze, it was only natural that their hair and skin felt sticky.

And as a post-bath refreshment, it wouldn’t be strange at all for the cold fruit-infused water to taste absolutely delicious.

“Gurr? Gurrr…”

“Give me another one,” Set growled at Rei.

In response to Set’s plea, Rei sighed in resignation and poured some fruit water into the bowl in front of him.

(It might be a good idea to preserve fruits harvested in spring, summer, and fall, freeze them in the snow during winter, and then store them in the Misty Ring. …No, actually, if I asked Marina, she’d probably handle it easily with her spirit magic.)

If I used water spirit magic, I could probably handle ice just fine.

It wasn’t as if he’d noticed Rei’s thoughts, but Set paused mid-sip of the fruit water and turned his gaze toward Rei.

“It’s nothing. Just a thought that popped into my head.”

“Grrrr?”

“Oh?” Set growled, and, seemingly satisfied with Rei’s explanation, began drinking the fruit juice again.

As he stroked Set’s body, Rei gently closed his eyes.

As his vision darkened, his hearing grew sharper… and the sounds around him became crystal clear.

The voices of Elena and the other women, and the sounds of their work. But what Rei wanted to hear wasn’t that… it was the sound of the night insects.

Since summer was drawing to a close, it seemed the chirping had become much quieter than it had been a short while ago, but as the season progressed a little further, the autumn insects would surely begin to make their presence known with a lively chorus.

(It was noisy in the summer and fall back in Japan, too.)

Rei lived right next to the mountains… a place that, depending on the context, might even be described as being deep in the mountains.

Naturally, there were plenty of insects, and on summer and autumn nights, something that could only be called an “insect concert” took place almost every night.

...No, actually, these "insect concerts" weren’t limited to just the night.

During the day, the sound of cicadas chirping echoed so loudly it was almost annoying.

I wonder why I suddenly found myself reminiscing about my time in Japan.

Rei didn’t know either, but even so, the sound of the insects was a delight to his ears.

As she enjoyed the sounds of the insects, she naturally closed her eyes, and drowsiness began to overtake her.

The fact that Set was nearby—ready to react immediately if anything happened—probably played a part in it.

Eventually, the hand that had been stroking Set stopped, and she leaned her weight against him, giving in to the drowsiness and drifting off to sleep.

“Oh, come on. Look at that. Rei, really.”

Though her words might seem like a slight complaint, her eyes held a gentle glow.

Seeing Bistrol like this, those who heard her voice followed her gaze and understood what she meant.

With the hood of the Dragon Robe removed, it was a sight that, given her usual personality, was utterly uncharacteristic… yet there she was, sleeping with an expression that could only be described as innocent.

Given Rei’s usual personality, it was an incredibly innocent expression.

Perhaps because she naturally has feminine features, that innocence seemed even more pronounced to those who saw her.

Especially when it comes to maternal instincts, Bistrel possesses some of the strongest among everyone present.

Seeing Rei like this, she couldn’t help but think, “Shouldn’t I put something over her so she doesn’t catch a cold?”

Besides, the Dragon Robe functions like a portable air conditioner, and since he’s leaning against Set, there’s no need to worry about him catching a cold.
