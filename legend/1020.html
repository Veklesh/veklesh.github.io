About ten days had passed since the Zubozu incident was resolved, and Rei had been traveling with four others: Elena, Vihera, Byune, and Ara.

To be precise, Set and Yellow were also part of the group, so it should be described as four people and two creatures.

Elena and Ara, due to their status, weren't registered as adventurers, but since they were traveling with Rei and the others, they were effectively part of the party.

They weren't exactly taking on quests to slay monsters or anything; they'd mostly just been checking the forest where the cyclops incident had happened before to see if anything was amiss.

Other than that, they'd finished dismantling the cyclops they'd left unfinished during the kobold attack.

In truth, outside of quests, Rei was being dragged around by the four... or more accurately, by Elena and Vihera.

Accompanying Elena on her tour of Gilm, or engaging in mock battles with Vihera, that sort of thing.

Spending their days like this, Rei and the others were now heading toward a certain section of Gilm.

"...Why do we need to come too? Rei was the only one who asked Azimov for work, right?"

Vihera seemed a bit annoyed, likely because she had planned to spar with Elena today.

Vihera and Elena had been about to leave Gilm to hold the mock battle when a letter from Azimov arrived.

"Well, I get why Vihera's annoyed. Honestly, if he was going to send a letter, he could've just come in person."

Considering Azimov's personality, he'd probably come see them in person rather than bother with delivering a letter.

Everyone else seemed to agree with Rei's words. Even Vihera, who had been complaining, had her curiosity piqued and her mood lifted as they made their way through the streets of Gilm.

A procession of beauties and young maidens, with the griffin Set in their midst, and Yellow riding upon Set's back. The only one who didn't stand out was Rei, hooded in his Dragon Robe. While they attracted an impossible amount of attention on the main street, the quieter back alleys offered far fewer prying eyes.

Even though most in the group were accustomed to being noticed, entering such a secluded place still enveloped them in a sense of relief.

Being accustomed to being seen didn't mean they weren't tired of it.

As they walked down the increasingly deserted street, Rei's house and research facility, which he had visited several times before, soon came into view.

"Hmm, that building... It seems slightly larger than the others around it, but outwardly, it doesn't really look any different from the other buildings."

That was Elena's first impression upon seeing Asimov's house.

Indeed, judging solely by its exterior, it looked like an ordinary building. ...Its exterior, that is.

"I suppose we'll find out once we're inside."

What kind of reaction would Elena have upon seeing that mess?

With that slightly mischievous thought in mind, Rei knocked on the door.

(He's probably engrossed in another experiment and won't come out...)

...she probably won't come out.

Contrary to Rei's expectations, the door opened the very next moment.

"Hey, you're late."

"...Pamidor?"

Emerging from the door Rei had knocked on was Pamidor, wearing a vicious expression that could only be described as that of a notorious bandit leader.

Rei glanced behind him. Elena was reaching for the hilt of Mirage, and Ara was reaching for the power axe on her back.

"Calm down, you two. He looks dangerous, and he is dangerous... Huh?"

Rei had intended to explain that there was no danger, but any detailed description of Pamidor would inevitably make him sound like a dangerous individual.

And after hearing Rei's explanation, Elena and the others naturally concluded the person before them was the intruder who broke into Azimov's house...

"No, wait! This guy is Azimov's friend!"

Rei's voice halted Elena and the others in their tracks.

They turned their gaze toward Rei to verify his words, but Rei redirected their attention to Set standing behind Elena and the others, opening his mouth.

"Look, even Set isn't particularly wary of Pamidor, right?"

It was true. Having met Pamidor many times before, Set showed no particular concern, merely clearing his throat as if to say "long time no see." Yellow was napping on top of Set, basking in the spring sunshine.

Seeing this, Elena and the others finally relaxed their guard.

The one watching Rei's actions with an exasperated look was Vihera. Yet, she wore a smile that seemed to say Elena and Ara's attitudes weren't surprising either.

As for Byune, he watched the whole commotion unfold without changing his expression in the slightest.

"Sorry about that, Pamidor."

Leading the way, Rei and the others offered their apologies to Pamidor.

To Elena and the others, Pamidor shook his head, indicating he wasn't particularly bothered, and spoke.

"Ah, it's nothing unusual. Don't worry about it. More importantly, you've brought quite the beautiful ladies with you."

A hint of envy in Rei's tone struck Rei as notable.

...Perhaps because he said it with such a sinister expression, to an outsider it looked like he was threatening Rei to leave the woman behind.

"Is it okay for you to say that? You're married, aren't you? ...Unfortunately, I've never met anyone crazy enough to marry Pamidor."

"Cut it out. She's not the type to get jealous over something like that."

After that somewhat easygoing exchange, Pamidor spoke up.

"More importantly, you were summoned by Azimov too, right?"

"Yeah, I was. ...So you were too? I haven't heard anything about progress on the spear, though."

Rei, Pamidor, and Azimov. Seeing all three together made Rei wonder for a moment if there had been progress on the spear he'd asked about. But Pamidor shook her head.

"No, not the spear. ...It's just... it's not entirely unrelated to your spear business either... that's the gist of it."

It seemed Pamiador already understood why Azimov had summoned Rei and the others.

"That's vague again."

"I didn't get any detailed explanation either. I just talked with Azimov and figured that's probably what it was. Anyway, come on up. ...Not that I should be the one saying it."

"It's fine coming from you, Pamidor. You and Azimov are close. ...Set."

"Grr."

Set, called by Rei, seemed to understand what Rei wanted just from that. He gave a short growl, then lay down beside the entrance to Azimov's house, still carrying Yellow on his back.

"Kyuu? Kyu-kyu, KYUUU!"

Noticing Set had laid down, Yellow, who had been sleeping on his back, hopped down and called out to Set, "Let's play!"

"Guru... Guru, gururururu..."

Set responded to Yellow by using his tail to play with him.

Watching this soothing scene, Rei wanted to linger and watch for a while longer. But remembering he'd been called by Azimov, he entered the house.

The others felt the same way. Most of them would have preferred to stay where they were, but since Azimov had summoned them via letter, they assumed he must have something important to discuss and headed inside as well.

...Though their hearts were tugged by the sight of the two adorable creatures.

Entering the house, they were led by Pamidor to meet Ajimov, who greeted them with a beaming expression.

This was unusual for Azimov, who often seemed in a bad mood, and it was enough to make them think something significant must have happened.

"Thank you for coming! Actually, I called you today because I have something I wanted to report."

Anyone seeing Azimov now could understand he was in high spirits.

He was that different from his usual sullen self.

"When you called Pamidor and me, I assumed it was about the spear... but it's not?"

"Ah. Well, to be precise, it does relate to the spear matter. ...You remember that hammer Rei brought, the one supposedly used by the Cyclops?"

"Yeah, that one."

The experimental magic item created by Zubozo flashed through Rei's mind: a combat auto-reaction nerve that sent lightning through the struck area, granted the user powerful regenerative abilities, and ultimately took over the body by embedding roots within it.

After defeating the Cyclops and obtaining that hammer, he'd offered it to be used for the spear matter he'd asked Azimov about...

"So, did you learn anything from that hammer?"

"That's right. If we play our cards right, this could be a discovery of the century. ...But it all hinges on that hammer, so first we need to figure out how to create the material for it."

"The material, huh."

Despite his many flaws and eventual breakdown, Zuboz was still an exceptionally capable alchemist.

Judging by Azimov's current state, Rei couldn't help but think so.

"So, what happened to that hammer?"

"...Simply put, it might make mass-producing magic items possible."

Silence. The room fell silent.

It was because what Azimov had just said was so unexpected.

The first to speak in that silence was Pamidor, who had known Azimov the longest.

"Azimov, just... just to be clear, when you say mass production, you don't mean everyday magic items like lamps or fire starters, right?"

"Of course not!"

Azimov responded instantly to Pamidor's words.

Magic items for light or fire are simple to make and are already mass-produced.

There was no need to mass-produce those items again.

"Mass production in this case, to put it plainly, refers to items like Magic Swords. ...Of course, the capabilities of mass-produced Magic Swords will never match those crafted by artisans. But they still possess enough performance to be called Magic Swords. Hmm, you could say they're Magic Swords positioned between ordinary longswords and true Magic Swords."

"If it's mass production, then I suppose it can't be helped. ...So, specifically, how do you make these mass-produced Magic Swords?"

Intrigued by the term "mass-produced Magic Sword," Pamidor's eyes sparkled with curiosity as she asked Azimov.

As if he'd been waiting for this, Azimov produced an ordinary, unremarkable longsword.

"Listen up. First, wash the blade of this longsword with water created by magic. This is to wash away blood, rust, dirt, and such. It's absolutely necessary, even for a freshly forged blade."

After stating this, Azimov submerged the blade into the water created by magic and wiped it with a cloth also imbued with alchemical magic.

Next, he prepared something that looked like nothing more than ordinary clay at first glance.

"This clay has been infused with magic to enhance its affinity with the hammer brought by Rei. I'll infuse it further with my own magic while sliding it along the blade..."

True to his words, he slid the clay along the blade.

Perhaps due to the magic within the clay, a faint glow appeared on the blade where it was rubbed.

After sliding the clay over the entire blade, it was finally enveloped in a soft glow.

"While this light is present... this is it."

Next, he prepared a container precisely the same length as the longsword's blade.

It was made of metal, and inside the container was some kind of powder.

(This kinda feels like making cutlets. Like flour, eggs, and breadcrumbs or something.)

As Rei, who was feeling a bit peckish, watched, Azimov placed the container holding the sword into the magic circle and chanted an incantation... Finally, a blinding light flared for just an instant, and the longsword's blade changed to a slightly different color than before.

"It's complete. ...Though, as I mentioned before, the Magic Sword isn't particularly powerful. It can still attack ghost-like, incorporeal undead monsters, but it only deals damage at best."

Even so, Azimov said it would definitely sell well, as it was cheaper than a typical Magic Sword.

"It'll sell, sure... but how does that ultimately affect the hammer problem?"

"This."

Saying that, Azimov showed him a pile of metal powder.

Seeing this, even Rei understood what this metal powder was made of.

"You turned that hammer into metal powder?"

"That's right. Oh, and of course, I've set aside enough for your spear. This is made from the rest of it."

"That's fine, but doesn't that mean we still can't mass-produce it without that hammer?"

"Exactly. That's why I called Rei. If you get your hands on the material for that hammer, could you pass it on to us? With a bit more research, we might be able to achieve the same results with other materials."

At Azimov's words, Rei simply replied, "If I get it..." He then asked about the spear's progress before leaving Azimov's house.

"Mass-producing magic items should be something Rei would be happy about, right?"

On the way back, emerging from a back alley onto the main street, Elena—who knew about Rei's hobby of collecting magic items—made the remark. Rei's expression turned difficult.

"Even if they're magic items, they're just cheap knockoffs, right? If that's the case..."

"Rei!"

Before Rei could finish, that voice echoed around them.
