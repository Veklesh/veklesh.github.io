Gamellion.

A Rank C monster, its strength makes the goblins Rei has fought in this dungeon so far seem like nothing.

Its fangs could easily crush a human along with their armor, and its ears, sharpened like blades, were sharper than the strike of a clumsy adventurer. It whipped its long tail like a whip and sometimes thrust the tip like a spear.

Its body, standing two to three meters tall, is packed with muscle. Its attacks, utilizing the rabbit's characteristic leaping ability, combined with its sheer mass, are impossible for an average warrior to block with a shield; evasion is likely the best they can hope for.

Its ferocious temperament, unimaginable for a rabbit-based monster, is fitting for a Rank C monster.

A monster that a Rank C party can barely match when facing just one. That is the Gamerion.

...And now, nearly fifty such Gamelions stood before Rei, all directing their hostility toward him.

The first ten or so that attacked him lay slashed to pieces on the ground.

Perhaps the Gamerians hadn't expected their comrades to be killed so easily either. Instead of charging recklessly now, they were watching for Rei's opening.

But facing these Gamerians, Rei flaunted the crimson magic power enveloping his body.

Even in his normal state, he would have been more than capable of handling the Gamerians.

But facing this many Gamerians, Rei judged they were worthy opponents and fought with his own magic enveloping his body.

The Crimson Armor of the Flame Emperor. It was Rei's strongest skill, possessing the power to fight on equal terms with the unshakable Noise, a Rank S adventurer.

"What's the matter? If you're not going to attack, I'll come to you."

Holding the Death Scythe in his right hand, he swung his left arm wide.

What shot forth from it was the magic power that had been compressing around Rei's body, now visible.

That magic tore away from Rei's left hand like it was being ripped off and flew toward Gamerion.

Gamellion, sensing the danger of the attack, immediately dodged the magic... the Deep Flame... with his signature leap.

However, several others were unable to evade in time. The Deep Flames that struck the nearby ground engulfed their bodies in the flames they created, burning them alive.

Though Gamellion's fur color varied by individual, brown and white fur burned. The pain caused them to thrash about, spreading the flames and burns to other nearby companions.

"GAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAARGH!"

It wasn't necessarily revenge for its fallen comrade, but the Gamellion closest to Rei let out a fierce cry. With its sharp ears, it kicked off the ground, aiming to tear Rei apart as it charged.

It had witnessed its first fallen comrade attempt to tear Rei's slender body apart with its fangs, only to be dodged and killed by a counter-like swing of the Deathscythe.

To attack with fangs, it needed to pause its movement in front of Rei.

Having been struck by an attack exploiting that opening, he likely decided to use his ears to sense when to pass by and slash through Rei.

That reasoning was certainly sound.

It might be natural for a Rank C monster, but not repeating the same mistake could be seen as Gamellion, a monster specialized in combat, showing its true colors.

But...

"Do you really think a speed like that could do anything to me!?"

Catching Gamellion's ear as it lunged toward him, intent on cleaving his abdomen in two, Rei shouted sharply.

If any other adventurer had witnessed this scene, they wouldn't have believed their eyes.

Because Rei hadn't blocked Gamellion's attack with his Death Scythe... He'd blocked the ear with just his bare right hand.

The Gamellion attacking Rei now wasn't the largest among those in this hall. Even so, it stood over two meters tall, and its weight was beyond imagination.

Rei, who had caught Gamellion's attack with just his right hand, then grabbed the base of its right ear—the one without the blade—and lifted it up... before slamming it into the ground.

"Gyaaah!"

Its head slammed into the ground. The sound of its skull shattering, combined with the scream of the Gamellion, echoed around them, the result of its own weight and Rei's sheer strength.

From the sidelines, it looked like soft feathers drifting through the air, but mixed in were scattered bits of brain matter, bone, blood, and flesh.

The other Gamerians surrounding Rei must have realized the scene was clearly abnormal. Though each growled, none made any move to attack him.

"What's the matter? Come on. I won't run or hide. I'll fight you head-on right here."

He muttered provocatively, stowing the Death Scythe in his left hand back into the Mistling, clearly showing the Gamerians he was now empty-handed.

But with each gesture, the red magical energy swirling around Rei's body visibly rippled, making it clear even to Gamerion and the others that this was dangerous.

But... even so, all Gamellion and the others could do was surround him like this, other than attacking.

Being summoned here by the dungeon's core was fine, but his body was too large to leave this room.

Rei's ogre opponent had been similar in height, but the width was different.

At that point, it had become impossible for him to even leave this hall.

Under normal circumstances, even a Gamellion wouldn't want to fight the current Rei.

Though Gamerians are ferocious by nature, some would surely prefer to flee if possible.

But... with only one exit, and being unable to pass through it, escape was impossible.

The result was the current state of Rei being surrounded.

"Looks like you're not planning on coming over there... Well then, I'll come over here instead."

He muttered softly and took a step forward.

They probably didn't understand Rei's words, but they likely judged it dangerous to have Rei, cloaked in red magic, approaching them. The Gamerians surrounding Rei began to retreat...

"Hmph!"

But in the next instant, Gamellion was startled to find Rei suddenly standing right before him.

"Haaah!"

A fist swung with a battle cry shattered the skull of the Gamellion closest to Rei.

Gameriions' heads scattered around them like water balloons bursting upon hitting the ground.

Among them, several were wounded by Gamellion's ears, now transformed into blades, and his sharp fangs.

"...I misjudged the force."

Gamerions, as monsters, weren't just prized for their meat; naturally, other parts fetched high prices too.

Even the fur from the ones wounded by that strike was quite valuable.

Having damaged it, Rei could only call it a failure.

"Gahh, GAAAAAHHHH!"

The Gamellion, struck in the eye by a fragment of its own fang, roared in pain and rage.

Following suit, the Gamellion whose limbs had been sliced by the ears also let out a growl.

Then, one after another, the Gamerions began to cry out, following their comrades.

They were inherently highly aggressive monsters. Even if they had initially been intimidated by Rei's strength, once their comrades were wounded and let out cries of rage, their fear was swept away, replaced by aggression.

Furthermore, their intelligence allowed them to understand they had absolutely no escape. That too was likely a cause of this outcome.

There was no way they could defeat Rei alone, and there was nowhere to run.

Moreover, Rei had no choice but to eliminate the Gamerians.

Given that, the only way for the Gamerians to survive was to defeat the terrifying, threatening foe before them.

Following their instinctive urge, the Gamerians charged at Rei all at once.

"Finally getting serious, huh?"

Realizing that such numbers would be troublesome without weapons, Rei drew the Deathscythe from Mistilteinn and prepared to meet the Gamerians charging toward him.

"GAAAAAAAAAAHHH!"

Gamerians attacked from three directions: left, right, and front.

Behind him was the passageway, with no gap wide enough for the Gamerians to circle around. ...But even before that, the Gamerians, now consumed by bloodlust, had no thought of flanking him from behind.

As for above, the ceiling blocked that route.

Attacking from diagonally in front was also impossible given the Gamellion's size.

As a result, at most three Gamerians could attack simultaneously.

He crouched to dodge the whip-like strike launched from the right. The Gamellion charging from the left bared its fangs, but the stone tip of the Death Scythe struck its jaw upward. The Gamellion charging head-on, fangs bared, had its attack halted when he grabbed its fangs with his free left hand and flung it toward the Gamellion attacking with its right tail.

Even for the Gamellion on the right, it was completely unexpected that someone with a body as small as Rei's could lift a Gamellion with one hand. Unable to dodge the thrown Gamellion, it was blown away along with the others...

"First, two down!"

As those words echoed, both Gamerians were cleaved in two, their consciousnesses sinking into darkness.

Though Gamerians normally possessed resistance to slashing attacks, even they couldn't block a single strike from Rei's Death Scythe.

"GAAAAAAAAAAH!"

The Gamerion charged toward Rei once more.

As the Gamellion charged straight at him, Rei leapt, activating the Sleipnir Boots. He kicked off mid-air, reaching the ceiling, and swung the Death Scythe there.

The blade of the Death Scythe sliced through the stalactites protruding from the ceiling without any trouble whatsoever, and they fell to the ground. ...Rei's figure suddenly vanished, reappearing above Gamerion's head, who had lost sight of him.

The raw, squelching sound of crushed flesh echoed around them, but no one heard it.

The battlefield had already shifted from the edge of the hall to its center.

Using Sleipnir's boots to kick off the air and move, Rei dodged the tail strikes launched from the ground towards him while severing those tails with the Death Scythe.

Arriving at the spot where Gamellion's forces were densely packed, he swung the Deathscythe as he fell toward the ground.

"Gyaaah!"

Seeing Rei descending, they must have judged it dangerous. The Gamerion directly beneath Rei tried to evade, but with other Gamerions nearby, it could barely move and only managed to shift its body slightly.

As a result, the Gamellion targeted by Rei had its right front leg cleanly severed by the blade of the Death Scythe.

Of course, Rei's attack didn't end there.

Activating the Flame Emperor's Crimson Armor and wielding the Death Scythe infused with magic power, with nothing but Gamerians all around him, Rei's next move was predetermined.

"Haahhh!"

He casually severed the neck of the Gamellion whose right front leg had been cut off mere seconds before, using the momentum to spin the hilt of the Death Scythe in his hand.

As a result, the Gamerians surrounding Rei were struck indiscriminately, their legs severed, ears severed, tails severed, torsos severed, heads severed.

It was an attack method unimaginable from someone who had been worrying about fur damage just moments before. But with this many Gamerians, he must have judged that other materials, including the fur, could be dealt with somehow.

Seconds... Though Rei had swung the Death Scythe for only a few seconds, nearly ten Gamerians had already met their end.

And not just any deaths—they were torn apart, dismembered, utterly brutal deaths.

It was a frenzied dance of blades bringing death, yet its movements possessed a beauty that bordered on sword dance.

Gamellion blood rained down, but it instantly evaporated and vanished, absorbed by the Crimson Armor of the Flame Emperor that enveloped Rei.

Rei had originally earned the nickname "Crimson" during the Spring War, when he was drenched in the blood of Bestia Empire soldiers while being swept up in a fire whirlwind.

Though this was entirely different from that time, had anyone seen Rei now, clad in the crimson magic of the Flame Emperor's Red Armor, they would likely have given him the nickname Crimson once more.

No, rather, he was far more deserving of the nickname Crimson now.

Rei continued to slash at Gamerion, swinging Deathscythe with unrestrained ferocity.

At this moment, Rei had absolutely no intention of letting his guard down.

The entire hall was filled with Gamerians, and there were only enemies around him.

But... precisely because of that, the incident occurred.

A sensation unlike slicing through Gamellion's flesh or bone—as if cutting through something hard—transmitted through the Deathscythe to Rei's hand.

And then...

【Deathscythe has acquired the skill 'Terrain Manipulation Lv.2'】

The announcement flashed through Rei's mind.

"What!?"

Rei stopped Deathscythe's wild slashing and saw the dungeon's core split cleanly in two at an angle.

"...Why... is the dungeon core granting skills again?"

All Rei could do was mutter in a daze while swinging Death Size at the attacking Gamellion.
