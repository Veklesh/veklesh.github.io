It had been a few days since they started holding combat training three times a day—morning, noon, and evening.

A town came into view ahead of Rei and his companions.

Located between the Kingdom of Reburina and the Kingdom of Mireana, it is the last town in the Kingdom of Reburina.

For that reason alone, it was a fairly large town that derived significant profit from travelers passing through from the Kingdom of Mireana.

And... in front of that town, called Asura, stood a figure that Rei recognized.

“Set-chan! Set-chan, Set-chan, Set-chan!”

She was waving her arms so vigorously that Ray could see her from a distance, and the fact that her voice carried even though they were still quite far apart made it clear just how loud she was shouting.

In fact, even from where he sat in the carriage, Rei could see that some of the people waiting in line to enter the town were trying to keep their distance from Milein.

“So we’ve finally managed to meet up, then.”

Rei muttered quietly as he opened the door leading to the coachman’s box.

He had expected this, but he’d thought they might be able to meet up a little sooner… It seems that was a bit of a naive assumption.

But as long as they’ve managed to meet up, there’s no problem.

“...No, wait, there is.”

Being a Set enthusiast, Milein is still waving at Set even now.

But it goes without saying what the women in Rei’s group—especially those who rely on Set as their emotional pillar—must be thinking upon seeing this.

They thought Set was theirs alone, but in reality, he’s being doted on by a stranger.

In reality, Milein was the one who accepted Set by a landslide, but no one here realizes that.

Even as they were thinking this, Rei’s group pressed on and eventually arrived before Asura.

When traveling through the Kingdom of Reburina, it was not uncommon for the guards to panic at the sight of Rei’s large group.

However, Ronitas, who was leading the delegation, must have informed Asura in advance.

Even when they saw a large group like Rei’s, the guards were surprised, but they didn’t panic to that extent.

As the carriages came to a stop in front of the main gate, Ronitas immediately approached the carriage where Rei was sitting.

“Ronitas?”

“Ah, it’s been a while… well, not that long, but a few days. I’m glad to see you’re doing well. I’ve already cleared things up with them regarding your group, so there shouldn’t be any issues. No need for formalities—just have your key people go right inside.”

“Is that okay?”

“Yeah. However, I’ll have the rest of your group wait outside for a while to avoid causing a commotion. …Is that okay with you?”

It wasn’t a question, but a tone seeking confirmation.

Hearing that, Rei, who had no particular objections, nodded in response.

“Got it, that’s fine. But a lot of the guys are looking forward to seeing the town, so I’d appreciate it if you could let them in as soon as possible.”

“I’ll tell them to do it as soon as possible. …Let’s go.”

At Ronitas’s words, Rei, Elena, Marina, Vihera, Byune, Ryutas, Gimekara, and Sura all entered the Earthra.

Meanwhile, Set was being showered with affection by Milein, whom he hadn’t seen in several days, so he wasn’t in a state to be let into the town.

(Since we can go in, Set probably could have gone in without any problems too… but I guess there’s nothing we can do about that. If we took Set into town in this situation, it would undoubtedly cause an unnecessary commotion.)

Glancing at the few women shooting hostile glares at Milein as she doted on Set, Rei simply walked into Earthra.

He understood that if he said anything in this situation, it would only cause more trouble.

Given that, he figured it would be best to just go with the flow.

……Though it probably also had something to do with the fact that Sulunin emerged from the Earthra just as Rei and the others were passing by.

Don’t trouble yourself. No, it’s fine.

They hadn’t actually exchanged words, but there was no doubt that their exchange of glances had allowed them to communicate clearly.

(As long as Sulunin is here, it shouldn’t be a problem. I don’t think Milein would resort to such violent measures against an amateur either. …Yeah. At least for now, she’s definitely still classified as an amateur.)

Combat training is held three times a day.

For the first few days, it was only those assigned to guard duty who participated in the combat training... but now, quite a few of the women are taking part as well.

I suppose the fact that they were forcibly brought here by the people of Jaya is having an effect.

If I were just a little stronger, I wouldn’t have resorted to selling my virginity for money.

Everyone understands that this group has a small number of guards.

For that very reason, there are certainly quite a few who are thinking about how to protect themselves in case of an emergency.

Of course, not everyone in the group feels that way, but they are still volunteers selected from a group of nearly a thousand people.

Naturally, that number is quite large… and among them, there are quite a few who possess exceptional talent for combat.

If they continued combat training all the way until they reached Gilm, wouldn’t they be able to gain a certain level of strength? That was Rei’s prediction.

In fact, he wouldn’t be surprised if some of them gained enough strength to easily handle low-rank adventurers.

After all, there are those whom Marina, Vihera, and others would vouch for—people with enough talent to make it as an adventurer the moment they arrive in Gilm.

“This way. It’s the inn where we’re staying, so there shouldn’t be any risk of eavesdropping.”

Saying that, Ronitas led the way to an inn not too far from the main gate.

It was clearly a high-class inn just from its appearance.

“You’re staying at a nice place.”

“Of course.”

Ronitas replied immediately to Rei’s remark.

“To be honest, I personally prefer a more laid-back kind of inn… but we’re a delegation from Gilm. For someone like me to stay at a cheap inn would be a disgrace to Gilm’s name.”

“Ah, I see.”

Ronitas’s words made the general situation clear to Rei.

If Ronitas were to stay at a cheap inn under these circumstances, it would imply that the Gilm delegation lacked sufficient financial resources, which would invite contempt.

Some people judge a person’s character based on the kind of inn they stay at.

Given that, there was no way Ronitas and his companions—who carried the name of Gilm—could possibly stay at such a cheap inn.

As they entered the inn, which bore the sign “Bounty of the Earth,” a staff member approached them immediately.

“Welcome back, Lord Ronitas.”

“Ah, these are my guests. Let me use the lounge for a bit. Also, I’d like some drinks and food.”

“Certainly.”

After a brief exchange, the employee walked away.

“Huh.”

There was something refined about his manner, proving that the Earth’s Bounty Inn was a high-class inn not just in appearance, but in substance as well.

(Personally, I prefer cozy, homey inns like the Dusk Wheat inn… but I guess there are plenty of people who like this kind of place.)

Impressed by this, Rei and the others were shown to a room on the second floor.

Just as Ronitas had mentioned, the room seemed designed for several people to converse.

There was no one else in sight, but it was likely set up that way for comfort. Thanks to a magic item that acted as an air conditioner, the room’s temperature was pleasantly cool, even for summer.

They each took a seat on the sofas, and Ronitas was the first to speak.

“Well then… shall we start with introductions? Though, of course, that excludes Rei and the others, since we already know each other.”

“I’d be happy to do that. …Well then, I’ll go first. I’m Gimekara from the Zolgue Trading Company, and I’m assisting Rei and the others.”

Remaining seated on the sofa, Gimekara bowed his head.

“Then I suppose I’m next. I’m Ryutas, formerly of Jaya. I have a few personal bodyguards, but I left them back there.”

Normally, it would be unthinkable for bodyguards to separate from the person they’re protecting.

But as long as Rei and the others were with them, it was clear to the bodyguards that they would be absolutely safe no matter what happened.

In fact, if Jaya’s pursuers were to make a move in this situation, there was no doubt that Rei would wipe them out.

Not only that, but it would also mean they’d laid a hand on the delegation from Gilm, which would certainly put Jaya at an even greater disadvantage.

“I’ll go last. I’m Sura, leader of the Resistance.”

Seeing Sura, Ronitas’s face flashed a look of surprise for just a moment.

He had heard about Sura from Rei and the others, but he must have thought she was still too young.

Ronitas’s surprise vanished quickly, but Sura wasn’t about to let that slip by unnoticed.

The reason she said nothing was that Sura herself believed it to be true—that she was young.

(Should I at least be glad that I don’t look older than I am?)

Thinking this, as if trying to convince herself, Suura smiled as if to say she hadn’t noticed Ronitas’s reaction at all.

As Sura smiled like that, the last person—Ronitas—spoke up.

“I am Ronitas. I imagine you’ve heard from Rei, but I am the one leading the delegation heading to the Kingdom of Reburina. …Well, now that the introductions are out of the way, there’s something I’d like to confirm first…”

Pausing mid-sentence, Ronitas turned his gaze toward Ryutas.

Realizing the meaning behind that gaze, Ryutas nodded at Ronitas before speaking.

“We’re going to head straight to Rossi with you, Ronitas.”

It was an action that could truly be called a round trip, but for Ronitas, Ryutas’s accompaniment was indispensable in terms of obtaining detailed information about Jaya and the Kingdom of Reburina.

He figured he could certainly gather various bits of information about the villages and towns they would stop at along the way, but the fact that Ryutas had actually been there meant his information held greater value than that of others.

“I see. That’s a relief. So… can the Zolgé Trading Company secure replacements for Ryutas and his guards?”

“In terms of sheer numbers, we can provide as many as you need… but when it comes to personality, or skill—or someone who possesses both—it’s a bit… no, to be honest, I’d say it’s quite difficult.”

“You’re a merchant, yet you’re giving up before you even try? I don’t think you’ll fare well in Gilm with that attitude.”

Ronitas spoke in a somewhat provocative tone, but he was likely still concerned that Gimekara was a merchant from the Zolge Trading Company who had been cooperating with Jaya.

In that sense, Ryutas was a candidate to succeed Jaya, yet his attitude toward Ryutas was surprisingly friendly.

(Ryutas came here as an individual seeking asylum. …Well, I’m not sure if “asylum” is the right term in this case. But Gimekara moved the entire Zolgue Trading Company. I suppose that’s the difference.)

The influence exerted on those around them differs between an individual and a guild itself.

Gimekara, in particular, went so far as to tell Daskar outright that once the Zolgue Trading Company had solidified its foothold and gained power in Gilm, it would make contact with the underworld.

Naturally, Ronitas had heard about these circumstances from Daskar, so it was only natural that his attitude would become more stern.

Ronitas also loves the city of Gilm, and for that reason, it was difficult for him to be friendly toward anyone who seemed likely to harm Gilm.

Perhaps Gimekara understood Ronitas’s feelings, for despite the stern rebuke, he smiled and spoke up.

“That’s true. However, while that might be possible in Gilm, it’s a different story elsewhere… In the villages and towns near the Kingdom of Reburina, we simply can’t manage to assemble a group of skilled adventurers. Of course, we’ll do everything within our power, but we can’t claim we can do what we can’t.”

Hearing that, Ronitas couldn’t say anything more.

If he were to force the merchant to claim they could do the impossible, that would be imposing an unreasonable burden on them.

And that, coming from a member of the Gilm delegation, was a serious matter.

“...I understand. Well then, let’s start by exchanging information again. I’ve already exchanged information with Rei and the others, but this is my first time with the rest of you.”

With that, Ronitas began to speak in detail about the Kingdom of Reburina and Jaya… and about Rossi and Mejogo.
