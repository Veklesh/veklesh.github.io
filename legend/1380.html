The day after the troublesome commotion ended, Rei and the others showed up at the guild, avoiding the busiest morning hours. Lenora, who had been waiting, spoke up.

"Rei, instead of transporting the felled timber today, could you join the other adventurers in investigating Treant Forest?"

Rei had half-expected those words.

Well, to be precise, Marina had mentioned it during breakfast earlier that day.

...It wasn't that he didn't wonder why Marina, who should have a house in the Noble District, was eating breakfast at the Dusk Wheat inn.

The cause was undoubtedly influenced by yesterday's incident.

Given that, it would be a lie to say Rei wasn't concerned.

After all, the magic stone that should absolutely exist within a monster was missing.

How such a creature could exist was a mystery, but precisely because of that, this incident weighed heavily on his mind.

That was precisely why, upon hearing Lenora's words, Rei nodded in response without any particular hesitation.

"Understood. ...Have you heard about yesterday's incident?"

"Yes. Well, I'm in charge of Rei... or more accurately, the Crimson Wings."

"If you don't like it, I'm always willing to take over for you. ...Seriously."

Kenny, who had been watching Lenora talk to Rei alone, chimed in, demanding to be included in the conversation.

Then again, it was Kenny, not Lenora, who brought Rei the urgent request last night.

Given that, it wasn't really strange for her to butt in like this. ...Though, when Rei returned to the guild last night, it was already past closing time, so Kenny wasn't there.

"Look, it's not like I mind. More importantly, what about the paperwork?"

"Done. Here."

At Lenora's words, Kenny handed over the paperwork he'd been processing.

Though Kenny might seem like she was just fooling around from the outside, when it came to work, she could do it properly if she put her mind to it.

Otherwise, she wouldn't be able to work as a receptionist for the guild, which has strict hiring standards.

Though it was also true that it took him a while to actually get motivated.

Receiving the documents from Kenny, Lenora quickly scanned them for any omissions or mistakes.

Confirming they were flawless, she opened her mouth with a hint of disappointment.

"Perfect."

"...Why do you look disappointed? Isn't this the place to praise me more?"

If she left it at that, an argument would start.

Anyone familiar with Lenora and Kenny's personalities would understand why she thought that.

"Okay, that's enough."

That's precisely why Marina stepped in like that.

"Stop bickering and get back to the topic at hand?"

"Lady Marina..."

"Ugh!"

Lenora and Kenny stopped moving the moment Marina's name was called.

Of course. Lenora deeply respected Marina, and while Kenny respected her too, he was even more uncomfortable around her.

Admonished by Marina, Lenora spoke again.

"Anyway, the investigation team is currently preparing to head to Treant Forest. We'd like Rei and the others to assist them there as well."

"...They haven't left yet?"

Rei was slightly surprised by Lenora's words.

Of course. It was still morning, but the guild's busiest hours had already passed.

In other words, it wouldn't be strange if the adventurers who accepted the Treant Forest investigation request had already departed.

No, considering the distance to Treant Forest, it wouldn't be strange if they'd already begun the investigation.

"Yes. This investigation itself was decided on suddenly, so we've been a bit delayed with preparations on our end. We're currently loading the supplies onto the carriage."

"If I'd come to the guild any later, would they have left without me?"

Rei and the others usually arrive at the guild around this time.

But since they weren't bound by any specific agreement, arriving thirty minutes or even an hour late wasn't unusual.

In fact, there was even the possibility they might not show up at the guild for some reason.

What would have happened then?

Lenora smiled at Rei's questioning gaze and spoke.

"I figured Rei would show up around this time... and besides, even if he was late or didn't come at all, Kenny is here at the guild."

Being a feline beastman, Kenny possessed exceptional physical abilities.

Of course, he couldn't fight on equal footing with those trained as adventurers, but his running speed was undoubtedly far superior to Lenora's.

Kenny, caught in Rei's gaze, hesitated, unsure if he was being praised or just being used... but in the end, since it was in front of Rei, he smiled and spoke up.

"Right. If you're looking for Rei, leave it to me."

"...If possible, I'd like you to put that much effort into matters other than Rei-san too."

Lenora sighed.

She'd known Kenny for quite some time, yet she still hadn't gotten used to this side of him.

Then again, it was undeniably part of Kenny's personality, and along with his man-pleasing physique, it was one reason adventurers took a liking to him.

It was probably inevitable that most adventurers who took a liking to Kenny were men.

Conversely, Lenora, who handled her work reliably, was popular among female adventurers.

"Anyway, please stop talking about yourselves. Byune looks bored."

Marina's gaze fell on Byune, who had been looking towards the bar. At those words, she turned to look at Marina.

It would be more accurate to say she wasn't bored, but rather her attention had been captured by the aroma of food wafting from the tavern.

In the tavern, several adventurers were eating a slightly late breakfast, perhaps having decided today was a day off.

...In fact, there were even some tough guys who had been partying since yesterday and hadn't stopped yet.

The drifting aroma of food was mixed with the smell of alcohol... but fortunately, or perhaps unfortunately, Byune seemed to have his appetite stimulated even in such a situation.

"Hmm."

Byune muttered a word to Marina, but what it meant was clear enough from the way she turned her gaze back toward the tavern.

"Now, now, you just ate. Lunch can wait until later."

Vihera, who had been silently listening to the conversation, said this upon seeing Byune's expression.

At Vihera's words, Byune lowered her head slightly.

Her expression hadn't changed, but it was clear she was disappointed nonetheless.

Once the conversation settled, Rei spoke up.

"Alright, today's mission is to investigate Treant Forest. Is everyone ready?"

"Yes, I'm ready."

"Me too."

"Mm."

Marina and the others each replied.

Even Byune, who had been left behind yesterday, nodded firmly now.

(Ah... well, I suppose so. It's just an investigation today, so having Byune come along shouldn't be a problem. After all, the monsters that appeared were just Treants and the like.)

Though the treant possessed the unusual trait of lacking magic stones, making it hard to believe it was a monster, its strength itself wasn't particularly noteworthy.

In fact, Ray thought Byune could probably defeat it without much trouble now.

As Rei thought this, he noticed Byune looking at him.

"You're not leaving me behind today, right?" Rei, receiving that gaze filled with such intent, smiled slightly and opened his mouth.

"Yeah, I'll take you with me, don't worry. Though, based on all the times we've been to Treant Forest before, I doubt today's investigation will reveal anything new."

"Hmph!"

At Rei's words, Byune's expression didn't change... yet she replied with slightly more force in her voice than usual.

"Oh, Rei. You're coming too? Well, that's a relief."

When Rei and the others arrived at the location where the survey team was preparing, several carriages were ready, and luggage was being loaded into each one.

One of the people working there... Runo, seeing Rei and the others arrive, smiled happily.

Beside her, Slesha bowed her head toward Rei and the others.

There were also quite a few adventurers serving as guards, and they all wore welcoming expressions upon seeing Rei and the others arrive.

Since most of those who had gone to Treant Forest yesterday as the woodcutter's escort were present, Rei felt relieved that no one looked down on him or tried to pick a fight, sparing him a lot of trouble.

That said, some who had seen Rei yesterday did cast jealous glances his way, particularly at the stunning beauties accompanying him—Marina and Vihera, whose mere presence captivated the eye.

...Others were captivated by Byune, and Rei kept a close eye on such individuals to ensure they didn't act strangely.

"Because of yesterday's incident. ...Are you listening?"

"Of course. Frankly, I wouldn't even consider conducting an investigation otherwise. ...In that sense, good work."

A hint of pity even flickered in Runo's gaze.

But that was only natural.

If it were Runo, being suddenly summoned after finishing today's work while relaxing or drinking, and being told to go to Treant Forest, would be completely unacceptable.

Though, given he was drinking, he probably wouldn't have been asked anyway.

"Yeah. It was really tough in so many ways. And then we ended up encountering something like a monster without a magic stone."

"I heard about that, but is it true? I can't believe there are monsters without magic stones."

Runo wasn't the only one thinking that.

Those around them, loading luggage onto the carriage or checking their own weapon equipment, also turned their attention to Runo and Rei's words.

Monsters always possess magic stones.

It was precisely because of that understanding that they couldn't fully believe in the existence of monsters without magic stones.

If Rei hadn't brought Treant's corpse as proof, it was unclear whether Walker would have believed it either.

That's precisely why it was only natural for Runo to seek confirmation from Rei like this.

Aware of the eyes on him, Rei nodded in response.

"Yeah, no doubt about it. I personally examined Treant's body, and I brought the still-intact corpse to the guild too. ...Well, if the Treant they examined at the guild had a magic stone inside, then maybe I could've been mistaken..."

Though Rei said this, he was already certain it wasn't a mistake.

And that was precisely why this investigation of the Treant Forest was happening now.

"Yeah... I don't know if I should say it's unfortunate or fortunate in a case like this."

If a monster without magic stones were made public, it would cause a huge stir.

If that happened, Rei's name would naturally become widely known as the first discoverer of that monster.

Many already knew of Rei, the one with the Crimson Nickname, but discovering a monster without a magic stone would mean even adventurers and... even researchers who normally had no interest in worldly matters would come to know of Rei's existence.

But making a name for yourself also invites trouble.

If researchers solely focused on their own studies learned of Rei, trouble would surely follow.

Understanding this, Runo had deliberately been vague.

"Well, if it comes to that, there are ways to handle it. Like, disappearing somewhere for a while."

Those words were possible precisely because of the existence of Set, who could fly.

In fact, Rei had already evacuated from Gilm several times before by riding Set.

But... that was only possible for Rei and Set alone...

"Oh dear, Rei. What about us then? We could cling to Set's legs to move, but it wouldn't be for long, would it? We're delicate women, after all."

"...Fragile?"

The words Marina spoke made Rei mutter reflexively.

At least in Rei's mind, a dark elf who wielded spirit magic with unrestrained skill, capable of facing countless enemies, or a being who craved battle against formidable foes, was not what she would call a delicate maiden.

Still, perhaps realizing that saying it outright would lead to trouble, she averted her gaze to escape the intense stares Marina and Vihera were fixing on her...

"Preparations are complete. We're heading to Treant Forest!"

The adventurer participating in the investigation's voice echoed, and seizing the opportunity, he began walking toward them.

Leaving behind two beautiful women with seductive smiles and one girl, still expressionless as ever.
