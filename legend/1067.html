The giant cicada that appeared measured about twenty meters in total length. It possessed five legs on each side, and the tips of these legs bore several visibly sharp spines.

Four transparent wings sprouted from its back, so clear you could see through them.

However, the apparent thinness of the wings did not mean they were fragile.

In fact, even though it hadn't fully completed its molting, it managed to deflect attacks like Set's Wind Arrows and Ice Arrows.

True to its cicada-like form, its face also resembled that of a cicada.

Rei had lived in the mountains in Japan, so he'd seen cicadas since childhood—enough to make him dislike them intensely.

But... even so, the cicadas Rei had seen were only palm-sized, nowhere near the enormous twenty-meter-long giant cicada before him.

Only now did Rei truly grasp how vastly different the impression could be simply due to size.

"But... if you just move away from the World Tree, I can attack you to my heart's content! Flying Slash!"

Spreading his wings, Rei swung his Death Scythe toward the giant cicada just as it was about to take flight from the World Tree.

The blade unleashed a skill Rei was well-accustomed to wielding.

The slash flew straight toward the giant cicada...

"Giiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiiii

Wings spread wide, the sound reverberated violently throughout the area.

Realizing the source of that sound, Rei instantly shouted.

"Magic Shield!"

A shield of light materialized, only to instantly vanish into particles of light.

"Ultrasonic waves... and that too..."

His gaze shifted from where the Magic Shield had vanished to the ground.

What lay there was grass flattened in all directions... but that wasn't all.

Several patches of ground were torn up, indicating the ultrasonic wave just released was more powerful than the one Rei had taken head-on.

Still, since it could be blocked by the magic shield, it was effectively the same to Rei.

Glancing behind him for just an instant, he saw Set standing in front, shielding the orb, blood flowing from several places on his body.

A look of pain mingled with surprise flashed across Rei's face.

The soft fur growing from Set's body had a silk-like texture, yet it also boasted formidable defensive strength.

Indeed, cutting through it with a mere blade was nearly impossible.

Yet, the ultrasonic blast unleashed by the giant cicada had inflicted deep, bleeding cuts on Set.

Just contemplating the sheer power behind that attack made Rei's face harden.

(Once this fight is over, I'll heal you with a potion right away.)

He thought this as he watched Set, then turned back toward the giant cicada... Only to see the cicada, newly emerged from its chrysalis, trembling violently. Grasping Death Scythe, he closed the distance in one swift motion.

But... Rei's decision was too late. Or rather, in this case, the giant cicada's actions were too fast.

It fluttered its wings, lowering its body for an instant...

"Not a chance!"

Realizing what the giant cicada intended to do, Rei... refusing to let it escape into the sky, gripped the Death Scythe and used Sleipnir's boots to surge upward through the air.

Then, the Death Scythe came crashing down. But just as the blade was about to touch the giant cicada, it suddenly flapped its four wings and took flight.

The blade of the Death Scythe sliced through the air where the giant cicada's body had been just an instant before.

"Dammit! Flying Slash!"

In a last desperate attempt, he unleashed a Flying Slash toward the giant cicada in flight. Yet, the giant cicada deftly tilted its body diagonally using its four wings and easily evaded it.

「Set！」

"GRRRRRR!"

Realizing he couldn't handle this alone while the cicada flew, Rei called out Set's name.

Sleipnir's boots granted the ability to walk in the air, but even so, it was only for a few steps... ten steps was impossible.

After all, Set's presence was indispensable for Rei to face aerial enemies.

Set answered instantly to Rei's call, as expected.

As Rei moved to mount Set's back... he froze upon seeing the light sphere that had approached alongside Set suddenly flash brightly.

The orb was already weakened, drained of magic and sap by the giant cicada.

Despite this dire situation, flashing so violently now could only shorten its own life.

Rei wondered if it was trying to convey something so desperately, but in that instant, the image of the giant cicada flashed through his mind.

Of course, the only reason the World Tree was this weakened was the giant cicada.

But what came to Rei's mind was the direction that giant cicada had flown. Namely... straight up.

And directly above this settlement... more precisely, he knew what lay encircling the settlement.

"A barrier barrier!?"

What existed there was a barrier barrier protecting this settlement from monsters, wild animals, or slave traders.

And the one maintaining that barrier was the World Tree itself...

"Stop it!"

Rei shouted, gazing up at the sky, but the giant cicada showed no sign of caring about his voice at all, flying higher and higher.

Of course. To the giant cicada, Rei was nothing but a troublesome nuisance clinging to it.

An enemy whose single strike could inflict significant wounds even on its massive body was naturally something it wanted to avoid if possible.

It was a strangely intriguing opponent, but it remained completely unaware that the very magic that had made it so powerful was due to Ray.

Though the giant cicada had gained immense power through Rei's magic and the sap of the World Tree, its intelligence was another matter entirely.

The choice the giant cicada made to distance itself from Rei as quickly as possible... was to ascend straight upward.

"Gigi gigigi!"

The giant cicada, possessing the ability to wield similar barriers, instinctively understood what lay ahead in its path.

It emitted ultrasonic waves similar to those it had just fired at Rei... but this time, it directed them straight ahead.

It would not tolerate anything obstructing its path. Whether it truly felt that way was unclear, but in this instance, the strike aimed at the barrier's protective field proved effective.

Clang!

A sound like shattered glass echoed throughout the surroundings.

That sound didn't just echo where Rei stood by the World Tree. Everyone in the settlement heard it.

And just as the sound of shattering glass suggested, the barrier shatters into fragments, falling toward the ground. Before touching the earth, they dissolve into particles of light and vanish.

Elena and the Dark Elves who had been fighting before the barrier's protective force stopped in their tracks at the sight.

...The barrier that had protected the settlement until now shattered and vanished, as if it were glass or something similar.

No one who witnessed it was unaffected by the shock.

The anomaly wasn't confined to the barrier's protective field alone. The monsters attempting to destroy it were similarly affected.

Until now, the monsters had acted without a glimmer of will in their eyes. But the instant they heard the sound of the barrier shattering, that light of will abruptly returned to their eyes.

Like creatures abruptly awakened from sleep, they looked around in confusion, utterly unable to comprehend their current situation.

Had the monsters remained in their previous state at that moment, the Dark Elves would undoubtedly have suffered catastrophic damage.

Indeed, the settlement itself might have suffered damage beyond recovery.

But... the overwhelming majority gathered around the settlement were monsters.

And most monsters fundamentally regarded any creature not of their own kind as an enemy.

While some monsters could coexist with others, such creatures were only a tiny minority.

Moreover, the monsters were densely packed.

In other words, many monsters found themselves surrounded by nothing but enemies.

Dark Elves were present too, but monsters vastly outnumbered them.

As a result, when the Dark Elves snapped back to reality after the barrier's protective field shattered, what they witnessed was a scene that could be called monsters devouring each other.

"What!? Attack the monsters! They must not be allowed into the settlement! There are many women and children inside!"

Ragdo shouted, and the dark elves, snapped back to their senses by his voice, attacked the monsters.

Even so, they didn't attack indiscriminately.

They made the monsters fight each other, striking the victor when it showed an opening.

Now that the barrier protecting the settlement had vanished, the monsters could enter from anywhere.

Therefore, it was crucial to divert the monsters' attention away from the dark elves and keep them focused on other nearby monsters.

(We're managing for now... but if this continues...)

A sense of urgency overwhelmed Ragdo.

Currently, the sheer number of monsters meant their attention was focused elsewhere, allowing things to be managed. But if the situation continued like this, monsters would inevitably start noticing the Dark Elves and the settlement.

He had to devise a plan before that happened, but there was nothing he could do in the current situation.

(The barrier's destruction means something definitely happened to the World Tree... Which means that pulsation earlier was undoubtedly the cause. Something... I have to think of a way to break this deadlock.)

He turned his gaze toward the direction of the World Tree... Shocked to see it clearly visible despite the barrier that should have hidden it, Ragdo desperately searched for a way to prevent damage to the settlement.

While Ragdo pondered a way to break the current stalemate, Rei had already leaped down from Set's back.

Normally, he should be pursuing the giant cicada that had destroyed the barrier and flown away.

But he couldn't do that now. There was a decisive reason why.

"Set! Sorry, but I need to deal with the World Tree! You take care of chasing down that giant cicada! And since the barrier's protective spell is destroyed, we also need to handle the monsters gathering around here! If you can manage that too, I'd appreciate it!"

"GRRRRRR!"

Set let out a sharp cry at Rei's voice, then took flight into the sky, chasing after the giant cicada.

Seeing Set take flight, Rei ran toward the World Tree.

A flickering orb of light followed after Rei, but the light was so faint it seemed ready to vanish at any moment.

"Dammit, wait for me! I won't let you die. Absolutely not!"

Reaching the base of the World Tree, Rei touched his palm to the trunk and channeled his magic.

He recalled the time he and Marina had channeled magic into the World Tree together.

He channels magic into the World Tree using the same method... but the amount of magic he pours in is orders of magnitude greater than last time.

It was several times, dozens of times, even hundreds of times greater than before.

Normally, pouring this much magic into a tree would cause it to wither and die. ...If handled poorly, the infused magic could even turn it into a monster.

But the tree Rei was pouring magic into now was the World Tree.

The World Tree that had protected the Dark Elf settlement in this land for an eternity.

A World Tree possessing such immense power would never become a monster simply by absorbing the magic Rei was channeling. Believing this, Rei continued to pour his magic into it.

In truth, the World Tree's magic had already been drained nearly to its limit by the giant cicada.

It wasn't just absorbed from the roots. When it became a pupa clinging to the trunk, it dug its feet into the wood, absorbing not only magic power but also the sap vital to the World Tree's very survival.

The proof was that the World Tree had begun withering rapidly.

Ultimately, it forcibly shattered the barrier the World Tree had deployed, and the backlash dealt a fatal blow to the tree.

As the flickering light of the orb was truly fading away indicated, if left as it was, the World Tree would have completely withered... and lost its life.

That was when Rei poured a massive amount of magic power into it.

It was a scene that could truly be called a last-minute rescue.

Just a few more minutes... no, even a few more seconds, and the World Tree would have withered away completely.

Just before the World Tree died completely, a massive amount of magic was poured into it... and the World Tree managed to barely survive.

For several minutes, Rei continued pouring magic into it... and gradually, the leaves of the World Tree, which had been completely withered, became filled with vibrant, lush vitality.

Beside Rei, the orb of light that had fallen to the ground unnoticed began to move slightly, its flickering glow growing stronger.

"...Somehow, I managed to save the World Tree."

Looking up at the sky, watching Set and the giant cicada battle there... she breathed a sigh of relief that the World Tree had been spared.
