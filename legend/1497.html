The day after Ilse's revenge was complete, Rei awoke not at the usual Dusk Wheat inn... but at Marina's house.

It turned out that after the battle ended yesterday, Rei's associates and Ilse had come to Marina's house, talked for a while, and then fallen asleep.

...Rei should have been sleeping on the sofa in the living room, but when he came to, he was in bed.

"Hm...?"

Still sleep-deprived, Rei looked around, but faced with unfamiliar surroundings, all he could do was stretch.

Well, if Rei hadn't been so groggy, it might have been different, but right now, Rei was completely out of it.

Lack of sleep was part of it, but being groggy was definitely the biggest factor.

"Ah..."

He silently surveyed his surroundings for about five minutes.

Finally, as if his consciousness had recovered from its dazed state, Rei looked around again.

"Come to think of it, I stayed at Marina's place yesterday, didn't I? ...I'd completely forgotten."

Muttering to himself, he finished getting ready and left the room.

"Oh, good morning. You could have slept a little longer."

Marina said this from the living room.

Seeing her cooking with a certain cheerfulness, Rei noticed the absence of anyone besides himself and Marina.

"Where's everyone else?"

Even as he asked, Rei could tell Elena and Vihera were in the courtyard.

The reason was clear—the sounds of intense combat were echoing inside the house.

Turning his gaze that way, he saw Elena wielding a whip-like Mirage, and Vihera dodging it while closing the distance.

It was a fight that was a bit too intense to call a mock battle.

Of course, for the two actually fighting, it was probably still considered a mock battle.

But to an uninformed observer witnessing this scene, it would look like nothing less than a duel.

Both possessed power that could be called first-rate... no, beyond first-rate, truly exceptional. That's why, even if it was only a mock battle to them, it appeared completely different to everyone else.

However, Rei was one of the few who could be called super-elite, and Marina was the same.

That's precisely why, even with such an intense battle raging in the garden, they understood it was a mock battle and didn't make a fuss over it.

"Ara and Ilse are still asleep. Oh, come to think of it, Byune is still asleep too."

"Byune aside... that's unusual,"

The reason he muttered that wasn't because of Ilse, but because he'd heard Ara was still asleep.

For Ara, Elena came before everything else.

To the point where it wouldn't be strange for Ara to prioritize Elena over her own life; that's how deeply Ara was devoted to Elena.

That's precisely why it surprised Rei that Elena was still asleep... despite having woken up and even engaged in a mock battle with Vihera.

"She must have been exhausted from yesterday's incident. Seems she was talking with Ilse late into the night."

"Ah... I see."

If asked who, among those currently in the house—including the Crimson Wings—was closest to Ilse, Rei would likely answer Ara.

Of course, Ara's full name was Ara Scarley, and she held the position of third daughter of the Scarley Count's household.

In terms of being a noble lady, she was completely different from Ilse, a commoner. However, most of the people present here were, in various ways, far from ordinary.

Among them, Ara, with her position as an earl's daughter, was arguably closer to an ordinary person.

...Of course, Ara still possessed far greater physical strength than the average adventurer.

But... even so, it was undeniable that she was overwhelmingly inferior compared to Rei and the others' combat power.

That said, in terms of pure combat strength, Byune was actually weaker than Ara. The problem with Byune, however, was his inability to communicate effectively with others.

Vihera could understand Byune's intentions perfectly, but expecting Ilse, who had barely interacted with Byune, to do the same was simply unreasonable.

For that reason, it was only natural that Ara became the closest to Ilse among Rei and the others.

While ability might not be the deciding factor in becoming friends, it certainly helped in this instance.

"Then, we can let Ara and Ilse rest, right? Fortunately, Ara wasn't working to begin with, and while Ilse does work, she's not exactly irreplaceable."

"But doesn't Ara have work too? Like guarding Elena or cooking?"

"...Well, Elena's guard duties aside, cooking isn't exactly the kind of work a knight commander should be doing, is it?"

Even though Ara knows she's the commander of the knight order... Elena's personal guard, she still does things a commander normally wouldn't.

This is largely because Ara is Elena's childhood friend.

Indeed, even now, she's left the knight order in the hands of her deputy and come to Gilm alone with Elena.

Of course, as the commander whose duty is to protect Elena, what she does isn't entirely wrong.

"That's exactly why it's so typical of Ara. If she were a knight commander who acted like a typical knight commander, she wouldn't have become this familiar with us."

"I won't deny that."

Rei nodded at Marina's words... Marina then turned her gaze out the window, toward the courtyard.

"Well, dinner should be ready soon. Could you go fetch those two? Rei is probably the best person to stop them."

"...I suppose so."

Drawn by Marina's gaze, Rei looked in that direction too, then nodded with a hint of resignation.

It was a surprisingly fierce battle, yet the courtyard showed no damage.

Both of them understood this was a mock battle, which was likely why, despite appearing so spectacular, the courtyard remained undamaged.

Rei understood this too, so even though he was still groggy from waking up and feeling a bit irritable, he headed toward the courtyard.

"Ahaha! Ahahahahaha! Yes, yes! That's the spirit!"

Vihera, wearing a smile that showed she was enjoying the fight, tried to close the distance to Elena directly.

"You think I'll let you get that close so easily?!"

To keep Vihera at bay, Elena wields her whip-like Mirage in a flurry of horizontal and vertical strikes.

It was as if the mirage itself had formed a barrier.

At first glance, it wouldn't be surprising to think so, given the speed at which the Mirage was being swung.

(I know this is just a mock battle, but they're really going all out within the limits.)

Elena wasn't using dragon language magic, and Vihera wasn't using one-hit-kill skills like Immersion Palm either.

(I'm the go-to guy for fighting large groups, but honestly, Elena's dragon language magic can cover a pretty wide area too. In fact, I hear she did quite well using it during the war against the Bestir Empire.)

Compared to Elena, Vihera doesn't possess area-of-effect attacks.

...That said, it'd be wrong to assume Vihera isn't dangerous.

Where Rei and Elena excel at area attacks, Vihera's combat style is the opposite—specialized in one-on-one combat.

No matter how sturdy the armor, the fact that a single touch can bypass its defense and deliver a shock directly to the body is, by any standard, extremely troublesome.

(Anyway...)

Rei shifted his focus.

He could stay and watch the fight, but he'd come to announce breakfast time, so he couldn't just stay like this.

Turning to the two who were clearly fighting just for the money, Rei took a deep breath and spoke.

"That's enough! Mock battle, over!"

Rei's voice echoed around them.

It wasn't a shout filled with anger, but a pure cry meant to reach the two of them.

"Whoa!"

"Oh?"

Rei's voice reached their ears as if it were only natural.

The Mirage that had been creating the barrier returned to its longsword form, and Vihera, who had been trying to leap into Elena's embrace through a gap in the barrier, dropped her guard.

"Honestly, after all that commotion yesterday, you two are full of energy."

"Well, even if you say that, the commotion itself was big, but there weren't any strong opponents."

"So, we're actually a bit frustrated," she added.

Vihera declared this, still feeling the afterglow of battle.

"I didn't feel that way myself, but facing Vihera makes for good training."

Elena, lightly swinging Mirage before sheathing it, said this with a smile.

"Well, I understand both of your points, but breakfast should be ready soon. We have work today, so maybe we should stop moving our bodies around now? ...Well, I suppose Elena will be staying here on standby."

"Kyuu!"

As Rei spoke, a cry answered in Elena's stead.

Recognizing the sound, Rei turned his gaze toward the source of the cry.

And there, as expected, or perhaps inevitably... there was Yellow.

Yellow, riding on Set's back, let out a happy cry upon seeing Rei.

"Grrrrr"

And Set, as if it were only natural, purred happily in response.

It had only been a few hours since Rei had parted ways with Set.

Even so, Set, who loved to be affectionate with Rei, probably just felt like being affectionate like this.

"Set and Yellow seem to be doing well too. ...Well, they probably don't have the stamina to be completely exhausted after yesterday's commotion."

"Gururu!"

"Kyuu!"

At Rei's words, the two responded with cries that seemed to say, "Of course!"

As he stroked them, Set narrowed his eyes happily, but Yellow, satisfied after about ten seconds of petting, flew straight toward his owner... Elena.

"Heh heh, what's up? Does Yellow want to be spoiled like Set? Here."

Elena stroked Yellow over his scales.

"...I'm a little jealous."

Watching the two, Vihera murmurs, sounding genuinely a little envious.

Rei had Set, Elena had Yellow—they each had someone to cherish, someone to be their pet.

But Vihera had no such presence.

(If only I could have made it work when absorbing Ambris... but it was impossible.)

To begin with, there was no such luxury when Vihera absorbed Ambris.

She had fought desperately just to win this life.

If asked if she'd want to go through something like that again, she'd absolutely refuse.

"Vihera has Byune, right?"

As Rei murmured while stroking Set, Vihera, hearing this, couldn't help but burst out laughing.

"If you tried to put Byune in that category, you'd get scolded, you know."

"Is that so? In Byune's case, I think just feeding her until she's satisfied is enough to keep her content."

"...Well..."

Hearing Rei continue, Vihera tried to retort but found herself at a loss for words.

In truth, perhaps influenced by her childhood—though she's still quite small now—Byune has a strong fixation on food.

She eats so much you wonder where it all goes.

The only reason she doesn't gain weight is probably because she moves more than she eats during training and missions.

"Hmph!"

And now, just as Byune woke up and peered out the window, she let out a call for him to hurry.

Though Byune usually showed little change in expression, that changed when food was involved.

Hearing Byune's voice, Rei and the others head back inside the house.

Set and Yellow also moved closer to the house and lay down.

Until just now, the two had kept their distance because of Elena and Vihera's fight, but now that the mock battle was over, they had come closer.

Upon entering the living room, a breakfast spread was already laid out on the table: freshly baked bread, soup, ham, and salad.

"You're back. Well then, shall we eat? ...Rei and Vihera seem like they'll be busy again today. Especially Rei, with what happened yesterday, he might get called in by the guild."

"From the guild? Not from Lord Daskar?"

"Yes. Daskar is quite busy with other matters right now. Of course, I expect the final word will come from him, but given the location..."

"...Are you hiding something?"

Rei asked Marina, who murmured meaningfully, though he was suspicious. But the only response was her usual, enchanting smile.

"Well, you'll find out soon enough. No need to worry about it now. Besides, it's about time we ate."

Though still feeling somewhat uneasy, Rei couldn't resist the appetizing aroma wafting over and sat down at the table.
