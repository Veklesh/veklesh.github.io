"Hey, nice to meet you!"

Five men and women bowed deeply to Marina.

It was likely because they knew that Sinarama held Marina in such high regard that they bowed to her—not to Gimekara, who had negotiated with Guild Master Sinarama, or to Rei (the male party leader).

Moreover, these five men and women—the adventurers hired as bodyguards—had been saved by Sinarama on several occasions in the past.

Given that she was someone Sinarama respected, it was only natural, in a sense, that Marina would become the most important person to these newly joined adventurers.

“Hehe, I’m flattered that you’re being so polite to me, but I’m not exactly the leader of this group, you know?”

With a radiant smile that left all five of them—men and women alike—mesmerized, Marina turned her gaze toward Rei, who was drinking fruit water at the tavern attached to the guild.

The five of them had been captivated by Marina’s smile, but as they followed her gaze… and looked at Rei standing there, they all let out a collective “Huh?” in surprise.

Given the current situation, it meant that Rei, whom Marina was looking at, was effectively leading a group of nearly a thousand people—but he certainly didn’t look like he was.

However, no matter what the new bodyguards might think, as long as Marina was displaying that attitude, they couldn’t voice any complaints or doubts.

The only concern was Guild Master Sinarama’s attitude.

Marina, whom they respected and held in high regard, was serving under such a person.

Given Sinarama’s temperament, would he be able to tolerate that? That was what they were thinking… but the Sinarama the five of them saw didn’t seem particularly angry.

However, he was directing a gaze at Rei that was far more serious than one of anger.

It was the kind of gaze that made one feel he was thoroughly sizing up the person known as Rei.

The five adventurers didn’t know why Shinrama was looking at Rei that way. They didn’t know… but still, given that Shinrama was acting that way, it was clear that Rei was not the person he appeared to be.

Ignoring the gazes of the five of them—and the other adventurers watching from the sidelines—Shinara addressed Marina.

“Um, Marina-sama…”

“You don’t need to call me ‘Lady.’ I’m not the Guild Master or anything like that anymore—I’m just an adventurer now.”

Shinara wasn’t the only one surprised by those words.

The other adventurers nearby turned their eyes toward Marina in disbelief.

But judging by her appearance alone, Marina still looked young—she looked to be in her early twenties.

It was simply impossible to believe she was a former Guild Master.

…Though, looking at her pointed ears and tanned skin, it was immediately clear she was a dark elf.

Even so, Marina’s appearance simply didn’t give off the vibe of a former guild master.

“Is that so? But calling me by my first name is a bit much… At the very least, please let me be called Marina-san.”

“If that’s what it takes to satisfy you, Sinarama, then fine. …So?”

“What’s this about?” Marina asked. Shinara glanced at Rei once more before speaking.

“I heard the person with you, Marina, was called Rei.”

“That’s right. Rei. I think you’re probably right, Shinara.”

“So!?”

Shinara’s face contorted in shock as she realized that person might be Crimson.

The adventurers in the guild didn’t pay much attention when they heard the name Rei, but that was due to Rei’s appearance.

However, as the Guild Master, Shinrama naturally knew more than just the rumors circulating about Rei—he was aware of other details as well.

Namely, that despite her strength, Rei was petite and had a feminine face.

“That’s right. Rei is Crimson.”

A murmur ripples through the room.

Those who had been listening to Sinarama and Marina’s conversation turned their eyes toward Rei in astonishment.

It was only now that they finally realized Rei was the adventurer known by the alias “Crimson.”

And while they knew nothing of the situation, many now looked at the man who had provoked Rei with pity.

After all, he had picked a fight with someone far stronger than himself based solely on appearances—a man who, in a sense, wouldn’t be out of place being called a hero.

That said, since that man is guaranteed to receive a lecture from Sinarama along with the title of “hero”—or rather, “hero in a sense”—no one actually envies him.

Rei, who was the center of attention, didn’t seem particularly bothered by it.

It was an attitude born of being accustomed to being the center of attention, including the incident with Set.

“Is that so? That kid… no, that man…”

At Marina’s words, Shinrama muttered thoughtfully.

“Oh, so you’re the ones who’ve joined the escort starting today. I’m Rocks, and I’ve been put in charge of this group’s security. Nice to meet you.”

Saying this, he greeted the five new guards with a casual air. He was Rocks, a Rank C adventurer who had previously served as a guard for the Ronitas delegation but, by order of Daskar, had been assigned to travel with Rei and the others.

A member of the Gilm Guild and a skilled adventurer, he is a man in his late twenties who, as he himself mentioned, has been entrusted with the role of leading the escort.

The members of the Resistance led by Sura also acknowledge that, given Rox’s skills and personality, he is more than capable of leading them.

To begin with, the fact that Suura was leading the Resistance was largely a matter of circumstance.

For Sura, having a seasoned adventurer like Rocks—especially a reliable, mature man—lead them was perfectly fine.

If Rocks were the type to hit on the women and cause trouble, he likely wouldn’t have been accepted by everyone. However, Rocks—who might exchange a few light words but never casts a lustful glance, let alone touches anyone—is fully trustworthy in the eyes of the women as well.

At first, there were suggestions that since Rei and her group were leading the way, they should handle the escort duties… but Rei and her group wouldn’t be traveling with this party indefinitely.

In fact, once they determined that the security detail was sufficiently robust and that things would run smoothly even without them, they intended to return to Gilm.

There was no way Rei and the others could handle the security themselves, so in that sense, Rocks was the perfect person for the job.

“Yes, we’ll do our best!”

The five of them spoke in perfect unison and bowed in perfect unison.

“Oh… oh? You guys are really in sync again. What’s this? Are the five of you a party?”

Adventurer parties are generally made up of two to four people.

However, five-person parties aren’t all that rare, and there are even some who form parties of ten or more.

That’s exactly why, when Rock saw the five people in front of him, he thought they might be part of the same party…

“No, I’m in a party with this guy, and those three over there are in a different party. Of course, we’re not complete strangers or anything.”

One of the five spoke up on behalf of the group, replying to Rocks.

Looking at that man and the other four, Rocks couldn’t help but notice how well they seemed to work together, considering their circumstances.

Judging by Rocks’ attitude, they must have realized how they were being perceived. The man smiled and spoke.

“Well, the Guild Master told me to make sure I never act disrespectfully toward everyone I’ll be working with from now on.”

Having seen at the guild what happened to the man who had picked a fight with Rei, he had no intention of doing anything that might upset them.

“Hmm. Well, whatever. Anyway, as you can see, this is quite a large group. It’ll be a lot of work to guard them all… but you’ll get used to it as you go.”

Rocks turned his gaze toward the large group outside the city, preparing to depart.

Most of those preparing were women, and as expected of a group gathered by Jaya to work as prostitutes, many of them were quite attractive.

The men in the group of five looked at the women and couldn’t help but smile.

“I can understand why men would be drawn to women like you, but don’t you dare even think about hitting on them. If that happens, I can’t guarantee what will happen to you… to be honest, I can’t even guarantee your lives.”

“Yes!”

Three of the men in the group immediately responded to Rox’s words.

That’s because his tone didn’t sound like he was joking at all.

...Meanwhile, the two women were glaring at the men, but for better or worse the men didn’t seem to notice.

“Alright, next… look over there.”

The five of them looked in the direction Rocks indicated and noticed a group of women standing there.

Even though they were about to depart, for some reason the women hadn’t gotten into their carriages yet.

“Um, what’s that? It looks like a strange crowd has gathered.”

Why were so many women gathered there? And why were the women in the crowd wearing such joyful smiles?

Unable to figure it out, one of the men asked.

Rocks responded to the man’s question with a smile.

It wasn’t a smirk meant to mock someone who didn’t know any better, but rather a smile that seemed to say, “We used to have that same question ourselves.”

That’s why none of the five harbored any ill will toward Rocks.

“Set is over there. Set… Would you know him if I said he’s Rei’s partner?”

“Griffon!?”

At Rocks’ words, all five of them let out cries of astonishment in unison once again.

In reality, the existence of a high-rank monster like a Griffin is something you might see once in a lifetime, if at all... no, it’s practically impossible to even catch a glimpse of one.

So, hearing that it was right in the center of those women’s group, it was only natural for them to be shocked.

And along with that surprise came fear.

While not quite on the level of a dragon, a griffin is still a monster that, if encountered, is said to spell the end of one’s life.

“Calm down. Take a closer look at those women. They certainly don’t look like they’re despairing, terrified, or sad, do they? …Well, maybe there are a few who are sad.”

There are those who are sad because moving means they won’t be able to admire Set for a few hours.

That said, that sadness isn’t the kind of hopeless despair you’d feel if you were facing death.

Seeing the women’s demeanor, the five of them must have realized this as well.

When Rock was met with questioning glances asking “Why?”, he opened his mouth as if to say he fully understood their feelings.

“Griffon, you don’t need to worry about Set in particular. Basically, as long as we don’t go provoking him, he won’t harm us. Just because Set is a high-rank monster doesn’t mean you should get any funny ideas. Set is Rei’s familiar, after all. Plus, Set serves as the emotional backbone of this group.”

“A pillar of strength…”

Rocks heard the words muttered in disbelief.

In fact, for the five of them, who didn’t know much about this group, it must be completely incomprehensible why things were like that.

“Don’t worry too much about that. If you stick with this group, you’ll get used to it eventually. At least, I’ve only been with them for a short while, but I’ve already gotten used to it.”

He said that, but in Rox’s case, he was an adventurer affiliated with Gilm.

Naturally, he knows how Set is treated in Gilm, and he’s also witnessed Milein—who could be called the foremost Set enthusiast—admiring Set.

To someone like Rocks, the sight of Set being doted on isn’t strange at all.

(I wonder how long it’ll take them to accept Set… Well, they’re still young, so I don’t think they’d say anything reckless like “Kill him even if he’s a familiar” just because he’s a monster. Besides, I don’t think they’d do anything as foolish as the Azoth Trading Company did.)

If this were the Rei of the past—back when he was still a low-rank adventurer—it might be a different story, but the Rei of today is a high-rank adventurer with a nickname.

What’s more, among his companions is Marina, a former guild master, and Elena, the daughter of the Duke of Kerebel and known by the nickname “Princess General.”

Only someone who’s practically asking to be killed would mess with people like that.

“Anyway, setting aside your affection for Set, just don’t go making any strange moves. If you do that, you should be able to make it to Gilm safely. …Let me ask again: it’s okay for us to go to Gilm, right?”

“Yes. I’ve heard that Gilm is currently undergoing expansion, so there’s no shortage of work there… and since I’ve always wanted to visit Gilm, I have no problem with it.”

And so, with a new escort in tow... Rei and the others set off toward Gilm.
