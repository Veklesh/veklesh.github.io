"Grrrrrrrrrrrrrrr!"

Set let out a loud roar, as if to make sure the whole area could hear him, and flapped his wings.

It had already been about thirty minutes since they set off toward Gilm, but Set was flying freely through the sky filled with cumulonimbus clouds.

Until now, he had had to travel on foot in order to keep up with the carriage.

It wasn’t that Set disliked walking, but perhaps due to his instincts as a griffin, he still preferred to fly.

In that sense, flying at full speed all the way to Gilm this time was the ultimate change of pace for Set… no, it was probably even a form of play for him.

Although he was flying while carrying the Set Cage, that amount of weight was no problem at all for Set.

Set was enjoying flying to his heart’s content for the first time in a while… but the real pitiable ones were the animals and monsters in the forest below.

Just as they were sleeping as usual, or walking through the forest in search of water and food, Set’s cry suddenly echoed throughout the area.

The moment they heard that cry, they realized they were no match for him, so the animals and monsters held their breath, trying their best to let Set pass by.

...However, some were so thrown off by the sheer intensity of Set’s cry that they panicked, running aimlessly around and crashing into trees.

Unaware of the commotion he was causing on the ground, Set flapped his wings and pressed on.

“As long as I stick to the road, I shouldn’t get lost, right?”

“Grr!”

In response to Rei’s words, Set lets out a cry to indicate everything is fine.

……Although Set let out a cry to indicate everything was fine, Rei couldn’t quite bring himself to fully trust it.

After all, with Set, it’s not uncommon for him to head off in that direction before you know it if he spots something interesting… or someone.

That said, it’s not just Set; Rei isn’t much different either.

The difference is that, unlike Set, Rei’s “interesting things” tend to be bandits, which makes his situation a bit more dangerous.

“Huh, are those… bandits?”

Rei muttered as he spotted nearly ten men moving through the woods below.

But just because nearly ten men are moving doesn’t mean you can immediately assume they’re bandits.

After all, one couldn’t rule out the possibility that they were adventurers from a nearby village or town, or perhaps hunters.

It would be a different story if someone were being attacked, but given the current situation, I don’t want to go out of my way to head down into the woods just to ask, “Are you bandits?”

(Though, thinking of Gimekara and the others, it would probably be best to eliminate bandits whenever possible.)

Even so, going down to the ground every time I see a similar scene just to check if they’re bandits would be a huge hassle.

Especially since I’m not just carrying Set and Rei—I’m also carrying the Set Cage.

If I were to go down to the ground, I’d have to set the Set Cage down first, and only then…

When asked if he’d have to repeat that process countless times from now on, Rei’s honest feeling was that it was, no matter how you looked at it, a hassle.

“Well, whatever. The guards aren’t causing any trouble anymore, and some of the women have started to hold their own in battle. …Besides, if they think of it as a way to relieve the stress of Set being gone, they’ll probably give it their all.”

“Grr?”

Did Set hear Rei say his name? Or was something wrong? Set turned his gaze toward Rei, who was riding on his back, but Rei shook his head, indicating nothing was wrong.

“Don’t worry about it. Set, just fly as fast as you can so we can get to Gilm as soon as possible. Of course, if anything happens, it’s okay to give them a little nudge.”

“Grrrrrrrrr!”

Hearing that he could fly as fast as he wanted, Set flapped his wings.

Flying even faster than before, if anyone had been watching from the ground, he would have vanished from their sight in an instant. …Though, thanks to the camouflage function of the Set Cage, he was invisible from the ground anyway.

And so, a few hours after Set began flying to his heart’s content… by the time it was nearly noon, he had covered quite a distance from where he’d started.

"Rei, Byune says she's hungry. Shouldn't we have lunch soon?"

Vihera’s voice came from inside the Set Cage.

Looking up at the sun, which was already directly overhead, Vihera’s suggestion that it was time for lunch was certainly not wrong.

“Got it. I’ll look for a good spot to rest, so hold on a little longer!”

Rei called out to Vihera and the others in the Set Cage, then looked around again… but the ground below was nothing but a vast expanse of grassland.

In the summery sunlight, eating in a place with no shade was best avoided, so Rei looked for a spot—not quite a forest, but at least somewhere with a few trees.

But perhaps the location was poor; he couldn’t find any such trees.

“There aren’t any mountains either…”

If they went to the mountains, there would naturally be trees there.

But there were no mountains near where Rei and the others were currently located.

To be precise, she could see the outline of a mountain in the distance, but even at Set’s speed, it would take a considerable amount of time to reach it, and Rei’s honest feeling was that it would be a hassle to go all the way there just for lunch.

“Hmm… Oh, is that a river over there?”

It wasn’t nearby—it was quite a distance away—but he could still see a river flowing in a spot closer than the mountains.

And where there’s a river, naturally, there are trees growing nearby.

He didn’t know if there were any large trees that would provide shade, but even so, he figured he could spend some cool time by the river without succumbing to the summer heat.

With that in mind, and since he hadn’t eaten much fish lately, he signaled to Set to head toward the river.

It was a simple signal—just a light tap on Set’s neck—but it seemed to get through to him just fine.

He flapped his wings and flew toward the river, which lay off the main road.

(Summer river fish, huh... Ayu comes to mind first, but I doubt there are any ayu around here.)

Back in Japan, whenever I went swimming in summer rivers, I’d use a spear to catch river fish like ayu, iwana, yamame, and kajika.

It wasn’t uncommon to bring them home and serve them as a side dish for lunch or dinner.

...In fact, it happened so often that my parents would sometimes even say they were getting tired of it.

That was the kind of life we led, sharing the catch with our neighbors.

Thinking back on those days, Rei felt like eating sweetfish, but there were no sweetfish in Elgin.

At least, Rei had never seen any for sale in stores or while playing in the river.

(Maybe I’ll go look for some after I finish lunch. With my current abilities, I can catch a ton of fish and preserve them with Misty Ring.)

Or I could dry them.

Even as he thought this, the river drew closer.

It would take quite a while to get there on foot… or even by carriage.

But if I fly there using Set, even a place that’s quite far away wouldn’t take that long… and soon I arrive at the riverbank.

The river is about five meters wide—not particularly wide.

However, the flowing water was very clear and looked perfectly safe to drink.

“Growl!”

Perhaps Set was pleased to see the river, or perhaps his spirits were lifted, for he let out a low growl.

“What’s wrong? Is it an enemy?”

Hearing Set’s growl, Elena wondered if an enemy had appeared. She called out to Rei from inside Set Cage.

“No, I just spotted a river a little further ahead. I guess that’s what made Set happy. Anyway, it’s not long until lunch, so please tell Byune to hold on a bit longer.”

“Got it. But please do it as soon as possible.”

In response to Elena’s words, Rei replied that he understood.

Of course, just because he said he understood didn’t mean they’d arrive at the river any sooner.

A few minutes later... they finally arrive at the river.

The flowing water glistened as it reflected the sunlight, and just looking at it gave a sense of coolness.

It wasn’t actually that simply looking at it made him feel cool, but it was probably a matter of perception.

(Wind chimes probably have that same effect. ...If you clear your mind, even fire feels cool? Or is it "illness stems from the mind"? Regardless of the exact nuance, it’s roughly that kind of feeling.)

Even as Rei gazed at the river, the river gradually drew nearer as Set descended to the ground.

The Set Cage Set had been holding was released, and the sound of it hitting the ground echoed around them.

Having released the Set Cage, Set soared through the sky once more... and eventually landed on the ground.

“Hmm, this cold soup is delicious. Usually, with cold soup, the fat from the meat solidifies and makes it hard to eat, but there’s none of that here.”

What had been prepared for lunch was, as Vihera had mentioned, cold soup.

It was a soup made by carefully skimming the fat that rose to the surface during cooking, so even when cold, no meat fat or cooking oil floated on top.

The pot containing the soup had been sufficiently chilled before being brought into the Misty Ring, so its coolness didn’t give way to warmth, making it the perfect dish for a summer meal.

“Cold soup and freshly baked bread… It’s not bad, but it’s a strange combination.”

“I see what Elena means. Normally, the soup would be warm and the bread cold, right? I didn’t realize that just switching them around would feel so off.”

The one who said this was Marina, who was using spirit magic to keep the pot of soup cold.

While inside the Misty Ring, it doesn’t get warm, but naturally, once it’s brought outside like this… and especially on a summer day, it can’t possibly stay cold.

It was Marina’s spirit magic that was preventing that.

...If other spirit magic users found out she was using her magic for something like this, they would undoubtedly be astonished by the sight.

“Look, the fish is done for now.”

Rei said this as he placed the fish, which had been skewered and grilling by the campfire, onto a plate.

Originally, he had planned to catch the fish after they finished eating, but when he saw a large stone—no, a boulder—in the river, he suddenly remembered a fishing method he’d seen in a manga back in Japan.

Specifically, the method of hitting the boulder as hard as possible to stun the fish near it with the impact.

So he asked Set to deliver a blow with his front paw—just hard enough to stun the fish without shattering the boulder… and as Rei had predicted, they successfully caught about ten stunned fish.

I released the smaller ones right back into the river—since they were unconscious, they just floated away—but the larger ones were more than enough for Rei and the others to eat.

That said, it wasn’t enough to fill us up, but it was certainly enough for a light meal.

So, everyone ate the fish, which had been sprinkled with salt and grilled.

Since they had been gutted and washed before grilling, there was no need to worry about that.

True to river fish, the mild flavor naturally brought smiles to everyone’s faces.

“The salt really brings it out. Salt really is the best for grilled fish, isn’t it?”

“That’s true. Steamed with herbs is delicious too… but for a meal like this, I think salt is best.”

At Rei’s words, Vihera smiled and replied.

When it comes to river fish dishes, salt-grilled is probably the most common.

Above all, the fact that it’s easy to prepare is a major plus.

The herb-steamed dishes Vihera mentioned take a fair amount of time, simply because they’re steamed.

Since herbs are included in the Misty Ring, it’s not impossible, but…

In any case, after finishing lunch while chatting like that, they took a short lunch break.

And once the break began, as expected, Rei and Set began to focus on catching fish.

They caught fish by hitting rocks to stun them, as they had done earlier, or by wading into the river to quickly spear them, or by blowing them onto the ground.

With one person and one fish doing that, it was only natural that the others would find it interesting.

Unusually, Elena also entered the river with a look of curiosity, while Marina, having been born and raised in the forest, caught fish using spirit magic without any particular difficulty, and Vihera used her physical abilities to grab fish with her bare hands.

Amidst all this, the one catching fish most earnestly was, as expected, Byune.

Instead of using her usual long spear, she threw stones she’d picked up nearby at the swimming fish to catch them.

Amidst the group, Yellow was thoroughly enjoying playing in the water, engaging in fierce battles with the river crabs.
