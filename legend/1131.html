Upon receiving reports that the research facility had been attacked, Daldora immediately departed from Augusto's mansion and headed for the facility.

At that time, Augusto requested that Rei accompany them as well, reasoning that with Set remaining at the mansion, it should be safe. Rei complied with the request and headed to the research facility with Daldora.

Daldora was at odds with Augusto over the matter of the Tira Tree, and she had no desire to bring Rei, one of Augusto's allies, to the research facility. However, considering Rei's combat strength, the escort insisted it was absolutely necessary to bring him along, and thus Rei's accompaniment was permitted.

And so, Rei rode along in the carriage Daldora had arrived in, heading for the research facility...

"This is terrible, huh?"

One of the guards muttered as they approached the research facility.

Located on the outskirts of town, opposite the side of the city where Augusto's mansion stood, the building was visibly damaged even from the outside, clearly showing signs of a recent attack.

The fact that it was the research facility where Daldora conducted his studies on the Tira trees, surrounded by few other buildings and located in a sparsely populated area, likely contributed to this attack.

With few people around, even if the attackers brought a large force, they wouldn't be noticed.

That the research facility itself wasn't burned down was perhaps the only silver lining.

...It was already nearing evening, and the surroundings were bathed in the red hues of the setting sun. Depending on how one looked at it, it might have been mistaken for the research facility burning.

"Guh!"

Probably to suppress the frustration within himself. As he looked at the research facility, a strange sound escaped Daldora's mouth.

Though not as grand as Augusto's mansion, the building was large enough to house several typical homes in Gosh. It bore numerous scorch marks, as if someone had deliberately set it on fire to cause chaos.

Yet the fire hadn't grown large enough to consume the entire lab, suggesting they never intended to burn it down completely from the start.

Other cuts, seemingly from slashes, were visible. Parts of the building had been shattered by some impact, and blood, enough to be considered fatal, stained the ground and walls.

The scene—more accurately described as the ruins of a research facility than a functioning one—was more than enough to fill Daldora with extreme irritation.

"This... Why so much... Who on earth..."

One of the guards muttered in a daze. Did he know someone who worked here... or was there someone even closer to him?

His expression seemed to say he couldn't believe his own eyes, and though they didn't voice it, the other guards felt the same.

(There's a pretty strong smell of blood lingering. And then there's that burnt smell, probably from the fire used to cause those scorch marks. ...The only saving grace is that the attackers didn't try to burn the entire lab to the ground. Well, it probably wasn't a choice made out of concern for the lab.)

Probably because setting it ablaze before storming inside would have burned the research materials inside too.

That was Rei's conclusion, but it left the question: Why didn't they burn it down before leaving the facility?

"...We're going in."

"It's dangerous!"

As Daldora tried to enter, one of the guards instinctively protested.

"It looks safe enough from the outside, but we can't be sure there aren't still attackers inside the lab."

"Then what do you want me to do?"

Daldora's reply to the guard carried a hint of irritation, and the objector understood it. However, as a guard, he absolutely could not take Daldora into the research facility.

"We're going in. Please wait here with the other guards."

"Rejected. To begin with, we don't have that many guards with us. That means we'd have to reduce either the number storming inside or the number guarding me outside. I can't risk needlessly endangering my guards."

"But... well, Rei is here..."

One of the guards turned a pleading gaze toward Rei, but Rei shook his head at the guard.

"Personally, I'd prefer to have you assigned inside the lab. These attackers are exceptionally skilled. They're probably not still inside, but just in case any remain, I definitely want to capture them. We need to extract information from them too."

"I'd rather you didn't go inside the lab, Rei."

Perhaps calmed slightly by Rei's words, Daldora immediately rejected his suggestion.

"So you want me to just wait here?"

"Yes. That's what we'd prefer."

"...You said there's probably no one left inside, but that's not certain. Are you sure you don't want to take me, the most skilled one, with you?"

More skilled than us. If told that, the guards wouldn't normally stay silent.

They were hired by Daldora for a hefty reward precisely because they were skilled adventurers.

But when it was Rei who said it, they couldn't possibly argue.

He was a man of renown, with a griffin as his familiar. Regardless of his appearance, they understood all too well that he was overwhelmingly superior in pure strength.

If anyone could stand a chance against Rei, it would only be Narsus, who was currently absent, recovering from his injuries.

...Even Narsus, though, would likely only delay the inevitable a little longer.

"Daldora-san. If the worst happens, I don't know if this group can protect you. If you truly want to be absolutely certain, you should take Rei with you."

A male bodyguard in his twenties told Daldora.

He was confident in his own abilities, but considering Rei's strength, it was better to stay with him.

It wasn't about pride in his own skills; what mattered most now was the safety of his charge, Daldora.

"Hmm... But..."

He understood the advice that it would be safer for his own protection to act with Rei. But this research facility was studying the Tira Tree.

Bringing Rei, who was on the opposing side of the Tira Tree issue with Augusto, into the facility seemed far too dangerous in every sense.

The escort surely understood this too, but right now, Daldora's safety was the absolute priority.

Their differing perspectives led to a clash of opinions, but in the end, it was Daldora who relented.

She remembered the attackers who had stormed the lab, and the arrows that had suddenly flown from nowhere during her own assault today.

If those who attacked the lab were still hiding inside the building... and if those hiding were the same ones who shot the arrows back then.

Thinking this way, being by the side of a skilled adventurer like Rei promised the utmost safety.

"Understood. Then, Rei. I'll be counting on you."

"...Well, I'm not exactly your bodyguard or anything. But if we're going in to catch whoever might be inside the lab, I suppose that's how it'll turn out."

Still, if it meant getting inside the lab, Rei figured he could handle guarding Daldora. Leaving several guards to scout the perimeter, Rei, Daldora, and the other guards entered the lab.

Upon entering the lab, the first thing they noticed was the thick, pungent smell of rust.

Bloodstains, not yet fully dried, were splattered everywhere on the floors and walls, and several corpses lay sprawled on the floor.

Some wore white coats, others what appeared to be civilian clothes, but the number visible just from the entrance easily exceeded ten.

"What... you mean they killed all these people so easily?"

The female escort murmured in horror.

Of course, the woman was also an adventurer, recruited by Daldora. She was confident in her own skills and had killed thieves and the like on previous quests.

But even so, the people she killed were always those who had opposed her.

She couldn't fathom someone capable of killing innocent people so effortlessly.

"...Let's go. The attackers are probably gone from the lab by now, but there might still be researchers alive."

One of the guards muttered, and Daldora seemed to snap back to reality at the sound, stepping inside the lab.

And if their charge, Daldora, was moving forward, the guards couldn't very well just stand still.

Laying slightly behind the group, Rei advanced.

It wasn't that he didn't trust Daldora and the others—he didn't trust them completely, but—it was so he could react immediately if something happened.

(Judging by the bodies, each was killed with a single blow. ...Not just the researchers who worked here, but even those who appeared to be guards. If that's the case, there's no doubt a highly skilled attacker came here.)

Judging by the bodies lying around, some wearing leather armor and carrying longswords or daggers, this lab definitely had security personnel stationed here.

Considering even such individuals were killed so easily, it was impossible not to be wary.

...Though, walking down the corridor like this, nothing unusual happened as I proceeded.

"This is it. Beyond this door lies the most crucial section of the entire facility."

Daldora paused mid-sentence, shifting his gaze toward Rei.

He probably didn't want to let Rei, one of Augusto's allies, inside.

Yet he opened the door without comment, perhaps understanding that anything said now would be meaningless.

As the door swung open, a thicker, more pungent smell of rusty iron wafted out from the room the next instant.

"What the—!"

One of the guards instinctively kicked off the floor, shielding Daldora.

He stood behind Daldora, sword drawn, but no one emerged from the room to attack.

Exhaling in relief, everyone, including Daldora and Rei, entered the room.

"Kutoga!?"

The moment they entered the room, Daldora shouted and started running.

Daldora and her guards rushed toward the figure lying on the floor.

Only Rei didn't understand why Daldora seemed so flustered, but even so, he grasped that the man called Kuto was someone important to Daldora.

"Who is he?"

When he asked one of the nearby guards, the guard hesitated for a moment, unsure if he should speak. But seeing Augusto and Daldora join hands, and considering he had already guided them into the lab, he must have decided there was no point in hiding anything. Watching Daldora rush toward Kuto Ga, he opened his mouth.

"He's the most important person in this lab. He was in charge of the Tira tree research."

Hearing this, Rei looked around the room again.

Then, he truly grasped the size of the room.

In the center of the room, which was about forty tatami mats in size, sand was visible, and several Tira trees were planted.

There were also Tira trees planted in enormous pots, confirming the guard's statement that Tira tree research was conducted here.

"I see. ...So then, those people found dead around that Kuto-ga guy were researchers too?"

The guard nodded at Rei's muttered words.

"Yeah. I hear Daldora spent a lot of money on this lab. If that's the case, this attack must be a serious blow."

"The research will be delayed too, huh?"

Rei recalled what he'd heard from Augusto: that planting Tira trees caused them to attack people.

This research facility, which was studying that phenomenon, had been attacked, and all the researchers had been slaughtered at the same time.

Given that outcome, it was obvious what would happen to the Tira tree research.

(Whoever did it, it could be seen as working to Augusto's advantage. Could they have chosen such a troublesome method just to make Augusto suspect them?)

When an incident occurs, the first to be suspected are those who stand to gain from it.

Especially since Augusto, while opposing Daldora, is significantly outmatched in terms of influence within Gosh.

To bridge that gap, drastic measures would be necessary... and considering that, an attack on this research facility studying the Tira Tree—the very source of their conflict—would clearly grant Augusto a major advantage.

...However, the greatest miscalculation of whoever carried out this attack was likely that they never imagined Augusto and Daldora would join forces, even temporarily.

Then again, since it was only possible because of Rei, it must have been completely unexpected for whoever planned this incident as well.

"Still... really, who on earth would do such a thing..."

One of the guards muttered as he approached a corpse lying a short distance from Ktga.

Rei watched the guard's movements, but suddenly noticed the body the guard was heading towards twitch slightly.

(It moved? Alive? ...No, that can't be!)

He didn't know what basis he had for that judgment.

Perhaps it was because he thought it would be natural for someone injured to cry out for help.

"Get away!"

Regardless, almost reflexively, Rei shouted as he kicked off the floor.
