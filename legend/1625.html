About an hour after Rei and the others parted ways with Vihera and the others... The border between the Kingdom of Mireana and the Kingdom of Reburina should have come into view by now, but they still couldn't spot the envoy's party.

Rei wondered if they hadn't even left their villages or towns yet. But since the sun was already up, it was impossible they hadn't departed.

Of course, if some reason—like someone in the delegation falling ill or getting caught up in trouble—had caused a delay, that would be a different story. But if that were the case, it would likely be difficult for Rei and the others to find them in the first place.

"Grrr?"

"No, nothing. It's just... well, if we keep searching for a while longer and still find nothing, maybe we should start asking around in the villages and towns along the way."

Thinking it was probably unlikely, Rei said this while petting Set.

If anyone in the envoy had a Paired Orb, they could find out where they were now via the Daskar.

But a Paired Orb was an extremely rare item.

Even the ones Rei and Elena possessed had been incredibly difficult to obtain.

(So then, what about carrier pigeons or something like that? Or perhaps accompanying them with summoning magic or a tamer to deliver letters... The Gilm delegation might be able to manage that.)

Given its location on the frontier, Gilm attracts all sorts of people.

Many possess rare skills, so it wouldn't be surprising if some maintain contact with Gilm... or more accurately, with Daskar, through some means.

(But if someone with that kind of ability were with them, it wouldn't be strange if Daskar had informed them beforehand, right?)

Gazing at the road, Rei pondered such things... Around the time they entered the Kingdom of Mireana, Set suddenly made a throat-clearing sound.

「Set？」

At first, Rei couldn't understand why Set had made that sound.

But when he stared intently in the direction Set was looking—the direction they were heading—a speck, or something even smaller than that, caught his eye.

Of course, it was a sight only Rei, with his vision far sharper than that of ordinary people, could have spotted.

And the reason Set had spotted it before Rei was simply because Set's eyesight was sharper than Rei's.

"Is that... a thief?"

They were still too far away for Rei to make out any faces clearly, but even from that distance, it looked to him like something was fighting.

It could have been humanoid monsters, but from this vantage point, it somehow felt different.

Starting with goblins and orcs, humanoid monsters came in many varieties.

But even so, they were humanoid and not human, which created a certain sense of incongruity.

What Rei was watching now was a scene without that sense of incongruity.

In other words, it was human.

Of course, there are elves, dwarves, beastmen... and many other races besides, so this method of identification isn't always reliable.

"Rei, what's wrong?"

Elena's voice came from inside the Set Cage.

She must have sensed something was wrong from Rei's reaction.

"Way ahead... no, they're getting closer now. Anyway, it looks like someone's being attacked by bandits or something."

Even after Rei spotted the victims of the bandit attack, Set kept flying.

Given Set's flying speed, reaching even a somewhat distant location quickly wasn't difficult.

"Bandits? Should we help?"

"Yeah, though... whether they need help is a bit questionable. Seems like there are quite a few skilled ones among them."

In the direction he was looking, the bandits who had been attacking three carriages were being taken down one after another by several guards.

The number of bandit corpses already littering the ground was considerable. In terms of sheer numbers, the bandits had undoubtedly held an overwhelming advantage.

Seeing that scene, one might wonder: were the bandits unexpectedly weak, or were the guards unexpectedly strong?

Which reason it was... well, now that we could clearly see their movements, it was obvious.

"So, what now? Since bandits showed up, should we indulge Rei's hobby?"

Hearing Marina's words, who had been listening to Elena and Rei's conversation with interest, Rei hesitated slightly.

As Marina had mentioned, hunting bandits was indeed one of Rei's hobbies.

While some might call it a slightly questionable hobby, since it reduced the number of bandits attacking honest merchants and travelers, Rei judged that this activity, combining pleasure with practical benefit, deserved praise rather than blame.

...Which was precisely why thieves feared Rei, calling him the Bandit Eater.

But judging by the current situation on the ground, the outcome was already half decided.

The bandits were trying to exploit gaps in the guards' formation to escape.

The guards had no intention of letting them escape. Those who turned their backs were met with arrows from archers, their lives taken instantly.

Those who tried to flee without turning their backs were swiftly closed in upon and finished off by guards wielding longswords and spears.

If everyone turned their backs simultaneously and fled in different directions, some might escape... But even though they're part of the same bandit group, the thought of sacrificing themselves for their comrades probably makes such a move impossible.

If their sense of camaraderie had been stronger, they might have managed something even in this situation.

"If we get involved, we'd just end up stealing their prey. Best to leave them be. If the bandits had the upper hand, it'd be different, but the guards clearly have the advantage. ...Still, those guards are skilled."

Perhaps because he'd been seeing adventurers and guards from the Kingdom of Reburina lately, the men protecting what looked like quite luxurious carriages to Rei's eyes—three of them, no less—seemed exceptionally skilled.

They were so strong, he thought they wouldn't look out of place even in Gilm, not just in a place like this.

The bandits had clearly picked the wrong target to attack.

Then again, seeing three such luxurious carriages together probably made it seem like a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity for a big score.

(Their biggest mistake was failing to realize that carriages that luxurious would naturally have hired skilled guards.)

Whether the bandits knew this and attacked anyway, or if they were too blind to even notice such a thing.

Rei didn't know the details, but given the current situation, he could guess it was probably the latter.

"Anyway, we..."

"Grr?"

...keep searching for the envoy.

Rei was about to say that when Set growled, cutting him off.

「Set？」

Rei, who didn't believe Set would act like that without meaning, turned his gaze toward Set, wondering what was wrong.

Receiving Rei's gaze, Set purred happily.

(Pleased?)

For a few seconds, Rei couldn't figure out why, but when he looked back at the guards, the reason became immediately clear.

Among the guards, there were several familiar faces.

Seeing one of them, there was no doubt why Set had purred happily.

Because...

"Milein? So that means that guard has Burning Wind mixed in?"

Yes, the party led by Milein—who was top-tier even among Gilms when it came to doting on Set... who devoted herself entirely to cherishing Set above her own livelihood, ultimately running out of money for living expenses altogether—was none other than Rank C Party, Scorching Wind.

They'd been involved with Rei and the others for quite some time now.

Being a Rank C party, their skill is undeniable, and they even have a rare mage in their ranks.

With such individuals, it was only natural they could overwhelm the thieves with superior force.

Considering there were several other guards besides Milein and her group, it was obvious that ordinary bandits had no chance if they attacked.

"And... the fact that Milein is here..."

The fact that Milein, who usually operates in Gilm, was here as a bodyguard... near the border between the Kingdom of Mireana and the Kingdom of Reburina, was clearly not normal.

But Rei had an idea why this unusual situation had arisen.

Since Daskar had sent an embassy, it was only natural that the embassy would have been assigned guards.

And the Scorching Wind, led by Milein, possessed sufficient strength to serve as guards without issue.

Furthermore, the three carriages Milein and her group were protecting were all luxurious, making it obvious at a glance that someone of some standing was riding inside.

Indeed, it was easy to understand how the bandits, dazzled by the carriages' opulence, ended up being crushed by Milein's group.

"Set, get down. Looks like we've found our target. ...Ah, just to let them know we're not the enemy, don't make a sound."

"GRRR!"

Set nodded at Rei's words.

And so, with the Set Cage dangling below him, he descended toward the ground, letting out a growl.

"GRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRR!"

"Huh? That sound..."

Milein, who had defeated all the attacking bandits, looked around at the sudden cry.

The others who had been escorted with Milein also looked around, but their expressions were completely different from Milein's.

While Milein, finding it hard to believe, smiled at the familiar cry, the others' expressions had grown stern.

"Stay alert!"

"It's fine."

One of the guards shouted nervously, but Milein immediately called out.

Naturally, the man who shouted "Stay alert!" and the others' gazes turned toward Milein, who had suddenly spoken those words. Yet Milein showed no particular sign of tension... no, quite the opposite. She opened her mouth with a smile that seemed to come from the very depths of her heart.

"Because it's just Set-chan who came."

Everyone present, including Milein, were guards hired by Gilm.

Naturally, they all knew Set's name, and some were aware of Milein's intense affection for him.

『Set！？』

Yet, the question of why Set was here remained unanswered, and that exclamation escaped everyone's lips.

Had they known that Rei and the others were heading to the Kingdom of Reburina for revenge against Jaya, they wouldn't have been this confused.

But that matter had been kept secret—something the adventurer guards shouldn't know.

Rather, it was likely that Milein, who understood Set's cries in such a situation, was the exceptional one.

"If Milein judged it so, then it must be true,"

Sulunin, the mage in the same party who knew how much Milein doted on Set... or rather, how much she had indulged him, put it bluntly.

The archer, Ekril, silently agreed with a nod at Sulunin's words.

But while the others could somewhat believe those words, they couldn't truly, completely believe them from the bottom of their hearts.

They didn't let go of their weapons, instead checking their surroundings... and upon confirming Set was nowhere to be seen, they performed the complex act of relaxing in relief while simultaneously hardening their expressions.

"Was that just my imagination?"

The cry could be heard, but no figure appeared.

In that situation, perhaps the cry they'd heard earlier had been an auditory illusion after all.

The man blurted out that thought, though he understood perfectly well it hadn't been.

He tensed, expecting a monster to attack from somewhere at any moment... when suddenly, a voice came from the direction of the carriage.

"Hey, what's wrong? If we've taken care of all the bandits, then let's move on..."

The voice belonged to one of the men chosen from Daskar to be part of this envoy.

He had stepped out of the carriage, puzzled that no one had come to report the bandits were defeated, even though the sounds of battle outside had ceased.

Naturally, he didn't believe the escorting adventurers would lose to mere bandits... especially the kind that appeared in such a rural area.

His actions stemmed from his trust in the strength of Gilm's adventurers, as a subordinate of Daskar.

But while such actions might be acceptable under normal circumstances, this time, it was nothing short of reckless.

"What!?"

Just as the male adventurer guard was about to tell him to get back in the carriage, a sudden loud noise—not quite a boom, but still a significant sound—and a shockwave spread through the area.

Those who instinctively looked toward the source of the sound and shock saw... an unfamiliar, enormous cage... commonly known as a Set Cage.
