Seeing Rei descend to the surface with Set, those participating in the massive public works project of Gilm's expansion cheerfully wave their hands.

Of course, carpenters are the main players in this work, but relying solely on carpenters summoned from surrounding villages and towns would make this construction project impossible.

If this were a simple village or town, carpenters alone might have managed.

But... while Gilm is nominally a town, its population and scale are practically on par with a city.

To expand it... and by about fifty percent, no less—there's no way the gathered carpenters alone could suffice.

Nearly hundreds of carpenters have gathered, divided into teams, and daily meetings are held to discuss how to proceed with the construction.

For the carpenters themselves, participating in such a large-scale expansion project is an honor.

Naturally, each strives to utilize their skills to the fullest... but with ten people, ten different ways—and carpenters being largely craftsmen at heart—they each insist on doing things their own way.

When such carpenters gather, it's only natural that reaching agreement won't be easy.

In the early days, it wasn't uncommon for disputes over methods to escalate into fistfights.

Carpenters, naturally engaged in physical labor like hauling wood, tended to be more hot-headed and physically strong than many adventurers.

For that very reason, the adventurers present at first couldn't handle the sheer number of carpenters... until Daskar appeared.

Whether it was his background as a former knight or simply his imposing presence, Daskar managed to bring the carpenters to heel with remarkable authority.

In any case, the carpenters now gathered under Daskar and proceeded with discussions regarding the expansion of Gilm.

Meanwhile, the adventurers, including Rei, had various tasks to handle: securing materials for the expansion, gathering tools, arranging housing for the carpenters, procuring food supplies... the list went on.

Among these tasks, Rei was assigned to transport the timber cut in Treant Forest, as Walker had instructed him before.

"Nearly two months since then..."

Sitting atop Set as he descended toward the ground, Rei murmured while gazing up at the cloudless blue sky.

Yes, nearly two months had already passed since that battle with the Giganto Treant in Treant Forest.

The season had already shifted into what could rightly be called early summer, and the sky seemed higher than it had two months prior.

(I really wanted to make progress on dismantling the Giganto Turtle...)

He glanced at the Misty Ring embedded in his right arm from the sky and let out a small sigh.

He had thought that, ideally, he would have started the very next day after the Treant Forest incident was settled...

But dismantling a colossal monster like the Giganto Turtle obviously wouldn't be finished in a day or two.

Given that, it would require hiring a certain number of people for an extended period.

It was Walker who put the brakes on that.

It was only natural. With the expansion of Gilm about to begin, they would be short on adventurers no matter how many they had.

If Rei were to hire a large number of adventurers at such a time, it would cause all sorts of problems for the guild.

Therefore, the request was made: could the dismantling of the Giganto Turtle perhaps wait until after the expansion work... or more accurately, until the adventurers' busiest period had settled down? Hearing this, Marina negotiated with Walker. The deal was struck: the guild would cover all costs associated with dismantling the Giganto Turtle—the adventurers' rewards, meals, tools, and all other expenses.

...The remark about Walker's soot-covered back after the negotiations ended came from Lenora, the female receptionist at the guild.

Rei wanted to extract at least the magic stones as soon as possible, but for now, that was on hold.

Additionally, regarding the reward for handling the Treant Forest matter, he had already received the necessary amount of fire ore.

Since he was still working, he would likely receive additional amounts later.

"Ah, sorry to keep you waiting. Where should I take it?"

"This way!"

Rei dismounted from Set, who had landed on the ground, and entered the guild with the waiting adventurer.

Normally, procedures would be required to enter the city, but those recognized by the guild as trustworthy adventurers, starting with Rei, were permitted to enter freely without formalities.

...Of course, given the condition of being trusted by the guild, only a very small number actually had this free pass.

In that sense, the adventurer waiting for Rei must also be someone trusted by the guild.

"Hey, Rei. Good work."

Rei responded with a light wave to the greeting from the guard.

He would have liked to chat about various things, but Rei was in a hurry himself, and more importantly, the guard was busy processing those entering Gilm.

Many had become busier due to Gilm's expansion, but the guards were probably the busiest of them all.

Expanding the guild naturally required massive amounts of materials.

But it wasn't just that. Carpenters and other craftsmen, adventurers summoned from nearby villages and towns, people seeking work, merchants looking to capitalize on the influx... all sorts of people were heading for Gilm.

And naturally, the guards must process entry procedures for all these people at Gilm.

It was busier than even spring, which was normally the busiest time of the year.

That's precisely why, even if he wanted to talk to Rei, he simply didn't have the time.

"Hey! Why is that kid allowed into Gilm without going through the procedures?!"

Having waited in line for a long time, her frustration had clearly reached its peak.

A merchant woman in her thirties demanded this of the guard. ...Or rather, she practically shouted it.

Why was that child the only one getting in, when they had been waiting in line for so long?

Having waited over half a day, the woman didn't even notice Set's presence.

She only glared irritably at Rei, who had entered Gilm without any particular formalities.

Among those waiting, some had visited Gilm several times before, and those individuals shot the woman disbelieving looks.

But... While Gilm usually had many such regulars, there were also many people visiting Gilm for the first time now.

Among them, some naturally eyed Rei with envy.

Still, seeing Rei accompanied by Set... the Griffin, many understood she was not what she appeared to be.

And those somewhat in the know had deduced that Rei, because he had a griffin with him, was the adventurer known by the Crimson Nickname.

"He's an adventurer who takes on various commissions. For that reason, he's been granted permission to enter the guild without formalities, specifically during this expansion period."

The guard tried to calm the female merchant by explaining this.

(But until the expansion is finished... that'll take years. Does that mean Rei won't need to go through procedures the whole time? Well, in Rei's case, he has an item box, so inspections would be pointless anyway.)

When it came to the procedures for entering the city, the most time-consuming part was, unsurprisingly, the luggage inspection.

Illegal goods that shouldn't be brought in. Or items requiring special permission... Beyond that, there are those who try to bring all sorts of things into Gilm.

Especially now, with so many people gathering here who don't usually come to Gilm.

Naturally, baggage inspections have become stricter as a result... but as long as Rei's item box, Misty Ring, is stored there, the guards can't do anything about it.

In that regard, they could only rely on Rei.

Even so, he wasn't overly concerned, likely because he and Rei had known each other for several years now.

Also, regarding the Gilm expansion, it's not entirely true that nothing could be done without Rei... but there was no doubt that having Rei present made a huge difference in efficiency.

"Hey! Are you listening?!"

The guard lost in thought about Rei snapped back to reality at the female merchant's shout.

"Ah... well, you see..."

Trying to calm the furious person in front of him, the guard opened his mouth to persuade her.

"Wow, this is incredible. I've been at Gilm for several years now, but this is the first time I've seen such a line at the main gate outside of wartime."

An adventurer who had come to greet Rei excitedly announced as they walked down the main street.

It was no wonder. Rei had simply ridden down on Set and avoided the line, but a queue hundreds of meters long had formed before the main gate.

Considering the baggage checks and various procedures for every single one of those people, it was impossible to know how long it would take.

The line was that long.

"Yeah, I'm relieved I didn't have to wait in that line either. ...Is this the right place?"

"Ah, yes. They said this timber should be placed at the third location."

Previously, timber harvested from Treant Forest was secretly transported by the Knights.

However, the city's expansion plans had already been announced and were underway.

That's precisely why secrecy was no longer necessary, allowing Rei to transport it directly like this.

Materials like this were now consolidated in several locations... One of those was the place called the Third, which was where Rei needed to go this time.

"Got it. ...Wait a moment."

After giving the adventurer a quick word, Rei headed for a nearby food stall.

"Give me three skewers."

"Sure thing. Looks like you've got your hands full, Rei."

The shopkeeper, a familiar face to Rei, said with a smile and quickly handed over the freshly grilled skewers.

Paying for them, Ray smiled faintly at the vendor's words and opened his mouth.

"It's true things got crazy busy these past two months."

"Ah... yeah. Since we're expanding Gilm more than ever before, it's not just a little bit busier, is it? Well, thanks to that, business is booming over here though."

The increase in people at Gilm naturally meant more people eating there.

Considering the number of people drawn by this special demand, it was only natural that the restaurants serving them would see increased sales.

Inns were nearly full, and many people with acquaintances in Gilm were crashing at their friends' places.

Given this situation, everyone who originally opened a shop in Gilm started boasting considerable sales.

Moreover, the various dishes Rei introduced—or rather, the chefs introduced—like meat buns, pizza, and udon noodles, were extremely rare and captured the stomachs of those flocking to Gilm.

Sure, you could probably get udon in towns like Abuero or Saburusta... but even so, it's no wonder many people would rather eat authentic udon if given the chance.

"Well, I'll go deliver the goods."

"Yeah, good luck with that."

Exchanging a few words with the shopkeeper, Rei set off again toward the Third Supply Depot.

He had one skewer for himself, one for Set, and one for the adventurer guiding them.

Each of them ate their skewers as they walked down the main street.

New adventurers arriving in Gilm, merchants, those seeking work... Seeing such people startled by Set's appearance had become a familiar sight for Rei.

In terms of distinguishing between Gilm's residents and outsiders, it was actually quite useful.

"So, how's the prep work for the expansion going?"

"The move is already finished, and the houses near the wall are almost completely demolished. Even the last remaining residents seemed to come around in the end."

"Ah, that man..."

Rei's face twisted in displeasure.

If they didn't want to move because it was their hometown or a house they'd lived in since childhood and had grown attached to, Rei could have understood.

But that man had demanded more money.

On top of that, the house he lived in was extremely old—the kind that looked like it would collapse naturally within a few years.

He was the last person Ray felt inclined to listen to.

Recalling the man in his forties with that visibly vulgar grin, Rei opened his mouth reluctantly.

"Did you raise the amount?"

"No. I brought someone he couldn't stand up to and had them give him a stern talking-to. ...Then, immediately—"

"Ah... I see. So that's how it goes."

Talking like that, Rei and the others headed toward the Third Materials Storage Area.
